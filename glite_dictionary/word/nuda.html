Nuda

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːdə / (AmE), / ˈnjuːdə / (BrE)
A class of ctenophores (comb jellies) lacking tentacles, comprising a single genus, Beroe.
class of tentacle-less ctenophores

Examples

The class Nuda includes comb jellies without any tentacles.
Scientists have studied the feeding mechanisms of Nuda species extensively.
Unlike other ctenophores, Nuda possess a large mouth and lack tentacles entirely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ctenophora - phylum of marine animals known as comb jellies

Etymology

From Latin 'nuda', feminine form of 'nudus' (naked, bare). Used in taxonomy to indicate absence of tentacles.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnuːdə / (AmE), / ˈnjuːdə / (BrE)
A class of ctenophores (comb jellies) characterized by the absence of tentacles, comprising the single genus Beroe.
class of tentacleless ctenophores

Examples

The class Nuda consists of comb jellies that do not have tentacles.
Scientists observed members of the Nuda in the deep sea, feeding on other ctenophores.
Unlike Tentaculata, Nuda species capture prey using their large mouths rather than tentacles.

Etymology

From Latin *nudus* (bare, naked), referring to the absence of tentacles.
