dumb

1. ADJ

/ dʌm / (AmE), / dʌm / (BrE)
(dated, sometimes offensive) Unable to speak, especially due to congenital deafness; mute.
unable to speak (dated, sometimes offensive)
archaic offensive

Examples

The school once had special classes for dumb children who could not speak.
Historically, the term dumb was used to describe people who were born deaf and unable to speak.
Outdated medical texts often refer to someone as dumb if they were mute from birth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mute - unable to speak

Etymology

Old English *dumb*, from Proto-Germanic *dumbaz* (mute, silent).

2. ADJ

/ dʌm / (AmE), / dʌm / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) lacking the power of speech; unable to speak.
unable to speak

Examples

Many species are considered dumb because they cannot use language as humans do.
The horse stood there, dumb and unmoving, as if it could not utter a sound.
Before modern medicine, some children born deaf were wrongly thought to be dumb as well.

Synset Examples

dumb animals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mute - unable to speak; silent

Etymology

Old English dumb ('mute, speechless'), from Proto-Germanic *dumbaz.

3. ADJ

/ dʌm / (AmE), / dʌm / (BrE)
temporarily unable to speak, often due to shock, surprise, or emotion
temporarily unable to speak

Examples

She was struck dumb when she saw the surprise party.
He stood dumb before the audience, unable to say a word.

Related Terms

Etymology

Calling someone dumb is both rude and unhelpful.
She thought the puzzle was too dumb for adults, but the kids enjoyed it.
