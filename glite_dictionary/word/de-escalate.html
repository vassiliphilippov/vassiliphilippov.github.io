to de-escalate

1. VERB

/ ˌdiːˈɛskəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˌdiːˈɛskəleɪt / (BrE)
to reduce the intensity, seriousness, or scope of a situation, especially a conflict or crisis
reduce the intensity or seriousness of something

Examples

The negotiator worked hard to de-escalate tensions between the two groups.
Effective communication can de-escalate conflicts before they turn violent.
The company introduced new policies to de-escalate customer complaints swiftly.

Synset Examples

de-escalate a crisis

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to escalate - increase the intensity or seriousness of a situation

Etymology

Formed from the prefix de- (indicating reduction) and escalate (to increase in intensity or extent), modeled after 'escalate'.

2. VERB

/ ˌdiˈɛskəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˌdiːˈɛskəleɪt / (BrE)
to reduce the size, scope, or intensity of something, especially a conflict or tense situation
reduce intensity, especially of conflict or crisis

Examples

The negotiator worked hard to de-escalate tension between the two groups.
New safety protocols helped de-escalate the crisis quickly and effectively.
She tried to de-escalate the argument before it got out of hand.

Synset Examples

The war of words between them de-escalated with time

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to escalate - to increase or intensify, especially in seriousness or severity
  • Synonym: to lessen - to become or make less; diminish

Etymology

From the prefix 'de-' meaning 'down', combined with 'escalate', which comes from 'escalator'.
