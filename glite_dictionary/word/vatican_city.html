Vatican City

1. PROPN

/ ˈvætɪkən ˈsɪti / (AmE), / ˈvætɪkən ˈsɪti / (BrE)
an independent city-state enclaved within Rome, Italy, serving as the residence of the Pope and the administrative center of the Roman Catholic Church; the smallest sovereign state in the world
smallest independent city-state, papal residence

Examples

Every year, thousands of tourists visit Vatican City to see St. Peter's Basilica.
The Pope delivers his Christmas message from Vatican City to Catholics around the world.
Vatican City became an independent state with the signing of the Lateran Treaty in 1929.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Holy See - the jurisdiction of the Pope and the central administration of the Roman Catholic Church

Etymology

From Latin 'Vaticanus', relating to the Vatican Hill, location of the city.
