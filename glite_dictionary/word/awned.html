awned

1. ADJ

/ ɔnd / (AmE), / ɔːnd / (BrE)
(of a plant or part of a plant) having awns, which are bristlelike or hairlike appendages on the flowering parts, especially in cereals and grasses.
having bristlelike appendages (awns)

Examples

The awned variety of barley is more common in wild populations.
Botanists examined several awned grasses growing in the field.
The farmer chose awned wheat because it is more resistant to certain pests.

Synset Examples

awned wheatgrass

Related Terms

  • Antonym: awnless - lacking awns or bristlelike appendages

Etymology

From awn (a bristlelike appendage) + -ed.
