pep

1. NOUN

/ pɛp / (AmE), / pɛp / (BrE)
energy and enthusiasm; liveliness or vigor, especially in someone's behavior or actions.
liveliness; energy; enthusiasm
informal

Examples

The cheerleaders brought lots of pep to the football game.
After a strong cup of coffee, he felt full of pep again.
Her pep made her a favorite among her colleagues at work.

Synset Examples

this tonic is guaranteed to give you more pep

Related Terms

  • Synonym: energy - physical or mental vigor and vitality
  • Synonym: spirit - lively or energetic quality
  • Antonym: sluggishness - the quality of lacking energy or alertness

Etymology

Shortened from 'pepper', originally referring to spirit or energy, possibly influenced by the stimulant qualities of pepper.
