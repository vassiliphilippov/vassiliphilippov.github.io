Oncidium

1. NOUN

/ ɑnˈsɪdiəm / (AmE), / ɒnˈsɪdiəm / (BrE)
A large genus of showy orchids native to tropical and subtropical America, often grown as epiphytes or lithophytes.
a genus of showy American orchids

Examples

Many collectors admire the vibrant flowers of Oncidium species for their unique shapes and patterns.
The Oncidium genus is well-known among horticulturists for its extensive variety of orchid hybrids.
Botanists have identified hundreds of Oncidium varieties thriving in the rainforests of Central America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchid - a plant of the family Orchidaceae, noted for striking flowers

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'onkos' (swelling), referring to a feature of the plant's column.
