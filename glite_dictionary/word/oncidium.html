oncidium

1. NOUN

/ ɑnˈsɪdiəm / (AmE), / ɒnˈsɪdiəm / (BrE)
A genus of orchids characterized by slender, branching sprays of small yellow and brown flowers, often grown as houseplants or ornamentals.
a genus of orchids with yellow and brown flowers

Examples

She has a beautiful oncidium displayed in her living room.
Many collectors prize oncidiums for their delicate, branching flower sprays.
After months of care, the oncidium finally produced its distinctive yellow and brown blooms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchid - a plant of the orchid family, known for often showy flowers

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek onkos (swelling), referring to a callus on the lip of the flower.
