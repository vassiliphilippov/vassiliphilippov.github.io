Connochaetes

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑː.noʊˈkiːtiːz / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.əˈkiːtiːz / (BrE)
A genus of large African antelopes, commonly known as gnus or wildebeest, characterized by their robust build and curved horns.
genus of African antelopes (gnus, wildebeests)

Examples

The Connochaetes genus includes both the blue and black wildebeest species.
Researchers observed the migration habits of Connochaetes in the Serengeti.
The horns of Connochaetes are a distinctive feature used to identify them in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gnu - common name for species of Connochaetes
  • Synonym: wildebeest - another common name for species of Connochaetes

Etymology

From Greek 'kónnos' (beard) and 'khaítē' (flowing hair, mane), referring to characteristic features of these animals.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɒnəˈkiːtiːz / (AmE), / ˌkɒnəʊˈkiːtiːz / (BrE)
A genus of antelopes in the family Bovidae, commonly known as gnus or wildebeests, native to Africa.
genus of antelopes (gnus/wildebeests)

Examples

The Connochaetes genus includes both the blue and black wildebeest.
Scientists observed the migration patterns of Connochaetes across the Serengeti.
Members of Connochaetes are well adapted to life on African savannas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gnu - large African antelope, also called wildebeest
  • Synonym: wildebeest - another common name for gnus; member of genus Connochaetes

Etymology

From Greek 'konnos' (beard) and 'khaite' (long hair or mane).
