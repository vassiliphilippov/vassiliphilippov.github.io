deprecating

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɛprəˌkeɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdɛprɪkeɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
showing disapproval or making something seem unimportant; tending to disparage or belittle
showing disapproval or belittlement

Examples

She gave a deprecating laugh when praised for her achievements.
His deprecating glance suggested that he doubted the plan would succeed.
The author's deprecating remarks about her own work were meant to be modest.

Synset Examples

belittling comments
managed a deprecating smile at the compliment
deprecatory remarks about the book
a slighting remark

Related Terms

  • Synonym: belittling - making someone or something seem unimportant
  • Antonym: approving - showing approval or positive regard

Etymology

From Latin deprecat-, past participle of deprecari (to ward off by prayer).
