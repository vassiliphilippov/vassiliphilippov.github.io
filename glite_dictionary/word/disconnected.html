disconnected

1. ADJ

/ ˌdɪskəˈnɛktɪd / (AmE), / ˌdɪskəˈnɛktɪd / (BrE)
(of a device or appliance) not plugged in or connected to a power source.
not plugged in or connected to power

Examples

The lamp remained disconnected after I cleaned the table.
Before making repairs, ensure all equipment is disconnected from the power outlet.
She noticed her laptop was disconnected, so she plugged it in to charge.

Synset Examples

the iron is disconnected

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unplugged - not connected to an electrical source or device

Etymology

From the verb 'disconnect' (Latin dis- 'apart' + connect 'join together').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01656051-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˌdɪs.kəˈnɛk.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.kəˈnɛk.tɪd / (BrE)
lacking connection or unity; having been divided so that parts no longer form a whole
not connected or unified; divided

Examples

His argument felt disconnected from the main topic and was hard to follow.
The once close-knit team became disconnected after several disagreements.
She found it difficult to relate to her disconnected thoughts during the stressful period.

Synset Examples

Congress … gave the impression of … a confusing sum of disconnected local forces
a league of disunited nations
a fragmented coalition
a split group

Related Terms

  • Antonym: connected - joined or linked together; having unity
  • Synonym: fragmented - broken into separate parts

Etymology

From Latin prefix dis- (apart) + connect (from Latin connectere, to fasten together).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02490690-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˌdɪskəˈnɛktɪd / (AmE), / ˌdɪskəˈnɛktɪd / (BrE)
(of writing, speech, or thought) marked by sudden changes in subject and lacking smooth transitions between ideas.
lacking smooth transitions; abrupt

Examples

The author's disconnected writing style made the essay hard to follow.
Her argument was so disconnected that listeners struggled to understand her main point.
The report felt disconnected, jumping from one topic to another without warning.

Synset Examples

abrupt prose

Related Terms

  • Antonym: coherent - logical and well-organized, with smooth transitions between ideas

Etymology

From prefix dis- (apart) + connected.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02301854-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ˌdɪskəˈnɛktɪd / (AmE), / ˌdɪskəˈnɛktɪd / (BrE)
Lacking logical or orderly connection; not forming a continuous whole.
lacking logical or continuous connection

Examples

Her essay felt disconnected, jumping from one idea to the next without any clear links.
The movie was hard to follow because it consisted of several disconnected scenes.
After the interruption, his thoughts became disconnected and difficult to organize.

Synset Examples

a confused set of instructions
a confused dream about the end of the world
disconnected fragments of a story
scattered thoughts

Related Terms

  • Antonym: connected - joined together in a logical or continuous way

Etymology

From 'dis-' (apart) + 'connected', from Latin 'connectere' (to bind together).

Source: WordNet
