cubic

1. ADJ

/ ˈkjuː.bɪk / (AmE), / ˈkjuː.bɪk / (BrE)
(of an equation or expression) involving the cube (third power) and no higher power of a quantity or variable.
involving the cube (third power)

Examples

The mathematician explained how to solve a cubic equation.
A cubic polynomial has the highest degree of three.
Finding the roots of cubic equations can be more complex than solving linear or quadratic ones.

Synset Examples

a cubic equation

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: quadratic - involving the square and no higher power of a variable

Etymology

From Latin cubicus, from Greek kubikos, from kubos (cube).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkjuː.bɪk / (AmE), / ˈkjuː.bɪk / (BrE)
having the shape of a cube; having three dimensions of equal length, width, and height
having the shape of a cube

Examples

The new building featured a cubic structure that stood out in the city skyline.
They measured the box to confirm it was perfectly cubic in shape.
A sugar cube is a simple example of a cubic object.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: square - having the shape of a square
  • Degree Lower: rectangular - having the shape of a rectangle

Etymology

From Latin 'cubicus', from Greek 'kubikos' (of a cube), from 'kubos' (cube, die).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00660221-a
Synset POS: a
