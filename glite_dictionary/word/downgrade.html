downgrade

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊnˌɡreɪd / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnɡreɪd / (BrE)
a downward slope, especially on a road or railway
a downward slope

Examples

The car gained speed as it rolled down the downgrade.
Cyclists must be careful when descending a steep downgrade on slick roads.
The train picked up momentum as it traveled along the downgrade section of the track.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: upgrade - an upward slope, especially on a road or railway
  • Synonym: decline - a downward slope or movement

Etymology

Formed by analogy with 'upgrade', from 'down' + 'grade' (slope).

2. VERB

/ ˈdaʊnˌɡreɪd / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnɡreɪd / (BrE)
to reduce to a lower grade, rank, value, or level of importance; to treat as less important or worthy
to reduce in status or value

Examples

The credit agency decided to downgrade the company's rating after its profits fell sharply.
After the incident, he was downgraded to a less prestigious position within the firm.
Many users chose to downgrade their software when the new version caused compatibility issues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to demote - to move someone to a lower rank or position
  • Antonym: to promote - to raise to a higher rank or position

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'down-' and 'grade,' originally meaning a sloping downward; later used figuratively.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
