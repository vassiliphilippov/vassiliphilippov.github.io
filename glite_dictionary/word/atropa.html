Atropa

1. NOUN

/ əˈtroʊpə / (AmE), / əˈtrəʊpə / (BrE)
A genus of poisonous Eurasian herbs in the nightshade family, including the deadly nightshade or belladonna.
genus of poisonous herbs, including belladonna

Examples

The genus Atropa includes the infamous deadly nightshade.
Atropa species are known for their powerful alkaloids and toxic properties.
The medicinal risks and benefits of plants in the Atropa genus have fascinated herbalists for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: belladonna - the deadly nightshade plant, Atropa belladonna

Etymology

From Greek 'Atropos', one of the three Fates in Greek mythology, who cut the thread of life.

2. NOUN

/ əˈtroʊpə / (AmE), / əˈtrəʊpə / (BrE)
A genus of poisonous perennial herbs of the nightshade family, including deadly nightshade (belladonna).
genus of poisonous nightshade plants

Examples

The genus Atropa includes several highly toxic plants such as deadly nightshade.
Many alkaloids are derived from plants of the Atropa genus, especially for medicinal purposes.
Poisoning by Atropa berries can be fatal if not treated promptly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: belladonna - A poisonous plant, also known as deadly nightshade, Atropa belladonna

Etymology

Named after Atropos, one of the Fates in Greek mythology, who cuts the thread of life.
