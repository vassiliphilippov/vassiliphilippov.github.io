to enter

1. VERB

/ ˈɛn.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈɛn.tə / (BrE)
to record information by writing it down or inputting it into a system, so that it becomes part of an official record
record or write down officially

Examples

All applicants must enter their details on the registration form.
The clerk entered the transaction into the company’s records.
She carefully entered the data into the computer system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: record - to set down in writing or other permanent form for later reference

Etymology

From Old French entrer, from Latin intrare (to go in, enter).
