perch

1. NOUN

/ pɝtʃ / (AmE), / pɜːtʃ / (BrE)
a former unit of land area, commonly equal to a square rod or about 25.29 square meters.
unit of land area (square rod)
archaic

Examples

The old deed listed the property size as twenty perches.
Historically, land was often measured in perches and acres rather than modern metric units.
Surveyors marked out each perch using wooden poles as a guide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rod - a unit of length equal to 5.5 yards; also used as a square measurement for land

Etymology

From Old French *perche*, from Latin *pertica* (pole; measuring rod).
