Scaphiopus bombifrons

1. NOUN

/ skæˈfaɪ.oʊ.pəs ˌbɒm.bɪˈfrɒnz / (AmE), / skæˈfiː.ə.pəs ˌbɒm.bɪˈfrɒnz / (BrE)
A species of spadefoot toad native to plains, hills, and river bottoms east of the Rocky Mountains, typically found in areas with low rainfall.
species of spadefoot toad of central North America

Examples

The Scaphiopus bombifrons is well adapted to arid environments due to its burrowing behavior.
Researchers observed several juvenile Scaphiopus bombifrons near the edge of a temporary pond.
During spring rains, the Scaphiopus bombifrons emerges from its burrow to breed in shallow pools.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spadefoot toad - any toad in the family Scaphiopodidae, characterized by a spade-like projection on the foot

Etymology

From scientific Latin: genus name 'Scaphiopus' (from Greek 'skapheios' meaning shovel-like + 'pous' meaning foot) and species name 'bombifrons'.
