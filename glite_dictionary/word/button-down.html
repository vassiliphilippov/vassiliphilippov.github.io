button-down

1. ADJ

/ ˈbʌtənˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈbʌtənˌdaʊn / (BrE)
(of a shirt or collar) having the ends of the collar fastened down by buttons.
shirt with collar fastened by buttons

Examples

He wore a crisp button-down shirt to the interview.
Classic button-down collars are a staple in men's business fashion.
I prefer button-down styles because they look neat even without a tie.

Synset Examples

Brooks Brothers button-down shirts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: button-up - (of a shirt) having buttons down the front
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00297801-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈbʌtənˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈbʌt.ən.daʊn / (BrE)
(of a person or environment) unimaginatively conventional; very conservative or lacking in originality, especially in appearance or behavior.
very conventional or conservative

Examples

Despite his young age, his button-down attitude fit right in with the firm’s culture.
Her button-down approach to problem-solving often stifled creativity in the team.
He felt out of place in the button-down environment of corporate finance.

Synset Examples

a colorful character in the buttoned-down, dull-grey world of business

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conservative - favoring traditional values and resistant to change
  • Antonym: unconventional - not based on or conforming to what is generally done or believed

Etymology

From the literal sense of 'button-down' shirts, extending metaphorically to describe conventional or conservative behavior or environments.
