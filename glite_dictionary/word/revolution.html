revolution

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛvəˈluʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛvəˈluːʃən / (BrE)
a sudden and significant change in political power, especially the forcible overthrow of a government by the people governed
forcible overthrow of a government

Examples

The French revolution fundamentally changed the nation's political landscape.
They studied the causes of the Russian revolution in their history class.
After years of oppression, the people began to call for revolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coup - a sudden, often violent, seizure of power from a government
  • Synonym: insurrection - a violent uprising against an authority or government
  • Synonym: revolt - an attempt to overthrow the government, usually less organized than a revolution

Etymology

From Latin 'revolutio' (a turn around), from 'revolvere' (to turn, roll back).

2. NOUN

/ ˌrɛvəˈluːʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛvəˈluːʃən / (BrE)
a sudden and significant change in methods, ideas, or social structures, often transforming society or an area of human activity
a major change in ideas or behavior

Examples

The internet sparked a revolution in the way we communicate and access information.
Her ideas led to a revolution in teaching methods at the university.
A digital revolution is transforming industries around the world.

Synset Examples

the industrial revolution was also a cultural revolution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transformation - a thorough or dramatic change in form or character
  • Synonym: reform - making changes to improve a system or practice
  • Antonym: stagnation - a lack of change, growth, or development

Etymology

From Latin revolutio (a turn around), from revolvere (to revolve).

3. NOUN

/ ˌrɛvəˈluːʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛvəˈluːʃən / (BrE)
a single complete turn or movement around a central point, especially of a planet, wheel, or other object
a complete turn around a center

Examples

The gears must complete one revolution for the clock to tick forward.
Each revolution of the Ferris wheel offers a new view of the city.
The Earth completes a revolution around the Sun every year.

Synset Examples

the plane made three rotations before it crashed
the revolution of the earth about the sun takes one year

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rotation - the act of turning around a central point

Etymology

From Latin revolutio (a turn around), from revolvere (to turn, roll back).
