Liposcelis

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈpɑːsəˌlɪs / (AmE), / lɪˈpɒsɪlɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small, soft-bodied insects in the family Psocidae, often known as booklice or barklice.
genus of insects (booklice)

Examples

Scientists identified several species of Liposcelis in the stored grain samples.
Infestations of Liposcelis can cause problems in libraries and archives.
The tiny insects belonging to the genus Liposcelis are commonly referred to as booklice.

Etymology

From Greek 'leipo' (leave) and 'skelos' (leg), referencing a feature of the insects' legs.

2. NOUN

/ laɪˈpɑs.sə.lɪs / (AmE), / lɪˈpɒs.sɪ.lɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small insects in the family Psocidae, commonly known as booklice, found in various habitats including stored products and domestic environments.
genus of small insects (booklice)

Examples

Species of the genus Liposcelis are often pests in stored grain products.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Liposcelis genus in a tropical forest.
A high population of Liposcelis can indicate poor storage conditions for food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: booklouse - small, soft-bodied insect commonly found among old books or papers

Etymology

From Greek 'leipo-' meaning 'to leave' and 'skelos' meaning 'leg', referring to the insect's slender legs.
