Sarcodes

1. NOUN

/ sɑrˈkoʊdiːz / (AmE), / sɑːˈkəʊdiːz / (BrE)
A genus of leafless, red, parasitic plants known as snow plants, native to western North America.
genus of parasitic snow plants

Examples

The bright red Sarcodes appears soon after the snow melts in mountain forests.
Unlike most plants, Sarcodes lacks chlorophyll and relies on fungi to obtain nutrients.
Many hikers are excited to spot a blooming Sarcodes during spring in California.

Etymology

From Greek sarkodes (fleshy).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːrkoʊdiːz / (AmE), / ˈsɑːkəʊdiːz / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants comprising the snow plant, native to western North America and sometimes classified in the family Pyrolaceae.
genus of flowering plants (snow plant)

Examples

The striking red color of Sarcodes makes it easily recognizable in pine forests.
Botanists study Sarcodes because of its unusual biology and lack of chlorophyll.
Sarcodes is sometimes placed in the family Pyrolaceae, though more often in Ericaceae.

Etymology

From Greek 'sarx, sarkos' (flesh), referring to the plant's reddish color.
