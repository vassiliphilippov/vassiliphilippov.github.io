interpreter

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɝːprətɚ / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːprɪtə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to translate spoken words from one language into another, typically in real time
person who translates spoken language

Examples

The UN meeting included an interpreter to ensure everyone understood the discussions.
She works as an interpreter for visiting foreign delegations.
Without an experienced interpreter, communication would have been impossible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: translator - person who translates written language

Etymology

From Latin interpretari (to explain), via Old French interprete.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10232171-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɜrprətər / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːprɪtə / (BrE)
a person who expresses or represents something, especially a work or subject, through art, performance, or another creative medium
person who creatively expresses or represents something

Examples

As an interpreter of ancient myths, her choreography was both original and insightful.
The artist became known as an interpreter of everyday life in her sculptures.
He was a talented interpreter of classic piano works, bringing new emotion to familiar pieces.

Synset Examples

his paintings reveal a sensitive interpreter of nature
she was famous as an interpreter of Shakespearean roles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: performer - someone who acts, sings, or otherwise presents a creative work

Etymology

From Latin interpretari (to explain, translate), from interpres (explainer, translator).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10232450-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɝːprətɚ / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːprɪtə / (BrE)
(Computing) a computer program that translates and executes each line of source code one at a time, rather than compiling the entire program first.
program that executes code line by line

Examples

An interpreter executes the source code line by line rather than translating it all at once.
If you are learning Python, you can run your scripts directly in the interpreter.
Many scripting languages, such as Ruby, rely on an interpreter rather than a compiler.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: compiler - a program that translates source code into machine code before execution
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06588108-n
Synset POS: n
