Pluvianus

1. NOUN

/ pluːˈvaɪ.ə.nəs / (AmE), / pluːˈvaɪ.ən.əs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Glareolidae, containing the Egyptian plover.
genus of birds; Egyptian plover

Examples

The Pluvianus is notable for including the bird commonly known as the Egyptian plover.
Taxonomists recently revised the classification of the Pluvianus genus.
Many ornithology texts describe Pluvianus as a monotypic genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Egyptian plover - a bird species, Pluvianus aegyptius, sole member of Pluvianus genus

Etymology

From Latin pluvianus (of rain); named for its association with riverbanks.

2. NOUN

/ pluːˈveɪni.əs / (AmE), / pluːˈvɪə.nəs / (BrE)
A genus of wading birds in the family Pluvianidae, known for the Egyptian plover.
genus of birds including the Egyptian plover

Examples

The Pluvianus is notable for containing the Egyptian plover, a bird found along the Nile River.
Scientists have debated the classification of Pluvianus based on its unique morphological traits.
The nesting habits of birds in the genus Pluvianus are adapted to sandy riverbanks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Egyptian plover - a wading bird species in the genus Pluvianus

Etymology

From Latin pluvia (rain), as a reference to its habitat or behavior.
