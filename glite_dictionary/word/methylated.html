methylated

1. ADJ

/ ˈmiːθəleɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈmiːθəleɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a molecule or compound) having had a methyl group added by a chemical process.
chemically altered by addition of a methyl group

Examples

The methylated compound exhibited different chemical properties than its original form.
Scientists analyzed the methylated DNA to study gene regulation.
Many solvents in the lab are methylated to reduce their misuse.

Synset Examples

methylated alcohol

Related Terms

  • Antonym: demethylated - having had a methyl group removed

Etymology

From 'methyl' (from French 'méthyle') + '-ated' (forming adjectives meaning 'supplied or combined with').
