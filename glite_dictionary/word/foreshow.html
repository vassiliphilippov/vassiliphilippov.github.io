to foreshow

1. VERB

/ fɔrˈʃoʊ / (AmE), / fɔːˈʃəʊ / (BrE)
to foretell or predict (an event), especially by divine or supernatural inspiration
foretell or predict, especially divinely

Examples

The strange dreams seemed to foreshow the hardships that awaited the travelers.
Some believed that the comet would foreshow a time of great change.
Ancient prophecies foreshow the rise of a new king in the land.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to foretell - to predict the future
  • Synonym: to prophesy - to predict with divine inspiration

Etymology

From Old English foresceawian, from fore- (before) + sceawian (to look at, see).
