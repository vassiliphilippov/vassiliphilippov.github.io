II Thessalonians

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛkənd θɛsəˈloʊniənz / (AmE), / ˌsɛkənd θɛsəˈləʊniənz / (BrE)
the second book in the New Testament consisting of Saint Paul's second letter to the Thessalonians
New Testament book; Paul's second letter to Thessalonians

Examples

Many scholars believe that II Thessalonians was written to address concerns about the return of Christ.
My favorite passage in II Thessalonians encourages believers to stand firm in their faith.
The church studied II Thessalonians as part of their New Testament series.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: First Thessalonians - the first book of Paul's letters to the Thessalonians
  • Synonym: New Testament - the second part of the Christian Bible

Etymology

From Latin 'Thessalonicenses', from Thessalonica (ancient city in Macedonia), named for Paul's epistle to its early Christian community.
