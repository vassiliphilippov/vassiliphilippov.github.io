withholder

1. NOUN

/ wɪθˈhoʊldər / (AmE), / wɪθˈhəʊldə / (BrE)
a person who restrains, checks, or holds back something, especially information, emotion, or resources
person who holds back or restrains

Examples

The withholder of crucial evidence can impede the course of justice.
As a known withholder of affection, she struggled to express her true feelings.
The company identified the tax withholder responsible for submitting payroll deductions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to withhold - to refuse to give something that is due or desired

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'withhold' + agent suffix '-er'.

2. NOUN

/ wɪðˈhoʊldər / (AmE), / wɪðˈhəʊldə / (BrE)
a person who refrains from giving, granting, or allowing something, especially payment or information
person who refrains from granting or giving

Examples

The company accused him of being a withholder of crucial information.
As a habitual withholder of praise, she rarely complimented her staff.
Tax authorities penalized the withholder of the payments for violating regulations.

Synset Examples

a withholder of payments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to withhold - to hold back or refuse to give something

Etymology

From the verb 'withhold' + agentive suffix '-er'.
