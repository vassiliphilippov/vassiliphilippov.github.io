Tamaricaceae

1. NOUN

/ təˌmærɪˈkeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / təˌmærɪˈkeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of mostly salt-tolerant or drought-resistant shrubs and trees, including the tamarisks
plant family of tamarisks and relatives

Examples

Tamaricaceae includes desert plants adapted to saline soils and arid climates.
The Tamaricaceae family is best known for the tamarisk, a salt-tolerant shrub.
Botanists have discovered several new species within the Tamaricaceae in Central Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tamarisk - any shrub or tree of the genus Tamarix in the Tamaricaceae family

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Tamarix plus the standard botanical suffix -aceae.
