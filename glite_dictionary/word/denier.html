denier

1. NOUN

/ dəˈnaɪər / (AmE), / dɪˈnaɪə / (BrE)
a unit of measurement used to indicate the fineness or thickness of fibers such as silk, nylon, or rayon, based on the weight in grams of 9,000 meters of the fiber
unit of fiber thickness

Examples

These tights are made with 15 denier nylon for a delicate look.
The label specifies the denier of the yarn used in these stockings.
Lower denier numbers indicate sheerer fabrics, ideal for summer wear.

Synset Examples

with an evening dress one wears 10 denier stockings

Etymology

From French *denier* (a coin; unit of weight), from Latin *denarius*.

2. NOUN

/ dəˈnaɪər / (AmE), / ˈdɛn.i.eɪ / (BrE)
a former European coin of various denominations, used especially in France and Italy in the Middle Ages
old European coin
archaic

Examples

The merchant paid in deniers during the late Middle Ages.
A rare collection of medieval deniers was on display at the museum.
Archaeologists discovered several silver deniers at the dig site.

Etymology

From Old French *denier*, from Latin *denarius* (Roman coin).

3. NOUN

/ dəˈnaɪər / (AmE), / dɪˈnaɪə / (BrE)
a person who denies something, especially someone who refuses to admit the truth of a statement or a widely accepted fact
person who denies or rejects a claim

Examples

The climate change denier refused to accept the overwhelming scientific evidence.
She was labeled a denier of the historical facts presented in court.
Despite the proof, the document denier insisted it was a forgery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: denialist - person who rejects evidence-based consensus
  • Antonym: admitter - person who admits or acknowledges something

Etymology

From Old French *denier* (‘one who denies’), from Latin *denegare* (‘to deny’).
