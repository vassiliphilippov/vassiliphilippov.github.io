to vex

1. VERB

/ vɛks / (AmE), / vɛks / (BrE)
to subject (an issue or question) to prolonged discussion, examination, or deliberation, often causing difficulty or annoyance
prolonged discussion or examination (formal)

Examples

The committee continued to vex the issue despite previous discussions.
Lawmakers have vexed this matter for years without reaching consensus.
This motion has been vexed repeatedly in court hearings.

Synset Examples

vex the subject of the death penalty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debate - to discuss a subject formally or at length
  • Antonym: resolve - to settle a problem or question

Etymology

From Latin vexare (to shake, harass, trouble).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00814544-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ vɛks / (AmE), / vɛks / (BrE)
to cause someone to feel confused or perplexed; to be a mystery or puzzle to someone
to bewilder or puzzle

Examples

The complexity of quantum mechanics continues to vex even the most brilliant scientists.
It greatly vexes her how the magician performed that astonishing trick.
The riddle on the old parchment vexed the treasure hunters for weeks before they found a clue.

Synset Examples

This beats me!
Got me — I don't know the answer!
a vexing problem
This question really stuck me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puzzle - to bewilder or confuse someone; to be difficult to understand
  • Synonym: to confound - to mix up in confusion; to baffle or perplex

Etymology

From Latin vexare (to shake, disturb, annoy), originally to move violently.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00624056-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ vɛks / (AmE), / vɛks / (BrE)
to annoy, disturb, or upset someone, especially with minor or repeated irritations
annoy or irritate repeatedly

Examples

The ongoing noise from the construction site began to vex the residents.
It vexes me when people interrupt during meetings.
The complexity of the problem had vexed her for weeks.

Synset Examples

Mosquitoes buzzing in my ear really bothers me
It irritates me that she never closes the door after she leaves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to annoy - to make someone slightly angry or irritated
  • Synonym: to irritate - to cause someone to feel annoyed or impatient
  • Antonym: please - to make someone feel happy or satisfied

Etymology

From Latin vexare (to shake, disturb, harass), via Old French vexer.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01791774-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ vɛks / (AmE), / vɛks / (BrE)
to make someone feel annoyed, worried, or distressed, especially over a prolonged period
to annoy or distress someone

Examples

His constant interruptions began to vex everyone in the meeting.
The delay in receiving an answer vexed her for days.
It can vex parents when children ignore their advice.

Synset Examples

I cannot sleep — my daughter's health is worrying me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to annoy - to irritate or disturb someone slightly
  • Synonym: to irritate - to make someone feel impatient or angry
  • Antonym: to soothe - to calm or comfort someone

Etymology

From Latin 'vexare' (to shake, harass).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
