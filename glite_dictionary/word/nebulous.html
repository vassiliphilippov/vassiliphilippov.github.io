nebulous

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɛb.jə.ləs / (AmE), / ˈnɛb.jʊ.ləs / (BrE)
Relating to or resembling a nebula; cloudy, hazy, or diffuse in appearance, especially in reference to astronomy.
relating to a nebula; diffuse or cloudy

Examples

The astronomer described the nebulous shapes visible in the deep-space images.
This region of the galaxy is filled with nebulous clouds of gas and dust.
Traditionally, the solar system's formation was explained by the nebulous hypothesis.

Synset Examples

the nebular hypothesis of the origin of the solar system

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nebular - relating to or resembling a nebula

Etymology

From Latin 'nebulosus' (cloudy), from 'nebula' (mist, cloud).

2. ADJ

/ ˈnɛb.jə.ləs / (AmE), / ˈnɛb.jʊ.ləs / (BrE)
(of ideas, concepts, or distinctions) not clear, having no definite shape, form, or limits; vague or ill-defined.
vague or ill-defined; lacking clear form

Examples

The details of the plan remain nebulous and subject to change.
Her explanation for the missing money was strangely nebulous.
There was only a nebulous distinction between the two proposals.

Synset Examples

gropes among cloudy issues toward a feeble conclusion
nebulous distinction between pride and conceit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vague - not clearly expressed or defined
  • Antonym: distinct - clearly different or separate

Etymology

From Latin 'nebulosus' (full of mist), from 'nebula' (mist, fog).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈnɛbjələs / (AmE), / ˈnɛbjʊləs / (BrE)
not clear or having no definite shape, form, or content; vague or ill-defined.
vague; lacking definite form or clarity

Examples

The committee rejected the proposal because of its nebulous objectives.
Her memories of childhood are nebulous and fragmented.
They gave only nebulous instructions, which confused the whole team.

Synset Examples

nebulous reasons
unfixed as were her general notions of what men ought to be

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vague - not clear or definite in meaning or form
  • Antonym: definite - clearly stated or decided; not vague or doubtful

Etymology

From Latin nebulosus (cloudy, misty), from nebula (mist, cloud).
