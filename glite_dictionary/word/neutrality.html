neutrality

1. NOUN

/ nuˈtræləti / (AmE), / njuːˈtræləti / (BrE)
the state of not supporting or assisting any side in a conflict, dispute, or war
not supporting a side in conflict or war

Examples

Switzerland is famous for its long-standing policy of neutrality during international conflicts.
The media is often criticized for abandoning neutrality in political reporting.
By maintaining neutrality, the organization gained the trust of all parties involved.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impartiality - the quality of treating all rivals or disputants equally
  • Antonym: belligerence - the state of being engaged in war or conflict

Etymology

From French *neutralité*, from Latin *neutralitas*, meaning 'the quality of being neutral'.

2. NOUN

/ nuːˈtræləti / (AmE), / njuːˈtræləti / (BrE)
The state of having a pH value of 7, indicating neither acidity nor alkalinity.
state of pH 7 in chemistry

Examples

Distilled water is often used in experiments because of its neutrality at pH 7.
The neutrality of the solution was confirmed when it did not affect the color of the indicator paper.
Maintaining neutrality in the tank is essential for the health of the fish.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acidity - the quality of being acid, typically with a pH less than 7
  • Antonym: alkalinity - the quality of being alkaline, typically with a pH greater than 7

Etymology

From Late Latin neutralitas (the quality of being neutral).

3. NOUN

/ nuːˈtræləti / (AmE), / njuːˈtrælɪti / (BrE)
the state of not supporting or helping either side in a conflict or dispute; lack of involvement or bias
lack of involvement or bias

Examples

The country declared its neutrality during the conflict, refusing to support either side.
Her neutrality in the argument helped mediate a peaceful solution.
Journalists are expected to maintain neutrality and avoid showing personal opinions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impartiality - the quality of treating all equally or without bias
  • Antonym: bias - prejudice in favor of or against one thing, person, or group

Etymology

From French 'neutralité', from Medieval Latin 'neutralitas', from Latin 'neutralis' (neither).
