Pawnee

1. NOUN

/ pɔːˈniː / (AmE), / pɔːˈniː / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people formerly inhabiting Nebraska and Kansas, now principally living in Oklahoma
member of a Native American people

Examples

The Pawnee were known for their skill in agriculture and their complex religious ceremonies.
A descendant of the Pawnee nation now lives in Oklahoma.
Historians have studied the migration of the Pawnee from Nebraska to Indian Territory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sioux - member of a Native American people of the northern Mississippi Valley and Plains regions

Etymology

From Pawnee *pahní* (a self-designation, meaning 'men of men').

2. NOUN

/ pɔːˈni / (AmE), / pɔːˈniː / (BrE)
the Caddoan language traditionally spoken by the Pawnee people of the central United States
Caddoan language of the Pawnee people

Examples

Less than a hundred people still speak Pawnee as their first language.
She studied Pawnee as part of her research on Indigenous languages.
Efforts to revitalize Pawnee include creating new educational materials for children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pawnee - member of the Pawnee people

Etymology

From the name of the Pawnee people, possibly from their own self-designation meaning 'men of men.'
