flameflower

1. NOUN

/ ˈfleɪmˌflaʊər / (AmE), / ˈfleɪmˌflaʊə / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Kniphofia, known for its long narrow leaves and tall spikes of bright red or yellow drooping flowers.
plant of the genus Kniphofia

Examples

The flameflower brightened the border with its stunning red spikes.
Gardeners often choose flameflowers for their drought tolerance and vivid blooms.
A row of flameflowers lined the walkway, attracting bees and butterflies.

Etymology

From 'flame' (for the fiery color) and 'flower.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈfleɪmˌflaʊər / (AmE), / ˈfleɪmˌflaʊə / (BrE)
A plant found in the southwestern United States, noted for its fleshy roots, erect stems, narrow succulent leaves, and a single reddish-orange flower in each upper leaf axil.
succulent plant with reddish-orange flowers

Examples

The flameflower blooms in the rocky deserts each spring, adding a splash of color to the landscape.
Native Americans once cooked the roots of the flameflower as part of their diet.
Gardeners value the flameflower for its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
