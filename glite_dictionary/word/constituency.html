constituency

1. NOUN

/ kənˈstɪtʃuənsi / (AmE), / kənˈstɪtʃuənsi / (BrE)
the body of voters in a specified area who elect a representative to a legislative body
body of voters in an electoral area

Examples

The city's northern constituency overwhelmingly supported the new candidate in the election.
Every member of parliament must answer to their constituency for their decisions.
After the boundary changes, the constituency included several new towns and villages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electorate - all the people in a country or area who are entitled to vote

Etymology

From Latin 'constituere' (to establish, appoint), via 'constituent'.
