emblematic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛm.bləˈmæt.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛm.bləˈmæt.ɪk / (BrE)
serving as a symbol or representative illustration of a particular quality or idea
symbolic; representative

Examples

The Statue of Liberty is emblematic of freedom in the United States.
Her struggle became emblematic of the fight for equal rights.
That painting is emblematic of the artist's unique style.

Synset Examples

the free discussion that is emblematic of democracy
an action exemplary of his conduct

Related Terms

  • Synonym: symbolic - serving as a symbol
  • Synonym: representative - standing for something else; symbolic

Etymology

From Medieval Latin emblematicus, from Greek emblēma (inlaid work, ornament).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɛm.bləˈmæt.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛm.bləˈmæt.ɪk / (BrE)
serving as a symbol that represents a particular idea, quality, or concept
serving as a symbol

Examples

The Statue of Liberty is emblematic of freedom and hope for many immigrants.
Her actions were emblematic of the determination shown by the whole team.
The red poppy became emblematic of remembrance for fallen soldiers.

Synset Examples

a crown is emblematic of royalty
the spinning wheel was as symbolic of colonical Massachusetts as the codfish

Related Terms

  • Synonym: symbolic - serving as a symbol
  • Synonym: representative - typical of a particular group or thing
  • Antonym: literal - taking words in their usual or most basic sense; the opposite of figurative or symbolic

Etymology

From French *emblématique*, from Medieval Latin *emblematicus* (pertaining to an emblem).
