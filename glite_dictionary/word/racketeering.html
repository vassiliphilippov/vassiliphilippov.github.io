racketeering

1. NOUN

/ ˌrækɪˈtɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌrækɪˈtɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of engaging in dishonest and fraudulent business dealings, especially as part of organized crime
engaging in organized criminal activity
legal

Examples

The company was accused of racketeering and faced serious criminal charges.
Federal agents launched an investigation into alleged racketeering activities within the organization.
He was convicted on multiple counts of fraud and racketeering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extortion - the practice of obtaining something through force or threats
  • Synonym: fraud - wrongful or criminal deception intended to result in financial or personal gain

Etymology

From 'racket' (fraudulent scheme) and '-eering' (as in 'pioneering'); popularized in American English in the early 20th century.
