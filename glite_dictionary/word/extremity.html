extremity

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstrɛm.ə.ti / (AmE), / ɪkˈstrɛm.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the part of a limb that is farthest from the torso, such as a hand or foot
farthest part of a limb (hand or foot)
medical

Examples

Frostbite most commonly affects the extremities, such as fingers and toes.
She lost feeling in her lower extremities after the accident.
Doctors checked each extremity for signs of circulation and movement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appendage - a body part that is attached to the main structure

Etymology

From Latin extremitas (the end, utmost point), from extremus (outermost, last).
