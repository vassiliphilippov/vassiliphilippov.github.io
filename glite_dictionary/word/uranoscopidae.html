Uranoscopidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌjʊr.ə.nəˈskɑː.pə.diː / (AmE), / ˌjʊə.rə.nɒˈskɒ.pɪ.diː / (BrE)
A family of marine fishes known as stargazers, recognized for their upward-facing eyes and mouth, often burying themselves in sand to ambush prey.
family of fishes called stargazers

Examples

The Uranoscopidae are fascinating for their ability to bury themselves in the sand, leaving only their eyes visible.
Many members of the Uranoscopidae family are well camouflaged on the ocean floor.
Research on the Uranoscopidae family has revealed unique adaptations in their electrical organs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stargazer - common name for fishes in the family Uranoscopidae

Etymology

From New Latin Uranoscopus (type genus), from Greek ouranos (sky) + skopos (observer).
