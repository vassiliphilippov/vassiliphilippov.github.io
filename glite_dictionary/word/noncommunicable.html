noncommunicable

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɑnkəˈmjunɪkəbəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkəˈmjuːnɪkəbl / (BrE)
(of a disease) not able to be transmitted from one person to another; not infectious.
not transmissible disease
medical

Examples

Diabetes is a noncommunicable disease that cannot be spread through contact with others.
The government launched a campaign to raise awareness about noncommunicable diseases.
Unlike the flu, cancer is a noncommunicable condition.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: communicable - (of a disease) able to be transmitted from one person to another

Etymology

Formed from 'non-' (not) + 'communicable' (from Latin 'communicare', to share).
