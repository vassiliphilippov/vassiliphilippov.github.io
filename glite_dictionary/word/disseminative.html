disseminative

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈsɛm.əˌneɪ.tɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛm.ɪ.nə.tɪv / (BrE)
Tending to spread widely, especially by diffusion.
spreading by diffusion

Examples

The disseminative properties of the virus made it particularly dangerous in crowded areas.
Education can be a disseminative force, spreading knowledge throughout a community.
Her research focused on the disseminative mechanisms involved in plant pollination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diffusive - causing something to spread out freely; tending to diffuse
  • Antonym: concentrated - gathered closely together, not spreading

Etymology

From Latin 'disseminare' (to scatter, to spread).
