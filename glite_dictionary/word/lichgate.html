lichgate

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪtʃˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɪtʃɡeɪt / (BrE)
a roofed gateway to a churchyard, formerly used as a temporary shelter for a coffin during funerals
roofed churchyard gate for funerals
archaic

Examples

The old church still has its original lichgate standing at the entrance to the graveyard.
During the Victorian era, mourners would gather under the lichgate before the burial service.
The lichgate provided shelter from the rain as the funeral procession paused outside the church.

Etymology

From Old English 'līc' (body, corpse) + 'gate' (gate, entrance).
