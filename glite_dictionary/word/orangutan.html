orangutan

1. NOUN

/ oʊˌræŋ.uːˈtæn / (AmE), / ɒˌræŋ.uːˈtæn / (BrE)
a large, long-armed ape native to the rainforests of Borneo and Sumatra, known for its arboreal lifestyle and reddish-brown hair
large arboreal ape of Borneo and Sumatra

Examples

Conservationists are working hard to protect the remaining orangutans in the wild.
The orangutan carefully swung from branch to branch, searching for ripe fruit.
Many visitors were fascinated by the orangutan's intelligence and gentle demeanor at the zoo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ape - a tailless primate of the family Hominoidea

Etymology

From Malay 'orang utan' meaning 'person of the forest' (orang: person, utan: forest).

2. NOUN

/ əˈræŋəˌtæn / (AmE), / ɒˈræŋʊtæn / (BrE)
a large, long-armed, tree-dwelling ape found in the rainforests of Borneo and Sumatra
large arboreal ape of Borneo and Sumatra

Examples

The orangutan swung effortlessly from branch to branch in the rainforest canopy.
She watched a documentary about the endangered orangutan of Borneo.
Zoo visitors were fascinated by the intelligence of the young orangutan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ape - a large primate without a tail
  • Degree Lower: gorilla - largest living primate, native to Africa
  • Degree Lower: gibbon - small slender ape with long arms
  • Degree Lower: chimpanzee - a large African ape closely related to humans

Etymology

From Malay 'orang utan', meaning 'person of the forest' ('orang' = person, 'hutan' = forest).
