muskrat

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌsˌkræt / (AmE), / ˈmʌs.kræt / (BrE)
the soft brown fur of a muskrat used especially for making coats and hats
the brown fur of a muskrat

Examples

She wore a warm coat lined with muskrat during the cold winter months.
The hat was made from high-quality muskrat to ensure durability and warmth.
Demand for muskrat declined as synthetic furs became more popular.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmʌsˌkræt / (AmE), / ˈmʌs.kræt / (BrE)
a beaver-like aquatic rodent native to North America, with dark, glossy brown fur and a long, scaly tail
aquatic North American rodent

Examples

A muskrat swam quietly across the pond at dawn.
Farmers sometimes worry that muskrats may damage irrigation ditches.
The dense fur of a muskrat helps it stay warm in cold water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beaver - large semiaquatic rodent known for building dams

Etymology

From musk + rat, due to its musky odor and rat-like appearance.
