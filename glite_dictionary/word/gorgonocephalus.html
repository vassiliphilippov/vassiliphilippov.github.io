Gorgonocephalus

1. NOUN

/ ɡɔrɡəˌnoʊsəˈfæləs / (AmE), / ɡɔːɡənəʊˌsiːˈfæləs / (BrE)
A genus of basket stars found in coldwater environments, including the Arctic, Antarctic, and deep-sea habitats.
genus of coldwater basket stars

Examples

The Gorgonocephalus is known for its distinctive branching arms which help it catch plankton.
Researchers have discovered a new species in the Gorgonocephalus genus living near the Arctic seafloor.
Specimens of Gorgonocephalus can often be seen clinging to rocks in deep-sea habitats.

Etymology

From Greek 'gorgṓn' (Gorgon, mythical creature) and 'kephalē' (head), referring to its appearance.
