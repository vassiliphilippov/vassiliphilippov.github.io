cosmogeny

1. NOUN

/ kɒzˈmɑdʒəni / (AmE), / kɒzˈmɒdʒəni / (BrE)
the branch of astrophysics concerned with the origin, evolution, and structure of the universe
study of the origin and evolution of the universe

Examples

Cosmogeny has provided profound insights into the Big Bang and the early stages of the universe.
Scientists specializing in cosmogeny analyze cosmic microwave background radiation for clues about the universe's beginnings.
Her research in cosmogeny addresses fundamental questions about how galaxies and stars first formed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cosmology - the science of the origin and development of the universe
  • Synonym: cosmogony - a theory or story about the origin of the universe

Etymology

From Greek kosmos (order, world) + -geny (origin, creation).
