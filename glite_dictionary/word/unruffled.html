unruffled

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈrʌfəld / (AmE), / ʌnˈrʌfəld / (BrE)
(of water) not disturbed by wind or waves; smooth and calm.
calm, smooth (of water)

Examples

Early in the morning, the lake was perfectly unruffled, mirroring the sky above.
The fisherman preferred to row out only when the sea was unruffled and quiet.
After the storm passed, the pond returned to its usual unruffled state.

Synset Examples

a ribbon of sand between the angry sea and the placid bay
the quiet waters of a lagoon
a lake of tranquil blue water reflecting a tranquil blue sky
a smooth channel crossing
scarcely a ripple on the still water
unruffled water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calm - not disturbed by wind, waves, or noise
  • Antonym: agitated - disturbed or in motion (of water)

Etymology

From un- (not) + ruffled (disturbed).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈrʌfəld / (AmE), / ʌnˈrʌfəld / (BrE)
Calm and not easily upset, disturbed, or agitated; remaining composed under pressure.
calm and composed

Examples

Despite the chaos around her, she remained unruffled and focused on her work.
He answered the difficult questions in an unruffled manner, impressing the interviewers.
The team’s coach appeared unruffled even when his players made mistakes.

Synset Examples

the waiters were unflurried and good natured
with contented mind and unruffled spirit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other strong emotions
  • Antonym: agitated - feeling or appearing troubled or nervous

Etymology

From the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'ruffled' (disturbed or agitated).

WordNet ID: oewn-00533292-s
