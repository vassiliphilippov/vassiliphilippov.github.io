patroller

1. NOUN

/ pəˈtroʊlər / (AmE), / pəˈtrəʊlə / (BrE)
a person who patrols an area, especially as part of their job or duty
person who performs patrol duty

Examples

A security patroller made rounds every hour to ensure the building was safe.
During the festival, several patrollers monitored the crowds for safety.
The park hired an additional patroller to cover the newly opened trails.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patrolman - a male police officer or guard who patrols an area
  • Synonym: guard - person who protects or watches over a place

Etymology

From 'patrol' (to walk around and guard) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
