arteria femoralis

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈtɪəriə fəˈmɔrəlɪs / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪəriə fɪˈmɔːrəlɪs / (BrE)
the chief artery of the thigh, which is a continuation of the external iliac artery, supplying blood to the lower limb
main artery of the thigh
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully exposed the arteria femoralis before proceeding with the bypass surgery.
An injury to the arteria femoralis can result in severe blood loss and requires immediate medical attention.
Ultrasound is often used to locate the arteria femoralis for catheter insertion during certain medical procedures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: femoral artery - chief artery of the thigh; supplies blood to the leg

Etymology

From Latin: 'arteria' meaning artery and 'femoralis' relating to the femur (thigh bone).
