invader

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈveɪdər / (AmE), / ɪnˈveɪdə / (BrE)
a person or group that enters a place by force in order to conquer or take control of it
person who enters by force to conquer

Examples

The city fell after the invader breached the ancient walls.
Every invader faces stiff resistance from the local population.
The history book described how a powerful invader changed the region forever.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conqueror - a person who conquers a country, people, or place
  • Antonym: defender - a person who protects against attack or invasion

Etymology

From Latin 'invadere' (to go into, attack).
