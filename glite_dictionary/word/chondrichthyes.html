Chondrichthyes

1. NOUN

/ kɑnˈdrɪkθiˌiz / (AmE), / kɒnˈdrɪkθiːz / (BrE)
A class of fishes whose skeletons are composed mainly of cartilage rather than bone, including sharks, rays, and skates.
class of cartilaginous fishes
medical

Examples

Many species within the Chondrichthyes are threatened by overfishing and habitat loss.
Sharks and rays, both members of the Chondrichthyes class, have unique adaptations for survival.
Unlike bony fish, Chondrichthyes have skeletons made of cartilage, making them lighter and more flexible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cartilaginous fish - fish with skeletons made of cartilage
  • Antonym: Osteichthyes - class of bony fishes

Etymology

From Greek khondros (cartilage) + ichthys (fish).

2. NOUN

/ kɒnˈdrɪkθiːz / (AmE), / kɒnˈdrɪkθiːz / (BrE)
A class of fishes whose skeletons are made primarily of cartilage rather than bone, including sharks, rays, and skates.
class of cartilaginous fishes

Examples

All sharks and rays belong to the class Chondrichthyes and have skeletons made of cartilage.
The biology textbook explains that Chondrichthyes are distinct from bony fishes in their skeletal structure.
Scientists discovered a new species in the Chondrichthyes class while exploring deep ocean habitats.

Etymology

From Greek 'chondros' (cartilage) + 'ichthys' (fish).
