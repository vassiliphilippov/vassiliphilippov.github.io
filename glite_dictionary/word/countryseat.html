countryseat

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌn.tri.siːt / (AmE), / ˈkʌn.tri.siːt / (BrE)
A large estate or house located in the countryside, often serving as the main residence of a landowner.
large estate or house in the countryside

Examples

The family spent every summer at their countryseat in the rolling hills of Surrey.
He inherited the centuries-old countryseat from his great-uncle, along with its sweeping grounds.
Lavish parties were often hosted by the duke at his secluded countryseat.

Etymology

Formed from 'country' and 'seat' (meaning 'residence' or 'dwelling').
