disfranchised

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈfrænˌtʃaɪzd / (AmE), / dɪsˈfræn.tʃaɪzd / (BrE)
Deprived of the rights of citizenship, especially the right to vote.
deprived of citizenship rights

Examples

The new law left many people disfranchised and unable to participate in elections.
After the changes to the constitution, formerly disfranchised groups gained the right to vote.
Organizations are working to support disfranchised communities and restore their civil rights.

Synset Examples

labor was voiceless
disenfranchised masses took to the streets

Related Terms

  • Antonym: enfranchised - having the rights of citizenship, especially the right to vote
  • Synonym: disenfranchised - deprived of the right to vote or other rights of citizenship

Etymology

From 'disfranchise', based on 'franchise' (right to vote) from Old French 'franc' (free).
