Haemulon

1. NOUN

/ hiːˈmjuːlɒn / (AmE), / hiːˈmjuːlɒn / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish in the family Haemulidae, commonly known as grunts.
genus of marine fish (grunts)

Examples

The Haemulon genus includes several species found in tropical and subtropical waters.
Marine biologists studied the behavior of Haemulon in coral reef ecosystems.
Many anglers recognize Haemulon as a type of grunt commonly caught along the coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grunts - Common name for fish in the family Haemulidae

Etymology

From Greek 'haimaleos', meaning 'bloody' or 'abundant in blood', referring to the reddish gills of some species.
