patter

1. NOUN

/ ˈpætər / (AmE), / ˈpætə / (BrE)
a quick series of light, rapid sounds, often made by small objects or animals moving or hitting a surface
quick, light, repeated sound

Examples

Rain began to patter against the rooftop as the storm rolled in.
She heard the patter of her dog's paws on the wooden floor.
The quiet patter of leaves in the wind soothed him to sleep.

Synset Examples

the patter of mice
the patter of tiny feet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tap - a light, quick sound or touch
  • Antonym: thud - a dull, heavy sound made by an object falling or hitting something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07403770-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈpætɚ / (AmE), / ˈpætə / (BrE)
to make light, rapid, and repeated sounds, often as of rain or small feet
make light, repeated sounds

Examples

The rain began to patter gently on the windowpane as evening fell.
I could hear tiny feet pattering across the kitchen floor early in the morning.
Leaves pattered down onto the roof during the storm.

Synset Examples

gently pattering rain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drum - to strike a surface repeatedly to make a rhythmic sound

Etymology

Imitative of the sound itself, first recorded in the 17th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02189633-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈpætər / (AmE), / ˈpætə / (BrE)
quick, smooth, and often insincere talk, especially used by salespeople or entertainers to impress or persuade others.
glib or persuasive talk

Examples

The salesman delivered his usual patter to try to convince the customers.
She listened to the guide’s rapid patter about the history of the old building.
He practiced his comedy patter until the jokes sounded spontaneous.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banter - playful and friendly exchange of teasing remarks
  • Synonym: spiel - a long or fast speech intended to persuade

Etymology

Originally imitative of the sound of quick speech or light tapping, dating to the 18th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07026516-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈpætər / (AmE), / ˈpætə / (BrE)
(of rain) to fall gently and lightly, making a soft, rapid sound.
fall gently (of rain)

Examples

I love listening to the rain patter softly on the roof as I fall asleep.
The gentle spring shower began to patter on the windows.
As we drank our tea, the rain continued to patter outside.

Synset Examples

It has only sprinkled, but the roads are slick

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drizzle - to rain lightly in very fine drops
  • Degree Higher: to pour - to rain heavily
