mellitate

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛlɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɛlɪˌteɪt / (BrE)
a salt or ester of mellitic acid, often found in chemical contexts
a salt or ester of mellitic acid

Examples

The laboratory prepared a mellitate solution for further chemical analysis.
Researchers examined the crystal structure of sodium mellitate under a microscope.
The presence of mellitate ions affected the solubility of the compound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mellitic acid - an organic acid from which mellitate salts are derived

Etymology

From mellitic (relating to mellitic acid) + -ate (a salt or ester).
