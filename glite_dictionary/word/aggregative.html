aggregative

1. ADJ

/ ˈæɡ.rə.ɡə.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈaɡ.rɪ.ɡə.tɪv / (BrE)
formed or consisting of separate units gathered into a collective mass or whole; relating to aggregation.
formed by gathering units into a whole

Examples

The aggregative nature of the data made it suitable for statistical analysis.
In ecology, some species display aggregative behavior during the winter months.
Her research focuses on aggregative processes in social networks.

Synset Examples

aggregate expenses include expenses of all divisions combined for the entire year
the aggregated amount of indebtedness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aggregate - formed or calculated by combining several separate elements

Etymology

From Latin aggregatus (collected together), from aggregare (to add to), with the suffix -ive.
