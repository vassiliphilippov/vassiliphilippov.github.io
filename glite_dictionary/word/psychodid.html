psychodid

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈkoʊdɪd / (AmE), / saɪˈkəʊdɪd / (BrE)
A fly belonging to the family Psychodidae, commonly known as drain flies or moth flies.
fly of the family Psychodidae

Examples

The psychodid is often found in damp areas such as bathrooms and kitchens.
Researchers discovered a new psychodid species in the tropical rainforest.
The larvae of a psychodid can thrive in decaying organic matter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moth fly - a common name for flies of the family Psychodidae
  • Synonym: drain fly - a common name for flies of the family Psychodidae

Etymology

From New Latin Psychodidae, the family name, from Greek psukhē (soul, butterfly) + -id (taxonomic suffix).
