Cebuano

1. NOUN

/ sɛˈbwɑːnoʊ / (AmE), / sɛˈbwɑːnəʊ / (BrE)
the Austronesian language spoken by the people of Cebu and surrounding regions in the Philippines; a major regional language of the country
the language spoken in Cebu, Philippines

Examples

Many Filipinos from the Visayas region speak Cebuano as their first language.
The Cebuano language contains many words that have made their way into Filipino.
She is learning Cebuano to communicate with her relatives in Cebu.

Etymology

From Cebu (an island in the Philippines) + Spanish suffix -ano (denoting pertaining to)

2. NOUN

/ sɛˈbwaːnoʊ / (AmE), / sɛˈbwɑːnəʊ / (BrE)
the Austronesian language spoken by the people of Cebu and surrounding areas in the Philippines
language spoken in Cebu, Philippines

Examples

She is fluent in both Tagalog and Cebuano.
Many songs in the Philippines are performed in Cebuano.
The Cebuano language has influenced Filipino vocabulary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tagalog - A major Filipino language and basis of the national language Filipino

Etymology

From Spanish 'Cebú', the name of the island of Cebu, with the suffix '-ano' meaning 'inhabitant or relating to'.

3. NOUN

/ sɛˈbwɑːnoʊ / (AmE), / sɛˈbwɑːnəʊ / (BrE)
a language spoken in the central Philippines, primarily on the island of Cebu and surrounding areas; one of the major languages of the Philippines
a language of the central Philippines

Examples

Many people in the southern Philippines speak Cebuano as their first language.
The Cebuano language has influenced the vocabulary of Filipino, the national language.
She is fluent in both Tagalog and Cebuano.

Etymology

From Cebu, the name of the island in the Philippines, plus Spanish suffix -ano (denoting origin).
