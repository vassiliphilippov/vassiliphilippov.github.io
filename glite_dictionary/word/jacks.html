jacks

1. NOUN

/ dʒæks / (AmE), / dʒæks / (BrE)
A children's game in which small objects (jackstones) are thrown, then picked up in groups while a small rubber ball is bounced.
children's game with jackstones and a ball

Examples

The children sat in a circle and played jacks during recess.
She carried a bag of jacks and a rubber ball wherever she went.
He remembers playing jacks on the kitchen floor with his sister.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jackstone - one of the small objects thrown and picked up in the game of jacks

Etymology

From the plural of 'jack', referring to the small playing pieces used in the game.
