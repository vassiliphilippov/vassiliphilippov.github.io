ecru

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪkru, ˈɛkru / (AmE), / ˈeɪkruː, ˈɛkruː / (BrE)
a pale shade of brown, often resembling unbleached linen; very light brown or beige
a very light brown color

Examples

The curtains were a soft shade of ecru that matched the walls perfectly.
She chose an ecru dress for the summer wedding because it felt both classic and fresh.
The tablecloth was ecru, giving the dining room a warm, elegant look.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beige - a pale sandy yellowish-brown color
  • Degree Lower: tan - a pale tone of brown
  • Degree Higher: cream - a pale yellowish-white color

Etymology

From French 'écru' meaning 'raw' (unbleached), originally referring to the color of unbleached linen.
