Aengus

1. PROPN

/ ˈeɪŋɡəs / (AmE), / ˈeɪŋɡəs / (BrE)
In Celtic mythology, a god of love and beauty, often regarded as the patron deity of young men and women.
Celtic god of love and beauty

Examples

According to legend, Aengus lived on the banks of the River Boyne in Ireland.
The myths describe Aengus as charming and eternally youthful.
Some modern pagans honor Aengus as a symbol of love and inspiration.

Etymology

From Old Irish Oíngus or Aengus, possibly meaning 'one strength' or 'unique choice'.
