Tarsioidea

1. NOUN

/ tɑr.siˈɔɪ.di.ə / (AmE), / tɑː.sɪˈɔɪ.dɪ.ə / (BrE)
A superfamily of small primates, including tarsiers, sometimes classified within the suborder Prosimii.
superfamily of primates (tarsiers)

Examples

Scientists have long debated the exact placement of Tarsioidea within the primate order.
Members of the Tarsioidea superfamily are known for their large eyes and long fingers.
The evolutionary history of Tarsioidea provides insight into the divergence of primates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tarsius - genus of small leaping Asian primates (tarsiers)

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Tarsius' (genus of tarsiers) + '-oidea' (denoting taxonomic superfamilies).
