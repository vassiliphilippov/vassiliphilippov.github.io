battle

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæt̬əl / (AmE), / ˈbætəl / (BrE)
a violent fight between armed forces, especially as part of a war
a fight between armed forces

Examples

The battle lasted for three days and resulted in heavy losses on both sides.
They reenacted a famous battle from the American Civil War at the historical site.
The general devised a new strategy to win the battle and turn the tide of the war.

Synset Examples

Grant won a decisive victory in the battle of Chattanooga
he lost his romantic ideas about war when he got into a real engagement

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: skirmish - a small or brief fight between opposing forces
  • Degree Higher: war - a state of armed conflict between countries or groups

Etymology

From Old French *bataille*, from Late Latin *battualia* (exercise of soldiers and gladiators), from Latin *battuere* (to beat).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbæt̬.əl / (AmE), / ˈbæ.təl / (BrE)
a determined and energetic effort to achieve or overcome something difficult
energetic effort to achieve something

Examples

Finishing the marathon was a real battle for her.
He faces a daily battle with anxiety at work.
Securing funding for the project became an uphill battle.

Synset Examples

getting through the crowd was a real struggle
he fought a battle for recognition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: struggle - a determined effort to achieve or deal with something difficult
  • Synonym: fight - a vigorous effort or contest to achieve something or defend against something
  • Antonym: surrender - to give up or cease to make an energetic effort

Etymology

From Old French 'bataille', from Late Latin 'battualia' (fighting, exercise of soldiers and gladiators).

3. VERB

/ ˈbæt̬.əl / (AmE), / ˈbæt.əl / (BrE)
to fight or struggle against someone or something, especially in a difficult or prolonged conflict
fight or struggle against

Examples

The company continues to battle fierce competition in the technology market.
Despite her illness, she battled bravely and never gave up hope.
They have been battling over the property rights for years.

Synset Examples

The Kurds are combating Iraqi troops in Northern Iraq
We must combat the prejudices against other races
they battled over the budget

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fight - to take part in a violent struggle; to contend
  • Synonym: to contend - to struggle or compete against someone
  • Antonym: surrender - to stop fighting and admit defeat

Etymology

From Old French 'bataille', from Late Latin 'battualia' (military exercise, fencing, battle).

4. NOUN

/ ˈbæt̬əl / (AmE), / ˈbætəl / (BrE)
a violent fight between large organized groups or armed forces, or a serious conflict between individuals or groups
a violent fight or serious conflict

Examples

The two armies met in a fierce battle that lasted for hours.
After a heated battle, the union secured better working conditions for its members.
Police had to intervene to break up the battle between rival football fans.

Synset Examples

the harder the conflict, the more glorious the triumph
police tried to control the battle between the pro- and anti-abortion mobs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fight - a violent confrontation between individuals or small groups
  • Degree Higher: war - a prolonged conflict involving organized armed forces
  • Degree Lower: skirmish - a minor or brief fight between small parts of armies

Etymology

From Old French *bataille* (battle, fight), from Late Latin *battualia* (military or gladiatorial exercises).
