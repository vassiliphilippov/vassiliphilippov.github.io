songbird

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔŋ.bɝːd / (AmE), / ˈsɒŋ.bɜːd / (BrE)
a bird belonging to the group of birds known for their musical or melodious calls, especially those in the suborder Passeri.
a bird with a melodious call

Examples

The songbird filled the morning with beautiful melodies.
Many people keep a songbird in a cage for its sweet singing.
Scientists study the migration patterns of various songbirds each spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warbler - a small songbird often noted for its singing
  • Synonym: passerine - a perching bird, most of which are songbirds
