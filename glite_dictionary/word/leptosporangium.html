leptosporangium

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛptoʊspəˈrændʒiəm / (AmE), / ˌlɛptəʊspəˈrandʒɪəm / (BrE)
A type of sporangium that develops from a single epidermal cell, characteristic of most modern ferns (Filicales).
fern sporangium from a single cell
medical

Examples

The leptosporangium is an important structure distinguishing most modern ferns from other plant groups.
Scientists examined the leptosporangium under a microscope to study its development.
Each leptosporangium releases spores when mature, aiding in fern reproduction.

Etymology

From Greek 'leptos' (thin, delicate) and 'sporangium' (spore case).
