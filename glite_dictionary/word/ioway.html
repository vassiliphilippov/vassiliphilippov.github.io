Ioway

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ.oʊ.weɪ / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.ə.weɪ / (BrE)
the dialect of the Chiwere language spoken by the Iowa people
dialect of Chiwere language

Examples

Linguists have worked to document the Ioway dialect before it disappears.
He studied Ioway as part of his research on indigenous languages of North America.
Many traditional stories are still told in Ioway within the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chiwere - language family including Ioway, Otoe, and Missouria dialects

Etymology

From the name of the Iowa people, self-designation and tribe name.

2. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ.oʊ.weɪ / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.ə.weɪ / (BrE)
A member of a Native American people formerly living in areas of Iowa, Minnesota, and Missouri, belonging to the Siouan language family.
a member of a Native American Siouan people

Examples

The Ioway were known for their farming and trading along Midwest rivers.
Several descendants of the Ioway now live in Oklahoma and Kansas.
The museum features artifacts from the historic Ioway culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Iowa - A U.S. state named after the Ioway people

Etymology

From the French 'Aiouez', from Dakota or Omaha-Ponca 'ayúxba' (sleepy ones), the native autonym of the tribe.
