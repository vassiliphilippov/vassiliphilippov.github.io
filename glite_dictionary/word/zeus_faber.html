Zeus faber

1. NOUN

/ zuːs ˈfɑːbər / (AmE), / zjuːs ˈfeɪbə / (BrE)
A species of dory, also known as the John Dory, found in European waters, recognized by its deep, laterally compressed body and a distinctive dark spot on each side.
species of European dory fish (John Dory)

Examples

The chef prepared the Zeus faber with a delicate lemon butter sauce.
Marine biologists conducted a study on the behavior of Zeus faber in coastal habitats.
The Zeus faber, also known as John Dory, is prized for its firm white flesh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: John Dory - Common English name for Zeus faber

Etymology

From Latin: Zeus (a genus of fish) and faber ('smith', possibly referencing the fish's shape or 'St. Peter's thumbprint').
