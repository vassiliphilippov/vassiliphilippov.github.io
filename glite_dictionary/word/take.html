to require or need something, typically as necessary, appropriate, or sufficient
require or need (something)

Examples

It takes a lot of courage to speak in public for the first time.
Fixing the car takes special tools that most people don't have.
Learning a new language takes patience and consistent effort.

Synset Examples

It takes nerve to do what she did
success usually requires hard work
This job asks a lot of patience and skill
This position demands a lot of personal sacrifice
This dinner calls for a spectacular dessert
This intervention does not postulate a patient's consent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to require - to need something as necessary
  • Synonym: demand - to need or require (especially with emphasis)
  • Synonym: to call for - to require, need, or demand (often used for events or situations)

Etymology

From Old English *tacan* (to take, seize), from Old Norse *taka*.

41. VERB

/ teɪk / (AmE), / teɪk / (BrE)
(with illness) To become affected by or contract a particular illness or condition, often expressed in the form 'take a chill' or 'take a cold'.
to contract an illness (as in 'take a chill')
archaic

Examples

She was out all night in the rain and took a chill.
He feared he might take cold after being caught in the storm.
After getting drenched, the child took a fever and had to rest.

Synset Examples

He got AIDS
She came down with pneumonia
She took a chill

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catch - to contract or become affected by an illness
  • Antonym: to recover - to return to a normal state of health; antonym in the context of illness
