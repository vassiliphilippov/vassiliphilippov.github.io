homogeneousness

1. NOUN

/ hoʊˌmoʊˈdʒiːniəsnəs / (AmE), / həˌməʊˈdʒiːniəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being homogeneous; similarity or uniformity in kind or nature
quality of being homogeneous or uniform

Examples

The homogeneousness of the group's opinions made reaching a consensus much easier.
Critics argue that too much homogeneousness in media content can suppress creative diversity.
The scientist measured the homogeneousness of the solution before proceeding with the experiment.

Synset Examples

there is a remarkable homogeneity between the two companies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homogeneity - the quality of being homogeneous
  • Antonym: heterogeneity - the quality of being diverse in kind or nature

Etymology

Formed from 'homogeneous' (from Greek 'homogenēs', meaning 'of the same kind') + '-ness'.
