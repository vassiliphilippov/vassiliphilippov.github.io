to excoriated

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈskɔriˌeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ɪkˈskɔːrɪeɪtɪd / (BrE)
criticized or denounced harshly, especially in public or in written reviews
harshly criticized or denounced

Examples

The mayor was excoriated in the press for failing to respond to the crisis.
Several critics excoriated the film for its lack of originality.
She excoriated her opponent's policies during the debate.

Synset Examples

The politician was excoriated by the media for his comments.
His review excoriated the author's lack of depth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to criticize - express disapproval of someone or something—synonym
  • Antonym: praise - express approval or admiration—antonym

Etymology

From Latin excoriare (to strip the skin), from ex- (out) + corium (skin, hide); figuratively means to criticize harshly.
