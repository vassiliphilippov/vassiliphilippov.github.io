Pydna

1. PROPN

/ ˈpɪd.nə / (AmE), / ˈpɪd.nə / (BrE)
an ancient battle in 168 BC in which the Romans defeated the Macedonians, leading to the fall of the Macedonian kingdom
ancient battle, Roman defeat of Macedonians (168 BC)

Examples

Historians view the Battle of Pydna as a turning point in Roman expansion.
The defeat at Pydna marked the end of Macedonian power in the ancient world.
Many artifacts recovered near Pydna shed light on Roman military tactics.

Etymology

From the name of the ancient city Pydna in Macedonia, site of the battle.
