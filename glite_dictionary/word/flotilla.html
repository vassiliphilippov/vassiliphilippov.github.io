flotilla

1. NOUN

/ floʊˈtɪlə / (AmE), / fləʊˈtɪlə / (BrE)
A group of small ships or boats, especially when acting together as a fleet.
group of small ships or boats

Examples

A flotilla of fishing boats sailed out at dawn to catch the day's haul.
During the parade, a flotilla of decorated canoes passed under the bridge.
The navy sent a flotilla to assist with the coastal rescue operation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fleet - a large group of ships sailing together

Etymology

From Spanish 'flotilla', diminutive of 'flota' (fleet).

2. NOUN

/ floʊˈtɪl.ə / (AmE), / fləʊˈtɪl.ə / (BrE)
A fleet of small warships, especially one consisting of two or more squadrons, usually used in the United States Navy or similar naval forces.
fleet of small naval vessels

Examples

The navy deployed a flotilla of patrol boats to secure the coastline.
A flotilla of small warships participated in the annual maritime exercise.
Reports indicated that a flotilla was patrolling the disputed waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fleet - a large group of ships, especially warships, operating together

Etymology

From Spanish 'flotilla', diminutive of 'flota' (fleet).
