comforting

1. ADJ

/ ˈkʌmfərtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkʌmfətɪŋ / (BrE)
Giving comfort or consolation; making someone feel less worried, upset, or unhappy.
providing comfort or solace

Examples

She found his gentle words deeply comforting after a difficult day.
The warm blanket was comforting on a cold winter night.
It was comforting to know her friends were always there for her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reassuring - making someone feel less worried or fearful
  • Antonym: disturbing - causing worry or anxiety

Etymology

From the verb 'comfort' + '-ing'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkʌmfərtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkʌmfətɪŋ / (BrE)
making someone feel less anxious, upset, or worried; giving comfort or reassurance.
giving comfort or reassurance

Examples

Her words had a comforting effect during the difficult times.
The sound of rain falling outside was strangely comforting to him.
It is comforting to know that help is only a phone call away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reassuring - making someone feel less worried or afraid
  • Antonym: disturbing - causing worry or upset

Etymology

Present participle of 'comfort', from Old French 'conforter', from Latin 'confortare' (to strengthen greatly).
