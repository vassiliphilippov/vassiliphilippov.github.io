rosita

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈsiːtə / (AmE), / rəʊˈsiːtə / (BrE)
An erect plant native to the southwestern United States, with small clusters of pink, trumpet-shaped flowers.
plant with pink trumpet-shaped flowers

Examples

The rosita blooms each spring, adding bright color to the desert landscape.
Gardeners admire the delicate flowers of the rosita for their unusual trumpet shape.
Hikers stopped to photograph a wild rosita growing beside the path.

Etymology

From Spanish diminutive of 'rosa' meaning 'rose'.
