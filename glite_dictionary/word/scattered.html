scattered

1. ADJ

/ ˈskæt̬ɚd / (AmE), / ˈskætəd / (BrE)
Spread or distributed over a wide area or spaced irregularly in time or space.
spread irregularly over an area

Examples

The tiny village consisted of several scattered houses along the hillside.
After the storm, there were scattered branches all over the park.
Weather reports suggest only scattered showers throughout the afternoon.

Synset Examples

scattered showers
scattered villages

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clustered - gathered or growing closely together

Etymology

From Middle English 'scateren', of Germanic origin.

2. ADJ

/ ˈskæt̬.ɚd / (AmE), / ˈskæt.əd / (BrE)
lacking order or continuity; spread out or disorganized in a way that lacks structure.
disordered or lacking continuity

Examples

She found her scattered notes difficult to organize before the meeting.
His mind was filled with scattered thoughts after the stressful day.
The scattered details in the report made it challenging to understand the main point.

Synset Examples

a confused set of instructions
a confused dream about the end of the world
disconnected fragments of a story
scattered thoughts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disorganized - lacking order or structure
  • Antonym: orderly - neatly organized or structured
