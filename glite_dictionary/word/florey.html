Florey

1. PROPN

/ ˈflɔːri / (AmE), / ˈflɔːri / (BrE)
(1898–1968) British pathologist who isolated and purified penicillin, significantly advancing its use as an antibiotic.
British pathologist; helped develop penicillin

Examples

Many medical historians credit Florey with turning penicillin into a practical medicine.
The Nobel Prize was awarded to Florey for his contribution to antibiotic research.
Without the work of Florey, penicillin might never have reached the public.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fleming - Scottish bacteriologist who discovered penicillin
