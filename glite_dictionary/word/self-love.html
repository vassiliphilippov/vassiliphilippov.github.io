self-love

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlfˈlʌv / (AmE), / ˌsɛlfˈlʌv / (BrE)
regard for one's own happiness or interests; excessive admiration of oneself
regard for one's own happiness or interests

Examples

Practicing self-love helped her develop more confidence and resilience.
Some critics see his attitude as bordering on self-love rather than healthy self-esteem.
Without a sense of self-love, people may struggle to set boundaries or care for themselves properly.

Synset Examples

self-love that shut out everyone else

Related Terms

  • Synonym: narcissism - excessive interest in oneself and one's physical appearance
  • Antonym: selflessness - concern for the needs and wishes of others

Etymology

From self- + love, first recorded in English in the 16th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlfˈlʌv / (AmE), / ˌsɛlfˈlʌv / (BrE)
Excessive pride or admiration for oneself; an inordinate regard for one's own abilities or appearance.
excessive pride in oneself

Examples

Her self-love made it difficult for her to accept any criticism, no matter how constructive.
The leader's self-love was evident in every speech he gave about his achievements.
Too much self-love can alienate friends and colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: egotism - the practice of talking and thinking about oneself excessively because of an undue sense of self-importance
  • Antonym: humility - a modest view of one's own importance; humbleness

Etymology

Formed from 'self' + 'love', attested since the early 16th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɛlf lʌv / (AmE), / ˈsɛlf lʌv / (BrE)
The regard and care that one shows for one's own well-being and happiness.
regard for one's own well-being and happiness

Examples

Learning self-love helped him recover from years of insecurity.
Many therapists encourage self-love as a foundation for healthy relationships.
She wrote a book on the importance of self-love in overcoming adversity.

Synset Examples

Practicing self-love is essential for mental health.
Self-love involves accepting oneself without conditions.
Her journey to self-love transformed her life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-esteem - confidence in one's own worth or abilities
  • Antonym: self-hatred - intense dislike or hatred of oneself

Etymology

Formed from 'self' (Old English 'self', own person) + 'love' (Old English 'lufu').
