hardiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑr.di.nəs / (AmE), / ˈhɑː.dɪ.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being strong and able to withstand difficult conditions or illness
ability to withstand hardship or illness

Examples

The hardiness of the mountain climbers amazed everyone watching the expedition.
Certain plants are bred specifically for their hardiness in cold climates.
Despite the harsh winter, the villagers showed incredible hardiness and determination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resilience - the ability to recover quickly from difficulties; toughness
  • Antonym: fragility - the quality of being easily broken or damaged

Etymology

From hardy + -ness; ultimately from Old French 'hardi' (bold, courageous).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːrdinəs / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being willing to undertake things that involve risk or danger; boldness or courage
willingness to take risks or face danger

Examples

His hardiness was evident when he volunteered for the perilous expedition.
Few leaders possess the hardiness required to make such difficult decisions in times of crisis.
The hardiness of the mountain climbers impressed everyone back at base camp.

Synset Examples

the proposal required great boldness
the plan required great hardiness of heart

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boldness - willingness to take risks; confidence and courage
  • Antonym: timidity - lack of courage or confidence; shyness

Etymology

From Old French *hardi* (bold, daring), itself from hardir (to harden, make bold).
