polyunsaturated

1. ADJ

/ ˌpɑːliˌʌnˈsætʃəˌreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˌʌnˈsætʃəreɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of fats or other organic compounds) containing several double or triple bonds between carbon atoms in the chain, especially referring to fatty acids.
having multiple double bonds (esp. in fats)

Examples

A diet high in polyunsaturated fats may help reduce cholesterol levels.
Sunflower oil is rich in polyunsaturated fatty acids.
Nutritionists often recommend replacing saturated fats with polyunsaturated ones for better heart health.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: saturated - (of fats) containing no double bonds
  • Degree Lower: monounsaturated - (of fats) having one double bond

Etymology

From poly- (many) and unsaturated, indicating multiple double bonds in the molecule.
