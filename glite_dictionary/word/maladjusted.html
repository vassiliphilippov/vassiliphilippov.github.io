maladjusted

1. ADJ

/ mæləˈdʒʌstɪd / (AmE), / ˌmaləˈdʒʌstɪd / (BrE)
Not able to cope well with the demands and stresses of normal daily life; having difficulty adapting to social or emotional situations.
unable to adjust to life's demands

Examples

The school provides counseling for maladjusted students who struggle to fit in.
Growing up in a chaotic household left him feeling maladjusted as an adult.
A maladjusted teenager may have trouble building lasting friendships.

Synset Examples

a maladjusted child

Related Terms

  • Antonym: well-adjusted - able to cope well with life's demands

Etymology

From Latin 'mal-' (badly) + 'adjusted', meaning badly adjusted.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00352919-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˌmæl.əˈdʒʌs.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌmal.əˈdʒʌs.tɪd / (BrE)
Not properly adjusted, especially to the demands of a situation, environment, or system.
not properly adjusted; ill-adapted

Examples

After years of frequent moves, he became a maladjusted teenager struggling to fit in at school.
The foster dog was maladjusted due to neglect in its early life.
A maladjusted carburetor can cause an engine to run poorly.

Synset Examples

a maladjusted carburetor

Related Terms

  • Antonym: well-adjusted - properly adapted to environment or circumstance

Etymology

From mal- (bad) + adjusted, modeled after 'maladjustment,' first used in the early 20th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00352521-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˌmæl.əˈdʒʌs.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌmal.əˈdʒʌs.tɪd / (BrE)
emotionally disturbed and unable to cope successfully with the demands of normal social life
unable to adjust emotionally or socially

Examples

As a teenager, he was considered maladjusted and struggled to make friends at school.
Social workers often help children who are maladjusted to adapt better to their environment.
The maladjusted student frequently acted out in class and had difficulty following rules.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: well-adjusted - emotionally stable and able to cope with life

Etymology

From prefix 'mal-' (badly) + 'adjusted', meaning badly adjusted.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
