reflexiveness

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈflɛksɪvnəs / (AmE), / rɪˈflɛksɪvnɪs / (BrE)
(logic, mathematics) the property of a relation whereby it holds between any element and itself.
property of a relation; holds between element and itself

Examples

In mathematics, the reflexiveness of the equality relation is taken for granted.
The reflexiveness of a relation means every element is related to itself.
To prove reflexiveness, you must show that each object stands in the relation to itself.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reflexivity - the quality or state of being reflexive; especially in mathematics, the property that a relation holds between an element and itself

Etymology

Formed from 'reflexive' + '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈflɛksɪvnəs / (AmE), / rɪˈflɛksɪvnɪs / (BrE)
the grammatical property or relation in which a reflexive pronoun refers back to its own subject or antecedent
property of reflexive pronouns relating to their subject

Examples

Linguists study reflexiveness to understand how pronouns relate to their antecedents in different languages.
The reflexiveness of the pronoun 'herself' links it to the subject of the sentence.
Accurate use of reflexiveness is essential when teaching English grammar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reflexivity - the quality of referring back to oneself or itself in grammar

Etymology

From 'reflexive' + '-ness', denoting the state or quality.
