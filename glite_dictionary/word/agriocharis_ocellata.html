Agriocharis ocellata

1. NOUN

/ ˌæɡ.ri.oʊˈkɛər.ɪs oʊˈsɛl.ə.tə / (AmE), / ˌaɡ.ri.əʊˈkɛər.ɪs əʊˈsɛl.ə.tə / (BrE)
a species of wild turkey native to Central America and northern South America, known as the ocellated turkey
a wild turkey species of Central America

Examples

The Agriocharis ocellata, or ocellated turkey, inhabits forests of the Yucatán Peninsula.
Researchers studied the mating displays of Agriocharis ocellata in their natural environment.
Conservation efforts are in place to protect the declining populations of Agriocharis ocellata.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ocellated turkey - common name for Agriocharis ocellata
  • Degree Lower: wild turkey - bird species native to North America (Meleagris gallopavo)

Etymology

From Greek 'agrios' (wild), 'charis' (grace), and Latin 'ocellatus' (having little eyes), referring to the eye-like spots on feathers.
