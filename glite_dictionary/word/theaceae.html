Theaceae

1. NOUN

/ θiˈeɪəsiˌi / (AmE), / θɪˈiːɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of evergreen trees and shrubs, including the tea plant, native mainly to Asia and the Americas
family of evergreen shrubs and trees; tea family

Examples

The Theaceae family includes the tea plant, which is cultivated worldwide for its leaves.
Several garden shrubs belong to the Theaceae, valued for their attractive flowers.
Botanists classify camellias as part of the Theaceae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Camellia - a genus in the Theaceae family, including ornamental plants and the tea plant

Etymology

From New Latin, based on 'Thea' (an old name for the tea plant) + '-aceae' (plant family suffix)

2. NOUN

/ θiːˈeɪ.əˌsiː / (AmE), / θiːˈiː.əˌsiː / (BrE)
A family of trees and shrubs that includes the tea plant and closely related species, belonging to the order Ericales.
family of trees and shrubs, including the tea plant
medical

Examples

The tea plant, Camellia sinensis, is a member of the Theaceae family.
Researchers discovered several new species in the Theaceae during their expedition.
Plants in the Theaceae are often cultivated for their ornamental flowers.

Etymology

From modern Latin Theaceae, from Thea, former genus name for tea.
