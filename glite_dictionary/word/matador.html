matador

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæt̬.ə.dɔr / (AmE), / ˈmat.ə.dɔː / (BrE)
the principal bullfighter in a bullfight, who is responsible for making the final passes and killing the bull
chief bullfighter in a bullfight

Examples

The crowd erupted in applause as the matador delivered the final blow to the bull.
She studied the precise movements of the matador during the dramatic bullfight.
After years of practice, he achieved his dream of becoming a famous matador in Spain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bullfighter - a person who fights bulls in a bullfight

Etymology

From Spanish 'matador' (killer), from 'matar' (to kill).
