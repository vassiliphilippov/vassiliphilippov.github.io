consummated

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɑnsəˌmeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɒnsjʊˌmeɪtɪd / (BrE)
brought to completion; fully completed or finalized, especially in a formal or legal context.
brought to completion

Examples

The merger was officially consummated after both companies signed the agreement.
They celebrated their consummated marriage during the wedding reception.
No contract is valid until the transaction is fully consummated.

Synset Examples

a consummated transaction

Related Terms

  • Antonym: incomplete - not yet finished or brought to completion

Etymology

From Latin consummatus, past participle of consummare (to sum up, complete).
