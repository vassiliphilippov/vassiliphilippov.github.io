Malacosoma americana

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæləkəˈsoʊmə əˌmɛrɪˈkænə / (AmE), / ˌmaləkəˈsəʊmə əˌmɛrɪˈkanə / (BrE)
A species of moth whose larvae are known as eastern tent caterpillars, often recognized by their communal silk tents in trees.
a moth species, the eastern tent caterpillar

Examples

The larvae of Malacosoma americana build large silk tents in cherry trees each spring.
Gardeners often find Malacosoma americana on ornamental trees during the early months of the year.
Scientists are studying the population dynamics of Malacosoma americana in forested areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eastern tent caterpillar - larval stage of Malacosoma americana, known for forming silk tents

Etymology

From Greek 'malakos' (soft) and Latin 'soma' (body), with 'americana' denoting its American distribution.
