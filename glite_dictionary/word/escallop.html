escallop

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈskæləp / (AmE), / ɪˈskæl.əp / (BrE)
an edible muscle from a mollusk with a fan-shaped shell, often served broiled, poached, or in various dishes
edible muscle of a fan-shaped shell mollusk

Examples

The chef prepared grilled escallop with a garlic butter sauce.
She enjoys adding chopped escallop to her seafood salad for extra flavor.
The restaurant's signature dish features baked escallop in a creamy sauce.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scallop - edible muscle of a mollusk, commonly called 'scallop'

Etymology

Variant of 'scallop', from Old French *escalope* (shell).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈskæləp / (AmE), / ɪˈskæləp / (BrE)
A thin slice of meat, especially veal, usually fried or broiled.
thin slice of meat (esp. veal), usually fried or broiled

Examples

The chef prepared a delicious escallop of veal with a light lemon sauce.
At the restaurant, I ordered the chicken escallop with mushrooms and cream.
She carefully browned each escallop in the skillet before serving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scallop - a thin slice of meat (alternative spelling); also a shellfish

Etymology

From French *escalope* (shell, scallop), referring to the original shape of the meat slices.

3. NOUN

/ ɛˈskæl.əp / (AmE), / ɪˈskæl.əp / (BrE)
an edible marine bivalve mollusk with a fluted fan-shaped shell, able to swim by rapidly clapping its shell valves together
a type of edible shellfish with a fan-shaped shell

Examples

The chef prepared a delicious starter using fresh escallops from the market.
Divers collected several escallops along the rocky coast during low tide.
The fluted shell of the escallop is often used for decorative purposes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scallop - an edible marine bivalve mollusk with a ribbed fan-shaped shell

Etymology

Variant of 'scallop', from Old French 'escalope' (shell).

4. VERB

/ ɪˈskɑl.əp / (AmE), / ɪˈskæl.əp / (BrE)
To bake (food) in a sauce, often with milk, and usually with breadcrumbs on top.
bake food with sauce and breadcrumbs

Examples

My grandmother loves to escallop potatoes for Sunday dinners.
We decided to escallop some fish with a creamy dill sauce.
The chef escalloped the vegetables, adding a golden breadcrumb topping before baking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scallop - to prepare (food) in a similar manner

Etymology

From 'scallop', originally referring to cooking and serving food in scallop shells.
