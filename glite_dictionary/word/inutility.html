inutility

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌnuːˈtɪləti / (AmE), / ɪˌnjuːˈtɪləti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being useless or serving no practical purpose.
lack of usefulness; uselessness

Examples

The sheer inutility of the device became apparent after several failed attempts to use it.
He lamented the inutility of memorizing outdated facts for the exam.
Despite its elegance, the sculpture's inutility made it unpopular among those who valued function over form.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uselessness - the state of being useless
  • Antonym: utility - the state of being useful

Etymology

From Late Latin inūtilitas, from Latin inūtilis (useless).
