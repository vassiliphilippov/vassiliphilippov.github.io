Lampyridae

1. NOUN

/ læmˈpɪr.i.aɪd / (AmE), / lamˈpɪər.ɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of beetles commonly known as fireflies or lightning bugs, noted for their ability to produce light.
biological family of fireflies

Examples

The Lampyridae family includes over 2,000 species of fireflies found worldwide.
Scientists study the luminous organs in Lampyridae to better understand bioluminescence.
Lampyridae are most active during warm summer evenings, when their flashing lights attract mates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firefly - a beetle known for emitting light

Etymology

From New Latin 'Lampyridae', based on Greek 'lampyris' (glow-worm).
