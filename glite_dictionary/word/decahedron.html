decahedron

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛkəˈhiːdrən / (AmE), / ˌdɛkəˈhiːdrən / (BrE)
A solid shape in geometry with ten flat faces.
solid shape with ten flat faces

Examples

The geometric model on the table is a decahedron with clearly marked faces.
In mathematics class, we studied how to calculate the surface area of a decahedron.
Unlike a cube, a decahedron has ten faces, making it more complex.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: polyhedron - a solid in geometry with many flat faces

Etymology

From Greek deka (ten) + hēdra (seat, base, face).
