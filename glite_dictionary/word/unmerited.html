unmerited

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈmɛrɪtɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈmɛrɪtɪd / (BrE)
Not deserved or justified; not earned or warranted.
not deserved; not earned

Examples

His promotion was widely seen as unmerited by his colleagues.
She was embarrassed by the unmerited praise heaped upon her efforts.
I felt that the criticism was harsh and completely unmerited.

Synset Examples

received an unmerited honorary degree

Related Terms

  • Synonym: undeserved - not deserved or justified
  • Antonym: merited - deserved or justified

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'merited', from Latin 'meritus' (deserved).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈmɛrɪtɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈmɛrɪtɪd / (BrE)
Not deserved or justified; not merited.
not deserved or justified

Examples

His apology felt unmerited given the circumstance.
The criticism she received was completely unmerited.
They enjoyed unmerited praise after the project succeeded.

Synset Examples

unmerited treatment of a potentially fine subject

Related Terms

  • Synonym: undeserved - not warranted or justified; not deserved
  • Antonym: merited - deserved or earned; justified

Etymology

Formed by prefixing 'un-' to 'merited', from Latin meritum (reward, due).
