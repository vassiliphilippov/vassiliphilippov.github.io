clutch

1. NOUN

/ klʌtʃ / (AmE), / klʌtʃ / (BrE)
a device for connecting and disconnecting the power transmission from a driving shaft to a driven shaft, especially in vehicles
mechanical device connecting/disconnecting power

Examples

She had trouble shifting gears because the clutch was worn out.
The mechanic replaced the clutch in my car last week.
Press the clutch pedal fully before starting the engine.

Synset Examples

this year's model has an improved clutch

Etymology

From Old English 'clyccan' (to grasp or seize).
