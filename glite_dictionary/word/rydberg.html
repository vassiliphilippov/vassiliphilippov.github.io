Rydberg

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪdˌbɜrɡ / (AmE), / ˈrɪdˌbɜːɡ / (BrE)
a unit of wave number characteristic of the spectral lines of an element, used in atomic spectroscopy
wave number in atomic spectra

Examples

The transition energy can be measured in Rydberg units for convenience in quantum calculations.
Each element has a different characteristic Rydberg constant.
The Rydberg formula accurately predicts the wavelengths of hydrogen spectral lines.

Etymology

Named after Swedish physicist Johannes Rydberg (1854–1919).
