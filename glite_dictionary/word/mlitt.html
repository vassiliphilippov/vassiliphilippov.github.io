MLitt

1. NOUN

/ ɛm ˈlɪt / (AmE), / ɛm ˈlɪt / (BrE)
a master's degree in literature or related subjects, typically awarded after postgraduate study at some universities
Master of Letters degree

Examples

After completing her MLitt, she decided to pursue a PhD in English literature.
The university's MLitt program attracts students from around the world.
He proudly added the MLitt to his list of academic qualifications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: MA - Master of Arts degree, another type of postgraduate academic degree

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'Master of Letters'.
