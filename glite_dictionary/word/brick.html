brick

1. NOUN

/ brɪk / (AmE), / brɪk / (BrE)
a person who is helpful, reliable, and trustworthy; a good, dependable fellow
helpful, reliable person
archaic

Examples

Everyone agrees that Richard is a real brick who can always be counted on in a crisis.
You’ve been an absolute brick, helping me move house in the rain.
Though the job was difficult, she proved herself a brick by staying until the end.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pillar - a person who is a mainstay or support of a group

Etymology

Used figuratively since the 19th century, alluding to the qualities of strength and reliability in bricks.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09893894-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ brɪk / (AmE), / brɪk / (BrE)
A rectangular block made of clay, baked and hardened, used as a building or paving material.
rectangular block for building or paving

Examples

The new walkway was paved with red brick to match the house.
She built a small herb garden using leftover bricks from the construction site.
Workers stacked each brick carefully to create a stable wall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stone - naturally occurring hard material used for building
  • Synonym: block - solid piece of hard material, often used for building

Etymology

Middle English, from Old French *briche*, from Old High German *brihhan* (to break).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02901103-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ brɪk / (AmE), / brɪk / (BrE)
(of an electronic device) to stop working completely, typically as a result of a software or hardware failure, so as to become as useless as a brick.
(of a device) to stop working completely
slang

Examples

If you interrupt the update, you might brick your phone.
The technician accidentally bricked the router during the firmware upgrade.
Many users fear bricking their devices while trying to install unofficial software.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: break - to cause to stop working properly or entirely

Etymology

Figurative use of 'brick', likening a non-functioning device to a literal brick.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-90011761-v
Synset POS: v
