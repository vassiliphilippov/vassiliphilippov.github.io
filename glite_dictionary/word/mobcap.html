mobcap

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːbˌkæp / (AmE), / ˈmɒbˌkæp / (BrE)
a large, high, frilly cap with a full crown, formerly worn indoors by women, especially in the 18th and early 19th centuries
frilly woman's indoor cap (historical)

Examples

In her costume, she wore a white cotton mobcap tied with a blue ribbon.
Paintings from the era often depict women in elegant dresses and a mobcap.
The actress complemented her colonial outfit with an authentic-looking mobcap.

Etymology

Probably from 'mob' (slang for 'a fashionable woman' in the 18th century) + 'cap'.
