holonymy

1. NOUN

/ ˌhoʊˈlɑːnəmi / (AmE), / həˈlɒnɪmi / (BrE)
the semantic relationship between a whole and its constituent parts, where the whole is termed a holonym and the parts are meronyms
semantic relationship: whole to its parts

Examples

In linguistics, 'tree' demonstrates holonymy with respect to 'branch' and 'leaf'.
Understanding holonymy helps distinguish how words are related as wholes and parts.
The dictionary uses holonymy to organize entries about complex objects and their components.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: meronymy - the semantic relationship of part to whole, opposite of holonymy

Etymology

Formed from 'holonym' (from Greek 'holos' meaning 'whole' and 'onyma' meaning 'name') + '-y', following the pattern of terms like 'synonymy' and 'antonymy'.
