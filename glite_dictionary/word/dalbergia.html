Dalbergia

1. NOUN

/ dælˈbɜrdʒiə / (AmE), / dælˈbɜːdʒiə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical trees with pinnate leaves and branched clusters of flowers, cultivated for their attractively grained and colored hardwoods, such as rosewood.
genus of tropical trees producing valued timbers

Examples

Some of the most prized rosewood comes from trees of the Dalbergia genus.
Overharvesting of Dalbergia species has led to concerns about their conservation status.
The unique patterns in Dalbergia timber make it highly sought after by furniture makers.

Etymology

Named after Swedish botanist Nils Dahlberg (1736–1820).

2. NOUN

/ dælˈbɜrdʒiə / (AmE), / dælˈbɜːdʒɪə / (BrE)
a large genus of tropical trees with pinnate leaves and paniculate flowers, cultivated for their attractively grained and colored timbers such as rosewood and tulipwood.
genus of tropical trees valued for timber
medical

Examples

The wood of Dalbergia is highly prized for making fine furniture and musical instruments.
Many species in the Dalbergia genus are threatened due to illegal logging for their valuable timber.
Rosewood, sourced from Dalbergia trees, is renowned for its beautiful grain and rich color.

Etymology

Named after Nils and Carl Dalberg, Swedish botanists.
