Juncus effusus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌŋkəs ɪˈfjuːsəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌŋkəs ɪˈfjuːsəs / (BrE)
A tall rush with soft, erect or arching stems, commonly found in wetlands across Eurasia, Australia, New Zealand, and North America.
species of tall wetland rush

Examples

Juncus effusus is often planted in constructed wetlands for its ability to filter water.
The soft, cylindrical stems of Juncus effusus are a common sight in marshes and along pond edges.
Many birds and insects find shelter among the dense clumps of Juncus effusus.

Etymology

From Latin *juncus* (rush) and *effusus* (spreading out, loose).
