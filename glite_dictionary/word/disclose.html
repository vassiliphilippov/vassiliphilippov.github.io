to disclose

1. VERB

/ dɪsˈkloʊz / (AmE), / dɪsˈkləʊz / (BrE)
to make information known to people, especially information that was previously secret or not widely known
make known information previously secret

Examples

The company refused to disclose the details of the settlement agreement to the public.
Applicants must disclose any criminal convictions in their application forms.
After months of speculation, the results of the investigation were finally disclosed by the committee.

Synset Examples

The auction house would not disclose the price at which the van Gogh had sold
The actress won't reveal how old she is
bring out the truth
he broke the news to her
unwrap the evidence in the murder case
The newspaper uncovered the President's illegal dealings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reveal - to make something known that was previously secret or unknown
  • Antonym: to conceal - to hide or keep something secret

Etymology

From Old French 'desclos', past participle of 'desclore', from Latin 'dis-' (apart) + 'claudere' (to close).

2. VERB

/ dɪsˈkloʊz / (AmE), / dɪsˈkləʊz / (BrE)
to make something visible or known by removing a covering or by revealing it
reveal to view or make known

Examples

The curtain parted to disclose a hidden doorway.
Excavation of the site helped disclose ancient ruins buried under the earth.
She drew aside her cloak to disclose the beautiful necklace.

Synset Examples

The curtain rose to disclose a stunning set

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reveal - to make previously hidden or unknown information visible
  • Antonym: to conceal - to hide or keep something secret

Etymology

From Old French *desclos* (unclosed), from Latin *discludere* (to open, remove a barrier).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02142175-v
Synset POS: v
