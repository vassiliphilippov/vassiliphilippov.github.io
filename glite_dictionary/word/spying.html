spying

1. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspaɪɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of secretly observing others to gather information, especially for political or military purposes
act of secretly observing for information

Examples

The government accused her of spying for a foreign agency.
Movies about spying often feature secret gadgets and elaborate disguises.
He was caught spying on the military facility late at night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: espionage - the practice of spying or using spies, typically by governments

Etymology

From verb 'spy' + -ing; 'spy' from Old French *espier*, from Frankish *spāhōn* (to observe).

2. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspaɪɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of watching someone or something secretly, especially to obtain information
secret watching or surveillance

Examples

The agency was accused of spying on foreign diplomats during the negotiations.
Her spying was eventually discovered by the security staff.
There are strict laws to prevent industrial spying between competing companies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: espionage - the practice of spying, typically by governments to obtain political or military information

Etymology

From 'spy' (verb), from Old French espier (to observe), of Germanic origin.
