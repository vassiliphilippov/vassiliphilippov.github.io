sabayon

1. NOUN

/ sæˈbaɪ.ən / (AmE), / sæˈbaj.ɒ̃ / (BrE)
A light, foamy dessert made by whisking egg yolks, sugar, and wine (often Marsala) over gentle heat, served hot or chilled.
foamy custard-like dessert

Examples

The chef served a warm sabayon with fresh berries for dessert.
For a summer treat, try a chilled sabayon over poached peaches.
He delicately folded the fruit into the airy sabayon before plating it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zabaglione - An Italian dessert of whipped egg yolks, sugar, and wine, similar to sabayon

Etymology

From French sabayon, from Italian zabaglione (same meaning).
