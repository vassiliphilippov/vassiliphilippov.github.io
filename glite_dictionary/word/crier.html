crier

1. NOUN

/ ˈkraɪər / (AmE), / ˈkraɪə / (BrE)
(historically) an official employed to make public announcements, often in a town or court.
official who made public announcements
archaic

Examples

In medieval England, the crier would walk through the streets announcing the latest royal decrees.
The town crier rang his bell before reading the proclamation aloud in the market square.
Court proceedings began only after the crier declared the session open.

Etymology

From Old French *crier* (to cry, proclaim), from Latin *quiritare* (to cry out).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkraɪ.ər / (AmE), / ˈkraɪ.ə / (BrE)
a person who cries or weeps, especially often or easily
a person who cries or weeps

Examples

She was always a crier at sad movies, unable to hold back her tears.
As a baby, he was quite a crier and kept his parents up at night.
Despite being tough, he's actually a bit of a crier when it comes to farewells.

Related Terms

3. NOUN

/ ˈkraɪ.ər / (AmE), / ˈkraɪ.ə / (BrE)
a person, especially a peddler, who publicly shouts to advertise goods for sale
peddler who shouts to sell goods

Examples

The street crier called out the day's fresh produce loudly as he passed through the market.
Old city squares were once filled with criers advertising bread, fish, and other necessities.
As dawn broke, the crier began to hawk his wares to early shoppers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawker - a person who travels about selling goods, especially by shouting to attract customers

Etymology

From Old French 'crier' (to cry), from Latin 'quiritare' (to cry out).
