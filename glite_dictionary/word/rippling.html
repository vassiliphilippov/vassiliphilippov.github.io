rippling

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪplɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɪplɪŋ / (BrE)
A small wave or undulation on the surface of a liquid.
small wave on a liquid's surface

Examples

The gentle rippling of the stream soothed her nerves after a long day.
Light danced across the rippling of the lake at sunset.
Silence was broken only by the soft rippling of water against the boat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ripple - a small wave or series of waves on the surface of water

Etymology

From the verb 'ripple', which likely originates as a frequentative of 'rip' (to tear), influenced by 'ripple' meaning a small wave.
