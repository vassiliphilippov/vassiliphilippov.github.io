craton

1. NOUN

/ ˈkreɪ.tɑn / (AmE), / ˈkreɪ.tɒn / (BrE)
a large, stable block of the earth's crust forming the ancient core of a continent, usually dating from the Precambrian era
stable central mass of a continent

Examples

The Canadian Shield is one of the world's largest cratons and forms the ancient core of North America.
Many mineral deposits are often found at the margins of a craton.
Geologists study craton stability to understand continental evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shield - a large area of exposed Precambrian crystalline rocks within a craton

Etymology

From German 'Kraton', from Greek 'kratos' (strength, power).
