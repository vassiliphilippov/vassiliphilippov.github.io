emf

1. NOUN

/ iː ɛm ɛf / (AmE), / iː ɛm ɛf / (BrE)
the electrical potential or energy per unit charge supplied by a source in a circuit, measured in volts
electrical potential supplied by a source

Examples

The battery provides an emf of 12 volts to the circuit.
When the circuit is broken, no emf is produced by the generator.
He used a voltmeter to measure the emf across the terminals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voltage - electrical potential difference, often used interchangeably with emf, though technically distinct

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'electromotive force'.
