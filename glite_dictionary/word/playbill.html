playbill

1. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪ.bɪl / (AmE), / ˈpleɪ.bɪl / (BrE)
a printed program or notice for a theatrical performance, typically listing the cast and other details of the production
theatrical program or notice

Examples

She looked through the playbill to find out who was starring in the show.
The theater handed out a playbill to every guest at the entrance.
After the performance, he kept the playbill as a souvenir.

Synset Examples

he couldn't find her name on the playbill

Related Terms

  • Synonym: program - a schedule or listing of an event's proceedings
