buckshot

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌkˌʃɑt / (AmE), / ˈbʌkˌʃɒt / (BrE)
small lead pellets used as ammunition in shotgun shells, typically for hunting large game such as deer
small lead pellets for shotguns

Examples

The hunter loaded his shotgun with buckshot before heading into the woods.
Police found several shells of buckshot at the scene of the incident.
Using buckshot is common for controlling larger pests in rural areas.

Etymology

From 'buck' (male deer) + 'shot' (pellets fired from a gun), indicating its use for hunting deer.
