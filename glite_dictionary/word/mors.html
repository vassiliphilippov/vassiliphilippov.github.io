Mors

1. PROPN

/ mɔrs / (AmE), / mɔːs / (BrE)
In Roman mythology, the god of death, equivalent to the Greek Thanatos.
Roman god of death

Examples

In ancient Roman belief, Mors was the divine personification of death.
The poet referred to Mors to underscore the inevitability of mortality.
Some sculptures in Rome depict Mors as a winged figure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Thanatos - Greek god of death

Etymology

From Latin 'Mors' meaning 'death'.
