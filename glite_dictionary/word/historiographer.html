historiographer

1. NOUN

/ hɪˌstɔriˈɑɡrəfər / (AmE), / hɪˌstɒriˈɒɡrəfə / (BrE)
a person who studies, researches, and writes about history, often as an authority in the field; sometimes serving as an official chronicler of events.
an authority on history; a chronicler

Examples

The official historiographer documented every major event in the kingdom's history.
As a renowned historiographer, she published several works on ancient civilizations.
He was appointed court historiographer due to his expertise and meticulous research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: historian - a person who studies or writes about history

Etymology

From Late Latin historiographus, from Greek historiographos (history writer).
