Afrikaner

1. NOUN

/ ˌæfrɪˈkɑnər / (AmE), / ˌafrɪˈkɑːnə / (BrE)
A member of a South African ethnic group descended mainly from Dutch settlers, whose first language is Afrikaans.
South African of Dutch descent, Afrikaans speaker

Examples

Many Afrikaner farmers own land that has been passed down for generations.
She is an Afrikaner and speaks Afrikaans at home with her family.
During the 20th century, the Afrikaner community played a significant role in South African politics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Boer - A South African of Dutch descent, especially a farmer
  • Synonym: Afrikaans - The language spoken by Afrikaners

Etymology

From Afrikaans 'Afrikaner', meaning 'African (person)', derived from Dutch 'Afrikaander'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌæf.rɪˈkɑː.nər / (AmE), / ˌæf.rɪˈkɑː.nə / (BrE)
belonging or relating to white South Africans of Dutch descent or their language, Afrikaans
relating to South Africans of Dutch descent

Examples

She attended an Afrikaner cultural festival in Cape Town.
The Afrikaner community has a rich history in South Africa.
Several Afrikaner traditions are celebrated during the national holiday.

Synset Examples

an Afrikaans couple
Afrikaner support

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Afrikaans - the language spoken by Afrikaners
  • Synonym: Boer - a member of the Dutch and Huguenot population in South Africa

Etymology

From Afrikaans 'Afrikaner', meaning 'African', formed from 'Afrika' (Africa) + '-aner' (person of).
