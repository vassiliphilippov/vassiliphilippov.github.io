Moses Maimonides

1. PROPN

/ ˈmoʊzɪz maɪˈmɒnɪˌdiːz / (AmE), / ˈməʊzɪz maɪˈmɒnɪdiːz / (BrE)
(1135–1204) A medieval Spanish philosopher and Jewish scholar, regarded as the greatest Jewish thinker of the Middle Ages and known for codifying Jewish law and his influential writings on philosophy and religion.
medieval Jewish philosopher and scholar

Examples

Many scholars regard Moses Maimonides as one of the most influential Jewish philosophers of all time.
In his writings, Moses Maimonides sought to reconcile faith with reason and philosophy.
The works of Moses Maimonides continue to be studied in universities and religious institutions around the world.
