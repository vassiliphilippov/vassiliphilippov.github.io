Aegean

1. ADJ

/ əˈdʒiən / (AmE), / iˈdʒiːən / (BrE)
of or relating to the Aegean Sea or the region surrounding it, including its islands and coasts.
relating to the Aegean Sea or its region

Examples

The Aegean islands are popular tourist destinations in summer.
Many ancient civilizations flourished around the Aegean region.
She studied the unique pottery styles of the Aegean Bronze Age.

Synset Examples

Aegean islands

Etymology

From Greek Aigaíos (Αἰγαῖος), relating to the Aegean Sea.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03028538-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ əˈdʒiːən / (AmE), / ɪˈdʒiːən / (BrE)
the part of the Mediterranean Sea located between Greece and Turkey, known for its historical significance as a trade route for ancient civilizations
sea between Greece and Turkey

Examples

Many beautiful islands are scattered throughout the Aegean Sea.
Ancient traders frequently sailed across the Aegean to exchange goods and ideas.
The clear blue waters of the Aegean attract visitors from all over the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mediterranean - the sea connected to the Atlantic Ocean and surrounded by the Mediterranean region

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Aigeios (Αἰγαῖος), the mythological eponym of the sea.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09211506-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ɪˈdʒiən / (AmE), / iˈdʒiːən / (BrE)
relating to the prehistoric civilization of the region bordering the Aegean Sea, especially as it existed before classical Greek culture
relating to prehistoric Aegean civilization

Examples

Archaeologists have uncovered new Aegean artifacts on the island of Crete.
The Aegean culture greatly influenced later Greek art and mythology.
Many Aegean ruins date back to the second millennium BCE.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Minoan - relating to the Bronze Age civilization on Crete and other Aegean islands

Etymology

From the name of the Aegean Sea, ultimately from Greek Aigaion (Πέλαγος) meaning 'Aegean (Sea)'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03028672-a
Synset POS: a
