deprecatio

1. NOUN

/ dɛprɪˈkeɪʃioʊ / (AmE), / dɛprɪˈkeɪsɪəʊ / (BrE)
A prayer or plea asking for the removal or prevention of evil, often directed against oneself or others.
prayer for removal of evil
archaic

Examples

During the ritual, the priest recited a deprecatio to seek protection for the community.
In classical texts, a deprecatio expressed concern about impending disaster and appealed for its removal.
The senator referenced a traditional deprecatio in his speech against corruption.

Synset Examples

Cicero's defence of Milo is in essence a deprecatio.

Etymology

From Latin deprecatio (prayer for deliverance), from deprecari (to ward off by prayer).
