reprehensibly

1. ADV

/ ˌrɛprɪˈhɛnsəbli / (AmE), / ˌrɛprɪˈhɛnsəbli / (BrE)
In a way that deserves blame or strong criticism.
in a blameworthy manner

Examples

His actions were reprehensibly dishonest and shocked everyone in the community.
The politician behaved reprehensibly by spreading misinformation to gain votes.
She treated her employees reprehensibly, showing no regard for their well-being.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blameworthily - in a manner deserving blame
  • Antonym: commendably - in a manner deserving praise or approval

Etymology

From Latin 'reprehensibilis' (deserving censure), via 'reprehendere' (to blame).
