almanac

1. NOUN

/ ˈælmənˌæk / (AmE), / ˈælmənæk / (BrE)
an annual publication containing a calendar, weather forecasts, astronomical information, and other data for the year
annual calendar and information publication

Examples

Sarah checked the almanac for this year's weather predictions before planning her garden.
Farmers have relied on the almanac for generations to guide their planting seasons.
The almanac included lunar phases as well as important national holidays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calendar - a chart or series of pages showing the days, weeks, and months of a particular year

Etymology

From late Middle English, via Old French from medieval Latin almanach.

2. NOUN

/ ˈælməˌnæk / (AmE), / ˈælmənæk / (BrE)
an annual publication containing a calendar, statistical data, and various information, often relating to a specific subject or general interest
annual reference book or calendar

Examples

I use an almanac every year to check sunrise and sunset times.
The farmer consulted the almanac before planting the crops.
That old almanac contains weather predictions and astronomical data for each month.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yearbook - an annual publication with information or reports about the previous year

Etymology

From Middle English 'almanak', probably from Medieval Latin 'almanachus', of uncertain origin, perhaps from Arabic.
