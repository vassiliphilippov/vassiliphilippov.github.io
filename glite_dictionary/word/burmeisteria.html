2. NOUN

/ bɜrˌmaɪˈstɪəriə / (AmE), / bɜːˌmaɪˈstɪəriə / (BrE)
A genus of armadillos in the family Dasypodidae, including certain armored mammals native to South America.
a genus of armadillos

Examples

The genus Burmeisteria is distinguished by its unique skeletal structure among armadillos.
Scientists discovered a fossil belonging to Burmeisteria in South America.
Species within the genus Burmeisteria are known for their distinctive carapace patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dasypodidae - the family of mammals including armadillos

Etymology

Named after German zoologist Hermann Burmeister (1807–1892).
