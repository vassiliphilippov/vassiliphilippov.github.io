bisque

1. NOUN

/ bɪsk / (AmE), / bɪsk / (BrE)
A rich, creamy soup made with shellfish, typically blended until smooth.
creamy shellfish soup

Examples

The restaurant’s lobster bisque was rich and full of flavor.
She served a creamy shrimp bisque as the first course at dinner.
Crab bisque is a favorite starter at many French bistros.

Etymology

From French 'bisque', of uncertain origin, but possibly related to Biscay (as in Bay of Biscay) or the Italian 'biscotto' (twice cooked), referring to the soup being cooked twice.
