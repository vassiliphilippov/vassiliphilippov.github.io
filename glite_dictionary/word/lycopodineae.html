Lycopodineae

1. NOUN

/ laɪ.kəˌpɑː.dɪˈniː.i / (AmE), / laɪ.kəˌpəʊ.dɪˈniː.iː / (BrE)
an alternative name for the class Lycopsida, a group of vascular plants that includes clubmosses and their relatives
alternative name for the class Lycopsida (plants)

Examples

The Lycopodineae are among the oldest groups of vascular plants on Earth.
Recent studies have improved our understanding of Lycopodineae evolution and classification.
Many extinct fossil plants are assigned to the Lycopodineae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lycopsida - a class of vascular plants including clubmosses

Etymology

From New Latin, based on 'Lycopodium' (the genus of clubmosses) and the botanical suffix '-ineae'.
