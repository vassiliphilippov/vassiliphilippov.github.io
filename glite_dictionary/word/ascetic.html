ascetic

1. ADJ

/ əˈsɛtɪk / (AmE), / əˈsɛtɪk / (BrE)
characterized by severe self-discipline and the avoidance of all forms of indulgence, typically for religious or spiritual reasons.
practicing severe self-denial

Examples

He led an ascetic lifestyle, refusing all luxuries and comforts.
The monk's ascetic habits astonished his visitors.
In pursuit of enlightenment, she adopted an ascetic diet and daily routine.

Synset Examples

Be systematically ascetic … do … something for no other reason than that you would rather not do it
a desert nomad's austere life
a spartan diet
a spartan existence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: austere - severe or strict in manner, attitude, or appearance
  • Synonym: spartan - showing or characterized by austerity or a lack of comfort or luxury

Etymology

From Greek askētikos (practicing rigorous self-discipline), from askētēs (monk, hermit).

/ əˈsɛtɪk / (AmE), / əˈsɛtɪk / (BrE)

Examples

3. NOUN

/ əˈsɛtɪk / (AmE), / əˈsɛtɪk / (BrE)
a person who practices severe self-discipline and abstention from all forms of indulgence, typically for religious or spiritual reasons
a person practicing self-denial for spiritual discipline

Examples

The ascetic lived alone in the mountains, dedicating his life to meditation and prayer.
Some monks embrace an ascetic existence, rejecting worldly comforts.
Her ascetic lifestyle was marked by strict fasting and silence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hermit - a person living in solitude as a religious discipline

Etymology

From Greek askētikos (practicing self-discipline), from askētēs (monk, hermit).
