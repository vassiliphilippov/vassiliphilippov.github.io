Pleurobrachiidae

1. NOUN

/ plurəˌbrækiɪˌdiː / (AmE), / pljʊərəˌbrækiɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine animals within the phylum Ctenophora, commonly known as sea gooseberries, distinguished by their transparent, round bodies and rows of ciliary combs.
family of marine animals (sea gooseberries)
medical

Examples

Researchers collected several specimens from the Pleurobrachiidae family during the expedition.
The transparent bodies of Pleurobrachiidae help them blend into the ocean environment.
Members of the Pleurobrachiidae are often called sea gooseberries because of their distinctive shape.

Etymology

From the genus name Pleurobrachia, from Greek 'pleura' (side) and 'brachion' (arm), plus Latin '-idae' (family suffix).
