Fratercula corniculata

1. NOUN

/ frəˈtɛrkjʊlə kɔrnɪˈkjuleɪtə / (AmE), / frəˈtɜːkjʊlə kɔːnɪˈkjʊːleɪtə / (BrE)
A species of puffin found in the northern Pacific, known as the horned puffin, notable for the fleshy black "horns" above its eyes.
a puffin species of the northern Pacific

Examples

The Fratercula corniculata can be identified by the distinctive fleshy "horns" above its eyes.
During the summer months, many Fratercula corniculata nest on rocky cliffs along the Alaskan coast.
Researchers observed the feeding habits of Fratercula corniculata in the northern Pacific islands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horned puffin - puffin species with "horns" above its eyes, same as Fratercula corniculata

Etymology

From Latin: 'fratercula' meaning 'little brother' (the genus name for puffins), 'corniculata' meaning 'horned'.
