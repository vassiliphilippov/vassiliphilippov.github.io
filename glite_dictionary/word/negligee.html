negligee

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɛlɪˈʒeɪ / (AmE), / ˈnɛlɪʒeɪ / (BrE)
a woman's light, loose-fitting dressing gown, typically made of a soft, flowing fabric and worn as nightwear or loungewear
a woman's loose nightdress or gown

Examples

She wore a sheer negligee that flowed elegantly around her as she walked through the room.
He bought his wife a silk negligee for their anniversary, wrapped in a gift box.
The actress appeared on stage in a vintage pink negligee during the play's second act.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nightgown - a loose garment worn for sleeping by women or girls
  • Synonym: dressing gown - a long, loose robe worn over nightwear or clothes at home

Etymology

From French 'négligé', meaning 'negligent' or 'casual', originally referring to informal dress.
