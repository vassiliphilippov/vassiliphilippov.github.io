impecuniousness

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪm.pɪˈkjuː.ni.əs.nɪs / (AmE), / ˌɪm.pɪˈkjuː.ni.əs.nɪs / (BrE)
the state of having little or no money; poverty or pennilessness
state of lacking money

Examples

His impecuniousness forced him to live in a small, shared apartment during college.
Despite her impecuniousness, she never lost hope of a brighter future.
The charity was established to support those struggling with impecuniousness in the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poverty - the state of being extremely poor
  • Antonym: wealth - an abundance of valuable possessions or money

Etymology

From Latin 'impecuniosus' (without money), from 'im-' (not) + 'pecunia' (money).
