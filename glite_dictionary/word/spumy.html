spumy

1. ADJ

/ ˈspjuːmi / (AmE), / ˈspjuːmi / (BrE)
full of or producing foam or froth; emitting or covered with bubbles, as from carbonation or agitation
full of foam or bubbles

Examples

The waves crashed against the rocks, sending spumy spray into the air.
He poured the beer quickly, resulting in a spumy head overflowing the glass.
The spumy surface of the river glistened in the afternoon sunlight.

Synset Examples

bubbling champagne
foamy (or frothy) beer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foamy - full of or producing foam
  • Synonym: frothy - covered with or resembling froth or bubbles

Etymology

From the noun 'spume' (foam), from Latin 'spuma' (foam) with the suffix -y forming an adjective.
