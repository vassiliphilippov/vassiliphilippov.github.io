softish

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɔftɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈsɒftɪʃ / (BrE)
Somewhat soft; moderately soft in texture, feel, or quality.
somewhat soft

Examples

The fabric on this chair is a little softish, so it's comfortable but not too plush.
He prefers his fruit slightly softish for eating rather than very firm or mushy.
The ground was softish after last night's rain, making it easy to dig.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: soft - having a smooth or gentle texture; not hard
  • Antonym: hard - firm and solid; not soft

Etymology

Formed from 'soft' with the suffix '-ish' meaning 'somewhat' or 'approximately'.
