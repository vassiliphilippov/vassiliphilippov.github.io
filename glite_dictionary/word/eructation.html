eructation

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌrʌkˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌrʌkˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of a volcano emitting fumes, lava, or other material during an eruption.
volcanic emission of fumes or lava

Examples

The sudden eructation of ash and lava from the volcano startled the nearby villagers.
Scientists monitored the eructation to assess the scale of volcanic activity.
Each eructation deposited new layers of rock across the surrounding landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eruption - an act or instance of erupting, especially of a volcano

Etymology

From Latin eructatio (a belching, eruption), from eructare (to belch out).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˌrʌkˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌrʌkˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of noisily expelling gas from the stomach through the mouth; a burp or belch
expelling gas from the stomach by mouth
medical

Examples

After drinking soda quickly, John experienced a loud eructation that startled the people around him.
The doctor explained that frequent eructation can be a symptom of digestive disorders.
She apologized to the table for her accidental eructation during dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: belch - to expel gas noisily from the stomach through the mouth
  • Synonym: burp - to let out gas from the stomach through the mouth

Etymology

From Latin eructatio (a belching), from eructare (to belch out).
