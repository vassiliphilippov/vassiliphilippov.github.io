to dynamize

1. VERB

/ ˈdaɪnəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈdʌɪnəmʌɪz / (BrE)
to make something or someone more energetic, forceful, or dynamic
make more dynamic or energetic

Examples

The new manager hoped to dynamize the sales team with a fresh approach.
Modern dance routines are meant to dynamize traditional forms with unexpected movements.
She was dynamized by her desire to go to grad school.

Synset Examples

She was dynamized by her desire to go to grad school

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to energize - to give energy to; invigorate

Etymology

From Greek dynamis (power, force) via French dynamiser.

2. VERB

/ ˈdaɪnəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈdʌɪnəˌmʌɪz / (BrE)
(especially in homeopathy) to make a drug or substance effective, often by a process believed to activate its properties.
make (a drug) effective (homeopathy)
medical

Examples

Some practitioners claim that succussing a solution will dynamize the medicine, making it more potent.
The controversial remedy was said to be dynamized through repeated dilution and shaking.
Supporters of homeopathy believe you must dynamize drugs to unlock their healing powers.

Synset Examples

dynamized medicine

Etymology

From Greek dynamis (power) + -ize.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00552795-v
Synset POS: v
