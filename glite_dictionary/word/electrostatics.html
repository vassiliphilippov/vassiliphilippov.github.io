electrostatics

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɛktroʊˈstætɪks / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊˈstætɪks / (BrE)
the branch of physics concerned with the study of electric charges at rest and the forces between them
physics of static electricity

Examples

She studied electrostatics to understand how charged particles interact when at rest.
The course covered both electrostatics and electrodynamics.
Many practical applications in electronics are based on principles of electrostatics.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: electrodynamics - the branch of physics that studies moving electric charges

Etymology

From electro- (relating to electricity) + statics (study of forces in equilibrium), from Greek 'statikos' (causing to stand).
