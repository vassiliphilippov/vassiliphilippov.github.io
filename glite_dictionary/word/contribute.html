to contribute

1. VERB

/ kənˈtrɪbjut / (AmE), / kənˈtrɪbjuːt / (BrE)
to help to cause or bring about a particular result; to be one of the factors leading to something
help to cause a particular result

Examples

Regular exercise can contribute to improved mental health.
Poor communication often contributes to misunderstandings in the workplace.
Her hard work contributed to the team's overall success.

Synset Examples

The use of computers in the classroom led to better writing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lead to - to result in, to cause as a consequence
  • Antonym: hinder - to make it difficult for something to happen

Etymology

From Latin contribuere (to bring together, to add).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02561616-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kənˈtrɪbjuːt / (AmE), / kənˈtrɪbjuːt / (BrE)
to give money, help, ideas, etc. to a common fund or for a particular purpose; to help cause something to happen
give or help towards a cause

Examples

Many employees contribute a small amount to the company charity fund each month.
Scientists from different countries contribute to this important global research.
Your ideas contribute greatly to the success of the project.

Synset Examples

I gave at the office

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to donate - to give money or goods to help a person or organization
  • Antonym: to withhold - to refuse to give something that is expected or needed

Etymology

From Latin 'contributus', past participle of 'contribuere' (to bring together, add).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02313721-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ kənˈtrɪbjut / (AmE), / kənˈtrɪbjuːt / (BrE)
to add a particular quality or feature to something or help make it successful, interesting, or distinctive
add a particular quality or feature

Examples

Her cheerful attitude contributes a positive energy to the office environment.
Unique artwork contributes character and charm to the living room.
The singer’s voice contributes an emotional depth to the performance.

Synset Examples

Her presence lends a certain cachet to the company
The music added a lot to the play
She brings a special atmosphere to our meetings
This adds a light note to the program

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to add - to put together with something else so as to increase the number, size, or importance
  • Antonym: to detract - to reduce or take away the worth or value of something

Etymology

From Latin contribuere (to bring together, add).

4. VERB

/ kənˈtrɪbjut / (AmE), / kənˈtrɪbjuːt / (BrE)
to give or provide something, especially in order to help achieve or produce something as part of a group effort
to give or provide for a common purpose

Examples

Each member is expected to contribute a small amount to the project.
Local businesses contributed generously to the festival's success.
They are contributing their time and expertise to help the community.

Synset Examples

The city has to put up half the required amount

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to donate - to give something, especially to a good cause
  • Antonym: to withdraw - to take something back or remove it

Etymology

From Latin 'contributus', past participle of 'contribuere' (to bring together, add).
