trustingness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrʌstɪŋnəs / (AmE), / ˈtrʌstɪŋnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being willing to believe that other people are honest and reliable
willingness to trust others

Examples

Her trustingness made her an easy target for con artists.
The child's trustingness was evident in the way he greeted strangers with a smile.
Sometimes trustingness is seen as a virtue, but it can also make people vulnerable.

Synset Examples

the experience destroyed his trust and personal dignity

Related Terms

  • Antonym: suspiciousness - the quality of being inclined to distrust others
  • Degree Higher: trust - firm belief in the reliability, truth, or ability of someone or something

Etymology

Formed from 'trusting' + '-ness', denoting the state or quality of being trusting.
