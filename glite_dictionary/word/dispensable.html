dispensable

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈspɛnsəbl̩ / (AmE), / dɪˈspensəbl̩ / (BrE)
not necessary; able to be done without or omitted without serious consequence.
not necessary; able to be dispensed with

Examples

Many companies consider paper records dispensable in the digital age.
Volunteers helped with tasks that were important but dispensable if time ran short.
He packed only essentials, leaving all dispensable items behind.

Synset Examples

dispensable items of personal property

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indispensable - absolutely necessary; essential

Etymology

From Medieval Latin dispensabilis (that may be dispensed with), from Latin dispensare (to distribute, dispense).
