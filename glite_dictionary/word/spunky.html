spunky

1. ADJ

/ ˈspʌŋki / (AmE), / ˈspʌŋki / (BrE)
full of courage and determination; spirited and brave.
courageous and spirited
informal

Examples

Despite her small size, the spunky puppy wasn't afraid to bark at the bigger dogs.
She gave a spunky reply to the unfair question, impressing everyone in the room.
The spunky young woman stood up for what she believed in, even when it wasn't popular.

Synset Examples

the champion is faced with a feisty challenger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brave - showing courage and fearlessness
  • Antonym: timid - lacking courage or confidence; easily frightened

Etymology

From 'spunk' (courage, spirit), possibly from Scottish Gaelic 'spong' (tinder, touchwood).

2. ADJ

/ ˈspʌŋki / (AmE), / ˈspʌŋki / (BrE)
Courageous and full of spirit; willing to face danger.
courageous; full of spirit

Examples

Her spunky attitude inspired everyone on the team to do their best.
The spunky little dog barked at the much larger intruder, showing no fear.
Despite the challenge, he gave a spunky reply and refused to back down.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courageous - having or showing courage
  • Antonym: timid - lacking in courage or confidence

Etymology

From 'spunk' (courage, spirit) + '-y', originally Scots dialect, 18th century.
