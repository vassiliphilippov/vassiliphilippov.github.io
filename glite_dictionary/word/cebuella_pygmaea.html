Cebuella pygmaea

1. NOUN

/ sɛˈbjuː.ələ pɪɡˈmiːə / (AmE), / sɛˈbjuː.ələ pɪɡˈmiːə / (BrE)
A species of very small monkey, known as the pygmy marmoset, native to the tropical forests of the Amazon basin.
smallest monkey; Amazon rainforest species

Examples

The Cebuella pygmaea is admired for its agility in the dense Amazon rainforest canopy.
Many primatologists study the social behavior of Cebuella pygmaea in its natural habitat.
A Cebuella pygmaea can weigh as little as 100 grams, making it the smallest monkey in the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pygmy marmoset - common name for Cebuella pygmaea

Etymology

From Latin: Cebuella (genus name) + pygmaea, feminine of 'pygmaeus' meaning 'dwarfish' or 'pygmy'.
