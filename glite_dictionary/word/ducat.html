ducat

1. NOUN

/ ˈduːkət / (AmE), / ˈdjuːkət / (BrE)
a gold coin formerly used in various European countries, especially during the Middle Ages and Renaissance
former European gold coin
archaic

Examples

Many wealthy merchants paid for their goods using ducats rather than local currency.
In Shakespeare's plays, a ducat is often mentioned as a standard of wealth.
He inherited a chest full of old ducats from his ancestors.

Etymology

From Old Italian ducat(o), from Medieval Latin ducatum, originally meaning 'duke's coin'.
