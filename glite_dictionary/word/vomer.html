vomer

1. NOUN

/ ˈvoʊmər / (AmE), / ˈvəʊmə / (BrE)
a thin, flat bone forming the posterior and lower part of the nasal septum in the skull
bone forming part of the nasal septum
medical

Examples

A fracture to the vomer can adversely affect the structure of the nasal septum.
The surgeon carefully avoided the vomer while repairing the nasal cavity.
In human anatomy, the vomer is essential for dividing the left and right nasal passages.

Etymology

From Latin 'vomer' meaning 'ploughshare', due to its shape.
