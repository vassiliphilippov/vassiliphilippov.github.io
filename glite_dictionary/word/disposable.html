disposable

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈspoʊzəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈspəʊzəbl / (BrE)
(of resources or assets) available to be used as needed or at one's disposal.
free or available for use

Examples

The company allocated all its disposable funds to the emergency relief effort.
She calculated her disposable income before making travel plans.
Military leaders discussed how many disposable units were available for immediate deployment.

Synset Examples

every disposable piece of equipment was sent to the fire
disposable assets

Etymology

From dispose + -able, in the sense 'able to be disposed (of or used)'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00780977-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈspoʊzəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈspəʊzəbəl / (BrE)
intended to be used once or for a short time and then thrown away
intended for single or short-term use

Examples

Please remember to put your disposable gloves in the bin after use.
She prefers using disposable contact lenses when she travels.
The hotel provided disposable razors for its guests.

Synset Examples

disposable paper cups

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reusable - able to be used more than once

Etymology

From 'dispose' (to get rid of) + '-able' (capable of).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00780129-a
Synset POS: a

3. NOUN

/ dɪˈspoʊzəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈspəʊzəbəl / (BrE)
an item designed to be used once or only a few times before being thrown away
item designed for single use

Examples

More people are switching from plastic disposables to reusable products.
Single-use disposables create a significant environmental burden if not recycled properly.
At the party, we used paper disposables so that cleaning up would be easy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: throwaway - something intended to be discarded after use

Etymology

Formed from 'dispose' + '-able', first used in the late 19th century; as a noun in 20th century commercial contexts.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03216724-n
Synset POS: n
