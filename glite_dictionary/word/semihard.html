semihard

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛmiˌhɑrd / (AmE), / ˈsɛmiˌhɑːd / (BrE)
partly but not completely hard; having a consistency between soft and hard, often used to describe certain types of cheese or materials.
partly but not completely hard

Examples

The cheesemonger recommended a semihard cheese for the tasting platter.
This plastic is classified as semihard, making it flexible yet sturdy.
They prefer mattresses that are semihard rather than very firm or too soft.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: soft - not hard; easy to compress, shape, or cut
  • Degree Higher: hard - solid, firm, and not easily broken or bent

Etymology

From semi- (Latin, meaning 'half' or 'partially') + hard.
