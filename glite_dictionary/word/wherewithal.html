wherewithal

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛr.wɪˌθɔl / (AmE), / ˈwɛə.wɪˌθɔːl / (BrE)
the money, resources, or means needed to do something, especially financially
means or resources (especially money) needed for something

Examples

She didn't have the wherewithal to pay for the repairs after her car broke down.
The company lacked the wherewithal to expand into international markets.
If you have the wherewithal, investing in real estate can be quite profitable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: means - resources or money needed to achieve something

Etymology

From Middle English, meaning 'with which'; formed from 'where' + 'withal'.
