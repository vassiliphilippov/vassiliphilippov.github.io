Eden

1. NOUN

/ ˈiː.dən / (AmE), / ˈiː.dən / (BrE)
a place or state of perfect happiness, bliss, and peace; often used metaphorically for an ideal paradise
an ideal paradise; place of perfect happiness

Examples

After years of hard work, she finally found her own Eden in a peaceful mountain cabin.
The island was a hidden Eden, untouched by modern civilization and filled with natural beauty.
He described their summer together as an Eden he wished would never end.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paradise - a place or state of perfect happiness; heaven
  • Antonym: hell - a place or state of misery, torment, or wickedness

Etymology

From the biblical Garden of Eden in Hebrew *ʿēḏen* (delight, pleasure).

2. PROPN

/ ˈiːdən / (AmE), / ˈiːdən / (BrE)
In the Bible, the garden where Adam and Eve lived before being expelled after the Fall; a place of great natural beauty and innocence.
the biblical garden of paradise

Examples

She imagined her grandmother’s cottage was an Eden far from the troubles of the world.
The poet described the untouched valley as a modern Eden.
For some, the remote island became their own private Eden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paradise - an ideal or perfect place, especially as in religious belief

Etymology

From Hebrew ‘ēḏen meaning 'delight' or 'pleasure'.

Synset POS: n
