muscularity

1. NOUN

/ ˌmʌskjəˈlærəti / (AmE), / ˌmʌskjʊˈlærəti / (BrE)
The quality or state of having well-developed muscles or muscular strength.
well-developed muscles; muscular strength

Examples

The muscularity of the athlete was apparent during the competition.
Many fitness enthusiasts strive to increase their muscularity through strength training.
His impressive muscularity set him apart from other bodybuilders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muscular - having well-developed muscles
  • Antonym: frailty - lack of physical strength or weakness

Etymology

From Latin 'muscularis' (muscular) + '-ity' (quality or state).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmʌskjəˈlærəti / (AmE), / ˌmʌskjʊˈlærəti / (BrE)
the quality or state of having well-developed muscles or a muscular physique
the quality of being muscular

Examples

The bodybuilder's impressive muscularity was evident during the competition.
Regular resistance training can significantly improve muscularity and physical strength.
Genetic factors can influence a person's natural muscularity and body shape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muscular - having well-developed muscles or strong muscles
  • Antonym: leanness - the quality of having little fat on the body

Etymology

From Latin 'muscularis' (of muscle) + English '-ity'.

3. NOUN

/ ˌmʌskjəˈlærəti / (AmE), / ˌmʌskjʊˈlærəti / (BrE)
a style, especially of writing, that is marked by energy, force, or vitality
energy or forcefulness in style

Examples

Her novel is admired for the muscularity of its prose, full of vivid descriptions and bold metaphors.
The critic praised the muscularity of his arguments in the essay, noting their clarity and vigor.
Some readers prefer a lighter touch, but others enjoy the sheer muscularity of his style.

Synset Examples

his writing conveys great energy
a remarkable muscularity of style

Related Terms

  • Synonym: energy - liveliness or vigor in style or writing
  • Synonym: forcefulness - quality of being strong and vigorous, especially in literary style
  • Antonym: delicacy - quality of being gentle or subtle in writing or art; opposite of muscularity

Etymology

From Latin 'muscularis' (muscular), referring figuratively to vigor or strength in style.
