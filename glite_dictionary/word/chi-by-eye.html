to chi-by-eye

1. VERB

/ ˌtʃaɪ.baɪ.ˈaɪ / (AmE), / ˌkaɪ.baɪ.ˈaɪ / (BrE)
to estimate a value, such as distance, weight, or size, by visual judgment rather than by measurement or calculation
estimate by visual judgment
informal slang

Examples

The technician decided to chi-by-eye the length rather than measure it with a tape.
Sometimes, you just have to chi-by-eye things to keep the job moving.
I usually chi-by-eye the weight of the package when shipping.

Synset Examples

You can chi-by-eye the distance if you're experienced.
She prefers to chi-by-eye rather than use a scale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eyeball - to estimate something visually, especially roughly
  • Synonym: guess - to estimate or form an opinion without sufficient information

Etymology

Originated as engineering slang, from ‘chi’ as an approximation for a visual guess ('by eye').
