Fasciolopsis

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæʃiəˈlɑpsɪs / (AmE), / ˌfasɪəˈlɒpsɪs / (BrE)
A genus of parasitic flatworms (trematodes) in the family Fasciolidae, including the species Fasciolopsis buski, which infects the intestines of mammals.
genus of parasitic flatworms
medical

Examples

The genus Fasciolopsis includes significant intestinal parasites found in Asia.
Researchers are studying the life cycle of Fasciolopsis to understand how to prevent infections.
The diagnosis revealed an infection caused by Fasciolopsis buski in the patient.

Etymology

From Modern Latin, based on Latin 'fascia' (band, strip) and Greek 'opsis' (appearance).

2. NOUN

/ fæsiəˈlɑpsɪs / (AmE), / fæsiəˈlɒpsɪs / (BrE)
A genus of parasitic flatworms (trematodes) in the family Fasciolidae, which includes Fasciolopsis buski, a significant intestinal parasite in humans and pigs.
genus of parasitic flatworms
medical

Examples

The life cycle of Fasciolopsis involves both snails and mammals as hosts.
Fasciolopsis infections are most common in areas with poor sanitation.
Scientists studied the characteristics of Fasciolopsis to develop effective treatments.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'fasciola' (small band or stripe) and Greek 'opsis' (appearance).
