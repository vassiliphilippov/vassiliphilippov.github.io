Prenanthes serpentaria

1. NOUN

/ priˈnænθiːz ˌsɝːpənˈtɛriə / (AmE), / prɪˈnanθiːz ˌsɜːpənˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
A perennial herb native to the southern and eastern United States, with drooping clusters of pinkish flowers and thick basal leaves shaped like a lion's foot.
a perennial herb with drooping pinkish flowers and thick basal leaves

Examples

Prenanthes serpentaria grows abundantly in shaded woodlands throughout the southeastern United States.
The thick, lobed leaves of Prenanthes serpentaria are said to resemble a lion’s paw.
Gardeners appreciate Prenanthes serpentaria for its delicate, drooping clusters of pinkish flowers in late summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lion's foot - another common name for Prenanthes serpentaria

Etymology

Prenanthes from Greek prēnēs (drooping) and anthos (flower); 'serpentaria' refers to a supposed resemblance to a serpent or medicinal use.
