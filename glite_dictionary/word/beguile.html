to beguile

1. VERB

/ bɪˈɡaɪl / (AmE), / bɪˈɡaɪl / (BrE)
to attract or enchant someone, often in a way that is deceptive or charming
to attract or enchant, often deceptively

Examples

Her gentle voice beguiled everyone who heard her into following her advice.
He was beguiled by her wit and beauty, unable to look away.
The storyteller beguiled the children with tales of faraway lands.

Synset Examples

She captured all the men's hearts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charm - to delight or attract someone greatly
  • Synonym: to enchant - to attract and delight someone completely
  • Antonym: to repel - to drive away or be distasteful to someone

Etymology

From Middle English 'beguile', from be- + Old French 'guile' (deceit).

2. VERB

/ bɪˈɡaɪl / (AmE), / bɪˈɡaɪl / (BrE)
to influence someone by slyness, charm, or trickery; to deceive or mislead in an enticing way
to deceive or charm cunningly

Examples

He tried to beguile her with compliments and captivating stories.
The magician beguiles his audience with clever tricks and illusions.
She was beguiled by the con artist's charming manner and lost all her savings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deceive - to make someone believe something that is not true
  • Synonym: to mislead - to cause someone to have a wrong idea or impression
  • Antonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and sincere

Etymology

From Middle English *beguile* (deceive, delude), from be- + guile (trickery, deceit).
