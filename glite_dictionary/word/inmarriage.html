inmarriage

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌmærɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌmarɪdʒ / (BrE)
the custom or practice of marrying within one's own tribe, social group, or community, often as required by law or tradition
marriage within one's social group or tribe

Examples

The tribe strictly enforced inmarriage to preserve its cultural traditions.
Laws requiring inmarriage have shaped the community's social structure for centuries.
Debates about inmarriage versus exogamy are common in anthropology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endogamy - the custom of marrying only within the limits of a local community, clan, or tribe
  • Antonym: exogamy - the custom of marrying outside one's social group, clan, or tribe

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'in-' (within) + 'marriage'.
