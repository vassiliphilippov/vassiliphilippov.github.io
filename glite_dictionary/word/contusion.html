contusion

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtuːʒən / (AmE), / kənˈtjuːʒən / (BrE)
An injury to the tissue beneath the skin that does not break the surface but causes discoloration, typically known as a bruise.
injury causing skin discoloration without breaking skin
medical

Examples

After falling off his bike, he developed a large contusion on his thigh.
The doctor examined the patient's leg and confirmed it was a contusion rather than a fracture.
Although the skin was not broken, a deep contusion caused significant pain and swelling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bruise - an injury that appears as a purple mark on the skin without breaking it

Etymology

From Latin 'contusio' meaning 'a bruising', from 'contundere' (to beat, bruise).

2. NOUN

/ kənˈtuːʒən / (AmE), / kənˈtjuːʒən / (BrE)
An injury in which the skin is not broken, but blood vessels under the skin are damaged, causing a bruise.
medical term for a bruise
medical

Examples

The doctor examined the contusion on her arm to check for any underlying fractures.
After falling off his bike, he developed a large contusion on his thigh.
The autopsy revealed several minor contusions but no broken bones.

Synset Examples

the bruise resulted from a contusion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bruise - an injury appearing as an area of discolored skin on the body, caused by a blow or impact

Etymology

From Latin 'contusio', from 'contundere' (to bruise).
