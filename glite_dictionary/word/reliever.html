reliever

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈlivər / (AmE), / rɪˈliːvə / (BrE)
a person who takes over the duties or role of another, especially in demanding or hazardous situations
person who takes over for another

Examples

During the night shift, a reliever arrives to take over when workers need a break.
The pitcher called a reliever to finish the game in the final innings.
In emergencies, a trained reliever is always ready to step in for the main operator.

Synset Examples

the star had a stand-in for dangerous scenes
we need extra employees for summer fill-ins

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substitute - a person who replaces another
  • Synonym: stand-in - a person who temporarily takes someone else's place

Etymology

From relieve + -er, meaning one who relieves.

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈliːvər / (AmE), / rɪˈliːvə / (BrE)
a person who lessens the severity of something, especially by calming fears or anxiety
person who calms or lessens anxiety

Examples

The therapist proved to be a true reliever of the patient's overwhelming anxiety.
She has always been a reliever in stressful situations, helping her friends stay calm.
As a reliever of fear, the counselor reassured everyone during the emergency.

Synset Examples

a reliever of anxiety
an allayer of fears

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alleviator - a person who makes something less severe
  • Synonym: allayer - someone who calms or lessens strong feelings or fears

Etymology

From 'relieve' + '-er', meaning one who relieves.

3. NOUN

/ rɪˈlivɚ / (AmE), / rɪˈliːvə / (BrE)
(Baseball) A pitcher who enters the game after the starting pitcher, usually to replace them and continue pitching.
baseball pitcher who does not start the game

Examples

The team brought in a reliever after the starter gave up five runs.
As a rookie, she quickly established herself as a dependable reliever for the club.
Managers often rely on a reliever to get the team out of difficult situations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: starter - the pitcher who begins the baseball game for a team
  • Degree Higher: closer - a relief pitcher designated to pitch in the final innings

Etymology

From 'relieve' + '-er', indicating one who takes over or replaces another.
