pagan

1. ADJ

/ ˈpeɪɡən / (AmE), / ˈpeɪɡən / (BrE)
(of a person, beliefs, or practices) not acknowledging the God of Christianity, Judaism, or Islam; relating to religious beliefs other than the main world religions, especially before the spread of monotheism.
not acknowledging the God of main monotheistic religions

Examples

The ancient Greeks followed pagan religions before the advent of Christianity.
She was accused of practicing pagan rituals during the festival.
Some holidays have roots in pagan traditions predating Christianity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heathen - a person who does not belong to a widely held religion, especially one who is not a Christian, Jew, or Muslim
  • Antonym: monotheistic - believing in a single god, especially the God of Christianity, Judaism, or Islam

Etymology

From Latin paganus (country dweller, civilian), later used for non-Christian.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪ.ɡən / (AmE), / ˈpeɪ.ɡən / (BrE)
A person who follows a polytheistic or ancient religion, especially one other than Christianity, Judaism, or Islam.
follower of a polytheistic or ancient religion

Examples

In ancient Rome, most citizens were considered pagans by later Christians.
The festival had its origins in pagan traditions celebrating the changing seasons.
She studied the beliefs of various pagan cultures for her thesis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heathen - person who does not belong to a widely held religion, especially Christianity, Judaism, or Islam

Etymology

From Latin 'paganus' (villager, rustic, civilian, later non-Christian).

3. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪɡən / (AmE), / ˈpeɪɡən / (BrE)
a person who follows a polytheistic or non-mainstream religion, especially one other than Christianity, Judaism, or Islam
follower of a polytheistic or pagan religion

Examples

The village was once home to many pagans who worshipped nature gods.
During the festival, people dressed in traditional pagan costumes to honor the old deities.
Some ancient Roman practices were considered pagan by early Christian leaders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heathen - a person who does not belong to a widely held religion; nonbeliever

Etymology

From Latin paganus (country dweller, later 'heathen')
