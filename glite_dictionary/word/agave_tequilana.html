Agave tequilana

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡɑːveɪ tɛˌkiːˈlɑːnə / (AmE), / əˈɡɑːvi tɛˌkiːˈlɑːnə / (BrE)
A species of agave native to Mexico, cultivated primarily for the production of tequila.
Mexican plant used to make tequila

Examples

The blue Agave tequilana is an essential crop in the Mexican state of Jalisco.
Tequila can only be produced from the Agave tequilana species by law.
Farmers carefully harvest Agave tequilana plants once they reach maturity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tequila - A distilled alcoholic beverage made from Agave tequilana

Etymology

From Latin 'Agave', a genus name, and 'tequilana', referring to Tequila, a region in Mexico.
