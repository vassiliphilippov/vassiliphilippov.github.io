Schistosoma

1. NOUN

/ skɪs.təˈsoʊ.mə / (AmE), / skɪs.təˈsəʊ.mə / (BrE)
A genus of parasitic flatworms, known as blood flukes, that cause schistosomiasis in humans and animals.
genus of parasitic blood flukes
medical

Examples

Schistosoma species are responsible for millions of cases of schistosomiasis worldwide.
Researchers study the life cycle of Schistosoma to develop better treatments for the disease.
The eggs of Schistosoma can damage internal organs if left untreated in humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schistosomiasis - An infection caused by Schistosoma parasites

Etymology

From Greek 'skhistos' (split) + 'soma' (body), referring to the split appearance of the fluke's body.
