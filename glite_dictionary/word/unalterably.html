unalterably

1. ADV

/ ʌnˈɔltɚəbli / (AmE), / ʌnˈɔːltərəbli / (BrE)
In a manner that cannot be changed or altered.
in an unchangeable manner

Examples

Her decision was unalterably opposed to the proposal.
The rules were set unalterably by the founding committee.
He remained unalterably committed to his principles, despite all opposition.

Synset Examples

his views were unchangeably fixed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unchangeably - in a way that cannot be changed

Etymology

From un- (not) + alterable (able to be changed), based on Latin *alterare* (to change).
