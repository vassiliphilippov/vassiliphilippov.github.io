fen

1. NOUN

/ fɛn / (AmE), / fɛn / (BrE)
A low-lying wetland with grassy vegetation, often serving as a transition zone between land and water.
grassy low-lying wetland

Examples

The rare orchid thrives in the fen where the soil is always moist.
She walked for miles across the flat, open fens of East Anglia.
Conservationists are working to restore the natural water levels in the ancient fen.

Synset Examples

thousands of acres of marshland
the fens of eastern England

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marsh - an area of low-lying wetland, often waterlogged and with grasses
  • Synonym: bog - wet, spongy ground with decaying vegetation, more acidic than a fen

Etymology

Old English *fen*, from Proto-Germanic *fanjam* (marsh, mud).

2. NOUN

/ fən / (AmE), / fɛn / (BrE)
A monetary unit of China, equal to one hundredth of a yuan.
a Chinese monetary unit, 1/100 yuan

Examples

The price of the item was reduced by twenty fen during the sale.
Coins of one fen are rarely used in China these days.
She found a fen coin among her collection of foreign currency.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: yuan - the basic unit of money in China

Etymology

From Mandarin Chinese 分 (fēn), meaning 'a division' or 'cent'.
