hangdog

1. ADJ

/ ˈhæŋˌdɔɡ / (AmE), / ˈhæŋˌdɒɡ / (BrE)
having an expression that shows guilt, embarrassment, or shame.
showing shame or guilt

Examples

He walked into the room with a hangdog expression after breaking the vase.
She gave him a hangdog look, clearly embarrassed by her mistake.
The children sat in hangdog silence after being caught cheating on the test.

Synset Examples

a guilty look
the hangdog and shamefaced air of the retreating enemy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shamefaced - showing embarrassment or shame
  • Synonym: guilty - feeling or showing guilt
  • Antonym: defiant - showing resistance or bold disobedience

Etymology

Originally referring to a contemptible person, literally 'dog for hanging', from the practice of hanging stray dogs.

2. ADJ

/ ˈhæŋˌdɔɡ / (AmE), / ˈhæŋ.dɒɡ / (BrE)
having a dejected, guilty, or shamefaced appearance; looking as if one has been frightened into submission or is feeling embarrassed or ashamed.
dejected or guilty in appearance

Examples

He gave me a hangdog look when I asked about the broken window.
With a hangdog expression, she admitted her mistake to the group.
The child's hangdog face showed he knew he'd done something wrong.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: downcast - looking downward or appearing sad or discouraged
  • Antonym: defiant - showing resistance or disobedience

Etymology

From 'hang' and 'dog', referring to a dog with its head and tail down; first used in the 17th century to describe a contemptible person.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00079852-s
Synset POS: s
