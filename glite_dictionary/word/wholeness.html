wholeness

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊlnəs / (AmE), / ˈhəʊlnəs / (BrE)
a state of robust good health and physical wellbeing
state of robust good health

Examples

Years of self-care and proper diet restored his sense of wholeness and increased his vitality.
Yoga and meditation are believed to support both emotional balance and physical wholeness.
After the surgery, she felt a renewed wholeness she hadn't experienced in years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: health - the state of being free from illness or injury
  • Antonym: illness - a condition of poor health or disease

Etymology

From Old English 'hālness' (soundness, health) from 'hāl' (whole, uninjured).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊl.nɪs / (AmE), / ˈhəʊl.nɪs / (BrE)
the state of being complete and undivided; the quality of being whole, with nothing missing or broken
state of being complete or whole

Examples

After years of personal growth, she finally felt a sense of wholeness in her life.
The therapist emphasized the importance of emotional wholeness in recovering from trauma.
Some spiritual traditions focus on achieving wholeness through meditation and self-reflection.

Synset Examples

the integrity of the nervous system is required for normal development
he took measures to insure the territorial unity of Croatia

Related Terms

  • Synonym: completeness - the state of being complete or finished; nothing lacking
  • Synonym: integrity - the state of being whole and undivided
  • Antonym: fragmentation - the process or state of breaking or being broken into small parts

Etymology

Formed from the word 'whole' and the noun suffix '-ness'.
