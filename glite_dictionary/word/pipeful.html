pipeful

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪpfəl / (AmE), / ˈpaɪpfəl / (BrE)
An amount of tobacco sufficient to fill one pipe for smoking at one time.
amount to fill a pipe (tobacco)

Examples

He sat by the fire, enjoying a pipeful after dinner.
She packed a fresh pipeful before joining her friends on the porch.
He knocked the ash from the bowl and refilled it with another pipeful of tobacco.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bowlful - the quantity that can fill the bowl of a pipe or similar container

Etymology

Formed from 'pipe' + suffix '-ful', denoting an amount that fills a pipe.
