Apresoline

1. NOUN

/ ˌæprɪˈsoʊlin / (AmE), / ˌæprɪˈsəʊliːn / (BrE)
A trademarked antihypertensive drug (hydralazine) used to dilate blood vessels, often prescribed with a diuretic to treat high blood pressure and congestive heart failure.
antihypertensive drug; trade name for hydralazine
medical

Examples

The patient was prescribed Apresoline to manage her high blood pressure.
Doctors often use Apresoline in combination with other medications for heart failure.
It is important to monitor blood pressure regularly when taking Apresoline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydralazine - the generic name for Apresoline, an antihypertensive drug
