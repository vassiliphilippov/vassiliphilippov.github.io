Ornithomimida

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔrnɪθoʊˈmɪmɪdə / (AmE), / ˌɔːnɪθəʊˈmɪmɪdə / (BrE)
an extinct family of lightly built, medium-sized theropod dinosaurs that resembled modern ostriches
family of ostrich-like theropod dinosaurs

Examples

Fossils of Ornithomimida have been found on several continents, revealing their global distribution during the Cretaceous.
The lightly built skeletons of Ornithomimida suggest they were fast, agile runners.
Museum exhibits often feature life-sized models of Ornithomimida to illustrate dinosaur evolution.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'ornis' (bird) + 'mimos' (imitator), referring to their bird-like appearance.
