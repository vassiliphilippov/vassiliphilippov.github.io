Puebla de Zaragoza

1. PROPN

/ ˈpwɛb.lə deɪ zəˈrɑːɡə / (AmE), / ˈpwɛb.lə deɪ zəˈrɑːɡə / (BrE)
A city in south-central Mexico, southeast of Mexico City, located on the edge of the central Mexican plateau.
city in south-central Mexico

Examples

Many tourists travel to Puebla de Zaragoza to experience its rich colonial architecture and vibrant culture.
The Battle of Puebla de Zaragoza is commemorated annually as Cinco de Mayo.
Universities in Puebla de Zaragoza attract students from across Mexico and abroad.

Etymology

From Spanish 'Puebla de Zaragoza', named after Ignacio Zaragoza, Mexican general.
