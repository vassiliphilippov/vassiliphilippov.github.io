epanodos

1. NOUN

/ ɛˈpænəˌdɒs / (AmE), / ɛˈpænədɒs / (BrE)
a rhetorical device in which a group of words is repeated in reverse order for emphasis or effect
repetition of words in reverse order

Examples

The poet employed epanodos to create symmetry within the final stanza of her work.
A master of rhetoric, he used epanodos to highlight opposing ideas in his speech.
Literary critics often point out Shakespeare's skillful use of epanodos for dramatic emphasis.

Etymology

From Greek epanodos (return, recurrence), from epi- (upon) + anodos (way up).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛpəˈnɑːdəs / (AmE), / ˌɛpəˈnəʊdɒs / (BrE)
A rhetorical device involving the repetition or recapitulation of main ideas in a speech or text, especially in reverse order.
recapitulation of main ideas, often in reverse order

Examples

The orator skillfully used epanodos to revisit his main arguments in reverse order.
In her essay, the structure of epanodos emphasized the interconnectedness of the themes.
Classic speeches often employ epanodos to reinforce their central ideas for the audience.

Etymology

From Greek epanodos (return, recurrence), from epi (upon) + anodos (way up, ascent).
