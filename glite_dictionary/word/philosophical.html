philosophical

1. ADJ

/ ˌfɪləˈsɑːfɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌfɪləˈsɒfɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to philosophy or philosophers; concerned with the study of fundamental nature of knowledge, reality, and existence.
relating to philosophy or philosophers

Examples

His research is based on philosophical principles that have been debated for centuries.
She has a philosophical interest in the meaning of existence and reality.
Many students struggle to understand philosophical arguments in their first year at university.

Synset Examples

philosophical writing
a considerable knowledge of philosophical terminology

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theoretical - concerned with theory rather than practical application
  • Antonym: practical - concerned with actual use or practice rather than theory or ideas

Etymology

From Greek philosophikos (philosophical), from philosophia (philosophy).

2. ADJ

/ ˌfɪl.əˈsɑː.fɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌfɪl.əˈsɒf.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
Calmly accepting difficulties or trouble without getting upset; showing reasoned detachment or composure, especially in challenging situations.
calmly accepting trouble; detached composure

Examples

Despite the unexpected loss, she remained philosophical and focused on what could be learned from the experience.
He responded to the criticism with a philosophical shrug, refusing to let it disturb his peace of mind.
Even when things went wrong, Mark managed to stay philosophical and kept a positive outlook.

Synset Examples

philosophical resignation
a philosophic attitude toward life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stoic - enduring pain or hardship without complaint
  • Synonym: resigned - accepting something unpleasant calmly
  • Antonym: emotional - showing strong feelings or reactions

Etymology

From Greek philosophikos (philosophical), from philosophia (philosophy).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00861240-s
Synset POS: s
