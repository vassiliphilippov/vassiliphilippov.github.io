miserly

1. ADJ

/ ˈmaɪzərli / (AmE), / ˈmʌɪzəli / (BrE)
Unwilling to give or spend money; lacking generosity.
unwilling to spend money or share

Examples

Even though he was wealthy, his miserly nature stopped him from donating to charity.
She gave her waiter a miserly tip after an excellent meal, which surprised everyone.
The company's miserly budget made it hard to buy new equipment.

Synset Examples

a mean person
he left a miserly tip

Related Terms

  • Antonym: generous - willing to give more of something, especially money, than is strictly necessary or expected
  • Synonym: stingy - unwilling to give or spend; ungenerous

Etymology

From Latin 'miser' (wretched, unhappy, stingy) via Old French 'miserable' and English 'miser'.
