Judaism

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːdiˌeɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːdəɪɪzəm / (BrE)
The religion, philosophy, and way of life of the Jewish people, based on the teachings of the Torah and Talmud; the collective body of Jews who practice this religion.
religion and collective body of Jews

Examples

Many people who follow Judaism observe the Sabbath from Friday evening to Saturday evening.
The traditions of Judaism date back thousands of years.
She studied world religions, including Judaism, Christianity, and Islam, at university.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jewish - relating to Jews or Judaism
  • Antonym: Christianity - the religion based on the life and teachings of Jesus
  • Antonym: Islam - the religion founded by Muhammad

Etymology

From Latin Iudaismus, from Greek Ioudaismos, from Hebrew Yehudith (Judah, Jew).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːdiˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːdiːɪzəm / (BrE)
The monotheistic religion of the Jewish people, based on the teachings and laws found in the Torah and further elaborated in the Talmud.
monotheistic religion of the Jews

Examples

She has studied Judaism extensively as part of her religious education program.
Many traditions in Judaism date back thousands of years and continue to shape Jewish life today.
He converted to Judaism after marrying his wife, who grew up in an Orthodox Jewish community.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Christianity - The religion based on the teachings of Jesus Christ
  • Antonym: Islam - The monotheistic religion of Muslims founded on the teachings of Muhammad

Etymology

From Latin 'Iudaismus', from Greek 'Ioudaismos', from Hebrew 'Yehudah' (Judah).
