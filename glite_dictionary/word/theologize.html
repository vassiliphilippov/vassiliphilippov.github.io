to theologize

1. VERB

/ θiˈɑlədʒaɪz / (AmE), / θiˈɒlədʒaɪz / (BrE)
to discuss or speculate about theological subjects or make theoretical reflections on theology
discuss or speculate about theology

Examples

The professor tends to theologize every topic, linking it back to religious doctrine.
They spent hours theologizing about the nature of sin and redemption.
She prefers practical ministry to simply theologizing about spiritual matters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to philosophize - to theorize or reflect on philosophy

Etymology

From Medieval Latin theologizare, from Greek theologizein (to discourse about the gods).

2. VERB

/ θiˈɑlədʒaɪz / (AmE), / θɪˈɒlədʒaɪz / (BrE)
to discuss, interpret, or treat a subject from a theological perspective; to render something theological in character
discuss or treat theologically

Examples

It annoyed her when he would theologize every moral issue that arose in their discussions.
The writer tends to theologize simple stories, giving them layers of religious meaning.
Scholars gathered at the conference to theologize about the significance of ancient texts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to philosophize - to speculate or reason about matters in a philosophical way

Etymology

From Greek 'theologein' (to discourse about the gods) via Late Latin 'theologizare'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01035990-v
Synset POS: v
