Iliamna remota

1. NOUN

/ ɪliˈæmnə rɪˈmoʊtə / (AmE), / ɪliˈamnə rɪˈməʊtə / (BrE)
A rare North American mallow found only in Illinois, resembling the hollyhock and bearing pale rose-mauve flowers; sometimes classified under the genus Sphaeralcea.
a rare Illinois mallow with pale rose-mauve flowers

Examples

The endangered Iliamna remota blooms in midsummer along prairie remnants in Illinois.
Conservationists are working to protect Iliamna remota from habitat loss.
During our field trip, we were fortunate to spot a cluster of Iliamna remota in bloom.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'ilios' (sun) and 'amna' (container); species epithet 'remota' from Latin, meaning 'remote' or 'distant'.
