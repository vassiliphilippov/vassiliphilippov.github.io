Aramean

1. NOUN

/ ˌærəˈmiːən / (AmE), / ˌærəˈmiːən / (BrE)
a member of a Semitic people who inhabited Aram and parts of Mesopotamia from the 11th to the 8th centuries BC
historical Semitic people of Aram/Mesopotamia

Examples

Ancient inscriptions mention the migration of the Aramean tribes into Mesopotamia.
Scholars continue to study the language and culture of the Arameans today.
The Aramean influence can be seen in the religious practices of their neighbors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Assyrian - a member of an ancient Semitic people of northern Mesopotamia
  • Synonym: Babylonian - a member of an ancient Semitic people who lived in Babylon

Etymology

From Latin Aramaeus, from Greek Aramaios, relating to Aram (an ancient region in Syria).

2. ADJ

/ ˌærəˈmiːən / (AmE), / ˌærəˈmiːən / (BrE)
relating to Aram, its people, culture, or language, especially in ancient times
of or relating to ancient Aram or its people

Examples

The Aramean language became widespread throughout the ancient Near East.
Several artifacts from the Aramean period were discovered during the excavation.
He studied the influence of Aramean culture on early Semitic civilizations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Syrian - relating to Syria or its people; sometimes historically related

Etymology

From Latin Aramaeus (an Aramean), from Greek Aramaios, from Aram (ancient region in Syria).
