verticillated

1. ADJ

/ vərˈtɪsɪˌleɪtɪd / (AmE), / vəˈtɪsɪleɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of plant parts) arranged in one or more whorls, especially referring to leaves or branches in a circular formation around a stem.
arranged in a whorl (botany)

Examples

The plant's verticillated leaves formed distinct circular patterns around each stem.
Some species in this genus display verticillated arrangements of flowers instead of the more usual alternately placed ones.
The botanist noted the verticillated branches, which helped distinguish the new species from its relatives.

Etymology

From Latin 'verticillus' meaning 'little whirl', from 'vertex' (whirl, crown of the head).
