to converge

1. VERB

/ kənˈvɝrdʒ / (AmE), / kənˈvɜːdʒ / (BrE)
(of a sequence, series, or function) to approach a definite limit as more terms are added or as a variable approaches a certain value.
(math) approach a limit as terms increase

Examples

If the infinite series converges, its sum will approach a finite number.
The function converges to zero as x approaches infinity.
Their model proved that the iterative process converges rapidly under certain conditions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to diverge - (math) to fail to approach a limit as terms increase

Etymology

From Late Latin convergĕre (to incline together), from Latin com- (together) + vergere (to bend).

2. VERB

/ kənˈvɝrdʒ / (AmE), / kənˈvɜːdʒ / (BrE)
to come together from different directions so as to form a single result or product
come together to form a single outcome

Examples

Economic factors and political interests converged to create an unlikely alliance.
All routes converge at the town square, making it a central meeting point.
The research teams from different universities converged on a similar solution to the problem.

Synset Examples

Social forces converged to bring the Fascists back to power

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to diverge - to move or draw apart; become different or separate

Etymology

From Latin convergere (to incline together), from com- (together) + vergere (to bend, turn).

3. VERB

/ kənˈvɝːdʒ / (AmE), / kənˈvɜːdʒ / (BrE)
to come together from different directions so as to meet or join at a point
come together and meet

Examples

All the main roads converge at the city center, making it easy to navigate.
Their ideas gradually converged during the group discussion, leading to a consensus.
The rivers converge just before the falls, creating a dramatic scene.

Synset Examples

The lines converge at this point

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to diverge - to move or extend in different directions from a common point

Etymology

From Latin 'convergere', from 'com-' (together) + 'vergere' (to bend)

(of people or things) to come together from different directions and meet at a particular place.

Three roads converge at the town square, making it a busy intersection.
