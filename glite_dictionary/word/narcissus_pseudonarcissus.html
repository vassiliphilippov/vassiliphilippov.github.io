Narcissus pseudonarcissus

1. NOUN

/ nɑrˈsɪsəs ˌsuːdoʊ.nɑrˈsɪsəs / (AmE), / nɑːˈsɪs.əs ˌsjuːdəʊ.nɑːˈsɪs.əs / (BrE)
A species of narcissus with showy, often yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers; commonly known as the wild daffodil.
wild daffodil species

Examples

Every spring, the woods were carpeted with Narcissus pseudonarcissus in full bloom.
The Narcissus pseudonarcissus is prized for its delicate yellow flowers and natural beauty.
Botanists study Narcissus pseudonarcissus to understand its role in the ecology of temperate woodlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daffodil - common name for several Narcissus species, especially Narcissus pseudonarcissus

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Narcissus' from Greek Narkissos (a mythological figure), and 'pseudonarcissus' meaning 'false narcissus.'
