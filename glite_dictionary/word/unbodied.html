unbodied

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈbɑdid / (AmE), / ʌnˈbɒdid / (BrE)
Having no physical or material body; incorporeal.
having no material body

Examples

The novel describes unbodied spirits haunting the old mansion.
Some philosophers debate the nature of unbodied consciousness.
Legends tell of unbodied voices whispering through the ancient forest.

Synset Examples

bodiless ghosts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bodiless - without a body; incorporeal

Etymology

un- (not) + bodied (having a body).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈbɑdɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈbɒdɪd / (BrE)
having no physical body; incorporeal or immaterial
without a physical body

Examples

Some philosophers discuss unbodied existence as a possible state of being.
The ghost appeared to be an unbodied presence in the room.
Poetry often refers to spirits as unbodied souls wandering among the living.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incorporeal - not having a material body; immaterial
  • Antonym: bodied - having or possessing a body

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'bodied.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00631987-a
Synset POS: a
