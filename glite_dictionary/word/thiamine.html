thiamine

1. NOUN

/ ˈθaɪ.əˌmin / (AmE), / ˈθaɪ.ə.miːn / (BrE)
a water-soluble B vitamin (vitamin B1) essential for normal metabolism, nerve function, and prevention of beriberi
a B vitamin essential for metabolism
medical

Examples

Whole grains are a good source of thiamine, which helps maintain energy levels.
A deficiency in thiamine can lead to serious neurological problems.
Doctors often prescribe thiamine supplements to treat beriberi.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vitamin B1 - another name for thiamine, a B vitamin essential for health

Etymology

From thio- (containing sulfur) and amine (containing an amino group).
