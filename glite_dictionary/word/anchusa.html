anchusa

1. NOUN

/ ænˈkjuːsə / (AmE), / anˈkjuːsə / (BrE)
Any of various Old World herbaceous plants of the genus Anchusa, which have one-sided clusters of trumpet-shaped flowers, usually blue or purple.
a genus of Old World herbaceous plants

Examples

The borders of the garden were lined with anchusa, their blue flowers attracting bees.
She identified the wildflower as an anchusa while hiking through the meadow.
Many gardeners plant anchusa for its vivid color and resilience.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin anchusa, from Greek ankhoúsa, a plant used for rouge.
