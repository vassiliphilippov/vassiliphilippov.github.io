coherency

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈhɪrənsi / (AmE), / kəʊˈhɪərənsi / (BrE)
the quality of being logical, orderly, and consistent in arrangement or relation of parts
quality of being coherent or consistent

Examples

The essay's coherency made it easy to follow the author's argument.
Engineers strive for coherency in design to ensure all parts work smoothly together.
Her speech lacked coherency, making it difficult for the audience to understand her message.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coherence - the quality of being logical and consistent

Etymology

From Latin 'cohaerentia', from 'cohaerēre' (to stick together)

2. NOUN

/ koʊˈhɪrənsi / (AmE), / kəʊˈhɪərənsi / (BrE)
the quality of being logically connected or holding together as a whole
quality of sticking or holding together

Examples

The coherency of his argument made it persuasive to the audience.
There is a lack of coherency in the final report due to multiple authors.
A good essay depends on the coherency of its ideas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coherence - the quality of being logical and consistent
  • Antonym: incoherency - lack of logical connection or consistency

Etymology

From Latin 'cohaerentia', from 'cohaerere' (to stick together).
