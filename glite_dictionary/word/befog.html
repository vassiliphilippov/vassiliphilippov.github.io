to befog

1. VERB

/ bɪˈfɑɡ / (AmE), / bɪˈfɒɡ / (BrE)
to make something less visible or clear; to obscure or cloud
make less visible or clear

Examples

The mist began to befog the city skyline as night fell.
Confusing explanations can befog the main points for the audience.
His memory was befogged by years of neglect and time.

Synset Examples

The stars are obscured by the clouds
the big elm tree obscures our view of the valley

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obscure - to make difficult to see or understand
  • Antonym: to clarify - to make something clearer or easier to understand

Etymology

From be- (prefix) + fog (to cover with fog), first recorded in English in the 17th century.
