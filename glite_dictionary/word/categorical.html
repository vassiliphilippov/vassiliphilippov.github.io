categorical

1. ADJ

/ ˌkætəˈɡɔrɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌkatɪˈɡɒrɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to or included in a category or categories; concerning classes or divisions.
relating to categories or classes

Examples

The data was divided into categorical groups for easier analysis.
She made a categorical distinction between fact and opinion.
Each item is assigned to a categorical label based on its characteristics.

Etymology

From Late Latin categoricus, from Greek katēgorikos (accusatory, categorical), from katēgorein (to accuse, to assert).

2. ADJ

/ ˌkæt̬.əˈɡɔːr.ɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌkæt.ɪˈɡɒr.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
expressed without any doubt or qualification; unconditional and explicit.
unconditional; without reservations

Examples

She gave a categorical assurance that the contract would not be changed.
The CEO issued a categorical denial of the rumors.
His refusal was categorical and left no room for negotiation.

Synset Examples

a categorical denial
a flat refusal

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conditional - subject to one or more conditions or requirements

Etymology

From Late Latin categoricus, from Greek katēgorikos (affirming, assertive).

Synset POS: s
