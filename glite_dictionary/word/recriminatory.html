recriminatory

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈkrɪməˌtɔri / (AmE), / rɪˈkrɪmɪnətəri / (BrE)
(of remarks or arguments) involving or tending to make accusations in return; characterized by mutual accusations.
involving mutual accusations

Examples

The debate quickly turned recriminatory as both sides began blaming each other for the failures.
Their divorce proceedings became increasingly recriminatory, with each partner raising past grievances.
Instead of resolving the issue, the discussion became recriminatory, fueling further hostility.

Synset Examples

recriminatory arguments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accusatory - indicating or suggesting blame or accusation
  • Synonym: defensive - intended to defend against criticism or attack

Etymology

From Latin recriminari (to make counteraccusations), from re- (again) + criminari (to accuse).
