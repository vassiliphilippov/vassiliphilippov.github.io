lachrymal

1. ADJ

/ ˈlæk.rə.məl / (AmE), / ˈlæk.rɪ.məl / (BrE)
relating to or situated near the organ that produces tears, especially the tear glands or ducts.
relating to tear-producing organs
medical

Examples

The lachrymal glands are responsible for producing most of the tears in your eyes.
An injury near the lachrymal duct can interfere with normal tear drainage.
Doctors examined the patient for signs of lachrymal gland inflammation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lacrimal - relating to tear glands or tears (alternative spelling)

Etymology

From Latin 'lacrima' meaning 'tear'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈlækrɪməl / (AmE), / ˈlakrɪməl / (BrE)
Relating to tears or the secretion of tears.
relating to tears

Examples

The doctor explained that the lachrymal glands produce tears to keep our eyes moist.
He suffered from a blockage in the lachrymal duct, which caused excessive tearing.
During the surgery, the specialist carefully avoided the lachrymal structures near the eye.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tearful - full of or shedding tears
  • Synonym: lacrimal - alternative spelling: relating to tears

Etymology

From Latin 'lacrima' (tear), via French 'lacrymal'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a
