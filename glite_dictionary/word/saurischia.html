Saurischia

1. NOUN

/ sɔːˈrɪskiə / (AmE), / sɔːˈrɪskɪə / (BrE)
a large order of extinct reptiles, including the theropod and sauropod dinosaurs, characterized by a lizard-like pelvic structure
order of dinosaurs with lizard-like hips

Examples

The Saurischia include some of the most famous dinosaurs, such as Tyrannosaurus and Brachiosaurus.
Paleontologists differentiate Saurischia from Ornithischia based on their hip structure.
Saurischia were dominant during the Jurassic and Cretaceous periods.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Ornithischia - order of dinosaurs with bird-like pelvic structure

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek sauros (lizard) + ischion (hip joint).

2. NOUN

/ sɔːˈrɪskiə / (AmE), / sɔːˈrɪskiə / (BrE)
a major order of extinct terrestrial reptiles comprising theropods (typically carnivorous, bipedal dinosaurs) and sauropods (large, herbivorous, long-necked dinosaurs), characterized by having a lizard-like pelvic structure
order of dinosaurs with lizard-like pelvis

Examples

The Saurischia included both the towering sauropods and the swift, predatory theropods.
Paleontologists discovered a new dinosaur fossil belonging to the Saurischia order.
Differences in pelvic structure distinguish Saurischia from their dinosaurian counterparts, the Ornithischia.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Ornithischia - order of dinosaurs with bird-like pelvis

Etymology

From Greek sauros (lizard) + ischion (hip joint).
