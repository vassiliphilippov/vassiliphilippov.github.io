tweet

1. NOUN

/ twit / (AmE), / twiːt / (BrE)
a weak, high-pitched chirping sound, especially one made by a small bird
a small bird's chirping sound

Examples

The tweet of a sparrow filled the early morning air.
We heard the faint tweet of chicks hidden in the bushes.
The forest was quiet except for the occasional tweet from a nest nearby.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chirp - a short, sharp sound made by small birds or insects
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07413627-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ twit / (AmE), / twiːt / (BrE)
A message or post published on the social media platform Twitter.
post on Twitter

Examples

She shared an interesting tweet about climate change.
His last tweet received hundreds of likes and retweets.
The company announced the update in a tweet early this morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: post - a message published on an online platform or forum

Etymology

Originally an imitation of a bird's chirping sound; extended in 21st century to digital posts on Twitter.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-90000061-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ twiːt / (AmE), / twiːt / (BrE)
to post a short message on the social media platform Twitter
post a message on Twitter

Examples

He likes to tweet about the latest technology news every morning.
She tweeted her support for the new environmental initiative.
Many celebrities tweet live updates during major events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retweet - to repost a tweet on Twitter

Etymology

Originally imitative for a bird’s call; later extended to Twitter messages, as popularized by the Twitter brand (founded 2006).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-90000001-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ twiːt / (AmE), / twiːt / (BrE)
(of a small bird) to make a short, high-pitched, chirping sound.
make a high-pitched bird sound

Examples

Every morning, the robins tweet outside my window as the sun rises.
We watched as the canary tweeted cheerfully in its cage.
In the quiet forest, a sparrow suddenly tweeted from a nearby branch.

Synset Examples

the small bird was tweeting in the tree

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chirp - make a short, sharp sound, especially by a bird or insect

Etymology

Imitative of the natural sound made by small birds.
