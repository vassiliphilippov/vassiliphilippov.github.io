Juniperus virginiana

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒuːnəˈpɪərəs vɜːrdʒɪˈneɪniə / (AmE), / ˌdʒuːnɪˈpɛərəs vɜːdʒɪˈneɪnɪə / (BrE)
A small evergreen coniferous tree, native to eastern North America, with a conical crown, reddish-brown bark that peels in shreds, and small, sharp needles; also known as eastern red cedar.
a North American juniper tree (eastern red cedar)

Examples

The wood of the Juniperus virginiana is highly valued for making pencils and cedar chests.
We planted a Juniperus virginiana in our backyard to attract birds with its berries.
The bark of Juniperus virginiana peels off in narrow strips, giving the tree a distinctive appearance.

Etymology

From Latin: Juniperus (the juniper genus) + virginiana (of Virginia, in reference to Virginia, USA).
