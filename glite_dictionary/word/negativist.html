negativist

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛɡətɪvɪst / (AmE), / ˈnɛɡətɪvɪst / (BrE)
a person who consistently resists, refuses, or opposes requests or suggestions, often by doing the opposite of what is asked
person who habitually resists or opposes requests

Examples

He is seen as a negativist, always disagreeing with the team's proposals.
The teacher struggled to motivate the negativist in the classroom who refused to participate.
Though many offered to help, the negativist insisted on rejecting every suggestion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contrarian - a person who opposes or rejects popular opinion or current practice
  • Antonym: complier - a person who consistently does what is asked or expected

Etymology

From 'negative' + '-ist' (one who practices or is concerned with), patterned after other agent nouns.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɛɡətɪvɪst / (AmE), / ˈnɛɡətɪvɪst / (BrE)
a person who habitually sees only the negative side of things or is resigned to defeat without offering positive suggestions
a person with a negative, defeatist attitude

Examples

As a negativist, he seldom offered solutions and focused mostly on problems.
The team's negativist attitude affected the morale of everyone involved.
It can be difficult to work with a negativist who consistently dismisses new ideas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pessimist - someone who habitually expects the worst
  • Antonym: optimist - someone who expects the best in all things

Etymology

From negative + -ist, modeled on similar English formations.
