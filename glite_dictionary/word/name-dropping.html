name-dropping

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪmˌdrɑpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈneɪmˌdrɒpɪŋ / (BrE)
The practice of casually mentioning famous or important people in conversation, especially to impress others.
mentioning famous people to impress

Examples

His constant name-dropping at parties quickly became annoying to everyone.
The politician's interview was full of name-dropping to show off her connections.
He tried to impress us through name-dropping but it just made him seem insecure.

Synset Examples

the hard thing about name-dropping is to avoid being too obvious about it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bragging - talking with excessive pride about oneself
  • Antonym: humility - a modest or low view of one's importance

Etymology

Formed from "name" + "dropping," originally mid 20th century, referencing the act of 'dropping' names into conversation.
