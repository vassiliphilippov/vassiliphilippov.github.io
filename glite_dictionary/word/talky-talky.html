talky-talky

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɑki ˌtɑki / (AmE), / ˈtɔːki ˌtɔːki / (BrE)
excessively focused on dialogue or chatty in manner, especially in a way that may seem tedious or verbose
excessively chatty or dialogue-driven
informal

Examples

The director's latest film felt a bit too talky-talky for my taste.
I sometimes enjoy a talky-talky play, but this one dragged on for hours.
The novelist's talky-talky style isn't for everyone, but some readers find it engaging.

Synset Examples

The film was a bit too talky-talky for my taste.
I prefer action movies over talky-talky dramas.
His talky-talky style kept the audience engaged.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: talky - containing a lot of talk or dialogue
