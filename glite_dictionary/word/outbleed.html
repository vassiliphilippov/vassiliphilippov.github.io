to outbleed

1. VERB

/ aʊtˈbliːd / (AmE), / aʊtˈbliːd / (BrE)
to surpass others in displaying exaggerated emotion or dramatic flair
to surpass others in dramatic flair
informal

Examples

Nobody in the class could outbleed him during the heated debate.
She always manages to outbleed her co-stars on stage with her passionate performances.
Even in casual conversations, he tends to outbleed everyone else when telling stories.

Synset Examples

She can outbleed anyone when it comes to drama.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to outdo - to do better than someone else
  • Synonym: to overact - to act in an exaggerated way, especially in theater

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'out-' (meaning to surpass) and 'bleed', here used figuratively for showing emotion.
