Arctonyx

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrkˈtoʊnɪks / (AmE), / ɑːkˈtɒnɪks / (BrE)
A genus of mammals in the mustelid family, known as hog badgers, found in Asia.
genus of mustelid mammals (hog badgers)

Examples

The Arctonyx genus includes the largest members of the badger family.
Researchers observed several Arctonyx individuals foraging in the forest undergrowth.
The distribution of Arctonyx extends across Southeast Asia and parts of China.

Etymology

From Greek arktos (bear) + onyx (claw).

2. NOUN

/ ɑrkˈtoʊ.nɪks / (AmE), / ɑːkˈtɒ.nɪks / (BrE)
A genus of mammals in the family Mustelidae that includes the hog badgers found in Asia.
genus of Asian hog badgers

Examples

The Arctonyx genus comprises several species of hog badgers native to Asia.
Researchers recently discovered new adaptations in the Arctonyx population.
The Arctonyx is noted for its stout body and distinctive snout.

Etymology

From Greek arkton (bear) and onyx (claw).
