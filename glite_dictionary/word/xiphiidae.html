Xiphiidae

1. NOUN

/ zaɪˈfaɪɪˌdiː / (AmE), / zɪˈfɪɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of fish comprising the common swordfishes, known for their elongated, sword-like upper jaws.
family of swordfish

Examples

Scientists classify the common swordfish in the family Xiphiidae.
The Xiphiidae are well known for their elongated bills resembling swords.
Marine biologists have studied the migration patterns of several species in the Xiphiidae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swordfish - large predatory fish with a long, flattened, sword-like upper jaw

Etymology

From Greek xiphos (sword) + -idae, a typical zoological family suffix.
