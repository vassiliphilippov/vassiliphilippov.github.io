Blattella germanica

1. NOUN

/ bləˈtɛlə dʒɚˈmænɪkə / (AmE), / bləˈtɛlə dʒɜːˈmænɪkə / (BrE)
A small, light-brown species of cockroach, commonly known as the German cockroach, often found as a household pest.
German cockroach species

Examples

The Blattella germanica is notorious for infesting kitchens and quickly multiplying in homes.
Pest control professionals often identify Blattella germanica as the most common cockroach in apartment buildings.
Researchers study Blattella germanica to understand resistance to insecticides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockroach - a nocturnal insect pest
  • Degree Lower: oriental cockroach - a species of cockroach common in damp places
  • Degree Higher: American cockroach - a larger, reddish-brown species of cockroach

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus name Blattella (diminutive of Latin blatta, cockroach) and the species epithet germanica ('German').
