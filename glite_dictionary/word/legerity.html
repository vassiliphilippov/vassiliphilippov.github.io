legerity

1. NOUN

/ ləˈdʒɛrəti / (AmE), / lɪˈdʒɛrɪti / (BrE)
gracefulness and agility, especially in quick, nimble movements
graceful nimbleness or agility

Examples

The dancer's legerity impressed the entire audience during the performance.
Thanks to her natural legerity, she darted through the crowd effortlessly.
The cat moved with such legerity that it seemed to float across the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agility - ability to move quickly and easily
  • Antonym: clumsiness - lack of grace or agility in movement

Etymology

From Latin 'legeritas' (lightness), from 'levis' (light).
