Mother Teresa

1. PROPN

/ ˈmʌðɚ təˈreɪsə / (AmE), / ˈmʌðə təˈreɪzə / (BrE)
(1910–1997) An Albanian-born Indian Roman Catholic nun and missionary renowned for her lifelong dedication to helping the poor in India, particularly in Kolkata.
Albanian-born Indian nun and missionary, Nobel laureate

Examples

Mother Teresa devoted her life to caring for the sick and destitute in Kolkata.
Many consider Mother Teresa a symbol of compassion and selflessness.
The work of Mother Teresa has inspired countless people worldwide to volunteer for charitable causes.

Etymology

Born Anjezë Gonxhe Bojaxhiu; 'Mother' as a religious title, 'Teresa' adopted as her religious name in honor of St. Thérèse of Lisieux.
