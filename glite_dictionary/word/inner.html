inner

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪnɚ / (AmE), / ˈɪnə / (BrE)
Situated or occurring within, or closer to a central point or area.
located within or closer to the center

Examples

The library has an inner courtyard filled with plants.
We waited in the inner office until we were called for the meeting.
Tourists are not allowed inside the temple’s inner sanctum.

Synset Examples

an inner room

Related Terms

  • Antonym: outer - situated on or relating to the outside; farther from the center
  • Synonym: internal - existing or situated within something; interior

Etymology

From Old English 'innera', comparative of 'inne' (inside).
