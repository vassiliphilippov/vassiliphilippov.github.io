difference

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪfərəns / (AmE), / ˈdɪfərəns / (BrE)
the quality of being unlike or dissimilar
state of being unlike or dissimilar

Examples

It's easy to spot the difference between these two photographs.
The difference in taste between the brands was noticeable.
Their opinions show a clear difference in perspective.

Synset Examples

there are many differences between jazz and rock

Related Terms

  • Antonym: similarity - the state of being similar or alike

Etymology

From Latin 'differentia' (difference), from 'differre' (to differ).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɪfɚəns / (AmE), / ˈdɪfərəns / (BrE)
a variation or amount by which something deviates from a standard, norm, or average
deviation from a standard or norm

Examples

The difference between the test result and the average value was significant.
A small difference from the expected outcome can change the entire experiment.
Statisticians analyze the difference between observed and theoretical values to understand the data.

Synset Examples

the deviation from the mean

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deviation - an act or instance of departing from an established course or norm
  • Degree Lower: mean - the average value in a set of numbers

Etymology

From Latin differentia, from differre (to differ).

3. NOUN

/ ˈdɪf.ɚ.əns / (AmE), / ˈdɪf.ər.əns / (BrE)
a disagreement or argument about something important, especially between two or more people or groups
disagreement or argument

Examples

A difference of opinion led to heated debates in the meeting.
They tried to resolve their differences before signing the contract.
The couple cited irreconcilable differences as the reason for their divorce.

Synset Examples

he had a dispute with his wife
there were irreconcilable differences
the familiar conflict between Republicans and Democrats

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dispute - an argument or debate, especially over matters of opinion
  • Synonym: conflict - a serious disagreement or argument, typically a protracted one
  • Antonym: agreement - harmony in opinion or feeling; a negotiated and typically legally binding arrangement

Etymology

From Old French 'difference', from Latin 'differentia' (difference, diversity).
