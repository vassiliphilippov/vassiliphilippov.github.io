partitioning

1. NOUN

/ pɑrˈtɪʃ.ən.ɪŋ / (AmE), / pɑːˈtɪʃ.ən.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of dividing something into separate, mutually exclusive categories or parts, especially for analytical or organizational purposes
dividing into exclusive categories or parts

Examples

The partitioning of the data set allowed each category to be analyzed independently.
Mathematical partitioning is essential for organizing complex problems into simpler subsets.
Effective partitioning of responsibilities can improve teamwork and efficiency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classification - the process of grouping things based on shared qualities or characteristics
  • Antonym: aggregation - the act of collecting things into a group or whole

Etymology

From 'partition' (from Latin partitio 'a sharing, partition'), with the noun-forming suffix '-ing'.

2. NOUN

/ pɑrˈtɪʃ.ə.nɪŋ / (AmE), / pɑːˈtɪʃ.ən.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of dividing something into parts or creating boundaries that separate areas or groups
the act of dividing or separating

Examples

The partitioning of the office created private workspaces for each employee.
Database partitioning improves performance by organizing large datasets efficiently.
The partitioning of the land led to disputes among the neighbors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: division - the action of separating something into parts
  • Antonym: joining - the action of connecting or combining things; opposite of partitioning

Etymology

From 'partition' + -ing; ultimately from Latin 'partitio' (a division) via Old French and Middle English.
