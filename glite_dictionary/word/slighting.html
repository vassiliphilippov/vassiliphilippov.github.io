slighting

1. ADJ

/ ˈslaɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈslaɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
expressing disrespect or contempt; intended to insult or belittle someone or something
disparaging or belittling

Examples

The manager's slighting remarks discouraged many of his employees.
He gave her a slighting glance and walked away without speaking.
Critics often make slighting comments about popular films.

Synset Examples

belittling comments
managed a deprecating smile at the compliment
deprecatory remarks about the book
a slighting remark

Related Terms

  • Synonym: belittling - expressing the idea of making someone or something seem unimportant
  • Antonym: complimentary - expressing praise or approval

Etymology

From the verb 'slight', from Middle English 'slight' (smooth, sleek, thin), influenced by Old Norse 'slétt' (level, smooth) and sense development to 'of little value'.
