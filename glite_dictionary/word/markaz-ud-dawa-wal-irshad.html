Markaz-ud-Dawa-wal-Irshad

1. PROPN

/ mɑːrkəz ʊd daːwɑ wæl ɪrˈʃɑd / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkəz ʊd ˌdɑːwɑː wæl ɪˈʃɑːd / (BrE)
A Sunni Islamic organization founded in 1989 in Pakistan, known for its association with the armed group Lashkar-e-Tayyiba and opposition to Western missionary activity.
Sunni Islamic group founded in Pakistan in 1989

Examples

The Pakistani government placed Markaz-ud-Dawa-wal-Irshad under surveillance for its alleged links to militant activities.
International reports have detailed the connections between Markaz-ud-Dawa-wal-Irshad and Lashkar-e-Tayyiba.
Many analysts consider Markaz-ud-Dawa-wal-Irshad a significant force in the region's religious and political landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lashkar-e-Tayyiba - armed wing of Markaz-ud-Dawa-wal-Irshad

Etymology

From Arabic, meaning 'Center for Preaching and Guidance.'
