criollo

1. NOUN

/ kriˈoʊjoʊ / (AmE), / krɪˈɒljəʊ / (BrE)
A person of pure or mostly Spanish descent born in Spanish America, especially in colonial times.
person of Spanish descent born in Spanish America

Examples

During the colonial period, criollo elites often held significant power in Spanish America.
The revolution was largely led by criollo leaders seeking independence from Spain.
Many criollo families traced their ancestry directly to Spanish settlers.

Synset Examples

Mexico is a country of mestizos, criollos, and indigenes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mestizo - a person of mixed European and Indigenous descent, especially in Latin America
  • Antonym: peninsular - a person born in Spain residing in the Spanish colonies

Etymology

Spanish 'criollo', originally meaning 'native to a locality, not imported', possibly from Portuguese 'crioulo' (a locally born person of foreign ancestry).

2. NOUN

/ kriˈoʊjoʊ / (AmE), / kriˈɒljoʊ / (BrE)
A variety of cocoa bean considered to be of superior quality, prized for its fine flavor and aroma.
a superior variety of cocoa bean

Examples

Many artisanal chocolate makers prefer using criollo beans for their delicate and complex taste.
The region is famous for cultivating rare criollo cocoa prized by fine chocolatiers worldwide.
Due to its low yield and susceptibility to disease, criollo cocoa is more expensive than other varieties.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: forastero - a common variety of cocoa bean with a stronger, less refined flavor
  • Synonym: trinitario - a hybrid variety of cocoa bean combining Criollo and Forastero traits

Etymology

From Spanish 'criollo', originally meaning 'native' or 'local', now used for this cocoa variety.
