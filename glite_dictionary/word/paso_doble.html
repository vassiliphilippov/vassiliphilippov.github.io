paso doble

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːsoʊ ˈdoʊbleɪ / (AmE), / ˌpæsəʊ ˈdəʊbleɪ / (BrE)
A style of lively march music, originally from Spain, composed for dancing the paso doble and often played at bullfights.
lively Spanish march music for dance/bullfights

Examples

The orchestra played a traditional paso doble as the matadors entered the arena.
The dramatic rhythm of a paso doble energizes the dancers' performance.
Every year, the festival opens with a lively paso doble played by the town band.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paso doble - a quick Spanish dance modeled after a bullfight

Etymology

Spanish, literally 'double step', referring to the pace of the dance or march.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːsoʊ ˈdoʊbleɪ / (AmE), / ˌpæsəʊ ˈdəʊbleɪ / (BrE)
A fast-paced ballroom dance that originated in Spain, characterized by dramatic movements meant to represent a bullfight.
Spanish ballroom dance, bullfight-inspired

Examples

The couple gave a passionate performance of the paso doble at the dance competition.
The paso doble is often chosen for its dramatic flair and Spanish rhythms.
He's learning the steps of the paso doble for his next ballroom dance class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ballroom dance - any form of partner dance performed socially and competitively

Etymology

Spanish, literally 'double step'.
