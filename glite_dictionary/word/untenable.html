untenable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtɛnəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈtɛnəbəl / (BrE)
(of an argument, theory, or position) not able to be defended against criticism or attack; indefensible.
(of a theory, etc.) cannot be defended or justified

Examples

After the new evidence was presented, his original claim became untenable.
The scientist withdrew her untenable hypothesis when it was disproven by multiple experiments.
It became increasingly clear that their position was untenable in the face of public criticism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indefensible - not able to be defended or justified
  • Antonym: defensible - able to be defended or justified

Etymology

From Latin 'tenere' (to hold) with prefix 'un-' (not) + '-able'.
