to assure

1. VERB

/ əˈʃʊr / (AmE), / əˈʃʊə / (BrE)
to tell someone something confidently to remove their doubts, or to make a promise or commitment to do something
promise or state confidently

Examples

I can assure you that everything will be completed on time.
She assured her friend that the exam wasn't as difficult as expected.
They assured us of their full support during the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: promise - to state you will definitely do something or that something will happen

Etymology

From Old French 'asseurer', from Latin 'ad-' (to) + 'securus' (secure).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00885532-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ əˈʃʊr / (AmE), / əˈʃɔː / (BrE)
to make certain that something will happen or be the case; to guarantee
make certain; guarantee

Examples

Extra checks will assure the quality of the final product.
The teacher tried to assure his success by offering extra help after class.
Careful planning can assure a smooth transition to the new office.

Synset Examples

This nest egg will ensure a nice retirement for us

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ensure - to make certain that something will happen
  • Synonym: guarantee - to promise that something will definitely happen

Etymology

From Old French 'asseurer', from Latin 'ad-' (to) + 'securus' (secure).

3. VERB

/ əˈʃʊr / (AmE), / əˈʃɔː / (BrE)
to tell someone something with confidence in order to remove their doubts or fears
to confidently tell to dispel doubt

Examples

I can assure you that the results will arrive on time.

The manager assured everyone of the project's success during the meeting.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: doubt - to feel uncertain or lack confidence about something

Etymology

From Old French *aseurer*, from Latin *ad* (to) + *securus* (safe, secure).

5. VERB

/ əˈʃʊr / (AmE), / əˈʃɔː / (BrE)
to make certain that something happens; to guarantee or ensure that something takes place or is done
make certain something happens

Examples

Please assure that all the windows are locked before you leave.
The team works hard to assure quality in every product.
He assured the delivery would arrive on time by double-checking the schedule.

Synset Examples

He verified that the valves were closed
See that the curtains are closed
control the quality of the product

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ensure - to make certain that something happens
  • Synonym: guarantee - to promise that something will certainly happen or be done

Etymology

From Old French assurer, from Vulgar Latin *assecurare, from Latin ad- (to) + securus (secure).
