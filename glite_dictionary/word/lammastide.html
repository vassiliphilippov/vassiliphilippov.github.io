Lammastide

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæm.əs.taɪd / (AmE), / ˈlam.əs.taɪd / (BrE)
The season or period around Lammas, traditionally referring to early August in the English calendar.
the period around Lammas (early August)
archaic british

Examples

The fair was held during Lammastide when the harvest was just beginning.
Old traditions dictate special loaves are baked for Lammastide.
In medieval England, Lammastide brought communal gatherings and festivity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lammas - a Christian holiday marking the wheat harvest, celebrated on August 1st

Etymology

From Lammas (an Anglo-Saxon festival) + tide (Old English tīd meaning time or season).
