overhaul

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvərˌhɔl / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈhɔːl / (BrE)
a thorough examination, repair, or renovation, especially to improve condition or function
thorough repair or renovation

Examples

After several years of neglect, the factory finally underwent a complete overhaul.
The company announced an overhaul of its safety protocols.
A major overhaul of the tax system is expected next year.

Synset Examples

they are pursuing a general program of renovation to the entire property
a major overhaul of the healthcare system was proposed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: renovation - the process of making something new or restored
  • Synonym: repair - the act of fixing or restoring something broken or damaged

Etymology

From Dutch 'overhalen' (to pull over, repair a ship), later used in English in the sense of making thorough repairs.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvɚˌhɔl / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈhɔːl / (BrE)
a thorough inspection and repair of a machine, vehicle, or system, especially as part of periodic maintenance
thorough inspection and repair; maintenance

Examples

The car needed an overhaul before the long journey across the country.
After years of heavy use, the engine finally received a complete overhaul.
Regular overhaul of machinery helps prevent breakdowns and costly repairs.

Synset Examples

it was time for an overhaul on the tractor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: service - routine inspection and maintenance of a vehicle or machine
  • Synonym: repair - the process of fixing something that is broken or damaged

Etymology

From over- + haul, originally referring to pulling rigging over a ship to check for wear.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00268956-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈhɔl / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈhɔːl / (BrE)
to examine thoroughly and make necessary repairs, renovations, or adjustments; to renovate or update comprehensively
to repair or renovate thoroughly

Examples

The company decided to overhaul its outdated computer systems to improve efficiency.
It was necessary to overhaul the entire curriculum to keep up with new educational standards.
Before selling his boat, he spent weeks overhauling the engine and fixing minor issues.

Synset Examples

You should overhaul your car engine
overhaul the health care system

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repair - to restore to good condition after damage or wear
  • Synonym: to renovate - to restore or improve something, especially a building
  • Antonym: to destroy - to put an end to the existence of something by damaging or attacking it

Etymology

From earlier sense 'to haul over' in nautical use, meaning to examine and repair rigging.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00262962-v
Synset POS: v
