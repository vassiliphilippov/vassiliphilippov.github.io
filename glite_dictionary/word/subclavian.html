subclavian

1. ADJ

/ səbˈkleɪviən / (AmE), / sʌbˈkleɪvɪən / (BrE)
situated or occurring beneath the clavicle (collarbone)
situated beneath the clavicle
medical

Examples

The subclavian artery supplies blood to the arms and some chest structures.
A blockage in the subclavian vein can cause swelling of the arm.
During the procedure, a catheter was inserted into the subclavian region.

Etymology

From Latin 'sub-' (under) + 'clavicula' (little key, collarbone).
