barefaced

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɛrˌfeɪst / (AmE), / ˈbɛə.feɪst / (BrE)
shameless and undisguised; done openly and without any attempt to hide one's behavior or feelings.
shamelessly open or undisguised

Examples

He told a barefaced lie in court, astonishing everyone present.
The barefaced nature of the scam made it even more shocking to the victims.
Her barefaced admission surprised her friends, who had expected her to deny everything.

Synset Examples

a barefaced lie

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blatant - done openly and unashamedly
  • Antonym: covert - not openly acknowledged or displayed

Etymology

Originally referring to having a shaved face, later extended to mean 'shameless' due to the openness of being uncovered.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbɛrˌfeɪst / (AmE), / ˈbeə.feɪst / (BrE)
(of actions or behavior) shameless, unconcealed, or done openly without any attempt to hide one's intentions; bold and unrestrained by convention or propriety.
shamelessly bold or unconcealed

Examples

It was a barefaced lie that everyone recognized immediately.
Her barefaced audacity shocked even her closest friends.
He gave a barefaced denial despite the clear evidence against him.

Synset Examples

an audacious trick to pull
a barefaced hypocrite
the most bodacious display of tourism this side of Anaheim
bald-faced lies
brazen arrogance
the modern world with its quick material successes and insolent belief in the boundless possibilities of progress

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brazen - shameless and bold
  • Synonym: bald-faced - openly shameless; unashamed
  • Synonym: shameless - not feeling or showing shame; audacious

Etymology

Originally referred to having the face uncovered (without a beard or mask); later extended metaphorically to mean 'shameless' or 'open.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00156411-s
Synset POS: s
