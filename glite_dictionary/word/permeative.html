permeative

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɝː.mi.eɪ.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.mi.eɪ.tɪv / (BrE)
Spreading or tending to spread throughout an area, substance, or group.
spreading throughout

Examples

The artist used a permeative light to create a dreamlike atmosphere in the painting.
Armed with permeative irony, he was quick to expose pretentious behavior.
A permeative sense of hope filled the community after the renovation.

Synset Examples

armed with permeative irony … he punctures affectations
the pervasive odor of garlic
an error is pervasive if it is material to more than one conclusion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pervasive - spreading widely throughout an area or group

Etymology

From Latin "permeare" (to pass through, to penetrate) + English suffix "-ive".
