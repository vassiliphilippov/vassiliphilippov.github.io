strenuosity

1. NOUN

/ ˌstrɛniˈɑsəti / (AmE), / ˌstrɛnjʊˈɒsəti / (BrE)
the quality or state of showing or requiring intense energy and effort
intense energy or effort

Examples

Despite his age, the athlete performed with remarkable strenuosity throughout the competition.
The strenuosity of her efforts impressed everyone in the office.
His latest novel was written with a strenuosity that reflected his passion for the subject.

Synset Examples

his music is characterized by a happy athleticism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intensity - the state of being intense or highly energetic
  • Synonym: energy - vigorous or intense activity; vitality
  • Antonym: lethargy - a lack of energy and enthusiasm

Etymology

From Latin 'strenuosus' (active, vigorous), via strenuosity as an English noun form.
