tungstate

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌŋˌsteɪt / (AmE), / ˈtʌŋsteɪt / (BrE)
a salt formed by the combination of tungstic acid with a base, containing the tungstate ion (WO4^2−)
a salt of tungstic acid
medical

Examples

Sodium tungstate is often used as a catalyst in chemical reactions.
Researchers have studied the effects of tungstate salts on enzyme activity.
The crystal structure of this mineral reveals that it is a natural tungstate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: molybdate - a salt of molybdic acid, similar in structure to tungstate

Etymology

From tungsten, the element, combined with '-ate', a suffix for salts.
