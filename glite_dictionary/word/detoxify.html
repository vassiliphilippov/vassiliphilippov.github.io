to detoxify

1. VERB

/ diˈtɑksɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / diˈtɒksɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to treat someone to remove toxic substances from their body, especially in the context of alcohol or drug dependence
treat to remove drugs or alcohol
medical

Examples

Many patients choose to detoxify in a medical facility for safety.
He was determined to detoxify after years of alcohol dependence.
The clinic offers programs to help individuals detoxify before starting rehabilitation.

Synset Examples

He was detoxified in the clinic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rehabilitate - to restore someone to health or normal life, especially after drug or alcohol addiction

Etymology

From de- (removal) + toxify (make toxic), from Latin toxicum (poison).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00079541-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ diˈtɑksɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / diˈtɒksɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to remove toxic or harmful substances from something, especially to render it harmless
remove toxins or poisons

Examples

Special processes are used to detoxify industrial waste before it is released into the river.
Doctors worked quickly to detoxify the patient after a drug overdose.
Some plants can naturally detoxify contaminated soil through a process called phytoremediation.

Synset Examples

detoxify the soil

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to purify - to make pure by removing contaminants

Etymology

From de- (removal) + toxify (make toxic), after 'toxin'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00506430-v
Synset POS: v
