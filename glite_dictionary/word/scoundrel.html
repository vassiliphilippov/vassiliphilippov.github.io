scoundrel

1. NOUN

/ ˈskaʊndrəl / (AmE), / ˈskaʊndrəl / (BrE)
a person who behaves dishonestly or in a morally wrong way
dishonest or wicked person

Examples

The authorities finally arrested the scoundrel who had been cheating elderly people out of their savings.
Everyone in the village knew him as a notorious scoundrel with no regard for the law.
She called her ex-business partner a scoundrel after she discovered his deceitful actions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: villain - a person who is evil or cruel
  • Synonym: rogue - a dishonest or unprincipled man
  • Antonym: hero - a person admired for bravery, the opposite of a scoundrel

Etymology

Perhaps from obsolete French *escoundre* (to hide) or from Italian *scondro* (rogue).
