Zauschneria californica

1. NOUN

/ zaʊʃˈnɛriə ˌkælɪˈfɔrnɪkə / (AmE), / zaʊʃˈnɪərɪə ˌkælɪˈfɔːnɪkə / (BrE)
A small perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and Mexico, notable for its bright scarlet tubular flowers; also known as California fuchsia.
plant with scarlet flowers native to southwestern US

Examples

The hummingbirds were drawn to the vibrant blooms of the Zauschneria californica in her garden.
Native plant enthusiasts often recommend planting Zauschneria californica due to its drought tolerance and showy flowers.
During late summer, the Zauschneria californica adds a striking red hue to the dry landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: California fuchsia - Common name for Zauschneria californica, a plant with bright red flowers

Etymology

From the genus name Zauschneria, honoring botanist Johann Baptista Josef Zauschner, and the species name californica, referring to California.
