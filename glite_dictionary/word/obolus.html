obolus

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑː.bə.ləs / (AmE), / ˈɒb.ə.ləs / (BrE)
a Greek unit of weight, historically equal to one tenth of a gram
Greek weight unit (1/10 gram)

Examples

The ancient Greeks used the obolus as both a coin and a measure of weight.
One obolus was equal to about one tenth of a gram in ancient Greek measurements.
Archaeologists discovered an obolus among the other artifacts at the excavation site.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: drachma - an ancient Greek silver coin and unit of weight

Etymology

From Ancient Greek ὀβολός (obolós), originally referring to a small coin or weight.
