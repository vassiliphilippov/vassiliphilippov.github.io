maleate

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪliˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈmaleɪeɪt / (BrE)
a salt or ester of maleic acid, commonly used in chemistry and pharmacology
salt or ester of maleic acid

Examples

The medication contains maleate as an active ingredient to enhance its effectiveness.
In organic chemistry, maleate salts are often used to study reaction mechanisms.
Doctors may prescribe a maleate form of a drug for better absorption.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fumarate - a salt or ester of fumaric acid
  • Synonym: succinate - a salt or ester of succinic acid

Etymology

From maleic acid, itself from Latin malum (apple), due to origin from apple juice.
