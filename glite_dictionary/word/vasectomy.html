vasectomy

1. NOUN

/ vəˈsɛk.tə.mi / (AmE), / vəˈsɛk.tə.mi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure for male sterilization by cutting or sealing the vas deferens to prevent sperm from entering the semen
surgical male sterilization procedure
medical

Examples

After his third child was born, Mark decided to have a vasectomy.
A vasectomy is considered a safe and effective form of permanent birth control.
Some men wonder if a vasectomy can be reversed if they change their mind later.

Etymology

From Latin 'vas' (vessel) + Greek 'ektomē' (excision, cutting out).
