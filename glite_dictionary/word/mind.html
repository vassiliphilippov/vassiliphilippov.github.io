mind

1. NOUN

/ maɪnd / (AmE), / maɪnd / (BrE)
the part of a person that thinks, perceives, reasons, and remembers; a person's intellectual ability or knowledge
intellect; mental ability

Examples

She has a brilliant mind and solves problems quickly.
Reading challenging books stimulates the mind.
The university attracts some of the brightest minds in the world.

Synset Examples

he reads to improve his mind
he has a keen intellect

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intellect - the faculty of reasoning and understanding objectively

Etymology

From Old English 'mynd' (memory, thought), related to Old High German 'minna' (love, memory).
