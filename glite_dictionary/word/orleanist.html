Orleanist

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrliənɪst / (AmE), / ˈɔːliənɪst / (BrE)
A supporter of the Orléans branch of the Bourbon royal family in France, descended from a younger brother of Louis XIV, especially in the context of 19th-century French politics.
supporter of the French Orléans royal family

Examples

Throughout the 19th century, the Orleanists vied for power against other royalist factions in France.
As an Orleanist, he believed the throne belonged to the descendants of the Duke of Orléans.
The Orleanists lost much of their influence after the fall of the July Monarchy.

Etymology

From French Orléaniste, from Orléans (a city in France, seat of the ducal house) + -ist.
