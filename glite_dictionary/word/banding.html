banding

1. NOUN

/ ˈbændɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbændɪŋ / (BrE)
an adornment consisting of a strip of a contrasting color or material, used as decoration
a decorative strip of different color or material

Examples

The dress features silver banding along the sleeves and hem.
Architects added black banding to the building's facade for contrast.
The wallpaper has gold banding that catches the light beautifully.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trim - decorative edging or border on clothing or fabric
  • Synonym: border - a strip forming the outer edge, often decorative

Etymology

From the noun 'band' (a strip or loop), plus the suffix '-ing'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbændɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbændɪŋ / (BrE)
a pattern of one or more stripes of contrasting color, especially as found on animals or objects
stripes of contrasting color

Examples

The caterpillar displays vivid banding along its entire length.
Some species of fish are easily identified by the banding on their fins.
The artist used bold banding to create a dramatic effect in the painting.

Synset Examples

chromosomes exhibit characteristic bands
the black and yellow banding of bees and wasps

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stripe - a long narrow band or strip, typically one of several that are of different color or texture

Etymology

From the noun 'band' (a strip) + -ing (forming a noun indicating action or result).
