sanction

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæŋkʃən / (AmE), / ˈsaŋkʃən / (BrE)
official permission or approval for an action, especially from an authority
official permission or approval

Examples

The company needed government sanction to begin construction on the new highway.
Without parental sanction, students are not allowed to leave the school premises.
The project received official sanction from the city council last month.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authorization - official permission or power to do something
  • Antonym: prohibition - an official order that forbids something

Etymology

From Latin 'sanctio' (decree), from 'sanctus' (made holy).
