Ageratina

1. NOUN

/ ˌædʒərəˈtiːnə / (AmE), / ˌædʒərəˈtiːnə / (BrE)
A genus of annual to perennial herbs or shrubs native to eastern United States and Central and South America, often known for clusters of small flowers.
genus of herbs or shrubs

Examples

Many native bees are attracted to the white blossoms of Ageratina in late summer.
The Ageratina genus includes species used in traditional medicine in Central America.
Gardeners appreciate Ageratina for its hardiness and late-blooming flowers.

Etymology

From Greek ageratos (ageless) + Latin -ina, referring to the long-lasting flowers.
