Consolida

1. NOUN

/ kənˈsoʊlɪdə / (AmE), / kənˈsɒlɪdə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants related to the larkspur, characterized by flowers similar to larkspur's but differing in the arrangement of the petals; sometimes included in the genus Delphinium.
genus of flowering plants related to larkspur

Examples

The gardener planted several Consolida species alongside the roses for a burst of blue and purple color.
Many botanists debate whether Consolida should remain a separate genus or be merged with Delphinium.
Dried Consolida flowers are often used in ornamental arrangements because of their vibrant hue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Delphinium - a genus of flowering plants closely related to Consolida

Etymology

From Latin 'consolida', used for herbs believed to have healing properties.

2. NOUN

/ kənˈsoʊlɪdə / (AmE), / kɒnˈsɒlɪdə / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the buttercup family (Ranunculaceae), distinguished by flowers similar to those of larkspur but differing in petal arrangement; sometimes considered a subset of the genus Delphinium.
a genus of flowering plants related to larkspur

Examples

The garden featured a colorful display of Consolida among the other annuals.
Botanists often debate whether Consolida should be classified separately from Delphinium.
She planted several species of Consolida to attract pollinators to her garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Delphinium - another genus of flowering plants closely related to Consolida

Etymology

From Latin 'consolida' (heal, consolidate), originally referring to plants used medicinally.
