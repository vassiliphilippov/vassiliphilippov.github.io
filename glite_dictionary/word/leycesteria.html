Leycesteria

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈsɛstəriə / (AmE), / laɪˈsɛstəriə / (BrE)
A small genus of shrubs native to the western Himalayas and China, known for their ornamental value.
genus of Himalayan and Chinese shrubs

Examples

In her garden, she planted a Leycesteria for its lovely pendant flowers.
The Leycesteria is often called Himalayan honeysuckle due to its origin and appearance.
Gardeners prize Leycesteria for their ability to attract birds with their berries.

Etymology

Named after William Leycester, a British botanist.

2. NOUN

/ leɪˈsɛstəriə / (AmE), / leɪˈsɛstəriə / (BrE)
A genus of small shrubs native to the western Himalayas and China, belonging to the honeysuckle family.
genus of small Himalayan/Chinese shrubs

Examples

The Leycesteria is often grown in gardens for its ornamental berries and flowers.
Some species of Leycesteria are valued for their ability to attract birds and pollinators.
Native to the Himalayas, Leycesteria thrives in temperate climate gardens.

Etymology

Named after William Leycester, a British colonial botanist.
