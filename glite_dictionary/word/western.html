Western

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstərn / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən / (BrE)
a film, television show, or novel about life in the western United States during the period of exploration and settlement, typically featuring cowboys, outlaws, and frontier settings
film/genre about the American Old West

Examples

He grew up watching classic Westerns every Saturday morning with his grandfather.
Her favorite movie is an old Western starring John Wayne.
The director decided to make a modern Western set in the 21st century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowboy film - a film genre featuring cowboys and the American West
  • Antonym: musical - a film genre characteristic for integration of songs and music

Etymology

From 'western', meaning 'of the West', used in the late 19th to early 20th century to describe stories set in the American frontier.
