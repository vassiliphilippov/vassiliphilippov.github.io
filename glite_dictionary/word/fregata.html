Fregata

1. NOUN

/ friːˈɡeɪtə / (AmE), / friːˈɡɑːtə / (BrE)
A genus of large seabirds known as frigatebirds, characterized by long wings and a deeply forked tail.
genus of seabirds called frigatebirds

Examples

The genus Fregata includes the magnificent frigatebird and several related species.
Biologists classified the specimen under Fregata after analyzing its plumage and wingspan.
The unique features of Fregata make these seabirds fascinating subjects for ornithological study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frigatebird - Large tropical seabird of the family Fregatidae

Etymology

From Latin 'fregata' (frigate), referring to the bird's resemblance to a fast ship.
