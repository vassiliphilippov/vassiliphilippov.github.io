Nelumbo

1. NOUN

/ nəˈlʌm.boʊ / (AmE), / nɪˈlʌm.bəʊ / (BrE)
A genus of aquatic plants known as the true lotuses, with large, showy flowers and broad floating leaves, sometimes placed in the family Nymphaeaceae.
genus of aquatic lotus plants

Examples

Botanists classify the sacred lotus as part of the genus Nelumbo.
Species of Nelumbo are admired for their large, fragrant flowers.
In the pond, you can see both water lilies and Nelumbo plants.

Etymology

From Latin 'nelumbo', adapted from Sinhalese 'nelum', a name for the lotus.

2. NOUN

/ nəˈlʌmboʊ / (AmE), / nɪˈlʌmbəʊ / (BrE)
A genus of aquatic plants known as lotuses, including the sacred lotus and American lotus, sometimes classified in the water lily family (Nymphaeaceae).
genus of aquatic lotuses

Examples

The seeds of the Nelumbo genus are known for their remarkable longevity.
Nelumbo species grow in shallow ponds and slow-moving rivers across Asia and North America.
The sacred lotus, or Nelumbo nucifera, is revered in many religious traditions.

Etymology

From Sinhalese 'nelum' (lotus).
