Proteidae

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈtiːɪdiː / (AmE), / prəʊˈtiːɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of aquatic salamanders known commonly as mud puppies, native to North America and Europe.
family of aquatic salamanders (mud puppies)

Examples

The Proteidae are often studied for their unique respiratory systems.
Members of the Proteidae family remain aquatic throughout their entire lives.
Biologists discovered a new species within the Proteidae in Eastern Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mudpuppy - a common name for members of Proteidae, a large aquatic salamander

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Proteus + -idae (family suffix in zoological taxonomy).

2. NOUN

/ proʊˈtiːɪˌdi / (AmE), / prəʊˈtiːɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of aquatic salamanders, commonly known as mudpuppies, found in North America and parts of Europe and characterized by external gills and an entirely aquatic lifestyle.
a family of aquatic salamanders (mudpuppies)

Examples

The Proteidae are well known for their retention of external gills throughout their lives.
Biologists studied the habitats of several species within the Proteidae family in North American rivers.
Unlike many amphibians, members of the Proteidae remain aquatic from birth to adulthood.

Etymology

From New Latin Proteus, the type genus, after the Greek sea god Proteus.
