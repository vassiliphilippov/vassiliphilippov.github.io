patronage

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪtrənɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpætrənɪdʒ / (BrE)
the support or business provided by customers to a shop, restaurant, or other commercial establishment
business or support from customers

Examples

Thank you for your patronage; we appreciate your continued support of our business.
Since opening last year, the café has enjoyed steady patronage from local residents.
The theatre relies on the patronage of its loyal audience to survive.

Synset Examples

even before noon there was a considerable patronage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: custom - the habitual patronage of a particular shop or business

Etymology

From Latin 'patronatus', meaning 'right of a patron'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪtrənɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpætrənɪdʒ / (BrE)
The power or practice of giving government jobs, contracts, or other favors to supporters, especially in return for political support.
political favors or appointments in exchange for support

Examples

The mayor was accused of using patronage to reward his loyal campaign workers.
A new administration promised to end patronage in government hiring and contracts.
Senator Smith benefited from the state's long tradition of political patronage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cronyism - the appointment of friends and associates to positions of authority, without proper regard to their qualifications
  • Antonym: meritocracy - a system in which appointments or selections are based on ability and merit rather than favoritism

Etymology

From Latin *patronatus* (protection, guardianship), from *patronus* (patron).

4. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪtrənɪdʒ, ˈpætrənɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpætrənɪdʒ / (BrE)
the support, encouragement, or financial aid that an individual or organization bestows to another
support or sponsorship by a patron

Examples

The museum relies on the patronage of local businesses to fund its exhibitions.
Without royal patronage, many artists would have struggled to survive in that era.
Her patronage of the arts has made her well-known in cultural circles.

Synset Examples

his vigorous backing of the conservatives got him in trouble with progressives

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - the act of helping or backing someone or something
  • Synonym: sponsorship - the position of being a sponsor; financial or other support
  • Antonym: opposition - active resistance or dissent; contrast to support

Etymology

From Latin 'patronatus' (protection, office of a patron), from 'patronus' (patron).

5. VERB

/ ˈpeɪtrənˌeɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpeɪtrənɪdʒ / (BrE)
(formal) To support, sponsor, or act as a patron to a person, group, or activity.
to act as a patron or sponsor

Examples

The wealthy family chose to patronage several promising young artists in the city.
During the festival, the council agreed to patronage art programs for local schools.
The organization hoped the celebrities would patronage their fundraising events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sponsor - to support financially or through resources
  • Synonym: support - to give assistance or backing

Etymology

From Old French 'patronage', from Medieval Latin 'patronagium', from 'patronus' (patron).
