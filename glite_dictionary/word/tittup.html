to tittup

1. VERB

/ ˈtɪt.ʌp / (AmE), / ˈtɪt.ʌp / (BrE)
to move or walk with a lively, bouncing, or proud gait, often to attract attention or impress others
walk or move with a lively, proud gait

Examples

The little girl tittupped down the path, her ribbons bouncing in her hair.
He tittupped into the meeting room, eager to make an entrance.
The ponies tittupped through the field, their manes bobbing with each step.

Synset Examples

He struts around like a rooster in a hen house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strut - walk with a stiff, erect, and apparently arrogant gait
  • Synonym: swagger - walk or behave in a very confident and typically arrogant or aggressive way

Etymology

Imitative origin; originally referring to a lively movement, especially of horses, in the 18th century.
