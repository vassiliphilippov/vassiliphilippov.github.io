lightheaded

1. ADJ

/ ˌlaɪtˈhɛdɪd / (AmE), / ˌlaɪtˈhɛdɪd / (BrE)
feeling weak or dizzy, as though you might faint; not fully conscious.
feeling dizzy or faint

Examples

I felt lightheaded after standing up too quickly.
She became lightheaded from not eating breakfast.
The hot room made everyone feel a little lightheaded.

Synset Examples

suddenly felt faint from the pain
was sick and faint from hunger
felt light in the head
a swooning fit
light-headed with wine
light-headed from lack of sleep

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dizzy - feeling as if everything is spinning around
  • Synonym: faint - close to losing consciousness

2. ADJ

/ ˈlaɪtˌhɛdɪd / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtˌhɛdɪd / (BrE)
Not serious or sensible; inclined to act in a frivolous or silly way.
not serious; frivolous or silly

Examples

She can be a bit lightheaded when it comes to important decisions.
The movie is perfect for anyone in a lightheaded mood who just wants to laugh.
Their lightheaded behavior embarrassed their more mature friends.

Synset Examples

a dizzy blonde
light-headed teenagers
silly giggles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silly - lacking in seriousness or good sense; frivolous or foolish
  • Antonym: serious - acting or speaking earnestly, without frivolity

Etymology

From 'light' (not heavy, frivolous) + 'headed'.
