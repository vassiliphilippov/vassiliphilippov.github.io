lacunar

1. NOUN

/ ləˈkjuːnər / (AmE), / ləˈkjuːnə / (BrE)
a sunken panel or decorative recessed panel in a ceiling or dome, often ornately designed
ornamental sunken ceiling panel

Examples

The ornate lacunar in the cathedral's dome drew the tourists' attention.
Renaissance architects often favored a richly decorated lacunar in their ceiling designs.
Painted scenes adorned each lacunar of the grand council hall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coffer - sunken panel in a ceiling or dome

Etymology

From Latin 'lacunar' (panelled ceiling), from 'lacuna' (hollow, cavity, pit).
