phonologist

1. NOUN

/ foʊˈnɑːlədʒɪst / (AmE), / fəˈnɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
a person who specializes in the study of phonology, the systematic organization of sounds in languages
specialist in phonology

Examples

The conference attracted a prominent phonologist from the university.
As a trained phonologist, she researches how children acquire sound systems in language.
Questions about vowel shifts were best answered by the department's resident phonologist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phonetician - a specialist in phonetics, the study of speech sounds

Etymology

From Greek phōnē (sound, voice) + -logist (one who studies a subject)
