treasonable

1. ADJ

/ ˈtriːzənəbəl / (AmE), / ˈtriːzənəbl / (BrE)
Showing or involving the betrayal of one's country; constituting or characteristic of treason.
relating to or involving treason

Examples

The judge declared the defendant's actions to be treasonable and a threat to national security.
She was accused of spreading treasonable material during a time of war.
His treasonable activities were discovered by the intelligence agency.

Synset Examples

the faithless Benedict Arnold
a lying traitorous insurrectionist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: traitorous - behaving like a traitor; betraying trust or allegiance
  • Synonym: faithless - disloyal or untrustworthy
  • Antonym: loyal - showing firm and constant support or allegiance

Etymology

From Old French *traisonable*, from *traison* (treason), from Latin *traditionem* (betrayal).
