purchasable

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɝː.tʃəs.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.tʃə.sə.bəl / (BrE)
able to be bought or acquired through payment; available for purchase
able to be bought

Examples

Many of the artworks in the gallery are purchasable by interested collectors.
The software you are using is not purchasable anymore; it has been discontinued.
Only purchasable items can be added to your online shopping cart.

Synset Examples

purchasable goods
many houses in the area are for sale

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buyable - able to be bought or purchased
  • Antonym: unavailable - not available for purchase or use

Etymology

From purchase + -able, modeled on Latin-derived forms ending in -able.

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɜrtʃəsəbəl / (AmE), / ˈpɜːtʃəsəbl / (BrE)
(of a person, position, or honor) susceptible to being bribed or corrupted; able to be bought in a dishonorable way.
capable of being corrupted or bribed
archaic

Examples

It was widely believed that the purchasable official had accepted bribes to overlook the violations.
Critics accused the entire committee of being purchasable and lacking integrity.
She refused to work in a system where judgments were purchasable rather than based on justice.

Synset Examples

corruptible judges
dishonest politicians
a purchasable senator
a venal police officer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corruptible - easily bribed or morally corrupt
  • Synonym: venal - willing to behave dishonestly in exchange for money
  • Antonym: incorruptible - not susceptible to corruption or bribery

Etymology

From purchase + -able, originally meaning 'able to be bought', later also 'able to be bribed'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00623826-s
Synset POS: s
