to chivy

1. VERB

/ ˈtʃɪvi / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪvi / (BrE)
to annoy or pester someone continually; to harass or nag repeatedly
to annoy or pester repeatedly
british

Examples

She wouldn't stop chivying him about his messy room.
He felt chivied by constant demands from his supervisor.
If you chivy her too much, she'll just quit the project.

Synset Examples

He is known to harry his staff when he is overworked
This man harasses his female co-workers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to harass - to disturb or trouble someone persistently
  • Synonym: to pester - to annoy someone with repeated requests or interruptions
  • Synonym: nag - to annoy or criticize someone repeatedly

Etymology

Perhaps from Romani chiv (to throw, to thrust), originally meaning 'to chase'.
