Bombax

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑm.bæks / (AmE), / ˈbɒm.bæks / (BrE)
A genus of large tropical trees, chiefly native to South America, with showy flowers and soft, fibrous wood.
genus of tropical South American trees

Examples

The Bombax tree is admired for its striking red blossoms during the dry season.
Many local communities use fibers from the Bombax for stuffing pillows and mattresses.
Some species of Bombax are important for their ecological value in tropical forests.

Etymology

From Latin *bombax* (cotton), from Greek *bombux* (silkworm, silk).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːm.bæks / (AmE), / ˈbɒm.bæks / (BrE)
a genus of tropical trees, mostly native to South America, known for their large, softwood trunks and showy, often red flowers
genus of tropical South American trees

Examples

The Bombax tree can grow to impressive heights in the forests of Brazil.
Researchers studied the flowers of Bombax to learn about pollination in tropical climates.
Many species within the Bombax genus are valued for their lightweight wood.

Etymology

From Latin Bombax, and Greek bombax (cotton), originally referring to cotton trees.
