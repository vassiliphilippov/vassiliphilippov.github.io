Spiraea

1. NOUN

/ spaɪˈriːə / (AmE), / spaɪˈriːə / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous shrubs in the rose family (Rosaceae), known for their clusters of small white or pink flowers.
genus of flowering shrubs in the rose family

Examples

Spiraea bushes are popular in gardens for their beautiful spring blossoms.
Many landscapers choose Spiraea for borders because of its dense growth and attractive flowers.
The pink flowers of Spiraea provide a splash of color in midsummer.

Etymology

From Latin 'spiraea', from Greek 'speiraia' (plant tied in a wreath), from 'speira' (coil, spiral).
