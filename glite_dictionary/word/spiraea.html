spiraea

1. NOUN

/ spaɪˈriːə / (AmE), / spɪˈreɪə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering shrubs in the rose family, with clusters of small white or pink flowers.
a genus of flowering shrubs

Examples

The spiraea was in full bloom, covering the garden with delicate pink flowers.
Gardeners often use spiraea as a decorative hedge for its attractive blossoms.
She planted a row of spiraea along the fence to add color to her yard.

Etymology

From Latin Spiraea, from Greek speiraia (a plant coiled in a spiral), related to speira (coil).

2. NOUN

/ spəˈriːə / (AmE), / spaɪˈriːə / (BrE)
a shrub of the genus Spiraea, especially varieties cultivated for their dense clusters of small flowers in various colors, often used in gardens and by florists for decorative purposes
a flowering shrub with dense clusters of small flowers

Examples

The garden was lined with blooming spiraea that attracted bees and butterflies.
For Easter arrangements, florists often use forced spiraea branches to add delicate color.
She trimmed the spiraea in late spring to encourage next season's blossoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bridal wreath - a common garden cultivar of Spiraea

Etymology

From modern Latin Spiraea, from Greek speiraia (wreath or coil), referring to the plant's spiral clusters of flowers.
