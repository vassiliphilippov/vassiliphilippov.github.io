cheep

1. VERB

/ tʃip / (AmE), / tʃiːp / (BrE)
(of a young bird) to make a short, high-pitched sound.
make a high-pitched bird sound

Examples

The hungry chicks cheep loudly whenever their mother returns to the nest.
I heard the tiny birds cheeping in the early morning.
Whenever they're frightened, the ducklings cheep in quick, anxious bursts.

Synset Examples

the birds were chirping in the bushes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chirp - make short, sharp, high-pitched sounds (typically of small birds or insects)
  • Synonym: tweet - make a soft, high-pitched, bird-like sound

Etymology

Imitative in origin, representing the sound itself.

2. NOUN

/ tʃip / (AmE), / tʃiːp / (BrE)
the short, weak, high-pitched cry made by a young bird
the weak cry of a young bird

Examples

The tiny chick let out a faint cheep as it searched for its mother.
We heard the cheep of sparrow nestlings coming from the hedge.
Every morning, the first cheep from the nest signaled the start of a new day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chirp - a short, sharp sound made by a small bird or insect
  • Synonym: peep - a short, high-pitched sound, especially by a young bird

Etymology

Imitative of the sound itself, similar to Middle English chepen.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07393694-n
Synset POS: n
