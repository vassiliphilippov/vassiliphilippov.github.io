medic

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛdɪk / (AmE), / ˈmɛdɪk / (BrE)
a person trained to provide medical care, especially a member of the armed forces who treats wounded soldiers in the field
military medical practitioner

Examples

The wounded soldier was quickly attended to by the medic on the battlefield.
When the alarm sounded, the medic rushed to help the injured.
After training, she was assigned as a medic with the infantry unit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doctor - a person qualified to practice medicine

Etymology

From Latin 'medicus' (physician).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛdɪk / (AmE), / ˈmɛdɪk / (BrE)
any of several plants of the genus Medicago, native to the Old World, with small flowers and trifoliate leaves.
a plant of the Medicago genus

Examples

Several species of medic are commonly grown as forage crops.
The leaves of the medic resemble those of clover.
Farmers often plant medic to improve soil fertility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alfalfa - a leguminous plant (Medicago sativa) also known as lucerne, related species

Etymology

From genus name Medicago, from Latin mēdicāgo (alfalfa).
