YHWH

1. PROPN

/ jɑːˈweɪ / (AmE), / jɑːˈweɪ / (BrE)
a transliterated name for God in the Hebrew Bible, often rendered from the Hebrew consonants YHVH
the Hebrew name for God in the Old Testament

Examples

In ancient Hebrew texts, YHWH is used as the sacred name of God.
Scholars debate the proper pronunciation of YHWH since the original vowels were not written.
Translations of the Bible often replace YHWH with 'the LORD' out of reverence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yahweh - alternative form and common vocalization of YHWH, name of God in the Old Testament
  • Synonym: Jehovah - another vocalized form of the tetragrammaton, name of God in Christianity

Etymology

From ancient Hebrew tetragrammaton YHVH, traditionally unpronounced; also rendered as Yahweh.
