soapwort

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊpˌwɜrt / (AmE), / ˈsəʊpˌwɜːt / (BrE)
a European plant with pink or white flowers, whose leaves produce a soap-like substance when crushed
plant with cleansing leaves and pink/white flowers

Examples

Many gardeners grow soapwort for its attractive blooms and practical use as a natural cleanser.
The leaves of soapwort can be crushed to make a gentle detergent for delicate fabrics.
She identified the wildflower in the field as soapwort by its pale pink petals.

Etymology

From 'soap' + 'wort', where 'wort' is an Old English word for plant or herb.
