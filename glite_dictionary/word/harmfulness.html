harmfulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrmfəlnɪs / (AmE), / ˈhɑːmfəlnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of causing or likely to cause harm or damage.
the quality of being harmful

Examples

There is increasing concern about the harmfulness of plastic waste to marine life.
Regulations are needed to limit the harmfulness of industrial emissions.
Doctors warned patients about the harmfulness of smoking.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: harmlessness - the quality of not causing harm

Etymology

Formed from 'harmful' + '-ness', a common English noun-forming suffix.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrmf(ə)lnəs / (AmE), / ˈhɑːmf(ə)lnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being likely to cause harm or injury.
quality of causing harm

Examples

The harmfulness of the chemical was confirmed by several independent studies.
Parents are concerned about the harmfulness of certain online content to children.
Research is ongoing to assess the harmfulness of air pollution in urban areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dangerousness - the quality of being likely to cause danger or injury
  • Antonym: harmlessness - the quality of not causing harm or injury

Etymology

From 'harmful' + '-ness', with 'harmful' from 'harm' (Old English hearm) + '-ful'.
