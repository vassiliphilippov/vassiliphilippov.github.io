Chelifer cancroides

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɛlɪfər kænˈkrɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / ˌkɛlɪfə kænˈkrɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A species of tiny pseudoscorpion sometimes found in old books and papers.
species of tiny pseudoscorpion

Examples

The Chelifer cancroides can often be found in dusty libraries feeding on booklice.
Scientists discovered a Chelifer cancroides specimen hidden among the pages of an old manuscript.
Although harmless to humans, Chelifer cancroides play a crucial role in controlling other insect pests.

Etymology

From scientific Latin: genus Chelifer + species epithet cancroides (crab-like).
