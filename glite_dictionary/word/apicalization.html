apicalization

1. NOUN

/ ˌæpɪkəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌæpɪkələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
(Phonetics) The articulation of a sound, typically a consonant, by using the tip of the tongue to obstruct the airflow.
articulation with tip of the tongue (phonetics)

Examples

In some languages, apicalization can distinguish between two different consonants.
The process of apicalization involves raising the tongue tip to touch the alveolar ridge.
Researchers noted a marked increase in apicalization among speakers of the regional dialect.

Synset Examples

Concerning the phonological factors, both a coronal consonant and a long vowel as the preceding segment categorically block apicalization in monomorphemic words.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: laminalization - articulation using the blade of the tongue (phonetics)

Etymology

From 'apical' (relating to the apex or tip) plus '-ization'.
