Arabis glabra

1. NOUN

/ ˈær.ə.bɪs ˈɡlæb.rə / (AmE), / ˈær.ə.bɪs ˈɡlæb.rə / (BrE)
an erect species of cress, widely distributed throughout Europe, also known as tower mustard
a European species of cress (tower mustard)

Examples

The wildflower enthusiast identified the tall, slender plant as Arabis glabra during the nature walk.
Arabis glabra is valued by some gardeners for its delicate yellow flowers and upright form.
Extensive fields of Arabis glabra can be seen blooming along roadsides in late spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tower mustard - common name for Arabis glabra, a European cress

Etymology

From Latin: 'Arabis' (the plant genus), 'glabra' meaning 'smooth' or 'hairless'.
