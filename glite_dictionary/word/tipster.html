tipster

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪpstər / (AmE), / ˈtɪpstə / (BrE)
a person who gives or sells advice or information about gambling or financial speculation, especially at racetracks
person who gives gambling advice

Examples

The tipster claimed he had inside information about the favorite horse.
Many gamblers rely on a tipster to improve their odds at the track.
Police arrested the tipster for selling betting advice without a license.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: informant - person who provides information, often confidentially

Etymology

Formed from 'tip' (an item of advice) + '-ster' (denoting a person).
