unwillingness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈwɪlɪŋnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈwɪlɪŋnɪs / (BrE)
the state or quality of not being willing to do something; reluctance or lack of readiness to act
reluctance; lack of willingness

Examples

Her unwillingness to apologize made it difficult to resolve the conflict.
The company's unwillingness to change its policies led to protests from employees.
Despite his unwillingness to participate, he was eventually persuaded by his friends.

Synset Examples

his unwillingness to cooperate vetoed every proposal I made
in spite of our warnings he plowed ahead with the involuntariness of an automaton

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reluctance - the state of being unwilling or disinclined to do something
  • Antonym: willingness - the quality of being ready and eager to do something

Etymology

From 'un-' (not) + 'willingness', derived from Middle English 'wille' (will, desire).
