flier

1. NOUN

/ ˈflaɪər / (AmE), / ˈflaɪə / (BrE)
a person who operates or flies an aircraft, especially as a pilot
someone who flies an aircraft

Examples

The experienced flier navigated through the storm with ease.
She dreamed of becoming a commercial flier since childhood.
Many military fliers received honors for their bravery in battle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pilot - a person who operates the controls of an aircraft

Etymology

From the verb 'fly' + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈflaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈflaɪə / (BrE)
a person who travels by airplane; an air traveler
someone who travels by airplane

Examples

The frequent flier program rewards loyal customers with air miles.
As an experienced flier, she always packs lightly for her trips.
Each flier received a complimentary beverage during the flight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: passenger - a person who is traveling in a vehicle but is not operating it

3. NOUN

/ ˈflaɪər / (AmE), / ˈflaɪə / (BrE)
a small printed notice, advertisement, or leaflet intended for wide distribution, usually to publicize an event, product, or service
advertising leaflet for wide distribution

Examples

They handed out fliers advertising the grand opening of the new café.
A colorful flier was taped to the lamp post, announcing a neighborhood garage sale.
Did you see the fliers about the upcoming concert in the park?

Synset Examples

he mailed the circular to all subscribers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flyer - alternative spelling of 'flier', meaning a leaflet or advertising sheet
