ramification

1. NOUN

/ ˌræməfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌræmɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a structure made up of branches or branching parts; a branching arrangement
branching structure or arrangement

Examples

The river delta's complex ramification created a rich habitat for wildlife.
The ramification of veins in the leaf is clearly visible under a microscope.
We studied the ramification of roots in different soil types.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: branch - a part of a tree which grows out from the trunk or from a bough

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *ramificatio* (branching), from Latin *ramus* (branch) + *facere* (to make).

2. NOUN

/ ˌræm.ə.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌræm.ɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of branching out or dividing into branches; a branch or offshoot that stems from a main structure
branching or division into branches

Examples

The ramification of the tree was clearly visible as its limbs spread in all directions.
Scientists studied the ramification of the plant's roots underground.
The intricate ramification of the river into smaller streams provided habitats for many species.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *ramificatio* (branching), from Latin *ramus* (branch) + *-ficare* (to make).

3. NOUN

/ ˌræməfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌræmɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a complex or unwelcome consequence of an action or decision, especially one that makes a situation more complicated
complicating consequence or result

Examples

The new law could have serious ramifications for small businesses across the country.
She did not consider the possible ramifications of her decision before acting.
Political leaders are worried about the economic ramifications of the trade sanctions.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consequence - a result or effect of an action or condition
  • Synonym: result - something that happens because of something else
  • Antonym: cause - a reason for an action or condition; the opposite of a result

Etymology

From French ramification, from Latin ramificare ‘to form branches’, from ramus ‘branch’.
