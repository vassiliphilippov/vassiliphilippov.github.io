Torpediniformes

1. NOUN

/ tɔrˌpɛdɪˈnɪfərˌiːz / (AmE), / tɔːˌpiːdɪˈnɪfəmiːz / (BrE)
an order of cartilaginous fish comprising the electric rays, with bodies shaped like torpedoes
an order of electric rays (fish)

Examples

Scientists have discovered a new species within the Torpediniformes that has unique electric organs.
The Torpediniformes are well known for their ability to generate electric shocks.
Many members of the Torpediniformes order can be found in warm and temperate Oceans around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ray - a type of cartilaginous fish with flattened bodies and enlarged pectoral fins

Etymology

From Latin *torpedo* (numbness, also a type of ray) + *-iformes* (having the form of).
