naturalness

1. NOUN

/ ˈnætʃrəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈnatʃrəl.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being natural, genuine, or in accordance with nature or natural principles
quality of being natural or genuine

Examples

The naturalness of her laughter made everyone feel at ease.
Many admired the naturalness of the garden's design, which blended seamlessly with the landscape.
He spoke with such naturalness that the audience trusted him instantly.

Synset Examples

he accepted the naturalness of death
the spontaneous naturalness of his manner

Related Terms

  • Antonym: artificiality - the quality of being artificial or unnatural
  • Synonym: genuineness - the quality of being genuine or authentic

Etymology

From 'natural' + '-ness', forming a noun indicating the state or quality.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnætʃrəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈnatʃrəlnəs / (BrE)
the quality of behaving in a simple, innocent, or naive manner, free from artificiality or affectation
innocent naivete or simplicity

Examples

Children often have a naturalness that makes their questions both charming and profound.
Her naturalness during the interview made everyone feel at ease.
There was a disarming naturalness in the way he greeted new people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: innocence - lack of experience or guile; purity
  • Antonym: artificiality - the quality of being artificial or affected

3. NOUN

/ ˈnætʃrəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈnatʃrəlnəs / (BrE)
the quality of a representation or performance being like or true to nature or reality
being lifelike or true to reality

Examples

The director worked hard to capture the naturalness of everyday conversation in the film.
Audio engineers value naturalness when mixing sounds for virtual reality environments.
Her acting impressed critics with its exceptional naturalness and emotional depth.

Synset Examples

engineers strove to increase the naturalness of recorded music

Related Terms

  • Synonym: realism - the quality of representing a subject faithfully
  • Antonym: artificiality - the quality of being artificial, not natural

Etymology

Formed from natural + -ness; 'natural' from Latin naturalis (by birth, according to nature).
