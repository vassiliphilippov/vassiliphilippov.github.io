snooty

1. ADJ

/ ˈsnuːti / (AmE), / ˈsnuːti / (BrE)
behaving in a way that suggests you think you are better than other people; arrogant or conceited, especially in a snobbish way
arrogant or snobbish
informal

Examples

She gave the waiter a snooty look and complained about the service.
Some people think private schools turn students into snooty adults.
He sounded a bit snooty when he described his favorite wine.

Synset Examples

a snotty little scion of a degenerate family
they're snobs — stuck-up and uppity and persnickety

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snobbish - having or showing the attitude of people who think they are better than others
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low view of one's own importance

Etymology

Probably formed as a variant of 'snout', via 'snoot' (nose); developed in American English in early 20th century.
