elation

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈleɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
A feeling of great happiness and excitement, often accompanied by pride or optimism.
great happiness and excitement

Examples

She was filled with elation after receiving the good news about her promotion.
Winning the championship brought a sense of pure elation to the entire team.
His face radiated elation as he stepped onto the awards stage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joy - a feeling of great pleasure and happiness
  • Antonym: depression - a state of low mood or sadness
  • Degree Higher: ecstasy - an overwhelming feeling of great happiness
  • Degree Lower: contentment - a state of satisfaction or happiness

Etymology

From Latin elatio, elationis (a lifting up), from efferre (to lift up).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a feeling of great joy, pride, or high spirits
feeling of great joy or pride

Examples

Winning the championship filled him with elation that lasted for days.
She could not hide her elation when she heard the good news.
The crowd erupted in elation as their team scored the final goal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joy - a feeling of great happiness
  • Degree Higher: ecstasy - an overwhelming feeling of great happiness or joyful excitement
  • Synonym: pride - a feeling of deep pleasure or satisfaction derived from achievements
  • Antonym: depression - a state of severe despondency

Etymology

From Latin 'elatus', past participle of 'efferre' (to raise up, exalt).
