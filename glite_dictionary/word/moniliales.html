Moniliales

1. NOUN

/ moʊˌnɪliˈeɪliz / (AmE), / məʊˌnɪlɪˈɑːliːz / (BrE)
An order of imperfect fungi characterized by the absence of conidiophores or by the presence of superficial conidiophores not enclosed in a pycnidium.
order of imperfect fungi
medical

Examples

Many plant diseases are caused by fungi in the Moniliales order.
Research on Moniliales has advanced our understanding of fungal reproduction.
The classification of Moniliales has evolved with new genetic information.

Etymology

From Latin *monile* (necklace), referring to the bead-like chains of spores produced by some species.
