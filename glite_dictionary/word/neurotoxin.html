neurotoxin

1. NOUN

/ ˈnʊroʊˌtɑksɪn / (AmE), / ˈnjʊərəʊˌtɒksɪn / (BrE)
A toxin that is poisonous or destructive to nerve tissue.
toxin affecting nerve tissue
medical

Examples

Some snake venoms contain a potent neurotoxin that can cause paralysis.
Exposure to the neurotoxin led to a rapid decline in the patient's motor functions.
Scientists are developing antidotes to counteract neurotoxin effects in cases of poisoning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toxin - a poisonous substance produced by living cells

Etymology

From neuro- (relating to nerves) + toxin (poison), from Greek 'neuron' (nerve) and 'toxikon' (poison).
