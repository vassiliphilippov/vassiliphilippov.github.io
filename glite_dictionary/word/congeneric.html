congeneric

1. ADJ

/ ˌkɑːn.dʒəˈner.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.dʒəˈner.ɪk / (BrE)
Belonging to the same genus as another organism or group of organisms.
belonging to the same genus

Examples

The two species are congeneric and share many morphological features.
In his research, he compared several congeneric plants to determine evolutionary relationships.
Some diseases affect only congeneric hosts within the same family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: congener - an organism of the same genus as another

Etymology

From Latin congener (of the same kind), from com- (together) + genus (kind, race).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑn.dʒəˈnɛr.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.dʒəˈnɛr.ɪk / (BrE)
An animal or plant belonging to the same genus as another organism.
organism of the same genus

Examples

The two species are considered congenerics as they both belong to the same genus.
Researchers compared the behaviors of congenerics to understand evolutionary differences.
Many congenerics share similar physical traits due to their common ancestry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: congener - an organism in the same genus as another

Etymology

From Latin 'congener', from 'con-' (together) + 'genus' (kind, race).
