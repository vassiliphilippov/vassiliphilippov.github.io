weatherstrip

1. VERB

/ ˈwɛðərˌstrɪp / (AmE), / ˈwɛðəˌstrɪp / (BrE)
to fit or equip (a door, window, etc.) with weatherstripping to prevent air, rain, or cold from entering
fit with material to block air, water, etc.

Examples

We decided to weatherstrip all the windows before winter arrived.
It is important to weatherstrip your doors to save on heating costs.
The contractor will weatherstrip the basement to prevent drafts.

Synset Examples

The window must be weatherstripped before the cold weather sets in

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seal - to close tightly so that air or liquid cannot enter or escape

Etymology

Formed from 'weather' + 'strip', referring to material used to block weather effects.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɛðərˌstrɪp / (AmE), / ˈwɛðəˌstrɪp / (BrE)
A narrow strip of material installed to cover the joint of a door or window, preventing the entry of cold air, moisture, or dust.
strip that seals windows or doors

Examples

We decided to replace the old weatherstrip around our front door to keep the drafts out.
A good weatherstrip can help reduce heating costs during the winter months.
The windows were leaking until a weatherstrip was installed along their edges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sealant - substance used to block passage of fluids through joints
  • Synonym: draught excluder - British term for a device to block cold air at the bottom of a door

Etymology

Formed from 'weather' and 'strip', reflecting its function as a strip protecting against weather.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04575156-n
Synset POS: n
