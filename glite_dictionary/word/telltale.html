telltale

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɛlˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈtɛlˌteɪl / (BrE)
(especially of a sign, object, or part) revealing or indicating something that is hidden or has happened, often unintentionally.
revealing hidden information or evidence

Examples

The detective noticed the telltale fingerprints on the window.
A telltale sign of water damage is the musty smell in the basement.
Her telltale blush revealed that she was embarrassed.

Synset Examples

a telling smile
a telltale panel of lights
a telltale patch of oil on the water marked where the boat went down

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revealing - making something known that was hidden
  • Antonym: concealing - keeping something hidden or secret

Etymology

From the verb phrase 'tell tale' (to reveal a secret), in use since the 16th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛlˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈtɛl.teɪl / (BrE)
a person, especially a child, who reveals secrets or informs on others, often indiscreetly or out of spite
person who reveals secrets or informs on others

Examples

No one wanted to play with the telltale who always ran to the teacher with gossip.
She accused her little brother of being a telltale after he revealed her secret.
In the playground, being called a telltale was considered an insult among the children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: informer - a person who informs, especially to authorities
  • Synonym: snitch - a person who betrays others by disclosing secrets or informing

Etymology

Compound of 'tell' (to narrate) and 'tale' (story or gossip), originally in reference to someone who tells tales.
