daredeviltry

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛrˌdɛvəltri / (AmE), / ˈdɛəˌdɛvɪltri / (BrE)
reckless or bold behavior, especially that which is daring and risky
reckless daring behavior

Examples

The stuntman's daredeviltry amazed the audience, but some called it foolish.
She admired his daredeviltry but worried about his safety.
The novel is full of daredeviltry and high-speed chases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recklessness - the trait of acting without considering the risk
  • Antonym: caution - careful attention to avoid danger or risk

Etymology

From 'daredevil' (a recklessly daring person) + '-try' (forming nouns denoting behavior or action).
