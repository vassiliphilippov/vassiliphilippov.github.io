Camassia scilloides

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmæsiə skɪˈlɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / kəˈmæsɪə skɪˈlɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A North American flowering plant, also known as eastern camas, native to eastern and central United States and valued for its edible bulbs.
a North American plant; eastern camas

Examples

The bulbs of Camassia scilloides were once an important food source for Native Americans.
Camassia scilloides blooms in the spring with tall spikes of pale blue flowers.
Gardeners appreciate Camassia scilloides for its attractive blossoms and hardiness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: camas - any of several plants of the genus Camassia, with edible bulbs

Etymology

From genus name Camassia (from Native American origins referring to edible bulbs) and Latin species name scilloides ('resembling Scilla').
