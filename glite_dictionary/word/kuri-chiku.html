kuri-chiku

1. NOUN

/ kuːriːˈtʃiːkuː / (AmE), / kuːriːˈtʃiːkuː / (BrE)
A type of small bamboo with thin green stems that turn shiny black as they mature, commonly cultivated for its ornamental value.
small bamboo with green culms turning black

Examples

The garden's centerpiece was a cluster of kuri-chiku, their black stems gleaming in the sun.
Many landscapers use kuri-chiku for its striking color contrast in modern designs.
After a few years, the kuri-chiku in her yard developed beautiful, shining black culms.

Etymology

From Japanese 'kuri-chiku', meaning 'chestnut bamboo'.
