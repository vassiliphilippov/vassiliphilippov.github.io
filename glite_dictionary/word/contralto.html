contralto

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtræltoʊ / (AmE), / kənˈtræl.təʊ / (BrE)
a woman singer with the lowest female adult singing voice, or the range of that voice
lowest female adult singing voice

Examples

The lead role was sung by a renowned contralto with a rich, deep tone.
Many famous operas have challenging parts written specifically for the contralto voice.
She trained as a soprano but later discovered her true range was contralto.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: alto - a voice or singer with the range just above contralto
  • Degree Higher: soprano - highest female singing voice

Etymology

From Italian 'contralto', from 'contra-' (against) + 'alto' (high).

2. NOUN

/ kənˈtræltoʊ / (AmE), / kənˈtræl.təʊ / (BrE)
the lowest adult female singing voice, or a singer who has such a voice
lowest female singing voice

Examples

The choir's contralto added a rich depth to the harmony.
She is recognized as a leading contralto in the world of opera.
Many famous arias are written specifically for the contralto voice.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: soprano - the highest female singing voice
  • Degree Higher: mezzo-soprano - a female singing voice between soprano and contralto

Etymology

From Italian 'contralto', from 'contra-' (against) + 'alto' (high), referring to singing the part below the alto.

3. ADJ

/ kənˈtræltoʊ / (AmE), / kənˈtræl.təʊ / (BrE)
(of a voice or singer) having the lowest range of the female voice.
having the lowest female vocal range

Examples

Her contralto voice lent a rich depth to the choir's performance.
The lead character was portrayed by a singer with a powerful contralto tone.
Critics praised her unique, contralto timbre, which stood out among the cast.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: soprano - the highest female singing voice
  • Degree Higher: mezzo-soprano - the female voice between soprano and contralto

Etymology

From Italian 'contralto', from 'contra-' (against) + 'alto' (high).
