amitriptyline

1. NOUN

/ ˌæməˈtrɪptɪliːn / (AmE), / ˌæmɪˈtrɪptɪliːn / (BrE)
a tricyclic antidepressant drug used to treat depression, chronic pain, and other conditions, known for its potential side effects and interactions
tricyclic antidepressant drug
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed amitriptyline to help manage her chronic nerve pain.
Side effects from amitriptyline can include drowsiness and dry mouth in some patients.
Before starting amitriptyline, inform your healthcare provider about any other medications you are taking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antidepressant - a medicine used to treat depression

Etymology

From 'amino' and 'triptyline' (itself from chemical components related to its structure).
