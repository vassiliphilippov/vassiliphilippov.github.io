Silurus glanis

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈlʊrəs ˈɡleɪnɪs / (AmE), / sɪˈlʊərəs ˈɡlaːnɪs / (BrE)
a large species of catfish native to central and eastern Europe, known for its elongated body and impressive size
large catfish species of Europe

Examples

Fishermen in the Danube river are known for catching enormous Silurus glanis.
The Silurus glanis can grow to lengths of more than two meters.
Researchers are studying the habits of the Silurus glanis to understand its impact on local ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wels catfish - common name for Silurus glanis

Etymology

From genus name Silurus (from Latin, meaning 'catfish'), and species name glanis (from Greek γλανίς, another word for catfish).
