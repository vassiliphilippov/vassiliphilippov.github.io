Ti

1. NOUN

/ ti / (AmE), / ti / (BrE)
the chemical symbol for titanium, a light, strong, corrosion-resistant metallic element often used in strong, lightweight alloys
symbol for titanium (chemical element)

Examples

The aerospace industry uses Ti alloys for manufacturing aircraft parts due to their strength and lightness.
The medical implant was made from Ti because of its biocompatibility.
Rutile and ilmenite are the principal ores from which Ti is extracted.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: titanium - a light, strong, corrosion-resistant metallic element (symbol: Ti)

Etymology

Abbreviation of titanium, from New Latin 'Titanium', named after the Titans of Greek mythology.
