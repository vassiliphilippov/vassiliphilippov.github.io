obliqueness

1. NOUN

/ əˈbliːknəs / (AmE), / əˈbliːknɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being not direct, clear, or straightforward in meaning, action, or course; indirectness
quality of being indirect or not straightforward

Examples

Her obliqueness made it difficult for the audience to grasp her point.
I found the obliqueness of his narrative style both intriguing and frustrating.
The obliqueness in her reply suggested she was hiding something.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indirectness - the quality of being not direct
  • Antonym: directness - the quality of being straightforward

Etymology

From oblique + -ness, meaning state or quality.

2. NOUN

/ əˈbliːknəs / (AmE), / əbˈliːknɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being neither parallel nor perpendicular, but at an angle; slanting or inclined position
the state of being at an angle

Examples

The obliqueness of the road made it difficult for drivers to see oncoming traffic.
Artists often use obliqueness in their work to create a sense of depth or movement.
The obliqueness of the sun's rays at dawn casts long shadows across the landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obliquity - the condition of being oblique or at an angle

Etymology

From Latin obliquus (slanting, sideways) + English -ness.
