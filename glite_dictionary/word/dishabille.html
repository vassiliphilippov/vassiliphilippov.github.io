dishabille

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsəˈbiːl / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈbiːl / (BrE)
the state of being only partly or carelessly dressed
state of being partially dressed

Examples

She answered the door in dishabille, clearly not expecting visitors.
The artist painted his subject in a charming state of dishabille.
Wrapped in a robe and slippers, he wandered through the house in morning dishabille.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: negligee - a woman's light dressing gown, often associated with informal or partial dress

Etymology

From French 'déshabillé', past participle of 'déshabiller' (to undress).
