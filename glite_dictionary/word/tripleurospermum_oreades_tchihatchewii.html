Tripleurospermum oreades tchihatchewii

1. NOUN

/ ˌtrɪp.liˌʊr.oʊˈspɜr.məm oʊˈriː.ad.iːz tʃɪ.xɪˈtʃeɪ.wi.aɪ / (AmE), / ˌtrɪp.lɪˌjʊərəʊˈspɜː.məm əʊˈriː.ə.diːz tʃɪ.hætˈtʃeɪ.wiː / (BrE)
A mat-forming perennial herb native to Asia Minor, sometimes included in the genus Matricaria.
a mat-forming perennial herb of Asia Minor

Examples

The rocky hillsides of Anatolia are home to Tripleurospermum oreades tchihatchewii.
Botanists debate whether Tripleurospermum oreades tchihatchewii belongs in the genus Matricaria.
The mat-forming habit of Tripleurospermum oreades tchihatchewii makes it useful for erosion control in its native region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Matricaria - a related genus of flowering plants

Etymology

From scientific Latin, based on Greek roots: 'tripleuro-' (three-ribbed), 'spermum' (seed), and a regional epithet.
