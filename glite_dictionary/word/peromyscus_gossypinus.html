Peromyscus gossypinus

1. NOUN

/ pəˌroʊˈmɪskəs ˌɡɒsɪˈpiːnəs / (AmE), / pɛˌrɒˈmɪskəs ˌɡɒsɪˈpiːnəs / (BrE)
A species of large, dark-colored mouse native to the southeastern United States, commonly known as the cotton mouse.
species of mouse in southeastern US

Examples

Researchers set humane traps to monitor the population of Peromyscus gossypinus in the wetland preserve.
Peromyscus gossypinus prefers habitats with dense ground cover, such as forests and thickets.
Diseases carried by Peromyscus gossypinus can affect both other wildlife species and humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cotton mouse - common name for Peromyscus gossypinus

Etymology

From New Latin, where 'Peromyscus' is from Greek 'pera' (pouch) and 'mys' (mouse); 'gossypinus' derives from Latin 'gossypium' (cotton), referring to its habitat.
