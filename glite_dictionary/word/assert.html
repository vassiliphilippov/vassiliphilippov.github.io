to assert

1. VERB

/ əˈsɝt / (AmE), / əˈsɜːt / (BrE)
to state firmly or insist on having one's opinions or rights recognized
to insist on one's opinions or rights

Examples

It's important to assert your boundaries respectfully in any relationship.
She learned how to assert herself during meetings at work.
If you don't assert your needs, they may go unnoticed.

Synset Examples

Women should assert themselves more!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: claim - to state that something is true, typically without proof
  • Antonym: to deny - to state that something is not true or refuse a request

Etymology

From Latin 'assertus', past participle of 'asserere' (to claim, to affirm).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02378876-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ əˈsɜrt / (AmE), / əˈsɜːt / (BrE)
to state or declare something firmly, confidently, and often forcefully, as a fact or belief
to state firmly and confidently

Examples

The president chose to assert his authority during the meeting.
She constantly asserts that her approach is better than anyone else's.
During the interview, he asserted his innocence clearly and confidently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to declare - to say something officially or emphatically
  • Antonym: to deny - to state that something is not true

Etymology

From Latin 'assertus', past participle of 'asserere' (to claim, to affirm).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01018787-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ əˈsɜrt / (AmE), / əˈsɜːt / (BrE)
to state or declare something confidently and formally as true, especially in a firm or emphatic way
declare confidently as true

Examples

She asserted her innocence repeatedly throughout the trial.
The company asserts that its products are safe and effective.
He calmly asserted that he had never broken the rules.

Synset Examples

Before God I swear I am innocent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to affirm - to state something as true in a confident way
  • Antonym: to deny - to state that something is not true

Etymology

From Latin 'assertus', past participle of 'asserere' (to claim, affirm).

4. VERB

/ əˈsɝːt / (AmE), / əˈsɜːt / (BrE)
to state or declare a fact, opinion, or right confidently and forcefully
state something confidently and forcefully

Examples

She asserted her right to speak at the meeting, despite interruptions.
The company asserts that it provides the best customer service in the industry.
During the debate, he asserted that climate change is the greatest challenge of our time.

Synset Examples

The letter asserts a free society

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to maintain - to state something strongly as if it were true
  • Antonym: to deny - to state that something is not true or to refuse to accept it

Etymology

From Latin 'assertus', past participle of 'asserere' (to claim, lay claim to).
