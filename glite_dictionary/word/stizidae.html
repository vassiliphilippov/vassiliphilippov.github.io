Stizidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪzɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈstɪzɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of large solitary wasps known as cicada killers, which hunt cicadas to feed their larvae.
family of cicada killer wasps

Examples

The Stizidae family includes several species of wasps renowned for preying on cicadas.
Researchers observed that Stizidae construct underground burrows for their larvae.
Farmers often welcome the presence of Stizidae since they control cicada populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cicada killer - a large solitary wasp that hunts and feeds on cicadas

Etymology

From the genus name Stizus, with the zoological family suffix -idae.
