exponential

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛkspəˈnɛnʃəl / (AmE), / ˌɛkspəˈnɛnʃəl / (BrE)
a mathematical function in which an independent variable appears in the exponent, such as f(x) = a^x
mathematical function with a variable as an exponent

Examples

The graph of an exponential rises rapidly as the value of x increases.
Scientists often use an exponential to model population growth.
An exponential differs from a linear function in its rate of change.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: logarithm - the inverse mathematical function of an exponential

Etymology

From Latin exponens, exponentis (present participle of exponere, meaning 'to put forth'). The adjective became a noun in mathematical contexts.

2. ADJ

/ ˌɛk.spəˈnɛn.tʃəl / (AmE), / ˌɛk.spəˈnɛn.tʃəl / (BrE)
relating to or involving exponents; increasing at a rate that becomes more rapid in proportion to the growing total number or size.
relating to exponents or rapid growth

Examples

The population of the city experienced exponential growth over the past decade.
This process follows an exponential curve, doubling in value each year.
The scientist described the trend as exponential, not linear.

Synset Examples

exponential growth

Related Terms

  • Antonym: logarithmic - relating to logarithms

Etymology

From Latin 'exponens' (present participle of 'exponere', meaning 'to put forth').
