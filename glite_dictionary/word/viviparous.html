viviparous

1. ADJ

/ vɪˈvɪpərəs / (AmE), / vɪˈvɪpərəs / (BrE)
(of an animal) bringing forth live young that have developed inside the body rather than laying eggs.
giving birth to live young (not eggs)

Examples

Most mammals are viviparous, giving birth to live offspring rather than laying eggs.
Certain species of sharks are viviparous, with embryos developing internally until birth.
Unlike birds, which are usually oviparous, many reptiles can be viviparous.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: oviparous - producing eggs that hatch outside the body

Etymology

From Latin 'viviparus', from 'vivus' (alive) + 'parere' (to bring forth).
