Carcharhinus limbatus

1. NOUN

/ kɑr.kəˈraɪ.nəs lɪmˈbeɪ.təs / (AmE), / kɑː.kəˈraɪ.nəs lɪmˈbeɪ.təs / (BrE)
a species of shallow-water shark, commonly known as the blacktip shark, characterized by black tips on its fins and found in warm coastal waters worldwide
species of shallow-water shark

Examples

The Carcharhinus limbatus is frequently seen by divers near coral reefs.
Marine biologists track the migration patterns of Carcharhinus limbatus along tropical coastlines.
A female Carcharhinus limbatus can give birth to several live pups at once.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blacktip shark - common name for Carcharhinus limbatus, a shark with black-tipped fins

Etymology

From Latin: genus 'Carcharhinus' meaning 'shark' and species 'limbatus' meaning 'bordered' or 'edged'.
