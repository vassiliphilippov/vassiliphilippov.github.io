Sium

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪ.əm / (AmE), / ˈsiː.əm / (BrE)
a genus of perennial plants found in wet and marshy places in the Northern Hemisphere, commonly known as water parsnips
genus of water parsnip plants

Examples

Sium thrives in marshy areas throughout the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
Many wetland conservationists monitor Sium populations as indicators of ecosystem health.
The roots of some Sium species have historically been used in herbal remedies.

Etymology

From Latin Sium, from Ancient Greek sion (water plant).
