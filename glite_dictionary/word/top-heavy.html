top-heavy

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɑp ˌhɛvi / (AmE), / ˈtɒp ˌhɛvi / (BrE)
Unstable or likely to tip over because of having too much weight in the upper part.
having too much weight at the top

Examples

The bookshelf became top-heavy after she stacked too many books on the upper shelves.
That old truck is top-heavy, so take the turns slowly to avoid tipping it over.
A top-heavy cake can easily collapse if it isn't supported properly.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bottom-heavy - having most of the weight in the lower part

2. ADJ

/ ˈtɑpˌhɛvi / (AmE), / ˈtɒpˌhɛvi / (BrE)
Having too much weight or mass at the top, so as to be unbalanced or likely to fall over; also, figuratively, feeling unsteady or dizzy, especially from too much alcohol, or imbalanced in composition.
having excessive weight at the top; unbalanced

Examples

The bookshelf was top-heavy and threatened to tip over if not secured to the wall.
After several strong drinks, she felt top-heavy and struggled to walk straight.
Their cake was top-heavy with frosting, making it difficult to slice evenly.

Synset Examples

The dessert was delicious but top-heavy with drink.
He felt top-heavy with drink after the party.
The cocktail was top-heavy with drink and lacked balance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imbalanced - not balanced; lacking equilibrium
  • Antonym: bottom-heavy - having more weight at the bottom; the opposite of top-heavy

Etymology

Formed from 'top' + 'heavy', meaning weighted or burdened at the top.
