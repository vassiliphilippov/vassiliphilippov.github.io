guncotton

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌnˌkɑtn̩ / (AmE), / ˈɡʌnˌkɒtn̩ / (BrE)
a highly flammable compound made by treating cotton with nitric acid, used in explosives and lacquers
highly flammable cotton-based explosive

Examples

The factory specializes in producing guncotton for industrial use.
Early military tests showed that guncotton was far more powerful than traditional black powder.
Because of its explosive properties, guncotton must be handled with extreme care.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nitrocellulose - another name for guncotton

Etymology

From English 'gun' + 'cotton', referring to its original use as a propellant and its cotton origin.
