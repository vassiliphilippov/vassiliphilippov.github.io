indictment

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdaɪtmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdaɪtmənt / (BrE)
A formal accusation or charge of wrongdoing or a crime; also, a statement expressing strong disapproval or criticism.
formal charge of wrongdoing or criticism

Examples

The journalist's article was a powerful indictment of government corruption.
Many viewed the report as an indictment of the entire industry.
Her speech was a strong indictment of social inequality.

Synset Examples

the book is an indictment of modern philosophy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charge - a formal accusation of a crime
  • Antonym: acquittal - a formal declaration that someone is not guilty of the crime

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *enditement*, from Old French *enditer* (to accuse, indict).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdaɪtmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdaɪtmənt / (BrE)
A formal written statement by a prosecuting authority charging a person with a criminal offense, typically presented before a court.
formal charge of a crime
legal

Examples

The grand jury returned an indictment against the suspect after reviewing the evidence.
He faced a federal indictment for fraud and embezzlement.
An indictment does not mean a person is guilty; it is merely a formal charge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accusation - a charge or claim that someone has done something illegal or wrong

Etymology

From Old French *enditement*, from Latin *indictare* meaning 'to declare' or 'to accuse'.
