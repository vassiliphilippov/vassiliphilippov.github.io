flavouring

1. NOUN

/ ˈfleɪvərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfleɪvərɪŋ / (BrE)
A substance added to food or drink to enhance or impart a particular taste.
substance added to enhance taste
british

Examples

The chef added a natural flavouring to enhance the taste of the dessert.
Artificial flavouring is often used in processed foods to mimic real fruit tastes.
She prefers to use vanilla extract as a flavouring in her cakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flavoring - substance added to food for taste; American spelling
  • Synonym: spice - an aromatic substance used to flavor food

Etymology

From 'flavour' + suffix -ing, primarily British spelling of 'flavoring'.
