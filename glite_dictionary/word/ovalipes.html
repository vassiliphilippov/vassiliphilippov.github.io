Ovalipes

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvəˌlaɪpiːz / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəˌlaɪpiːz / (BrE)
a genus of swimming crabs in the family Portunidae, found in coastal and estuarine waters in various parts of the world.
a genus of swimming crabs

Examples

The genus Ovalipes includes several species of swimming crabs important to both fisheries and marine ecosystems.
Researchers observed the behavior of Ovalipes in their natural habitat along the sandy shore.
Identification of Ovalipes species relies on specific features of their carapace and legs.

Etymology

From Latin 'ovalis' (oval) and Greek 'pous, podos' (foot), referring to the shape of the crabs' appendages.
