joker

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒoʊkər / (AmE), / ˈdʒəʊkə / (BrE)
a playing card usually marked with the image of a jester, used as a wild card or for special purposes in various card games
playing card with a jester image

Examples

I drew the joker and won the game instantly.
In some games, the joker can substitute for any card needed.
She removed the joker from the deck before they started playing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild card - a card that can represent any other card; often used in the same way as joker
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03607177-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒoʊkər / (AmE), / ˈdʒəʊkə / (BrE)
a person who enjoys telling or playing jokes; someone who frequently makes others laugh with jokes or playful behavior
person fond of telling jokes

Examples

Everyone at the office knows Tom as the joker who always has a funny story to share.
She's such a joker—her quick wit keeps everyone laughing.
Even as a child, he was a natural joker, always pulling pranks on his friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jester - someone whose job is to entertain by telling jokes and acting playfully
  • Synonym: comedian - a professional entertainer who tells jokes or acts comically
  • Antonym: killjoy - a person who spoils the fun of others

Etymology

From joke + -er, first recorded in the 18th century.

/ ˈdʒoʊkər / (AmE), / ˈdʒəʊkə / (BrE)
an inconspicuous clause or element in a document or bill that unexpectedly changes its meaning or effect.
hidden clause altering a document's meaning

Examples

Always read the fine print to avoid being caught by a joker in the agreement.
She thought the contract was straightforward until the joker was revealed in court.

informal
