finicky

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɪnɪki / (AmE), / ˈfɪnɪki / (BrE)
Difficult to please; concerned with or requiring great attention to detail.
Very hard to please; fussy

Examples

Our cat is so finicky that it refuses to eat anything but fresh fish.
She has a finicky taste when it comes to choosing fabrics for her dresses.
The watchmaker's finicky attention to detail ensures high-quality results.

Synset Examples

a finicky eater
fussy about clothes
very particular about how her food was prepared

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fussy - hard to please or very concerned about details
  • Synonym: particular - very concerned about details or standards
  • Antonym: easygoing - relaxed and tolerant in attitude; not fussy

Etymology

Perhaps from 'finick', a variant of 'finical' (fussy), influenced by 'finick' meaning fuss or concern.
