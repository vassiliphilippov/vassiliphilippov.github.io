pentagon

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛn.təˌɡɑn / (AmE), / ˈpɛn.təˌɡɒn / (BrE)
a flat geometric shape with five straight sides and five angles
five-sided polygon

Examples

The artist drew a perfect pentagon on the board with a ruler.
A regular pentagon has five equal sides and five equal angles.
We learned to calculate the area of a pentagon in math class.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: hexagon - a six-sided polygon
  • Degree Lower: quadrilateral - a four-sided polygon

Etymology

From Greek *pentagōnon* ('five-angled'), from *penta-* ('five') and *gōnia* ('angle').
