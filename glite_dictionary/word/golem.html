golem

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊ.ləm / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊ.ləm / (BrE)
(in Jewish folklore) an artificial being, typically made of clay or mud, brought to life by mystical or supernatural means.
animated being from Jewish folklore

Examples

In the ancient tale, the rabbi created a golem to protect the community from danger.
The concept of a golem has inspired many modern stories about artificial life.
Some believe the golem represents the unintended consequences of human ambition.

Etymology

From Hebrew גולם (gōlem), meaning 'formless mass' or 'shapeless body'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊ.ləm / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊ.ləm / (BrE)
A mechanism, automaton, or robot capable of moving automatically, especially one created or brought to life through magical or unnatural means.
automaton or moving mechanism

Examples

The inventor displayed his new golem, which could fetch items on its own.
Science fiction often features a golem that rebels against its creators.
Early depictions of automatons drew inspiration from the legend of the golem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: automaton - a self-operating machine or mechanism
  • Synonym: robot - a machine capable of carrying out complex actions automatically

Etymology

From Hebrew golem (shapeless mass or unformed), in Jewish folklore an animated being created from inanimate matter.

Synset POS: n
