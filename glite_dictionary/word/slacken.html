to slacken

1. VERB

/ ˈslæk.ən / (AmE), / ˈslæk.ən / (BrE)
to become looser, less tight, or less firmly held
become looser or less tight

Examples

As the storm passed, the strong winds began to slacken.
The tension on the rope will slacken if you stop pulling.
After hours of work, his grip on the shovel gradually slackened.

Synset Examples

the rope slackened

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to tighten - to make or become tighter

Etymology

From Old English slæccan (to loosen), related to slack.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00422361-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈslækən / (AmE), / ˈslækən / (BrE)
to make or become less tight, firm, or intense; to reduce tension or pressure
make less tight or firm

Examples

She had to slacken the rope so that the boat could move freely.
As the rain fell harder, he slackened his pace to avoid slipping on the pavement.
The mechanic advised them to slacken the bolts before removing the cover.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to loosen - to make less tight or more relaxed
  • Antonym: to tighten - to make more tight, firm, or tense

Etymology

From Old English 'sleacian', from 'sleac' meaning 'slack, loose'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00422078-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈslækən / (AmE), / ˈslækən / (BrE)
to become slower, less active, or less intense; to decrease in speed or activity
become slower or less active

Examples

After several hours, the wind began to slacken and the sea calmed.
Productivity tends to slacken toward the end of the workday.
His pace noticeably slackened as he climbed the steep hill.

Synset Examples

Production slowed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to slow down - to reduce speed or activity
  • Antonym: to speed up - to increase speed

Etymology

From Old English slæccan (to make slack), influenced by slack (loose, not tight).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00441535-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈslækən / (AmE), / ˈslækən / (BrE)
to make or become less active, intense, or fast
become less active or fast

Examples

He began to slacken his pace as he grew tired from the long walk.
As the rain continued, the workers decided to slacken their efforts and take a short break.
Traffic tends to slacken in the late evening, making it easier to drive across town.

Synset Examples

He slackened his pace as he got tired
Don't relax your efforts now

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slow - to reduce speed; move at a slower rate
  • Antonym: to tighten - to make or become tighter or more tense

Etymology

From Middle English slaken, from Old English slācan (to loosen, to slacken); related to 'slack'.
