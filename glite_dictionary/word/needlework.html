needlework

1. NOUN

/ ˈniːdlwɜrk / (AmE), / ˈniːdlwɜːk / (BrE)
An item or piece created by sewing, embroidery, or similar work using needle and thread.
item created by sewing or embroidery

Examples

Her grandmother's needlework was displayed in a glass frame on the wall.
The shop sells beautiful handmade needlework from local artisans.
She spent hours perfecting every detail in her latest piece of needlework.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embroidery - decorative needlework with thread or yarn
  • Synonym: sewing - the craft of fastening or attaching objects using stitches

Etymology

From needle + work; 'needle' from Old English 'nædl', and 'work' from Old English 'weorc'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈniːdəlˌwɝːk / (AmE), / ˈniːdəlˌwɜːk / (BrE)
Work such as sewing, embroidery, or similar crafts done with a needle.
sewing or embroidery done by hand

Examples

She displayed her beautiful needlework at the local craft fair.
His grandmother taught him traditional needlework techniques when he was a child.
The museum features an impressive collection of antique needlework.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embroidery - the art of decorating fabric with needle and thread
  • Synonym: sewing - the activity of joining or attaching things with stitches

Etymology

From Middle English nedelwerke, from needle + work.
