truant

1. ADJ

/ ˈtruːənt / (AmE), / ˈtruːənt / (BrE)
absent from duty or school without permission
absent without permission

Examples

The truant student was caught wandering downtown during school hours.
She was called into the principal’s office for her truant behavior.
Many schools have policies to address truant pupils and help them return to class.

Synset Examples

truant schoolboys
the soldier was AWOL for almost a week

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absent - not present in a place

Etymology

From Old French *truant* (beggar, vagabond), of uncertain origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtruːənt / (AmE), / ˈtruːənt / (BrE)
A student who stays away from school without permission.
student absent from school without leave

Examples

The school called his parents after discovering he was a truant.
Habitual truants may face disciplinary action from the school authorities.
Being caught as a truant can lead to serious consequences for students.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absentee - a person who is expected to be somewhere but is not present

Etymology

From Old French *truand* (beggar, vagabond), possibly of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10751389-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈtruːənt / (AmE), / ˈtruːənt / (BrE)
a person, especially a student, who stays away from duty, work, or school without permission
someone who avoids duty or school

Examples

The teacher noticed that one truant had not shown up for class in over a week.
Schools often have policies in place to deal with chronic truants.
As a truant, he spent his days wandering around the city instead of studying.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absentee - a person who is habitually absent from work or school
  • Synonym: shirker - a person who avoids work or duty
  • Antonym: attendee - a person who is present at a place or event

Etymology

From Old French *truand* (beggar, rogue), from Medieval Latin *truan(t)-*, of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10382616-n
Synset POS: n
