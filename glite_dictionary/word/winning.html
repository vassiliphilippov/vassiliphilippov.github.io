winning

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of achieving victory or success, especially when it is gained with difficulty.
the act of achieving victory or success

Examples

He celebrated his first major winning after years of perseverance.
For her, winning meant overcoming many personal obstacles along the way.
Although winning was tough, the taste of success was sweet.

Synset Examples

winning is not everything

Related Terms

  • Synonym: victory - success in a contest or struggle
  • Antonym: loss - the fact of being defeated or losing

Etymology

From the verb 'win', from Old English *winnan* (to struggle, contend, gain).

2. ADJ

/ ˈwɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person or manner) charming, attractive, or endearing; capturing people's interest or affection.
charming or attractive (personality, smile, etc.)

Examples

She gave him a winning smile as she entered the room.
His winning personality made him popular with everyone at the party.
The candidate's winning manner helped her connect with voters.

Synset Examples

a fetching new hairstyle
something inexpressibly taking in his manner
a winning personality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charming - pleasant or attractive in a way that makes people like you
  • Synonym: captivating - attracting the attention or affection; enchanting
  • Antonym: off-putting - unpleasant, disconcerting, or repellent

Etymology

Present participle of 'win'; figurative sense from 'having qualities that win (attract) affection or approval.'
