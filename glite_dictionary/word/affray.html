affray

1. NOUN

/ əˈfreɪ / (AmE), / əˈfreɪ / (BrE)
A public fight or noisy disturbance, especially one that causes alarm or upset.
a noisy public fight or disturbance

Examples

A street affray broke out outside the club, attracting a crowd of bystanders.
The police quickly intervened to stop the affray in the marketplace.
He was arrested and charged with causing an affray during the football match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brawl - a rough or noisy fight, especially in a public place
  • Synonym: melee - a confused fight among a number of people
  • Degree Higher: riot - a violent disturbance of the peace by a crowd

Etymology

From Old French 'affrai', from 'effrayer' (to frighten).

2. NOUN

/ əˈfreɪ / (AmE), / əˈfreɪ / (BrE)
A public incident involving a noisy or violent quarrel, typically resulting in a disturbance of the peace.
public fight or noisy quarrel
legal

Examples

The two men were arrested after their affray outside the nightclub attracted police attention.
An affray broke out in the market, alarming bystanders and shopkeepers alike.
He was fined for participating in an affray during the protest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brawl - a noisy and disorderly fight, usually in public
  • Synonym: disturbance - an interruption of public peace or order

Etymology

From Old French 'afrai' (fright, disturbance) based on 'effrayer' (to frighten), from Latin 'exfridare'.
