mum

1. NOUN

/ mʌm / (AmE), / mʌm / (BrE)
a state of not saying anything about a secret or confidential matter; silence or secrecy
silence; secrecy

Examples

When the subject of the missing funds came up, everyone kept mum.
Remember, mum about the surprise party until Saturday!
He signaled for mum during the confidential meeting.

Synset Examples

mum's the word

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silence - the absence of sound or speech

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English *mum* (silent, as in 'keep mum') imitative of the sound made with closed lips.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04659702-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ mʌm / (AmE), / mʌm / (BrE)
not speaking; remaining silent, especially when expected to speak
not speaking; silent

Examples

He stayed mum about the incident despite repeated questions.
Please keep mum about the surprise party until Friday.
She remained mum during the entire meeting, offering no comments.

Synset Examples

the witness remained silent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silent - not making any sound; not speaking
  • Antonym: talkative - fond of talking; given to talking a lot

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from Middle English 'mum', an imitation of the sound made when keeping one's lips closed.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00503732-s
Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ mʌm / (AmE), / mʌm / (BrE)
(informal, especially British) one’s mother.
informal term for mother
informal british

Examples

My mum always knows how to cheer me up when I'm feeling down.
We're visiting my mum this weekend for her birthday.
I called my mum to tell her the good news.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mom - informal term for mother (American English)
  • Synonym: mother - female parent

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin, similar to 'mamma' and 'mom'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10297825-n
Synset POS: n
