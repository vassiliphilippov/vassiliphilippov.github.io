anaplasia

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.əˈpleɪ.ʒə / (AmE), / ˌan.əˈplɛɪ.zɪ.ə / (BrE)
a loss of structural differentiation in cells, often leading to increased growth and associated with malignancy
loss of cell differentiation (medical)
medical

Examples

The presence of anaplasia in the tumor was a sign of aggressive cancer.
Pathologists look for anaplasia to help determine the prognosis of a malignancy.
Cells showing anaplasia typically lack the normal appearance and function of the original tissue.

Etymology

From Greek 'ana-' (back, again) + 'plasis' (formation).
