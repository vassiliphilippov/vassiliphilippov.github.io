plentifully

1. ADV

/ ˈplɛn.tɪ.fə.li / (AmE), / ˈplɛn.tɪ.fəl.i / (BrE)
In large amounts; in a way that is abundant or more than enough.
in abundance; in large amounts

Examples

Fruit grows plentifully in this region thanks to the fertile soil.
The evidence was plentifully supplied throughout the article.
She decorated the room plentifully with flowers for the celebration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abundantly - In large quantities; plentifully
  • Antonym: meagerly - In small or insufficient amounts

Etymology

From 'plentiful' + '-ly', where 'plentiful' is from Anglo-Norman French 'plenteuf' (from Latin 'plenitudo', fullness).
