Boselaphus tragocamelus

1. NOUN

/ boʊsəˈlæfəs trægoʊkəˈmiːləs / (AmE), / bəʊsɪˈlæfəs træɡəʊkəˈmiːləs / (BrE)
a large antelope native to the Indian subcontinent, commonly known as the nilgai; the male is blue-grey with white markings and the female is brownish and hornless.
large Indian antelope (nilgai)

Examples

The Boselaphus tragocamelus is one of the largest antelopes found in India.
Zookeepers carefully monitored the health of their Boselaphus tragocamelus population.
A male Boselaphus tragocamelus stands out due to its distinctive blue-grey coloration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nilgai - common name for Boselaphus tragocamelus, a large Indian antelope

Etymology

From New Latin: genus 'Boselaphus' (from Latin 'bos', ox, and Greek 'elaphos', deer), species 'tragocamelus' (from Greek 'tragos', goat, and 'kamēlos', camel).
