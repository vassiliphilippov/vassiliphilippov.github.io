Armenia

1. PROPN

/ ɑrˈminiə / (AmE), / ɑːˈmiːniə / (BrE)
a landlocked country in the South Caucasus region of Eurasia, located between Europe and Asia; formerly part of the Soviet Union, with a rich history as one of the world's oldest civilizations.
country in the South Caucasus, Eurasia

Examples

My friend traveled to Armenia last summer to visit ancient monasteries and explore its mountainous landscape.
Many members of the Armenian diaspora maintain strong ties to Armenia through cultural and family traditions.
The cuisine of Armenia is known for its use of fresh herbs, grilled meats, and flavorful breads.

Etymology

From Latin Armenia, from Ancient Greek Armenía.
