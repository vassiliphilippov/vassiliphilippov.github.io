Iliamna

1. NOUN

/ ɪliˈæmnə / (AmE), / ɪliˈamnə / (BrE)
A small genus of perennial herbs or subshrubs in the mallow family, primarily found in North America.
genus of perennial herbs/subshrubs (mallow family)

Examples

The rare Iliamna species blooms in the mountainous regions each spring.
Several conservation groups are working to protect endangered Iliamna plants.
Botanists recently discovered a new population of Iliamna in the wild.

Etymology

Named after Lake Iliamna in Alaska, United States.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪliˈæmnə / (AmE), / ˌɪliˈæmnə / (BrE)
A small genus of perennial herbs or subshrubs in the mallow family, native to North America.
genus of North American mallow plants

Examples

The Iliamna genus includes several attractive wildflower species native to the central United States.
Botanists continue to debate the proper classification of Iliamna within the mallow family.
The tall, pink-flowered Iliamna thrives in sunny prairies and open woodlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mallow - plant family Malvaceae, to which Iliamna belongs

Etymology

Named after Lake Iliamna in Alaska.
