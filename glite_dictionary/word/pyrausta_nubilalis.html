Pyrausta nubilalis

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈrɔstə nuːˈbɪləlɪs / (AmE), / paɪˈraʊstə njuːˈbɪlælɪs / (BrE)
A species of moth native to Europe, whose larvae bore into the stems and crowns of corn and other plants, causing significant agricultural damage.
species of moth harmful to crops

Examples

Pyrausta nubilalis is a significant pest in maize-producing regions of the United States.
Farmers often implement control measures to reduce the impact of Pyrausta nubilalis on their crops.
Research on Pyrausta nubilalis has led to the development of resistant corn varieties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: European corn borer - common name for Pyrausta nubilalis, a major agricultural pest

Etymology

Modern Latin; 'Pyrausta' is the genus, 'nubilalis' is the species epithet.

2. NOUN

/ paɪˈrɔstə ˌnjuːbɪˈleɪlɪs / (AmE), / paɪˈraʊstə ˌnjuːbɪˈleɪlɪs / (BrE)
the larva of the European corn borer moth, a serious pest that damages maize crops
larva of European corn borer moth; maize pest

Examples

Maize fields across the Midwest suffered heavy losses due to infestations of Pyrausta nubilalis.
Researchers are developing new techniques to control Pyrausta nubilalis without harming beneficial insects.
Farmers monitor their crops regularly for signs of Pyrausta nubilalis larvae feeding on corn stalks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: European corn borer - a common name for the pest moth whose larvae attack maize

Etymology

From New Latin: Pyrausta (the genus), from Greek pur (fire) + austos (burnt); nubilalis from Latin nubilus (cloudy).
