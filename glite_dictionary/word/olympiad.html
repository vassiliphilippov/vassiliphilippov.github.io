Olympiad

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɪmpiˌæd / (AmE), / əˈlɪmpiˌad / (BrE)
the modern revival of the ancient Olympic Games, held once every four years in a selected country
modern Olympic Games event

Examples

The 2020 Tokyo Olympiad was postponed due to the pandemic.
Athletes from all over the world strive to compete in the Olympiad.
Each Olympiad showcases both traditional and new sporting events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Olympics - the modern international sporting event held every four years

Etymology

From Greek Olympia (the site of the ancient Olympics) and the suffix -ad (denoting a cycle), referring to the four-year period between Olympic Games.

2. NOUN

/ əˈlɪmpiˌæd / (AmE), / əˈlɪmpɪad / (BrE)
a period of four years between Olympic Games, used as a time measurement especially in ancient Greece
four-year interval between Olympic Games

Examples

The ancient Greeks counted years by the Olympiad, starting from 776 BC.
Historians use the term Olympiad to refer to the interval between successive Olympic Games.
The games were held every fourth year, marking the beginning of a new Olympiad.

Etymology

From Greek *Olympias*, from Olympus, site of the ancient Olympic Games.
