luteal

1. ADJ

/ ˈluːtiəl / (AmE), / ˈluːtɪəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the corpus luteum, a temporary endocrine structure in the ovary
relating to the corpus luteum
medical

Examples

The luteal phase is the latter part of the menstrual cycle, following ovulation.
During the luteal stage, progesterone levels increase to prepare the uterus for pregnancy.
He studied the hormonal changes that occur during the luteal period in mammals.

Etymology

From Latin 'luteus' (yellow), referring to the yellow color of the corpus luteum.
