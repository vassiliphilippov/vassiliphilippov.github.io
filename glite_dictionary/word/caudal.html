caudal

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɔdəl / (AmE), / ˈkɔːdəl / (BrE)
(anatomy, zoology) Situated in or directed toward the tail or hind part of the body.
toward the tail or rear (anatomy)
medical

Examples

The fish's caudal fins help it steer through the water.
The injury was located at the caudal end of the animal's spine.
In some species, coloration varies along the caudal region.

Synset Examples

caudal fins
the caudal end of the body

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cranial - situated toward the head (anatomy)

Etymology

From Latin cauda (tail), with '-al' forming an adjective.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkɑdəl / (AmE), / ˈkɔːdəl / (BrE)
relating to or situated near the tail or posterior end of an organism.
of or near the tail (biology/anatomy)
medical

Examples

The fish’s caudal fin helps it steer and balance in the water.
Damage to the caudal vertebrae can affect an animal’s movement.
The veterinarian noted swelling in the caudal region of the dog’s spine.

  • Antonym: cranial - relating to the head or skull

3. ADV

/ ˈkɔdəl / (AmE), / ˈkɔːdəl / (BrE)
In anatomical terms, toward the rear or tail end of an organism's body.
toward the rear/tail end (anatomy)
medical

Examples

The incision was made caudal to the last rib.
The tumor is located caudal to the lumbar region.
Nerves run caudal along the spinal column to reach the lower limbs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anterior - toward the front of the body (anatomy)
  • Antonym: ventral - toward the belly or lower side (anatomy)
  • Synonym: posterior - toward the back of the body (anatomy)

Etymology

From Latin 'cauda' (tail).

4. ADJ

/ ˈkɔdəl / (AmE), / ˈkɔːdəl / (BrE)
resembling a tail or relating to the tail or hind part of the body, especially in animals or anatomical contexts.
relating to or like a tail
medical

Examples

The caudal fin of the fish helps it steer and propel through water.
During the procedure, an injection was made near the caudal end of the vertebral column.
In most mammals, the caudal vertebrae form part of the tail structure.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cranial - relating to the skull or head end

Etymology

From Latin 'cauda' (tail) with the suffix '-al' forming adjectives.
