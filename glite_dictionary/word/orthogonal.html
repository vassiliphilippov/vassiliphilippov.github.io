orthogonal

1. ADJ

/ ɔrˈθɑɡənl̩ / (AmE), / ɔːˈθɒɡənl̩ / (BrE)
(of lines, axes, or planes) at right angles to each other; perpendicular.
at right angles; perpendicular

Examples

In geometry, the term orthogonal describes two vectors that meet at a right angle.
The motion of the particle was measured along three orthogonal axes.
The grid lines on the map are orthogonal to each other, forming perfect squares.

Synset Examples

wind and sea may displace the ship's center of gravity along three orthogonal axes
a rectangular Cartesian coordinate system

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perpendicular - at an angle of 90° to a given line, plane, or surface
  • Antonym: parallel - side by side and having the same distance continuously between them

Etymology

From Greek 'orthogonios' meaning 'right-angled', from 'orthos' (straight, right) + 'gōnia' (angle).

irrelevant or unrelated to the matter under consideration; not directly connected
irrelevant; not directly related

Examples

The committee raised several orthogonal points that did not address the main issue.
Her comments were orthogonal to the topic we were discussing.
Let's focus on the problem at hand and set those orthogonal concerns aside for now.

Synset Examples

an issue extraneous to the debate
the price was immaterial

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irrelevant - not connected with the subject at issue
  • Antonym: pertinent - relevant or applicable to a particular matter

Etymology

From Greek orthogonios (right-angled), ultimately from orthos (right) + gonia (angle); extended in English metaphorically from mathematics to mean 'independent' or 'irrelevant'.

3. ADJ

/ ɔrˈθɑɡənəl / (AmE), / ɔːˈθɒɡənəl / (BrE)
(of statistical variables, functions, or factors) independent or uncorrelated; having no statistical relationship.
statistically unrelated; independent

Examples

In the analysis, the two variables are considered orthogonal because their correlation is nearly zero.
Principal component analysis seeks orthogonal axes to reduce the dimensionality of the data.
The researchers designed the experiment so that the factors were statistically orthogonal to avoid confounding effects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: independent - not influenced or affected by others; statistically uncorrelated
  • Antonym: correlated - having a mutual relationship or connection; statistically related

Etymology

From Greek orthogonios (right-angled), from orthos (right) + gōnia (angle); extended in mathematics to mean 'independent'.
