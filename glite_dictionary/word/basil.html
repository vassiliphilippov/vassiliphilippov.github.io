basil

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæzəl, ˈbeɪzəl / (AmE), / ˈbæzəl, ˈbɑːzəl / (BrE)
An aromatic annual or perennial herb of the mint family, native to tropical Asia, with small white flowers and fragrant leaves widely used in cooking.
aromatic herb used in cooking

Examples

She sprinkled fresh basil leaves over the pasta before serving.
The garden is full of fragrant basil growing next to the tomatoes.
Thai cuisine often features basil in spicy stir-fry dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mint - an aromatic plant of the genus Mentha, related herb in the same family

Etymology

From Greek 'basileus' (king), through Latin 'basilicum' and Old French 'basile'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbæzəl, ˈbeɪzəl / (AmE), / ˈbæzɪl / (BrE)
an aromatic annual or perennial herb of the genus Ocimum, often used in cooking, especially in Mediterranean cuisine
aromatic culinary herb (Ocimum)

Examples

She added fresh basil leaves to the tomato sauce for extra flavor.
A pot of basil sat on the kitchen windowsill, filling the air with a sweet scent.
The recipe calls for chopped basil as a garnish on the pasta.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oregano - aromatic herb used in cooking
  • Synonym: mint - aromatic herb of the genus Mentha

Etymology

From Greek *basilikon* (royal), via Latin *basilicum* and Old French *basile*.

3. NOUN

/ ˈbæzəl / (AmE), / ˈbæzɪl / (BrE)
The leaves of the basil plant, used as a culinary herb, either fresh or dried.
aromatic herb used in cooking

Examples

She sprinkled chopped basil over the pasta before serving.
Fresh basil gives the salad a vibrant flavor.
Dried basil is often used in tomato sauces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oregano - An aromatic herb used in Mediterranean cooking

Etymology

From Greek *basilikon* (royal) via Latin *basilicum*.
