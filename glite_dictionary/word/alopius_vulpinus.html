Alopius vulpinus

1. NOUN

/ əˈloʊpiəs vʊlˈpaɪnəs / (AmE), / əˈləʊpiəs vʊlˈpɪnəs / (BrE)
a large pelagic shark of warm seas, known as the thresher shark, with a long, whiplike tail used for hunting small fish
a species of large pelagic shark (thresher shark)

Examples

The Alopius vulpinus can be identified by its distinctive long tail, which it uses to herd fish.
Many divers hope to catch a glimpse of the elusive Alopius vulpinus during their excursions in tropical seas.
Research shows that the Alopius vulpinus plays a crucial role in maintaining the balance of marine ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thresher shark - common name for Alopius vulpinus, a large shark with a long tail

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name Alopias (variant Alopius) and species epithet vulpinus, meaning 'fox-like'.
