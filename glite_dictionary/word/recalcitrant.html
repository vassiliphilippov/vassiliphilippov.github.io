recalcitrant

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈkælsətrənt / (AmE), / rɪˈkalsɪtrənt / (BrE)
unwilling to obey authority, orders, or rules; strongly resistant to control or discipline
resistant to authority or control

Examples

The recalcitrant student refused to follow the teacher's instructions even after repeated warnings.
City officials struggled to deal with a recalcitrant landowner who resisted all efforts at negotiation.
Some diseases are recalcitrant to treatment, making them difficult for doctors to cure.

Synset Examples

a fractious animal that would not submit to the harness
a refractory child

Related Terms

  • Antonym: obedient - willing to comply with rules or authority

Etymology

From Latin 'recalcitrant-', present participle of 'recalcitrare' (to kick back), from 're-' (back) + 'calcitrare' (to kick).

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈkælsɪtrənt / (AmE), / rɪˈkælsɪtrənt / (BrE)
stubbornly resisting authority or control; difficult to manage or deal with
resistant to authority; stubborn

Examples

The new policies struggled to gain acceptance among the recalcitrant employees.
Teachers found the recalcitrant student difficult to discipline.
Despite repeated warnings, the group remained recalcitrant and refused to leave the premises.

Synset Examples

the University suspended the most recalcitrant demonstrators

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstinate - stubbornly refusing to change one's opinion or course of action
  • Antonym: compliant - willing to obey or conform to authority

Etymology

From Latin recalcitrantem, present participle of recalcitrare (to kick back, be disobedient), from re- (back) + calx, calc- (heel).
