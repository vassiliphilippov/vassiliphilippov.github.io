Siphonaptera

1. NOUN

/ saɪfəˈnæptərə / (AmE), / sɪfəˈnæptərə / (BrE)
An order of small, wingless insects comprising the fleas, which are external parasites of mammals and birds.
order of insects comprising fleas

Examples

The order Siphonaptera includes all species of fleas commonly found on mammals and birds.
Researchers studied the life cycle of Siphonaptera to understand how fleas transmit diseases.
The morphological characteristics of Siphonaptera distinguish them from other parasitic insects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flea - a small, wingless, parasitic insect that feeds on blood

Etymology

From Greek 'siphōn' (tube, pipe) + 'aptera' (wingless), describing the insects' tube-like mouthparts and lack of wings.
