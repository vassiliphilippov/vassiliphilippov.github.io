homeless

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊm.ləs / (AmE), / ˈhəʊm.ləs / (BrE)
People who do not have a home to live in, especially those living on the streets or in temporary shelters.
people without a home

Examples

The city has increased funding to support homeless during the winter months.
Many homeless face significant challenges obtaining healthcare and employment.
Several local organizations provide meals and shelter for the homeless.

Synset Examples

the homeless became a problem in the large cities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vagrant - A person without a settled home or regular work who wanders from place to place.

Etymology

From Old English hām (home) + -less, meaning 'without a home'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈhoʊm.lɪs / (AmE), / ˈhəʊm.ləs / (BrE)
Without a home; lacking permanent shelter, stability, or security, either physically or in a figurative or spiritual sense.
without a home or security

Examples

After losing his job, Mark felt completely homeless in the bustling city.
The charity provides support and shelter to homeless families during the winter.
She sometimes described herself as homeless in spirit, searching for a place to belong.

Synset Examples

made a living out of shepherding dispossessed people from one country to another

Related Terms

  • Synonym: destitute - without the basic necessities of life
  • Antonym: housed - having a home or shelter

Etymology

From Middle English 'hom' (home) + '-less' (without).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01054302-s
Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊm.lɪs / (AmE), / ˈhəʊm.ləs / (BrE)
A person without a home; someone who does not have regular or permanent housing.
person without a home

Examples

Every winter, the city shelters struggle to accommodate the many homeless seeking warmth.
The documentary focused on the daily challenges faced by the homeless in urban areas.
A local charity organized an event to provide meals for the homeless in the community.

Synset Examples

a homeless was found murdered in Central Park

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vagrant - a person without a settled home or regular work who wanders from place to place
  • Antonym: housed - having a home or accommodation

Etymology

From home + -less, meaning 'without a home'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
