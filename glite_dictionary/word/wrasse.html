wrasse

1. NOUN

/ ˈræs / (AmE), / ˈras / (BrE)
A chiefly tropical marine fish, typically brightly colored, with fleshy lips and strong teeth, belonging to the family Labridae.
a bright, tropical marine fish with fleshy lips

Examples

The diver spotted a colorful wrasse swimming among the coral reefs.
Several species of wrasse help keep other fish clean by eating parasites.
A large wrasse darted out from behind a rocky ledge.

Etymology

From Cornish *wragh* (old woman) or Welsh *gwrach*, referencing their appearance.
