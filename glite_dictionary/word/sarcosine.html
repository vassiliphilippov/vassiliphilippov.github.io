sarcosine

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːr.kəˌsiːn / (AmE), / ˈsɑː.kəˌsiːn / (BrE)
a sweet-tasting crystalline amino acid found in muscles and other tissues, used in biochemical research and as an intermediate in various syntheses
a sweet amino acid found in muscle
medical

Examples

Researchers studied the effects of sarcosine on cognitive function in their clinical trial.
Sarcosine is sometimes used as a supplement in psychiatric research.
The laboratory ordered a new supply of sarcosine to use in amino acid analysis.

Etymology

From Greek sarkos (flesh) + -ine (chemical suffix), first described in the mid-19th century.
