sod

1. VERB

/ sɑd / (AmE), / sɒd / (BrE)
to cover (an area of ground) with a layer of grass and its roots held together by soil (sod)
cover with grass and soil

Examples

The landscapers will sod the new lawn next week.
It took hours to sod the entire football field by hand.
After construction, the crew sodded the bare patches around the building.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turf - to cover with turf (grass and earth)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01338393-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ sɑd / (AmE), / sɒd / (BrE)
A layer of earth with grass and its roots bound together, typically used for lawns or turf.
surface layer of grass and soil

Examples

After the construction was finished, fresh sod was laid to restore the lawn.
Heavy rains made the sod soft and muddy under my feet.
The landscapers delivered rolls of sod to cover the new playing field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turf - grass and the surface layer of earth held together by roots, often used synonymously with sod

Etymology

From Middle English sodde, of uncertain origin; related to Middle Dutch zode (turf), Middle Low German sode (turf).
