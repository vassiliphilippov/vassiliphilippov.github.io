carioca

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæriˈoʊkə / (AmE), / ˌkæriˈɒkə / (BrE)
a lively ballroom dance that is similar to the samba, typically characterized by lively rhythms and expressive movements
lively ballroom dance similar to samba

Examples

The dancers performed the carioca with impressive skill at the competition.
She learned the carioca during her trip to Brazil and fell in love with its rhythms.
At the party, everyone was invited to try the carioca on the ballroom floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: samba - a lively Brazilian dance with African influences

Etymology

From Portuguese 'carioca', originally meaning a native of Rio de Janeiro.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑriˈoʊkə / (AmE), / ˌkæriˈɒkə / (BrE)
a style of lively Brazilian music composed for dancing the carioca, typically featuring syncopated rhythms
lively Brazilian dance music

Examples

The band played a carioca that got everyone on their feet.
She learned to dance to the fast rhythms of the carioca.
During the festival, traditional carioca music filled the streets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: samba - a lively Brazilian dance and musical genre

Etymology

Portuguese, from 'Carioca' (native of Rio de Janeiro).
