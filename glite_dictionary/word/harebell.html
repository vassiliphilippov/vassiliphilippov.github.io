harebell

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛrˌbɛl / (AmE), / ˈheə.bɛl / (BrE)
A small perennial plant of the northern hemisphere with slender stems and delicate, bell-shaped blue flowers.
small plant with blue bell-shaped flowers

Examples

The delicate harebell swayed gently in the summer breeze.
She picked a small bouquet of harebells from the meadow.
In poetry, the harebell is often admired for its fragile beauty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bluebell - a flower similar to the harebell, often confused with it

Etymology

From the plant's bell-shaped flowers and association with hares in folklore.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛrˌbɛl / (AmE), / ˈheəˌbɛl / (BrE)
a slender, perennial wildflower with blue, bell-shaped flowers, commonly found in Europe and North America, especially in grasslands and rocky places.
delicate wildflower with blue bell-shaped blooms

Examples

Clusters of harebell flowers nodded gently in the summer breeze.
The poet described the soft blue hue of the harebell in her countryside verses.
Hikers admired the delicate harebell growing between the rocks along the trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bluebell - another wildflower with blue, bell-shaped flowers, found in woodland areas

Etymology

From 'hare' (the animal) + 'bell', referring to the bell-like shape of the flower and its association with open fields where hares are found.
