to exist

1. VERB

/ ɪɡˈzɪst / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɪst / (BrE)
to have actual being; to be real or present in the world or in reality
to be; to have actual being

Examples

Does intelligent life exist elsewhere in the universe?
The traditions still exist today in many rural communities.
Many doubt that ghosts actually exist.

Synset Examples

Is there a God?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: live - to be alive; to remain alive
  • Antonym: to perish - to cease to exist or to die

Etymology

From Latin 'existere' (to emerge, appear, exist).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02609706-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪɡˈzɪst / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɪst / (BrE)
(of a person) to manage to live, especially under difficult conditions, often with minimal resources.
to manage to live or survive

Examples

He could barely exist on such a small income after losing his job.
After the war, many families had to exist on bread and potatoes alone.
The refugees managed to exist in harsh conditions until help arrived.

Synset Examples

he could barely exist on such a low wage
Can you live on $2000 a month in New York City?
Many people in the world have to subsist on $1 a day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to survive - to continue to live, especially in difficult conditions
  • Synonym: to subsist - to maintain or support oneself, especially at a minimal level
  • Antonym: to thrive - to grow, develop, or be successful

Etymology

From Latin 'existere' (to appear, to arise, to be).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02622766-v
Synset POS: v
