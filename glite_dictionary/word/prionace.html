Prionace

1. NOUN

/ praɪˈoʊnəsi / (AmE), / praɪˈəʊnəsi / (BrE)
A genus of sharks in the family Carcharhinidae, commonly known as blue sharks.
genus of blue sharks

Examples

The genus Prionace includes only one species, the blue shark.
Marine biologists studied Prionace populations in the Atlantic Ocean.
The distinctive slender body shape is a characteristic of Prionace sharks.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, coined from Greek 'prion' (saw) and 'akis' (point, tip).
