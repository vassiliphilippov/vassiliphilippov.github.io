charity

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃær.ə.t̬i / (AmE), / ˈtʃær.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
An organization set up to provide help and raise money for those in need or to support a public cause.
organization providing help or support

Examples

The local charity organizes food drives every winter to help families in need.
She left a portion of her estate to a charity supporting children’s education.
Several charities have come together to address homelessness in the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonprofit - an organization not established for profit

Etymology

From Old French charité and Latin caritas (dearness, esteem, affection).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæɹəti / (AmE), / ˈtʃærəti / (BrE)
an organization set up to provide help or raise money for those in need
organization that helps people in need

Examples

The hospital is run by a well-known charity that supports cancer research.
She donates a portion of her salary to a local charity every month.
The charity organized a fundraising event to help homeless families.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foundation - an institution supported by an endowment, often for charitable purposes

Etymology

From Old French 'charité', from Latin 'caritas' (dearness, affection, charity).

3. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛrəti / (AmE), / ˈtʃærəti / (BrE)
a kindly and forgiving attitude toward others, showing generosity of spirit and tolerance
kindly and lenient attitude toward people

Examples

She treated her critics with charity, never holding a grudge against them.
He spoke with remarkable charity about his opponent, praising his good intentions.
In moments of frustration, it is easy to forget the importance of charity toward others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leniency - the quality of being more merciful or tolerant than expected
  • Synonym: forgiveness - the act of forgiving or being forgiven
  • Antonym: severity - harshness or strictness in judgment or discipline

Etymology

From Old French *charité*, from Latin *caritas* (dearness, affection, charity).

4. NOUN

/ ˈtʃærəti / (AmE), / ˈtʃærəti / (BrE)
An activity or gift that is intended to help people in need or benefit the public at large.
an act or gift for public benefit

Examples

The local community center relies on charity to fund its activities.
After the earthquake, many people donated money and clothes to charity.
Organizing a food drive is a wonderful form of charity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: philanthropy - the desire to promote the welfare of others, often by generous donations
  • Synonym: altruism - selfless concern for the well-being of others
  • Antonym: selfishness - lack of consideration for others; concern only with one's own advantage or pleasure

Etymology

From Old French *charité*, from Latin *caritas* (dearness, love, affection, charity).
