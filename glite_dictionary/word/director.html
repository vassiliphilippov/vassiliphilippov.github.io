director

1. NOUN

/ dəˈrɛktər, daɪˈrɛktər / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛktə, daɪˈrɛktə / (BrE)
a person who oversees and manages the creative aspects and overall production of a film
person in charge of making a film

Examples

The director worked closely with the actors to bring the script to life.
After years of working as an assistant, she finally became a director.
Many critics praised the film for its talented director and innovative style.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: producer - person responsible for financial and managerial aspects of a film

Etymology

From Latin *dirigere* (to direct), via French *directeur*.

2. NOUN

/ dəˈrɛktər / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛktə / (BrE)
a person who leads and guides a group of musicians, such as a choir, band, or orchestra, especially during rehearsals and performances
person who leads a musical group

Examples

The director signaled the choir to begin the opening hymn.
As a talented director, she brought out the best in every musician onstage.
Orchestra members rely on their director for guidance during both rehearsal and performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conductor - person who directs the performance of an orchestra or choir

Etymology

From Latin dirigere (to direct, to guide).

4. NOUN

/ dəˈrɛktər, daɪˈrɛktər / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛktə, daɪˈrɛktə / (BrE)
a person who manages or oversees resources and expenditures, especially in an organization or institution

Examples

The finance director is responsible for budgeting and allocating company funds.
She was appointed as the director of operations for the new branch.
As director of the charity, he oversaw all resource management and spending.

Related Terms

Etymology

From Latin dirigere (to direct, guide), via director (one who directs).

5. NOUN

/ dəˈrɛktər / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛktə / (BrE)
A person who is a member of a board of directors and is responsible for helping to govern or manage an organization, company, or institution.

Examples

Several directors attended the annual general meeting to discuss corporate strategy.
The director voted against the proposal during the board meeting.
