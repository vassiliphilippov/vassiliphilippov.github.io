Arachis hypogaea

1. NOUN

/ əˈrækɪs haɪpəˈdʒiə / (AmE), / əˈrækɪs hɪpəˈdʒiːə / (BrE)
A species of leguminous plant native to South America, widely cultivated for its edible seeds known as peanuts.
cultivated plant; source of peanuts

Examples

Farmers in many countries plant Arachis hypogaea for both food and oil production.
The yellow flowers of Arachis hypogaea are followed by seed pods that develop underground.
Botanists study Arachis hypogaea to improve crop yields and resistance to disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peanut - the common name for the edible seeds of Arachis hypogaea

Etymology

From Latin, adopted from Greek *aráchis* (type of legume) and *hypogaea* (‘under the earth’).
