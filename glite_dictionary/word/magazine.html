magazine

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæɡəˌzin / (AmE), / ˈmæɡəziːn / (BrE)
a container or chamber in a camera that holds film and supplies it for exposure as required
film-holding chamber in a camera

Examples

The photographer carefully loaded the fresh magazine into the movie camera before filming began.
If you don't attach the magazine securely, the film may jam during exposure.
Technicians checked each magazine to ensure there was enough film for the shoot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cassette - a case containing film or tape for use in a camera or recorder

Etymology

From French *magasin* (storehouse), originally from Italian *magazzino*, from Arabic *makhzan* (storehouse, depot).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈmæɡəˌzin / (AmE), / ˈmæɡəziːn / (BrE)
a metal container or frame that holds cartridges and can be inserted into a firearm to supply ammunition automatically
firearms: container for cartridges

Examples

The soldier reloaded his rifle by inserting a fresh magazine.
Always check that the magazine is emptied before cleaning your gun.
The pistol jammed because the magazine was not properly seated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clip - a device for holding cartridges for loading into a firearm

Etymology

From Arabic *makhzan* (storehouse) via Italian and French.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02976476-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˌmæɡəˈziːn / (AmE), / ˌmaɡəˈziːn / (BrE)
a paperback publication issued periodically, containing articles, photographs, and advertisements, usually on a particular subject or for a targeted audience
periodic paperback publication

Examples

She picked up a magazine from the coffee table and started reading an article about technology.
The dentist's waiting room was filled with stacks of old magazines.
He subscribes to a science magazine that comes out every month.

Synset Examples

tripped over a pile of magazines

Related Terms

  • Synonym: journal - a periodical publication focused on a particular subject, often scholarly
  • Synonym: newspaper - a publication containing news, articles, and advertisements, usually issued daily or weekly

Etymology

From French 'magasin' (storehouse), originally referring to a storehouse for goods or information.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

a periodical publication with articles and pictures

Examples

5. NOUN

/ məˈɡæzɪn / (AmE), / məˈɡæziːn / (BrE)
a storage place, especially on a ship or military installation, for weapons and ammunition
storage for weapons and ammunition

Examples

The ship's crew carefully checked the magazine to ensure it was secure.
An explosion in the magazine caused severe damage to the warship.
All weapons must be returned to the magazine after the exercise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: armory - a place where weapons are kept
  • Synonym: depot - a place for storage, often of military supplies

Etymology

From Arabic *makhzan* (storehouse), via French and Italian.
