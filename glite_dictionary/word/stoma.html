stoma

1. NOUN

/ ˈstoʊ.mə / (AmE), / ˈstəʊ.mə / (BrE)
a tiny pore found on the surface of a leaf or stem, through which gases and water vapor can pass
tiny pore in a plant's leaf or stem
medical

Examples

The stoma regulates the exchange of gases between the plant and its environment.
Water vapor exits the leaf mainly through the stoma during transpiration.
Each stoma can open or close in response to environmental conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pore - a minute opening through which substances can pass

Etymology

From Greek stóma (mouth).

2. NOUN

/ ˈstoʊ mə / (AmE), / ˈstəʊ mə / (BrE)
an artificial opening created by surgery on the surface of the body to allow passage to or from an internal organ
surgically created body opening
medical

Examples

After the surgery, a stoma was created to allow waste to exit the body.
The nurse taught the patient how to care for his stoma at home.
Different types of clothing can be worn comfortably over a stoma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ostomy - a surgical procedure to create an opening (stoma) in the body

Etymology

From Greek stóma (mouth).
