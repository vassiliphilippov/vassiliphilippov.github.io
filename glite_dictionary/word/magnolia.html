magnolia

1. NOUN

/ mæɡˈnoʊ.li.ə / (AmE), / mæɡˈnəʊ.li.ə / (BrE)
a tree or shrub of the genus Magnolia, known for their large, fragrant flowers and longevity
tree with large fragrant flowers

Examples

A beautiful magnolia stood in full bloom outside the old farmhouse.
Every spring, the magnolia fills the garden with a sweet, distinctive fragrance.
They planted a row of magnolias along the driveway for their rich, creamy blossoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhododendron - a shrub or small tree with large clusters of bell-shaped flowers, similar ornamental use

Etymology

Named after Pierre Magnol, French botanist.

2. NOUN

/ mæɡˈnoʊliə / (AmE), / mæɡˈnəʊliə / (BrE)
the dried bark of various magnolia trees, used in traditional and folk medicine
dried bark of magnolia used medicinally

Examples

In traditional Chinese medicine, magnolia is valued for its supposed anti-inflammatory properties.
The herbalist recommended a tea made from magnolia to ease anxiety and stress.
Researchers are studying compounds extracted from magnolia bark for their potential health benefits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnolia - a tree or shrub with large, typically fragrant white or pink flowers

Etymology

From Latin Magnōlia, named after Pierre Magnol, a French botanist.
