to ordain

1. VERB

/ ɔːrˈdeɪn / (AmE), / ɔːˈdeɪn / (BrE)
to officially make someone a priest or minister, especially in a Christian church, by a special ceremony
appoint as clergy (church)

Examples

Only bishops have the authority to ordain priests in that denomination.
After years of study, she was finally ordained as a minister.
The church plans to ordain three new deacons this Sunday.

Synset Examples

he was ordained in the Church

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consecrate - to make or declare sacred, especially for religious purposes
  • Antonym: to depose - to remove from office or position, especially in church context

Etymology

From Latin 'ordinare' (to appoint, arrange).

2. VERB

/ ɔrˈdeɪn / (AmE), / ɔːˈdeɪn / (BrE)
to order or decree something officially, especially by authority
order or decree officially

Examples

The council ordained that no vehicles would be allowed in the square after dark.
The constitution ordains equal rights for all citizens.
After much debate, the committee ordained a new set of rules.

Synset Examples

The King ordained the persecution and expulsion of the Jews
the legislature enacted this law in 1985

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decree - to order officially by authority
  • Synonym: to enact - to make into law or decree officially
  • Antonym: to rescind - to revoke, cancel, or repeal an order or law

Etymology

From Latin 'ordinare' (to put in order, arrange), via Old French 'ordener'.

3. VERB

/ ɔrˈdeɪn / (AmE), / ɔːˈdeɪn / (BrE)
to order or decree something officially, especially by authority or law
order or decree officially

Examples

A new policy was ordained to ensure equal opportunity for all citizens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: command - to give an authoritative order
  • Antonym: to forbid - to order not to do something

4. VERB

/ ɔːrˈdeɪn / (AmE), / ɔːˈdeɪn / (BrE)
to confer holy orders on someone, making them a priest, minister, or other religious official
appoint as a priest or minister

Examples

She was ordained as a minister in 2015, after completing her theological studies.
The bishop will ordain three new priests during the ceremony on Sunday.
He felt called to serve and hoped to be ordained by the end of the year.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consecrate - to make or declare something sacred
  • Antonym: to depose - to remove from office, especially from a religious position
