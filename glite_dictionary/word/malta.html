Malta

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɔl.tə / (AmE), / ˈmɔːl.tə / (BrE)
An island country in the Mediterranean Sea, located south of Sicily, known for its strategic position and rich history.
island country south of Sicily

Examples

The ancient city of Mdina, located in Malta, is a popular tourist attraction.
Britain maintained a naval base on Malta for many years because of its strategic position.
She traveled to Malta to enjoy its mild climate and rich cultural heritage.

2. PROPN

/ ˈmɔltə / (AmE), / ˈmɔːltə / (BrE)
an island country in the Mediterranean Sea, south of Sicily, which became an independent republic in 1964
Mediterranean island country

Examples

She traveled to Malta to explore its ancient temples and sunny beaches.
Malta joined the European Union in 2004, strengthening its ties with Europe.
Many people are attracted to Malta for its rich history and vibrant culture.

Etymology

From Latin 'Melita', an ancient name for the island.
