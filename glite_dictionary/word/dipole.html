dipole

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪˌpoʊl / (AmE), / ˈdaɪ.pəʊl / (BrE)
an antenna consisting of two straight rods of equal length connected to a transmission line at their center, typically half a wavelength long
type of antenna with two rods

Examples

A simple dipole can be used for many radio transmission applications.
He installed a dipole antenna to improve his shortwave radio reception.
The dipole is one of the most widely used basic antenna types in broadcasting.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monopole - an antenna with a single rod or pole

Etymology

From di- (two) + pole, indicating two poles or terminals.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪˌpoʊl / (AmE), / ˈdaɪ.pəʊl / (BrE)
a pair of equal and opposite electric charges or magnetic poles separated by a small distance
pair of opposite charges or magnetic poles

Examples

The water molecule has a permanent dipole due to differences in electronegativity.
An antenna can be designed as a dipole for effective radio wave transmission.
The experiment measured the magnetic dipole moment of the particle.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monopole - a single electric charge or magnetic pole

Etymology

From Greek di- (two) + polos (axis, pivot).
