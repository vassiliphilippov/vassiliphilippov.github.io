unsized

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsaɪzd / (AmE), / ʌnˈsaɪzd / (BrE)
(of clothing or other items) not made or provided in specific sizes; intended to fit a range of users rather than being tailored to individual measurements.
not made in specific sizes

Examples

The store carried several unsized hats that adjusted easily to different head shapes.
She bought a pair of unsized gloves for convenience.
Many disposable masks are intentionally unsized so they fit most people.

Synset Examples

unsized gloves: one size fits all

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'sized' (given a particular size).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02232929-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsaɪzd / (AmE), / ʌnˈsaɪzd / (BrE)
(of paper or fabric) not treated or coated with sizing to alter its absorbency or finish.
not treated with sizing (esp. paper, fabric)

Examples

The artist preferred unsized paper for her watercolor paintings.
This fabric is unsized, so it feels rougher than the finished version.
Using unsized canvas allows paint to soak in more deeply.

Synset Examples

unsized paper

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sized - (of paper or fabric) treated with sizing to modify absorbency or finish

Etymology

Formed by prefixing un- (not) to sized (treated with sizing).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02233245-a
Synset POS: a
