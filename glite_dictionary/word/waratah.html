waratah

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔːrətɑː / (AmE), / ˈwɒrətɑː / (BrE)
a tall shrub native to eastern Australia, known for its broad leaves and bright red flowers in dense clusters
Australian shrub with red flowers

Examples

The waratah is the floral emblem of New South Wales.
During our hike, we spotted several waratahs blooming along the trail.
A single waratah stood out among the forest greenery with its vivid red flowers.

Etymology

From the Dharug (Aboriginal Australian) word 'warada' meaning 'red flowering tree'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔːrəˌtɑː / (AmE), / ˈwɒrətɑː / (BrE)
an Australian shrub or small tree with narrow leaves and large, bright red flowers in dense globular clusters
Australian shrub with red flowers

Examples

The waratah is the floral emblem of New South Wales in Australia.
A single waratah bloomed brightly among the green foliage.
Botanists studied the unique structure of the waratah's globular flowers.

Etymology

From Dharug (an Australian Aboriginal language) *warada* (seen in early Sydney records).
