Baphia

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪ.fi.ə / (AmE), / ˈbæf.i.ə / (BrE)
A small genus of shrubs, lianas, and trees native to Africa and Madagascar, belonging to the pea family (Fabaceae).
genus of African shrubs/trees (Fabaceae)

Examples

Many species of Baphia are valued for their ornamental flowers and medicinal properties.
Researchers collected samples of Baphia from various African forests for genetic studies.
Traditional dyes are sometimes made from the wood or bark of Baphia species.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek *baphē* (dye), referring to the use of some species for dye production.
