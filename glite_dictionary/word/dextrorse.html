dextrorse

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɛkstrɔrs / (AmE), / ˈdɛkstrɔːs / (BrE)
(of a plant or other structure) spiraling or curving upward from left to right, especially as viewed from below.
spiraling upward from left to right
medical

Examples

Many morning glory plants have dextrorse stems that twist from left to right as they grow.
The botanist noted the dextrorse coiling of the vine as it climbed the trellis.
Certain climbing plants can be identified by their dextrorse or sinistrorse twining direction.

Synset Examples

dextrorse vines

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sinistrorse - (of spirals or coils) winding in the opposite direction, from right to left

Etymology

From Latin 'dexter' (right) + '-orsus', past participle of 'vertere' (to turn).
