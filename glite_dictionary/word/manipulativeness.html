manipulativeness

1. NOUN

/ məˈnɪpjəˌleɪtɪvnəs / (AmE), / məˈnɪpjʊlətɪvnəs / (BrE)
The tendency or quality of being manipulative, especially by controlling or influencing others cleverly, unfairly, or indirectly.
tendency to manipulate others

Examples

Some leaders are criticized for their manipulativeness in dealing with subordinates.
Her manipulativeness made it difficult for others to trust her intentions.
Psychologists often explore the causes of manipulativeness in interpersonal relationships.

Synset Examples

Nevertheless, to block Stanley's efforts to maintain power and control, Fromm advised me to confront the manipulativeness of his behavior head on.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manipulation - the act of manipulating or controlling someone or something
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being honest and straightforward

Etymology

From 'manipulative' (from Latin manipulatus, past participle of manipulare, to handle) + -ness.
