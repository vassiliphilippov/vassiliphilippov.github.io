arraignment

1. NOUN

/ əˈreɪnmənt / (AmE), / əˈreɪnmənt / (BrE)
a legal document or proceeding by which a person is formally charged in court and called to answer an indictment
formal legal charge in court
legal

Examples

The arraignment was scheduled for Tuesday morning at the county courthouse.
At her arraignment, she pleaded not guilty to all charges.
The defense attorney argued that the arraignment was improperly conducted.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indictment - a formal charge or accusation of a serious crime
  • Antonym: acquittal - a judgment that a person is not guilty of the crime with which they have been charged

Etymology

From Old French 'araignement', from 'araigner' (to arraign), from Latin 'ad-' (to) + 'rationem' (reason, account)
