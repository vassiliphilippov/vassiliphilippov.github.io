vexing

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɛksɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈvɛksɪŋ / (BrE)
Causing annoyance, frustration, or worry.
causing annoyance or frustration

Examples

The delay in receiving the package was especially vexing for customers who needed it urgently.
She found his constant questions vexing, but answered them politely anyway.
It was vexing to realize that a simple error had caused so much trouble.

2. ADJ

/ ˈvɛksɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈvɛksɪŋ / (BrE)
Extremely annoying or upsetting; causing irritation or distress.
causing irritation or annoyance

Examples

The constant interruptions during the meeting were particularly vexing to everyone present.
It's vexing when plans are changed at the last minute without warning.
Her vexing behavior made it difficult to remain calm.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annoying - causing irritation; making someone feel slightly angry
  • Synonym: irritating - making someone feel annoyed or impatient
  • Antonym: pleasing - giving pleasure or satisfaction

Etymology

From the verb 'vex', which comes from Latin 'vexare' (to shake, disturb).
