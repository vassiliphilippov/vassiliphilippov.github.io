petabyte

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛtəˌbaɪt / (AmE), / ˈpiːtəˌbaɪt / (BrE)
A unit of digital information storage equal to 1,000 terabytes or 10^15 bytes.
unit of digital information (1,000 TB)

Examples

A single data center may store several petabytes of information.
With modern technology, it's possible to process a petabyte of data in just a few hours.
Cloud services now offer petabytes of storage capacity for enterprise clients.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: terabyte - a unit of information equal to 1,000 gigabytes
  • Degree Higher: exabyte - a unit of information equal to 1,000 petabytes

Etymology

Formed from the metric prefix 'peta-' (denoting a factor of 10^15) and 'byte'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛtəˌbaɪt / (AmE), / ˈpiːtəˌbaɪt / (BrE)
A unit of digital information equal to 1,024 terabytes or 2^50 bytes.
unit of digital information

Examples

Modern data centers are capable of storing several petabytes of information.
Downloading the entire contents of a digital library might require more than one petabyte of storage.
As technology advances, the cost of a petabyte of storage continues to decrease.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: terabyte - a unit of information equal to 2^40 bytes
  • Degree Higher: exabyte - a unit of information equal to 2^60 bytes

Etymology

From 'peta-' (a prefix denoting 10^15, modeled after 'tera-') + 'byte' (a unit of data).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13651674-n
Synset POS: n
