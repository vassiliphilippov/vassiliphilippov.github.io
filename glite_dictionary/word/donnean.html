Donnean

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʌniən / (AmE), / ˈdʌniən / (BrE)
Relating to, characteristic of, or in the style of John Donne, the English poet and cleric.
of or like John Donne (English poet)

Examples

Her poetry displays a distinctly Donnean use of metaphysical conceits.
The Donnean style is marked by intellectual complexity and clever wordplay.
Critics noted the Donnean wit present throughout his latest collection.

Etymology

From the name John Donne (1572–1631), English poet and cleric, with the suffix -an.
