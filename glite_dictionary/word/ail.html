to ail

1. VERB

/ eɪl / (AmE), / eɪl / (BrE)
to cause pain, trouble, or illness to (a person)
cause pain or sickness

Examples

Long hours and stress can ail even the healthiest workers.
The mysterious disease continues to ail the village, baffling doctors and nurses alike.
What exactly seems to ail the company's finances this quarter?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to afflict - to cause pain or suffering to someone or something
  • Antonym: to heal - to make or become healthy or whole again; to cure

Etymology

From Old English eglan (to trouble, plague).

2. VERB

/ eɪl / (AmE), / eɪl / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) to suffer from an illness or to feel unwell.
to be ill or unwell

Examples

The child seems to ail after eating the suspicious food.
What ails you today? You look quite pale.
My grandmother has been ailing for several weeks, but she is slowly getting better.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be ill - to be in a state of illness
  • Antonym: to recover - to return to a normal state of health

Etymology

From Old English eglian (to trouble, plague).
