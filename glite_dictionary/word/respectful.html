respectful

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈspɛktfəl / (AmE), / rɪˈspɛktfʊl / (BrE)
showing or feeling respect towards someone or something; polite and considerate in attitude or behavior
showing respect; polite

Examples

He gave a respectful nod to the teacher as he entered the room.
The children were always respectful towards their elders.
She listened in a respectful silence while her friend spoke.

Synset Examples

respectful behavior
a respectful glance

Related Terms

  • Antonym: disrespectful - showing a lack of respect
  • Synonym: polite - having good manners; showing consideration for others

Etymology

From respect + -ful, dating from late 16th century.

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈspɛktfəl / (AmE), / rɪˈspɛktfʊl / (BrE)
showing or feeling deference, admiration, or veneration toward someone or something; demonstrating respect.
showing deference or veneration

Examples

The students listened in respectful silence as the principal spoke.
She addressed the elderly man in a respectful manner.
He gave a respectful bow before leaving the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reverent - showing deep respect or veneration
  • Antonym: disrespectful - showing lack of respect or courtesy

Etymology

From respect + -ful, modelled after Latin respectus (regard, consideration).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02019429-s
Synset POS: s
