trivially

1. ADV

/ ˈtrɪviəli / (AmE), / ˈtrɪvɪəli / (BrE)
In a way that requires little effort or presents no difficulty; obviously or self-evidently.
in an obvious or simple manner

Examples

The statement can be trivially verified by checking a single case.
She completed the assignment trivially, as it was far below her skill level.
In mathematics, some results follow trivially from the definitions.

Synset Examples

we can prove trivially that this theorem is false

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obviously - in a way that is easily perceived or understood
  • Synonym: easily - with little difficulty or effort

Etymology

From Latin trivialis (commonplace, ordinary), from trivium (place where three roads meet).

2. ADV

/ ˈtrɪviəli / (AmE), / ˈtrɪviəli / (BrE)
In a way that is considered trivial, unimportant, or insignificant.
in an unimportant or insignificant way

Examples

The error can be trivially fixed by checking the input.
These concerns were dismissed trivially by the committee as unimportant.
In some cases, the answer is trivially obvious to all participants.

Synset Examples

trivially motivated requests

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trivial - of little value or importance
  • Antonym: significantly - in a meaningful or important way

Etymology

From 'trivial' + '-ly'. 'Trivial' from Latin 'trivialis' (commonplace, ordinary).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00483431-r
Synset POS: r
