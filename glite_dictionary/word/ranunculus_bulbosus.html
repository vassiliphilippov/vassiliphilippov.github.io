Ranunculus bulbosus

1. NOUN

/ rəˈnʌŋkjʊləs bʊlˈboʊsəs / (AmE), / rəˈnʌŋkjʊləs bʊlˈbəʊsəs / (BrE)
a perennial plant of the buttercup family, native to Europe and western Asia, with golden yellow flowers blooming in late spring to early summer; also known as bulbous buttercup
a species of buttercup with yellow flowers

Examples

Ranunculus bulbosus is often found in meadows and pastures across Europe and parts of North America.
The bulbous roots of Ranunculus bulbosus help distinguish it from other buttercups.
In late May, fields may be bright with the golden flowers of Ranunculus bulbosus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buttercup - common name for members of the Ranunculus genus, including yellow-flowered meadow plants

Etymology

From Latin: 'ranunculus' meaning 'little frog' (from 'rana' meaning 'frog'), and 'bulbosus' meaning 'bulbous'.
