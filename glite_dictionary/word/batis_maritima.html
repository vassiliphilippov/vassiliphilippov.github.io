Batis maritima

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪtɪs məˈrɪtɪmə / (AmE), / ˈbætɪs məˈrɪtɪmə / (BrE)
A low-growing, strong-smelling shrub native to coastal areas of the Americas, with unisexual flowers in cone-like spikes and thick, succulent leaves.
coastal shrub native to the Americas

Examples

Batis maritima is commonly found in salt marshes along the Gulf Coast.
Researchers studied the salt tolerance of Batis maritima in restoring coastal habitats.
The thick, fleshy leaves of Batis maritima help it survive in saline soils.

Etymology

From Latin: 'batis' (a plant name) and 'maritima' (of the sea, maritime), referring to its coastal habitat.
