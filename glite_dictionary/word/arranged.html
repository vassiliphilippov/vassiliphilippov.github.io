arranged

1. ADJ

/ əˈreɪndʒd / (AmE), / əˈreɪndʒd / (BrE)
disposed or organized in a particular order or way
ordered or organized

Examples

The newly arranged flowers brightened the entire room.
Books on the shelves were arranged by subject and author.
Seats had been arranged in a circle for the meeting.

Synset Examples

the carefully arranged chessmen
haphazardly arranged interlobular septa
comfortable chairs arranged around the fireplace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: organized - ordered or arranged in a systematic way
  • Antonym: disordered - not arranged or organized; lacking order
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01685687-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ əˈreɪndʒd / (AmE), / əˈreɪndʒd / (BrE)
deliberately planned or organized, especially in order to produce a specific effect or impression
deliberately organized for effect

Examples

The flowers on the table were arranged in a perfect semicircle to impress the guests.
Her speech sounded natural, but every word was carefully arranged for maximum impact.
They toured the gallery, admiring the arranged photographs that told a powerful story.

Synset Examples

one of those artfully staged photographs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: staged - deliberately arranged or planned for effect, especially in the arts
  • Antonym: spontaneous - done without being planned or arranged

Etymology

Past participle of 'arrange,' from Old French 'arangier' and Medieval Latin 'arrangiare'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01576262-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ əˈreɪndʒd / (AmE), / əˈreɪndʒd / (BrE)
organized or planned in advance, especially by someone other than those directly involved.
planned or organized in advance

Examples

Their parents insisted on an arranged marriage rather than letting them choose their own partners.
The conference included several arranged meetings between business leaders.
We attended an arranged tour of the historic city center.

Synset Examples

an arranged marriage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: planned - decided on or arranged before happening; deliberate
  • Antonym: spontaneous - done or occurring without premeditation or external stimulus
