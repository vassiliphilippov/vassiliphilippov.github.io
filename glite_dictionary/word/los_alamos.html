Los Alamos

1. PROPN

/ loʊs ˈæləˌmoʊs / (AmE), / lɒs ˈæləməʊs / (BrE)
a town in north central New Mexico, known for being the site where the first atomic bombs were developed during World War II
historically significant town in New Mexico

Examples

Many scientists moved to Los Alamos in the 1940s to work on the Manhattan Project.
The legacy of Los Alamos is closely tied to the development of nuclear weapons.
Tours of Los Alamos often highlight its important role in World War II history.

Etymology

From Spanish, meaning 'the poplars'.
