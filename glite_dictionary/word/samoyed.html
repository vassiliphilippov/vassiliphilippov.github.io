Samoyed

1. NOUN

/ səˈmɔɪˌɛd / (AmE), / səˈmɔɪɪd / (BrE)
a member of an indigenous people speaking Samoyedic languages, traditionally inhabiting northwestern Siberia
a person of the Samoyedic-speaking Siberian people

Examples

The Samoyed people have a long tradition of reindeer herding in Siberia.
A Samoyed community celebrated their New Year with traditional music and dance.
Anthropologists have studied the culture and language of the Samoyed population for decades.

Etymology

From Russian Самоед (Samoyed), formerly used for various indigenous peoples of northern Siberia.

2. NOUN

/ səˈmɔɪ.ɛd / (AmE), / ˈsæm.ɔɪɛd / (BrE)
A breed of large, white or cream-colored dog from Siberia, known for its thick fur, friendly temperament, and upturned mouth that gives a 'smiling' expression.
Siberian breed of white spitz dog

Examples

The Samoyed is well known for its thick, fluffy coat and friendly demeanor.
Their Samoyed won best in show at the local dog club.
Many families choose the Samoyed because of its gentle nature and smiling face.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spitz - A type of dog characterized by a thick fur coat and pointed ears and muzzle

Etymology

From Russian 'Samoyed', after the Samoyedic peoples of Siberia.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02114541-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈsæmɔɪˌɛd / (AmE), / ˈsæməʊjɛd / (BrE)
any of several Uralic languages spoken by the Samoyed people of northwestern Siberia.
Uralic language of Siberian Samoyed people

Examples

Few people today speak the Samoyed languages in their traditional form.
Linguists study Samoyed dialects to understand Uralic language development.
The Samoyed linguistic group has influenced neighboring Siberian tongues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nenets - a Samoyedic language spoken by the Nenets people
  • Degree Higher: Uralic - family of languages including Samoyedic and Finno-Ugric

Etymology

From Russian *Samoyed* (Самоед), referring to the indigenous people.
