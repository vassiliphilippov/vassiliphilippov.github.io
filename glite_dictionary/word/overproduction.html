overproduction

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvɚprəˈdʌkʃən / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəprəˈdʌkʃən / (BrE)
the process or result of producing more of something than is needed or can be sold
production of excess quantity

Examples

The company faced losses due to overproduction of unsellable goods.
Prolonged overproduction often leads to falling prices and wasted resources.
Environmental concerns were raised about the overproduction of plastic packaging.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: underproduction - production of less than is needed or expected

Etymology

From 'over-' (excess) + 'production'.
