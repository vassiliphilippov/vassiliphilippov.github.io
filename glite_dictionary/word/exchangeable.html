exchangeable

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈstʃeɪndʒəbəl / (AmE), / ɪksˈtʃeɪndʒəbl / (BrE)
able to be traded, swapped, or replaced for something else of equal value
able to be exchanged or replaced

Examples

The coupon is exchangeable for a free drink at any participating store.
Many collectibles are not exchangeable due to their unique characteristics.
This ticket is exchangeable for another date if you contact us in advance.

Synset Examples

convertible securities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interchangeable - able to be exchanged with another without difference

Etymology

From 'exchange' + '-able', indicating capability or suitability.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00919820-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ɪksˈtʃeɪndʒəbl̩ / (AmE), / ɪksˈtʃeɪndʒəbl̩ / (BrE)
able to be replaced or substituted with another person or thing of a similar kind without loss of function or suitability
able to be mutually substituted

Examples

The tickets are exchangeable up to 24 hours before the event.
Many computer components are designed to be exchangeable for easy repair or upgrade.
The two employees found that their roles were almost entirely exchangeable within the organization.

Synset Examples

interchangeable electric outlets
interchangeable parts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interchangeable - able to be swapped or substituted for each other

Etymology

From 'exchange' + '-able', influenced by Old French 'echangier' (to exchange).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01985596-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ɪksˈtʃeɪndʒəbl̩ / (AmE), / ɪksˈtʃeɪndʒəbl̩ / (BrE)
suitable to be exchanged for something else; able to be traded or replaced by another item of the same kind.
able to be exchanged or traded

Examples

The tickets are exchangeable for any performance this season.
All parts in the machine are exchangeable, making repairs easy and cost-effective.
Their gift cards are exchangeable at thousands of stores nationwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interchangeable - able to be switched or substituted without difference
  • Antonym: irreplaceable - impossible to replace or exchange

Etymology

From 'exchange' + '-able', with 'exchange' from Old French 'eschangier', from Late Latin 'excambiare'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00920727-a
Synset POS: a
