Beta Crucis

1. PROPN

/ ˌbeɪtə ˈkruːsɪs / (AmE), / ˌbiːtə ˈkruːsɪs / (BrE)
the second brightest star in the Southern Cross constellation, also known as Mimosa
star; second brightest in Southern Cross

Examples

Beta Crucis is easily visible in the night sky over the Southern Hemisphere.
The constellation features Beta Crucis as one of its most prominent points.
Astronomers use Beta Crucis as a reference to locate other stars in the region.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Alpha Crucis - brightest star in the Southern Cross; also called Acrux

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'the second (beta) of the cross (crucis)'.
