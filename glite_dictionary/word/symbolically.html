symbolically

1. ADV

/ sɪmˈbɑlɪkli / (AmE), / sɪmˈbɒlɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that represents something else or stands for a deeper meaning, rather than being literal.
in a symbolic manner

Examples

The dove is symbolically associated with peace in many cultures.
She chose to express her feelings symbolically through her artwork rather than with words.
The president symbolically planted a tree to mark the beginning of the new project.

Synset Examples

symbolically accepted goals

Related Terms

  • Antonym: literally - in a literal manner; actually; exactly

Etymology

From 'symbolic' (from Greek symbolikos) + '-ally', forming the adverb.

2. ADV

/ sɪmˈbɑːlɪkli / (AmE), / sɪmˈbɒlɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that uses or represents symbols rather than literal meaning
in a symbolic manner

Examples

The peace treaty was signed symbolically on the anniversary of the conflict's end.
He placed his hand on the book symbolically, representing a new beginning.
The statue was symbolically destroyed to mark the end of the old regime.

Synset Examples

symbolically expressed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: literally - in a literal manner; exactly as stated
  • Synonym: symbolic - serving as a symbol

Etymology

From symbolic + -ally, based on Greek symbolikos (of a symbol).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00127617-r
Synset POS: r
