gidgee

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪdʒi / (AmE), / ˈɡɪdʒi / (BrE)
a species of Australian acacia bush or small tree (Acacia cambagei), known for its scraggy appearance and extremely foul-smelling flowers
a scrubby Australian acacia tree

Examples

The smell from the blooming gidgee drifted across the outback, unmistakable and pungent.
Many farmers avoid planting near gidgee because of its overpowering odor during flowering season.
The gidgee is well adapted to the dry and harsh conditions of inland Australia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acacia - a type of tree or shrub with clusters of yellow or white flowers

Etymology

From a native Australian (Aboriginal) language, probably from the Bidjara or related languages.
