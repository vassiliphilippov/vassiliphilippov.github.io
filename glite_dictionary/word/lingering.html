lingering

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪŋɡərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɪŋɡərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of remaining in a place or situation for longer than is usual or necessary; the action of staying or delaying longer than expected
the act of staying longer than usual

Examples

His lingering in the hallway made everyone else late for the meeting.
There was a lingering at the end of the play, as the audience hesitated to leave.
Her lingering after dinner showed she was enjoying the company.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delay - the act of causing something to happen at a later time

Etymology

From the verb 'linger' (Middle English 'lengeren'), of Germanic origin, meaning 'to dwell' or 'to delay'.
