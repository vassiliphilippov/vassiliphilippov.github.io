immoderately

1. ADV

/ ɪˈmɑdɚətli / (AmE), / ɪˈmɒdərətli / (BrE)
To a degree that exceeds what is reasonable or moderate; excessively.
excessively; beyond reasonable limits

Examples

The dessert was immoderately sweet, making it difficult to enjoy more than a few bites.
He spent immoderately on luxury items, far beyond his means.
The politician spoke immoderately, ignoring the time limits set for the debate.

Synset Examples

his prices are unreasonably high

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excessively - to a degree that is more than necessary, normal, or desirable
  • Antonym: moderately - to a limited or reasonable degree

Etymology

From Latin 'immoderatus' (unrestrained) + English adverbial suffix '-ly'.

2. ADV

/ ɪˈmɑdɚətli / (AmE), / ɪˈmɒdərətli / (BrE)
In a way that is excessive or lacking in moderation; to an undue or excessive degree.
to an excessive or undue degree

Examples

He spent immoderately on luxury items, soon exhausting his savings.
The critics argued that the film relied immoderately on special effects, overshadowing the story.
Eating immoderately can lead to various health issues over time.

Synset Examples

he eats immoderately

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excessively - to a degree exceeding what is normal or necessary
  • Antonym: moderately - to a moderate or reasonable degree

Etymology

From Latin *immoderatus* (excessive, unrestrained) + English adverbial suffix -ly.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r
