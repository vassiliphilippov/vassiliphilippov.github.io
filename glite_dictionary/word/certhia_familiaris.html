Certhia familiaris

1. PROPN

/ sɜːrˈθaɪ.ə fəˌmɪlˈjɛərɪs / (AmE), / ˈkɜːθɪə fəˌmɪlɪˈɑːrɪs / (BrE)
The scientific name for the Eurasian treecreeper, a common European brown-and-buff bird with a down-curved bill that climbs tree trunks in search of insects.
Eurasian treecreeper (bird species)

Examples

The Certhia familiaris is often seen spiraling up tree trunks in European woodlands.
Researchers studied the nesting habits of Certhia familiaris during the spring season.
Birdwatchers easily identified the Certhia familiaris by its slender, down-curved bill and mottled brown plumage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eurasian treecreeper - Common European tree-dwelling songbird with brown-and-buff plumage and down-curved bill

Etymology

From Latin: 'Certhia' (a treecreeper), 'familiaris' (familiar, common).
