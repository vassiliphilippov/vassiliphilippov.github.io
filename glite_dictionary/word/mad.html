mad

1. ADJ

/ mæd / (AmE), / mad / (BrE)
angry; feeling or showing strong displeasure or annoyance.
angry, annoyed
american

Examples

She was mad at her brother for breaking her phone.
I get mad when people don't keep their promises.
He sounded mad during the entire phone call.

Synset Examples

sore over a remark

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angry - feeling or showing strong displeasure
  • Antonym: calm - not showing anger or strong emotion

2. ADJ

/ mæd / (AmE), / mad / (BrE)
showing or characterized by uncontrolled excitement or extreme emotion
wildly excited; emotionally uncontrollable

Examples

The crowd became mad with excitement as the band appeared on stage.
After winning the lottery, she did a mad dance around her living room.
There was a mad rush for tickets when the event was announced.

Synset Examples

a crowd of delirious baseball fans
something frantic in their gaiety
a mad whirl of pleasure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frantic - wild or distraught with fear, anxiety, or other emotion
  • Synonym: delirious - in a state of wild excitement or ecstasy
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other strong emotions

Etymology

From Old English 'gemǣd' (out of one's mind).

3. ADJ

/ mæd / (AmE), / mad / (BrE)
(of ideas or actions) very foolish or lacking in good sense; wildly impractical or irrational.
very foolish or impractical

Examples

It was a mad plan to try to cross the desert without enough water.
She came up with a mad idea for turning the old bus into a flying machine.
His friends thought it was mad to invest all his savings in such a risky project.

Synset Examples

harebrained ideas
took insane risks behind the wheel
a completely mad scheme to build a bridge between two mountains

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harebrained - very silly or foolish
  • Synonym: crazy - extremely foolish or impractical

Etymology

From Old English *gemæd* (insane, foolish), originally from Proto-Germanic *gamaiþaz*.

4. ADJ

/ mæd / (AmE), / mad / (BrE)
affected with a serious mental illness; insane or mentally disturbed
insane; affected by mental illness

Examples

He was declared mad by the court after his erratic behavior.
After years of isolation, he seemed to have gone completely mad.
The villagers believed the old man was mad and avoided his house.

Synset Examples

a man who had gone mad

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insane - having a disordered mind; mentally ill
  • Antonym: sane - mentally healthy; not insane

Etymology

From Old English 'gemæd' meaning 'out of one's mind, insane.'
