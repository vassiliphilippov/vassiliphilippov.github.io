Robert Koch

1. PROPN

/ ˈroʊbɚt kɑk / (AmE), / ˈrəʊbət ˈkɒx / (BrE)
(1843–1910) A German bacteriologist known for isolating the bacilli responsible for anthrax, tuberculosis, and cholera.
German bacteriologist, discovered key disease-causing bacilli

Examples

Robert Koch made groundbreaking discoveries in the field of bacteriology.
Many awards in microbiology are named after Robert Koch to honor his contributions.
The isolation of the tuberculosis bacillus by Robert Koch was a turning point in medical science.
