to deactivate

1. VERB

/ diˈæktɪˌveɪt / (AmE), / diːˈæktɪveɪt / (BrE)
to remove from active service, especially in the military or other official capacity
to remove from active service

Examples

After the war, many units were deactivated and their members returned to civilian life.
The organization decided to deactivate its emergency response team once the crisis ended.
He was officially deactivated from the military after serving for seven years.

Synset Examples

The men were deactivated after five years of service

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to activate - to make active or put into operation

Etymology

Formed from the prefix de- (removal, reversal) and activate, from Latin activus (active).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00192060-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˌdiːˈæktɪˌveɪt / (AmE), / ˌdiːˈæktɪveɪt / (BrE)
to make something inactive or no longer effective, especially by turning off a device, function, or system
make inactive; turn off or disable

Examples

Please deactivate your alarm system before entering the house.
She had to deactivate her account due to security concerns.
The technician will deactivate the old access code today.

Synset Examples

they deactivated the file

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to activate - to make active or operative

Etymology

From de- (removal) + activate, formed in English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00191849-v
Synset POS: v
