vernation

1. NOUN

/ vərˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / vəːˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
(Botany) The arrangement of young leaves within a bud before it opens.
arrangement of leaves in a bud
medical

Examples

The pattern of vernation can help botanists identify different plant species.
In most ferns, the vernation is coiled, a feature known as circinate vernation.
Studying vernation reveals important insights about how plants adapt to their environments.

Etymology

From Latin vernatio ('springing, budding'), from vernare ('to flourish, grow in spring').
