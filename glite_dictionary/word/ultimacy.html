ultimacy

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌltəˌmesi / (AmE), / ˈʌltɪməsi / (BrE)
the quality or state of being ultimate; the final or highest degree, stage, or point.
state of being ultimate or final

Examples

Many philosophers debate the ultimacy of moral principles in guiding our actions.
The ultimacy of the mountain's peak attracted climbers from all over the world.
She questioned the ultimacy of technological progress as the highest human achievement.

Synset Examples

the ultimacy of these social values

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finality - the state of being final or settled
  • Synonym: supremacy - the state of being supreme or highest in rank

Etymology

From ultimate + -cy, modeled on words like supremacy.
