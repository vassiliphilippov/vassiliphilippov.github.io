Cleome pinnata

1. NOUN

/ kliˈoʊmi pɪˈneɪtə / (AmE), / kliˈəʊmi pɪˈneɪtə / (BrE)
A perennial plant of the southwestern United States with leathery blue-green pinnatifid leaves and thick, plume-like spikes of yellow flowers; sometimes classified within the genus Cleome.
a perennial plant of the southwestern US

Examples

The desert landscape was dotted with clusters of Cleome pinnata in full bloom.
Botanists studied the distinctive leaves of the Cleome pinnata for their adaptation to arid climates.
Gardeners admired the tall, yellow flower spikes of the Cleome pinnata in the botanical display.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cleome - a genus of flowering plants in the family Cleomaceae
