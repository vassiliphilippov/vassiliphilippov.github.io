Ismailian

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪs.mɑːˈiː.li.ən / (AmE), / ˌɪs.meɪˈliː.ən / (BrE)
An adherent of Ismailism, a branch of Shia Islam distinguished by its own interpretation of the faith.
adherent of Ismailism (Shia Islam branch)

Examples

Many Ismailians live in regions of South Asia, Central Asia, and East Africa.
The spiritual leader of the Ismailians is known as the Aga Khan.
As an Ismailian, he observes religious practices unique to his branch of Shia Islam.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ismaili - adherent of Ismailism, a sect of Shia Islam

Etymology

From Arabic Ismāʿīlī, relating to Ismāʿīl, the seventh imam recognized by this sect.
