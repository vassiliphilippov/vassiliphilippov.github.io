apropos

1. ADV

/ ˌæp.rəˈpoʊ / (AmE), / ˌæp.rəˈpəʊ / (BrE)
At a suitable or appropriate time; opportunely.
at an opportune or suitable time

Examples

His suggestion came apropos and helped resolve the issue quickly.
The announcement was made apropos of the recent developments in the company.
Your advice arrived apropos when I needed it most.

Synset Examples

your letter arrived apropos

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timely - at a suitable or opportune moment
  • Antonym: inopportune - at a bad or unsuitable time

Etymology

From French à propos (to the purpose).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00275183-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADJ

/ ˌæp.rəˈpoʊ / (AmE), / ˌæp.rəˈpəʊ / (BrE)
appropriate or relevant to what is being discussed; pertinent.
appropriate; relevant

Examples

Her comments were perfectly apropos to the discussion about workplace safety.
The director made an apropos observation that changed the course of the meeting.
His remarks seemed oddly apropos of the recent events in the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relevant - closely connected or appropriate to what is being done or considered
  • Antonym: irrelevant - not connected with or relevant to something

Etymology

From French 'à propos' (to the purpose), from Latin 'propositum' (purpose).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00139437-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADV

/ ˌæ.prəˈpoʊ / (AmE), / ˌaprəˈpəʊ / (BrE)
Used to introduce a new, relevant, or tangential topic; incidentally.
used to introduce another topic

Examples

Apropos, have you seen the latest figures from the sales team?
I was discussing the conference and, apropos, did you check your email about the venue change?
Apropos of nothing, do you still have your old record collection?

Synset Examples

incidentally, I won't go to the party

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incidentally - used to introduce a different or additional point

Etymology

From French 'à propos' (to the purpose, concerning).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00157363-r
Synset POS: r
