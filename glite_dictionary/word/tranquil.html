tranquil

1. ADJ

/ ˈtræŋ.kwəl / (AmE), / ˈtræŋ.kwɪl / (BrE)
(of a body of water) calm and free from disturbance by waves or wind.
calm, undisturbed (used of water)

Examples

The lake remained tranquil despite the storm clouds gathering above.
They enjoyed a boat ride across the tranquil waters of the bay at sunset.
In the early morning, the river looked tranquil, like a mirror reflecting the pale pink sky.

Synset Examples

a ribbon of sand between the angry sea and the placid bay
the quiet waters of a lagoon
a lake of tranquil blue water reflecting a tranquil blue sky
a smooth channel crossing
scarcely a ripple on the still water
unruffled water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calm - (of water) free from roughness or agitation
  • Antonym: rough - (of water) agitated or disturbed by waves

Etymology

From Latin 'tranquillus' (calm, still).

2. ADJ

/ ˈtræŋ.kwɪl / (AmE), / ˈtræŋ.kwɪl / (BrE)
calm and peaceful; free from disturbance or agitation
calm and peaceful

Examples

The lake looked particularly tranquil in the early morning light.
She spoke in a tranquil tone, soothing everyone in the room.
After meditation, he felt completely tranquil and refreshed.

Synset Examples

spoke in a calm voice
remained calm throughout the uproar
he remained serene in the midst of turbulence
a serene expression on her face
she became more tranquil

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serene - calm, peaceful, and untroubled
  • Synonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other emotions
  • Antonym: agitated - feeling or appearing troubled or nervous

Etymology

From Latin 'tranquillus' (quiet, calm).
