brocket

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɑkɪt / (AmE), / ˈbrɒkɪt / (BrE)
A male red deer in its second year.
male red deer in its second year

Examples

The young brocket wandered away from the herd, exploring the forest on its own.
Hunters are restricted from shooting any brocket during the early season.
A brocket can be identified by its slender antlers, distinct from older stags.

Etymology

From Old French 'broquet', a diminutive of 'broche' (spike), referring to the animal's small antlers.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbrɑːkɪt / (AmE), / ˈbrɒkɪt / (BrE)
A small South American deer, typically with short, unbranched antlers.
small South American deer

Examples

The brocket is known for its reddish-brown coat and elusive nature.
Researchers observed several brocket deer feeding quietly near the forest edge.
Conservationists are concerned about the declining population of the brocket in the Amazon region.

Etymology

From French 'broquet', diminutive of 'broc' (spike, antler).
