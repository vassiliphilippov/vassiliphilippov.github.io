glum

1. ADJ

/ ɡlʌm / (AmE), / ɡlʌm / (BrE)
looking or feeling sad, gloomy, or silently ill-tempered; showing a sullen or moody attitude
gloomy or silently unhappy

Examples

He gave a glum reply when asked about the team's defeat.
She looked glum as she waited for the results to be announced.
After the argument, he sat in glum silence in the corner.

Synset Examples

a dark scowl
the proverbially dour New England Puritan
a glum, hopeless shrug
he sat in moody silence
a morose and unsociable manner
a saturnine, almost misanthropic young genius
a sour temper
a sullen crowd

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sullen - bad-tempered and sulky; gloomy
  • Antonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic

Etymology

Perhaps from Scots glum (morose, sullen, gloomy), likely from Middle English gloumen (to look sullen).

2. ADJ

/ ɡlʌm / (AmE), / ɡlʌm / (BrE)
looking or feeling sad, gloomy, or dejected; lacking cheerfulness.
looking or feeling sad or gloomy

Examples

She gave him a glum smile, trying to hide her disappointment.
After losing the match, the players looked glum in the locker room.
He sat by the window on a rainy day, feeling glum about the future.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cheerful - happy and optimistic
  • Synonym: gloomy - depressingly dark, dim, or pessimistic

Etymology

Of unknown origin; first recorded in the 18th century.
