Gempylidae

1. NOUN

/ dʒɛmˈpɪlɪˌdi / (AmE), / dʒɛmˈpɪlɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine fish known as snake mackerels, characterized by elongated bodies and predatory habits.
family of snake mackerels (fish)

Examples

Fish in the family Gempylidae are found in tropical and subtropical oceans worldwide.
The Gempylidae family includes several species prized by commercial fisheries for their meat.
Scientists recently discovered a new species in the Gempylidae off the coast of Indonesia.

Etymology

From New Latin, formed from genus name Gempylus plus the zoological suffix -idae indicating a family.
