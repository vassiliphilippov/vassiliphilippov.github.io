right-on

1. ADJ

/ ˌraɪtˈɑn / (AmE), / ˌraɪtˈɒn / (BrE)
completely accurate, appropriate, or in agreement with current ideas or values, especially in a socially positive sense
completely accurate or appropriate (informal)
informal

Examples

Her comments on climate change were right-on and earned her a standing ovation.
That's a right-on suggestion for improving the project.
The film's message is right-on for today's audience.

Synset Examples

Her speech about equality was right-on and inspiring.
That is a right-on idea for the campaign!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spot-on - exactly correct or accurate
