Onychophora

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːnɪˈkɑːfərə / (AmE), / ˌɒnɪˈkɒfərə / (BrE)
a phylum of small, elongated, wormlike invertebrates found in damp habitats of warm regions, known for their resemblance to slugs with legs and sometimes considered a link between arthropods and annelids
a phylum of wormlike invertebrates

Examples

The velvet worms of the phylum Onychophora inhabit moist forests in tropical regions.
Scientists study Onychophora to understand evolutionary links between arthropods and annelids.
Members of Onychophora possess distinctive legs tipped with claws.

Etymology

From Greek 'onycho-' (claw) + '-phora' (bearing), referring to their clawed legs.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑː.nɪˈkɑː.fə.rə / (AmE), / ˌɒ.nɪˈkɒ.fə.rə / (BrE)
A class of small, elongated, wormlike terrestrial invertebrates found in damp, dark habitats in warm regions; they have soft bodies, resemble slugs with legs, and are distinct from annelids and arthropods.
class of wormlike terrestrial invertebrates
medical

Examples

The discovery of a new species within the Onychophora has excited evolutionary biologists.
Members of the Onychophora are often called velvet worms because of their velvety skin texture.
Scientists debate whether the Onychophora represent a link between annelid worms and arthropods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: velvet worm - common name for animals of the class Onychophora
  • Antonym: annelid - member of the phylum Annelida, segmented worms
  • Antonym: arthropod - member of the phylum Arthropoda, including insects and crustaceans

Etymology

From Greek 'onycho-' (claw) + 'phora' (bearing), referring to their claw-bearing limbs.
