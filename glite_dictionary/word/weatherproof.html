weatherproof

1. VERB

/ ˈwɛðɚˌpruf / (AmE), / ˈwɛðəˌpruːf / (BrE)
to make (something) resistant to the effects of bad weather, especially rain and wind
make resistant to bad weather

Examples

The company plans to weatherproof all the outdoor furniture for the rainy season.
You should weatherproof your windows before the winter storms arrive.
Technicians weatherproofed the roof as part of the renovation project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waterproof - to make impervious to water

Etymology

Formed from 'weather' and 'proof' (meaning impervious or resistant).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00166653-v
Synset POS: v

2. ADJ

/ ˈwɛðərˌpruf / (AmE), / ˈwɛðəˌpruːf / (BrE)
able to resist damage or deterioration when exposed to weather conditions such as rain, wind, or sunlight
resistant to weather conditions

Examples

The hikers wore weatherproof jackets to stay dry during the storm.
We installed weatherproof outlets outside for safety in all seasons.
This tent is weatherproof, making it ideal for camping in unpredictable conditions.

Synset Examples

weatherproof electric wiring
a weatherproof coat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waterproof - not allowing water to pass through

Etymology

Formed from 'weather' + 'proof', indicating resistance to weather.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01892340-s
Synset POS: s
