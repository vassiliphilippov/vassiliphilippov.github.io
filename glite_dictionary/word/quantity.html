quantity

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑːn.t̬ə.ti / (AmE), / ˈkwɒn.tɪ.ti / (BrE)
an amount or number of something that can be measured or counted
amount or number of something

Examples

We have a large quantity of fresh fruit available this season.
Only a small quantity of water remained in the pitcher after the party.
The teacher asked the students to measure the quantity of salt needed for the experiment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amount - a quantity of something, often used interchangeably with 'quantity'
  • Antonym: quality - the standard or grade of something, not its quantity

Etymology

From Latin 'quantitas', from 'quantus' meaning 'how great, how much'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑn.t̬ə.ti / (AmE), / ˈkwɒn.tɪ.ti / (BrE)
a property that can be measured or expressed with a magnitude, especially as represented in mathematical terms by a constant or variable
measurable amount or magnitude

Examples

The quantity of water in the tank can be measured in liters.
In algebra, each variable represents a different quantity.
Scientists must account for the quantity of each substance used in the experiment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amount - a quantity of something, especially the total of a thing or things in number, size, value, or extent

Etymology

From Latin 'quantitas' (how much, greatness in size or amount).
