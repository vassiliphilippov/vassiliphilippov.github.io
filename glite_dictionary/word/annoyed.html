annoyed

1. ADJ

/ əˈnɔɪd / (AmE), / əˈnɔɪd / (BrE)
feeling slightly angry or irritated, often because of something unpleasant or repeated
feeling irritated or slightly angry

Examples

She was annoyed by the persistent noise coming from the construction site.
He looked annoyed when I interrupted his meeting.
It's easy to get annoyed if you have to wait in a long queue.

Synset Examples

made an irritated gesture
feeling nettled from the constant teasing
peeved about being left out
felt really pissed at her snootiness
riled no end by his lies
roiled by the delay

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritated - feeling slightly angry, similar meaning to 'annoyed'
  • Antonym: calm - not feeling angry or upset
  • Degree Higher: furious - feeling extremely angry, a much higher degree than 'annoyed'
  • Degree Lower: content - feeling satisfied or at ease, lower degree than 'annoyed'

Etymology

From the verb 'annoy,' which comes from Old French *anoier*, from Latin *inodiare* (to make loathsome).

2. ADJ

/ əˈnɔɪd / (AmE), / əˈnɔɪd / (BrE)
feeling slightly angry or bothered, especially by something petty or repetitive
feeling slightly angry or bothered

Examples

She sounded annoyed when I asked her the same question again.
I get annoyed by people who talk loudly on their phones.
After waiting for an hour, he was clearly annoyed with the delay.

Synset Examples

harassed working mothers
a harried expression
her poor pestered father had to endure her constant interruptions
the vexed parents of an unruly teenager

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritated - showing impatience or slight anger
  • Antonym: calm - not feeling annoyed or angry
  • Degree Higher: angry - having strong feelings of displeasure
  • Synonym: bothered - slightly troubled or inconvenienced

Etymology

From Old French 'anoier' (to weary, vex), from Late Latin 'inodiare' (to make loathsome), based on Latin 'odium' (hatred).
