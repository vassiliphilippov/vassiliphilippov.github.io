parakeet

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛr əˌkit / (AmE), / ˌpar ə ˈkiːt / (BrE)
a small, slender parrot with a long tail, found in many species and often kept as a pet
small, long-tailed parrot

Examples

A parakeet can learn to mimic human speech if trained from a young age.
Many children ask for a parakeet as their first pet because of its playful nature.
The bright green feathers of the parakeet made it easy to spot among the trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parrot - a bird, often brightly colored, with a strong curved beak, including parakeets, macaws, and cockatoos

Etymology

From Spanish 'periquito' (parrot), possibly of unclear origin.
