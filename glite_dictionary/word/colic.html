colic

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːlɪk / (AmE), / ˈkɒlɪk / (BrE)
Severe, often fluctuating abdominal pain, especially common in infants.
acute abdominal pain, often in infants
medical

Examples

His newborn cried for hours each night due to colic.
Doctors assured her the baby's colic would eventually subside.
Many parents struggle to soothe infants suffering from colic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stomachache - pain in the stomach or abdominal area

Etymology

From Latin 'colicus', from Greek 'kōlikos', relating to the colon.
