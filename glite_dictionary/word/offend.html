to offend

1. VERB

/ əˈfɛnd / (AmE), / əˈfɛnd / (BrE)
to cause someone to feel hurt, upset, or annoyed, often by something you say or do
to hurt someone's feelings

Examples

I didn't mean to offend you with my comment.
She was offended when she wasn't invited to the party.
Try not to offend anyone with your jokes.

Synset Examples

She hurt me when she did not include me among her guests
This remark really bruised my ego

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insult - to speak to or treat with disrespect or scorn
  • Antonym: please - to make someone feel happy or satisfied

Etymology

From Latin 'offendere', meaning 'to strike against, offend'.

2. VERB

/ əˈfɛnd / (AmE), / əˈfɛnd / (BrE)
to break a law, rule, contract, or promise; to act in violation of agreed standards or obligations
break a law, rule, or promise

Examples

He didn't mean to offend the contract, but his actions broke the agreement.
If you offend the company's code of conduct, you may face disciplinary action.
She was careful never to offend any regulations while working on the project.

Synset Examples

offend all laws of humanity
violate the basic laws or human civilization
break a law
break a promise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to violate - to break or fail to comply with a rule or law
  • Synonym: break - to fail to observe a law, rule, or promise

Etymology

From Latin 'offendere' (to strike against, to displease, to transgress).
