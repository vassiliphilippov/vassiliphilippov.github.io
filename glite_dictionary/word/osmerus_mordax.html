Osmerus mordax

1. NOUN

/ ɑzˈmɛɹəs ˈmɔrdæks / (AmE), / ɒzˈmɛərəs ˈmɔːrdaks / (BrE)
a species of smelt important as a food fish in eastern North America and Alaska, found in both marine and freshwater habitats
species of smelt fish of North America

Examples

The Osmerus mordax is widely fished for its delicate flavor and is popular in New England cuisine.
Biologists study the migration patterns of Osmerus mordax in both freshwater and marine environments.
Commercial catches of Osmerus mordax have declined in certain regions due to habitat changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smelt - a small, silvery fish of the family Osmeridae

Etymology

Modern Latin, from Greek 'osmeros' (smelt) and Latin 'mordax' (biting).
