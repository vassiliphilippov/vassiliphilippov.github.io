fabrication

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæbrɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfæbrɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making or constructing something, especially from raw materials
making or constructing something

Examples

The fabrication of aircraft parts requires precision and skill.
Advances in technology have revolutionized the fabrication of medical devices.
Local businesses specialize in the fabrication of custom metalwork.

Synset Examples

the synthesis and fabrication of single crystals
an improvement in the manufacture of explosives
manufacturing is vital to Great Britain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manufacture - the process of making goods on a large scale

Etymology

From Latin 'fabricatio', from 'fabricare' (to make, construct).

2. NOUN

/ ˌfæb.rɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌfæb.rɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The act of inventing false information or a lie, especially intentionally to deceive.
a deliberate lie or falsehood

Examples

The entire story he told was a clever fabrication meant to mislead the investigators.
Journalists exposed the company's report as a complete fabrication.
Her accusation was later shown to be a fabrication without any basis in fact.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lie - a false statement made with the intent to deceive
  • Antonym: truth - the quality or state of being true

Etymology

From Latin 'fabricatio', from 'fabricare' (to fashion, build).
