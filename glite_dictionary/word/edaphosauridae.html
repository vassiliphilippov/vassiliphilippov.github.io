Edaphosauridae

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛdəfoʊˈsɔːrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌɛdəfəˈsɔːrɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of extinct early reptiles within the order Pelycosauria, known from the late Paleozoic era
extinct family of early reptiles

Examples

Fossils attributed to the Edaphosauridae have been discovered in North America and Europe.
Members of the Edaphosauridae were notable for their distinct sail-like structures along their backs.
The Edaphosauridae family played a significant role in early vertebrate evolution during the late Carboniferous period.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'edaphos' (ground) and 'sauros' (lizard), with the family suffix '-idae'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛdəfoʊsɔːˈrɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌɛdəfəsɔːˈrɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of extinct early reptiles of the order Pelycosauria, known for their distinctive sail-like back structures
family of extinct early reptiles (Pelycosauria)

Examples

The fossils of Edaphosauridae have provided important clues about the evolution of early synapsids.
Distinctive spines along the backbone are a hallmark of the Edaphosauridae family.
Paleontologists study Edaphosauridae to understand their role in prehistoric ecosystems.

Etymology

From Greek 'edaphos' (ground, floor) and the zoological suffix '-idae' denoting a family.
