Sir Henry Morgan

1. PROPN

/ sɚ ˈhɛnri ˈmɔrɡən / (AmE), / sə ˈhɛnri ˈmɔːɡən / (BrE)
(1635–1688) A Welsh privateer and buccaneer who led raids on Spanish colonies in the Caribbean for England.
Welsh buccaneer and Caribbean raider (1635–1688)

Examples

Sir Henry Morgan became famous for his daring raids against the Spanish in the Caribbean.
Many stories of piracy in the West Indies mention Sir Henry Morgan by name.
The exploits of Sir Henry Morgan inspired legends and books about pirates.
