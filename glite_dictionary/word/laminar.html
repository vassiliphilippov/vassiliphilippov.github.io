laminar

1. ADJ

/ ˈlæm.ɪ.nɚ / (AmE), / ˈlam.ɪ.nə / (BrE)
arranged in, consisting of, or forming thin layers or plates
arranged in thin layers or plates

Examples

The scientist observed the laminar structure of the mineral under the microscope.
Air flows smoothly in a laminar fashion through certain types of pipes.
The laminar arrangement of tissues is important in leaf anatomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stratified - formed or arranged in layers

Etymology

From Latin 'lamina' (thin plate, layer) + '-ar', forming adjectives.
