Levitical

1. ADJ

/ lɪˈvɪtɪkəl / (AmE), / lɪˈvɪtɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the book of Leviticus in the Bible or to the practices, laws, or priests associated with it
of or relating to Leviticus in the Bible

Examples

The priest performed the Levitical rituals as described in the Old Testament.
Many ancient laws have a Levitical origin, especially concerning purity and offerings.
The scholar wrote an article analyzing Levitical traditions in early Judaism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Leviticus - third book of the Old Testament, containing laws

Etymology

From Late Latin Leviticus (the biblical book) + -al.
