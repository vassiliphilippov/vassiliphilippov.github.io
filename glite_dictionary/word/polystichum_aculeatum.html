Polystichum aculeatum

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊliˈstɪkəm ˌækjʊˈleɪtəm / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈstɪkəm ˌækjʊˈleɪtəm / (BrE)
A species of fern with glossy, holly-like fronds found in tropical and temperate regions.
species of fern with holly-like fronds

Examples

The garden features a beautiful display of Polystichum aculeatum among the shaded rocks.
Polystichum aculeatum thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade.
Botanists admire Polystichum aculeatum for its distinctive, holly-like fronds.

Etymology

From Latin: 'polystichum' meaning many rows and 'aculeatum' meaning prickly.
