Pepin III

1. PROPN

/ ˈpɛpɪn ðə ˈθɜrd / (AmE), / ˈpɛpɪn ðə ˈθɜːd / (BrE)
(714–768) King of the Franks and father of Charlemagne, who defended papal interests and founded the Carolingian dynasty in 751.
Frankish king and founder of Carolingian dynasty

Examples

Pepin III was crowned King of the Franks in 751, marking the start of the Carolingian dynasty.
As the father of Charlemagne, Pepin III played a crucial role in shaping medieval European history.
The alliance between the papacy and Pepin III strengthened the position of both the French monarchy and the Church.

Etymology

From Old French Pépin, of uncertain origin; Roman numeral III indicates he was the third of that name.
