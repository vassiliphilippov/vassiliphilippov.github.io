Hydrastis canadensis

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈdræstɪs kænəˈdɛnsɪs / (AmE), / hɪˈdræstɪs kænəˈdɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A perennial herb native to northeastern North America, with a thick, knotted yellow rootstock and large rounded leaves; also known as goldenseal.
North American perennial herb (goldenseal)
medical

Examples

Traditional healers used the Hydrastis canadensis root to make medicinal remedies.
Hydrastis canadensis thrives in the shaded forests of the northeastern United States.
Many alternative medicines include extracts from Hydrastis canadensis, commonly known as goldenseal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goldenseal - Common name for Hydrastis canadensis, a medicinal herb

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'hydor' (water) and 'rastis' (root), and Latin 'canadensis' (from Canada).
