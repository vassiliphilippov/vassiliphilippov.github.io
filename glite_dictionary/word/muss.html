muss

1. VERB

/ mʌs / (AmE), / mʌs / (BrE)
to make (something) messy or untidy
make messy or untidy

Examples

He playfully mussed her hair before she left for school.
Don't muss up the tablecloth right before dinner guests arrive.
After napping on the couch, his clothes were completely mussed.

Synset Examples

the child mussed up my hair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mess up - to make untidy or disordered
  • Antonym: tidy - to make neat and orderly

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly a variant of 'mess'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01476516-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ mʌs / (AmE), / mʌs / (BrE)
A state of confusion or disorder; a mess.
a state of disorder or mess

Examples

After the party, the living room was in a complete muss.
She tried to organize her desk, but it still looked like a muss.
His hair was in a muss after he took off his hat.

Synset Examples

the house was a mess
she smoothed the mussiness of the bed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mess - a situation that is dirty, untidy, or disorganized

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly related to 'mess' or from Middle English 'muss' (a scramble).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14523631-n
Synset POS: n
