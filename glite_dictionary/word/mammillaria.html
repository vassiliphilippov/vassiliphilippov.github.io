Mammillaria

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæməˈlɛriə / (AmE), / ˌmamɪˈlɛːrɪə / (BrE)
A large genus of cacti characterized chiefly by nipple-shaped protuberances, commonly found in Mexico and the southwestern United States.
a genus of cacti with tuberculate stems

Examples

The desert garden features several rare Mammillaria species thriving in the arid climate.
Many collectors seek out Mammillaria for their distinctive, patterned tubercles and tiny flowers.
After years of careful cultivation, her Mammillaria produced dozens of delicate pink blooms.

Etymology

From Latin 'mamilla' (nipple), referring to the plant's tubercles.

2. NOUN

/ mæməˈlɛriə / (AmE), / mamɪˈlɑːrɪə / (BrE)
A genus of small, globular cacti characterized by tubercles and often bearing spines and flowers, native to the Americas.
a genus of small, tuberculate cacti

Examples

She collects rare Mammillaria species to add to her cactus garden.
The Mammillaria bloomed with tiny pink flowers in the spring.
Many Mammillaria cacti are prized by horticulturists for their unique shapes and spines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cactus - a type of succulent plant often found in arid regions

Etymology

From Latin *mammilla* (nipple), referring to the nipple-shaped tubercles.
