brainstorming

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪnˌstɔrmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbreɪnˌstɔːmɪŋ / (BrE)
A group problem-solving technique in which members generate and share ideas or solutions freely and spontaneously.
group idea-generation technique

Examples

We scheduled a brainstorming session to come up with marketing strategies.
The team's brainstorming produced several creative solutions to the problem.
During brainstorming, everyone is encouraged to contribute ideas without criticism.

Synset Examples

we had an intense brainstorming session today

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idea generation - the process of creating new ideas
  • Degree Lower: discussion - the action or process of talking about something to reach a decision or to exchange ideas

Etymology

Formed from 'brainstorm' + '-ing'; 'brainstorm' (originally meaning 'sudden inspiration') dates from early 20th century.
