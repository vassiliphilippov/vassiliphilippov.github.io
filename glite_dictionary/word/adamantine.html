adamantine

1. ADJ

/ ˌædəˈmæntaɪn / (AmE), / ˌædəˈmæntaɪn / (BrE)
extremely hard; having the hardness or qualities of adamant (diamond-like); unyielding or firm
extremely hard or unbreakable
archaic

Examples

The fortress was said to be protected by adamantine walls that no weapon could breach.
Her adamantine will helped her overcome all obstacles in her path.
The diamond shone with an adamantine brilliance under the light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hard - not easily broken or bent; solid and firm to the touch

Etymology

From Latin 'adamantinus', from Greek 'adamantinos' (of steel, hard as steel), from 'adamant-' (adamant, diamond).

2. ADJ

/ ˌædəˈmæntaɪn / (AmE), / ˌædəˈmæntaɪn / (BrE)
Extremely hard or unbreakable, like a diamond.
extremely hard; diamond-like

Examples

The cathedral's doors were made of adamantine steel, impervious to any tool.
She gazed into his adamantine eyes, unyielding and brilliant as diamonds.
The legend spoke of an adamantine shield that no weapon could penetrate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hard - solid, firm, and resistant to pressure

Etymology

From Latin 'adamantinus', from Greek 'adamantinos', from 'adamās' (untameable, diamond).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01154749-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˌædəˈmæntaɪn / (AmE), / ˌadəˈmantɪn / (BrE)
firmly unyielding or unable to be persuaded; completely inflexible to requests or reasoning.
unyielding; inflexible

Examples

Her adamantine resolve was evident as she refused to reconsider her decision.
Despite repeated offers, the manager remained adamantine about the company's policy.
He confronted the criticism with adamantine certainty, unwilling to concede any flaws.

Synset Examples

he is adamant in his refusal to change his mind
Cynthia was inexorable: she would have none of him
an intransigent conservative opposed to every liberal tendency

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inexorable - impossible to stop or persuade
  • Synonym: intransigent - refusing to change one's views
  • Antonym: flexible - willing to change or compromise

Etymology

From Latin adamantinus (of adamant), from Greek adamas (unconquerable, hard metal).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01028427-s
Synset POS: s
