boisterousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔɪstərəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪ.stər.əs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being noisy, lively, and lacking restraint
noisy and lively behavior

Examples

Their boisterousness was evident as soon as they entered the room, laughing and talking loudly.
The playful boisterousness of the children made it hard to concentrate on my work.
Some people find his boisterousness exhausting, while others find it contagious.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rowdiness - noisy and disorderly behavior
  • Antonym: quietness - the state of being quiet or calm

Etymology

From 'boisterous' + '-ness'; 'boisterous' from Middle English 'boistous' (rough, coarse).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɔɪstərəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪstərəsnəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being turbulent and stormy, especially referring to the sea
turbulent or stormy condition (esp. of the sea)

Examples

The boisterousness of the ocean made sailing impossible that night.
After the storm passed, the boisterousness of the sea gradually subsided.
Fishermen are accustomed to the boisterousness that often follows a strong gale.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calmness - the state of being free from disturbance or agitation, especially relating to the sea
  • Synonym: roughness - the quality of having a disturbed or agitated surface, especially referring to water

Etymology

From Middle English 'boistous' (rough, wild) + '-ness'.
