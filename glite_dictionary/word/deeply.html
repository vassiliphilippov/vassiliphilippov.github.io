deeply

1. ADV

/ ˈdiːp.li / (AmE), / ˈdiːp.li / (BrE)
to a great depth; far down or in.
to a great depth; far down

Examples

The submarine descended deeply into the ocean, far below the surface.
The miners dug deeply into the earth searching for rare minerals.
He inhaled deeply before beginning his speech.

Synset Examples

dived deeply
dug deep

Related Terms

  • Antonym: shallowly - to a small depth, not far in

Etymology

From 'deep' + '-ly', with 'deep' from Old English 'dēop'.

2. ADV

/ ˈdiːpli / (AmE), / ˈdiːpli / (BrE)
In a way that is intense or profound, especially with regard to feelings or emotions.
with great emotional intensity

Examples

She was deeply moved by the heartfelt speech.
He deeply regrets the decision he made years ago.
They deeply admire their mother's strength and compassion.

Synset Examples

They felt the loss deeply
she loved him intensely

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intensely - with strong or extreme force or feeling
  • Antonym: lightly - in a way that is not serious or intense, especially emotionally

Etymology

From deep + -ly; 'deep' from Old English 'dēop', with -ly forming an adverb.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r
