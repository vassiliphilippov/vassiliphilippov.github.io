cytophotometric

1. ADJ

/ ˌsaɪtoʊˌfoʊtəˈmɛtrɪk / (AmE), / ˌsaɪtəʊˌfəʊtəˈmɛtrɪk / (BrE)
relating to the study or measurement of cells using a cytophotometer.
measuring cells with a cytophotometer
medical

Examples

The cytophotometric analysis revealed significant differences in cellular DNA content between the samples.
Researchers used cytophotometric techniques to measure enzyme activity within individual cells.
The accuracy of cytophotometric detection improves with proper calibration of the instrument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cytophotometry - the quantitative measurement of substances within cells using a cytophotometer

Etymology

From cyto- (cell) + photometric (measuring light), referring to measurement techniques involving cells and light.
