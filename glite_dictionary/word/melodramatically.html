melodramatically

1. ADV

/ ˌmɛlədrəˈmætɪkli / (AmE), / ˌmɛlədrəˈmatɪkli / (BrE)
In an exaggeratedly emotional or dramatic manner, especially so as to seem unrealistic or over the top.
in an overly emotional or dramatic way

Examples

She sighed melodramatically when she saw the long queue at the bank.
He melodramatically threw himself onto the couch, complaining about how tired he was.
The actress recounted her story melodramatically, making everyone in the room roll their eyes.

Synset Examples

she acted melodramatically when she called for help

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: dramatically - in a dramatic or theatrical manner

Etymology

From 'melodramatic' + '-ally'; 'melodramatic' from 'melodrama', which comes from French 'mélodrame'.

2. ADV

/ ˌmɛl.ə.drəˈmæt.ɪ.kli / (AmE), / ˌmɛl.ə.drəˈmat.ɪ.kli / (BrE)
In an exaggeratedly emotional or dramatic manner, as in a melodrama.
in an overly emotional manner

Examples

She sighed melodramatically and flung herself onto the couch, hoping someone would notice her distress.
The actor melodramatically declared his love, complete with tears and grand gestures.
He recounted his minor misfortune melodramatically, making it sound far worse than it really was.

Synset Examples

here, the hero is melodramatically reunited with the heroine

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: dramatically - in a way that is intended to create an effect or be noticed

Etymology

From 'melodramatic' + '-ally', ultimately from Greek 'melos' (song) and French 'drame' (drama).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00401299-r
Synset POS: r
