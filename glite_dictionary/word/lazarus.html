Lazarus

1. PROPN

/ ˈlæzərəs / (AmE), / ˈlazərəs / (BrE)
the poor beggar featured in Jesus' parable of the rich man and the beggar in the New Testament, representing suffering and reward in the afterlife
beggar in Jesus' parable (Bible)

Examples

The story of Lazarus and the rich man is often read in church services.
Many artists have depicted Lazarus as a symbol of hope and redemption.
In theological discussions, Lazarus is contrasted with the wealthy man to illustrate moral lessons.

Etymology

From Greek Lazaros, from Hebrew Eleazar, meaning 'God has helped.'

2. PROPN

/ ˈlæzərəs / (AmE), / ˈlazərəs / (BrE)
In the New Testament, the man whom Jesus is said to have raised from the dead after four days in the tomb.
man raised from the dead by Jesus (Bible)

Examples

Many artists have depicted the story of Lazarus being raised from the dead.
The miracle of Lazarus is an important event in Christian theology.
She referenced Lazarus in her speech about hope and renewal.

Etymology

From Greek Lazaros, from Hebrew Elʿāzār meaning 'God has helped'.
