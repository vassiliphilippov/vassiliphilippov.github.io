quits

1. ADJ

/ kwɪts / (AmE), / kwɪts / (BrE)
on equal terms after payment or compensation; having no further debt or obligation to each other
even, no longer owing anything

Examples

After he paid me back, we were quits and had no reason to argue.
Lena wanted to be quits with her landlord before moving out.
They shook hands, both satisfied they were finally quits after settling the score.

Synset Examples

we're now quits
finally quits with the loan

Related Terms

  • Synonym: even - having no outstanding debts or obligations

Etymology

From Middle English "quyte" meaning "free, clear (of debt)"; related to Old French "quiter" (to release).
