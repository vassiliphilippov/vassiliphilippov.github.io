jejunitis

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛdʒuˈnaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛdʒuˈnaɪtɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of the jejunum, which is the middle section of the small intestine
inflammation of the jejunum
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with jejunitis after experiencing severe abdominal pain and discomfort.
Chronic jejunitis may require dietary changes and medication for effective management.
A biopsy confirmed that the inflammation observed was indeed jejunitis.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: enteritis - inflammation of the intestine

Etymology

From Latin jejunum (the middle portion of the small intestine) + -itis (denoting inflammation).
