griseofulvin

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡriːzi.oʊˈfʊlvɪn / (AmE), / ˌɡrɪsi.əˈfʊlvɪn / (BrE)
an antibiotic used to treat fungal infections, especially those of the skin, hair, and nails, produced by molds of the genus Penicillium
antifungal antibiotic for skin infections
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed griseofulvin to clear up the persistent ringworm infection.
Patients taking griseofulvin should avoid alcohol due to potential interactions.
Treatment with griseofulvin can last several weeks to completely eradicate the fungus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antifungal - substance that prevents the growth of fungi

Etymology

From Latin griseus (gray) + fulvus (tawny), referring to the appearance of the mold.
