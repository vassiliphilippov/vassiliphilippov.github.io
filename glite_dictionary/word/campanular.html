campanular

1. ADJ

/ kæmˈpæn.jələr / (AmE), / kamˈpan.jʊlə / (BrE)
shaped like a bell; bell-shaped, often used to describe flowers or other structures.
bell-shaped; shaped like a bell

Examples

The lily's campanular petals hung delicately from the stem.
Botanists often describe certain wildflowers as having a campanular shape.
The architect admired the campanular curves of the cathedral's bell tower.

Synset Examples

campanulate flowers of the genus Campanula

Related Terms

  • Synonym: campanulate - shaped like a bell; bell-shaped
  • Antonym: tubular - shaped like a tube

Etymology

From Late Latin 'campanula' (little bell), diminutive of 'campana' (bell).
