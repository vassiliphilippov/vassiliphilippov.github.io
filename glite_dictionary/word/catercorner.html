catercorner

1. ADJ

/ ˈkætərˌkɔrnər / (AmE), / ˈkætəkɔːnə / (BrE)
In a position that is diagonally across from something; situated at an angle from the corner.
diagonally across from
american

Examples

The bakery is catercorner from the post office on Main Street.
Our apartment is catercorner to the park, so it's easy to walk there.
The two buildings stood catercorner across the busy intersection.

Synset Examples

set off in a catty-corner direction across the vacant lot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diagonal - going in a slanted direction across a space or object

Etymology

Alteration of 'catercornered', from Middle English 'cater' (four) from Old French 'quatre' (four), and 'corner' (angle, corner).
