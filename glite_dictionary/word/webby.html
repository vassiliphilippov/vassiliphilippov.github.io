webby

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɛbi / (AmE), / ˈwɛbi / (BrE)
having open spaces or interstices like a web; resembling a web in structure or appearance.
resembling a web; having open spaces

Examples

The fungus grew a webby network of filaments on the old bread.
Some fabrics have a webby texture, making them light and breathable.
He brushed away the webby threads that clung to the corners of the ceiling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: web-like - having the appearance or characteristics of a web

Etymology

Formed from 'web' + '-y', denoting 'having the qualities of a web'.
