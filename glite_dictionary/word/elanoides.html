Elanoides

1. NOUN

/ ɛˌlænɔɪˈdiːz / (AmE), / ɛˌlæ.nɔɪˈdiːz / (BrE)
A genus of kites in the bird family Accipitridae, known for the species swallow-tailed kite.
genus of kites (birds)

Examples

The Elanoides genus is best known for the swallow-tailed kite, a striking bird found in the Americas.
Ornithologists observed several members of the Elanoides genus soaring gracefully above the wetlands.
Research on Elanoides helps scientists understand the evolution of flight in raptors.

Etymology

From Greek 'elanos' (kite) and '-oides' (resembling).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˌlænoʊˈaɪdiːz / (AmE), / ɪˌlanəʊˈiːdiːz / (BrE)
A genus of kites, a group of medium-sized predatory birds of prey in the family Accipitridae.
genus of kites (birds of prey)

Examples

The swallow-tailed kite, classified under the genus Elanoides, is known for its striking black and white plumage.
Researchers observed nesting behavior in several members of the genus Elanoides during their field study.
The genus Elanoides contains only one extant species, the swallow-tailed kite.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kite - a medium-sized predatory bird of prey in the family Accipitridae

Etymology

From Greek 'elanos' (kite) + '-oides' (resembling).
