Agdistis

1. PROPN

/ æɡˈdɪstɪs / (AmE), / aɡˈdɪstɪs / (BrE)
A title or epithet given to the goddesses Rhea or Cybele, particularly in ancient Asia Minor mythology.
epithet for Rhea or Cybele in ancient mythology

Examples

In ancient texts, Agdistis is often identified with the Phrygian mother goddess Cybele.
The cult of Agdistis was prominent in Asia Minor during Hellenistic times.
Scholars debate the origins and roles of Agdistis in early mythology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cybele - an ancient Phrygian mother goddess, often identified with Rhea
  • Synonym: Rhea - a Titaness, mother of the gods in Greek mythology

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἀγδίστις (Agdístis), an epithet of Cybele.
