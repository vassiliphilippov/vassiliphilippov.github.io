weld

1. NOUN

/ wɛld / (AmE), / wɛld / (BrE)
A European plant (Reseda luteola), cultivated for its yellow dye, and naturalized in North America.
European plant used for yellow dye

Examples

Traditional weavers still use weld to produce vibrant yellow fabrics.
The weld in her garden thrived and provided plenty of dye for her crafts.
Early settlers in North America cultivated weld for its valuable pigment.

Etymology

From Middle English *welde*, from Old English *wēalde* (the plant), related to the act of dyeing.

2. NOUN

/ wɛld / (AmE), / wɛld / (BrE)
A joint between pieces of metal formed by heating them until they fuse or by hammering them together.
a fused metal joint

Examples

The weld was inspected for any cracks or weak points.
A high-quality weld is essential for structural safety.
After cooling, the weld held the two steel plates firmly together.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joint - the place where two things are joined

3. VERB

/ wɛld / (AmE), / wɛld / (BrE)
to join together or unite closely or intimately, especially in a way that makes separation difficult
join or unite closely

Examples

The shared experience of hardship welded the team together.
Her deep loyalty welded her to the organization for decades.
Years of collaboration have welded them into an inseparable partnership.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

From the literal sense 'to fuse metals by heating', from Old English *wellan* (to boil, bubble up) and influenced by earlier welding techniques.

4. VERB

/ wɛld / (AmE), / wɛld / (BrE)
to join pieces of metal or other materials together by heating their surfaces and pressing them together
join materials by heating them

Examples

The technician skillfully welded two steel pipes together to repair the leak.
Modern artists often weld pieces of scrap metal to create abstract sculptures.
It is important to wear eye protection while you weld to avoid injury.

Synset Examples

weld metal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solder - to join metal objects using a melted filler metal
  • Synonym: to braze - to join by heating and melting a non-ferrous filler metal into the joint

Etymology

From Old English *wellan* (to boil, weld), influenced by Middle English *welden* (to wield).
