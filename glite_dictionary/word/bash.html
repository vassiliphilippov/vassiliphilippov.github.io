bash

1. NOUN

/ bæʃ / (AmE), / bæʃ / (BrE)
An informal party or celebration, especially one that is lively or boisterous.
informal party or celebration
informal

Examples

We're throwing a bash for her birthday this weekend.
He was invited to an exclusive bash at the downtown club.
The office bash lasted well into the night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: party - a social gathering of invited guests, typically involving eating, drinking, and entertainment

2. VERB

/ bæʃ / (AmE), / bæʃ / (BrE)
to strike something or someone with great force; to hit hard
hit something with force

Examples

He bashed his elbow against the doorframe and winced in pain.
The two wrestlers bashed each other with steel chairs during the match.
If you bash that computer any harder, you'll break it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strike - to hit forcefully
  • Synonym: smash - to break or hit forcibly and noisily
  • Degree Lower: tap - to strike lightly
  • Degree Higher: to pummel - to strike repeatedly, typically with fists
