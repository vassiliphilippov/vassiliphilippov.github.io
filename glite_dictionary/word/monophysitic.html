Monophysitic

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɑːnəfəˈsɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəfɪˈsɪtɪk / (BrE)
Relating to Monophysitism or the Monophysites, a doctrine in Christian theology holding that Christ has a single divine nature.
relating to Monophysitism or Monophysites

Examples

The council debated the Monophysitic interpretation of Christ’s nature for several hours.
Early Christian controversies often centered on Monophysitic and Dyophysitic doctrines.
His thesis focused on the influence of Monophysitic beliefs in eastern Christianity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Monophysite - a person who believes in the single nature of Christ
  • Antonym: Dyophysitic - relating to the belief that Christ has two natures

Etymology

From Monophysite (from Greek 'monos' meaning single and 'physis' meaning nature) + -ic.
