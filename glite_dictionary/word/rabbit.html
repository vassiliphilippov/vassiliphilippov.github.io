rabbit

1. NOUN

/ ˈræbɪt / (AmE), / ˈræbɪt / (BrE)
the flesh of a rabbit or hare, used as food
rabbit or hare meat as food

Examples

The chef prepared a delicious stew using fresh rabbit.
For dinner, they served roasted rabbit with seasonal vegetables.
He had never tasted rabbit before visiting the French countryside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hare - the flesh of a hare eaten as food

4. NOUN

/ ˈræbɪt / (AmE), / ˈræbɪt / (BrE)
a small furry animal with long ears and a short tail, which lives in burrows and is sometimes kept as a pet or used for food
small burrowing animal with long ears

Examples

We spotted a rabbit hopping across the lawn early this morning.
Her pet rabbit loves to nibble on carrots.
Farmers sometimes keep rabbits for their meat and fur.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hare - a similar animal, larger and with longer legs and ears

Etymology

From Middle English 'rabet', of uncertain origin, possibly from Old French 'rabotte'.
