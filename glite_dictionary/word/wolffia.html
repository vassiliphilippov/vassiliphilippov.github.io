Wolffia

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊl.fi.ə / (AmE), / ˈwɒl.fi.ə / (BrE)
a genus of minute, rootless aquatic plants with tiny, globular fronds that float on or near the water surface, each bearing a single flower
a genus of tiny rootless aquatic plants

Examples

Wolffia is considered the smallest flowering plant in the world.
Many aquatic birds rely on dense mats of Wolffia as a food source.
Scientists study Wolffia for its rapid reproduction and unique biology.

Etymology

Named after German botanist Johann Friedrich Wolff (1759–1800).

2. NOUN

/ ˈwʊl.fi.ə / (AmE), / ˈwʊl.fi.ə / (BrE)
a genus of minute rootless aquatic plants with globular, floating fronds, each bearing a single flower
genus of tiny, rootless aquatic plants

Examples

Wolffia species are the smallest flowering plants known to science.
Aquatic ecologists study Wolffia to understand nutrient cycling in ponds and wetlands.
Due to their rapid growth, some researchers are investigating Wolffia as a potential food source.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duckweed - free-floating aquatic plants of the family Lemnaceae, which includes Wolffia

Etymology

Named after German botanist Johann Friedrich Wolff (1778–1806).
