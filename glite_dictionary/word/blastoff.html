blastoff

1. NOUN

/ ˈblæstˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈblɑːstɒf / (BrE)
the moment a missile, spacecraft, or rocket is launched from the ground
launching of a missile or spacecraft

Examples

NASA engineers gathered in the control room to witness the blastoff of the new Mars rover.
The excitement in the crowd was palpable as the countdown reached zero and blastoff occurred.
We watched the live coverage of the blastoff from our classroom, amazed by the power and noise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liftoff - the act of a rocket or spacecraft leaving the ground at the start of a flight

Etymology

Formed from 'blast' + 'off', referring to the explosive propulsion at launch.
