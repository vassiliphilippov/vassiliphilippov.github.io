poplar

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑː.plɚ / (AmE), / ˈpɒp.lə / (BrE)
a tall tree of northern temperate regions, typically with light, soft wood and catkin flowers, belonging to the genus Populus
type of tall temperate tree

Examples

The avenue was lined with tall poplar trees swaying gently in the breeze.
Many artists have painted landscapes featuring clusters of poplar in the background.
He used poplar wood to build lightweight furniture for his workshop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aspen - a type of poplar tree with trembling leaves
  • Synonym: willow - deciduous tree with slender branches, related to poplars

Etymology

From Latin 'populus', meaning 'poplar tree'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːplɚ / (AmE), / ˈpɒplə / (BrE)
The soft, lightweight, pale wood obtained from the poplar tree, often used for making furniture, plywood, and paper but not very durable.
soft, light wood from a poplar tree

Examples

The cabinet was made from poplar because of its smooth finish and affordable price.
Some musical instruments use poplar for their bodies due to its light weight.
He learned that poplar is not suitable for outdoor construction because it is not durable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poplar - a tall, fast-growing deciduous tree with catkins, of the genus Populus
