to re-explain

1. VERB

/ ˌriː ɪkˈspleɪn / (AmE), / ˌriː ɪkˈspleɪn / (BrE)
to explain something again, especially in a different or clearer way or from a new perspective
explain again or differently

Examples

Could you please re-explain the instructions so everyone understands?
After seeing their confused faces, she decided to re-explain the concept from a different angle.
Sometimes teachers have to re-explain difficult topics to ensure all students are following.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to explain - to make something clear by describing it in more detail or revealing relevant facts
