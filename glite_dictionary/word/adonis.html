Adonis

1. PROPN

/ əˈdoʊnɪs / (AmE), / əˈdəʊnɪs / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a handsome youth loved by Aphrodite and Persephone, noted for his beauty and tragic fate.
beautiful youth in Greek mythology

Examples

The painting depicts Adonis lying wounded in the arms of Aphrodite.
Many poems have been inspired by the tragic love story of Adonis and Persephone.
In the play, the character is compared to Adonis because of his remarkable looks.

Synset Examples

when Adonis died Zeus decreed that he should spend winters in the underworld with Persephone and spend summers with Aphrodite

Etymology

From Greek Adōnis, from Phoenician Adon ('lord').

a genus of flowering plants (buttercup family)

Examples

3. NOUN

/ əˈdɑːnɪs / (AmE), / əˈdəʊnɪs / (BrE)
A genus of annual or perennial flowering plants in the buttercup family (Ranunculaceae), known for their bright red or yellow flowers.
genus of flowering plants

Examples

The vivid red flowers of the Adonis make it a striking addition to spring gardens.
Botanists have classified several species under the genus Adonis for their medicinal properties.
Many wildflower enthusiasts seek out Adonis blooms during nature walks in early summer.

Etymology

From Greek Adōnis (Ἄδωνις), referencing the mythological figure Adonis, symbolizing beauty.
