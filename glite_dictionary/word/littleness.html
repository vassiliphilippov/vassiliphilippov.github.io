littleness

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪt(ə)lnəs / (AmE), / ˈlɪt(ə)lnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being small in size
smallness in size

Examples

The littleness of the kitten made everyone want to protect it.
He was surprised by the littleness of the old cottage.
Despite its littleness, the gemstone was incredibly valuable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smallness - the quality or state of being small
  • Antonym: bigness - the quality or state of being large

Etymology

From Middle English 'litel' (little) + '-ness' (noun-forming suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɪt.əl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈlɪt.əl.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality or condition of having little strength, force, or vigor; weakness or feebleness
lack of strength or vigor

Examples

Despite the littleness of his voice, his words were confident and clear.
She was aware of the littleness in her handshake, a sign of her exhaustion.
The author's writing captured the littleness of human power against nature.

Synset Examples

the smallness of her voice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weakness - the state or condition of lacking strength

Etymology

From 'little' + '-ness', forming a noun meaning 'the state of being little.'

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈlɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈlɪt.lɪnəs / (BrE)
pettiness or lack of generosity, especially in trivial matters
pettiness; lack of generosity

Examples

His littleness became apparent when he quibbled over a few cents.
She showed remarkable patience in the face of such littleness from her colleagues.
Their constant littleness about minor details made negotiations unpleasant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pettiness - concern with trivial or unimportant matters, especially in a mean-spirited way
  • Antonym: generosity - the quality of being kind and generous

Etymology

From 'little' + '-ness', Middle English 'litelnes'.
