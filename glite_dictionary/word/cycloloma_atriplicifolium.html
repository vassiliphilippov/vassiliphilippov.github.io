Cycloloma atriplicifolium

1. NOUN

/ saɪ.kəˌloʊ.mə æˌtrɪ.plɪ.sɪˈfoʊ.li.əm / (AmE), / saɪ.kəˌləʊ.mə æˌtrɪ.plɪ.sɪˈfəʊ.li.əm / (BrE)
A bushy annual weed native to central North America, characterized by greenish flowers and winged seeds.
North American bushy annual weed

Examples

Cycloloma atriplicifolium is commonly found along roadsides and disturbed areas across the Midwest.
The winged seeds of Cycloloma atriplicifolium help it spread efficiently during windy days.
Many gardeners consider Cycloloma atriplicifolium to be a troublesome weed due to its rapid growth.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'kyklos' (circle) and 'loma' (border or fringe), and Latin 'atriplicifolium' (with leaves like Atriplex).
