repository

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈpɑzəˌtɔri / (AmE), / rɪˈpɒzɪtəri / (BrE)
A place or facility where things are stored or kept for safekeeping.
facility for storage or safekeeping

Examples

The museum is a repository for ancient artifacts from around the world.
All the samples are kept in a secure repository for future study.
The library serves as a repository of historical documents and rare books.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: storehouse - a building for storing goods
  • Synonym: warehouse - a large building for storing goods
  • Synonym: depository - a place where something is deposited for storage

Etymology

From Latin 'repositum' (storehouse), from 'reponere' (to put back).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈpɑːzɪˌtɔri / (AmE), / rɪˈpɒzɪtəri / (BrE)
a person who is trusted with secrets or confidential information
person trusted with secrets

Examples

She became the repository of all his fears and ambitions.
As a trusted repository of their secrets, she never betrayed their confidence.
He was the repository for generations of family stories and private matters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confidant - a person with whom one shares a secret or private matter

Etymology

From Latin 'repositōrium' (storehouse), from 'reponere' (to put back, store).

3. NOUN

/ rɪˈpɑːzɪˌtɔri / (AmE), / rɪˈpɒzɪtəri / (BrE)
a burial vault, often used for the interment of a notable person
burial vault or tomb, esp. for a notable person

Examples

The king's remains were placed in a grand repository beneath the cathedral.
Scholars visited the ancient repository to pay tribute to the famous poet.
A marble repository was constructed to honor the city's founding leader.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tomb - an enclosed burial place for a corpse
  • Synonym: mausoleum - a large stately tomb or burial place

Etymology

From Latin repositōrium (storehouse), from reponere (to put back).
