Diapensiales

1. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪəˌpɛnsiˈeɪliz / (AmE), / ˌdaɪəˌpɛnsiˈeɪliːz / (BrE)
a former order of flowering plants, now usually included in the family Diapensiaceae in some botanical classifications
former order of flowering plants

Examples

The classification of Diapensiales has changed over time as botanical understanding has improved.
Some taxonomists no longer recognize Diapensiales as a separate order.
In older texts, species now in Diapensiaceae were placed within the Diapensiales.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Diapensiaceae - a family of small evergreen shrubs; includes genus Diapensia

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on the type genus Diapensia plus the taxonomic suffix -ales.
