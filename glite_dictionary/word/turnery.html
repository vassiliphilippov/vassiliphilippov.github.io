turnery

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɜrnəri / (AmE), / ˈtɜːnəri / (BrE)
articles or products made by shaping wood or other materials on a lathe.
products made on a lathe

Examples

Skilled craftsmen produced intricate turnery for the restoration of the old mansion.
The exhibit featured antique turnery such as candlesticks and decorative spindles.
He collects Victorian turnery from English workshops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lathe - a machine for shaping wood, metal, or other materials

Etymology

From 'turn' + '-ery', denoting a place or products of turning (shaping on a lathe).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːnəri / (AmE), / ˈtɜːnəri / (BrE)
a workshop or establishment where objects, especially of wood, are shaped on a lathe
workshop for shaping objects on a lathe

Examples

The craftsman worked with great skill in his turnery, creating elegant wooden bowls.
Many of the town's residents found employment at the local turnery during the 19th century.
The museum displayed old tools and finished products from a Victorian-era turnery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lathe - machine for shaping wood, metal, or other material

Etymology

From 'turn' (to shape on a lathe) + '-ery' (denoting a place of activity)
