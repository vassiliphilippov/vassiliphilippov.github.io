campion

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæmp.i.ən / (AmE), / ˈkæm.pɪ.ən / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Silene, typically with brightly colored flowers, often found in meadows and grasslands
a flowering plant of the genus Silene

Examples

A pink campion bloomed along the edge of the country lane.
Gardeners often choose campion for its bright, decorative flowers.
The meadow was full of wild campion and other native plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catchfly - another plant of the genus Silene, known for sticky stems

Etymology

From Old French 'campion' (corn cockle), possibly from Late Latin 'campio' meaning 'champion', referring to its showy appearance.
