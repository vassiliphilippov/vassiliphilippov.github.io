sorter

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔrtər / (AmE), / ˈsɔːtə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to arrange or separate items, such as mail, according to type or category
person who sorts items

Examples

The sorter at the post office organizes thousands of letters every day.
She worked as a sorter in the warehouse, separating parcels by destination.
Automated sorters have increased the efficiency of mail delivery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classifier - a person or thing that classifies items
  • Antonym: assembler - a person who puts things together, often as a job

Etymology

From sort + -er, forming an agent noun meaning 'one who sorts'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɔrt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɔːtə / (BrE)
a machine or device that sorts things into different classes or categories, such as letters, data cards, or objects
machine for sorting items into groups

Examples

The mailroom installed a new sorter to automatically arrange letters by postcode.
A card sorter was once essential equipment for managing large batches of punched cards in early computing.
The recycling center uses a laser-based sorter to separate different types of plastics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classifier - a machine or device that classifies or arranges items

Etymology

From sort + -er, formed in English to denote something or someone that sorts.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04268226-n
Synset POS: n
