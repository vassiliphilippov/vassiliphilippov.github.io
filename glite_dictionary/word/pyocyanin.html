pyocyanin

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪ.oʊˈsaɪ.ən.ɪn / (AmE), / ˌpʌɪ.ə(ʊ)ˈsʌɪ.ən.ɪn / (BrE)
a toxic blue crystalline antibiotic derived from certain bacteria, found in green pus and used in scientific research
toxic blue bacterial antibiotic
medical

Examples

The laboratory identified pyocyanin in the sample, confirming the presence of Pseudomonas aeruginosa.
Researchers are studying how pyocyanin contributes to infection and tissue damage.
Green pus in wounds often contains the pigment pyocyanin produced by certain bacteria.

Etymology

From Greek 'pyo' (pus) + 'kyanos' (blue).
