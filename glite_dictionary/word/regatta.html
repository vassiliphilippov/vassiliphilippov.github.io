regatta

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈɡɑtə / (AmE), / rɪˈɡatə / (BrE)
A series of boat races, typically involving rowing or sailing, held as a sporting event.
series of competitive boat races

Examples

The annual regatta attracts teams from universities all over the world.
Spectators lined the riverbanks to watch the exciting regatta unfold.
Winning the regatta was a major achievement for the rowing club.

Etymology

From Italian 'regata' (competition, race).
