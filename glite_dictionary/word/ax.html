/ æks / (AmE), / æks / (BrE)
to chop or split something using an ax

Examples

She axes the branches before stacking them.

2. NOUN

/ æks / (AmE), / æks / (BrE)
A tool with a heavy bladed head mounted across a handle, used for chopping wood and other materials.
tool for chopping, with heavy blade

Examples

He used an ax to split the firewood for the campfire.
The lumberjack skillfully swung his ax into the thick tree trunk.
An old, rusty ax was found in the shed behind the house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hatchet - a small ax with a short handle
  • Antonym: saw - a tool with a toothed blade for cutting through materials, especially wood

Etymology

From Old English æx, from Proto-Germanic *agwisō.

3. VERB

/ æks / (AmE), / æks / (BrE)
(informal) to put an end to suddenly; to terminate or cancel something, especially a program, project, or job.
end or cancel suddenly (informal)
informal

Examples

The company decided to ax 200 jobs due to budget cuts.
With disappointing ratings, the network chose to ax the new TV series.
After months of uncertainty, the city council finally axed the expansion project.

Synset Examples

they will ax the research program

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cancel - to decide that something planned will not happen
  • Synonym: sack - to dismiss someone from a job
  • Antonym: keep - to continue without ending or terminating

Etymology

Extension of 'ax' (noun), originally meaning to cut with an axe, figuratively used for sudden termination.
