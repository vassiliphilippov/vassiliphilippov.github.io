Anoa mindorensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌænoʊ.ə mɪnˌdɔːˈrɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˌænəʊ.ə mɪnˌdɒˈrɛnsɪs / (BrE)
a small, endangered wild buffalo native to the island of Mindoro in the Philippines, also called the Mindoro dwarf buffalo or tamaraw
small wild buffalo of Mindoro, Philippines

Examples

Conservationists are working hard to protect the endangered Anoa mindorensis from habitat loss.
The Anoa mindorensis, also known as the tamaraw, is unique to the island of Mindoro.
Sightings of the elusive Anoa mindorensis have become increasingly rare in recent decades.

Etymology

From scientific Latin Anoa (genus of dwarf buffalo) + species name from Mindoro (island in the Philippines) + -ensis (Latin, 'pertaining to').
