well-worn

1. ADJ

/ ˌwɛlˈwɔrn / (AmE), / ˌwɛlˈwɔːn / (BrE)
(of phrases, remarks, or ideas) used or repeated so often that it is no longer interesting or effective; overfamiliar through overuse.
overused or overfamiliar (of phrases or ideas)

Examples

His speech was full of well-worn clichés that failed to inspire the audience.
She quoted a well-worn proverb everyone had heard before.
The movie follows a well-worn formula that has been used countless times.

Synset Examples

his remarks were trite and commonplace
hackneyed phrases
a stock answer
repeating threadbare jokes
parroting some timeworn axiom
the trite metaphor ‘hard as nails’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trite - lacking originality or freshness; overused and unoriginal
  • Synonym: hackneyed - (of a phrase or idea) lacking significance through having been overused
  • Antonym: original - not derived from something else; fresh or novel

2. ADJ

/ ˌwɛlˈwɔrn / (AmE), / ˌwɛlˈwɔːn / (BrE)
showing clear signs of heavy use or handling, often from being used many times
showing signs of much use

Examples

His well-worn jacket had several patches and faded colors.
The well-worn path through the forest showed that many people had traveled there.
She picked up her well-worn copy of the novel and started reading again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worn - damaged or changed from use or age
  • Antonym: pristine - in its original condition; unspoiled
