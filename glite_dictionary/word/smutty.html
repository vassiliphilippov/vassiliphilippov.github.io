smutty

1. ADJ

/ ˈsmʌti / (AmE), / ˈsmʌti / (BrE)
containing or referring to obscene or indecent language or material; dirty or vulgar.
obscene or indecent; dirty
vulgar

Examples

He told a smutty joke that made everyone at the table blush.
The comedian’s routines were often criticized for being too smutty.
She refused to read the smutty magazine her brother had bought.

Synset Examples

had a filthy mouth
foul language
smutty jokes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filthy - containing explicit obscenity or indecency
  • Antonym: clean - free from indecent or obscene content

Etymology

From 'smut' meaning a stain or mark, later used figuratively for obscene material.

2. ADJ

/ ˈsmʌti / (AmE), / ˈsmʌti / (BrE)
(of an object or person) covered in or marked with soot, dirt, or grime.
soiled with dirt or soot

Examples

His shoes were smutty after walking through the dusty street.
She wore a smutty apron by the end of her shift in the bakery.
The children’s faces became smutty after playing around the campfire.

Synset Examples

with feet black from playing outdoors
his shirt was black within an hour

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dirty - covered with dirt; unclean
  • Antonym: clean - free from dirt, marks, or stains

Etymology

From 'smut' (dirt, soot) + -y.
