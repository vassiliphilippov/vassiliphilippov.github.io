eroded

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈroʊdɪd / (AmE), / ɪˈrəʊdɪd / (BrE)
worn away or gradually destroyed by natural forces such as water, wind, or ice
worn away by natural forces

Examples

The coastline had been eroded by the relentless pounding of the waves.
The ancient monument's carvings are now barely visible because the stone has eroded over centuries.
Wind and rain have slowly eroded the surface of the mountain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weathered - worn and changed in appearance by exposure to weather
  • Antonym: intact - not damaged or impaired in any way

Etymology

From Latin 'erodere' (to gnaw away).
