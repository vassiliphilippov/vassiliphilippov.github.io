pantheist

1. NOUN

/ ˈpænθiɪst / (AmE), / ˈpænθɪɪst / (BrE)
A person who believes that God and the universe are identical, or that everything collectively is God.
believer that God and the universe are one

Examples

As a pantheist, she sees divinity in every part of nature.
The philosopher Spinoza is often considered a pantheist.
Being a pantheist influences his appreciation of the universe and life.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: atheist - person who disbelieves or lacks belief in God
  • Synonym: theist - person who believes in the existence of a god or gods

Etymology

From Greek 'pan' (all) + 'theos' (god).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpænθiɪst / (AmE), / ˈpanθɪɪst / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of pantheism or pantheists.
relating to pantheism

Examples

The author presented a pantheist perspective in her writings about the natural world.
Some philosophers advocate a pantheist worldview, believing God is synonymous with the universe.
In the discussion, he was criticized for his pantheist interpretations of religious texts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pantheistic - of or relating to pantheism

Etymology

From Greek 'pan-' (all) + 'theos' (god), via French 'panthéiste'.
