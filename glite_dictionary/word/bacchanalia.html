bacchanalia

1. NOUN

/ ˌbækəˈneɪliə / (AmE), / ˌbækəˈneɪlɪə / (BrE)
A wild celebration or party marked by excessive drinking, revelry, and often promiscuity.
wild, drunken party or revelry

Examples

The senator was disgraced after photos surfaced of him at a bacchanalia in the countryside.
Some ancient Roman festivals became notorious for turning into wild bacchanalia.
The novel describes the university party as a modern-day bacchanalia filled with music and excess.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orgy - wild party especially involving excessive drinking and sexual activity
  • Synonym: revelry - lively and noisy festivities
  • Antonym: sobriety - state of not being intoxicated or of moderation

Etymology

From Latin Bacchanalia, festivals honoring Bacchus, the Roman god of wine.
