Bacchanalia

1. NOUN

/ ˌbækəˈneɪliə / (AmE), / ˌbækəˈneɪliə / (BrE)
In ancient Rome and Greece, a festival of wild and drunken revelry held in honor of Bacchus (Dionysus).
ancient festival of Bacchus; wild revelry

Examples

The Roman Senate famously tried to suppress the Bacchanalia because of their wild excesses.
During the Bacchanalia, worshippers would dance, sing, and indulge in wine to honor their god.
Today, the term Bacchanalia is sometimes used to describe any party marked by wild revelry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orgy - wild party involving excessive drinking and unrestrained behavior
  • Synonym: revelry - lively and noisy festivities, often involving drinking

Etymology

From Latin Bacchanalia, plural of Bacchanal, referring to the festivals of Bacchus.
