bitthead

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪtˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈbɪtˌhɛd / (BrE)
The top or upper end of a bitt, a post on a ship used for securing ropes or cables.
upper part of a ship's bitt

Examples

The sailor tied the mooring line securely around the bitthead.
Rust had formed on the bitthead after months at sea.
Maintenance of the bitthead is crucial for safe docking operations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bitt - a strong post of wood or iron on a ship's deck used for securing lines

Etymology

From 'bitt' (a strong post on a ship) + 'head' (top or upper part).
