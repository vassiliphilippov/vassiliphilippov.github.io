windward

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪndˌwɝd / (AmE), / ˈwɪnd.wəd / (BrE)
the direction from which the wind is blowing, especially as contrasted with the side sheltered from the wind
direction from which the wind blows

Examples

The sailors adjusted their course toward the windward to make better speed.
You should always position the tent on the windward side of the hill for stability.
Clouds often gather on the windward slopes of the island.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: leeward - the direction away from the wind or the sheltered side

Etymology

Formed from 'wind' + 'ward' (toward); 'ward' from Old English 'weard' (guard, direction).

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪndˌwɚd / (AmE), / ˈwɪndˌwəd / (BrE)
the side or direction from which the wind is blowing, especially with regard to a specific object or place
side facing the wind

Examples

The tents were set up on the windward side to catch the cool breeze.
Plants grow shorter on the windward slopes of the mountain due to harsh winds.
The windward side of the island receives most of the rainfall.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: leeward - the side sheltered from the wind

Etymology

From 'wind' + 'ward' (direction); first recorded in English in the 16th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08627580-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈwɪndwɚd / (AmE), / ˈwɪndwəd / (BrE)
on or facing the side exposed to the wind, especially in relation to land, sea, or an object
on the side facing the wind

Examples

The hikers set up camp on the windward side of the mountain for better ventilation.
Sailors adjusted their course to keep the ship on the windward edge of the channel.
The windward slopes receive much more rainfall than the leeward ones.

Synset Examples

the windward islands

Related Terms

  • Antonym: leeward - on or toward the side sheltered from the wind

Etymology

From 'wind' + '-ward', originating in Middle English as an adjective for the direction from which the wind blows.

4. ADV

/ ˈwɪnd.wɚd / (AmE), / ˈwɪnd.wəd / (BrE)
Towards the direction from which the wind is blowing.
toward the direction of the wind

Examples

The sailors struggled to steer windward against the strong current.
To reach the cove, we had to tack windward for several miles.
The fleet moved windward at dawn to gain a tactical advantage.

Synset Examples

they were sailing windward

Related Terms

  • Antonym: leeward - away from the wind; the side sheltered from the wind

Etymology

From 'wind' + 'ward', Old English 'wind' (air in motion) + 'weard' (toward).
