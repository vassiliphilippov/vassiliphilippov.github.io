to customise

1. VERB

/ ˈkʌstəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkʌstəmaɪz / (BrE)
to modify or make something according to individual or specific requirements
modify to suit requirements
british

Examples

You can customise your phone with different themes and ringtones.
The chef is happy to customise any dish to meet dietary restrictions.
She decided to customise her laptop with unique stickers and decals.

Synset Examples

customize a car

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to customize - to make according to requirements (American spelling)

Etymology

From 'custom' + '-ise'; influenced by French 'customiser', based on 'custom'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00388590-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈkʌstəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkʌstəmaɪz / (BrE)
to make or modify something according to a particular individual's requirements or preferences
make to individual specifications
british

Examples

They decided to customise their wedding invitations with a unique design.
You can customise your computer settings to suit your needs.
The car was customised with special wheels and a new paint job.

Synset Examples

I had this desk custom-made for me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to customize - American spelling: make or modify to individual requirements
  • Antonym: to standardize - to make uniform or bring to a standard form

Etymology

From 'custom' + '-ise', modelled after American 'customize'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01626526-v
Synset POS: v
