importunately

1. ADV

/ ˌɪm.pɔrˈtju.nət.li / (AmE), / ˌɪm.pɔːˈtjuː.nət.li / (BrE)
In a persistent and urgent manner that is often considered annoying or intrusive.
persistently and urgently, often annoyingly

Examples

He knocked importunately at the door until someone finally answered.
The journalist importunately demanded answers from the reluctant official.
She called her friend importunately, refusing to give up until he agreed to help.

Synset Examples

‘You must help me,’ she said imploringly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: urgently - in a way that expresses a sense of pressing importance
  • Synonym: insistently - in a persistent and repeated way
  • Antonym: politely - in a respectful and considerate manner

Etymology

From 'importunate', from Latin 'importunus' (inconvenient, unsuitable).
