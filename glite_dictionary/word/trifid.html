trifid

1. ADJ

/ ˈtraɪfɪd / (AmE), / ˈtraɪfɪd / (BrE)
(of a leaf, petal, or other organ) divided into three lobes or segments.
divided into three lobes or parts

Examples

The flower has trifid petals that distinguish it from other species.
The trifid leaves made identification of the plant much easier for the botanists.
You can often see trifid structures in marine algae under a microscope.

Synset Examples

trifid petals

Etymology

From Latin 'trifidus' meaning 'three-cleft': 'tri-' (three) + 'findere' (to split).
