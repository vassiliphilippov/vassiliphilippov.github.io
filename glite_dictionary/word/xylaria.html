Xylaria

1. NOUN

/ zaɪˈlɛəriə / (AmE), / zaɪˈlɑːrɪə / (BrE)
A genus of fungi characterized by erect, black, woody fruiting bodies with perithecia concentrated in the upper parts, belonging to the family Xylariaceae.
a genus of fungi with woody, black fruiting bodies

Examples

Xylaria species are commonly found growing on decaying wood in forests.
The most famous member of the genus Xylaria is Xylaria polymorpha, also known as dead man's fingers.
Mycologists collected several specimens of Xylaria for further examination under the microscope.

Etymology

From Greek xylon (wood) + aria (a suffix), referring to the genus' wood-inhabiting nature.

2. NOUN

/ zaɪˈlɛəriə / (AmE), / zaɪˈlɑːrɪə / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Xylariaceae, characterized by perithecia located in the upper part of erect, black, woody fruiting bodies (stromata).
genus of black woody fungi

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species of Xylaria growing on decaying wood in the forest.
The Xylaria genus is known for its distinctive black, finger-like fruiting bodies.
Some species of Xylaria play an important role in decomposing plant material.

Etymology

From Greek xylon (wood) + Latin -aria (connected with), referring to its wood-associated growth.
