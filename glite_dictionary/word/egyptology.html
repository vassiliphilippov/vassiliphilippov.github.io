Egyptology

1. NOUN

/ iːˌdʒɪpˈtɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / iːˌdʒɪpˈtɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the scientific study of the history, language, literature, religion, and artifacts of ancient Egypt
study of ancient Egypt

Examples

After earning her degree in Egyptology, she joined an archaeological excavation near Luxor.
His book explores the impact of Egyptology on modern interpretations of ancient cultures.
Interest in Egyptology surged following the discovery of Tutankhamun's tomb.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archaeology - the study of human history through excavation and analysis of artifacts

Etymology

From French égyptologie, from égypt- (Egypt) + -logie (study of), following the pattern of similar academic terms.
