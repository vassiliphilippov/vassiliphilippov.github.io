apostate

1. ADJ

/ əˈpɑːsteɪt / (AmE), / əˈpɒsteɪt / (BrE)
not faithful or no longer loyal to a religion, party, or cause; having abandoned former beliefs or allegiance
having abandoned one's faith or allegiance

Examples

Some saw his apostate views as a betrayal of the movement's ideals.
Several apostate members publicly criticized the church's new direction.
He was labeled apostate after openly rejecting the party line.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: faithful - remaining loyal and steadfast; keeping faith

Etymology

From Latin apostata, from Greek apostatēs (deserter, runaway slave), from aphistēmi (to revolt).

2. NOUN

/ əˈpɑːsteɪt / (AmE), / əˈpɒsteɪt / (BrE)
a person who renounces or abandons their religion, political party, or cause, especially in a way considered disloyal or traitorous
person who renounces a faith or cause

Examples

He was labeled an apostate after abandoning the church to join a rival group.
The regime hunted down every known apostate and punished them harshly.
Once a committed activist, she became an apostate to the movement after the scandal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: traitor - a person who betrays their country, cause, or friends
  • Antonym: loyalist - a person who remains loyal to a cause or party

Etymology

From Late Latin apostata (renegade; runaway slave), from Greek apostatēs (deserter).

Synset POS: n
