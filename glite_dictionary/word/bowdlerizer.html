bowdlerizer

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊd.ləˌraɪ.zər / (AmE), / ˈbəʊd.ləˌraɪ.zə / (BrE)
a person who edits a text by removing allegedly offensive or objectionable words or passages
person who removes offensive content from texts

Examples

The new editor acted as a bowdlerizer, cutting several scenes from the novel.
Some readers criticized him as a bowdlerizer for sanitizing the language of classic works.
She refused to be a bowdlerizer and insisted the play be performed in its original form.

Synset Examples

Thomas Bowdler was a famous expurgator

Related Terms

  • Synonym: censor - a person who examines material and removes or suppresses unacceptable parts

Etymology

From the name of Thomas Bowdler (1754–1825), who published expurgated editions of Shakespeare.
