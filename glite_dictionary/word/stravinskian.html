Stravinskian

1. ADJ

/ strəˈvɪnskiən / (AmE), / strəˈvɪnskiən / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of the style or manner of Igor Stravinsky, especially his music.
in the style of Stravinsky

Examples

The new symphony features Stravinskian rhythms and bold, unexpected harmonies.
Her choreography had a distinctly Stravinskian edge, full of angular movements and irregular patterns.
Critics praised the opera's Stravinskian blend of modernism and tradition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Stravinsky - Igor Stravinsky, Russian-born composer known for works like The Rite of Spring

Etymology

From the name of Igor Stravinsky (1882–1971), Russian-born composer, with English adjectival suffix -ian.
