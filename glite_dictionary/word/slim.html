slim

1. ADJ

/ slɪm / (AmE), / slɪm / (BrE)
small in amount, degree, or number; not large or substantial
small in amount or degree

Examples

There's only a slim chance we'll catch the last train.
The company reported a slim profit margin this quarter.
Our hopes of success are slim after the recent setback.

Synset Examples

slender wages
a slim chance of winning
a slender surplus

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slender - small in amount or degree
  • Synonym: meager - lacking in quantity or quality
  • Antonym: substantial - of considerable amount or importance

2. VERB

/ slɪm / (AmE), / slɪm / (BrE)
to become thinner or lose weight, often for reasons of health or appearance
to lose weight; become thinner

Examples

She decided to slim before her wedding and started exercising regularly.
Many people try to slim by following a strict diet plan.
He managed to slim after giving up sugary drinks and fast food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to slim down - to become thinner or reduce weight
  • Antonym: to fatten - to make or become fatter
Source: WordNet

3. ADJ

/ slɪm / (AmE), / slɪm / (BrE)
having a thin, delicate, or slender build; not thick or bulky
thin or slender in build

Examples

She looked especially slim in her new black dress.
The model maintained a slim figure through regular exercise and a healthy diet.
He slipped through the narrow gap thanks to his slim build.

Synset Examples

she was slender as a willow shoot is slender
a slim girl with straight blonde hair
watched her slight figure cross the street

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slender - gracefully thin or narrow in form
  • Synonym: thin - having little fat or substance; not thick
  • Antonym: plump - slightly fat in a pleasant way
  • Degree Higher: skinny - very thin, often unhealthily so

Etymology

Originated in the early 17th century, perhaps from Low German 'slim' meaning 'slender, bad, weak'.
