matrikin

1. NOUN

/ ˈmætrɪˌkɪn / (AmE), / ˈmætrɪˌkɪn / (BrE)
A person or people related through one's mother's side of the family.
relative(s) on the mother's side

Examples

Genealogists traced her matrikin back to the 18th century.
Anthropologists observed that inheritance was often restricted to one's matrikin in that culture.
Both matrikin and patrikin may influence a person's legal standing in some societies.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: patrikin - relatives on the father's side

Etymology

From Latin 'mater' (mother) and 'kin'.
