Zhou Enlai

1. PROPN

/ dʒoʊ ɛnˈlaɪ / (AmE), / dʒəʊ ɛnˈlaɪ / (BrE)
(1898–1976) Chinese revolutionary and communist leader who served as the first Premier of the People's Republic of China and played a major role in Chinese politics and diplomacy.
Chinese revolutionary and leader, 1898–1976

Examples

Zhou Enlai was instrumental in shaping modern China's foreign policy.
Many historians credit Zhou Enlai with helping to improve Sino-Western relations during the 1970s.
During his tenure as Premier, Zhou Enlai worked closely with Mao Zedong.
