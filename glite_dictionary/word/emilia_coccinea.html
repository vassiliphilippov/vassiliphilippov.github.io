Emilia coccinea

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmɪliə kɒkˈsɪniə / (AmE), / ɪˈmɪliə kɒkˈsiːniə / (BrE)
A tropical African annual plant with scarlet tassel-shaped flower heads, sometimes classified in the genus Cacalia.
tropical African plant with scarlet flowers

Examples

The bright red flowers of Emilia coccinea attract butterflies and bees to the garden.
In botanical gardens, Emilia coccinea is often grown for its ornamental value.
Some taxonomists place Emilia coccinea in the genus Cacalia, though it is commonly known as tassel flower.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus name Emilia (named after Italian botanist Emilio) and coccinea, meaning 'scarlet' in Latin.
