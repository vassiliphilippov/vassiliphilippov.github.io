rubric

1. NOUN

/ ˈruː.brɪk / (AmE), / ˈruː.brɪk / (BrE)
a set of directions or rules for the conduct of Christian church services, often printed in red in prayer books
instructions for church services (often printed in red)

Examples

The prayer book includes a rubric outlining the steps for communion.
According to the rubric, the congregation should stand at this point in the service.
Clergy members consult the rubric to ensure proper liturgical procedure.

Etymology

From Latin rubrica (red ochre, red earth), because such directions were traditionally printed in red.
