levator

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈveɪtər / (AmE), / lɪˈveɪtə / (BrE)
(Anatomy) A muscle that lifts or elevates a part of the body, such as the eyelid or lip.
muscle that lifts a body part
medical

Examples

The levator muscle helps you raise your upper eyelid.
Injury to the levator can make it difficult to smile evenly.
Doctors identified weakness in her levator after the accident.

Etymology

From Latin 'levare' (to raise, lift).
