serviceberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜr.vɪsˌbɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.vɪsˌbɛr.i / (BrE)
any of several North American shrubs or small trees of the genus Amelanchier, with white flowers and edible blue-black or purplish berries
North American shrub/tree with edible berries

Examples

The serviceberry trees in our yard are covered with delicate white flowers each spring.
She made a delicious pie from the ripe serviceberries she picked near the river.
Many birds are attracted to serviceberry bushes because of their sweet fruit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shadbush - another name for Amelanchier species
  • Synonym: juneberry - Amelanchier species known for fruiting in June

Etymology

Possibly a variant of 'sarvisberry,' from 'service tree,' by folk etymology. The name may relate to the timing of flowering near Easter 'service' in Appalachia.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɝː.vɪsˌbɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.vɪsˌbɛr.i / (BrE)
the edible purple or red berry of certain North American trees and shrubs of the genus Amelanchier
edible berry from the Amelanchier plant

Examples

The serviceberry is often used in jams and pies because of its sweet taste.
Birds are attracted to the serviceberries that ripen in early summer.
She picked a bowl of fresh serviceberries from the bush in her backyard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: juneberry - another common name for the edible berry of Amelanchier

Etymology

From 'service' in 'service tree' (a related plant name) and 'berry', the fruit.
