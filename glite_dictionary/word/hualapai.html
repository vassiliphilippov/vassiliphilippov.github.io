Hualapai

1. NOUN

/ ˌwɑːləˈpaɪ / (AmE), / ˌwɑːləˈpaɪ / (BrE)
the Yuman language spoken by the Hualapai people of northwestern Arizona
a Yuman language of northwestern Arizona

Examples

Few people today are fluent in Hualapai, making language preservation efforts especially important.
Anthropologists have documented traditional stories in Hualapai as part of their research.
She is learning to speak Hualapai from her grandparents to keep the language alive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yuman - a family of languages spoken in the southwestern United States and northern Mexico

Etymology

From the Hualapai people's autonym, meaning 'People of the Tall Pines' in their own language.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɑːləˌpaɪ / (AmE), / ˈwɑːləˌpaɪ / (BrE)
A member of a Native American people indigenous to the Colorado River valley in northwestern Arizona.
member of a Native American people of Arizona

Examples

Several Hualapai elders attended the cultural festival to share their traditions.
The Hualapai have a reservation near the Grand Canyon in Arizona.
He is proud to be a Hualapai and works to preserve the language and customs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Havasupai - a closely related Native American people of Arizona

Etymology

From the Hualapai word 'Hwal’bay', meaning 'people of the tall pines'.
