Xerxes I

1. PROPN

/ ˈzɜrksiːz ðə fɜrst / (AmE), / ˈzɜːksiːz ðə fɜːst / (BrE)
(c. 518–465 BC) King of Persia who led a major invasion of Greece, winning the Battle of Thermopylae but ultimately suffering defeat; son of Darius I.
king of Persia, led invasion of Greece

Examples

Xerxes I is remembered for his ambitious campaign against Greece during the early fifth century BC.
Many historians study the reign of Xerxes I to understand the rise and fall of the Achaemenid Empire.
The defeat of Xerxes I at the Battle of Salamis marked a turning point in the Greco-Persian Wars.

Etymology

From Greek Ξέρξης (Xérxēs), from Old Persian Xšayārša.
