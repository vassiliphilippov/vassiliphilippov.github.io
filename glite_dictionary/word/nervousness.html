nervousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɜːrvəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈnɜːvəsnəs / (BrE)
A feeling of worry, anxiety, or unease, often about something with an uncertain outcome.
a state of worry or anxiety

Examples

Despite his nervousness, he spoke confidently in front of the audience.
Her nervousness was obvious as she waited for the exam results.
A sense of nervousness filled the room before the big announcement.

Synset Examples

he suffered an attack of nerves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anxiety - A feeling of worry, nervousness, or unease about something with an uncertain outcome
  • Antonym: calmness - A mental state of being free from agitation or strong emotion

Etymology

From 'nervous' + '-ness', with 'nervous' from Latin 'nervosus' (sinewy, vigorous).

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɜrvəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈnɜːvəsnəs / (BrE)
A feeling of anxiety or unease; the state of being nervous.
state of being nervous or anxious

Examples

She could not hide her nervousness before the big presentation.
His persistent nervousness made it difficult for him to relax during interviews.
There was a sense of nervousness in the crowd as the results were announced.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anxiety - a feeling of worry, nervousness, or unease
  • Antonym: calmness - the state of being free from agitation, nervousness, or anxiety

Etymology

From 'nervous' + '-ness', where 'nervous' comes from Latin 'nervosus' (sinewy, vigorous).

3. NOUN

/ ˈnɝː.vəs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈnɜː.vəs.nəs / (BrE)
a quality of being easily agitated or prone to anxiety; a sensitive or highly strung temperament
state of being nervous or highly strung

Examples

His nervousness was apparent during the job interview.
She tried to hide her nervousness before giving the big presentation.
Many actors feel a moment of nervousness before stepping onto the stage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anxiety - a feeling of worry, nervousness, or unease
  • Antonym: calmness - state of being free from agitation or excitement

Etymology

From nervous + -ness; 'nervous' from Latin nervosus (sinewy, vigorous).
