profusion

1. NOUN

/ prəˈfjuʒən / (AmE), / prəˈfjuːʒən / (BrE)
an extremely large amount or abundance of something
an abundance; large quantity

Examples

A profusion of wildflowers covered the hills in spring.
She admired the profusion of artwork in the gallery.
There was a profusion of choices on the restaurant's menu.

Synset Examples

the profusion of detail
the idiomatic richness of English

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abundance - a very large quantity of something
  • Antonym: scarcity - the state of being in short supply

Etymology

From Latin profusio (a pouring forth), from profundere (to pour forth).
