tragifarce

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrædʒiˌfɑrs / (AmE), / ˈtrædʒiˌfɑːs / (BrE)
A literary or dramatic work that blends tragic and farcical elements, creating an extreme form of tragicomedy.
extreme form of tragicomedy

Examples

The director's latest film is a bold tragifarce that keeps audiences laughing and cringing in equal measure.
Many critics described the absurd political standoff as a real-life tragifarce.
The play received praise for pushing the boundaries of tragifarce, mixing slapstick comedy with deep personal loss.

Synset Examples

The Suicide of Solitude, a ‘tragifarce’ production, is currently being performed by the local theatre troupe at Rochford Winery in Healesville.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tragicomedy - a play or story that contains both tragic and comic elements

Etymology

Formed by combining 'tragedy' and 'farce', on the model of related dramatic terms.
