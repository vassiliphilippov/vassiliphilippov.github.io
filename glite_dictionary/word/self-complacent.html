self-complacent

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛlf kəmˈpleɪsənt / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf kəmˈpleɪsənt / (BrE)
Showing excessive satisfaction with oneself or one's actions, often without awareness of possible faults or shortcomings.
overly self-satisfied

Examples

After receiving repeated praise, she grew self-complacent and stopped striving for improvement.
His self-complacent attitude prevented him from seeing his mistakes.
The manager warned the team not to become self-complacent despite recent successes.

Synset Examples

he had become complacent after years of success
his self-satisfied dignity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complacent - pleased with oneself; self-satisfied
  • Antonym: self-critical - inclined to find fault with oneself

Etymology

Formed from 'self-' and 'complacent,' which comes from Latin 'complacēre' (to please).
