quip

1. NOUN

/ kwɪp / (AmE), / kwɪp / (BrE)
a clever or witty remark, often made spontaneously
witty or clever remark

Examples

She always has a quip ready for every occasion.
His quip during the meeting made everyone laugh.
The comedian ended his set with a clever quip about politics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: witticism - a witty remark or saying
  • Synonym: joke - something said or done to provoke laughter
  • Antonym: insult - a disrespectful or scornful remark

Etymology

From Latin 'quippe' (indeed, forsooth), originally used ironically.

2. NOUN

/ kwɪp / (AmE), / kwɪp / (BrE)
a clever or witty remark or saying, often intended to amuse or provoke thought
witty or clever remark

Examples

He made a sharp quip during the meeting that made everyone laugh.
Her quick quip about the weather lightened the tense atmosphere.
Whenever awkward silence falls at dinner, Mark always has a quip ready.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: witticism - a clever or witty remark
  • Synonym: joke - something said to provoke laughter
  • Synonym: repartee - a quick, witty reply

Etymology

From Latin 'quippe' (indeed, to be sure), used ironically in English from the 16th century.

3. VERB

/ kwɪp / (AmE), / kwɪp / (BrE)
to say something witty or clever, often as a joke; to make a witty remark
make a witty or clever remark

Examples

She couldn't help but quip about the weather during the dull meeting.
Whenever the conversation grew tense, Mark would quip to lighten the mood.
"At least my coffee's still hot," she quipped after the power went out.

Synset Examples

The students were gagging during dinner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joke - to say something amusing or humorous
  • Synonym: jest - to speak in a joking manner

Etymology

From Latin 'quippe' (indeed, to be sure), used humorously in the 16th century.
