unbecomingness

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌnbɪˈkʌmɪŋnəs / (AmE), / ˌʌnbɪˈkʌmɪŋnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of not being appropriate, suitable, or fitting, especially in appearance or behavior.
quality of being inappropriate or unsuitable

Examples

His rude remarks displayed a certain unbecomingness that surprised everyone present.
There was an unbecomingness to her actions that did not reflect her usual professionalism.
The judge criticized the lawyer for the unbecomingness of his courtroom conduct.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unbecoming - not appropriate or suitable in appearance or behavior
  • Antonym: appropriateness - the quality of being suitable or proper

Etymology

Formed from 'unbecoming' + '-ness', patterning after other -ness nouns.

2. NOUN

/ ˌʌnbɪˈkʌmɪŋnəs / (AmE), / ˌʌnbɪˈkʌmɪŋnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of not being attractive, suitable, or appropriate for a particular situation.
state of not being attractive or appropriate

Examples

She worried about the unbecomingness of her casual attire at the formal dinner.
Critics commented on the unbecomingness of his rude remarks during the speech.
The unbecomingness of the color made her choose a different dress.

Synset Examples

Too stunned to resist, I allowed her to dress my hair, tying back the sidelocks with primrose ribbon, clucking over the unfeminine unbecomingness of my shoulder-length bob.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unbecoming - not appropriate or not attractive
  • Antonym: becomingness - the quality of being attractive or appropriate

Etymology

From unbecoming (adjective) + -ness (noun suffix).
