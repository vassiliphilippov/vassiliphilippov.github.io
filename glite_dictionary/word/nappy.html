nappy

1. ADJ

/ ˈnæpi / (AmE), / ˈnæpi / (BrE)
(of hair) having small, tight curls; tightly coiled or densely textured.
hair with small, tight curls

Examples

Her nappy hair looked beautiful styled in a natural afro.
He struggled to find hair products for his nappy curls.
The stylist specialized in caring for nappy textures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curly - having curls or a curved shape
  • Antonym: straight - (of hair) having no curls or waves

2. NOUN

/ ˈnæpi / (AmE), / ˈnæpi / (BrE)
A piece of soft cloth or other material fastened around a baby's bottom and between its legs to absorb and contain urine and feces; known as a diaper in American English.
baby's absorbent undergarment (British)
british

Examples

In the UK, parents often use reusable nappies made from cloth.
She changed the baby's nappy before putting him down for a nap.
It's important to dispose of a used nappy properly to prevent odors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diaper - baby's absorbent undergarment (American English)

Etymology

Shortened from napkin, itself from Middle English nappe (cloth).
