middlebrow

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪd.əlˌbraʊ / (AmE), / ˈmɪd.əlˌbraʊ / (BrE)
a person whose tastes or interests are regarded as neither highly intellectual nor very unsophisticated, but in between
someone with moderately cultured tastes

Examples

Despite being called a middlebrow, she enjoyed a wide range of books from popular to literary fiction.
His plays were dismissed as middlebrow entertainment, appealing to neither critics nor the masses.
The magazine aimed to attract the middlebrow reader with articles on art, travel, and culture.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: highbrow - a person with highly cultured or intellectual tastes
  • Antonym: lowbrow - a person with unsophisticated, unrefined tastes

Etymology

Formed by analogy with 'highbrow' and 'lowbrow', referring to perceived levels of culture or intellect.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪdəlˌbraʊ / (AmE), / ˈmɪdəlˌbraʊ / (BrE)
art, literature, or other cultural works regarded as having moderate intellectual or artistic value, appealing to average tastes
moderately intellectual art or literature

Examples

Many popular book clubs choose middlebrow novels that are easy to read but still thought-provoking.
The festival was criticized for featuring mostly middlebrow performances instead of challenging works.
He preferred avant-garde films to middlebrow entertainment commonly shown at mainstream theaters.

Synset Examples

The gallery features mostly middlebrow art that attracts tourists.
She enjoys middlebrow literature that doesn’t challenge her too much.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: highbrow - art or literature considered highly intellectual or cultured
  • Degree Lower: lowbrow - art or literature considered lacking intellectual or cultural value

Etymology

Formed from 'middle' + 'brow', patterned after 'highbrow' (intellectual) and 'lowbrow' (non-intellectual).
