huffily

1. ADV

/ ˈhʌfɪli / (AmE), / ˈhʌfɪli / (BrE)
In a manner showing annoyance or offence; irritably or indignantly.
in an annoyed or offended manner

Examples

She replied huffily when asked why she was late to the meeting.
He huffily shut the door behind him after the argument.
The child huffily refused to eat his vegetables.

Synset Examples

‘Don't bother to call me back,’ he said huffily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: huffy - easily annoyed or offended
  • Synonym: irritably - in an easily annoyed manner

Etymology

From "huffy" (irritable, easily offended) + "-ly" (adverbial suffix).
