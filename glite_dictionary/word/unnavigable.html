unnavigable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈnæv.ɪ.ɡə.bəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈnæv.ɪ.ɡə.bəl / (BrE)
(of a river, sea, or route) impossible or extremely difficult to navigate or travel through, especially by ship or boat.
impossible to navigate

Examples

Heavy rains left the mountain roads unnavigable for several days.
The ice made the river completely unnavigable during winter.
Thick fog can render an otherwise safe harbor unnavigable at night.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: navigable - (of a waterway or route) able to be navigated or traveled by ships or boats

Etymology

From un- (not) + navigable, from Latin navigabilis (that may be sailed upon).
