slender

1. ADJ

/ ˈslɛndər / (AmE), / ˈslɛndə / (BrE)
(of an amount or degree) small in quantity, value, or extent.
small in amount or degree

Examples

She had only a slender chance of passing the exam without studying.
After paying the bills, there was a slender amount of money left.
Their slender profits made expansion impossible this year.

Synset Examples

slender wages
a slim chance of winning
a slender surplus

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slim - small in amount or chance
  • Synonym: meager - lacking in quantity or quality
  • Antonym: ample - more than enough in amount or extent

Etymology

From Middle English slendre, slender, probably from Middle Dutch slinder, slendere.

2. ADJ

/ ˈslɛndɚ / (AmE), / ˈslɛndə / (BrE)
very narrow in width or diameter, especially compared to length or height
very narrow or thin

Examples

A slender column supported the balcony above.
The snake's slender body slipped silently through the grass.
She drew a slender line down the center of the page.

Synset Examples

a thin line across the page

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thin - having little thickness or depth; not wide
  • Antonym: broad - having a great width; wide

Etymology

From Middle English slender, likely from Old Dutch slinder (thin, slender).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02573296-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈslɛndər / (AmE), / ˈslɛndə / (BrE)
thin in a graceful or delicate way; having little width compared to length or height.
thin and narrow in proportion

Examples

A slender pole stood at the edge of the field.
The tree has a slender trunk that bends in the wind.
She admired the slender stems of the wildflowers.

Synset Examples

a slender pole

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slim - slightly built; thin in an attractive way
  • Synonym: thin - having little thickness; not fat
  • Antonym: thick - having a large distance between two opposite sides

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French 'slendre' or 'eslendre', of uncertain origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02423821-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ˈslɛn.dɚ / (AmE), / ˈslen.də / (BrE)
thin in an attractive or delicate way; having a graceful, slim build or shape
thin and graceful in build

Examples

The bride looked slender in her elegant white gown as she walked down the aisle.
He preferred a slender glass for his sparkling wine, believing it kept the bubbles fresh.
A willow tree has long, slender branches that sway gently in the breeze.

Synset Examples

she was slender as a willow shoot is slender
a slim girl with straight blonde hair
watched her slight figure cross the street

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slim - thin, especially in an attractive way
  • Synonym: skinny - very thin, sometimes to an unhealthy degree
  • Antonym: stocky - broad and sturdily built

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old English 'slinder', related to 'slinnan' (to recede, lean); possibly of Germanic origin.
