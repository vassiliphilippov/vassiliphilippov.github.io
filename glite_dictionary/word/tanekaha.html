tanekaha

1. NOUN

/ ˌtænəˈkɑː / (AmE), / ˌtanɪˈkɑː / (BrE)
a medium-tall New Zealand tree (Phyllocladus trichomanoides) known as the celery pine, with slender branches and thin, scale-like leaves
New Zealand celery pine tree

Examples

The timber of the tanekaha is valued for its strength and durability.
We saw a stand of tanekaha while hiking through the North Island forest.
The bark of the tanekaha has traditionally been used by Māori for dyeing flax.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celery pine - another name for Phyllocladus trichomanoides, the New Zealand tree

Etymology

From Māori tanekaha, the indigenous name for the tree.
