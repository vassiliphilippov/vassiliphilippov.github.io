Malvaviscus

1. NOUN

/ mæl.vəˈvɪs.kəs / (AmE), / mal.vəˈvɪs.kəs / (BrE)
A small genus of shrubs in the mallow family (Malvaceae), native to Central and South America, known as wax mallows.
genus of Central/South American shrubs (wax mallows)

Examples

The Malvaviscus genus includes shrubs commonly called wax mallows for their shiny, hibiscus-like flowers.
Gardeners in tropical regions often cultivate Malvaviscus for its bright red blooms that attract hummingbirds.
Botanists discovered a new Malvaviscus species growing wild in the rainforests of Central America.

Etymology

From Latin 'malva' (mallow) and 'viscus' (sticky), referring to the mucilaginous nature of the plant.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmæl.vəˈvɪs.kəs / (AmE), / ˌmal.vəˈvɪs.kəs / (BrE)
A small genus of shrubs native to Central and South America, known as wax mallows, characterized by brightly colored flowers.
a genus of South American wax mallows

Examples

The flowers of Malvaviscus are often used to attract hummingbirds to gardens.
Botanists classify the wax mallow under the genus Malvaviscus.
Several species in the Malvaviscus genus are valued for their ornamental blooms.

Etymology

From Latin *malva* (mallow) and *viscus* (sticky), referring to the mucilaginous nature of the plant.
