Bocconia

1. NOUN

/ bəˈkoʊniə / (AmE), / bəˈkəʊniə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American trees or shrubs in the poppy family, closely related to the genus Macleaya.
a genus of tropical American trees or shrubs

Examples

The leaves of Bocconia are large and deeply lobed, making the plant attractive for ornamental gardens.
Several species within the genus Bocconia are valued for their striking flowers and foliage.
Botanists are studying Bocconia to better understand its relationship with other members of the poppy family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Macleaya - a closely related genus of flowering plants in the poppy family

Etymology

Named after Paolo Boccone, an Italian botanist (1633–1704).
