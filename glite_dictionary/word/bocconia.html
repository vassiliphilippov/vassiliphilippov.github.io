bocconia

1. NOUN

/ bɑːˈkoʊniə / (AmE), / bɒˈkəʊniə / (BrE)
A small Central American tree or shrub of the genus Bocconia, having loose clusters of purple-tinted green flowers.
Central American tree with purple-tinted flowers

Examples

The garden features a beautiful bocconia in full bloom near the pond.
Many botanists are interested in the unique flowers of bocconia species.
The leaves of the bocconia shimmered in the afternoon sun.

Etymology

Named after Italian botanist Paolo Boccone (1633–1704).

2. NOUN

/ bəˈkoʊniə / (AmE), / bəˈkəʊniə / (BrE)
A herbaceous plant native to China and Japan, widely cultivated for its creamy white, plume-like clusters of flowers.
herbaceous plant with plume-like white flowers

Examples

The garden featured a tall bocconia with its striking panicles of white flowers.
She decided to plant bocconia along the fence for a dramatic floral display.
The unique foliage of bocconia makes it a favorite among landscape designers.

Etymology

From Latinized form of botanist Paolo Boccone's surname.
