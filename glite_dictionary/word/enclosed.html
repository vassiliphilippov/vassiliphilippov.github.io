enclosed

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈkloʊzd / (AmE), / ɪnˈkləʊzd / (BrE)
(of a space or object) closed in or surrounded by something; contained within boundaries.
surrounded or contained within boundaries

Examples

The children played in the enclosed garden behind the house.
Please find the enclosed document with all the necessary information.
They built an enclosed porch to enjoy the view while staying sheltered from the wind.

Synset Examples

an enclosed porch
an enclosed yard
the enclosed check is to cover shipping and handling

Related Terms

  • Antonym: open - not surrounded or closed in; the opposite state of being enclosed

Etymology

From Middle English *enclose*, from Old French *enclos*, past participle of *enclore*.
