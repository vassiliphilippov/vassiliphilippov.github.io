deferentially

1. ADV

/ ˌdɛfəˈrɛnʃəli / (AmE), / ˌdɛfəˈrɛnʃəli / (BrE)
in a way that shows respectful submission or deference to someone or something
in a respectful, deferential manner

Examples

He bowed deferentially to the elderly professor before entering the room.
The staff spoke deferentially to the hospital director during the meeting.
She listened deferentially as her mentor shared advice from years of experience.

Synset Examples

he listened deferentially

Related Terms

  • Synonym: respectfully - in a way that shows respect

Etymology

From 'deferential' + '-ly', formed from Latin 'deferre' (to yield).

2. ADV

/ ˌdɛfəˈrɛnʃəli / (AmE), / ˌdɛfəˈrɛnʃəli / (BrE)
in a manner that shows respect, submission, or yielding to someone else's authority or opinions
in a respectful or submissive manner

Examples

She nodded deferentially when her manager entered the room, eager to show her respect.
The assistant always spoke deferentially to the board members during meetings.
He stepped aside deferentially to let the guests pass first.

Synset Examples

he always acts so deferentially around his supervisor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: respectfully - in a way that shows politeness or deference
  • Antonym: insolently - in a manner that shows a lack of respect

Etymology

From deferential + -ly, ultimately from Latin deferre (to bring down, to yield).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r
