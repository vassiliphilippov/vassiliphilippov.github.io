capable

1. ADJ

/ ˈkeɪ.pə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ.pə.bəl / (BrE)
(of a thing or situation) potentially susceptible to or allowing a specified action or state; open to.
permitting; open to something

Examples

This statement is capable of several interpretations depending on the reader's perspective.
The data is capable of being misread if examined out of context.
Such an agreement is capable of causing confusion among those involved.

Synset Examples

a passage capable of misinterpretation
open to interpretation
an issue open to question
the time is fixed by the director and players and therefore subject to much variation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: susceptible - likely or liable to be influenced or harmed by a particular thing
  • Synonym: open - allowing or susceptible to something (as in 'open to interpretation')

Etymology

From Late Latin 'capabilis', from Latin 'capere' (to take, hold).

WordNet ID: oewn-02370095-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈkeɪ.pə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ.pə.bəl / (BrE)
having the ability, qualities, or resources needed to do something successfully
having the needed ability or qualities

Examples

She is a capable manager who handles every challenge with skill and confidence.
The new software is capable of processing large amounts of data in seconds.
Despite his age, he remains capable of hiking the most challenging trails.

Synset Examples

equal to the task
the work isn't up to the standard I require

Related Terms

  • Synonym: competent - having the necessary ability or skills
  • Antonym: incapable - lacking ability or qualities needed for something
  • Synonym: able - having sufficient power, skill, or resources

Etymology

From Late Latin 'capabilis', from Latin 'capere' (to take, hold).
