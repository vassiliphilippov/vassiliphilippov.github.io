fortune

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrtʃən / (AmE), / ˈfɔːtʃuːn / (BrE)
A power or force, often considered as chance, that unpredictably brings good outcomes or luck.
chance bringing good outcomes

Examples

By sheer fortune, the lost wallet was found by an honest person and returned.
Many ancient sailors believed that fortune controlled the winds and waves of the sea.
Sometimes fortune smiles on those who least expect it.

Synset Examples

it was my good luck to be there
they say luck is a lady
it was as if fortune guided his hand

Related Terms

  • Synonym: luck - success or failure apparently brought by chance rather than through one's own actions
  • Antonym: misfortune - bad luck; an unfortunate condition or event

Etymology

From Latin fortuna (luck, fate, fortune), personified as the goddess Fortuna.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrtʃən / (AmE), / ˈfɔːtʃuːn / (BrE)
the force or power that is believed to control events and outcomes, especially those involving chance or luck
chance or luck controlling events

Examples

Some believe that fortune played a large part in his success.
It was pure fortune that we missed the storm by minutes.
No one can control fortune, but we can control our response to it.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: luck - success or failure apparently brought by chance rather than through one's own actions
  • Synonym: fate - the development of events outside a person's control, regarded as predetermined
  • Antonym: misfortune - bad luck or an unlucky event

Etymology

From Latin fortūna (luck, fate, fortune).

3. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrtʃən / (AmE), / ˈfɔːtʃuːn / (BrE)
a large amount of money or wealth; great prosperity
large amount of wealth

Examples

He made a fortune investing in real estate before the market boomed.
It cost a fortune to renovate the old mansion.
She inherited a fortune from her great aunt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wealth - an abundance of valuable possessions or money
  • Antonym: poverty - the state of being extremely poor

Etymology

From Old French 'fortune', from Latin 'fortuna' (chance, luck).
