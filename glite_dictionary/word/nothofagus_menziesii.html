Nothofagus menziesii

1. NOUN

/ ˌnoʊθəˈfeɪɡəs mɛnˈziːzi.aɪ / (AmE), / ˌnɒθəˈfeɪɡəs menˈziːzi.aɪ / (BrE)
A species of beech tree native to New Zealand, known for its pale silvery bark; commonly called the silver beech.
species of New Zealand beech tree

Examples

The Nothofagus menziesii forests are a distinctive feature of New Zealand's South Island landscape.
Botanists are studying the growth patterns of Nothofagus menziesii to understand its response to climate change.
The bark of the Nothofagus menziesii reflects light, giving it a striking silvery appearance in the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silver beech - Common name for Nothofagus menziesii, a New Zealand tree with silvery bark

Etymology

From New Latin Nothofagus (genus name) + specific epithet menziesii, honoring Scottish botanist Archibald Menzies.
