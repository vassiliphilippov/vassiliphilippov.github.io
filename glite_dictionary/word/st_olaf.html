St. Olaf

1. PROPN

/ ˌseɪnt ˈoʊləf / (AmE), / ˌseɪnt ˈəʊlæf / (BrE)
a king of Norway (c. 995–1030) who became the country's patron saint, known for spreading Christianity
Norwegian king and patron saint (995–1030)

Examples

Every year, Norwegians commemorate St. Olaf with traditional festivities.
Many churches across Scandinavia are dedicated to St. Olaf.
In medieval history, St. Olaf is credited with firmly establishing Christianity in Norway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Olaf II - King of Norway, later canonized as St. Olaf

Etymology

From Old Norse Óláfr, name of the king; 'St.' for 'saint'.
