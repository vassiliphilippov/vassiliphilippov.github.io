tineoid

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪniˌɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈtɪniɔɪd / (BrE)
a small, dull-colored moth with chewing mouthparts, often belonging to the superfamily Tineoidea
a type of small, dull moth

Examples

The old wooden box had been damaged by a tineoid that burrowed into its surface.
Some tineoids are known for eating stored textiles and natural fibers.
Researchers collected several tineoid moths during their night survey of the forest.

Etymology

From New Latin Tineoidea, the superfamily name, from 'Tinea' (a genus of moths) and '-oid' (resembling).
