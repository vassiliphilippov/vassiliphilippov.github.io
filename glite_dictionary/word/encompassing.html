encompassing

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈkʌmpəsɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈkʌmpəsɪŋ / (BrE)
including a wide range of people, ideas, or things; covering or affecting everything or everyone within its scope.
including many things; broad in scope

Examples

The report offers an encompassing overview of the current economic situation.
Her interests are so encompassing that she can talk about nearly any subject.
We support an encompassing approach to solving environmental problems.

Synset Examples

across-the-board pay increases
an all-embracing definition
blanket sanctions against human-rights violators
an invention with broad applications
a panoptic study of Soviet nationality
granted him wide powers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comprehensive - including all or nearly all elements or aspects
  • Antonym: limited - restricted in size, amount, or extent

Etymology

From 'encompass' (from Middle English, from Old French 'encompasser').

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈkʌmpəsɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈkʌmpəsɪŋ / (BrE)
closely surrounding or encircling something; forming a boundary around.
closely surrounding or encircling

Examples

The forest was bordered by encompassing mountains that stretched for miles.
The city's encompassing walls once provided protection from invaders.
They admired the encompassing beauty of the vast, open countryside.

Synset Examples

encompassing mountain ranges
the surrounding countryside

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surrounding - located all around a place or thing

Etymology

Present participle of 'encompass', from Middle English 'encompassen', from Old French 'en-' (in) + 'compasser' (to measure, pace out).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00451366-s
Synset POS: s
