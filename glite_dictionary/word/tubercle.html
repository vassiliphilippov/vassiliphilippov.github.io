tubercle

1. NOUN

/ tuˈbɝːkəl / (AmE), / tjuːˈbɜːkəl / (BrE)
a small rounded projection or protuberance on a bone, especially one serving as an attachment point for a muscle or ligament
small projection on a bone
medical

Examples

The deltoid tubercle serves as an important attachment site for the shoulder muscle.
A small tubercle on the rib marks where the ligament connects.
The doctor pointed out a tubercle on the X-ray of my vertebra.

Etymology

From Latin tuberculum, diminutive of tuber (swelling, lump).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtuː.bər.kəl / (AmE), / ˈtjuː.bə.kəl / (BrE)
A small, rounded swelling or nodule, especially one that is a characteristic lesion caused by tuberculosis.
swelling or nodule from tuberculosis
medical

Examples

The doctor identified a tubercle in the patient's lung during the X-ray scan.
A tubercle is often found in tissues infected by the tuberculosis bacterium.
During autopsy, several tubercles were observed in the affected organs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nodule - a small swelling or lump of tissue

Etymology

From Latin 'tuberculum', diminutive of 'tuber' (lump, swelling).

3. NOUN

/ ˈtuːbərkəl / (AmE), / ˈtjuːbəkəl / (BrE)
a small, rounded, wartlike protuberance on a plant, especially one found on roots or stems
small rounded protuberance on a plant

Examples

Several tubercles can be seen on the surface of the plant root.
The stem develops tubercles that help in nitrogen fixation.
You can identify this species by the small tubercle at each leaf base.

Etymology

From Latin tuberculum, diminutive of tuber (swelling, lump).
