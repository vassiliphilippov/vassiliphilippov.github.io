unconditioned

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.kənˈdɪʃ.ənd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.kənˈdɪʃ.ənd / (BrE)
(of a response or reflex) not acquired or modified by conditioning or learning; innate or natural.
not learned or conditioned

Examples

The doctor explained that a sneeze is an unconditioned reflex rather than a learned behavior.
Infants display several unconditioned responses to stimuli, such as blinking or grasping.
Unlike trained behaviors, unconditioned responses do not require previous experience.

Synset Examples

an unconditioned reflex

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conditioned - acquired or modified by conditioning or learning

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (not) + 'conditioned' (modified or learned through experience).

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.kənˈdɪʃ.ənd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.kənˈdɪʃ.ənd / (BrE)
not limited or restricted by conditions; absolute
not limited by conditions

Examples

The monk pursued a state of unconditioned happiness, free from all external influences.
Scientists studied the animal's unconditioned responses to various stimuli.
His loyalty was unconditioned, unaffected by any circumstances or requirements.

Synset Examples

unconditional surrender

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unconditional - not subject to any conditions; absolute
  • Antonym: conditional - subject to one or more conditions or requirements

Etymology

From un- (not) + conditioned, from Latin 'condicionare' (to make a condition).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00558935-a
Synset POS: a
