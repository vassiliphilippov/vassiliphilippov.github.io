husk

/ hʌsk / (AmE), / hʌsk / (BrE)

2. NOUN

/ hʌsk / (AmE), / hʌsk / (BrE)
The dry outer covering of some seeds or fruits, especially grain, that is usually removed before eating or processing.
dry outer covering of seeds or fruits

Examples

The rice husk is removed before the grain is sold for consumption.
Several birds were picking at the husk left after harvesting the corn.
The floor was covered in sunflower seed husk after the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shell - hard outer covering of nuts, eggs, or some seeds

Etymology

Probably from Middle Dutch *hūsch* (covering, husk).

3. NOUN

/ hʌsk / (AmE), / hʌsk / (BrE)
the dry outer covering of certain fruits or seeds, especially one that is thin and membranous
dry outer covering of fruits or seeds

Examples

The peanut's husk must be removed before roasting the nut.
They used the husk of the corn to make crafts for the festival.
After threshing, only the husk remained on the ground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shell - the hard outer covering of some fruits, nuts, or animals
  • Antonym: core - the central part of a fruit, containing the seeds

Etymology

Probably from Middle Dutch *huusken* (little house, shell), diminutive of *huis* (house).
