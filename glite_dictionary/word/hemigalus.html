Hemigalus

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛmɪˈɡæləs / (AmE), / ˌhɛmɪˈɡæləs / (BrE)
A genus of small, nocturnal mammals known as banded palm civets, native to Southeast Asia.
genus of banded palm civets

Examples

The Hemigalus is distinguished by its striped body and long tail.
Researchers observed Hemigalus in the dense forests of Borneo.
Unlike other civets, the Hemigalus exhibits unique foraging behaviors.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'hēmi-' (half) and 'galos' (weasel).

2. NOUN

/ hɛmɪˈɡæləs / (AmE), / hɛmɪˈɡaləs / (BrE)
A genus of small carnivorous mammals, including the banded palm civet, native to Southeast Asia.
genus of banded palm civet

Examples

The Hemigalus is known for its strikingly banded fur pattern.
Scientists recently studied the habitat of Hemigalus in the rainforests of Indonesia.
The Hemigalus is rarely seen due to its nocturnal habits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banded palm civet - a small, civet-like mammal with distinctive bands, member of the genus Hemigalus

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek roots hēmi- (half) and galos (weasel).
