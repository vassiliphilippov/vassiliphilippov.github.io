Pinus albicaulis

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪnəs ˌælbɪˈkɔlɪs / (AmE), / ˈpaɪnəs ˌal.bɪˈkɔː.lɪs / (BrE)
a species of pine tree native to western North America, with smooth grey-white bark and soft, brittle wood, also known as whitebark pine
species of pine tree (whitebark pine)

Examples

The Pinus albicaulis is vital for high mountain ecosystems, providing food for numerous animals.
Many conservationists are working to protect Pinus albicaulis from diseases and climate change.
You can identify Pinus albicaulis by its distinctive white bark and clusters of five needles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whitebark pine - common name for Pinus albicaulis

Etymology

From Latin; 'Pinus' meaning 'pine', 'albicaulis' from 'albus' (white) and 'caulis' (stem).
