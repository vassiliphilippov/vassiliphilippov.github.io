yucky

1. ADJ

/ ˈjʌki / (AmE), / ˈjʌki / (BrE)
unpleasant or disgusting, especially to the sense of taste or smell
unpleasant; disgusting
informal

Examples

That milk smells yucky—don't drink it!
She refused to eat the yucky vegetables on her plate.
The garbage had a yucky odor that filled the whole kitchen.

Synset Examples

a disgusting smell
distasteful language
a loathsome disease
the idea of eating meat is repellent to me
revolting food
a wicked stench

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disgusting - repellent; causing strong dislike or distaste
  • Antonym: delicious - highly pleasant in taste or smell

Etymology

Imitative formation, likely from 'yuck', representing the sound of disgust.
