puzzle

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌzəl / (AmE), / ˈpʌz.əl / (BrE)
a question, game, or problem that tests a person's ingenuity or knowledge and has a correct solution
problem or game to be solved

Examples

Solving a challenging crossword puzzle is his favorite weekend activity.
The detective finally cracked the murder puzzle after examining all the clues.
Today's math puzzle took her more than an hour to figure out.

Synset Examples

he loved to solve chessmate puzzles
that's a real puzzler

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puzzler - a baffling question or riddle

Etymology

From Old French *pusle* (confusion, perplexity), of uncertain origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06797496-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈpʌzəl / (AmE), / ˈpʌzl / (BrE)
to be difficult to understand or explain; to cause someone to feel confused or perplexed
cause confusion or perplexity

Examples

The instructions completely puzzled me the first time I read them.
She was puzzled by his sudden change in attitude.
This scientific phenomenon has long puzzled experts around the world.

Synset Examples

This beats me!
Got me — I don't know the answer!
a vexing problem
This question really stuck me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confuse - to make someone uncertain or unable to understand something
  • Synonym: to bewilder - to cause to become confused and unable to think clearly

Etymology

Probably from Old French *pusle* (confusion).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00624056-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈpʌz.əl / (AmE), / ˈpʌz.əl / (BrE)
A game or toy designed to test a person's ingenuity or knowledge by requiring them to solve a problem or put pieces together correctly.
game testing ingenuity or knowledge

Examples

She enjoys working on a puzzle every evening to relax.
The children solved the challenging puzzle together during the party.
He bought a new jigsaw puzzle as a gift for his niece.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: riddle - a question or statement intentionally phrased to require ingenuity in ascertaining its answer

Etymology

From Old French *pusle* (bewilder), possibly related to the verb 'pose'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04034791-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈpʌzəl / (AmE), / ˈpʌzəl / (BrE)
to think about or try to understand something difficult or confusing; to be uncertain about something
to be confused or uncertain about

Examples

He puzzled over the meaning of the cryptic message for hours.
Scientists continue to puzzle about how the ancient monument was built.
She puzzled over whether she had made the right decision.

Synset Examples

We puzzled over her sudden departure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wonder - to feel curious or doubtful about something
  • Antonym: to understand - to grasp the meaning of something (opposite sense)

Etymology

From Old French *pusle* (bewilder), of uncertain origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01835250-v
Synset POS: v
