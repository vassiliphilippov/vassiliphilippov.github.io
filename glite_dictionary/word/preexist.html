to preexist

1. VERB

/ ˌpriːɪɡˈzɪst / (AmE), / ˌpriːɪɡˈzɪst / (BrE)
to exist before a particular time or event
exist before a particular time

Examples

It is unclear whether the symptoms preexist the diagnosis.
Some cultural beliefs preexist written history.
Scientists debated if the ecosystem preexisted the last ice age.

Synset Examples

Did this condition pre-exist?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to precede - come before in time
  • Antonym: to postdate - to occur later than a particular time or event

Etymology

From Latin prae- (before) + exist (to exist).
