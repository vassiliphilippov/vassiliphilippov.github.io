signaller

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪnələr / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡnələ / (BrE)
a person whose job is to send or interpret signals for communication, especially in the military or railways.
person who communicates by signals

Examples

The signaller raised a red flag to stop the approaching train.
During the exercise, a signaller transmitted coded messages to the command post.
In the army, a skilled signaller ensures that vital information is communicated quickly and accurately.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: signalman - person who operates signals, especially on railways

Etymology

From signal + -er, indicating a person who signals.
