trichodesmium

1. NOUN

/ trɪˌkoʊˈdɛzmiəm / (AmE), / trɪˌkəʊˈdɛzmiəm / (BrE)
A genus of large colonial cyanobacteria commonly found in tropical open-ocean waters, important for carbon and nitrogen fixation.
genus of tropical ocean cyanobacteria
medical

Examples

Trichodesmium blooms can sometimes be seen floating on the surface of tropical oceans.
Marine biologists study trichodesmium to better understand oceanic nitrogen cycles.
Large colonies of trichodesmium contribute significantly to primary production in nutrient-poor waters.

Etymology

From Greek 'tricho-' (hair) and 'desmos' (bond), referring to its filamentous form.
