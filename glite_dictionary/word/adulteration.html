adulteration

1. NOUN

/ əˌdʌl.təˈreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / əˌdʌl.təˈreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of making something poorer in quality by adding another substance, often illegally or fraudulently
the act of making something impure

Examples

The adulteration of foods can pose serious health risks to consumers.
Authorities are working to prevent the adulteration of medicines in the market.
The company was fined for the adulteration of its dairy products with water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contamination - the presence of unwanted substances
  • Antonym: purification - the act of making something pure

Etymology

From Latin adulteratio (corruption, falsification), from adulterare (to corrupt).

2. NOUN

/ əˌdʌltəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌdʌltəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
The act or process of making something impure by adding foreign or inferior substances; the result of such an act.
the process of making something impure

Examples

The adulteration of milk with water was a widespread problem in the nineteenth century.
Strict regulations are in place to prevent the adulteration of food products.
After testing, authorities discovered evidence of adulteration in several imported medicines.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: purification - the process of making something pure
  • Synonym: contamination - the presence of an unwanted substance in something

Etymology

From Latin 'adulteratio', from 'adulterare' (to corrupt).
