riboflavin

1. NOUN

/ ˌraɪbəˈfleɪvɪn / (AmE), / ˌraɪbəʊˈfleɪvɪn / (BrE)
a B vitamin essential for the body's energy production, growth, and development, also known as vitamin B2
a B vitamin (vitamin B2)

Examples

Milk is a rich source of riboflavin, which is important for maintaining healthy skin.
The doctor recommended a supplement containing riboflavin for improved energy levels.
A deficiency in riboflavin can lead to cracked lips and skin disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vitamin B2 - another term for riboflavin
  • Degree Higher: niacin - a different B vitamin (vitamin B3)
  • Degree Lower: thiamine - a different B vitamin (vitamin B1)

Etymology

From Latin 'ribo(se)' (a sugar) + 'flavin' (from Latin 'flavus', meaning yellow), referring to its color.
