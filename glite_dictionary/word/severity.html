severity

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈvɛrəti / (AmE), / sɪˈvɛrəti / (BrE)
the degree or extent of something undesirable, such as pain, difficulty, or weather conditions
degree of unpleasantness or harshness

Examples

The severity of the storm forced residents to evacuate their homes.
Doctors were concerned about the severity of her injuries after the accident.
Weather reports warned of the increasing severity of the heatwave.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harshness - quality of being harsh or severe
  • Antonym: gentleness - quality of being gentle

Etymology

From Latin severitas (sternness, seriousness), from severus (stern, strict).

/ səˈvɛrəti / (AmE), / sɪˈvɛrəti / (BrE)

Examples

Her severity as a judge made her both respected and feared in the courtroom.
