currency

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɝːənsi / (AmE), / ˈkʌrənsi / (BrE)
The system of money in general use in a particular country, especially in the form of coins and paper notes.
system of money in use

Examples

The country's official currency is the euro, which replaced the previous national money.
Travelers must exchange their dollars for local currency before making purchases.
Due to inflation, the value of the national currency dropped significantly in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: money - general term for medium of exchange, including coins and banknotes

Etymology

From Latin 'currens', present participle of 'currere' (to run), via Old French 'curance'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɜrənsi / (AmE), / ˈkʌrənsi / (BrE)
the state of being widely accepted, used, or prevalent at a particular time
widespread acceptance or use

Examples

The currency of these ideas in academic circles shows their influence.
Some words gain currency quickly through social media.
The theory never achieved much currency outside of specialist communities.

Synset Examples

the currency of ideas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prevalence - the fact or condition of being prevalent; widespread
  • Synonym: popularity - the state of being liked, enjoyed, or supported by many people

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'currentia', from Latin 'currere' (to run), referring to something in circulation.
