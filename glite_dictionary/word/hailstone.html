hailstone

1. NOUN

/ ˈheɪlˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈheɪlˌstəʊn / (BrE)
a small, hard ball of ice that falls from the sky during a hailstorm
small ball of ice from a hailstorm

Examples

The sudden storm left the ground covered in hailstones.
She found a hailstone the size of a golf ball in her garden.
As the thunder rumbled, hailstones clattered against the windows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hail - pellets of frozen rain falling in showers

Etymology

From 'hail' (ice pellets from the sky) + 'stone', referring to its hard, stone-like shape.
