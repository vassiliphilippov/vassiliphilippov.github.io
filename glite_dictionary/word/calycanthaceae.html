Calycanthaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæ.lɪ.kænˈθeɪ.si.i / (AmE), / ˌkæl.ɪ.kænˈθeɪ.si.iː / (BrE)
a family of shrubs or small trees with aromatic bark, native to the eastern United States and eastern Asia
a family of aromatic shrubs or trees

Examples

The Calycanthaceae are prized in gardens for their fragrant bark and attractive flowers.
Botanists have found several species within the Calycanthaceae in eastern Asia.
Many plants in the Calycanthaceae family are valued for ornamental use.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Calycanthus (from Greek ‘kalyx’ meaning cup + ‘anthos’ meaning flower).
