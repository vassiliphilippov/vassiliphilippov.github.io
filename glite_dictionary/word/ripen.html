to ripen

1. VERB

/ ˈraɪpən / (AmE), / ˈraɪpən / (BrE)
to become or make ripe; to develop fully, especially so as to be ready to eat or use
become or make ripe

Examples

The tomatoes will ripen faster if you leave them in the sun.
It takes several weeks for cheese to ripen fully.
Farmers wait for their wheat to ripen before harvesting.

Synset Examples

The plums ripen in July

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mature - to reach full development or maturity
  • Antonym: decay - to rot; to undergo decomposition, opposite of ripen

Etymology

From Middle English ripen, from ripe (adjective) + -en (verb-forming suffix).

2. VERB

/ ˈraɪ.pən / (AmE), / ˈraɪ.pən / (BrE)
to make or become fully grown, developed, or ready to be used or eaten, especially by natural processes
make or become fully ripe or developed

Examples

The summer heat helps ripen the tomatoes much earlier than usual.
Farmers sometimes use chemicals to ripen bananas before sending them to market.
Time and proper storage conditions ripen cheese, improving its flavor and texture.

Synset Examples

The sun ripens the fruit
Age matures a good wine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mature - to become fully developed or grown

Etymology

From Old English 'ripian', related to 'ripe' (mature, ready to eat).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
