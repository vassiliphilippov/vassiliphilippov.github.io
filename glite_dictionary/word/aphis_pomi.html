Aphis pomi

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.fɪs ˈpoʊ.mi / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.fɪs ˈpəʊ.mi / (BrE)
A bright green species of aphid that feeds on apple trees, causing leaves to curl.
a bright green aphid species affecting apple trees

Examples

An infestation of Aphis pomi severely damaged the orchard's apple leaves this spring.
Gardeners often check for Aphis pomi if they notice curling leaves on their apple trees.
Scientists study Aphis pomi to understand its effects on fruit tree health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aphid - a small sap-sucking insect

Etymology

From Latin: 'Aphis' (aphid) + 'pomi' (of apple).
