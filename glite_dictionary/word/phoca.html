Phoca

1. PROPN

/ ˈfoʊkə / (AmE), / ˈfəʊkə / (BrE)
The genus of earless seals (true seals) that is the type genus of the family Phocidae.
genus of earless seals

Examples

The genus Phoca includes several species of true seals, such as the harbor seal.
Marine biologists are studying the behavior of Phoca in the wild.
Historical classifications sometimes placed more seal species in the genus Phoca.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seal - a marine mammal of the family Phocidae or Otariidae

Etymology

From Latin 'phoca', from Ancient Greek 'phōkē' (seal).
