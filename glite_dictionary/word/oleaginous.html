oleaginous

1. ADJ

/ oʊliˈædʒənəs / (AmE), / əʊliˈadʒɪnəs / (BrE)
containing, producing, or covered with oil or grease; oily or greasy.
containing or producing oil; oily

Examples

The chef explained that oleaginous seeds like sunflower and flax are high in healthy fats.
She wiped her hands after picking up the oleaginous machinery part.
Many tropical crops produce oleaginous fruit important for the food industry.

Synset Examples

greasy hamburgers
oily fried potatoes
oleaginous seeds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oily - covered with, containing, or resembling oil
  • Synonym: greasy - containing or covered with grease or oil

Etymology

From Latin 'oleaginus' (of the olive tree), from 'oleum' (oil).

2. ADJ

/ ˌoʊliˈædʒɪnəs / (AmE), / ˌəʊliˈædʒɪnəs / (BrE)
excessively and unpleasantly suave, ingratiating, or insincere in manner or speech; overly eager to please, often in a way that is perceived as oily or smarmy.
excessively ingratiating; oily in manner

Examples

The politician's oleaginous compliments made the audience roll their eyes in disbelief.
She found his oleaginous manner insincere and repulsive, despite his polite words.
His oleaginous attempts to win favor with the boss were obvious to everyone.

Synset Examples

buttery praise
gave him a fulsome introduction
an oily sycophantic press agent
oleaginous hypocrisy
smarmy self-importance
the unctuous Uriah Heep
soapy compliments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unctuous - excessively flattering or insincerely earnest
  • Synonym: smarmy - excessively flattering in a way that is perceived as insincere or ingratiating
  • Antonym: genuine - sincere, honest, and real
  • Antonym: sincere - genuine in feeling or expression, not pretended or affected

Etymology

From Latin 'oleaginus' (of the olive tree, oily), from 'oleum' (oil). The figurative sense refers to 'oily' mannerisms.
