Decapoda

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛkəˈpoʊdə / (AmE), / ˌdɛkəˈpəʊdə / (BrE)
an order of crustaceans that includes lobsters, crabs, shrimps, crayfish, and prawns, typically having five pairs of walking legs
order of crustaceans (lobsters, crabs, shrimps, etc.)

Examples

Members of the Decapoda order include familiar seafood such as lobsters and crabs.
The body structure of Decapoda is distinguished by five pairs of legs.
Marine biologists study the diversity of Decapoda species around coral reefs.

Etymology

From Greek deka (ten) + pous/pod- (foot), referring to the ten legs.

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈkæpədə / (AmE), / dɪˈkapədə / (BrE)
A taxonomic order of cephalopod mollusks that includes squids and cuttlefishes, characterized by having ten arms.
order of cephalopods: squids, cuttlefishes

Examples

Marine biologists often study Decapoda to understand the evolutionary adaptations of squids and cuttlefishes.
The order Decapoda is distinguished by its members' ten arms and complex nervous systems.
Research on Decapoda has led to new insights into camouflage mechanisms in marine animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cephalopod - class of mollusks including squids, octopuses, and cuttlefishes

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek deka- (ten) + pous, podos (foot).
