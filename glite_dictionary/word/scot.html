Scot

1. NOUN

/ skɑt / (AmE), / skɒt / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Scotland
person from Scotland

Examples

As a proud Scot, she always celebrates St. Andrew's Day.
The Scot moved to Canada to pursue a new career.
He is the only Scot on the university's rowing team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Scotsman - a man from Scotland
  • Synonym: Scotswoman - a woman from Scotland

Etymology

From Old English 'Scottas', Latin 'Scoti', referring originally to Gaelic-speaking people of Ireland and later to those in Scotland.
