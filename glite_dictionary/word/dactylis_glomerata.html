Dactylis glomerata

1. NOUN

/ ˌdæk.tɪ.lɪs ɡloʊˈmɛr.ə.tə / (AmE), / ˌdæk.tɪ.lɪs ˌɡlɒˈmɛr.ə.tə / (BrE)
A robust Old World grass species widely cultivated for hay and pasture, also known as orchard grass.
a grass species used for hay and pasture

Examples

Dactylis glomerata is often grown in temperate regions as a reliable hay crop.
Farmers prefer Dactylis glomerata for its high nutritional value for livestock.
Wildlife grazing meadows often contain a mix of Dactylis glomerata and other grasses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchard grass - common name for Dactylis glomerata, a pasture grass

Etymology

From Greek dactylis (finger), referring to the finger-like flower clusters; glomerata from Latin glomerare (to form into a ball or cluster).
