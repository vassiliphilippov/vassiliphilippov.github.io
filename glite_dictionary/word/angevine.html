Angevine

1. NOUN

/ ˈændʒəˌvaɪn / (AmE), / ˈændʒɪvaɪn / (BrE)
A native or inhabitant of Anjou, a historical region in western France.
person from Anjou, France

Examples

The Angevine nobility played a significant role in medieval European politics.
She traced her ancestry back to an Angevine who settled in England centuries ago.
Many Angevines emigrated during the wars of the sixteenth century.

Etymology

From French Angevin, from Medieval Latin Andegavīnus, from Andegavia (Anjou).
