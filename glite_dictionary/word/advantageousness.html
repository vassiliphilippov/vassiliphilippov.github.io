advantageousness

1. NOUN

/ ˌæd.vænˈteɪ.dʒəs.nəs / (AmE), / ˌæd.vænˈteɪ.dʒəs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being advantageous; the condition of offering advantages or promoting success
the quality of being advantageous

Examples

The advantageousness of her plan was clear to everyone in the meeting.
We must assess the advantageousness of each proposal before making a decision.
His argument focused on the advantageousness of the partnership compared to other options.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benefit - an advantage or profit gained from something
  • Antonym: disadvantageousness - the quality of being disadvantageous

Etymology

Formed from 'advantageous' + '-ness', denoting quality or state.
