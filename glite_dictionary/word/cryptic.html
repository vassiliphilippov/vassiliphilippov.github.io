cryptic

1. ADJ

/ ˈkrɪp.tɪk / (AmE), / ˈkrɪp.tɪk / (BrE)
having a meaning that is mysterious or difficult to understand; obscure.
having a mysterious or obscure meaning

Examples

The professor's explanation was so cryptic that most students left the lecture confused.
She received a cryptic message that left her wondering about its true meaning.
The treasure map was covered with cryptic symbols that needed to be deciphered.

Synset Examples

the new insurance policy is written without cryptic or mysterious terms
a deep dark secret
the inscrutable workings of Providence
in its mysterious past it encompasses all the dim origins of life
rituals totally mystifying to visitors from other lands

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mysterious - difficult to understand or explain; full of mystery
  • Synonym: obscure - not clear or hard to understand
  • Antonym: clear - easy to perceive, understand, or interpret

Etymology

From Greek kryptikos (fit for concealing), from kryptein (to hide).
