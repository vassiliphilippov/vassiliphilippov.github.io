Czar Alexander I

1. PROPN

/ zɑr ˌælɪɡˈzændər ðə fɜrst / (AmE), / zɑː ˌælɪɡˈzændə ðə fɜːst / (BrE)
the emperor of Russia from 1801 to 1825, known for his attempts at reform and his role in defeating Napoleon
Russian emperor, reigned 1801–1825

Examples

Many history books detail how Czar Alexander I played a pivotal role in defeating Napoleon.
Czar Alexander I introduced several reforms in Russia, although many remained unfulfilled.
The Congress of Vienna in 1815 was greatly influenced by Czar Alexander I and his policies.
