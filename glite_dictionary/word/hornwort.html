hornwort

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrnˌwɝt / (AmE), / ˈhɔːnˌwɜːt / (BrE)
a submerged aquatic plant of the genus Ceratophyllum, forming dense masses in ponds and slow-moving streams
submerged aquatic plant (genus Ceratophyllum)

Examples

Hornwort is often used by aquarists to help control algae in freshwater aquariums.
You can find dense mats of hornwort growing in many garden ponds.
The presence of hornwort in the stream created a safe habitat for fish fry.

Etymology

From 'horn' referring to its forked, horn-like leaves, and 'wort' meaning plant or herb.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrnˌwɝt / (AmE), / ˈhɔːnwɜːt / (BrE)
A small non-vascular plant of the division Anthocerotophyta, characterized by slender, hornlike spore capsules.
non-vascular plant with hornlike capsules

Examples

The scientist examined the hornwort under the microscope, noting its hornlike capsule structure.
Unlike mosses, hornworts have a single large chloroplast in each cell.
Hornwort thrives in damp, shady environments alongside other non-vascular plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liverwort - non-vascular plant in the division Marchantiophyta
  • Synonym: moss - a small, green, non-vascular plant of the division Bryophyta

Etymology

From the hornlike shape of its spore capsules and 'wort' meaning plant or herb.
