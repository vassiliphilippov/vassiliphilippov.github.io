pliability

1. NOUN

/ ˌplaɪəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌplaɪəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality of being easily influenced or adaptable in mind or character.
adaptability or flexibility of mind

Examples

Her pliability allowed her to adjust quickly to new roles within the team.
He admired the pliability of her opinions during difficult negotiations.
Companies often seek employees with pliability to respond to changing demands.

Synset Examples

he was valued for his reliability and pliability
he increased the leanness and suppleness of the organization

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flexibility - the quality of being able to change or adapt easily
  • Antonym: rigidity - the quality of being unwilling to change or adapt

Etymology

From Latin 'plicare' (to fold) via Old French 'pliable' (bendable) + -ity.

2. NOUN

/ ˌplaɪəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌplaɪəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
The quality of being easily bent or shaped without breaking.
ability to bend easily

Examples

The pliability of the material made it ideal for creating intricate designs.
Her yoga routine improved the pliability of her muscles over time.
The pliability of clay allows artists to mold it into various shapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flexibility - the ability to bend easily without breaking
  • Antonym: rigidity - the quality of being stiff and not easily bent

Etymology

From Latin 'plicare' (to fold), via French 'pliable'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05029875-n
Synset POS: n
