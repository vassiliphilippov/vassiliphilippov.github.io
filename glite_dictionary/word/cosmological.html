cosmological

1. ADJ

/ ˌkɑːzməˈlɑːdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌkɒzməˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the branch of philosophy or science that studies the origin, structure, and laws of the universe.
relating to the universe or its origins

Examples

The cosmological constant is a term introduced in Einstein's theory of general relativity.
Several cosmological models attempt to explain the origin of the universe.
She specializes in cosmological arguments for the existence of a first cause.

Synset Examples

cosmologic philosophy
a cosmological argument is an argument that the universe demands the admission of an adequate external cause which is God

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cosmology - the science of the origin and development of the universe

Etymology

From Greek kosmos (universe) and -logia (study of).

2. ADJ

/ ˌkɑːzməˈlɑːdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌkɒzməˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the science of the origin, evolution, and structure of the universe, especially as studied in cosmology.
relating to the universe's origin and structure

Examples

The cosmological constant remains a topic of debate among physicists.
She specializes in cosmological theories about the early universe.
Recent discoveries have changed our understanding of cosmological processes.

Synset Examples

cosmologic science
cosmological redshift
cosmogonic theories of the origin of the universe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cosmological - of or relating to cosmology
  • Degree Lower: astronomical - relating to astronomy, the scientific study of celestial bodies

Etymology

From Greek kosmos (world, universe) + -logical (relating to study or science).
