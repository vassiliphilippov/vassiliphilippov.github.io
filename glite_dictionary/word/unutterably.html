unutterably

1. ADV

/ ʌnˈʌt̬ɚəbli / (AmE), / ʌnˈʌtərəbli / (BrE)
to an extent that cannot be expressed in words; indescribably.
to an inexpressible or indescribable degree

Examples

The landscape was unutterably beautiful, leaving us all speechless with awe.
He felt unutterably alone after moving to a new city.
Her kindness was unutterably moving, touching everyone who met her.

Synset Examples

she was looking very young tonight, and, as usual, indescribably beautiful, in a simple strapless dress of a green and white silky cotton

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indescribably - to a degree that cannot be described

Etymology

Formed from 'unutterable' (not able to be spoken) plus the adverbial suffix '-ly'.
