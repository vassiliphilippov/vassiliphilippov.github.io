Lebistes reticulatus

1. NOUN

/ lɛˈbɪstiːz ˌrɛtɪˈkjʊlətəs / (AmE), / lɛˈbɪstiːz ˌrɛtɪˈkjʊlətəs / (BrE)
a small freshwater fish native to South America and the West Indies, often kept in aquariums; also known as the guppy
small aquarium fish; guppy

Examples

Many aquarium enthusiasts choose Lebistes reticulatus for their vibrant colors and active behavior.
The natural habitat of Lebistes reticulatus ranges from South America to the West Indies.
Researchers study Lebistes reticulatus to learn more about rapid evolution in fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guppy - another common name for Lebistes reticulatus

Etymology

From New Latin: 'Lebistes' (genus name) + Latin 'reticulatus' (netted, reticulated).
