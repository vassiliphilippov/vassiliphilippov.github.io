Thryothorus

1. NOUN

/ θraɪəˈθɔrəs / (AmE), / θraɪəˈθɔːrəs / (BrE)
A genus of small perching birds in the wren family, native to the Americas; includes the Carolina wren.
genus of wrens; Carolina wren

Examples

The Carolina wren, classified under the genus Thryothorus, is common in the southeastern United States.
Ornithologists have studied various species in the Thryothorus genus for their song patterns.
Sightings of birds from the Thryothorus genus delight birdwatchers throughout North and South America.

Etymology

From Greek 'thryon' (reed) and 'thouros' (leaping), alluding to the bird's habitat and behavior.
