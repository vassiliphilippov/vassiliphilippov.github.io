Lasiurus

1. NOUN

/ læsiˈʊrəs / (AmE), / læziˈjʊərəs / (BrE)
A genus of bats in the family Vespertilionidae, commonly known as hairy-tailed bats, native to the Americas.
genus of hairy-tailed bats

Examples

The red bat, a species within the genus Lasiurus, is commonly found across North America.
Researchers discovered a new species belonging to Lasiurus in South America last year.
Characteristics of Lasiurus include a densely furred tail membrane and bright coloration.

Etymology

From Greek 'lasios' (hairy) + 'oura' (tail), referring to the distinctive hairy tail feature of these bats.
