Argemone mexicana

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˌdʒɛməni ˌmɛksɪˈkeɪnə / (AmE), / ɑːˌdʒɛməni ˌmɛksɪˈkeɪnə / (BrE)
An annual herb with prickly stems and large yellow flowers, native to the southern United States, West Indies, and Mexico.
annual herb with yellow flowers

Examples

The garden featured several specimens of Argemone mexicana in full bloom.
Local healers sometimes use Argemone mexicana in traditional remedies despite its toxicity.
We identified the plant as Argemone mexicana by its distinctive yellow flowers and prickly stems.

Etymology

From Latin; genus name 'Argemone' from Greek 'argemōnē'; species epithet meaning 'Mexican'.
