alveolitis

1. NOUN

/ ˌæl.vi.oʊˈlaɪ.tɪs / (AmE), / ˌal.vi.əʊˈlaɪ.tɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of the alveoli in the lungs, often due to inhaled dust, which can become chronic with repeated exposure
inflammation of lung alveoli
medical

Examples

The doctor suspected alveolitis after noting the patient's persistent cough and difficulty breathing.
Exposure to moldy hay can lead to a form of alveolitis known as farmer's lung.
Chronic alveolitis may require long-term medication and avoidance of dust exposure.

Etymology

From Latin 'alveolus' (small cavity) + '-itis' (inflammation).

2. NOUN

/ ˌælvi(oʊ)ˈlaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌalvɪəˈlaɪtɪs / (BrE)
Inflammation of the tooth socket, often occurring after a tooth is extracted and a blood clot fails to form.
inflammation of a tooth socket
medical

Examples

The patient experienced severe pain due to alveolitis following his tooth extraction.
Dentists recommend certain precautions to minimize the risk of alveolitis after dental surgery.
Prompt treatment of alveolitis can significantly reduce discomfort and speed up recovery.

Etymology

From New Latin alveolus (small cavity) + -itis (inflammation).
