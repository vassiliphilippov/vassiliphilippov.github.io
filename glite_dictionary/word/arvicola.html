Arvicola

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈvɪkələ / (AmE), / ɑːˈvɪkələ / (BrE)
A genus of small rodents, including the European water vole, in the subfamily Arvicolinae.
genus of rodents, includes water voles

Examples

The European water vole is classified in the genus Arvicola.
Researchers examined several species belonging to Arvicola for their habitats and diet.
Arvicola is distinguished from other rodents by its semi-aquatic lifestyle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Microtus - another genus of voles, sometimes considered synonymous with Arvicola

Etymology

From Latin arvum (field) + colere (to inhabit); scientific term coined in 18th century.

2. NOUN

/ ɑrˈvɪkələ / (AmE), / ɑːˈvɪkələ / (BrE)
a genus of voles in the rodent family Cricetidae, sometimes regarded as synonymous with Microtus in some classifications
a genus of voles (rodents)

Examples

The water vole, belonging to the genus Arvicola, is native to Europe and Asia.
Recent studies suggest some species of Arvicola may be reclassified under Microtus.
Members of the genus Arvicola are known for their semi-aquatic habits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Microtus - a genus of voles in the rodent family Cricetidae

Etymology

From Latin arvicola, meaning 'field-dweller' (arvum, 'field' + colere, 'to dwell').
