certainly

1. ADV

/ ˈsɝː.tən.li / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.tən.li / (BrE)
Without any doubt; used to emphasize that something is true or will happen.
without doubt; definitely

Examples

She will certainly be at the meeting, so you can ask her then.
It will certainly take more than a week to finish the project.
He certainly knows how to make a good impression.

Synset Examples

the results are surely encouraging
she certainly is a hard worker
it's going to be a good day for sure
they are coming, for certain
they thought he had been killed sure enough
he'll win sure as shooting
they sure smell good
sure he'll come

Related Terms

  • Synonym: definitely - without doubt; certainly
  • Degree Lower: probably - most likely but not certain
  • Degree Higher: absolutely - completely; with no qualification; used for emphasis
  • Antonym: uncertainly - in a way that is not sure or definite; lacking certainty

Etymology

From Middle English certeinly, from Old French certain, from Latin certus (sure, fixed).
