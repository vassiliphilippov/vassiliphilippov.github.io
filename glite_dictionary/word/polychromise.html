to polychromise

1. VERB

/ ˈpɑːliˌkroʊˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪˌkrəʊmaɪz / (BrE)
to decorate or color with a variety of colors; to make polychrome
to color with many colors

Examples

The artist decided to polychromise the statue to enhance its visual impact.
Ancient temples were often polychromised in vibrant reds and blues.
Restorers are carefully polychromising the ceiling according to historical records.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polychrome - to color or decorate with several colors

Etymology

From French 'polychromiser', based on Greek 'poly-' (many) and 'chroma' (color).
