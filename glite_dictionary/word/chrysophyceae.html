Chrysophyceae

1. NOUN

/ kraɪˌsɒfəˈsiːiː / (AmE), / kraɪˌsɒfɪˈsiːiː / (BrE)
A class of mostly freshwater yellow-green algae characterized by flagella of unequal length.
a class of yellow-green algae

Examples

Researchers observed Chrysophyceae under the microscope to study their unique flagella.
Many lakes are home to various species within the Chrysophyceae class of algae.
The color of the water changed due to a bloom of Chrysophyceae during the summer months.

Etymology

From Greek 'chrysos' (gold) + 'phyke' (seaweed).

2. NOUN

/ kraɪˌsɑːfəˈsiːiː / (AmE), / krɪˌsɒfɪˈsiːiː / (BrE)
A class of yellow-green algae characterized by flagella of unequal length, primarily found in freshwater environments.
class of yellow-green algae

Examples

The Chrysophyceae are often found in nutrient-poor freshwater lakes around the world.
Some species in the Chrysophyceae class can form massive blooms under certain conditions.
Scientists study Chrysophyceae to understand their ecological role and evolutionary history.

Etymology

From Greek 'chrysos' (gold) and 'phyke' (seaweed), referring to the golden color of the algae.
