rabbiteye

1. NOUN

/ ˈræb.iˌaɪ / (AmE), / ˈræb.ɪt.aɪ / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States, grown commercially for its edible berries, especially for canning.
shrub grown for edible berries (SE US)

Examples

Farmers in the region often plant rabbiteye to supply the canning factories.
The rabbiteye flourishes in the acidic soils of southeastern states.
We picked baskets full of rabbiteye berries during the summer harvest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blueberry - edible berry of several species in the genus Vaccinium

Etymology

Named for the resemblance of its unripened fruit to a rabbit's eye.
