Acanthisitta chloris

1. NOUN

/ əˌkænθɪˈsɪtə ˈklɔːrɪs / (AmE), / əˌkanθɪˈsɪtə ˈklɔːrɪs / (BrE)
A small green-and-bronze bird, commonly known as the rifleman, native to New Zealand and recognized as one of the smallest bird species there.
small green-bronze New Zealand bird (rifleman)

Examples

The Acanthisitta chloris is notable for its tiny size and insectivorous diet.
Birdwatchers in New Zealand often hope to spot the elusive Acanthisitta chloris during their field trips.
The plumage of Acanthisitta chloris provides excellent camouflage among the mossy branches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rifleman - common English name for Acanthisitta chloris, small New Zealand bird

Etymology

From Greek akanthis (a kind of small bird) and Latin chloris (greenish-yellow).
