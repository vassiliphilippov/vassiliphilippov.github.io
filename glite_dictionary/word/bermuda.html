Bermuda

1. PROPN

/ bərˈmjuːdə / (AmE), / bəˈmjuːdə / (BrE)
A British Overseas Territory consisting of a group of islands in the Atlantic Ocean off the coast of the Carolinas, known for its resorts and beaches.
Atlantic island group; British Overseas Territory

Examples

Many tourists visit Bermuda for its beautiful pink sand beaches and clear blue waters.
He grew up in Bermuda and often talks about the island's unique culture.
The ship was last seen near Bermuda before it disappeared in the Atlantic.

Etymology

Named after the Spanish explorer Juan de Bermúdez, who discovered the islands in the early 16th century.
