chivalry

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪvəlri / (AmE), / ˈʃɪvəlri / (BrE)
polite and courteous behavior, especially that shown by men towards women
courtesy and gallantry toward women

Examples

He opened the door for her as a gesture of chivalry.
Many people regard chivalry as an outdated code of conduct.
Her father’s old-fashioned chivalry always embarrassed her in public.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gallantry - polite attention or respect given by men to women

Etymology

From Old French *chevalerie* (knighthood), ultimately from Late Latin *caballārius* (horseman).

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪvəlri / (AmE), / ˈʃɪvəlri / (BrE)
The medieval system of values and conduct associated with knighthood, such as honor, bravery, and courteousness, especially toward women.
medieval code of knightly conduct

Examples

The young squire aspired to the ideals of chivalry promoted by the knights of the realm.
Many legends praise the chivalry and bravery of medieval heroes such as King Arthur's knights.
The code of chivalry demanded that knights defend the weak and act honorably in battle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knighthood - the rank or character of a knight
  • Synonym: courtesy - polite behavior, especially towards women
  • Antonym: cowardice - lack of bravery

Etymology

From Old French *chevalerie* (knighthood, horse soldiery), from *chevalier* (knight), from Late Latin *caballarius* (horseman).
