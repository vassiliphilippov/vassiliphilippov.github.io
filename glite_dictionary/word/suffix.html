suffix

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌfɪks / (AmE), / ˈsʌfɪks / (BrE)
A letter or group of letters added at the end of a word to form a new word or to change its grammatical function.
word ending added to alter meaning

Examples

The plural form 'cats' is made by adding the suffix '-s' to the word 'cat'.
The suffix '-ness' turns the adjective 'happy' into the noun 'happiness'.
Can you identify the suffix in the word 'careless'?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prefix - an affix added to the beginning of a word

Etymology

From Latin suffixum, neuter of suffixus, past participle of suffigere (to fasten underneath).

2. VERB

/ ˈsʌfɪks / (AmE), / ˈsʌfɪks / (BrE)
to add a suffix to the end of a word or base form
add a suffix to

Examples

In linguistic exercises, students often suffix '-ed' to indicate the past tense.
You can suffix '-ness' to 'happy' to form the noun 'happiness'.
Some teachers encourage children to suffix words to create new vocabulary.

Synset Examples

suffix words

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prefix - to add a prefix to a word

Etymology

From Latin 'suffixus', past participle of 'suffigere' (to fasten underneath).
