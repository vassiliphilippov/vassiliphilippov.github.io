centerpiece

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛntɚˌpis / (AmE), / ˈsɛntəˌpiːs / (BrE)
the most important or central feature of something
main or most important feature

Examples

The new research facility will be the centerpiece of the university’s science program.
The company’s latest smartphone is the centerpiece of its holiday marketing campaign.
Public safety remains the centerpiece of the mayor’s policy agenda.

Synset Examples

education was the centerpiece of the Democratic Party's political platform

Related Terms

  • Synonym: highlight - the most important, interesting, or enjoyable part of something
  • Synonym: cornerstone - an important quality or feature something depends on

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛntɚˌpis / (AmE), / ˈsɛntəˌpiːs / (BrE)
an object, often decorative, placed in the center of a table or other surface, especially as an ornament during a meal or event
decorative object placed at a table's center

Examples

The beautiful floral centerpiece drew everyone's attention at the dinner party.
She arranged candles and leaves as a festive centerpiece for the table.
At the wedding, the crystal vase served as the main centerpiece on each table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ornament - an object used to decorate something

Etymology

From 'center' + 'piece'; modeled after French 'pièce centrale'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɛn.tɚˌpis / (AmE), / ˈsɛntəˌpiːs / (BrE)
the most important or prominent item in a decorative arrangement, often placed at the center of a table or space
main object in a decorative display

Examples

The vase of lilies was the centerpiece on the dining table, drawing everyone's attention.
A beautiful sculpture served as the centerpiece of the hotel lobby's decor.
They chose a large candle arrangement to be the centerpiece for the wedding reception.

Synset Examples

The white telephone was the centerpiece of the decor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: highlight - the most outstanding part of something
  • Degree Lower: sidepiece - an object placed at the side, less important than the centerpiece

Etymology

From 'center' + 'piece', first used in reference to ornaments for the center of a dining table in the 19th century.
