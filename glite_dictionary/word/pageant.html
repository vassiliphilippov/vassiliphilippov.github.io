pageant

1. NOUN

/ ˈpædʒənt / (AmE), / ˈpædʒənt / (BrE)
An elaborate public entertainment consisting of a procession or performance, often involving costumed participants and historical or thematic scenes.
elaborate public procession or entertainment

Examples

The town's annual pageant celebrates its founding with a colorful parade of floats and costumes.
She wore a beautiful gown in the historical pageant last weekend.
The children acted out famous events from history in the school pageant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parade - a public procession, often celebratory
  • Synonym: spectacle - an impressive or grand public display

Etymology

From Middle English *pagyn* or *pagent*, possibly from Medieval Latin *pagina* (scene in a play), influenced by the word 'page.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈpædʒənt / (AmE), / ˈpædʒənt / (BrE)
a rich and splendid public ceremony or celebration, often involving a procession, costumes, or elaborate displays
elaborate public ceremony or celebration

Examples

The royal wedding was marked by a grand pageant through the city streets.
The annual flower festival is a colorful pageant that attracts visitors from around the world.
Children dressed in historical costumes participated in the school’s medieval pageant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parade - a public procession, especially one celebrating a special day or event
  • Synonym: spectacle - an impressive public display or performance

Etymology

From Middle English 'pagyn', perhaps from Latin 'pagina' (page, scene), referring to a scene or tableau in a festival.
