destroyable

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈstrɔɪ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɔɪ.ə.bəl / (BrE)
capable of being destroyed; able to be damaged so much that it no longer exists or functions.
able to be destroyed

Examples

The data is stored in a destroyable format for easy deletion.
Ancient artifacts are often extremely fragile and destroyable by improper handling.
Unlike the old concrete bunker, the temporary shelter was completely destroyable in a storm.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indestructible - impossible to destroy
  • Synonym: destructible - able to be destroyed (synonymous term)

Etymology

From 'destroy' + suffix '-able', meaning capable of being so acted upon.
