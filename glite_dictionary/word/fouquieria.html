Fouquieria

1. NOUN

/ fuːˈkɪəriə / (AmE), / fuːˈkɪəriə / (BrE)
A genus of resinous, succulent trees or shrubs native to desert and semidesert regions of the southwestern United States, often leafless for much of the year.
genus of desert succulent shrubs or trees

Examples

The ocotillo is a well-known species within the genus Fouquieria.
Fouquieria plants have adapted to survive in arid conditions with minimal water.
Many bird species build nests in the thorny branches of Fouquieria shrubs.

Etymology

Named after French physician Pierre Fouquier (1776–1850).

2. NOUN

/ fuːˈkiːəriə / (AmE), / fuːˈkiːərɪə / (BrE)
A genus of resinous succulent trees or shrubs native to desert and semi-desert regions of the southwestern United States, which are leafless for much of the year.
genus of desert succulent shrubs/trees

Examples

The ocotillo, a member of the Fouquieria genus, thrives in harsh desert climates.
Researchers studied how Fouquieria species adapt to long periods without rain.
Many birds nest in the tall, spiny branches of Fouquieria plants.

Etymology

Named after French physician Pierre Fouquier (1776–1850).
