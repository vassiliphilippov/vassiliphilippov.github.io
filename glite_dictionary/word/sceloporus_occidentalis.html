Sceloporus occidentalis

1. NOUN

/ skəˌlɑːpərəs ɑˌksɪdən'tælɪs / (AmE), / skɛˌlɒpərəs ɒksɪdən'tælɪs / (BrE)
A species of common western lizard, also known as the western fence lizard, often seen on logs or rocks in North America.
species of western North American lizard

Examples

The Sceloporus occidentalis is easily spotted basking in the sun on a warm day.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Sceloporus occidentalis in the Sierra Nevada.
Many birds prey upon Sceloporus occidentalis due to its frequent exposure on rocks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: western fence lizard - common name for Sceloporus occidentalis

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Sceloporus' meaning 'leg pore' and 'occidentalis' meaning 'western'.
