automat

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑː.təˌmæt / (AmE), / ˈɔː.təˌmæt / (BrE)
a self-service restaurant or vending machine where prepared food can be bought by inserting coins or tokens
self-service food vending machine

Examples

After a long day, she grabbed a sandwich from the automat on the corner.
Many New Yorkers have fond memories of eating at the old automat in the city.
The invention of the automat made quick, affordable meals more accessible to the public.

Etymology

From German 'Automat', meaning an automatic machine, from Greek 'automatos' (self-acting).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔː.təˌmæt / (AmE), / ˈɔː.təˌmæt / (BrE)
a cafeteria in which food and drink are obtained from vending machines or small compartments, usually requiring coins or tokens
cafeteria with food from machines

Examples

The old automat downtown still serves fresh coffee and sandwiches.
Many people enjoyed the convenience of grabbing lunch at an automat during the 20th century.
After the show, they stopped at an automat for a quick meal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cafeteria - a restaurant where people serve themselves from a counter and pay before eating

Etymology

From German 'Automat', meaning 'automatic machine'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
