antialiasing

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.tiˈeɪ.li.əs.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌæn.tiˈeɪ.li.əs.ɪŋ / (BrE)
A technique in computer graphics used to smooth jagged edges in curves and diagonal lines, making them appear smoother.
computer graphics edge-smoothing technique

Examples

The antialiasing filter improved the quality of the rendered images.
You can enable antialiasing to reduce jagged lines in the graphics settings.
Modern graphics cards provide hardware support for antialiasing.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: aliasing - visual distortion in digital images, such as jagged edges

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'anti-' (against) and 'aliasing', referring to the reduction of aliasing artifacts in graphics.
