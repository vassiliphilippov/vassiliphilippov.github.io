salaat

1. NOUN

/ səˈlɑːt / (AmE), / səˈlɑːt / (BrE)
The ritual prayer of Muslims, performed five times a day facing Mecca, which is one of the Five Pillars of Islam.
Islamic ritual prayer performed five times daily

Examples

Muslims around the world perform salaat five times a day as an essential practice of their faith.
He woke up before sunrise to make sure he did not miss the dawn salaat.
The call to salaat echoes through the streets five times daily in many Muslim countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prayer - an act of communicating with a deity (especially as a petition or in adoration or contrition or thanksgiving)

Etymology

From Arabic ṣalāt (prayer).
