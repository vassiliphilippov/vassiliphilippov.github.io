Corse

1. PROPN

/ kɔrs / (AmE), / kɔːs / (BrE)
an island in the Mediterranean Sea, with nearby islets, forming a region of France; commonly known as Corsica.
island region of France in the Mediterranean

Examples

The rugged mountains of Corse attract hikers from all over the world.
Many traditional dishes in Corse reflect its unique blend of French and Italian influences.
He spent his summer vacation exploring the coastal villages of Corse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Corsica - the Italian and most common English name for Corse, a Mediterranean island region of France

Etymology

From French Corse, from Italian Corsica, Latin Corsica.
