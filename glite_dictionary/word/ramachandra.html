Ramachandra

1. PROPN

/ ˌrɑːməˈtʃʌndrə / (AmE), / ˌrɑːməˈtʃʌndrə / (BrE)
a hero in Hindu mythology, regarded as the seventh incarnation of Vishnu and the central figure of the epic Ramayana
heroic Hindu deity, incarnation of Vishnu

Examples

Many devotees celebrate the birth anniversary of Ramachandra as Ram Navami every year.
The epic Ramayana tells the story of Ramachandra's quest to rescue Sita.
In Hindu tradition, Ramachandra symbolizes virtue, courage, and devotion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rama - alternative name for Ramachandra, Hindu deity and hero of the Ramayana
  • Degree Lower: Vishnu - principal Hindu god, the preserver, of whom Ramachandra is an incarnation
  • Degree Higher: Krishna - another major incarnation of Vishnu in Hindu mythology

Etymology

From Sanskrit रामचन्द्र (Rāmacandra), meaning 'Rama with the moon' (chandra = moon).
