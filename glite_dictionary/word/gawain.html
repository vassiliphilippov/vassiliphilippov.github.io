Gawain

1. PROPN

/ ɡəˈweɪn / (AmE), / ɡəˈweɪn / (BrE)
(in Arthurian legend) a nephew of King Arthur and one of the knights of the Round Table, known for his bravery and courtesy.
Arthurian knight; nephew of King Arthur

Examples

The legend tells of Gawain's quest to find the Green Knight.
Many poets have written about Sir Gawain and his adventures.
Gawain was admired for both his courage and his sense of honor.

Etymology

From Old French Gawain, ultimately of Welsh origin (Gwalchmei).
