Listera convallarioides

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪstərə kənˌvælɛriˈoʊdiːz / (AmE), / ˈlɪstərə kənˌvalərɪˈəʊdiːz / (BrE)
A small North American orchid with two elliptic leaves and a slender raceme of small green flowers.
a small North American orchid species

Examples

The Listera convallarioides can often be found in moist wooded areas in western North America.
Botanists were excited to discover a colony of Listera convallarioides during their field study.
The tiny green flowers of Listera convallarioides are easy to overlook on the forest floor.

Etymology

From genus name Listera and Latin convallarioides (resembling lily of the valley, Convallaria).
