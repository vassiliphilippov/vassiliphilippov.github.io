meteorite

1. NOUN

/ ˈmiː.ti.əˌraɪt / (AmE), / ˈmiː.ti.ə.raɪt / (BrE)
A stony or metallic object from outer space that survives passage through Earth's atmosphere and lands on the Earth's surface.
rock or metal from space that lands on Earth

Examples

A meteorite was discovered in the desert, attracting scientists from around the world.
The museum displays a large meteorite that fell to Earth over a century ago.
After the bright light in the sky, they found a small meteorite in the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meteor - a small body of matter from outer space that enters the Earth's atmosphere and burns up

Etymology

From French 'météorite', from 'météore' (meteor).
