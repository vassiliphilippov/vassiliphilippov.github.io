piddling

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɪd.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɪd.lɪŋ / (BrE)
(informal) very small or insignificant; unimportant.
trivial; of little importance
informal

Examples

He was paid a piddling amount for all his hard work.
The committee spent hours debating a piddling issue.
She was upset over such a piddling mistake.

Synset Examples

a fiddling sum of money
a footling gesture
our worries are lilliputian compared with those of countries that are at war
a little (or small) matter
a dispute over niggling details
limited to petty enterprises
piffling efforts
giving a police officer a free meal may be against the law, but it seems to be a picayune infraction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trivial - of little value or importance
  • Synonym: petty - of secondary or minor importance; trivial
  • Antonym: significant - important; of consequence

Etymology

Diminutive form of the verb 'piddle', meaning to waste time or act in a trivial manner.
