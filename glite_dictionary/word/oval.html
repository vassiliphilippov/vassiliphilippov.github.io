oval

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ.vəl / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.vəl / (BrE)
a closed curve on a plane, shaped like an elongated circle or ellipse, having no sharp corners and a single continuous edge
an elongated round shape; ellipse-like curve

Examples

The artist painted a large oval on the canvas to represent the face.
Please draw an oval around your answer on the test sheet.
The stadium was built in the shape of an oval to accommodate the track.

Synset Examples

the sums of the distances from the foci to any point on an ellipse is constant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ellipse - a regular oval shape, traced by a point moving so that the sum of its distances from two other points is constant

Etymology

From Latin 'ovum' meaning 'egg', referencing the egg-like shape.

2. ADJ

/ ˈoʊ.vəl / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.vəl / (BrE)
having a rounded and slightly elongated shape, like that of an egg or ellipse.
egg-shaped; elliptical

Examples

The artist chose an oval frame for her painting to complement its delicate lines.
The table had an oval top that could seat ten people.
He wore glasses with thin, oval lenses that suited his face.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elliptical - having the shape of an ellipse; oval-shaped
  • Degree Lower: round - having the shape of a circle or sphere
  • Degree Lower: circular - having the form of a circle

Etymology

From Latin 'ovum' meaning 'egg', via French 'oval'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
