springer

1. NOUN

/ ˈsprɪŋɚ / (AmE), / ˈsprɪŋə / (BrE)
the lowest stone or block in an arch, from which the arch itself rises or springs
lowest stone in an arch

Examples

The architect carefully positioned the springer at each side of the archway.
Damage to the springers could threaten the stability of the entire bridge.
Each springer must be cut precisely to ensure the arch's strength.

Etymology

From 'spring', referring to the point from which the arch springs or rises.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsprɪŋər / (AmE), / ˈsprɪŋə / (BrE)
a breed of large spaniel with a wavy, silky coat, usually black or liver and white, originally bred for flushing game
a large spaniel breed of dog

Examples

The springer bounded across the field in pursuit of a pheasant.
She has owned a springer since she was a child, and they went on long walks together.
Their springer loves swimming and retrieving sticks from the lake.

Etymology

From its original function of 'springing' (flushing) game for hunters.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02104513-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈsprɪŋɚ / (AmE), / ˈsprɪŋə / (BrE)
a cow that is about to give birth to a calf
cow about to give birth

Examples

The farmer checked on the springer every few hours to ensure a safe delivery.
They separated the springers from the rest of the herd to monitor them more closely.
A springer usually shows signs of calving within a few days.

Etymology

Derived from the verb 'spring', referring to the cow coming into milk or 'springing' with calf.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
