off-center

1. ADJ

/ ˌɔfˈsɛntər / (AmE), / ˌɒfˈsɛntə / (BrE)
Not exactly in the center; situated away from the central point or axis.
not positioned in the center

Examples

The painting looked crooked because it was hung off-center on the wall.
She placed the candle off-center to create a more dynamic table arrangement.
His tie knot was slightly off-center, giving him a casual appearance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: centered - placed exactly in the center
