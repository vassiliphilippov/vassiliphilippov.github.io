acetophenetidin

1. NOUN

/ əˌsiːtoʊfəˈnɛtɪdɪn / (AmE), / əˌsiːtəʊfəˈnɛtɪdɪn / (BrE)
A white crystalline compound formerly used as an analgesic and antipyretic.
analgesic and antipyretic compound
medical

Examples

Acetophenetidin was once commonly found in over-the-counter headache remedies.
The use of acetophenetidin has declined due to concerns about its safety.
Researchers studied the effects of acetophenetidin on liver function.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phenacetin - another name for acetophenetidin, a pain-relieving drug

Etymology

From 'acet-' (relating to acetic acid) + 'phenetidin', itself from the chemical structure (phenetidine).
