inkstand

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋkˌstænd / (AmE), / ˈɪŋk.stænd / (BrE)
a small container or stand holding ink for writing, typically used for dipping a pen
container for holding writing ink

Examples

The antique inkstand on her desk was made of crystal and silver.
He dipped his quill into the inkstand before continuing the letter.
Collectors often value an ornate Victorian inkstand for its craftsmanship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inkpot - a small container for ink, often used as a synonym for inkstand

Etymology

From 'ink' + 'stand', indicating a stand or holder for ink.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋkˌstænd / (AmE), / ˈɪŋk.stænd / (BrE)
a small tray or stand designed to hold ink and writing implements such as pens and quills
tray or stand for ink and pens

Examples

The antique desk was adorned with an ornate inkstand and a matching letter opener.
She dipped her pen into the inkstand before beginning to write her letter.
A silver inkstand stood in the center of the governor's writing table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inkwell - a small container for holding ink, often part of an inkstand

Etymology

From 'ink' + 'stand', describing its purpose as a stand for ink.
