hitter

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪtər / (AmE), / ˈhɪtə / (BrE)
a person who hits, especially in sports or other activities involving striking
a person who hits

Examples

The baseball team needs a strong hitter to drive in runs.
She has a reputation as a powerful hitter on the volleyball court.
The coach praised him for being the hardest hitter in the league.

Synset Examples

a hard hitter
a fine striker of the ball
blacksmiths are good hitters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: striker - a person who strikes, especially in sports
  • Antonym: non-hitter - a person who does not hit (rare, opposite sense)

Etymology

From 'hit' (verb) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɪtər / (AmE), / ˈhɪtə / (BrE)
a baseball player who is batting or known for batting skill
baseball player who bats

Examples

The team's best hitter stepped up to the plate.
He's always been a consistent hitter throughout his baseball career.
The new coach is looking for a left-handed hitter to strengthen the lineup.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: batter - a player batting in baseball or cricket
