Ledum groenlandicum

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːdəm ˌɡroʊnlænˈdɪkəm / (AmE), / ˈliːdəm ˌɡrəʊnlænˈdɪkəm / (BrE)
An evergreen shrub native to eastern North America, with white or creamy bell-shaped flowers and dark green hairy leaves, historically used to make tea during the American Revolution.
evergreen North American shrub used for tea

Examples

During the American Revolution, people used Ledum groenlandicum leaves as a substitute for imported tea.
The wetland was filled with the fragrant scent of Ledum groenlandicum in bloom.
Botanists collected samples of Ledum groenlandicum to study its medicinal properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Labrador tea - common name for Ledum groenlandicum, a shrub used to make herbal tea

Etymology

From Neo-Latin using Linnaean binomial: genus Ledum + specific epithet groenlandicum (relating to Greenland).
