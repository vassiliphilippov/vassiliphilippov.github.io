abominably

1. ADV

/ əˈbɑː.mə.nə.bli / (AmE), / əˈbɒ.mɪ.nəb.li / (BrE)
In a manner that causes moral revulsion or strong dislike; in an offensive or hateful way.
in an offensive or hateful manner

Examples

He behaved abominably towards his guests and made them feel unwelcome.
The prisoners were treated abominably during their captivity.
She found his remarks abominably rude and refused to respond.

Synset Examples

I don't know anyone who could have behaved so abominably

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appallingly - in a shocking or horrifying way
  • Synonym: offensively - in an insulting or objectionable manner
  • Antonym: admirably - in a manner that deserves respect or approval

Etymology

Derived from Latin *abominabilis* (detestable), through the adjective 'abominable'.

2. ADV

/ əˈbɑː.mə.nəb.li / (AmE), / əˈbɒm.ɪ.nəb.li / (BrE)
In a very unpleasant or detestable manner; extremely badly or offensively.
in an extremely unpleasant or bad way

Examples

The food at that restaurant was abominably prepared and tasted awful.
He behaved abominably towards his friends, refusing to help when they needed him.
She was treated abominably by the company after years of loyal service.

Synset Examples

she sings terribly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horribly - in a very unpleasant or terrible way
  • Synonym: terribly - in an extremely bad or unpleasant manner

Etymology

From Latin abominabilis ('detestable'), from abominari ('to detest').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00055639-r
Synset POS: r
