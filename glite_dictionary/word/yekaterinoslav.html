Yekaterinoslav

1. PROPN

/ jɛkəˌtɛrɪnəˈslɑv / (AmE), / jɛkəˌtɛrɪnəˈslɑːv / (BrE)
the former name for the city of Dnipro in east-central Ukraine, situated on the Dnieper River and known as a major center of the metallurgical industry
former name for Dnipro, Ukraine city

Examples

During the 19th century, Yekaterinoslav grew rapidly as an industrial hub along the Dnieper River.
Many historical documents refer to the Ukrainian city now called Dnipro as Yekaterinoslav.
The family emigrated from Yekaterinoslav to Western Europe in the early 1900s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dnipro - a major city in east-central Ukraine, formerly known as Yekaterinoslav

Etymology

From Russian Екатериносла́в (Yekaterinosláv), named in honor of Empress Catherine II of Russia.
