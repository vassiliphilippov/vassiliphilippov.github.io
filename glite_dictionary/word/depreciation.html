depreciation

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌpriʃiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌpriːsiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
A reduction in the value of an asset over time, especially due to wear and tear, age, or obsolescence.
decrease in asset value over time

Examples

The rapid depreciation of computer equipment means businesses must upgrade frequently.
He calculated the annual depreciation of his company’s vehicles for tax purposes.
Over the years, the house showed little sign of depreciation despite frequent use.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: appreciation - an increase in the value of an asset

Etymology

From Late Latin 'depretiatio' (devaluation), from 'depretiare' (to reduce in value).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˌpriʃiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌpriːsiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of belittling or demeaning someone or something in speech or writing; disparagement.
belittling or disparaging remark

Examples

Her constant depreciation of his achievements began to affect his confidence.
The review was laced with subtle depreciation of the author's work.
Such depreciation can damage a person's self-esteem over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disparagement - the act of speaking about someone in a derogatory manner
  • Antonym: praise - expression of approval; the opposite of belittling

Etymology

From Late Latin 'depretiatio' (a lowering of value), from Latin 'depretium' (to lower in price).

3. NOUN

/ dəˌpriːʃiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌpriːsiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
a reduction in the value or price of something, especially over time or in relation to another currency
decrease in value or price

Examples

The rapid depreciation of the local currency caused concern among investors.
Several factors led to the depreciation of property values in the neighborhood.
The company accounted for the annual depreciation of its equipment in its financial report.

Synset Examples

depreciation of the dollar against the yen

Related Terms

  • Antonym: appreciation - an increase in value or price
  • Synonym: devaluation - the reduction of the value of a currency

Etymology

From Late Latin depreciatio (a lowering in value), from depreciare (to depreciate).
