ursinia

1. NOUN

/ ɜrˈsɪniə / (AmE), / ɜːˈsɪniə / (BrE)
a genus of plants native to southern Africa, cultivated for their bright yellow, orange, or white daisy-like flowers
genus of flowering plants with bright rayed flowers

Examples

The vibrant orange ursinia in her garden bloomed early in the spring.
Gardeners often choose ursinia to add color to rockeries and borders.
After planting ursinia seeds, she was delighted by the profusion of yellow flowers.

Etymology

Named after German botanist Johann Ursinus (1538–1600).
