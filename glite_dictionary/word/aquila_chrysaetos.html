Aquila chrysaetos

1. NOUN

/ əˈkwɪlə kraɪˈsiətəs / (AmE), / əˈkwɪlə krɪˈsiːətɒs / (BrE)
A large eagle of mountainous regions in the Northern Hemisphere, distinguished by its golden-brown head and neck; commonly known as the golden eagle.
golden eagle species (bird)

Examples

The Aquila chrysaetos is revered for its hunting prowess and majestic flight.
Researchers tracked the migration patterns of several Aquila chrysaetos across the European Alps.
The distinctive golden feathers on the head make Aquila chrysaetos easy to identify among eagles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golden eagle - another common name for Aquila chrysaetos

Etymology

From Latin: 'Aquila' meaning 'eagle'; 'chrysaetos' from Greek 'chrysos' (gold) and 'aetos' (eagle).
