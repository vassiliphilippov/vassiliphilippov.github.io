cleaning

1. NOUN

/ ˈkliːnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkliːnɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of making something clean, especially by removing dirt or impurities
the act of making things clean

Examples

The cleaning of the house took all afternoon.
She scheduled a professional cleaning for her carpets.
He forgot to do the cleaning this week, so the kitchen was messy.

Synset Examples

he gave his shoes a good cleaning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: washing - the act of washing something to clean it
  • Antonym: dirtying - the act of making something dirty

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'clean' + -ing (noun-forming suffix).
