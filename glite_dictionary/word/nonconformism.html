nonconformism

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɑn.kənˈfɔrˌmɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.kənˈfɔː.mɪz.əm / (BrE)
The practice or principle of not conforming to established customs, attitudes, or doctrines, especially in religion or culture.
refusal to conform to orthodox beliefs

Examples

Her nonconformism made her stand out in a society focused on tradition.
The movement was rooted in nonconformism and challenged established beliefs.
His nonconformism often put him at odds with strict religious authorities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonconformity - refusal to conform to established practice or belief
  • Antonym: orthodoxy - authorized or generally accepted theory, doctrine, or practice

Etymology

From non- (not) + conformism (adherence to conventions), modeled on conformism.

2. NOUN

/ nɑn.kənˈfɔrˌmɪzəm / (AmE), / nɒn.kənˈfɔː.mɪzəm / (BrE)
the practice of not conforming to established customs, attitudes, or norms, especially in religion or society
practice of not conforming

Examples

Despite pressure from his peers, his nonconformism made him stand out at school.
The movement was rooted in nonconformism, challenging traditional religious doctrines.
Her art reflects a spirit of nonconformism and creative independence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonconformity - the state or practice of not conforming to established customs, attitudes, or norms
  • Antonym: conformism - conforming to accepted standards, rules, or customs

Etymology

From nonconformist + -ism; ultimately based on Latin roots: non- (not) + conformis (like, of the same form).
