to hypothesize

1. VERB

/ haɪˈpɑθəˌsaɪz / (AmE), / haɪˈpɒθɪsaɪz / (BrE)
to suggest or propose an explanation or theory based on limited evidence as a starting point for further investigation
suggest as a possible explanation

Examples

Scientists often hypothesize about the origins of mysterious archaeological findings.
The researchers hypothesized that the chemical would speed up the reaction.
She likes to hypothesize possible solutions before gathering any data.

Synset Examples

Scientists supposed that large dinosaurs lived in swamps

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to suppose - to assume something to be true without proof
  • Degree Lower: guess - to form an opinion without sufficient information
  • Degree Higher: to conclude - to reach a decision or judgment after considering the facts

Etymology

From Greek 'hypotithenai' (to suppose), from 'hypo-' (under) + 'tithenai' (to place).
