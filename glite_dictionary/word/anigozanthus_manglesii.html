Anigozanthus manglesii

1. NOUN

/ ˌænɪɡoʊˈzænθəs ˌmæŋˈɡliːaɪ / (AmE), / ˌænɪɡəʊˈzænθəs ˌmæŋˈɡliːaɪ / (BrE)
A sedge-like, spring-flowering Australian herb with clustered red and green woolly flowers, commonly known as the red-and-green kangaroo paw.
Australian flowering herb; red-and-green kangaroo paw

Examples

The striking flowers of the Anigozanthus manglesii are a symbol of Western Australia.
Gardeners value Anigozanthus manglesii for its vibrant red and green blooms.
On our hike, we spotted several Anigozanthus manglesii thriving in the spring sunshine.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'anisos' (unequal) and 'anthos' (flower); species named after James Mangles, British horticulturist.
