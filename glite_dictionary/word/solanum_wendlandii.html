Solanum wendlandii

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈlænəm wɛndˈlændi.aɪ / (AmE), / səʊˈlɑːnəm wɛndˈlændɪ.aɪ / (BrE)
A climbing vine native to Costa Rica, notable for its sparse hooklike spines and large lilac-blue flowers.
Costa Rican vine with lilac-blue flowers

Examples

The Solanum wendlandii produces stunning clusters of lilac-blue flowers in the summer months.
Gardeners often train Solanum wendlandii to cover fences or trellises for its ornamental value.
Beware of the hooklike spines when pruning Solanum wendlandii in your tropical garden.

Etymology

From the genus name Solanum (Latin, meaning 'nightshade') and the specific epithet wendlandii in honor of German botanist Hermann Wendland.
