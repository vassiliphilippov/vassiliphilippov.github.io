homothermic

1. ADJ

/ ˌhoʊməˈθɜrmɪk / (AmE), / ˌhəʊməˈθɜːmɪk / (BrE)
(of birds and mammals) having a constant and relatively high body temperature, independent of the environment; warm-blooded.
maintaining constant, high body temperature; warm-blooded

Examples

Most mammals are homothermic, enabling them to survive in a wide range of climates.
Birds are homothermic animals, maintaining a steady body temperature even in cold weather.
The homothermic nature of these species allows for high levels of metabolic activity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: poikilothermic - having a body temperature that varies with the environment; cold-blooded
  • Synonym: endothermic - capable of generating internal heat to maintain body temperature
  • Antonym: ectothermic - relying on environmental heat sources to regulate body temperature

Etymology

From Greek 'homoios' (similar) and 'thermē' (heat).
