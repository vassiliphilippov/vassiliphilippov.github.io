flatfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈflætˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈflætˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
The flesh of any of several thin-bodied, flat marine fish, typically served as white, lean fillets.
lean white flesh of flat marine fish

Examples

The restaurant's special tonight is grilled flatfish served with lemon butter sauce.
She prefers the mild flavor of flatfish over other kinds of seafood.
They carefully filleted the fresh flatfish for the chef to prepare.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flounder - a type of flat, edible fish from the same family
  • Synonym: sole - another species of flat, edible fish often used in cooking

Etymology

From 'flat' + 'fish'; refers to the fish's flattened body shape.

2. NOUN

/ ˈflætˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈflætˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a fish with a flattened body that swims on its side along the sea floor, with both eyes on the upper side
a fish with a flattened body and both eyes on one side

Examples

The flatfish buried itself in the sand to hide from predators.
Fishermen often catch various species of flatfish near the ocean floor.
Because both its eyes are on one side, the flatfish has excellent camouflage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flounder - a type of flatfish commonly found in coastal waters
  • Synonym: sole - a type of flatfish known for its edible qualities
  • Synonym: halibut - a very large flatfish with a diamond-shaped body

Etymology

From 'flat' + 'fish', describing its body shape.
