Actinidia polygama

1. NOUN

/ ˌæktɪˈnɪdiə pəˈlɪɡəmə / (AmE), / ˌæktɪˈnɪdiːə pəˈlɪɡəmə / (BrE)
An ornamental vine native to eastern Asia, known for its yellow edible fruit and leaves with silver-white markings.
ornamental Asian vine with yellow fruit

Examples

The leaves of Actinidia polygama are often used as a decorative element in Asian gardens.
Cats are attracted to Actinidia polygama in a manner similar to catnip.
The yellow fruit of Actinidia polygama is both edible and ornamental.

Etymology

From Latinized botanical nomenclature; genus name from Greek 'aktis' (ray) and species name from Greek 'polys' (many) + 'gamos' (married), referring to flower structure.
