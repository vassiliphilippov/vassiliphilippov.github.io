idiopathic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪdiəˈpæθɪk / (AmE), / ˌɪdiəˈpæθɪk / (BrE)
(of a disease or condition) arising spontaneously or from an unknown cause.
arising from an unknown cause (medical)
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that his symptoms were considered idiopathic since no clear cause could be found.
Researchers are studying idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis to better understand its origins.
Many patients are frustrated when their illness is labeled as idiopathic by specialists.

Synset Examples

idiopathic epilepsy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idiogenic - arising internally, not from external causes
  • Antonym: iatrogenic - resulting from the activity of physicians or treatment

Etymology

From Greek idios (one's own) + pathos (suffering, disease).
