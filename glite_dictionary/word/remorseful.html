remorseful

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈmɔrsfəl / (AmE), / rɪˈmɔːsfʊl / (BrE)
filled with or expressing deep regret or sorrow for wrongdoing or sin.
full of regret or sorrow for wrongdoing

Examples

He was truly remorseful after realizing the harm he had caused.
Her remorseful apology moved everyone in the room.
The defendant appeared remorseful as he addressed the victim's family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contrite - feeling or expressing sincere remorse for wrongdoing
  • Antonym: unrepentant - not feeling or showing regret for wrongdoing

Etymology

From 'remorse' + '-ful', with 'remorse' from Latin 'remorsus' (bitten again, tormented).
