delivery

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈlɪvəri / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪvəri / (BrE)
the act of throwing a baseball or softball by the pitcher toward home plate to begin a play
pitcher's throw to begin play (baseball/softball)

Examples

The pitcher's delivery was unusually fast, surprising the batter.
She studied the mechanics of his delivery to improve her own pitching.
After an awkward delivery, the pitcher lost control of the ball.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitch - the act of throwing the ball to the batter (baseball/softball)

Etymology

From Old French delivrer, from Latin deliberare (set free).

6. NOUN

/ dɪˈlɪvɚi / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪvəri / (BrE)
the manner or style in which someone expresses themselves orally, especially in public speaking or performance
style of oral expression

Examples

Her delivery during the debate was calm and persuasive, capturing the audience's attention.
The comedian's delivery of each joke made the performance even funnier.
Critics praised the actor’s delivery of his lines for its natural flow and emotion.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elocution - the skill of clear and expressive speech, especially of distinct pronunciation and articulation
  • Synonym: presentation - the manner in which someone delivers a speech or performance

Etymology

From Old French delivrer (to set free), later developing the sense of 'handing over, presenting,' and by extension, 'the act or style of presenting speech.'
