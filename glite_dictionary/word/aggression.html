aggression

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡrɛʃ.ən / (AmE), / əˈɡrɛʃ.ən / (BrE)
violent or hostile behavior or actions, especially when unprovoked
hostile or violent behavior; unprovoked attack

Examples

The neighboring country condemned the military aggression as an act of war.
Some animals show aggression when they feel threatened or cornered.
Unprovoked aggression between the two groups led to several injuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hostility - hostile or unfriendly behavior
  • Antonym: peace - freedom from disturbance; absence of war or conflict

Etymology

From Latin aggressio (attack), from aggredi (to attack)
