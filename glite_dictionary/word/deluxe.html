deluxe

1. ADJ

/ dəˈlʌks / (AmE), / dɪˈlʌks / (BrE)
of a higher quality and more luxurious than usual; elegant and sumptuous.
luxurious; of superior quality

Examples

The hotel offered a deluxe suite with a private balcony and ocean view.
Their deluxe edition of the album includes bonus tracks and special packaging.
She treated herself to a deluxe spa treatment for her birthday.

Synset Examples

a deluxe car
luxe accommodations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: luxurious - very comfortable and expensive; giving great pleasure
  • Antonym: economy - intended to save money by being less expensive and less luxurious

Etymology

From French 'de luxe' (of luxury).

2. ADJ

/ dəˈlʌks / (AmE), / dɪˈlʌks / (BrE)
Of a very high standard or quality; luxurious or richly appointed, often in an ostentatious way.
of superior quality; luxurious

Examples

We booked a deluxe room with a view of the ocean for our anniversary trip.
The restaurant offers a deluxe tasting menu featuring rare and expensive ingredients.
This limited edition watch comes in a deluxe presentation box with gold trim.

Synset Examples

a princely sum
gilded dining rooms
these architecture magazines are full of the lush interiors of the rich and famous

Related Terms

  • Synonym: luxurious - extremely comfortable or elegant, especially in a sumptuous way
  • Antonym: economy - providing the minimum required and lacking luxury; the opposite end of accommodation/service quality

Etymology

From French 'de luxe', meaning 'of luxury'.
