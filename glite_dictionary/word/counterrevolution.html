counterrevolution

1. NOUN

/ ˌkaʊntərˌrɛvəˈluːʃən / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntəˌrɛvəˈluːʃən / (BrE)
A political movement or revolution aimed at reversing the changes brought about by a previous revolution.
a movement to reverse a prior revolution

Examples

The counterrevolution attempted to restore the monarchy that had been overthrown.
After the initial uprising, supporters of the old regime launched a counterrevolution.
Historians debate whether the movement was a true counterrevolution or just political unrest.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: revolution - a forcible overthrow of a government or social order

Etymology

Formed from 'counter-' (against) and 'revolution', modeled after similar formations.
