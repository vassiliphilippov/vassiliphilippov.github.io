Nynorsk

1. NOUN

/ niːˈnɔrsk / (AmE), / ˈniːnɔːsk / (BrE)
one of the two official written standards of the Norwegian language, based primarily on rural dialects
Norwegian language standard based on rural dialects

Examples

Many students in Norway learn both Nynorsk and Bokmål in school.
The official documents were translated into Nynorsk to reach a wider audience.
He prefers reading novels written in Nynorsk because of their authentic rural flavor.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Bokmål - the other official standard of written Norwegian, based mainly on Danish-influenced language

Etymology

From Norwegian 'Nynorsk', literally 'new Norwegian', formed from 'ny' (new) + 'norsk' (Norwegian).
