duration

1. NOUN

/ dəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / djʊəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the length of time that something lasts or continues
length of time something lasts

Examples

The duration of the meeting was two hours.
You'll have access to the equipment for the duration of your course.
For the duration of the storm, everyone stayed indoors.

Synset Examples

the ceremony was of short duration
he complained about the length of time required

Related Terms

  • Synonym: period - a length or portion of time
  • Degree Lower: moment - a very short period of time
  • Degree Higher: eternity - an infinite or unending length of time

Etymology

From Latin 'dūrātiō' (a lasting, continuance), from 'dūrāre' (to last).

3. NOUN

/ dəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / djʊəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
The length of time during which something continues or exists.
length of time something lasts

Examples

The movie's duration is just under two hours.
She waited for the duration of the storm before leaving the house.
Please remain seated for the duration of the performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: period - a length or portion of time
  • Synonym: interval - a period between two events

Etymology

From Latin 'duratio' (lasting), from 'durare' (to last, endure).
