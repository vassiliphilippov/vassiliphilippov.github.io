indorser

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdɔrsər / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɔːsə / (BrE)
a person who expresses strong approval or support for something or someone
person who shows strong approval

Examples

The mayor became a passionate indorser of the community outreach program.
She was the first prominent indorser of the candidate’s proposal.
As a longtime indorser of environmental causes, he frequently speaks at public events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endorser - a person who publicly supports or approves something or someone

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'endorser', from endorse + -er.

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdɔrsər / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɔːsə / (BrE)
a person who signs a check or negotiable instrument to transfer their ownership interest to another.
person who transfers interest by signing a negotiable instrument
legal

Examples

The indorser of the check must sign the back before it can be deposited.
As an indorser, she transferred the title of the bond to the new owner.
Banks often require the indorser to provide identification to process the transfer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endorser - a person who endorses a check or document

Etymology

From endorse + -er, with older legal spelling 'indorse'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10077079-n
Synset POS: n
