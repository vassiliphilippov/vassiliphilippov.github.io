to skive

1. VERB

/ skaɪv / (AmE), / skaɪv / (BrE)
to remove a thin layer or slice from the surface of something, especially leather, typically to reduce its thickness
remove a thin surface layer (esp. of leather)

Examples

To make the leather more flexible, the craftsman began to skive its edges.
This machine can skive layers off the rubber to achieve the right thickness.
She carefully skived the hide to prepare it for bookbinding.

Synset Examples

skive leather

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shave - to cut thin slices from the surface of something
  • Synonym: plane - to smooth or reduce the thickness of wood or another material by shaving or cutting

Etymology

Of Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian *skifa* (to slice off).
