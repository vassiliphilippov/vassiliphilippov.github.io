slaying

1. NOUN

/ ˈsleɪ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsleɪ.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the unlawful killing of a person, especially one that is intentional and premeditated
unlawful killing of a person

Examples

The police are still investigating the slaying of a local businessman.
In the novel, the mysterious slaying shocked the quiet town.
She was arrested in connection with the brutal slaying last year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: murder - the unlawful premeditated killing of one human being by another
  • Degree Lower: manslaughter - the unlawful killing of a person without premeditation

Etymology

From the verb 'slay', from Old English 'slean' (to strike, slay).
