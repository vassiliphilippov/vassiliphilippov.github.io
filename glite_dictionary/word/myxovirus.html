myxovirus

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪk.soʊˌvaɪ.rəs / (AmE), / ˈmɪk.səʊˌvaɪ.rəs / (BrE)
any of a group of RNA viruses that includes those causing influenza and mumps.
group of RNA viruses (e.g. influenza, mumps)
medical

Examples

Scientists isolated the myxovirus responsible for the outbreak to better understand how it spreads.
The researcher studied how the myxovirus affects respiratory cells in different animal species.
Vaccines can help prevent diseases caused by members of the myxovirus group, such as influenza.

Etymology

From Greek myxa (mucus) + virus, referring to the viruses' association with mucous membranes.
