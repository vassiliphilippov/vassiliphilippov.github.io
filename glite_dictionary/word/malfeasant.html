malfeasant

1. NOUN

/ mælˈfisənt / (AmE), / mælˈfiːzənt / (BrE)
A person who is guilty of wrongdoing or illegal conduct, especially in a public office.
one guilty of wrongdoing or malfeasance
legal

Examples

The court concluded that the public official was a malfeasant who abused his position for personal gain.
Historians debated whether the governor was truly a malfeasant or simply misguided in his decisions.
A whistleblower exposed the actions of several malfeasants within the corporation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malefactor - person who commits a crime or offense
  • Synonym: wrongdoer - person who does something morally or legally wrong

Etymology

From the French 'malfaisant' (doing evil), from 'mal-' (badly) + 'faisant' (doing).
