cherimoya

1. NOUN

/ ˌtʃɛrɪˈmɔɪə / (AmE), / ˌtʃɛrɪˈmɔɪə / (BrE)
A small tropical American tree bearing large, green, round or oblong fruit with sweet, creamy flesh; also, the fruit of this tree.
tropical fruit tree; its sweet fruit

Examples

The creamy flesh of the cherimoya is highly prized in many tropical countries.
We bought a ripe cherimoya at the market and enjoyed it for dessert.
The cherimoya tree thrives in subtropical climates with mild winters.

Etymology

From Spanish 'cherimoya', from Quechua 'chirimuya' (cold seeds).

2. NOUN

/ ˌtʃɛrəˈmɔɪə / (AmE), / ˌtʃɛrɪˈmɔɪə / (BrE)
A large, tropical, green fruit with leathery skin and sweet, creamy pulp, related to the custard apple.
tropical fruit with creamy pulp

Examples

The ripe cherimoya tasted sweet and creamy, like a blend of banana and pineapple.
She bought a cherimoya at the market to try a new tropical fruit.
Farmers in South America harvest cherimoyas during the rainy season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: custard apple - a tropical fruit similar to cherimoya with sweet, soft flesh

Etymology

From Spanish 'cherimoya', from Quechua 'chirimoya'.
