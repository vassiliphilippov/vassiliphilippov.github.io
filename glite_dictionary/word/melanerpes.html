Melanerpes

1. NOUN

/ mɛˈlænərˌpiːz / (AmE), / mɛˈlanəˌpiːz / (BrE)
A genus of woodpeckers found in the New World, especially in North and South America, characterized by distinctive patterns and colors.
a genus of woodpeckers

Examples

The Melanerpes genus includes the acorn woodpecker, which is known for storing acorns in tree bark.
Scientists have identified more than twenty species within the Melanerpes genus across the Americas.
Unlike other woodpeckers, birds of the Melanerpes genus often exhibit social nesting behaviors.

Etymology

From Greek melas, melanos (black) + herpēs (creeper), referring to the bird's color and climbing habits.

2. PROPN

/ ˌmɛləˈnɜrpiːz / (AmE), / ˌmɛləˈnɜːpiːz / (BrE)
A genus of New World woodpeckers in the family Picidae, found primarily in the Americas.
genus of New World woodpeckers

Examples

The red-bellied woodpecker belongs to the genus Melanerpes.
Several species in Melanerpes have distinctive markings and calls.
Researchers observed a Melanerpes colony nesting in the palm trees.

Etymology

From Greek 'melas' (black) and 'herpēs' (creeper).
