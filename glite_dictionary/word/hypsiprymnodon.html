Hypsiprymnodon

1. NOUN

/ hɪpˌsɪprɪmˈnɒdɑn / (AmE), / hɪpˌsɪprɪmˈnɒdɒn / (BrE)
A genus of small marsupials commonly known as musky rat-kangaroos, native to Australia.
genus of musky rat-kangaroos

Examples

The Hypsiprymnodon is the only living member of its family, found in the rainforests of Queensland.
Scientists are studying Hypsiprymnodon to learn more about marsupial evolution.
A Hypsiprymnodon looks similar to other small marsupials, but has distinctive musky scent glands.

Etymology

From Greek 'hypsi-' (high), 'prymnos' (hindmost), and 'odous' (tooth), referring to dental features.
