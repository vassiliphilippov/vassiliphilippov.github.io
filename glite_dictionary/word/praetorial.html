praetorial

1. ADJ

/ priːˈtɔːriəl / (AmE), / priːˈtɔːriəl / (BrE)
relating to a Roman praetor, or to the office or authority of a praetor in ancient Rome
relating to a Roman praetor

Examples

The senator argued that the new law extended praetorial powers beyond traditional limits.
Historical texts analyze the influence of praetorial authority in ancient Roman governance.
Legal scholars debate the scope of praetorial intervention in provincial matters.

Synset Examples

praetorial powers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: praetorian - relating to a Roman praetor or the imperial guard

Etymology

From Latin praetorius, from praetor (magistrate of ancient Rome) + -ial.
