wight

1. NOUN

/ waɪt / (AmE), / waɪt / (BrE)
(Archaic) A living being, especially a person; often used in poetry and old literature.
archaic: a living being or person
archaic

Examples

The poor wight wandered the streets alone, searching for shelter.
In many old tales, a brave wight confronts monsters and evil spirits.
No mere wight could endure such hardships without losing hope.

Etymology

From Old English 'wiht' (creature, thing), related to German 'Wicht' (creature, little person).

2. NOUN

/ waɪt / (AmE), / waɪt / (BrE)
A supernatural being, often depicted as a malevolent or haunting spirit, especially in folklore and fantasy literature.
malevolent or haunting spirit

Examples

The villagers whispered that a wight haunted the ruins at night, preying on the unwary.
Legends tell of a wight that rises from its grave during the full moon.
The wight glided silently through the mist, searching for lost souls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ghost - the soul of a dead person, especially as a shadowy figure
  • Synonym: spirit - a supernatural being or essence

Etymology

From Old English 'wiht' (creature, being), related to German 'Wicht'.
