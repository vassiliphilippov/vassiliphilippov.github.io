Byblos

1. PROPN

/ ˈbɪbloʊs / (AmE), / ˈbɪblɒs / (BrE)
an ancient Mediterranean seaport and city-state in Phoenicia, located in present-day Lebanon, historically important for papyrus trade
ancient Phoenician city in Lebanon

Examples

Archaeologists have uncovered fascinating ruins in Byblos, shedding light on Phoenician civilization.
The ancient port of Byblos played a key role in the export of papyrus to Egypt and beyond.
Today, tourists visit the site of Byblos to explore its historical landmarks and vibrant old souks.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Byblos, from Phoenician (Gubla).
