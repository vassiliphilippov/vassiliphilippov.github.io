Stokesia

1. NOUN

/ stoʊkˈsiːə / (AmE), / stəʊkˈsiːə / (BrE)
A genus of North American perennial herbs in the daisy family, best known for Stokes' aster, which has showy blue or purple flowers.
a genus of perennial herbs (Stokes' aster)

Examples

The vibrant blue flowers of the Stokesia attract many pollinators to the garden.
Gardeners often plant Stokesia for its long blooming season in late summer.
Botanists regard Stokesia as one of the most ornamental native asters.

Etymology

Named after Jonathan Stokes (1755–1831), English physician and botanist.
