vaporousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪpərəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈveɪpərəsnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being filled with vapor, haze, or mist, making something appear cloudy or indistinct.
cloudiness from haze, mist, or vapor

Examples

The heavy vaporousness of the morning made it difficult to see the distant hills.
The painting captured the vaporousness of the fog as it rolled across the harbor.
She described the forest air's vaporousness after a night of rain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cloudiness - the state of being cloudy or obscured, especially in the air
  • Antonym: clearness - the quality of being free from cloudiness or haze

Etymology

From 'vaporous' + '-ness'; 'vaporous' from Latin 'vapor' (steam, heat, exhalation).
