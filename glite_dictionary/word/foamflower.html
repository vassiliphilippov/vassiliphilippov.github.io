foamflower

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊmˌflaʊər / (AmE), / ˈfəʊmˌflaʊə / (BrE)
A woodland plant with small, white, foamy-looking flowers that blooms in spring, typically found in North America.
woodland plant with white foamy flowers

Examples

The shady corner of our garden is filled with blooming foamflower in late April.
Hikers admired the delicate foamflower growing along the forest trail.
She planted foamflower next to the ferns for a natural, woodland look.

Etymology

Named for its clusters of small, frothy-appearing white flowers resembling foam.
