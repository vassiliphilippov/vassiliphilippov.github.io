reorganisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːˌɔrɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌɔːɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of arranging or structuring something in a new or different way, often involving significant or extensive changes
the process of organizing differently
british

Examples

The company announced a major reorganisation to improve efficiency and adapt to market changes.
After the merger, the university underwent a complete reorganisation of its academic departments.
The government's reorganisation of the health service caused widespread debate among professionals.

Synset Examples

a committee was appointed to oversee the reorganization of the curriculum
top officials were forced out in the cabinet shakeup

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reorganization - the process of arranging differently; American spelling

Etymology

From re- (again) + organisation, modelled after French réorganisation.
