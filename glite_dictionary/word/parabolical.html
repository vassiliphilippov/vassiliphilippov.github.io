parabolical

1. ADJ

/ ˌpærəˈbɑlɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌpærəˈbɒlɪkəl / (BrE)
having the shape or form of a parabola
having the form of a parabola

Examples

The satellite's dish has a parabolical shape to focus the signal efficiently.
We studied the motion of an object following a parabolical trajectory.
The architect designed a roof with a parabolical curve for aesthetic appeal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parabolic - having the form of a parabola

Etymology

From 'parabola', from Greek 'parabolē' (comparison, parabola), with the suffix '-ical'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌpærəˈbɑlɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌparəˈbɒlɪkəl / (BrE)
expressed in the form of a parable or resembling a parable; allegorical or symbolic in nature, often used in reference to stories or speech.
expressed like a parable; allegorical

Examples

The priest's parabolical sermon was filled with stories that carried deep moral lessons.
Her writing style is often parabolical, relying on symbolic tales to convey complex ideas.
The fable had a parabolical tone, inviting readers to search for hidden meanings beneath the surface.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allegorical - using symbolic figures or actions to convey deeper meaning
  • Antonym: literal - taking words in their usual or most basic sense

Etymology

From Medieval Latin parabolicus (relating to a parable) + -al.
