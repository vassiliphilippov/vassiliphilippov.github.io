shoat

1. NOUN

/ ʃoʊt / (AmE), / ʃəʊt / (BrE)
A young pig, especially one that is newly weaned.
a young pig

Examples

The farmer bought several shoats to raise on his land.
The shoat trotted after its mother around the pen.
At the county fair, children competed to show the healthiest shoat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piglet - a very young pig

Etymology

Probably from Middle Low German *schōte* (a young, newly-weaned pig).
