vogue

1. NOUN

/ voʊɡ / (AmE), / vəʊɡ / (BrE)
the state of being popular and widely accepted or used at a particular time
state of being popular or in fashion

Examples

The mini skirt was once very much in vogue in the 1960s.
These kinds of diets are no longer in vogue these days.
Minimalist design has come into vogue in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: fad - an intense and widely shared enthusiasm for something, especially one that is short-lived
  • Synonym: trend - a general direction in which something is developing or changing
  • Antonym: outdated - no longer in fashion or use

Etymology

From French 'vogue' (drift, fashion), from voguer (to row, to sail).

2. NOUN

/ voʊɡ / (AmE), / vəʊɡ / (BrE)
the prevailing fashion or general style and taste at a particular time
prevailing fashion or style

Examples

The color green is back in vogue this season, appearing in many designer collections.
Short skirts were once in vogue during the 1960s cultural revolution.
Technology companies often try to predict what will be in vogue several years in advance.

Synset Examples

leather is the latest vogue
he followed current trends
the 1920s had a style of their own

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trend - a general direction in which something is developing or changing
  • Degree Lower: fad - an intense and widely shared enthusiasm for something, especially one that is short-lived

Etymology

From French 'vogue' (fashion), originally meaning 'wave, course' from Old French 'voguer' (to row, to move forward).
