Apodemus sylvaticus

1. NOUN

/ ˌæpəˈdiːməs sɪlˈvætɪkəs / (AmE), / ˌæpəˈdiːməs sɪlˈvatɪkəs / (BrE)
A nocturnal yellowish-brown mouse native to woods, fields, and gardens in Europe and western Asia; also known as the wood mouse.
a species of wood mouse

Examples

The Apodemus sylvaticus is frequently seen darting across woodland floors at night.
Researchers tracked the movements of Apodemus sylvaticus to study its habitat preferences.
Gardeners sometimes encounter Apodemus sylvaticus while digging in flowerbeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wood mouse - common English name for Apodemus sylvaticus

Etymology

From Greek 'apodēmos' (away from home) and Latin 'sylvaticus' (of the woods).
