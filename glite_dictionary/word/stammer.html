stammer

1. NOUN

/ ˈstæmər / (AmE), / ˈstamə / (BrE)
A speech disorder characterized by involuntary pauses, repetitions, or prolongations of sounds, syllables, or words.
speech disorder with hesitations/repetitions

Examples

Because of his stammer, he often felt anxious about speaking in public.
The speech therapist helped children cope with their stammer and gain confidence.
Her stammer became less noticeable as she practiced daily exercises.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stutter - a similar speech disorder involving involuntary repetition or prolongation of sounds

Etymology

From Middle English stameren, from Old English stamerian (to stammer).

Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ ˈstæmɚ / (AmE), / ˈstæmə / (BrE)
to speak with involuntary pauses or repetitions, often making speech hesitant or difficult to understand
speak with pauses or repetitions

Examples

Whenever he was nervous, he would stammer over his words during presentations.
She tried not to stammer when answering the teacher's questions.
He stammered an apology before quickly leaving the room.

Synset Examples

The speaker faltered when he saw his opponent enter the room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stutter - to speak with involuntary repetition or prolongation of sounds

Etymology

From Middle English stammeren, from Old English stamerian or stamerian, of imitative origin.

Synset POS: v
