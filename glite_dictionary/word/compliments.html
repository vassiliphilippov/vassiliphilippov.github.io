compliments

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmpləˌmɛnts / (AmE), / ˈkɒmplɪmənts / (BrE)
polite expressions of goodwill or respect, often used in the phrase 'give/send my compliments' to convey good wishes to someone.
polite expressions of good wishes or respect

Examples

Please give my compliments to your parents when you see them.
He sent his compliments and hoped to see us soon.
With her compliments, she included a small gift in the package.

Synset Examples

give him my kind regards
my best wishes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regards - polite expressions of good wishes
  • Synonym: wishes - expressions of hope for someone's happiness or welfare

Etymology

From Latin 'complēmentum' (that which fills up, completion) via French 'complément', adopted into English in the 17th century.
