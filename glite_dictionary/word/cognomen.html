cognomen

1. NOUN

/ kɑgˈnoʊmən / (AmE), / kɒgˈnəʊmən / (BrE)
a surname or family name, as distinguished from a given name; especially used in ancient Rome to denote a family within a clan
surname or family name

Examples

Many Roman citizens had a cognomen that indicated their family lineage.
Her cognomen was widely recognized among scholars of history.
The ancient inscription revealed both the man's praenomen and his cognomen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surname - the family name passed from one generation to another
  • Antonym: given name - the personal name given to an individual at birth or baptism, as opposed to the family name

Etymology

From Latin cognōmen, meaning 'surname' or 'additional name'.

2. NOUN

/ kɑɡˈnoʊ.mən / (AmE), / kɒɡˈnəʊ.mən / (BrE)
a familiar or informal name for a person, often a shortened or affectionate form of their given name
familiar or informal name; nickname

Examples

Her friends always called her by her cognomen instead of her full name.
The famous general's cognomen reflected his fearless reputation on the battlefield.
Although his birth name was Alexander, he was known everywhere by his cognomen, Lex.

Synset Examples

Joe's mother would not use his nickname and always called him Joseph
Henry's nickname was Slim

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nickname - an informal, often affectionate, name for a person or thing

Etymology

From Latin cognōmen (‘surname, nickname’), from co- (‘together’) + nōmen (‘name’).
