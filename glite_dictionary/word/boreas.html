Boreas

1. PROPN

/ ˈbɔːriəs / (AmE), / ˈbɔːriəs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the god who personified the north wind.
Greek god of the north wind

Examples

In ancient tales, Boreas would sweep down from the mountains, bringing icy winds to Greece.
The painter depicted Boreas as a strong, bearded figure with billowing robes and wings.
Many poets invoked the power of Boreas to symbolize the harshness of winter winds.

Synset Examples

Boreas was pictured as bearded and powerful and winged and draped against the cold

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Βορέας (Boréas), the god of the north wind.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɔːriəs / (AmE), / ˈbɔːrɪəs / (BrE)
(literary) a north wind; in Greek mythology, the god of the north wind.
north wind; god of the north wind (Greek myth)

Examples

According to legend, Boreas swept across the mountains, bringing a sudden chill to the village.
In ancient poems, Boreas is often invoked as a powerful and fearsome force.
The sailors feared the arrival of Boreas, as it signaled rough northern storms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: north wind - wind coming from the north

Etymology

From Greek Boreas (Βορέας), god of the north wind.
