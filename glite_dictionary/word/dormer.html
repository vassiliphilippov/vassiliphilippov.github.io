dormer

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɔrmɚ / (AmE), / ˈdɔːmə / (BrE)
A window set vertically in a small gable projecting from a sloping roof, often to provide light and space to an attic.
window projecting from a sloping roof

Examples

The house was remodeled to add a dormer that brightened the attic space.
From the street, the quaint cottage with its single dormer caught everyone's eye.
She looked out the dormer window and watched the snow fall gently on the roof.

Etymology

From French 'dormir' (to sleep), originally referring to a sleeping room, later to roof windows in such rooms.
