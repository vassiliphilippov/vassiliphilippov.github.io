to omit

1. VERB

/ oʊˈmɪt / (AmE), / əʊˈmɪt / (BrE)
to leave out or fail to include something, either intentionally or accidentally
to leave out; not include

Examples

The editor decided to omit the final chapter from the published version.
If you omit any required fields on the form, it will not be processed.
She accidentally omitted his name from the guest list.

Synset Examples

How could I miss that typo?
The workers on the conveyor belt miss one out of ten

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to include - to contain as part of the whole
  • Synonym: to exclude - to deliberately leave something out

Etymology

From Latin omittere (to let go, disregard), from ob- (away) + mittere (send).

2. VERB

/ əˈmɪt / (AmE), / əˈmɪt / (BrE)
to leave out or not include someone or something, either deliberately or accidentally
leave out; fail to include

Examples

She decided to omit the final paragraph from her essay.
Certain sensitive data was omitted from the official report.
If you omit any details, your application might be rejected.

Synset Examples

The bad results were excluded from the report
Leave off the top piece

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exclude - to deliberately not include something
  • Antonym: to include - to make someone or something part of a group or list

Etymology

From Latin omittere (to let go, neglect), from ob- (away) + mittere (to send).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00617464-v
Synset POS: v
