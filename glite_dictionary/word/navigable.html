navigable

1. ADJ

/ ˈnævɪɡəbəl / (AmE), / ˈnævɪɡəbl / (BrE)
(of a waterway) wide or deep enough to allow ships or boats to travel through or across it safely.
able to be sailed on safely

Examples

Large ships can only travel on navigable rivers that are deep enough to avoid running aground.
The authorities assessed whether the flood had made the canal navigable again.
The map marks all navigable channels to help sailors plot a safe course.

Synset Examples

navigable waters
a navigable channel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: passable - (of a route or road) able to be traveled along or through
  • Antonym: impassable - not able to be traveled through or across

Etymology

From Latin 'navigabilis', from 'navigare' (to sail).
