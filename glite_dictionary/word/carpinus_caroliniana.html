Carpinus caroliniana

1. NOUN

/ kɑrˈpaɪnəs ˌkɛr.ə.lɪˈnaɪ.ə / (AmE), / kɑːˈpʌɪnəs ˌkær.ə.lɪˈneɪ.nɪə / (BrE)
a small North American tree with smooth grey bark and blue-green leaves that turn red-orange in autumn, also known as American hornbeam
a North American tree; American hornbeam

Examples

The shady grove featured a magnificent Carpinus caroliniana near the riverbank.
In the autumn, the leaves of Carpinus caroliniana turn brilliant shades of red and orange.
Gardeners often appreciate Carpinus caroliniana for its attractive smooth bark and dense foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American hornbeam - common name for Carpinus caroliniana

Etymology

From Latin 'Carpinus' (hornbeam) and 'caroliniana', referring to Carolina, USA.
