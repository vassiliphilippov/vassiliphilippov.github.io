myology

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / maɪˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the scientific study of the structure, function, and diseases of muscles
study of muscles
medical

Examples

Advances in myology have improved our understanding of muscular disorders.
She decided to specialize in myology because she was fascinated by muscle anatomy.
Research in myology often overlaps with studies in neurology and orthopedics.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: physiology - the branch of biology dealing with normal functions of living organisms

Etymology

From French 'myologie', based on Greek 'mys' (muscle) and '-logy' (study).
