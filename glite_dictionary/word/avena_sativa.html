Avena sativa

1. NOUN

/ əˈviːnə səˈtiːvə / (AmE), / əˈviːnə səˈtiːvə / (BrE)
A species of grass widely cultivated in temperate regions for its edible seeds, commonly known as oats.
the oat plant (cultivated cereal grass)

Examples

Avena sativa is a major cereal crop grown for its nutritious grains.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Avena sativa in cool climates.
Commonly known as oats, Avena sativa is found in many breakfast foods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oat - the grain or the plant from Avena sativa

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'cultivated oat.'
