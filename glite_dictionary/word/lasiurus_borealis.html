Lasiurus borealis

1. NOUN

/ læˈsaɪjʊrəs bɔːˈreɪəlɪs / (AmE), / læˈsɪjʊərəs bɔːˈriːəlɪs / (BrE)
A North American bat species with brick-red or rusty red fur tipped in white, commonly known as the eastern red bat.
North American brick-red bat species

Examples

The Lasiurus borealis is commonly seen fluttering through wooded areas at dusk.
Researchers tagged several Lasiurus borealis specimens to track their migration patterns.
Unlike other bats, Lasiurus borealis often roosts singly in trees rather than in caves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eastern red bat - Common name for Lasiurus borealis

Etymology

From Latin; 'Lasiurus' meaning 'hairy tail' from Greek 'lasios' (hairy) and 'oura' (tail), and 'borealis' meaning 'northern'.
