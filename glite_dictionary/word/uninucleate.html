uninucleate

1. ADJ

/ ˌjuːnɪˈnuːkliˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪˈnjuːkliˌeɪt / (BrE)
(of a cell or organism) having a single nucleus.
having one nucleus
medical

Examples

Most bacteria are uninucleate cells, containing only one nucleus.
The researchers observed a uninucleate stage during the organism's life cycle.
A uninucleate cell is simpler in structure compared to a multinucleate cell.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: binucleate - having two nuclei
  • Antonym: multinucleate - having more than one nucleus

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'uni-' (one) + 'nucleus' (kernel, core) + '-ate', meaning 'having'.
