Lepiota rubrotincta

1. NOUN

/ ˌliːpiˈoʊtə ˌruːbroʊˈtɪŋktə / (AmE), / ˌlɛpɪˈəʊtə ˌruːbrɒˈtɪŋktə / (BrE)
A species of agaric mushroom characterized by a relatively small pink to red cap and white gills and stalk.
a mushroom with a pink to red cap

Examples

The forest floor was dotted with Lepiota rubrotincta, their red caps standing out among the leaves.
Some mushroom guides caution against eating Lepiota rubrotincta due to its resemblance to toxic species.
Botanists gathered samples of Lepiota rubrotincta for further study in the laboratory.

Etymology

From New Latin; 'Lepiota' is the genus name, 'rubrotincta' means 'red-tinged' in Latin.
