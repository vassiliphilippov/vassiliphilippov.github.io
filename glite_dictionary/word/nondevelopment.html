nondevelopment

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɑːndɪˈvɛləpmənt / (AmE), / ˌnɒndɪˈvɛləpmənt / (BrE)
The failure of normal development to occur in a person, organism, or system.
failure of normal development

Examples

The nondevelopment of certain skills in childhood can indicate a developmental disorder.
Chronic malnutrition may result in the nondevelopment of vital organs.
Environmental factors are often cited as causes for the nondevelopment of the region.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: development - the process of developing or being developed

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'non-' (not) and 'development'.
