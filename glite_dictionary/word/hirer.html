hirer

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪərə / (BrE)
a person who employs or selects workers for a job
person who employs workers

Examples

The hirer conducted interviews with all shortlisted candidates before making a decision.
As the company's hirer, she ensures that only qualified applicants are selected.
The hirer was praised for creating a diverse and effective team.

Synset Examples

the boss hired three more men for the new job

Related Terms

  • Synonym: employer - a person or organization that employs people
  • Antonym: employee - a person employed for wages or salary

Etymology

From the verb 'hire' (Old English hyran, of Germanic origin) + -er, denoting a person who does an action.
