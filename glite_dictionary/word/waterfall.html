waterfall

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtɚˌfɔl / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəˌfɔːl / (BrE)
a place where water flows over a vertical drop or a series of drops in the course of a stream or river
a place where water flows over a steep drop

Examples

Tourists from around the world came to see the majestic waterfall cascading down the cliffs.
We hiked for hours before finally reaching the hidden waterfall in the forest.
The sound of the waterfall was soothing as it echoed through the valley.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cascade - a small waterfall, typically one of several that fall in stages
  • Degree Lower: rapids - fast-flowing section of a river, less steep than a waterfall

Etymology

Formed from 'water' + 'fall', meaning a place where water falls downward.
