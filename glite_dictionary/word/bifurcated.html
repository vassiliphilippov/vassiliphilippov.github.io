bifurcated

1. ADJ

/ ˈbaɪfərˌkeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈbʌɪfəˌkeɪtɪd / (BrE)
divided into two branches or parts.
divided into two parts

Examples

The river has a bifurcated channel created by a small island in its center.
Our society remains bifurcated along economic lines, with clear divisions between the rich and poor.
She examined the bifurcated leaves, noting how each one split neatly into two lobes.

Synset Examples

socially bifurcated populations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: split - divided into parts
  • Antonym: united - joined together as a single entity

Etymology

From Latin 'bifurcus' (two-pronged).
