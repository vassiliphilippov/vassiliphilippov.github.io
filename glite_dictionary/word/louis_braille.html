Louis Braille

1. PROPN

/ luˈiː brɛl / (AmE), / ˈluːi ˈbreɪl / (BrE)
(1809–1852) A French educator who became blind at three and invented the Braille system of writing and printing for blind people.
French inventor of the Braille system

Examples

Louis Braille revolutionized reading and writing for blind people with his innovative system.
Many schools for the visually impaired teach about Louis Braille and his remarkable contributions.
The anniversary of Louis Braille's birth is celebrated around the world by organizations serving the blind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Braille - A system of writing for the blind using raised dots
