to equalize

1. VERB

/ ˈikwəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈiːkwəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make things equal or uniform in amount, status, or value
make things equal or uniform

Examples

The new policy aims to equalize pay between men and women.
Heavy rainfall can equalize water levels in the connected lakes.
We must equalize the workload so that no one feels overwhelmed.

Synset Examples

let's equalize the duties among all employees in this office
The company matched the discount policy of its competitors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: balance - to bring into or keep in equal or satisfying proportion or harmony
  • Synonym: match - to make equal or suitable in corresponding respects
  • Antonym: to differentiate - to recognize or state the difference between things

Etymology

From French 'égaliser', from 'égal' (equal), from Latin 'aequalis'.

2. VERB

/ ˈiːkwəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈiːkwəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make things equal, especially to make a score or amount the same for all sides or participants
make equal, especially scores

Examples

The team managed to equalize just before halftime, bringing the score to 2–2.
After trailing for most of the match, she equalized with a stunning goal in the last minute.
They are hoping to equalize the scores in the next game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: level - to make a score or amount equal
  • Antonym: to surpass - to go beyond in amount or degree, often a score

Etymology

From Latin aequalis (equal) + -ize (verb-forming suffix)
