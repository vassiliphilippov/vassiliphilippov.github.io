to reposit

1. VERB

/ rɪˈpoʊzɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈpɒzɪt / (BrE)
to put or store something away, especially for safekeeping
store or put away for safekeeping

Examples

The curator decided to reposit the ancient manuscripts in a climate-controlled vault.
After the ceremony, the crown was carefully reposited in its display case.
He plans to reposit his grandfather's letters in the family archive for future generations.

Synset Examples

the treasure found in the ancient tomb was reposited in the museum

Related Terms

  • Synonym: store - to put something away for future use
  • Synonym: deposit - to put something in a place for safekeeping

Etymology

From Latin reponere (to put back, store away), from re- (back) + ponere (to place).
