Rhododendron viscosum

1. NOUN

/ ˌroʊdəˈdɛndrən vɪˈkɑsəm / (AmE), / ˌrəʊdəˈdɛndrən vɪˈkɒsəm / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub native to swamps of the eastern United States, with small white to pinkish, fragrant flowers resembling honeysuckle.
swamp shrub with fragrant white or pink flowers

Examples

The Rhododendron viscosum thrives in moist, acidic soils found in woodland swamps.
Gardeners appreciate Rhododendron viscosum for its fragrant, honeysuckle-like blossoms in the late spring.
We spotted a cluster of Rhododendron viscosum near the edge of the marsh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swamp azalea - another common name for Rhododendron viscosum

Etymology

From the genus name 'Rhododendron', Greek *rhódon* (rose) + *déndron* (tree), and specific epithet *viscosum*, Latin for 'sticky' or 'viscous'.
