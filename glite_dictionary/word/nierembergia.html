Nierembergia

1. NOUN

/ niːˌɛrɛmˈbɜːrdʒiə / (AmE), / niːˌɛrɛmˈbɜːdʒɪə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American herbs, either upright or creeping, with solitary flowers, belonging to the nightshade family.
genus of tropical American flowering herbs

Examples

Gardeners often plant Nierembergia for its attractive, violet-colored flowers.
The creeping species of Nierembergia make an excellent ground cover in warm climates.
Botanists studied the floral structure of several Nierembergia specimens found in South America.

Etymology

Named after Juan Eusebio Nieremberg, Spanish Jesuit and naturalist.
