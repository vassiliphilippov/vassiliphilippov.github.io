Arenaria

1. NOUN

/ ˌær.əˈnɛr.i.ə / (AmE), / ˌær.əˈnɛː.rɪ.ə / (BrE)
A genus of wading birds known as turnstones, characterized by their habit of flipping over stones in search of food.
genus of birds (turnstones)

Examples

The Arenaria species are often seen along rocky shorelines during migration.
Researchers studied the feeding habits of Arenaria in their natural habitat.
Several birdwatchers spotted an Arenaria turning over stones in search of insects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turnstone - a wading bird that turns over stones to find food

Etymology

From Latin 'arenarius' (pertaining to sand), from 'arena' (sand), referring to the bird’s coastal habitat.
