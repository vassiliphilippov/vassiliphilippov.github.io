sluicing

1. ADJ

/ ˈsluːsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsluːsɪŋ / (BrE)
(of rain or liquid) falling or pouring in a heavy, continuous flow, as if from a sluice.
falling or pouring heavily (as liquid)

Examples

The sluicing rain beat down on the rooftops all night.
Water was sluicing through the broken gutter, creating a small flood.
They arrived at the shelter, soaked from the sluicing downpour outside.

Synset Examples

the sluicing rain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pouring - flowing rapidly in large quantities
  • Degree Lower: drizzling - raining lightly

Etymology

From the verb 'sluice' (to let out or pour liquid through a sluice), from Middle English, from Old French escluse, from Latin exclusa (sluice).
