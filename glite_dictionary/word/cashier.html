cashier

1. NOUN

/ kæˈʃɪr / (AmE), / kæˈʃɪə / (BrE)
A person employed by a bank to receive and pay out money to customers.
bank employee handling money

Examples

The cashier at the bank helped me deposit a check this morning.
If you need to withdraw cash, please go to the cashier at the front counter.
After counting the bills, the cashier handed the customer a receipt and wished her a good day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teller - a person who works in a bank receiving and paying out money

Etymology

From French caissier (treasurer, cashier), from caisse (money box, cash desk).

2. NOUN

/ kæˈʃɪr / (AmE), / kəˈʃɪə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to receive and process payments for goods and services, especially in shops, banks, or restaurants
person who handles payments, especially in stores

Examples

The cashier quickly scanned my items and handed me the receipt.
At the supermarket, the cashier smiled as she counted my change.
He waited in line until the cashier was ready to check him out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clerk - a person employed to keep records or accounts, or to provide sales or customer service

Etymology

From French 'caissier', from 'caisse' (money box, cash desk).

3. VERB

/ kæˈʃɪr / (AmE), / kæˈʃɪə / (BrE)
to dismiss someone from a position, especially in the military, in disgrace or with dishonor.
dismiss with dishonor (esp. military)

Examples

After the scandal, the officer was cashiered from the army in disgrace.
He was cashiered for disobeying direct orders.
The general threatened to cashier any soldier found guilty of theft.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dismiss - to remove someone from a position or job
  • Synonym: to expel - to force someone to leave, especially from a job or position

Etymology

From French 'casser' (to annul, break), influenced by Dutch 'kaseren'.

to dismiss someone from a position or office, especially with dishonor or disgrace
dismiss from office, often in disgrace

Examples

She feared her actions would cause the board to cashier her from the committee.
Following the scandal, the general was cashiered in disgrace.
