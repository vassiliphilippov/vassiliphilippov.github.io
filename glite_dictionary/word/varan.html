varan

1. NOUN

/ vəˈræn / (AmE), / vəˈrɑːn / (BrE)
A large tropical carnivorous lizard found in Africa, Asia, and Australia, sometimes believed in folklore to warn of crocodiles; also known as a monitor lizard.
large tropical monitor lizard

Examples

The varan is a skilled hunter, often preying on smaller mammals and birds.
Villagers sometimes believe the varan warns them of crocodiles lurking nearby.
During our trip to Australia, we spotted a large varan basking in the sun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monitor lizard - Another term for a large lizard of the genus Varanus

Etymology

From French 'varan,' from Arabic 'waran,' referring to a monitor lizard.
