jerkiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɜrkɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːkɪnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being jerky; characterized by abrupt, spasmodic, or irregular movements or progress.
quality of being jerky or irregular

Examples

The jerkiness of the old film made it difficult to follow the action.
She noticed a certain jerkiness in his walk after the accident.
Driving on the rough road added a noticeable jerkiness to the ride.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spasmodic - occurring in brief, irregular bursts
  • Antonym: smoothness - the quality of being smooth or even

Etymology

From 'jerky' + '-ness', where 'jerky' is related to 'jerk' (sudden movement).
