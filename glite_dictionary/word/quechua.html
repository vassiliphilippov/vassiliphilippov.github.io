Quechua

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛtʃwə / (AmE), / ˈkɛtʃwə / (BrE)
a member of an Indigenous people of the Andes, especially in Peru, who were formerly the ruling class of the Inca Empire
member of Peruvian Indigenous Andean people

Examples

Many Quechua still live in the highland villages of Peru, preserving their ancient traditions.
Anthropologists have studied Quechua communities to learn more about Inca history.
The Quechua played a central role in the governance of the Inca Empire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Inca - member of the Indigenous people of Peru historically ruling the Inca Empire

Etymology

From Spanish 'Quechua', from Quechua 'Qhichwa'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɛtʃwɑː / (AmE), / ˈkɛtʃwə / (BrE)
a member of an indigenous people of the Andes, especially in Peru, who were formerly the ruling class of the Incan Empire
member of an Andean indigenous group in Peru

Examples

Many Quechua continue to live in the highlands of Peru and maintain traditional customs.
The Quechua were central to the administration of the Incan Empire.
Modern Quechua communities are known for their colorful weaving and festivals.

Etymology

From Quechua *qichwa* (the Quechua people or their language).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɛtʃwə / (AmE), / ˈkɛtʃwə / (BrE)
the language or group of languages spoken by the Quechua people of the Andes, notably the language of the Inca Empire, still widely used in parts of South America
Andean indigenous language, once spoken by the Incas

Examples

Many people in Peru and Bolivia still speak Quechua in their daily lives.
He studied Quechua to better understand Inca history and culture.
The documentary featured songs and stories performed in Quechua.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Aymara - another major indigenous language of the Andes

Etymology

From Spanish 'quechua', from native Quechua 'qichwa' (temperate valley).

Synset POS: n
