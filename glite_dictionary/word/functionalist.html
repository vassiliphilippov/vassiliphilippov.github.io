functionalist

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʌŋkʃənəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈfʌŋkʃənəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who follows or advocates functionalism, especially in philosophy, sociology, or architecture
supporter of functionalism

Examples

As a leading functionalist, she emphasized the practical role of education in society.
The functionalist argued that every aspect of society serves a particular purpose.
Architects with a functionalist approach design buildings primarily for utility, not ornamentation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: structuralist - person who supports structuralism (often contrasted with functionalist)

Etymology

From 'functionalism' + '-ist' (denoting an adherent or advocate).
