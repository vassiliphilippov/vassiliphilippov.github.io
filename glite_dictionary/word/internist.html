internist

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtɜrnɪst / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːnɪst / (BrE)
a doctor who specializes in internal medicine, focusing on the diagnosis and non-surgical treatment of adult diseases
specialist in internal medicine
medical

Examples

After years of training, she became a respected internist at the city hospital.
The internist referred the patient to a cardiologist for further evaluation.
Many adults see an internist for regular check-ups and management of chronic conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: physician - a person qualified to practice medicine, especially one who diagnoses and treats diseases

Etymology

From French *interniste* (specialist in internal medicine) from *interne* (intern).
