myriagram

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪriəˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈmɪriəˌɡræm / (BrE)
A metric unit of mass equal to 10,000 grams or 10 kilograms.
metric mass unit (10,000 grams)

Examples

The shipment weighed exactly one myriagram, making calculations for export simple.
In early 19th-century France, the myriagram was occasionally used as a commercial unit of mass.
A myriagram is equal to ten kilograms, though the term is now rarely encountered.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: gram - basic metric unit of mass
  • Degree Higher: centner - a metric unit equal to 100 kilograms

Etymology

From French ‘myriagramme’, from Greek ‘myrios’ (countless, 10,000) + ‘gramme’ (gram).
