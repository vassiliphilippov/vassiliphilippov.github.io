monosaccharide

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑnəˈsækəˌraɪd / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈsækəraɪd / (BrE)
A simple sugar that cannot be broken down into simpler sugars; the most basic unit of carbohydrates, such as glucose or fructose.
simple sugar; basic carbohydrate unit
medical

Examples

Glucose is a common monosaccharide found in the bloodstream of animals.
The body quickly absorbs monosaccharides due to their simple structure.
Fructose, a monosaccharide present in fruit, is known for its sweet taste.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: disaccharide - a sugar composed of two monosaccharide units
  • Degree Higher: polysaccharide - a carbohydrate whose molecules consist of a number of sugar molecules bonded together
  • Degree Higher: carbohydrate - a group of organic compounds including sugars, starch, and cellulose

Etymology

From Greek 'monos' (single) + 'sakkharon' (sugar).
