childhood

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪld.hʊd / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪld.hʊd / (BrE)
The period of a person's life when they are a child; the state or time of being a child.
the time of being a child

Examples

She spent most of her childhood in the countryside, surrounded by nature.
His happiest memories are from his early childhood.
Moving to a new city in childhood can be both exciting and challenging.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: adulthood - the period of life after childhood

Etymology

From Old English 'cildhad', meaning the state or period of being a child.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪldˌhʊd / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪldhʊd / (BrE)
the period in a person's life when they are a child, between infancy and adolescence
the period between infancy and adolescence

Examples

She spent most of her childhood in a small village by the sea.
Memories from my childhood still influence the way I see the world.
He moved to another country during his childhood, which shaped his outlook on life.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: adulthood - the state or condition of being fully grown or mature; the period after childhood
  • Degree Lower: infancy - the state or period of early childhood or babyhood
  • Degree Higher: adolescence - the period following childhood and preceding adulthood, typically involving puberty

Etymology

From Old English 'cildhād', combining 'child' and '-hood' (state or condition).
