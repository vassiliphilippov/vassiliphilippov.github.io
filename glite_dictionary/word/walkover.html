walkover

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔkˌoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːkˌəʊvə / (BrE)
A gymnastic movement in which a person performs a backbend followed by passing the legs over the body until standing again, often incorporating a handstand position.
gymnastic backbend passing through handstand

Examples

She flawlessly executed a back walkover in her floor routine.
During practice, he hurt his wrist attempting a one-handed walkover.
Learning the walkover is often a milestone for young gymnasts.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔkˌoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈwɔːkˌəʊvə / (BrE)
an undertaking or contest that is easily won or accomplished, requiring little effort
easy task or victory

Examples

The election was a walkover for the popular candidate, who won by a landslide.
With such inexperienced opponents, the game turned out to be a walkover.
What was expected to be a tough negotiation became a walkover after the other side agreed immediately.

Synset Examples

marketing this product will be no picnic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cakewalk - something easily accomplished or won
  • Synonym: no-brainer - a decision or task that is very easy to do
  • Antonym: struggle - a difficult or strenuous effort

Etymology

Originally a term in horseracing from a situation where only one horse competed and simply had to walk over the course to claim victory.
