ale

1. NOUN

/ eɪl / (AmE), / eɪl / (BrE)
A type of beer brewed with a top-fermenting yeast, typically with a distinctive bitter taste; in some regions, defined as a brew with more than 4% alcohol by volume.
type of beer brewed with top-fermenting yeast

Examples

He ordered a pint of ale at the local pub.
This brewery is famous for its strong, bitter ale.
The festival offered a wide variety of ales from around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beer - Alcoholic drink made from the fermentation of cereals, usually barley.
  • Antonym: lager - Type of beer made with bottom-fermenting yeast and usually lighter in taste than ale.

Etymology

Old English *alu*; related to Old Norse *öl*, Old High German *alu* (ale, beer).
