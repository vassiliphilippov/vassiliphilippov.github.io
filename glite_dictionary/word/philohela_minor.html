Philohela minor

1. NOUN

/ faɪloʊˈhiːlə ˈmaɪnər / (AmE), / fɪləʊˈhiːlə ˈmɪnə / (BrE)
A small species of long-billed woodcock, native to North America, prized as a game bird; also known as the American woodcock.
species of North American woodcock

Examples

The Philohela minor is sought after by hunters for its flavorful meat.
During migration, flocks of Philohela minor are a common sight in eastern woodlands.
The distinctive long bill of the Philohela minor helps it probe for earthworms in soft soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American woodcock - common name for Philohela minor, a game bird native to North America

Etymology

From New Latin: 'Philohela' (genus name from Greek philo- 'loving' + helos 'marsh') and 'minor' (Latin for 'smaller').
