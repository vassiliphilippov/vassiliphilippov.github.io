flunk

1. VERB

/ flʌŋk / (AmE), / flʌŋk / (BrE)
to fail to achieve a passing grade in an examination or course
fail to pass a test or class

Examples

If you flunk the exam, you might have to repeat the class.
She was so worried she would flunk chemistry, but she passed in the end.
He didn't study for the test and ended up flunking it.

Synset Examples

She studied hard but failed nevertheless
Did I fail the test?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fail - to be unsuccessful in achieving a passing grade

Etymology

Probably a variant of dialectal English "flink" (to fall short), origin obscure.

Source: WordNet

2. NOUN

/ flʌŋk / (AmE), / flʌŋk / (BrE)
A failure, especially in a test or course; the act of failing to achieve a passing grade.
failure in an exam or course
informal american

Examples

He was upset about getting a flunk in mathematics last semester.
If you get another flunk, you might have to repeat the year.
The college officially notified him of his flunk in chemistry.

Synset Examples

his failing the course led to his disqualification
he got two flunks on his report

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fail - To be unsuccessful in achieving a passing grade
  • Antonym: pass - To succeed in meeting the required standard in an exam or course

Etymology

Originated in late 19th-century American English, possibly imitative or related to "flinch".

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00067721-n
Synset POS: n
