scandalousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈskændələsnəs / (AmE), / ˈskændələsnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being scandalous; disgracefulness that shocks or offends public morals
quality of being scandalous or shocking

Examples

The scandalousness of the politician's actions shocked the entire nation.
Many newspapers thrived on the scandalousness of celebrities' private lives.
He was drawn to the scandalousness of the rumors circulating through the small town.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scandalous - causing general public outrage by a perceived offense against morality or law
  • Antonym: respectability - the quality of being socially acceptable or proper
