mouthpiece

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊθˌpis / (AmE), / ˈmaʊθˌpiːs / (BrE)
the part of a telephone or similar device into which a person speaks
part of a phone one speaks into

Examples

Please speak clearly into the mouthpiece so I can hear you.
Old telephones had a separate mouthpiece and earpiece connected by a cord.
She covered the mouthpiece with her hand to whisper something privately.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: receiver - the part of a telephone you listen to

Etymology

mouth + piece; named for being the part placed near the mouth.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊθ.piːs / (AmE), / ˈmaʊθ.piːs / (BrE)
A protective device worn inside the mouth, especially by athletes, to shield the teeth and mouth from injury.
protective device for the mouth (sports)

Examples

The boxer replaced his mouthpiece before the final round began.
Coaches always remind players to wear a mouthpiece during practice to prevent injuries.
After losing his mouthpiece, the game was paused so the referee could retrieve it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gumshield - device worn to protect the teeth and gums during sports

/ ˈmaʊθˌpis / (AmE), / ˈmaʊθ.piːs / (BrE)
the part of a pipe or cigarette holder that is placed in the mouth when smoking
part of pipe or cigarette holder you put in your mouth

Examples

He replaced the old mouthpiece of his pipe with a new one.
The cigarette holder had an ivory mouthpiece that felt cool against her lips.
After years of use, the mouthpiece became discolored and needed cleaning.

Etymology

From 'mouth' + 'piece', first used in the 17th century.

4. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊθˌpis / (AmE), / ˈmaʊθ.piːs / (BrE)
a part of a device or instrument that goes over or into the mouth, especially to allow breathing or to play an instrument
part of a device fitting over the mouth

Examples

She placed the mouthpiece of the inhaler gently between her lips before taking a deep breath.
The diver checked that his mouthpiece was securely attached before entering the water.
Each child was given a new mouthpiece for the recorder lesson to ensure proper hygiene.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
