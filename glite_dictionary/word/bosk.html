bosk

1. NOUN

/ bɑsk / (AmE), / bɒsk / (BrE)
a small area of trees or thicket; a small wooded area
small wooded area; thicket

Examples

The winding path led us through a dense bosk on the edge of the estate.
A family of deer grazed quietly in the morning light within the bosk.
We picnicked beneath the cool shade of a bosk overlooking the meadow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grove - a small group of trees
  • Degree Higher: forest - a large area covered chiefly with trees and undergrowth

Etymology

From Middle English *bosk* (bush, thicket), likely from Old French *bosque* or Italian *bosco*, both meaning 'wood' or 'grove'.
