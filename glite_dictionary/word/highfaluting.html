highfaluting

1. ADJ

/ ˌhaɪfəˈluːtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌhaɪfəˈluːtɪŋ / (BrE)
(of language, manners, or ideas) characterized by pretentiousness or an affected sense of importance; pompous or snobbish.
pretentious; affectedly important
informal

Examples

He responded with a highfaluting speech that no one really understood.
She thought their highfaluting manners just covered their insecurity.
The article was too highfaluting for the average reader to enjoy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretentious - attempting to impress by affecting greater importance or merit
  • Antonym: modest - unassuming or moderate in the estimation of one's abilities or achievements

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly an alteration of 'highfalutin', itself of American origin, combining 'high' (lofty) and an invented, playful term.
