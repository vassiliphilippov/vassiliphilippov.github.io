ugly

1. ADJ

/ ˈʌɡli / (AmE), / ˈʌɡli / (BrE)
(of a mood or behavior) likely to become angry or violent, or having a menacing character.
menacing or likely to become angry

Examples

The conversation took an ugly turn when tempers began to flare.
There was an ugly tension in the crowd before the fight started.

3. ADJ

/ ˈʌɡli / (AmE), / ˈʌɡli / (BrE)
Unpleasant or unattractive in appearance; displeasing to the senses.
unattractive, unpleasant in appearance

Examples

The wallpaper in the living room was so ugly that they decided to replace it.
She wore a pair of ugly shoes that clashed with her outfit.
The sculpture may be ugly to some, but others find it fascinating.

Synset Examples

ugly furniture

Related Terms

  • Antonym: beautiful - pleasing to the senses or mind
  • Antonym: attractive - pleasing or appealing to the senses
  • Synonym: unattractive - not pleasing in appearance

Etymology
