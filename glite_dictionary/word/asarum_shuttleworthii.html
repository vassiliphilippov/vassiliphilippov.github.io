Asarum shuttleworthii

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɛr.əm ʃʌt.l̩ˈwɝθ.i.aɪ / (AmE), / əˈsæɹ.əm ˌʃʌt.l̩ˈwɜː.θɪ.aɪ / (BrE)
A species of wild ginger with persistent, heart-shaped, pungent leaves, native to the region from West Virginia to Alabama.
a wild ginger species of Southeastern US

Examples

The leaves of Asarum shuttleworthii have a distinct aroma often compared to ginger.
Botanists discovered a rare colony of Asarum shuttleworthii in the Appalachian woods.
In shaded gardens, Asarum shuttleworthii thrives as a ground cover.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild ginger - any plant of the genus Asarum, commonly called wild ginger

Etymology

From the genus name Asarum and species named in honor of botanist Robert J. Shuttleworth.
