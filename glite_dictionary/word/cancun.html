Cancun

1. PROPN

/ kænˈkuːn / (AmE), / kanˈkuːn / (BrE)
a resort city in southeastern Mexico, located on the northeastern coast of the Yucatan Peninsula, known for its beaches and tourist attractions
Mexican resort city on the Yucatan Peninsula

Examples

Cancun attracts millions of tourists every year with its beautiful white-sand beaches and clear turquoise waters.
Last winter, we spent a week relaxing at an all-inclusive resort in Cancun.
The flight to Cancun from New York takes about four hours.

Etymology

From Mayan 'kaan kun' meaning 'nest of snakes'.
