beguiling

1. ADJ

/ bɪˈɡaɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / bɪˈɡaɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
charming or attractive in a way that often tricks or deceives; highly appealing or tempting
charmingly attractive, often deceptively

Examples

She gave him a beguiling smile that made him forget his worries.
The novel's beguiling storyline kept me reading late into the night.
They were lured in by the beguiling promise of quick riches.

Synset Examples

an alluring prospect
her alluring smile
the voice was low and beguiling
difficult to say no to an enticing advertisement
a tempting invitation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alluring - powerfully attractive or charming
  • Synonym: enticing - attractive or tempting; alluring
  • Antonym: repellent - causing disgust or distaste; repulsive

Etymology

From Middle English 'begilen', from be- (prefix) + guile (deceit or trickery).

2. ADJ

/ bɪˈɡaɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / bɪˈɡaɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
charming or enchanting, often in a deceptive way
charmingly deceptive

Examples

The magician had a beguiling smile that put the entire audience at ease.
She listened to his beguiling stories, unsure of what to believe.
The advertisement was so beguiling that many people bought the product without hesitation.

Synset Examples

taken in by beguiling tales of overnight fortunes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charming - pleasant or attractive in a way that delights
  • Synonym: deceptive - giving an appearance or impression different from the true one; misleading
  • Antonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and sincere

Etymology

From Middle English 'begilen', from 'be-' (thoroughly) + 'guile' (deceit).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
