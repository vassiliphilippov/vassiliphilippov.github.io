benumbed

1. ADJ

/ bɪˈnʌmd / (AmE), / bɪˈnʌmd / (BrE)
Deprived of physical sensation or feeling, typically as a result of cold, shock, or emotion.
lacking physical sensation

Examples

After the long walk in the snow, her fingers were completely benumbed.
He sat motionless, benumbed by the shock of the news.
The climber's toes became benumbed from exposure to the icy wind.

Synset Examples

my foot is asleep
numb with cold

Related Terms

  • Synonym: numb - deprived of the power of sensation

Etymology

Past participle of 'benumb', from Middle English 'benumen' (to deprive of sensation), from 'be-' (thoroughly) + 'numb'.

2. ADJ

/ bɪˈnʌmd / (AmE), / bɪˈnʌmd / (BrE)
deprived of feeling, responsiveness, or sensitivity, especially as a result of overexposure or excessive stimulation
deprived of feeling or responsiveness

Examples

After watching nonstop news coverage, she felt benumbed to the tragedy.
The children became benumbed by repetitive lectures at school.
His benumbed emotions made it hard for him to react to good or bad news.

Synset Examples

the mind of the audience is becoming dulled
the benumbed intellectual faculties can no longer respond

Related Terms

  • Synonym: numb - deprived of physical or emotional sensation
  • Antonym: stimulated - having increased responsiveness or interest

Etymology

From the prefix be- and 'numb', meaning to make numb.
