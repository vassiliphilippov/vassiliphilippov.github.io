armada

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈmɑdə / (AmE), / ɑːˈmɑːdə / (BrE)
A large fleet of ships, especially a war fleet.
large fleet of ships

Examples

The ancient empire assembled an armada to defend its coastline from invaders.
The company launched an armada of trucks to deliver supplies across the country.
An armada of fishing boats left the harbor before sunrise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fleet - a group of ships sailing together

Etymology

From Spanish 'armada' (armed force, fleet), from Latin 'armata' (armed).
