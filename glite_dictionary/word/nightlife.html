nightlife

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪtˌlaɪf / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtˌlaɪf / (BrE)
Entertainment and social activities that are available at night in a town or city, such as bars, clubs, and shows.
entertainment and activities at night

Examples

New York is famous for its vibrant nightlife and 24-hour entertainment.
Many young people are attracted to the nightlife in big cities like London and Tokyo.
After dinner, they explored the local nightlife by visiting a few popular clubs.

Synset Examples

a futile search for intelligent nightlife

Related Terms

  • Antonym: daylife - the activities that happen during the day

2. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪtˌlaɪf / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtˌlaɪf / (BrE)
Entertainment and social activities that take place at night, such as going to bars, clubs, theaters, or music venues.
entertainment and activities at night

Examples

The city's nightlife is famous for its lively music and vibrant dance clubs.
Many tourists come to enjoy the nightlife along the riverfront during summer evenings.
She prefers a quiet dinner to the excitement of downtown nightlife.

Synset Examples

in the summer the nightlife shifts to the dance clubs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nightspot - a place for nighttime entertainment, such as a bar or club
  • Antonym: daytime - the period of the day when it is light and most people are awake

Etymology

Formed from 'night' + 'life', first recorded in the early 20th century.
