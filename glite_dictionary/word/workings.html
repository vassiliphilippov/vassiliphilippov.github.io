workings

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɝːkɪŋz / (AmE), / ˈwɜːkɪŋz / (BrE)
the internal mechanisms or parts that make something operate, especially a machine or system.
internal mechanism or operation

Examples

The engineer explained the workings of the new engine in detail.
She was fascinated by the complex workings of the antique clock.
It took years to uncover the hidden workings of the corrupt organization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mechanism - a system of parts working together in a machine; a piece of machinery

Etymology

From 'work' (Old English 'weorc') + '-ing', with plural denoting multiple internal processes or components.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɝːkɪŋz / (AmE), / ˈwɜːkɪŋz / (BrE)
a place, such as a mine or quarry, where minerals are extracted from the earth, especially one that is currently in use or has been used in the past
a worked mine or quarry

Examples

Explorers discovered ancient workings deep in the forest, evidence of early mining activity.
The tunnel leads directly to the old silver workings beneath the hillside.
Safety measures were tightened around the active workings after the recent inspection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mine - an excavation from which minerals and other materials are extracted
  • Synonym: quarry - an open excavation for extracting stone, slate, etc.

Etymology

From 'work' + '-ing,' here used in the plural to denote the area subjected to mining or excavation.
