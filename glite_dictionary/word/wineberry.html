wineberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪnˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈwaɪnbəri / (BrE)
a deciduous shrub or small tree with attractive leaves and red berries that turn black when ripe, used especially for making wine
shrub/tree with edible berries used for wine

Examples

The wineberry produces clusters of fruit that are popular for homemade preserves.
In late summer, the wineberry bushes are covered in ripe, dark berries ready to be picked.
Many gardeners appreciate the wineberry for both its ornamental value and culinary uses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raspberry - a small, soft, red fruit and the plant that bears it

Etymology

Formed from 'wine' and 'berry' to denote a berry used for winemaking.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪnˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈwaɪnbəri / (BrE)
A species of raspberry native to China and Japan, grown for its ornamental pale pink flowers and small, red, tart fruit.
species of raspberry with ornamental flowers and tart red fruit

Examples

The gardener planted a row of wineberry bushes along the fence for their attractive blooms and fruit.
We picked ripe wineberry fruit from the tangled vines during our summer hike.
Some chefs use wineberry to add a tart flavor to gourmet desserts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raspberry - an edible soft fruit, related to the wineberry
