to cadge

1. VERB

/ kædʒ / (AmE), / kædʒ / (BrE)
to obtain something from someone by persuasion or without paying for it, often by imposing on their generosity
get by persuasion, often without paying

Examples

He tried to cadge a lift from his colleague after the meeting.
It's rude to cadge drinks every time we go out together.
She managed to cadge an extra ticket to the concert from a friend.

Synset Examples

he is always shnorring cigarettes from his friends

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mooch - to ask for or obtain something without paying
  • Synonym: to beg - to ask for something earnestly or humbly

Etymology

Of uncertain origin; possibly from Middle English 'cadgen' (to carry, hawk wares).

2. VERB

/ kædʒ / (AmE), / kædʒ / (BrE)
to ask for and obtain something such as food, money, or favors for free, often habitually or without intent to repay
to obtain something for free by asking

Examples

He would often cadge cigarettes from his co-workers during lunch breaks.
Is it all right if I cadge a ride home with you?
She didn't bring lunch, hoping to cadge some food from her friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to scrounge - seek to obtain something, typically food or money, at the expense of others

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *cadge* (to carry, hawk, peddle), of uncertain origin.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
