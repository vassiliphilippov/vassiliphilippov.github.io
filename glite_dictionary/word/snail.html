2. NOUN

/ sneɪl / (AmE), / sneɪl / (BrE)
An edible terrestrial mollusc, often served as food prepared in the shell with a sauce of melted butter and garlic.
edible terrestrial mollusc (used as food)

Examples

The restaurant served snail with garlic butter as a delicacy.
She was surprised to find snail listed on the appetizer menu.
At the French market, you can buy fresh snail ready to cook.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escargot - an edible snail, especially as prepared in France as food

Etymology

From Old French *esnaile*, from Vulgar Latin *cochleārium* (snail shell), from Latin *cochlea* (snail).

3. NOUN

/ sneɪl / (AmE), / sneɪl / (BrE)
A small, slow-moving animal with a soft, moist body and a spiral shell, found on land or in water.
small mollusk with spiral shell

Examples

A snail slowly inched its way across the garden path after the rain.
Children often collect snails from the garden to observe their spiral shells.
The snail withdrew into its shell when it sensed danger.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: slug - a gastropod mollusk similar to a snail but lacking a shell

Etymology

From Old French *escargol*, from Vulgar Latin *coculīna*, a diminutive of *coclea* (snail, spiral shell), from Greek *kokhlias*.
