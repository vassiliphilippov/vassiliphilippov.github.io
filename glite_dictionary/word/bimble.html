to bimble

1. VERB

/ ˈbɪmbəl / (AmE), / ˈbɪmbəl / (BrE)
to walk or move about in a leisurely or aimless way
move or walk leisurely or aimlessly
informal british

Examples

After lunch, we decided to bimble around the park and enjoy the fresh air.
On Sundays, she enjoys bimbling through local markets without any particular plan.
They bimbled along the country lanes, chatting and taking in the scenery.

Synset Examples

We love to bimble along the beach during sunset.
Let's bimble around town and see what we find.
They decided to bimble through the park after lunch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amble - to walk at a slow, relaxed pace
