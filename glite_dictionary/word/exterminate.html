to exterminate

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈstɝː.məˌneɪt / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɜː.mɪ.neɪt / (BrE)
to destroy or get rid of completely; to eliminate all traces of something, often by deliberate action
destroy or get rid of completely

Examples

The government launched a campaign to exterminate the invasive species from the local ecosystem.
During the pest control operation, experts managed to exterminate nearly all the rodents in the warehouse.
His goal was to exterminate every trace of corruption from the organization.

Synset Examples

the vestiges of political democracy were soon uprooted
root out corruption

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to eradicate - to destroy or remove completely

Etymology

From Latin exterminare (drive out, banish) via Latin exterminatus, past participle of exterminare.

2. VERB

/ ɪkˈstɝːməˌneɪt / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɜːmɪneɪt / (BrE)
to kill or destroy all of a group of people, animals, or things, especially deliberately and systematically
to kill or destroy completely

Examples

The government launched an initiative to exterminate invasive species threatening the ecosystem.
The regime sought to exterminate entire communities during the conflict.
Pest control companies are often called to exterminate termites from homes.

Synset Examples

Hitler wanted to exterminate the Jews, Gypsies, Communists, and homosexuals of Europe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to eradicate - to destroy or get rid of something completely
  • Antonym: preserve - to keep something safe from harm or destruction

Etymology

From Latin exterminare (to drive out, banish).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
