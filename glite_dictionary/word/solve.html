to solve

1. VERB

/ sɑlv / (AmE), / sɒlv / (BrE)
to find the answer to a problem or question, especially by thinking or calculating
find an answer to a problem

Examples

She worked all night to solve the complicated math problem before the exam.
The detective was able to solve the mysterious case after finding a hidden clue.
Can you solve this puzzle faster than anyone else in your class?

Synset Examples

solve an equation
solve for x

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resolve - find a solution to a problem
  • Synonym: fix - repair or find a solution to something
  • Antonym: break - cause something to stop working or be unsolved

Etymology

From Latin 'solvere' (to loosen, unfasten, solve).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00735067-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ sɑlv / (AmE), / sɒlv / (BrE)
(formal or dated) To settle or discharge (a debt or obligation).
to settle a debt
archaic

Examples

The company was finally able to solve all its outstanding debts.
He worked hard for years to solve the old family loan.
It took a generous benefactor to solve their financial obligations.

Synset Examples

clear a debt
solve an old debt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pay off - to completely pay a debt
  • Synonym: discharge - to settle a debt or obligation

Etymology

From Latin solvere (to loosen, pay).

3. VERB

/ sɑlv / (AmE), / sɒlv / (BrE)
to find an answer to a problem or question; to understand or explain something that is difficult or puzzling
find an answer to a problem or question

Examples

She was able to solve the difficult math equation after several attempts.
Detectives are still trying to solve the mystery behind the disappearance.
We need to solve this problem before it gets any worse.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resolve - to find a solution to a problem, dispute, or difficulty
  • Synonym: answer - a response or solution to a question or problem
  • Antonym: unsolved - not having been solved
  • Synonym: to explain - to make something clear or easy to understand

Etymology

From Latin 'solvere' (to loosen, release, solve).
