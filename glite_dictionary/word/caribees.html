Caribees

1. NOUN

/ ˈkærɪˌbiːz / (AmE), / ˈkærɪˌbiːz / (BrE)
a group of islands in the southeastern West Indies, also known as the Caribbean Islands
group of islands in the southeastern West Indies

Examples

The Caribees attract tourists from around the world with their beautiful beaches and vibrant culture.
During the colonial era, the Caribees were highly prized for their sugar plantations.
She studied the history and ecology of the Caribees for her dissertation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Caribbean - region consisting of the Caribbean Sea, its islands, and the surrounding coasts

Etymology

From 'Carib', the name of the indigenous people of the region, plus plural -ees.
