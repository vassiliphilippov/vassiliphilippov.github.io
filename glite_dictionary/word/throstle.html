throstle

1. NOUN

/ ˈθrɑsəl / (AmE), / ˈθrɒsəl / (BrE)
a spinning machine once used for twisting and winding fibers of cotton or wool continuously
old textile spinning machine

Examples

The factory once relied on the throstle for spinning cotton threads efficiently.
A restored throstle is now displayed in the local textile museum.
Workers monitored each throstle closely to prevent thread breakages.

Etymology

Transferred sense from 'throstle' (old term for song thrush), comparing the machine's sound to the bird's song.

2. NOUN

/ ˈθrɑːsəl / (AmE), / ˈθrɒsəl / (BrE)
A common Old World songbird, the song thrush, known for its melodious song.
Old World song thrush
archaic

Examples

In the quiet woodland, a throstle sang sweetly atop a mossy branch.
Many poets have written about the beauty of the throstle's song in springtime.
The garden was filled with the melodious notes of a throstle at dawn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: song thrush - A common European bird, Turdus philomelos, known for its song

Etymology

From Old English *þrostle*, related to German *Drossel* (thrush).
