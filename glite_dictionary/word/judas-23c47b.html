judas

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːdəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːdəs / (BrE)
a small opening or device in a door, usually fitted with a lens, that allows one to look through from one side only, especially for security purposes
one-way peephole in a door

Examples

He checked who was at the door through the judas before opening it.
The hotel room door was equipped with a judas for added security.
She peered through the judas to see if the corridor was empty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peephole - a small hole or lens in a door through which one can look

Etymology

Named after Judas Iscariot, alluding to betrayal and secrecy; originally from Latin 'Iudas', from Greek 'Ioudas', from Hebrew 'Yehudah'.
