bluebonnet

1. NOUN

/ ˈbluˌbɑː.nət / (AmE), / ˈbluːˌbɒn.ɪt / (BrE)
a brimless dark blue Scottish cap with a flat top, often adorned with a plume on one side
brimless flat Scottish cap

Examples

The soldier wore a traditional bluebonnet as part of his Scottish regimental uniform.
A bluebonnet with a bright red plume caught everyone’s attention at the parade.
My grandfather’s old bluebonnet is displayed proudly in our family home.

Etymology

From the color blue and 'bonnet', referring to the cap’s typical color and shape.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbluːˌbɑː.nət / (AmE), / ˈbluːˌbɒn.ɪt / (BrE)
A low-growing annual plant of the southwestern United States, especially Texas, with silky foliage and blue flowers; known to be toxic to livestock.
plant with blue flowers, Texas

Examples

In the spring, bluebonnets cover the Texas hillsides in vibrant color.
Farmers warn that grazing cattle may be poisoned by eating bluebonnet plants.
The bluebonnet is celebrated as the state flower of Texas.

Etymology

Named for the blue color of its flowers and the resemblance of the flower's shape to a bonnet.
