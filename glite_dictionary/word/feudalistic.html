feudalistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌfjuːdəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌfjuːdəˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
Relating to, characteristic of, or resembling feudalism.
pertaining to feudalism

Examples

The country maintained a feudalistic social structure well into the nineteenth century.
Some critics argue that the corporate hierarchy is almost feudalistic in its power dynamics.
The novel vividly depicts the feudalistic customs of medieval Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feudal - relating to or characteristic of the feudal system
  • Antonym: modern - characteristic of the present or recent times

Etymology

From 'feudalism' + '-istic', ultimately from Medieval Latin 'feudalis' (holding of a fief).
