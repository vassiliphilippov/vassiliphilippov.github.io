nigh

1. ADJ

/ naɪ / (AmE), / naɪ / (BrE)
(in equestrian and agricultural contexts) on or relating to the left side of a team or pair of horses or oxen.
on the left side (team of horses/oxen)
archaic

Examples

In traditional ploughing, the nigh horse was always placed on the left.
The farmer kept the nigh ox closer to the furrow for better control.
Instructions said to hitch the nigh animal on the left, and the off animal on the right.

Synset Examples

the near or nigh horse is the one on the left
the animal's left side is its near or nigh side

Related Terms

  • Antonym: off - on the right side (of a pair of horses or oxen)

Etymology

From Old English nēah (near).

2. ADJ

/ naɪ / (AmE), / naɪ / (BrE)
(literary) not far away in time, space, degree, or circumstances; near or almost.
not far; near in space or time
archaic

Examples

The end of the year draws nigh, and everyone is preparing for the celebrations.
It was nigh impossible to see through the heavy fog that morning.
With the enemy camp so nigh, the soldiers prepared for battle.
