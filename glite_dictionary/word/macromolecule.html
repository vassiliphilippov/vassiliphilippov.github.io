macromolecule

1. NOUN

/ ˌmækroʊˈmɑːlɪˌkjuːl / (AmE), / ˌmakrəʊˈmɒlɪkjuːl / (BrE)
a very large molecule composed of many smaller units, such as proteins, nucleic acids, or synthetic polymers
very large molecule, e.g., protein or DNA

Examples

Proteins and DNA are examples of macromolecules essential to all living things.
The study focused on the structure of a complex macromolecule found in plant cells.
Scientists use special techniques to analyze large biological macromolecules such as enzymes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polymer - a substance made of many repeating units

Etymology

From macro- (large) + molecule; adopted in scientific English in the early 20th century.
