intrepid

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈtrɛpɪd / (AmE), / ɪnˈtrɛpɪd / (BrE)
fearless and adventurous, showing no fear in dangerous or difficult situations
fearless and adventurous

Examples

The intrepid reporter traveled to the war zone to cover the unfolding events.
Despite the storm, the intrepid sailors pressed on toward their destination.
She proved herself an intrepid leader during the crisis, inspiring confidence in her team.

Synset Examples

audacious explorers
fearless reporters and photographers
intrepid pioneers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fearless - lacking fear; very brave
  • Antonym: timid - showing a lack of courage or confidence; easily frightened

Etymology

From Latin 'intrepidus': in- (not) + trepidus (alarmed).
