Aggeus

1. PROPN

/ əˈdʒiːəs / (AmE), / əˈdʒiːəs / (BrE)
an Old Testament book that records the prophecies of Haggai, mainly concerned with the rebuilding of the Temple after the Babylonian Captivity
Old Testament book of Haggai's prophecies

Examples

Many older Bibles refer to the Book of Haggai as Aggeus.
The sermons focused on messages from Aggeus about rebuilding the Temple.
She studied the prophecies found in Aggeus during her theology course.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Haggai - Old Testament book by the prophet Haggai

Etymology

From Latin 'Aggeus', from Greek 'Aggaios', from Hebrew 'Ḥaggai'.

2. PROPN

/ əgˈdʒiː.əs / (AmE), / agˈdʒiː.əs / (BrE)
A Hebrew minor prophet, considered the first of three post-exile prophets after the Neo-Babylonian Exile of Judah; more commonly known as Haggai.
biblical minor prophet, also called Haggai

Examples

The book of Aggeus is found among the Minor Prophets in some versions of the Bible.
Scholars discuss how Aggeus encouraged the rebuilding of the Temple in Jerusalem.
Few people recognize Aggeus by this name; he is often called Haggai.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Haggai - another name for Aggeus, Hebrew biblical prophet

Etymology

From Latin Aggeus, from Greek Aggaios, from Hebrew Ḥaggay (Haggai).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-11041912-n
Synset POS: n
