Rutherford B. Hayes

1. PROPN

/ ˈrʌðɚfɝd biː ˈheɪz / (AmE), / ˈrʌðəfəd biː ˈheɪz / (BrE)
(1822–1893) the 19th President of the United States, known for ending the Reconstruction Era by withdrawing federal troops from the South.
19th U.S. President, ended Reconstruction

Examples

Rutherford B. Hayes served as the 19th President of the United States from 1877 to 1881.
Historians often debate the impact of Rutherford B. Hayes's decision to end Reconstruction.
The election of Rutherford B. Hayes was one of the most controversial in American history.
