wily

1. ADJ

/ ˈwaɪli / (AmE), / ˈwaɪli / (BrE)
Skilled at gaining an advantage, especially by using clever or deceptive methods.
clever and cunning, often deceitful

Examples

The wily fox managed to slip past the farmer every night.
She gave a wily smile when asked about her next move in the game.
The wily negotiator secured the best possible deal for his client.

Synset Examples

cunning men often pass for wise
a foxy scheme
a slick evasive answer
sly as a fox
tricky Dick
a wily old attorney

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cunning - skilled at achieving goals by deceit or evasion
  • Synonym: sly - having or showing a cunning and deceitful nature
  • Antonym: honest - free of deceit and untruthfulness; sincere
  • Antonym: naive - showing a lack of experience or sophistication; innocent

Etymology

From Old English wīl (trick, craft) + -y.
