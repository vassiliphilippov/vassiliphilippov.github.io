unrefined

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnrɪˈfaɪnd / (AmE), / ˌʌnrɪˈfaɪnd / (BrE)
(of a substance) in its natural or raw state; not having been processed or purified.
not processed or purified

Examples

The factory receives unrefined sugar and processes it into white granules.
Many health enthusiasts prefer unrefined grains over processed ones.
The unrefined oil must undergo several treatments before it can be used as fuel.

Synset Examples

unrefined ore
crude oil

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crude - in a natural or raw state; not yet processed or refined
  • Antonym: refined - having been purified or processed

Etymology

From un- (not) + refined, from refine (to make pure).

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnrɪˈfaɪnd / (AmE), / ˌʌnrɪˈfaɪnd / (BrE)
(of a person or their behavior) lacking refinement, good manners, or sophistication; uncouth.
lacking manners or sophistication

Examples

Despite his wealth, his manners remained unrefined and abrasive.
Her unrefined comments made others uncomfortable at the formal dinner.
The movie portrays the hero as an unrefined but good-hearted man.

Synset Examples

how can a refined girl be drawn to such an unrefined man?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: refined - having or showing good taste, manners, or sophistication
  • Synonym: uncouth - lacking good manners or refinement

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' to 'refined', meaning not refined.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01955971-a
Synset POS: a
