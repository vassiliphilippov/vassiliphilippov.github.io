lipoidaemia

1. NOUN

/ lɪˌpɔɪdəˈmiə / (AmE), / lɪˌpɔɪdiˈmiːə / (BrE)
A medical condition in which there is an excess of lipids in the blood.
excess lipids in the blood (medical)
medical british

Examples

The doctor diagnosed him with lipoidaemia after routine blood tests showed elevated cholesterol.
Treatment for lipoidaemia often includes dietary changes and medication.
Unchecked lipoidaemia can increase the risk of cardiovascular disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyperlipidemia - abnormally high concentration of fats or lipids in the blood

Etymology

From Greek 'lipos' (fat) + New Latin '-aemia' (blood condition).
