Prionace glauca

1. NOUN

/ praɪ.əˈneɪs ˈɡlaʊ.kə / (AmE), / praɪ.əˈneɪs ˈɡlaʊ.kə / (BrE)
A species of slender, pelagic shark with a blue body that shades to a white belly, found in oceans worldwide and potentially dangerous to humans.
species of blue shark

Examples

The Prionace glauca is often seen in the open oceans of temperate and tropical waters.
Researchers observed a group of Prionace glauca hunting near the surface at dusk.
Due to its widespread distribution, Prionace glauca plays a crucial role in marine ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue shark - a common name for Prionace glauca

Etymology

From Latin roots: Prionace (genus name) and glauca meaning 'bluish-gray'.
