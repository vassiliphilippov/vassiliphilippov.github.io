Canna

1. NOUN

/ ˈkænə / (AmE), / ˈkanə / (BrE)
A genus of perennial, lily-like herbs native to the tropical and subtropical Americas, known for their large leaves and brightly colored flowers; the sole genus of the family Cannaceae.
genus of tropical, lily-like herbs

Examples

Many gardeners grow Canna for its showy flowers and lush foliage.
The Canna genus is the only member of the family Cannaceae.
After winter, the dormant Canna rhizomes begin to sprout again.

Etymology

From Latin *canna* meaning 'reed', originally from Greek *kánna* (reed, cane).
