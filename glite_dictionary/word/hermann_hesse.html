Hermann Hesse

1. PROPN

/ hɛrˌmɑːn ˈhɛsə / (AmE), / hɛˈmaːn ˈhɛsə / (BrE)
(1877–1962) A Swiss writer, born in Germany, known for novels and poems reflecting Eastern spiritual themes.
German-Swiss writer (Siddhartha, Steppenwolf)

Examples

Hermann Hesse won the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1946 for his influential works.
Many readers find Hermann Hesse's novels, like 'Siddhartha', deeply philosophical and inspiring.
The themes of self-discovery in Hermann Hesse's writing continue to resonate with new generations.
