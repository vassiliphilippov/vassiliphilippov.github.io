Matricaria tchihatchewii

1. NOUN

/ ˌmætrɪˈkɛriə tʃɪhɪtʃəˈwiː.i / (AmE), / ˌmætrɪˈkɛːriə tʃɪhɪtʃəˈwiː.i / (BrE)
A low, densely tufted perennial herb native to Turkey, with small white flowers, sometimes used as a ground cover in dry areas.
herbaceous plant native to Turkey

Examples

Matricaria tchihatchewii is often planted in gardens for its delicate white flowers and low growth.
The dry slopes were covered in Matricaria tchihatchewii, providing a soft green carpet throughout the summer.
Botanists debated whether Matricaria tchihatchewii should be classified in its own genus or within Matricaria.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Matricaria - the genus comprising chamomile and related herbs
  • Synonym: chamomile - a related species used as an herbal remedy

Etymology

Genus name from Latin 'matricaria' (chamomile); species named after Russian botanist Tchihatchew.
