sugarcane

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌɡərˌkeɪn / (AmE), / ˈsʌɡəˌkeɪn / (BrE)
A tall tropical grass with thick, juicy stems from which sugar is extracted commercially.
tall plant yielding commercial sugar

Examples

The workers harvested the sugarcane fields under the hot tropical sun.
Fresh sugarcane juice is a popular drink in many countries.
The factory processes sugarcane into refined white sugar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sugar beet - plant whose root is another source of commercial sugar

Etymology

From 'sugar' + 'cane', after Spanish 'caña de azúcar'

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃuɡərˌkeɪn / (AmE), / ˈʃʊɡəˌkeɪn / (BrE)
A tall tropical grass with stout, jointed stems, cultivated for its sweet sap which is a major source of sugar.
tropical grass, source of sugar

Examples

Much of the world's refined sugar is made from sugarcane grown in tropical regions.
The farmers harvested the sugarcane early in the morning to avoid the midday heat.
Molasses is a byproduct obtained during the processing of sugarcane into sugar.

Etymology

From "sugar" + "cane"; "cane" from Old French "cane", from Latin "canna" (reed, cane).
