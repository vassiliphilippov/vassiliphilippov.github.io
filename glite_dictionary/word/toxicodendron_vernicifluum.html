Toxicodendron vernicifluum

1. NOUN

/ toʊkˌsɪkoʊˈdɛndrən vərˌnɪsɪˈfluəm / (AmE), / tɒkˌsɪkəʊˈdɛndrən vɜːˌnɪsɪˈfluːəm / (BrE)
a small Asian tree that produces a toxic sap used to make lacquer
tree used for lacquer; toxic sap

Examples

The resin of Toxicodendron vernicifluum is central to traditional Japanese lacquerware.
Contact with Toxicodendron vernicifluum can cause skin irritation due to its toxic sap.
Artisans carefully harvest the sap of Toxicodendron vernicifluum to produce high-quality lacquer.

Etymology

From Greek 'toxikos' (poison) and 'dendron' (tree), and Latin 'vernicifluum' (yielding varnish).
