rectifier

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛktɪˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈrɛktɪfaɪə / (BrE)
a person who corrects or sets things right
a person who corrects or makes right

Examples

She has always been a rectifier of injustices in her community.
The editor saw herself as a rectifier of factual errors in the article.
History remembers him as a rectifier of past wrongs.

Synset Examples

a rectifier of prejudices

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corrector - a person who corrects errors

Etymology

From Latin 'rectificare' (to make right), via French 'rectifier'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10532575-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛktəˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈrɛktɪfaɪə / (BrE)
an electrical device that converts alternating current (AC) into direct current (DC)
device converting AC to DC

Examples

The rectifier in the power supply converts AC from the wall into the DC needed by your devices.
When the rectifier failed, the entire electrical circuit stopped working correctly.
Modern LED lights have a built-in rectifier to ensure a steady flow of electricity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inverter - device converting DC to AC

Etymology

From Latin 'rectus' (right, straight) and English '-ifier' (one who makes).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04072744-n
Synset POS: n
