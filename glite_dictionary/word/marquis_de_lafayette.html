Marquis de Lafayette

1. PROPN

/ mɑrˌkiː də læfəˈjɛt / (AmE), / ˌmɑːkwiː də læfəˈjɛt / (BrE)
A French aristocrat and military officer who fought alongside American forces in the American Revolution (1757–1834).
French military leader in American Revolution

Examples

The Marquis de Lafayette played a crucial role in securing French support for the American colonies.
Historians often praise Marquis de Lafayette for his commitment to liberty and equal rights.
A statue of the Marquis de Lafayette stands in Lafayette Square in Washington, D.C.

Etymology

From French title 'marquis' (noble rank) and family name 'Lafayette'.
