Humulus japonicus

1. NOUN

/ ˈhjuːmjələs dʒəˈpoʊnɪkəs / (AmE), / ˈhjuːmjʊləs dʒæˈpɒnɪkəs / (BrE)
an ornamental climbing plant native to eastern Asia, cultivated for its variegated leaves
an ornamental vine native to eastern Asia

Examples

Humulus japonicus is often used in gardens for its striking foliage and fast growth.
Gardeners appreciate Humulus japonicus for its ability to cover trellises quickly.
Some people cultivate Humulus japonicus alongside other climbing plants for a varied look.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Japanese hop - common name for Humulus japonicus

Etymology

From Latin 'Humulus' (hop plant) and 'japonicus' (of Japan).
