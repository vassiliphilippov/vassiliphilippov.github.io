tumidness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuːmɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈtjuːmɪdnəs / (BrE)
the state of being swollen or enlarged, especially of an organ or part of the body
state of being swollen, especially in body parts
medical

Examples

The tumidness of her ankle was apparent after the fall.
Doctors examined the tumidness in the patient's abdomen for signs of infection.
Mild tumidness may occur as a reaction to the new medication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swelling - an abnormal enlargement of a body part or area
  • Synonym: tumefaction - the process or result of swelling

Etymology

From Latin tumidus (swollen), from tumēre (to swell) + -ness.
