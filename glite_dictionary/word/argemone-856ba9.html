Argemone

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈdʒɛməni / (AmE), / ɑːˈdʒɛməni / (BrE)
A genus of prickly poppies with showy flowers, native to the Americas and known for their spiny leaves and stems.
a genus of prickly poppies (plants)

Examples

The medicinal properties of Argemone have been studied for centuries.
Many species within the Argemone genus display bright yellow or white flowers.
Argemone plants often grow in arid environments and have distinctive prickly leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poppy - a plant with brightly colored flowers, from the family Papaveraceae

Etymology

From Greek argemōnē, a name used for a poppy.
