Lepidothamnus

1. NOUN

/ lɛpɪdoʊˈθæmnəs / (AmE), / lɛpɪdəʊˈθamnəs / (BrE)
A small genus of usually shrubby conifers native to New Zealand and southern South America.
genus of shrubby conifers

Examples

The Lepidothamnus species are commonly found in the forests of New Zealand.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Lepidothamnus under different climate conditions.
The shrubby appearance of Lepidothamnus distinguishes it from other conifers in the region.

Etymology

From Greek lepid- (scale) + thamnos (shrub), referring to the plant's scale-like leaves.

2. NOUN

/ ˌlɛpɪdoʊˈθæmnəs / (AmE), / ˌlɛpɪdəʊˈθamnəs / (BrE)
A small genus of usually shrubby coniferous trees native to New Zealand and southern South America.
genus of shrubby coniferous trees

Examples

The Lepidothamnus genus includes rare species of conifer found in New Zealand's wetlands.
Scientists study Lepidothamnus to understand plant adaptation in cool, moist climates.
One characteristic of Lepidothamnus is its small, scaly leaves typical of the genus.

Etymology

From Greek 'lepis' (scale) and 'thamnos' (shrub), naming its scaly and shrubby form.
