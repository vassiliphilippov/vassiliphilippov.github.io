Mastodon

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæs.təˌdɑn / (AmE), / ˈmæs.təˌdɒn / (BrE)
A genus of extinct large, elephant-like mammals of the family Mammutidae, known commonly as mastodons, that lived from the Miocene to the Pleistocene epochs.
genus of extinct elephant-like mammals

Examples

Fossils of the Mastodon have been found throughout North America.
Scientists believe the Mastodon went extinct about 10,000 years ago.
The structure of a Mastodon's teeth is distinct from that of a mammoth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mammoth - another extinct large proboscidean closely related to elephants
  • Synonym: elephant - modern large proboscidean mammal

Etymology

From Greek mastos (breast) + odous (tooth), referring to the nipple-like projections on the animal's molar teeth.
