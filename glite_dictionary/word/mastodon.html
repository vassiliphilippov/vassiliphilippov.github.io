mastodon

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæs.təˌdɑn / (AmE), / ˈmæs.təˌdɒn / (BrE)
an extinct large, elephant-like mammal that lived from the Miocene to Pleistocene epochs, distinguished from mammoths by the shape of its molar teeth
extinct elephant-like mammal

Examples

A fossilized mastodon skeleton was unearthed in the remote valley.
Unlike the mammoth, the mastodon had smaller, more pointed molars.
Scientists believe that the mastodon disappeared about 10,000 years ago.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mammoth - a large extinct elephant species with long curved tusks, closely related to the mastodon

Etymology

From Greek 'mastós' (breast) + 'odous' (tooth), referring to the nipple-shaped cusps on the animal's molar teeth.
