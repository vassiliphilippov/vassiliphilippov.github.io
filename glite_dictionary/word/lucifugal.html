lucifugal

1. ADJ

/ luːˈsɪfjəɡəl / (AmE), / luːˈsɪfjʊɡəl / (BrE)
(of a living organism) avoiding or tending to avoid light.
avoiding light

Examples

Some nocturnal animals are lucifugal and hide during the day to avoid sunlight.
The lucifugal behavior of these insects makes them difficult to study in daylight.
Scientists observed that the fungus displayed lucifugal growth, spreading only in dark environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lucifugous - avoiding light; tending to shun light
  • Synonym: photophobic - having an aversion to light; shunning light

Etymology

From Latin 'lux' (light) + '-fugus' (fleeing), meaning 'light-fleeing'.
