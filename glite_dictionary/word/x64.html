x64

1. NOUN

/ ɛks ˌsɪksti ˈfɔr / (AmE), / ɛks ˌsɪksti ˈfɔː / (BrE)
the 64-bit version of the x86 computer architecture used in many modern processors and operating systems
64-bit x86 computer architecture

Examples

Most modern laptops are powered by x64 processors capable of running 64-bit operating systems.
The software is available in both x86 and x64 versions for compatibility with different hardware.
When installing Windows, make sure your computer supports x64 architecture.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: x86 - the family of instruction set architectures based on Intel's 8086 CPU

Etymology

Formed from 'x' (as in x86) and '64' indicating 64-bit architecture.
