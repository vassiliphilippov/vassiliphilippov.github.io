variolic

1. ADJ

/ ˌvɛəriˈɑlɪk / (AmE), / ˌværɪˈɒlɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of smallpox
relating to smallpox
medical

Examples

The doctor recognized the variolic scars on the patient’s face as evidence of a past smallpox infection.
A variolic rash is considered characteristic in the diagnosis of smallpox.
Historical medical reports often described the spread of variolic disease among city populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: variola - the virus that causes smallpox; smallpox itself

Etymology

From Latin variola (smallpox).
