enrichment

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈrɪtʃ.mənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈrɪtʃ.mənt / (BrE)
the act of improving the quality or value of something, especially by making it more meaningful, rewarding, or substantial
making something more valuable or rewarding

Examples

The new program focuses on the enrichment of classroom experiences for young learners.
Travel can offer cultural enrichment that broadens one's perspective.
The museum aims to provide enrichment through educational exhibits and workshops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: improvement - the act of making something better

Etymology

From French *enrichissement*, from *enrichir* (to enrich) + -ment (noun suffix), ultimately from Latin *in-* + *ricus* (rich).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈrɪtʃmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈrɪtʃmənt / (BrE)
a gift or donation that substantially increases the wealth of the recipient
gift that increases someone's wealth

Examples

The sudden enrichment of the charity allowed it to expand its programs nationwide.
She viewed the inheritance as an unexpected enrichment that changed her life.
Legal disputes often arise when families argue over the enrichment of one member through generous gifts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: windfall - an unexpected gain or piece of good fortune, often monetary

Etymology

From Old French enrichir (make rich), from en- (make) + riche (rich).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13292521-n
Synset POS: n
