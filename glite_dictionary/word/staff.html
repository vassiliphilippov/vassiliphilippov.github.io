staff

1. NOUN

/ stæf / (AmE), / stɑːf / (BrE)
the group of teachers and administrators working at a school or educational institution
teachers and administrators at a school

Examples

The school staff gathered for a meeting to discuss new policies.
She has worked on the university staff for over a decade.
The principal praised the dedication of the staff during the challenging school year.

Synset Examples

the dean addressed the letter to the entire staff of the university

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faculty - the teaching staff at a university or college

Etymology

From Old English stæf (stick, rod), later extended to mean a supporting group of workers.

Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ stæf / (AmE), / stɑːf / (BrE)
to provide an organization, office, or place with employees or workers needed for a particular job or function.
provide with employees or workers

Examples

The hospital is well staffed with experienced nurses and doctors.
During the holidays, extra workers are staffed at the customer service desk.
Sometimes, smaller branches are staffed by only one or two people.

Synset Examples

This position is not always staffed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: employ - to hire someone for a job
  • Antonym: to vacate - to leave a position unfilled or empty

3. NOUN

/ stæf / (AmE), / stɑːf / (BrE)
(music) A set of five horizontal lines and four spaces used in musical notation to represent different musical pitches.
lines for writing musical notes

Examples

The notes were written on the staff using both treble and bass clefs.
Each line and space on the staff indicates a different musical pitch.
As a beginner, she practiced drawing a staff before writing any notes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stave - Alternate term for 'staff' in musical contexts

5. NOUN

/ stæf / (AmE), / stɑːf / (BrE)
a long rod or stick carried as a symbol of authority, office, or ceremony
rod symbolizing authority

Examples

The king entered the hall holding his staff as a sign of office.
During the ceremony, the leader raised the staff to silence the crowd.
The bishop carried a jeweled staff symbolizing his position.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scepter - a decorated rod carried by a sovereign as a symbol of authority

Etymology

From Old English stæf (stick, rod, staff), related to Old High German stab, Old Norse stafr.

7. NOUN

/ stæf / (AmE), / stɑːf / (BrE)
the group of people employed by an organization or working for a particular person, especially those who assist in carrying out assigned duties
group of employees or assistants

Examples

The staff at the hotel were extremely helpful throughout our stay.
After the restructuring, many new staff were hired to improve efficiency.
Teachers rely on their support staff to help manage the school.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crew - a group of people who work together, especially on a ship or aircraft
  • Synonym: personnel - the people who work for an organization

Etymology

From Old French 'estaff', from Old High German 'stapfa' (staff, stick); figurative sense of 'a supporting group' developed later.

/ stæf / (AmE), / stɑːf / (BrE)
A building material made from plaster and hair, used for covering external surfaces of temporary structures or for decorative purposes.
plaster and hair building material

Examples

The temporary pavilion was constructed using staff for both the walls and columns.
During the exposition, ornate statues made of staff adorned the entrance to the main hall.
Architects chose staff because it was lightweight and easy to mold into intricate designs.

Etymology

From French *staff* (stucco), possibly of Germanic origin.
