powdery

1. ADJ

/ ˈpaʊdəri / (AmE), / ˈpaʊdəri / (BrE)
consisting of or resembling fine, loose particles like powder
consisting of fine particles

Examples

The freshly fallen snow had a powdery texture, perfect for skiing.
She dusted the cake with powdery sugar before serving it.
His hands were covered in a powdery substance after working with chalk.

Synset Examples

powdered cellulose
powdery snow
pulverized sugar is prepared from granulated sugar by grinding

Related Terms

  • Antonym: granular - consisting of small grains or particles

Etymology

Formed from 'powder' + '-y', meaning 'like powder'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈpaʊdəri / (AmE), / ˈpaʊdəri / (BrE)
(of a substance, surface, or color) having a soft, fine, dry texture or appearance like that of powder.
having a powder-like texture or appearance

Examples

The artist chose a powdery blue for the mural, giving it a soft and delicate look.
After walking through the old attic, his clothes were covered in a powdery layer of dust.
The makeup has a powdery finish that leaves the skin looking smooth and matte.

Synset Examples

a powdery blue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dusty - covered with or resembling dust

Etymology

From 'powder' + '-y', forming adjectives meaning 'like powder'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00410396-s
Synset POS: s
