Hezekiah

1. PROPN

/ ˌhɛzɪˈkaɪə / (AmE), / ˌhɛzɪˈkaɪə / (BrE)
(in the Bible) a king of Judah who ruled from 715 to 687 BC and is noted for abolishing idolatry.
king of Judah in the Old Testament

Examples

Many biblical scholars have studied the reign of Hezekiah and his religious reforms.
The Assyrian invasion during Hezekiah's reign is a major event in Old Testament history.
A statue of Hezekiah stands in the museum's exhibit on ancient kings.

Etymology

From Hebrew Ḥizqiyyāh, meaning 'Yahweh strengthens'.
