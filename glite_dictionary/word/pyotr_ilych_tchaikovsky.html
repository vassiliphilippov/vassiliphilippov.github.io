Pyotr Ilych Tchaikovsky

1. PROPN

/ piˈɔtər ˈil.jɪtʃ tʃaɪˈkɑfski / (AmE), / ˈpjɔːtə ɪˈlɪtʃ tʃaɪˈkɒfski / (BrE)
a Russian composer (1840–1893) renowned for his expressive melodies and major works, including ballets, symphonies, and concertos
Russian composer (1840–1893)

Examples

Many orchestras perform works by Pyotr Ilych Tchaikovsky every year, especially his famous ballets.
As a child, she was inspired after hearing Pyotr Ilych Tchaikovsky's "Swan Lake" for the first time.
The influence of Pyotr Ilych Tchaikovsky on later composers is still studied in music schools today.

Etymology

Transliteration of the Russian name Пётр Ильи́ч Чайко́вский (Pyotr Ilyich Chaykovsky).
