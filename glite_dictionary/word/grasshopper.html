grasshopper

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡræsˌhɑpər / (AmE), / ˈɡrɑːsˌhɒpə / (BrE)
a plant-eating insect with long hind legs adapted for jumping, typically having a green or brown body and found on grass or in fields
a jumping plant-eating insect

Examples

A grasshopper landed on my arm while I was sitting in the garden.
Farmers often see grasshoppers eating the leaves of their crops in the summer.
The children tried to catch a grasshopper as it leaped through the tall grass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: locust - a type of grasshopper that forms large swarms and can cause crop damage

Etymology

From Old English *græshoppa*, literally 'grass' + 'hopper', referring to its habitat and jumping behavior.
