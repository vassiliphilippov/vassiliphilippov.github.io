Purcell

1. PROPN

/ pərˈsɛl / (AmE), / pəˈsɛl / (BrE)
Henry Purcell (1659–1695), an English composer and organist known for his influential sacred, instrumental, and theatrical music.
English Baroque composer (1659–1695)

Examples

Henry Purcell composed some of the most celebrated music of the English Baroque period.
The choir performed an anthem by Purcell at the cathedral service.
Many musicologists consider Purcell to be England's greatest native composer.
