ANOVA

1. NOUN

/ əˈnoʊ.və / (AmE), / əˈnəʊ.və / (BrE)
a statistical method used to compare the means of two or more groups to determine if there are significant differences among them
statistical method for comparing group means
medical

Examples

The researcher used ANOVA to analyze the differences in test scores across the three groups.
After running the ANOVA, they found a significant effect of diet on weight loss.
A two-way ANOVA was performed to examine the interaction between age and gender.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: analysis of variance - the full term for ANOVA, same statistical method
  • Degree Lower: t-test - statistical test for comparing two means

Etymology

Acronym for 'Analysis of Variance'.
