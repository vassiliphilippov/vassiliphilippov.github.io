arrowsmith

1. NOUN

/ ˈæroʊˌsmɪθ / (AmE), / ˈarəʊˌsmɪθ / (BrE)
a person who makes arrows, especially as a craft or trade
person who makes arrows
archaic

Examples

The village arrowsmith supplied weapons to the archers before each battle.
As an arrowsmith, he took great pride in crafting perfectly balanced arrows.
In medieval times, the arrowsmith worked alongside the blacksmith and the bowyer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fletcher - a person who makes arrows, especially the feathering part

Etymology

Formed from 'arrow' + 'smith', following the pattern of occupational surnames.
