Alces alces

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.siːz ˈæl.siːz / (AmE), / ˈæl.siːz ˈæl.siːz / (BrE)
the scientific name for a large northern deer species with broad, flattened antlers in males; known as the moose in North America and the elk in Europe
scientific name for moose/European elk

Examples

The Alces alces is commonly found in boreal forests and northern regions.
Male Alces alces have distinctive broad, palmate antlers that they shed each year.
Researchers observed several Alces alces grazing near the edge of the wetlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moose - large North American deer with broad antlers
  • Synonym: elk - the term for this species in Europe, but a different animal in North America

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Alces' referred to an elk or moose, adapted from ancient Greek 'álkē' (elk).
