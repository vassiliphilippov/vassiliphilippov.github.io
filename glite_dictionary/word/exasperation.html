exasperation

1. NOUN

/ ɪɡˌzæspəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪɡˌzɑːspəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
A feeling of intense irritation or annoyance.
feeling of irritation or annoyance

Examples

She let out a sigh of exasperation when the computer froze again.
His repeated lateness was starting to cause her considerable exasperation.
With growing exasperation, the teacher addressed the noisy class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annoyance - A feeling of being irritated or bothered
  • Antonym: calm - A state of being peaceful, not annoyed

Etymology

From Latin exasperatio (irritation), from exasperare (to irritate).

2. NOUN

/ ɪɡˌzæspəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪɡˌzɑːspəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
actions or behavior that cause intense irritation or annoyance; the feeling of being extremely irritated or exasperated.
actions causing great irritation; intense annoyance

Examples

After several delays, his exasperation with the service grew obvious.
She slammed the door in exasperation after another disagreement with her brother.
The constant interruptions were a source of exasperation for the teacher.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritation - a feeling of being annoyed or provoked
  • Antonym: calm - a state of tranquility, free from agitation or strong emotion

Etymology

From Latin exasperatio (irritation), from exasperare (to make rough, irritate).
