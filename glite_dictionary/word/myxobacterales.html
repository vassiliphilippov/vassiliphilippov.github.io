Myxobacterales

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪksoʊˌbæktəˈreɪliːz / (AmE), / ˌmɪksəʊˌbæktəˈreɪliːz / (BrE)
An order of higher bacteria known for their complex life cycles and the production of fruiting bodies.
order of higher bacteria

Examples

Scientists study Myxobacterales to better understand the diversity and evolution of bacteria.
Members of Myxobacterales often exhibit coordinated movement and form complex fruiting bodies.
Soil samples revealed several new species belonging to the Myxobacterales order.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'myxo-' (slime) and 'bacter' (bacterium), with the taxonomic suffix '-ales'.
