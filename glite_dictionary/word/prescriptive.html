prescriptive

1. ADJ

/ prɪˈskrɪptɪv / (AmE), / prɪˈskrɪptɪv / (BrE)
relating to or laying down rules, directions, or norms; imposing standards or requirements for correct practice or behavior.
giving rules or setting standards

Examples

The teacher used a prescriptive approach when enforcing classroom rules.
Linguists often debate the merits of prescriptive versus descriptive grammar.
The organization's prescriptive guidelines specify exactly how staff should manage reports.

Synset Examples

prescriptive grammar is concerned with norms of or rules for correct usage

Related Terms

  • Antonym: descriptive - relating to describing, rather than prescribing, rules or norms

Etymology

From Medieval Latin praescriptivus, from Latin praescribere (to prescribe).
