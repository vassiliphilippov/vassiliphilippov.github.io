Alauda arvensis

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɔdə ɑrˈvɛnsɪs / (AmE), / əˈlɔːdə ɑːˈvɛnsɪs / (BrE)
a brown-speckled European lark known for singing melodiously while hovering high in the sky; commonly called the skylark
European skylark species

Examples

We listened to the Alauda arvensis singing as it hovered over the fields.
The Alauda arvensis is admired for its melodic flight song in European literature.
Farmers welcome the presence of Alauda arvensis as a sign of healthy grasslands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skylark - a common name for Alauda arvensis, a songbird found in Europe and Asia

Etymology

From Latin: genus name Alauda (lark) and species arvensis (of the field).
