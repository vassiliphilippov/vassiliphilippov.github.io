slangy

1. ADJ

/ ˈslæŋi / (AmE), / ˈslaŋi / (BrE)
(of language or expression) containing, characteristic of, or resembling slang; given to the use of slang.
containing or resembling slang

Examples

His jokes were filled with slangy phrases that only teenagers understood.
The dialogue in the novel is fast-paced and slangy, giving it an authentic feel.
She avoided slangy language during her presentation to sound more professional.

Synset Examples

a slangy expression
slangy speech

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colloquial - (of language) used in ordinary or familiar conversation; not formal or literary
  • Antonym: formal - following established conventions and not using colloquial or slang expressions

Etymology

From 'slang' + '-y', forming adjectives meaning 'full of or characterized by'.
