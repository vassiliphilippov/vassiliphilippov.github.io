nylon

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪˌlɑn / (AmE), / ˈnaɪlɒn / (BrE)
A strong, lightweight synthetic material made from aliphatic polyamides, used especially for making fibers, textiles, and plastics.
synthetic fabric or material

Examples

The jacket is made from waterproof nylon ideal for rainy weather.
She wore a pair of nylon stockings to the party.
Manufacturers often choose nylon for its durability and strength.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polyester - a category of synthetic polymers used as a fabric or plastic

Etymology

Coined in 1938 by DuPont; possibly derived from 'New York' and 'London,' though this is disputed.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪ.lɑn / (AmE), / ˈnaɪ.lɒn / (BrE)
A strong, resilient synthetic material made from polyamide, widely used in fabrics and plastics.
synthetic polyamide material/fiber

Examples

She wore a jacket made from nylon to keep dry during the rain.
The tent is constructed from lightweight nylon fabric.
Production of nylon revolutionized the textile industry in the twentieth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polyester - a category of synthetic polymers used as fibers and plastics
  • Antonym: wool - natural fiber from sheep, often used for clothing

Etymology

Coined in 1938 by DuPont; possibly arbitrary or partly from 'New York' and 'London', or from 'nyl' and 'on' (typical fiber suffix).
