Pedilanthus pavonis

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛdɪˈlænθəs pəˈvoʊnɪs / (AmE), / ˌpɛdɪˈlanθəs pəˈvəʊnɪs / (BrE)
A species of wax-coated shrub native to Mexico, belonging to the spurge family (Euphorbiaceae) and related to Euphorbia antisyphilitica.
wax-coated Mexican shrub species

Examples

The Pedilanthus pavonis in our garden attracts many butterflies with its striking flowers.
Botanists study Pedilanthus pavonis for its potential medicinal properties and unique waxy coating.
In arid landscapes, Pedilanthus pavonis is valued for its drought resistance and ornamental appeal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Euphorbia antisyphilitica - A related species of spurge also known for its waxy coating

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Pedilanthus' (from Greek 'pedilon' meaning 'slipper' and 'anthos' meaning 'flower') and species name 'pavonis,' Latin for 'of a peacock.'
