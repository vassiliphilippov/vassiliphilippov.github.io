threaded

1. ADJ

/ ˈθrɛdɪd / (AmE), / ˈθrɛdɪd / (BrE)
having one or more helical grooves cut or formed on the surface, as on bolts or screws, so they can be screwed into something
having screw threads (bolts, screws, etc)

Examples

The threaded end of the bolt must be properly aligned before you start tightening.
She used a threaded screw to attach the bracket securely to the wall.
Most modern pipes are threaded to make installation easier and reduce the chance of leaks.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unthreaded - not having screw threads

Etymology

From verb 'thread' + -ed.
