vigilantism

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɪdʒɪˈlæntɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌvɪdʒɪˈlæntɪzəm / (BrE)
the practice of taking the law into one's own hands and attempting to administer justice without legal authority
taking the law into one's own hands

Examples

Many believe that vigilantism undermines the authority of the police and the legal system.
The rise in vigilantism was a response to the community's frustration with crime.
Laws were enacted to prevent vigilantism after several violent incidents in the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vigilante - a person who takes the law into their own hands

Etymology

From 'vigilante' (Spanish for 'watchman', from Latin 'vigilans', present participle of 'vigilare' meaning 'to keep watch') plus '-ism'.
