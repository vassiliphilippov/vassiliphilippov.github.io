Lepisosteidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛpɪsɑːˈstiːɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌlɛpɪsɒˈstiːɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of primitive freshwater fishes, commonly known as gars, found in North and Central America.
family of fishes (gars)

Examples

The Lepisosteidae are known for their elongated bodies and sharp teeth.
Fossil records show that Lepisosteidae have existed since the Cretaceous period.
Several species within the Lepisosteidae family inhabit rivers and lakes in North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gar - a long, predatory freshwater fish of the family Lepisosteidae

Etymology

From the genus name Lepisosteus, from Greek lepis (scale) + osteon (bone).
