archdiocese

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrtʃˈdaɪəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌɑːtʃˈdaɪəsɪs / (BrE)
A district under the jurisdiction of an archbishop in certain Christian churches.
district under an archbishop's jurisdiction

Examples

The new archbishop was assigned to lead the archdiocese in the nation's capital.
Several parishes make up a single archdiocese, overseen by an archbishop.
Historic buildings in the archdiocese attract visitors from around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diocese - the district under the care of a bishop

Etymology

From Medieval Latin archidiœcēsis, from Greek arkhī- (chief) + diocese (district under a bishop).
