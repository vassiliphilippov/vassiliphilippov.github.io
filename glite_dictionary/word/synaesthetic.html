synaesthetic

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɪnəˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɪnɪsˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or involving synesthesia; characterized by the blending or association of two or more senses, such as perceiving colors when hearing music.
relating to synesthesia or mixed senses

Examples

The artist used synaesthetic techniques to create paintings that evoke the sensation of music.
Some people have a synaesthetic experience when they see colors while listening to certain sounds.
Her poetry has a synaesthetic quality, blending visual imagery with scents and musical tones.

Synset Examples

synesthetic response to music
synesthetic metaphor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: synesthetic - alternative spelling; relating to synesthesia
  • Antonym: monosensory - involving only one sense

Etymology

From Greek 'syn-' (together) + 'aisthēsis' (sensation).
