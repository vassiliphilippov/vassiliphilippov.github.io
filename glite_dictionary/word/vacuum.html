vacuum

1. VERB

/ ˈvæk.juːm / (AmE), / ˈvæk.juːm / (BrE)
to clean something, especially a floor or carpet, using a vacuum cleaner
clean using a vacuum cleaner

Examples

Could you vacuum the living room before our guests arrive?
She vacuumed the carpet thoroughly to remove all the pet hair.
After dinner, he spent an hour vacuuming every room in the house.

Synset Examples

vacuum the carpets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hoover - to clean with a vacuum cleaner (British English)

2. NOUN

/ ˈvæ.kjuːm / (AmE), / ˈvæk.juːm / (BrE)
an electrical appliance used to clean floors and carpets by sucking up dust and dirt
electrical cleaning appliance

Examples

She used the vacuum to clean the living room carpet.
Our old vacuum finally broke down after years of heavy use.
Don't forget to empty the dust bin after you use the vacuum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vacuum cleaner - an appliance that cleans by suction

3. NOUN

/ ˈvæk.juːm / (AmE), / ˈvæk.juːm / (BrE)
a space completely empty of matter, especially air
space empty of matter

Examples

Scientists created a vacuum in the chamber to test the behavior of objects without air resistance.
Outer space is often described as a near-perfect vacuum.
The experiment requires a complete vacuum for accurate results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: void - completely empty space
  • Antonym: atmosphere - the envelope of gases surrounding a planet

Etymology

From Latin 'vacuus' (empty), via Middle Latin 'vacuum' (empty space).

a space that is completely empty, especially one from which all air or other gas has been removed, or a situation in which something is missing or lacking

5. NOUN

/ ˈvæk.juːm / (AmE), / ˈvæk.juːm / (BrE)
a space or region that is completely empty of matter, especially air; absence of substance in a given area
space empty of matter

Examples

A science experiment was conducted in a vacuum to prevent air from affecting the results.
Deep space is considered the closest natural example to a perfect vacuum.
The bell jar created a partial vacuum when connected to the pump.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: void - an empty space, especially one that is completely empty of matter

Etymology

From Latin 'vacuum' (empty space), from 'vacuus' (empty).
