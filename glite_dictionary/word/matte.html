matte

1. VERB

/ mæt / (AmE), / mat / (BrE)
to change the texture of a material so that it becomes matted, dense, and felt-like, often as a result of moisture or friction
make or become matted and felt-like

Examples

If you wash this wool sweater on hot, it will matte and shrink.
The dog's fur began to matte after playing in the rain for hours.
Repeated washing can cause delicate fabrics to matte over time.

Synset Examples

The fabric felted up after several washes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: felt - to mat together fibers into a dense fabric

Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ mæt / (AmE), / mæt / (BrE)
A surface quality that lacks shine, gloss, or reflective highlights; a dull finish.
having a dull, non-glossy surface

Examples

The photograph was printed with a matte finish to reduce glare.
She preferred matte lipstick because it looked more natural than gloss.
The walls were painted in a matte light gray, giving the room a modern look.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: glossy - having a shiny or reflective surface
  • Degree Higher: satin - having a surface sheen between matte and glossy

Etymology

From French 'mat', meaning 'dull, not shiny'.

3. ADJ

/ mæt / (AmE), / mat / (BrE)
(of a surface or finish) not shiny; not reflecting light; having a dull or non-glossy appearance.
not shiny; dull finish

Examples

She painted her bedroom walls with a matte finish for a subtle look.
The artist preferred matte paper for her photographs to avoid glare.
The matte black car stood out among the shiny vehicles in the lot.

Synset Examples

flat wall paint
a photograph with a matte finish

Related Terms

  • Antonym: glossy - shiny and reflecting light

Etymology

From French mat (dull, not shiny), from Old French, of uncertain origin.

4. NOUN

/ mæt / (AmE), / mæt / (BrE)
a mixture of sulfides, especially of metals such as copper and iron, formed as an intermediate product when sulfide metal ores are smelted
mixture of sulfides from smelting ore

Examples

Molten matte collects below the slag during the copper smelting process.
The converter removes iron and sulfur from the matte to obtain pure metal.
After the first stage of smelting, a layer of matte separates from the rest of the material.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slag - the glass-like byproduct left over after the desired metal has been separated from its raw ore

Etymology

From French *matte* (a mass of metal sulfides), originally meaning 'dead' or 'dull'.
