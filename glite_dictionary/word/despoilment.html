despoilment

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈspɔɪlmənt / (AmE), / dɪˈspɔɪlmənt / (BrE)
the act of forcefully stripping or taking away something, especially property, valuables, or natural resources
forcible act of stripping or taking away

Examples

The despoilment of the ancient city left it in ruins for centuries.
Environmentalists warn that unchecked despoilment of natural resources will have dire consequences.
Historical records detail the despoilment of villages during invasions throughout the Middle Ages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plunder - the violent and dishonest acquisition of property
  • Synonym: depredation - an act of attacking or plundering
  • Antonym: restoration - the act of returning something to a former owner, place, or condition

Etymology

From Middle English 'despoylen', from Old French 'despoiller', from Latin 'despoliare' (to strip, rob).
