artery

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːr.t̬ɚ.i / (AmE), / ˈɑː.tər.i / (BrE)
A blood vessel that carries blood away from the heart to other parts of the body.
blood vessel carrying blood from heart
medical

Examples

The surgeon repaired the damaged artery to restore normal blood flow.
Plaque buildup in an artery can lead to serious heart problems.
Each artery supplies oxygen-rich blood to specific tissues in the body.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vein - a blood vessel that carries blood toward the heart

Etymology

From Latin 'arteria', from Greek 'artēria' (windpipe, artery).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑrtəri / (AmE), / ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
A main road, route, or channel that carries significant traffic, serving as a principal means of transportation or communication.
major route or thoroughfare

Examples

This road is the main artery connecting the city center to the airport.
After the accident, the artery was closed, causing major traffic delays.
Riverside Drive serves as an important artery for daily commuters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: highway - a main road, especially one connecting major towns or cities
  • Synonym: bypass - a road passing around a town or its center to provide an alternative route

Etymology

From Latin *arteria* (windpipe, artery), originally from Greek *artēria*.
