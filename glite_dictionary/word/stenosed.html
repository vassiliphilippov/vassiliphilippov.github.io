stenosed

1. ADJ

/ stɪˈnoʊzd / (AmE), / stɪˈnəʊzd / (BrE)
(of a body canal or passage) abnormally narrowed or constricted, often resulting from disease or injury.
abnormally narrowed (medical)
medical

Examples

The patient’s stenosed artery required immediate medical intervention.
A stenosed esophagus can make swallowing difficult or painful.
Surgery was performed to widen the stenosed heart valve.

Synset Examples

a stenosed coronary artery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stenotic - relating to or affected by stenosis (narrowing)

Etymology

From Greek 'stenos' (narrow) and the English suffix '-ed'.
