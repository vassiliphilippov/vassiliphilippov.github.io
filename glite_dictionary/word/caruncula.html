caruncula

1. NOUN

/ kəˈrʌŋkjələ / (AmE), / kəˈrʌŋkjʊlə / (BrE)
a small, fleshy outgrowth on a plant or animal, such as a fowl's wattle or a protuberance near the hilum of some seeds
small fleshy outgrowth on plant or animal

Examples

The turkey's caruncula became more prominent during mating season.
Botanists observed a bright red caruncula at the base of the seed.
Some birds have a noticeable caruncula on their heads.

Etymology

From Latin 'caruncula' meaning 'little piece of flesh'.
