intimately

1. ADV

/ ˈɪntəmətli / (AmE), / ˈɪntɪmətli / (BrE)
in a way that involves a close connection, familiarity, or personal relationship; closely or deeply.
in a close or familiar manner

Examples

The two families have been intimately involved in the business for generations.
She intimately understands the challenges of living in a foreign country.
The novel intimately explores the emotions of its characters.

Synset Examples

the two phenomena are intimately connected
the person most nearly concerned

Related Terms

  • Synonym: closely - in a way that shows a strong connection or relation

Etymology

From intimate + -ly, based on Latin intimatus (made known, familiar) and English adverbial suffix -ly.

2. ADV

/ ˈɪn.t̬ə.mət.li / (AmE), / ˈɪn.tɪ.mət.li / (BrE)
in a way that involves very close, detailed, or personal knowledge or understanding
in a very close or personal way

Examples

She intimately understood the challenges faced by the community.
The two scientists have worked intimately on projects for over a decade.
He knows the city intimately, having lived there his whole life.

Synset Examples

we knew them well

Related Terms

  • Synonym: closely - in a way that shows careful attention or detailed knowledge
  • Antonym: superficially - in a way that lacks depth, detail, or full understanding

Etymology

From intimate + -ly.
