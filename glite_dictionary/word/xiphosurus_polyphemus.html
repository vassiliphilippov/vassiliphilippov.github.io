Xiphosurus polyphemus

1. NOUN

/ ˌzɪfəsəˈrʊs ˌpɒlɪˈfiːməs / (AmE), / ˌzɪfəsjʊˈrʊs ˌpɒlɪˈfiːməs / (BrE)
a large marine arthropod of the Atlantic coast of North America, commonly known as the Atlantic horseshoe crab, with a domed, horseshoe-shaped carapace and a stiff pointed tail
Atlantic horseshoe crab (marine arthropod)

Examples

Scientists often study the blood of Xiphosurus polyphemus for its remarkable clotting properties.
The shell of Xiphosurus polyphemus is sometimes found washed up on Atlantic beaches.
Some aquariums feature living specimens of Xiphosurus polyphemus in their marine exhibits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horseshoe crab - common name for Xiphosurus polyphemus, a marine arthropod with a hard horseshoe-shaped shell

Etymology

From Greek *xiphos* (sword) and *oura* (tail) for genus; *Polyphemus* from the mythological Cyclops.
