Saturnia pavonia

1. NOUN

/ səˈtɜrniə pəˈvoʊniə / (AmE), / səˈtɜːniə pəˈvəʊniə / (BrE)
a large Eurasian moth of temperate forests, known as the small emperor moth, with heavily scaled, transparent wings
a large Eurasian moth species

Examples

The Saturnia pavonia is easily recognized by the eye-like spots on its wings.
Many nature enthusiasts seek out the Saturnia pavonia during its brief flight period in spring.
Caterpillars of Saturnia pavonia feed on heather and other low shrubs.

Etymology

From Latin 'Saturnia' (a genus of moths) + 'pavonia', from Latin 'pavo' (peacock), referring to eye spots on the wings.
