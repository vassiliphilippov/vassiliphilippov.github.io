Polystichum scopulinum

1. NOUN

/ pɑː.lɪˈstɪk.əm skoʊˈpuː.lɪ.nəm / (AmE), / pɒ.lɪˈstɪk.əm skɒˈpjuː.lɪ.nəm / (BrE)
A species of North American fern, commonly known as mountain holly fern or rock sword fern, found in rocky habitats.
a North American fern species

Examples

Polystichum scopulinum is commonly found growing in rocky areas throughout western North America.
The mountain holly fern, or Polystichum scopulinum, is valued for its hardiness in garden rockeries.
Researchers studied the distribution of Polystichum scopulinum in several mountainous regions.

Etymology

From Greek 'poly-' (many) + 'stichos' (row/line), referring to the arrangement of sori; 'scopulinum' from Latin 'scopulus' (rock, cliff), referring to its habitat.
