lousy

1. ADJ

/ ˈlaʊzi / (AmE), / ˈlaʊzi / (BrE)
Infested with lice.
infested with lice

Examples

The refugees were forced to burn their lousy blankets to prevent the spread of disease.
After weeks without proper sanitation, the prisoners' hair became lousy.
Inspectors found that several garments in the camp were lousy and needed to be destroyed.

Synset Examples

burned their lousy clothes

Etymology

From louse + -y; patterned after similar formations for adjectives from nouns.

2. ADJ

/ ˈlaʊzi / (AmE), / ˈlaʊzi / (BrE)
Very unpleasant, despicable, or contemptible; deserving strong dislike or contempt.
despicable; contemptible
informal

Examples

He played a lousy trick on his friend, which no one could forgive.
Most people considered the criminal's actions lousy and beneath contempt.
It was a lousy thing to betray his trust like that.

Synset Examples

a dirty (or lousy) trick
a filthy traitor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vile - extremely unpleasant or morally bad
  • Synonym: despicable - deserving hatred or contempt
  • Antonym: admirable - deserving respect or approval

Etymology

From louse + -y, originally meaning 'infested with lice', later extended metaphorically.

Source: WordNet

3. ADJ

/ ˈlaʊzi / (AmE), / ˈlaʊzi / (BrE)
Very bad or of poor quality.
very bad; poor quality
informal

Examples

The food at that restaurant was absolutely lousy.
He did a lousy job fixing the sink, so it still leaks.
I've had a lousy day and just want to go home.

Synset Examples

a lousy play
it's a stinking world

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awful - very bad or unpleasant
  • Synonym: terrible - extremely bad
  • Antonym: excellent - extremely good

Etymology

From Middle English *lousi*, meaning 'infested with lice'.
