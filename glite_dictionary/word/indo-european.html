Indo-European

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪndoʊ jʊˈroʊpiən / (AmE), / ˌɪndəʊ jʊəˈrəʊpiən / (BrE)
relating to the large family of languages that includes most languages of Europe and many in Southwest and South Asia, or to the people who speak these languages
relating to the Indo-European language family

Examples

The Indo-European languages dominate much of Europe and parts of Asia.
Latin is an ancient Indo-European language that influenced many modern European tongues.
Scholars study Indo-European roots to trace the connections among these languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Indo-European - member of the Indo-European language family (noun sense)

Etymology

Formed from 'Indo-', referring to India, and 'European', indicating the geographical extent of the language family.

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪndoʊ jʊˈrɪpiən / (AmE), / ˌɪndəʊ jʊəˈrɪpiən / (BrE)
of or relating to the ancient group of peoples who spoke Indo-European languages, or to their languages, culture, or migrations
relating to ancient Indo-European peoples or languages

Examples

The Indo-European migrations had a significant impact on the development of European societies.
Scholars study Indo-European roots to understand connections between modern languages.
The Indo-European family includes languages as diverse as English, Hindi, and Russian.

3. NOUN

/ ˌɪndoʊ jʊˈroʊpiən / (AmE), / ˌɪndəʊ jʊəˈrəʊpiən / (BrE)
a member of the prehistoric people believed to have spoken Proto-Indo-European, the ancestral language of the Indo-European language family
a person of the Proto-Indo-European-speaking people

Examples

Archaeologists study the migrations of early Indo-Europeans across Eurasia.
Some scholars debate the original homeland of the Indo-European people.
The spread of Indo-Europeans profoundly influenced the development of many modern languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Indo-European - a large family of languages spoken in Europe and Asia

Etymology

From Indo- (relating to India) and European, referring to the geographic range of languages descended from Proto-Indo-European.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˌɪndoʊ jʊˈroʊpiən / (AmE), / ˌɪndəʊ jʊəˈrəʊpiən / (BrE)
a family of languages that were spoken throughout most of Europe and parts of Asia by 1000 BC, and include most of the major languages of Europe and western Asia
family of related European and Asian languages

Examples

The Indo-European languages include English, Hindi, and Russian.
Scholars study the origins of the Indo-European language family to trace ancient migrations.
Latin is a member of the Indo-European group of languages.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Romance languages - languages derived from Latin, a branch of the Indo-European family
  • Degree Lower: Germanic languages - languages including English, German, and others, a branch of Indo-European

Etymology

Formed from 'Indo-' (relating to India) and 'European', indicating the geographic range of these languages.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
