Calosoma scrutator

1. NOUN

/ kæləˈsoʊmə skruˈteɪtər / (AmE), / kæləˈsəʊmə skruˈteɪtə / (BrE)
a large metallic blue-green ground beetle native to North America, known for preying on caterpillars
large North American ground beetle species

Examples

The vibrant Calosoma scrutator helps keep caterpillar populations in check in North American forests.
Many gardeners welcome Calosoma scrutator because it feeds on destructive larvae.
I spotted a metallic blue Calosoma scrutator crawling across the garden path.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: genus name Calosoma from Greek kalos (beautiful) + soma (body); species scrutator meaning 'examiner, searcher' in Latin.
