oligosaccharide

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊlɪgoʊˈsækəˌraɪd / (AmE), / ˌɒlɪgəʊˈsækəraɪd / (BrE)
A carbohydrate that, when hydrolyzed, yields a small number (typically 3–10) of monosaccharide molecules.
carbohydrate yielding few monosaccharides

Examples

Some beans contain high levels of oligosaccharides that can cause digestive discomfort in some people.
Researchers are studying the effects of dietary oligosaccharides on gut health and immunity.
The supplement includes several types of oligosaccharide to promote beneficial bacteria in the intestines.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: polysaccharide - a carbohydrate that yields many monosaccharides on hydrolysis
  • Degree Lower: monosaccharide - a carbohydrate that cannot be hydrolyzed to simpler sugars

Etymology

From oligo- (few) + saccharide (sugar), from Greek 'oligos' (few) and 'sakcharon' (sugar).
