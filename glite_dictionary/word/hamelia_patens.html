Hamelia patens

1. NOUN

/ həˈmiːliə ˈpeɪtənz / (AmE), / həˈmiːliə ˈpeɪtənz / (BrE)
a tropical shrub with showy orange to scarlet or crimson flowers, native to Florida, the West Indies, Mexico, and Brazil
a tropical flowering shrub species

Examples

The Hamelia patens in her garden blooms with vibrant red flowers throughout the summer.
Butterflies and hummingbirds are often attracted to the bright blossoms of Hamelia patens.
In some regions, Hamelia patens is commonly planted as an ornamental hedge.

Etymology

From the genus name Hamelia, honoring French botanist Henri-Louis Duhamel du Monceau, and the Latin specific epithet patens meaning 'spreading'.
