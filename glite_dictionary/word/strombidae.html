Strombidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrɒm.bɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˈstrɒm.bɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of tropical marine gastropod mollusks known as conchs, characterized by their spiral shells.
family of conchs (marine mollusks)

Examples

The Strombidae family includes some of the most beautifully patterned marine snails.
Shell collectors greatly value specimens from the Strombidae family.
Marine biologists study Strombidae to understand the diversity of tropical gastropods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conch - a tropical marine mollusk with a spiral shell

Etymology

From New Latin Strombidae, based on the type genus Strombus.

2. NOUN

/ strɑmˈbɪdiː / (AmE), / strɒmˈbɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine gastropod mollusks commonly known as the conch family, characterized by their spiral shells.
family of marine conchs

Examples

The Strombidae are renowned for their beautiful spiral shells and are often collected as curios.
Several species in the Strombidae family play important roles in Caribbean marine ecosystems.
Biologists are studying how members of the Strombidae adapt to changing ocean conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conch - a type of marine mollusk with a spiral shell

Etymology

From the genus name Strombus, from Greek *strombos* (a spiral shell).
