equivalent

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈkwɪvələnt / (AmE), / ɪˈkwɪvələnt / (BrE)
equal in value, amount, function, meaning, or effect to something else
equal in value or effect

Examples

His promise was equivalent to an admission of guilt in the eyes of the public.
Paying in euros is equivalent to paying in dollars at this exchange rate.
The two statements are equivalent in meaning, though worded differently.

Synset Examples

a wish that was equivalent to a command
his statement was tantamount to an admission of guilt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tantamount - having the same effect or value as something else
  • Antonym: unequal - not equal in value, degree, or amount

Etymology

From Latin aequivalentem, from aequus (equal) + valere (to be worth).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈkwɪvələnt / (AmE), / ɪˈkwɪvələnt / (BrE)
a person or thing that is equal to another in value, amount, function, meaning, or importance
thing or person equal to another

Examples

Is there a French equivalent for this English expression?

No one can find an equivalent to her unique talent.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equal - person or thing considered the same in status or value

  • Antonym: opposite - something that is completely different from another

Etymology

From Late Latin aequivalent-, aequivalens (having equal power), from aequi- (equal) + valere (to be strong, be worth).

3. NOUN

/ ɪˈkwɪvələnt / (AmE), / ɪˈkwɪvələnt / (BrE)
(Chemistry) The amount of a substance that reacts with or is chemically equivalent to a fixed amount of another substance, used especially with respect to atomic weight; for elements, commonly measured relative to 8 parts of oxygen.
chemical quantity equivalent to another element

Examples

In chemistry, an equivalent refers to the amount of a substance that has the same combining power as a set quantity of another.
One equivalent of hydrogen will combine with eight equivalents of oxygen to form water.
The concept of a chemical equivalent is fundamental to understanding stoichiometry.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin equivalent-, equivalentem, from Latin aequivalēns ('equal in value').
