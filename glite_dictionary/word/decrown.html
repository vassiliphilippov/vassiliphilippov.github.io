to decrown

1. VERB

/ diˈkraʊn / (AmE), / dɪˈkraʊn / (BrE)
to remove someone from a position of authority or power, especially from a throne
remove from power or throne

Examples

The revolutionaries vowed to decrown the corrupt ruler and restore justice.
The board voted to decrown the CEO after the scandal emerged.
After many years of tyranny, people finally managed to decrown their oppressive leader.

Synset Examples

It was a shocking moment when they chose to decrown him.
They decided to decrown the king after the rebellion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dethrone - to remove a monarch from the throne
  • Antonym: to enthrone - to install as a monarch or in a position of power

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'de-' (down, away) + 'crown' (from Old French 'corone', from Latin 'corona').

2. VERB

/ diˈkraʊn / (AmE), / diˈkraʊn / (BrE)
to remove a monarch from power or dethrone them
remove a monarch from power

Examples

The rebels plotted to decrown the monarch and establish a republic.
History books recount how the people rose together to decrown the king.
It is rare for a revolution to successfully decrown a monarch without violence.

Synset Examples

To decrown the monarch would require widespread support.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dethrone - to remove a monarch from the throne; depose

Etymology

From the prefix de- (removal) and crown.
