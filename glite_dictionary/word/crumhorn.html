crumhorn

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrʌmˌhɔrn / (AmE), / ˈkrʌmˌhɔːn / (BrE)
a Renaissance woodwind instrument with a double reed and a characteristically curved tube, producing a buzzing sound
Renaissance curved woodwind instrument

Examples

The medieval ensemble featured a crumhorn alongside other period instruments.
He mastered the technique of playing the crumhorn for the Renaissance concert.
A collection of rare crumhorns was displayed at the music museum.

Etymology

From German Krummhorn, from krumm (curved) + Horn (horn).
