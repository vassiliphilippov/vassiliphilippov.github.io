Corynebacterium diphtheriae

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔːrɪnoʊbækˈtɪəriəm dɪfˈθɪəri.aɪ / (AmE), / ˌkɒrɪnəbækˈtɪəriəm dɪfˈθɪəri.aɪ / (BrE)
a species of bacterium that causes the infectious disease diphtheria in humans
bacterium causing diphtheria
medical

Examples

Corynebacterium diphtheriae is responsible for causing acute cases of diphtheria worldwide.
The vaccine protects individuals from infection by Corynebacterium diphtheriae.
Doctors confirmed that the bacteria found in the patient's throat was Corynebacterium diphtheriae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diphtheria - a serious infectious disease affecting the throat and airways

Etymology

From Greek 'korynē' (club) + 'bakterion' (rod) and 'diphtheria' (leather, relating to the throat membranes in diphtheria).
