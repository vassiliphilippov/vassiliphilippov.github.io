prestigious

1. ADJ

/ prɛˈstɪdʒəs / (AmE), / prɛˈstɪdʒəs / (BrE)
having a high reputation; respected and admired for excellence, achievement, or quality
respected and highly regarded

Examples

She graduated from a prestigious university known for its rigorous academic standards.
Winning the award at such a prestigious event was a tremendous honor for the young scientist.
His novel received positive reviews from several prestigious literary journals.

Synset Examples

our esteemed leader
a prestigious author

Related Terms

  • Synonym: esteemed - highly respected or regarded
  • Synonym: distinguished - successful, authoritative, and commanding great respect
  • Antonym: unremarkable - not particularly special or impressive

Etymology

From Latin 'praestigiosus' (full of tricks), later shifting in meaning to denote respectability and reputation.

2. ADJ

/ prəˈstɪdʒəs / (AmE), / prɛˈstɪdʒəs / (BrE)
respected, admired, or having influence because of high status, reputation, or achievement
respected due to status or achievement

Examples

He received an award from a prestigious institution for his scientific contributions.
She was offered a position at the prestigious law firm after graduating.
The university is known for its prestigious academic programs.

Synset Examples

a prestigious professor at a prestigious university

Related Terms

  • Synonym: esteemed - highly respected or admired
  • Synonym: distinguished - very successful and admired by others
  • Antonym: obscure - not well known or prominent

Etymology

From Latin 'praestigiosus' (full of tricks), later shifting sense in English to refer to impressive reputation.

Synset POS: s
