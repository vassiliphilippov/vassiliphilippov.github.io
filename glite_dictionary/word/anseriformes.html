Anseriformes

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.sə.rəˈfɔr.miːz / (AmE), / ˌan.sə.rɪˈfɔː.miːz / (BrE)
An order of birds that includes ducks, geese, swans, and screamers, characterized by webbed feet and a broad, flat bill.
order of birds—ducks, geese, swans, etc.

Examples

Scientists have observed that members of the Anseriformes are highly adapted to aquatic environments.
Swans, which belong to the Anseriformes, are known for their graceful appearance and distinctive calls.
The fossil record indicates that Anseriformes have a long evolutionary history dating back millions of years.

Etymology

From Modern Latin *Anseriformes*, from *anser* (goose) + *-iformes* (having the form of).

2. NOUN

an order of birds that includes ducks, geese, swans, and screamers, characterized by webbed feet and a broad, flat bill
order of birds: ducks, geese, swans, screamers

Examples

Scientists have classified ducks, geese, and swans in the order Anseriformes.
Members of the Anseriformes are well adapted for swimming with their webbed feet.
The fossil record shows that Anseriformes have existed since the Late Cretaceous period.

Etymology

From Latin anser (goose) + -iformes (form, shape).
