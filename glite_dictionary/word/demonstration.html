demonstration

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛmənˈstreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛmənˈstreɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
an act of showing or explaining how something works or is done, especially to an audience
act of showing or displaying something

Examples

The teacher gave a brilliant demonstration of the new software in class.
There will be a live demonstration of glassblowing at the museum today.
Before buying the machine, the customer requested a demonstration of its features.

Synset Examples

the presentation of new data
he gave the customer a demonstration

Related Terms

  • Synonym: presentation - the act of giving or showing something to an audience
  • Synonym: exhibition - a public display of works of art or other items of interest

Etymology

From Latin demonstratio (showing, pointing out), from demonstrare (to demonstrate).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɛmənˈstreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛmənˈstreɪʃən / (BrE)

Examples

That clever demonstration delayed the invasion by several days.

4. NOUN

/ ˌdɛmənˈstreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛmənˈstreɪʃən / (BrE)
A logical proof or argument that establishes the truth of a proposition or conclusion.
logical proof or argument

Examples

The professor gave a clear demonstration of the theorem during the lecture.
Euclid's Elements contains a rigorous demonstration for each geometric proposition.
Her demonstration of the principle convinced even the skeptics in the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proof - evidence or argument that establishes the truth of a statement
  • Antonym: refutation - the act of proving a statement or theory to be wrong or false

Etymology

Examples

Police were present to keep order during the peaceful demonstration on Sunday afternoon.
Yesterday’s demonstration attracted national media attention and led to several streets being closed.

Related Terms
