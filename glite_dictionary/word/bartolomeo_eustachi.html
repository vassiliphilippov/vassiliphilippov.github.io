Bartolomeo Eustachi

1. PROPN

/ ˌbɑrtəloʊˈmeɪoʊ juːˈstæki / (AmE), / ˌbɑːtələˈmeɪəʊ juːˈstæki / (BrE)
(1520–1574) An Italian anatomist known as a father of modern anatomy, noted for his detailed descriptions of the ear and the heart.
Italian anatomist, father of modern anatomy

Examples

Bartolomeo Eustachi made significant contributions to anatomical science during the Renaissance.
The Eustachian tube is named after Bartolomeo Eustachi, who first described it in detail.
Many medical students study the work of Bartolomeo Eustachi while learning about the human ear.
