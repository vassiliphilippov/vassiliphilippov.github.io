Merodach

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɛrəˌdæk / (AmE), / ˈmɛrəˌdæk / (BrE)
the chief god of the Babylonian pantheon, also known as Marduk, whose consort was Sarpanitu
Babylonian chief god (Marduk)

Examples

Ancient texts often refer to Merodach as the supreme deity of Babylon.
The temple dedicated to Merodach was a center of religious life in the city.
Legends describe Merodach as a powerful god who defeated chaos to create order.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Marduk - chief god of Babylon, alternative name for Merodach

Etymology

From Akkadian Marduk, rendered as Merodach in the Hebrew Bible.
