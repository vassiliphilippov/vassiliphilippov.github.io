knave

1. NOUN

/ neɪv / (AmE), / neɪv / (BrE)
a dishonest or unscrupulous man; a villain or scoundrel
dishonest man; scoundrel
archaic

Examples

The crafty knave tricked the villagers out of their money.
In the story, the knave plotted against the king.
No honest man would associate with such a knave.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scoundrel - a dishonest or unscrupulous person
  • Synonym: villain - a wicked or evil person
  • Antonym: gentleman - a chivalrous, honorable man

Etymology

From Old English cnafa (boy, male servant), later meaning a dishonest man.

2. NOUN

/ neɪv / (AmE), / neɪv / (BrE)
a playing card that pictures a young man, formerly called the 'knave', now usually called the 'jack'
historic term for the jack in playing cards
archaic

Examples

He drew the knave of clubs and smiled at his good fortune.
In old card games, the knave ranked below the queen but above the ten.
Collectors cherish antique decks that still use the word knave instead of 'jack'.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jack - the playing card showing a young man, formerly 'knave'

Etymology

From Old English *cnafa* (boy, servant), later used in playing cards for a male youth.
