andantino

1. ADJ

/ ˌændænˈtiːnoʊ / (AmE), / ˌandæn'tiːnəʊ / (BrE)
(of music) in a tempo that is generally moderately fast, slightly faster than andante.
moderately fast tempo in music

Examples

The second movement is marked andantino, giving it a gracefully flowing character.
Please play the piece andantino, paying attention to the lightness of the melody.
The composer chose andantino to bring out a mood that is bright but not rushed.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: andante - a moderately slow tempo in music
  • Degree Higher: allegro - a fast and lively tempo in music

Etymology

From Italian 'andantino', diminutive of 'andante' (going, walking), used as a tempo marking in music.
