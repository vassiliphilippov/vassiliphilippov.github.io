Taxaceae

1. NOUN

/ tækˈseɪiaɪ / (AmE), / tækˈseɪɪiː / (BrE)
a family of coniferous trees and shrubs, commonly known as the yew family, sometimes classified under the order Taxales
family of coniferous trees and shrubs (yew family)

Examples

The Taxaceae are commonly found in temperate regions across the northern hemisphere.
Yews, belonging to the Taxaceae, are known for their distinctive red berry-like arils.
Botanists recently debated whether Taxaceae should be placed in its own order or grouped with other conifers.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus name Taxus (yew).

2. NOUN

/ tækˈseɪsiaː / (AmE), / tækˈseɪsiː-iː / (BrE)
a family of coniferous plants, commonly known as the yew family, sometimes classified under the order Taxales
family of coniferous plants (yew family)
medical

Examples

The Taxaceae include trees and shrubs widely used in landscaping for their evergreen foliage.
Research on Taxaceae has contributed to advances in cancer treatment due to compounds found in yew trees.
Several species within the Taxaceae are protected because of their ecological importance.

Etymology

From Latin *Taxus* (yew tree) + -aceae, the standard suffix for plant family names.
