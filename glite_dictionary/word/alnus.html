Alnus

1. PROPN

/ ˈælnəs / (AmE), / ˈal.nəs / (BrE)
a genus of deciduous trees and shrubs known as alders, belonging to the birch family (Betulaceae)
genus of trees; alders

Examples

The botanist specialized in the study of Alnus species found along riverbanks.
Several members of the genus Alnus are important for stabilizing soil in wet environments.
Pollen from Alnus can cause allergic reactions in sensitive individuals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alder - a type of deciduous tree or shrub of the genus Alnus

Etymology

From Latin *Alnus* (alder).
