peasantry

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛzəntri / (AmE), / ˈpɛzəntri / (BrE)
The class of rural people, especially small farmers or agricultural laborers, who typically have low social status and limited wealth or power.
rural class of peasants

Examples

The medieval peasantry played a crucial role in the development of agriculture.
Her research focuses on the lives of the European peasantry in the 19th century.
Economic reforms greatly improved the living conditions of the local peasantry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peasant - a poor farmer of low social status

Etymology

From the French 'paysannerie', from 'paysan' meaning 'peasant'.
