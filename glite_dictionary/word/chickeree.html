chickeree

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪkəri / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪkəri / (BrE)
A small, tree-dwelling squirrel of the far western United States, similar to the red squirrel.
western U.S. squirrel species

Examples

The chickeree is often seen gathering pine cones in the forests of northern California.
Unlike the eastern gray squirrel, the chickeree has a reddish-brown coat and smaller size.
Hikers sometimes hear the sharp calls of the chickeree echoing through the tall trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red squirrel - a small tree squirrel with a reddish coat, found in North America and Eurasia
