mal rosso

1. NOUN

/ mal ˈroʊsoʊ / (AmE), / mal ˈrɒsəʊ / (BrE)
A disease caused by deficiency of niacin or tryptophan, marked by skin inflammation, gastrointestinal distress, and mental disturbances; also known as pellagra.
disease caused by niacin deficiency (pellagra)
medical

Examples

Outbreaks of mal rosso were common in rural communities affected by poor nutrition.
The classic symptoms of mal rosso made diagnosis straightforward for experienced physicians.
Chronic alcoholism can increase the risk of developing mal rosso due to impaired nutrient absorption.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pellagra - a disease caused by niacin deficiency

Etymology

From Italian 'mal rosso' (red sickness), referring to the red skin rash symptom.
