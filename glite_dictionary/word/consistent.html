consistent

1. ADJ

/ kənˈsɪstənt / (AmE), / kənˈsɪstənt / (BrE)
marked by logical, orderly, or harmonious connection or agreement among the parts
logically or harmoniously connected

Examples

Her explanation was consistent and easy to follow, with each point building on the last.
The novel's tone is remarkably consistent from start to finish, making it a compelling read.
A consistent design theme ties all the rooms together beautifully.

Synset Examples

a coherent argument

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coherent - logically connected and consistent
  • Antonym: incoherent - lacking orderly or logical connection among parts

Etymology

From Latin 'consistere' (to stand firm).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00466504-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ kənˈsɪstənt / (AmE), / kənˈsɪstənt / (BrE)
(often followed by 'with') in agreement with or compatible with something else; not contradictory.
in agreement or compatible with

Examples

Her actions were consistent with her words, demonstrating her honesty.
The results of the experiment are consistent with earlier studies.
His explanation was not consistent with the facts already established.

Synset Examples

testimony consistent with the known facts
I have decided that the course of conduct which I am following is consistent with my sense of responsibility as president in time of war

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compatible - able to exist or work together without conflict
  • Antonym: inconsistent - not in agreement or not compatible with something else

Etymology

From Latin 'consistere' (to stand firm, stop, remain) via late Latin and Old French.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00579031-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ kənˈsɪstənt / (AmE), / kənˈsɪstənt / (BrE)
(of results or actions) capable of being reproduced with the same outcome; yielding similar or reliable results each time.
capable of being reproduced; reliable in results

Examples

The scientist was pleased that the experiment produced consistent results across multiple trials.
We need to ensure our testing method is consistent so the data can be trusted.
These findings are only valuable if they are consistent every time the procedure is repeated.

Synset Examples

astonishingly reproducible results can be obtained

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reproducible - (of results) able to be duplicated under the same conditions

Etymology

From Latin 'consistens', present participle of 'consistere' (to stand firm, take a stand).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01873946-a
Synset POS: a

4. ADJ

/ kənˈsɪstənt / (AmE), / kənˈsɪstənt / (BrE)
having a uniform structure or composition throughout; not varying or changing in quality or character
unchanging in structure or composition

Examples

The mixture must remain consistent to achieve reliable test results.
The company maintains consistent quality across all its products.
Her performance has been consistent throughout the entire season.

Synset Examples

bituminous coal is often treated as a consistent and homogeneous product

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homogeneous - of the same or a similar kind throughout
  • Antonym: variable - liable to change or vary

Etymology

From Latin 'consistens', present participle of 'consistere' meaning 'to stand firm'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01203638-s
Synset POS: s
