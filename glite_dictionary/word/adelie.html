Adelie

1. NOUN

/ əˈdiːli / (AmE), / əˈdiːli / (BrE)
a medium-sized species of penguin (Pygoscelis adeliae) native to the coastal regions of Antarctica, especially the Adelie Coast
species of Antarctic penguin

Examples

The Adelie is known for its distinctive white eye ring and energetic behavior.
Large colonies of Adelie can be seen nesting along the Antarctic shoreline.
Scientists monitor Adelie populations to study the effects of climate change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penguin - a flightless seabird of the southern hemisphere

Etymology

Named after the Adélie Coast of Antarctica, itself named by French explorer Jules Dumont d'Urville after his wife, Adèle.
