Clematis texensis

1. NOUN

/ kləˈmætɪs tɛkˈsɛnɪs / (AmE), / klɪˈmætɪs tɛkˈsɛnɪs / (BrE)
A species of woody vine native to Texas, noted for its showy, solitary, nodding scarlet flowers.
woody Texan vine with scarlet flowers

Examples

The garden's centerpiece was a stunning Clematis texensis covered in bright scarlet blossoms.
Many Texas gardeners choose Clematis texensis for its resilience and vibrant flowers.
Hummingbirds are often attracted to the nodding blooms of Clematis texensis.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Clematis' (genus of climbing plants), 'texensis' (of or from Texas).
