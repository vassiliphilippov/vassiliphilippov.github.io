Actias

1. NOUN

/ ˈæk.ti.əs / (AmE), / ˈæk.ti.əs / (BrE)
a genus of large, striking moths in the family Saturniidae, known as luna moths
genus of luna moths

Examples

The Actias genus includes several species of visually impressive moths.
Researchers studied the lifecycle of Actias to better understand metamorphosis.
The bright green wings of Actias luna make it easy to identify.

Related Terms

2. NOUN

/ ˈæk.ti.əs / (AmE), / ˈæk.ti.əs / (BrE)
A genus of large, greenish moths in the family Saturniidae, including the luna moth and related species.
genus of large moths, including the luna moth

Examples

The Actias genus is well-known for its strikingly beautiful moths with long, trailing tails.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the Actias genus in Southeast Asia.
The Actias luna moth is famous for its bright green wings and graceful flight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: luna moth - A North American moth species of the genus Actias, known for its green wings and long tails.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on Greek 'Aktias', an epithet of Artemis, associated with the seashore.
