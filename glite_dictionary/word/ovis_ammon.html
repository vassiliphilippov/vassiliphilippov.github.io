Ovis ammon

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvɪs ˈæmɒn / (AmE), / ˈəʊvɪs ˈamɒn / (BrE)
A species of wild sheep native to the semidesert and mountainous regions of central Asia, also known as argali.
wild sheep of central Asia

Examples

The Ovis ammon is distinguished by its impressive curved horns, which can reach remarkable lengths.
During their expedition to Mongolia, researchers observed several herds of Ovis ammon grazing on the mountain slopes.
Conservationists are concerned about the declining population of Ovis ammon due to habitat loss and poaching.

Etymology

From Latin genus and species names: Ovis (sheep) and ammon (from the Egyptian god Ammon, often depicted with ram's horns).
