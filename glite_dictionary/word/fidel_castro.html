Fidel Castro

1. PROPN

/ fəˈdɛl ˈkæstroʊ / (AmE), / fɪˈdɛl ˈkæstrəʊ / (BrE)
(1926–2016) The Cuban socialist leader who overthrew the Batista regime in 1959 and established a Marxist government in Cuba.
Cuban socialist leader (1926–2016)

Examples

Fidel Castro ruled Cuba for nearly five decades, shaping the nation's history and politics.
The speeches of Fidel Castro were broadcast to the Cuban people regularly.
Many consider Fidel Castro a key figure in Cold War history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Che Guevara - Argentinian revolutionary involved in the Cuban Revolution
  • Synonym: Raúl Castro - Cuban politician and Fidel Castro's brother
