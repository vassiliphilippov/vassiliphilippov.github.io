wearable

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛr.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈweə.rə.bəl / (BrE)
an item or covering designed to be worn on the body, especially clothing or electronic devices integrated into clothing
item designed to be worn on the body

Examples

Many tech companies are launching new wearables that monitor your health and fitness.
The exhibition showcased eco-friendly wearables made from recycled materials.
Smart wearables have become popular among athletes for tracking their performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garment - a piece of clothing

Etymology

From 'wear' + '-able', originally an adjective meaning 'able to be worn', later also used as a noun.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03055525-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈwer.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈwɛə.rə.bəl / (BrE)
suitable for wearing; able to be worn comfortably or practically on the body
suitable for wearing

Examples

These shoes are stylish but also very wearable for long days.
The designer focused on creating wearable garments for everyday life.
Lightweight fabrics make this jacket extremely wearable in spring and summer.

Synset Examples

wearable evening clothes
a wearable hearing aid

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unwearable - not able or suitable to be worn

Etymology

From the verb 'wear' + suffix '-able', meaning 'able to be worn'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02550103-a
Synset POS: a
