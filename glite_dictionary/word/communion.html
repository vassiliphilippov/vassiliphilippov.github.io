communion

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmjunjən / (AmE), / kəˈmjuːnjən / (BrE)
a group of Christians who share the same religious faith and rites
group of Christians with shared faith

Examples

The Anglican communion includes churches from different countries around the world.
He was raised in the Lutheran communion and attended services every Sunday.
After the schism, a new communion of believers was formed.

Etymology

From Latin *communio* (sharing in common), from *communis* (common).

2. NOUN

/ kəˈmjunjən / (AmE), / kəˈmjuːnjən / (BrE)
the sharing or exchanging of intimate thoughts and feelings, especially on a spiritual or emotional level
intimate sharing of feelings and thoughts

Examples

During their retreat, the group experienced a profound sense of communion with each other.
There was an unspoken communion between the artist and her audience.
The best friendships are built on mutual trust and communion of the heart.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: communication - the act of conveying information or expressing thoughts and feelings
  • Antonym: alienation - the state of being isolated from a group or activity

Etymology

From Latin 'communio' (sharing in common, fellowship), from 'communis' (common, shared).
