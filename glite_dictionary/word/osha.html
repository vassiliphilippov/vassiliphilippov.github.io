OSHA

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊʃə / (AmE), / ˈəʊʃə / (BrE)
The Occupational Safety and Health Administration, a US government agency responsible for ensuring safe and healthy working conditions.
US workplace safety agency
american

Examples

All companies must comply with OSHA regulations regarding workplace safety.
The inspector from OSHA visited the site to check for hazards.
Employers are required to post OSHA information in a visible area for all employees.

Etymology

Acronym from Occupational Safety and Health Administration.
