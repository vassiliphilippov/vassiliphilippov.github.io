to ionate

1. VERB

/ aɪˈoʊneɪt / (AmE), / aɪˈəʊneɪt / (BrE)
to add ions to (a substance); to subject (a substance) to a process that makes it ionic
add ions to (a substance)

Examples

Scientists ionate water to improve its conductivity in these experiments.
The lab technician carefully ionated the sample before measuring its properties.
They are developing a new method to ionate different types of gases more efficiently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ionize - to convert into ions or to become ionic

Etymology

From 'ion' + '-ate', a suffix forming verbs meaning 'to treat or combine with'.
