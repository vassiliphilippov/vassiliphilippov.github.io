emotionlessness

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmoʊʃənləsnəs / (AmE), / ɪˈməʊʃənləsnəs / (BrE)
the state of showing or feeling no emotions; the quality of being unemotional
lack of emotion or feeling

Examples

His emotionlessness during the crisis surprised everyone in the room.
The robot's emotionlessness made its responses feel unnatural and cold.
She hid her sadness behind a mask of emotionlessness at the funeral.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apathy - lack of interest or concern
  • Synonym: stoicism - endurance of pain or hardship without showing feelings
  • Antonym: emotionality - the quality of being emotional; showing feelings

Etymology

From 'emotion' + '-less' (without) + '-ness' (state or quality).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈmoʊʃənləsnəs / (AmE), / ɪˈməʊʃənləsnəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of having no emotion or seeming not to feel anything
absence of emotion

Examples

Her emotionlessness during the funeral surprised everyone who knew how close she was to her grandfather.
The robot's emotionlessness made it difficult for humans to relate to it.
He answered the difficult questions with complete emotionlessness, betraying nothing of his true feelings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apathy - lack of feeling, emotion, or interest
  • Synonym: impassivity - lack of emotional expression or response
  • Antonym: emotionality - display of emotion; ability to feel or express emotions

Etymology

From 'emotion' + '-less' + '-ness', denoting the state of not feeling emotion.
