Zizania aquatica

1. NOUN

/ zɪˈzeɪniə əˈkwætɪkə / (AmE), / zɪˈzeɪniə əˈkwætɪkə / (BrE)
a perennial aquatic grass native to North America, cultivated for its edible grain known as wild rice
a species of wild rice from North America

Examples

Many Native American tribes traditionally harvested Zizania aquatica from lakes and rivers.
The grains of Zizania aquatica are marketed as wild rice in grocery stores.
Zizania aquatica plays an important role in supporting wetland wildlife.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild rice - the edible grain harvested from aquatic grasses of the genus Zizania

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Zizania (the genus) and aquatica from Latin 'aqua' (water), indicating its aquatic habitat.
