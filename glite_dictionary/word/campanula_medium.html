Campanula medium

1. NOUN

/ kæmˈpæn.ju.lə ˈmiː.di.əm / (AmE), / kæmˈpan.juː.lə ˈmiː.di.əm / (BrE)
a European biennial plant, widely cultivated for its striking blue, violet, or white bell-shaped flowers; commonly known as the Canterbury bell
European biennial plant; Canterbury bell

Examples

The Campanula medium produces elegant bell-shaped blooms in vivid shades of blue and white.
Gardeners value Campanula medium for its long flowering period in early summer.
I planted several Campanula medium around the borders to add color to the garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Canterbury bell - common name for Campanula medium

Etymology

From Latin: 'campanula' meaning 'little bell', due to the bell-shaped flowers; 'medium' refers to its moderate size.
