Ornithorhynchidae

1. NOUN

/ ɔːrˌnɪθəˈrɪŋkɪdi / (AmE), / ɔːˌnɪθərɪŋˈkɪːdiː / (BrE)
The scientific family of egg-laying mammals that includes only the platypus.
family of mammals including the platypus

Examples

The Ornithorhynchidae family is native to eastern Australia and Tasmania.
Scientists study the unique characteristics of Ornithorhynchidae to understand mammalian evolution.
Of all monotremes, the Ornithorhynchidae are perhaps the most distinctive due to their duck-like bills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: platypus - an egg-laying aquatic mammal native to Australia

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'ornitho-' (bird) and 'rhynchos' (snout).
