ocher

1. ADJ

/ ˈoʊkɚ / (AmE), / ˈəʊkə / (BrE)
of a moderate orange-yellow color; resembling the color of ocher pigment.
moderate orange-yellow color

Examples

The artist painted the walls an ocher shade to create a warm atmosphere.
She wore an ocher scarf that complemented the autumn leaves.
The pottery was colored with a distinctive ocher pigment.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: yellow - a color resembling that of ripe lemons; primary color between green and orange in the spectrum
  • Degree Higher: golden - having a deep, rich yellow color

Etymology

From Greek 'ōkhra' (pale yellow), via Latin 'ochra'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊkər / (AmE), / ˈəʊkə / (BrE)
a moderate yellow-orange to orange color, resembling the natural earth pigment of the same name
yellow-orange color

Examples

The artist chose to paint the walls in a warm ocher shade.
Sunset bathed the fields in golden ocher light.
She wore a scarf dyed a deep ocher to brighten her outfit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ochre - alternative spelling for 'ocher'
  • Degree Lower: amber - a yellowish-brown color
  • Degree Higher: orange - a brighter orange color

Etymology

From Greek ōkhra (pale yellow), via Latin ochra and Old French ocre.

Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ ˈoʊkər / (AmE), / ˈəʊkə / (BrE)
A natural earth pigment containing silica, alumina, and ferric oxide, used as a yellow or reddish-brown coloring agent.
natural earth pigment used for coloring

Examples

The walls were painted a warm shade of ocher that glowed in the afternoon light.
Artists have been using natural ocher as a pigment for thousands of years.
She mixed red ocher with oil to create earthy tones in her landscape painting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ochre - alternative British spelling for the same pigment

Etymology

From Greek ōkhra (pale yellow) via Latin ochra and Old French ocre.
