to violate

1. VERB

/ ˈvaɪ.ə.leɪt / (AmE), / ˈvaɪ.ə.leɪt / (BrE)
to act in disregard of a law, rule, contract, or promise; to break or fail to observe an agreement or principle
disregard a law, rule, or promise

Examples

The company will face penalties if they violate the terms of the agreement.
He promised to keep my secret, but he violated my trust by telling everyone.
You must not violate the school rules, or you could be suspended.

Synset Examples

offend all laws of humanity
violate the basic laws or human civilization
break a law
break a promise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: break - to fail to observe a law or agreement
  • Antonym: to observe - to comply with or respect a rule or promise

Etymology

From Latin violare (to treat with violence, dishonor), from vis (force).
