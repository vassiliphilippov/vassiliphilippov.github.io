barnacle

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑrnəkəl / (AmE), / ˈbɑːnəkəl / (BrE)
A small European goose (Branta leucopsis) that breeds in the Arctic and migrates south for the winter.
a species of European goose

Examples

The barnacle can often be seen grazing on salt marshes along the northern coast.
Flocks of barnacle were observed flying south for the winter.
The distinctive black and white markings make the barnacle easy to identify among other geese.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brant - a species of goose similar in size and habitat to the barnacle goose

Etymology

Middle English barnakille, from Anglo-Latin bernaca, referring to the barnacle goose.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːrnəkəl / (AmE), / ˈbɑːnəkl / (BrE)
a small marine crustacean that attaches itself permanently to rocks, boat hulls, and other submerged surfaces, forming a hard shell as an adult
small marine crustacean with a hard shell

Examples

The hull of the old ship was covered in thick barnacle growth.
Marine biologists study the way barnacles attach themselves to underwater surfaces.
It takes special scrapers to remove barnacles from boats.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *bernatica*, possibly from Vulgar Latin *bernacula* (a type of mollusk).
