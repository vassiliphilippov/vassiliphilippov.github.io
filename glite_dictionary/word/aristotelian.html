Aristotelian

1. ADJ

/ ˌærɪstəˈtiliən / (AmE), / ˌærɪstəˈtiːliən / (BrE)
relating to Aristotle or his philosophy, methods, or ideas
related to Aristotle or his philosophy

Examples

Her research is based on Aristotelian logic and methods.
Many medieval philosophers were influenced by Aristotelian thought.
The Aristotelian approach contrasts with modern scientific methodologies.

Synset Examples

Aristotelean logic

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Platonic - relating to Plato or his philosophy

Etymology

From Aristotle, the name of the ancient Greek philosopher, plus the English suffix -ian.

2. NOUN

/ ˌærəstəˈtɪliən / (AmE), / ˌærɪstəˈtɪliən / (BrE)
A follower of Aristotle or a person who adheres to the philosophy and methods of Aristotle.
follower of Aristotle or his philosophy

Examples

Many scholars in the Middle Ages were considered Aristotelians for their devotion to Aristotle’s works.
The Aristotelian argued that reality is best understood through empirical observation and logic.
In philosophical debates, the Aristotelians often opposed the Platonists.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Platonist - a follower of Plato or his philosophy

Etymology

From Aristotle (the Greek philosopher) + -ian, denoting adherence or relation.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09827503-n
Synset POS: n
