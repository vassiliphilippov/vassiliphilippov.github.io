brothel

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɑːθəl / (AmE), / ˈbrɒθəl / (BrE)
A house or other place where people pay to have sex with prostitutes.
place where prostitutes work

Examples

Police raided the brothel after several complaints from neighbors.
The city enacted new laws to regulate brothels in certain districts.
She explained that her grandmother once worked in a famous Parisian brothel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bordello - a house where people may visit prostitutes

Etymology

From Middle English brothel (worthless person), from Old English broþen (corrupted, decayed), sense extended to the establishment by the 16th century.
