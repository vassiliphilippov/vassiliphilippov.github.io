arachnidian

1. ADJ

/ əˈræk.nɪ.di.ən / (AmE), / əˈrak.nɪ.dɪ.ən / (BrE)
relating to or resembling a member of the class Arachnida (such as spiders, scorpions, and related animals)
relating to arachnids (spiders, scorpions, etc.)

Examples

The arachnidian features of the fossil indicated it was closely related to modern spiders.
Some artists use arachnidian patterns for dramatic effect in costume design.
The biologist gave a lecture on arachnidian anatomy, focusing on the unique structure of their legs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arachnid - any member of the class Arachnida (spiders, scorpions, etc.)

Etymology

From Modern Latin 'Arachnida' (the class name), from Greek 'aráchnē' (spider), with the English suffix '-ian'.
