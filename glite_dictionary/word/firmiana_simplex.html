Firmiana simplex

1. NOUN

/ fɜːrˈmaɪ.ə nə ˈsɪm.plɛks / (AmE), / fɜːˈmɪː.ə.nə ˈsɪm.plɛks / (BrE)
A deciduous tree native to Asia, often grown as an ornamental in the southern United States, known for its maple-like foliage and long racemes of yellow-green flowers followed by distinctive leaflike pods.
an ornamental deciduous tree native to Asia

Examples

The Firmiana simplex in our garden draws attention with its broad, maplelike leaves and unusual pods.
Many southern gardens feature Firmiana simplex for its decorative foliage and clusters of greenish-yellow flowers.
Botanists appreciate the adaptability of Firmiana simplex to various soil conditions in warm climates.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus name Firmiana (after Karl Joseph von Firmian, 18th-century Austrian statesman) and the Latin simplex (simple or single).
