temperamental

1. ADJ

/ ˌtɛmpəɹəˈmɛntəl / (AmE), / ˌtɛmpərəˈmɛntəl / (BrE)
Relating to or determined by a person's temperament or inherent disposition.
relating to temperament or disposition

Examples

Her temperamental indifference to rules often caused friction with her teachers.
The artist's temperamental nature was evident in his unpredictable moods.
Some plants have temperamental requirements for sunlight and water.

Synset Examples

temperamental indifference to neatness
temperamental peculiarities

Etymology

From Latin temperamentum (mixture, temperament) via French tempéramental.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02821491-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˌtɛmpəɹəˈmɛnɾəl / (AmE), / ˌtɛmpərəˈmɛntəl / (BrE)
liable to unpredictable changes of mood or behavior; moody or volatile.
liable to mood swings

Examples

The director found the actor too temperamental to cast in the leading role.
Her temperamental nature made working with her challenging at times.
Although the car is powerful, it can be temperamental and difficult to start in cold weather.

Synset Examples

a temperamental opera singer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moody - often given to frequent changes of mood; sulky or temperamental
  • Antonym: stable - not likely to change suddenly; steady

Etymology

From Latin 'temperamentum' (proper mixture), via French 'tempérament'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00859512-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˌtɛmpərəˈmɛntəl / (AmE), / ˌtɛmpərəˈmɛntəl / (BrE)
liable to behave or perform unpredictably; subject to sudden changes in mood or function.
unpredictable in mood or function

Examples

The temperamental machine refused to work whenever we needed it most.
Our old car is so temperamental that you never know if it will start.
Her temperamental pet bird sometimes sings beautifully and other times refuses to make a sound.

Synset Examples

erratic winds are the bane of a sailor
a temperamental motor: sometimes it would start and sometimes it wouldn't
that beautiful but temperamental instrument the flute

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unpredictable - not able to be predicted; changeable in behavior or outcomes
  • Antonym: reliable - consistently good in quality or performance; able to be trusted

Etymology

From temperament, based on the Late Latin temperamentum (mixture), with the suffix -al.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00728671-s
Synset POS: s
