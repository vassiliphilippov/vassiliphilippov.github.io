to sift

1. VERB

/ sɪft / (AmE), / sɪft / (BrE)
To examine something thoroughly so as to distinguish and separate out what is important or useful.
examine and separate out

Examples

She had to sift through hundreds of applications to find the right candidate.
Investigators will sift the evidence to uncover the truth.
As I sift through the data, I look for any unusual patterns.

Synset Examples

sift through the job candidates

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sort - arrange or group according to type, class, or order
  • Synonym: filter - remove unwanted elements from a group or collection

Etymology

From Old English 'siftan', from 'sife' (sieve).

2. VERB

/ sɪft / (AmE), / sɪft / (BrE)
to put a substance through a sieve or similar device to separate finer parts from coarser ones
separate with a sieve

Examples

She sifts powdered sugar over the cake as a finishing touch.

3. VERB

/ sɪft / (AmE), / sɪft / (BrE)
to search or examine something thoroughly, especially in order to find what is important or useful
search or examine thoroughly

Examples

The investigators sifted through thousands of documents to find a single clue.
She sifted through old photographs in the attic, searching for her grandmother's portrait.
Rescue workers sifted through the debris after the storm, hoping to find survivors.

Synset Examples

The soldiers sifted through the woods

Related Terms

  • Synonym: search - to look carefully to find something
  • Antonym: skim - to read or glance through quickly

Etymology

From Old English *siftan* (to sift), related to 'sieve'.
