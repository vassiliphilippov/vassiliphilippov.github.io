to editorialize

1. VERB

/ ˌɛdɪˈtɔːriəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˌɛdɪˈtɔːrɪəlaɪz / (BrE)
to express personal opinions or bias, especially when expected to be neutral or objective, as in reporting or writing
insert personal opinions in reporting or writing

Examples

Journalists should avoid the temptation to editorialize in their news reports.
He tends to editorialize when summarizing political speeches, revealing his own viewpoints.
The professor warned not to editorialize in academic writing, which should remain impartial.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: report - to give a factual account without personal opinion

Etymology

Formed from the noun 'editorial' + '-ize'. 'Editorial' from Latin 'editor' (one who produces a written work).
