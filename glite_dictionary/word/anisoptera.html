Anisoptera

1. NOUN

/ ˌænɪˈsɑptərə / (AmE), / ˌænɪˈsɒptərə / (BrE)
A suborder of insects comprising the true dragonflies, characterized by having two pairs of strong, typically unequal wings and aquatic larvae.
suborder of true dragonflies

Examples

The biologist studied several species of Anisoptera in the wetlands during the summer.
Members of the Anisoptera are distinguished from damselflies by their wing structure and flight patterns.
The larvae of Anisoptera are aquatic and play an important role in freshwater ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dragonfly - a predatory insect with large wings and a long body

Etymology

From Greek 'anisos' (unequal) and 'pteron' (wing), referring to the shape of the wings.
