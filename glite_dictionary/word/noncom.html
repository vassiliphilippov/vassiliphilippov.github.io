noncom

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑnˌkɑm / (AmE), / ˈnɒnˌkɒm / (BrE)
a noncommissioned officer, a military officer appointed from enlisted personnel rather than commissioned ranks
noncommissioned military officer

Examples

After ten years of service, he was promoted to noncom and received more responsibility.
The noncom supervised his squad during the training exercise.
Many soldiers look up to their noncom for guidance and support.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: noncommissioned officer - a military officer appointed from enlisted personnel, not holding a commission

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'noncommissioned officer.'
