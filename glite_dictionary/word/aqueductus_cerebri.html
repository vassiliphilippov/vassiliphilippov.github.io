aqueductus cerebri

1. NOUN

/ ˌækwəˌdʌktəs səˈriːbraɪ / (AmE), / ˌakwɪˌdʌktəs səˈriːbrɪ / (BrE)
A narrow canal in the midbrain that connects the third and fourth ventricles, allowing the flow of cerebrospinal fluid.
canal connecting third and fourth brain ventricles
medical

Examples

A blockage in the aqueductus cerebri can cause hydrocephalus in infants.
The MRI scan showed a narrowing of the aqueductus cerebri.
The flow of cerebrospinal fluid through the aqueductus cerebri is essential for normal brain function.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cerebral aqueduct - alternative common English term for aqueductus cerebri

Etymology

From Latin 'aqueductus' (water channel) and 'cerebri' (of the brain).
