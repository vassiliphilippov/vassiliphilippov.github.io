Annamese

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈmiz / (AmE), / ˌænəˈmiːz / (BrE)
an old term for the Vietnamese language, belonging to the Mon-Khmer language family, spoken in Vietnam
old name for the Vietnamese language

Examples

During the colonial era, official documents were sometimes translated into Annamese for local use.
Linguists classified Annamese as part of the Mon-Khmer language family for historical reasons.
Few people today refer to Vietnamese as Annamese, as the term has become outdated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Vietnamese - the official language of Vietnam

Etymology

From French 'Annamite', referring to Annam, a historical region of Vietnam.

2. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈmiz / (AmE), / ˌanəˈmiːz / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of the region of Annam in Vietnam, especially in historical contexts; sometimes used to refer to a Vietnamese person in general
a native or inhabitant of Annam, Vietnam

Examples

The French colonial records often referred to the people as Annamese during the 19th century.
Many Annamese migrated to neighboring countries during times of unrest.
The traditional clothing worn by Annamese varied by social class and region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Vietnamese - a native or inhabitant of Vietnam

Etymology

From 'Annam,' a former region of central Vietnam, plus the English suffix '-ese' denoting nationality or language.
