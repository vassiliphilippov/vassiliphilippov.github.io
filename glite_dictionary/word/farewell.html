farewell

1. NOUN

/ fɛrˈwɛl / (AmE), / ˌfeəˈwɛl / (BrE)
an act of saying goodbye politely when leaving
polite act of saying goodbye

Examples

She waved a final farewell as the train pulled away from the station.
Their farewell was brief but heartfelt after the long meeting.
At the end of the dinner, he made a touching farewell speech to his colleagues.

Synset Examples

he disliked long farewells
he took his leave
parting is such sweet sorrow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goodbye - an expression or act of parting
  • Synonym: parting - the action or process of leaving

Etymology

From Middle English 'farewel', from 'fare well', meaning 'travel well' or 'be well'.

2. NOUN

/ ferˈwɛl / (AmE), / feəˈwɛl / (BrE)
an expression of good wishes used when leaving or parting from someone
expression of good wishes at parting

Examples

We said our farewells before she boarded the plane.
The entire office gathered to give him a heartfelt farewell.
They threw a party as his farewell to the company.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goodbye - expression used when parting

Etymology

From Middle English 'far wel', equivalent to 'fare well'.
