Lophophorus

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈfɒfərəs / (AmE), / ləˈfɒfərəs / (BrE)
A genus of brightly colored Himalayan pheasants known as monals, found in mountainous regions of Asia.
a genus of Himalayan pheasants (monals)

Examples

The Himalayas are home to several species of Lophophorus, prized for their iridescent plumage.
The male Lophophorus displays vibrant colors during the mating season.
Researchers observed a group of Lophophorus feeding at the edge of the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monal - a brightly colored Himalayan pheasant of the genus Lophophorus

Etymology

From Greek lophos (crest) + phoros (bearing), referring to the crested feature of these birds.
