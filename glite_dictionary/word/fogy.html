fogy

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊɡi / (AmE), / ˈfəʊɡi / (BrE)
a person whose ideas or style are considered old-fashioned or out of date
person with old-fashioned style or ideas

Examples

Tom is a bit of a fogy when it comes to modern technology.
The club was full of fogy members who resisted any change.
People called her a fogy because she still wore clothes from the 1970s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fogey - an old-fashioned person; usually spelled 'fogey' in modern usage

Etymology

Probably related to 'fogy', a dialectal variant of 'fogey', of unknown origin.
