to exclude

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈsklud / (AmE), / ɪkˈskluːd / (BrE)
to deliberately leave out or not include (someone or something)
to leave out or not include

Examples

The membership fee excludes taxes and other charges.
He felt excluded from the conversation by his colleagues.
The policy excludes any pre-existing medical conditions.

Synset Examples

The cost for the trip excludes food and beverages

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to include - to make someone or something part of a group or whole
  • Synonym: to omit - to leave out; fail to include

Etymology

From Latin 'excludere', from 'ex-' (out) + 'claudere' (to shut).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02639795-v
Synset POS: v

/ ɪkˈskluːd / (AmE), / ɪkˈskluːd / (BrE)
to deliberately prevent someone or something from being included, considered, or accepted
prevent from being included or considered

Examples

Certain foods are excluded from his diet for health reasons.
She felt hurt when she was excluded from the invitation list.

/ ɪkˈskluːd / (AmE), / ɪkˈskluːd / (BrE)
to prevent someone from entering a place, taking part in an activity, or being included in a group

Examples

The policy does not exclude anyone based on their background.
