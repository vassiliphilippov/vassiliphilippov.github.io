stirrer

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɜrər / (AmE), / ˈstɜːrə / (BrE)
a utensil or tool used for stirring liquids or mixtures
tool for stirring

Examples

She used a stirrer to mix sugar into her coffee.
The bartender grabbed a metal stirrer to blend the cocktail.
Plastic stirrers are often provided with takeaway drinks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spoon - a utensil consisting of a small shallow bowl on a handle, used for mixing or eating

Etymology

From 'stir' (to mix or agitate) + agent noun suffix '-er'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɪrɚ / (AmE), / ˈstɜːrə / (BrE)
a person who deliberately spreads rumors or causes trouble to provoke others
person who causes trouble or spreads rumors
informal

Examples

Don't be such a stirrer—there's no need to create drama where none exists.
She gained a reputation as a stirrer for constantly spreading gossip at work.
Every group has its stirrer, always trying to provoke arguments among friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: troublemaker - a person who causes difficulties or problems
  • Antonym: peacemaker - a person who tries to make peace or resolve conflict
