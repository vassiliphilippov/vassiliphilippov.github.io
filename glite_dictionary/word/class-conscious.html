class-conscious

1. ADJ

/ ˈklæs ˌkɑn.ʃəs / (AmE), / ˈklɑːs ˌkɒn.ʃəs / (BrE)
aware of or influenced by social or economic class distinctions, especially in a manner that acknowledges or emphasizes social hierarchy.
aware of social class distinctions

Examples

The new policy was criticized for appealing mainly to class-conscious voters.
Her class-conscious attitude shaped her interactions with coworkers from different backgrounds.
In a class-conscious society, social mobility can be difficult to achieve.

Synset Examples

American society is becoming increasingly stratified

Related Terms

  • Antonym: class-blind - unaware of or indifferent to social class distinctions

Etymology

Formed from 'class' (social division) and 'conscious' (aware), first used in late 19th to early 20th century social theory.
