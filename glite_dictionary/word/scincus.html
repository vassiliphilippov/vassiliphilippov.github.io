Scincus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋ.kəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋ.kəs / (BrE)
a genus of skinks, comprising certain species of lizards found in North Africa and southwestern Asia
a genus of skink lizards

Examples

The desert-dwelling Scincus has adapted to a life of burrowing in sand.
Researchers observed a group of Scincus moving swiftly across the dunes.
The genus Scincus includes the well-known sandfish lizard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skink - any lizard of the family Scincidae

Etymology

From Latin Scincus, from Ancient Greek skinkos (a kind of lizard).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋkəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋkəs / (BrE)
the type genus of the family Scincidae, comprising certain skinks, especially those native to North Africa and Asia.
genus of skinks (lizards)

Examples

The genus Scincus includes the well-known sandfish lizard found in desert regions.
Scientists discovered a new species belonging to the Scincus genus in North Africa.
Members of Scincus are recognized for their smooth, shiny scales and burrowing habits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skink - any lizard of the family Scincidae

Etymology

From Latin *Scincus*, from Greek *skinkos*, meaning type of lizard.
