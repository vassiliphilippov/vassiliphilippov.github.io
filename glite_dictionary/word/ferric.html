ferric

1. ADJ

/ ˈferɪk / (AmE), / ˈfɛrɪk / (BrE)
relating to or containing iron, especially in the trivalent (Fe3+) state.
relating to iron, especially trivalent state

Examples

The water contained high levels of ferric ions, making it unsafe to drink.
Researchers studied the reaction between the ferric compound and other chemicals.
Rust is formed when iron reacts with oxygen to become ferric oxide.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ferrous - relating to iron in the divalent (Fe2+) state

Etymology

From Latin ferrum (iron) + -ic (forming adjectives related to chemical elements).
