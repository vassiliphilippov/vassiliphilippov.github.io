bawbee

1. NOUN

/ bɔːˈbi / (AmE), / bɔːˈbiː / (BrE)
an old Scottish coin of low value, used especially in the 16th to 19th centuries
old low-value Scottish coin
archaic british

Examples

In the old days, a loaf of bread might cost just a single bawbee.
She found a rusty bawbee while digging in the garden.
Collectors at the fair traded rare bawbees and other Scottish coins.

Etymology

From Scots 'bawbee', possibly from the estate of Sillebawby in Fife, Scotland, where the coin was first minted.
