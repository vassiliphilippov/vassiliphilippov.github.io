prattler

1. NOUN

/ ˈprætələr / (AmE), / ˈprætlə / (BrE)
a person who talks in a childish, foolish, or inconsequential way; someone who chatters or babbles
someone who chatters childishly

Examples

The children were such prattlers, filling the room with endless giggles and stories.
He became annoyed with the prattler in the next seat who refused to be quiet.
As a prattler, she often distracted her classmates with her constant chatter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chatterbox - a person who talks a lot, especially about trivial matters
  • Synonym: babble - to speak rapidly and continuously in a foolish, excited, or incomprehensible way
  • Antonym: silent - not speaking or making any sound

Etymology

From 'prattle' (to talk in a childish way) + -er (agent suffix).
