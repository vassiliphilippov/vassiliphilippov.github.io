litchee

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːtʃi / (AmE), / ˈlɪtʃi / (BrE)
A small, round fruit with a thin, brittle shell, sweet white flesh, and a single seed, native to China and often eaten fresh or dried.
a sweet, jellylike Chinese fruit

Examples

The dessert was topped with fresh litchee pieces and a drizzle of honey.
She enjoys eating dried litchees as a sweet snack in the afternoon.
At the market, they sold both litchee and mangosteen from local orchards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lychee - the more common spelling for this fruit

Etymology

From Cantonese 荔枝 (lai6 zi1), sometimes rendered as 'litchee' in older English texts.
