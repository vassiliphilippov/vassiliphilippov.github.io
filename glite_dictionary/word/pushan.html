Pushan

1. PROPN

/ ˈpuːʃɑːn / (AmE), / ˈpuːʃɑːn / (BrE)
A Hindu god known as the celestial shepherd and conductor of souls to the afterlife.
Hindu shepherd and soul-conducting god

Examples

In ancient hymns, Pushan is praised as the protector of travelers and cattle.
Worshippers offer prayers to Pushan for guidance and safety during journeys.
The Rigveda describes Pushan as leading souls of the departed to the afterlife.

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'Pūṣan', meaning 'nourisher' or 'protector'.
