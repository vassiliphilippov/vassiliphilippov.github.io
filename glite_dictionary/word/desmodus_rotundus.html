Desmodus rotundus

1. NOUN

/ dɛzˈmoʊdəs roʊˈtʌndəs / (AmE), / dɛzˈməʊdəs rəʊˈtʌndəs / (BrE)
A small, tropical bat species of Central and South America that feeds on the blood of birds and mammals; commonly known as the common vampire bat.
species of blood-feeding bat

Examples

Desmodus rotundus is often studied due to its unique feeding behavior of drinking blood from mammals and birds.
Researchers captured several Desmodus rotundus bats to observe their role in spreading certain viruses to livestock.
Unlike other bats, Desmodus rotundus is highly specialized for hematophagy, or feeding on blood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vampire bat - Any bat that feeds on the blood of other animals

Etymology

From Latin: 'Desmodus' from Greek 'desmos' (bond) and 'odous' (tooth); 'rotundus' meaning round.
