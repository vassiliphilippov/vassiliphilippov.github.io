wrack

1. NOUN

/ ræk / (AmE), / ræk / (BrE)
dried seaweed, especially that which is cast ashore by the sea
dried seaweed cast ashore

Examples

The shore was strewn with wrack left behind by the retreating tide.
Children searched among the wrack for shells and small sea creatures.
The smell of drying wrack filled the coastal air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seaweed - a general term for marine algae

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *wrak* (something driven, seaweed).

2. NOUN

/ ræk / (AmE), / ræk / (BrE)
a growth of marine vegetation, especially large seaweeds such as rockweed and kelp, found along the seashore
type of large seaweed or marine vegetation

Examples

At low tide, the shore was covered with wrack and other marine plants.
Scientists studied the diversity of wrack along the rocky coastline.
Seabirds often forage among the wrack washed up on the beach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seaweed - a general term for marine algae, especially those growing on shores
  • Synonym: kelp - large brown seaweed of the order Laminariales
  • Synonym: rockweed - a type of brown seaweed growing on rocks at the shore

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *wrak* (something driven by the sea), related to wreck.

3. VERB

/ ræk / (AmE), / ræk / (BrE)
to smash or break forcefully; to destroy or damage severely
smash or break forcefully

Examples

The storm wracked the old barn, leaving little but debris.
Careless handling could easily wrack that fragile vase.
They feared the explosion would wrack the entire building beyond repair.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wreck - to destroy or severely damage

Etymology

Alteration of 'wreck', from Middle English 'wrek', from Old Norse 'reka' (to drive, push).
