dementedness

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈmɛntɪdnəs / (AmE), / dɪˈmɛntɪdnɪs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being demented, especially as characterized by mental deterioration of organic or functional origin
state of being demented; mental deterioration
medical

Examples

His increasing dementedness made it difficult for the family to care for him at home.
The doctor attributed the patient's behavior to the onset of dementedness.
Research into the causes of dementedness has helped develop new treatments for age-related cognitive decline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dementia - chronic or persistent mental disorder with memory loss and cognitive decline
  • Antonym: sanity - the state of having a healthy mind

Etymology

From 'demented' + '-ness', where 'demented' derives from Latin 'dementare' (to drive mad).
