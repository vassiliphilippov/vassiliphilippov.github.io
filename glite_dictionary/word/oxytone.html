oxytone

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑksɪˌtoʊn / (AmE), / ˈɒksɪtəʊn / (BrE)
A word in which the stress falls on the last syllable.
word with stress on final syllable

Examples

In the word 'café', the stress is on the final syllable, making it an oxytone.
Greek linguistics often classifies words as oxytone if the accent is on the last syllable.
Unlike an oxytone, a paroxytone has the stress on the second-to-last syllable.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: paroxytone - word with stress on the penultimate (second to last) syllable

Etymology

From Greek oxýtonos, from oxýs (sharp) + tónos (tone, accent).
