hackbut

1. NOUN

/ ˈhækˌbʌt / (AmE), / ˈhakbʊt / (BrE)
an obsolete type of firearm with a long barrel, used from the 15th to 17th centuries
obsolete firearm with a long barrel
archaic

Examples

The museum displayed a rare hackbut from the sixteenth century.
Soldiers fired their hackbuts in a volley during the reenactment.
While obsolete, the hackbut was a key weapon in early modern warfare.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arquebus - an early type of portable gun supported on a tripod or forked rest
  • Synonym: musket - a heavy, large-caliber firearm used before the invention of the rifle

Etymology

From French 'arquebuse', with alteration possibly by influence of 'hack', relating to early firearm terminology.
