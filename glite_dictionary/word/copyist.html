copyist

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.pi.ɪst / (AmE), / ˈkɒp.i.ɪst / (BrE)
a person who makes written copies of documents or manuscripts, especially by hand
person who makes written copies

Examples

The museum displayed several ancient manuscripts preserved by a skilled copyist.
Before the invention of the printing press, every book was painstakingly reproduced by a copyist.
The accuracy of the document depended on the attention to detail of the copyist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scribe - a person who copies out documents, especially in historical contexts
  • Synonym: transcriber - a person who puts speech or data into written or printed form

Etymology

From 'copy' + '-ist', indicating a person who copies.
