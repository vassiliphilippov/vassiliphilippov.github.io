chaperon

1. VERB

/ ˈʃæpəˌroʊn / (AmE), / ˈʃapərəʊn / (BrE)
to accompany and look after someone, typically a young person, to ensure their proper behavior, especially in social situations
to accompany as a guardian

Examples

The teacher agreed to chaperon the students on their trip to the museum.
Parents are often asked to chaperon school dances to supervise the children.
She chaperoned her younger brother during the neighborhood Halloween party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escort - to accompany someone somewhere, especially for protection or as a courtesy

Etymology

From French 'chaperon' (protector), originally meaning 'hood' or 'cowl'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃæpəˌrɑn / (AmE), / ˈʃapərɒn / (BrE)
A person, usually an older woman, who accompanies and supervises a young woman or a group of young people at social events to ensure proper behavior.
person supervising young people at events

Examples

The school required a chaperon to attend the overnight field trip.
At the dance, Mrs. Grant acted as a chaperon for the students.
She reluctantly agreed to serve as a chaperon at her niece’s birthday party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escort - a person accompanying another for protection or guidance
  • Synonym: guardian - someone who watches over or protects someone

Etymology

From French 'chaperon' meaning 'protector, guide', originally 'hood, cape'.

Synset POS: n
