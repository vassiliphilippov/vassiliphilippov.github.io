peccable

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɛkəbəl / (AmE), / ˈpɛkəbəl / (BrE)
Capable of sinning; liable to make moral errors or commit sins.
liable to sin; capable of sinning

Examples

In some theological traditions, only humans are considered peccable beings.
The philosopher argued that all rulers are peccable and should be held accountable.
Unlike most deities, the mythical hero was portrayed as peccable and prone to human failings.

Synset Examples

a frail and peccable mortal

Related Terms

  • Antonym: impeccable - not liable to sin; free from fault

Etymology

From Latin peccābilis (liable to sin), from peccāre (to sin).
