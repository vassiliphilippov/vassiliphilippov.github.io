motherboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌðɚˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈmʌðəˌbɔːd / (BrE)
the main circuit board in a computer, containing the principal components and connectors for other devices
main circuit board in a computer

Examples

The technician carefully installed the new motherboard in the desktop computer.
If the motherboard fails, the entire computer may stop working.
Modern motherboards support a wide range of processors and memory types.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mainboard - another term for the main circuit board in a computer

Etymology

From 'mother' + 'board', referring metaphorically to its central function.
