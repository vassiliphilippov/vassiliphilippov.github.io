to foment

1. VERB

/ foʊˈmɛnt / (AmE), / fəʊˈmɛnt / (BrE)
to try to cause or encourage the development of something negative, especially trouble or violence
to stir up or incite trouble or unrest

Examples

The rebel leaders hoped to foment unrest among the population.
Social media was used by some groups to foment public outrage against the new law.
His inflammatory speech was intended to foment discord within the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to incite - to encourage or stir up violent or unlawful behavior
  • Antonym: to suppress - to put an end to something, especially forcibly

Etymology

From Latin fomentare (to warm, foment), from fomentum (a poultice, application), ultimately from fovere (to warm).

2. VERB

/ foʊˈmɛnt / (AmE), / fəʊˈmɛnt / (BrE)
to bathe a part of the body with warm water or medicated lotions, especially as a treatment for pain or inflammation; now rare
bathe with warm water or medicated lotions (rare)
archaic medical

Examples

The doctor instructed the nurse to foment the injured ankle twice daily.
In the past, people would foment sore muscles using herbal infusions.
The physician recommended that his wounds be fomented with warm compresses.

Synset Examples

His legs should be fomented

Etymology

From Latin 'fomentum' (a poultice, application), from 'fovēre' (to warm, cherish).
