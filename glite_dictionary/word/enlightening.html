enlightening

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈlaɪtnɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈlaɪtənɪŋ / (BrE)
giving greater knowledge and understanding about a subject or situation, especially to encourage intellectual or moral improvement
providing insight or knowledge

Examples

Her speech was truly enlightening and changed the way I thought about the issue.
The museum tour was enlightening, revealing aspects of history I had never imagined.
I always find his lectures to be both entertaining and enlightening.

Synset Examples

the paintings in the church served an edifying purpose even for those who could not read

Related Terms

  • Synonym: edifying - providing moral or intellectual instruction
  • Synonym: instructive - useful and informative
  • Antonym: misleading - giving the wrong idea or impression

Etymology

From the verb 'enlighten', from Old English 'inlēhtan', meaning 'to shed light upon'.

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈlaɪtnɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈlaɪtənɪŋ / (BrE)
giving new information or insight that increases understanding or dispels ignorance.
giving insight or increasing understanding

Examples

The professor gave an enlightening explanation of quantum mechanics that everyone understood.
Her trip to Asia was truly enlightening and changed her perspective on life.
Reading about ancient civilizations proved to be an enlightening experience for the students.

Synset Examples

an enlightening glimpse of government in action
an illuminating lecture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illuminating - helping to clarify or explain; giving new insight

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'enlighten' plus the adjective-forming suffix '-ing'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01328729-a
Synset POS: a
