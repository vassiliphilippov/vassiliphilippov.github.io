romanticist

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈmæntɪsɪst / (AmE), / rəʊˈmantɪsɪst / (BrE)
an artist, writer, or thinker who is a follower of the Romantic Movement or influenced by Romanticism, especially in art, literature, or philosophy
a follower or artist of the Romantic Movement

Examples

The painter was a leading romanticist in nineteenth-century Europe.
As a romanticist, she believed in the power of emotion over reason.
Many romanticists sought inspiration from nature and the sublime.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: romantic - having characteristics of the Romantic Movement in art, literature, or philosophy

Etymology

From 'romantic' + '-ist', denoting someone who practices or is a follower of a movement.

2. NOUN

/ roʊˈmæntɪsɪst / (AmE), / rəʊˈmæntɪsɪst / (BrE)
a person who is inclined to indulge in excessive sentimentality or idealism, especially regarding love, beauty, or art.
person indulging in sentimentality or idealism

Examples

As a true romanticist, she cherished handwritten letters and long walks under the stars.
The novel's protagonist is a romanticist who views the world through rose-colored glasses.
Some critics dismissed him as a mere romanticist, unable to face the harsher aspects of reality.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: realist - a person who accepts a situation as it is and is prepared to deal with it accordingly

Etymology

From 'romantic' + '-ist', after the artistic and literary movement Romanticism.
