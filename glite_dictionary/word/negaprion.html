Negaprion

1. NOUN

/ ˌniːɡəˈpraɪən / (AmE), / ˌniːɡəˈpraɪən / (BrE)
A genus of large requiem sharks, commonly known as lemon sharks, found in shallow subtropical waters.
genus of lemon sharks

Examples

The genus Negaprion includes the well-known lemon shark found in coastal regions.
Researchers observed a group of Negaprion swimming near the coral reef.
Juvenile Negaprion are often studied to understand shark behavior and ecology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lemon shark - a large yellowish shark found in subtropical waters, of the genus Negaprion

Etymology

From Greek 'nega' (unknown origin) and 'prion' (saw), alluding to features of some sharks, coined in scientific Latin nomenclature.

2. NOUN

/ ˌnɛɡəˈpraɪən / (AmE), / ˌnɛɡəˈpraɪ.ən / (BrE)
A genus of large, yellowish-brown sharks commonly known as lemon sharks, found in warm coastal waters.
genus of lemon sharks

Examples

The Negaprion genus includes the well-known lemon shark, prevalent in subtropical waters.
Many scientists study Negaprion to understand shark behavior and coastal ecosystems.
Juvenile members of the Negaprion genus are often found in shallow nurseries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lemon shark - a large yellowish-brown shark of warm coastal waters, belonging to the genus Negaprion

Etymology

From Greek 'nega-' (unknown origin) and '-prion' meaning 'saw', referring to the dentition.
