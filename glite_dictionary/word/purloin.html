to purloin

1. VERB

/ pɚˈlɔɪn / (AmE), / pəˈlɔɪn / (BrE)
to steal or take something, especially in a secret or dishonest way
to steal or take secretly

Examples

He managed to purloin several confidential documents from the office.
A mischievous child tried to purloin a cookie from the kitchen before dinner.
The thief purloined her jewelry while she was out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steal - to take something that does not belong to you without permission
  • Synonym: to pilfer - to steal things of little value or in small amounts, often repeatedly

Etymology

From Old French *porloigner* (to remove), from Latin *prolongare* (to prolong, remove far off).
