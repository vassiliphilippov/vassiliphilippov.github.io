Jerevan

1. PROPN

/ jɛrəˈvɑn / (AmE), / jɛrəˈvæn / (BrE)
the capital and largest city of Armenia, located on the Hrazdan River in the west of the country
capital city of Armenia

Examples

Jerevan became Armenia's capital in 1918 after the collapse of the Russian Empire.
The architecture of Jerevan reflects a blend of Soviet-era structures and modern designs.
She traveled from Tbilisi to Jerevan to attend a cultural festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yerevan - capital of Armenia (alternative spelling)

Etymology

From Armenian Երեւան (Yerevan).
