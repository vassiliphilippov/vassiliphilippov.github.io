fecundity

1. NOUN

/ fɪˈkʌndəti / (AmE), / fɪˈkʌndɪti / (BrE)
The ability to produce abundant growth or offspring; the quality of being fertile or productive.
ability to produce abundant growth or offspring

Examples

The fecundity of the rainforest allows countless plants and animals to thrive.
Her imagination was remarkable for its fecundity and inventiveness.
The fecundity of the soil made the region ideal for farming.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fertility - the ability to produce offspring or fruit
  • Antonym: infertility - the inability to produce offspring or fruit

Etymology

From Latin 'fecunditas' meaning 'fruitfulness, fertility', from 'fecundus' (fruitful).

3. NOUN

/ fɪˈkʌndəti / (AmE), / fɪˈkʌndɪti / (BrE)
the ability to produce an abundance of offspring or new growth; fertility
fertility; ability to produce offspring

Examples

The fecundity of the region's soil allows for abundant crop harvests year after year.
Certain fish species are known for their remarkable fecundity, producing thousands of eggs in a single season.
Her fecundity as a writer was evident in the numerous novels and poems she published throughout her career.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fertility - the quality of being able to produce offspring or fruit
  • Antonym: infertility - the inability to produce offspring or new growth

Etymology

From Latin 'fecunditas', from 'fecundus' (fruitful, fertile).
