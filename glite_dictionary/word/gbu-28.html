GBU-28

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒiː biː juː ˈtwɛnti eɪt / (AmE), / ˌdʒiː bjuː ˈtwɛnti eɪt / (BrE)
a 5,000-pound laser-guided bomb designed to penetrate hardened underground facilities before detonating
a laser-guided bunker-busting bomb

Examples

The military deployed a GBU-28 to destroy the underground command bunker.
Engineers designed the GBU-28 to penetrate thick concrete before detonating.
Reports confirmed that the GBU-28 was instrumental in the operation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bunker buster - bomb designed to penetrate and destroy hardened structures

Etymology

Acronym for 'Guided Bomb Unit 28'.
