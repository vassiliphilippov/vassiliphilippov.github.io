sceptre

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛptər / (AmE), / ˈsɛptə / (BrE)
Imperial or royal authority symbolized by a ceremonial staff, especially in monarchies.
imperial authority symbolized by a staff

Examples

The new king ascended the throne and claimed the sceptre of his ancestors.
After the revolution, the sceptre no longer held its former power over the people.
Their constitution abolished the sceptre as a symbol of central imperial authority.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crown - a symbol of monarchy or royal power

Etymology

From Old French 'ceptre', from Latin 'sceptrum', from Greek 'skeptron' (staff, baton).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛp.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɛp.tə / (BrE)
a ceremonial staff carried by a ruler as a symbol of authority
ceremonial staff of authority
british

Examples

The king held the golden sceptre as he addressed his subjects.
During the coronation, the queen was presented with an ornate sceptre.
In many cultures, a sceptre symbolizes royal power and legitimacy.

Related Terms

From Old French sceptre, from Latin sceptrum, from Greek skeptron (staff, baton).
