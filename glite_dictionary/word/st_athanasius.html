St. Athanasius

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt æθəˈneɪʃəs / (AmE), / seɪnt æθəˈneɪsiəs / (BrE)
A Greek patriarch of Alexandria (c. 293–373), a major church father who defended Christian orthodoxy against Arianism and is recognized as a saint and Doctor of the Church.
Greek patriarch and Christian theologian, c. 293–373

Examples

St. Athanasius was renowned for his vigorous defense of Christian doctrine against Arian beliefs.
The writings of St. Athanasius are still influential among theologians today.
Many churches around the world celebrate the feast day of St. Athanasius on May 2.

Etymology

Athanasius from Greek Athanasios, meaning 'immortal.'
