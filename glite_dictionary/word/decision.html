decision

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsɪʒ.ən / (AmE), / dɪˈsɪʒ.ən / (BrE)
the quality of being resolute and determined in making choices or taking action; firmness of character or purpose
resoluteness or firmness of purpose

Examples

Her decision was evident in the way she calmly led the team through the crisis.
He approached every challenge with a level of decision that inspired those around him.
In leadership, a clear sense of decision is often more valuable than technical expertise.

Synset Examples

a man of unusual decisiveness

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indecision - the inability to make decisions quickly or firmly
  • Synonym: determination - the quality of being firm and resolute

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈsɪʒən / (AmE), / dɪˈsɪʒən / (BrE)
a conclusion or judgment reached after careful consideration; a choice made after thinking through options
a conclusion or judgment after consideration

Examples

It's difficult to make a good decision without all the relevant facts.
Her decision to move abroad surprised everyone in her family.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: determination - the act of coming to a decision or making up one's mind

3. NOUN

/ dɪˈsɪʒ.ən / (AmE), / dɪˈsɪʒ.ən / (BrE)

Examples

He was disappointed to lose by split decision after such a close fight.
She celebrated her unanimous decision win and thanked her coaches for their support.
