caliper

1. VERB

/ ˈkælɪpər / (AmE), / ˈkælɪpə / (BrE)
to measure the diameter or thickness of something using calipers
measure with calipers

Examples

The technician calipered the metal rod to ensure it met the required thickness.
She carefully calipers each sample before recording the measurements.
He calipered the bone fragments for the lab’s research project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gauge - to measure or estimate, often using an instrument

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæləpər / (AmE), / ˈkælɪpə / (BrE)
an instrument used for measuring the distance between two opposite sides of an object, often with precise accuracy
instrument for measuring distances

Examples

The mechanic used a caliper to check the diameter of the piston.
Digital calipers provide highly accurate measurements for engineering tasks.
Before ordering the replacement part, she measured the bolt with a caliper.

Etymology

From Latin *caliper* (instrument for measuring), possibly influenced by French *calibre* (standard, measure).
