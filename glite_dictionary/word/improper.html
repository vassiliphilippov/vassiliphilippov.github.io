improper

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈprɑːpɚ / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɒpə / (BrE)
not suitable, right, or appropriate for a particular situation or purpose
not suitable or appropriate

Examples

It would be improper to speak during the ceremony.
Wearing jeans to the interview was considered improper.
The manager warned the employee about improper use of company resources.

Synset Examples

slightly improper to dine alone with a married man
improper medication
improper attire for the golf course

Related Terms

  • Antonym: proper - suitable or appropriate for a particular purpose

Etymology

From Latin 'improprius' (not appropriate, not proper).

2. ADJ

/ ɪmˈprɑːpər / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɒpə / (BrE)
not suitable or appropriate for a particular purpose or occasion; not in accordance with accepted standards
not suitable or appropriate

Examples

Wearing jeans to a formal wedding is considered improper attire.
He was dismissed for improper conduct during the meeting.
Making jokes during the serious ceremony was highly improper.

Synset Examples

said all the wrong things

Related Terms

  • Antonym: appropriate - suitable or proper in the circumstances
  • Synonym: unsuitable - not appropriate to a particular occasion or use

Etymology

From Latin 'improprius' meaning 'not suitable, not one's own'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00137256-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ɪmˈprɑːpər / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɒpə / (BrE)
not conforming to accepted standards of legality, morality, or social convention; unsuitable or inappropriate in behavior, action, or practice
not conforming to accepted standards; inappropriate

Examples

The employee was dismissed for improper conduct in the workplace.
It is considered improper to speak loudly during a formal ceremony.
The company faced penalties for using improper accounting methods.

Synset Examples

an unconventional marriage
improper banking practices

Related Terms

  • Antonym: proper - following accepted rules or standards
  • Synonym: inappropriate - not suitable or proper in the circumstances

Etymology

From Latin 'improprius' (not proper), from 'in-' (not) + 'proprius' (one's own, proper).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01968236-s
Synset POS: s
