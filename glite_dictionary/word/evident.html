evident

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛvɪdənt / (AmE), / ˈɛvɪdənt / (BrE)
clearly seen or understood; obvious or apparent to the mind or senses.
obvious, clearly seen or understood

Examples

Her disappointment was evident to everyone in the room.
It became evident that the company needed to change its strategy.
The tension in his voice was evident, even though he tried to hide it.

Synset Examples

the effects of the drought are apparent to anyone who sees the parched fields
evident hostility
manifest disapproval
patent advantages
made his meaning plain
it is plain that he is no reactionary
in plain view
a palpable lie

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obvious - easily perceived or understood; clear, self-evident
  • Synonym: apparent - clearly visible or understood; obvious
  • Antonym: hidden - not visible or easily detected; concealed

Etymology

From Latin 'evidens' (clear, obvious).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɛvɪdənt / (AmE), / ˈɛvɪdənt / (BrE)
Clearly seen or understood; obvious or easily noticed.
obvious; easy to see or notice

Examples

The benefits of regular exercise became evident after a few weeks.
His nervousness was evident during the interview.
It was evident from her smile that she was pleased with the results.

Synset Examples

a discernible change in attitude
a clearly evident erasure in the manuscript
an observable change in behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obvious - easily perceived or understood; clear, self-evident
  • Synonym: apparent - clearly visible or understood; obvious
  • Antonym: hidden - not visible or easily noticed

Etymology

From Latin 'evidens', 'evidentis' (clear, obvious).
