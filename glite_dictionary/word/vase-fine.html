vase-fine

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪsˌfaɪn / (AmE), / ˈvɑːzˌfaɪn / (BrE)
A scandent subshrub native to the southeastern United States, noted for its large, red-purple, bell-shaped flowers with leathery, recurved sepals.
plant with bell-shaped red-purple flowers

Examples

The vase-fine added a burst of color to their garden all summer long.
Botanists admire the unique leathery sepals of the vase-fine.
Hummingbirds are often drawn to the vibrant flowers of the vase-fine.
