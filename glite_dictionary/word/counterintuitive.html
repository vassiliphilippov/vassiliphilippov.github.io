counterintuitive

1. ADJ

/ ˌkaʊntərɪnˈtuːɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntərɪnˈtjuːɪtɪv / (BrE)
Contrary to what one would expect or to common sense; not intuitive.
not what common sense would suggest

Examples

It may seem counterintuitive, but adding salt to ice makes it melt faster.
Her counterintuitive solution solved the problem more quickly than anyone expected.
Eating more to lose weight sounds counterintuitive, but in some diets, it works.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intuitive - easy to understand without teaching; instinctive

Etymology

Formed from 'counter-' (against) and 'intuitive' (from Latin *intueri*: to look at, contemplate).
