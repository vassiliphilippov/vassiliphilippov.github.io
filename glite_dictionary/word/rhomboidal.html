rhomboidal

1. ADJ

/ rɑmˈbɔɪdəl / (AmE), / rɒmˈbɔɪdəl / (BrE)
having the shape of a rhombus or rhomboid
shaped like a rhombus or rhomboid

Examples

The crystals formed rhomboidal patterns on the surface of the rock.
She drew a rhomboidal design for her geometry homework.
Certain minerals naturally break into rhomboidal shapes.

Synset Examples

rhomboidal shapes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhombic - having the shape of a rhombus

Etymology

From Latin rhomboidalis, from Greek rhomboeidēs (rhomboid-shaped), from rhombos (rhombus).
