lately

1. ADV

/ ˈleɪtli / (AmE), / ˈleɪtli / (BrE)
recently; in the period just before the present.
recently; in the recent past

Examples

Lately, she has been feeling much more optimistic about her future.
Have you noticed any changes in your neighborhood lately?
I've been working extra hours lately to finish the project.

Synset Examples

he was in Paris recently
lately the rules have been enforced
as late as yesterday she was fine
feeling better of late
the spelling was first affected, but latterly the meaning also

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recently - at a recent time; not long ago
  • Antonym: formerly - in the past; previously

Etymology

From Middle English 'lateli' (recently), from 'late' + '-ly'.
