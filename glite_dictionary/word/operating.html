operating

1. ADJ

/ ˈɑːpəˌreɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɒpəˌreɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a system, business, service, etc.) currently functioning or in effect.
currently functioning or in effect

Examples

The factory has three operating assembly lines running day and night.
During the holidays, there will be limited operating hours at the clinic.
Even with the renovation, the museum remains operating for visitors.

Synset Examples

de facto apartheid is still operational even in the ‘new’ African nations
bus service is in operation during the emergency
the company had several operating divisions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: operational - in use or ready for use; functioning
  • Antonym: inactive - not working or not in operation

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'operate', from Latin 'operari' (to work).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɑː.pəˌreɪ.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɒp.əˌreɪ.tɪŋ / (BrE)
relating to or involved in a particular process, activity, or operation, especially as part of an ongoing activity
relating to a process or operation

Examples

The operating manual explains how to use the machine safely.
We must monitor the operating temperature to prevent overheating.
The company reported lower operating costs this year.

Synset Examples

the operating conditions of the oxidation pond

Related Terms

  • Synonym: functional - serving a purpose or function; working correctly
