photogravure

1. NOUN

/ ˌfoʊtəˈɡrævjʊr / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəˈɡrævjʊə / (BrE)
A printing process in which a photographic image is transferred to a metal plate and then used to make high-quality prints.
printing process using photographic techniques

Examples

The museum displayed several original photogravure prints from the early twentieth century.
He learned the photogravure process to reproduce his photographs with exceptional detail and tone.
Many artists appreciate photogravure for its ability to capture subtle gradations of light and shadow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heliogravure - a similar method for photomechanical printing

Etymology

From French 'photogravure', from 'photo-' (light) + 'gravure' (engraving).

2. NOUN

/ ˌfoʊtəɡrəˈvjʊr / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəɡrəˈvjʊə / (BrE)
a printing process using a metal plate produced by photographic methods, for making high-quality reproductions of photographs and artwork
printing process from photographic intaglio plate

Examples

The artist chose photogravure to reproduce his detailed etchings for a limited-edition book.
Early 20th-century magazines often featured photographs printed by photogravure for their rich tonal range.
She admired the sharp detail and depth of tone achieved with the photogravure process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intaglio - a printing technique where the image is incised into a surface

Etymology

From French 'photogravure', from 'photo-' (light) + 'gravure' (engraving).

3. NOUN

/ ˌfoʊtəˈɡrævjʊr / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəˈɡrævjʊə / (BrE)
an intaglio printing process in which a photographic image is transferred to a metal plate and then etched, or a print produced by this process
intaglio print made using photogravure process

Examples

The museum displayed a beautiful photogravure of an early 20th-century cityscape.
He collected rare books featuring original photogravures of landscapes.
The artist chose photogravure for its rich tonal quality and fine detail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gravure - intaglio printing process involving etching into a plate
  • Synonym: etching - printmaking technique using acid to cut into a metal surface

Etymology

From French 'photogravure', from 'photo-' (light) + 'gravure' (engraving).
