family Grossulariaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡrɒsjʊˌlɛriˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌɡrɒsjʊˌlɑːrɪˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants, sometimes separated from the Saxifragaceae, whose members produce berries; includes currants and gooseberries.
a plant family including currants and gooseberries

Examples

The family Grossulariaceae includes popular fruits such as currants and gooseberries.
Botanists debate whether to classify these plants within the family Grossulariaceae or Saxifragaceae.
Species in the family Grossulariaceae are mostly shrubs bearing edible berries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Saxifragaceae - a related family sometimes considered to include Grossulariaceae
  • Synonym: currant - a berry-bearing plant in the family Grossulariaceae

Etymology

From the genus name Grossularia, Latinized from 'grossular', referring to gooseberry.
