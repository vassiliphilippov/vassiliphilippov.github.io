to malversate

1. VERB

/ ˈmæl.vɚ.seɪt / (AmE), / ˈmæl.və.seɪt / (BrE)
to fraudulently appropriate property or funds entrusted to one's care for one's own use
fraudulently misuse entrusted property
archaic legal

Examples

The corrupt official was discovered to have malversated public funds over many years.
Historians revealed that the treasurer had malversated charitable donations.
Anyone who malversates should face strict legal consequences.

Synset Examples

The accountant embezzled thousands of dollars while working for the wealthy family

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to embezzle - to steal or misuse money placed in one's trust

Etymology

From French 'malverser', from Medieval Latin 'malversari' meaning 'to behave improperly'.
