caliculus

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɪkjələs / (AmE), / kæˈlɪkjʊləs / (BrE)
a small cup-shaped structure in anatomy or zoology, such as a taste bud, optic cup, or the cavity in coral containing a polyp
small cup-shaped anatomical structure
medical

Examples

The tongue is covered with numerous caliculi that house the taste buds.
Each coral polyp resides within its own caliculus in the coral skeleton.
During development, the optic caliculus forms as a cup-shaped outgrowth from the brain.

Etymology

From Latin caliculus (diminutive of calix, meaning 'cup').
