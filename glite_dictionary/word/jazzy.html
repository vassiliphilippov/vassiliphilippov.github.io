jazzy

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒæzi / (AmE), / ˈdʒazi / (BrE)
having qualities characteristic of jazz, especially in rhythm or style; lively and bright in appearance, sound, or manner
lively or resembling jazz

Examples

The band played a jazzy tune that got everyone dancing.
She wore a jazzy red jacket to brighten up her outfit.
His piano solos are always jazzy and energetic, full of rhythm and life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lively - full of life and energy
  • Antonym: dull - lacking interest or excitement

Etymology

From jazz + -y, first recorded in the early 20th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˈdʒæzi / (AmE), / ˈdʒazi / (BrE)
Brightly or showily stylish and attracting attention, especially in reference to clothing or appearance.
brightly stylish; conspicuously showy

Examples

She wore a jazzy red scarf that made her stand out in the crowd.
The new restaurant has a jazzy interior with bold colors and patterns.
He prefers jazzy ties to add some flair to his suits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flashy - showy in a way that attracts attention
  • Antonym: subdued - not bright or showy; restrained

Etymology

From 'jazz', referring to the vibrant style and music, + '-y' forming an adjective.
