to mystify

1. VERB

/ ˈmɪstəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɪstɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to make something mysterious or obscure; to render difficult to understand or explain
make mysterious or obscure

Examples

The author tried to mystify the plot by adding unexpected twists.
Complex language can sometimes mystify a simple concept.
The painting was deliberately designed to mystify its viewers.

Synset Examples

mystify the story

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obscure - to make unclear or difficult to see or understand
  • Antonym: to clarify - to make clear or easier to understand

Etymology

From French *mystifier*, from *mystère* (mystery), ultimately from Greek *mystērion*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00519733-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈmɪstəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɪstɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to confuse or puzzle someone by making something difficult to understand
to puzzle or bewilder

Examples

The magician's tricks mystified the entire audience, leaving them wondering how he did it.
Her sudden change in attitude mystifies me; I can't figure out what's wrong.
Scientists were mystified by the strange results of the experiment.

Synset Examples

This beats me!
Got me — I don't know the answer!
a vexing problem
This question really stuck me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baffle - to confuse completely; to puzzle
  • Synonym: to bewilder - to cause someone to become perplexed and confused
  • Antonym: to clarify - to make (an idea or statement) clear

Etymology

From French mystifier, based on mystère (mystery).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00624056-v
Synset POS: v
