duette

1. NOUN

/ duˈɛt / (AmE), / djuˈɛt / (BrE)
A performance or piece for two performers or singers who perform together.
two performers or singers together

Examples

The talented sisters gave a beautiful duette at the concert last night.
They rehearsed their duette until every note was perfectly synchronized.
The musical included a touching duette between the two lead characters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duet - a performance by two people, especially singers or musicians

Etymology

From Italian 'duetto', diminutive of 'duo' (two).

2. NOUN

/ duːˈɛt / (AmE), / djuːˈɛt / (BrE)
a musical composition designed for two performers, typically singers or instrumentalists
musical piece for two performers

Examples

The violinists played a beautiful duette at the concert last night.
She composed a duette for piano and cello.
The competition required each pair to perform a classical duette.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duet - a piece of music for two performers

Etymology

From Italian 'duetto', diminutive of 'duo' (two).
