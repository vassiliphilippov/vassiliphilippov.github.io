HSV-2

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪtʃ ˌɛs ˈvi tu / (AmE), / ˌeɪtʃ ˌɛs ˈviː ˈtuː / (BrE)
Herpes simplex virus type 2; a virus that commonly causes genital herpes.
herpes virus causing genital herpes
medical

Examples

A blood test can detect HSV-2 even when no symptoms are present.
Most cases of recurrent genital herpes are caused by HSV-2.
Doctors typically prescribe antiviral medication to manage HSV-2 infections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herpes simplex virus - A family of viruses causing herpes infections

Etymology

Abbreviation of Herpes simplex virus type 2.
