to extricate

1. VERB

/ ˈɛk.strɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛk.strɪ.keɪt / (BrE)
to free someone or something from a difficult or complicated situation or from something that limits movement
to free from a difficult situation

Examples

She managed to extricate herself from the awkward conversation.
Firefighters worked quickly to extricate victims from the wrecked car.
He struggled to extricate his foot from the tangled rope.

Synset Examples

I cannot extricate myself from this task

Related Terms

  • Synonym: free - to release from captivity or confinement
  • Antonym: to entangle - to become caught or twisted together

Etymology

From Latin extricatus, past participle of extricare (to disentangle), from ex- (out) + tricae (perplexities).
