Diospyros

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈɑspɪrɒs / (AmE), / dɪˈɒspɪrɒs / (BrE)
A genus of trees and shrubs, including the ebony and persimmon, valued for their attractive and durable wood.
genus of ebony and persimmon trees

Examples

The Diospyros genus includes species that produce some of the world's most valuable hardwoods.
Botanists recently discovered a new species of Diospyros in the tropical rainforest.
Some types of Diospyros yield the delicious fruit known as persimmon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ebony - dense black hardwood from trees in the genus Diospyros
  • Synonym: persimmon - edible fruit from species in the genus Diospyros

Etymology

From Greek *diospyros*, meaning 'fruit of Zeus' (from *dios* 'of Zeus' + *pyros* 'wheat, grain, or fruit').

2. NOUN

/ daɪˈɑspɪrˌoʊs / (AmE), / dɪˈɒspɪrɒs / (BrE)
A genus of trees or shrubs known for their attractive and valuable wood, including species such as ebony and persimmon.
genus of trees with valuable wood

Examples

The wood of the Diospyros genus is highly valued for making fine furniture and instruments.
Several species in Diospyros produce edible fruits, such as the common persimmon.
Botanists are studying the diversity within the Diospyros group to improve conservation efforts.

Etymology

From Greek 'diospyros', meaning 'divine pear' or 'fruit of Zeus'.
