Prinia

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪniə / (AmE), / ˈprɪniə / (BrE)
a genus of small, insectivorous songbirds in the family Sylviidae, commonly known as wren-warblers, found in parts of Asia and Africa
genus of small birds called wren-warblers

Examples

The Prinia can often be seen flitting through tall grasses in search of insects.
Several species of Prinia display striking variations in plumage during the breeding season.
Researchers observed a nest of the Prinia cleverly hidden among reeds.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type species Prinia gracilis.

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɪniə / (AmE), / ˈprɪniə / (BrE)
A genus of small, insect-eating birds in the family Sylviidae, commonly known as wren-warblers, found in Africa and Asia.
genus of small birds called wren-warblers

Examples

Several species within the Prinia genus are known for their distinctive songs and long tails.
The graceful movements of the Prinia can often be observed in grasslands across India.
Researchers discovered a new species of Prinia in the dense forests of Southeast Asia.

Etymology

From modern Latin, coined as the scientific name for this genus.
