Calostoma cinnabarina

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæl.əˈstoʊ.mə ˌsɪn.ə.bəˈraɪ.nə / (AmE), / ˌkæl.əˈstɒ.mə ˌsɪn.ə.bəˈraɪ.nə / (BrE)
A species of gasteromycete fungus with a leathery stalk and a globose fruiting body containing a red spore case.
a type of gasteromycete fungus

Examples

During the hike, we spotted a rare Calostoma cinnabarina growing among the mossy logs.
Mycology students were excited to study the distinctive red spore case of Calostoma cinnabarina.
The leathery stalk and globular head make Calostoma cinnabarina easy to identify in its natural habitat.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining Greek 'kalos' (beautiful), 'stoma' (mouth), and Latin 'cinnabarinus' (red, like cinnabar).
