smidgeon

1. NOUN

/ ˈsmɪdʒən / (AmE), / ˈsmɪdʒɪn / (BrE)
A very small or barely noticeable amount of something.
a tiny amount

Examples

Could I have just a smidgeon of sugar in my tea, please?
The soup only needs a smidgeon of salt to enhance the flavor.
She showed not even a smidgeon of interest in the conversation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bit - a small piece or amount
  • Synonym: trace - a very small quantity
  • Synonym: smidgen - an alternative spelling meaning a tiny amount

Etymology

Probably a diminutive of 'smidgen', whose origin is uncertain, possibly Scots.
