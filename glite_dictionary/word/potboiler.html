potboiler

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑtˌbɔɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈpɒtˌbɔɪlə / (BrE)
A book, play, or other artistic work produced quickly and of low quality, intended mainly to earn money for its creator.
inferior work made for profit

Examples

The author's latest potboiler sold well despite its predictable plot and shallow characters.
Critics dismissed the film as a forgettable potboiler made only to cash in on a popular trend.
She admitted that her early novels were written as potboilers to pay the bills.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: masterpiece - a work of outstanding artistry or skill

Etymology

From 'potboil', referencing making enough money to keep the pot boiling (i.e., to survive), first used in the 19th century.
