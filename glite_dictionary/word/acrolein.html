acrolein

1. NOUN

/ əˈkroʊliɪn / (AmE), / əˈkrəʊliːɪn / (BrE)
a pungent, colorless, unsaturated liquid aldehyde used mainly as an intermediate in chemical manufacturing and as a biocide
a pungent colorless liquid aldehyde
medical

Examples

Acrolein is produced during the combustion of organic matter, making it a common air pollutant in industrial areas.
Researchers studied the effects of acrolein exposure on human respiratory health.
Due to its toxicity, handling acrolein requires special safety precautions in laboratories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aldehyde - any compound containing the -CHO group

Etymology

From French *acroléine*, from Greek *akros* (highest) + Latin *oleum* (oil), referring to its production from oil.
