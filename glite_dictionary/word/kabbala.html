Kabbala

1. NOUN

/ kəˈbɑːlə / (AmE), / kəˈbɑːlə / (BrE)
A form of Jewish mysticism and theosophy based on interpretations of Hebrew scriptures, originating among rabbis and developed from the 7th to 18th centuries.
Jewish mystical tradition

Examples

Many scholars study the Kabbala to gain insights into Jewish mystical beliefs.
She attended a seminar on the teachings of the Kabbala and their influence on Western thought.
The Kabbala emphasizes the hidden meanings within the Hebrew scriptures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kabbalah - alternative spelling of the same concept

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'cabala', from Hebrew 'qabbālāh' (tradition, received lore).
