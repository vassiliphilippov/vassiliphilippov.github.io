reek

1. VERB

/ rik / (AmE), / riːk / (BrE)
(of a situation, event, or behavior) to strongly suggest or seem to be full of (something unpleasant or disapproved of).
suggest strongly (of something negative)

Examples

Her explanation reeked of dishonesty, making everyone suspicious of her motives.
The entire situation reeks of corruption and cover-ups from top officials.
His letter reeked of insincerity despite the polite language.

Synset Examples

his speeches smacked of racism
this passage smells of plagiarism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smack - have a trace or suggestion of something
  • Synonym: stink - be strongly suggestive of something (often something bad)

Etymology

Old English 'rēocan' (emit smoke, smell), influenced by later figurative uses.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02199169-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ rik / (AmE), / riːk / (BrE)
to smell strongly and unpleasantly; to give off a foul odor
smell strongly and unpleasantly

Examples

The kitchen began to reek of burnt toast after the mishap.
Her clothes reeked of cigarette smoke when she got home.
If you leave food out in the heat, it will soon start reeking badly.

Synset Examples

The building reeks of smoke

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stink - to have a strong, unpleasant smell
  • Synonym: smell - to have or emit an odor

Etymology

From Old English *rēocan* (emit smoke), related to German *rauchen* (to smoke).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02128571-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ rik / (AmE), / riːk / (BrE)
to give off smoke, fumes, vapor, or steam; to emit or exhale (something, such as warmth or mist)
emit smoke, fumes, or vapor

Examples

The chimneys reeked with thick black smoke after the fire began.
On cold mornings, the river often reeks with mist before sunrise.
After the storm, the wet earth reeked with steam in the afternoon sun.

Synset Examples

Marshes reeking in the sun

Etymology

Old English *rēocan* (to emit smoke); related to German *rauchen* (to smoke).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02774078-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ riːk / (AmE), / riːk / (BrE)
A strong and unpleasant smell; a stench.
strong, unpleasant smell

Examples

The reek of rotting garbage filled the alley on a hot summer day.
Even after washing the clothes, a reek of smoke lingered.
He wrinkled his nose at the reek coming from the old fishing boat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stench - a strong and very unpleasant smell
  • Antonym: fragrance - a pleasant, sweet smell

Etymology

Old English *rēoc* (smoke, vapor, steam), related to German *Rauch* (smoke).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05722841-n
Synset POS: n

5. VERB

/ riːk / (AmE), / riːk / (BrE)
(of a person or surface) to be covered or saturated with sweat or blood.
be saturated with sweat or blood

Examples

After the long race, his face reeked with sweat.
The soldier's hands reeked with blood after the battle.
Her forehead reeked with perspiration under the scorching sun.

Etymology

From Old English *rēocan* (to emit smoke or steam).
