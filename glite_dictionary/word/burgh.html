burgh

1. NOUN

/ bɜrɡ / (AmE), / bɜːɡ / (BrE)
(in Scotland) a town or municipality with a degree of administrative autonomy, historically governed by a charter.
Scottish town or municipality with local authority
british

Examples

Aberdeen is a historic burgh on the northeast coast of Scotland.
The burgh council passed new regulations regarding land use.
Many Scottish towns became burghs through royal charters in the Middle Ages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: borough - an incorporated town with local government in England or the US

Etymology

From Middle English 'burh', from Old English 'burh' (fortified place, town); related to German 'Burg'.
