Acacia xanthophloea

1. NOUN

/ əˈkeɪʃə ˌzænθəˈfloʊiə / (AmE), / əˈkeɪsɪə ˌzænθəˈfləʊɪə / (BrE)
a species of African acacia tree, commonly known as the fever tree, which is found in swampy areas and has distinctive yellow-green bark
an African acacia tree species (fever tree)

Examples

The Acacia xanthophloea thrives in the wetlands along African riverbanks.
Locals often refer to the Acacia xanthophloea as the fever tree due to its striking yellow bark.
Wildlife is commonly seen resting in the shade of the Acacia xanthophloea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fever tree - another name for Acacia xanthophloea

Etymology

From Latinized Greek: 'acacia' from Greek akakia (thorny Egyptian tree), 'xanthophloea' from Greek xanthos (yellow) + phloios (bark), referring to its yellow bark.
