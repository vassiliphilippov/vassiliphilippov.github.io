foetoprotein

1. NOUN

/ ˌfiːtoʊˈproʊtin / (AmE), / ˌfiːtəʊˈprəʊtiːn / (BrE)
any of several proteins produced by a fetus, sometimes found at high levels in adults with certain cancers
protein antigen produced by fetus
medical british

Examples

Elevated levels of foetoprotein were detected during the prenatal screening.
Certain cancers in adults may cause an increase in foetoprotein levels.
Researchers are studying how foetoprotein can be used as a diagnostic marker.

Etymology

From Latin 'foetus' (offspring) and 'protein'.
