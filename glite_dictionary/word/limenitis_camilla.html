Limenitis camilla

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈmɛnɪtɪs kəˈmɪlə / (AmE), / lɪˈmiːnɪtɪs kəˈmɪlə / (BrE)
A Eurasian butterfly with brown wings and distinctive white markings, commonly known as the white admiral.
Eurasian butterfly, white admiral

Examples

The beautiful Limenitis camilla can often be seen gliding through woodland clearings in summer.
While hiking in central Europe, she photographed a Limenitis camilla resting on a leaf.
Researchers observed the mating habits of Limenitis camilla in their natural habitat.

Etymology

From the genus name Limenitis and the species epithet camilla, used in biological classification.
