garb

1. NOUN

/ ɡɑrb / (AmE), / ɡɑːb / (BrE)
clothing of a particular type or style, especially for a specific occasion or role.
distinctive style of clothing

Examples

The chef wore his traditional garb while preparing the meal.
Monks are easily recognized by their brown garb and simple sandals.
She arrived at the costume party in medieval garb, complete with a velvet cloak.

Synset Examples

formal attire
battle dress

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attire - clothes, especially formal or distinctive clothing
  • Synonym: costume - a set of clothes worn to look like someone or something else, or for a special occasion

Etymology

From Old French *garbe* (dress, appearance), of Germanic origin.

2. VERB

/ ɡɑrb / (AmE), / ɡɑːb / (BrE)
to dress or provide someone with clothing, especially in a particular style or type of clothes
to dress; to clothe someone

Examples

The actors were garbed in elaborate costumes for the historical drama.
She was garbed in a flowing white gown for the ceremony.
Volunteers garbed the children in warm coats before sending them outside.

Synset Examples

Parents must feed and dress their child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dress - to put clothes on someone or oneself
  • Antonym: undress - to remove someone's clothes

Etymology

From Italian *garbo* (grace, style), likely via French *garbe*, related to Medieval Latin *garba* (grace, appearance).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00047662-v
Synset POS: v
