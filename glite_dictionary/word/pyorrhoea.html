pyorrhoea

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪəˈrioʊə / (AmE), / ˌpaɪəˈriːə / (BrE)
A discharge of pus, especially from the gums due to infection.
discharge of pus, especially from gums
medical british

Examples

If left untreated, pyorrhoea can result in tooth loss and severe gum damage.
The dentist explained that pyorrhoea is often caused by poor oral hygiene.
Regular dental checkups are crucial for preventing pyorrhoea and other gum diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: periodontitis - serious gum infection that damages gums and can destroy the jawbone

Etymology

From Greek 'pyorrhoia', from 'pyo-' (pus) + 'rhein' (to flow).

2. NOUN

/ paɪəˈriə / (AmE), / ˌpaɪ.əˈriː.ə / (BrE)
A severe gum disease characterized by the discharge of pus from inflamed tooth sockets, also known as chronic periodontitis.
purulent inflammation of the gums
medical british

Examples

The dentist diagnosed him with pyorrhoea after noticing persistent gum inflammation and pus.
If left untreated, pyorrhoea can result in tooth loss and severe discomfort.
Early signs of pyorrhoea include swollen gums and a foul taste in the mouth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: periodontitis - inflammation of the tissue around the teeth, often leading to loss of the teeth

Etymology

From Greek 'pyorrhoia', from 'pyo-' (pus) + 'rhein' (to flow).
