Hottonia inflata

1. NOUN

/ hoʊˈtoʊniə ɪnˈfleɪtə / (AmE), / hɒˈtɒniə ɪnˈfleɪtə / (BrE)
a species of aquatic plant native to the eastern United States, known for its submerged spongy, inflated flower stalks and white flowers
aquatic plant with spongy stalks and white flowers

Examples

The rare Hottonia inflata can be found in shallow ponds across the eastern United States.
Botanists were excited to observe the spongy flower stalks of the Hottonia inflata during their field survey.
The white flowers of Hottonia inflata stand out beautifully against its green aquatic foliage.

Etymology

From the genus name Hottonia, named after Dutch botanist Petrus Hotton, and the species epithet inflata, from Latin 'inflatus' meaning 'inflated'.
