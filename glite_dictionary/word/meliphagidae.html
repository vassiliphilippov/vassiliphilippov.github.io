Meliphagidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛlɪˈfædʒɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌmɛlɪˈfædʒɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small to medium-sized birds known as honeyeaters, native to Australia and surrounding regions, which are specialized for feeding on nectar.
family of nectar-feeding birds (honeyeaters)

Examples

The Meliphagidae include many species native to Australia and New Guinea.
Scientists study the feeding adaptations of the Meliphagidae to understand nectarivory in birds.
A member of the Meliphagidae was spotted feeding among the blossoms in the botanical garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honeyeater - nectar-feeding bird in the family Meliphagidae

Etymology

From Modern Latin, based on Greek 'meli' (honey) + 'phagos' (eating).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛləˈfægɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˌmiːlɪˈfæɡɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of small to medium-sized birds, commonly known as honeyeaters, found mainly in Australia and the surrounding regions.
a family of honeyeaters (birds)

Examples

The Meliphagidae are known for their specialized brush-tipped tongues used to collect nectar.
Australian gardens are often visited by members of the Meliphagidae family seeking flowers to feed on.
Researchers have studied the migration patterns of several Meliphagidae species throughout the year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honeyeater - any bird belonging to the family Meliphagidae

Etymology

From New Latin Meliphagidae, based on Greek meli (honey) + phagos (eating).
