interview

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.t̬ɚˌvju / (AmE), / ˈɪn.tə.vjuː / (BrE)
a formal meeting in which someone meets and talks with a person of importance, often to exchange information or for consultation
formal meeting with an important person

Examples

The ambassador was granted an interview with the president to discuss foreign policy.
After months of requests, the scientist finally secured an interview with the Nobel laureate.
The mayor scheduled an interview for citizens who wanted to present their concerns directly.

Synset Examples

he had a consultation with the judge
he requested an audience with the king

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consultation - a meeting to seek advice or discuss something
  • Synonym: audience - a formal meeting with a person of rank or power

Etymology

From French *entrevue*, from s'entrevoir (to see one another).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07158332-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈɪn.t̬ɚ.vjuː / (AmE), / ˈɪn.tə.vjuː / (BrE)
to attend a meeting where one is assessed for a job or position
attend a job interview

Examples

After college, she interviewed at several tech companies before getting an offer.
Many students interview for internships each summer to gain experience.
He interviewed well, but another candidate was ultimately selected for the role.

Synset Examples

The job candidate interviewed everywhere

Related Terms

  • Synonym: audition - to perform in order to obtain a job, especially in entertainment

Etymology

From French 'entrevue', from 'entrevoir' (to see between), ultimately from Latin components.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00811115-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈɪntərˌvju / (AmE), / ˈɪntəvjuː / (BrE)
a formal conversation in which someone is asked questions to obtain information, especially by a journalist or for a job
formal questioning, often by a journalist

Examples

The journalist conducted an interview with the author about her new book.
After the controversial match, the coach gave a brief interview to the press.
She prepared thoroughly for her interview with the renowned scientist.

Synset Examples

my interviews with teenagers revealed a weakening of religious bonds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interrogation - formal, often aggressive questioning, especially by police

Etymology

From French ‘entrevue’, from ‘s'entrevoir’ (to see each other), based on Latin ‘inter-’ (between) + ‘videre’ (to see).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07210735-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈɪn.tərˌvju / (AmE), / ˈɪn.tə.vjuː / (BrE)
to formally ask questions and discuss with someone, especially in order to evaluate them for a job, position, or opportunity
formally question for evaluation

Examples

We plan to interview all the applicants before making a decision.
She interviewed several candidates for the manager position last week.
They are interviewing potential interns throughout the month of June.

Synset Examples

We interviewed the job candidates

Related Terms

  • Synonym: question - ask questions to obtain information
  • Synonym: to interrogate - ask questions closely, aggressively, or formally

Etymology

From French 'entrevue', from 'entrevoir' (to see between, interview), based on Latin 'inter-' (between) + 'videre' (to see).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00810910-v
Synset POS: v

5. VERB

/ ˈɪntərˌvju / (AmE), / ˈɪntəvjuː / (BrE)
to question someone in order to obtain information for a television, newspaper, or radio report
question for news or reporting

Examples

The journalist will interview the mayor about the recent policy changes.
She has interviewed dozens of experts for her latest documentary.
We are interviewing several candidates for the television segment tonight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: question - to ask someone questions

Etymology

From French 'entrevue', from 'entrevoir' (to see between/interview), itself from 'entre-' (between) + 'voir' (to see).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00810517-v
Synset POS: v
