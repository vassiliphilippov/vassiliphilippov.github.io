levanter

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈvæntər / (AmE), / lɪˈvæntə / (BrE)
A strong easterly wind that blows in the western Mediterranean region.
easterly wind in western Mediterranean

Examples

The levanter brought cool air from the east, refreshing the coastal city.
Fishermen stayed ashore as the levanter swept across the Mediterranean.
Clouds gathered quickly above Gibraltar whenever the levanter was blowing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sirocco - a hot, dry wind from North Africa to southern Europe

Etymology

From Spanish 'levante' (east), referring to the direction from which the wind blows.
