sanatorium

1. NOUN

/ ˌsænəˈtɔriəm / (AmE), / ˌsanəˈtɔːrɪəm / (BrE)
a hospital or establishment where people with chronic illnesses or those recovering from illness receive medical care and rest, often over a long period
hospital for chronic illness or recovery

Examples

After his surgery, he was sent to a sanatorium to regain his strength.
The mountain air was believed to benefit patients at the sanatorium.
The city closed the old sanatorium after newer hospitals were built.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sanitarium - an alternative term with similar meaning, especially in American English

Etymology

From Latin 'sanatorius' (curative), from 'sanare' (to heal).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsænəˈtɔriəm / (AmE), / ˌsan.əˈtɔː.ri.əm / (BrE)
A dated and sometimes pejorative term for a hospital for people with mental illness, especially in the past; an insane asylum.
dated term for an insane asylum
archaic

Examples

In the novel, the character was sent to a sanatorium after suffering a breakdown.
Some communities resisted having a sanatorium nearby, fearing the stigma associated with mental illness.
The old sanatorium on the outskirts of town was later converted into a museum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asylum - an institution offering shelter and support to people with mental illness
  • Synonym: psychiatric hospital - a hospital specializing in the treatment of mental illness

Etymology

From Latin 'sanatorius' (curative), originally denoting a place for healing.
