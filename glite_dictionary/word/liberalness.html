liberalness

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪbərəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈlɪb(ə)rəlnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being generous and open-minded in behavior and temperament
generosity; open-mindedness

Examples

Her liberalness was evident in the way she always helped those in need.
The liberalness of the new policy encouraged creativity and collaboration in the workplace.
Many admired his liberalness when it came to sharing resources with less fortunate communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: generosity - the quality of being generous
  • Synonym: liberality - the quality of giving freely; generosity

Etymology

Formed from 'liberal' + '-ness', denoting a quality or state.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɪbərəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈlɪb(ə)r(ə)lnəs / (BrE)
the quality or tendency of supporting progress, open-mindedness, and individual freedom
quality of being liberal; open-mindedness

Examples

Her liberalness was reflected in her support for free speech and social reforms.
Many admired his liberalness when it came to embracing new ideas and perspectives.
The organization's liberalness attracted members who valued openness and progress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liberality - the quality of being open to new ideas and free from prejudice
  • Antonym: illiberalism - an inclination to oppose individual freedom or progress

Etymology

From 'liberal' + '-ness'. 'Liberal' from Latin 'liberalis' (of freedom); '-ness' forms nouns indicating a state or quality.
