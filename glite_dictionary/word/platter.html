platter

1. NOUN

/ ˈplædɚ / (AmE), / ˈplætə / (BrE)
a large, flat, shallow dish used for serving food
large serving dish

Examples

The roast chicken was brought out on a beautiful silver platter.
She arranged the sliced fruit neatly on a large platter for the guests.
After dinner, he cleared the empty platter from the table.

Etymology

Middle English, from Old French 'plat' (flat dish).

2. NOUN

/ ˈplæt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈplætə / (BrE)
(informal) A phonograph record; a disk with a continuous groove that reproduces recorded sound when rotated on a turntable.
phonograph record (disk)
informal

Examples

He put an old jazz platter on the turntable and turned up the volume.
Collectors often seek out rare platters from the 1950s and 1960s.
The DJ spun vintage platters all night at the retro dance party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: record - a thin plastic disk with recorded sound
