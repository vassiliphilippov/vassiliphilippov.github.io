nyctophobia

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɪktəˈfoʊbiə / (AmE), / ˌnɪktəˈfəʊbiə / (BrE)
An extreme or irrational fear of the night or darkness.
fear of the dark/night
medical

Examples

As a child, she suffered from nyctophobia and needed a nightlight to sleep.
People with nyctophobia may experience panic attacks when left in the dark.
His nyctophobia made camping trips challenging, even with friends nearby.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: phobia - an extreme or irrational fear of or aversion to something

Etymology

From Greek 'nyx' (night) + 'phobos' (fear).
