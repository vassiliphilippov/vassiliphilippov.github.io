to prohibit

1. VERB

/ pɹəˈhɪbɪt / (AmE), / prəˈhɪbɪt / (BrE)
to officially forbid something by law, rule, or authority
formally forbid; make illegal

Examples

The school prohibits smoking on its premises.
The law prohibits the use of certain chemicals in food production.
Her doctor prohibited strenuous exercise until her shoulder healed.

Synset Examples

I forbid you to call me late at night
Mother vetoed the trip to the chocolate store
Dad nixed our plans

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to forbid - to order someone not to do something; refuse to allow
  • Antonym: permit - to allow something to happen
  • Antonym: to allow - to let someone do something

Etymology

From Latin 'prohibere' (to hold back, restrain), from 'pro-' (away) + 'habere' (to hold).
