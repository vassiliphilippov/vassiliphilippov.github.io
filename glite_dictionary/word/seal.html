SEAL

1. NOUN

/ siːl / (AmE), / siːl / (BrE)
a member of a United States Navy Special Warfare unit trained for operations on sea, air, and land
member of U.S. Naval Special Warfare unit

Examples

My brother is a SEAL and has completed several missions overseas.
The rigorous training program required to become a SEAL is known for its difficulty.
A SEAL team rescued the hostages in a daring nighttime operation.

Synset Examples

SEAL is an acronym for Sea Air and Land

Etymology

Acronym from 'Sea, Air, and Land'.
