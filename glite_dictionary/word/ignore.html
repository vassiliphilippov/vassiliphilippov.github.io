to ignore

1. VERB

/ ɪɡˈnɔr / (AmE), / ɪɡˈnɔː / (BrE)
to deliberately pay no attention to; to refuse to acknowledge or consider something or someone
deliberately pay no attention to

Examples

She chose to ignore the criticism and focus on her work.
Please ignore the background noise during the meeting.
Many drivers ignore the speed limit sign on that road.

Synset Examples

Disregard the errors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disregard - to pay no attention to; to ignore
  • Antonym: heed - to pay careful attention to

Etymology

From Latin ignorare (not to know, disregard), from in- (not) + gnoscere (to know).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00618547-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪɡˈnɔr / (AmE), / ɪɡˈnɔː / (BrE)
to fail to notice or pay attention to someone or something
fail to notice or pay attention

Examples

The manager chose to ignore my suggestions during the meeting.
It is easy to ignore small details when you are in a hurry.
She sometimes ignores emails that are not work-related.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overlook - fail to notice something, especially a mistake
  • Antonym: notice - become aware of; pay attention to

Etymology

From Latin 'ignorare' (not to know, disregard).
