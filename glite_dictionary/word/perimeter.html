perimeter

1. NOUN

/ pəˈrɪm.ə.tɚ / (AmE), / pəˈrɪm.ɪ.tə / (BrE)
the total length of the boundary or outer edge of a two-dimensional shape or area
distance around a shape

Examples

To find the perimeter of a rectangle, add together all four sides.
The perimeter of the garden needs to be measured before we buy the fence.
The coach asked the players to run along the perimeter of the field during warm-up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: circumference - distance around a circle or circular object

Etymology

From Greek *perimetros* (circumference), from *peri-* (around) + *metron* (measure).

2. NOUN

/ pəˈrɪmɪtɚ / (AmE), / pəˈrɪmɪtə / (BrE)
the continuous line forming the boundary of a closed geometric figure or area
boundary line of an area

Examples

The security guard walked along the perimeter of the fence to make sure there were no gaps.
To calculate the perimeter of a rectangle, add together the lengths of all four sides.
A tall hedge grew around the perimeter of the garden, providing both privacy and shade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: circumference - distance around a circle
  • Antonym: center - the middle point of an area or object

Etymology

From Greek 'perimetros' (measuring around), from 'peri-' (around) + 'metron' (measure).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13894091-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ pəˈɹɪmətɚ / (AmE), / pəˈɹɪmɪtə / (BrE)
the outer boundary or the area immediately inside the outer boundary of a surface or figure
outer boundary of an area

Examples

The security guards patrolled the perimeter of the building every hour.
Children are not allowed to go beyond the perimeter fence during recess.
Plants and shrubs lined the perimeter of the garden, creating a natural border.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boundary - a real or imagined line marking the limits of an area

Etymology

From French périmètre, from Latin perimetros, from Greek perímetros (around measure).
