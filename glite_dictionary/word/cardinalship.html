cardinalship

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːr.dɪ.nəl.ʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.dɪ.nəl.ʃɪp / (BrE)
the office, dignity, or position of a cardinal in the Roman Catholic Church
the office of a cardinal

Examples

He devoted his life to serving the Church during his cardinalship.
After decades of service, his cardinalship was marked by humility and reform.
The responsibilities of the cardinalship include advising the Pope and electing new pontiffs.

Synset Examples

following the scandal, the cardinal resigned his cardinalate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cardinalate - the office or dignity of a cardinal

Etymology

Formed from 'cardinal' + '-ship', denoting state or office.
