scoop

1. NOUN

/ skuːp / (AmE), / skuːp / (BrE)
a hollow concave shape or indentation formed by removing material from a surface
a hollow made by removal of material

Examples

Rainwater collected in the scoop left in the sand by the children's shovels.
The archaeologist noticed a scoop in the clay, indicating something had been removed.
The potter shaped a scoop into the side of the bowl before it was fired.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depression - a sunken or hollow area on a surface
