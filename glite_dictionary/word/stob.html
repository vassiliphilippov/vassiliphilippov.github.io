stob

1. NOUN

/ stɑb / (AmE), / stɒb / (BrE)
a short, straight stick or stake of wood, often used as a marker or support
short straight wooden stick or stake

Examples

The gardener placed a stob in the ground to mark where the new plant would go.
He tripped over a hidden stob while walking through the field.
The fence was constructed from a series of sturdy stobs set in a row.

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'stub' (stump), related to Old English 'stybb'.
