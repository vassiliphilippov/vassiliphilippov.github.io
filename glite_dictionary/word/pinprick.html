pinprick

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪnˌprɪk / (AmE), / ˈpɪnˌprɪk / (BrE)
A very small hole or puncture made by or as if by a pin.
very small puncture or hole

Examples

I felt a sharp pinprick on my finger as I sewed the button.
The doctor assured her that the vaccine would only cause a slight pinprick.
Tiny pinpricks of light shone through the holes in the curtain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puncture - a small hole made by a pointed object

Etymology

Formed from 'pin' and 'prick', meaning a small hole made by a pin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɪnˌprɪk / (AmE), / ˈpɪn.prɪk / (BrE)
a minor irritation or annoyance; something slight but bothersome
minor irritation or annoyance

Examples

Though the noise outside was a mere pinprick, it still disturbed her concentration.
These little setbacks are just pinpricks compared to the challenges we've overcome.
He treated the criticism as no more than a pinprick and moved on with his day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nuisance - a person, thing, or situation causing inconvenience or annoyance
  • Synonym: irritation - something that annoys or irritates
  • Antonym: comfort - a state of physical ease and freedom from pain or constraint

Etymology

From 'pin' (a small, sharp object) and 'prick' (a small puncture), reflecting the idea of a slight but sharp annoyance.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14430615-n
Synset POS: n
