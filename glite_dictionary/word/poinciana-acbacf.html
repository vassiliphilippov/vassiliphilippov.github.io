poinciana

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɔɪn.siˈæn.ə / (AmE), / ˌpɔɪn.sɪˈɑː.nə / (BrE)
A tropical shrub or small tree with bright orange or red flowers, sometimes classified in the genus Poinciana.
tropical flowering shrub or tree

Examples

The bright flowers of the poinciana add a splash of color to the garden in summer.
We planted a poinciana in our backyard for its striking blossoms.
The poinciana is admired for its feathery leaves and vibrant orange blooms.

Etymology

From French 'poinciana', named after Philippe de Longvilliers de Poincy, a French governor in the West Indies.
