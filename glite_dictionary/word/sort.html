sort

1. NOUN

/ sɔrt / (AmE), / sɔːt / (BrE)
an action or process of arranging or separating items into groups based on specific criteria
arrangement into groups

Examples

The assembly line was slowed down by the sort that separated defective parts from the rest.
After the harvest, workers performed a careful sort to grade the apples by size and color.
The mailroom handles the initial sort so that letters reach the correct departments.

Synset Examples

the bottleneck in mail delivery is the process of sorting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorting - the process of ordering or arranging items by criterion

Etymology

From Old French *sort* (kind, category), from Latin *sortem* (lot, fate, share).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13579899-n
Synset POS: n
