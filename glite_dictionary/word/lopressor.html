Lopressor

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈprɛsər / (AmE), / ləʊˈprɛsə / (BrE)
the proprietary name for metoprolol, a beta-blocker medication used to treat high blood pressure, angina, arrhythmia, and heart attacks
trade name for metoprolol, a beta-blocker medicine
medical

Examples

My doctor prescribed Lopressor to help control my high blood pressure.
Patients taking Lopressor should monitor for possible side effects such as fatigue or dizziness.
After his heart attack, he was started on Lopressor as part of his recovery regimen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metoprolol - a beta-blocker medication for heart conditions and high blood pressure
