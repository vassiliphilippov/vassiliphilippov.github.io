effective

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈfɛktɪv / (AmE), / ɪˈfɛktɪv / (BrE)
Producing a desired or intended result; successful in achieving the intended purpose.
producing a desired result

Examples

The new marketing strategy proved to be very effective in increasing sales.
This medicine is especially effective against coughs caused by allergies.
Her speech was so effective that it changed many people's minds.

Synset Examples

an effective reprimand
a lotion that is effective in cases of prickly heat

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ineffective - not producing the desired result
  • Synonym: efficient - working well with minimal waste or effort

Etymology

From Latin 'effectivus' (creative, effective), from 'effectus' (accomplishment).

6. ADJ

/ ɪˈfɛktɪv / (AmE), / ɪˈfɛktɪv / (BrE)
(of something abstract, such as a rule, demand, or amount) existing in fact, not just in theory; real or actual.
real or actual, not just theoretical

Examples

After adjusting for inflation, the effective cost of the project was much higher than anticipated.
The policy looks good on paper, but its effective impact remains to be seen.
The country’s effective population is lower than its total population due to emigration.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: actual - existing in fact, not just potentially
  • Antonym: theoretical - existing only in theory, not in fact

Etymology

From Latin effectivus (creative, productive), from effectus (accomplishment, effect).
