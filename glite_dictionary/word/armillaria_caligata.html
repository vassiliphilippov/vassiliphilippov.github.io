Armillaria caligata

1. NOUN

/ ɑːr.mɪˈlɛər.i.ə kəˈlɪɡ.ə.tə / (AmE), / ɑː.mɪˈlɛə.rɪ.ə kəˈlɪɡ.ə.tə / (BrE)
A species of fungus with a brown cap, white gills, and a membranous ring located halfway up its stalk.
species of fungus with brown cap and white gills

Examples

The forest ranger identified the mushroom as Armillaria caligata during his field survey.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Armillaria caligata in different soil types.
Foragers should exercise caution, as Armillaria caligata can be easily confused with similar but poisonous fungi.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honey fungus - species of Armillaria fungi, some known as honey fungus

Etymology

From Latin; 'Armillaria' from 'armilla' (bracelet), referring to the ring, and 'caligata' meaning 'booted', likely referring to a boot-like feature on the stalk.
