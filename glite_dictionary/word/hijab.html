hijab

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈdʒɑb / (AmE), / hɪˈdʒɑːb / (BrE)
A scarf or covering worn by some Muslim women to conceal the hair and neck, and sometimes the shoulders, as a sign of modesty.
Muslim women's hair and neck covering

Examples

Many Muslim women choose to wear a hijab as an expression of their faith.
She bought a colorful new hijab for the festival.
Some schools have discussed whether to allow students to wear the hijab as part of their uniform.

Etymology

From Arabic ḥijāb (cover, veil, screen, curtain).

2. NOUN

/ hɪˈdʒɑːb / (AmE), / hɪˈdʒɑːb / (BrE)
The practice, among some Muslim women, of dressing modestly in public, typically by wearing a headscarf covering the hair, neck, and sometimes the shoulders.
Muslim modest dress or headscarf

Examples

Many women choose to wear the hijab as a sign of modesty and faith.
She explained the cultural importance of the hijab at the seminar.
Debate about the hijab in public schools continues in several countries.

Synset Examples

she observes the hijab and does not wear tight clothing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: veil - A piece of material worn by women to cover the face or head

Etymology

From Arabic ḥijāb (veil, cover, curtain).
