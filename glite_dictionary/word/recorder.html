recorder

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈkɔrdər / (AmE), / rɪˈkɔːdə / (BrE)
in the UK, a barrister or solicitor who serves as a part-time judge in certain towns or boroughs
part-time judge in the UK
legal british

Examples

After years as a barrister, she was appointed recorder of the borough.
The recorder presided over the magistrates' court on Thursday.
He served as a recorder before becoming a full-time judge.

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈkɔrdər / (AmE), / rɪˈkɔːdə / (BrE)
a tubular woodwind instrument with eight finger holes and a fipple mouthpiece, played by blowing into one end and covering the holes to produce different notes
a woodwind instrument with finger holes

Examples

She learned to play the recorder in her elementary school music class.
The recorder is often used in early music ensembles.
He brought his wooden recorder to the Renaissance fair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flute - a woodwind instrument played by blowing across a hole

Etymology

From Old French 'recordeur', from 'recorder' meaning 'to recite, repeat', with reference to its use for learning music by ear.

3. NOUN

/ rɪˈkɔrdər / (AmE), / rɪˈkɔːdə / (BrE)
a device used to capture and store audio, video, or other data for later playback or analysis
device that records audio, video, etc.

Examples

The police used a recorder to capture the suspect's confession during the interview.
She always keeps a small recorder in her bag to save her song ideas.
The meeting was documented using a digital recorder placed in the center of the table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tape recorder - device that records and plays audio on magnetic tape

Etymology

From Latin 'recordari' (to remember, call to mind), via Old French 'recordeur'.

4. NOUN

/ rɪˈkɔrdər / (AmE), / rɪˈkɔːdə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to keep official records or accounts in an organization or institution.
person who keeps official records

Examples

The recorder of the meeting carefully documented every decision that was made.
As city recorder, she is responsible for maintaining all municipal records.
A trained recorder ensures that important information is accurately preserved for the future.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secretary - an official who is responsible for keeping records, minutes, and correspondence
  • Synonym: clerk - a person employed to keep records and accounts or perform office work

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *recorder* (to bring to mind, recall), later denoting one who records information.
