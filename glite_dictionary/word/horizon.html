horizon

1. NOUN

/ həˈraɪzən / (AmE), / həˈraɪzn̩ / (BrE)
a distinct layer of soil or subsoil in a vertical section of land, differing in composition and characteristics from layers above or below
layer of soil or subsoil

Examples

Soil scientists examine each horizon to determine its composition and fertility.
The A horizon is usually rich in organic matter and nutrients.
Beneath the topsoil, the B horizon often contains more clay and minerals.

/ həˈraɪzən / (AmE), / həˈraɪzn̩ / (BrE)
the limit or range of a person's knowledge, experience, or interest
limit of knowledge or experience

Examples

The discovery pushed the horizon of scientific understanding far beyond previous limits.

Many students are encouraged to broaden their horizon through reading and exploration.

3. NOUN

/ həˈraɪzən / (AmE), / həˈraɪzən / (BrE)
(astronomy) The great circle on the celestial sphere whose plane passes through the sensible horizon and the center of the Earth.
circle marking visible boundary in astronomy

Examples

In astronomy, the horizon is defined as the circle on the celestial sphere that passes through the observer's zenith and nadir.
Navigators use the horizon as a reference when measuring the altitude of stars above the Earth's surface.
At sea, the apparent meeting of sky and water closely matches the astronomical horizon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horizon - the line where the earth and sky appear to meet (general sense)

Etymology

From Greek 'horizōn' (limiting), from 'horizein' (to limit).

4. NOUN

/ həˈraɪzən / (AmE), / həˈraɪzn̩ / (BrE)
the line in the distance where the Earth and sky seem to meet
the distant line where sky meets land or sea

Examples

As the sun set, the orange glow spread across the horizon.
From the mountain peak, the horizon seemed endless and clear.
Clouds gathered on the horizon, signaling an approaching storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skyline - the outline of land against the sky
  • Antonym: zenith - the point in the sky directly above an observer

Etymology

From Greek horizōn (limiting), via Latin horizon and Old French orizon.
