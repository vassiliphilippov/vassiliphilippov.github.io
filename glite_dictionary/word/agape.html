agape

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡɑːpeɪ / (AmE), / əˈɡɑːpeɪ / (BrE)
A religious meal or feast symbolizing love and fellowship among participants, especially in early Christian communities.
religious communal meal symbolizing fellowship

Examples

The congregation gathered for an agape after the service, sharing food and conversation.
Early Christians often celebrated agape meals to strengthen their sense of community.
The annual agape at the church fostered friendship and unity among its members.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek ἀγάπη (agápē), meaning 'love'.

2. NOUN

/ əˈɡɑːpeɪ / (AmE), / əˈɡɑːpeɪ / (BrE)
(In Christian theology) the love of God or Christ for humankind; unconditional, selfless love.
unconditional love of God or Christ for mankind

Examples

The New Testament often refers to agape as the highest form of love.
Many Christians strive to show agape in their daily actions toward others.
The sermon focused on the meaning and importance of agape in building a compassionate community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charity - Christian love of one's neighbor; benevolence toward others
  • Degree Lower: philia - friendship or affectionate love in Greek philosophy
  • Degree Lower: eros - romantic or passionate love, as contrasted in Greek philosophy

Etymology

From Ancient Greek ἀγάπη (agápē) meaning 'love, charity'.

4. NOUN

/ əˈɡeɪpi / (AmE), / əˈɡɑːpeɪ / (BrE)
selfless, unconditional love for others, especially of a spiritual nature, without sexual intent
selfless, spiritual love; unconditional affection

Examples

Many religious teachings emphasize agape as the highest form of love for humanity.
The community was built on the principle of agape, caring for everyone without expecting anything in return.
Her compassion and acts of agape inspired others to help those in need.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: philia - friendship or affectionate love that is less selfless than agape
  • Degree Higher: eros - romantic or passionate love with sexual desire

Etymology

From Ancient Greek agapē (ἀγάπη), meaning ‘love’ (especially spiritual or selfless love).
