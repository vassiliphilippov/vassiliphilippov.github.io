marked

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɑrkt / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkt / (BrE)
having a distinguishing mark or noticeably characterized as specified; often used in combination
having a distinguishing mark; noticeably characterized

Examples

The path to the summit was marked with red flags.
She entered the room with a marked look of concern on her face.
The animal had a marked stripe along its back that made it easy to identify.

Synset Examples

played with marked cards
a scar-marked face
well-marked roads

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unmarked - not having any distinguishing marks

Etymology

From the verb 'mark' + -ed, meaning to have been marked.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01483493-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɑrkt / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkt / (BrE)
Clearly noticeable or evident; easily observed or recognized.
easily noticeable; evident

Examples

The patient spoke with a marked improvement in confidence after the therapy sessions.
There was a marked difference between the two proposals.
His resignation led to a marked drop in company morale.

Synset Examples

walked with a marked limp
a pronounced flavor of cinnamon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pronounced - very noticeable or strongly marked
  • Synonym: obvious - easily perceived or understood; clear and apparent
  • Antonym: subtle - not obvious or easily noticeable

Etymology

From past participle of Middle English marken (to mark).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01290810-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈmɑrkt / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkt / (BrE)
singled out for special attention or especially for an imminent, usually negative, outcome
singled out for attention or fate

Examples

After testifying, he became a marked man in his old neighborhood.
The witness knew she was marked after revealing the secret.
His actions at work have made him a marked employee among management.

Synset Examples

a marked man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: targeted - made a target of; singled out for negative attention
  • Antonym: anonymous - not identified by name; not singled out
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00583035-s
Synset POS: s
