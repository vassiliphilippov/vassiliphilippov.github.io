holmium

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊlmiəm / (AmE), / ˈhɒlmiəm / (BrE)
a trivalent metallic element of the lanthanide series, used in specialized alloys and for its strong magnetic properties
a metallic chemical element (Ho)

Examples

The laboratory used holmium to calibrate the spectrometer for precise measurements.
Because of its high magnetic strength, holmium is valuable in some nuclear control rods.
Researchers discovered small traces of holmium in the mineral sample from Sweden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dysprosium - a neighboring rare earth metal element
  • Synonym: yttrium - another rare earth element often found with holmium

Etymology

From Latin 'Holmia', the Latinized name for Stockholm, where it was discovered.
