tamandua

1. NOUN

/ təˈmænduə / (AmE), / təˈmændjʊə / (BrE)
a small anteater of tropical Central and South America, with a prehensile tail and four large claws on its forelimbs, feeding mainly on insects.
Central/South American small anteater

Examples

The tamandua used its long tongue to extract ants from a hollow tree.
During our hike in the rainforest, we spotted a tamandua climbing a branch.
A tamandua relies on its strong claws to tear open insect nests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anteater - a mammal that eats ants and termites, including the tamandua

Etymology

From Portuguese *tamanduá*, from Tupi *tamanduá* (anteater).

2. NOUN

/ təˈmænduə / (AmE), / təˈmandjʊə / (BrE)
A small anteater found in Central and South America, known for its prehensile tail and four-clawed forelimbs.
small anteater native to Central and South America

Examples

The tamandua uses its long, sticky tongue to catch ants and termites in the rainforest.
We saw a tamandua climbing a tree during our trip to Brazil.
Both species of tamandua are adapted to a life spent partly in trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anteater - a mammal that feeds mainly on ants and termites

Etymology

From Portuguese 'tamanduá', from Tupi 'tamanduá' (ant catcher).
