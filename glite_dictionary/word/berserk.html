berserk

1. NOUN

/ bərˈzɜrk / (AmE), / bəˈzɜːk / (BrE)
A member of a group of ancient Norse warriors who worked themselves into a wild frenzy before battle and fought with reckless savagery.
frenzied Norse warrior

Examples

The berserk charged into battle, feared by both enemies and allies.
Stories of the berserks describe their trance-like fury and disregard for pain.
Legends say a true berserk felt no steel and knew no fear in battle.

Etymology

From Old Norse *berserkr*, probably from *ber* (bear) + *serkr* (shirt), referring to wearing a bear skin.

2. ADJ

/ bərˈzɜrk / (AmE), / bəˈzɜːk / (BrE)
out of control with anger or excitement; frenzied or wild
wildly frenzied or out of control

Examples

She went berserk when she found out her phone was missing.
The fans were berserk with excitement after their team won the championship.
He drove berserk through the streets, ignoring every traffic light.

Synset Examples

the soldier was completely amuck
berserk with grief
a berserk worker smashing windows

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frenzied - wildly uncontrolled or agitated
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other strong emotions

Etymology

From Old Norse 'berserkr' (literally 'bear-shirt', a wild Norse warrior); adopted into modern English in the 19th century.
