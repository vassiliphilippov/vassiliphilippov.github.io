crock

1. NOUN

/ krɑk / (AmE), / krɒk / (BrE)
a thick earthenware pot or jar, typically used for storing food or liquids
earthenware jar or pot

Examples

She placed the pickles in a large crock in the pantry.
They found an old crock buried in the garden while digging.
The farmhouse kitchen displayed a collection of antique crocks on the shelves.

Etymology

From Middle English crokke, from Old English crocc (pot, vessel).
