ependymA

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈpɛn.dɪ.mə / (AmE), / ɪˈpɛn.dɪ.mə / (BrE)
the thin epithelial membrane that lines the ventricles of the brain and the central canal of the spinal cord
membrane lining brain ventricles and spinal canal
medical

Examples

The ependyma plays a crucial role in the production and circulation of cerebrospinal fluid.
Damage to the ependyma can have serious neurological consequences.
The pathologist observed changes in the ependyma under the microscope.

Etymology

From Greek 'ependyma' (an upper garment, covering), from 'epi-' (over) + 'endyma' (garment).
