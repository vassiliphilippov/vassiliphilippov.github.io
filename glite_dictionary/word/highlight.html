highlight

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈhaɪˌlaɪt / (BrE)
the most interesting, exciting, or memorable part of an event or period of time
most memorable part

Examples

The highlight of the evening was a spectacular fireworks display.
Winning the championship was the definite highlight of her career.
Our trip to the Grand Canyon was the highlight of the summer.

Synset Examples

the highlight of the tour was our visit to the Vatican

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lowlight - the least interesting or most disappointing part
  • Synonym: climax - the most intense or important moment
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13832353-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈhaɪˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ.laɪt / (BrE)
to attract attention to or emphasize something important or notable
emphasize or make prominent

Examples

The report highlighted several areas where improvements could be made.
She used bold type to highlight the most important points in her presentation.
The documentary highlights the challenges faced by small businesses during the pandemic.

Synset Examples

The introduction highlighted the speaker's distinguished career in linguistics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to emphasize - to give special importance or prominence to something in speaking or writing

Etymology

From 'high' + 'light', originally meaning to illuminate strongly; later figurative use to mean emphasize.
