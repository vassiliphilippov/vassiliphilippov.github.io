eyedrop

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪˌdrɑp / (AmE), / ˈaɪˌdrɒp / (BrE)
A liquid medicine that is administered in drops to the eyes, typically to relieve dryness or treat eye conditions.
liquid medicine for eyes

Examples

The doctor prescribed a new eyedrop to treat his allergies.
She used an eyedrop every morning to relieve her dry eyes.
Please tilt your head back before I apply the eyedrop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eyewash - a liquid for washing the eyes

2. NOUN

/ ˈaɪˌdrɑp / (AmE), / ˈaɪˌdrɒp / (BrE)
A single drop of liquid, especially medicinal, intended to be administered into the eye.
a drop of liquid for the eye
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed eyedrop medication to reduce the redness.
You should use one eyedrop in each eye every morning.
After the surgery, he needed a special eyedrop to prevent infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eye dropper - a tool used to dispense drops into the eye

Etymology

From 'eye' + 'drop', denoting a drop used in the eye.
