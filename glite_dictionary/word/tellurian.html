tellurian

1. NOUN

/ təˈlʊriən / (AmE), / tɛˈljʊəriən / (BrE)
an inhabitant of the earth; a human being
an inhabitant of the earth

Examples

Science fiction often refers to humans as tellurians when depicting life on other planets.
The alien ambassador greeted the assembled group of tellurians with guarded curiosity.
In old adventure novels, explorers might be described as bold tellurians braving the unknown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earthling - an inhabitant of the earth, especially as contrasted with a being from another planet
  • Antonym: extraterrestrial - a being from outside the earth or its atmosphere

Etymology

From Latin 'tellus' (earth) + '-ian' (pertaining to).

2. ADJ

/ təˈlʊriən / (AmE), / tɛˈljʊəriən / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of land, as distinct from the sea or air
relating to land (not sea or air)

Examples

The tellurian environment supports a wide variety of animal and plant life.
Scientists studied both marine and tellurian ecosystems to compare their biodiversity.
Unlike aquatic organisms, tellurian species face challenges such as drought and soil erosion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrestrial - relating to the earth or land
  • Antonym: marine - relating to the sea
  • Antonym: aerial - relating to the air

Etymology

From Latin 'tellus, telluris' (earth, land).
