suffragism

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌfrəˌdʒɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈsʌfrədʒɪzəm / (BrE)
The belief in or movement advocating the extension of the right to vote, especially to women.
belief in extending voting rights

Examples

The rise of suffragism in the nineteenth century marked a turning point in the struggle for women's rights.
Many prominent activists devoted their lives to advancing the cause of suffragism.
Historical accounts of suffragism reveal the determination and resilience of its supporters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suffrage - the right to vote in political elections

Etymology

From suffrage (from Latin suffragium, 'vote') + -ism, denoting a movement or ideology.
