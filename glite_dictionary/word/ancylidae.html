Ancylidae

1. NOUN

/ ænˈsɪlɪˌdi / (AmE), / ænˈsɪlɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, freshwater snails in the class Gastropoda, often found attached to rocks or aquatic plants.
family of freshwater snails

Examples

Members of the Ancylidae are commonly found in clear streams and ponds.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the family Ancylidae.
The Ancylidae are known for their limpets, which cling tightly to submerged surfaces.

Etymology

From Greek ankylos (curved, bent) and the taxonomic suffix -idae.

2. NOUN

/ ænˈsɪlɪˌdi / (AmE), / ænˈsɪlɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, freshwater, air-breathing gastropod mollusks, commonly known as freshwater limpets.
family of freshwater limpets

Examples

The Ancylidae are found in various freshwater habitats around the world.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Ancylidae family in the river.
Members of Ancylidae are known for their limpet-like shells.

Etymology

From New Latin Ancylidae, based on the type genus Ancylus.
