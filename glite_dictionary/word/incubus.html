incubus

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋ.kjə.bəs / (AmE), / ˈɪŋ.kjʊ.bəs / (BrE)
A situation or feeling that causes great distress or anxiety, resembling a terrifying dream.
distressing or nightmarish situation

Examples

The shadow of war hung over the region like an incubus for years.
His guilt became an incubus that haunted him day and night.
The economic crisis proved to be an incubus for the country's leaders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nightmare - a frightening or unpleasant dream or experience

Etymology

From Latin 'incubus' (nightmare, evil spirit), from 'incubare' (to lie upon).

3. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋkjəˌbəs / (AmE), / ˈɪŋkjʊbəs / (BrE)
(in folklore) a male demon believed to lie upon sleeping persons, especially women, and engage in sexual activity with them.
male demon in folklore; attacks sleepers

Examples

According to legend, an incubus visits women in their sleep, causing disturbing nightmares.
During the Middle Ages, people believed that an incubus was responsible for unexplained pregnancies.
The old book details rituals to ward off an incubus from a sleeping household.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: succubus - a female demon believed to have sexual intercourse with sleeping men

Etymology

From Late Latin 'incubus' (nightmare, evil spirit), from Latin 'incubare' (to lie upon).
