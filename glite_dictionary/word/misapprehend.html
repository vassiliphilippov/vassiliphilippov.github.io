to misapprehend

1. VERB

/ ˌmɪs.æ.prɪˈhɛnd / (AmE), / ˌmɪs.æ.prɪˈhɛnd / (BrE)
to interpret or understand (something) incorrectly; to misunderstand
to interpret incorrectly

Examples

It is easy to misapprehend the tone of an email if you read it quickly.
She misapprehended the main point of his argument and responded defensively.
They misapprehend the law if they think it supports their case.

Synset Examples

Don't misinterpret my comments as criticism
She misconstrued my remarks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to misunderstand - to fail to understand correctly
  • Antonym: to apprehend - to understand or perceive

Etymology

From Latin mis- (wrongly) + apprehendere (to seize, grasp).
