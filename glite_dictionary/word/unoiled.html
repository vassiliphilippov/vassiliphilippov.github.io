unoiled

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɔɪld / (AmE), / ʌnˈɔɪld / (BrE)
not treated or coated with oil; lacking an application of oil.
not treated with oil

Examples

The mechanic explained that the gears became rusty because they were unoiled.
She avoided sitting on the old, unoiled wooden bench because it looked rough and splintery.
The wheels turned with difficulty along the dusty, unoiled road.

Synset Examples

dusty unoiled roads
a dull unoiled table

Related Terms

  • Antonym: oiled - treated or coated with oil
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01961969-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɔɪld / (AmE), / ʌnˈɔɪld / (BrE)
not having oil applied or rubbed into the surface; lacking a coating of oil.
without oil on the surface

Examples

Always start cooking pancakes on an unoiled frying pan for best results.
The unoiled wood will absorb moisture and may eventually crack.
It is important to keep machinery unoiled only when instructed by the manufacturer.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: oiled - having oil applied to the surface
