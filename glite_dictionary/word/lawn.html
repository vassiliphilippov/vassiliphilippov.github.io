lawn

1. NOUN

/ lɔn / (AmE), / lɔːn / (BrE)
an area of short, even grass that is regularly mowed, especially in gardens, parks, or around buildings
an area of regularly mown grass

Examples

The children played soccer on the freshly cut lawn behind the house.
She spent Saturday morning watering the lawn and trimming the hedges.
A picnic blanket was spread out on the green lawn in the park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meadow - a field of grass, often with wildflowers, not regularly mowed

Etymology

From Middle English launde (glade, pasture), from Old French lande (heath, moor).
