coenobitical

1. ADJ

/ siːnəˈbɪtɪkəl / (AmE), / siːnəˈbɪtɪkəl / (BrE)
of or relating to cenobites or their practices of communal living, especially in monasteries
relating to cenobites or communal monastic living

Examples

The monks adhered to a coenobitical lifestyle, sharing all possessions and daily routines.
Early Christian monasteries were often organized according to coenobitical principles.
Their coenobitical existence centered on collective worship, labor, and study.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: eremitical - relating to hermits or solitary religious life

Etymology

From Late Latin coenobiticus, from coenobium (monastery), from Greek koinobion (community).
