curium

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjʊriəm / (AmE), / ˈkjʊəriəm / (BrE)
a radioactive metallic chemical element (symbol Cm), produced by bombarding plutonium with helium nuclei; a member of the actinide series
a radioactive metallic chemical element (Cm)

Examples

Scientists first produced curium by bombarding plutonium with alpha particles in a laboratory.
The radioactive properties of curium make it useful as a power source in space probes.
Handling curium requires specialized equipment due to its intense radioactivity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plutonium - a radioactive metallic element used in nuclear reactors and weapons
  • Degree Higher: americium - a synthetic radioactive element; member of the actinide series

Etymology

Named after Marie and Pierre Curie, pioneering scientists in radioactivity.
