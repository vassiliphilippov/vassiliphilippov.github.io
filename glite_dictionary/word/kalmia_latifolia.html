Kalmia latifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˈkælmi.ə læˈtɪfəliə / (AmE), / ˈkalmɪə latɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
a North American evergreen shrub with glossy leaves and white or rose-colored flowers, commonly known as mountain laurel
North American evergreen shrub (mountain laurel)

Examples

The garden's centerpiece is a beautiful Kalmia latifolia in full bloom.
Botanists often study the growth patterns of Kalmia latifolia in Appalachian forests.
Our hiking trail was lined with flowering Kalmia latifolia shrubs this spring.

Etymology

Genus named after Swedish botanist Pehr Kalm; 'latifolia' from Latin 'latus' (broad) and 'folium' (leaf).
