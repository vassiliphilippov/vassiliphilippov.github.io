nutcracker

1. NOUN

/ ˈnʌtˌkrækər / (AmE), / ˈnʌtˌkrækə / (BrE)
A device consisting of two levers hinged together, used to crack open the shells of nuts.
device for cracking nuts open

Examples

Can you pass me the nutcracker? I can't open this pecan without it.
She bought a new nutcracker shaped like a squirrel for the kitchen.
The nutcracker made it easy to break the hard walnut shells.

Etymology

Formed from 'nut' + 'cracker', referring to its function.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnʌtˌkrækər / (AmE), / ˈnʌtˌkrækə / (BrE)
a type of small, short-tailed songbird of the crow family (genus Nucifraga) with strong feet and a sharp beak, that feeds on small nuts and insects
a type of songbird that feeds on nuts and insects

Examples

The spotted nutcracker is often seen in alpine forests searching for pine seeds.
During our hike, we spotted a nutcracker feeding on insects near the trail.
The nutcracker is known for its habit of storing nuts for the winter.

3. NOUN

/ ˈnʌtˌkrækər / (AmE), / ˈnʌtˌkrækə / (BrE)
a large, speckled bird of the crow family (genus Nucifraga) that feeds mainly on nuts, especially pine nuts
a bird that feeds on nuts

Examples

The Siberian nutcracker is famous for storing thousands of pine seeds for winter.
We spotted a nutcracker foraging in the alpine forest during our hike.
Several nutcrackers gathered around the tree, feeding on its cones.
