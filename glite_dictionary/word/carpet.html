carpet

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːr.pət / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.pɪt / (BrE)
a thick or extensive layer covering a surface, especially when resembling a carpet in appearance or effect
a surface layer resembling a carpet

Examples

After the storm, a carpet of leaves covered the sidewalk.
In the spring, the meadow was transformed by a carpet of wildflowers.
A thick carpet of moss blanketed the forest floor.

Synset Examples

a carpet of flowers
the larvae of some moths spin a web that resembles a carpet

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːr.pət / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.pɪt / (BrE)
A thick woven or tufted fabric used as a floor covering, usually with a nap or pile.
thick fabric floor covering

Examples

We replaced the old carpet in the living room with a new one.
He accidentally spilled coffee on the beige carpet.
The soft carpet muffled the sound of footsteps in the hallway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rug - a small piece of thick fabric for covering part of a floor

Etymology

From Old French *carpite*, from Medieval Latin *carpita* (thick woolen cloth), from Latin *carpere* (to pluck).
