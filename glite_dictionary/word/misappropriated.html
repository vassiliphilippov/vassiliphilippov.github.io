misappropriated

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɪs.əˈproʊ.pri.eɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌmɪs.əˈprəʊ.pri.eɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
(of money or property) dishonestly taken or used for one's own purposes, especially when entrusted to one's care.
illegally or dishonestly taken or used
legal

Examples

The organization found that several accounts held misappropriated funds, triggering an investigation.
He was fired after the audit revealed he had used misappropriated company resources for personal projects.
Investigators discovered a trail of misappropriated assets traced back to the charity's former director.

Synset Examples

the banker absconded with embezzled funds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embezzled - (especially of money) stolen by someone entrusted with its care

Etymology

From 'misappropriate' (to take dishonestly for one's own use), from Latin 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'appropriate'.
