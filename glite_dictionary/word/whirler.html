whirler

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɜrlər / (AmE), / ˈwɜːlə / (BrE)
a mechanism or device that revolves or spins around a central point
revolving mechanism or device

Examples

The playground features a whirler that children can push and ride around.
Engineers adjusted the whirler to ensure smooth, balanced rotation of the parts.
The museum displayed an antique whirler used in early electrical experiments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spinner - a device or person that spins
  • Synonym: rotator - a device or part that rotates or causes rotation

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'whirl' plus the suffix '-er' denoting an agent or thing that performs an action.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɝːlər / (AmE), / ˈwɜːlə / (BrE)
A member of a Sufi sect, especially a dervish, who performs ecstatic dances involving spinning or whirling as a form of spiritual practice.
Sufi dancer who spins ecstatically

Examples

The whirler moved gracefully, her skirts billowing as she spun faster.
Crowds gathered to watch the whirler perform the traditional Sufi dance.
Known as a whirler, he trains for years to master the art of spiritual spinning.

Etymology

From English 'whirl' (to spin rapidly) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
