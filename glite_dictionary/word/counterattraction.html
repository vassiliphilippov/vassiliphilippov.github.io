counterattraction

1. NOUN

/ ˌkaʊntərəˈtrækʃən / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntərəˈtrækʃən / (BrE)
a rival attraction that competes for attention with another
a competing or rival attraction

Examples

The new amusement park served as a counterattraction to the annual county fair.
A major sporting event was scheduled as a counterattraction to keep viewers from watching the rival network's show.
They needed a strong counterattraction to prevent guests from leaving early for the downtown concert.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: attraction - something that draws attention or interest

Etymology

Formed from 'counter-' (against, opposing) and 'attraction' (something that draws attention).
