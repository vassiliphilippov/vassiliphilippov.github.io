foamy

1. ADJ

/ ˈfoʊmi / (AmE), / ˈfəʊmi / (BrE)
(of a liquid or substance) containing or covered with a mass of small bubbles; frothy.
filled or covered with bubbles

Examples

The waves crashed onto the shore, leaving behind a foamy layer on the sand.
Pouring the soda too fast made it foamy at the top of the glass.
She enjoyed a foamy cappuccino at her favorite café.

Synset Examples

bubbling champagne
foamy (or frothy) beer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frothy - (of a liquid) full of or covered with bubbles

Etymology

From foam + -y, dating from Middle English.

2. ADJ

/ ˈfoʊmi / (AmE), / ˈfəʊmi / (BrE)
producing or covered with foam, especially lathery sweat or saliva due to exhaustion or disease
covered with foam or froth (from sweat or saliva)

Examples

The horse's neck was foamy with sweat after the race.
He collapsed at the finish line, his mouth foamy from exhaustion.
The rabid dog was found with a foamy mouth, alarming the villagers.

Synset Examples

the rabid animal's frothing mouth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frothy - full of or covered with a mass of small bubbles

Etymology

From 'foam' + '-y', with 'foam' from Old English 'fām'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01180331-s
Synset POS: s
