thumbtack

1. VERB

/ ˈθʌmˌtæk / (AmE), / ˈθʌmˌtæk / (BrE)
to fasten or secure something using thumbtacks
fasten with thumbtacks
american

Examples

She thumbtacked her favorite photo to the corkboard above her desk.
They thumbtacked the map to the wall before the meeting started.
You can easily thumbtack these posters to the classroom bulletin board.

Synset Examples

The teacher thumbtacked the notice on the bulletin board

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pin - to attach or fasten with a pin

Etymology

From 'thumb' + 'tack', first used in 19th-century America.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01360359-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈθʌmˌtæk / (AmE), / ˈθʌm.tæk / (BrE)
A short pin with a broad, flat head, used for fastening papers to a board or wall.
short pin with broad head for fastening papers

Examples

She used a thumbtack to pin the note on the bulletin board.
After searching the drawer, I finally found a thumbtack for my map.
Be careful not to step on a thumbtack left on the floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pushpin - A small pin for attaching papers to a board; often with a cylindrical plastic head (American usage)
  • Degree Higher: pin - A small slender object for fastening or attaching items

Etymology

Formed from 'thumb' and 'tack', first used in late 19th century America.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04438879-n
Synset POS: n
