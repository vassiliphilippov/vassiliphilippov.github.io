felted

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɛltɪd / (AmE), / ˈfɛltɪd / (BrE)
made by combining fibers with a binder using heat, moisture, and pressure to form a dense material
made by felting fibers together

Examples

The craftsman made a felted hat using traditional techniques.
She decorated her living room with a beautiful felted rug.
The museum displayed felted artworks created from dyed wool.

Synset Examples

felt is a felted cloth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: felt - a fabric made by matting fibers, especially wool

Etymology

From the verb "felt" meaning to make into felt, from Old High German *filz* (felt).
