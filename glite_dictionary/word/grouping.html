grouping

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡruːpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡruːpɪŋ / (BrE)
the action or process of putting people or things together into groups based on shared characteristics or purpose
the activity of forming groups

Examples

The grouping of the students by ability made the lessons more effective.
She spent the afternoon on the grouping of the data for her research project.
Different grouping strategies can lead to very different results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classification - the act of arranging things into categories or classes

Etymology

From 'group' + '-ing', from French 'groupe'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡruːpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡruːpɪŋ / (BrE)
a system or arrangement for classifying things or people into groups based on shared characteristics
system of classification into groups

Examples

The new biological grouping reflects recent discoveries in genetics.
The company introduced a grouping of products to better serve different customer needs.
European countries have often formed economic groupings to enhance cooperation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classification - the process of categorizing or arranging into groups
  • Synonym: division - the act or process of dividing into parts or groups

Etymology

From the verb 'group' + suffix '-ing', first attested in the late 18th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05741035-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡruːpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡruːpɪŋ / (BrE)
a number of people or things that are considered or classed together as a unit
a set of things or people classed together

Examples

His paintings featured an unusual grouping of colors.
We need to create a grouping of students for the project.
This report analyzes the grouping of species by habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: group - a number of people or things that are together or considered together

Etymology

From the verb 'group' + -ing, denoting action or result.
