to deprive

1. VERB

/ dɪˈpraɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈpraɪv / (BrE)
to take something important or necessary away from someone or something
take something away from

Examples

The new policy may deprive some families of essential benefits.
No one should deprive children of an education.
Chronic illness can deprive people of their independence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rob - to take property unlawfully from a person or place
  • Antonym: to restore - to give back or return something that was taken away

Etymology

From Latin privare, meaning 'to bereave, rob, deprive'.

2. VERB

/ dɪˈpraɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈpraɪv / (BrE)
to prevent someone from having or enjoying something, especially something considered necessary or desirable
prevent from having something

Examples

The new law will deprive many people of their basic rights.
No one should deprive children of access to education.
Being isolated like that can deprive a person of human contact.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rob - to take something away from someone by force or threat
  • Antonym: grant - to give or allow someone something

Etymology

From Latin 'deprivare' (to bereave, deprive), from 'de-' (removal) + 'privare' (to rob).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02318230-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ dɪˈpraɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈpraɪv / (BrE)
to take something important or necessary away from someone, often causing them to lack it
take something away from someone

Examples

The new law will deprive low-income families of vital support.
She was deprived of sleep for several nights due to the noise outside.
His illness may deprive him of the chance to travel this year.

Synset Examples

The Nazis stripped the Jews of all their assets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strip - to take something away from someone, especially rights or possessions
  • Synonym: to rob - to take property from a person or place illegally
  • Antonym: to restore - to give back what was taken away

Etymology

From Latin deprivare (to deprive, rob), from de- (removal) + privare (to deprive, release).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02319255-v
Synset POS: v
