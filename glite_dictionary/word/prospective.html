prospective

1. ADJ

/ prəˈspɛktɪv / (AmE), / prəˈspɛktɪv / (BrE)
relating to or likely to happen in the future; expected to become something or someone.
relating to the future; expected

Examples

The company is reviewing the prospective benefits of the new project.
She is a prospective candidate for the job opening next month.
All prospective students must submit their applications by July 31.

Synset Examples

prospective earnings
a prospective mother
a prospective bride
the statute is solely prospective in operation

Related Terms

  • Antonym: retrospective - looking back on or dealing with past events

Etymology

From Latin 'prospectivus' (looking forward), from 'prospicere' (to look forward).
