Hesperis matronalis

1. NOUN

/ hɛsˈpɛrɪs ˌmætrəˈneɪlɪs / (AmE), / hɛsˈpɛːrɪs ˌmætrəˈneɪlɪs / (BrE)
A Eurasian herbaceous plant of the mustard family, grown for its fragrant lilac or white flowers, often called dame's violet.
fragrant plant, also known as dame's violet

Examples

The Hesperis matronalis in her garden gave off a sweet scent at dusk.
Many wildflower mixes now include Hesperis matronalis for its fragrant spring blooms.
Botanists noted that Hesperis matronalis has spread widely across North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dame's violet - common English name for Hesperis matronalis

Etymology

From Latin genus Hesperis and species epithet matronalis, meaning 'of a matron' or 'lady's.'
