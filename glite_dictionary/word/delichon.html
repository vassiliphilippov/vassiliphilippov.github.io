Delichon

1. NOUN

/ dɛˈlaɪkɒn / (AmE), / dɛˈliːkɒn / (BrE)
A genus of swallows in the family Hirundinidae, known as house martins, comprising three species found in Eurasia.
genus of house martins (swallows)

Examples

The genus Delichon includes several species commonly called house martins.
Ornithologists studied the migration patterns of birds in the Delichon genus.
Many Delichon nests can be found under the eaves of village houses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: house martin - a small migratory songbird of the swallow family Hirundinidae

Etymology

From Greek δελῑχών (delikhōn), an old name for the swallow.

2. NOUN

a genus of swallows in the family Hirundinidae, comprising species commonly known as house martins
a genus of house martins (swallows)

Examples

The genus Delichon is well known for its distinctive mud nests under buildings.
Researchers have identified three species within the Delichon genus.
In Europe, the common house martin belongs to the genus Delichon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: house martin - a small migratory songbird of the swallow family, genus Delichon
  • Degree Higher: Hirundinidae - the family of birds known as swallows and martins

From Greek delichos (a swallow, bird name)
