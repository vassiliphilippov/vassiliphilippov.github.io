satisfier

1. NOUN

/ ˈsætɪsˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈsætɪsˌfaɪə / (BrE)
a person or thing that brings satisfaction or fulfills needs or desires
person or thing that brings satisfaction

Examples

The new company policy proved to be a real satisfier for many employees.
Money can be a powerful satisfier, but it does not guarantee happiness.
In the study, feedback was identified as the primary satisfier for student motivation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benefactor - a person who gives help or advantage; similar sense of providing something desirable
  • Antonym: dissatisfier - a person or thing that causes dissatisfaction or fails to fulfill needs

Etymology

From satisfy + -er, originally from Latin satisfacere (to satisfy) + English agent suffix -er.
