calumniation

1. NOUN

/ kəˌlʌm.niˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / kəˌlʌm.niˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of making false and malicious statements about someone to damage their reputation; slander or false accusation
false and malicious accusation; slander

Examples

The newspaper was sued for its calumniation of the politician’s character.
He suffered greatly from public calumniation despite his innocence.
Acts of calumniation can irreparably harm both personal and professional reputations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defamation - the act of damaging someone's good reputation
  • Synonym: slander - the action of making a false spoken statement damaging to a person's reputation
  • Antonym: vindication - the action of clearing someone of blame or suspicion

Etymology

From Latin calumniatio (false accusation, slander), from calumniari (to accuse falsely).
