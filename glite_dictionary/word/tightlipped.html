tightlipped

1. ADJ

/ ˌtaɪtˈlɪpt / (AmE), / ˌtaɪtˈlɪpt / (BrE)
unwilling to talk or give information; secretive or reserved in speech
unwilling to reveal information

Examples

When asked about the negotiations, the spokesperson remained tightlipped and refused to give any details.
He was always tightlipped about his personal life, never sharing much with others.
Despite reporters' persistent questions, the mayor stayed tightlipped concerning the upcoming policy changes.

Synset Examples

although they knew her whereabouts her friends kept close about it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reticent - unwilling to speak freely; reserved or restrained
  • Antonym: talkative - inclined to talk a lot

Etymology

From the words 'tight' and 'lipped,' literally meaning to keep one's lips tightly closed, figuratively extended to secrecy.
