stubbiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈstʌbinəs / (AmE), / ˈstʌbinɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being short and thick or broad in shape
quality of being short and thick

Examples

The stubbiness of the pencil made it difficult to hold while writing.
He admired the stubbiness of the puppy's little legs as it played.
The plant's stubbiness allows it to survive strong winds in open fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stubby - short and thick; broad and blunt
  • Antonym: slenderness - the property of being thin or slim

Etymology

From 'stubby' + '-ness'; 'stubby' meaning short and thick.
