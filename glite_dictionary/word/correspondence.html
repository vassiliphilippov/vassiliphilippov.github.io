correspondence

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔːr.əˈspɑːn.dəns / (AmE), / ˌkɒr.ɪˈspɒn.dəns / (BrE)
agreement or similarity between two or more things, especially in results or observations
agreement; similarity (especially in results)

Examples

The two sets of results showed a remarkable correspondence in their findings.
There is no correspondence between the predicted outcome and the actual data.
Scientists look for correspondence between theory and experiment to validate models.

Synset Examples

there was no agreement between theory and measurement
the results of two tests were in correspondence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agreement - harmony or consistency among facts or results
  • Antonym: discrepancy - lack of agreement or similarity between facts

Etymology

From Medieval Latin correspondentia, from Latin correspondere (to correspond).

3. NOUN

/ ˌkɔːrəˈspɑːndəns / (AmE), / ˌkɒrɪˈspɒndəns / (BrE)
(mathematics) The property by which one part of a figure or object exactly matches another part on the opposite side of a dividing line or plane; symmetry.
exact matching on opposite sides (mathematics)

Examples

The butterfly's wings show perfect correspondence across the body’s midline.
In geometry, a line of correspondence divides a figure into two mirror images.
The artist studied the correspondence of shapes on either side of the sculpture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: symmetry - the quality of being made up of exactly similar parts facing each other

Etymology

From Medieval Latin correspondentia, from Latin correspondēre (to correspond).
