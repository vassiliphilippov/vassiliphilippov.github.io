Cotinga

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈtɪŋɡə / (AmE), / kəˈtɪŋɡə / (BrE)
A genus of brightly colored South American birds in the family Cotingidae, known for their vibrant plumage and distinctive calls.
a genus of South American birds

Examples

The Cotinga is admired for its brilliant blue and purple feathers in the rainforests of South America.
Birdwatchers often travel to the Amazon to spot rare species within the Cotinga genus.
Scientists recently discovered a new member of the Cotinga lineage in the remote cloud forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cotingidae - the family of birds to which Cotinga belongs

Etymology

From Portuguese 'cotinga', from Tupi 'catingá', meaning a type of bird.
