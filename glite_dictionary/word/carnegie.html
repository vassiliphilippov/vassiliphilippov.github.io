Carnegie

1. PROPN

/ kɑrˈnɛɡi / (AmE), / kɑːˈnɛːɡi / (BrE)
Andrew Carnegie (1835–1919), a United States industrialist and philanthropist who contributed greatly to education, libraries, and research through his endowments.
U.S. industrialist and philanthropist (1835–1919)

Examples

Carnegie built a steel empire and later gave much of his fortune to charitable causes.
Many American libraries were funded by Carnegie's philanthropy in the early twentieth century.
Carnegie is remembered for his contributions to education and the arts.
