to discomfit

1. VERB

/ dɪsˈkʌmfɪt / (AmE), / dɪsˈkʌmfɪt / (BrE)
to make someone feel uneasy, confused, or embarrassed; to unsettle someone so that they lose their composure
make someone lose composure

Examples

Her unexpected question seemed to discomfit the confident speaker.
The team was clearly discomfited by the intense pressure from their opponents.
He tried to discomfit his rival with a sudden, challenging remark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to embarrass - to make someone feel self-conscious or ill at ease
  • Synonym: to confuse - to make someone unable to think clearly or act with composure
  • Antonym: to reassure - to make someone feel less anxious or more confident

Etymology

From Old French *desconfire* (to defeat), from Vulgar Latin *discomfitare*, from Latin *dis-* (apart) + *comficere* (to make, accomplish).
