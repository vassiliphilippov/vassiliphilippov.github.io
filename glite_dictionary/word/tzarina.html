tzarina

1. NOUN

/ tsɑːˈriːnə / (AmE), / tsɑːˈriːnə / (BrE)
the wife or widow of a czar, especially in Russia; an empress
wife or widow of a czar

Examples

The coronation of the new tzarina was celebrated with great pomp throughout Russia.
After the czar's death, the tzarina played an influential role in court politics.
Historians have written extensively about the lives of the Russian czars and their tzarinas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: czarina - wife or widow of a czar (alternative spelling)

Etymology

From Russian 'tsaritsa', feminine form of 'tsar'; 'tzarina' influenced by Italian 'zarina' and French 'tsarine'.
