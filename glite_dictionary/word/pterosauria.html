Pterosauria

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛrəˈsɔriə / (AmE), / ˌtɛrəˈsɔːriə / (BrE)
an order of extinct flying reptiles that lived during the Mesozoic Era, known as pterosaurs
order of extinct flying reptiles

Examples

Fossils of Pterosauria have been found in many parts of the world.
Scientists believe that Pterosauria were the first vertebrates to achieve powered flight.
The extinction of Pterosauria coincided with the end of the Cretaceous period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pterosaur - any individual member of Pterosauria, an extinct flying reptile

Etymology

From Greek pteron (wing) + sauros (lizard), referring to winged lizards.

2. NOUN

/ tɛr.əˌsɔr.i.ə / (AmE), / tɛr.əˈsɔː.rɪ.ə / (BrE)
an order of extinct flying reptiles that lived during the Mesozoic Era, known as pterosaurs
order of extinct flying reptiles (pterosaurs)

Examples

Fossils of Pterosauria have been discovered on every continent.
The wings of Pterosauria were formed by a membrane of skin, muscle, and other tissues.
Scientists believe that members of Pterosauria were the first vertebrates capable of powered flight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pterosaur - an extinct flying reptile of the order Pterosauria

Etymology

From Greek pteron (wing) + sauros (lizard), referring to their winged form.
