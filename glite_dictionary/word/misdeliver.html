to misdeliver

1. VERB

/ ˌmɪsdɪˈlɪvɚ / (AmE), / ˌmɪsdɪˈlɪvə / (BrE)
to deliver something to the wrong address or recipient by mistake
deliver to the wrong address

Examples

The courier accidentally misdelivered my package to a neighbor's house.
If you misdeliver an important letter, it can cause serious problems for the recipient.
She realized she had misdelivered several parcels after checking her delivery list.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to deliver - to bring or hand over (a letter, parcel, or ordered goods) to the proper recipient or address

Etymology

Formed by the prefix 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'deliver'.
