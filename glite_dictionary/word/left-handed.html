left-handed

1. ADJ

/ ˌlɛftˈhændɪd / (AmE), / ˌlɛftˈhændɪd / (BrE)
using the left hand by preference, or designed to be used with the left hand
using or for the left hand

Examples

My brother is left-handed and always uses special golf clubs.
She bought a pair of left-handed scissors for her art class.
These left-handed desks are designed for students who write with their left hand.

Synset Examples

left-handed golfers need left-handed clubs
left-handed scissors

Related Terms

  • Antonym: right-handed - using or intended for the right hand

2. ADJ

/ ˌlɛftˈhændɪd / (AmE), / ˌlɛftˈhændɪd / (BrE)
(of a rotation or movement) turning or rotating to the left, especially in a counterclockwise direction.
rotating or turning to the left

Examples

The screw has a left-handed thread, which means it tightens counterclockwise.
In physics, certain molecules can have left-handed or right-handed rotation.
He replaced the part with a left-handed version to match the machinery's requirements.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: right-handed - rotating or turning to the right

Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ lɛft ˈhændɪd / (AmE), / lɛft ˈhændɪd / (BrE)
(of marriages) illicit or informal; not officially recognized by law or religious authorities.
illicit or informal (of marriages)
archaic

Examples

In the 18th century, left-handed marriages were often kept secret from society.
Some historical records mention left-handed unions between settlers and indigenous women.
Legal authorities at the time did not acknowledge left-handed marriages as valid.

Synset Examples

in Colonial America left-handed marriages between Frenchmen and Indians were frequent

Etymology

From the association of the left hand with irregular or unconventional acts, in contrast to the 'right' hand as representing legitimacy.

4. ADJ

/ ˈlɛftˌhændɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɛftˌhændɪd / (BrE)
(of a remark or compliment) made in an insincere or ironic way so as to convey a hidden insult or ambiguity rather than genuine praise.
insincere or ironic (of remark or compliment)

Examples

She gave him a left-handed compliment that sounded more like a criticism.
His apology felt left-handed, as if he didn't actually regret what he'd done.
The review offered several left-handed praises that left the author feeling insulted.

Synset Examples

a left-handed compliment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: backhanded - indirect or insincere (of remark or compliment)
  • Antonym: genuine - sincere and honest (of remark or compliment)

Etymology

From the traditional association of the left side with awkwardness or untrustworthiness in European symbolism.

5. ADJ

/ lɛft ˈhændɪd / (AmE), / lɛft ˈhændɪd / (BrE)
(of a marriage) between a person of royal or noble birth and a person of lower rank, valid but with the agreement that the lower-ranking spouse's status does not change and their children do not inherit the titles or property of the noble parent.
marriage of noble with lower rank, limiting rights

Examples

Historically, a left-handed marriage allowed a prince to wed a commoner without granting her royal privileges.
Offspring from a left-handed marriage could not inherit their father's title or lands.
The concept of a left-handed marriage reflects strict aristocratic traditions on succession and rank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morganatic - (of a marriage) a union of unequal social rank, with limitation on inheritance and titles

Etymology

Refers to the 'left hand' in wedding ceremonies to symbolize unequal union; linked to the custom of using the left hand in morganatic marriages in Europe.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01409933-s
Synset POS: s
