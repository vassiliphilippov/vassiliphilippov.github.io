spray-dried

1. ADJ

/ spreɪˌdraɪd / (AmE), / spreɪˌdraɪd / (BrE)
dried by spraying a liquid substance into hot air, causing it to form a powder.
dried by spraying into hot air to powder

Examples

Many instant coffee products are spray-dried to preserve flavor and extend shelf life.
The spray-dried milk powder dissolves quickly in water, making it convenient for use.
Researchers tested the stability of the spray-dried pharmaceutical compound under various conditions.

Etymology

Formed from 'spray' + past participle of 'dry', describing the drying process.
