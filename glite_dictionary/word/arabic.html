Arabic

1. ADJ

/ ˈærəbɪk / (AmE), / ˈærəbɪk / (BrE)
relating to the people, language, or culture of Arabs.
relating to Arabs or their language/culture

Examples

She is fluent in Arabic and English.
The museum has a collection of Arabic manuscripts.
We enjoyed traditional Arabic music at the festival.

Synset Examples

Arabic languages

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arabian - relating to Arabia or its people, often more geographically focused

Etymology

From Latin Arabicus, from Greek Arabikos (of Arabia), from Arabia.

2. NOUN

/ ˈærəbɪk / (AmE), / ˈærəbɪk / (BrE)
the Semitic language of the Arabs, spoken in many countries in various regional dialects and used as the language of the Qur'an
Semitic language spoken in Arab countries

Examples

Many people study Arabic to read the Qur'an in its original language.
She speaks fluent Arabic after living in Cairo for five years.
There are different dialects of Arabic spoken across North Africa and the Middle East.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hebrew - another major Semitic language
  • Antonym: English - the Germanic language spoken in England and widely worldwide

Etymology

From Latin Arabicus, from Greek Arabikos, from Arabia.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07000717-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈærəbɪk / (AmE), / ˈærəbɪk / (BrE)
the Semitic language of the Arabs, spoken across the Middle East and North Africa in various dialects
the language of the Arabs

Examples

Many people around the world study Arabic to better understand Middle Eastern culture and history.
The holy book of Islam, the Quran, is written in Arabic.
She speaks fluent Arabic as well as English and French.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arabic script - the script used for writing Arabic and several other languages
  • Synonym: Aramaic - another Semitic language related to Arabic

Etymology

From Latin Arabicus, from Greek Arabikos ('Arabian'), from Arabia.

Synset POS: n
