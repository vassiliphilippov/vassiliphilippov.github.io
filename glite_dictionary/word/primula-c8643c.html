primula

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪmjələ / (AmE), / ˈprɪmjʊlə / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Primula, typically with short stems, tufted basal leaves, and clusters of colorful, showy flowers.
a plant genus with clusters of showy flowers

Examples

The garden was filled with bright yellow primula blooming in early spring.
She planted several varieties of primula along the edge of the pathway.
A single primula added a splash of color to the rock garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: primrose - a common species of Primula with pale yellow flowers

Etymology

From modern Latin Primula, diminutive of Latin primus (first), referring to its early flowering.
