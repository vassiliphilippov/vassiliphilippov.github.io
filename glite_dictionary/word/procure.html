to procure

1. VERB

/ prəˈkjʊr / (AmE), / prəˈkjʊə / (BrE)
to obtain or arrange sexual partners for someone, especially in an illegal or immoral way
arrange sexual partners for others

Examples

He was arrested for attempting to procure a prostitute for his client.
The organization was accused of illegally procuring women for wealthy businessmen.
Laws strictly prohibit anyone from procuring sexual services for others.

Etymology

From Latin procurare (to take care of, manage).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01183272-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ prəˈkjʊr / (AmE), / prəˈkjʊə / (BrE)
to obtain something, especially with effort or by special means
obtain with special effort

Examples

She managed to procure a rare book that was out of print for years.
The agents were tasked to procure vital information from the informant.
It can be difficult to procure tickets for a popular concert at the last minute.

Synset Examples

He procured extra cigarettes even though they were rationed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to obtain - to get something, especially by effort
  • Synonym: to acquire - to come into possession of something

Etymology

From Latin 'procurare', meaning to take care of, manage.
