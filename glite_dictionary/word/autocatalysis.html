autocatalysis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔtoʊkəˈtæləsɪs / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəkəˈtæləsɪs / (BrE)
a chemical reaction in which one of the products acts as a catalyst for the same reaction
product-driven catalysis in chemistry
medical

Examples

The process of autocatalysis accelerates as more product is formed.
Scientists studied autocatalysis to better understand complex reaction mechanisms.
Some biochemical pathways rely on autocatalysis for efficient self-amplification.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catalysis - acceleration of a chemical reaction by a catalyst

Etymology

From auto- (self) + catalysis (acceleration of a chemical reaction by a catalyst).
