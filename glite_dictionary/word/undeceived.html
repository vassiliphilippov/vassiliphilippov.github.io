undeceived

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.dɪˈsivd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.dɪˈsiːvd / (BrE)
no longer under a false belief or impression; having realized the truth about something
no longer deceived; aware of the truth

Examples

After examining the evidence, she was finally undeceived about his intentions.
He left the meeting undeceived by the politician’s promises.
The children soon became undeceived when the magic trick was explained.

Synset Examples

some people are still not disabused of the old idea that the universe revolves around the Earth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disabused - no longer holding a false idea; corrected in thinking
  • Antonym: deceived - misled or caused to believe something false

Etymology

From un- (not) + deceived, past participle of deceive; meaning 'no longer deceived'.
