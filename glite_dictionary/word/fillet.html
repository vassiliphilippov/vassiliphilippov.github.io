fillet

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪlɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɪlɪt / (BrE)
A type of lace characterized by a square mesh, often used in decorative work.
lace with a square mesh

Examples

The antique tablecloth was made from delicate fillet lace.
She spent months crafting a doily using the fillet technique.
Fillet lace patterns are popular among textile enthusiasts for their geometric designs.

Etymology

From Old French *filet* (net), diminutive of *fil* (thread), from Latin *filum* (thread).

8. NOUN

Examples

The restaurant specializes in grilled fillet served with seasonal vegetables.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: tenderloin - the whole tenderloin portion of beef from which fillets are cut
  • Degree Lower: sirloin - another cut of beef, generally less tender than fillet
  • Degree Higher: steak - a slice of meat, especially beef, cut across the grain

Etymology

From Old French *filet* (a thread, strip), diminutive of *fil* (thread), originally referring to a strip or band.
