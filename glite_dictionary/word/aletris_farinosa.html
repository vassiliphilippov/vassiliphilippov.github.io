Aletris farinosa

1. NOUN

/ əˈliːtrɪs fəˈrinoʊsə / (AmE), / əˈliːtrɪs fəˈrɪnəʊsə / (BrE)
A North American perennial herb of the lily family, known as colicroot, with white flowers and a scurfy perianth, found in the southeastern United States.
a perennial herb also called colicroot

Examples

Aletris farinosa is valued in traditional herbal medicine for its use in treating colic.
The sandy soils of pine woodlands are home to Aletris farinosa.
In bloom, Aletris farinosa produces a spike of small, white, star-shaped flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colicroot - another name for Aletris farinosa, a North American herb

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Aletris' and species epithet 'farinosa' meaning 'mealy' or 'covered with flour'.
