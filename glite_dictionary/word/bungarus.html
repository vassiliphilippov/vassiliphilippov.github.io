Bungarus

1. NOUN

/ bʌŋˈɡɑːrəs / (AmE), / bʌŋˈɡɑːrəs / (BrE)
a genus of highly venomous snakes known as kraits, native to South and Southeast Asia
genus of venomous snakes (kraits)

Examples

The genus Bungarus comprises several species of kraits found across Asia.
A scientist described a new species in the Bungarus group from Myanmar.
Most bites attributed to Bungarus occur during the warm, rainy season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: krait - a highly venomous snake of the genus Bungarus

Etymology

From modern Latin, from Sanskrit 'bhūngarā' (krait).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʌŋ.ɡə.rəs / (AmE), / ˈbʌŋ.ɡə.rəs / (BrE)
A genus of highly venomous snakes commonly known as kraits, found in South and Southeast Asia.
genus of venomous kraits
medical

Examples

The Bungarus genus includes some of the most venomous snakes in Asia.
Scientists are studying the venom composition of Bungarus species for medicinal research.
A bite from a Bungarus can be fatal without prompt medical treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: krait - any snake of the genus Bungarus, noted for its venom

Etymology

From Latinized form of Telugu 'bangaru' or Sanskrit 'bhāṅgāra', names for the krait.
