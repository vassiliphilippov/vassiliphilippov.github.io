to offload

1. VERB

/ ɑfˈloʊd / (AmE), / ɒfˈləʊd / (BrE)
to remove goods or cargo from a vehicle or container
remove goods from a vehicle or container

Examples

The workers began to offload the cargo as soon as the truck arrived.
It took two hours to offload all the supplies from the ship.
Can you help me offload these boxes from the van?

Synset Examples

unload the truck
offload the van

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to unload - to remove goods from a vehicle, container, or place

Etymology

Formed from 'off' + 'load', modeled on 'unload'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01490749-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈɔfˌloʊd / (AmE), / ˈɒfˌləʊd / (BrE)
to transfer computer data to a peripheral device, such as moving files from a computer to an external storage device
transfer computer data to another device

Examples

It's important to offload large media files to an external drive to free up space.
The technician offloaded all the logs onto a USB stick for backup.
Some systems automatically offload old data to a server overnight.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to upload - to transfer data to a central or remote computer system
  • Antonym: to download - to transfer data from a remote system to a local device

Etymology

Formed from 'off-' (away) + 'load'; figurative extension from physical to data transfer.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02237863-v
Synset POS: v
