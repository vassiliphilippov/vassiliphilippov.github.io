frequent

1. VERB

/ friˈkwɛnt / (AmE), / frɪˈkwent / (BrE)
to visit a place regularly or often
visit regularly

Examples

He used to frequent a coffee shop on Main Street every morning before work.
Many artists frequent the local gallery to find inspiration and meet others.
In his youth, he frequented jazz clubs across the city.

Synset Examples

She haunts the ballet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: haunt - to visit a place regularly

Etymology

From Latin frequentare (to visit often), from frequens (crowded, frequent).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01848373-v
Synset POS: v

2. ADJ

/ ˈfriːkwənt / (AmE), / ˈfriːkwənt / (BrE)
occurring or appearing often or at short intervals
happening often; recurring

Examples

This is a frequent problem in large cities.
He made frequent visits to his grandmother's house.
Power outages have become more frequent during the summer months.

Synset Examples

a frequent (or common) error is using the transitive verb ‘lay’ for the intransitive ‘lie’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: common - occurring often; frequently encountered
  • Antonym: rare - not occurring often; infrequent

Etymology

From Latin frequens, frequent- (crowded, repeated).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00488978-s
Synset POS: s

3. VERB

/ ˈfriːkwənt / (AmE), / ˈfriːkwənt / (BrE)
to visit a place regularly, especially as a customer or patron
visit a place regularly

Examples

Many artists frequent the small café on the corner for inspiration.
She used to frequent the old bookstore every Saturday morning.
Tourists often frequent the local markets when visiting the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to patronize - to be a regular customer of a shop or business

Etymology

From Latin frequentare (to visit regularly), from frequens (crowded, repeated).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02471557-v
Synset POS: v

4. ADJ

/ ˈfriːkwənt / (AmE), / ˈfriːkwənt / (BrE)
happening often or at short intervals; occurring repeatedly or habitually.
happening often; recurring regularly

Examples

The trains to the city are frequent during rush hour.
She is a frequent visitor to the local art gallery.
Complaints about the noise have become increasingly frequent over the past month.

Synset Examples

a frequent guest
frequent complaints

Related Terms

  • Antonym: infrequent - happening rarely or at long intervals
  • Synonym: regular - occurring at uniform intervals or patterns
  • Degree Lower: occasional - happening only from time to time
  • Degree Higher: constant - happening continuously without interruption

Etymology

From Latin 'frequens, frequentis' (crowded, often repeated).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01070231-a
Synset POS: a
