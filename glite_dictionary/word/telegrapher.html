telegrapher

1. NOUN

/ təˈlɛɡrəfər / (AmE), / tɪˈlɛɡrəfə / (BrE)
a person who sends and receives messages using a telegraph system, especially as a profession
a person who operates a telegraph

Examples

The telegrapher tapped out the urgent message late into the night.
My great-grandfather worked as a telegrapher on the railroad in the early 1900s.
During the war, the army telegrapher played a crucial role in communication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: telegraphist - a person who operates a telegraph, especially in British English

Etymology

Formed from 'telegraph' + '-er', indicating a person who operates a telegraph.
