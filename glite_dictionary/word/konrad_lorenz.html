Konrad Lorenz

1. PROPN

/ ˈkɑn.rɑd loʊˈrɛnts / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.rad lɒˈrɛnts / (BrE)
(1903–1989) An Austrian zoologist known for his pioneering studies of animal behavior, especially in birds, and for emphasizing the role of innate behavior patterns.
Austrian zoologist and pioneer in animal behavior

Examples

Konrad Lorenz is best known for his research on the imprinting behavior of geese and ducks.
Many students of psychology and biology study the work of Konrad Lorenz in their courses.
The theories developed by Konrad Lorenz have had a lasting impact on the field of ethology.
