Volvocaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɑːlvoʊˈkeɪ.si.iː / (AmE), / ˌvɒl.və(ʊ)ˈkeɪ.si.iː / (BrE)
A family of unicellular or colonial, biflagellate, free-swimming green algae found in freshwater environments.
family of biflagellate green algae
medical

Examples

The Volvocaceae are known for their spherical colonies that swim using coordinated flagella.
Research on Volvocaceae has advanced our understanding of multicellularity in algae.
Many freshwater ponds contain species belonging to the Volvocaceae family.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus 'Volvox' and the suffix '-aceae' denoting plant families.
