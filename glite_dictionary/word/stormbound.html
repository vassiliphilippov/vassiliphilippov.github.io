stormbound

1. ADJ

/ ˈstɔːrmbɑʊnd / (AmE), / ˈstɔːmbaʊnd / (BrE)
unable to proceed, leave, or move because of a storm; delayed or confined by stormy weather.
trapped or delayed by a storm

Examples

The hikers were stormbound in their mountain cabin for three days.
Hundreds of stormbound travelers filled the hotel lobby waiting for the weather to clear.
The ferry was stormbound, unable to leave the harbor until the waves subsided.

Synset Examples

the airport was filled with stormbound passengers

Etymology

Formed from 'storm' + 'bound'.
