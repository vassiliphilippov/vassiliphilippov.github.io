amazon

1. NOUN

/ ˈæməzɑn / (AmE), / ˈaməzən / (BrE)
a woman who is tall, strong, and powerful, often perceived as aggressive or commanding
tall, strong, powerful woman

Examples

The lead actress played an amazon who commanded respect from everyone around her.
At over six feet tall and fiercely independent, she was often called an amazon by her friends.
The novel features an amazon who defies traditional gender roles in her quest for justice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: virago - a woman of great strength or courage, often with a domineering personality

Etymology

From Greek Amazon (Ἀμαζών), legendary tribe of warrior women in Greek mythology.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæm.əˌzɑn / (AmE), / ˈæm.ə.zɒn / (BrE)
A chiefly green, tropical American parrot of the genus Amazona, known for its ability to mimic sounds and its popularity as a pet.
a type of tropical green parrot

Examples

The amazon squawked loudly from its perch beside the window.
He has kept a pet amazon for over ten years and taught it many words.
Wild amazons are often seen in the forests of Central and South America.

Etymology

From the scientific genus name 'Amazona', named after the Amazon region in South America.
