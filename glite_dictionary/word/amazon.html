Amazon

1. NOUN

/ ˈæm.əˌzɑn / (AmE), / ˈæm.ə.zən / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a member of a legendary race of women warriors from Scythia, reputed for their strength, bravery, and archery skills.
female warrior of Greek myth

Examples

The myth of the Amazon has inspired countless stories about powerful women in history and legend.
Ancient Greek art often depicted an Amazon in battle attire, wielding a bow and shield.
She commanded her troops with the courage of an Amazon from the old epics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warrior - a person experienced in warfare or combat

Etymology

From Greek 'Amazōn', name of a mythical race of female warriors.

2. PROPN

/ ˈæm.əˌzɑn / (AmE), / ˈam.ə.zən / (BrE)
The second longest river in the world, flowing from the Andes in western South America eastward into the South Atlantic Ocean.
the second longest river in the world, in South America

Examples

The Amazon supports an incredibly diverse ecosystem within its vast rainforest.
Many explorers have tried to navigate the entire length of the Amazon.
Flooding along the Amazon can have widespread effects on local communities.

Etymology

From Spanish 'Amazonas', from Portuguese 'Amazonas', ultimately from Greek 'Amazōn', a mythical race of warrior women, due to early European explorers' reports.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
