saccharase

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæk.əˌreɪs / (AmE), / ˈsæk.ə.reɪs / (BrE)
an enzyme that catalyzes the breakdown of sucrose into glucose and fructose
enzyme breaking down sucrose
medical

Examples

Saccharase is essential for digesting sucrose in many organisms.
Scientists studied the activity of saccharase in yeast cultures.
A deficiency in saccharase can lead to problems metabolizing certain sugars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sucrase - an enzyme that also catalyzes the hydrolysis of sucrose

Etymology

From sacchar- (referring to sugar) + -ase (enzyme suffix).
