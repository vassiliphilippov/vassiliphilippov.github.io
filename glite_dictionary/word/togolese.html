Togolese

1. NOUN

/ ˌtoʊɡəˈliːz / (AmE), / ˌtəʊɡəˈliːz / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Togo
native or inhabitant of Togo

Examples

The Togolese are known for their rich cultural traditions.
She met a group of Togolese while traveling in West Africa.
As a Togolese, he takes pride in his country's history.

Etymology

From Togo (country in West Africa) + -ese (denoting origin/nationality).

2. ADJ

/ ˌtoʊɡəˈliz / (AmE), / ˌtəʊɡəˈliːz / (BrE)
relating to Togo or its people or culture
of or relating to Togo or its people

Examples

She enjoyed the flavors of traditional Togolese cuisine while visiting Lomé.
The Togolese government announced new education reforms last year.
He met a group of Togolese students at the international conference.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
