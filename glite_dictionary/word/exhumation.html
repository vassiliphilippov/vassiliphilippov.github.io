exhumation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛksjuˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛksjʊˈmeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the act of digging up and removing something, especially a corpse, from the ground after it has been buried
the act of digging up something buried

Examples

The court ordered the exhumation of the grave to re-examine the evidence.
After the exhumation, forensic experts conducted new tests on the remains.
The family opposed the exhumation on religious grounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disinterment - the act of digging up a body; synonym for exhumation
  • Antonym: burial - the act of placing a dead body in the ground; opposite of exhumation

Etymology

From Latin exhumare (to unearth), from ex- (out of) + humus (ground).
