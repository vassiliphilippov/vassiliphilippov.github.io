prophase

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊˌfeɪz / (AmE), / ˈprəʊˌfeɪz / (BrE)
the first stage of mitosis, during which the chromosomes condense and become visible, and the nuclear envelope begins to break down
first stage of mitosis in cell division
medical

Examples

During prophase, the chromosomes become visible under a microscope as they condense.
The breakdown of the nuclear envelope occurs in prophase before the chromosomes align.
Biology students often identify the cell's stage by recognizing the features of prophase.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: metaphase - second stage of mitosis when chromosomes align at the center of the cell

Etymology

From German 'Prophase,' based on Greek 'pro-' (before) and 'phasis' (appearance)

2. NOUN

/ ˈproʊˌfeɪz / (AmE), / ˈprəʊˌfeɪz / (BrE)
the first stage of cell division in which chromosomes condense and become visible, occurring in both mitosis and meiosis
first stage of cell division
medical

Examples

During prophase, chromosomes become visible under a light microscope.
The nuclear envelope starts to break down in prophase of meiosis.
Errors that occur in prophase can lead to problems in genetic division.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: metaphase - the stage of cell division following prophase in which chromosomes align in the center of the cell

Etymology

From Greek 'pro-' (before) + 'phasis' (appearance).
