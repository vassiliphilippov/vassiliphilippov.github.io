Salvelinus namaycush

1. NOUN

/ sælˈviːlɪnəs ˌnæmeɪˈkʊʃ / (AmE), / salˈviːlɪnəs ˌnamɪˈkʊʃ / (BrE)
A large, fork-tailed trout native to lakes in Canada and the northern United States, known commonly as the lake trout.
species of large North American lake trout

Examples

The pristine waters of the northern lakes are home to the Salvelinus namaycush.
Anglers often prize the Salvelinus namaycush for its size and fighting ability.
Research has shown that populations of Salvelinus namaycush are sensitive to changes in water temperature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lake trout - large trout found in North American lakes

Etymology

From Neo-Latin genus Salvelinus; 'namaycush' is from Ojibwe 'namegos' (trout).
