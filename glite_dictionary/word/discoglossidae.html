Discoglossidae

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌskɑːɡˈlɒsɪdiː / (AmE), / dɪˌskɒɡˈlɒsɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of Old World toads characterized by a fixed, disklike tongue.
family of toads with fixed tongue

Examples

The Discoglossidae are known for their unique, immobile tongues.
Species within the Discoglossidae family are native to Europe and parts of northern Africa.
Scientists classified several rare amphibians under Discoglossidae after extensive study.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'diskos' (disk) and 'glōssa' (tongue), referring to the shape of the tongue.
