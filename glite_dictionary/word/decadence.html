decadence

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛkədəns / (AmE), / dɪˈkeɪdəns / (BrE)
moral or cultural decline as characterized by excessive indulgence in pleasure or luxury
moral or cultural decline

Examples

The novel depicts the decadence of a once-great empire.
Some critics argue that modern art is a sign of cultural decadence.
During the 1920s, Paris was known for its glamour and decadence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: degeneracy - the state of being degenerate or morally declined
  • Antonym: prosperity - the state of being successful or flourishing, often used as an antonym of decline

Etymology

From French 'décadence', from Medieval Latin 'decadentia', from Latin 'decadere' (to fall down or away).
