prosthetics

1. NOUN

/ prɑːsˈθɛtɪks / (AmE), / prɒsˈθɛtɪks / (BrE)
The branch of medicine concerned with the design, production, and use of artificial body parts.
branch of medicine making artificial body parts
medical

Examples

She decided to specialize in prosthetics after witnessing the impact of artificial limbs on patients' lives.
Recent advances in prosthetics have greatly improved mobility for amputees.
The hospital has an entire department dedicated to prosthetics and orthotics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prosthesis - an artificial body part

Etymology

From Greek prosthesis (an addition) via Latin prostheticus, referring to putting in place of something.
