madam

1. NOUN

/ ˈmædəm / (AmE), / ˈmadəm / (BrE)
a woman who runs or manages a house of prostitution (brothel)
woman managing a brothel

Examples

The police arrested the madam after an undercover investigation at the brothel.
She became a madam after years of working in the sex industry.
The notorious madam controlled several establishments in the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brothel keeper - a person who manages or owns a brothel

Etymology

From French 'madame' (my lady), used as a polite or formal form of address.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmædəm / (AmE), / ˈmadəm / (BrE)
A polite or formal way to refer to a woman, especially one of refinement or high social status.
polite term for a refined woman

Examples

The butler addressed her as madam when she entered the dining room.
Excuse me, madam, may I help you with your luggage?
As the guests arrived, the host greeted each madam with a courteous bow.

Synset Examples

a chauffeur opened the door of the limousine for the grand lady

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lady - a polite or formal term for a woman

Etymology

From Old French 'ma dame' meaning 'my lady'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈmædəm / (AmE), / ˈmadəm / (BrE)
a polite or formal way of addressing or referring to a woman, especially one in a position of authority
formal term of address for a woman

Examples

Excuse me, madam, may I help you with your bags?
The waitress addressed the customer as madam during dinner service.
In court, the judge was respectfully called madam by the lawyers.

Synset Examples

The teacher called her madam during the meeting.

Related Terms

From Old French 'ma dame' (my lady).
