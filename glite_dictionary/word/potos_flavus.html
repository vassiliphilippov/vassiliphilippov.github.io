Potos flavus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊtoʊs ˈfleɪvəs / (AmE), / ˌpəʊtɒs ˈfleɪvəs / (BrE)
An arboreal fruit-eating mammal of tropical America, also known as the kinkajou, with a long prehensile tail.
arboreal fruit-eating mammal of tropical America

Examples

The Potos flavus is more commonly known as the kinkajou and is native to Central and South America.
With its long tail, the Potos flavus skillfully moves through the rainforest canopy searching for fruit.
Many zoos house a Potos flavus to educate visitors about rainforest biodiversity.

Etymology

From Latin: 'potos' (genus name) + 'flavus' meaning 'yellow'.
