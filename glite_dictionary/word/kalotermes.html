Kalotermes

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæləˈtɜrˌmiz / (AmE), / ˌkæl.ə(ʊ)ˈtɜː.miːz / (BrE)
A genus of termites within the family Kalotermitidae, some species of which are known for being destructive to wood and living trees.
genus of wood-destructive termites

Examples

Kalotermes species are often found infesting dead branches and wooden structures.
The wood in the old house was severely damaged by Kalotermes termites.
Researchers studied the life cycle of Kalotermes to develop better pest control methods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: termite - a social insect that eats wood and causes damage to wooden structures

Etymology

From Greek 'kalos' (beautiful) + 'termes' (woodworm/termite).

2. NOUN

A genus of termites in the family Kalotermitidae, known for being destructive to wood and living trees.
genus of wood-destroying termites

Examples

Several species within the genus Kalotermes are considered serious pests of structural timber.
The biology of Kalotermes has been extensively studied due to its economic impact.
Researchers observed a colony of Kalotermes infesting a living tree in the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: termite - an insect of the order Isoptera, destructive to wood

From Greek 'kalos' (beautiful) + 'termes' (woodworm, termite).
