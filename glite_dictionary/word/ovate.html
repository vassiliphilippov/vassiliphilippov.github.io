ovate

1. ADJ

/ ˈoʊˌveɪt / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.veɪt / (BrE)
having an oval or egg-like shape, with a broader base tapering toward the top, especially used to describe leaves and similar objects.
egg-shaped; oval, broader at base

Examples

The leaves of that plant are distinctly ovate in shape, making identification easier.
Some seeds are perfectly ovate, while others are more irregularly shaped.
He admired the delicate, ovate petals of the rare wildflower.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oval - having the general form, shape, or outline of an egg; elliptical

Etymology

From Latin 'ovatus', from 'ovum' meaning 'egg'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈoʊˌveɪt / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.veɪt / (BrE)
(of a leaf) egg-shaped, with the broadest part near the base.
egg-shaped (leaf), broadest near base

Examples

The plant's leaves are distinctly ovate, with the widest part closest to the stem.
This species can be recognized by its ovate foliage, which tapers towards the tip.
Botanists often describe certain shrubs as having ovate leaves for identification purposes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: elliptic - having the shape of an ellipse (leaf shape)
  • Degree Higher: obovate - egg-shaped (leaf) with the broader end at the apex

Etymology

From Latin ovatus (egg-shaped), from ovum (egg).
