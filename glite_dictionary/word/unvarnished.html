unvarnished

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈvɑrnɪʃt / (AmE), / ʌnˈvɑːnɪʃt / (BrE)
expressed in a direct and honest way, without trying to soften or disguise the truth
plain, not softened or disguised

Examples

She gave an unvarnished account of what happened during the meeting.
The documentary offered an unvarnished look at life in the city.
He appreciated their unvarnished opinions, even when the truth was difficult to hear.

Synset Examples

the plain and unvarnished truth
the unvarnished candor of old people and children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plain - clearly and simply stated; not elaborate
  • Synonym: candid - truthful and straightforward; frank
  • Antonym: embellished - having added details or decoration, often to exaggerate or soften the truth

Etymology

Formed from un- (not) + varnished; originally meant 'not coated with varnish', later used figuratively.

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈvɑrnɪʃt / (AmE), / ʌnˈvɑːnɪʃt / (BrE)
(of a surface, especially wood) not having a coating of stain or varnish; not covered with a protective or decorative finish.
not coated with varnish or stain

Examples

The carpenter left the table unvarnished to preserve its natural look.
Only unvarnished wood is allowed in this environmentally friendly house.
The unvarnished bench felt rough to the touch compared to one with a polished finish.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: varnished - having a coating of varnish

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'varnished' (from Old French 'vernis', from Medieval Latin 'vernix').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01718859-s
Synset POS: s
