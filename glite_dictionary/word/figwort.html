figwort

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪɡˌwɝt / (AmE), / ˈfɪɡˌwɜːt / (BrE)
any of various tall, coarse plants of the genus Scrophularia, typically found in woodlands and known for their clusters of small, tubular flowers.
tall woodland plant of the genus Scrophularia

Examples

The figwort grows best in damp, shaded woodland areas.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of the figwort for centuries.
Clusters of small flowers give the figwort its distinctive appearance in the summer.

Etymology

From Middle English 'figwurt', from 'fig' + 'wort' (plant).
