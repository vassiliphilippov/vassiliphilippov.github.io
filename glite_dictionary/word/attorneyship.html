attorneyship

1. NOUN

/ əˈtɝniˌʃɪp / (AmE), / əˈtɜːniˌʃɪp / (BrE)
the position, role, or office of an attorney
the role or office of attorney

Examples

After years of dedicated service, she finally earned her attorneyship at the prestigious law firm.
His attorneyship requires him to uphold high ethical standards in representing clients.
The young lawyer aspired to obtain an attorneyship in a major city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attorney - a person appointed to act for another in legal matters

Etymology

From 'attorney' + suffix '-ship', denoting state or office.
