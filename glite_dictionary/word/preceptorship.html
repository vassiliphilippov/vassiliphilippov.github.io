preceptorship

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈsɛptɚˌʃɪp / (AmE), / prɪˈsɛptəʃɪp / (BrE)
the position, office, or period of being a preceptor, especially as a supervisor or mentor in a professional setting.
position of being a preceptor or mentor

Examples

During her nursing preceptorship, she gained valuable hands-on experience under a seasoned professional.
The hospital offers a preceptorship program to help new graduates transition into clinical practice.
His preceptorship at the university lasted for one academic year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mentorship - the position or practice of mentoring

Etymology

From preceptor (Latin praecēptor, teacher) + -ship (denoting state or condition).
