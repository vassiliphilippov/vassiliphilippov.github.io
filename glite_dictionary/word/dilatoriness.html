dilatoriness

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈleɪ.tər.i.nəs / (AmE), / dɪˈleɪ.tər.i.nəs / (BrE)
Tendency to delay or procrastinate; slowness caused by not acting promptly.
tendency to delay or procrastinate

Examples

The dilatoriness of the committee resulted in missed deadlines and frustrated members.
Her dilatoriness when starting assignments often led to unnecessary stress.
He was criticized for his dilatoriness in responding to important emails.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: procrastination - the action of delaying or postponing something
  • Antonym: promptness - the quality of acting quickly or without delay

Etymology

From Latin 'dilatorius' (tending to delay), from 'dilatare' (to delay).
