Marquis de Sade

1. PROPN

/ mɑrˌki də ˈsɑd / (AmE), / ˌmɑːki də ˈsɑːd / (BrE)
(1740–1814) A French nobleman, soldier, and writer known for his erotic works that explored sexual cruelty and gave rise to the term ‘sadism’.
French writer associated with 'sadism'

Examples

Many consider the writings of Marquis de Sade controversial for their explicit depiction of violence and sexuality.
The word 'sadism' is derived from the infamous Marquis de Sade.
Scholars have debated the philosophical impact of Marquis de Sade in literature and psychology.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: sadism - the tendency to derive pleasure from inflicting pain or humiliation

Etymology

From French title Marquis de Sade, the family name of Donatien Alphonse François de Sade.
