type

1. NOUN

/ taɪp / (AmE), / taɪp / (BrE)
printed characters used in books, newspapers, or other printed materials, especially with reference to their style or size
printed characters; style or size of print

Examples

The small type in the document made it difficult to read.
Books often use different type to distinguish chapter titles from the main text.
He prefers a clear, bold type for his presentations.

Synset Examples

small type is hard to read

Related Terms

  • Synonym: font - style and size of printed letters

Etymology

From French *type*, from Latin *typus* (figure, form), from Greek *typos* (impression, mark).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06838170-n
Synset POS: n

/ taɪp / (AmE), / taɪp / (BrE)
a small block of metal with a raised letter or character on one end, used in letterpress printing to produce printed characters when inked and pressed onto paper
metal block for letterpress printing

Examples

The printer carefully arranged the type by hand before starting the press.
Old newspapers were often produced using individual pieces of metal type.
He accidentally spilled a drawer full of type on the floor.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sort - a single piece of type in letterpress printing

From French 'type', from Latin 'typus' (figure, form), from Greek 'typos' (impression, mark).

3. NOUN

/ taɪp / (AmE), / taɪp / (BrE)
(biology) A taxonomic group whose characteristics are used to define the next higher taxon.
standard group in taxonomy

Examples

In evolutionary biology, a type species anchors the scientific name of a genus.
The type for mammals is the species whose characteristics are most representative of the group.
Each family in taxonomy is based on the type of one of its included genera.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taxon - a group of organisms in a classification system

Etymology

From Latin typus (a figure, form), from Greek typos (a dent, impression, form, pattern).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08128385-n
Synset POS: n
