Gregory I

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡrɛɡəri ðə fɜrst / (AmE), / ˈɡrɛɡəri ðə fɜːst / (BrE)
(Roman Catholic Church) an Italian pope (c. 540–604), renowned for his spiritual and temporal leadership, recognized as a saint and Doctor of the Church.
early Pope and saint (c. 540–604)

Examples

Gregory I introduced significant reforms to the liturgy of the Roman Catholic Church.
Many historians consider Gregory I to be one of the most influential of the early popes.
The Gregorian Chant is traditionally attributed to Gregory I and bears his name.
