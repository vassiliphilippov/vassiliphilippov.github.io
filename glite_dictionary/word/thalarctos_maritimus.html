Thalarctos maritimus

1. NOUN

/ θəˈlɑrktəs məˈrɪtɪməs / (AmE), / θəˈlɑːrktɒs məˈrɪtɪməs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the polar bear, a large white bear native to Arctic regions
scientific name for the polar bear

Examples

The Thalarctos maritimus has developed unique adaptations to survive the Arctic cold.
Scientists observed several Thalarctos maritimus hunting on the sea ice near the North Pole.
Conservation efforts aim to protect Thalarctos maritimus from the effects of climate change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polar bear - large white-furred bear native to Arctic regions

Etymology

From Greek 'thalassa' (sea) and 'arktos' (bear), Latin 'maritimus' (of the sea).
