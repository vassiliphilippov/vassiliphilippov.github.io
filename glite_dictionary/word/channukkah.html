Channukkah

1. NOUN

/ xɑːˈnukə / (AmE), / xəˈnʊkə / (BrE)
An eight-day Jewish festival commemorating the rededication of the Temple of Jerusalem in 165 BC, celebrated with the lighting of candles each night.
Jewish festival commemorating the temple rededication

Examples

Every year, her family gathers to light the Channukkah candles and share traditional foods.
The school organized a special assembly to teach students about the meaning of Channukkah.
He bought a menorah to celebrate Channukkah for the first time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hanukkah - An eight-day Jewish festival, alternative spelling

Etymology

From Hebrew ḥănukkāh (dedication).
