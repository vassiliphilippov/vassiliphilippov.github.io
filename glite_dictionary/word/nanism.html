nanism

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈneɪnɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by abnormally short stature, often due to a genetic abnormality.
abnormal short stature (medical)
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that his short stature was caused by a rare form of nanism.
Genetic counseling can help families who have a history of nanism.
Research into the causes of nanism has led to improved treatment options.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dwarfism - medical condition of unusually small stature

Etymology

From French 'nanisme', from 'nain' (dwarf), from Latin 'nanus'.
