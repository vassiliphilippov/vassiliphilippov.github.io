priming

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪ.mɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpraɪ.mɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of preparing something or someone for use or action
act of preparing (something) for use

Examples

The technician completed the priming of the pump before starting the machine.
Good priming of the wall ensures that paint adheres smoothly.
Mentors play a crucial role in the priming of young professionals entering the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preparation - the process of getting something ready
  • Antonym: neglect - failure to care for or prepare something

Etymology

From the verb 'prime', ultimately from Latin 'primus' meaning 'first'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpraɪmɪŋ / (BrE)
an igniter or substance used to initiate the burning of a propellant, such as in firearms or explosives.
substance/device to ignite propellant

Examples

The artillery shell failed to fire due to a faulty priming.
Early muskets required careful priming before they could be discharged.
Handling the priming requires caution, as it is sensitive to sparks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: primer - a cap or device providing the initial ignition in firearms

Etymology

From 'prime' meaning to prepare, especially to prepare a charge for ignition.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03412032-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpraɪmɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of applying a first or preparatory coat of paint or sealant to a surface before the main finish
preliminary coat of paint or sealant

Examples

Before painting the walls, make sure the priming has dried completely.
Applying proper priming ensures that the final coat adheres smoothly and lasts longer.
The artist spent hours on the priming of her canvas before starting the portrait.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: primer - a preparatory coating put on materials before painting

Etymology

From Latin 'primus' (first), via the verb 'prime' meaning to make ready.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
