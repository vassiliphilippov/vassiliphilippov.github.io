pitiably

1. ADV

/ ˈpɪtiəbli / (AmE), / ˈpɪtiəbli / (BrE)
In a way that arouses pity or compassion.
in a manner that arouses pity

Examples

He looked pitiably thin after his long illness.
The child wept pitiably when her toy broke.
They begged for help pitiably, hoping someone would stop.

Synset Examples

the sick child cried pathetically

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pathetically - in a manner arousing pity or sadness

Etymology

From 'pitiable' + '-ly', with 'pitiable' from Old French *piteable*, from Latin *pietas* (piety, compassion).
