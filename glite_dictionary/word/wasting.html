wasting

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈweɪstɪŋ / (BrE)
A gradual decrease in size or mass of body tissue or an organ, typically due to disease or lack of use.
decrease in size of body tissue or organ
medical

Examples

The patient experienced severe wasting of muscle tissue after months of immobility.
Malnutrition can lead to wasting in children if left untreated.
Doctors observed wasting of the affected limb in the weeks following the injury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atrophy - the wasting away or decrease in size of a body part, often from lack of use

2. NOUN

/ ˈweɪstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈweɪstɪŋ / (BrE)
A gradual reduction in strength and vitality of the body (or mind), especially as a result of chronic illness.
loss of strength due to illness
medical

Examples

The disease led to severe wasting of his muscles over several months.
HIV/AIDS can cause wasting, leaving patients extremely weak and frail.
She was diagnosed with a condition that resulted in progressive wasting of both body and mind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atrophy - gradual decline in muscle or tissue strength, often from disuse or disease
