villa

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪlə / (AmE), / ˈvɪlə / (BrE)
A country house in ancient Rome, typically comprising residential quarters and farm buildings arranged around a courtyard.
Roman country house with courtyard

Examples

We visited the ruins of a Roman villa with intricate mosaic floors and ancient walls.
The excavated villa revealed how wealthy Romans lived in the countryside.
Each villa was centered around a spacious courtyard used for gatherings and farm work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mansion - large impressive house

Etymology

From Latin villa (country house, farm).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɪlə / (AmE), / ˈvɪlə / (BrE)
A large, luxurious country house, often with extensive grounds.
large, luxurious country residence

Examples

They spent the summer in a magnificent villa overlooking the lake.
The villa was surrounded by beautifully landscaped gardens and high walls.
She dreamed of retiring to a villa in the Italian countryside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mansion - a large, impressive house
  • Degree Lower: cottage - a small, modest country house

Etymology

From Italian 'villa', from Latin 'villa' (country house, farm).

3. NOUN

/ ˈvɪlə / (AmE), / ˈvɪlə / (BrE)
A large detached or semidetached house in the suburbs or countryside, often luxurious or used as a holiday home.
large detached or semidetached house

Examples

They spent their summer vacation at a beautiful villa overlooking the sea.
The real estate agent showed us a spacious villa in the suburbs.
Many retired couples dream of owning a villa in the countryside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mansion - a large, impressive house
  • Degree Lower: cottage - a small, typically rural house
  • Degree Higher: palace - an exceptionally large and grand residence

Etymology

From Italian 'villa', from Latin 'villa' (country house, farm).
