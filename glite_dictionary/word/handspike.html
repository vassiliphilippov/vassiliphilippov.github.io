handspike

1. NOUN

/ ˈhændˌspaɪk / (AmE), / ˈhændˌspaɪk / (BrE)
A metal bar or length of pipe used as a lever to move or lift something heavy.
metal bar used as a lever

Examples

He used a handspike to pry open the crate.
The sailors worked together, each grabbing a handspike to turn the capstan.
After wedging the handspike beneath the rock, they managed to move it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lever - a rigid bar used to move or lift something

Etymology

From hand + spike, referring to a spike or bar operated by hand.
