hayfield

1. NOUN

/ ˈheɪˌfiːld / (AmE), / ˈheɪfiːld / (BrE)
A field of grass or alfalfa grown to be cut and dried as hay, typically used as animal fodder.
field for growing hay

Examples

Each summer, the hayfield behind our house is mowed and baled for winter feed.
Deer often graze quietly in the hayfield at dusk.
After the rain, the hayfield was greener than ever.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meadow - a field of grass, often natural, sometimes used for hay

Etymology

From 'hay' (Old English hēg, dried grass) + 'field' (Old English feld, open land).
