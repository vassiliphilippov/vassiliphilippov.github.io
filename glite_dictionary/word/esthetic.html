esthetic

1. ADJ

/ ɛsˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / iːsˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
concerned with beauty or the appreciation of beauty; relating to aesthetics
relating to beauty or aesthetics
american

Examples

The building's esthetic appeal was evident in its striking design.
Her choice of color demonstrated a refined esthetic sense.
The museum based its exhibit on esthetic values rather than historical accuracy.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aesthetic - concerned with beauty or the appreciation of beauty

4. NOUN

/ ɛsˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / iːsˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
a set of principles or theory regarding what is considered beautiful, especially in art or philosophy
philosophical theory of beauty

Examples

She appreciated the esthetic of the modern art gallery.
Minimalism has its own esthetic that emphasizes simplicity and functionality.
Many debates in architecture center around the dominant esthetic of a period.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aesthetic - a set of principles concerning the appreciation of beauty

Etymology

From Greek aisthetikos (perceptible by the senses), via German Ästhetik and French esthétique.
