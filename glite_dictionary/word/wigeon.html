wigeon

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪdʒən / (AmE), / ˈwɪdʒən / (BrE)
A freshwater duck found in Eurasia and northern Africa, related to mallards and teals, known for its distinctive plumage and whistling call.
a freshwater duck of Eurasia and northern Africa

Examples

We spotted a flock of wigeon by the edge of the lake.
The male wigeon is known for its striking chestnut head and creamy crown.
Birdwatchers often travel to wetlands to observe migratory wigeon during the winter.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: duck - a waterbird with a broad blunt bill, short legs, webbed feet, and a waddling gait

Etymology

Probably from an Old French word, of uncertain origin.
