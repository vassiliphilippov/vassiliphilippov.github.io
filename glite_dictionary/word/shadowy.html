shadowy

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃædoʊi / (AmE), / ˈʃædəʊi / (BrE)
difficult to see or understand; lacking clarity or distinctness.
difficult to see or unclear

Examples

We could barely make out the shadowy outline of the boat in the fog.
The details of his plan remain shadowy and uncertain.
She had only a shadowy memory of her childhood home.

Synset Examples

a dim figure in the distance
only a faint recollection
shadowy figures in the gloom
saw a vague outline of a building through the fog
a few wispy memories of childhood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vague - not clearly expressed or seen
  • Synonym: dim - not clear or bright
  • Antonym: clear - easy to perceive, understand, or interpret

Etymology

From 'shadow' + suffix '-y', meaning 'like a shadow'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈʃædoʊi / (AmE), / ˈʃædəʊi / (BrE)
(of a place or object) filled with shade or not in direct sunlight; partly dark or obscured by shadows.
filled with shade; dimly lit

Examples

We found a shadowy corner in the park to escape the afternoon heat.
The shadowy corridors of the old house gave it a mysterious atmosphere.
The deer rested in a shadowy glen, hidden from the midday sun.

Synset Examples

the shady side of the street
the surface of the pond is dark and shadowed
we sat on rocks in a shadowy cove
cool umbrageous woodlands

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shady - cast or full of shade; giving shelter from the sun
  • Antonym: sunny - bright with sunlight; full of sunlight

Etymology

From 'shadow' + '-y', meaning 'having the quality of shadow'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00277728-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈʃædoʊi / (AmE), / ˈʃædəʊi / (BrE)
lacking in substance; not clearly defined or real; insubstantial or vague.
insubstantial or indistinct; lacking substance

Examples

The distant figure appeared shadowy and indistinct in the fading light.
He spoke of shadowy threats that no one could prove existed.
Her memories of childhood are shadowy, blurred by the passage of time.

Synset Examples

strange fancies of unreal and shadowy worlds
dim shadowy forms
a wraithlike column of smoke

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insubstantial - not solid or real; lacking substance
  • Synonym: ethereal - extremely delicate and light; seeming too perfect for this world
  • Antonym: substantial - having substance or being solid, real, or meaningful

Etymology

From 'shadow' + '-y', formed in Middle English.

Source: WordNet
