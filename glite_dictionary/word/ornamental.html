ornamental

1. ADJ

/ ˌɔrnəˈmɛntəl / (AmE), / ˌɔːnəˈmɛntəl / (BrE)
serving a decorative or aesthetic purpose rather than a practical one
decorative, not functional

Examples

The garden was filled with ornamental plants that added color but produced no food.
Some people install ornamental fences purely for appearance, not for security.
She wore an ornamental brooch pinned to her jacket lapel.

Synset Examples

cosmetic fenders on cars
the buildings were utilitarian rather than decorative

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decorative - serving to make something look attractive
  • Antonym: utilitarian - designed to be useful rather than decorative

Etymology

From Latin ornamentum (equipment, decoration), from ornare (to equip, adorn).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɔːrnəˈmɛntəl / (AmE), / ˌɔːnəˈmɛntəl / (BrE)
A plant cultivated for decorative purposes rather than for food or practical use.
plant grown for decoration

Examples

Many people choose ornamentals like roses and tulips to brighten up their front gardens.
The nursery has a wide selection of ornamentals for all types of landscaping projects.
Unlike vegetables, ornamentals are grown mainly for their attractive flowers and foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ornamental plant - A plant grown specifically for its decorative value
