anaglyphy

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈɡlɪfi / (AmE), / ˌænəˈɡlɪfi / (BrE)
the process of creating stereoscopic images in contrasting colors that appear three-dimensional when viewed through glasses with differently colored lenses
3D image creation using color separation

Examples

The movie used anaglyphy to create stunning 3D effects visible with special glasses.
Early comic books experimented with anaglyphy to give readers an illusion of depth.
Modern virtual reality rarely relies on anaglyphy, preferring other stereoscopic techniques.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anaglyph - a three-dimensional image composed of two color components

Etymology

From Greek 'anagluphos' (carved in relief), from 'ana-' (up) + 'gluphein' (to carve).
