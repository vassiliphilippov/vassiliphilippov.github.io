Astilbe chinensis pumila

1. NOUN

/ æˈstɪl.bi tʃɪˈnɛn.sɪs pjuˈmaɪ.lə / (AmE), / æˈstɪl.bi tʃɪˈnɛn.sɪs pjuˈmɪ.lə / (BrE)
A mat-forming evergreen Asian plant with finely divided leaves and small pink to burgundy flowers, grown as ground cover.
a ground cover plant with pink to burgundy flowers

Examples

The shady border was filled with Astilbe chinensis pumila to provide vibrant ground cover in summer.
Gardeners recommend Astilbe chinensis pumila for its drought tolerance and attractive foliage.
A cluster of Astilbe chinensis pumila bloomed in delicate shades of pink beneath the old oak tree.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: 'Astilbe' (genus name from Greek 'a-' meaning 'without' + 'stilbe' meaning 'brightness'), 'chinensis' meaning 'Chinese', 'pumila' from Latin meaning 'dwarf'.
