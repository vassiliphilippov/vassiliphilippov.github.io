Arethusa

1. NOUN

/ ˌærəˈθjuːzə / (AmE), / ˌærɪˈθjuːzə / (BrE)
A genus of bog orchids with one or two showy pink or purple flowers, found in North America.
genus of bog orchids with showy flowers

Examples

We discovered an Arethusa orchid while exploring the wetlands.
Many botanists consider the Arethusa a highlight of bog flora due to its vibrant color.
The Arethusa blooms in late spring and is easily recognized by its single pink flower.

Etymology

From Greek Arethousa, the name of a nymph in Greek mythology; used in botanical naming conventions.

2. NOUN

/ ˌærəˈθjuːsə / (AmE), / ˌærɪˈθjuːsə / (BrE)
A genus of bog orchids native to North America and Japan, known for their showy flowers.
genus of bog orchids

Examples

The Arethusa thrives in wet boggy areas across eastern North America.
Many botanists are fascinated by the striking pink flowers of the Arethusa genus.
In her study, she identified several rare orchids, including one classified under Arethusa.

Etymology

From Greek Arethousa, a nymph in Greek mythology.
