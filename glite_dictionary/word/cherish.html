to cherish

1. VERB

/ ˈtʃɛrɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛrɪʃ / (BrE)
to feel or show deep affection for someone or something; to hold something dear and treat it with care or respect
to hold dear or feel deep affection

Examples

She will always cherish the memory of her childhood home.
He cherished the letters she wrote to him during their time apart.
We should cherish our friendships and never take them for granted.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: value - to consider someone or something important or beneficial
  • Antonym: to despise - to feel contempt or a strong dislike for someone or something

Etymology

From Old French *cherir* (to hold dear), from *cher* (dear), from Latin *carus* (dear, beloved).
