to perpetuate

1. VERB

/ pərˈpɛtʃuˌeɪt / (AmE), / pəˈpɛtjueɪt / (BrE)
to cause something to continue to exist or be repeated, often undesirably
cause to continue; make lasting

Examples

Rumors can easily perpetuate harmful stereotypes in a community.
She feared that the reforms would perpetuate existing inequalities rather than resolve them.
By ignoring the problem, we only perpetuate the myth that everything is fine.

Synset Examples

perpetuate a myth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to prolong - to make something last longer
  • Antonym: halt - to stop, come or bring to an end

Etymology

From Latin perpetuatus, past participle of perpetuare (to make perpetual), from perpetuus (continuous, perpetual).
