non-zero

1. ADJ

/ nɑn ˈzɪəroʊ / (AmE), / nɒn ˈzɪərəʊ / (BrE)
not equal to zero; having a value that is not zero.
not equal to zero

Examples

Any non-zero number has an inverse in mathematics.
The company reported a non-zero chance of success despite the odds.
If the result is non-zero, the process will continue.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: zero - exactly nothing or no amount

Etymology

From 'non-' (Latin origin, meaning 'not') + 'zero' (from Italian 'zero', meaning 'nought')
