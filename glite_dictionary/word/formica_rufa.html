Formica rufa

1. NOUN

/ fɔrˈmaɪkə ˈruːfə / (AmE), / fɔːˈmaɪkə ˈruːfə / (BrE)
A species of reddish-brown ant native to Europe, commonly found living in anthills in woodlands.
a European woodland ant species

Examples

The Formica rufa is known for building large, dome-shaped anthills in forests.
Scientists study Formica rufa to understand complex ant social behaviors.
I spotted a nest of Formica rufa while hiking through the oak woods.

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Formica' meaning 'ant' and 'rufa' meaning 'red'.
