imperium

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɪriəm / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɪəriəm / (BrE)
supreme power or absolute authority, especially as exercised by a sovereign state or ruler.
supreme authority; absolute power

Examples

During the height of the Roman Empire, the emperor held absolute imperium over his territories.
Some political theorists argue that true imperium belongs only to the people.
The general was granted imperium to make decisions on behalf of the entire region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sovereignty - supreme power or authority of a state to govern itself
  • Synonym: dominion - the power or right of governing and controlling; sovereign authority
  • Antonym: subordination - the state of being under authority or control

Etymology

From Latin imperium (command, power, empire).

2. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɪriəm / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɪəriəm / (BrE)
the territory or domain ruled by an emperor or empress; supreme authority or dominion of an empire
domain ruled by an emperor

Examples

During the height of the Roman imperium, its influence extended across three continents.
The expansion of the British imperium was marked by both conquest and colonization.
Historians debate the moral responsibilities of powers that hold an imperium over diverse peoples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: empire - a group of countries under a single authority, especially an emperor or empress

Etymology

From Latin imperium (command, power, empire).
