brachycephalic

1. ADJ

/ ˌbræki.səˈfæl.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌbræk.ɪ.səˈfæl.ɪk / (BrE)
having a short, broad head, especially with a cephalic index of over 80
having a short, broad head
medical

Examples

The bulldog's brachycephalic skull shape can cause breathing difficulties.
Certain breeds of cats are known for their brachycephalic features.
In anthropology, a brachycephalic head is one that is almost as wide as it is long.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dolichocephalic - having a long, narrow head with a low cephalic index

Etymology

From Greek brakhy- (short) + kephalē (head).

2. NOUN

/ ˌbræki səˈfæl ɪk / (AmE), / ˌbræk ɪ səˈfæl ɪk / (BrE)
An adult whose head is short and broad in proportion to its length; a person with a brachycephalic skull.
person with a short, broad head
medical

Examples

The anthropologist noted that the remains belonged to a brachycephalic individual.
Certain breeds of dogs, as well as some humans, may be classified as brachycephalic.
The medical report described the patient as brachycephalic based on skull measurements.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dolichocephalic - an adult with a long, narrow head

Etymology

From Greek *brachys* (short) + *kephalē* (head).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09891930-n
Synset POS: n
