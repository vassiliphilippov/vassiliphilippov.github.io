suspended

1. ADJ

/ səˈspɛndɪd / (AmE), / səˈspɛndɪd / (BrE)
(of solid particles in a liquid or gas) kept floating or dispersed within a fluid, without settling or attaching to a surface.
particles kept afloat in a fluid

Examples

The river water appears cloudy due to suspended particles of clay and silt.
Air quality can be affected by suspended dust after construction work.
Tiny droplets of oil remained suspended in the salad dressing, giving it a cloudy look.

Synset Examples

suspended matter such as silt or mud …
dust particles suspended in the air
droplets suspended in a gas

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dissolved - mixed into a liquid so as to form a solution

Etymology

From Latin suspendere, from sub- (under) + pendere (to hang).
