unsophisticated

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.səˈfɪs.tɪˌkeɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.səˈfɪs.tɪ.keɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
lacking experience or knowledge of the world and its ways; not worldly or shrewd.
not experienced or worldly

Examples

Her unsophisticated nature made her an easy target for experienced salespeople.
I find his unsophisticated honesty rather refreshing in the business world.
Despite their unsophisticated manners, the villagers welcomed us with genuine warmth.

Synset Examples

either too unsophisticated or too honest to promise more than he could deliver
this helplessly unworldly woman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naive - lacking experience, wisdom, or judgement; easily trusting
  • Antonym: worldly - experienced and knowledgeable about life and the world

Etymology

Formed from un- (not) + sophisticated.

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.səˈfɪs.tɪ.keɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.səˈfɪs.tɪ.keɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
not complicated, advanced, or refined; simple and lacking complexity.
simple or lacking complexity

Examples

The design of the cabin was unsophisticated, but it served its purpose well.
They preferred the unsophisticated charm of rural life to the bustle of the city.
Although the solution was unsophisticated, it worked perfectly for their needs.

Synset Examples

small and uncomplicated cars for those really interested in motoring
an unsophisticated machine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simple - easily understood or done; presenting no difficulty
  • Antonym: complex - consisting of many different and connected parts

Etymology

Formed with the prefix un- (not) and sophisticated, from Medieval Latin sophisticatus (tampered with), from Greek sophistikos (clever).

3. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.səˈfɪs.tɪˌkeɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.səˈfɪs.tɪ.keɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
lacking experience, refinement, or knowledge of the world; simple and not elegant or cultured
lacking refinement or worldliness

Examples

The unsophisticated villagers were amazed by the bustling city life.
She found his tastes charmingly unsophisticated, preferring home-cooked meals over fine dining.
Many unsophisticated tourists are easily impressed by the simplest attractions.

Synset Examples

bumpkinly country boys
rustic farmers
a hick town
the nightlife of Montmartre awed the unsophisticated tourists

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naive - lacking experience or sophistication
  • Antonym: sophisticated - having experience and refinement

Etymology

From un- (not) + sophisticated (from Medieval Latin sophisticatus, altered, tampered with).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00639749-s
Synset POS: s
