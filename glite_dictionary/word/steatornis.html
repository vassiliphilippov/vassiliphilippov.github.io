Steatornis

1. NOUN

/ stiːəˈtɔrnɪs / (AmE), / stiːəˈtɔːnɪs / (BrE)
A genus of birds, consisting of the oilbird, and the only genus in the family Steatornithidae.
genus of birds including the oilbird

Examples

The Steatornis is remarkable for its ability to navigate in complete darkness using echolocation.
Biologists studied Steatornis populations in the caves of northern South America.
The oilbird, classified under the genus Steatornis, primarily feeds on fruit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oilbird - a nocturnal fruit-eating bird found in South America

Etymology

From Greek 'stear' meaning fat and 'ornis' meaning bird, referring to the fat-rich nestlings.
