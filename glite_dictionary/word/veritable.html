veritable

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɛrɪtəbl / (AmE), / ˈvɛrɪtəbl / (BrE)
used to emphasize that something is truly or very much what it is said to be; real or genuine.
real or genuine; used for emphasis

Examples

He was a veritable expert in the field, with decades of experience.
The garden was a veritable paradise, filled with exotic flowers.
During the festival, the streets became a veritable sea of colors and music.

Synset Examples

an authentic signature
a bona fide manuscript
an unquestionable antique
photographs taken in a veritable bull ring

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authentic - genuine; real, not a copy
  • Synonym: bona fide - genuine; real, done in good faith

Etymology

From Old French 'veritable', from Latin 'veritas' (truth).

2. ADJ

/ ˈvɛrɪtəbəl / (AmE), / ˈvɛrɪtəbl̩ / (BrE)
used to emphasize that something is truly or very much so, often for dramatic effect.
used for emphasis; genuine

Examples

The museum is a veritable treasure trove of ancient artifacts.
After the rain, the garden became a veritable jungle of weeds overnight.
She is a veritable genius when it comes to solving complex problems.

Synset Examples

a regular morass of details
a regular nincompoop
he's a veritable swine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genuine - truly what something is said to be; authentic
  • Synonym: real - actually existing as a thing or occurring in fact
  • Antonym: counterfeit - made in imitation so as to be passed off fraudulently; not genuine

Etymology

From Old French 'veritable', from Latin 'veritas' (truth).
