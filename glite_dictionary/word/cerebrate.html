to cerebrate

1. VERB

/ ˈsɛr.əˌbreɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɛr.ɪˌbreɪt / (BrE)
to use the mind or one's power of reason; to think or deliberate, especially in a serious or intellectual way
to think or reason intellectually

Examples

The philosopher would often cerebrate for hours over a single question.
Before coming to a conclusion, she prefers to quietly cerebrate in solitude.
Experts recommend you cerebrate on the pros and cons before making major life decisions.

Synset Examples

I've been thinking all day and getting nowhere

Related Terms

  • Synonym: think - to use one's mind to consider or reason about something
  • Synonym: to ponder - to think about something carefully, especially before making a decision

Etymology

From Latin 'cerebrum' (brain), influenced by the pattern of verbs like 'deliberate'.
