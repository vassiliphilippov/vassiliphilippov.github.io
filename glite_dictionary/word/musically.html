musically

1. ADV

/ ˈmjuːzɪkli / (AmE), / ˈmjuːzɪkli / (BrE)
In a musical way; with qualities or characteristics of music, such as melody or harmony.
in a musical manner

Examples

The orchestra played the difficult passage very musically, blending each instrument beautifully.
He spoke so musically that his words seemed to flow like a gentle melody.
Her laughter rang out musically across the garden, uplifting everyone's mood.

Synset Examples

She sang very musically

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tunefully - in a tuneful or melodious manner
  • Antonym: discordantly - in a way that is harsh or lacking harmony

Etymology

From musical + -ly, formed from Latin 'musica' (music) via Old French 'musical'.
