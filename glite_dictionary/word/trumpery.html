trumpery

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrʌmpəri / (AmE), / ˈtrʌmpəri / (BrE)
Decorative or ornamental objects that are of little value or worth.
worthless ornamental objects

Examples

The mantelpiece was covered with trumpery collected from various travels.
She cleared out all the trumpery from the old jewelry box.
His desk was cluttered with trumpery of no real use or significance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bauble - a small, cheap trinket or ornament
  • Synonym: trinket - a small ornament, typically of little value

Etymology

From Middle French *tromperie* (deception, trickery), from *tromper* (to deceive).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrʌmpəri / (AmE), / ˈtrʌmpəri / (BrE)
worthless or nonsensical talk, ideas, or things; trivial or showy nonsense
worthless or nonsensical talk; trivial nonsense

Examples

The politician's speech was dismissed as mere trumpery by his critics.
Amid all the trumpery in the article, there was not a single useful fact.
He grew tired of listening to the endless trumpery at the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonsense - speech or writing that has no meaning or makes no sense
  • Synonym: drivel - silly, senseless, or meaningless talk or writing

Etymology

From Old French 'tromper' (to deceive); originally referred to something deceitful or showy but valueless.
