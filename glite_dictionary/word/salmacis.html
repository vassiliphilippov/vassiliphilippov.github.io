Salmacis

1. PROPN

/ sælˈmækɪs / (AmE), / sælˈmækɪs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, a nymph who merged with Hermaphroditus to form one body, resulting in a single being with both male and female characteristics.
mythological nymph merged with Hermaphroditus

Examples

According to legend, Salmacis fell in love with Hermaphroditus and prayed to be joined with him forever.
The story of Salmacis and Hermaphroditus explores the theme of androgyny in ancient myths.
Artists have depicted Salmacis in various paintings and sculptures inspired by Greek mythology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hermaphroditus - mythological figure who merged with Salmacis

Etymology

From Greek Σαλμακίς (Salmakís), the name of a nymph and also a fountain near Halicarnassus.
