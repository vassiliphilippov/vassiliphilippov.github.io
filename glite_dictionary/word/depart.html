to depart

1. VERB

/ dɪˈpɑrt / (AmE), / dɪˈpɑːt / (BrE)
to leave, especially in order to start a journey
to leave or go away

Examples

The train will depart from platform 4 at 8 o'clock.
She had to depart early to catch her flight.
After the meeting, everyone departed in different directions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leave - to go away from a place or person
  • Antonym: to arrive - to reach a place at the end of a journey

Etymology

From Old French *departir* (to divide, distribute), from Latin *departire*, *departiri* (to divide).

2. VERB

/ dɪˈpɑrt / (AmE), / dɪˈpɑːt / (BrE)
To wander or deviate from a direct or straight course.
deviate from a direct course

Examples

Related Terms

Etymology

From Latin 'departire', meaning 'to divide'.
