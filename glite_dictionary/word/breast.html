breast

1. NOUN

/ brɛst / (AmE), / brɛst / (BrE)
the front part of the body between the neck and the abdomen, often referring to the chest area
the front part of the torso

Examples

He clutched his breast in surprise when he heard the news.
The knight beat his breast to show his grief.
She wore a brooch pinned to her breast.

Synset Examples

he beat his breast in anger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chest - the upper front part of the body between the neck and the abdomen

Etymology

Old English 'brēost', related to Old High German 'brust', meaning 'chest'.

2. NOUN

/ brɛst / (AmE), / brɛst / (BrE)
the front part of the chest in animals, corresponding to the human chest
the chest area of an animal

Examples

The breast of the bird was covered in bright feathers.
Chicken breast is a popular choice for healthy meals.
The hunter aimed just below the breast of the deer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chest - the front surface of a person's or animal's body between the neck and the abdomen

Etymology

Old English brēost, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch borst and German Brust.

Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ brɛst / (AmE), / brɛst / (BrE)
either of the two soft, rounded, milk-secreting organs on the front of a woman's chest.
the front chest organs on a woman

Examples

The doctor examined her breast for any unusual lumps.
During breastfeeding, a mother produces milk in her breasts.
She wore a dress that revealed her bare breasts.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: chest - the front part of the body between the neck and the stomach
  • Degree Lower: nipple - the small pointed part in the center of the breast

Etymology

From Old English 'brēost', related to Old High German 'brust' and Old Norse 'brjóst'.

4. VERB

/ brɛst / (AmE), / brɛst / (BrE)
to move forward and push through or reach something with the chest, especially as in breasting the tape at the finish line in a race
to push through or reach with the chest

Examples

The runner breasted the tape just ahead of her rivals.
They breasted the steep hill despite strong winds.
As he reached the summit, he proudly breasted the final ridge.

Synset Examples

The runner breasted the tape

Etymology

From Old English brēost (chest, breast), used as a verb meaning to confront or push with the chest.

5. VERB

/ brɛst / (AmE), / brɛst / (BrE)
to confront or meet something, especially a difficulty or obstacle, directly and with determination
face or confront directly

Examples

Despite the force of the wind, he breasted the storm and reached the shore safely.
She was determined to breast every challenge that lay ahead of her.
The hikers breasted the mountain pass before sunset, pressing forward despite exhaustion.

Synset Examples

breast the storm

Related Terms

  • Synonym: face - to confront or deal with something difficult
  • Synonym: brave - to confront something dangerous or difficult with courage

Etymology

From Old English *brēost* (breast, chest). The usage refers to meeting something with the chest, i.e., head-on.

6. NOUN

/ brɛst / (AmE), / brɛst / (BrE)
the meat or flesh cut from the breast of a bird, especially poultry such as chicken or turkey, used as food

Examples

I'll have the grilled breast of chicken with a salad on the side.
She prefers eating breast meat because it is leaner than the thigh.
The recipe calls for two turkey breasts, boned and skinned.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chicken breast - breast meat specifically from a chicken
  • Antonym: thigh - meat from the upper leg of a bird, especially poultry

Etymology

From Old English breost, of Germanic origin.
