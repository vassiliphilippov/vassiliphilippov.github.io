mayor

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈmeɪ.ə / (BrE)
the elected head of the government of a city, town, or municipality
elected city head; leader of local government

Examples

The mayor announced a new initiative to improve public transportation.
After the election, the city's new mayor took office amid high expectations.
Several citizens attended the mayor's town hall meeting to voice their concerns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: governor - the elected executive head of a state in the US
  • Synonym: councilor - a member of a local government council
  • Antonym: citizen - an inhabitant of a city or town

Etymology

From Old French 'maire', from Latin 'major' (greater).
