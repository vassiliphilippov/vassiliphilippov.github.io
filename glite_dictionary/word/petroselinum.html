Petroselinum

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛtroʊsəˈlaɪnəm / (AmE), / ˌpɛtrəsɪˈliːnəm / (BrE)
A genus of aromatic plants in the family Apiaceae, best known for parsley.
the genus of parsley plants

Examples

The genus Petroselinum includes several species, the most well known being garden parsley.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of different Petroselinum species.
Many herbs in the Petroselinum genus have culinary and ornamental uses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parsley - a common herb used in cooking, especially as a garnish

Etymology

From Latin 'petroselinum', from Greek 'petroselinon' (rock parsley).
