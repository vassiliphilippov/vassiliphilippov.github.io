impregnably

1. ADV

/ ɪmˈprɛɡ.nəb.li / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɛɡ.nəb.li / (BrE)
In a way that cannot be overcome, captured, or defeated; in an unassailable manner.
in an unassailable manner

Examples

The ancient fortress was impregnably situated atop the rocky hill, deterring all invaders.
She defended her position impregnably, leaving no room for doubt or counterargument.
The castle was impregnably built, with thick stone walls and a deep moat surrounding it.

Synset Examples

the sight of that bland, impregnably righteous face has been enough to make their blood run cold

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impregnable - unable to be captured or overcome
  • Antonym: vulnerably - in a way that is open to attack or harm

Etymology

From 'impregnable' (from Old French 'impregnable'), plus the adverbial suffix '-ly.'
