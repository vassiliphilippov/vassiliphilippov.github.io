strictness

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrɪkt.nəs / (AmE), / ˈstrɪkt.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being very careful to follow rules or pay attention to details
quality of being strict or precise

Examples

The teacher's strictness ensured that all students completed their assignments on time.
His strictness about following procedures made the laboratory very safe.
Some people appreciate her strictness, while others find it intimidating.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rigor - strictness, severity, or thoroughness
  • Antonym: leniency - the quality of being less strict or more tolerant

Etymology

From strict + -ness, based on Latin 'strictus' (drawn tight, rigid).

2. NOUN

/ ˈstrɪktnəs / (AmE), / ˈstrɪktnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being firm, uncompromising, or rigorous in standards, rules, or discipline
firmness or rigor in discipline or standards

Examples

The strictness of the new regulations surprised everyone in the company.
Her strictness as a teacher ensured that all students paid attention in class.
Parents sometimes debate the right level of strictness when raising children.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: leniency - the quality of being more permissive or tolerant
  • Synonym: rigor - severity or strictness in judgment or conduct

Etymology

From strict + -ness, ultimately from Latin strictus (drawn tight).
