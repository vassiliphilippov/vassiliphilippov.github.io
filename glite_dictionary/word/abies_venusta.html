Abies venusta

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.bi.iːz vəˈnʌstə / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.bɪ.iːz vɪˈnʌstə / (BrE)
A pyramidal fir tree native to southwestern California, characterized by spiny pointed leaves and cone scales with long spines.
a fir tree species of southwestern California

Examples

The Abies venusta is well adapted to the mountainous regions of southwestern California.
Botanists studied the unique cone structure of the Abies venusta.
Conservation efforts have increased to protect the habitat of Abies venusta.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fir - a coniferous evergreen tree of the genus Abies

Etymology

From Latin; genus name Abies (fir tree) and species epithet venusta (charming, graceful).
