orbitale

1. NOUN

/ ɔːrˈbɪtəli / (AmE), / ɔːˈbɪtɑːli / (BrE)
The craniometric point at the lowest part of the lower edge of the eye socket (orbit).
lowest point on lower edge of the eye socket
medical

Examples

During the cranial measurement, the technician identified the orbitale as a key reference point.
Accurate location of the orbitale is essential for certain dental and facial analyses.
The position of the orbitale may vary slightly between individuals.

Etymology

From New Latin "orbitale," derived from Latin "orbita" (track, orbit), referring to the eye socket.
