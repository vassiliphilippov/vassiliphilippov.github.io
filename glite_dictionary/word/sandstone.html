sandstone

1. NOUN

/ ˈsændˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈsæn(d)stəʊn / (BrE)
A sedimentary rock made of consolidated sand, typically held together by minerals such as clay, quartz, or calcite.
sedimentary rock made of consolidated sand

Examples

The ancient temple was built from local sandstone, giving it a warm golden hue.
Layers of sandstone in the canyon walls reveal millions of years of geological history.
Many famous sculptures are carved from blocks of sandstone due to its workable texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limestone - a sedimentary rock consisting mainly of calcium carbonate
  • Antonym: granite - a hard, granular igneous rock

Etymology

Formed from 'sand' + 'stone', first attested in the 14th century.
