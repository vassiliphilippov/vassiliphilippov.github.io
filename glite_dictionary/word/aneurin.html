aneurin

1. NOUN

/ əˈnʊrɪn / (AmE), / əˈnjʊərɪn / (BrE)
A vitamin of the B complex (B1), also known as thiamine, which is essential for the prevention of beriberi and for maintaining a healthy appetite and growth.
vitamin B1, prevents beriberi
medical

Examples

A deficiency of aneurin can lead to serious neurological disorders.
The doctor recommended supplements containing aneurin to treat the condition.
Foods like whole grains and legumes are good sources of aneurin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thiamine - another name for vitamin B1

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (without) + 'neuron' (nerve), so named for its role in preventing nerve diseases.
