transmutation

1. NOUN

/ ˌtrænz.mjuːˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌtrænz.mjuːˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of changing from one form, nature, or substance into another
act of changing form or substance

Examples

The ancient alchemists sought the transmutation of base metals into gold.
The novel explores the transmutation of pain into art.
Nuclear transmutation occurs when the nucleus of an atom is changed by a nuclear reaction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transformation - the act of transforming or the state of being transformed
  • Synonym: conversion - the process of changing or causing something to change from one form to another

Etymology

From Latin transmutatio(n-), from transmutare (to change across).

2. NOUN

/ ˌtrænz.mjuˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌtrænz.mjuːˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a complete or fundamental change from one form or substance into another; a transformation, especially of a significant or qualitative kind
a fundamental change; transformation

Examples

The transmutation of base metals into gold fascinated alchemists for centuries.
A period of rapid social transmutation followed the country's political revolution.
The artist's work is a transmutation of personal pain into universal beauty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transformation - a thorough or dramatic change in form or appearance
  • Degree Lower: change - the process of becoming different

Etymology

From Latin transmutatio (change of state or form), from trans- (across) + mutare (to change).

3. NOUN

/ ˌtrænsˌmjuːˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtranz.mjuːˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
in physics, the process by which one chemical element is changed into another, typically by nuclear reactions such as radioactive decay or bombardment
change of one element into another (physics)

Examples

Nuclear reactors can achieve the transmutation of uranium into plutonium.
The ancient dream of transmutation drove many early chemists to search for a way to create gold.
Modern physics has made transmutation a routine process in particle accelerators.

Synset Examples

  • Synonym: conversion - the process of changing from one form to another
