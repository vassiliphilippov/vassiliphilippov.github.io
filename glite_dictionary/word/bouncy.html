bouncy

1. ADJ

/ ˈbaʊnsi / (AmE), / ˈbaʊnsi / (BrE)
full of energy and liveliness; lively or capable of bouncing.
lively; capable of bouncing

Examples

The children's laughter made the party feel especially bouncy and joyful.
She played a bouncy tune on the piano that made everyone want to dance.
Her bouncy steps showed just how excited she was for the day.

Synset Examples

a bouncing gait
bouncy tunes
the peppy and interesting talk
a spirited dance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lively - full of life and energy
  • Synonym: energetic - showing or involving great activity or vitality
  • Antonym: dull - lacking interest or energy

2. ADJ

/ ˈbaʊnsi / (AmE), / ˈbaʊnsi / (BrE)
Able to rebound easily after being compressed or dropped; elastic or springy.
able to spring back or rebound easily

Examples

This mattress has a bouncy feel that makes it comfortable to sleep on.
She loves how her hair feels light and bouncy after washing it.
The children enjoyed playing with the bouncy rubber ball in the park.

Synset Examples

clean bouncy hair
a lively tennis ball
as resilient as seasoned hickory
springy turf

Related Terms

  • Synonym: springy - able to return quickly to its original shape after being pressed
  • Synonym: resilient - able to recover shape or position quickly
  • Synonym: elastic - able to resume its normal shape after being stretched or compressed

Etymology

From the verb 'bounce' + -y.
