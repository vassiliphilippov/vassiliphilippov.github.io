zamburak

1. NOUN

/ zæmˈbʊrɑk / (AmE), / zæmˈbʊəræk / (BrE)
A small cannon mounted on a camel and used by Arab, Afghan, Persian, and Indian armies in the early modern era.
camel-mounted cannon used in Asian armies

Examples

The Mughal army deployed a zamburak unit during their campaign.
Historians often describe the zamburak as an innovative but unstable weapon platform.
A line of zamburaks was positioned at the edge of the battlefield.

Etymology

From Persian *zambūrak* (small hornet, also the device's name).
