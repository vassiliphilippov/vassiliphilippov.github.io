slickness

1. NOUN

/ ˈslɪknəs / (AmE), / ˈslɪknɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being smooth and slippery to the touch
the state of being smooth or slippery

Examples

She noticed the slickness of the sidewalk after the rain.
The slickness of the oil made it hard to clean up the spill.
Wearing gloves improved her grip and reduced the slickness of the surface.

Synset Examples

he could feel the slickness of the tiller

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slipperiness - the quality of being slippery

Etymology

From 'slick' + '-ness', forming a noun indicating the state or quality.

2. NOUN

/ ˈslɪknəs / (AmE), / ˈslɪknɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being clever or deceptive, especially in speech, intended to take advantage of someone.
clever or deceptive speech or behavior

Examples

The salesman's slickness made it hard to tell if he was telling the truth.
She admired his slickness in negotiation, but didn't fully trust him.
The politician's slickness was evident in the way he dodged every tough question.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cunning - skill in achieving one's ends by deceit
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being honest

Etymology

From 'slick' (smooth, clever) + -ness (state or quality).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈslɪknəs / (AmE), / ˈslɪknɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being smooth, fluent, and often superficially attractive, sometimes suggesting a lack of depth or sincerity
superficial smoothness or fluency

Examples

The lawyer's slickness in handling questions impressed the jury, but some doubted his sincerity.
She admired the slickness of the new ad campaign, even if it felt a bit superficial.
His slickness when answering reporters' questions made him seem evasive rather than trustworthy.

Synset Examples

the glibness of a high-pressure salesman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glibness - the quality of being fluent and insincere
  • Antonym: sincerity - the quality of being honest or genuine

Etymology

From 'slick' + '-ness', where 'slick' is of Middle English origin meaning smooth or glossy.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05102586-n
Synset POS: n
