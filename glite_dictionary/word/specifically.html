specifically

1. ADV

/ spəˈsɪfɪkli / (AmE), / spəˈsɪfɪkli / (BrE)
used to indicate something that is mentioned in distinction from others or as being particularly focused; in a precise and exact way.
in a distinct or precise way

Examples

The course was designed specifically for beginners with no prior knowledge.
She asked specifically about the delivery date in her email.
We are looking specifically for volunteers who can work weekends.

Synset Examples

a program specifically for teenagers
he is interested specifically in poisonous snakes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: particularly - to a higher degree than usual or expected; especially

Etymology

From specific + -ly; 'specific' from Latin 'specificus' (constituting a kind).
