acapnia

1. NOUN

/ æˈkæpniə / (AmE), / əˈkapnɪə / (BrE)
a medical condition in which the level of carbon dioxide in the blood is lower than normal, often caused by deep or rapid breathing
low blood carbon dioxide (medical)
medical

Examples

The patient developed acapnia as a result of hyperventilation after the panic attack.
Chronic acapnia can disrupt the body's acid-base balance and lead to unpleasant symptoms.
Medical staff monitored her closely for signs of acapnia following the prolonged period of rapid breathing.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hypercapnia - an excess of carbon dioxide in the blood
  • Synonym: hypocapnia - a lower than normal level of carbon dioxide in the blood; synonym for acapnia

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (without) + 'kapnos' (smoke), indicating absence of carbon dioxide.
