potful

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑt.fʊl / (AmE), / ˈpɒt.fʊl / (BrE)
An amount or quantity that fills a pot.
quantity that fills a pot

Examples

She made a potful of soup for the whole family.
We drank a potful of coffee while we talked.
After picking the berries, she boiled a potful of jam.

Etymology

Formed from 'pot' + 'ful' (suffix meaning 'full of, as much as will fill').
