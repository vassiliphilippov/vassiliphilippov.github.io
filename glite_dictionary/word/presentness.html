presentness

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛzəntnəs / (AmE), / ˈprɛzntnɪs / (BrE)
the state or quality of existing or occurring at this moment; the characteristic of being present or in the present time
quality of being present or current

Examples

The presentness of her emotions made the performance incredibly moving.
Some philosophers debate the presentness of experience versus the memory of past events.
His art captures the fleeting presentness of modern life.

Synset Examples

a study of the pastness of the present and … of the presentness of the past

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pastness - the quality of being the past
  • Synonym: contemporaneity - the state of being contemporary or of the present time

Etymology

From 'present' + '-ness', forming a noun to denote the state or quality.
