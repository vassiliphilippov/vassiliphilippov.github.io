phyllo

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːloʊ / (AmE), / ˈfɪləʊ / (BrE)
A type of pastry dough rolled into extremely thin sheets, used especially in Greek and Middle Eastern dishes such as baklava and spanakopita.
thin pastry sheets used in cooking

Examples

The recipe calls for several layers of phyllo brushed with melted butter.
She bought a package of phyllo to make homemade spanakopita for the party.
Be careful when handling phyllo, as the delicate sheets can tear easily.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filo - alternative spelling of phyllo, used for the same type of pastry

Etymology

From Greek φύλλο (phýllo), meaning 'leaf,' referring to the thinness of the sheets.
