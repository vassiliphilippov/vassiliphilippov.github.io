columbium

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlʌmbiəm / (AmE), / kəˈlʌmbiəm / (BrE)
a former name for the chemical element niobium (symbol Nb), used especially in older scientific literature
former name for niobium

Examples

The mineral sample was found to contain a high concentration of columbium.
In older chemistry texts, you may still find niobium referred to as columbium.
The use of columbium alloys helped improve the strength of jet engine components.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: niobium - the chemical element with symbol Nb and atomic number 41

Etymology

From New Latin 'Columbia' (America), because it was first discovered in a mineral from America.
