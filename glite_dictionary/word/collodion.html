collodion

1. NOUN

/ kəˈloʊdiən / (AmE), / kəˈləʊdiən / (BrE)
a colorless, syrupy solution of pyroxylin in ether and alcohol, used as a coating for wounds or photographic films
syrupy solution used in medicine and photography

Examples

The surgeon applied a thin layer of collodion to seal the wound.
Early photographers used collodion to create glass plate negatives for their cameras.
Handle collodion with care, as it is highly flammable.

Etymology

From French 'collodion', from Greek 'kolla' (glue).
