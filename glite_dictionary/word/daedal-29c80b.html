daedal

1. ADJ

/ ˈdiːdəl / (AmE), / ˈdiːdəl / (BrE)
ingeniously intricate and complex in design or function; skillfully crafted or elaborate
ingeniously intricate or complex
archaic

Examples

The architect created a daedal structure that fascinated visitors with its detail.
Poets often describe nature as having a daedal beauty beyond human creation.
She admired the daedal machinery with its intricate gears and clever mechanisms.

Synset Examples

the daedal hand of nature

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ingenious - clever, original, and inventive
  • Synonym: elaborate - involving many carefully arranged parts or details
  • Antonym: simple - easily understood or done; presenting no difficulty

Etymology

From Latin 'daedalus', from Greek 'daidalos' (skillful, ingenious), referring to the mythological craftsman Daedalus.
