achromasia

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ.kroʊˈmeɪ.zi.ə / (AmE), / ˌeɪ.krəʊˈmeɪ.zi.ə / (BrE)
a condition marked by an abnormal or unnatural lack of color in the skin, often resulting from bruising, illness, or emotional distress
abnormal lack of color in the skin
medical

Examples

Her illness left her with noticeable achromasia on her face.
Doctors noted achromasia in the patient, especially after the accident.
The achromasia was so severe that her skin appeared almost white.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pallor - an unhealthy pale appearance

Etymology

From Greek a- (not) + chroma (color).
