Hypochaeris radicata

1. NOUN

/ haɪpoʊˈkɛrɪs ræˈdɪkətə / (AmE), / hɪˌpɒkəˈɪərɪs ræˈdɪkətə / (BrE)
A European plant with yellow flowers and leaves resembling a cat's ears, widely naturalized in North America.
a plant with yellow flowers and cat's-ear-like leaves

Examples

The field was covered in Hypochaeris radicata, its yellow flowers bright against the green grass.
Gardeners often remove Hypochaeris radicata to prevent it from spreading in lawns.
Many insects feed on the nectar of Hypochaeris radicata during the summer months.

Etymology

From Greek 'hypo' (under) + 'chaeris' (joy), species name from Latin 'radicata' (rooted).
