trump

1. VERB

/ trʌmp / (AmE), / trʌmp / (BrE)
(of an animal, especially an elephant) to produce a sound that resembles that of a trumpet.
make a sound like a trumpet

Examples

The elephant suddenly trumped as a warning to the herd.
In the early morning, the distant elephants trump across the savannah.
She laughed as the baby elephant trumped joyfully at the zookeepers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trumpet - to blow a trumpet or make a trumpet-like sound

Etymology

Imitative of the sound of a trumpet; related to 'trumpet'.

2. NOUN

/ trʌmp / (AmE), / trʌmp / (BrE)
(in card games) the suit that is chosen to rank above the others for the duration of a hand, enabling cards of that suit to win over cards of other suits.
winning suit in card games

Examples

He played a trump to win the trick and take the lead.
Diamonds were declared as the trump suit for this round.
When you have no cards of the suit led, you may play a trump.

Synset Examples

clubs were declared trumps
a trump can take a trick even when a card of a different suit is led

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trump card - a card of the trump suit

Etymology

From Middle English 'trumpe' and Old French 'triomphe' (triumph), originally referring to a winning card.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04498154-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ trʌmp / (AmE), / trʌmp / (BrE)
(in card games) a playing card of the suit chosen to rank above the others, able to win over cards of different suits.
card of winning suit in card games

Examples

He played a trump and won the trick easily.
If you don't have any hearts, you can play a trump instead.
Holding the highest trump gives you a big advantage in this game.

Synset Examples

the ace of trumps is a sure winner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trump suit - the suit chosen as trumps in a hand of card games
  • Antonym: discard - a card played when unable or unwilling to follow suit or play trumps

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English trompe, from Old French trompe (trumpet), as trump cards were originally signaled by a trumpet.

4. VERB

/ trʌmp / (AmE), / trʌmp / (BrE)
(in card games) to play a card of the suit chosen as the trump, thereby potentially winning the trick.
play a trump card in a card game

Examples

She cleverly trumped her opponent’s ace with the king of hearts.
If you can't follow suit, you may have to trump to win the trick.
He trumped the lead card and secured the round for his team.

Etymology

From Old French 'trompe' (trumpet), originally referring to a winning card or suit in cards.
