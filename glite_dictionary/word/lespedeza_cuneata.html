Lespedeza cuneata

1. NOUN

/ lɛspəˈdiːzə kjuːniˈeɪtə / (AmE), / lɛspɪˈdiːzə kjuːnɪˈɑːtə / (BrE)
A perennial plant species widely grown for forage and as a hay crop, especially on poor or marginal land.
perennial forage and hay plant species

Examples

Farmers often plant Lespedeza cuneata to restore fertility to overworked soil.
Some regions use Lespedeza cuneata as an important hay crop for livestock.
The rapid growth of Lespedeza cuneata makes it valuable for preventing soil erosion.

Etymology

From the genus name Lespedeza (named after Vicente Manuel de Céspedes, a Spanish governor of Florida) and Latin 'cuneata' meaning 'wedge-shaped'.
