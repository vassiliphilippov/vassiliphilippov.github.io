to percuss

1. VERB

/ pərˈkʌs / (AmE), / pəˈkʌs / (BrE)
to strike or tap a part of the body, especially to examine it medically
to tap or strike, especially for medical examination
medical

Examples

The physician gently percussed her abdomen to check for tenderness.
To assess lung sounds, the doctor will often percuss the patient's chest.
He percussed the area over the liver to determine its size.

Synset Examples

the doctor percussed his chest and back

Etymology

From Latin percussus, past participle of percutere (to strike through).
