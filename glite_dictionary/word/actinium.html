actinium

1. NOUN

/ ækˈtɪniəm / (AmE), / akˈtɪnɪəm / (BrE)
a radioactive chemical element of the actinide series, found in uranium ores and used in research
radioactive chemical element (Ac)

Examples

The radioactive properties of actinium make it valuable in scientific research.
Uranium ores often contain trace amounts of actinium.
Scientists isolate actinium to study its emission of alpha particles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radium - another radioactive element discovered in uranium ores

Etymology

From Greek aktinos (ray) + -ium; named for its strong radioactivity.
