to ameliorate

1. VERB

/ əˈmiliəˌreɪt / (AmE), / əˈmiːliəreɪt / (BrE)
to make something better or become better; to improve a situation or condition
make or become better; improve

Examples

The new healthcare policy should ameliorate the living conditions of many citizens.
Effective communication can ameliorate conflicts in the workplace.
Despite their efforts, the reforms only partially ameliorated the economic crisis.

Synset Examples

The weather improved toward evening

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to improve - to make or become better
  • Antonym: to worsen - to become or make worse

Etymology

From Latin *ameliorare* (to make better), from *melior* (better).

2. VERB

/ əˈmiːliəˌreɪt / (AmE), / əˈmiːliəreɪt / (BrE)
to make something better or improve a bad situation
to make better; improve

Examples

The new policies are designed to ameliorate living conditions in the city.
International aid helped to ameliorate the crisis after the natural disaster.
He sought to ameliorate the situation by offering a sincere apology.

Synset Examples

The editor improved the manuscript with his changes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to improve - to make or become better
  • Antonym: to worsen - to make or become worse

Etymology

From Latin 'meliorare', from 'melior' meaning 'better'.
