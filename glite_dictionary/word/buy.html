buy

1. VERB

/ baɪ / (AmE), / baɪ / (BrE)
to obtain something in exchange for paying money; to acquire by purchasing
obtain by paying money

Examples

I need to buy a new laptop for work.
She always buys fresh vegetables at the market every weekend.
We bought our house three years ago.

Synset Examples

The family purchased a new car
The conglomerate acquired a new company
She buys for the big department store

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purchase - to buy something; formal synonym of 'buy'
  • Antonym: sell - to give something to someone in exchange for money; antonym of 'buy'

Etymology

From Old English bycgan, related to Old Norse byggja (‘to buy, settle’), of Germanic origin.
