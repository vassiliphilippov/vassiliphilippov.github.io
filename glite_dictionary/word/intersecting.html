intersecting

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪntərˈsɛktɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈsɛktɪŋ / (BrE)
(of lines, paths, etc.) crossing each other, especially to form an X shape.
crossing each other, especially in an X shape

Examples

The two streets are intersecting at a sharp angle downtown.
In the diagram, you can see several intersecting lines forming geometric shapes.
The park features a network of intersecting paths for walkers and cyclists.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: parallel - (of lines) side by side and never meeting

Etymology

From Latin intersectus, past participle of intersecare (to cut between).
