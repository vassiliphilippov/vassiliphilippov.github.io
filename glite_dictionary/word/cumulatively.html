cumulatively

1. ADV

/ ˈkjuːmjələtɪvli / (AmE), / ˈkjuːmjʊlətɪvli / (BrE)
In a way that increases or builds up gradually by successive additions.
in a cumulative or additive manner

Examples

The damage caused by pollution tends to add up cumulatively over several years.
You will notice progress only cumulatively, after many small efforts have added up.
These expenses may seem minor individually, but their impact is significant when considered cumulatively.

Synset Examples

mind has become self-reproducing through man's capacity to transmit experience and its products cumulatively

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cumulative - increasing by successive additions

Etymology

From 'cumulative' (Latin cumulātīv-us, from cumulāre 'to heap up') + '-ly'.
