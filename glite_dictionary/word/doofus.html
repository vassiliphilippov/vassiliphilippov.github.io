doofus

1. NOUN

/ ˈduː.fəs / (AmE), / ˈduː.fəs / (BrE)
an unintelligent or incompetent person, often regarded as silly or foolish
a foolish or incompetent person
informal american

Examples

Don't be such a doofus; the instructions are right in front of you.
Jake forgot his own birthday—what a doofus!
She called him a doofus after he walked into the glass door.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fool - a person who acts unwisely or imprudently
  • Synonym: idiot - a person of low intelligence or who acts in a stupid manner
  • Antonym: genius - an exceptionally intelligent person

Etymology

Probably an alteration of 'doof', dialectal variant of 'dof' meaning stupid, or influenced by 'goof'. First appeared in American English in the mid-20th century.
