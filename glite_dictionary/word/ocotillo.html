ocotillo

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊ.kəˈtiː.joʊ / (AmE), / ˌɒk.əˈtiː.jəʊ / (BrE)
A desert shrub of the southwestern United States and Mexico with slender, spiny branches that produce leaves and clusters of red flowers after rainfall.
desert shrub of the US Southwest and Mexico

Examples

The ocotillo blooms spectacularly after a heavy rain in the desert.
Clusters of bright red flowers appeared on the ocotillo in early spring.
Hikers admired the tall, spiny stems of the ocotillo along the trail.

Etymology

From Mexican Spanish 'ocotillo', diminutive of 'ocote' (pine, torchwood).
