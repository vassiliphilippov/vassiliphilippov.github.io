inventory

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪn.vənˌtɔɹ.i / (AmE), / ˈɪn.vən.tə.ri / (BrE)
A complete list of items, such as goods or materials, in stock or on hand, typically including details of quantities and value.
itemized list of goods or supplies on hand

Examples

The store manager completed an inventory of the products on the shelves.
We take an inventory every three months to keep track of supplies.
Missing items were discovered during the annual inventory check.

Synset Examples

an inventory may be necessary to see if anything is missing
they held an inventory every month

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stock - goods kept on hand for use or sale
  • Synonym: inventory control - the process of managing and regulating inventories

Etymology

From Latin inventarium (a detailed list of items), from invenire (to find).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01013367-n
Synset POS: n

/ ˈɪnvənˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈɪnvəntri / (BrE)
