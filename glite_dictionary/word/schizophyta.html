Schizophyta

1. NOUN

/ skɪˈzɑːfəˌtə / (AmE), / skɪˈzɪfəˌtə / (BrE)
a former term in biological classification referring to a group of simple, algae-like organisms, now generally called Cyanophyta or blue-green algae
former biological group (Cyanophyta)

Examples

Some older scientific texts refer to blue-green algae as Schizophyta.
The classification of Schizophyta has been largely replaced by the modern term Cyanobacteria.
Students may encounter the term Schizophyta when studying the history of microbiology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cyanophyta - division of blue-green algae now known as cyanobacteria

Etymology

From Greek 'schizo-' (splitting) and '-phyta' (plants).
