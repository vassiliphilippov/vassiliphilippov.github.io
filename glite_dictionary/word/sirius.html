Sirius

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɪriəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪəriəs / (BrE)
the brightest star in the night sky, located in the constellation Canis Major
the brightest star in the night sky

Examples

Sirius is often called the Dog Star due to its position in the constellation Canis Major.
During winter, Sirius shines brightly in the southern night sky.
Ancient Egyptians associated the rising of Sirius with the annual flooding of the Nile.

Etymology

From Greek Seirios (glowing, scorching), the name of the star in ancient times.
