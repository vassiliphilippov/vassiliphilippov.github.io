to unline

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈlaɪn / (AmE), / ʌnˈlaɪn / (BrE)
to remove the lining from fabric, papers, or other materials
remove the lining from something

Examples

Before you start sewing, carefully unline the jacket to avoid damaging the fabric.
The restorer needed to unline the painting before cleaning it.
After years of use, she decided to unline the old curtains and replace the material.

Synset Examples

Please unline the fabric before starting the project.
They decided to unline the papers to recycle them.

Etymology

From un- (meaning 'remove') + line (meaning 'lining').
