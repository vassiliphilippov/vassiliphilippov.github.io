deathbed

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛθˌbɛd / (AmE), / ˈdɛθˌbɛd / (BrE)
the last hours of a person's life, especially as spent in bed before dying
final hours before death

Examples

His family gathered around his deathbed to say their final goodbyes.
She confessed her secret to her daughter from her deathbed.
Many important decisions have been made on someone's deathbed.

Etymology

From the combination of 'death' and 'bed'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɛθˌbɛd / (AmE), / ˈdɛθ.bɛd / (BrE)
the bed on which a person dies, or the period when someone is close to death
bed where a person dies

Examples

His family gathered around his deathbed to say their final goodbyes.
On her deathbed, she revealed a secret she had kept for decades.
The nurse sat quietly by the old man's deathbed, offering comfort in his final hours.

Etymology

From Old English 'death' + 'bed', indicating the bed in which someone dies.
