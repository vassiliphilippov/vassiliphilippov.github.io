axolotl

1. NOUN

/ ˈæksəˌlɑːtəl / (AmE), / ˈæksəlɒtl / (BrE)
A salamander (Ambystoma mexicanum) native to lakes in Mexico, which usually remains in its aquatic larval form and does not undergo metamorphosis into a terrestrial adult.
aquatic salamander native to Mexico

Examples

The axolotl is known for its remarkable ability to regenerate lost limbs and even parts of its heart and brain.
Many researchers study the axolotl for its unique regenerative properties in medical science.
Unlike most salamanders, the axolotl remains in its aquatic larval state throughout its life.

Etymology

From Nahuatl (Aztec language) 'āxōlōtl', meaning 'water monster'.
