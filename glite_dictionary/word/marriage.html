marriage

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmarɪdʒ / (BrE)
The legal or formal union of two people as partners in a personal relationship, typically recognized by law.
the state of being married

Examples

They celebrated fifty years of marriage with a family reunion.
Their marriage has stood the test of time despite many challenges.
Some cultures arrange marriage for their children at a young age.

Synset Examples

a long and happy marriage
God bless this union

Related Terms

  • Antonym: divorce - the legal dissolution of a marriage
  • Synonym: wedding - a ceremony at which marriage is formalized

Etymology

From Old French *mariage*, from Latin *maritare* (to marry).
