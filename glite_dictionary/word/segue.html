segue

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛɡ.weɪ / (AmE), / ˈsɛɡ.weɪ / (BrE)
a smooth transition from one state, subject, or section to another
smooth transition from one thing to another

Examples

The conversation included a clever segue from serious topics to lighthearted banter.
The musician made a seamless segue from one song to the next during the concert.
Her speech had an awkward segue between two unrelated points.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transition - the process or a period of changing from one state to another

Etymology

From Italian 'segue', third person singular of 'seguire' (to follow), from Latin 'sequi'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00202246-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈsɛɡ.weɪ / (AmE), / ˈsɛɡ.weɪ / (BrE)
to move smoothly and unhesitatingly from one state, subject, or section to another, especially in music or conversation
move smoothly to the next section or topic

Examples

The speaker segued seamlessly from the introduction into the main argument.
Without pausing, the band segued into their next song.
She often segues from one anecdote to another during her presentations.

Synset Examples

He segued into another discourse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transition - to change from one state or subject to another

Etymology

From Italian 'segue', meaning 'it follows', from 'seguire' (to follow).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00783538-v
Synset POS: v
