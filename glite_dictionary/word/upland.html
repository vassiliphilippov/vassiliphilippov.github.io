upland

1. ADJ

/ ˈʌp.lænd / (AmE), / ˈʌp.lənd / (BrE)
(of land) situated in high or hilly country, away from low-lying areas.
located in high or hilly country

Examples

The river floods rarely reach the upland farms.
They hiked through the upland areas to get a better view of the valley.
The climate in upland regions is generally cooler than in the lowlands.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lowland - situated in low or level country

Etymology

From Old English 'ūpland' (high land; land higher than the sea).

2. NOUN

/ ˈʌpˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈʌplənd / (BrE)
an area of high or elevated land, especially one that is not mountainous but stands higher than the surrounding country; often contrasted with lowland
high or elevated land area

Examples

The sheep graze on the upland during the summer months.
Much of the region consists of upland dotted with small villages and farms.
Water from the upland flows down to feed the fertile valleys below.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lowland - area of land that is at a low elevation, usually near sea level

Etymology

Old English upland, from up (up) + land (land).
