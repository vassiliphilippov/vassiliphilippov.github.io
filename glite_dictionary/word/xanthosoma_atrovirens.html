Xanthosoma atrovirens

1. NOUN

/ zænˌθoʊˈsoʊmə æˌtroʊvəˈrɛnz / (AmE), / zanˌθəʊˈsəʊmə aˌtrəʊvɪˈrɛnz / (BrE)
A tropical American plant of the aroid family, cultivated for its edible tubers, which are cooked and eaten like yams or potatoes.
tropical plant with edible tubers

Examples

Farmers in the region grow Xanthosoma atrovirens for its nutritious and versatile tubers.
The chef prepared a traditional dish using Xanthosoma atrovirens cooked with herbs and spices.
Researchers are studying Xanthosoma atrovirens as a potential crop to improve food security in tropical areas.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin genus Xanthosoma, from Greek xanthos (yellow) + soma (body); species name from Latin atrovirens (dark green).
