F. G. Banting

1. PROPN

/ ˌɛf ˌdʒi ˈbæntɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌɛf ˌdʒi ˈbæntɪŋ / (BrE)
(1891–1941) A Canadian physiologist who discovered insulin with C. H. Best and used it to treat diabetes.
Canadian physiologist who discovered insulin

Examples

F. G. Banting received the Nobel Prize for the discovery of insulin.
The pioneering work of F. G. Banting transformed the treatment of diabetes worldwide.
Medical textbooks often reference F. G. Banting for his role in developing insulin therapy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insulin - a hormone discovered by Banting and Best for regulating blood sugar
