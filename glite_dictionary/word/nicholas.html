Nicholas

1. PROPN

/ ˈnɪkələs / (AmE), / ˈnɪkələs / (BrE)
A bishop of Myra in Asia Minor in the 4th century, venerated as Saint Nicholas and associated with the traditions of Santa Claus.
4th-century bishop, Saint Nicholas

Examples

Many churches around the world are named after Nicholas, the patron saint of children.
According to legend, Nicholas performed many miracles during his lifetime.
The story of Nicholas inspired the modern image of Santa Claus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Santa Claus - the legendary figure based on Saint Nicholas who brings gifts at Christmas

Etymology

From Greek Nikolaos, from nikē (victory) + laos (people).
