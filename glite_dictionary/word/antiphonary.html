antiphonary

1. NOUN

/ ænˈtɪfənˌɛri / (AmE), / ænˈtɪfənəri / (BrE)
A book containing a collection of antiphons, used in liturgical services, especially in Christian religious traditions.
book of liturgical antiphons

Examples

The medieval choir used an ancient antiphonary during the evening prayers.
She researched Gregorian chants preserved in a 15th-century antiphonary.
The library's collection includes a beautifully illuminated antiphonary from the Renaissance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antiphon - a short chant in Christian ritual, sung as a refrain

Etymology

From Medieval Latin antiphonarium, from antiphona (antiphon).
