self-repper

1. NOUN

/ sɛlfˈɹɛpər / (AmE), / sɛlfˈɹɛpə / (BrE)
a person who repairs or repurposes their own belongings, such as clothing and personal items, rather than replacing them
person who repairs own items

Examples

Being a self-repper saves money and reduces waste.
He has always been a self-repper, preferring to mend things rather than throw them away.
During lockdown, many people discovered their inner self-repper by fixing clothes and gadgets at home.

Synset Examples

Being a self-repper saves money and promotes sustainability.
He's a self-repper, always fixing his own clothes instead of buying new ones.
She became a self-repper out of necessity during tough financial times.

Etymology

From 'self-' (oneself) + shortened form of 'repair'.
