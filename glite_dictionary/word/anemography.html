anemography

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈmɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌænɪˈmɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the process of recording or measuring wind speed and direction, especially using specialized instruments
recording wind measurements

Examples

Advances in anemography have improved our understanding of local wind patterns.
Researchers relied on anemography to gather data during the storm season.
Accurate anemography is crucial in designing wind turbines for maximum efficiency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anemometry - the measurement of wind speed and direction

Etymology

From Greek 'anemos' (wind) and '-graphy' (writing, recording).
