Cassia fistula

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæsiə ˈfɪstjʊlə / (AmE), / ˌkæsiə ˈfɪstjʊlə / (BrE)
A tropical tree native to Asia and other warm regions, with fragrant yellow flowers and seed pods used in traditional medicine.
tropical tree with yellow flowers and medicinal pods

Examples

The Cassia fistula is admired for its cascading yellow blossoms during the summer months.
Traditional healers use the pulp from Cassia fistula pods to treat various ailments.
Gardeners often plant Cassia fistula to add vibrant color and fragrance to parks and streets.

Etymology

From Latin: cassia (a type of aromatic plant) + fistula (pipe, tube), referring to the tree's long pod.
