netting

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈnɛtɪŋ / (BrE)
A fabric made of loosely woven threads with open spaces, often used for veils, curtains, or decorative purposes.
loosely woven open fabric

Examples

The bride's veil was made of delicate netting that shimmered in the sunlight.
Gardeners often use netting to protect their plants from birds and insects.
She draped a layer of white netting over the table as a decorative touch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mesh - material made of a network of wire or thread
  • Synonym: lace - delicate fabric made of yarn or thread in an open weblike pattern

Etymology

From 'net' + -ing, indicating material made in the form of a net.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɛtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈnɛtɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of making nets, especially for fishing or trapping
act of making nets

Examples

The fishermen spent the afternoon netting in preparation for the upcoming season.
Traditional netting requires skill to ensure strong, durable knots.
Many communities rely on netting as a major source of income.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: net - an open-meshed fabric made of string, rope, or wire

Etymology

From the verb 'net' + -ing, indicating the action or process.
