Caprella

1. NOUN

/ kəˈprɛlə / (AmE), / kəˈprɛlə / (BrE)
A genus of slender, elongated marine crustaceans commonly known as skeleton shrimp, found in oceans worldwide.
genus of skeleton shrimp

Examples

Marine biologists recently discovered a new species within the genus Caprella off the Pacific coast.
The transparent body of a Caprella makes it resemble a tiny skeleton.
Researchers observed Caprella clinging to seaweed in the tidal pools.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skeleton shrimp - common name for crustaceans of the genus Caprella

Etymology

From Latin 'caprella', meaning 'little goat', referring to the shape or movement.

2. NOUN

/ kəˈprɛlə / (AmE), / kəˈprɛlə / (BrE)
a genus of small, slender marine crustaceans known as skeleton shrimp, characterized by their elongated bodies and grasping appendages
a genus of skeleton shrimp

Examples

The unusual shape of the Caprella allows it to move easily among seaweed and hydroids.
Marine biologists often study Caprella to understand crustacean adaptation.
I spotted several Caprella clinging to underwater vegetation during my dive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skeleton shrimp - common name for crustaceans in the family Caprellidae

Etymology

From New Latin Caprella, diminutive of Latin capra (goat), referencing the animal's shape.
