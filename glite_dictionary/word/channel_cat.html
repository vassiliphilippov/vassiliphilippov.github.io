channel cat

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃænəl kæt / (AmE), / ˈtʃænəl kat / (BrE)
a widespread North American freshwater catfish (Ictalurus punctatus), popular as a food fish, found especially in rivers and lakes of the central United States
North American freshwater catfish species

Examples

The lake is famous for its large channel cat population, attracting anglers from across the country.
He caught a fifteen-pound channel cat near the old bridge last summer.
Many Southern recipes include fried channel cat as a regional delicacy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catfish - a fish with whisker-like barbels around the mouth
