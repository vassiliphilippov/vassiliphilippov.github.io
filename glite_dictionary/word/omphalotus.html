Omphalotus

1. NOUN

/ ɑmˈfælətəs / (AmE), / ɒmˈfalətəs / (BrE)
A genus of brightly colored fungi, known for their deeply depressed cap and including species such as the jack-o'-lantern mushroom.
genus of fungi with a depressed cap

Examples

The Omphalotus genus includes the well-known jack-o'-lantern mushroom.
Scientists collected several Omphalotus specimens during their field study.
Some species within Omphalotus are notable for their bioluminescent properties.

Etymology

From Greek 'omphalos' (navel) + '-otus', relating to the navel-like depression in the cap.

2. NOUN

/ ɑmˈfælətəs / (AmE), / ɒmˈfalətəs / (BrE)
A genus of fungi characterized by a depressed disc in the cap, including species known for their bioluminescent properties.
genus of fungi with a depressed cap center

Examples

The Omphalotus genus includes the well-known jack-o'-lantern mushroom.
Researchers studied the bioluminescent qualities of Omphalotus species found in the forest.
Some mushrooms in the Omphalotus genus are toxic and should not be eaten.

Etymology

From Greek 'omphalos' (navel) and '-otus', referring to the navel-like depression in the cap.
