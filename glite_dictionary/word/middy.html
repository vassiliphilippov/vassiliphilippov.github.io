middy

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪdi / (AmE), / ˈmɪdi / (BrE)
A woman's or girl's blouse with a sailor collar, typically inspired by naval uniforms.
blouse with sailor collar

Examples

She wore a white middy with a blue skirt to the summer party.
The school's uniform included a navy middy trimmed with white stripes.
Vintage fashion enthusiasts often seek out original middies from the early twentieth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sailor suit - a suit based on a naval uniform, especially for children

Etymology

Shortened from 'middy blouse', from 'midshipman', referring to naval uniforms.
