Rebekah

1. PROPN

/ rɪˈbɛkə / (AmE), / rɪˈbiːkə / (BrE)
(in the Old Testament) the wife of Isaac and the mother of Jacob and Esau.
wife of Isaac in the Old Testament

Examples

Rebekah was known for her kindness and hospitality to strangers.
Isaac and Rebekah are important ancestral figures in the book of Genesis.
The story of Rebekah drawing water at the well is often retold in Sunday school.

Etymology

From Hebrew Rivqāh, a biblical name.
