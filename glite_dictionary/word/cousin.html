cousin

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌzən / (AmE), / ˈkʌz(ə)n / (BrE)
the child of your aunt or uncle
the child of your aunt or uncle

Examples

My cousin lives in Australia and visits us every summer.
She invited her cousin to the family reunion this year.
After school, I often play soccer with my cousin at the park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relative - a person connected by blood or marriage
  • Antonym: sibling - a brother or sister

Etymology

From Old French 'cosin', from Latin 'consobrinus' (child of a mother's sister).
