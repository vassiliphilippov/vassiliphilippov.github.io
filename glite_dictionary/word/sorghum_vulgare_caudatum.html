Sorghum vulgare caudatum

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔːrɡəm vʌlˈɡɛəri kɔːˈdeɪtəm / (AmE), / ˈsɔːɡəm vʌlˈɡɛəri kɔːˈdeɪtəm / (BrE)
A variety of sorghum originating from Sudan, notable for its exceptionally large, soft, white grains.
Sudanese sorghum with large white grains

Examples

The farmers chose to plant Sorghum vulgare caudatum because of its high grain yield and resilience.
Researchers are studying Sorghum vulgare caudatum for its potential in drought-prone areas.
The bread was made from flour milled from Sorghum vulgare caudatum grains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorghum - A genus of grasses cultivated for grain

Etymology

Latin taxonomic nomenclature: 'Sorghum' from Italian, originally from Latin; 'caudatum' meaning 'tailed' in Latin, referring to the plant's panicle shape.
