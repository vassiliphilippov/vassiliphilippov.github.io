referential

1. ADJ

/ ˌrɛfəˈrɛnʃəl / (AmE), / ˌrɛfəˈrɛnʃəl / (BrE)
(of language or symbols) referring to or pointing out something; relating to reference.
referring to or about something

Examples

In linguistics, a referential expression is one that points to a specific entity in the real world.
Many signs are referential, directly pointing to something or someone.
Her analysis focused on the referential meaning of words in the poem.

Synset Examples

symbols are inherently referential

Related Terms

  • Synonym: denotative - relating to the direct, explicit meaning of a word or symbol

Etymology

From Latin 'referre' (to bring back, refer) + English '-ial'.
