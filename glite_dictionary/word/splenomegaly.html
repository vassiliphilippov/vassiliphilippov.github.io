splenomegaly

1. NOUN

/ ˌspliːnəˈmɛɡəli / (AmE), / ˌspliːnəˈmɛɡəli / (BrE)
abnormal enlargement of the spleen, often as a result of infection, liver disease, or other disorders
abnormal enlargement of the spleen
medical

Examples

The doctor ordered an ultrasound to confirm the presence of splenomegaly.
Infectious mononucleosis can sometimes lead to splenomegaly in patients.
Careful evaluation of splenomegaly is essential to determine its underlying cause.

Etymology

From Greek *splēn* (spleen) + *-megaly* (enlargement).
