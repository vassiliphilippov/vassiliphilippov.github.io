Manichaean

1. ADJ

/ ˌmænəˈkiːən / (AmE), / ˌmanɪˈkiːən / (BrE)
relating to Manichaeism, an ancient dualistic religious system that taught the coexistence of good and evil
of or relating to Manichaeism

Examples

The philosopher wrote extensively on the Manichaean concept of dualism.
Some historians debate the influence of Manichaean beliefs on early Christianity.
Her thesis analyzes the Manichaean worldview in medieval literature.

Etymology

From Late Latin Manichaeus (Manes, founder of Manichaeism) + -an.

2. ADJ

/ ˌmænəˈkiən / (AmE), / ˌmanɪˈkiːən / (BrE)
relating to the religious or philosophical doctrine of Manichaeism, especially its belief in a dualistic conflict between good and evil
relating to the doctrine of dualism (good vs evil)

Examples

Some philosophers criticize Manichaean thinking as an oversimplification of complex issues.
The novel explores Manichaean themes of light versus darkness.
His rhetoric often takes on a strongly Manichaean tone, dividing the world into clear good and evil.

Synset Examples

a Manichaean conflict between good and evil

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dualistic - involving two opposing forces or principles
  • Antonym: monistic - relating to the doctrine that reality is a single principle or entity

Etymology

From Late Latin Manichaeus, from Greek Manichaios, after Mani, the founder of Manichaeism.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02958589-a
Synset POS: a

3. NOUN

/ ˌmænɪˈkiːən / (AmE), / ˌmanɪˈkiːən / (BrE)
A follower or adherent of Manichaeism, a major religious movement founded by the prophet Mani in the 3rd century AD.
adherent of Manichaeism

Examples

As a Manichaean, he advocated a dualistic view of the world.
Many Manichaeans faced persecution for their religious beliefs in the medieval period.
The writings of a Manichaean priest were discovered among ancient manuscripts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Manichaeism - the religious system founded by Mani in the 3rd century AD

Etymology

From Late Latin Manichaeus (follower of Mani), from Mani (the founder of Manichaeism) + -an (denoting follower).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10310474-n
Synset POS: n
