titillation

1. NOUN

/ tɪt̬əˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / tɪtəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of lightly touching someone so as to cause them to laugh or squirm; tickling
the act of tickling

Examples

The titillation of his feet made him burst into laughter.
Her playful titillation quickly turned the mood lighthearted.
Children often giggle uncontrollably at even the slightest titillation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tickling - the act of touching someone lightly to cause laughter

Etymology

From Latin 'titillatio', from 'titillare' (to tickle).

2. NOUN

/ ˌtɪtəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtɪtɪˈleɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
The act of exciting or stimulating the senses or emotions in a pleasurable or agreeable way.
pleasurable stimulation or excitement

Examples

The titillation provided by the suspenseful movie kept the audience on the edge of their seats.
Some people enjoy the titillation of reading gossip magazines, even if they know the stories aren't true.
Advertisers often rely on the titillation of the senses to attract attention to their products.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stimulation - the act of encouraging something to happen or develop

Etymology

From Latin 'titillatio', from 'titillare' (to tickle, to excite).

3. NOUN

/ ˌtɪtəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtɪtɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a pleasurable or excited sensation, often caused by teasing, tickling, or arousing interest
excited or pleasurable sensation

Examples

The comedian's jokes provided gentle titillation to the otherwise serious audience.
There was a certain titillation in reading the scandalous headlines.
She felt a mild titillation as the feather brushed her neck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrill - a sudden feeling of excitement or pleasure

Etymology

From Latin 'titillatio', from 'titillare' (to tickle).
