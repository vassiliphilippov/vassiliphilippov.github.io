Salvadoraceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌsælvədɔːˈreɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌsalvədɔːˈreɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of Old World shrubs and trees in the order Gentianales, related to the olive family but with four stamens and four petals
family of shrubs and trees in Gentianales

Examples

The Salvadoraceae are primarily found in arid regions of Africa and Asia.
Botanists classified the mysterious shrub as part of the Salvadoraceae family.
Members of the Salvadoraceae exhibit unique floral structures with four distinct stamens and petals.

Etymology

From the genus name Salvadoria, named for Juan Salvador, a Spanish apothecary and botanist, plus Latin -aceae (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsæl.vəˌdɔːˈreɪ.siˌiː / (AmE), / ˌsal.və.dɔːˈreɪ.siː / (BrE)
a family of Old World shrubs and trees in the order Gentianales, related to Oleaceae but distinguished by having four stamens and four petals
family of Old World shrubs and trees (Gentianales)

Examples

The Salvadoraceae are distributed primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.
Several species within Salvadoraceae have traditional medicinal uses.
Botanists debate the phylogenetic relationships between Salvadoraceae and related families.

Etymology

Named after the type genus Salvadoria, itself named for Juan Salvador, a Spanish botanist.
