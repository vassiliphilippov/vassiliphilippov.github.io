sup

1. NOUN

/ sʌp / (AmE), / sʌp / (BrE)
a small quantity of liquid, especially a swallow or sip of a drink or liquid food
a small amount of liquid

Examples

He took a cautious sup of the hot tea before speaking.
Just a sup of soup was left in the bowl.
She asked for a sup of wine to warm herself.

Synset Examples

a sup of ale

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sip - a small mouthful of liquid

Etymology

From Old English *suppan* (to sip or swallow).

2. VERB

/ sʌp / (AmE), / sʌp / (BrE)
to take liquid or soft food into the mouth a little at a time, especially by drinking or eating with a spoon
take small sips or spoonfuls
archaic

Examples

He supped the hot soup carefully, blowing on each spoonful before tasting it.
She likes to sup a cup of herbal tea while reading in the evening.
The elderly man supped slowly, savoring each bite of the stew.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sip - to drink in small amounts

Etymology

Old English supan (to sip, swallow slowly), related to German suppe (to sip).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01208079-v
Synset POS: v
