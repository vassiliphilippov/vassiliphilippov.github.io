poignant

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɔɪnjənt / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪnjənt / (BrE)
evoking a keen sense of sadness or distress; deeply affecting the emotions
evoking sadness or distress

Examples

The film’s poignant ending left many people in tears.
Her speech was especially poignant given the recent events in the community.
There is a poignant beauty in memories of childhood.

Synset Examples

poignant anxiety

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moving - evoking strong feelings, especially of sadness or sympathy
  • Antonym: unaffecting - not evoking any strong emotion or feeling

Etymology

From Old French poignant, present participle of poindre (to prick), from Latin pungere (to prick).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɔɪnjənt / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪnjənt / (BrE)
Evoking a keen sense of sadness or emotional intensity; deeply moving.
evoking sadness or strong emotion

Examples

The film's ending was so poignant that many viewers were moved to tears.
She felt a poignant sense of nostalgia as she walked through her childhood home.
There is a poignant beauty in the way the story captures loss and hope.

Synset Examples

the homecoming of the released hostages was an affecting scene
poignant grief cannot endure forever
his gratitude was simple and touching

Related Terms

  • Synonym: touching - evoking feelings of tenderness or sympathy
  • Synonym: moving - causing strong feelings of emotion
  • Antonym: unemotional - not causing emotion; lacking feeling

Etymology

From Old French *poignant* (pricking), from Latin *pungens*, present participle of *pungere* (to prick).
