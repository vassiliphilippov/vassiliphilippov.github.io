unity

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuːnəti / (AmE), / ˈjuːnəti / (BrE)
the state of being joined together or forming a whole, especially in agreement or harmony
state of being unified or whole

Examples

The leaders emphasized the importance of unity in overcoming national challenges.
After the merger, there was a strong sense of unity among the employees.
National unity is crucial during times of crisis.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: division - the act or process of separating into parts or groups
  • Synonym: harmony - agreement or concord

Etymology

From Latin 'unitas' (oneness, sameness) from 'unus' (one).

2. NOUN

/ ˈjuːnəti / (AmE), / ˈjuːnɪti / (BrE)
the state of being undivided or complete, forming a whole with nothing missing
state of being whole or undivided

Examples

The country's unity was threatened by internal conflict.
We must strive for unity in our team to succeed.
The piece achieves a sense of unity through its consistent style.

Synset Examples

the integrity of the nervous system is required for normal development
he took measures to insure the territorial unity of Croatia

Related Terms

  • Synonym: integrity - the state of being whole and undivided
  • Antonym: disunity - lack of unity or harmony; division

Etymology

From Latin unitas (oneness), from unus (one).

3. NOUN

/ ˈjuːnəti / (AmE), / ˈjuːnəti / (BrE)
The number one; the quality or state of being one.
the number one

Examples

In mathematics, the multiplicative unity is simply the number one.
The ratio of any number to unity yields the number itself.
Unity is often represented by the digit '1' in equations.

Synset Examples

he has the one but will need a two and three to go with it
they had lunch at one

Related Terms

  • Synonym: one - the lowest cardinal number; a single unit

Etymology

From Latin 'unitas', from 'unus' (one).
