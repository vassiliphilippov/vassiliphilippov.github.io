miller

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪlər / (AmE), / ˈmɪlə / (BrE)
a person who operates or works in a mill, especially a grain mill that grinds wheat or other cereal grains into flour
person who works in a grain mill

Examples

The miller worked long hours grinding grain into flour for the village.
Her grandfather was a skilled miller who ran the old watermill by the river.
Local farmers would bring their wheat to the miller each harvest season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baker - a person who makes and sells bread and cakes

Etymology

From Middle English *miller*, from Old English *mylnere*, from late Latin *molendinarius* (a miller), from *molendinum* (mill).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɪlə / (BrE)
a machine tool in which a workpiece secured to a carriage is fed against rotating cutters to shape or cut metal
machine tool for shaping metal

Examples

The company bought a new miller to increase the efficiency of its production line.
An experienced operator can produce precise components with a miller.
The miller needs regular maintenance to ensure its cutters stay sharp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milling machine - machine tool used for shaping solid materials by cutting with rotary cutters

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɪlə / (BrE)
any of various moths, especially those with powdery wings, resembling flour-dusted millers
a moth with powdery wings

Examples

The miller rested quietly on the windowsill, its wings looking dusted with white flour.
Many gardeners dislike finding millers on their plants, as some species can be pests.
The powdery wings of the miller help it blend into light backgrounds during the day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moth - an insect related to butterflies, typically nocturnal

Etymology

So called because their wings appear dusted like a miller's clothing covered in flour.
