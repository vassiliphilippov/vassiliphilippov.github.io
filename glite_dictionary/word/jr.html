Jr.

1. NOUN

/ dʒuːn.jɚ / (AmE), / dʒuːn.jə / (BrE)
a title used after a son's name when he has the same first and last name as his father
title for a son with the same name as his father

Examples

The invitation was addressed to John Smith Jr. instead of his father.
Martin Luther King Jr. was a prominent civil rights leader in the United States.
Their mailbox displayed "Robert Johnson Jr." to avoid confusion with his father.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Sr. - title used for a father with the same name as his son

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'junior', from Latin 'iunior' (younger).

2. ADJ

/ ˈdʒuːniər / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːniə / (BrE)
A title added to a man's name to indicate that he is the son of his father who has the same name, especially used to distinguish between two family members.
title for son with same name as father

Examples

After his father passed away, Michael added Jr. to his name to honor the family tradition.
On official documents, his full name appears as Anthony Walker, Jr.
The award was given to Charles H. Smith, Jr., to distinguish him from his father.

Synset Examples

John Junior
John Smith, Jr.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Sr. - title for a father sharing his name with his son

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'junior', from Latin 'iunior' (younger).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02108662-s
Synset POS: s
