Bosnia

1. PROPN

/ ˈbɑːzniə / (AmE), / ˈbɒzniə / (BrE)
the northern region of Bosnia-Herzegovina, forming part of the country alongside Herzegovina
northern region of Bosnia-Herzegovina

Examples

Many refugees fled from Bosnia to neighboring countries during the war.
The landscape of Bosnia is characterized by rolling hills and rivers.
After traveling through Herzegovina, we continued our journey north into Bosnia.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Herzegovina - the southern region of Bosnia-Herzegovina

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'Bosna', likely derived from the Bosna river.

2. PROPN

/ ˈbɑːzniə / (AmE), / ˈbɒzniə / (BrE)
A mountainous region and historical country in south-central Europe, formerly part of Yugoslavia, that became independent in 1992.
country/region in south-central Europe

Examples

During our trip through Europe, we spent a week exploring Bosnia's beautiful mountains and villages.
The war in Bosnia during the 1990s had a profound impact on its people and culture.
Many tourists visit Bosnia each year to experience its rich history and diverse cuisine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bosnia and Herzegovina - The country consisting of Bosnia and Herzegovina, officially recognized after its independence in 1992

Etymology

From the medieval Latin 'Bosna', of uncertain origin; possibly from the Bosna River.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08835482-n
Synset POS: n
