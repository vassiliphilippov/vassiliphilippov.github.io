gospeler

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑːspələr / (AmE), / ˈɡɒspələr / (BrE)
A preacher of the Christian gospel.
Christian gospel preacher
archaic

Examples

The gospeler spoke passionately about faith at the evening revival.
Centuries ago, a gospeler would travel from town to town spreading the message of salvation.
As a gospeler, he dedicated his life to preaching the teachings of Christ.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evangelist - a person who seeks to convert others to the Christian faith, especially by public preaching

Etymology

From 'gospel' + '-er' (one who proclaims or preaches).
