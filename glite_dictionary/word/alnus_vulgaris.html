Alnus vulgaris

1. NOUN

/ ˈælnəs vʌlˈɡɛərɪs / (AmE), / ˈal.nəs vʌlˈɡɛər.ɪs / (BrE)
A species of alder tree native to Europe and western Asia, with brown-black bark, woody catkins, and leaves hairy beneath; also known as the common alder.
species of alder tree (common alder)

Examples

The banks of the river are lined with Alnus vulgaris, providing shade for fish.
Botanists studied the leaf structure of Alnus vulgaris in different climates.
In wetland restoration projects, Alnus vulgaris is often planted to improve soil quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: common alder - another common name for Alnus vulgaris

Etymology

From Latin: Alnus (alder), vulgaris (common).
