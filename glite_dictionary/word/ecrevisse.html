ecrevisse

1. NOUN

/ eɪˈkrɛvɪs / (AmE), / eɪˈkrɛvɪs / (BrE)
A small freshwater crustacean resembling a lobster, especially as used in French cuisine.
small freshwater crustacean; culinary term

Examples

The chef served ecrevisse in a rich wine sauce as the appetizer.
We observed several ecrevisses hiding among the rocks in the shallow stream.
A traditional French recipe calls for freshly caught ecrevisse boiled with herbs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crayfish - a small freshwater crustacean resembling a small lobster

Etymology

From French écrevisse, meaning crayfish.

2. NOUN

/ eɪˈkrɛvɪs / (AmE), / eɪˈkrɛvɪs / (BrE)
A small freshwater crayfish found in Europe, Asia, and western North America.
a small freshwater crayfish

Examples

The chef prepared the soup with fresh ecrevisse from the local river.
Her research focused on the habitat of the European ecrevisse.
We spotted an ecrevisse in the shallow stream during our hike.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crayfish - freshwater crustacean resembling a small lobster

Etymology

From French écrevisse (crayfish).
