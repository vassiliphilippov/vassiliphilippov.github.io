invoice

1. VERB

/ ˈɪnˌvoɪs / (AmE), / ˈɪnvɔɪs / (BrE)
to send someone a bill for goods or services provided
send a bill to someone

Examples

The firm will invoice you for any additional work required.
She invoiced the client as soon as the project was finished.
Please invoice us at the end of each month.

Synset Examples

She invoiced the company for her expenses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bill - to charge or send a bill to someone

Etymology

From Middle French 'envois' (dispatch), from 'envoyer' (to send), influenced by English 'bill' and 'account'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02325883-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌvɔɪs / (AmE), / ˈɪnvɔɪs / (BrE)
a detailed statement listing goods supplied or services rendered, with the amount due for payment
itemized bill for goods or services

Examples

The company sent an invoice to the client for the completed project.
Please make sure to pay the invoice by the end of the month.
We received an invoice yesterday for the office supplies we ordered.

Synset Examples

he paid his bill and left
send me an account of what I owe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bill - a statement of money owed for goods or services
  • Antonym: receipt - a written acknowledgment of having received goods, money, etc.

Etymology

From Middle French 'envois' (dispatches, messages), from 'envoyer' (to send).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06528946-n
Synset POS: n
