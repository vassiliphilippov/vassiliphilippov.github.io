Viscount St. Albans

1. PROPN

/ ˈvaɪkaʊnt sənt ˈɔːlbənz / (AmE), / ˈvaɪkaʊnt sənt ˈɔːlbənz / (BrE)
The title held by Sir Francis Bacon (1561–1626), an English statesman and philosopher known for establishing the foundations of empirical scientific method and advocating inductive reasoning.
title of Francis Bacon, English philosopher

Examples

Viscount St. Albans was instrumental in developing the scientific method through his writings and public service.
Many historians credit Viscount St. Albans with laying the foundation for modern empirical science.
The philosophical works attributed to Viscount St. Albans have had a lasting impact on scientific inquiry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Francis Bacon - English statesman and philosopher (1561–1626)

Etymology

Viscount is from Old French visconte, from Medieval Latin vicecomes (deputy count); St. Albans refers to the town in Hertfordshire, England.
