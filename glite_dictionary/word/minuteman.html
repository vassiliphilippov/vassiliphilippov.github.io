Minuteman

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnɪtˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪtˌmæn / (BrE)
A member of the American colonial militia who was ready to fight at a minute's notice during the period leading up to and during the American Revolution.
colonial American militia member

Examples

The Minuteman played a crucial role in the early battles of the American Revolution.
Stories about the bravery of a typical Minuteman are often told in American history classes.
A statue of a Minuteman stands at the entrance to the park, commemorating the militia.

Etymology

From minute + man, referring to militiamen who could be ready "at a minute's notice".

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪnɪtˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪtˌman / (BrE)
a U.S. intercontinental ballistic missile system carrying nuclear warheads and housed in underground silos
U.S. intercontinental ballistic missile system

Examples

The Air Force continues to maintain and upgrade the Minuteman missile system.
The Minuteman was a key part of the U.S. nuclear deterrent during the Cold War.
Tourists visited the missile site to learn about the history of the Minuteman program.

Etymology

Named after the colonial American Minutemen, reflecting readiness for rapid response.
