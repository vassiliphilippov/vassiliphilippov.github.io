to exalt

1. VERB

/ ɪɡˈzɔlt / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɑːlt / (BrE)
to raise someone or something to a higher rank, position, or level of importance; to praise highly
raise in rank or status; praise highly

Examples

The ceremony was intended to exalt the achievements of the nation's heroes.
She was exalted to a position of great influence after years of hard work.
Great leaders are often exalted by their followers for their vision and courage.

Synset Examples

exalted the humble shoemaker to the rank of King's adviser

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to promote - to raise to a higher position or rank
  • Antonym: to degrade - to lower in rank or status

Etymology

From Latin exaltare, from ex- (out, up) + altus (high).

3. VERB

/ ɪɡˈzɑlt / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɑːlt / (BrE)
to praise someone or something highly; to glorify or honor
praise or glorify highly

Examples

The community came together to exalt the achievements of the local heroes.
In her speech, she exalted the importance of compassion and understanding.
Writers often exalt the beauty of nature in their works.

Synset Examples

glorify one's spouse's cooking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to extol - to praise enthusiastically
  • Synonym: to glorify - to praise or honor highly
  • Antonym: to demean - to cause a severe loss in the dignity of and respect for someone

Etymology

From Latin ex(s)altāre, meaning 'to raise high'.

/ ɪɡˈzɔlt / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɑːlt / (BrE)
to fill (someone) with a noble or heightened emotion; to elate or inspire intensely
to fill with noble or intense emotion

Examples

He was exalted by the news of his long-awaited promotion.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
