precedency

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈsiːdənsi / (AmE), / prɪˈsiːdənsi / (BrE)
the condition of being considered more important or urgent than others; the status of having priority
status of priority or importance

Examples

The ambassador's rank determines his precedency at official ceremonies.
In historical disputes, questions of precedency often caused diplomatic tension.
The judge decided the case should be given precedency due to its urgency.

Synset Examples

… its precedence as the world's leading manufacturer of pharmaceuticals
national independence takes priority over class struggle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: precedence - the condition of being considered more important than someone or something else; priority
  • Synonym: priority - something regarded as more important than another
  • Antonym: inferiority - the state of being lower in status, quality, or importance

Etymology

From Latin 'praecedentia' (the act of going before).

2. NOUN

/ prɪˈsiːdənsi / (AmE), / prɪˈsiːdənsi / (BrE)
The state or act of coming before in time, order, or rank, especially in ceremonial contexts.
priority in time, order, or rank

Examples

In royal processions, precedency is determined by strict protocol.
The precedency of the members was a subject of heated debate among the officials.
He was granted precedency over other dignitaries at the state banquet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: precedence - the condition of being considered more important than someone or something else; priority in rank

Etymology

From Latin *praecedentia* (the state of preceding).

3. NOUN

/ prɪˈsiːdənsi / (AmE), / prɪˈsiːdənsi / (BrE)
The fact of preceding in time or order; the condition of coming before something else.
the state of preceding in time or order

Examples

The precedency of safety regulations over convenience was made clear in the new guidelines.
Historians debated the precedency of the two discoveries and which truly came first.
His argument rested on the precedency of written records compared to oral accounts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: precedence - the condition of being considered more important or coming before in importance, time, or order

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'praecedentia' (precedence), based on Latin 'praecedere' (to go before).
