globe

1. NOUN

/ ɡloʊb / (AmE), / ɡləʊb / (BrE)
an object that has the shape of a sphere
a spherical object

Examples

The chandelier was made from a crystal globe that sparkled in the light.
She placed the glass globe carefully on the shelf, afraid it might roll off.
The ancient astronomer constructed a metal globe to represent the stars.

Synset Examples

a ball of fire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sphere - a perfectly round three-dimensional object, like a ball
  • Synonym: ball - a round object often used in games and sports

Etymology

From Latin globus (sphere, ball).

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ɡloʊb / (AmE), / ɡləʊb / (BrE)
a spherical model on which a map, especially of the earth or another celestial body, is represented
a spherical map model of the earth

Examples

She spun the globe to show her students where Africa is located.
A dusty globe sat in the corner of his office, untouched for years.
The children enjoyed tracing their fingers along the raised mountains of the classroom globe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atlas - a book of maps or charts

Etymology

From Latin globus (a round mass, sphere).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03445436-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ɡloʊb / (AmE), / ɡləʊb / (BrE)
the planet Earth; the world or the third planet from the sun.
the planet Earth

Examples

The astronauts looked down on the blue globe from space.
He traveled the globe in search of new adventures.
Climate change is a challenge facing the entire globe.

Synset Examples

the Earth moves around the sun
he sailed around the world

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Earth - the planet we live on, third from the sun
  • Synonym: world - the Earth, our planet; also the whole of human civilization

Etymology

From Latin globus (sphere, ball).
