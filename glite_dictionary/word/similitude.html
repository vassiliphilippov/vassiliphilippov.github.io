similitude

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈmɪlɪˌtud / (AmE), / sɪˈmɪlɪˌtjuːd / (BrE)
the quality or state of being similar or alike; a resemblance in appearance, character, or nature between persons or things
similarity or likeness

Examples

The similitude between the two paintings was striking, though they were created decades apart.
In literature, authors often use similitude to draw comparisons between unrelated ideas.
Her speech had a certain similitude to his, suggesting they shared similar viewpoints.

Synset Examples

man created God in his own likeness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: likeness - the fact or quality of being alike; resemblance
  • Antonym: difference - a point or way in which people or things are not the same

Etymology

From Latin 'similitudo', from 'similis' meaning similar.

2. NOUN

/ sɪˈmɪlɪˌtjud / (AmE), / sɪˈmɪlɪtjuːd / (BrE)
a person or thing that closely resembles another; a duplicate or counterpart.
a duplicate or close copy

Examples

The artist created a similitude of the original painting for the exhibition.
Each coin bore a remarkable similitude to its predecessor, making them hard to distinguish.
In the museum, a similitude of the ancient vase was displayed alongside the authentic piece.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duplicate - an exact copy of something
  • Antonym: original - the first or initial form from which copies are made

Etymology

From Latin 'similitudo' (likeness), from 'similis' (like).
