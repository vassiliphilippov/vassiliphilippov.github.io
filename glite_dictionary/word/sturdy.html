sturdy

1. ADJ

/ ˈstɝːdi / (AmE), / ˈstɜːdi / (BrE)
physically strong and solid; able to withstand fatigue, hardship, or rough treatment
physically strong and robust

Examples

The hiker relied on his sturdy boots to cross the rocky terrain safely.
Their sturdy determination helped them finish the marathon despite the harsh weather.
She chose a sturdy table that could support heavy loads without wobbling.

Synset Examples

hardy explorers of northern Canada
proud of her tall stalwart son
stout seamen
sturdy young athletes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robust - strong and healthy; vigorous
  • Antonym: frail - easily broken or weak
  • Synonym: stout - brave and determined; strong in body

Etymology

From Middle English 'stordi', probably from Old French 'estourdi' (bold, wild).

2. ADJ

/ ˈstɝːdi / (AmE), / ˈstɜːdi / (BrE)
strongly and solidly built or made; not easily damaged or broken
strongly and solidly built

Examples

These sturdy boots are perfect for hiking in rough terrain.
The table is made of sturdy oak that will last for years.
She built a sturdy fence to keep the animals safe.

Synset Examples

sturdy steel shelves
sturdy canvas
a tough all-weather fabric
some plastics are as tough as metal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robust - strong and healthy; sturdy in form, constitution, or construction
  • Antonym: fragile - easily broken or damaged; delicate

Etymology

From Old French *estourdi* (bold, dazed), from *estordir* (to stun), of Germanic origin.
