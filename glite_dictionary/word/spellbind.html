to spellbind

1. VERB

/ ˈspɛlˌbaɪnd / (AmE), / ˈspɛlˌbaɪnd / (BrE)
to hold the complete attention of someone as if by magic; to fascinate or captivate intensely
hold the attention of; fascinate

Examples

The magician's performance managed to spellbind the entire audience for over an hour.
She knew how to spellbind her readers with vivid descriptions and thrilling plots.
His powerful speech spellbound everyone in the hall, leaving them silent and attentive.

Synset Examples

The snake charmer fascinates the cobra

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to captivate - to attract and hold the interest and attention of; charm
  • Synonym: to mesmerize - to hold someone's attention as if by hypnosis
  • Antonym: bore - to make someone feel weary and uninterested

Etymology

From 'spell' (charm, incantation) + 'bind' (to tie or secure).
