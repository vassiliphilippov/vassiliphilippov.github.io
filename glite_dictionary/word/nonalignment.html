nonalignment

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɑn.əˈlaɪn.mənt / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.əˈlaɪn.mənt / (BrE)
the policy or practice of not aligning with major power blocs or groups, especially in politics or international relations
policy of not aligning with major power blocs

Examples

The country's policy of nonalignment kept it out of major military alliances during the Cold War.
India was a prominent advocate for nonalignment in international politics.
Many nations pursued nonalignment to maintain independence in their foreign affairs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neutrality - the state of not supporting or helping either side in a conflict

Etymology

Formed from 'non-' (not) + 'alignment' (state of being allied or arranged in line).

2. NOUN

/ nɑnəˈlaɪnmənt / (AmE), / nɒnəˈlaɪnmənt / (BrE)
the policy or condition of not aligning with any group, party, or country, especially in relation to international agreements or alliances.
not aligning with any group, especially in international relations

Examples

Many countries adopted a policy of nonalignment during the Cold War.
Her stance of nonalignment allowed her to mediate between the conflicting parties.
The movement for nonalignment gained momentum in the mid-20th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neutrality - the state of not supporting or helping either side in a conflict
  • Antonym: alignment - the state of being aligned with a particular group or side

Etymology

From prefix 'non-' (not) + 'alignment' (arrangement in a line or in proper position).
