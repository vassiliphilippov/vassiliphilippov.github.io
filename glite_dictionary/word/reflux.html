reflux

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːˌflʌks / (AmE), / ˈriːˌflʌks / (BrE)
an abnormal backward flow of body fluids, especially of stomach contents into the esophagus
abnormal backward flow of fluids in the body
medical

Examples

Many people suffer from acid reflux after eating spicy foods.
The doctor explained that reflux can cause a burning sensation in the chest.
Proper medication can help control the symptoms of reflux.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regurgitation - the backward flowing of food or fluid, especially from the stomach to the mouth

Etymology

From Latin 'refluere' meaning to flow back.

2. NOUN

/ ˈriːˌflʌks / (AmE), / ˈriːˌflʌks / (BrE)
the movement of the tide as it flows away from the shore; the ebbing of the tide.
outward flow of the tide

Examples

The fishermen waited until the reflux before setting out to sea.
At reflux, the exposed sandbars became visible along the coast.
The reflux of the tide revealed hidden shells scattered across the beach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ebb - the movement of the tide out to sea

Etymology

From Latin refluxus (a flowing back), from re- (back) + fluere (to flow).
