Fouquieria splendens

1. NOUN

/ fuˈkwiːriə splɛnˈdɛnz / (AmE), / fuːˈkwɪərɪə splɛnˈdɛnz / (BrE)
A desert shrub native to the southwestern United States and Mexico, with slender spiny stems that bear leaves and red flowers after rain; commonly called ocotillo.
desert shrub of SW US and Mexico (ocotillo)

Examples

The Fouquieria splendens thrives in arid regions where few other plants can survive.
Clusters of bright red flowers make the Fouquieria splendens stand out against the desert landscape in spring.
Botanists often study Fouquieria splendens to understand how plants adapt to harsh environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ocotillo - common name for Fouquieria splendens, a desert shrub

Etymology

From Latinized genus name honoring French physician Pierre Fouquier and species Latin 'splendens' (shining, bright).
