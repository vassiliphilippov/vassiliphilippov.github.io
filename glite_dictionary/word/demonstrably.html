demonstrably

1. ADV

/ dɪˈmɑnstrəbli / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒnstrəbli / (BrE)
In a way that can be clearly shown to be true or proved; obviously or provably.
in a clearly provable way

Examples

The claims made by the scientist were demonstrably true after the experiment was repeated.
His argument was demonstrably flawed, as the data did not support his conclusions.
It was demonstrably false to say that no one objected to the plan.

Synset Examples

his documentary sources are demonstrably wrong

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indisputably - in a way that cannot be challenged or denied
  • Antonym: questionably - in a way that is open to doubt or challenge

Etymology

From demonstrable + -ly; ultimately from Latin demonstrare (to show).
