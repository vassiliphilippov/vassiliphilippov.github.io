Mayday

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈmeɪˌdeɪ / (BrE)
an internationally recognized radio signal for distress, used especially by ships and aircraft to request help urgently
distress signal used over radio

Examples

The pilot issued a Mayday after experiencing engine failure over the ocean.
Rescue teams responded immediately when they received the Mayday call from the sinking vessel.
If you ever have a true emergency at sea, you should broadcast a Mayday on the radio.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: SOS - an internationally recognized Morse code distress signal

Etymology

From French 'm'aidez' meaning 'help me', used as a radio distress call.
