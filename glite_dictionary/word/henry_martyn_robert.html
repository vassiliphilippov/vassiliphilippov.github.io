Henry Martyn Robert

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɛnri ˈmɑːrtɪn ˈrɑːbərt / (AmE), / ˈhɛnri ˈmɑːtɪn ˈrɒbət / (BrE)
(1837–1923) United States army officer and engineer, best known as the author of Robert's Rules of Order, a widely used manual of parliamentary procedure.
U.S. author of Robert's Rules of Order

Examples

Henry Martyn Robert is remembered for standardizing meeting procedures through his famous book.
Many organizations follow the guidelines established by Henry Martyn Robert to conduct effective meetings.
The influence of Henry Martyn Robert is still evident in parliamentary practice today.
