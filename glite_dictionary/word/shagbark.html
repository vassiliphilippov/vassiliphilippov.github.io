shagbark

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃæɡˌbɑrk / (AmE), / ˈʃæɡˌbɑːk / (BrE)
A North American hickory tree with loose, shaggy gray bark and edible nuts, especially the shagbark hickory.
hickory tree with loose bark and edible nuts

Examples

The shagbark can be easily recognized by its peeling, shaggy bark.
We gathered nuts from the shagbark during our walk in the woods.
A mature shagbark provides food and shelter for many forest animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shagbark hickory - the specific North American hickory tree with shaggy bark and edible nuts
  • Degree Lower: shellbark - another hickory species with similar qualities

Etymology

From 'shag' meaning 'long, rough hair' and 'bark', describing the tree's distinctive appearance.
