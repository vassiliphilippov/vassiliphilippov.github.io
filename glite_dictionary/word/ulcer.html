ulcer

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌl.sɚ / (AmE), / ˈʌl.sə / (BrE)
a sore on the skin or a mucous membrane, often with tissue breakdown that may lead to the formation of pus
painful sore with tissue breakdown
medical

Examples

The patient developed a painful ulcer on his lower leg that required medical treatment.
Stomach ulcers can cause severe abdominal pain and discomfort.
If left untreated, a small cut can sometimes turn into an ulcer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sore - an area of the body affected by pain, infection, or injury
  • Synonym: wound - an injury to living tissue, especially one involving a break in the skin
  • Synonym: abscess - a swollen area in body tissue containing pus

Etymology

From Latin 'ulcus' (sore, ulcer).
