central

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛntrəl / (AmE), / ˈsɛntrəl / (BrE)
a building or facility where telephone lines are connected to allow communication between telephones; a telephone exchange.
telephone exchange facility

Examples

The technician was sent to repair a fault at the central after reports of widespread outages.
In the early days of telephony, every town had its own central.
Calls from rural areas are routed through the main central in the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: telephone exchange - a facility where telephone calls are routed and connected

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɛntrəl / (AmE), / ˈsɛntrəl / (BrE)
Located in or near the center; forming or constituting a center.
in or near the center

Examples

The park is in a central location, making it easy for everyone to reach.
Her office occupies a central position in the building.
He wanted to live in a central area close to shops and restaurants.

Synset Examples

a central position

Related Terms

  • Antonym: peripheral - on or near the outer edge of something
  • Synonym: middle - the part equidistant from the ends or sides

Etymology

From Latin centralis (of or pertaining to a center), from centrum (center).

/ ˈsɛntrəl / (AmE), / ˈsɛntrəl / (BrE)
of the greatest importance; essential or fundamental.
most important or essential

Examples
