stiffener

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪfənər / (AmE), / ˈstɪfənə / (BrE)
a material or device added to provide rigidity, support, or structure to something
something that adds rigidity or support

Examples

The engineers installed a steel stiffener to reinforce the bridge beam.
His shirt had a plastic stiffener sewn into the collar to keep it sharp.
Carpenters often use plywood as a stiffener in door construction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - something that bears weight or provides reinforcement

Etymology

From 'stiffen' (to make rigid) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɪfənɚ / (AmE), / ˈstɪfənə / (BrE)
an additional structural element attached to a beam or plate to prevent it from buckling
structural element to prevent buckling

Examples

The engineer recommended installing a stiffener to support the weakened beam.
Steel stiffeners were welded to the web of the bridge girder.
Without a proper stiffener, the panel could buckle under heavy loads.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reinforcement - something added to strengthen or support a structure

Etymology

From 'stiffen' + '-er', meaning something that stiffens or strengthens.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-92460091-n
Synset POS: n
