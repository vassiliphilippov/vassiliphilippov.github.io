to actuate

1. VERB

/ ˈæktʃueɪt / (AmE), / ˈæktʃueɪt / (BrE)
to make a machine or device start working or to cause someone or something to take action
cause to take action; put into operation

Examples

The engineer pressed the button to actuate the emergency shutdown system.
Modern cars often use sensors to actuate the airbags during a collision.
Strong beliefs can actuate people to pursue ambitious goals.

Synset Examples

trigger a reaction
actuate the circuits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to activate - to make something active or operative
  • Antonym: to deactivate - to make something inactive or stop operating

Etymology

From Medieval Latin actuatus, past participle of actuare (to put in action), from Latin actus (act).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01647199-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈæk.tʃu.eɪt / (AmE), / ˈæk.tʃu.eɪt / (BrE)
to make someone behave in a particular way or to cause something to happen; to motivate or incite into action
motivate to act; put into action

Examples

His desire to prove himself actuated every decision he made.
The threat of losing her job actuated her to work harder than ever.
A sense of duty actuates many people to volunteer in their communities.

Synset Examples

This moved me to sacrifice my career

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to motivate - to provide someone with a reason to do something
  • Synonym: to incite - to encourage or stir up to action, especially something violent or unlawful

Etymology

From Medieval Latin actuatus, past participle of actuare, from Latin actus (act).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01653541-v
Synset POS: v
