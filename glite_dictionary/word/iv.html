IV

1. NOUN

/ aɪ ˈvi / (AmE), / aɪ ˈviː / (BrE)
a procedure in which fluids or nutrients are administered directly into a vein, often through a drip; short for intravenous injection or therapy
intravenous administration of fluids or nutrients
medical

Examples

The patient had to receive an IV to stay hydrated during surgery.
Nurses checked the IV bag to ensure the medication was being delivered correctly.
She felt dizzy until the doctor started her on an IV drip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intravenous - existing or taking place within, or administered into, a vein

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'intravenous', from Latin 'intra-' (within) + 'vena' (vein).

2. NUM

/ aɪ viː / (AmE), / aɪ viː / (BrE)
The Roman numeral for the number four.
Roman numeral for four

Examples

The clock tower displays the number IV at the four o'clock position.
She numbered the chapters using Roman numerals: I, II, III, IV, and so on.
The monarch was known as Henry IV throughout his reign.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: four - The number 4, written in Arabic numerals

Etymology

From Latin numerals: 'IV' represents 4, subtracting 1 (I) from 5 (V).
