entresol

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːntrəˌsɔl / (AmE), / ˈɒntrəsɒl / (BrE)
an intermediate floor between the ground floor and the first floor of a building, often projecting as a mezzanine
intermediate floor above ground level

Examples

The restaurant occupies the entresol above the lobby, offering a pleasant view of the street below.
They converted the entresol into a small office to maximize the space in their apartment.
A narrow staircase leads to the entresol, which is used as a reading area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mezzanine - a low storey between two others in a building, typically above the ground floor

Etymology

From French 'entresol', from 'entre' (between) + 'sol' (floor).
