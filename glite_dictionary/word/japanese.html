Japanese

1. ADJ

/ ˌdʒæpəˈniz / (AmE), / ˌdʒæpəˈniːz / (BrE)
of or relating to Japan, its people, culture, or language.
relating to Japan or its people

Examples

The Japanese tradition of tea ceremonies fascinates many visitors.
She drives a Japanese car that is known for its reliability.
The Japanese language uses three different writing systems.

Synset Examples

the Japanese Emperor
Japanese cars

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Japan - an island country in East Asia

Etymology

From Japan + -ese, based on Portuguese 'japonês' or Dutch 'Japansch'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02976588-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˌdʒæpəˈniz / (AmE), / ˌdʒapəˈniːz / (BrE)
the language spoken in Japan, used by the Japanese people
language spoken in Japan

Examples

She studied Japanese for three years before visiting Tokyo.
Many signs in the city are written in both English and Japanese.
He is fluent in Japanese and often works as a translator.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: English - the language spoken in England and many other countries
  • Antonym: Chinese - the language spoken in China

Etymology

From Japan + -ese, a suffix from Latin -ēsis, used for languages and peoples.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06942056-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˌdʒæpəˈniz / (AmE), / ˌdʒapəˈniːz / (BrE)
a person who is a native or inhabitant of Japan
a native or inhabitant of Japan

Examples

Many Japanese live in Tokyo, the country's largest city.
As a Japanese, she celebrates traditional festivals every year.
The Japanese have a rich cultural heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Japanese - the language spoken in Japan

Etymology

From Japan + -ese, modeled after other demonyms; ultimately from Portuguese 'Japão'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09737613-n
Synset POS: n
