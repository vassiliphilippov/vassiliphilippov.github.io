to overcome

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvɚˈkʌm / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈkʌm / (BrE)
(of a person) to be strongly affected or overwhelmed by emotions or sensations.
be overwhelmed by emotions or sensations

Examples

She was overcome with emotion as she accepted the award.
I was completely overcome by the beauty of the sunset.
He overcame with grief when he heard the sad news.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overwhelm - to overpower emotionally or physically

Etymology

From Old English ofercuman, from ofer (over) + cuman (come).

2. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈkʌm / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈkʌm / (BrE)
to successfully deal with or control a problem, feeling, or difficulty
deal with or control successfully

Examples

After months of hard work, she finally overcame her fear of public speaking.
He struggled with injury during the season but managed to overcome it for the final match.
The community worked together to overcome the challenges caused by the storm.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

3. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈkʌm / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈkʌm / (BrE)
to be affected so strongly by something, especially an emotion or a force, that you cannot resist or fight against it; to be defeated or overwhelmed, often through no direct fault or weakness of one’s own.
to be defeated or overwhelmed by something

Examples

She was overcome by grief after hearing the sad news.
Even the strongest athletes can be overcome by exhaustion in extreme conditions.
He tried to stay calm, but was overcome with emotion during the ceremony.

Synset Examples

Heart disease can get the best of us

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to succumb - to fail to resist pressure or temptation
  • Antonym: to resist - to withstand the action or effect of something

Etymology

Old English ofercuman, from ofer (over) + cuman (come).
