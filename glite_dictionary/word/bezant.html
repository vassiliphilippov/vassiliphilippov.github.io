bezant

1. NOUN

/ ˈbiː.zənt / (AmE), / ˈbiː.zənt / (BrE)
a gold coin of the Byzantine Empire, widely used in Europe during the Middle Ages
Byzantine gold coin used in the Middle Ages

Examples

The merchant paid for the spices with a single bezant.
Medieval hoards often included the rare bezant among other coins.
A bezant was highly prized for its purity and weight in gold.

Etymology

From Old French *besant*, from Latin *Byzantius* (of Byzantium).
