Eryngium yuccifolium

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈrɪndʒiəm ˌjʌkˌsaɪˈfoʊliəm / (AmE), / ɪˈrɪndʒiəm ˌjʌkˌsɪˈfəʊliəm / (BrE)
a coarse, prickly perennial herb native to the United States, also known as rattlesnake master, historically thought to cure rattlesnake bites
prickly perennial herb native to the U.S.

Examples

The tall spikes of Eryngium yuccifolium attract butterflies to prairie gardens.
Native Americans once used Eryngium yuccifolium as a traditional remedy for snake bites.
Eryngium yuccifolium is often called rattlesnake master due to its folk medicinal uses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rattlesnake master - common name for Eryngium yuccifolium

Etymology

From New Latin, combining genus Eryngium (from Greek ērungion, a kind of thistle) with Latin yuccifolium ('yucca-leaved').
