put-down

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʊt ˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈpʊt daʊn / (BrE)
a remark made to belittle or humiliate someone; an insulting or disparaging comment
insulting or belittling remark
informal

Examples

James was hurt by her sarcastic put-down during the meeting.
The comedian is well known for his clever put-downs of hecklers in the audience.
She responded to the criticism with a sharp put-down of her own.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insult - a disrespectful or scornful remark or action
  • Antonym: compliment - a polite expression of praise or admiration
