Decumaria barbata

1. PROPN

/ ˌdɛk.juˈmɛr.i.ə bɑrˈbeɪ.tə / (AmE), / ˌdɛk.juˈmɑː.ri.ə bɑːˈbɑː.tə / (BrE)
A woody climbing plant native to the southeastern United States, with white flowers arranged in compound terminal clusters.
woody climbing plant with white clustered flowers

Examples

The Decumaria barbata is often found climbing trees along riverbanks in the southeastern United States.
Botanists studied the blooming patterns of Decumaria barbata in springtime.
Gardeners appreciate Decumaria barbata for its attractive clusters of white flowers.

Etymology

From Latin 'decumarius', relating to the plant genus Decumaria, and 'barbata' meaning 'bearded'.
