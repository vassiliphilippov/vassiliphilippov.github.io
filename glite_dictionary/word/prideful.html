prideful

1. ADJ

/ ˈpraɪd.fəl / (AmE), / ˈpraɪd.fəl / (BrE)
having or showing an arrogant sense of superiority or disdain towards others; excessively proud
arrogantly superior; disdainful

Examples

Despite criticism, he remained prideful and refused to admit he was wrong.
Her prideful attitude made it difficult for her to accept help from anyone.
The prideful glance he gave the others revealed he considered himself superior.

Synset Examples

some economists are disdainful of their colleagues in other social disciplines
haughty aristocrats
his lordly manners were offensive
walked with a prideful swagger
very sniffy about breaches of etiquette
his mother eyed my clothes with a supercilious air
a more swaggering mood than usual

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrogant - having an exaggerated sense of one's own importance or abilities
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low view of one's importance

Etymology

Formed from 'pride' + suffix '-ful', indicating 'full of pride'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈpraɪd.fəl / (AmE), / ˈpraɪd.fəl / (BrE)
feeling or showing strong pride, especially as a result of achievement or success; having an attitude of self-respect and satisfaction.
having strong pride, especially from success

Examples

The prideful winner raised his trophy high before an applauding crowd.
She gave her parents a prideful smile after receiving the award.
Despite her prideful attitude, she remained grateful for her team's support.

Synset Examples

rejoicing crowds filled the streets on VJ Day
a triumphal success
a triumphant shout

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proud - feeling deep pleasure or satisfaction as a result of one's own achievements
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance

Etymology

From 'pride' + '-ful', meaning 'full of pride'.
