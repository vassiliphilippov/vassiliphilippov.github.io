to nazify

1. VERB

/ ˈnɑːzɪfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈnɑːzɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to cause or force a person, institution, or country to adopt Nazi beliefs, policies, or characteristics
make Nazi in character or policy

Examples

During the 1930s, the regime attempted to nazify every aspect of German society.
Some historians examine how the curriculum was nazified in schools under the new government.
The party leaders sought to nazify local institutions to ensure ideological conformity.

Synset Examples

Hitler nazified Germany in the 1930's
The arts were nazified everywhere in Germany

Etymology

Formed from 'Nazi' + '-fy' (to make or become).
