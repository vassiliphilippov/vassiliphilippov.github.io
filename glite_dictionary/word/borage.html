borage

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈbɒrɪdʒ / (BrE)
an annual herb with hairy leaves and blue star-shaped flowers, whose leaves are used to flavor sauces, punches, and salads
an herb with edible leaves and blue flowers

Examples

Fresh borage leaves add a subtle cucumber flavor to summer salads.
Gardeners often grow borage to attract bees with its bright blue flowers.
The chef used borage to garnish the herbal punch at the party.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin borago, of uncertain origin, possibly from Arabic.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɔrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈbɒrɪdʒ / (BrE)
a hairy annual herb with blue flowers, native to Europe, often used in herbal medicine and as a salad green or cooked vegetable
blue-flowered European herb used in salads and medicine

Examples

She sprinkled fresh borage leaves over the summer salad for a mild cucumber flavor.
The blue blossoms of borage attract bees to the garden all season long.
Some herbal teas include borage because of its traditional medicinal uses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herb - a plant valued for flavor, scent, or medicinal properties

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'borago', possibly from Arabic 'abu arak' (father of sweat), referring to its medicinal properties.
