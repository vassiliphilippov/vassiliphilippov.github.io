loanblend

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊnˌblɛnd / (AmE), / ˈləʊnˌblɛnd / (BrE)
A word formed by combining elements from different languages.
word made from parts of different languages

Examples

The word ‘television’ is a classic loanblend, using Greek and Latin roots.
Many scientific terms in English are loanblends derived from multiple languages.
Linguists study loanblend formation to trace the influence of language contact.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hybrid word - a word formed from elements from different languages

Etymology

From English 'loan' (borrowed word) + 'blend' (mixture).
