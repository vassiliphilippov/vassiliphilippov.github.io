ratables

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪtəˌbəlz / (AmE), / ˈreɪtəblz / (BrE)
Properties within a jurisdiction that are subject to local taxation and provide tax revenue for local governments.
properties subject to local taxation
legal

Examples

The town increased its ratables by encouraging new commercial development.
A shrinking number of ratables puts more pressure on residential taxpayers.
The city relies on its ratables to fund public services such as schools and police.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taxable property - property that is subject to tax

Etymology

From 'rate', meaning a tax or assessment, plus the suffix '-able', meaning 'that may be' or 'subject to'.
