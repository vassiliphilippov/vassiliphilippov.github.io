Heuchera

1. NOUN

/ ˈhjuːkərə / (AmE), / ˈhjuːkərə / (BrE)
A genus of North American perennial herbs, known for their rounded or heart-shaped leaves and small clustered flowers, often called coral bells.
genus of North American perennial herbs

Examples

Many gardeners add Heuchera to their shade beds for its colorful foliage.
The Heuchera in my garden has produced delicate pink flowers this spring.
Botanists have classified several species of Heuchera native to North America.

Etymology

Named after Johann Heinrich von Heucher (1677–1747), a German botanist.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhjuːkərə / (AmE), / ˈhjuːkərə / (BrE)
A genus of North American perennial herbs, known for their rounded or heart-shaped basal leaves and clusters of small flowers, commonly grown as ornamental plants.
genus of ornamental North American herbs

Examples

The gardener planted several varieties of Heuchera to add color to the flowerbed.
Many species within the Heuchera genus are valued for their distinctive foliage.
Heuchera prefers partially shaded areas and well-drained soil.

Etymology

Named after Johann Heinrich von Heucher, an 18th-century German botanist.
