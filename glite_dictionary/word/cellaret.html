cellaret

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛləˈrɛt / (AmE), / ˌsɛləˈrɛt / (BrE)
a small cabinet or sideboard with compartments for storing bottles, especially of wine or liquor
cabinet for storing bottles

Examples

The antique cellaret in the dining room was filled with fine wines.
She opened the cellaret to select a bottle for her guests.
Many Victorian homes featured a decorative cellaret near the dining table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sideboard - a piece of dining-room furniture with cupboards and drawers

Etymology

Diminutive of 'cellar'. From French 'cellarette', from 'cellier' (cellar).
