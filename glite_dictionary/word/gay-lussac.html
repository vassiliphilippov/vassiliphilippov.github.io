Gay-Lussac

1. PROPN

/ ɡeɪ luːˈsæk / (AmE), / ɡeɪ lʊˈsæk / (BrE)
(1778–1850) A French chemist and physicist noted for isolating boron and formulating Gay-Lussac's law about the behavior of gases under constant pressure.
French chemist and physicist (1778–1850)

Examples

Gay-Lussac conducted pioneering experiments on gases in the early 19th century.
The law of combining gas volumes is often attributed to Gay-Lussac.
Boron was first isolated by Gay-Lussac and his colleague Louis Jacques Thénard.

Etymology

From the surname of Joseph Louis Gay-Lussac, French scientist.
