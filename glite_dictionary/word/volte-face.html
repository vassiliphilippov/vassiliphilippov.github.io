volte-face

1. NOUN

/ voʊlt ˈfæs / (AmE), / vɒlt ˈfas / (BrE)
a complete and sudden change in opinion, attitude, or policy
complete change of opinion or policy

Examples

The company performed a remarkable volte-face on its decision after public outcry.
Her sudden volte-face surprised everyone in the meeting.
His volte-face regarding environmental policies confused his supporters.

Synset Examples

an about-face on foreign policy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: about-face - a sudden change in direction or opinion
  • Synonym: U-turn - a complete reversal of policy or opinion

Etymology

From French 'volte-face' (turn-face), from Italian 'voltafaccia'.
