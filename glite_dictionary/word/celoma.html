celoma

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈloʊmə / (AmE), / sɪˈləʊmə / (BrE)
A cavity within the mesoderm of an embryo, which develops into the pleural, pericardial, and peritoneal cavities in humans.
embryonic body cavity
medical

Examples

During human embryonic development, the celoma divides to form various body cavities.
The mesoderm splits to create the celoma during early development.
Scientists observed abnormalities in the formation of the celoma in experimental studies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coelom - main body cavity in most animals

Etymology

From Greek koilōma (cavity, hollow).
