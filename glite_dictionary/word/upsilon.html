upsilon

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌp.sɪˌlɒn / (AmE), / ˈʌp.sɪ.lɒn / (BrE)
the 20th letter of the Greek alphabet, represented in uppercase as Υ and in lowercase as υ.
20th letter of the Greek alphabet

Examples

The lowercase upsilon is often used in mathematics to represent certain variables.
Greek words like 'hysteria' begin with the letter upsilon in their original form.
Scholars studied how the pronunciation of upsilon changed during ancient times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gamma - third letter of the Greek alphabet
  • Synonym: beta - second letter of the Greek alphabet

Etymology

From Greek 'ýpsilon', meaning 'simple u' (u + psilon, 'simple') to distinguish from 'oi' (upsilon-iota in diphthongs).
