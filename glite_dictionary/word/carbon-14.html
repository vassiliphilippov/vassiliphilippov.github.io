carbon-14

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑrbən fɔrˈtin / (AmE), / ˌkɑːbən fɔːˈtiːn / (BrE)
a radioactive isotope of carbon, used especially in radiocarbon dating
radioactive carbon isotope

Examples

Carbon-14 dating has revolutionized archaeology by providing accurate estimates of artifact ages.
Researchers measured the amount of carbon-14 remaining in the fossil to determine its age.
The half-life of carbon-14 makes it ideal for dating once-living materials up to 50,000 years old.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radiocarbon - radioactive carbon, especially carbon-14

Etymology

The number '14' refers to its atomic mass (6 protons and 8 neutrons).
