breakout

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪkˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈbreɪkaʊt / (BrE)
an escape from jail, especially one involving force or a sudden incident
escape from jail

Examples

The prisoners staged a daring breakout and evaded the guards overnight.
Authorities foiled a planned breakout before it could begin.
After the breakout, police launched a massive manhunt in the surrounding area.

Synset Examples

the breakout was carefully planned

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escape - the act of breaking free from confinement or control

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'break out', meaning to escape or emerge suddenly.
