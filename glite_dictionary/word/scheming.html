scheming

1. ADJ

/ ˈskiːmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskiːmɪŋ / (BrE)
given to making secret or underhanded plans; acting in a devious or calculating way to achieve a particular goal
making secret, devious plans

Examples

Her scheming nature made her difficult to trust in the office.
The scheming villain devised a plan to steal the treasure.
Even among friends, his scheming behavior set him apart.

Synset Examples

the most calculating and selfish men in the community

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calculating - acting in a shrewd or scheming way
  • Antonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and sincere

Etymology

From the verb 'scheme' + -ing, used as an adjective from the 18th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˈskiːmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskiːmɪŋ / (BrE)
using secret or cunning plans to achieve one's own advantage, often in a dishonest or deceptive way
making secret or crafty plans

Examples

Her scheming nature made it hard for others to trust her intentions.
The film's scheming villain kept the heroes guessing at every turn.
Rumors spread quickly about a scheming colleague trying to undermine his manager.

Synset Examples

a selfish and designing nation obsessed with the dark schemes of European intrigue
a scheming wife
a scheming gold digger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devious - using clever but often dishonest tactics to achieve goals
  • Antonym: honest - free from deceit; truthful

Etymology

From the verb 'scheme' + -ing, meaning engaging in scheming behavior.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00150120-s
Synset POS: s
