Hyperotreta

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpəˈtroʊtə / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəˈtrɛtə / (BrE)
A group of jawless fish comprising the hagfishes, distinguished from the lampreys.
group of jawless fish (hagfishes)

Examples

The Hyperotreta are noted for having simple eyes and producing copious slime.
Unlike lampreys, Hyperotreta lack true vertebrae in their structure.
Recent studies have explored the evolutionary history of Hyperotreta and their unique survival adaptations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lamprey - a jawless, eel-like parasitic fish distinguished from hagfish

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'hyper' (over) + 'otreon' (hole), referring to anatomical features.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpəˈtroʊtə / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəˈtriːtə / (BrE)
A taxonomic suborder of jawless fish comprising hagfishes, distinguished from lampreys.
suborder of hagfishes

Examples

Recent research has provided new insights into the anatomy of Hyperotreta.
Unlike lampreys, members of the Hyperotreta possess unique slime-producing glands.
The suborder Hyperotreta includes some of the most primitive living vertebrates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Myxinoidea - superclass or order including hagfishes
  • Antonym: Petromyzontida - taxonomic group containing lampreys, distinguished from Hyperotreta

Etymology

From modern Latin Hyperotreta: 'hyper-' (above) + Greek 'trētos' (perforated).
