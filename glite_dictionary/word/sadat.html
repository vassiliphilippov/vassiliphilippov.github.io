Sadat

1. PROPN

/ səˈdɑt / (AmE), / səˈdɑːt / (BrE)
Anwar Sadat (1918–1981), Egyptian president who negotiated a peace treaty with Israel and was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize.
Egyptian president (1918–1981)

Examples

Sadat was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 1978 for his efforts to achieve peace in the Middle East.
The assassination of Sadat in 1981 was a turning point in Egyptian history.
Sadat became president of Egypt following the death of Gamal Abdel Nasser.
