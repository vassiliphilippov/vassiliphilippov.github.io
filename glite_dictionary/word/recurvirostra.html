Recurvirostra

1. NOUN

/ rɪˌkɝːvəˈrɑːstrə / (AmE), / rɪˌkɜːvɪˈrɒstrə / (BrE)
A genus of wading birds known as avocets, distinguished by their long, upturned bills and long legs.
genus of birds; avocets

Examples

The genus Recurvirostra includes several species of avocets found around the world.
Scientists observed members of Recurvirostra feeding in the shallow wetlands.
The distinctive upward-curved bill is a characteristic feature of all Recurvirostra species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: avocet - a long-legged wading bird with an upturned bill

Etymology

From Latin recurvus (bent backward) + rostrum (beak).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˌkɜrvɪˈrɒstrə / (AmE), / rɪˌkɜːvɪˈrɒstrə / (BrE)
A genus of wading birds in the family Recurvirostridae, comprising the avocets.
genus of wading birds (avocets)

Examples

The Pied Avocet belongs to the genus Recurvirostra, known for its distinctive upturned bill.
There are several species within Recurvirostra, most of which inhabit shallow wetlands.
Ornithologists observed that all members of Recurvirostra share unique feeding behaviors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: avocet - A long-legged wading bird of the genus Recurvirostra, with an upturned bill.

Etymology

From Latin 'recurvus' (bent backward) and 'rostrum' (beak), referring to the upturned bill of avocets.
