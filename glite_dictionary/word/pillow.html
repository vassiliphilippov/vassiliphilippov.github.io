pillow

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˈpɪləʊ / (BrE)
A soft cushion used to support the head, especially while sleeping.
soft cushion for the head (sleeping)

Examples

She placed her tired head on the soft pillow and quickly fell asleep.
He hugged his pillow tightly during the thunderstorm.
Feathers often spill out if a pillow's seam comes undone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cushion - a soft bag of material used to make a seat or other object more comfortable

Etymology

From Middle English 'pilwe', from Old English 'pyle', from Latin 'pulvinus' (cushion).

2. VERB

/ ˈpɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˈpɪləʊ / (BrE)
To rest (something), especially the head, on or as if on a pillow for comfort or support.
rest (the head) on a pillow or as if on a pillow

Examples

She pillowed her head on his shoulder and closed her eyes.
He pillowed his arm beneath his cheek and drifted off to sleep.
The cat pillowed itself on a soft cushion in the sun.

Synset Examples

pillow your head

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rest - to lean or place on a surface for support

/ ˈpɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˈpɪləʊ / (BrE)
to rest (especially the head) on or as if on a pillow
rest (the head) as on a pillow

Examples

She pillowed her head on his shoulder, feeling comforted by his presence.
He pillowed his cheek on his folded arms and drifted off to sleep.
The child pillowed herself against the soft cushions and closed her eyes.
