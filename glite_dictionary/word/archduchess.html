archduchess

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑrtʃˈdʌtʃəs / (AmE), / ˌɑːtʃˈdʌtʃəs / (BrE)
a female member of the Austrian imperial family, either by birth or as the wife or widow of an archduke
female Austrian royal title

Examples

The young archduchess attended the coronation ceremony with her family.
After her husband's death, she was known as the Dowager archduchess of Austria.
Historical records detail the lives of every archduchess in the Habsburg dynasty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archduke - a male member of the Austrian imperial family

Etymology

Formed from 'archduke' + the feminine suffix '-ess', based on the Latin 'archi-' (chief) and 'dux' (leader).
