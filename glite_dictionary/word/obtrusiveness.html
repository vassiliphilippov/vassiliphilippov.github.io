obtrusiveness

1. NOUN

/ əbˈtruːsɪvnəs / (AmE), / əbˈtruːsɪvnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being noticeable or prominent in an unwelcome or intrusive way
being conspicuously unwelcome or intrusive

Examples

The obtrusiveness of the advertisement disrupted the natural beauty of the park.
She disliked the obtrusiveness of her colleague's questions at the meeting.
Modern architecture can sometimes be criticized for its obtrusiveness in historic neighborhoods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intrusiveness - the quality of being intrusive or unwelcome
  • Antonym: subtlety - the quality of being subtle or not obvious

Etymology

From Latin 'obtrudere' (to thrust upon), via the verb 'obtrude' and the adjective 'obtrusive'.
