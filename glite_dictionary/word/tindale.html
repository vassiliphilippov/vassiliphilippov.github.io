Tindale

1. PROPN

/ ˈtɪn.deɪl / (AmE), / ˈtɪn.deɪl / (BrE)
William Tindale (c.1494–1536), English scholar and translator who produced the first English translation of the Bible from Hebrew and Greek sources; executed for heresy in 1536.
English Bible translator and martyr (1494–1536)
american british

Examples

Many historians regard Tindale's translation as a major turning point in English religious history.
The church authorities strongly opposed Tindale for translating the Bible into English.
Some of the phrases coined by Tindale are still used in modern English Bibles.
