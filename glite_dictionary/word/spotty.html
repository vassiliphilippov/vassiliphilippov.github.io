spotty

1. ADJ

/ ˈspɑti / (AmE), / ˈspɒti / (BrE)
marked with spots or patches of contrasting color or texture; having spots.
having spots or patches

Examples

The puppy had a spotty coat with white and brown patches.
She bought a spotty dress decorated with bright red dots.
The artist painted a spotty pattern on the wall to create a playful effect.

Synset Examples

a field patched with ice and snow
the wall had a spotty speckled effect
a black-and-white spotted cow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spotted - covered with or having spots

Etymology

From spot + -y, meaning 'having spots'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈspɑti / (AmE), / ˈspɒti / (BrE)
lacking consistency or regularity; uneven in quality or performance.
inconsistent; uneven quality

Examples

His attendance at work has been rather spotty this year, causing concern among his managers.
Internet service in rural areas can be spotty, making remote work challenging.
The review praised the restaurant's food but described the service as spotty throughout the evening.

Synset Examples

the golfer hit the ball well but his putting was spotty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inconsistent - not staying the same throughout; lacking regularity
  • Synonym: patchy - existing or happening in some areas but not others; uneven
  • Antonym: reliable - consistently good in performance or quality
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00581013-s
Synset POS: s
