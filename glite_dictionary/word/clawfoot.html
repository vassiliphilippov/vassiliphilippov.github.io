clawfoot

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɔˌfʊt / (AmE), / ˈklɔːfʊt / (BrE)
A deformity of the foot characterized by an abnormally high arch and hyperextension of the toes, giving the foot a claw-like appearance.
foot deformity with high arch and curled toes
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with clawfoot after complaining of chronic pain in her arches and toes.
Surgery may be recommended for severe cases of clawfoot that do not respond to physical therapy.
Children with neurological disorders are sometimes at increased risk of developing clawfoot as they grow.
