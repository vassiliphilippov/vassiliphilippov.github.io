smolder

1. NOUN

/ ˈsmoʊldər / (AmE), / ˈsməʊldə / (BrE)
A fire that burns slowly with smoke but without flame.
a fire burning with smoke but no flame

Examples

The campfire was reduced to a smolder after hours of burning.
A thick smolder drifted from the ruins after the fire was put out.
They watched the faint smolder in the darkness, waiting for it to die.

Synset Examples

the smoulder suddenly became a blaze

Related Terms

  • Antonym: blaze - a strong, bright flame or fire

Etymology

From Middle English 'smolderen', probably a frequentative of 'smolden' (to smolder), of Germanic origin.

2. VERB

/ ˈsmoʊldər / (AmE), / ˈsməʊldə / (BrE)
to show or feel a strong emotion, especially anger or resentment, that is not expressed openly but is hardly contained
show suppressed strong feelings

Examples

She tried to hide her irritation, but it continued to smolder beneath the surface.
During the meeting, anger began to smolder in his eyes, though he said nothing.
Resentment smoldered within her after the unfair treatment she had received.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to seethe - to be filled with intense but unexpressed emotion
  • Synonym: fume - to feel or express anger in a restrained or suppressed manner
  • Antonym: to explode - to express emotion, especially anger, suddenly and openly

Etymology

From Middle English smolderen (to smolder), variant of smolderen (to smother), of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01776620-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈsmoʊldɚ / (AmE), / ˈsməʊldə / (BrE)
to burn slowly with smoke but without a flame
burn slowly and without a flame

Examples

The campfire continued to smolder long after the campers left.
Wet leaves often smolder for hours before finally extinguishing.
An old log in the fireplace was still smoldering this morning.

Synset Examples

a smoldering fire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burn - to be on fire
  • Degree Higher: to ignite - to set on fire; to start burning

Etymology

From Middle English smolderen, related to Middle Low German smūlen (to smolder).
