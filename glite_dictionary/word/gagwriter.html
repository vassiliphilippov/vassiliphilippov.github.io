gagwriter

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæɡˌraɪtər / (AmE), / ˈɡæɡˌraɪtə / (BrE)
a person who writes jokes or comic material for comedians or other public performers
writer of jokes for performers

Examples

He worked as a gagwriter for several famous television comedians in the 1950s.
The stand-up comic credited her success to her talented gagwriter.
Many late-night hosts rely on a skilled gagwriter to keep their routines fresh and funny.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gagman - a person who writes jokes or comic material, chiefly for performers

Etymology

From 'gag' (a joke or comic line) + 'writer'.
