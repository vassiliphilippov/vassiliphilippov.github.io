Townsendia

1. NOUN

/ taʊnˈsɛndiə / (AmE), / taʊnˈsɛndiə / (BrE)
a genus of low-growing tufted herbs native to western North America, commonly known as Easter daisy.
genus of western North American tufted herbs

Examples

The wild Townsendia bloomed early in the spring across the mountain meadows.
Botanists recorded several species of Townsendia during the field survey.
Many gardeners appreciate Townsendia for its delicate, daisy-like flowers.

Etymology

Named after David Townsend, American botanist (1787–1858).
