insufflation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.səˈfleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.sʌˈfleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of blowing or breathing air or gas into or upon something, especially as part of a medical procedure or a ritual
the act of blowing air into or onto something

Examples

The anesthesiologist performed insufflation of oxygen during the surgery to assist the patient's breathing.
Certain religious ceremonies include insufflation as a symbolic gesture of spiritual cleansing.
The technician used carbon dioxide insufflation to examine the patient's colon during the procedure.

Etymology

From Latin insufflare (to blow into), from in- (into) + sufflare (to blow).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪnsəˈfleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnsʌˈfleɪʃən / (BrE)
(medicine) The act of blowing air, gas, or medicated powder into a body cavity, especially the lungs, for medical purposes.
medical procedure of blowing air or powder into the body
medical

Examples

The anesthesiologist carefully performed insufflation to ensure the patient’s lungs were properly inflated during surgery.
Some nasal medications are administered by insufflation, delivering the powder directly into the nostrils.
Laparoscopic surgeries often require abdominal insufflation with carbon dioxide to provide adequate working space.

Etymology

From Latin insufflare (to blow into), from in- (into) + sufflare (to blow).
