acidic

1. ADJ

/ əˈsɪdɪk / (AmE), / əˈsɪdɪk / (BrE)
having the properties of an acid; containing acid or having a pH below 7.
having acid properties or a pH below 7

Examples

The lemon juice tasted extremely acidic on my tongue.
Poor soil can become acidic, making it hard for some plants to grow.
The scientist measured the solution and confirmed it was highly acidic.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: alkaline - having a pH greater than 7; opposite of acidic
  • Synonym: acid - a substance with acidic properties

Etymology

From acid + -ic, modeling after similar adjectives like 'basic.'

2. ADJ

/ əˈsɪdɪk / (AmE), / əˈsɪdɪk / (BrE)
having a sour or sharp taste, like that of acid; tasting sour
having a sour or sharp taste

Examples

The lemon juice was so acidic that it made my lips pucker.
This salad dressing has an acidic tang from the vinegar.
Some apples can taste quite acidic, especially before they are ripe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sour - having an acidic or sharp taste
  • Degree Lower: bitter - having a sharp, unpleasant taste, not sweet

Etymology

From acid + -ic, modeled after 'acidic' in scientific use.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02377903-s
Synset POS: s
