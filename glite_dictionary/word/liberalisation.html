liberalisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪbərəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌlɪbərəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making laws or rules less strict, especially in economic or political systems
making laws or rules less strict

Examples

The government announced the liberalisation of trade policies to encourage foreign investment.
Economic liberalisation has led to rapid growth in several sectors.
Many citizens welcomed the liberalisation of the press, which brought greater freedom of expression.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deregulation - the removal of regulations or restrictions, especially in a particular industry
  • Antonym: tightening - the act of making laws, rules, or restrictions stricter

Etymology

From 'liberalise' + '-ation'; 'liberalise' from Latin 'liberalis' (of freedom).
