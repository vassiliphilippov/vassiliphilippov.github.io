caffre

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæfɚ / (AmE), / ˈkæfə / (BrE)
An offensive and insulting term historically used to refer to Black Africans, especially in South Africa; now widely recognized as extremely offensive and unacceptable.
offensive term for Black Africans
offensive

Examples

The word caffre is now understood to be deeply offensive and should never be used.
He was shocked to hear someone utter the term caffre in conversation.
Historical texts sometimes contain the word caffre, reflecting past prejudices.

Etymology

From Arabic *kāfir* (infidel, nonbeliever), adopted into Afrikaans and colonial English.
