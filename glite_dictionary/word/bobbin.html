bobbin

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːbɪn / (AmE), / ˈbɒbɪn / (BrE)
a small cylindrical or spool-shaped object on which thread, wire, or film is wound, often used in sewing machines and other machinery
spool for winding thread or film

Examples

She replaced the empty bobbin before continuing to sew the fabric.
The movie projector jammed when the bobbin was overloaded with film.
Many old clocks use a bobbin to hold the fine wire for their chimes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spool - a cylindrical device on which thread or film is wound

Etymology

From French 'bobine' (small cylinder or spool).
