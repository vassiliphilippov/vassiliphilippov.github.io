maverick

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæv.ɚ.ɪk / (AmE), / ˈmæv.ər.ɪk / (BrE)
an unbranded animal, especially a stray calf, on the open range that is not marked as belonging to any owner
an unbranded stray animal, especially a calf
american

Examples

The ranchers searched for mavericks to add to their herds during the spring roundup.
A maverick was often claimed by the first cowboy who managed to brand it.
Many mavericks wandered across the open plains, unclaimed by any owner.

Etymology

Named after Samuel A. Maverick (1803–1870), a Texas rancher who did not brand his cattle.

2. ADJ

/ ˈmævərɪk / (AmE), / ˈmævərɪk / (BrE)
an independent person who has different ideas and behaviors from others, especially one who resists adhering to established rules or traditions
independent-minded person

Examples

His maverick approach to business often surprised his colleagues.
She has always been considered a maverick in the scientific community.
The company values maverick thinkers who challenge the status quo.

3. NOUN

/ ˈmævərɪk / (AmE), / ˈmævərɪk / (BrE)
a person who thinks and acts independently, often refusing to follow conventional rules or group opinions
independent-minded person

Examples

As a political maverick, she often challenged her party's traditional views.
He was considered a maverick in the scientific community for questioning established theories.
The startup hired a maverick who brought fresh ideas and broke the mold.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonconformist - a person who does not conform to prevailing ideas or practices

Etymology

From the name of Samuel A. Maverick (1803–1870), a Texas rancher who did not brand his cattle.
