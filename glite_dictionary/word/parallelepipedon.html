parallelepipedon

1. NOUN

/ ˌpærəˌlɛləˈpaɪpɪˌdɒn / (AmE), / ˌparəlɛlɪˈpʌɪpɪˌdɒn / (BrE)
A prism whose bases are parallelograms, resulting in a six-faced solid where each face is a parallelogram.
a prism with parallelogram bases

Examples

The crystal had the shape of a perfect parallelepipedon, with all faces forming parallelograms.
In geometry class, we learned how to calculate the volume of a parallelepipedon.
Renaissance artists sometimes used a parallelepipedon as a model for drawing objects in perspective.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parallelepiped - a solid figure with six parallelogram faces

Etymology

From Greek parallelepipedon, from 'parallēlos' (parallel) + 'epipedon' (plane surface).
