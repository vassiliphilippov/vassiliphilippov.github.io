to proclaim

1. VERB

/ proʊˈkleɪm / (AmE), / prəˈkleɪm / (BrE)
to state or announce something officially or publicly, especially in a formal or emphatic way
announce officially or publicly

Examples

The president will proclaim a national holiday next week.
She stood before the crowd to proclaim her innocence.
The government proclaimed a state of emergency during the crisis.

Synset Examples

‘I am not a Communist,’ he exclaimed
The King will proclaim an amnesty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to announce - to make a public or formal statement about something
  • Synonym: to declare - to state something in a clear and formal way
  • Antonym: to retract - to withdraw a statement or announcement

Etymology

From Latin proclamare (to cry out publicly), from pro- (forth) + clamare (to shout).

2. VERB

/ proʊˈkleɪm / (AmE), / prəˈkleɪm / (BrE)
to state something officially or publicly, often declaring a new status or title
formally announce or declare

Examples

The government will proclaim the results of the election tomorrow.
She was proclaimed the winner after an official count of the votes.
They proclaimed him emperor in a lavish public ceremony.

Synset Examples

He was proclaimed King

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to announce - to make something known publicly
  • Synonym: to declare - to formally state something
  • Antonym: revoke - to officially cancel or take back a title or status

Etymology

From Latin proclamare (to shout out, proclaim), from pro- (forth) + clamare (to cry out).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
