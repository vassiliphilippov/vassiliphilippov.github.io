Diospyros virginiana

1. NOUN

/ daɪˌɑːspɪˌrɑːs vɜrˌdʒɪˈneɪnə / (AmE), / daɪˌɒspɪˌrɒs vɜːdʒɪˈneɪnə / (BrE)
A medium-sized deciduous tree native to the southern and eastern United States, known for its yellow or orange astringent fruit (American persimmon) that becomes edible when fully ripe.
American persimmon tree species

Examples

The fruit of the Diospyros virginiana is sweet and edible when it fully ripens.
Several Diospyros virginiana trees grow along the edge of the old pasture.
Native Americans traditionally used the wood and fruit of Diospyros virginiana for various purposes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American persimmon - Common name for Diospyros virginiana, the species and its fruit

Etymology

From Greek *diospyros* ('divine fruit'), and Latinized form of Virginia, the region where the species is common.
