Mahound

1. PROPN

/ məˈhaʊnd / (AmE), / məˈhaʊnd / (BrE)
(obsolete, often offensive) A historical and literary term for Muhammad, the prophet and founder of Islam, especially as used in Medieval Christian contexts.
historical/literary name for Muhammad
archaic offensive

Examples

In medieval literature, Mahound was often depicted as a figure representing Islam.
Some early Christian texts refer to Mahound in derogatory terms.
The character in the old play invoked Mahound as a symbol of the Saracen enemy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Muhammad - the prophet and founder of Islam (570–632)

Etymology

From Old French Mahom, from Medieval Latin Mahometus, distorted forms of Muhammad, the Islamic prophet.
