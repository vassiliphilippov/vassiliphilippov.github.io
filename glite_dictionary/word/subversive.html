subversive

1. NOUN

/ səbˈvɜrsɪv / (AmE), / səbˈvɜːsɪv / (BrE)
a person who seeks to undermine or overthrow an established system, especially a government or political authority
person seeking to overthrow authority

Examples

The government cracked down on the subversive who was organizing protests in the capital.
Police arrested several suspected subversives believed to be plotting against the regime.
He was accused of being a subversive simply because he criticized the authorities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revolutionary - someone who actively supports or takes part in a revolution
  • Synonym: agitator - a person who urges others to protest or rebel

Etymology

From Latin 'subversus' (overturned), from 'subvertere' (to overturn).

2. ADJ

/ səbˈvɝː.sɪv / (AmE), / səbˈvɜː.sɪv / (BrE)
Intended to undermine or overthrow an established government or authority.
seeking to undermine authority or government

Examples

The government banned the book, labeling it as subversive propaganda.
He was arrested for distributing subversive leaflets that criticized the regime.
Some view her subversive ideas as dangerous to national stability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revolutionary - actively supporting complete political or social change
  • Antonym: loyal - faithful to a government, cause, or person

Etymology

From Medieval Latin subversivus, from Latin subvertere (to overthrow).
