Usnea barbata

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌzniə bɑrˈbɑtə / (AmE), / ˈʌzniə bɑːˈbɑːtə / (BrE)
a greenish-grey lichen with long, hanging strands that grows on trees
a type of hanging lichen found on trees

Examples

The old oak tree was draped in Usnea barbata, giving it a mystical appearance.
Scientists collected samples of Usnea barbata to study its air-filtering properties.
Hikers marveled at how the Usnea barbata hung in long, greenish-grey strands from the branches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beard lichen - a common name for Usnea barbata and similar lichens

Etymology

From Latin Usnea (a genus of lichens) and barbata (bearded), referring to its threadlike appearance.
