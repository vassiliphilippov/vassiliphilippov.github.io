Bellerophon

1. PROPN

/ bəˈlɛrəˌfɒn / (AmE), / bəˈlɛrəfən / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, a hero of Corinth known for taming the winged horse Pegasus and killing the monster Chimera.
mythical Greek hero; tamer of Pegasus

Examples

The legend says that Bellerophon rode Pegasus into battle against the fearsome Chimera.
Many ancient stories recount the heroic exploits of Bellerophon.
In the myth, Bellerophon is celebrated as the tamer of Pegasus and the slayer of monsters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pegasus - mythical winged horse tamed by Bellerophon
  • Synonym: Chimera - fire-breathing monster slain by Bellerophon

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Βελλεροφῶν (Bellerophōn).
