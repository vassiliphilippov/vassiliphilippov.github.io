Larix occidentalis

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛrɪks ɒkˌsɪdəntˈælɪs / (AmE), / ˈlærɪks ɒksɪˈdɛntælɪs / (BrE)
a species of tall larch native to western North America, characterized by pale green, sharply pointed leaves and oblong cones; valued as a timber tree
species of larch tree native to western North America

Examples

The Larix occidentalis is highly valued for its durable timber.
Several Larix occidentalis trees grow in the forests of the Pacific Northwest.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Larix occidentalis in different soil conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: western larch - common name for Larix occidentalis

Etymology

From Latin: 'Larix' means 'larch'; 'occidentalis' means 'western'.
