yttrium

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪt.ri.əm / (AmE), / ˈɪt.ri.əm / (BrE)
a silvery metallic chemical element, found in rare-earth minerals and used in alloys with magnesium and aluminum
a metallic chemical element

Examples

Research teams used yttrium to improve the strength of lightweight metal alloys.
The rare-earth mineral xenotime is an important source of yttrium.
Yttrium compounds are also used in the production of color television tubes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: element - a substance that cannot be broken down into simpler substances by chemical means

Etymology

From the Swedish village Ytterby, where its mineral was first discovered.
