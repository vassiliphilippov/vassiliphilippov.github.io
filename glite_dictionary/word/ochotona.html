Ochotona

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊ.kəˈtoʊ.nə / (AmE), / ˌɒ.kəˈtəʊ.nə / (BrE)
The genus of small mammals comprising the pikas, and the type genus of the family Ochotonidae.
genus of small mammals (pikas)

Examples

The Ochotona genus includes several species of pikas found in Asia and North America.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Ochotona in the alpine meadows.
All members of Ochotona are adapted to cold, mountainous environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pika - A small mammal belonging to the genus Ochotona

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on Russian 'ochótka' meaning pika.

2. NOUN

/ ˌoʊ.kəˈtoʊ.nə / (AmE), / ˌɒ.kəˈtəʊ.nə / (BrE)
A genus of small mammals in the family Ochotonidae, commonly known as pikas.
genus of pikas (mammals)

Examples

The genus Ochotona includes several species of pikas found across Asia and North America.
Researchers are studying the habitat preferences of Ochotona in high-altitude regions.
Members of the genus Ochotona are well adapted to cold, mountainous environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pika - a small mountain-dwelling mammal of Asia and North America

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, ultimately from Russian *ochotona* (pika).
