biblical

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɪblɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈbɪblɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the Bible, its times, or its people
of or relating to the Bible

Examples

The ancient city was described in biblical terms by the guide.
He wore a long, flowing robe that looked almost biblical in style.
The drought reached biblical proportions, affecting thousands of families.

Synset Examples

biblical styles in writing
a beard of biblical proportions
biblical costumes

Etymology

From Late Latin 'biblicus', from 'biblia' (Bible).

WordNet ID: oewn-02865632-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈbɪb.lɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈbɪb.lɪ.kəl / (BrE)
relating to, contained in, or in accordance with the Bible
relating to the Bible

Examples

Many of the values taught in this school are biblical in origin.
She is studying biblical archaeology at the university.
The choir performed several biblical hymns during the service.

Synset Examples

biblical names
biblical Hebrew

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scriptural - relating to or characteristic of scripture, especially the Bible

Etymology

From Latin biblicus, from Greek biblion (book), originally referring to the Bible.
