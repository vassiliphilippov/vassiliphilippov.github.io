apathetic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæpəˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌapəˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
showing or feeling little or no interest, enthusiasm, or concern; indifferent or unresponsive.
lacking interest or emotion

Examples

Many students remained apathetic during the election despite efforts to encourage participation.
After years in the same job, he became increasingly apathetic about his daily tasks.
Her apathetic response surprised everyone who expected her to be excited about the news.

Synset Examples

a woman who became active rather than apathetic as she grew older

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indifferent - having no particular interest or sympathy; unconcerned
  • Antonym: passionate - having or showing strong feelings or beliefs

Etymology

From Greek apathēs, from a- (without) + pathos (feeling).

2. ADJ

/ ˌæpəˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌapəˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
showing or feeling little or no emotion or interest; indifferent.
showing little or no interest or emotion

Examples

Despite the excitement around her, she remained apathetic and unmoved.
The teacher was frustrated by the students' apathetic responses to her questions.
He grew increasingly apathetic towards politics after years of disappointment.

Synset Examples

an apathetic audience
the universe is neither hostile nor friendly; it is simply indifferent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indifferent - having no particular interest or sympathy; unconcerned
  • Synonym: uninterested - not showing interest; unconcerned
  • Antonym: enthusiastic - showing intense and eager enjoyment, interest, or approval

Etymology

From Greek apathēs (without feeling), from a- (without) + pathos (feeling).
