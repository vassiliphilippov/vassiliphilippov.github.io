necromantical

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɛk.rəˈmæn.tɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌnɛk.rəˈmæn.tɪ.kəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of necromancy, the practice of communicating with the dead, especially to predict the future
relating to necromancy

Examples

The old castle was the site of necromantical rituals on stormy nights.
Her interest in necromantical lore led her to study ancient grimoires.
The novel features a secret society engaged in necromantical practices.

Synset Examples

mysterious necromantic rites

Related Terms

  • Synonym: necromantic - relating to necromancy; also the standard adjective form

Etymology

Formed from 'necromancy' + '-ical', patterned after 'necromantic'. 'Necromancy' is from Late Latin 'necromantia' and Greek 'nekromanteia' (divination by the dead).
