snatch

1. VERB

/ snætʃ / (AmE), / snætʃ / (BrE)
to quickly seize or grasp something, often suddenly or eagerly
quickly seize or grasp

Examples

The thief managed to snatch her purse before she noticed.
As soon as the bell rang, eager children snatched the last pieces of cake.
He tried to snatch the letter out of my hand, but I pulled away.

Synset Examples

Before I could stop him the dog snatched the ham bone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grab - to take or seize suddenly
  • Synonym: to seize - to take hold of something quickly and firmly

Etymology

Of imitative origin; related to Middle English *snacchen* (to snap at).
