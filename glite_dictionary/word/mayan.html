Mayan

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪ.ən / (AmE), / ˈmaɪ.ən / (BrE)
A member of an indigenous people of Yucatán, Belize, and Guatemala, known for their advanced civilization, especially in architecture, pottery, and astronomy, which reached its peak between AD 300 and 900.
member of an indigenous Mesoamerican people

Examples

The Mayan civilization left behind impressive stone cities and pyramids in Central America.
A Mayan astronomer once calculated celestial events with remarkable precision.
Some descendants of the ancient Mayan people still speak their ancestral languages today.

Synset Examples

Mayans had a system of writing and an accurate calendar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Maya - the indigenous people collectively or their civilization

Etymology

From Spanish 'maya', adopted from indigenous self-designation.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪ.ən / (AmE), / ˈmaɪ.ən / (BrE)
a family of related American Indian languages spoken by the Maya people of Mesoamerica
family of American Indian languages

Examples

The Mayan languages are still spoken by millions of people in Mexico and Central America.
Scholars debate how the Mayan writing system represented sounds and meanings.
She learned basic phrases in a Mayan language while visiting Guatemala.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Maya - member of the Maya people of Mesoamerica

Etymology

From Maya (the name of the people) + -an (denoting languages or belonging).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06931991-n
Synset POS: n
