rappeller

1. NOUN

/ rəˈpɛlər / (AmE), / rəˈpɛlə / (BrE)
a person who descends a steep or vertical surface using a doubled rope secured at a high point
person who descends with a rope

Examples

The rappeller clipped into the rope and began his slow descent down the cliff.
Every rappeller should check their harness and knots carefully before starting.
As a skilled rappeller, she led the group safely down the canyon wall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abseiler - a person who descends a rock face using a rope (British term)

Etymology

From the verb 'rappel', from French 'rappeler' (to recall), used in mountaineering sense in late 19th century.
