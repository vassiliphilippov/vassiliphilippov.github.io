saddlebow

1. NOUN

/ ˈsædəlˌboʊ / (AmE), / ˈsædəlˌbəʊ / (BrE)
the curved or raised front part of a saddle that serves as a handgrip
front part of a saddle (handgrip)

Examples

She gripped the saddlebow tightly as the horse began to gallop.
The intricate leatherwork on the saddlebow caught the sunlight.
When mounting, he steadied himself with one hand on the saddlebow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pommel - the rounded knob on the front of a saddle

Etymology

From 'saddle' + 'bow', referring to its arched shape.
