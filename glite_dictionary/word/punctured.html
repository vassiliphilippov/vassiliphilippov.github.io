punctured

1. ADJ

/ ˈpʌŋktʃɚd / (AmE), / ˈpʌŋktʃəd / (BrE)
Having a hole pierced or cut through, especially as the result of being pierced by a sharp object.
having a hole made by piercing

Examples

The cyclist was frustrated by his punctured tire half a mile from home.
Doctors quickly treated the patient’s punctured lung after the accident.
She discovered a punctured juice box leaking in her lunch bag.

Synset Examples

pierced ears
a perforated eardrum
a punctured balloon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pierced - having a hole made by piercing, especially intentionally
  • Synonym: perforated - having a number of holes or small openings, often in a pattern

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'puncture', from Latin 'punctura' (a pricking).
