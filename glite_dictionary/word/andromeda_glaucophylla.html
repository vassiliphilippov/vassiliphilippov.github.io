Andromeda glaucophylla

1. NOUN

/ ænˈdrɑːmədə ˌɡlɔːkoʊˈfɪlə / (AmE), / ænˈdrɒmɪdə ˌɡlɔːkəʊˈfɪlə / (BrE)
A wiry evergreen shrub native to wet, acidic areas in Arctic regions, Canada, and the northeastern United States, recognized by its pendent clusters of white or pink flowers.
wiry evergreen shrub with white or pink flowers

Examples

Andromeda glaucophylla is often found growing in boggy, acidic soils in northern climates.
The delicate clusters of pink flowers make Andromeda glaucophylla a charming addition to natural wetland gardens.
Botanists studied the adaptation of Andromeda glaucophylla to cold arctic environments.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; genus name Andromeda from Greek mythology, and species epithet from Greek 'glaukos' (bluish-grey) + 'phyllon' (leaf).
