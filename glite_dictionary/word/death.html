death

1. NOUN

/ dɛθ / (AmE), / dɛθ / (BrE)
the permanent end of all life functions in a person, animal, or organism
the end of life

Examples

He was deeply affected by the sudden death of his friend.
The film explores themes of love, loss, and death.
Many cultures have rituals to mark the death of a community member.

Synset Examples

the animal died a painful death

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demise - death, especially used formally or as a euphemism
  • Antonym: birth - the beginning of life

Etymology

From Old English 'dēaþ', related to Old Norse 'dauthi', German 'Tod', from Proto-Germanic.
