argus-eyed

1. ADJ

/ ˈɑrɡəsˌaɪd / (AmE), / ˈɑːɡəsˌaɪd / (BrE)
carefully observant and alert to possible danger; vigilant.
vigilant; very observant

Examples

The argus-eyed security guard noticed the smallest details in the busy hallway.
An argus-eyed editor can catch mistakes that others might miss.
With argus-eyed attention, the teacher watched the playground for any signs of trouble.

Synset Examples

a policy of open-eyed awareness
the vigilant eye of the town watch
there was a watchful dignity in the room
a watchful parent with a toddler in tow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vigilant - keeping careful watch for possible danger
  • Synonym: watchful - paying close attention to potential danger or trouble

Etymology

From Argus, a giant in Greek mythology said to have had a hundred eyes.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɑrɡəs aɪd / (AmE), / ˈɑːɡəs aɪd / (BrE)
having very keen or watchful eyes; extremely observant or vigilant.
extremely observant; keen-sighted

Examples

The argus-eyed security guard noticed the slightest movement in the crowd.
She was argus-eyed when reviewing the details of the contract, missing nothing.
Reporters are often argus-eyed, always searching for the truth behind each story.

Synset Examples

quick-sighted as a cat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vigilant - keeping careful watch for possible danger or difficulties
  • Antonym: inattentive - not paying attention

Etymology

From Argus, a giant in Greek mythology said to have a hundred eyes, who was always watchful.
