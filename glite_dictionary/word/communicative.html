communicative

1. ADJ

/ kəˈmjunɪˌkeɪtɪv / (AmE), / kəˈmjuːnɪkətɪv / (BrE)
relating to or involving the act of communication; concerned with exchanging information, ideas, or feelings
relating to communication

Examples

The teacher uses communicative methods to encourage student participation.
Her job requires strong communicative skills and collaboration with others.
The communicative arts include writing, speaking, and media studies.

Synset Examples

communicative arts

Etymology

From Latin communicativus, from communicare (to share).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02967917-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ kəˈmjunɪˌkeɪtɪv / (AmE), / kəˈmjuːnɪkətɪv / (BrE)
willing or able to talk to people and share information or feelings; inclined to communicate.
willing to talk or share information

Examples

The new manager is very communicative with her team, always encouraging open discussion.
Children tend to become more communicative as they grow comfortable in their environment.
After the meeting, he was unusually communicative about the company's future plans.

Synset Examples

was a communicative person and quickly told all she knew

Related Terms

  • Synonym: talkative - fond of talking; given to talking a lot
  • Antonym: reticent - not revealing one's thoughts or feelings readily

Etymology

From Latin communicativus (relating to communication).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00496952-a
Synset POS: a
