Arnica montana

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑrnɪkə mɒnˈteɪnə / (AmE), / ˌɑːnɪkə mɒnˈtɑːnə / (BrE)
a perennial herb found in Europe and western Asia, with orange-yellow daisylike flower heads, used medicinally to treat bruises and swellings
herb used to treat bruises and swellings
medical

Examples

Many herbal remedies for bruises contain Arnica montana extracts as their active ingredient.
The orange-yellow flowers of Arnica montana are harvested in early summer for medicinal use.
Researchers studied the effects of Arnica montana on reducing swelling after surgery.

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Arnica' is the genus and 'montana' means 'of the mountains'.
