Manihot esculenta

1. NOUN

/ ˌmænɪˌhɑt ɛˈskjʊlɛntə / (AmE), / ˌmænɪˌhɒt ɛˈskjʊlɛntə / (BrE)
A tropical plant also known as cassava, cultivated for its starchy, edible root, used as food and for making products like tapioca.
tropical plant; source of cassava root

Examples

Manihot esculenta is widely cultivated in tropical regions for its nutritious roots.
The roots of Manihot esculenta are processed to produce flour and tapioca pearls.
Researchers study Manihot esculenta to improve food security in developing countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cassava - tropical plant with starchy edible roots; also called yuca or manioc

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Manihot', species 'esculenta' meaning 'edible'.
