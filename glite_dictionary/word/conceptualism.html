conceptualism

1. NOUN

/ kənˈsɛp.tʃu.əˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / kənˈsɛp.tjʊ.ə.lɪz.əm / (BrE)
(Philosophy) The doctrine that universals exist only within the mind and that general terms are mediated by mental concepts rather than by tangible universals.
philosophical doctrine about mental universals

Examples

Some philosophers argue that conceptualism bridges the gap between realism and nominalism.
Medieval debates often revolved around the validity of conceptualism in understanding abstract ideas.
His thesis provided a modern defense of conceptualism in the theory of universals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nominalism - the doctrine that universals or general ideas are mere names, without any corresponding reality
  • Antonym: realism - the belief that universals exist independently of the mind

Etymology

From French conceptuel and -ism, ultimately from Latin conceptus (thought, concept).
