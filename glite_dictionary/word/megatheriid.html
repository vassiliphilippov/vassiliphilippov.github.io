megatheriid

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛɡəˈθɪriɪd / (AmE), / ˌmɛɡəˈθɪrɪɪd / (BrE)
A member of an extinct family of large ground-dwelling sloths (Megatheriidae) that lived in South and Central America during the Cenozoic era.
extinct large ground sloth

Examples

Fossils of the megatheriid were discovered in Pleistocene deposits in South America.
The megatheriid was one of the largest terrestrial mammals of its time.
Scientists believe that megatheriids became extinct about 10,000 years ago.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ground sloth - extinct large, slow-moving sloths that lived on the ground
  • Synonym: megatherium - type genus of large extinct ground sloths

Etymology

From New Latin Megatheriidae, based on Megatherium (type genus) + -id (member of family).
