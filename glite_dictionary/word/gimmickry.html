gimmickry

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪmɪkɹi / (AmE), / ˈɡɪmɪkɹi / (BrE)
the use of gimmicks, especially in order to attract attention or publicity
use of gimmicks to attract attention

Examples

The politician's campaign was criticized for its excessive gimmickry and lack of substance.
Modern advertising often relies on gimmickry to stand out in a crowded market.
Some viewers found the film's visual gimmickry distracting rather than impressive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gimmick - a trick or device intended to attract attention

Etymology

From 'gimmick' + '-ry,' denoting a practice or collection related to the root noun.
