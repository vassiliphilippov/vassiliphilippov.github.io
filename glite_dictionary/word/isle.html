isle

1. NOUN

/ aɪl / (AmE), / aɪl / (BrE)
a small island, especially used in poetic or literary contexts
a small island

Examples

We spent the summer on a remote isle off the coast of Scotland.
The poem describes the beauty of the green isle set in the sparkling sea.
Legends tell of a hidden isle inhabited by mysterious creatures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: island - a piece of land surrounded by water

Etymology

From Old French 'ile', from Latin 'insula', with the 's' reintroduced due to influence from Latin spelling.
