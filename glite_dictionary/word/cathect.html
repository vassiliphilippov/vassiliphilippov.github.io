to cathect

1. VERB

/ kəˈθɛkt / (AmE), / kəˈθɛkt / (BrE)
to invest emotional or psychic energy in a person, idea, or object, especially in psychoanalytic theory
invest emotional energy (psychoanalytic)
medical

Examples

In therapy, the patient may cathect strong feelings onto the therapist.
She struggled to cathect her emotions after the loss of her job.
The child has cathected much of his energy onto his favorite toy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to invest - to devote time, effort, or resources to something

Etymology

From German 'kathektisieren', from Greek 'kathexis' (holding, retention).
