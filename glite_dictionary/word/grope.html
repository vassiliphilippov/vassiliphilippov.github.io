grope

1. VERB

/ ɡroʊp / (AmE), / ɡrəʊp / (BrE)
to search for something by feeling with the hands, especially in the dark or when one cannot see clearly
search with hands blindly

Examples

She had to grope for the light switch when the power went out.
I watched him grope around the desk, searching for his keys.
In total darkness, we groped our way down the hallway.

Synset Examples

She groped for her glasses in the darkness of the bedroom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fumble - to use the hands clumsily while doing or handling something

Etymology

From Middle English 'grope', from Old English 'grāpian' (to feel, touch).

Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ ɡroʊp / (AmE), / ɡrəʊp / (BrE)
to search or feel about with uncertainty or without clear direction, often using the hands or figuratively in the mind
search blindly or uncertainly

Examples

He had to grope in the dark for the light switch.
She groped for the right words to express her feelings.
Their minds groped for an explanation that made sense.

Synset Examples

His mind groped to make the connection

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fumble - to do or handle something clumsily or uncertainly

Etymology

From Middle English *gropen*, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch *grope* and German *greifen* (to grasp).

Source: WordNet

/ ɡroʊp / (AmE), / ɡrəʊp / (BrE)
An act of touching or feeling someone or something with the hands, often clumsily or inappropriately, especially as an act of sexual advance without consent.
act of groping (touching, often inappropriately)

Examples

The victim reported that the grope happened while she was on the crowded subway.
His awkward grope for the light switch startled her in the dark hallway.
There was outrage after the politician was accused of an unwanted grope at a public event.
