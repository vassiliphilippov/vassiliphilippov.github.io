Salix vitellina

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪlɪks vɪtəˈlaɪnə / (AmE), / ˈseɪlɪks vɪtɛˈliːnə / (BrE)
A European willow tree with greyish leaves and distinctive yellow-orange twigs, often used in basketry.
a European willow with yellow-orange twigs

Examples

The bright yellow twigs of the Salix vitellina make it valuable for decorative winter gardens.
Traditional artisans often use Salix vitellina branches to weave durable baskets.
In early spring, the Salix vitellina stands out for its colorful shoots among other willows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: willow - deciduous tree or shrub of the genus Salix

Etymology

From Latin: 'salix' meaning 'willow', 'vitellina' meaning 'pertaining to yolk' (yellow color).
