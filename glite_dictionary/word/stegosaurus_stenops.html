Stegosaurus stenops

1. NOUN

/ ˌstɛɡəˈsɔːrəs ˈstɛˌnɑps / (AmE), / ˌstiːɡəˈsɔːrəs ˈstɛnɒps / (BrE)
a species of large herbivorous dinosaur with distinctive bony plates along its back and a spiked tail, believed to have lived during the Late Jurassic period
species of plated herbivorous dinosaur

Examples

The Stegosaurus stenops had large, upright plates running along its back and a powerful spiked tail.
Paleontologists discovered a well-preserved Stegosaurus stenops fossil in North America.
Children are often fascinated by the appearance of Stegosaurus stenops in dinosaur exhibitions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stegosaur - dinosaur genus with bony plates along the back

Etymology

From Greek 'stegos' (roof) + 'sauros' (lizard); specific epithet 'stenops' from Greek 'stenos' (narrow) + 'ops' (face).
