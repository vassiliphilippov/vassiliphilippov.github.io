Yucatan

1. PROPN

/ ˌjuː.kəˈtæn / (AmE), / ˌjuː.kəˈtæn / (BrE)
a state in southeastern Mexico, located on the northern part of the Yucatan Peninsula
state in southeastern Mexico

Examples

We visited Yucatan last summer to explore the ancient Mayan ruins.
Many flamingos can be seen along the northern coast of Yucatan.
The cuisine of Yucatan is known for its unique blend of Mexican and Mayan flavors.

Etymology

From Spanish 'Yucatán', possibly derived from Maya language.

2. PROPN

/ ˌjuːkəˈtæn / (AmE), / ˌjuːkəˈtæn / (BrE)
a peninsula in southeastern Mexico, extending into the Gulf of Mexico between the Bay of Campeche and the Caribbean Sea
peninsula in southeastern Mexico

Examples

The Yucatan Peninsula is known for its ancient Mayan ruins and beautiful coastlines.
Many tourists visit Yucatan to explore its cenotes and vibrant local culture.
Hurricanes frequently approach the shores of the Yucatan before entering the Gulf of Mexico.

Etymology

From Spanish Yucatán, name of the region in southeastern Mexico.
