Polydactylus virginicus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːliˌdækˈtɪləs vəˈdʒɪnɪkəs / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˌdækˈtɪləs vəˈdʒɪnɪkəs / (BrE)
A species of threadfin fish found along the western Atlantic coast, commonly known as the Atlantic threadfin.
species of fish found in the western Atlantic

Examples

The Polydactylus virginicus is often identified by its long pectoral filaments near the coast.
Researchers studied the behavior of juvenile Polydactylus virginicus in estuarine environments.
Commercial fishers occasionally catch Polydactylus virginicus while targeting other coastal species.

Etymology

From Greek 'poly-' (many) + 'daktylos' (finger), and Latinized species name referencing Virginia.
