rationality

1. NOUN

/ ˌræʃəˈnæləti / (AmE), / ˌræʃəˈnæləti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being reasonable, based on facts or logic, and having sound judgment
quality of being reasonable or logical

Examples

We must approach this problem with rationality instead of acting on impulse.
Her rationality helped the team reach a fair decision.
The scientist's rationality was evident in every aspect of his research.

Synset Examples

his rationality may have been impaired
he had to rely less on reason than on rousing their emotions

Related Terms

  • Antonym: irrationality - the quality of being illogical or unreasonable

Etymology

From Latin rationalitas, from rationalis (of reason, reasonable).

2. NOUN

/ ˌræʃəˈnæləti / (AmE), / ˌræʃəˈnælɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being guided by reason or logic rather than emotion or impulse
the quality of being guided by logic

Examples

The debate was marked by the rationality of both sides, making it productive and enlightening.
She admired his rationality when faced with difficult decisions.
Investors rely on the rationality of markets when making financial choices.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: irrationality - the quality of being not guided by reason or logic
  • Synonym: reasonableness - the quality of being fair and sensible

Etymology

From Latin 'rationalitas' (reasonableness), from 'rationalis' (of reason).
