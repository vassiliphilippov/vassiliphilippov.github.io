stretched

1. ADJ

/ stɹɛtʃt / (AmE), / stɹɛtʃt / (BrE)
extended or spread out over a wide area or distance
extended or spread out

Examples

The landscape seemed endless, with fields stretched to the horizon in every direction.
A vast carpet of flowers stretched before them, glowing in the afternoon sun.
The mountain range stretched as far as the eye could see, creating a dramatic skyline.

Synset Examples

broad fields lay stretched on both sides of us

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extended - made longer or wider; expanded in length or area

2. ADJ

/ stɹɛtʃt / (AmE), / stɹɛtʃt / (BrE)
(of muscles) made less stiff or more flexible as a result of stretching exercises.
relieved of stiffness by stretching (muscles)

Examples

After warming up, the stretched muscles were ready for the competition.
Physical therapists recommend keeping your muscles stretched to prevent injuries.
Yoga classes often leave participants with pleasantly stretched and relaxed muscles.

Synset Examples

well-stretched muscles are less susceptible to injury

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: flexible - capable of bending easily without breaking; easily modified
  • Antonym: tense - (of muscles) in a state of contraction and stiffness

Etymology

Past participle of 'stretch', from Old English *streccan* (to stretch, extend).
