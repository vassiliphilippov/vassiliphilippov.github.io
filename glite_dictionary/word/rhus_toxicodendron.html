Rhus toxicodendron

1. NOUN

/ ɹʌs ˌtɑksɪkoʊˈdɛndrən / (AmE), / ɹʌs ˌtɒksɪkəʊˈdɛndrən / (BrE)
A poisonous shrub native to the southeastern United States that causes a rash on contact with the skin, commonly known as poison oak.
poisonous shrub causing skin rash
medical

Examples

Hikers should be cautious of Rhus toxicodendron when walking through southeastern woodlands.
Contact with Rhus toxicodendron can result in an itchy, uncomfortable rash.
Botanists often study Rhus toxicodendron to understand allergic skin reactions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poison ivy - a poisonous North American plant causing skin irritation

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus name 'Rhus' and Greek 'toxikodendron' meaning 'poison tree'.
