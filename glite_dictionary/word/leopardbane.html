leopardbane

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛpərdˌbeɪn / (AmE), / ˈlɛpədˌbeɪn / (BrE)
any of several perennial herbs of the genus Doronicum, cultivated for their long-stemmed yellow flowers and alternate clasping leaves
herbaceous plant of the genus Doronicum

Examples

The gardener planted several leopardbane along the edge of the path for a splash of yellow.
Wild leopardbane thrives in woodland areas, producing bright flowers in the spring.
Some people believe leopardbane can be harmful if ingested by livestock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Doronicum - genus of flowering plants known as leopardbane

Etymology

Formed from 'leopard' and 'bane' (poison), reflecting the plant's supposed toxicity to animals.
