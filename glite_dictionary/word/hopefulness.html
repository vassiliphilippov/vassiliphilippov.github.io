hopefulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊpfəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈhəʊpflnəs / (BrE)
the feeling or quality of having hope; a confident expectation of positive outcomes
the quality of having hope

Examples

Her hopefulness inspired everyone around her to keep striving for success.
Despite the setbacks, there was still a sense of hopefulness in the room.
The doctor's hopefulness reassured the anxious patient.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hopelessness - the state of having no hope; despair
  • Synonym: optimism - the tendency to expect the best possible outcome

Etymology

Formed from 'hopeful' + '-ness', a common noun-forming suffix in English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊpfəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈhəʊpfl̩nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of feeling or showing hope; optimism about future events or outcomes
the state of having hope or optimism

Examples

Her hopefulness inspired everyone in the group to keep trying despite setbacks.
Even in difficult times, his hopefulness never faded.
There was a sense of hopefulness in the air as the election results came in.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hopelessness - the state of having no hope; despair
  • Synonym: optimism - hopefulness and confidence about the future

Etymology

From 'hopeful' + '-ness', formed in English.
