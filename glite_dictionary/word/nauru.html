Nauru

1. PROPN

/ nɑˈuːru / (AmE), / nɑːˈuːruː / (BrE)
a small island country in Micronesia, in the Central Pacific, known for its phosphate mining and exports.
island country in the Central Pacific

Examples

The Republic of Nauru is one of the smallest countries in the world.
Phosphate mining has played a significant role in the economy of Nauru.
Many tourists are attracted to the beautiful beaches of Nauru.

2. PROPN

/ nɔˈruː, ˈnaʊruː / (AmE), / nɔːˈruː, ˈnaʊruː / (BrE)
a small island country in the central Pacific Ocean, southwest of Hawaii and west of the Gilbert Islands; part of Micronesia
a Pacific island country in Micronesia

Examples

Many tourists are fascinated by the unique history of Nauru.
The phosphate industry has played a significant role in the economy of Nauru.
After gaining independence in 1968, Nauru became one of the world's smallest republics.

Etymology

From the local Nauruan word *Nauru*, possibly from *anáoero* ('I go to the beach').
