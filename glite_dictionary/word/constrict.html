to constrict

1. VERB

/ kənˈstrɪkt / (AmE), / kənˈstrɪkt / (BrE)
to become tighter or narrower; to make tighter or narrower by pressure or shrinking
to become tight or narrower

Examples

Her throat constricted with fear when she heard the strange noise.
As the temperature dropped, the metal pipes began to constrict.
The snake's body slowly constricted around its prey.

Synset Examples

Her throat constricted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tighten - to make or become tight or tighter
  • Antonym: to loosen - to make or become less tight

Etymology

From Latin constrictus, past participle of constringere (to bind together).

2. VERB

/ kənˈstrɪkt / (AmE), / kənˈstrɪkt / (BrE)
to make something narrower by squeezing or pressing; to become tighter or smaller in size
to tighten, squeeze, or become narrower

Examples

A tight collar can constrict your breathing if worn for too long.
The snake began to constrict its prey, making escape impossible.
Blood vessels constrict in response to cold temperatures to conserve heat.

Synset Examples

she compressed her lips
the spasm contracted the muscle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contract - to decrease in size, number, or range
  • Antonym: to expand - to become or make larger or more extensive

Etymology

From Latin constrictus, past participle of constringere (to bind together, constrict).

Source: WordNet
