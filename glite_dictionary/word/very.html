very

1. ADV

/ ˈvɛri / (AmE), / ˈvɛri / (BrE)
used to emphasize the exact identity, time, place, or quality of something or someone mentioned.
emphasizing exact identity or precision

Examples

The answer was on the very last page of the book.
She arrived at the very moment we needed her help.
This is the very place where they first met.

Synset Examples

on the very next page
he expected the very opposite

Etymology

From Old French *verai* (true), from Latin *verax* (truthful).

4. ADV

/ ˈvɛri / (AmE), / ˈvɛri / (BrE)
Used to emphasize an adjective or adverb, indicating a high degree or extreme extent.
emphasizer for adjectives/adverbs

Examples

She was very gifted and excelled in every subject at school.
The restaurant is very popular with locals and tourists.
He played very well, impressing everyone with his performance.

Synset Examples

she was very gifted
he played very well
a really enjoyable evening
I'm real sorry about it
a rattling good yarn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: really - used for emphasis like 'very'
  • Degree Lower: slightly - by a small amount; less than 'very'
  • Degree Higher: extremely - to a very great degree; more than 'very'

Etymology

From Old French *verai* (true, real), from Latin *verax* (truthful).
