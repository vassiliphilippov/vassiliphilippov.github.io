Cardium

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːr.di.əm / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.dɪ.əm / (BrE)
the type genus of the family Cardiidae, comprising cockles—marine bivalve mollusks with rounded, ribbed shells
genus of cockles (marine bivalves)

Examples

The genus Cardium includes several species commonly found in sandy coastal waters.
Scientists identified the shell as belonging to Cardium edule, a widespread species of cockle.
In their study, they compared shell patterns of Cardium with related bivalve genera.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockle - a common edible bivalve mollusk, typically with a rounded shell

Etymology

From Greek *kardion* (little heart), from *kardia* (heart), referring to the heart-shaped shell.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑr.di.əm / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.dɪ.əm / (BrE)
a genus of marine bivalve mollusks in the family Cardiidae, commonly known as cockles.
genus of marine cockles

Examples

Many species in the genus Cardium are harvested for food in coastal communities.
The Cardium shell is often heart-shaped, inspiring its name from the Greek word for 'heart'.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Cardium genus along the Mediterranean coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockle - a small, edible, heart-shaped marine bivalve mollusk

Etymology

From Latin *cardium*, from Greek *kardion* (a kind of cockle, literally 'little heart', from the heart-shaped shell).
