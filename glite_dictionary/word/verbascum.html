Verbascum

1. NOUN

/ vərˈbæskəm / (AmE), / vəˈbæskəm / (BrE)
a genus of coarse herbs and subshrubs, mostly with woolly leaves, including species commonly known as mulleins
a plant genus of coarse, woolly-leaved herbs (mulleins)

Examples

The tall Verbascum flowers brightened the corner of the garden.
Botanists studied different species within the Verbascum genus for their medicinal properties.
Woolly leaves are a distinctive trait of most Verbascum plants.

Etymology

From Latin 'verbascum', the classical name for the mullein plant.

2. NOUN

/ vərˈbæskəm / (AmE), / vəˈbæskəm / (BrE)
A genus of coarse herbs and subshrubs, mostly with woolly leaves, commonly known as mullein.
genus of woolly-leaved herbs (mullein)

Examples

Many traditional herbal remedies use the dried leaves of Verbascum plants.
The field was dotted with tall, yellow-flowered Verbascum.
Gardeners appreciate Verbascum for its resilience to drought and poor soil.

Etymology

From Latin Verbascum, the classical name for mullein.
