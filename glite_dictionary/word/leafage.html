leafage

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːfɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈliːfɪdʒ / (BrE)
all the leaves of a plant or collective foliage of plants.
all the leaves of a plant

Examples

The tree's thick leafage provided welcome shade from the summer sun.
Autumn winds scattered the leafage across the garden.
The dense leafage made it difficult to see the birds hidden in the branches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foliage - plant leaves collectively

Etymology

From 'leaf' + '-age', modeled after 'foliage', Middle English via Old French 'foille' (leaf).
