vocalic

1. ADJ

/ voʊˈkælɪk / (AmE), / vəʊˈkælɪk / (BrE)
consisting of, containing, or relating to vowels.
relating to vowels

Examples

The linguist explained that the word has a highly vocalic structure.
Irish is known for its rich vocalic system, with many different vowel sounds.
She noticed the vocalic harmony in the choir's performance.

Synset Examples

vocalic sounds
the Gaelic language being uncommonly vocalic

Related Terms

  • Antonym: consonantal - relating to or consisting of consonants

Etymology

From Latin vocalis (sounding, having a voice), from vox (voice).

2. ADJ

/ voʊˈkælɪk / (AmE), / vəʊˈkælɪk / (BrE)
relating to or consisting of vowels; associated with vowels in linguistics or phonetics.
relating to vowels

Examples

In spoken language, vocalic sounds are produced without any significant constriction of airflow.
The linguist analyzed the vocalic elements in each word of the sentence.
Changes in the vocalic pattern can affect the meaning in some languages.

Synset Examples

vocalic segments
the vocalic ablaut

Related Terms

  • Antonym: consonantal - relating to or consisting of consonants

Etymology

From Latin 'vocalis' (relating to voice or vowel).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02836924-a
Synset POS: a
