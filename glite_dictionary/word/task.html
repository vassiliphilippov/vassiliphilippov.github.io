task

1. VERB

/ tæsk / (AmE), / tɑːsk / (BrE)
to assign a piece of work or responsibility to someone
assign a responsibility or job

Examples

Her manager tasked her with organizing the annual conference.
The young officer was tasked to lead the search party into the mountains.
He tasks his team with meeting strict deadlines every month.

Synset Examples

I tasked him with looking after the children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assign - to give someone a particular job or duty
  • Synonym: charge - to assign a duty or responsibility to someone

Etymology

From Old French 'tasque', from Medieval Latin 'tasca' (tax, duty), of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ tæsk / (AmE), / tɑːsk / (BrE)
a piece of work that needs to be done, especially one that is assigned or undertaken as part of one's duties
piece of work to be done

Examples

She completed the task ahead of schedule and impressed her manager.
Organizing the annual conference proved to be a challenging task.
Each student was given a different task during the group project.

Synset Examples

he prepared for great undertakings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: job - paid position of regular employment or a specific piece of work
  • Synonym: assignment - a specific task or piece of work assigned to someone
  • Synonym: duty - a moral or legal obligation; a task or action someone is required to perform

Etymology

From Old French *tasque*, from Medieval Latin *tasca*, from Vulgar Latin *taxare* (to evaluate, tax).

3. NOUN

/ tæsk / (AmE), / tɑːsk / (BrE)
a specific piece of work that needs to be done, especially as part of one's duties or for a particular purpose
piece of work to be done

Examples

Each employee was given a different task to complete by the end of the day.
Finishing the project on time was a difficult task for the team.
The teacher assigned a writing task for homework.

Synset Examples

estimates of the city's loss on that job ranged as high as a million dollars
the job of repairing the engine took several hours
the endless task of classifying the samples
the farmer's morning chores

Related Terms

  • Synonym: job - a paid position of regular employment or a particular piece of work
  • Synonym: chore - a routine or minor task, often household-related
  • Synonym: assignment - a task or piece of work assigned to someone
  • Synonym: duty - a moral or legal obligation; a responsibility

Etymology

From Old French *tasque*, from Medieval Latin *tasca* (a duty, assessment), from Vulgar Latin *taxa* (tax, charge).
