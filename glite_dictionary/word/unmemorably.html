unmemorably

1. ADV

/ ʌnˈmɛmərəbli / (AmE), / ʌnˈmɛmərəbli / (BrE)
In a way that is not memorable; without leaving any lasting impression.
in an unmemorable way

Examples

The lecture ended unmemorably, with no applause or questions from the audience.
She performed the song unmemorably, failing to stand out among the other contestants.
The evening passed unmemorably, blending in with countless others.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: memorably - in a memorable manner; in a way that is remembered
