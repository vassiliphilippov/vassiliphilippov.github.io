semi-evergreen

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛmi-ˈɛvərˌɡriːn / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈɛvəɡriːn / (BrE)
(of a plant) retaining some, but not all, of its leaves during the year; partially evergreen and partially deciduous.
partially evergreen plant

Examples

Some semi-evergreen trees may keep part of their foliage through mild winters.
The garden features a mix of semi-evergreen and deciduous shrubs to maintain year-round interest.
Semi-evergreen plants are especially useful for regions with variable climates.

Synset Examples

Brambles or blackberry are deciduous, semi-evergreen scrambling shrub

Related Terms

  • Antonym: deciduous - (of a plant) shedding its leaves annually
  • Degree Higher: evergreen - (of a plant) retaining green leaves throughout the year

Etymology

Formed from 'semi-' (from Latin 'semi-' meaning 'half' or 'partly') and 'evergreen'.
