junk

1. NOUN

/ dʒʌŋk / (AmE), / dʒʌŋk / (BrE)
a type of Chinese boat with a flat bottom, high stern, and large lugsails
Chinese sailing boat

Examples

The fisherman sailed his junk across the South China Sea.
A traditional Chinese junk is easily recognized by its distinct shape and sails.
Tourists enjoyed a sunset cruise aboard a restored junk in Hong Kong Harbor.

Etymology

From Portuguese 'junco', from Malay 'jong', from Javanese 'jong' (large ship).

2. VERB

/ dʒʌŋk / (AmE), / dʒʌŋk / (BrE)
to throw away or discard something as useless or no longer needed
to discard as useless or old

Examples

After the printer stopped working, we decided to junk it.
He junked his old bike after it became rusted and unusable.
They are junking outdated equipment to make space for new models.

Synset Examples

trash these old chairs
junk an old car
scrap your old computer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrap - to discard something as useless or no longer wanted
  • Antonym: keep - to retain possession of something

3. NOUN

/ dʒʌŋk / (AmE), / dʒʌŋk / (BrE)
old, broken, or useless things, especially those that have been destroyed or are no longer needed or wanted
old or broken useless things

Examples

He threw all the junk in the garage into the dumpster.
The backyard was littered with junk after the storm destroyed the fence.
She collects junk and turns it into art pieces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rubbish - waste material; things that are no longer wanted
  • Antonym: treasure - valuable things; objects considered to be precious

Etymology

Of uncertain origin, possibly from Old French 'jonc' (reed) or from a nautical term.
