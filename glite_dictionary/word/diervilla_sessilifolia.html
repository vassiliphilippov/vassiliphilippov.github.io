Diervilla sessilifolia

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈɜːrvɪlə sɛˌsɪlɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / daɪˈɜːvɪlə sɛˌsɪlɪˈfəʊliə / (BrE)
A species of bush honeysuckle native to the southeastern United States, characterized by large clusters of sulfur-yellow flowers.
a southeastern U.S. bush honeysuckle species

Examples

Diervilla sessilifolia thrives best in partial shade with moist, well-drained soil.
Gardeners often choose Diervilla sessilifolia for its showy yellow flowers and adaptability.
Pollinators such as bees are attracted to the sulfur-yellow blooms of Diervilla sessilifolia in midsummer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bush honeysuckle - a common name for species in the Diervilla genus

Etymology

From the genus name Diervilla, honoring French surgeon N. Dierville, and the species name *sessilifolia*, from Latin meaning 'with stalkless leaves.'
