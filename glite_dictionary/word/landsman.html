landsman

1. NOUN

/ ˈlændzmən / (AmE), / ˈlændzmən / (BrE)
a man who is inexperienced at sea, especially a sailor on his first voyage
inexperienced male sailor

Examples

On his first day aboard the ship, the landsman struggled to keep his balance on the deck.
The seasoned sailors teased the landsman for not knowing the names of the ropes.
Every landsman must learn quickly if he hopes to become a true sailor.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: seaman - an experienced sailor or member of a ship's crew

Etymology

Formed from 'land' + 'man', indicating a man from the land rather than the sea.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlændzmən / (AmE), / ˈlændzmən / (BrE)
a man who lives and works on land, especially as opposed to someone who works at sea
man who lives and works on land

Examples

Having always been a landsman, he was fascinated by tales of sailors and distant voyages.
As a landsman, he found the life aboard the ship challenging and unfamiliar.
The crew treated the landsman with good-natured amusement, knowing he had never seen the ocean before.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: seaman - a person who works at sea, especially as a sailor

Etymology

From German 'Landsmann' (countryman, fellow countryman).
