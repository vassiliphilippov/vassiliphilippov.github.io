joiner

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɔɪnər / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔɪnə / (BrE)
a skilled worker who constructs and assembles wooden items by joining pieces of wood, especially doors, window frames, and furniture
skilled woodworker who joins pieces of wood

Examples

The joiner carefully assembled the window frames by hand.
They hired a skilled joiner to build custom bookshelves for their office.
As a joiner, she specialized in crafting elegant staircases from oak.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carpenter - a person who makes and repairs wooden structures and frameworks

Etymology

From Middle English joinour, from Old French joigneor (joiner), from joindre (to join).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɔɪnər / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔɪnə / (BrE)
a person who readily joins groups, clubs, or organizations, often seeking social involvement
person who eagerly joins groups

Examples

As a natural joiner, she became a member of every club in her school.
He is such a joiner that he can't resist signing up for new organizations.
Not everyone is a joiner; some people prefer to keep to themselves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: socialite - a person who is well known in fashionable society and social circles
