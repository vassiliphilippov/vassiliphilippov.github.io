idolater

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈdɑːlətər / (AmE), / aɪˈdɒlətə / (BrE)
a person who worships idols or images as gods
person who worships idols

Examples

The ancient temple attracted many idolaters from distant lands.
He was condemned as an idolater by his devout peers.
In some religions, being called an idolater carried severe social consequences.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: iconoclast - a person who opposes or destroys religious images or idols
  • Synonym: idol - an image or representation of a god used as an object of worship

Etymology

From Greek eidōlolatrēs (idol worshipper), from eidōlon (idol) + latreia (worship).
