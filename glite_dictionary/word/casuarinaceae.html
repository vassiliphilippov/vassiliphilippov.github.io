Casuarinaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæʒuˌɛrəˈneɪsiˌaɪ / (AmE), / ˌkæzjʊəˈrɪnɪˌsiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants commonly known as the she-oak family, containing the genus Casuarina and related genera, native to Australia, Southeast Asia, and the Pacific islands.
family of trees and shrubs (she-oak family)

Examples

Several species within the Casuarinaceae family are cultivated for their use as windbreaks and timber.
The coastal forests were dominated by members of the Casuarinaceae, with tall she-oaks lining the dunes.
Researchers studied the drought tolerance of plants in the Casuarinaceae to understand their adaptation in arid regions.

Etymology

From the genus name Casuarina (from Malay 'kasuari' meaning 'cassowary', referring to the resemblance of the plant's branches to the bird's feathers) and the standard botanical family suffix '-aceae'.
