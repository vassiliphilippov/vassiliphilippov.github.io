subartesian

1. ADJ

/ ˌsʌb.ɑrˈtiː.ʒən / (AmE), / ˌsʌb.ɑːˈtiː.zi.ən / (BrE)
(Of water) rising naturally in a well to a height above the surrounding water table but not reaching the surface to flow out of the well.
water rising in a well above water table but not flowing out

Examples

In this region, most wells are subartesian rather than artesian, so pumps are needed to bring water to the surface.
The subartesian conditions mean that water will rise in the well but not overflow without additional pressure.
Geologists measured several subartesian wells before deciding where to drill for a sustainable water supply.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: artesian - (Of water) rising in a well under pressure and flowing out at the surface without pumping

Etymology

Formed from sub- (below) and artesian, referring to artesian wells.
