Kurdish

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɝːdɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkɜːdɪʃ / (BrE)
relating to Kurdistan, the Kurds, or their language and culture
of Kurdistan, the Kurds, or their culture

Examples

She is fluent in Kurdish and often translates for her family.
The Kurdish region in Iraq has a semi-autonomous government.
Traditional Kurdish music features unique instruments and vibrant rhythms.

Synset Examples

Kurdish Moslems

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kurd - a member of the Kurdish people

Etymology

From Kurd, referring to the Kurdish people, plus the English adjectival suffix -ish.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03090260-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɜːrdɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkɜːdɪʃ / (BrE)
an Iranian language spoken by the Kurdish people, primarily in Turkey, Iran, Iraq, Syria, and surrounding regions
Iranian language spoken by Kurds

Examples

Many people in northern Iraq speak Kurdish as their first language.
She is studying Kurdish at university to better understand Kurdish culture.
Official documents in some regions are translated into Arabic, Turkish, and Kurdish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Persian - a major Iranian language spoken in Iran and Afghanistan
  • Antonym: Arabic - the Semitic language widely spoken in the Middle East and North Africa

Etymology

From Kurd, the name of the Kurdish people, plus -ish.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06987218-n
Synset POS: n
