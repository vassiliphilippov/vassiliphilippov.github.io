susurration

1. NOUN

/ suːsəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / sjuːsəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
A soft, whispering or rustling sound; a murmur.
soft whispering or murmuring sound

Examples

The susurration of the breeze through the trees created a peaceful atmosphere in the garden.
A constant susurration of voices filled the crowded hall as people waited for the show to begin.
She listened to the susurration of the stream as it flowed gently over the rocks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: murmur - a low, continuous sound; a soft, indistinct voice
  • Synonym: rustle - a soft, muffled crackling sound like leaves or paper

Etymology

From Latin susurratio (a murmuring, whisper), from susurrare (to whisper).

2. NOUN

/ suːˌsɜːˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / suːˌsʌˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a soft, indistinct sound like whispering or murmuring, especially made by many voices or the wind
soft, murmuring sound or whisper

Examples

The susurration of the audience before the concert created a gentle, comforting background noise.
As the wind moved through the leaves, a gentle susurration filled the garden.
He found the constant susurration of voices in the library oddly soothing.

Synset Examples

a soft susurrus of conversation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: murmur - a soft, low, continuous sound
  • Synonym: whisper - a soft or confidential tone of voice

Etymology

From Latin susurratio (a whispering, murmuring), from susurrare (to whisper).
