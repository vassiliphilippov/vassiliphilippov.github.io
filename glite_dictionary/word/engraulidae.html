Engraulidae

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˈɡrɔlɪˌdi / (AmE), / ɛnˈɡrɔːlɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, schooling, saltwater fish known as anchovies, found in marine and brackish waters worldwide.
the anchovy family of fish

Examples

The family Engraulidae includes many species commonly referred to as anchovies.
Recent studies on Engraulidae have revealed interesting migration patterns in the Mediterranean Sea.
Members of the Engraulidae are important for both commercial fisheries and marine ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anchovy - a small, silvery fish of the Engraulidae family

Etymology

From modern Latin, from Greek 'engraulis' (anchovy) plus '-idae' (family suffix in zoological taxonomy).
