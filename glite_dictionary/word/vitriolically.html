vitriolically

1. ADV

/ ˌvɪtriˈɑlɪkli / (AmE), / ˌvɪtrɪˈɒlɪkli / (BrE)
In a manner that is bitterly scathing or caustically critical.
in a scathingly critical manner

Examples

The critic spoke vitriolically about the new play, leaving the cast disheartened.
She vitriolically dismissed his proposal during the meeting, shocking everyone present.
Online forums can sometimes become places where users argue vitriolically over trivial issues.

Synset Examples

he addressed her caustically

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caustically - in a severely critical or sarcastic manner

Etymology

From vitriolic (from Latin vitriolum, 'sulphate of iron') + -ally (adverbial suffix).
