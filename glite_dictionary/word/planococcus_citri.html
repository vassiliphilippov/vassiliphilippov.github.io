Planococcus citri

1. NOUN

/ plænəˈkɑkəs ˈsaɪtraɪ / (AmE), / plænəˈkɒkəs ˈsɪtraɪ / (BrE)
a species of mealybug that is a major pest of citrus and other cultivated plants
a citrus-damaging mealybug species

Examples

Orchards suffered heavy losses when Planococcus citri infestations went unchecked.
The gardener identified the white clusters on the leaves as evidence of Planococcus citri.
Regular inspections are needed to control Planococcus citri on citrus trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: citrus mealybug - common name for Planococcus citri, a pest of citrus

Etymology

From modern Latin, with 'Planococcus' meaning 'flat grain', and 'citri' referring to citrus.
