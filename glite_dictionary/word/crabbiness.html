crabbiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkræbinəs / (AmE), / ˈkræbinɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being easily annoyed, irritable, or bad-tempered
irritable or bad-tempered disposition

Examples

Despite his crabbiness, his friends still enjoyed spending time with him.
Her early morning crabbiness vanished after a cup of coffee.
The manager's crabbiness made the workplace tense during stressful times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritability - quality of being easily irritated
  • Antonym: good-naturedness - kind or pleasant temperament

Etymology

Formed from 'crabby' plus the noun suffix '-ness'. 'Crabby' likely derived from 'crab', used metaphorically for irritability.
