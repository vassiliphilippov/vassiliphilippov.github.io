Pedioecetes phasianellus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpiːdioʊˈsiːtiːz ˌfeɪziəˈnɛləs / (AmE), / ˌpiːdiəʊˈiːsiːtiːz ˌfeɪziəˈnɛləs / (BrE)
a large grouse native to prairies and open forests of western North America, commonly known as the sharp-tailed grouse
a North American grouse species

Examples

The Pedioecetes phasianellus displays its unique courtship dance during the breeding season.
Researchers observed a flock of Pedioecetes phasianellus feeding in the grasslands.
Conservation efforts are focused on protecting the Pedioecetes phasianellus from habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sharp-tailed grouse - common name for Pedioecetes phasianellus

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; genus 'Pedioecetes', probably from Greek 'pedion' (plain) and 'oikētēs' (inhabitant), and species 'phasianellus', diminutive of 'phasianus' (pheasant).
