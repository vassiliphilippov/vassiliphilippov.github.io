pilus

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪləs / (AmE), / ˈpiːləs / (BrE)
A cylindrical hair or hair-like filament that grows from the skin of a mammal.
hair or hair-like filament in mammals
medical

Examples

The dermatologist observed a broken pilus under the microscope.
Each pilus emerges from a follicle located in the skin.
Some mammals have more than one type of pilus covering their bodies.

Synset Examples

there is a hair in my soup

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hair - threadlike strand growing from the skin of mammals

Etymology

From Latin pilus (hair).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪləs / (AmE), / ˈpɪləs / (BrE)
A hairlike structure on the surface of a cell or microorganism, especially bacteria, often involved in attachment or transfer of genetic material.
hairlike structure on a cell or microorganism
medical

Examples

The bacterium uses its pili to adhere to host cells during infection.
Scientists observed that each cell had a single long pilus at its tip.
The transfer of DNA between bacteria can occur through a specialized pilus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fimbria - A similar hairlike appendage on bacteria, often interchangeable with pilus

Etymology

From Latin 'pilus' meaning 'hair'.

Synset POS: n
