appalled

1. ADJ

/ əˈpɔld / (AmE), / əˈpɔːld / (BrE)
filled with shock, horror, or dismay; greatly dismayed or horrified.
shocked or horrified

Examples

She was appalled by the amount of litter in the park.
The teacher looked appalled when he saw the students' exam results.
We were appalled at the way the animals were treated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horrified - feeling or showing horror or shock
  • Antonym: unconcerned - not worried or anxious

Etymology

From Latin *pallere* (to grow pale), via Old French *appaller*.
