antidromic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.tɪˈdrɑː.mɪk / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈdrɒ.mɪk / (BrE)
(of a nerve impulse) transmitted in the direction opposite to the normal flow along a nerve fiber.
nerve impulse in the opposite direction to normal
medical

Examples

The scientist observed antidromic impulses traveling along the nerve fiber during the experiment.
Stimulation at one end of the neuron caused an antidromic response detected at the other end.
Some researchers use antidromic activation to map neural pathways in animal studies.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: orthodromic - conducting nerve impulses in the normal direction

Etymology

From Greek anti- (against) + dromos (running, course).
