Castanea sativa

1. NOUN

/ kæsˈteɪniə səˈtiːvə / (AmE), / kæsˈteɪniə səˈtiːvə / (BrE)
the sweet chestnut tree, native to southern Europe and Asia Minor, cultivated for its edible nuts and wood
the sweet chestnut tree (species)

Examples

The ancient Castanea sativa in the village is over five hundred years old.
Edible nuts from Castanea sativa are a traditional food in many Mediterranean countries.
Researchers studied the spread of Castanea sativa across southern Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet chestnut - common name for Castanea sativa, the tree and its edible nut

Etymology

From Latin: Castanea (chestnut) + sativa (cultivated).
