to recycle

1. VERB

/ riˈsaɪkl̩ / (AmE), / riˈsaɪkl̩ / (BrE)
to process used materials or objects so that they can be used again
process materials for reuse

Examples

Many cities encourage residents to recycle paper, plastic, and glass.
The company recycles old computers to reduce electronic waste.
After the event, volunteers recycled all the empty water bottles.

Synset Examples

We must recycle the cardboard boxes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reuse - to use something again instead of throwing it away
  • Antonym: discard - to throw something away

Etymology

From re- (again) + cycle, influenced by the idea of cycling materials through repeated use.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01164741-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ riˈsaɪ.kəl / (AmE), / riˈsaɪ.kəl / (BrE)
to cause (a process, object, or material) to repeat or go through a cycle again, especially for reuse or further processing
cause to repeat a cycle

Examples

The power plant is designed to recycle water through the cooling system.
Sometimes, writers recycle themes or characters from their earlier work.
The company plans to recycle old data by feeding it back into their analysis software.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reuse - to use something again

Etymology

From re- (again) + cycle, from Greek kyklos (circle).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02056801-v
Synset POS: v
