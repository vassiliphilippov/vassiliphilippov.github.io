wagon

1. NOUN

/ ˈwæɡən / (AmE), / ˈwæɡən / (BrE)
A small four-wheeled cart, especially one used by children as a toy, sometimes for coasting.
child's four-wheeled toy cart

Examples

The children pulled their red wagon through the park, laughing as they went.
She loaded her stuffed animals into the wagon for a pretend picnic in the backyard.
He raced downhill in his trusty old wagon, steering carefully to avoid the rocks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cart - a vehicle with either two or four wheels, often drawn by animals or pushed by humans

Etymology

From Dutch 'wagen' or German 'Wagen', both meaning 'cart' or 'car'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwæɡən / (AmE), / ˈwæɡən / (BrE)
a car with a long body, a rear door, and an extended space for cargo behind the rear seats; also called a station wagon
car with large rear cargo area

Examples

They loaded their camping gear into the back of the wagon before heading out on vacation.
She prefers driving a wagon because of the extra space for groceries and luggage.
The family’s old wagon had enough room to carry bicycles and sports equipment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: station wagon - a car with an extended rear cargo area and rear door

Etymology

From earlier use referring to four-wheeled vehicles for transporting goods; automotive sense by extension.

3. NOUN

/ ˈwæɡən / (AmE), / ˈwæɡən / (BrE)
A police van used to transport prisoners.
police van for transporting prisoners

Examples

After the arrest, the suspects were loaded into the wagon for transport to the police station.
The old wagon pulled up outside the courthouse to collect the prisoners.
Police officers waited by the wagon while the detainees were escorted out of the building.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paddy wagon - a police van for transporting detainees or prisoners

4. NOUN

/ ˈwæɡən / (AmE), / ˈwæɡən / (BrE)
a vehicle with four wheels, usually pulled by animals or a tractor, used for transporting goods or people
wheeled vehicle for carrying goods or people

Examples

The farmer loaded hay onto the wagon and drove it back to the barn.
Tourists rode through the park in a horse-drawn wagon during the festival.
The old wagon creaked as it traveled down the dirt road.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cart - a small open vehicle with two or four wheels, typically pulled by a horse or a person

Etymology

From Dutch 'wagen' or German 'Wagen', both meaning 'vehicle', ultimately from Proto-Germanic.
