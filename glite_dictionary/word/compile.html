to compile

1. VERB

/ kəmˈpaɪl / (AmE), / kəmˈpaɪl / (BrE)
(computing) to use a computer program to translate source code written in a particular programming language into machine code that can be executed by a computer.
convert source code into executable machine code

Examples

You need to compile the program before running it on your computer.
The software failed to compile due to a missing semicolon in the code.
After editing the source file, she tried to compile it again to check for errors.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to interpret - to translate and execute source code line by line

Etymology

From Latin compilare (to plunder, copy), via French compiler; computing sense developed in the 20th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01630142-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kəmˈpaɪl / (AmE), / kəmˈpaɪl / (BrE)
to collect information or materials and arrange them in a list, book, report, or other form
put together information or materials

Examples

She was asked to compile a detailed report using data from various departments.
The editor will compile the articles into a comprehensive magazine issue.
It took several hours to compile a complete list of all the conference attendees.

Synset Examples

compile a list

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assemble - to put together different parts to form a whole
  • Synonym: gather - to collect things together

Etymology

From Latin compilare (to plunder, collect), via French compiler.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01629175-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ kəmˈpaɪl / (AmE), / kəmˈpaɪl / (BrE)
to collect information, documents, or materials from different sources and bring them together in a single place or book
gather and collect information

Examples

She spent years compiling data for her research project.
The editor will compile the essays into a single volume.
He compiled a list of local restaurants for tourists.

Synset Examples

I am accumulating evidence for the man's unfaithfulness to his wife
She is amassing a lot of data for her thesis
She rolled up a small fortune

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collect - to gather things together
  • Synonym: to assemble - to gather together in one place or group

Etymology

From Latin compilare (to plunder, compile), from com- (together) + pilare (to plunder).

Source: WordNet
