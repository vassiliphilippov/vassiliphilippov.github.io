First Marquess Cornwallis

1. PROPN

/ fɜrst mɑrˈkwɛs kɔrnˈwɑlɪs / (AmE), / fɜːst mɑːˈkwɪs kɔːnˈwɒlɪs / (BrE)
(1738–1805) British military commander and colonial administrator; led British forces in the American War of Independence and was defeated at Yorktown.
British commander in American War of Independence

Examples

First Marquess Cornwallis played a pivotal role in the final stages of the American War of Independence.
The British defeat at Yorktown under First Marquess Cornwallis marked the end of major combat operations in the American Revolution.
After the war, First Marquess Cornwallis went on to serve as governor-general of India.
