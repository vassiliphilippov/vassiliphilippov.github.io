to underdevelop

1. VERB

/ ˌʌndərdɪˈvɛlʌp / (AmE), / ˌʌndədəˈvɛləp / (BrE)
to process a photographic film or plate for less than the required time, or in an ineffective solution, or at too low a temperature, resulting in a faint or incomplete image
process film insufficiently in photography

Examples

If you underdevelop the negative, the picture will come out much too faint.
She accidentally underdeveloped the photographs, so they lacked contrast and detail.
Careless handling in the darkroom can cause you to underdevelop a roll of film.

Synset Examples

These photos are underdeveloped

Related Terms

  • Antonym: overdevelop - to process photographic film for too long or in too strong a solution
