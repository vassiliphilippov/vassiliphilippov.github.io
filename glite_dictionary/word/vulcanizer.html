vulcanizer

1. NOUN

/ ˈvʌl.kəˌnaɪ.zɚ / (AmE), / ˈvʌl.kə.naɪ.zə / (BrE)
a person who vulcanizes rubber to increase its strength and durability
person who vulcanizes rubber

Examples

The experienced vulcanizer repaired the damaged tire with great skill.
As a vulcanizer, she worked long hours in the factory treating rubber goods.
Every vulcanizer must understand the precise conditions needed to create strong, resilient rubber.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to vulcanize - treat rubber with sulfur to improve strength and elasticity

Etymology

From 'vulcanize', referring to Vulcan, the Roman god of fire, coined in the 19th century for the chemical process.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvʌlkəˌnaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈvʌl.kə.naɪ.zə / (BrE)
a machine or device used to vulcanize rubber by treating it with heat and chemicals to improve its strength and elasticity
device for vulcanizing rubber

Examples

The old factory still uses a vulcanizer to process natural rubber into durable tires.
After the rubber mixture cooled, it was placed in the vulcanizer for final treatment.
A malfunction in the vulcanizer halted production for several hours.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to vulcanize - to treat rubber with heat and chemicals to make it stronger

Etymology

From 'vulcanize', named after Vulcan, the Roman god of fire, plus '-er' for agent noun.
