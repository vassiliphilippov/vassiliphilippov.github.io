laryngospasm

1. NOUN

/ ləˈrɪŋɡəˌspæzəm / (AmE), / ləˈrɪŋɡəˌspazəm / (BrE)
A sudden, involuntary closure of the larynx that temporarily blocks the flow of air to the lungs.
sudden closure of the larynx
medical

Examples

The patient experienced a laryngospasm during surgery, requiring immediate intervention.
A severe laryngospasm can make it difficult or impossible to breathe for a short period.
Treatments for laryngospasm focus on relaxing the throat muscles and restoring normal breathing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bronchospasm - a sudden constriction of the muscles in the walls of the bronchioles

Etymology

From laryngo- (relating to the larynx) + spasm (sudden muscular contraction).
