bilious

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɪliəs / (AmE), / ˈbɪliəs / (BrE)
suffering from or affected by a liver disorder or gastric distress, or appearing as if one is; having a sickly, yellowish look.
suffering from liver or stomach disorder

Examples

After eating the spoiled food, he felt bilious and spent the night awake with nausea.
Her bilious complexion suggested she was unwell and possibly suffering from a stomach complaint.
He grew bilious after the rich meal, complaining of discomfort and queasiness.

Etymology

From Latin 'biliosus' (full of bile), from 'bilis' (bile).

3. ADJ

/ ˈbɪliəs / (AmE), / ˈbɪliəs / (BrE)
Feeling or showing a bad temper or irritability, especially as if affected by indigestion.
irritable or bad-tempered

Examples

His bilious remarks made the dinner conversation tense and uncomfortable.
She grew bilious after a long day of petty annoyances and disappointments.
The critic's bilious review insulted both the actors and their audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritable - easily annoyed or angered
  • Antonym: amiable - friendly and pleasant in manner

Etymology

From Latin 'biliosus' (full of bile), from 'bilis' (bile).
