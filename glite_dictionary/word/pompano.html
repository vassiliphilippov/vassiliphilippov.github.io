pompano

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːmpəˌnoʊ / (AmE), / ˈpɒmpənəʊ / (BrE)
The flesh of a pompano, a warm-water, fatty fish prized for its delicate flavor; also refers to the fish itself.
a warm-water fish, or its edible flesh

Examples

Grilled pompano is a popular dish in many coastal restaurants.
She bought fresh pompano at the seafood market for dinner.
The delicate taste of pompano pairs well with citrus-based sauces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mackerel - a type of edible, oily fish related in cooking and appearance

Etymology

From Spanish 'pámpano', originally referring to certain types of fish.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːmpəˌnoʊ / (AmE), / ˈpɒmpənəʊ / (BrE)
a deep-bodied marine fish found chiefly in warm waters of the western Atlantic and the Gulf of Mexico, valued as food
a type of food fish

Examples

The chef recommended the grilled pompano with a lemon butter sauce.
Fishermen in Florida often catch pompano near the shorelines in spring.
We enjoyed a delicious pompano fillet at the seaside restaurant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trevally - a similar deep-bodied fish, often confused with pompano

Etymology

From Spanish pámpano, originally 'vine tendril', later used for the fish due to its shape.
