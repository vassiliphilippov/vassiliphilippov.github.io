anguish

1. NOUN

/ ˈæŋɡwɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡwɪʃ / (BrE)
Severe mental or emotional pain or distress.
severe mental or emotional pain

Examples

Her face was marked by anguish after hearing the tragic news.
He struggled to hide his anguish during the difficult conversation.
The loss of his beloved dog filled him with deep anguish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agony - intense physical or mental suffering
  • Synonym: misery - a state of great suffering and unhappiness
  • Antonym: joy - a feeling of great pleasure and happiness

Etymology

From Old French 'anguisse', from Latin 'angustia' (tightness, distress), from 'angere' (to press tight).

3. VERB

/ ˈæŋɡwɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡwɪʃ / (BrE)
to cause someone severe mental or emotional pain; to make someone feel deep distress
cause severe emotional pain

Examples

The thought of losing her family deeply anguished her for months.
It would anguish me to let my friends down in such a critical moment.
The unfair accusations anguished him to the point of sleepless nights.

Synset Examples

It pains me to see my children not being taught well in school

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torment - to cause severe physical or mental suffering

4. VERB

/ ˈæŋɡwɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡwɪʃ / (BrE)
to suffer or cause to suffer intense physical or mental pain or distress
suffer intense pain or distress

Examples

He continued to anguish over the decision for weeks, unable to find peace.
The loss of her friend caused her to anguish night after night.
Watching the disaster unfold, he could only anguish helplessly from afar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to suffer - to experience or be subjected to pain or distress
  • Synonym: to agonize - to undergo great mental distress

Etymology

From Old French 'anguissier', from Latin 'angustia' (distress, narrowness) related to 'angere' (to strangle, distress).
