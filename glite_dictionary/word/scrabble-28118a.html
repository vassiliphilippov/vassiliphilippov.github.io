scrabble

1. NOUN

/ ˈskræb.əl / (AmE), / ˈskræb.əl / (BrE)
a rough or aimless drawing, usually made absentmindedly or without artistic purpose
rough or aimless drawing

Examples

She filled the margins of her notebook with little scrabbles during the long meeting.
He absentmindedly made a scrabble with his pen while talking on the phone.
A child's scrabble often covers more paper than their intended drawing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doodle - a rough or aimless drawing made while a person’s attention is otherwise occupied
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04162371-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈskræbəl / (AmE), / ˈskræbəl / (BrE)
to search for something by feeling with one's hands, especially in an awkward or hurried way
search or grope with hands

Examples

She scrabbled for her phone in the bottom of her bag.
In the darkness, he scrabbled along the wall to find the light switch.
The children scrabbled through the sand looking for shells.

Synset Examples

She groped for his keys in the dark

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grope - to search blindly or uncertainly with the hands

Etymology

Perhaps imitative of the sound of scratching, or related to Middle Dutch *schrabben* ('to scrape').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01317100-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈskræb.əl / (AmE), / ˈskræb.əl / (BrE)
to write or draw something quickly and carelessly, often making it hard to read
write hurriedly and carelessly

Examples

He scrabbled his signature at the bottom of the page before rushing off.
She scrabbled a few notes during the chaotic meeting.
The doctor scrabbled a prescription that was almost impossible to read.

Etymology

Origin unknown, but possibly related to Middle English 'scrabben' (to scratch or scrape).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01008284-v
Synset POS: v
