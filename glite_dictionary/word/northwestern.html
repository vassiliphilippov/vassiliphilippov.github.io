northwestern

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɔrθˈwɛstərn / (AmE), / ˌnɔːθˈwɛstən / (BrE)
of or relating to the northwestern part of the United States, generally including Washington, Oregon, Idaho, and sometimes Montana and Wyoming.
of the northwestern United States

Examples

Heavy rainfall is common in the northwestern states during the winter.
Many tech companies are based in the northwestern region of the country.
The forests of the northwestern United States are rich in biodiversity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: northwest - the northwestern part of a region, especially the United States
  • Antonym: southeastern - of or relating to the southeastern region

Etymology

From north + western, referring to direction and region.

2. ADJ

/ ˌnɔrθˈwɛstərn / (AmE), / ˌnɔːθˈwɛstən / (BrE)
Situated in or oriented toward the northwest.
situated in or facing the northwest

Examples

They settled in the northwestern corner of the state near the mountains.
The northwestern wind brought cooler temperatures throughout the region.
Many northwestern cities are known for their beautiful forests and frequent rain.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: northeastern - situated in or oriented toward the northeast
  • Antonym: southwestern - situated in or oriented toward the southwest
  • Synonym: northwest - the direction midway between north and west

Etymology

Formed from 'north' and 'west', with the adjectival suffix '-ern'.
