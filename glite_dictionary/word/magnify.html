to magnify

1. VERB

/ ˈmæɡ.nə.faɪ / (AmE), / ˈmæɡ.nɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to make something appear larger than it is, especially by using a lens or device
make something appear larger

Examples

The scientist used a microscope to magnify the tiny cells for detailed study.
You can magnify the photo on your phone by pinching outwards on the screen.
The lens will magnify the text, making it easier to read for people with poor eyesight.

Synset Examples

blow up an image

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enlarge - make something bigger or greater in size
  • Antonym: to reduce - make smaller or less in size, amount, or degree

Etymology

From Latin 'magnificare', from 'magnus' (great, large).

2. VERB

/ ˈmæɡ.nəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmæɡ.nɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to make something seem greater, more important, or more serious than it really is; to exaggerate
exaggerate; make something seem greater

Examples

It is easy to magnify minor issues until they seem overwhelming.
He tends to magnify his achievements when telling stories.
Anxiety can magnify your perception of potential problems.

Synset Examples

tended to romanticize and exaggerate this ‘gracious Old South’ imagery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exaggerate - to make something seem larger or more important than it really is
  • Antonym: to understate - to make something seem less important or smaller than it really is

Etymology

From Latin 'magnificare', from 'magnus' (great) + 'facere' (to make).

3. VERB

/ ˈmæɡ.nəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmæɡ.nɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to make something appear larger or more important than it really is; to increase the size, volume, or significance of something
make appear larger or more significant

Examples

The microscope can magnify tiny cells so that they are visible to the naked eye.
She tends to magnify her problems instead of putting them in perspective.
Reports in the media often magnify the significance of minor events.

Synset Examples

Her terror was magnified in her mind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enlarge - to make something bigger in size or amount
  • Synonym: to amplify - to increase the strength or intensity of something
  • Antonym: to minimize - to make something appear smaller or less important

Etymology

From Latin 'magnificare', from 'magnus' (great, large) + '-ficare' (to make).
