paratroops

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæɹəˌtruːps / (AmE), / ˈpæɹəˌtruːps / (BrE)
military units consisting of infantry soldiers trained and equipped to parachute from aircraft into combat or other operations
airborne infantry soldiers

Examples

The paratroops landed behind enemy lines under the cover of darkness.
During the battle, paratroops played a vital role in securing the bridge.
Many paratroops trained rigorously to prepare for their mission.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paratrooper - a soldier who is trained to parachute into an operation

Etymology

From 'parachute' and 'troops,' first used in the context of airborne operations in World War II.
