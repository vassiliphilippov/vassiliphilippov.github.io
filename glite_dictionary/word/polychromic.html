polychromic

1. ADJ

/ ˌpɑliˈkroʊmɪk / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈkrəʊmɪk / (BrE)
Having or exhibiting many colors; multicolored.
having many colors

Examples

The artist used polychromic tiles to create a vibrant mosaic mural.
Under the microscope, the mineral appears polychromic, showing a wide array of hues.
They marveled at the butterfly's polychromic wings as it fluttered through the garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polychromatic - having or exhibiting many colors

Etymology

From Greek polychrōmos (many-colored), from polys (many) + chrōma (color), with -ic forming the adjective.
