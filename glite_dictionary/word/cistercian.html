Cistercian

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈstɜrʃən / (AmE), / sɪˈstɜːʃən / (BrE)
a member of a Roman Catholic monastic order founded in 1098, known for its strict observance, austerity, and adherence to a rule of silence
member of a strict Catholic monastic order

Examples

The Cistercian monks dedicated their lives to prayer, manual labor, and simplicity.
She visited a historic Cistercian abbey while traveling in France.
Many Cistercian monasteries were built in remote rural areas to encourage solitude.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin Cisterciānus, from Cistercium (Latin name for Cîteaux, site of the original monastery in France).
