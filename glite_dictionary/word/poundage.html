poundage

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊndɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpaʊndɪdʒ / (BrE)
the total weight of something expressed in pounds
weight in pounds

Examples

The box's poundage was clearly marked on its side.
Please report the total poundage harvested this season.
Airlines may charge extra if your luggage exceeds a certain poundage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weight - the measure of how heavy something is

Etymology

From 'pound' (unit of weight) + suffix '-age' (denoting a related amount or fee).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊndɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpaʊndɪdʒ / (BrE)
the act of placing private property in the custody of a law officer, typically in legal proceedings
placing property in legal custody
legal

Examples

The court ordered the poundage of the defendant's car until the trial was over.
After a series of unpaid fines, poundage was carried out on his property by the authorities.
The law allows for the poundage of goods found to be in violation of import regulations.

Etymology

From 'pound' (enclosure for animals or property) + '-age' (denoting action or result); 'pound' from Old English 'pund'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊndɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpaʊndɪdʒ / (BrE)
a fee charged for the recovery of impounded animals
fee for recovering impounded animals

Examples

The farmer had to pay a significant poundage to retrieve his lost sheep.
If your pet is picked up by animal control, you may be charged a poundage.
The council increased the poundage for cattle to offset shelter maintenance costs.

Etymology

From 'pound' (place where animals are kept) + '-age', denoting a fee or charge.

4. NOUN

/ ˈpaʊndɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpaʊndɪdʒ / (BrE)
an amount of money charged or paid according to the weight measured in pounds, often as a fee or tax
charge based on weight in pounds

Examples

The shipping company applies a poundage to all international parcels based on their total weight.
Certain medieval taxes included a poundage on imported goods.
Farmers protested the increase in poundage for transporting produce.

Etymology

From pound (unit of weight) + -age (forming nouns denoting action or charge).
