to arborize

1. VERB

/ ˈɑːrbəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈɑːbərʌɪz / (BrE)
(of a structure, especially in anatomy or biology) to branch out or spread in a tree-like manner.
to branch out like a tree

Examples

The nerve fibers arborize into complex patterns within the brain.
Bronchioles arborize extensively as they reach the alveoli in the lungs.
The artist watched the roots arborize beneath the surface of the soil.

Synset Examples

nerve fibers arborize

Related Terms

  • Synonym: branch - to divide into separate parts or subdivisions

Etymology

From Latin arbor (tree) + -ize.
