tiller

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪlər / (AmE), / ˈtɪlə / (BrE)
a machine or tool used for breaking up and turning over soil in preparation for planting
garden tool for breaking up soil

Examples

The farmer used a tiller to prepare the field for planting.
After winter, the soil was compacted, so she rented a tiller for her garden.
A mechanical tiller saves time compared to manual digging in large plots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cultivator - a farm implement for loosening the soil and destroying weeds

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɪlər / (AmE), / ˈtɪlə / (BrE)
a person who tills or prepares the soil for planting crops
person who tills soil for planting

Examples

The tiller worked from dawn to dusk to prepare the fields for spring planting.
Many ancient societies depended on the labor of the tiller for their survival.
As a skilled tiller, she understood the importance of rotating crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farmer - a person who owns or manages a farm
  • Synonym: ploughman - a person who guides a plough

Etymology

From Old English 'tilian' (to cultivate, aim at).

3. VERB

/ ˈtɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɪlə / (BrE)
(of a plant) to produce new shoots, especially from the base, forming clumps or stools.
(of plants) produce new basal shoots

Examples

Wheat plants often tiller vigorously when growing conditions are favorable.
After fertilization, the grass began to tiller and spread across the field.
Certain varieties of barley tiller more, resulting in denser crops.

4. NOUN

/ ˈtɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɪlə / (BrE)
a shoot or stem that grows from the base of a grass plant, often forming part of a cluster
shoot at the base of a grass

Examples

Each tiller produces its own leaves and can develop into a separate plant.
Farmers often count the number of tillers to estimate crop yield.
Some grasses form many tillers to help them spread across the ground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shoot - a young branch or stem of a plant

5. NOUN

/ ˈtɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɪlə / (BrE)
a long handle or lever attached to the rudder of a boat, used for steering
lever for steering a boat

Examples

He held the tiller firmly as the boat navigated the narrow channel.
With a quick push on the tiller, she was able to change the direction of the yacht.
The old wooden tiller creaked as the rudder swung to starboard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: helm - the steering apparatus of a ship or boat

Etymology

From Old English *teler* (stalk, handle) and Middle English *teler* (stock of a crossbow), relating to something extended for control.
