eastward

1. ADV

/ ˈiːstwɚd / (AmE), / ˈiːstwəd / (BrE)
toward the east; in the direction of the east
toward the east

Examples

The birds travel eastward every spring in search of food.
The winds blew eastward across the open plains.
Explorers moved eastward to discover new lands.

Synset Examples

they migrated eastward to Sweden

Related Terms

  • Antonym: westward - toward the west
  • Antonym: northward - toward the north
  • Synonym: east - the direction toward the rising sun

Etymology

From Old English eastweard, from east + -ward (direction suffix).

2. ADJ

/ ˈistwɚd / (AmE), / ˈiːstwəd / (BrE)
moving or directed toward the east
moving toward the east

Examples

The eastward migration of the birds was observed every spring.
An eastward wind brought cool air from the ocean.
The train made an eastward journey across the state.

Synset Examples

eastbound trains

Related Terms

  • Antonym: westward - moving or directed toward the west
  • Synonym: eastbound - (of a vehicle or journey) going toward the east

Etymology

From Old English 'ēastweard', from 'ēast' (east) + '-weard' (toward).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00827124-s
Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ ˈistwərd / (AmE), / ˈiːstwəd / (BrE)
the direction or point on the compass that is at 90 degrees, corresponding to the east
direction at 90 degrees (east)

Examples

The ship set a course directly for eastward to reach the new continent.
Many ancient temples were built facing eastward to greet the rising sun.
A steady breeze was blowing from the eastward as we began our journey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: east - the cardinal direction at 90 degrees
  • Antonym: westward - the cardinal direction opposite to eastward

Etymology

From Old English 'ēastweard', from 'ēast' (east) + '-weard' (toward).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13854554-n
Synset POS: n
