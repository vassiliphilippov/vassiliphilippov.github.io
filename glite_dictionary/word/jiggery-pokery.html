jiggery-pokery

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɪɡəri ˈpoʊkəri / (AmE), / ˌdʒɪɡəri ˈpəʊkəri / (BrE)
dishonest or suspicious behavior meant to deceive or trick people
trickery or deceitful behavior

Examples

The auditors uncovered some jiggery-pokery with the accounts that had gone unnoticed for years.
Politicians are often accused of jiggery-pokery when campaign funds are mismanaged.
If you suspect any jiggery-pokery in the contract, it's wise to consult a lawyer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trickery - the practice of deception or fraud
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being honest or truthful

Etymology

Originated in late 19th century, probably a playful reduplication influenced by 'hokey-pokey.'
