Mahagauri

1. PROPN

/ mɑːhəˈɡɔːri / (AmE), / mɑːhəˈɡɔːri / (BrE)
In Hinduism, a goddess representing purity and auspiciousness, known as a benevolent form of Devi and revered for her shining beauty.
Hindu goddess of purity; form of Devi

Examples

During Navaratri, devotees worship Mahagauri as the goddess of purity and serenity.
Many legends describe Mahagauri as the radiant form of Parvati who removed all darkness and fear.
The eighth day of the festival honors Mahagauri for her compassion and brilliance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Devi - The principal goddess in Hinduism, representing the divine feminine power
  • Synonym: Parvati - A principal form of the goddess and wife of Shiva in Hinduism

Etymology

From Sanskrit महागौरी (mahāgaurī), meaning 'extremely fair' or 'brilliant', from mahā ('great') and gaurī ('fair, white')
