photalgia

1. NOUN

/ foʊˈtældʒiə / (AmE), / fəʊˈtaldʒɪə / (BrE)
Pain in the eye caused by exposure to bright light, often associated with conditions such as albinism.
eye pain from bright light
medical

Examples

The patient reported severe photalgia after being exposed to direct sunlight.
Wearing sunglasses can help reduce photalgia in people with sensitive eyes.
Those with albinism frequently experience photalgia due to the lack of pigment in their eyes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: photophobia - extreme sensitivity to light causing discomfort or pain in the eyes

Etymology

From Greek 'phōs' (light) + 'algos' (pain).
