erasure

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈreɪʒər / (AmE), / ɪˈreɪʃə / (BrE)
A mark or area on a surface where something has been erased or removed.
area where something was erased

Examples

He could see the erasure on the page where the answer had been changed.
The document showed clear signs of erasure near the signature line.
A faint erasure was visible beneath the newly written date.

Synset Examples

another word had been written over the erasure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deletion - the removal of written or printed matter

Etymology

From Latin 'erasura', from 'eradere' (to scrape out).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈreɪʒər / (AmE), / ɪˈreɪʃə / (BrE)
a correction or mark made by erasing something, especially in a written or printed text
a correction made by erasing

Examples

Due to several erasures on the test paper, the teacher asked for a clean copy.
The old manuscript had many erasures and corrections throughout its pages.
You should avoid making too many erasures on official documents.

Synset Examples

there were many erasures in the typescript

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deletion - the removal of written, printed, or recorded material
  • Antonym: insertion - the action of adding something to text

Etymology

From Latin 'erasure', from 'eradere' meaning 'to scrape out'.

3. NOUN

/ ɪˈreɪʒɚ / (AmE), / ɪˈreɪʒə / (BrE)
the act of removing or deleting something, especially from a document or record
the act of deleting or removing

Examples

The erasure of several paragraphs significantly altered the meaning of the letter.
Digital erasure of files does not always guarantee complete removal from the system.
Historians lament the erasure of cultural traditions over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deletion - the process of removing or eliminating something
  • Antonym: insertion - the act of adding something, especially into a document or record

Etymology

From Latin 'erasure' via French 'érasure', from 'erasure' (to erase).
