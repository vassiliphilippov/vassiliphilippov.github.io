weed

1. NOUN

/ wid / (AmE), / wiːd / (BrE)
A black band worn on the arm or hat as a sign of mourning, especially by men.
mourning band worn on arm or hat
archaic

Examples

After his father's death, he wore a weed on his sleeve for several months.
In Victorian times, a weed was a common symbol of mourning among gentlemen.
The black weed on his hat told everyone of his recent loss.

Etymology

From Old English 'wæd' (garment), related to wear.

3. NOUN

/ wid / (AmE), / wiːd / (BrE)
A wild plant growing where it is not wanted and in competition with cultivated plants.
unwanted plant competing with crops

Examples

After a few weeks, weeds began to take over the once-pristine vegetable garden.
The farmer spent hours pulling weeds to protect his crops from being choked out.
Crabgrass is a common weed that thrives in lawns and competes with grass.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: herb - plant used for flavoring or medicine, often not unwanted
  • Synonym: pest - destructive organism competing with desirable plants

Etymology

Old English wēod (grass, herb, weed).
