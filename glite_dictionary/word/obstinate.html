obstinate

1. ADJ

/ ˈɑb.stə.nət / (AmE), / ˈɒb.stɪ.nət / (BrE)
refusing to change one's opinion or chosen course of action, despite attempts to persuade one to do so; stubborn.
stubborn; refusing to yield

Examples

Even when proven wrong, he remained obstinate in his beliefs.
The obstinate child refused to eat any vegetables.
Negotiations stalled due to the obstinate attitude of both parties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stubborn - unreasonably or perversely unyielding
  • Antonym: flexible - willing to change or compromise

Etymology

From Latin obstinatus, past participle of obstinare (to persist).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɑb.stə.nət / (AmE), / ˈɒb.stɪ.nət / (BrE)
unreasonably stubborn, especially in refusing to change one's behavior or opinion despite persuasion; stubbornly persistent in wrongdoing
stubbornly refusing to change or relent

Examples

Despite everyone's advice, he remained obstinate and refused to apologize.
Her obstinate attitude made it difficult to reach any agreement.
The child was obstinate in his refusal to eat vegetables.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stubborn - unwilling to change one's mind or actions; difficult to move or remove
  • Antonym: compliant - willing to agree with others or obey rules

Etymology

From Latin 'obstinatus', past participle of 'obstinare' (to persist, be stubborn).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01964522-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈɑb.stə.nət / (AmE), / ˈɒb.stɪ.nət / (BrE)
refusing to change one's opinion or behavior despite attempts to persuade or discipline; stubbornly resistant.
stubbornly refusing to change; resistant to guidance

Examples

The obstinate child refused to apologize, no matter how many times his parents asked.
Despite the team's suggestions, she remained obstinate in her opinion.
His obstinate refusal to listen frustrated everyone at the meeting.

Synset Examples

Mary, Mary, quite contrary
an obstinate child with a violent temper
a perverse mood
wayward behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stubborn - unwilling to change one's attitude or position; synonym for obstinate
  • Antonym: pliant - easily influenced or guided; opposite of obstinate

Etymology

From Latin obstinatus, past participle of obstinare (to persist).
