adience

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪdiəns / (AmE), / ˈædɪəns / (BrE)
(psychology) an urge or tendency to approach or accept a situation, object, or stimulus, rather than avoiding it.
psychological tendency to approach or accept
medical

Examples

Children with strong adience are eager to explore new environments.
The psychologist explained that adience plays a key role in social interactions.
Therapy can help balance adience and avoidance behaviors.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: abience - urge to avoid or withdraw from a situation or object (psychology)

Etymology

From Latin 'ad-' (toward) + 'ientia' (going), modeled after 'abience'.
