jumpsuit

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌmpˌsuːt / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌmp.suːt / (BrE)
a one-piece garment that covers the body and legs, typically worn as outerwear and originally styled after a parachutist's uniform
one-piece outer garment

Examples

She wore a stylish jumpsuit to the party instead of a traditional dress.
Mechanics often choose a jumpsuit for its practicality and comfort while working.
The fashion designer unveiled a new collection featuring brightly colored jumpsuits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overalls - a garment covering the body and legs, usually with a bib and shoulder straps

Etymology

Formed from 'jump' and 'suit', originally referencing parachutists' uniforms.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌmp.suːt / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌmp.suːt / (BrE)
a one-piece garment with sleeves and legs, originally worn by parachutists but now also used as casual or fashionable attire
one-piece garment with sleeves and legs

Examples

The skydiver put on his jumpsuit before boarding the plane.
During the 1940s, the jumpsuit became popular beyond its military use.
She wore a bright orange jumpsuit that made her easy to spot in the crowd.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overall - a loose-fitting one-piece work garment covering the body

Etymology

From 'jump' + 'suit', originally referring to attire for parachutists (jumping from planes).

3. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌmpˌsut / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌmp.suːt / (BrE)
a one-piece garment covering the body and legs, typically made in a style similar to a parachutist's uniform, often worn for fashion or practical purposes
one-piece garment covering body and legs

Examples

She wore a stylish jumpsuit to the party instead of a dress.
The mechanic changed into a blue jumpsuit before starting work.
Fashion designers released a new collection of denim jumpsuits this season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overalls - a one-piece protective garment; similar protective or casual clothing

Etymology

From 'jump' (as in parachute jump) + 'suit', originally referring to parachutists' uniforms.
