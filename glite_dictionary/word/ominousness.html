ominousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑː.mə.nəs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈɒ.mɪ.nəs.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of suggesting that something bad or unpleasant is going to happen; a sense of being threatening, portentous, or inauspicious.
quality of being threatening or portentous

Examples

The ominousness of the dark clouds made everyone hurry indoors.
There was a certain ominousness in his tone that made us uneasy.
Critics praised the ominousness of the film's soundtrack, which built suspense throughout.

Synset Examples

Hewitt introduces some notes of dissonance, emphasizing the ominousness of the movie's befuddled hero's situation, along with his sense of disconnection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ominous - giving the impression that something bad is going to happen
  • Antonym: auspiciousness - the quality of being conducive to success; favorable

Etymology

From 'ominous' + '-ness'; 'ominous' from Latin 'omen' (sign, token).
