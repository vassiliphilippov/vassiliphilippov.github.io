pye-dog

1. NOUN

/ paɪ dɔg / (AmE), / paɪ dɒg / (BrE)
An ownerless, half-wild mongrel dog commonly found in Asian villages, especially in India.
ownerless mongrel dog in Asia, esp. India

Examples

In rural India, you might see a pye-dog wandering near the village market.
The pye-dog survived by scavenging scraps left behind by the locals.
Animal welfare groups occasionally rescue a pye-dog from the streets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pariah dog - an ownerless, semi-feral dog found in India and other parts of Asia

Etymology

From Hindi 'pāhī' (outsider, stranger) + English 'dog'.
