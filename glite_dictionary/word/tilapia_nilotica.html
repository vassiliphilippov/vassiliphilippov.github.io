tilapia nilotica

1. NOUN

/ tɪˌleɪpiə naɪˈlɑtɪkə / (AmE), / tɪˌleɪpɪə nɪˈlɒtɪkə / (BrE)
an important species of tilapia native to the Nile and other rivers in Africa and Asia Minor, widely cultivated for food
Nile tilapia fish species

Examples

Tilapia nilotica is commonly farmed due to its rapid growth and high yield.
Fishermen along the Nile have harvested tilapia nilotica for centuries.
The nutritional value of tilapia nilotica makes it a popular choice in many cuisines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tilapia - any of several species of freshwater fish in the tilapia family

Etymology

From New Latin, with 'tilapia' from African roots and 'nilotica' referring to the Nile River.
