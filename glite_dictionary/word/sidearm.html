sidearm

1. ADJ

/ ˈsaɪdˌɑrm / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdˌɑːm / (BrE)
(of a throw or pitch) made with the arm moving parallel to the ground, rather than above the shoulder.
with arm moving parallel to the ground

Examples

The pitcher threw a sidearm curveball that surprised the batter.
Her sidearm delivery gave the softball extra spin and speed.
Some players find it easier to make accurate throws with a sidearm motion.

Synset Examples

a sidearm pitch
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03156397-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADV

/ ˈsaɪdˌɑrm / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdˌɑːm / (BrE)
(in sports) with the arm moving in a horizontal, sideways motion, rather than overhand or underhand.
with a sideways arm motion (esp. in sports)

Examples

The pitcher threw the ball sidearm, surprising the batter with an unusual angle.
Many softball players prefer to deliver the ball sidearm for better control.
He managed to flick the frisbee sidearm across the field to his teammate.

Synset Examples

he prefers to throw sidearm
