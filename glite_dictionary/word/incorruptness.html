incorruptness

1. NOUN

/ ɪn.kəˈrʌpt.nəs / (AmE), / ɪn.kəˈrʌpt.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being morally upright and not susceptible to bribery or corruption
state of being incorrupt; integrity

Examples

Her incorruptness was admired by all who knew her.
The committee valued incorruptness above all other qualities in its members.
Despite the challenges, he maintained his incorruptness throughout his career.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: integrity - the quality of being honest and having strong moral principles
  • Antonym: corruption - dishonest or fraudulent conduct by those in power

Etymology

From Latin 'incorruptus' (unspoiled, uncorrupted) + English suffix '-ness'.
