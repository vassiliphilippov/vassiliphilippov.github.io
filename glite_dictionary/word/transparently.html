transparently

1. ADV

/ trænˈsper.ənt.li / (AmE), / trænˈspær.ənt.li / (BrE)
In a way that allows light to pass through so that objects behind can be seen clearly; in a transparent manner.
in a transparent manner

Examples

Sunlight filtered transparently through the thin white curtains, illuminating the room softly.
The water in the glass bowl shone transparently under the kitchen lights.
A sheet of paper held up transparently against the window clearly revealed the shape of the object behind it.

Synset Examples

the red brilliance of the claret shines transparently in our glasses

Related Terms

  • Antonym: opaquely - in a way that does not allow light to pass through; antonym of 'transparently'

Etymology

From 'transparent' (from Latin 'trans-' (through) + 'parere' (to appear)) + '-ly'.

2. ADV

/ trænˈsperəntli / (AmE), / trænˈspærəntli / (BrE)
in a manner that is easy to understand or see through; plainly or obviously
in an obvious or easily understood way

Examples

She explained the process transparently, leaving no room for confusion.
His motives were transparently selfish, as everyone could see.
The company has dealt with its clients transparently to maintain their trust.

Synset Examples

his transparently lucid prose
his transparently deceitful behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obviously - in a way that is easily perceived or understood
  • Antonym: opaquely - in a way that is not easily understood or seen through

Etymology

Formed from 'transparent' + '-ly', from Latin 'trans-' (through) + 'parere' (to appear).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00482882-r
Synset POS: r
