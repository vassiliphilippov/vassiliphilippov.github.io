contrarily

1. ADV

/ kənˈtrɛrəli / (AmE), / kənˈtreərəli / (BrE)
In a manner that is opposite or opposed to what is expected or stated; on the contrary.
in an opposite or unexpected manner

Examples

She expected him to be upset, but contrarily, he seemed relieved by the news.
The weather forecast predicted rain; contrarily, it was sunny all day.
Most students accepted the policy change, but several spoke contrarily at the meeting.

Synset Examples

he didn't stay home; on the contrary, he went out with his friends

Related Terms

  • Synonym: on the contrary - used to introduce a statement that says the opposite of the previous statement

Etymology

From 'contrary' plus the adverbial suffix '-ly'. 'Contrary' ultimately from Latin 'contrarius' (opposite).

2. ADV

/ kənˈtrɛrɪli / (AmE), / kənˈtrɛːrɪli / (BrE)
In a manner that is opposite, opposing, or disobedient; in an opposing or contrary way.
in an opposing or disobedient manner

Examples

She acted contrarily to her teacher's instructions, refusing to complete the assignment.
Even when everyone agreed, he often argued contrarily just for the sake of it.
The child looked at her mother and contrarily did exactly the opposite of what she was told.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oppositely - in a manner that is opposite
  • Antonym: obediently - in a manner that is compliant or obeying orders

Etymology

From 'contrary' + '-ly', ultimately from Latin 'contrarius' (opposite).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00249448-r
Synset POS: r
