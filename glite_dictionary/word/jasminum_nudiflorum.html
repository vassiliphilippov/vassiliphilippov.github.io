Jasminum nudiflorum

1. NOUN

/ ˈjæzmɪnəm ˌnjuːdɪˈflɔrəm / (AmE), / ˈjæzmɪnəm ˌnjuːdɪˈflɔːrəm / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub native to China, widely grown for its yellow flowers that bloom in winter.
winter-blooming yellow-flowered shrub

Examples

The Jasminum nudiflorum in my garden blooms even when there's snow on the ground.
Gardeners often plant Jasminum nudiflorum for early color in winter months.
Cutting branches of Jasminum nudiflorum brings cheerful yellow flowers indoors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winter jasmine - common name for Jasminum nudiflorum

Etymology

From Latin: 'Jasminum' meaning jasmine, and 'nudiflorum' meaning 'with naked flowers'.
