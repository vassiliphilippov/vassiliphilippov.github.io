cholesterin

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɛstərɪn / (AmE), / kəˈlɛstərɪn / (BrE)
An animal sterol produced in the liver, identical to cholesterol; the most abundant steroid in animal tissues.
archaic term for cholesterol
medical archaic

Examples

The chemist analyzed the sample for cholesterin content to understand its composition.
Older medical texts often refer to cholesterol as cholesterin.
Early researchers noted that cholesterin is present in brain tissue and nerve cells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cholesterol - a sterol found in animal tissues and blood plasma

Etymology

From Greek 'chole' (bile) + 'stereos' (solid) + '-in'.
