occupation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑː.kjəˈpeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɒk.jʊˈpeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a job or profession; the principal activity that a person does to earn a living
a person's job or profession

Examples

She has worked in the medical occupation for over twenty years.
Please state your occupation on the application form.
Teaching is a very demanding occupation that requires patience and dedication.

Synset Examples

he's not in my line of business

Related Terms

  • Synonym: profession - A paid occupation, especially one that requires advanced education and training
  • Synonym: vocation - A person's employment or main occupation
  • Antonym: unemployment - The state of not having a paid job

Etymology

From Latin 'occupatio' (a taking possession, business, employment), via Old French.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑːkjəˈpeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɒkjʊˈpeɪʃən / (BrE)
an activity that occupies a person's time or attention
activity that occupies one's attention

Examples

His main occupation during the holidays was reading science fiction novels.
She was so deep in her occupation with the crossword puzzle that she didn't hear the phone ring.
Gardening became a peaceful occupation for him after retirement.

Synset Examples

he missed the bell in his occupation with the computer game

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pastime - an activity done for enjoyment in one's spare time
  • Synonym: distraction - something that takes your attention away
  • Synonym: preoccupation - the state of being absorbed in thought or activity

Etymology

From Latin occupatio (a taking possession, business, employment).

3. NOUN

/ ˌɑːkjəˈpeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɒkjʊˈpeɪʃən / (BrE)
the period during which a place, position, or country is controlled or taken over by a foreign power or force
Control period by occupying force

Examples

The city struggled to maintain daily life during the occupation by enemy forces.
Many civilians fled their homes at the onset of the foreign occupation.
After the long occupation, the region finally regained its independence.

Synset Examples

during the German occupation of Paris

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: invasion - an instance of invading a country or region with an armed force
  • Degree Higher: annexation - the act of adding territory by conquest or occupation

Etymology

From Latin occupatio (a taking possession), from occupare (to seize, occupy).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-15166446-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˌɑː.kjəˈpeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɒk.jʊˈpeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the control of a country or region by military forces of a foreign power
military control of territory

Examples

During the Second World War, the occupation of France lasted for four years.
The country's economy suffered greatly under foreign occupation.
After a decade of military occupation, the region was finally liberated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invasion - the act of entering a place by force, often marking the beginning of military occupation
  • Antonym: liberation - the act of setting a country or region free from occupation

Etymology

From Latin occupatio (a taking possession), from occupare (to seize).
