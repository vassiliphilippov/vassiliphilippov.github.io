craftiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkræft̬.i.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkrɑːft.i.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being clever and skilled at achieving one's aims, often by deceit or trickery
cleverness, often involving deceit

Examples

Her craftiness allowed her to outsmart her opponents at every turn.
The detective admired the craftiness with which the thief covered his tracks.
Despite his craftiness, he was eventually caught by the authorities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cunning - cleverness or skill in achieving aims by deceit
  • Synonym: guile - sly or cunning intelligence
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and free from deceit

Etymology

From Middle English 'crafty' (skillful, cunning) + '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkræft.i.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkrɑːft.i.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being clever at achieving one's aims by indirect or deceitful methods
clever and often deceitful quality

Examples

The fox was known for its craftiness in avoiding traps set by hunters.
Her craftiness allowed her to negotiate a better deal than anyone expected.
Many admired the politician's craftiness, though some questioned his honesty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guile - sly or cunning intelligence
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being honest or truthful
  • Synonym: cunning - skill in achieving one's ends by deceit

Etymology

From 'crafty' + '-ness'. 'Crafty' from Old English 'cræftig' (strong, skilled).
