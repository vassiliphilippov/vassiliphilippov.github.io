revolutionariness

1. NOUN

/ rɪˌvɑːluːˈʃɛneri.nɪs / (AmE), / rɪˌvɒljʊˈʃɛnərɪnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being revolutionary, especially in ideas, actions, or tendencies toward significant change.
quality of being revolutionary

Examples

The revolutionariness of her proposals shocked even the most open-minded members of the board.
Historians debated the revolutionariness of the reforms introduced during that period.
He admired the revolutionariness of the art movement for breaking away from tradition.

Synset Examples

It is a short step from the natural pre-revolutionariness of one who creates scandal (with his life or with his work; and better if with his life through his work) to revolutionariness proper, and it is solely dependent on the real outside circumstances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revolutionary - involving or causing a complete or dramatic change; radically new or innovative

Etymology

Formed from 'revolutionary' + '-ness', indicating a quality or state.
