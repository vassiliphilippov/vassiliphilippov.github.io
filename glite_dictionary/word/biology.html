biology

1. NOUN

/ baɪˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / baɪˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the plant and animal life of a particular region
plant and animal life of a region

Examples

The unique biology of the island has fascinated scientists for decades.
Pollution can severely impact the biology of river ecosystems.
The biology of the Amazon rainforest is incredibly rich and diverse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fauna - the animals of a particular region
  • Synonym: flora - the plants of a particular region

Etymology

From Greek bios (life) + -logia (study, discourse).

2. NOUN

/ baɪˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / baɪˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The science that studies living organisms, including their structure, function, growth, evolution, and distribution.
study of living organisms

Examples

She has always been interested in biology and plans to become a scientist.
During the biology class, we learned about cell structure and genetics.
Advances in biology have led to new medical treatments and discoveries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zoology - the study of animals
  • Synonym: botany - the scientific study of plants

Etymology

From Greek bios (life) + -logia (study, discourse).
