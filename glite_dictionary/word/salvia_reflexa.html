Salvia reflexa

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæl.vi.ə rɪˈflɛk.sə / (AmE), / ˈsal.vɪ.ə rɪˈflɛk.sə / (BrE)
A species of sage native to western North America and Central America, known for its violet-blue flowers and common use in cultivation.
a sage species with violet-blue flowers

Examples

The garden included several Salvia reflexa plants in full bloom.
Botanists identified the wildflower as Salvia reflexa during their field expedition.
Many gardeners cultivate Salvia reflexa for its attractive, violet-blue flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sage - any plant of the genus Salvia, especially the common culinary herb

Etymology

From Latin: 'Salvia' meaning sage and 'reflexa' meaning bent back or turned back, describing the flower's shape.
