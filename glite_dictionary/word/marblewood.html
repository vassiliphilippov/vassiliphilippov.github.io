marblewood

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːrbəlˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈmɑːbəlˌwʊd / (BrE)
A hard wood with a marbled or variegated appearance, often used for decorative purposes.
hard wood with marbled appearance

Examples

The craftsman selected marblewood to create a beautifully patterned jewelry box.
Due to its unique grain, marblewood is prized by furniture makers.
Collectors admire the distinct swirling designs found in marblewood.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːrbəlˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈmɑːbəlˌwʊd / (BrE)
a large tree of Asia with hard, marbled wood, sometimes known as zebrawood
Asiatic tree with hard, marbled wood

Examples

The furniture was crafted from marblewood, giving it a striking and unique appearance.
Botanists traveled to Southeast Asia to study the rare marblewood tree in its natural habitat.
Because of its durability, marblewood is highly valued for making fine veneers and decorative items.

Etymology

From marble (referring to the wood's veined pattern) + wood.
