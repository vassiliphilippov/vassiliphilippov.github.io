Podargidae

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈdɑrdʒɪdiː / (AmE), / pəʊˈdɑːdʒɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of nocturnal birds known as frogmouths, found in parts of Asia and Australia, characterized by wide, flattened beaks.
family of birds called frogmouths

Examples

The Podargidae are best known for their excellent camouflage among tree branches.
Researchers documented three species of Podargidae in the rainforest during their expedition.
Unlike owls, Podargidae rely on stillness and mimicry to avoid predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frogmouth - nocturnal birds with wide beaks, members of Podargidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'podargos' (swift-footed), with zoological family suffix '-idae'.

2. NOUN

/ poʊˈdɑːrdʒɪdi / (AmE), / pəˈdɑːdʒɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of nocturnal birds known as frogmouths, native to Australia and Southeast Asia, characterized by wide gaping mouths and cryptic plumage.
family of birds known as frogmouths

Examples

The Podargidae are well known for their excellent camouflage among tree branches.
Members of the Podargidae family are commonly referred to as frogmouths because of the shape of their beaks.
Researchers in Australia often observe Podargidae nesting quietly in dense forest habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frogmouth - a nocturnal bird with a wide, frog-like mouth

Etymology

From New Latin Podargidae, based on the type genus Podargus, from Ancient Greek 'podargos' (swift-footed).
