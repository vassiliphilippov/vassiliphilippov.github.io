forbearance

1. NOUN

/ fɔrˈberəns / (AmE), / fɔːˈbeərəns / (BrE)
the quality of being patient and able to control oneself when faced with provocation, delay, or incompetence; patience and restraint
patience and self-control

Examples

She exercised great forbearance in dealing with repeated mistakes from her colleague.
The teacher's forbearance with the restless students was impressive.
Through years of hardship, he maintained a remarkable forbearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patience - the capacity to accept or tolerate delay or suffering without getting angry
  • Antonym: impatience - lack of patience; restlessness due to delay or discomfort

Etymology

From Old English 'forberan' (to endure), influenced by Latin 'forbearantia'.

2. NOUN

/ fɔrˈberəns / (AmE), / fɔːˈbeərəns / (BrE)
the quality of being patient and self-controlled, especially when provoked or forced to wait; the act of refraining from exercising a legal right, such as enforcing a debt
patient self-control; refraining from action

Examples

She showed remarkable forbearance during the long legal proceedings.
The lender granted a period of forbearance on the loan repayment.
His forbearance in the face of constant criticism earned him respect from his colleagues.

Synset Examples

his forbearance to reply was alarming

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patience - the capacity to accept or tolerate delay or trouble
  • Antonym: impatience - lack of patience or tolerance
  • Synonym: restraint - the action of keeping something under control

Etymology

From Old English 'forberan' (to bear, endure, abstain). Influenced by Latin 'forbearantia' (endurance).
