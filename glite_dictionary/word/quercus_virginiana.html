Quercus virginiana

1. PROPN

/ ˈkwərkəs ˌvɜrdʒɪˈneɪnə / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs ˌvɜːdʒɪˈneɪnɪə / (BrE)
A medium-sized evergreen oak tree native to the southeastern United States and eastern Mexico, commonly known as the live oak, valued for its broad, spreading crown and very hard, durable wood.
species of oak tree (live oak) native to southeastern US

Examples

The Quercus virginiana is famous for its arching branches and dense canopy.
Many southern landscapes feature the majestic Quercus virginiana as a shade tree.
Shipbuilders once favored Quercus virginiana for its exceptionally strong timber.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: live oak - common name for Quercus virginiana

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Quercus' (oak) and species name 'virginiana' (of Virginia, referring to the region where first described).
