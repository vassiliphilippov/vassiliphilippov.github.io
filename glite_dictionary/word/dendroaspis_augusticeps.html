Dendroaspis augusticeps

1. NOUN

/ dɛn.droʊˈæspɪs ˌɔɡ.ɪˈtiː.sɛps / (AmE), / dɛn.drəʊˈaspɪs ˌɔːɡ.ɪˈtiː.sɛps / (BrE)
A highly venomous species of mamba native to southern Africa, known for its speed and readiness to bite.
southern African venomous mamba snake

Examples

The Dendroaspis augusticeps is notorious for its agility and potent venom.
Herpetologists often study the Dendroaspis augusticeps due to its dangerous nature.
Encounters with a Dendroaspis augusticeps can be fatal without prompt medical treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mamba - a genus of highly venomous snakes found in Africa

Etymology

From Greek dendron (tree) + aspis (shield, used for 'asp, snake'), and Latin augustus (majestic) + -ceps (headed).
