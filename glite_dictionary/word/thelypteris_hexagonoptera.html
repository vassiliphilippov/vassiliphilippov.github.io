Thelypteris hexagonoptera

1. NOUN

/ θəˌlɪptəɹɪs ˌhɛksəɡoʊˈnɒptərə / (AmE), / θɛlˌɪptərɪs ˌhɛksəɡəʊˈnɒptərə / (BrE)
A species of beech fern native to North American woodlands, characterized by straw-colored stems and fronds.
a North American beech fern species

Examples

Thelypteris hexagonoptera thrives in shady, moist forests throughout eastern North America.
The fronds of Thelypteris hexagonoptera are noted for their delicate structure and straw-colored stripes.
Botanists identified several specimens of Thelypteris hexagonoptera during their woodland survey.

Etymology

From New Latin, Thelypteris (a fern genus) and Greek hexagonos (six-angled) + pteron (wing/fern).
