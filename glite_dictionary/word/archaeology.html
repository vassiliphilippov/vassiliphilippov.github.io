archaeology

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑrkiˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌɑːkiˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the scientific study of past human life and culture by examining material remains such as artifacts, structures, and other physical evidence
the study of past human life and cultures

Examples

She decided to study archaeology after discovering ancient pottery on her family's land.
New breakthroughs in archaeology have helped reveal how prehistoric people lived.
The professor has written several books about the methods used in archaeology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anthropology - the study of humans, human behavior, and societies in the past and present

Etymology

From Greek arkhaiologia (‘discussion of ancient things’), from arkhaios (‘ancient’) + -logia (‘study of’).
