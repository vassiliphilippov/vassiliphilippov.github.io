myrtle

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɝːtəl / (AmE), / ˈmɜːtl̩ / (BrE)
A small evergreen shrub with dark green, glossy leaves and usually blue-violet or white flowers, often grown as a ground cover.
evergreen shrub with shiny leaves and blue-violet flowers

Examples

We planted a row of myrtle along the walkway for its lush appearance.
The myrtle was in full bloom, displaying delicate blue-violet flowers.
Gardeners often use myrtle as groundcover in shaded areas.

Etymology

From Latin myrtus (myrtle), from Ancient Greek myrtos.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɜrtəl / (AmE), / ˈmɜːtəl / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub or small tree of the genus Myrtus, with aromatic leaves and white or pinkish flowers, often grown as an ornamental plant
evergreen shrub of the genus Myrtus

Examples

The myrtle in her garden blooms beautifully every spring.
Ancient Greeks considered the myrtle a symbol of love and immortality.
He picked a sprig of myrtle and placed it in a vase on the table.

Etymology

From Latin myrtus (the myrtle tree), from Ancient Greek myrtos.
