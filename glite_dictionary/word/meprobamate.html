meprobamate

1. NOUN

/ məˈproʊbəˌmeɪt / (AmE), / mɛˈprɒbəmɛɪt / (BrE)
A drug used as a sedative and tranquilizer, mainly to treat anxiety and muscle tension.
sedative and anti-anxiety drug
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed meprobamate to help manage her anxiety symptoms.
Because of its calming effects, meprobamate was popular in the 1950s and 1960s.
Patients are advised not to operate machinery while taking meprobamate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tranquilizer - a drug used to reduce tension or anxiety

Etymology

From 'methyl' + 'propyl' + 'carbamate', referring to its chemical composition.
