Cabomba

1. NOUN

/ kəˈbɒmbə / (AmE), / kəˈbɒmbə / (BrE)
A genus of American aquatic plants with finely divided submerged leaves, often cultivated in aquariums.
genus of aquatic plants

Examples

The aquarium was decorated with lush Cabomba to create a natural habitat for the fish.
Some gardeners use Cabomba as an ornamental plant in outdoor ponds.
The delicate leaves of Cabomba provide excellent shelter for aquatic animals.

Etymology

From Latinized form of an indigenous Brazilian (Tupi-Guarani) name for the plant.

2. NOUN

/ kəˈbɑːmbə / (AmE), / kəˈbɒmbə / (BrE)
A small genus of American aquatic plants, sometimes classified in the water lily family (Nymphaeaceae), known for their finely divided submerged leaves and floating flowers.
a genus of American aquatic plants

Examples

The aquarium was decorated with several stems of Cabomba for the fish to hide in.
Many gardeners choose Cabomba because it thrives in both ponds and aquariums.
Botanists debated whether Cabomba should be placed in the water lily family or its own family.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, possibly from an indigenous Brazilian name.
