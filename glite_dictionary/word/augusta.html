Augusta

1. PROPN

/ əˈɡʌstə / (AmE), / ɔːˈɡʌstə / (BrE)
the capital city of the state of Maine, United States
capital of the U.S. state of Maine

Examples

Augusta is known for its rich history and picturesque location on the Kennebec River.
The Maine State Museum is located in Augusta, attracting visitors from across the state.
Legislators gathered in Augusta to debate new education policies.

Etymology

Named after Augusta Dearborn, daughter of American statesman Henry Dearborn.

2. PROPN

/ əˈɡʌstə / (AmE), / ɔːˈɡʌstə / (BrE)
a city in eastern Georgia, USA, famous for hosting major golf tournaments, particularly The Masters.
city in Georgia, USA; site of major golf tournaments

Examples

Every spring, thousands of fans travel to Augusta to watch The Masters golf tournament.
The historic downtown district of Augusta boasts beautiful architecture and lively cultural events.
After moving to Augusta, she quickly became involved in the local community.

Etymology

Named from Princess Augusta of Saxe-Gotha, mother of King George III of Britain.
