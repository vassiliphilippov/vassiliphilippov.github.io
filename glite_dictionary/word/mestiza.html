mestiza

1. NOUN

/ meˈstiːzə / (AmE), / mɛˈstiːzə / (BrE)
A woman of mixed racial ancestry, especially of European and Native American descent, chiefly in Latin America.
woman of mixed (European and Native American) ancestry

Examples

The legendary figure La Malinche is often identified as a mestiza in Mexican history.
As a proud mestiza, she embraces both her Spanish and Indigenous heritage.
The novel's protagonist is a young mestiza navigating the complexities of multicultural identity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mestizo - man of mixed (European and Native American) ancestry

Etymology

Feminine form of Spanish 'mestizo' from Latin 'mixticius' (mixed).
