monotone

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑː.nəˌtoʊn / (AmE), / ˈmɒn.ə.təʊn / (BrE)
a way of speaking or singing in which the tone or pitch does not change, making it sound dull or unvaried
unchanging tone of voice

Examples

The lecturer's monotone made it difficult for the students to stay awake.
She tried to mask her excitement, but her voice slipped into a monotone.
He read the announcement in a flat monotone, showing no emotion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drone - a continuous low monotonous sound or speech

Etymology

From Greek monotonia, from monos (single) + tonos (tone).

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɑː.nəˌtoʊn / (AmE), / ˈmɒn.ə.təʊn / (BrE)
(of a sound or voice) sounded or spoken in a single, unvarying tone of pitch.
spoken/sounded in a single, unvarying pitch

Examples

His speech was delivered in a monotone voice that made it hard to stay attentive.
The teacher's monotone way of speaking bored the students.
She recited the poem in a flat monotone, without emotion.

Synset Examples

the owl's faint monotonous hooting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monotonous - dull and unvarying, especially in sound or activity

Etymology

From Greek 'monotonos' (of one tone), from 'monos' (single) and 'tonos' (tone).

3. ADJ

/ ˈmɑːnəˌtoʊn / (AmE), / ˈmɒnətəʊn / (BrE)
(of a sequence or function) Consistently increasing or consistently decreasing in value; never reversing direction.
consistent (mathematical) increase or decrease

Examples

The function is said to be monotone if it never decreases throughout its domain.
This sequence is strictly monotone, as each term is larger than the one before.
A monotone decreasing function will never increase at any point.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monotonic - (of a function or sequence) always increasing or always decreasing

Etymology

From Greek 'monotonos', meaning 'single-toned' or 'of one tone'.

4. NOUN

/ ˈmɑː.nəˌtoʊn / (AmE), / ˈmɒn.ə.təʊn / (BrE)
a single unvarying tone, especially in speech or singing, often repeated with different words or rhythms, as in certain liturgical texts
single unvarying tone, especially in speech or music

Examples

During the reading, the chant followed a monotone that did not change pitch.
The priest recited the prayer in a monotone, as required by the ancient ritual.
In Gregorian chant, a monotone is sometimes used to recite psalms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drone - a continuous low humming sound or monotone in speech or music
  • Antonym: intonation - the rise and fall of the voice in speaking, opposite of monotone

Etymology

From Greek monotonos (of one tone), from monos (single) + tonos (tone).
