sabot

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæboʊ / (AmE), / ˈsæbəʊ / (BrE)
a shoe carved from a single block of wood, traditionally worn by peasants in parts of Europe
wooden shoe

Examples

In the museum, a pair of traditional sabots is displayed next to the farming tools.
The craftsman demonstrated how to carve a sabot from a single block of wood.
She wore sabots during the heritage festival to celebrate her ancestors' way of life.

Etymology

From French sabot (wooden shoe).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsæboʊ / (AmE), / ˈsæbəʊ / (BrE)
a shoe, typically made of wood, that is traditionally worn in parts of Europe, especially France and the Netherlands
wooden shoe worn in Europe

Examples

The museum displayed a pair of traditional sabots from northern France.
She wore wooden sabots on her feet during the folk festival.
The artisan skillfully carved a sabot from a single block of wood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clog - a shoe with a thick wooden sole

Etymology

From French sabot (wooden shoe), probably from Middle French, possibly of Old French or Frankish origin.
