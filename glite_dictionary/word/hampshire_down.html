Hampshire Down

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæmpʃər daʊn / (AmE), / ˈhæmpʃə daʊn / (BrE)
a British breed of large, hornless domestic sheep with a dark face, known for its high-quality meat and wool
British breed of hornless, dark-faced sheep
british

Examples

The Hampshire Down is prized by farmers for its rapid growth and strong build.
We visited a farm where a flock of Hampshire Downs grazed peacefully in the fields.
The dark face and woolly body make the Hampshire Down easy to identify among other breeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Suffolk - a British breed of black-faced, hornless sheep

Etymology

Named after Hampshire, a county in southern England, where the breed originated.
