Calandrinia

1. NOUN

/ kəˌlænˈdrɪniə / (AmE), / kəˌlanˈdrɪnɪə / (BrE)
A genus of low-growing herbaceous plants, often with basal leaves and clusters of short-lived purplish flowers, found in tropical and warm temperate regions.
genus of low-growing herbs with purplish flowers

Examples

Several species of Calandrinia are valued for their vibrant flowers in rock gardens.
The desert landscape was dotted with blooming Calandrinia after the rains.
Some gardeners choose Calandrinia for its drought-resistant qualities and attractive foliage.

Etymology

Named after Jean Louis Calandrini (1703–1758), Swiss scientist and botanist.
