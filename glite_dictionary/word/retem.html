retem

1. NOUN

/ rəˈtɛm / (AmE), / rɪˈtɛm / (BrE)
a desert shrub found in Syria and Arabia, with small white flowers, identified as the juniper in the Old Testament and sometimes placed in the genus Genista
desert shrub of Syria and Arabia

Examples

The Bedouins often use the branches of the retem shrub for firewood in the desert.
Biblical references to the retem describe its importance as a sheltering plant in arid regions.
The delicate white flowers of the retem can be seen blooming after rare desert rains.

Etymology

From Hebrew *retēm* (רֶתֶם), appearing in biblical texts.
