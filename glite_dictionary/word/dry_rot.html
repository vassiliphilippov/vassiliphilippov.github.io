dry rot

1. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪ ˌrɑt / (AmE), / ˈdraɪ ˌrɒt / (BrE)
A fungal disease that causes wood to decay, resulting in a dry, crumbly texture.
a fungus causing wood decay

Examples

The old beams in the attic were weakened by dry rot.
The inspector found evidence of dry rot in the floorboards.
Owners must treat dry rot quickly to prevent structural damage.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wet rot - fungal decay of wood with a wet, softened texture
  • Synonym: decay - the process of rotting or decomposing
