distally

1. ADV

/ ˈdɪstəli / (AmE), / ˈdɪstəli / (BrE)
at or towards a point far from the center or point of attachment, especially in anatomy or medicine.
far from the center (anatomically)
medical

Examples

In the human arm, the fingers are located distally relative to the shoulder.
The catheter tip must be placed distally from the valve for accurate measurement.
Swelling that appears distally in the leg can indicate circulatory problems.

Synset Examples

the bronchus is situated distally

Related Terms

  • Antonym: proximally - nearer to the center or point of attachment, especially anatomically

Etymology

From 'distal' + '-ly'; 'distal' from Latin 'distans' (standing apart).
