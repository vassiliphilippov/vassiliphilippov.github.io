perpetual

1. ADJ

/ pərˈpɛtʃuəl / (AmE), / pəˈpɛtʃuəl / (BrE)
Continuing forever or for a very long time without stopping; never-ending.
never-ending, continuous

Examples

The ancient city was built to withstand the perpetual winds that swept across the plains.
Her perpetual optimism inspired everyone around her, even in the most difficult times.
The region is covered in perpetual snow, making it inaccessible during most of the year.

Synset Examples

the ageless themes of love and revenge
eternal truths
life everlasting
hell's perpetual fires
the unending bliss of heaven

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eternal - lasting or existing forever, without end
  • Antonym: temporary - lasting for only a limited period

Etymology

From Latin 'perpetuus' meaning 'continuous, uninterrupted.'

2. ADJ

/ pərˈpɛtʃuəl / (AmE), / pəˈpɛtjuəl / (BrE)
continuing for a very long time without stopping or being interrupted; endless or unceasing
never-ending; continuous

Examples

The region is known for its perpetual fog that blankets the coast.
She lived in a state of perpetual worry about her children.
The engine's hum became a perpetual background noise in the laboratory.

Synset Examples

the ceaseless thunder of surf
in constant pain
night and day we live with the incessant noise of the city
the never-ending search for happiness
the perpetual struggle to maintain standards in a democracy
man's unceasing warfare with drought and isolation
unremitting demands of hunger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: continuous - without interruption or break
  • Antonym: intermittent - occurring at irregular intervals; not continuous

Etymology

From Latin perpetuus (continuous, uninterrupted).
