seedy

1. ADJ

/ ˈsiːdi / (AmE), / ˈsiːdi / (BrE)
(of fruit or plants) containing or full of seeds.
full of seeds (of fruit/plants)

Examples

The watermelon was so seedy that it was difficult to eat without spitting them out.
This variety of tomato is less seedy than most, making it popular in salads.
After cutting the fig open, she saw its seedy interior glistening in the sunlight.

Synset Examples

as seedy as a fig

Related Terms

  • Antonym: seedless - (of fruit or plants) without seeds

Etymology

From 'seed' + '-y', indicating abundance or possession of seeds.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02262825-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈsidi / (AmE), / ˈsiːdi / (BrE)
(of a place or person) sordid and disreputable; morally degraded and likely connected to unpleasant or illegal activities.
sordid or disreputable; morally degraded

Examples

The police raided a seedy nightclub suspected of illegal activities.
After dark, the area becomes quite seedy and unsafe for tourists.
He avoided the seedy part of town known for its crime and vice.

Synset Examples

a seedy district
the seamy side of life
sleazy characters hanging around casinos
sleazy storefronts with … dirt on the walls
the sordid details of his orgies stank under his very nostrils
the squalid atmosphere of intrigue and betrayal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sordid - involving immoral or dishonorable actions; dirty or squalid
  • Synonym: sleazy - morally low; shabby or disreputable
  • Antonym: respectable - regarded by society as good, proper, or correct; not disreputable

Etymology

From seed (noun), in sense ‘infested with seeds’ and later ‘shabby’; figurative use after mid-19th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01991889-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈsiːdi / (AmE), / ˈsiːdi / (BrE)
shabby, dirty, or untidy in appearance; looking neglected or run-down
shabby or untidy in appearance

Examples

The old motel looked distinctly seedy compared to the newer buildings nearby.
He wore a seedy jacket that hadn’t seen a wash in years.
After years of neglect, the once-grand theater had a seedy atmosphere.

Synset Examples

a surge of ragged scruffy children
he was soiled and seedy and fragrant with gin

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shabby - in poor condition through long use or lack of care
  • Synonym: scruffy - shabby and untidy or dirty
  • Antonym: smart - neat, tidy, and stylish in appearance

Etymology

From 'seed' + '-y', originally referring to something full of seeds and later to a worn-out appearance, as of someone past their prime.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02593709-s
Synset POS: s
