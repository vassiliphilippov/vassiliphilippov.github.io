sea-rocket

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiˌrɑkɪt / (AmE), / ˈsiːˌrɒkɪt / (BrE)
a salt-tolerant annual plant found on seashores, known for its fragrant rose or violet flowers and fleshy grey-green leaves
a coastal flowering plant

Examples

The sea-rocket thrives along sandy beaches above the high tide line.
We spotted patches of sea-rocket blooming near the dunes in early summer.
The leaves of sea-rocket are succulent and grey-green, helping it conserve water in salty environments.

Etymology

Formed from 'sea' and 'rocket' (the plant name), referring to its coastal habitat.
