botany

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːtəni / (AmE), / ˈbɒtəni / (BrE)
The branch of biology that deals with the scientific study of plants.
the scientific study of plants

Examples

She decided to specialize in botany during her university studies.
The museum has a wonderful exhibit on the history of botany.
His passion for botany started after he received a microscope as a gift.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: biology - the science of life and living organisms
  • Antonym: zoology - the scientific study of animals

Etymology

From Greek botane (pasture, herb, plant) via Latin botanica.
