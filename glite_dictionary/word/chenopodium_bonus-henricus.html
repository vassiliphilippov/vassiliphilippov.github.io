Chenopodium bonus-henricus

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɛnoʊˈpoʊdiəm ˌboʊnəs hɛnˈriːkəs / (AmE), / ˌkɛnnəˈpəʊdiəm ˌbəʊnəs hɛnˈriːkəs / (BrE)
a perennial European plant of the goosefoot family, naturalized in North America and often collected as a potherb; also known as Good King Henry.
European perennial plant, potherb

Examples

In some rural areas, Chenopodium bonus-henricus is still foraged and used as a leafy vegetable.
Gardeners appreciate Chenopodium bonus-henricus for its resilience and nutritional value.
The chef added young leaves of Chenopodium bonus-henricus to the spring soup.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Good King Henry - common name for Chenopodium bonus-henricus, edible wild plant

Etymology

From Latin, combining 'chenopodium' (goosefoot, the genus) and the species name 'bonus-henricus' (Good Henry).
