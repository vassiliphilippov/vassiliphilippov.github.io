assessment

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɛsmənt / (AmE), / əˈsɛsmənt / (BrE)
the act of forming a judgment about someone or something, or the process of making a decision about their value, quality, or importance
the act of judging or evaluating

Examples

The manager's assessment of the team's performance was fair and detailed.
Teachers use regular assessment to track students' progress.
After the interview, a thorough assessment of the candidate's skills was conducted.

Synset Examples

they criticized my judgment of the contestants

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evaluation - the process of judging or calculating the quality, importance, amount, or value of something
  • Synonym: appraisal - the act of examining someone or something in order to judge their qualities, success, or needs
  • Synonym: judgment - the act of forming an opinion or decision after careful thought

Etymology

From assess + -ment, originally from Latin *assessus* (to sit by, assist as a judge).

2. NOUN

the act of judging or evaluating the quality, value, or importance of someone or something

Examples

A careful assessment of the situation is necessary before making a decision.
After a full assessment of the damaged building, repairs were scheduled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evaluation - the process of judging the value or worth of something
  • Synonym: appraisal - the act of assessing someone's or something's value

Etymology

From assess + -ment, from Latin 'assessus' (to sit by, value) + -ment.

3. NOUN

/ əˈsɛsmənt / (AmE), / əˈsɛsmənt / (BrE)
an amount of money that is officially determined as due or payable, often as a charge, tax, or fee for public or group expenses.
amount determined as payable, e.g. as a fee or tax

Examples

The club members were upset by the unexpected assessment for roof repairs.
Each homeowner received a special assessment to fund the new community playground.
The annual assessment for trash collection must be paid by July 1st.

Synset Examples

the assessment for repairs outraged the club's membership

Related Terms

  • Synonym: levy - an amount of money collected officially, especially for government purposes
  • Synonym: tax - a compulsory contribution to state revenue
  • Antonym: assessment - the act of evaluating or appraising a person or situation

Etymology

From assess + -ment; from Latin 'assessus' (a sitting by, a session, a judging).

4. NOUN

/ əˈsɛsmənt / (AmE), / əˈsɛsmənt / (BrE)
the determination of the value of assets, especially for tax or insurance purposes
valuation of assets

Examples

The city conducted an assessment to determine the property’s new tax rate.
Insurance companies require an assessment of assets before issuing a policy.
A professional assessment established the building's market value for the sale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: valuation - the act of estimating or setting the value of something

Etymology

From assess + -ment, ultimately from Latin 'assessus' (a sitting by, a session) related to setting a value.
