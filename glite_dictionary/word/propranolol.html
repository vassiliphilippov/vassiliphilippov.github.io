propranolol

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈprænəˌlɔl / (AmE), / prəˈprænəlɒl / (BrE)
a drug used as a beta blocker to treat high blood pressure, angina, and certain heart or circulatory conditions
beta blocker drug for hypertension and heart conditions
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed propranolol to help manage her high blood pressure.
Migraine sufferers are sometimes treated with propranolol to reduce the frequency of their headaches.
After starting propranolol, he noticed a significant improvement in his tremors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atenolol - another beta blocker used for similar medical conditions
  • Synonym: metoprolol - a different beta blocker medication

Etymology

From proprietary name (Inderal) with the suffix -ol, denoting an alcohol, reflecting its chemical structure.
