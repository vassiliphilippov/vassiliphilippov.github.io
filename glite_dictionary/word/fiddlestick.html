fiddlestick

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪdəlˌstɪk / (AmE), / ˈfɪdəlstɪk / (BrE)
a bow used for playing the violin or a similar stringed instrument
bow for playing violin or fiddle

Examples

The violinist tightened the hair on her fiddlestick before beginning the concerto.
He lost his fiddlestick just before the performance and had to borrow one from a friend.
A new fiddlestick made from fine wood can greatly enhance a musician's sound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bow - a curved stick with horsehair used for playing stringed instruments

Etymology

From 'fiddle' (a stringed instrument) + 'stick', referring to the stick used to play the instrument.
