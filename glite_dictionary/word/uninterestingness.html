uninterestingness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈɪntrəstɪŋnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈɪntrəstɪŋnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of failing to attract or hold interest; lack of interest or appeal
lack of interest or appeal

Examples

The uninterestingness of his speech made it difficult to stay awake.
She complained about the uninterestingness of the museum exhibit.
Despite the movie's length, its uninterestingness was its biggest flaw.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: interestingness - the quality of being interesting or engaging

Etymology

From 'uninteresting' (not interesting) + '-ness' (forming nouns denoting a quality or state).
