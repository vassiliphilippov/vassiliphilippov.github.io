hypha

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪfə / (AmE), / ˈhʌɪfə / (BrE)
any of the long, threadlike filaments that make up the body (mycelium) of a fungus
threadlike filament of a fungus
medical

Examples

The hypha grows outward, spreading the fungus across the soil.
Each hypha is responsible for absorbing nutrients from the environment.
Under the microscope, we could clearly see the branching structure of the hypha.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: mycelium - the mass of hyphae forming the body of a fungus

Etymology

From Greek huphē (web, tissue).
