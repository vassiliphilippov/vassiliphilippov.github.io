disagreement

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsəˈɡrimənt / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈɡriːmənt / (BrE)
a situation in which people have different opinions or attitudes and do not agree
conflict of opinions or attitudes

Examples

There was a disagreement between the two colleagues about the best way to proceed.
The meeting ended in disagreement rather than consensus.
Despite their disagreement, they remained good friends.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: agreement - harmony or accord in opinion or feeling
  • Synonym: conflict - a serious disagreement or argument

Etymology

From dis- (expressing reversal) + agreement, from Old French 'agreer'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsəˈɡriːmənt / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈɡriːmənt / (BrE)
an instance of expressing a different opinion or a lack of consensus; the act of arguing, disputing, or not agreeing
the act of differing in opinion

Examples

There was a heated disagreement between the two colleagues during the meeting.
They managed to resolve their disagreement before it got out of hand.
Even close friends can have a disagreement from time to time.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: agreement - the act of agreeing or coming to a shared view

Etymology

From dis- + agreement, ultimately from Latin 'dis-' (apart) + 'agreement'.

3. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsəˈɡriːmənt / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈɡriːmənt / (BrE)
a situation in which people or things have different opinions, ideas, or beliefs, often leading to conflict or lack of harmony
difference of opinion; conflict

Examples

The two scientists had a disagreement over the interpretation of the data.
A minor disagreement between friends shouldn’t ruin a lifelong relationship.
There was obvious disagreement among the committee members about the final decision.

Synset Examples

a growing divergence of opinion

Related Terms

  • Antonym: agreement - harmony or sameness in opinion or feeling
  • Synonym: conflict - a serious disagreement or argument

Etymology

From dis- (expressing reversal) + agreement; first recorded in the late 16th century.
