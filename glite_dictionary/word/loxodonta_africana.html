Loxodonta africana

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɑksəˈdɑntə əˈfrɪkənə / (AmE), / ˌlɒksəˈdɒntə əˈfrɪkənə / (BrE)
the scientific name for the African elephant, a large mammal native to Africa characterized by large ears and tusks
scientific name for the African elephant

Examples

Conservationists are working to protect Loxodonta africana from poaching and habitat loss.
The Loxodonta africana is distinguished by its large ears, which help regulate body temperature.
Scientists are studying the migration patterns of Loxodonta africana across the savannas of Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: African elephant - large elephant species native to Africa
  • Antonym: Asian elephant - a different species of elephant native to Asia

Etymology

From Greek loxos (slanting) + odous, odont- (tooth); combined with Neo-Latin Africana (of Africa).
