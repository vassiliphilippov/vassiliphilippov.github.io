Inconel

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnkəˌnɛl / (AmE), / ˈɪnkənɛl / (BrE)
a trademarked nickel-chromium-iron alloy known for its resistance to corrosion and heat, used in applications such as gas-turbine blades and chemical processing equipment
nickel-chromium-iron alloy, corrosion-resistant

Examples

Many jet engines rely on Inconel parts to withstand high temperatures and stress.
The chemical plant upgraded its piping to Inconel to prevent corrosion from harsh acids.
Researchers tested the fatigue strength of Inconel under repeated cycles of heating and cooling.

Etymology

Trademark; coined name for a family of nickel-chromium alloys developed in the 20th century.
