proficient

1. ADJ

/ prəˈfɪʃənt / (AmE), / prəˈfɪʃənt / (BrE)
having a high degree of skill or competence in a particular field or activity; skilled, expert, or capable.
skilled and competent

Examples

She is proficient in three languages and often interprets for her company.
As a proficient software developer, he quickly solved the technical issue.
His proficient handling of the situation impressed his supervisors.

Synset Examples

adept in handicrafts
an adept juggler
an expert job
a good mechanic
a practiced marksman
a proficient engineer
a lesser-known but no less skillful composer
the effect was achieved by skillful retouching

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skilled - having the ability or training to perform a task well
  • Antonym: incompetent - not having the necessary skills or ability

Etymology

From Latin proficient-, present participle of proficere (to accomplish, make progress).
