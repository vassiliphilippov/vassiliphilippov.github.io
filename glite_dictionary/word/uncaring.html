uncaring

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈkeə.rɪŋ / (BrE)
not worrying about or interested in other people’s needs or feelings; lacking concern or sympathy.
lacking concern or sympathy

Examples

Her response to the tragedy was surprisingly uncaring and cold.
He accused the manager of being uncaring about his employees' wellbeing.
Living in an uncaring society can make people feel isolated.

Synset Examples

the thoughtless saying of a great princess on being informed that the people had no bread: ‘Let them eat cake’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indifferent - having no particular interest or sympathy; unconcerned
  • Antonym: compassionate - showing sympathy and concern for others

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (not) + 'caring' (present participle of care).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkɛr.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈkeə.rɪŋ / (BrE)
not showing affection, concern, or sympathy for others; indifferent or insensitive.
not showing concern or affection

Examples

The manager was criticized for his uncaring response to the team's concerns.
She grew up in an uncaring environment and struggled to trust others.
His uncaring attitude made it difficult for his colleagues to approach him.

Synset Examples

an uncaring person

Related Terms

  • Antonym: caring - showing concern or kindness for others
  • Synonym: indifferent - having no particular interest or sympathy; unconcerned

Etymology

From un- (not) + caring.
