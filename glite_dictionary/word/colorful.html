colorful

1. ADJ

/ ˈkʌlərfəl / (AmE), / ˈkʌləfəl / (BrE)
having bright or varied colors
having many or bright colors

Examples

The parade included colorful floats decorated with ribbons and flowers.
She wore a colorful scarf that brightened up her outfit.
Our garden is especially colorful in the spring, when the tulips and daffodils bloom.

Synset Examples

colorful autumn leaves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: multicolored - having many different colors
  • Antonym: colorless - having no color or lacking vividness

2. ADJ

/ ˈkʌlɚfəl / (AmE), / ˈkʌləfʊl / (BrE)
Full of variety or interest; striking and vivid in character, appearance, or style.
full of variety or interest

Examples

She has led a colorful life, full of adventure and travel.
The city’s colorful markets attract visitors from around the world.
He’s known for telling colorful stories about his time overseas.

Synset Examples

a colorful period of history
a colorful character
colorful language

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vivid - producing powerful feelings or strong, clear images in the mind
  • Antonym: dull - lacking interest or excitement

Etymology

From 'color' + '-ful', meaning 'full of color'; figurative use attested since the late 19th century.
