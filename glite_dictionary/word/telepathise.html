to telepathise

1. VERB

/ təˈlɛpəˌθaɪz / (AmE), / tɛˈlɛpəθaɪz / (BrE)
to communicate information or thoughts directly from one mind to another without using speech, writing, or other normal sensory channels
communicate mentally (by telepathy)

Examples

Some people believe they can telepathise with loved ones across great distances.
The twins claim to telepathise whenever one is in trouble or distress.
In science fiction, characters often telepathise to share secret messages.

Synset Examples

some people believe they can telepathize with others around the world

Related Terms

  • Synonym: telepathy - the supposed communication of thoughts or ideas by means other than the known senses

Etymology

From 'telepathy' + '-ise', modeled after the noun telepathy (from Greek 'tele-' meaning 'distant' and 'pathos' meaning 'feeling').
