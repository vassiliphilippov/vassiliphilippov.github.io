Christoph Willibald von Gluck

1. PROPN

/ ˈkrɪstɒf ˈvɪlɪbɑld fɒn ɡlʊk / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstɒf ˈvɪlɪbɑld fɒn ɡlʊk / (BrE)
(1714–1787), German composer best known for his influential operas, which helped reform opera in the 18th century.
German composer known for reforming opera

Examples

The music of Christoph Willibald von Gluck had a profound impact on the development of classical opera.
Many scholars have studied the operatic reforms introduced by Christoph Willibald von Gluck.
Christoph Willibald von Gluck composed over 100 operas during his prolific career.
