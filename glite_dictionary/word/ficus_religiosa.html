Ficus religiosa

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪkəs rɪˌlidʒiˈoʊsə / (AmE), / ˈfaɪkəs rɪˌliːdʒiˈəʊsə / (BrE)
A species of fig tree native to India, notable for its size, longevity, and religious significance in Buddhism and Hinduism; commonly called the sacred fig or Bo tree.
the sacred fig tree of India, revered in Buddhism

Examples

The Ficus religiosa is often planted near temples in India for its spiritual significance.
Buddhists revere the Ficus religiosa as the tree under which Siddhartha Gautama attained enlightenment.
Unlike the banyan, the Ficus religiosa does not have prop roots but can live for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bodhi tree - the sacred fig tree under which the Buddha attained enlightenment; another name for Ficus religiosa

Etymology

From Latin: 'ficus' meaning 'fig', and 'religiosa' meaning 'religious', indicating its sacred status.
