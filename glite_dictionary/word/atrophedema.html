atrophedema

1. NOUN

/ ˌæt.roʊ.fɪˈdiː.mə / (AmE), / ˌæ.trəʊ.fɪˈdiː.mə / (BrE)
a condition characterized by sudden, recurrent, large circumscribed areas of subcutaneous swelling, usually disappearing within 24 hours and often caused by an allergic reaction
sudden, recurrent swelling of the skin
medical

Examples

The patient experienced atrophedema after eating shellfish for the first time.
Her episodes of atrophedema often resolved within a few hours without treatment.
Doctors identified the swelling as atrophedema due to its sudden onset and disappearance.
