to spurn

1. VERB

/ spɝːn / (AmE), / spɜːn / (BrE)
to reject someone or something with disdain or contempt
reject with contempt or disdain

Examples

She spurned his invitation, making it clear she wanted nothing to do with him.
Many employees spurn the new company policy, calling it unfair and restrictive.
He spurned all offers of help, insisting on handling the problem alone.

Synset Examples

She spurned his advances

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reject - to refuse to accept, believe in, or agree with something or someone

Etymology

From Old English *spurnan* (to kick away, reject), related to German *sporn* (to kick).

2. VERB

/ spɝn / (AmE), / spɜːn / (BrE)
to reject someone or something disdainfully or with contempt
reject with contempt

Examples

They decided to spurn the offer after reading the contract's fine print.
Despite her friends' invitations, she spurned every suggestion and stayed home.
The artist felt wounded when critics spurned his new style of painting.

Synset Examples

She spurned his advances

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reject - to refuse to accept, believe in, or agree with something
  • Antonym: to accept - to receive or agree to something offered

Etymology

From Old English *spurnan* (to kick away, reject).
