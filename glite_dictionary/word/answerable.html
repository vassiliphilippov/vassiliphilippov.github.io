answerable

1. ADJ

/ ˈæn.sɚ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈɑːn.sər.ə.bəl / (BrE)
(of a question or problem) capable of being answered or solved.
able to be answered

Examples

The professor assured the students that every exam question was answerable with the material provided.
Many mysteries of the universe may never be answerable through science alone.
Your inquiry is perfectly answerable, but it requires some research to address thoroughly.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unanswerable - impossible to answer or refute

Etymology

From Middle English 'answarable', from 'answer' + '-able', influenced by Old French 'responable'.

Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈæn.sɚ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈɑːn.sər.ə.bəl / (BrE)
legally or morally responsible to a higher authority; required to explain or justify actions to someone else
legally or morally responsible

Examples

As the team leader, you are answerable to the board for any mistakes.
The government is answerable to the public for its actions.
Teachers are answerable for the safety of their students while at school.

Synset Examples

parents are answerable for their child's acts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: responsible - having an obligation to do something, or having control over or care for someone, as part of one's job or role

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French 'ansurable', based on 'answer'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02004321-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈæn.sɚ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈɑːn.sər.ə.bəl / (BrE)
responsible for explaining or justifying one's actions to someone; liable to be called to account
liable or responsible to answer for actions

Examples

As manager, she is answerable for the team's performance.
The minister is answerable to Parliament for his decisions.
Parents can be held answerable for the actions of their children.

Synset Examples

governments must be accountable to someone beside themselves
fully accountable for what they did
the court held the parents answerable for their minor child's acts of vandalism
he was answerable to no one

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accountable - required or expected to justify actions or decisions
  • Synonym: responsible - having an obligation to do something or having control over or care for someone

Etymology

From Old French 'respondre' (to answer), based on Latin 'respondere'.
