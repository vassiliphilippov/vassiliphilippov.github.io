Procyon cancrivorus

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈsaɪ.ɒn ˌkæŋ.krɪˈvɔːr.əs / (AmE), / prəˈsʌɪ.ən ˌkaŋ.krɪˈvɔː.rəs / (BrE)
a South American species of raccoon, commonly known as the crab-eating raccoon
South American crab-eating raccoon

Examples

The Procyon cancrivorus is often found near rivers and mangroves in South America.
Unlike the common raccoon, the Procyon cancrivorus has a preference for aquatic prey.
Researchers observed a group of Procyon cancrivorus foraging along the Amazon River.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crab-eating raccoon - common name for Procyon cancrivorus
  • Degree Higher: raccoon - medium-sized mammal from the genus Procyon

Etymology

From Latin: 'Procyon' (a type of animal, literally 'before the dog') and 'cancrivorus' (crab-eating).
