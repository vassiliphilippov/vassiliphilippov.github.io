Corynebacterium

1. NOUN

/ kɔˌrɪnɪbækˈtɪəriəm / (AmE), / kɒˌrɪnɪbækˈtɪəriəm / (BrE)
any of a genus of Gram-positive, rod-shaped bacteria, some of which can cause disease in humans and animals
genus of rod-shaped bacteria
medical

Examples

Corynebacterium diphtheriae is responsible for causing diphtheria in humans.
Some species of Corynebacterium live harmlessly on the skin as part of the normal flora.
The laboratory confirmed the presence of Corynebacterium in the patient's throat culture.

Etymology

From Greek 'korynē' (club) + 'bakterion' (little rod).

2. NOUN

/ kɔːˌrinəbækˈtɪriəm / (AmE), / kɒˌrɪnɪbækˈtɪəriəm / (BrE)
A genus of Gram-positive bacteria, many of which are found in diverse environments; some species are notable for causing disease in humans and animals.
genus of bacteria, some pathogenic
medical

Examples

Corynebacterium diphtheriae is the bacterium responsible for causing diphtheria in humans.
Veterinarians identified Corynebacterium species as the cause of the infection in livestock.
Some strains of Corynebacterium are harmless and naturally occur on human skin.

Etymology

From Greek 'korune' (club) + 'bakterion' (small rod), referring to the club-shaped appearance of the bacteria.
