teredinid

1. NOUN

/ təˈrɛdəˌnɪd / (AmE), / təˈrɛdɪnɪd / (BrE)
A wormlike marine bivalve mollusc that bores into wood, such as piers and ships, using shell-adapted boring mechanisms; shipworm.
wood-boring marine bivalve mollusc

Examples

The old dock was riddled with holes made by teredinids.
Scientists study teredinids to understand how they degrade submerged wood.
The wooden hull of the ship was destroyed by teredinids over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shipworm - a common name for several wood-boring marine bivalves, including teredinids

Etymology

From the genus name Teredo, from Latin, from Greek tēreidōn (a type of wood-boring worm).
