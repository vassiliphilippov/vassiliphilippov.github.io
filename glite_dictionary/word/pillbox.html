pillbox

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪlˌbɑks / (AmE), / ˈpɪlˌbɒks / (BrE)
a small, round, brimless woman's hat with a flat crown
small round woman's hat

Examples

She wore a stylish pillbox to the reception, complementing her elegant dress.
The classic pillbox became fashionable in the 1960s, popularized by Jackie Kennedy.
Her red pillbox drew many compliments at the charity event.

Etymology

So called because its shape resembles that of a small pill container.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɪlˌbɑks / (AmE), / ˈpɪlˌbɒks / (BrE)
a small container used for storing pills or tablets, often with separate compartments for different doses or days
small container for pills

Examples

My grandmother keeps her medication organized in a pillbox on her bedside table.
He bought a new pillbox with compartments labeled for each day of the week.
The nurse reminded the patient to fill his pillbox every Sunday evening.

Etymology

From 'pill' (medicine tablet) + 'box' (container).

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɪlˌbɑks / (AmE), / ˈpɪlˌbɒks / (BrE)
A small, usually concrete, fortified structure with openings through which guns can be fired, used as a defense in military operations.
small fortified gun structure

Examples

The soldiers took cover behind a pillbox as enemy fire intensified.
Several abandoned pillboxes still line the old battlefield.
A machine gunner was stationed inside the pillbox during the assault.

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to a small box for holding pills.
