Natrix sipedon

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪ.trɪks saɪˈpiː.dɑn / (AmE), / ˈneɪ.trɪks sɪˈpiː.dɒn / (BrE)
A species of nonvenomous water snake native to North America, often found near water; also called the northern water snake.
species of North American water snake

Examples

The Natrix sipedon is commonly observed basking on rocks along riverbanks.
Unlike many snakes, the Natrix sipedon spends much of its time in and around water.
Wildlife biologists studied the diet of Natrix sipedon in several lakes and streams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: northern water snake - common name for Natrix sipedon

Etymology

Genus name from Latin *natrix* (‘water snake’); species name from Greek *sipedon* (‘of the riverbank’).
