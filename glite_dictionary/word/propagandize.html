to propagandize

1. VERB

/ ˌprɑː.pəˈɡæn.daɪz / (AmE), / ˌprɒ.pəˈɡæn.daɪz / (BrE)
to spread or promote ideas, information, or rumors, especially in a biased or misleading way, for the purpose of influencing opinion
promote propaganda or biased ideas

Examples

The regime tried to propagandize the public through state-controlled media.
Activists accused the organization of attempting to propagandize young students with misleading information.
During wartime, governments often propagandize to maintain public support for their actions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to promote - to support or actively encourage
  • Synonym: to advertise - to make publicly known; to announce or promote

Etymology

Formed from 'propaganda' + '-ize', modeled on similar English verb formations.

2. VERB

/ ˌprɑːpəˈɡændaɪz / (AmE), / ˌprɒpəˈɡændaɪz / (BrE)
to subject someone or something to propaganda; to promote or spread propaganda.
promote or subject to propaganda

Examples

The regime attempted to propagandize the public through state-controlled media.
Schools should not propagandize students with political ideology.
The documentary exposed how the group propagandized its message to gain supporters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to brainwash - to make someone accept a set of ideas by repeatedly telling them that these ideas are correct

Etymology

From 'propaganda' + '-ize'; 'propaganda' from Italian, originally meaning 'congregation for propagating the faith'.
