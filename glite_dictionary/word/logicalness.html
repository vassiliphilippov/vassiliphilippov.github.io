logicalness

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑːdʒɪkəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈlɒdʒɪkəlnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being logical or showing correct and valid reasoning.
quality of being logical

Examples

The logicalness of her argument won over even her most skeptical critics.
He admired the logicalness of the mathematical proof.
Sometimes, creativity is more important than strict logicalness when solving problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: logic - reasoning conducted according to strict principles
  • Antonym: illogicality - quality of lacking logic or valid reasoning

Etymology

From 'logical' + '-ness'; 'logical' from Greek 'logikos' (pertaining to reasoning).
