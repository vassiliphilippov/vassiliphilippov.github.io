Laminariaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌlæməˌnɛriˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌlæmɪˌnɛərɪˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
A large family of marine brown algae, chiefly in northern waters, that includes many economically important kelps.
family of large marine brown algae (kelps)

Examples

The Laminariaceae family contains several species of kelp harvested for human consumption.
Researchers are studying the ecological impact of Laminariaceae in northern marine habitats.
Many products use extracts from Laminariaceae due to their rich content of useful compounds.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus Laminaria and standard botanical family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ ləˌmɛr.i.əˈsi.aɪ / (AmE), / læˌmɛər.i.əˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
A large family of marine brown algae, including many economically important kelps found mainly in northern waters.
family of brown marine algae (kelps)

Examples

Many species in the Laminariaceae family play a vital role in coastal marine ecosystems.
Commercial harvesting of Laminariaceae kelps is important for the production of alginate.
Research on Laminariaceae has improved our understanding of marine biodiversity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kelp - large brown seaweed of shallow oceans, often harvested

Etymology

From modern Latin Laminariaceae, based on genus Laminaria.
