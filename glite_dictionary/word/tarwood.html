tarwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑrˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈtɑːˌwʊd / (BrE)
a New Zealand silver pine (Manoao colensoi) with a conical shape and long, slender, flexible branches, adapted to cold, wet summers and high altitudes
a New Zealand silver pine tree

Examples

The tarwood thrives in regions with high rainfall and cool temperatures.
Botanists studied the unique adaptations of the tarwood in New Zealand's mountainous areas.
A dense grove of tarwoods covered the slopes, providing vital shelter for birds.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɑrˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈtɑːˌwʊd / (BrE)
a shrub native to New Zealand, known for its resinous wood and distinctive foliage
New Zealand shrub with resinous wood

Examples

The tarwood is well adapted to the coastal environment of New Zealand.
Traditional Maori used tarwood for making tools because of its hard resinous properties.
