downwardly

1. ADV

/ ˈdaʊnwɚdli / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnwədli / (BrE)
In a direction or manner from a higher to a lower level, position, or status.
from a higher to a lower position or level

Examples

Housing prices moved downwardly over the past year due to market changes.
The data indicated that profits were trending downwardly across multiple sectors.
She gazed downwardly from the edge of the tall building, feeling a rush of vertigo.

Synset Examples

don't fall down
rode the lift up and skied down
prices plunged downward

Related Terms

  • Antonym: upwardly - in a direction or manner from a lower to a higher level or status

Etymology

Formed from 'downward' (from Old English 'dūnweard') with adverbial suffix '-ly'.
