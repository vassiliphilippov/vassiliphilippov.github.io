Chrysophyllum

1. NOUN

/ ˌkraɪsoʊˈfɪləm / (AmE), / ˌkrɪsəʊˈfɪləm / (BrE)
a genus of tropical American evergreen trees or shrubs, some of which produce edible fruit such as the star apple
genus of tropical evergreen trees or shrubs

Examples

The Chrysophyllum genus includes the well-known star apple tree.
Many Chrysophyllum species are cultivated for their attractive foliage and fruit.
Botanists discovered a new species of Chrysophyllum in the Amazon rainforest.

Etymology

From Greek khrusos (gold) + phyllon (leaf), referring to the golden hairs on the leaves of some species.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkraɪsəˈfɪləm / (AmE), / ˌkrɪsəˈfɪləm / (BrE)
a genus of tropical American evergreen trees or shrubs, some species of which produce edible fruit such as the star apple
genus of tropical American evergreen trees

Examples

Many species in the Chrysophyllum genus are valued for their attractive foliage and edible fruit.
The star apple, a fruit from the Chrysophyllum genus, is popular in many tropical countries.
Researchers discovered a rare Chrysophyllum tree deep in the Amazon rainforest.

Etymology

From Greek *chrysos* (gold) + *phyllon* (leaf), referring to the golden underside of the leaves in some species.
