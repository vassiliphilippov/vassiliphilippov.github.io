homoeroticism

1. NOUN

/ ˌhoʊmoʊɪˈrɑːtɪsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌhəʊməʊɪˈrɒtɪsɪzəm / (BrE)
sexual attraction to or the depiction of romantic or sexual desire between people of the same sex, often in literature or art
same-sex sexual attraction or desire

Examples

The novel explores themes of homoeroticism through the intense friendships between its male characters.
Some works of art from the Renaissance are noted for their subtle homoeroticism.
Critics have debated the presence of homoeroticism in classic poetry and literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homoerotic - relating to same-sex sexual attraction or desire

Etymology

From homo- (same) + eroticism, after Greek homos (same) and eros (sexual love).
