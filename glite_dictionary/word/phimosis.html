phimosis

1. NOUN

/ faɪˈmoʊsɪs / (AmE), / fɪˈməʊsɪs / (BrE)
a medical condition in which the foreskin of the penis is so tight that it cannot be pulled back over the glans
tightness of the foreskin preventing retraction
medical

Examples

Surgery may be required if phimosis causes pain or recurrent infections.
Some boys experience phimosis naturally, but the condition often resolves on its own.
Persistent phimosis can sometimes lead to difficulty with urination.

Etymology

From Greek phimosis (a muzzling, closure), from phimoun (to muzzle).
