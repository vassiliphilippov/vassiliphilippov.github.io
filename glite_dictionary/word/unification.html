unification

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuːnəfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of bringing things or people together to form a single unit or whole
the process of being united or made one

Examples

The unification of East and West Germany was a historic event.
European unification has led to closer economic and political ties.
The unification of the departments improved communication within the company.

Synset Examples

there is strength in union

Related Terms

  • Synonym: union - the action or fact of joining or being joined
  • Antonym: division - the action of separating or being separated into parts

Etymology

From Late Latin unificare (to make one), from Latin unus (one) + facere (to make).

3. NOUN

/ ˌjunɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of combining or joining separate things or groups into a single unit, entity, or group
the act of uniting into one

Examples

The unification of East and West Germany was a historic event in 1990.
Efforts toward unification have brought together several small companies under one brand.
Their country's unification process faced many difficult political challenges.

Synset Examples

the union of opposing factions
he looked forward to the unification of his family for the holidays

Related Terms

  • Synonym: union - the action of joining or being joined
  • Antonym: division - the act of separating into parts

Etymology

From Medieval Latin unificatio (making one), from Latin unus (one) + facere (to make).
