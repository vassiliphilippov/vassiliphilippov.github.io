Rosa laevigata

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊzə leɪˈvɪɡətə / (AmE), / ˈrəʊzə liːˈvɪɡətə / (BrE)
a species of climbing rose native to China, with fragrant white flowers and glossy leaves
Chinese climbing rose with white blossoms

Examples

The garden was filled with the delicate scent of Rosa laevigata in full bloom.
Botanists recommend Rosa laevigata for its hardiness and beautiful climbing habit.
She planted Rosa laevigata along the fence to create a natural, fragrant barrier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cherokee rose - common name for Rosa laevigata

Etymology

From Latin; 'Rosa' means 'rose', 'laevigata' means 'smooth' or 'polished'.
