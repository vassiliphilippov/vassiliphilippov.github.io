Enlightenment

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈlaɪtnmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈlaɪtnmənt / (BrE)
a European intellectual movement of the late 17th and 18th centuries emphasizing reason, individualism, and skepticism of traditional doctrines
18th-century European intellectual movement

Examples

The Enlightenment encouraged scientific inquiry and led to numerous discoveries across Europe.
Philosophers like Voltaire and Rousseau were central figures in the Enlightenment.
Many modern political ideas trace their origins back to the Enlightenment.

Synset Examples

the Enlightenment brought about many humanitarian reforms

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Renaissance - cultural revival of classical learning in Europe starting in the 14th century
  • Degree Higher: Romanticism - artistic and intellectual movement emphasizing emotion, succeeding the Enlightenment
