harper

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrpər / (AmE), / ˈhɑːpə / (BrE)
a person who plays the harp, especially one who does so professionally or as a musician
person who plays the harp

Examples

The talented harper captivated the audience with a haunting melody.
In medieval courts, every nobleman wanted a skilled harper to entertain guests.
She became a renowned harper after performing at international music festivals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harpist - a person who plays the harp

Etymology

From Old English *hearpera*, from *hearp(e)* (‘harp’) + *-er* (‘one who plays/does’).
