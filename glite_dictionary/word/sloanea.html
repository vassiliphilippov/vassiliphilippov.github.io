Sloanea

1. NOUN

/ sloʊˈæniə / (AmE), / sləʊˈæniə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical hardwood timber trees native to various parts of the world, valued for their wood.
genus of tropical hardwood trees

Examples

Many species within the genus Sloanea are sought after for their durable wood.
Botanists conducted fieldwork to document the diversity of Sloanea in the rainforest.
The leaves and fruit of Sloanea trees are important for local wildlife.

Etymology

Named after British botanist Hans Sloane (1660–1753).
