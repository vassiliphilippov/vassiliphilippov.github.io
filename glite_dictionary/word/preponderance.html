preponderance

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈpɑːndərəns / (AmE), / prɪˈpɒndərəns / (BrE)
the fact of being greater in number, quantity, or importance than something else
superiority in number or amount

Examples

The preponderance of evidence suggested that the company was responsible for the spill.
There was a clear preponderance of women at the conference this year.
A preponderance of expert opinions supports the new policy.

Synset Examples

a preponderance of evidence against the defendant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: majority - the greater number or part of something

Etymology

From Medieval Latin praepondērantia, from Latin praeponderare (to outweigh, to be heavier).

2. NOUN

/ prɪˈpɑndərəns / (AmE), / prɪˈpɒndərəns / (BrE)
the state or quality of being greater in weight, power, influence, or importance than something else
greater weight or importance

Examples

A slight preponderance on one side caused the balance to tip.
The preponderance of evidence suggests the defendant is guilty.
In elections, even a minor preponderance of votes can decide the outcome.

Synset Examples

the least preponderance in either pan will unbalance the scale

Related Terms

  • Synonym: superiority - the state of being superior to others in quality or importance
  • Antonym: inferiority - the condition of being lower in status or quality

Etymology

From Latin 'praeponderantia', from 'prae-' (before) + 'ponderare' (to weigh).

3. NOUN

/ prɪˈpɑːndərəns / (AmE), / prɪˈpɒndərəns / (BrE)
the state of being greater in number, power, or influence than others
superiority in number, power, or influence

Examples

There is a clear preponderance of evidence supporting the new theory.
The preponderance of power rested with the central government after the reforms.
In some debates, the preponderance of opinion favored immediate action.

Synset Examples

the preponderance of good over evil
the preponderance of wealth and power

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dominance - the state of being dominant; control or power over others
  • Antonym: inferiority - the state of being lower in status, quality, or importance

Etymology

From Latin praeponderantia, from prae- 'before' + ponderare 'to weigh'.
