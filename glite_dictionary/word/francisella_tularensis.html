Francisella tularensis

1. NOUN

/ frænˌsɪsələ tuˌlɑːrənsɪs / (AmE), / frænˌsɪsɪlə tjuːləˈrɛnsɪs / (BrE)
a species of bacterium that causes tularemia in humans and animals; known to be highly infectious and considered a potential bioweapon
bacterium causing tularemia
medical

Examples

The laboratory was equipped to safely handle Francisella tularensis for research purposes.
Cases of tularemia are caused by the highly infectious bacterium Francisella tularensis.
There is concern about the potential use of Francisella tularensis as a biological weapon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tularemia - infectious disease caused by Francisella tularensis

Etymology

Named after Edward Francis, an American bacteriologist, and Latinized with reference to tularemia.
