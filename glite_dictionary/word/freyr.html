Freyr

1. PROPN

/ ˈfreɪ.ər / (AmE), / ˈfrɜː.ər / (BrE)
In Norse mythology, the god of earth’s fertility, peace, and prosperity; son of Njorth and brother of Freya, associated with the Vanir and later the Aesir.
Norse god of fertility and prosperity

Examples

According to legend, Freyr was worshipped as a bringer of peace and good harvests.
Scholars often discuss the role of Freyr in Norse religious ceremonies.
The statue represented Freyr, holding his famous golden boar Gullinbursti.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Freya - Norse goddess of love and fertility; sister of Freyr

Etymology

From Old Norse 'Freyr', meaning ‘lord’.
