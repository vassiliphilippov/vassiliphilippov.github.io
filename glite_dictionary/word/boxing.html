boxing

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑksɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɒksɪŋ / (BrE)
A sport in which two people fight by hitting each other with their fists, usually wearing gloves and following set rules.
fighting sport with fists

Examples

He began training in boxing when he was only twelve years old.
Many people watch boxing matches on television for excitement.
Women’s boxing has become increasingly popular in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrestling - A sport in which opponents grapple and try to pin each other to the ground
  • Synonym: martial arts - Various sports or skills of fighting, like judo or karate

Etymology

From the verb 'box' (to fight with the fists), with -ing.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑksɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɒksɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of enclosing something in a box or package
the act of putting into a box

Examples

The boxing of fragile items requires careful use of padding materials.
She spent the afternoon on the boxing of holiday gifts for her family.
Efficient boxing can help reduce shipping costs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: packaging - the process of packing goods for shipment or storage

Etymology

From the verb 'box' (to put in a box) + '-ing'.
