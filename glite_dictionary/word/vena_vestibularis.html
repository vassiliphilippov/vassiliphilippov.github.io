vena vestibularis

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪ.nə vəˌstɪbjəˈlɛrɪs / (AmE), / ˈveː.nə vɛs.tɪ.bjʊˈlɑː.rɪs / (BrE)
A vein that drains blood from the saccule and utricle in the inner ear.
vein draining inner ear structures
medical

Examples

Damage to the vena vestibularis can affect balance due to its role in draining the inner ear.
The surgeon carefully avoided the vena vestibularis during the delicate ear procedure.
Inflammation near the vena vestibularis may lead to swelling within the vestibular system.

Etymology

From Latin, 'vena' (vein) and 'vestibularis' (of the vestibule).
