Meriones longifrons

1. NOUN

/ məˈraɪ.əˌniːz lɒŋˈaɪ.frɒnz / (AmE), / mɛˈrɪ.əˌniːz ˌlɒŋ.ɪˈfrɒnz / (BrE)
A species of gerbil native to southern Europe, known for its long snout.
a species of southern European gerbil

Examples

Researchers observed the Meriones longifrons in its natural habitat along the Mediterranean coast.
The Meriones longifrons differs from other gerbils due to its distinctive elongated snout.
Conservationists are studying the population of Meriones longifrons to ensure its survival in the wild.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, where 'Meriones' is a rodent genus name, and 'longifrons' means 'long-snouted' in Latin.
