wits

1. NOUN

/ wɪts / (AmE), / wɪts / (BrE)
the ability to think quickly and make sensible decisions; the basic capacity for intelligent thought and perception
mental sharpness or intelligence

Examples

He survived in the wild thanks to his wits and resourcefulness.
The detective relied on her wits to solve the complex case.
When the lights went out, she was scared out of her wits.

Synset Examples

he used his wits to get ahead
I was scared out of my wits
he still had all his marbles and was in full possession of a lively mind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intelligence - the ability to acquire and apply knowledge and skills
  • Synonym: wit - the natural ability to perceive and understand; keen intelligence
  • Antonym: stupidity - lack of intelligence or common sense

Etymology

Plural of "wit," from Old English *witt* (mind, understanding).
