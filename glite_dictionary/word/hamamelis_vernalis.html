Hamamelis vernalis

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæməˈmiːlɪs vərˈnælɪs / (AmE), / ˌhaməˈmiːlɪs vəːˈnaːlɪs / (BrE)
A fragrant shrub native to the lower Mississippi valley, with very small flowers appearing from midwinter to spring; also known as Ozark witch hazel.
fragrant shrub, Ozark witch hazel

Examples

Hamamelis vernalis blooms in late winter, bringing color to otherwise bare gardens.
Gardeners value Hamamelis vernalis for its fragrance and early flowering.
The bright yellow flowers of Hamamelis vernalis stand out against snowy backgrounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: witch hazel - A shrub of the genus Hamamelis, known for its medicinal bark and fragrant yellow flowers.

Etymology

From New Latin: Hamamelis (the genus, from Greek 'hamamēlis' meaning 'medlar or service tree') + Latin 'vernalis' (of the spring).
