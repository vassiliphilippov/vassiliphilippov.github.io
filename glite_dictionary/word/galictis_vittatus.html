Galictis vittatus

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈlɪktɪs vɪˈteɪtəs / (AmE), / ɡəˈlɪktɪs vɪˈteɪtəs / (BrE)
A carnivorous mammal found in Central and South America, resembling a weasel, with a greyish-white back and dark underparts; also known as the greater grison.
species of grison, a mustelid mammal

Examples

The Galictis vittatus is known for its sleek, elongated body and striking coloration.
Unlike the lesser grison, the Galictis vittatus is larger and more widely distributed in tropical regions.
Zoologists observed a Galictis vittatus hunting small rodents in the rainforest understory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greater grison - common name for Galictis vittatus, a mustelid mammal

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Galictis' from Greek 'gale' (weasel) and 'iktis' (marten), species epithet 'vittatus' meaning 'striped'.
