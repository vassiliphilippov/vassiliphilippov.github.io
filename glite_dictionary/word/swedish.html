Swedish

1. ADJ

/ ˈswidɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈswiːdɪʃ / (BrE)
Relating to Sweden, its people, language, or culture.
of Sweden or its people, language, or culture

Examples

She decorated her home in a distinctively Swedish style, favoring simplicity and light colors.
The Swedish language uses several unique characters that do not appear in English.
We enjoyed listening to traditional Swedish music at the festival.

Synset Examples

the Swedish King
Swedish punch
Swedish umlauts

Etymology

From Sweden + English suffix -ish.

2. NOUN

/ ˈswidɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈswiːdɪʃ / (BrE)
the language spoken mainly in Sweden and parts of Finland, belonging to the North Germanic branch of the Indo-European family
the official language of Sweden

Examples

He is fluent in Swedish as well as English and German.
Many signs in Finland are written in both Finnish and Swedish.
She decided to learn Swedish before her trip to Stockholm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Swede - a native or inhabitant of Sweden
  • Degree Lower: Norwegian - the language spoken in Norway
  • Degree Lower: Danish - the language spoken in Denmark

Etymology

From Sweden + -ish, modeled after similar English demonyms.
