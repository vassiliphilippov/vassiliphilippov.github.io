malt

1. VERB

/ mɔlt / (AmE), / mɔːlt / (BrE)
to convert grain, especially barley, into malt by soaking, germinating, and drying
convert grain into malt

Examples

The brewery will malt the barley before beginning the fermentation process.
Farmers in the region often malt their own grain for local craft beer production.
It takes several days to malt the grain properly, ensuring the right flavor in the final product.

Etymology

From Old English *mealt*, from Proto-Germanic *maltam* (malted barley).

2. NOUN

/ mɔlt / (AmE), / mɔːlt / (BrE)
barley or another cereal grain that has been soaked, germinated, and dried for use in brewing, distilling, or food production.
grain germinated and dried for brewing

Examples

The brewer selected the finest malt for the new batch of beer.
Scotch whisky is traditionally made using malted barley, known simply as malt.
The bakery uses malt to give a distinct flavor to their bread.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barley - a grain commonly used to make malt

Etymology

From Old English mealt, of Germanic origin.

5. NOUN

/ mɔlt / (AmE), / mɔːlt / (BrE)
A type of strong lager with a high alcohol content, which by law is sometimes considered too alcoholic to be labeled simply as lager or beer.
a strong lager with high alcohol content
american

Examples

The store only sells malt in the back cooler because of its high alcohol content.
He prefers malt over regular beer when he's looking for something stronger.
Some states have strict regulations on where and how malt can be sold.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: beer - an alcoholic drink made from fermented malted barley, hops, and water
  • Degree Lower: lager - a type of pale, cold-fermented beer
  • Degree Lower: ale - a type of beer brewed using top-fermenting yeast

Etymology

From Old English *mealt* (malt), referring originally to germinated grain used in brewing.
