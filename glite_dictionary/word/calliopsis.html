calliopsis

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæl.iˈɑp.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌkæl.iˈɒp.sɪs / (BrE)
A North American annual plant widely cultivated for its yellow flowers with purple-red to brownish centers, sometimes classified as part of a subgenus Calliopsis.
a North American flowering annual plant

Examples

The vibrant yellow petals of the calliopsis brightened the summer garden.
Many gardeners choose calliopsis for easy-to-grow, colorful bedding plants.
In some botanical texts, calliopsis is considered a subgenus of Coreopsis.

Etymology

From Greek 'kallos' (beauty) and 'opsis' (appearance), referring to the attractive appearance of the flowers.
