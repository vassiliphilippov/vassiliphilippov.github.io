ignition

1. NOUN

/ ɪgˈnɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪgˈnɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the act of causing something to start burning or catching fire
the act of setting on fire

Examples

The ignition of the dry grass resulted in a rapidly spreading wildfire.
The engineer explained how precise timing of ignition is critical in car engines.
Careless ignition of flammable materials can have disastrous consequences.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: extinguishment - the act of putting something out

Etymology

From Latin ignitio (a setting on fire), from ignire (to set on fire).

2. NOUN

/ ɪɡˈnɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪɡˈnɪʃən / (BrE)
the mechanism in an internal-combustion engine that ignites the fuel-air mixture to start the engine
device that ignites engine fuel

Examples

The car wouldn't start because the ignition was faulty.
He turned the key in the ignition and the engine roared to life.
Modern vehicles often use push-button ignition systems instead of traditional keys.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: starter - device that starts an engine

Etymology

From Latin ignitio (setting on fire), from ignire (to set on fire), from ignis (fire).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03565747-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ɪɡˈnɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪɡˈnɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of starting combustion or causing something to catch fire
process of starting combustion

Examples

The ignition of the engine occurred as soon as she turned the key.
A single spark can lead to the ignition of flammable materials.
Scientists studied the ignition point of various chemicals in the lab.

Etymology

From Latin 'ignitio' (a kindling, setting on fire), from 'ignire' (to set on fire).
