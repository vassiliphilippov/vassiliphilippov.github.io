catcher

1. NOUN

/ ˈkætʃər / (AmE), / ˈkatʃə / (BrE)
in baseball, the player who crouches behind home plate and catches pitches that the batter does not hit
baseball player who catches pitches

Examples

The team's catcher called for a fastball on the next pitch.
As a catcher, she had to wear a lot of protective gear.
The catcher quickly threw the ball to second base to catch the runner.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pitcher - baseball player who throws the ball to the batter

2. NOUN

/ ˈkætʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈkatʃə / (BrE)
the player on a baseball team who is positioned behind home plate and catches pitches thrown by the pitcher
player behind home plate in baseball

Examples

The team's catcher signaled for a fastball in the ninth inning.
As a catcher, she needs to stay alert for every pitch.
The new catcher quickly adapted to working with different pitchers.

Synset Examples

a catcher needs a lot of protective equipment
a catcher plays behind the plate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitcher - the player who throws the baseball to the catcher
