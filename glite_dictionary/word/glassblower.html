glassblower

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlæsˌbloʊər / (AmE), / ˈɡlɑːsˌbləʊə / (BrE)
a person who shapes molten glass by blowing air into it, especially to make bottles and other objects
person skilled at shaping glass by blowing

Examples

The glassblower shaped an intricate vase from the glowing molten glass.
As a skilled glassblower, she crafts delicate ornaments by hand.
Visitors watched the glassblower demonstrate his craft at the museum workshop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glazier - a person who fits glass into windows and doors
  • Synonym: potter - a person who makes pottery from clay

Etymology

From 'glass' + 'blower', indicating one who blows (forms) glass.
