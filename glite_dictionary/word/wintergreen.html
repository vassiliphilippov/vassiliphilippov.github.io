wintergreen

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪn.tɚˌɡrin / (AmE), / ˈwɪn.təˌɡriːn / (BrE)
an evergreen perennial plant of the genus Pyrola or related genera, often found in woodlands and known for aromatic leaves
an evergreen woodland plant, genus Pyrola

Examples

The forest floor was dotted with patches of wintergreen, their shiny leaves catching the light.
She identified the plant as wintergreen by its distinctive aroma and small white flowers.
Many herbalists admire wintergreen for its fragrant leaves and medicinal properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pyrola - the genus of herbaceous evergreen plants known as wintergreen

Etymology

Named for its foliage, which remains green in winter.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪntərˌɡrin / (AmE), / ˈwɪntəɡriːn / (BrE)
a low-growing evergreen plant of the heath family with spicy red berries, which is the source of wintergreen oil; especially the North American species Gaultheria procumbens.
evergreen plant with spicy red berries, source of oil

Examples

The woods were dotted with patches of wintergreen and their bright red berries.
She picked a few wintergreen leaves to make a soothing tea.
The aroma of wintergreen oil is commonly used in candies and medicinal products.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teaberry - another term for wintergreen (Gaultheria procumbens)
  • Synonym: evergreen - plant that retains green leaves throughout the year

Etymology

Named for its evergreen foliage that stays green in winter; from 'winter' + 'green'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈwɪntɚˌɡrin / (AmE), / ˈwɪntəˌɡriːn / (BrE)
a low, creeping North American shrub (Gaultheria procumbens) with shiny aromatic leaves, white bell-shaped flowers, and spicy red berries, often used to produce wintergreen oil
a North American shrub with aromatic leaves

Examples

The aroma of wintergreen filled the room after she rubbed the leaves between her fingers.
Hikers found patches of wintergreen along the forest trail in the summer.
Wintergreen oil is often used as a flavoring in candies and chewing gum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teaberry - another name for wintergreen or its berry

Etymology

Refers to evergreen plants that remain green through winter; from 'winter' + 'green'.
