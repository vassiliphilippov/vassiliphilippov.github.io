chromoblastomycosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌkroʊ.moʊˌblæstəˌmaɪˈkoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌkrəʊ.məʊˌblæstəʊ.maɪˈkəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A chronic fungal infection of the skin, characterized by itchy, warty nodules and plaques.
fungal skin infection with warty nodules
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with chromoblastomycosis after developing persistent, warty lesions on his leg.
Treatment for chromoblastomycosis often requires long-term antifungal medication and sometimes surgical removal of lesions.
Farmers working barefoot in tropical regions are at higher risk of contracting chromoblastomycosis.

Etymology

From Greek 'chroma' (color), 'blastos' (germ, bud), and 'mycosis' (fungal infection).
