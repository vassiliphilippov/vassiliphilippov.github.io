Erianthus

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈraɪænθəs / (AmE), / ɪˈraɪænθəs / (BrE)
A genus of tall, reedlike grasses with spikes clustered in a panicle covered by long silky hairs.
a genus of reedlike grasses

Examples

Species of Erianthus are often used as ornamental grasses in gardens.
The tall stems of Erianthus sway gracefully in the wind.
Researchers studied the distribution of Erianthus in subtropical regions.

Etymology

From Greek 'erion' (wool) and 'anthos' (flower), referring to the silky hairs on the spikelets.

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈraɪənθəs / (AmE), / ɪˈraɪənθəs / (BrE)
A genus of reedlike grasses with dense flower spikes covered by long silky hairs, found mainly in tropical and subtropical regions.
genus of reedlike grasses

Examples

The botanist discovered an unusual species of Erianthus growing near the wetlands.
Several varieties of Erianthus are used for ornamental purposes in gardens.
Because of its tall, reedlike appearance, Erianthus can often be mistaken for sugarcane.

Etymology

From Greek erion (wool) and anthos (flower), referring to the silky hairs on the flowers.
