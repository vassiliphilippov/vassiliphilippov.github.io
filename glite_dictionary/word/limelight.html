limelight

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪmˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈlaɪmˌlaɪt / (BrE)
public attention and interest, especially when someone is the center of it
public attention; center of interest

Examples

After her breakthrough performance, the actress found herself in the limelight for months.
Many politicians relish the limelight, but others prefer to work behind the scenes.
The discovery brought the scientist into the limelight after years of unnoticed research.

Synset Examples

he enjoyed being in the limelight
when Congress investigates it brings the full glare of publicity to the agency

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spotlight - a focus of public attention

Etymology

Originally referred to a type of stage lighting using quicklime, from the 19th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪmˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈlaɪmlaɪt / (BrE)
a type of stage lighting, formerly used in theaters, in which a flame is directed at a cylinder of lime to produce a bright white light
historical stage lighting device

Examples

The actors performed under the limelight throughout the entire play.
In the 19th century, theaters commonly used limelight for stage illumination.
The invention of electric lights eventually replaced limelight in theaters.

Etymology

From 'lime' (the chemical compound, calcium oxide) and 'light', referring to the method of illumination.
