discouraging

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈskɝːɪdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪsˈkʌrɪdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
Causing someone to lose confidence, hope, or enthusiasm, often deterring them from taking action.
causing loss of confidence or hope

Examples

The news was discouraging, but we decided to keep trying anyway.
Her discouraging remarks made him reconsider his plans.
It can be discouraging to study hard and still get poor results.

Synset Examples

where never is heard a discouraging word

Related Terms

  • Antonym: encouraging - giving someone support or confidence; supportive

Etymology

From discourage (from Old French decouragier, from de- + corage ‘courage’).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00870521-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ dɪsˈkɝː.rɪ.dʒɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪsˈkʌr.ɪ.dʒɪŋ / (BrE)
expressing disapproval or lack of support in order to prevent or dissuade someone from doing something
expressing disapproval to dissuade

Examples

Her discouraging comments made him reconsider his decision to apply for the job.
The teacher's discouraging tone affected the students' motivation.
It can be difficult to pursue your goals when facing discouraging feedback from others.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: encouraging - giving support, confidence, or hope to someone

Etymology

From discourage + -ing, derived from Old French 'descoragier' (to deprive of courage).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01775905-s
Synset POS: s
