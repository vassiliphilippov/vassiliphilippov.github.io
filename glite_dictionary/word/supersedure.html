supersedure

1. NOUN

/ suː.pɚˈsiː.dʒɚ / (AmE), / suː.pəˈsiː.dʒə / (BrE)
the act of replacing one person or thing by another, especially by someone or something considered superior
the act of replacement by a superior

Examples

The board's supersedure of the old manager was met with mixed reactions.
Sudden supersedure of regulations can disrupt established business practices.
In beekeeping, supersedure occurs when a new queen replaces the old one.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: replacement - the act of replacing one thing with another

Etymology

From Latin supersedēre (‘to sit above, to supersede’).
