coastward

1. ADV

/ ˈkoʊstwɚd / (AmE), / ˈkəʊstwəd / (BrE)
toward or in the direction of the coast
toward the coast

Examples

The birds began to fly coastward as winter approached.
We drove coastward for several miles before stopping at a small village.
Clouds moved coastward ahead of the approaching storm.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inland - toward the interior of a country or land, away from the coast

Etymology

Formed from 'coast' + 'ward', following the productive English suffix for direction.
