to outdo

1. VERB

/ aʊtˈdu / (AmE), / aʊtˈduː / (BrE)
to do better than someone or something; to surpass in performance or achievement
do better than; surpass

Examples

Every year, the company strives to outdo its previous results.
She managed to outdo her competitors with her creative presentation.
The chef tried to outdo himself with an even more elaborate dessert.

Synset Examples

her performance surpasses that of any other student I know
She outdoes all other athletes
This exceeds all my expectations
This car outperforms all others in its class

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to surpass - to be better than; to exceed; to go beyond
  • Synonym: to exceed - to be greater in number or degree; to go beyond a limit
  • Antonym: to fall short - to fail to reach a standard or expectation

2. VERB

/ aʊtˈdu / (AmE), / aʊtˈduː / (BrE)
to do better than someone else; to surpass in performance, achievement, or quality
to surpass; do better than

Examples

Each year the neighbors try to outdo one another with holiday decorations.
She managed to outdo all her previous performances in last night's concert.
No matter how hard I try, my sister always seems to outdo me in everything.

Synset Examples

the goal was to best the competition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to surpass - to do or be better than someone or something
  • Synonym: to excel - to be exceptionally good at or proficient in an activity or subject
  • Antonym: to fall short - to not reach the required or expected standard

Etymology

From Old English 'ūtdōn' (to do out, surpass).
