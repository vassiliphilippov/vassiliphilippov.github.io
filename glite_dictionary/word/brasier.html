brasier

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪ.ʒər / (AmE), / ˈbræz.i.ə / (BrE)
a large metal container in which coal or charcoal is burned to provide warmth, especially outdoors
large metal container for burning fuel outdoors

Examples

The construction crew gathered around the brasier to warm their hands on the cold morning.
A brasier stood in the center of the market square, providing heat for the vendors.
The security guards used a brasier to stay warm during their overnight shift outside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brazier - a portable heater consisting of a pan or stand for holding lighted coals

Etymology

From French 'brasier', meaning 'brazier' or 'hearth'.
