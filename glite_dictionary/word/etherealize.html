to etherealize

1. VERB

/ ɪˈθɪriəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ɪˈθɪəriəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make ethereal, or to render something delicate, airy, or spiritual in quality
make ethereal or spiritual

Examples

The artist sought to etherealize the landscape, using light colors and flowing brushstrokes.
Her music seems to etherealize even the most ordinary emotions, making them feel otherworldly.
In his poetry, he tried to etherealize the mundane aspects of daily life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to spiritualize - to make spiritual or invest with a spiritual meaning

Etymology

From ethereal + -ize; ethereal from Latin 'aetherius', Greek 'aitherios' (of the upper air), plus English verb-forming suffix '-ize'.
