Arabidopsis lyrata

1. NOUN

/ ˌær.ə.bɪˈdɑp.sɪs lɪˈreɪ.tə / (AmE), / ˌær.ə.bɪˈdɒp.sɪs lɪˈrɑː.tə / (BrE)
A small, noninvasive, cross-pollinating plant with white flowers, closely related to Arabidopsis thaliana; commonly used in plant genetics research.
species of noninvasive flowering plant

Examples

Arabidopsis lyrata is frequently used in genetic studies due to its close relation to Arabidopsis thaliana.
Unlike its relative, Arabidopsis lyrata prefers open, rocky habitats in northern regions.
Researchers have found that Arabidopsis lyrata exhibits natural variation in drought resistance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arabidopsis thaliana - a related species widely used as a model organism in plant biology

Etymology

From New Latin; genus name Arabidopsis from Arabic + Greek -opsis (appearance), species epithet lyrata (lyre-shaped) from Latin.
