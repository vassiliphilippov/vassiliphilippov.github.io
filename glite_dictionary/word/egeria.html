Egeria

1. NOUN

/ əˈdʒɪəriə / (AmE), / ɪˈdʒɪəriə / (BrE)
a small genus of tropical aquatic plants, consisting of dioecious species commonly cultivated in aquariums
genus of tropical aquatic plants

Examples

Egeria is often used in aquariums because of its rapid growth and oxygenating abilities.
Some species of Egeria can become invasive outside their native habitats.
The aquatic plant Egeria densa is popular among aquarium enthusiasts worldwide.

Etymology

From Latin Egeria, the name of a Roman nymph, after whom the genus was named.
