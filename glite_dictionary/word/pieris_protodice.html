Pieris protodice

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪ.ərɪs prəˈtoʊ.daɪs / (AmE), / ˈpaɪərɪs prəˈtəʊdɪse / (BrE)
A species of white butterfly found in North America, commonly known as the veined white or Southern cabbage butterfly.
a species of North American white butterfly

Examples

A Pieris protodice was resting on the leaf, its veined wings catching the sunlight.
Gardeners often mistake the Pieris protodice for its close relative, the cabbage white.
The Pieris protodice population increases rapidly during the warmer months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cabbage butterfly - a common name for white butterflies of the genus Pieris

Etymology

From New Latin; genus name 'Pieris' is of Greek origin, species name 'protodice' is from Greek mythology.
