lifelessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪf.ləs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈlaɪf.ləs.nəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being without movement, energy, or vitality
absence of movement or vitality

Examples

The lifelessness of the deserted building made the atmosphere eerie.
A certain lifelessness pervaded the landscape after the storm passed.
Her voice carried a lifelessness that suggested deep exhaustion.

Synset Examples

the utter motionlessness of a marble statue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motionlessness - the state of not moving; stillness
  • Antonym: animation - liveliness; the state of being full of life or movement

Etymology

From 'lifeless' + '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪf.ləs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈlaɪf.ləs.nəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of having no life, energy, or vitality
lack of life or vitality

Examples

There was a disturbing lifelessness in his eyes after the accident.
The lifelessness of the abandoned house made it seem even colder and more unwelcoming.
Critics complained about the lifelessness of the actor's performance in the leading role.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vitality - the state of being strong and active; energy
  • Antonym: animation - the presence of liveliness or spirit

Etymology

Formed from 'lifeless' + '-ness', where 'lifeless' comes from 'life' + '-less'.
