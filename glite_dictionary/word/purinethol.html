Purinethol

1. NOUN

/ pʊˈrɪnəˌθɑl / (AmE), / pjʊˈrɪnəˌθɒl / (BrE)
A trademarked drug used to treat acute lymphocytic leukemia by interfering with purine metabolism; brand name for mercaptopurine.
drug for leukemia; trade name for mercaptopurine
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Purinethol as part of her chemotherapy treatment for leukemia.
Some patients taking Purinethol may experience side effects that require close monitoring.
Purinethol works by interfering with the body's purine metabolism, slowing the growth of cancer cells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mercaptopurine - the generic drug name for Purinethol

Etymology

Trade name coined from 'purine' (a nitrogenous compound) and '-thol', possibly referencing chemical structure.
