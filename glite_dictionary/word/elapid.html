elapid

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛləpɪd / (AmE), / ˈɛləpɪd / (BrE)
a snake belonging to the family Elapidae, which includes many venomous species such as cobras and coral snakes, typically found in warmer regions worldwide
venomous snake of the Elapidae family

Examples

The coastal taipan is an extremely dangerous elapid found in Australia.
Many species of elapids possess highly potent neurotoxic venom.
Unlike vipers, elapid snakes generally have fixed fangs at the front of their mouths.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viper - a venomous snake of the family Viperidae

Etymology

From modern Latin Elapidae, from Elaps (a genus name) and Greek elaps (a kind of snake).
