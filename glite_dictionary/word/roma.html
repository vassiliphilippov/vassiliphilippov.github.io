Roma

1. PROPN

/ ˈroʊmə / (AmE), / ˈrəʊmə / (BrE)
the Italian name for Rome, the capital and largest city of Italy, on the Tiber River, historically significant as the seat of the Roman Catholic Church and the former capital of the Roman Republic and Roman Empire
Italian name for Rome, Italy's capital

Examples

The Pope resides in Roma, the heart of the Roman Catholic Church.
Many tourists visit Roma each year to see its ancient ruins.
The story is set in Roma during the height of the Roman Empire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rome - capital city of Italy; seat of the Roman Catholic Church

Etymology

From Italian 'Roma', from Latin 'Roma'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈroʊmə / (AmE), / ˈrəʊmə / (BrE)
A member of a people of traditionally itinerant lifestyle, originally from northern India, now living mainly in Europe, North Africa, and North America, who speak the Romany language.
member of the Roma people

Examples

Many Roma faced discrimination when migrating to Western European countries.
The cultural traditions of the Roma have enriched music across the continent.
She studies the language and history of the Roma in Eastern Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Romani - the language spoken by the Roma people
  • Synonym: Gypsy - now often considered offensive term for Roma people

Etymology

Plural of Latin 'Romanus' (Roman), used in Romani language as a self-designation by the Roma people.
