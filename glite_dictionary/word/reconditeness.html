reconditeness

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈkɑndɪtnɪs / (AmE), / rɪˈkɒndɪtnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being difficult to understand or obscure; abstruseness.
quality of being obscure or hard to understand

Examples

Scholars sometimes delight in the reconditeness of philosophical texts, challenging their readers to think deeply.
The reconditeness of his lecture left many in the audience puzzled and lost.
Some critics argue that the novel's reconditeness detracts from its emotional impact.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abstruseness - the quality of being difficult to understand
  • Antonym: clarity - the quality of being easy to understand or clear

Etymology

Formed from 'recondite' + '-ness'; ultimately from Latin 'reconditus' (hidden, concealed).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈkɑn.dɪt.nəs / (AmE), / rɪˈkɒn.dɪt.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being difficult to understand because of being profound, abstruse, or complex
state of being obscure or profound

Examples

The reconditeness of the scholar's thesis made it inaccessible to most readers.
She was fascinated by the reconditeness of certain philosophical arguments.
The reconditeness of the poetry demanded careful study and interpretation.

Synset Examples

the anthropologist was impressed by the reconditeness of the native proverbs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obscurity - the state of being unknown, inconspicuous, or difficult to understand
  • Antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear and easy to understand

Etymology

From recondite (from Latin reconditus, from recondere 'put away, conceal') + -ness.
