mojarra

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈhɑːrə / (AmE), / məʊˈhɑːrə / (BrE)
a small silvery schooling fish with a protrusible mouth, found in warm coastal waters, especially of the family Gerridae
a type of small silvery coastal fish

Examples

Fishermen caught several mojarra near the mangrove shore early this morning.
The restaurant served grilled mojarra with fresh lime and rice.
Schools of mojarra often swim close to the surface in tropical bays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bream - a type of freshwater or marine fish, often used as a synonym for mojarra in some contexts

Etymology

From Spanish 'mojarra' (type of fish), likely from Arabic 'muxarra' (pointed, sharp), referring to the fish's body shape.
