Coleus aromaticus

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈliː.əs ˌær.əˈmæt.ɪ.kəs / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ.li.əs ˌær.əˈmæt.ɪ.kəs / (BrE)
an aromatic fleshy herb native to India, Sri Lanka, and southern Africa, sometimes classified in the genus Plectranthus
aromatic herb native to India and Africa

Examples

Coleus aromaticus is widely grown in home gardens for its medicinal properties.
Tea made from Coleus aromaticus leaves is used in traditional remedies.
The aroma of Coleus aromaticus makes it popular in culinary and ornamental gardening.

Etymology

From modern Latin, combining genus Coleus and Latin aromaticus ('aromatic').
