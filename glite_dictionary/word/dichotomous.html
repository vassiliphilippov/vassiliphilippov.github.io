dichotomous

1. ADJ

/ daɪˈkɑːtəmoʊs / (AmE), / daɪˈkɒtəməs / (BrE)
Consisting of or involving two sharply defined or mutually exclusive parts or categories.
divided into two distinct parts

Examples

The debate presented a dichotomous view of politics, reducing choices to two starkly opposed options.
Many problems are not as dichotomous as they first appear; real life often involves shades of gray.
The classification system is dichotomous, separating organisms strictly into two groups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: binary - relating to or composed of two things
  • Synonym: dual - consisting of two parts or elements
  • Antonym: unified - formed or united into a whole

Etymology

From Greek dikhotomos (cut in two), from dikho- (in two) + tomos (a cutting).
