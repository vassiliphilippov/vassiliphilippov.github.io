AFL

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ ˌɛf ˈɛl / (AmE), / ˌeɪ ˌɛf ˈɛl / (BrE)
an abbreviation for American Federation of Labor, a federation of North American labor unions that merged with the Congress of Industrial Organizations in 1955
federation of North American labor unions

Examples

The AFL played a major role in advocating for workers' rights in the early 20th century.
In 1955, the AFL officially merged with the CIO, forming a larger labor organization.
Leaders of the AFL were influential in shaping labor policies in the United States.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: AFL–CIO - the merged federation of the American Federation of Labor and Congress of Industrial Organizations

Etymology

Abbreviation of American Federation of Labor.
