cytokinin

1. NOUN

/ saɪ.t̬oʊˈkaɪ.nɪn / (AmE), / saɪ.təʊˈkaɪ.nɪn / (BrE)
any of a class of plant hormones that stimulate cell division and delay aging in plant tissues, especially leaves
plant hormone promoting cell division
medical

Examples

The application of cytokinin helped the researchers keep the plant leaves green for a longer period.
High concentrations of cytokinin can induce rapid cell division in tissue cultures.
A deficiency in cytokinin may lead to premature aging of leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: auxin - a plant hormone that regulates growth and development
  • Synonym: gibberellin - a plant hormone that promotes growth and elongation of cells

Etymology

From Greek 'kytos' (cell) and 'kinin' (that which moves), reflecting its role in stimulating cell activity.
