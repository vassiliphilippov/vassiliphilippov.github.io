oxyacetylene

1. NOUN

/ ɑksiəˈsɛtəliːn / (AmE), / ɒksiəˈsɛtɪliːn / (BrE)
a mixture of oxygen and acetylene gases, used to produce a high-temperature flame for cutting or welding metals
oxygen and acetylene gas mixture for welding/cutting

Examples

The mechanic used an oxyacetylene torch to weld the broken parts together.
Proper ventilation is crucial when working with oxyacetylene equipment to ensure safety.
Training is required before operating any oxyacetylene cutting tools in the workshop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acetylene - a colorless flammable gas used as a fuel in welding
  • Synonym: oxygen - a colorless, odorless gas that supports combustion

Etymology

Formed from 'oxy-' (short for oxygen) and 'acetylene' (a hydrocarbon gas), indicating the mixture's components.
