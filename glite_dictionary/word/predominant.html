predominant

1. ADJ

/ prɪˈdɑːmənənt / (AmE), / prɪˈdɒmɪnənt / (BrE)
more frequent, common, or powerful than others; most prevalent or widespread.
most frequent or common

Examples

Bamboo is the predominant plant species in this forest.
English is the predominant language spoken in the region.
The predominant color in the painting is blue.

Synset Examples

prevailing winds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prevalent - widespread or common in a particular area or at a particular time

Etymology

From Latin 'praedominari' (to rule over), from 'prae-' (before) + 'dominari' (to dominate).

2. ADJ

/ prɪˈdɑmɪnənt / (AmE), / prɪˈdɒmɪnənt / (BrE)
having more power, influence, or importance than others; most common or noticeable
having more power or influence; most important

Examples

The predominant color in her paintings is blue.
English is the predominant language spoken in the region.
His predominant emotion after hearing the news was relief.

Synset Examples

the predominant mood among policy-makers is optimism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dominant - having the greatest power or influence
  • Antonym: subordinate - lower in rank or position

Etymology

From Latin prae- (before) + dominari (to rule).

3. ADJ

/ prɪˈdɑːmɪnənt / (AmE), / prɪˈdɒmɪnənt / (BrE)
Having superior power, influence, or importance; most common or noticeable within a group or situation.
having greater power or influence

Examples

The predominant language spoken in the region is Spanish.
A predominant theme in her novels is the struggle for independence.
The predominant view among scientists is that climate change is a serious threat.

Synset Examples

the predominant mood among policy-makers is optimism

Related Terms

  • Antonym: secondary - less important or influential
  • Synonym: chief - the most important, main

Etymology

From Latin prae- (before) + dominari (to rule).
