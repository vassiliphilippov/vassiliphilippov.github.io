scamper

1. VERB

/ ˈskæmpɚ / (AmE), / ˈskampə / (BrE)
to move quickly and playfully, especially with small, hurried steps
move quickly or playfully with small steps

Examples

The children scampered across the playground as soon as the bell rang.
A squirrel scampered up the tree to escape the barking dog.
She scampered down the hallway, her footsteps echoing behind her.

Synset Examples

so terrified by the extraordinary ebbing of the sea that they scurried to higher ground

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scurry - to move quickly with short, hurried steps
  • Antonym: to dawdle - to move slowly and waste time

Etymology

Probably from Middle Dutch *schampen* (to slip away).

2. NOUN

/ ˈskæmpər / (AmE), / ˈskæmpə / (BrE)
a hurried, playful, or undignified run or movement
a quick, playful run

Examples

The children broke into a scamper as soon as the bell rang for recess.
A sudden scamper of feet was heard in the hallway.
After the thunder, there was a scamper of animals seeking shelter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dash - a quick or sudden run
  • Antonym: saunter - to walk in a slow, relaxed manner

Etymology

Of uncertain origin, perhaps imitative or related to Scandinavian roots meaning 'skirt, brush, or brisk movement'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00557179-n
Synset POS: n
