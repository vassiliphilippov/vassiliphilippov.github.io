shamed

1. ADJ

/ ʃeɪmd / (AmE), / ʃeɪmd / (BrE)
feeling or showing a sense of guilt or disgrace; suffering from shame.
feeling ashamed or disgraced

Examples

He walked out of the courtroom shamed and humiliated by the verdict.
The student felt shamed after being caught cheating on the test.
She looked shamed as she apologized for her mistake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ashamed - feeling shame or embarrassment

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'shame', from Old English *scamu*.

2. ADJ

/ ʃeɪmd / (AmE), / ʃeɪmd / (BrE)
feeling or showing a sense of guilt, embarrassment, or remorse
feeling or displaying guilt or embarrassment

Examples

He looked shamed after being caught in the act.
The shamed student apologized for cheating on the test.
She wore a shamed expression as she admitted her mistake.

Synset Examples

a guilty look
the hangdog and shamefaced air of the retreating enemy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ashamed - feeling shame or guilt; embarrassed
  • Antonym: proud - feeling deep pleasure or satisfaction as a result of achievements, qualities, or possessions
