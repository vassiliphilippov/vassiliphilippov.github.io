Siddhartha

1. PROPN

/ sɪˈdɑr tə / (AmE), / sɪˈdɑːtə / (BrE)
the founder of Buddhism, also known as Gautama Buddha, who lived in ancient India circa 563–483 BC
founder of Buddhism; Gautama Buddha

Examples

Many consider Siddhartha to be one of the most influential spiritual leaders in world history.
The teachings of Siddhartha form the foundation of Buddhist philosophy and ethics.
Statues of Siddhartha can be found in temples throughout Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Buddha - title given to Siddhartha Gautama and other enlightened ones in Buddhism
  • Synonym: Gautama Buddha - another name for Siddhartha; the historical Buddha

Etymology

From Sanskrit Siddhārtha, meaning 'one who has attained his aim'.
