insubstantiality

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.səbˌstæn.ʃiˈæ.lə.ti / (AmE), / ˌɪn.səbˌstæn.ʃiˈæl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality or state of lacking substance, strength, or reality; the condition of being insubstantial.
lack of substance or reality

Examples

The insubstantiality of his argument was quickly exposed during the debate.
In the moonlight, the shadowy figures gained an eerie insubstantiality.
She dismissed the rumor, recognizing its complete insubstantiality.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: substantiality - the quality of having substance or being substantial
  • Synonym: insubstantial - lacking physical substance or reality (adjective)

Etymology

From 'insubstantial' + '-ity', following Latin pattern of forming nouns from adjectives.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪnsəbˌstænʃiˈæləti / (AmE), / ˌɪnsəbˌstænʃɪˈælɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of lacking solid substance, strength, or reality.
lack of substance or strength

Examples

The insubstantiality of his argument was quickly exposed during the debate.
She was dismayed by the insubstantiality of the building's walls.
Some critics noted the insubstantiality of the film's plot, saying it lacked depth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insubstantial - lacking solidity or strength
  • Antonym: substantiality - the quality of having substance or strength

Etymology

From Latin 'substantia' (substance) with the prefix 'in-' (not) and the suffix '-ity'.
