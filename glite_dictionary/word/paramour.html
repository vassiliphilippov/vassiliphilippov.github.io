paramour

1. NOUN

/ ˈpær.əˌmɔr / (AmE), / ˈpær.əˌmɔː / (BrE)
a lover, especially one in a romantic or sexual relationship that is secret or illicit
secret or illicit lover

Examples

Rumors spread quickly about the king and his mysterious paramour.
The story revolves around a noblewoman and her secret paramour.
When confronted by her husband, she finally admitted to meeting her paramour in the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lover - a person having a romantic or sexual relationship
  • Antonym: spouse - a married partner

Etymology

From Old French 'par amour' (through love); 'amor' meaning 'love'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpærəˌmʊr / (AmE), / ˈpærəmɔː / (BrE)
a lover, especially one with whom a person is having a romantic or sexual relationship in secret or outside of marriage
lover, especially a secret one

Examples

The king kept his paramour hidden away from the court.
Rumors spread quickly about the celebrity's new paramour.
She ended her relationship with her paramour after the affair became public.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lover - a person having a romantic or sexual relationship with someone
  • Antonym: spouse - a husband or wife; a married partner

Etymology

From Old French *par amour* (by love).
