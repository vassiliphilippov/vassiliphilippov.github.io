Tarsius glis

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑr.si.əs ɡlɪs / (AmE), / ˈtɑː.sɪ.əs ɡlɪs / (BrE)
A species of small nocturnal primate found in Southeast Asia, commonly known as a tarsier.
a species of tarsier (primate)

Examples

The Tarsius glis is known for its enormous eyes and long fingers adapted for leaping.
Scientists have studied the habitat of Tarsius glis in Malaysian rainforests.
Unlike other primates, Tarsius glis feeds almost exclusively on insects and small animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tarsier - a small, nocturnal prosimian primate of Southeast Asia

Etymology

From New Latin, Tarsius (genus of tarsiers) + glis (Latin for 'dormouse').
