Lunda cirrhata

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌndə sɪˈreɪtə / (AmE), / ˈlʌndə sɪˈrɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of puffin found in the northern Pacific, notable for its large yellow tufts extending from above each eye.
tufted puffin species of the northern Pacific

Examples

The Lunda cirrhata is easily identified by its distinctive yellow facial plumes during the breeding season.
Researchers observed several Lunda cirrhata nesting on the rocky cliffs of the Aleutian Islands.
Unlike other puffins, the Lunda cirrhata spends much of its year at sea, only returning to land to breed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tufted puffin - a common name for Lunda cirrhata

Etymology

From the genus name Lunda and species name cirrhata, from Latin 'cirratus', meaning 'having tendrils'.
