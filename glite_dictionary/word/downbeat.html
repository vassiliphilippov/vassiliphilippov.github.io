downbeat

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊnˌbiːt / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnˌbiːt / (BrE)
The first beat of a musical measure, typically marked by a downward gesture from the conductor.
first beat of a musical measure

Examples

The conductor signaled the downbeat, and the orchestra began to play in perfect unison.
Every measure starts on the downbeat, making it crucial for musicians to stay in time.
The drummer emphasized the downbeat to help keep everyone together during the performance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: upbeat - the last beat of a measure, preceding the downbeat; often marked by an upward conductor motion

Etymology

From 'down' + 'beat', referring to the downward motion of the conductor's baton marking the first beat.
