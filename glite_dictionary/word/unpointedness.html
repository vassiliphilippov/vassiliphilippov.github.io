unpointedness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈpɔɪntɪdnɪs / (AmE), / ʌnˈpɔɪntɪdnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of having only a dull or rounded tip, or none at all.
state of lacking a sharp point

Examples

The unpointedness of the tool made it unsuitable for piercing the material.
You could feel the unpointedness of the pencil after so much use.
The unpointedness of her remarks softened the criticism.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pointedness - the quality or state of having a sharp or definite point

Etymology

Formed from 'unpointed' (not having a point) + '-ness' (state or quality).
