Lepidocybium flavobrunneum

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛpɪdoʊˈsaɪbiəm ˌflævoʊˈbrʌniəm / (AmE), / ˌlɛpɪdəʊˈsɪbɪəm ˌflævəʊˈbrʌnɪəm / (BrE)
A large species of snake mackerel known for the rings that resemble spectacles around its eyes, commonly called escolar.
large snake mackerel; escolar

Examples

The Lepidocybium flavobrunneum is often marketed under the name escolar in seafood restaurants.
Scientists studied the migratory patterns of Lepidocybium flavobrunneum in the Atlantic Ocean.
Consumption of Lepidocybium flavobrunneum can sometimes cause digestive discomfort due to its high oil content.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escolar - common name for Lepidocybium flavobrunneum

Etymology

From Greek 'lepis' (scale) + 'kybion' (small cube) and Latin 'flavus' (yellow) + 'brunneus' (brown), referring to its appearance.
