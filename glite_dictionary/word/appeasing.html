appeasing

1. ADJ

/ əˈpizɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈpiːzɪŋ / (BrE)
intended to pacify or calm someone by giving in to their demands or making concessions.
seeking to pacify by making concessions

Examples

The government's appeasing approach only encouraged more demands from the protestors.
She gave her son an appeasing smile to calm him down after his tantrum.
Critics argue that an appeasing policy can lead to greater risks in the future.

Synset Examples

the appeasing concessions to the Nazis at Munich
placating (or placative) gestures
an astonishingly placatory speech

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conciliatory - intended to gain goodwill or reduce hostility
  • Antonym: provocative - causing anger or another strong reaction, opposite of appeasing

Etymology

Present participle of 'appease', from Old French 'apaisier', from Vulgar Latin *apasiare.
