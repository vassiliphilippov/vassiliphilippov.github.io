frat

1. NOUN

/ fræt / (AmE), / fræt / (BrE)
an informal social club for male undergraduates, especially in North American colleges and universities
informal male student club (US)
informal american

Examples

James joined a frat during his first year at university.
The frat organized a charity event for the local community.
Some people think life in a frat is all about parties and pranks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fraternity - a social organization for male students at a college or university
  • Antonym: sorority - a social organization for female students at a college or university

Etymology

Shortened form of 'fraternity' (Latin fraternitas, brotherhood).
