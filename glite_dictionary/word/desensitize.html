to desensitize

1. VERB

/ diˈsɛnsɪˌtaɪz / (AmE), / diˈsɛnsɪˌtaɪz / (BrE)
to make someone or something less sensitive to physical or emotional stimuli, often by repeated exposure or experience
make less sensitive

Examples

Repeated exposure to violent movies can desensitize viewers to real-life violence.
Years of working in the emergency room have desensitized her to the sight of blood.
Some allergy treatments aim to desensitize patients to specific allergens.

Synset Examples

His military training desensitized him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: numb - deprive of feeling or responsiveness
  • Antonym: to sensitize - make sensitive or more responsive to stimulus

Etymology

From de- (removal, reversal) + sensitize.

2. VERB

/ diˈsɛnsɪtaɪz / (AmE), / diˈsɛnsɪtaɪz / (BrE)
to make someone or something less sensitive to physical or emotional stimuli, or to reduce a reaction to a particular event or situation
make less sensitive

Examples

Continuous exposure to violent media can desensitize viewers to real-life suffering.
Doctors sometimes desensitize patients to allergens through gradual exposure.
Repeated warnings about storms may desensitize people, causing them to ignore alerts.

Synset Examples

The war desensitized many soldiers
The photographic plate was desensitized

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to sensitize - make more sensitive

Etymology

From de- (expressing reversal) + sensitize (from sense).

Synset POS: v
