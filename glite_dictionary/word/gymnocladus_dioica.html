Gymnocladus dioica

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪm.nəˌklæd.əs daɪˈoʊ.ɪ.kə / (AmE), / dʒɪm.nəˌklæd.əs dɪˈɒɪ.kə / (BrE)
A species of tree native to central and eastern North America, known for its large bipinnate leaves, greenish-white flowers, and brown woody pods whose seeds have been used as a coffee substitute; commonly called the Kentucky coffee tree.
species of tree (Kentucky coffee tree)

Examples

The Gymnocladus dioica is easily recognized by its large, leathery pods hanging from the branches in autumn.
Botanists value Gymnocladus dioica for its resilience to urban pollution and unique foliage.
Seeds from Gymnocladus dioica were once roasted and used as a coffee substitute in pioneer times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kentucky coffee tree - North American tree also known as Gymnocladus dioica

Etymology

From Greek 'gymnos' (naked) + 'klados' (branch) and Latin 'dioica' (of two houses, referring to separate male and female trees).
