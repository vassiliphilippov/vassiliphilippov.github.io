enlarger

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈlɑrdʒər / (AmE), / ɪnˈlɑːdʒə / (BrE)
a device used in photography to project and enlarge images from negatives onto photographic paper
photographic device for enlarging images

Examples

The photographer used an enlarger to create a larger print of the negative.
She carefully adjusted the enlarger lens to bring the image into sharp focus.
Most darkrooms are equipped with at least one enlarger for making photographic prints.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: projector - device for displaying images or film by projecting light

Etymology

From 'enlarge' + '-er', denoting an agent or device that enlarges.
