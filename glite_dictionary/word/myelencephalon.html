myelencephalon

1. NOUN

/ maɪ.əˌlɛnˈsɛf.əˌlɑn / (AmE), / maɪ.əˌlɛnˈsɛf.əlɒn / (BrE)
the posterior part of the embryonic hindbrain in vertebrates, which develops into the medulla oblongata in adults
posterior part of the embryonic hindbrain
medical

Examples

During fetal development, the myelencephalon gives rise to the medulla oblongata.
Damage to the myelencephalon can affect vital autonomic functions in vertebrates.
Researchers observed the early differentiation of the myelencephalon in amphibian embryos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: medulla oblongata - the lower half of the brainstem derived from the myelencephalon

Etymology

From Greek myelos (marrow) + enkephalos (brain).
