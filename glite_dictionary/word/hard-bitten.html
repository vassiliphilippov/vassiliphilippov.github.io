hard-bitten

1. ADJ

/ hɑrdˈbɪtən / (AmE), / hɑːdˈbɪtən / (BrE)
tough and unemotional, especially as a result of difficult or harsh experiences
tough and unemotional from experience

Examples

The hard-bitten detective didn't flinch at the crime scene.
Years in the army had made him truly hard-bitten and fearless.
She played a hard-bitten journalist in the film.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tough - strong and able to deal with difficult situations
  • Synonym: callous - emotionally insensitive or unfeeling
  • Antonym: soft-hearted - kind and easily moved to pity or compassion

Etymology

From 'hard' + 'bitten', originally referring to something that has been bitten or affected harshly, later figuratively applied to people.
