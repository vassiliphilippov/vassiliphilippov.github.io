Magna Charta

1. PROPN

/ ˌmæɡnə ˈkɑrtə / (AmE), / ˌmaɡnə ˈkɑːtə / (BrE)
the royal charter of rights agreed to by King John of England in 1215, seen as a foundational document in the development of constitutional government
historic English charter of rights (1215)

Examples

The principles established by the Magna Charta continue to influence modern legal systems.
Many people regard the Magna Charta as the cornerstone of democracy in England.
Historians often debate the immediate impact of the Magna Charta after it was signed in 1215.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Great Charter - an alternate name for the Magna Charta

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'Magna Carta', meaning 'Great Charter'.
