pervert

1. VERB

/ pərˈvɝt / (AmE), / pəˈvɜːt / (BrE)
to alter something so that it serves a wrong or improper purpose; to distort or corrupt its original meaning or function
to distort from original purpose or meaning

Examples

He accused the officials of trying to pervert the original goals of the organization.
When laws are perverted for personal gain, justice is undermined.
Some technology can be perverted from its intended use to cause harm.

Synset Examples

Don't abuse the system
The director of the factory misused the funds intended for the health care of his workers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abuse - to use something for the wrong purpose
  • Synonym: misuse - to use something incorrectly or wrongly
  • Antonym: preserve - to maintain something in its original or existing state

Etymology

From Latin pervertere (to overturn, corrupt), from per- (thoroughly) + vertere (to turn).

2. NOUN

/ pərˈvɝt / (AmE), / pəˈvɜːt / (BrE)
a person whose behavior is considered abnormal or unacceptable, especially in a sexual context
person with unacceptable sexual behavior
offensive

Examples

Some people unfairly label others as a pervert based on rumors or misunderstandings.
The film portrays its villain as a dangerous pervert who preys on the vulnerable.
Calling someone a pervert can be deeply hurtful and is often considered offensive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deviant - a person with socially unacceptable behavior, especially sexual

Etymology

From Latin 'pervertere' (to turn around, corrupt).

/ pərˈvɜrt / (AmE), / pəˈvɜːt / (BrE)
to corrupt someone morally or lead them into immoral or improper behavior, especially by means of intemperance or sensuality.
to corrupt morally or lead astray

Examples

Some critics claimed the film would pervert the morals of its young audience.
Unscrupulous leaders may pervert public values for personal gain.
The teacher warned against books that could pervert impressionable minds.

From Latin pervertere (to turn around, corrupt), from per- (thoroughly) + vertere (to turn).

/ pərˈvɜrt / (AmE), / pəˈvɜːt / (BrE)
to deliberately distort or twist the meaning of something, especially to mislead or deceive
distort or twist meaning to mislead

Examples

He tried to pervert the facts to protect himself from blame.
Some politicians will pervert the truth to sway public opinion.
Please don't pervert what I said to fit your own agenda.

Synset Examples

Don't twist my words

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to distort - to change something so that it is no longer true or accurate
  • Synonym: to falsify - to alter information or evidence so as to mislead
  • Antonym: to clarify - to make something clear or easier to understand

Etymology

From Latin 'pervertere' (to overturn, corrupt), from 'per-' (thoroughly) + 'vertere' (to turn).
