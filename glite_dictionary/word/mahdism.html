Mahdism

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːˌdɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈmɑːˌdɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief in the appearance of the Mahdi, or devotion to a Mahdi, especially within Islamic tradition
belief in the coming of the Mahdi

Examples

Many followers of Mahdism believe in the imminent arrival of a divinely guided leader.
The rise of Mahdism had a significant impact on the region's history.
Academic debates often explore the influence of Mahdism on modern Islamic movements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Messianism - belief in a messiah or savior

Etymology

From Arabic mahdī (the guided one) + English -ism.
