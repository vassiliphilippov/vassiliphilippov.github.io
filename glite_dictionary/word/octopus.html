octopus

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑktəˌpʊs / (AmE), / ˈɒktəpəs / (BrE)
the tentacles of an octopus prepared and served as food, especially in Mediterranean or Asian cuisine
octopus tentacles as food

Examples

Grilled octopus is a delicacy in many Mediterranean countries.
She ordered octopus with lemon and olive oil at the seafood restaurant.
The chef served tender slices of marinated octopus as an appetizer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calamari - squid prepared as food

Etymology

From Greek 'oktōpous' (eight-footed), referring to the animal and its appendages.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑk.tə.pʊs / (AmE), / ˈɒk.tə.pəs / (BrE)
A sea animal with a soft oval body and eight long, flexible tentacles; a type of cephalopod.
sea mollusk with eight arms

Examples

The octopus quickly changed color to blend in with the rocks.
Scientists discovered a new species of octopus in the deep sea.
At the aquarium, children watched the octopus squeeze through a narrow opening.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squid - another type of cephalopod with a more elongated body and ten arms
  • Synonym: cuttlefish - a marine cephalopod with a broad body, eight arms, and two tentacles

Etymology

From Greek 'oktōpous' (eight feet).
