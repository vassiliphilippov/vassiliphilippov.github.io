out-and-outer

1. NOUN

/ ˌaʊt ən ˈaʊtər / (AmE), / ˌaʊt ən ˈaʊtə / (BrE)
a person who is exceptionally good at something; an outstanding individual
an outstanding or excellent person

Examples

Everyone agreed that she was an out-and-outer when it came to organizing events.
With his final performance, he proved himself an out-and-outer among athletes.
If you want advice on gardening, talk to Paul—he’s a real out-and-outer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: out-and-out - complete; thorough

Etymology

Formed from the phrase 'out-and-out', meaning 'completely', with '-er' added to indicate a person.
