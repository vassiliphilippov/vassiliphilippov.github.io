bye

1. NOUN

/ baɪ / (AmE), / baɪ / (BrE)
an instance in a tournament when a player or team automatically advances to the next round without having to compete, usually due to an uneven number of participants
automatic advance in a tournament

Examples

Maria received a bye in the first round because there were only seven players.
Due to the odd number of teams, one team was given a bye into the quarterfinals.
He was disappointed not to play, but the bye guaranteed his place in the next stage.

Synset Examples

he had a bye in the first round

Etymology

Originally a cricket term in the 18th century, possibly from the idea of 'passing by' a round.

2. NOUN

/ baɪ / (AmE), / baɪ / (BrE)
an instance or act of saying farewell; a remark or gesture made when parting
an act or remark of saying farewell

Examples

Everyone exchanged byes at the end of the party.
Their tearful bye at the airport was hard to watch.
She waved and called out a quick bye as the train pulled away.

Synset Examples

they said their good-byes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farewell - an act of saying goodbye
  • Antonym: greeting - an expression of welcome or salutation

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'goodbye', which is a contraction of 'God be with you'.
