pyrolatry

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈrɑːlətri / (AmE), / paɪˈrɒlətri / (BrE)
the worship of fire as a deity or sacred element
worship of fire

Examples

Some ancient cultures practiced pyrolatry, believing fire to be a purifying and divine force.
In the temple, pyrolatry ceremonies were marked by ritual bonfires.
The practice of pyrolatry has fascinated historians studying early human religions.

Etymology

From Greek 'pyr' (fire) and 'latreia' (worship).
