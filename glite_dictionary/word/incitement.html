incitement

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsaɪtmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈsaɪtmənt / (BrE)
the act of encouraging or provoking someone to take action, especially something unlawful or violent
the act of urging someone to action

Examples

His speech was accused of being an incitement to violence among the protesters.
The law strictly prohibits the incitement of hatred against any group.
She faced charges for the incitement of a public disturbance.

Synset Examples

the incitement of mutiny

Related Terms

  • Synonym: provocation - the action of provoking or stirring up
  • Antonym: deterrence - the act of discouraging an action through fear or doubt

Etymology

From Latin incitare (to urge on, excite), via Old French incitement.

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsaɪtmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈsaɪtmənt / (BrE)
the act of encouraging, provoking, or urging someone to take action, especially in a way that may lead to trouble or violence
the act of provoking or urging action

Examples

The leader's speech was seen as an incitement to violence by many people.
Publishing such content could be regarded as an incitement to break the law.
There was no clear incitement to riot in the article, according to the investigation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: instigation - the act of causing an event or action to happen, often something negative

Etymology

From Latin incitāmentum, from incitāre (to urge on).

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsaɪtmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈsaɪtmənt / (BrE)
the act of urging or persuading someone to take action, especially to do something wrong or unlawful
the act of urging or persuading action

Examples

His speech was seen as an incitement to violence by the authorities.
There was no evidence of incitement to commit any criminal act.
The government passed laws to prevent incitement of hatred between different groups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: encouragement - the act of giving someone support or confidence to do something
  • Antonym: deterrent - something that discourages someone from taking action

Etymology

From Latin 'incitamentum', from 'incitare' (to urge forward).

4. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsaɪtmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈsaɪtmənt / (BrE)
the act of encouraging or urging someone to behave in a particular way, especially to do something bad or illegal
the act of encouraging, urging, or provoking

Examples

The speaker's words acted as an incitement to the crowd to take action.
He was arrested on charges of incitement to violence.
The article was criticized for its incitement of racial hatred.

Synset Examples

the result was a provocation of vigorous investigation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: provocation - an action or statement that makes someone angry or incites them
  • Synonym: encouragement - the act of giving support, confidence, or hope to someone
  • Antonym: discouragement - the act of trying to prevent something by making it seem difficult or by making someone less willing to do it

Etymology

From Latin incitare (to urge on), via Old French incitement.
