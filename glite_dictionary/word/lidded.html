lidded

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɪdɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɪdɪd / (BrE)
having a lid; covered or equipped with a lid.
having a lid

Examples

She stored the cookies in a lidded jar to keep them fresh.
The market sold beautiful lidded baskets made by local artisans.
He poured tea from a lidded teapot into each cup.

Synset Examples

milk in a heavy lidded mug
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02451099-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈlɪdɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɪdɪd / (BrE)
having a lid or lids; covered with a lid or appearing as if with a lid, often used in combination.
covered with or having a lid

Examples

She drank her tea from a lidded travel mug to avoid spills.
The old chest was lidded, keeping its contents safe from dust.
He gazed at her with heavy-lidded eyes, half asleep.

Synset Examples

milk is left in a large lidded mug
heavy-lidded eyes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lidless - without a lid or lids; uncovered
