Loire

1. PROPN

/ lwaːr / (AmE), / lwaː(r) / (BrE)
the longest river in France, rising in the Massif Central and flowing north and west to the Atlantic Ocean
longest French river

Examples

The Loire Valley is famous for its vineyards and historic castles.
Many artists have been inspired by the picturesque landscapes along the Loire.
The city of Orléans lies on the banks of the Loire river.

Etymology

From French Loire, from Latin Liger (name of the river).
