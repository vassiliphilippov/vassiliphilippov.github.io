Ocyurus

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈsaɪərəs / (AmE), / əʊˈsaɪərəs / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish in the snapper family, known for the yellowtail snapper species.
a genus of snappers (fish)

Examples

The Ocyurus genus is best known for its most prominent member, the yellowtail snapper.
Marine biologists conducted a study on several species within the Ocyurus genus.
Commercial fisheries often target Ocyurus for their vibrant coloration and popularity as food fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snapper - a type of marine fish, especially in the family Lutjanidae

Etymology

From Greek 'okyos' (swift) and 'oura' (tail), referring to the fish's swift tail movements.

2. NOUN

/ oʊˈsaɪərəs / (AmE), / əʊˈsaɪərəs / (BrE)
A genus of snappers, marine fish found in tropical and subtropical waters, known for their bright coloration and importance as food fish.
a genus of snappers (fish)

Examples

The yellowtail snapper belongs to the genus Ocyurus, which is native to the Atlantic Ocean.
Marine biologists recently studied migration patterns in the Ocyurus genus.
Commercial fisheries often target fish from the Ocyurus genus due to their popularity as seafood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snapper - A type of marine fish in the family Lutjanidae

Etymology

From Greek 'okyos' meaning swift and 'oura' meaning tail, referring to the fish's speed and tail.
