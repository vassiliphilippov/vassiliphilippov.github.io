Campylorhynchus

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæm.pɪ.ləˈrɪŋ.kəs / (AmE), / ˌkam.pɪ.ləˈrɪŋ.kəs / (BrE)
A genus of largely Central and South American wrens, including the cactus wrens.
genus of wrens (cactus wrens)

Examples

The genus Campylorhynchus includes some of the largest wrens.
Ornithologists have studied Campylorhynchus species for their complex social structures.
Cactus wrens, classified under Campylorhynchus, are well adapted to arid environments.

Etymology

From Greek kampylos (curved) + rhynchos (beak).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkæmpɪloʊˈrɪŋkəs / (AmE), / ˌkampɪləˈrɪŋkəs / (BrE)
A genus of wrens known as cactus wrens and related species, found primarily in the Americas.
genus of cactus wrens

Examples

The Campylorhynchus genus includes some of the largest wrens in the Americas.
Researchers observed nesting behavior in Campylorhynchus species across the desert region.
Birdwatchers are often excited to spot a Campylorhynchus near cactus patches.

Etymology

From Greek 'kampylos' (curved) + 'rhynchos' (beak), referring to the curved bill of these wrens.
