isogone

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪsəˌɡoʊn / (AmE), / ˈaɪsəˌɡəʊn / (BrE)
An imaginary line connecting points on the Earth's surface where the magnetic declination is the same.
line of equal magnetic declination

Examples

The navigator traced the isogone on the map to account for magnetic variation.
Researchers updated charts to reflect shifts in the isogone over the last decade.
Crossing the isogone indicates a change in the magnetic declination experienced by compasses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isogon - an imaginary line connecting points of equal angle or bearing

Etymology

From Greek iso- (equal) and -gonia (angle).
