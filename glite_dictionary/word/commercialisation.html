commercialisation

1. NOUN

/ kəˌmɝː.ʃə.ləˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / kəˌmɜː.ʃə.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of managing or running something principally for financial gain or for commercial purposes
the process of making something commercial

Examples

The commercialisation of traditional holidays has faced criticism from cultural groups.
Many scientists worry that the commercialisation of research will compromise academic integrity.
Rapid commercialisation of the technology led to its widespread adoption.

Synset Examples

my father considered the commercialization of Christmas to be a sacrilege
the government tried to accelerate the commercialization of this development
both companies will retain control over the commercialization of their own products

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commercialization - the American spelling of 'commercialisation'

Etymology

From commercial + -isation, after commercialize.
