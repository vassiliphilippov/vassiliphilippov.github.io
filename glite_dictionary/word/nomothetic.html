nomothetic

1. ADJ

/ ˌnoʊməˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌnɒməˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or involving the search for general laws or universal principles, especially in science or philosophy.
relating to general laws or universal principles

Examples

Modern science often takes a nomothetic approach, seeking laws that apply to all cases.
The psychologist distinguished between idiographic and nomothetic methods in research.
A nomothetic explanation aims to identify general patterns that predict human behavior.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: idiographic - relating to the study of individual cases or events, especially in contrast to general laws

Etymology

From Greek *nomos* (law) + *thetikos* (fit for placing), via French *nomothétique*.
