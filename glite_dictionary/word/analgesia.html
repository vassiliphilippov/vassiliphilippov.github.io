analgesia

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.əlˈdʒi.zi.ə / (AmE), / ˌæn.ælˈdʒiː.zi.ə / (BrE)
the absence of the sensation of pain without loss of consciousness, especially as induced by medical treatment
absence of pain sensation
medical

Examples

Certain medications can produce analgesia without affecting mental alertness.
The patient was given a spinal block to achieve analgesia during the procedure.
Researchers are developing new drugs that provide analgesia with fewer side effects.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: anesthesia - loss of sensation, often with loss of consciousness
  • Antonym: hyperalgesia - increased sensitivity to pain

Etymology

From Greek an- (without) + algēsis (sense of pain).
