pathologist

1. NOUN

/ pəˈθɑːlədʒɪst / (AmE), / pəˈθɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
A doctor who specializes in the diagnosis of diseases by examining tissues, cells, and bodily fluids.
doctor specializing in disease diagnosis
medical

Examples

The pathologist examined the biopsy samples to determine the nature of the tumor.
Our hospital relies on a skilled pathologist to interpret complex lab results.
During the investigation, the pathologist testified about the cause of death.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diagnostician - a person skilled in making medical diagnoses

Etymology

From Greek pathos (suffering, disease) + -logist (one who studies).
