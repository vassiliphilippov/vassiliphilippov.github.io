insufficiently

1. ADV

/ ˌɪn.səˈfɪʃ.ənt.li / (AmE), / ˌɪn.səˈfɪʃ.ənt.li / (BrE)
to an insufficient degree; not enough to meet a particular requirement or standard
not enough; to an insufficient degree

Examples

The report was rejected because it was insufficiently detailed for the committee's needs.
She felt insufficiently qualified for the managerial position.
Their proposal was insufficiently supported by evidence to convince the board.

Synset Examples

he was insufficiently prepared

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sufficiently - to an adequate or enough degree
  • Synonym: adequately - to a satisfactory or acceptable degree

Etymology

From 'insufficient' (Latin insufficiens) + '-ly'.
