seaborne

1. ADJ

/ ˈsiˌbɔrn / (AmE), / ˈsiːbɔːn / (BrE)
(of goods, people, or activities) conveyed or carried by sea.
conveyed by sea

Examples

A large percentage of international trade consists of seaborne cargo transported by container ships.
The seaborne invasion was launched at dawn under cover of fog.
Rising fuel costs have made seaborne transport more expensive in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: airborne - conveyed by air
  • Antonym: landborne - conveyed by land

Etymology

Formed from 'sea' + 'borne', with 'borne' from Old English 'beran' (to bear, carry).
