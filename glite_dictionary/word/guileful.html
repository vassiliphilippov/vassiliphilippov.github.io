guileful

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡaɪl.fəl / (AmE), / ˈɡaɪl.fəl / (BrE)
skilled at deceiving people or being cunning; deceitful.
deceitful; cunning

Examples

Her guileful smile made it difficult to know if she was telling the truth.
With a guileful plan, he managed to trick his opponents and win the game.
The novel’s villain is a guileful character, always plotting behind the scenes.

Synset Examples

cunning men often pass for wise
a foxy scheme
a slick evasive answer
sly as a fox
tricky Dick
a wily old attorney

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cunning - clever at deceiving people; crafty
  • Synonym: wily - skilled at using clever tricks; crafty
  • Antonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and sincere

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French *guile* (deceit) + *-ful* (full of).
