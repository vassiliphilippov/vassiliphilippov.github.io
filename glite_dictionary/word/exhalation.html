exhalation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.səˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛks.həˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the breath or air that is expelled from the lungs when exhaling
breath or air expelled from the lungs

Examples

A cloud of warm exhalation was visible in the chilly morning air.
Doctors analyzed the patient's exhalation for signs of infection.
Each exhalation carried a faint scent of the flowers she had just picked.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inhalation - the act or process of drawing breath into the lungs
  • Synonym: to exhale - to breathe out air from the lungs

Etymology

From Latin exhalatio (a breathing out), from exhalare (to breathe out).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛkˌsæˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛksˌhəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of breathing out air from the lungs
the act of breathing out

Examples

During meditation, focus on each exhalation as you release tension from your body.
The slow exhalation of breath fogged up the cold windowpane.
After a deep exhalation, she felt noticeably calmer and more relaxed.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inhalation - the act of breathing in air into the lungs

Etymology

From Latin exhalatio (a breathing out), from exhalare (to breathe out).
