worrier

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɜriər / (AmE), / ˈwʌriə / (BrE)
a person who tends to feel anxious or concerned about possible problems or difficulties
person who worries a lot

Examples

My mother is a natural worrier who always imagines the worst-case scenario.
Although I'm not usually a worrier, I couldn't help feeling anxious before the interview.
Being a constant worrier can make it hard to relax and enjoy life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pessimist - a person who tends to see the worst aspect of things or believe the worst will happen
  • Antonym: optimist - a person who tends to be hopeful and confident about the future

Etymology

From 'worry' + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
