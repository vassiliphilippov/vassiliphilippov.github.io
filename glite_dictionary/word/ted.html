Ted

1. NOUN

/ tɛd / (AmE), / tɛd / (BrE)
(chiefly British) A young man, especially in the 1950s and 1960s, who wore Edwardian-style clothes and was associated with a subculture noted for its stylish dress and rebellious attitude.
British youth subculture of the 1950s-60s
british

Examples

In 1950s London, it was easy to spot a Ted by his long jacket and fancy shoes.
My grandfather used to tell stories about being a Ted and going to rock 'n' roll dances.
The Teds were known for their striking clothes and rebellious attitude.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Teddy boy - A young man of the 1950s and 1960s in Britain known for wearing Edwardian-style clothes

Etymology

Shortened from 'Teddy boy', itself from 'Edwardian' (referring to Edward VII of England).
