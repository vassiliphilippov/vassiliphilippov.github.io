pussley

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌsli / (AmE), / ˈpʌsli / (BrE)
A weedy, mat-forming herb with bright yellow flowers, cultivated for its edible, mildly acidic leaves used in salads and cooking, especially in Indian, Greek, and Middle Eastern cuisines; also known as purslane.
edible herb with yellow flowers (purslane)

Examples

The garden was overrun with pussley after the heavy rains.
Many Mediterranean salads feature pussley for its tart flavor and crisp texture.
She picked fresh pussley leaves to cook with olive oil and garlic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purslane - another common name for pussley (Portulaca oleracea)
