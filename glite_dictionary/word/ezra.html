Ezra

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛzrə / (AmE), / ˈɛz.rə / (BrE)
a Jewish priest and scribe who, according to the Bible, was sent by the Persian king to restore Jewish law and worship in Jerusalem
Jewish priest and biblical scribe

Examples

Ezra played a pivotal role in the restoration of Jewish religious practices after the exile.
The Book of Ezra details the return of the Jews to Jerusalem.
Many scholars see Ezra as a key figure in the development of Judaism.

Etymology

From Hebrew 'Ezra', meaning 'help'.

2. PROPN

/ ˈɛzrə / (AmE), / ˈɛzrə / (BrE)
A book of the Old Testament describing the efforts of the priest Ezra to restore Jewish law and worship in Jerusalem after the Babylonian Exile.
Old Testament book about Ezra's reforms

Examples

Many scholars study the book of Ezra to understand the post-exilic period of Jewish history.
Ezra contains accounts of the rebuilding of the Temple in Jerusalem.
The priest Ezra is remembered for his dedication to restoring Jewish law.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nehemiah - An Old Testament book describing events after Ezra, often paired with Ezra
