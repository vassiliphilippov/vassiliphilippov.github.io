Psittacidae

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈtækəˌdi / (AmE), / sɪˈtækɪdiː / (BrE)
the scientific family comprising parrots and related birds, coextensive with the order Psittaciformes
family of parrots (zoology)

Examples

The Psittacidae family includes all true parrots found worldwide.
Scientists have studied the vocal abilities of the Psittacidae in both natural and captive environments.
Many species within the Psittacidae are recognized for their intelligence and bright plumage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parrot - a type of colorful, often tropical bird known for its ability to mimic sounds

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek psittakos (parrot) + -idae (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ sɪˈtækɪˌdiː / (AmE), / sɪˈtakɪdiː / (BrE)
the family of birds that includes true parrots, coextensive with the order Psittaciformes
parrot family in bird classification

Examples

The Psittacidae family includes many popular pet parrots such as macaws and amazons.
Researchers study Psittacidae to learn more about intelligence and vocal learning in birds.
Some species within the Psittacidae are endangered due to habitat loss and the pet trade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parrot - a brightly colored bird of the family Psittacidae

Etymology

From New Latin Psittacidae, from Latin psittacus (parrot), from Greek psittakos (parrot).
