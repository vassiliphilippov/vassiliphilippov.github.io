herringbone

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛr.ɪŋˌboʊn / (AmE), / ˈhɛr.ɪŋˌbəʊn / (BrE)
A twilled fabric featuring a distinctive V-shaped weaving pattern resembling the bones of a herring fish.
fabric with a V-shaped weaving pattern

Examples

His jacket was made from a traditional herringbone fabric.
The tailor recommended herringbone for a classic, sophisticated look.
She admired the subtle pattern of the herringbone weave on the coat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tweed - rough-surfaced woolen cloth, often with a twilled pattern

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to the bones of a herring fish.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛr.ɪŋˌboʊn / (AmE), / ˈhɛr.ɪŋ.bəʊn / (BrE)
a pattern consisting of columns of short parallel lines where the lines in adjacent columns slope in opposite directions, commonly used in weaving, masonry, and flooring
V-shaped zigzag pattern used in design

Examples

The tailor suggested a suit in a classic herringbone pattern for a timeless look.
The floor was laid in elegant herringbone parquet that added character to the room.
She admired the intricate herringbone stitch on the embroidered scarf.

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to the skeleton of a herring fish.
