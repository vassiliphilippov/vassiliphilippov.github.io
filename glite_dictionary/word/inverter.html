inverter

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈvɝːtər / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɜːtə / (BrE)
An electrical device that converts direct current (DC) into alternating current (AC).
device converting DC to AC electricity

Examples

We installed an inverter to power our appliances during power outages.
Solar panels require an inverter to convert stored energy into usable electricity for the house.
The technician checked the inverter after the lights suddenly went out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: converter - a device that changes something from one form to another, especially electricity

Etymology

From Latin 'invertere' (to turn upside down), through English 'invert' + '-er'.
