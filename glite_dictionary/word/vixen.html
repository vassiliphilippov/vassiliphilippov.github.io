vixen

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪksən / (AmE), / ˈvɪksən / (BrE)
a female fox
female fox

Examples

The hunter caught a glimpse of a vixen leading her cubs through the woods.
We saw a vixen near the den at dusk, returning with food.
A vixen is usually more elusive than the male fox during the breeding season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fox - a small wild animal with a bushy tail, known for its cunning

Etymology

From Old English *fyxen* (female fox).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɪksən / (AmE), / ˈvɪksən / (BrE)
a woman who is regarded as spiteful or having a fierce temper
malicious or hot-tempered woman

Examples

Many people found the character in the novel to be a true vixen who stirred up trouble at every turn.
She was unfairly labeled a vixen because of her outspoken nature and passion.
Rumors in the office branded her as a vixen, though few had seen her lose her temper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrew - a woman with a violent, scolding, or nagging temperament
  • Synonym: harpy - a scolding, nagging, or bad-tempered woman

Etymology

From Old English *fyxen* (female fox).

Synset POS: n
