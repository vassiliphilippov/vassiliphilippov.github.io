Harold Clayton Lloyd

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɛrəld ˈkleɪtən lɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈhærəld ˈkleɪtən lɔɪd / (BrE)
(1893–1971) An American comic actor famed for his roles in silent films, known for incorporating physical danger into his comedy.
American silent film comic actor

Examples

Harold Clayton Lloyd was renowned for performing his own dangerous stunts in silent films.
Film historians often cite Harold Clayton Lloyd as one of the most influential comedians of the early 20th century.
The trademark round glasses worn by Harold Clayton Lloyd became an iconic symbol of silent comedy.
