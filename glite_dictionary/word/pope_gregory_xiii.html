Pope Gregory XIII

1. PROPN

/ poʊp ˈɡrɛɡəri ðə ˌθɜrˈtiːnθ / (AmE), / pəʊp ˈɡrɛɡəri ðə ˌθɜːˈtiːnθ / (BrE)
(1502–1585) the pope from 1572 to 1585 who ordered the reform of the calendar, resulting in the modern Gregorian calendar.
pope who reformed the calendar

Examples

Many historians credit Pope Gregory XIII with introducing the Gregorian calendar in 1582.
The reforms of Pope Gregory XIII aimed to correct errors in the Julian calendar.
Without the changes made by Pope Gregory XIII, our modern calendar would be very different.
