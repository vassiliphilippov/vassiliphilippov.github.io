itraconazole

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌtrækəˈnoʊzɔl / (AmE), / ɪˌtrækəˈnəʊzɒl / (BrE)
an oral antifungal medication used to treat fungal infections, especially those affecting the nails
oral antifungal medication
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed itraconazole to treat her persistent toenail infection.
Some fungal infections are resistant to other drugs but respond well to itraconazole.
Patients must take itraconazole with food to improve its absorption.

Etymology

From chemical nomenclature: 'itra-' (modification of triazole) + 'conazole' (a common suffix for antifungals derived from imidazole/triazole).
