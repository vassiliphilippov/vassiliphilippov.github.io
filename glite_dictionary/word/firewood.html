firewood

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪrˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəˌwʊd / (BrE)
wood that is used as fuel for a fire, especially in a fireplace or campfire
wood used as fuel for a fire

Examples

We stacked the firewood neatly next to the cabin for the winter.
Dry firewood burns more efficiently and produces less smoke.
After the storm, fallen branches were collected and chopped into firewood.

Synset Examples

they collected and cut their own firewood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fuel - a material used to produce heat or power through combustion

Etymology

From Middle English 'fyrwode', combining 'fire' and 'wood'.
