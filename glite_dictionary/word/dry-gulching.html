dry-gulching

1. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪˌɡʌltʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdraɪˌɡʌltʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of attacking or killing someone by ambush, especially in a secluded or isolated place
killing or ambushing by surprise

Examples

The outlaw was notorious for his dry-gulching of travelers along the dusty trail.
In the Old West, dry-gulching was considered the most cowardly form of murder.
Stories of dry-gulching often featured desperate gunmen hiding among the rocks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ambush - a surprise attack from a hidden position

Etymology

From American English 'dry gulch' (a ravine without water, often in the West), figuratively meaning to ambush in such a place.
