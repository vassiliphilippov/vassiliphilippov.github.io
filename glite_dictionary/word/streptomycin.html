streptomycin

1. NOUN

/ ˌstrɛptəˈmaɪsɪn / (AmE), / ˌstrɛptəˈmaɪsɪn / (BrE)
an antibiotic produced by the bacterium Streptomyces griseus, used especially to treat tuberculosis and other bacterial infections
antibiotic used to treat tuberculosis
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed streptomycin to help treat the patient's tuberculosis infection.
Some bacteria have developed resistance to streptomycin, making treatment more challenging.
Research on streptomycin has contributed significantly to the fight against infectious diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antibiotic - substance that destroys or inhibits the growth of bacteria

Etymology

From New Latin Streptomyces (the genus name) + -in, a suffix used in chemical names.
