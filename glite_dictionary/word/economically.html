economically

1. ADV

/ ˌikəˈnɑmɪkli / (AmE), / ˌiːkəˈnɒmɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to economics or the economy.
in relation to economics or the economy

Examples

Economically, this proposal makes no sense for the company.
The region is economically dependent on agriculture and tourism.
Economically, the country has grown steadily over the past decade.

Synset Examples

economically this proposal makes no sense

Related Terms

  • Synonym: financially - in a way that relates to finance or money
  • Antonym: ineconomically - in a way that is not economical or prudent with resources

Etymology

From economic + -ally, modelled after the formation of adverbs from adjectives.

2. ADV

/ ˌɛkəˈnɑmɪkli, ˌikəˈnɑmɪkli / (AmE), / ˌiːkəˈnɒmɪkli, ˌɛkəˈnɒmɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that uses money, resources, or effort carefully so as to avoid waste; in an economical manner.
in an economical way

Examples

The company must operate economically to survive in a competitive market.
She cooks economically by using leftovers for multiple meals.
They heated their home economically by sealing the windows and using efficient insulation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thriftily - in a thrifty or economical way
  • Antonym: wastefully - in a wasteful or extravagant manner

Etymology

Formed from 'economic' + '-ally' (adverbial suffix).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ ˌɛkəˈnɑmɪkli / (AmE), / ˌiːkəˈnɒmɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to or concerns the economic system, finances, or economy of a country or organization
in relation to the economy or finances

Examples

Economically, the policy has had both benefits and drawbacks for the local population.
The region has been growing economically over the past decade.
Many countries are economically dependent on their exports of natural resources.

Synset Examples

economically the country is worse off

Related Terms

  • Synonym: financially - in a way that relates to finance or money

Etymology

from 'economic' + '-ally', 'economic' from French 'économique', from Latin 'oeconomicus', from Greek 'oikonomikos' (pertaining to household management)
