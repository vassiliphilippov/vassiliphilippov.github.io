idly

1. ADV

/ ˈaɪdli / (AmE), / ˈaɪdli / (BrE)
In a lazy or aimless manner, without much activity or purpose.
in a lazy or aimless way

Examples

She stared idly out of the window, lost in thought.
The flag fluttered idly in the gentle breeze.
He idly flipped through the pages of the magazine while waiting.

Synset Examples

this is what I always imagined myself doing in the south of France, sitting idly, drinking coffee, watching the people

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idle - not active or not doing anything productive

Etymology

From idle + -ly, following the typical English pattern for making adverbs from adjectives.
