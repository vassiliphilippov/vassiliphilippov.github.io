callout

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔlˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈkɔːl.aʊt / (BrE)
an instance of publicly acknowledging or mentioning someone, especially on social media or in a public setting
public acknowledgment or mention, esp. online

Examples

The author gave a callout to her supporters on social media.
He received a special callout from the conference organizers.
Her charity work earned her a callout on the school's Facebook page.

Synset Examples

She received a callout on social media for her achievements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shoutout - an act of publicly greeting or praising someone, esp. online

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɔlˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈkɔːlˌaʊt / (BrE)
an announcement requesting volunteers or help for a particular task or event
request for volunteers or help

Examples

The charity posted a callout for volunteers to help with the event.
After the hurricane, there was a callout for people with medical training.
We responded to the callout and spent the weekend helping rebuild homes.

Synset Examples

He made a callout for volunteers during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appeal - an urgent request for people to do something or give help

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'call out', meaning to summon or request; first used in this noun sense in the early 20th century.
