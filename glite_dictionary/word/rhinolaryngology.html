rhinolaryngology

1. NOUN

/ ˌraɪnoʊˌlærɪnˈɡɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌraɪnəʊˌlarɪŋˈɡɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of medicine concerned with diseases and disorders of the nose and larynx (voice box)
medical study of the nose and larynx
medical

Examples

She decided to specialize in rhinolaryngology because she was fascinated by disorders of the nose and throat.
Advances in rhinolaryngology have greatly improved treatments for patients with chronic sinus problems.
The conference on rhinolaryngology attracted doctors from around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: otolaryngology - the study of ear, nose, and throat diseases

Etymology

From Greek 'rhino-' (nose) + 'laryng-' (larynx) + '-logy' (study of).
