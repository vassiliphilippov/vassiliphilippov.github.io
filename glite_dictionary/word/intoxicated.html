intoxicated

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈtɑksɪˌkeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɒksɪˌkeɪtɪd / (BrE)
Experiencing the effects of alcohol or drugs so as to lose control of faculties or behavior; or overwhelmed by a strong emotion.
under the influence of alcohol or emotion

Examples

She was clearly intoxicated after several glasses of wine at the party.
He felt intoxicated by the thrill of winning the championship.
Even a small amount of alcohol can leave some people feeling intoxicated.

Synset Examples

felt intoxicated by her success
drunk with excitement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drunk - affected by alcohol to the point of losing control
  • Antonym: sober - not affected by alcohol; not intoxicated

Etymology

From Latin 'intoxicatus', past participle of 'intoxicare' (to poison).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈtɑksɪˌkeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɒksɪˌkeɪtɪd / (BrE)
Having lost control of one's faculties or behavior because of the consumption of alcohol or another drug.
affected by alcohol or drugs

Examples

The officer suspected the driver was intoxicated after noticing erratic movements.
Guests were asked to leave if they appeared intoxicated during the party.
She had never seen her friend so intoxicated before.

Synset Examples

a noisy crowd of intoxicated sailors
helplessly inebriated

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sober - not affected by alcohol or drugs
  • Synonym: drunk - affected by alcohol to the point of impairment

Etymology

From Latin 'intoxicatus', past participle of 'intoxicare' (to poison), from 'in-' (in) + 'toxicum' (poison).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00800854-a
Synset POS: a
