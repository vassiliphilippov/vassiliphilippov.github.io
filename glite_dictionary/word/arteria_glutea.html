arteria glutea

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈtɪəriə ɡluːˈtiːə / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪəriə ɡljuːˈtiːə / (BrE)
Either of two arteries, superior or inferior, branching from the internal iliac artery to supply blood to the gluteal region and hip joint.
artery supplying the gluteal region
medical

Examples

The arteria glutea branches from the internal iliac artery to supply the gluteus maximus.
Injury to the arteria glutea can compromise blood flow to the hip joint.
Surgeons must identify the arteria glutea when operating in the gluteal region.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: internal iliac artery - main artery of the pelvis from which the gluteal arteries branch
  • Synonym: gluteal artery - alternative name for the arteria glutea

Etymology

From Latin 'arteria' (artery) and 'gluteus' (buttock).
