Zoysia

1. NOUN

/ ˈzoʊziə / (AmE), / ˈzɔɪziə / (BrE)
A type of low-growing, perennial grass native to southeastern Asia and New Zealand, commonly cultivated for lawns in warm climates.
type of warm-climate lawn grass

Examples

The landscaper recommended planting Zoysia for a durable and drought-resistant lawn.
In warmer regions, Zoysia is favored for its dense, carpet-like appearance.
After switching to Zoysia, their backyard stayed green throughout the summer heat.

Etymology

Named after Karl von Zois, an 18th-century Slovenian botanist.

2. NOUN

/ ˈzɔɪ.zi.ə / (AmE), / ˈzɔɪ.zɪ.ə / (BrE)
a genus of creeping grasses native to southeastern Asia and New Zealand, widely cultivated as turf in warm climates
a genus of creeping lawn grasses

Examples

The new golf course was planted with Zoysia to create a resilient, green playing surface.
Many homeowners prefer Zoysia because of its drought resistance and fine texture.
Experts recommend Zoysia for lawns in regions with hot summers and mild winters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grass - plant with narrow leaves growing from the base

Etymology

Named after the Austrian botanist Karl von Zois.
