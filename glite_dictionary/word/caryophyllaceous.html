caryophyllaceous

1. ADJ

/ ˌkɛriəfəˈleɪʃəs / (AmE), / ˌkarɪəfəˈleɪʃəs / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of plants in the family Caryophyllaceae, such as pinks and carnations.
relating to the pink family of plants

Examples

Many popular garden flowers, such as carnations, are caryophyllaceous plants.
The botanist specialized in the study of caryophyllaceous species.
Caryophyllaceous leaves are often opposite, with swollen nodes.

Etymology

From modern Latin Caryophyllaceae, the botanical family name, from Greek karyon (nut) + phyllon (leaf).
