Oleandraceae

1. NOUN

/ oʊˌliː.ænˈdreɪ.siˌiː / (AmE), / əʊˌliː.ænˈdræ.siˌiː / (BrE)
A family of ferns comprising three genera and about sixty species, formerly included in Polypodiaceae in some classification systems.
family of ferns

Examples

The ferns belonging to the Oleandraceae family are native to tropical regions.
Recent studies have revised the classification of Oleandraceae based on molecular data.
Botanists debated whether to separate Oleandraceae from Polypodiaceae in modern taxonomies.

Etymology

From Latinized form of genus name Oleandra + standard botanical family suffix -aceae.
