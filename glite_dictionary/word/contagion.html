contagion

1. NOUN

/ kənˈteɪdʒən / (AmE), / kənˈteɪdʒən / (BrE)
the transmission of an infectious disease from one person or organism to another, or an instance of this occurring
transmission of infectious disease

Examples

The hospital took every measure to prevent contagion among patients and staff.
During the outbreak, authorities struggled to identify the source of contagion.
Fear of contagion led to strict quarantine protocols in the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infection - the invasion and multiplication of microorganisms in body tissues

Etymology

From Latin contagio (contact, contagion), from contingere (to touch).

2. NOUN

/ kənˈteɪdʒən / (AmE), / kənˈteɪdʒən / (BrE)
the rapid spread of an emotion, attitude, or feeling among a group of people
the spread of emotion or attitude among people

Examples

A contagion of laughter swept through the classroom, making it impossible for the teacher to continue.
Her optimism proved to be a contagion, boosting everyone's spirits during tough times.
There was a contagion of anxiety in the office after the announcement of restructuring.

Synset Examples

a contagion of mirth
the infection of his enthusiasm for poetry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infection - the spreading of emotion or attitude in a group
  • Antonym: isolation - the state of being apart and unaffected by others' emotions or attitudes

Etymology

From Latin contagio (contact, touch, infection).

3. NOUN

/ kənˈteɪdʒən / (AmE), / kənˈteɪdʒən / (BrE)
a disease that is easily spread through direct or indirect contact between individuals
disease spread by contact

Examples

The rapid contagion of measles alarmed the entire community.
Doctors worked to prevent the contagion from spreading to neighboring towns.
During the outbreak, authorities warned people about the risk of contagion through close contact.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infection - the process of being infected or the result of infection, especially by microorganisms
  • Synonym: contamination - the process of making something impure by exposure to or addition of a poisonous or polluting substance
  • Antonym: immunity - the state of being immune to a disease, usually by virtue of previous exposure or vaccination

Etymology

From Latin contagio (contact, contagion), from contingere (to touch upon).

4. NOUN

/ kənˈteɪdʒən / (AmE), / kənˈteɪdʒən / (BrE)
an infectious disease or the transmission of such a disease by direct or indirect contact; the process by which disease is spread
infectious disease; disease transmission

Examples

During the 14th century, Europe was devastated by contagion that wiped out millions.
Strict quarantine measures helped prevent the contagion from spreading to other regions.
Doctors worked tirelessly to contain the contagion in the overcrowded city.

Synset Examples

They feared the contagion would disrupt the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infection - the invasion and multiplication of microorganisms in body tissues
  • Antonym: immunity - the state of being immune, especially to disease

Etymology

From Latin 'contagionem', from 'contagion-', from 'contingere' (to touch upon).
