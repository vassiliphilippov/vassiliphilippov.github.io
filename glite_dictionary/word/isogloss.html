isogloss

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪsəˌɡlɑs / (AmE), / ˈaɪsəˌɡlɒs / (BrE)
A line on a map marking the boundary between areas with different linguistic features, such as pronunciation, vocabulary, or syntax.
linguistic boundary on a map

Examples

The isogloss separating northern and southern dialects is clearly marked on this map.
Linguists study the spread of linguistic features by mapping each isogloss.
An isogloss can shift over time as languages and dialects evolve.

Synset Examples

A major isogloss in American English has been identified as the North-Midland isogloss, which demarcates numerous linguistic features, including the Northern Cities vowel shift.

Etymology

From Greek 'isos' (equal) and 'glōssa' (language, tongue).
