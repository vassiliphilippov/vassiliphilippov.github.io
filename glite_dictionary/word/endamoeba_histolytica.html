Endamoeba histolytica

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛn.doʊˈmiː.bə ˌhɪs.toʊˈlɪtɪkə / (AmE), / ˌɛn.dəʊˈmiː.bə ˌhɪs.təˈlɪtɪkə / (BrE)
a species of parasitic amoeba that causes amebic dysentery in humans
parasitic amoeba causing dysentery
medical

Examples

Endamoeba histolytica infection is a major cause of diarrhea in developing countries.
Doctors commonly test for Endamoeba histolytica when patients show symptoms of severe gastrointestinal distress.
Proper sanitation helps prevent the spread of Endamoeba histolytica and the illnesses it causes.

Etymology

From New Latin: 'Endamoeba' (genus name) + 'histolytica' (from Greek 'histos' meaning 'tissue' + 'lytikos' meaning 'able to loosen/dissolve').
