railway

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪlˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈreɪl.weɪ / (BrE)
a company or organization that operates trains for carrying passengers or freight
train-operating company or organization

Examples

The national railway announced a new timetable for the summer season.
After several mergers, the railway became the largest in the region.
The railway invested heavily in modernizing its fleet of trains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: railroad - a company that operates trains (chiefly US usage)

Etymology

From rail + way, first used in 19th-century English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈreɪlˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈreɪl.weɪ / (BrE)
A track made of steel rails along which trains run; a system of tracks and related infrastructure for train transportation.
track for trains

Examples

The children were fascinated by the old railway running through the countryside.
A new railway would boost trade and travel between the two cities.
Maintenance crews work overnight to repair the damaged railway tracks.

Synset Examples

he walked along the railroad track

Related Terms

  • Synonym: railroad - A line of tracks built for train travel, especially used in American English

Etymology

Formed from 'rail' (from Old French 'rail') + 'way' (from Old English 'weg', meaning road or path).
