underestimate

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌndɚˈɛstəˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈestɪmeɪt / (BrE)
an estimate that is lower than the actual value or true amount
estimate lower than actual value

Examples

His sales forecast turned out to be a serious underestimate of customer demand.
The cost was much higher than the original underestimate provided by the contractor.
We had to adjust our budget because of an underestimate in the initial project plan.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: overestimate - an estimate that is higher than the actual value

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'under-' + 'estimate'.

2. VERB

/ ˌʌndɚˈɛstəˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈɛstɪmeɪt / (BrE)
to assess something as being less important, valuable, or costly than it actually is, often deliberately
assess as less than actual

Examples

It is easy to underestimate the costs involved in such a large project.
You should never underestimate your competitors' abilities.
The company deliberately underestimated the time required to complete the construction.

Synset Examples

The construction company wanted the contract badly and lowballed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: overestimate - to estimate something as being more important, valuable, or costly than it actually is
  • Synonym: to underrate - to rate or evaluate as lower in value, ability, or importance than deserved

Etymology

From under- (meaning 'below') + estimate, from Latin aestimare ('to value').

3. VERB

to form too low an opinion of the value, amount, or importance of something or someone
assess as less than actual value or importance

Examples

It's easy to underestimate how long these repairs will take.
Never underestimate the determination of someone who truly cares.
He realized he had underestimated the complexity of the task.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: overestimate - to form too high an opinion of the value, amount, or importance of something

Etymology

4. VERB

to think that someone or something is less important, effective, or valuable than they really are; to assign too low a value to.
assign too low a value to

Examples

Many people underestimate the amount of time needed to complete the project.
She underestimated the cost of living in the city, which led to financial problems.
Don't underestimate his determination; he always finds a way to succeed.

Synset Examples

Don't underestimate the value of this heirloom-you may sell it at a good price

Related Terms

  • Antonym: overestimate - assign too high a value to
  • Synonym: to undervalue - consider something as worth less than its true value

Etymology

Formed from under- (meaning too little) + estimate, from Latin aestimare (to value).
