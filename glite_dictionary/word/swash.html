swash

1. VERB

/ swɑʃ / (AmE), / swɒʃ / (BrE)
(of water or objects) to move with violent, noisy, or splashing motions.
move noisily or violently, splash

Examples

The boat swashed through the rough waves, sending spray high into the air.
Water swashes against the rocks during the coming tide.
She swashing her broom angrily across the floor, making a loud racket.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splash - to strike and scatter liquid about noisily
  • Synonym: to slosh - to move with a splashing sound

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *swaschen* (to dash), related to the sound of splashing.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02093349-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ swɑʃ / (AmE), / swɒʃ / (BrE)
to dash or splash a liquid on or against something with force
splash or dash liquid forcefully

Examples

Waves would often swash the deck during heavy storms.
The gardener swashed water onto the hot pavement to cool it down.
As the tide came in, the sea swashes against the rocky shoreline.

Synset Examples

The mother splashed the baby's face with water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splash - to cause (liquid) to strike or fall on something in scattered drops

Etymology

Originally imitative of the sound of splashing water; related to 'swash' (noun) meaning a splash or surging movement of water.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01376685-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ swɑʃ / (AmE), / swɒʃ / (BrE)
to behave in an arrogant, showy, or swaggering manner
to swagger or act arrogantly
archaic

Examples

The arrogant knight would often swash through the halls, boasting of his deeds.
He likes to swash about the office as if he owns the place, but no one is impressed.
Actors in old plays sometimes swashed across the stage, full of bluster and pride.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swagger - to walk or behave in a very confident and arrogant way

Etymology

Possibly from Middle English 'swasshen' (to dash or strike; to swagger), of imitative origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00012427-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ swɑʃ / (AmE), / swɒʃ / (BrE)
the movement or sound made by water, especially as waves break on a shore
the movement or sound of water, especially waves

Examples

The swash of the sea against the rocks was soothing after a long day.
She listened to the gentle swash as the waves lapped the boat's hull.
Seagulls called above the rhythmic swash on the sandy beach.

Synset Examples

the swash of waves on the beach

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splash - the sound or effect of something wet hitting a surface
  • Degree Higher: crash - a loud sound of something breaking or hitting, often related to waves

Etymology

Probably imitative in origin, suggesting the sound or action of water.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07359128-n
Synset POS: n
