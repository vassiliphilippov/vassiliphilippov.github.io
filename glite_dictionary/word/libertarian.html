libertarian

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪbərˈtɛriən / (AmE), / ˌlɪbəˈteəriən / (BrE)
a person who believes in or advocates the doctrine of libertarianism, emphasizing individual freedom and minimal government intervention
advocate of individual liberty/minimal government

Examples

As a proud libertarian, she opposes most government regulations.
The debate featured a libertarian arguing for lower taxes and less intervention.
Many libertarians advocate for strong protection of civil liberties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anarchist - a person who believes in abolishing all government
  • Antonym: authoritarian - a person favoring strict obedience to authority

Etymology

From Latin 'libertas' (freedom), modeled after 'egalitarian'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌlɪbərˈtɛriən / (AmE), / ˌlɪbəˈteəriən / (BrE)
A person who believes in the doctrine of free will, especially in the philosophical sense that human actions are not determined by external causes.
believer in free will (philosophy)

Examples

As a libertarian, she rejects the notion that fate controls people's actions.
The philosopher argued from a libertarian perspective that humans are morally responsible for their choices.
Many debates in metaphysics contrast the libertarian view with determinism.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: determinist - someone who believes all events, including moral choices, are determined by existing causes

Etymology

From Latin 'libertas' (freedom) + '-arian' (suffix meaning pertaining to a person).
