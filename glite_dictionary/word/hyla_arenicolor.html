Hyla arenicolor

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪlə ˌærɪniˈkɔlɚ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪlə ˌærɪniˈkɔːlə / (BrE)
A small ground-dwelling tree frog native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, typically found close to water.
a species of small North American tree frog

Examples

Researchers observed the Hyla arenicolor near several streams in the Arizona desert.
The coloration of Hyla arenicolor helps it blend in with sandy surroundings.
Conservationists are studying habitat loss effects on Hyla arenicolor populations in northern Mexico.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tree frog - a frog of the family Hylidae that typically lives in trees or shrubs

Etymology

From New Latin; 'Hyla' is a genus name, 'arenicolor' from Latin 'arena' (sand) and 'color' (color), referring to its sandy coloration.
