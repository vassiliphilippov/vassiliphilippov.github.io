Ahmad Shah Masoud

1. PROPN

/ ɑːˈmɑːd ʃɑː məˈsuːd / (AmE), / ɑːˈmɑːd ʃɑː məˈsuːd / (BrE)
(1953–2001) Afghan military and political leader who famously resisted Soviet occupation in the 1980s, led forces against the Taliban, and was assassinated in 2001.
Afghan anti-Soviet and anti-Taliban leader (1953–2001)

Examples

Ahmad Shah Masoud was often called the 'Lion of Panjshir' for his resilience against the Soviets and the Taliban.
Many Afghans regard Ahmad Shah Masoud as a national hero and a symbol of resistance.
Documentaries have chronicled the life and legacy of Ahmad Shah Masoud after his assassination in 2001.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Taliban - Islamic fundamentalist militant group that ruled Afghanistan from 1996 to 2001
