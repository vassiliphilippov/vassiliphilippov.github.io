decorous

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɛkərəs / (AmE), / ˈdɛkərəs / (BrE)
marked by proper behavior, dignity, and good taste in manners and conduct
proper and dignified in manners

Examples

Her decorous behavior impressed everyone at the formal dinner.
They maintained a decorous silence during the ceremony.
He gave a decorous response to the critical question.

Synset Examples

the tete-a-tete was decorous in the extreme

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indecorous - lacking propriety or good taste; improper
  • Synonym: proper - conforming to accepted standards of behavior or morals

Etymology

From Latin 'decorus' (becoming, seemly), from 'decor' (beauty, grace).

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɛkərəs / (AmE), / ˈdɛkərəs / (BrE)
behaving in a polite, proper, and socially acceptable way; marked by good taste and propriety
proper and polite in behavior

Examples

The ambassador's decorous conduct at the ceremony impressed everyone present.
She spoke in a decorous manner, never raising her voice or interrupting others.
Their decorous attire reflected the formal nature of the event.

Synset Examples

her becoming modesty
comely behavior
it is not comme il faut for a gentleman to be constantly asking for money
seemly behavior

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indecorous - not proper or in good taste; improper
  • Synonym: seemly - fitting or proper with respect to accepted standards

Etymology

From Latin decorus (becoming, seemly), from decor (grace, beauty).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01884969-s
Synset POS: s
