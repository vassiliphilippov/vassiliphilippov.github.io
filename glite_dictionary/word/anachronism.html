anachronism

1. NOUN

/ əˈnækrəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / əˈnækrənɪzəm / (BrE)
A thing belonging or appropriate to a period other than that in which it exists, especially something conspicuously old-fashioned or out of its time.
something out of its proper time

Examples

A wristwatch in a movie set in ancient Rome is a clear anachronism.
Calling a smartphone a 'walkie-talkie' is a funny anachronism in today's world.
The use of horses in a modern city is considered by some to be an anachronism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anachronistic - belonging to a period other than that being portrayed; out of date

Etymology

From Greek anachronismos (mistake in chronology, an error in dates), from ana- (against) + khronos (time).

2. NOUN

/ əˈnækrəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / əˈnækrənɪzəm / (BrE)
a person or thing that belongs to a period other than that in which it exists, especially one that seems out of place in the present
thing or person out of its time

Examples

My grandfather is an anachronism in this digital age, still writing letters by hand.
The movie portrays her as an anachronism, holding onto outdated manners in a modern world.
In a room full of smartphones, his rotary phone was a charming anachronism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: throwback - a person or thing having the characteristics of an earlier time

Etymology

From Greek anakhrōnismos, from ana- (against) + khronos (time).

3. NOUN

/ əˈnækrəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / əˈnækrənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
something or someone that seems to belong to an earlier period and is out of place in the present time
thing out of its time period

Examples

Calling a modern smartphone in a medieval movie an anachronism is a common criticism by historians.
He was considered an anachronism in the digital age because he still wrote letters by hand.
Wearing a top hat today would be a definite anachronism in most social contexts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artifact - an object made by a human being, typically of historical interest

Etymology

From Greek anachronismos, from ana- (against) + khronos (time).
