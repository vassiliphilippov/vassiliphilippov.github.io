furring

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɜrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɜːrɪŋ / (BrE)
a coating resembling fur, especially a layer of matter that forms on the tongue or inside pipes
a coating of matter (e.g., on the tongue)
medical

Examples

The doctor examined the patient's tongue and noticed a furring that indicated possible infection.
Heavy furring of the tongue can be a sign of poor oral hygiene or illness.
After recovering from the flu, she saw the furring on her tongue gradually disappear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coating - a thin layer covering a surface
  • Synonym: deposit - a layer of a substance accumulated in a particular place

Etymology

From 'fur', referring to the soft thick covering in animals, extended figuratively to describe a similar appearance.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɜrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɜːrɪŋ / (BrE)
A strip of wood or metal attached to a wall, ceiling, or floor to provide a level surface for attaching wallboard, paneling, or similar materials.
strip for leveling surfaces in construction

Examples

Before installing drywall, the builders added furring to even out the basement walls.
The ceiling required extra furring strips to create a flat surface for the tiles.
Metal furring is often used in commercial buildings for stronger support.
