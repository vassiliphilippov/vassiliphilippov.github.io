continuousness

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtɪn.ju.əs.nəs / (AmE), / kənˈtɪn.juː.əs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being continuous; unbroken or uninterrupted existence or operation
state of being continuous

Examples

The continuousness of the rain made it impossible to go outside all day.
He admired the continuousness of her efforts to improve the environment.
The engineer ensured the continuousness of the workflow in the production line.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: continuity - the unbroken and consistent existence or operation of something over a period of time

Etymology

From 'continuous' + '-ness', patterning after other abstract nouns in English.
