Bromus japonicus

1. NOUN

/ ˈbroʊ.məs dʒəˈpoʊ.nɪ.kəs / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊ.məs dʒəˈpəʊ.nɪ.kəs / (BrE)
A species of grass native to the Mediterranean region and temperate parts of Asia, also known as Japanese brome.
a grass species native to Asia and Mediterranean

Examples

Bromus japonicus often invades disturbed soils along roadsides and fields in many temperate regions.
Farmers are concerned about the rapid spread of Bromus japonicus in their wheat crops.
The research focused on the ecological impact of Bromus japonicus in Mediterranean habitats.

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Bromus' and specific epithet 'japonicus' meaning 'of Japan'.
