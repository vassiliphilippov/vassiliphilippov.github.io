squizz

1. NOUN

/ skwɪz / (AmE), / skwɪz / (BrE)
an informal, quick look or glance
quick, informal glance
informal

Examples

She gave a squizz through the window to see who was arriving.
He took a squizz at the answers before turning in his test.
Curious, I had a quick squizz at the document on his desk.

Synset Examples

He managed a squizz through the window at the event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glance - a brief or hurried look
  • Synonym: peek - a quick or furtive look
  • Antonym: stare - to look at something for a long time

Etymology

Origin unknown, first recorded in Australia in the early 20th century.
