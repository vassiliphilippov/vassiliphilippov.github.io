labyrinth

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæb.əˌrɪnθ / (AmE), / ˈlæb.ər.ɪnθ / (BrE)
A complex structure of interconnected passages or paths, often difficult to navigate or escape from.
maze of complex paths or tunnels

Examples

The children played hide and seek in the labyrinth of hedges behind the old mansion.
Archaeologists explored the ancient labyrinth beneath the city, searching for hidden chambers.
I got lost in the labyrinth of narrow alleys in the old quarter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maze - a network of paths or hedges designed as a puzzle

Etymology

From Greek 'labyrinthos' (maze).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlæbəˌrɪnθ / (AmE), / ˈlæb.ər.ɪnθ / (BrE)
a complex system of interconnecting cavities within the inner ear, involved in hearing and balance
inner ear organ for hearing and balance
medical

Examples

The delicate structures of the labyrinth are essential for our sense of balance.
An infection can sometimes affect the labyrinth, leading to dizziness and hearing loss.
Doctors examined the patient's inner ear to check for damage to the labyrinth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cochlea - spiral-shaped cavity in the inner ear essential for hearing
  • Synonym: vestibule - central part of the bony labyrinth in the inner ear

Etymology

From Latin 'labyrinthus', from Greek 'labýrinthos' (maze).
