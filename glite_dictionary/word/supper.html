supper

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌpɚ / (AmE), / ˈsʌpə / (BrE)
A social event, usually in the evening, at which a light meal is served.
evening social meal event

Examples

She held an elegant supper for her colleagues at her home on Friday night.
The annual charity supper helped raise funds for the community center.
At their weekend supper, guests enjoyed light dishes and lively conversation.

Synset Examples

her suppers often included celebrities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dinner - main meal of the day, often social; similar event with a heavier meal

Etymology

From Old French *souper* (to eat the evening meal), from *soupe* (soup).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsʌp.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈsʌp.ə / (BrE)
a light evening meal, typically eaten either early in the evening if the main meal (dinner) is at midday, or late in the evening before bedtime
light evening meal

Examples

We had a simple supper of soup and bread before going to bed.
She invited us over for supper after a long day at work.
Many people prefer a light supper instead of a heavy dinner in the evening.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: dinner - the main meal of the day, often eaten in the evening
  • Antonym: breakfast - the first meal of the day, eaten in the morning

Etymology

From Old French 'souper', from 'souper' (to eat supper), ultimately from Latin 'suppāre' (to soak).
