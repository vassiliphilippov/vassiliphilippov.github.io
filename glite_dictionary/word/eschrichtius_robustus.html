Eschrichtius robustus

1. NOUN

/ ɛsˈkrɪktiəs roʊˈbʌstəs / (AmE), / ɛsˈkrɪktɪəs rəʊˈbʌstəs / (BrE)
a medium-sized species of whale, commonly known as the gray whale, found in the northern Pacific Ocean
gray whale species of the northern Pacific

Examples

The migration of the Eschrichtius robustus is one of the longest among marine mammals.
Researchers observed a group of Eschrichtius robustus feeding near the coast.
The conservation status of Eschrichtius robustus has improved due to international protection efforts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gray whale - common name for Eschrichtius robustus, a baleen whale of the northern Pacific

Etymology

From New Latin, named after Danish zoologist Daniel Eschricht (1798–1863), plus Latin 'robustus' (strong, robust).
