to disassemble

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪs.əˈsɛm.bəl / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.əˈsɛm.bəl / (BrE)
to take apart something into its component pieces
take apart into pieces

Examples

The technician needed to disassemble the computer to locate the faulty part.
Before moving the furniture, we had to disassemble the table to fit it through the door.
He carefully disassembled the engine for cleaning and inspection.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to assemble - to put together the parts of something

Etymology

From Latin dis- (apart) + assemble (from Latin assemblare, to gather together).
