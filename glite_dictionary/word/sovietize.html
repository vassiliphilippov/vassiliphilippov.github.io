to sovietize

1. VERB

/ ˈsoʊ.vi.əˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsəʊ.vi.əˌtaɪz / (BrE)
to bring a country or area under Soviet control or influence, especially by imitating the Soviet system of government or culture
bring under Soviet control

Examples

After the annexation, the government began to sovietize the educational system.
Historically, there were efforts to sovietize Eastern European countries after World War II.
He resisted attempts to sovietize local customs and traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: russify - to make something Russian in character

Etymology

From Russian 'Sovet' (council) + English suffix '-ize', meaning to make or become like.

2. VERB

/ ˈsoʊ.vi.əˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsəʊ.vi.əˌtaɪz / (BrE)
to model a country's social, political, or economic structures on those of the Soviet Union, typically by adopting its policies and governmental systems
make like the Soviet Union

Examples

After the revolution, the new leaders attempted to sovietize the nation's institutions.
The government implemented policies to rapidly sovietize agriculture and industry.
Historians debate how successfully the regime managed to sovietize its population during the early years.

Synset Examples

Castro sovietized Cuba

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Russify - to make Russian in character or adopt Russian customs

Etymology

From Soviet + -ize, meaning to make or become like the Soviet Union.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01748147-v
Synset POS: v
