invidious

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈvɪdiəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɪdi.əs / (BrE)
(of an action, remark, or comparison) likely to arouse resentment, anger, or be unfairly prejudicial.
unfairly prejudicial; likely to cause resentment

Examples

The manager was criticized for making invidious distinctions between team members.
She avoided invidious remarks that could offend her colleagues.
His invidious comparison of the two candidates led to controversy.

Synset Examples

discriminatory attitudes and practices
invidious comparisons

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unfair - not based on or behaving according to the rules of justice
  • Synonym: prejudiced - having or showing a dislike or distrust that is derived from prejudice
  • Antonym: impartial - treating all rivals or disputants equally; fair and just

Etymology

From Latin invidiosus (envious), from invidia (envy).
