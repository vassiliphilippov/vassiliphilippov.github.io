convalescent

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːnvəˈlɛsənt / (AmE), / ˌkɒnvəˈlesənt / (BrE)
a person who is recovering after an illness or medical treatment
person recovering from illness

Examples

After surgery, the convalescent spent several weeks regaining his strength at home.
The hospital's garden was arranged to provide fresh air and comfort to every convalescent.
Volunteers organized activities to help entertain the convalescent during her slow recovery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patient - person receiving medical treatment
  • Synonym: invalid - person made weak or disabled by illness

Etymology

From Latin 'convalescentem', present participle of 'convalescere' (to recover).

2. ADJ

/ ˌkɑːnvəˈlesənt / (AmE), / ˌkɒnvəˈlesənt / (BrE)
recovering from illness or medical treatment; returning to health after being sick or debilitated
recovering after illness

Examples

After surgery, the convalescent patient needed plenty of rest and light exercise.
The charity runs a home for convalescent children who are recovering from serious illnesses.
She wrote letters to her friends while she was still convalescent after the flu.

Synset Examples

convalescent children are difficult to keep in bed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recovering - in the process of returning to normal health after illness
  • Antonym: ill - suffering from illness; not healthy

Etymology

From Latin convalescentem, present participle of convalescere (to recover, grow strong).
