to enfold

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈfoʊld / (AmE), / ɪnˈfəʊld / (BrE)
to surround or cover completely with or as if with a covering
surround or cover completely

Examples

The soft blanket enfolded the sleeping child, keeping her warm and secure.
Mist began to enfold the valley as evening fell, blurring the distant hills.
She let his arms enfold her, offering comfort after a long, hard day.

Synset Examples

Fog enveloped the house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to envelop - to wrap up, cover, or surround completely

Etymology

From Old English 'befaldan', meaning to fold in or wrap around.
