Verbesina encelioides

1. NOUN

/ vɚˌbiːsɪnə ɛnˌsiːliˈɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / vəˌbiːsɪnə ɛnˌsiːliˈɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A coarse, greyish-green annual herb with yellow flowers, native to the southwestern United States and Mexico.
annual yellow-flowered herb, southwestern US and Mexico

Examples

The Verbesina encelioides thrived in the arid landscape, adding color to the roadside.
Gardeners often plant Verbesina encelioides for its resilience and bright yellow blossoms.
Wildlife in the southwest depend on Verbesina encelioides as a source of food and shelter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowpen daisy - Common name for Verbesina encelioides

Etymology

From Latin: genus Verbesina, species epithet encelioides indicating resemblance to Encelia genus.
