Paleo-Amerind

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪlioʊ əˈmɛrɪnd / (AmE), / ˌpeɪlɪəʊ əˈmɛrɪnd / (BrE)
a member of the early human populations who inhabited North and South America during the late Pleistocene epoch
early inhabitant of the Americas

Examples

Archaeologists found artifacts believed to have been created by Paleo-Amerind hunters.
Studies suggest that Paleo-Amerinds migrated to the Americas over 13,000 years ago.
The stone tools discovered were attributed to Paleo-Amerind cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Paleo-Indian - a member of the first peoples to inhabit the Americas during the late Pleistocene

Etymology

Formed from 'Paleo-', from Greek 'palaios' (ancient), and 'Amerind', short for 'American Indian'.
