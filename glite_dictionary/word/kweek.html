kweek

1. NOUN

/ kwiːk / (AmE), / kwiːk / (BrE)
A trailing grass native to Europe, now found in warm regions worldwide, often used for lawns and pastures, especially in the southern United States and India.
trailing grass used for lawns and pastures

Examples

The farmer planted kweek to stabilize the soil and provide grazing for cattle.
Because of its resilience in hot climates, kweek is a popular choice for lawns in southern regions.
Weeds like kweek can quickly spread throughout a neglected garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: couch grass - a similar or related grass species, often used interchangeably with kweek

Etymology

From Afrikaans 'kweek', referring to couch grass species.
