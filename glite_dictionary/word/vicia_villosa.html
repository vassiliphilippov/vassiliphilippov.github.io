Vicia villosa

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪʃiə vɪˈloʊsə / (AmE), / ˈvɪsɪə vɪˈləʊsə / (BrE)
A species of vetch native to Europe, commonly grown for use as forage and as a cover crop.
species of vetch used as forage and cover crop

Examples

Many farmers plant Vicia villosa to enrich the soil with nitrogen before growing vegetables.
Vicia villosa is often recommended as a winter cover crop for its hardiness and soil benefits.
The field bloomed with purple flowers from the Vicia villosa sown last autumn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hairy vetch - common name for Vicia villosa

Etymology

From Latin: 'Vicia' (vetch) and 'villosa' (hairy), referring to the plant's hairy stems and leaves.
