Scaphiopus

1. NOUN

/ skæˈfaɪ.oʊ.pəs / (AmE), / skæˈfɪə.pəs / (BrE)
A genus of North American spadefoot toads known for their spade-shaped hind feet used for digging.
genus of North American spadefoot toads

Examples

The Scaphiopus species are well adapted to arid environments with sandy soils.
A Scaphiopus can remain buried underground for months, emerging after heavy rains.
Scientists studied the breeding behavior of Scaphiopus in temporary desert pools.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spadefoot - a type of toad with spade-like projections on its rear feet

Etymology

From Greek skaphē (spade, shovel) + pous (foot), referring to the shape of their hind feet.
