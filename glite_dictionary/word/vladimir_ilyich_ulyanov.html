Vladimir Ilyich Ulyanov

1. PROPN

/ vlɑːˈdɪmɪr ɪˈliːɪtʃ uːlˈjɑːnɒf / (AmE), / vlæˈdɪmɪə ɪˈlɪɪtʃ uːlˈjænɒf / (BrE)
The Russian revolutionary leader, better known as Lenin, founder of the Bolshevik Party, leader of the Russian Revolution of 1917, and the first head of the Soviet Union (1870–1924).
Russian revolutionary leader, known as Lenin

Examples

Vladimir Ilyich Ulyanov led the October Revolution, dramatically changing the course of Russian history.
Many political movements in the 20th century were influenced by the writings of Vladimir Ilyich Ulyanov.
The mausoleum in Moscow contains the preserved body of Vladimir Ilyich Ulyanov.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lenin - the revolutionary name adopted by Vladimir Ilyich Ulyanov
