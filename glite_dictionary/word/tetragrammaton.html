Tetragrammaton

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛtrəˈɡræmətən / (AmE), / ˌtɛtrəˈɡræmətən / (BrE)
the four Hebrew letters (YHWH or JHVH) representing the biblical name of God, considered too sacred to be spoken aloud by Jews
the four-letter biblical name of God (YHWH)

Examples

The ancient manuscript contained the Tetragrammaton in its original Hebrew script.
Jewish tradition forbids pronouncing the Tetragrammaton during prayer or conversation.
Many biblical scholars debate the correct pronunciation of the Tetragrammaton.

Etymology

From Greek tetragrammaton (τετραγράμματον), meaning 'four letters', from tetra- ('four') + gramma ('letter').
