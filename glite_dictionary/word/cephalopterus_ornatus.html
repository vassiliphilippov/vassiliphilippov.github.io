Cephalopterus ornatus

1. NOUN

/ sɛfəˈlɒptərəs ɔrˈneɪtəs / (AmE), / ˌsiːfəˌlɒptəˈrʌs ɔːˈneɪtəs / (BrE)
A black tropical bird native to South America, known for its large overhanging crest and long feathered wattle, also called the Amazonian umbrellabird.
a black tropical South American bird with a large crest

Examples

The Cephalopterus ornatus is easily identified by its dramatic crest and striking appearance.
Birdwatchers travel to the Amazon rainforests to catch a glimpse of the elusive Cephalopterus ornatus.
Male Cephalopterus ornatus use their long feathered wattles in elaborate courtship displays.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, with 'Cephalopterus' meaning 'head-wing' and 'ornatus' meaning 'ornate' or 'adorned.'
