to instantiate

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈstæn.si.eɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈstæn.si.eɪt / (BrE)
to represent or express (an abstract concept or principle) by a concrete example or instance
represent by a specific example

Examples

The researcher chose to instantiate the theory with data from recent experiments.
In object-oriented programming, you must instantiate a class before using its methods.
Her novel instantiates many of the themes found in contemporary literature.

Synset Examples

This word instantiates the usage that the linguists claimed to be typical for a certain dialect

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exemplify - to illustrate or clarify by giving an example

Etymology

From medieval Latin instantiāre (to bring forward), based on Latin instantia (presence, urgency).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02160229-v

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈstænʃieɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈstænʃieɪt / (BrE)
to provide or find an example of something, especially a word or usage, in actual use
to give or find an example of

Examples

The linguist tried to instantiate the new word by quoting a passage from local newspapers.
It can be difficult to instantiate rare meanings of common words in contemporary speech.
Can you instantiate this grammatical pattern with a few examples from modern literature?

Synset Examples

The linguists could not instantiate this sense of the noun that he claimed existed in a certain dialect

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exemplify - to illustrate or clarify by giving an example

Etymology

From Medieval Latin instantiare (to bring forward as proof), from Latin instantia (presence, urgency).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02159923-v
Synset POS: v
