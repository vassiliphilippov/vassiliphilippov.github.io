Mitrula elegans

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪ.tru.lə iːˈlɛɡ.ənz / (AmE), / ˈmɪ.truː.lə ɪˈliː.ɡænz / (BrE)
A species of fungus characterized by a 3–8 cm high fruiting body with an orange to yellow fertile tip and white or pinkish stalk, often found partially submerged in water.
species of aquatic fungus

Examples

Mitrula elegans is commonly found in shallow, slow-moving streams and ponds.
The stalk of Mitrula elegans is often partially submerged, making it distinct from many other fungi.
Researchers collected several specimens of Mitrula elegans for their field study on aquatic fungi.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; Mitrula from Latin 'mitra' (headband, mitre) and Latin 'elegans' (elegant, graceful).
