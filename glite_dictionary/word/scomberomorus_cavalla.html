Scomberomorus cavalla

1. NOUN

/ skəmˌbɛrəˌmɔrəs kəˈvælə / (AmE), / skɒmˌbɛrəˌmɔːrəs kəˈvælə / (BrE)
A large species of mackerel with a long pointed snout, valued as an important food and game fish found along the eastern Atlantic coast southward to Brazil.
large Atlantic food and game mackerel

Examples

The Scomberomorus cavalla is highly prized by sport fishermen in the Gulf of Mexico.
Many restaurants serve Scomberomorus cavalla grilled or smoked as a delicacy.
Researchers tagged several Scomberomorus cavalla to study their migration patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: king mackerel - common name for Scomberomorus cavalla

Etymology

From New Latin; 'Scomberomorus' from Greek *skombros* (mackerel) and *moros* (foolish); 'cavalla' from Spanish, meaning 'horse mackerel.'
