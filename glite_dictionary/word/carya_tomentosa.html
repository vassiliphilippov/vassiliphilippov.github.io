Carya tomentosa

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛəriə toʊmɛnˈtoʊsə / (AmE), / ˈkɛːrɪə tɒmɛnˈtəʊsə / (BrE)
A species of North American hickory tree with smooth bark, 7 to 9 leaflets, and a hard-shelled edible nut.
a North American hickory tree species

Examples

Several Carya tomentosa trees grow in the forest near my house, providing shade and edible nuts.
The Carya tomentosa is prized for its hard wood as well as its nutritious nuts.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Carya tomentosa across different regions of the eastern United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mockernut hickory - common name for Carya tomentosa

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Carya' (from Greek καρυα 'walnut tree') and species name 'tomentosa' meaning 'covered with dense, short hairs'.
