Vela

1. PROPN

/ ˈveɪlə / (AmE), / ˈviːlə / (BrE)
a constellation in the Southern Hemisphere, located between Carina and Pyxis, often called 'the Sails'
constellation in the Southern Hemisphere

Examples

The constellation Vela is best seen in the Southern Hemisphere during autumn nights.
Astronomers have discovered several pulsars in the Vela region.
Vela was once part of the larger constellation Argo Navis.

Synset Examples

because of its configuration Vela is sometimes called ‘the Sails’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Carina - a constellation adjacent to Vela in the Southern Hemisphere
  • Synonym: Pyxis - a constellation adjacent to Vela, representing a compass

Etymology

From Latin *vēla* meaning 'sails', referring to the sails of a ship.
