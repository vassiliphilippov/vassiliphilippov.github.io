fibromyositis

1. NOUN

/ ˌfaɪbroʊˌmaɪəˈsaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌfɪbrəʊˌmaɪəˈsaɪtɪs / (BrE)
A medical condition involving localized inflammation of muscle and connective tissue, often causing pain and stiffness.
inflammation of muscle and connective tissue
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with fibromyositis after complaining of persistent muscle pain and sensitivity.
Treatment for fibromyositis usually includes anti-inflammatory medications and physical therapy.
Chronic fibromyositis can significantly impact a person's mobility and quality of life.

Etymology

From Latin 'fibra' (fiber), Greek 'mys' (muscle), and '-itis' (inflammation).
