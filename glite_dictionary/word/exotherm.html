exotherm

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛk.soʊ.θɜrm / (AmE), / ˈɛk.səʊ.θɜːm / (BrE)
A compound that releases heat during its formation and absorbs heat when it decomposes.
compound that releases heat when formed

Examples

When an exotherm is formed, energy is released in the form of heat.
The reaction produced an exotherm, rapidly increasing the surrounding temperature.
Researchers measured the heat given off by the exotherm during the polymerization process.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: endotherm - a compound that absorbs heat during formation and releases it during decomposition

Etymology

From 'exo-' (outside) and Greek 'thermē' (heat).
