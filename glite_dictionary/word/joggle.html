joggle

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɑɡəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒɡəl / (BrE)
a fastener inserted into holes in two adjacent pieces to hold them together, especially in construction or carpentry
a fastener joining adjacent pieces

Examples

The carpenter used a joggle to securely join the two wooden panels.
By fitting a joggle into the pre-drilled holes, the frame became much sturdier.
Joggles are frequently used in traditional timber framing to keep parts properly aligned.

Etymology

Possibly related to earlier 'joggle' meaning to shake slightly or joint with a projection fitting into a notch, found in English since the 17th century.

2. VERB

/ ˈdʒɑɡəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒɡəl / (BrE)
to fasten or join together by making interlocking projections or notches so the pieces fit securely
fasten by interlocking projections

Examples

The carpenter decided to joggle the wooden beams for extra stability.
Steel plates are often joggled to fit together tightly in construction.
To repair the old bridge, workers had to joggle the stone blocks into place.

Etymology

Probably from Middle English jogelen, frequentative of jog (to shake slightly).

3. VERB

/ ˈdʒɑɡəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒɡl / (BrE)
to move or shake something lightly and repeatedly back and forth or up and down
move or shake lightly back and forth

Examples

She tried not to joggle the camera while recording the video.
The bus ride joggled the passengers so much that they could barely read.
Make sure you don't joggle the table, or the cards will fall off.

Synset Examples

Don't jiggle your finger while the nurse is putting on the bandage!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jiggle - to move something up and down or side to side quickly with small movements
  • Synonym: shake - to move quickly back and forth or up and down

Etymology

Possibly frequentative form of 'jog', of uncertain origin, dating back to the late 16th century.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɑɡəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒɡəl / (BrE)
a slight irregular shaking or jerking movement
slight shaking motion

Examples

The carriage gave a joggle as it passed over the uneven ground.
I felt a slight joggle when the elevator started moving.
With each joggle of the table, the glass came closer to the edge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shake - an act of moving something back and forth or up and down with quick movements
  • Synonym: jolt - a sudden rough movement

Etymology

Probably from the frequentative form of 'jog'.
