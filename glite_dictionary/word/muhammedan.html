Muhammedan

1. NOUN

/ məˈhɛmɪdən / (AmE), / mʊˈhæmɪdən / (BrE)
an archaic term for a follower of the prophet Mohammed; now considered inappropriate and largely replaced by 'Muslim'
archaic term for a Muslim
archaic

Examples

The term Muhammedan is now rarely used and discouraged in modern writing.
Several 19th-century authors referred to the followers of Islam as Muhammedans.
Historians caution against using Muhammedan because it misrepresents Islamic beliefs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Muslim - a follower of the religion of Islam

Etymology

From the name Mohammed + -an, modeled after terms like 'Christian'.
