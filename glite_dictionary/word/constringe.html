to constringe

1. VERB

/ kənˈstrɪndʒ / (AmE), / kənˈstrɪndʒ / (BrE)
to make or become tight, especially by contracting or compressing
make or become tight

Examples

The medicine can constringe blood vessels, helping to slow bleeding.
His face seemed to constringe in pain as he received the news.
Cold water may constringe the pores of your skin, making them appear smaller.

Synset Examples

Her throat constricted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to constrict - to become narrower or tighter

Etymology

From Latin 'constringere' (to bind together, to tighten).
