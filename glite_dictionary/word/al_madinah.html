Al Madinah

1. PROPN

/ æl məˈdiːnə / (AmE), / æl məˈdiːnə / (BrE)
A city in western Saudi Arabia, considered the second holiest city in Islam and the site of the tomb of Muhammad.
city in Saudi Arabia; holy site in Islam

Examples

Thousands of pilgrims visit Al Madinah every year as part of the Hajj and Umrah.
Al Madinah is revered in Islam as the burial place of the Prophet Muhammad.
The modern city of Al Madinah blends ancient mosques with vibrant markets.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Mecca - the holiest city in Islam, birthplace of Muhammad
  • Synonym: Islam - the religion based on the teachings of Muhammad

Etymology

From Arabic 'al-Madīnah' meaning 'the city', short for 'al-Madīnah al-Munawwarah' (the radiant city).
