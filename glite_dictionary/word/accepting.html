accepting

1. ADJ

/ əkˈsɛptɪŋ / (AmE), / əkˈsɛptɪŋ / (BrE)
willing to tolerate or receive new ideas, people, or situations without protest or prejudice.
tolerant; open to new ideas or people

Examples

Her parents were very accepting of her decision to move abroad.
We try to create an accepting environment for all students.
Despite their differences, he was always accepting of new ideas.

Synset Examples

always more accepting of coaching suggestion than her teammates
the atmosphere was judged to be more supporting and accepting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tolerant - willing to accept behavior and beliefs different from your own
  • Synonym: open-minded - willing to consider new ideas or opinions
  • Antonym: intolerant - not willing to accept ideas, behavior, or people that are different

Etymology

Present participle of 'accept', from Latin acceptare (to take or receive).
