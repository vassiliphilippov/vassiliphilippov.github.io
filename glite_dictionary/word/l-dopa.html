L-dopa

1. NOUN

/ ɛl ˈdoʊpə / (AmE), / ɛl ˈdəʊpə / (BrE)
the levorotatory form of dopa used as a drug to treat Parkinson's disease
drug for Parkinson's disease
medical

Examples

Patients with Parkinson's disease are often prescribed L-dopa to manage their symptoms.
The doctor adjusted the dosage of L-dopa to help control his tremors.
Extensive research has improved our understanding of how L-dopa works in the brain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: levodopa - another name for L-dopa, drug for Parkinson's disease

Etymology

L- refers to the levorotatory (left-turning) form; 'dopa' comes from dihydroxyphenylalanine.
