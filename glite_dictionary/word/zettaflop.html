zettaflop

1. NOUN

/ ˈzɛtəˌflɑp / (AmE), / ˈzɛtəˌflɒp / (BrE)
A unit for measuring computing speed, equal to one sextillion (10²¹) floating-point operations per second.
one sextillion floating-point operations per second
medical

Examples

Scientists predict supercomputers capable of one zettaflop will revolutionize climate modeling and artificial intelligence.
As technology progresses, achieving a zettaflop of processing speed is becoming a realistic goal for high-performance computing.
The research team's simulations required computing resources close to a zettaflop to process massive data in real time.

Synset Examples

Erik P. DeBenedictis of Sandia National Laboratories theorizes that a zettaFLOPS (ZFLOPS) computer is required to accomplish full weather modeling of two week time span.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: petaflop - one quadrillion floating-point operations per second
  • Degree Lower: exaflop - one quintillion floating-point operations per second
  • Degree Higher: yottaflop - one septillion floating-point operations per second

Etymology

Formed from 'zetta-', denoting a factor of 10²¹ (from Italian 'sette' meaning 'seven', indicating the seventh order of 1,000 in metric prefixes), and 'flop', an abbreviation of 'floating-point operations per second'.
