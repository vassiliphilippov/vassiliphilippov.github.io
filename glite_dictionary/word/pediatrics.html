pediatrics

1. NOUN

/ ˌpiːdiˈætrɪks / (AmE), / ˌpiːdɪˈætrɪks / (BrE)
The branch of medicine concerned with the medical care and treatment of infants, children, and adolescents.
medical care of children
medical

Examples

She decided to specialize in pediatrics after medical school.
Advances in pediatrics have greatly improved child survival rates worldwide.
He has over twenty years of experience practicing pediatrics in urban hospitals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paediatrics - British spelling of 'pediatrics', medicine for children

Etymology

From Greek 'pais, paidos' (child) + 'iatros' (doctor).
