agapanthus

1. NOUN

/ ˌæɡəˈpænθəs / (AmE), / ˌaɡəˈpanθəs / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Agapanthus, known for its round clusters of showy blue or purple flowers
plant with blue/purple flower clusters

Examples

The garden was adorned with tall agapanthus blooming in vibrant shades of blue.
She planted several agapanthuses along the path to add color in summer.
A single stalk of agapanthus made a striking centerpiece on the dining table.

Etymology

From Greek 'agapē' (love) and 'anthos' (flower), meaning 'flower of love'.
