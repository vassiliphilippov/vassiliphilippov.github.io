lapel

1. NOUN

/ ləˈpɛl / (AmE), / ləˈpɛl / (BrE)
the part on each side of a coat or jacket immediately below the collar which is folded back on either side of the front opening
folded part of a coat or jacket below the collar

Examples

He pinned a red rose to his lapel for the wedding.
The suit's wide lapels give it a vintage look.
She brushed a speck of dust from his lapel before he entered the meeting.

Etymology

From diminutive of 'lap' (edge or flap).
