numbat

1. NOUN

/ ˈnʌmˌbæt / (AmE), / ˈnʌmˌbæt / (BrE)
a small, insectivorous marsupial native to Australia, with a long snout and strong claws, known for feeding on termites and now endangered
Australian termite-eating marsupial

Examples

The numbat is easily recognized by its striped back and bushy tail.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect the endangered numbat from extinction.
We saw a numbat in the wildlife sanctuary during our trip to Western Australia.

Etymology

From the Noongar Aboriginal Australian word 'noombat', adopted into English in the 19th century.
