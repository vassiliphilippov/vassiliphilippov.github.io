atresia

1. NOUN

/ əˈtriʒə / (AmE), / əˈtriːziə / (BrE)
A medical condition in which a normal opening or passage in the body is abnormally closed or absent.
absence or closure of a body opening
medical

Examples

Congenital atresia of the esophagus requires surgical intervention soon after birth.
The child's symptoms were ultimately traced to biliary atresia.
Doctors explained that atresia can affect various parts of the body, including the intestines or ears.

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (not) + 'trēsis' (a perforation).
