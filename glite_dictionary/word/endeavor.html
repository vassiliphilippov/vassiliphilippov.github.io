endeavor

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdɛvər / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛvə / (BrE)
an attempt to achieve something, especially one that involves serious effort and determination
serious effort to achieve something

Examples

Her endeavor to start her own business was met with both challenges and successes.
Scientific endeavor has led to many important discoveries.
The team was praised for their endeavor to improve the community center.

Synset Examples

made an effort to cover all the reading material
wished him luck in his endeavor
she gave it a good try

Related Terms

  • Synonym: effort - a vigorous or determined attempt
  • Synonym: attempt - an act of trying to do something
  • Antonym: idleness - lack of activity or effort

Etymology

From Old French *endeverer*, influenced by Old French *en* (in) + *devoir* (duty).

WordNet ID: oewn-00787849-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdɛvər / (AmE), / ɪnˈdevə / (BrE)
an attempt to achieve a goal, especially one that requires effort or determination
purposeful or strenuous effort

Examples

Her latest endeavor to write a novel took nearly three years to complete.
The scientific endeavor to cure the disease continues despite setbacks.
Global cooperation is vital in the endeavor to tackle climate change.

Synset Examples

he had doubts about the whole enterprise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enterprise - a project or undertaking, especially one that is difficult or requires effort
  • Synonym: attempt - an effort to achieve or complete a difficult task or action

Etymology

From Old French 'endevoir', based on Latin 'debere' (to owe).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00798547-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ɪnˈdɛvər / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛvə / (BrE)
to try hard to do or achieve something; to make an earnest effort.
make an earnest effort

Examples

We always endeavor to provide the highest quality service to our clients.
She endeavored to finish the project despite numerous challenges.
He is endeavoring to learn a new language in his spare time.

Synset Examples

we endeavor to make our customers happy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to strive - to make great efforts to achieve or obtain something
  • Synonym: attempt - to try to do something, especially something difficult
  • Antonym: neglect - to fail to care for or make an effort

Etymology

From Middle English 'endeveren', from the phrase 'put in dever', with 'dever' from Old French 'devoir' (duty).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02536865-v
Synset POS: v
