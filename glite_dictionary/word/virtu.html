virtu

1. NOUN

/ vərˈtu / (AmE), / vɜːˈtjuː / (BrE)
A love of or taste for fine objects of art, or the appreciation and collection of such objects.
love/taste for fine art objects
archaic

Examples

His collection revealed not only great wealth but true virtu and discernment.
She displayed remarkable virtu in identifying the origins of Renaissance paintings.
In the world of antiques, virtu is valued as much as the objects themselves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: connoisseurship - expertise or appreciation in the fine arts

Etymology

From Italian 'virtù' (excellence, worth), ultimately from Latin 'virtus' (virtue, excellence).

/ vɚˈtu / (AmE), / vɜːˈtjuː / (BrE)
Love or appreciation of fine art, or refined artistic taste; the quality of being artful or displaying connoisseurship.
love or appreciation of fine art

Examples

Her collection demonstrates a remarkable sense of virtu and understanding of Renaissance painters.
The exhibition was curated to appeal to those with a deep appreciation for virtu.
He was admired not only for his wealth, but also for his virtu in matters of art and antiques.

Etymology

From Italian 'virtù' (virtue, excellence, skill), itself from Latin 'virtus' (virtue).
