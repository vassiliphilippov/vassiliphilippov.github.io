ham

1. NOUN

/ hæm / (AmE), / hæm / (BrE)
A person who is licensed to operate amateur radio equipment.
licensed amateur radio operator

Examples

As a dedicated ham, she communicates with fellow enthusiasts around the globe every weekend.
The ham set up his antenna before the big storm hit to help with emergency communications.
Many hams take pride in building their own radio equipment from scratch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amateur radio operator - a person who uses radio equipment for non-commercial exchange of messages

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from 'ham-fisted' (clumsy) in early radio telegraphy slang, later applied to amateur radio operators.

2. NOUN

/ hæm / (AmE), / ham / (BrE)
the meat from the upper part of a pig's leg, usually cured and sometimes smoked, and eaten cold or hot
cut of pork from a pig's hind leg

Examples

She made a sandwich with sliced ham and cheese for lunch.
The holiday meal featured a glazed ham as the main course.
He prefers ham to turkey in his breakfast omelet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bacon - a type of cured pork meat, usually from the side or back of the pig

Etymology

Old English *ham* (the back of the knee; later also 'the thigh of a pig'), probably from a Germanic root.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ hæm / (AmE), / ham / (BrE)
an unskilled actor who overacts, especially in an exaggerated or theatrical manner
an unskilled, overacting performer
slang

Examples

He's such a ham that every line he delivers turns into a dramatic monologue.
The young actor tended to ham it up even in minor roles, drawing laughter from the crew.
People warned me not to be a ham on stage, but I couldn't resist making the audience laugh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overact - to act in an exaggerated way
  • Antonym: to underplay - to act with restraint or subtlety

Etymology

Perhaps from 'hamfatter', originally an amateur performer, possibly from the minstrel song 'The Ham-Fat Man'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10177603-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ hæm / (AmE), / hæm / (BrE)
(of an actor) to perform with exaggerated expressions or gestures, often to an excessive or theatrical degree.
to act in an exaggerated or theatrical way

Examples

He loves to ham whenever he's on stage, drawing all attention to himself.
The comedian hammed his role to make the audience laugh even harder.
Whenever there's a camera around, she can't help but ham for it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overact - to act in an exaggerated manner
  • Antonym: to underact - to act with too little emotion or restraint

Etymology

From 'ham actor', originally slang for an overacting or amateurish performer, possibly from 'hamfatter', a minstrel show term.

Source: WordNet
