staddle

1. NOUN

/ ˈstæd.əl / (AmE), / ˈstæd.əl / (BrE)
A base or platform, often made of stone or wood, on which hay, corn, or other crops are stacked to keep them off the ground.
base or platform for stacking crops

Examples

The old barn still had a staddle in the corner, once used to store hay safely.
Farmers built a staddle to prevent moisture from spoiling their corn stacks.
Several staddles remained in the field as reminders of traditional harvesting methods.

Etymology

From Old English *stadol* (foundation, support), related to German *Stadel* (barn).
