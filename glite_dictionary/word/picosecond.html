picosecond

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪkoʊˌsɛkənd / (AmE), / ˈpɪkəʊˌsɛkənd / (BrE)
A unit of time equal to one trillionth (10^-12) of a second.
one trillionth of a second

Examples

Ultrafast lasers can emit pulses lasting only a few picoseconds.
The reaction takes place in less than a picosecond.
Researchers measured the signal change with picosecond accuracy.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: nanosecond - one billionth (10^-9) of a second
  • Degree Lower: femtosecond - one quadrillionth (10^-15) of a second

Etymology

From International Scientific Vocabulary: combining prefix 'pico-' (from Spanish 'pico', meaning 'small amount') and 'second'.
