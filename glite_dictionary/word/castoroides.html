Castoroides

1. NOUN

/ kæs.təˈrɔɪ.diːz / (AmE), / kas.təˈrɔɪ.diːz / (BrE)
an extinct genus of giant beavers from the Pleistocene epoch, formerly found in eastern and southern North America
an extinct genus of giant beavers

Examples

The fossils of Castoroides indicate it was much larger than modern beavers.
Paleontologists discovered a skull belonging to Castoroides in a Florida riverbank.
During the Pleistocene, Castoroides inhabited the wetlands of what is now the eastern United States.

Etymology

From Latinized Greek 'castor' (beaver) + '-oides' (resembling), meaning 'resembling a beaver'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkæstəˈrɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / ˌkæstəˈrɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
an extinct genus of giant beavers that lived during the Pleistocene epoch in eastern and southern North America
extinct genus of giant beavers

Examples

Fossils of the Castoroides reveal it was much larger than modern beavers.
During the Pleistocene, Castoroides inhabited wetlands across what is now the United States.
The discovery of a Castoroides skull provided insight into giant beaver behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beaver - large, semi-aquatic rodent known for building dams

Etymology

From Latinized Greek, combining 'castor' (beaver) and '-oides' (resembling).
