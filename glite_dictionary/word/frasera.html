Frasera

1. NOUN

/ frəˈzɪrə / (AmE), / frəˈzɪərə / (BrE)
A genus of North American herbs in the gentian family, including the plant columbo and related species.
genus of North American herbs

Examples

The rare Frasera species bloomed late in the high mountain meadow.
Botanists studied several varieties of Frasera in the field.
Some herbal remedies were historically made from the roots of Frasera.

Etymology

Named after John Fraser (1750–1811), Scottish botanist.

2. NOUN

/ frəˈzɪrə / (AmE), / frəˈzɪərə / (BrE)
A genus of North American herbaceous plants commonly known as columbo, sometimes included in the genus Swertia.
genus of North American herbs (columbo)

Examples

The rare Frasera was found growing on the rocky slopes of the mountain.
Several species in the Frasera genus are valued for their medicinal properties.
Botanists debated whether to classify the plant as Frasera or under a different genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Swertia - a related genus sometimes including Frasera species

Etymology

Named after John Fraser (1750–1811), a Scottish botanist and explorer.
