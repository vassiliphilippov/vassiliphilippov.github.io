apotheosis

1. NOUN

/ əˌpoʊθiˈoʊsɪs / (AmE), / əˌpɒθiˈəʊsɪs / (BrE)
the perfect example or highest point of something; a person or thing regarded as a model of excellence or perfection
the perfect example; pinnacle of perfection

Examples

The pianist was regarded as the apotheosis of classical virtuosity.
Her new novel is considered the apotheosis of modern literature.
This building represents the apotheosis of 19th-century architecture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paragon - a person or thing regarded as a perfect example of a quality
  • Synonym: exemplar - someone or something serving as a model or ideal

Etymology

From Late Latin apotheosis (deification), from Greek apotheōsis, from apotheoun (to deify).

2. NOUN

/ əˌpɑːθiˈoʊsɪs / (AmE), / əˌpɒθɪˈəʊsɪs / (BrE)
the elevation of someone to divine status; the highest point in the development of something; a culmination or climax
elevation to divine status; climax

Examples

Many viewed the emperor's lavish funeral as the apotheosis of his reign.
She reached the apotheosis of her career with that groundbreaking performance.
The ancient Romans sometimes enacted the apotheosis of deceased emperors, declaring them gods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deification - the act of making someone into a god

Etymology

From Greek apotheōsis, from apotheoun ‘make a god’, from apo ‘from’ + theos ‘god’.
