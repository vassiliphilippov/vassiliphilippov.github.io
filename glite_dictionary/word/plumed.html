plumed

1. ADJ

/ plumd / (AmE), / pluːmd / (BrE)
decorated with or having one or more long, soft feathers or plumes; feathered or adorned with plumes
adorned with feathers or plumes

Examples

The parade featured dancers in plumed costumes of vibrant colors.
A plumed hat was once a symbol of high status.
White plumed egrets stood still in the shallow water.

Synset Examples

the plumed serpent
white-plumed egrets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feathered - covered with or made of feathers

Etymology

From 'plume' (feather), from Old French plume, from Latin pluma (feather, down).

2. ADJ

/ plumd / (AmE), / pluːmd / (BrE)
(of a helmet or similar item) decorated with a plume or feather, especially as worn by knights or in ceremonial dress.
having a decorative plume (esp. on helmet)

Examples

The knight wore a plumed helmet into battle, its colors catching the sunlight.
At the festival, children admired the guards' elaborate plumed hats.
The statue depicted a warrior with a plumed crest on his headgear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feathered - having feathers covering the surface

Etymology

From 'plume' (a feather), ultimately from Latin 'pluma' (small soft feather) + '-ed' forming an adjective.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00058121-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ plumd / (AmE), / pluːmd / (BrE)
decorated with a plume or feather-like tuft, especially as an ornament
adorned with a feather or plume

Examples

The dancer wore a plumed headdress during the carnival parade.
A majestic plumed helmet adorned the knight’s head.
Several tropical birds flashed their plumed tails in the sunlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feathered - covered with or resembling feathers

Etymology

From Middle French *plumé*, from Latin *plumatus* (feathered).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01002269-s
Synset POS: s
