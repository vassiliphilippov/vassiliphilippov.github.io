commissionaire

1. NOUN

/ kəˌmɪʃəˈnɛr / (AmE), / kəˌmɪʃəˈneə / (BrE)
a uniformed attendant, especially one who acts as a doorman at a hotel, theatre, or similar establishment
uniformed doorman or attendant
british

Examples

The commissionaire greeted guests at the entrance of the grand hotel.
A smartly dressed commissionaire opened the theatre doors with a flourish.
We handed our keys to the commissionaire as we arrived for the evening.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doorman - a person whose job is to stand at the entrance of a building and admit people

Etymology

From French 'commissionnaire' (agent, messenger), from 'commission' (charge, assignment).
