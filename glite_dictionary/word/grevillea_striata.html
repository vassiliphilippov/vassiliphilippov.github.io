Grevillea striata

1. NOUN

/ ɡrəˈviliə straɪˈɑːtə / (AmE), / ɡrɪˈviːlɪə straɪˈɑːtə / (BrE)
a tree native to Australia, known for its hard, heavy, reddish wood, commonly called beefwood
Australian tree with hard reddish wood

Examples

The Grevillea striata is prized for its dense, durable wood used in cabinetry.
Several Grevillea striata trees grow along the edges of the outback cattle station.
Indigenous Australians traditionally used Grevillea striata for making tools and implements.

Etymology

Genus name from Charles Francis Greville (1749–1809), a British botanist; 'striata' from Latin meaning 'striped'.
