kohlrabi

1. NOUN

/ koʊlˈræbi / (AmE), / kəʊlˈrɑːbi / (BrE)
A cabbage cultivar with an enlarged, bulbous edible stem, eaten as a vegetable.
cabbage with an edible swollen stem

Examples

She added diced kohlrabi to the salad for extra crunch.
Farmers' markets often sell fresh kohlrabi during the summer months.
Roasting kohlrabi brings out its natural sweetness.

Etymology

From German 'Kohlrabi', from 'Kohl' (cabbage) + 'Rübe' (turnip).

2. NOUN

/ koʊlˈrɑːbi / (AmE), / kəʊlˈrɑːbi / (BrE)
a type of vegetable with a thick, round, edible stem, resembling a turnip, from the cabbage family
edible stem vegetable, cabbage family

Examples

She added sliced kohlrabi to the salad for a crisp texture.
Farmers markets often sell fresh kohlrabi during spring and early summer.
Roasting kohlrabi brings out its mild, sweet flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turnip - a round root vegetable, white or purple outside with white flesh

Etymology

From German Kohlrabi, from Kohl (cabbage) + Rübe (turnip).
