paradoxically

1. ADV

/ ˌpærəˈdɑksɪkli / (AmE), / ˌpærəˈdɒksɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that seems self-contradictory or goes against common expectations.
in a self-contradictory or unexpected way

Examples

Paradoxically, the region with the most rain often suffers the most from droughts.
He acted paradoxically by refusing help even though he needed it the most.
Paradoxically, investing less effort sometimes produces better results.

Synset Examples

paradoxically, ice ages seem to occur when the sun gets hotter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ironically - used when something is the opposite of what is expected
  • Antonym: logically - in a manner consistent with logic or reason

Etymology

From 'paradoxical' (from Greek 'paradoxos', contrary to expectation) + '-ly'.
