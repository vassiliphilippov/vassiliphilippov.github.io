Microscopium

1. PROPN

/ ˌmaɪkrəˈskoʊpiəm / (AmE), / ˌmɪkrəˈskəʊpɪəm / (BrE)
a faint constellation in the southern sky, located near Sagittarius and Capricornus
a faint southern constellation

Examples

The constellation Microscopium is not easily visible to the naked eye due to its faint stars.
During our trip to the southern hemisphere, we tried to locate Microscopium in the night sky.
Many amateur astronomers overlook Microscopium because it is overshadowed by its brighter neighbors.

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'the microscope', named by the French astronomer Nicolas Louis de Lacaille in the 18th century.
