xanthoma

1. NOUN

/ zænˈθoʊmə / (AmE), / zænˈθəʊmə / (BrE)
a medical condition characterized by yellowish fatty deposits or nodules under the skin, often associated with cholesterol disorders
yellowish fatty skin deposits
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the yellowish growths on his eyelids as xanthoma.
People with high cholesterol may develop xanthomas on their neck or back.
Treatment of the underlying condition can sometimes reduce the appearance of xanthoma lesions.

Etymology

From Greek 'xanthos' (yellow) + '-oma' (tumor).
