Archeozoic

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrkioʊˈzoʊɪk / (AmE), / ˌɑːkɪəʊˈzəʊɪk / (BrE)
The geological eon from about 3,800 million to 2,500 million years ago, during which the Earth's crust formed and the earliest unicellular life appeared.
ancient geological eon; Earth's crust formed

Examples

Scientists study rocks from the Archeozoic to understand Earth's earliest history.
The Archeozoic is characterized by the formation of the planet's first solid crust.
Complex life had not yet evolved during the Archeozoic era.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Archaean - the main alternative term for this early geological eon

Etymology

From Greek 'arkhaios' (ancient) + 'zōē' (life).
