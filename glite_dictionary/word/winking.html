winking

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪŋkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɪŋkɪŋ / (BrE)
the rapid closing and opening of one eye, usually as a reflex or signal
rapid closing and opening of one eye

Examples

Her constant winking made it clear she was trying to send a secret message.
The doctor observed the patient's winking to check for nerve reflexes.
He tried to hide his nervousness with repeated winking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blink - to shut and open the eyes quickly

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪŋkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɪŋkɪŋ / (BrE)
Closing the eyes repeatedly and rapidly, often as a reaction to light or irritation.
rapid, repeated eye closing

Examples

The allergy caused him to start winking uncontrollably during the meeting.
In the harsh wind, she was winking constantly to protect her eyes.
The bright lights on the stage left the actor winking for several minutes.

Synset Examples

he stood blinking in the bright sunlight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blinking - closing and opening the eyes quickly and repeatedly

Etymology

From Middle English 'winken', meaning to close the eyes quickly; related to Old English 'wincian'.
