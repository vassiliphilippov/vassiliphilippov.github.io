to abut

1. VERB

/ əˈbʌt / (AmE), / əˈbʌt / (BrE)
(of land or a building) to be adjacent to or share a boundary with something.
be adjacent to or share a boundary

Examples

The new shopping center will abut the city park on its eastern edge.
Their backyard fences abut each other, so the neighbors often chat over the line.
The river abuts the edge of the farmland, providing essential irrigation.

Synset Examples

Canada adjoins the U.S.
England marches with Scotland

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to adjoin - to be next to or joined with
  • Synonym: border - to form an edge along or beside

Etymology

From Old French 'abouter', from 'a-' (to) + 'but' (end, goal).
