allograph

1. NOUN

/ ˈæləˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈaləɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a variant form of a letter or other grapheme, such as a different style or case of the same written character
variant form of a letter or symbol

Examples

Both print and cursive letterforms are considered allographs of the same grapheme.
The capital 'A' and the lowercase 'a' are allographs representing the same letter.
Handwritten versions of a letter can show many different allographs in personal styles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grapheme - the smallest unit of a writing system

Etymology

From Greek 'allos' (other) + 'graphein' (to write).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæl.əˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈæl.ə.ɡrɑːf / (BrE)
A signature signed by one person on behalf of another person; especially, a signature not made by the named individual.
signature written by someone else

Examples

The expert determined the document was signed with an allograph rather than the original signature.
A forgery can sometimes be detected if an allograph is inconsistent with a person's usual handwriting.
His lawyer argued the contract was invalid because the signature was actually an allograph.

Etymology

From Greek allos (other) + -graph (writing).
