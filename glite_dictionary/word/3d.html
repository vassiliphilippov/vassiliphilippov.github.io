3D

1. NOUN

/ ˌθriː diː / (AmE), / ˌθriː ˈdiː / (BrE)
a movie or visual presentation that gives the appearance of three dimensions to enhance realism
three-dimensional movie presentation

Examples

We saw the new superhero film in 3D and the effects were amazing.
Some people prefer traditional movies to watching them in 3D.
Tickets for the 3D screening sold out quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: three-dimensional - having or appearing to have length, breadth, and depth

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'three-dimensional.'

WordNet ID: oewn-06631444-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˌθriː ˈdiː / (AmE), / ˌθriː ˈdiː / (BrE)
having three dimensions; giving the illusion of depth, as in real life, movies, or graphics.
three-dimensional form or appearance

Examples

The movie was really impressive in 3D, with effects that jumped off the screen.
She used advanced software to create a realistic 3D model of the building.
Many video games offer both 2D and 3D gameplay modes now.

Synset Examples
