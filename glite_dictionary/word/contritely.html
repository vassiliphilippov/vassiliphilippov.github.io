contritely

1. ADV

/ kənˈtraɪtli / (AmE), / kənˈtraɪtli / (BrE)
In a way that shows sincere remorse or guilt for wrongdoing.
in a remorseful or guilty manner

Examples

She apologized contritely for arriving late to the meeting.
After breaking the vase, he spoke contritely to his mother.
The student looked contritely at the floor after being caught cheating.

Synset Examples

‘I made a big mistake,’ he said ruefully

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remorsefully - in a manner showing deep regret or guilt
  • Antonym: unrepentantly - in a manner not showing remorse or regret

Etymology

From Latin contrītus (worn out, crushed; penitent), via 'contrite' + -ly.
