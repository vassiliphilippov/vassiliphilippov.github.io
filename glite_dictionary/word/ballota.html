Ballota

1. NOUN

/ bəˈloʊtə / (AmE), / bəˈlɒtə / (BrE)
a genus of perennial herbs or subshrubs of the mint family, native mainly to the Mediterranean region, including the black horehound
a genus of Mediterranean herbs (black horehound)

Examples

Ballota is commonly found growing along roadsides in southern Europe.
The leaves of Ballota have a distinctive, unpleasant odor when crushed.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of various Ballota species.

Etymology

From Latin 'ballota', from Greek 'ballote', a kind of plant, possibly meaning 'rejected thing', referring to its unpleasant taste.

2. NOUN

/ bəˈloʊtə / (AmE), / bəˈlɒtə / (BrE)
A genus of perennial herbs or subshrubs in the mint family, native mainly to the Mediterranean region, including black horehound.
genus of Mediterranean perennial herbs

Examples

The Ballota genus includes several aromatic herbs used in traditional herbal medicine.
Many gardens in southern Europe feature Ballota for its resilience and silvery foliage.
Black horehound, a common Ballota species, is well known for its pungent scent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black horehound - a species in the genus Ballota, especially Ballota nigra

Etymology

From Latin 'ballota', from Greek 'ballōtē', a plant name.
