beanfeast

1. NOUN

/ ˈbinˌfist / (AmE), / ˈbiːn.fiːst / (BrE)
An annual celebration or dinner party given by an employer for their employees, traditionally involving plentiful food and entertainment.
annual employer-hosted employee party
british archaic

Examples

The company organized a grand beanfeast to thank its employees for their hard work.
Every summer, the beanfeast was eagerly anticipated by workers and their families.
In Victorian times, a beanfeast often included games and plenty of beer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outing - a trip or event for pleasure, often for a group
  • Synonym: banquet - a formal large meal for many people, often as a celebration

Etymology

Originally British; from 'bean' (the food) + 'feast', referring to feasts where beans were served.
