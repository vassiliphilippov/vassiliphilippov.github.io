accommodational

1. ADJ

/ əˌkɑːməˈdeɪʃənəl / (AmE), / əˌkɒməˈdeɪʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to the process by which the lens of the eye adjusts to focus on objects at different distances
of lens adjustment in the eye
medical

Examples

The doctor examined the patient's accommodational response to different visual tests.
Reduced accommodational ability can be a sign of presbyopia in older adults.
Special exercises are sometimes prescribed to improve accommodational function in children with focusing difficulties.

Synset Examples

accommodational strain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accommodation - the process by which the eye's lens changes shape to focus on near or far objects

Etymology

From 'accommodation' + '-al', denoting relation to the physiological process of focusing in the eye.
