homely

1. ADJ

/ ˈhoʊmli / (AmE), / ˈhəʊmli / (BrE)
(especially British English) Pleasantly simple, cozy, and comfortable like home.
cozy and comfortable like home
british

Examples

The cottage had a homely charm that made everyone feel at ease.
She prefers a homely atmosphere to the formality of a luxury hotel.
The tearoom was unmistakably homely, with mismatched chairs and homemade cakes.

Synset Examples

the homely everyday atmosphere
a homey little inn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homey - pleasantly comfortable, reminiscent of home
  • Antonym: uninviting - not offering a pleasant or welcoming atmosphere

Etymology

From Old English *hāmlīc* (of the home).

2. ADJ

/ ˈhoʊmli / (AmE), / ˈhəʊmli / (BrE)
simple, plain, and unpretentious; not elaborate or showy.
plain and unpretentious

Examples

Her homely advice helped me see things more clearly.
The restaurant serves homely meals that remind me of my childhood.
He admired the homely simplicity of their wedding ceremony.

Synset Examples

homely truths
letters to his son full of homely advice
homely fare

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simple - not complicated; easy to understand or do
  • Antonym: elaborate - involving many careful details; complicated and showy

Etymology

From home + -ly, meaning 'like home' or 'characteristic of home'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01797914-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈhoʊmli / (AmE), / ˈhəʊmli / (BrE)
plain or lacking artificial refinement or elegance; simple and unpretentious in appearance or manner
plain and lacking refinement

Examples

The homely decor gave the cottage a modest, welcoming feel.
She prefers homely meals to gourmet dishes served in fancy restaurants.
Despite its homely appearance, the chair was sturdy and comfortable.

Synset Examples

plain homely furniture
homely manners

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plain - simple or ordinary in appearance or style; not elaborate
  • Antonym: elegant - graceful and stylish in appearance or manner

Etymology

From Middle English *homly* (domestic, familiar), from Old English *hāmlīc* (of the home).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00854767-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ˈhoʊmli / (AmE), / ˈhəʊmli / (BrE)
(of a person or thing) unattractive in appearance; lacking in physical beauty or proportion.
unattractive in appearance

Examples

Despite her homely looks, her kindness made her popular among her classmates.
The homely building stood out among the modern glass towers downtown.
He always felt self-conscious about being the homely one in his family.

Synset Examples

a homely child
several of the buildings were downright homely
a plain girl with a freckled face

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plain - not beautiful or attractive in appearance
  • Antonym: attractive - pleasing or appealing in appearance
  • Synonym: ugly - unpleasant or unattractive in appearance

Etymology

From Middle English *homly* (domestic, familiar), later extending (in American English) to the sense of plain or unattractive.
