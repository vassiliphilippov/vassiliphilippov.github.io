Guenevere

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡwɛnəˌvɪr / (AmE), / ˈɡwɛnɪvɪə / (BrE)
(in Arthurian legend) the wife of King Arthur, sometimes portrayed as the lover of Sir Lancelot, whose affair is said to have brought about the downfall of the Knights of the Round Table.
wife of King Arthur in Arthurian legend

Examples

The tragic romance between Guenevere and Lancelot is a central theme in many Arthurian tales.
Many poets have written about Guenevere's beauty and the consequences of her choices.
In some stories, Guenevere is depicted as both a devoted queen and a flawed woman.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arthur - legendary king of Britain in Arthurian legend
  • Synonym: Lancelot - knight of the Round Table and lover of Guenevere in Arthurian legend

Etymology

From Old French Guenièvre, from Welsh Gwenhwyfar, meaning 'fair one'.
