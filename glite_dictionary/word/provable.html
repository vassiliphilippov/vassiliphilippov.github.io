provable

1. ADJ

/ ˈproʊvəbəl / (AmE), / ˈpruːvəbəl / (BrE)
able to be demonstrated to be true or proven through evidence or argument
able to be proved or demonstrated

Examples

His claims are not provable without additional evidence.
The theory is elegant, but is it provable in practice?
Every mathematical statement in the system must be either provable or unprovable.

Synset Examples

a demonstrable lack of concern for the general welfare
practical truth provable to all men

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demonstrable - able to be shown to exist or be true
  • Antonym: unprovable - not able to be proven

Etymology

From prove + -able, by analogy with words like moveable.
