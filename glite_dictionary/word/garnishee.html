garnishee

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡɑːrnɪˈʃi / (AmE), / ˌɡɑːnɪˈʃi / (BrE)
a person or organization whose money, usually wages, is subject to a court-ordered garnishment, typically to satisfy a debt
person whose wages are garnished
legal

Examples

As a garnishee, his employer was required by law to withhold part of his wages.
The court notified the garnishee to submit payment by the end of the month.
A garnishee must comply with the instructions specified in the garnishment order.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debtor - a person who owes a sum of money
  • Antonym: garnisher - the party who obtains the garnishment order

Etymology

From 'garnish' in the legal sense (to warn or notify), of Old French origin.

2. VERB

/ ˌɡɑːrnəˈʃi / (AmE), / ˌɡɑːnɪˈʃiː / (BrE)
To legally seize part of someone's wages or assets to pay a debt, typically by court order.
seize wages/assets by court order
legal

Examples

The court ordered the company to garnishee his wages to recover unpaid taxes.
If you fail to pay off your loans, the lender may garnishee your bank account.
Her salary was garnisheed after she missed several child support payments.

Synset Examples

His employer garnished his wages in order to pay his debt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garnish - to legally take money or property to satisfy a debt

Etymology

From garnish (to warn, to attach) + -ee, originally referring to the third party instructed to hand over assets.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02278924-v
Synset POS: v
