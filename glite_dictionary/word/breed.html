breed

1. VERB

/ brid / (AmE), / briːd / (BrE)
(of animals) to produce offspring; to reproduce.
(of animals) reproduce; have young

Examples

Pandas rarely breed in captivity.
Horses breed once a year, usually in the spring.
Some fish breed only in freshwater rivers despite living in the ocean.

Synset Examples

pandas rarely breed in captivity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reproduce - to produce offspring

Etymology

Old English *brēdan* (to nurture, breed).

4. NOUN

/ brid / (AmE), / briːd / (BrE)
A group of domesticated animals within a species having distinctive characteristics developed by selective breeding.
variety of domesticated animal species

Examples

The farmer raised a rare breed of sheep on his land.
Labradors are a popular breed of dog due to their friendly nature.
She specializes in training a particular breed that excels in agility competitions.

Synset Examples

he experimented on a particular breed of white rats
he created a new strain of sheep

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strain - a group of animals or plants within a species, having distinctive characteristics; less formal than 'breed'
  • Degree Higher: species - a group of living organisms capable of interbreeding; broader than 'breed'

Etymology

From Old English 'bredan', related to 'brood', meaning to produce offspring.
