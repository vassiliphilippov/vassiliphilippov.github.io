to ascertain

1. VERB

/ ˌæs ərˈteɪn / (AmE), / ˌæs əˈteɪn / (BrE)
to find out or learn something with certainty, usually by making an inquiry or effort
determine with certainty

Examples

The investigator tried to ascertain the cause of the incident before making any statements.
We need to ascertain whether the data has been corrupted during the transfer.
It is important to ascertain the facts before drawing any conclusions.

Synset Examples

I want to see whether she speaks French
See whether it works
find out if he speaks Russian
Check whether the train leaves on time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to determine - to find out or establish exactly, typically as a result of research or calculation
  • Synonym: to discover - to find out something previously unknown
  • Antonym: guess - to estimate or suppose without sufficient information

Etymology

From Old French *ascertener*, from Latin *certus* (certain).

2. VERB

/ ˌæs.ərˈteɪn / (AmE), / ˌæs.əˈteɪn / (BrE)
to find out or learn something with certainty; to make sure of
find out with certainty

Examples

The investigator tried to ascertain the cause of the accident.
Before making a decision, she wanted to ascertain all the facts.
It took several hours to ascertain that the documents were genuine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to determine - to establish or find out exactly by research or calculation; synonym
  • Synonym: to confirm - to establish the truth or correctness of something; synonym
  • Antonym: guess - to form an opinion without sufficient information; antonym in context of certainty

Etymology

From Old French 'acertener', from 'a-' (to) + 'certener' (make certain), based on Latin 'certus' (certain).

3. VERB

/ ˌæsɚˈteɪn / (AmE), / ˌæsəˈteɪn / (BrE)
to make sure or find out that something is definitely true, correct, or will happen
make sure; find out for certain

Examples

The manager asked her assistant to ascertain that all the lights were switched off before leaving.
We need to ascertain whether everyone has received the updated schedule for tomorrow's meeting.
Before publishing the article, the reporter tried to ascertain all the facts from multiple sources.

Synset Examples

He verified that the valves were closed
See that the curtains are closed
control the quality of the product

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to verify - to make sure or demonstrate that something is true, accurate, or justified
  • Synonym: to confirm - establish the truth or correctness of something
  • Antonym: doubt - to feel uncertain about something; lack certainty

Etymology

From Old French 'acertener' (to make certain), from 'a-' (to) + 'certainer' (to certify), from Latin 'certus' (sure).
