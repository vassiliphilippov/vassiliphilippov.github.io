shriveled

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃrɪvəld / (AmE), / ˈʃrɪvəld / (BrE)
lean, wrinkled, and shrunken as a result of age, illness, or drying out
shrunken and wrinkled

Examples

During the drought, the once juicy grapes became shriveled and inedible.
The old man's shriveled hands trembled as he reached for the cup.
After being left in the sun all day, the flower looked completely shriveled.

Synset Examples

the old woman's shriveled skin
he looked shriveled and ill
a shrunken old man
a lanky scarecrow of a man with withered face and lantern jaws
he did well despite his withered arm
a wizened little man with frizzy grey hair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: withered - dried up and shriveled; often due to aging or lack of moisture
  • Synonym: wizened - shrunken and wrinkled, usually from age
  • Antonym: plump - having a full and rounded shape, not shriveled

Etymology

From Middle English 'shrivel', probably of Scandinavian origin.

2. ADJ

/ ˈʃrɪvəld / (AmE), / ˈʃrɪvəld / (BrE)
having lost moisture and become dry, wrinkled, or shrunken, especially as a result of age or lack of water.
dried out and shrunken

Examples

The shriveled leaves on the plant showed it hadn't been watered in weeks.
After months of drought, the once lush grass turned brittle and shriveled.
She tossed the shriveled grapes into the compost bin.

Synset Examples

dried-up grass
the desert was edged with sere vegetation
shriveled leaves on the unwatered seedlings
withered vines

Related Terms

  • Synonym: withered - dried and shriveled, especially through loss of moisture
  • Synonym: sere - (of vegetation) dry or withered

Etymology

From Middle English *shrivel*, of uncertain origin, related to Old Norse *skrifa* (to graze, scratch).

3. ADJ

/ ˈʃrɪvəld / (AmE), / ˈʃrɪvəld / (BrE)
reduced in vitality, strength, or effectiveness; diminished in intensity or importance.
diminished in strength or intensity

Examples

After months of neglect, their shriveled hopes for success finally disappeared.
The shriveled attendance numbers concerned the event organizer.
Once a booming business, it now survives on shriveled profits.

Synset Examples

our shriveled receipts during the storm
as the project wore on she found her enthusiasm shriveled
the dollar's shrunken buying power

Related Terms

  • Synonym: withered - dried up or diminished in strength or vitality
  • Synonym: faded - having lost brightness, strength, or intensity
  • Antonym: robust - strong and healthy; vigorous

Etymology

From Middle English 'shrivel,' of uncertain origin.
