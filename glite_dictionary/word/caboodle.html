caboodle

1. NOUN

/ kəˈbudl̩ / (AmE), / kəˈbuːd(ə)l / (BrE)
the whole collection or group of things or people; everything together
entire collection or group
informal

Examples

She inherited the caboodle after her grandmother passed away.
We're moving, so we're packing up the whole caboodle this weekend.
If you win the lottery, you could buy the entire caboodle of cars on display.

Synset Examples

she bought the whole caboodle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kit and caboodle - the whole lot; everything

Etymology

Probably a humorous alteration of 'boodle', itself from Dutch 'boedel' (property, estate).
