to roam

1. VERB

/ roʊm / (AmE), / rəʊm / (BrE)
to move about or travel aimlessly or without a fixed destination, often in search of something
move about aimlessly; wander

Examples

After dinner, they liked to roam the neighborhood without any particular plan.
The wolves roam freely through the forest in search of prey.
During the summer, she would often roam the countryside for hours, enjoying the peace and quiet.

Synset Examples

The gypsies roamed the woods
roving vagabonds
the wandering Jew
The cattle roam across the prairie
the laborers drift from one town to the next
They rolled from town to town

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wander - to walk or move in a leisurely, casual, or aimless way
  • Synonym: drift - to move slowly, aimlessly, or without direction
  • Antonym: stay - to remain in the same place

Etymology

From Middle English 'romen', probably of Germanic origin.
