Carassius auratus

1. NOUN

/ kəˈræsiəs ɔːˈreɪtəs / (AmE), / kəˈræsɪəs ɔːˈreɪtəs / (BrE)
a small, golden or orange-red freshwater fish native to Eurasia, commonly kept as a pond or aquarium pet; also known as the goldfish.
species of goldfish

Examples

The pet shop had several varieties of Carassius auratus swimming energetically in their tanks.
Children enjoy watching Carassius auratus glide gracefully through ponds and aquariums.
Researchers use Carassius auratus in studies on animal learning and behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goldfish - common name for Carassius auratus, a small golden fish kept as a pet

Etymology

From Latin: 'Carassius' (a genus of fish) and 'auratus' (gilded, golden).
