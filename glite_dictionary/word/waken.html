to waken

1. VERB

/ ˈweɪkən / (AmE), / ˈweɪkən / (BrE)
to stop sleeping or to cause someone to stop sleeping; to wake up
stop sleeping; cause to stop sleeping

Examples

I did not waken until the sun was high in the sky.
A loud crash wakened her from a deep sleep.
He tries not to waken his roommate when he leaves early in the morning.

Synset Examples

She woke up to the sound of the alarm clock

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wake - to emerge or cause to emerge from sleep; become awake
  • Antonym: sleep - to rest with eyes closed and mind and body inactive

Etymology

From Old English *wacan* (to wake up) and *wacian* (to be awake), influenced by Scandinavian forms.

2. VERB

/ ˈweɪkən / (AmE), / ˈweɪkən / (BrE)
to cause (someone) to become awake or conscious; to wake up
cause to become awake or conscious

Examples

The loud thunder wakened the children in the middle of the night.
Could you waken me before sunrise tomorrow?
I tried not to waken anyone when I left early this morning.

Synset Examples

He was roused by the drunken men in the street
Please wake me at 6 AM.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wake - to stop sleeping; to become awake
  • Synonym: to awaken - to cause to stop sleeping; to rouse

Etymology

From Old English *wæcnan* (to awake, arise), related to *wacan* (to wake).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00018806-v
Synset POS: v
