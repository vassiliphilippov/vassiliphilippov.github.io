temptingness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛmptɪŋnəs / (AmE), / ˈtɛmptɪŋnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being tempting or attractive, especially by arousing desire or interest
the quality of being tempting or attractive

Examples

The temptingness of the offer made it hard to refuse.
She underestimated the temptingness of the luxurious desserts displayed in the window.
Despite the temptingness of a higher salary, he decided to stay at his current job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allure - the quality of being powerfully attractive or fascinating
  • Synonym: attractiveness - the quality of being attractive or pleasing
  • Antonym: repulsiveness - the quality of being repulsive or unattractive

Etymology

From 'tempting' + '-ness', following standard English word formation.
