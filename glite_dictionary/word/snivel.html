snivel

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnɪvəl / (AmE), / ˈsnɪvəl / (BrE)
the act or sound of whining or complaining in a tearful or self-pitying way
tearful whining or complaining

Examples

His constant snivel about unfair treatment annoyed everyone in the office.
She ignored the child's snivel and explained that rules apply to everyone.
There was not a single snivel from him despite the difficult conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whine - a long, high-pitched complaining cry or sound

Etymology

From Old English *snofl* (mucus from the nose), related to 'sniffle'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00870201-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈsnɪvəl / (AmE), / ˈsnɪvəl / (BrE)
to cry or complain in a tearful, sniffling, or whining manner, often with snuffling noises
cry or whine with snuffling

Examples

He began to snivel when his request was denied.
Don't snivel about your chores—just get them done.
The child continued to snivel long after the argument ended.

Synset Examples

Stop snivelling — you got yourself into this mess!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whine - to complain in a feeble or petulant way
  • Synonym: to complain - to express dissatisfaction or annoyance about something

Etymology

From Old English *snyflan* (to run at the nose).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00067045-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈsnɪvəl / (AmE), / ˈsnɪvəl / (BrE)
to speak in a tearful, whining, or self-pitying manner
talk in a whining or tearful way

Examples

He began to snivel when told he couldn't have any more candy.
Stop sniveling and explain what happened calmly.
She sniveled about how unfair the decision was.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whimper - to make low, feeble sounds expressive of fear, pain, or unhappiness
  • Synonym: whine - to complain in a feeble or petulant way

Etymology

From Middle English *snivelen*, probably of imitative origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01044403-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈsnɪvəl / (AmE), / ˈsnɪvəl / (BrE)
to inhale or draw mucus up through the nose, especially quietly or repeatedly
to draw mucus up through the nose

Examples

He tried not to snivel when he caught a cold at school.
She sniveled quietly in the back row, hoping no one would notice her runny nose.
Children often snivel during winter when the air is cold and dry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sniffle - to sniff repeatedly, usually because of a runny nose

Etymology

From Old English *snoflian* (to run at the nose), related to 'sniffle'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00101533-v
Synset POS: v
