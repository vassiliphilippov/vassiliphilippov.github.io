Nerium

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɪriəm / (AmE), / ˈnɪəriəm / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen shrubs in the dogbane family, Apocynaceae, which includes the oleander.
plant genus including oleander

Examples

The toxic qualities of Nerium make it important to keep away from pets and children.
Gardeners often plant Nerium as an ornamental shrub despite its toxicity.
The beautiful pink flowers of Nerium bloom throughout summer in warm climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oleander - a poisonous evergreen shrub of the genus Nerium

Etymology

From Latin 'nerium', from Ancient Greek 'nērion' (oleander).

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɪəriəm / (AmE), / ˈnɪərɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen shrubs in the dogbane family (Apocynaceae), containing a single species known as oleander.
plant genus (oleander)

Examples

The only species in the genus Nerium is commonly called oleander.
Botanists classify the beautiful but poisonous oleander under the genus Nerium.
Nerium is often grown as an ornamental plant in warm climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oleander - an evergreen shrub with poisonous leaves and fragrant flowers

Etymology

From Latin 'Nerium', an ancient name for oleander, possibly derived from Greek 'nēris' or 'nērion'.
