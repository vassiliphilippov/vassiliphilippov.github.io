to restock

1. VERB

/ riˈstɑk / (AmE), / riˈstɒk / (BrE)
to fill something, such as a store or supply, with goods or materials again after they have been used or sold
to fill with goods again

Examples

The shopkeeper had to restock the shelves after the weekend rush.
We need to restock our pantry before the storm hits.
After selling out of toys, they quickly restocked for the holiday shoppers.

Synset Examples

He restocked his land with pheasants

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stock - to supply with goods or merchandise
  • Antonym: to deplete - to reduce or use up the supply of something

Etymology

From the prefix re- (again) + stock (to supply goods).
