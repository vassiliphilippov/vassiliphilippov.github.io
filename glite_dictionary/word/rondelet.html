rondelet

1. NOUN

/ rɒn.dəˈleɪ / (AmE), / ˌrɒn.dəˈleɪ / (BrE)
A short poem of French origin, consisting of seven lines with a fixed rhyme scheme and a repeated refrain, and considered a shorter form of the rondeau.
short French fixed-form poem of seven lines

Examples

She composed a rondelet for the poetry competition, impressing the judges with her mastery of form.
The student struggled to match the tricky rhyme scheme of the rondelet.
Many writers enjoy experimenting with traditional forms like the rondelet to challenge their creativity.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: rondeau - French verse form with a refrain, typically longer than a rondelet

Etymology

From French 'rondelet', diminutive of 'rondeau' (little round), referring to the poem's repetitive structure.
