Raleigh

1. PROPN

/ ˈrɑːli / (AmE), / ˈrɔːli / (BrE)
Sir Walter Raleigh (1552–1618), English courtier, explorer, and writer, known for his attempts to colonize Virginia and for popularizing tobacco and potatoes in England.
English courtier and explorer (1552–1618)

Examples

Raleigh was a favorite at the court of Queen Elizabeth I and was later executed under King James I.
Many historians credit Raleigh with introducing the potato and tobacco to England.
In 1585, Raleigh sponsored an expedition to establish a colony on Roanoke Island.
