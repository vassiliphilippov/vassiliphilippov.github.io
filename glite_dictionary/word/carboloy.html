Carboloy

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrbəˌlɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈkɑːbəlɔɪ / (BrE)
an alloy of tungsten carbide with cobalt or nickel as a binder, used especially for manufacturing metal-cutting tools
tungsten carbide alloy for tools

Examples

The engineer recommended using Carboloy inserts for the new lathe to increase cutting efficiency.
Modern drills with Carboloy tips can cut through hardened steel with ease.
To extend the tool life, machinists often prefer Carboloy over conventional steel alloys.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tungsten carbide - a hard, brittle, chemical compound used for cutting tools

Etymology

Blend of 'carbide' and 'alloy', trademark name first used in the early 20th century.
