vaccinated

1. ADJ

/ ˈvæksɪˌneɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈvaksɪneɪtɪd / (BrE)
Having received a vaccine so as to become protected against a particular disease.
having received a vaccine

Examples

All healthcare workers must be vaccinated against hepatitis B.
Most children are vaccinated before they start school.
The country aims to have 80% of its population vaccinated by the end of the year.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unvaccinated - not having received a vaccine
  • Synonym: immunized - having become immune, especially by vaccination

Etymology

From 'vaccine', which comes from Latin 'vaccinus' (from cows), referring to the origin of the first smallpox vaccine.
