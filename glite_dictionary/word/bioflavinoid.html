bioflavinoid

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪ.oʊˈfleɪ.vəˌnɔɪd / (AmE), / ˌbʌɪ.əʊˈfleɪ.və.nɔɪd / (BrE)
Any of a group of vitamins that help maintain the strength and resistance of cell and capillary walls.
type of vitamin for cell and capillary health
medical

Examples

Many fruits are rich in bioflavinoids, which help support the body's natural defenses.
Some vitamin supplements contain bioflavinoids to strengthen blood vessels.
Researchers are studying the health benefits of bioflavinoid intake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flavonoid - any of a group of plant compounds with antioxidant effects

Etymology

From 'bio-' (life) + 'flavin' (yellow pigment) + '-oid' (resembling), referring to a group of plant-derived compounds.
