to inflict

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈflɪkt / (AmE), / ɪnˈflɪkt / (BrE)
to make someone experience something unpleasant or harmful, such as pain, punishment, or suffering
impose something unpleasant or harmful

Examples

The new law will inflict severe penalties on offenders.
He did not want to inflict any pain on his friends with his decision.
She accidentally inflicted a minor injury on herself while cooking.

Synset Examples

The principal visited his rage on the students

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to impose - to force something to be accepted or put in place
  • Synonym: to administer - to give or apply (punishment, remedy, etc.)
  • Antonym: to suffer - to experience or be subjected to something bad

Etymology

From Latin 'inflictus', past participle of 'infligere' (to strike against, inflict) from 'in-' (in, on) + 'fligere' (to strike).
