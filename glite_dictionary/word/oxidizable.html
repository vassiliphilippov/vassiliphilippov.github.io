oxidizable

1. ADJ

/ ˌɑksɪˈdaɪzəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˌɒksɪˈdaɪzəbl̩ / (BrE)
capable of undergoing a chemical reaction with oxygen, especially by combining with it or being changed by it
able to react with oxygen

Examples

Iron is highly oxidizable, which is why it rusts when exposed to air and moisture.
Some metals are more oxidizable than others and react more quickly with oxygen.
The oxidizable nature of the compound was demonstrated by its rapid change in color upon exposure to air.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reducible - capable of being reduced in a chemical reaction

Etymology

From oxidize + -able, indicating the capacity for oxidation.
