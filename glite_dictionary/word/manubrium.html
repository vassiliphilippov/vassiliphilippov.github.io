manubrium

1. NOUN

/ məˈnuːbriəm / (AmE), / məˈnjuːbriəm / (BrE)
the broad, upper part of the sternum (breastbone) with which the clavicles and first ribs articulate
upper part of the breastbone
medical

Examples

The manubrium connects to the clavicle via the sternoclavicular joint.
A fracture of the manubrium is relatively uncommon compared to other parts of the sternum.
The surgeon carefully examined the manubrium during the chest procedure.

Etymology

From Latin 'manubrium' (handle), due to its handle-like shape in anatomy.
