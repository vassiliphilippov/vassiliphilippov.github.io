Squatinidae

1. NOUN

/ skwɑːˈtɪnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / skwəˈtɪnɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of bottom-dwelling sharks with flattened bodies and broad pectoral fins, resembling rays.
family of ray-like bottom-dwelling sharks

Examples

Members of the Squatinidae family are commonly known as angel sharks.
The Squatinidae exhibit distinct adaptations for life on the ocean floor.
Many species in Squatinidae are threatened by overfishing due to their slow reproductive rates.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Squatinidae', based on the genus name 'Squatina'.
