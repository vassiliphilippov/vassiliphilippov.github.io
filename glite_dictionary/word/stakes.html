stakes

1. NOUN

/ steɪks / (AmE), / steɪks / (BrE)
the money or valuables risked on the outcome of a bet, contest, or game
money risked in gambling or competition

Examples

The stakes were high in last night's poker game.
He decided to raise the stakes to make the game more exciting.
Whenever you gamble, you should only risk stakes you can afford to lose.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bet - an amount of money risked on the outcome of a possible event
  • Synonym: wager - an amount of money risked in a bet

Etymology

From Old English 'staca' (stake, post); later sense of 'money risked' from the custom of marking bets with a stake or post.
