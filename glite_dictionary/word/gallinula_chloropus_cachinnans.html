Gallinula chloropus cachinnans

1. NOUN

/ ɡæləˈnuːlə kloʊˈrəʊpəs kəˈkɪnænz / (AmE), / ɡalɪˈnjuːlə klɒˈrəʊpəs kəˈkɪnænz / (BrE)
a subspecies of the common moorhen found in North America, characterized by dark bluish-grey plumage
North American subspecies of moorhen

Examples

The Gallinula chloropus cachinnans can be recognized by its distinct dark bluish-grey plumage.
Researchers observed a pair of Gallinula chloropus cachinnans nesting in the wetland reserve.
Unlike its Eurasian relatives, the Gallinula chloropus cachinnans is unique to North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gallinula chloropus - the common moorhen, a widespread waterbird species

Etymology

From Latin: Gallinula (diminutive of 'hen'), chloropus ('green-footed'), and subspecies name cachinnans.
