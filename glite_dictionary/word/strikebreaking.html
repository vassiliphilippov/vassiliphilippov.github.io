strikebreaking

1. NOUN

/ ˈstraɪkˌbreɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstraɪkˌbreɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of taking measures, often confrontational, to disrupt or end a strike by workers
the act of disrupting a workers' strike

Examples

The company was accused of strikebreaking when it brought in temporary workers during the protest.
Unions strongly oppose any form of strikebreaking as it undermines collective bargaining.
The act of strikebreaking has led to numerous confrontations in labor history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strikebreaker - a person who works or is employed in place of others who are on strike

Etymology

From 'strike' (work stoppage) + 'breaking' (ending or disrupting).
