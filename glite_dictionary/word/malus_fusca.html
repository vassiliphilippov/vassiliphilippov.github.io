Malus fusca

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪləs ˈfʌskə / (AmE), / ˈmeɪləs ˈfʌskə / (BrE)
A species of crab apple native to the western United States, with white blossoms and small yellow or red fruit.
species of crab apple tree

Examples

The Malus fusca thrives in moist coastal regions of the Pacific Northwest.
Botanists are studying the genetic diversity of Malus fusca populations.
Native peoples traditionally used the fruit of Malus fusca for food and medicine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pacific crab apple - Common name for Malus fusca, a crab apple species native to western North America

Etymology

From Latin 'Malus' (apple tree) and 'fusca' (dusky, dark).
