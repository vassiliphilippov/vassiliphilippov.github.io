macrozamia

1. NOUN

/ ˌmækrəˈzeɪmiə / (AmE), / ˌmakrəˈzeɪmɪə / (BrE)
A genus of treelike cycads native to Australia, having erect trunks, pinnate leaves, and large cones with sometimes edible nuts.
Australian genus of treelike cycads

Examples

The seeds of the macrozamia are sometimes used as bush food by Indigenous Australians.
A macrozamia can be recognized by its stiff, glossy leaves and massive cones.
After the fire, the ancient macrozamia sprouted fresh growth from its trunk.

Etymology

From Greek makros (large) and zamia (pine cone, a kind of plant).
