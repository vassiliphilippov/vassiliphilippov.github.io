Macrozamia

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæk.rəˈzeɪ.mi.ə / (AmE), / ˌmæk.rəˈzeɪ.mi.ə / (BrE)
A genus of large evergreen Australian cycads, sometimes placed in the family Cycadaceae.
a genus of Australian cycads

Examples

The Macrozamia grow naturally in the forests of eastern Australia.
Some species within the Macrozamia genus are cultivated as ornamental plants.
Botanists recently discovered a new species of Macrozamia in Queensland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cycad - any plant of the division Cycadophyta, palm-like but not a true palm

Etymology

From Greek makros (large) + zamia (pine cone, a cycad genus).
