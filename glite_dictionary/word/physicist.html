physicist

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪzɪsɪst / (AmE), / ˈfɪzɪsɪst / (BrE)
a scientist who specializes in the study of physics
scientist specializing in physics

Examples

Albert Einstein was a renowned physicist who changed our understanding of the universe.
The physicist gave a lecture on quantum mechanics to the university students.
Many physicists work at research institutions or universities around the world.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: scientist - a person who is studying or has expertise in science
  • Antonym: physician - a medical doctor

Etymology

From physics + -ist, indicating a practitioner of a field.
