Chrysanthemum morifolium

1. NOUN

/ krɪˌsænθəməm mɔːˌraɪfəˈliəm / (AmE), / krɪˌsænθəməm mɒːˌrɪfəˈliːəm / (BrE)
A species of chrysanthemum native to China, widely cultivated for its large, colorful, and decorative flowers.
a species of ornamental chrysanthemum

Examples

The vibrant Chrysanthemum morifolium blooms are popular in autumn floral displays.
She planted several Chrysanthemum morifolium bushes along the garden path for their striking colors.
Tea made from Chrysanthemum morifolium flowers is enjoyed throughout East Asia.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: chrysanthemum - a plant of the genus Chrysanthemum, with showy flowers

Etymology

From Greek *chrysos* (gold) + *anthemon* (flower); species name from Latin *morus* (mulberry) and *folium* (leaf), referring to leaf shape.
