anticholinergic

1. NOUN

/ ˌæntiˌkoʊləˈnɜrdʒɪk / (AmE), / ˌantiˌkəʊlɪˈnɜːdʒɪk / (BrE)
a substance that opposes or blocks the action of acetylcholine in the nervous system
substance blocking acetylcholine
medical

Examples

Some medications used to treat allergies have anticholinergic properties that can cause drowsiness.
Elderly patients are more susceptible to side effects from anticholinergic drugs.
The doctor prescribed an anticholinergic to help control the patient's overactive bladder.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cholinergic - substance or effect resulting from acetylcholine activity

Etymology

From anti- (against) + cholinergic (relating to acetylcholine).

2. ADJ

/ ˌæn.tiˌkoʊ.lɪˈnɜːr.dʒɪk / (AmE), / ˌæn.tiˌkəʊ.lɪˈnɜː.dʒɪk / (BrE)
(of a drug or substance) inhibiting or blocking the action of acetylcholine at its receptor sites in the nervous system.
blocking acetylcholine activity (medical)
medical

Examples

Doctors often prescribe anticholinergic medications to treat overactive bladder symptoms.
Some anticholinergic drugs can cause side effects like dry mouth and blurred vision.
Elderly patients may be more sensitive to the effects of anticholinergic substances in medications.

Synset Examples

anticholinergic drugs

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cholinergic - releasing or responding to acetylcholine

Etymology

From 'anti-' (against) and 'cholinergic' (pertaining to acetylcholine), formed from 'choline' and Greek 'ergon' (work).
