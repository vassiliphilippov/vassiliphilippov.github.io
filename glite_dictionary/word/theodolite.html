theodolite

1. NOUN

/ θiˈɑdəˌlaɪt / (AmE), / θɪˈɒdəlaɪt / (BrE)
a surveying instrument with a rotating telescope used to measure horizontal and vertical angles
surveying instrument for measuring angles

Examples

The engineer set up the theodolite to survey the new construction site accurately.
Measuring the angle between landmarks was easy with a precise theodolite.
Modern theodolites often include digital displays for increased accuracy.

Etymology

From Modern Latin theodolitus, probably coined by 16th-century mathematician Leonard Digges.
