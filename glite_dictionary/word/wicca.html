Wicca

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪkə / (AmE), / ˈwɪkə / (BrE)
A modern polytheistic religion, often connected with witchcraft, centering on a mother goddess, and claiming origins in pre-Christian pagan traditions of western Europe.
modern pagan witchcraft religion

Examples

Many people are drawn to Wicca because of its close connection to nature and seasonal festivals.
She has studied Wicca for years, focusing on its rituals and beliefs.
The influence of Wicca can be seen in many modern pagan practices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: witchcraft - the practice of magical skills and abilities, especially in Wiccan or pagan traditions
  • Synonym: paganism - religious practices outside main world religions, especially pre-Christian faiths of Europe

Etymology

Coined in the 20th century, from Old English 'wicca' meaning 'male witch.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪkə / (AmE), / ˈwɪkə / (BrE)
A modern pagan religion whose followers worship nature and practice witchcraft, often honoring a goddess and a god.
modern pagan religion; witchcraft-based faith

Examples

The practice of Wicca centers on the reverence for nature and seasonal festivals.
Many people are drawn to Wicca for its emphasis on spiritual freedom and personal growth.
She joined a group that studies Wicca and related earth-based religions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Wiccan - a follower or adherent of the Wicca religion
  • Degree Higher: paganism - broad term for polytheistic or nature-based religions, including Wicca

Etymology

Coined in the mid-20th century from Old English *wicca* (male witch).
