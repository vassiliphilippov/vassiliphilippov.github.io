Sonchus oleraceus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑntʃəs oʊləˈreɪsiəs / (AmE), / ˈsɒŋkəs ɒləˈreɪsɪəs / (BrE)
An annual Eurasian sow thistle with soft spiny leaves and yellow, rayed flower heads, commonly found as a weed.
annual Eurasian sow thistle

Examples

Sonchus oleraceus is often seen growing as a weed in urban gardens and fields.
Many foragers use Sonchus oleraceus leaves in salads because of their mild, edible flavor.
Researchers study Sonchus oleraceus to understand its impact on local ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sow thistle - any of various annual or perennial plants of the genus Sonchus

Etymology

From Latin; 'Sonchus' is from Greek 'sonchos' (sow thistle), 'oleraceus' meaning 'vegetable-like' or 'edible'.
