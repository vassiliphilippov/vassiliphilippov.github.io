coccobacillus

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑkoʊbəˈsɪləs / (AmE), / ˌkɒkəʊbəˈsɪləs / (BrE)
a bacterium with a shape intermediate between spherical (coccus) and rod-shaped (bacillus); a very short rodlike bacterium
bacterium intermediate in shape between coccus and bacillus
medical

Examples

Some bacteria, such as Haemophilus influenzae, are classified as coccobacillus due to their intermediate shape.
Under the microscope, the pathogen appeared as a coccobacillus rather than a typical coccus or bacillus.
Accurate identification of a coccobacillus can be essential for proper diagnosis and treatment.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: coccus - spherical bacterium
  • Degree Higher: bacillus - rod-shaped bacterium

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'coccus' (berry, spherical bacterium) and 'bacillus' (rod-shaped bacterium).
