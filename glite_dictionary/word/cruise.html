cruise

1. NOUN

/ kruːz / (AmE), / kruːz / (BrE)
a journey on a large ship taken for pleasure, usually visiting several places
ocean trip for pleasure

Examples

They are going on a cruise to the Caribbean next month.
The couple enjoyed a luxury cruise for their anniversary.
During the cruise, they visited several islands and explored new cities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voyage - a long journey by sea or in space
  • Synonym: expedition - a journey with a particular purpose, especially that of exploration
  • Degree Lower: excursion - a short journey made for pleasure
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00313758-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ kruːz / (AmE), / kruːz / (BrE)
to travel at a steady, moderate speed, especially comfortably or for pleasure
travel at a steady, moderate speed

Examples

The airplane will cruise at an altitude of 30,000 feet for most of the flight.
We decided to cruise along the coast to enjoy the scenery at a relaxed pace.
The ship cruised steadily through calm waters as the passengers looked out at the sunset.

Synset Examples

Please keep your seat belt fastened while the plane is reaching cruising altitude

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: speed - move quickly; travel at high speed
  • Degree Lower: crawl - move slowly; travel at a very slow speed

Etymology

From Dutch 'kruisen' (to cross), influencing nautical usage in the 17th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01848801-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ kruːz / (AmE), / kruːz / (BrE)
(of a person) to move around a public area in search of a sexual partner, typically in a casual or discreet manner.
search for a sexual partner in public

Examples

He used to cruise the bars downtown, hoping to meet someone.
The police were aware that some people would cruise the park at night.
She didn't realize that certain areas were popular with people who cruised for dates.

Synset Examples

The men were cruising the park

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hook up - to meet and have a sexual encounter
  • Degree Lower: flirt - to behave in a way that shows romantic or sexual attraction

4. VERB

/ kruːz / (AmE), / kruːz / (BrE)
to sail or travel about for pleasure, relaxation, or sightseeing, typically without a fixed destination
travel for pleasure by ship or vehicle

Examples

They decided to cruise around the Mediterranean for their summer holiday.
Every winter, hundreds of tourists cruise along the Alaskan coast to admire the glaciers.
We spent the afternoon cruising on the lake, enjoying the sunshine and scenery.

Synset Examples

We were cruising in the Caribbean

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voyage - a long journey involving travel by sea or in space
  • Synonym: sail - to travel on water in a ship or boat, especially as a leisure activity

Etymology

From Dutch 'kruisen' (to cross), used in the sense of moving to and fro at sea.

Source: WordNet
