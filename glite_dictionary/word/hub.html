hub

1. NOUN

/ hʌb / (AmE), / hʌb / (BrE)
the central part of a wheel, fan, or propeller through which the axle or shaft passes
central part of a wheel or rotating object

Examples

The bicycle mechanic replaced the damaged hub before installing a new wheel.
Dust often collects around the hub of a ceiling fan and needs to be cleaned regularly.
The car's wheel hub was reinforced to handle rough terrain.

Etymology

Possibly from the earlier 'hubbe,' of unknown origin, referring to a block or lump; later specialized in meaning.

2. NOUN

/ hʌb / (AmE), / hʌb / (BrE)
the central point of activity, interest, or importance in a particular place or situation
central point of activity or importance

Examples

The new café quickly became the hub of the neighborhood's social life.
This city serves as a major transportation hub for the region.
Their office is the hub where all important decisions are made.

Synset Examples

the playground is the hub of parental supervision
the airport is the economic hub of the area

Related Terms

  • Synonym: center - the middle point or part; a central location or focus
  • Antonym: periphery - the outer limits or edge of an area or object; not central

Etymology

From the earlier sense 'solid center of a wheel' (early 19th century, of uncertain origin).
