Caledonia

1. PROPN

/ ˌkælɪˈdoʊniə / (AmE), / ˌkælɪˈdəʊniə / (BrE)
a poetic and historical name for Scotland, originally referring to the area north of the Antonine Wall in Roman times
poetic name for Scotland

Examples

The misty mountains of Caledonia have inspired poets for centuries.
Many songs and poems celebrate the beauty of Caledonia.
During his trip through Caledonia, he learned about the region's rich history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Scotland - a country that is part of the United Kingdom

Etymology

From Latin Caledonia, the Roman name for northern Scotland.
