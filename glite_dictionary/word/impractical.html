impractical

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpræktɪkəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpræktɪkəl / (BrE)
not sensible, realistic, or workable; not suited to practical use or action
not workable; not practical

Examples

The plan was interesting but completely impractical for our small team.
His impractical ideas often clash with the realities of business.
Buying such an expensive car is impractical given your budget.

Synset Examples

refloating the ship proved impractical because of the expense
he is intelligent but too impractical for commercial work
an impractical solution

Related Terms

  • Antonym: practical - sensible and realistic; able to be done or used

Etymology

From im- (not) + practical, first recorded in the early 19th century.

2. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpræktɪkəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpræktɪkəl / (BrE)
not useful or sensible for a particular situation; not likely to work or be effective in practice
not workable or sensible

Examples

Buying such an expensive car is completely impractical for a family on a tight budget.
His plan to commute by helicopter was exciting but totally impractical.
She often dismisses impractical ideas that can't be put into action.

Synset Examples

airy theories about socioeconomic improvement
visionary schemes for getting rich

Related Terms

  • Antonym: practical - sensible and effective in real situations
  • Synonym: unworkable - not able to be done or to work successfully

Etymology

From im- (not) + practical, based on Latin practicus (practical).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02508026-s
Synset POS: s
