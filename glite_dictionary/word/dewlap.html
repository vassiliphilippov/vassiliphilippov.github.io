dewlap

1. NOUN

/ ˈduːˌlæp / (AmE), / ˈdjuːˌlæp / (BrE)
a fold of loose skin that hangs from the neck, especially in older people or some animals
loose fold of skin on neck

Examples

The elderly man gently touched the dewlap that had formed under his chin over the years.
As she aged, a noticeable dewlap appeared, making her neck look looser.
In some people, the dewlap becomes more prominent due to loss of skin elasticity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wattle - a fleshy, often pendant, lobe or appendage, such as that found on some birds and animals

Etymology

Originally from Middle English, probably from Old English *lappa* (loose piece or flap).
