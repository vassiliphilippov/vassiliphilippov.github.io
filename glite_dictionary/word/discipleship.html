discipleship

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsaɪpəlˌʃɪp / (AmE), / dɪˈsaɪplʃɪp / (BrE)
the state or condition of being a disciple; the process of following and learning from a teacher, leader, or philosophy, especially in a religious context.
state of being a disciple

Examples

During his years of discipleship, he closely followed the teachings of his spiritual mentor.
The church places great emphasis on discipleship as a lifelong journey of growth and learning.
Her commitment to discipleship inspired others to explore their own spiritual paths.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discipline - training or practice that develops skill or character; often related by origin to 'disciple'
  • Antonym: apostasy - the abandonment or renunciation of a religious or political belief

Etymology

Formed from 'disciple' and the noun-forming suffix '-ship'. 'Disciple' is from Latin 'discipulus' (pupil, learner).
