typographic

1. ADJ

/ ˌtaɪ.pəˈɡræf.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌtʌɪ.pəˈɡraf.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to the design, style, or arrangement of printed text and letters, especially as used in printing or typesetting.
relating to typography or printed text

Examples

The designer focused on the typographic details of the new logo.
A typographic error in the headline changed the entire meaning of the article.
Her research explored the typographic trends of digital magazines.

Synset Examples

the typographic art
a typographical error

Related Terms

  • Synonym: typographical - alternative form, especially in reference to errors in printed text

Etymology

From typography + -ic; ultimately from Greek typos (impression, mark) + -graphia (writing).
