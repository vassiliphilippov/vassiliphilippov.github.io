Deneb

1. PROPN

/ ˈdɛnɛb / (AmE), / ˈdɛnɛb / (BrE)
the brightest star in the constellation Cygnus and one of the brightest stars in the night sky
brightest star in Cygnus constellation

Examples

Deneb is easily visible in the northern hemisphere during the summer months.
The distinctive shape of Cygnus is anchored by its brightest star, Deneb.
Photographers often capture Deneb as part of the Summer Triangle in night sky images.

Etymology

From Arabic ḏanab (tail), as in ḏanab al-dajāja (tail of the hen).
