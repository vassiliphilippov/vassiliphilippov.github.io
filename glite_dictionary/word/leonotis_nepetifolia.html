Leonotis nepetifolia

1. NOUN

/ liˈɑnətɪs ˌnɛpɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / liˈɒnətɪs ˌnɛpɪˈfəʊliə / (BrE)
A tropical herbaceous plant of the mint family, widely naturalized, with whorls of bright, tubular, lipped orange flowers.
a tropical herb with striking orange flowers

Examples

The garden's centerpiece was a tall Leonotis nepetifolia, its orange flowers attracting hummingbirds.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of Leonotis nepetifolia in tropical regions.
In some areas, Leonotis nepetifolia is grown as an ornamental herb for its vibrant blooms.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, genus name 'Leonotis' (from Greek leon meaning 'lion' and otis meaning 'ear') and species name 'nepetifolia' (with leaves like Nepeta, catmint).
