arson

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrsən / (AmE), / ˈɑːsən / (BrE)
The criminal act of deliberately setting fire to property.
criminal act of intentionally burning property
legal

Examples

The old warehouse was destroyed in a case of arson last night.
He was arrested and charged with arson after investigators found evidence of deliberate ignition.
The insurance company refused payment because the fire was determined to be arson.

Synset Examples

the British term for arson is ‘fire-raising’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fire-raising - the British term for arson
  • Degree Lower: fire - the process of combustion marking burning; not criminal

Etymology

From Old French *arson* (burning), from Late Latin *arsio* (a burning), from Latin *ardere* (to burn).
