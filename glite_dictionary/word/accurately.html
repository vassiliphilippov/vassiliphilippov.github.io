accurately

1. ADV

/ ˈækjərətli / (AmE), / ˈækjʊrətli / (BrE)
in a way that is correct and free from errors; precisely or exactly.
in a correct and precise manner

Examples

The machine was able to measure the temperature accurately to within half a degree.
She described the suspect's appearance accurately to the police officer.
Without the correct data, it is difficult to accurately predict the results.

Synset Examples

repeated the order accurately

Related Terms

  • Synonym: precisely - in an exact and accurate way
  • Antonym: incorrectly - in an incorrect or mistaken way

Etymology

Formed from 'accurate' + '-ly', where 'accurate' comes from Latin 'accuratus', past participle of 'accurare' (to take care).

2. ADV

/ ˈæk.jɚ.ət.li / (AmE), / ˈæk.jʊ.rət.li / (BrE)
in a way that is correct and free from mistakes; precisely or exactly
in a correct or exact way

Examples

She was able to accurately predict the outcome of the experiment.
It is important to measure the ingredients accurately when baking a cake.
The witness accurately described what happened during the incident.

Synset Examples

he works very accurately

Related Terms

  • Synonym: precisely - in an exact and accurate way
  • Antonym: inaccurately - in a way that is not correct or exact

Etymology

From the adjective 'accurate' + adverbial suffix '-ly'. 'Accurate' is from Latin 'accuratus' (done with care).
