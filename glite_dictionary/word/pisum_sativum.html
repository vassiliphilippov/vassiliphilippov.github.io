Pisum sativum

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪzəm səˈtiːvəm / (AmE), / ˈpiːzəm səˈtɪvəm / (BrE)
A climbing annual plant of the legume family, cultivated for its edible green peas, usually eaten fresh rather than dried.
plant producing edible green peas

Examples

The garden was full of Pisum sativum, ready to be harvested for fresh peas.
Scientists studied Pisum sativum to understand genetic inheritance.
Farmers often rotate crops with Pisum sativum to enrich soil nitrogen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pea - a round green seed, or the plant that produces it

Etymology

From Latin 'pisum' (pea) and 'sativum' (cultivated).
