fluidness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfluɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈfluːɪdnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of flowing easily; the property of being fluid.
the property of flowing easily

Examples

The fluidness of the paint allowed it to spread smoothly across the canvas.
Engineers tested the fluidness of different mixtures to find the optimal one for the pipeline.
Her writing style is notable for the fluidness of its prose, which captivates readers.

Synset Examples

adding lead makes the alloy easier to cast because the melting point is reduced and the fluidity is increased
they believe that fluidity increases as the water gets warmer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fluidity - the quality of being fluid or able to flow easily
  • Antonym: viscosity - the property of a liquid to resist flowing, thickness

Etymology

From 'fluid' (from Latin fluidus, meaning 'flowing, liquid') + '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfluːɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈfluːɪdnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being fluid, especially the capacity to change or adapt easily; changeability.
quality of being changeable or adaptable

Examples

His speech had a unique fluidness that captivated the audience.
The fluidness of the design allows it to fit various spaces.
Some admire the fluidness with which she moves between different artistic styles.

Synset Examples

a charming Oriental fluidity of manner
a certain fluidness in his perception of time made him an unpredictable colleague
demographers try to predict social fluidity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fluidity - the quality of being fluid or able to flow easily; adaptability

Etymology

From 'fluid' + '-ness', formed on the model of similar English nouns.
