briary

1. ADJ

/ ˈbraɪəri / (AmE), / ˈbraɪəri / (BrE)
Covered with or resembling briars; having many thorns or prickles.
full of or like briars, thorny

Examples

We hiked through a briary patch that scratched our legs.
The briary hedgerow formed a natural barrier between the fields.
His garden was overgrown with briary vines and wild roses.

Synset Examples

a horse with a short bristly mane
bristly shrubs
burred fruits
setaceous whiskers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thorny - full of or covered with thorns
  • Synonym: prickly - having or covered with sharp points or prickles

Etymology

From 'briar' (prickly bush) + '-y' (adjectival suffix).
