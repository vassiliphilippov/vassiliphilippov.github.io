threescore

1. ADJ

/ ˈθriˌskɔr / (AmE), / ˈθriːskɔː / (BrE)
amounting to sixty; being three times twenty.
sixty; three times twenty
archaic

Examples

The king had reigned for threescore years before he passed away.
In ancient texts, people often lived for threescore and ten years.
They celebrated their threescore wedding anniversary with a grand party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sixty - the cardinal number equal to 60

Etymology

From Middle English 'thre sco(ru)', meaning 'three times twenty'. 'Score' as a counting unit comes from Old Norse 'skor'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈθriˌskɔr / (AmE), / ˈθriːˌskɔː / (BrE)
the number sixty; three times twenty
the number sixty
archaic

Examples

He lived to the age of threescore and ten years, as the Bible says.
The army counted threescore soldiers among its ranks.
She inherited threescore acres of land from her family.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: score - the number twenty
  • Degree Higher: fourscore - the number eighty

Etymology

From Middle English 'threscore', from Old English 'þrīe' (three) + 'scoru' (score, i.e., twenty).
