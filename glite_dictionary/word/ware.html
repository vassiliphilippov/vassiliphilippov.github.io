ware

1. NOUN

/ wɛr / (AmE), / wɛə / (BrE)
(usually in combination) manufactured articles or goods of a particular kind or material.
manufactured articles of a kind or material

Examples

The shop displays a variety of ceramic ware on its shelves.
Many vendors were selling their handmade wares at the market.
The factory specializes in kitchen ware made from stainless steel.

Etymology

Old English 'waru' (merchandise, goods).

2. NOUN

/ wɛr / (AmE), / wɛə / (BrE)
an item or items offered for sale, especially manufactured goods or commodities considered collectively
goods or merchandise for sale

Examples

The market was bustling with vendors eager to sell their wares to tourists.
She displayed her handmade pottery ware at the local craft fair.
Online shops often advertise their ware through social media platforms.

Synset Examples

good business depends on having good merchandise
that store offers a variety of products

Related Terms

  • Synonym: merchandise - goods that are bought and sold
  • Synonym: goods - things that are produced to be sold
  • Antonym: service - an action done for someone rather than a physical item sold

Etymology

From Old English 'waru' (article of merchandise, commodity).
