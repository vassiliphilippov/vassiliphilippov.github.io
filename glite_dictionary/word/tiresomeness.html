tiresomeness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪərsəmnəs / (AmE), / ˈtaɪəsəmnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being dull, boring, or tedious, especially because of length or slowness.
quality of being dull or tedious

Examples

The tiresomeness of the speech made it hard to stay awake.
Many students complained about the tiresomeness of the lengthy lecture.
He was frustrated by the tiresomeness of the long, slow-moving process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tedium - the state of being tedious or boring
  • Synonym: boredom - the state of feeling bored

Etymology

From 'tiresome' (tedious, boring) + '-ness' (state or quality).
