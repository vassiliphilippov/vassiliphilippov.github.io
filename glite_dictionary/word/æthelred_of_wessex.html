Æthelred of Wessex

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛθəlˌrɛd əv ˈwɛsɛks / (AmE), / ˈɛθəlˌrɛd əv ˈwɛsɪks / (BrE)
(died 871) King of Wessex and Kent from 865 to 871, elder brother of Alfred the Great, remembered for leading defense against Danish invaders.
King of Wessex (died 871); brother of Alfred the Great

Examples

Æthelred of Wessex ruled before his famous brother Alfred the Great.
The reign of Æthelred of Wessex was marked by frequent battles with Danish invaders.
Historians credit Æthelred of Wessex with playing a vital role in the defense of southern England.

Etymology

Æthelred from Old English Æþelræd, meaning 'noble counsel.'
