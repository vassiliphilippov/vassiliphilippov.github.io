Cestum

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛstəm / (AmE), / ˈsɛstəm / (BrE)
A genus of ctenophores, commonly known as Venus's girdle, characterized by its ribbon-like, transparent, and luminescent body found in warm oceans.
ribbon-shaped marine ctenophore (Venus's girdle genus)

Examples

The Cestum moves gracefully through the water, its shimmering body resembling a transparent ribbon.
Researchers observed that Cestum glides by undulating its entire length.
Bright flashes appear when schools of Cestum reflect sunlight in the open ocean.

Etymology

From Latin 'cestum', meaning 'girdle', referring to its ribbon-like shape.
