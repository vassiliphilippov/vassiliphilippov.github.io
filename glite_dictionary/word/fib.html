fib

1. NOUN

/ fɪb / (AmE), / fɪb / (BrE)
a small or unimportant lie, often told to avoid hurting someone's feelings or to avoid trouble
a trivial or harmless lie

Examples

It was just a harmless fib to avoid hurting her feelings.
Kids sometimes tell a fib when they’re scared of getting into trouble.
He admitted that the story he told was a little fib and not the whole truth.

Synset Examples

he told a fib about eating his spinach
how can I stop my child from telling stories?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lie - an intentionally false statement
  • Antonym: truth - something that is true or factual
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06769768-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ fɪb / (AmE), / fɪb / (BrE)
to tell a small or unimportant lie, especially one not meant to cause harm
tell a small or harmless lie

Examples

She promised not to fib about her homework anymore.
If you fib to your friends, they may not trust you.
He admitted he had fibbed to avoid hurting her feelings.

Synset Examples

Fibbing is not acceptable, even if you don't call it lying

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lie - to say something that is not true with the intention to deceive

Etymology

Origin unknown; perhaps from the noun 'fib,' first recorded in the late 17th century.
