buttercup

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌt̬ɚˌkʌp / (AmE), / ˈbʌtəkʌp / (BrE)
a wild plant with bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers, belonging to the genus Ranunculus
wild plant with yellow flowers

Examples

A single buttercup bloomed in the middle of the grassy field.
Children often pick buttercups to test if friends like butter by holding them under the chin.
The garden was dotted with wild buttercup flowers after the spring rain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ranunculus - the genus of plants that includes buttercups

Etymology

From Middle English 'butercoppe', likely referring to the plant's color and cup-shaped flowers.
