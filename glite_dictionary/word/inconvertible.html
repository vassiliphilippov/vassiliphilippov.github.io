inconvertible

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪnkənˈvɝːtəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˌɪnkənˈvɜːtəbl̩ / (BrE)
(of a currency or asset) not able to be exchanged for another currency or asset of equal value.
not able to be exchanged for another currency

Examples

The country's inconvertible currency made international trade complicated for businesses.
After the new regulations, their notes became inconvertible and could not be exchanged for foreign money.
In times of crisis, some governments issue inconvertible paper money to control outflow of capital.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: convertible - able to be exchanged for another currency of equal value

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + convertibilis (able to be converted).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00920321-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪnkənˈvɝːtəbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪnkənˈvɜːtəbəl / (BrE)
not able to be changed into something else; incapable of conversion.
not able to be converted

Examples

Due to its chemical stability, gold is considered an inconvertible metal.
The contract stated that the offer was inconvertible and could not be exchanged for cash.
Some currencies were once inconvertible on international markets.

Synset Examples

the alchemists were unable to accept the inconvertible nature of elemental metals

Related Terms

  • Antonym: convertible - able to be changed into a different form or use

Etymology

From Latin 'inconvertibilis', from 'in-' (not) + 'convertibilis' (convertible).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00350040-s
Synset POS: s
