undigested

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.dɪˈdʒɛstɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.dɪˈdʒɛstɪd / (BrE)
(of food or information) not processed, absorbed, or assimilated by the digestive system or the mind.
not digested or processed

Examples

The doctor found traces of undigested food in the patient's stomach.
Some of the data in the report appeared undigested and unorganized.
He felt discomfort due to undigested material in his digestive tract.

Synset Examples

undigested food

Related Terms

  • Antonym: digested - processed or absorbed (of food or information)

Etymology

From the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'digested', meaning not digested.

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.dɪˈdʒɛstɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.dɪˈdʒɛstɪd / (BrE)
(of ideas, facts, or information) not thoroughly considered, analyzed, or integrated mentally; not absorbed or assimilated by the mind.
not mentally absorbed or analyzed

Examples

The lecture left me with an undigested collection of theories and no clear understanding.
He quoted an undigested mass of statistics without putting them into context.
Too much undigested information can overwhelm rather than enlighten readers.

Synset Examples

an undigested mass of facts gathered at random

Related Terms

  • Antonym: assimilated - fully incorporated or absorbed into the mind

Etymology

From un- (not) + digested, past participle of 'digest', from Latin 'digestus' (carried apart, arranged).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01382287-s
Synset POS: s
