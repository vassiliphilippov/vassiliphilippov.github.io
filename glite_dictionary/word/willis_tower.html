Willis Tower

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɪlɪs ˈtaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɪlɪs ˈtaʊ.ə / (BrE)
a skyscraper in Chicago, completed in 1974, formerly known as the Sears Tower; one of the tallest buildings in the United States.
skyscraper in Chicago, formerly Sears Tower

Examples

The Willis Tower is a popular tourist attraction, offering breathtaking views of Chicago from its Skydeck.
Many Chicagoans still refer to the Willis Tower by its former name, the Sears Tower.
The Willis Tower was once the tallest building in the world when it was completed in 1974.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sears Tower - former name of the Willis Tower

Etymology

Named after the Willis Group, which acquired naming rights in 2009; originally called Sears Tower (named for the Sears, Roebuck & Co.).
