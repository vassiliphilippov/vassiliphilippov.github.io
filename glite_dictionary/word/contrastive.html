contrastive

1. ADJ

/ kənˈstræstɪv / (AmE), / kənˈstrɑːstɪv / (BrE)
(of two or more things) showing or expressing a difference, especially in order to distinguish or emphasize; used to describe features, meanings, or forms that contrast with each other.
showing or expressing contrast

Examples

The linguist explained how contrastive pairs help identify distinct sounds in a language.
Their contrastive arguments highlighted the fundamental differences between their views.
This table provides a contrastive analysis of the two competing theories.

Synset Examples

‘rich’ and ‘hard-up’ are contrastive terms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contrasting - differing strikingly; showing contrast

Etymology

From contrast + -ive, modeled after French contrastif.

2. ADJ

/ kənˈstræstɪv / (AmE), / kənˈstrɑːstɪv / (BrE)
showing a clear difference when compared with something else, or tending to emphasize differences.
showing or emphasizing differences

Examples

The artist used contrastive colors to make the painting more vibrant.
Linguists study contrastive features to understand distinctions between languages.
Her writing style is highly contrastive when compared to her earlier works.

Synset Examples

contrasting (or contrastive) colors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contrasting - showing a clear difference with something else
  • Antonym: similar - resembling without showing significant difference

Etymology

From 'contrast' + '-ive', patterned after French 'contrastif'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02073546-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ kənˈstræstɪv / (AmE), / kənˈstrɑːstɪv / (BrE)
expressing or involving a contrast between two elements, especially in language or grammar
expressing a contrast, especially in language

Examples

The linguist explained how contrastive stress can change the meaning of a sentence.
In this lesson, we explore contrastive analysis in the study of phonetics.
'But' is often used as a contrastive conjunction to highlight opposing ideas.

Synset Examples

disjunctive conjunctions like ‘but’, ‘or’, or ‘though’ serve a contrastive function

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contrasting - showing a difference when compared
  • Antonym: complementary - serving to complete or enhance something else

Etymology

From 'contrast' + '-ive', formed on analogy with other adjectives.

Source: WordNet
