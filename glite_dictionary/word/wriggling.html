wriggling

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɪɡlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɪɡlɪŋ / (BrE)
moving with continual twisting or squirming motion, like a worm or snake
moving in a twisting or squirming way

Examples

The child was wriggling in her seat, unable to sit still during the long ceremony.
She picked up a wriggling fish and quickly threw it back into the water.
The puppy escaped my arms with a wriggling motion and dashed away.

Synset Examples

wiggly worms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squirming - moving with writhing, twisting motions

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'wriggle,' probably of Low German origin.
