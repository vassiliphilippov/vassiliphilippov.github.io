Egretta garzetta

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈɡrɛtə ɡɑrˈzɛtə / (AmE), / ɪˈɡrɛtə ɡɑːˈzɛtə / (BrE)
The scientific name for the little egret, a small white heron found in parts of Europe, Africa, Asia, and Australia.
scientific name for the little egret

Examples

The wetlands are home to the graceful Egretta garzetta during the breeding season.
A group of birdwatchers spotted an Egretta garzetta near the riverbank.
Conservation efforts have helped increase the population of Egretta garzetta in the area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: little egret - a small white heron with slender black bill and long neck

Etymology

From Latin; 'Egretta' meaning a type of heron, and 'garzetta' from Italian 'garzetta' (little heron).
