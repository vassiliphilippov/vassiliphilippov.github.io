Equisetaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛkwɪˈsɛtəˌsiː / (AmE), / ˌɛkwɪˌsiːtəˈsiː / (BrE)
a family of primitive vascular plants, known as horsetails, comprising the sole surviving members of the order Equisetales
family of horsetail plants
medical

Examples

The Equisetaceae are easily recognized by their jointed stems and distinctive brush-like appearance.
Fossil records show that Equisetaceae were once much more diverse and widespread than they are today.
Modern Equisetaceae are found mostly in damp environments around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horsetail - common name for plants in the Equisetaceae family

Etymology

From modern Latin, from genus name Equisetum + -aceae (the family suffix in botanical Latin).

2. NOUN

/ ˌekwɪsɪˈteɪsiːˌaɪ / (AmE), / ˌɛkwɪsɪˈtɛɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of non-flowering, spore-producing plants known as horsetails, the only surviving family of the order Equisetales
family of horsetail plants

Examples

The Equisetaceae are easily recognized by their hollow, jointed stems.
Ancient relatives of Equisetaceae once grew as large trees in prehistoric swamps.
Members of the Equisetaceae are often found in wet soils along streams and ponds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horsetail - common name for plants of the family Equisetaceae

Etymology

From the genus name Equisetum (Latin for 'horsehair') plus the standard botanical suffix -aceae indicating a plant family.
