singularity

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪŋɡjəˈlærəti / (AmE), / ˌsɪŋɡjʊˈlærəti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being unique or different from others; distinctiveness
uniqueness; distinctiveness

Examples

Her singularity set her apart in a room full of ordinary thinkers.
The artist's singularity attracted admirers from all over the world.
He was known for the singularity of his vision and unconventional approach.

Synset Examples

that singularity distinguished him from all his companions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uniqueness - the quality of being the only one of its kind
  • Antonym: ordinary - lacking special or distinctive features; normal

Etymology

From Late Latin singularitas (uniqueness), from Latin singularis (single, unique).

2. NOUN

/ sɪŋˈɡjælərɪti / (AmE), / sɪŋˈɡjʊləriti / (BrE)
the state or quality of being remarkable, unusual, or distinctive; strangeness
the quality of being unusual or remarkable

Examples

The singularity of his style made him stand out among other artists.
Her singularity of character intrigued everyone she met.
The old mansion has a singularity that makes it the talk of the town.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uniqueness - the quality of being one of a kind
  • Antonym: ordinariness - the quality of being ordinary or not unusual

Etymology

From Late Latin *singularitas* (uniqueness, peculiarity), from Latin *singularis* (alone, unique).
