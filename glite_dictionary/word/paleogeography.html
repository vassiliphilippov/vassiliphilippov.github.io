paleogeography

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪlioʊdʒiˈɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌpalɪəʊdʒɪˈɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the study of the physical geography of ancient times or ancient geological epochs
study of ancient physical geography
medical

Examples

She specialized in paleogeography, mapping the continents as they appeared millions of years ago.
The course in paleogeography included studying fossil evidence to reconstruct ancient landforms.
Research in paleogeography has helped scientists understand the movement of tectonic plates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paleontology - the scientific study of prehistoric life and fossils

Etymology

From Greek palaios (ancient) + geography; patterned after geology terms.
