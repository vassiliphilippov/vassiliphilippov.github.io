foolhardiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfulˌhɑrdinəs / (AmE), / ˈfuːlˌhɑːdɪnəs / (BrE)
the quality of taking reckless or unnecessary risks and giving little thought to danger or consequences
recklessness; disregard for danger

Examples

His foolhardiness led him to attempt the climb without any safety gear.
Everyone was astonished by the foolhardiness of her proposal, given the obvious risks involved.
Acting out of foolhardiness, he challenged the storm despite all the warnings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recklessness - the quality of being without concern for the consequences of actions
  • Antonym: prudence - wisdom or caution in practical affairs

Etymology

From foolhardy (from Old French folhardi, from fol 'foolish' + hardi 'bold') + -ness.
