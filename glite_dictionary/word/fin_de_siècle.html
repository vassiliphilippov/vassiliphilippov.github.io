fin de siècle

1. ADJ

/ fæ̃ də siˈɛkl̩ / (AmE), / fæ̃ də siˈɛkl / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of the end of a century, especially the cultural, artistic, or social styles prominent at the end of the 19th century.
of or relating to the end of a century, especially the 19th

Examples

The decadent themes of the novel are typical of fin de siècle literature.
Many artists embraced fin de siècle sensibilities, fascinated by both beauty and decay.
The mood of uncertainty and innovation defined the fin de siècle period in European culture.

Synset Examples

fin de siecle art

Etymology

From French, literally 'end of century'.

2. NOUN

/ fæn də siˈɛkl / (AmE), / fɛ̃ də sjɛkl / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of the end of a century, especially the 19th century, often suggesting fashionable decadence or world-weariness.
end of a century, esp. the 19th century

Examples

The novel captures the fin de siècle mood of Paris, filled with both anxiety and excitement.
Many artists of the fin de siècle period were obsessed with themes of decadence and decline.
Discussions about culture during the fin de siècle often focused on changing values and artistic innovation.

Etymology

From French 'fin de siècle' (end of the century).

WordNet ID: oewn-15293376-n
Synset POS: n
