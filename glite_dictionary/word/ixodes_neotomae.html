Ixodes neotomae

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈzoʊdiːz niːˈɑtəmiː / (AmE), / ɪkˈsəʊdiːz niːˈɒtəmiː / (BrE)
A species of tick that typically does not bite humans but transmits the Lyme disease spirochete to dusky-footed wood rats.
species of tick affecting wood rats

Examples

The dusky-footed wood rat is often host to the tick Ixodes neotomae.
Unlike some other species, Ixodes neotomae rarely bites humans directly.
Researchers studied how Ixodes neotomae transmits Lyme disease bacteria to local rodent populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ixodes scapularis - species of tick, commonly known as the deer tick, also transmits Lyme disease to humans

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Ixodes' (tick) and species epithet 'neotomae' after the rodent genus Neotoma (wood rats).
