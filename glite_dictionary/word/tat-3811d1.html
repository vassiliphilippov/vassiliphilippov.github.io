tat

1. VERB

/ tæt / (AmE), / tæt / (BrE)
to make lace or decorative edging by knotting or looping thread using a small shuttle
make lace by knotting or looping thread

Examples

She learned to tat delicate edging for her wedding veil.
Watching her grandmother tat lace always fascinated her as a child.
He spent hours tatting intricate patterns for the new tablecloth.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: lace - a delicate fabric made of yarn or thread in an open web-like pattern

Etymology

Origin uncertain; perhaps imitative or from French 'tatting', a kind of lacework.

2. NOUN

/ tæt / (AmE), / tæt / (BrE)
Cheap, tasteless, or vulgar goods or material; items of little value or quality, often considered gaudy or trashy.
cheap or tasteless items
british informal

Examples

The market was filled with tat pretending to be antiques.
She can't resist buying a bit of tat when she travels abroad.
Most of the souvenirs were just cheap tat made for tourists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: junk - useless or unwanted items of little value
  • Synonym: trash - worthless material, especially household refuse
  • Antonym: treasure - something of great value or worth

Etymology

19th-century slang, probably a shortened form of 'tatters' (rags).
