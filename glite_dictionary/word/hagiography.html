hagiography

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæɡiˈɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌhæɡiˈɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
A biography that idealizes its subject, especially one that treats a saint or religious figure with excessive reverence.
idealizing biography, especially of a saint

Examples

The author's hagiography of the leader leaves out any mention of his faults.
Many medieval hagiographies exaggerate the miracles attributed to the saints.
Her speech sounded more like a hagiography than an objective assessment.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: biography - an account of someone's life written by someone else

Etymology

From Greek *hagio-* (holy) + *-graphia* (writing).
