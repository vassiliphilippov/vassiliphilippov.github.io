human chorionic gonadotrophin

1. NOUN

/ ˌhjuːmən kɔːˈriːɒnɪk ˌɡoʊnəˈdoʊtrəˌfin / (AmE), / ˌhjuːmən kɔːˈrɪɒnɪk ˌɡɒnədəˈtrəʊfɪn / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the placenta early in pregnancy, the presence of which in blood or urine forms the basis for some pregnancy tests
a hormone detected in pregnancy tests
medical

Examples

Doctors measure human chorionic gonadotrophin levels to confirm early pregnancy.
A surge in human chorionic gonadotrophin is detected by home pregnancy tests.
High human chorionic gonadotrophin levels can indicate certain medical conditions during pregnancy.

Etymology

From Greek 'chorion' (membrane), 'gonad' (reproductive organ), and 'trophe' (nourishment).
