razz

1. NOUN

/ ræz / (AmE), / ræz / (BrE)
a sound or cry, such as a raspberry, made to express mockery, contempt, or displeasure
mocking or jeering noise

Examples

The angry crowd gave the referee a loud razz after the controversial call.
He blew a razz at his friend to tease him.
A chorus of razzes erupted from the audience when the comedian made a bad joke.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raspberry - a sound made by blowing air between the lips to show derision

Etymology

Shortened form of 'raspberry', from rhyming slang 'raspberry tart' (fart).

2. VERB

/ ræz / (AmE), / ræz / (BrE)
to tease or mock someone in a playful or persistent way
tease or mock persistently
informal

Examples

The boys razzed their friend for arriving late to the party.
She was constantly razzed about her unusual accent in school.
As soon as he wore a suit to work, his colleagues began razzing him.

Synset Examples

The children teased the new teacher
Don't ride me so hard over my failure
His fellow workers razzed him when he wore a jacket and tie

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tease - to make fun of or attempt to provoke in a playful way
  • Synonym: mock - to laugh at or make fun of, especially by imitating

Etymology

Probably a shortening of 'razzberry,' a variant of 'raspberry' in the sense of a noise made to show derision.
