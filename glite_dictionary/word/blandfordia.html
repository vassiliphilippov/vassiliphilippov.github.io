Blandfordia

1. NOUN

/ blændˈfɔːrdiə / (AmE), / blændˈfɔːdɪə / (BrE)
A genus of Australian perennial herbs with tuberous roots, known for their brightly colored, bell-shaped flowers.
genus of Australian perennial herbs

Examples

The bright red flowers of Blandfordia make it a favorite among Australian native plant enthusiasts.
Blandfordia species are commonly called Christmas bells due to their distinctive shape and holiday blooming period.
Researchers traveled to eastern Australia to study the unique habitats of Blandfordia in the wild.

Etymology

Named after George Spencer-Churchill, Marquis of Blandford.
