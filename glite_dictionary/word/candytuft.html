candytuft

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæn.di.tʌft / (AmE), / ˈkæn.dɪ.tʌft / (BrE)
a flowering plant of the genus Iberis, widely cultivated for its clusters of white, pink, or purple flowers, native to the Mediterranean region
garden plant with showy flower clusters

Examples

The bright candytuft bloomed early in the spring, lining the garden path with white and pink flowers.
Gardeners often plant candytuft for its ability to thrive in rock gardens and borders.
A cluster of purple candytuft added a colorful touch to the flowerbed.

Etymology

From 'candy' (perhaps referring to the sweet-like appearance of the flowers) + 'tuft' (cluster).
