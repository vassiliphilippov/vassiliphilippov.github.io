accoutrement

1. NOUN

/ əˈkuːtɚmənt / (AmE), / əˈkuːtrɪmənt / (BrE)
An additional item of dress or equipment, typically worn or carried for a particular activity or purpose.
additional item of clothing or equipment

Examples

The knight's armor was impressive, but his feathered accoutrement drew the most attention.
She added every possible accoutrement to her costume, including jewelry and scarves.
The travelers checked their suitcases for any forgotten accoutrement before heading to the airport.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accessory - an extra item that is worn or used with something for practical or decorative purposes

Etymology

From French 'accoutrement', from 'accoutrer' (to equip).
