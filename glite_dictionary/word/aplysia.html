Aplysia

1. NOUN

/ əˈplɪʒiə / (AmE), / əˈplɪzɪə / (BrE)
A genus of large sea slugs, known as sea hares, belonging to the family Aplysiidae.
a genus of sea hares (marine gastropods)

Examples

The neuroscience experiment used Aplysia to study simple forms of learning.
Researchers observed the defensive response of Aplysia when touched.
Many marine biologists are familiar with the life cycle of Aplysia species.

Etymology

From Greek aplous (simple) + -ia (Latinized suffix).

2. NOUN

/ əˈplɪziə / (AmE), / əˈplɪzɪə / (BrE)
A genus of large sea hares, marine mollusks found in warm and temperate seas, and the type genus of the family Aplysiidae.
genus of large sea hares

Examples

Researchers studied the nervous system of Aplysia to learn about learning and memory.
Aplysia can be found in coastal waters around the world, particularly in warmer regions.
The distinctive purple ink released by Aplysia species helps them evade predators.

Etymology

From New Latin *Aplysia*, derived from Greek *aplysia* (not washed), originally referencing a kind of sea slug.
