bourgeois

1. NOUN

/ bʊrˈʒwɑ / (AmE), / ˈbʊəʒwɑː / (BrE)
a person belonging to the middle class, especially in a capitalist society
member of the middle class

Examples

He regarded himself as a proud bourgeois with solid values.
The revolution was aimed at the wealthy bourgeois who controlled industry.
As a bourgeois, she enjoyed privileges not available to the working class.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: proletarian - a member of the working class

Etymology

From French 'bourgeois', from Old French 'burgeis' (town dweller), from 'bourg' (town).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ bʊrˈʒwɑ / (AmE), / ˈbʊəʒwɑː / (BrE)
belonging to or characteristic of the middle class, typically with reference to its perceived materialistic values or conventional attitudes.
of or relating to the middle class

Examples

Many consider bourgeois lifestyles to be overly concerned with comfort and material possessions.
Her bourgeois upbringing influenced her tastes and preferences throughout her life.
The play criticizes the narrow values often associated with bourgeois families.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: middle-class - related to the social group between the upper and working classes

Etymology

From French 'bourgeois', from Old French 'burgeis' (town-dweller), from 'bourg' (town).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00261561-s
Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ bʊrˈʒwɑ / (AmE), / ˈbʊəʒwɑː / (BrE)
A member of the middle class, especially one who owns property or engages in business or industrial enterprise.
member of the middle class; business owner

Examples

As the city grew, many families joined the bourgeois and opened their own small businesses.
The bourgeois played a significant role in the expansion of the industrial economy.
She married into a wealthy bourgeois family who owned several factories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capitalist - a person who owns or controls capital and invests it in business enterprises
  • Antonym: proletarian - a member of the working class

Etymology

From French 'bourgeois', from Old French 'burgeis' (town dweller), from 'bourg' (town).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09902168-n
Synset POS: n

4. ADJ

/ bʊrˈʒwɑ / (AmE), / ˈbʊəʒwɑː / (BrE)
Conforming to the standards and conventions typical of the middle class, especially with reference to conventional values, materialism, or respectability.
typical of the middle class; conventional

Examples

She rejected his bourgeois values in favor of a more adventurous lifestyle.
The decor was tasteful but rather bourgeois for his eclectic taste.
Critics mocked the play for its bourgeois sentimentality and lack of originality.

Synset Examples

a bourgeois mentality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conventional - based on or in accordance with what is generally done or believed
  • Antonym: bohemian - socially unconventional, especially in an artistic way

Etymology

From French 'bourgeois', from Old French 'burgeis' (town dweller), ultimately from Latin 'burgus' (fortress, castle).

5. ADJ

/ bʊrˈʒwɑ / (AmE), / ˈbʊəʒwɑː / (BrE)
(in Marxist thought) Belonging to or characteristic of the property-owning, capitalist class, considered as exploiting the working class.
relating to property-owning, capitalist class (Marxist sense)

Examples

The revolutionaries denounced the bourgeois attitudes of the wealthy elite.
His tastes were seen as distinctly bourgeois, favoring comfort and security over radical change.
In Marxist theory, bourgeois values are often contrasted with those of the proletariat.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: proletarian - relating to or characteristic of the working class (Marxist sense)

Etymology

From French bourgeois (town dweller, member of the middle class), from Old French burgeis, from bourg (town).
