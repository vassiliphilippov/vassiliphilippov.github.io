claudication

1. NOUN

/ ˌklɔːdɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌklɔːdɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
Difficulty walking due to pain, cramping, or weakness in the legs caused by restricted blood flow or other issues.
difficulty walking due to leg pain
medical

Examples

The patient complained of claudication after walking a short distance.
Intermittent claudication is often a symptom of peripheral artery disease.
She was diagnosed with claudication following a vascular examination.

Etymology

From Latin claudicatio (limping), from claudicare (to limp).
