Sauria

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔːriə / (AmE), / ˈsɔːrɪə / (BrE)
A suborder of reptiles that includes the true lizards, such as chameleons and geckos.
suborder of true lizards

Examples

The class Reptilia includes Sauria, comprising all true lizards.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Sauria suborder in Madagascar.
Chameleons, as members of Sauria, possess unique adaptations for camouflage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lizard - Any of numerous squamate reptiles typically having a long body and tail, movable eyelids, and usually four legs.

Etymology

From New Latin Sauria, from Greek saura (lizard).
