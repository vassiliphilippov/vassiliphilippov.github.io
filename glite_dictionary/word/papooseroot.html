papooseroot

1. NOUN

/ pəˈpusˌrut / (AmE), / pəˈpuːsˌruːt / (BrE)
A tall perennial herb native to eastern North America and Asia, known for its blue berrylike fruit and thick knotty rootstock, formerly used in traditional medicine.
a perennial herb with medicinal rootstock

Examples

Native Americans once used the papooseroot as a remedy for various ailments.
The blue berries of the papooseroot resemble those of some other woodland plants.
Botanists have studied the medicinal properties of papooseroot for decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue cohosh - another common name for the plant Caulophyllum thalictroides

Etymology

From 'papoose' (a Native American word for an infant) and 'root', due to its use as a traditional remedy for women and children.
