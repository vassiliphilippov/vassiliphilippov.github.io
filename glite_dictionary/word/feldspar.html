feldspar

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛldˌspɑr / (AmE), / ˈfɛldˌspɑː / (BrE)
any of a group of hard crystalline minerals consisting of aluminum silicates of potassium, sodium, calcium, or barium, commonly found in igneous, metamorphic, and sedimentary rocks
a group of common rock-forming minerals

Examples

Granite is composed mainly of feldspar, quartz, and mica.
Geologists identified plagioclase feldspar as a key mineral in the rock sample.
Potassium feldspar gives some granites their characteristic pink color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mica - a group of silicate minerals with layered structures
  • Synonym: quartz - a hard, crystalline mineral composed of silicon and oxygen atoms

Etymology

From German Feldspat, from Feld (field) + Spat (spar, a mineral).
