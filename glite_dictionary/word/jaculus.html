Jaculus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒækjʊləs / (AmE), / ˈdʒækjʊləs / (BrE)
A genus of jerboas, small jumping rodents found in deserts and semi-arid regions of northern Africa and Asia.
genus of jerboas (jumping rodents)

Examples

The Jaculus can leap several times its body length to escape predators.
Researchers discovered a new species within the genus Jaculus in North Africa.
Unlike other rodents, the Jaculus uses its long hind legs for hopping rather than running.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jerboa - small jumping desert rodent

Etymology

From Latin 'jaculus', meaning 'darting' or 'throwing', referring to the jumping motion.
