ninepence

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪnˌpɛns / (AmE), / ˈnaɪnpəns / (BrE)
a former British coin worth nine pennies, used especially in the 18th and 19th centuries.
former British coin worth nine pennies
british archaic

Examples

The merchant demanded a ninepence for the goods.
He discovered an old ninepence in a box of coins.
In the eighteenth century, one could buy a meal for a ninepence.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: shilling - a former British coin worth twelve pennies
  • Degree Lower: sixpence - a former British coin worth six pennies

Etymology

From 'nine' + 'pence' (plural of penny), referring to a coin of that value.
