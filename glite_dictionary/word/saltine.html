saltine

1. NOUN

/ sɔlˈtin / (AmE), / sɔːlˈtiːn / (BrE)
a thin, crisp cracker sprinkled with salt before baking, typically square or rectangular in shape
thin, salted cracker

Examples

She ate a saltine with her soup to settle her stomach.
The recipe called for crushed saltines as a crunchy topping.
Kids often snack on saltine crackers with a slice of cheese.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cracker - a dry, thin, crisp biscuit or bread; often eaten as a snack

Etymology

From 'salt', referring to the salted topping, and the diminutive suffix '-ine'.
