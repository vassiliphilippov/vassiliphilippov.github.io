tactile

1. ADJ

/ ˈtæk.təl, ˈtæk.taɪl / (AmE), / ˈtæk.taɪl / (BrE)
relating to or producing a sensation of touch; perceptible by touch
perceptible by touch; touch-related

Examples

The museum offers a tactile exhibit where visitors can handle historical artifacts.
She enjoys the tactile sensation of soft fabrics against her skin.
Braille is a tactile system that allows visually impaired people to read.

Synset Examples

tactile qualities
the tactual luxury of stroking silky human hair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tactual - relating to the sense of touch
  • Synonym: palpable - able to be touched or felt

Etymology

From Latin tactilis (tangible), from tangere (to touch).

2. ADJ

/ ˈtæk.taɪl / (AmE), / ˈtæk.taɪl / (BrE)
relating to or involving the sense of touch.
relating to the sense of touch

Examples

The museum's tactile exhibits allow visitors to experience art through touch.
They developed a tactile map to help visually impaired travelers navigate the station.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

Etymology
