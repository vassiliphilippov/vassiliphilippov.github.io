stag

1. NOUN

/ stæɡ / (AmE), / stæɡ / (BrE)
an adult male deer, especially of the red deer species
adult male deer

Examples

We caught a glimpse of a magnificent stag in the misty forest this morning.
During the rutting season, the stag fiercely defends his territory.
The hunter tracked the stag across the rocky hills for hours.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: doe - an adult female deer
  • Synonym: buck - a male deer, especially of smaller species
  • Antonym: hind - a female red deer

Etymology

From Old English *stagga* (male deer).

3. VERB

/ stæg / (AmE), / stæg / (BrE)
To attend a dance or party without a female companion.
attend an event without a female companion

Examples

John decided to stag the dance since he didn't have a date.
Several students chose to stag the prom rather than skip it entirely.
He was nervous about stagging the party but ended up having a great time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solo - alone; without a companion or partner
  • Antonym: accompanied - to go somewhere with someone; with a companion

Etymology

From the noun 'stag' (male deer), reflecting solo male attendance.
