to smuggle

1. VERB

/ ˈsmʌɡəl / (AmE), / ˈsmʌɡl / (BrE)
to import or export goods secretly and illegally, especially without paying customs duties or complying with prohibitions
illegally import or export goods

Examples

The gang attempted to smuggle rare animals into the country.
She was arrested for trying to smuggle jewelry through customs.
Officials found an attempt to smuggle drugs hidden in the car's tires.

Synset Examples

She smuggled cigarettes across the border

Related Terms

  • Synonym: traffic - deal or trade in something illegally (especially drugs, weapons)
  • Antonym: export - to send goods to another country for sale
  • Antonym: import - to bring goods into a country from abroad for sale

Etymology

From Low German *smuggeln* (to sneak), probably related to Old English *smugan* (to creep, to conceal).
