glamorous

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡlæmərəs / (AmE), / ˈɡlæm(ə)rəs / (BrE)
Having an attractive or exciting quality that makes certain people or things seem especially appealing or charming.
attractive and exciting in an appealing way

Examples

She wore a glamorous dress to the awards ceremony.
The city looks especially glamorous at night with all its lights.
Many people dream of living a glamorous lifestyle full of travel and parties.

Synset Examples

glamorous movie stars

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alluring - very attractive or tempting; enticing
  • Antonym: plain - lacking beauty or special features

Etymology

From 'glamour' + '-ous'. 'Glamour' from Scots, originally meaning 'magic, enchantment', from English grammar (via association with occult learning).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡlæmərəs / (AmE), / ˈɡlæmərəs / (BrE)
having a quality of being attractive, exciting, and full of charm and allure, often in a romantic or stylish way
full of charm and excitement

Examples

She looked absolutely glamorous in her evening gown.
The city has a glamorous nightlife scene, attracting celebrities and tourists alike.
Despite the glamorous image of acting, the profession is often very demanding.

Synset Examples

glamorous movie stars

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alluring - powerfully attractive or charming; seductive
  • Antonym: dull - lacking interest or excitement

Etymology

From glamour + -ous; 'glamour' derives from Scots, alteration of 'grammar' (once meaning occult knowledge).
