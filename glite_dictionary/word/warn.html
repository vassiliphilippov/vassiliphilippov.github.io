to warn

1. VERB

/ wɔrn / (AmE), / wɔːn / (BrE)
to inform someone in advance about a danger, potential harm, or risk
inform of danger or risk

Examples

The weather service warned residents about the approaching storm.
She warned him not to touch the hot stove.
I warn you, the road ahead is very slippery.

Synset Examples

The director warned him that he might be fired
The doctor warned me about the dangers of smoking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caution - to advise someone to be careful or take care
  • Synonym: alert - to warn someone of danger or a problem
  • Antonym: to reassure - to say or do something to remove someone's doubts or fears

Etymology

From Old English 'wearnian' (to take heed, admonish), related to Proto-Germanic roots.

2. VERB

/ wɔrn / (AmE), / wɔːn / (BrE)
to order someone to go away or stay away from a place as a warning, often because they are not welcome or are trespassing
order to stay away or leave

Examples

The landowner warned the hikers to leave his property immediately.
Security warned us not to enter the construction site without permission.
She warned the children off the dangerous frozen lake.

Synset Examples

The old man warned the children off his property

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warn off - to order someone to keep away from a place

Etymology

From Old English *warnian* (to take heed, beware), from Proto-Germanic *warnōną*.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ wɔrn / (AmE), / wɔːn / (BrE)
to advise someone to be careful or to tell them about possible danger or problems
to advise of possible danger or problems

Examples

The teacher warned the students not to leave their bags unattended.
I must warn you that the road can be slippery after rain.
She warned him about the risks of sharing too much online.

Synset Examples

I warned him not to go too far
I warn you against false assumptions
She warned him to be quiet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to advise - to offer counsel or recommendations
  • Synonym: caution - to warn or advise against possible danger
  • Antonym: to encourage - to urge someone forward or support them to act

Etymology

From Old English 'warnian', related to Old High German 'warnōn' (to take heed).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00872241-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ wɔrn / (AmE), / wɔːn / (BrE)
to inform someone about possible danger, trouble, or a negative event, often in advance
inform of possible danger or trouble

Examples

The weather service warned residents about the approaching storm.
She warned him not to touch the hot surface.
I repeatedly warned them of the risks, but they ignored my advice.

Synset Examples

I warned you that I would ask some difficult questions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caution - to advise someone to be careful or to warn against something
  • Synonym: alert - to warn someone about a possible danger so they can prepare
  • Antonym: to reassure - to say or do something to remove someone's doubts or fears

Etymology

From Old English *warnian* (to take heed, guard against), from Proto-Germanic *warnōną*.
