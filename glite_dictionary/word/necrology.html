necrology

1. NOUN

/ nɪˈkrɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / nɪˈkrɒlədʒi / (BrE)
A published notice of a person's death, often with a brief biography or account of their life.
published death notice with brief biography

Examples

The local newspaper published a detailed necrology of the celebrated author.
His achievements were recognized in a touching necrology after his passing.
Researchers found valuable historical information in the university's archived necrology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obituary - a notice of a person's death in a newspaper, often including a brief biography

Etymology

From Greek nekros (dead body, corpse) + -logia (study, account).

2. NOUN

/ nəˈkrɑlədʒi / (AmE), / nɪˈkrɒlədʒi / (BrE)
a list or record of people who have died recently, often published in a periodical or report
list of recent deaths

Examples

The annual report included a necrology of members who had passed away during the year.
After reading the necrology, she realized how many prominent citizens the town had lost.
The church newsletter featured a necrology listing the recently deceased parishioners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obituary - a notice of someone's death, often with a brief biography

Etymology

From Greek nekrologia, from nekros (dead body) and -logia (study, account).
