pollinator

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːləˌneɪtər / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪˌneɪtə / (BrE)
an insect or animal that transfers pollen from one flower to another, enabling fertilization
animal or insect that transfers pollen

Examples

Bees are one of the most important pollinators in many ecosystems.
Some crops depend almost entirely on animal pollinators for successful reproduction.
Butterflies, birds, and bats can also act as pollinators for various plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pollination - transfer of pollen to fertilize plants

Etymology

From pollinate (to fertilize with pollen) + -or (agent noun suffix).
