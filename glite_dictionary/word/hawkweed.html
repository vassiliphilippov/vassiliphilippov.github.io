hawkweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔkˌwid / (AmE), / ˈhɔːkwiːd / (BrE)
any of various often hairy plants of the genus Hieracium, having yellow or orange flowers that resemble dandelions
a plant of the genus Hieracium

Examples

Clusters of hawkweed brightened the roadside with their vivid yellow blooms.
The botanist collected a sample of orange hawkweed for her study on invasive species.
Many gardeners struggle to remove persistent hawkweed from their lawns.

Etymology

From hawk + weed; possibly so called because it was believed hawks ate the plant to improve their eyesight.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɔkˌwid / (AmE), / ˈhɔːkˌwiːd / (BrE)
Any of various perennial plants of the genus Pilosella, with usually yellow or orange flower heads, found chiefly in temperate regions.
a plant of the genus Pilosella

Examples

The field was covered in bright yellow hawkweed during the summer months.
Gardeners often struggle to remove hawkweed because of its invasive nature.
We identified the plant as a type of hawkweed based on the shape of its leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dandelion - a common wild plant with yellow flowers and fluffy seeds

Etymology

From the belief that hawks ate the plant to improve their eyesight, combined with 'weed' for a wild plant.
