Pseudomonas solanacearum

1. NOUN

/ suːdəˈmoʊnəs ˌsoʊləneɪˈserəm / (AmE), / sjuːdəˈməʊnəs ˌsɒlənəˈseərəm / (BrE)
a species of bacterium that causes brown rot in crops such as tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco
bacterium causing brown rot in crops
medical

Examples

Pseudomonas solanacearum is a major threat to potato crops in many countries.
Researchers are studying ways to control Pseudomonas solanacearum infections in tomatoes.
The brown rot caused by Pseudomonas solanacearum can devastate tobacco fields.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; 'Pseudomonas' from Greek 'pseudo-' (false) + 'monas' (unit), 'solanacearum' indicating relation to the Solanaceae plant family.
