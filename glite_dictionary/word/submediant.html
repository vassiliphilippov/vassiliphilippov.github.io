submediant

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˈmiːdiənt / (AmE), / sʌbˈmiːdiənt / (BrE)
(music) the sixth note of a major or minor scale, or the degree a third below the tonic.
sixth note of a musical scale

Examples

In the key of C major, the submediant is the note A.
The composer modulated from the tonic to the submediant to create a warmer sound.
Harmonies built on the submediant chord can add depth to a musical progression.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mediant - the third degree of a musical scale
  • Degree Higher: tonic - the first degree of a musical scale

Etymology

From Latin 'sub' (below) + 'mediant' (the third degree of the scale).
