wheedler

1. NOUN

/ ˈwiːdlər / (AmE), / ˈwiːdlə / (BrE)
a person who tries to persuade others by flattery or gentle urging
one who persuades by flattery or coaxing

Examples

The clever wheedler managed to get a second helping of dessert from the waiter.
She was such a wheedler that her friends often found themselves agreeing to her suggestions without noticing.
Every office has at least one wheedler who charms their way into favorable assignments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coaxer - a person who persuades by gentle urging or flattery
  • Synonym: flatterer - a person who gives excessive compliments for personal gain
  • Antonym: critic - a person who expresses adverse or disapproving comments

Etymology

From 'wheedle', likely of uncertain origin, possibly related to German 'wedeln' (to wag the tail).
