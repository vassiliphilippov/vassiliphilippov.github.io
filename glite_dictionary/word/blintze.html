blintze

1. NOUN

/ ˈblɪnts / (AmE), / ˈblɪnts / (BrE)
A thin pancake, typically of Jewish origin, folded around a filling such as cheese or fruit and then fried or baked.
filled thin pancake, often Jewish

Examples

For breakfast, she served homemade blintzes filled with sweetened ricotta cheese.
The restaurant offers traditional blintzes topped with fresh berries and sour cream.
He learned to make blintzes from his grandmother's family recipe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crepe - a thin pancake, usually French, often with a filling

Etymology

From Yiddish *blintse*, from Russian *blinets* (diminutive of *blin*, pancake).
