cocklebur

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːkəlˌbɜr / (AmE), / ˈkɒkəlˌbɜː / (BrE)
a coarse weed of the genus Xanthium, known for its spiny burrs that adhere to fur and clothing
a weed with spiny burrs

Examples

The pasture was overrun with cockleburs that clung to the sheep’s wool.
Farmers often struggle to control cocklebur populations in their fields.
After our hike, my socks were covered with cockleburs that took ages to remove.

Etymology

From 'cockle' (a weed) and 'bur' (prickly seed case).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑkəlˌbɜr / (AmE), / ˈkɒkəlˌbɜː / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Xanthium, especially Xanthium strumarium, with heart-shaped leaves and prickly burs, found in open woodlands, hedgerows, and rough grassland of Europe and Asia Minor; sometimes cultivated for medicinal and culinary purposes.
a prickly-leaved plant of the genus Xanthium

Examples

The field was overgrown with cocklebur and wild grasses.
Farmers often try to remove cocklebur because its burs cling to animals' fur.
Some traditional remedies use cocklebur in herbal infusions.
