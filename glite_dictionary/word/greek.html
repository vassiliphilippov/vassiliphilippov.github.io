Greek

1. ADJ

/ ɡrik / (AmE), / ɡriːk / (BrE)
of or relating to Greece, its people, or the Greek language
relating to Greece or its people/language

Examples

She wore a beautiful Greek dress to the cultural festival.
The museum features an exhibition on Greek mythology and art.
He is fluent in Greek and often visits Athens.

Synset Examples

Greek mythology
a Grecian robe

Etymology

From Latin Graecus, from Greek Graikos (Greek).

2. NOUN

/ ɡrik / (AmE), / ɡriːk / (BrE)
the language of the Greeks, belonging to the Hellenic branch of the Indo-European language family
language of the Greeks

Examples

He is studying Greek at university as part of his linguistics degree.
The New Testament was originally written in Greek.
She is fluent in both English and Greek.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Modern Greek - the most recent form of the Greek language, spoken in Greece today
  • Degree Higher: Ancient Greek - the form of Greek used during classical antiquity

Etymology

From Latin 'Graecus', from Greek 'Graikos' (Greek).

3. NOUN

/ ɡrik / (AmE), / ɡriːk / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Greece
native or inhabitant of Greece

Examples

My best friend at university is a Greek who moved to London last year.
The Greeks are known for their rich history and culture.
As a proud Greek, she celebrates traditional holidays with her family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Grecian - a native of Greece or relating to Greece (archaic/classical usage)
  • Antonym: foreigner - a person from another country

Etymology

From Latin 'Graecus', from Greek 'Γραικός' (Graikos), the name of a Boeotian tribe.
