Ornithorhynchus

1. NOUN

/ ɔrˌnɪθəˈrɪŋkəs / (AmE), / ɔːˌnɪθəˈrɪŋkəs / (BrE)
the genus of monotreme mammals that includes the platypus, native to Australia
genus of monotreme mammals (platypus)

Examples

The Ornithorhynchus is notable for its duck-billed snout and webbed feet.
Scientists debated the classification of Ornithorhynchus when it was first discovered.
The only living species in the genus Ornithorhynchus is the platypus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: platypus - a semiaquatic egg-laying mammal endemic to eastern Australia

Etymology

From Greek 'ornitho-' (bird) + 'rhynchos' (snout), referring to the bird-like bill.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɔːrnɪθoʊˈrɪŋkəs / (AmE), / ˌɔːnɪθəˈrɪŋkəs / (BrE)
A genus of monotreme mammals that includes the platypus, the only living representative of the family Ornithorhynchidae.
genus of monotreme mammals (platypus)

Examples

The single living species within the genus Ornithorhynchus is commonly known as the platypus.
Researchers studied fossils to trace the evolutionary history of Ornithorhynchus.
Ornithorhynchus is notable for its unique mix of mammalian and reptilian features.

Etymology

From Greek 'ornitho-' (bird) + 'rhynchos' (snout), referring to the bird-like bill of the platypus.
