thaw

1. NOUN

/ θɔ / (AmE), / θɔː / (BrE)
A period of weather when snow and ice melt due to rising temperatures after a freeze.
period when snow and ice melt

Examples

After a long winter, the thaw brought relief to the entire village.
Heavy rains during the thaw often lead to flooding in the lowlands.
The early spring thaw made hiking trails muddy and slippery.

Synset Examples

they welcomed the spring thaw

Related Terms

  • Antonym: freeze - a period of extremely cold weather resulting in ice formation

Etymology

From Old Norse *þeyja* (to thaw), related to Middle English *thawen*.

2. NOUN

/ θɔ / (AmE), / θɔː / (BrE)
A period of warmer weather after a freeze, when snow and ice begin to melt.
period of melting after a freeze

Examples

The river flooded its banks during the thaw.
Many roads became muddy after the early spring thaw.
Wildlife becomes more active once the thaw begins.

Synset Examples

3. NOUN

/ θɔ / (AmE), / θɔː / (BrE)
the process of becoming liquid or soft as a result of warming after being frozen
process of becoming unfrozen by heat

Examples

The sudden thaw in early spring caused the river to flood.
Food left out during a power outage will begin to thaw quickly.
After the long winter, the countryside slowly emerged from thaw.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: freeze - the process of becoming solid due to cold

4. NOUN

/ θɔ / (AmE), / θɔː / (BrE)
a period when previously tense or hostile relations become more relaxed or friendly, especially between countries or groups
relaxation of tension in relations

Examples

Following years of tension, the summit marked a dramatic thaw in diplomatic relations.
There has been a noticeable thaw in the usually adversarial relationship between the two leaders.
The historic agreement signaled a thaw after decades of mistrust.

Synset Examples

the thaw between the United States and Russia has led to increased cooperation in world affairs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detente - the easing of strained relations, especially between countries
  • Antonym: freeze - a period of no progress or heightened tension, especially in relations
