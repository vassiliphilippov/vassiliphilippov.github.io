to ovulate

1. VERB

/ ˈoʊvjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈɒvjʊleɪt / (BrE)
(of a female or female organism) to produce and release one or more eggs from the ovary.
produce and discharge eggs (biology)
medical

Examples

Most women ovulate once in each menstrual cycle.
Female dogs typically ovulate just a few times per year.
Some fertility tests can help determine when a woman is about to ovulate.

Synset Examples

women ovulate about once every month

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to menstruate - to discharge blood and other material from the lining of the uterus

Etymology

From Latin ovulatus, from ovum (egg) + -ate.
