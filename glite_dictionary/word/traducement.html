traducement

1. NOUN

/ trəˈdusmənt / (AmE), / trəˈdjuːsmənt / (BrE)
A false or malicious statement that damages someone's reputation; an act of slander or misrepresentation.
false or malicious accusation; slander

Examples

The politician sued his rival for traducement after unfounded rumors circulated during the campaign.
She refused to let the traducement of her character go unchallenged in court.
Years later, the traducement against him was proven to be completely fabricated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defamation - the act of damaging someone's good reputation
  • Synonym: slander - spoken false or malicious statement about someone
  • Synonym: libel - written or published false statement damaging to a person's reputation

Etymology

From Latin 'traducere' (to lead across, transfer, slander).
