Mara

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɑːrə / (AmE), / ˈmɑːrə / (BrE)
In Hindu mythology, the god of death, regarded as the opposite of Kama.
Hindu god of death

Examples

In Hindu mythology, Mara is often depicted as the god who ends mortal life.
The stories describe Mara as opposing the creative force of desire represented by Kama.
Artists throughout India have portrayed Mara in paintings and sculptures for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Kama - Hindu god of love and desire

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'Māra', meaning 'death' or 'destroyer'.
