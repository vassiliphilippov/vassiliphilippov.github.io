dactylomegaly

1. NOUN

/ ˌdæk.tɪ.loʊˈmɛɡ.ə.li / (AmE), / ˌdæk.tɪ.ləʊˈmɛɡ.ə.li / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by abnormally large fingers or toes.
abnormally large fingers or toes (medical)
medical

Examples

The pediatrician diagnosed the child's condition as dactylomegaly after examining her unusually large toes.
People with dactylomegaly may experience difficulty finding properly fitting gloves or shoes.
Surgical treatment is sometimes considered for severe cases of dactylomegaly to improve function and appearance.

Etymology

From Greek 'daktylos' (finger, toe) + 'megale' (greatness, largeness).
