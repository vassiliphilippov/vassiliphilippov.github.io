belvedere

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛlvəˌdɪr / (AmE), / ˈbɛlvɪdɪə / (BrE)
a structure, such as a gazebo or pavilion, built to provide a beautiful view of the surrounding area
structure for enjoying a view

Examples

The garden's belvedere offers stunning views of the entire valley.
They enjoyed afternoon tea in the shaded belvedere overlooking the lake.
The architect designed a rooftop belvedere to take advantage of the city skyline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gazebo - a small building with open sides in a garden, often used for relaxation

Etymology

From Italian 'belvedere', from 'bel' (beautiful) + 'vedere' (to see).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɛlvəˌdɪr / (AmE), / ˈbɛlvɪdɪə / (BrE)
A densely branched Eurasian plant, whose foliage turns purple-red in autumn.
Eurasian shrub with purple-red autumn foliage

Examples

The belvedere in her garden turned a striking shade of red as autumn approached.
Horticulturists admire the belvedere for its dense branches and seasonal color change.
A row of belvederes provided vibrant color along the park walkway in October.
