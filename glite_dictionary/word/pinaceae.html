Pinaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪˈneɪ.si.i / (AmE), / pɪˈneɪ.si.iː / (BrE)
A family of coniferous trees and shrubs, including pines, firs, spruces, and cedars, distinguished by needle-shaped leaves and woody cones.
family of coniferous trees and shrubs

Examples

Many forests in the Northern Hemisphere are dominated by the Pinaceae family, especially pines and spruces.
The cones of the Pinaceae are key characteristics used for identifying different genera within the family.
Botanists classify firs and cedars under the Pinaceae due to their similar needle-like leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conifer - a tree that produces cones and has needle-like leaves, such as pine or fir

Etymology

From Latin *pīnus* (pine tree) with botanical suffix *-aceae* indicating a plant family.

2. NOUN

/ paɪˈneɪsiːˌiː / (AmE), / pɪˈneɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of coniferous trees and shrubs including pines, firs, spruces, and larches, characterized by needle-like leaves and woody cones
the pine family of coniferous trees

Examples

The Pinaceae family includes some of the most widespread forest trees in the Northern Hemisphere.
Many species within the Pinaceae are commercially valuable for timber and paper production.
Spruce and fir trees both belong to the Pinaceae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pine - any coniferous tree of the genus Pinus

Etymology

From Latin *pinus* (pine tree) + -aceae (a suffix used in botanical family names).
