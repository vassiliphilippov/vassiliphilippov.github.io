Cucumis sativus

1. NOUN

/ kuˈkjuːmɪs səˈtaɪvəs / (AmE), / kjuˈkjuːmɪs səˈtaɪvəs / (BrE)
The scientific name for the cucumber plant, a melon vine cultivated for its edible cylindrical green fruit.
scientific name for the cucumber plant
medical

Examples

Botanists classify the Cucumis sativus as part of the gourd family.
Farmers have been cultivating Cucumis sativus for thousands of years.
The fruits of Cucumis sativus are popularly eaten fresh or pickled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cucumber - a long, green, edible fruit of the Cucumis sativus plant

Etymology

From Latin: Cucumis (cucumber) and sativus (cultivated).
