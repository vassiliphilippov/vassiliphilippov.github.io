wretched

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɛtʃɪd / (AmE), / ˈrɛtʃɪd / (BrE)
of extremely poor quality or in a very bad condition
of poor quality or bad condition

Examples

The refugees lived in wretched conditions during the harsh winter.
She was appalled by the wretched state of the abandoned house.
The company's wretched service led to numerous customer complaints.

Synset Examples

deplorable housing conditions in the inner city
woeful treatment of the accused
woeful errors of judgment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deplorable - deserving strong condemnation; shockingly bad in quality
  • Synonym: woeful - full of woe; very bad or miserable
  • Antonym: excellent - extremely good; of the highest quality

Etymology

From Middle English *wrecched*, from Old English *wrecced* (accursed, miserable).
