Echeneididae

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛkəˈnaɪɪˌdaɪ / (AmE), / ˌɛkɪˈniːɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of fishes, commonly called remoras, characterized by a sucking disk on their head which is used to cling to other fishes and to ships.
family of fishes with a sucking disk (remoras)

Examples

The Echeneididae are known for attaching themselves to sharks using their sucking disks.
Scientists observed a member of the Echeneididae clinging to the underside of a boat.
Unlike most fish, Echeneididae can remain attached to large marine animals for long periods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remora - a fish of the Echeneididae family, known for attaching to larger animals

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek ekhene (remora) and -idae (zoological family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ɛkəˌniːəˈdaɪdiː / (AmE), / ɛkɪˌniːɪˈdɪːdiː / (BrE)
a family of fishes characterized by a sucking disk on the head, used for attaching to other fishes and to ships
a family of fishes with sucking disks on their heads

Examples

The Echeneididae are known for their remarkable adaptation of a sucking disk on their heads.
Many members of the Echeneididae family attach themselves to larger marine animals like sharks or whales.
Scientists study the Echeneididae to better understand their unique symbiotic relationships.

Etymology

From scientific Latin Echeneididae, from the type genus Echeneis.
