Geum virginianum

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒi.əm vɜːrˌdʒɪn.iˈæn.əm / (AmE), / ˌdʒiː.əm vɜːˌdʒɪn.iˈæn.əm / (BrE)
A species of avens native to Virginia, having pale or greenish yellow flowers.
a species of avens with pale yellow flowers

Examples

The woodlands of Virginia are home to the delicate Geum virginianum.
Many gardeners appreciate Geum virginianum for its subtle, pale yellow blooms.
Geum virginianum prefers moist soil and partially shaded environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: avens - a plant of the genus Geum, with typically yellow or red flowers

Etymology

From Latin: genus Geum + specific epithet 'virginianum', meaning 'of Virginia'.
