Siphonophora

1. NOUN

/ saɪˌfɒnəˈfɔrə / (AmE), / sɪˌfɒnəˈfɔːrə / (BrE)
an order of marine colonial hydrozoans, including organisms like the Portuguese man-of-war, composed of specialized, physically connected individuals (zooids) functioning together as a single organism
order of marine colonial hydrozoans

Examples

Some of the most spectacular open-ocean animals are the Siphonophora, which include the Portuguese man-of-war.
Scientists studied several Siphonophora colonies to better understand their complex organization.
The Siphonophora display remarkable cooperation between their different zooids.

Etymology

From Greek *siphōn* (tube, pipe) and *phoros* (bearing).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪfəˈnɑfərə / (AmE), / ˌsɪfənəˈfɔːrə / (BrE)
an order of marine colonial hydrozoan animals, many of which form colonies made up of specialized polyps and medusoids, including the Portuguese man-of-war
order of marine colonial hydrozoans

Examples

The Portuguese man-of-war is perhaps the best known member of the Siphonophora.
Researchers discovered a new colony belonging to the Siphonophora order deep in the ocean.
Many Siphonophora possess stinging cells used to capture prey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrozoan - small predatory animals of the class Hydrozoa, related to jellyfish

Etymology

From Greek siphōn (tube, pipe) and -phora (bearing).
