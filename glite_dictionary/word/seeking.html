seeking

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsiːkɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of searching for or trying to obtain something
the act of searching for something

Examples

Her seeking of happiness led her to travel the world.
The company is involved in seeking new business opportunities.
Many people are constantly seeking answers to life's big questions.

Synset Examples

a quest for diamonds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: searching - the act of looking for something
  • Synonym: quest - a long or arduous search
  • Antonym: finding - the act of discovering or obtaining something

Etymology

From the verb 'seek', which comes from Old English *sēcan* (to seek, look for).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsiːkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsiːkɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of trying to discover, obtain, or achieve something
attempt to discover or obtain

Examples

After years of seeking, she finally found the answer she was looking for.
His seeking of the truth led him to travel all over the world.
Job seeking can be a long and sometimes frustrating process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: searching - the act of looking for something carefully
  • Synonym: quest - a long search for something difficult to find
  • Antonym: avoidance - the act of avoiding or keeping away from something

Etymology

From the verb 'seek' + -ing, forming a noun meaning the act of seeking.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00790023-n
Synset POS: n
