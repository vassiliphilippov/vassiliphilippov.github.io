Hesperus

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɛspərəs / (AmE), / ˈhɛspərəs / (BrE)
The planet Venus as seen in the evening sky, especially at or after sunset.
Venus when visible in the evening

Examples

In ancient poetry, Hesperus is often invoked as a symbol of approaching night.
The sky was clear, and Hesperus shone brightly above the horizon.
Many cultures identified Hesperus with deities of love and beauty.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Phosphorus - Venus as seen in the morning sky, the morning star

Etymology

From Greek 'Hesperos' (evening), name of the evening star in mythology.
