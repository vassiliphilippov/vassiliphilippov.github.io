Phascolarctos

1. NOUN

/ ˌfæs.kəˈlɑːrk.təs / (AmE), / ˌfaskəˈlɑːktɒs / (BrE)
A genus of marsupials that includes the koala, native to Australia.
genus of marsupial including the koala

Examples

The genus Phascolarctos consists of only one living species, the koala.
Scientists classify the koala as Phascolarctos cinereus based on anatomical and genetic evidence.
Extinct members of the genus Phascolarctos are known from the fossil record in Australia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: koala - an arboreal Australian marsupial in the genus Phascolarctos

Etymology

From Greek phaskolos (pouch) + arktos (bear).
