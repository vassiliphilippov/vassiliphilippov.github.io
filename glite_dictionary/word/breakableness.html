breakableness

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪk.ə.bəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈbreɪk.ə.bəl.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being easy to break under pressure or force
quality of being easy to break

Examples

The breakableness of the glass made it unsuitable for use in children's playrooms.
Scientists tested the breakableness of the new material to ensure its safety.
She worried about the breakableness of the porcelain figurines during the move.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fragility - the quality of being easily broken or damaged
  • Antonym: durability - the ability to withstand wear, pressure, or damage

Etymology

From 'breakable' + '-ness', forming a noun indicating the state or quality.
