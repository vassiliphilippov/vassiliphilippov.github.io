holographic

1. ADJ

/ ˌhɑːləˈɡræfɪk / (AmE), / ˌhɒləˈɡræfɪk / (BrE)
(of a document) written entirely by the hand of its author.
written in the author's own hand
legal

Examples

The court recognized the validity of the holographic will because it was entirely handwritten.
She discovered a holographic letter among her grandfather's old documents.
In some states, a holographic document does not require witnesses to be considered legally binding.

Synset Examples

holographic document

Etymology

From Late Latin holographus, from Greek holos ('whole') + graphein ('write').

WordNet ID: oewn-03012786-a

2. ADJ

/ ˌhoʊ.ləˈɡræf.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌhɒl.əˈɡræf.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to or using holography or holograms; involving the creation or display of three-dimensional images using laser light
relating to holography or holograms

Examples

The museum featured a holographic display of ancient artifacts that appeared three-dimensional to visitors.
Her presentation included a holographic projection of the solar system, fascinating the audience.
Holographic ticketing, using holographic images for authentication, is becoming more widespread in event security.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hologram - a three-dimensional image formed by the interference of light beams from a laser or other coherent light source
  • Synonym: holography - the science and practice of making holograms

Etymology

From Greek holos (whole) and -graph (writing), patterned after holography.

3. ADJ

/ ˌhoʊ.ləˈɡræf.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌhɒl.əˈɡræf.ɪk / (BrE)
(of a document, especially a will) written entirely in the handwriting of the person whose signature it bears.
written by the signer's own hand (esp. legal)
legal

Examples

The court accepted the holographic will because it was entirely in his own handwriting.
A holographic letter is considered more personal than a typed one.
Some states do not recognize holographic wills unless they meet strict requirements.

Synset Examples

a holographic will

Related Terms

  • Antonym: typed - written using a typewriter or computer, not by hand
  • Synonym: manuscript - a document written by hand, especially before printing

Etymology

From Greek 'hólos' (whole) + '-graphos' (written).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02293214-s
Synset POS: s
