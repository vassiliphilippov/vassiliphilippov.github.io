Hypopachus

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpəˈpækəs / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəˈpækəs / (BrE)
A genus of small burrowing frogs known as sheep frogs, native to the Americas.
genus of burrowing sheep frogs

Examples

The Hypopachus genus includes several species of burrowing sheep frogs found in Central America.
Researchers observed the calling behavior of Hypopachus during the rainy season.
Unlike many frogs, Hypopachus can survive in drier environments due to its burrowing habits.

Etymology

From Greek hypo- (under) and pachys (thick), used as a scientific genus name.

A genus of small burrowing frogs known as sheep frogs, native to Central and North America.
a genus of burrowing sheep frogs

Examples

The Hypopachus genus is easily recognizable by its distinctive call during the rainy season.
Many researchers study Hypopachus to learn more about burrowing amphibian behaviors.
A Hypopachus was spotted in the wetlands after a heavy rainfall.

Etymology

From Greek hypo- (under) + pachys (thick), referencing features of the frog's body.
