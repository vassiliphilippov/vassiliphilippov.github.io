debasement

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈbeɪsmənt / (AmE), / dɪˈbeɪsmənt / (BrE)
the act or process of reducing the quality, value, or purity of something by adding inferior or extraneous material; the product resulting from such adulteration
the act or result of lowering quality by adulteration

Examples

The debasement of the coinage made it less valuable to traders.
Food safety laws were created to prevent the debasement of essential products like milk and flour.
After the scandal, critics accused the company of allowing the debasement of its once-respected brand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adulteration - the act of making something poorer in quality by adding another substance
  • Antonym: purification - the process of making something pure by removing contaminants

Etymology

From 'debase' + '-ment'; 'debase' from Old French 'debaser', from 'de-' (down) + 'base' (low, mean).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈbeɪsmənt / (AmE), / dɪˈbeɪsmənt / (BrE)
the act or process of reducing someone or something to a lower, less respected, or less valuable state
the act of lowering in quality or status

Examples

The debasement of the currency led to economic instability in the country.
Many critics lament the debasement of cultural values in modern society.
His constant lies resulted in the debasement of his reputation among his peers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: degradation - the process of being brought to a lower or less respected state
  • Antonym: uplift - the act of raising to a higher or more respected state

Etymology

From 'debase' + '-ment', originally from Old French 'de-'' (down) and 'bas' (low)
