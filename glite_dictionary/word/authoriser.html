authoriser

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːθəˌraɪzər / (AmE), / ˈɔːθəˌraɪzə / (BrE)
a person who has the power to authorize people or actions
person with authority to approve actions

Examples

Only the authoriser can grant you access to the restricted area.
The contract was not valid without the signature of the authoriser.
Please consult your department’s authoriser before proceeding with expenses.

Synset Examples

the agents report back to their authorizers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authorizer - alternative spelling of 'authoriser', mainly US English
  • Synonym: approver - a person who formally agrees to or accepts something
  • Antonym: refuser - a person who denies authorization or approval

Etymology

From 'authorize' (from Old French autoriser, from Medieval Latin auctorizare) + agentive suffix '-er'.
