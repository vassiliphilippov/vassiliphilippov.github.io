Leucothoe fontanesiana

1. NOUN

/ ˌluːkəˈθoʊ.i fɒnˌtænˈziː.ə.nə / (AmE), / ˌljuːkəˈθəʊ.i fɒnˌtænˈziː.ənə / (BrE)
a fast-growing evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States, with arching interlaced branches and racemes of white flowers
evergreen shrub of southeastern US

Examples

Gardeners often choose Leucothoe fontanesiana for shady borders in woodland gardens.
The delicate white flowers of Leucothoe fontanesiana bloom in late spring.
Landscapers appreciate Leucothoe fontanesiana for its attractive arching branches and evergreen foliage.

Etymology

Leucothoe from Greek mythology; fontanesiana named after French botanist René Louiche Desfontaines.
