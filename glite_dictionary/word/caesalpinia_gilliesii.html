Caesalpinia gilliesii

1. NOUN

/ ˌsiːzəlˈpɪniə ɡɪlˈiːziːaɪ / (AmE), / ˌsiːzælˈpɪniə ɡɪlˈiːziːaɪ / (BrE)
A tropical shrub with bright orange or red flowers, often cultivated for ornamental purposes; sometimes classified in the genus Poinciana.
a tropical ornamental shrub with red or orange flowers

Examples

The Caesalpinia gilliesii in our garden blooms beautifully every summer.
Botanists often refer to Caesalpinia gilliesii as the bird of paradise bush.
The striking flowers of Caesalpinia gilliesii attract many butterflies and hummingbirds.

Etymology

Named after the botanist George Gillies; genus named for Italian botanist Andrea Caesalpini.
