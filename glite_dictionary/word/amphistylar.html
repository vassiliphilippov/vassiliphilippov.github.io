amphistylar

1. ADJ

/ æmˈfɪstəˌlɑr / (AmE), / æmˈfɪstɪlə / (BrE)
(of a building, especially a temple) having columns at both ends or at both sides.
having columns at both ends/sides (architecture)

Examples

The ancient temple's amphistylar design allowed views from both entrances.
Several Hellenistic buildings employed the amphistylar layout for aesthetic balance.
The architect proposed an amphistylar structure inspired by classical Greek temples.

Etymology

From Greek 'amphi-' (on both sides) + 'stylos' (column).

2. ADJ

/ ˌæm.fɪˈstaɪ.lər / (AmE), / ˌam.fɪˈstaɪ.lə / (BrE)
(of a building or structure) having free-standing columns in porticoes at both ends or at both sides.
having columned porticoes at both ends or sides

Examples

The ancient temple is amphistylar, with impressive columns gracing both its eastern and western facades.
Architectural plans show the building as amphistylar, creating a grand entrance on either side.
The amphistylar design was popular in classical Greek architecture for its symmetry and elegance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peristylar - having a colonnade surrounding a court or building

Etymology

From Greek 'amphi-' (on both sides) + 'stylos' (column).
