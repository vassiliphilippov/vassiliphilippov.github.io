gobbler

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑblɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡɒblə / (BrE)
a male turkey
male turkey

Examples

Every autumn, hunters head into the woods hoping to spot a large gobbler.
The gobbler strutted around the farmyard, showing off its colorful feathers.
During mating season, the call of the gobbler can be heard echoing through the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tom - a male turkey

Etymology

From 'gobble', the call of a turkey, plus '-er'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑblər / (AmE), / ˈɡɒblə / (BrE)
A person who eats quickly and greedily, often swallowing large mouthfuls at a time.
someone who eats quickly and greedily
informal

Examples

He's such a gobbler that his plate is always empty first.
The teacher warned the students not to be gobblers during lunch and to chew their food properly.
Watching her brother eat like a gobbler made her lose her appetite.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glutton - a person who eats and drinks excessively or voraciously
  • Antonym: nibbler - a person who eats small amounts slowly

Etymology

From the verb 'gobble' (to eat quickly), plus the agent noun suffix '-er'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10153802-n
Synset POS: n
