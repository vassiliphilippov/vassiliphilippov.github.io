endospore

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛndoʊˌspɔr / (AmE), / ˈɛndəʊˌspɔː / (BrE)
a tough, dormant, asexual spore formed within the cells of certain bacteria and algae, capable of surviving harsh conditions
dormant spore inside bacteria or algae
medical

Examples

Some bacteria produce an endospore to survive extreme environmental conditions.
The scientist observed the formation of an endospore under the microscope.
When nutrients are scarce, certain species develop an endospore for protection.

Etymology

From endo- (inside) + spore, from Greek 'endon' (within) and 'spora' (seed).
