coprophagia

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑː.prəˈfeɪ.dʒi.ə / (AmE), / ˌkɒ.prəˈfeɪ.dʒi.ə / (BrE)
the act of eating feces, observed in some animals and, rarely, as a symptom in humans with certain psychiatric disorders
the eating of feces
medical

Examples

Coprophagia is a behavior commonly observed in some species of dogs and rabbits.
The veterinarian explained that coprophagia in pets can be caused by dietary deficiencies.
In certain psychiatric conditions, coprophagia may occur as an unusual symptom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coprophagy - the eating of feces (alternate spelling/variant)

Etymology

From Greek 'kopros' (dung) + 'phagein' (to eat).
