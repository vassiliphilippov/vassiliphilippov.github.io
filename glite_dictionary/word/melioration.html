melioration

1. NOUN

/ ˌmiːliəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmiːliəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of making something better; improvement or amelioration, especially of social, environmental, or living conditions
the act of making something better; improvement

Examples

Recent policy changes are aimed at the melioration of public health across the region.
The melioration of soil quality was essential for successful farming in the area.
Efforts at social melioration have led to improved living standards among disadvantaged groups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amelioration - the act of making something better; improvement
  • Antonym: deterioration - the process of becoming worse

Etymology

From Late Latin melioratio(n-), from Latin meliorare (to improve), from melior (better).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmiːliəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmiːliəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a word or expression develops a more positive or elevated meaning over time
improvement of a word's meaning over time

Examples

One example of melioration is how ‘knight’ changed from meaning ‘servant’ to ‘noble warrior’.
The word ‘nice’ underwent melioration and now expresses approval rather than foolishness.
In historical linguistics, melioration is contrasted with pejoration, where meanings become more negative.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pejoration - the process by which a word develops a more negative meaning over time

Etymology

From Latin 'melioratio' (improvement), from 'melior' (better).
