to reduplicate

1. VERB

/ ˌriːˈduː.plɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈdjuː.plɪ.keɪt / (BrE)
to repeat or do something again, especially in a way that closely copies or doubles the original
repeat or copy again

Examples

In linguistics, some words reduplicate a syllable to form new meanings.
The scientist tried to reduplicate the experiment to confirm her findings.
Echoes reduplicate the original sound in the empty hall.

Synset Examples

He could never replicate his brilliant performance of the magic trick

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duplicate - to make an exact copy of something
  • Synonym: to replicate - to make a copy of; to repeat

Etymology

From Latin 'reduplicare', from 're-' (again) + 'duplicare' (to double).

2. VERB

/ rɪˈduːplɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈdjuːplɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to repeat or double a sound, syllable, or part of a word, especially in linguistic forms
repeat a sound or part of a word

Examples

In some languages, verbs reduplicate to indicate repeated actions.
The word 'bye-bye' is created when you reduplicate the word 'bye'.
Linguists often study how certain prefixes or syllables reduplicate to convey new meanings.

Synset Examples

The consonant reduplicates after a short vowel
The morpheme can be reduplicated to emphasize the meaning of the word

Etymology

From Latin 'reduplicatus', past participle of 'reduplicare' (to double), from 're-' (again) + 'duplicare' (to double).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
