nonallele

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɑnəˈliːl / (AmE), / ˌnɒnəˈliːl / (BrE)
a gene that is located at a different locus from another gene and is not an allele of it
a gene at a different locus; not an allele

Examples

Two nonalleles may interact to affect a phenotype, even though they occupy different loci.
A mutation in one nonallele can sometimes compensate for a defect in another gene.
Researchers distinguish between alleles and nonalleles when mapping genetic traits.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: allele - one of two or more alternative forms of a gene at a given locus

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'non-' meaning 'not' and 'allele', from Greek 'allelon' meaning 'of each other'.
