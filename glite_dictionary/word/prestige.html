prestige

1. NOUN

/ prɛˈstiʒ / (AmE), / prɛˈstiːʒ / (BrE)
the respect and admiration that someone or something receives because of their success, influence, or wealth
respect or admiration from success or status

Examples

Winning the award gave the scientist great prestige in her field.
The company enjoys considerable prestige among tech enthusiasts.
He accepted the position mainly for the prestige it brought, not for the salary.

Synset Examples

he wanted to achieve power and prestige

Related Terms

  • Synonym: status - social or professional position; rank or standing
  • Synonym: honor - high respect or esteem
  • Antonym: obscurity - the state of being unknown or unimportant

Etymology

From French 'prestige' (illusion, magic, later 'prestige, prestige'), from Latin 'praestigiae' (illusions, conjuring tricks).
