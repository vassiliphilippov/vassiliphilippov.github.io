caimito

1. NOUN

/ kaɪˈmiːtoʊ / (AmE), / kaɪˈmiːtəʊ / (BrE)
An evergreen tree native to the West Indies and Central America, with edible purple fruit that is star-shaped in cross-section and leaves with golden, silky undersides.
West Indian/central American tree with star-shaped fruit

Examples

The caimito tree produces a sweet fruit enjoyed throughout the Caribbean.
He picked a ripe caimito and admired its star-shaped cross section.
Locals often eat fresh caimito or use it in traditional desserts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: star apple - Another name for the fruit of the caimito tree

Etymology

From Spanish 'caimito', probably from a local indigenous Caribbean language.
