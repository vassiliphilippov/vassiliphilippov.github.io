symptom

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪmptəm / (AmE), / ˈsɪmptəm / (BrE)
A physical or mental feature that is regarded as indicating a condition of disease, particularly one experienced by the patient.
an indication of disease or disorder
medical

Examples

If you develop a cough or fever, these could be a symptom of infection.
She reported experiencing severe headaches as her primary symptom.
Unusual fatigue can be an early symptom of many illnesses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sign - an objective indication of a medical condition observed by others

Etymology

From Greek symptōma (a happening, accident), from sympiptein (to befall).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪmptəm / (AmE), / ˈsɪmptəm / (BrE)
a sign or indication of the existence of something, especially an undesirable situation or condition
sign indicating something's existence

Examples

Increased anxiety is often a symptom of chronic stress.
The company's declining sales were seen as a symptom of larger economic problems.
Fever can be a symptom of many different illnesses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sign - an indication that something exists or is happening
  • Antonym: cause - the reason something happens, as distinct from a sign

Etymology

From Greek symptōma (occurrence, that which befalls), from sympinein (to happen).
