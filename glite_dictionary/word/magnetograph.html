magnetograph

1. NOUN

/ mæɡˈnɛtəˌɡræf / (AmE), / mæɡˈniːtəɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a scientific instrument used to record and measure magnetic variations, especially in the Earth's magnetic field
instrument that measures magnetic variations

Examples

The observatory uses a magnetograph to monitor changes in the Earth's magnetic field.
Data from the magnetograph helped scientists understand the effects of solar storms.
A solar magnetograph is essential for studying sunspot activity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnetometer - instrument that measures the strength and direction of magnetic fields

Etymology

From magneto- (referring to magnetism) + -graph (something that writes or records).
