Oenothera

1. NOUN

/ iːˈnɒθərə / (AmE), / iːˈnɒθərə / (BrE)
A genus of chiefly North American herbs, commonly known as evening primroses, with usually nocturnal flowers.
a genus of evening primroses

Examples

The Oenothera blossoms open at dusk and attract night-flying pollinators.
Gardeners often plant Oenothera for its showy yellow flowers that bloom in the evening.
Researchers studied the genetic diversity within several species of Oenothera.

Etymology

From New Latin Oenothera, the genus name introduced by Linnaeus.

2. NOUN

/ iːˈnɑːθərə / (AmE), / iːˈnɒθərə / (BrE)
A genus of chiefly North American herbs, commonly known as evening primroses, that typically have flowers opening at night.
a genus of evening primrose plants

Examples

The Oenothera genus is admired for its large, showy flowers that bloom at night.
Botanists have identified over a hundred species within the Oenothera group.
Gardeners often plant Oenothera for its ability to attract nocturnal pollinators like moths.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evening primrose - a common name for plants in the genus Oenothera

Etymology

From New Latin Oenothera, the genus name used in scientific classification.
