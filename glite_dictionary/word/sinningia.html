Sinningia

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈnɪndʒiə / (AmE), / sɪˈnɪndʒiə / (BrE)
a genus of perennial tuberous herbs and shrubs native to Central and South America, including several popular ornamental plants
genus of tuberous herbs and shrubs

Examples

The florist recommended a Sinningia hybrid for its beautiful, bell-shaped flowers.
Many collectors prize the unusual foliage and blossoms of the Sinningia species.
Several Sinningia varieties thrive in indoor gardens with moderate sunlight.

Etymology

Named after German horticulturist Wilhelm Sinning (1792–1874).
