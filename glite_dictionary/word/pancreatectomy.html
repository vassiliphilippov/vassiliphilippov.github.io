pancreatectomy

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæŋkriəˈtɛktəmi / (AmE), / ˌpaŋkriːəˈtɛktəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure to remove part or all of the pancreas
removal of the pancreas (surgical)
medical

Examples

The patient required a pancreatectomy due to the presence of a malignant tumor.
After undergoing a pancreatectomy, she was closely monitored in the intensive care unit.
A partial pancreatectomy may be performed to treat chronic pancreatitis.

Etymology

From pancreas + -ectomy, the latter from Greek ektemnein (to cut out).
