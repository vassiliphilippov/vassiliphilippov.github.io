ACLANT

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ ˌsiː ˈlænt / (AmE), / ˈeɪ ˌsiː ˈlænt / (BrE)
A major strategic headquarters of NATO, situated in the United States, under the authority of the North Atlantic Council.
NATO strategic headquarters in the U.S.

Examples

The NATO command known as ACLANT played a crucial role during the Cold War.
After the restructuring, ACLANT's responsibilities were transferred to a new command center.
Military historians often reference ACLANT when discussing NATO's Atlantic defense strategy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: NATO - North Atlantic Treaty Organization, a military alliance.

Etymology

Abbreviation of Allied Command Atlantic.
