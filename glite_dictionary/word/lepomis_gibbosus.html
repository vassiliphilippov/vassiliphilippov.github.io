Lepomis gibbosus

1. NOUN

/ lɛˈpoʊmɪs ɡɪˈboʊsəs / (AmE), / lɛˈpoʊmɪs ɡɪˈbəʊsəs / (BrE)
A small, vividly colored freshwater sunfish native to North America, also known as the pumpkinseed.
species of North American sunfish

Examples

The brightly patterned Lepomis gibbosus is a common sight in shallow North American lakes.
Anglers often catch Lepomis gibbosus while fishing for larger species.
Scientists study the adaptability of Lepomis gibbosus in non-native habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pumpkinseed - another name for Lepomis gibbosus, a small sunfish

Etymology

From Greek 'lepis' (scale) and 'omos' (shoulder), plus Latin 'gibbosus' (humped).
