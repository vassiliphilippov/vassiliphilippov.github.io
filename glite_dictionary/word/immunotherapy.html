immunotherapy

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌmjunəˈθɛrəpi / (AmE), / ɪˌmjuːnəʊˈθɛrəpi / (BrE)
the treatment of disease by stimulating or enhancing the body's immune response
therapy using the immune system
medical

Examples

The patient was prescribed immunotherapy to help combat the cancer.
Recent advances in immunotherapy have improved the survival rates for several diseases.
She experienced fewer side effects with immunotherapy compared to traditional chemotherapy.

Etymology

From 'immune' (from Latin 'immunis', exempt, free) + 'therapy' (from Greek 'therapeia', healing).
