axis

1. NOUN

/ ˈæksɪs / (AmE), / ˈæksɪs / (BrE)
the second cervical vertebra, which forms a pivot that allows the head to turn from side to side
second cervical vertebra (neck bone)
medical

Examples

The axis plays a crucial role in supporting the head's rotational movement.
A fracture of the axis can result in serious spinal cord injury.
Doctors examined the alignment of both the atlas and axis after the accident.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: atlas - the first cervical vertebra that supports the skull

Etymology

From Latin axis (axle, pivot, axis).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæk.sɪs / (AmE), / ˈæk.sɪs / (BrE)
the main stem or central part of a plant, around which organs such as leaves or branches are arranged
central stem or part of a plant

Examples

The flower cluster grows along the axis of the plant, giving it a distinctive shape.
In many trees, branches radiate from a central axis to form a symmetrical structure.
The researchers studied how leaves are attached at specific points along the axis of the stem.

Etymology

From Latin axis (axle, pivot, axis).

3. NOUN

/ ˈæksɪs / (AmE), / ˈaksɪs / (BrE)
a group of countries that are allied, especially for military or political purposes, often used in reference to the Axis powers in the Second World War
group of allied countries (esp. in war)

Examples

During World War II, Germany, Italy, and Japan formed the axis against the Allies.
The new alliance was seen as a potential axis threatening global security.
Historians still debate the strategies adopted by the axis during the conflict.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alliance - a union or association of countries or organizations for mutual benefit
  • Antonym: enemy - countries or groups in opposition, especially at war

Etymology

From Latin axis (axle, pivot). The political sense derives from the Axis powers of World War II (Germany, Italy, and Japan).

4. NOUN

/ ˈæksɪs / (AmE), / ˈæksɪs / (BrE)
an imaginary or real line around which an object rotates or is symmetrically arranged
center line of rotation

Examples

The Earth rotates around its axis once every twenty-four hours.
A spinning top wobbles as its axis tilts.
The wheel was turning smoothly on its central axis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: axle - rod or spindle for rotating wheels or gears

Etymology

From Latin axis (axle, pivot, axis).

5. NOUN

/ ˈæksɪs / (AmE), / ˈaksɪs / (BrE)
A straight line around which a body or geometric figure rotates, or which divides a body into symmetrical parts.
straight line for rotation or symmetry

Examples

A planet spins on its axis as it orbits the sun.
The graph has an x-axis and a y-axis to plot data points.
The sculpture balances perfectly on its central axis.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: center - the middle point or part of something

Etymology

From Latin axis (axle, pivot, axis).
