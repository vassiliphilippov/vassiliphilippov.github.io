vicissitude

1. NOUN

/ vɪˈsɪs.əˌtud / (AmE), / vɪˈsɪs.ɪ.tjuːd / (BrE)
a change, especially an unexpected or unwelcome one, in circumstances or fortune at different times in life or in the course of something
change in circumstances or fortune

Examples

The family endured many vicissitudes over the years, but always managed to stay together.
Explorers must be prepared for the vicissitudes of nature and fortune during their journeys.
She faced the vicissitudes of the real estate market with remarkable composure.

Synset Examples

the project was subject to the usual vicissitudes of exploratory research

Related Terms

  • Synonym: change - the act or process through which something becomes different
  • Antonym: stability - the state of being unchanged or consistent

Etymology

From Latin 'vicissitudo' (change, alternation), from 'vicis' (change, alternation).

2. NOUN

/ vɪˈsɪsɪˌtud / (AmE), / vɪˈsɪsɪˌtjuːd / (BrE)
a change of circumstances or fortune, typically one that is unwelcome or unpleasant; the quality or state of being changeable; alternation between opposite or contrasting things.
a change or alternation in circumstance

Examples

He faced the vicissitudes of a long and difficult career with remarkable resilience.
Families must sometimes endure the vicissitudes of economic hardship and sudden fortune.
The novel explores the vicissitude of human existence, moving from happiness to tragedy and back again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: change - the act or instance of making or becoming different
  • Antonym: stability - the state of being stable or unchanging

Etymology

From Latin vicissitudo (change, alternation), from vicis (turn, change).
