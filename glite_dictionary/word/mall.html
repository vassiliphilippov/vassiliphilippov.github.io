mall

1. NOUN

/ mɑl / (AmE), / mɔːl / (BrE)
a public area, often a wide walk or promenade, set aside for pedestrians
public pedestrian promenade

Examples

The city redesigned the downtown area to include a central mall for pedestrians.
Street performers and artists regularly gather along the waterfront mall.
We enjoyed a pleasant evening stroll through the tree-lined mall in the park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boulevard - a wide street in a city, often with trees along the sides
  • Synonym: walkway - a passage or path for walking, especially outdoors

Etymology

From Middle English 'malle' (a former game played on alleys), later applied to walkways where the game was played.

2. NOUN

/ mɑl / (AmE), / mɔːl / (BrE)
A large indoor shopping center with various stores, restaurants, and common areas, often with parking facilities.
large indoor shopping center

Examples

The city built a new mall with dozens of stores, a food court, and a cinema.
Teenagers often gather at the mall on weekends to shop and socialize.
During the holiday season, the mall is decorated and filled with shoppers.

Synset Examples

a good plaza should have a movie house
they spent their weekends at the local malls

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shopping center - a complex of shops and stores in one location
  • Degree Lower: marketplace - an open place or building for buying and selling goods

Etymology

Shortened from 'shopping mall,' originating in the mid 20th century; originally from 'The Mall,' a promenade in London.
