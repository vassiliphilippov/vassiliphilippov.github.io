abandon

1. NOUN

/ əˈbændən / (AmE), / əˈbændən / (BrE)
a lack of restraint or inhibition; carefree freedom or impulsiveness
lack of restraint; carefree impulsiveness

Examples

She danced with abandon, not caring what anyone thought.
He laughed with such abandon that everyone else joined in.
The children ran through the fields with abandon, enjoying their freedom.

Synset Examples

she danced with abandon

Related Terms

  • Antonym: restraint - self-control or moderation

Etymology

From Old French abandoner (to surrender), from Latin ad (to) + bandon (order, decree).

Synset POS: n

/ əˈbændən / (AmE), / əˈbændən / (BrE)

Examples
