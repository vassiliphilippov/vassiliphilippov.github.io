toecap

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊˌkæp / (AmE), / ˈtəʊˌkæp / (BrE)
a protective or decorative covering for the toe of a boot or shoe, typically made of leather, steel, or another material
protective or decorative shoe covering for the toe

Examples

His boots had a steel toecap to protect his feet from injury.
The toecap on the right shoe was decorated with intricate stitching.
She scuffed the toecap of her shoes while walking on the rough pavement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toe box - the part of a shoe that surrounds and protects the toes

Etymology

Formed from 'toe' + 'cap'.
