Boletus pulcherrimus

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈliːtəs pʊlˈkɛrɪməs / (AmE), / bəʊˈliːtəs pʊlˈkɛrɪməs / (BrE)
a species of poisonous bolete mushroom with a brown cap, scarlet pores, and a thick netted stalk, found in North America
a poisonous bolete mushroom species

Examples

Foragers should be cautious of the Boletus pulcherrimus, as it is a beautiful but poisonous mushroom.
The thick, reticulate stalk is a key identifying feature of Boletus pulcherrimus.
In mycology books, Boletus pulcherrimus is often photographed for its striking coloration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bolete - a type of mushroom with pores instead of gills

Etymology

From Latin: 'boletus' (mushroom) and 'pulcherrimus' (most beautiful).
