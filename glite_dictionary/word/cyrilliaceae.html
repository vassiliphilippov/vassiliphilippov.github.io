Cyrilliaceae

1. NOUN

/ sɪˌrɪliˈeɪsi.iː / (AmE), / sɪˌrɪlɪˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of shrubs and trees with leathery leaves and small white flowers in racemes, including the genera Cyrilla and Cliftonia
a family of shrubs and trees (Cyrilla, Cliftonia)

Examples

The family Cyrilliaceae comprises shrubs and trees native to the Americas.
Botanists recently revised the classification of Cyrilliaceae based on new genetic evidence.
Members of the Cyrilliaceae often have glossy, leathery leaves and small white flowers.

Etymology

From the genus name Cyrilla, with the standard botanical suffix -aceae for plant families.
