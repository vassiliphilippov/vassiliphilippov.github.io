Opisthocomidae

1. NOUN

/ oʊˌpɪs.θəˈkɑː.mɪ.diː / (AmE), / əʊˌpɪs.θəˈkɒ.mɪ.diː / (BrE)
the biological family comprising the hoatzins, a group of tropical birds found in South America
family of tropical birds (hoatzins)

Examples

The Opisthocomidae are notable for being the only bird family with foregut fermentation.
Members of the Opisthocomidae family are native to the Amazon and Orinoco basins.
Unlike most birds, the Opisthocomidae possess a specialized digestive system to process leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hoatzin - a tropical South American bird with a blue face and spiky crest, the only member of its family

Etymology

From New Latin Opisthocomidae, from Greek opisthen (behind) + kome (hair), referring to the bird's crest.
