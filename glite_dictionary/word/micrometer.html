micrometer

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈkrɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / maɪˈkrɒmɪtə / (BrE)
an instrument used for measuring extremely small distances or thicknesses with great accuracy, especially in mechanical engineering and machining
precision instrument for small measurements

Examples

The technician used a micrometer to check the thickness of the metal sheet.
Precision engineers often rely on a micrometer for accurate measurements during manufacturing.
He calibrated the micrometer before measuring the diameter of the wire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caliper - instrument for measuring dimensions or distance between two points

Etymology

From Greek mikros (small) + metron (measure).

2. NOUN

/ maɪˈkrɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / maɪˈkrɒmɪtə / (BrE)
a metric unit of length equal to one millionth of a meter (0.000001 m)
one millionth of a meter

Examples

A bacterium typically measures about one micrometer in length.
The tool can detect particles as small as a few micrometers.
Fibers with diameters less than one micrometer are used in specialized filters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: micron - another name for a micrometer as a unit of length
  • Degree Higher: millimeter - a metric unit of length equal to one thousandth of a meter

Etymology

From Greek 'mikros' (small) and 'metron' (measure).
