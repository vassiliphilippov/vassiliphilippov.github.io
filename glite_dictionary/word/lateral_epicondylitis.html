lateral epicondylitis

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæt̬.ər.əl ˌɛp.ɪˌkɒn.dəˈlaɪ.tɪs / (AmE), / ˈlat.ər.əl ˌɛp.ɪˌkɒn.dɪˈlaɪ.tɪs / (BrE)
Painful inflammation of the tendon at the outer border of the elbow, usually caused by overuse of the lower arm muscles.
painful elbow tendon inflammation
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her persistent elbow pain as lateral epicondylitis after a physical examination.
Years of playing tennis made him susceptible to developing lateral epicondylitis.
Physical therapy is often recommended to treat lateral epicondylitis and relieve discomfort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tennis elbow - common name for lateral epicondylitis

Etymology

From Latin 'lateralis' (side), Greek 'epi-' (upon) and 'kondylos' (knuckle), with the medical suffix '-itis' meaning inflammation.
