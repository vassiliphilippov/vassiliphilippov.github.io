physiologically

1. ADV

/ ˌfɪziəˈlɑdʒɪkli / (AmE), / ˌfɪziəˈlɒdʒɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to the functions and processes of living organisms or their parts.
in relation to body processes

Examples

After months of training, her body became physiologically adapted to high altitudes.
Some people are physiologically predisposed to caffeine sensitivity.
The frog is physiologically capable of surviving extreme dehydration.

Synset Examples

physiologically ready
physiologically addicted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biologically - in a way that relates to biology or living things

Etymology

From 'physiology' + '-ally', with 'physiology' from Greek 'physis' (nature) + 'logia' (study of).
