shiok

1. ADJ

/ ʃiˈɔk / (AmE), / ʃiˈɒk / (BrE)
(Singapore/Malaysia slang) Extremely delicious or very enjoyable; used to express great pleasure or satisfaction.
very delicious or enjoyable (slang, Singapore/Malaysia)
slang

Examples

The spicy noodles were so shiok that I ordered a second bowl.
Winning the game after weeks of practice felt absolutely shiok.
That ice cream is really shiok on a hot day.

Synset Examples

The food at that restaurant is absolutely shiok!
After a long day, a bubble bath is so shiok.

Etymology

From Hokkien or Malay, originally meaning 'good' or 'pleasing'.

2. ADJ

/ ʃiˈɒk / (AmE), / ʃiːˈɒk / (BrE)
(in Singapore and Malaysia) Extremely pleasurable or delicious; giving a strong feeling of satisfaction or delight.
extremely pleasurable; highly satisfying (Singapore/Malaysia usage)
informal

Examples

After a long run in the heat, an ice-cold drink just shiok the whole body.
The spicy noodles were so shiok I couldn't stop eating them.
She stretched out on the beach and said, 'This is really shiok!'

Synset Examples

Winning the game felt shiok, a real thrill!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delicious - highly pleasing to the senses, especially taste
  • Synonym: pleasurable - giving pleasure; enjoyable
  • Antonym: disgusting - causing revulsion or strong dislike

Etymology

From Malay "syok" (pleased, satisfied), influenced by English "shock" in sound.
