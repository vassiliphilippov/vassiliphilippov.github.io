efflux

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛˌflʌks / (AmE), / ˈɛflʌks / (BrE)
the process of flowing out or the action of a substance moving outward from a particular place
the process of flowing out

Examples

Sodium ions experience efflux from the cell during this stage of the process.
The efflux of water from the reservoir caused the levels to drop sharply.
Environmental regulations monitor the efflux of chemicals into nearby rivers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: influx - the process of flowing in

Etymology

From Latin 'effluxus', past participle of 'effluere' (to flow out), from 'ex-' (out) + 'fluere' (to flow).
