breath

1. NOUN

/ brɛθ / (AmE), / brɛθ / (BrE)
a slight movement of air; a very small amount or trace of wind
slight movement of air

Examples

Not a breath stirred among the trees that night.
The room was so stuffy, there wasn't a breath of air coming through the window.
Early in the morning, a faint breath of wind rippled across the lake.

Synset Examples

there wasn't a breath of air in the room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whiff - a slight gust or trace of something carried in the air
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-11452376-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ brɛθ / (AmE), / brɛθ / (BrE)
the air taken into or expelled from the lungs in a single act of breathing
air taken in or expelled during breathing

Examples

He took a deep breath before giving his speech.
After running up the stairs, she paused to catch her breath.
Her breath was visible in the icy morning air.

Synset Examples

he took a deep breath and dived into the pool
he was fighting to his last breath

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to breathe - to take air into the lungs and expel it

Etymology

From Old English 'bræth' (odor, exhalation, vapor).

/ brɛθ / (AmE), / brɛθ / (BrE)

4. NOUN

/ brɛθ / (AmE), / brɛθ / (BrE)
a brief period of rest or pause from activity, allowing time to recover or regroup
a brief pause or respite

Examples

After running up the stairs, she needed a breath before continuing the conversation.
The meeting was intense, but the coffee break gave everyone a much-needed breath.
He worked tirelessly for hours without so much as a breath between tasks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pause - a temporary stop in activity
  • Synonym: break - a short period of rest from work

Etymology

From Old English 'bræth' (odor, scent, breath), related to Old Norse 'broethr' (smell, breath).

Examples
