onward

1. ADV

/ ˈɑːnwərd / (AmE), / ˈɒnwəd / (BrE)
Continuing forward in time, order, or degree; from a specified point and after.
forward from a point in time or order

Examples

The museum is open from 10 a.m. onward every day.
From that point onward, the company expanded rapidly.
Many traditions have continued from the 18th century onward.

Synset Examples

from that time forth
from the sixth century onward

Etymology

From Middle English onward, from Old English 'onweard' (toward), from 'on' (on) + '-weard' (toward).

/ ˈɑnwɚd / (AmE), / ˈɒnwəd / (BrE)
in a forward direction; continuing ahead or further

Examples

The research moves onward, breaking new ground every year.
