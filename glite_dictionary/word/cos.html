cos

1. NOUN

/ kɑz / (AmE), / kɒz / (BrE)
the ratio of the length of the adjacent side to the length of the hypotenuse in a right-angled triangle; the cosine function
trigonometric ratio; cosine

Examples

The value of cos 60° is 0.5.
You can calculate the length of the side using the cos function.
In trigonometry class, we learned how to graph the cos curve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cosine - trigonometric function: ratio of adjacent to hypotenuse
  • Antonym: sin - trigonometric ratio: opposite side to hypotenuse

Etymology

Short for 'cosine', from Latin 'com-' (together) and 'sinus' (curve, sine).

2. NOUN

/ kɑs / (AmE), / kɒs / (BrE)
A variety of lettuce with long, crisp, dark-green leaves and an elongated, loosely packed head, also known as romaine.
variety of lettuce with long, dark-green leaves

Examples

I prefer using cos in Caesar salads for its crunchy texture.
The market had a fresh supply of organic cos today.
We grew cos in our garden this year alongside other leafy greens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: romaine - another name for cos lettuce

Etymology

From Cos, the Greek island where this type of lettuce was believed to have originated.
