hedgehog

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdʒˌhɑɡ / (AmE), / ˈhɛdʒˌhɒɡ / (BrE)
a small nocturnal mammal with a coat of stiff, sharp spines and a pointed snout, found in Europe, Asia, and Africa
small spiny nocturnal mammal

Examples

A hedgehog curled into a tight ball, protecting itself with its spines.
We spotted a hedgehog in the garden last night just after sunset.
The children were excited to see a hedgehog crossing the road.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porcupine - a large rodent with long, sharp spines, native to America, Africa, and southern Asia

Etymology

Old English 'hegehoge', from 'hege' (hedge) + 'hoge' (hog).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdʒˌhɑɡ / (AmE), / ˈhɛdʒˌhɒɡ / (BrE)
A small nocturnal mammal with a spiny coat and short legs, known for rolling itself into a ball for defense.
small spiny mammal

Examples

We saw a hedgehog scurrying across the garden at dusk.
The hedgehog curled itself into a tight ball when it sensed danger.
Children are often fascinated by the prickly spines of a hedgehog.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porcupine - A large rodent with long, sharp quills

Etymology

From Old English 'hege-' (hedge) + 'hog' (hog), so called because it frequents hedgerows and has a snout like a pig.
