Sicilia

1. PROPN

/ siˈtʃiliɑ / (AmE), / siˈtʃiːliə / (BrE)
the Italian name for Sicily, the largest island in the Mediterranean Sea and an autonomous region of Italy
the Italian name for Sicily (Mediterranean island)

Examples

The cuisine of Sicilia is famous for its rich flavors and fresh ingredients.
Many tourists visit Sicilia to explore its ancient ruins and beautiful coastline.
Mount Etna, Europe's most active volcano, is located in Sicilia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sicily - largest island in the Mediterranean, autonomous region of Italy

Etymology

Italian form of 'Sicily', from Latin 'Sicilia'.

2. PROPN

/ siˈtʃiːliə / (AmE), / siˈtʃiːliə / (BrE)
the Italian name for Sicily, a region and the largest island of Italy in the Mediterranean Sea
Italian name for Sicily (region/island)

Examples

Many authentic wines from Sicilia have gained international recognition in recent years.
The cuisine of Sicilia is famous for its rich flavors and diverse influences.
Her grandparents emigrated from Sicilia to the United States in the early twentieth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sicily - the English name for the region and island known in Italian as Sicilia

Etymology

Italian, from Latin *Sicilia*.
