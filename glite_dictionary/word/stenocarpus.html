Stenocarpus

1. NOUN

/ ˌstɛnoʊˈkɑːrpəs / (AmE), / ˌstiːnəˈkɑːpəs / (BrE)
A genus of timber trees native to Australia and Malaysia, some species of which are valued for their wood and ornamental use.
genus of timber trees (Australia to Malaysia)

Examples

The Stenocarpus is renowned for its attractive foliage and timber quality.
Botanists discovered a new species within the Stenocarpus genus in Western Australia.
Several Stenocarpus trees have been planted in the botanical garden for research.

Etymology

From Greek 'stenos' (narrow) and 'karpos' (fruit), referring to the narrow fruit of these trees.
