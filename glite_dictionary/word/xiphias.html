Xiphias

1. NOUN

/ ˈzɪfiəs / (AmE), / ˈzɪfɪas / (BrE)
A genus of large, predatory marine fish in the family Xiphiidae, consisting of the swordfish.
genus including the swordfish

Examples

The genus Xiphias contains only one species, the swordfish.
Researchers studied migratory patterns of Xiphias in the Atlantic Ocean.
Fossil evidence reveals that Xiphias has existed for millions of years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swordfish - a large marine fish with a long, flat bill, member of the genus Xiphias

Etymology

From Greek xiphias (swordfish), from xiphos (sword).

2. NOUN

/ ˈzɪfiˌæs / (AmE), / ˈzɪfiːəs / (BrE)
A genus of large, predatory fish known as swordfish, constituting the only member of the family Xiphiidae.
genus of swordfish

Examples

The genus Xiphias includes the well-known swordfish found in tropical and temperate oceans.
In taxonomic classification, Xiphias is the only genus in the family Xiphiidae.
Scientists studied the evolutionary history of Xiphias to better understand billfish diversity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swordfish - a large, predatory fish with a long, sword-like bill

Etymology

From Greek xiphias, meaning 'swordfish', from xiphos (sword).
