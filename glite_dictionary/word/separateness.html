separateness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛpɚrətnɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɛp(ə)rətnɪs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being separate or distinct from others
state of being separate or distinct

Examples

The separateness between the two communities was evident in their cultural practices.
He valued the separateness of his work and personal life.
Modern technology can make the separateness of individuals less pronounced.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: separation - the act or process of separating or the state of being separated

Etymology

Formed from 'separate' + noun suffix '-ness'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɛpərɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈsɛpərətnɪs / (BrE)
the state of being politically independent or separate from other entities
political independence or separation

Examples

They valued their country's separateness and resisted foreign influence.
Debates over Scottish separateness have intensified in recent years.
Some regions have campaigned for greater separateness from the central government.

Synset Examples

seeking complete political separateness for Taiwan

Related Terms

  • Synonym: independence - freedom from control by another country or authority
  • Antonym: union - the act of being united or joined, especially politically

Etymology

From separate + -ness; 'separate' from Latin separatus, past participle of separare, meaning 'to put apart'.
