semiopaque

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛmi.oʊˈpeɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɛmi.əʊˈpeɪk / (BrE)
Allowing only some light to pass through; partially opaque.
partially opaque

Examples

The semiopaque curtains let in a gentle glow of morning light.
She poured the liquid into a semiopaque plastic container for storage.
Mist rose from the lake, creating a semiopaque veil over the water.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: opaque - not letting light pass through; not transparent or translucent
  • Degree Lower: translucent - allowing light, but not detailed shapes, to pass through; semi-transparent

Etymology

From semi- (meaning 'half' or 'partly') + opaque, from Latin opacus (shaded, dark).
