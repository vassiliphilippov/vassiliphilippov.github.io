utricle

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuːtrɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈjuːtrɪkəl / (BrE)
A small sac or pouch in the inner ear into which the semicircular canals open, playing a role in balance.
small pouch in the inner ear
medical

Examples

The utricle detects linear accelerations and head tilts in the vestibular system.
Damage to the utricle can cause problems with a person's sense of balance.
In anatomy class, we studied the structure of the utricle and its function in the inner ear.

Etymology

From Latin utriculus (a small bag or pouch), diminutive of uter (leather bag).
