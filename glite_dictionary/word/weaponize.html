to weaponize

1. VERB

/ ˈwɛpəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈwɛpənaɪz / (BrE)
to adapt or use something as a weapon or for military purposes
to convert for use as a weapon

Examples

Some countries fear their rivals may weaponize new scientific discoveries.
Social media platforms can be weaponized to spread misinformation during elections.
The regime was accused of weaponizing food supplies as a means of control.

Synset Examples

Will modern physicists weaponize String Theory?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to disarm - to take weapons away or remove the military potential
  • Synonym: to militarize - to give a military character to something

Etymology

Formed from 'weapon' + '-ize', first recorded in the 20th century.
