allegoricalness

1. NOUN

/ əˌlɛɡəˈrɪkəlnəs / (AmE), / əˌlɛɡəˈrɪkəlnəs / (BrE)
The state or quality of being allegorical; the characteristic of expressing something through allegory.
the state of being allegorical

Examples

The allegoricalness of the film allows viewers to interpret its message in many ways.
Some readers appreciate the allegoricalness in classic works of literature.
The critic noted the subtle allegoricalness that permeates the entire novel.

Synset Examples

Without intending to, Slocum wrote an allegory of human aloneness, and the allegoricalness of his book has made it one of the classic personal records.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allegory - a story, poem, or picture with a hidden meaning

Etymology

From 'allegorical' (from Medieval Latin *allegoricus*, from Greek *allēgorikos*) + suffix '-ness'.
