obfuscation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑb.fəˈskeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɒb.fʌˈskeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of making something unclear or difficult to see or understand; the process of making something obscure
the act of making something unclear or difficult to see

Examples

The technical jargon served only as obfuscation, hiding the true meaning of the contract.
Deliberate obfuscation of data can make analysis difficult for outsiders.
Mist and shadows led to the obfuscation of the path through the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obscuration - the act of making something unclear or less visible
  • Antonym: clarification - the act of making something clear or easier to understand

Etymology

From Latin obfuscatio (darkening), from obfuscare (to darken).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑb.fəˈskeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɒb.fjuˈskeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of making something unclear, confusing, or difficult to understand; a state of confusion or obscurity caused by a lack of clarity
making unclear or confusing

Examples

The contract was filled with legal obfuscation that made it hard to understand.
Political speeches are sometimes criticized for their deliberate obfuscation of the facts.
Technical documents should avoid obfuscation and use plain language whenever possible.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clarification - the act of making something clearer or easier to understand
  • Synonym: confusion - the state of being bewildered or unclear

Etymology

From Latin obfuscatio, from obfuscare meaning 'to darken'.

3. NOUN

/ ˌɑːbfəˈskeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɒbfʌˈskeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making something unclear or difficult to understand, often deliberately
deliberate act of making unclear

Examples

The company's financial statement was filled with obfuscation to hide its true earnings.
Her explanation only added to the obfuscation and failed to clarify the situation.
Political speeches sometimes employ obfuscation to avoid answering direct questions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clarification - the act of making something clear or easier to understand
  • Synonym: confusion - the state of being unclear or hard to understand

Etymology

From Late Latin obfuscatio (a darkening), from obfuscare (to darken).
