Caspian

1. PROPN

/ ˈkæspiən / (AmE), / ˈkaspɪən / (BrE)
the largest inland body of water in the world, a saltwater lake located between southeastern Europe and western Asia, bordered by countries including Iran and Russia
largest inland sea between Iran and Russia

Examples

The Caspian is famous for its caviar-producing sturgeon.
Oil reserves under the Caspian have made the region economically significant.
Scientists have studied the declining water levels in the Caspian for environmental reasons.

Etymology

From Latin 'Caspium', referring to the Caspians, an ancient people living near the sea's southwestern shores.
