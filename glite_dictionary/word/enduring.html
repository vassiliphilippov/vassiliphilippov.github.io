enduring

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈdʊrɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈdjʊərɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person or quality) patient and able to bear continual wrongs, difficulties, or trouble without complaint.
patiently bearing continual wrongs or trouble

Examples

She was remarkably enduring despite the ongoing challenges in her life.
His enduring patience through years of hardship inspired everyone around him.
An enduring spirit is often admired in stories of survival and resilience.

Synset Examples

an enduring disposition
a long-suffering and uncomplaining wife

Related Terms

  • Synonym: long-suffering - showing patient endurance of pain or trouble

Etymology

From Latin 'indurare' meaning 'to harden, make lasting'.

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈdʊrɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈdjʊərɪŋ / (BrE)
lasting for a long time; continuing without fading or being lost
lasting for a long time

Examples

She found enduring happiness in her work as a teacher.
Their friendship is an enduring bond that has lasted for decades.
The novel explores the enduring power of hope in difficult times.

Synset Examples

an abiding belief
imperishable truths

Related Terms

  • Synonym: permanent - lasting or intended to last indefinitely
  • Antonym: temporary - lasting for only a limited period of time; not permanent
  • Synonym: abiding - continuing for a long time; enduring

Etymology

Present participle of 'endure', from Latin 'indurare' meaning 'to harden'.
