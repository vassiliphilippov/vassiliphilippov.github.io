geomancer

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiː.oʊˌmæn.sər / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.əˌmæn.sə / (BrE)
a person who practices geomancy, a form of divination by interpreting markings on the ground or patterns formed by tossed handfuls of soil, sand, or stones
person who practices geomancy

Examples

The village geomancer was often consulted before any important construction project began.
As a skilled geomancer, she claimed to read the patterns in the sand to foresee the future.
Legends say that an ancient geomancer once saved the city by interpreting the cracks in the earth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fortune-teller - a person who predicts the future, especially using mystical or supernatural means
  • Synonym: diviner - a person who practices divination

Etymology

From geomancy, which derives from Medieval Latin 'geomantia', from Greek 'gē' (earth) + 'manteia' (divination).
