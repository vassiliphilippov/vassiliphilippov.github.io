thurifer

1. NOUN

/ ˈθʊrɪfər / (AmE), / ˈθʊərɪfə / (BrE)
a person, typically an acolyte, who carries and swings a thurible (censer) during religious ceremonies.
acolyte carrying a censer

Examples

The thurifer led the procession down the nave, swinging the censer gently.
During High Mass, the thurifer filled the church with fragrant incense.
A new thurifer was trained to assist in the upcoming Easter service.

Etymology

From Latin 'thurifer', from 'thus, thuris' meaning 'incense' and '-fer' meaning 'bearing'.
