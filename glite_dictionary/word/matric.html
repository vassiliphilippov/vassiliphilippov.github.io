matric

1. NOUN

/ məˈtrɪk / (AmE), / məˈtrɪk / (BrE)
admission to a group, especially the formal process of being enrolled in a college or university.
formal enrollment in a college or university

Examples

After receiving her acceptance letter, she looked forward to her matric at the university.
All incoming students must complete their matric before attending classes.
His matric marked a significant milestone in his academic journey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: matriculation - the formal process of enrolling at a college or university

Etymology

Abbreviated from 'matriculation', from Medieval Latin 'matriculatio', the act of enrolling.
