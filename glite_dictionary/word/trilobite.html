trilobite

1. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪ.ləˌbaɪt / (AmE), / ˈtrɪl.ə.baɪt / (BrE)
an extinct marine arthropod with a three-lobed, three-segmented form, common in the Paleozoic era
extinct marine arthropod of Paleozoic era

Examples

Fossils of the trilobite provide important clues about early marine life.
The trilobite flourished in Earth's oceans over 250 million years ago.
She admired the detailed exoskeleton of the trilobite fossils in the museum.

Etymology

From Greek 'tri-' (three) + 'lobos' (lobe), referring to its three-part body structure.
