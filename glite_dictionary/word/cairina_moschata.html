Cairina moschata

1. NOUN

/ kaɪˈriːnə mɒsˈkeɪtə / (AmE), / kaɪˈriːnə mɒsˈkɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of large, crested duck native to Central and South America, widely domesticated and known as the Muscovy duck.
species of large, crested duck (Muscovy duck)

Examples

The Cairina moschata is renowned for its quiet demeanor compared to other domestic ducks.
Many farmers raise Cairina moschata for their flavorful meat and eggs.
Wild populations of Cairina moschata can still be found in South American wetlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Muscovy duck - common name for Cairina moschata, a large duck species

Etymology

From Latin: genus 'Cairina' and species 'moschata' (meaning 'musk-scented').
