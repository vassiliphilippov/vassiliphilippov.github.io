Sartre

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɑːr trə / (AmE), / ˈsɑː trə / (BrE)
(1905–1980) French writer and existentialist philosopher, renowned for works such as 'Being and Nothingness' and his influence on existentialism.
French existentialist philosopher and writer

Examples

Sartre's writings played a pivotal role in shaping modern existentialist thought.
Many university courses discuss the impact of Sartre on twentieth-century philosophy.
Sartre refused the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1964.

Etymology

French surname, notably borne by Jean-Paul Sartre.
