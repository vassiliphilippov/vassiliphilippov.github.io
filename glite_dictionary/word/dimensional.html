dimensional

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈmɛnʃənəl / (AmE), / dɪˈmɛnʃənəl / (BrE)
having qualities or characteristics that give something depth, character, or substance, especially referring to the presence of distinct features or aspects
having depth or distinct qualities; multi-faceted

Examples

The novel's protagonist is a truly dimensional character, with both virtues and flaws.
Her dimensional approach to problem-solving considers both emotional and logical factors.
Unlike his earlier, more dimensional roles, this performance felt flat and unconvincing.

Synset Examples

never matures as a dimensional character: he is pasty, bland, faceless

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: one-dimensional - lacking depth or complexity; simple or superficial
  • Degree Higher: multi-dimensional - having many aspects or features; complex

Etymology

From Latin 'dimensio' (measuring), via French 'dimension'.

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈmɛnʃənəl / (AmE), / dɪˈmɛnʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to or having dimensions, or involving measurement in one or more directions
relating to or involving dimensions

Examples

The artist created a stunning three-dimensional sculpture for the exhibition.
This problem requires a careful dimensional analysis to check the units.
Modern televisions often feature high-dimensional graphics for a more realistic viewing experience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dimensionality - the number of dimensions something has
  • Antonym: dimensionless - not having, or not defined in terms of, dimensions

Etymology

From dimension + -al, indicating 'pertaining to dimension'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02996534-a
Synset POS: a
