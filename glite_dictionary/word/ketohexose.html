ketohexose

1. NOUN

/ kitoʊˈhɛksoʊs / (AmE), / kiːtəʊˈhɛksəʊz / (BrE)
A monosaccharide that contains six carbon atoms and a ketone group.
six-carbon sugar with a ketone group
medical

Examples

Fructose is a well-known example of a ketohexose found in many fruits.
Scientists studied the metabolic pathway of the ketohexose to understand how it is processed in the body.
The chemical structure of a ketohexose differs from that of an aldohexose due to the presence of a ketone group.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: aldose - a monosaccharide containing an aldehyde group
  • Synonym: hexose - a monosaccharide with six carbon atoms

Etymology

From 'keto-' (relating to the ketone group) and 'hexose' (a six-carbon sugar), both from Greek roots.
