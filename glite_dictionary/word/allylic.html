allylic

1. ADJ

/ əˈlɪlɪk / (AmE), / əˈlɪlɪk / (BrE)
relating to or containing the allyl group, especially in reference to a position or structure adjacent to an alkene in organic compounds.
relating to the allyl group in chemistry
medical

Examples

The allylic position is highly reactive under these reaction conditions.
Chemists studied the allylic rearrangement to understand the mechanism of the transformation.
An allylic halide is more reactive than its saturated counterpart.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allyl - a univalent group derived from propylene (C3H5−)

Etymology

From 'allyl' (from Latin 'allium', meaning 'garlic') + '-ic' (forming adjectives).
