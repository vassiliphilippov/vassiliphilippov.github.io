hairball

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛrˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈheəˌbɔːl / (BrE)
a compact mass of hair that forms in the stomach or digestive tract, especially in animals as a result of licking their fur
clump of hair in an animal's stomach

Examples

The cat coughed up a hairball on the living room carpet this morning.
Frequent hairball formation can indicate an issue with your pet’s grooming habits.
Veterinarians recommend a special diet to help prevent hairball problems in long-haired cats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trichobezoar - a mass found trapped in the gastrointestinal system composed of hair

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛrˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈheə.bɔːl / (BrE)
a compact mass of hair that forms in the stomach or intestines, especially of animals such as cats, as a result of ingesting hair while grooming
mass of hair in an animal's stomach

Examples

The cat coughed up a hairball on the living room carpet.
Veterinarians recommend special diets to help prevent hairballs in long-haired cats.
A hairball can sometimes cause serious digestive problems if it grows too large.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bezoar - a mass found trapped in the gastrointestinal system, sometimes of hair
