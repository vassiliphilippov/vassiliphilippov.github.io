pitch-dark

1. ADJ

/ pɪtʃ dɑrk / (AmE), / pɪtʃ dɑːk / (BrE)
Completely or extremely dark; having no light at all.
completely dark

Examples

Without any windows, the basement was pitch-dark at night.
It felt pitch-dark as we walked through the tunnel without a flashlight.
The room became pitch-dark when the power went out during the storm.

Synset Examples

a black moonless night
through the pitch-black woods
it was pitch-dark in the cellar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitch-black - completely black or dark
  • Degree Lower: dark - lacking or having very little light
  • Degree Higher: black - completely black in color or darkness

Etymology

From 'pitch' (a dark, sticky substance) + 'dark', suggesting total darkness like that of pitch.
