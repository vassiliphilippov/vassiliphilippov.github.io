basilican

1. ADJ

/ bəˈsɪlɪkən / (AmE), / bəˈsɪlɪkən / (BrE)
of or relating to a basilica, or resembling the architectural style of a basilica.
of or relating to a basilica

Examples

The cathedral's basilican design features a long nave flanked by aisles and a high vaulted ceiling.
Many early Christian churches in Rome were built in the basilican style.
The basilican plan became influential in Western architecture for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basilica - large, important church building or ancient Roman public hall

Etymology

From 'basilica', which is from Latin basilica (royal hall), from Greek basilikē (royal), plus the English adjective-forming suffix '-an'.
