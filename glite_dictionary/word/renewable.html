renewable

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈnuəbəl / (AmE), / rɪˈnjuːəbl / (BrE)
able to be renewed, extended, or continued for a new period of time.
able to be renewed or extended

Examples

The library offers renewable memberships that can be extended every year.
Employees sign renewable contracts for fixed terms of two years.
Their agreement comes with a renewable clause, allowing for future extensions.

Synset Examples

a renewable lease
renewable subscriptions

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonrenewable - not able to be renewed or extended

Etymology

Formed from 'renew' + '-able', from Latin 'renovare' (to make new again).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈnuːəbl̩ / (AmE), / rɪˈnjuːəbl̩ / (BrE)
(of a resource or contract) capable of being replaced or replenished naturally or through repeated processes; able to be extended or continued.
able to be renewed, replaced, or extended

Examples

The government is investing in renewable energy sources like wind and solar power.
Your subscription is renewable every year unless you decide to cancel.
Thanks to strict conservation efforts, the forest has become a renewable resource.

Synset Examples

renewable energy such as solar energy is theoretically inexhaustible

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonrenewable - not able to be replaced or replenished once used
  • Synonym: replaceable - able to be replaced

Etymology

From 'renew' + '-able', patterned after 'renew' (Middle English, from Old French 'renouveler', from Late Latin 'renovellare').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00929061-s
Synset POS: s
