Urginea maritima

1. NOUN

/ ɜːrˈdʒɪniə məˈrɪtɪmə / (AmE), / ɜːˈdʒɪnɪə məˈrɪtɪmə / (BrE)
a Mediterranean plant with dense spikes of small white flowers, known for its large bulb with medicinal properties
Mediterranean plant with medicinal bulb

Examples

The bulbs of Urginea maritima have long been used in traditional medicine.
Botanists studied the habitat preferences of Urginea maritima along the Mediterranean coast.
She recognized Urginea maritima by its tall stalk and white flowers.

Etymology

From Latin *Urginea* (the genus), and Latin *maritima* (of the sea), referring to its coastal habitat.
