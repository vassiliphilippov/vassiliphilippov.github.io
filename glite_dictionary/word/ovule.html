ovule

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ.vjul / (AmE), / ˈɒv.juːl / (BrE)
a small structure in seed plants that contains the female germ cell and, after fertilization, develops into a seed
structure in seed plants containing the female germ cell

Examples

The ovule of a flowering plant becomes a seed after fertilization.
Each ovule contains a single egg cell ready for pollination.
Scientists examined the structure of the ovule under a microscope.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: seed - the mature ovule of a plant, containing an embryo

Etymology

From Latin ovulum, diminutive of ovum (egg).

2. NOUN

/ ˈəv.juːl / (AmE), / ˈɒv.juːl / (BrE)
a small or immature ovum, especially in animals or plants before fertilization
small or immature ovum
medical

Examples

The scientist examined the ovule under a microscope to study early cell development.
Each flower contains an ovule that can develop into a seed after fertilization.
In mammals, the ovule matures into an ovum as it completes its development.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: ovum - a mature female reproductive cell (egg cell)
  • Degree Higher: embryo - an early stage of development after an ovule or ovum is fertilized

Etymology

From Latin 'ovulum', diminutive of 'ovum' (egg).
