Thymus serpyllum

1. NOUN

/ ˈθaɪməs sərˈpɪləm / (AmE), / ˈtʌɪməs səˈpɪləm / (BrE)
a low-growing aromatic shrub, also known as wild thyme, commonly found on banks and hillsides in Europe and naturalized in the United States
aromatic dwarf shrub (wild thyme)

Examples

Thymus serpyllum is often used as a decorative groundcover in rock gardens.
Herbalists value Thymus serpyllum for its aromatic leaves and potential medicinal properties.
The gentle aroma of Thymus serpyllum filled the hillside in early summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild thyme - common English name for Thymus serpyllum

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Thymus', species 'serpyllum' meaning 'wild thyme'.
