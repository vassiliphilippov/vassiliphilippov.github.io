ethic

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛθɪk / (AmE), / ˈɛθɪk / (BrE)
A system of moral principles that governs a person's or group's behavior.
system of moral principles

Examples

Many doctors follow a strict ethic of honesty and compassion in their practice.
Every profession has its own ethic guiding the behavior of its members.
The company's corporate ethic is focused on social responsibility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morality - principles concerning distinction between right and wrong
  • Synonym: ethics - the branch of philosophy dealing with moral principles

Etymology

From Greek ēthikos (ethical), from ēthos (character, custom).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛθɪk / (AmE), / ˈɛθɪk / (BrE)
A set of moral principles or values that guide the behavior of an individual or social group.
set of moral principles or values

Examples

His strong work ethic earned him the respect of his colleagues.
Many religions promote an ethic of kindness and compassion.
The Puritan ethic influenced American culture for centuries.

Synset Examples

the Puritan ethic
a person with old-fashioned values

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morality - principles concerning the distinction between right and wrong
  • Antonym: immorality - absence of moral principles; evil or wicked behavior

Etymology

From Greek ēthikos (moral), from ēthos (custom, character).
