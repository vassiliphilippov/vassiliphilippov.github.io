manic-depressive

1. ADJ

/ ˌmænɪk dɪˈprɛsɪv / (AmE), / ˌmænɪk dɪˈprɛsɪv / (BrE)
suffering from a mental disorder characterized by alternating periods of mania and depression; having bipolar disorder.
having alternating mania and depression
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that her symptoms were typical of a manic-depressive patient.
His manic-depressive episodes made daily life unpredictable and sometimes exhausting.
She was diagnosed as manic-depressive in her early twenties, before the term 'bipolar disorder' was widely used.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bipolar - having or relating to bipolar disorder; characterized by episodes of mania and depression

Etymology

Formed from "manic" (relating to mania) + "depressive" (relating to depression).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmænɪk dɪˈprɛsɪv / (AmE), / ˌmænɪk dɪˈprɛsɪv / (BrE)
a person who suffers from manic-depressive illness (bipolar disorder), experiencing alternating periods of mania and depression
person with bipolar disorder
medical

Examples

Living with a manic-depressive can be emotionally demanding for family members.
The manic-depressive was prescribed medication to help stabilize his moods.
She wrote a memoir about her experiences as a manic-depressive in a society that misunderstood her condition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bipolar disorder - a mental illness with episodes of mania and depression

Etymology

From 'mania' and 'depressive', forming a compound for the condition known as manic-depressive illness; now called bipolar disorder.
