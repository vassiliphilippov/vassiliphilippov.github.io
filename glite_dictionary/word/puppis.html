Puppis

1. PROPN

/ ˈpʌpɪs / (AmE), / ˈpʌpɪs / (BrE)
a constellation in the Southern Hemisphere, situated between Vela and Canis Major, representing the stern of a ship
constellation in the Southern Hemisphere

Examples

The constellation Puppis is best observed during southern hemisphere autumn evenings.
Many stars in Puppis have been studied for their variable brightness.
Sailors once used Puppis to help navigate by the stars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Vela - a neighboring constellation representing the sails of a ship
  • Synonym: Canis Major - a neighboring constellation containing the star Sirius

Etymology

From Latin 'puppis' meaning 'stern (of a ship)'.
