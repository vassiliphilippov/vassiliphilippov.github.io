to domesticate

1. VERB

/ dəˈmɛstəˌkeɪt / (AmE), / dəˈmɛstɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to adapt a wild plant or uncultivated land for human use or cultivation
adapt wild plants or land for cultivation

Examples

Modern farmers have learned how to domesticate wild grains for large-scale agriculture.
Early civilizations worked to domesticate the river valleys into productive farmland.
Scientists are seeking new ways to domesticate native plants for food production.

Synset Examples

domesticate oats
tame the soil

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cultivate - prepare and use land for crops or gardening

Etymology

From Latin domesticare, from domesticus (of the home).

2. VERB

/ dəˈmɛstɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / dəˈmɛstɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to make (an animal or plant) tame or suitable for use by humans, especially by training or selective breeding; to make someone or something more gentle, tractable, or civilized
to tame or make suitable for human use

Examples

Early humans began to domesticate wolves thousands of years ago, leading to the development of dogs.
Farmers domesticate wild plants to improve crop yields and adapt them to local conditions.
It took years of careful training to domesticate the horses and make them safe for children.

Synset Examples

He tames lions for the circus
reclaim falcons

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tame - to make less wild or easier to control
  • Antonym: wild - not domesticated or tamed, living in a natural state

Etymology

From Latin domesticare, from domesticus (of the house).

3. VERB

/ dəˈmɛstəˌkeɪt / (AmE), / dəˈmɛstɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to train or adapt a wild animal or plant for use in homes, farms, or to benefit humans; to tame or cultivate for human purposes
tame or adapt for human use

Examples

Humans began to domesticate wild animals thousands of years ago.
Scientists are trying to domesticate certain wild plants to improve food security.
It can take generations to successfully domesticate a new species.

Synset Examples

The horse was domesticated a long time ago
The wolf was tamed and evolved into the house dog

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tame - make less wild; domesticated
  • Antonym: wild - (of animals) living in natural state; not tamed or domesticated

Etymology

From Latin domesticus (of the home), from domus (house).
