Coreopsis tinctoria

1. NOUN

/ kɔːˈɹiː.əp.sɪs tɪŋkˈtɔɹ.i.ə / (AmE), / kɒːˈriː.əp.sɪs tɪŋkˈtɔː.rɪ.ə / (BrE)
A North American annual plant widely cultivated for its bright yellow flowers with purple-red to brown centers; also called plains coreopsis or calliopsis.
North American flowering annual; 'plains coreopsis'

Examples

Many gardeners plant Coreopsis tinctoria for its vibrant yellow blooms throughout the summer.
In wildflower meadows, Coreopsis tinctoria attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
The dye from Coreopsis tinctoria petals has been used in traditional crafts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calliopsis - another name for Coreopsis tinctoria

Etymology

From New Latin; 'Coreopsis' from Greek 'koris' (bug) and 'opsis' (appearance); 'tinctoria' from Latin, meaning 'used for dyeing'.
