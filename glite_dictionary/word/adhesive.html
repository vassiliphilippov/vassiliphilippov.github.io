adhesive

1. NOUN

/ ədˈhisɪv / (AmE), / ədˈhiːsɪv / (BrE)
a substance that bonds surfaces together by sticking them to each other
substance that sticks things together

Examples

She applied a thin layer of adhesive to the back of the wallpaper.
Strong adhesive is needed to fix the broken vase.
Spray adhesive is often used for arts and crafts projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glue - a sticky substance used for joining things together
  • Synonym: cement - a binding substance used to join materials together
  • Antonym: solvent - a substance that dissolves other substances (has opposite function to adhesive)

Etymology

From Latin adhaesivus, from adhaerere (to stick to).

2. ADJ

/ ədˈhisɪv / (AmE), / ədˈhiːsɪv / (BrE)
tending to stick to a surface or object; having the property of adhering
able to stick or cling to surfaces

Examples

The adhesive quality of the tape makes it perfect for wrapping gifts.
Because the paint wasn't adhesive enough, it quickly peeled off the wall.
He wore an adhesive badge on his shirt during the event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cohesive - tending to stick together as a unified whole
  • Antonym: nonadhesive - not tending to stick or adhere

Etymology

From Latin adhaesivus, from adhaesus (past participle of adhaerere, ‘to stick to’).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00052912-a
Synset POS: a
