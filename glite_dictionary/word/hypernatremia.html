hypernatremia

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpərnəˈtriːmiə / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpənəˈtriːmɪə / (BrE)
A medical condition in which there is an abnormally high concentration of sodium in the blood.
high sodium in the blood (medical)
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the patient with hypernatremia after reviewing the abnormal blood test results.
Treatment for hypernatremia often includes controlled hydration and monitoring sodium intake.
Elderly patients are particularly at risk for developing hypernatremia during hot weather.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hyponatremia - low concentration of sodium in the blood

Etymology

From Greek 'hyper-' (over, excessive) + 'natrium' (sodium, from Latin) + '-emia' (blood condition).
