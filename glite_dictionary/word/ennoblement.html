ennoblement

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈnoʊbəlmənt / (AmE), / ɪˈnəʊblmənt / (BrE)
the act of making someone a noble or raising them to a higher social or moral status; the state of being made noble
the act or state of becoming noble

Examples

The ennoblement of the scientist was recognized by granting him a title.
Her actions led to the ennoblement of her entire family in the community.
The novel explores themes of ennoblement through self-sacrifice and courage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ennoble - to make someone a noble or to elevate in dignity or honor
  • Antonym: degradation - the act or process of lowering in moral or social status

Etymology

From Old French 'ennoblir' (to ennoble), from Latin 'nobilis' (noble).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈnoʊbəlmənt / (AmE), / ɪˈnəʊblmənt / (BrE)
the act of elevating someone to the rank of nobility or granting them a noble title
act of making someone a noble

Examples

The king's ennoblement of loyal subjects was a common practice in the 18th century.
He viewed his ennoblement as both an honor and a responsibility.
Following her ennoblement, she took part in many royal ceremonies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ennoble - to make someone a member of the nobility

Etymology

From French *ennoblir* (to ennoble), from Latin *nobilis* (noble).
