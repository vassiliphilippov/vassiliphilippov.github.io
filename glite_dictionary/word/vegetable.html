vegetable

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛdʒ.tə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈvɛdʒ.tɪ.bəl / (BrE)
A plant or part of a plant, such as a root, leaf, seed, or flower, that is used as food.
edible plant or plant part used as food

Examples

Eating a variety of vegetables is important for a balanced diet.
They grow fresh vegetables in their backyard garden every summer.
Broccoli is a vegetable that many children dislike, but it is very nutritious.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fruit - the sweet and fleshy product of a tree or other plant containing seed

Etymology

From Latin vegetabilis (growing, flourishing), from vegetare (to enliven).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɛdʒtəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈvɛdʒtəb(ə)l / (BrE)
A plant or part of a plant, such as roots, stems, leaves, or fruits, that is used as food, typically not sweet and often eaten as part of savory dishes.
edible part of a plant used as food

Examples

She made a salad with fresh vegetable from the garden.
Carrots are a root vegetable rich in vitamins.
Eating a variety of vegetable daily is important for good health.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fruit - the sweet and fleshy product of a tree or other plant that contains seed

Etymology

From Old French 'vegetable', from Latin 'vegetabilis' (animating, enlivening), from 'vegetare' (to enliven).
