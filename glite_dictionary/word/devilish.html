devilish

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɛvəlɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈdɛvəlɪʃ / (BrE)
Playful in an appealingly bold or mischievous way.
playfully bold or mischievous

Examples

She gave him a devilish wink before racing off down the hall.
His devilish sense of humor kept the whole class laughing.
A devilish grin spread across her face as she revealed her clever plan.

Synset Examples

a roguish grin

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mischievous - causing or showing a fondness for causing trouble in a playful way
  • Antonym: angelic - having the qualities of an angel, especially innocence or goodness

Etymology

From 'devil' + '-ish', meaning 'of or relating to a devil'; figurative playful sense emerged later.

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɛvəlɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈdɛvəlɪʃ / (BrE)
showing cunning, ingenuity, or wickedness typical of a devil; extremely mischievous or fiendish
cunning or wicked like a devil

Examples

He hatched a devilish plan to outwit his rivals.
Her devilish grin made everyone suspicious of her intentions.
The children played a devilish prank on their unsuspecting teacher.

Synset Examples

devilish schemes
the cold calculation and diabolic art of some statesmen
the diabolical expression on his face
a mephistophelian glint in his eye

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diabolical - extremely evil or cruel; resembling a devil

Etymology

From 'devil' + '-ish', meaning 'like a devil'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01136874-s
Synset POS: s
