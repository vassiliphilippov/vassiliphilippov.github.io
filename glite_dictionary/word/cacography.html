cacography

1. NOUN

/ kəˈkɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / kəˈkɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
bad or illegible handwriting; poor spelling.
bad or illegible handwriting

Examples

The student’s cacography made it difficult for the teacher to read the assignment.
Despite his intelligence, his cacography often gave the wrong impression in written exams.
The physician’s cacography on the prescription led to a misunderstanding at the pharmacy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calligraphy - decorative or beautiful handwriting
  • Synonym: penmanship - the art or skill of writing by hand
  • Degree Higher: illegible - not able to be read

Etymology

From Greek kakographia, from kakos (bad) + graphein (to write).
