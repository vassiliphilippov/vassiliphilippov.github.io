Siouan

1. NOUN

/ suːˈɑːn / (AmE), / sɪˈuːən / (BrE)
A family of North American Indigenous languages, including those spoken by the Sioux and related peoples.
family of Native American languages

Examples

The Siouan languages are spoken across a wide region of North America.
Linguists have studied various Siouan dialects to understand the migration of Native peoples.
Lakota is one of several Siouan languages still spoken today.

Etymology

From 'Sioux,' the name of one of the principal peoples speaking these languages, plus the English suffix -an.

2. ADJ

/ ˈsuː.ən / (AmE), / ˈsuː.ən / (BrE)
of or relating to the Sioux people, their languages, or their culture
relating to the Sioux people or their language

Examples

The museum features a large collection of Siouan artifacts.
Several Siouan languages are still spoken in North America today.
He specializes in the history of Siouan tribes of the Great Plains.

Etymology

From Sioux, the name of a group of Native American peoples, plus -an.

3. NOUN

/ suˈɑːn / (AmE), / sɪˈuːən / (BrE)
a member of a Native American group whose peoples historically ranged from Lake Michigan to the Rocky Mountains and spoke a Siouan language
member of a North American Native American group

Examples

Archaeologists have uncovered tools used by ancient Siouan communities near the Missouri River.
Many Siouan people migrated to new territories during the 18th century.
The museum features artifacts and pottery made by various Siouan tribes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sioux - a group of Native American peoples, part of the Siouan family

Etymology

From Sioux (a major group within the Siouan language family) plus the suffix '-an'.
