Dytiscidae

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈtɪsɪˌdi / (AmE), / daɪˈtɪsɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of large aquatic beetles commonly known as diving beetles, found in fresh water around the world.
family of aquatic diving beetles

Examples

The pond is home to several species of Dytiscidae, which are efficient aquatic predators.
Researchers studied the life cycles of Dytiscidae in temperate wetlands.
Children found a large Dytiscidae swimming under the lily pads.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diving beetle - common name for beetles in the Dytiscidae family

Etymology

From Modern Latin Dytiscidae, formed from genus name Dytiscus, from Greek dytikos 'able to dive'.
