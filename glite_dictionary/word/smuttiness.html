smuttiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsmʌt̬.i.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsmʌt.i.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being obscene or indecent, especially in speech or writing
obscenity; indecent quality

Examples

The comedian was criticized for the smuttiness of his jokes during the performance.
Some people enjoy television shows with a touch of smuttiness, while others prefer clean humor.
The editor removed passages of smuttiness from the novel to make it suitable for younger readers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obscenity - offensive language or content
  • Antonym: decency - the quality of being decent or proper

Etymology

From 'smutty,' which originates from 'smut' (dirt, indecency), ultimately from Middle Low German 'smutte' (dirt).
