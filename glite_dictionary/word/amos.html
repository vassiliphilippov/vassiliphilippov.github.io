Amos

1. PROPN

/ ˈeɪˌmoʊs / (AmE), / ˈeɪmɒs / (BrE)
A Hebrew shepherd and minor prophet in the Old Testament, known for the Book of Amos.
minor prophet of the Old Testament

Examples

Amos is recognized as one of the earliest of the twelve minor prophets in the Old Testament.
The Book of Amos focuses on themes of social justice and divine judgment.
Many scholars reference Amos when discussing prophetic literature of ancient Israel.

Etymology

From Hebrew 'ʿĀmōs, meaning 'carried [by God]'.

2. PROPN

/ ˈeɪˌməs / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.mɒs / (BrE)
A book of the Old Testament containing the prophecies of the prophet Amos.
Old Testament book of prophecies

Examples

Many scholars study the themes found in Amos to understand social justice in ancient Israel.
The Book of Amos warns of consequences for neglecting the poor.
In the sermon, the pastor quoted several passages from Amos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hosea - An Old Testament book of prophecies by Hosea
  • Synonym: Micah - An Old Testament book of prophecies by Micah

Etymology

From the Hebrew name *ʿāmōs*, meaning 'borne (by God)'.
