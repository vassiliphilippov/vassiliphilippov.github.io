turtledove

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɜr.tl̩ˌdʌv / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.tl̩ˌdʌv / (BrE)
a small wild dove of the Old World, known for its soft cooing and often symbolizing devoted love
a small wild Old World dove

Examples

We saw a turtledove perched on the fence, gently cooing in the early morning.
The turtledove is often mentioned in poetry as a symbol of love and fidelity.
Researchers observed several turtledoves migrating across southern Europe during the spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dove - a bird in the pigeon family, generally smaller and slenderer than pigeons

Etymology

From Old English turtle (from Latin turtur, imitating its call) + dove.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɝː.təlˌdʌv / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.tl̩ˌdʌv / (BrE)
a small dove with a soft, gentle cooing call; refers especially to certain species, such as the European or Australian turtledove
a small dove species

Examples

A turtledove rested quietly on the branch, cooing softly as the sun set.
The turtledove is often used as a symbol of love and devotion in poetry.
In Australia, you can spot the peaceful turtledove in parks and gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dove - a bird of the pigeon family with soft cooing call

Etymology

From Old English 'turtledoufa', from Latin 'turtur', imitative of the bird's call.
