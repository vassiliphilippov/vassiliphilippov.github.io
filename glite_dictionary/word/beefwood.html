beefwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈbiːfˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈbiːfwʊd / (BrE)
any of several Australian trees of the genus Casuarina that produce heavy, hard, reddish wood used in cabinetwork.
Australian tree with hard red wood

Examples

The furniture was crafted from durable beefwood known for its rich red hue.
Carpenters in Australia often prefer beefwood for making high-quality cabinets and panels.
The dense grain of beefwood makes it ideal for fine woodworking projects.

Etymology

From the resemblance of the wood's color to raw beef.

/ ˈbifˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈbiːfwʊd / (BrE)

reddish tropical hardwood (botany/timber)

Examples

The furniture maker preferred beefwood for its durability and rich color.
Several species known as beefwood are valued for their dense timber.
Because of its hardness, beefwood can be challenging to carve.
