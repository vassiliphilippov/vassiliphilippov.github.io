backup

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈbækʌp / (BrE)
a musical part, vocal or instrumental, that provides support or accompaniment for the main parts in a performance or recording
musical support part or accompaniment

Examples

The singer was joined by a backup vocal group during the chorus.
He plays backup guitar for the lead performer on tour.
Several musicians provided backup in the recording studio.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accompaniment - a musical part that supports the main melody
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07045239-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈbækʌp / (BrE)
a person who substitutes for or supports another, especially when the original is unable to perform a task or when extra help is needed
a substitute or supporting person

Examples

When the lead singer lost her voice, the backup stepped in for the concert.
The security team always has a backup ready in case things go wrong.
If our main driver gets tired, we have a backup to take over.

Synset Examples

the star had a stand-in for dangerous scenes
we need extra employees for summer fill-ins

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stand-in - a person who replaces another, especially temporarily
  • Synonym: substitute - a person who acts in place of another
  • Synonym: replacement - a person who takes over someone else's role
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈbæk.ʌp / (BrE)
help or support given to someone, especially when needed to strengthen their position or argument.
support, especially moral or institutional

Examples

We had no backup in the negotiations, so our proposal was quickly dismissed.
The police called for backup when the situation escalated unexpectedly.
Her claims had little backup from the scientific community.

Synset Examples

his vigorous backing of the conservatives got him in trouble with progressives

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - help or approval that you give to someone or something

Etymology

From the phrase 'back up', meaning 'support', first attested in the early 19th century.

4. NOUN

/ ˈbæk.ʌp / (AmE), / ˈbæk.ʌp / (BrE)
A copy of a file, program, or data stored separately for security, especially in case the original is lost or damaged.
copy of data kept for security

Examples

Remember to create a backup of your important documents before updating the software.
After the hard drive failure, the company restored data from the latest backup.
It's a good habit to make a regular backup of your computer files.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
