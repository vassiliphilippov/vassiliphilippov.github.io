Swietinia mahogani

1. NOUN

/ swɪˌiːˈtɪniə məˈhɑːɡəni / (AmE), / swɪˌiːˈtɪniə məˈhɒɡəni / (BrE)
A species of mahogany tree native to the West Indies, known for its valuable hardwood used in furniture and construction.
West Indian mahogany tree species

Examples

The wood of Swietinia mahogani is highly valued for making fine furniture.
Many parks in the Caribbean protect native Swietinia mahogani trees.
The craftsman selected Swietinia mahogani because of its rich color and durability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mahogany - Strong reddish-brown wood from several tropical trees

Etymology

From the Latinized genus name 'Swietinia', honoring Polish botanist J. E. Swieten, and mahogani, from 'mahogany'.
