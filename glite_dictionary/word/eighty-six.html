eighty-six

1. ADJ

/ ˌeɪtiˈsɪks / (AmE), / ˌeɪtiˈsɪks / (BrE)
the number 86; being six more than eighty
the number 86

Examples

There are eighty-six students signed up for the class this semester.
My grandfather turned eighty-six years old last week.
The shipment contained eighty-six boxes of supplies for the relief effort.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: eighty-five - the number 85
  • Degree Higher: eighty-seven - the number 87

Etymology

Formed from "eighty" (from Old English "eahtatig") and "six" (from Old English "siex"), compounded to express the number 86.
