rassling

1. NOUN

/ ˈræslɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈraslɪŋ / (BrE)
An informal or regional term for wrestling, especially hand-to-hand sport where opponents try to throw each other down.
informal term for wrestling
informal american

Examples

The county fair hosted a rassling match that drew a big crowd.
He learned rassling from his older brothers on their family farm.
Some folks around here still call wrestling rassling, just like our grandparents did.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrestling - the sport of hand-to-hand fighting where opponents try to throw each other

Etymology

Informal or dialect variant of 'wrestling', from the pronunciation and spelling shift in regional American English.
