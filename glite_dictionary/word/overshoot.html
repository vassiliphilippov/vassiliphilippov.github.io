to overshoot

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈʃuːt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈʃuːt / (BrE)
to shoot or go beyond the target, mark, or intended point
to go beyond a target or point

Examples

The pilot accidentally overshot the runway during landing.
If you overshoot the target, adjust your aim downward next time.
The archer overshot by aiming too high in the competition.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to undershoot - to fall short of reaching or hitting a target

Etymology

From over- (beyond) + shoot (to discharge a projectile), first used in English in the late Middle Ages.

2. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈʃuːt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈʃuːt / (BrE)
to go beyond a target or intended goal, especially by aiming or projecting too far
go beyond a target or limit

Examples

If you overshoot your destination, you'll need to turn around and come back.
The company overshot its sales targets this quarter by a significant margin.
Be careful not to overshoot the runway when landing the plane.

Synset Examples

The plan overshoots its aim

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to undershoot - to fall short of a target or intended goal

Etymology

From over- (beyond) + shoot (to discharge a missile); first recorded in the 16th century.
