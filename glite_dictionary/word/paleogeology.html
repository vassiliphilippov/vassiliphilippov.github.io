paleogeology

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪlioʊdʒiˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌpeɪlɪəʊdʒɪˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of geology concerned with the study of ancient geologic features that were once at the earth’s surface but are now buried beneath other rocks
study of ancient buried geologic features

Examples

Researchers in paleogeology reconstruct the environments of Earth’s distant past.
A background in paleogeology is essential for understanding mineral deposits formed millions of years ago.
The conference featured a session on advances in paleogeology and subsurface exploration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geology - the science that deals with the earth's physical structure and substance

Etymology

From paleo- (ancient) + geology, ultimately from Greek 'palaios' (ancient) and 'geōlogia' (study of the earth).
