tussur

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌsər / (AmE), / ˈtʌsə / (BrE)
an oriental moth that produces brownish silk, commonly used in textiles
moth producing brown silk

Examples

The tussur is prized for its strong, naturally brown silk.
Farmers in India often rear tussur moths to harvest their valuable silk cocoons.
Unlike common silkworms, the tussur produces a coarser fabric preferred for certain garments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tussore - another spelling of tussur, refers to the same moth or silk
  • Synonym: silkworm - insect larva that produces silk

Etymology

From Hindi *tasar*, referring to the moth and its silk.
