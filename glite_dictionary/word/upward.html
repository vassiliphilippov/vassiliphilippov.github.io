upward

1. ADJ

/ ˈʌpwərd / (AmE), / ˈʌpwəd / (BrE)
moving or extending toward a higher place, position, or level.
moving or extending to a higher place

Examples

The upward trend in stock prices reassured investors.
He glanced at the upward sloping path ahead of them.
There was an upward surge in demand for new technology this year.

Synset Examples

the up staircase
a general upward movement of fish

Related Terms

  • Antonym: downward - moving or extending toward a lower place
  • Synonym: upwards - toward a higher place (adverb form)

Etymology

From Old English 'upweard', from 'up' (up) + 'weard' (toward).

2. ADJ

/ ˈʌpwɚd / (AmE), / ˈʌpwəd / (BrE)
pointing or moving towards a higher position or level; directed up
directed or moving up

Examples

Synset Examples

/ ˈʌpwɚd / (AmE), / ˈʌpwəd / (BrE)

continuing to a later time or stage

Examples
