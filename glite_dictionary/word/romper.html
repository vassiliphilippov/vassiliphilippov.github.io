romper

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːmpər / (AmE), / ˈrɒmpə / (BrE)
a person who frolics or plays energetically and boisterously
person who plays energetically

Examples

As a child, he was a real romper, always running and laughing in the garden.
The teacher watched the rompers at recess and admired their boundless energy.
Known as the neighborhood romper, she loved making up new games for her friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frolicker - a person who frolics

Etymology

From the verb 'romp' (to play or frolic energetically) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːmpər / (AmE), / ˈrɒmpə / (BrE)
A one-piece garment for young children, typically used for play, with short legs and sometimes sleeves, resembling a combination of shirt and shorts.
one-piece play garment for children

Examples

The toddler wore a brightly colored romper to the park.
She dressed her baby in a soft cotton romper for nap time.
Many parents choose rompers for their convenience and comfort during play.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: onesie - a one-piece garment for infants

Etymology

From 'romp' (to play or frolic) + '-er', denoting something used for a particular purpose.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04111864-n
Synset POS: n
