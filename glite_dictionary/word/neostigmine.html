neostigmine

1. NOUN

/ nioʊˈstɪɡˌmin / (AmE), / niːˈɒstɪɡmiːn / (BrE)
a drug that increases the activity of acetylcholine, used especially to treat myasthenia gravis and certain eye conditions
cholinergic drug treating myasthenia gravis and eye conditions
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed neostigmine to manage the patient’s muscle weakness.
Ophthalmologists sometimes use neostigmine in treating certain glaucoma cases.
She reacted positively to the initial dose of neostigmine at the hospital.

Etymology

From neo- (new) + stigmine (from physostigmine, a related compound).
