unblinking

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈblɪŋkɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈblɪŋkɪŋ / (BrE)
showing no emotion or reaction, especially in the face of something difficult or significant
showing no emotion or reaction

Examples

She sat unblinking as the verdict was read aloud in the courtroom.
His unblinking stare made everyone in the room uncomfortable.
The soldier remained unblinking despite the chaos around him.

Synset Examples

stood unblinking and accepted a sentence of a year

Etymology

Formed with the prefix 'un-' (not) and 'blinking', from 'blink' (to close and open the eyes quickly).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈblɪŋkɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈblɪŋkɪŋ / (BrE)
not showing fear, hesitation, or any emotional response, especially when facing danger; unwavering.
not showing fear or emotion; unwavering

Examples

He faced the fierce criticism with an unblinking calm.
Her unblinking stare gave nothing away, even when threatened.
With unblinking resolve, the soldier marched forward into the line of fire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unflinching - not shrinking from difficulty or danger; steadfast

Etymology

from prefix 'un-' (not) + 'blinking' (showing reaction or hesitation)

Synset POS: s
