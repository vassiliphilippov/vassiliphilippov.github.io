silicide

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlɪˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɪlɪsʌɪd / (BrE)
a chemical compound formed between silicon and a more electropositive element or radical
compound of silicon with a metal or radical

Examples

Nickel silicide is used in microelectronics for its low resistivity.
The formation of a metal silicide layer can improve device performance.
Scientists studied new methods to synthesize rare earth silicides for advanced applications.

Etymology

From silicon + -ide, modeled after chemical naming conventions.
