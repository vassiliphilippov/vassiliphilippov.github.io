Konstantin Stanislavsky

1. PROPN

/ kənˈstɑn.tin stəˌnɪ.slɑvˈski / (AmE), / kɒnˈstan.tɪn stænɪsˈlævski / (BrE)
(1863–1938) Russian actor and theater director renowned for pioneering a system of actor training that emphasizes psychological motivation and naturalistic performance.
Russian theater director and acting theorist

Examples

Many modern actors study the methods of Konstantin Stanislavsky to improve their craft.
The influence of Konstantin Stanislavsky on twentieth-century theater is widely recognized.
She read a biography of Konstantin Stanislavsky for her theater history course.
