Catharacta skua

1. NOUN

/ kæθəˈræktə ˈskjuːə / (AmE), / kaθəˈræktə ˈskjuːə / (BrE)
A large brown seabird of the skua family found in the northern Atlantic, known for its aggressive behavior and predation.
large brown seabird found in the northern Atlantic

Examples

The Catharacta skua is known to aggressively defend its territory from intruders.
Birdwatchers spotted a Catharacta skua circling above the rocky cliffs.
Researchers study the migration patterns of the Catharacta skua in the northern Atlantic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: great skua - common name for Catharacta skua, a large predatory seabird

Etymology

From Neo-Latin genus name 'Catharacta' and species name 'skua', which is from Faroese 'skúgvur' (skua).
