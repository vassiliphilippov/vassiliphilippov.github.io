walk-through

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔkˌθru / (AmE), / ˈwɔːkˌθruː / (BrE)
a detailed explanation or demonstration of each step in a process or procedure
detailed step-by-step explanation or demonstration

Examples

The manager provided a walk-through of the new software to help employees understand its features.
Before the event, there will be a walk-through of the safety procedures for all staff.
I found an online walk-through that shows exactly how to assemble the furniture.

Synset Examples

she gave me a walk-through of my new duties

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demonstration - the act of showing how something works
  • Degree Lower: overview - general summary or review of a topic
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07296994-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɑk θru / (AmE), / ˈwɔːk θruː / (BrE)
an informal rehearsal of a play or performance in which actors read their lines and go through the motions, often with scripts in hand
preliminary rehearsal in theatre

Examples

The director scheduled a walk-through for the cast to become familiar with the stage movements.
During the walk-through, actors were allowed to refer to their scripts if needed.
Tonight’s walk-through gave everyone a chance to practice their entrances and exits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rehearsal - practice session before a public performance, especially in theater
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00899306-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈwɔkˌθru / (AmE), / ˈwɔːkˌθruː / (BrE)
an act or occasion of going through a place, especially to inspect or examine it
act of touring or inspecting a place

Examples

Before purchasing the house, the couple scheduled a walk-through with their agent.
We did a final walk-through of the venue before the event to check the decorations.
The landlord joined us for a walk-through to note any damage before we moved out.

Synset Examples

the realtor took her on a walk-through of the apartment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inspection - careful examination, often of a place or thing
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
