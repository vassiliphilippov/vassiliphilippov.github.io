influence

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnfluəns / (AmE), / ˈɪnflʊəns / (BrE)
the effect that someone or something has on the way a person thinks or behaves, or on the way that something develops
effect of someone or something on another

Examples

His parents had a strong influence on his choice of career.
The media can have a powerful influence over public opinion.
Global warming is partly due to human influence on the environment.

Synset Examples

the influence of mechanical action

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impact - strong effect or influence

Etymology

From Latin 'influēntia' (a flowing in), originally from 'influere' (to flow into).
