film

1. NOUN

/ fɪlm / (AmE), / fɪlm / (BrE)
a thin, usually transparent sheet of plastic material used to wrap or cover objects
thin plastic sheet for wrapping or covering

Examples

She covered the leftovers with a layer of film to keep them fresh.
A thin film protected the new device from scratches during shipping.
Before painting, remove the plastic film from the surface of the product.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plastic wrap - a thin, clingy plastic film used in food storage
  • Antonym: foil - a thin sheet of metal, used for wrapping food

4. NOUN

/ fɪlm / (AmE), / fɪlm / (BrE)
photographic material consisting of a thin strip coated with a light-sensitive emulsion, used for making photographs, negatives, or transparencies
photographic material for making images

Examples

I found an old roll of film in my grandfather's camera bag.
Make sure the film is loaded properly before you start taking pictures.
Digital photography has largely replaced traditional film in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: photograph - an image made using a camera
  • Degree Lower: negative - an image on film showing light and shade reversed, used to make photographs
  • Degree Higher: transparency - a positive photographic image on film, viewed with a projector or light

Etymology

From Old English 'filmen' (membrane, thin skin), related to Old High German 'filmina'.
