to haemagglutinate

1. VERB

/ hiːməˈɡluːtəˌneɪt / (AmE), / hiːməˈɡluːtɪneɪt / (BrE)
to cause red blood cells to clump together, especially as a reaction to a specific agent or antibody
cause red blood cells to clump
medical british

Examples

Certain viruses can haemagglutinate red blood cells in laboratory tests.
When specific antibodies are added, they often haemagglutinate the sample rapidly.
The researchers observed that the patient’s serum did not haemagglutinate any of the tested cells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agglutinate - to cause particles or cells to stick together

Etymology

From Greek 'haima' (blood) and Latin 'agglutinare' (to glue together).
