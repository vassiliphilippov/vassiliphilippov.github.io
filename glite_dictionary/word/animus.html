animus

1. NOUN

/ ˈænɪməs / (AmE), / ˈanɪməs / (BrE)
strong feeling of hostility or ill will toward someone or something
hostile feeling; ill will

Examples

Her animus toward her former employer was evident in every conversation.
The negotiations collapsed under a cloud of mutual animus.
Despite their history of animus, they managed to work together professionally.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enmity - a state or feeling of active opposition or hostility
  • Antonym: benevolence - the quality of being well meaning; kindness

Etymology

From Latin animus (mind, spirit, intent, hostility).
