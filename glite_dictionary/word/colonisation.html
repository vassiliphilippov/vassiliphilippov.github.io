colonisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːlənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒlənʌɪˈzeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the act or process of establishing control over a territory and settling it with people from another country
establishment of colonies

Examples

The colonisation of large parts of Africa had profound cultural and political effects.
European colonisation transformed the Americas in the 16th century.
Historians continue to debate the long-term impact of colonisation on indigenous populations.

Synset Examples

the British colonization of America

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colonization - the act or process of establishing control over a territory and settling it with people from another country (American spelling)
  • Antonym: decolonization - the process of a colony gaining independence from a colonizing country

Etymology

From Latin 'colonia' (settlement, farm) via French 'colonisation'.
