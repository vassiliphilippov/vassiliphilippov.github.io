Trogonidae

1. NOUN

/ troʊˈɡɒnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / trəʊˈɡɒnɪˌdiː / (BrE)
The family of brightly colored tropical birds known as trogons, found mainly in Central and South America, Africa, and Asia.
family of tropical birds (trogons)

Examples

The Trogonidae include some of the most vividly colored birds in the tropics.
Members of the Trogonidae family are known for their unique nesting habits.
Researchers observed several species from the Trogonidae during their expedition to the rainforest.

Etymology

From Modern Latin, from Greek trōgōn (gnawer, trogon) + -idae (zoological family suffix).
