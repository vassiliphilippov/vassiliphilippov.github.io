Rheum emodi

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːəm ˈɛmoʊdi / (AmE), / ˈruːəm ˈɛməʊdi / (BrE)
A species of rhubarb native to the Himalayas, known for its large leaves and medicinal rhizomes.
Himalayan rhubarb species

Examples

The roots of Rheum emodi are widely used in traditional Himalayan medicine.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of Rheum emodi extracts in various clinical applications.
The large leaves of Rheum emodi are a distinguishing characteristic of this mountain herb.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhubarb - a common garden plant with edible stalks; genus Rheum

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Rheum' and specific epithet 'emodi', referring to 'Emodus' (the Himalayas).
