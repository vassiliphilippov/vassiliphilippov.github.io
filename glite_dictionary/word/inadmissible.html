inadmissible

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪnədˈmɪs.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˌɪn.ədˈmɪs.ɪ.bəl / (BrE)
Not allowed to be considered or accepted, especially in a court or formal context.
not allowed or accepted, especially in court

Examples

The judge ruled that the documents were inadmissible in the trial.
His excuse was deemed inadmissible by the committee.
Certain types of hearsay evidence are inadmissible in most courts.

Synset Examples

inadmissible evidence

Related Terms

  • Antonym: admissible - allowed or valid, especially in a court of law

Etymology

From Latin 'in-' (not) + 'admissibilis' (that may be admitted).
