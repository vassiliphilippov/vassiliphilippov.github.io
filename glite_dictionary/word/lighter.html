lighter

1. VERB

/ ˈlaɪtər / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtə / (BrE)
(of a ship or cargo) to transport or transfer goods in a flat-bottomed boat, especially from a ship to the shore or to another vessel.
transport goods in a flat-bottomed boat

Examples

The crew used a barge to lighter cargo from the anchored ship to the quay.
Due to the shallow harbor, containers were lightered ashore by small boats.
Special vessels are designed to lighter oil from tankers at sea.

Etymology

From the noun 'lighter' (flat-bottomed boat), itself from Middle Dutch 'lichter' (unloader).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪtər / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtə / (BrE)
a small device that produces a flame for lighting cigarettes, candles, or fires
device for creating a flame

Examples

He borrowed my lighter to light his cigarette.
Please keep the lighter out of reach of children.
She bought a new lighter because she lost her old one.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

Etymology

From the verb 'light' (to set fire to) + -er (agent noun suffix).

3. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪtər / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtə / (BrE)
a flat-bottomed barge or boat used for transporting heavy loads, especially on rivers or canals, often to or from larger ships
flat-bottomed cargo boat or barge

Examples

The goods were transferred from the ship to a lighter for transport along the canal.
A heavily loaded lighter drifted slowly past the docks as workers prepared to unload it.
The port's operations relied on the use of lighters to carry cargo where larger vessels could not reach.

Etymology

Probably from Middle Dutch ‘lichter’, from ‘lichten’ meaning ‘to lighten, to unload’.

Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪtər / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtə / (BrE)
a substance or device used to ignite or kindle a fire, such as to start a flame for a cigarette or campfire
device or substance to start fire

Examples

He reached into his pocket for a lighter to ignite the campfire.
The barbecue wouldn't start until someone found a lighter.
Always keep a lighter handy when hiking in case you need to make a fire.
