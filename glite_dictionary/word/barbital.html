barbital

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑrbɪˌtɔl / (AmE), / ˈbɑːbɪtəl / (BrE)
A barbiturate drug used as a sedative and hypnotic to induce sleep.
barbiturate sedative and hypnotic drug
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed barbital to help the patient sleep through the night.
Research has shown that barbital has mood-altering effects as well as sedative properties.
Due to the risk of dependence, the use of barbital is closely monitored in clinical settings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barbiturate - any of a group of drugs derived from barbituric acid, used as sedatives and hypnotics

Etymology

From 'barbituric acid' + '-al' (as in chemical alcohols), coined in the early 20th century.
