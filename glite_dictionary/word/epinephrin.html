epinephrin

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpəˈnɛfrɪn / (AmE), / ˌiːpɪˈnɛfrɪn / (BrE)
a hormone and neurotransmitter secreted by the adrenal medulla in response to stress, increasing heart rate and blood pressure; also known as adrenaline
hormone and neurotransmitter (adrenaline)
medical

Examples

Doctors may inject epinephrin to treat severe allergic reactions such as anaphylaxis.
The body releases epinephrin in response to stress or danger, triggering the 'fight or flight' response.
Pharmacists often refer to epinephrin when discussing emergency treatments for asthma or cardiac arrest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adrenaline - alternative name for epinephrin, used especially in British English

Etymology

From Greek 'epi' (upon) + 'nephros' (kidney), referring to its source above the kidneys.
