invigilator

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈvɪdʒ.ə.leɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɪdʒ.ɪ.leɪ.tə / (BrE)
a person who supervises candidates during an examination to ensure rules are followed and prevent cheating
person who monitors exams

Examples

The invigilator walked quietly around the room, observing the students as they wrote their exams.
If you need more paper, raise your hand and an invigilator will assist you.
During the test, the invigilator ensured that everyone followed the instructions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proctor - a person who supervises an examination, especially in American English

Etymology

From Latin vigilare (to watch) via invigilate, with suffix -ator.
