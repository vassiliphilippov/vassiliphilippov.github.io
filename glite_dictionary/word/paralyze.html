to paralyze

1. VERB

/ ˈpærəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈparəˌlʌɪz / (BrE)
to make someone or something powerless and unable to function or act
make powerless; disable

Examples

If fear paralyzes you, you may be unable to respond in an emergency.
The sudden strike paralyzed the city's transportation system for days.
Excessive regulations can sometimes paralyze innovation in a company.

Synset Examples

The bureaucracy paralyzes the entire operation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to immobilize - to make unable to move
  • Synonym: cripple - to make ineffective or powerless
  • Antonym: to revive - to bring back to activity or effectiveness

Etymology

From French *paralyser*, from Greek *paralusis* (disablement).

2. VERB

/ ˈpærəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈpærəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make someone or something unable to move or function, either physically or metaphorically
make unable to move or act

Examples

The sudden accident seemed to paralyze the entire city with shock.
The venom can paralyze its prey within minutes.
Sometimes anxiety can paralyze people from making important decisions.

Synset Examples

The poison paralyzed him
Fear paralyzed her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to immobilize - to prevent something or someone from moving
  • Antonym: to stimulate - to encourage or cause something to become more active

Etymology

From French 'paralyser', from 'paralysie' (paralysis), from Greek 'paralusis' (paralysis).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00269363-v
Synset POS: v
