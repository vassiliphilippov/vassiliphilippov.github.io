alliteration

1. NOUN

/ əˌlɪtəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌlɪtəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the occurrence of the same letter or sound at the beginning of adjacent or closely connected words, especially in poetry
repetition of consonant sounds at the beginning of words

Examples

The poet used alliteration to create a musical quality in the verse.
Children often enjoy tongue twisters because of their playful alliteration.
Phrases like 'Peter Piper picked' are classic examples of alliteration.

Synset Examples

initial rhyme such as ‘around the rock the ragged rascal ran’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assonance - repetition of vowel sounds in nearby words

Etymology

From Latin 'ad' (to) + 'littera' (letter).
