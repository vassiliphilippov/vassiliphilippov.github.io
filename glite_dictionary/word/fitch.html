fitch

1. NOUN

/ fɪtʃ / (AmE), / fɪtʃ / (BrE)
A small dark brown mammal of the weasel family, found in the woodlands of Eurasia, which emits a strong odor when threatened.
Eurasian mustelid (polecat)

Examples

The fitch is known for its distinctive musky odor, which it uses as a defense mechanism.
Early trappers valued fitch fur for making hats and other garments.
During our walk in the woods, we spotted a fitch darting between the trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polecat - a small carnivorous mammal of the weasel family
  • Synonym: ferret - a domesticated form of the European polecat

Etymology

From Old French 'fich', from Late Latin 'ficticius', meaning 'of the polecat'.
