carpospore

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑr.poʊˌspɔr / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.pəˌspɔː / (BrE)
A nonmotile spore produced by red algae, from which a new individual may develop.
nonmotile spore of red algae
medical

Examples

Red algae release carpospores as part of their complex reproductive cycle.
A carpospore develops from the fertilized carpogonium in many red algae species.
Scientists studied the germination of carpospores under different salinity conditions.

Etymology

From Greek karpos (fruit) + spora (seed, sowing).
