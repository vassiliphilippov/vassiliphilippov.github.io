custody

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌstədi / (AmE), / ˈkʌstədi / (BrE)
the legal right or duty to care for and control someone, especially a child, after a divorce or separation; guardianship.
legal guardianship, especially of a child

Examples

After the divorce, she was granted custody of their two children.
The parents are fighting over custody in court.
He lost custody because he failed to provide a safe home environment.

Synset Examples

my fate is in your hands
too much power in the president's hands
your guests are now in my custody
the mother was awarded custody of the children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guardianship - the position of being legally responsible for someone else, especially a child

Etymology

From Latin 'custodia' (guarding, custody), from 'custos' (guardian).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʌstədi / (AmE), / ˈkʌstədi / (BrE)
the state of being kept in detention by the police or authorities, especially while awaiting trial
detention by police or authorities

Examples

The suspect remains in custody while the investigation continues.
After his arrest, he was taken into police custody.
She was released from custody after posting bail.

Synset Examples

the suspect is in custody

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detention - the act of detaining someone, especially by the authorities
  • Synonym: arrest - the act of seizing someone by legal authority
  • Antonym: release - the act of setting someone free from custody or detention

Etymology

From Latin custodia (guarding, keeping) via Old French and Middle English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01149860-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈkʌstədi / (AmE), / ˈkʌstədi / (BrE)
the state of being kept in detention or confinement, especially by the police or legal authorities
detention or confinement by authorities

Examples

The suspect was taken into custody shortly after the incident.
After several hours in custody, she was released without charge.
The police held him in custody while they conducted their investigation.

Synset Examples

his detention was politically motivated
the prisoner is on hold
he is in the custody of police

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detention - the act of detaining or holding someone in custody
  • Antonym: release - the action of setting someone free from custody

Etymology

From Latin 'custodia' (guard, protection), from 'custos' (guardian).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14023148-n
Synset POS: n
