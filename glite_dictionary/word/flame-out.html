flame-out

1. NOUN

/ ˈfleɪmˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈfleɪmˌaʊt / (BrE)
the failure of a jet engine caused by interrupted fuel supply or faulty combustion, resulting in loss of power
jet engine failure due to fuel or combustion issues

Examples

The pilot reacted quickly after the sudden flame-out of the engine.
A flame-out at high altitude can be extremely dangerous if not managed properly.
Engineers are investigating the cause of yesterday’s flame-out during the test flight.

2. NOUN

/ fleɪmˌaʊt / (AmE), / fleɪmˌaʊt / (BrE)
A sudden, complete, and often conspicuous failure, especially after a period of success.
sudden, conspicuous failure

Examples

After years of success, the company's sudden flame-out shocked investors and employees alike.
The politician's campaign ended in a spectacular flame-out after a series of missteps.
Many tech startups experience a rapid rise followed by an equally rapid flame-out.

Synset Examples

the spectacular flame-out of the company's stock cost many people their life savings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: failure - lack of success or the inability to meet expectations

Etymology

Originally from aviation, where 'flameout' referred to the sudden failure of a jet engine, now used figuratively.
