to combust

1. VERB

/ kəmˈbʌst / (AmE), / kəmˈbʌst / (BrE)
to catch fire and burn; to undergo combustion
catch fire and burn

Examples

If the chemicals are mixed incorrectly, they may combust unexpectedly.
Some gases are able to combust at room temperature.
Dry leaves can easily combust on a hot summer day.

Synset Examples

Maple wood burns well

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burn - to be destroyed or altered by fire
  • Antonym: to extinguish - to cause a fire or light to stop burning

Etymology

From Latin combustus, past participle of comburere (to burn up).
