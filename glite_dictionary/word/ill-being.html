ill-being

1. NOUN

/ ɪlˈbiɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪlˈbiːɪŋ / (BrE)
A state of lacking happiness, health, or prosperity; the condition of being unwell or deprived of well-being.
lack of happiness or health

Examples

Many factors, such as poverty and isolation, can contribute to a person's ill-being.
Chronic stress has a profound effect on both mental well-being and ill-being.
Policies that ignore environmental issues may ultimately lead to greater social ill-being in the community.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: well-being - a state of being comfortable, healthy, or happy

Etymology

Formed by prefixing 'ill-' (negative) to 'being,' after the pattern of 'well-being.'
