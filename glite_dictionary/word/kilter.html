kilter

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪltɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɪltə / (BrE)
the proper or usual condition or order; good working order
proper condition or order

Examples

After the renovations, the office was finally back in kilter and running smoothly again.
His sleeping schedule is completely out of kilter since starting the night shift.
It's important to keep your finances in kilter to avoid unnecessary stress.

Synset Examples

out of kilter
in good kilter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: order - a state in which everything is in its correct or appropriate place
  • Antonym: disorder - a state of confusion or lack of order

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɪltər / (AmE), / ˈkɪltə / (BrE)
a state of proper or working order; condition in which things are functioning well
state of working order or condition

Examples

After the long flight, my sleep schedule was out of kilter for days.
Everything in the factory is finally in kilter again after the repairs.
If your plans seem out of kilter, maybe it's time to rethink your approach.

Synset Examples

out of kilter
in good kilter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: order - proper arrangement or working condition
  • Antonym: disorder - state of confusion or not functioning correctly

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from dialectal English kelter or kilter (good condition), 17th century.
