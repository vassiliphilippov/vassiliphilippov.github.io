armorer

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrmərər / (AmE), / ˈɑːmərə / (BrE)
a person or company that manufactures or supplies firearms and other weapons
manufacturer or supplier of firearms

Examples

The armorer was responsible for making the rifles used by the soldiers.
After years of apprenticeship, he became an expert armorer known for his fine craftsmanship.
The museum displayed several antique guns created by a renowned 18th-century armorer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gunsmith - a person who repairs, modifies, or builds guns

Etymology

From Old French 'armurier', from 'armure' (armor).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑrmərər / (AmE), / ˈɑːmərə / (BrE)
A person skilled in making armor or arms, such as weapons or protective equipment.
maker of armor or arms

Examples

The castle employed a skilled armorer to craft suits of armor for its knights.
A medieval armorer would spend years perfecting the techniques to create strong, flexible chain mail.
She visited a local armorer to learn about the ancient process of forging swords.

Synset Examples

a sword made by a famous English armorer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gunsmith - a person who makes or repairs firearms

Etymology

From Old French 'armurier', from 'arme' (weapon), from Latin 'arma'.

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrmɚɚ / (AmE), / ˈɑːmərə / (BrE)
a person, especially in the military, responsible for maintaining and repairing small arms and other weapons
military weapons maintenance specialist

Examples

The armorer inspected every rifle before the soldiers went on patrol.
After several hours at the range, the armorer cleaned and serviced all the firearms.
As an armorer, he was responsible for ensuring all weapons were safe to use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weaponsmith - a person who makes or repairs weapons

Etymology

From Old French 'armurier', from 'armure' (armor).
