amidships

1. ADV

/ əˈmɪdˌʃɪps / (AmE), / əˈmɪdʃɪps / (BrE)
in or toward the middle part of a ship; near the center of a vessel
near the middle of a ship

Examples

The crew gathered amidships to prepare for docking.
Water began to leak amidships during the storm.
The dining hall is located amidships for better stability.

Synset Examples

in the late 19th century, engines were placed in front, amidships, and at the rear

Related Terms

  • Antonym: aft - towards the rear of a ship
  • Antonym: bow - the front section of a ship
  • Synonym: midship - the middle part of a ship (noun and adjective)

Etymology

From 'amid' + 'ships', dating from early 17th century nautical usage.
