eparchy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛpɑːrki / (AmE), / ˈɛpɑːki / (BrE)
A district under the pastoral care of a bishop in the Eastern Orthodox Church; an ecclesiastical province or diocese.
Eastern Orthodox Church diocese

Examples

The new bishop was assigned to lead the eparchy in the northern region.
Several parishes within the eparchy gathered for the annual festival.
He has served as a priest in the eparchy for over twenty years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diocese - a district under the care of a Christian bishop

Etymology

From Greek eparchia, meaning 'province' or 'district'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛˌpɑrki / (AmE), / ˈɛpɑːki / (BrE)
A province or administrative district, originally in ancient Greece or later in certain Eastern Christian churches.
province or district (esp. ancient Greece, church)

Examples

The bishop was responsible for overseeing the eparchy and its clergy.
In ancient Greece, each eparchy had its own local government.
The church council met to discuss important matters concerning the eparchy.

Etymology

From Greek eparchia (province), from epi- (upon) + archein (to rule).
