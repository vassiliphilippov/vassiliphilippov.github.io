Cassite

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæsˌaɪt / (AmE), / ˈkæs.aɪt / (BrE)
a member of an ancient people who ruled Babylonia between about 1600 and 1200 BC
member of ancient Babylonia-ruling people

Examples

The ancient Cassite dynasty contributed significantly to Babylonian history.
Archaeologists discovered artifacts belonging to the Cassite period in Mesopotamia.
The Cassite people introduced new administrative systems to Babylonia.

Etymology

From French 'Cassite', from Akkadian 'Kassītu', referring to the people.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæs.aɪt / (AmE), / ˈkas.aɪt / (BrE)
an ancient language spoken by the Kassite people in Mesopotamia, now extinct and unattested except for some names and words
ancient language of the Kassites

Examples

Little is known about the Cassite language, as only a few words survive in texts.
Scholars debate the classification of Cassite within ancient Near Eastern languages.
The Kassites brought the Cassite language to prominence after conquering Babylon.

Etymology

From Kassite, the name of the people who spoke the language.
