Raphidiidae

1. NOUN

/ ræˌfɪdiˈɪdiː / (AmE), / ræˌfɪdiˈɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of insects known as snakeflies, belonging to the suborder Raphidioptera, characterized by their elongated prothorax and predatory larvae.
family of insects: snakeflies

Examples

Researchers collected several species from the family Raphidiidae in forested mountain regions.
The elongated neck of Raphidiidae distinguishes them from other similar insects.
Studies of Raphidiidae contribute to our understanding of insect evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snakefly - common name for any insect of the family Raphidiidae

Etymology

From New Latin Raphidiidae, based on the type genus Raphidia.

2. NOUN

/ ræˌfɪdiˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / ræˌfɪdiˈaɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of insects in the order Raphidioptera, commonly known as snakeflies, characterized by their elongated prothorax and predatory larvae
a family of insects (snakeflies)

Examples

The Raphidiidae are known for their distinctive elongated necks.
Fossils show that Raphidiidae have existed since at least the Jurassic period.
Researchers study Raphidiidae to understand the evolution of predatory insects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snakefly - any insect of the family Raphidiidae

Etymology

From the type genus Raphidia (from Greek rhaphis, 'needle') + -idae, a zoological family suffix.
