croton

1. NOUN

/ ˈkroʊ.t̬ən / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊ.tən / (BrE)
a tropical plant with brightly colored, variegated leaves, often grown as a houseplant or ornamental shrub
tropical plant with colorful leaves

Examples

Many people choose the croton for its vibrant foliage and easy care indoors.
The garden was filled with different varieties of croton, each displaying striking colors.
She placed a potted croton on her desk to brighten up the office.

Etymology

From Latin *croton*, the name of a plant genus.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkroʊtn̩ / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊtən / (BrE)
a tropical shrub of the genus Croton, native to Asia, known as the source of croton oil
a tropical Asian shrub; source of croton oil

Examples

The seeds of the croton are used to extract croton oil, which has medicinal applications.
Botanists studied the growth patterns of the croton in its native habitat.
They planted a croton in their greenhouse to observe its vivid foliage.

Etymology

From Latin *croton*, the plant name, from Greek *krotōn* (castor oil plant).
