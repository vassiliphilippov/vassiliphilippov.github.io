achene

1. NOUN

/ əˈkiːn / (AmE), / əˈkiːn / (BrE)
a small, dry fruit that does not open to release the seed, with the seed separate from the fruit wall
small dry fruit with separate seed

Examples

The sunflower produces achenes that are commonly mistaken for seeds.
Botanists examined the structure of the achene under a microscope.
The dandelion's familiar tuft helps to disperse its achene on the wind.

Etymology

From French 'akène', from Greek 'a-' (not) + 'khaínein' (to gape), referring to the fruit not opening.
