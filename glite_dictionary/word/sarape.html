sarape

1. NOUN

/ səˈrɑːpeɪ / (AmE), / səˈrɑːpeɪ / (BrE)
A long, brightly colored woolen shawl or blanket, often with fringed ends, traditionally worn by Mexican men.
Mexican brightly colored shawl or blanket

Examples

He draped the sarape over his shoulders to keep warm during the chilly evening.
Tourists admired the colorful patterns woven into each traditional sarape at the market.
The artist depicted a man in a sarape sitting beneath a wide-brimmed sombrero.

Etymology

From Spanish 'sarape', likely of Nahuatl origin.
