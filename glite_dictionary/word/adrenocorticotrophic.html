adrenocorticotrophic

1. ADJ

/ əˌdriːnoʊˌkɔːrtɪkoʊˈtroʊfɪk / (AmE), / əˌdriːnəʊˌkɔːtɪkəʊˈtrɒfɪk / (BrE)
stimulating or acting on the adrenal cortex, especially concerning hormones or agents that control the activity of the adrenal cortex
stimulating the adrenal cortex
medical

Examples

The pituitary gland secretes an adrenocorticotrophic hormone that influences the adrenal cortex.
Treatment with adrenocorticotrophic agents can help manage certain endocrine disorders.
Researchers measured the adrenocorticotrophic response following administration of the test compound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adrenocorticotropic - having a stimulating effect on the adrenal cortex (alternative spelling)

Etymology

From adreno- (relating to the adrenal glands) + cortico- (relating to the cortex) + -trophic (having an effect on growth or activity), from Greek 'trophē' (nourishment).
