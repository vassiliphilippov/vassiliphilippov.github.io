underage

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌndɚˈeɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈeɪdʒ / (BrE)
(of a person) not having reached the age legally required for something, especially adulthood or a particular activity.
not of legal age; a minor

Examples

The bar was fined for serving alcohol to underage customers.
You must be over 18 to enter; underage guests are not permitted.
The movie is rated R, and underage viewers will need parental guidance.

Synset Examples

minor children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minor - a person under the age of legal adulthood
  • Antonym: of age - having reached the age at which a person is legally recognized as an adult

Etymology

Formed from 'under' + 'age', meaning 'below a certain age'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01474869-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌndɚˈeɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈeɪdʒ / (BrE)
(of a person) legally too young to do something, such as vote, drink alcohol, or marry.
too young by law for something

Examples

The club was fined for serving alcohol to an underage customer.
She was disqualified from the competition because she was underage.
It is illegal for underage individuals to buy cigarettes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minor - a person under the age of legal responsibility or adulthood
  • Antonym: of age - legally old enough; having reached the age at which a person has full legal rights

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'under-' (meaning 'below') and 'age'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00730925-s
Synset POS: s
