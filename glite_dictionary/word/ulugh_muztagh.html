Ulugh Muztagh

1. PROPN

/ uːˈluː muːzˈtɑːg / (AmE), / uːˈluː muːzˈtɑːg / (BrE)
a mountain in the Kunlun Mountains of China, reaching 25,340 feet (7,720 meters) in elevation
mountain in the Kunlun range, China

Examples

Few mountaineers have attempted to climb Ulugh Muztagh due to its remote location.
The breathtaking view from the summit of Ulugh Muztagh attracts adventurous travelers.
Researchers study the glaciers on Ulugh Muztagh to better understand climate change in the region.

Etymology

From Turkic languages, where 'ulugh' means 'great' and 'muztagh' means 'ice mountain'.
