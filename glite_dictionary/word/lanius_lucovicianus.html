Lanius lucovicianus

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæniəs ˌluːkoʊˌvɪʃiːˈænəs / (AmE), / ˈlæniəs ˌluːkəʊˌvɪʃɪˈænəs / (BrE)
A species of shrike found in the southeastern United States, known for its black bands around the eyes.
bird species; loggerhead shrike

Examples

The Lanius lucovicianus is easily recognized by the broad black mask across its eyes.
Researchers tracked the migration patterns of Lanius lucovicianus throughout southern regions.
Birdwatchers often spot the Lanius lucovicianus perched on barbed wire searching for prey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loggerhead shrike - Common name for Lanius lucovicianus, a North American shrike

Etymology

From Latin: genus 'Lanius' (butcher), species 'lucovicianus' (relating to Louisiana).
