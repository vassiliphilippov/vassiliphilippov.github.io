Triplochiton

1. NOUN

/ trɪpˌloʊkɪˈtaɪtən / (AmE), / trɪpˌlɒkɪˈtaɪtən / (BrE)
A small genus of tropical African trees with leaves resembling those of maple trees.
genus of tropical African trees

Examples

The tall Triplochiton trees dominate large areas of West African rainforest.
Scientists have studied Triplochiton for its valuable lightweight timber.
Leaves of the Triplochiton are often mistaken for those of maple due to their similar shape.

Etymology

From Greek 'triploos' (triple) and 'chiton' (covering), referring to features of the seed or fruit.
