vituperative

1. ADJ

/ vɪˈtuːpəˌreɪtɪv / (AmE), / vɪˈtjuːpərətɪv / (BrE)
containing or expressed in bitter and abusive language; harshly critical.
harshly abusive or critical

Examples

The senator launched a vituperative attack on his opponent during the debate.
Her vituperative comments shocked everyone at the meeting.
Online forums can sometimes turn vituperative when discussions become heated.

Synset Examples

his scathing remarks about silly lady novelists
her vituperative railing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scathing - bitterly severe or critical

Etymology

From Latin 'vituperativus', from 'vituperare' (to blame, censure).
