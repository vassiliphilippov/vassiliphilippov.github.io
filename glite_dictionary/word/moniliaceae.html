Moniliaceae

1. NOUN

/ moʊˌnɪliˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / məʊˌnɪlɪˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of imperfect fungi having white or brightly colored hyphae, with spores produced directly on the mycelium rather than in fruiting bodies.
a family of imperfect fungi
medical

Examples

The Moniliaceae include several species that are common plant pathogens.
Identification of Moniliaceae often requires careful microscopic examination.
Members of the Moniliaceae can produce brightly colored spores on decaying fruit.

Etymology

From modern Latin Moniliaceae, based on the type genus Monilia.

2. NOUN

/ moʊˌnɪliˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / məʊˌnɪliˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of imperfect fungi with white or brightly colored hyphae and spores produced directly on the mycelium, not aggregated in fruiting bodies
a family of fungi lacking fruiting bodies

Examples

Several species within the Moniliaceae are studied for their role in plant diseases.
The Moniliaceae family includes fungi that produce spores directly on the mycelium.
Researchers identified a new member of the Moniliaceae in the forest sample.

Etymology

From genus name Monilia, from Latin monile (necklace), referring to the chain-like formation of spores.
