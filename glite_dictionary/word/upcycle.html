to upcycle

1. VERB

/ ˈʌpˌsaɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈʌp.sʌɪ.kəl / (BrE)
to transform a discarded or old product into a new item of higher value or quality, often for a different use
transform old products into better ones

Examples

Many artists upcycle old furniture into unique pieces of art for their studios.
She decided to upcycle her worn-out jeans by turning them into a trendy handbag.
Instead of throwing away the bottles, we upcycled them into colorful flower vases.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to recycle - to process used materials to make them reusable

Etymology

Blend of 'up' and 'recycle', modeled after 'recycle'.
