Dennstaedtia

1. NOUN

/ dɛnˈstɛdtiə / (AmE), / dɛnˈstɛdtiə / (BrE)
A genus of chiefly terrestrial ferns, commonly found in temperate and tropical regions.
a genus of terrestrial ferns

Examples

Dennstaedtia contains several species prized for their delicate, lacy fronds in horticulture.
Botanists have recorded Dennstaedtia ferns in forested areas of Southeast Asia and South America.
Some varieties of Dennstaedtia are known as hay-scented ferns due to their fragrance.

Etymology

Named after German botanist August W. Dennstaedt (1776–1826).

2. NOUN

/ dɛnˈstædiə / (AmE), / dɛnˈstædtiə / (BrE)
A genus of chiefly terrestrial ferns in the Dennstaedtiaceae family, found in tropical and temperate regions.
genus of terrestrial ferns

Examples

The fronds of Dennstaedtia species can be easily recognized by their delicate texture.
Several types of Dennstaedtia are found throughout the forests of Southeast Asia.
Botanists studied Dennstaedtia to understand fern diversity in that region.

Etymology

Named after German botanist August W. Dennstaedt (18th–19th century).
