hotline

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑtˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈhɒtˌlaɪn / (BrE)
A telephone line providing direct and immediate access to help, information, or advice about a specific subject.
direct line for urgent help or information

Examples

If you need immediate support, call the suicide prevention hotline at any hour.
Parents can reach out to the school advice hotline for guidance on remote learning.
The government set up a special hotline to answer questions about the new policy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: helpline - telephone line offering assistance or information

Etymology

Formed from 'hot' plus 'line', with 'hot' suggesting urgency or priority.
