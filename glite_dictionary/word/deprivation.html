deprivation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛprəˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛprɪˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
The state of being kept from possessing or enjoying something necessary; a condition of severe lack, especially of basic needs such as food, shelter, or social contact.
the state of lacking necessities

Examples

Many children in the region suffer from deprivation due to ongoing conflict and instability.
The deprivation of sleep over many nights can seriously impact your health.
Years of economic downturn led to widespread deprivation in the urban areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poverty - the state of being extremely poor
  • Synonym: privation - lack of basic necessities or comforts of life

Etymology

From Medieval Latin deprivatio (a taking away), from Latin deprivare (to deprive).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɛprəˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛprɪˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
The state or process of having something important taken away, especially necessities such as food, money, or rights.
the act of taking away necessities

Examples

Prolonged deprivation of sleep can seriously impact your health.
The citizens protested against the deprivation of their civil rights.
Many children in the region suffer from nutritional deprivation.

Synset Examples

nutritional privation
deprivation of civil rights

Related Terms

  • Synonym: privation - the lack of basic necessities or comforts of life

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'deprivatio' (a removing), from Latin 'deprivare' (to deprive).

3. NOUN

/ ˌdɛprəˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛprɪˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
the lack or loss of something that is considered necessary or desirable, resulting in disadvantage or hardship
state of lacking or losing something important

Examples

The long winter caused serious deprivation for many families.
Sleep deprivation can have negative effects on health and mood.
He grew up in an environment of poverty and deprivation.

Synset Examples

losing him is no great deprivation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: privation - lack of the basic necessities of life
  • Synonym: loss - the state of no longer having something
  • Antonym: abundance - a very large quantity of something

Etymology

From Latin 'deprivatio' (a taking away), from 'deprivare' (to deprive).
