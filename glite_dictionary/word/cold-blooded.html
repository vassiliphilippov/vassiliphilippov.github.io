cold-blooded

1. ADJ

/ ˌkoʊldˈblʌdɪd / (AmE), / ˌkəʊldˈblʌdɪd / (BrE)
(of animals) having a body temperature that varies with the environment, not internally regulated.
having body temperature varying with environment (animals)

Examples

Reptiles like snakes and lizards are cold-blooded animals.
A cold-blooded fish's activity level often depends on the water temperature.
Many amphibians are cold-blooded, so they bask in the sun to warm up.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: warm-blooded - (of animals) having a constant body temperature that is internally regulated

2. ADJ

/ ˌkoʊldˈblʌdɪd / (AmE), / ˌkəʊldˈblʌdɪd / (BrE)
showing or feeling no sympathy or pity; deliberately cruel or callous
deliberately cruel or unfeeling

Examples

The murderer was described as cold-blooded in his disregard for human life.
The crime was a cold-blooded act of violence carried out without remorse.
She showed a cold-blooded determination to succeed, regardless of who got hurt.

Synset Examples

in cold blood
cold-blooded killing
insensate destruction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: callous - emotionally insensitive or unfeeling
  • Antonym: compassionate - showing sympathy and concern for others

Synset POS: s
