pendant

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɛn.dənt / (AmE), / ˈpɛn.dənt / (BrE)
(of an object) hanging down or suspended from above.
hanging or suspended from above

Examples

The chandelier had several pendant crystals that caught and reflected the light.
A pendant branch swayed gently in the breeze above our heads.
Botanists noted the pendant flower clusters on the rare tree species.

Synset Examples

a pendant bunch of grapes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pendent - hanging down; suspended

Etymology

From Latin pendere (to hang).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02359909-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛn.dənt / (AmE), / ˈpɛn.dənt / (BrE)
an ornament or piece of jewelry that hangs from a chain, necklace, or earring
ornament hanging from jewelry

Examples

She wore a beautiful silver pendant on a delicate gold chain.
His gift was a heart-shaped pendant engraved with her initials.
The diamond pendant sparkled as it caught the light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charm - a small ornament worn on a chain or bracelet
  • Synonym: amulet - an ornament or small piece of jewelry thought to give protection against evil or danger

Etymology

From Old French 'pendant', from Latin 'pendere' (to hang).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03914950-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɛn.dənt / (AmE), / ˈpɛn.dənt / (BrE)
a branched and often ornate lighting fixture that hangs from the ceiling
ornate ceiling light fixture

Examples

The antique pendant in the dining room casts a warm glow over the table.
They chose a modern pendant to hang above the kitchen island for both style and light.
An ornate glass pendant graced the entryway of the old mansion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chandelier - a large, decorative hanging light with branches for several bulbs or candles

Etymology

From Old French pendant, present participle of pendre (to hang), from Latin pendēre.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03008889-n
Synset POS: n
