pilothouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪlˌthaʊs / (AmE), / ˈpaɪlˌhaʊs / (BrE)
an enclosed compartment on a ship or boat from which it is navigated
enclosed navigation compartment on a vessel

Examples

The captain remained in the pilothouse during the storm to keep the ship on course.
Visitors were given a tour of the pilothouse, where all the navigation equipment was displayed.
From the pilothouse, you could see the entire harbor stretching before the vessel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wheelhouse - a compartment on a ship where the steering wheel is located

Etymology

Formed from 'pilot' + 'house', referring to a compartment for the pilot of a vessel.
