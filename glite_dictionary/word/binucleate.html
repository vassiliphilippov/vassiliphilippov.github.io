binucleate

1. ADJ

/ baɪˈnjuː.kli.eɪt / (AmE), / baɪˈnjuː.klɪ.eɪt / (BrE)
(of a cell) having two nuclei.
having two cell nuclei
medical

Examples

Some liver cells are naturally binucleate, containing two separate nuclei within a single cell membrane.
The binucleate state is often observed during certain stages of cell division.
Researchers found that binucleate muscle fibers were more common after the treatment.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mononucleate - having a single nucleus
  • Degree Higher: multinucleate - having more than two nuclei

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'bi-' (two) + 'nucleus' (kernel, core) + suffix '-ate'.
