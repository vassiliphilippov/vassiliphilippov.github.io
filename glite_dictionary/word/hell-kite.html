hell-kite

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛlˌkaɪt / (AmE), / ˈhɛlˌkaɪt / (BrE)
A person who is extremely fierce or savage, especially in fighting; used poetically or in older literature.
very fierce or savage fighter (literary/archaic)
archaic

Examples

In the ancient tale, the hell-kite tore through enemy ranks without mercy.
The general was renowned as a hell-kite on the battlefield, feared even by his own troops.
Her fury made her a hell-kite in court, demolishing every argument against her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warrior - a person engaged in or experienced in warfare
  • Antonym: pacifist - a person who believes that war and violence are unjustifiable

Etymology

Formed from 'hell' (the abode of evil) and 'kite' (a predatory bird), notably used by Shakespeare.
