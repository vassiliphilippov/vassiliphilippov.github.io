scriptwriter

1. NOUN

/ ˈskrɪptˌraɪtər / (AmE), / ˈskrɪptˌraɪtə / (BrE)
a person who writes scripts for plays, films, or broadcast programs
writer of scripts for plays or media

Examples

The scriptwriter spent months revising the dialogue to make the film more authentic.
As a successful scriptwriter, she was often invited to collaborate on new television dramas.
Many aspiring scriptwriters attend workshops to learn about screenplay formatting and structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: screenwriter - someone who writes scripts specifically for films
  • Synonym: playwright - a writer of plays
  • Antonym: novelist - a writer of novels

Etymology

Formed from 'script' (from Latin 'scriptum', something written) + 'writer'.
