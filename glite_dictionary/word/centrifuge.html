centrifuge

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛntrəˌfjudʒ / (AmE), / ˈsentrɪfjuːdʒ / (BrE)
a machine that uses rapid spinning to separate substances of different densities, especially particles from a liquid
machine for separating particles by spinning

Examples

The scientist placed the blood samples in the centrifuge to separate the plasma.
After spinning in the centrifuge, the different components of the mixture were clearly visible.
Modern laboratories rely on a centrifuge for many of their tests.

Etymology

From French 'centrifuge', from Latin 'centrum' (center) and 'fugere' (to flee).

2. VERB

/ ˈsɛntrəˌfjudʒ / (AmE), / ˈsɛntrɪˌfjuːdʒ / (BrE)
to rotate something at high speed in order to separate liquids from solids, or different substances from each other, by centrifugal force
to separate by spinning at high speed

Examples

The lab technician will centrifuge the samples before analyzing them.
We centrifuged the blood to separate the plasma from the cells.
Before testing the mixture, they are centrifuging it for ten minutes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spin - to turn around quickly, especially in a circular motion

Etymology

From French *centrifuger*, from 'centrifuge', meaning 'that drives away from the center'.
