Fasciolidae

1. NOUN

/ fæsiˈɑlɪdiː / (AmE), / fæsɪˈɒlɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of parasitic flatworms (trematodes) that includes several genera, notably Fasciola, many of which infect the livers of various mammals.
family of parasitic flatworms (trematodes)
medical

Examples

Livestock are sometimes infected by trematodes belonging to the Fasciolidae family.
The life cycle of Fasciolidae species often involves snails as intermediate hosts.
Veterinarians monitor farm animals for diseases caused by Fasciolidae parasites.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fasciola - a genus of parasitic flatworms within Fasciolidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus 'Fasciola' plus the family suffix '-idae'.

2. NOUN

/ fæʃiˈɑːlɪˌdiː / (AmE), / fæsiˈɒlɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of parasitic flatworms (trematodes) that includes several genera, notably Fasciola, affecting the livers of various mammals
family of parasitic liver flukes

Examples

Several species in the Fasciolidae family are responsible for infecting the livers of livestock.
Scientists studied the life cycle of Fasciolidae to help control parasitic diseases in cattle.
The Fasciolidae are known for their complex development involving snails as intermediate hosts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fasciola - a genus of parasitic flatworms in the Fasciolidae family

Etymology

From New Latin, from the genus name 'Fasciola' and the suffix '-idae' (used for animal families).
