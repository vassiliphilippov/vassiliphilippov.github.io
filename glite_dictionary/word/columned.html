columned

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɑl.əmd / (AmE), / ˈkɒl.əmnd / (BrE)
having one or more columns, or resembling a column, especially used to describe buildings or objects with column-like features
having columns or resembling a column

Examples

The museum had a grand columned entrance that impressed all its visitors.
They admired the elegant white-columned mansion on the hill.
The architect designed a columned walkway lined with marble pillars.

Synset Examples

a columned portico
trees with columned trunks
white-columned houses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: columnar - having the shape or form of a column

Etymology

From 'column' + '-ed', forming adjectives indicating possession or likeness.
