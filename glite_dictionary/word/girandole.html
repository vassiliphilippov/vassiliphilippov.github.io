girandole

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪrəndoʊl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪərəndəʊl / (BrE)
an ornate branched candlestick or chandelier, often featuring a mirror to reflect the light
ornate branched candle holder, often with a mirror

Examples

The antique girandole on the mantelpiece gave the living room a touch of elegance.
He restored the elaborate girandole, carefully polishing the mirror and gold leafed arms.
A pair of 18th-century girandoles decorated the grand hallway, reflecting candlelight around the space.

Etymology

From French 'girandole', from Italian 'girandola' (small revolving object or firework), related to 'girare' (to turn).
