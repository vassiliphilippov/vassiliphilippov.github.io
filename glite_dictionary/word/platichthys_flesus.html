Platichthys flesus

1. NOUN

/ plætɪˌkaɪθiz ˈflɛsəs / (AmE), / ˌplætɪˈkɪθɪs ˈflɛsəs / (BrE)
A species of flatfish, commonly called the European flounder, found in European coastal waters and valued as a food fish.
a European species of flatfish

Examples

Fishermen in the Baltic Sea often catch Platichthys flesus in their nets.
The population of Platichthys flesus has declined due to pollution in European waters.
Chefs prepare Platichthys flesus in a variety of traditional European dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flounder - a general name for several species of flatfish, including Platichthys flesus

Etymology

From Greek 'platys' (flat) + 'ichthys' (fish); modern scientific Latin naming.
