aeolotropic

1. ADJ

/ iˌɑlɵˈtrɑpɪk / (AmE), / iˌɒlɵˈtrɒpɪk / (BrE)
(of a material or property) having different values when measured along different axes; exhibiting physical properties that vary with direction.
having properties that vary with direction

Examples

Crystals can be aeolotropic, meaning their conductivity varies depending on the direction measured.
Due to its aeolotropic nature, this composite material behaves differently along its length than across its width.
The engineer explained that the aeolotropic properties of the material must be considered in the design process.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: isotropic - having identical properties in all directions

Etymology

From Greek aiolos (quick-moving, changeable) + trope (turn).
