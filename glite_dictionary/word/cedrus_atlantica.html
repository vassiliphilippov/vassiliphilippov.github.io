Cedrus atlantica

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːdrəs ætˈlæntɪkə / (AmE), / ˈsiːdrəs ətˈlæntɪkə / (BrE)
A tall evergreen tree native to the Atlas Mountains of North Africa, with blue-green leaves, often planted as an ornamental.
Atlas cedar tree species

Examples

The Cedrus atlantica is prized for its striking blue-green foliage and stately form in parks.
Many gardeners choose Cedrus atlantica as a centerpiece in large landscapes.
Young Cedrus atlantica trees require regular watering to establish healthy growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Atlas cedar - common name for Cedrus atlantica
  • Degree Higher: cedar - any of various coniferous trees of the genus Cedrus

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'cedar of the Atlas (mountains)'.
