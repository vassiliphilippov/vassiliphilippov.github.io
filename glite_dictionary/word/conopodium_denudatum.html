Conopodium denudatum

1. NOUN

/ ˌkoʊ.nəˈpoʊ.di.əm dɪˈnuː.də.təm / (AmE), / ˌkɒ.nəˈpəʊ.dɪ.əm dɪˈnjuː.də.təm / (BrE)
A common European plant whose edible tubers have a flavor similar to roasted chestnuts.
European plant with edible chestnut-flavored tubers

Examples

The tubers of Conopodium denudatum are sought after for their sweet, nutty taste.
In rural parts of Europe, Conopodium denudatum has long been foraged as a wild food.
Many gardeners cultivate Conopodium denudatum for its attractive foliage and edible underground parts.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; genus name Conopodium + species epithet denudatum, meaning 'bare' or 'naked' in Latin.
