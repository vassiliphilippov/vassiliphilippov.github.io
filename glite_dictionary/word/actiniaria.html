Actiniaria

1. NOUN

/ ækˌtɪniˈɛəriə / (AmE), / ækˌtɪnɪˈeəriə / (BrE)
the order of marine animals commonly known as sea anemones, characterized by a polyp body form and tentacles surrounding the mouth
order of animals: sea anemones

Examples

The order Actiniaria includes many species known as sea anemones.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Actiniaria during their deep-sea expedition.
Actiniaria are important for understanding evolutionary relationships among Cnidaria.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea anemone - a brightly colored marine animal with a polyp body and tentacles

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek aktis (ray) + -aria (relating to) for their radiating structure.
