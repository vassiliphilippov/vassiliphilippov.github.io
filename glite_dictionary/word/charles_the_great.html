Charles the Great

1. PROPN

/ ˈtʃɑrlz ðə ɡreɪt / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːlz ðə ɡreɪt / (BrE)
(742–814) King of the Franks and Holy Roman Emperor, known for uniting much of western and central Europe during the early Middle Ages.
King of the Franks and Holy Roman Emperor (742–814)

Examples

Charles the Great expanded his empire across much of Western Europe during his reign.
Historians often credit Charles the Great with laying the foundations for modern Europe.
The coronation of Charles the Great as Holy Roman Emperor marked a significant moment in history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Charlemagne - Alternative name, especially in French and English histories, for Charles the Great
