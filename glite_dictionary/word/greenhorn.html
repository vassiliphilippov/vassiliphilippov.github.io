greenhorn

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡriːnˌhɔrn / (AmE), / ˈɡriːnhɔːn / (BrE)
a person, especially a youth, who is awkward or inexperienced in a particular activity or general situation
an inexperienced or awkward person

Examples

As a greenhorn, she struggled to keep up with the team's fast pace.
When I started my first job, I felt like a complete greenhorn.
The seasoned hikers could spot a greenhorn by the way he packed his gear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: novice - a person new to a job or activity
  • Synonym: rookie - a person who is new to an organization or activity
  • Antonym: expert - a person with a lot of skill or knowledge in a particular area

Etymology

Originally an American English term from 'green' (inexperienced) + 'horn' (metaphoric reference to young animals with new, soft horns), first used in the early 19th century.
