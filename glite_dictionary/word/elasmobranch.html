elasmobranch

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlæzməˌbræŋk / (AmE), / ɪˈlasməˌbraŋk / (BrE)
Any of a group of cartilaginous fishes of the class Chondrichthyes, including sharks, rays, and skates, characterized by having a skeleton made of cartilage and placoid scales.
cartilaginous fish group (sharks, rays, skates)

Examples

Sharks and rays are both considered elasmobranchs in zoological classification.
The skeletons of elasmobranchs are made of cartilage rather than bone.
Research on elasmobranch populations helps scientists monitor the health of ocean ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shark - large predatory fish of the cartilaginous class Chondrichthyes
  • Synonym: ray - flat-bodied cartilaginous fish of the class Chondrichthyes

Etymology

From Greek 'elasmos' (plate) + 'branchia' (gills).
