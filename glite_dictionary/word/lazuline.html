lazuline

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæzjʊˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈlæzjʊliːn / (BrE)
A light shade of blue.
a light shade of blue

Examples

The artist chose lazuline for the sky to give it a softer, more tranquil feel.
Her dress was a delicate lazuline, reminiscent of clear summer mornings.
Decorators often recommend lazuline hues to brighten a small room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: azure - a bright blue color, like the sky

Etymology

From French 'lazuline', from Medieval Latin 'lazulinus', from 'lazulum', related to lapis lazuli.
