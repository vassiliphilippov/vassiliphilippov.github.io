monthly

1. ADV

/ ˈmʌnθli / (AmE), / ˈmʌnθli / (BrE)
happening or appearing once a month; every month.
occurring every month

Examples

We schedule team meetings monthly to review our progress.
The magazine is published monthly and covers a wide range of topics.
Rent must be paid monthly by the fifth of each month.

Synset Examples

they meet monthly

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: weekly - occurring once a week
  • Degree Lower: annually - occurring once a year

Etymology

From Old English mōnathlīc (monthly), based on 'month'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00256458-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADJ

/ ˈmʌnθ.li / (AmE), / ˈmʌnθ.li / (BrE)
Happening, done, or produced once every month.
happening every month

Examples

Our rent is due on a monthly basis.
She receives a monthly magazine at her address.
The employees have a monthly meeting to discuss progress.

Synset Examples

monthly payments
the monthly newsletter

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: weekly - happening every week
  • Degree Higher: yearly - happening every year

Etymology

From Middle English monthli, from month + -ly.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01976772-s
Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ ˈmʌnθli / (AmE), / ˈmʌnθli / (BrE)
A magazine or periodical that is published once each month.
periodical published every month

Examples

She looked forward to receiving her favorite monthly in the mail every month.
The science monthly features articles from leading researchers around the world.
Several monthlies rely on subscriptions to fund their in-depth journalism.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: weekly - periodical published every week
  • Degree Higher: quarterly - periodical published every three months

Etymology

From 'month' + '-ly', denoting frequency or timing.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06606709-n
Synset POS: n
