Ulmus hollandica

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌlməs hɒˈlændɪkə / (AmE), / ˈʌlməs hɒˈlændɪkə / (BrE)
A group of hybrid ornamental European elm trees of varying size, grown as shade trees.
hybrid European elm tree

Examples

Several mature Ulmus hollandica trees provide ample shade in the city park.
The horticulturist explained that Ulmus hollandica cultivars are prized for their adaptability.
A disease-resistant variety of Ulmus hollandica has been developed for urban landscaping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elm - a type of deciduous tree with serrated leaves and samaras

Etymology

From Latin, combining 'Ulmus' (elm) and 'hollandica' (of Holland), indicating its Dutch botanical origins.
