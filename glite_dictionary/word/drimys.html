Drimys

1. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪmɪs / (AmE), / ˈdrɪmɪs / (BrE)
A genus of shrubs and trees native to the Southern Hemisphere, known for their aromatic foliage.
genus of aromatic shrubs and trees

Examples

The leaves of Drimys are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties.
Several species of Drimys can be found in the forests of South America and Australasia.
Botanists are interested in Drimys for its adaptation to cool, temperate climates.

Etymology

From Greek 'drimys' meaning 'pungent, acrid', referring to the taste or smell of the foliage.
