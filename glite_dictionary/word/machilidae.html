Machilidae

1. NOUN

/ məˈkɪlɪˌdiː / (AmE), / məˈkɪlɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of primitive, wingless insects known as jumping bristletails, characterized by a long body and tail-like appendages.
family of jumping bristletail insects

Examples

The family Machilidae includes several species of jumping bristletails found in leaf litter and under stones.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Machilidae during their fieldwork in the forest.
Because Machilidae are sensitive to humidity, they are mostly found in damp habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bristletail - a primitive, wingless insect with bristle-like appendages, including members of Machilidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus name Machilis + -idae (a zoological suffix for families).

2. NOUN

/ məˈkɪlɪˌdiː / (AmE), / məˈkɪlɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of primitive, wingless insects known as jumping bristletails, characterized by their elongated bodies and long bristles at the end of the abdomen.
family of jumping bristletail insects

Examples

The Machilidae are often found living under stones in damp environments.
Scientists study the unique jumping mechanism of insects in the Machilidae family.
Members of the Machilidae are considered living fossils due to their ancient lineage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jumping bristletail - primitive, wingless insect with long tail bristles

Etymology

From New Latin Machilidae, based on the type genus Machilis.
