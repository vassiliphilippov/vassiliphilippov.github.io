Lactuca sativa asparagina

1. NOUN

/ lækˈtuːkə səˈtaɪvə æsˌpærəˈdʒaɪnə / (AmE), / lækˈtjuːkə səˈteɪvə æsˌparəˈdʒaɪnə / (BrE)
a variety of lettuce cultivated chiefly for its edible thick stems rather than its leaves
lettuce grown for edible stems

Examples

Lactuca sativa asparagina is commonly used in Asian cuisine for its crisp, tender stems.
Farmers in some regions grow Lactuca sativa asparagina instead of regular lettuce to diversify their crops.
In the market, Lactuca sativa asparagina is often sold under the common name “celtuce.”

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celtuce - another name for Lactuca sativa asparagina, the stem lettuce

Etymology

Scientific binomial name from Latin, with 'Lactuca sativa' meaning cultivated lettuce and 'asparagina' referencing a resemblance to asparagus in its edible stem.
