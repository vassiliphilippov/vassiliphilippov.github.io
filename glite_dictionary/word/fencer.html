fencer

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛnsər / (AmE), / ˈfɛnsə / (BrE)
a person who is skilled in or practises the sport of fencing, typically with a sword such as a foil, epee, or sabre
person skilled in fencing (sport)

Examples

The young fencer impressed the judges with her agility and precision.
As a competitive fencer, he trains six days a week to improve his technique.
The Olympic fencer won a silver medal in the team event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fencing - the sport of fighting with swords such as foils, epees, or sabres

Etymology

From Middle English *fensour*, from Old French *defenser* (to defend), related to 'fence'.
