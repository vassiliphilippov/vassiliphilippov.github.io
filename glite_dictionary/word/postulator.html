postulator

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊstjəˈleɪtər / (AmE), / ˌpɒstjʊˈleɪtə / (BrE)
(in the Roman Catholic Church) a person who officially proposes or supports a candidate for beatification or canonization.
Catholic official proposing sainthood

Examples

The postulator gathered evidence to support the canonization of the local priest.
During the process, the postulator interviewed witnesses who attested to the candidate's miracles.
A new postulator was appointed to advance the cause of the saint.

Etymology

From Latin postulator (one who asks or demands), from postulare (to demand or ask).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊstjəˌleɪtər / (AmE), / ˈpɒstjʊleɪtə / (BrE)
a person who assumes or proposes something as a basis for reasoning or argument
one who puts forward an assumption or postulate

Examples

The postulator of the theory assumed several unproven premises.
As the postulator in the debate, she established the foundation for further discussion.
A skilled postulator can make a weak argument appear stronger by framing the right assumptions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assumer - a person who assumes something as true
  • Synonym: postulate - to suggest or assume the existence or truth of something as a basis for reasoning

Etymology

From Latin postulator (one who claims or assumes), from postulare (to demand, claim).
