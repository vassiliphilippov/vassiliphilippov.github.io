mismatch

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪsˌmætʃ / (AmE), / ˈmɪsˌmatʃ / (BrE)
A combination of people or things that do not go well together or are not suitable for each other.
a bad or unsuitable match

Examples

The color scheme was a mismatch that clashed with the overall decor.
There was a clear mismatch between the candidate's skills and the job requirements.
Their personalities were enough of a mismatch that they decided not to date again.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: match - a suitable pair or partnership
  • Synonym: incompatibility - the state of being unable to exist or work together in harmony

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'mis-' (wrongly) and 'match' (to pair together).

2. VERB

/ ˌmɪsˈmætʃ / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˈmætʃ / (BrE)
to match badly or to pair things or people that do not go well together
to match things or people badly

Examples

She accidentally mismatched her socks this morning before work.
The decorator warned us not to mismatch colors when choosing paint for the room.
If you mismatch the plugs and outlets, the devices might not work.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: match - to pair or put together things that go well together

Etymology

Formed from 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'match'.
