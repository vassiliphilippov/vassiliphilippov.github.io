innocently

1. ADV

/ ˈɪnəsəntli / (AmE), / ˈɪnəsəntli / (BrE)
in a manner showing a lack of guilt, knowledge, or malice; in a naively honest or harmless way
in an innocent or harmless way

Examples

The child looked up innocently when asked about the broken vase.
She innocently admitted her mistake, not realizing its seriousness.
He innocently believed everything he was told.

Synset Examples

she smiled at him innocently

Related Terms

  • Antonym: guiltily - in a manner showing guilt

Etymology

From 'innocent' + '-ly'; 'innocent' from Latin *innocens* (harmless, blameless).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00377640-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ ˈɪnəsəntli / (AmE), / ˈɪnəsəntli / (BrE)
in a way that shows no guilt, wrongdoing, or intention to break the law
without guilt or unlawful intent

Examples

He innocently believed the documents were genuine and signed them without hesitation.
She protested innocently that she knew nothing about the missing money.
They claimed to have acted innocently, with no intention of breaking any rules.

Synset Examples

he claimed to have purchased the contraband innocently

Related Terms

  • Antonym: guiltily - in a way that shows guilt or awareness of wrongdoing

Etymology

From 'innocent' + '-ly'; 'innocent' from Latin 'innocens' (not harming).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00377757-r
Synset POS: r
