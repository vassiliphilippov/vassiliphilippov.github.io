bulla

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊl.ə / (AmE), / ˈbʊl.ə / (BrE)
A round leaden seal attached to a papal bull as a mark of authenticity.
leaden seal on papal documents

Examples

The bulla was still affixed to the ancient papal document, proving its authenticity.
Scholars studied the inscription on the lead bulla to trace the document's origin.
The museum displayed several medieval bullae alongside the original papal decrees.

Etymology

From Latin 'bulla' (bubble, knob, seal), referring to the shape.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʊl.ə / (AmE), / ˈbʊl.ə / (BrE)
(medical) A large blister or bubble-like elevation of the skin filled with serous fluid.
skin blister filled with fluid (medical)
medical

Examples

The patient developed a painful bulla on her heel after wearing new shoes.
A bulla can indicate severe skin damage or an underlying medical condition.
Doctors drained the bulla to relieve pressure and prevent infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blister - a small pocket of fluid within the upper layers of the skin

Etymology

From Latin *bulla* (bubble, knob, boss, amulet).
