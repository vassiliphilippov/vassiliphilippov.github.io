jobbery

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɑːbəri / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒbəri / (BrE)
the practice of using public office or position for personal gain or corrupt advantage
corrupt practices by public officials

Examples

The investigative report exposed widespread jobbery within the department.
Reformers campaigned against jobbery in municipal government.
The public lost trust in officials after repeated scandals of jobbery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corruption - dishonest or fraudulent conduct, especially by those in power

Etymology

From 'job' (piece of work) + '-ery' (denoting actions, practices, or their results), first recorded in the 18th century.
