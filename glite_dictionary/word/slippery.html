slippery

1. ADJ

/ ˈslɪpəri / (AmE), / ˈslɪpəri / (BrE)
likely to cause someone or something to slip or slide because of being smooth, wet, or icy
likely to cause slipping

Examples

Be careful walking on the slippery sidewalk after the rain.
The kitchen floor became slippery when oil spilled on it.
She lost her grip on the slippery bar of soap in the shower.

Synset Examples

slippery sidewalks
a slippery bar of soap
the streets are still slippy from the rain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slick - smooth and slippery, usually due to being wet or oily
  • Antonym: rough - having an uneven or coarse surface; not smooth or slippery

Etymology

From Middle English 'slipperi', from 'slippen' (to slip) + '-y'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈslɪpɚi / (AmE), / ˈslɪpəri / (BrE)
(of a person or thing) not to be relied on or trusted; likely to evade or deceive.
not to be trusted or relied upon

Examples

The lawyer had a slippery reputation, so his clients were always on guard.
She found the politician’s answers slippery and evasive during the interview.
You have to be careful—he’s a slippery character who never gives a straight answer.

Synset Examples

how extraordinarily slippery a liar the camera is

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deceitful - dishonest or misleading; not telling the truth
  • Antonym: trustworthy - able to be relied on as honest or truthful
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02475957-s
Synset POS: s
