Nuphar advena

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuːfɑr ædˈviːnə / (AmE), / ˈnjuːfɑːr ədˈviːnə / (BrE)
A species of water lily native to eastern and central North America, known for its broad leaves and globe-shaped yellow flowers, commonly found in sluggish freshwater or slightly brackish environments.
a North American species of water lily

Examples

The pond was covered with the broad leaves of Nuphar advena, their yellow flowers bobbing in the water.
Researchers studied the habitat of Nuphar advena to understand its role in local ecosystems.
Many nature enthusiasts enjoy photographing the distinctive yellow blooms of Nuphar advena during summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water lily - aquatic plants with broad floating leaves and showy flowers

Etymology

From Latin genus name Nuphar, from Arabic nāfar (type of water lily); species name advena means 'stranger' or 'foreigner' in Latin.
