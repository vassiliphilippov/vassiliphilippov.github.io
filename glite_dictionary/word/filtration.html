filtration

1. NOUN

/ fɪlˈtreɪʃən / (AmE), / fɪlˈtreɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of passing a liquid or gas through a filter to remove unwanted particles
process of filtering liquids or gases

Examples

The water purification system uses filtration to remove impurities and contaminants.
Air filtration is essential in operating rooms to maintain a sterile environment.
The laboratory conducted tests on the effectiveness of different filtration methods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filtering - the act of removing unwanted substances from something

Etymology

From Medieval Latin filtratio, from Latin filtrum (felt used as a filter).

2. NOUN

/ fɪlˈtreɪʃən / (AmE), / fɪlˈtreɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of separating solid matter from a liquid or gas by passing it through a filter
separation by passing through a filter

Examples

The filtration of drinking water removes harmful contaminants.
Our laboratory uses a special system for rapid filtration of samples.
During filtration, the residue remains on the filter paper while the liquid passes through.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filtering - the action or process of passing something through a filter

Etymology

From Medieval Latin filtratio, from Latin filtrum (felt used as a filter).
