Sciurus griseus

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈjʊərəs ɡraɪˈsiːəs / (AmE), / saɪˈjʊərəs ɡrɪˈsiːəs / (BrE)
A large gray squirrel native to the far western regions of the United States, also known as the western gray squirrel.
a large gray squirrel of the west U.S.

Examples

The Sciurus griseus is easily recognized by its bushy tail and silvery-gray fur.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect populations of Sciurus griseus in California.
Unlike the eastern gray squirrel, Sciurus griseus is native to the far western United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: western gray squirrel - common name for Sciurus griseus

Etymology

From Latin: 'sciurus' (squirrel) and 'griseus' (gray).
