2. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːpəl / (AmE), / ˈpɜːpəl / (BrE)
(historical) The position or status of imperial power, especially in ancient Rome.
imperial status in ancient Rome

Examples

His family was born to the purple, granting him privileges from birth.
After the emperor's abdication, his nephew ascended to the purple.
The general was always ambitious for the purple but never dared stage a coup.

Synset Examples

he was born to the purple

Related Terms

  • Synonym: throne - the symbolic seat of power of a monarch or ruler

Etymology

From Latin 'purpura', referring both to the purple dye and to robes worn by Roman emperors, hence imperial authority.

5. NOUN

/ ˈpɜr.pəl / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.pəl / (BrE)
a color intermediate between red and blue, typically associated with royalty or luxury

Examples

She wore a beautiful purple dress to the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violet - a bluish-purple color, lighter than purple
  • Degree Lower: red - a primary color at one end of the spectrum next to orange and opposite violet
  • Degree Higher: blue - a primary color between green and violet in the spectrum

Etymology

From Old English 'purpul', from Latin 'purpura' (purple dye), from Greek 'porphyra' (purple fish, dye).

7. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːpəl / (AmE), / ˈpɜːpəl / (BrE)
The official dress worn by a cardinal in the Roman Catholic Church, traditionally of a deep violet or Tyrian purple color.
official dress of a cardinal (Roman Catholic)

Examples

The new cardinal wore his purple with great dignity during the ceremony.
Traditionally, the purple symbolizes the authority and rank of a cardinal in the Church.
After the investiture, the bishop was seen admiring the cardinal's purple robes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: scarlet - official dress of certain other church officials

Etymology

From Latin 'purpura' (purple dye, shellfish producing purple dye), referring to garments colored with Tyrian purple.
