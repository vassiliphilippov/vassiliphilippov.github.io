manner

1. NOUN

/ ˈmænər / (AmE), / ˈmænə / (BrE)
a kind or type; a particular variety or sort of something
a kind or sort

Examples

What manner of creature could make such strange noises?
He was troubled by all manner of doubts.
This manner of mistake is quite common among beginners.

Synset Examples

what manner of man are you?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sort - a category or kind of thing
  • Synonym: type - a category or class of things having common characteristics

Etymology

From Old French 'maniere', from Latin 'manuarius' (belonging to the hand, manual).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmænɚ / (AmE), / ˈmænə / (BrE)
the way in which something is done, happens, or is experienced
the way something is done

Examples

Her calm manner put everyone at ease during the interview.
He apologized in such a casual manner that I wasn't sure he was sincere.
This guide explains the correct manner of handling customer complaints.

Synset Examples

in an abrasive fashion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mode - a particular way of doing something
  • Synonym: style - a particular way of doing or presenting things
  • Synonym: fashion - a particular or characteristic way of acting or behaving

Etymology

From Old French maniere, from Latin manuarius (of the hand, manual), from manus (hand).

3. NOUN

/ ˈmænər / (AmE), / ˈmænə / (BrE)
a way in which a person acts, behaves, or does something
way of acting or behaving

Examples

He spoke in a calm and respectful manner during the meeting.
The teacher explained the rules in a clear manner so everyone could understand.
Her manner of addressing the problem impressed her colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: style - a particular way in which something is done, created, or performed
  • Synonym: fashion - a particular or current way of behaving, dressing, or living
  • Synonym: method - a particular procedure for accomplishing or approaching something

Etymology

From Old French maniere, from Latin manuarius (belonging to the hand), from manus (hand).
