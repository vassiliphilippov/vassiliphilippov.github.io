dimity

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪməti / (AmE), / ˈdɪmɪti / (BrE)
A strong, lightweight cotton fabric with a raised pattern, typically used for bedcovers and curtains.
cotton fabric with raised pattern

Examples

She chose a pale blue dimity for the bedroom curtains.
The antique shop displayed a quilt made of faded dimity.
Summer dresses were sometimes sewn from lightweight dimity fabric.

Etymology

From Latin 'dimities', from Greek 'dimitos' meaning 'of two threads'.
