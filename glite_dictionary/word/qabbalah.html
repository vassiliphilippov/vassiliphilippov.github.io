Qabbalah

1. NOUN

/ kəˈbɑːlə / (AmE), / kəˈbælə / (BrE)
A system of Jewish mystical and esoteric theology based on interpreting the Hebrew scriptures, developed from the 7th to 18th centuries.
Jewish mystical and esoteric theology

Examples

Many scholars have dedicated their lives to studying the texts and traditions of Qabbalah.
Some contemporary artists draw inspiration from the symbols found in Qabbalah.
He became interested in Qabbalah after reading about Jewish mysticism and philosophy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kabbalah - Alternative spelling of the same system of Jewish mysticism

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'cabala', from Hebrew 'qabbālāh' meaning 'reception, tradition'.
