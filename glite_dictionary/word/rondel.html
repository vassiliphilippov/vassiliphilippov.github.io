rondel

1. NOUN

/ rɒnˈdɛl / (AmE), / ˈrɒndəl / (BrE)
A French verse form consisting of 10 or 13 lines with two rhymes, in which the opening phrase is repeated as a refrain in the second and third stanzas.
French poetic form with repeated refrain

Examples

The medieval poet composed a beautiful rondel to honor the coming of spring.
Students were tasked with writing a rondel using only two rhymes throughout the poem.
Her favorite poem was a rondel known for its musical repetition of the opening line.

Etymology

From Old French, diminutive of 'ronde' (round), referring to the circular repetition of lines.
