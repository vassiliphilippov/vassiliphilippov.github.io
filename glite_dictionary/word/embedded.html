embedded

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈbɛdɪd / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɛdɪd / (BrE)
fixed firmly and deeply in a surrounding mass, or enclosed closely within something else.
fixed firmly within something

Examples

Tiny crystals are embedded in the rock, giving it a sparkling appearance.
The journalist traveled with soldiers, embedded in the unit throughout their mission.
Pieces of glass were embedded in the cushion after the window shattered.

Synset Examples

found pebbles embedded in the silt
stone containing many embedded fossils
peach and plum seeds embedded in a sweet edible pulp

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inserted - put into something else
  • Antonym: exposed - not enclosed or covered; visible

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'embed', from 'em-' (in) + 'bed', meaning 'to lay in a bed or layer'.

2. ADJ

/ ɪmˈbɛdɪd / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɛdɪd / (BrE)
firmly and deeply fixed in a surrounding mass or as an essential part of a whole; inserted as an integral part.
fixed or inserted as part of something larger

Examples

The archaeologists found embedded pottery shards in the layer of ancient soil.
She read the story, struggling with the embedded technical terms.
The software includes an embedded help function for new users.

Synset Examples

confused by the embedded Latin quotations
an embedded subordinate clause

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inserted - placed or put into something
  • Antonym: detached - separate, not attached

Etymology

From the verb 'embed', based on 'bed' (resembling to fix in a bed or mass), first used in the 18th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01331371-s
Synset POS: s
