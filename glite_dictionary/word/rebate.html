rebate

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːˌbeɪt / (AmE), / ˈriːbeɪt / (BrE)
an amount of money that is returned to someone who has paid too much or as a partial refund
partial refund of money paid

Examples

Many stores offer a rebate on new appliances to encourage customers to buy energy-efficient models.
After filing the appropriate paperwork, she received a tax rebate a few weeks later.
The manufacturer promised a $50 rebate for every laptop purchased during the promotional period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refund - an amount of money that is given back, especially because of overpayment or returns

Etymology

From Middle English *rebate*, from Old French *rabatre* (to beat down, reduce), from re- + abattre (to beat down).

2. VERB

/ ˈriːˌbeɪt / (AmE), / ˈriːbeɪt / (BrE)
to cut a groove or recess (a rebate) in the edge of a piece of wood, stone, etc., so that it can be joined to another piece.
cut a groove for joining material

Examples

The carpenter will rebate the edges of the door to ensure a snug fit.
You need to rebate the timber before assembling the window frame.
Masons skillfully rebate stone blocks for a seamless joint.

Synset Examples

rebate the pieces of timber and stone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rabbet - to cut a groove or recess in wood or stone

Etymology

From Old French *rabattre* (to beat down), later used in woodworking for a recess or groove.

3. VERB

/ ˈriˌbeɪt / (AmE), / ˈriːbeɪt / (BrE)
to cut a groove or recess in the edge of timber or stone, typically so that another piece can fit into it
make a groove or recess in material

Examples

The carpenter used a special tool to rebate the edge of the door for the glass panel.
Before fitting the frame, be sure to rebate both sides of the timber.
Stonemasons often rebate the blocks to allow them to interlock securely.

Etymology

From Old French *rabattre* (to beat down), via Anglo-Norman, originally meaning to reduce or cut down.

4. NOUN

/ ˈriˌbeɪt / (AmE), / ˈriːbeɪt / (BrE)
A rectangular groove cut along the edge of a piece of wood or other material to hold or join another piece.
groove for joining pieces (woodworking)

Examples

The carpenter cut a rebate along the edge to fit the back panel securely.
A window frame often has a rebate to hold the glass in place.
He used a chisel to finish the rebate on the door frame.

Etymology

From Old French 'rabatre' (to beat down), related to the sense of reducing or removing material.

5. VERB

/ ˈriˌbeɪt / (AmE), / ˈriːbeɪt / (BrE)
to give a partial refund or reduction in the price of a product, often during a special sale or promotion
give a price reduction or partial refund

Examples

The electronics store will rebate $50 on any new laptop purchased this weekend.
Some manufacturers rebate part of the price directly to the customer after the sale.
The company decided to rebate the installation fee as part of its promotion.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

From Old French 'rabattre', from re- (back) + abattre (to beat down).
