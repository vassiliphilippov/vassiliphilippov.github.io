sublet

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌb.lɛt / (AmE), / ˈsʌb.lɛt / (BrE)
a property or unit that a tenant rents to another person for a period within the term of the original lease
property rented by a tenant to someone else

Examples

They found a sublet while their apartment was being renovated.
I'm looking for a summer sublet close to campus.
The sublet will expire at the end of July.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sublease - a lease granted by a lessee to a third party

Etymology

From 'sub-' (meaning under) and 'let' (to rent), based on the verb 'to sublet'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13269827-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ sʌbˈlɛt / (AmE), / sʌbˈlɛt / (BrE)
to lease or rent all or part of a leased or rented property to another person
lease out rented property to another

Examples

We plan to sublet our apartment while we travel abroad this year.
She sublet the extra bedroom to a college student for the summer.
After losing his job, he decided to sublet part of his office space.

Synset Examples

We sublet our apartment over the summer

Etymology

From sub- (Latin, meaning under) + let (Old English lettan, meaning to allow).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02214043-v
Synset POS: v
