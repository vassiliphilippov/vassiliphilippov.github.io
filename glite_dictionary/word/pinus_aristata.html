Pinus aristata

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪnəs əˈrɪstətə / (AmE), / ˈpɪnʊs ˌarɪˈsteɪtə / (BrE)
a small, slow-growing pine tree native to the Rocky Mountains of the western United States, known for its longevity and dense branches, with short needles in bundles of five and cones with thorn-tipped scales
a long-lived pine tree of the Rocky Mountains

Examples

The ancient Pinus aristata trees in the Rocky Mountains are renowned for their remarkable age.
Scientists study Pinus aristata to understand how these trees survive harsh conditions over millennia.
Hikers were awestruck by a massive Pinus aristata growing along the mountain trail.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Pinus' (pine) and 'aristata' (awned, bristled), describing the thorn-tipped cone scales.
