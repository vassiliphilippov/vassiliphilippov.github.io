Muhlenbergia schreberi

1. NOUN

/ ˌmjuːlənˈbɜːrɡiə ˈʃrɛbəri / (AmE), / ˌmjuːlənˈbɜːɡiə ˈʃreɪbəri / (BrE)
a slender, branching grass native to central United States, valued to some extent for grazing
a North American grass species for grazing

Examples

Muhlenbergia schreberi grows widely in open woods and along roadsides throughout the central United States.
Farmers sometimes use Muhlenbergia schreberi as a supplementary grazing grass for livestock.
Botanists identified a healthy patch of Muhlenbergia schreberi thriving near the creek bank.

Etymology

Named after Gotthilf Heinrich Ernst Muhlenberg, American botanist, with species name honoring John Schreber, German naturalist.
