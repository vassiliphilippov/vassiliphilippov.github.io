photosensitivity

1. NOUN

/ ˌfoʊtoʊˌsɛn.səˈtɪv.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊˌsɛns.ɪˈtɪv.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
abnormal or heightened sensitivity to radiant energy, especially light, sometimes resulting in a physiological reaction
abnormal sensitivity to light or radiant energy
medical

Examples

Some medications can increase photosensitivity, making the skin more prone to sunburn.
Patients with lupus often experience photosensitivity and must avoid direct sunlight.
The scientist studied the photosensitivity of various chemicals exposed to UV light.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: photosensitivity reaction - an abnormal or pathological reaction to sunlight or artificial light, particularly affecting the skin

Etymology

From photo- (relating to light) + sensitivity.
