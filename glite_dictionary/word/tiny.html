tiny

1. ADJ

/ ˈtaɪni / (AmE), / ˈtaɪni / (BrE)
Very small in size; extremely small.
very small in size

Examples

The puppy was so tiny it could fit in the palm of my hand.
She wore a tiny silver necklace around her neck every day.
A tiny crack in the glass let in a cold draft.

Synset Examples

diminutive in stature
a lilliputian chest of drawers
her petite figure
tiny feet
the flyspeck nation of Bahrain moved toward democracy

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: small - of limited size; not large
  • Synonym: miniature - very small copy or model
  • Synonym: minute - extremely small

Etymology

From English 'tinie', a diminutive of 'teeny', probably itself a diminutive of 'teen' (small amount or degree), of uncertain origin.
