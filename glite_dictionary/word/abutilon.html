Abutilon

1. NOUN

/ əˈbjuːtɪˌlɒn / (AmE), / əˈbjuːtɪlən / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the mallow family, including shrubs and small trees with maple-like leaves, often grown as ornamentals.
genus of flowering mallow plants

Examples

The Abutilon blooms added a splash of color to the conservatory.
Gardeners often choose Abutilon for its attractive, maple-like foliage.
Several species of Abutilon are prized for their bell-shaped flowers.

Etymology

From Arabic 'aubūṭīlūn', possibly via Medieval Latin 'abutilon', used by botanists.

2. NOUN

/ əˈbjuːtɪˌlɒn / (AmE), / əˈbjuːtɪlɒn / (BrE)
A genus of herbs, shrubs, or small trees in the mallow family, known for their maple-like leaves and colorful flowers.
a genus of flowering plants (Indian mallow)

Examples

Several species of Abutilon are popular as ornamental plants because of their bright, showy flowers.
The Abutilon genus includes both annual and perennial species.
Gardeners often refer to Abutilon as 'flowering maple' due to the resemblance of its leaves to those of maple trees.

Etymology

From Arabic *aubūtīlūn*, adopted into New Latin as the plant genus name.
