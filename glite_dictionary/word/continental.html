continental

1. ADJ

/ ˌkɑːntɪˈnɛntəl / (AmE), / ˌkɒntɪˈnɛntəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a continent.
relating to a continent

Examples

The continental climate is typically marked by hot summers and cold winters.
Scientists study continental drift to understand how Earth's landmasses move over time.
She collected data on the continental flora unique to Southeast Asia.

Synset Examples

the continental divide
continental drift

Etymology

From Latin 'continentālis', from 'continens' (continent).

2. ADJ

/ ˌkɑːn.tɪˈnɛn.t̬l / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.tɪˈnen.tl / (BrE)
Relating to the American colonies during and immediately after the American Revolutionary War.
of the American Revolutionary period

Examples

The Continental Congress played a crucial role in declaring independence from Britain.
Many soldiers joined the Continental Army to fight for America's freedom.
The continental currency issued during the war rapidly lost its value.

Synset Examples

the Continental Congress

3. ADJ

/ ˌkɑːn.tənˈen.t̬əl / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.tɪˈnen.təl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a continent, especially mainland Europe or North America as distinct from surrounding islands or regions.
relating to a continent, especially Europe or North America

Examples

California is located in the continental United States, while Hawaii is not.
Many French dishes are considered part of continental cuisine in the United Kingdom.
The climate in continental Europe is often more extreme than in coastal regions.

Synset Examples

the continental United States
continental Europe
continental waters

Etymology

From Latin 'continentālis', from 'continens' (continent).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a
