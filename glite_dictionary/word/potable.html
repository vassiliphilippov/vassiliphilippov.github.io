potable

1. ADJ

/ ˈpoʊ.tə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈpəʊ.tə.bəl / (BrE)
(of water or other liquids) safe and suitable for drinking.
safe to drink

Examples

The river water is not potable without being treated first.
Only potable water is allowed in the lab for consumption.
After the filtration process, the water became potable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drinkable - suitable or fit for drinking
  • Antonym: nonpotable - not safe for drinking

Etymology

From Late Latin potable (drinkable), from Latin potare (to drink).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00800668-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊ.t̬ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈpəʊ.tə.bəl / (BrE)
a drinkable liquid; any beverage suitable for drinking
drinkable liquid; beverage

Examples

The festival featured a variety of potables from local breweries and vineyards.
Please place all potables on the bottom shelf for easy access.
She sampled every potable offered at the tasting event.

Synset Examples

may I take your beverage order?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beverage - a drink, especially one other than water
  • Synonym: drink - any liquid that can be swallowed as refreshment

Etymology

From Late Latin potabilis, from Latin potare (to drink).
