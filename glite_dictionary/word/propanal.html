propanal

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈpænæl / (AmE), / prəʊˈpænæl / (BrE)
A colorless liquid aldehyde with the chemical formula C3H6O, used in organic synthesis and as a disinfectant.
a colorless liquid aldehyde (C3H6O)

Examples

Propanal is used in the production of plastics and other chemicals.
Exposure to propanal vapors should be minimized due to its irritant properties.
The laboratory ordered more propanal for the next set of experiments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propionaldehyde - another name for propanal

Etymology

From propane + -al (indicating an aldehyde group).
