Eolian

1. NOUN

/ iˈoʊliən / (AmE), / iˈəʊliən / (BrE)
A member of one of the four major divisions of prehistoric Greeks, associated with regions such as Boeotia, Thessaly, Lesbos, and Greek colonies in Asia Minor.
member of a prehistoric Greek group

Examples

The Eolians played a significant role in the spread of Greek culture across the Aegean.
Ancient poets often referenced the customs of the Eolians in their works.
Linguists study the dialect of the Eolians to learn about prehistoric Greek settlements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dorian - member of another major division of prehistoric Greeks
  • Synonym: Ionian - member of another major division of prehistoric Greeks
  • Synonym: Achaean - member of another major division of prehistoric Greeks

Etymology

From Greek 'Aiolios', relating to Aeolus, a legendary ancestor and eponym of the Aeolian people.
