unidimensional

1. ADJ

/ ˌjuː.ni.dɪˈmɛn.ʃən.əl / (AmE), / ˌjuː.nɪ.dɪˈmɛn.ʃən.əl / (BrE)
Having only one dimension; relating to or concerned with a single aspect or element; lacking depth or complexity.
having only one dimension or aspect

Examples

The data was analyzed using a unidimensional scale, focusing solely on customer satisfaction.
Critics found the movie's protagonist to be unidimensional and lacking in development.
His approach to the problem was unidimensional, ignoring its social and economic factors.

Synset Examples

a prose statement of fact is unidimensional, its value being measured wholly in terms of its truth
a novel with one-dimensional characters

Related Terms

  • Antonym: multidimensional - having many dimensions or aspects
  • Synonym: one-dimensional - having only one dimension or aspect; similar meaning

Etymology

Formed from Latin 'uni-' meaning 'one' and 'dimensional', from Latin 'dimensio' (measuring).
