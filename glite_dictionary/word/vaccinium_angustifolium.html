Vaccinium angustifolium

1. NOUN

/ vækˈsɪniəm æŋˌɡʌstəˈfoʊliəm / (AmE), / vækˈsɪniəm æŋˌɡʌstəˈfəʊliəm / (BrE)
A low-growing deciduous shrub native to northeastern North America, producing sweet dark blue berries commonly known as lowbush blueberries.
species of lowbush blueberry

Examples

The fields were covered with Vaccinium angustifolium, offering a rich harvest of wild blueberries each summer.
Researchers study Vaccinium angustifolium for its antioxidant-rich berries.
Wild bears in the region often feed on Vaccinium angustifolium during berry season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blueberry - a small sweet blue-black edible berry

Etymology

From Latin: 'Vaccinium' (blueberry genus) and 'angustifolium' meaning 'narrow-leaved'.
