Phellodendron amurense

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɛloʊˈdɛndrən ˌæmjʊˈrɛnseɪ / (AmE), / ˌfɛləʊˈdɛndrən ˌamjʊˈrɛnsɪ / (BrE)
a deciduous tree native to China and Manchuria, known for its turpentine-scented bark, compound leaves that turn yellow in autumn, and deeply fissured, corky bark
species of deciduous tree (Amur cork tree)

Examples

The Phellodendron amurense is often planted in parks for its ornamental value and distinctive bark.
Botanists studied the medicinal properties of compounds found in Phellodendron amurense.
In autumn, the leaves of the Phellodendron amurense turn a striking yellow, enhancing the landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Amur cork tree - another name for Phellodendron amurense

Etymology

From Greek *phellos* (cork) + *dendron* (tree) and Latinized epithet *amurense* (from the Amur region).
