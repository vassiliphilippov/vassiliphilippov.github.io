endoscopic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛndoʊˈskɑpɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛndəˈskɒpɪk / (BrE)
relating to or performed by means of an endoscope or endoscopy.
relating to endoscopy or an endoscope
medical

Examples

The surgeon used an endoscopic camera to view the inside of the patient's stomach.
Advances in endoscopic techniques have improved recovery times for many procedures.
Biopsies can often be taken during an endoscopic examination of the digestive tract.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endoscopy - the practice of examining the interior of a body using an endoscope

Etymology

From 'endoscope' (from Greek endon 'within' + skopein 'to look') + -ic.
