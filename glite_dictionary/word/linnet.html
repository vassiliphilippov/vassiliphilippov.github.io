linnet

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪnɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɪnɪt / (BrE)
a small Old World finch, especially the male, which has a reddish breast and forehead
a small finch with a red breast

Examples

A linnet perched on the garden fence, singing sweetly in the early morning.
The male linnet is easy to recognize by its reddish breast.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitat of the endangered linnet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finch - a small songbird of the family Fringillidae

Etymology

From Old French 'linet', from 'lin' (flax), referring to the bird's fondness for flax seeds.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɪnət / (AmE), / ˈlɪnɪt / (BrE)
a small songbird of the finch family, originally found in the western United States and Mexico, known for its melodious song and brownish or grayish plumage.
a small finch native to western US and Mexico

Examples

A bright red linnet perched on the fence, singing joyfully in the morning sun.
The children watched as a flock of linnets flew over the meadow.
Naturalists often study the distinctive song of the linnet in the western regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finch - a small songbird with a short, conical beak

Etymology

From Middle French 'linette', from Old French, diminutive of 'lin' (flax), because the bird feeds on flax seeds.
