transit

1. NOUN

/ ˈtræn.sɪt / (AmE), / ˈtræn.zɪt / (BrE)
a surveying instrument for measuring horizontal and vertical angles, consisting of a small telescope mounted on a tripod
surveying instrument for measuring angles

Examples

The engineer set up the transit to accurately measure the property's boundaries.
Modern surveyors often use a transit equipped with digital readouts for greater precision.
After adjusting the tripod, she looked through the transit to take the necessary angular measurements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theodolite - a surveying instrument for measuring horizontal and vertical angles

2. NOUN

/ ˈtræn.sɪt / (AmE), / ˈtræn.zɪt / (BrE)
a system or facility providing the means and equipment for transporting passengers or goods from one place to another
transportation system or facility

Examples

The city is working to improve its transit to reduce traffic congestion.
Many commuters rely on public transit to get to work every day.
The new airport transit hub will connect trains and buses for easier travel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transportation - the action of transporting someone or something

Etymology

From Latin 'transitus' (a going across), from 'transire' (to cross).

3. VERB

/ ˈtræn.zɪt / (AmE), / ˈtræn.zɪt / (BrE)
(of the telescope of a surveying instrument) to revolve about its horizontal axis in order to reverse its direction.
revolve a surveyor's telescope to reverse direction

Examples

The surveyor transited the telescope to take measurements in the opposite direction.
Before making the next reading, be sure to transit the instrument carefully.
He quickly transited the scope to ensure accurate alignment with the new reference point.

/ ˈtræn.sɪt / (AmE), / ˈtræn.zɪt / (BrE)
to cause or enable (someone or something) to pass through a place or area, especially in the course of transportation or movement
enable to pass through

Examples

After customs clearance, the goods were transited through the port to their final destination.

Related Terms

7. VERB

/ ˈtræn.sɪt / (AmE), / ˈtræn.zɪt / (BrE)
(of a celestial body) to pass across the face of another body or the meridian of a place, as observed from a particular point.
to pass across (in astronomy)

Examples

The planet Venus will transit the sun next year, offering a rare astronomical event.
Astronomers observed the satellite transit Jupiter's disk through their telescope.
The comet is predicted to transit the meridian at midnight, making it visible to the naked eye.

Synset Examples

The comet will transit on September 11

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cross - to move or extend across something

Etymology

From Latin 'transitus', past participle of 'transīre' (to go across).
