giant

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒaɪənt / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɪənt / (BrE)
Extremely large in size, mass, or volume; huge and bulky.
very large or massive

Examples

The company built a giant new factory outside the city.
A giant wave crashed onto the shore during the storm.
They displayed a giant pumpkin at the county fair.

Synset Examples

a jumbo jet
jumbo shrimp

Related Terms

  • Synonym: huge - very large in size, amount, or degree
  • Synonym: massive - large and heavy; solid or substantial
  • Antonym: tiny - extremely small

3. NOUN

/ ˈdʒaɪənt / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɪənt / (BrE)
an imaginary being of enormous size and strength, often found in folklore and fairy tales
mythical being of great size

Examples

There are many stories about a giant who lives at the top of a beanstalk.
In folklore, a giant often represents a formidable challenge for the hero.
Children listened in awe as the tale described a fearsome giant with a booming voice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ogre - a monstrous, often man-eating, being in folklore
  • Antonym: dwarf - a (mythical) being of very small size in folklore

Etymology

From Latin gigantem, from Ancient Greek gígās (giant).

5. NOUN

/ ˈdʒaɪənt / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɪənt / (BrE)
an extremely bright star with a large diameter and low density compared to the Sun, found in a late stage of stellar evolution
large, bright star with low density

Examples

Betelgeuse is a well-known example of a red giant in the constellation Orion.
Many giant stars expand late in their lifecycles as they exhaust their core hydrogen.
When a sun-like star becomes a giant, it grows enormously in size and brightness.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: supergiant - an even larger and more luminous type of giant star
  • Degree Lower: dwarf - a star much smaller and less luminous than a giant star, such as a red dwarf
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
