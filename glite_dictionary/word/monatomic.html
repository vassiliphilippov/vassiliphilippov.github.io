monatomic

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɑːnəˈtɑːmɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈtɒmɪk / (BrE)
(of a chemical element or molecule) consisting of single atoms, not bonded with others of the same element.
consisting of single atoms
medical

Examples

Helium is a monatomic gas under standard conditions.
Because neon is monatomic, it does not form molecules under normal circumstances.
Some noble gases, such as argon, exist naturally as monatomic substances.

Synset Examples

helium and argon are monatomic gases

Related Terms

  • Antonym: diatomic - consisting of two atoms
  • Antonym: polyatomic - consisting of multiple atoms

Etymology

From mono- (one) + atomic, meaning 'having one atom.'
