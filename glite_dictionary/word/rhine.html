Rhine

1. PROPN

/ raɪn / (AmE), / raɪn / (BrE)
a major river in western Europe, flowing from the Swiss Alps through Germany and the Netherlands to the North Sea
major river in western Europe

Examples

The Rhine has long served as a vital waterway for trade in Europe.
We took a river cruise along the scenic Rhine last summer.
The Lorelei rock rises above the Rhine, attracting many tourists each year.

Etymology

From Latin 'Rhenus', from Proto-Germanic 'Rīnaz', referring to the Rhine river.
