breadfruit

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɛdˌfrut / (AmE), / ˈbrɛdˌfruːt / (BrE)
A large round fruit with starchy flesh, native to the Pacific Islands, that is edible and has a texture similar to bread when cooked.
large starchy edible fruit of the Pacific

Examples

The breadfruit was roasted over the fire and served with fish.
Many Pacific islanders rely on breadfruit as a staple food in their diet.
She had never tasted breadfruit before her trip to Tahiti.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jackfruit - large tropical fruit with sweet yellow flesh

Etymology

Named for its bread-like texture when cooked.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbrɛdˌfruːt / (AmE), / ˈbrɛd.fruːt / (BrE)
A large, round, seedless or seeded tropical fruit with starchy flesh resembling bread when cooked; eaten boiled, baked, roasted, or ground into flour.
large tropical fruit with starchy flesh

Examples

The locals served boiled breadfruit alongside grilled fish for dinner.
You can roast breadfruit over an open fire to make a tasty snack.
Ground breadfruit can be used as a gluten-free flour in baking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jackfruit - large tropical fruit with fibrous, starchy flesh

Etymology

From its bread-like texture when cooked and its fruit nature.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07770427-n
Synset POS: n
