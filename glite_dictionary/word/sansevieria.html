Sansevieria

1. NOUN

/ ˌsænsəˈvɪəriə / (AmE), / ˌsænsɪˈvɪəriə / (BrE)
A genus of Old World tropical herbaceous perennials in the agave family, known for their long, stiff, upright leaves, often grown as houseplants.
genus of tropical perennials with stiff leaves

Examples

The Sansevieria is a popular choice for homes because it requires minimal care.
Several varieties of Sansevieria are often called 'snake plants' or 'mother-in-law's tongue'.
Botanists have reclassified Sansevieria species into the Dracaena genus in recent years.

Etymology

Named after Raimondo di Sangro, Prince of San Severo, an Italian nobleman.
