broadaxe

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɔdˌæks / (AmE), / ˈbrɔːdˌæks / (BrE)
a large axe with a broad, flat blade, traditionally used for hewing logs into beams
axe with a broad blade, for hewing

Examples

The carpenter used a broadaxe to shape the beams for the cabin.
Historically, the broadaxe was an essential tool for loggers building wooden structures.
He admired the old broadaxe hanging in the workshop as a reminder of traditional craftsmanship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: axe - a tool with a bladed head for chopping

Etymology

Formed from 'broad' + 'axe', describing the shape of the blade.
