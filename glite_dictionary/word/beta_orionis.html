Beta Orionis

1. PROPN

/ ˌbeɪtə ɔːˈraɪənɪs / (AmE), / ˌbiːtə ɒˈraɪənɪs / (BrE)
the brightest star in the constellation Orion, also known as Rigel
the brightest star in Orion

Examples

Astronomers observe Beta Orionis to study massive star evolution.
Beta Orionis is easily visible in the winter night sky.
The team measured the luminosity of Beta Orionis using advanced telescopes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rigel - another name for Beta Orionis, the brightest star in Orion

Etymology

From Latin 'Orion' (the constellation) and Greek letter 'Beta', being the Bayer designation.
