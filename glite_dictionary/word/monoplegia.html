monoplegia

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːnəˈpliːdʒiə / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈpliːdʒɪə / (BrE)
paralysis affecting a single limb or a single muscle group.
paralysis of one limb
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with monoplegia of his right arm after the accident.
Physical therapy is important for individuals suffering from monoplegia to maintain muscle strength.
Doctors traced the cause of her monoplegia to nerve damage in her spinal cord.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: hemiplegia - paralysis of one side of the body
  • Degree Higher: paraplegia - paralysis of both lower limbs
  • Synonym: paralysis - loss of the ability to move a body part

Etymology

From Greek 'monos' (single) and 'plēgē' (strike, paralysis).
