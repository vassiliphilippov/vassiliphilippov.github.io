egotism

1. NOUN

/ ˈiɡəˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈiːɡəˌtɪzəm / (BrE)
the fact of thinking that you are better or more important than anyone else; excessive self-importance
excessive self-importance

Examples

His egotism made it difficult for him to work as part of a team.
Critics accused the leader of egotism and arrogance in handling the crisis.
She mistook his confidence for egotism and kept her distance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-importance - an attitude of believing oneself to be more important than others
  • Antonym: humility - the quality of having a modest or low view of one's importance

Etymology

From Latin 'ego' (I) + English suffix '-ism', modeled after French 'égoïsme'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈiːɡəˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈiːɡətɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The practice of talking and thinking about oneself excessively because of an exaggerated sense of one's own importance.
excessive self-importance or self-focus

Examples

His constant bragging is a sign of egotism that annoys his coworkers.
She mistook her egotism for self-confidence.
Political leaders are often accused of egotism when they ignore the advice of others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-importance - an inflated sense of one's own value or significance
  • Antonym: humility - a modest or low view of one's own importance; opposite of egotism

Etymology

From Latin 'ego' (I) + English suffix '-ism'.
