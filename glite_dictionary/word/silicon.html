silicon

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlɪˌkɑn / (AmE), / ˈsɪlɪkən / (BrE)
a chemical element (symbol Si), a hard, brittle crystalline solid used especially as a semiconductor in electronics and found abundantly in the Earth's crust as part of minerals such as quartz and sand
a chemical element used in electronics and found in minerals

Examples

Silicon chips are the foundation of modern computers and smartphones.
Granite is rich in silicon, making it a key material in construction.
Scientists are researching new ways to improve silicon solar cells for greater efficiency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silica - dioxide form of silicon found in quartz and sand
  • Synonym: semiconductor - a substance, such as silicon, with electrical conductivity between conductors and insulators

Etymology

From Latin 'silex' (flint); named in the early 19th century.
