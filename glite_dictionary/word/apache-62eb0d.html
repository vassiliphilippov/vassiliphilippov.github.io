apache

1. NOUN

/ əˈpɑːʃ / (AmE), / əˈpaʃ / (BrE)
(historical) A member of a Parisian underworld gang, especially in the early 20th century.
Parisian gangster (historical)
archaic

Examples

The apache was infamous for his brutality in the backstreets of Paris.
In early 1900s literature, the word apache referred to a dangerous Parisian criminal.
She danced with the flair of an apache, bold and untamed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gangster - a member of an organized group of criminals

Etymology

From French 'apache', adopted from the name of the Apache Native American people due to perceived lawlessness.
