Ilium

1. PROPN

/ ˈɪliəm / (AmE), / ˈɪliəm / (BrE)
an ancient city in northwest Asia Minor, also known as Troy, famous as the site of the Trojan War in classical literature
ancient city, site of the Trojan War

Examples

The ruins of Ilium attract archaeologists and tourists alike, seeking to explore the legendary site.
Many epic poems, including the Iliad, recount the story of the siege of Ilium.
Some historians debate the exact location of ancient Ilium in modern-day Turkey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Troy - another name for the same ancient city, site of the Trojan War

Etymology

From Latin 'Ilium', from Ancient Greek 'Ἴλιον' (Ílion), the name for Troy.
