Desmidium

1. NOUN

/ dɛzˈmɪdiəm / (AmE), / dɛzˈmɪdiəm / (BrE)
A genus of single-celled green algae found in freshwater, characterized by distinctive symmetrical shapes.
genus of green freshwater algae

Examples

Desmidium is frequently studied in laboratories for its unusual cell division and symmetry.
The pond sample contained several species of Desmidium and other green algae.

2. NOUN

/ dɛzˈmɪdiəm / (AmE), / dɛzˈmɪdiəm / (BrE)
A genus of green algae found in freshwater, characterized by long filamentous chains of cells.
genus of filamentous green algae

Examples

Scientists often study Desmidium to understand freshwater ecosystems.
The filamentous chains of Desmidium are easily observed under a microscope.
Many species within Desmidium contribute to the biodiversity of ponds and lakes.

Etymology

From New Latin Desmidium, a diminutive of Greek desmis (a bundle), referring to the shape of the cells.
