Jesuit

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒɛzjuɪt / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛzjʊɪt / (BrE)
Having qualities characteristic of Jesuits or their doctrines, often associated with subtle reasoning or casuistry.
relating to Jesuits or their qualities

Examples

The school provided a Jesuit education that emphasized rigorous intellectual training.
His argument was criticized for its Jesuit subtlety and complexity.
The Jesuit approach to teaching encourages moral reflection and discipline.

Synset Examples

Jesuitical education

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jesuitical - having qualities associated with Jesuits, especially subtle reasoning

Etymology

From Medieval Latin Jesuita, from Jesus (the Society of Jesus).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛʒuɪt / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛzjuɪt / (BrE)
a member of the Society of Jesus, a Roman Catholic religious order of priests and brothers founded in 1540
member of the Society of Jesus

Examples

Many prominent schools around the world were founded by Jesuits.
He became a Jesuit after many years of study and reflection.
The Jesuits are known for their contributions to education and science.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: priest - an ordained minister of the Catholic, Orthodox, or Anglican Church

Etymology

From French *jésuite*, from Jesus + -ite (member of). The Society of Jesus was founded by Ignatius of Loyola in 1540.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10241190-n
Synset POS: n
