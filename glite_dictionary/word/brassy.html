brassy

1. ADJ

/ ˈbræsi / (AmE), / ˈbrɑːsi / (BrE)
(of a sound) resembling that produced by a brass musical instrument; loud, bright, and metallic.
like the sound of a brass instrument

Examples

His trumpet solo had a distinctively brassy tone that filled the hall.
The alarm rang out in a brassy blast, making everyone jump.
She disliked the brassy quality of the recording, finding it too sharp and harsh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metallic - having a sharp, ringing sound like metal

Etymology

From 'brass' (a yellow metal) + '-y', forming an adjective to indicate similarity.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbræsi / (AmE), / ˈbrɑːsi / (BrE)
(of a person or behavior) boldly confident in a way that is rude or shows a lack of respect for traditional standards; shameless or brazen.
bold and shameless; brazen

Examples

She made a brassy entrance, ignoring everyone who stared at her unusual outfit.
His brassy remarks during the presentation shocked the entire boardroom.
The comedian is known for her brassy style and unapologetic jokes.

Synset Examples

an audacious trick to pull
a barefaced hypocrite
the most bodacious display of tourism this side of Anaheim
bald-faced lies
brazen arrogance
the modern world with its quick material successes and insolent belief in the boundless possibilities of progress

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brazen - shamelessly bold or without respect for convention
  • Synonym: barefaced - shameless; without disguise or concealment
  • Antonym: modest - having a humble or reserved manner; not brash

Etymology

Originally from 'brass', used figuratively to mean 'impudence' or 'shamelessness'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈbræsi / (AmE), / ˈbrɑːsi / (BrE)
(of a person, appearance, or thing) tastelessly showy or flashy, especially in a way that is loud and lacking in subtlety.
tastelessly showy or flashy

Examples

The singer wore a brassy dress covered in sequins to the party.
His brassy jewelry caught everyone's eye, but not in a good way.
The room was decorated in a brassy style that some found overwhelming.

Synset Examples

a flash car
a flashy ring
garish colors
a gaudy costume
loud sport shirts
a meretricious yet stylish book
tawdry ornaments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flashy - showy in a way that attracts attention
  • Synonym: gaudy - excessively bright or showy, lacking in good taste
  • Antonym: subtle - not obvious or showy; understated

Etymology

From 'brass', suggesting the shiny, yellowish look of the metal, often associated with cheapness or lack of refinement.

Source: WordNet
