to rid

1. VERB

/ rɪd / (AmE), / rɪd / (BrE)
To make someone or something free from an unwanted person or thing.
make free from something unwanted

Examples

We need to rid the office of unnecessary paperwork before the inspection.
She struggled to rid herself of her bad habits.
The company hired professionals to rid the building of mold and pests.

Synset Examples

Rid the house of pests

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clear - to remove or get rid of something unwanted
  • Synonym: to eliminate - to completely remove or get rid of something
  • Antonym: to infest - to be present in large numbers in a way that causes harm

Etymology

From Old English 'hriddan' (to clear land).
