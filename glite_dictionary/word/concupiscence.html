concupiscence

1. NOUN

/ kənˈkjuːpəsəns / (AmE), / kɒnˈkjuːpɪsəns / (BrE)
strong sexual desire; lust
strong sexual desire

Examples

The ancient text warns against concupiscence as a path to moral downfall.
Religious teachings often discuss concupiscence as an innate human inclination.
His poetry was filled with references to concupiscence and forbidden love.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lust - strong sexual desire

Etymology

From Latin 'concupiscentia' (desire, longing), from 'concupiscere' (to desire strongly).
