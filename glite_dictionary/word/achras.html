Achras

1. NOUN

/ ˈækrəs / (AmE), / ˈækrəs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical trees with papery leaves and large fruit, especially known for the sapodilla.
genus of tropical fruit trees

Examples

The Achras genus is best known for producing the sapodilla fruit.
Botanists discovered a new species within the Achras genus in South America.
Many Achras trees have distinctive papery leaves and sweet edible fruit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sapodilla - an edible tropical fruit from the Achras genus

Etymology

From Greek 'akhras', a wild pear tree.
