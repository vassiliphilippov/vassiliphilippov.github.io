cattle

1. NOUN

/ ˈkætl̩ / (AmE), / ˈkætl / (BrE)
Domesticated bovine animals as a group, regardless of sex or age, often kept for milk, meat, or work.
domesticated bovine animals, collectively

Examples

The farmers moved their cattle from one pasture to another to ensure fresh grazing.
A severe drought can threaten the health of cattle in many regions.
Vaccinations help protect cattle from dangerous diseases.

Synset Examples

so many head of cattle
wait till the cows come home
seven thin and ill-favored kine
a team of oxen

Etymology

From Old French 'catel' (property, livestock), from Latin 'capitalis' (of the head, principal).
