dogging

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɔɡɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdɒɡɪŋ / (BrE)
Relentlessly persistent in pursuit or as if in pursuit; unceasing and indefatigable.
relentlessly persistent or unceasing

Examples

She couldn't escape the dogging suspicion that she had made a mistake.
The team's progress was hindered by dogging criticism from the press.
He was troubled by dogging doubts throughout his career.

Synset Examples

impossible to escape the dogging fears

Related Terms

  • Synonym: persistent - continuing firmly or obstinately in a course of action
  • Synonym: relentless - refusing to give up or let up; persistent
  • Antonym: sporadic - occurring at irregular intervals; not continuous

Etymology

From the verb 'dog' meaning 'to follow or pursue relentlessly', from Old English 'docga' (dog).
