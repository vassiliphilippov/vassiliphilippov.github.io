Squatina

1. NOUN

/ skwəˈtiːnə / (AmE), / skwəˈtiːnə / (BrE)
A genus of angel sharks in the family Squatinidae, characterized by flattened bodies and broad pectoral fins.
genus of angel sharks

Examples

The genus Squatina includes several species of angel sharks found in coastal waters.
Researchers observed a Squatina specimen during their diving expedition in the Mediterranean Sea.
All Squatina species are characterized by their broad, flattened bodies and wing-like fins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angel shark - a species of shark with a flattened body, in the genus Squatina

Etymology

From Latin *squatina*, referring to a kind of shark.

genus of angel sharks

Examples

Researchers have studied Squatina to better understand the adaptations of bottom-dwelling sharks.
The habitat range of Squatina extends across continental shelves in temperate and tropical waters.
