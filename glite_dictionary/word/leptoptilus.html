Leptoptilus

1. NOUN

/ lɛpˈtɒptɪləs / (AmE), / lɛpˈtɪlɪəs / (BrE)
a genus of large storks, including adjutant birds and marabous, native to Africa and southern Asia
genus of large storks

Examples

The Leptoptilus genus includes some of the largest storks in the world.
Researchers observed a Leptoptilus near the wetlands during migration season.
Many members of Leptoptilus are recognized by their massive bills and bare heads.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marabou - a large African stork with a bare head and neck
  • Synonym: adjutant bird - a large stork of southern Asia, member of Leptoptilus genus

Etymology

From Greek 'leptos' (slender) + 'ptilon' (feather).

2. NOUN

/ lɛpˈtɑptɪləs / (AmE), / lɛpˈtɪləs / (BrE)
a genus of large storks, including the adjutant birds and marabous, found in Africa and southern Asia.
a genus of large storks

Examples

The Leptoptilus genus is known for its distinctive bare-headed appearance.
Several species within Leptoptilus are found near large wetlands in Africa and Asia.
Scientists observed nesting behavior in Leptoptilus storks during the rainy season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stork - a long-legged, long-necked wading bird in the family Ciconiidae
  • Synonym: marabou - a large African stork with a naked head and neck

Etymology

From Greek leptos (slender) + ptilon (feather), referring to their appearance.
