Monarda punctata

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈnɑːrdə pʌŋkˈteɪtə / (AmE), / məˈnɑːdə pʌŋkˈteɪtə / (BrE)
A tall, erect perennial or annual plant with lance-shaped leaves and creamy flowers spotted with purple, native to North America and Mexico.
species of flowering plant; spotted horsemint

Examples

Monarda punctata is commonly known as spotted horsemint and attracts many pollinators in summer gardens.
Gardeners often choose Monarda punctata for its drought tolerance and showy, speckled flowers.
Native to the United States, Monarda punctata thrives in sandy soils and open prairies.

Etymology

Monarda named after Spanish botanist Nicolás Monardes (1493–1588); punctata from Latin 'punctatus' meaning 'spotted'.
