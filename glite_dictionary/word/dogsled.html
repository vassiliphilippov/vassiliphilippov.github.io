dogsled

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɔɡˌslɛd / (AmE), / ˈdɒɡˌslɛd / (BrE)
a sled that is pulled by dogs, typically used for travel or transport over snow and ice
sled pulled by dogs

Examples

The explorers loaded their supplies onto the dogsled before setting out across the frozen landscape.
Racing a dogsled requires both strength and skill from the musher and the dogs.
Children gathered to watch the dogsled team arrive at the winter festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sled - a vehicle for traveling over snow, usually on runners

Etymology

From English 'dog' + 'sled'.

2. VERB

/ ˈdɔɡˌslɛd / (AmE), / ˈdɒɡˌslɛd / (BrE)
to travel or transport by means of a sled pulled by dogs, especially over snow or ice.
travel by sled pulled by dogs

Examples

Every winter, tourists from around the world come to Alaska to dogsled across the frozen landscape.
He had never dogsledded before, but quickly learned to command the eager huskies.
They spent the morning dogsledding through the snowy forest with a team of eight Siberian huskies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mush - to drive or travel with a dogsled, especially in the command form
