Esox niger

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːsɑks ˈnaɪɡər / (AmE), / ˈiːsɒks ˈnaɪɡə / (BrE)
A species of freshwater fish known as the chain pickerel, common in quiet waters of the eastern United States.
species of chain pickerel fish

Examples

The Esox niger is prized by anglers for its agility and fighting spirit.
Lakes in the southeastern region are home to many Esox niger specimens.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Esox niger in marshy areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chain pickerel - Freshwater fish species Esox niger

Etymology

From Latin; 'Esox' meaning pike and 'niger' meaning black.
