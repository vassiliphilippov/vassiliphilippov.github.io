aeromedicine

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛroʊˈmɛdɪsɪn / (AmE), / ˌeərəʊˈmɛdɪsɪn / (BrE)
the branch of medicine concerned with the study and treatment of diseases and disorders associated with flying, especially in aircraft and space flight
medicine related to flight and aviation
medical

Examples

Advances in aeromedicine have greatly improved the safety and health of pilots.
The team specializes in aeromedicine, focusing on the effects of zero gravity on the human body.
She published several papers on aeromedicine after her research with astronauts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aviation medicine - the medical study of problems associated with flying

Etymology

From aero- (relating to air or flight) + medicine.
