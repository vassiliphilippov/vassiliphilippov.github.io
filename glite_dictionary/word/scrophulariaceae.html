Scrophulariaceae

1. NOUN

/ skroʊˌfjʊləˌriˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / skrəˌfjʊləˌrɪˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants that includes figworts, snapdragons, foxgloves, toadflaxes, speedwells, and mulleins
a family of flowering plants (figwort family)

Examples

Many garden favorites, such as the snapdragon, belong to the Scrophulariaceae family.
Recent taxonomic changes have moved some species out of Scrophulariaceae.
The medicinal herb foxglove is a notable member of the Scrophulariaceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: figwort - a plant of the genus Scrophularia in the Scrophulariaceae family

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Scrophularia plus the standard botanical suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ skrəˌfjuljəˈreɪsiˌi / (AmE), / skrəˌfjʊljʊˈreɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
A family of flowering plants, including figworts, snapdragons, foxgloves, toadflaxes, speedwells, and mulleins, traditionally placed in the order Scrophulariales or Polemoniales.
a family of flowering plants (figwort family)

Examples

The Scrophulariaceae are known for their diverse and showy flowers in many gardens.
Digitalis, a well-known genus in the Scrophulariaceae, is valued for its medical uses.
Recent studies have led to the reclassification of several genera formerly included in the Scrophulariaceae family.

Etymology

From New Latin Scrophularia (the type genus) + -aceae (a standard family suffix in botanical nomenclature).
