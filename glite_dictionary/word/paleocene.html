Paleocene

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪ.li.əˌsin / (AmE), / ˈpeɪ.li.əˌsiːn / (BrE)
the geological epoch from about 66 to 56 million years ago, following the extinction of the dinosaurs and marked by the diversification of mammals and birds
geological epoch after dinosaurs

Examples

Many important mammal groups first appeared during the Paleocene epoch.
The extinction of the dinosaurs marked the beginning of the Paleocene period.
Fossils from the Paleocene reveal significant changes in Earth's climate and life.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Eocene - the geological epoch following the Paleocene
  • Degree Lower: Cretaceous - the geological period preceding the Paleocene

Etymology

From Greek 'palaios' (ancient) and '-cene' (new, recent), used in geological epochs.
