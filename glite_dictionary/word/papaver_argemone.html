Papaver argemone

1. NOUN

/ pəˈpeɪvər ɑrˈdʒɛməni / (AmE), / pəˈpɑːvə ɑːˈdʒɛməni / (BrE)
An annual species of poppy native to Europe and western Asia, having orange-red flowers and bristly fruit.
annual poppy with orange-red flowers

Examples

Many wildflowers grew in the meadow, including the distinctive Papaver argemone with its orange-red blooms.
Botanists studied the Papaver argemone to better understand its adaptation to dry soils.
The bristly fruit of Papaver argemone makes it easy to distinguish from other poppy species.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Papaver' meaning poppy, 'argemone' referring to a leprous condition or a plant used to treat ulcers in ancient times.
