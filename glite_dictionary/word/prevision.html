prevision

1. NOUN

/ priˈvɪʒən / (AmE), / prɪˈvɪʒ(ə)n / (BrE)
the ability or act of foreseeing or anticipating what will happen in the future
act or ability to foresee the future

Examples

Her remarkable prevision allowed her to prepare for the challenges ahead.
The company relied on financial prevision to avoid potential crises.
Many regard accurate prevision of trends as vital in the technology sector.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foresight - the ability to predict what will happen or be needed in the future
  • Synonym: premonition - a strong feeling that something is about to happen, especially something unpleasant

Etymology

From Latin 'praevisio' (foreseeing), from 'praevidere' (to foresee).
