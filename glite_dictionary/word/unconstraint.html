unconstraint

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌn.kənˈstɹeɪnt / (AmE), / ˌʌn.kənˈstɹeɪnt / (BrE)
the state of being free from restraint, inhibition, or worry
lack of restraint or inhibition

Examples

There was a sense of unconstraint among the guests at the informal gathering.
The children's laughter expressed pure unconstraint as they played in the park.
The novel is admired for the unconstraint in its narrative style.

Synset Examples

she danced with abandon

Related Terms

  • Antonym: restraint - the action of keeping something under control or within limits
  • Synonym: abandon - lack of inhibition or restraint

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) and 'constraint' (from Latin 'constringere', to bind together).
