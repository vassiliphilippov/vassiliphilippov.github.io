Rembrandt

1. PROPN

/ ˈrɛmˌbrænt / (AmE), / ˈrɛmˌbrænt / (BrE)
(1606–1669) A highly influential Dutch painter and etcher, famous for his portraits, self-portraits, and mastery of light and shadow.
famous Dutch painter (1606–1669)

Examples

A painting by Rembrandt is considered a masterpiece of the Dutch Golden Age.
Many art historians have studied Rembrandt's remarkable use of light and shadow.
The museum's new exhibit features several lesser-known works by Rembrandt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Vermeer - Dutch painter, famous for domestic interior scenes and masterful use of light
  • Synonym: Rubens - Flemish Baroque painter known for dramatic compositions and vibrant color

Etymology

From Rembrandt Harmenszoon van Rijn, the artist's full name.
