candelilla

1. NOUN

/ ˌkændəˈliːjə / (AmE), / ˌkændəlˈɪlə / (BrE)
A wax-coated shrub native to Mexico and the southwestern United States, related to Euphorbia antisyphilitica, whose stems are used to produce candelilla wax.
Mexican wax-producing shrub

Examples

Candelilla wax is harvested from the dried stems of the candelilla plant.
The candelilla shrub thrives in the arid climates of northern Mexico.
Local farmers collect candelilla for use in cosmetics and food products.

Etymology

From Spanish 'candelilla', diminutive of 'candela' (candle), referring to its use in making wax.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkændəˈliːjə / (AmE), / ˌkændɪˈliːjə / (BrE)
a small wax-coated shrub (Euphorbia antisyphilitica) native to northern Mexico and the southwestern United States, valued for its wax.
a wax-coated shrub of North America

Examples

The candelilla plant is harvested for its valuable natural wax.
Near the Texas border, fields of candelilla thrive in the arid climate.
Many cosmetics contain candelilla wax as a natural ingredient.

Etymology

From Spanish 'candelilla,' diminutive of 'candela' (candle), referring to the plant's wax used for candles.
