Hume

1. PROPN

/ hjuːm / (AmE), / hjuːm / (BrE)
(1711–1776) A Scottish philosopher known for his influential work on empiricism and skepticism, arguing that human knowledge is limited to what is perceived through the senses.
Scottish philosopher (1711–1776)

Examples

Hume's arguments about causality profoundly influenced modern philosophy.
Many consider Hume a central figure in the Scottish Enlightenment.
Philosophy students often study the works of Hume to understand empiricism.
