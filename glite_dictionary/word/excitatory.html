excitatory

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈsaɪtəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ɪkˈsaɪtətəri / (BrE)
(of a substance, nerve, or stimulus) causing or able to cause excitation or stimulation, especially in nerve cells or muscles.
causing excitation or stimulation
medical

Examples

Some neurotransmitters have an excitatory effect on the nervous system, increasing activity in the brain.
The drug acts as an excitatory agent, stimulating the patient's heart rate.
In biological research, scientists study how excitatory signals lead to muscle contraction.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inhibitory - causing or able to cause inhibition (opposite of excitatory)

Etymology

From Latin 'excitare' (to excite) + English '-ory' (having the quality of).
