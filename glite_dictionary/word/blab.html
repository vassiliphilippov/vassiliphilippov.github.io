to blab

1. VERB

/ blæb / (AmE), / blab / (BrE)
to talk at length, often about trivial or secret matters, sometimes without thinking or being careful
talk excessively or carelessly

Examples

He tends to blab about his weekend plans even when no one asks.
Don’t blab our secret to anyone at work.
She blabbed on the phone for hours about reality TV shows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chatter - to talk rapidly about trivial matters
  • Synonym: gossip - to talk about other people's private lives

Etymology

Probably imitative in origin, suggesting aimless or idle talk.

2. VERB

/ blæb / (AmE), / blæb / (BrE)
to reveal confidential information or secrets, often carelessly or thoughtlessly
reveal secrets carelessly

Examples

I asked her not to blab about our plans, but she told everyone at work.
He can't be trusted with secrets; he always ends up blabbing.
If you blab to the press, the deal will be off.

Synset Examples

Be careful — his secretary talks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to divulge - to make known (private or sensitive information)
  • Antonym: to conceal - to keep something secret or hidden

Etymology

Probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Old Norse blabbra (to talk rapidly, babble).
