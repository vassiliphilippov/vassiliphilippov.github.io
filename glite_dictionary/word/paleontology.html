paleontology

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪliɑnˈtɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌpæliɒnˈtɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The scientific study of fossils and ancient forms of life preserved in rocks.
science of fossils and ancient life

Examples

After studying paleontology, she joined a dig searching for dinosaur bones in Montana.
His passion for paleontology began after he visited a museum and saw a giant fossil.
Advances in paleontology have greatly expanded our understanding of extinct species.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: archaeology - the study of human history through material remains

Etymology

From French 'paléontologie', from Greek 'palaios' (ancient), 'on' (being), and 'logia' (study).
