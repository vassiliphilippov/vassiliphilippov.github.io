Chilomycterus

1. NOUN

/ kɪˌloʊmɪkˈtɛrəs / (AmE), / kɪləʊmɪkˈtɪərəs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical marine fishes known as burrfishes, characterized by a round body covered with spines that can inflate when threatened.
genus of spiny tropical fish (burrfishes)

Examples

The Chilomycterus relies on its ability to inflate its spiny body as a defense against predators.
Researchers studied several species within the genus Chilomycterus found in Caribbean waters.
Local fishermen occasionally catch Chilomycterus, also known as burrfish, during their nightly trips.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burrfish - a fish with a globular spiny body that can inflate; member of Chilomycterus genus

Etymology

From Greek 'cheilos' (lip) + 'mykter' (nose), referencing features of these fishes.

2. NOUN

/ kaɪloʊˈmɪktərəs / (AmE), / kɪləʊˈmɪktərəs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical marine fishes in the family Diodontidae, commonly known as burrfishes, characterized by their spiny bodies and ability to inflate themselves as a defense mechanism.
genus of tropical burrfish

Examples

The Chilomycterus genus includes several species of spiny puffers found in warm ocean waters.
A marine biologist discovered a new species within the Chilomycterus genus off the coast of Brazil.
When threatened, fish of the Chilomycterus genus can inflate their bodies and display sharp spines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burrfish - a fish with spines that can inflate itself

Etymology

From Greek 'cheilos' (lip) and 'mykter' (nose), referring to features of the fish.
