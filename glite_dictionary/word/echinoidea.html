Echinoidea

1. NOUN

/ ɛˌkaɪnəˈɔɪdiə / (AmE), / ɪˌkɪnɔɪˈdiːə / (BrE)
a class of marine echinoderms that includes sea urchins and sand dollars, characterized by a hard, spiny shell
class of sea urchins and sand dollars

Examples

The class Echinoidea includes all sea urchins and sand dollars found in oceans worldwide.
Scientists study the skeletons of Echinoidea to learn about marine evolution.
Echinoidea are known for their spiny shells and unique locomotion mechanisms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea urchin - a spiny marine animal in the Echinoidea class
  • Synonym: sand dollar - a flat burrowing sea urchin in the Echinoidea class

Etymology

From Greek ekhinos (sea urchin) + -oeidēs (form, likeness).
