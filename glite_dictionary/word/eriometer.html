eriometer

1. NOUN

/ ɛriˈɑːmətər / (AmE), / ˌɪəriˈɒmɪtə / (BrE)
An instrument used for measuring the diameters of very small particles or fibers by examining the diffraction patterns of light.
instrument for measuring tiny diameters via light diffraction

Examples

The scientist used an eriometer to determine the thickness of the fiber samples.
Advances in eriometer technology have improved measurements in textile research.
By analyzing the diffraction patterns, the eriometer provided precise data on particle size.

Etymology

From Greek 'erion' (wool) + 'meter' (measure).
