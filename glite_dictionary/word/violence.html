violence

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪə.ləns / (AmE), / ˈvaɪə.ləns / (BrE)
the use of physical force intended to harm people or damage property, often resulting in injury or destruction
use of physical force causing harm or destruction

Examples

The protests erupted into violence after nightfall, leaving several injured.
Many communities are working to reduce violence among young people.
The movie was criticized for its graphic depiction of violence.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: peace - a state or period in which there is no war or violence
  • Synonym: aggression - hostile or violent behavior or attitudes

Etymology

From Latin violentia (vehemence, impetuosity).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪələns / (AmE), / ˈvaɪələns / (BrE)
the quality of being wild, forceful, or turbulent, especially in natural forces or events
wild or turbulent quality

Examples

The violence of the hurricane left the coastline devastated for miles.
We marveled at the violence of the river's rapids after the heavy rain.
The mountain shook with the violence of the eruption.

Synset Examples

the storm's violence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ferocity - the state of being fierce or savagely intense
  • Antonym: gentleness - the quality of being mild or calm

Etymology

From Latin violentia (vehemence, impetuosity), from violentus (violent).

3. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪə.ləns / (AmE), / ˈvaɪə.ləns / (BrE)
the use of physical force to harm someone or damage something, especially as an act of aggression against resistance
use of physical force or aggression

Examples

The protesters condemned all forms of violence during the demonstrations.
He believed that violence was never a solution to any problem.
There was a sudden outbreak of violence in the city last night.

Synset Examples

he may accomplish by craft in the long run what he cannot do by force and violence in the short one

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aggression - hostile or violent behavior
  • Antonym: peace - absence of violence or conflict

Etymology

From Latin 'violentia', from 'violentus' (vehement, forceful).
