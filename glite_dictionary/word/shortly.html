shortly

1. ADV

/ ˈʃɔrtli / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːtli / (BrE)
for a short time; for a brief period
for a brief period

Examples

He waited shortly for the bus before deciding to walk instead.
The meeting only lasted shortly because everyone agreed quickly.
She stayed shortly in the café before heading home.

Synset Examples

he was at the airport shortly before she was expected to arrive

Related Terms

  • Synonym: briefly - for a short time

Etymology

From Middle English shortly, from short + -ly.

2. ADV

/ ˈʃɔrtli / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːtli / (BrE)
in a short time from now; soon
in the near future

Examples

The guests will arrive shortly, so please set the table.
The announcement will be made shortly after the meeting begins.
Our train is scheduled to depart shortly.

Synset Examples

the doctor will soon be here

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soon - in a short time; before long
  • Synonym: imminently - happening very soon
  • Antonym: eventually - at some time in the future; not immediately

Etymology

From Middle English 'shortli', from Old English 'sceortlīċe', from 'sceort' (short).

4. ADV

/ ˈʃɔːrtli / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːtli / (BrE)
in a curt, abrupt, and often discourteous manner; in a blunt or terse way.
in a blunt, curt manner

Examples

He replied shortly and then walked away, clearly not interested in continuing the conversation.
The manager spoke shortly to the team, making his dissatisfaction obvious.
She was known to answer questions shortly when she was in a bad mood.

Synset Examples

he told me curtly to get on with it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curtly - in a curt, abrupt, or brusque manner
  • Synonym: abruptly - in an abrupt, sudden, or brusque manner

Etymology

From Middle English shortli, from Old English sceortlīce (‘in a short manner’), referring originally to time or length.
