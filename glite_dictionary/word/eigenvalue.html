eigenvalue

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪɡənˌvælju / (AmE), / ˈaɪɡənˌvæljuː / (BrE)
(mathematics) a scalar associated with a square matrix; any number such that subtracting it times the identity matrix from the given matrix results in a matrix with zero determinant.
value associated with a matrix in linear algebra
medical

Examples

The eigenvalues of the matrix determine the stability of the system.
To solve the equation, first calculate the eigenvalue of the given matrix.
Each eigenvalue corresponds to a specific eigenvector of the matrix.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eigenvector - vector associated with a matrix that is scaled by the eigenvalue

Etymology

From German *Eigenwert*, from *eigen* (own, characteristic) + *Wert* (value).

2. NOUN

/ ˈaɪɡənˌvæl.ju / (AmE), / ˈaɪɡənˌval.juː / (BrE)
(Mathematics) a number such that, for a given square matrix, subtracting that number times the identity matrix from the matrix results in a zero determinant.
a special number associated with a matrix

Examples

Each eigenvalue of the matrix corresponds to a unique solution of the characteristic equation.
The largest eigenvalue often determines the stability of the system.
Calculating the eigenvalue is a key step in many linear algebra problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eigenvector - a nonzero vector that changes at most by a scalar factor when a linear transformation is applied

Etymology

From German 'eigen' (own, proper) + English 'value'; introduced in mathematical contexts in German.

3. NOUN

/ ˈaɪɡənˌvælju / (AmE), / ˈaɪɡənˌvaljuː / (BrE)
(Mathematics) A scalar associated with a square matrix that, when subtracted from the matrix multiplied by the identity matrix, results in a matrix with zero determinant; equivalently, a number λ for which there exists a nonzero vector v such that Av = λv.
number associated with a square matrix in linear algebra
medical

Examples

The eigenvalue problem is central to studying vibrations in engineering systems.
Each eigenvalue tells us something important about the stability of the matrix.
The matrix has three distinct eigenvalues corresponding to different directions in space.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eigenvector - a nonzero vector changed only in scale when a linear transformation is applied

Etymology

From German Eigenwert, from eigen (own) + Wert (value); adapted into English in mathematical usage.
