Ulmus rubra

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌlməs ˈruːbrə / (AmE), / ˈʌlməs ˈruːbrə / (BrE)
A species of elm native to North America, distinguished by rough leaves that are reddish when young, and known for its hard wood.
North American elm tree species

Examples

The inner bark of the Ulmus rubra has long been used in herbal remedies.
Many North American forests contain large Ulmus rubra trees.
Furniture makers value Ulmus rubra for its hard, durable wood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slippery elm - common name for Ulmus rubra

Etymology

From Latin: genus name Ulmus (elm), species rubra (red), referring to the reddish young leaves.
