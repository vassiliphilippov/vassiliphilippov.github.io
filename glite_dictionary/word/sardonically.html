sardonically

1. ADV

/ sɑːrˈdɑːnɪkli / (AmE), / sɑːˈdɒnɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that shows irony, mockery, or bitter sarcasm.
in a mocking or sarcastic way

Examples

He smiled sardonically when asked if he enjoyed the meeting.
"That went well," she remarked sardonically, after the disastrous presentation.
The director sardonically thanked the critics for their "helpful" reviews.

Synset Examples

‘Ah, now we're getting at the truth,’ he interposed sarcastically

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sarcastically - in a sarcastic manner

Etymology

From 'sardonic' + '-ally', 'sardonic' from Greek 'sardonios' (bitter or scornful laughter).
