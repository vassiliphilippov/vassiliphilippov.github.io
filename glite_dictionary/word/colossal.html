colossal

1. ADJ

/ kəˈlɑsəl / (AmE), / kəˈlɒsəl / (BrE)
extremely large in size, amount, or degree; immense or gigantic, often causing awe or admiration
extremely large; immense

Examples

The ancient amphitheater was a truly colossal structure, capable of seating thousands of spectators.
They faced a colossal task in rebuilding the city after the earthquake.
The company suffered colossal losses due to the failed merger.

Synset Examples

colossal crumbling ruins of an ancient temple
has a colossal nerve
a prodigious storm
a stupendous field of grass
stupendous demand

Related Terms

  • Synonym: huge - very large in size, amount, or degree
  • Synonym: gigantic - extremely large, like a giant
  • Antonym: tiny - extremely small
  • Synonym: enormous - very large in size, quantity, or extent

Etymology

From French *colossal*, from Italian *colossale*, from Latin *colossus* (a giant statue).
