schwa

1. NOUN

/ ʃwɑ / (AmE), / ʃwɑː / (BrE)
the mid-central, neutral vowel sound, typically occurring in unstressed syllables, represented in IPA by the symbol ə.
mid-central neutral vowel sound

Examples

The schwa sound is very common in English, especially in unstressed syllables.
In the word 'banana', the first and last vowels are pronounced as schwa.
Many beginner learners struggle to identify the schwa when listening to native speech.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neutral vowel - a vowel sound produced with tongue in a neutral position

Etymology

From Hebrew *shĕwā* (meaning 'emptiness' or 'zero'), adopted into German as 'Schwa' and then into English.

2. NOUN

/ ʃwɑ / (AmE), / ʃwɑː / (BrE)
the unstressed mid-central vowel sound (ə) found in many English words, occurring in syllables that are not stressed
unstressed mid-central vowel sound

Examples

The word 'sofa' ends with a schwa sound.
In English, the schwa often occurs in unaccented syllables.
Children learn that the schwa can be spelled with different letters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ə - phonetic symbol for the schwa sound

Etymology

From German 'Schwa', from Hebrew 'shĕwāʼ' (meaning 'emptiness', a grammatical term).
