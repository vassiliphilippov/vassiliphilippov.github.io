deportment

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈpɔrtmənt / (AmE), / dɪˈpɔːtmənt / (BrE)
The way in which a person behaves, especially in relation to others; conduct or manner.
a person's behavior or conduct

Examples

Her excellent deportment impressed everyone at the formal event.
The teacher praised the student's deportment in the classroom.
Good deportment is expected during the ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conduct - a person's behavior in a particular place or situation
  • Synonym: behavior - the way in which one acts or conducts oneself, especially toward others

Etymology

From French *déportement* (conduct), from Latin *deportare* (to carry away).
