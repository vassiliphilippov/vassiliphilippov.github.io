Canis adustus

1. NOUN

/ ˌkeɪ.nɪs əˈdʌs.təs / (AmE), / ˌkaː.nɪs əˈdʌs.təs / (BrE)
A species of jackal native to sub-Saharan Africa, characterized by a distinctive stripe along each side.
a species of African jackal

Examples

The Canis adustus is well adapted to the savannah and woodland habitats of southern Africa.
Researchers observed a pack of Canis adustus hunting small mammals at dusk.
Unlike other jackals, the Canis adustus prefers habitats with thick vegetation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: side-striped jackal - a common name for Canis adustus, an African jackal species

Etymology

From Latin: 'Canis' meaning dog and 'adustus' meaning scorched or burned (referring to the coloration).
