disloyalty

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈlɔɪəlti / (AmE), / dɪsˈlɔɪəlti / (BrE)
Lack of loyalty; the quality of not being faithful or supportive to a person, group, or cause.
lack of loyalty

Examples

Her disloyalty to the team surprised everyone after years of commitment.
Acts of disloyalty can deeply damage relationships and trust.
He regretted his disloyalty when he realized what he had lost.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: loyalty - the quality of being loyal

Etymology

From prefix 'dis-' expressing reversal + 'loyalty', from Old French 'loialté'.
