to counterpose

1. VERB

/ ˌkaʊntərˈpoʊz / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntəˈpəʊz / (BrE)
to constitute a counterweight or counterbalance to something
act as a counterbalance

Examples

The company uses innovative strategies to counterpose the risks faced in the marketplace.
Her optimism seemed to counterpose his constant pessimism during the meeting.
The new policy was designed to counterpose the negative effects of rising costs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: counterbalance - to offset or balance the effect of something

Etymology

From Latin 'contra' (against) + 'pose' (put, place).
