memsahib

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛmˌsɑːhɪb / (AmE), / ˈmem.sɑː.hɪb / (BrE)
(historical, in South Asia) a term of respect for a European woman, especially the wife of a British official.
European woman in colonial India

Examples

During the British Raj, the local staff addressed her as memsahib.
In the novel, the memsahib lives in a grand bungalow outside the city.
The memsahib supervised the servants and organized lavish tea parties.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sahib - term of respect for a man, especially in colonial South Asia

Etymology

From Hindi 'mem sāhib', combining 'mem' (ma'am, madam) and 'sāhib' (sir, master).
