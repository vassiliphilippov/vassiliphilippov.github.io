forgiveness

1. NOUN

/ fərˈɡɪvnəs / (AmE), / fəˈɡɪvnəs / (BrE)
The act of forgiving someone for a mistake or wrong, showing a readiness to stop feeling anger or resentment.
the act of forgiving someone

Examples

She found forgiveness in her heart for those who had wronged her.
Many religions emphasize the importance of forgiveness and compassion.
His request for forgiveness was met with understanding and grace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pardon - the act of excusing a mistake or offense
  • Antonym: vengeance - punishment inflicted in return for a wrong

Etymology

From Old English 'forgiefnes', from 'forgiefan' (to forgive).

2. NOUN

/ fərˈɡɪvnəs / (AmE), / fəˈɡɪvnɪs / (BrE)
The act of forgiving someone for a mistake or offense; the process of stopping blaming or being angry with someone.
the act of forgiving someone

Examples

Her forgiveness of his mistake brought relief to both of them.
He begged for forgiveness after realizing the harm he had caused.
Practicing forgiveness can help heal emotional wounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pardon - the act of forgiving or being forgiven for an error or offense
  • Antonym: revenge - the action of hurting someone in return for something they did

Etymology

From Old English 'forgiefenes', from 'forgiefan' (to forgive).
