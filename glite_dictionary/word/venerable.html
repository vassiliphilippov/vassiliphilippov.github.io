venerable

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɛn(ə)rəbəl / (AmE), / ˈvɛn(ə)rəbl / (BrE)
deserving a great deal of respect, especially because of age, wisdom, or character.
deserving deep respect

Examples

The venerable professor was known for his decades of groundbreaking research.
They entered the venerable old cathedral in silence, awed by its history.
The award was presented to a venerable leader in the community for her lifelong service.

Synset Examples

revered holy men

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revered - deeply respected or admired
  • Synonym: respected - admired or esteemed by others
  • Antonym: disreputable - not considered to be respectable

Etymology

From Latin venerabilis (worthy of respect), from venerari (to revere).

2. ADJ

/ ˈvɛnərəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈvɛnərəbl̩ / (BrE)
deserving respect because of great age, wisdom, or character; impressive by reason of age
respected or impressive due to age

Examples

The venerable oak tree has stood in the village square for over 300 years.
We visited a venerable library founded in the Middle Ages.
The family gathered to celebrate their venerable grandmother's 100th birthday.

Synset Examples

a venerable sage with white hair and beard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revered - deeply respected or admired
  • Degree Higher: ancient - very old; having existed for a very long time

Etymology

From Latin venerabilis (worthy of reverence), from venerari (to revere).
