highball

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈhaɪˌbɔːl / (BrE)
A mixed drink consisting of alcoholic liquor combined with water or a carbonated beverage and served in a tall glass.
a mixed drink with liquor and carbonated beverage

Examples

He ordered a highball at the bar before joining his friends at their table.
The waiter served the highball in a tall glass filled with ice and soda.
For a refreshing summer drink, try mixing whiskey and ginger ale for a classic highball.

Etymology

Probably from the American railroad signal term 'highball' meaning clear track ahead, later used to signify a quick drink.
