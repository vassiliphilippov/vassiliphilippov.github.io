Malope trifida

1. NOUN

/ məˈloʊpi traɪˈfaɪdə / (AmE), / məˈləʊpi trɪˈfɪdə / (BrE)
A flowering plant native to the western Mediterranean, known for its deep purple-red flowers surrounded by three large heart-shaped bracts.
Mediterranean flowering plant with purple-red flowers

Examples

Malope trifida brightens many Mediterranean gardens each spring with its vibrant blooms.
The three large bracts make Malope trifida a distinctive addition to floral arrangements.
Gardeners appreciate how easily Malope trifida can be grown from seed in sunny spots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mallow - flowering plant of the Malvaceae family

Etymology

From Latin; 'Malope' is the genus, and 'trifida' means 'three-cleft' or 'three-parted', referring to the bracts.
