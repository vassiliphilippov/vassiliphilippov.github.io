parity

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛr.ɪ.ti / (AmE), / ˈpær.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
(Computer science) a technique in which an extra bit is added to a group of binary digits to make the total number of 1's either odd or even, used for error detection.
error-checking bit in computing

Examples

The system uses parity to detect simple transmission errors in the data.
When the parity check fails, the hardware flags an error.
Odd parity adds a bit so the sum of ones is always odd.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parity bit - a bit that is added to ensure parity

Etymology

From Latin paritas (equality), from par (equal).

5. NOUN

/ ˈpɛr.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˈpær.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
(Physics) The property of a physical system where the equations describing its laws remain unchanged if the system's spatial coordinates are inverted, as in a mirror reflection.
symmetry under spatial inversion (physics)
medical

Examples

The principle of parity conservation was once thought to be universal in physics.
Experimental results showed that parity is not conserved in weak nuclear interactions.
Physicists discuss whether certain subatomic processes violate parity.
