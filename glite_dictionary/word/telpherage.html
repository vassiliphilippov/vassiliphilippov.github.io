telpherage

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛlfərɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈtɛlfərɪdʒ / (BrE)
a system of transportation in which electrically powered cars are suspended from cables and used to carry goods or people
electrical cable transport system

Examples

The mining company relies on telpherage to move ore across difficult terrain.
Many factories in the early 20th century installed telpherage for efficient material handling.
This old photograph shows workers maintaining the telpherage lines above the valley.

Etymology

From Greek 'tel-' (far) + 'pherein' (to carry), modeled after 'telpher'.
