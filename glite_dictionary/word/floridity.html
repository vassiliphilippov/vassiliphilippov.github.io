floridity

1. NOUN

/ fləˈrɪdəti / (AmE), / fləˈrɪdəti / (BrE)
Extravagant or elaborate style, especially in writing or speech, characterized by ornate or flowery language.
elaborate or flowery style

Examples

The author's floridity distracted from the main message of the essay.
Some critics argue that excessive floridity can make poetry hard to understand.
Her speeches were known for their floridity and elaborate metaphors.

Synset Examples

he wrote with great flamboyance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flamboyance - showy or elaborate style; extravagant display, especially in art or literature
  • Antonym: plainness - lack of embellishment; the quality of being simple and unadorned

Etymology

From Latin 'floridus' (flowery), via French and Late Latin.
