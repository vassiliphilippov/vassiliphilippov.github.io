derelict

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛrəlɪkt / (AmE), / ˈdɛrəlɪkt / (BrE)
a ship that has been abandoned on the high seas and left without crew or claim
abandoned ship at sea

Examples

The crew was rescued, but their derelict drifted for weeks before being found.
Maritime law has special rules for salvaging a derelict.
The old sailor told stories of encountering a mysterious derelict in foggy waters.

Etymology

From Latin derelictus (forsaken, abandoned), past participle of derelinquere (to abandon).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɛrəˌlɪkt / (AmE), / ˈdɛrɪˌlɪkt / (BrE)
a person without a home, job, or property, often neglected by society
homeless and jobless person

Examples

The city created new shelters to support every derelict living on the streets.
He offered a warm meal to a derelict he met near the station.
Society too often ignores the plight of the modern derelict.

Etymology

From Latin derelictus (abandoned), from derelinquere (to abandon).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10025743-n
Synset POS: n

/ ˈdɛrəlɪkt / (AmE), / ˈdɛrəlɪkt / (BrE)
(of a building or place) abandoned and neglected, especially by the owner or inhabitants.

Examples

Several families moved away, leaving the apartment block derelict and overgrown with weeds.
Photographers love exploring derelict factories for their eerie, atmospheric beauty.
