expiative

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛkspiˌeɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈɛkspɪətɪv / (BrE)
having the power to atone for wrongdoing or serving as a means of expiation or propitiation.
serving to atone for wrongdoing

Examples

The ritual was considered an expiative act, intended to cleanse the community of guilt.
Many ancient cultures offered expiative sacrifices to appease their gods.
His apology struck a deeply expiative note, acknowledging the hurt he had caused.

Synset Examples

expiatory (or propitiatory) sacrifice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expiatory - serving to expiate or atone for something

Etymology

From Latin expiare (to atone for) via Late Latin expiativus.
