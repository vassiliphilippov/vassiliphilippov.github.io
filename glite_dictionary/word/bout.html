bout

1. NOUN

/ baʊt / (AmE), / baʊt / (BrE)
A short period during which someone suffers from an illness or experiences a particular condition or activity.
a short period of illness or condition

Examples

She had a severe bout of flu that kept her in bed for a week.
After a brief bout of anxiety, he felt much better.
The child experienced a bout of vomiting after eating spoiled food.

Synset Examples

a bout of fever
a bout of depression

Related Terms

  • Synonym: episode - a single occurrence or period of a condition or event

Etymology

Perhaps from late Middle English bought, probably of Scandinavian origin; related to Old Norse bauta (to strike or fell).

2. NOUN

/ baʊt / (AmE), / baʊt / (BrE)
a contest or fight, especially in boxing or wrestling
contest or fight, esp. in boxing/wrestling

Examples

The championship bout lasted for twelve intense rounds before a winner was declared.
He trained hard for months to prepare for the upcoming bout against his rival.
Spectators cheered loudly throughout the wrestling bout at the arena.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: match - an organized contest between two or more people or teams

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *bouten* (to alternate), related to the notion of turn or round.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07471697-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ baʊt / (AmE), / baʊt / (BrE)
A short period during which a particular activity, especially excessive eating or drinking, takes place.
brief period of excessive activity

Examples

After his exam, he went on a bout of overeating to celebrate.
The group gathered for a bout of drinking that lasted late into the night.
Following a stressful week, she indulged in a bout of comfort food.

Synset Examples

they went on a bust that lasted three days

Related Terms

  • Synonym: binge - a period of excessive indulgence, especially in eating or drinking

Etymology

Probably from French 'bout' (end, limit, turn).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00511734-n
Synset POS: n
