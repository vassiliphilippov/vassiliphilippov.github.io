suite

1. NOUN

/ swit / (AmE), / swiːt / (BrE)
a set of matching furniture pieces designed to be used together, typically for a particular room
matching set of furniture

Examples

We bought a new living room suite with a sofa and two armchairs.
The bedroom suite includes a bed, two nightstands, and a wardrobe.
She chose a dining suite that matched the style of her home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: set - a collection of things that belong together or are used together

Etymology

From French 'suite' (sequence, set), from Old French, from 'suivre' (to follow).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ swit / (AmE), / swiːt / (BrE)
A group of attendants or followers accompanying an important person, especially in a formal or ceremonial context.
group of attendants to a person

Examples

The king entered the hall, surrounded by his loyal suite.
She traveled across Europe with a small suite of advisors and companions.
At the royal wedding, the princess was accompanied by her official suite.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retinue - a group of advisors or attendants accompanying an important person
  • Synonym: entourage - a group of people attending or surrounding an important person

Etymology

From French 'suite', from Old French 'siute', meaning 'act of following'.

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ swit / (AmE), / swiːt / (BrE)
a set of musical pieces, usually in the same key, performed together as a single composition
a set of connected musical pieces

Examples

The orchestra performed a renowned suite composed by Bach.
She enjoys listening to the Nutcracker suite during the holidays.
Each movement in the suite has its own unique character.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sonata - a musical composition typically of three or four movements for solo instrument, often with piano accompaniment

Etymology

From French 'suite' (a sequence), from Latin 'sequī' (to follow).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07061069-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ swit / (AmE), / swiːt / (BrE)
A set of connected rooms, especially in a hotel, used as a single living unit.
set of connected rooms in a hotel

Examples

The presidential suite offers breathtaking views of the city skyline.
We booked a spacious suite for our family vacation at the resort.
Their honeymoon suite included a private balcony and a hot tub.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apartment - a set of rooms used as a dwelling
  • Degree Lower: room - a single enclosed space in a building

Etymology

From French 'suite' (a sequence), derived from Old French 'suir' (to follow).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04358628-n
Synset POS: n
