inhospitable

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.hɑːˈspɪ.t̬ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˌɪn.hɒˈspɪ.tə.bəl / (BrE)
(of a place or environment) harsh and difficult to live in; not offering shelter or support to life or growth.
harsh and difficult to live in

Examples

The climbers faced inhospitable weather conditions at the summit.
Few plants can survive the inhospitable environment of the Arctic tundra.
The abandoned house stood in an inhospitable stretch of land far from civilization.

Synset Examples

the barren inhospitable desert
inhospitable mountain areas

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hospitable - welcoming and friendly, especially to guests or strangers

Etymology

From Medieval Latin inhospitabilis (unwelcoming), from Latin in- (not) + hospitabilis (hospitable).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.hɑːˈspɪ.t̬ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˌɪn.hɒˈspɪ.tə.bəl / (BrE)
(of a person or environment) unwelcoming or harsh; not friendly or suitable for living or visiting.
unwelcoming; harsh or unfriendly

Examples

The Arctic tundra is one of the most inhospitable regions on Earth.
His inhospitable manner made guests feel unwelcome at the party.
The abandoned house had an inhospitable atmosphere that kept people away.

Synset Examples

they are extremely inhospitable these days
her greeting was cold and inhospitable

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hospitable - welcoming and friendly to guests or strangers

Etymology

From in- (not) + hospitable; ultimately from Latin 'hospes' (guest, host).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01247069-a
Synset POS: a
