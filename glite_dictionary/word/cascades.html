Cascades

1. PROPN

/ kæsˈkeɪdz / (AmE), / kasˈkeɪdz / (BrE)
a major mountain range in the northwestern United States, extending through Washington, Oregon, and northern California, known for its volcanic peaks
mountain range in northwestern US

Examples

Mount Rainier is the highest peak in the Cascades and a popular destination for climbers.
Heavy snowfall is common in the Cascades during the winter months.
Many rivers in Washington State originate from glaciers in the Cascades.

Etymology

From French cascade (waterfall), referring to leaping or falling water, likely inspired by waterfalls found in the range.
