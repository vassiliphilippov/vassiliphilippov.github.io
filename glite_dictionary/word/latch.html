latch

1. NOUN

/ lætʃ / (AmE), / lætʃ / (BrE)
A fastening device for a door or gate, often spring-loaded, that holds it closed and can only be opened from the outside with a key.
spring-loaded doorlock requiring a key

Examples

He fumbled with the key, trying to open the latch on the front door.
Make sure that the latch is locked before you leave the house.
The old garden gate has a rusty latch that sticks in cold weather.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lock - a mechanical device used to fasten doors, requiring a key or combination

Etymology

From Middle English lacche, from Old English læccan (to catch).

2. VERB

/ lætʃ / (AmE), / lætʃ / (BrE)
to fasten (a door, gate, or window) with a latch
fasten with a latch

Examples

Please remember to latch the door before you leave the house.
She carefully latched the window to keep out the cold air.
Make sure you latch the gate so the dog doesn't get out.

Synset Examples

latch the door

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unlatch - to open or unfasten a latch

Etymology

From Middle English la(t)che, from Old French lache (latch, fastening).

Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ lætʃ / (AmE), / lætʃ / (BrE)
a device for keeping a door or gate closed, consisting of a bar that drops or slides into a groove or notch
fastening device for doors or gates

Examples

He heard the latch click as the door closed behind him.
Before leaving, she slid the latch into place to secure the gate.
The old wooden chest had a rusty latch that was difficult to open.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bolt - metal bar for securing doors or gates
  • Synonym: lock - mechanical device for securing doors

Etymology

Probably from Middle English lacche, from Old English læcce (catch, trap), related to 'catch' and 'lock'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03650172-n
Synset POS: n
