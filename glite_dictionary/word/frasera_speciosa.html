Frasera speciosa

1. PROPN

/ frəˈsɪərə spəˈsioʊsə / (AmE), / frəˈsɪərə spɛˈsiːəʊsə / (BrE)
A tall herb of the gentian family with panicles of white flowers tinged with green, native to the northwestern United States; sometimes classified in the genus Swertia.
tall herb with white-green flowers, NW US

Examples

The meadows were dotted with the tall Frasera speciosa, its white-green blossoms standing out among the grasses.
Botanists sometimes debate whether Frasera speciosa should be classified under the genus Swertia.
During our hike in Idaho, we admired the blooming Frasera speciosa along the trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Swertia - A related genus of flowering plants sometimes including Frasera species

Etymology

From the genus name Frasera, honoring Scottish botanist John Fraser, and Latin speciosa meaning 'showy' or 'splendid'.
