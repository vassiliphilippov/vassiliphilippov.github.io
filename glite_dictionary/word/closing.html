closing

1. NOUN

/ ˈkloʊzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkləʊzɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of shutting or bringing something to an end, especially a door, meeting, or business activity
the act of shutting or ending something

Examples

The sudden closing of the store surprised many customers.
We attended the closing of the conference on Friday afternoon.
The closing of the door echoed through the empty hallway.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: opening - the act of making something open

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'close' plus the suffix '-ing'.

4. NOUN

/ ˈkloʊ.zɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkləʊ.zɪŋ / (BrE)
the final or concluding part of an event, process, or activity
final part or conclusion of something

Examples

The ceremony ended with a formal closing and everyone applauded.
Her remarks served as the closing to the conference.
At the closing of the store, the doors were locked.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ending - the final part of something
  • Antonym: opening - the beginning or first part of something

Etymology

From the verb 'close', from Old French 'clore', from Latin 'claudere' (to shut).
