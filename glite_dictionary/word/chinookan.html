Chinookan

1. NOUN

/ tʃɪˈnʊkən / (AmE), / tʃɪˈnʊkən / (BrE)
A Penutian language traditionally spoken by the Chinook people of the Pacific Northwest region of the United States.
language of the Chinook people

Examples

She is studying Chinookan as part of her research on indigenous languages.
The dictionary contains many entries for words in Chinookan.
Only a few elders in the community still speak Chinookan fluently.

Etymology

From the name Chinook, the indigenous people of the lower Columbia River, plus English -an, denoting association.
