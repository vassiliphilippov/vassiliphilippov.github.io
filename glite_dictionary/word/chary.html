chary

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃɛri / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛːri / (BrE)
cautious or wary; unwilling to take risks.
cautious; wary

Examples

She was chary of investing her savings in the volatile stock market.
He gave a chary response, avoiding any firm commitments.
Many people remain chary about using new technology until it has been proven safe.

Synset Examples

a cagey avoidance of a definite answer
chary of the risks involved
a chary investor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cautious - careful to avoid potential problems or dangers
  • Antonym: reckless - without thinking or caring about the consequences of an action

Etymology

From Old English 'cēariġ' (sorrowful, careful).
