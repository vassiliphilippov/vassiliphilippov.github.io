thermocouple

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɜr.moʊˌkʌp.əl / (AmE), / ˈθɜː.məʊˌkʌp.əl / (BrE)
A device consisting of two wires made from different metals joined at both ends, used to measure temperature by producing a voltage dependent on the temperature difference between the junctions.
temperature-measuring device of two wires

Examples

The engineer used a thermocouple to monitor the furnace temperature precisely during the experiment.
A thermocouple generates a small voltage when there is a temperature difference between its two junctions.
The technician installed a new thermocouple in the boiler to ensure accurate readings.

Etymology

From thermo- (relating to heat) + couple (pair of things).
