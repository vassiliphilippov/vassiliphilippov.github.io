haematocoele

1. NOUN

/ hiːˈmætəˌkoʊl / (AmE), / hɪˈmætəkəʊl / (BrE)
A swelling caused by the accumulation of blood in a body cavity, especially under the membrane covering the testis.
swelling from blood in a cavity, esp. around the testis
medical

Examples

The surgeon diagnosed a haematocoele after noticing the swelling in the patient's scrotum.
Immediate treatment was necessary to prevent complications from the haematocoele.
A haematocoele can occur following trauma to the testicular area.

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) + koilia (hollow, cavity), via medical Latin.
