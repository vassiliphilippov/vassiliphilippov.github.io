matriarchy

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪ.triˌɑr.ki / (AmE), / ˈmeɪ.triː.ɑː.ki / (BrE)
A social system in which the mother or a female leader is the head of the family or community, and descent and inheritance are traced through the female line.
female-led social system

Examples

Some anthropologists have studied communities structured as a matriarchy, where women hold primary leadership roles.
The inheritance laws in the matriarchy ensured property was passed from mother to daughter.
Legends often describe a golden age when society was governed by a matriarchy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: patriarchy - a social system where the father or male is the head of the family and descent is traced through the male line

Etymology

From Latin 'matriarchia', from 'mater' (mother) + Greek 'arkhē' (rule).
