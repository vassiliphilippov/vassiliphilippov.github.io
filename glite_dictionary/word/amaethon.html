Amaethon

1. PROPN

/ əˈmaɪ.əθ.ɒn / (AmE), / əˈmaɪ.əθ.ɒn / (BrE)
A deity in Welsh mythology, regarded as the ancient god of agriculture and farming.
Welsh god of agriculture

Examples

In Welsh mythology, Amaethon is revered as the patron of farmers and crops.
Legends tell that Amaethon brought agriculture and prosperity to the land.
The story of Amaethon emphasizes the importance of farming in ancient Welsh culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Demeter - Greek goddess of the harvest and agriculture
  • Synonym: Ceres - Roman goddess of agriculture

Etymology

From Welsh 'Amaethon', related to 'amaeth' meaning 'ploughman' or 'farmer'.
