giddily

1. ADV

/ ˈɡɪdɪli / (AmE), / ˈɡɪdɪli / (BrE)
in a way that shows or involves a feeling of dizziness or light-headedness, or a sense of silly excitement
in a dizzy or light-hearted manner

Examples

She spun giddily around the room, laughing at nothing in particular.
After hearing the good news, he smiled giddily and couldn't sit still.
The children ran giddily through the field, filled with excitement and energy.

Synset Examples

he walked around dizzily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dizzily - in a way that is affected by dizziness or vertigo

Etymology

From 'giddy' + '-ly'; 'giddy' from Old English 'gidig' (insane, mad).
