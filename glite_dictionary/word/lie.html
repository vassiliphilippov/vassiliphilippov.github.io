lie

1. VERB

/ laɪ / (AmE), / laɪ / (BrE)
to be in a horizontal or resting position on a surface
be in a horizontal position

Examples

The cat likes to lie in the sun all afternoon.
Several coats were lying on the floor by the door.
He had lain awake on the bed for hours, staring at the ceiling.

Synset Examples

The sick man lay in bed all day
the books are lying on the shelf

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to recline - to lean or lie back in a relaxed position
  • Antonym: to sit - to be in a position in which your body is resting on a chair or the ground but not lying

Etymology

From Old English *licgan*, related to German *liegen*.
