firebox

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪɚˌbɑks / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəbɒks / (BrE)
the chamber of a furnace, especially in a steam locomotive, where fuel is burned to produce heat
furnace chamber for burning fuel

Examples

The engineer opened the firebox to check if there was enough coal for the journey.
A sudden burst of heat escaped when he stoked the firebox.
Modern wood stoves may also have a firebox lined with firebrick.
