Banksia

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæŋk.si.ə / (AmE), / ˈbaŋk.sɪə / (BrE)
A genus of Australian evergreen shrubs or trees with leathery leaves and spikes of yellowish flowers, noted for their distinctive cone-like flower heads.
genus of Australian evergreen shrubs or trees

Examples

The Banksia is a well-known symbol of Australian native flora.
Gardeners often cultivate Banksia species for their striking flower spikes and ability to attract birds.
We observed a magnificent Banksia tree blooming along the coastal path.

Etymology

Named after Sir Joseph Banks (1743–1820), British naturalist and explorer who collected specimens during Captain Cook's voyage to Australia.
