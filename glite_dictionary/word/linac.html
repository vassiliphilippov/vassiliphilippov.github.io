linac

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪˌnæk / (AmE), / ˈlɪn.ak / (BrE)
a device in which charged particles are accelerated in a straight line by a sequence of alternating voltages, used in physics and medicine
linear particle accelerator

Examples

The hospital recently installed a new linac for more precise cancer treatments.
Engineers calibrated the linac to achieve higher particle energies.
Graduate students used the linac in their physics experiments on particle collisions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: linear accelerator - a machine that accelerates charged particles in a straight line

Etymology

Shortened form of 'linear accelerator'.
