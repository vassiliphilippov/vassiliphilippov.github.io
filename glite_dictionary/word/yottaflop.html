yottaflop

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɑtəˌflɑp / (AmE), / ˈjɒtəˌflɒp / (BrE)
(computer science) A unit of computing speed equal to one septillion (10^24) floating-point operations per second.
unit: one septillion floating-point operations per second

Examples

The new supercomputer can execute one yottaflop of calculations per second, setting a world record.
Scientists predict that machines reaching yottaflop speeds will revolutionize climate modeling.
Achieving sustained yottaflop performance is a major milestone in high-performance computing.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: petaflop - A unit of computing speed equal to one quadrillion floating-point operations per second

Etymology

Formed from 'yotta-' (a prefix denoting 10^24, from Italian 'otto' meaning eight, for the eighth power in SI units) and 'flop' (floating-point operations per second).
