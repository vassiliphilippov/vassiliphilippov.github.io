thickness

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɪknəs / (AmE), / ˈθɪknəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of a line or mark being wide or broad from side to side
width of a line or mark

Examples

The thickness of the line made the drawing stand out.
You can adjust the thickness of the brush in the software.
Markers come in various thicknesses for different artistic effects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: width - the measurement or extent of something from side to side
  • Antonym: thinness - the quality of being narrow or not wide

2. NOUN

/ ˈθɪknəs / (AmE), / ˈθɪknəs / (BrE)
(of speech) the quality of being indistinct or difficult to understand, often due to slurring or loss of clarity, especially as a result of intoxication or illness.
indistinctness of speech

Examples

The thickness of her words made it hard to understand what she was saying.
After his surgery, a noticeable thickness crept into his speech.
There was a thickness to his voice that suggested he was exhausted.

Synset Examples

judging from the thickness of his speech he had been drinking heavily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slurring - Speaking indistinctly so that words run together

3. NOUN

/ ˈθɪknəs / (AmE), / ˈθɪknɪs / (BrE)
The quality of a liquid that makes it resistant to flow; viscosity.
resistance of a liquid to flow

Examples

Honey has a greater thickness than water, which makes it pour more slowly.
The thickness of the paint determines how easily it spreads on the wall.
Engine oil is chosen for its thickness to ensure proper lubrication in different temperatures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viscosity - the state of being thick, sticky, and semifluid in consistency, due to internal friction
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈθɪknəs / (AmE), / ˈθɪknəs / (BrE)
the dimension of something measuring the distance through it from one surface to its opposite, distinct from its length or width
distance through an object

Examples

The thickness of the glass protects it from breaking easily.
This board comes in several thicknesses for different types of construction.
Engineers measured the thickness of the metal before starting the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depth - the distance from the top or surface to the bottom of something
  • Antonym: width - the measurement or extent of something from side to side
  • Antonym: length - the measurement or extent of something from end to end

Etymology

From Old English 'thicness' (thickness), related to 'thick' + '-ness'.
