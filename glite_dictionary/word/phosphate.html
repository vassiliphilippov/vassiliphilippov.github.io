phosphate

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊsˌfeɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɒs.feɪt / (BrE)
a salt or ester of phosphoric acid, often used in fertilizers and food additives
a salt or ester of phosphoric acid

Examples

Many farmers add phosphate to their soil to promote plant growth.
Soft drinks often contain phosphate as a food additive.
High levels of phosphate in water can lead to environmental issues like algal blooms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phosphoric acid - a mineral acid containing phosphorus, used to make phosphates

Etymology

From French *phosphate*, from New Latin *phosphatum*, from *phosphorus* (phosphorus).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɑsˌfeɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɒsˌfeɪt / (BrE)
A sweet, carbonated soft drink, popular in the late 19th and early 20th centuries, made with fruit syrup and a small amount of phosphoric acid.
old-fashioned carbonated fruit drink

Examples

On hot days, children lined up at the soda fountain for a cherry phosphate.
The old diner still serves classic flavors like vanilla and chocolate phosphate.
He remembered the sharp, tangy taste of a lemon phosphate from his childhood.

Etymology

From phosphate, referring to the phosphoric acid used in the drink.
