noria

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔriə / (AmE), / ˈnɔːriə / (BrE)
a water wheel with buckets attached to the rim, used to lift water for irrigation or supply channels
water-lifting device with a wheel and buckets

Examples

The ancient noria once supplied water to the fields all summer long.
Visitors admired the restored noria turning slowly beside the river.
A traditional noria is still used in some Spanish villages for irrigation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water wheel - a wheel driven by flowing water, used to power machinery or lift water

Etymology

From Arabic *nā‘ūra*, meaning water-wheel.
