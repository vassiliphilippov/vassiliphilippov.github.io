invertible

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈvɝːtəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɜːtɪbəl / (BrE)
(of a mathematical object) having an inverse, especially an additive or multiplicative inverse.
having an inverse (mathematics)

Examples

This matrix is invertible, so its determinant is nonzero.
An invertible function has an inverse that undoes its action.
Not every element in the ring is invertible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inverse - something that is the opposite or reverse of something else, especially in mathematics

Etymology

From 'invert' + suffix '-ible', from Latin 'invertere' (to turn upside down).
