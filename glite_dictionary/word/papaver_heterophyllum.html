Papaver heterophyllum

1. NOUN

/ pəˈpeɪvər ˌhɛtəroʊˈfɪləm / (AmE), / pəˈpeɪvə ˌhɛtərəʊˈfɪləm / (BrE)
A species of wild poppy native to California, known for its bright red flowers.
California wild poppy, with red flowers

Examples

The brilliant red flowers of Papaver heterophyllum are a common sight in California’s spring meadows.
Botanists classify Papaver heterophyllum as a distinct species within the poppy family.
We photographed a field covered with blooming Papaver heterophyllum during our hike in the Sierra foothills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poppy - a flowering plant of the genus Papaver

Etymology

From Latin: *Papaver* (poppy) and *heterophyllum* (with different leaves), reflecting its variable leaf shapes.
