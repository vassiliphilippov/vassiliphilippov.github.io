silence

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪləns / (AmE), / ˈsaɪləns / (BrE)
A complete absence of sound or noise.
absence of sound

Examples

After the storm passed, a deep silence fell over the village.
She enjoyed the silence of the empty library.
A sudden silence filled the room when the principal entered.

Synset Examples

he needed silence in order to sleep
the street was quiet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quiet - a lack of noise or bustle
  • Antonym: noise - a loud or unpleasant sound

Etymology

From Latin 'silentia', from 'silere' (to be quiet).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪləns / (AmE), / ˈsaɪləns / (BrE)
the quality or state of refraining from disclosing information; the act of keeping something secret
trait of keeping things secret

Examples

He maintained a stubborn silence about the details of the agreement.
Her silence on the matter only increased people's curiosity.
The company's silence regarding the complaint was interpreted as an admission of guilt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secrecy - the action of keeping something secret or the state of being kept secret
  • Antonym: disclosure - the action of making new or secret information known

Etymology

From Latin 'silentia' (a being silent), from 'silens', present participle of 'silere' (to be silent).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04659534-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪləns / (AmE), / ˈsaɪləns / (BrE)
the refusal or failure to speak when speech is expected or appropriate
refusal to speak when expected

Examples

Her silence during the meeting was quite unusual.
He was met with silence when he asked for volunteers.
The lawyer's question was answered only by silence from the witness.

Synset Examples

his silence about my contribution was surprising

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reticence - the quality of being reserved or inclined to keep one's thoughts to oneself
  • Antonym: disclosure - the action of making new or secret information known

Etymology

From Latin silentium (a being silent), from silēre (to be quiet).

Source: WordNet

4. VERB

/ ˈsaɪ.ləns / (AmE), / ˈsaɪ.ləns / (BrE)
to prevent someone from expressing their opinions, especially by using threats or force
prevent from expressing opinion

Examples

The government tried to silence its critics by threatening arrest.
Journalists who reported on corruption were quickly silenced.
She refused to be silenced by intimidation and continued to speak out.

Synset Examples

All dissenters were silenced when the dictator assumed power

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muzzle - to prevent someone from expressing themselves freely
  • Synonym: to suppress - to forcibly put an end to something, especially speech or expression
  • Antonym: to encourage - to give support or confidence to someone, especially to speak out

Etymology

From Latin 'silentium' (a being silent), from 'silēre' (to be silent).

6. VERB

/ ˈsaɪləns / (AmE), / ˈsaɪləns / (BrE)
to cause someone or something to become quiet or stop speaking or making noise
make someone or something quiet

Examples

The referee motioned to silence the noisy crowd.
She quickly tried to silence her phone before the meeting began.
He managed to silence his critics with a convincing argument.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quiet - to make less noisy
  • Synonym: to shush - to tell or make someone to be quiet, often abruptly

Etymology

From Old French *silence*, from Latin *silentium* (state of being still, quiet).
