Helianthus annuus

1. NOUN

/ hiˌliˈænθəs ˈænjuəs / (AmE), / hiˌliːˈænθəs ˈænjuːəs / (BrE)
The scientific name for the common sunflower, a tall annual plant grown for its seeds and oil.
scientific name of common sunflower

Examples

Farmers in the Midwest often plant Helianthus annuus for its high-yielding oilseeds.
The bright yellow blooms of Helianthus annuus can be seen across the prairies every summer.
Researchers are studying Helianthus annuus to develop more drought-resistant crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sunflower - a tall plant with large yellow flowers and edible seeds

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Helianthus' from Greek 'helios' (sun) + 'anthos' (flower) and 'annuus' meaning 'yearly' or 'annual'.
