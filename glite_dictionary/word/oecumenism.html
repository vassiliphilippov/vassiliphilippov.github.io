oecumenism

1. NOUN

/ ˌiːˈkjuː.məˌnɪ.zəm / (AmE), / ˌiːˈkjuː.mə.nɪ.zəm / (BrE)
the principle or movement promoting unity or cooperation among different religions, especially among Christian churches
promotion of unity among religions, esp. Christians

Examples

The council emphasized oecumenism as a way to bridge the differences between various Christian denominations.
Modern oecumenism seeks to foster dialogue and collaboration among diverse religious communities.
Her commitment to oecumenism led her to participate in interfaith conferences around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ecumenism - the principle or movement promoting unity among Christian churches

Etymology

From Greek oikoumenē (‘the inhabited world’), via Late Latin oecumenicus (‘general, universal’).
