to commandeer

1. VERB

/ ˌkɑː.mənˈdɪr / (AmE), / ˌkɒm.ənˈdɪə / (BrE)
to take possession or control of something, especially for military or official use, often without permission or by force
take possession by force or for official use

Examples

During the emergency, the government decided to commandeer several trucks for relief efforts.
The soldiers tried to commandeer a civilian building to use as their headquarters.
Angry protesters attempted to commandeer the meeting by shouting down the speakers.

Synset Examples

The Cubans commandeered the plane and flew it to Miami

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to seize - to take hold of suddenly and forcibly
  • Synonym: confiscate - to take possession of something by authority
  • Antonym: surrender - to give up possession of something

Etymology

From Afrikaans 'commandeer', from French 'commander' (to command), reflecting military requisition.
