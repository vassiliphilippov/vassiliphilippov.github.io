exertion

1. NOUN

/ ɪɡˈzɝːʃən / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɜːʃən / (BrE)
physical or mental effort; the use of energy to accomplish something difficult
physical or mental effort

Examples

She collapsed on the sofa, exhausted from her exertion during the long run.
Mental exertion is just as tiring as physical labor for some people.
The task required great exertion from everyone involved.

Synset Examples

he got an A for effort
they managed only with great exertion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: effort - the use of physical or mental energy to achieve something
  • Antonym: rest - a period of relaxing, not using energy

Etymology

From Latin 'exsertio' (a putting forth, effort), from 'exserere' (to put forth).
