Kaiser Wilhelm

1. PROPN

/ ˈkaɪzər ˈvɪlhɛlm / (AmE), / ˈkaɪzə ˈvɪlhɛlm / (BrE)
the German Emperor and King of Prussia from 1888 to 1918, known for his role in World War I
German Emperor, 1888–1918; major WWI figure

Examples

Kaiser Wilhelm was widely regarded as one of the most influential figures leading up to World War I.
Many historians continue to debate the true intentions and actions of Kaiser Wilhelm during his reign.
The abdication of Kaiser Wilhelm in 1918 marked the end of the German monarchy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Wilhelm II - German Emperor (Kaiser) from 1888 to 1918

Etymology

'Kaiser' from German, meaning 'emperor', from Latin 'Caesar'; 'Wilhelm' is the German form of 'William'.
