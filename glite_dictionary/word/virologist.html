virologist

1. NOUN

/ vaɪˈrɑlədʒɪst / (AmE), / vaɪˈrɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
A scientist or medical specialist who studies viruses and viral diseases.
a virus expert or specialist
medical

Examples

The virologist explained the process of virus replication to the audience at the science fair.
After years of research, the virologist made a breakthrough in understanding how the disease spreads.
Hospitals often consult a virologist during outbreaks of new infectious diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: virology - the study of viruses

Etymology

Formed from 'virology' (the study of viruses) + '-ist' (person who practices or is concerned with something).
