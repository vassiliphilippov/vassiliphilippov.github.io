lifespan

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪfspæn / (AmE), / ˈlaɪfspæn / (BrE)
the length of time for which a person, animal, or thing exists or is functional
period something exists or functions

Examples

The average human lifespan has increased due to advances in medicine.
This product has a very short lifespan compared to its competitors.
During its lifespan, the bridge withstood several major earthquakes.

Synset Examples

the battery had a short life
he lived a long and happy life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lifetime - the duration of the life of someone or something

Etymology

Formed from 'life' + 'span', first recorded in the early 20th century.
