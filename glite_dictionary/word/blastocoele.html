blastocoele

1. NOUN

/ ˈblæstəˌsiːl / (AmE), / ˈblæstəˌsiːl / (BrE)
the fluid-filled cavity within a blastula, formed early during embryonic development
fluid-filled cavity in a blastula
medical

Examples

The blastocoele forms during the early stages of embryonic development.
Nutrients are transported to the cells lining the blastocoele by surrounding fluids.
Scientists studied the changes in the blastocoele as the embryo progressed to later stages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blastula - an early stage of an embryo consisting of a sphere of cells surrounding a cavity

Etymology

From Greek blastos (germ, bud) + koilos (hollow).
