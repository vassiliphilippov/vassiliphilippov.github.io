trafficker

1. NOUN

/ ˈtræfɪkər / (AmE), / ˈtræfɪkə / (BrE)
a person who buys and sells goods or services, especially illegally or dishonestly
person involved in trade, often illegal

Examples

The police arrested the trafficker as he attempted to sell illegal drugs downtown.
Human traffickers often exploit vulnerable individuals for profit.
The authorities cracked down on arms traffickers operating near the border.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dealer - a person who buys and sells goods
  • Synonym: smuggler - a person who illegally transports goods
  • Antonym: buyer - a person who purchases goods or services

Etymology

From 'traffic' (trade, exchange) + '-er' (agent noun suffix), ultimately from Italian 'traficare' (to trade).
