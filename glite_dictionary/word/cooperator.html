cooperator

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈɑːpəˌreɪtər / (AmE), / kəʊˈɒpəˌreɪtə / (BrE)
a person who works or acts together with others in a shared activity or for a common purpose
person who works with others

Examples

As a valued cooperator, Maria contributed key insights to the research project.
The company sought out skilled cooperators to expand its international reach.
He became a trusted cooperator in the community initiative to support local artists.

Synset Examples

the musician and the librettist were collaborators
sexual partners

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collaborator - a person who works jointly with others, especially in an intellectual endeavor

Etymology

From Latin 'cooperari' (to work together), from 'co-' (together) + 'operari' (to work).
