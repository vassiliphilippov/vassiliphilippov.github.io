wolfsbane

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊlfsˌbeɪn / (AmE), / ˈwʊlfsˌbeɪn / (BrE)
a poisonous Eurasian perennial herb (Aconitum species), with broad rounded leaves, yellow or blue flowers, and a fibrous rootstock
poisonous Eurasian herb (Aconitum)

Examples

The gardener warned us not to touch the wolfsbane because of its toxic properties.
In medieval times, wolfsbane was believed to protect against werewolves and witches.
Botanists studied the wolfsbane to understand how its poison affects the nervous system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aconite - another name for wolfsbane or closely related poisonous plants in the genus Aconitum

Etymology

From Old English 'wulfes bān' meaning 'wolf's bane', referring to its ancient use as a poison for wolves.
