Chemnitz

1. PROPN

/ ˈkɛm.nɪts / (AmE), / ˈkɛm.nɪts / (BrE)
a city in east central Germany, formerly known as Karl-Marx-Stadt until 1990, noted for its textile manufacturing industry
city in east central Germany, formerly Karl-Marx-Stadt

Examples

The city of Chemnitz underwent extensive redevelopment after German reunification.
During the Cold War, Chemnitz was known as Karl-Marx-Stadt.
My grandfather worked in the textile mills of Chemnitz before emigrating.

Etymology

From the Chemnitz River, name of Slavic origin.
