Hygrophorus borealis

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌɡrɑːˈfɔːrəs bɔːˈriːəlɪs / (AmE), / haɪˌɡrɒˈfɔːrəs bɔːˈriːəlɪs / (BrE)
A species of fungus characterized by a white convex cap, arcuate white gills, and a stalk that tapers toward the base.
a species of white-capped fungus

Examples

The scientist collected samples of Hygrophorus borealis from the damp forest floor.
We identified Hygrophorus borealis by its distinctive white cap and tapered stalk.
Mushroom enthusiasts often seek out Hygrophorus borealis during the autumn months.

Etymology

From New Latin: 'Hygrophorus' (genus meaning 'moist bearer') and 'borealis' (Latin for 'northern').
