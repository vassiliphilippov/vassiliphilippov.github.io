coca

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊ.kə / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ.kə / (BrE)
A South American shrub (Erythroxylum coca) whose leaves are traditionally chewed by natives of the Andes and are a natural source of cocaine.
South American shrub; source of cocaine

Examples

The indigenous people often chew coca leaves to combat altitude sickness.
Scientists study the alkaloids present in coca plants for medicinal uses.
The cultivation of coca has significant cultural importance in parts of South America.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: cocaine - a powerful stimulant drug derived from coca leaves

Etymology

From Spanish 'coca', from Quechua 'kúka'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊkə / (AmE), / ˈkəʊkə / (BrE)
The dried leaves of the coca plant, especially those chewed by Andean people for their stimulant effects.
dried leaves of the coca plant, used as a stimulant

Examples

Traditional healers in the Andes often chew coca leaves to combat fatigue and hunger.
The government regulates the cultivation of coca due to its connection with cocaine production.
Farmers in Peru use coca as part of cultural rituals and local medicine.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: cocaine - A powerful stimulant drug derived from coca leaves

Etymology

From Spanish 'coca', from Quechua 'kuka' (the plant and its leaves).
