Muscardinus avellanarius

1. NOUN

/ mʌsˌkɑːrdɪnʌs əˌvɛləˈnɛəriəs / (AmE), / mʊsˌkɑːdɪnəs əˌvɛləˈneəriəs / (BrE)
A small arboreal rodent of Europe and western Asia, commonly known as the hazel dormouse, noted for its golden-brown fur and hibernation habits.
species of hazel dormouse

Examples

The population of Muscardinus avellanarius has declined in many parts of Europe due to habitat loss.
Muscardinus avellanarius is protected by law in several countries because of its vulnerable status.
Researchers observed the feeding habits of Muscardinus avellanarius in a restored woodland area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dormouse - small rodent known for long periods of hibernation

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Muscardinus' and species name 'avellanarius' meaning 'of hazels'.
