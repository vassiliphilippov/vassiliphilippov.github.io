freehold

1. NOUN

/ ˈfriːˌhoʊld / (AmE), / ˈfriːˌhəʊld / (BrE)
Legal ownership of land or property for an indefinite period, especially as opposed to leasehold.
ownership of property for an unlimited period
legal

Examples

He bought the freehold of the house and became its outright owner.
Purchasing a freehold property means you own both the building and the land.
The freehold was transferred to the new landlord after the sale.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: leasehold - ownership of property for a fixed period under a lease

Etymology

From Middle English 'fre' (free) + 'hold' (holding, possession).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfriˌhoʊld / (AmE), / ˈfriːhəʊld / (BrE)
A tenure of real property by which an estate is held for an unlimited duration, either in fee simple or for life.
ownership of land for an unlimited period
legal

Examples

He purchased the freehold to avoid paying rent to a landlord.
Owning a freehold property gives you more security than a leasehold arrangement.
The freehold of the building was transferred to the new owner.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: leasehold - a property tenure under a lease for a fixed period

Etymology

From Middle English 'fre hold', literally 'free hold' (free possession).

Synset POS: n
