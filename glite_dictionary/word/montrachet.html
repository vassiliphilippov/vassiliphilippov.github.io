Montrachet

1. NOUN

/ mɔnˈrɑːʃeɪ / (AmE), / mɒnˈraʃeɪ / (BrE)
A high-quality dry white Burgundy wine made from Chardonnay grapes, produced in the Montrachet vineyard of Burgundy, France.
a premium white Burgundy wine

Examples

We enjoyed a chilled bottle of Montrachet with our seafood dinner last night.
Collectors prize rare vintages of Montrachet for their exceptional quality and aging potential.
The sommelier recommended a Montrachet to complement the flavors of the dish.

Etymology

From French Montrachet, the name of the vineyard in Burgundy, France.
