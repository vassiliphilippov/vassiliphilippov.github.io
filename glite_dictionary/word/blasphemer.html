blasphemer

1. NOUN

/ blæsˈfimɚ / (AmE), / blæsˈfiːmə / (BrE)
A person who speaks or acts disrespectfully towards sacred things, especially religious beliefs or deities.
person who commits blasphemy

Examples

He was denounced as a blasphemer for questioning the church's authority.
Many consider the artist a blasphemer because his work challenged traditional religious symbols.
The medieval inquisition often punished suspected blasphemers severely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blasphemer - person committing blasphemy
  • Synonym: heretic - person holding beliefs contrary to orthodox doctrine

Etymology

From Late Latin 'blasphemator', from Greek 'blasphēmatōr', related to 'blaspheme'.
