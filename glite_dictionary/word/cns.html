CNS

1. NOUN

/ si ɛn ɛs / (AmE), / si ɛn ɛs / (BrE)
the part of the nervous system consisting of the brain and spinal cord, which coordinates and controls activities in the body
brain and spinal cord; central nervous system
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that the CNS is responsible for processing sensory information.
Certain diseases can severely affect the CNS, leading to loss of motor function.
Research on the CNS has advanced our understanding of neurological disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: central nervous system - the brain and spinal cord collectively
  • Antonym: PNS - peripheral nervous system; nerves outside the brain and spinal cord

Etymology

Acronym for ‘central nervous system’.
