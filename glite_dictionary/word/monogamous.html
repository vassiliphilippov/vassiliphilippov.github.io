monogamous

1. ADJ

/ məˈnɑːɡəməs / (AmE), / məˈnɒɡəməs / (BrE)
having or involving a sexual relationship with only one partner at a time, or being married to only one person at a time.
having one mate or partner

Examples

Swans are often monogamous and stay with the same partner for life.
Many cultures expect couples to remain monogamous throughout marriage.
She prefers a monogamous relationship because it offers stability and trust.

Synset Examples

monogamous marriage
monogamous for life

Related Terms

  • Antonym: polygamous - having more than one mate or partner at the same time

Etymology

From Late Latin monogamus, from Greek monogamos (married to one person) – mono- (one) + gamos (marriage).
