to humify

1. VERB

/ ˈhjuːməˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈhjuːmɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to convert organic matter, especially plant remains, into humus
convert organic matter into humus

Examples

Soil microorganisms help humify dead leaves and other plant material over time.
Researchers studied how quickly compost piles humify different types of organic waste.
Proper moisture and aeration are crucial to humify crop residues efficiently in the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to decompose - to break down into simpler parts, especially by decay

Etymology

From 'humus' (soil component) + '-ify' (to make or become).
