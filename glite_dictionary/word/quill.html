quill

1. NOUN

/ kwɪl / (AmE), / kwɪl / (BrE)
a stiff, hollow, sharp spine growing on a porcupine or hedgehog, serving as a protective covering
protective spine of a porcupine or hedgehog

Examples

The porcupine raised its quills when it felt threatened by the predator.
A hedgehog's quills provide excellent protection against many animals.
Scientists studied the structure of quills to develop new types of protective gear.

Etymology

From Middle English quil (shaft of a feather).

2. NOUN

/ kwɪl / (AmE), / kwɪl / (BrE)
the hollow central shaft of a feather, especially one that was formerly used as a writing instrument
hollow shaft of a feather

Examples

The porcupine's quill can easily detach and stick into predators.
He dipped the quill in ink before writing the letter.
A bird's quill can be used in traditional handicrafts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shaft - the long central part of a feather

Etymology

From Middle English *quil*, of uncertain origin; related to Low German *Quill* (quill, pen).

3. NOUN

/ kwɪl / (AmE), / kwɪl / (BrE)
a pen made from a bird's feather, used especially in the past for writing
pen made from a bird feather

Examples

The ancient manuscript was written with a quill and ink.
She dipped her quill into the inkwell and began to write.
His collection included a beautifully carved quill from the 18th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pen - an instrument for writing or drawing with ink

Etymology

From Middle English quil, of unknown origin, possibly related to Low German quiele (large feather or wing).

4. NOUN

/ kwɪl / (AmE), / kwɪl / (BrE)
any of the large stiff feathers from the wing or tail of a bird
large wing or tail feather of a bird

Examples

The eagle lost a quill from its left wing as it soared through the sky.
Writers in the past often used a quill from a goose to pen letters.
She found a beautiful quill on the forest path and kept it as a souvenir.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: feather - one of the light structures covering a bird's body

Etymology

From Middle English quil (shaft of a feather, stalk of a plant).
