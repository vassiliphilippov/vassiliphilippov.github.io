loquat

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊˌkwɑt / (AmE), / ˈləʊˌkwɑːt / (BrE)
A small yellow, olive-sized fruit with a large stone and little flesh, grown in warm climates and often used for making jellies.
small yellow fruit with large stone

Examples

The tree in our backyard produces sweet loquats every spring.
She made delicious jam from the loquats she picked at the orchard.
Although a loquat has little flesh, its taste is remarkably refreshing.

Etymology

From Chinese (Cantonese) lou^6gwat^1, via Portuguese loquat.

2. NOUN

/ ˈloʊˌkwɑt / (AmE), / ˈləʊkwət / (BrE)
an evergreen tree (Eriobotrya japonica) native to China and Japan, cultivated for its small, yellow, edible fruit
evergreen tree with edible yellow fruit

Examples

The loquat tree in my backyard is covered with ripe fruit every spring.
She made a delicious jam from fresh loquats picked at the market.
Loquat leaves are sometimes brewed to make herbal teas in East Asia.

Etymology

From Cantonese lou4 gwat1, ultimately from Chinese.
