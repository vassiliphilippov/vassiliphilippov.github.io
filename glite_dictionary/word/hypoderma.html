Hypoderma

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpəˈdɜrmə / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəˈdɜːmə / (BrE)
a genus of large flies (warble flies) that are parasitic on mammals, forming subcutaneous larvae commonly known as warbles
genus of warble flies parasitic on mammals
medical

Examples

Hypoderma larvae cause swelling under the skin of cattle, resulting in significant economic loss.
Veterinarians must be vigilant for infections caused by Hypoderma species in livestock.
The life cycle of Hypoderma involves migration through the host's tissues before emerging as adult flies.

Etymology

From Greek hypo- (under) + derma (skin), referring to larvae developing under the skin.

2. NOUN

/ haɪˈpɑdərmə / (AmE), / hɪˈpɒdəmə / (BrE)
A genus of warble flies, some of which are pests of cattle, considered the type genus of the family Hypodermatidae.
genus of warble flies (insects)

Examples

Hypoderma larvae burrow under the skin of cattle, causing discomfort and economic losses.
Veterinarians monitor livestock closely for infestations of Hypoderma species.
The life cycle of Hypoderma makes it challenging to control the spread of these flies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warble fly - large fly whose larvae develop under the skin of mammals

Etymology

From Greek 'hypo-' (under) + 'derma' (skin), referring to the larvae living under the skin of hosts.
