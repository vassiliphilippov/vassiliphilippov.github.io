pyxidium

1. NOUN

/ pɪkˈsɪdiəm / (AmE), / pɪkˈsɪdɪəm / (BrE)
A type of seed capsule in which the upper part falls off to release the seeds, as in the plantain.
seed capsule with a detachable top

Examples

The plantain releases its seeds when the pyxidium loses its upper part.
Many botanists study the structure of the pyxidium in various plant species.
The dried pyxidia can often be found scattered beneath the parent plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capsule - a dry fruit that opens to release seeds

Etymology

From New Latin 'pyxidium', diminutive of Greek 'pyxis' (box).
