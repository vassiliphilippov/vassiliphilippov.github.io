Aphis fabae

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪfɪs ˈfeɪ.bi / (AmE), / ˈɑː.fɪs ˈfɑː.beɪ / (BrE)
A species of blackish aphid that commonly infests beans, sugar beets, and other plants.
black aphid pest of beans and beets

Examples

The infestation by Aphis fabae caused severe damage to the bean crop this year.
Farmers monitor their fields regularly to detect the presence of Aphis fabae.
Biological control methods can help reduce the impact of Aphis fabae on sugar beet plantations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aphid - small sap-sucking insect

Etymology

From Latin: genus *Aphis* (aphid) and species *fabae* (of the bean).
