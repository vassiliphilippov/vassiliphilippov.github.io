featherbed

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛðərˌbɛd / (AmE), / ˈfɛðəˌbɛd / (BrE)
A mattress filled with feathers, used to provide softness and comfort when sleeping.
mattress stuffed with feathers

Examples

The old farmhouse had a featherbed that made sleeping so much more comfortable.
She sank into the soft featherbed after a long, tiring day.
In winter, a thick featherbed kept everyone warm through the night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mattress - a large pad for supporting the body in sleeping

2. VERB

/ ˈfɛðɚˌbɛd / (AmE), / ˈfɛðəbɛd / (BrE)
To provide more jobs than are necessary, often to comply with union demands or prevent unemployment.
hire more workers than necessary

Examples

The company was accused of featherbedding to keep union members employed.
Management resisted pressure to featherbed despite demands from labor unions.
Some critics argue that featherbedding increases operational costs without improving productivity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overstaff - to employ more staff than needed
  • Antonym: understaff - to employ too few staff

Etymology

From the noun 'featherbed.' Sense derives from the practice of making something easy or comfortable, metaphorically applied to employment.

3. VERB

/ ˈfɛðərˌbɛd / (AmE), / ˈfɛðəbɛd / (BrE)
to treat someone with excessive comfort or indulgence, often more than is necessary or appropriate
treat with excessive comfort or indulgence

Examples

The company was accused of trying to featherbed its employees by reducing their workload.
If we featherbed the students, they won't learn to be independent.
He refuses to featherbed his children, preferring to teach them responsibility.

Synset Examples

grandparents often pamper the children
Let's not mollycoddle our students!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pamper - to treat with excessive care or attention
  • Synonym: spoil - to give a child or person everything they want, often with negative results
  • Antonym: neglect - to fail to care for properly

Etymology

From the noun 'feather bed', referring to the softness and comfort of such a bed.
