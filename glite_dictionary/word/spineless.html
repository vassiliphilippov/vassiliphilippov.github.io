spineless

1. ADJ

/ ˈspaɪn.ləs / (AmE), / ˈspaɪn.ləs / (BrE)
(of a body part, especially a fin) lacking spiny processes or projections.
lacking spines or spiny projections

Examples

The goldfish has spineless fins that distinguish it from some other species.
Unlike its relatives, the catfish features a spineless dorsal fin.
Their spineless tails allow for smoother swimming in calm waters.

Synset Examples

spineless fins

Etymology

From 'spine' + '-less', meaning 'without spines'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02286591-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈspaɪnləs / (AmE), / ˈspaɪnləs / (BrE)
(of an animal) lacking a backbone or spinal column; invertebrate.
lacking a backbone

Examples

Earthworms are spineless creatures found in most gardens.
Most insects are spineless animals, unlike reptiles or mammals.
Jellyfish are spineless marine animals that float freely in the sea.

Synset Examples

worms are an example of invertebrate animals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invertebrate - an animal lacking a backbone

Etymology

Formed from spine + -less, meaning 'without a spine'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02520331-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˈspaɪnləs / (AmE), / ˈspaɪnləs / (BrE)
(of a plant) lacking spines, thorns, or similar sharp protective growths.
(of a plant) without thorns or spines

Examples

The cactus variety in the corner is completely spineless and safe to touch.
Some roses have been bred to be spineless to make them easier to handle.
Gardeners often prefer spineless plants to avoid accidental injury.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: thorny - having many sharp thorns or spines

Etymology

From spine + -less. 'Spine' from Latin 'spina' meaning 'thorn, spine'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00147396-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ˈspaɪn.ləs / (AmE), / ˈspaɪn.ləs / (BrE)
Lacking courage, determination, or strength of character; weak and unable to stand up for oneself.
lacking courage or resolve

Examples

Many considered the manager too spineless to confront his team's ongoing problems.
She refused to be seen as spineless and stood up for what she believed in.
His spineless attitude made it impossible for him to make tough decisions.

Synset Examples

he was a yellow gutless worm
a spineless craven fellow

Related Terms

  • Antonym: courageous - having or showing courage
  • Synonym: gutless - lacking courage; cowardly

Etymology

Formed from 'spine' + '-less', metaphorically referring to lack of a backbone as a sign of weakness.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00267851-a
Synset POS: a
