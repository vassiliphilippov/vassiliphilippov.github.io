disobedience

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪs.əˈbi.di.əns / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.əˈbiː.di.əns / (BrE)
refusal or failure to obey rules, authority, or instructions
refusal to obey

Examples

His repeated disobedience eventually led to his suspension from school.
The parent was frustrated by the child's constant disobedience at home.
Civil disobedience has often been used as a means of protesting unjust laws.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: obedience - the act or practice of obeying rules or authority
  • Synonym: defiance - open resistance to authority

Etymology

From Latin 'disobedientia', from 'dis-' (not) + 'oboedientia' (obedience).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsəˈbiːdiəns / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈbiːdiəns / (BrE)
Refusal or failure to obey rules, instructions, or authority.
failure or refusal to obey

Examples

His constant disobedience caused problems at school.
The dog's disobedience frustrated its owner during training sessions.
Teachers must address disobedience promptly to maintain classroom order.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: obedience - the act of obeying rules or authority
  • Synonym: defiance - open resistance or bold disobedience

Etymology

From Latin disobedientia, from dis- (opposite of) + obedientia (obedience).
