pressor

1. ADJ

/ ˈprɛsər / (AmE), / ˈprɛsə / (BrE)
(of a substance or agent) causing an increase in blood pressure.
raising blood pressure (medical)
medical

Examples

The doctor administered a pressor agent to help stabilize the patient’s blood pressure.
Certain medications have pressor effects that must be closely monitored.
Research into pressor responses is vital for understanding cardiovascular diseases.

Synset Examples

pressor reflexes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: depressor - causing a decrease in blood pressure

Etymology

From Latin 'pressare' (to press).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02795364-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɛsər / (AmE), / ˈprɛsə / (BrE)
a substance or agent that raises blood pressure by causing the constriction of blood vessels
agent raising blood pressure
medical

Examples

The doctor administered a pressor to stabilize the patient’s blood pressure.
Certain medications act as pressors during episodes of hypotension.
Nicotine is classified as a pressor because it constricts blood vessels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vasopressor - an agent that causes narrowing of blood vessels and increases blood pressure

Etymology

From Latin 'pressare' (to press).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
