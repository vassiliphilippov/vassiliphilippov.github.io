opsin

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑpsɪn / (AmE), / ˈɒpsɪn / (BrE)
a protein in the retina that, when combined with retinal, forms the light-sensitive pigment rhodopsin
retinal protein in visual pigments
medical

Examples

Researchers discovered that opsin plays a key role in the conversion of light into electrical signals in the eye.
Mutations in the gene coding for opsin can lead to color blindness or other vision disorders.
The breakdown of rhodopsin separates retinal from opsin, which is necessary for visual adaptation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhodopsin - a light-sensitive pigment in the retina formed by opsin and retinal

Etymology

From Greek 'opsis' (sight, vision) + '-in' (protein suffix).
