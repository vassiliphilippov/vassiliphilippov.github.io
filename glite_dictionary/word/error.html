error

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛrɚ / (AmE), / ˈɛrə / (BrE)
a mistaken belief or opinion based on incorrect information
misconception from incorrect information

Examples

He developed an error after reading an outdated textbook.
Her argument was based on an error about the causes of climate change.
Many people act on error because they trusted unreliable sources.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mistake - an action or judgment that is wrong
  • Antonym: truth - the quality or state of being true

Etymology

From Latin 'error', from 'errare' meaning 'to wander, to err'.

4. NOUN

/ ˈɛrɚ / (AmE), / ˈɛrə / (BrE)
(Baseball) A misplay by a defensive player that allows a batter or base runner to continue playing when an out could have been made with ordinary effort.
misplay by a defensive player in baseball

Examples

The shortstop committed an error that allowed the runner to reach second base.
That play was ruled an error instead of a hit by the official scorer.
He set a record for fewest errors in a single season by a first baseman.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mistake - an action or judgment that is wrong
  • Antonym: catch - to capture a ball successfully in baseball or other sports

Etymology

From Latin error (wandering, mistake), but the baseball sense developed in 19th-century American English.
