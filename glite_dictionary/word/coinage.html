coinage

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔɪ.nɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɔɪ.nɪdʒ / (BrE)
the invention of a new word or phrase
invention of a word or phrase

Examples

The coinage of the word 'blog' reflected the rise of the internet era.
Shakespeare was famous for his creative coinage of new terms.
Some expressions quickly fade despite their initial coinage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neologism - a newly coined word or expression

Etymology

From Old French 'coignier' (to coin), from Latin 'cuneus' (wedge, die for stamping).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɔɪnɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɔɪnɪdʒ / (BrE)
Coins collectively; the coins produced by a government or authority.
coins collectively

Examples

The government introduced new coinage after gaining independence.
Ancient coinage can reveal much about a civilization's history.
His collection features rare examples of Roman coinage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: currency - system of money in use in a particular country
  • Antonym: paper money - money in the form of banknotes, not coins

Etymology

From Old French 'coignage', from 'coin' (wedge, die for stamping money).

newly invented word or phrase

Examples

The word 'blog' is a modern coinage that became popular with the rise of the internet.
Every generation creates its own unique coinage to express new ideas and trends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neologism - a newly coined word or expression

From Middle English 'coynage', from Old French, from 'coignier' (to coin), from Latin 'cuneus' (wedge, used as a die for stamping coins).
