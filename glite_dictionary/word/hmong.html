Hmong

1. ADJ

/ mɑŋ / (AmE), / mɒŋ / (BrE)
of or relating to the Hmong people, their language, or their culture
related to the Hmong people or language

Examples

The Hmong tradition of storytelling is an important part of their culture.
Her grandmother still cooks using traditional Hmong recipes.
Many Hmong students are bilingual in English and their heritage language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hmong - the Hmong people, an ethnic group

Etymology

From the Hmong people's autonym, Hmong.

2. NOUN

/ mɔŋ / (AmE), / mɒŋ / (BrE)
the language spoken by the Hmong people, mainly in China and Southeast Asia
language of the Hmong people

Examples

Many refugees arriving in the United States speak Hmong as their first language.
She is studying Hmong to better communicate with her grandparents.
The conference offered translation services in Hmong for local residents.

Etymology

From the Hmong people’s self-designation for their language and ethnicity.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06949875-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ mɔŋ / (AmE), / mɒŋ / (BrE)
a member of an indigenous people traditionally living in the mountainous regions of southern China, Vietnam, Laos, and Thailand, many of whom have emigrated to the United States and other countries
an ethnic group from Southeast Asia

Examples

Many Hmong settled in Minnesota after the Vietnam War.
The Hmong have a rich tradition of textile arts and storytelling.
She is studying the history of the Hmong people in Southeast Asia.

Etymology

From the Hmong language, where "Hmong" means 'free person' or 'human being'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09733160-n
Synset POS: n
