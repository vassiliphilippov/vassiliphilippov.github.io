banausic

1. ADJ

/ bəˈnɔsɪk / (AmE), / bəˈnɔːsɪk / (BrE)
(formal) Concerned with or characteristic of ordinary, utilitarian, or mechanical activities; lacking refinement or intellectual value.
ordinary, utilitarian, not refined

Examples

Many critics dismissed the novel as banausic and lacking any literary merit.
He regarded manual labor as a banausic pursuit beneath his intellectual ambitions.
The professor warned against adopting a banausic approach to the study of art.

Synset Examples

he felt contempt for all banausic occupations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mundane - lacking interest or excitement; dull
  • Antonym: refined - cultivated and elegant in manner or taste

Etymology

From Greek banausikos (mechanical, vulgar, pertaining to craftsmen), from banausos (artisan, mechanic).
