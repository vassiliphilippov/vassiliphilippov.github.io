judiciously

1. ADV

/ dʒuˈdɪʃəsli / (AmE), / dʒuˈdɪʃəsli / (BrE)
in a way that shows good judgment, care, or discretion
in a manner showing good judgment

Examples

The funds were invested judiciously to ensure stable growth over time.
Use social media judiciously to avoid wasting productive hours online.
He distributed the supplies judiciously, making sure everyone received a fair share.

Synset Examples

let's use these intelligence tests judiciously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wisely - in a way that shows wisdom or good judgment
  • Antonym: recklessly - without regard to danger or consequences; carelessly

Etymology

From judicious + -ly, based on Latin judicium (judgment).
