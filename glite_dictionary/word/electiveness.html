electiveness

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛktɪvnəs / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktɪvnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being elective; the condition where choice or discretion is allowed.
quality of allowing choice or discretion

Examples

The electiveness of the program enabled students to tailor their courses to their interests.
Teachers praised the electiveness in the curriculum, allowing learners more autonomy.
Because of its electiveness, participation in the activity was lower than expected.

Synset Examples

The usual optionality of these tasks entails the risk of students not fulfilling them in an effective way.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: optionality - the state of being optional or discretionary

Etymology

From 'elective' + '-ness', formed from Latin 'eligere' (to choose).
