soviet

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊ.vi.ət / (AmE), / ˈsəʊ.vi.ət / (BrE)
an elected governmental council in a communist country, especially in the former Soviet Union
elected council in a communist country

Examples

The factory soviet played a crucial role during the early days of the revolution.
Each city elected its own soviet to administer local affairs.
He studied the structure of the soviets established after the Bolshevik Revolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: council - a group of people formally meeting for discussion or decision-making

Etymology

From Russian 'sovet' meaning 'council'.
