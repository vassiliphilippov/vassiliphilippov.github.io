tessellation

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛsəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtɛsɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
an arrangement of shapes closely fitted together, especially in a repeated pattern without gaps or overlaps
pattern of shapes fitted together

Examples

The artist created a stunning tessellation of triangles across the wall.
Mathematicians study tessellation patterns in both nature and art.
A honeycomb is a natural example of a tessellation using hexagons.

Synset Examples

a tessellation of hexagons

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tiling - the process of covering a surface with tiles or similar shapes in a repeated pattern

Etymology

From Latin 'tessella' (a small cube, used in mosaics), diminutive of 'tessera'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌtɛsəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtɛsɪˈleɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the process or result of covering a surface with a pattern of flat shapes, especially tiles or stones, without gaps or overlaps, often in decorative mosaic work
decorative tiling or mosaic patterning

Examples

The ancient Romans were masters of tessellation in their bathhouses and villas.
Modern artists use tessellation techniques to create stunning floor patterns.
The museum's hallway is a fine example of tessellation with colored stones and glass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mosaic - a picture or pattern produced by arranging small colored pieces of hard material
  • Synonym: tiling - the arrangement of tiles on a surface

Etymology

From Latin 'tessella' (small cube, tile) + '-ation'.
