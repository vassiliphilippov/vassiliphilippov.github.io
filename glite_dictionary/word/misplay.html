misplay

1. NOUN

/ mɪsˈpleɪ / (AmE), / mɪsˈpleɪ / (BrE)
(Baseball) a mistake by a defensive player in failing to make an out when a routine play would have sufficed.
baseball defensive mistake

Examples

The shortstop's misplay in the seventh inning cost the team a crucial run.
One costly misplay can change the momentum of a baseball game.
Despite his earlier misplay, the player redeemed himself with a spectacular catch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: error - mistake by a fielder in baseball

Etymology

From mis- (wrongly) + play.

2. VERB

/ ˌmɪsˈpleɪ / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˈpleɪ / (BrE)
to play something incorrectly or in an unskillful manner, especially in games or sports
play incorrectly or unskillfully

Examples

He misplayed the ball and gave the opposition a clear chance to score.
During the chess match, she misplayed her queen and lost her advantage.
The team misplayed several crucial points in the final set.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blunder - to make a careless or serious mistake
  • Antonym: play well - to play in a skilled or effective manner

Etymology

From prefix 'mis-' meaning 'wrongly' + 'play'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01077437-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ mɪsˈpleɪ / (AmE), / mɪsˈpleɪ / (BrE)
to play or perform incorrectly, especially in games, music, or sports
play or perform incorrectly

Examples

The pianist accidentally misplayed a note during the concert.
If you misplay your cards, you can lose the entire hand.
She misplayed the ball, giving the other team an easy goal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blunder - make a careless or serious mistake
  • Antonym: play correctly - to perform or play without error

Etymology

From prefix 'mis-' (wrongly, badly) + 'play'.
