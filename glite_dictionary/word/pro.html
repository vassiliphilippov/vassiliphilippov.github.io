pro

1. ADJ

/ proʊ / (AmE), / prəʊ / (BrE)
in favor of or supporting a particular idea, action, or proposal
in favor of something

Examples

Most members cast a pro vote for the new policy.
She is clearly pro environmental reform in this debate.
The committee divided into pro and anti factions during the discussion.

Synset Examples

a pro vote

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anti - opposed to or against a particular idea, action, or proposal

Etymology

From Latin pro (for, in favor of).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01822805-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ proʊ / (AmE), / prəʊ / (BrE)
an athlete who plays a sport as a paid professional rather than as an amateur
professional athlete

Examples

After college, he signed a contract and became a pro in the league.
Many young athletes dream of making it as a pro someday.
The tournament is open to both pros and amateurs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: amateur - a person who engages in a sport or activity for pleasure rather than financial benefit

Etymology

Shortened from 'professional'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10500168-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ proʊ / (AmE), / prəʊ / (BrE)
an argument or consideration in favor of something
argument in favor

Examples

He carefully weighed every pro and con before making a decision.
One pro of working from home is the flexible schedule.
The committee discussed several pros of the new policy before voting.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: con - an argument against something

Etymology

From Latin 'pro' (for, on behalf of) used in contrasting arguments.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06661968-n
Synset POS: n

4. ADV

/ proʊ / (AmE), / prəʊ / (BrE)
in support of or in favor of a particular proposition, opinion, or course of action
in favor; in support of

Examples

Several speakers argued pro the new policy at the meeting.
Let's list the pro points before we discuss the cons.
He spoke pro the proposal, highlighting its many benefits.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: con - in opposition to a proposition or opinion

Etymology

From Latin 'pro' (for, on behalf of).
