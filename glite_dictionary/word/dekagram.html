dekagram

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛkəˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈdɛkəɡræm / (BrE)
A metric unit of mass equal to 10 grams.
metric unit equal to 10 grams

Examples

The recipe calls for exactly twenty dekagrams of sugar.
A dekagram is not a commonly used measurement in the United States.
She bought fifty dekagrams of cheese at the market.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: gram - metric unit of mass equal to one thousandth of a kilogram
  • Degree Higher: kilogram - metric unit of mass equal to 1,000 grams

Etymology

From Greek 'deka' (ten) + 'gramma' (a small weight).
