paternoster

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪtərˈnɑstər / (AmE), / ˌpɑːtəˈnɒstə / (BrE)
A form of the Lord's Prayer, especially in Latin, used in Christian liturgy.
Lord's Prayer (especially in Latin)

Examples

The monk recited the paternoster in the quiet of the chapel.
During the ceremony, each parishioner said a paternoster for peace.
He learned the paternoster by heart while studying Latin prayers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lord's Prayer - The Christian prayer beginning with 'Our Father', central to Christian worship

Etymology

From Latin *Pater noster* (our father), the opening words of the prayer.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪtərˈnɑːstər / (AmE), / ˌpɑːtəˈnɒstə / (BrE)
a type of elevator consisting of a chain of open compartments which move continuously in a loop, allowing passengers to step on or off at each floor without the lift stopping
a continuously moving open elevator

Examples

The old office building still has a working paternoster that fascinates visitors.
Some people feel nervous using a paternoster for the first time because it never stops moving.
The university decided to keep its historic paternoster as a symbol of tradition and innovation.

Etymology

From Latin 'Pater noster' (Our Father), originally referring to the prayer, then applied to the elevator due to its chain's resemblance to rosary beads.
