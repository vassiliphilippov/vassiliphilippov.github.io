stepson

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɛpˌsʌn / (AmE), / ˈstɛp.sʌn / (BrE)
A son of one's spouse by a previous marriage or relationship.
husband's or wife's son by a former marriage

Examples

After his mother remarried, Tom became her new husband's stepson.
My stepson will be joining us for dinner this evening.
She treats her stepson like her own child.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stepdaughter - the daughter of one's spouse by a previous marriage

Etymology

Formed from 'step-' (Middle English, meaning 'related by remarriage') + 'son'.
