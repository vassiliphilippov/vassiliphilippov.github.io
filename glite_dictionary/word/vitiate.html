to vitiate

1. VERB

/ ˈvɪʃieɪt / (AmE), / ˈvɪʃieɪt / (BrE)
to make something, especially a legal document or agreement, ineffective or invalid; to spoil or impair the validity of something
make legally invalid or ineffective
legal

Examples

The court ruled that the misleading clause would vitiate the entire contract.
Any evidence obtained illegally may vitiate the prosecution's case.
A procedural error can vitiate the legal validity of an agreement.

Synset Examples

invalidate a contract

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to invalidate - to make something no longer legally or officially acceptable

Etymology

From Latin 'vitiat-', past participle of 'vitiare' (to spoil), from 'vitium' (fault).

Source: WordNet

3. VERB

/ ˈvɪʃiˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈvɪʃieɪt / (BrE)

Examples

Excess luxury and indulgence can vitiate one's character over time.
