brownie

1. NOUN

/ ˈbraʊni / (AmE), / ˈbraʊni / (BrE)
A small, square or rectangular piece of rich chocolate cake, often containing nuts.
chocolate cake bar, often with nuts

Examples

I baked a fresh batch of brownies for the party.
She offered me a brownie with walnuts and chocolate chips.
The brownie was so moist and rich that I had to ask for the recipe.

Etymology

Probably from 'brown' + diminutive suffix '-ie', referring to its color and small size.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbraʊni / (AmE), / ˈbraʊni / (BrE)
(in folklore) a small, helpful or mischievous fairy, usually depicted as living in houses and doing household tasks secretly at night.
mischievous household fairy (folklore)

Examples

According to Scottish legend, a brownie would help with the chores if treated with respect.
The children hoped to catch a glimpse of the brownie who supposedly lived in their old house.
Stories about a mischievous brownie often appear in British folklore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elf - a small supernatural being in folklore, often with mischievous tendencies
  • Synonym: goblin - a mischievous, ugly creature in folklore
  • Synonym: sprite - a small or elusive supernatural being

Etymology

From Scots 'broonie', diminutive of 'brown', perhaps from the fairy's brown garments.
