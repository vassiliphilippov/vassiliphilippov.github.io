inhabitant

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈhæbɪtənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈhæbɪtənt / (BrE)
A person or animal that lives in or occupies a particular place.
a person or animal living in a place

Examples

The first inhabitant of the village greeted travelers warmly.
Polar bears are native inhabitants of the Arctic regions.
Each inhabitant must register their address with the authorities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resident - a person who lives somewhere permanently or on a long-term basis
  • Synonym: occupant - a person who lives or is present in a house, vehicle, etc., at a given time
  • Antonym: visitor - someone who visits a place but does not live there

Etymology

From Latin 'inhabitantem', present participle of 'inhabitare' (to inhabit).
