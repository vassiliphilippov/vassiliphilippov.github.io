Menziesia ferruginea

1. NOUN

/ mɛnˈziːziə ˌfɛrəˈɡiːniə / (AmE), / mɛnˈziːziə ˌfɛrəˈɡiːniə / (BrE)
A straggling shrub native to northwestern North America, with bluish-tinged foliage and umbels of small, bell-shaped flowers.
northwestern North American shrub with bluish foliage

Examples

Menziesia ferruginea is commonly found in moist, forested areas of the Pacific Northwest.
The delicate, bell-shaped flowers of Menziesia ferruginea bloom in late spring.
Gardeners appreciate Menziesia ferruginea for its attractive foliage and subtle floral display.

Etymology

Named after Scottish botanist Archibald Menzies; 'ferruginea' from Latin meaning 'rust-colored', referring to its hair.
