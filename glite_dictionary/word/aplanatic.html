aplanatic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæpləˈnætɪk / (AmE), / ˌæpləˈnætɪk / (BrE)
(of a lens or mirror) free from or corrected for spherical aberration.
free from spherical aberration (optics)

Examples

The telescope uses an aplanatic lens to produce sharper images.
She chose an aplanatic mirror for her research to minimize distortion.
Modern microscopes often employ aplanatic objectives for more accurate observations.

Synset Examples

an aplanatic mirror

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (not) + 'planasthai' (to wander), by analogy with the correction of image deviations.
