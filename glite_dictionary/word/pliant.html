pliant

1. ADJ

/ ˈplaɪənt / (AmE), / ˈplaɪənt / (BrE)
easily bent, flexible, or adaptable without breaking.
easily bent or flexible

Examples

The pliant branches swayed in the wind without snapping.
She chose a pliant material for the sculpture so she could shape it easily.
The yoga instructor admired his pliant muscles.

Synset Examples

a flexible wire
a pliant young tree

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flexible - capable of bending easily without breaking

Etymology

From Old French *pliant* (bending), present participle of *plier* (to bend), from Latin *plicare* (to fold).
