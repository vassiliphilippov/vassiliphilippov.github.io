Araneus

1. NOUN

/ əˈreɪniəs / (AmE), / əˈreɪniəs / (BrE)
a genus of orb-weaving spiders that includes common garden spiders and barn spiders
genus of orb-weaving spiders

Examples

The Araneus genus includes many of the common orb-weaving spiders found in gardens.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the Araneus genus.
The distinctive web of an Araneus spider can be seen in many backyards during autumn.

Etymology

From Latin *araneus* (spider-like), related to *aranea* (spider).

2. NOUN

/ əˈreɪniəs / (AmE), / əˈreɪniəs / (BrE)
A genus of orb-weaving spiders that includes the common garden spider and the barn spider.
genus of orb-weaving spiders

Examples

The Araneus genus includes many familiar spiders found in gardens and forests.
Scientists discovered a new species belonging to the Araneus genus in South America.
Many Araneus spiders are known for their distinctive orb-shaped webs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spider - an arachnid with eight legs, usually spinning webs

Etymology

From Latin *araneus* meaning 'spider'.
