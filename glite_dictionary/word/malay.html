Malay

1. NOUN

/ məˈleɪ / (AmE), / məˈleɪ / (BrE)
a member of an ethnic group native to the northern Malay Peninsula, Malaysia, and parts of the western Malay Archipelago
a member of the Malay ethnic group of Southeast Asia

Examples

The Malay people have a rich tradition of music and dance.
She is a Malay from Penang, Malaysia.
Many Malays celebrate Hari Raya Aidilfitri together with their families.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Malaysian - a native or inhabitant of Malaysia

Etymology

From Malay 'Melayu', the name of the ethnic group.

2. ADJ

/ məˈleɪ / (AmE), / məˈleɪ / (BrE)
of or relating to the people, language, or culture of Malaysia, the northern Malay Peninsula, or the western Malay Archipelago
relating to Malaysia or its people/language

Examples

She is studying Malay history at university.
Many traditional Malay dishes use coconut milk as a key ingredient.
The Malay language is spoken in several Southeast Asian countries.

Synset Examples

Malay peoples
Malayan syllable structure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Malaysian - relating to Malaysia or its people; broader than Malay, as Malaysia is multiethnic

Etymology

From Malay *Melayu*, the name of the ethnic group and language.

3. NOUN

/ məˈleɪ / (AmE), / məˈleɪ / (BrE)
a group of closely related Austronesian languages spoken in Malaysia, Indonesia, Brunei, and Singapore, forming a western subfamily of the Western Malayo-Polynesian languages.
Austronesian language family of Maritime Southeast Asia

Examples

The Malay language has many regional dialects across Southeast Asia.
She is fluent in Malay as well as English and Mandarin.
Linguists classify Malay as part of the Austronesian language family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Indonesian - the official language of Indonesia, a standardized register of Malay
  • Antonym: Javanese - an unrelated major language of Indonesia

Etymology

From Malay 'Melayu,' referring to the ethnic group and their language.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06951664-n
Synset POS: n
