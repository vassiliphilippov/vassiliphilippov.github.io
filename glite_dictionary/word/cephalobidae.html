Cephalobidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛfəˈlɑːbəˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌsɛfəˈləʊbɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of nematode worms characterized by their simple body structure, found in soil and decaying organic matter.
family of nematode worms

Examples

The Cephalobidae are commonly found in rich soil environments around the world.
Scientists study Cephalobidae to understand their role in breaking down organic matter.
Many species in the Cephalobidae family are harmless to crops, unlike some other nematodes.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Cephalobus (from Greek 'kephalē' meaning 'head' + 'lobos' meaning 'lobe'), combined with the taxonomic suffix '-idae' (family).

2. NOUN

/ sɛfəˈlɒbəˌdiː / (AmE), / sɛfəˈlɒbɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of nematode worms including many free-living soil species and some parasites
family of nematode worms

Examples

The Cephalobidae are commonly found living in soil and are studied for their ecological impact.
Scientists recently identified a new species within the Cephalobidae family.
Members of Cephalobidae can sometimes act as plant parasites, affecting crop health.

Etymology

From Greek kephalē (head) + -bidae (a taxonomic suffix for families).
