Pyrrhula pyrrhula

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈruːlə pɪˈruːlə / (AmE), / pɪˈrʊlə pɪˈrʊlə / (BrE)
A common European finch with mostly black and white plumage and a red throat and breast, also known as the Eurasian bullfinch.
European finch with red breast

Examples

The Pyrrhula pyrrhula is easy to spot thanks to its bright red breast.
Birdwatchers were excited to see a Pyrrhula pyrrhula in the nearby forest.
During the winter, Pyrrhula pyrrhula often feeds on seeds and berries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bullfinch - A small Eurasian finch with a thick neck and a red breast

Etymology

From Latin; 'Pyrrhula' is the genus, derived from Greek 'pyrrhos' (flame-colored).
