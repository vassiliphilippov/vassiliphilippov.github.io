columbine

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːləmˌbaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɒl.əm.baɪn / (BrE)
a perennial plant of the genus Aquilegia, characterized by irregular, showy, spurred flowers, native to temperate regions, especially mountains
a flowering plant with spurred blooms (Aquilegia)

Examples

The columbine bloomed early in the rocky mountain spring, adding color to the hillside.
Gardeners prize the columbine for its unique, spurred flowers and delicate foliage.
Several varieties of columbine attract hummingbirds with their vibrant flowers.

Etymology

From Latin columbina (dove-like), referring to the flower's resemblance to a cluster of doves.
