dekameter

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛkəˌmiːtər / (AmE), / ˈdɛkəˌmiːtə / (BrE)
a metric unit of length equal to ten meters
metric unit of length (10 meters)

Examples

The field measured exactly three dekameters in length.
Scientists sometimes use dekameters to describe the dimensions of geographical features.
One dekameter is equal to ten meters, making conversion simple.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: meter - the base unit of length in the metric system
  • Degree Higher: hectometer - a metric unit of length equal to 100 meters
  • Degree Lower: decimeter - a metric unit of length equal to one-tenth of a meter

Etymology

From French 'décamètre', from Ancient Greek 'deka' (ten) + 'mètre' (meter).
