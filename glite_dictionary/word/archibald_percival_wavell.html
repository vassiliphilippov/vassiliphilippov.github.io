Archibald Percival Wavell

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑːrtʃɪˌbɔld pərˈsɪvəl ˈweɪvəl / (AmE), / ˈɑːtʃɪbɑːld pəˈsɪvəl ˈweɪvəl / (BrE)
(1883–1950) British field marshal and military commander during World War II, known for leading Allied forces in North Africa and the Middle East.
British field marshal in WWII

Examples

Archibald Percival Wavell commanded British forces during crucial campaigns in North Africa.
Many historians study the leadership decisions of Archibald Percival Wavell in World War II.
The defeat of the Italians was a significant achievement for Archibald Percival Wavell before later setbacks.
