blowy

1. ADJ

/ ˈbloʊ.i / (AmE), / ˈbləʊ.i / (BrE)
(of weather) characterized by strong winds or abundant breezes; windy.
windy or breezy (weather)

Examples

Yesterday was so blowy we stayed indoors most of the afternoon.
On a blowy morning, the leaves danced across the garden path.
The seaside town is often blowy in early spring.

Synset Examples

blowy weather
a windy bluff

Related Terms

  • Synonym: windy - characterized by or abounding in wind

Etymology

From 'blow' (to move air), with adjective-forming suffix '-y'.
