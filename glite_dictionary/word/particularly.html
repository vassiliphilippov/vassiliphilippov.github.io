particularly

1. ADV

/ pərˈtɪkjələrli / (AmE), / pəˈtɪkjʊləli / (BrE)
In a way that is characteristic of a person, place, or thing; in a manner that is unique or especially typical.
uniquely or characteristically

Examples

The painting was particularly French in its style and composition.
This novel is particularly notable for its use of regional dialects.
The recipe is particularly associated with the southern region of the country.

Synset Examples

these peculiarly cinematic elements
a peculiarly French phenomenon
everyone has a moment in history which belongs particularly to him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uniquely - in a way that is one of a kind or distinctive
  • Antonym: generally - in most cases; usually

Etymology

From particular + -ly; 'particular' from Late Latin particularis (of a part), from Latin particula (small part).

2. ADV

/ pərˈtɪkjələrli / (AmE), / pəˈtɪkjʊləli / (BrE)
in a way that is more than usual or specific compared to others; especially or specifically
especially; more than others

Examples

She enjoys classical music, particularly the works of Mozart and Beethoven.
I wasn't particularly interested in going out tonight, but I agreed anyway.
This region has been particularly affected by the recent storms.

Synset Examples

loves Bach, particularly his partitas
recommended one book in particular
trace major population movements for the Pueblo groups in particular

Related Terms

  • Synonym: especially - used to single out one person or thing over all others
  • Antonym: generally - in most cases; usually

Etymology

From particular + -ly, indicating manner or degree.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00250244-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ pərˈtɪkjələrli / (AmE), / pəˈtɪkjʊləli / (BrE)
to a greater extent than usual; especially or more than others or more than usual
especially; to a greater extent

Examples

I am particularly interested in modern art and sculpture.
The weather in June is particularly unpredictable in this region.
She was particularly proud of her latest achievement.

Synset Examples

he was particularly fussy about spelling
a particularly gruesome attack
under peculiarly tragic circumstances
an especially (or specially) cautious approach to the danger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: especially - to a great extent; in particular

Etymology

From 'particular' + '-ly', originating from Late Latin 'particularis' (of a part).
