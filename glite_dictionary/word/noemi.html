Noemi

1. PROPN

/ noʊˈiːmi / (AmE), / nəʊˈiːmi / (BrE)
(in the Bible) the mother-in-law of Ruth, whose story is told in the Book of Ruth in the Old Testament.
biblical mother-in-law of Ruth

Examples

Many biblical scholars study the relationship between Ruth and Noemi to understand themes of loyalty and faith.
Noemi's journey from hardship to hope is a central narrative in the Book of Ruth.
In Sunday school, we read about how Noemi supported Ruth after the loss of her sons.

Etymology

From Hebrew נָעֳמִי (Naʻămi), meaning 'pleasantness'.
