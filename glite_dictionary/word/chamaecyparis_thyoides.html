Chamaecyparis thyoides

1. NOUN

/ kəˌmiːsɪˈpɛərɪs θaɪˈɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / kəˌmiːsɪˈpɛːrɪs θaɪˈɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
a slow-growing medium-sized evergreen tree native to the eastern coast of the United States, also known as Atlantic white cedar
Atlantic white cedar tree species

Examples

The swamps on the eastern seaboard are often dominated by Chamaecyparis thyoides trees.
Many conservationists are working to preserve Chamaecyparis thyoides habitats along the Atlantic Coast.
Chamaecyparis thyoides is valued for its durable, aromatic wood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Atlantic white cedar - common name for Chamaecyparis thyoides
  • Synonym: arborvitae - a similar North American evergreen tree

Etymology

From Greek *chamai* (on the ground) + *kyparissos* (cypress) and Neo-Latin, referring to resemblance to cypress and arborvitae.
