adjusted

1. ADJ

/ əˈdʒʌstɪd / (AmE), / əˈdʒʌstɪd / (BrE)
(of garments) made to fit better by being changed in style or size.
altered for better fit (of clothing)

Examples

The tailor returned my adjusted jacket, which now fits perfectly around the shoulders.
She wore a beautifully adjusted dress that hugged her figure elegantly.
After alterations, the adjusted trousers finally reached the right length.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: altered - changed in form or appearance, especially clothing

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'adjust', from Old French 'ajuster', from Late Latin 'ad-juxtare' (to bring near).

2. ADJ

/ əˈdʒʌstɪd / (AmE), / əˈdʒʌstɪd / (BrE)
altered or changed in order to fit particular requirements or conditions, or to bring something into proper relation
changed to meet specific requirements

Examples

The technician ensured all settings were adjusted before restarting the machine.
Her schedule was adjusted to accommodate her new classes.
All mirrors should be adjusted for maximum visibility before driving.

Synset Examples

an adjusted insurance claim
the car runs more smoothly with the timing adjusted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: modified - changed in form or character
  • Antonym: unaltered - not changed; remaining the same

Etymology

Past participle of 'adjust', from Old French *ajuster*, from Late Latin *adjuxtare* (to bring near).

3. ADJ

/ əˈdʒʌstɪd / (AmE), / əˈdʒʌstɪd / (BrE)
(of a person) having adapted successfully to one's environment or circumstances; stable and well balanced emotionally or socially.
well adapted to environment or circumstances

Examples

After moving to the city, she quickly became an adjusted member of the community.
The program helps children become better adjusted to school life.
He is a well-adjusted adult despite his difficult childhood.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: maladjusted - unable to adapt to the environment or circumstances

Etymology

From the past participle of 'adjust', from Old French 'ajuster', from Late Latin 'adjuxtare' (to make near, fit).

4. ADJ

/ əˈdʒʌstɪd / (AmE), / əˈdʒʌstɪd / (BrE)
(of a person) successfully adapted to the demands of daily life; showing good emotional stability.
emotionally stable; adapted to life

Examples

Although he faced many challenges as a child, he has become a well adjusted adult.
The counselor praised the students for being socially adjusted and emotionally balanced.
Children who feel secure at home tend to be better adjusted at school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: well-adjusted - (of a person) mentally healthy and able to deal with life well
  • Antonym: maladjusted - unable to cope with the demands of a normal social environment

Etymology

Past participle of 'adjust', from Old French 'ajuster' (to make straight), based on Latin 'ad-' (to) + 'iustus' (right).
