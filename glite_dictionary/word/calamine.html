calamine

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæləˌmaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkæl.əˌmiːn / (BrE)
a white or pinkish mineral, primarily zinc carbonate or zinc silicate, which is a common ore of zinc
white or pinkish zinc ore mineral

Examples

The mine produced large quantities of calamine for use in zinc refining.
Samples of calamine often display a pale pink or white color.
Early brass was made by combining copper and calamine in a furnace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smithsonite - a mineral form of zinc carbonate, also known as calamine
  • Synonym: hemimorphite - a mineral form of zinc silicate, historically also called calamine

Etymology

From Latin calamina, a corruption of Greek καλαμίνα (kalamina), related to 'Kadmeia gē' (Theban earth), referring to ores found near Thebes.
