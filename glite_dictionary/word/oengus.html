Oengus

1. PROPN

/ ˈeɪnɡəs / (AmE), / ˈeɪŋɡəs / (BrE)
In Irish mythology, a Celtic god of love and beauty, regarded as the patron deity of young men and women.
Celtic god of love and beauty

Examples

Many legends describe Oengus as a youthful and handsome deity associated with love.
During the festival, offerings were made to Oengus in hopes of finding true love.
Scholars debate the origins of Oengus within the context of Celtic religious beliefs.

Etymology

From Old Irish 'Oengus' (later 'Aengus'), a name of uncertain origin, known from early Irish mythology.
