Lobipes lobatus

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈbaɪpiːz loʊˈbeɪtəs / (AmE), / ˈlɒbɪpiːz ləˈbeɪtəs / (BrE)
A small shorebird, commonly known as the red-necked phalarope, that breeds in Arctic regions and is often seen in large flocks at sea.
species of Arctic-breeding shorebird

Examples

During migration, Lobipes lobatus can be observed in large numbers on open oceans.
The breeding grounds of Lobipes lobatus are located in the high Arctic tundra.
Researchers studied the feeding behavior of Lobipes lobatus along the coastal wetlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red-necked phalarope - Common name for Lobipes lobatus, a small Arctic shorebird

Etymology

From Neo-Latin binomial nomenclature; *Lobipes* from Latin 'lobus' (lobe) and Greek 'pous' (foot), and *lobatus* from Latin for 'lobed', referring to the bird's lobed toes.
