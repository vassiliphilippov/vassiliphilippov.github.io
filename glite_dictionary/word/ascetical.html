ascetical

1. ADJ

/ əˈsɛtɪkəl / (AmE), / əˈsɛtɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the practice of rigorous self-discipline and abstention from indulgence, typically for religious reasons
relating to rigorous self-discipline

Examples

The monks led an ascetical life, devoting themselves to prayer and meditation.
Her ascetical approach to diet impressed her friends with its strictness.
The ascetical traditions of the order demanded long periods of silence and fasting.

Synset Examples

ascetic practices

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ascetic - practicing strict self-denial as a measure of personal and spiritual discipline

Etymology

From Late Latin 'asceticus', from Greek 'asketikos' (rigorously self-disciplined), with the English suffix '-al'.

2. ADJ

/ əˈsɛtɪkəl / (AmE), / əˈsɛtɪkəl / (BrE)
Characterized by or related to the practice of severe self-discipline and abstention from all forms of indulgence, typically for religious or spiritual reasons.
practicing severe self-discipline; abstinent

Examples

The monk lived an ascetical life, rejecting all material comforts and luxuries.
Her ascetical habits included fasting and meditating for hours each day.
Many admire his ascetical dedication to spiritual growth over worldly pleasure.

Synset Examples

Be systematically ascetic … do … something for no other reason than that you would rather not do it
a desert nomad's austere life
a spartan diet
a spartan existence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ascetic - practicing severe self-discipline and abstention; rigorously self-denying
  • Synonym: austere - severely simple; without luxury or comfort
  • Antonym: indulgent - allowing oneself or others excess or luxury; yielding to desires

Etymology

From Greek askētikos (rigorously self-disciplined), from askētēs (monk or hermit).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
