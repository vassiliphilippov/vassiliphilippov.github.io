enterokinase

1. NOUN

/ ˌentəroʊˈkaɪneɪs / (AmE), / ˌentərəʊˈkaɪneɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme in intestinal juice that activates trypsinogen by converting it into trypsin
intestinal enzyme that activates trypsinogen
medical

Examples

Enterokinase plays a crucial role in the digestion of proteins in the small intestine.
A deficiency in enterokinase can lead to problems with protein metabolism.
Scientists studied how enterokinase activates trypsinogen during the digestive process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trypsin - digestive enzyme activated by enterokinase

Etymology

From Greek 'enteron' (intestine) + 'kinase' (enzyme that causes movement or change).
