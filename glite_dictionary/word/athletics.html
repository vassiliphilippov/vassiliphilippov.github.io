athletics

1. NOUN

/ əˈθlɛtɪks / (AmE), / æθˈlɛtɪks / (BrE)
organized competitive sports and physical activities, especially as part of a school's extracurricular program.
competitive sports activities

Examples

She excels in athletics and has represented her school in several track events.
Participation in athletics helps students develop teamwork and discipline.
The college places great emphasis on both academics and athletics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sport - physical activities involving skill and competition

Etymology

From Greek athlētikos (pertaining to contest), from athlētēs (athlete).

2. NOUN

/ əˈθlɛtɪks / (AmE), / æθˈlɛt.ɪks / (BrE)
Sports and physical contests involving competitive physical activity, especially running, jumping, and throwing.
competitive sports, especially track and field

Examples

She has excelled in athletics since she was a child.
The school is known for having an outstanding athletics program.
Many countries send their best to compete in the Olympic athletics events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sport - all kinds of physical games and activities, especially those that are competitive
  • Synonym: game - an activity that one engages in for amusement or competition

Etymology

From Greek 'athlētikos' (of or for contests), from 'athlētēs' (contestant).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07471429-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ əˈθlɛtɪks / (AmE), / æθˈlɛtɪks / (BrE)
physical sports and games involving competition and exercise, especially organized events such as running, jumping, and throwing
competitive physical sports or games

Examples

She excels in athletics and competes at national tournaments every year.
Many schools encourage students to participate in athletics to promote teamwork and fitness.
After his injury, he decided to retire from professional athletics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sport - an activity involving physical exertion and skill in which an individual or team competes

Etymology

From Greek athlētikos (pertaining to contest or athletes), from athlētēs (athlete).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00524569-n
Synset POS: n
