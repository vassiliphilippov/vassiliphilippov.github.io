to evade

1. VERB

/ ɪˈveɪd / (AmE), / ɪˈveɪd / (BrE)
to use cunning or deceit to escape or avoid (something or someone)
escape or avoid using cunning or deceit

Examples

The thief managed to evade the police by hiding in an abandoned building.
She tried to evade his questions by changing the subject.
Many companies attempt to evade taxes through complex financial arrangements.

Synset Examples

The con man always evades

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to avoid - to keep away from or stop oneself from doing (something)
  • Synonym: to elude - to avoid or escape from (someone or something), typically in a skillful or cunning way

Etymology

From Latin 'evadere', from 'e-' (out of) + 'vadere' (to go).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00812701-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪˈveɪd / (AmE), / ɪˈveɪd / (BrE)
to escape from or avoid (someone or something), especially in a clever or indirect way; to fail to be grasped, achieved, or understood
escape or avoid (physically or mentally)

Examples

The company tried to evade taxes by using offshore accounts.
No matter how hard she tried, the answer continued to evade her.
Despite their best efforts, the snake managed to evade capture.

Synset Examples

The thief eluded the police
This difficult idea seems to evade her
The event evades explanation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to elude - to escape from or avoid (a danger, enemy, or pursuer), typically in a skillful or cunning way
  • Antonym: to confront - to face up to and deal with (a problem or difficult situation)

Etymology

From Latin evadere, from e- (out) + vadere (to go).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02078305-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɪˈveɪd / (AmE), / ɪˈveɪd / (BrE)
to avoid or try to avoid fulfilling, answering, or performing something, especially a duty, question, or issue
avoid fulfilling a duty or answering a question

Examples

He tried to evade the question by changing the subject.
Some companies use loopholes to evade paying taxes.
She skillfully evaded her responsibilities during the meeting.

Synset Examples

He dodged the issue
she skirted the problem
They tend to evade their responsibilities
he evaded the questions skillfully

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to avoid - to keep away from or prevent oneself from doing something
  • Antonym: to confront - to face a problem, duty, or question directly

Etymology

From Latin evadere, meaning 'to escape' (ex 'out of' + vadere 'to go').

4. VERB

/ ɪˈveɪd / (AmE), / ɪˈveɪd / (BrE)
to avoid or escape from someone or something, especially by cleverness or trickery
avoid or escape by cleverness

Examples

He tried to evade answering the direct question.
For years, the spy managed to evade capture by the authorities.
She skillfully evaded the obstacles in her path.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to elude - to escape from or avoid (someone or something)
  • Antonym: to confront - to face up to and deal with a problem or difficult situation

Etymology

From Latin 'evadere' (to escape), from 'e-' (out) + 'vadere' (to go).
