Mentzelia lindleyi

1. NOUN

/ mɛnˈtsɛliə lɪndˈlaɪ.i / (AmE), / mɛnˈtsɛliə lɪndˈliː.i / (BrE)
an annual plant grown especially for its fragrant golden flowers that bloom at night, commonly known as golden bartonia or Lindley's blazing star
a night-blooming annual with golden flowers

Examples

Gardeners prize Mentzelia lindleyi for its showy, golden blooms that open at dusk.
We planted Mentzelia lindleyi along the garden path to enjoy its night-time fragrance.
The bright flowers of Mentzelia lindleyi attract pollinators long after sunset.

Etymology

Named after English botanist John Lindley; Mentzelia from botanist Christian Mentzel.
