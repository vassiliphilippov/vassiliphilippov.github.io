beleaguering

1. NOUN

/ bəˈliɡərɪŋ / (AmE), / bɪˈliːɡərɪŋ / (BrE)
the military action of surrounding a fortified place and cutting it off while continuing attacks
military siege or surrounding attack

Examples

The beleaguering of the fortress lasted for several months without a decisive victory.
During the beleaguering of the city, supplies quickly ran low inside the walls.
Historical accounts describe the beleaguering as both brutal and relentless.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: siege - a military operation of surrounding and attacking a fortified place

Etymology

From 'beleaguer' (to surround with armed forces), from Dutch 'belegeren'.
