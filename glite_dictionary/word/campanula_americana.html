Campanula americana

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæm.pəˈnjuː.lə əˌmɛr.ɪˈkɑː.nə / (AmE), / ˌkæm.pəˈnjuː.lə əˌmɛr.ɪˈkɑː.nə / (BrE)
An annual or perennial plant native to eastern North America, having long spikes of blue or white bell-shaped flowers.
plant with blue or white spiked flowers

Examples

Campanula americana is often found in moist woodland areas throughout the eastern United States.
Gardeners admire Campanula americana for its elegant blue spikes that bloom in late summer.
The distinctive white flowers of Campanula americana make it a favorite among native plant enthusiasts.

Etymology

From Latin campanula (little bell), referring to the bell-shaped flowers, and americana (of America).
