Attacus atlas

1. NOUN

/ ˈætəkəs ˈætləs / (AmE), / ˈætəkəs ˈætləs / (BrE)
a very large moth of the Saturniidae family, native to Asia, known for its impressive wingspan and sometimes raised for silk production
large Asian moth, sometimes raised for silk

Examples

The Attacus atlas is one of the largest moths in the world, with a wingspan that can exceed 25 centimeters.
Researchers studied the silk produced by the Attacus atlas to explore new textile possibilities.
Many nature enthusiasts travel to Southeast Asia hoping to catch a glimpse of the magnificent Attacus atlas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silk moth - any moth whose caterpillar produces silk

Etymology

From Latin Attacus (genus name) and atlas (Greek mythological figure known for great strength, likely referring to the moth's large size).
