gammy

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡæmi / (AmE), / ˈɡæmi / (BrE)
(British informal) injured, painful, or not functioning properly, especially referring to a limb or joint.
(Brit. informal) injured or lame, usually of a limb
informal british

Examples

He couldn't walk far because of his gammy leg.
After twisting her ankle, she ended up with a rather gammy foot.
Granddad complains that his gammy knee aches whenever it rains.

Synset Examples

a gammy foot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lame - unable to walk properly due to injury or illness
  • Synonym: crippled - severely damaged or impaired, especially physically
  • Antonym: healthy - in good health; not injured
