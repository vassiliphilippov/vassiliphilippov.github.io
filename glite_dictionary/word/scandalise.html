to scandalise

1. VERB

/ ˈskændəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈskændəlaɪz / (BrE)
to shock or offend someone by violating accepted standards of morality or propriety
shock or offend morally

Examples

The corrupt politician's actions scandalised the entire community.
She was scandalised by the language used on the TV show.
It would scandalise her conservative parents if they knew she had a tattoo.

Synset Examples

The scandalous behavior of this married woman shocked her friends

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shock - to cause surprise and disbelief, sometimes moral outrage
  • Synonym: outrage - to cause anger or indignation in someone
  • Antonym: delight - to give great pleasure or satisfaction

Etymology

From Old French 'scandaliser', from Late Latin 'scandalizare', from 'scandalum' (cause of offense).
