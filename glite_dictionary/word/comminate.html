to comminate

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑːməˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒmɪneɪt / (BrE)
to solemnly threaten with divine punishment or proclaim to be accursed
threaten with divine punishment
archaic

Examples

In the old days, priests would commimate those they deemed heretics.
He saw it as his duty to commimate the city for its wickedness.
Rarely now do people commimate offenders with ancient curses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curse - invoke or wish evil upon; utter a curse
  • Antonym: to bless - to invoke or wish well-being or good fortune

Etymology

From Latin 'comminari' (to threaten).
