blight

1. NOUN

/ blaɪt / (AmE), / blaɪt / (BrE)
A plant disease that causes withering, typically without the plant parts rotting.
disease causing withering in plants

Examples

The potato crop failed due to a sudden outbreak of blight in the fields.
Farmers struggled to contain the blight that swept through their tomato plants.
Early detection of blight can help save some of the harvest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fungus - a type of organism that often causes blight

Etymology

Origin unknown, first recorded in the early 17th century.

2. VERB

/ blaɪt / (AmE), / blaɪt / (BrE)
to cause to suffer from blight, especially by damaging growth or ruining prospects for success
to cause harm or ruin; damage growth

Examples

A sudden frost can blight the crops overnight, leaving farmers with huge losses.
The economic crisis threatened to blight the progress the city had made in recent years.
Harsh criticism can sometimes blight a young musician's enthusiasm and confidence.

Synset Examples

Too much rain may blight the garden with mold

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruin - to spoil or destroy completely
  • Synonym: to wither - to become dry and shriveled

Etymology

Probably from Old English *blycean* (to grow pale, blister).

3. NOUN

/ blaɪt / (AmE), / blaɪt / (BrE)
A state or condition of being damaged, deteriorated, or ruined, especially by neglect or adverse circumstances.
state of deterioration or ruin

Examples

The abandoned buildings contributed to the sense of blight in the neighborhood.
Efforts to revitalize the downtown area aimed to remove the urban blight.
Economic decline led to widespread blight throughout the once-prosperous city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruin - serious damage or destruction to something
  • Synonym: decay - gradual decline in strength or soundness
  • Antonym: prosperity - the state of being successful or thriving
