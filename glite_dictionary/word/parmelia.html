Parmelia

1. NOUN

/ pɑːrˈmiːliə / (AmE), / pɑːˈmiːlɪə / (BrE)
a genus of chiefly alpine, leaf-like lichens in the family Parmeliaceae
genus of alpine, leaf-like lichens

Examples

The scientist studied several species within the genus Parmelia found in high mountain regions.
Many Parmelia lichens are used as indicators of air quality because of their sensitivity to pollution.
Researchers recently described a new Parmelia species with distinctive leaf-like lobes.

Etymology

From New Latin Parmelia, dating to 18th-century taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ pɑrˈmiːliə / (AmE), / pɑːˈmiːliə / (BrE)
A genus of mostly alpine, leaf-like lichens in the family Parmeliaceae, serving as the type genus of the family.
a genus of leaf-like lichens

Examples

The alpine rocks were covered with Parmelia species, adding color to the landscape.
Botanists studied the distribution of Parmelia in mountainous regions.
Many Parmelia varieties thrive in cold, unpolluted air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lichens - composite organisms arising from algae or cyanobacteria living among filaments of fungi

Etymology

From Neo-Latin *Parmelia*, a genus name created in modern taxonomy.
