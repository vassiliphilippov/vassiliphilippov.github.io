blowfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈbloʊˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbləʊˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A type of marine fish with a spiny, elongated body that can inflate itself into a globe when threatened; some species contain a powerful nerve toxin.
inflating spiny marine fish, some poisonous

Examples

Some species of blowfish are considered a delicacy in Japan, despite their deadly poison.
The blowfish instantly puffed up when the diver approached too closely.
Scientists study the toxins produced by blowfish to understand their effects on the nervous system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pufferfish - another name for a blowfish or similar fish

2. NOUN

/ ˈbloʊˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbləʊˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a type of fish that can inflate its body and is considered a delicacy in some countries, though it contains a dangerous nerve poison, especially in the ovaries and liver
a fish; poisonous culinary delicacy

Examples

The chef expertly prepared the blowfish, taking extra care to remove the poisonous organs.
Many people travel to Japan to taste authentic blowfish dishes despite the risks involved.
Consuming improperly prepared blowfish can be fatal due to its potent toxin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pufferfish - a fish that can inflate its body as a defense mechanism
