merchantability

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɜːrtʃəntəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌmɜːtʃəntəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality of being suitable to be bought or sold, especially meeting the standards expected for sale in the market
quality of being fit for sale
legal

Examples

The warranty ensures the merchantability of all goods sold by the company.
Questions about merchantability often arise during contract negotiations between retailers and suppliers.
Lack of merchantability can make a product difficult or even illegal to sell.

Etymology

From 'merchant' + '-ability'; directly associated with commercial law terminology.
