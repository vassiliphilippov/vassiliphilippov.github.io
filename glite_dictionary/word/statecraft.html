statecraft

1. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪtˌkræft / (AmE), / ˈsteɪtˌkrɑːft / (BrE)
the skill and wisdom in managing the affairs of a state or conducting government policy
skillful management of government affairs

Examples

Her expertise in statecraft made her a trusted advisor to several world leaders.
The course focuses on diplomacy and statecraft in modern international relations.
Centuries of statecraft have shaped the nation's political traditions and institutions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: statesmanship - skill in leading a country, especially wisely and honorably

Etymology

Formed from 'state' + 'craft', indicating the art or skill of governing.
