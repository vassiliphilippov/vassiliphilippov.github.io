Rhizobiaceae

1. NOUN

/ raɪˌzoʊbiˈeɪsiːˌiː / (AmE), / raɪˌzəʊbiːˈeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
A family of rod-shaped bacteria, some of which are important in nitrogen fixation in plants.
family of rod-shaped bacteria
medical

Examples

Some members of the Rhizobiaceae family are essential for nitrogen-fixing symbiosis in legumes.
Scientists have studied Rhizobiaceae to better understand their role in agriculture.
The soil sample contained several species belonging to the Rhizobiaceae.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus name Rhizobium plus the standard family suffix -aceae.
