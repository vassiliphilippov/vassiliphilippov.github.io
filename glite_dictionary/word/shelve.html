to shelve

1. VERB

/ ʃɛlv / (AmE), / ʃɛlv / (BrE)
to place (something) on a shelf or shelves
put on a shelf

Examples

Every morning, the librarian would shelve the newly returned books.
After cleaning the kitchen, she shelved the jars neatly above the counter.
He spent the afternoon shelving his collection of mystery novels.

Synset Examples

shelve books

Etymology

From shelf + -e (verb formation), modelled after similar English verbs.

2. VERB

/ ʃɛlv / (AmE), / ʃɛlv / (BrE)
to decide not to proceed with a plan, project, or idea either temporarily or indefinitely
postpone or suspend a plan or idea

Examples

Due to budget constraints, the committee decided to shelve the new building project.
We may have to shelve our travel plans until next year.
The proposal was shelved after much debate by the board.

Synset Examples

let's postpone the exam

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to postpone - to delay something until a later time
  • Antonym: abandon - to give up on a plan or idea

Etymology

From shelf (noun), used metaphorically since the late 19th century for putting something aside as though placing it on a shelf.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02648898-v

3. VERB

/ ʃɛlv / (AmE), / ʃɛlv / (BrE)
to decide not to proceed with a plan, proposal, or project, either temporarily or permanently; to postpone or set aside.
put plans on hold or set aside

Examples

Because of the budget cuts, the company had to shelve its expansion plans.
They decided to shelve the proposal until more research could be done.
She reluctantly shelved her plans to move abroad when a new opportunity arose at home.

Synset Examples

She chose to shelve her plans for a vacation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to postpone - to delay something to a later time
  • Antonym: abandon - to give up a plan or intention completely

Etymology

From shelf (noun), referring figuratively to putting something on a shelf out of active use.
