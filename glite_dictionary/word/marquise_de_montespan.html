Marquise de Montespan

1. PROPN

/ mɑrˈkiz də mɑnˈtɛspæn / (AmE), / mɑːˈkiːz də mɒnˈtɛspæn / (BrE)
(1641–1707) A French noblewoman who was the official mistress of King Louis XIV of France until his attentions shifted to Madame de Maintenon.
French noblewoman, mistress of Louis XIV

Examples

The Marquise de Montespan was renowned for her influence at the court of Louis XIV.
Many scandals surrounded the relationship between Louis XIV and the Marquise de Montespan.
After falling out of favor, the Marquise de Montespan led a more secluded life.

Etymology

French title 'marquise' (noblewoman), family name 'Montespan', from her marriage to Louis Henri de Pardaillan de Gondrin, Marquis de Montespan.
