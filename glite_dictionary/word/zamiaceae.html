Zamiaceae

1. NOUN

/ zæmiˈeɪsiiː / (AmE), / zamiːˈeɪsɪiː / (BrE)
a family of tropical and subtropical cycad plants that includes the zamias, often distinguished from the closely related Cycadaceae.
family of cycads including zamias

Examples

The Zamiaceae family is primarily found in tropical and subtropical regions.
Many members of the Zamiaceae are grown as ornamental plants for their unique foliage.
Unlike the Cycadaceae, the Zamiaceae have leaflets with parallel veins.

Etymology

From the genus name Zamia, combined with the standard plant family suffix -aceae, used in botanical Latin.

2. NOUN

/ ˌzæmiˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌzæmɪˈeɪsɪiː / (BrE)
A family of cycads, often called zamias, comprising tropical and subtropical plants with stout trunks and pinnate leaves.
a family of cycads (zamias)

Examples

The Zamiaceae are a family of cycads found mainly in tropical and subtropical regions.
Members of the Zamiaceae family are often grown as ornamental plants for their striking foliage.
Botanists debated whether Zamiaceae should be classified separately from Cycadaceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cycadaceae - another family of cycads closely related to Zamiaceae

Etymology

From the genus name Zamia, from Latin 'zamia' (possibly related to pine cone), plus the standard botanical family suffix '-aceae'.
