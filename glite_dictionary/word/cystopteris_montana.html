Cystopteris montana

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈstɑptərɪs mɑnˈtænə / (AmE), / sɪˈstɒptərɪs mɒnˈtænə / (BrE)
a species of fern found in rocky mountainous regions of the Northern Hemisphere
a fern species of mountains

Examples

The delicate fronds of Cystopteris montana are well adapted to the cool, rocky slopes of alpine regions.
Botanists conducted a survey to monitor populations of Cystopteris montana in the northern forests.
Cystopteris montana is often found in moist crevices where it can avoid direct sunlight.

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Cystopteris' plus Latin 'montana' (mountainous).
