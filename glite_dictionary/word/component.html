component

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈpoʊnənt / (AmE), / kəmˈpəʊnənt / (BrE)
a part or element of a larger whole, especially one that can be separated or replaced, such as in a system or machine
part of a larger whole, often separable

Examples

The technician replaced the faulty component in the computer to restore its functionality.
Each component of the engine must be checked regularly for wear and tear.
The stereo system was made up of several separate components that could be upgraded individually.

Synset Examples

spare components for cars
a component or constituent element of a system

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constituent - an element or part of something
  • Synonym: element - a part or aspect of something larger
  • Antonym: whole - something that is complete, an entity with all its parts

Etymology

From Latin 'componere' (to put together).

2. NOUN

/ kəmˈpoʊnənt / (AmE), / kəmˈpəʊnənt / (BrE)
a part or element that makes up a larger whole or system
part or element of a whole

Examples

Each component of the device was carefully assembled by hand.
Trust is a vital component of any successful relationship.
This machine has several interchangeable components for different functions.

Synset Examples

he wanted to feel a part of something bigger than himself
I read a portion of the manuscript
the smaller component is hard to reach
the animal constituent of plankton

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constituent - an essential part or component of something
  • Synonym: part - a piece or segment of something larger
  • Antonym: whole - something that is complete in itself, comprised of parts

Etymology

From Latin componere (to put together), via Medieval Latin component- (putting together).

3. NOUN

/ kəmˈpoʊ.nənt / (AmE), / kəmˈpəʊ.nənt / (BrE)
an abstract or essential part of something, especially one that combines with others to make a whole
an abstract part or element

Examples

Trust is a crucial component of a healthy relationship.
The curriculum has several components, including mathematics and science.
Each component of her plan was carefully thought out and executed.

Synset Examples

jealousy was a component of his character
two constituents of a musical composition are melody and harmony
the grammatical elements of a sentence
a key factor in her success
humor: an effective ingredient of a speech

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constituent - an essential part or component of something
  • Synonym: element - a basic or essential part of something abstract
  • Antonym: whole - all of something considered together as a single thing

Etymology

From Latin componere (to put together), via Medieval Latin componentem (putting together).
