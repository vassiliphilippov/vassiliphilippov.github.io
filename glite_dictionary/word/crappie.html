crappie

1. NOUN

/ ˈkræpi / (AmE), / ˈkræpi / (BrE)
A small North American freshwater fish, found especially in central United States rivers, belonging to the sunfish family.
a small North American freshwater fish

Examples

Many anglers enjoy fishing for crappie in the spring when they are most active.
The lake is known for its large population of crappie and bass.
He caught a dozen crappie before noon using small jigs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sunfish - a family of freshwater fish in North America

2. NOUN

/ ˈkræpi / (AmE), / ˈkræpi / (BrE)
A small freshwater fish of the genus Pomoxis, native to rivers and lakes in central North America, popular with anglers.
a small North American freshwater fish

Examples

He proudly showed off the crappie he caught at the lake this morning.
The river is known for its abundant crappie during spring spawning season.
Fried crappie is a favorite dish among local anglers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sunfish - a North American freshwater fish of the family Centrarchidae
