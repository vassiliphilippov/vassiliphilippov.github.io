unqualified

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkwɑlɪfaɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈkwɒlɪfaɪd / (BrE)
not having the necessary qualifications, skills, or training for a particular job or activity.
lacking necessary qualifications or training

Examples

Because he was unqualified for the position, the company decided not to hire him.
Allowing an unqualified worker to operate machinery can be dangerous.
She felt unqualified to give advice on the complicated legal issue.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: qualified - having the necessary qualifications or training

Etymology

From un- (not) + qualified, meaning not equipped with required qualifications.

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkwɑːləˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈkwɒlɪfaɪd / (BrE)
not limited or restricted; without reservation or qualification
not limited or restricted

Examples

His statement was an unqualified denial of any wrongdoing.
The plan was met with unqualified support from the entire team.
She received unqualified praise for her performance in the play.

Synset Examples

an unqualified denial

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absolute - not qualified or diminished in any way; total
  • Antonym: conditional - subject to one or more conditions or requirements being met; made or granted on certain terms

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'qualified'.

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkwɑlɪˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈkwɒlɪfaɪd / (BrE)
not having the legal qualifications or authority required for a particular action or position.
not legally qualified
legal

Examples

Because she was still a minor, she was unqualified to sign the contract.
The evidence was dismissed because the witness was unqualified to testify in court.
As the candidate lacked the required certification, he was deemed unqualified for the legal proceedings.

Synset Examples

a wife is usually considered unqualified to testify against her husband
incompetent witnesses

Related Terms

  • Antonym: qualified - having the required legal authority or credentials

Etymology

Formed by combining the prefix 'un-' (not) with 'qualified', from Latin 'qualificare' (give a quality to).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00514186-a
Synset POS: a
