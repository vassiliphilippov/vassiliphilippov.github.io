linen

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪnən / (AmE), / ˈlɪnən / (BrE)
Sheets, tablecloths, or clothing made from linen cloth, especially household items such as bedding and table coverings.
household sheets or clothing made from linen cloth

Examples

She changed the linen on all the beds before the guests arrived.
Please leave your dirty linen in the hamper for housekeeping.
Fresh, crisp linen made the room feel especially welcoming.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laundry - clothes or linens that need washing or have been washed

Etymology

From Latin 'linum' (flax), via Old French 'lin'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɪnən / (AmE), / ˈlɪnɪn / (BrE)
A high-quality paper made from linen fibers or with a linen-like texture or finish, often used for fine stationery and certificates.
high-quality paper made from linen or with linen finish

Examples

The invitation was printed on elegant linen to give it a sophisticated feel.
He chose linen for his résumé to make a strong impression.
This certificate is issued on official linen for added prestige.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rag paper - Paper made from cotton or linen rags

Etymology

From Old English *līn* (flax) and Old French *lin*, referring originally to linen cloth.

3. NOUN

/ ˈlɪnən / (AmE), / ˈlɪnɪn / (BrE)
A fabric woven from fibers of the flax plant, valued for its strength, absorbency, and coolness.
fabric made from flax fibers

Examples

The tablecloth was made of pure linen and felt cool to the touch.
She wore a light linen dress to stay comfortable in the summer heat.
Many hotels use white linen sheets for their guest rooms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cotton - a soft white fibrous substance made from the cotton plant, used as textile fiber
  • Synonym: wool - fiber obtained from sheep and other animals, used to make fabric
  • Synonym: silk - a fine, smooth fiber produced by silk moths, used to make fabric

Etymology

From Old English 'līn', from Latin 'linum' (flax).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
