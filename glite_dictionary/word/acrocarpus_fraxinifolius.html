Acrocarpus fraxinifolius

1. NOUN

/ ˌækroʊˈkɑrpəs ˌfræksɪnɪˈfoʊliəs / (AmE), / ˌakrəʊˈkɑːpəs ˌfræksɪnɪˈfəʊliəs / (BrE)
An East Indian timber tree with hard, durable wood, especially used for making tea boxes.
East Indian timber tree used for tea boxes

Examples

The wood of Acrocarpus fraxinifolius is prized for its durability in manufacturing tea boxes.
Farmers in India often cultivate Acrocarpus fraxinifolius for its valuable timber.
The leaves of Acrocarpus fraxinifolius resemble those of the ash tree.

Etymology

From Greek 'akron' (top), 'karpos' (fruit) and Neo-Latin 'fraxinifolius' (ash-leafed).
