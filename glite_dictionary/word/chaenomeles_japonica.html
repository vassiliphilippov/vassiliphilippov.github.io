Chaenomeles japonica

1. NOUN

/ kiːˌnoʊˈmiːliːz dʒəˈpɑnɪkə / (AmE), / kiːˌnɒˈmiːliːz dʒəˈpænɪkə / (BrE)
A deciduous thorny shrub native to Japan, known for its red blossoms; commonly called Japanese quince.
shrub with red blossoms, Japanese quince

Examples

The Chaenomeles japonica in the garden blooms with stunning red flowers each spring.
Botanists appreciate Chaenomeles japonica for its hardiness and ornamental value.
Many gardeners use Chaenomeles japonica as a decorative hedge due to its dense, thorny branches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Japanese quince - common name for Chaenomeles japonica

Etymology

From New Latin genus name Chaenomeles (from Greek 'chainein' to gape + 'melon' apple), and Latin 'japonica', meaning 'of Japan'.
