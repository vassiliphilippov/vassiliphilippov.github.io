blintz

1. NOUN

/ blɪnts / (AmE), / blɪnts / (BrE)
A thin pancake, typically of Jewish origin, rolled or folded with a filling such as cheese or fruit, and then fried or baked.
thin pancake with filling, fried or baked

Examples

She served cheese blintz with a topping of fresh strawberries for breakfast.
At the festival, I tried my first sweet blintz filled with apricot jam.
The restaurant's signature dish is a savory blintz stuffed with mushrooms and herbs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crepe - a very thin pancake, usually without filling inside

Etymology

From Yiddish *blintse*, from Russian *blinets*, diminutive of *blin* (pancake).
