unbowed

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈbaʊd / (AmE), / ʌnˈbaʊd / (BrE)
not having submitted or yielded to pressure, defeat, or authority; not forced to bow down or give in
not defeated or humbled

Examples

Despite numerous setbacks, she remained unbowed and continued her fight for justice.
The country emerged from the crisis unbowed and determined to rebuild.
He faced his critics unbowed, refusing to apologize for his beliefs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defiant - showing resistance or disobedience
  • Antonym: submissive - willing to submit or yield to authority

Etymology

From prefix un- (not) + bowed (from bow, to bend in submission).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈbaʊd / (AmE), / ʌnˈbaʊd / (BrE)
not bent or curved; remaining upright, especially in posture or spirit.
not bent; upright in posture or spirit

Examples

She faced her critics with an unbowed posture, refusing to show weakness.
Even after years of hardship, his spirit remained unbowed.
The tree stood unbowed through the strong winds of the storm.

Synset Examples

sit straight
stood defiantly with unbowed back

Related Terms

  • Synonym: upright - vertical; not bending or slumping
  • Antonym: stooped - bent forward posture

Etymology

From un- (not) + bowed (past participle of bow, to bend).
