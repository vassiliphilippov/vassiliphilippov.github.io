Whitsun

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪt.sən / (AmE), / ˈwɪt.sən / (BrE)
The week beginning with Whitsunday, especially referring to the Christian festival commemorating the descent of the Holy Spirit at Pentecost.
the week of Whitsunday in Christianity

Examples

Many towns in England hold special Whitsun celebrations and parades.
Schools traditionally closed for a week during Whitsun in the United Kingdom.
The village fair was scheduled for the first day of Whitsun each year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pentecost - Christian festival commemorating the descent of the Holy Spirit

Etymology

From Middle English 'Whitson', alteration of 'Whitsunday', from 'white Sunday', possibly referring to the white garments worn by newly baptized Christians.
