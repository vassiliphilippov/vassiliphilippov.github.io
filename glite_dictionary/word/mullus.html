Mullus

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌləs / (AmE), / ˈmʌləs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Mullidae, comprising certain species of marine fish known as goatfishes.
type genus of goatfishes

Examples

The Mediterranean Mullus is prized for its delicate flavor.
Many species within the genus Mullus are characterized by their barbels.
Fishermen in the region often catch Mullus for local markets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goatfish - a marine fish of the family Mullidae

Etymology

From Latin *mullus* (red mullet), referring to a type of fish admired by the Romans.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmʌləs / (AmE), / ˈmʌləs / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish in the family Mullidae, known as goatfishes, characterized by barbels on the chin and found in warm seas.
genus of marine goatfishes

Examples

The Mediterranean red mullet belongs to the genus Mullus.
Scientists study species in the Mullus genus to understand goatfish evolution.
Several colorful fish in the aquarium are members of Mullus.

Etymology

From Latin *mullus* (a kind of fish).
