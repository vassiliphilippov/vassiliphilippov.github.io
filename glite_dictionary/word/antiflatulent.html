antiflatulent

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.tiˈflætʃ.u.lənt / (AmE), / ˌæn.tiˈflætjʊ.lənt / (BrE)
a substance that reduces or prevents the formation of intestinal gas
agent reducing intestinal gas
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended an antiflatulent to help ease her digestive discomfort.
Simethicone is commonly used as an antiflatulent in over-the-counter medications.
After eating beans, he took an antiflatulent to reduce bloating.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carminative - a substance that relieves flatulence

Etymology

From anti- (against) + flatulent (producing gas), from Latin 'flatus' (blowing, breath).
