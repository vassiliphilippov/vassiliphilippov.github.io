to tantalize

1. VERB

/ ˈtæn.təˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈtan.tə.laɪz / (BrE)
to harass or tease someone persistently with criticism or cutting remarks
harass or tease persistently with criticism

Examples

His coworkers would tantalize him about his meticulous habits.
The fans would tantalize the rival team with constant jeers from the stands.
She was often tantalized by her classmates for speaking up so much in class.

Synset Examples

The children teased the new teacher
Don't ride me so hard over my failure
His fellow workers razzed him when he wore a jacket and tie

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tease - to make fun of or attempt to provoke in a playful or unkind way
  • Synonym: torment - to cause severe mental or physical suffering
  • Antonym: to encourage - to give support, confidence, or hope

Etymology

From Latin Tantalus, a mythological figure who was eternally tormented.
