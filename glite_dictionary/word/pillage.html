pillage

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
goods or money taken by force, especially during war; plunder
goods taken by force or theft

Examples

The invading army carried off a vast pillage after sacking the city.
The museum's collection included artifacts believed to be pillage from wartime looting.
He boasted about the pillage he had acquired during his years as a mercenary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plunder - goods taken by force, especially in war

Etymology

From Old French 'pillage' (act of plundering), from 'piller' (to plunder).

2. VERB

/ ˈpɪlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
to steal goods using force, especially during war or civil disorder
steal goods by force, especially in war

Examples

The invading army began to pillage the town, taking anything of value they could find.
After the storm, some people tried to pillage the abandoned shops.
During the conflict, soldiers were accused of pillaging the local villages and taking their food and supplies.

Synset Examples

During the earthquake people looted the stores that were deserted by their owners

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plunder - to steal goods, typically using force and in a time of disorder
  • Synonym: loot - to steal things from houses or stores during a war or disaster
  • Antonym: to protect - to keep safe from harm or injury

Etymology

From Old French *piller* (to plunder), of uncertain origin.

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɪlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
the act of stealing goods, especially during war or violent unrest; plundering or looting
the act of plundering or looting

Examples

The invading army's pillage left the town in ruins.
Centuries of pillage have stripped the museum of many treasures.
After the revolt, the city suffered extensive pillage and destruction.

Synset Examples

the plundering of the Parthenon
his plundering of the great authors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plunder - the act of stealing goods, especially during war
  • Synonym: loot - goods or money obtained illegally or by force, or the act thereof

Etymology

From Old French *pillage* (action of plundering), from *piller* (to plunder).
