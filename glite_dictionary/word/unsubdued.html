unsubdued

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.səbˈduːd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.səbˈdjuːd / (BrE)
not brought under control or restrained; still wild or undisciplined.
not controlled; still unruly or wild

Examples

After hours of effort, the wild cat remained unsubdued by the rescuers.
Her unsubdued spirit made her stand out in the face of adversity.
The unrest in the region was still unsubdued despite government efforts.

Synset Examples

the horse remained unsubdued

Related Terms

  • Antonym: subdued - brought under control; quieted or restrained

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (not) + 'subdued' (from Latin 'subduco' meaning to subdue).
