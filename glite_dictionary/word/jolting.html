jolting

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒoʊltɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒltɪŋ / (BrE)
causing sudden, rough movements or shocks; characterized by abrupt irregular motion.
causing sudden, rough movements

Examples

The jolting train ride made it hard to keep my coffee steady.
A jolting pain shot through his leg as he stumbled over the uneven ground.
She braced herself for the jolting descent during the turbulent flight.

Synset Examples

a rough ride

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shaking - causing to move rapidly back and forth or up and down
  • Antonym: smooth - free from roughness or abrupt movements

Etymology

From the verb 'jolt' (to shake abruptly), with the adjective-forming suffix '-ing'.
