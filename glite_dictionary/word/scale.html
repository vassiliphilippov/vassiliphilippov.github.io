scale

1. NOUN

/ skeɪl / (AmE), / skeɪl / (BrE)
An instrument or device having a graduated sequence of marks used for measuring or indicating quantities.
measuring instrument with marked divisions

Examples

The thermometer has a scale marked in both Celsius and Fahrenheit.
Adjust the dial until the pointer is on the correct number on the scale.
A new color-coded scale was added to improve accuracy in reading measurements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gauge - an instrument for measuring or indicating a quantity

From Latin scala (ladder, staircase) via Old French escale, referring to something with steps or increments.
