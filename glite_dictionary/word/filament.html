filament

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪləmənt / (AmE), / ˈfɪləmənt / (BrE)
the slender stalk of a stamen in a flower, supporting the anther
slender stalk of a stamen

Examples

The filament supports the anther at the top of the stamen.
Each stamen consists of a filament and an anther where pollen is produced.
The length of the filament affects how the flower disperses pollen.

Etymology

From Latin 'filamentum', from 'filum' meaning 'thread'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɪləmənt / (AmE), / ˈfɪləmənt / (BrE)
a slender, threadlike structure, as found in biological cells or fibers
threadlike structure or fiber

Examples

Under the microscope, a single filament of algae appeared as a chain of tiny cells.
The light bulb's tungsten filament glowed brightly when switched on.
Biologists studied each filament to understand how the cells functioned together.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fiber - a thread or filament from which a vegetable tissue, mineral substance, or textile is formed

Etymology

From Latin filamentum (a thread), from filum (thread).

3. NOUN

/ ˈfɪləmənt / (AmE), / ˈfɪləmənt / (BrE)
a very slender thread or fiber, especially one that is natural or synthetic in origin
slender fiber or thread

Examples

A single filament of silk can be incredibly strong for its size.
Thick ropes are often made of many smaller filaments twisted together.
Synthetic filaments are widely used in the production of textiles and plastics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fiber - a thread or filament from which a vegetable tissue, mineral substance, or textile is formed

Etymology

From Latin 'filamentum', from 'filum' (thread).

4. NOUN

/ ˈfɪləmənt / (AmE), / ˈfɪləmənt / (BrE)
a thin wire, often made of tungsten, that is heated to produce light in electric bulbs and similar devices
thin wire in a light bulb

Examples

The tungsten filament inside the bulb glowed brightly when the lamp was switched on.
If the filament breaks, the light bulb will no longer work.
Early light bulbs used a carbon filament instead of tungsten.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wire - a thin, flexible strand of metal
  • Synonym: bulb - a glass enclosure containing a filament emitting light

Etymology

From Latin 'filamentum', from 'filum' meaning 'thread'.
