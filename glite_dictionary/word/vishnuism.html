Vishnuism

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪʃnuːˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈvɪʃnjuːˌɪzəm / (BrE)
the worship or religious tradition centered on Vishnu, one of the three principal gods of Hinduism
worship focused on the Hindu god Vishnu

Examples

Modern Vishnuism has influenced the development of many Hindu festivals.
Scholars have written extensively on the philosophical aspects of Vishnuism.
The spread of Vishnuism in South India led to the construction of grand temples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Vaishnavism - the sect of Hinduism that worships Vishnu as the supreme deity

Etymology

Formed from 'Vishnu', the name of the Hindu god, plus the suffix '-ism' denoting a religious movement or system.
