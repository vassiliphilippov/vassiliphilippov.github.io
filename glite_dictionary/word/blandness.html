blandness

1. NOUN

/ ˈblænd.nəs / (AmE), / ˈblænd.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of showing no emotion, embarrassment, or concern in a situation where some is expected
lack of emotion or concern

Examples

The blandness of her response left everyone confused about her true feelings.
Despite the difficult questions, he maintained a remarkable blandness throughout the interview.
The blandness of his confession enraged the judge.

Synset Examples

the blandness of his confession enraged the judge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impassivity - lack of emotion or expression
  • Antonym: emotion - a strong feeling deriving from one's circumstances, mood, or relationships

Etymology

From bland + -ness, where 'bland' comes from Latin blandus (smooth, mild, flattering).

2. NOUN

/ ˈblænd.nəs / (AmE), / ˈblænd.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being pleasantly smooth, unremarkable, or ingratiating in manner or appearance
the quality of being bland or ingratiating

Examples

His blandness made him likable at first, but over time, people found him insincere.
The diplomat's blandness allowed her to ease tensions during the heated negotiations.
Critics accused the presenter of blandness, saying his remarks lacked originality or passion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bland - pleasantly smooth or unremarkable in manner, taste, or appearance
  • Synonym: insipidity - lack of interesting or distinctive qualities; dullness

Etymology

From Latin 'blandus' (smooth, mild, flattering) via Old French and Middle English.

3. NOUN

/ ˈblænd.nəs / (AmE), / ˈblænd.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of lacking strong or interesting flavor, taste, or character
lack of strong taste or character

Examples

The blandness of the soup left everyone unimpressed.
Many critics noted the blandness of the restaurant's signature dish.
She complained about the blandness of the food served at the hospital.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insipidity - lack of flavor or interest
  • Antonym: flavor - distinctive taste of food or drink

Etymology

From 'bland' + '-ness', where 'bland' comes from Latin blandus (smooth, mild, flattering).
