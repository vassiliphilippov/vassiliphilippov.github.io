silver lining

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvɚ ˈlaɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə ˈlaɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
a hopeful or positive aspect that emerges from a difficult or unpleasant situation
positive aspect in a difficult situation

Examples

Even though he lost his job, the silver lining was that he found a better one soon after.
Every difficult period in life usually has a silver lining if you look hard enough.
After the storm, the community found a silver lining in the way neighbors came together to help each other.

Synset Examples

every cloud has a silver lining
look on the bright side of it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bright side - the positive or hopeful aspect of something

Etymology

From the phrase "every cloud has a silver lining," with the image of bright edges surrounding dark clouds.
