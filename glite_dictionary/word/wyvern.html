wyvern

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪvərn / (AmE), / ˈwɪvən / (BrE)
A mythical two-legged, winged dragon, often depicted in medieval heraldry with a barbed tail.
mythical dragon with two legs and wings

Examples

The family crest displayed a golden wyvern with its wings spread wide.
Legends say the wyvern could poison its enemies with its barbed tail.
The festival's main attraction was a giant sculpture of a fiery wyvern perched on the castle walls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dragon - A large, mythical, fire-breathing reptile, usually with wings and four legs

Etymology

From Middle English wyver (viper, serpent), from Old French wivre, from Latin vipera (viper, snake).
