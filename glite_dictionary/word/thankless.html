thankless

1. ADJ

/ ˈθæŋk.ləs / (AmE), / ˈθæŋk.ləs / (BrE)
not feeling or showing gratitude; ungrateful
not showing gratitude; ungrateful

Examples

Despite her hard work, her efforts went completely thankless.
His thankless attitude made it difficult for others to help him.
Raising teenagers can sometimes feel like a thankless task.

Synset Examples

ungrateful heirs
How sharper than a serpent's tooth it is / To have a thankless child!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ungrateful - not showing gratitude or thanks
  • Antonym: grateful - feeling or showing thanks

Etymology

Formed from 'thank' + '-less' (without); '-less' from Old English 'lēas', meaning 'without'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈθæŋkləs / (AmE), / ˈθæŋkləs / (BrE)
(of a task or activity) unlikely to be appreciated or rewarded; not giving satisfaction or recognition.
unlikely to be appreciated or rewarded

Examples

She knew that volunteering in the shelters would be a thankless job, but she did it anyway.
Cleaning up after the party was a thankless task that nobody wanted to do.
Many managers feel that enforcing rules can sometimes be a thankless responsibility.

Synset Examples

grading papers is a thankless task

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rewarding - providing satisfaction or benefit
  • Antonym: appreciated - valued or recognized with gratitude

Etymology

From Middle English thancles, from Old English þanclēas (thankless, ungrateful).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02023359-s
Synset POS: s
