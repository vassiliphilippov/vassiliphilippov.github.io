Julian

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒuːliən / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːliən / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of Julius Caesar, especially used in reference to the Julian calendar.
relating to Julius Caesar (esp. calendar)

Examples

The Julian calendar was introduced by Julius Caesar in 45 BC.
According to Julian dates, the year begins on January 1.
Some Orthodox Christian churches still follow the Julian calendar for religious holidays.

Synset Examples

the Julian calendar

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Gregorian - relating to Pope Gregory XIII (esp. calendar)

Etymology

From Latin Iulianus, from Iulius (Julius).
