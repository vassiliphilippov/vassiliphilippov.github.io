exigency

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛk.sə.dʒən.si / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɪdʒ.ən.si / (BrE)
an urgent situation or emergency that requires immediate action
urgent situation; emergency

Examples

The government responded quickly to the exigency created by the natural disaster.
Medical teams must be prepared to act in any exigency that arises during surgery.
During times of war, nations often pass laws to meet the exigency of the moment.

Synset Examples

he never knew what to do in an emergency

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emergency - a serious, unexpected situation needing immediate action
  • Synonym: crisis - a time of intense difficulty or danger

Etymology

From Latin exigentia, from exigere (to demand).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛksɪdʒənsi / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɪdʒənsi / (BrE)
an urgent need or demand; a pressing or critical situation that requires immediate action
urgent need or demand

Examples

Due to the exigency of the situation, the team worked throughout the night to complete the project.
The government acted quickly in response to the exigency caused by the flood.
In times of war, leaders must be prepared for any exigency that arises.

Synset Examples

the health-care exigency

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emergency - a serious, unexpected, and often dangerous situation requiring immediate action
  • Synonym: crisis - a time of intense difficulty or danger
  • Antonym: luxury - a state of great comfort or extravagant living, the opposite of emergency or urgent need

Etymology

From Latin exigentia (pressure, necessity), from exigere (to demand, enforce).

3. NOUN

/ ˈɛksədʒənsi / (AmE), / ˈɛksɪdʒənsi / (BrE)
a need or demand that must be dealt with in a particular situation, often in urgent or difficult circumstances
urgent need or demand

Examples

The general had to make quick decisions to meet the exigencies of battle.
Hospitals must be prepared for the exigencies of a sudden health crisis.
The project changed direction several times in response to the exigencies of funding and resources.

Synset Examples

The trench maps used in the Great War resulted from the exigencies of war.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emergency - a serious, unexpected, and often dangerous situation requiring immediate action
  • Synonym: necessity - something that is needed or required

Etymology

From Latin exigentia (urgency), from exigere (to demand).
