Apis mellifera scutellata

1. NOUN

/ ˈæpɪs məˈlɪfərə ˌskjuːtɪˈleɪtə / (AmE), / ˈæpɪs mɛˈlɪfərə ˌskjuːtɪˈleɪtə / (BrE)
a subspecies of honey bee native to Africa and known for being more aggressive than other honey bees; also called the Africanized honey bee when crossbred in the Americas
African subspecies of honey bee

Examples

Apis mellifera scutellata colonies are known for their defensive behavior, making them challenging to manage for beekeepers.
Researchers studied the spread of Apis mellifera scutellata across South America and into southern states in the U.S.
The temperament of Apis mellifera scutellata distinguishes it from other honey bee subspecies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Africanized bee - a hybrid of African and European honey bees, known for aggressive behavior
  • Degree Lower: honey bee - a common bee species known for producing honey

Etymology

From Latin: Apis (bee), mellifera (honey-bearing), scutellata (with a small shield).
