pedicab

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛdɪˌkæb / (AmE), / ˈpiːdɪkæb / (BrE)
a small three-wheeled vehicle, typically propelled by pedalling, used for carrying passengers for hire
three-wheeled passenger vehicle powered by pedalling

Examples

The tourists enjoyed a ride through the city in a pedicab.
He decided to take a pedicab instead of walking to the market.
Many cities have introduced pedicabs as an eco-friendly transport option.

Synset Examples

boys who once pulled rickshaws now pedal pedicabs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rickshaw - a two-wheeled passenger vehicle for hire, usually pulled by one person

Etymology

Blend of 'pedal' and 'cab' (short for taxicab).
