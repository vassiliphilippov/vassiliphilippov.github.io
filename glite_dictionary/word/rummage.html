rummage

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌmɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈrʌmɪdʒ / (BrE)
a collection of miscellaneous items, especially things to be given away or sold at a rummage sale
assorted items to be given away or sold

Examples

We gathered all the rummage for the neighborhood charity sale.
She dropped off a box of old rummage at the church.
The garage was full of rummage waiting to be sorted and donated.

Etymology

From Middle French *rommage* (arrangement of cargo), later used in English nautical slang.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14524403-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈrʌmɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈrʌmɪdʒ / (BrE)
a thorough search for something, especially one that causes disorder or confusion
thorough search, often disorderly

Examples

After a quick rummage through her bag, she found her keys.
He had a rummage in the garage to look for old photographs.
A rummage of the bookshelf turned up an interesting novel.

Synset Examples

he gave the attic a good rummage but couldn't find his skis

Related Terms

  • Synonym: search - an act of looking carefully for something

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *rommelen* (to rumble, make a noise), related to searching through things noisily.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00948622-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈrʌmɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈrʌmɪdʒ / (BrE)
to search through something in a hurried or disorderly way, especially by moving things around
search hurriedly and without order

Examples

She began to rummage through her bag, looking for her keys.
The children rummaged in the attic for old toys.
He rummaged around in the drawer until he found a pen.

Synset Examples

We rummaged through the drawers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: search - to try to find something by looking or otherwise seeking carefully

Etymology

Probably from Middle Dutch *rommelen* (to rumble or make noise).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01321709-v
Synset POS: v
