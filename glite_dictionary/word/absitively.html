absitively

1. ADV

/ ˌæb.sɪˈtɪv.li / (AmE), / ˌæb.sɪˈtɪv.li / (BrE)
used for emphasis to mean 'absolutely; emphatically'; often humorous or informal.
emphatically; absolutely (humorous, informal)
informal

Examples

He absitively refused to change his mind, no matter how much they argued.
I absitively love this idea—there's no doubt about it!
She said she would absitively not attend the party under any circumstances.

Synset Examples

He absitively refused to change his mind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absolutely - completely; totally

Etymology

Humorous alteration of 'absolutely,' probably modeled after other playful alterations like 'posilutely.'
