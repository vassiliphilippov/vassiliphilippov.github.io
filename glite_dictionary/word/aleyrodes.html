Aleyrodes

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɛɪ.əˌroʊdiːz / (AmE), / əˈlɛɪ.ərəʊdiːz / (BrE)
A genus of small whiteflies, serving as the type genus of the Aleyrodidae family.
genus of small whiteflies

Examples

The greenhouse was infested with Aleyrodes, causing damage to many plants.
Researchers are studying Aleyrodes species to understand whitefly behavior.
Several agricultural pests belong to the genus Aleyrodes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Aleyrodidae - the family of whiteflies

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek aleuron (flour), referring to the white powdery wings of these insects.

2. PROPN

/ əˈlɛɪəˌroʊdiːz / (AmE), / əˈlɛɪərəʊdiːz / (BrE)
A genus of whiteflies, serving as the type genus of the family Aleyrodidae.
genus of whiteflies (Aleyrodidae)

Examples

The Aleyrodes genus contains several species that are significant agricultural pests.
Entomologists often study Aleyrodes to understand whitefly behavior and biology.
Some members of Aleyrodes can transmit plant viruses to crops.

Etymology

From Greek 'aleyron' (flour) due to the floury wax coating of the insects.
