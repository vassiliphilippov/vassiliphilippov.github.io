Saul

1. PROPN

/ sɔl / (AmE), / sɔːl / (BrE)
(in the Bible) the first king of Israel, known for defending Israel against its enemies, notably the Philistines.
first king of Israel in the Bible

Examples

The story of Saul is told in the First Book of Samuel in the Bible.
According to tradition, Saul was chosen by God to be the first king of Israel.
Many paintings depict Saul in scenes of battle against the Philistines.

Etymology

From Hebrew Sha'ul meaning 'asked for, prayed for'.

2. PROPN

/ sɔl / (AmE), / sɔːl / (BrE)
(in the New Testament) the original name of Paul the Apostle, a Christian missionary and author of several Epistles.
original name of Paul the Apostle

Examples

Before his conversion, the Apostle Paul was known as Saul.
Saul became a prominent figure in the early Christian church after his dramatic encounter on the road to Damascus.
Many biblical scholars study the transformation of Saul into Paul as a pivotal moment in Christian history.

Synset Examples

Paul's name was Saul prior to his conversion to Christianity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Paul - Christian missionary and Apostle, author of several New Testament Epistles

Etymology

From Hebrew Sha'ul meaning 'asked for' or 'prayed for'.
