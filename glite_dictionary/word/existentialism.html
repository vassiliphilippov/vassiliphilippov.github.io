existentialism

1. NOUN

/ ɛɡˌzɪstənˈʃɪzəm / (AmE), / ɛɡˌzɪstənˈʃɪlɪzəm / (BrE)
A philosophical movement, especially in 20th-century Europe, which asserts that individuals are free and responsible for their own development through acts of the will.
20th-century philosophy on individual freedom and responsibility

Examples

Many modern novels are heavily influenced by existentialism and its focus on individual freedom.
She wrote her dissertation on the key ideas of existentialism in French philosophy.
Debates about meaning and personal choice often reference the principles of existentialism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absurdism - A philosophical perspective related to existentialism, focusing on the inherent meaninglessness of life
  • Degree Lower: nihilism - Belief that life is without objective meaning, purpose, or intrinsic value
  • Antonym: humanism - Belief in the value and agency of human beings, often contrasted with existentialism

Etymology

Formed from French 'existentialisme', from 'existence' + '-isme' (ism).
