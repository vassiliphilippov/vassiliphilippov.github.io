shrug

1. VERB

/ ʃrʌɡ / (AmE), / ʃrʌɡ / (BrE)
to raise and lower one's shoulders slightly to show that one does not know something or does not care
raise shoulders to show indifference or uncertainty

Examples

When asked about the loss, he simply shrugged and walked away.
She shrugged her shoulders, unsure of how to respond to the question.
Despite criticism, the manager just shrugged and continued with the plan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shrug off - dismiss something as unimportant

Etymology

Probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian *skrugga* (to draw up the shoulders).

2. NOUN

/ ʃrʌɡ / (AmE), / ʃrʌɡ / (BrE)
An act of raising and lowering the shoulders to express uncertainty, indifference, or lack of knowledge.
shoulder gesture showing indifference or uncertainty

Examples

With a careless shrug, he turned away from the question.
She answered my request with only a shrug and a smile.
A simple shrug told me he didn't know the answer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrug (verb) - to raise and lower the shoulders to indicate doubt or indifference
