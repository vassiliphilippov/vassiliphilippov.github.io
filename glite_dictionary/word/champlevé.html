champlevé

1. ADJ

/ ʃæmˈlɛv / (AmE), / ʃɑ̃ːˈlɛveɪ / (BrE)
(of metalwork) having areas that are separated by metal ridges and filled with colored enamel, which is then fired to fuse the enamel.
enamel technique with metal ridges and colored inlays

Examples

The museum displayed a beautiful champlevé enamel plaque from the twelfth century.
She studied the champlevé technique to create colorful patterns on her jewelry pieces.
The artist used champlevé decoration to enhance the surface of the bronze sculpture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cloisonné - decorative work using metal strips to separate enamel areas

Etymology

From French 'champlevé', literally 'raised field' (champ 'field' + levé 'raised').
