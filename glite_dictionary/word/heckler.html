heckler

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛklər / (AmE), / ˈhɛklə / (BrE)
a person who interrupts a public speaker or performer with derisive or challenging comments, questions, or jokes
person who interrupts with jeers or challenges

Examples

The comedian handled the heckler in the audience with sharp wit.
A heckler kept shouting during the politician's speech, trying to disrupt the event.
Performers often have to deal with at least one heckler at live shows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interrupter - someone who interrupts another person
  • Antonym: supporter - someone who supports or encourages a speaker

Etymology

From 'heckle', originally meaning to comb flax with a heckle (a comb), later evolving to mean questioning public speakers.
