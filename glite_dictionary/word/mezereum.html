mezereum

1. NOUN

/ məˈzɪriəm / (AmE), / məˈzɪəriəm / (BrE)
the dried bark of the mezereon shrub, formerly used in herbal medicine as a remedy for various ailments
dried bark of mezereon shrub (medicinal)
medical

Examples

The herbalist recommended mezereum as a natural treatment for skin conditions.
Historical records mention the use of mezereum to alleviate joint pain.
Although mezereum was once widely used, it is now considered dangerous due to its toxicity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mezereon - a small Eurasian shrub with poisonous red berries and fragrant pink flowers

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'mezereum', from Arabic 'mazariyun', meaning the mezereon plant.
