Sphaeralcea remota

1. NOUN

/ sfɪˈrælsiə rɪˈmoʊtə / (AmE), / sfɪˈralsɪə rɪˈməʊtə / (BrE)
a rare species of mallow native to Illinois, resembling hollyhock and bearing pale rose-mauve flowers
rare mallow species native to Illinois

Examples

The rare Sphaeralcea remota blooms in late spring, brightening the Illinois prairies with its rose-mauve flowers.
Botanists are working to preserve habitats suitable for Sphaeralcea remota to ensure its continued survival.
Unlike its common relatives, Sphaeralcea remota is found only in specific areas of Illinois.

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Sphaeralcea' (from Greek 'sphaira' (sphere) and 'alkēa', variant of 'malakhe', mallow), and species epithet 'remota' (Latin for 'remote' or 'distant').
