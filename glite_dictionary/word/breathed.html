breathed

1. ADJ

/ briːðt / (AmE), / briːðt / (BrE)
spoken very softly or uttered without voice; barely audible or whispered
spoken very softly; whispered

Examples

She spoke a breathed apology that barely reached his ears.
The room was silent except for her breathed plea for help.
In the darkness, a breathed warning was hardly audible.

Synset Examples

could hardly hear her breathed plea, ‘Help me’
voiceless whispers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whispered - spoken in a quiet, soft voice
  • Antonym: voiced - spoken aloud with vocal sound

Etymology

From the verb 'breathe', from Old English 'brǣþ' (odor, exhalation).
