Dalea

1. NOUN

/ dəˈliːə / (AmE), / dəˈliːə / (BrE)
A genus of herbs and shrubs in the pea family, commonly known as indigo bush, native to the Americas.
genus of plants (indigo bush)

Examples

The Dalea blooms in late spring, producing striking purple flowers.
Botanists are studying how Dalea species adapt to arid environments.
Many pollinators are attracted to the nectar of Dalea plants.

Etymology

Named after Samuel Dale (1659–1739), English botanist and physician.

2. NOUN

/ dəˈliːə / (AmE), / dəˈliːə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the pea family (Fabaceae), commonly known as indigo bush, native to the Americas.
genus of flowering plants (indigo bush)

Examples

The Dalea species thrive in arid regions of North and South America.
Many gardeners appreciate the vibrant purple flowers of Dalea plants.
Some Dalea varieties are cultivated for their ability to attract pollinators.

Etymology

Named after English botanist Samuel Dale (1659–1739).
