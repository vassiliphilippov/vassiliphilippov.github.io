Gertrude Stein

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡɝːtruːd staɪn / (AmE), / ˈɡɜːtruːd staɪn / (BrE)
(1874–1946) An American writer known for her experimental literary style and for serving as a central figure in Parisian modernist circles.
American writer and art patron

Examples

Gertrude Stein was known for her avant-garde writing and her influential literary salon in Paris.
Many credit Gertrude Stein with mentoring artists like Hemingway and Picasso during the early twentieth century.
The poetry of Gertrude Stein often challenges traditional ideas of language and meaning.
