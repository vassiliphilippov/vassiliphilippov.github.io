behavior

1. NOUN

/ bɪˈheɪvjɚ / (AmE), / bɪˈheɪvjə / (BrE)
the way in which a person acts, especially towards others or in a particular situation; the manner of conducting oneself
way of acting or conducting oneself

Examples

The teacher praised the student's behavior during the assembly.
Good behavior is expected from all employees at work.
His behavior changed noticeably after the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conduct - the manner in which a person behaves, especially on a particular occasion or in a particular context
  • Antonym: misbehavior - bad or improper behavior

Etymology

From Old French 'behaviour', from Anglo-Norman 'behaivour', from 'behave'.

2. NOUN

/ bɪˈheɪvjər / (AmE), / bɪˈheɪvjə / (BrE)
The actions, responses, or movements made by an organism, especially as observed in psychology.
the actions or responses of an organism

Examples

The psychologist studied the behavior of rats in the maze.
Her child's unusual behavior at school concerned the teacher.
Researchers measured the behavior of the monkeys after introducing a new toy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conduct - manner in which a person behaves
  • Antonym: misbehavior - bad or inappropriate behavior

Etymology

From Latin 'habitus' via Old French 'behaviour', meaning manner of conducting oneself.

3. NOUN

/ bɪˈheɪvjɚ / (AmE), / bɪˈheɪviə / (BrE)
the manner in which an object, substance, or system acts or responds under particular conditions, especially as observed in scientific or technical studies
manner of response of objects or substances

Examples

Engineers analyzed the behavior of the metal at high temperatures to ensure safety.
The behavior of this chemical changes when exposed to sunlight.
Understanding the behavior of electrons is crucial in quantum physics.

4. NOUN

/ bɪˈheɪvjɚ / (AmE), / bɪˈheɪvjə / (BrE)
the way in which a person acts or conducts themselves, especially toward others
way a person acts toward others

Examples

His polite behavior at the party impressed everyone.
The teacher praised the student's respectful behavior toward her classmates.
She was rewarded for her good behavior during the school trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conduct - the manner in which a person behaves, especially in a particular place or situation
  • Synonym: demeanor - outward behavior or bearing

Etymology

From Latin 'habere' (to have, to hold) via Old French 'behaviour'.
