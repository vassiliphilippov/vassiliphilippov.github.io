fawn-coloured

1. ADJ

/ fɔnˈkʌlɚd / (AmE), / fɔːnˈkʌləd / (BrE)
of a pale yellowish-brown color, resembling that of a young deer (fawn)
pale yellowish-brown in color
british

Examples

She wore a fawn-coloured coat that matched her boots perfectly.
The walls were painted a soft fawn-coloured hue to create a cozy atmosphere.
A fawn-coloured Labrador dozed peacefully beside the fireplace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fawn-colored - American spelling of fawn-coloured

Etymology

From 'fawn' (a young deer) + 'coloured', after the typical coat color of a fawn.
