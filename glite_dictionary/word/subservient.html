subservient

1. ADJ

/ səbˈsɜːr.vi.ənt / (AmE), / səbˈsɜː.vi.ənt / (BrE)
serving or acting as a means or aid; useful in achieving a purpose
serving as a means or aid

Examples

Her careful planning was subservient to the project’s eventual success.
The tool was merely subservient to his larger goals.
Education is often seen as subservient to economic development.

Synset Examples

instrumental in solving the crime

Related Terms

  • Synonym: instrumental - serving as a means or agent in achieving something

Etymology

From Latin 'subservient-', from 'subservire' (to serve under).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01200318-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ səbˈsɜr.vi.ənt / (AmE), / səbˈsɜː.vi.ənt / (BrE)
too willing to obey other people or serve their needs; excessively obedient or compliant to authority.
excessively obedient to authority

Examples

He disliked working under managers who preferred subservient employees to independent thinkers.
Journalists must avoid being subservient to government interests if they want to remain credible.
She adopted a subservient manner in hopes of pleasing her superiors.

Synset Examples

editors and journalists who express opinions in print that are opposed to the interests of the rich are dismissed and replaced by subservient ones

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obedient - willing to obey
  • Antonym: defiant - openly resisting authority or control

Etymology

From Latin subservientem, present participle of subservīre 'to serve under'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02338056-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ səbˈsɝː.vi.ənt / (AmE), / səbˈsɜː.vi.ənt / (BrE)
excessively willing to obey others; having or showing an eagerness to serve or obey, often to the point of being slavish or servile.
excessively obedient or servile

Examples

Her subservient attitude made it easy for her manager to take advantage of her.
He refused to play the subservient role any longer in the relationship.
The servant delivered his message in a subservient tone, bowing deeply as he left.

Synset Examples

slavish devotion to her job ruled her life
a slavish yes-man to the party bosses
she has become submissive and subservient

Related Terms

  • Synonym: servile - having or showing an excessive willingness to serve or please others
  • Synonym: slavish - showing no originality; blindly imitative or submissive
  • Antonym: assertive - confident and self-assured; not submissive

Etymology

From Latin 'subservientem', present participle of 'subservire' (to serve under).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00794246-s
Synset POS: s
