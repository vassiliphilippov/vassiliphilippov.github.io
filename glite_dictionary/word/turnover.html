turnover

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːnˌoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɜːnˌəʊvə / (BrE)
A baked or fried dish made by enclosing a filling, such as fruit or meat, in a piece of pastry that is folded over.
pastry folded over a filling

Examples

She bought an apple turnover from the bakery for breakfast.
The cafe serves a delicious spinach and cheese turnover at lunch.
Hot cherry turnovers were cooling on the kitchen windowsill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pastie - a small pie with a folded pastry crust and filling, especially in British English
  • Synonym: empanada - a baked or fried turnover consisting of pastry and filling
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07640075-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːnˌoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɜːnˌəʊvə / (BrE)
the rate at which employees leave a workforce and are replaced during a particular period of time
rate at which employees are replaced

Examples

The company's high turnover rate made it difficult to maintain consistent quality.
Reducing employee turnover is a key goal for many human resources departments.
Seasonal jobs often have a naturally high turnover compared to permanent positions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attrition - gradual reduction of work force by employees leaving and not being replaced
  • Antonym: retention - the ability to keep employees in an organization

Etymology

From the phrase 'turn over', referring to the 'turning over' or replacement of staff members.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13848434-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːnˌoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɜːnəʊvə / (BrE)
the amount of business done, measured by the total value of goods or services sold during a particular period
total sales value in a period

Examples

The company's turnover reached a record high last quarter.
Small businesses often struggle to maintain a steady turnover in their early years.
The annual turnover is an important indicator of a firm's growth.

Synset Examples

the store's dollar volume continues to rise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revenue - total income generated from sales before expenses are deducted
  • Antonym: profit - financial gain, the difference between revenue and expenses

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'turn over', meaning to transact or do business; in use since 17th century in commercial contexts.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05119608-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːnˌoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɜːnˌəʊvə / (BrE)
the act of overturning or upsetting something, especially so that it is upside down or on its side
act of overturning something

Examples

The canoe experienced a turnover when it hit the rapids, tossing everyone into the water.
Unexpected wind during the race caused a turnover of several small boats.
Wearing a helmet is crucial in case of a sled turnover on icy hills.

Synset Examples

he was badly bruised by the upset of his sled at a high speed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overturn - to turn something upside down or cause it to capsize

Etymology

From the verbal phrase 'turn over'.
