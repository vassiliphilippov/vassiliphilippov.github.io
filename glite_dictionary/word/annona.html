Annona

1. NOUN

/ əˈnoʊnə / (AmE), / əˈnəʊnə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American trees and shrubs in the family Annonaceae, including species such as cherimoya and soursop.
genus of tropical American trees/shrubs

Examples

The genus Annona includes several important tropical fruit trees such as the soursop and the cherimoya.
Botanists are studying the medicinal properties of different Annona species.
Fruit from Annona trees is widely consumed across Central and South America.

Etymology

From Latin annōna (fruit or produce), adopted as a genus name by Linnaeus.

2. NOUN

/ əˈnoʊnə / (AmE), / əˈnəʊnə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American trees and shrubs in the family Annonaceae, including the custard apple and related species.
tropical American plant genus (Annonaceae)

Examples

The fruit of some Annona species, such as the cherimoya, is highly prized for its flavor.
Botanists discovered a new species within the genus Annona in the Amazon rainforest.
Several Annona trees are cultivated in tropical regions for their edible fruits.

Etymology

From Latin *Annōna*, genus name given by Carl Linnaeus.
