logistics

1. NOUN

/ ləˈdʒɪstɪks / (AmE), / ləˈdʒɪstɪks / (BrE)
the detailed organization and implementation of a complex operation, especially involving the movement and supply of people and materials
organization of supplying and moving resources

Examples

The company improved its logistics to ensure faster delivery times to customers.
Coordinating the logistics for the international conference required months of careful planning.
Shipping vaccines to remote areas presents unique logistics challenges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supply chain - the sequence of processes involved in the production and distribution of a commodity
  • Synonym: distribution - the action of sharing something out among recipients
  • Synonym: planning - the process of making plans for something

Etymology

From French 'logistique', from Greek 'logistikē' (art of calculation); modern sense developed in military usage.
