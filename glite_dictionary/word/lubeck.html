Lubeck

1. PROPN

/ ˈluː.bɛk / (AmE), / ˈluː.bɛk / (BrE)
a city in northwestern Germany, known for its historic old town and its former role as a leading port of the Hanseatic League
historic port city in northwestern Germany

Examples

The medieval architecture of Lubeck attracts tourists from around the world.
During the Middle Ages, Lubeck was a prominent center of trade in Northern Europe.
She visited Lubeck to explore its famous Brick Gothic buildings and maritime museums.

Etymology

From German Lübeck, named after the Slavonic tribe of the Liubice.
