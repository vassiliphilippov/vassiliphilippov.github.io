palmate

1. ADJ

/ ˈpæl.meɪt / (AmE), / ˈpæl.meɪt / (BrE)
(of a leaf) having lobes or leaflets that spread out from a single point, like the fingers of a hand.
(of a leaf) leaflets radiating from a point

Examples

The horse chestnut tree has palmate leaves with five to seven spreading leaflets.
Some species of maple display striking palmate leaf patterns.
Her botany textbook included a diagram of a palmate leaf next to a pinnate one.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pinnate - (of a leaf) with leaflets arranged on either side of a central axis

Etymology

From Latin palma (palm of the hand).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpæl.meɪt / (AmE), / ˈpæl.meɪt / (BrE)
(of the feet of water birds) having three toes connected by a thin fold of skin.
having webbed toes (in birds)

Examples

Many ducks have palmate feet that help them swim efficiently in water.
The biologist described the bird’s palmate toes as an adaptation for an aquatic lifestyle.
Gulls are among the birds with palmate feet, which aid in propulsion while swimming.

Etymology

From Latin 'palmatus' (shaped like an open hand, webbed).
