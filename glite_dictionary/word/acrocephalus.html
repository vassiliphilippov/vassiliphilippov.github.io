Acrocephalus

1. NOUN

/ ˌækroʊˈsɛfələs / (AmE), / ˌakrə(ʊ)ˈsɛfələs / (BrE)
a genus of small, chiefly Old World songbirds in the family Sylviidae, commonly known as marsh warblers and reed warblers
genus of warblers (marsh and reed warblers)

Examples

The Acrocephalus genus includes many species found in European wetlands.
Researchers studied the migratory patterns of Acrocephalus warblers in Asia.
Several Acrocephalus species are threatened by habitat loss.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, from Greek akros (highest) + kephalē (head).

2. NOUN

/ ˌækrəˈsɛfələs / (AmE), / ˌakrə(ʊ)ˈsɛfələs / (BrE)
A genus of small, mainly Old World birds in the family Sylviidae, known as marsh warblers and reed warblers.
genus of marsh and reed warblers

Examples

The Acrocephalus genus includes a variety of marsh warbler species found in Europe and Asia.
Scientists studied the migration patterns of the Acrocephalus birds along the riverbanks.
Several members of the Acrocephalus genus are threatened due to habitat loss.

Etymology

From Greek 'akros' (highest) + 'kephalē' (head), referring to the shape of the bird's head.
