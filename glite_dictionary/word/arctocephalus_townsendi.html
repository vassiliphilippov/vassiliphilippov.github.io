Arctocephalus townsendi

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑrktoʊˈsɛfələs ˈtaʊnzəndˌaɪ / (AmE), / ˌɑːktəʊˈsiːfələs ˈtaʊnzəndˌiː / (BrE)
A species of fur seal found along the Pacific coast of California and farther south, known for its thick fur.
species of fur seal of California coast

Examples

The endangered Arctocephalus townsendi is under strict protection along parts of the Baja California coastline.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of Arctocephalus townsendi to understand how climate change affects marine mammals.
A colony of Arctocephalus townsendi can often be seen resting on rocky shores during breeding season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fur seal - a seal of the Otariidae family noted for its dense fur

Etymology

From Greek 'arktos' (bear) + 'kephalē' (head) and named after Charles Haskins Townsend, American zoologist.
