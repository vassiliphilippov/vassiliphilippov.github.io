soman

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈsəʊˌmæn / (BrE)
a highly toxic nerve agent that inhibits cholinesterase and is easily absorbed into the body, causing severe harm when inhaled or ingested
a toxic nerve agent used in chemical warfare

Examples

Exposure to soman can be fatal within minutes if proper protective gear is not worn.
The military developed special detectors to identify soman on the battlefield.
Scientists study the effects of soman to improve treatments for nerve agent poisoning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sarin - a highly toxic nerve agent similar to soman
  • Synonym: tabun - another toxic nerve agent used in chemical warfare

Etymology

From German Soman, coined in Germany in the 1940s; possibly from German 'sofort' (immediate) and 'Mann' (man), referring to its rapid action.
