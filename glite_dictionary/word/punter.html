punter

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌntər / (AmE), / ˈpʌntə / (BrE)
a person who propels a flat-bottomed boat with a pole, especially on rivers or shallow water
someone who poles a boat

Examples

The punter skillfully guided the boat down the narrow river channels.
During the tour, the punter explained the history of each landmark they passed.
Some tourists tried their hand at being a punter, finding it harder than it looked.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boatman - a person who works on or operates a boat

Etymology

From 'punt' (flat-bottomed boat) + '-er' (agent suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpʌn.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈpʌn.tə / (BrE)
a person who places bets, especially on horse races or sporting events
a person who places bets
british

Examples

At the racetrack, the seasoned punter studied the odds before placing his bet.
Many punters were disappointed when the favorite horse lost the race.
The online casino attracts thousands of new punters every month.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bettor - a person who bets or makes wagers
  • Synonym: gambler - someone who takes risky chances, especially by betting money

Etymology

Originally British slang; probably from 'punt', to gamble, of uncertain origin.

WordNet ID: oewn-09871284-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈpʌnɚ / (AmE), / ˈpʌntə / (BrE)
a player in football who drops the ball from their hands and kicks it before it touches the ground, usually to clear it downfield
football player who punts the ball

Examples

The team's punter had an amazing game, averaging over fifty yards per kick.
As a rookie punter, he quickly earned the coach's trust with his consistent performance.
The punter lined up and booted the ball deep into the opponent's territory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kicker - a football player who kicks the ball during kickoffs, field goals, or extra points

Etymology

From the verb 'punt' (to kick the ball before it touches the ground) + -er (agent noun suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10512784-n
Synset POS: n
