Lynx pardina

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪŋks pɑrˈdiːnə / (AmE), / ˈlɪŋks pɑːˈdiːnə / (BrE)
A rare wild cat species native to the Iberian Peninsula, also known as the Iberian lynx, characterized by its spotted coat and short tail.
rare wild cat of the Iberian Peninsula

Examples

Conservationists are working tirelessly to protect the dwindling population of Lynx pardina in Spain.
The Lynx pardina is distinguished from other lynx species by its distinctive spotted fur.
Captive breeding programs have helped increase the number of Lynx pardina in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Iberian lynx - another name for Lynx pardina

Etymology

From Latin; 'lynx' refers to the wild cat genus, 'pardina' from 'pardus' (leopard), referencing its spotted coat.
