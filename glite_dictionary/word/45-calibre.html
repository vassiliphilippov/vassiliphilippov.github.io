.45-calibre

1. ADJ

/ pɔɪnt fɔrti faɪv ˈkælɪbər / (AmE), / pɔɪnt fɔːti faɪv ˈkælɪbə / (BrE)
of or relating to the bore of a firearm or its ammunition that measures forty-five hundredths of an inch in diameter.
measuring .45 inches in bore diameter

Examples

The detective carried a .45-calibre revolver during the investigation.
He purchased a box of .45-calibre ammunition from the store.
The museum displays several historic .45-calibre handguns used in the early 20th century.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: .38-calibre - of or relating to a gun bore or ammunition measuring thirty-eight hundredths of an inch in diameter
  • Degree Higher: .50-calibre - of or relating to a gun bore or ammunition measuring fifty hundredths of an inch in diameter

Etymology

From the number 0.45 (forty-five hundredths) denoting diameter in inches + 'calibre', from French calibre.
