vomitive

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɑːm.ɪ.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈvɒm.ɪ.tɪv / (BrE)
a medicine or substance that causes nausea and induces vomiting
medicine that induces vomiting
medical

Examples

The doctor administered a vomitive to counteract the effects of the poison.
After ingesting the berries, a vomitive was given to induce vomiting quickly.
Some ancient remedies included the use of a vomitive when poisoning was suspected.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emetic - a medicine that induces vomiting

Etymology

From Latin vomitivus (causing vomiting), from vomitare (to vomit).
