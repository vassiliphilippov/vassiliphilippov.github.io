experimentation

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˌspɛrəmən'teɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˌspɛrɪmɛnˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of performing controlled tests or investigations to discover, demonstrate, or test a hypothesis or theory
the process of conducting experiments

Examples

Scientific progress often relies on careful experimentation to validate new ideas.
Her approach to cooking involves a lot of experimentation with flavors and techniques.
The process of experimentation is essential in both science and technology development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: experiment - a scientific procedure undertaken to make a discovery or test a hypothesis

Etymology

From experiment + -ation, modeled after Latin-based formations.

2. NOUN

/ ɪkˌspɛrəmənˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˌspɛrɪmɛnˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of trying out new ideas, methods, or activities to discover what effect they have or whether they are successful
the process of testing ideas or methods

Examples

Her approach to art is characterized by constant experimentation with new materials and styles.
The success of the project required some experimentation to find the best solution.
Educational reform often involves experimentation with alternative teaching methods.

Synset Examples

it was an experiment in living
not all experimentation is done in laboratories

Related Terms

  • Synonym: experiment - a test carried out to discover something unknown

Etymology

From experiment + -ation, based on Latin experimentum (a trial, test).
