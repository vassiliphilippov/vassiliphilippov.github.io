pimple

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪm.pəl / (AmE), / ˈpɪm.pəl / (BrE)
a small, swollen, red spot on the skin, often filled with pus, caused by inflammation or infection of a pore
a small inflamed skin spot, often with pus

Examples

She was embarrassed by the pimple that appeared on her nose before the party.
Teenagers often get pimples as a result of hormonal changes.
He tried not to touch the pimple on his chin to avoid making it worse.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: acne - a skin condition causing pimples

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *pimpe* (a small swelling or lump), of uncertain origin.
