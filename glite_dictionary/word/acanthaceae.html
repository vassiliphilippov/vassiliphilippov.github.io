Acanthaceae

1. NOUN

/ əˌkænˈθeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / əˌkanˈθeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a large family of mostly tropical herbs, shrubs, and trees, known commonly as the acanthus family, with characteristic bracted flowers
family of tropical flowering plants
medical

Examples

Many species in the Acanthaceae family are valued as ornamental garden plants.
The Acanthaceae exhibit a remarkable diversity of flower shapes and leaf forms.
Researchers studied various medicinal uses of plants from the Acanthaceae family.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'akantha' (thorn or spine), referring to spiny bracts in some species, and the standard botanical family suffix '-aceae'.

2. NOUN

/ əˌkænθəˈsiː.iː / (AmE), / əˌkanθəˈsiːiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants including widely distributed herbs, shrubs, and trees, often with spiny leaves or stems, sometimes placed in the order Scrophulariales
a family of flowering plants (herbs, shrubs, or trees)

Examples

The Acanthaceae family is known for its ornamental flowers and includes many tropical species.
Several members of the Acanthaceae are used in traditional herbal medicine.
Botanists have debated the proper classification of certain plants within the Acanthaceae.

Etymology

From the type genus Acanthus, itself from Greek akanthos (thorned plant).
