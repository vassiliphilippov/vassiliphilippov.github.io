inequity

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈnɛkwəti / (AmE), / ɪˈnɛkwɪti / (BrE)
lack of fairness; injustice or unfair treatment
unfairness; injustice

Examples

The school system is working to address educational inequity across different neighborhoods.
There are long-standing inequities in healthcare access between rural and urban communities.
Combating social inequity is a major goal of many non-profit organizations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inequality - state of not being equal, especially in status, rights, or opportunities
  • Antonym: justice - fairness; the quality of being just

Etymology

From Latin 'inaequitas' (unevenness, unfairness), from 'in-' (not) + 'aequitas' (evenness, fairness).
