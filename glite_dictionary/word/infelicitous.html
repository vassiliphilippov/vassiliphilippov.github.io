infelicitous

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.fəˈlɪ.sɪ.təs / (AmE), / ˌɪn.fəˈlɪ.sɪ.təs / (BrE)
causing or marked by unhappiness; unfortunate or inappropriate in a particular situation
unfortunate; causing unhappiness

Examples

The timing of his comment was infelicitous and made everyone uncomfortable.
An infelicitous remark can turn a friendly conversation awkward.
They met under infelicitous circumstances, which set a difficult tone for their relationship.

Synset Examples

infelicitous circumstances
unhappy caravans, straggling afoot through swamps and canebrakes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unfortunate - unlucky or having bad consequences
  • Antonym: happy - showing or experiencing pleasure and contentment

Etymology

From Latin 'infelix, infelicis' meaning 'unfortunate' + English suffix '-ous'.

Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˌfəˈlɪsɪtəs / (AmE), / ɪnˌfɛlɪˈsɪtəs / (BrE)
not suitable, appropriate, or well chosen; awkward or unfortunate in effect.
not appropriate or well chosen

Examples

Her choice of words was rather infelicitous at the formal dinner.
The design suffered from several infelicitous features that confused users.
His infelicitous timing made the joke fall flat with the audience.

Synset Examples

an infelicitous remark
infelicitous phrasing
the infelicitous typesetting was due to illegible copy

Related Terms

  • Antonym: felicitous - well chosen or suited to the circumstances
  • Synonym: inappropriate - not suitable or proper in the circumstances

Etymology

From Latin 'infelix' (unfortunate, unhappy), patterned after 'felicitous.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01004300-a
Synset POS: a
