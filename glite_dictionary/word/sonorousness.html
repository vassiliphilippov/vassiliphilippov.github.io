sonorousness

1. NOUN

/ səˈnɔr.əs.nəs / (AmE), / səˈnɒr.əs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of having a deep, full, and resonant sound
the quality of being resonant or sonorous

Examples

The sonorousness of the church bells echoed throughout the village.
Critics praised the sonorousness of her baritone voice in the performance.
The sonorousness of the old wooden floor added character to the grand hall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resonance - the quality of being deep, full, and reverberating in sound

Etymology

From Latin sonor (sound) + -ous (full of) + -ness (quality).
