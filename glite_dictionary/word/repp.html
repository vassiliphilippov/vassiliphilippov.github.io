repp

1. NOUN

/ rɛp / (AmE), / rɛp / (BrE)
A fabric, typically silk, wool, or cotton, characterized by prominent rounded crosswise ribs.
fabric with crosswise ribs

Examples

She wore a tie made of repp with subtle blue and red stripes.
The designer chose repp fabric for the upholstery to add texture to the chairs.
Classic repp silk is often used for high-quality neckties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rep - ribbed fabric, alternate spelling

Etymology

From French 'reps,' of uncertain origin.
