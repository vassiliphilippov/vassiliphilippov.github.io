Arcellidae

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈsɛlɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ɑːˈsɛlɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of single-celled protozoa found in soil and freshwater, distributed worldwide.
family of soil and freshwater protozoa

Examples

Scientists discovered several new species of Arcellidae in samples from the lake.
Arcellidae play an important role in soil ecosystems by recycling nutrients.
Some members of Arcellidae can be indicators of environmental change in freshwater habitats.

Etymology

From New Latin Arcellidae, based on the genus Arcella, with the suffix -idae used for animal families.
