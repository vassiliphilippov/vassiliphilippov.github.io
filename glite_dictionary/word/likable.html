likable

1. ADJ

/ ˈlaɪkəbəl / (AmE), / ˈlaɪkəbl / (BrE)
pleasant and easy to like; agreeable or attractive in a way that evokes affection or approval.
pleasant and easy to like

Examples

She has a likable personality that makes everyone feel at ease.
The team's new coach is very likable and quickly won the players' respect.
Despite his flaws, there's something genuinely likable about him.

Synset Examples

an attractive and likable young man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agreeable - pleasant and easy to like
  • Antonym: unlikable - unpleasant and difficult to like

Etymology

From 'like' + '-able', meaning fit to be liked.

2. ADJ

/ ˈlaɪkəbəl / (AmE), / ˈlaɪkəbəl / (BrE)
(of a person or character) pleasant and easy to like; evoking friendly or sympathetic feelings.
pleasant and easy to like

Examples

Despite his flaws, the main character remains very likable throughout the novel.
Her friendly attitude makes her one of the most likable people in the office.
The film succeeds largely because the cast is so likable and relatable.

Synset Examples

the sympathetic characters in the play

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pleasant - giving a sense of happy satisfaction or enjoyment
  • Antonym: unlikable - not pleasant or easy to like

Etymology

From like + -able, meaning 'able to be liked.'
