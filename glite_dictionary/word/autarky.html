autarky

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːˌtɑrki / (AmE), / ˈɔːˌtɑːki / (BrE)
A national policy or condition of economic independence, in which a country aims to be self-sufficient and limits reliance on imports.
policy of economic self-sufficiency

Examples

The country adopted autarky to reduce its reliance on foreign imports.
During wartime, some nations strive for autarky to ensure survival.
Critics argue that autarky can lead to economic inefficiency and isolation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-sufficiency - the quality or condition of being self-sufficient
  • Antonym: dependence - the state of relying on or being controlled by someone or something else

Etymology

From Greek autarkeia (self-sufficiency), from autos (self) + arkein (to suffice).
