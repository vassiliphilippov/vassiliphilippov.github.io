hardback

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrdˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdˌbæk / (BrE)
a book with stiff, rigid covers, typically made of cardboard covered with cloth, paper, or leather, rather than paper covers
a book with a hard cover

Examples

She prefers hardback editions because they are more durable than paperbacks.
The library ordered several hardback copies of the new novel for its collection.
Collectors often seek out first editions in hardback rather than paperback.

Synset Examples

hardback books

Related Terms

  • Antonym: paperback - a book with a flexible paper cover

Etymology

From 'hard' + 'back', referring to the rigid backing or cover of the book.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrdˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdˌbæk / (BrE)
A book bound with stiff covers made of cardboard, cloth, or leather, rather than paperback.
book with rigid covers

Examples

She prefers to buy novels in hardback because they are more durable.
The library received a new hardback edition of the classic.
I lent him my signed hardback, so please take good care of it.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: paperback - a book bound in flexible paper covers
