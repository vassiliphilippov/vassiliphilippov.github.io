cairned

1. ADJ

/ kɛrnd / (AmE), / kɛːnd / (BrE)
(of a path, summit, or location) marked by one or more cairns.
marked by cairns

Examples

The cairned trail was easy to follow through the rocky terrain.
Hikers reached a cairned summit just before sunset.
A series of cairned ridges guided us safely across the mountain.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uncairned - not marked by cairns

Etymology

From 'cairn', meaning a pile of stones used as a marker, plus the adjective-forming suffix '-ed'.
