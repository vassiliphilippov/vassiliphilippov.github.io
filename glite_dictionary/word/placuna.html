Placuna

1. NOUN

/ pləˈkuːnə / (AmE), / pləˈkjuːnə / (BrE)
A genus of marine bivalve mollusks commonly known as windowpane oysters, valued for their translucent shells.
genus of windowpane oysters

Examples

The translucent shells of Placuna are often used in decorative crafts and windowpanes.
Researchers collected several Placuna specimens during their marine survey in the Philippines.
Unlike other bivalves, Placuna shells are prized for their pearly luster and unique appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: windowpane oyster - a common name for oysters of the genus Placuna, known for translucent shells

Etymology

From New Latin 'Placuna', the genus name assigned in scientific classification.
