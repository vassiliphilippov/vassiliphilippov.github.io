pentamethylenetetrazol

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛntəˌmɛθəˌlaɪliˈnɛtərəˌzɑl / (AmE), / ˌpɛntəˌmɛθɪliːniˈtɛtrəzɒl / (BrE)
a drug formerly used as a circulatory and respiratory stimulant and in shock therapy; in larger doses, it induces convulsions
drug used as stimulant and in shock therapy
medical

Examples

Researchers once used pentamethylenetetrazol to stimulate the central nervous system in certain patients.
The use of pentamethylenetetrazol has declined in modern medicine due to its severe side effects.
During the procedure, a small dose of pentamethylenetetrazol was administered to observe its convulsive action.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metrazol - trademarked name for pentamethylenetetrazol

Etymology

From 'penta-' (five), 'methylene' (–CH2– group), and 'tetrazole' (a ring with four nitrogen atoms).
