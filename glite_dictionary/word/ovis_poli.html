Ovis poli

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊ.vɪs ˈpoʊ.laɪ / (AmE), / ˌəʊ.vɪs ˈpəʊ.laɪ / (BrE)
a species of Asiatic wild sheep with exceptionally large horns, sometimes considered a variety of the argali (Ovis ammon)
species of Asiatic wild sheep

Examples

The Ovis poli is renowned for its impressive, spiraled horns.
Scientists trekked into the Pamir Mountains to observe Ovis poli in its natural habitat.
Many believe that Ovis poli deserves full species status, separate from the argali.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: argali - a large wild sheep of central Asia (Ovis ammon)

Etymology

From genus name *Ovis* (Latin for sheep) and specific epithet *poli*, after Marco Polo, who described it.
