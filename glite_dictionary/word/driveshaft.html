driveshaft

1. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪvˌʃæft / (AmE), / ˈdraɪvʃɑːft / (BrE)
A rotating shaft that transmits mechanical power from an engine to the point where it is needed, such as the wheels of a vehicle.
rotating power-transmitting shaft in machinery or vehicles

Examples

The mechanic replaced the broken driveshaft to restore the car's ability to move.
A damaged driveshaft can cause vibrations and unusual noises while driving.
Most rear-wheel-drive vehicles use a long driveshaft to connect the transmission to the rear axle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propeller shaft - shaft transmitting power to the propeller or wheels

Etymology

Formed from 'drive' + 'shaft,' referring to its function of transmitting drive power.
