Radyera farragei

1. NOUN

/ ˌræd.iˈɛr.ə fəˈrædʒ.aɪ / (AmE), / ˌræd.iˈɛə.rə fəˈrædʒ.iː / (BrE)
A shrub native to southern and western Australia, with unlobed or shallowly lobed toothed leaves and purple flowers; sometimes classified in the genus Hibiscus.
Australian shrub with purple flowers

Examples

The Radyera farragei is known for its attractive purple flowers and distinctive leaves.
Botanists sometimes debate whether Radyera farragei should be placed in the genus Hibiscus.
During our trip to Western Australia, we spotted several Radyera farragei shrubs growing wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hibiscus - a genus of flowering plants including hibiscus shrubs and trees
