crowning

1. ADJ

/ ˈkraʊnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkraʊnɪŋ / (BrE)
representing the highest or most significant achievement; culminating or supreme
highest or most significant achievement

Examples

Winning the Nobel Prize was the crowning achievement of her scientific career.
The statue at the top was the crowning feature of the monument.
Their victory was the crowning moment of the tournament.

Synset Examples

the crowning accomplishment of his career

Related Terms

  • Synonym: culminating - reaching the highest or most important point
  • Synonym: ultimate - being the best or most extreme example

Etymology

From the verb 'crown' meaning 'to honor as the best or final' + -ing.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkraʊnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkraʊnɪŋ / (BrE)
forming or providing a crown or highest point; representing the most important or impressive part of something
forming a crown; most important or final

Examples

The statue was the crowning feature of the historic building.
Winning the award was the crowning moment of his career.
A cherry was the crowning touch on the cake.

Synset Examples

the crowning star on a Christmas tree
her hair was her crowning glory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: culminating - reaching the highest point or final stage
  • Synonym: crowning achievement - someone's greatest or most important achievement

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'crown', from Old French 'coroner', from Latin 'corona' (crown).

Source: WordNet
