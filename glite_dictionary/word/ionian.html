Ionian

1. ADJ

/ aɪˈoʊniən / (AmE), / aɪˈəʊniən / (BrE)
relating to the ancient Ionians of Attica and neighboring territories, their Ionic dialect of Greek, or their culture.
of or relating to the ancient Ionians or their dialect/culture

Examples

The Ionian Greeks played a significant role in the development of classical philosophy.
She studied the Ionian dialect when reading ancient Greek poetry.
Artifacts from the Ionian period are displayed in the museum's Greek antiquities section.

Etymology

From Latin Ionianus, from Ancient Greek Ἰών (Iṓn), name of the legendary ancestor of the Ionians.

2. NOUN

/ aɪˈoʊniən / (AmE), / aɪˈəʊniən / (BrE)
a member of an ancient Greek people who lived in Attica and the region of Ionia, noted for their distinctive dialect and culture
ancient Greek inhabitant of Attica or Ionia

Examples

The ancient Ionians played a crucial role in the development of Greek philosophy.
Many Ionian cities flourished along the coast of Asia Minor.
Her research focuses on the traditions and customs of the Ionian people.

Etymology

From Latin 'Ionianus', from Greek 'Iōnios', pertaining to Ionia or the Ionians.

3. NOUN

/ aɪˈoʊniən / (AmE), / aɪˈəʊniən / (BrE)
a member of an ancient Greek people originating from regions around the central west coast of Asia Minor and certain Aegean islands, known for speaking the Ionic dialect
member of ancient Greek Ionian people

Examples

The ancient Ionians played a crucial role in the spread of Greek culture across the Aegean.
Many philosophers of the classical era were of Ionian descent.
Excavations revealed artifacts belonging to a once-flourishing Ionian settlement.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Dorian - member of another ancient Greek people, traditionally rivals of the Ionians
  • Synonym: Ionic - relating to the Ionic dialect spoken by the Ionians

Etymology

From Latin Ionianus, from Ancient Greek Ἰωνικός (Iōnikós, pertaining to the Ionians).
