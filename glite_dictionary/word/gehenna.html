Gehenna

1. NOUN

/ gəˈhɛnə / (AmE), / gəˈhɛnə / (BrE)
(in Jewish and Christian belief) a place or state where the wicked are punished after death.
place of punishment after death (theology)

Examples

Some ancient texts describe Gehenna as a place of eternal fire for the damned.
Preachers often warned sinners of the torments awaiting them in Gehenna.
The concept of Gehenna has influenced many depictions of hell in Western art and literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hell - the place where, in some religions, the souls of evil people are punished after death

Etymology

From Hebrew 'Gehinnom', meaning 'Valley of Hinnom', a valley outside ancient Jerusalem associated with the punishment of the wicked.
