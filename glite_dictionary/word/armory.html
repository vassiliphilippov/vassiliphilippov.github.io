armory

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrməri / (AmE), / ˈɑːməri / (BrE)
a place where weapons and ammunition are manufactured or stored
place where arms are made or kept

Examples

The old armory was converted into a museum displaying historical weapons.
Guards were posted around the armory to protect the newly manufactured rifles.
The city’s armory played a crucial role during the war by supplying weapons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arsenal - a place where weapons and military equipment are stored or made

Etymology

From Old French *armorie*, from Latin *armarium* (closet, chest for arms).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrməri / (AmE), / ˈɑːməri / (BrE)
A place where weapons, ammunition, and military equipment are stored, and where training in the use of arms is provided.
building for storing weapons and military training

Examples

The soldiers gathered at the armory before heading out on their assignment.
Each town maintained a well-stocked armory for emergencies and training exercises.
The new recruits spent hours every week at the armory learning to handle firearms safely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arsenal - a place where weapons and military equipment are stored or made

Etymology

From Old French *armorie*, from Medieval Latin *armaria* (place for arms).

3. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrməri / (AmE), / ˈɑːməri / (BrE)
a collection of resources or skills that are available for use, especially to help achieve something
collection of resources or skills

Examples

She drew upon her vast armory of negotiation skills to resolve the dispute.
A good leader possesses an armory of strategies to handle different situations.
Writers often dip into their armory of experiences when creating new stories.

Synset Examples

he dipped into his intellectual armory to find an answer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arsenal - a collection of resources, weapons, or tools available for use

Etymology

From Old French armorie, from Latin armarium (a chest for arms), from arma (arms, weapons).

4. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrməri / (AmE), / ˈɑːməri / (BrE)
all the weapons and military equipment that a country possesses
a country's weapons and equipment

Examples

The nation's armory has been expanded with advanced missile systems.
A well-equipped armory is crucial for ensuring national security.
The treaty requires both countries to reduce their nuclear armory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arsenal - a collection or supply of weapons or military equipment

Etymology

From Old French armoirie, from arme (weapon), ultimately from Latin arma (arms, weapons).
