ectoplasm

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛk.təˌplæz.əm / (AmE), / ˈɛk.təʊˌplæz.əm / (BrE)
(In spiritualism) a substance believed to emanate from the body of a medium during a spiritualist trance, said to form materializations of spirits.
spiritualist substance from medium's body

Examples

During the séance, the medium claimed that ectoplasm was flowing from her hands.
Many skeptics regard ectoplasm as a trick rather than genuine paranormal activity.
The appearance of ectoplasm was once considered proof of spirit communication.

Etymology

From Greek ektos (outside) + plasma (formed or molded substance).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛk.təˌplæzəm / (AmE), / ˈɛk.təʊˌplazəm / (BrE)
the outer, clear, and nongranular layer of cytoplasm in a cell, especially in protozoa
outer clear layer of cytoplasm
medical

Examples

The ectoplasm of the amoeba is essential for its movement and shape.
Scientists observed that the ectoplasm remains clear while the endoplasm contains granules.
The ectoplasm forms a protective barrier around the cell.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: endoplasm - the inner, granulated part of cytoplasm

Etymology

From Greek ektos (outside) + plasma (something formed or molded).
