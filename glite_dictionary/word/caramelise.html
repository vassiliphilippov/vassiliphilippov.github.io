to caramelise

1. VERB

/ ˈkærəməˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkærəməlaɪz / (BrE)
to convert (sugar or a food containing sugar) into caramel by heating, often to produce a browned, sweet flavour
convert to caramel by heating

Examples

To develop a rich flavour, gently caramelise the onions over low heat for thirty minutes.
If you caramelise the sugar too quickly, it will taste burnt.
The chef taught us how to caramelise apples for the dessert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to caramelize - spelling variant in American English

Etymology

From French *caraméliser*, from 'caramel'.

2. VERB

/ ˈkærəməˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkærəməlaɪz / (BrE)
(of sugar or food) to be converted into caramel by heating, typically resulting in a brown color and distinctive flavor.
become caramel by heating
british

Examples

As the onions cooked slowly, they began to caramelise and turn golden brown.
The sugar will caramelise if you leave it on the heat too long.
During roasting, the sweet potatoes naturally caramelise, giving them a deep, rich flavor.

Synset Examples

The sugar caramelized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to caramelize - (of sugar or food) to be converted into caramel by heating (American spelling)

Etymology

From 'caramel' (from French caramel) + '-ise', a verb-forming suffix.
