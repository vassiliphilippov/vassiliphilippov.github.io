Copernicia prunifera

1. NOUN

/ koʊˌpɝːˈnɪʃi.ə pruːˈnɪfərə / (AmE), / kəʊˌpɜːˈnɪsi.ə pruːˈnɪfərə / (BrE)
A Brazilian fan palm with an edible root, used as a source of leaf fiber and yellowish wax.
Brazilian fan palm species

Examples

The Copernicia prunifera is cultivated in Brazil for its valuable carnauba wax.
Artisans weave baskets from the tough leaves of the Copernicia prunifera.
Scientists are studying the edible roots of Copernicia prunifera for use in nutrition.

Etymology

From Latin, genus name Copernicia after Nicolaus Copernicus, species prunifera meaning 'bearing plums'.
