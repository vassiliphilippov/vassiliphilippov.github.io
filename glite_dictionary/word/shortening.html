shortening

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɔrtənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːtənɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of making something shorter in length, size, or duration
act of making something shorter

Examples

The tailor recommended shortening the sleeves for a better fit.
After shortening the lecture, the professor finished on time.
We discussed the benefits of shortening the meeting to increase productivity.

Synset Examples

the dress needs shortening

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reduction - the act of making something less or smaller
  • Antonym: extension - the act of making something longer or larger

Etymology

From the verb 'shorten' plus the suffix '-ing'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃɔrt.nɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːt.nɪŋ / (BrE)
Fat, such as butter, lard, or solid vegetable fat, used in baking to make pastry or dough crumbly or flaky.
fat used in baking

Examples

The recipe calls for a cup of shortening to make the cookies tender.
Using shortening instead of butter results in a flakier pie crust.
She measured out the shortening and mixed it with the flour for the dough.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butter - a yellow fat spread made from milk or cream
  • Synonym: lard - fat from the abdomen of a pig, used in cooking and baking
  • Synonym: margarine - a butter substitute made from vegetable oils or animal fats

Etymology

From the verb 'shorten', meaning to make crumbly, reflecting its effect on baked goods.
