pellucid

1. ADJ

/ pəˈluːsɪd / (AmE), / pəˈluːsɪd / (BrE)
(of language or style) clear and easy to understand; transparently lucid.
very clear in expression or style

Examples

Her pellucid explanations made complex theories accessible to everyone in the audience.
The author was praised for her pellucid prose, which left little room for confusion.
In academic writing, a pellucid style is often more effective than flowery language.

Synset Examples

writes in a limpid style
lucid directions
a luculent oration
pellucid prose
a crystal clear explanation
a perspicuous argument

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lucid - clear and easy to understand (especially of speech or writing)
  • Synonym: limpid - (of language or writing) clear and easily understandable
  • Antonym: obscure - not clearly expressed or easily understood

Etymology

From Latin pellucidus, from per- (through) + lucidus (clear).

2. ADJ

/ pəˈluːsɪd / (AmE), / pɛˈljuːsɪd / (BrE)
(especially of a liquid or material) transparently clear; allowing light to pass through so that objects behind can be distinctly seen.
transparently clear; allowing light through

Examples

Sunlight poured through the pellucid waters of the mountain stream, revealing the smooth stones beneath.
The vase was crafted from pellucid glass, allowing every detail inside to be seen.
She admired the pellucid sky, a flawless blue expanse stretching overhead.

Synset Examples

the cold crystalline water of melted snow
crystal clear skies
could see the sand on the bottom of the limpid pool
lucid air
a pellucid brook
transparent crystal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transparent - allowing light to pass through so that objects behind can be seen clearly
  • Antonym: opaque - not able to be seen through; not transparent
  • Degree Lower: translucent - allowing light to pass through diffusely

Etymology

From Latin pellucidus, from per- (through) + lucidus (clear).
