to espy

1. VERB

/ ɪˈspaɪ / (AmE), / ɪˈspaɪ / (BrE)
to catch sight of, especially something only partly visible or at a distance
catch sight of; notice

Examples

He managed to espy his friends in the crowd just before the concert started.
From the hilltop, she could espy the distant towers of the city.
As the fog lifted, the sailors espied land on the horizon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spot - to see or notice someone or something, often quickly or briefly
  • Antonym: overlook - to fail to notice something

Etymology

From Old French *espier* (to watch, observe), from Frankish *spehōn* (to spy); related to 'spy'.
