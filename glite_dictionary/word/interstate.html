interstate

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪntərˌsteɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪntəsteɪt / (BrE)
A major highway that is part of the system linking major cities across the contiguous United States.
a major U.S. highway system road
american

Examples

We spent most of the day driving along the interstate from New York to Chicago.
Accidents can cause major delays on the interstate during holiday weekends.
Many rest stops are located at convenient points on the interstate for travelers.

Etymology

Formed from 'inter-' meaning 'between' and 'state', relating to travel between states.

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪntərˈsteɪt / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈsteɪt / (BrE)
Involving or relating to mutual relations between states, especially within a single country such as the United States.
between or involving different states
american

Examples

The interstate highway system transformed long-distance travel across the country.
There are new regulations governing interstate trade within the United States.
The interstate agreement improved cooperation between state governments.

Synset Examples

Interstate Highway Commission
interstate highways
Interstate Commerce Commission
interstate commerce

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intrastate - existing or occurring within a single state

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'inter-' (between) and 'state', first popularized in the late 19th to early 20th century in the United States.
