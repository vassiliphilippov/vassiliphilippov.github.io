to originate

1. VERB

/ əˈrɪdʒəˌneɪt / (AmE), / əˈrɪdʒɪneɪt / (BrE)
to cause something to begin; to bring something into existence
to bring into existence

Examples

The new company plans to originate several innovative projects next year.
Many popular dances originate in urban communities before spreading nationwide.
He helped originate the idea of community-supported agriculture.

Synset Examples

He initiated a new program
Start a foundation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: initiate - to start or set in motion
  • Synonym: to create - to bring something into existence
  • Antonym: to destroy - to put an end to the existence of something

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'originatus', past participle of 'originari' (to rise, originate), from Latin 'origo' (beginning, source).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01631641-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ əˈrɪdʒ.ə.neɪt / (AmE), / əˈrɪdʒ.ɪ.neɪt / (BrE)
to come into existence or begin; to have a specified beginning
to begin; to come into existence

Examples

The tradition is said to originate from ancient harvest festivals.
Many modern diseases originate in animals before spreading to humans.
The rumor originated on social media and quickly spread throughout the town.

Synset Examples

A new religious movement originated in that country
a love that sprang up from friendship
the idea for the book grew out of a short story
An interesting phenomenon uprose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to begin - to start; to come into being or operation
  • Synonym: to arise - to come into being; to emerge
  • Antonym: end - to stop or cease to exist; to finish

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'originatus', past participle of 'orīginārī' (to arise, originate), from Latin 'origo' (beginning, origin).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02630344-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ əˈrɪdʒəˌneɪt / (AmE), / əˈrɪdʒɪneɪt / (BrE)
(of a service or vehicle, especially a train, bus, or plane) to begin its journey at a specified place.
start a journey from a place (vehicles, transport)

Examples

This train originates in Chicago and continues all the way to Los Angeles.
The 7:00 a.m. flight originates at Heathrow Airport.
All buses on this route originate from the downtown terminal.

Synset Examples

The flight originates in Calcutta

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to depart - to leave, especially when starting a journey

Etymology

From Latin 'originare' (to begin, to rise).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02749218-v
Synset POS: v
