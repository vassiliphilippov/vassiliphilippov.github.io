morphological

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɔr.fəˈlɑ.dʒɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌmɔː.fəˈlɒ.dʒɪ.kəl / (BrE)
relating to the structure, formation, or study of words and their parts in a language, especially how words are formed from morphemes
relating to word structure or formation

Examples

The linguist conducted a morphological analysis of the language's verb forms.
English uses various morphological processes to create new words from existing ones.
Her research focuses on morphological changes in borrowed vocabulary.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: syntactic - relating to syntax, the arrangement of words and phrases in sentences

Etymology

From Greek morphē (form, shape) + -logia (study, discourse) via New Latin morphological.

Source: WordNet

2. ADJ

/ ˌmɔːrfəˈlɑːdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌmɔːfəˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the form and structure of rocks, landforms, or the earth's surface, especially as studied in geology or geomorphology
relating to geological structure or form

Examples

The Black Hills region is known for its unique morphological features and rich mineral deposits.
Geologists studied the morphological characteristics of the granite outcrop to understand its formation.
Tectonic activity over millions of years produced dramatic morphological changes in the landscape.

Synset Examples

geomorphological features of the Black Hills
morphological features of granite
structural effects of folding and faulting of the earth's surface

Related Terms

  • Synonym: structural - relating to the arrangement or structure of something, especially in geology

Etymology

From Greek morphē (form, shape) + -logia (study of); originally from biological sciences, later adopted in geology.

Source: WordNet

3. ADJ

/ ˌmɔːr.fəˈlɑː.dʒɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌmɔː.fəˈlɒdʒ.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
relating to the form and structure of organisms or their specific features, especially in biology.
relating to structure or form (biology)

Examples

The scientists observed morphological changes in the plant cells under the microscope.
There are clear morphological differences between these two species of birds.
Her research focuses on the morphological similarities of fossils from different regions.

morphological differences

Related Terms

  • Synonym: structural - relating to structure; similar to morphological in some contexts

/ ˌmɔːrfəˈlɑːdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌmɔːfəˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the form, structure, or morphology of plants, animals, or words
concerning form or structure, especially in biology or linguistics

Certain diseases can cause morphological changes in the cells.
The morphological features of the leaf help identify the plant species.
