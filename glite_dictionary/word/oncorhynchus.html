Oncorhynchus

1. NOUN

/ ɑnkəˈraɪŋkəs / (AmE), / ɒŋkəˈrɪŋkəs / (BrE)
A genus of Pacific salmon and trout that includes species such as sockeye, chinook, chum, and coho salmon.
genus of Pacific salmon and trout

Examples

The spawning migration of Oncorhynchus species is critical for the ecosystem.
Researchers studied the life cycle of Oncorhynchus in Alaskan rivers.
Commercial fisheries often rely on several species within the genus Oncorhynchus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Salmo - genus including the Atlantic salmon and trout

Etymology

From Greek onkos (hook) + rhynchos (snout), referring to the hooked jaws of males during spawning.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑŋ.kəˈrɪŋ.kəs / (AmE), / ˌɒŋ.kəˈrɪŋ.kəs / (BrE)
A genus of fish in the salmon family, including several Pacific salmon species such as the sockeye, chinook, chum, and coho salmon.
genus of Pacific salmon species

Examples

Several species within the genus Oncorhynchus migrate from the ocean to freshwater to spawn.
Scientists have studied the life cycles of Oncorhynchus to understand salmon migration patterns.
Both sockeye and chinook salmon belong to the Oncorhynchus genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salmon - a large edible fish of the salmon family, found in the North Atlantic and Pacific

Etymology

From Greek onkos (hook) and rhynchos (snout), referring to the hooked snout of adult males.
