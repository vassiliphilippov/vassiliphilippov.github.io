blunder

1. VERB

/ ˈblʌndər / (AmE), / ˈblʌndə / (BrE)
to make a careless or serious mistake, especially because of confusion or ignorance
make a serious or careless mistake

Examples

He blundered into the wrong meeting room by mistake.
The company blundered when it released a faulty product to the public.
She realized she had blundered only after seeing the disappointed faces around her.

Synset Examples

I blundered during the job interview

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to err - make a mistake or error
  • Synonym: to misjudge - form a wrong opinion or conclusion

Etymology

Probably from Old Norse *blundra* (to shut one's eyes).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02572142-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈblʌndɚ / (AmE), / ˈblʌndə / (BrE)
a careless or embarrassing mistake, often causing embarrassment or problems
an embarrassing mistake

Examples

His speech was ruined by a major blunder in the introduction.
The new employee made a costly blunder during her first week at work.
Calling the wrong name at the ceremony was an unforgettable blunder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: error - a mistake, especially one made by failing to notice something
  • Synonym: gaffe - an unintentional act or remark causing embarrassment
  • Antonym: success - the accomplishment of an aim or purpose, opposite of mistake

Etymology

From Middle English blonder (to stumble), of uncertain origin; perhaps Scandinavian.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00075610-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈblʌndɚ / (AmE), / ˈblʌndə / (BrE)
to say something impulsively and often foolishly, without careful consideration
speak impulsively or foolishly

Examples

Without thinking, she blundered the truth to the entire group.
He sometimes blunders inappropriate remarks during meetings.
During the interview, I blundered details I should have kept private.

Synset Examples

He blurted out the secret
He blundered his stupid ideas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to blurt - to say something suddenly and without thinking

Etymology

From Middle English 'blondren', probably of Scandinavian origin, related to Swedish 'blundra' (to move blindly).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00983976-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈblʌndɚ / (AmE), / ˈblʌndə / (BrE)
to move or progress in a clumsy or confused way, often without clear direction
move clumsily or blindly

Examples

In the pitch-black hallway, he blundered into the furniture.
She blundered through the crowd, looking for the exit.
The lost hiker blundered onward, unsure of which path to take.

Synset Examples

He fumbled towards the door

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stumble - to trip or lose balance while moving

Etymology

From Old Norse 'blundra' (to shut one's eyes).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02055471-v
Synset POS: v
