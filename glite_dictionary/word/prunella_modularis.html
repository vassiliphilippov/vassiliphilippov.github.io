Prunella modularis

1. NOUN

/ pruˈnɛlə ˈmɑdjuˌlɛrɪs / (AmE), / pruˈnɛlə ˌmɒdjʊˈlɑːrɪs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the dunnock, a small brownish European songbird found in gardens, woodlands, and hedges
scientific name for the dunnock bird

Examples

The scientific name for the dunnock is Prunella modularis.
Researchers observed that Prunella modularis prefers dense hedgerows for nesting sites.
Birdwatchers in the UK often spot Prunella modularis in gardens during spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dunnock - a small brown and grey European songbird, also known as the hedge sparrow

Etymology

From Latin; 'Prunella' is the genus name for accentors, and 'modularis' refers to singing or making a melody.
