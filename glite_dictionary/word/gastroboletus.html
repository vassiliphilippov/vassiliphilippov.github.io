Gastroboletus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡæstroʊboʊˈliːtəs / (AmE), / ˌɡæstrəʊbəʊˈliːtəs / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Secotiaceae that resemble boletes but do not discharge spores from the basidium.
genus of fungi in the Secotiaceae family

Examples

The rare Gastroboletus species were discovered during a survey of the old-growth forest.
Researchers are studying the unique spore development of Gastroboletus fungi.
Unlike most boletes, Gastroboletus does not release its spores from the basidium.

Etymology

From Greek 'gastro-' (stomach) and 'boletus' (a type of mushroom).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɡæstroʊboʊˈliːtəs / (AmE), / ˌɡæstrəʊbəʊˈliːtəs / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Secotiaceae that resemble boletes, but whose spores are not discharged from the basidium.
a genus of fungi resembling boletes

Examples

The rare Gastroboletus species was discovered growing under pine trees in the forest.
Researchers have studied the unusual spore release mechanism of Gastroboletus fungi.
Unlike most boletes, Gastroboletus does not discharge its spores from the basidium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Boletus - a genus of mushrooms with pores, related to Gastroboletus

Etymology

From Greek 'gastro-' (stomach) and 'Boletus' (a genus of mushrooms), referring to the stomach-like form and relation to boletes.
