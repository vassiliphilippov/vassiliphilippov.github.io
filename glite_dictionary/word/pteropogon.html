pteropogon

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛrəˈpoʊɡən / (AmE), / ˌtɛrəˈpəʊɡən / (BrE)
A southern Australian plant distinguished by feathery hairs surrounding its fruit, belonging to the genus Pteropogon.
southern Australian plant genus with feathery-fruited species

Examples

The pteropogon thrives in the dry regions of southern Australia.
Scientists studied the unique feathery fruits of the pteropogon for their aerodynamic properties.
A pteropogon can be easily identified by the distinctive hairs surrounding its fruit.

Etymology

From Greek 'pterón' (feather, wing) + 'pōgōn' (beard), likely referring to the feathery hairs of the fruit.
