retro

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɛtroʊ / (AmE), / ˈrɛtrəʊ / (BrE)
(of a law, payment, or policy) taking effect from a date in the past; relating to or affecting things that have already happened.
taking effect from a past date

Examples

She received a retro pay adjustment for the work she did last year.
The company issued a retro bonus after reviewing performance.
A retro policy ensured that employees would be compensated for prior overtime.

Synset Examples

retroactive tax increase
an ex-post-facto law
retro pay

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retroactive - (of a law or decision) taking effect from a date in the past
  • Antonym: prospective - relating to or effective in the future

Etymology

From Latin retro (backward, behind).

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛˌtroʊ / (AmE), / ˈrɛtrəʊ / (BrE)
a style or fashion that imitates or recalls trends from the recent past, typically from the mid-20th century
a style imitating the past

Examples

Her outfit was pure retro, with bell-bottom jeans and a tie-dye shirt.
The restaurant's decor is full of 1950s retro touches.
He's a fan of retro, collecting old vinyl records and classic arcade games.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vintage - classic style, usually from a previous era

Etymology

From French rétro, short for rétrograde, meaning 'backward, retrograde'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05759427-n
Synset POS: n
