Nageia nagi

1. NOUN

/ ˈnædʒi.ə ˈnɑːɡi / (AmE), / ˈnadʒɪə ˈnɑːɡi / (BrE)
A medium-sized evergreen tree with glossy, lance-shaped leaves, native to southern China, Taiwan, and southern Japan.
a tree species of East Asia

Examples

The temple gardens featured several ancient Nageia nagi towering above the shrine.
Botanists studied the leaf structure of Nageia nagi to understand its adaptation to the humid climate.
Traditional medicines in Japan sometimes included extracts from the bark of Nageia nagi.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Nageia' (Neo-Latin), and the Japanese name 'nagi' for the tree.
