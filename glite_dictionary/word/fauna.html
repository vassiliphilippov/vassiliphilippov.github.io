fauna

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔnə / (AmE), / ˈfɔːnə / (BrE)
all the animal life of a particular region, habitat, or geological period
animals of a region or period

Examples

The unique fauna of Madagascar includes many species found nowhere else on Earth.
Scientists are studying the marine fauna of the coral reef to understand environmental change.
Over time, the fauna of the island adapted to its volcanic landscape.

Synset Examples

the fauna of China
the zoology of the Pliocene epoch

Related Terms

  • Antonym: flora - all the plant life in a particular region or period

Etymology

From Latin fauna (animals, collective animal life), after Fauna, a Roman goddess of the earth and fertility.
