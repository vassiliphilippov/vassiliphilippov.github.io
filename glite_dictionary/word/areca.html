areca

1. NOUN

/ əˈrɛkə / (AmE), / əˈriːkə / (BrE)
A tall tropical palm native to southeastern Asia, especially known for producing egg-shaped nuts used in betel chewing.
tropical palm with egg-shaped nuts

Examples

The areca palm is widely cultivated for its nuts, which are commonly chewed with betel leaves.
Farmers in Southeast Asia harvest areca nuts as an important cash crop.
The leaves of the areca palm are also used for making traditional decorations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: betel nut - nut of the areca palm, commonly chewed in Asia

Etymology

From Portuguese *areca*, from Malayalam *adakka*, both referring to the areca nut.
