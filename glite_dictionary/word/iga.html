IgA

1. NOUN

/ aɪ ˌdʒiː ˈeɪ / (AmE), / aɪ ˌdʒiː ˈeɪ / (BrE)
A class of immunoglobulin that is the main antibody present in mucous membranes, especially in the gastrointestinal and respiratory tracts.
a class of immunoglobulin antibody
medical

Examples

Elevated IgA levels may indicate an ongoing immune response in the digestive tract.
Doctors often test for IgA deficiency in patients with recurrent respiratory infections.
Secretory IgA plays a key role in protecting mucosal surfaces from pathogens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immunoglobulin - any of the five major classes of protein antibodies in the immune system

Etymology

Abbreviation of immunoglobulin A.
