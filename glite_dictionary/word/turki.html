Turki

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɜrki / (AmE), / ˈtɜːki / (BrE)
A subfamily of Altaic languages that includes Turkish and other Turkic languages spoken across Central Asia and surrounding regions.
subfamily of Altaic languages

Examples

The Turki languages are spoken by millions across Central Asia.
Researchers are studying the origins of different Turki dialects.
Uzbek and Kazakh are both considered Turki languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Turkic - a branch of the Altaic language family, including Turkish, Uzbek, Kazakh, and others

Etymology

From Turkish 'Türki', meaning 'Turkic' or 'of the Turks'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɜːrki / (AmE), / ˈtɜːki / (BrE)
A member of any of the peoples who speak a Turkic language, especially in Central Asia or historical contexts.
a speaker of a Turkic language

Examples

The ancient Turki migrated across Central Asia over the centuries.
Many Turki still maintain their traditional customs despite modern influences.
Researchers studied the linguistic patterns of different Turki tribes in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Turkic - of or relating to the group of languages spoken in Turkey, Central Asia, and surrounding regions
  • Synonym: Turkish - relating to the people or language of Turkey

Etymology

From Persian ترکى (turkī, 'Turkish').
