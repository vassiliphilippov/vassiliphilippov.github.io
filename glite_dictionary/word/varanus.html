Varanus

1. NOUN

/ vəˈrænəs / (AmE), / vəˈrɑːnəs / (BrE)
a genus of large lizards known as monitor lizards, native to Africa, Asia, and Oceania, and the only living genus in the family Varanidae
genus of monitor lizards

Examples

The Komodo dragon is a member of the genus Varanus and is the largest living lizard.
All species within Varanus are commonly referred to as monitor lizards.
Scientists recently discovered a new Varanus species in the Philippines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monitor lizard - large lizards of the genus Varanus

Etymology

From Latin 'Varanus', from Arabic 'waran' (monitor lizard).

2. NOUN

/ vəˈreɪnəs / (AmE), / vəˈrɑːnəs / (BrE)
A genus of large, carnivorous lizards known as monitor lizards, the only extant genus in the family Varanidae.
genus of monitor lizards

Examples

The Komodo dragon is the largest living species in the genus Varanus.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Varanus genus in the Indonesian islands.
All monitor lizards belong to the genus Varanus, which includes more than 70 species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monitor lizard - Large lizards of the genus Varanus

Etymology

From New Latin, borrowed from Arabic 'waran' or Coptic 'ouran' (lizard).
