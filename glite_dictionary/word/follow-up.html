follow-up

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑːloʊˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈfɒləʊˌʌp / (BrE)
an activity or piece of work that builds upon or continues earlier work.
work continuing previous work

Examples

The study was a follow-up to the research done last year.
We are planning a follow-up to our previous investigation.
Her latest novel is a follow-up to her bestseller.

Synset Examples

his new software is a follow-up to the programs they started with

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sequel - a published, broadcast, or recorded work that continues the story or develops the theme of an earlier one
  • Antonym: prequel - a work that precedes the original work in chronology

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'follow up', meaning to pursue or continue.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɑːloʊ ʌp / (AmE), / ˈfɒləʊ ʌp / (BrE)
An action or activity that continues, monitors, or repeats a previous one to ensure progress or resolution.
continued action after an initial event

Examples

The doctor scheduled a follow-up appointment to check her progress after the surgery.
The study included a six-month follow-up to assess long-term effects.
After the meeting, he sent a follow-up email to summarize the discussion and next steps.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: review - an evaluation or assessment of something after completion
  • Antonym: initial - being the first or beginning; occurring at the start

Etymology

From the verb phrase 'follow up', meaning to pursue or continue, first recorded in the early 20th century.

work building on previous work

Examples
