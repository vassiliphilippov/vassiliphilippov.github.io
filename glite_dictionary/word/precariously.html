precariously

1. ADV

/ prɪˈkɛriəsli / (AmE), / prɪˈkeəriəsli / (BrE)
in a way that is not securely held or in a dangerous or unstable manner
in a dangerous or unstable manner

Examples

The ladder was balanced precariously against the wall, making it unsafe to climb.
She walked precariously along the narrow ledge, afraid she might slip and fall.
The vase sat precariously on the edge of the table, ready to tip at any moment.

Synset Examples

being a precariously dominant minority is a difficult position for human nature to cope with

Related Terms

  • Synonym: precarious - not securely held or in a dangerous situation
  • Antonym: securely - in a stable or safe manner

Etymology

From 'precarious' + '-ly', where 'precarious' comes from Latin 'precarius' (obtained by entreaty, depending on favor).
