overall

1. ADJ

/ ˌoʊvərˈɔl / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərˈɔːl / (BrE)
considering or including all the main aspects or features; general or total.
general; considering as a whole

Examples

The overall pattern of his life became clear after reading his biography.
Her overall impression of the event was positive, despite a few minor issues.
The overall cost of the project exceeded our original budget.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: general - including only main features or aspects
  • Antonym: specific - limited to precise details, not general

3. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvɚˌɔl / (AmE), / ˈəʊvərɔːl / (BrE)
A loose protective garment, such as a coverall or smock, worn over ordinary clothes to keep them clean during work.
protective garment worn over clothes

Examples

The mechanic put on his overalls before starting work on the car.
She wore blue overalls to paint the house and keep her clothes clean.
Factory workers are required to wear overalls and safety helmets at all times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coverall - a one-piece protective outer garment

Etymology

From 'over' + 'all', originally denoting garments worn over all other clothes.

4. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvɚˌɔl / (AmE), / ˈəʊvərˌɔːl / (BrE)
(usually plural) A piece of clothing consisting of trousers with a bib and shoulder straps, typically made of denim and worn as workwear.
work trousers with shoulder straps (overalls)

Examples

He wore overalls to protect his clothes while painting the house.
The mechanic quickly wiped his hands on his overalls before answering the phone.
Children often wear overalls because they're comfortable and durable for play.

Related Terms
