pearl

1. VERB

/ pɝːl / (AmE), / pɜːl / (BrE)
to gather pearls from oysters in the ocean
gather pearls from oysters

Examples

During the summer months, locals would pearl along the coast for valuable treasures.
She learned to pearl from her grandmother, diving in the clear blue waters.
Many divers risk their safety to pearl in search of rare finds.

2. NOUN

/ pɝl / (AmE), / pɜːl / (BrE)
a smooth, hard, usually round, lustrous substance produced within the shell of certain mollusks and highly valued as a gemstone
round, lustrous gemstone from oysters or clams

Examples

She wore a beautiful necklace made of pearls to the gala event.
Divers risk their lives searching for the finest pearl deep in the ocean.
A single pearl sat at the center of the oyster, gleaming softly in the light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gem - precious or semi-precious stone, valued as a jewel
  • Antonym: diamond - a precious stone consisting of a clear and colorless crystalline form of pure carbon

Etymology

From Old French 'perle,' of uncertain origin.

3. NOUN

/ pɝl / (AmE), / pɜːl / (BrE)
a pale shade of white resembling the color of a pearl or bleached bones
a pale, slightly lustrous shade of white

Examples

The decorator chose pearl for the living room walls to give them a soft, elegant look.
She wore a dress in pearl that complimented her skin tone beautifully.
The car's exterior gleamed in a pearl finish under the afternoon sun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ivory - a creamy-white color resembling ivory
  • Synonym: off-white - a white color with a slight tint of another color

Etymology

From Old French perle, probably from Medieval Latin perla (pearl).
