groin

1. NOUN

/ ɡrɔɪn / (AmE), / ɡrɔɪn / (BrE)
the area between the abdomen and the inner part of the thigh, often including the crease at their junction and the external genitals
area between abdomen and thigh

Examples

He pulled a muscle in his groin while playing football.
She complained of pain in her groin after running the marathon.
The doctor examined the swollen area in his groin for signs of infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inguinal region - the medical term for the area of the groin

Etymology

Probably of Old French origin, related to 'groin' meaning 'snout' or 'projecting part', later extended to anatomy.

2. VERB

/ ɡrɔɪn / (AmE), / ɡrɔɪn / (BrE)
to construct or form (an architectural surface, such as a vault or ceiling) with groins or intersecting arches
to build with intersecting arches

Examples

The medieval cathedral was carefully groined to create its impressive vaulted ceilings.
Modern architects sometimes groin ceilings to add structural strength and distinctive beauty.
In the restoration, specialists meticulously groined the arches to match the original style.

Synset Examples

The ceiling was groined

Etymology

From Old French groin (snout, projecting edge), applied in architecture to the edge or intersection of vaults.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01659650-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ɡrɔɪn / (AmE), / ɡrɔɪn / (BrE)
(in architecture) the curved edge formed by the intersection of two vaults or arches.
curved edge where vaults/arches intersect (architecture)

Examples

The cathedral's nave featured a series of intersecting groins supporting the arched ceiling.
Architects in the Gothic era often decorated the groin to emphasize the intersection of vaults.
Careful stonework was required to construct the groin of the vaulted corridor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vault - an arched structure forming a roof or ceiling

Etymology

From Old French groin (muzzle, snout); influenced by Old English grēne, in architectural sense from analogy to projecting features.

Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ ɡrɔɪn / (AmE), / ɡrɔɪn / (BrE)
a structure of stone, concrete, or other material built out from a shore into the water to prevent beach erosion by interrupting water flow and limiting the movement of sediment
structure to prevent beach erosion

Examples

The town built a new groin to protect the beach from erosion during winter storms.
Many vacationers noticed the groin jutting out into the sea along the coastline.
Shifting sands often make it necessary to extend the existing groin further into the bay.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jetty - a structure built out into the water to influence currents or protect a harbor
  • Synonym: breakwater - a barrier built in the sea to protect a coast or harbor from the force of waves

Etymology

Probably from Old French *groignet*, diminutive of *gros* (large), or related to the architectural sense of 'groin' (the edge formed by the intersection of two vaults).
