autocratically

1. ADV

/ ˌɔt̬əˈkrætɪkli / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəˈkrætɪkli / (BrE)
In a manner characteristic of an autocrat; using absolute power or authority without considering others' opinions.
in an autocratic, absolute manner

Examples

The manager ran the department autocratically, rarely listening to staff feedback.
Some leaders have governed their countries autocratically throughout history.
Decisions made autocratically often ignore the needs and voices of the people.

Synset Examples

the Czars ruled Russia autocratically

Related Terms

  • Antonym: democratically - in a democratic manner

Etymology

From 'autocratic' + '-ally', ultimately from Greek 'autokratēs' (holding absolute power).

2. ADV

/ ˌɔtəˈkrætɪkli / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəˈkrætɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that is domineering or characteristic of an autocrat; as a dictator.
in a domineering, dictatorial manner

Examples

The CEO managed the company autocratically, making all the key decisions without input from others.
She autocratically dismissed any criticism from her team during the meeting.
The board members complained that the chairperson behaved autocratically when allocating resources.

Synset Examples

this manager acts dictatorially toward his colleagues

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dictatorially - in a manner characteristic of a dictator
  • Antonym: democratically - in a manner that involves people having equal say in decisions

Etymology

From 'autocratic' (from Greek 'autokratēs': autos 'self' + kratos 'power, rule') + '-ally' (adverbial suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00312823-r
Synset POS: r
