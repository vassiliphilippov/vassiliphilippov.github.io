Hachiman

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɑː.tʃiˌmɑːn / (AmE), / ˈha.tʃiˌman / (BrE)
A major Shinto god of war and divination, venerated as the protector of Japan and its people.
Shinto god of war

Examples

Many samurai worshipped Hachiman as their patron deity.
The shrine dedicated to Hachiman attracts visitors from all over Japan.
Legends describe Hachiman as both a god of war and a guardian of peasants.

Etymology

From Japanese 八幡 (Hachiman), literally meaning 'eight banners.'
