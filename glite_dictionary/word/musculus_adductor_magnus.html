musculus adductor magnus

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌs.kjʊ.ləs ædˈʌk.tɚ ˈmæɡ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈmʌs.kjʊ.ləs adˈʌk.tə ˈmaɡ.nəs / (BrE)
a large muscle in the inner thigh that adducts and extends the thigh at the hip joint
large thigh muscle for adduction and extension
medical

Examples

The musculus adductor magnus plays a crucial role in bringing the thighs together during movement.
Injury to the musculus adductor magnus can cause pain in the inner thigh and difficulty walking.
Surgeons must be careful not to damage the musculus adductor magnus during hip replacement procedures.

Etymology

From Latin: 'musculus' (muscle), 'adductor' (one who brings toward), 'magnus' (large).
