Hugoesque

1. ADJ

/ ˌhjuːɡoʊˈɛsk / (AmE), / ˌhjuːɡəʊˈɛsk / (BrE)
In the manner, style, or spirit of Victor Hugo, especially marked by dramatic, expansive, or romantic qualities.
in the style of Victor Hugo

Examples

The novelist's Hugoesque descriptions brought the city to life in vivid and dramatic detail.
Critics described the film's Hugoesque themes as grandiose and deeply romantic.
Her writing takes a Hugoesque turn, blending social commentary with emotional intensity.

Etymology

Formed from the name Victor Hugo + English suffix -esque (in the manner of).
