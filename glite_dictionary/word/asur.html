Asur

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑː.sʊr / (AmE), / ˈɑː.sʊə / (BrE)
an ancient city on the Tigris River, historically the capital of Assyria, located just south of modern Mosul in Iraq
ancient Assyrian capital city

Examples

Archaeologists have uncovered ancient temples in Asur dating back to the early Assyrian empire.
The city of Asur played a crucial role in the rise of Assyrian civilization.
Artifacts from Asur are on display in museums around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nineveh - ancient Assyrian city north of Asur, also a former capital

Etymology

From Akkadian Aššur, the name of the city and the Assyrian national deity.
