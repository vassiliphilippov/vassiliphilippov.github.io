impeccable

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɛkəbəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛkəb(ə)l / (BrE)
in accordance with the highest standards; faultless or perfect
perfect; faultless

Examples

Her impeccable manners impressed everyone at the dinner party.
He always delivers work of impeccable quality, no matter the deadline.
The chef is known for his impeccable attention to detail in every dish he prepares.

Synset Examples

faultless logic
speaks impeccable French
timing and technique were immaculate
an immaculate record

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faultless - without any fault; perfect
  • Antonym: imperfect - not perfect; having faults or errors

Etymology

From Latin 'impeccabilis', from 'in-' (not) + 'peccare' (to sin).

2. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɛkəbəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛkəbl̩ / (BrE)
(Theology) Not capable of sin; incapable of doing wrong or committing sin.
incapable of sin (theological)

Examples

Some theologians argue that Christ was impeccable, unable to commit sin by his very nature.
The doctrine held that angels, unlike humans, were impeccable beings.
He believed in the impeccable purity of the divine, untouched by sin or temptation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sinless - without sin; morally pure
  • Antonym: fallible - capable of making mistakes or being wrong

Etymology

From Latin impeccabilis, from in- (not) + peccare (to sin).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02523579-s
Synset POS: s
