cow

1. NOUN

/ kaʊ / (AmE), / kaʊ / (BrE)
(informal, offensive) An unpleasant or disliked woman, especially one who is large or seen as difficult.
unpleasant or disliked woman (offensive, informal)
informal offensive british

Examples

I can't believe she said that—what a cow!
He called his boss a cow after she yelled at him in front of everyone.
Stop being such a cow and help us for once.

2. NOUN

/ kaʊ / (AmE), / kaʊ / (BrE)
an adult female of domestic cattle, commonly raised for milk production or breeding purposes
an adult female domestic cattle

Examples

The farmer led the cow back to the barn after milking.
Each cow in the herd has a unique identification tag.
A cow can produce several gallons of milk every day.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bull - adult male of domestic cattle

Etymology

Old English *cū* (cow), from Proto-Germanic *kūz*; related to Dutch *koe*, German *Kuh*.

3. NOUN

/ kaʊ / (AmE), / kaʊ / (BrE)
A mature female of certain mammals, especially cattle, in species where the adult male is called a bull.
female adult of species where male is 'bull'

Examples

The dairy cow provides milk for the entire farm.
A cow can produce a calf once a year.
In this herd, each cow is tagged for easy identification.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bull - adult male of species where female is 'cow'

Etymology

From Old English 'cū', from Proto-Germanic '*kūz', ultimately from Proto-Indo-European '*gʷōus' (cow, ox).

4. VERB

/ kaʊ / (AmE), / kaʊ / (BrE)
to make someone submit or behave in a certain way by frightening or intimidating them
to intimidate or subdue

Examples

The protesters refused to be cowed by threats from the authorities.
He tried to cow his rivals with harsh words, but they stood their ground.
She was not easily cowed and spoke up for her beliefs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to intimidate - to frighten or overawe someone, especially to make them do what one wants
  • Synonym: to daunt - to make someone feel intimidated or apprehensive
  • Antonym: to hearten - to give courage or confidence to

Etymology

Probably from Old Norse *kuga* (to oppress).
