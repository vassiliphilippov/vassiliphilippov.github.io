valiantly

1. ADV

/ ˈvæliəntli / (AmE), / ˈvæliəntli / (BrE)
In a way that shows great courage or determination; with bravery.
with courage or determination

Examples

Despite overwhelming odds, he defended his beliefs valiantly throughout the trial.
The firefighters acted valiantly during the rescue operation, saving many lives.
She valiantly resisted the temptation to give up and finally succeeded.

Synset Examples

he fought valiantly until the end

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bravely - in a brave manner
  • Antonym: cowardly - in a cowardly manner, lacking courage

Etymology

From Old French 'vaillant', from Latin 'valentem' (strong, powerful), with '-ly' adverbial suffix.
