thiothixene

1. NOUN

/ θaɪ.oʊˈθɪk.sin / (AmE), / θaɪ.əˈθɪk.siːn / (BrE)
a tranquilizer used in the treatment of schizophrenia and other psychotic disorders, known under the trade name Navane
a drug for treating schizophrenia
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed thiothixene to help manage her schizophrenia symptoms.
Patients taking thiothixene should be monitored for possible side effects.
Thiothixene is less commonly used today but remains an option for treating psychotic disorders.

Etymology

From 'thio-' indicating sulfur and 'thixene', related to thioxanthene antipsychotics.
