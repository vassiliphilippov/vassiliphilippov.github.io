ablism

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.bəl.ɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.bəl.ɪz.əm / (BrE)
Discrimination or prejudice in favor of able-bodied people and against people with disabilities.
discrimination favoring the able-bodied

Examples

The organization works to combat ablism in the workplace.
Discussions about ablism are becoming more common in the media.
Policies must be reviewed to ensure they do not promote ablism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ableism - discrimination in favor of the able-bodied

Etymology

From 'able' + '-ism', modeled after 'racism' and similar terms.
