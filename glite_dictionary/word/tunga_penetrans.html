Tunga penetrans

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʊŋɡə ˌpɛnɪˈtrænz / (AmE), / ˈtʊŋɡə ˌpɛnɪˈtranz / (BrE)
a small tropical flea whose fertile female burrows under the skin of its host, including humans
tropical parasitic flea species
medical

Examples

The infestation was caused by Tunga penetrans burrowing into the skin of bare feet.
Health workers explained how Tunga penetrans could be prevented by wearing shoes.
Researchers collected several specimens of Tunga penetrans for the laboratory study.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Tunga' and species name 'penetrans' (penetrating).
