subgroup

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌbˌɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈsʌbɡruːp / (BrE)
a smaller group that is part of a larger group, often with its own distinguishing features or functions
smaller group within a larger group

Examples

In the survey, a subgroup of participants showed a different response than the majority.
The committee split into smaller subgroups to tackle different tasks.
Chimpanzees in the wild often form subgroups based on age, gender, or dominance.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: group - a number of people or things that are together or have something in common

Etymology

From Latin 'sub-' (under, below) + 'group', influenced by French 'sous-groupe'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsʌbˌɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌɡruːp / (BrE)
(Mathematics) A subset of a group that is itself a group under the same operation.
group subset (mathematics)

Examples

A subgroup of a group must include the identity element of the original group.
The set of even integers forms a subgroup of the group of all integers under addition.
Every subgroup inherits the group operation from its parent group.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: group - (mathematics) a set with a binary operation satisfying certain axioms

Etymology

From Latin 'sub-' (under, below) + 'group'.
