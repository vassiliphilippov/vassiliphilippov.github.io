semidiameter

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛmiˌdaɪˈæmətər / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˌdaɪˈɑːmɪtə / (BrE)
the apparent radius of a celestial body, such as the Sun or Moon, as observed from Earth
apparent radius of a celestial body

Examples

The Moon's semidiameter changes slightly as its distance from Earth varies.
Astronomers calculate the Sun's semidiameter to help predict solar eclipses.
The apparent semidiameter of Mars was measured during the observation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radius - the distance from the center to the edge of a circle or sphere

Etymology

From Latin semi- (half) + diameter (line passing through the center of a circle).
