horehound

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrˌhaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈhɔːˌhaʊnd / (BrE)
any of several aromatic herbs of the genus Marrubium, especially Marrubium vulgare, with wrinkled leaves and small white flowers, traditionally used as a flavoring or medicinal plant
aromatic herb of the genus Marrubium

Examples

The herbalist recommended horehound tea to soothe a sore throat.
Ancient remedies often included extracts from the horehound plant.
She grew horehound in her garden for its medicinal properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marrubium - the genus to which horehound belongs
  • Degree Lower: mint - aromatic herb from a related family

Etymology

From Old English 'hārhūne', meaning 'downy plant'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɔːrˌhaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈhɔːhˌaʊnd / (BrE)
A hard candy flavored with an extract of the horehound plant, traditionally used as a soothing remedy for coughs.
herbal-flavored candy

Examples

My grandmother always kept a tin of horehound in her purse for sore throats.
Some people find the taste of horehound candy a bit bitter, but others love it.
We visited an old-fashioned sweet shop that still sold horehound among other classic candies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lozenge - medicinal candy for soothing the throat

Etymology

Named after the horehound plant (from Old English 'hārhūne'), which is used to flavor the candy.
