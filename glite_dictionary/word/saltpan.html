saltpan

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔltˌpæn / (AmE), / ˈsɔːlt.pæn / (BrE)
A natural shallow basin, typically found in arid regions, in which water has evaporated leaving a crust or deposit of salt and other minerals such as gypsum.
natural shallow basin with salt deposits

Examples

The vast saltpan stretched across the landscape, shimmering in the afternoon sun.
Animals rarely venture near the saltpan due to the scarcity of fresh water.
Researchers collected mineral samples from the ancient saltpan to study its formation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: playa - a dry, flat area at the lowest part of an undrained desert basin, that becomes a lake after heavy rain
