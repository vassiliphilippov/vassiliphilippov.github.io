porthole

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔrtˌhoʊl / (AmE), / ˈpɔːthəʊl / (BrE)
An opening in a wall, ship, or armored vehicle, often circular, used for firing weapons through.
opening for firing through

Examples

The soldiers aimed their rifles through the porthole of the armored vehicle.
A cannon was sticking out of the porthole on the side of the old warship.
He peered through the porthole to get a better view of the enemy's position.

Etymology

From port (opening in a ship's side) + hole.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɔrtˌhoʊl / (AmE), / ˈpɔːthəʊl / (BrE)
a small, usually circular window in the side of a ship or an aircraft
window in a ship or airplane

Examples

The ocean was calm, and I watched the waves through the porthole in my cabin.
Sunlight streamed in from the airplane porthole as we approached the runway.
He pressed his face against the porthole, hoping to catch a glimpse of land.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: window - an opening in a wall or vehicle to admit light and air, usually fitted with glass

Etymology

From port (side of a ship) + hole.
