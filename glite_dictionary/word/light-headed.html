light-headed

1. ADJ

/ ˈlaɪtˌhɛdɪd / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtˌhɛdɪd / (BrE)
feeling dizzy, weak, or as if you might faint, often because of lack of food, illness, or sudden movement
feeling dizzy or faint

Examples

After skipping breakfast, she began to feel light-headed and had to sit down.
Standing up too quickly can sometimes make you light-headed.
He became light-headed after spending hours working in the sun.

Synset Examples

suddenly felt faint from the pain
was sick and faint from hunger
felt light in the head
a swooning fit
light-headed with wine
light-headed from lack of sleep

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dizzy - feeling as if you might lose your balance or fall
  • Synonym: faint - to feel weak and as if you are about to lose consciousness
  • Antonym: clear-headed - able to think in a calm and sensible way

2. ADJ

/ ˈlaɪtˌhɛdəd / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtˌhɛdɪd / (BrE)
silly or lacking seriousness; inclined to frivolous or giddy behavior.
silly; frivolous; not serious

Examples

During the meeting, her light-headed remarks lightened the mood but distracted from serious discussion.
His light-headed approach to responsibility worried his teachers and parents.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

3. ADJ

/ ˈlaɪtˌhɛdəd / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtˌhɛdɪd / (BrE)
feeling dizzy or as if you might faint, often due to a lack of blood to the brain or sudden changes in movement
feeling dizzy or faint

Examples

Skipping lunch made me feel light-headed during the afternoon meeting.
Climbing the stairs too quickly can leave some people light-headed for a moment.
She felt light-headed after donating blood at the clinic.

Synset Examples

He felt light-headed after skipping breakfast.
She became light-headed from the sudden change in altitude.
After standing up too quickly, I felt light-headed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dizzy - feeling as if you or everything around you is spinning
  • Degree Higher: faint - to lose consciousness for a short time; feeling weak and close to losing consciousness

Etymology

Formed from 'light' + 'headed', referring to a sensation as if the head is 'light'.
