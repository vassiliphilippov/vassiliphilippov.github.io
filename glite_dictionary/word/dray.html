dray

1. NOUN

/ dreɪ / (AmE), / dreɪ / (BrE)
a low, heavy cart without sides, usually pulled by horses, used for hauling goods
low heavy horse-drawn cart for hauling

Examples

The brewery still delivers beer in a dray pulled by powerful horses.
Workers loaded barrels onto the dray for transport to the warehouse.
A century ago, drays were a common sight on city streets.

Etymology

From Middle English draye (drag, sledge), related to 'drag'.
