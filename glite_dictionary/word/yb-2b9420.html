YB

1. NOUN

/ waɪ bi / (AmE), / waɪ bi / (BrE)
an abbreviation for yottabyte, a unit of digital information equal to one septillion (10^24) bytes or 1000 zettabytes.
abbreviation for yottabyte (10^24 bytes)

Examples

A petabyte is much smaller than a YB, which is almost unimaginably large.
Storing a YB of data would require an enormous infrastructure.
Cloud providers are not yet offering services that can handle a YB of storage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yottabyte - a unit of digital information equal to 10^24 bytes
  • Degree Lower: zettabyte - a unit of digital information equal to 10^21 bytes

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'yotta-' (representing 10^24) and 'byte', a unit of information.

2. NOUN

/ waɪ bi / (AmE), / waɪ bi / (BrE)
a unit of digital information equal to 1,024 zebibytes or 2^80 bytes, used in computing to measure data storage
unit of data: 1,024 zebibytes

Examples

A single YB of data can store vast amounts of information about the universe.
Modern databases have not yet reached the YB scale, but technology is steadily advancing.
The YB is rarely used outside theoretical data analysis.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: ZB - a unit of information equal to 1,024 exabytes or 2^70 bytes
  • Degree Lower: byte - a unit of digital information typically consisting of eight bits

Etymology

YB is an abbreviation for 'yobibyte', formed from 'yotta' (the SI prefix for 10^24) and 'binary byte'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13653625-n
Synset POS: n
