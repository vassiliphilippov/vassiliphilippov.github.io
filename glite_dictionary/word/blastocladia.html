Blastocladia

1. NOUN

/ ˌblæstəˈkleɪdiə / (AmE), / ˌblastəˈkleɪdɪə / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Blastocladiaceae, found chiefly in aquatic and damp habitats.
a genus of aquatic fungi

Examples

The genus Blastocladia is notable for its role in aquatic ecosystems.
Researchers discovered several new species within the Blastocladia genus in freshwater ponds.
The reproduction of Blastocladia involves both sexual and asexual spores.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin ‘Blastocladia’, based on Greek ‘blastos’ (germ, sprout) and ‘klados’ (branch).

2. NOUN

/ ˌblæstəˈkleɪdiə / (AmE), / ˌblastəˈkleɪdɪə / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Blastocladiaceae, found in freshwater and damp soil environments.
genus of aquatic fungi

Examples

Species in the genus Blastocladia can be found in freshwater habitats worldwide.
Researchers discovered a new member of the Blastocladia genus in the pond.
The life cycle of Blastocladia involves both sexual and asexual reproduction.

Etymology

From Greek 'blastos' (sprout) + 'klados' (branch), referring to the branching structure of these fungi.
