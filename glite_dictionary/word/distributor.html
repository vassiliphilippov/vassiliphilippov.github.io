distributor

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈstrɪbjətər / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɪbjʊtə / (BrE)
a company or person that supplies goods to shops and businesses for resale to the public
company selling goods to retailers

Examples

The electronics manufacturer partnered with a new distributor to expand its reach in Asia.
As a leading distributor of books, the company supplies thousands of bookstores nationwide.
The distributor ensures that products are delivered to retailers in a timely manner.

Synset Examples

his company is a large distributor of software products

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supplier - a person or company that provides goods or services

Etymology

From Latin 'distribuere' (to divide, deal out) via Middle English and Old French.

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈstrɪbjətər / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɪbjʊtə / (BrE)
a person or company that supplies goods to shops or other businesses, especially as an intermediary between producers and retailers
person/company that supplies goods to sellers

Examples

The distributor delivered the new products to all local stores on Monday.
As a movie distributor, the company negotiates with theaters nationwide.
They became the exclusive distributor of imported Italian food products in their region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supplier - a person or organization that provides something needed such as a product or service
  • Degree Lower: retailer - a person or business that sells goods to the public in relatively small quantities for use or consumption
  • Degree Higher: wholesaler - a person or business that sells goods in large quantities at low prices, typically to retailers

Etymology

From Latin 'distributus', past participle of 'distribuere' (to divide, distribute).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10038586-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ dɪˈstrɪb.ju.tɚ / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɪb.jʊ.tə / (BrE)
a person or organization that allocates, distributes, or supplies something to others
person or organization that allots or distributes

Examples

The company hired a new distributor to manage the allocation of goods across the region.
Each distributor was responsible for delivering resources to different departments.
As the distributor of the estate, she ensured that everyone received their fair share.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supplier - a person or organization that provides something needed
  • Antonym: collector - a person who gathers or collects items

Etymology

From Latin distributus (shared out), past participle of distribuere (to divide).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09804148-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ dɪˈstrɪb.jə.tɚ / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɪb.jʊ.tə / (BrE)
an electrical device in a gasoline engine that distributes voltage to the spark plugs in the proper firing order
device distributing voltage to spark plugs

Examples

The mechanic replaced the faulty distributor to restore the engine's performance.
A worn distributor can cause misfires and prevent the engine from running smoothly.
Modern cars often use electronic ignition systems instead of a traditional distributor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ignition coil - device that transforms battery's voltage to create sparks in engine

Etymology

From Latin distributus, past participle of distribuere (to divide, distribute).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
