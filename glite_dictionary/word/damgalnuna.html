Damgalnuna

1. PROPN

/ dæmˌɡɑlˈnuːnə / (AmE), / dæmˌɡɑːlˈnuːnə / (BrE)
In Babylonian mythology, the earth goddess, consort of Ea and mother of Marduk.
Babylonian earth goddess

Examples

In ancient texts, Damgalnuna is often revered as the nurturing mother of Marduk.
The temple was dedicated to both Ea and Damgalnuna, honoring their divine union.
Scholars discuss the role of Damgalnuna in Babylonian mythology and her influence as an earth goddess.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ea - Babylonian god of water and wisdom, husband of Damgalnuna
  • Synonym: Marduk - Babylonian chief god, son of Damgalnuna and Ea

Etymology

Babylonian mythology; name from Sumerian origins, meaning 'great lady of the waters'.
