amok

1. ADJ

/ əˈmʌk / (AmE), / əˈmɒk / (BrE)
Behaving in a frenzied, uncontrollable, or violent way.
frenzied and out of control

Examples

After the alarm sounded, the crowd ran amok through the hallways.
Rumors can quickly spread and cause people to act amok in a panic.
The players were so excited after their win that they went amok on the field.

Synset Examples

the soldier was completely amuck
berserk with grief
a berserk worker smashing windows

Related Terms

  • Synonym: berserk - wild or frenzied; out of control

Etymology

From Malay 'amuk' (to attack, go berserk).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00086117-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADV

/ əˈmʌk / (AmE), / əˈmɒk / (BrE)
In a wildly uncontrolled or frenzied manner; without self-control or restraint.
in a frenzied or uncontrolled way

Examples

When the alarm sounded, the crowd ran amok in the theater, causing chaos.
The children went amok in the playground after being let outside.
Rumors of treasure made some explorers go amok in their search, disregarding all caution.

Synset Examples

when the restaurant caught fire the patrons ran amuck, blocking the exit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: berserk - in a wild or frenzied state

Etymology

From Malay 'amuk', meaning 'a bout of frenzy'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00274935-r

3. ADV

/ əˈmʌk / (AmE), / əˈmɒk / (BrE)
in a wildly uncontrolled or violent manner, especially with a murderous frenzy
in a violent, uncontrolled way

Examples

During the chaos, the crowd ran amok, destroying everything in their path.
Rumors about the new policy ran amok through the office, causing widespread confusion.
If left unchecked, the children would have run amok in the playground.

Synset Examples

rioters running amuck and throwing sticks and bottles and stones

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rampage - to rush about wildly or destructively

Etymology

From Malay 'amuk' (frenzied attack).
