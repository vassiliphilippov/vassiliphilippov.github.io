masochism

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæsəˌkɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈmasəkɪzəm / (BrE)
The tendency to derive sexual gratification from one's own pain or humiliation, or the enjoyment of what appears to be painful or tiresome.
pleasure from pain or humiliation

Examples

He was open about his interest in masochism during therapy sessions.
Some people explore masochism as part of consensual relationships.
The novel delves into themes of masochism and psychological complexity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sadism - sexual pleasure derived from inflicting pain on others

Etymology

From the name of Leopold von Sacher-Masoch, Austrian writer, whose works depicted such behavior, plus -ism.
