Colaptes chrysoides

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈlæptiːz ˌkrɪsiˈɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / kəʊˈlæptiːz ˌkrɪsiˈɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A species of woodpecker found in the southwestern United States, similar to the yellow-shafted flicker but without the red neck marking.
woodpecker species of the southwestern US

Examples

The Colaptes chrysoides is often spotted nesting in saguaro cacti in Arizona.
Unlike the yellow-shafted flicker, the Colaptes chrysoides lacks a red mark on its neck.
Birdwatchers hope to see a Colaptes chrysoides during spring migration near the Mexican border.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gilded Flicker - Common name for Colaptes chrysoides
  • Degree Higher: yellow-shafted flicker - A similar North American flicker species with a red neck marking

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; Colaptes (woodpecker genus) + Greek chrysoides (golden-like, referencing its coloring).
