LSD

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛl ˌɛs ˈdi / (AmE), / ˌɛl ˌɛs ˈdiː / (BrE)
A powerful hallucinogenic drug derived from lysergic acid, known for producing altered mental states and sensory perceptions.
hallucinogenic drug; lysergic acid diethylamide

Examples

Many artists in the 1960s experimented with LSD to explore their creativity.
The film explores the cultural impact of LSD during the counterculture movement.
He was arrested for possessing a small amount of LSD at the festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acid - slang term for LSD
  • Synonym: psychedelic - relating to drugs producing hallucinations, such as LSD
  • Synonym: mescaline - another hallucinogenic drug
  • Synonym: psilocybin - hallucinogenic compound found in certain mushrooms

Etymology

Initialism of lysergic acid diethylamide; coined in German as Lysergsäurediethylamid.
