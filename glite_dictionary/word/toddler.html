toddler

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑːdlər / (AmE), / ˈtɒdlə / (BrE)
a young child, especially one who has just started to walk, typically between the ages of one and three
young child just beginning to walk

Examples

The toddler took his first steps across the living room today.
She read stories aloud to her toddler every night before bedtime.
Most toddlers are curious and eager to explore their surroundings.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: infant - a very young child or baby, especially one under one year old
  • Degree Higher: preschooler - a young child who is not yet old enough to attend kindergarten or school

Etymology

From 'to toddle' (to walk with short unsteady steps) + '-er'.
