digitalization

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪdʒɪtəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪdʒɪtəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the administration of digitalis to a patient, especially for the treatment of certain heart disorders
administration of digitalis (medical)
medical

Examples

The patient underwent digitalization to control his irregular heartbeat.
Careful monitoring is required during digitalization to avoid toxicity.
Rapid digitalization can be risky in elderly patients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: digitalis - a drug obtained from the foxglove plant, used to treat heart conditions

Etymology

From digitalis (a plant genus used as a cardiac drug) + -ization.
