brickwork

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪkˌwɜrk / (AmE), / ˈbrɪkˌwɜːk / (BrE)
The part of a building or structure made using bricks and mortar; masonry constructed with bricks.
masonry made from bricks and mortar

Examples

The old house had beautiful brickwork around its windows and doors.
Repairing the damaged brickwork required skilled craftsmanship and matching materials.
The brickwork was left exposed to highlight the building's traditional style.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: masonry - stonework or brickwork in construction
