doxazosin

1. NOUN

/ ˌdoʊkˈsæzəsɪn / (AmE), / ˌdɒkˈsazəsɪn / (BrE)
A drug used to treat high blood pressure and benign prostatic hyperplasia by relaxing blood vessels and improving blood flow.
drug for high blood pressure and prostate
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed doxazosin to manage his patient's high blood pressure.
Some men take doxazosin to relieve symptoms of an enlarged prostate.
She experienced dizziness as a side effect after starting doxazosin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cardura - brand name for doxazosin
  • Synonym: antihypertensive - drug that lowers high blood pressure

Etymology

From dox(a)- (possibly referencing chemical structure) + -azosin (a common drug suffix for related compounds).
