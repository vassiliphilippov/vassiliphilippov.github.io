pancreatin

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæŋkriˈætɪn / (AmE), / ˌpaŋkriˈatɪn / (BrE)
A mixture of digestive enzymes obtained from animal pancreas, used in medicine to aid digestion or treat disorders involving insufficient pancreatic secretions.
enzyme extract from animal pancreas
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed pancreatin to help with her digestive problems.
Some patients with chronic pancreatitis benefit from taking pancreatin supplements.
Pancreatin is often derived from the pancreas of pigs or cows for pharmaceutical use.

Etymology

From pancreas + -in, indicating a substance from the pancreas.
