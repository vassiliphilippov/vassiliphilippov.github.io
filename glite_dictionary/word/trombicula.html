Trombicula

1. NOUN

/ trɒmˈbɪkjələ / (AmE), / trɒmˈbɪkjʊlə / (BrE)
A genus of mites in the family Trombiculidae, whose larvae are known as chiggers and can cause skin irritation in humans and animals.
genus of mites; chiggers

Examples

The larvae of Trombicula mites, commonly called chiggers, can cause itchy welts on the skin.
Scientists studied the distribution of Trombicula species across different habitats.
Infestation by Trombicula can lead to irritation, especially in warm, moist environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chigger - the larval form of mites in the genus Trombicula

Etymology

From New Latin Trombicula, possibly derived from Greek trombos (lump, clot) and Latin diminutive suffix -cula.

2. NOUN

/ trɒmˈbɪkjʊlə / (AmE), / trɒmˈbɪkjʊlə / (BrE)
a genus of mites in the family Trombiculidae, including the larvae known as chiggers which can cause dermatitis in humans and animals
genus of mites (Trombiculidae family)

Examples

Trombicula species are known for their larvae, commonly called chiggers, which bite humans and animals.
Researchers studied the life cycle of Trombicula mites in various environments.
The dermatitis was traced to exposure to Trombicula larvae in the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mite - small arthropod belonging to the subclass Acari

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'trombos' (lump, clot) + diminutive suffix '-cula.'
