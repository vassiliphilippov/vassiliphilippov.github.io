slur

1. NOUN

/ slɝː / (AmE), / slɜː / (BrE)
an insulting or disparaging remark intended to damage someone's reputation or dignity
an insulting or disparaging remark

Examples

The politician demanded an apology after the newspaper published a slur against his character.
She felt deeply hurt by the racist slur uttered in the corridor.
Any slur on his honesty will not be tolerated in this classroom.

Synset Examples

in the 19th century any reference to female sexuality was considered a vile aspersion
it is difficult for a woman to understand a man's sensitivity to any slur on his virility

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insult - a remark or action that is rude or offensive
  • Antonym: compliment - a polite expression of praise or admiration

Etymology

Probably from Middle English sluren, meaning to drag or soil.

2. VERB

/ slɝː / (AmE), / slɜː / (BrE)
(of a musician) to play or sing notes smoothly and connectedly, without a break between them.
play or sing notes smoothly (legato)

Examples

The violinist was careful to slur two notes in each phrase for a smoother transition.
During the rehearsal, the conductor asked the flute section to slur the fast passages.
If you slur those notes, the melody will sound much more fluid and expressive.

Synset Examples

the pianist slurred the most beautiful passage in the sonata

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legato - In music, playing or singing notes smoothly and connectedly
  • Antonym: staccato - In music, playing or singing notes sharply separated

Etymology

From music terminology; ultimately from Old English 'slurian' (to slip, slide).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ slɝː / (AmE), / slɜː / (BrE)
to speak about someone in an insulting or disparaging way; to make a derogatory or offensive remark about someone
speak insultingly or disparagingly

Examples

He was accused of trying to slur his opponent's reputation during the debate.
It is wrong to slur people based on their background or beliefs.
The article appeared to slur several members of the community.

Synset Examples

your comments are slurring your co-workers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insult - to speak or treat with disrespect or scorn
  • Antonym: praise - to express warm approval or admiration of someone

4. NOUN

/ slɜːr / (AmE), / slɜː / (BrE)
(in music) a curved line placed over or under a group of notes to indicate that they are to be played or sung smoothly and connectedly (legato).
a curved line denoting legato in music

Examples

The composer marked a slur over the first four notes of the melody.
Playing the passage under a slur gives it a more flowing, connected sound.
Don't forget to draw the slur when you copy the music by hand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tie - a curved line in music connecting two of the same note indicating they are played as a single note
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

5. VERB

/ slɜr / (AmE), / slɜː / (BrE)
to speak words indistinctly so that they run together and are difficult to understand
to speak indistinctly

Examples

After the third glass of wine, he began to slur his words noticeably.
Tired and barely awake, she would sometimes slur her speech in late-night calls.
The actor deliberately slurred his lines to portray drunkenness on stage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mumble - to speak quietly and not clearly enough for the words to be understood
  • Antonym: to enunciate - to pronounce words clearly and distinctly
