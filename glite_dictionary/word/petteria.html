Petteria

1. NOUN

/ pəˈtɪriə / (AmE), / pɛˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae, represented by the Dalmatian laburnum.
genus of flowering plants (Fabaceae)

Examples

The garden features a rare specimen of Petteria in full bloom each spring.
Botanists are studying the ecological role of Petteria in its native habitats.
Petteria is often admired for its bright yellow flowers and decorative foliage.

Etymology

Named after Italian botanist F. Petter (d. 1822).

2. PROPN

/ pɛˈtɪəriə / (AmE), / pɛˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the pea family Fabaceae, containing a single species known as the Dalmatian laburnum.
genus of flowering plant (Dalmatian laburnum)

Examples

The Petteria is found mainly in the Balkan region.
Botanists admire the bright yellow flowers of Petteria for their ornamental value.
Petteria belongs to the Fabaceae family, related to laburnums and other peas.

Etymology

Named after the Italian botanist Carlo Petter (1807–1875).
