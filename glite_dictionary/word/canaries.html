Canaries

1. PROPN

/ kəˈnɛriz / (AmE), / kəˈneəriːz / (BrE)
a group of mountainous islands in the Atlantic Ocean, off the northwest coast of Africa, forming Spanish provinces; also known as the Canary Islands
Spanish islands off Africa (Canary Islands)

Examples

Many tourists visit the Canaries each year to enjoy the warm climate and beaches.
The traditional music and dance of the Canaries reflect a blend of Spanish and African influences.
Flights to the Canaries are often available from major European cities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Canary Islands - group of Spanish islands off northwest Africa

Etymology

From Spanish 'Canarias', possibly from Latin 'Canariae Insulae' (Islands of Dogs), named after large dogs found on the islands.
