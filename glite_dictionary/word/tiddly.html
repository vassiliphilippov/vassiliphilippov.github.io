tiddly

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɪdli / (AmE), / ˈtɪdli / (BrE)
Slightly intoxicated; tipsy.
slightly intoxicated
informal british

Examples

He felt a bit tiddly after his second glass of wine.
After the party, everyone was a little tiddly but in good spirits.
She got tiddly on just one cocktail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tipsy - slightly drunk
  • Degree Higher: drunk - intoxicated by alcohol
  • Antonym: sober - not affected by alcohol

Etymology

Probably from the colloquial form 'tiddlywink', meaning an alcoholic drink (late 19th century).
