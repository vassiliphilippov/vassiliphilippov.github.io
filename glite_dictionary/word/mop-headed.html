mop-headed

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɑpˌhɛdɪd / (AmE), / ˈmɒpˌhɛdɪd / (BrE)
Having a thick, bushy, or rounded top resembling a mop, often used for plants, trees, or people with abundant, untidy hair.
having a bushy, mop-like top

Examples

The mop-headed palms were easy to spot along the coastline.
She trimmed the mop-headed rosemary bush to a neater shape.
Several mop-headed children played in the park, their curls bouncing as they ran.

Synset Examples

mop-headed cabbage palms

Etymology

Formed from 'mop' (a bundle of thick yarn or rags for cleaning) and 'headed' (having a head or top), referring to a resemblance to the appearance of a mop.
