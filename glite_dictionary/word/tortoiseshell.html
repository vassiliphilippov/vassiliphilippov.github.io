tortoiseshell

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔr.t̬əsˌʃɛl / (AmE), / ˈtɔː.tə.səl / (BrE)
a brightly colored butterfly, especially one whose larvae feed on nettles, such as the small tortoiseshell of Eurasia (Aglais urticae)
a brightly colored butterfly

Examples

The tortoiseshell alighted gracefully on the purple thistle flowers in the meadow.
She identified the butterfly as a small tortoiseshell because of its vivid orange and black pattern.
Several tortoiseshells were seen fluttering along the hedgerows, their larvae feeding on nettles nearby.

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to the mottled shell of a tortoise.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɔrt̬əsˌʃɛl / (AmE), / ˈtɔːtəsʃɛl / (BrE)
a cat with a coat marked by a mixture of black, cream, and yellow-orange patches
cat with mottled black, cream, and orange fur

Examples

Her new cat is a beautiful tortoiseshell with striking orange and black fur.
The shelter has several tortoiseshell kittens available for adoption this weekend.
Many tortoiseshell cats are known for their feisty and independent personalities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calico - a cat with a tri-colored (white, black, and orange) coat, often with larger patches

Etymology

From the resemblance of the cat's coloring to the mottled shell of a tortoise.

3. NOUN

/ ˈtɔrtəsˌʃɛl / (AmE), / ˈtɔːtəsʃɛl / (BrE)
the mottled, yellow-and-brown, hard substance forming the shell of certain turtles, formerly used for decorative objects and jewelry.
mottled material from some turtle shells

Examples

The comb was made of polished tortoiseshell and glimmered in the sunlight.
Victorian craftsmen often used tortoiseshell to create inlaid jewelry boxes and hairpins.
Trade in authentic tortoiseshell is now banned to protect endangered turtle species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mother-of-pearl - the iridescent inner shell layer of some mollusks, used decoratively

Etymology

From 'tortoise' + 'shell', referring to the shell of certain turtles.

Source: WordNet
