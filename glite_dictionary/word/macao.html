Macao

1. PROPN

/ məˈkaʊ / (AmE), / məˈkaʊ / (BrE)
a former Portuguese territory on the south coast of China, comprising a peninsula and two islands, which was returned to Chinese administration in 1999 and is now a Special Administrative Region of China.
former Portuguese territory; Chinese Special Administrative Region

Examples

Many tourists visit Macao for its casinos and vibrant nightlife.
After reversion in 1999, Macao became a Special Administrative Region of China.
The unique blend of Portuguese and Chinese influences makes Macao a fascinating place to explore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hong Kong - a former British colony; Chinese Special Administrative Region

Etymology

From Portuguese Macau, itself deriving from the Cantonese name for the area, 妈阁 (Ma Kok), a temple to the sea goddess Mazu.
