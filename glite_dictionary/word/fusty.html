fusty

1. ADJ

/ ˈfʌsti / (AmE), / ˈfʌsti / (BrE)
Old-fashioned and out of date; having stale or musty qualities often associated with antiquity.
old-fashioned; out of date

Examples

The company was criticized for its fusty management style, which resisted innovation.
Some traditions seem appealing despite their fusty nature.
His fusty wardrobe made him look decades older than he actually was.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: modern - characteristic of the present, not old-fashioned
  • Synonym: musty - having a stale, moldy smell; also used to mean old-fashioned

Etymology

From Old French *fusté* (of casks), from *fust* (wooden cask).

2. ADJ

/ ˈfʌsti / (AmE), / ˈfʌsti / (BrE)
smelling stale, stuffy, or unclean, typically due to age or lack of fresh air
stale or musty-smelling

Examples

The room had a fusty odor, as if it hadn't been aired out in years.
She opened the ancient box and was greeted by a wave of fusty air.
Despite the fusty smell, the shop was filled with charming old books.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: musty - having a stale, moldy, or damp smell

Etymology

From Old French *fusté* (tasting of the cask), from *fust* (wine cask).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01057614-s
Synset POS: s
