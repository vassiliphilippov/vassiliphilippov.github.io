bumper

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌmpɚ / (AmE), / ˈbʌmpə / (BrE)
a horizontal bar fixed at either end of a motor vehicle to absorb impact in a collision and protect the vehicle's bodywork
bar on vehicle to absorb impact

Examples

The car's front bumper was slightly scratched in the accident.
He replaced his damaged bumper after the minor collision.
Modern vehicles have bumpers designed to absorb more impact energy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fender - a part of a car that covers or protects the wheel area, chiefly in American English

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʌmpɚ / (AmE), / ˈbʌmpə / (BrE)
a glass filled to the brim, especially when used for a toast
glass filled to the brim for a toast
archaic

Examples

He raised a bumper of wine to toast the bride and groom.
The old tavern rang with laughter as everyone downed a bumper in celebration.
They filled his glass with a bumper of ale to mark his return home.

Synset Examples

we quaffed a bumper of ale

Etymology

Probably from 'bump' (verb: to fill up), with the suffix -er forming agent nouns. Historical use in drinking toasts.
