Leibniz

1. PROPN

/ ˈlaɪbnɪts / (AmE), / ˈlaɪbnɪts / (BrE)
(1646–1716) A German philosopher and mathematician known for his concept of monads and for developing calculus independently of Newton.
German philosopher and mathematician (1646–1716)

Examples

Leibniz developed a theory of monads to explain the nature of reality.
The debate between Newton and Leibniz over the invention of calculus is famous in mathematical history.
Many philosophers have been influenced by the writings of Leibniz on metaphysics and logic.

Etymology

German surname; from Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz.
