Halaka

1. NOUN

/ hɑˈlɑkə / (AmE), / haˈlɑːkə / (BrE)
The body of Jewish law consisting of legal parts of the Talmud and rabbinic interpretations of the Hebrew Scriptures.
Jewish law from Talmudic literature

Examples

The rabbi explained how Halaka governs many aspects of daily Jewish life.
He studied Halaka to better understand the roots of Jewish tradition.
Legal questions are often answered by consulting Halaka in the Talmud.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: halakha - The body of Jewish law and tradition

Etymology

From Hebrew *halakhah* (law, the way to walk).
