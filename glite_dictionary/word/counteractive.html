counteractive

1. ADJ

/ ˌkaʊn.tɚˈæk.tɪv / (AmE), / ˌkaʊn.tərˈæk.tɪv / (BrE)
acting against something in order to weaken, neutralize, or mitigate its effect
neutralizing or opposing in effect

Examples

The new medication has a counteractive effect on the symptoms of the disease.
His calm demeanor was counteractive to the rising panic in the room.
Regulations serve a counteractive purpose against potential environmental harm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neutralizing - making something ineffective by countering its effect
  • Synonym: mitigating - making less severe or less intense

Etymology

From 'counter-' (against) and 'active', reflecting opposing action.
