bibliopole

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪbliəˌpoʊl / (AmE), / ˈbɪbliəˌpəʊl / (BrE)
a person who buys and sells rare or secondhand books, especially those that are old or unusual
dealer in rare or secondhand books

Examples

The local bibliopole proudly displayed a collection of rare first editions in his shop window.
As an avid collector, she often visited the renowned bibliopole to find unique volumes.
After years as a bibliopole, he developed a keen eye for identifying valuable manuscripts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bookseller - a person who sells books, especially as a business

Etymology

From Greek 'biblion' (book) + 'pōlēs' (seller).
