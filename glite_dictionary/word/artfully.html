artfully

1. ADV

/ ˈɑrt.fəli / (AmE), / ˈɑːt.fəli / (BrE)
in a way that is clever and often deceitful, especially to achieve one's aims; with skill or cunning
in a cunning or skillful manner

Examples

She artfully dodged the interviewer’s probing questions about her past.
The lawyer artfully twisted the facts to benefit his client.
She artfully avoided giving a direct answer to the question.

Synset Examples

disingenuously, he asked leading questions about his opponent's work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cunningly - in a clever and sometimes deceitful way
  • Antonym: honestly - in a truthful and sincere way

Etymology

From 'artful' + '-ly'. 'Artful' from Middle English, influenced by 'art' (skill).

2. ADV

/ ˈɑrtfəli / (AmE), / ˈɑːtfəli / (BrE)
In a clever, skillful, or cunning way, often with the intention to deceive or achieve something by subtle means.
in a clever or cunning manner

Examples

She artfully avoided answering the question, changing the subject with ease.
He artfully arranged the evidence to support his story.
The logo was artfully designed to attract attention without being flashy.

Synset Examples

he craftily arranged to be there when the decision was announced
had ever circumstances conspired so cunningly?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: craftily - in a clever or cunning way
  • Antonym: innocently - in a harmless or simple manner

Etymology

From 'artful' + '-ly', where 'artful' derives from 'art' meaning skill.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00295240-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ ˈɑrt.fəli / (AmE), / ˈɑːt.fəli / (BrE)
In a way that shows creative skill or cleverness.
with skill or cleverness

Examples

She arranged the flowers artfully in the vase to create a beautiful centerpiece.
The lawyer artfully avoided the question, steering the conversation in another direction.
He artfully painted the landscape, capturing every detail with precision and creativity.

Synset Examples

her foot pointed artfully toward tapering toes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skillfully - in a skillful manner
  • Synonym: deftly - in a quick and skillful way
  • Antonym: awkwardly - in an awkward or clumsy way

Etymology

From 'artful' + '-ly', where 'artful' comes from 'art' (Old French, from Latin 'ars', meaning skill).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r
