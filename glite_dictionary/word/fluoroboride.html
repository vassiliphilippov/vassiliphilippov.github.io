fluoroboride

1. NOUN

/ flʊˈɔːrəˌbɔːraɪd / (AmE), / flʊəˈrɒbəraɪd / (BrE)
a chemical compound that is a salt of fluoroboric acid, typically containing the tetrafluoroborate anion (BF4−)
salt of fluoroboric acid
medical

Examples

A fluoroboride can be formed by the reaction of a metal with fluoroboric acid.
Scientists often use fluoroboride in electroplating processes.
The stability of a fluoroboride depends on its constituent metal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tetrafluoroborate - anion BF4− derived from fluoroboric acid

Etymology

Formed from 'fluoro-' referring to fluorine, and 'boride' referencing boron compounds.
