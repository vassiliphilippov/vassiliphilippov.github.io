CPI

1. NOUN

/ si pi aɪ / (AmE), / siː piː aɪ / (BrE)
an abbreviation for 'consumer price index', an index measuring the average change over time in the prices paid by consumers for goods and services
consumer price index (economic indicator)

Examples

The CPI rose sharply last month, indicating higher inflation rates.
Many economists use the CPI to track changes in the cost of living.
An unexpected drop in the CPI surprised financial markets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consumer price index - index measuring average price changes paid by consumers

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'consumer price index'.

2. NOUN

/ si pi aɪ / (AmE), / siː piː aɪ / (BrE)
a standardized self-report questionnaire used to assess personality traits and behaviors, known as the California Psychological Inventory.
California Psychological Inventory, a personality test
medical

Examples

The psychologist administered the CPI to better understand the patient's behavioral tendencies.
Researchers often use the CPI to assess leadership qualities in candidates.
Many corporate assessment programs include the CPI as part of their evaluation process.

Etymology

Abbreviation of California Psychological Inventory.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06486643-n
Synset POS: n
