brink

1. NOUN

/ brɪŋk / (AmE), / brɪŋk / (BrE)
the extreme edge or verge of a steep place, such as a cliff or a bank
the edge of a steep place

Examples

Standing on the brink of the canyon, she felt a rush of excitement and fear.
The boulder teetered dangerously at the brink of the cliff.
He paused at the brink of the ravine, peering into the darkness below.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: edge - the outside limit of an object or surface

Etymology

From Middle Dutch brincke (edge, verge).

2. NOUN

/ brɪŋk / (AmE), / brɪŋk / (BrE)
the point at which something, typically something important or disastrous, is about to happen; the edge or border of a steep place or critical situation
point just before something happens

Examples

The company was on the brink of collapse before the new investors stepped in.
Scientists warned that the species is on the brink of extinction due to habitat loss.
Standing on the brink of the cliff, she looked out over the valley below.

Synset Examples

on the verge of tears
on the brink of bankruptcy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: verge - the point at which something is likely to begin

Etymology

From Middle Dutch or Middle Low German 'brink' meaning 'edge, grassland, slope'.

3. NOUN

/ brɪŋk / (AmE), / brɪŋk / (BrE)
the extreme edge or border of a surface or area; the point at which something, especially something momentous or significant, is about to happen
the edge or verge of something

Examples

He stood nervously on the brink of the cliff, staring at the drop below.
Scientists say we are on the brink of a major technological breakthrough.
The company was on the brink of bankruptcy before the new CEO took over.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: edge - the outside limit of an object or area
  • Synonym: verge - an edge or border
  • Antonym: center - the middle point of something

Etymology

From Middle Dutch 'brincke' (edge, verge).
