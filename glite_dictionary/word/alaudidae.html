Alaudidae

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɔdəˌdiː / (AmE), / əˈlɔːdɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small to medium-sized songbirds known as larks, found mainly in the Old World.
family of songbirds (larks)

Examples

The Alaudidae are well known for their elaborate songs and display flights.
Most members of the Alaudidae family inhabit open grasslands and deserts.
Several species within the Alaudidae are threatened by habitat loss and agricultural expansion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lark - a small songbird, especially of the family Alaudidae

Etymology

From New Latin Alaudidae, based on Latin alauda (lark).
