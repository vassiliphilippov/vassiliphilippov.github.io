Egypt

1. PROPN

/ ˈiːdʒɪpt / (AmE), / ˈiːdʒɪpt / (BrE)
an ancient empire north-east of Africa, centered on the Nile River and ruled by pharaohs, renowned for its civilization and influence on world history
ancient empire along Nile River

Examples

Many archaeological discoveries have been made in Egypt, shedding light on ancient civilizations.
The pyramids of Egypt are considered one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World.
Biblical stories often refer to Egypt as a powerful nation in the time of Moses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Egypt - a country in North Africa

Etymology

From Greek Aigyptos, from ancient Egyptian Hikuptah, meaning 'home of the soul of Ptah'.

2. PROPN

/ ˈiː.dʒɪpt / (AmE), / ˈiː.dʒɪpt / (BrE)
a country in northeastern Africa with a rich history, famous for its ancient civilization and monuments such as the pyramids and the Sphinx.
country in northeastern Africa; ancient civilization

Examples

Ancient Egypt was home to one of the world's earliest and most influential civilizations.
After college, I traveled through Egypt to see the pyramids and the Nile River.
Many tourists visit Egypt each year to explore its famous archaeological sites.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cairo - capital city of Egypt
  • Synonym: Nile - major river flowing through Egypt

Etymology

From Greek Aigyptos, ultimately from ancient Egyptian Ḥwt-kꜣ-Ptḥ, meaning 'home of the soul of Ptah'.
