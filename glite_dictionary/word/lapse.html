lapse

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæps / (AmE), / ˈlæps / (BrE)
a temporary pause or interruption in the continuity of an activity or process
temporary pause or interruption

Examples

There was a lapse of several hours before the police responded.
After a brief lapse in communication, the negotiations resumed.
The lapse between updates frustrated many users.

Synset Examples

a lapse of three weeks between letters

Etymology

From Latin lapsus (a slipping, fall), from labi (to slip).

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈlæps / (AmE), / ˈlæps / (BrE)
a temporary failure to maintain a particular standard or expected level of behavior, performance, or attention
a temporary failure or decline

Examples

After years of careful work, his sudden lapse surprised his colleagues.
She apologized for her lapse in judgment during the meeting.
A momentary lapse of concentration led to a costly mistake.

Related Terms
