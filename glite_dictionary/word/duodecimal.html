duodecimal

1. NOUN

/ ˌduːoʊˈdɛsɪməl / (AmE), / ˌdjuːəˈdɛsɪməl / (BrE)
One part out of twelve equal parts; a twelfth.
one part in twelve equal parts

Examples

In the ancient system, a duodecimal was often used to measure parts of a foot.
Each duodecimal represents one-twelfth of the whole unit.
The recipe called for a duodecimal portion of the spice mixture.

Etymology

From Latin 'duodecimus' (twelfth), from 'duodecim' (twelve).

2. ADJ

/ ˌduː.oʊˈdɛs.ə.məl / (AmE), / ˌdjuː.əˈdɛs.ɪ.məl / (BrE)
relating to or based on the number twelve; using twelve as a base, especially in counting or number systems
based on the number twelve

Examples

Ancient Babylonians used a duodecimal system for certain calculations.
A clock face is organized around a duodecimal division of hours.
Some cultures developed a duodecimal method of counting instead of decimal.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: decimal - relating to or based on the number ten; base-10 number system

Etymology

From Latin 'duodecim' (twelve) and '-al' (pertaining to).
