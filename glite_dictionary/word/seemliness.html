seemliness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːmlinəs / (AmE), / ˈsiːmlɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being proper, appropriate, or considerate towards others, especially in social or moral conduct
propriety; appropriateness in conduct

Examples

Her seemliness made her a welcome guest at any formal occasion.
The teacher praised the students for their seemliness during the ceremony.
He conducted himself with seemliness at the meeting, showing respect for everyone present.

Synset Examples

a place where the company of others must be accepted with good grace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propriety - the state or quality of conforming to conventionally accepted standards of behavior or morals
  • Antonym: impropriety - failure to conform to accepted standards of behavior or morals

Etymology

From 'seemly' (proper, fitting) + -ness, ultimately from Old Norse 'sœmiligr'.
