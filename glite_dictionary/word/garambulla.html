garambulla

1. NOUN

/ ɡɑːrəmˈbuːjə / (AmE), / ɡarəmˈbʊlə / (BrE)
A small, berrylike fruit native to certain regions of Central America, often eaten fresh or used in preserves.
small berrylike fruit

Examples

The market was selling freshly picked garambulla imported from Mexico.
She made a delicious jam from ripe garambulla she gathered on her trip.
Native to Central America, the garambulla is prized for its unique sweet-tart flavor.

Etymology

From Mexican Spanish 'garambullo', referring to the fruit of certain cacti (e.g., Myrtillocactus geometrizans).

2. NOUN

/ gəˌrɑːmˈbuːjə / (AmE), / gəˌræmˈbʊljə / (BrE)
an arborescent cactus of western Mexico (Myrtillocactus geometrizans) that bears small, oblong, edible, berrylike fruit
Mexican cactus with edible fruit

Examples

Farmers in Mexico harvest the garambulla when the fruit ripens in summer.
The sweet, dark berries of the garambulla are often used in traditional desserts.
We tasted fresh garambulla during our trip to western Mexico.

Etymology

From Mexican Spanish 'garambullo', name for the cactus and its fruit.
