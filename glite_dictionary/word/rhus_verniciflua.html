Rhus verniciflua

1. NOUN

/ ɹʌs vɝːˌnɪsəˈfluːə / (AmE), / ɹʌs vəːˌnɪsɪˈfluːə / (BrE)
a small Asian tree whose toxic sap is used to produce lacquer
Asian tree; source of lacquer

Examples

The sap of Rhus verniciflua is traditionally used in East Asia to make lacquerware.
Painters prized the high-gloss finish created with Rhus verniciflua resin.
Contact with Rhus verniciflua can cause allergic reactions in some people.

Etymology

From Latin, combining the genus 'Rhus' and 'verniciflua' meaning 'producing varnish'.
