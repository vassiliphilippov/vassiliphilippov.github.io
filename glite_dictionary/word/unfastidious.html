unfastidious

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.fæˈstɪd.i.əs / (AmE), / ˌʌn.fæˈstɪd.i.əs / (BrE)
not showing attention to detail; not concerned with cleanliness or precision
not attentive to detail or cleanliness

Examples

Despite her creative flair, she was rather unfastidious when it came to organizing her workspace.
The restaurant's unfastidious approach to cleanliness worried some of its customers.
He remained unfastidious about his appearance, preferring comfort over style.

Synset Examples

unfastidious in her dress

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fastidious - showing great attention to detail or cleanliness

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' (not) to 'fastidious' (from Latin 'fastidiosus', disdainful).

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.fæˈstɪd.i.əs / (AmE), / ˌʌn.fæˈstɪd.i.əs / (BrE)
not demanding or particular, especially about food or other requirements; not fastidious.
not demanding or particular

Examples

Unlike their fastidious cousins, some birds are remarkably unfastidious about what they eat.
The plant is unfastidious and can grow in a wide range of soil conditions.
His unfastidious habits made traveling together much easier.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fastidious - very attentive to detail, hard to please

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' to 'fastidious'. 'Fastidious' from Latin 'fastidiosus' (disdainful, squeamish).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00989100-a
Synset POS: a
