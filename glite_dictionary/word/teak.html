teak

1. NOUN

/ tik / (AmE), / tiːk / (BrE)
A hard, strong, and durable yellowish-brown wood from the teak tree, prized for its resistance to insects and warping, used in furniture, shipbuilding, and construction.
hard, durable wood from the teak tree

Examples

The deck was crafted from solid teak, giving the yacht both beauty and durability.
We chose teak for our garden table because it withstands the weather so well.
Antique furniture made from teak can last for generations with minimal care.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mahogany - a reddish-brown hard wood used for expensive furniture

Etymology

From Malayalam *tēkka*, via Portuguese *teca*.

2. NOUN

/ tik / (AmE), / tiːk / (BrE)
a tall tropical tree (Tectona grandis) native to South and Southeast Asia, valued for its hard, durable wood used in furniture and construction
tropical tree; source of hard, durable wood

Examples

The garden was shaded by a magnificent teak tree, its broad leaves rustling in the wind.
Most high-quality outdoor furniture is made from teak because of its resistance to weather.
They imported teak from India to construct the ship's deck due to its durability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mahogany - a tropical hardwood tree valued for its reddish-brown wood

Etymology

From Portuguese 'teca', from Malayalam 'tekka' (native name for the tree).
