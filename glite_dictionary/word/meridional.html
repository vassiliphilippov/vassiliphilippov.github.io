meridional

1. ADJ

/ məˈrɪdiənl̩ / (AmE), / məˈrɪdɪənl / (BrE)
of or relating to a meridian; situated or moving along a north-south direction, especially in geography or astronomy.
relating to a meridian (north-south direction)

Examples

The meridional distance between the two cities was carefully measured by the team of geographers.
In meteorology, meridional winds are those that flow from north to south.
The ancient astronomers tracked the meridional passage of the sun across the sky.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: zonal - relating to zones, especially east-west bands on a sphere such as Earth

Etymology

From Latin 'meridionalis', meaning 'southern' (from 'meridies' meaning 'midday' or 'south').

2. ADJ

/ məˈrɪdiənl̩ / (AmE), / məˈrɪdɪənl / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of southern regions or people; situated in the south.
of the south; southern

Examples

The climate in the meridional regions is generally warmer and sunnier.
Her research focused on the culinary traditions of meridional France.
This plant thrives best in meridional parts of the country, where winters are mild.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: northern - Relating to or characteristic of the north or northern people or places

Etymology

From Latin 'meridionalis' (southern), from 'meridies' (midday, south).

Synset POS: s
