meretriciousness

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛr.əˈtrɪʃ.əs.nəs / (AmE), / ˌmɛr.ɪˈtrɪʃ.əs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being attractive in a cheap, flashy, or superficial way without real value or integrity
showy but insincere or superficial attractiveness

Examples

The meretriciousness of the show's decorations distracted from its lack of substance.
Critics accused the politician's promises of meretriciousness and empty spectacle.
Despite its meretriciousness, the product attracted many customers with flashy advertising.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gaudiness - flashy and tasteless ornamentation
  • Antonym: elegance - graceful and stylish appearance or manner

Etymology

From Latin 'meretricius' (of or pertaining to prostitutes), from 'meretrix' (prostitute).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛrəˈtrɪʃəsnəs / (AmE), / ˌmɛrɪˈtrɪʃəsnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of appearing attractive or plausible on the surface while lacking real value or genuineness; superficial allure
superficial attractiveness; deceptive appearance

Examples

The meretriciousness of the advertisement led many to buy the product despite its lack of quality.
Critics noted the meretriciousness of the film's special effects, which distracted from the weak plot.
He saw through the meretriciousness of her charm and questioned her true intentions.

Synset Examples

the speciousness of his argument

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speciousness - the quality of seeming true or plausible but being actually false
  • Antonym: genuineness - the quality of being authentic or real

Etymology

From Latin meretricius (of or pertaining to prostitutes), from meretrix (prostitute).
