netted

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɛtɪd / (AmE), / ˈnɛtɪd / (BrE)
having an open mesh structure or resembling a web
having an open mesh or web-like appearance

Examples

The butterfly was caught in a netted veil used for studying insects.
She wore a beautiful dress with netted sleeves at the party.
Farmers use netted bags to store onions and other produce.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mesh - an open network of threads or wires
  • Synonym: lacy - having a delicate, openwork structure like lace

Etymology

Formed from 'net' + past participle suffix '-ed'; 'net' from Old English 'net'.
