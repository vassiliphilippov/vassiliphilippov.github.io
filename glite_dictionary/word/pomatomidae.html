Pomatomidae

1. NOUN

/ poʊˌmætəˈmɪdiː / (AmE), / pəʊˌmætəʊˈmɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine fish, including food and game species related to pompanos, notably the bluefish.
family of marine fish related to pompanos

Examples

The Pomatomidae family includes the well-known bluefish, a popular target among anglers.
Marine biologists study different species within Pomatomidae to understand their migratory patterns.
Some members of the Pomatomidae are widely sought after for both commercial and recreational fishing.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus *Pomatomus*.

2. NOUN

/ poʊˌmætəˈmɪdiː / (AmE), / pɒˌmætəˈmɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of food and game fishes, closely related to the pompanos, including species like the bluefish.
a family of food and game fishes

Examples

The Pomatomidae family includes the well-known bluefish, prized by anglers worldwide.
Several species in the Pomatomidae are important in both commercial and recreational fisheries.
Members of the Pomatomidae thrive in coastal temperate and subtropical waters.

Etymology

From New Latin Pomatomidae, based on Pomatomus (type genus) and -idae (family suffix).
