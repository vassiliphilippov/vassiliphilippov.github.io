Anpu

1. PROPN

/ ˈæn.puː / (AmE), / ˈæn.puː / (BrE)
An ancient Egyptian god associated with tombs and the afterlife, usually depicted as a man with the head of a jackal.
Egyptian god of tombs and the afterlife

Examples

Many ancient texts describe Anpu as the protector of the dead and overseer of mummification.
In Egyptian mythology, Anpu guided souls to the afterlife.
Archaeologists discovered a statue of Anpu in the ruins of an old temple.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Anubis - Greek name for the Egyptian god of tombs and embalming

Etymology

From ancient Egyptian *Anpu* (also rendered Anubis in Greek).
