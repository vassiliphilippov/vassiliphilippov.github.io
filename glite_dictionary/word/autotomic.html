autotomic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɔːtəˈtɑːmɪk / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəˈtɒmɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or involving autotomy, the ability of an organism to shed a body part intentionally, often as a defense mechanism.
relating to autotomy (self-amputation)
medical

Examples

Some lizards have developed autotomic tails that can detach to evade predators.
The autotomic response allows certain sea stars to escape when threatened by cutting off an arm.
Researchers observed autotomic behavior in several species of crustaceans under laboratory conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autotomy - the casting off of a part of the body by an animal for defense

Etymology

From autotomy (Greek autos 'self' + tomia 'cutting'), with suffix -ic forming adjectives.
