Plantago major

1. NOUN

/ plænˈteɪgoʊ ˈmeɪdʒər / (AmE), / plænˈteɪgəʊ ˈmeɪdʒə / (BrE)
a common European perennial plant, known as broadleaf plantain, naturalized worldwide and often considered a troublesome weed
broadleaf plantain weed species

Examples

The leaves of Plantago major are sometimes used in herbal remedies for minor wounds.
Plantago major can quickly invade lawns and gardens if not controlled.
Many people mistake Plantago major for an unremarkable weed, but it is highly adaptable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plantain - a common name for several plants of the genus Plantago

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Plantago' is the genus name and 'major' means 'greater' or 'larger.'
