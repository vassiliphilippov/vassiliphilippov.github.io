to quantify

1. VERB

/ ˈkwɑːn.t̬ə.faɪ / (AmE), / ˈkwɒn.tɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to express or measure the quantity of something, especially in numerical terms
express or measure as a quantity

Examples

It is difficult to quantify the impact of climate change on local economies.
The researchers attempted to quantify happiness using a detailed survey.
Can you quantify how much time this new process will save?

Synset Examples

Can you quantify your results?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: measure - to ascertain the size, amount, or degree of something

Etymology

From Medieval Latin quantificare, from Latin quantus 'how much' + -ficare 'to make'.
