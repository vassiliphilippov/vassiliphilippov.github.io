centromeric

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛntɹəˈmɛɹɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɛntrəˈmɛrɪk / (BrE)
relating to the centromere, the specialized region of a chromosome to which spindle fibers attach during cell division
relating to a chromosome's centromere
medical

Examples

The researchers observed a centromeric region on each chromosome during the process of mitosis.
Certain genetic disorders are linked to centromeric abnormalities in human chromosomes.
Antibodies were used to stain centromeric proteins for microscopic analysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: centromere - the specialized region of a chromosome where spindle fibers attach during mitosis and meiosis

Etymology

From 'centromere' + '-ic', meaning 'of or pertaining to the centromere'.
