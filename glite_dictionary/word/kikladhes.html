Kikladhes

1. PROPN

/ kikˈlɑːðɛs / (AmE), / kɪkˈlædiz / (BrE)
a group of over 200 islands in the southern part of the Aegean Sea, southeast of mainland Greece, known for their distinctive architecture and history.
group of islands in the southern Aegean Sea

Examples

Tourists often visit the Kikladhes for their beautiful beaches and white-washed villages.
The architecture of the Kikladhes is famous for its blue-domed churches and narrow streets.
Sailing between the islands of the Kikladhes offers breathtaking views of the Aegean Sea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cyclades - the more common English name for Kikladhes, a group of Greek islands in the Aegean Sea

Etymology

From Modern Greek Κυκλάδες (Kykládes), meaning 'circular islands'.
