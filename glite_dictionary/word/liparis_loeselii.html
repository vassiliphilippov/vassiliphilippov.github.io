Liparis loeselii

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈpɛərɪs loʊˈɛsəliː / (AmE), / lɪˈpærɪs ləʊˈɛsəliː / (BrE)
A small terrestrial orchid native to eastern North America and Europe, characterized by two nearly basal leaves and dull yellow-green racemose flowers.
a species of terrestrial orchid

Examples

The wet meadows provide a habitat for Liparis loeselii to thrive.
Botanists were excited to discover a rare specimen of Liparis loeselii in the nature reserve.
Conservation efforts focus on protecting the delicate environment needed by Liparis loeselii.

Etymology

Liparis from Greek 'liparos' (fat, shiny, oily) referring to the plant's glossy leaves; loeselii named after botanist Johannes Loesel.
