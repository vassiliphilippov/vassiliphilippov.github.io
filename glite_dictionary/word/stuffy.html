stuffy

1. ADJ

/ ˈstʌfi / (AmE), / ˈstʌfi / (BrE)
(of a person, place, or activity) excessively conventional, narrow-minded, and therefore dull or uninteresting.
excessively conventional and dull

Examples

The club has a stuffy atmosphere where everyone follows old traditions.
She found the meeting too stuffy for her creative ideas to be appreciated.
It's hard to have fun at such a stuffy dinner party.

Synset Examples

why is the middle class so stodgy, so utterly without a sense of humor?
a stodgy dinner party

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stodgy - dull and unimaginative; lacking originality or excitement
  • Antonym: lively - full of life and excitement; energetic or animated

2. ADJ

/ ˈstʌfi / (AmE), / ˈstʌfi / (BrE)
(of a part of the body) feeling blocked or obstructed, making breathing or normal sensation difficult or uncomfortable.
blocked or obstructed sensation (body/airways)

Examples

After catching a cold, he complained of a stuffy nose and difficulty breathing.
Spending the night in that dry, heated room left me feeling stuffy the next morning.
During allergy season, my chest can become quite stuffy and uncomfortable.

Synset Examples

a stuffy feeling in my chest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: congested - blocked with fluid or another substance, especially in the nose or chest
  • Antonym: clear - not blocked or obstructed (of airways or passages)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01626214-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈstʌfi / (AmE), / ˈstʌfi / (BrE)
(of a room or building) lacking fresh air and unpleasant to breathe in.
lacking fresh air

Examples

The office became stuffy after the windows were closed all afternoon.
It felt stuffy in the small classroom with so many people inside.
We had to open a door because it was too stuffy during the meeting.

Synset Examples

a dusty airless attic
the dreadfully close atmosphere
hot and stuffy and the air was blue with smoke

Related Terms

  • Synonym: airless - lacking air or ventilation
  • Antonym: ventilated - allowing air to flow through; having good airflow
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02519542-s
Synset POS: s
