Toscanini

1. PROPN

/ toʊskəˈniːni / (AmE), / tɒskəˈniːni / (BrE)
Arturo Toscanini (1867–1957), an Italian conductor renowned for leading many major orchestras worldwide.
Italian conductor (1867–1957)

Examples

The orchestra performed under the baton of Toscanini to great acclaim.
Many music lovers regard Toscanini as one of the finest conductors of the 20th century.
Recordings by Toscanini continue to inspire musicians and audiences alike.

Etymology

Italian surname; Arturo Toscanini's name has become synonymous with excellence in orchestral conducting.
