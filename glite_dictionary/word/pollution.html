pollution

1. NOUN

/ pəˈluːʃən / (AmE), / pəˈluːʃən / (BrE)
The presence in or introduction into the environment of substances or things that cause harm or discomfort to living organisms or make the environment unsafe or unsuitable.
contamination of the environment with harmful substances

Examples

The factory's pollution caused severe damage to the local river ecosystem.
Air pollution in big cities can lead to serious health problems for residents.
Many governments are enacting stricter laws to reduce water pollution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contamination - the act of making something impure by contact with something unclean
  • Antonym: purification - the process of removing contaminants or impurities

Etymology

From Latin 'pollutio', from 'polluere' (to soil, defile).

2. NOUN

/ pəˈluːʃən / (AmE), / pəˈluːʃən / (BrE)
The presence or introduction of harmful substances or products into the environment.
contamination of the environment

Examples

There are strict laws to control industrial pollution in the city.
River pollution has become a major concern for local residents and environmentalists.
Air pollution can have serious effects on public health and the climate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contamination - the action of making something impure by exposure to or addition of a poisonous or polluting substance
  • Antonym: purity - the state of being pure or clean

Etymology

From Latin pollutio (defilement), from polluere (to defile).

3. NOUN

/ pəˈluʃən / (AmE), / pəˈluːʃən / (BrE)
The presence in or introduction into the environment of substances which have harmful or poisonous effects, especially as a result of human activity.
harmful contamination of the environment

Examples

The levels of pollution in the city have increased dramatically over the last decade.
Industrial waste is a major source of water pollution worldwide.
Many laws have been implemented to reduce air pollution and protect public health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contamination - the act of making something impure by exposure to or addition of a harmful substance
  • Antonym: purification - the process of removing contaminants and making something pure

Etymology

From Latin 'pollutio' (defilement), from 'polluere' (to defile, soil).
