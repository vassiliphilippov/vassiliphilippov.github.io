closed

1. ADJ

/ kloʊzd / (AmE), / kləʊzd / (BrE)
(of an interval in mathematics) containing both of its endpoints.
math: including both endpoints in an interval

Examples

A closed interval like [2, 5] includes both 2 and 5.
The function is continuous on the closed interval [0, 1].
Unlike an open interval, a closed interval contains its boundary points.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: open - (math) not containing its endpoints, as of an interval
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ kloʊzd / (AmE), / kləʊzd / (BrE)
(of an event, meeting, or place) not open to the general public or to everyone; restricted to particular people.
restricted, not open to all

Examples

The committee held a closed session to discuss confidential matters.
Only employees were allowed at the closed event.
Because it was a closed meeting, journalists were not permitted to attend.

Synset Examples

a closed meeting

Related Terms

  • Antonym: open - allowing free or public access

5. ADJ

/ kloʊzd / (AmE), / kləʊzd / (BrE)
(of something) not open; having been shut so as to prevent access, passage, or view.
not open; shut

Examples

The store was already closed when we arrived at nine o'clock.
Please ensure all windows are closed before leaving the house.
The museum doors remained closed during the renovation period.

Synset Examples

the door slammed shut

Related Terms

  • Antonym: open - allowing access, passage, or view; not closed
  • Synonym: shut - in a state where access is blocked; not open

Etymology

Past participle of 'close', from Old French 'clos', past participle of 'clore', from Latin 'claudere' (to shut).
