Halacha

1. NOUN

/ hɑːˈlɑːxə / (AmE), / haˈlaxa / (BrE)
The collective body of Jewish religious laws derived from the Written and Oral Torah and the Talmud, governing religious and daily life.
Jewish religious law

Examples

Many observant Jews follow Halacha in their daily routines and decision-making.
A rabbi was consulted to clarify an issue concerning Halacha.
Debates about modern technology are frequently addressed within the framework of Halacha.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jewish law - Religious laws in Judaism

Etymology

From Hebrew *halakhah* (הֲלָכָה), meaning 'path' or 'way to walk'.
