cheerless

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃɪrləs / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪələs / (BrE)
Having a gloomy or depressing quality; lacking in cheer or comfort.
gloomy; lacking cheer

Examples

The hotel lobby had a cheerless atmosphere that made guests feel unwelcome.
After the argument, the house felt strangely cheerless and cold.
She gazed out at the cheerless winter landscape, wishing for spring.

Synset Examples

the economic outlook is depressing
something cheerless about the room
a moody and uncheerful person
an uncheerful place

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gloomy - dark or poorly lit, especially so as to appear depressing or frightening
  • Synonym: depressing - causing unhappiness or low spirits
  • Antonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic

Etymology

From 'cheer' (joy, mood) + '-less' (without).
