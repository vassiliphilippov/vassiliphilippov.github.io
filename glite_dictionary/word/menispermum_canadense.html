Menispermum canadense

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛn.ɪˈspɝː.məm ˌkæn.əˈdɛns / (AmE), / ˌmɛn.ɪˈspɜː.məm ˌkæn.əˈdens / (BrE)
A woody vine of eastern North America with large oval leaves, small white flowers, and purple to blue-black fruits, also known as Canadian moonseed.
North American woody vine; Canadian moonseed

Examples

The berries of Menispermum canadense resemble grapes but are highly toxic if ingested.
Hikers should be cautious when handling Menispermum canadense in the wild due to its poisonous fruit.
Botanists often study Menispermum canadense for its unique leaf shape and climbing habit.

Etymology

From Latin: Menispermum (genus name, from Greek 'menis' (moon) + 'sperma' (seed)) + canadense (of Canada).
