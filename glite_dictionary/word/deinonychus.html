Deinonychus

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈnɑnɪkəs / (AmE), / daɪˈnɒnɪkəs / (BrE)
a swift, agile, wolf-sized bipedal dinosaur of the Cretaceous period, known for its large curved claw on each hind foot
a wolf-sized bipedal dinosaur with a large claw

Examples

The Deinonychus used its claws to hunt prey in packs during the Cretaceous period.
A fossil of a Deinonychus was discovered in Montana by paleontologists.
Children were fascinated by the speed and agility of the Deinonychus in the museum exhibit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Velociraptor - a smaller, closely related predatory dinosaur

Etymology

From Greek 'deinos' (terrible) + 'onyx, onychos' (claw).

2. NOUN

/ daɪˈnɑːnɪkəs / (AmE), / daɪˈnɒnɪkəs / (BrE)
a genus of advanced bipedal carnivorous dinosaur from the Cretaceous period, known for its sickle-shaped claw and containing the single species Deinonychus antirrhopus
genus of predatory bipedal dinosaur

Examples

The paleontologist discovered a nearly complete Deinonychus skeleton in the Montana badlands.
Children marveled at the life-sized model of Deinonychus at the natural history museum.
Recent studies suggest that Deinonychus may have hunted in packs.

Etymology

From Greek deinos (terrible) + onyx, onychos (claw).
