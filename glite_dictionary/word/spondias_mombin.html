Spondias mombin

1. NOUN

/ spɒnˈdiːəs məmˈbɪn / (AmE), / spɒnˈdiːəs mɒmˈbɪn / (BrE)
A tropical American tree of the cashew family with edible yellow fruit, also known as yellow mombin or hog plum.
tropical tree with edible yellow fruit

Examples

The fruit of the Spondias mombin is widely used in juices and jams across South America.
Researchers are studying the medicinal properties of Spondias mombin leaves and bark.
Farmers cultivate Spondias mombin for both its fruit and its value in reforestation projects.

Etymology

From Latin genus name Spondias, and a local Cariban name for the tree.
