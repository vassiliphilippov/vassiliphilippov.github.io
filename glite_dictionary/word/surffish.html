surffish

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜrfˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈsɜːfˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A small to medium-sized shallow-water fish found along the Pacific coast of North America.
small Pacific coast shallow-water fish

Examples

Anglers lined the shore, hoping to catch a surffish in the gentle waves.
The surffish is often seen darting through the shallows near kelp beds.
Marine biologists studied the habitat preferences of several local surffish species.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɝːfˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈsɜːfˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a fish found along the Pacific coast of North America, belonging to the family Embiotocidae; commonly seen in surf zones
Pacific coastal fish (Embiotocidae)

Examples

Anglers on the beach often catch surffish in the shallow waters of the Pacific.
The surffish is well adapted to swimming in the breaking waves near shore.
Many local recipes feature fresh surffish caught early in the morning.

Etymology

From 'surf' (referring to the waves near shore) + 'fish'.
