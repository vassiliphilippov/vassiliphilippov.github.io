disingenuously

1. ADV

/ ˌdɪsɪnˈdʒɛnjuəsli / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˈdʒɛnjʊəsli / (BrE)
in a manner that is not candid or sincere, typically by pretending to know less about something than one really does
in an insincere or dishonest manner

Examples

She disingenuously pretended to be surprised by the news that everyone already knew.
He disingenuously claimed not to understand the policy he had helped create.
The spokesperson disingenuously avoided answering the reporter’s direct question.

Synset Examples

disingenuously, he asked leading questions about his opponent's work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dishonestly - in a manner that is not honest or trustworthy
  • Antonym: genuinely - in a truthful or sincere manner

Etymology

From 'disingenuous' (not candid), formed from Latin 'dis-' (not) + 'ingenuus' (native, freeborn, candid).
