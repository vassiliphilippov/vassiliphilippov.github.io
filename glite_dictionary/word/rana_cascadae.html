Rana cascadae

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪ.nə kæˈskeɪ.diː / (AmE), / ˈreɪ.nə kasˈkɑː.diː / (BrE)
A species of frog known as the Cascades frog, native to the northwestern United States and northern California, typically found near mountain streams and lakes.
Cascades frog species of the northwest US

Examples

The Rana cascadae is often seen basking near mountain lakes during summer.
Conservationists are closely monitoring the population of Rana cascadae due to habitat loss.
Research on Rana cascadae provides valuable information about amphibian health in the Pacific Northwest.

Etymology

From Latin; genus name 'Rana' meaning frog and species 'cascadae' referring to the Cascade Range where it is found.
