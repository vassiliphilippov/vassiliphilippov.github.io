drugget

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrʌɡɪt / (AmE), / ˈdrʌɡɪt / (BrE)
a rug or covering made from a coarse fabric, typically with a cotton warp and a wool filling, used especially as a floor covering
coarse fabric rug or covering

Examples

The worn drugget in the hallway showed years of heavy use.
They covered the old wooden floor with a plain drugget to protect it during the party.
She remembered the smell of the drugget runners in her grandmother’s house.

Etymology

From French 'droghet', probably of Dutch origin; related to textile manufacturing.
