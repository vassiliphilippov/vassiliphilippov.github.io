calcific

1. ADJ

/ kælˈsɪfɪk / (AmE), / kælˈsɪfɪk / (BrE)
involving or resulting from calcification; relating to the formation or presence of calcium salts in tissue
relating to calcification
medical

Examples

The doctor noted a calcific deposit on the patient's shoulder X-ray.
Chronic inflammation can sometimes lead to calcific changes in soft tissues.
The pathologist described the mass as having a calcific component indicating mineralization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calcification - the process of depositing calcium salts

Etymology

From Latin 'calx, calcis' (lime) + '-ific' (making or causing).
