Rhus glabra

1. NOUN

/ ɹʌs ˈɡleɪbɹə / (AmE), / ˈruːs ˈɡleɪbrə / (BrE)
A common shrub native to eastern North America, with waxy compound leaves, green paniculate flowers, and red berries; also known as smooth sumac.
a North American shrub; smooth sumac

Examples

The leaves of Rhus glabra turn bright red in the fall, making it a popular ornamental plant.
Native Americans used the berries of Rhus glabra to make a refreshing beverage.
Rhus glabra thrives in open fields and along roadsides throughout eastern North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smooth sumac - common name for Rhus glabra, a nonpoisonous North American shrub

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Rhus' is the classical name for sumacs and 'glabra' means 'smooth'.
