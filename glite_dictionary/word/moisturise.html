to moisturise

1. VERB

/ ˈmɔɪstʃəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɔɪstəraɪz / (BrE)
to make something slightly wet or increase its humidity, especially to keep it from becoming dry
make something more moist or humid
british

Examples

It's important to moisturise your skin daily to prevent dryness.
This device will moisturise the air throughout the winter.
She applied lotion to moisturise her hands after washing them.

Synset Examples

We have a machine that humidifies the air in the house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to humidify - make the air more humid
  • Antonym: dry - make or become free from moisture

Etymology

From 'moist' + '-urise' (variant of '-ize'), based on 'moisturize' in American English.
