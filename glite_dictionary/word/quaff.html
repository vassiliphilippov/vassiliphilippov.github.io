quaff

1. VERB

/ kwɑf / (AmE), / kwɒf / (BrE)
to drink something, especially an alcoholic beverage, heartily or in large draughts
drink something heartily

Examples

After the long hike, they quaffed cold water at the campsite.
She quaffed a glass of wine as the celebration began.
The victorious team quaffed their drinks at the pub, toasting their hard-earned win.

Synset Examples

The men gulped down their beers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gulp - to swallow quickly and in large amounts
  • Antonym: sip - to drink in small amounts

Etymology

Probably from Dutch 'kwaffen' (to gulp down) or Low German 'quabben' (to drink noisily).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01204688-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ kwɑf / (AmE), / kwɒf / (BrE)
a hearty or copious drink; an act of drinking deeply
a hearty drink; act of drinking deeply

Examples

He raised the mug in a salute and took a deep quaff of ale.
The weary traveler enjoyed a refreshing quaff at the village tavern.
With a hearty quaff, she finished the last of her wine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: draft - a portion of liquid to be drunk

Etymology

Perhaps imitative or from Dutch 'kwaffen' (to gulp).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07900157-n
Synset POS: n
