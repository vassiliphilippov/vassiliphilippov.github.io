talaria

1. NOUN

/ təˈlɛəriə / (AmE), / təˈlɛːrɪə / (BrE)
(in classical mythology) winged sandals, as worn by Hermes or Mercury in Greco-Roman art and legend.
winged sandals in mythology

Examples

The talaria carried Hermes swiftly across the sky as he delivered messages for the gods.
In the ancient statue, Mercury is depicted with his talaria on his feet, ready for flight.
Writers often reference the talaria when describing messengers of the gods in classical literature.

Etymology

From Latin talaria (winged sandals), plural of talare, from talus (ankle).
