anatomy

1. NOUN

/ əˈnæt̬.ə.mi / (AmE), / əˈnæt.ə.mi / (BrE)
A detailed examination or analysis of something.
detailed analysis or examination

Examples

The professor's book offers an anatomy of modern political corruption.
Her documentary presents an anatomy of the events leading up to the crisis.
We spent hours discussing the anatomy of the team's unexpected victory.

Synset Examples

he studied the anatomy of crimes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: analysis - detailed examination of something

Etymology

From Greek anatomē (‘dissection’), via Latin anatomia.

2. NOUN

/ əˈnætəmi / (AmE), / əˈnætəmi / (BrE)
the scientific study of the structure of living organisms, especially their internal parts
study of bodily structure

Examples

He is studying anatomy at medical school to become a surgeon.
The book includes detailed drawings to illustrate bird anatomy.
Comparing the anatomy of different mammals helps scientists understand evolutionary relationships.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: physiology - study of functions and processes of living organisms

Etymology

From Greek anatomē (dissection), from ana- (up) + temnein (to cut).
