Abelmoschus

1. NOUN

/ ˌæbəlˈmɑskəs / (AmE), / ˌæbɛlˈmɒskəs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical coarse herbs with large lobed leaves and often yellow flowers, including okra.
genus of tropical herbs (botany)

Examples

The okra plant belongs to the genus Abelmoschus.
Several species of Abelmoschus are cultivated for their edible pods.
Botanists debated reclassifying certain plants under Abelmoschus instead of Hibiscus.

Etymology

From Arabic 'abu-l-misk' (father of musk), referring to the musky odor of some species' seeds.

2. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ.bəlˈmɑːs.kəs / (AmE), / ˌiː.bəlˈmɒs.kəs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical coarse herbs, including species with large lobed leaves and often yellow flowers, such as okra.
genus of tropical coarse herbs

Examples

The okra plant belongs to the genus Abelmoschus, which is native to tropical regions.
Members of the genus Abelmoschus are known for their large, showy flowers.
Researchers studied several species within the Abelmoschus genus for their agricultural potential.

Etymology

From Arabic 'abu l-misk' (father of musk), referring to the musky scent of the seeds in some species.
