athwart

1. ADV

/ əˈθwɔrt / (AmE), / əˈθwɔːt / (BrE)
at an oblique or slanting angle; diagonally across something
at an oblique angle; diagonally

Examples

The curtain fell athwart the doorway, blocking the fading light from the hall.
A beam of sunlight shot athwart the dusty attic, illuminating hidden corners.
She cast her scarf athwart her shoulders before stepping outside.

Synset Examples

the sun shone aslant into his face

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aslant - at a slant; obliquely
  • Synonym: across - from one side to the other

Etymology

From Middle English 'athwert', from Old Norse 'þvert' (across).

2. ADV

/ əˈθwɔrt / (AmE), / əˈθwɔːt / (BrE)
at right angles to the center line of a ship; across from side to side, especially in a nautical context.
at right angles to a ship's center line

Examples

The captain ordered the crew to lay the planks athwart the deck for support.
In case of rough seas, the sailors secured the barrels athwart to prevent rolling.
She stood athwart the ship, feeling the vessel sway beneath her feet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abreast - side by side, facing in the same direction, often used in nautical context

Etymology

From Middle English 'athwert' (across), from 'a-' (on) + 'thwert' (across, thwart).
