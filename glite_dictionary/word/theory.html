theory

1. NOUN

/ ˈθiəri / (AmE), / ˈθɪəri / (BrE)
an idea or set of ideas intended to explain facts or events, especially one not yet proven but that could account for them
tentative explanation of facts or phenomena

Examples

Einstein's theory of relativity transformed our understanding of time and space.
The scientist presented a new theory that could explain the unusual results.
Before it gained widespread support, the germ theory of disease faced significant skepticism.

Synset Examples

a scientific hypothesis that survives experimental testing becomes a scientific theory
he proposed a fresh theory of alkalis that later was accepted in chemical practices

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hypothesis - a proposed explanation made on the basis of limited evidence
  • Degree Higher: law - a statement based on repeated experimental observations that describes some aspect of the world
  • Antonym: fact - something that is known or proven to be true

Etymology

From Greek theōria (contemplation, speculation); related to theorein (to look at, view).

2. NOUN

/ ˈθɪri, ˈθiəri / (AmE), / ˈθɪəri, ˈθɪəri / (BrE)
A well-substantiated explanation of some aspect of the natural world, based on an organized system of accepted knowledge and principles.
scientific explanation or system of principles

Examples

Einstein's relativity is a famous theory that fundamentally changed our understanding of space and time.
The evolution theory explains the diversity of species on Earth through natural selection.
A scientific theory must be supported by evidence from repeated experiments and observations.

Synset Examples

theories can incorporate facts and laws and tested hypotheses
true in fact and theory

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hypothesis - a proposed explanation made on the basis of limited evidence; precursor to a theory

Etymology

From Greek theōria (contemplation, speculation), from theōrein (to look at, consider).

Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ ˈθɪri / (AmE), / ˈθɪəri / (BrE)
a belief or principle that influences or directs behaviour or decisions, especially one not yet proven or generally accepted
belief or principle guiding actions

Examples

She worked on the theory that honesty was the best policy.
His reluctance was based on a theory that the risks outweighed the benefits.
They acted on the theory that avoiding the area would keep them safe.

Synset Examples

the architect has a theory that more is less
they killed him on the theory that dead men tell no tales

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypothesis - a suggested explanation or idea that is not yet proven
  • Synonym: assumption - something that is accepted as true without proof
  • Antonym: fact - a thing that is known or proved to be true

Etymology

From Greek theōria (contemplation, speculation), from theōros (spectator).
