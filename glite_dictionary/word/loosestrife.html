loosestrife

1. NOUN

/ ˈluːsˌstraɪf / (AmE), / ˈluːsˌstraɪf / (BrE)
any of various herbaceous plants of the genus Lysimachia, typically having attractive spikes of yellow or purple flowers, commonly found in wet or marshy ground.
herbaceous plant of the genus Lysimachia

Examples

Yellow loosestrife thrives in the damp soil along the riverbank.
We planted several loosestrife species to attract pollinators to our garden.
Purple loosestrife can become invasive in wetland areas if not controlled.

Etymology

From late Middle English, the plant's name is believed to come from Latin 'lysimachia', from the name Lysimachus, said to have discovered its calming properties.

2. NOUN

/ ˈluːsˌstraɪf / (AmE), / ˈluːsˌstraɪf / (BrE)
Any of several herbs or subshrubs of the genus Lythrum, typically found in wetlands and known for their spikes of colorful flowers.
herbaceous plant of the genus Lythrum

Examples

Purple loosestrife has become invasive in many North American wetlands.
The garden was bordered by tall loosestrife in full bloom.
Several species of loosestrife are popular in ornamental water gardens.

Etymology

From Middle English 'lousestref', from Old French 'lianestre', probably from Greek 'lythron' (blood), referring to the color of some species.
