Jean Monnet

1. PROPN

/ ʒɑn mɔˈneɪ / (AmE), / ʒɑ̃ mɔˈneɪ / (BrE)
(1888–1979) A French economist and diplomat noted for advocating the creation of a Common Market in Europe and recognized as one of the founding figures of European integration.
French economist and European integration pioneer

Examples

Jean Monnet played a key role in the creation of the European Economic Community.
Many historians regard Jean Monnet as one of the architects of modern Europe.
The vision of Jean Monnet continues to influence European policy makers today.
