drawl

1. NOUN

/ drɔl / (AmE), / drɔːl / (BrE)
a slow and lazy way of speaking, with elongated vowel sounds
slow speech with prolonged vowels

Examples

Her Southern drawl was unmistakable as soon as she spoke.
He spoke with a lazy drawl that made him sound relaxed.
The comedian exaggerated his drawl to mimic a Texan accent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twang - a nasal way of speaking, often associated with certain regional accents
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07146449-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ drɔl / (AmE), / drɔːl / (BrE)
to speak in a slow, lazy way with prolonged vowel sounds
speak slowly with prolonged vowels

Examples

He tends to drawl his words when he's tired, making him sound relaxed.
She drawled out her name in a thick Southern accent.
During the interview, the actor drawled each answer slowly and deliberately.

Synset Examples

drawl one's vowels

Related Terms

  • Antonym: slur - to speak words unclearly and run them together

Etymology

Perhaps from Dutch *dralen* (to linger, delay).
