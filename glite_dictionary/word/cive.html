cive

1. NOUN

/ sɪv / (AmE), / sɪv / (BrE)
A perennial plant with hollow, cylindrical leaves, used as a seasoning in cooking; closely related to chives.
perennial herb used as seasoning
archaic

Examples

The recipe called for a handful of fresh cive to enhance the flavor of the soup.
In medieval gardens, cive was commonly cultivated alongside onions and garlic.
He sprinkled chopped cive over the baked potatoes before serving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chive - a similar perennial herb used as seasoning, more commonly known

Etymology

From Old French 'cive', from Latin 'cepa' meaning ‘onion’.
