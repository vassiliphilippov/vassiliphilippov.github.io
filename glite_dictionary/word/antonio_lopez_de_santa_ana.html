Antonio Lopez de Santa Ana

1. PROPN

/ ænˌtoʊnioʊ loʊˈpɛz deɪ ˌsæntə ˈænə / (AmE), / ænˌtəʊniəʊ ˈlɒpez deɪ ˌsæntə ˈænə / (BrE)
(1795–1876) Mexican general and president, known for attempting to suppress the Texas Revolution and for his leadership during the Mexican-American War.
Mexican general and president, 1795–1876

Examples

Antonio Lopez de Santa Ana played a decisive role during the Texas Revolution and the Mexican-American War.
The defeat of Antonio Lopez de Santa Ana at San Jacinto was a turning point in Texan independence.
Historians debate the strategies employed by Antonio Lopez de Santa Ana during his multiple terms as president of Mexico.
