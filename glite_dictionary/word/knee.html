knee

1. NOUN

/ niː / (AmE), / niː / (BrE)
the joint in the human leg where the thigh meets the lower leg, connecting the femur, tibia, and fibula, and protected in front by the patella
the leg joint between thigh and lower leg

Examples

He injured his knee while playing football last weekend.
She knelt down on one knee to tie her shoe.
Arthritis in the knee can make walking difficult for older people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elbow - the joint connecting the upper and lower parts of the arm

Etymology

Old English cnéow, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch knie and German Knie.

2. NOUN

/ ni / (AmE), / niː / (BrE)
The joint between the femur and tibia in a quadruped, corresponding to the human knee.
quadruped's joint between femur and tibia

Examples

The horse injured its knee while running in the pasture.
Veterinarians examined the dog’s knee for signs of arthritis.
In quadrupeds, the knee supports much of the animal’s weight during movement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knee - joint between thigh and lower leg in humans

Etymology

Old English cneow, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch knie and German Knie.

Source: WordNet
