Gambusia

1. NOUN

/ ɡæmˈbuːziə / (AmE), / ɡæmˈbjuːziə / (BrE)
A genus of small freshwater fish, commonly called mosquitofish, often used to control mosquito larvae in ponds and streams.
a genus of small freshwater mosquitofish

Examples

Gambusia have been introduced to ponds worldwide to reduce mosquito populations.
The aquarium featured several Gambusia swimming among the aquatic plants.
Many environmentalists monitor the impact of Gambusia on native fish species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mosquitofish - Small freshwater fish of the genus Gambusia, used for mosquito control

Etymology

From Latinized modern scientific name given by Charles Frédéric Girard in 1859, of uncertain origin.

2. NOUN

/ ɡæmˈbuːziə / (AmE), / ɡamˈbjuːziə / (BrE)
A genus of small, live-bearing freshwater fish known as mosquitofish, often used for mosquito control.
a genus of freshwater mosquitofish

Examples

The Gambusia species are widely used in mosquito control programs around the world.
Many aquarists keep Gambusia in their ponds to help control insect larvae.
The introduction of Gambusia to new habitats has sometimes led to ecological imbalances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mosquitofish - a small freshwater fish of the genus Gambusia, used to control mosquitoes

Etymology

From New Latin, derived from Cuban Spanish 'gambusia' (a kind of fish).
