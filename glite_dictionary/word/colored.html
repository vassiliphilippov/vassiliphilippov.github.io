colored

1. ADJ

/ ˈkʌlɚd / (AmE), / ˈkʌləd / (BrE)
(of something) having a color that has been artificially produced or altered, rather than occurring naturally.
artificially colored; not natural

Examples

The flowers in the bouquet were colored to match the bride's dress.
Many children's drinks contain colored liquids to attract their attention.
The artist preferred colored fabrics over natural ones for her designs.

Synset Examples

a bleached blonde

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dyed - having had color added by means of a dye
  • Antonym: natural - existing in nature; not made or caused by people

2. ADJ

/ ˈkʌlɚd / (AmE), / ˈkʌləd / (BrE)
(dated, offensive) Having skin rich in melanin pigments; formerly used to describe people of African or other nonwhite descent.
dated term for people with dark skin
offensive archaic

Examples

The old sign outside the building read 'Colored Entrance Only,' a painful reminder of segregation.
In much of the early twentieth century, schools were designated as either 'white' or 'colored'.
Her grandmother still recalls the era when buses had separate sections for 'colored' passengers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Black - of or relating to people of African descent
  • Antonym: white - of or relating to people of European descent (in terms of skin color)

4. NOUN

/ ˈkʌlɚd / (AmE), / ˈkʌləd / (BrE)
(offensive, archaic, US) A term formerly used in the United States to refer to Black people; now considered offensive.
dated, offensive US term for a Black person
offensive archaic american

Examples

Signs reading 'Whites Only' and 'Colored' were common in the segregated South.
The organization, formerly known as the National Association for the Advancement of Colored People, is now called the NAACP.
He found a drinking fountain marked 'Colored' while traveling through the state in the 1950s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black - a Black person
  • Synonym: African American - a person of African ancestry in the US
