constable

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑnstəbəl / (AmE), / ˈkʌnstəbl / (BrE)
A police officer of the lowest rank, especially in the UK and some other countries.
police officer of lowest rank

Examples

The constable directed traffic at the busy intersection after the lights went out.
She reported the theft to a constable at the local police station.
Two constables were assigned to patrol the festival throughout the evening.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: police officer - a member of the police force
  • Degree Higher: sergeant - a police officer of higher rank than a constable

Etymology

From Old French 'conestable', from Late Latin 'comes stabuli' (count of the stable).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑn.stə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.stə.bəl / (BrE)
a police officer, typically with less authority and jurisdiction than a sheriff, who enforces the law and maintains public order
a police officer with limited local authority

Examples

The constable patrolled the village square, making sure everything was in order.
A constable directed traffic around the accident scene.
She reported the theft to the local constable immediately.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: sheriff - a law enforcement officer with higher authority and jurisdiction, especially in rural areas

Etymology

From Old French 'conestable', from Late Latin 'comes stabuli' (count of the stable, i.e., officer of the stable).
