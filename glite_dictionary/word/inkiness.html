inkiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪŋkinəs / (AmE), / ˈɪŋkɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being inky, especially deep blackness or darkness
quality of being inky or very dark

Examples

The inkiness of the night made it impossible to see anything outside the window.
There was an inkiness to the paint that gave the artwork a dramatic effect.
She admired the inkiness of his hair, which was as dark as a raven's wing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackness - the quality or state of being black
  • Antonym: whiteness - the quality or state of being white

Etymology

From 'inky' + '-ness', after 'ink'.
