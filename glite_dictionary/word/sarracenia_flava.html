Sarracenia flava

1. NOUN

/ sæɹ.əˈsiː.ni.ə ˈflæ.və / (AmE), / sar.əˈsiː.ni.ə ˈflɑː.və / (BrE)
A species of pitcher plant native to the southeastern United States, with erect yellow, trumpet-shaped pitchers featuring wide mouths and upright lids.
yellow pitcher plant species of southeastern U.S.

Examples

The Sarracenia flava thrives in the wetlands of the southeastern United States.
We spotted a tall Sarracenia flava with bright yellow pitchers during the field trip.
Botanists admire the unique trap mechanism of Sarracenia flava for capturing insects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitcher plant - a type of carnivorous plant with pitcher-shaped leaves

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Sarracenia' honoring French physician Michel Sarrazin; 'flava' meaning 'yellow.'
