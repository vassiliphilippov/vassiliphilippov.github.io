chyliferous

1. ADJ

/ kaɪˈlɪfərəs / (AmE), / kaɪˈlɪfərəs / (BrE)
(of a vessel or structure) conveying or transmitting chyle.
transmitting chyle (anatomical)
medical

Examples

The small intestine contains numerous chyliferous vessels that absorb nutrients.
Damage to chyliferous ducts can lead to leakage of lymphatic fluid.
Researchers studied the structure of chyliferous capillaries in detail.

Synset Examples

chyliferous vessels

Etymology

From Latin 'chylifer' (chyle-bearing), from 'chylus' (chyle) + 'ferre' (to bear).
