allophonic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæləˈfɑnɪk / (AmE), / ˌaləˈfɒnɪk / (BrE)
relating to allophones, or the variants of a phoneme in a particular language context.
relating to allophones (phonetic variants)

Examples

English speakers often overlook the allophonic differences between the 'p' in 'spin' and 'pin'.
The allophonic rule explains why the pronunciation changes in this context.
Linguists analyze allophonic variation to understand how sounds behave in natural speech.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: phonemic - relating to phonemes (distinct units of sound in a language)

Etymology

From allophone + -ic, meaning 'relating to allophones'.
