precious

1. ADJ

/ ˈprɛʃəs / (AmE), / ˈprɛʃ.əs / (BrE)
Highly valued or esteemed, especially for intrinsic qualities or emotional significance.
highly valued; treasured

Examples

The diary was a precious gift from her grandmother and she cherished it dearly.
He shared some precious moments with his childhood friends during the reunion.
These old photographs are precious to me because they remind me of my family.

Synset Examples

a valued friend
precious memories

Related Terms

  • Synonym: valuable - worth a lot of value; important or worth much
  • Antonym: worthless - having no value or use

Etymology

From Old French precieus, from Latin pretiosus (costly, valuable).

2. ADV

/ ˈprɛʃəs / (AmE), / ˈprɛʃ.əs / (BrE)
(informal) Used to emphasize that there is very little of something or that something is very true; very, extremely.
used to emphasize 'very' or 'extremely'
informal

Examples

There was precious little hope of success after the announcement.
We have precious few options left at this point.
He made precious little progress with his homework over the weekend.

Synset Examples

there is precious little time left

Related Terms

  • Synonym: very - to a high degree; extremely
  • Synonym: scarcely - barely; only just
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00431167-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADJ

/ ˈprɛʃəs / (AmE), / ˈprɛʃ.əs / (BrE)
(of a performance, manner, etc.) affectedly or excessively delicate, cute, or charming, often in a way that seems contrived or insincere.
affectedly cute or charming

Examples

The actor's precious delivery made the audience cringe rather than laugh.
Her precious gestures seemed more irritating than endearing.
The critic described the film as charming at times but occasionally too precious for its own good.

Synset Examples

an insufferably precious performance
a child with intolerably cute mannerisms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affected - artificial or insincere in manner
  • Antonym: natural - genuine, not affected or contrived in manner
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00149910-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ˈprɛʃəs / (AmE), / ˈprɛʃ.əs / (BrE)
of great value; highly esteemed, especially because of being rare or costly
of great value or cost

Examples

This antique vase is extremely precious to our family.
She wore a necklace made of precious gems to the event.
Water became more precious than gold during the long drought.

Synset Examples

diamonds, sapphires, rubies, and emeralds are precious stones

Related Terms

  • Synonym: valuable - worth a lot of money; important or useful
  • Antonym: worthless - having no value or use

Etymology

From Latin pretiosus (costly, valuable), from pretium (price, value).

much loved; cherished

Examples
