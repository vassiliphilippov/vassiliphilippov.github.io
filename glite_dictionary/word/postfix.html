postfix

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊstˌfɪks / (AmE), / ˈpəʊstˌfɪks / (BrE)
a linguistic element (affix) that is added to the end of a word to change its meaning or form
an affix added to the end of a word

Examples

In some languages, a postfix can be used to indicate tense or plurality.
The word 'happily' is formed by adding the '-ly' postfix to 'happy'.
Grammatical rules specify where a postfix may appear within a sentence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suffix - an affix added at the end of a word

Etymology

From Latin 'post-' (after) + 'fixus' (fixed, fastened).
