Molech

1. PROPN

/ ˈmoʊlɛk / (AmE), / ˈməʊlɛk / (BrE)
An ancient god worshipped by the Canaanites and Phoenicians, to whom children were ritually sacrificed.
Canaanite/Phoenician god associated with child sacrifice

Examples

Several biblical passages warn the Israelites against sacrificing their children to Molech.
Archaeological findings have provided new insights into the worship of Molech in ancient cultures.
References to Molech appear in early religious and historical writings.

Etymology

From Hebrew Molek or Moloch, mentioned in the Old Testament as a deity to whom children were sacrificed.
