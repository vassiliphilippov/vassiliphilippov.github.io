rattled

1. ADJ

/ ˈræɾəld / (AmE), / ˈræt.əld / (BrE)
informal. Feeling nervous, confused, or upset, especially because of a sudden disturbance or unexpected event.
nervous or upset (informal)
informal

Examples

She looked rattled after hearing the unexpected news from her manager.
The sudden loud noise left the children feeling rattled and uneasy.
Even the most experienced pilots can become rattled in severe turbulence.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - not showing nervousness or agitation
  • Antonym: unflappable - remaining calm even in a crisis
