to analogise

1. VERB

/ əˈnæl.ə.ˌdʒaɪz / (AmE), / əˈnæl.ə.ˌdʒaɪz / (BrE)
to make an analogy; to show or suggest a likeness between different things
make an analogy
british

Examples

It is tempting to analogise the brain to a computer, but the comparison is imperfect.
Scientists often analogise complex concepts to everyday experiences for easier understanding.
He analogised the economic system to a living organism in his speech.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to analogize - to make an analogy (American spelling)

Etymology

From Greek 'analogos' (proportionate) via French 'analogie'; patterned after 'analyse'/'analyze'.
