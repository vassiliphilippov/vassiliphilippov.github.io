Arbutus menziesii

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈbjuːtəs mɛnˈziːziˌaɪ / (AmE), / ɑːˈbjuːtəs menˈziːziːaɪ / (BrE)
an evergreen tree native to the Pacific coast of North America, characterized by glossy, leathery leaves, orange-red edible berries, and smooth reddish bark; also known as madrone, its wood is used for furniture and its bark for tanning.
evergreen tree of the Pacific coast of North America

Examples

The Arbutus menziesii stood tall along the coastal trail, its smooth red bark gleaming in the sunlight.
Locals collect the berries of Arbutus menziesii to make jams and preserves.
Furniture makers prize the wood of Arbutus menziesii for its strength and attractive grain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: madrone - another name for Arbutus menziesii
  • Degree Higher: arbutus - genus of broad-leaved evergreen trees and shrubs

Etymology

From Latin 'arbutus' (strawberry tree) and commemorating Archibald Menzies, a Scottish naturalist.
