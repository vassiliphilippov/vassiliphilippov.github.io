twill

1. NOUN

/ twɪl / (AmE), / twɪl / (BrE)
A strong fabric weave in which the threads are interlaced to produce a pattern of parallel diagonal ribs.
fabric weave with diagonal ribs

Examples

The designer chose a thick twill for the winter jackets to keep them warm and durable.
Denim jeans are often made using a classic twill weave.
He examined the subtle diagonal lines characteristic of twill fabrics under the bright light.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: plain weave - a basic weaving pattern with no diagonal effect

Etymology

From Old English 'twili' (double thread), from Latin 'bilix' (double-threaded).

2. VERB

/ twɪl / (AmE), / twɪl / (BrE)
to weave (textiles) so that they have a pattern of diagonal lines
weave cloth with diagonal lines

Examples

The artisan skillfully twilled the fabric to create a subtle pattern.
Some premium jeans are twilled to enhance their strength and appearance.
She demonstrated how to twill the threads using a traditional handloom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weave - to make cloth by crossing threads over and under each other

Etymology

From Old English 'twili' (double thread), related to Latin 'bilix' (two-threaded).

3. NOUN

/ twɪl / (AmE), / twɪl / (BrE)
A strong fabric with a distinctive diagonal pattern, produced by passing the weft thread over one or more warp threads and then under two or more warp threads in a regular sequence.
fabric with a diagonal rib pattern

Examples

She wore a smart jacket made of navy twill to the interview.
Classic jeans are often made from a sturdy cotton twill called denim.
You can recognize twill by its distinctive diagonal lines on the fabric's surface.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: denim - a durable twilled cotton fabric, typically blue
  • Antonym: plain weave - a simple woven fabric with no diagonal rib

Etymology

From Old French 'tuil' (woven fabric), related to Middle English 'twyll', from Old English 'twilic', ultimately from 'twī-' (two, double) + '-lic' (like).
