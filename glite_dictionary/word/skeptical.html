skeptical

1. ADJ

/ ˈskɛptɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈskɛptɪkəl / (BrE)
having or expressing doubt about accepted beliefs or claims, especially concerning religion; not easily convinced.
doubting accepted beliefs, especially religious claims

Examples

Her skeptical attitude made her question every religious story she heard.
Many scientists remain skeptical about claims of supernatural miracles.
He gave a skeptical look when asked to believe in the existence of ghosts.

Synset Examples

a skeptical approach to the nature of miracles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doubtful - feeling unsure or lacking conviction
  • Antonym: credulous - willing to believe things easily

Etymology

From Greek *skeptikos* (inquiring, to consider, to examine), via Latin.

2. ADJ

/ ˈskɛptɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈskɛptɪkəl / (BrE)
having or showing doubt about something; not easily convinced that something is true or real
doubtful; not easily convinced

Examples

She gave him a skeptical look when he explained his incredible story.
Many scientists remain skeptical about the effectiveness of the new treatment.
Despite his skeptical attitude, he decided to give the proposal a chance.

Synset Examples

a skeptical attitude
a skeptical listener

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cynical - believing people are motivated by self-interest; distrustful of sincerity
  • Antonym: credulous - willing to believe or trust too readily

Etymology

From Ancient Greek 'skeptikos' (thoughtful, inquiring, reflective).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02473075-s
Synset POS: s
