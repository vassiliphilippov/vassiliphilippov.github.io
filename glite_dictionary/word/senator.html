senator

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛn.ə.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.ə.tə / (BrE)
a member of a senate, especially in a legislative or governing body
member of a senate

Examples

The senator delivered a speech on healthcare reform in front of the packed chamber.
She was elected as the youngest senator in the country's history.
The senator met with local business leaders to discuss economic policy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legislator - a person who makes laws
  • Antonym: representative - a member of a legislative body, especially the lower house

Etymology

From Latin senator (member of a senate), from senatus (senate), from senex (old man).
