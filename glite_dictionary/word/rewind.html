to rewind

1. VERB

/ riˈwaɪnd / (AmE), / ˌriːˈwaɪnd / (BrE)
to wind a device, such as a watch or tape, back to its original position or start
wind back to start; return to initial position

Examples

I had to rewind the tape before playing it again.
Every night, he would rewind his old mechanical watch.
She forgot to rewind the film after taking the pictures.

Synset Examples

the mechanical watch needs rewinding every day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wind - to turn or coil something, especially a mechanism

Etymology

From re- (again) + wind (to turn or coil).
