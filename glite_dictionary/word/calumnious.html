calumnious

1. ADJ

/ kəˈlʌm.ni.əs / (AmE), / kəˈlʌm.ni.əs / (BrE)
(of statements or accusations) false and defamatory; intended to damage someone's reputation.
false and defamatory; slanderous

Examples

The politician denied making any calumnious remarks about her opponent.
Those calumnious allegations severely damaged his reputation in the community.
She filed a lawsuit over the calumnious statements published about her online.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slanderous - (of language) making false spoken statements damaging to a person's reputation
  • Synonym: libelous - (of language) containing written defamation
  • Antonym: praiseworthy - worthy of praise or approval

Etymology

From Latin 'calumniosus', from 'calumnia' meaning 'false accusation'.
