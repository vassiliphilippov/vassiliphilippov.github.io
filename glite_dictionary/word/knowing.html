knowing

1. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈnəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
A clear and certain understanding or awareness; the state of being aware or conscious of something.
clear and certain understanding

Examples

The philosopher spoke of a knowing that transcends words or logic.
Her deep knowing of the truth was evident despite her silence.
In times of uncertainty, I rely on my inner knowing to guide me.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knowledge - facts, information, and skills acquired through experience or education

Etymology

From the verb 'know', Old English 'cnāwan' (to know).
