homogenate

1. NOUN

/ hoʊˈmɑdʒəˌneɪt / (AmE), / həʊˈmɒdʒɪneɪt / (BrE)
a material, especially tissue, that has been mechanically ground and mixed to form a uniform blend, often used for laboratory analysis
material ground and mixed into a uniform blend
medical

Examples

After centrifuging the homogenate, researchers collected the supernatant for analysis.
The scientist prepared a liver homogenate to study enzyme activity.
Each sample was turned into a homogenate to ensure consistency during the tests.

Synset Examples

liver homogenate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: homogenized - having been made homogeneous, especially by mechanical means
  • Degree Lower: extract - a preparation containing the active ingredient of a substance in concentrated form

Etymology

From 'homogenize' (to make homogeneous) + '-ate' (denoting something that is the product of a process).
