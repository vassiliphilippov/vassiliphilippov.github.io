tent

1. NOUN

/ tɛnt / (AmE), / tɛnt / (BrE)
a web spun by certain insects, such as caterpillars, that resembles a tent or carpet in shape
web resembling a tent or carpet (entomology)

Examples

The caterpillars surrounded the tree branches with a thick tent spin.
The tent of webs protected the larvae from predators.
After a few days, the tent had grown to cover much of the shrub.
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09479040-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ tɛnt / (AmE), / tɛnt / (BrE)
a portable shelter made of cloth, supported by poles and ropes, and fastened to the ground, used especially for camping outdoors.
portable shelter for camping

Examples

We set up our tent under the trees near the river.
The children slept in a tent during their summer camping trip.
After the storm, their tent was blown over by the wind.

Synset Examples

he pitched his tent near the creek

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canopy - a covering fixed over a seat or shelter
  • Antonym: cabin - a small, simple house or shelter, often in the woods

Etymology

From Latin 'tentorium' (a tent), from 'tendere' (to stretch).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ tɛnt / (AmE), / tɛnt / (BrE)
to live or take shelter in a tent or as if in a tent, especially temporarily
to live or shelter in a tent

Examples

We tented by the lake for a week during our vacation.
They chose to tent in the backyard rather than book a hotel room.
During the festival, many visitors tent on the open fields.

Synset Examples

Can we go camping again this summer?
The circus tented near the town
The houseguests had to camp in the living room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: camp - to live temporarily in tents or temporary shelters
