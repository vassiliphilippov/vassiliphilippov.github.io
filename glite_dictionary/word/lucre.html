lucre

1. NOUN

/ ˈluːkɚ / (AmE), / ˈluːkə / (BrE)
money or profits, especially when regarded as gained in a dishonest or morally questionable way.
monetary profit (often with disapproval)

Examples

The scheme was more about lucre than community benefits.
She refused to sacrifice her principles for mere lucre.
Many are tempted by easy lucre despite the risks involved.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: profit - financial gain, especially the difference between the amount earned and the amount spent

Etymology

From Latin lucrum (gain, profit).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlukɚ / (AmE), / ˈljuːkə / (BrE)
money, especially when regarded as gained in a dishonest or morally questionable way
money, often from dubious sources
informal

Examples

He was motivated by the promise of easy lucre rather than genuine passion.
Politicians are often accused of seeking power for personal lucre.
The film depicts the underworld's obsession with illegal lucre.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: money - general term for currency and medium of exchange

Etymology

From Latin lucrum (gain, profit).
