to isomerize

1. VERB

/ aɪˈsɑːməˌraɪz / (AmE), / aɪˈsɒməraɪz / (BrE)
to convert a molecule into one of its isomers; to alter the arrangement of atoms in a compound to form an isomer
convert into an isomer (chemistry)

Examples

The enzyme catalyzes the reaction that isomerizes glucose-6-phosphate into fructose-6-phosphate.
Under certain conditions, heat can isomerize the compound and increase its reactivity.
Chemists were able to isomerize the molecule to obtain a more stable structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isomer - a molecule with the same formula but different arrangement of atoms

Etymology

From 'isomer' and the verb-forming suffix '-ize'. 'Isomer' is from Greek 'isos' (equal) + 'meros' (part).

2. VERB

/ ˈaɪsəməˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈaɪsəməˌraɪz / (BrE)
to cause (a compound) to change into one or more isomers
cause to become an isomer (chemistry)

Examples

The catalyst was used to isomerize the straight-chain hydrocarbon into its branched isomer.
Chemists can isomerize certain sugars under specific conditions.
Light can sometimes isomerize molecules, altering their chemical properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isomerization - the process of converting a compound into its isomer

Etymology

From isomer + -ize, based on chemical nomenclature.
