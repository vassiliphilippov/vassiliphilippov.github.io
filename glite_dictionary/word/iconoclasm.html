iconoclasm

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈkɑːnəˌklæzəm / (AmE), / aɪˈkɒnəˌklazəm / (BrE)
the belief in or practice of attacking or rejecting cherished beliefs and institutions or established values and practices; originally, the rejection or destruction of religious images or icons
rejection of cherished beliefs or icons

Examples

His outspoken iconoclasm shocked the conservative community.
The artist's iconoclasm challenged conventional ideas about beauty and form.
Religious iconoclasm led to the destruction of many valuable works of art.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iconoclast - a person who attacks or criticizes cherished beliefs or institutions

Etymology

From Greek eikonoklasmos, from eikon ('image') + klan ('to break').
