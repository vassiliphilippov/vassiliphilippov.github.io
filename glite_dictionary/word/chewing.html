chewing

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃuː.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtʃuː.ɪŋ / (BrE)
The action or process of biting and grinding food in the mouth to make it soft enough to swallow.
the action of biting and grinding food

Examples

Good chewing is important for proper digestion of food.
The loud chewing sounds distracted everyone at the table.
She reminded her son that careful chewing prevents choking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mastication - the process of chewing food
  • Degree Higher: swallowing - the act of making food pass from the mouth to the stomach

Etymology

From Middle English 'chewen' (to chew) + '-ing' (action or result).
