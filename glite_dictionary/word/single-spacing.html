single-spacing

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋɡəl ˌspeɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡl̩ ˌspeɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of typing or printing text so that each line of text is separated by the minimum space, with no blank lines in between
typing with no blank lines between lines

Examples

Please use single-spacing for the entire report unless otherwise specified.
The editor requested single-spacing to save paper during the review process.
Switching from single-spacing to double spacing made the document much longer.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: double-spacing - typing or printing with a full blank line between each line of text
