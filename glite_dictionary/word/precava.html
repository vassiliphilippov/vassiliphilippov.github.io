precava

1. NOUN

/ priˈkeɪvə / (AmE), / priˈkeɪvə / (BrE)
a large vein that receives blood from the head, arms, and chest and empties it into the right atrium of the heart; also known as the superior vena cava
large vein bringing blood to the heart
medical

Examples

A blockage in the precava can lead to serious circulatory problems.
The surgeon carefully examined the precava before proceeding with the operation.
Blood from the arm travels through the precava to reach the heart.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: superior vena cava - the main vein returning blood from the upper body to the heart
  • Antonym: postcava - the inferior vena cava, the main vein returning blood from the lower body to the heart

Etymology

From Latin prae- (before) + cava (hollow, vein), referring to its position relative to the heart.
