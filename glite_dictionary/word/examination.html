examination

1. NOUN

/ ɪɡˌzæməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪɡˌzæmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
an act of closely and reflectively reviewing one's thoughts, actions, or conscience, especially as a daily religious practice
detailed self-review, esp. religious

Examples

The Jesuit novice performed his nightly examination of conscience in silence.
A personal examination each day is an essential part of the religious routine.
During Lent, many people practice a thorough examination of their moral choices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-examination - the process of examining one's own thoughts and motives

Etymology

From Latin 'examinatio' (a weighing, examination), from 'examinare' (to weigh, examine).
