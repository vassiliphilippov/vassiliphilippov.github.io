manumitter

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæn.jəˈmɪt̬.ɚ / (AmE), / ˌmæn.juˈmɪt.ər / (BrE)
a person who frees another from slavery or bondage
person who frees a slave

Examples

The plantation owner's son became a noted manumitter in the region.
As a manumitter, she faced criticism from other landowners who opposed freeing slaves.
The history book discussed several influential manumitters who changed the fate of many families.

Synset Examples

Lincoln is known as the Great Emancipator

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emancipator - a person who frees another from bondage or oppression

Etymology

From Latin 'manumittere' (to free a slave), from 'manus' (hand) + 'mittere' (to send).
