scant

1. VERB

/ skænt / (AmE), / skænt / (BrE)
to deal with or provide something inadequately or superficially; to do something hastily without proper attention
deal with something inadequately or hastily

Examples

The contractor chose to scant the finishing details, leaving the work shoddy.
Do not scant your responsibilities if you want to earn trust.
She scanted her research in order to meet the deadline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neglect - to give little or no attention to something

Etymology

From Old Norse *skamt*, neuter of *skammr* (short).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02522103-v
Synset POS: v

2. ADJ

/ skænt / (AmE), / skænt / (BrE)
(of an amount) barely sufficient or less than what is needed, expected, or permitted.
barely sufficient; less than needed

Examples

She measured out a scant cup of flour for the recipe.
Due to budget cuts, the committee only gave a scant amount of funding.
The evidence provided was scant and failed to convince the jury.

Synset Examples

a light pound
a scant cup of sugar
regularly gives short weight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scarce - insufficient for the demand
  • Antonym: ample - more than enough; plentiful

Etymology

From Old Norse 'skamt' meaning 'scanty, short'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02345399-s
Synset POS: s

3. VERB

/ skænt / (AmE), / skɑːnt / (BrE)
to supply or provide with an insufficient or limited amount; to restrict the amount given
provide in restricted amounts

Examples

They were careful not to scant the soldiers' rations during the harsh winter.
He would never scant his friends when generosity was needed most.
Some companies scant their workers on benefits to save money.

Synset Examples

stint with the allowance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stint - to supply an inadequate amount of something

Etymology

From Old Norse 'skamt', meaning 'short in quantity or number'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02230521-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ skænt / (AmE), / skɑːnt / (BrE)
to provide or use less of something than is needed or is usual; to limit in amount or quality.
limit in quantity or quality

Examples

The committee chose to scant the funding for the project despite its importance.
He felt his achievements were scanted by his manager during the meeting.
Suppliers sometimes scant the quality of their products to save costs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limit - to restrict the amount or extent of something
  • Synonym: to restrict - to put a limit on; keep under control
  • Antonym: supply - to provide what is needed

Etymology

From Old Norse 'skamt', meaning 'short' or 'scanty'.
