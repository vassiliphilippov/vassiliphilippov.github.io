racketeer

1. NOUN

/ ˌræk.əˈtɪr / (AmE), / ˌræk.ɪˈtɪə / (BrE)
a person who engages in criminal activity, especially one who obtains money by fraud, extortion, or other illegal means
person involved in organized crime

Examples

The police arrested a notorious racketeer accused of running an extortion ring.
He started out as a small-time racketeer before joining a powerful crime syndicate.
Jurors heard testimony about how the racketeer laundered money through fake businesses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: criminal - a person who commits crimes
  • Antonym: law-abiding citizen - a person who obeys the law

Etymology

From 'racket' (dishonest scheme) + '-eer' (one who does).

WordNet ID: oewn-10522840-n

2. VERB

/ ˌrækəˈtɪr / (AmE), / ˌrækəˈtɪə / (BrE)
to engage in illegal business activities, especially by extorting money or running fraudulent enterprises
engage in illegal criminal business

Examples

Authorities arrested several men suspected to racketeer in the area for years.
The mob used threats and violence to racketeer money from local businesses.
If you racketeer, you risk severe legal consequences and prison time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to extort - to obtain something by force or threats

Etymology

From 'racketeer' (noun), derived from 'racket' (fraudulent scheme).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02450653-v
Synset POS: v
