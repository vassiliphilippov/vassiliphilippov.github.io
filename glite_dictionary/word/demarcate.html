to demarcate

1. VERB

/ dɪˈmɑːrˌkeɪt / (AmE), / diːˈmɑːkeɪt / (BrE)
to set or mark the boundaries or limits of something
set or mark boundaries

Examples

The surveyors will demarcate the property lines before construction begins.
A fence was erected to demarcate the two farms from one another.
Clear road markings demarcate the bicycle lane from the main traffic area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to delimit - set the limits or boundaries of something
  • Synonym: to define - mark out the boundary or limits of
  • Antonym: blur - make less distinct or clear (the opposite of setting clear boundaries)

Etymology

From Spanish 'demarcar', from 'de-' (off) + 'marcar' (to mark), ultimately from Latin 'marca' (mark).

2. VERB

/ diˈmɑrˌkeɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈmɑːkeɪt / (BrE)
to set the boundaries or limits of something; to separate clearly by marking boundaries
set boundaries or limits

Examples

The fence was built to demarcate the boundary between the two properties.
Clearly demarcating responsibilities ensures that every team member knows their role.
The river demarcates the official border between the two countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mark off - to separate or indicate boundaries

Etymology

From Spanish 'demarcar' (to mark out), from Latin 'de-' (off, down) + 'marcare' (to mark).
