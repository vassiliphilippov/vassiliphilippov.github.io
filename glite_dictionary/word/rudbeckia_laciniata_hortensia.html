Rudbeckia laciniata hortensia

1. NOUN

/ ɹʌdˈbɛkiə ləˌsɪniˈɑːtə hɔːˈtɛnsiə / (AmE), / ɹʌdˈbɛkɪə ləˌsɪnɪˈɑːtə hɔːˈtɛnsɪə / (BrE)
A very tall, branching herbaceous plant variety with showy, much-doubled yellow flower heads, cultivated as an ornamental.
ornamental plant with large yellow double flowers

Examples

The Rudbeckia laciniata hortensia in her garden bloomed with huge, eye-catching yellow flowers in late summer.
Gardeners prize Rudbeckia laciniata hortensia for its impressive height and long-lasting blooms.
We planted Rudbeckia laciniata hortensia along the back fence for a striking floral display.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Rudbeckia' honoring Swedish botanist Olof Rudbeck; 'laciniata' meaning 'torn' or 'slashed', referring to divided leaves; 'hortensia' possibly referring to gardens.
