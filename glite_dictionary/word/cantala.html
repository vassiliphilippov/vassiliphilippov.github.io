cantala

1. NOUN

/ kænˈtɑːlə / (AmE), / kanˈtɑːlə / (BrE)
A Philippine plant of the agave family (Agave cantala) that yields a strong, hard fiber used especially for making coarse twine and rope.
Philippine agave plant; source of hard fiber

Examples

The cantala is prized locally for its durable fiber used in industrial products.
Farmers harvested cantala to supply the rope-making factories.
Unlike sisal, cantala thrives best in certain Philippine climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sisal - a fiber from another agave species used similarly for rope and mats
  • Synonym: henequen - a fiber-producing agave plant related to cantala

Etymology

From Filipino (Tagalog) *kantála*, denoting the agave species.

2. NOUN

/ kænˈtɑːlə / (AmE), / kanˈtɑːlə / (BrE)
a hard fiber obtained from Philippine agave plants, used especially for making coarse twine and rope
Philippine agave fiber for twine/rope

Examples

The ropes were made from cantala due to its strength and durability.
Many traditional Filipino crafts use cantala as a raw material.
Demand for cantala increased as industries sought natural fibers for eco-friendly products.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sisal - a similar hard fiber from another agave plant, used for cordage

Etymology

From Spanish 'cantala', likely from Tagalog or related Philippine languages.
