flood

1. NOUN

/ flʌd / (AmE), / flʌd / (BrE)
The act of flooding, especially filling or covering with water beyond normal limits or to overflowing.
the act of flooding or overflowing

Examples

The sudden flood destroyed the crops and forced villagers to evacuate.
A broken water main caused a flood in the basement.
The stadium was at risk when the river threatened to flood the surrounding area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deluge - a severe flood
  • Antonym: drought - a prolonged period without rain

Etymology

Old English 'flōd' (a flowing of water, deluge), from Germanic roots.
