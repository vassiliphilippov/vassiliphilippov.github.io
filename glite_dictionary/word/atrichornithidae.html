Atrichornithidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌætrɪkɔːrˈnɪθɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌætrɪkˌɔːnɪˈθɪːdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, shy birds known as scrubbirds, native to Australia.
family of Australian scrubbirds

Examples

The Atrichornithidae are elusive, ground-dwelling birds found in the forests of Australia.
Researchers have studied the vocalizations of several Atrichornithidae species.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect the rare Western Atrichornithidae from extinction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrubbird - a small, secretive Australian bird of the family Atrichornithidae

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining Greek 'a-' (without), 'trichos' (hair), and 'ornis' (bird), referring to the birds' lack of bristles on their heads.

2. NOUN

/ ˌætrɪkɔrˈnɪθɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌatrɪkɔːˈnɪθɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of Australian passerine birds known as scrubbirds, characterized by their secretive habits and powerful songs.
family of Australian scrubbirds

Examples

The Atrichornithidae are rare, ground-dwelling birds native to the forests of Australia.
Ornithologists study the unique vocalizations of the Atrichornithidae family.
Habitat loss is a major threat to the survival of Atrichornithidae species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrubbird - a bird of the genus Atrichornis, member of Atrichornithidae

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'a-' (without), 'trichos' (hair), and 'ornis' (bird), referring to their bristleless faces.
