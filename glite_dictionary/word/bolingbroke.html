Bolingbroke

1. PROPN

/ ˈboʊlɪŋˌbroʊk / (AmE), / ˈbəʊlɪŋˌbrʊk / (BrE)
the title used for Henry IV, the first Lancastrian king of England (1399–1413), who deposed Richard II and suppressed several rebellions.
Henry IV of England (king 1399–1413)

Examples

In Shakespeare's play, Bolingbroke emerges as a cunning and ambitious noble.
Bolingbroke was crowned King Henry IV after seizing the throne from Richard II.
The reign of Bolingbroke was marked by political turmoil and frequent uprisings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Henry IV - King of England from 1399 to 1413, also known as Bolingbroke

Etymology

From the place name Bolingbroke in Lincolnshire, England. Used as a title for Henry IV.
