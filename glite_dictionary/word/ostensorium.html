ostensorium

1. NOUN

/ ɑːˌstɛnˈsɔriəm / (AmE), / ɒˌstɛnˈsɔːriəm / (BrE)
(in the Roman Catholic Church) a vessel, usually made of gold or silver, in which the consecrated Host is displayed for adoration.
vessel for displaying the consecrated Host

Examples

The priest placed the consecrated Host in the ostensorium for all to see during the ceremony.
An ornate ostensorium was carried in the procession through the church.
The gold-plated ostensorium was a gift from a parishioner in the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monstrance - a vessel used to display the consecrated Host, especially in Roman Catholic rites

Etymology

From Medieval Latin ostensorium, from Latin ostendere (to show).
