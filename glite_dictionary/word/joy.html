joy

1. NOUN

/ dʒɔɪ / (AmE), / dʒɔɪ / (BrE)
a feeling of great happiness or pleasure
a feeling of great happiness

Examples

She was filled with joy when she heard the good news.
The birth of their child brought them immense joy.
He jumped for joy when his team won the championship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delight - great pleasure or satisfaction
  • Antonym: sorrow - a feeling of deep distress caused by loss, disappointment, or other misfortune
  • Degree Higher: bliss - perfect happiness; great joy
  • Degree Lower: happiness - the state of being happy

Etymology

From Old French *joie*, from Latin *gaudia* (joys), plural of *gaudium* (joy, delight).
