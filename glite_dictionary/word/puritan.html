Puritan

1. NOUN

/ ˈpjʊrətən / (AmE), / ˈpjʊərɪtən / (BrE)
a member of a group of English Protestants in the 16th and 17th centuries who sought to simplify religious practices and promote strict morality
member of strict English Protestant group

Examples

The Puritans played a major role in shaping early American society.
She studied the influence of Puritan beliefs on English literature.
As a Puritan, he rejected elaborate church rituals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Calvinist - a follower of the teachings of John Calvin in the Protestant Reformation

Etymology

From Late Latin *puritanus* (pure), relating to 'purity'.
