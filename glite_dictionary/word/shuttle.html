shuttle

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃʌt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈʃʌtl̩ / (BrE)
A light, feathered or synthetic object with a rounded base used as a projectile in the game of badminton.
badminton projectile; feathered object

Examples

She served the shuttle expertly over the net.
The player accidentally crushed the shuttle during the match.
You need a new shuttle if the feathers get damaged.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shuttlecock - feathered projectile used in badminton
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04219349-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃʌtəl / (AmE), / ˈʃʌt.əl / (BrE)
A device in a loom that carries the weft thread back and forth between the warp threads during weaving.
device in loom for carrying weft

Examples

The shuttle moved swiftly across the loom, weaving the fabric together.
She had to replace the shuttle after it wore out from constant use.
Traditional handlooms use a wooden shuttle to pass the thread between the warp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bobbin - A cylinder or spool on which thread is wound

Etymology

From Old English scytel (dart, missile), related to shoot, reflecting its movement.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04218924-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈʃʌt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈʃʌtl̩ / (BrE)
a bus, train, or aircraft that travels frequently between two places, usually making repeated trips back and forth
transport that runs back and forth between two points

Examples

The airport shuttle leaves every 30 minutes from the arrivals terminal.
We took the hotel shuttle to the convention center.
A shuttle service runs between the city center and the train station.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commuter train - train used for regular journeys between home and work
  • Synonym: ferry - boat or ship transporting people or vehicles across water regularly

Etymology

Originally from Middle English 'shutel', from Old English 'scytel' (dart, missile); later applied to transportation devices moving back and forth.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04219037-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈʃʌt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈʃʌtl̩ / (BrE)
to travel frequently back and forth between two places, often as part of a regular service
travel back and forth repeatedly

Examples

Each day, she shuttles between her home and the city for work.
A small boat shuttled passengers back and forth across the river all day.
The company shuttles its employees between the main office and the remote warehouse every hour.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commute - to travel regularly between home and work

Etymology

From the noun 'shuttle', originally referring to the weaving instrument, by analogy to its back-and-forth movement.
