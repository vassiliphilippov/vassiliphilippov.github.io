walnut

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔl.nʌt / (AmE), / ˈwɔːl.nʌt / (BrE)
the edible nut of a walnut tree, with a wrinkled, two-lobed seed and a hard shell
edible nut of a walnut tree

Examples

She sprinkled chopped walnut pieces over her oatmeal for added crunch.
The recipe calls for one cup of crushed walnut to give the cake a nutty flavor.
He cracked open the walnut with a nutcracker and enjoyed the fresh seed inside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pecan - nut from the pecan tree, similar family to walnuts
  • Synonym: almond - edible seed from the almond tree, similar kind of nut

Etymology

Old English *walhnutu*, from Proto-Germanic *walhaz* (foreign) and *hnut* (nut), originally meaning a foreign or Roman nut.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔl.nət / (AmE), / ˈwɔːl.nʌt / (BrE)
the hard, dark-brown wood of a walnut tree, used especially for making furniture and paneling
hard wood from walnut trees, used in furniture

Examples

The cabinet was made from polished walnut and had intricate carvings.
They chose walnut for the flooring because of its color and durability.
An antique desk crafted from walnut adds elegance to the study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mahogany - hard reddish-brown wood used for furniture

Etymology

From Old English *walhhnutu*, from *wealh* (foreign) + *hnutu* (nut), originally referring to the nut; later extended to tree and wood.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-12339132-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈwɔlˌnʌt / (AmE), / ˈwɔːlnʌt / (BrE)
a deciduous tree of the genus Juglans, known for its pinnate leaves and edible nuts
type of tree (Juglans)

Examples

The tall walnut in our backyard provides shade throughout the summer.
Some people plant walnut trees for their valuable timber and nuts.
Squirrels often collect the walnut fruit before it falls to the ground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walnut - the edible nut from the walnut tree

Etymology

From Old English *walhnutu*, meaning 'foreign nut' (walh + nutu).
