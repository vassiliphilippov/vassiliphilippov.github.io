doctorate

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑktərət / (AmE), / ˈdɒktərət / (BrE)
one of the highest academic degrees awarded by a university, typically requiring original research
highest academic degree

Examples

After years of study, she finally received her doctorate in biology.
A doctorate typically requires the completion of a dissertation based on original research.
He was proud to add a doctorate to his academic achievements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: PhD - a specific type of doctorate, Doctor of Philosophy
  • Degree Lower: master's degree - an academic degree lower than a doctorate, usually awarded after a bachelor's

Etymology

From Latin doctoratus (the degree of a doctor), from doctor (teacher).
