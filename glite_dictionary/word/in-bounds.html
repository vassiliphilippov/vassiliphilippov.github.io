in-bounds

1. ADJ

/ ɪn ˈbaʊndz / (AmE), / ɪn ˈbaʊndz / (BrE)
(in sports, especially baseball) inside the area between the two boundary lines, such as the first and third base lines, where play is allowed.
within boundary lines in sports

Examples

The coach reminded the players to stay in-bounds during the drill.
If the ball remains in-bounds, it is still in play.
The runner was careful to touch the base while staying in-bounds.

Synset Examples

the ball landed in fair territory
the left fielder stayed in bounds to make the catch

Related Terms

  • Antonym: out-of-bounds - outside the boundary lines where play is not allowed
