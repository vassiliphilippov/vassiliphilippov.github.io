labile

1. ADJ

/ ˈleɪˌbaɪl / (AmE), / ˈleɪbʌɪl / (BrE)
(of a substance or system, especially in chemistry or biology) readily undergoing change or breakdown; unstable or easily altered.
unstable; easily changed or broken down
medical

Examples

The enzyme is labile at high temperatures, making it unsuitable for certain industrial processes.
Iron in its +2 oxidation state is more labile than in the +3 state.
Some neurotransmitters are labile and degrade easily outside the nervous system.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stable - not likely to change; resistant to change or breakdown
  • Synonym: unstable - prone to change or breakdown; not stable

Etymology

From Latin 'labilis' (liable to slip, unstable).

2. ADJ

/ ˈleɪ.baɪl, ˈlæ.baɪl / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.baɪl, ˈlæ.baɪl / (BrE)
likely to change rapidly or easily; unstable.
liable to change; unstable

Examples

The patient's mood was labile, shifting from tears to laughter within minutes.
This compound is labile when exposed to heat or light, so store it in a cool, dark place.
After a period of stability, his blood pressure became labile and hard to control.

Synset Examples

an emotionally labile person

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stable - firmly fixed; not likely to change

Etymology

From Latin 'labilis' (liable to slip, unstable), from 'labi' (to slip).
