to compartmentalise

1. VERB

/ kəmˌpɑrtˈmɛntəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / kəmˌpɑːtˈmentəlaɪz / (BrE)
to separate something into distinct sections, categories, or compartments, often so that each is isolated from the others
divide into separate sections
british

Examples

It's not healthy to compartmentalise your emotions and refuse to discuss your feelings.
She tends to compartmentalise her work life and personal life to avoid stress.
Managers often compartmentalise tasks to improve efficiency within teams.

Synset Examples

You cannot compartmentalize your life like this!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to compartmentalize - to separate into categories or compartments (U.S. spelling)

Etymology

From 'compartment' + '-alise', modeled after 'compartmentalize' (U.S. spelling), ultimately from Latin 'compartiri' (to divide).
