savorlessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪvərləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈseɪvələsnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of having no flavor; lack of taste
absence of flavor or taste

Examples

The savorlessness of the soup left the diners unimpressed and disappointed.
Some people complain about the savorlessness of mass-produced foods.
He added extra herbs to counteract the savorlessness of the boiled vegetables.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insipidity - lack of flavor; blandness
  • Antonym: flavor - distinctive taste of a substance or food

Etymology

From 'savor' (meaning taste or flavor) + '-less' (without) + '-ness' (state or quality).
