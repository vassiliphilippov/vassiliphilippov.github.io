to abnegate

1. VERB

/ ˈæb.nɪˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈab.nɪ.ɡeɪt / (BrE)
to deliberately deny oneself something; to restrain oneself, especially from indulging in pleasures
to deny oneself; to restrain

Examples

She chose to abnegate luxury in order to live a simpler life.
Many monks abnegate worldly pleasures as part of their spiritual practice.
He decided to abnegate his desire for sweets during the health retreat.

Synset Examples

She denied herself wine and spirits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to abstain - to deliberately refrain from something, especially pleasurable activities
  • Antonym: to indulge - to allow oneself to enjoy pleasure

Etymology

From Latin abnegare (to refuse, deny), from ab- (away) + negare (to deny).

2. VERB

/ ˈæb.nɪ.ˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈab.nɪ.ɡeɪt / (BrE)
to formally give up or renounce (power, rights, or a position)
formally surrender power or position

Examples

After years as chairman, he decided to abnegate his authority and let others lead.
The monarch was pressured to abnegate the throne during the political crisis.
In her farewell address, she formally abnegated her position as CEO of the company.

Synset Examples

The King abnegated his power to the ministers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to renounce - to formally give up a right, claim, or position
  • Synonym: to relinquish - to voluntarily give up something

Etymology

From Latin abnegare (to refuse, deny), from ab- (away) + negare (to deny).

3. VERB

/ ˈæb.nəˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈab.nɪ.ɡeɪt / (BrE)
to renounce or reject something, especially a belief, cause, or habit
to renounce or reject

Examples

He chose to abnegate all personal desires for the sake of his family.
Some religious traditions encourage followers to abnegate worldly pleasures.
After years of addiction, she decided to abnegate harmful habits and start anew.

Synset Examples

They abnegated their gods

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to renounce - to formally declare one's abandonment of a claim, right, or possession
  • Synonym: reject - to refuse to accept, believe in, or agree with something
  • Antonym: embrace - to accept or support something willingly or enthusiastically

Etymology

From Latin abnegare (to refuse, deny), from ab- (away) + negare (to deny).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
