loquaciously

1. ADV

/ loʊˈkweɪʃəsli / (AmE), / ləˈkweɪʃəsli / (BrE)
in a very talkative or chatty manner; tending to talk a great deal
in a talkative manner

Examples

She loquaciously recounted every detail of the event at the dinner table.
The host loquaciously guided the guests through the exhibits, sharing countless anecdotes.
He loquaciously described his travels, rarely pausing for breath.

Synset Examples

‘When I was young,’ she continued loquaciously, ‘I used to do all sorts of naughty things’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: talkatively - in a talkative manner

Etymology

Formed from 'loquacious' + '-ly'; 'loquacious' from Latin 'loquax' (talkative), from 'loqui' (to speak).
