Holocaust

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊləkɔst / (AmE), / ˈhɒləkɒst / (BrE)
the systematic mass murder of Jews and other groups by the Nazi regime in Germany from 1941 to 1945
mass murder of Jews by Nazis, 1941–45

Examples

Many survivors of the Holocaust later emigrated to the United States and Israel.
The museum is dedicated to preserving the memory of the victims of the Holocaust.
Teaching about the Holocaust is an important part of modern history curriculum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genocide - the deliberate killing of a large group of people, especially those of a particular nation or ethnic group
  • Synonym: pogrom - an organized massacre of a particular ethnic group, especially Jews
  • Degree Lower: Holodomor - the genocide by starvation of Ukrainians in the Soviet Union

Etymology

From Greek holokauston, meaning 'completely burned', originally referring to a burnt offering.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːləˌkɔst / (AmE), / ˈhɒləkɒst / (BrE)
the systematic genocide of Jews and other groups by the Nazis during World War II, resulting in millions of deaths
genocide of Jews in World War II

Examples

The Holocaust claimed the lives of approximately six million Jews across Europe.
Many survivors of the Holocaust shared their stories to educate future generations.
The city built a memorial to honor the victims of the Holocaust.

Synset Examples

The museum educates visitors about the horrors of the Holocaust.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genocide - the deliberate killing of a large group of people, especially of a particular nation or ethnic group
  • Synonym: pogrom - an organized massacre, especially of Jews
  • Synonym: persecution - persistent abuse and harassment, especially for religious or political reasons

Etymology

From Greek holokauston, meaning 'a thing wholly burnt', used in English since the 17th century and later applied to this historical event.
