spirometry

1. NOUN

/ spaɪˈrɑːmətri / (AmE), / spaɪˈrɒmətri / (BrE)
the measurement of lung capacity and function, typically by means of a spirometer
measurement of lung capacity
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended spirometry to assess the patient's lung function after his respiratory illness.
Regular spirometry is essential for monitoring chronic obstructive pulmonary disease.
She performed a spirometry test during her annual physical check-up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spirometer - an instrument for measuring lung capacity

Etymology

From Latin 'spiro' (to breathe) + Greek 'metron' (measure).
