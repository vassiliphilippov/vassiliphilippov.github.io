kayak

1. VERB

/ ˈkaɪ.æk / (AmE), / ˈkaɪ.æk / (BrE)
to travel or move in a kayak, a small, narrow boat propelled by a double-bladed paddle.
to travel in a small canoe

Examples

Last summer, we kayaked along the rugged coastline in Alaska.
She loves to kayak on the lake early in the morning when it's quiet.
They kayaked down the river, enjoying the scenery and wildlife on both banks.

Synset Examples

we kayaked down the river

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canoe - to travel or move in a canoe

Etymology

From Greenlandic 'qajaq' (small boat used by Inuit).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkaɪ.æk / (AmE), / ˈkaɪ.æk / (BrE)
a small, narrow boat with a light frame and watertight covering, traditionally made from animal skins, propelled with a double-bladed paddle; originally used by Inuit and other Arctic peoples
a small, narrow, watertight boat

Examples

They paddled their kayak down the river, enjoying the beautiful scenery.
A traditional kayak is covered with sealskin to keep out the water.
She learned how to maneuver a kayak during her summer in Alaska.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canoe - a light, narrow boat pointed at both ends and usually open on top, propelled by paddles

Etymology

From Greenlandic 'qajaq', referring to the traditional boat of the Inuit.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03614448-n
Synset POS: n
