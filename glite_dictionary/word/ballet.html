ballet

1. NOUN

/ bæˈleɪ / (AmE), / ˈbæleɪ / (BrE)
music composed specifically to accompany a ballet performance.
music for a ballet performance

Examples

Tchaikovsky's ballet 'Swan Lake' is famous for its rich orchestration.
Prokofiev composed the ballet 'Romeo and Juliet' in 1935.
Many students study Stravinsky's ballet 'The Firebird' in their music classes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ballet score - the written musical notation for ballet music

Etymology

From French 'ballet', from Italian 'balletto', diminutive of 'ballo' (dance).

2. NOUN

/ bæˈleɪ / (AmE), / ˈbæleɪ / (BrE)
A theatrical dance performance in which a story is told through expressive and highly formalized movements set to music.
theatrical dance performance

Examples

The Moscow company performed the famous ballet 'Swan Lake' to a packed audience.
She has been training in ballet since she was six years old.
The school's annual ballet attracts dance enthusiasts from all over the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dance - an act of moving rhythmically to music, typically following a set sequence of steps

Etymology

From French 'ballet', from Italian 'balletto', diminutive of 'ballo' (dance).
