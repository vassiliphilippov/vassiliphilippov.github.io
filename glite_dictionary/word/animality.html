animality

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈmæləti / (AmE), / ˌænɪˈmalɪti / (BrE)
the physical or instinctual side of human nature, as opposed to the spirit or intellect
physical or instinctual side of human nature

Examples

The novel explores the conflict between human intellect and animality.
He struggled to keep his animality in check and act rationally.
Some philosophers argue that our animality plays a crucial role in shaping our behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: animal nature - basic physical or instinctive side of a person
  • Antonym: spirituality - the quality of being concerned with the human spirit or soul

Etymology

From Latin animalitas (quality of being an animal), from animal.
