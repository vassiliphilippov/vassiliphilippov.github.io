wrapper

1. NOUN

/ ˈræpər / (AmE), / ˈræpə / (BrE)
a loose outer garment, especially a lightweight dressing gown worn by women.
loose dressing gown for women

Examples

After her bath, she slipped into her wrapper before heading to the kitchen.
The elegant silk wrapper was a gift from her grandmother.
She lounged on the sofa in her floral wrapper reading a novel.

2. NOUN

/ ˈræpər / (AmE), / ˈræpə / (BrE)
a loose cloak, shawl, or garment that is folded or wrapped around a person, typically for warmth or modesty
loose garment wrapped around a person

Examples

She draped a wrapper around her shoulders before stepping onto the porch.
The old photograph shows women in colorful wrappers at the market.
He handed her a thick wrapper to keep her warm in the cool evening air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shawl - a piece of fabric worn over the shoulders or head

Etymology

From wrap + -er, denoting something that wraps.

3. NOUN

/ ˈræpər / (AmE), / ˈræpə / (BrE)
a piece of paper, cellophane, or other material used to cover or enclose something, especially food or small items
covering material for wrapping items

Examples

He threw the candy wrapper in the bin after finishing the sweet.
The sandwich was still fresh inside its plastic wrapper.
Please remove the wrapper before eating the chocolate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cover - something that lies on, over, or around something else
  • Antonym: unwrapped - not covered with a wrapper or not enclosed

Etymology

From wrap + -er, meaning something that wraps.
