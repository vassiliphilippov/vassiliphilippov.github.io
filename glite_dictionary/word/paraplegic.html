paraplegic

1. ADJ

/ ˌpærəˈpliːdʒɪk / (AmE), / ˌpærəˈpliːdʒɪk / (BrE)
Suffering from complete paralysis of the lower half of the body, typically due to damage to the spinal cord.
person with lower-body paralysis
medical

Examples

After the accident, she became a paraplegic and had to use a wheelchair to get around.
The hospital has special facilities to support paraplegic patients during rehabilitation.
As a paraplegic athlete, he competed in the Paralympic Games.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: quadriplegic - person paralyzed in all four limbs
  • Antonym: able-bodied - not affected by any physical disability

Etymology

From French 'paraplégique', from Greek 'paraplēgikos' (paralyzed on one side).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpærəˈplidʒɪk / (AmE), / ˌpærəˈpliːdʒɪk / (BrE)
a person who is paralyzed from the waist down, typically as a result of spinal cord injury or disease
person paralyzed from the waist down
medical

Examples

After the accident, John became a paraplegic and had to use a wheelchair.
The event organizers ensured all facilities were accessible to every paraplegic participant.
She is a talented athlete and a proud paraplegic who has won several medals.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: quadriplegic - a person paralyzed in both arms and both legs
  • Antonym: able-bodied - a person who is not disabled; antonym of physically disabled

Etymology

From French 'paraplégique', from Greek 'paraplēgikos' (relating to paralysis).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
