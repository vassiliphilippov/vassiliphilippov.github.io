to harass

1. VERB

/ həˈræs, ˈhærəs / (AmE), / həˈræs, ˈhærəs / (BrE)
To repeatedly attack or pressure an enemy or opponent in order to exhaust or wear them down.
to attack repeatedly to exhaust

Examples

The cavalry would harass the enemy from the flanks during the battle.
They continued to harass the supply lines until reinforcements arrived.
Smaller ships were sent to harass the larger vessels throughout the night.

Synset Examples

harass the enemy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attack - to take aggressive action against
  • Synonym: to pester - to trouble or annoy persistently

Etymology

From Old French *harasser* (to tire, vex), of uncertain origin.

2. VERB

/ həˈræs / (AmE), / həˈrɑːs / (BrE)
to annoy or trouble someone repeatedly or persistently, often making them feel worried or uncomfortable
annoy or trouble persistently

Examples

The manager was accused of trying to harass employees who disagreed with company policy.
Some people use social media to harass others anonymously.
She threatened to call the police if her neighbor continued to harass her.

Synset Examples

He is known to harry his staff when he is overworked
This man harasses his female co-workers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pester - to annoy someone repeatedly with requests or interruptions
  • Synonym: torment - to cause severe mental or physical suffering
  • Antonym: comfort - to ease someone's distress or suffering

Etymology

From French *harasser*, of uncertain origin, possibly from Old French *harer* (to set a dog on).
