trifoliata

1. NOUN

/ trɪˌfoʊliˈɑːtə / (AmE), / trɪˌfəʊliˈɑːtə / (BrE)
A small, fast-growing, spiny deciduous tree native to China, with sweetly scented flowers and inedible, decorative fruit; often used as rootstock for grafting and for hedges.
Chinese spiny orange tree used for grafting

Examples

The trifoliata is often grown as a hardy rootstock for citrus trees in cooler climates.
Hedges of trifoliata can deter animals due to their strong thorns.
Although the fruit of the trifoliata is decorative, it is generally regarded as inedible.

Etymology

From Latin 'trifoliatus', meaning 'three-leaved', referring to its leaves divided into three leaflets.
