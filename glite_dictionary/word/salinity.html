salinity

1. NOUN

/ səˈlɪnəti / (AmE), / səˈlɪnɪti / (BrE)
the amount of salt present in a solution, especially in water or soil
amount of salt in a solution

Examples

The salinity of ocean water affects marine life in various ways.
Irrigating with high salinity water can damage crops over time.
Scientists measured the salinity of the lake to track environmental changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saltiness - the quality of being salty; having a salty taste or character

Etymology

From Latin salinitas (saltiness), from sal (salt).

2. NOUN

/ səˈlɪnəti / (AmE), / səˈlɪnɪti / (BrE)
the degree to which a substance or solution tastes salty; the sensation of saltiness
the saltiness or salty taste

Examples

The salinity of the soup overwhelmed the other flavors.
She noticed a distinct salinity as she tasted the seawater.
Over time, the salinity in the homemade pickles increased and made them too salty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saltiness - the quality of tasting salty

Etymology

From Latin salinitas (saltiness), from sal (salt).
