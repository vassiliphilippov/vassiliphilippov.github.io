rigorousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪɡərəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈrɪɡərəsnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being extremely thorough, strict, or severe; excessive sternness.
quality of being strict or severe

Examples

The rigorousness of the training program pushed many recruits to their limits.
Critics have noted the rigorousness of the new regulations imposed by the council.
She admired the rigorousness with which the scientist conducted his experiments.

Synset Examples

severity of character
the harshness of his punishment was inhuman
the rigors of boot camp

Related Terms

  • Synonym: severity - the quality of being severe
  • Antonym: leniency - the quality of being more merciful or less harsh

Etymology

From rigorous + -ness, based on Latin rigor (stiffness, severity).

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɪɡərəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈrɪɡərəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being extremely harsh, severe, or difficult to endure
extreme severity or harshness

Examples

The rigorousness of the military training pushed many recruits to their limits.
Only the strongest plants can survive the rigorousness of an alpine winter.
She was taken aback by the rigorousness of the exam, which tested every detail of the syllabus.

Synset Examples

the asperity of northern winters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rigor - severity or strictness
  • Antonym: gentleness - kindness or mildness rather than severity

Etymology

From rigorous + -ness, ultimately from Latin rigor (stiffness, severity).
