Tanacetum vulgare

1. NOUN

/ təˈnæsətəm vʌlˈgɛəri / (AmE), / təˈnæsɪtəm vʌlˈgɛəri / (BrE)
A perennial aromatic herb native to Eurasia, with yellow button-like flower heads and bitter-tasting pinnate leaves, sometimes used medicinally.
aromatic Eurasian perennial herb (tansy)

Examples

The garden was dotted with clusters of Tanacetum vulgare in full bloom.
Traditional remedies sometimes included extracts from Tanacetum vulgare leaves.
We planted Tanacetum vulgare along the fence for its bright flowers and aromatic scent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tansy - common name for Tanacetum vulgare

Etymology

From Latin: Tanacetum (tansy) + vulgare (common).
