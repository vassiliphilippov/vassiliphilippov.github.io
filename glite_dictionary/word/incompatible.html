incompatible

1. ADJ

/ ɪn.kəmˈpæt.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ɪn.kəmˈpæt.ɪ.bəl / (BrE)
(of drugs, muscles, etc.) counteracting or neutralizing each other's effects; unable to function together harmoniously, especially in a medical or physiological context.
unable to work together (medical, drugs, muscles)
medical

Examples

These two antibiotics are incompatible and should not be administered together.
When taken together, the drugs have incompatible effects that can reduce their effectiveness.
Certain muscle groups are incompatible and cannot contract at the same time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antagonistic - acting in opposition, especially in a physiological or pharmacological sense
  • Antonym: compatible - able to exist or occur together without conflict

Etymology

From Latin incompatibilis, from in- (not) + compatibilis (compatible).

Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪnkəmˈpætəbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪnkəmˈpætəbəl / (BrE)
(of substances) incapable of blending into a stable homogeneous mixture, especially used for solids or solutions.
not able to mix homogeneously

Examples

Water and oil are incompatible and will not form a uniform solution.
Some chemicals are incompatible and must never be mixed for safety reasons.
The paints proved incompatible, resulting in a lumpy and uneven surface.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: compatible - (of substances) able to mix or blend without separation or reaction

Etymology

From Latin incommodus (inconvenient, unsuitable) via Medieval Latin incompatibilis (not compatible).
