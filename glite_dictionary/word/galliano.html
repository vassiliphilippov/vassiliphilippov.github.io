Galliano

1. NOUN

/ ɡæliˈɑːnoʊ / (AmE), / ɡalɪˈɑːnəʊ / (BrE)
a golden Italian liqueur flavored with herbs and vanilla, often used in cocktails
Italian herbal liqueur

Examples

The bartender added a splash of Galliano to the cocktail, giving it a rich golden hue.
My favorite digestif is Galliano because of its unique blend of herbs and spices.
He ordered a Harvey Wallbanger, which is made with vodka, orange juice, and Galliano.

Etymology

From the name of Arturo Vaccari's liqueur creation, named after Italian war hero Giuseppe Galliano.
