wardress

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔrˌdrɛs / (AmE), / ˈwɔːdrɪs / (BrE)
a woman who is in charge of, or works as a guard in, a women's prison or section of a prison
female prison warder
archaic

Examples

The wardress patrolled the corridors, ensuring all the inmates were safely locked in their cells.
It was the wardress who first noticed the missing prisoner during the morning roll call.
In the memoir, the protagonist recalls a particularly strict wardress from her days in prison.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warder - person who guards prisoners
  • Synonym: jailer - person in charge of prisoners in a jail

Etymology

From 'warder' (prison guard) + feminine suffix '-ess'.
