Typhon

1. PROPN

/ ˈtaɪ.fɒn / (AmE), / ˈtʌɪ.fən / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, a monstrous giant with a hundred heads who breathed fire, considered the father of Cerberus, the Chimera, and the Sphinx.
mythical fire-breathing monster in Greek mythology

Examples

The legend says that Typhon fought Zeus in a battle that shook the heavens.
According to the myth, Typhon was the father of several other famous monsters.
Artists often depict Typhon with serpentine coils and fire-breathing heads.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Τυφῶν (Tuphôn).
