furniture

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɝnɪtʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɜːnɪtʃə / (BrE)
large movable objects, such as chairs, tables, and beds, used to make a room suitable for living or working in
movable items for a room or building

Examples

We bought new furniture for the living room when we moved in.
This store offers a wide selection of office furniture to fit every budget.
The apartment looked empty without any furniture in it.

Synset Examples

they had too much furniture for the small apartment
there was only one piece of furniture in the room

Etymology

From French 'fourniture' (a supply, the action of furnishing), from 'fournir' (to furnish), from Old French 'fournir'.
