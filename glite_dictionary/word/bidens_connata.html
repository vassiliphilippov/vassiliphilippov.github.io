Bidens connata

1. NOUN

/ baɪˈdɛnz kəˈneɪtə / (AmE), / bɪˈdɛnz kəˈnɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of bur marigold native to eastern and northern United States and Canada, commonly found in wet pastures and meadows.
a North American bur marigold plant

Examples

Bidens connata thrives in wetlands across eastern North America, often forming dense colonies.
Botanists surveyed a meadow and recorded the presence of Bidens connata among other wildflowers.
The hooked seeds of Bidens connata easily stick to clothing and animal fur.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bur marigold - common name for several species of plants in the genus Bidens

Etymology

From Latin: 'Bidens' meaning 'two-toothed' (referring to the shape of the seeds) and 'connata', meaning 'joined together'.
