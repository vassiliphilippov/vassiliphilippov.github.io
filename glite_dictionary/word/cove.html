cove

1. NOUN

/ koʊv / (AmE), / kəʊv / (BrE)
A small, sheltered bay or inlet along a coastline.
small sheltered bay or inlet

Examples

The kayak glided silently into the cove, hidden from the open sea.
We spent the afternoon swimming in a quiet cove along the coast.
Seagulls circled above the rocky cove at sunrise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bay - a broad inlet of the sea where the land curves inward
  • Degree Higher: gulf - a deep inlet of the sea almost surrounded by land

Etymology

From Old English 'cofa' (chamber, den, cave).

2. NOUN

/ koʊv / (AmE), / kəʊv / (BrE)
A small or narrow cave in the side of a cliff or mountain.
small cave in cliff or mountain

Examples

The explorers discovered a hidden cove in the side of the mountain.
They took shelter from the wind inside a narrow cove high on the cliff.
Hikers often stop and rest in the natural cove before continuing their climb.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cave - a large natural underground chamber

Etymology

From Old English *cofa* (chamber, cave, den).
