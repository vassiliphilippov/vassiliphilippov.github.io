farthing

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑrðɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɑːðɪŋ / (BrE)
a former British bronze coin worth a quarter of a penny, used until 1960
former British coin, quarter of a penny
british archaic

Examples

Back then, you could buy a loaf of bread for just a farthing.
The collector was pleased to find an 1890 farthing in excellent condition.
My grandmother remembers when a farthing was still accepted in shops.

Etymology

From Old English feorðing (a fourth part), from feorða (fourth).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɑrðɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɑːðɪŋ / (BrE)
A former British coin and monetary unit equal to one quarter of a penny; used figuratively to mean a very small amount of money or anything considered negligible.
a former small British coin; a very small amount
archaic british

Examples

She wouldn't spend a farthing on that old book.
He doesn't care a farthing about your opinion.
After hours of work, he was paid hardly a farthing for his trouble.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: penny - a former British coin equal to one twelfth of a shilling
  • Synonym: mite - a very small amount of money

Etymology

From Old English feorðing (quarter of a penny), from feorða (fourth).
