tambour

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæm.bɔr / (AmE), / ˈtæmbʊə / (BrE)
a small drum, typically one used in certain traditional or historical music settings
a small drum

Examples

The musician played the tambour during the folk festival.
She collected rare percussion instruments, including a 19th-century tambour.
The ornate tambour added a unique sound to the ensemble's performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drum - a percussion instrument sounded by being struck with sticks or hands

Etymology

From French 'tambour', from Arabic 'ṭanbūr' (a type of drum).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtæm.bʊr / (AmE), / ˈtæm.bʊə / (BrE)
A circular frame, often consisting of two hoops, used to hold fabric taut while embroidering.
embroidery frame with hoops

Examples

The intricate design was stitched carefully onto the fabric stretched across the tambour.
She placed the cloth in the tambour to keep it steady while she embroidered.
A wooden tambour makes embroidery easier by holding the material taut.

Etymology

From French 'tambour' meaning 'drum', from Arabic 'ṭanbūr' (drum).
