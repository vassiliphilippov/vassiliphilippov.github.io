technetium

1. NOUN

/ tɛkˈniːʃiəm / (AmE), / tɛkˈniːʃɪəm / (BrE)
a silvery-gray, radioactive metallic element (chemical symbol Tc) that does not occur naturally and is produced as a byproduct of uranium fission.
chemical element, radioactive metal

Examples

Scientists use technetium in medical imaging because of its radioactive properties.
No significant amounts of technetium are found naturally on Earth.
The discovery of technetium in uranium fission marked an important development in nuclear chemistry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: molybdenum - a similar metallic element in the periodic table
  • Synonym: rhenium - a metallic element chemically similar to technetium

Etymology

From Greek 'technētos' (artificial), reflecting its method of discovery.
