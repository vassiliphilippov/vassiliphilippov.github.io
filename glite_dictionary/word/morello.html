morello

1. NOUN

/ məˈrɛloʊ / (AmE), / məˈrɛləʊ / (BrE)
a cultivated variety of sour cherry with dark-colored skin and juice, often used in cooking and baking
a type of sour cherry with dark skin

Examples

The chef used morello cherries to give the pie a rich, tart flavor.
You can find jars of morello cherries in many European supermarkets.
Her favorite jam is made from morello cherries harvested in late summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sour cherry - a species of cherry tree with tart fruits, Prunus cerasus

Etymology

From Italian 'morello', diminutive of 'moro' (dark-skinned, blackberry).

2. NOUN

/ məˈrɛloʊ / (AmE), / məˈrɛləʊ / (BrE)
A variety of cultivated sour cherry tree bearing fruit with dark skin and juice.
variety of sour cherry tree

Examples

The morello is prized by bakers for its tart, richly colored fruit.
We planted a morello in our garden to enjoy homemade cherry pies.
Unlike sweet cherries, the morello is often used for preserves and jams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sour cherry - a type of cherry tree that produces tart fruit
  • Antonym: sweet cherry - a type of cherry tree that produces sweet fruit

Etymology

From Italian 'morello' (diminutive of 'moro', meaning 'dark-skinned' or 'Moor'), referring to the dark color of the cherries.
