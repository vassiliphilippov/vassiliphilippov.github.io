tapa

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑːpə / (AmE), / ˈtɑːpə / (BrE)
a thin, fibrous material made from the bark of the paper mulberry or similar trees, especially in Polynesian cultures
thin bark cloth from mulberry or similar trees

Examples

Traditional Polynesian clothing was often made from tapa cloth.
We admired the intricate patterns hand-painted on the tapa hanging in the museum.
The artist used tapa as a canvas for her designs inspired by island culture.

Etymology

From Samoan and Tahitian 'tapa', meaning bark cloth.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɑːpə / (AmE), / ˈtɑːpə / (BrE)
A paperlike cloth made in the South Pacific by pounding the inner bark of certain trees, especially the paper mulberry.
paperlike cloth from pounded bark, Pacific Islands

Examples

Traditional ceremonies in Tonga often feature tapa decorated with intricate patterns.
The museum's collection includes a beautifully preserved piece of tapa from Fiji.
They demonstrated the process of making tapa using bark and natural dyes.

Etymology

From Tahitian, Samoan, and other Polynesian languages, all referring to this traditional bark cloth.
