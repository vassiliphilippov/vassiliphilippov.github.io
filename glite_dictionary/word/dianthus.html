Dianthus

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈænθəs / (AmE), / daɪˈænθəs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants that includes carnations and pinks, known for their bright, often fragrant flowers.
genus of flowering plants (carnations, pinks)

Examples

The gardener planted a bed of Dianthus to add color and fragrance to the border.
Many varieties of Dianthus bloom in early summer and are prized for their vibrant pink colors.
Florists often use Dianthus in bouquets due to their long-lasting, scented flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carnation - a common cultivated flower in the Dianthus genus
  • Synonym: pink - a flower of the genus Dianthus

Etymology

From Greek *dios* (divine) + *anthos* (flower), meaning 'divine flower'.

2. NOUN

/ daɪˈænθəs / (AmE), / daɪˈænθəs / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants in the family Caryophyllaceae, including carnations and pinks
genus of flowering plants (carnations, pinks)

Examples

The Dianthus genus is well-known for its beautiful and fragrant flowers.
She planted several Dianthus varieties in her garden, including carnations and pinks.
Horticulturists often study Dianthus for its diverse flower shapes and colors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carnation - a double-flowered cultivated variety of Dianthus caryophyllus
  • Synonym: pinks - various species of the genus Dianthus, known for their pink or white flowers

Etymology

From Greek 'dios' (divine) + 'anthos' (flower), meaning 'divine flower'.
