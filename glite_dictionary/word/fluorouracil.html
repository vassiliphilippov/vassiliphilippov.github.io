fluorouracil

1. NOUN

/ flʊroʊˈjʊrəsɪl / (AmE), / flʊərəˈjʊərəsɪl / (BrE)
a synthetic antimetabolite drug used to treat certain types of cancer, especially of the colon, stomach, and breast
anticancer drug; antimetabolite
medical

Examples

The patient was prescribed fluorouracil as part of her chemotherapy regimen.
Topical fluorouracil is sometimes used to treat skin cancers and pre-cancerous lesions.
Severe side effects may occur if fluorouracil is not administered properly.

Etymology

Formed from 'fluoro-' (relating to fluorine) and 'uracil' (a component of RNA), indicating its structure as a fluorinated analog of uracil.
