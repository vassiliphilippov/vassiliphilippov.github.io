oxyphenbutazone

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːksiˌfɛnbjuːˈtæzoʊn / (AmE), / ˌɒksɪˌfɛnbjuːˈtazəʊn / (BrE)
An anti-inflammatory drug formerly used to treat arthritis and bursitis.
anti-inflammatory drug for arthritis
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed oxyphenbutazone to manage the patient’s joint inflammation.
Researchers studied the side effects of oxyphenbutazone in long-term arthritis treatment.
Adverse reactions led to oxyphenbutazone being withdrawn from some markets.

Etymology

Formed from 'oxy-' (oxygen), 'phenyl', 'butane', and '-one' indicating its chemical structure.
