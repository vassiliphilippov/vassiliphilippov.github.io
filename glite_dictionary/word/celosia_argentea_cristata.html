Celosia argentea cristata

1. NOUN

/ səˈloʊʒə ɑːrˈdʒɛn.ti.ə krɪsˈteɪ.tə / (AmE), / sɛˈləʊ.si.ə ɑːˈdʒen.tɪ.ə krɪsˈtɑː.tə / (BrE)
A garden annual plant with brightly colored, crested, featherlike spikes of red or yellow flowers, commonly grown as an ornamental.
ornamental plant with crested flower spikes

Examples

The Celosia argentea cristata added vibrant colors to the summer flowerbed.
Many gardeners prize Celosia argentea cristata for its unique crested blooms.
She planted several rows of Celosia argentea cristata along the garden path.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockscomb - another common name for Celosia argentea cristata

Etymology

From Latin; 'Celosia' from Greek 'kelos' (burned, referring to flame-like flowers); 'cristata' meaning crested.
