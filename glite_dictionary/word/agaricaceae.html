Agaricaceae

1. NOUN

/ əˌɡærɪˈkeɪsiː / (AmE), / əˌɡarɪˈkeɪsiː / (BrE)
a large family of fungi including many familiar mushrooms with gills, such as the common field mushroom
family of gilled mushrooms

Examples

Many edible mushrooms belong to the Agaricaceae family.
The Agaricaceae are known for their characteristic gilled caps.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Agaricaceae during the forest survey.

Etymology

From modern Latin Agaricaceae, from the genus name Agaricus.

2. NOUN

/ əˌɡærɪˈkeɪsiː / (AmE), / əˌɡarɪˈkeɪsiː / (BrE)
a large family of fungi that includes many familiar gilled mushrooms, such as the common mushroom and its relatives
family of gilled mushrooms

Examples

The Agaricaceae are known for their prominent cap-and-stem mushrooms found in forests and fields.
Edible species within the Agaricaceae family include the common button mushroom.
Researchers have discovered new genera in the Agaricaceae due to advances in genetic analysis.

Etymology

From New Latin Agaricaceae, from Agaricus (a genus of mushrooms) + -aceae (a suffix for plant and fungi family names).
