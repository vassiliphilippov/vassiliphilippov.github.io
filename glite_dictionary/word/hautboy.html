hautboy

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊbɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈhəʊbɔɪ / (BrE)
an early form of the oboe; a slender woodwind instrument with a conical bore and a double-reed mouthpiece
early type of oboe; double-reed woodwind

Examples

A talented musician played the hautboy during the Baroque ensemble's performance.
The mellow tones of the hautboy added a unique character to the orchestra.
In 18th-century Europe, the hautboy was a popular instrument in royal courts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oboe - a modern woodwind instrument developed from the hautboy

Etymology

From French 'hautbois', meaning 'high wood' or 'high-pitched woodwind'.
