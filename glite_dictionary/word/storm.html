storm

1. VERB

/ stɔrm / (AmE), / stɔːm / (BrE)
(Of weather) to rain, hail, or snow very hard with strong winds, often accompanied by thunder or lightning.
to rain, hail, or snow hard with wind (weather)

Examples

It was storming so badly that the streets quickly flooded.
The forecast says it may storm for most of the afternoon.

3. NOUN

/ stɔrm / (AmE), / stɔːm / (BrE)
a direct and violent attack against a fortified place or stronghold, typically by military forces
violent assault on a fortress

Examples

The army launched a storm on the enemy fortress at dawn.
The final storm broke through the city walls, ending the siege.
After a heavy artillery barrage, they prepared for the storm on the castle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attack - an aggressive and violent action against a person or place
  • Antonym: defense - the action of resisting attack

Etymology

From Old English 'storm', originally meaning tumult or disturbance, later extended to attacks.
