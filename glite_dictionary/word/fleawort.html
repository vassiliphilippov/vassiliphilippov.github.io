fleawort

1. NOUN

/ ˈfliˌwɜrt / (AmE), / ˈfliːˌwɜːt / (BrE)
A Mediterranean plantain (genus Plantago) whose seeds swell when moist and are used as a mild laxative.
Mediterranean plantain used as a laxative

Examples

The seeds of fleawort are used in herbal medicine for their mild laxative effect.
You can find fleawort growing naturally in dry Mediterranean soils.
She added ground fleawort seeds to her drink as a natural remedy.

Etymology

From Old English *flēawyrt*, from *flēa* (flea) + *wyrt* (plant), referring to the seed's resemblance to fleas.
