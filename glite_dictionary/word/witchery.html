witchery

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪtʃəri / (AmE), / ˈwɪtʃəri / (BrE)
the practice or art of magic or sorcery, often associated with witches
the art of sorcery or magic

Examples

The old legends speak of a land steeped in witchery and ancient spells.
Some believed her remarkable powers were the result of witchery rather than natural talent.
He wrote about the mysterious witchery that fascinated so many in the village.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorcery - the use of magic, especially by a witch
  • Synonym: witchcraft - the practice of magic, especially as used by witches

Etymology

From Middle English wicchecraft, from wicche (witch) + -ery (denoting a practice or condition).
