Leontocebus oedipus

1. NOUN

/ liˌɑːntəˈsiːbəs ˈiːdɪpəs / (AmE), / liˌɒntəˈsiːbəs ˈiːdɪpəs / (BrE)
A small South American monkey, also known as the cotton-top tamarin, distinguished by a long white crest of hair on its head.
cotton-top tamarin species

Examples

The critically endangered Leontocebus oedipus is native to northwestern Colombia.
Researchers observed Leontocebus oedipus in the wild to study its complex social behavior.
A Leontocebus oedipus can be recognized by its distinctive white crest of hair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cotton-top tamarin - Common name for Leontocebus oedipus, a small South American monkey with a tufted white head

Etymology

From New Latin, with 'Leontocebus' from Greek 'leōn' (lion) and 'kēbos' (monkey), and 'oedipus' from the Greek mythological figure Oedipus.
