merciful

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɝː.sɪ.fəl / (AmE), / ˈmɜː.sɪ.fəl / (BrE)
(of royalty or high nobility) gracious or benevolent, especially as a title or epithet.
gracious (of royalty or nobility)

Examples

The document was addressed to "our merciful queen," acknowledging her compassion.
In his speech, the ambassador praised the merciful king for his generosity.
The people celebrated their merciful ruler, who was known for acts of grace and kindness.

Synset Examples

our merciful king

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gracious - showing kindness, especially by someone of higher status

Etymology

From Old French *mercifuel*, from Latin *mercifulus* (full of mercy).

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɝːsɪfəl / (AmE), / ˈmɜːsɪfəl / (BrE)
showing kindness and forgiveness, especially toward someone who is suffering or has done wrong; compassionate and willing to spare others from harm or punishment.
showing mercy or compassion

Examples

The judge was merciful and gave the young man a second chance.
It was a merciful decision to end the animal's suffering quickly.
She prayed for a merciful outcome to the difficult situation.

Synset Examples

sought merciful treatment for the captives
a merciful god

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compassionate - showing sympathy and concern for others' suffering
  • Antonym: cruel - willingly causing pain or suffering

Etymology

From Old French *mercif* (full of mercy), from Latin *mercēs* (reward, favor, pity).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01510813-a
Synset POS: a
