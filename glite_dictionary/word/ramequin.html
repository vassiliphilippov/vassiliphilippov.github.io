ramequin

1. NOUN

/ ˈræm.ə.kwɪn / (AmE), / ˈræm.ɪ.kɪn / (BrE)
A baked dish made with cheese, eggs, and bread crumbs, typically served in small individual fireproof dishes.
cheese-based baked dish in small dish

Examples

The chef prepared a delightful ramequin for each guest, filled with creamy cheese and herbs.
For the brunch menu, we baked several ramequins and served them hot out of the oven.
He ordered a ramequin as a starter, enjoying its rich and savory flavor.

Etymology

From French 'ramequin', originally Dutch 'rameken'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈræm.ə.kwɪn / (AmE), / ˈræm.ɪ.kwɪn / (BrE)
A small, usually round ovenproof dish used for baking and serving individual portions of food.
small ovenproof baking dish

Examples

She baked the soufflés in individual ramequins to serve at dinner.
Each guest received a ramequin filled with creamy macaroni and cheese.
Please place the chocolate lava cake in a ramequin before putting it in the oven.

Etymology

From French ramequin, likely from Low German Rampkin (a kind of cheese dish).
