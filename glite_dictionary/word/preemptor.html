preemptor

1. NOUN

/ priˈɛmptər / (AmE), / priːˈɛmptə / (BrE)
A bridge player who makes a preemptive bid, attempting to disrupt opponents' communication by opening the bidding at a high level with a weak hand.
bridge player making a preemptive bid

Examples

As a skilled preemptor, she frequently disrupted the opponents' bidding strategy.
The team's success often depended on having an effective preemptor at the table.
A good preemptor knows when to take calculated risks to put pressure on the other side.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preemptive bid - an intentionally high early bid in bridge to disrupt the opponents

Etymology

From 'preempt' + '-or', meaning one who preempts; 'preempt' ultimately from Latin 'praeemere' (to buy before).

2. NOUN

/ priˈɛmptər / (AmE), / priˈɛmptə / (BrE)
a person who acquires land or property by claiming the right of preemption, especially under historical US land laws
person who acquires land by preemption
legal

Examples

The preemptor filed his claim to secure the disputed territory before others had the chance.
As a preemptor, she was required to meet certain residency requirements to keep the land.
Historical records show that many preemptors moved westward seeking new opportunities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preemption - the act of acquiring land by the right of preemption

Etymology

From preempt, patterned after Latin praeemere (to buy before) + -or (agent suffix).
