puritanic

1. ADJ

/ ˌpjʊrəˈtænɪk / (AmE), / ˌpjʊə.rɪˈtæn.ɪk / (BrE)
Having or showing very strict moral or religious standards; morally rigorous and strict.
morally strict or rigorous

Examples

His puritanic views made him unpopular among his more liberal friends.
She imposed a puritanic set of rules on her household.
The school's puritanic policies forbade even minor displays of affection.

Synset Examples

puritanic distaste for alcohol
she was anything but puritanical in her behavior
blue laws

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puritanical - having or displaying very strict moral attitudes

Etymology

From Puritan (member of a 16th–17th century English Protestant group noted for strictness) + -ic (forming adjectives).
