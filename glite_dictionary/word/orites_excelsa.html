Orites excelsa

1. NOUN

/ ɔːˈraɪtiːz ɪkˈsɛlsə / (AmE), / ɒˈraɪtiːz ɪkˈsɛlsə / (BrE)
An Australian tree with alternate simple leaves, sometimes pinnate and prickly when young, and slender spikes of white flowers.
Australian tree with white flower spikes

Examples

The dense foliage of the Orites excelsa provided shade along the rainforest trail.
Botanists studied the unique white flowers of Orites excelsa found in eastern Australia.
The young leaves of Orites excelsa are often pinnate with prickly teeth.

Etymology

From New Latin; 'Orites' from Greek 'orites' (mountaineer), 'excelsa' from Latin meaning 'lofty'.
