leprosy

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛprəsi / (AmE), / ˈlɛprəsi / (BrE)
A chronic infectious disease caused by the bacterium Mycobacterium leprae, characterized by skin lesions, nerve damage, and muscle weakness.
chronic infectious disease (Hansen's disease)
medical

Examples

Cases of leprosy have significantly decreased due to improved treatment and early detection.
The stigma associated with leprosy often leads to social isolation for those affected.
He volunteered at a hospital that specializes in treating leprosy patients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hansen's disease - another name for leprosy, especially in medical contexts

Etymology

From Late Latin leprosia, from leprosus (scaly), from Greek lepros (scaly).
