conventicle

1. NOUN

/ kənˈvɛntɪkəl / (AmE), / kənˈvɛntɪkəl / (BrE)
a building where people, especially Nonconformists such as Quakers, gather for religious worship or assembly
building for religious assembly (Nonconformists)

Examples

The old stone conventicle still stands at the edge of the village, a testament to its history.
Many early Quakers met secretly in a small conventicle to avoid persecution.
The authorities closely monitored every conventicle for signs of dissent in the region.

Etymology

From Latin 'conventiculum' (assembly), diminutive of 'conventus' (meeting).

2. NOUN

/ kənˈvɛn.tɪ.kəl / (AmE), / kənˈvɛntɪkəl / (BrE)
a secret or unauthorized meeting, especially for religious worship, held in defiance of official regulations
secret religious meeting

Examples

During the persecution, villagers gathered at a conventicle deep in the forest to worship in secret.
The government outlawed the conventicle, fearing it would spread dissent against the established church.
Early Protestants risked imprisonment for attending a conventicle outside the state-sanctioned churches.

Etymology

From Latin 'conventiculum', diminutive of 'conventus' (assembly), from 'convenire' (to come together).
