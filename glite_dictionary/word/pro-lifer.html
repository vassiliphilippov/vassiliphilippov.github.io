pro-lifer

1. NOUN

/ proʊ ˈlaɪfər / (AmE), / prəʊ ˈlaɪfə / (BrE)
a person who opposes legalized abortion and supports legal protection for embryos and fetuses
someone opposed to legalized abortion

Examples

The pro-lifer held a sign outside the courthouse during the demonstration.
As a committed pro-lifer, she campaigns for stricter abortion laws.
Many politicians have met with both pro-lifers and pro-choice activists to discuss the issue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anti-abortionist - someone who opposes abortion
  • Antonym: pro-choice - a person who supports the legal right to choose abortion

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'pro-' (in favor of) and 'life,' reflecting advocacy for fetal life.
