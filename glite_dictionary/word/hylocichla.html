Hylocichla

1. NOUN

/ haɪloʊˈsɪklə / (AmE), / hɪləʊˈsɪklə / (BrE)
A genus of American thrushes that includes the wood thrush and related species.
genus of American thrushes

Examples

The Hylocichla genus is well known for beautiful songs produced by species like the wood thrush.
Ornithologists have debated the classification of birds within Hylocichla.
Many birdwatchers hope to spot a member of the Hylocichla genus during spring migration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Turdus - genus including other thrush species such as the American robin

Etymology

From Greek hylē (wood, forest) + kichlē (thrush).
