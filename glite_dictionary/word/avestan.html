Avestan

1. ADJ

/ əˈvɛstən / (AmE), / əˈvɛstən / (BrE)
relating to the Avesta, the sacred text of Zoroastrianism, or to the ancient language in which it was written
relating to the Avesta or its language

Examples

The Avestan language is studied by scholars of ancient Persian texts.
He translated several hymns from the Avestan scriptures into English.
A detailed understanding of Avestan beliefs helps contextualize the origins of Zoroastrianism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Avesta - the sacred book of Zoroastrianism

Etymology

From Avesta, the sacred writings of Zoroastrianism, with the English suffix -an.

2. NOUN

/ əˈvɛstən / (AmE), / əˈvɛstən / (BrE)
an ancient Iranian language, chiefly known as the language of the Zoroastrian sacred texts, the Avesta
ancient Iranian language of Zoroastrian scripture

Examples

The Avestan language is primarily preserved in the Zoroastrian scriptures known as the Avesta.
Linguists study Avestan to understand the early history of Indo-Iranian languages.
The ritual prayers were originally composed in Avestan.

Etymology

From Avesta (sacred Zoroastrian texts) + -an (denoting association or belonging).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06986601-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ əˈvɛstən / (AmE), / əˈvɛstən / (BrE)
the script in which the ancient Persian language of the Avesta is written
ancient Persian script of the Avesta

Examples

Scholars have studied the Avestan script to better understand Zoroastrian religious texts.
The inscription was written entirely in Avestan, making it difficult for most people to read.
Many ancient manuscripts in Avestan have been carefully preserved in Iranian museums.

Etymology

From Avesta, the name of the Zoroastrian sacred texts, with the suffix -an.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06363708-n
