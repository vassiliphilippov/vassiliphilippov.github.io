Conyza canadensis

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːnɪzə ˌkænəˈdɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˈkɒnɪzə ˌkanəˈdɛnsɪs / (BrE)
a species of North American weed with narrow leaves and small yellowish flower heads; widely naturalized in temperate regions
North American weed species

Examples

Conyza canadensis is often found growing in disturbed soils along roadsides and fields.
Farmers consider Conyza canadensis a troublesome weed due to its resistance to herbicides.
The small yellow-green flowers of Conyza canadensis appear in late summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horseweed - common name for Conyza canadensis
  • Synonym: fleabane - common name for plants in the genus Erigeron, closely related

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; genus name 'Conyza' from Greek 'konyza' (kind of plant), species epithet 'canadensis' meaning 'of Canada'.
