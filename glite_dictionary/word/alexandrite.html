alexandrite

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɛk.sənˌdraɪt / (AmE), / əˈlɛk.səndraɪt / (BrE)
a gemstone that is a green variety of chrysoberyl, known for changing color under different lighting
a green chrysoberyl gemstone

Examples

The rare alexandrite in her ring shimmered with changing colors in the evening light.
Collectors value alexandrite for its unique ability to appear green in daylight and red in incandescent light.
He inherited a stunning alexandrite brooch from his grandmother.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chrysoberyl - a hard, yellowish or green mineral used as a gemstone

Etymology

Named after Russian Tsar Alexander II, as it was first discovered in Russia in 1830.
