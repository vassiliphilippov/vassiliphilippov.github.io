bast

1. NOUN

/ bæst / (AmE), / bɑːst / (BrE)
(Botany) Plant tissue that transports synthesized food from the leaves to other parts of the plant, consisting mainly of sieve tubes.
plant tissue for food transport

Examples

The bast of the tree plays a crucial role in transporting nutrients throughout the plant.
Damage to the bast can severely affect a plant's ability to distribute food produced by photosynthesis.
Scientists studied the structure of bast to understand how plants move carbohydrates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phloem - vascular tissue in plants that transports sugars and nutrients

Etymology

From Old English bæst (bark, inner bark of trees), related to Old Norse, German, and Dutch forms.

2. NOUN

/ bæst / (AmE), / bɑːst / (BrE)
Strong woody fibers obtained especially from the phloem of certain plants, used for making rope, mats, and similar products.
woody plant fiber from phloem, used in rope or mats

Examples

The hemp plant is cultivated for its bast, which is processed into strong ropes.
Traditionally, sandals were made from bast obtained from the inner bark of trees.
The craftsmen separated the bast from the stems before weaving baskets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fiber - threadlike structure from plant or animal sources, used in textiles

Etymology

From Old English bæst (inner bark of the linden tree).
