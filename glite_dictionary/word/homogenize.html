to homogenize

1. VERB

/ həˈmɑdʒəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / həˈmɒdʒənaɪz / (BrE)
to make something uniform or homogeneous, especially by mixing different elements thoroughly
make uniform by mixing

Examples

Before bottling, the company will homogenize the milk to ensure consistency.
The chef used a blender to homogenize the soup's ingredients.
Modern manufacturing often requires machines to homogenize raw materials before further processing.

Synset Examples

homogenize the main ingredients

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to standardize - to make things conform to a standard
  • Antonym: heterogenize - to make or become heterogeneous, not uniform

Etymology

From French "homogénéiser", from Greek "homogenēs" (of the same kind).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00458055-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ həˈmɑdʒəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / həˈmɒdʒənaɪz / (BrE)
to break up the fat globules in a liquid, especially milk, so that they are evenly distributed and do not separate
break up fat in milk or liquids
medical

Examples

The company homogenizes its milk to ensure a smooth texture and prevent cream separation.
Before bottling, the dairy workers homogenized the milk for consistency.
Most commercially sold milk is homogenized to distribute the fat evenly throughout the liquid.

Synset Examples

homogenized milk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to emulsify - to mix two liquids that normally do not combine easily

Etymology

From Greek homos (same) + genos (kind, race) + -ize.

3. VERB

/ həˈmɑdʒəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / həˈmɒdʒənaɪz / (BrE)
to make something uniform or similar throughout, especially by thoroughly mixing its components
make uniform or similar by mixing

Examples

The milk must be homogenized to prevent the cream from separating.
Over time, the cultures in the city began to homogenize due to increased interaction.
The two liquids homogenized quickly when stirred together in the beaker.

Synset Examples

The two liquids homogenized in the blender

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mix - to combine or blend into a single entity
  • Antonym: separate - to set or keep apart

Etymology

From French homogénéiser, from homogène (homogeneous), from Greek homogenēs (of the same kind).
