contrasty

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɑn.træsti / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.trɑːsti / (BrE)
having sharp differences between light and dark areas, especially in photographs or images
having strong contrasts in images

Examples

The photographer preferred a contrasty look to emphasize the drama in his images.
Old black-and-white films often have a contrasty appearance due to the film stock used.
If your photo is too contrasty, you can reduce the effect with careful editing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contrasting - marked by differences, especially between colors or tones
  • Antonym: flat - lacking contrast, appearing uniform in tone
