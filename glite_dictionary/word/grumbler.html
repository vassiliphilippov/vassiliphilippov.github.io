grumbler

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrʌmbələr / (AmE), / ˈɡrʌmbələ / (BrE)
a person who habitually complains, especially in a petty or persistent way
person who habitually complains

Examples

Even on sunny days, the office grumbler finds something to moan about.
The children grew tired of listening to the old grumbler at the park.
She dismissed him as just a grumbler who never saw the positive side of things.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complainer - a person who expresses dissatisfaction frequently
  • Synonym: whiner - a person who complains in a childish or annoying way
  • Antonym: optimist - a person who tends to be hopeful and confident about the future

Etymology

From 'grumble' (to complain in a low voice) + '-er' (agent suffix).
