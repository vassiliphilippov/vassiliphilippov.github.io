enlivened

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈlaɪvənd / (AmE), / ɪnˈlaɪvənd / (BrE)
made more lively, spirited, or cheerful; given new energy or excitement.
made more lively or cheerful

Examples

The music enlivened the party and got everyone dancing.
Her enlivened spirit was evident after the good news.
The dull classroom was instantly enlivened by his witty jokes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enliven - to make more lively or cheerful
  • Antonym: deadened - made less lively or spirited

Etymology

From the verb 'enliven', which is formed from 'en-' (make) + 'liven', related to 'life'.

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈlaɪvənd / (AmE), / ɪnˈlaɪvənd / (BrE)
made more lively, interesting, or cheerful; filled with energy or spirit.
made lively or spirited

Examples

The party was enlivened by her witty anecdotes and laughter.
The film was greatly enlivened by an energetic soundtrack.
His presence enlivened the otherwise dull meeting.

Synset Examples

a meal enlivened by the music
a spirited debate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enliven - to make (something) more lively, interesting, or entertaining
  • Synonym: animate - to bring to life or make lively
  • Antonym: dull - lacking interest or excitement

Etymology

From 'enliven' + past participle '-ed'; 'enliven' from en- (cause to) + 'liven' (make alive).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
