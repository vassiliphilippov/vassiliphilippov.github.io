asyndeton

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɪn.dəˌtɑn / (AmE), / əˈsɪn.dɪ.tɒn / (BrE)
The omission of conjunctions between parts of a sentence where they would normally be used, typically to create emphasis or a specific effect.
omission of conjunctions in a sentence

Examples

Julius Caesar’s famous phrase 'Veni, vidi, vici' is an example of asyndeton.
Writers often use asyndeton to add drama, speed, or emphasis to their sentences.
The use of asyndeton in her speech made each point more impactful and memorable.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: polysyndeton - the use of several conjunctions in close succession

Etymology

From Greek 'asyndeton', from 'a-' (not) + 'syndeton' (bound together, conjunction).
