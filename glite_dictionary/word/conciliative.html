conciliative

1. ADJ

/ kənˈsɪli.ə.tɪv / (AmE), / kənˈsɪl.i.ə.tɪv / (BrE)
intended or tending to placate or reconcile; making efforts to calm or soothe disagreements.
intended to placate or reconcile

Examples

Her conciliative words helped ease the tension in the room.
The manager adopted a conciliative approach to resolve the ongoing dispute between departments.
He spoke in a conciliative manner, hoping to mend the broken relationship.

Synset Examples

spoke in a conciliating tone
a conciliatory visit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conciliatory - intended or likely to placate or pacify
  • Antonym: hostile - unfriendly; antagonistic

Etymology

From Latin 'conciliatus', past participle of 'conciliare' (to bring together, unite); related to 'conciliate'.
