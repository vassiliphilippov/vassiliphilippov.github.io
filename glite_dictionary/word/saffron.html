saffron

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæfrən / (AmE), / ˈsæfrən / (BrE)
a deep yellow color tinged with orange, resembling the color of the saffron spice
deep yellow-orange color

Examples

The curtains in the living room were a vivid saffron that brightened the space.
She chose a saffron dress for the summer party because of its vibrant hue.
As the sun set, the sky glowed with a saffron light over the horizon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amber - a yellowish-orange color
  • Degree Lower: yellow - the color between green and orange in the spectrum
  • Degree Higher: orange - the color between red and yellow in the spectrum

Etymology

From Arabic 'zaʿfarān', via Old French 'safran'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsæfrən / (AmE), / ˈsæfrən / (BrE)
a spice consisting of the dried stigmas of the saffron crocus, valued for its distinctive flavor, aroma, and yellow color, used especially in cooking and dyeing
valuable spice from crocus stigmas

Examples

The recipe calls for a pinch of saffron to enhance both color and flavor.
She carefully measured out the saffron threads before adding them to the paella.
Genuine saffron is expensive because it must be hand-harvested from thousands of flowers.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: turmeric - a yellow spice often used as a cheaper alternative to saffron

Etymology

From Arabic 'zaʿfarān', via Old French and Medieval Latin.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsæfrən / (AmE), / ˈsæfrən / (BrE)
A crocus plant native to southwest Asia, with purple or white flowers and bright orange stigmas, which are dried to make the spice used in cooking and as coloring.
a crocus whose stigmas are used as a spice

Examples

The fields of saffron blossomed in autumn, their purple petals carpeting the landscape.
Farmers carefully harvested the precious stigmas from the saffron flowers at dawn.
This region is famous for its cultivation of saffron, which is prized around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saffron (spice) - the spice derived from the dried stigmas of the saffron crocus

Etymology

From Arabic *zaʿfarān*, via Old French *safran*.
