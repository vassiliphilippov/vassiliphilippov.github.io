unburdened

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈbɝːdnd / (AmE), / ʌnˈbɜːdnd / (BrE)
Not having or being weighed down by difficulties, responsibilities, or worries.
free from burdens or worries

Examples

After paying off her debts, she felt completely unburdened for the first time in years.
He entered the new job unburdened by prior experience or preconceived notions.
Their laughter was light and unburdened as they walked along the beach.

Synset Examples

unburdened by an overarching theory

Related Terms

  • Antonym: burdened - weighed down with a load, duty, or difficulty
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00873611-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈbɝːdənd / (AmE), / ʌnˈbɜːdənd / (BrE)
Not carrying a physical burden or load; not encumbered.
not carrying a burden

Examples

She walked unburdened by heavy bags through the quiet streets.
The horse trotted freely, unburdened for the first time in weeks.
He was grateful to be unburdened after unloading the crates from his back.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: burdened - carrying a load or burden
  • Synonym: unencumbered - not burdened or weighed down

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' meaning 'not' and 'burdened'.
