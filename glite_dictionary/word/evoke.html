to evoke

1. VERB

/ ɪˈvoʊk / (AmE), / ɪˈvəʊk / (BrE)
to cause a feeling, memory, image, response, or event to be brought to mind or to occur
to bring to mind or cause to happen

Examples

The song managed to evoke memories of her childhood summers.
His speech evoked a strong emotional response from the audience.
The scent of pine trees evokes images of winter holidays for me.

Synset Examples

Her behavior provoked a quarrel between the couple

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to provoke - to stimulate a reaction or feeling, often a strong one

Etymology

From Latin evocare (to call out, summon), from e- (out) + vocare (to call).

2. VERB

/ ɪˈvoʊk / (AmE), / ɪˈvəʊk / (BrE)
to bring a feeling, memory, or image into someone's mind; to produce a particular reaction or emotion
bring forth a reaction or feeling

Examples

The old photograph managed to evoke memories of his childhood.
The film's soundtrack was designed to evoke strong emotions.
Her speech evoked sympathy from the entire audience.
