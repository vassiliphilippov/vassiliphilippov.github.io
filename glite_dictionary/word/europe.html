Europe

1. PROPN

/ ˈjʊr.əp / (AmE), / ˈjʊə.rəp / (BrE)
the countries of the European continent considered collectively, especially in political, economic, or cultural contexts
the nations of the European continent

Examples

After the Second World War, Europe focused on rebuilding its economies and infrastructure.
Trade agreements among Europe's nations have strengthened their global influence.
Europe responded collectively to the challenges of the migrant crisis.

Synset Examples

the Marshall Plan helped Europe recover from World War II

Etymology

From Latin Europa, from Ancient Greek Ευρώπη (Eurṓpē), a mythological Phoenician princess.

2. PROPN

/ ˈjʊr.əp / (AmE), / ˈjʊə.rəp / (BrE)
the continent located west of Asia and north of Africa, consisting of many countries including France, Germany, and Italy; sometimes excluding the British Isles in British usage.
continent west of Asia, north of Africa

Examples

Many countries in Europe share a rich and interconnected cultural history.
She traveled through Europe last summer, visiting major cities like Paris and Rome.
The climate in southern Europe is generally warm and dry.

Etymology

From Latin 'Europa', from Ancient Greek 'Εὐρώπη' (Eurṓpē).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
