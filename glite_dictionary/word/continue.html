to continue

1. VERB

/ kənˈtɪn.ju / (AmE), / kənˈtɪn.juː / (BrE)
to do something repeatedly, showing no intention of stopping
do something repeatedly without stopping

Examples

Despite the criticism, she continued giving her speeches with confidence.
The rain continued for days, causing widespread flooding in the region.
If you continue asking the same question, you'll eventually get an answer.

Synset Examples

We continued our research into the cause of the illness
The landlord persists in asking us to move

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to persist - to continue firmly in a course of action despite difficulty
  • Antonym: to cease - to stop an action or activity

Etymology

From Latin 'continuare' (to join together), from 'continuus' (uninterrupted).
