redbrick

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɛdˌbrɪk / (AmE), / ˈrɛdˌbrɪk / (BrE)
Relating to British universities founded in the late 19th or early 20th century, especially those outside Oxford and Cambridge.
pertaining to newer British universities
british

Examples

Many of the redbrick universities were established to serve the educational needs of growing industrial cities.
He is a professor at a redbrick university in northern England.
The redbrick institutions played a vital role in expanding higher education beyond the elite.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ancient university - older British universities founded before the 19th century
  • Degree Higher: plate glass university - British universities founded in the mid-20th century

Etymology

Refers to the red brick architecture typical of many such universities.
