homeopathy

1. NOUN

/ hoʊˈmiːəˌpæθi / (AmE), / ˌhəʊmiˈɒpəθi / (BrE)
a system of alternative medicine based on treating diseases with minute doses of substances that, in larger amounts, would produce symptoms of the disease in healthy people
alternative medical system using minute doses
medical

Examples

Many people turn to homeopathy as a natural approach to healing minor ailments.
Debates about the effectiveness of homeopathy continue within the medical community.
Her interest in homeopathy grew after she experienced relief from recurring headaches.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: allopathy - conventional medical practice, especially in contrast to homeopathy

Etymology

From Greek 'homoios' (similar) + 'pathos' (suffering).
