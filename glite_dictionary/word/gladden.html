to gladden

1. VERB

/ ˈɡlæd.ən / (AmE), / ˈɡlæd.ən / (BrE)
to make someone feel glad or happy; to bring joy or pleasure to
make happy or joyful

Examples

Her unexpected visit truly gladdened his heart after a long, hard week.
Nothing gladdens me more than seeing my children laugh together.
The news of their victory gladdened the entire village.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delight - to give great pleasure or joy
  • Synonym: cheer - to make someone feel happier or more hopeful
  • Antonym: to sadden - to make someone feel sad

Etymology

From Old English 'glædian' (to make glad), from 'glæd' (glad, bright).
