centralisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛntrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛntrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act or process of concentrating control, power, or activity under a single central authority or in a central location.
the act of concentrating control in a center
british

Examples

The centralisation of power in the capital led to more efficient decision-making.
Digital centralisation allows companies to streamline their operations and reduce overhead costs.
Some critics argue that too much centralisation can stifle local innovation and autonomy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: decentralisation - the process of distributing power away from a central authority
  • Synonym: centralization - the process of concentrating control at a center (US spelling)

Etymology

From French 'centralisation', from 'centraliser' (to centralize), from Latin 'centrum' (center).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛntrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛntrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process or act of bringing activities or power under a single, central authority.
the act of bringing control to a central authority

Examples

The new policy led to a significant centralisation of power within the government.
Many employees expressed concern about the centralisation of decision-making processes.
The centralisation of resources helped improve efficiency at the headquarters.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: decentralisation - the transfer of authority from central to local government or management

Etymology

From French 'centralisation', from 'centraliser' (to centralize) + '-ation'.
