Phanerogamae

1. NOUN

/ ˌfænəˈrɑːɡəˌmiː / (AmE), / ˌfanəˈrɒɡəmiː / (BrE)
an outdated group of plants in former classification systems, comprising all seed-bearing plants; later replaced by the division Spermatophyta
obsolete group of seed-bearing plants

Examples

The system classified flowering plants under the group Phanerogamae.
Botanists now rarely use Phanerogamae, preferring the term Spermatophyta instead.
Many 19th-century texts refer to all seed-bearing plants as Phanerogamae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Spermatophyta - plants comprising all seed-bearing species

Etymology

From Greek 'phaneros' (visible) + 'gamos' (marriage), referring to visible reproductive organs.
