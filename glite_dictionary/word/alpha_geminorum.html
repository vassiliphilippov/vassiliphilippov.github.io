Alpha Geminorum

1. PROPN

/ ˌælfə dʒɛˈmɪnərəm / (AmE), / ˌalfə dʒɛˈmɪnərəm / (BrE)
the second brightest star in the constellation Gemini, a multiple star system with six components, also known as Castor
bright multiple star in Gemini (Castor)

Examples

Astronomers have studied Alpha Geminorum to better understand multiple star systems.
From Earth, Alpha Geminorum appears as a single star, but it's actually a complex system of six stars.
Through a telescope, Alpha Geminorum and Pollux shine brightly in the night sky side by side.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Pollux - the brightest star in Gemini (Beta Geminorum)
  • Synonym: Castor - another name for Alpha Geminorum

Etymology

From Latin: 'Alpha' (the first letter of the Greek alphabet), plus 'Geminorum', genitive plural of 'Gemini', indicating the star's position as the brightest in the constellation Gemini.
