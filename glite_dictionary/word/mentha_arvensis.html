Mentha arvensis

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛnθə ɑːrˈvɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˈmɛnθə ɑːˈvɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A species of mint native to Europe and Asia, commonly known as wild mint or field mint, and naturalized in the United States.
species of wild mint

Examples

Mentha arvensis is often found growing along riverbanks and in damp meadows.
The essential oil extracted from Mentha arvensis is widely used in aromatherapy.
Botanists recorded a dense patch of Mentha arvensis thriving in the newly restored wetland area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wild mint - common name for Mentha arvensis
  • Degree Lower: Mentha spicata - species of mint known as spearmint
  • Degree Higher: Mentha piperita - species of mint known as peppermint

Etymology

From Latin: 'mentha' (mint) and 'arvensis' (of the field).
