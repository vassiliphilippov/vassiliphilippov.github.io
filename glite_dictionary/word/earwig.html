earwig

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪrˌwɪɡ / (AmE), / ˈɪə.wɪɡ / (BrE)
a small elongated insect of the order Dermaptera, with a pair of prominent pincer-like appendages at the rear of its abdomen, found in many regions worldwide
insect with pincer-like appendages

Examples

She found an earwig crawling on the windowsill in the kitchen.
The garden was full of earwigs after the rainstorm last night.
Some people mistakenly believe that an earwig can crawl into a person's ear while they sleep.

Etymology

From Old English *ēarwicga*, literally 'ear insect.'
