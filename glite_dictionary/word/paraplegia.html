paraplegia

1. NOUN

/ ˌpærəˈpliːdʒiə / (AmE), / ˌpærəˈpliːdʒɪə / (BrE)
Paralysis of the lower half of the body, typically as a result of injury or disease of the spinal cord.
paralysis of the lower body
medical

Examples

After his accident, he was diagnosed with paraplegia and began rehabilitation therapy.
Modern advances in medicine have improved outcomes for people living with paraplegia.
The patient's sudden onset of paraplegia was caused by a spinal cord injury.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: quadriplegia - paralysis of all four limbs
  • Degree Lower: hemiplegia - paralysis of one side of the body

Etymology

From Greek 'para-' (beside) + 'plēgē' (stroke).
