pedicel

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛdɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈpɛdɪsəl / (BrE)
a small stalk that supports a single flower within an inflorescence
small flower-bearing stalk
medical

Examples

Each flower is attached to the stem by a short pedicel.
The pedicel plays a crucial role in supporting and nourishing the developing bloom.
Botanists measured the length of every pedicel in the inflorescence for their experiment.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: peduncle - the main stalk bearing a cluster of flowers

Etymology

From Latin pedicellus, diminutive of pes (foot).
