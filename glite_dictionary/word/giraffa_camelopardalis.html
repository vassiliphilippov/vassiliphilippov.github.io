Giraffa camelopardalis

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪˈræfə ˌkæməloʊˈpɑːrdəlɪs / (AmE), / dʒɪˈræfə ˌkæmələʊˈpɑːdəlɪs / (BrE)
The scientific name for the giraffe, the tallest living land animal, native to the savannas of tropical Africa, characterized by its long neck, long legs, and spotted coat.
scientific name for the giraffe

Examples

The Giraffa camelopardalis is recognized for its remarkably long neck, which helps it reach leaves high up in trees.
Wildlife documentaries often focus on the Giraffa camelopardalis as an iconic species of the African savannah.
Researchers monitored several Giraffa camelopardalis to study their migration patterns during the dry season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giraffe - the common English name for Giraffa camelopardalis

Etymology

From Latin, combining Greek 'giraffa' (giraffe) and 'kamelopardalis' (camelopard), referring to the animal's camel-like shape and leopard-like spots.
