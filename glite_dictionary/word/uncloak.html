to uncloak

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈkloʊk / (AmE), / ʌnˈkləʊk / (BrE)
to remove a cloak from someone or something; to make visible or reveal, especially something hidden or disguised
remove a cloak or reveal something hidden

Examples

The spy managed to uncloak without attracting the guards' attention.
When the ship began to uncloak, it appeared out of thin air before our eyes.
The magician uncloaked the statue, revealing its true form to the astonished audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reveal - to make something visible or known
  • Synonym: to disclose - to make secret information known
  • Antonym: to conceal - to hide or keep out of sight

Etymology

From un- (not) + cloak (outer garment or cover).

2. VERB

/ ʌnˈkloʊk / (AmE), / ʌnˈkləʊk / (BrE)
to reveal or expose something previously hidden, especially the true nature, identity, or intentions of someone or something
reveal something hidden

Examples

The investigation managed to uncloak the true motives behind the company's sudden merger.
After months of secrecy, the scientist decided to uncloak her latest invention in a public demonstration.
The spy feared that any mistake would uncloak his real identity to the enemy.

Synset Examples

The journal article unmasked the corrupt politician

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to unmask - to expose or reveal the true character or hidden nature of someone
  • Synonym: to reveal - to make something previously unknown or secret known to others
  • Antonym: to conceal - to keep something hidden or secret

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (meaning reverse action) and 'cloak' (a covering garment), implying removal of a covering.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00937663-v
Synset POS: v
