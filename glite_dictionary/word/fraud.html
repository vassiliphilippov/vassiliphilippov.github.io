fraud

1. NOUN

/ frɔd / (AmE), / frɔːd / (BrE)
the crime or act of deceiving someone in order to gain an unfair or unlawful advantage
deception for personal gain

Examples

The company was accused of fraud after falsifying its financial statements.
She lost all her savings because she fell victim to an online fraud.
Insurance fraud can result in serious legal consequences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deceit - the act of deceiving someone by concealing or misrepresenting the truth
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and sincere

Etymology

From Latin 'fraus' (deceit, injury).

3. NOUN

/ frɔd / (AmE), / frɔːd / (BrE)
a person who pretends to be something they are not, usually to deceive others
a deceiver; a person pretending falsely

Examples

He turned out to be a complete fraud and had lied about his qualifications.
Many were shocked when the popular author was exposed as a fraud.
She felt like a fraud accepting praise for something she didn't do.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impostor - a person who pretends to be someone else to deceive others
  • Synonym: charlatan - a person falsely claiming to have special knowledge or skill
  • Antonym: honest person - a person who is truthful and does not deceive

Etymology

From Latin 'fraus, fraudis' meaning 'deceit, injury'.
