tumbling

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌmblɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtʌmblɪŋ / (BrE)
the performance of acrobatic movements, such as somersaults and handsprings, typically on the floor or on a mat
gymnastic acrobatic movements

Examples

She began tumbling at the age of six and quickly excelled in the sport.
The circus performers impressed everyone with their daring tumbling routines.
He injured his ankle while practicing tumbling in the gym.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acrobatics - the performance of spectacular gymnastic feats
  • Synonym: gymnastics - physical exercises requiring balance, strength and coordination

Etymology

Present participle of 'tumble', from Middle English 'tomblen', of uncertain origin.
