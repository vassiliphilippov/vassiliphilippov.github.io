chub

1. NOUN

/ tʃʌb / (AmE), / tʃʌb / (BrE)
A European freshwater fish with a thick, spindle-shaped body, commonly found in rivers and streams and often sought by anglers.
European freshwater game fish

Examples

Anglers often try to catch chub in the fast-flowing streams of England.
The chub has a thick, rounded body and silvery scales.
Several chub were seen swimming under the stone bridge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dace - A small freshwater fish related to the chub

Etymology

Probably from Old English 'cubb' (a block, lump), referring to its shape.
