arbitration

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrbɪˈtreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɑːbɪˈtreɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of settling a dispute by having an impartial person or panel make a decision that is usually binding on all parties involved
settlement of a dispute by an impartial decision
legal

Examples

The contract states that any disagreement must go to arbitration rather than to court.
After months of failed negotiations, both companies agreed to arbitration to resolve their dispute.
The employees relied on arbitration to settle their wage claims fairly.

Synset Examples

they submitted their disagreement to arbitration

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mediation - settlement of a dispute by an impartial third party who helps the parties reach a mutual agreement

Etymology

From Latin arbitratio, from arbitrari (to judge).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑːr.bɪˈtreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɑː.bɪˈtreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The process of resolving a dispute by having an impartial third party make a decision that is usually binding on both sides.
settlement of disputes by third party
legal

Examples

The contract required both sides to submit their disagreements to arbitration rather than going to court.
After months of negotiation failed, the labor dispute was finally settled through arbitration.
Many companies now include arbitration clauses in their service agreements to avoid lengthy lawsuits.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: mediation - intervention to help parties in a dispute reach a voluntary agreement
  • Degree Higher: litigation - the process of taking legal action in court

Etymology

From Latin arbitratio (judgment, decision), from arbitrari (to judge).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07165558-n
Synset POS: n
