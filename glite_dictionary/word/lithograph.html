lithograph

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪθəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈlɪθəɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a print made by lithography, a printing process using a flat surface prepared to repel or absorb ink where needed
a print made by lithography

Examples

The museum displayed an original lithograph by Toulouse-Lautrec.
She bought a limited-edition lithograph to add to her art collection.
Many artists in the 19th century created posters using the lithograph process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engraving - a print made from an engraved plate, block, or other surface
  • Synonym: etching - a print made from a metal plate that has been etched with acid

Etymology

From Greek lithos (stone) + -graph (something written or drawn).

2. VERB

/ ˈlɪθəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈlɪθəɡrɑːf / (BrE)
to produce an image or print using the lithography process
produce by lithography

Examples

The artist decided to lithograph a limited edition of her latest drawing.
They have lithographed posters for the anniversary exhibition.
The magazine cover was beautifully lithographed using traditional techniques.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: print - to produce text or images on paper

Etymology

From French 'lithographie', from Greek 'lithos' (stone) + 'graphein' (to write).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01753202-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈlɪθəˌgræf / (AmE), / ˈlɪθəgrɑːf / (BrE)
a print made by drawing on a flat stone or metal plate and printing from it using lithography
a print made by lithography

Examples

The museum owns a rare lithograph by Pablo Picasso.
She bought a beautiful lithograph to hang in her living room.
This limited edition lithograph was signed by the artist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engraving - a print made by cutting a design into a surface

Etymology

From Greek lithos (stone) + -graph (something written).
