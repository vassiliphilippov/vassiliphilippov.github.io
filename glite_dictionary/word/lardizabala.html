Lardizabala

1. NOUN

/ lɑːrdɪzəˈbɑːlə / (AmE), / lɑːdɪzəˈbɑːlə / (BrE)
a genus of evergreen climbing plants native to South America, known for producing dark mauve-blue edible berries
genus of South American climbing plants

Examples

Lardizabala is valued in gardens for its attractive foliage and edible fruit.
The berries of the Lardizabala can be eaten raw or used in jams and desserts.
Botanists discovered a new species within the genus Lardizabala in the forests of Chile.

Etymology

Named after Spanish botanist Miguel de Lardizábal y Uribe.
