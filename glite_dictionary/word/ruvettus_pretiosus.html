Ruvettus pretiosus

1. NOUN

/ ruːˈvɛtəs priːˈʃoʊsəs / (AmE), / ruːˈvɛtəs priːˈʃəʊsəs / (BrE)
a very large deep-water species of snake mackerel found in tropical and subtropical seas, sometimes known as the oilfish
species of large deep-water snake mackerel

Examples

The Ruvettus pretiosus is often mistaken for other large, oily fish in restaurants.
Fishermen sometimes catch Ruvettus pretiosus in deep tropical waters.
Eating Ruvettus pretiosus can cause digestive discomfort due to its high oil content.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Ruvettus' (genus name) and 'pretiosus' meaning 'precious'.
