skirting

1. ADJ

/ ˈskɝː.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskɜː.tɪŋ / (BrE)
forming or being all around the edges; enclosing or bordering something.
enclosing or bordering the edges

Examples

The house featured a white fence skirting the entire property.
There was a narrow path skirting the lake, perfect for walking.
A row of pine trees skirting the meadow created a natural boundary.

Synset Examples

his encircling arms
the room's skirting board needs painting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: encircling - being all around the edges; surrounding completely
  • Antonym: central - in the center; not on the edges

Etymology

From the verb 'skirt' (to go or pass around the edge of), from Old Norse 'skyrta' (shirt), via Middle English 'skirt' (border, edge).
