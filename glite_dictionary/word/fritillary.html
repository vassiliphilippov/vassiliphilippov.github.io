fritillary

1. NOUN

/ frɪˈtɪləri / (AmE), / frɪˈtɪləri / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Fritillaria, a member of the lily family, with nodding, bell-shaped flowers of various colors.
plant of the genus Fritillaria

Examples

The snake's-head fritillary is famed for its distinctive checkered petals.
Several species of fritillary thrive in woodland meadows across Europe and Asia.
Gardeners often plant the crown imperial fritillary for its striking orange blooms.

Etymology

From Latin fritillaria, from fritillus (dice-box), referring to the checkered pattern on the flowers of some species.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfrɪtəˌlɛri / (AmE), / frɪˈtɪləri / (BrE)
A butterfly of the family Nymphalidae, typically having brownish wings marked with black and silver spots.
butterfly with brown-and-silver wings

Examples

We watched a fritillary fluttering among the wildflowers in the meadow.
The wings of the fritillary are beautifully patterned with silver and black spots.
Several fritillaries can often be seen near streams during summer.

Etymology

From Latin 'fritillaria,' referring to a chessboard pattern, originally from 'fritillus' (dice-box), referencing the checkered pattern on the wings.
