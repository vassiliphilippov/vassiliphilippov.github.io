copyright

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑːpiˌraɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒpiˌraɪt / (BrE)
to secure the legal right to reproduce, publish, or sell a literary, artistic, or musical work
secure exclusive rights to a work

Examples

The author decided to copyright her new novel before sending it to publishers.
It's important to copyright your designs to prevent unauthorized use.
Did you remember to copyright your screenplay before sharing it online?

Synset Examples

did you copyright your manuscript?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trademark - register a trademark to legally protect a brand or logo
  • Synonym: patent - obtain a patent to exclusively protect an invention

Etymology

From the noun 'copyright' (legal right to reproduce a work), itself from 'copy' + 'right'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02244315-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːpiraɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒpiraɪt / (BrE)
An official document granting the exclusive legal right to publish, reproduce, and sell a literary, musical, or artistic work.
document granting exclusive publication rights
legal

Examples

The author applied for a copyright to protect her novel from unauthorized copying.
You must obtain copyright before selling your original song to music companies.
The museum owns the copyright to several famous paintings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patent - exclusive right granted for an invention
  • Synonym: trademark - exclusive right to use a specific name or symbol for goods/services
  • Antonym: public domain - works not protected by copyright and free for public use

Etymology

From 'copy' + 'right', originally referring to the right to make copies of a work.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06484839-n
Synset POS: n
