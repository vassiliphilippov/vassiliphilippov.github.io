Asperula tinctoria

1. NOUN

/ æˈspɛrjʊlə tɪŋkˈtɔriə / (AmE), / æˈspɪərjʊlə tɪŋkˈtɔːrɪə / (BrE)
a creeping European perennial plant with red or pinkish-white flowers and red roots that are sometimes used as a substitute for madder in dyeing
European plant used in dyeing

Examples

The roots of Asperula tinctoria were historically used to produce red dye for fabrics.
Botanists have identified patches of Asperula tinctoria growing wild in Central Europe.
Because madder was scarce, they turned to Asperula tinctoria as an alternative dye source.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: madder - a plant whose roots are used to produce red dye

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Asperula' and species epithet 'tinctoria' meaning 'used for dyeing'.
