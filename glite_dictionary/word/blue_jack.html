blue jack

1. NOUN

/ bluː dʒæk / (AmE), / bluː dʒæk / (BrE)
a small salmon found along the northern Pacific coasts and in the Great Lakes.
a small salmon of northern Pacific and Great Lakes

Examples

Many anglers hope to catch a blue jack during the spring migration.
The restaurant served a smoked blue jack caught fresh from the Great Lakes.
Scientists are studying the habitat requirements of the blue jack population.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salmon - a large edible fish of the family Salmonidae
