Mithridates VI

1. PROPN

/ ˌmɪθrɪˈdeɪtiz sɪks / (AmE), / ˌmɪθrɪˈdeɪtiːz sɪks / (BrE)
(c. 132–63 BC) Ancient king of Pontus who expanded his kingdom and fought against Rome, eventually being defeated by Pompey.
king of Pontus, opponent of Rome

Examples

Mithridates VI resisted Roman expansion and became a legendary foe in ancient history.
The rule of Mithridates VI is remembered for his military campaigns and tenacity against Rome.
Many ancient sources recount the dramatic downfall of Mithridates VI at the hands of Pompey.

Etymology

From Greek 'Mithridates', derived from Old Persian 'Mithradatha' meaning 'given by Mithra' (a Persian god).
