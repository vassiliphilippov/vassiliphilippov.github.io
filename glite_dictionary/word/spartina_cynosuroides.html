Spartina cynosuroides

1. NOUN

/ spɑːrˈtaɪnə ˌsaɪnəsɔːˈrɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / spɑːˈtaɪnə ˌsɪnəsɔːˈrɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A tall, reed-like grass native to salt meadows and coastal marshes of eastern North America.
tall grass of salt meadows

Examples

Spartina cynosuroides is often found along the brackish edges of tidal marshes.
Ecologists study communities where Spartina cynosuroides dominates the landscape.
Birds nest among the dense stands of Spartina cynosuroides in the summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cordgrass - any of various grasses of the genus Spartina found in coastal wetlands

Etymology

From Greek 'spartina' (a kind of cord grass) and 'cynosuroides,' meaning dog-tail-like.
