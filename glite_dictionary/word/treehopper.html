treehopper

1. NOUN

/ ˈtriˌhɑpər / (AmE), / ˈtriːˌhɒpə / (BrE)
A small, jumping insect that feeds on the juices of branches and twigs, belonging to the family Membracidae.
small jumping insect on plants

Examples

A treehopper uses its powerful legs to leap away from predators.
Several treehoppers were spotted on the branches of the maple tree.
The peculiar shape of a treehopper helps it blend into twigs and leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leafhopper - a similar insect that feeds on plants, in the family Cicadellidae
