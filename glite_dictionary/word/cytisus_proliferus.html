Cytisus proliferus

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈtaɪsəs prəˈlɪfərəs / (AmE), / sɪˈtɪsəs prəˈlɪfərəs / (BrE)
A shrub native to the Canary Islands, with bristle-tipped, oblanceolate leaves, cultivated as cattle fodder.
shrub from Canary Islands used as fodder

Examples

Farmers in the Canary Islands rely on Cytisus proliferus to feed their livestock during dry seasons.
The Cytisus proliferus shrub has adapted well to poor soils and arid climates.
Researchers are studying Cytisus proliferus for its potential to improve sustainable agriculture.

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Cytisus' (a leguminous shrub) and species epithet 'proliferus', meaning 'producing offspring'.
