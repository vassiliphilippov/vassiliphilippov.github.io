Saint Baeda

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈbiːdə / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈbiːdə / (BrE)
An English monk and scholar (672–735) known for his historical and theological writings, especially the 'Ecclesiastical History of the English People.'
English monk and scholar, 672–735

Examples

Saint Baeda is often called the father of English history for his influential works.
Medieval scholars frequently referred to the writings of Saint Baeda in their studies.
The feast day of Saint Baeda is celebrated on May 25th in the Roman Catholic Church.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Venerable Bede - English monk and historian, author of 'Ecclesiastical History of the English People'

Etymology

Baeda is the Latinized form of the Old English name Bǣda, often referred to as the Venerable Bede.
