to quell

1. VERB

/ kwɛl / (AmE), / kwɛl / (BrE)
to stop or reduce a strong emotion, feeling, or situation; to suppress or overcome
suppress or overcome (emotion or situation)

Examples

Police were called in to quell the violent protest.
He drank water to quell his thirst after the long run.
She tried to quell her anxiety before the big presentation.

Synset Examples

quell my hunger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to suppress - to put an end to something, typically forcibly
  • Synonym: to soothe - to calm or reduce a strong emotion or pain
  • Antonym: to intensify - to make something stronger or more extreme

Etymology

From Old English 'cwellan' (to kill, destroy).

2. VERB

/ kwɛl / (AmE), / kwɛl / (BrE)
to put an end to something, typically by the use of force; to suppress or subdue completely
suppress or put an end to forcefully

Examples

The police were called in to quell the violent protests downtown.
Her calm words helped to quell the rising panic in the room.
Troops were sent to quell the uprising before it could spread further.

Synset Examples

squelch any sign of dissent
quench a rebellion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to suppress - forcibly put an end to
  • Synonym: crush - forcefully put an end to; suppress

Etymology

From Old English 'cwellan' (to kill, destroy).
