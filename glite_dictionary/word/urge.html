urge

1. VERB

/ ɜrdʒ / (AmE), / ɜːdʒ / (BrE)
to strongly encourage or try to persuade someone to do something
strongly encourage or persuade

Examples

His friends tried to urge him to apply for the job before the deadline.
Parents often urge their children to behave responsibly.
She urged us to remain calm during the emergency.

Synset Examples

I urged him to finish his studies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to encourage - to give support, confidence, or hope to someone
  • Antonym: to dissuade - to persuade someone not to take a particular course of action

Etymology

From Latin urgēre (to press, push, drive).

3. NOUN

/ ɝdʒ / (AmE), / ɜːdʒ / (BrE)
a strong desire or impulse to do something, especially one that is difficult to control
strong impulse or desire

Examples

Despite her urge to laugh, she managed to keep a straight face.
The urge to travel became stronger after months of staying at home.
