Hippocampus

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɪpoʊˈkæmpəs / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəˈkæmpəs / (BrE)
A genus of small marine fish known as seahorses, characterized by horse-like heads and curled tails.
genus of seahorses

Examples

The Hippocampus genus is well known for its unique method of reproduction, where males carry the eggs.
Marine biologists discovered a new species within the Hippocampus group in Southeast Asian waters.
The aquarium features several Hippocampus species, offering a close look at these fascinating seahorses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seahorse - a small marine fish of the genus Hippocampus

Etymology

From Greek 'hippos' (horse) and 'kampos' (sea monster).
