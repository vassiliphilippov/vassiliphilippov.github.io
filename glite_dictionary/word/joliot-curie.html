Joliot-Curie

1. PROPN

/ ʒoʊlioʊ kʊˈri / (AmE), / ʒɒliɒ kjʊˈriː / (BrE)
Frédéric Joliot-Curie (1900–1958), French nuclear physicist, assistant to Marie Curie, and husband of Irène Joliot-Curie, who together discovered artificial radioactivity.
French nuclear physicist (1900–1958)

Examples

Frédéric Joliot-Curie was awarded the Nobel Prize in Chemistry in 1935 alongside his wife.
The discoveries of Joliot-Curie and his wife paved the way for advances in nuclear science.
Scientists today still reference the work of Joliot-Curie when discussing artificial radioactivity.

Etymology

From the surnames Joliot and Curie, taken by Frédéric upon marrying Irène Curie.
