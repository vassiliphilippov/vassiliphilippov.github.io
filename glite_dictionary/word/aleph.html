aleph

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːlɛf / (AmE), / ˈɑːlɛf / (BrE)
the first letter of the Hebrew alphabet, corresponding to 'A'.
first letter of the Hebrew alphabet

Examples

The Hebrew word begins with aleph, giving it special significance.
He studied the shape and sound of aleph in his language class.
An aleph was engraved on the cover of the ancient manuscript.

Etymology

From Hebrew 'āleph' (first letter of the Hebrew alphabet).
