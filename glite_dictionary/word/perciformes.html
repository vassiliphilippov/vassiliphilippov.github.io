Perciformes

1. NOUN

/ pɚˈsɪfɔːrˌmiːz / (AmE), / pəˈsɪfɔːˌmiːz / (BrE)
a large order of fishes, both marine and freshwater, including perches, basses, and tunas
order of fishes including perches and basses

Examples

ThePerciformes is considered the largest order in the class of ray-finned fishes.
Many freshwater species in the Perciformes thrive in North American lakes and rivers.
Scientists discovered a new genus within the Perciformes during their deep-sea expedition.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Latin 'perca' (perch) and '-formes' (having the form of).

2. NOUN

/ pɚˈsɪfɔːrˌmiːz / (AmE), / ˌpɜːsɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
a large order of ray-finned fishes that includes true perches, basses, tuna, and many other marine and freshwater species
an order of ray-finned fishes, including perches and tuna

Examples

More species of fish belong to the Perciformes than to any other order.
The Perciformes include popular game fish such as bass and tuna.
Scientists often study Perciformes due to their wide distribution and ecological diversity.

Etymology

From Latin 'perca' (perch) + 'forma' (form), referring to perch-like fishes.
