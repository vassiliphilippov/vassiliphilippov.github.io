unenforced

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌ.nɪnˈfɔrst / (AmE), / ˌʌn.ɪnˈfɔːst / (BrE)
(of a law, rule, or regulation) not carried out, applied, or enforced, especially by legal or police action.
not enforced (law, rule, etc.)

Examples

Many local ordinances remain unenforced due to lack of resources.
The existence of unenforced regulations undermines public trust in government.
An unenforced law is often ignored by the community.

Synset Examples

too many unenforced laws can breed contempt for law

Related Terms

  • Antonym: enforced - carried out or compelled by force or law

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'enforced' (from Old French *enforcier*, from Latin *inforciare*, to force or strengthen).
