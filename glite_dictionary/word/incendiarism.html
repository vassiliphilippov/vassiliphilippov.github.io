incendiarism

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsɛndiˌɛrɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɛndiːˌærɪzəm / (BrE)
the act of deliberately setting fire to property in order to cause damage or destruction
arson; malicious fire-setting

Examples

The police suspected incendiarism after several unexplained fires broke out in the neighborhood.
Laws against incendiarism have become stricter due to an increase in arson cases.
The investigators concluded that the fire was caused by deliberate incendiarism, not by accident.

Synset Examples

the British term for arson is ‘fire-raising’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arson - the crime of deliberately setting fire to property

Etymology

From Latin 'incendium' (fire, conflagration) + '-ism'.
