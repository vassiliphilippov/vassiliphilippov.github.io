pancake

1. NOUN

/ ˈpænˌkeɪk / (AmE), / ˈpænkeɪk / (BrE)
A thin, flat cake made from batter and fried on both sides, typically served as a breakfast food or dessert.
thin, flat fried cake

Examples

She made blueberry pancakes for breakfast on Sunday morning.
The children love to pour syrup over their pancakes.
He flipped the pancake in the air with skill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crepe - a very thin French-style pancake
  • Synonym: waffle - a cake made from batter cooked between two patterned plates

Etymology

From 'pan' (the cooking vessel) and 'cake'; first recorded in Middle English.
