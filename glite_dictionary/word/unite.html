to unite

1. VERB

/ juˈnaɪt / (AmE), / juˈnaɪt / (BrE)
to join together or combine with others to form a single unit or group
to join or combine

Examples

The two companies decided to unite to compete more effectively in the market.
During the festival, people unite in celebration of their shared heritage.
The new policy united employees from different departments toward a common goal.

Synset Examples

We merged our resources

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to merge - to combine or cause to combine to form a single entity
  • Antonym: separate - to move or be apart; to divide into distinct parts

Etymology

From Latin 'unire' (to unite, make one), from 'unus' (one).
