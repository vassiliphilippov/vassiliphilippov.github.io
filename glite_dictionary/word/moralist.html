moralist

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔrəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈmɒrəlɪst / (BrE)
a philosopher or thinker who specializes in morals and moral issues
philosopher specializing in morals

Examples

The moralist argued passionately about the ethical implications of artificial intelligence.
Throughout history, every age has produced at least one renowned moralist.
She consulted a moralist to better understand the complexities of human behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethicist - a person who specializes in ethical theory

Etymology

From French moraliste, from Latin mōrālis ('moral').

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɔrəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈmɒrəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who demands strict conformity to established rules or moral standards
person insisting on strict moral rules

Examples

The new principal was a strict moralist who enforced every rule without exception.
As a self-proclaimed moralist, he criticized anyone he saw breaking social conventions.
Many regarded her as a moralist because of her unwavering insistence on traditional values.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puritan - a person with strict moral beliefs, especially about pleasure
  • Antonym: libertine - a person who rejects accepted morals, especially in sexual matters

Etymology

From French moraliste, from Latin moralis (moral).

Synset POS: n
