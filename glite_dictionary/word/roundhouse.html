roundhouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈraʊndˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈraʊndˌhaʊs / (BrE)
A circular building used for servicing and repairing locomotives, typically with rail tracks radiating from a central turntable.
circular locomotive repair building

Examples

The old roundhouse is now a railway museum open to visitors.
Engineers guided the train into the roundhouse for routine maintenance.
Several locomotives were stored in the massive roundhouse overnight.

Etymology

From 'round' + 'house', referring to the building's circular shape.

2. NOUN

/ ˈraʊn(d)ˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈraʊn(d)ˌhaʊs / (BrE)
A punch thrown with a broad, sweeping motion, typically coming from the side rather than straight on.
a sweeping, wide hook punch

Examples

The fighter surprised his opponent with a powerful roundhouse that sent him reeling.
She dodged the roundhouse just in time and countered with a quick jab.
He had a reputation for knocking out his rivals with a single, devastating roundhouse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hook - a punch in boxing delivered with a bent arm, usually to the side of the head or body
