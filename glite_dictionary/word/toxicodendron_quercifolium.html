Toxicodendron quercifolium

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɑksɪkoʊˈdɛndrən ˌkwɜrsɪˈfoʊliəm / (AmE), / ˌtɒksɪkəʊˈdɛndrən ˌkwɜːsɪˈfəʊliəm / (BrE)
A poisonous shrub native to the southeastern United States that causes a rash upon contact with skin.
poisonous shrub causing skin rash

Examples

Hikers should avoid touching Toxicodendron quercifolium as it can cause severe skin irritation.
The garden guide showed us how to identify Toxicodendron quercifolium growing near the trail.
Researchers are studying remedies for rashes caused by Toxicodendron quercifolium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poison ivy - a similar poisonous plant causing skin irritation

Etymology

From Greek 'toxikon' (poison) + 'dendron' (tree); Latin 'quercifolium' meaning 'oak-leaved'.
