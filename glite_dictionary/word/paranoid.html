paranoid

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɛrəˌnɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈpærənɔɪd / (BrE)
Suffering from paranoia or showing unreasonable fear or suspicion of others.
suffering from paranoia

Examples

She became increasingly paranoid after the break-in, always suspecting someone was watching her.
His paranoid thoughts made it difficult for him to trust even his closest friends.
Some people may act paranoid when facing high levels of stress or uncertainty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suspicious - having or showing a cautious distrust of someone or something
  • Antonym: trusting - willing to rely on the honesty or reliability of others

Etymology

From Greek 'paranoia' (madness), via modern Latin 'paranoides'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpær.ə.nɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈpær.ə.nɔɪd / (BrE)
a person who suffers from paranoia, having irrational fears or suspicions of others
person with paranoia

Examples

The paranoid kept checking the locks on his doors, convinced someone was watching him.
As a paranoid, she found it hard to trust even her closest friends.
Doctors tried to help the paranoid overcome his constant suspicions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paranoiac - a person afflicted with paranoia

Etymology

From Greek 'paranoia' (madness) via late Latin and French; originally as an adjective, later also used as noun.
