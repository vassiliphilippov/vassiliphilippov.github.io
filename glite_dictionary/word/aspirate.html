aspirate

1. NOUN

/ ˈæspəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈaspɪreɪt / (BrE)
a consonant produced with a strong burst of breath as it is pronounced, such as the English 'p' in 'pin'.
consonant pronounced with a burst of breath

Examples

In English, the 'h' sound is considered an aspirate.
The initial 'k' in 'kite' is an aspirate in many dialects.
Linguists study how the aspirate is pronounced in different languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plosive - a consonant produced by stopping the airflow, then releasing it

Etymology

From Latin 'aspirare' (to breathe upon).

2. VERB

/ ˈæspəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈaspɪreɪt / (BrE)
to remove a fluid or substance from a body or container by means of suction
remove by suction
medical

Examples

The nurse had to aspirate fluid from the patient's lungs during the procedure.
Doctors may aspirate pus from an abscess using a syringe.
Technicians routinely aspirate samples in the laboratory to prepare them for analysis.

Synset Examples

aspirate the wound

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suction - the act or process of removing something by sucking it out

Etymology

From Latin aspirare, meaning 'to breathe toward' or 'to draw in breath.'

3. VERB

/ ˈæ.spəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈæ.spɪˌreɪt / (BrE)
to pronounce (a speech sound, especially a stop) with an audible breath, as in the initial 'p' in 'pat'
pronounce with a breathy sound (phonetics)

Examples

When teaching English pronunciation, she reminds students to aspirate the initial 't' in 'top'.
In Hindi, certain consonants are always aspirated for proper pronunciation.
Linguists study how and when children begin to aspirate different sounds.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to devoice - to pronounce without voice; phonetics term

Etymology

From Latin 'aspirare' (to breathe upon), from 'ad-' (to) + 'spirare' (to breathe).

4. VERB

/ ˈæspɚˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈaspɪreɪt / (BrE)
to inhale (air, liquid, or other substances) into the lungs, often accidentally or in a medical context
inhale liquid, air, or substances
medical

Examples

The patient accidentally aspirated some water while swimming.
Doctors warn that young children can aspirate small objects if not supervised.
If food is not swallowed properly, it can be aspirated into the lungs and cause infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inhale - to breathe in
  • Antonym: to exhale - to breathe out

Etymology

From Latin aspiratus, past participle of aspirare (to breathe upon).

5. NOUN

/ ˈæs.pɚ.eɪt / (AmE), / ˈæs.pɪ.reɪt / (BrE)
a speech sound pronounced with an audible burst of breath, such as the initial 'p' in 'pot' or the letter 'h' in 'hat'
speech sound with a puff of breath

Examples

The word 'hat' begins with an aspirate sound in English.
In Hindi, some consonants are distinguished by whether they are aspirate or not.
When teaching English pronunciation, instructors often highlight the difference between aspirate and unaspirated consonants.

Synset Examples

The ‘p’ in English ‘pot’ is an aspirate, but the ‘p’ in ‘spot’ is not.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unaspirated - a speech sound produced without an audible breath
  • Synonym: consonant - a speech sound produced by obstructing the airflow

Etymology

From Latin aspiratus, past participle of aspirare, meaning 'to breathe upon'.
