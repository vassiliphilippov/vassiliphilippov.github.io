anosmatic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.ɑːzˈmæt.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌæn.ɒzˈmæt.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to or affected by an impairment or loss of the sense of smell
having loss of sense of smell
medical

Examples

Patients with anosmatic symptoms may not notice spoiled food by its odor.
An anosmatic individual faces challenges in daily life, especially when cooking or eating.
The study focused on both anosmatic and normosmic participants for comparison.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anosmia - loss or absence of the sense of smell

Etymology

From 'anosmia', from Greek 'an-' (without) + 'osme' (smell), with the suffix '-atic'.
