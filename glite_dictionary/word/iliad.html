Iliad

1. PROPN

/ ˈɪliˌæd / (AmE), / ˈɪliːəd / (BrE)
an ancient Greek epic poem attributed to Homer, recounting the events of the Trojan War, especially the siege of Troy.
epic Greek poem about the Trojan War

Examples

He spent the summer reading Homer's Iliad and studying ancient Greek culture.
Many scholars have analyzed the themes of fate and heroism in the Iliad.
The Iliad describes the dramatic events leading up to the fall of Troy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Odyssey - an ancient Greek epic poem by Homer about Odysseus's journey home after the Trojan War

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἰλιάς (Iliás), from Ἴλιον (Ilion), the Greek name for Troy.
