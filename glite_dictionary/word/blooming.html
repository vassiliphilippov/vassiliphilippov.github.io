blooming

1. NOUN

/ ˈbluː.mɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbluː.mɪŋ / (BrE)
the process by which a plant produces flowers
the process of producing flowers

Examples

The blooming of cherry trees attracts tourists from around the world each spring.
Proper lighting can encourage the blooming of indoor orchids.
The garden reached its peak blooming in late May, filling the air with fragrance.

Synset Examples

you will stop all bloom if you let the flowers go to seed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flowering - the process of producing flowers
  • Degree Higher: fruiting - the stage following flowering when a plant produces fruit

Etymology

From the verb 'bloom' + -ing, originally from Old Norse 'blóma' (to bloom).

2. ADV

/ ˈbluːmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbluːmɪŋ / (BrE)
Used as an intensifier to emphasize annoyance, admiration, or surprise, often in informal British English.
informal intensifier (esp. British)
informal british

Examples

That was a blooming good meal, thank you!
He made a blooming mess of the kitchen while cooking.
I'm blooming tired of waiting for the train.

Synset Examples

what a bally (or blinking) nuisance
a bloody fool
a crashing bore
you flaming idiot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bloody - Used as an intensifier in British English, sometimes considered rude.
  • Synonym: flaming - Another informal euphemistic intensifier, chiefly British.

Etymology

Originally referring to flowers; later adopted as an informal euphemism, especially in British English.
