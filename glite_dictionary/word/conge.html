conge

1. NOUN

/ kɑnˈʒeɪ / (AmE), / kɒnˈʒeɪ / (BrE)
(Architecture) A concave molding, especially at the base of a column, where it meets the pedestal or plinth.
concave molding in architecture

Examples

The Roman column featured a prominent conge at its base.
Restorers carefully replicated the original conge to maintain the building's historical accuracy.
A subtle conge transitions the shaft of the column to its plinth.

Etymology

From French 'congé,' meaning bevel or molding, from Latin 'congius.'

2. NOUN

/ kɑnˈʒeɪ / (AmE), / kɒnˈʒeɪ / (BrE)
an abrupt or unceremonious dismissal or rejection, especially from employment or a social situation
an abrupt dismissal or rejection

Examples

After years of loyal service, he was given the conge without any explanation.
At the end of the evening, she received a cold conge from her hosts.
Rumors spread that several managers would soon face the conge as part of restructuring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dismissal - the act of being removed from a position or job
  • Synonym: rejection - the act of refusing or dismissing someone or something
  • Antonym: retention - the act of keeping someone rather than dismissing them

Etymology

From French 'congé' (leave, dismissal), from Latin 'commeatus' (leave of absence).
