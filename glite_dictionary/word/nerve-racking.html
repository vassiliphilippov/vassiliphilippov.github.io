nerve-racking

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɝːv ˌrækɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈnɜːv ˌrækɪŋ / (BrE)
causing a lot of stress or worry; extremely stressful or irritating to the nerves
extremely stressful or worrying

Examples

Waiting for the test results was a nerve-racking experience for everyone involved.
Job interviews can be nerve-racking, especially if it's for your dream job.
The final minutes of the game were particularly nerve-racking for the fans.

Synset Examples

nerve-racking noise
the stressful days before a war
a trying day at the office

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stressful - causing mental or emotional stress
  • Antonym: relaxing - making you feel relaxed

Etymology

Formed from 'nerve' + 'racking', with 'rack' meaning to torture or cause severe stress.
