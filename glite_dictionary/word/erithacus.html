Erithacus

1. NOUN

/ əˈrɪθ.ə.kəs / (AmE), / ɪˈrɪθ.əkəs / (BrE)
A genus of Old World thrushes that includes the European robin and related species.
genus of thrushes (robins)

Examples

The European robin belongs to the genus Erithacus.
Ornithologists have classified several species within the genus Erithacus.
Characteristics of Erithacus include a compact body and a melodious song.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robin - a small Old World songbird of the thrush family with a red breast

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek ērīthakos, meaning 'robin.'

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛrɪˈθeɪkəs / (AmE), / ˌɛrɪˈθækəs / (BrE)
A genus of small Old World thrushes, including the European robin and similar birds.
genus of thrushes (robins)

Examples

The European robin belongs to the genus Erithacus.
Ornithologists recently studied the migration patterns of several species within Erithacus.
The genus Erithacus includes birds known for their melodic songs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robin - A small Old World songbird, typically with a red breast, included in the genus Erithacus.

Etymology

From New Latin Erithacus, the genus name, ultimately from Greek erithakos (robin).
