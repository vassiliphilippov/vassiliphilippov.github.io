mind-boggling

1. ADJ

/ ˈmaɪnd ˌbɑɡlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnd ˌbɒɡlɪŋ / (BrE)
extremely surprising or difficult to understand; intellectually or emotionally overwhelming.
extremely surprising or overwhelming

Examples

The scale of the universe is truly mind-boggling and hard to imagine.
The museum's collection of rare artifacts is simply mind-boggling.
Solving that complex equation was a mind-boggling challenge for the students.

Synset Examples

a mind-boggling display
a mind-boggling puzzle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astonishing - extremely surprising; hard to believe
  • Synonym: overwhelming - very great in effect; difficult to deal with
  • Antonym: mundane - lacking interest or excitement

Etymology

From 'mind' + present participle of 'boggle' (to be overwhelmed or amazed), first recorded in the mid-20th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˈmaɪnd ˌbɑɡlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnd ˌbɒɡlɪŋ / (BrE)
extremely surprising, confusing, or difficult to comprehend
extremely surprising or confusing
informal

Examples

The number of stars in the universe is truly mind-boggling.
She found the math problem to be mind-boggling at first.
It's mind-boggling how fast technology is advancing these days.

Synset Examples

The concept of a black hole is mind-boggling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astonishing - extremely surprising; amazing
  • Synonym: confusing - difficult to understand; puzzling
  • Antonym: simple - easy to understand; not complicated

Etymology

Formed from the noun 'mind' and the verb 'boggle', meaning to be astonished or overwhelmed, in the mid-20th century.
