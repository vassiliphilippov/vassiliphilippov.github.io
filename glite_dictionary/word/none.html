none

1. NOUN

/ nʌn / (AmE), / nʌn / (BrE)
(in Christian liturgy) the canonical hour occurring at the ninth hour after sunrise, traditionally observed with prayers around 3 p.m.
canonical hour, ninth after sunrise
archaic

Examples

The monks gathered in the chapel to pray at none each day.
In medieval times, the observance of none was a daily ritual.
She studied the traditions of none in early Christian monastic communities.

Etymology

From Latin 'nona (hora)' meaning 'ninth (hour)', referring to the ninth hour after sunrise.

/ nʌn / (AmE), / nʌn / (BrE)
a fixed time of prayer in the Christian Church, formerly observed at 3 PM (the ninth hour after sunrise), now often earlier
a Christian canonical hour of prayer

Examples

During Lent, the observance of none became especially meaningful for the community.
The ancient order still marks none each day as part of its rhythm of worship.
