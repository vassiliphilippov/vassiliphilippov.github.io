commodiousness

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmoʊdiəsnəs / (AmE), / kəˈməʊdiəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being spacious and convenient, especially inside a building
spaciousness or roominess

Examples

The commodiousness of the new apartment impressed all the guests at the housewarming party.
Despite its commodiousness, the hall still felt welcoming and intimate during the event.
They chose the venue for its commodiousness, ensuring all attendees would be comfortable.

Synset Examples

the capaciousness of Santa's bag astounded the child
roominess in this size car is always a compromise
his new office lacked the spaciousness that he had become accustomed to

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spaciousness - the quality of having ample space
  • Antonym: crampedness - the quality of being uncomfortably small or confined

Etymology

From Latin 'commodus' (convenient, suitable) via Late Middle English
