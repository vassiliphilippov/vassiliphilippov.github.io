conidiophore

1. NOUN

/ kəˈnɪdi.əˌfɔr / (AmE), / kəˈnɪdɪ.əˌfɔː / (BrE)
A specialized hyphal structure in fungi that bears or produces conidia (asexual spores).
fungal hypha bearing asexual spores
medical

Examples

The conidiophore extends above the surface of the mold colony to release spores.
Penicillium species can be identified by the structure of their conidiophores.
A single conidiophore may produce thousands of conidia under the right conditions.

Etymology

From Greek 'konidion' (dust, referring to spores) + '-phore' (bearer).
