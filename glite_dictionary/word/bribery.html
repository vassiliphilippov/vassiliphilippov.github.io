bribery

1. NOUN

/ ˈbraɪbəri / (AmE), / ˈbraɪbəri / (BrE)
The act or practice of offering money or other inducements to someone in order to persuade them to do something dishonest or illegal.
the act of offering inducements for illicit gain

Examples

The politician was arrested on charges of bribery after accepting money from contractors.
Many companies have strict policies to prevent bribery in business dealings.
Cases of bribery can undermine public trust in government institutions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corruption - dishonest or fraudulent conduct by those in power, typically involving bribery
  • Antonym: integrity - the quality of being honest and having strong moral principles

Etymology

From Middle English bribery (theft, extortion), from bribe (a bribe or alms), from Old French briber (to beg), later shifting to the modern sense.
