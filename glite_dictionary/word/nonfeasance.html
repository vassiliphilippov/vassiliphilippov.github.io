nonfeasance

1. NOUN

/ nɑnˈfizəns / (AmE), / nɒnˈfiːzəns / (BrE)
failure to perform an act that is required by law or duty, especially when there is an obligation to act.
failure to carry out a legal duty
legal

Examples

The company was sued for nonfeasance after failing to repair the unsafe equipment as required by law.
Nonfeasance by the government resulted in harm to the community when officials ignored public safety warnings.
In court, the defense argued that the actions amounted to nonfeasance rather than misconduct.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: malfeasance - wrongful or illegal act, especially by a public official
  • Degree Higher: misfeasance - the improper performance of a lawful act

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'non-' (not) + Old French 'faisance' (doing, making), from 'faire' (to do).
