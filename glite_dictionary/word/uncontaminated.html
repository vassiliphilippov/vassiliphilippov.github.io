uncontaminated

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.kənˈtæm.əˌneɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.kənˈtæm.ɪ.neɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
not made impure or corrupted by contact or association with something undesirable
not made impure or corrupted

Examples

The scientists required an uncontaminated water sample for their experiments.
She valued his uncontaminated perspective on the issue.
They entered a remote valley that remained uncontaminated by modern development.

Synset Examples

there is no culture uncontaminated by contact with the outside world

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pure - not mixed with any other substance or element
  • Antonym: contaminated - made impure or polluted by contact

Etymology

From un- (not) + contaminated (from Latin contaminatus, past participle of contaminare, to pollute).

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.kənˈtæm.əˌneɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.kənˈtæm.ɪ.neɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
not made impure or polluted; free from harmful or unwanted substances.
not polluted or made impure

Examples

The researchers found an uncontaminated water source deep in the forest.
Only uncontaminated soil samples were used in the experiment.
The area remains largely uncontaminated by industrial activity.

Synset Examples

unpolluted streams
an uncontaminated lake

Related Terms

  • Antonym: polluted - containing harmful or poisonous substances; made impure
  • Synonym: pure - free from any contamination or impurities

Etymology

From un- (not) + contaminated (from Latin contaminatus, past participle of contaminare 'to make impure').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01914092-s
Synset POS: s
