bivalved

1. ADJ

/ ˈbaɪˌvælvd / (AmE), / ˈbʌɪˌvalvd / (BrE)
(of a mollusk or similar organism) having a shell consisting of two hinged parts or valves.
having two hinged shells or valves (zoology)

Examples

Most clams are bivalved animals that filter food from the water.
The bivalved structure allows the shell to open and close for protection.
Oysters and mussels are well-known examples of bivalved mollusks.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: univalve - having a single shell or valve (zoology)

Etymology

Formed from 'bi-' meaning two and 'valved', relating to valves or shells.
