DES

1. NOUN

/ di.i.ɛs / (AmE), / diː.iː.ɛs / (BrE)
a synthetic nonsteroidal estrogen formerly prescribed to treat certain conditions, now largely discontinued due to its association with health risks in offspring
synthetic estrogen, former medication
medical

Examples

After prenatal exposure to DES, many daughters developed rare forms of cancer.
The use of DES in pregnant women was discontinued after links to health problems became evident.
Researchers studied the long-term effects of DES on multiple generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diethylstilbestrol - chemical name for DES, a synthetic estrogen

Etymology

Abbreviation for diethylstilbestrol, from 'diethyl' (containing two ethyl groups) and 'stilbestrol' (a synthetic estrogen compound).

2. NOUN

/ di i θɛl stɪlˈbɛstrɑl / (AmE), / diː ˌɛθɪl stɪlˈbɛstrɒl / (BrE)
a synthetic estrogen, diethylstilbestrol, formerly used in medicine and as a feed additive for livestock and poultry
synthetic estrogen (diethylstilbestrol)
medical

Examples

Physicians once prescribed DES to pregnant women to prevent miscarriages.
Studies have linked DES exposure to certain health risks in offspring.
The use of DES in livestock feed was banned due to safety concerns.

Etymology

Acronym from diethylstilbestrol, a synthetic hormone first synthesized in 1938.
