to stagnate

1. VERB

/ ˈstæɡ.neɪt / (AmE), / ˈstæɡ.neɪt / (BrE)
to stop developing, growing, or progressing; to remain at the same level or state without change
stop progressing or moving

Examples

If innovation is not encouraged, the company will stagnate and fall behind its competitors.
Water left in the pond for too long tends to stagnate and smell unpleasant.
Her career began to stagnate after she stopped seeking new challenges.

Synset Examples

Industry will stagnate if we do not stimulate our economy

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to thrive - to grow, develop, or be successful
  • Antonym: to proliferate - to grow or increase rapidly
  • Synonym: stall - to stop making progress

Etymology

From Latin 'stagnare' (to stand still, pool of water).

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈstæɡˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈstæɡ.neɪt / (BrE)
to cause (a liquid or process) to become stagnant or to cease flowing, developing, or progressing
to cause to become stagnant

Examples

The construction of the dam may stagnate the river's natural flow and harm the ecosystem.
Poor leadership can stagnate a once-thriving company, preventing innovation and growth.
Excessive bureaucracy tends to stagnate decision-making in large organizations.

Synset Examples

There are marshes that stagnate the waters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stagnant - not flowing; motionless and stale
  • Antonym: to invigorate - to give life and energy to; to make more active

Etymology

From Latin stagnatus, past participle of stagnare (to stagnate), from stagnum (standing water, pool).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈstæɡˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈstæɡ.neɪt / (BrE)
to stop developing or progressing; to become inactive or dull over time
to stop developing; become inactive

Examples

After years without investment, the company began to stagnate and lose its market edge.
If we don't try new things, our skills may stagnate over time.
The local economy continued to stagnate despite government efforts to revive it.

Synset Examples

The old man sat and stagnated on his porch
He slugged in bed all morning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to languish - to become weak or inactive, often from neglect
  • Antonym: flourish - to grow or develop in a healthy or vigorous way

Etymology

From Latin stagnare (to be stagnant), from stagnum (standing water).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02422604-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈstæɡˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈstæɡ.neɪt / (BrE)
(of a fluid or process) to cease to flow or move; to become inactive or motionless.
cease to flow or move; become inactive

Examples

The pool began to stagnate after the stream feeding it dried up.
If economic growth stagnates, unemployment can rise significantly.
Water left standing in vases may stagnate and become a breeding ground for mosquitoes.

Synset Examples

Stagnating waters
blood stagnates in the capillaries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to languish - to lose vitality or become weak; to stagnate in progress or growth
  • Antonym: flourish - to grow or develop in a healthy or vigorous way

Etymology

From Latin stagnatus, past participle of stagnare (to stand still, form a pool), from stagnum (standing water, pool).

Synset POS: v
