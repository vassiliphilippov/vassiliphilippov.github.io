naturalization

1. NOUN

/ ˌnætʃərəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnatʃərələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
(Linguistics) The process of adapting the pronunciation of a borrowed word to conform with the phonological system of the borrowing language.
adaptation of a borrowed word's pronunciation

Examples

The naturalization of French terms into English often modifies their original pronunciation.
Linguists study the naturalization of borrowed words across different languages.
Over time, naturalization makes foreign words sound native to English speakers.

Synset Examples

the naturalization in English of many Italian words

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anglicization - the act of making something English in form or character

Etymology

From naturalize + -ation, ultimately from Latin 'natura' (nature).

2. NOUN

/ ˌnætʃrəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnatʃərəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the formal process by which a foreign citizen is granted the rights and privileges of citizenship in a country
the process of gaining citizenship
legal

Examples

After years of living in the country, she finally completed her naturalization and became a citizen.
The lawyer specializes in cases involving naturalization and immigration.
Strict requirements must be met before naturalization is granted to applicants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: citizenship - the status of being a legal member of a particular country
  • Antonym: expatriation - the act of renouncing one's native citizenship

Etymology

From French 'naturalisation', from Latin 'natura' (nature).

3. NOUN

/ ˌnætʃərəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnatʃrəlʌɪˈzeɪʃn / (BrE)
the process of introducing animals or plants to a new region where they are not native, allowing them to establish and thrive
introduction of non-native species

Examples

The naturalization of rabbits in Australia has had a major impact on the environment.
Several invasive plant species spread rapidly after their naturalization in North America.
Ecologists monitor the naturalization of non-indigenous fish in local rivers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: introduction - the act of bringing a species into a new area
  • Antonym: eradication - the complete removal of a species from an area

Etymology

From Medieval Latin naturalisatio (the act of making natural), related to 'naturalize'.
