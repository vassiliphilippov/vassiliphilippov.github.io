bee

1. NOUN

/ bi / (AmE), / biː / (BrE)
A flying insect with a hairy body, known for collecting nectar and pollen, and often living in colonies; includes both social and solitary species.
hairy-bodied nectar-collecting insect

Examples

A bee landed on the flower and began collecting pollen.
Many people are afraid of being stung by a bee during the summer.
The honey produced by a bee is stored in hexagonal wax cells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wasp - a slender-bodied, often stinging insect related to bees and ants
  • Synonym: ant - a social insect living in organized colonies

Etymology

Old English *bēo*, of Germanic origin.

2. NOUN

/ bi / (AmE), / biː / (BrE)
a social event where people gather to work together on a communal task or take part in a competition, such as a spelling bee or quilting bee
communal work or contest gathering

Examples

The community held a bee to sew blankets for the shelter.
Every December, our local club hosts a fundraising bee for charity.
Grandma remembers attending a quilting bee when she was a girl.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spelling bee - contest where participants are asked to spell words
  • Synonym: quilting bee - social gathering for making quilts collectively

Etymology

Probably from dialect English, originally referring to a gathering of neighbours to help with a task, of uncertain origin.

Synset POS: n
