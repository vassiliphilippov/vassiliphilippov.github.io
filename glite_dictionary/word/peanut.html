peanut

1. NOUN

/ ˈpinʌt / (AmE), / ˈpiːnʌt / (BrE)
(informal) A young child who is small for their age, often used affectionately.
small young child (informal, affectionate)
informal

Examples

She calls her little sister peanut because she's the smallest in her class.
Come here, peanut, and let me tie your shoes.
Although he's just a peanut, he's got a big personality.

2. ADJ

/ ˈpiː.nʌt / (AmE), / ˈpiː.nʌt / (BrE)
(informal) Having little importance or power; minor or insignificant.
of little importance; minor
informal

Examples

Many considered the local official a mere peanut in the world of politics.
The company was fined what amounted to a peanut sum compared to its annual profits.
He resented being assigned only peanut responsibilities in the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insignificant - having little or no importance
  • Synonym: trivial - of little value or importance
  • Antonym: important - having great significance or value

3. NOUN

/ ˈpiː.nʌt / (AmE), / ˈpiː.nʌt / (BrE)
A small, oval, edible seed that grows in pods underground, commonly eaten roasted or used to make peanut butter.
edible underground seed/pod

Examples

She packed a handful of peanuts as a quick snack for the hike.
Some people are allergic to peanuts and must avoid them.
The peanut grows underground, unlike most other nuts that grow on trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: groundnut - another name for the peanut, especially outside North America
  • Antonym: tree nut - edible seeds from trees, as contrasted with peanuts which are legumes

Etymology

From Spanish 'cacahuete', via American English, possibly influenced by 'pea' and 'nut' due to its appearance and use.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈpiˌnʌt / (AmE), / ˈpiːnʌt / (BrE)
A widely cultivated plant of the pea family, with showy yellow flowers whose stalks bend down so that the seed pods develop underground, yielding edible seeds.
plant yielding edible underground seeds

Examples

The peanut plant has yellow flowers that bend to the soil as the pods develop underground.
Farmers in Georgia grow thousands of acres of peanuts each year.
After the rains, the peanut fields looked lush and green.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: groundnut - another common name for the peanut plant, especially outside the US

Etymology

From 'pea' + 'nut', referring to the plant's underground, nut-like seeds.

Source: WordNet

5. NOUN

/ ˈpiːnʌt / (AmE), / ˈpiːnʌt / (BrE)
a small, oval, edible seed that grows in a pod underground and is commonly eaten as a snack or used to make peanut butter; the plant producing these seeds.
edible seed in a pod; peanut plant

Examples

She packed a bag of peanuts to take with her on the hike.
He is allergic to peanuts and must avoid them at all costs.
The recipe calls for roasted peanut halves sprinkled on top.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: groundnut - another word for peanut, especially in British English
  • Synonym: monkey nut - British term for a peanut in its shell

Etymology

From American English, alteration of earlier 'pindar', from Mandingo 'pi(n)di'.
