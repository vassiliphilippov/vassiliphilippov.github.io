Guarani

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡwɑːrəˈniː / (AmE), / ˌɡwɑːrəˈniː / (BrE)
a member of an indigenous people living chiefly in Paraguay and Bolivia
member of a South American indigenous group

Examples

Many Guarani maintain their traditional customs despite modern influences.
The Guarani have a rich oral history passed down through generations.
In Paraguay, the Guarani are recognized as an important part of the nation's heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Guarani - the language spoken by the Guarani people

Etymology

From Spanish 'guaraní', from the Guarani language 'Guaraní', meaning 'warrior' or 'people'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɡwɑːrəˈniː / (AmE), / ˌɡwɑːrəˈniː / (BrE)
the indigenous language spoken by the Guarani people, mainly in Paraguay and parts of Bolivia, Brazil, and Argentina
indigenous language of Paraguay and parts of South America

Examples

The official languages of Paraguay are Spanish and Guarani.
She is fluent in both Guarani and Spanish.
Linguists have noted the unique structure of the Guarani language.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Spanish - the Romance language spoken in Spain and much of Latin America

Etymology

From Guarani (self-designation of the people), adopted into Spanish as 'guaraní'.
