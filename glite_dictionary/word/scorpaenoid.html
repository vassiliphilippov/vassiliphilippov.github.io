scorpaenoid

1. NOUN

/ skɔrˈpiː.nɔɪd / (AmE), / skɔːˈpiː.nɔɪd / (BrE)
A fish having the head armored with bony plates, belonging to the group Scorpaenoidei.
fish with bony-plated head

Examples

The researcher studied a specimen of scorpaenoid collected from the coral reef.
Many scorpaenoids possess venomous spines for defense against predators.
The armored skull of a scorpaenoid is distinctive among marine fishes.

Etymology

From New Latin Scorpaenoidei, from genus Scorpaena + Greek -oeidēs (form, resembling).
