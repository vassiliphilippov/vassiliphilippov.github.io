Arnica cordifolia

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈniːkə kɔːr.dɪˈfoʊ.li.ə / (AmE), / ɑːˈniːkə kɔː.dɪˈfəʊ.li.ə / (BrE)
A perennial wildflower of alpine areas in western North America, with heart-shaped leaves and broad yellow flower heads.
North American wildflower with heart-shaped leaves

Examples

The slopes were dotted with clusters of Arnica cordifolia in full bloom.
Researchers studied the effects of Arnica cordifolia extract in traditional medicine.
Hikers admired the bright yellow flowers of Arnica cordifolia along the alpine trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arnica - genus of plants including several wildflowers with yellow blooms

Etymology

From New Latin, where 'Arnica' is the genus name and 'cordifolia' means 'heart-leaved' (from Latin 'cor' heart + 'folia' leaves).
