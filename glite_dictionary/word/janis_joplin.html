Janis Joplin

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒænɪs ˈdʒɒplɪn / (AmE), / ˈdʒanɪs ˈdʒɒplɪn / (BrE)
(1943–1970) An American rock and blues singer known for her powerful voice and emotive performances, who became an icon of the 1960s.
American rock and blues singer (1943–1970)

Examples

The music of Janis Joplin continues to inspire fans decades after her death.
Many critics consider Janis Joplin's performance at Woodstock to be legendary.
Biographies have examined the turbulent life and career of Janis Joplin.
