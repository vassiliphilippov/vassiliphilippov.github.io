antitypic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.tɪˈtɪp.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈtɪp.ɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of an antitype, especially as a symbol or counterpart in religious or symbolic contexts.
relating to an antitype (symbolic counterpart)

Examples

The lamb was seen as an antitypic figure representing Christ in theological discussions.
Her research focused on antitypic elements in biblical symbolism.
The ritual was interpreted as having antitypic significance in both Judaism and Christianity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antitype - A person or thing that is symbolized or foreshadowed by a type; the ultimate fulfillment of a symbolic archetype.

Etymology

From antitype (from Greek antitupos, 'corresponding as a copy') + -ic.

2. ADJ

/ ˌæn.tɪˈtɪp.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈtɪp.ɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of an antitype, especially one that represents an opposite or contrasting figure or thing.
relating to an antitype or opposite

Examples

The prophet's actions were seen as antitypic to the rites of the Old Testament.
In this painting, Christ is portrayed in an antitypic role, contrasting with Adam.
The scholar wrote about the antitypic characters representing themes of reversal throughout the text.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: typical - having the distinctive qualities of a particular type
  • Synonym: antitype - a person or thing that is represented or foreshadowed by an earlier type

Etymology

From Greek 'antitupos' (opposite, corresponding), via antitype + -ic.
