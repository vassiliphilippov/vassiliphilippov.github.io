Mugilidae

1. NOUN

/ mjuːˈdʒɪlɪˌdiː / (AmE), / mjuːˈdʒɪlɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of mainly coastal marine fish, commonly known as grey mullets, characterized by elongated bodies and widely separated dorsal fins
family of grey mullet fish

Examples

Scientists observed several species from the Mugilidae family in the estuary.
The diet of Mugilidae includes algae and detritus found in shallow waters.
Fishermen often target Mugilidae in coastal regions for their commercial value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grey mullet - a common name for fish in the Mugilidae family

Etymology

From Latin Mugil (mullet) + -idae (family suffix in zoological taxonomy).

2. NOUN

/ mjuːˈdʒɪlɪˌdiː / (AmE), / mjuːˈdʒɪlɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of mainly coastal fish known as grey mullets, found in tropical and temperate waters worldwide
family of grey mullet fish

Examples

The Mugilidae are commonly known as grey mullets and are found in coastal waters worldwide.
Many species within the Mugilidae family are important in commercial fisheries and aquaculture.
Juvenile Mugilidae often inhabit estuaries before moving to the open sea as adults.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grey mullet - a common name for fish in the Mugilidae family

Etymology

From Modern Latin Mugilidae, from Mugil (the type genus), from Latin mūgil (a kind of fish).
