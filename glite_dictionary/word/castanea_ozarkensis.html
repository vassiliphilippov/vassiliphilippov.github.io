Castanea ozarkensis

1. NOUN

/ kæsˈteɪniə ˌoʊzɑːrˈkɛnsɪs / (AmE), / kæsˈteɪniə ˌəʊzɑːkˈɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A shrubby tree native to the southern midwestern United States, closely related to the Allegheny chinkapin but with larger leaves.
shrubby tree of the southern midwestern US

Examples

The rare Castanea ozarkensis is found primarily in the Ozark Mountains.
Conservationists are working to protect Castanea ozarkensis from blight and habitat loss.
The leaves of Castanea ozarkensis are larger than those of its close relative, the Allegheny chinkapin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ozark chinkapin - Common name for Castanea ozarkensis, a shrubby chestnut tree
  • Degree Lower: Allegheny chinkapin - A closely related species, Castanea pumila

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Castanea' (chestnut) and 'ozarkensis,' referring to the Ozark region.
