Claude Monet

1. PROPN

/ ˈklɔd moʊˈneɪ / (AmE), / ˈklɔːd ˈmɒneɪ / (BrE)
(1840–1926) A French impressionist painter, famed for his landscape paintings and a founder of Impressionism.
French impressionist painter (1840–1926)

Examples

Claude Monet revolutionized painting by focusing on the effects of light and atmosphere.
The Water Lilies series is one of Claude Monet's most celebrated achievements.
Art students often study the techniques used by Claude Monet to better understand impressionism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impressionism - A style of painting emphasizing light and color, pioneered by Monet and others
