bunker

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌŋkɚ / (AmE), / ˈbʌŋkə / (BrE)
a large container for storing fuel, especially on a ship or at an industrial site
large container for storing fuel

Examples

The engineer inspected the bunker before loading more coal for the journey.
During the voyage, the crew checked the fuel levels in the bunker daily.
Old steamships had massive bunkers below deck to hold their coal supplies.

Synset Examples

the ship's bunkers were full of coal

Etymology

Probably from Scots *bunker* (seat, bench, chest), of uncertain origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʌŋkər / (AmE), / ˈbʌŋkə / (BrE)
A strongly built shelter, usually below ground, used for protection against attacks, especially in military situations.
underground fortification or shelter

Examples

The soldiers took cover in the bunker during the air raid.
Underground bunkers were built to protect civilians from bombings in the war.
He designed a reinforced bunker to withstand heavy artillery fire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shelter - structure giving protection from danger
  • Synonym: trench - long, narrow ditch used in military defense

Etymology

From Scottish English 'bunker' (bench, chest), later extended in military use.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02923887-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈbʌŋ.kər / (AmE), / ˈbʌŋ.kə / (BrE)
an obstacle on a golf course, typically a hollow filled with sand, designed to challenge players
sand hazard on a golf course

Examples

The ball landed in the bunker, making his next shot much more challenging.
She practiced getting out of the bunker before her big golf tournament.
A well-placed bunker can dramatically increase the difficulty of a golf hole.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hazard - danger or obstacle in a golf course

Etymology

Originally a Scottish term; precise origins uncertain, but used in golf since at least the 18th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02923753-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈbʌŋkɚ / (AmE), / ˈbʌŋkə / (BrE)
to fill a ship's fuel compartment (bunker) with coal or oil; to refuel a ship.
refuel a ship with coal or oil

Examples

The crew began to bunker the ship before the long voyage.
All vessels must bunker in port to ensure they have sufficient fuel.
They bunkered with oil at the last stop to prepare for crossing the Atlantic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to refuel - to supply fuel to a vehicle, aircraft, ship, etc.

Etymology

From Scottish 'bunker' (bench, chest, or storage), later adapted in maritime usage to mean ship's fuel compartment.
