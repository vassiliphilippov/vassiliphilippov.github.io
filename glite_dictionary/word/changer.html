changer

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃeɪndʒər / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪndʒə / (BrE)
an automatic device on a record player or similar machine that selects and plays new records or discs without manual intervention
automatic device for changing records or discs

Examples

He replaced the old changer in his record player to enjoy seamless music playback.
The vintage stereo included a changer that could stack and play multiple vinyl records automatically.
Many early jukeboxes used a sophisticated changer to switch between songs without stopping the music.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃeɪndʒər / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪndʒə / (BrE)
a person or thing that changes something
person or thing that changes something

Examples

He was known as a changer of attitudes within the organization.
Their new coach proved to be a real changer for the team's fortunes.
She's an inveterate changer of her daily routine.

Synset Examples

an inveterate changer of the menu

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transformer - a person or thing that transforms something

Etymology

From Old French 'changier' (to change), from Latin 'cambiare' (to exchange).
