Rabindranath Tagore

1. PROPN

/ rəˌbɪndrənɑːθ təˈɡɔr / (AmE), / ˌræbɪndrənʌθ təˈɡɔː / (BrE)
(1861–1941) Indian writer, poet, and philosopher known for pioneering modern Bengali literature and composing India's national anthem.
Indian writer, poet, and philosopher (1861–1941)

Examples

Rabindranath Tagore was the first non-European to win the Nobel Prize in Literature.
Many of Rabindranath Tagore's poems are studied in schools throughout India.
The works of Rabindranath Tagore have been translated into many languages.
