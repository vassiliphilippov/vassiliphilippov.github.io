Priodontes giganteus

1. NOUN

/ praɪˈɑːdɒntiz ʤaɪˈgæn(t)iəs / (AmE), / praɪˈɒdɒntiz dʒɪˈgæn(t)iːəs / (BrE)
A large species of armadillo native to South America, notable for its great size, reaching up to about three feet long excluding the tail.
species of giant armadillo

Examples

The Priodontes giganteus is the largest armadillo species found in South America.
Researchers observed a Priodontes giganteus foraging for insects in the rainforest.
The shell of Priodontes giganteus provides excellent protection against predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giant armadillo - Large armadillo species native to South America

Etymology

From Latin; Priodontes from Greek 'prio' (saw) + 'dontes' (teeth), giganteus meaning 'giant'.
