weakly

1. ADV

/ ˈwikli / (AmE), / ˈwiːkli / (BrE)
In a way that shows a lack of strength or vitality.
in a feeble or frail manner

Examples

She smiled weakly after her illness, unable to stand for long.
He spoke weakly, barely audible over the noise of the crowd.
The injured bird flapped its wings weakly, struggling to take flight.

Synset Examples

a feeble old woman
her body looked sapless

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feebly - In a weak or feeble manner

Etymology

From the adjective 'weak' + the adverbial suffix '-ly'.

2. ADV

/ ˈwikli / (AmE), / ˈwiːkli / (BrE)
In a weak or feeble manner; to a slight or minor degree.
in a weak or feeble manner

Examples

She smiled weakly as she tried to get out of bed.
He protested weakly, not really wanting to argue.
The light shone weakly through the thick curtains.

Synset Examples

weakly agreed to a compromise
wheezed weakly
he was weakly attracted to her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feebly - in a way that lacks strength or force
  • Antonym: strongly - in a strong or forceful manner

Etymology

From weak + -ly, the adverbial suffix.
