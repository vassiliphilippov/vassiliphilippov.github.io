nonconformance

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɑnkənˈfɔrməns / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkənˈfɔːməns / (BrE)
failure or refusal to comply with accepted standards, rules, or requirements.
failure to comply with standards

Examples

The audit revealed several instances of nonconformance with safety regulations.
Any nonconformance to company policy must be reported and corrected promptly.
Her persistent nonconformance to traditional expectations made her stand out in the group.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conformance - compliance with standards or rules
  • Synonym: noncompliance - failure to comply with rules or regulations

Etymology

Formed from 'non-' (not) + 'conformance' (from 'conform'), modeled on similar negative formations.

2. NOUN

/ ˌnɑnkənˈfɔrməns / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkənˈfɔːməns / (BrE)
the state of not conforming to established standards, rules, or traditional beliefs, especially in thoughts or convictions
lack of conformity to beliefs or rules

Examples

His nonconformance with traditional doctrine made him unpopular among conservative scholars.
The audit revealed several cases of nonconformance to the safety regulations.
Courageous nonconformance to societal expectations often leads to innovative thinking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonconformity - refusal to conform to established customs or beliefs
  • Antonym: conformance - compliance with rules, standards, or laws

Etymology

From non- (not) + conformance (conforming); first attested in the early 20th century.
