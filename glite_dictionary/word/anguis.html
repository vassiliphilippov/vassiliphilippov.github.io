Anguis

1. NOUN

/ ˈænɡjuɪs / (AmE), / ˈaŋɡwɪs / (BrE)
A genus of legless lizards commonly known as slowworms or blindworms, belonging to the family Anguidae.
genus of legless lizards (slowworms)

Examples

The Anguis genus includes several species of slow-moving, legless lizards.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Anguis in central Europe.
Unlike snakes, members of Anguis have eyelids and can shed their tails as a defense mechanism.

Etymology

From Latin 'anguis' (snake), named for the serpentine appearance of these reptiles.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæŋɡwɪs / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡwɪs / (BrE)
A genus of legless lizards in the family Anguidae, commonly known as blindworms.
genus of legless lizards (blindworms)

Examples

The Anguis genus is characterized by its snake-like appearance and absence of limbs.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Anguis genus in the forests of Eastern Europe.
Unlike true snakes, members of Anguis have eyelids and external ear openings.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Anguidae - the family of lizards to which Anguis belongs
  • Synonym: slowworm - a common name for species in the genus Anguis

Etymology

From Latin 'anguis' meaning 'snake' or 'serpent', reflecting the legless appearance.
