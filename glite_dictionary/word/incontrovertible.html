incontrovertible

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪnˌkɑntrəˈvɜrtəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˌɪnˌkɒntrəˈvɜːtɪbl̩ / (BrE)
impossible to deny or dispute; unquestionably true or certain
impossible to dispute; unquestionably true

Examples

The evidence in the report was incontrovertible and left no room for doubt.
She presented incontrovertible proof that the process works as described.
After the audit, his innocence became incontrovertible.

Synset Examples

demonstrable truths

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indisputable - impossible to question or doubt
  • Antonym: controvertible - able to be disputed or denied

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + controvertibilis (able to be disputed), from controversus (disputed).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.kɑn.trəˈvɝː.tə.bəl / (AmE), / ˌɪn.kɒn.trəˈvɜː.tɪ.bəl / (BrE)
impossible to deny or dispute; not open to question or argument.
impossible to deny or dispute

Examples

The DNA evidence provided incontrovertible proof of his innocence.
She presented incontrovertible facts at the conference, leaving no room for doubt.
Experts agree that climate change is supported by incontrovertible scientific data.

Synset Examples

incontrovertible proof of the defendant's innocence
proof positive
an irrefutable argument

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indisputable - cannot be disputed or doubted
  • Synonym: unquestionable - not open to doubt; certain
  • Antonym: controvertible - able to be disputed or opposed

Etymology

From late Latin 'incontrovertibilis', from 'in-' (not) + 'controvertibilis' (able to be disputed), from Latin 'controvertere' (to dispute).
