guilt

1. NOUN

/ ɡɪlt / (AmE), / ɡɪlt / (BrE)
The state of having committed a crime or other wrongdoing; responsibility for an offense.
responsibility for a wrongdoing or offense

Examples

The jury had to decide on the guilt or innocence of the accused.
After the investigation, his guilt became apparent to everyone.
They denied any guilt in the matter, insisting they had followed all the rules.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: innocence - the state of not having committed an offense
  • Synonym: blameworthiness - deserving of blame for a wrongdoing

Etymology

From Old English 'gylt' (offense, crime).

2. NOUN

/ ɡɪlt / (AmE), / ɡɪlt / (BrE)
a feeling of remorse or responsibility for having committed an offense or wrongdoing
remorse for wrongdoing

Examples

She felt overwhelming guilt after lying to her best friend.
His face showed guilt when the subject of the missing money was raised.
Therapy helped him work through the guilt he carried for years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remorse - deep regret or guilt for a wrong committed
  • Antonym: innocence - the state of not being guilty of a crime or offense

Etymology

From Old English *gylt* (crime, offense, sin).
