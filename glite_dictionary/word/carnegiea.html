Carnegiea

1. NOUN

/ kɑːrˈneɪɡi.ə / (AmE), / kɑːˈnɛɡɪə / (BrE)
A genus of cacti consisting of a single species, the saguaro (Carnegiea gigantea), native to the Sonoran Desert of North America.
genus of cacti including the saguaro

Examples

The Carnegiea genus is represented solely by the saguaro cactus in North America.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Carnegiea in different regions of the Sonoran Desert.
Carnegiea gigantea can reach impressive heights, making it a symbol of the desert landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saguaro - large cactus native to the Sonoran Desert

Etymology

Named after Andrew Carnegie (1835–1919), the American industrialist and philanthropist.

2. NOUN

/ kɑːrˈneɪdʒiə / (AmE), / kɑːˈniːdʒiə / (BrE)
A genus of cactuses in the family Cactaceae containing only one species, the saguaro (Carnegiea gigantea), native to the southwestern United States and Mexico.
genus of saguaro cactus

Examples

Carnegiea is distinguished from other cacti by its enormous size and columnar form.
The only species in the genus Carnegiea is the iconic saguaro cactus.
Researchers studied the genetic diversity within Carnegiea populations across the Sonoran Desert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saguaro - a large, tree-like cactus native to the Sonoran Desert

Etymology

Named in honor of Andrew Carnegie (1835–1919), the American industrialist and philanthropist.
