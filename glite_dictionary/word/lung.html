lung

1. NOUN

/ lʌŋ / (AmE), / lʌŋ / (BrE)
either of the two saclike organs in the chest that are responsible for breathing by removing carbon dioxide from and adding oxygen to the blood in vertebrates
respiratory organ in the chest
medical

Examples

The human body has two lungs that are vital for breathing.
Smoking can seriously damage your lungs over time.
Doctors listened to her lungs to check for any signs of infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lung - one of a pair of organs for breathing in vertebrates

Etymology

From Old English 'lungen', from Proto-Germanic '*lungwō'.
