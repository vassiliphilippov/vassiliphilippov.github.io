observer

1. NOUN

/ əbˈzɝː.vɚ / (AmE), / əbˈzɜː.və / (BrE)
an expert who watches an event or process and provides commentary or analysis on it
expert who comments on something

Examples

The conference invited an observer to provide insights during the panel discussions.
As a keen observer of politics, she was asked to comment on the new policy.
The match was attended by a respected observer who later wrote a detailed analysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commentator - person who discusses or analyzes events, especially for the public

Etymology

From Latin observare (to watch, heed) via Middle French observer.

2. NOUN

/ əbˈzɜrvɚ / (AmE), / əbˈzɜːvə / (BrE)
a person who notices or pays attention to something, especially through watching or perceiving events
person who notices or perceives events

Examples

The observer carefully noted every detail of the experiment.
An impartial observer might have seen things differently.
As a casual observer, she was amazed by the dancers' skill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: witness - a person who sees an event, typically a crime or accident, take place
  • Antonym: participant - a person who takes part in something, as opposed to merely watching

Etymology

From Latin observare (to watch, to regard).
