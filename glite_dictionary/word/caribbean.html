Caribbean

1. NOUN

/ kəˈrɪbiən, ˌkærɪˈbiən / (AmE), / kəˈrɪbiən, ˌkærɪˈbiːən / (BrE)
the region consisting of the Caribbean Sea, its islands, and the surrounding coasts, especially those of Central America and the West Indies
region of islands and sea in the Americas

Examples

Many tourists visit the Caribbean for its beautiful beaches and warm climate.
Hurricanes can be especially destructive in the Caribbean during the summer months.
The cruise made several stops at different islands throughout the Caribbean.

Etymology

From the name of the Carib people, the indigenous inhabitants of the region.

2. NOUN

/ kəˈrɪbiən, ˌkærɪˈbiən / (AmE), / kəˈrɪbiən, ˌkærɪˈbiːən / (BrE)
the part of the Atlantic Ocean situated between North and South America, including the islands of the West Indies
sea between North and South America

Examples

Many cruise ships travel through the Caribbean during the winter months.
The intense blue waters of the Caribbean attract tourists from around the world.
Hurricanes sometimes form over the Caribbean before moving towards the United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Caribbean Sea - the sea between the West Indies and Central/South America

Etymology

From Spanish *Caribe*, referring to the indigenous Carib people.
