monoclinous

1. ADJ

/ məˈnɑːklɪnəs / (AmE), / məˈnɒklɪnəs / (BrE)
(of a flower) having both pistils and stamens in the same flower; bisexual.
having both male and female parts (flowers)

Examples

Many herbaceous plants bear monoclinous flowers to ensure effective pollination.
A monoclinous bloom contains both stamens and pistils within a single floral structure.
Unlike unisexual species, monoclinous plants can often self-pollinate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bisexual - having both male and female reproductive organs (of flowers)
  • Antonym: unisexual - having only male or only female reproductive organs (of flowers)

Etymology

From Greek monos (single) + klinein (to lean), referring to one bed for both sexes.
