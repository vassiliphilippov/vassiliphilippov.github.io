orlop

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔr.lɑp / (AmE), / ˈɔː.lɒp / (BrE)
the lowest deck of a ship, especially one where cables are stored
lowest deck of a ship

Examples

The cannons on the orlop deck were rarely used during battle.
Much of the ship’s repair work was conducted on the orlop.
Water seeped into the orlop after the storm had passed.

Etymology

From Dutch 'overloop' (covering or deck).
