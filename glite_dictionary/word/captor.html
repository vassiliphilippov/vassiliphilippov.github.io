captor

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæp.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈkæp.tə / (BrE)
a person or animal that captures and holds another person or animal, especially by force
one who captures and holds someone or something

Examples

The captor demanded a ransom for the safe return of the hostages.
The rabbit tried to escape from its captor as soon as the cage was left open.
Rescue teams negotiated with the captor to free the prisoners unharmed.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: captive - a person who has been taken prisoner or confined
  • Synonym: kidnapper - a person who seizes and carries away a person by force

Etymology

From Latin 'captor', from 'capere' meaning 'to seize'.
