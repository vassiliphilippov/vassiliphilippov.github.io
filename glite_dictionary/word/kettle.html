kettle

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈkɛt.əl / (BrE)
A container, usually made of metal and with a lid, used for boiling water or stewing food, often with a handle and spout.
metal vessel for boiling water or food

Examples

She filled the kettle with water and set it on the stove.
The old iron kettle hung over the open fire, boiling stew for dinner.
Before making tea, he always rinses the kettle to ensure it's clean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teapot - a vessel with a spout used specifically for brewing and serving tea

Etymology

From Old Norse 'katill' or Old English 'cetel', meaning a cauldron or kettle.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɛt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈkɛt.əl / (BrE)
(Geology) A depression in the ground, often filled with water to form a lake, created by the melting of a mass of ice left behind in glacial deposits.
geological hollow formed by melting glacial ice

Examples

The lake at the center of the park is a classic kettle formed after the last ice age.
Many small kettles dot the landscape, creating unique ecosystems in the region.
Geologists studied the sediments found in the kettle to understand its glacial past.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɛt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈkɛt.əl / (BrE)
a large hemispherical brass or copper percussion instrument with a tunable drumhead, also known as a kettle drum or timpani.
a large tunable percussion drum (timpani)

Examples

The timpani player adjusted the tension on the kettle before the concert began.
The orchestra featured a new kettle with a rich, resonant sound.
A skilled percussionist can coax a wide range of tones from a single kettle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kettle drum - a large bowl-shaped drum with a tunable membrane, also called timpani
  • Synonym: timpani - a set of large kettle drums used in orchestras
