incidental

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.sɪˈden.təl / (AmE), / ˌɪn.sɪˈden.tl̩ / (BrE)
an expense that is not planned or specified in advance, often minor or additional to main costs
unplanned or unspecified expense

Examples

My company gave me a daily allowance to cover meals and incidentals during my business trip.
Don't forget to budget for incidentals like tips and local transportation.
The hotel bill included a separate charge for incidentals such as phone calls and snacks.

Synset Examples

he requested reimbursement of $7 for incidental expenses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miscellaneous - various items, often minor or diverse (expenses)

Etymology

From Latin 'incidentalis', from 'incidere' (to fall upon).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13327458-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.sɪˈden.t̬əl / (AmE), / ˌɪn.sɪˈden.təl / (BrE)
Happening as a minor or secondary consequence or accompanying feature; not essential or primary.
happening as a secondary consequence

Examples

There were several incidental costs that we had not anticipated when we started the renovation.
Her promotion was incidental to the major changes happening in the company.
The damage to the fence was incidental and did not affect its function.

Synset Examples

incidental expenses
the road will bring other incidental advantages
extra duties incidental to the job
labor problems incidental to a rapid expansion
confusion incidental to a quick change

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secondary - not primary, of minor importance
  • Antonym: primary - of first importance, main

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'incidentalis', from Latin 'incident-', meaning 'falling into, happening to'.

3. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.sɪˈden.t̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˌɪn.sɪˈden.təl / (BrE)
not of primary importance; happening as a minor or secondary part in relation to something else.
not important; secondary or minor

Examples

The noise is incidental compared to the benefits the renovation will bring.
Her interest in art was always incidental to her passion for science.
