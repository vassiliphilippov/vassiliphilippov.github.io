unguarded

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɡɑrdɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈɡɑːdɪd / (BrE)
not showing caution or watchfulness; lacking protection or defense; open to risk or being careless.
not cautious or protected

Examples

He regretted his unguarded comment as soon as he said it.
The door was left unguarded, allowing the thief easy access.
She spoke in an unguarded moment, revealing more than she intended.

Synset Examples

an unguarded remark

Related Terms

  • Antonym: guarded - showing caution or self-restraint

Etymology

From un- (not) + guarded (past participle of 'guard').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00164745-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɡɑrdɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈɡɑːdɪd / (BrE)
not protected or watched over by a guard; exposed to danger or attack.
not protected; lacking a guard

Examples

The house was left unguarded while the owners were on vacation.
She found an unguarded moment to slip through the door unnoticed.
With the trophy unguarded, it was easy for the thief to steal it.

Synset Examples

an unguarded gate
his unguarded queen was open to attack

Related Terms

  • Antonym: guarded - protected or watched over; kept safe by a guard
  • Synonym: unprotected - not protected; exposed to harm or danger

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'guarded' (watched over or protected).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02535217-s
Synset POS: s
