mastocyte

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæstəˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈmastəˌsaɪt / (BrE)
a large cell found in connective tissue, containing histamine, heparin, and serotonin, released during allergic reactions or inflammation
cell involved in allergic reactions and inflammation
medical

Examples

Mast cells, also known as mastocytes, play a crucial role in allergy responses.
Scientists observed an increase in mastocytes in inflamed tissue samples.
The release of histamine by mastocytes can trigger itching and swelling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mast cell - a cell filled with basophil granules, involved in allergic reactions and inflammation

Etymology

From Greek 'mastós' (breast) and '-cyte' from Greek 'kytos' (cell).
