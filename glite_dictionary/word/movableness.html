movableness

1. NOUN

/ ˈmuː.və.bəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈməʊ.və.bəl.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of being able to be moved or rearranged.
quality of being movable

Examples

The movableness of the furniture makes cleaning the room much easier.
Engineers considered the movableness of the structure during the design phase.
Her design emphasizes the movableness of every component for flexible use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: movability - the quality of being movable or capable of movement
  • Antonym: immovability - the quality of being immovable; unable to be moved

Etymology

From 'movable' + '-ness', denoting the state or quality.
