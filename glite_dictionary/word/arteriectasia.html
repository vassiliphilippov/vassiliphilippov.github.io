arteriectasia

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːr.tə.ri.ɛkˈteɪ.ʒə / (AmE), / ˌɑː.tər.i.ekˈteɪ.si.ə / (BrE)
An abnormal dilation or expansion of an artery.
abnormal widening of an artery
medical

Examples

The ultrasound revealed arteriectasia in the patient's carotid artery.
Early detection of arteriectasia can help prevent complications such as aneurysm formation.
Chronic hypertension is sometimes associated with the development of arteriectasia.

Etymology

From Greek 'arteria' (artery) + 'ektasis' (extension, dilation).
