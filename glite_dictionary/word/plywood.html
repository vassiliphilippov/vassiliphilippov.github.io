plywood

1. NOUN

/ ˈplaɪˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈplaɪˌwʊd / (BrE)
A type of material made from thin layers of wood glued together, with the grain of each layer at right angles to adjacent layers.
laminated wood material

Examples

The walls were constructed using sheets of plywood for added strength.
She painted the plywood panels before installing them as shelves.
The sign was made from a piece of plywood cut to size.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laminate - a material made by bonding layers together

Etymology

From 'ply' (a layer) + 'wood'.
