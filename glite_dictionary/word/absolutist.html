absolutist

1. ADJ

/ ˈæb.sə.lu.tɪst / (AmE), / ˈæb.sə.ljuː.tɪst / (BrE)
Relating to or supporting the principle of absolute, unrestricted power, especially in government or politics.
supporting total or absolute power, esp. in government

Examples

The new regime followed an absolutist model, granting power to a single ruler.
Her approach to governance was criticized as excessively absolutist and intolerant of dissent.
Many historians have debated whether the emperor's policies truly reflected an absolutist ideology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authoritarian - favoring or enforcing strict obedience to authority
  • Antonym: democratic - relating to or supporting democracy or its principles

Etymology

From absolute + -ist, modeled after French 'absolutiste'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæbsəˌluːtɪst / (AmE), / ˌæbsəˈluːtɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes in or advocates political absolutism or absolute power in government
supporter of absolute power or absolutism

Examples

As an absolutist, he supported the monarch's unchecked authority over the state.
The doctrine of the absolutist became unpopular during the rise of constitutional democracies.
Many historians have studied the influence of absolutist thinkers in seventeenth-century Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absolutism - a political doctrine advocating absolute, unrestricted power, especially by a monarch
  • Antonym: constitutionalism - the doctrine that the authority of government derives from and is limited by a body of fundamental law

Etymology

Formed from 'absolute' and the suffix '-ist', indicating a person who believes in or practices a specified doctrine.
