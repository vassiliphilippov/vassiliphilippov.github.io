Polyborus plancus

1. NOUN

/ pɑːliˈbɔːrəs ˈplæŋkəs / (AmE), / pɒlɪˈbɔːrəs ˈplæŋkəs / (BrE)
a large South American bird of prey, also known as the Southern Caracara, found in open and semi-open areas
a South American bird of prey

Examples

The Polyborus plancus often scavenges for food as well as hunting small animals.
Researchers observed a Polyborus plancus nesting in the grasslands of Argentina.
The distinctive crest makes the Polyborus plancus easy to identify among other raptors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caracara - a bird of prey found in the Americas

Etymology

From Neo-Latin genus name Polyborus and species name plancus.
