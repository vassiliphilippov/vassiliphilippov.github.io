orthogonality

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔrθəˈɡæn.əl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌɔːθəˈɡæn.əl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being at right angles; the condition of two lines, planes, or vectors intersecting at a right angle, especially in mathematics and engineering.
quality of being at right angles

Examples

The orthogonality of the axes makes mathematical calculations much simpler.
In signal processing, orthogonality between waveforms allows for better separation and analysis.
Engineers often rely on orthogonality when designing support structures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perpendicularity - the quality of being perpendicular; forming a right angle

Etymology

From Greek orthogonios (right-angled): orthos (right) + gonía (angle) + English -ality.

2. NOUN

the state or condition of being at right angles; the property of being mutually perpendicular or independent, especially in mathematics or geometry
the property of being at right angles

Examples

The orthogonality of the axes allows for precise calculations in geometry.
Signal processing relies on the orthogonality of certain waveforms to separate channels.
In this system, orthogonality ensures that the vectors do not interfere with each other.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perpendicularity - the quality or state of being perpendicular

Etymology

From Greek orthogonios (right-angled), from orthos (right) + gonia (angle)
