Azadirachta indica

1. NOUN

/ ˌæzə.dɪˈræktə ˈɪndɪkə / (AmE), / ˌazədɪˈraktə ˈɪndɪkə / (BrE)
a large semi-evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent, known as the neem tree; valued for its medicinal bark, aromatic seed oil, and gum.
the neem tree, a medicinal tree species

Examples

Farmers often plant Azadirachta indica along field borders to benefit from its natural pest-repellent properties.
The seeds of Azadirachta indica are processed to obtain neem oil, which is used in cosmetics and traditional medicine.
Extracts from Azadirachta indica bark have been used for centuries as a tonic in Ayurvedic medicine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neem - common name for Azadirachta indica
  • Synonym: Melia azedarach - a related tree sometimes confused with neem

Etymology

From Modern Latin, based on Persian 'azad-darakht' (noble tree) and Latin 'indica' (of India).
