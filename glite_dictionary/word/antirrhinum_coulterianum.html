Antirrhinum coulterianum

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.tɪˈraɪ.nəm koʊlˈtɪəri.ənəm / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈraɪ.nəm kəʊlˈtɪəri.ənəm / (BrE)
A species of Antirrhinum native to California, having slender racemes of white flowers.
a California plant with white flower clusters

Examples

The Antirrhinum coulterianum blooms in early spring throughout parts of southern California.
Botanists conducted a survey and found a large population of Antirrhinum coulterianum thriving in the nature reserve.
Gardeners admire the delicate white flower spikes of Antirrhinum coulterianum during its peak flowering season.

Etymology

Genus from Greek anti- (like) + rhis (nose); species named after American botanist Thomas Coulter.
