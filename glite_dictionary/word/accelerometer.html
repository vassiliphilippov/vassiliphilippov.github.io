accelerometer

1. NOUN

/ əkˌsɛlɚˈɑmɪtɚ / (AmE), / əkˌsɛl.əˈrɒm.ɪ.tə / (BrE)
An instrument for measuring the acceleration of an object, especially used in aircraft or rockets.
instrument measuring acceleration

Examples

The engineer used an accelerometer to measure the rocket's velocity changes during launch.
Modern smartphones contain a tiny accelerometer to detect orientation and movement.
Data from the accelerometer revealed a sudden deceleration before the aircraft landed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seismometer - an instrument for measuring ground motion, especially during earthquakes

Etymology

From Latin 'accelerare' (to hasten) + '-meter' (measuring device).
