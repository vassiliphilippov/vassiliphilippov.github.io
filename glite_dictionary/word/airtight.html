airtight

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛr.taɪt / (AmE), / ˈeə.taɪt / (BrE)
impossible to find fault with or break through; free from flaws or weaknesses.
free from flaws or weaknesses

Examples

The lawyer presented an airtight case that convinced the jury beyond a doubt.
Her explanation was so airtight that nobody could find a single weakness in it.
We need to make sure our business strategy is airtight before launching the new product.

Synset Examples

an airtight defense
an airtight argument

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invulnerable - impossible to harm or damage
  • Antonym: flawed - having weaknesses or defects

Etymology

From 'air' + 'tight', originally describing containers impervious to air, later extended figuratively.

so well constructed or defended that no flaws or weaknesses are present; impossible to challenge or undermine.
impossible to challenge or undermine

Examples

3. ADJ

/ ˈɛrˌtaɪt / (AmE), / ˈeə.taɪt / (BrE)
Not allowing air or gas to pass in or out; completely sealed.
not allowing air to pass in or out

Examples

The food is stored in an airtight container to keep it fresh.
Make sure the lid is airtight before shaking the bottle.
They designed an airtight seal to prevent any leaks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watertight - not allowing water to pass in or out

Etymology

From air + tight, indicating sealed against air.
