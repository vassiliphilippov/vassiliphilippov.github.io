polemic

1. ADJ

/ poʊˈlɛmɪk / (AmE), / pəˈlɛmɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or involving strong criticism or controversy.
involving strong criticism or controversy

Examples

The article took a polemic stance against the new policy, sparking heated debate.
He was known for writing polemic essays that challenged the status quo.
Their polemic arguments often divided the audience into supporters and critics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polemical - giving or expressing strong criticism or controversy

Etymology

From Greek 'polemikos' (warlike, hostile), from 'polemos' (war).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00605207-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ poʊˈlɛmɪk / (AmE), / pəˈlɛmɪk / (BrE)
A strong written or spoken attack on a particular opinion, belief, or doctrine, especially in the context of controversy or dispute.
strong attack on opinions or beliefs

Examples

Her essay became a polemic against the injustices of the legal system.
The heated debate soon turned into a polemic about religious freedom.
His book is famous for its passionate polemic against modern art.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: controversy - a prolonged public dispute or debate
  • Synonym: argument - an exchange of diverging or opposite views
  • Antonym: apology - a defense of a belief, sometimes used as an antonym to polemic

Etymology

From French *polémique*, from Greek *polemikos* (warlike, hostile).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07198320-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ poʊˈlɛmɪk / (AmE), / pəˈlɛmɪk / (BrE)
a person who strongly attacks or defends a particular opinion or doctrine, especially in theological or philosophical argument
person who argues combatively, especially in theology

Examples

The famous polemic was known for his fierce debates on matters of faith.
As a seasoned polemic, she never shied away from challenging established views.
In the 18th century, a polemic would often write treatises attacking rival theologians.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: controversialist - a person who likes to argue controversial topics

Etymology

From Greek *polemikos* (warlike, hostile), from *polemos* (war).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10468244-n
Synset POS: n
