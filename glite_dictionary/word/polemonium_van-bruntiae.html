Polemonium van-bruntiae

1. NOUN

/ poʊˌlɛmoʊniəm vænˈbrʌntiaɪ / (AmE), / pəʊˌliːməʊniəm vænˈbrʌntɪaɪ / (BrE)
A perennial plant species with pinnate leaves and bright blue or white flowers, native to Europe.
species of perennial plant with blue or white flowers

Examples

Polemonium van-bruntiae thrives in moist, shaded wetlands across its native range.
The delicate blue flowers of Polemonium van-bruntiae make it popular with gardeners specializing in native plants.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect wild populations of Polemonium van-bruntiae in certain regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Polemonium caeruleum - another species in the Polemonium genus, known as Jacob's ladder

Etymology

Named for Mary van Brunt, an American botanist, and the genus Polemonium from Greek polémonion.
