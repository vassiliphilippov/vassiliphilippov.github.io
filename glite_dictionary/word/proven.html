proven

1. ADJ

/ ˈpruːvən / (AmE), / ˈpruːvən / (BrE)
demonstrated to be true, genuine, or effective; established beyond doubt by evidence or argument
established as true or certain

Examples

The new medication is a proven treatment for the disease.
She has a proven track record of success in sales.
Only candidates with proven leadership skills will be considered for the position.

Synset Examples

a proven liar
a Soviet leader of proven shrewdness

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unproven - not established as true or certain
  • Synonym: verified - confirmed as true or accurate

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'prove', from Latin probare (to test, prove).
