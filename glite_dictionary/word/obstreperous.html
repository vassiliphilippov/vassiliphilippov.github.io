obstreperous

1. ADJ

/ əbˈstrɛpərəs / (AmE), / əbˈstrɛpərəs / (BrE)
noisy and difficult to control, especially in a way that is aggressively boisterous or unruly
noisy and difficult to control

Examples

The obstreperous crowd drowned out the speaker's voice with their shouts and laughter.
Despite repeated warnings, the obstreperous children continued running and yelling through the museum halls.
His obstreperous behavior at the meeting disrupted the proceedings and annoyed everyone present.

Synset Examples

kept up an obstreperous clamor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unruly - difficult to control or discipline
  • Synonym: rowdy - noisy and disorderly
  • Antonym: quiet - making little or no noise

Etymology

From Latin obstrepere (to make a noise at, to oppose noisily), from ob- (against) + strepere (to make a noise).

2. ADJ

/ əbˈstrɛpərəs / (AmE), / əbˈstrepərəs / (BrE)
noisy and difficult to control, especially in a defiant or unruly manner
noisy and difficult to control

Examples

The obstreperous children refused to settle down during the assembly.
Her obstreperous behavior made the meeting impossible to conduct calmly.
Security struggled to manage the obstreperous fans after the match.

Synset Examples

obstreperous boys

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unruly - difficult to control or discipline
  • Antonym: docile - ready to accept control or instruction; submissive

Etymology

From Latin 'obstreperus', from 'ob-' (against) + 'strepere' (to make a noise).
