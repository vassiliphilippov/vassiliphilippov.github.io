to encircle

1. VERB

/ ɛnˈsɝkəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɜːkl / (BrE)
to surround or bind completely with something round or circular
to bind or surround in a circle

Examples

The castle was encircled by a deep, wide moat.
She used a silk scarf to encircle her waist and tie the dress.
A ring of children encircled the magician, eager for his next trick.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to surround - to be all around something or someone

Etymology

From the prefix en- (make, put in) + circle (from Latin circulus, small ring).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01305514-v

2. VERB

/ ɛnˈsɝkəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɜːkl / (BrE)
to form or draw a circle around someone or something
form or draw a circle around

Examples

She used a red pen to encircle all the correct answers on the test.
Police vehicles began to encircle the building after spotting suspicious activity.
His arms encircled her shoulders in a warm embrace.

Synset Examples

encircle the errors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to surround - to be all around something or someone

Etymology

From en- (make, put in) + circle; modeled after French encercler.
