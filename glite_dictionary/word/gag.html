gag

1. NOUN

/ ɡæɡ / (AmE), / ɡæɡ / (BrE)
a humorous remark, joke, or anecdote made to provoke laughter
humorous remark or joke

Examples

He opened his performance with a hilarious gag about airline food.
Sarah always has a new gag to make everyone at work laugh.
The comedian's best gag was the story about the talking dog.

Synset Examples

he told a very funny joke
he knows a million gags
thanks for the laugh
he laughed unpleasantly at his own jest
even a schoolboy's jape is supposed to have some ascertainable point

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joke - something said or done to provoke laughter
  • Synonym: jest - a thing said or done for amusement; a joke
  • Antonym: serious remark - a remark spoken with serious intent

Etymology

Possibly from Old Norse *gag-háls* (with the neck thrown back), referring to laughter.

2. NOUN

/ ɡæɡ / (AmE), / ɡæɡ / (BrE)
A device placed in a person's mouth to prevent them from speaking or making noise.
device to prevent speaking

Examples

The kidnapper used a gag to silence his victim.
She struggled against her bonds and tried to remove the gag.
In the film, the captive had a cloth gag tied tightly around his mouth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muzzle - a device placed over the mouth to prevent biting or eating

Etymology

From Old Norse *gag-háls* ('with neck thrown backward'), related to 'gag' meaning to choke or suffocate.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03415650-n
Synset POS: n

/ ɡæɡ / (AmE), / ɡæɡ / (BrE)

Examples
