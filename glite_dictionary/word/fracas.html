fracas

1. NOUN

/ ˈfreɪ.kəs / (AmE), / ˈfræk.æs / (BrE)
A noisy and often physical quarrel or disturbance, typically involving several people.
noisy quarrel or disturbance

Examples

A fracas erupted outside the stadium after the match.
The police quickly broke up the fracas between the two rival groups.
What started as a minor disagreement soon escalated into a full-blown fracas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brawl - a noisy fight in a crowd
  • Synonym: melee - a confused fight or scuffle involving many people

Etymology

From Italian 'fracasso' (crash, uproar), from 'fracassare' (to smash).
