2. NOUN

/ reɪˈɑl / (AmE), / reɪˈɑːl / (BrE)
the basic unit of currency in Brazil, equal to 100 centavos.
Brazilian currency unit

Examples

The price of the coffee was five reais at the café in Rio de Janeiro.
The real fell against the dollar during the recent economic crisis.
Travelers are advised to exchange their dollars for reais upon arrival in Brazil.

Etymology

Portuguese, from Latin 'realis' (real, actual), the name for the currency adopted officially in 1994.
