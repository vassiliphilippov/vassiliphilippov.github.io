consecrate

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑnsɪˌkreɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒnsɪˌkreɪt / (BrE)
to officially appoint someone to a clerical post, especially within a religious ceremony; to ordain as a member of the clergy
appoint to a clerical post

Examples

The bishop will consecrate the new priest during the ceremony next Sunday.
She was consecrated as a deacon in a solemn ritual attended by the whole congregation.
It is traditional for the archbishop to consecrate new bishops in an elaborate ceremony.

Synset Examples

he was ordained in the Church

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ordain - to officially make someone a priest or minister

Etymology

From Latin consecrare (to make sacred), from con- (together) + sacrare (to make sacred).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02391154-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈkɑːn.səˌkreɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.sɪ.kreɪt / (BrE)
formally dedicate someone or something to a sacred purpose, especially by a religious or solemn ceremony
to make sacred or dedicate religiously

Examples

The church will consecrate the new altar during a special ceremony next Sunday.
They decided to consecrate the building for use as a place of worship.
She chose to consecrate her life to helping the poor and needy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dedicate - to devote to a particular purpose
  • Antonym: to desecrate - to treat a sacred place or thing with disrespect

Etymology

From Latin consecratus, past participle of consecrare 'to make sacred', from con- (together) + sacrare (to make sacred).

3. VERB

devote entirely to a purpose or cause

Examples

They chose to consecrate their new home to the service of their community.
She decided to consecrate her life to medical research.
The artist wanted to consecrate his latest work to his late mentor.

Etymology

From Latin consecratus, past participle of consecrare ‘dedicate, make holy’, from con- ‘together’ + sacrare ‘to make sacred’.

5. VERB

/ ˈkɑnsəˌkreɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒnsɪkreɪt / (BrE)
to declare or make something sacred, especially by means of a formal religious ceremony
make sacred by religious ceremony

Examples

The bishop will consecrate the new church during a special ceremony this weekend.
They gathered to consecrate the altar, marking it as a holy place.
It is traditional to consecrate the bread and wine before distributing them to the congregation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bless - declare or make holy or sacred
  • Antonym: to desecrate - to treat (a sacred place or thing) with violent disrespect; violate

Etymology

From Latin 'consecratus', past participle of 'consecrare' (to make sacred), from 'con-' (together) + 'sacrare' (to make sacred).
