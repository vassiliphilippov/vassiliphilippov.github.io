to fine-tune

1. VERB

/ ˈfaɪn tuːn / (AmE), / ˈfaɪn tjuːn / (BrE)
to make small adjustments in order to improve or perfect something
make minor adjustments to improve

Examples

The chef spent hours to fine-tune the recipe until it was perfect.
Engineers fine-tune the engine's performance before every race.
She continually fine-tunes her presentation skills to stay ahead in her field.

Synset Examples

refine one's style of writing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to refine - to improve something by making small changes

Etymology

Formed from 'fine' (refined, detailed) and 'tune' (adjusting pitch), originally used in engineering and sound contexts.

3. VERB

/ faɪn tun / (AmE), / faɪn tjuːn / (BrE)
to make small and precise adjustments to something, especially a device or system, to achieve optimal performance or accuracy
make small, precise adjustments

Examples

The technicians will fine-tune the instrument before the experiment begins.
She spent hours fine-tuning her settings to improve the image quality.
The car’s suspension was carefully fine-tuned for maximum performance on the track.

Etymology

Formed from 'fine' (precise, subtle) + 'tune' (adjust), originally referring to musical tuning.
