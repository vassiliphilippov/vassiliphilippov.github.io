morbid

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɔrbɪd / (AmE), / ˈmɔːbɪd / (BrE)
characterized by or suggesting an unhealthy interest in disturbing or unpleasant subjects, especially death or disease
having an unhealthy interest in disturbing subjects

Examples

He had a morbid fascination with stories about crime and tragedy.
Her morbid curiosity led her to watch horror movies late at night.
The novel explores the protagonist's morbid thoughts and obsessions.

Synset Examples

morbid interest in death
morbid curiosity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ghoulish - having a morbid interest in death or disaster
  • Antonym: healthy - showing a normal or positive mental state

Etymology

From Latin morbidus (diseased), from morbus (disease).

3. ADJ

/ ˈmɔrbɪd / (AmE), / ˈmɔːbɪd / (BrE)
(of a part or process) caused by, relating to, or characteristic of disease; diseased or pathological.
diseased or pathological
medical

Examples

The doctor removed a morbid growth from the patient's liver.
Histological examination revealed morbid changes in the tissue structure.
Chronic infections can lead to morbid alterations in cellular function.

Synset Examples

pathological bodily processes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pathological - relating to or caused by disease
  • Antonym: healthy - in good health; free from disease

Etymology

From Latin morbidus (diseased), from morbus (disease).
