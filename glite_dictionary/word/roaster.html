roaster

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊstɚ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊstə / (BrE)
a person who cooks food by roasting it
person who cooks by roasting

Examples

She worked as a roaster in the kitchen, preparing meats for large banquets.
The roaster carefully checked the temperature to ensure the turkey was cooked perfectly.
At the outdoor event, the roaster spent hours tending the slow-cooked barbecue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cook - a person who prepares food

Etymology

From roast + -er, denoting a person who roasts.

2. NOUN

/ ˈroʊstər / (AmE), / ˈrəʊstə / (BrE)
the flesh of a large young chicken, typically over 3 1/2 pounds, suitable for roasting
meat from a roasting-size chicken

Examples

The supermarket was out of roaster so we bought two broilers instead.
For this recipe, you’ll need a whole roaster that weighs at least four pounds.
The chef seasoned the roaster before placing it in the oven for Sunday dinner.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: broiler - flesh of a young chicken suitable for broiling or frying, smaller than a roaster

Etymology

From 'roast' (to cook with dry heat), with the agent suffix '-er'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈroʊstər / (AmE), / ˈrəʊstə / (BrE)
a large, usually deep pan or dish used for roasting food, especially meat, in an oven.
pan for roasting food

Examples

She placed the turkey in the roaster and slid it into the oven.
The large roaster is perfect for preparing meals for a big family gathering.
After dinner, he washed the roaster and put it back in the cupboard.

Etymology

From roast + -er, denoting something used for roasting.

person delivering comedic criticism

Examples

The celebrity roaster had the audience laughing with his sharp wit and clever remarks.
At the annual event, each roaster took turns poking fun at the guest of honor.
She proved to be an expert roaster, delivering jokes with perfect timing and humor.
