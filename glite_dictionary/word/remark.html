remark

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈmɑrk / (AmE), / rɪˈmɑːk / (BrE)
attention or notice given to something; explicit acknowledgment or observation.
attention or explicit notice

Examples

Her absence from the meeting passed without remark from her colleagues.
The change in policy was so subtle it attracted little remark.
His error went unnoticed and without any remark from the supervisor.

Synset Examples

it passed without remark

Related Terms

  • Synonym: notice - attention or observation
  • Synonym: observation - the action of noticing or observing

Etymology

From French *remarque*, from remarquer ‘to note, observe’.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05718520-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈmɑrk / (AmE), / rɪˈmɑːk / (BrE)
a written or spoken statement expressing an opinion or adding information
statement expressing an opinion or information

Examples

She made a thoughtful remark during the meeting that changed everyone’s perspective.
His offhand remark offended several people in the audience.
The teacher’s positive remark encouraged her students to participate more.

Synset Examples

from time to time she contributed a personal comment on his account
we would appreciate input from our users on how we can improve our software

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comment - a spoken or written statement expressing an opinion or reaction
  • Synonym: observation - a remark, statement, or comment based on something one has seen, heard, or noticed

Etymology

From French remarquer (to notice, to observe), from re- + marquer (to mark).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06777755-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ rɪˈmɑrk / (AmE), / rɪˈmɑːk / (BrE)
to say or mention something as a comment; to state briefly or casually
make a brief comment or observation

Examples

She remarked that the meeting was running late.
Many attendees remarked on the speaker's sense of humor.
He remarked that the city had changed a lot since his last visit.

Synset Examples

She observed that his presentation took up too much time
They noted that it was a fine day to go sailing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comment - express an observation or opinion
  • Synonym: note - mention or observe briefly

Etymology

From French 'remarquer', from re- (again) + marquer (to mark).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01022014-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ rɪˈmɑrk / (AmE), / rɪˈmɑːk / (BrE)
to say or write something as a comment or observation
say or write as a comment

Examples
