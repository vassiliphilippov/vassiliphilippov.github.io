handicapper

1. NOUN

/ ˈhændiˌkæpər / (AmE), / ˈhændɪˌkæpə / (BrE)
a person who determines the odds for betting on a contest, especially in horse racing, based on calculations or expert judgment
person who sets betting odds

Examples

The experienced handicapper accurately predicted the favorite to win the race.
As the official handicapper, she set the odds for all the major horse races.
Many bettors rely on the advice of a skilled handicapper before placing their wagers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bookmaker - a person who takes bets, especially on sporting events

Etymology

From 'handicap', originally denoting a betting game; '-er' forms an agent noun.
