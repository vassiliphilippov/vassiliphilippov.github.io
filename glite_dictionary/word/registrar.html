registrar

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛdʒɪˌstrɑr / (AmE), / ˈrɛdʒɪstrɑː / (BrE)
an official in a college or university responsible for maintaining student records and academic regulations
official managing student records

Examples

The registrar confirmed that my transcript had been updated.
After graduation, students must collect their diplomas from the registrar's office.
The university registrar is in charge of processing enrollment documents.

Etymology

From Latin 'registrarius', from 'registrum' (register).

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛdʒ.əˌstrɑr / (AmE), / ˈrɛdʒ.ɪstrɑː / (BrE)
an official responsible for keeping records, especially of births, deaths, marriages, students, or other formal registrations
official in charge of records

Examples

The registrar updated the list of new students before the semester began.
Please submit your documents to the registrar to complete your registration.
The registrar is responsible for issuing birth certificates in this district.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clerk - an office worker who handles records or administrative tasks
  • Synonym: secretary - person employed to manage correspondence and records

Etymology

From Medieval Latin registrarius, from Latin registrum (register).

3. NOUN

/ ˌrɛdʒɪˈstrɑːr / (AmE), / ˌrɛdʒɪˈstrɑː / (BrE)
a person or company responsible for maintaining the record of owners of stocks and bonds issued by a firm
person or company keeping security ownership records

Examples

The company appointed a new registrar to oversee the transfer of stock ownership.
If you lose your share certificate, contact the registrar to arrange for a replacement.
The registrar maintains a complete and accurate list of all bondholders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transfer agent - a person or company that manages changes in ownership of securities

Etymology

From Medieval Latin registrarius (a recorder, registrar), from registrum (register).
