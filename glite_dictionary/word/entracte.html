entr'acte

1. NOUN

/ ɑnˈtɹækt / (AmE), / ˈɒn.tʁækt / (BrE)
an interval or interlude between two acts of a play, often featuring music or a performance
interlude between acts of a play

Examples

During the entr'acte, the audience mingled in the lobby and discussed the first act.
A short entr'acte featured a solo pianist before the curtain rose again.
The theater served refreshments during the entr'acte to keep guests entertained.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intermission - a short break between parts of a play or performance

Etymology

From French 'entr'acte', from 'entre' (between) + 'acte' (act).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑnˈtrækt / (AmE), / ˈɒn.trækt / (BrE)
a short performance, such as a piece of music or dance, presented between the acts of a longer theatrical or musical production
short performance between the acts of a show

Examples

During the entr'acte, the orchestra played a lively piece to entertain the audience.
Many theatergoers used the entr'acte as an opportunity to stretch their legs and discuss the first act.
The ballet featured a beautiful entr'acte performed by the younger dancers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interlude - a short entertainment performed between the parts of a play, concert, etc.

Etymology

From French 'entr'acte' (between the acts); 'entre' (between) + 'acte' (act).
