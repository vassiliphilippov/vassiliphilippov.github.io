irrigation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪr.ɪˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪr.ɪˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The artificial supply of water to land or crops to help growth, typically by means of channels, ditches, or pipes.
artificial watering of land or crops

Examples

The new dam has transformed farming in the region through improved irrigation.
Modern irrigation systems have made it possible to grow crops in the desert.
Proper irrigation is essential for rice cultivation in many countries.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: drainage - removal of excess water from land

Etymology

From Latin 'irrigatio', from 'irrigare' (to lead water to, to irrigate).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪr.əˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪr.ɪˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of cleaning a wound or body organ by flushing it with water or a medicated solution
medical flushing or washing out of a body part
medical

Examples

The surgeon performed irrigation of the wound to remove any debris before closing it.
Saline irrigation is often used to prevent infection after dental surgery.
Proper irrigation of the eye was necessary after exposure to the chemical.

Etymology

From Latin 'irrigatio', from 'irrigare' (to water, wash).
