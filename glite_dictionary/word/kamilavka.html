kamilavka

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑː.mɪˈlɑːv.kə / (AmE), / ˌkæ.mɪˈlæv.kə / (BrE)
A cylindrical hat worn by Orthodox Christian monks, clergy, and some laymen during religious ceremonies.
Orthodox Christian clerical headdress

Examples

During the service, the priest placed the kamilavka carefully on his head.
The black kamilavka distinguishes monastics from married Orthodox clergy.
He purchased a new kamilavka before his ordination ceremony.

Synset Examples

The kamilavka is a hat in the form of a rigid cylindrical head covering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kamilavkion - Variant or similar Orthodox clerical headwear

Etymology

From Greek καμιλαύκα (kamilávka), originally from Latin camellaucum (a type of head covering).
