Icterus galbula bullockii

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪktərəs ɡælˈbjuːlə bʊˈlɒkaɪ / (AmE), / ˈɪktərəs ɡælˈbjuːlə bʊˈlɒkaɪ / (BrE)
a western North American subspecies of the northern oriole, known as Bullock's oriole, with bright orange and black plumage.
western subspecies of northern oriole (Bullock's oriole)

Examples

The Icterus galbula bullockii is commonly found throughout the western United States during the breeding season.
Many birdwatchers travel to California hoping to catch a glimpse of the striking Icterus galbula bullockii.
Unlike the eastern Baltimore oriole, the Icterus galbula bullockii features a brighter orange breast and white wing patches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bullock's oriole - common name for Icterus galbula bullockii, a western oriole species
  • Degree Higher: northern oriole - the species complex including Icterus galbula and Icterus galbula bullockii

Etymology

Icterus from Greek 'ikteros' (jaundice/yellow bird); galbula from Latin for 'a small yellow bird'; bullockii after naturalist William Bullock.
