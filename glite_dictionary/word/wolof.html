Wolof

1. NOUN

/ ˈwoʊˌlɒf / (AmE), / ˈwɒlɒf / (BrE)
the language of the Wolof people of Senegal, Gambia, and Mauritania, belonging to the Atlantic branch of the Niger-Congo language family
West African language of the Wolof people

Examples

He studied the Wolof language while living in Senegal.
Many local markets conduct business in Wolof as well as French.
She can speak both Wolof and English fluently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fula - a West African language related to Wolof

Etymology

From Wolof 'Wolof', the self-designation of both people and language.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwoʊlɒf / (AmE), / ˈwɒlɒf / (BrE)
the West African language spoken mainly in Senegal, and also in the Gambia and Mauritania, by the Wolof people
West African language spoken by the Wolof people

Examples

Many people in Senegal speak Wolof as their first language.
He is learning Wolof to communicate better with local communities.
The radio broadcast was presented entirely in Wolof.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fula - a related West African language spoken by the Fula people

Etymology

From Wolof 'Wolof', the name of the people and their language.
