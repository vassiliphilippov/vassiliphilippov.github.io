subjunction

1. NOUN

/ səbˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / sʌbˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
the act of adding or supplementing something to another thing.
the act of adding or supplementing

Examples

The subjunction of additional details made the report more comprehensive.
Legal documents often require the subjunction of supplementary evidence.
The editor suggested the subjunction of references to support the author's claims.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supplementation - the act of adding something to make up for a deficiency

Etymology

From Late Latin subjunctio (a joining to), from Latin subjungere (to add, append).
