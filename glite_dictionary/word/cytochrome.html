cytochrome

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪtəˌkroʊm / (AmE), / ˈsaɪtəˌkrəʊm / (BrE)
a class of hemoproteins whose main biological function is to transfer electrons, especially in cellular respiration
electron-transferring hemoprotein
medical

Examples

The mitochondria contain several types of cytochrome that play vital roles in respiration.
Researchers studied the effect of mutations on the cytochrome involved in electron transport chains.
A deficiency in a particular cytochrome can lead to metabolic disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heme protein - protein containing a heme group that can bind or transfer electrons
  • Degree Higher: enzyme - protein that acts as a catalyst in biochemical reactions

Etymology

From Greek 'kytos' (cell) + 'chroma' (color).
