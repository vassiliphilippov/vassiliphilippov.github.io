to regorge

1. VERB

/ rɪˈɡɔrdʒ / (AmE), / rɪˈɡɔːdʒ / (BrE)
to eject the contents of the stomach through the mouth; to vomit or disgorge
eject stomach contents; vomit

Examples

The baby began to regorge his milk shortly after feeding.
Some birds will regorge food to feed their young.
After feeling ill, he had to regorge his meal.

Synset Examples

After drinking too much, the students vomited
He purged continuously
The patient regurgitated the food we gave him last night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vomit - to eject the contents of the stomach through the mouth
  • Synonym: to regurgitate - to bring swallowed food back up into the mouth

Etymology

From Old French 'regorgier', from 're-' (back) + 'gorge' (throat).
