bacteroid

1. ADJ

/ bækˈtɪə.rɔɪd / (AmE), / bækˈtɪə.rɔɪd / (BrE)
Resembling or similar to bacteria in form or function.
resembling bacteria

Examples

Under the microscope, the cells showed a bacteroid shape.
Certain soil microorganisms adopt a bacteroid form within plant root nodules.
The bacteroid structures were studied for their similarities to true bacteria.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bacterial - relating to bacteria

Etymology

From bacteria + -oid (resembling), influenced by Greek eidos (form, likeness).

WordNet ID: oewn-02668446-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈbæk.təˌrɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈbæktərɔɪd / (BrE)
a rod-shaped or branched bacterium, especially one found in the root nodules of nitrogen-fixing plants
rod-shaped bacterium in plant root nodules
medical

Examples

The presence of bacteroids in the root nodules enhances nitrogen fixation in legumes.
Scientists observed the transformation of bacteria into bacteroids during symbiosis with the host plant.
The microscopy slides clearly showed numerous bacteroids within the plant cells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bacterium - microscopic single-celled organism

Etymology

From 'bacterium' + Greek '-oeidēs' (form, shape).

Synset POS: n
