fatality

1. NOUN

/ fəˈtæləti / (AmE), / fəˈtælɪti / (BrE)
a death caused by an accident, disaster, or violence
death from accident or disaster

Examples

The police reported a fatality at the scene of the train derailment.
Wearing seatbelts has significantly reduced the risk of fatality in car accidents.
There was only one fatality during last night's severe storm.

Synset Examples

a decrease in the number of automobile fatalities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: casualty - a person killed or injured in an accident or war

Etymology

From Latin fatalitas (decree of fate, death as decreed by fate).

2. NOUN

/ fəˈtæləti / (AmE), / fəˈtælɪti / (BrE)
the quality or power of causing death or fatal outcomes
quality of causing death

Examples

The drug's hidden fatality was only discovered after several patients died unexpectedly.
Scientists warn that the fatality of the virus requires extreme caution in handling it.
Many were shocked by the fatality of the chemical, which could kill with minimal exposure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deadliness - the state of being deadly; ability to cause death

Etymology

From Latin fatalitas (decreed by fate), from fatum (fate) + -ity.
