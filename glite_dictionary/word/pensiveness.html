pensiveness

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛnsɪvnɪs / (AmE), / ˈpɛnsɪvnɪs / (BrE)
a state of deep, serious, or reflective thoughtfulness
deep serious thoughtfulness

Examples

There was a look of pensiveness on her face as she stared out the window.
His pensiveness during the meeting suggested he was contemplating something important.
The novel is filled with moments of pensiveness that invite readers to reflect on life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melancholy - a feeling of deep sadness, often with thoughtful reflection
  • Degree Lower: pensiveness - a state of deep, serious, or reflective thoughtfulness

Etymology

From Middle English *pensif*, from Old French *pensif*, from Latin *pensare* (to weigh, ponder).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛnsɪvnɪs / (AmE), / ˈpensɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being deep in thought, often accompanied by a serious or reflective mood
state of being deep in thought

Examples

His pensiveness was evident as he stared out the window, lost in thought.
There was a pensiveness in her eyes that suggested she was worried about something.
During the meeting, his pensiveness made him seem distant and unapproachable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melancholy - a feeling of deep sadness or gloom
  • Antonym: cheerfulness - the quality of being noticeably happy and optimistic

Etymology

From pensive + -ness; 'pensive' from Old French *pensif*, from penser (to think), from Latin *pensare* (to consider).
