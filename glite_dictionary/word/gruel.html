gruel

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡruːəl / (AmE), / ˈɡruːəl / (BrE)
a thin liquid food made by boiling ground cereal such as oatmeal or cornmeal in water or milk
thin porridge

Examples

The orphans were given only a bowl of gruel for dinner each night.
He stirred the gruel slowly over the fire, adding a pinch of salt for flavor.
In the old novel, gruel is often mentioned as a meager meal for the poor.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: porridge - a thick, soft food made by boiling oats or other meal in water or milk

Etymology

From Old French gruel (thin soup), from Medieval Latin grutellum, diminutive of grutum (coarse meal).
