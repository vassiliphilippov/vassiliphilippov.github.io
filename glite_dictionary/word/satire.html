satire

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæ.taɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈsæ.taɪə / (BrE)
the use of humor, irony, exaggeration, or ridicule to expose and criticize people's stupidity or vices, especially in the context of contemporary politics or other topical issues
literary genre using ridicule or irony for criticism

Examples

The novel is a brilliant satire of the upper classes and their social pretensions.
His latest play uses satire to highlight the absurdities within the political system.
Many comedians rely on satire to comment on current events and public figures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parody - an imitation of the style of a particular genre, artist, or work with deliberate exaggeration for comic effect
  • Synonym: irony - the expression of meaning by using language that normally signifies the opposite
  • Antonym: panegyric - a public speech or published text in praise of someone or something

Etymology

From Latin 'satira' (a mixture, medley, later humorous verse).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsætaɪr / (AmE), / ˈsætaɪə / (BrE)
the use of humor, irony, or ridicule to mock or criticize someone or something, often to expose foolishness or vice
witty mockery or criticism

Examples

The novel is rich in satire that targets the absurdities of modern politics.
Through biting satire, the comedian highlighted the flaws of celebrity culture.
Her article made clever use of satire to criticize corporate greed.

Synset Examples

he used sarcasm to upset his opponent
irony is wasted on the stupid
Satire is a sort of glass, wherein beholders do generally discover everybody's face but their own

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sarcasm - use of sharp or cutting remarks to mock or convey contempt
  • Synonym: irony - expression of meaning using language that normally signifies the opposite
  • Synonym: parody - imitation of a style or work for comic or critical effect

Etymology

From Latin 'satura' (medley, mixture), later associated with ridicule and criticism.
