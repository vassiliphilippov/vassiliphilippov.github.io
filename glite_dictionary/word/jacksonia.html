Jacksonia

1. NOUN

/ dʒækˈsoʊniə / (AmE), / dʒækˈsəʊniə / (BrE)
A genus of Australian shrubs with yellow flowers, lacking true leaves but with leaflike stems or branches; some species have spines.
genus of Australian shrubs with yellow flowers

Examples

The arid countryside is dotted with Jacksonia, adding a burst of yellow to the landscape.
Many species in the genus Jacksonia thrive in sandy Australian soils.
Unlike most shrubs, Jacksonia has stems that resemble leaves.

Etymology

Named after George Jackson, English botanist (1791–1811).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdʒækˈsoʊniə / (AmE), / ˌdʒækˈsəʊniə / (BrE)
A genus of Australian shrubs with yellow flowers, typically lacking true leaves but having leaflike stems or branches. Some species are spiny, others unarmed.
genus of Australian yellow-flowered shrubs

Examples

The arid plains were dotted with Jacksonia shrubs, their yellow blooms brightening the landscape.
Researchers collected several specimens of Jacksonia for the botanical survey.
Many Jacksonia species have adapted to survive in poor, sandy soils.

Etymology

Named after George Jackson, English botanist (1790–1811).
