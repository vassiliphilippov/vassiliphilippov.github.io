defacement

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfeɪsmənt / (AmE), / dɪˈfeɪsmənt / (BrE)
the act of spoiling or damaging the appearance or surface of something, especially deliberately
act of spoiling appearance of something

Examples

The museum took measures to prevent the defacement of its ancient statues.
Graffiti led to the defacement of the historic train station walls.
The city passed strict laws against the defacement of public property.

Synset Examples

the defacement of an Italian mosaic during the Turkish invasion
he objected to the dam's massive disfigurement of the landscape

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disfigurement - the act of spoiling the appearance of something

Etymology

From Middle English 'defacemente', from Old French 'defacement', from 'defacer' (to deface).
