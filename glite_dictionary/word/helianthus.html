helianthus

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːliˈænθəs / (AmE), / ˌhiːliˈænθəs / (BrE)
a genus of tall plants in the daisy family, notable for large flower heads with yellow petals and dark central disks, including the sunflower
genus of plants including sunflowers

Examples

The helianthus brightened up the entire garden with its vivid yellow petals.
Many species of helianthus are grown for their ornamental value and edible seeds.
He studied the growth patterns of helianthus in different soil types.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sunflower - a tall plant with a large yellow flower head (Helianthus annuus)

Etymology

From Greek hēlios (sun) + anthos (flower).
