Helianthus

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiliˈænθəs / (AmE), / ˌhiːliˈænθəs / (BrE)
A genus of tall, erect or branched American annual or perennial herbs with large showy flowers, including the sunflower.
genus of sunflowers and related plants

Examples

The common sunflower belongs to the genus Helianthus.
Several species of Helianthus are cultivated for their edible seeds and oil.
Botanists study Helianthus to understand plant adaptation and hybridization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sunflower - a tall plant with a large yellow flower head, Helianthus annuus

Etymology

From Greek hēlios (sun) + anthos (flower), referring to the sunflower.
