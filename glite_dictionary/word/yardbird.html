yardbird

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɑrdˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈjɑːdˌbɜːd / (BrE)
(slang) A person serving a sentence in jail or prison.
prisoner; inmate (slang)
slang

Examples

After years as a yardbird, he finally learned how the prison system worked.
The reporter interviewed a former yardbird about life behind bars.
Not everyone can survive the challenges faced by a yardbird on the inside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inmate - a person confined to an institution such as a prison or hospital
  • Synonym: prisoner - a person legally held in prison as a punishment for crimes

2. NOUN

/ ˈjɑrdˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈjɑːdˌbɜːd / (BrE)
(in U.S. military slang) a recruit who is assigned menial or routine tasks, especially around a barracks or yard.
military recruit assigned menial tasks
slang american

Examples

The new yardbird spent his first week cleaning floors and painting fences.
As a yardbird, she learned the importance of discipline and attention to detail.
Many yardbirds hoped for a quick promotion to escape the endless chores.

Etymology

Originally U.S. slang, likely derived from 'yard' (as in military yard or barracks area) plus 'bird' as slang for person.
