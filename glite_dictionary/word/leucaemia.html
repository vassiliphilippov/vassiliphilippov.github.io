leucaemia

1. NOUN

/ luːˈkiː.mi.ə / (AmE), / luːˈkiː.mi.ə / (BrE)
A type of cancer affecting the blood-forming tissues, characterized by an abnormal increase in white blood cells.
cancer of blood-forming tissues
medical british

Examples

The doctor explained that leucaemia is a cancer of the body's blood-forming tissues.
Her brother was diagnosed with acute leucaemia and immediately began treatment.
Research into leucaemia has led to improved survival rates for many patients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leukemia - American English spelling of leucaemia, cancer of blood-forming tissues

Etymology

From Greek 'leukos' (white) + 'haima' (blood), referring to the high numbers of white blood cells.
