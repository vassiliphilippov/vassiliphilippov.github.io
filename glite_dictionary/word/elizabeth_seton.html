Elizabeth Seton

1. PROPN

/ ɪˈlɪzəbəθ ˈsiːtən / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪzəbəθ ˈsiːtən / (BrE)
(1774–1821) United States religious leader who was the first person born in the United States to be canonized as a saint in the Roman Catholic Church.
first American-born Catholic saint

Examples

Saint Elizabeth Seton was canonized by Pope Paul VI in 1975.
Many schools and churches across America are named after Elizabeth Seton.
The life of Elizabeth Seton inspired generations of American Catholics.
