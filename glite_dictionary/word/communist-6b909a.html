communist

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɑːmjənɪst / (AmE), / ˈkɒmjʊnɪst / (BrE)
relating to or supporting the principles of communism or a Communist Party
relating to communism or its supporters

Examples

The country implemented several communist policies after the revolution.
She studied the history of communist movements in Eastern Europe.
Several communist governments collaborated during the Cold War.

Synset Examples

Communist Party
communist governments
communistic propaganda

Related Terms

  • Antonym: capitalist - relating to or supporting capitalism or capitalists

Etymology

From French *communiste*, from *communisme* (communism), ultimately from Latin *communis* (common, shared).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02886180-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.mjun.ɪst / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.juː.nɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports or believes in the principles of communism, especially a member of a Communist party
a believer in communism

Examples

Her grandfather was a lifelong communist who joined the party in the 1930s.
During the Cold War, being labeled a communist could damage your reputation.
The meeting attracted communists from all over the country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: socialist - a person who advocates socialism
  • Antonym: capitalist - a person who supports or believes in capitalism

Etymology

From French communiste, from communisme (communism), from commun (common, shared).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09964798-n
Synset POS: n
