T. S. Eliot

1. PROPN

/ ti ɛs ˈɛliət / (AmE), / tiː ɛs ˈɛliət / (BrE)
(1888–1965) a British poet, playwright, and literary critic, born in the United States, known for works such as 'The Waste Land' and for his contribution to modernist poetry.
British-American poet and Nobel laureate

Examples

T. S. Eliot wrote 'The Waste Land,' one of the most influential poems of the twentieth century.
Many critics regard T. S. Eliot as a central figure in the development of modernist literature.
T. S. Eliot's essays had a significant impact on the study of English literature.
