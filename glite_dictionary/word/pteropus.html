Pteropus

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛrəˌpʊs / (AmE), / ˈtɛrəpəs / (BrE)
a genus of large fruit bats known as flying foxes, found in tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Australia, and East Africa
genus of large fruit bats (flying foxes)

Examples

Pteropus species are commonly called flying foxes due to their foxlike faces and large size.
Scientists have studied Pteropus populations to understand the spread of certain diseases.
Most Pteropus bats feed primarily on fruit and nectar in tropical forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flying fox - a large bat of the genus Pteropus, known for its foxlike face

Etymology

From Greek pteron (wing) + pous, podos (foot).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛrəpəs / (AmE), / ˈtɛrəpəs / (BrE)
a genus of large fruit bats known as flying foxes, native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Australia
genus of large fruit bats (flying foxes)

Examples

The Pteropus genus includes some of the world's largest bat species.
Researchers are studying Pteropus to understand the spread of certain viruses.
Deforestation threatens many species within the Pteropus genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flying fox - a large fruit bat belonging to the genus Pteropus

Etymology

From Greek pteron (wing) + pous (foot), referring to the bat's winged feet.
