rabidity

1. NOUN

/ rəˈbɪdəti / (AmE), / rəˈbɪdəti / (BrE)
Unrestrained excitement or enthusiasm; wild intensity of feeling or action.
unrestrained excitement or enthusiasm

Examples

The crowd's rabidity at the concert created an electric atmosphere.
His rabidity for the cause inspired others to join the movement.
Some critics dismissed the fans' rabidity as mere hysteria.

Synset Examples

poetry is a sort of divine madness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fervor - intense and passionate feeling
  • Synonym: passion - strong and barely controllable emotion
  • Antonym: calmness - the state of being free from agitation or excitement

Etymology

From Latin 'rabidus' (raging), from 'rabere' (to rave).

2. NOUN

/ rəˈbɪdəti / (AmE), / rəˈbɪdəti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being extremely zealous, fanatical, or unreasonable in opinion; excessive enthusiasm or fervor, like that of someone with rabies.
extreme zeal or fanaticism

Examples

The rabidity of the political debate made it impossible for either side to compromise.
Her rabidity when discussing her favorite band surprised even her closest friends.
Media coverage sometimes exaggerates the rabidity of online fan communities.

Synset Examples

Ten years since Joss Whedon's western-in-space cult classic Firefly went off the air, the rabidity of its fandom, better known as the Browncoats, hasn't waned at all.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fanaticism - the quality of being fanatically devoted
  • Synonym: zealotry - excessive zeal or fanaticism
  • Antonym: moderation - the avoidance of excess or extremes

Etymology

From rabid + -ity, modeled after Latin rabies (madness, rage).
