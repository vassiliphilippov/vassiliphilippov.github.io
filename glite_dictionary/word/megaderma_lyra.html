Megaderma lyra

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛɡəˈdɜrmə ˈlaɪrə / (AmE), / ˌmɛɡəˈdɜːmə ˈlaɪrə / (BrE)
a large carnivorous Old World bat with very large ears, native to South and Southeast Asia
large Old World bat species

Examples

The Megaderma lyra hunts small prey using its acute hearing and silent flight.
In some regions, the Megaderma lyra, also known as the greater false vampire bat, inhabits old ruins and caves.
Researchers observed the feeding habits of Megaderma lyra in its natural forest environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bat - a nocturnal flying mammal capable of echolocation

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, genus Megaderma + species epithet lyra, referring to the lyre-shaped nose-leaf.
