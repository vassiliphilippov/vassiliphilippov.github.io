accumbent

1. ADJ

/ əˈkʌmbənt / (AmE), / əˈkʌmbənt / (BrE)
(formal) lying down or resting in a reclining position.
lying down; reclining

Examples

The patient was found accumbent on the examination table, awaiting the doctor.
In ancient Rome, guests were often accumbent as they dined at elaborate feasts.
After the long hike, she preferred to spend the afternoon accumbent on the soft grass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recumbent - lying down, especially in a position of rest

Etymology

From Latin accumbentem, present participle of accumbere (to recline at table).
