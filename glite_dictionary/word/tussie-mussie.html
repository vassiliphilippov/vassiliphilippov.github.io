tussie-mussie

1. NOUN

/ ˌtʌsiˈmʌsi / (AmE), / ˌtʌsiˈmʌsi / (BrE)
a small bouquet of flowers, typically given as a gift or carried for decoration
small bouquet of flowers

Examples

She carried a delicate tussie-mussie down the aisle on her wedding day.
Victorian ladies often exchanged tussie-mussies as tokens of affection.
He placed a fragrant tussie-mussie on her bedside table.

Synset Examples

She carried a lovely tussie-mussie of wildflowers.
A tussie-mussie makes a charming gift for any occasion.
He presented her with a tussie-mussie at the party.

Etymology

Originating in 15th-century English, likely reduplicated from 'tus' (a tuft or bunch).
