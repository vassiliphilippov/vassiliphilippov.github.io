choirboy

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwaɪɚˌbɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈkwaɪəˌbɔɪ / (BrE)
A boy who sings in a church choir, especially as part of the treble or soprano section.
boy who sings in a choir

Examples

The choirboy sang the solo beautifully during the Christmas service.
As a choirboy, he practiced after school almost every day.
Several choirboys lined up in their robes before the performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chorister - a member of a choir, often referring to a child or young singer

Etymology

From 'choir' and 'boy', after similar compound forms in English.
