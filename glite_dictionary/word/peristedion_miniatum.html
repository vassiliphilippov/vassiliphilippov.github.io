Peristedion miniatum

1. NOUN

/ pɛrɪˈstɪdiən ˌmɪniˈætəm / (AmE), / pɛrɪˈstɪdiən ˌmɪnɪˈætəm / (BrE)
A species of sea robin with bony scutes on the body and barbels on the chin, found mostly on the continental slope.
a species of sea robin

Examples

The Peristedion miniatum can be identified by the distinctive bony scutes along its body.
Marine biologists discovered several Peristedion miniatum specimens during their deep-sea exploration last summer.
With its chin barbels, the Peristedion miniatum searches the ocean floor for food.

Etymology

From the genus Peristedion and the Latin adjective miniatum, meaning 'colored with red lead.'
