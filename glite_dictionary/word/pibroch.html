pibroch

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiˌbrɑk / (AmE), / ˈpiːbrɒx / (BrE)
A form of traditional Scottish bagpipe music consisting of a theme and elaborate variations, often of a martial or ceremonial nature.
Scottish bagpipe music with variations

Examples

The sound of pibroch echoed across the moor during the ceremony.
He studied traditional pibroch for years to master the difficult variations.
A solemn pibroch was played at the memorial service.

Etymology

From Scottish Gaelic 'piobaireachd' (pipe music), from 'piobair' (piper).
