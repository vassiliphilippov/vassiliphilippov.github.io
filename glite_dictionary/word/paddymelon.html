paddymelon

1. NOUN

/ ˈpædiˌmɛlən / (AmE), / ˈpædɪˌmɛlən / (BrE)
a small reddish-brown wallaby found in scrubby areas of Australia and New Guinea
small wallaby of Australia and New Guinea

Examples

The paddymelon is often seen darting through the undergrowth at dusk.
Farmers sometimes view the paddymelon as a pest because it can damage crops.
We spotted a group of paddymelons feeding on grass near the forest edge.

Etymology

Possibly from Australian English, blending 'paddy' and 'melon' due to the animal's habitat in areas where wild melons grow.
