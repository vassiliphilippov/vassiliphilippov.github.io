Asphodeline lutea

1. NOUN

/ ˌæsfəˈdəliːn ˈluːtiə / (AmE), / ˌæs.fəˈdəliːn ˈluː.tɪ.ə / (BrE)
A Mediterranean perennial plant with a leafy stem and fragrant yellow flowers, commonly known as yellow asphodel.
Mediterranean plant with yellow flowers

Examples

Many Mediterranean gardens feature Asphodeline lutea for its tall, striking yellow blooms.
The Asphodeline lutea flourishes best in well-drained, sunny locations.
Botanists admire Asphodeline lutea for its resilience and fragrant flowers.

Etymology

From Latin, combining the genus name Asphodeline with the species epithet lutea meaning 'yellow'.
