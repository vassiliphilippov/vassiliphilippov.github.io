crook

1. VERB

/ krʊk / (AmE), / krʊk / (BrE)
to bend or cause something, especially a part of the body, to bend
to bend or cause to bend

Examples

She crooked her arm around his shoulders to comfort him.
The old man crooked his finger to gesture them closer.
He quickly crooked his leg as he dodged the obstacle in the path.

Synset Examples

He crooked his index finger
the road curved sharply

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bend - to shape or force something straight into a curve or angle

Etymology

From Old Norse 'krókr' (hook, bend).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02038516-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ krʊk / (AmE), / krʊk / (BrE)
a person who is dishonest or a criminal, especially one who cheats or steals
dishonest person; criminal

Examples

After years of fraud, the police finally arrested the notorious crook.
You can't trust that crook; he's lied about everything.
The company lost thousands of dollars because of a crook in the accounting department.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: criminal - a person who has committed a crime
  • Synonym: thief - someone who steals
  • Antonym: law-abiding citizen - someone who obeys the law

Etymology

Probably related to 'crook' (a bent thing), from Old Norse 'krókr' (hook).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09997190-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ krʊk / (AmE), / krʊk / (BrE)
a long staff with a hooked end, typically used by shepherds to manage sheep
shepherd's staff with a hooked end

Examples

The shepherd leaned on his crook while watching over the flock.
She carried a tall crook as part of her costume in the Christmas play.
The old crook had been passed down through generations of herders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: staff - a long stick used as a support or weapon
  • Synonym: hook - a curved device for catching, holding, or pulling

Etymology

From Old Norse 'krókr' (hook).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03138769-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ krʊk / (AmE), / krʊk / (BrE)
a bend or curved part, especially where something turns sharply from a straight line
a bend or curve in something

Examples

The river formed a sharp crook before flowing south.
There was a crook in the old wooden cane he carried.
They built the fence to follow every crook and turn of the path.

Synset Examples

a bend in the road
a crook in the path

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bend - a curve, especially a sharp one in a road or river
  • Synonym: curve - a smoothly bending line, without angles
  • Antonym: straight - extending in the same direction without curving

Etymology

From Old Norse 'krókr' (hook, bend).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13891966-n
Synset POS: n
