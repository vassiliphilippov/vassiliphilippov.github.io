Parthenocissus tricuspidata

1. NOUN

/ pɑːrˌθɛnoʊˈsɪsəs traɪˌkʌspɪˈdeɪtə / (AmE), / pɑːˌθɛnəʊˈsɪsəs traɪˌkʌspɪˈdeɪtə / (BrE)
An Asiatic climbing vine with three-lobed leaves and small purple berries, often used as an ornamental plant on buildings.
species of ornamental Asiatic vine

Examples

The old university building is covered in thick Parthenocissus tricuspidata that turns red every autumn.
Gardeners often plant Parthenocissus tricuspidata to create a green façade on stone walls.
Some birds are attracted to the purple berries produced by Parthenocissus tricuspidata in late summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Boston ivy - common name for Parthenocissus tricuspidata

Etymology

From Greek *parthenos* (virgin) and *kissos* (ivy); specific epithet *tricuspidata* from Latin for 'three-pointed', referring to the leaf shape.
