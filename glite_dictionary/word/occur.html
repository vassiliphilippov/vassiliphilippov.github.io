to occur

1. VERB

/ əˈkɝː / (AmE), / əˈkɜː / (BrE)
to happen or take place, especially without being planned or expected
happen; take place

Examples

Several minor problems occurred during the experiment, but they were quickly resolved.
An earthquake may occur at any time without warning.
It never occurred to me that he might not want the promotion.

Synset Examples

What is happening?
The meeting took place without an incident
Nothing occurred that seemed important

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to happen - to take place; to occur
  • Synonym: to take place - to happen; to come to pass

Etymology

From Latin 'occurrere' (to run to meet, befall).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00340744-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ əˈkɝː / (AmE), / əˈkɜː / (BrE)
to be found or present in a particular place or situation; to exist or happen
exist or be found in a place

Examples

Sexism can occur in many workplaces despite company policies.
Natural disasters like earthquakes occur all over the world.
Rare minerals occur naturally in certain regions of Africa.

Synset Examples

sexism occurs in many workplaces
precious stones occur in a large area in Brazil

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exist - to have being or reality; to be present
  • Synonym: to happen - to take place or come about

Etymology

From Latin 'occurere', meaning 'to run to meet or present itself'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02618403-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ əˈkɜr / (AmE), / əˈkɜː / (BrE)
(of an idea or thought) to come into someone's mind suddenly or unexpectedly.
(of an idea) come into the mind

Examples

It never occurred to me that the answer could be so simple.
The idea occurred to him while he was driving to work.
Did it ever occur to you that she might be right?

Synset Examples

It occurred to me that we should hire another secretary
A great idea then came to her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suggest itself - come to mind without deliberate effort
  • Antonym: to forget - fail to remember or come to mind

Etymology

From Latin 'occurrere' meaning 'to run to meet, present itself'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00725311-v
Synset POS: v
