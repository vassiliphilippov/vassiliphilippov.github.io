chrysotile

1. NOUN

/ ˈkraɪsəˌtaɪl / (AmE), / ˈkrɪs.ə.taɪl / (BrE)
A grey or green fibrous mineral that is the most common form of asbestos and a major source of commercial asbestos.
type of asbestos mineral
medical

Examples

The insulation material contained chrysotile, which is known to pose health risks if inhaled.
Scientists found significant amounts of chrysotile in the old building's roofing tiles.
Regulations now limit the use of chrysotile in construction due to its potential danger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asbestos - a group of silicate minerals used commercially, often associated with health risks

Etymology

From Greek 'chrysos' (gold) and 'tilos' (fiber).
