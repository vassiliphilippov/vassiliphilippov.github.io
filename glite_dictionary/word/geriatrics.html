geriatrics

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛriˈætrɪks / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛriˈætrɪks / (BrE)
the branch of medicine concerned with the health and care of elderly people
medical branch for elderly care
medical

Examples

After retiring, Dr. Howard decided to specialize in geriatrics to help improve the quality of life for older adults.
Advances in geriatrics have contributed to longer, healthier lives for the elderly population.
The hospital recently opened a new geriatrics ward focused on age-related diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gerontology - the study of aging and the problems of older adults

Etymology

From Greek gerōn (old man) + -iatric (relating to medical healing).
