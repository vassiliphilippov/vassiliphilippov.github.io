Isospondyli

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪsoʊˈspɒndɪlaɪ / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəˈspɒndɪlaɪ / (BrE)
an order of primitive teleost fishes with soft fins, including the salmon, trout, herring, shad, sardines, anchovies, whitefish, smelts, and tarpons
order of primitive soft-finned fishes

Examples

Salmon, trout, and sardines are all members of the Isospondyli.
Ichthyologists study the evolutionary development of the Isospondyli in relation to other teleosts.
The Isospondyli are characterized by their soft-finned structure and primitive features.

Etymology

From Greek iso- (equal) + spondylos (vertebra), referring to the structure of their vertebrae.
