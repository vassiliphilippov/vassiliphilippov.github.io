ductulus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌktjələs / (AmE), / ˈdʌktjʊləs / (BrE)
a very small duct, especially in anatomical or biological contexts
a very small duct
medical

Examples

The seminiferous tubules connect to the rete testis via tiny ductuli.
Damage to the ductulus can impair fluid transport in certain glands.
Each ductulus plays a crucial role in the movement of secretions.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: duct - a tube or channel that carries liquid or gas

Etymology

From Latin 'ductulus', diminutive form of 'ductus' (duct).
