Megaloptera

1. NOUN

/ mɛɡəˈlɑptərə / (AmE), / mɛɡəˈlɒptərə / (BrE)
an order or suborder of insects that includes alderflies, dobsonflies, and related species, sometimes considered distinct from other similar groups
order of insects including alderflies and dobsonflies

Examples

The larvae of Megaloptera are aquatic and play a role in stream ecosystems.
Some field guides classify snakeflies under Megaloptera due to their morphological similarities.
Entomologists debated whether Megaloptera should be considered a separate order from Neuroptera.

Etymology

From Greek 'megalo-' meaning 'large' and 'pteron' meaning 'wing', referring to their large wings.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛɡəˈlɑptərə / (AmE), / ˌmɛɡəˈlɒptərə / (BrE)
a suborder (sometimes considered a separate order) of insects that includes alderflies, dobsonflies, and related species
insect suborder: alderflies, dobsonflies, etc.

Examples

Researchers collected several specimens belonging to Megaloptera from the riverbank.
Unlike many other insects, members of Megaloptera have prominent mandibles in their larval stage.
The biology class studied the life cycles of Megaloptera as part of their entomology section.

Etymology

From Greek 'megas' (large) + 'pteron' (wing), referring to their large wings.
