Poinciana

1. NOUN

/ pɔɪnˈsiːənə / (AmE), / pɔɪnˈsiːənə / (BrE)
a small group of ornamental tropical shrubs or trees, sometimes classified as a subgenus, known for their showy flowers; not recognized in some modern classifications
ornamental tropical shrub or tree with showy flowers

Examples

The brilliant red blossoms of the Poinciana lit up the entire garden in summer.
Many tropical cities use the Poinciana as a decorative street tree for its shade and flowers.
Our neighbor planted a Poinciana in her front yard, and it has become quite the attraction.

Etymology

From the name of French botanist Philippe de Longvilliers de Poincy.
