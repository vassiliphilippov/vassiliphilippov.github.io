quandang

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑnˌdæŋ / (AmE), / ˈkwɒn.daŋ / (BrE)
a red, edible fruit native to Australia, often used for desserts and jams
Australian edible red fruit
british

Examples

The bush was heavy with ripe quandang, its bright fruit visible from a distance.
She made a delicious jam from freshly picked quandang.
Dried quandang is a popular ingredient in traditional Australian desserts.

Etymology

From Wiradjuri (an Australian Aboriginal language) *quandang*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑn.dæŋ / (AmE), / ˈkwɒn.dæŋ / (BrE)
An Australian tree of the genus Santalum, especially Santalum acuminatum, which bears edible fruit and a nutlike seed.
Australian tree with edible fruit and seed

Examples

The quandang thrives in arid regions of Australia and is valued for its fruit.
Many Indigenous Australians have traditionally used quandang as a food and medicine.
I tasted a dessert made from quandang at the local bush tucker café.

Etymology

From Wiradjuri (an Australian Aboriginal language) *quandong*, a name for the tree and its fruit.
