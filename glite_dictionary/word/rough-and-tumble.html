rough-and-tumble

1. NOUN

/ ˌrʌf ənd ˈtʌmbəl / (AmE), / ˌrʌf ən ˈtʌmbəl / (BrE)
a disorderly fight or struggle involving confusion and little regard for rules
disorderly fight or struggle

Examples

After the rough-and-tumble on the playground, both boys had scraped knees.
The world of politics can become a rough-and-tumble where only the tough survive.
Despite the rough-and-tumble of the championship match, the players showed good sportsmanship afterward.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brawl - a noisy quarrel or fight in a public place
  • Synonym: melee - a confused fight or scuffle
