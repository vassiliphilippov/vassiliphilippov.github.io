to unveil

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈveɪl / (AmE), / ʌnˈveɪl / (BrE)
to make something known or visible, especially for the first time; to reveal something that was hidden or secret
reveal or make visible for the first time

Examples

The company will unveil its new product next week at the conference.
She decided to unveil her latest painting at the art exhibition.
The investigation aims to unveil the truth behind the mysterious disappearance.

Synset Examples

Summer brings out bright clothes
He brings out the best in her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reveal - to make something known that was previously secret or hidden
  • Synonym: to disclose - to make secret or new information known
  • Antonym: to conceal - to hide or keep something secret

Etymology

From un- (not) + veil (from Old French 'veile', from Latin 'vela', meaning 'sail, curtain').

2. VERB

/ ʌnˈveɪl / (AmE), / ʌnˈveɪl / (BrE)
to remove a veil from, especially from a person's face or head
remove a veil from someone

Examples

In some traditions, brides unveil themselves at the end of the wedding ceremony.
The actress was reluctant to unveil herself to the public after years of seclusion.
Many women chose not to unveil themselves in front of strangers.

Synset Examples

Women must not unveil themselves in public in some religious societies

Related Terms

  • Antonym: veil - cover with or as if with a veil

Etymology

From un- (not) + veil, from Old French 'veile' (veil).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01486108-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ʌnˈveɪl / (AmE), / ʌnˈveɪl / (BrE)
to remove a covering from something, especially as part of a ceremonial presentation or to reveal for the first time
remove a covering; reveal for the first time

Examples

The museum will unveil the newly restored painting next week.
At the ceremony, the mayor helped unveil the commemorative plaque.
She was nervous as they prepared to unveil her latest sculpture to the public.

Synset Examples

unveil a painting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reveal - to make known or show something previously hidden

Etymology

From un- (not) + veil, from Old French 'veile' (veil), ultimately from Latin 'velum' (sail, covering).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
