Pseudotsuga macrocarpa

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuːdoʊˈtsuːɡə ˌmækroʊˈkɑːrpə / (AmE), / ˌsjuːdəʊˈtsuːɡə ˌmækrəʊˈkɑːpə / (BrE)
A species of Douglas fir native to California, distinguished by its large cones measuring 4–8 inches long.
Douglas fir species native to California

Examples

The Pseudotsuga macrocarpa is well adapted to California's dry, rocky soils.
Forest management efforts focus on preserving stands of Pseudotsuga macrocarpa due to their ecological importance.
Botanists identified the tree as Pseudotsuga macrocarpa by examining its impressively large, distinctive cones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Douglas fir - A common name for species in the Pseudotsuga genus

Etymology

From Greek 'pseudo-' (false) and 'tsuga' (Japanese for hemlock), with Latin 'macrocarpa' meaning 'large fruit'.
