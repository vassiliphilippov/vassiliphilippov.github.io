amenable

1. ADJ

/ əˈmiːnəbəl / (AmE), / əˈmiːnəbəl / (BrE)
willing to be influenced or controlled; easily persuaded or responsive to suggestions or advice.
open to influence or persuasion

Examples

The manager was amenable to our new proposal, so changes were quickly approved.
Her children are usually amenable to instructions if you explain the reasons behind them.
The board is not amenable to making exceptions, even for special circumstances.

Synset Examples

a tractable student
an amenable child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tractable - easy to control or influence
  • Antonym: intractable - hard to control or deal with

Etymology

From Old French *amener* (to lead) and ultimately from Latin *minare* (to drive, lead).

Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ əˈmiːnəbl̩ / (AmE), / əˈmiːnəbl̩ / (BrE)
(of a thing) capable of being acted upon in a particular way; responsive or susceptible to a proposed action or treatment.
capable of being acted upon

Examples

The new treatment proved amenable to the disease and improved the patient's condition.
Only patients whose conditions are amenable to this therapy should be enrolled in the trial.
Some stains are not amenable to standard cleaning methods and require special solvents.

Synset Examples

an amenable hospitalization should not result in untimely death
the tumor was not amenable to surgical treatment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: susceptible - likely or liable to be influenced or harmed by a particular thing
  • Antonym: resistant - opposing the effect or action of something

Etymology

From Old French *amener* (to bring, lead), from Latin *minare* (to drive).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02369880-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ əˈminəbəl, əˈmɛnəbəl / (AmE), / əˈmiːnəbl, əˈmɛnəbl / (BrE)
liable or accountable to a higher authority or jurisdiction; answerable.
answerable to authority

Examples

The president is amenable to the constitutional court and must follow its rulings.
Companies operating in this country are amenable to local regulations and laws.
As a government official, she is amenable to oversight by the parliament.

Synset Examples

the president is amenable to the constitutional court

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accountable - required or expected to justify actions or decisions; responsible
  • Antonym: unaccountable - not required to justify actions or not subject to authority

Etymology

From Anglo-French 'amenable', from Old French 'amener' (to bring to).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02004515-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ əˈmiːnəbəl / (AmE), / əˈmiːnəbl / (BrE)
willing to be influenced or controlled; open and responsive to suggestion or authority.
open or willing to be influenced

Examples

The staff is amenable to implementing the changes recommended by management.
She was amenable to new ideas and quickly adapted to the team's suggestions.
If the patient is amenable to treatment, the prognosis will be much better.

Synset Examples

someone amenable to the instruction of others

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compliant - willing to comply or agree with others
  • Antonym: obstinate - stubbornly refusing to change one's opinion or course of action

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman and Old French *amener* (to bring to), modeled after 'liable'.
