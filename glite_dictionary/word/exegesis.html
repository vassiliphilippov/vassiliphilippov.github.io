exegesis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛksəˈdʒisɪs / (AmE), / ˌɛksɪˈdʒiːsɪs / (BrE)
An explanation or critical interpretation of a text, especially a passage from the Bible.
critical explanation of a text

Examples

The scholar delivered an insightful exegesis of the ancient text.
Modern exegesis often incorporates historical and cultural analysis to better understand scripture.
Her exegesis of the poem revealed hidden layers of meaning to the students.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interpretation - the act of explaining or understanding the meaning of something

Etymology

From Greek exēgēsis (explanation, interpretation), from exēgeisthai (to explain, interpret), from ex- (out) + hēgeisthai (to guide).
