antineoplastic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.ti.ni.oʊˈplæs.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌæn.ti.ni.əʊˈplæs.tɪk / (BrE)
(of a substance or drug) inhibiting or preventing the growth and spread of tumors; used in the treatment of cancer.
inhibiting tumor growth; used in cancer treatment
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an antineoplastic drug to slow the tumor's growth.
Advances in antineoplastic therapies have improved survival rates for many cancers.
Researchers are testing new antineoplastic agents in clinical trials.

Synset Examples

anticancer drug
an antineoplastic effect

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anticancer - effective against cancer, especially in reference to drugs

Etymology

From Greek anti- (against) + neos (new) + plasis (formation).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæn.tiˌnioʊˈplæstɪk / (AmE), / ˌæntiːniːəˈplæstɪk / (BrE)
a drug or substance that prevents or inhibits the development and growth of tumors, used especially in cancer treatment
drug that inhibits tumor growth
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an antineoplastic to help combat the patient's cancer.
Many antineoplastics can cause severe side effects such as nausea and hair loss.
Recent advances in antineoplastic therapy have improved survival rates for many cancer patients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chemotherapy - treatment of disease, especially cancer, using chemical substances

Etymology

From Greek anti- (against) + neo- (new) + plasis (formation), referring to substances that act against new (abnormal) tissue growth.
