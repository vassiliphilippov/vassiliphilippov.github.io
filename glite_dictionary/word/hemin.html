hemin

1. NOUN

/ ˈhiːmɪn / (AmE), / ˈhiːmɪn / (BrE)
a reddish-brown compound derived from heme, used in laboratory tests to detect the presence of blood
compound used to detect blood in tests
medical

Examples

The forensic scientist used hemin crystals to confirm the presence of blood on the fabric.
When heated in a test tube, hemoglobin transforms into hemin, aiding in forensic investigations.
The hemin test is a classic method for detecting blood stains at a crime scene.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heme - the iron-containing component of hemoglobin

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood), via heme + -in (a chemical suffix).
