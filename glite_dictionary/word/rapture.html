rapture

1. NOUN

/ ˈræptʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈrap.tʃə / (BrE)
A feeling of intense joy or delight; a state of ecstatic happiness.
ecstatic happiness or delight

Examples

She listened in rapture to the beautiful music filling the hall.
The crowd cheered in rapture as their team scored the winning goal.
He gazed at the painting with rapture, unable to look away from its beauty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ecstasy - an overwhelming feeling of great happiness or joyful excitement
  • Synonym: joy - a feeling of great pleasure or happiness
  • Antonym: misery - a state of great suffering or unhappiness

Etymology

From Medieval Latin raptura (seizure, rapture), from Latin raptus (seized, carried off).

2. NOUN

/ ˈræptʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈrap.tʃə / (BrE)
A feeling of intense pleasure, joy, or ecstasy, often to the point of being carried away by emotion.
intense pleasure or ecstasy

Examples

She listened to the symphony in perfect rapture as the music soared.
The audience watched in rapture as the dancer floated across the stage.
His face was lit with rapture when he saw his family again.

Synset Examples

listening to sweet music in a perfect rapture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ecstasy - an overwhelming feeling of happiness or joyful excitement
  • Synonym: delight - great pleasure or joy
  • Antonym: misery - a state of great distress or discomfort

Etymology

From Latin raptura (a seizure or carrying off), from rapere (to seize).
