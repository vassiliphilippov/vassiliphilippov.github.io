fluvastatin

1. NOUN

/ fluˈvæstəˌtɪn / (AmE), / fluˈvæstətɪn / (BrE)
a statin drug used to lower cholesterol levels in the blood, usually taken orally; trade name Lescol.
cholesterol-lowering statin drug
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed fluvastatin to help reduce his cholesterol levels.
Patients are often advised to take fluvastatin at bedtime for maximum effectiveness.
She switched from another statin to fluvastatin because of fewer side effects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atorvastatin - another statin drug used to lower cholesterol
  • Synonym: simvastatin - statin drug used for lowering cholesterol
  • Degree Lower: cholesterol - a substance in the blood lowered by statins

Etymology

From 'flu-' (indicating fluorine in the chemical structure) and '-vastatin' (common suffix for statin drugs).
