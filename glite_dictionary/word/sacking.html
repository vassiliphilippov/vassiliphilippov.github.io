sacking

1. NOUN

/ ˈsækɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsækɪŋ / (BrE)
a coarse, heavy fabric used especially for making bags or sacks
coarse fabric for making sacks

Examples

The potatoes were stored in large sacking bags in the barn.
She spread a piece of sacking over the ground to sit on during the picnic.
Old carpets and bits of sacking were piled in the corner of the shed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burlap - a coarse canvas woven from jute, hemp, or similar fiber, used for sacks
  • Synonym: sackcloth - a kind of coarse cloth, often associated with mourning or penitence

Etymology

From 'sack' + '-ing', originally referring to material used to make sacks.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsækɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsækɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of dismissing someone from their job; being fired or removed from employment
dismissal from employment

Examples

The company announced the sacking of several senior managers last week.
After the scandal, many employees feared a sudden sacking from their positions.
His repeated lateness eventually led to his sacking from the firm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dismissal - official removal from employment or position
  • Synonym: firing - removal from a job or position, especially suddenly
  • Antonym: hiring - the act of giving someone a job

Etymology

From sack (verb, meaning 'dismiss from employment'), probably from the phrase 'give the sack', possibly from French 'mettre à la sac' (to put in the sack, i.e., to dismiss).
