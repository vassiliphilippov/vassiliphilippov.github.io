detected

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈtɛktɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈtɛktɪd / (BrE)
Perceived, discovered, or identified by investigation or observation; found out or recognized.
discovered or perceived

Examples

After several tests, the researchers confirmed the presence of detected bacteria in the sample.
The alarm sounded when a detected movement occurred in the restricted area.
Radiation levels above the norm were detected during routine monitoring.

Synset Examples

the detected micrometeoritic material

Related Terms

  • Antonym: undetected - not discovered or not perceived
  • Synonym: discovered - found out or identified, especially after a search

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'detect', from Latin 'detectus', past participle of 'detegere' (to uncover).

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈtɛktɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈtɛktɪd / (BrE)
noticed or discovered, especially by using the senses or the mind.
noticed or discovered

Examples

The detected error was quickly corrected by the engineer.
They continued searching for the source of the detected odor in the building.
His nervousness was barely detected by anyone in the audience.

Synset Examples

he winced at the detected flicker of irony in her voice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: noticed - observed or detected, especially by the senses
  • Antonym: undetected - not discovered or noticed

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'detect', from Latin 'detectus', past participle of 'detegere' (to uncover).
