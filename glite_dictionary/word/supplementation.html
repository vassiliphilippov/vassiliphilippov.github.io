supplementation

1. NOUN

/ ˌsʌpləmənˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsʌplɪmənˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of adding something to supply a deficiency or to complete a whole
the act of adding to fill a need or deficiency

Examples

Vitamin supplementation is often recommended for people with dietary restrictions.
Athletes sometimes use protein supplementation to aid muscle recovery.
Iron supplementation can help treat certain forms of anemia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supplement - something added to complete or enhance

Etymology

From supplement + -ation, from Latin supplementum (that which fills up or supplies a deficiency).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsʌpləmɛnˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsʌplɪmɛnˈteɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the act of adding something to complete or enhance it, especially in the context of diet, nutrition, or resources.
the act of adding something extra

Examples

Supplementation with vitamins is common among athletes aiming to boost performance.
The government prioritized iron supplementation programs to address anemia in children.
Some diets may require supplementation to ensure adequate nutrient intake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: addition - the process of adding something
  • Antonym: reduction - the state of making something smaller or less

Etymology

From Latin supplementum (that which fills up or completes) + -ation (forming nouns of action).
