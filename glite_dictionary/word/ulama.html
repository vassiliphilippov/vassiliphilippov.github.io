ulama

1. NOUN

/ uːˈlɑːmə / (AmE), / uːˈlɑːmə / (BrE)
the collective body of Muslim religious scholars who are experts in Islamic theology, law, and tradition
Muslim religious scholars

Examples

The ulama played a crucial role in interpreting religious law in medieval Islamic societies.
Many members of the ulama advised the caliph on religious and legal matters.
Contemporary ulama continue to influence debates on Islamic education and jurisprudence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mullah - a Muslim learned in Islamic theology and sacred law
  • Synonym: imam - a leader of prayer in a mosque or a Muslim religious leader

Etymology

From Arabic ʿulamāʾ, plural of ʿālim (a learned person, scholar).
