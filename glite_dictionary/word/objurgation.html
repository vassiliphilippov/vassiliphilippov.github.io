objurgation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːbdʒɚˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɒbdʒɜːˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
a harsh or severe rebuke; an expression of strong disapproval or criticism
a harsh rebuke or criticism

Examples

His teacher's objurgation left him silent and slightly embarrassed in front of the whole class.
Despite the fierce objurgation, she remained steadfast in her beliefs.
The committee's objurgation of the plan was both public and unequivocal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebuke - an expression of sharp disapproval or criticism
  • Antonym: praise - expression of approval or admiration

Etymology

From Latin objurgatio (rebuke, chiding), from objurgare (to chide, scold).
