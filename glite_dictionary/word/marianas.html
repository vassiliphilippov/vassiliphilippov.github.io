Marianas

1. PROPN

/ ˌmæriˈænəz / (AmE), / ˌmærɪˈɑːnəz / (BrE)
a chain of coral and volcanic islands in the western Pacific Ocean, including Guam and the Northern Mariana Islands, located between New Guinea and Japan
Pacific island chain (Guam, Northern Mariana Islands)

Examples

The Marianas are known for their unique marine biodiversity and beautiful coral reefs.
Spanish explorers first set foot on the Marianas in the early 16th century.
Tourists often visit the Marianas to enjoy pristine beaches and explore World War II history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mariana Islands - chain of islands in the western Pacific Ocean including Guam and the Northern Mariana Islands

Etymology

Named after Mariana of Austria, Queen Regent of Spain, in the 17th century.
