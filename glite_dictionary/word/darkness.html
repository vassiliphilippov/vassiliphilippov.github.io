darkness

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑrknəs / (AmE), / ˈdɑːknəs / (BrE)
A state or quality of lacking moral or spiritual values; wickedness or evil.
absence of moral or spiritual values

Examples

The story explores the darkness that can reside in the human heart.
Many feared the growing darkness in society as corruption spread.
He struggled to resist the darkness tempting him away from virtue.

Synset Examples

the powers of darkness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evil - profound immorality or wickedness
  • Antonym: goodness - the quality of being morally good or virtuous

Etymology

From Old English *deorcness*, from *deorc* (dark) + *-ness* (noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɑrknəs / (AmE), / ˈdɑːknəs / (BrE)
the total or partial absence of light; the state of being dark
absence of light

Examples

As night fell, a thick darkness enveloped the forest.
She was afraid of the darkness outside her window.
The electricity went out, plunging the entire building into darkness.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: light - the natural agent that makes things visible

Etymology

From Old English *deorcnys* (darkness), from *deorc* (dark) + *-ness*.
