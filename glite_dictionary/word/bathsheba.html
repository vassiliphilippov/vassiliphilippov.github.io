Bathsheba

1. PROPN

/ bæθˈʃiːbə / (AmE), / bæθˈʃiːbə / (BrE)
(in the Old Testament) the wife of Uriah and later of King David, and the mother of Solomon.
wife of King David and mother of Solomon (Bible)

Examples

In the biblical narrative, Bathsheba became the mother of Solomon after marrying King David.
Many painters, such as Rembrandt, have depicted the story of Bathsheba in their artwork.
The moral complexities involving Bathsheba have inspired analysis by theologians and scholars alike.

Etymology

From Hebrew Bathsheva, meaning 'daughter of the oath.'
