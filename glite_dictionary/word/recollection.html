recollection

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛkəˈlɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛkəˈlɛkʃən / (BrE)
a memory of something; the act or process of remembering something from the past
memory; act of remembering

Examples

Her vivid recollection of the event helped the police solve the case.
I have no recollection of meeting him before last night.
Certain smells can trigger a sudden recollection of childhood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: memory - something remembered from the past
  • Antonym: forgetfulness - the state of being unable to remember

Etymology

From Latin recollectio (a gathering together again), from recolligere (to gather again).

2. NOUN

/ ˌrɛkəˈlɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛkəˈlɛkʃən / (BrE)
the ability to remember past experiences or events; the power of recalling things to mind
the ability to remember past events

Examples

Her recollection of the events that night was remarkably clear despite the years that had passed.
With age, his recollection became less reliable and he struggled to recall names and faces.
Thanks to her sharp recollection, the detective was able to reconstruct the crime scene in detail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: memory - the faculty by which the mind stores and remembers information
  • Antonym: forgetfulness - the inability to remember or recall

Etymology

From Latin recollectionem, from recollegere (to gather again, recall).

the act of remembering

Examples

During the trial, his recollection of the events was called into question.
Meditation can help improve one's recollection of past experiences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: memory - the faculty by which the mind stores and remembers information
  • Antonym: forgetfulness - the inability to remember

From Latin recollectio (a gathering together again), from 're-' (again) + 'collegere' (to gather).
