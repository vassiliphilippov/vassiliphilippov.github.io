groat

1. NOUN

/ ɡroʊt / (AmE), / ɡrəʊt / (BrE)
a former English silver coin worth four pennies, used from the 13th to 19th centuries
old English silver coin (four pence)
archaic british

Examples

He found an old groat buried in the garden.
In Shakespeare's time, a groat could buy a decent meal.
Collectors often search for rare groats from different reigns.

Etymology

From Middle English *grot*, from Middle Dutch *groot* (a large coin, literally ‘great’).
