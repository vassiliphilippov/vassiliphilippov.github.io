Salix lasiolepis

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪlɪks ˌlæsiəˈlɛpɪs / (AmE), / ˈseɪlɪks ˌlasiəˈlepis / (BrE)
A species of willow native to the western United States, typically found as a shrub or small tree near streams and wetlands.
a willow species of western North America

Examples

The banks of the river were lined with Salix lasiolepis, providing shade for the local wildlife.
Botanists conducted a survey of Salix lasiolepis populations in western California.
Restoration projects often use Salix lasiolepis to stabilize stream banks due to its extensive root system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: willow - any tree or shrub of the genus Salix

Etymology

From Latin: Salix (willow) + lasiolepis (woolly scale), referring to the plant's downy leaves.
