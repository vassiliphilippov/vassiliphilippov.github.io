presbyter

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛzbɪtər / (AmE), / ˈprɛzbɪtə / (BrE)
A member of the governing body (elder) of the Presbyterian Church or similar Christian churches.
elder in Presbyterian Church

Examples

The presbyter led the prayers during the Sunday service.
After years of dedicated service, he was appointed as a presbyter in his local congregation.
In the Presbyterian Church, a presbyter helps to govern the affairs of the parish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elder - an official or leader in some churches
  • Synonym: minister - a member of the clergy, especially in Protestant churches

Etymology

From Greek presbyteros (elder), from presbys (old man).
