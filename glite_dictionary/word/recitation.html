recitation

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛsɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛsɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
A piece of written work that is memorized and spoken aloud from memory.
written work recited from memory

Examples

Each student was assigned a different recitation to memorize for the poetry contest.
Her flawless delivery of the recitation impressed the entire audience.
He practiced his recitation every night before the big presentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recital - a performance of a program of music or dance

Etymology

From Latin 'recitatio' (a reading aloud), from 'recitare' (to read out).

2. NOUN

/ ˌrɛsɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛsɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a regularly scheduled session in a course of study, often for review, discussion, or problem-solving, separate from the main lecture
classroom session for discussion or review

Examples

The class meets for lecture on Mondays and for a recitation session every Wednesday.
Attendance at the weekly recitation is required in this course.
During the recitation, students can ask questions and work through additional exercises.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lecture - an educational talk to an audience, especially students in a university or college

Etymology

From Latin 'recitatio' (public reading), from 'recitare' (to read out).

3. NOUN

/ ˌrɛsɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛsɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a public act of reciting or repeating something memorized, especially as part of a performance or event
public act of reciting memorized material

Examples

Her moving recitation of the poem captivated the entire audience.
He won first prize for his recitation at the school's annual talent show.
The evening included music, dance, and recitation of famous speeches.

Synset Examples

the program included songs and recitations of well-loved poems

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to recite - to repeat aloud from memory before an audience

Etymology

From Latin recitatio (a reading aloud), from recitare (to read out, repeat from memory).
