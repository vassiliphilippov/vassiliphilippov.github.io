disbursal

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈbɜrsəl / (AmE), / dɪsˈbɜːsəl / (BrE)
the act of paying out or distributing money, especially from a fund or account
the paying out of money

Examples

The disbursal of funds will begin once the paperwork is completed.
After approval, the disbursal of scholarships took place within a week.
The company tracks every disbursal to ensure transparency in financial matters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disbursement - the payment or distribution of money

Etymology

Formed from 'disburse' (to pay out) + suffix '-al', relating to action or process.

2. NOUN

/ dɪsˈbɝːsəl / (AmE), / dɪsˈbɜːsəl / (BrE)
the act of paying out money, especially for goods and services; a payment or expenditure
the act of paying out money

Examples

The company processed the disbursal of employee travel reimbursements every month.
After the project was approved, the disbursal of funds began immediately.
Each disbursal must be documented for auditing purposes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: payment - the act of paying money
  • Antonym: receipt - money received or the act of receiving money

Etymology

From 'disburse' (to pay out), itself from Old French 'desbourser', from 'des-' (out) + 'bours' (purse).
