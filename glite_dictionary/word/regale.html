to regale

1. VERB

/ rɪˈɡeɪl / (AmE), / rɪˈɡeɪl / (BrE)
to entertain or lavishly provide someone with a great quantity of food or drink
to entertain with plentiful food or drink

Examples

She loves to regale her guests with delicious homemade desserts and fine wine.
At the annual banquet, they regaled us with an endless array of gourmet dishes.
He delighted in regaling his friends with a lavish feast every holiday season.

Synset Examples

Don't worry about the expensive wine — I'm treating
She treated her houseguests with good food every night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to entertain - to provide amusement or hospitality to someone
  • Antonym: to starve - to deprive of food; go without eating

Etymology

From French 'régaler' meaning 'to entertain or feast.'
