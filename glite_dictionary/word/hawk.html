hawk

1. NOUN

/ hɑk / (AmE), / hɔːk / (BrE)
a square board with a handle underneath, used by masons to hold mortar or plaster while working
mason's handheld mortar board

Examples

The mason skillfully scraped mortar off the hawk and spread it onto the bricks.
She balanced a loaded hawk in one hand while plastering the wall with the other.
Every apprentice learns how to carry a hawk without spilling its contents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trowel - a small hand tool with a flat, pointed blade, used by masons for applying and spreading mortar or plaster
Source: WordNet

2. NOUN

/ hɑk / (AmE), / hɔːk / (BrE)
A diurnal bird of prey with short rounded wings and a long tail, often known for its keen eyesight and hunting skills.
bird of prey with long tail

Examples

The hawk soared high above the fields searching for its next meal.
A sharp-eyed hawk circled the barn, scaring away smaller birds.
She spotted a hawk perched on a fence post during her morning walk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: falcon - diurnal bird of prey with long pointed wings and notched beak
  • Degree Higher: eagle - large bird of prey with a massive hooked bill and long broad wings

Etymology

Old English *hafoc*, from Proto-Germanic *habukaz*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01608271-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ hɑk / (AmE), / hɔːk / (BrE)
to sell or offer goods for sale by calling out and moving from place to place, especially in public areas
sell goods by calling out in public places

Examples

Street vendors hawk their wares in the busy market every morning.
He used to hawk homemade jewelry door to door when he was a teenager.
Vendors hawking snacks shouted their offers as the train pulled into the station.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to peddle - to sell goods by going from place to place

Etymology

Probably from Middle Low German *hoken* (to peddle goods), related to hawking as a form of street trading.

Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ hɔk / (AmE), / hɔːk / (BrE)
a person who strongly supports aggressive or military action in international affairs
advocate of aggressive foreign policy

Examples

Some politicians are known as hawks because they favor military solutions over diplomacy.
During the debate, the hawks clashed with the doves over foreign intervention policies.
He has a reputation as a hawk due to his tough stance on national security.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dove - a person who prefers peaceful or conciliatory policies in international relations

Etymology

Figurative use from the predatory bird, contrasted with 'dove' (peace advocate).

Source: WordNet
