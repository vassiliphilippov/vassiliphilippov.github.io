marri

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæri / (AmE), / ˈmæri / (BrE)
A very large Australian eucalyptus tree (Corymbia calophylla), known for its rough bark and red gum.
large Australian eucalyptus tree

Examples

The forest was dominated by ancient marri trees stretching high above the undergrowth.
A sticky red gum oozes from the trunk of the marri, making it easy to identify.
Local wildlife often uses the hollows of marri trees for nesting and shelter.

Etymology

From Noongar (Aboriginal language of southwestern Australia) *marri* for this tree.
