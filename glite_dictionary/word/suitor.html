suitor

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuːtər / (AmE), / ˈsjuːtə / (BrE)
a man who courts or seeks to marry a woman; a man who shows romantic interest in a woman
a man who courts a woman

Examples

After months of visits and gifts, the suitor finally proposed to her.
Her family approved of the persistent suitor who called on her every Sunday.
Several suitors competed for the hand of the princess at the royal ball.

Synset Examples

a suer for the hand of the princess

Related Terms

  • Synonym: admirer - a person who is attracted to someone, often romantically
  • Synonym: beau - a male admirer or boyfriend
  • Degree Higher: fiancé - a man engaged to be married

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman and Old French *suitour* (one who sues, petitioner), based on Latin *sequi* (to follow).
