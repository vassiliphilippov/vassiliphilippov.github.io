schist

1. NOUN

/ ʃɪst / (AmE), / ʃɪst / (BrE)
a coarse-grained metamorphic rock that can be easily split into thin, often flaky layers due to its mineral structure
type of layered metamorphic rock

Examples

The walls of the canyon were composed mainly of schist, giving them a layered appearance.
Geologists studied samples of schist to determine the region's geological history.
The sparkle in the schist came from its high mica content.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: gneiss - a banded metamorphic rock, less easily split than schist

Etymology

From Greek 'skhistos' (split, divisible), from 'skhizein' (to split).
