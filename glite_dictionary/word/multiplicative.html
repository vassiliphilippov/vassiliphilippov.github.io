multiplicative

1. ADJ

/ ˌmʌl.təˈplɪk.ə.tɪv / (AmE), / ˌmʌl.tɪˈplɪk.ə.tɪv / (BrE)
tending or having the power to multiply or increase in number, quantity, or degree.
having the power to multiply

Examples

The multiplicative effect of compound interest can significantly increase your savings over time.
She explained the multiplicative relationship between variables in the equation.
A virus's multiplicative ability can cause an outbreak to grow rapidly.

Synset Examples

the multiplicative tendency of proportional representation

Related Terms

  • Antonym: additive - relating to, or having the property of addition

Etymology

From Latin multiplicativus, from multiplicare (to multiply).
