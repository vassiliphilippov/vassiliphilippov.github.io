closure

1. NOUN

/ ˈkloʊʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈkləʊʒə / (BrE)
a feeling that an emotional or difficult situation has been resolved or brought to an end
feeling of resolution or finality

Examples

After years of uncertainty, she finally found closure when the dispute was settled.
The family needed closure before they could move on from the tragedy.
His apology brought a sense of closure to their long-standing argument.

Synset Examples

they finally reached a settlement with the union
they never did achieve a final resolution of their differences
he needed to grieve before he could achieve a sense of closure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resolution - the act of solving or settling a problem or dispute
  • Antonym: unsettled - not resolved or brought to an end

Etymology

From Old French *closure* (act of closing), from Latin *clausura* (a closing, enclosure), from *claudere* (to close).
