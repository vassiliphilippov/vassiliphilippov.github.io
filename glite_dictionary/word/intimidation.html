intimidation

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌtɪməˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌtɪmɪˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making someone afraid or discouraged from doing something, often through threats or pressure
the act of making someone afraid or discouraged

Examples

Her supervisor used subtle intimidation to keep her from reporting the problem.
Attempts at intimidation will not stop us from expressing our views.
Some students dropped out of the contest because of the intimidation they faced from others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: threat - a statement of intention to inflict harm or punishment
  • Antonym: encouragement - the act of giving someone support or confidence

Etymology

From Medieval Latin intimidatio, from Latin intimidare (to make timid).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˌtɪməˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌtɪmɪˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
the feeling of being made to feel afraid or timid, especially by the actions of others
the feeling of being intimidated

Examples

She felt a wave of intimidation when she walked into the large, unfamiliar office.
His presence alone was enough to cause intimidation among the new employees.
Despite the intimidation, she spoke up and shared her opinion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fear - an unpleasant emotion caused by threat or danger
  • Antonym: confidence - a feeling of self-assurance, often contrasted with fear or intimidation

Etymology

From Latin intimidare (to frighten).

the act of frightening or threatening someone, especially to make them do what you want
the act of frightening or threatening someone

Examples

Many people spoke out against the intimidation used during the elections.
She reported the intimidation she experienced at school to the authorities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: threat - an expression of intent to inflict harm
  • Synonym: coercion - the act of making someone do something by force or threats
  • Antonym: encouragement - the act of giving support or confidence

Etymology

From Latin intimidatio, from intimidare (to make timid or frighten).

4. NOUN

/ ɪnˌtɪmɪˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌtɪmɪˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
the feeling of being discouraged or fearful because of someone else's greater fame, wealth, or status
discouragement caused by another's superiority

Examples

The team's long list of victories gave their opponents a sense of intimidation before the match even began.
She felt a wave of intimidation when she entered the room full of wealthy business executives.
Despite initial intimidation, he managed to speak with confidence during the interview.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to daunt - to make someone feel apprehensive or discouraged
  • Antonym: confidence - a feeling of self-assurance

Etymology

From Latin intimidatio (act of making timid), from intimidare (to frighten).
