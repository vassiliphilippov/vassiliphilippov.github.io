worse

1. ADJ

/ wɝs / (AmE), / wɜːs / (BrE)
of lower quality, less desirable, or in a poorer condition compared to something else; comparative of 'bad'.
comparative of 'bad'; of lower quality or condition

Examples

Her handwriting was even worse after the accident than before.
This winter is worse than last year’s in terms of storms.
He realized things could get worse if he didn’t act quickly.

Synset Examples

this road is worse than the first one we took
the road is in worse shape than it was
she was accused of worse things than cheating and lying

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bad - of low quality or poor standard
  • Degree Higher: worst - of the lowest possible quality or standard

Etymology

Old English 'wiersa', comparative of 'yfel' (bad, evil).

WordNet ID: oewn-00232844-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ wɝːs / (AmE), / wɜːs / (BrE)
in a poorer state of health or fitness than before, especially as a result of illness or injury
less healthy or fit than before

Examples

After the operation, his breathing became worse and he needed assistance.
My headache is much worse today than it was yesterday.
The medicine didn't help and she feels even worse now.

Synset Examples

I feel worse today
her cold is worse

Related Terms

  • Antonym: better - in a more improved state of health or fitness
  • Degree Lower: bad - not good; of poor quality or condition
  • Degree Higher: worst - in the poorest state of health or fitness; the lowest point

Etymology

Comparative form of 'bad', from Old English 'wyrsa', related to Old High German 'wirso'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00233660-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADV

/ wɝs / (AmE), / wɜːs / (BrE)
In a less effective, successful, or desirable manner; to a greater extent of severity or badness.
less effectively or successfully

Examples

After skipping practice, she performed worse in the final match than before.
His cold got worse after he went out in the rain.
The new version of the app works even worse than the previous one.

Synset Examples

he did worse on the second exam

Related Terms

  • Antonym: better - in a more effective or desirable manner
  • Degree Lower: badly - in a bad or unsatisfactory manner

Etymology

From Old English 'wiersa', comparative of 'yfel' (ill, bad).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00017539-r
Synset POS: r

4. NOUN

/ wɝs / (AmE), / wɜːs / (BrE)
A thing that is inferior in quality, condition, or effect compared to another; something more serious, bad, or undesirable.
something that is worse; something inferior or more serious

Examples

For better or for worse, she decided to move to a new city.
He was accused of lying and cheating, and possibly worse.
Sometimes we fear the worse even when there's little reason to do so.

Synset Examples

for better or for worse
accused of cheating and lying and worse

Related Terms

  • Antonym: better - something superior in quality or condition

Etymology

From Old English 'wiersa', comparative of 'yfel' (bad, evil).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05151700-n
Synset POS: n
