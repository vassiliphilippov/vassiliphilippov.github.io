Rubus strigosus

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːbəs strɪˈɡoʊsəs / (AmE), / ˈruːbəs strɪˈɡəʊsəs / (BrE)
The North American red raspberry, a species of raspberry native to North America, known for its edible red fruit.
North American red raspberry species

Examples

Wild Rubus strigosus plants grow abundantly in the forests of eastern Canada.
Many gardeners cultivate Rubus strigosus for its sweet and flavorful berries.
Botanists distinguish Rubus strigosus from its European relatives by examining the plant’s stem hairs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raspberry - an edible soft fruit, related to blackberries, of several species of the genus Rubus

Etymology

From Latin: 'Rubus' (bramble or blackberry) and 'strigosus' (having stiff hairs).
