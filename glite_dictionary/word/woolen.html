woolen

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊlən / (AmE), / ˈwʊlən / (BrE)
A fabric made from wool, especially the short, soft hairs of sheep; often used for clothing and blankets.
fabric made from sheep's wool

Examples

She wrapped herself in a thick woolen to stay warm during the cold night.
The tailor recommended a high-quality woolen for making winter suits.
Washing a woolen in hot water can cause it to shrink.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: worsted - a smooth, fine woolen yarn or fabric, different in texture from woolen

Etymology

From Old English 'wull' (wool) + '-en' (made of).

2. ADJ

/ ˈwʊlən / (AmE), / ˈwʊlən / (BrE)
made of or relating to wool.
made of wool

Examples

She wore a woolen scarf to keep warm during the winter.
The woolen blanket was soft and cozy on the bed.
My grandmother knit a woolen sweater for my birthday.

Synset Examples

a woolen sweater

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wool - the soft, thick hair that grows on sheep and some other animals, used for making cloth

Etymology

From Old English wull (wool) + -en (adjectival suffix).
