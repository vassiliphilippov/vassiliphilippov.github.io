leukemia

1. NOUN

/ luˈkimiə / (AmE), / luːˈkiːmiə / (BrE)
a serious cancer in which the bone marrow produces too many abnormal white blood cells, affecting the blood and immune system
cancer of blood-forming tissues
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that leukemia is a cancer affecting the body's blood-forming tissues.
After months of treatment, her leukemia finally went into remission.
Scientists are researching new drugs to treat leukemia more effectively.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lymphoma - a cancer of the lymphatic system
  • Antonym: anemia - a condition with a deficiency of red blood cells

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'leukos' (white) + '-emia' (condition of the blood).
