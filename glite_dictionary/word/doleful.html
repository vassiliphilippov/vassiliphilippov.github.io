doleful

1. ADJ

/ ˈdoʊlfəl / (AmE), / ˈdəʊlfəl / (BrE)
expressing or causing sadness; mournful
expressing or causing sadness

Examples

Her doleful eyes told the whole story without a single word.
He gave a doleful sigh as he read the disappointing letter.
The movie had a doleful ending that left many people in tears.

Synset Examples

the child's doleful expression
stared with mournful eyes
mournful news

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mournful - expressing sorrow or grief
  • Antonym: cheerful - showing happiness or optimism

Etymology

From Old French *dole* (grief) + *-ful* (full of).
