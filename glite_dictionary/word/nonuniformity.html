nonuniformity

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɑnˌjunəˈfɔrməti / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌjuːnɪˈfɔːmɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being not uniform; the condition of being diverse, varied, or lacking conformity.
the quality of being not uniform or varied

Examples

The nonuniformity of the landscape made the hike more interesting and visually appealing.
Researchers noticed a significant nonuniformity in the data collected from different regions.
Artisans value the nonuniformity in handmade pottery, believing it adds to each piece's uniqueness.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uniformity - the quality of being uniform or the same throughout
  • Synonym: diversity - the state of being diverse; variety

Etymology

From prefix 'non-' (not) + 'uniformity', from Latin 'uniformis' (of one form/shape).
