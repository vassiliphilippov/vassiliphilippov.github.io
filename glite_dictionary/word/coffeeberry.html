coffeeberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑfiˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈkɒfiˌbɛri / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub of the western United States, bearing small red or black berries
evergreen shrub with red or black berries

Examples

Several species of coffeeberry can be found growing wild in California's chaparral.
Birds are attracted to the ripe coffeeberries in the garden.
The coffeeberry is valued by native gardeners for its drought tolerance and attractive fruit.

Etymology

From "coffee" + "berry", referring to the plant's berry-like fruits and their resemblance to coffee beans.
