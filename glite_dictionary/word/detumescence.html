detumescence

1. NOUN

/ diˈtuːməsəns / (AmE), / diːˈtjuːməsəns / (BrE)
The reduction or subsiding of swelling, especially the decrease in size of a previously swollen body part.
reduction of swelling
medical

Examples

After the procedure, detumescence occurred quickly, and the area returned to normal size.
The doctor noted the detumescence of the injured tissue during the follow-up visit.
Prompt detumescence is a positive sign in the treatment of certain medical conditions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tumescence - the state of being swollen

Etymology

From Latin 'detumescere' (to subside from swelling), from 'de-' (down from) + 'tumescere' (to swell).
