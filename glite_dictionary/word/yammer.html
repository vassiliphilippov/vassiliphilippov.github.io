to yammer

1. VERB

/ ˈjæmɚ / (AmE), / ˈjamə / (BrE)
to complain or whine persistently and in an annoying way
complain in a whining manner

Examples

Stop yammering and try to solve the problem.
She yammered on about her troubles all evening.
The children began to yammer when they didn’t get dessert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whine - to complain in a feeble or petulant way
  • Synonym: grumble - to complain about something in a bad-tempered way

Etymology

Of imitative origin, possibly from Middle English 'yameren' (to lament).

2. VERB

/ ˈjæmɚ / (AmE), / ˈjamə / (BrE)
to make a loud, repetitive, and often complaining or wailing noise; to howl or whine, especially as an animal or person in distress
cry or complain loudly and repetitively

Examples

All night, the wolves yammered outside the cabin, unsettled by the storm.
The baby yammered for attention until someone picked her up.
In the distance, coyotes could be heard yammering in the moonlight.

Synset Examples

The coyotes were howling in the desert

Related Terms

  • Synonym: howl - to make a long, loud, doleful cry or wail, like an animal
  • Synonym: whine - to complain or utter with a complaining tone

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English yameren, of imitative origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01049253-v
Synset POS: v
