scarcely

1. ADV

/ ˈskɛrsli / (AmE), / ˈskɑːsli / (BrE)
only a very short time before; almost immediately before something else happens.
only a very short time before

Examples

She had scarcely finished her speech when the audience began to applaud.
They had scarcely arrived at the station before the train left.
The shop had scarcely opened when customers started to pour in.

Synset Examples

we hardly knew them
had scarcely rung the bell when the door flew open
would have scarce arrived before she would have found some excuse to leave

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hardly - only just; almost not; very shortly before

Etymology

From Old French 'escars' meaning 'scanty', and Latin 'excarpsus'.

2. ADV

/ ˈskɛrsli / (AmE), / ˈskɑːsli / (BrE)
Only just; almost not at all.
almost not; hardly

Examples

There was scarcely enough food to feed everyone at the party.
She had scarcely finished her speech when the audience began to applaud.
He could scarcely believe his eyes when he saw who had arrived.

Synset Examples

he hardly ever goes fishing
he was scarce sixteen years old
they scarcely ever used the emergency generator
I can hardly hear what she is saying
she barely seemed to notice him
we were so far back in the theater we could barely read the subtitles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hardly - almost not; only just
  • Synonym: barely - only just; by a very small margin

Etymology

From Latin 'excarpsus' via Old French 'escarse', meaning rare or scant.

Source: WordNet
