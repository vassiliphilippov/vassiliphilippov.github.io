Anemone pulsatilla

1. NOUN

/ əˈnɛməni ˌpʊl.səˈtɪl.ə / (AmE), / əˈnɛməni ˌpʊl.səˈtɪl.ə / (BrE)
A European perennial plant with usually violet or white flowers that bloom in spring, also known as the pasqueflower.
European spring-flowering perennial plant

Examples

The Anemone pulsatilla bloomed early this year, bringing a splash of violet to the meadow.
Gardeners often plant Anemone pulsatilla for its delicate spring flowers and feathery foliage.
The rare white variant of Anemone pulsatilla can sometimes be seen in European alpine regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pasqueflower - Another name for Anemone pulsatilla, a spring-flowering plant

Etymology

From Latin 'anemone' (windflower) and 'pulsatilla' (a diminutive of 'pulsatus', meaning beaten or struck; likened to the plant's nodding flowers).
