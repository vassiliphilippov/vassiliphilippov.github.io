ballistics

1. NOUN

/ bəˈlɪstɪks / (AmE), / bəˈlɪstɪks / (BrE)
the scientific study of the motion, behavior, and effects of projectiles, such as bullets or missiles, in flight.
science of projectile motion

Examples

He specialized in ballistics to better understand the trajectory of bullets in criminal investigations.
The officer called a ballistics expert to analyze the evidence found at the crime scene.
Modern ballistics has applications in both military technology and space exploration.

Etymology

From Greek 'ballistikos' (of throwing); related to 'ballein' (to throw).

2. NOUN

/ bəˈlɪstɪks / (AmE), / bəˈlɪstɪks / (BrE)
the science or study of the motion, dynamics, and effects of projectiles, especially the trajectory of objects in free flight
study of projectile motion

Examples

The detective called in an expert on ballistics to analyze the bullet's path.
Studying ballistics helped engineers improve the accuracy of space probes.
Military scientists use ballistics to predict where shells will land during training exercises.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trajectory - the path followed by a projectile flying or an object moving under the action of given forces

Etymology

From Greek 'ballistikos' (of throwing), from 'ballein' (to throw), via Latin 'ballista' (catapult).
