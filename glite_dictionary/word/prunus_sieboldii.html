Prunus sieboldii

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruːnəs siːˈbɒldiː / (AmE), / ˈpruːnəs siːˈbɒldiː / (BrE)
An ornamental tree species native to East Asia, cultivated for its attractive pink blossoms and inedible fruit.
ornamental tree with pink blossoms

Examples

The Prunus sieboldii is often planted in gardens for its spectacular pink blossoms.
Unlike most fruit trees, the Prunus sieboldii produces inedible fruit.
Botanists admire the resilience of Prunus sieboldii in urban landscaping.

Etymology

Named for Philipp Franz von Siebold, a German botanist, and from Latin genus Prunus (plum tree).
