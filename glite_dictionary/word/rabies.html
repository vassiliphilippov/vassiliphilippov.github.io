rabies

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪbiz / (AmE), / ˈreɪbiːz / (BrE)
A serious and often fatal viral disease of the nervous system affecting mammals, typically transmitted by the bite of an infected animal.
fatal viral disease causing nervous system infection
medical

Examples

The dog was quarantined after showing symptoms of rabies.
Vaccination can prevent rabies following exposure to a potentially infected animal.
The spread of rabies remains a public health concern in many parts of the world.

Etymology

From Latin 'rabies' (madness, rage), from 'rabere' (to rave).
