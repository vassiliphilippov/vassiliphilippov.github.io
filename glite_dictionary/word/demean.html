to demean

1. VERB

/ dɪˈmin / (AmE), / dɪˈmiːn / (BrE)
to lower someone or something in dignity, honor, or worth, especially by words or actions
lower in dignity or worth

Examples

It is wrong to demean others because of their background.
His comment was intended to demean her achievements, but she ignored him.
The advertisement was criticized for demeaning women.

Synset Examples

She tends to put down younger women colleagues
His critics took him down after the lecture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to degrade - to treat someone with disrespect or lower in dignity
  • Synonym: to humiliate - to make someone feel ashamed and lose respect for themselves
  • Antonym: to exalt - to raise someone or something to a higher rank or position

Etymology

From Latin 'deminere' (to debase), influenced by 'mean' (low in status).
