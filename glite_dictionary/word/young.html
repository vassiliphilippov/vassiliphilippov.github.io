young

1. NOUN

/ jʌŋ / (AmE), / jʌŋ / (BrE)
a group of people who are young, considered collectively as a demographic or social group
young people collectively

Examples

The new political movement gained most of its support from the young.
Modern fashion trends are often driven by the tastes of the young.
The city built a skate park to give the young a place to gather and exercise.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: youth - the state or group of being young; young people collectively

2. ADJ

/ jʌŋ / (AmE), / jʌŋ / (BrE)
having lived or existed for only a short time; in an early stage of life, development, or growth, especially of people or animals.
at an early stage of life or development

Examples

Many young children enjoy playing outdoors in the summer.
The company is looking for young talent to bring fresh ideas to the team.
As a young scientist, she was determined to make important discoveries.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: old - having lived for a long time; not young
  • Synonym: juvenile - relating to young people or animals

Etymology

From Old English *geong* (young), related to German *jung* and Dutch *jong*.

3. NOUN

/ jʌŋ / (AmE), / jʌŋ / (BrE)
An animal that has not yet reached maturity; an offspring of an animal.
immature animal; animal offspring

Examples

The birds were busy feeding their young in the nest.
Many animals protect their young until they are strong enough to survive on their own.
A lioness fiercely defends her young from potential threats.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: adult - an animal that has reached maturity
  • Synonym: offspring - an animal's child or young

Etymology

From Old English *geong* (young), related to German *jung* and Dutch *jong*.
