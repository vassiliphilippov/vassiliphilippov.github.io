dictator

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪkˌteɪtər / (AmE), / dɪkˈteɪtə / (BrE)
A person who behaves in an autocratic or tyrannical way, often imposing their will on others without regard for their opinions.
a person who acts in a domineering way

Examples

Everyone avoided arguing with the new manager, who acted like a dictator during meetings.
At home, her brother can be a real dictator about what to watch on TV.
The committee grew frustrated by the chairman’s dictator-like decisions.

Synset Examples

my boss is a dictator who makes everyone work overtime

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tyrant - a person who uses power cruelly or unjustly
  • Synonym: autocrat - a person with absolute power and authority
  • Antonym: democrat - a person who supports democracy and equal participation

Etymology

From Latin 'dictātor', meaning 'a person who declares' or 'gives orders'.

2. NOUN

/ dɪkˈteɪtər / (AmE), / dɪkˈteɪtə / (BrE)
a ruler who has absolute power, usually one who takes control by force and is not restricted by law or constitution
ruler with absolute power

Examples

The country suffered under the rule of a brutal dictator for over two decades.
Many people fled the nation to escape the dictator's oppressive regime.
After the revolution, the dictator was removed from power and democracy was restored.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autocrat - a ruler with unlimited power; a synonym for dictator
  • Synonym: tyrant - a cruel and oppressive ruler; another synonym for dictator
  • Antonym: democrat - someone who supports democracy; an antonym of dictator in political sense
  • Degree Lower: president - the elected head of a republic, often with limited powers; lesser degree than dictator

Etymology

From Latin 'dictator' (a magistrate with extraordinary powers), from 'dictare' (dictate).
