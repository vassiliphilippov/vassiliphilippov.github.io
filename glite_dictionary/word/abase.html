to abase

1. VERB

/ əˈbeɪs / (AmE), / əˈbeɪs / (BrE)
to make someone feel humiliated or degraded; to lower the dignity or self-respect of someone or oneself
to humiliate or degrade

Examples

The manager's harsh words served only to abase his employees.
She refused to abase herself in front of the crowd by apologizing for something she didn't do.
He would never abase his position by resorting to petty insults.

Synset Examples

He humiliated his colleague by criticising him in front of the boss

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to humiliate - to make someone feel ashamed or foolish
  • Synonym: to degrade - to treat or regard someone with contempt or disrespect
  • Antonym: to exalt - to glorify, praise, or raise in rank or dignity

Etymology

From Old French abaisser, from Latin ad- (to) + bassus (low, short).
