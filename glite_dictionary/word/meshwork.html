meshwork

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛʃˌwɝk / (AmE), / ˈmɛʃˌwɜːk / (BrE)
an open structure or fabric made of threads, wires, or similar materials woven together at regular intervals, creating a network-like pattern
open fabric of woven strands

Examples

The scientist examined the fine meshwork of nerves under the microscope.
A protective meshwork of wires surrounded the machinery for safety.
Light passed easily through the loose meshwork of the fishing net.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: network - an interconnected system of lines, wires, or people
  • Synonym: web - a strong, intricate, woven structure

Etymology

From 'mesh' (network of interlaced threads) + 'work' (construction, formation).
