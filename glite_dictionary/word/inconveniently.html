inconveniently

1. ADV

/ ˌɪn.kənˈviː.ni.ənt.li / (AmE), / ˌɪn.kənˈviː.ni.ənt.li / (BrE)
In a way that causes difficulty, discomfort, or trouble.
in an inconvenient manner

Examples

The meeting was inconveniently scheduled for early Sunday morning.
He found his keys had inconveniently fallen behind the heavy sofa.
The restaurant was inconveniently located far from public transportation.

Synset Examples

he arrived at an inconveniently late hour

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conveniently - in a convenient manner

Etymology

From 'inconvenient' + '-ly', modeled on the Latin prefix and English adverbial ending.
