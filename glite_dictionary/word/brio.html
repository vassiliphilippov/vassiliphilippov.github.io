brio

1. NOUN

/ ˈbriːoʊ / (AmE), / ˈbriːəʊ / (BrE)
energy, vivacity, or spiritedness in action or style.
energy or livacity

Examples

She performed the dance with such brio that the audience erupted in applause.
The pianist played Chopin's waltz with remarkable brio and precision.
His speech had a youthful brio that inspired everyone in the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vivacity - the quality of being attractively lively and animated
  • Synonym: spirit - enthusiasm, energy, or courage

Etymology

From Italian brio (liveliness, energy), probably from Spanish brío (energy, spirit).
