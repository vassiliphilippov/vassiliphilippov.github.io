Chrysopsis

1. NOUN

/ kraɪˈsɑpsɪs / (AmE), / kraɪˈsɒpsɪs / (BrE)
A genus of North American perennial herbs known as golden asters, with yellow, daisy-like flowers.
genus of golden asters

Examples

The fields were dotted with bright Chrysopsis blooming in the late summer sun.
Botanists collected samples of Chrysopsis to study their unique adaptation to sandy soils.
Several species of Chrysopsis are found only in the southeastern United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golden aster - A common name for plants in the genus Chrysopsis

Etymology

From Greek 'chrysos' (gold) + 'opsis' (appearance), referring to the plant's golden flowers.

2. NOUN

/ kraɪˈsɑpsɪs / (AmE), / kraɪˈsɒpsɪs / (BrE)
A genus of North American plants in the daisy family, commonly known as golden asters, characterized by their yellow flowers.
genus of golden aster plants

Examples

Many wildflowers in Florida belong to the Chrysopsis genus, displaying brilliant yellow blooms in autumn.
Botanists recently discovered a new species within the Chrysopsis group on the Gulf Coast.
The sandy soil of the region provides the ideal habitat for Chrysopsis to thrive.

Etymology

From Greek 'chrysos' (gold) and 'opsis' (appearance), referring to the golden color of the flowers.
