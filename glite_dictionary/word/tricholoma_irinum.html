Tricholoma irinum

1. NOUN

/ ˌtrɪkəˈloʊmə ɪˈraɪnəm / (AmE), / ˌtrɪkəˈləʊmə ɪˈrɪnəm / (BrE)
An edible mushroom species with large silky white caps and thick stalks, belonging to the genus Tricholoma.
an edible mushroom species

Examples

The chef prepared a dish using freshly picked Tricholoma irinum mushrooms from the forest.
Mycology enthusiasts often seek out Tricholoma irinum for its distinctive silky white cap.
While hiking, we spotted several Tricholoma irinum growing beneath the pine trees.

Etymology

From New Latin, with 'Tricholoma' meaning 'hairy fringe' (Greek trichos 'hair' + loma 'fringe').
