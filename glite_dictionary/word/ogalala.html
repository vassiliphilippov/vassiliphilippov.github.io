Ogalala

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊɡəˈlɑːlə / (AmE), / ˌəʊɡəˈlɑːlə / (BrE)
A member of the Siouan people forming a division of the Teton Sioux, historically living in the Black Hills region of western South Dakota.
member of a Teton Sioux people

Examples

She traced her ancestry back to the Ogalala who once roamed the Black Hills.
An Ogalala elder shared traditional stories with the children of the tribe.
Many Ogalala have worked to preserve their language and customs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sioux - a group of Native American peoples, including the Ogalala

Etymology

From Lakota Oglála, meaning 'they scatter their own' or 'to scatter one’s own.'

2. NOUN

/ ˌoʊɡəˈlɑːlə / (AmE), / ˌəʊɡəˈlɑːlə / (BrE)
a Siouan language spoken by the Oglala people, a subgroup of the Lakota
Siouan language of the Oglala people

Examples

Few people today are fluent speakers of Ogalala due to the decline in use among the younger generation.
The Ogalala language preserves many cultural stories and traditions unique to the Oglala tribe.
Linguists have documented Ogalala as part of their efforts to revitalize endangered indigenous languages.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Siouan - a family of North American Indian languages spoken by the Sioux and related peoples
  • Synonym: Lakota - the language of the Lakota people, closely related to Ogalala

Etymology

From the name of the Oglala, a subgroup of the Lakota people.
