disgruntlement

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈɡrʌntlmənt / (AmE), / dɪsˈɡrʌntlmənt / (BrE)
A feeling of dissatisfaction or sulky discontent.
sulky dissatisfaction or discontent

Examples

Management tried to ignore the growing disgruntlement among employees over the new policy.
Her disgruntlement was clear from the way she avoided speaking during the meeting.
Public disgruntlement may force the government to reconsider its plans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discontent - a state of dissatisfaction with one's situation
  • Antonym: contentment - a state of happiness and satisfaction

Etymology

From 'disgruntle' (to make discontented), which is from 'gruntle', a diminutive of 'grunt'.
