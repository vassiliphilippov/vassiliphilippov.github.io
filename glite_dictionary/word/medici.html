Medici

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɛdɪtʃi / (AmE), / ˈmɛdɪtʃi / (BrE)
an aristocratic Italian family of merchants and bankers who were powerful rulers of Florence during the 15th century and prominent patrons of the Renaissance.
powerful Florentine family; Renaissance patrons

Examples

The Medici family played a pivotal role in the art and politics of Renaissance Florence.
Lorenzo de' Medici was known as a great patron of artists like Michelangelo.
Many history tours in Florence explore the legacy of the Medici.

Etymology

Italian, plural of Medico (surname), literally 'doctor'.
