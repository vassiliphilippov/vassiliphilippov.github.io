incorrect

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.kəˈrɛkt / (AmE), / ˌɪn.kəˈrɛkt / (BrE)
(of a word or expression) not conforming to accepted grammatical rules.
not conforming to grammatical rules

Examples

The sentence 'She go store' is incorrect because it does not follow standard English grammar.
Using a double negative like 'I don't need no help' is usually considered incorrect.
Teachers often mark incorrect constructions with red ink to highlight grammar mistakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ungrammatical - not following accepted rules of grammar
  • Antonym: correct - conforming to accepted rules of grammar

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + correctus (corrected, set right).
