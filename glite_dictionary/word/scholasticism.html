Scholasticism

1. NOUN

/ skəˈlæstɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / skəˈlæstɪsɪzəm / (BrE)
The system of philosophy and theology that was dominant in medieval Europe, characterized by a critical method of philosophical analysis based on the writings of Aristotle and the Church Fathers.
medieval European philosophical and theological system

Examples

Many medieval universities taught Scholasticism as the foundation of their philosophy curriculum.
The primary aim of Scholasticism was to reconcile Christian theology with classical philosophy.
Thomas Aquinas is one of the most prominent figures associated with Scholasticism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Thomism - philosophical school based on the ideas of Thomas Aquinas
  • Synonym: Aristotelianism - philosophical tradition following Aristotle's teachings

Etymology

From Latin 'scholasticus' (scholar, schoolman), from Greek 'scholastikos' (devoted to learning).
