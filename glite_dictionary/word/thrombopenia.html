thrombopenia

1. NOUN

/ ˌθroʊm.boʊˈpiː.ni.ə / (AmE), / ˌθrɒm.bəˈpiː.ni.ə / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by an abnormally low number of platelets in the blood.
low platelet count (medical)
medical

Examples

A routine blood test revealed the patient had thrombopenia and was at risk of excessive bleeding.
Doctors monitor for thrombopenia in patients receiving chemotherapy, as it can lead to increased bruising and bleeding.
The presence of thrombopenia often prompts further investigation into possible bone marrow disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrombocytopenia - abnormally low number of platelets in the blood (exact synonym)

Etymology

From Greek *thrombos* (clot) and *-penia* (deficiency).
