metabolite

1. NOUN

/ məˈtæbəˌlaɪt / (AmE), / mɪˈtæbəlʌɪt / (BrE)
a substance formed in, or necessary for, metabolism within a living organism
a substance involved in metabolism
medical

Examples

Glucose is an important metabolite in cellular respiration.
The study measured changes in each metabolite present in the blood samples.
Antibiotics may target a specific metabolite necessary for bacterial survival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metabolism - the chemical processes by which a living organism maintains life

Etymology

From Greek 'metabolḗ' (change), via French 'métabolite'.
