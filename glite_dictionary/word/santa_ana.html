Santa Ana

1. PROPN

/ ˌsæntə ˈænə / (AmE), / ˌsæntə ˈænə / (BrE)
A city in southern California, east of Long Beach, known as the county seat of Orange County.
city in southern California

Examples

Many residents commute from Santa Ana to jobs throughout Orange County.
The annual art fair in Santa Ana attracts visitors from all over California.
After moving to Santa Ana, she quickly found a sense of community.

Etymology

From Spanish, meaning 'Saint Anne'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsæntə ˈænə / (AmE), / ˌsæntə ˈænə / (BrE)
a strong, hot, dry wind that blows from the deserts of southern California toward the Pacific coast, typically in winter and autumn
hot, dry wind in southern California
american

Examples

The Santa Ana winds are expected to increase the risk of wildfires this week.
Every autumn, the Santa Ana sweeps through Los Angeles, bringing unseasonably warm temperatures.
Residents prepared for the arrival of the Santa Ana by clearing dry brush from their yards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foehn - a warm, dry wind descending the leeward side of a mountain

Etymology

From Santa Ana Canyon in southern California, through which the winds commonly blow.
