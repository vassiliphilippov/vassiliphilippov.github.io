gangplank

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæŋˌplæŋk / (AmE), / ˈɡæŋˌplæŋk / (BrE)
A movable bridge used for boarding or leaving a ship at a dock.
temporary bridge for boarding a vessel

Examples

The sailors quickly lowered the gangplank so passengers could disembark.
She walked carefully across the gangplank to board the ship.
A crew member waited at the gangplank to greet new arrivals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gangway - a passage or temporary bridge for boarding or leaving a ship

Etymology

From 'gang' (meaning 'a going, path') + 'plank', originally nautical.
