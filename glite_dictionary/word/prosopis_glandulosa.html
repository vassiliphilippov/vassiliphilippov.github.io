Prosopis glandulosa

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈsoʊpɪs ɡlænˈduːloʊsə / (AmE), / prəˈsəʊpɪs ɡlanˈdjuːləsə / (BrE)
A thorny, deep-rooted, drought-resistant shrub native to the southwestern United States and Mexico, bearing sweet pods that are important as livestock feed; also known as honey mesquite.
thorny shrub (honey mesquite) native to SW U.S. and Mexico

Examples

The Prosopis glandulosa provides essential food for livestock in arid regions.
Thickets of Prosopis glandulosa can quickly dominate grasslands if not managed.
Researchers are studying the drought resistance of Prosopis glandulosa for habitat restoration projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honey mesquite - common name for Prosopis glandulosa

Etymology

From Latin: genus name Prosopis and species epithet glandulosa (meaning 'gland-bearing').
