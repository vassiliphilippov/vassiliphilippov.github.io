Eucalyptus rostrata

1. NOUN

/ juːˈkʌlɪptəs rɒˈstreɪtə / (AmE), / juːˈkælɪptəs rɒˈstrɑːtə / (BrE)
a species of somewhat crooked red gum tree, native to Australia, chiefly growing along rivers and valued for its durable reddish wood used in heavy construction
Australian red gum tree with durable wood

Examples

The Eucalyptus rostrata thrives near riverbanks, providing shade and habitat for local wildlife.
Builders prize the wood of the Eucalyptus rostrata for its exceptional durability in outdoor structures.
We spotted several towering Eucalyptus rostrata trees during our hike through the Australian bush.

Etymology

From Latin 'Eucalyptus' (from Greek 'eu-' meaning 'well' and 'kalyptos' meaning 'covered') and 'rostrata' meaning 'beaked'.
