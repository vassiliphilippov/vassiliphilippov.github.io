tragedian

1. NOUN

/ trəˈdʒiːdiən / (AmE), / trəˈdʒiːdiən / (BrE)
a writer, especially a playwright, who composes tragedies
writer of tragedies

Examples

Shakespeare is often regarded as the greatest tragedian in English literature.
The ancient Greek tragedian Sophocles wrote Oedipus Rex and Antigone.
Many aspiring playwrights study the works of a master tragedian to perfect their craft.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: comedian - a writer or performer of comedies

Etymology

From Latin 'tragoedia' (tragedy) via Old French 'tragedien'.

2. NOUN

/ trəˈdʒiːdiən / (AmE), / trəˈdʒiːdiən / (BrE)
an actor who specializes in performing tragic roles, especially in the theatre
actor specializing in tragic roles

Examples

The renowned tragedian delivered a moving performance of Hamlet.
Sarah aspired to become a great tragedian after watching classical Greek plays.
Throughout the 19th century, many famous tragedians toured Europe performing Shakespearean dramas.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: comedian - an actor who specializes in comic roles

Etymology

From Latin *tragoedia* (tragedy) + -an, influenced by Greek *tragōidios* (actor in tragedies).

Synset POS: n
