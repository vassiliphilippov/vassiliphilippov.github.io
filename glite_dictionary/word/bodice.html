bodice

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑdɪs / (AmE), / ˈbɒdɪs / (BrE)
the part of a woman's dress that covers the body from the neck or shoulders to the waist.
upper part of a woman's dress

Examples

The tailor stitched decorative lace onto the bodice of the wedding gown.
Her dress had a fitted bodice and a flowing skirt that reached the floor.
Historical costumes often feature a tight bodice to create a distinctive silhouette.

Etymology

From Old French *bust* (body, chest), influenced by 'body' + '-ice' (as in 'piece').
