Clethra

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɛθrə / (AmE), / ˈkliːθrə / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous shrubs or small trees in the family Clethraceae, including species such as sweet pepperbush and white alder, known for their fragrant white flowers.
genus of deciduous shrubs/trees (Clethraceae)

Examples

The Clethra in our garden fills the air with sweet fragrance every summer.
Many gardeners appreciate Clethra for its ability to thrive in moist, shady areas.
She planted several Clethra shrubs to attract butterflies and bees to her backyard.

Etymology

From Greek *klēthra* (alder tree).
