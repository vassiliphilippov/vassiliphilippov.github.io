Spraguea

1. NOUN

/ ˈspreɪɡiə / (AmE), / ˈspreɪɡiə / (BrE)
A genus of perennial herbs with deep woody taproots and flower heads arranged in umbels or cymes.
genus of perennial herbs

Examples

Members of Spraguea typically have woody taproots and produce blooms in umbels.

/ spræɡiə / (AmE), / sprɔːɡiə / (BrE)
a genus of small, usually perennial herbs with deep woody taproots and flower heads arranged in umbels or cymes
genus of perennial herbs with woody taproots

Examples

The Spraguea genus includes herbs known for their distinctive root systems.
Botanists discovered a new species within the Spraguea genus last year.
Members of Spraguea often flourish in rocky or dry soils.

Etymology

Named after American botanist Isaac Sprague (1811–1895).
