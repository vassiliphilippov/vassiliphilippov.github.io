Anguilla

1. PROPN

/ æŋˈɡwɪlə / (AmE), / æŋˈɡwɪlə / (BrE)
A British overseas territory in the Caribbean, comprising several islands in the northeast of the West Indies.
British territory in the Caribbean
british

Examples

Many tourists are drawn to Anguilla for its pristine beaches and clear waters.
The capital of Anguilla is The Valley, and it is known for its relaxed atmosphere.
After the hurricane, Anguilla received international aid to help with its recovery efforts.

Etymology

From Latin 'anguilla' meaning 'eel', likely derived from the shape of the main island.

2. NOUN

/ ænˈɡwɪl.ə / (AmE), / aŋˈɡwɪl.ə / (BrE)
the genus of eels in the family Anguillidae, comprising the true freshwater eels
genus of true freshwater eels

Examples

The European eel is classified under the genus Anguilla and is an important species in European rivers.
Scientists study the migratory habits of Anguilla to better understand its life cycle.
Several species in the genus Anguilla are now considered endangered due to overfishing and habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eel - elongated fish belonging to the order Anguilliformes

Etymology

From Latin 'anguilla' meaning 'eel'.
