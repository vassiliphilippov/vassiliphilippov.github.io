Dioscorea paniculata

1. NOUN

/ diˌɑːskoʊˌriə ˌpænɪˈkjuːlətə / (AmE), / dʌɪˌɒskəˈriːə ˌpanɪˈkjuːlətə / (BrE)
a species of wild yam, formerly used in dried form to treat rheumatism or liver disorders
species of wild yam

Examples

The root of Dioscorea paniculata was traditionally prepared as a remedy for joint pain.
Botanists studied Dioscorea paniculata due to its historical medicinal uses.
Some herbalists once recommended Dioscorea paniculata for liver complaints.

Etymology

From genus name Dioscorea (after Greek physician Dioscorides) and species epithet from Latin 'paniculatus' (having panicles).
