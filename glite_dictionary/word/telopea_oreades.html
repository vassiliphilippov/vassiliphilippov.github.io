Telopea oreades

1. NOUN

/ təˈloʊpiə oʊˈriːədiːz / (AmE), / təˈləʊpiə əʊˈriːədiːz / (BrE)
A tall shrub native to eastern Australia, with oblanceolate to obovate leaves and clusters of red flowers.
tall Australian shrub with red flower clusters

Examples

The Telopea oreades is admired for its striking red flowers and ornamental value in gardens.
Botanists often study the Telopea oreades to understand adaptation in Australian flora.
The dense clusters of blossoms make the Telopea oreades a favorite among native plant enthusiasts.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'telopos' (seen from afar); species name 'oreades' refers to mountain nymphs in Greek mythology, alluding to its habitat.
