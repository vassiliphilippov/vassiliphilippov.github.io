rentier

1. NOUN

/ rɑ̃nˈtjeɪ, ˈrɛn.ti.eɪ / (AmE), / rɒ̃n.tjeɪ, ˈrɛn.ti.eɪ / (BrE)
a person whose income derives from property rents, bond interest, or similar investments, rather than from active work
person living on investment income

Examples

He lived the life of a rentier, traveling Europe and managing his investments from afar.
During the nineteenth century, the city was home to many wealthy rentiers.
Unlike his peers, he never worked for a living, instead relying on his status as a rentier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: investor - a person who puts money into financial schemes, property, or a commercial venture

Etymology

From French rentier (person living on income from property), from rente (income, rent).
