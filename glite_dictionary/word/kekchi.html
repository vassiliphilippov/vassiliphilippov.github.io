Kekchi

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛk.tʃi / (AmE), / ˈkɛk.tʃi / (BrE)
A Mayan language spoken by the Kekchi people in parts of Central America, especially in Guatemala and Belize.
a Mayan language of Central America

Examples

The Kekchi language is widely spoken in the Alta Verapaz region of Guatemala.
Linguists are working to document and preserve the Kekchi language for future generations.
He learned basic Kekchi phrases to communicate with local villagers during his visit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Q'eqchi' - another spelling of Kekchi, referring to the same Mayan language

2. NOUN

/ kɛkˈtʃiː / (AmE), / kɛkˈtʃiː / (BrE)
A member of a Mayan people living in north central Guatemala.
member of a Mayan people of Guatemala

Examples

A group of Kekchi gathered to celebrate their traditional festival.
The Kekchi have preserved their language and customs for centuries.
Many Kekchi live in small villages in the Guatemalan highlands.

Etymology

From Q'eqchi' (the people's own name), a Mayan language.
