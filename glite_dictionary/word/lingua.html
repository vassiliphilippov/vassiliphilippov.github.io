lingua

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪŋɡwə / (AmE), / ˈlɪŋɡwə / (BrE)
the tongue, especially as an organ of speech or as found in animals; a mobile mass of muscular tissue in the mouth
the tongue (anatomy, zoology)
medical

Examples

The scientist examined the lingua of the frog under a microscope.
In many mollusks, the lingua is adapted for scraping food from surfaces.
Damage to the lingua can affect an animal's ability to feed and communicate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tongue - the muscular organ in the mouth used for tasting, swallowing, and speaking

Etymology

From Latin *lingua* (tongue, language).
