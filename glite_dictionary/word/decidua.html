decidua

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsɪdʒuə / (AmE), / dɪˈsɪdjuːə / (BrE)
the modified mucous membrane lining the uterus that forms the maternal part of the placenta during pregnancy
maternal uterine lining during pregnancy
medical

Examples

The decidua plays a crucial role in supporting the embryo during early pregnancy.
After childbirth, the decidua is shed as part of the afterbirth.
Abnormalities in the decidua can lead to complications in pregnancy.

Etymology

From Latin 'decidua' (that which falls off), referring to the fact that this lining is shed after childbirth.
