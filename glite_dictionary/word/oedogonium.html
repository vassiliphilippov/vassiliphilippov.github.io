Oedogonium

1. NOUN

/ ˌiːdoʊˈɡoʊniəm / (AmE), / ˌiːdəˈɡəʊniəm / (BrE)
A genus of freshwater green algae with long, unbranched filaments, typically free-floating when mature.
genus of freshwater green algae

Examples

Scientists collected samples of Oedogonium from the pond for further study.
The long filaments of Oedogonium can often be seen floating freely in freshwater habitats.
Algae such as Oedogonium play a crucial role in aquatic ecosystems.

Etymology

From Greek oidos (swelling) + gonos (offspring), referring to distinctive cell structures.
