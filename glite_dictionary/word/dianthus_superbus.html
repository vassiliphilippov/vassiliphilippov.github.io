Dianthus superbus

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈænθəs suːˈpɜːbəs / (AmE), / daɪˈænθəs suːˈpɜːbəs / (BrE)
A Eurasian perennial plant of the pink family, cultivated for its fragrant lilac or rose flowers with deeply fringed petals.
a Eurasian perennial flowering plant

Examples

The fragrant blooms of Dianthus superbus attract many pollinators to the garden each summer.
Botanists admire Dianthus superbus for its deeply fringed rose-colored petals.
Several varieties of Dianthus superbus are popular in ornamental horticulture.

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Dianthus' means 'divine flower' and 'superbus' means 'superb' or 'splendid'.
