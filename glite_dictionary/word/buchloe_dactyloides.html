Buchloe dactyloides

1. NOUN

/ buːˈkloʊ.iː dækˌtaɪ.lɔɪˈdiːz / (AmE), / buːˈkləʊ.iː dækˌtɪ.lɔɪˈdiːz / (BrE)
A short perennial grass native to the dry plains of central North America, commonly known as buffalo grass.
species of short prairie grass

Examples

Buchloe dactyloides is often used in drought-resistant lawns throughout the central United States.
The prairie was covered with Buchloe dactyloides, providing food for grazing animals.
Because Buchloe dactyloides needs little water, it's ideal for xeriscaping projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buffalo grass - common name for Buchloe dactyloides

Etymology

From genus name Buchloe and species name dactyloides, from Greek daktylos (fingered), referring to the shape of the grass's seed heads.
