Elaeagnus commutata

1. NOUN

/ ɪliˈæɡnəs kəˈmjuːteɪtə / (AmE), / ɪliˈæɡnəs kəˈmjuːteɪtə / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub native to North America, with silvery leaves and fruits, commonly known as silverberry or wolf-willow.
deciduous North American shrub with silvery leaves

Examples

The silver leaves of Elaeagnus commutata make it an attractive addition to gardens.
Native Elaeagnus commutata shrubs are often found along riverbanks in the northern United States and Canada.
Birds are attracted to the silvery fruit produced by Elaeagnus commutata in late summer.

Etymology

From Latin genus name *Elaeagnus*, and Latin *commutata* meaning 'changed' or 'altered'.
