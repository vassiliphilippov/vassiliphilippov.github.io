Spinus

1. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈspɪ.nəs / (BrE)
A genus of finches including siskins and New World goldfinches, sometimes classified as a subgenus of Carduelis.
bird genus: siskins and goldfinches

Examples

Several species within the genus Spinus are known for their vibrant yellow plumage.
The American goldfinch is now classified as Spinus tristis in many ornithological references.
Recent studies have reclassified some siskins from the genus Carduelis to Spinus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Carduelis - a genus of finches related to goldfinches and siskins

Etymology

From Latin 'spinus', meaning 'blackthorn', likely referring to the sharp bill or habitat association.

2. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈspʌɪ.nəs / (BrE)
A genus of small finch-like birds including siskins and New World goldfinches, formerly considered a subgenus of Carduelis.
a bird genus: siskins, goldfinches

Examples

The American goldfinch belongs to the genus Spinus.
Many siskins, previously classified under Carduelis, are now grouped in Spinus.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of several Spinus species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Carduelis - A related genus of finches, including European goldfinches and some siskins

Etymology

Modern Latin, used as a genus name in ornithology.
