laundress

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔn.drɪs / (AmE), / ˈlɔːn.drəs / (BrE)
a woman whose job is to wash and iron clothes, usually for other people
woman who washes clothes for others

Examples

The wealthy family hired a laundress to care for their laundry every week.
As a laundress, she worked long hours washing and pressing uniforms for the hospital.
In Victorian times, it was common for single women to earn a living as a laundress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: launderer - a person whose job is to wash and iron clothes

Etymology

From Old French ‘lavandière’, from ‘laver’ (to wash).
