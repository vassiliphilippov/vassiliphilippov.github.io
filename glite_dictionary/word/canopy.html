canopy

1. VERB

/ ˈkænəpi / (AmE), / ˈkanəpi / (BrE)
to cover or shelter with or as if with a canopy
to cover with a canopy

Examples

A thick layer of leaves canopied the forest floor, creating patches of shade.
The ancient king's throne was canopied with silk to denote his status.
Vines had grown over the old gazebo, canopying the entire structure in green.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cover - to place something over or on top of
  • Antonym: to uncover - to remove a cover from

Etymology

From Middle English canape, from Latin canopēum (mosquito net over a bed).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkænəpi / (AmE), / ˈkanəpi / (BrE)
the transparent cover over the cockpit of an aircraft
aircraft cockpit's transparent covering

Examples

The pilot quickly closed the canopy before starting the engine.
Rain splattered across the canopy as the jet sat on the runway.
The fighter jet's canopy provides a clear view for both the pilot and co-pilot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockpit - the area in an aircraft where the pilot sits and controls the plane

Etymology

From Middle English 'canape', via Old French from Medieval Latin 'canopeum' (bed-curtain, mosquito net), ultimately from Greek 'konopion'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02955673-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈkænəpi / (AmE), / ˈkænəpi / (BrE)
the cloth part of a parachute that opens and fills with air to slow descent
the main part of a parachute

Examples

The instructor checked that the canopy was fully open before beginning the descent.
If the canopy fails to inflate, the reserve chute must be deployed immediately.
The brightly colored canopy made it easy to spot the skydiver from the ground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parachute - device for slowing descent through the atmosphere

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'canopium', from Latin 'conopeum' (mosquito net, canopy).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02955545-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈkænəpi / (AmE), / ˈkænəpi / (BrE)
a covering, typically of fabric, that is used as a roof or shelter to protect an area from the sun or rain
a roof-like covering for shelter

Examples

We set up a canopy in the garden to provide shade during the party.
The outdoor market had a colorful canopy over each stall to protect vendors from rain.
As they walked, thick vines formed a natural canopy overhead, sheltering them from the sun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awning - a sheet of canvas or other material stretched on a frame and used to keep the sun or rain off a storefront, window, doorway, or deck
  • Synonym: roof - the structure forming the upper covering of a building or vehicle

Etymology

From Middle English 'canope', from Old French 'canope', from Medieval Latin 'canopia', from Greek 'kōnōpeion' (mosquito net).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02955279-n
Synset POS: n
