Albizzia julibrissin

1. NOUN

/ ælˈbɪzi.ə ˌdʒuːləˈbrɪsɪn / (AmE), / ælˈbɪzi.ə ˌdʒuːlɪˈbrɪsɪn / (BrE)
A species of small, ornamental tree native to Asia, with feathery, bipinnate leaves and clusters of pink, silky, stamen-rich flowers, commonly known as the silk tree or mimosa.
species of ornamental Asiatic tree

Examples

The Albizzia julibrissin in our garden attracts many bees and butterflies with its pink flowers.
Due to its delicate foliage, Albizzia julibrissin is often planted as an ornamental tree in parks.
After a storm, several branches of the Albizzia julibrissin were found scattered across the lawn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silk tree - another common name for Albizzia julibrissin
  • Synonym: mimosa - a common name sometimes used for Albizzia julibrissin

Etymology

Genus name from Italian naturalist Filippo degli Albizzi + specific epithet from Persian *gul-i abrisham* (silk flower).
