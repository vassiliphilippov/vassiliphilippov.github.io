triage

1. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪ.ɑʒ / (AmE), / ˈtriː.ɑːʒ / (BrE)
the process of sorting and prioritizing patients or resources according to the urgency of their need for medical treatment or aid
sorting by urgency of medical or other aid

Examples

After the earthquake, doctors performed triage to determine which patients needed immediate surgery.
In large-scale disasters, triage helps ensure that limited resources are used effectively.
The nurse was trained in triage procedures to quickly assess incoming patients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prioritization - the action of arranging items or people in order of importance or urgency

Etymology

From French *triage* (sorting), from *trier* (to sort).
