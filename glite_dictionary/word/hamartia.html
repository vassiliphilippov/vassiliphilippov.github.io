hamartia

1. NOUN

/ həˈmɑːrtiə / (AmE), / həˈmɑːtɪə / (BrE)
A fatal flaw or error in the character of a tragic hero, which leads to their downfall.
fatal flaw of a tragic hero

Examples

A sense of pride proved to be the king’s hamartia in the classic tragedy.
The play explores how a seemingly minor hamartia leads the protagonist to ruin.
Scholars often debate whether Macbeth’s ambition or his indecision is his tragic hamartia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tragic flaw - the character flaw or error of a tragic hero that leads to their downfall

Etymology

From Greek hamartia (failure, error), first used in Aristotle's Poetics.
