verbalised

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɜrbəlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈvɜːbəlaɪzd / (BrE)
communicated or expressed in words; put into speech or writing
expressed in words

Examples

His verbalised anger helped others understand his frustration.
The policy was first verbalised during the press conference.
She appreciated when her concerns were verbalised clearly by the spokesperson.

Synset Examples

frequently uttered sentiments

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unspoken - not expressed in words
  • Synonym: to verbalize - to express in words

Etymology

From the verb 'verbalise,' ultimately from Latin 'verbum' (word).
