asceticism

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɛtɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / əˈsɛtɪsɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The practice or doctrine of strict self-discipline and abstention from all forms of indulgence, typically for religious or spiritual reasons.
strict self-discipline; abstention from indulgence

Examples

The monks practiced asceticism, forsaking all material comforts to focus on spiritual growth.
Her commitment to asceticism was evident in her simple lifestyle and strict routine.
Many philosophies regard asceticism as a path to enlightenment or higher wisdom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-denial - the denial of one's own interests and needs
  • Antonym: hedonism - the pursuit of pleasure as the highest good

Etymology

From Greek askētismos (training, exercise), from askētēs (monk, hermit).

severe self-discipline and avoidance of all forms of indulgence, typically for religious or spiritual reasons

Related Terms

From Greek askētismos (training, exercise), from askētēs (monk, hermit), from askein (to exercise, train).
