insignificant

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.sɪɡˈnɪf.ɪ.kənt / (AmE), / ˌɪn.sɪɡˈnɪf.ɪ.kənt / (BrE)
too small or unimportant to be worth consideration or notice
unimportant; too small to matter

Examples

He dismissed the error as an insignificant mistake that would not affect the results.
The difference in price between the two models is insignificant.
Compared to the achievements of her career, this setback seemed insignificant.

Synset Examples

we stayed in a dinky old hotel
an insignificant sum of money

Related Terms

  • Antonym: significant - having meaning or importance

Etymology

From Latin insignificans, insignificantis, from in- (not) + significans (significant).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪnsɪɡˈnɪfɪkənt / (AmE), / ˌɪnsɪɡˈnɪfɪkənt / (BrE)
Too small or unimportant to be worth considering; having little or no meaning, influence, or effect.
not important or meaningful

Examples

Although he tried to contribute, his efforts seemed insignificant compared to hers.
The difference in price was so insignificant that no one noticed.
What once felt important now seems quite insignificant in the grand scheme of things.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: significant - important; meaningful; having effect or influence
  • Synonym: unimportant - not important; lacking significance

Etymology

From Latin insignificans, from in- (not) + significans (signifying, meaning).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02171958-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˌɪnsɪɡˈnɪfɪkənt / (AmE), / ˌɪnsɪɡˈnɪfɪkənt / (BrE)
Not important or not having much influence, power, or effect.
not important; having little influence

Examples

Although his contributions to the project seemed insignificant, they were appreciated by his team.
The error in the report was insignificant and did not affect the final outcome.
She was worried that her actions would be insignificant in the grand scheme of things.
