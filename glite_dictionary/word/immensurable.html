immensurable

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈmɛn.səˌɹə.bəl / (AmE), / ɪˈmɛn.sjə.ɹə.bəl / (BrE)
too great or extensive to be measured; impossible to measure
impossible to measure

Examples

The mountain's height was once thought to be immensurable by early explorers.
They faced immensurable odds during their quest for survival.
The depth of her gratitude was immensurable, beyond what words could describe.

Synset Examples

unmeasurable reaches of outer space
immeasurable suffering

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immeasurable - too large, extensive, or extreme to be measured

Etymology

From Late Latin immensurabilis, meaning 'unmeasurable'.
