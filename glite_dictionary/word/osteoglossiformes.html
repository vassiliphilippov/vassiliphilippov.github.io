Osteoglossiformes

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːsti.oʊˌɡlɑːsɪˈfɔːrmiːz / (AmE), / ˌɒsti.əˌɡlɒsɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
an order of teleost fishes distinguished by a bony tongue, including species such as the arowana and elephantfish
order of bony-tongued fishes

Examples

The Osteoglossiformes include some of the world's largest freshwater fish, such as the arapaima.
Researchers studied the unique jaw structure found in Osteoglossiformes to better understand their feeding habits.
Some species within the Osteoglossiformes are highly prized in the aquarium trade for their striking appearance.

Etymology

From Greek 'osteon' (bone) and 'glossa' (tongue), referring to the bony tongue structure.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑstioʊˌɡlɒsɪˈfɔrmiːz / (AmE), / ˌɒstiəʊˌɡlɒsɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
an order of freshwater teleost fishes characterized by bony tongues, including the arowanas and elephantfishes
order of fish with bony tongues
medical

Examples

The Osteoglossiformes are known for their distinctive bony tongues, which set them apart from other fish orders.
Some aquarium enthusiasts keep Osteoglossiformes, such as arowanas, for their unique appearance and behavior.
Research into Osteoglossiformes has revealed interesting evolutionary adaptations related to their feeding mechanisms.

Etymology

From Greek osteon (bone) + glōssa (tongue) + Latin -formes (having the form of).
