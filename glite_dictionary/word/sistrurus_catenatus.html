Sistrurus catenatus

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈstruːrəs kəˈteɪnətəs / (AmE), / sɪˈstrʊərəs kəˈteɪnətəs / (BrE)
A small species of rattlesnake found in moist habitats from the Great Lakes region to Mexico, feeding mainly on mice and small amphibians.
a species of small rattlesnake

Examples

The Sistrurus catenatus inhabits swamps and wet meadows across Central North America.
Wildlife researchers tracked Sistrurus catenatus populations to study their response to environmental changes.
Because Sistrurus catenatus is a venomous snake, it is important to observe it from a safe distance.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Sistrurus' (genus name), from Greek 'seistron' (rattle); 'catenatus' means 'chained', referencing patterning.
