Glasgow

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡlæsˌɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɡlɑːzɡəʊ / (BrE)
the largest city in Scotland, located on the River Clyde in west central Scotland, known for its shipbuilding industry and cultural institutions
largest city in Scotland; port on the River Clyde

Examples

She moved to Glasgow to attend university and fell in love with the city.
The shipyards in Glasgow were once among the most productive in the world.
Many famous bands have emerged from the vibrant music scene in Glasgow.

Etymology

From Gaelic 'Glaschu,' thought to mean 'green hollow.'
