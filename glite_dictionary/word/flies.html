flies

1. NOUN

/ flaɪz / (AmE), / flaɪz / (BrE)
(theater) the space above the stage, out of sight of the audience, used to store scenery and stage equipment.
theater: area above stage for scenery storage

Examples

The stagehands quickly raised the backdrop into the flies before the next act began.
Heavy lighting equipment is often stored in the flies when not in use.
Access to the flies is restricted to trained theater staff for safety reasons.
