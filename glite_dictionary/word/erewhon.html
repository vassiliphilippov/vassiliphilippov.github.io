Erewhon

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛrəˌwɑn / (AmE), / ˈɛərəˌwɒn / (BrE)
a satirical novel (1872) by Samuel Butler, or the imaginary land described in it, known for its inversion of conventional norms
satirical novel and mythical land by Samuel Butler

Examples

Samuel Butler's Erewhon offers a sharp critique of Victorian society.
The concept of morality in Erewhon challenges readers' expectations.
Many scholars have analyzed the philosophical themes present in Erewhon.

Etymology

An anagram of 'nowhere', coined by Samuel Butler for his novel.
