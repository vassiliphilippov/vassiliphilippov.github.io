inadequate

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈædɪkwət / (AmE), / ɪnˈadɪkwət / (BrE)
not enough or not good enough to meet a particular need, purpose, or standard.
not sufficient; not good enough

Examples

The report found the safety measures were inadequate to protect workers.
His explanation for being late was clearly inadequate and unconvincing.
Many students feel that support for mental health is still inadequate at the university.

Synset Examples

inadequate training
the staff was inadequate
she was unequal to the task

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insufficient - not enough; lacking in amount or degree
  • Antonym: adequate - good enough or sufficient for a particular purpose
  • Antonym: sufficient - enough; as much as is needed

Etymology

From Latin 'inadequatus', from 'in-' (not) + 'aequatus' (equalized, made equal).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈædəˌkwət / (AmE), / ɪnˈædɪkwət / (BrE)
not enough in quantity or quality to meet a particular need; insufficient.
not enough; insufficient

Examples

The shelter provided inadequate food and water for all the evacuees.
His explanation was inadequate and failed to address the main concerns.
Many students felt they had received inadequate preparation for the final exam.

Synset Examples

an inadequate income
a poor salary
money is short
on short rations
food is in short supply
short on experience
the jejune diets of the very poor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insufficient - not enough; inadequate
  • Antonym: sufficient - enough to meet a need

Etymology

From Latin 'inaequatus', past participle of 'inaequare' (to make unequal), from 'in-' (not) + 'aequare' (to equal).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02344882-s
Synset POS: s
