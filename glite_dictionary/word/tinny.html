tinny

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɪni / (AmE), / ˈtɪni / (BrE)
Made of or containing tin.
containing or made of tin

Examples

The old teapot felt tinny and lightweight when he picked it up.
Some antique toys were made from tinny materials that rusted easily.
She disliked the tinny taste of the metal cup.

Etymology

From tin + -y, denoting composition or association.

WordNet ID: oewn-02826692-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈtɪni / (AmE), / ˈtɪni / (BrE)
(of an object or material) of very poor quality or flimsy, often resembling tin in thinness, lightness, or insubstantiality.
of poor quality; flimsy

Examples

The walls of the cheap motel room felt tinny and offered little soundproofing.
Her tinny umbrella folded inside out at the slightest gust of wind.
The toy was made of such tinny plastic that it broke after one use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flimsy - lacking solidity or strength; easily broken

Etymology

From 'tin' + '-y', referring to something resembling tin, especially in being thin or insubstantial.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02354508-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈtɪni / (AmE), / ˈtɪni / (BrE)
(of a sound) having a thin, metallic quality and lacking fullness or resonance.
thin, metallic-sounding

Examples

The old radio produced a tinny noise that was hard to listen to.
Her tinny laugh echoed uncomfortably in the empty hall.
The cheap headphones made the music sound strangely tinny and flat.

Synset Examples

an unpleasant tinny voice

Related Terms

  • Antonym: resonant - (of sound) deep, clear, and continuing to sound or ring
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01533286-s
Synset POS: s
