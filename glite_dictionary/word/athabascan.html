Athabascan

1. NOUN

/ ˌæθəˈbæskən / (AmE), / ˌaθəˈbæskən / (BrE)
a member of any of the indigenous peoples of North America who speak an Athapaskan language and traditionally live in the subarctic regions of western Canada and central Alaska
member of a North American indigenous group

Examples

Many Athabascan people continue to practice traditional fishing methods in Alaska.
The Athabascan languages include a variety of dialects spoken across North America.
She learned about Athabascan culture during her travels through western Canada.

Etymology

From Lake Athabasca in Canada, from Cree ᐊᖬᑕᐸᐢᑲ (athapaskāw, meaning 'grass or reeds here and there').

2. NOUN

/ ˌæθ.əˈbæsk.ən / (AmE), / ˌaθ.əˈbæsk.ən / (BrE)
a group of related indigenous languages spoken in North America, primarily in Alaska, northwestern Canada, and the southwestern United States
group of indigenous North American languages

Examples

Linguists have studied the complex grammar of several Athabascan languages.
The Navajo language is a member of the Athabascan family.
Many Athabascan languages are still spoken in Alaska today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Na-Dene - a major language family that includes Athabascan languages

Etymology

From Lake Athabasca in Canada, combined with '-an', coined by linguist Edward Sapir.
