osmosis

1. NOUN

/ ɑːzˈmoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ɒzˈməʊsɪs / (BrE)
the process by which molecules of a solvent pass through a semipermeable membrane from a region of higher concentration to one of lower concentration, equalizing the concentrations on both sides
movement of molecules through a membrane

Examples

Water moves into plant roots by osmosis, helping the plant absorb nutrients.
The concept of osmosis is fundamental in understanding how cells interact with their environment.
Sugar cannot cross the membrane, so osmosis causes water to move toward the sugar solution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diffusion - the movement of particles from an area of higher concentration to one of lower concentration

Etymology

From Greek ōsmos (a pushing, impulse), from ōthein (to push).
