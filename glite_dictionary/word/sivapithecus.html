Sivapithecus

1. NOUN

/ ˌsiːvəpɪˈθiːkəs / (AmE), / ˌsɪvəpɪˈθiːkəs / (BrE)
an extinct genus of fossil primates that lived in the Miocene epoch, whose remains have been found in India and Pakistan, thought to be closely related to the orangutan
extinct Miocene primate genus

Examples

Fossils of Sivapithecus have provided important clues about primate evolution in Asia.
Many scientists believe that the modern orangutan shares a common ancestor with Sivapithecus.
The distinctive jaw structure of Sivapithecus suggests it had a diet similar to today's fruit-eating primates.

Etymology

From modern Latin, combining 'Siva' (a Hindu deity) with Greek 'pithekos' (ape).

2. NOUN

/ sɪˌvɑːpɪˈθiːkəs / (AmE), / sɪˌvapiːˈθiːkəs / (BrE)
an extinct genus of primates from the lower Pliocene, believed to be closely related to the modern orangutan
extinct genus of primates (lower Pliocene)

Examples

Fossils of Sivapithecus have provided valuable clues about primate evolution in South Asia.
Researchers believe that Sivapithecus may be an ancestor of the modern orangutan.
The jaw of Sivapithecus shows similarities to both apes and humans.

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'Shiva' (a Hindu deity) and Greek 'pithēkos' (ape).
