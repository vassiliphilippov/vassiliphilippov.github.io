Nageia

1. PROPN

/ nəˈdʒiːə / (AmE), / nəˈdʒiːə / (BrE)
A small genus of Asian evergreen trees with columnar crowns, distinguished by leaves that lack a midrib; native to eastern Asia, including India, the Philippines, and New Guinea.
genus of Asian evergreen trees

Examples

The rainforest in New Guinea is home to several species of Nageia.
Botanists collected leaves from a Nageia tree for scientific analysis.
Many Nageia species are valued for their distinctive evergreen foliage.

Etymology

From modern Latin, formed from an anagram of 'Agathis', another tree genus, as per botanical naming conventions.

2. NOUN

/ nəˈdʒiːə / (AmE), / nəˈdʒiːə / (BrE)
A small genus of Asian evergreen trees with columnar crowns and leaves lacking a midrib, found in eastern Asia including India, the Philippines, and New Guinea.
genus of Asian evergreen trees

Examples

The Nageia trees are notable for their unique leaves without a central midrib.
Botanists discovered a rare species belonging to the genus Nageia in New Guinea.
Several forest reserves in Southeast Asia are home to Nageia evergreens.

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Japanese 'Nagi'.
