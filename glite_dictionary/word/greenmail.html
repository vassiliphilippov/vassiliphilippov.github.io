greenmail

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrinˌmeɪl / (AmE), / ˈɡriːnmeɪl / (BrE)
the practice of buying a significant stake in a company as a threat of takeover, forcing the company to buy back the shares at a premium to prevent the takeover
corporate tactic to force share buyback at premium

Examples

The company paid millions of dollars in greenmail to avoid a hostile takeover.
Some investors accuse the billionaire of engaging in greenmail rather than pursuing a genuine business interest.
Greenmail became a controversial tactic on Wall Street during the 1980s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackmail - the act of demanding money by threatening to reveal negative information

Etymology

Blend of 'greenback' (slang for money) and 'blackmail', first used in the US in the 1980s.
