Salix humilis

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪ.lɪks ˈhjuː.mɪ.lɪs / (AmE), / ˈsaː.lɪks ˈhjuː.mɪ.lɪs / (BrE)
A slender, shrubby species of willow native to dry areas in North America.
a shrubby North American willow

Examples

Salix humilis is commonly found on prairies and sandy hills across the Midwest.
The slender branches of Salix humilis make it easy to identify among other willows.
Gardeners sometimes plant Salix humilis in areas with poor, dry soil conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: willow - a tree or shrub of the genus Salix, typically found near water

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Salix' meaning 'willow,' species name 'humilis' meaning 'low' or 'humble'.
