hardihood

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrdiˌhʊd / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdihʊd / (BrE)
the quality of being bold and willing to take risks or face danger
boldness; willingness to take risks

Examples

She showed remarkable hardihood in venturing alone into the stormy night.
The team's hardihood inspired others to follow their daring example.
Without a touch of hardihood, many great discoveries might never have happened.

Synset Examples

the proposal required great boldness
the plan required great hardiness of heart

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boldness - willingness to take risks; confidence
  • Antonym: timidity - lack of courage or confidence; shyness

Etymology

From Middle English hardihed, from Old French hardi (bold, daring) + -hood (state, condition).
