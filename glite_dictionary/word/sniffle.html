sniffle

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnɪfəl / (AmE), / ˈsnɪfəl / (BrE)
an act of breathing noisily through the nose, especially when the nose is blocked or one is about to cry
noisy breathing through the nose

Examples

She wiped her nose after a loud sniffle interrupted the quiet room.
A constant sniffle told us that he probably had a cold.
The child tried to hide her sniffle as she held back tears.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sniff - to inhale audibly through the nose

Etymology

Imitative of the sound made when sniffing.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00837810-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈsnɪf.əl / (AmE), / ˈsnɪf.əl / (BrE)
to cry or complain with frequent sniffing, as when someone is sniffling while upset or whiny
cry or complain with sniffling

Examples

He began to sniffle quietly after being scolded by his teacher.
Whenever she loses a game, she tends to sniffle and complain for hours.
Stop sniffling—you need to handle the situation more maturely.

Synset Examples

Stop snivelling — you got yourself into this mess!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snivel - to complain or cry with snuffling

Etymology

Imitative of the sound of sniffing.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00067045-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈsnɪfəl / (AmE), / ˈsnɪfəl / (BrE)
to inhale noisily and repeatedly through the nose, typically because of a cold or while crying
inhale noisily through the nose

Examples

Allergy season starts and I can't help but sniffle constantly.
She tried not to sniffle during the movie, but her cold made it impossible.
As the teacher spoke, a child continued to sniffle quietly in the back row.

Synset Examples

the sick student was sniffling in the back row

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sniff - inhale air through the nose, especially to detect a smell

Etymology

Imitative in origin, echoing the sound; related to 'sniff'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00007549-v
Synset POS: v
