shmear

1. NOUN

/ ʃmɪr / (AmE), / ʃmɪə / (BrE)
a collection or batch of things that go together, especially a set bought or considered as a whole
a batch or collection of things
informal american

Examples

He decided to order the whole shmear for his family’s brunch.
The car dealership tried to sell him the deluxe package—the full shmear.
When planning her party, she bought the decorations, the balloons, the food—the entire shmear.

Synset Examples

he bought the whole schmeer

Etymology

From Yiddish שמיר (shmir), possibly influenced by German schmieren (to smear, spread), extended in US English to mean 'the whole assortment'.
