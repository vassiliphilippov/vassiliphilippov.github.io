carpophagous

1. ADJ

/ kɑrˈpɑfəɡəs / (AmE), / kɑːˈpɒfəɡəs / (BrE)
(of an animal) feeding on fruit.
feeding on fruit (of animals)

Examples

Many tropical bats are carpophagous, relying mainly on fruit for their diet.
A carpophagous bird contributes to seed dispersal by eating fruit and later excreting the seeds.
Studies have shown that carpophagous insects play a significant role in shaping fruit crop yields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frugivorous - feeding primarily on raw fruits, nuts, seeds, and plants

Etymology

From Greek karpos (fruit) + phagein (to eat).
