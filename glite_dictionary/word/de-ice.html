to de-ice

1. VERB

/ diˈaɪs / (AmE), / diˈaɪs / (BrE)
to remove ice or frost from something, especially a vehicle or surface
remove ice or frost

Examples

The ground crew must de-ice the wings before the plane can take off.
I had to de-ice my car windshield after the overnight frost.
They are de-icing the runways to ensure safe landings.

Synset Examples

Defrost the car window

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to defrost - to remove frost or ice from; to thaw

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'de-' meaning removal, and 'ice', referring to frozen water.
