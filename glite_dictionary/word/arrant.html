arrant

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛrənt / (AmE), / ˈærənt / (BrE)
used to emphasize how bad or extreme someone or something is; complete or utter (usually pejorative)
complete; utter (emphasizing something negative)

Examples

You are an arrant liar, and nobody will believe your story.
What an arrant waste of time this meeting turned out to be.
The plan was dismissed as arrant nonsense by the experts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: utter - complete; absolute (used for emphasis)
  • Synonym: thoroughgoing - absolute; complete (often used for emphasis)
  • Antonym: partial - not complete; only in part

Etymology

From Middle English *arrant*, from Old French *arant* 'notorious', variant of *errant* 'wandering'.
