corundom

1. NOUN

/ kəˈrʌn.dəm / (AmE), / kəˈrʌn.dəm / (BrE)
a very hard mineral, consisting of aluminum oxide, used as an abrasive and as a gemstone when clear varieties occur
very hard mineral used as abrasive or gemstone

Examples

Industrial cutting tools often use corundom for its excellent hardness.
Ruby and sapphire are both forms of corundom with traces of different elements.
The abrasive powder made from corundom is essential in polishing optical lenses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aluminum oxide - chemical compound forming the basis of corundom

Etymology

From Tamil 'kurundam' via Sanskrit 'kuruvinda' (ruby).
