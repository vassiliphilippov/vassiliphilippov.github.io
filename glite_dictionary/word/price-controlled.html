price-controlled

1. ADJ

/ ˈpraɪs kənˌtroʊld / (AmE), / ˈpraɪs kənˌtrəʊld / (BrE)
(of a product or service) having its price regulated or controlled by a government authority.
regulated by government pricing

Examples

Many essential medicines are price-controlled to prevent exploitation of consumers.
The government introduced price-controlled housing to make it affordable for low-income families.
During periods of inflation, some food items may become price-controlled by law.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unregulated - not controlled or supervised by regulations or laws
