posse

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːsi / (AmE), / ˈpɒsi / (BrE)
A group of people summoned by a sheriff to help in maintaining law and order, often temporarily; a group acting as an unofficial police force.
temporary group acting as police

Examples

The sheriff rounded up a posse to track down the fugitives.
In old Western movies, a posse would ride out after outlaws.
The town quickly formed a posse when the bank was robbed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: militia - a military force raised from the population to supplement a regular army; often temporary or emergency service

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'posse comitatus' (power/authority of the county).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːsi / (AmE), / ˈpɒsi / (BrE)
a group of friends or close associates; a group of people who support or accompany someone
group of friends or associates
informal

Examples

Every Friday night, Jake goes out with his posse to the local bar.
She always travels with her posse of loyal friends.
The posse cheered loudly from the front row during the concert.

Synset Examples

The posse gathered at the old barn for a reunion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crew - a group of people working closely together
  • Synonym: gang - a group of people who associate together (sometimes with negative connotations)

Etymology

Originally from Medieval Latin 'posse comitatus' (power of the county), later popularized in American English and broadened in meaning.
