witchcraft

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪtʃˌkræft / (AmE), / ˈwɪtʃˌkrɑːft / (BrE)
the practice of magical skills, spells, and abilities, often associated with the use of supernatural powers
the practice of magic or sorcery

Examples

Many people in the village feared accusations of witchcraft during the seventeenth century.
She studied ancient books to learn the secrets of witchcraft and magical rituals.
Some cultures have different attitudes towards witchcraft, viewing it as either harmful or healing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorcery - the use of magical powers, especially evil ones
  • Synonym: magic - the use of supernatural powers to influence events

Etymology

From Old English 'wiccecræft', with 'wicce' meaning 'witch' and 'cræft' meaning 'craft, skill'.
