Charles Sanders Peirce

1. PROPN

/ ˈpɪərs / (AmE), / ˈpɜːs / (BrE)
(1839–1914) an American philosopher and logician, recognized as a pioneer of pragmatism.
American philosopher and logician

Examples

Many scholars consider Charles Sanders Peirce the founder of the modern philosophy of pragmatism.
The semiotic theory developed by Charles Sanders Peirce has influenced linguistics and communication studies.
Courses on logic often discuss the contributions of Charles Sanders Peirce to logical reasoning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pragmatism - American philosophical movement emphasizing practical consequences and function of beliefs
