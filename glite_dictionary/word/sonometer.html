sonometer

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːnəˌmiːtər / (AmE), / ˈsɒnəˌmiːtə / (BrE)
an instrument for measuring the sensitivity or acuity of hearing
device for measuring hearing sensitivity
medical

Examples

The audiologist used a sonometer to assess the patient's hearing threshold.
New advances in sonometer technology have improved the accuracy of hearing tests.
After the accident, doctors examined his hearing using a sonometer during his evaluation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: audiometer - an instrument for measuring hearing acuity

Etymology

From Latin 'sonus' (sound) + Greek 'metron' (measure).
