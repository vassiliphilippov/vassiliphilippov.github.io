Everglades

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛvərˌɡleɪdz / (AmE), / ˈɛvəˌɡleɪdz / (BrE)
a vast subtropical wetland region in southern Florida, known for its diverse wildlife and unique ecosystem
wetland region in southern Florida

Examples

We spent a day exploring the Everglades and spotted alligators and rare birds.
The Everglades are a UNESCO World Heritage Site due to their unique biodiversity.
Visitors must take precautions when hiking through the Everglades because of the wetlands and wildlife.

Etymology

From 'ever' and 'glade', first used in the mid-19th century to describe the region's appearance as endless open marsh.
