tranquility

1. NOUN

/ træŋˈkwɪləti / (AmE), / træŋˈkwɪlɪti / (BrE)
A state of calm and peacefulness, free from disturbance or agitation.
calm and peacefulness

Examples

After a busy day at work, she enjoyed the tranquility of her garden in the evening.
The tranquility of the mountain lake was breathtaking, with only the sound of birds to be heard.
He meditated every morning to find a sense of inner tranquility before starting his day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serenity - the state of being calm, peaceful, and untroubled
  • Antonym: agitation - a state of nervousness or restlessness

Etymology

From Latin 'tranquillitas', from 'tranquillus' (quiet, calm).
