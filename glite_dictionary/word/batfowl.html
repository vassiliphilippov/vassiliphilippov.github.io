to batfowl

1. VERB

/ ˈbætˌfaʊl / (AmE), / ˈbæt.faʊl / (BrE)
to catch birds by temporarily dazzling or blinding them with light, usually at night
catch birds by dazzling them with light
archaic

Examples

Farmers would batfowl in autumn when birds gathered in large flocks at dusk.
He batfowled along the riverbank, using lanterns to surprise sleeping birds.
In olden days, villagers sometimes batfowled to provide food for winter.

Etymology

From 'bat' (to hit or beat) and 'fowl' (bird), referring to the method of capturing birds.
