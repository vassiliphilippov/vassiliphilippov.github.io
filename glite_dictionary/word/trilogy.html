trilogy

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪlədʒi / (AmE), / ˈtrɪlədʒi / (BrE)
A set of three related literary or dramatic works, especially those forming a connected narrative or sharing a common theme.
set of three related works

Examples

The author recently completed the final book of her fantasy trilogy.
The movie studio announced plans to turn the popular novel series into a trilogy.
Many fans consider the second film in the trilogy to be the strongest installment.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: duology - a set of two related works
  • Degree Higher: tetralogy - a set of four related works

Etymology

From Greek 'trilogia', from 'tri-' (three) + 'logos' (story, discourse).
