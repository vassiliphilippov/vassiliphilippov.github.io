iambus

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈæmbəs / (AmE), / aɪˈæmbəs / (BrE)
A metrical foot in poetry consisting of an unstressed syllable followed by a stressed syllable.
metrical foot (unstressed-stressed) in poetry

Examples

Shakespeare frequently wrote in iambus, giving his lines a natural rhythm.
The line 'to be or not to be' is an example of the iambus used in English poetry.
Understanding the iambus helps students analyze the structure of many classic sonnets.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: trochee - a metrical foot consisting of a stressed syllable followed by an unstressed syllable

Etymology

From Latin 'iambus', from Greek 'ἴαμβος' (iambos, a kind of metrical foot).
