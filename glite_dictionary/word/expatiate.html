to expatiate

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈspeɪʃiˌeɪt / (AmE), / ɪkˈspeɪʃɪeɪt / (BrE)
to speak or write at length or in detail about a subject; elaborate
discuss at length; elaborate

Examples

The professor began to expatiate on the theory, citing examples from recent studies.
In his autobiography, he expatiates on his travels across Africa.
She was eager to expatiate further, but time was limited during the meeting.

Synset Examples

She elaborated on the main ideas in her dissertation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elaborate - to give more detail or information about something
  • Antonym: to condense - to make something shorter or more concise

Etymology

From Latin expatiari (to wander or digress), from ex- (out) + spatiari (to walk, stroll).
