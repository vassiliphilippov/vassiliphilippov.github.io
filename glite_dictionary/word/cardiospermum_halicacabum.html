Cardiospermum halicacabum

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːrdioʊˈspɜːrməm ˌhælɪkəˈkæbəm / (AmE), / ˌkɑːdɪəʊˈspɜːməm ˌhælɪkəˈkæbəm / (BrE)
A tropical woody perennial climbing plant with large ornamental, balloon-like seed pods, found in India, Africa, and America.
woody climbing plant with balloon-like seed pods

Examples

The seeds of Cardiospermum halicacabum are often used in traditional medicine across Asia.
Gardeners admire Cardiospermum halicacabum for its unique balloon-shaped pods and quick-growing vines.
During the summer, Cardiospermum halicacabum can quickly cover fences and trellises with its delicate foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: balloon vine - Common name for Cardiospermum halicacabum, a plant with balloon-like seed pods

Etymology

From Greek *kardia* (heart) + *sperma* (seed) and Latin *halicacabum* (balloon-vine).
