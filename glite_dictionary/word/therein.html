therein

1. ADV

/ ðɛrˈɪn / (AmE), / ðeərˈɪn / (BrE)
in or into that place or thing; in the place or thing just mentioned.
in that place or thing
archaic legal

Examples

The contract states the obligations of the parties therein in detail.
Ancient texts contain references to rituals practiced therein.
All relevant information can be found therein.

Synset Examples

they can read therein what our plans are

Related Terms

  • Antonym: herein - in this place or document
  • Synonym: thereof - of the thing just mentioned
  • Synonym: thereon - on that thing

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old English 'þǣrinne', equivalent to 'there' + 'in'.
