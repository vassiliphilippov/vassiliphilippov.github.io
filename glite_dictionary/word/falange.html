Falange

1. NOUN

/ fəˈlɑːndʒeɪ / (AmE), / fəˈlændʒeɪ / (BrE)
A Spanish fascist political party founded in 1933 by José Antonio Primo de Rivera, associated with the regime of Francisco Franco.
Spanish fascist party under Franco

Examples

The Falange played a significant role in supporting Franco during the Spanish Civil War.
Membership in the Falange was often required for holding certain government positions under Franco's regime.
Historians debate the true influence of the Falange in shaping Spanish policy during the dictatorship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fascism - an authoritarian and nationalistic right-wing system of government and social organization
  • Synonym: Francoism - the political regime of Francisco Franco in Spain

Etymology

From Spanish 'Falange', meaning 'phalanx', borrowed from Greek 'phalanx' (battle line).
