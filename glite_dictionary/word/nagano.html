Nagano

1. PROPN

/ nɑˈɡɑːnoʊ / (AmE), / nəˈɡɑːnəʊ / (BrE)
A city in central Honshu, Japan, northwest of Tokyo, known for its Buddhist shrine and as the host of the 1998 Winter Olympics.
city in central Japan

Examples

The 1998 Winter Olympics were held in Nagano, drawing visitors from around the world.
Many tourists travel to Nagano each year to see the famous Zenko-ji Buddhist temple.
After leaving Tokyo, we took the bullet train to Nagano for a weekend in the mountains.

Etymology

Japanese, from the local place name Nagano (長野) meaning 'long field'.
