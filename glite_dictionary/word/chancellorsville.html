Chancellorsville

1. PROPN

/ ˈtʃæn.sələrzˌvɪl / (AmE), / ˈtʃæn.sələzˌvɪl / (BrE)
a major battle of the American Civil War in 1863, where Confederate forces under Robert E. Lee defeated Union forces led by Joseph Hooker
Civil War battle site in 1863, Virginia

Examples

The Battle of Chancellorsville is considered one of Robert E. Lee’s greatest victories.
Many historians study the tactics used at Chancellorsville to understand Civil War strategy.
After Chancellorsville, the Confederate army gained confidence despite heavy losses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gettysburg - a major Civil War battle in Pennsylvania, 1863
  • Synonym: Antietam - a significant Civil War battle in Maryland, 1862

Etymology

Named after the Chancellorsville area in Virginia, United States.
