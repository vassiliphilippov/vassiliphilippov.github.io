historicism

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈstɔr.ɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / hɪˈstɒr.ɪˌsɪzəm / (BrE)
the theory that social and cultural events are determined by historical forces or context
theory that events are determined by history

Examples

The concept of historicism suggests that cultural norms arise from specific historical circumstances.
Her analysis of the novel was deeply rooted in historicism, examining how history shaped the characters' behaviors.
Critics argue that historicism can sometimes overshadow individual agency in interpreting social change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: determinism - the doctrine that all events are determined by causes external to the will
  • Antonym: ahistoricism - lack of concern for history or historical development

Etymology

From German 'Historismus', from 'Historie' (history) + '-ismus' (-ism).
