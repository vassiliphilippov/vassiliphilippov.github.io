to homogenise

1. VERB

/ həˈmɑdʒəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / həˈmɒdʒənaɪz / (BrE)
to break up the fat globules of a substance (especially milk) so that they are evenly distributed and do not separate
make fat spread evenly (esp. in milk)

Examples

Most milk is homogenised to prevent the cream from separating and floating to the top.
Modern dairies homogenise milk as part of the standard processing procedure.
The company homogenises its milk to ensure a consistent texture and taste.

Synset Examples

homogenized milk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to emulsify - to mix liquids so they become a single substance

Etymology

From Greek 'homogenēs' (of the same kind), via French 'homogénéiser'.

make uniform or similar by mixing
british

Examples

Milk is often homogenised to prevent the cream from separating.
Several cultures can homogenise over time if there is enough interaction.

3. VERB

/ həˈmɑdʒəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / hɒˈmɒdʒɪnaɪz / (BrE)
to make things uniform or similar in composition by mixing or blending thoroughly
make uniform by mixing

Examples

Before bottling, the milk is homogenised to prevent the cream from separating.
The new policy seeks to homogenise the curriculum across all schools in the district.
To achieve a smooth texture, thoroughly homogenise the sauce ingredients in a blender.

Synset Examples

homogenize the main ingredients

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to standardize - make consistent or uniform according to a standard
  • Antonym: to diversify - make diverse or varied

Etymology

From French homogénéiser, from homogène (homogeneous), originally from Greek homos (same) + genos (kind).
