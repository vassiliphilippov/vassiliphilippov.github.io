microbenthos

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪkroʊˈbɛnθoʊs / (AmE), / ˌmʌɪkrəʊˈbɛnθɒs / (BrE)
Microscopic organisms that live on or in the bottom sediments of water bodies, typically less than 0.1 mm in size.
microscopic benthic organisms

Examples

Scientists often study microbenthos to assess the health of aquatic ecosystems.
Algae and protozoa are common members of the microbenthos community.
The abundance of microbenthos can be affected by pollution in river beds.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: meiobenthos - organisms living in benthic environments that are slightly larger than microbenthos
  • Degree Higher: macrobenthos - larger benthic organisms visible to the naked eye

Etymology

From micro- (small) and benthos (from Greek, meaning depth of the sea or the organisms living there).
