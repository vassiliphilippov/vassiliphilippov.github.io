Tinca tinca

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɪŋkə ˈtɪŋkə / (AmE), / ˌtɪŋkə ˈtɪŋkə / (BrE)
a species of freshwater fish, commonly known as tench, found in Europe and western Asia and known for its resilience out of water
a species of freshwater fish (tench)

Examples

Tinca tinca is prized by anglers for its hardiness and the challenge it presents when caught.
The pond was stocked with several specimens of Tinca tinca for a local fishing competition.
Tinca tinca can tolerate low oxygen environments better than many other freshwater fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tench - common name for Tinca tinca, a freshwater fish

Etymology

Modern Latin binomial name, used in taxonomy.
