to provide

1. VERB

/ prəˈvaɪd / (AmE), / prəˈvʌɪd / (BrE)
to earn the money or resources needed to support oneself or others; to supply the necessities for living
earn a living; supply means of support

Examples

As the sole breadwinner, he provides for his family by working long hours.
Many parents work hard to provide for their children's education and well-being.
Despite challenges, she managed to provide for herself after moving to a new city.

Synset Examples

He provides for his large family by working three jobs
Women nowadays not only take care of the household but also bring home the bacon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: support - to give financial or other help to a person
  • Synonym: to maintain - to provide the necessities for someone or something

Etymology

From Latin providēre (to foresee, provide), from pro- (ahead) + vidēre (to see).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02224224-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ prəˈvaɪd / (AmE), / prəˈvaɪd / (BrE)
to give or supply something that is needed or useful to someone or something
give or supply something needed

Examples

The hotel will provide towels and toiletries for your stay.
Can you provide more information about the event?
The charity provides food to families in need.

Synset Examples

We provided the room with an electrical heater
The conference center offers a health spa
The hotel offers private meeting rooms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supply - to provide someone or something with what is needed or wanted
  • Synonym: offer - to present or give something for someone to accept or reject
  • Antonym: to withhold - to refuse to give something that is wanted or needed

Etymology

From Latin 'providere' (to foresee, supply), from 'pro-' (before) + 'videre' (to see).

5. VERB

/ prəˈvaɪd / (AmE), / prəˈvaɪd / (BrE)
to specify what is to happen in certain contingencies, especially in a legal document by stating a condition or stipulation
stipulate formally, often in a legal document
legal

Examples

The contract provides that either party may terminate with thirty days' notice.
Her will provides for the property to go to charity in the event of her death.
The agreement provides that disputes shall be resolved through arbitration.

Synset Examples

The will provides that each child should receive half of the money
The Constitution provides for the right to free speech

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stipulate - to demand or specify as part of an agreement
  • Synonym: to specify - to state clearly and explicitly

Etymology

From Latin providere (to foresee, provide for).
