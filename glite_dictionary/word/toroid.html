toroid

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔːrɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈtɔːrɔɪd / (BrE)
a surface or solid shaped like a ring, especially one formed by rotating a circle around an axis outside the circle
ring-shaped surface or solid

Examples

The magnetic field is confined within the toroid in this experiment.
A toroid can be visualized as a doughnut-shaped surface of revolution.
Engineers often use toroid cores in transformers to reduce energy loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torus - a doughnut-shaped solid formed by rotating a circle about an axis in its plane

Etymology

From Greek 'toros' (something rounded, bulge) + '-oid' (form, like).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɔːrɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈtɔːrɔɪd / (BrE)
a surface or solid having the shape of a doughnut, formed by rotating a circle around an axis that does not intersect the circle
doughnut-shaped surface or body

Examples

The magnetic field inside a toroid is uniform along its circular path.
Engineers designed a toroid to confine the plasma in the fusion reactor.
To illustrate his point, the professor drew a large toroid on the whiteboard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torus - a surface or solid formed by revolving a circle in three-dimensional space

Etymology

From Greek 'toros' (a bulge, protuberance) + -oid (resembling or like).
