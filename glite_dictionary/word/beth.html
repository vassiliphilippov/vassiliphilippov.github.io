beth

1. NOUN

/ bɛθ / (AmE), / bɛθ / (BrE)
the second letter of the Hebrew alphabet, represented as ב
the second Hebrew letter

Examples

The Hebrew word for house begins with the letter beth.
In Hebrew class, we learned that beth comes after aleph.
Each verse of the acrostic poem starts with a new Hebrew letter, such as beth.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: aleph - the first letter of the Hebrew alphabet
  • Degree Higher: gimel - the third letter of the Hebrew alphabet

Etymology

From Hebrew 'bet' (בֵּית), meaning 'house'.
