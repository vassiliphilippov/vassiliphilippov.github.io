oersted

1. NOUN

/ ˈɜrˌstɛd / (AmE), / ˈɜːstɛd / (BrE)
a unit of magnetic field strength in the CGS system, equal to the intensity of a magnetic field one centimeter from a unit magnetic pole
unit of magnetic field strength (CGS)

Examples

The strength of the magnet was measured in oersteds.
A field intensity of one oersted is considered the standard for this experiment.
Modern textbooks seldom use oersted in favor of the SI unit ampere per meter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gauss - unit of magnetic induction in the CGS system

Etymology

Named after Danish physicist Hans Christian Ørsted (1777–1851).
