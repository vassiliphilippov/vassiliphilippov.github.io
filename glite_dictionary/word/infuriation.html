infuriation

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌfjʊriˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌfjʊəriˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
A feeling of intense anger and impatience.
intense anger; rage

Examples

Her repeated lateness was a source of great infuriation to her manager.
He could barely hide his infuriation when the proposal was rejected without any explanation.
The delays at the airport led to widespread infuriation among the passengers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rage - violent, uncontrollable anger
  • Synonym: anger - a strong feeling of displeasure or hostility
  • Antonym: calm - a state of peace or tranquility

Etymology

From the verb 'infuriate' (to make furious), from Latin 'furia' (fury).
