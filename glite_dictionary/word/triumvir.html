triumvir

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈʌm.vɚ / (AmE), / traɪˈʌm.vɪə / (BrE)
one of a group of three people holding public office or exercising authority, especially in ancient Rome.
one of three officials (esp. in ancient Rome)

Examples

Julius Caesar served as a triumvir during the First Triumvirate in Rome.
The triumvir worked closely with his two colleagues to govern the territory.
Each triumvir had equal authority in the administration of ancient Rome.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: triumvirate - a group of three people holding power, especially in ancient Rome

Etymology

From Latin trīmvir, from trēs (three) + vir (man).
