termination

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɚməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtɜːməˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the end part of a word, such as a suffix, inflectional ending, or final morpheme
the ending of a word in linguistics

Examples

The word 'happiness' has the termination '-ness'.
Inflectional termination in Latin often indicates case and number.
Changing the termination of a word can alter its grammatical function.

Synset Examples

I don't like words that have ‘-ism’ as an ending

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suffix - an affix added to the end of a word

Etymology

From Latin terminatio (a bounding, setting limits), from terminare (to limit, end).

2. NOUN

/ tɝː.məˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / tɜː.mɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the final point or boundary at which something ends; the end or extremity of an object or process
the end point of something

Examples

The termination of the railway line is at the city’s western edge.
At the termination of the tunnel, sunlight streamed in and revealed the exit.
Each nerve fiber ends at a synaptic termination in the muscle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: end - the final point of something in time or space
  • Antonym: beginning - the point in time or space at which something starts

Etymology

From Latin terminatio (a bounding, a setting of limits), from terminare (to end, to limit).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˌtɜːrmɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtɜːmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of ending something, such as a contract, period, or agreement; the coming to an end of an arrangement or period
the ending of something, often a contract or period

Examples

The termination of his employment contract was effective as of June 30.
We received a notice regarding the termination of our lease agreement.
Upon termination of the insurance policy, coverage will no longer be provided.

Synset Examples

the expiry of his driver's license

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expiry - the end of the period for which something is valid
  • Synonym: expiration - the end of a fixed period, especially the valid period of a document
  • Antonym: commencement - the beginning, as opposed to the end, of something

Etymology

From Latin terminatio (a boundary, limit, end), from terminare (to limit, end).

Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ ˌtɜːrmɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtɜːmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of bringing something to an end or the state of being ended
the act of ending something

Examples

The sudden termination of the project surprised the entire team.
Her employment contract specified the grounds for termination.
Following the termination of the agreement, both parties moved on to new ventures.

Synset Examples

the termination of the agreement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: end - the point or act of stopping; a conclusion
  • Synonym: cessation - the process of ending or being brought to an end
  • Antonym: initiation - the act of beginning something

Etymology

From Latin terminatio, terminationis (a bounding, a limiting, ending), from terminare (to end), from terminus (boundary, end).
