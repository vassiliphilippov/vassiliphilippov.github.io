Linanthus

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈnænθəs / (AmE), / lɪˈnanθəs / (BrE)
A genus of herbs in the family Polemoniaceae, native to western North America.
genus of western North American herbs

Examples

Several species within the Linanthus genus produce delicate, star-shaped flowers.
The California desert is home to a variety of Linanthus species.
Botanists are studying the pollination patterns of Linanthus in their native habitats.

Etymology

From Greek 'linos' (flax) + 'anthos' (flower).
