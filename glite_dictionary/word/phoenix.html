phoenix

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiː.nɪks / (AmE), / ˈfiː.nɪks / (BrE)
a mythical bird that is said to burn itself to death and be reborn from its own ashes, symbolizing renewal or immortality
mythical bird that is reborn from ashes

Examples

The legend says the phoenix rises from its own ashes every five hundred years.
After the disaster, the city rebuilt itself like a phoenix rising anew.
Her spirit was compared to a phoenix, always able to recover from setbacks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firebird - a mythical bird associated with fire and renewal in various folklore

Etymology

From Latin 'phoenix', from Ancient Greek 'phoinix' (φοῖνιξ), referring to the legendary bird.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfiː.nɪks / (AmE), / ˈfiː.nɪks / (BrE)
a genus of tropical Asian and African palm trees with feathery, pinnate leaves, including the date palm
genus of palm trees (Phoenix)

Examples

The phoenix genus includes the familiar date palm, widely grown for its sweet fruit.
In the botanical garden, visitors admired several species of phoenix palms from Africa and Asia.
Farmers in arid regions often cultivate phoenix palms for both shade and economic value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: date palm - a species (Phoenix dactylifera) within the Phoenix genus, cultivated for its edible fruit

Etymology

From Latin *phoenix*, from Greek *phoinix*, referring also to the date palm.
