Charina bottae

1. NOUN

/ kəˈraɪnə ˈboʊti / (AmE), / kəˈraɪnə ˈbɒtaɪ / (BrE)
A small, non-venomous boa species native to western North America, known for its smooth, rubbery appearance and similar-looking head and tail.
species of non-venomous boa native to western North America

Examples

The Charina bottae is often mistaken for a worm due to its smooth, blunt body.
Researchers found the elusive Charina bottae hiding under a log in the forest.
Herpetologists value the Charina bottae for its calm temperament and unique appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rubber boa - common name for Charina bottae, a small western North American snake

Etymology

Named from Latinized form of the surnames of the Italian naturalists Paolo Botta and Charles Lucien Bonaparte (who named many species).
