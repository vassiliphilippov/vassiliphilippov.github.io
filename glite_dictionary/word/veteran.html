veteran

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛt̬.ɚ.ən / (AmE), / ˈvɛt.ər.ən / (BrE)
A person who has served in the armed forces, especially one who has seen active service in war.
experienced military servicemember

Examples

The parade honored every veteran who had served in the conflict.
My grandfather is a veteran of World War II.
The company hired a veteran to train the younger recruits.

Synset Examples

the veterans laughed at the new recruits

Related Terms

  • Antonym: novice - someone who is new to a job or activity

Etymology

From Latin veteranus, from vetus (old).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɛtərən / (AmE), / ˈvɛtərən / (BrE)
a person who has served in the armed forces, especially one who has fought in a war
former member of the armed forces

Examples

As a veteran of the navy, she often shares stories from her years at sea.
The government offers various benefits to every veteran returning from service.
At the ceremony, each veteran was honored for their dedication and sacrifice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ex-serviceman - a person who has previously served in the armed forces
  • Antonym: civilian - a person not serving in the armed forces

Etymology

From Latin veteranus (mature, old, experienced), from vetus (old).

3. NOUN

/ ˈvɛt̬.ɚ.ən / (AmE), / ˈvɛt.ər.ən / (BrE)
a person with long experience in a particular activity or field, especially one who has served in the armed forces
an experienced or long-serving person

Examples

The retired general is a veteran of many campaigns.
As a veteran teacher, she is respected by her colleagues for her deep experience.
The job requires a veteran in project management to handle its complexity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: novice - a person new to a field or activity; a beginner
  • Synonym: expert - a person who is highly skilled or knowledgeable in a particular area

Etymology

From Latin 'veteranus' (old, experienced), from 'vetus' (old).

4. ADJ

/ ˈvɛt̬.ɚ.ən / (AmE), / ˈvɛt.ər.ən / (BrE)
Having long experience or practice in a particular activity and therefore competent or skilled.
experienced; skilled through practice

Examples

After years working in the field, she became a veteran journalist respected by all her peers.
The company hired a veteran engineer to oversee the complex project.
Despite his young age, his veteran leadership was evident on the basketball court.

Synset Examples

a seasoned traveler
veteran steadiness
a veteran officer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seasoned - Having a lot of experience of doing something and therefore knowing how to do it well
  • Antonym: inexperienced - Lacking experience or skill

Etymology

From Latin veteranus (old, experienced), from vetus (old).
