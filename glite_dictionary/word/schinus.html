Schinus

1. NOUN

/ ˈskaɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈskɪnəs / (BrE)
a genus of evergreen shrubs and trees native to tropical and subtropical regions of South and Central America, the Canary Islands, and China
genus of evergreen shrubs and trees

Examples

The pink peppercorns commonly used in cooking are actually the dried fruits of Schinus trees.
Several species of Schinus have become invasive in areas outside their native range.
Botanists recently identified a new member of the Schinus genus in the subtropical forest.

Etymology

From Greek 'schinos' (mastic tree).

2. NOUN

/ ˈskiːnəs / (AmE), / ˈskiːnəs / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen shrubs and trees native to tropical and subtropical regions of South and Central America, the Canary Islands, and China, known for species such as the Peruvian pepper tree.
genus of evergreen shrubs and trees

Examples

The Schinus genus includes ornamental trees like the Brazilian pepper.
Some species of Schinus produce berries commonly called pink peppercorns.
Botanists studied the distribution of Schinus across different continents.

Etymology

From Greek skhinos (mastic tree).
