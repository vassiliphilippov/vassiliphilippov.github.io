molto

1. ADV

/ ˈmoʊltoʊ / (AmE), / ˈmɒltəʊ / (BrE)
(in musical directions) very; much—used to modify tempo or dynamics, e.g. 'allegro molto' (very fast).
(music) very; much, as in tempo/dynamics

Examples

Play the passage molto legato for a smoother effect.
The composer marked the section as allegro molto to indicate great energy.
The dynamic should increase to forte molto in this measure.

Synset Examples

allegro molto

Related Terms

  • Synonym: molto allegro - very fast (in music)
  • Antonym: poco - a little; used to modify tempo or dynamics in music

Etymology

From Italian 'molto' meaning 'much' or 'very'.
