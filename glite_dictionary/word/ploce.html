ploce

1. NOUN

/ ˈploʊ.si / (AmE), / ˈpləʊ.si / (BrE)
The repetition of a word or phrase in a sentence, usually to give special emphasis or to extend its meaning, especially as a rhetorical device.
repetition for emphasis (rhetoric)

Examples

The orator skillfully used ploce to emphasize his main argument in the speech.
In poetry, ploce can add rhythm and highlight important themes.
'I am that I am' is a classic example of ploce in rhetoric.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repetition - the act of repeating something for emphasis or effect

Etymology

From Greek *plokē* (complication, weaving) via Latin *plocē*.
