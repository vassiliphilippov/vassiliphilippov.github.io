belly

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛli / (AmE), / ˈbɛli / (BrE)
the underside or lower part of the body of certain vertebrates, such as snakes or fish
underside of animal's body

Examples

The snake slid along the ground on its belly.
The belly of the fish is usually lighter in color than its back.
She noticed the pattern of scales on the lizard's belly.

Etymology

From Old English 'belig' meaning 'bag, pouch'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɛli / (AmE), / ˈbɛli / (BrE)
a part of something that bulges outward or is rounded, especially the central part of an object such as a sail or a pipe
bulging or rounded part of something

Examples

The belly of the sail caught the wind and pushed the boat forward.
Engineers reinforced the belly of the pipeline to withstand higher pressure.
The artist painted the belly of the vase with a decorative pattern.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bulge - a rounded swelling or protuberance

3. NOUN

/ ˈbɛl.i / (AmE), / ˈbɛl.i / (BrE)
the part of the body between the chest and the pelvis, containing the stomach and other internal organs; the abdomen.
front part of the body below the chest

Examples

She placed her hand gently on her belly, feeling the baby kick.
After eating too much, his belly felt uncomfortably full.
He lay on his belly to watch the clouds in the sky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abdomen - the part of the body containing the digestive organs; the belly

Etymology

Old English belig (bag, pouch) related to the notion of a swollen or rounded body part.

4. NOUN

/ ˈbɛli / (AmE), / ˈbɛli / (BrE)
the hollow or central part inside something, especially a large structure such as a ship or an aircraft

Examples

The whale's huge belly held more than a ton of fish.

5. VERB

/ ˈbɛli / (AmE), / ˈbɛli / (BrE)
(of an object or structure) to swell outward or bulge, typically in response to pressure or force.
to bulge or swell outward

Examples

The sails bellied with the strong gusts of wind.
Over time, the fence bellied outward under the weight of the snow.
The tent’s sides belly in the breeze, making it look larger than it is.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bulge - to swell or protrude in a rounded shape

Etymology

From Old English *belig* (bag, bellows), related to the noun 'belly'.

6. NOUN

/ ˈbɛli / (AmE), / ˈbɛli / (BrE)
An abdomen that sticks out more than usual, often due to fat or bloating.
a protruding abdomen

Examples

He patted his belly after a large meal, satisfied and full.
Her cat has a round belly that wobbles when it walks.
The doctor examined the patient’s swollen belly for signs of discomfort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abdomen - the part of the body containing the digestive organs; the belly
  • Synonym: stomach - the front part of the body below the chest; often used as a synonym for belly

Etymology

Old English 'belig' (bag, pouch); related to Dutch 'buik', German 'Bauch'.
