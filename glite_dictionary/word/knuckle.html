knuckle

1. NOUN

/ ˈnʌkəl / (AmE), / ˈnʌk.l̩ / (BrE)
A joint of a finger, especially when the fist is closed, visible as a prominent bump.
joint of a finger

Examples

He cracked his knuckle loudly before starting the game.
Boxers often injure a knuckle during a tough fight.
She rubbed her sore knuckle after hitting it on the table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knuckle joint - the joint connecting two phalanges of a finger

Etymology

From Middle English 'knokel', diminutive of 'knoke' (bone, joint).

2. VERB

/ ˈnʌkəl / (AmE), / ˈnʌkəl / (BrE)
to press or rub something with the knuckles of the hand
press or rub with the knuckles

Examples

She knuckled the dough vigorously before letting it rise.
The artist knuckled the charcoal across the canvas to create texture.
He knuckled his tired eyes after hours of reading.
