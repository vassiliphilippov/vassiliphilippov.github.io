Irish

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ.rɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.rɪʃ / (BrE)
The people of Ireland or of Irish descent.
people of Ireland or Irish origin

Examples

Many Irish emigrated to the United States during the 19th century.
The Irish are known for their rich literary and musical traditions.
He takes pride in his Irish heritage.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: English - the people of England
  • Antonym: Scots - the people of Scotland

Etymology

From Old English *Īrisc*, from Īras (the Irish) + -isc (adjectival suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈaɪrɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈaɪərɪʃ / (BrE)
A type of whiskey made in Ireland, typically distilled from barley.
Irish whiskey made from barley

Examples

At the bar, he ordered a glass of Irish to enjoy after dinner.
Many connoisseurs believe that Irish has a smoother taste than Scotch whisky.
She bought a bottle of Irish as a gift for her friend who appreciates fine spirits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Scotch - whisky made in Scotland

Etymology

From the name of Ireland; used as a short form for Irish whiskey.

3. ADJ

/ ˈaɪ.rɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.rɪʃ / (BrE)
of or relating to Ireland or its people
relating to Ireland or its people

Examples

She loves to listen to traditional Irish music and dance.
The Irish countryside is renowned for its natural beauty.
Many Irish writers have made significant contributions to world literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Irishman - a man from Ireland
  • Synonym: Irishwoman - a woman from Ireland
  • Antonym: English - of or relating to England or its people

Etymology

From Old English *Īrisc* (of Ireland), from *Īras* (the Irish) + *-isc* (adjectival suffix).

4. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ.rɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.rɪʃ / (BrE)
the Celtic language that is native to Ireland, also known as Irish Gaelic
the Celtic language of Ireland

Examples

She is learning to speak Irish at her local language center.
Many schools in Ireland teach some classes through Irish.
He greeted us in Irish with a friendly smile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gaelic - the language family including Irish and Scottish Gaelic
  • Antonym: English - the language widely spoken in Ireland alongside Irish

Etymology

From Old English "Iras" (the Irish), from Old Norse "Írar", ultimately from Old Irish "Ériu" (Ireland).

Synset POS: n
