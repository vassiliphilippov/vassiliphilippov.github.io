to ratiocinate

1. VERB

/ ˌrætiˈɑːsɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˌrætiˈɒsɪneɪt / (BrE)
to reason in a methodical and logical way
reason logically and systematically

Examples

The philosopher tended to ratiocinate at length before reaching a conclusion.
Some people act on intuition, while others prefer to ratiocinate and analyze every decision.
In the debate, she tried to ratiocinate her arguments with meticulous logic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reason - think, understand, and form judgments logically

Etymology

From Latin ratiocinari (to reckon, calculate), from ratio (reason) + cinari (to make).
