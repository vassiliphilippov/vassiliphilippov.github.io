exactly

1. ADV

/ ɪɡˈzæktli / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzæktli / (BrE)
In a precise or accurate manner; without any error or deviation.
in a precise or accurate manner

Examples

He measured the distance exactly to avoid any mistakes.
Please repeat exactly what you heard in the meeting.
The twins look exactly alike from a distance.

Synset Examples

she always expressed herself precisely

Related Terms

  • Synonym: precisely - in a precise and clear way
  • Antonym: approximately - in a way that is not exact; nearly

Etymology

From exact + -ly, formed from the Latin root 'exactus' (completed, precise).

3. ADV

/ ɪɡˈzæk(t)li / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzæktli / (BrE)
in a manner that is completely correct or accurate; precisely.
in a precise or accurate way

Examples

She followed the instructions exactly as written and the recipe turned out perfectly.
The meeting started exactly at 9 a.m., not a minute late.
Can you tell me exactly what happened during the interview?

Synset Examples

he was doing precisely (or exactly) what she had told him to do
it was just as he said — the jewel was gone
it has just enough salt
Properly speaking, all true work is religion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: precisely - in a manner that is accurate or exact
  • Antonym: approximately - not exact, but close to the actual amount or number

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French *exacte*, from Latin *exactus* (past participle of *exigere*: 'to drive out, demand, execute').
