demise

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈmaɪz / (AmE), / dɪˈmaɪz / (BrE)
the end or failure of something, especially when referring to plans, institutions, or ideas
end or failure of something

Examples

The sudden demise of the project left everyone shocked and disappointed.
Many predicted the demise of the traditional newspaper in the digital age.
The company's demise was hastened by years of financial mismanagement.

Synset Examples

it was the death of all his plans
a dying of old hopes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: end - the conclusion or finishing point of something
  • Antonym: beginning - the moment at which something starts

Etymology

From Old French 'demis', past participle of 'demettre' (to dismiss), from Latin 'dimittere' (to send away).

2. VERB

/ dɪˈmaɪz / (AmE), / dɪˈmaɪz / (BrE)
(law) To transfer property by lease or by will.
transfer property by lease or will (legal)
legal

Examples

The landlord agreed to demise the premises to the tenant for a term of five years.
Under the will, the property was demised to his eldest son.
The company plans to demise several shop units through long-term leases.

Etymology

From Middle French *démettre* (to dismiss), from Latin *dimittere* (to send away).
