ocellus

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈsɛləs / (AmE), / əʊˈsɛləs / (BrE)
a marking on an animal that resembles an eye, typically a spot of color within a ring of another color, such as found on the wings of butterflies or the feathers of peacocks
eyelike marking (esp. in animals)

Examples

The butterfly's wings displayed a bright ocellus that resembled a real eye to scare off predators.
Each feather of the peacock features a distinctive blue and green ocellus.
Some fish have a black ocellus near their tails to confuse attackers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eyespot - an eyelike marking, especially in animals and plants

Etymology

From Latin *ocellus* (little eye), diminutive of *oculus* (eye).

2. NOUN

/ oʊˈsɛləs / (AmE), / əʊˈsɛləs / (BrE)
a simple eye with a single lens, found in many invertebrates such as insects and some jellyfish
simple eye with single lens

Examples

The honeybee has three small ocelli on top of its head in addition to its compound eyes.
Unlike the compound eye, the ocellus is primarily used to detect light intensity rather than form images.
Some jellyfish possess a single ocellus to help them sense changes in light.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: compound eye - an eye consisting of multiple facets, typical of many insects and crustaceans

Etymology

From Latin 'ocellus', diminutive of 'oculus' (eye).
