Afrasian

1. NOUN

/ ˌæ.friˈeɪ.ʒən / (AmE), / ˌæf.rəˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a family of related languages spoken in both Africa and Asia, also known as Afroasiatic
language family of Africa and Asia

Examples

Linguists have traced several Semitic languages back to the Afrasian family.
The Afrasian language group includes languages such as Arabic, Hebrew, and Amharic.
Debates about migration patterns in prehistory often involve speakers of Afrasian languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Afroasiatic - the same language family spoken in Africa and Western Asia

Etymology

From 'Afro-' (Africa) and '-Asian' (Asia) to indicate languages spoken on both continents.
