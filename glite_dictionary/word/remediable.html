remediable

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈmidiəbl / (AmE), / rɪˈmiːdiəbl / (BrE)
(of a problem or defect) capable of being remedied, corrected, or put right.
capable of being corrected or remedied

Examples

Fortunately, the error was remediable and quickly fixed by the technician.
Many environmental issues are not remediable unless action is taken early.
The teacher assured them that any gaps in their understanding were remediable with extra practice.

Synset Examples

remediable problems
a remediable setback
not a crime but only a remediable blunder

Related Terms

  • Antonym: irremediable - impossible to remedy or put right

Etymology

From Latin remediabilis (curable), from remedium (remedy).
