fastidiously

1. ADV

/ fæˈstɪdiəsli / (AmE), / fæˈstɪdiəsli / (BrE)
In a manner that shows great attention to detail or precision; with excessive care or delicacy.
with great attention to detail

Examples

She arranged the flowers fastidiously, making sure every stem was perfectly placed.
The manuscript was fastidiously edited before publication to avoid any errors.
He fastidiously cleaned his workshop at the end of each day.

Synset Examples

he writes extremely musical music, of which the sound is fastidiously calculated and yet agreeably spontaneous and imaginative

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meticulously - in a way that shows great attention to detail; very thoroughly
  • Antonym: carelessly - without care or attention; in a sloppy manner

Etymology

From 'fastidious', from Latin 'fastidiosus' (disdainful, squeamish); '-ly' turns the adjective into an adverb.

2. ADV

/ fæˈstɪdiəsli / (AmE), / fəˈstɪdiəsli / (BrE)
in a manner that shows great attention to detail and concern for accuracy or cleanliness
with great attention to detail

Examples

She fastidiously arranged the flowers in the vase until every petal was perfect.
The professor edited the manuscript fastidiously, leaving detailed notes on every page.
He fastidiously cleaned his workspace after finishing the project.

Synset Examples

painstakingly learn the routines of selling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meticulously - in a way that shows great attention to detail; very thoroughly
  • Antonym: carelessly - in a way that shows a lack of care or attention

Etymology

From Latin 'fastidiosus' (disdainful), via the adjective 'fastidious'.
