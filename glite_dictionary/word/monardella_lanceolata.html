Monardella lanceolata

1. NOUN

/ moʊˌnɑːrdɛlə ˌlænsioʊˈleɪtə / (AmE), / məˌnɑːdɛlə ˌlansiəˈleɪtə / (BrE)
a fragrant annual herb native to California, with lance-shaped leaves and clusters of rose-purple flowers
fragrant Californian herb with rose-purple flowers

Examples

The hillside was covered with blooming Monardella lanceolata in the early summer.
Botanists admire Monardella lanceolata for its aromatic leaves and vibrant flowers.
Gardeners often cultivate Monardella lanceolata for its fragrance and attractive purple blooms.

Etymology

From the genus name Monardella, honoring Spanish botanist Nicolás Monardes, and Latin lanceolata meaning 'lance-shaped.'
