2. NUM

/ ˌθɝːti ˈsɪks / (AmE), / ˌθɜːti ˈsɪks / (BrE)
the number 36; being six more than thirty
the number 36

Examples

She baked thirty-six cookies for the school fundraiser.
There are thirty-six students enrolled in the class this semester.
The package contains thirty-six colored pencils in different shades.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: thirty-five - the number 35; being one less than thirty-six
  • Degree Higher: thirty-seven - the number 37; being one more than thirty-six

Etymology

From 'thirty' and 'six,' combining the words for three tens and six.
