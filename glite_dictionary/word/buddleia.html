buddleia

1. NOUN

/ bʌˈdliə / (AmE), / ˈbʌd.li.ə / (BrE)
a tropical or subtropical shrub with clusters of white, violet, or yellow flowers, commonly grown as an ornamental plant
flowering ornamental shrub

Examples

The buddleia in her garden attracted dozens of butterflies each summer.
We planted a buddleia by the fence to add some color to the yard.
The purple blossoms of the buddleia brighten up the entire pathway.

Etymology

Modern Latin, named after the English botanist Adam Buddle (1662–1715).
