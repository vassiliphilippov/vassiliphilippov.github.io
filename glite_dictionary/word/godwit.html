godwit

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑdˌwɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡɒdˌwɪt / (BrE)
a large wading bird with a long, slightly upturned bill, resembling a curlew, found in wetlands of Europe, Asia, and North America
large wading bird with long bill

Examples

The godwit migrates thousands of miles every year between its breeding and wintering grounds.
We spotted a flock of godwits feeding along the muddy estuary at dawn.
The long, slightly upturned bill of the godwit helps it probe for food in shallow water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curlew - a large wading bird with a long, slender, downcurved bill

Etymology

Of uncertain origin, possibly from Middle Dutch *godwit*.
