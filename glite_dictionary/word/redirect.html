to redirect

1. VERB

/ ˌriːdəˈrɛkt, ˌriːdaɪˈrɛkt / (AmE), / ˌriːdɪˈrɛkt, ˌriːdaɪˈrɛkt / (BrE)
to direct something or someone to a different place, purpose, or focus
to direct to a different place or focus

Examples

The teacher tried to redirect the children's attention back to the lesson.
After the road was blocked, we had to redirect our route through the back streets.
She decided to redirect her efforts toward a more rewarding career.

Synset Examples

redirect your attention to the danger from the fundamentalists

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to divert - to change the direction or use of something
  • Antonym: focus - to direct attention or effort toward something

Etymology

From re- (again) + direct (from Latin dirigere, 'set straight').
