wayfaring

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪˌfɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈweɪˌfeərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of traveling, especially on foot, from place to place
traveling from place to place, esp. on foot

Examples

They spent the summer wayfaring across the countryside, exploring small villages and hidden trails.
Old legends often tell of wayfaring knights seeking adventure far from home.
His love for wayfaring led him on many journeys by foot through remote landscapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: traveling - the act of going from one place to another

Etymology

From Middle English 'wayfarere' (traveler), from 'way' (road, path) + 'fare' (to travel).

2. ADJ

/ ˈweɪˌfɛr.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈweɪˌfeə.rɪŋ / (BrE)
traveling, especially by walking from place to place
traveling, especially on foot

Examples

The wayfaring monk wandered from village to village, sharing his wisdom along the road.
During her youth, she lived a wayfaring life, exploring distant lands on foot.
They welcomed the wayfaring travelers who arrived at their cottage, offering them food and shelter.

Synset Examples

peripatetic country preachers
a poor wayfaring stranger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peripatetic - traveling from place to place, especially for work
  • Synonym: nomadic - moving from place to place without a fixed home

Etymology

From Middle English wayfaring, from 'way' (a road) + 'faring' (journeying, traveling).
