Sarcobatus

1. NOUN

/ sɑrˈkɑbətəs / (AmE), / sɑːˈkəʊbətəs / (BrE)
A small genus of North American shrubs in the family Sarcobataceae, best known for the species greasewood.
genus of North American shrubs, including greasewood

Examples

Sarcobatus is commonly found in arid regions of western North America.
The most well-known species within Sarcobatus is greasewood, used to stabilize soil.
Botanists study Sarcobatus for its adaptation to saline and alkaline soils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greasewood - a North American shrub in the genus Sarcobatus

Etymology

From Greek 'sarkos' (flesh) and 'batos' (bramble).

2. NOUN

/ sɑːrˈkoʊˌbeɪtəs / (AmE), / sɑːˈkɒbətəs / (BrE)
A small genus of shrubs native to western North America, known for the species greasewood.
a genus of North American shrubs (greasewood)

Examples

Sarcobatus is commonly found in alkaline soils across the Great Basin region.
The genus Sarcobatus includes the well-known greasewood shrub.
Botanists studied the adaptation of Sarcobatus to arid environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greasewood - a common shrub (Sarcobatus vermiculatus) in western North America

Etymology

From Greek sarkos (flesh) + batos (bramble), referring to the plant's appearance.
