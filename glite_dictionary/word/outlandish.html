outlandish

1. ADJ

/ aʊtˈlændɪʃ / (AmE), / aʊtˈlændɪʃ / (BrE)
strikingly unusual or unconventional; bizarre or unfamiliar to the point of being noticeable or strange
conspicuously unconventional or bizarre

Examples

Many found his outlandish ideas difficult to accept at first.
The performer wore an outlandish costume that captivated the audience.
She decorated her home with outlandish artwork from around the world.

Synset Examples

restaurants of bizarre design — one like a hat, another like a rabbit
famed for his eccentric spelling
a freakish combination of styles
his off-the-wall antics
the outlandish clothes of teenagers
outre and affected stage antics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bizarre - very strange or unusual
  • Antonym: conventional - following traditional forms or practices

Etymology

From Middle English 'outlandish' (foreign, strange), from 'outland' (foreign land) + '-ish'.
