Witold Gombrowicz

1. PROPN

/ ˈvi.toʊld ɡɔmˈbroʊ.vɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈvi.tɒld ɡɒmˈbrɒ.vɪtʃ / (BrE)
(1904–1969), Polish author known for his novels, plays, and essays characterized by their wit, psychological insight, and existential themes.
