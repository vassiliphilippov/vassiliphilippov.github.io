xanthomatosis

1. NOUN

/ zænˌθoʊməˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / zanˌθəʊməˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by the presence of multiple xanthomas, often associated with lipid metabolism disorders.
medical condition with xanthomas
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with xanthomatosis after yellowish nodules appeared on his elbows and knees.
Familial hypercholesterolemia can lead to xanthomatosis if not properly managed.
Treatment of xanthomatosis often focuses on controlling underlying lipid metabolism disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: xanthoma - a yellowish deposit of fat under the skin, often associated with high cholesterol

Etymology

From Greek xanthos (yellow) + -oma (tumor) + -osis (condition).
