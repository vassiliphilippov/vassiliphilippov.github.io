deftness

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛftnəs / (AmE), / ˈdɛftnəs / (BrE)
Skill and ease in performing tasks, especially with the hands; quick and clever ability.
skillful and quick performance

Examples

Her deftness with a paintbrush impressed the entire class.
The surgeon's deftness made the operation seem effortless to observers.
He handled the negotiation with remarkable deftness, securing a favorable outcome.

Synset Examples

his quick adeptness was a product of good design
he was famous for his facility as an archer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skill - ability to do something well
  • Antonym: clumsiness - lack of skill or grace

Etymology

From 'deft' + '-ness'; 'deft' from Old Norse *dǫftr* (fitting, apt).
