Cynodon dactylon

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈnoʊdɒn dækˈtaɪlɒn / (AmE), / sɪˈnəʊdən dækˈtɪlən / (BrE)
A species of grass, commonly known as Bermuda grass, native to Europe and now widely found in warm regions; often used for lawns and pastures.
type of grass; Bermuda grass

Examples

Cynodon dactylon is popular for golf courses because of its resilience to heavy foot traffic.
Farmers often plant Cynodon dactylon to create durable pastures for their livestock.
The spread of Cynodon dactylon has made it a common sight in warm climates around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bermuda grass - Common name for Cynodon dactylon, a type of grass used in lawns

Etymology

From Greek 'kynos' (dog) and 'odon' (tooth), referring to the shape of the grass; 'dactylon' from Greek 'daktylos' (finger).
