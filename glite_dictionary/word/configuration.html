configuration

1. NOUN

/ kənˌfɪɡjəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / kənˌfɪɡjʊˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the spatial arrangement or outline of parts or elements in a particular form
spatial arrangement or outline

Examples

The configuration of the furniture made the room feel much larger.
Scientists studied the molecular configuration to understand its unique properties.
From the top of the hill, the city's configuration looked like a sprawling puzzle.

Synset Examples

he could barely make out their shapes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shape - the external form or appearance of something
  • Synonym: arrangement - a way in which things are positioned or organized

Etymology

From Latin configuratio (a shaping), from configurare (to shape after).

2. NOUN

/ kənˌfɪɡ.jəˈreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / kənˌfɪɡ.jəˈreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the arrangement or pattern of parts or elements in something; the way in which components are organized or set up
arrangement of parts or elements

Examples

The new software allows you to customize the configuration of your workspace.
Engineers studied the wing configuration to improve aerodynamics.
Each network configuration has its own advantages and disadvantages.

Synset Examples

the outcome depends on the configuration of influences at the time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrangement - the way in which things are positioned or organized
  • Synonym: structure - the way in which parts of something are organized or arranged

Etymology

From Latin 'configuratio' (a shaping), from 'configurare' (to shape after), from 'con-' (together) + 'figurare' (to form, to shape).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05739724-n
Synset POS: n
