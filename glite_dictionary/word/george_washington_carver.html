George Washington Carver

1. PROPN

/ ˌdʒɔrdʒ ˈwɑʃɪŋtən ˈkɑrvər / (AmE), / ˌdʒɔːdʒ ˈwɒʃɪŋtən ˈkɑːvə / (BrE)
(1864–1943) An American botanist and agricultural chemist known for developing hundreds of products from peanuts, soybeans, and sweet potatoes.
American botanist and inventor (1864–1943)

Examples

George Washington Carver revolutionized Southern agriculture with his innovative work on crop rotation and soil improvement.
During Black History Month, schools often teach about George Washington Carver and his contributions to science.
Many scientific institutions in the United States bear the name of George Washington Carver in his honor.
