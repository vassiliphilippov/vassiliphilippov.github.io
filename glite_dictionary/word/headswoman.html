headswoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdzˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈhɛdzˌwʊmən / (BrE)
A female executioner who beheads people sentenced to death.
female executioner (beheading)

Examples

The notorious headswoman carried out the execution at dawn with unwavering precision.
As a headswoman, she was both feared and shunned by the townspeople.
Historical records mention a headswoman who served during the turbulent years of the revolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: headsman - an executioner who beheads the condemned

Etymology

Formed from 'head' + 'woman', paralleling 'headsman'.
