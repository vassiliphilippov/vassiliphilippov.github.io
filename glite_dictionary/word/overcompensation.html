overcompensation

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvərkəmˌpɛnˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəkəmˌpɛnˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
(in psychology) the process of attempting to make up for a perceived weakness or deficiency by excessively emphasizing its opposite.
exaggerated compensation for a weakness

Examples

His constant bragging about his wealth is a clear case of overcompensation for his insecurities.
Psychologists often observe overcompensation in people who feel inferior in certain areas of their lives.
Her strictness as a manager may be an overcompensation for feeling inexperienced in her role.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compensation - something given to make up for a loss, deficiency, or injury

Etymology

From over- (excessive) + compensation, first used in early 20th-century psychology.

2. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvɚˌkɑmpənˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəkɒmpənˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of compensating for a deficiency or weakness to an excessive degree, often by developing or emphasizing opposite traits or behaviors
excessive compensation for a weakness or fault

Examples

His loud boasting was seen by many as a form of overcompensation for his insecurity.
The company's generous refund policy may just be overcompensation for past customer complaints.
She bought expensive gifts for her friends, which some viewed as overcompensation for her feelings of guilt.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: compensation - the act of making up for a deficiency or loss

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' (excessively) + 'compensation' (from Latin compensare, to counterbalance).
