Oceanica

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊʃiˈænɪkə / (AmE), / ˌəʊsiˈænɪkə / (BrE)
a region consisting of a large group of islands in the south Pacific, including Melanesia, Micronesia, Polynesia, and sometimes Australasia and the Malay Archipelago
region of Pacific islands

Examples

Researchers are studying the biodiversity found throughout Oceanica to better understand tropical ecosystems.
Tourists are drawn to the exotic islands of Oceanica for their beautiful beaches and coral reefs.
The cultures of Oceanica are known for their rich traditions and unique art forms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Oceania - the region of the central and southern Pacific including Australia and neighboring islands

Etymology

From Latin 'Oceanica', relating to the ocean or Oceania.
