Myroxylon

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈrɑksiˌlɑn / (AmE), / mɪˈrɒksɪˌlɒn / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American trees characterized by pinnate leaves and white flowers, known for producing balsam resin.
genus of tropical American trees

Examples

Myroxylon is the genus of trees that produces Peruvian balsam.
The fragrant resin extracted from Myroxylon is used in perfumes and medicines.
Many botanists study Myroxylon due to its economic importance.

Etymology

From Greek 'myron' (perfume) + 'xylon' (wood), referring to the fragrant wood.

2. NOUN

/ maɪˈrɑksaɪlɒn / (AmE), / mɪˈrɒksɪlən / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American trees with pinnate leaves and white flowers, some species of which produce balsam.
genus of tropical American trees

Examples

Balsam of Peru is obtained from species of the Myroxylon genus.
The Myroxylon trees are valued for their aromatic resins.
Botanists studied several varieties of Myroxylon in the tropical rainforest.

Etymology

From Greek 'myron' (perfume) + 'xylon' (wood), referring to the fragrant resin produced by the trees.
