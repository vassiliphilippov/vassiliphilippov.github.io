bug

1. NOUN

/ bʌɡ / (AmE), / bʌɡ / (BrE)
A small insect or similar creeping or crawling invertebrate, commonly used as a general term for various small creatures.
small insect or crawling invertebrate

Examples

There was a bug crawling on the kitchen counter this morning.
Children love catching bugs in the garden during summer.
She hates it when bugs fly around her face while she's hiking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insect - small arthropod animal with six legs and generally one or two pairs of wings

Etymology

Probably from Middle English bugge (hobgoblin, scarecrow), later applied to insects.

4. NOUN

/ bʌɡ / (AmE), / bʌɡ / (BrE)
A true insect of the order Hemiptera, characterized by sucking mouthparts and forewings that are thickened and leathery at the base; includes species such as aphids and cicadas.
insect of the order Hemiptera

Examples

The assassin bug uses its mouthparts to feed on other insects.
Aphids are one type of bug that can damage crops.
The researcher collected several species of bug from the meadow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insect - small arthropod animal with three body parts and six legs

Etymology

Possibly from Middle English 'bugge' (something frightening), later applied to insects; scientific sense from technical usage.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02239548-n
Synset POS: n

5. NOUN

/ bʌɡ / (AmE), / bʌɡ / (BrE)
an error or flaw in a computer program, system, or machine that produces incorrect or unexpected results
error or flaw in a computer program

Examples

The software update fixed the bug that caused the program to crash unexpectedly.
She spent hours trying to track down a bug in the website's login system.
A critical bug in the code left users' data vulnerable to hackers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glitch - a minor, temporary malfunction or irregularity
  • Antonym: feature - a distinctive attribute or aspect of something, sometimes used ironically in programming to mean an intentional behavior that seems like a bug

Etymology

Originated from terminology used by engineers in the 19th century; popularized in computing in the 1940s after a literal insect was found causing a malfunction in an early computer.

Source: WordNet
