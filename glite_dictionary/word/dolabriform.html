dolabriform

1. ADJ

/ doʊˈlæbrəˌfɔrm / (AmE), / dəʊˈlæbrɪfɔːm / (BrE)
Having the shape of the head of an axe or cleaver; shaped like a hatchet blade.
axe- or cleaver-shaped

Examples

The leaves of that plant are distinctly dolabriform, resembling small axes.
Some fossil mollusks have dolabriform shells that set them apart from related species.
The scientist described the bone fragment as dolabriform due to its unusual cleaver-like shape.

Etymology

From Latin 'dolabra' (pickaxe, hatchet) + '-form' (having the form of).
