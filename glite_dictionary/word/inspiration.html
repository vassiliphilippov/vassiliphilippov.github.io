inspiration

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnspəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnspɪˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
A sudden or powerful influence that stimulates creative thought or action.
stimulus for creative activity

Examples

Her latest novel was written under a sudden burst of inspiration.
He found inspiration for his paintings in the beauty of nature.
Many inventors credit their success to a moment of inspiration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motivation - reason or enthusiasm for acting in a certain way
  • Antonym: apathy - lack of interest, enthusiasm, or concern

Etymology

From Latin 'inspiratio' (breathing in, inspiration), from 'inspirare' (to breathe into).

3. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.spəˈreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.spɪˈreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)

the act of inhaling
medical

Examples

During strenuous exercise, the rate of inspiration increases to supply more oxygen to the body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inhalation - the act of breathing in; drawing air into the lungs
  • Antonym: expiration - the act of breathing out; exhalation

Etymology
