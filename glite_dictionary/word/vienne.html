Vienne

1. PROPN

/ viˈɛn / (AmE), / viˈɛn / (BrE)
a town in south-central France, notable for being the site of a Roman Catholic council held from 1311 to 1313.
town in south-central France; site of 1311-13 church council

Examples

The Council of Vienne in 1311 marked a significant event in Church history.
We visited Vienne to explore its Roman ruins and medieval streets.
Scholars often refer to the decisions made at Vienne when studying medieval ecclesiastical reforms.

Etymology

From French 'Vienne', which comes from the Latin 'Vienna'.

2. NOUN

/ viˈɛn / (AmE), / viˈɛn / (BrE)
an ecumenical council held from 1311–1313 that addressed alleged crimes of the Knights Templar, planned a new crusade, and sought reformation of the clergy
Church council (1311–1313) addressing Knights Templar and reform

Examples

The Council of Vienne marked a turning point in the history of the Knights Templar.
Many reforms were proposed during the Vienne council to address clergy misconduct.
Historians often debate the outcomes of Vienne regarding its intended crusade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Council of Constance - an ecumenical council of the Catholic Church held from 1414–1418
  • Synonym: Council of Trent - an ecumenical council of the Catholic Church held from 1545–1563

Etymology

From the name of the city of Vienne in southeastern France, where the council was held.
