Heinrich Himmler

1. PROPN

/ ˈhaɪn.rɪk ˈhɪm.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪn.rɪç ˈhɪm.lə / (BrE)
(1900–1945) German Nazi leader who was chief of the SS and the Gestapo, overseeing the genocide of six million Jews during the Holocaust.
Nazi leader, SS and Gestapo chief

Examples

Heinrich Himmler played a central role in organizing the Holocaust during World War II.
The notorious crimes of Heinrich Himmler remain a grim chapter in history.
Few Nazi officials were as powerful and feared as Heinrich Himmler.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Adolf Hitler - German dictator; leader of the Nazi Party and instigator of World War II
  • Synonym: SS - Schutzstaffel; major paramilitary organization under Adolf Hitler and the Nazi Party
