talon

1. NOUN

/ ˈtælən / (AmE), / ˈtælən / (BrE)
a sharp, curved claw, especially one belonging to a bird of prey
a sharp claw of a bird of prey

Examples

The eagle gripped its prey tightly in its talons.
Vultures use their powerful talons to tear apart their food.
You could see the sharp talon curving from the hawk's foot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: claw - a sharp, usually curved nail on the foot of an animal or bird

Etymology

From Old French *talon* (heel or spur), from Latin *talus* (ankle).
