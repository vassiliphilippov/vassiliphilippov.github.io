trinuclear

1. ADJ

/ traɪˈnuːkliər / (AmE), / traɪˈnjuːkliə / (BrE)
(of a cell or molecule) having three nuclei.
having three nuclei

Examples

The trinuclear cell structure is unusual in most animal tissues.
Scientists observed a trinuclear complex in the chemical compound's crystalline form.
A trinuclear white blood cell is rarely found in healthy human samples.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: binuclear - having two nuclei
  • Degree Lower: mononuclear - having one nucleus

Etymology

From Latin 'tri-' (three) and 'nucleus' (kernel, core).
