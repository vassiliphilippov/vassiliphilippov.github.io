ADD

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ ˌdiː ˈdiː / (AmE), / ˌeɪ ˌdiː ˈdiː / (BrE)
a condition, primarily found in children, characterized by difficulty in maintaining attention and sometimes by hyperactivity and impulsive behavior; an earlier term now more commonly called attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD)
attention disorder, now mostly called ADHD
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed him with ADD after months of evaluation.
Students with ADD may struggle to concentrate in class and often appear restless.
Treatments for ADD usually include a combination of medication and behavioral therapy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ADHD - a condition with symptoms of inattention, hyperactivity, and impulsivity

Etymology

Abbreviation of attention deficit disorder.
