fusiform

1. ADJ

/ ˈfjuːsɪˌfɔrm / (AmE), / ˈfjuːsɪfɔːm / (BrE)
spindle-shaped; tapering at both ends, wider in the middle and narrowing toward each end.
spindle-shaped; tapering at both ends

Examples

The muscle had a characteristic fusiform appearance, widest in the middle and tapering at both ends.
Many fish have a fusiform body, which helps them swim quickly through the water.
The swelling was observed to be fusiform, narrowing gradually at both ends.

Etymology

From Latin 'fusus' (spindle) + '-form' (shape, form).
