Apocalypse

1. PROPN

/ əˈpɑːkəlɪps / (AmE), / əˈpɒkəlɪps / (BrE)
the last book of the New Testament, containing visions of heaven, the conflict between good and evil, and the end of the world, attributed to Saint John the Apostle
last book of the New Testament

Examples

Many Christians interpret the Apocalypse as predicting future events at the end of the world.
The vivid imagery found in the Apocalypse has inspired countless artists and writers.
She studied the Apocalypse to better understand the symbolism in early Christian theology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Revelation - another name for the last book of the New Testament

Etymology

From Greek apokalypsis (revelation, disclosure), via Latin.
