scrofulous

1. ADJ

/ ˈskrɑː.fjəl.əs / (AmE), / ˈskrɒf.jʊ.ləs / (BrE)
afflicted with or relating to scrofula, a form of tuberculosis affecting the lymph nodes, especially in the neck.
afflicted with scrofula (tuberculosis of the lymph nodes)
medical archaic

Examples

The surgeon confirmed that the boy had a scrofulous infection along his jawline.
Victorian novels sometimes reference scrofulous children as symbols of urban disease.
Many patients with scrofulous conditions benefitted from improved sanitation in the nineteenth century.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin scrofulosus, from Latin scrofula (a swelling of the glands of the neck).

archaic
