grammar

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡræmər / (AmE), / ˈɡræmə / (BrE)
the set of rules that explain how words are used in a language, including how they are combined to form sentences; the scientific study of these rules and structures
rules and structures of a language

Examples

She studied grammar to improve her writing skills.
The book provides a detailed explanation of English grammar.
Understanding basic grammar is essential for learning any new language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: syntax - the arrangement of words and phrases to create well-formed sentences
  • Synonym: morphology - the study of the forms of words

Etymology

From Greek 'grammatike' (art of letters), via Latin 'grammatica'.
