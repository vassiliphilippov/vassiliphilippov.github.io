Gentianaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛnʃiəˈneɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛnʃiəˈniːsiː / (BrE)
a family of chiefly herbaceous plants with showy, often blue flowers, including the gentians; some species are grown as ornamentals
family of plants including gentians

Examples

Many alpine gardens include members of the Gentianaceae for their striking blue flowers.
The Gentianaceae family is noted for its medicinal uses in traditional remedies.
Botanists classify over a thousand species under the Gentianaceae family.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Gentiana.

2. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛn.ʃəˈneɪ.si.i / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛn.tʃi.əˈneɪ.si.i / (BrE)
a large family of chiefly herbaceous plants with showy flowers, including the gentians; some are cultivated as ornamentals
a family of flowering plants (gentians)

Examples

Many alpine meadows are home to members of the Gentianaceae family with their blue and purple flowers.
The Gentianaceae include several species prized for ornamental gardening.
Botanists study the chemical compounds produced by Gentianaceae to understand their medicinal properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gentian - a plant of the genus Gentiana, known for their bright blue flowers

Etymology

From New Latin Gentianaceae, based on the type genus Gentiana.
