to intend

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈtɛnd / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛnd / (BrE)
to mean or aim to convey or express a particular idea or message
mean; aim to express

Examples

What did you intend by that remark?
His words intend only kindness, not criticism.
I never intended to imply anything negative with my comments.

Synset Examples

You never understand what I mean!
what do his words intend?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mean - to have as a meaning or intention

Etymology

From Latin intendere, meaning 'to stretch out, aim at'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00957180-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈtɛnd / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛnd / (BrE)
to have something in mind as a plan or purpose; to plan or mean to do something
to have as a plan or purpose

Examples

She intends to study medicine at university next year.
I intended to call you yesterday, but I got busy with work.
They intend this program to benefit the entire community.

Synset Examples

I mean no harm
I only meant to help you
She didn't think to harm me
We thought to return early that night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mean - to have as a purpose or intention
  • Synonym: plan - to intend to do something; to have a scheme for
  • Antonym: to forget - to fail to remember or intend to do something

Etymology

From Latin 'intendere' (to stretch out, aim at) via Old French 'intendre'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00710367-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɪnˈtɛnd / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛnd / (BrE)
to design or destine someone or something for a particular purpose or future; to have in mind as someone's or something's fate or role
to design or destine for a purpose or future

Examples

She was intended to become the director from an early age.
The room was intended for guests but was rarely used.
The funds are intended for educational purposes only.

Synset Examples

She was intended to become the director

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to destine - to determine something in advance as by fate or purpose
  • Synonym: to assign - to designate someone for a particular role or purpose

Etymology

From Latin intendere (to stretch out, direct attention), from in- (toward) + tendere (to stretch).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00711208-v
Synset POS: v
