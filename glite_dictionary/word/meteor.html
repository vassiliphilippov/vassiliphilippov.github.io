meteor

1. NOUN

/ ˈmiːdiər / (AmE), / ˈmiːtɪə / (BrE)
A small body from outer space that enters the Earth's atmosphere, becoming incandescent as a result of friction and often appearing as a streak of light.
piece of debris burning in Earth's atmosphere

Examples

We spotted a bright meteor streaking across the night sky during the shower.
Each meteor burns up as it enters the Earth's atmosphere, creating a brief flash of light.
Last night, I made a wish when I saw a meteor pass overhead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shooting star - a common name for a meteor seen as a streak of light
  • Degree Lower: meteorite - a meteor that survives its passage through the atmosphere and lands on Earth

Etymology

From Greek meteōros (high in the air).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmiːtiər / (AmE), / ˈmiːtɪə / (BrE)
A streak of light in the night sky caused by a meteoroid entering the Earth's atmosphere and burning up due to friction.
a streak of light from a burning meteoroid

Examples

We saw a bright meteor streak across the sky during the Perseid shower.
The child made a wish when a meteor flashed above the trees.
Last night, several meteors were visible due to the clear conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shooting star - a common term for a meteor
  • Degree Lower: meteoroid - a small body traveling through space, which can become a meteor

Etymology

From Greek meteōros (high in the air).
