suspect

1. NOUN

/ səˈspɛkt / (AmE), / səˈspɛkt / (BrE)
a person thought to be guilty of a crime or offense
person under suspicion

Examples

The police questioned the suspect for several hours about the robbery.
After reviewing the evidence, the detective identified a new suspect.
Each suspect was asked to provide an alibi for the night of the incident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accused - a person officially charged with a crime
  • Synonym: culprit - a person responsible for a crime or misdeed

Etymology

From Latin 'suspectus', past participle of 'suspicari' (to mistrust, suspect).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10700830-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ səˈspɛkt / (AmE), / səˈspɛkt / (BrE)
to believe someone is guilty of a crime or wrongdoing without certain proof
believe someone is guilty (verb)

Examples

The police suspect him of involvement in the robbery.
Many people suspected the manager but had no concrete evidence.
Investigators continue suspecting foul play in the mysterious disappearance.

Synset Examples

The U.S. suspected Bin Laden as the mastermind behind the terrorist attacks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doubt - to feel uncertain about something
  • Antonym: to know - to be aware of with certainty

Etymology

From Latin suspectare, from suspicere 'to look up at, mistrust'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00926844-v
Synset POS: v

3. ADJ

/ səˈspɛkt / (AmE), / səˈspɛkt / (BrE)
regarded with suspicion or doubt; not to be trusted or considered as possibly dishonest or false
regarded with suspicion or doubt

Examples

His explanation for being late sounded suspect to everyone in the room.
The police found the business arrangement suspect after reviewing the details.
Her alibi was somewhat suspect, so investigators continued to question her.

Synset Examples

there was something fishy about the accident
up to some funny business
some definitely queer goings-on
a shady deal
her motives were suspect
suspicious behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suspicious - having or showing a cautious distrust of someone or something
  • Antonym: trustworthy - able to be relied on as honest or truthful
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01923975-s
Synset POS: s

4. VERB

/ səˈspɛkt / (AmE), / səˈspɛkt / (BrE)
to believe something is true or likely without having definite proof
believe to be true or probable

Examples

I suspect she knows more than she is telling us.
Many people suspect that the company will announce layoffs soon.
Although he had no proof, he suspected that his friend was hiding something.

Synset Examples

I suspect he is a fugitive
I surmised that the butler did it

Related Terms

  • Antonym: doubt - to feel uncertain about something
  • Synonym: to suppose - to think that something is likely to be true

Etymology

From Latin 'suspectare', frequentative of 'suspīcere' (to look up at, mistrust).

Source: WordNet

5. VERB

/ səˈspɛkt / (AmE), / səˈspɛkt / (BrE)
to think that somebody may be dishonest, untrustworthy, or involved in something wrong, without certain proof; to regard with suspicion.
to have doubts about someone's honesty or intentions

Examples

The police began to suspect his story after finding inconsistencies in his account.
I can't help but suspect that she knows more than she's telling us.
After her keys went missing, Jane started to suspect her roommate.

Synset Examples

I suspect her true motives
she distrusts her stepmother

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distrust - to have no trust or confidence in someone or something
  • Antonym: trust - to believe that someone is honest and will not harm you

Etymology

From Latin 'suspectare', frequentative of 'suspicere' (to mistrust, look up at).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00689827-v
Synset POS: v
