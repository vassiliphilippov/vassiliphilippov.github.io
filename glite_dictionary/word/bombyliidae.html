Bombyliidae

1. NOUN

/ bɑːmˈbɪliːˌaɪdiː / (AmE), / bɒmˈbɪliːˌaɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of flies, commonly known as bee flies, many of which have a hairy body and mimic bees.
a family of flies called bee flies

Examples

Scientists observed several species of Bombyliidae hovering near the flowers.
The distinctive pattern and hairy bodies make Bombyliidae easy to recognize among insects.
Some gardeners welcome Bombyliidae because they are important pollinators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bee fly - a common name for members of the Bombyliidae family

Etymology

From New Latin, from genus Bombylius + -idae (family suffix for zoology).

2. NOUN

/ bɒmˌbɪl.iˈaɪ.diː / (AmE), / bɒmˌbɪl.iˈaɪ.diː / (BrE)
a family of flies, commonly called bee flies, known for their resemblance to bees and their hairy bodies; many species have long proboscises used for feeding on nectar.
family of flies known as bee flies

Examples

The Bombyliidae are often mistaken for bees due to their fuzzy appearance and flight patterns.
Many species within the Bombyliidae family play an important role in pollination.
The entomologist collected several specimens of Bombyliidae during his fieldwork in the desert.

Etymology

From New Latin *Bombyliidae*, based on the type genus *Bombylius*.
