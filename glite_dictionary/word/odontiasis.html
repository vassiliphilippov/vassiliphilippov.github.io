odontiasis

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊdɒnˈtaɪəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌəʊdɒnˈtʌɪəsɪs / (BrE)
the medical process by which a baby's first teeth emerge through the gums
eruption of baby teeth through gums
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that the fussiness was due to odontiasis and was perfectly normal.
Infants experiencing odontiasis may have swollen and tender gums.
Parents often seek remedies to soothe their babies during odontiasis.

Etymology

From Greek 'odous' (tooth) + '-iasis' (process or disease).
