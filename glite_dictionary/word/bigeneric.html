bigeneric

1. ADJ

/ baɪ.dʒəˈnɛr.ɪk / (AmE), / baɪ.dʒɪˈnɛr.ɪk / (BrE)
(of a hybrid) resulting from a cross between species belonging to different genera.
cross between different genera
medical

Examples

The orchid is a bigeneric hybrid produced by crossing two distinct genera.
Bigeneric hybrids are rare in nature due to reproductive barriers between genera.
Scientists documented a unique bigeneric plant created through artificial pollination.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: interspecific - resulting from a cross between species within the same genus

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'bi-' (two) + 'generic', from 'genus' (kind, type).
