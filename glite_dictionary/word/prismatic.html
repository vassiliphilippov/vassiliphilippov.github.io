prismatic

1. ADJ

/ prɪzˈmætɪk / (AmE), / prɪzˈmatɪk / (BrE)
having the form of a prism or relating to a prism; resembling the geometric shape of a prism or displaying colors like those produced by a prism.
relating to or shaped like a prism

Examples

The crystals had a prismatic structure that caught the light beautifully.
She admired the prismatic colors created by the sunlight passing through the glass.
The designer used a prismatic shape for the new lamp, giving it a modern look.

Synset Examples

prismatic form

Etymology

From French 'prismatique', from 'prisme' (prism), or from late Latin 'prismat-', from Greek 'prisma' (something sawed, a prism).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03114015-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ prɪzˈmætɪk / (AmE), / prɪzˈmatɪk / (BrE)
showing a range of bright colors like those produced when light passes through a prism; exhibiting spectral colors formed by refraction.
displaying spectral colors like a prism

Examples

The chandelier cast a prismatic light that danced across the walls.
Sunlight through the glass created a prismatic effect, scattering rainbows on the floor.
The artist was inspired by the prismatic hues visible in the crystal vase.

Synset Examples

prismatic light

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iridescent - showing luminous colors that seem to change when viewed from different angles

Etymology

From French prismatique, from 'prisme' (prism), ultimately from Greek 'prisma' (something sawed).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00404731-s
Synset POS: s
