imposter

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpoʊstər / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɒstə / (BrE)
A person who pretends to be someone else in order to deceive others, especially for personal gain.
person who pretends to be someone else

Examples

The police arrested the imposter who had been posing as a doctor for years.
She felt like an imposter at her new job until she gained more confidence.
An imposter successfully gained access to the building using forged identification.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fraud - a person who deceives others by pretending to be someone they are not
  • Antonym: authentic person - a genuine person who is not pretending to be someone else

Etymology

From French 'imposteur', from Latin 'impostor' (one who imposes, deceiver).
