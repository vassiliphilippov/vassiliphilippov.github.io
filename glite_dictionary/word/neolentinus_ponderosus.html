Neolentinus ponderosus

1. NOUN

/ niː.oʊˌlɛnˈtaɪnəs pɒn.dəˈroʊ.səs / (AmE), / niː.əʊˌlɛntɪˈnʌs pɒn.dəˈrəʊ.səs / (BrE)
a large edible mushroom with gills, featuring a cap up to 15 inches wide and a broad stalk, edible when young and tender
large edible gilled mushroom species

Examples

The massive Neolentinus ponderosus can sometimes be found growing at the base of conifer trees.
Foragers seek Neolentinus ponderosus when it is still young and tender for the best taste.
Identifying Neolentinus ponderosus requires examining its large gilled cap and thick stalk.

Etymology

From Neo- (new) + Lentinus (a genus of fungi) and ponderosus, Latin for 'weighty' or 'heavy'.
