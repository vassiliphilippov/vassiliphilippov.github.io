loosening

1. NOUN

/ ˈluːsənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈluːsənɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of making something less tight or more relaxed
the act of making less tight

Examples

The loosening of the belt made him feel much more comfortable.
Recent policies have led to the loosening of trade restrictions across the region.
With the loosening of the bandage, the patient reported less pain.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tightening - the act of making something tighter
  • Synonym: relaxing - the act of making something less tense or rigid

Etymology

From the verb 'loosen', formed by adding the suffix '-ing'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈluːsənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈluːsənɪŋ / (BrE)
the process or result of becoming less tight, controlled, or strict
act of becoming less tight or strict

Examples

After some negotiations, there was a noticeable loosening of restrictions.
The loosening of the rope allowed the boat to drift away from the dock.
With the loosening of his grip, the bag slipped from his hands.

Synset Examples

the relaxation of requirements
the loosening of his grip
the slackening of the wind

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tightening - the process of making or becoming tighter or more strict
  • Synonym: relaxation - the state or process of being made less strict or severe
  • Synonym: slackening - the process of becoming slower, less taut, or less intense

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'loosen' + '-ing'.
