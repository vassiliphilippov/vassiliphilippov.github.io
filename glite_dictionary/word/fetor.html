fetor

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːtər / (AmE), / ˈfiːtə / (BrE)
A strong and unpleasant smell; a distinctive, offensive odor, especially as a sign of disease.
offensive, unpleasant smell

Examples

The fetor from the garbage was overwhelming when I entered the alley.
Doctors noted a distinctive fetor on the patient's breath, indicating possible liver disease.
Even after cleaning, a persistent fetor lingered in the old refrigerator.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stench - a very unpleasant smell
  • Antonym: fragrance - a pleasant, sweet smell

Etymology

From Latin 'fetor' (a stench), from 'fetere' (to stink).
