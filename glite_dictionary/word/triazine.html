triazine

1. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪ.əˌziːn / (AmE), / ˈtraɪ.əˌziːn / (BrE)
Any of several chemical compounds with a six-membered ring containing three carbon and three nitrogen atoms, existing as three isomers.
chemical compound; six-membered ring with three nitrogens
medical

Examples

The herbicide atrazine belongs to the triazine group of chemicals.
Scientists are studying the properties of various triazine isomers for pharmaceutical use.
A ring structure with alternating nitrogen and carbon atoms characterizes a triazine.

Etymology

From 'tri-' (three) + 'azine', indicating a ring with three nitrogen atoms.
