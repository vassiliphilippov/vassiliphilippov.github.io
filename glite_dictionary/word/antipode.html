antipode

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.tɪˌpoʊd / (AmE), / ˈæn.tɪ.pəʊd / (BrE)
something that is the direct opposite of another thing.
direct opposite; diametric contrast

Examples

For many, the carefree lifestyle is the antipode of the corporate world.
Her outspoken nature was the antipode of his reserved demeanor.
The concept of chaos is often seen as the antipode of order.

Synset Examples

quiet: an antipode to focused busyness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antithesis - a person or thing that is the direct opposite of someone or something else

Etymology

From Greek antipodes (opposite feet), referring to people on the opposite side of the Earth.
