ardeb

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈdɛb / (AmE), / ɑːˈdɛb / (BrE)
a unit of dry measure formerly used in Egypt, varying regionally but typically equivalent to about 198 liters or 5.62 bushels
Egyptian unit of dry measure

Examples

The farmer sold his wheat by the ardeb at the local market.
Historical records indicate that taxes were sometimes calculated in ardebs of grain.
One ardeb was considered a significant quantity in agricultural transactions in Egypt.

Etymology

From Arabic *ardabb*, a traditional unit of measure.
