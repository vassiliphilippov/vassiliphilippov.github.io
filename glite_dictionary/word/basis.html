basis

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪsɪs / (AmE), / ˈbeɪsɪs / (BrE)
the underlying support or foundation for an idea, argument, process, or relationship
underlying support or foundation

Examples

She agreed to join the committee on a voluntary basis.
Their friendship was built on a solid basis of trust.
The policy was adopted on a temporary basis until a permanent solution was found.

Synset Examples

they were on a friendly footing
he worked on an interim basis

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foundation - the underlying base or support for something

Etymology

From Latin basis, from Greek basis (step, foundation).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪsɪs / (AmE), / ˈbeɪsɪs / (BrE)
a fundamental principle, assumption, or starting point from which something is begun, developed, or explained
the underlying principle or foundation

Examples

The judge made his decision on the basis of the evidence provided.
Their friendship forms the basis of the story.
On what basis are these claims being made?

Synset Examples

the whole argument rested on a basis of conjecture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foundation - an underlying basis or principle for something
  • Synonym: premise - a previous statement from which another is inferred or follows as a conclusion
  • Synonym: assumption - a thing that is accepted as true or as certain to happen, without proof

Etymology

From Latin basis (foundation), from Greek basis (step, base).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05801562-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪsɪs / (AmE), / ˈbeɪsɪs / (BrE)
the main underlying or essential part or ingredient of something
main or essential part

Examples

Orange juice forms the basis of this refreshing summer drink.
Trust is the basis of a good relationship between friends.
Strong evidence is the basis for making scientific conclusions.

Synset Examples

the basis of this drink is orange juice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foundation - the lowest load-bearing part of a building; figuratively, the groundwork or underlying principle of something
  • Synonym: core - the central or most important part of something
  • Antonym: superstructure - the part of a building or structure above its foundation; figuratively, something built on a foundation

Etymology

From Latin basis (foundation), from Greek basis (step, pedestal).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
