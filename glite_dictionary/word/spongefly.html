spongefly

1. NOUN

/ ˈspʌndʒˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈspʌn(d)ʒˌflʌɪ / (BrE)
A hairy-bodied insect, typically of the family Sisyridae, whose larvae feed on freshwater sponges.
insect whose larvae feed on sponges

Examples

The larvae of the spongefly can often be found on freshwater sponges in quiet streams.
Entomologists study the spongefly to understand its unique life cycle and relationship with aquatic sponges.
A spongefly adult is recognized by its delicate wings and hairy body.
