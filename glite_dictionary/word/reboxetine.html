reboxetine

1. NOUN

/ riˈbɑksətin / (AmE), / riˈbɒksətiːn / (BrE)
an antidepressant drug that works by blocking the reuptake of norepinephrine in the brain, used to treat depression
antidepressant drug; norepinephrine reuptake inhibitor
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed reboxetine to help manage her depressive symptoms.
Recent studies have examined the efficacy of reboxetine in treating major depressive disorder.
Some patients experience dry mouth as a side effect of reboxetine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antidepressant - drug used to alleviate symptoms of depression
