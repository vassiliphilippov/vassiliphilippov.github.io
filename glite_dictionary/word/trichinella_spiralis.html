Trichinella spiralis

1. NOUN

/ trɪˌkɪnɛlə ˈspaɪrəlɪs / (AmE), / trɪˌkɪnɛlə ˈspaɪrəlɪs / (BrE)
A species of parasitic roundworm that infects the intestines and muscles of pigs, rats, humans, and other animals, causing trichinosis.
species of parasitic roundworm
medical

Examples

The infection of Trichinella spiralis in pork is a major concern for food safety.
After eating undercooked wild game, he was diagnosed with Trichinella spiralis infestation.
Scientists are developing new tests to quickly detect Trichinella spiralis in meat products.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trichinosis - disease caused by eating raw pork infected with Trichinella spiralis

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'thrix' (hair) and 'nella' (diminutive), and Latin 'spiralis' (spiral) referring to its coiled form.
