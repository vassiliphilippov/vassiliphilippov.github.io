Araneae

1. NOUN

/ əˈreɪni.i / (AmE), / əˈreɪnɪ.iː / (BrE)
The order of arachnids that comprises all true spiders, characterized by having eight legs and the ability to spin silk.
the order of arachnids including all true spiders

Examples

Scientists classify all true spiders within the order Araneae.
The Araneae are distinguished from other arachnids by their silk-spinning abilities.
Studies of Araneae help researchers understand the evolution of web-building behavior among spiders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spider - an eight-legged arachnid known for spinning webs

Etymology

From Latin *aranea* (spider).
