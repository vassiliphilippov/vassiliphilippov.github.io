catalepsy

1. NOUN

/ ˈkætəˌlɛpsi / (AmE), / ˈkatəlɛpsi / (BrE)
A medical condition in which a person experiences a trance-like state marked by loss of voluntary movement and failure to respond to external stimuli.
trance-like loss of voluntary movement
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with catalepsy after remaining motionless for several hours despite external stimulation.
During states of catalepsy, he was unable to move or speak, though apparently awake.
Some forms of epilepsy can be accompanied by episodes of catalepsy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catatonia - state of unresponsiveness with rigidity
  • Synonym: stupor - a state of near-unconsciousness or insensibility

Etymology

From Greek katalēpsis (seizure, amazement), from katalambanein (to seize, surprise).
