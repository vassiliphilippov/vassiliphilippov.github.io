hepatoflavin

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛpətoʊˈfleɪvɪn / (AmE), / ˌhɛpətəʊˈfleɪvɪn / (BrE)
a vitamin of the B complex, also known as riboflavin, that helps prevent skin lesions and weight loss
a B vitamin (riboflavin)
medical

Examples

A deficiency of hepatoflavin can lead to skin disorders and fatigue.
Milk is a natural source of hepatoflavin, important for a healthy diet.
Doctors may prescribe hepatoflavin supplements to treat certain nutritional deficiencies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: riboflavin - a B vitamin, also called vitamin B2, necessary for energy production

Etymology

From Greek 'hepar' (liver) and Latin 'flavus' (yellow), referring to its discovery in the liver and its yellow color.
