saffranine

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæfrəˌnaɪn / (AmE), / ˈsæfrəniːn / (BrE)
any of a class of chiefly red synthetic dyes used in staining and as coloring agents, especially in biological and chemical applications
red synthetic organic dye

Examples

The tissue was stained with saffranine to make the cell structures more visible under the microscope.
Researchers used saffranine as a red dye in the laboratory experiment.
The vibrant red color of saffranine makes it useful in both scientific and industrial settings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: safranin - variant spelling of saffranine, red synthetic dye

Etymology

From French 'safranine', derived from 'safran' (saffron), referencing its color.
