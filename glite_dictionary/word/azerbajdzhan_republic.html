Azerbajdzhan Republic

1. PROPN

/ ˌɑːzərˈbɑːʤɑn rɪˈpʌblɪk / (AmE), / ˌæzəˈbɑːdʒɑːn rɪˈpʌblɪk / (BrE)
A landlocked country in southwestern Asia, bordered by the Caspian Sea, formerly part of the Soviet Union; also known as Azerbaijan.
country in southwestern Asia (Azerbaijan)

Examples

The Azerbajdzhan Republic declared its independence in 1991 after the collapse of the Soviet Union.
Oil exports are a major part of the economy of the Azerbajdzhan Republic.
The capital city of the Azerbajdzhan Republic is Baku.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Azerbaijan - country in southwestern Asia, also officially called the Republic of Azerbaijan

Etymology

From Russian Азербайджан (Azerbaydzhan), adopted as a variant English transliteration; connected to Persian 'Āzarbayjān'.
