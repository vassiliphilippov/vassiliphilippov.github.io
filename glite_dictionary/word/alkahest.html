alkahest

1. NOUN

/ ˈæl.kə.hɛst / (AmE), / ˈæl.kə.hɛst / (BrE)
a hypothetical universal solvent that alchemists believed could dissolve any substance
hypothetical universal solvent in alchemy

Examples

Medieval alchemists devoted their lives to discovering the elusive alkahest.
No scientific evidence has ever supported the existence of the alkahest described in alchemical texts.
The legend of the alkahest reflects humanity’s enduring quest to understand and master nature.

Etymology

Probably from pseudo-Arabic roots constructed in early modern Europe, blending Arabic 'al-' (the) with possibly 'koh'l' (antimony) or similar terms.
