Ovis aries

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvɪs ˈɛəriːz / (AmE), / ˈəʊvɪs ˈeəriːz / (BrE)
the scientific name for the domestic sheep, a species of domesticated mammal bred for wool, meat, and skin
domestic sheep species (scientific name)

Examples

The farm raises Ovis aries for both wool and meat production.
Research on Ovis aries genetics has led to improvements in sheep breeding.
A veterinarian explained that Ovis aries are highly adaptable animals found worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sheep - a domesticated ruminant mammal kept for its wool or meat

Etymology

From Latin: 'Ovis' meaning 'sheep', 'aries' meaning 'ram'.
