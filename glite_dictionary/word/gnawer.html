gnawer

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔɚɚ / (AmE), / ˈnɔːə / (BrE)
a small mammal, such as a rodent, with a single pair of continuously growing incisor teeth adapted for gnawing
mammal with gnawing teeth (rodent, etc.)

Examples

The beaver is a well-known example of a gnawer found in North America.
Many gnawers play crucial roles in the ecosystem as prey and seed dispersers.
Scientists studied the dental patterns of a rare gnawer discovered in the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rodent - a mammal of the order Rodentia, characterized by strong continuously growing incisors

Etymology

From the verb 'gnaw' (to bite or chew on persistently) + '-er' (agent suffix).
