COD

1. ADV

/ si oʊ ˈdi / (AmE), / siː əʊ ˈdiː / (BrE)
a system of paying for goods when they are delivered rather than in advance; collect on delivery.
payment upon delivery of goods

Examples

The company will ship your order COD if you don’t have a credit card.
You can request to pay COD when buying from some online stores.
All items sent COD must be paid for upon delivery.

Synset Examples

mail a package C.O.D.

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'cash on delivery' or 'collect on delivery', from commercial banking and postal practices.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00255418-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADJ

/ si oʊ ˈdi / (AmE), / siː əʊ ˈdiː / (BrE)
(of a letter, package, etc.) to be paid for by the recipient at the time of delivery.
payable on delivery (shipping term)

Examples

We ordered the furniture COD because we didn't want to pay until it arrived.
The package will be sent COD and must be paid for upon delivery.
You can request COD service if you prefer not to pay online.

Synset Examples

a collect call
the letter came collect
a COD parcel

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prepaid - (of mail or packages) paid for in advance

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'cash on delivery'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
