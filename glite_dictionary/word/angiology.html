angiology

1. NOUN

/ ˌændʒiˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌandʒiˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The branch of medical science concerned with the study of blood vessels, lymph vessels, and their disorders.
study of blood and lymph vessels
medical

Examples

She decided to specialize in angiology after developing an interest in vascular diseases.
Recent advances in angiology have improved treatments for patients with circulatory problems.
The medical textbook contained an in-depth section on angiology and the lymphatic system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cardiology - branch of medicine dealing with the heart and its functions
  • Synonym: hematology - branch of medicine concerned with blood and blood diseases

Etymology

From Greek angeion (vessel) + -logia (study).
