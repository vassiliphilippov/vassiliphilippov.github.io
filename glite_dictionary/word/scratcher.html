scratcher

1. NOUN

/ ˈskrætʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈskrætʃə / (BrE)
a person who scratches, especially to relieve an itch
person who scratches an itch

Examples

After being bitten by mosquitoes, the child became a frequent scratcher.
Dermatologists often advise habitual scratchers to avoid irritating their skin further.
Every scratcher knows the temporary relief is rarely worth the lasting marks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: itcher - a person who experiences or has an itch
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10583139-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈskrætʃər / (AmE), / ˈskrætʃə / (BrE)
a device used for scratching a surface, especially the body or an animal's object
device used for scratching

Examples

She bought a new scratcher for her cat to keep it from clawing the furniture.
A back scratcher is handy when you can't reach an itch.
The store sells several types of scratchers for pets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scratching post - a post for cats to scratch

Etymology

From 'scratch' + '-er', denoting something that scratches.
