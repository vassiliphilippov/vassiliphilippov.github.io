timothy

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪməθi / (AmE), / ˈtɪməθi / (BrE)
A type of grass with long cylindrical flower spikes, cultivated in northern parts of the United States and Europe for use as hay.
a grass grown for hay

Examples

Many farmers prefer timothy for feeding their horses because of its high fiber content.
The meadow was covered in tall timothy swaying gently in the breeze.
Bales of timothy hay were stacked neatly in the barn for winter use.

Etymology

Named after Timothy Hanson, an American farmer who promoted its cultivation around 1720.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɪməθi / (AmE), / ˈtɪməθi / (BrE)
a type of grass with cylindrical seed heads, widely grown for hay used as animal feed
a grass species grown for hay

Examples

Cows were grazing in a field of timothy and clover.
Farmers often choose timothy as a reliable hay crop for their livestock.
The timothy was cut and baled before the rainy season began.

Etymology

Named after Timothy Hanson, an American farmer who promoted its cultivation in the 18th century.
