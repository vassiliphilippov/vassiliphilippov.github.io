thulium

1. NOUN

/ ˈθuːliəm / (AmE), / ˈθjuːliəm / (BrE)
a soft, silvery metallic element of the rare earth group, symbol Tm, used especially in portable X-ray devices
a chemical element (Tm), soft rare earth metal

Examples

The radioactive isotope of thulium is used in compact X-ray devices.
Scientists discovered traces of thulium while analyzing samples from the mine.
Pure thulium is soft enough to be cut with a knife.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: erbium - another rare earth element
  • Synonym: ytterbium - rare earth metal related to thulium

Etymology

Named after 'Thule', an ancient place name (possibly Scandinavia) plus '-ium', used for elements.
