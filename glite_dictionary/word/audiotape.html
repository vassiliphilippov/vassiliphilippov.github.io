audiotape

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔdiˌteɪp / (AmE), / ˈɔːdiəʊteɪp / (BrE)
A magnetic tape on which sound is recorded.
magnetic tape for sound recording

Examples

She listened to an old audiotape of her grandmother singing.
The interview was preserved on a audiotape for future reference.
After digitizing, the fragile audiotape was stored in the archive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tape - magnetic tape used for recording or playing sound or video

Etymology

From 'audio' (relating to sound) + 'tape' (a long, narrow strip of magnetic material).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔːdiˌoʊˌteɪp / (AmE), / ˈɔːdiəʊˌteɪp / (BrE)
magnetic tape used for recording sound.
magnetic tape for recording sound

Examples

He found an old audiotape with his grandfather's voice on it.
The journalist used an audiotape to record the interview.
Many classic albums were originally released on audiotape before digital formats became popular.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cassette - plastic case containing audiotape for use in a tape recorder

Etymology

From audio (relating to sound) + tape (thin strip of material for recording).
