Circinus

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɜːrsənəs / (AmE), / ˈsɜːsɪnəs / (BrE)
a small, faint constellation in the southern sky near Musca and Triangulum Australe
a southern constellation

Examples

The constellation Circinus is visible only from the Southern Hemisphere.
Astronomers studied the stars within Circinus using a powerful telescope.
Circinus lies close to the brighter constellation of Triangulum Australe.

Etymology

From Latin 'circinus' meaning 'compass' (drawing tool), referring to its shape.
