hug

1. NOUN

/ hʌg / (AmE), / hʌg / (BrE)
an act of holding someone tightly in one's arms, typically as a sign of affection
an affectionate embrace

Examples

She gave her friend a warm hug before leaving.
After the long trip, he greeted his mother with a big hug.
A simple hug can make someone feel much better.

Synset Examples

come here and give me a big hug

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embrace - an act of holding someone closely in one's arms
  • Degree Higher: kiss - an act of touching with the lips, often as a sign of affection

Etymology

Origin unknown; first recorded in the late 16th century.

2. VERB

/ hʌɡ / (AmE), / hʌɡ / (BrE)
to hold someone tightly in your arms, especially to show affection
hold someone closely in your arms

Examples

She ran to her mother and hugged her tightly.
He always hugs his friends before saying goodbye.
They hugged after being apart for so long.

Related Terms

3. VERB

/ hʌɡ / (AmE), / hʌɡ / (BrE)
(of a piece of clothing or an object) to fit closely and tightly around something, usually a part of the body.
fit closely or tightly around

Examples

The new jacket hugged his shoulders perfectly, making him look slimmer.
Her yoga pants hug her legs and move with her every stretch.
The coastline hugged the winding road for miles, creating a stunning view.

Synset Examples

The dress hugged her hips

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cling - to hold on tightly or adhere closely
  • Antonym: loose - not fitting closely or tightly

Etymology

From Middle English 'huggen', probably of Scandinavian origin; originally meant 'to cherish' or 'embrace'.
