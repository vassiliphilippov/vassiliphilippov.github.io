to deduct

1. VERB

/ dɪˈdʌkt / (AmE), / dɪˈdʌkt / (BrE)
to keep back an amount of money from a payment, especially for tax or other obligatory reasons; to withhold and not pay out.
withhold an amount from payment

Examples

The company will deduct the cost of insurance from your paycheck each month.
Taxes are automatically deducted from employees' salaries before they receive payment.
When you return an item, a restocking fee may be deducted from your refund.

Synset Examples

My employer is withholding taxes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to withhold - to hold back or keep from giving

Etymology

From Latin 'deducere' (to lead away, subtract), via Latin 'deductus'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02219783-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ dɪˈdʌkt / (AmE), / dɪˈdʌkt / (BrE)
to take away an amount or part from a total, especially in calculating finances or accounts
subtract or take away from a total

Examples

The company will deduct taxes from your salary every month.
Please deduct any expenses from the total amount due.
She quickly deducted the discount before calculating the final price.

Synset Examples

subtract this amount from my paycheck

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to subtract - to take away (a number or amount) from another
  • Antonym: to add - to put together with another to increase the total

Etymology

From Latin 'deductus', past participle of 'deducere' (to lead away, deduct).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00642914-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ dɪˈdʌkt / (AmE), / dɪˈdʌkt / (BrE)
To reach a conclusion by reasoning from known facts; to infer or establish by deduction.
infer by reasoning from evidence

Examples

After examining the clues, the detective was able to deduct who committed the crime.
From her tone of voice, I could deduct that she was upset about the decision.
Scientists deduct patterns in nature by carefully analyzing large sets of data.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to infer - to reach a conclusion based on evidence and reasoning
  • Antonym: to induce - to draw a general conclusion from specific cases (opposite process of deduction)

Etymology

From Latin 'deducere' meaning 'to lead down or away', later used in logic as 'to deduct'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00638236-v
Synset POS: v
