choline

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊˌlin / (AmE), / ˈkəʊˌliːn / (BrE)
a water-soluble compound of the vitamin B complex, essential for fat metabolism and a constituent of lecithin
a B-vitamin essential for fat metabolism
medical

Examples

Eggs are an excellent source of choline, which supports brain health.
Choline plays a crucial role in fat metabolism and cell structure.
Doctors may recommend choline supplements during pregnancy for fetal development.

Etymology

From Greek 'chole' meaning 'bile', from its presence in bile compounds.
