ephemeral

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈfɛmərəl / (AmE), / ɪˈfɛmərəl / (BrE)
an organism or thing that lasts for a very short time, especially an insect that lives only briefly in its adult form
a short-lived organism or thing

Examples

The mayfly is a classic example of an ephemeral in the insect world.
These ephemerals burst into bloom for just a few days each spring.
Unlike most desert plants, ephemerals complete their life cycle quickly after rain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ephemera - things that exist or are used for only a short time

Etymology

From Greek ephēmeros (lasting only a day), from epi (upon) + hēmera (day).

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈfɛmərəl / (AmE), / ɪˈfiːmərəl / (BrE)
lasting for only a very short time; fleeting or transitory.
lasting a very short time

Examples

The beauty of the sunset was ephemeral, lasting only a few moments before fading away.
Fashion trends are often ephemeral, quickly replaced by the next popular style.
He realized that youth is ephemeral and should be cherished while it lasts.

Synset Examples

the ephemeral joys of childhood
a passing fancy
youth's transient beauty
love is transitory but it is eternal
fugacious blossoms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transient - lasting only for a short time
  • Antonym: permanent - lasting forever or for a very long time
  • Synonym: temporary - lasting or meant to last for only a short time

Etymology

From Greek ephēmeros (lasting only a day), from epi (upon) + hēmera (day).
