homespun

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊmˌspʌn / (AmE), / ˈhəʊmˌspʌn / (BrE)
a coarse, loosely woven fabric, originally made from yarn spun at home
coarse, loosely woven fabric

Examples

The villagers wore simple clothing made from homespun.
Early American settlers often relied on homespun for their everyday garments.
A piece of coarse homespun was draped over the rustic table.

Etymology

From 'home-' (referring to at home) + 'spun' (past participle of 'spin').

WordNet ID: oewn-03534624-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈhoʊmˌspʌn / (AmE), / ˈhəʊmˌspʌn / (BrE)
simple and unsophisticated; characteristic of country life or people, especially in contrast to urban sophistication.
simple, rural; characteristic of country life

Examples

His homespun manners and honesty quickly won over the townspeople.
The author’s homespun wisdom appeals to those seeking simple truths.
She preferred homespun advice to complicated psychological theories.

Synset Examples

cracker-barrel philosophy
the air of homespun country boys

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rustic - having a simplicity and charm typical of the countryside
  • Antonym: sophisticated - having, revealing, or involving a great deal of worldly experience and knowledge

Etymology

Originally referring to cloth spun or woven at home; later extended figuratively to mean simple or rural.

3. ADJ

/ ˈhoʊmˌspʌn / (AmE), / ˈhəʊmˌspʌn / (BrE)
(of fabric or yarn) having a rough, uneven surface, typically because it is handmade or loosely woven.
rough, loosely woven textile

Examples

She wrapped herself in a homespun blanket to keep warm on the porch.
The artisan displayed beautiful scarves made from homespun wool at the market.
His jacket, though homespun, felt both sturdy and comfortable against the cold.

Synset Examples

a sweater knitted of nubbly homespun yarns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coarse - having a rough or loose texture

Etymology

From 'home' + 'spun', originally referring to cloth spun at home rather than by machine.
