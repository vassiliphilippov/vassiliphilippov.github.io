preterit

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛt̬ərɪt / (AmE), / ˈpriːtərɪt / (BrE)
a grammatical term for the simple past tense, especially in languages such as Latin, Spanish, or Old English
grammatical term for past tense

Examples

In Old English, the preterit is formed differently from the present tense.
The Spanish preterit is used for actions completed at a specific time.
Linguists often discuss the differences between the preterit and the imperfect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: past tense - a verb tense expressing an action completed in the past

Etymology

From Latin 'praeteritus' (gone by, past participle of 'praeterire', to go by)
