Turdus pilaris

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːdəs pɪˈlɛrɪs / (AmE), / ˈtɜːdəs pɪˈlɑːrɪs / (BrE)
A medium-sized Eurasian thrush, commonly called the fieldfare, seen chiefly in winter.
a Eurasian thrush (fieldfare)

Examples

Large flocks of Turdus pilaris arrive from Scandinavia each winter.
The distinctive calls of Turdus pilaris can be heard in open countryside.
Researchers monitored the migration patterns of Turdus pilaris over several years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fieldfare - common name for Turdus pilaris, a Eurasian thrush

Etymology

From Latin: 'Turdus' meaning 'thrush' and 'pilaris' meaning 'of hair,' possibly referring to plumage.
