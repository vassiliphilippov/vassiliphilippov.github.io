peacefulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiːsfəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈpiːsfəlnəs / (BrE)
The state or quality of being calm, quiet, and without disturbance.
state of being calm and tranquil

Examples

The peacefulness of the countryside made her feel completely relaxed.
Yoga and meditation are known for promoting peacefulness and mental clarity.
He admired the peacefulness of the early morning hours before the city woke up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tranquility - state of being calm and free from disturbance
  • Synonym: serenity - state of being serene, calm, and untroubled
  • Antonym: restlessness - state of being unable to relax or remain still
  • Antonym: tension - state of mental or emotional strain

Etymology

From 'peaceful' + '-ness', 'peaceful' derived from 'peace'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpis.fəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈpiːs.fəl.nəs / (BrE)
a state of being free from mental stress, worry, or agitation; calmness or tranquility of mind
calmness of mind; absence of stress

Examples

The peacefulness of early morning always helps me start my day with a clear mind.
Meditation can bring a sense of peacefulness to those struggling with anxiety.
She admired the peacefulness of the countryside, far from the busy city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calmness - the quality of being calm and free from nervousness or agitation
  • Antonym: anxiety - a feeling of worry, nervousness, or unease

Etymology

From 'peaceful' + '-ness', with 'peaceful' from Old French 'paisible' and ultimately from Latin 'pax' (peace).
