deacon

1. NOUN

/ ˈdiːkən / (AmE), / ˈdiːkən / (BrE)
A member of the clerical order in some Christian churches who ranks just below a priest and is often responsible for certain services or functions.
cleric below a priest in Christian churches

Examples

The deacon assisted the priest during the Sunday service.
She was ordained as a deacon after years of theological study.
The deacon organized charity activities for the local community.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: priest - a clergy member ranking above deacon in most churches

Etymology

From Greek diakonos (servant, minister), via Latin diaconus.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdiː.kən / (AmE), / ˈdiː.kən / (BrE)
A lay member in certain Protestant churches who assists the minister in various functions, such as administration or conducting services.
Protestant lay church assistant

Examples

The deacon handed out hymn books before the service began.
After the sermon, the deacon assisted the minister in distributing communion.
Several deacons were elected to help with church administration this year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minister - a member of the clergy, especially in Protestant churches

Etymology

From Greek diakonos (servant, minister).
