awe-inspiring

1. ADJ

/ ˈɔː ɪnˌspaɪərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɔː ɪnˌspaɪə.rɪŋ / (BrE)
causing feelings of awe, admiration, or wonder; impressive in a way that inspires respect or amazement.
causing awe or admiration

Examples

The view from the top of the mountain was truly awe-inspiring.
The museum features an awe-inspiring collection of ancient artifacts.
Standing beneath the giant redwoods was an awe-inspiring experience.

Synset Examples

New York is an amazing city
the Grand Canyon is an awe-inspiring sight
the awesome complexity of the universe
this sea, whose gently awful stirrings seem to speak of some hidden soul beneath
Westminster Hall's awing majesty, so vast, so high, so silent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awesome - extremely impressive or daunting; inspiring awe
  • Antonym: mundane - lacking interest or excitement; commonplace

Etymology

From 'awe' (Old Norse 'agi') + 'inspiring' (from Latin 'inspirare', to breathe into).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɔː ɪnˌspaɪɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɔː ɪnˌspaɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
causing a feeling of respect, wonder, or fear; extremely impressive or amazing
causing awe; extremely impressive

Examples

The awe-inspiring view from the mountain peak left us speechless.
The awe-inspiring power of the waterfall drew tourists from around the world.
Her awe-inspiring performance on stage earned her a standing ovation.

Synset Examples

Watching the tornado form was both awe-inspiring and terrifying.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impressive - evoking admiration through size, quality, or skill
  • Antonym: unremarkable - not particularly interesting or impressive

Etymology

From 'awe' (from Old Norse 'agi', meaning fear or terror) + 'inspiring'.
