toby

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊbi / (AmE), / ˈtəʊbi / (BrE)
a drinking mug, usually in the form of a stout man wearing a three-cornered hat, often used as a decorative ornament or for serving drinks
a mug shaped like a stout man with a three-cornered hat

Examples

He collected antique tobies from various English auctions.
The old toby on the shelf belonged to my grandfather.
They drank ale from a large ceramic toby during the festival.

Etymology

Probably from Toby Fillpot, a character popularized in 18th-century English drinking culture.
