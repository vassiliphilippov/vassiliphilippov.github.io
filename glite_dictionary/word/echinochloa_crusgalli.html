Echinochloa crusgalli

1. NOUN

/ ɛˌkaɪnəˌkloʊə ˌkrʌsˈɡælaɪ / (AmE), / ɛˌkɪnəˌkləʊə ˌkrʌsˈɡælaɪ / (BrE)
a coarse annual grass, also known as barnyard grass, found worldwide as a common weed and sometimes used for hay or grazing
a cosmopolitan annual grass weed

Examples

Echinochloa crusgalli is a major weed in rice fields due to its rapid growth and high seed production.
Farmers sometimes use Echinochloa crusgalli as an emergency fodder when other grasses are scarce.
The seeds of Echinochloa crusgalli can contaminate harvested grain, making weed control essential.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barnyard grass - common name for Echinochloa crusgalli

Etymology

From Greek echinos (hedgehog, referring to the spiny seeds) and chloë (grass); Latin crus (leg) and galli (of a cock).
