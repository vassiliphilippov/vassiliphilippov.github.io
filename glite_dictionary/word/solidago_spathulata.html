Solidago spathulata

1. NOUN

/ soʊˌlɪˈdeɪɡoʊ spæθjʊˈleɪtə / (AmE), / sɒˌlɪˈdeɪɡəʊ spæθjʊˈlɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of goldenrod native to western North America, noted for its long, narrow clusters of small yellow flowers.
western North American goldenrod species

Examples

The Solidago spathulata blooms in late summer along the Pacific coast.
Botanists identified the plant as Solidago spathulata due to its unique yellow flower clusters.
Hikers often spot Solidago spathulata growing in open woodlands and sandy soils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goldenrod - any plant of the genus Solidago, with yellow flowers

Etymology

From Latin 'solidago', a genus name derived from 'solidare' (to make whole), and Latin 'spathulata', meaning 'spatula-shaped'.
