mediocre

1. ADJ

/ ˌmiːdiˈoʊkər / (AmE), / ˌmiːdiˈəʊkə / (BrE)
not very good; of only average or moderate quality
of only average quality

Examples

His mediocre performance disappointed the coach.
Most critics agreed the movie was mediocre at best.
The food at the new restaurant was neither terrible nor great—just mediocre.

Synset Examples

a novel of average merit
only a fair performance of the sonata
in fair health
the caliber of the students has gone from mediocre to above average
the performance was middling at best

Related Terms

  • Synonym: average - of a level considered typical or normal
  • Antonym: excellent - extremely good, of the highest quality
  • Antonym: outstanding - very much better than usual

Etymology

From Latin mediocris (moderate, ordinary).

2. ADJ

/ ˌmiːdiˈoʊkər / (AmE), / ˌmiːdiˈəʊkə / (BrE)
of only average quality, not very good; moderate to inferior in quality
of only average or inferior quality

Examples

The food at the restaurant was mediocre, so we probably won't go back.
Her performance in the competition was disappointingly mediocre compared to the others.
He delivered a mediocre speech that failed to impress the audience.

Synset Examples

they improved the quality from mediocre to above average

Related Terms

  • Synonym: average - typical, ordinary, neither very good nor very bad
  • Degree Lower: inferior - of lower quality or status
  • Antonym: excellent - of very high quality

Etymology

From Latin mediocris (moderate, ordinary), from medius (middle) and ocris (rugged mountain).

3. ADJ

/ ˌmiː.diˈoʊ.kɚ / (AmE), / ˌmiː.diˈəʊ.kə / (BrE)
of only average quality; not very good
of only average quality

Examples

The performance was merely mediocre and failed to impress the critics.
Despite his efforts, her cooking was always just mediocre.
They were disappointed by the mediocre service at the hotel.

Synset Examples

there have been good and mediocre and bad artists

Related Terms

  • Synonym: average - typical; neither very good nor very bad
  • Antonym: excellent - of the highest quality
  • Degree Higher: outstanding - exceptionally good
  • Degree Lower: poor - of low quality; not good

Etymology

From Latin mediocris (moderate, ordinary).
