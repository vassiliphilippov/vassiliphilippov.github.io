gruesomeness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡruːsəmnəs / (AmE), / ˈɡruːsəmnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being extremely unpleasant, horrifying, or ghastly, especially in appearance or detail
the quality of being gruesome

Examples

The gruesomeness of the crime scene shocked the investigators.
Some horror films rely on graphic gruesomeness to scare their audience.
She averted her eyes from the gruesomeness depicted in the painting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gruesome - extremely unpleasant or horrifying, especially in a shocking way

Etymology

From 'gruesome' + '-ness', formed in English.
