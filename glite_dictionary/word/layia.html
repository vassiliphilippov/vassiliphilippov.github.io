Layia

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪ.i.ə / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.i.ə / (BrE)
a genus of annual plants native to the western United States, noted for their showy yellow or white flowers
genus of annual plants with showy flowers

Examples

Botanists recently discovered a rare Layia species in the national park.
The fields were covered with blooming Layia during the spring.
Many gardeners appreciate Layia for its bright, cheerful flowers.

Etymology

Named after George Tradescant Lay, 19th-century English botanist.
