phenylacetamide

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɛniːl əˈsiːtəˌmaɪd / (AmE), / ˌfiːnɪl əˈsiːtəˌmaɪd / (BrE)
A white, solid, odorless chemical compound used in the synthesis of rubber accelerators, dyes, and camphor, and also as a metabolite related to paracetamol (acetaminophen).
a chemical compound used in synthesis and as a metabolite
medical

Examples

Researchers used phenylacetamide as an intermediate during the development of new dyes for the textile industry.
The laboratory ordered a fresh supply of phenylacetamide for their experiments on drug metabolism.
As a solid, phenylacetamide is typically odorless and can appear in a leaf-like or flake-like form.

Etymology

Formed from 'phenyl' (relating to a benzene ring) and 'acetamide' (an amide derived from acetic acid).
