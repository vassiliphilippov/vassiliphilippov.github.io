mephitis

1. NOUN

/ məˈfɪtɪs / (AmE), / mɪˈfɪtɪs / (BrE)
a foul or noxious odor; an offensively unpleasant smell
offensively unpleasant odor

Examples

The mephitis from the stagnant pond lingered in the evening air.
A sudden mephitis filled the laboratory when the chemical container broke.
He recoiled at the mephitis that seeped from the abandoned building.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stench - a very unpleasant smell
  • Antonym: fragrance - a pleasant, sweet smell

Etymology

From Latin mephitis (noxious vapor, foul smell).

Ancient legends often described places where mephitis would seep out and sicken local residents.
