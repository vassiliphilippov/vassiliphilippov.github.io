human T-cell leukemia virus-1

1. NOUN

/ ˈhjuːmən ˈtiː sɛl lɪˈkiːmiə ˈvaɪrəs wʌn / (AmE), / ˈhjuːmən ˈtiː sɛl lɪˈkiːmiə ˈvaɪərəs wʌn / (BrE)
a retrovirus that infects humans and causes T-cell leukemia, particularly adult T-cell leukemia/lymphoma
retrovirus causing T-cell leukemia in humans
medical

Examples

Researchers have discovered that human T-cell leukemia virus-1 can lead to adult T-cell leukemia in some individuals.
The prevalence of human T-cell leukemia virus-1 varies significantly across different geographic regions.
Testing for human T-cell leukemia virus-1 is important in blood donations to prevent transmission.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: HTLV-1 - abbreviation for human T-cell leukemia virus-1

Etymology

From 'human', 'T-cell' (a type of white blood cell), 'leukemia' (blood cancer), and 'virus'.
