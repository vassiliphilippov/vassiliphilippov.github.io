America

1. PROPN

/ əˈmɛɹɪkə / (AmE), / əˈmɛɹɪkə / (BrE)
The United States of America; a country in North America consisting of 50 states, including Alaska and Hawaii.
the United States of America

Examples

Many people dream of moving to America for new opportunities.
She traveled across America last summer, visiting several major cities.
The history of America includes waves of immigration from around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: United States - Another name for the United States of America, a country in North America

Etymology

From Italian *America*, from the Latinized name of explorer Amerigo Vespucci.

2. PROPN

/ əˈmɛrɪkə / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkə / (BrE)
The landmass comprising North America, South America, and Central America collectively.
the American continents (North, Central, and South)

Examples

Christopher Columbus is credited with the European discovery of America.
Many species are native to America and cannot be found elsewhere in the world.
The Andes are the longest continental mountain range in America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: the Americas - the continents of North and South America considered together
  • Antonym: United States - Country in North America often referred to informally as 'America'

Etymology

From Italian 'America', from Latinized form of Amerigo (Vespucci), an Italian explorer.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09218493-n
Synset POS: n
