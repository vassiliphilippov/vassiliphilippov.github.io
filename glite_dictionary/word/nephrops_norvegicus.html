Nephrops norvegicus

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɛfrɑps nɔrˈvɛdʒɪkəs / (AmE), / ˌnɛfrɒps nɔːˈvɛdʒɪkəs / (BrE)
a species of edible lobster found in European waters, also known as the Norway lobster or scampi, distinguished by its slender body compared to the American lobster
edible European lobster species

Examples

Nephrops norvegicus is considered a delicacy in many Mediterranean seafood restaurants.
Marine biologists conducted a survey to estimate the population of Nephrops norvegicus in the coastal waters of Scotland.
The chef prepared an exquisite dish using freshly-caught Nephrops norvegicus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scampi - another name for Nephrops norvegicus, especially in culinary contexts
  • Degree Higher: lobster - large marine crustacean with a long body and claws

Etymology

From Greek nephros (kidney) and Latin norvegicus (of Norway), referencing the animal's appearance and distribution.
