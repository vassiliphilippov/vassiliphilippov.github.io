Arcidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːr.sɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈɑː.sɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of marine bivalve mollusks commonly known as ark shells, characterized by thick, often ribbed shells
a family of bivalve mollusks (ark shells)

Examples

The Arcidae are distinguished by their robust, rectangular shells and taxodont hinge.
Many species within the Arcidae family are found in shallow coastal waters worldwide.
Marine biologists often study Arcidae to understand bivalve evolution and diversity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ark shell - common name for members of the Arcidae family of bivalves

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'arkē' (a chest or box); referring to the box-like shape of the shells.
