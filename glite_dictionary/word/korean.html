Korean

1. NOUN

/ kəˈriːən / (AmE), / kəˈriːən / (BrE)
the language spoken mainly in South Korea and North Korea
the language of Korea

Examples

She is fluent in both English and Korean.
Many students are learning Korean to watch their favorite dramas without subtitles.
The Korean alphabet is known as Hangul.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Japanese - the language of Japan
  • Synonym: English - the language spoken in the UK, US, and other countries

Etymology

From Korea + -an, denoting language or nationality.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06941948-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ kəˈriː.ən / (AmE), / kəˈriː.ən / (BrE)
of or relating to Korea, its people, or its language.
relating to Korea or its people

Examples

She loves Korean cuisine and often cooks bulgogi at home.
The Korean language has a unique writing system called Hangul.
Many students are interested in Korean history and culture.

Synset Examples

Korean handicrafts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Korea - East Asian country on the Korean Peninsula

Etymology

From Korea, after the country name, plus English adjective-forming suffix -an.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02979336-a
Synset POS: a

3. NOUN

/ kəˈriən / (AmE), / kəˈriːən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Korea, especially one who speaks the Korean language
a person from Korea

Examples

My best friend is a Korean who was born and raised in Seoul.
Many Koreans celebrate the Lunar New Year with special foods and traditions.
The Korean greeted us warmly and offered to help translate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Korean - the language spoken in Korea
  • Degree Lower: South Korean - a native or inhabitant of South Korea
  • Degree Lower: North Korean - a native or inhabitant of North Korea

Etymology

From Korea + -an (denoting nationality or ethnicity).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09738332-n
Synset POS: n
