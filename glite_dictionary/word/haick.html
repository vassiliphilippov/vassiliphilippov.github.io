haick

1. NOUN

/ haɪk / (AmE), / haɪk / (BrE)
a large piece of white cloth worn as an outer garment by men and women in northern Africa
North African outer garment

Examples

She wore a traditional haick as she walked through the market.
The haick protects people from the harsh desert sun and wind.
In Morocco, a white haick is often part of women's ceremonial dress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burnous - a long cloak with a hood worn in North Africa

Etymology

From Arabic *ḥā'ik* (cloth, wrapper).
