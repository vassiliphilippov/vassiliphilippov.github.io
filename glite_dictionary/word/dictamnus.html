Dictamnus

1. NOUN

/ dɪkˈtæmnəs / (AmE), / dɪkˈtamnəs / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants in the family Rutaceae, known for their aromatic leaves and showy flowers
a plant genus in the rutaceous family

Examples

The Dictamnus genus includes plants commonly known as gas plant or burning bush.
Many gardeners appreciate Dictamnus for its attractive, fragrant flowers.
Research on Dictamnus has revealed unique essential oils in its leaves.

Etymology

From Latin 'dictamnus', from Greek 'diktamnos', an aromatic plant from Mount Dicte in Crete.

2. NOUN

/ dɪkˈtæmnəs / (AmE), / dɪkˈtamnəs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the rue family (Rutaceae), known for their aromatic leaves and showy flowers, including the burning bush.
genus of aromatic flowering plants

Examples

The flowers of Dictamnus are known for emitting a flammable oil in hot weather.
Many gardeners appreciate Dictamnus for its unique scent and attractive blossoms.
Dictamnus is also called the burning bush because its volatile oils can sometimes ignite.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rutaceae - the rue or citrus family of flowering plants

Etymology

From Latin 'dictamnus', from Ancient Greek 'dikamnós', referring to a plant on Mount Dicte in Crete.
