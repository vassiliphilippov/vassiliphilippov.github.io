hospice

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːspɪs / (AmE), / ˈhɒspɪs / (BrE)
a lodging or shelter for travelers, especially one maintained by a religious order
shelter or lodging for travelers
archaic

Examples

During the Middle Ages, weary pilgrims often sought shelter in a hospice maintained by monks.
The travelers found safety and rest at a hospice high in the Alpine pass.
Many ancient hospices have been converted into modern inns or museums.

Etymology

From Latin hospitium (hospitality, guesthouse), via Old French hospice.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑspɪs / (AmE), / ˈhɒspɪs / (BrE)
A program or facility that provides medical and emotional care for people who are terminally ill, focusing on comfort rather than cure.
medical care for the terminally ill
medical

Examples

Her grandmother spent her final days in a hospice surrounded by caring staff.
The hospital referred the patient to a hospice for end-of-life care.
Many hospices offer support programs for the families of their patients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palliative care - medical care focusing on relief of symptoms for serious illness

Etymology

From French *hospice* (shelter for the needy), from Latin *hospitium* (guesthouse).
