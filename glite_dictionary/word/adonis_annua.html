Adonis annua

1. NOUN

/ əˈdoʊnɪs ˈænjuə / (AmE), / əˈdəʊnɪs ˈanjʊə / (BrE)
A Eurasian annual herb cultivated for its striking deep red flowers with dark centers, also known as pheasant's-eye.
a Eurasian herb with red flowers

Examples

The bright red flowers of Adonis annua make it a favorite in cottage gardens.
Botanists often grow Adonis annua for its ornamental value and easy cultivation.
Adonis annua is sometimes called pheasant's-eye due to its distinctive appearance.

Etymology

From Latin, genus name 'Adonis', and Latin 'annua' (annual).
