Brachinus

1. NOUN

/ brəˈkaɪnəs / (AmE), / brəˈkaɪnəs / (BrE)
A genus of beetles in the family Carabidae, commonly known as bombardier beetles, noted for their ability to eject a chemical spray as a defense mechanism.
genus of bombardier beetles

Examples

Brachinus species are famous for their ability to eject hot chemical sprays to deter predators.
Biologists collected several Brachinus specimens from the riverbank for study.
The defensive mechanism of Brachinus beetles has inspired research into chemical engineering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bombardier beetle - a beetle that can eject a chemical spray as a defense

Etymology

From Greek 'brachys' (short), possibly referring to body shape; used as genus name in entomology.

2. NOUN

/ brəˈkaɪnəs / (AmE), / brəˈkaɪnəs / (BrE)
A genus of beetles known as bombardier beetles, characterized by their ability to eject a hot, noxious spray as a defense mechanism.
genus of bombardier beetles

Examples

The Brachinus genus includes several species of beetles famous for their explosive defensive mechanism.
Scientists collected a Brachinus specimen near the riverbank for further study.
Many members of the Brachinus genus are recognized by their bright coloration and chemical defenses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bombardier beetle - a beetle known for ejecting a hot chemical spray for defense

Etymology

From New Latin *Brachinus*, based on Greek *brachys* (short), used in taxonomy.
