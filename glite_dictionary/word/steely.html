steely

1. ADJ

/ ˈstiːli / (AmE), / ˈstiːli / (BrE)
having a hardness, strength, or resolve similar to that of steel; resembling steel in quality, especially in being tough or determined.
hard or tough like steel

Examples

He gave her a steely glance that revealed his determination.
Her steely resolve helped her overcome all obstacles.
The officer's steely expression showed no sign of fear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resolute - admirably purposeful, determined, and unwavering
  • Antonym: soft - easy to mold, cut, compress, or fold; not hard or firm to the touch

Etymology

Formed from 'steel' + '-y' meaning 'like or characterized by'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈstiːli / (AmE), / ˈstiːli / (BrE)
Having a hard, strong, or resolute quality, like that of steel; cold and determined.
hard, resolute like steel

Examples

She met his gaze with a steely determination that showed she would not back down.
The manager gave a steely look to silence the noisy team.
Despite the chaos, her voice remained steely and calm throughout the crisis.

Synset Examples

steely eyes
steely nerves like those of a steeplejack

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resolute - determined; unwavering
  • Antonym: soft - lacking firmness or resolve; gentle

Etymology

From steel + -y, denoting resemblance.
