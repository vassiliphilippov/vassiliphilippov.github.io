neuromotor

1. ADJ

/ ˌnʊroʊˈmoʊtər / (AmE), / ˌnjʊərəʊˈməʊtə / (BrE)
relating to the nerves or nerve impulses involved in stimulating muscular movement or controlling motor effectors
relating to nerves and motor function
medical

Examples

The test measured neuromotor responses to different stimuli in the patient's limbs.
Early neuromotor development is critical for a child’s ability to control movement.
The disease interferes with neuromotor coordination, leading to muscle weakness and tremors.

Synset Examples

neuromotor impulses

Etymology

From Greek 'neuro-' (nerve) and Latin 'motor' (mover).
