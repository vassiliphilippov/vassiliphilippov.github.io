stimulant

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪmjələnt / (AmE), / ˈstɪmjʊlənt / (BrE)
a drug or substance that temporarily increases physiological or nervous activity in the body
substance increasing body's activity
medical

Examples

Caffeine is a common stimulant found in coffee, tea, and some soft drinks.
Doctors warn against the overuse of stimulant medications for attention disorders.
Some athletes have been banned for testing positive for banned stimulant substances.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: depressant - a drug that reduces functional or nervous activity
  • Synonym: amphetamine - a synthetic stimulant drug

Etymology

From Latin stimulans, stimulantis, present participle of stimulare 'to goad, incite'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɪmjələnt / (AmE), / ˈstɪmjʊlənt / (BrE)
something that causes increased activity or arouses action or excitement, especially an event or piece of information.
something that arouses action or excitement

Examples

The surprising election results acted as a stimulant for urgent political reform.
A good leader can use criticism as a stimulant to inspire improvement within a team.
The new policy provided a much-needed stimulant to the city's sluggish economy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incentive - something that motivates or encourages someone to do something
  • Antonym: deterrent - something that discourages or prevents an action

Etymology

From Latin 'stimulans', present participle of 'stimulare' (to goad, stimulate).

3. ADJ

/ ˈstɪmjələnt / (AmE), / ˈstɪmjʊlənt / (BrE)
having the property of stimulating or increasing activity, especially of mind or body.
having a stimulating effect
medical

Examples

The doctor warned that stimulant medications could cause insomnia if taken late in the day.
Some athletes use stimulant substances to enhance their performance, though this can be dangerous.
The beverage contains stimulant ingredients that help you stay awake and alert.
