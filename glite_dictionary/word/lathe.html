lathe

1. NOUN

/ leɪð / (AmE), / leɪð / (BrE)
a machine tool for shaping wood, metal, or other materials in which the workpiece is rotated against a fixed cutting tool
a machine for shaping rotating materials

Examples

The craftsman operated the lathe skillfully to create a smooth wooden bowl.
Before starting the project, she made sure the lathe was properly aligned and calibrated.
A metal lathe is essential for precision engineering work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milling machine - a machine tool for shaping metal or other solid materials using rotary cutters

Etymology

From Old English *lǣðe* (a lathe, support, or frame for turning), related to Proto-Germanic *laithō*.
