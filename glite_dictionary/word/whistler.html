whistler

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪslər / (AmE), / ˈwɪslə / (BrE)
a large North American mountain marmot, known for its high-pitched whistling alarm call
large North American mountain marmot

Examples

The hikers spotted a whistler perched on a rock, watching their every move.
A whistler gave a sharp call, warning its colony of the approaching coyote.
During summer, it's common to see whistlers sunning themselves in the alpine meadows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marmot - a type of large ground squirrel found in mountainous areas

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪslər / (AmE), / ˈwɪslə / (BrE)
A large-headed, fast-flying diving duck found in Arctic regions; also called 'goldeneye'.
a type of Arctic diving duck

Examples

The whistler is easily identified by its bright eyes and rapid wingbeats.
During their migration, flocks of whistlers are often seen along northern lakes.
A whistler dove underwater suddenly to catch its prey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goldeneye - a type of diving duck with a golden-colored eye, common in the same regions
