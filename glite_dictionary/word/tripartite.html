tripartite

1. ADJ

/ traɪˈpɑr.taɪt / (AmE), / trɪˈpɑː.taɪt / (BrE)
involving or made between three parties or elements
involving three parties or elements

Examples

The leaders signed a tripartite agreement to promote regional cooperation.
A tripartite division of power existed in the ancient government.
The committee was formed through a tripartite collaboration between universities, industry, and government.

Synset Examples

a tripartite treaty
a tripartite division
a three-way playoff

Related Terms

  • Synonym: threefold - having three parts or elements
  • Degree Lower: bipartite - involving or made between two parties or elements

Etymology

From Latin tripartitus, from tri- (three) + partitus (divided).
