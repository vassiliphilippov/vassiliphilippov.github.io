bootlicker

1. NOUN

/ ˈbuːtˌlɪkər / (AmE), / ˈbuːtˌlɪkə / (BrE)
a person who acts in an excessively obedient or servile manner towards someone in authority, showing exaggerated respect to gain favor
a servile flatterer

Examples

Everyone knew he was a bootlicker who never dared to challenge the boss.
She refused to become a bootlicker just to get promoted.
The politician surrounded himself with bootlickers who always agreed with him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sycophant - a person who flatters someone important for personal gain
  • Synonym: toady - a person who behaves obsequiously to someone important
  • Antonym: rebel - a person who opposes or resists authority

Etymology

Formed from 'boot' + 'licker', referring metaphorically to someone so servile they would lick another's boots.
