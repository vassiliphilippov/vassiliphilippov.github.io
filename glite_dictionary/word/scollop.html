scollop

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɑː.ləp / (AmE), / ˈskɒl.əp / (BrE)
An edible marine bivalve with a fluted, fan-shaped shell, able to swim by expelling water in snapping motions.
edible marine bivalve with fan-shaped shell

Examples

Fresh scollops are often served grilled with lemon and herbs.
The diver found a large scollop resting among the rocks.
She prepared a delicious pasta dish with cream and scollops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scallop - an edible marine bivalve with a fan-shaped shell (main US spelling)

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'scallop', from Old French *escalope* (shell).
