Numida

1. NOUN

/ nuːˈmaɪdə / (AmE), / njuːˈmaɪdə / (BrE)
A genus of African birds in the family Numididae, commonly known as guinea fowl.
genus of African guinea fowl

Examples

The Numida is well-known for its spotted gray plumage and distinctive helmeted head.
Farmers often raise Numida for their eggs and meat in various African countries.
Although native to Africa, Numida can now be found on farms around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guinea fowl - a large African bird with dark-spotted plumage, domesticated for food

Etymology

From Latin Numida (referring to Numidia, an ancient region of North Africa).

2. NOUN

/ nuːˈmaɪdə / (AmE), / njuːˈmiːdə / (BrE)
a genus of birds in the family Numididae, commonly known as the guinea fowls, native to Africa
a genus of African guinea fowl

Examples

The helmeted Numida is the best-known species of guinea fowl.
Scientists classified the specimen under the genus Numida.
Members of Numida are native to Africa but have been introduced elsewhere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guinea fowl - a bird found in Africa, known for its spotted plumage

Etymology

From Latin 'Numida' (a Numidian woman), from Numidia, an ancient region of North Africa.
