rapacity

1. NOUN

/ rəˈpæsəti / (AmE), / rəˈpæsɪti / (BrE)
extreme greed for wealth or food; the quality of being excessively grasping or voracious.
extreme greed or gluttony

Examples

The rapacity of the corrupt officials knew no limits.
Unchecked rapacity led to the depletion of natural resources in the region.
Her rapacity for sweets was well known among her friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greed - intense and selfish desire for something, especially wealth, power, or food
  • Synonym: avarice - extreme greed for wealth or material gain
  • Antonym: generosity - the quality of being kind and generous

Etymology

From Latin 'rapacitas' (greediness), from 'rapax' meaning 'grasping, greedy'.

2. NOUN

/ rəˈpæsəti / (AmE), / rəˈpæsəti / (BrE)
an insatiable or extreme greed for wealth or possessions, often regarded as morally wrong
extreme greed for wealth or possessions

Examples

The rapacity of the corporation led to widespread criticism and legal action.
Historians condemned the conqueror's rapacity as he stripped the land of all its riches.
Unchecked rapacity can undermine trust and stability in any society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greed - intense and selfish desire for something, especially wealth or power
  • Antonym: generosity - the quality of being kind and generous

Etymology

From Latin rapacitas (greed, rapaciousness), from rapax (grasping, greedy).

3. NOUN

/ rəˈpæsəti / (AmE), / rəˈpæsəti / (BrE)
The quality or behavior of subsisting on live prey, especially in reference to predatory animals such as birds of prey.
predatory behavior; subsisting on live prey

Examples

The rapacity of the eagles was observed as they hunted live fish in the lake.
Some species of hawks are known for their rapacity toward smaller birds.
Naturalists have long studied the rapacity of owls in relation to the local mouse population.

Synset Examples

The rapacity of carnivorous animals has been considered by some writers to have had a considerable effect on the distribution and even on the extinction of others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predation - the act of preying upon other animals for food

Etymology

From Latin rapacitas (greediness, rapaciousness), from rapax (snatching, greedy).
