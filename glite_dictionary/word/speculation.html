speculation

1. NOUN

/ ˌspɛkjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌspɛkjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
An idea or theory based on conjecture without firm evidence.
idea or theory without firm evidence

Examples

There was much speculation about who would win the award this year.
His theory remains pure speculation until more evidence can be found.
The company's silence led to wild speculation in the media.

Synset Examples

speculations about the outcome of the election
he dismissed it as mere conjecture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conjecture - an opinion or conclusion formed on the basis of incomplete information

Etymology

From Latin 'speculatio' (observation, contemplation), from 'speculari' (to observe, examine).

2. NOUN

/ ˌspɛkjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌspɛkjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
An idea or opinion formed without complete evidence; a guess or conjecture.
guess or opinion with incomplete evidence

Examples

There has been much speculation about who will win the election.
His theory is based on speculation, not on solid scientific evidence.
Rumors and speculation quickly spread through the media after the announcement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conjecture - an opinion or conclusion formed on the basis of incomplete information

Etymology

From Latin speculatio (observation), from speculari (to observe).

deep and continuous contemplation or musing on a subject, especially regarding abstract or philosophical matters
deep contemplation or philosophical thought

Examples

Philosophers often engage in lengthy speculation on questions that cannot be answered by science alone.
The book is the result of years of speculation and reflection on human consciousness.

Synset Examples

the habit of meditation is the basis for all real knowledge

Related Terms

Etymology

From Latin speculatio (observation, contemplation), from speculari (to observe).

4. NOUN

/ ˌspɛk.jʊˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌspɛk.jʊˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
an investment involving considerable risk with the possibility of substantial profit
risky investment for possible profit

Examples

Buying shares in the startup was a bold speculation that eventually paid off.
Real estate speculation can lead to big gains but comes with serious risks.
Many people lost money through reckless speculation during the market boom.

Synset Examples

he knew the stock was a speculation when he bought it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: investment - the act of putting money into something to make a profit
  • Synonym: gamble - an undertaking with uncertain outcome, often involving risk
  • Antonym: sure thing - an investment with virtually guaranteed return, opposite of speculation

Etymology

From Latin speculatio (contemplation, observation), from speculari (to observe).
