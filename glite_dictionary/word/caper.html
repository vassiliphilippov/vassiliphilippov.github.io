caper

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪpər / (AmE), / ˈkeɪpə / (BrE)
any of several Mediterranean shrubs of the genus Capparis, especially Capparis spinosa, whose flower buds are used as a condiment or garnish
a plant of the genus Capparis

Examples

The caper bush produces edible flower buds commonly used in Mediterranean cuisine.
She planted a caper in her herb garden to harvest the flavorful buds.
The leaves of the caper are sometimes used in salads in parts of Italy.

Etymology

From Latin capparis, from Greek kapparis (the caper bush).
