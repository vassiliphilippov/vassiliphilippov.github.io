restorative

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈstɔr.ə.tɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈstɒr.ə.tɪv / (BrE)
A device or agent used to restore health or treat injury or disease.
device for treating injury or disease

Examples

After the surgery, the doctor recommended a restorative to aid the patient's recovery.
Many dental procedures involve the use of a specialized restorative to repair damaged teeth.
The hospital invested in advanced restoratives for orthopedic rehabilitation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remedy - a medicine or treatment for illness or injury

Etymology

From Latin restōrāre (to restore).

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈstɔr.ə.tɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈstɒr.ə.tɪv / (BrE)
Having the ability to make someone feel better or stronger, especially after illness or fatigue; promoting recovery or health.
promoting recovery or health

Examples

A good night's sleep can have a remarkably restorative effect on your mood and energy.
She drank a restorative herbal tea to help her recover from the cold.
The spa offered a range of restorative treatments designed to help guests relax and heal.

A medicine or agent that restores health, strength, or well-being.
medicine that restores health

Examples

A warm drink at night was considered a traditional restorative in some cultures.
The herbal restorative quickly revived her spirits after a tiring day.

Related Terms
