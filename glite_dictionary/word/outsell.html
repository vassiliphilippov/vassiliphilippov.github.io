to outsell

1. VERB

/ aʊtˈsɛl / (AmE), / aʊtˈsɛl / (BrE)
To sell more of a product or service than someone or something else.
sell more than another

Examples

The new smartphone managed to outsell all previous models within its first month.
Despite its higher price, Brand A continued to outsell Brand B in most markets.
If we want to outsell the competition, we need a better marketing strategy.

Synset Examples

This salesman outsells his colleagues

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to outperform - to do better than someone or something

Etymology

From prefix 'out-' meaning 'more than' + 'sell'; modelled after similar formations like 'outdo'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01108788-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ aʊtˈsɛl / (AmE), / aʊtˈsɛl / (BrE)
to sell in greater quantities or numbers than a rival product or brand
sell more than a competitor

Examples

The new phone model has outsold all its competitors this year.
Despite a major advertising campaign, the older version continues to outsell the latest release.
That novel outsold every other book in the store last month.

Synset Examples

The new Toyota outsells the Honda by a wide margin

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sell - to exchange a product or service for money
  • Antonym: to undersell - to sell goods at a lower price than a competitor

Etymology

from 'out-' (surpassing) + 'sell', meaning to sell better than another

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01108915-v
Synset POS: v
