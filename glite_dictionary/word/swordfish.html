swordfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔrdˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈsɔːdˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
The flesh of the swordfish, served as steaks or fillets, commonly eaten as food.
meat of swordfish served as food

Examples

Grilled swordfish is a popular dish in Mediterranean cuisine.
She ordered a swordfish steak with a side of vegetables.
Many restaurants offer fresh swordfish during the summer months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tuna - flesh of tuna used as food
  • Synonym: salmon - flesh of salmon used as food

Etymology

From sword + fish, referring to the fish's long, sword-like bill.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɔrdˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈsɔːdˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a large marine fish with a long, flat, sword-shaped upper jaw, found in warm seas and known for its speed and ability to warm parts of its body
a large fish with a sword-like jaw

Examples

Fishermen hope to catch a giant swordfish off the coast tonight.
The swordfish uses its sharp bill to slash at smaller fish when hunting.
She ordered grilled swordfish at the seafood restaurant for dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marlin - large marine fish with a long, pointed snout

Etymology

From 'sword' + 'fish', referring to the fish's long, sword-like snout.
