mucopolysaccharide

1. NOUN

/ ˌmjuːkoʊˌpɑːliˈsækəˌraɪd / (AmE), / ˌmjuːkəʊˌpɒliˈsækəraɪd / (BrE)
a type of complex polysaccharide containing amino groups, found mainly as a component of connective tissue
complex compound in connective tissue
medical

Examples

He is studying the biological functions of mucopolysaccharide in human tissue.
Certain diseases are caused by defects in mucopolysaccharide metabolism.
The connective tissues are rich in mucopolysaccharide content.

Etymology

From Latin 'mucus' (slime) + 'poly-' (many) + 'saccharide' (a sugar compound).
