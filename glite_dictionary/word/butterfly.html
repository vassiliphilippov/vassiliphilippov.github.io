butterfly

1. VERB

/ ˈbʌt̬ɚˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈbʌtəˌflaɪ / (BrE)
(of a person or thing) to flutter or move lightly and irregularly like a butterfly.
move or flutter like a butterfly

Examples

She watched the papers butterfly to the floor in the breeze.
As the petals detached, they seemed to butterfly gently through the air.
His hands butterflied across the keyboard, dancing from key to key.
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01904149-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʌt̬ɚˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈbʌtəflaɪ / (BrE)
An insect typically active during the day, with a slender body, knobbed antennae, and broad, colorful wings.
diurnal insect with colorful wings

Examples

We watched a beautiful butterfly land on the sunflower in the garden.
A butterfly flapped its wings gently before taking off toward the trees.
Children chased butterflies across the meadow on a sunny afternoon.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: moth - nocturnal insect related to butterflies, often dull-colored

Etymology

Old English 'buttorfleoge', from 'buttor' (butter) + 'fleoge' (fly), possibly referring to the pale yellow color of some species.

Source: WordNet

3. VERB

/ ˈbʌt̬ərˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈbʌtəˌflaɪ / (BrE)
to cut and spread open (meat, fish, etc.) so that it lies flat, especially before cooking
cut and spread open (for cooking)

Examples

To cook the chicken evenly, first butterfly the breast by slicing it almost in half and opening it flat.
The recipe instructs you to butterfly the shrimp before grilling them on skewers.
She carefully butterflied the pork chop to make stuffing easier.

Synset Examples

butterflied shrimp

Etymology

Named for the resemblance of the opened food to a butterfly's wings.

4. NOUN

/ ˈbʌt̬ɚˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈbʌtəflaɪ / (BrE)
A swimming stroke performed by throwing both arms forward together out of the water while the legs kick up and down in a dolphin-like motion.
a swimming stroke with both arms moving together

Examples

Michael Phelps won a gold medal in the 100-meter butterfly at the Olympics.
She has been practicing her butterfly to improve her speed in competitions.
The butterfly requires great strength and coordination to master.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freestyle - a category of swimming stroke where any method is allowed, usually the front crawl
  • Synonym: breaststroke - a swimming stroke performed on the chest with the arms moving in halves of a heart shape and the legs doing a frog kick
  • Synonym: backstroke - a swimming stroke executed while lying on the back
Source: WordNet

flirt, act amorously without intent
