washboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑʃˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈwɒʃˌbɔːd / (BrE)
A broad plank or protective board along the side (gunwale) of a boat or ship, designed to prevent water from splashing over the side.
protective plank along a boat's side

Examples

The sailor secured the washboard to prevent water from splashing onto the deck.
Without a proper washboard, the fishing boat took on water during the storm.
They replaced the old washboard before setting out on their next voyage.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɑʃˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈwɒʃˌbɔːd / (BrE)
a board with a corrugated surface, used for scrubbing clothes by hand
board with a ribbed surface for washing clothes

Examples

In the past, people used a washboard and soap to clean their laundry by hand.
She found an old washboard at the antique shop and decided to display it in her kitchen.
After scrubbing the shirt on the washboard, the stains finally started to disappear.
