Dianthus caryophyllus

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈænθəs ˌkɛr.i.əˈfɪl.əs / (AmE), / daɪˈænθəs ˌkær.i.əˈfɪl.əs / (BrE)
a species of Eurasian plant with pink to purple-red, spice-scented, usually double flowers, widely cultivated in many varieties and colours; commonly known as the carnation.
species of flower; the carnation

Examples

The garden was filled with the sweet fragrance of Dianthus caryophyllus in full bloom.
Horticulturists often breed new varieties of Dianthus caryophyllus for their colorful and fragrant flowers.
He presented her with a bouquet of pink Dianthus caryophyllus to celebrate her birthday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carnation - another common name for Dianthus caryophyllus

Etymology

From Latin 'dianthus', from Greek 'dianthos' (divine flower), and Latin 'caryophyllus' (clove-scented).
