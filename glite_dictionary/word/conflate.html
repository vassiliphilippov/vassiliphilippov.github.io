to conflate

1. VERB

/ kənˈfleɪt / (AmE), / kənˈfleɪt / (BrE)
to combine two or more things, such as ideas, pieces of information, or texts, into one; to blend together different elements, often in a way that creates confusion or blurs distinctions.
to combine things into one

Examples

The author tends to conflate historical facts with personal opinions in her articles.
It is important not to conflate correlation with causation when interpreting scientific studies.
Many people conflate the two issues, but they are actually quite distinct.

Synset Examples

The colors blend well

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blend - to mix different substances so they combine smoothly
  • Antonym: separate - to keep or set apart; not mix

Etymology

From Latin conflare (to blow together, fuse), from com- (together) + flare (to blow).
