swamphen

1. NOUN

/ ˈswɑːmˌfɛn / (AmE), / ˈswɒmˌfɛn / (BrE)
a medium-sized aquatic bird of the genus Porphyrio, recognized by its large feet, frontal shield, and resemblance to a domestic hen
a water bird of the genus Porphyrio

Examples

The purple swamphen is commonly found in freshwater wetlands across Europe and Asia.
A swamphen strutted along the water's edge, its bright shield glinting in the sunlight.
Many birdwatchers visit the reserve to catch a glimpse of the rare black-backed swamphen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moorhen - a related rail-like water bird of the genus Gallinula
  • Synonym: coot - an aquatic bird of the rail family, often mistaken for moorhens and swamphens

Etymology

From 'swamp' + 'hen', referring to its habitat and chicken-like appearance.
