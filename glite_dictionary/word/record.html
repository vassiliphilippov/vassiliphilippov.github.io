record

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛkərd / (AmE), / ˈrɛkɔːd / (BrE)
something that provides permanent evidence or information about past events, such as a document, photograph, or audio recording
item serving as evidence of past events

Examples

The museum has a record of every artifact in its collection.
Her diary served as a record of her travel experiences.
There is no official record of that meeting in the archives.

Synset Examples

the film provided a valuable record of stage techniques

Related Terms

  • Synonym: document - a written or printed paper giving information or evidence
  • Synonym: archive - a collection of historical documents or records
  • Synonym: evidence - anything used to demonstrate or prove something

Etymology

From Latin 'recordari' (to recall, remember), via Old French 'recorder'.
