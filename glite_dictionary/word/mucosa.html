mucosa

1. NOUN

/ mjuˈkoʊsə / (AmE), / mjuˈkəʊsə / (BrE)
The membrane that secretes mucus and lines body cavities or passages that communicate with the exterior, such as the mouth, nose, and digestive tract.
membrane lining passages, secreting mucus
medical

Examples

The inner lining of the stomach is covered by mucosa that protects it from stomach acid.
Damage to the nasal mucosa can cause frequent nosebleeds.
Doctors took a biopsy of the colon mucosa to check for signs of disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mucous membrane - the membrane that secretes mucus and lines body cavities exposed to the outside environment

Etymology

From Latin 'mucosa membrana' meaning 'mucous membrane'.
