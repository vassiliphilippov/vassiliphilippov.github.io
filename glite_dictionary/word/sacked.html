sacked

1. ADJ

/ sækt / (AmE), / sækt / (BrE)
(of a place) having been robbed and destroyed by force and violence, especially by an invading army.
robbed and destroyed by force

Examples

The sacked village was left in ruins after the advancing army moved through.
Heritage sites across the region were tragically sacked during the conflict.
Archaeologists discovered artifacts among the remains of the once sacked city.

Synset Examples

the raped countryside

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devastated - completely destroyed or ruined, especially emotionally or physically
  • Synonym: pillaged - robbed violently, especially during a war
  • Antonym: intact - not damaged or impaired in any way

Etymology

From the verb 'to sack', ultimately from French 'sac' (bag), used in the sense of plunder, or from Italian 'sacco', referring to plundering during war.
