aluminium

1. NOUN

/ əˈluːmɪnəm / (AmE), / ˌaljʊˈmɪniəm / (BrE)
a silvery-white, lightweight, and ductile metallic element, commonly used in manufacturing and construction
a lightweight silvery metallic element (chemical)

Examples

Cans made from aluminium are light and easily recycled.
The window frames are constructed from durable aluminium.
Bauxite is the main ore used to extract aluminium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aluminum - American spelling of aluminium

Etymology

From Latin 'alumen' (alum), with the modern form established in early 19th-century scientific nomenclature.
