bowhead

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈbəʊˌhɛd / (BrE)
a large Arctic whale with a massive, bow-shaped head and a baleen mouth, known for its thick blubber and long lifespan
large Arctic baleen whale

Examples

The bowhead is uniquely adapted to survive in the icy waters of the Arctic.
Scientists have studied the migration patterns of the bowhead for many years.
A bowhead can live for more than 200 years, making it one of the longest-lived mammals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bowhead whale - common name for the bowhead, large Arctic baleen whale

Etymology

From the shape of its arched upper jaw resembling a bow.
