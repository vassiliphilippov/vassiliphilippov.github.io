mariachi

1. NOUN

/ məˈriːɑːtʃi / (AmE), / məˈriːatʃi / (BrE)
a group of Mexican street musicians, typically playing traditional music with stringed instruments and trumpets
Mexican street music group

Examples

A mariachi band performed lively songs in the town square during the festival.
The restaurant hired a mariachi to entertain guests on Saturday nights.
We danced all evening to traditional tunes played by the local mariachi.

Etymology

From Mexican Spanish 'mariachi', of uncertain origin, possibly from French 'mariage' (marriage) as such music was originally played at weddings.
