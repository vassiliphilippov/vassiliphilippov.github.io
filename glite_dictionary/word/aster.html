aster

1. NOUN

/ ˈæstər / (AmE), / ˈɑːstə / (BrE)
a star-shaped structure in the cytoplasm of a cell, consisting of radiating fibers that form around the centrosome during cell division (mitosis)
star-shaped cell structure in mitosis
medical

Examples

During mitosis, an aster helps to organize the spindle fibers.
The formation of the aster is essential for proper chromosome separation.
Scientists observed irregular asters in the mutant cells under the microscope.

Etymology

From Greek 'astēr' meaning 'star'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæstər / (AmE), / ˈɑːstə / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Aster or related genera, with star-shaped, often brightly colored flowers, typically flowering in the fall
a plant with star-shaped flowers, genus Aster

Examples

Every autumn the aster blooms add vibrant color to the garden.
She planted several varieties of aster along the front walkway.
The purple aster is a favorite among pollinators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daisy - a common wildflower with a yellow center and white petals

Etymology

From Greek astēr (star), referring to the star-like shape of the flowers.
