choke-full

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃoʊk fʊl / (AmE), / ˈtʃəʊk fʊl / (BrE)
Packed completely full to capacity; brimming or overflowing.
packed full to capacity
archaic

Examples

The pantry was choke-full of jars and preserves after the harvest.
Her suitcase was choke-full with clothes, leaving no room for souvenirs.
The theatre was choke-full on opening night, with every seat taken.

Synset Examples

chowder chockablock with pieces of fish

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chock-full - packed completely full; crammed

Etymology

Variant of 'chock-full', probably influenced by 'choke' and Middle English 'chokkeful', meaning stuffed or crammed full.
