to liberalize

1. VERB

/ ˈlɪbərəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈlɪbərəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make or become more open, free, or less restricted, especially by relaxing laws, rules, or controls
make or become more open or free

Examples

Several countries decided to liberalize their trade policies to encourage economic growth.
The government plans to liberalize restrictions on foreign investment next year.
After the election, the administration moved quickly to liberalize social and cultural policies.

Synset Examples

The laws liberalized after Prohibition

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to restrict - to limit or control something
  • Synonym: to relax - to make (a rule or restriction) less strict

Etymology

From French 'libéraliser', from 'libéral' (liberal) + '-iser' (-ize).

2. VERB

/ ˈlɪbərəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈlɪbərəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something, especially laws or rules, less strict and more open to individual freedom
make laws or rules less strict

Examples

The government decided to liberalize its immigration policies to attract more skilled workers.
Efforts to liberalize the economy have led to an increase in foreign investment.
Several countries are planning to liberalize their trade regulations in the coming years.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to tighten - make laws or rules more strict

Etymology

From 'liberal' + '-ize', modeled after French 'libéraliser'.

Synset POS: v
