Frederick William I

1. PROPN

/ ˈfrɛdərɪk ˈwɪljəm ðə ˈfɜrst / (AmE), / ˈfrɛdrɪk ˈwɪljəm ði ˈfɜːst / (BrE)
(1688–1740) King of Prussia from 1713 to 1740, noted for reforming and strengthening the Prussian army.
King of Prussia, 1713–1740

Examples

Frederick William I is remembered for transforming the Prussian army into a highly disciplined force.
The reign of Frederick William I marked a turning point in Prussian military history.
Historians credit Frederick William I with laying the groundwork for Prussia's later European dominance.
