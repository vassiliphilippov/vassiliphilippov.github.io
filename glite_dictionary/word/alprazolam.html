alprazolam

1. NOUN

/ ælˈpræzəlæm / (AmE), / ælˈpræzəlæm / (BrE)
a medication of the benzodiazepine class, used to treat anxiety and panic disorders; commonly known by the trade name Xanax
a benzodiazepine used for anxiety and panic
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed alprazolam to help control her anxiety symptoms.
Because alprazolam can cause drowsiness, do not drive until you know how it affects you.
Some patients develop dependence if they use alprazolam for an extended period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Xanax - trade name for alprazolam, used for anxiety and panic disorders
  • Synonym: diazepam - another benzodiazepine medication used for anxiety
