wainscotting

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪnskətɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈweɪnskətɪŋ / (BrE)
wooden paneling that lines the lower part of the walls of a room; collectively, the wainscoted surfaces of walls.
wooden paneling on lower walls

Examples

The old library's wainscotting gave it a warm, traditional atmosphere.
Dark oak wainscotting ran along the hallway, contrasting with the pale plaster above.
He decided to remove the damaged wainscotting and paint the entire wall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paneling - decorative or protective wooden panels used for covering walls

Etymology

From 'wainscot' (wooden panelling), from Middle Dutch 'wagenschot' (wagon-board).

2. NOUN

/ ˈweɪnskətɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈweɪnskətɪŋ / (BrE)
wooden panels or panelling that line the lower part of the interior walls of a room
wooden wall panelling

Examples

The living room was decorated with elegant wainscotting painted in a deep green.
They decided to remove the old wainscotting to give the room a more modern appearance.
Classic wainscotting can add warmth and character to any hallway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paneling - wooden panels fitted to interior walls

Etymology

From Middle Dutch 'wagenschot' (wall-board), via earlier English 'wainscot'.
