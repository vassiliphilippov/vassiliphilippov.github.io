twitch

1. NOUN

/ twɪtʃ / (AmE), / twɪtʃ / (BrE)
a sudden, small, involuntary muscle movement, often caused by nerves or stress
sudden involuntary muscle movement

Examples

He felt a twitch in his eyelid right before the interview.
The doctor explained that the twitch in my hand was nothing to worry about.
Her nervousness showed in the occasional twitch of her mouth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spasm - a sudden involuntary muscular contraction
  • Degree Higher: cramp - a painful involuntary contraction of a muscle

Etymology

Probably imitative of a sudden pulling movement; recorded since the 14th century.

2. VERB

/ twɪtʃ / (AmE), / twɪtʃ / (BrE)
to move or pull something with a sudden, quick motion, often involuntarily
move or pull with sudden motion

Examples

His eye began to twitch nervously during the interview.
She twitched the curtain aside to look outside.
The horse twitched its ears at every little sound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jerk - to move with a sudden sharp motion
  • Synonym: spasm - an involuntary sudden muscular movement

Etymology

Probably imitative of the quick, jerky movement involved.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01451371-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ twɪtʃ / (AmE), / twɪtʃ / (BrE)
to make a sudden, quick movement that is often small and uncontrolled, especially as a result of nervousness or a reflex
make a sudden, quick movement

Examples

She felt her eyelid twitch involuntarily as she grew nervous.
His fingers began to twitch after he drank too much coffee.
The horse's ear would twitch at the slightest sound.

Synset Examples

The patient's legs were jerking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jerk - to move with a quick, sharp, sudden motion

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin, first recorded in Middle English.

Source: WordNet

4. VERB

/ twɪtʃ / (AmE), / twɪtʃ / (BrE)
to move or toss something with a sudden, sharp motion that causes it to turn over in the air
move or toss sharply; turn over

Examples

She twitched the pancake into the air with a practiced flick of her wrist.
The fisherman twitched the line sharply, causing the lure to tumble.
He twitched his hat off, flipping it high above his head.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flip - to turn over with a sudden, quick movement
  • Synonym: jerk - to move or cause to move with a sudden quick motion
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

5. VERB

/ twɪtʃ / (AmE), / twɪtʃ / (BrE)
to make a sudden, quick, and usually uncontrolled movement; to move with a small, sharp jerk
make a sudden, jerky motion

Examples

He felt his right eyelid twitch during the meeting, but tried to ignore it.
Whenever she gets nervous, her fingers unconsciously twitch.
His mouth twitched slightly as he tried to hold back a smile.

Synset Examples

his face is twitching

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jerk - to move with a sudden, sharp motion
  • Synonym: spasm - a sudden involuntary muscular contraction
  • Antonym: freeze - to become motionless or stop moving suddenly

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin, first recorded in the 14th century.
