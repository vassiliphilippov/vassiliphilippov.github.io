family Kalotermitidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæməli ˌkæləˌtɜrmɪˈtaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæməli ˌkælətɜːmɪˈtiːdaɪ / (BrE)
A family of primitive termites found mainly in warm regions.
termite family in warm regions

Examples

The family Kalotermitidae includes species commonly known as drywood termites.
Members of the family Kalotermitidae are typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.
Scientists are studying the unique nesting habits of the family Kalotermitidae termites.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: termite - a social insect known for eating wood

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Kalotermes' (type genus) and '-idae' (family suffix in zoological taxonomy).
