Sison amomum

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪsən əˈmoʊməm / (AmE), / ˈsɪsən əˈməʊməm / (BrE)
a slender herb native to western Europe and the Mediterranean, with parsley-like leaves, white flowers, and aromatic seeds
a Mediterranean aromatic herb with parsley-like leaves

Examples

Sison amomum grows along country roads and is often mistaken for wild parsley.
The seeds of Sison amomum are sometimes used as a spice in regional Mediterranean cuisines.
Botanists surveyed the area for native herbs, including Sison amomum.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus name Sison and the species name amomum, meaning 'spice' or 'aromatic plant.'
