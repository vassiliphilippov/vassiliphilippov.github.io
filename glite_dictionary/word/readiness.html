readiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛd.i.nəs / (AmE), / ˈrɛd.i.nɪs / (BrE)
prompt willingness to do something; eagerness or preparedness.
prompt willingness or eagerness

Examples

She accepted the new responsibilities with surprising readiness.
His readiness to help others was admired by everyone in the department.
We appreciated their readiness to resolve the issue quickly.

Synset Examples

readiness to continue discussions
they showed no eagerness to spread the gospel
they disliked his zeal in demonstrating his superiority
he tried to explain his forwardness in battle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eagerness - keen or enthusiastic willingness to do something
  • Antonym: reluctance - unwillingness or hesitation to do something

Etymology

From Old English 'rǣdness,' meaning 'preparedness.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛdinəs / (AmE), / ˈrɛdinɪs / (BrE)
the state of being fully prepared for something, especially for use or action
state of being prepared

Examples

The troops maintained a high level of readiness throughout the exercise.
Her readiness to help made her a valued team member.
Regular drills keep the emergency services in a constant state of readiness.

Synset Examples

putting them in readiness
their preparation was more than adequate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preparedness - the quality or state of being prepared; readiness
  • Antonym: unpreparedness - the state of not being ready or prepared

Etymology

From ready + -ness; 'ready' from Old English 'rǣdege' or 'ræde', meaning prepared.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14054590-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈrɛd.i.nəs / (AmE), / ˈrɛd.i.nɪs / (BrE)
a natural ease or facility in doing something, especially in speech or movement
natural ease or facility

Examples

She answered every question with a readiness that impressed the interviewer.
His readiness in conversation made everyone feel at ease around him.
The pianist's readiness at the keyboard delighted the entire audience.

Synset Examples

they conversed with great facility
a happy readiness of conversation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: facility - ease or fluency in doing something
  • Antonym: awkwardness - lack of ease or natural ability

Etymology

From Old English 'rǣdness' (preparedness, readiness).
