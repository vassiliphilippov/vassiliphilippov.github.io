perceived

1. ADJ

/ pərˈsiːvd / (AmE), / pəˈsiːvd / (BrE)
felt or recognized by instinct or inference rather than by direct observation or evidence
felt or recognized without direct evidence

Examples

There was a perceived threat in the room even though nothing was visible.
The resignation stemmed from a perceived lack of support by management.
Her perceived slight influenced how she treated her colleagues.

Synset Examples

the felt presence of an intruder
a sensed presence in the room raised goosebumps on her arms
a perceived threat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensed - detected or felt without direct evidence
  • Antonym: real - actually existing or present

Etymology

Past participle of 'perceive', from Latin 'percipere' (to seize, understand).

2. ADJ

/ pərˈsivd / (AmE), / pəˈsiːvd / (BrE)
noticed or detected, especially through the senses, rather than directly measured or proven; observed or regarded in a particular way.
noticed or detected, often by the senses

Examples

There was a perceived difference in the way the two groups responded to the treatment.
The perceived risk was much higher than the actual risk involved.
Noise levels in the room were higher than perceived by most listeners.

Synset Examples

a perceived difference in temperature

Related Terms

  • Synonym: observed - noticed or perceived through actual observation
  • Antonym: unnoticed - not observed or detected; not perceived

Etymology

From Latin 'percipere' (to seize, understand) via Old French 'percevoir'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01613997-s
Synset POS: s
