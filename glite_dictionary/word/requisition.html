requisition

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛkwəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛkwɪˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
an official or formal demand for something, especially by a military or public authority for temporary use
official demand by authority

Examples

The army issued a requisition for additional supplies during the crisis.
A formal requisition was sent to the office requesting backup resources.
During wartime, the government made a requisition of private vehicles for military use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: request - an act of asking for something politely or formally
  • Synonym: order - a commanding instruction given by someone in authority
  • Antonym: surrender - the yielding of something to another's control, especially under authority or force

Etymology

From Latin requisitio, from requirere (to ask, seek).

2. VERB

/ ˌrɛkwəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛkwɪˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
to formally request the use or supply of something, especially for official or military purposes
to make a formal request

Examples

The department decided to requisition additional computers for the new staff.
During the emergency, the army requisitioned several trucks from local businesses.
We need to requisition office supplies before the end of the week.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: request - to ask for something formally or politely

Etymology

From Latin requisitio (search, demand), from requirere (to seek, ask for).

3. VERB

/ ˌrɛkwəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛkwɪˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
to officially demand and take for use, especially by military or public authority, typically for public service
to officially demand and take, esp. by authority

Examples

During the war, the army would often requisition private vehicles for military use.
The government could requisition land needed for building new roads.
Supplies were requisitioned by officials to support the emergency relief effort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to seize - to take hold of something suddenly or by force
  • Synonym: to commandeer - to officially take possession or control, especially for military purposes

Etymology

From Latin requisitio (a searching, demand), from requirere (to seek, ask for).

4. NOUN

/ ˌrɛkwəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛkwɪˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of seizing property that belongs to someone else and holding it until profits pay the demand for which it was seized, usually by legal or governmental authority
the seizing of property to pay a demand
legal

Examples

During the war, the government ordered the requisition of several factories to produce military supplies.
The requisition of private land was challenged in court by the owners.
Revolutionary governments sometimes resort to the requisition of essential goods in times of crisis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confiscation - the action of taking someone's property with authority

Etymology

From Latin *requisitio* (a searching, demanding), from *requirere* (to seek, demand).
