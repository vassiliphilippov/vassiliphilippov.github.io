ensuing

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈsuː.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈsjʊː.ɪŋ / (BrE)
happening or occurring immediately after and as a result of something that happened before.
following as a result

Examples

The storm and its ensuing floods caused widespread damage across the region.
In the ensuing chaos, several important documents were lost.
Her speech sparked heated discussions in the ensuing weeks.

Synset Examples

ensuing events confirmed the prediction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subsequent - coming after something in time; following
  • Antonym: preceding - coming before something in time or order

Etymology

From the verb 'ensue', which comes from Old French 'ensuivre', from Latin 'insequi' (to follow closely).
