schnorrer

1. NOUN

/ ʃnɔɹɚ / (AmE), / ʃnɒɹə / (BrE)
a person who habitually seeks to obtain things, especially money or food, from others without offering anything in return; a scrounger
person who habitually seeks handouts

Examples

Everyone knows at least one schnorrer who never contributes to group dinners but always shows up to eat.
He developed a reputation as a schnorrer, always asking friends for favors without ever reciprocating.
Tired of being treated like a schnorrer, she finally offered to pay for lunch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrounger - someone who takes advantage of others' generosity
  • Synonym: freeloader - a person who lives at others' expense
  • Antonym: benefactor - a person who gives help or support

Etymology

From Yiddish *shnorrer*, from German *schnorren* (to beg).
