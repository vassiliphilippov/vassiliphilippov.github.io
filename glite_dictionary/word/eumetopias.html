Eumetopias

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuːməˈtoʊpiəs / (AmE), / ˌjuːmɛˈtəʊpiəs / (BrE)
A genus of sea lions that includes the Steller sea lion (Eumetopias jubatus).
genus of sea lions

Examples

The Eumetopias genus is best represented by the Steller sea lion found along the northern Pacific coasts.
Scientists are studying the genetics of the Eumetopias to understand its evolutionary history.
Conservation efforts for the Eumetopias focus primarily on protecting Steller sea lion habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Steller sea lion - the largest species of sea lion, member of Eumetopias genus

Etymology

From Greek eu- (good, well) + metopion (forehead).

2. NOUN

/ juːˌmɛtəˈpaɪəs / (AmE), / juːˌmɛtəˈpaɪəs / (BrE)
A genus of sea lions that includes the Steller sea lion.
genus of sea lions; includes Steller sea lion

Examples

The Steller sea lion belongs to the genus Eumetopias and is the largest member of the otariid family.
Marine biologists are studying the population decline within Eumetopias along the North Pacific coast.
The taxonomic classification of Eumetopias helps scientists understand evolutionary relationships among sea lions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Steller sea lion - A species of sea lion in the genus Eumetopias

Etymology

From Greek 'eu-' (good) + 'metopias' (forehead), referring to the animal's prominent forehead.
