inconsistency

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.kənˈsɪs.tən.si / (AmE), / ˌɪn.kənˈsɪs.tən.si / (BrE)
The quality or state of being inconsistent, especially a lack of uniformity, agreement, or steadiness among things or parts.
lack of consistency or uniformity

Examples

The report drew attention to the inconsistency in the test results.
Her argument was weakened by an obvious inconsistency in her statements.
There is some inconsistency between the two versions of the story.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: consistency - the quality of always behaving or performing in a similar way
  • Synonym: contradiction - a situation or statement that is opposed to another

Etymology

From Latin 'inconsistentia'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.kənˈsɪs.tən.si / (AmE), / ˌɪn.kənˈsɪs.tən.si / (BrE)
A situation in which two or more statements, beliefs, or actions are so different that they cannot all be true or compatible at the same time.
contradiction; lack of logical agreement

Examples

There is a clear inconsistency between his most recent statement and his previous testimony.
The lawyer pointed out the inconsistency in the witness's version of events.
Logical inconsistency undermines the validity of the argument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contradiction - a situation in which inconsistent elements are present

Etymology

From Latin inconsistentia (lack of agreement) via inconsistent.
