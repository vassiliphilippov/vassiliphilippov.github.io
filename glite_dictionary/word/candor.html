candor

1. NOUN

/ ˈkændər / (AmE), / ˈkændə / (BrE)
the quality of being open, honest, and straightforward in expression or attitude
honesty; frankness

Examples

Her candor during the interview impressed everyone on the hiring panel.
People appreciated his candor when discussing difficult issues in the meeting.
With remarkable candor, she admitted her mistakes to her friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frankness - the quality of being open, honest, and direct
  • Synonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful or sincere
  • Antonym: deceit - the practice of deceiving; dishonesty

Etymology

From Latin 'candor' (whiteness, brightness, openness), from 'candēre' (to shine).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkændər / (AmE), / ˈkændə / (BrE)
the quality of being open and honest; the ability to make judgments free from discrimination or dishonesty
quality of openness and honesty

Examples

She spoke with remarkable candor about the challenges she had faced.
His candor made him a valuable and trusted advisor.
I appreciate your candor in answering those difficult questions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frankness - the quality of being open, honest, and direct
  • Antonym: insincerity - lack of honesty or openness

Etymology

From Latin 'candor' (whiteness, brightness, purity, openness).
