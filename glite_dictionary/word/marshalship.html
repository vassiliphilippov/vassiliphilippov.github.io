marshalship

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrʃəlˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈmɑːʃəlˌʃɪp / (BrE)
the office, rank, or position of a marshal
the position of a marshal

Examples

After years of service, his elevation to the marshalship marked the peak of his career.
The responsibilities attached to the marshalship were extensive and demanding.
She declined the offer of marshalship, preferring a quieter life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marshal - a high-ranking officer or official

Etymology

Formed from 'marshal' + '-ship' (denoting office or position)
