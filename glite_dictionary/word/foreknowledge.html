foreknowledge

1. NOUN

/ fɔrˈnɑlɪdʒ / (AmE), / fɔːˈnɒlɪdʒ / (BrE)
knowledge of an event or action before it actually happens.
knowledge before something happens

Examples

The prophet claimed to have foreknowledge of the disaster that would befall the city.
With her uncanny foreknowledge, she avoided the trouble before it started.
Scientists do not have foreknowledge of earthquakes, making them difficult to predict.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prescience - the fact of knowing something before it takes place
  • Antonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge or awareness

Etymology

From Old English 'fore' (before) + 'knowledge'.
