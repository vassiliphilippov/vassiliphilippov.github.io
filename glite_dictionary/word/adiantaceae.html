Adiantaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪdiˌænˈteɪsi.i / (AmE), / ˌadɪ anˈtiːsiːiː / (BrE)
a former family of ferns, now usually included in the family Pteridaceae, used in some classifications for certain genera, including maidenhair ferns
a family of ferns in some classifications

Examples

Many botanists now treat Adiantaceae as a subfamily within Pteridaceae.
The genus Adiantum was historically placed in the Adiantaceae family.
Taxonomic revisions have resulted in the reclassification of Adiantaceae in modern botany.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pteridaceae - a family of ferns in most modern classifications
  • Synonym: Polypodiaceae - a family of ferns in some older classifications

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Adiantum + -aceae (denoting plant family).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæd.i.ænˈteɪ.si.iː / (AmE), / ˌæd.i.ænˈteɪ.siː / (BrE)
a family of ferns that includes the maidenhair ferns and related genera, formerly classified separately in some systems
a family of ferns (maidenhair and relatives)

Examples

Some botanists classify the maidenhair ferns in the Adiantaceae family.
Recent research suggests that Adiantaceae should be merged with Pteridaceae.
The Adiantaceae are easily recognized by their delicate, fan-shaped fronds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Polypodiaceae - a larger family of ferns including Adiantaceae in some systems
  • Synonym: Pteridaceae - another family with which Adiantaceae is sometimes merged

Etymology

From genus Adiantum (maidenhair fern) + Latin suffix -aceae (denoting plant families).
