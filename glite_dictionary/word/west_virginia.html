West Virginia

1. PROPN

/ wɛst vəˈdʒɪn.jə / (AmE), / wɛst vəˈdʒɪn.i.ə / (BrE)
A state in the east central United States, with Charleston as its capital.
U.S. state; capital Charleston

Examples

They spent their summer hiking in West Virginia's scenic mountains.
The coal industry has historically been important to West Virginia's economy.
Charleston, the capital of West Virginia, hosts a vibrant annual arts festival.

Etymology

Named to distinguish it from the adjacent state of Virginia when it separated during the American Civil War (1863).
