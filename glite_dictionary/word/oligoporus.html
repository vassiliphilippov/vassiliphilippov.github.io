Oligoporus

1. NOUN

/ oʊˌlɪɡəˈpɔrəs / (AmE), / əˌlɪɡəˈpɔːrəs / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Polyporaceae, comprising about 10 species that often grow on wood and are characterized by their pore-bearing fruiting bodies.
genus of fungi in Polyporaceae family

Examples

The genus Oligoporus is known for its wood-decaying species found in temperate forests.
Researchers identified a new species in the Oligoporus genus on decaying birch trees.
Several Oligoporus fungi are studied for their potential role in forest ecosystems.

Etymology

From Greek 'oligos' (few) and 'poros' (pore), referring to the pore structure of the fungus.
