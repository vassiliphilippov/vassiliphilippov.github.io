knee-deep

1. ADV

/ niːˈdiːp / (AmE), / niːˈdiːp / (BrE)
(of liquid or another substance) reaching as high as one's knees.
reaching up to the knees

Examples

After the heavy rainfall, we were wading knee-deep through the flooded street.
The children played knee-deep in the pile of autumn leaves.
The workers stood knee-deep in mud as they tried to clear the blocked drain.

Synset Examples

we were standing knee-deep in the water

2. ADJ

/ ˌniːˈdiːp / (AmE), / ˌniːˈdiːp / (BrE)
reaching up to the knee or slightly below, especially of water, mud, or other substances
reaching up to the knee

Examples

The children waded through the knee-deep snow on their way to school.
After the heavy rain, the field was knee-deep in water.
We trudged through knee-deep mud to reach the campsite.

Etymology

Formed from 'knee' (Old English 'cnēow') and 'deep' (Old English 'dēop').

3. ADJ

/ niːˈdiːp / (AmE), / niːˈdiːp / (BrE)
deeply involved or immersed in an activity or situation, either literally or figuratively
deeply involved or immersed

Examples

We were knee-deep in paperwork by the end of the day.
She found herself knee-deep in financial problems after losing her job.
The children were knee-deep in snow, laughing and playing together.
