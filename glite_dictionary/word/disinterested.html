disinterested

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈɪntrəstɪd / (AmE), / dɪsˈɪntrɪstɪd / (BrE)
not influenced by personal feelings or interests; impartial.
unbiased; impartial

Examples

The judge remained disinterested throughout the trial to ensure a fair verdict.
A disinterested party is often chosen to mediate business disputes.
It's important that referees are entirely disinterested in the outcome of the match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unbiased - showing no prejudice for or against something; impartial
  • Synonym: impartial - treating all rivals or disputants equally
  • Antonym: biased - unfairly prejudiced for or against someone or something
  • Antonym: partial - favoring one side in a dispute above the other

Etymology

From dis- (expressing removal) + interested; first recorded in the 17th century.
