gulch

1. NOUN

/ ɡʌltʃ / (AmE), / ɡʌltʃ / (BrE)
a narrow and steep-sided ravine or gorge, typically with a stream running through it
narrow ravine with a stream

Examples

The hikers discovered a hidden gulch with clear water running at its base.
Coyotes are often heard at night in the deep gulch near the ranch.
Early settlers built their cabins along the gulch for easy access to water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ravine - a deep, narrow gorge with steep sides
  • Synonym: canyon - a deep gorge, typically one with a river flowing through it

Etymology

Probably of American English origin, perhaps related to Scottish gulch (to gulp, swallow greedily).
