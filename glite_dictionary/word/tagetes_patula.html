Tagetes patula

1. NOUN

/ təˈdʒiːtiːz pəˈtuːlə / (AmE), / təˈdʒiːtiːz pəˈtʃuːlə / (BrE)
A species of marigold, known as the French marigold, native to Mexico and Guatemala, with strongly scented, bushy annual growth and orange or yellow flower heads marked with red.
species of marigold (French marigold)

Examples

The vibrant blooms of Tagetes patula make it a popular choice for garden borders.
Botanists recommend Tagetes patula for natural pest control due to its strong scent.
Many gardeners plant Tagetes patula to attract pollinators and add color to their flower beds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: French marigold - common name for Tagetes patula, an ornamental garden plant

Etymology

From Latin, Tagetes (the marigold genus), named after the Etruscan deity Tages; patula meaning 'spreading' in Latin.
