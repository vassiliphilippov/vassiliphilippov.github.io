Myrica

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈriːkə / (AmE), / mɪˈrɪkə / (BrE)
a genus of deciduous aromatic shrubs or small trees, commonly known as bayberry or wax myrtle
genus of aromatic shrubs or small trees

Examples

Myrica species, such as the wax myrtle, are cultivated for their aromatic leaves.
The berries of some Myrica plants are used to produce wax for making candles.
Botanists have discovered a new Myrica shrub in the coastal region.

Etymology

From Latin Myrica, from Greek myrikē (tamarisk).
