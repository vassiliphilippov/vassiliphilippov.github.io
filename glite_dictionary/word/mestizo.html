mestizo

1. NOUN

/ mɛˈstizoʊ / (AmE), / mɛˈstiːzəʊ / (BrE)
a person of mixed European and Native American ancestry, especially in Latin America
person of mixed European and Native American ancestry

Examples

The majority of the country's population identifies as mestizo.
Her family history reflects her mestizo heritage, blending Indigenous and European roots.
During the colonial era, mestizo communities emerged throughout Latin America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mulatto - a person of mixed white and black ancestry

Etymology

From Spanish mestizo, from Latin mixticius (mixed).
