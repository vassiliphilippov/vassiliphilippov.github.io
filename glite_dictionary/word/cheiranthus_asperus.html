Cheiranthus asperus

1. NOUN

/ kaɪˈræŋθəs ˈæspərəs / (AmE), / kaɪˈrænθəs ˈæspərəs / (BrE)
A biennial or short-lived perennial plant of the prairie rocket group, with orange-yellow flowers, native to western North America and found as far east as Minnesota and Kansas; sometimes classified in the genus Cheiranthus.
prairie rocket plant with orange-yellow flowers

Examples

The wildflowers included Cheiranthus asperus, which brightened the prairie with its orange hue.
Botanists discovered a cluster of Cheiranthus asperus thriving at the edge of the forest.
In spring, Cheiranthus asperus blooms alongside other native prairie plants.

Etymology

From Greek 'cheir' (hand) and 'anthos' (flower), referring to the shape of the flower; species name 'asperus' from Latin meaning 'rough'.
