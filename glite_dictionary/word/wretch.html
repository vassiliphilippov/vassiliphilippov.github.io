wretch

1. NOUN

/ rɛtʃ / (AmE), / rɛtʃ / (BrE)
a person who is in a very unhappy or unfortunate state, often eliciting pity or compassion from others.
unfortunate or pitiable person

Examples

The poor wretch was shivering in the cold, with nothing but rags to cover him.
She helped the injured wretch who had collapsed on the side of the road.
After the disaster, many wretches wandered the streets in search of aid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outcast - a person who has been rejected by society
  • Synonym: pauper - a very poor person

Etymology

From Old English wrecca (exile, stranger, adventurer), related to wrecan (to drive out).

2. NOUN

/ rɛtʃ / (AmE), / rɛtʃ / (BrE)
a person who behaves in a wicked, despicable, or unpleasant way
wicked or despicable person

Examples

The cruel wretch stole food from the hungry children.
Only a true wretch would betray his closest friend for money.
After his crimes were revealed, everyone in the town shunned the wretch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: villain - a person who is evil or wicked
  • Synonym: scoundrel - a dishonest or unscrupulous person
  • Antonym: saint - a person of great virtue, regarded as good or holy

Etymology

From Old English wrecca (exile, one who is driven out) and related to Old Norse ræki (outcast).
