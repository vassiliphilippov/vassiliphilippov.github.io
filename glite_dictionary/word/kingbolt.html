kingbolt

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪŋˌboʊlt / (AmE), / ˈkɪŋbəʊlt / (BrE)
A main bolt that forms the pivotal joint for steering in the front axle of a vehicle such as a carriage or truck.
main steering bolt in vehicle axle

Examples

The mechanic replaced the worn kingbolt to restore proper steering.
A broken kingbolt can make driving dangerous by affecting the front axle alignment.
Older trucks often require regular kingbolt maintenance for safe operation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kingpin - the main pivot in the steering mechanism of a vehicle

Etymology

From 'king' (principal) + 'bolt', indicating its role as the main bolt in the mechanism.
