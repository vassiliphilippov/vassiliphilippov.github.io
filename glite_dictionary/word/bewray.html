to bewray

1. VERB

/ bɪˈreɪ / (AmE), / bɪˈreɪ / (BrE)
(archaic) To reveal or disclose (something), often unintentionally.
reveal or disclose (archaic)
archaic

Examples

He dared not bewray the secret entrusted to him by his king.
Her trembling voice did bewray her anxiety to the gathered council.
The letter, found in haste, would bewray their conspiracy if discovered.

Synset Examples

Her smile betrayed her true feelings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to betray - to reveal unintentionally or treacherously
  • Antonym: to conceal - to keep something secret or hidden

Etymology

From Old English 'be-' (intensive prefix) + 'wrēgan' (to accuse, reveal).
