Ebonics

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈbɑːnɪks / (AmE), / ɪˈbɒnɪks / (BrE)
A nonstandard variety of American English characteristically spoken by many African Americans, also known as African American Vernacular English (AAVE).
a variety of American English spoken by many African Americans

Examples

Linguists have extensively studied Ebonics to understand its unique grammar and usage.
Some educators advocate incorporating Ebonics into the classroom to better support African American students.
The debate over recognizing Ebonics as a distinct language variety continues in academic circles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: African American Vernacular English - a variety of English spoken by many African Americans; also known as AAVE

Etymology

A blend of 'ebony' (black) and 'phonics' (sounds). Coined in 1973.
