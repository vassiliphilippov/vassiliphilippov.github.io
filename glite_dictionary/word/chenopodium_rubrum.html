Chenopodium rubrum

1. NOUN

/ tʃiːˌnoʊˈpoʊdiəm ˈruːbrəm / (AmE), / kɛˌnɒˈpəʊdiəm ˈruːbrəm / (BrE)
A species of flowering plant in the amaranth family, known as red goosefoot, native to Eurasia and naturalized in North America.
species of weed, red goosefoot

Examples

Chenopodium rubrum often grows in disturbed soils along riverbanks and roadsides.
The leaves of Chenopodium rubrum turn a distinct red color in autumn.
Botanists documented the spread of Chenopodium rubrum across various North American regions.

Etymology

From Latin: genus Chenopodium, meaning 'goose foot' (referring to the leaf shape), and rubrum, meaning 'red'.
