to deride

1. VERB

/ dɪˈraɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈraɪd / (BrE)
to express contempt for someone or something; to ridicule or mock
to mock or ridicule

Examples

The critics openly derided the film for its lack of originality.
He was derided by his classmates for his strange ideas, but he didn't let it discourage him.
While some may deride new technologies, others embrace innovation.

Synset Examples

He derided his student's attempt to solve the biggest problem in mathematics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mock - to tease or laugh at in a scornful or contemptuous manner
  • Antonym: praise - to express warm approval or admiration of

Etymology

From Latin 'deridere' (to laugh at, scorn), from 'de-' (down) + 'ridere' (to laugh).
