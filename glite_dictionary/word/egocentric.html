egocentric

1. NOUN

/ ˌiːɡoʊˈsɛntrɪk / (AmE), / ˌiːɡəʊˈsɛntrɪk / (BrE)
a person who is excessively self-centered and shows little concern for the feelings or needs of others
self-centered person

Examples

She soon realized that Mark was an egocentric who cared little for other people's feelings.
An egocentric often dominates conversations and dismisses the opinions of others.
The psychologist described the patient as an extreme egocentric lacking empathy for those around him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-centered - concerned only with one's own needs
  • Antonym: altruist - a person who cares about the welfare of others

Etymology

From Latin ego (I) + Greek kentrikos (center)

2. ADJ

/ ˌiːɡoʊˈsɛntrɪk, ˌɛɡoʊˈsɛntrɪk / (AmE), / ˌiːɡəʊˈsɛntrɪk, ˌɛɡəʊˈsɛntrɪk / (BrE)
Thinking only of oneself, without regard for the feelings or desires of others; self-centered.
self-centered; concerned only with oneself

Examples

In a team environment, being egocentric often leads to poor collaboration and conflict.
Her egocentric behavior made it difficult for her to empathize with her friends.
The novel's main character is so egocentric that he rarely considers anyone else's needs.

Synset Examples

Selfish men were … trying to make capital for themselves out of the sacred cause of civil rights
The merely egoistic satisfactions of fame are easily nullified
However, because of their strong-minded nature, they are often perceived as difficult and egoistical individuals.
I will never forgive her for her egocentric and shameful conduct towards Gilliatt
What I can't believe is that people are so stupid and self-centered that they think they HAVE to comment on things they don't like.
We're asked to see Rachel as this spoilt, self-centred woman but the rest of them are just as bad, if not worse.
As the crying baby, we express purely the self-serving thoughts that arise in our minds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: selfish - lacking consideration for others; concerned chiefly with one's own personal profit or pleasure
  • Antonym: altruistic - showing a disinterested and selfless concern for the well-being of others; unselfish

Etymology

From Latin 'ego' (I) + 'centric' from Greek 'kentron' (center).
