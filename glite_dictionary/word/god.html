god

1. NOUN

/ ɡɑd / (AmE), / ɡɒd / (BrE)
an image or object that is worshipped as a deity or considered to embody divine power
an object or image worshipped as a deity

Examples

In ancient times, people would carve a god from stone and place it in temples.
For some, the golden calf was a tangible god worthy of devotion.
The villagers prayed to a wooden god for a good harvest.

Synset Examples

thou shalt not make unto thee any graven image
money was his god

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idol - an image or representation of a god used as an object of worship

Etymology

From Old English 'god', related to Old High German 'got' and Old Norse 'guð'.

2. NOUN

/ ɡɑd / (AmE), / ɡɒd / (BrE)
A being believed to have supernatural powers, worshipped as controlling some part of the world or personifying a force, especially in polytheistic religions.
supernatural being worshipped as a deity

Examples

Ancient Greeks believed that each aspect of life was watched over by a different god.
He prayed to the river god for a safe journey.
Many cultures have a god of war who is worshipped before battle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deity - a god or goddess; a divine being
  • Synonym: goddess - a female deity or god

Etymology

Old English god; related to German Gott and Dutch god.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09528550-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ɡɑd / (AmE), / ɡɒd / (BrE)
A man who is greatly admired for his superior abilities or qualities, often compared to a deity by others.
a man of extraordinary or divine qualities

Examples

The fans worshipped the actor as a god for his incredible talent on stage.
In the world of tech giants, some CEOs are treated like a god by their followers.
To his teammates, he was a god on the basketball court, performing feats no one else could.

Synset Examples

he was a god among men

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idol - a person who is greatly admired or loved

Etymology

From Old English 'god', of Germanic origin; related to Dutch 'god' and German 'Gott'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10152827-n
Synset POS: n
