God

1. NOUN

/ ɡɑd / (AmE), / ɡɒd / (BrE)
(in monotheistic religions) the all-powerful, all-knowing, and perfect being who is believed to be the creator and ruler of the universe, and the object of worship.
the all-powerful creator worshipped in monotheistic religions

Examples

Many people pray to God for guidance and strength in difficult times.
The concept of one God is central to Christianity, Islam, and Judaism.
Some people question the existence of God, while others have unwavering faith.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deity - a god or goddess; a divine being
  • Degree Lower: goddess - a female deity; a goddess

Etymology

From Old English *God*, related to German *Gott*, Dutch *God*, originally from Proto-Germanic *gudan*.
