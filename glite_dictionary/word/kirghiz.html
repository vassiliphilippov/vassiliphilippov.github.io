Kirghiz

1. NOUN

/ kɪrˈɡiz / (AmE), / kɪəˈɡiːz / (BrE)
the Turkic language spoken primarily by the Kirghiz people in Kyrgyzstan and neighboring regions.
a Turkic language; language of the Kirghiz

Examples

She studied the Kirghiz language as part of her research on Central Asian cultures.
The book was translated from Kirghiz into Russian and English.
Many folk tales have been preserved in Kirghiz for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kyrgyz - the Turkic language spoken by the Kyrgyz/Kirghiz people

Etymology

From Russian 'Kirghiz', ultimately from the self-designation 'Kyrgyz'.

2. NOUN

/ kɪrˈɡiz / (AmE), / kɪəˈɡiːz / (BrE)
a member of a Turkic-speaking ethnic group native to Kyrgyzstan and neighboring regions of Central Asia
member of a Central Asian ethnic group

Examples

The Kirghiz have a rich tradition of epic poetry and storytelling.
A Kirghiz man welcomed us into his yurt in the mountains of Kyrgyzstan.
Many Kirghiz still practice nomadic herding in remote areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kyrgyz - a member of a Central Asian people; alternative spelling of Kirghiz

Etymology

From Russian Киргиз (Kirgiz), itself from Kyrgyz кыргыз (kyrgyz).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09755744-n
Synset POS: n
