Alcelaphus

1. NOUN

/ æl.səˈlæf.əs / (AmE), / al.sɪˈlæf.əs / (BrE)
A genus of large African antelopes known as hartebeests, characterized by elongated heads and sloping backs.
genus of African antelopes (hartebeests)

Examples

The Alcelaphus genus includes several distinct species found throughout Africa.
Researchers studied the behavior of Alcelaphus in the savannah ecosystem.
Hartebeests belonging to the Alcelaphus genus are known for their speed and endurance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hartebeest - large African antelope with a sloping back

Etymology

From Greek *alkē* (strength) and *elaphos* (stag, deer).
