to Zionize

1. VERB

/ ˈzaɪ.əˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈzaɪ.ə.naɪz / (BrE)
to interpret or present events, especially historical ones, from a Zionist perspective or according to Zionist principles
interpret using a Zionist perspective

Examples

Some historians argue that recent books tend to Zionize the narrative of that period.
The controversy centered on whether the museum would Zionize its special exhibition.
He accused the author of trying to Zionize the legacy of key events.

Synset Examples

He criticized attempts to Zionize historical events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to politicize - to give a political character to something or interpret it politically

Etymology

Formed from 'Zion' (a symbol for Jerusalem or the Jewish homeland) and the suffix '-ize', meaning 'to make' or 'to apply'.
