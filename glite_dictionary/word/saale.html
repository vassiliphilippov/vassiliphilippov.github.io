Saale

1. PROPN

/ ˈzɑːlə / (AmE), / ˈzɑːlə / (BrE)
a river in central Germany that flows north to join the Elbe River
river in central Germany

Examples

The Saale valley is famous for its picturesque landscapes and vineyards.
After heavy rain, the Saale often overflows its banks in several towns.
Many historic castles can be found along the course of the Saale in Germany.

Etymology

From German 'Saale', the local name for the river.
