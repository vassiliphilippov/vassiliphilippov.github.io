tenseness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛn.sə.nəs / (AmE), / ˈtɛn.sə.nɪs / (BrE)
A state of mental or emotional strain, anxiety, or suspense.
mental or emotional strain

Examples

The tenseness in his voice made it clear he was worried about the interview.
After days of uncertainty, the tenseness in the office was almost palpable.
Deep breathing exercises can help reduce tenseness caused by stress.

Synset Examples

he suffered from fatigue and emotional tension
stress is a vasoconstrictor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tension - Mental or emotional strain; stress
  • Antonym: relaxation - The state of being free from tension and anxiety

Etymology

From 'tense' (meaning stretched or tight) + '-ness', forming a noun of state.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛn.sə.nəs / (AmE), / ˈtɛn.sə.nəs / (BrE)
The physical state of being stretched or strained; a condition in which muscles or the body are tight and not relaxed.
state of being tense or tight

Examples

He noticed the tenseness in her shoulders as she prepared to speak.
After hours at the computer, a tenseness crept into his neck muscles.
Yoga helped reduce the tenseness in my back after a stressful week.

Synset Examples

it places great tension on the leg muscles
he could feel the tenseness of her body

Related Terms

  • Antonym: relaxation - the state of being free from tension or anxiety
  • Synonym: tension - the state of being stretched tight

Etymology

From 'tense' + '-ness', where 'tense' derives from Latin 'tensus', past participle of 'tendere' (to stretch).
