phylliform

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɪlɪˌfɔrm / (AmE), / ˈfɪlɪfɔːm / (BrE)
Having the shape or form of a leaf.
having the shape of a leaf

Examples

The fossil showed a phylliform imprint, suggesting it was once a leaf-bearing plant.
Some insects have evolved phylliform wings to better camouflage themselves among foliage.
The mineral's phylliform crystals spread out like tiny green leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foliaceous - resembling or consisting of leaves

Etymology

From Greek 'phyllon' (leaf) + Latin 'forma' (form, shape).
