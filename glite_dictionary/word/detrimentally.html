detrimentally

1. ADV

/ ˌdɛtrəˈmɛntəli / (AmE), / ˌdɛtrɪˈmɛntəli / (BrE)
In a way that is damaging or harmful to someone or something.
in a damaging or harmful way

Examples

The decision could affect the community detrimentally if not carefully considered.
Using harsh chemicals may impact the environment detrimentally.
The merger will detrimentally influence competition in the market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harmfully - in a harmful manner

Etymology

From 'detrimental' + '-ly', ultimately from Latin detrimentum (loss, damage).
