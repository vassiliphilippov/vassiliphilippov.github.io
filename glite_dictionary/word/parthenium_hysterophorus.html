Parthenium hysterophorus

1. NOUN

/ pɑːrˈθiːniəm ˌhɪstɛrəˈfɔːrəs / (AmE), / pɑːˈθiːnɪəm ˌhɪstərəˈfɔːrəs / (BrE)
a tropical American annual weed with small radiate heads of white flowers, now widely spread as an invasive species in many regions
an invasive weed with white flowers

Examples

Parthenium hysterophorus has become a major invasive weed in parts of Asia and Africa.
Farmers are concerned about the rapid spread of Parthenium hysterophorus in their fields.
The government launched a campaign to control Parthenium hysterophorus due to its harmful effects on crops and livestock.

Etymology

From Latin genus Parthenium (from Greek parthenion, a plant name) and species hysterophorus (bearing a womb), used in botanical nomenclature.
