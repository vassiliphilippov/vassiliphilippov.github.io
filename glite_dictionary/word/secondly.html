secondly

1. ADV

/ ˈsɛkəndli / (AmE), / ˈsɛkəndli / (BrE)
used to introduce the second point or reason in a list or argument.
introducing a second point

Examples

Secondly, we need to address the concerns raised by the staff.
The report is well structured; secondly, it offers practical solutions.
First, we will discuss the budget; secondly, the project timeline.

Synset Examples

second, we must consider the economy

Related Terms

  • Antonym: firstly - used to introduce the first point or reason
  • Degree Higher: thirdly - used to introduce the third point or reason

Etymology

Formed from 'second' plus the adverbial suffix '-ly'.
