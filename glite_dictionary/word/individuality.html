individuality

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪndəˌvɪdʒuˈælədi / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˌvɪdjʊˈalɪti / (BrE)
the qualities that make one person or thing different from all others; the distinct personality or character of an individual
distinct personality; unique character

Examples

She has always been proud of her individuality and refuses to follow every trend.
The school's uniform policy was criticized for suppressing students' individuality.
Artists often express their individuality through unique styles and creative works.

Synset Examples

you can lose your identity when you join the army

Related Terms

  • Synonym: identity - the fact of being who or what a person or thing is
  • Antonym: conformity - compliance with standards or rules; behavior in accordance with socially accepted conventions

Etymology

From Medieval Latin individualitas, from Latin individuus (indivisible) + -alitas (-ality).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪndɪvɪdʒuˈælədi / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪvɪdjʊˈalɪti / (BrE)
the quality or character that distinguishes one person or thing from others; the state of being individual
quality of being individual; distinctiveness

Examples

Her art expresses a strong sense of individuality and originality.
In this uniformed society, individuality can sometimes be hard to maintain.
The teacher encouraged each student to develop their own individuality.

Synset Examples

so absorbed by the movement that she lost all sense of individuality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uniqueness - the quality of being unique

Etymology

From Medieval Latin individualitas, from individuus (indivisible, individual).
