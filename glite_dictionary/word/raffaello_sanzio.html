Raffaello Sanzio

1. PROPN

/ rəˌfɑːloʊ ˈsɑːntsioʊ / (AmE), / ra.faˈɛl.lo ˈsan.tsi.o / (BrE)
(1483–1520) An Italian painter and architect whose works embody the ideals of the High Renaissance.
Italian High Renaissance painter and architect

Examples

Raffaello Sanzio was renowned for his masterful frescoes in the Vatican.
Art historians often compare the techniques of Raffaello Sanzio to those of Michelangelo and Leonardo da Vinci.
The graceful composition of Raffaello Sanzio's paintings influenced generations of artists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Raphael - Italian High Renaissance painter and architect (another common name for Raffaello Sanzio)

Etymology

Italian personal name; Latinized as Raphael Santi (or Sanzio), known in English as Raphael.
