Sterculia apetala

1. NOUN

/ stɚˈkuːliə æˈpɛtələ / (AmE), / stɜːˈkjuːlɪə æˈpɛtələ / (BrE)
a large deciduous tree native to Central and South America, notable for its densely leafy crown and bare trunk; known as the Panama tree and believed to have given Panama its name
large tropical tree (the Panama tree)

Examples

The shade of the Sterculia apetala provides welcome relief in the hot Panamanian climate.
Botanists studied the unique trunk of the Sterculia apetala for its adaptive qualities.
The Sterculia apetala is often planted in urban parks in tropical countries for its large canopy.

Etymology

Genus name from Latin 'Sterculia', named after Sterculius, Roman god of manure; species name from Latin 'apetala' meaning 'without petals'.
