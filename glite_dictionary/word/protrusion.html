protrusion

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈtruːʒən / (AmE), / prəˈtruːʒən / (BrE)
the act or state of sticking out or extending beyond a surface or boundary
the act of sticking out

Examples

The broken bone caused a visible protrusion beneath the skin.
There was a small protrusion on the wall where a nail once was.
The lizard's tongue shot out in a quick protrusion to catch the insect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: projection - something that juts out from a surface
  • Antonym: recession - the act of receding or moving back

Etymology

From Latin protrudere (to thrust forward), via Middle French and Late Latin.

2. NOUN

/ proʊˈtruːʒən / (AmE), / prəˈtruːʒən / (BrE)
a part that extends outward beyond its surroundings; something that sticks out from a surface
something that sticks out

Examples

The protrusion on the wall made it difficult to move the furniture.
A small protrusion at the base of the statue gave it a unique look.
The doctor noticed a protrusion along the patient's spine during the examination.

Synset Examples

the gun in his pocket made an obvious bulge
the hump of a camel
he stood on the rocky prominence
the occipital protuberance was well developed
the bony excrescence between its horns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bulge - a rounded swelling or protuberance
  • Synonym: protuberance - something that protrudes or sticks out from a surface
  • Antonym: recess - a hollow or indentation in a surface, the opposite of protrusion

Etymology

From Latin protrudere (to thrust forward), via protrusio.
