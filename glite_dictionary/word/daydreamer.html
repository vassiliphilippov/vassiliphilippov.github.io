daydreamer

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪˌdriːmɚ / (AmE), / ˈdeɪˌdriːmə / (BrE)
a person who often indulges in idle or fanciful thoughts, typically while awake and not paying attention to reality
person given to daydreaming

Examples

As a child, she was called a daydreamer by her teachers.
It's easy for a daydreamer to lose track of time in class.
He was always the daydreamer in the group, imagining fantastic adventures instead of doing homework.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dreamer - a person who dreams or has visionary ideas

Etymology

From 'daydream' + '-er', first attested in the 19th century.
