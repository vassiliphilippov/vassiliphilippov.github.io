disciform

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɪs.ɪ.fɔrm / (AmE), / ˈdɪs.ɪ.fɔːm / (BrE)
having a round or oval shape like a disc
shaped like a disc or plate

Examples

The doctor noted a disciform lesion on the patient's back during the examination.
Some algae exhibit a disciform structure that allows them to float on the water's surface.
The shield had a disciform design, making it easy to identify among the collection.

Synset Examples

a disciform skin lesion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discoid - resembling a disc; disc-shaped

Etymology

From Latin 'discus' (disk, plate) + '-form' (having the form of).
