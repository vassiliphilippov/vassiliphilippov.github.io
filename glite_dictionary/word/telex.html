telex

1. NOUN

/ ˈtiːˌlɛks / (AmE), / ˈtiːˌlɛks / (BrE)
a teleprinter device used for sending written messages over a telegraph network, operating much like a typewriter
teleprinter for sending written messages via telegraph

Examples

The company received the urgent order by telex late last night.
Before email became common, businesses often relied on telex machines for international communication.
He sent the contract via telex to ensure quick delivery to their partners overseas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teleprinter - an electromechanical typewriter used to send and receive typed messages over wires

Etymology

From 'TELegraph EXchange', coined as a blend of 'telegraph' and 'exchange'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04411335-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈtiː.lɛks / (AmE), / ˈtiː.lɛks / (BrE)
to send a message by telex machine; to communicate using the telex system
send a message by telex

Examples

They telexed the contract to the European office early Monday morning.
Can you telex the results to our head office as soon as possible?
Before the advent of email, companies often telexed urgent messages across continents.

Synset Examples

We telexed the information to our sister company

Etymology

From TELeprinter EXchange, an early international communication system.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00792627-v
Synset POS: v
