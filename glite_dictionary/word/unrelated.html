unrelated

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.rɪˈleɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.rɪˈleɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
not connected by kinship; not belonging to the same family by birth or marriage.
not related by kinship

Examples

Although the two girls share a last name, they are completely unrelated.
He was raised by people who were unrelated to him by blood or marriage.
Many believe that adoption brings together unrelated people to form a family.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: related - connected by kinship; belonging to the same family

Etymology

Formed by prefixing un- (not) to related, from Latin relatus (brought back, related).

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnrɪˈleɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnrɪˈleɪtɪd / (BrE)
having no logical, causal, or familial connection; not connected or associated.
not connected; lacking relation

Examples

The two incidents are completely unrelated and occurred months apart.
Your question seems unrelated to the topic we are discussing.
She brought up an unrelated subject during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: related - connected by logic, cause, or family
  • Synonym: connected - having a connection; linked

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (not) + 'related', from Latin 'relatus', past participle of 'referre' (to bring back).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01981804-a
Synset POS: a
