telegnostic

1. ADJ

/ ˌtɛlɪɡˈnɑstɪk / (AmE), / ˌtɛlɪɡˈnɒstɪk / (BrE)
able to acquire knowledge of distant events without the use of normal sensory mechanisms; relating to perception of distant events through paranormal means.
perceiving distant events paranormally

Examples

Some claim that telegnostic abilities allow them to learn about distant events without physical communication.
Her supposed telegnostic experiences were a subject of great interest to parapsychologists.
The novel features a protagonist with telegnostic insights that defy explanation by science.

Etymology

From Greek tele- (far) + gnōsis (knowledge).
