expatriation

1. NOUN

/ ɛkˌspeɪtriˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɛksˌpætriˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of leaving one's native country to live in another
leaving one's native country to live abroad

Examples

After years of considering expatriation, she finally moved to France to start a new chapter of her life.
Political unrest led to the mass expatriation of citizens seeking safer environments.
Their decision to pursue expatriation was influenced by better job opportunities abroad.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emigration - leaving one's own country to settle elsewhere
  • Antonym: repatriation - the act of returning to one's own country

Etymology

From Medieval Latin expatriatio (banishment), from Latin ex- (out of) + patria (native country).

2. NOUN

/ ɛkˌspeɪtriˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɛkˌspætrɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of forcing someone to leave their native country; banishment or exile
the act of expelling someone from their native country

Examples

The government ordered the expatriation of citizens accused of collaborating with the enemy.
During the 19th century, expatriation was sometimes used as a punishment for political dissidents.
The sudden expatriation of the entire family left them with little hope of returning home soon.

Synset Examples

men in exile dream of hope
his deportation to a penal colony
the expatriation of wealthy farmers
the sentence was one of transportation for life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deportation - the act of removing someone from a country by official order
  • Synonym: exile - the state of being barred from one's native country
  • Antonym: repatriation - the act of returning someone to their own country

Etymology

From Medieval Latin expatriatio, from Latin ex- (out of) + patria (native country).
