materialist

1. NOUN

/ məˈtɪriəlɪst / (AmE), / məˈtɪəriəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that only physical matter exists and that all phenomena, including consciousness, can be explained by interactions of matter
person believing only matter exists

Examples

As a committed materialist, she argued that consciousness arises entirely from brain activity.
He described himself as a materialist who rejected the existence of the supernatural.
Throughout the debate, the materialist position was contrasted with that of the dualist.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: idealist - person who believes reality is fundamentally mental or spiritual

Etymology

From Latin 'materialis' (of matter) + '-ist' (denoting adherence or following); ultimately from 'materia' (matter).

2. NOUN

/ məˈtɪriəlɪst / (AmE), / məˈtɪəriəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who values material possessions and physical comfort above spiritual or intellectual things
person who values material possessions

Examples

As a materialist, he judged success by the size of his house and the luxury of his car.
Many people criticize modern society for encouraging materialist attitudes.
Though once a materialist, she later found greater fulfillment in helping others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consumerist - a person who advocates or emphasizes the consumption of goods and services
  • Antonym: spiritualist - a person who is concerned with spiritual rather than material matters

Etymology

From Latin 'materialis' (of matter), via French 'matérialiste'.
