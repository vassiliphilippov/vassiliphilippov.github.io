Amorphophallus paeonifolius

1. NOUN

/ əˌmɔr.foʊˈfæl.əs ˌpiː.ə.nɪˈfoʊ.li.əs / (AmE), / əˌmɒ.fəˈfæl.əs ˌpiː.ə.nɪˈfəʊ.li.əs / (BrE)
A tropical plant of southeastern Asia, especially the Philippines, known for its foul-smelling flower and edible tuber, often used as a food source.
tropical Asian plant with edible tuber

Examples

Farmers in the region cultivate Amorphophallus paeonifolius for its large edible tubers.
The flower of Amorphophallus paeonifolius is known for its strong, putrid odor.
In Filipino cuisine, Amorphophallus paeonifolius is sometimes used in traditional stews.

Etymology

From Greek *amorphos* (shapeless) and *phallos* (penis), referring to the plant's shape; *paeonifolius* refers to leaves resembling those of the peony.
