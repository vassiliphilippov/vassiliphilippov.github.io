casern

1. NOUN

/ kəˈzɝːn / (AmE), / kəˈzɜːn / (BrE)
A building or group of buildings used as military barracks, especially in a garrison town.
military barracks, especially in a garrison town

Examples

The soldiers were quartered in a large casern on the edge of the town.
After the parade, everyone returned to the casern for lunch and rest.
The old casern has now been converted into a museum about military history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barracks - a building or group of buildings used to house soldiers

Etymology

From French *caserne* (barracks), from Italian *caserna* (hut, barracks).
