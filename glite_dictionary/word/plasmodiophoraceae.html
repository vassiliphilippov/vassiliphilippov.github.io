Plasmodiophoraceae

1. NOUN

/ plæzˌmoʊdiˌɑfəˈreɪsiː / (AmE), / plazˌməʊdiˌɒfəˈriːsiː / (BrE)
A family of fungi that often cause abnormal growth or swelling (hypertrophy) in seed plants.
family of plant-parasitic fungi

Examples

Several species within the Plasmodiophoraceae family infect crops and cause significant agricultural losses.
The clubroot disease in cabbages is attributed to a member of the Plasmodiophoraceae.
Researchers studied the life cycle of Plasmodiophoraceae in soil and their impact on root health.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Plasmodiophora + suffix -aceae (used for plant and fungal families).
