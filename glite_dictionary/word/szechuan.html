Szechuan

1. PROPN

/ ˈʃɛdʒˌwɑn / (AmE), / ˈsɛtʃ.wɑːn / (BrE)
a populous province in south central China, noted for its spicy cuisine and cultural heritage
a province in south central China

Examples

Famous for its bold flavors, Szechuan cuisine is enjoyed around the world.
He traveled through Szechuan to learn about its unique history and landscapes.
Many dishes labeled as Szechuan in restaurants contain chili peppers and garlic.

Etymology

From Chinese 四川 (Sìchuān), meaning 'four rivers'.
