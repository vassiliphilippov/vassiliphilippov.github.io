dianoetic

1. ADJ

/ ˌdaɪəˈnoʊɾɪk / (AmE), / ˌdaɪəˈnəʊtɪk / (BrE)
proceeding to a conclusion by reasoning or argument rather than by intuition
relating to reasoning or argument

Examples

The philosopher emphasized a dianoetic process, insisting on reason and logic over gut feelings.
Her dianoetic approach to decision-making relies on careful analysis rather than instinct.
Scientific methods are primarily dianoetic, valuing systematic reasoning above intuition.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intuitive - relying on instinct or intuition rather than reasoning
  • Synonym: logical - using clear and sound reasoning

Etymology

From Greek dianoētikos, from dianoia (thought, thinking, understanding).
