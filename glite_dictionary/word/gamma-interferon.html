gamma-interferon

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡæmə ˌɪntərˈfɪrɑn / (AmE), / ˌɡæmə ˌɪntəˈfɪərɒn / (BrE)
a form of interferon produced mainly by T cells and macrophages that plays a role in activating phagocytes and regulating the immune response
immune system protein produced by T cells
medical

Examples

Researchers are studying the effects of gamma-interferon on autoimmune diseases.
The patient's immune response improved after treatment with gamma-interferon.
Macrophages become more effective when stimulated by gamma-interferon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interferon - a group of proteins released by host cells in response to pathogens

Etymology

From 'gamma', denoting the third form, and 'interferon', a class of proteins named for their ability to interfere with viral replication.
