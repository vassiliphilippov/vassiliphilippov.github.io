terror

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛr.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈter.ər / (BrE)
A child who is very difficult to control, causing trouble or annoyance; an unruly or troublesome child (informal).
troublesome or unruly child (informal)
informal

Examples

Their youngest son is an absolute terror whenever they visit friends' houses.
The twins were known as little terrors at school because of all their pranks.
After eating too much sugar, he turns into a real terror around bedtime.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rascal - a mischievous or cheeky person, especially a child
  • Antonym: angel - a well-behaved or good child

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛrər / (AmE), / ˈtɛrə / (BrE)
the use of extreme fear to control or coerce people, especially for political purposes
use of extreme fear to coerce

Examples

The dictator used terror to silence all opposition.
During the revolution, the population lived under a climate of terror.
Throughout history, some regimes have maintained power through terror and intimidation.

Synset Examples

he used terror to make them confess

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrorism - the unlawful use of violence and intimidation, especially against civilians, in the pursuit of political aims

Etymology

From Latin 'terror' (great fear), from 'terrēre' (to frighten).

3. NOUN

/ ˈtɛrər / (AmE), / ˈtɛrə / (BrE)
A strong and overwhelming feeling of fear and anxiety.
intense fear and anxiety

Examples

A look of terror crossed her face as the storm approached.
He woke up in the night, sweating with terror after the nightmare.
The sudden, loud explosion filled the crowd with terror.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fear - an unpleasant emotion caused by threat or danger
  • Antonym: calm - a state of being free from strong emotion or anxiety

Etymology

From Latin 'terror' meaning 'fear' or 'dread'.
