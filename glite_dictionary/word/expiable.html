expiable

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛkspiəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈɛkspiəbl̩ / (BrE)
(of an offense or sin) capable of being atoned for or forgiven.
able to be atoned for

Examples

The judge concluded that the defendant's crime was expiable because he had shown genuine remorse.
Some religious traditions believe that all sins are expiable through confession and repentance.
His betrayal was seen as expiable only if he made a sincere effort to make amends.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inexpiable - impossible to atone for

Etymology

From Latin expiabilis, from expiare (to atone for).
