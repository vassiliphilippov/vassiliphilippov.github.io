headwoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈhɛdˌwʊmən / (BrE)
the female leader of a tribe or clan
female tribal or clan leader

Examples

The headwoman welcomed the visitors warmly to the village.
After years of service, she was appointed as headwoman by the elders of the clan.
The council sought headwoman Mary's advice on the dispute.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: headman - male leader of a tribe or clan

Etymology

Formed from 'head' + 'woman', after 'headman' (male chief).
