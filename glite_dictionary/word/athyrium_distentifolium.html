Athyrium distentifolium

1. NOUN

/ əˈθɪriəm dɪˌstɛntɪˈfoʊliəm / (AmE), / əˈθɪəriəm dɪˌstɛntɪˈfəʊliəm / (BrE)
A species of lady fern with deeply divided leaves, found in mountainous regions such as the Rocky Mountains.
a species of lady fern

Examples

The Athyrium distentifolium is commonly found growing among moist rocks in alpine meadows.
Botanists studied the Athyrium distentifolium to understand its adaptations to cold environments.
Gardeners often plant Athyrium distentifolium for its attractive, feathery foliage in shaded areas.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus Athyrium and the species epithet distentifolium, meaning 'with spread-out leaves.'
