bream

1. VERB

/ bɹiːm / (AmE), / bɹiːm / (BrE)
to clean the bottom of a ship by applying heat to remove barnacles, seaweed, or other marine growths.
remove marine growth from ship's bottom using heat

Examples

Before setting sail, the crew had to bream the old vessel to improve her speed.
Shipwrights would bream the hull regularly to prevent damage from encrusting organisms.
It was common practice in earlier centuries to bream wooden ships before long voyages.

Etymology

Of uncertain origin; used in nautical contexts since the 16th century.

2. NOUN

/ brɛm / (AmE), / briːm / (BrE)
a species of freshwater fish with a compressed body and shiny scales, found in various genera, especially Lepomis, and often valued as food
a freshwater food fish with a compressed body

Examples

The lake is home to large bream and other freshwater fish.
Fishermen often catch bream using worms as bait near the shore.
Grilled bream is a popular dish in many European countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sunfish - a type of freshwater fish related to bream in the family Centrarchidae
  • Synonym: perch - a type of freshwater fish, sometimes mistaken for bream, with a similar body shape

Etymology

From Old French *bresme*, from Frankish *brahsima* or Germanic roots referring to a type of fish.

Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ brɛm / (AmE), / briːm / (BrE)
A type of edible freshwater or marine fish, especially one of the family Sparidae, with a deep, laterally compressed body.
a kind of freshwater or marine fish

Examples

We caught five bream during our fishing trip by the lake.
The chef prepared the bream with lemon and herbs for dinner.
Schools of bream can often be seen in shallow coastal waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perch - a type of freshwater game fish, often used as a comparison with bream

Etymology

From Old French *breme*, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch *braam* and German *Bräme*.

4. NOUN

/ brim / (AmE), / briːm / (BrE)
The flesh of various freshwater fishes, especially of the families Cyprinidae or Centrarchidae, used as food in North America or Europe.
flesh of various freshwater breams used as food

Examples

She ordered bream for dinner, served with a lemon butter sauce.
The chef prefers using fresh bream for his signature fish stew.
Local markets often sell smoked bream alongside other freshwater fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fish - the flesh of fish used as food

Etymology

From Old French *breme* (bream), from Frankish or Old High German *brahmo* (a kind of fish).

5. NOUN

/ brɛm / (AmE), / briːm / (BrE)

flesh of certain edible sea fish

Examples

She bought some fresh bream at the fish market for tonight's dinner.
Many Mediterranean dishes feature bream as the main ingredient.

Related Terms
