floccule

1. NOUN

/ ˈflɑˌkjuːl / (AmE), / ˈflɒkjʊˌl / (BrE)
A small, loosely aggregated mass of fine material, often suspended in or precipitated from a liquid.
small mass of fine material in liquid
medical

Examples

The scientist observed several floccules forming as the chemical reaction progressed.
Small floccules settled at the bottom of the test tube after mixing the solutions.
Water treatment often involves the removal of floccules to clarify the liquid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: floc - a small loosely aggregated mass of flocculent material

Etymology

From Latin flocculus, diminutive of floccus (a flock of wool).
