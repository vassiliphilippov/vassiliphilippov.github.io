liliopsid

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪliˈɑpsɪd / (AmE), / ˌlɪliˈɒpsɪd / (BrE)
A monocotyledonous flowering plant; a plant whose stem grows by deposits on its inside, also known as a monocot.
monocotyledonous flowering plant
medical

Examples

The orchid is a beautiful example of a liliopsid found in tropical forests.
Many grasses are classified as liliopsids due to their single seed leaf.
Rice, wheat, and maize are important liliopsid crops grown worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monocot - A plant with one embryonic seed leaf (cotyledon)
  • Antonym: dicot - A plant with two embryonic seed leaves (cotyledons)

Etymology

From the taxonomic group Liliopsida, based on Latin 'lilium' (lily) and Greek 'opsis' (appearance).
