Sabbatia campestris

1. NOUN

/ sæˈbeɪʃiə kæmˈpɛstrɪs / (AmE), / səˈbatiə kʌmˈpɛstrɪs / (BrE)
a prairie herbaceous plant with solitary lilac-colored flowers, native to central North America
prairie plant with lilac flowers

Examples

The Sabbatia campestris adds a splash of lilac color to the prairie landscape each spring.
Botanists conducted a survey of Sabbatia campestris in the protected grasslands.
Gardeners admire Sabbatia campestris for its delicate and solitary flowers.

Etymology

From the genus name Sabbatia (after Liberato Sabbati, 18th-century Italian botanist) and Latin campestris (meaning 'of the fields').
