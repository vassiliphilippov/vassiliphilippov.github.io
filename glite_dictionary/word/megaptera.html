Megaptera

1. NOUN

/ mɛɡˈæptərə / (AmE), / mɛɡˈæptərə / (BrE)
A genus of large baleen whales, commonly known as humpback whales, found in oceans worldwide.
genus of humpback whales

Examples

The scientific name for the humpback whale is Megaptera.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of Megaptera in the Pacific Ocean.
Megaptera is easily recognized by its long pectoral fins and distinctive body shape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humpback whale - a large baleen whale known for its long pectoral fins and acrobatic breaching

Etymology

From Greek 'mega-' (large) + 'pteron' (wing), referring to their large pectoral fins.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛɡ.əpˈtɛr.ə / (AmE), / ˌmiː.ɡəpˈtɪə.rə / (BrE)
A genus of baleen whales, comprising the humpback whale.
genus of humpback whales

Examples

Megaptera is best known for including the species commonly referred to as the humpback whale.
Marine biologists conducted a survey of Megaptera populations in the Southern Hemisphere.
The unique flipper shape of Megaptera distinguishes it from other baleen whale genera.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humpback whale - A species of large baleen whale in the genus Megaptera

Etymology

From Greek 'mega' (large) + 'pteron' (wing), referring to the whale's long flippers.
