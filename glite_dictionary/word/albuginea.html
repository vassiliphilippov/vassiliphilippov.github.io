albuginea

1. NOUN

/ ˌæl.bjuˈdʒɪn.i.ə / (AmE), / ˌal.bjuːˈdʒɪn.i.ə / (BrE)
A white fibrous tissue or tunic that covers certain organs, such as the testis or the eyeball, in the body.
white fibrous tunic in anatomy
medical

Examples

The tough albuginea protects the delicate internal tissues of the testis.
Damage to the albuginea can lead to complications in eye health.
Surgeons must take care not to tear the albuginea during certain procedures.

Etymology

From Latin 'albugineus' (whitish), from 'albus' (white).
