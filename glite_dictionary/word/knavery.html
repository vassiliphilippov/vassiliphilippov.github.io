knavery

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪvəri / (AmE), / ˈneɪvəri / (BrE)
dishonest or unscrupulous behavior; acts involving lying, cheating, or theft
dishonest or wicked behavior

Examples

The merchant's knavery was eventually uncovered, and he was driven out of town.
Such blatant knavery should not go unpunished in a civilized society.
She was disappointed to learn of the knavery lurking behind his charming smile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dishonesty - lack of honesty; fraudulent behavior
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being honest

Etymology

From Middle English knave (boy, servant) + -ry (suffix forming nouns indicating condition or practice).
