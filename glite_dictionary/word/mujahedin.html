mujahedin

1. NOUN

/ muːˌdʒɑːhəˈdiːn / (AmE), / mʊˌdʒɑːhɛˈdiːn / (BrE)
A group of Muslim guerrilla fighters engaged in jihad, especially noted for fighting in conflicts such as the Soviet-Afghan War.
Muslim guerrilla fighters in a jihad

Examples

The mujahedin played a key role in resisting the Soviet invasion during the 1980s.
Several former mujahedin fighters later became leaders in the region's new government.
News reports covered the activities of the mujahedin along the mountainous border.

Synset Examples

some call the mujahidin international warriors but others just call them terrorists

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mujahideen - Alternative spelling for 'mujahedin', also used in English

Etymology

From Arabic مُجَاهِدِين‎ (mujāhidīn), plural of مُجَاهِد‎ (mujāhid, 'one who struggles or fights for Islam').
