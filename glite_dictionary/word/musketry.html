musketry

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌskətri / (AmE), / ˈmʌskɪtri / (BrE)
The technique, practice, or art of using muskets and other small arms, especially in military contexts.
use and technique of muskets

Examples

The regiment's musketry was praised for its discipline and accuracy during the battle.
Advancements in musketry training greatly improved the effectiveness of the infantry.
He wrote a manual on musketry to instruct new recruits in proper shooting techniques.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marksmanship - skill in shooting or hitting a target

Etymology

From musket + -ry, denoting practice or technique.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmʌskətri / (AmE), / ˈmʌskɪtri / (BrE)
Musketeers and their muskets considered as a collective group, especially in a military context.
collective term for musketeers and muskets

Examples

The advancing army was supported by a line of musketry that echoed across the field.
During the battle, the sound of musketry filled the air as musketeers fired volley after volley.
Old manuscripts describe the discipline and precision of 17th-century musketry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infantry - soldiers marching and fighting on foot

Etymology

From 'musket', ultimately from Italian 'moschetto' (a kind of firearm).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
