canalis vertebralis

1. NOUN

/ kəˈnælɪs vɜːr.təˈbreɪlɪs / (AmE), / kəˈnɑːlɪs vɜː.tɪˈbrɑːlɪs / (BrE)
the canal formed by the alignment of vertebral foramina in the vertebral column, through which the spinal cord passes
spinal canal in vertebrae for spinal cord
medical

Examples

An injury to the canalis vertebralis may result in damage to the spinal cord.
The MRI scan revealed a narrowing of the canalis vertebralis.
Surgeons must take care to avoid harming the canalis vertebralis during spinal operations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spinal canal - the channel within the vertebral column through which the spinal cord passes

Etymology

From Latin canalis (‘canal, channel’) and vertebralis (‘of the vertebrae’).
