relaxant

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈlæksənt / (AmE), / rɪˈlæksənt / (BrE)
A drug or agent that relaxes muscles or relieves tension in the body.
drug that relieves muscle tension
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a relaxant to help ease his muscle spasms.
Some relaxants can cause drowsiness as a side effect, so use them with caution.
After the accident, she took a muscle relaxant for her back pain.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stimulant - a drug that increases physiological or nervous activity in the body
  • Synonym: muscle relaxant - a drug specifically used to reduce muscle tension

Etymology

From Latin 'relaxare' (to loosen, open, stretch out), with the suffix '-ant' forming agent nouns.
