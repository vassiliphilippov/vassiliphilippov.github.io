rollout

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊlˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈrəʊlˌaʊt / (BrE)
the official introduction or distribution of a new product or service to the public
official introduction of a new product or service

Examples

The company's latest software rollout was met with great enthusiasm by users.
We expect the nationwide rollout of the vaccine to be completed by summer.
Problems during the initial rollout delayed the product's success.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: launch - the act of officially starting something, especially a product or service
  • Antonym: withdrawal - the removal or discontinuation of a product or service from the market

Etymology

Formed from the phrasal verb 'roll out' meaning 'to launch or put into operation', used in business and technology contexts.
