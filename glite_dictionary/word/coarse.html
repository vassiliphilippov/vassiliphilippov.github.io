coarse

1. ADJ

/ kɔrs / (AmE), / kɔːs / (BrE)
Having a rough texture or composed of relatively large particles; not fine or smooth.
rough in texture; large particles

Examples

The rug felt coarse under my bare feet.
She sifted out the coarse grains from the flour.
We used coarse sand to improve drainage in the garden soil.

Synset Examples

coarse meal
coarse sand
a coarse weave

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fine - of textures that are smooth or composed of small particles

Etymology

From Middle English 'cors', from Old French 'cours', from Latin 'corsus' meaning coarse or rough.

2. ADJ

/ kɔrs / (AmE), / kɔːs / (BrE)
lacking refinement, cultivation, or good taste; crude or unpolished in manner or style
lacking refinement or good taste

Examples

She found his coarse jokes and loud laughter distasteful at the formal dinner.
His coarse table manners revealed his lack of upbringing.
The art critic dismissed the painting as having a coarse and unsophisticated style.

Synset Examples

he had coarse manners but a first-rate mind
behavior that branded him as common
an untutored and uncouth human being
an uncouth soldier — a real tough guy
appealing to the vulgar taste for violence
the vulgar display of the newly rich

Related Terms

  • Antonym: refined - showing high culture or good taste
  • Synonym: crude - lacking sophistication or subtlety
  • Synonym: vulgar - lacking sophistication or good taste; unrefined

Etymology

From Latin cohors (enclosure, yard), later used in Middle English to mean 'ordinary' or 'common'.

3. ADJ

/ kɔrs / (AmE), / kɔːs / (BrE)
of low or inferior quality or value; not fine or refined.
of low or inferior quality

Examples

The fabric was of such coarse quality that it wore out after just a few weeks.
Only coarse materials were used in the construction of the inexpensive housing.
The craftsman refused to work with coarse wood, preferring more refined materials.

Synset Examples

of what coarse metal ye are molded
produced … the common cloths used by the poorer population

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inferior - of lower quality or value
  • Synonym: poor - of poor quality
  • Antonym: fine - of high quality or delicacy

Etymology

From Middle English 'cors', from Old French 'cors' (rough), from Latin 'corsus', past participle of 'corrigere' (to correct, make straight).
