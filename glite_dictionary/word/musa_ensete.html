Musa ensete

1. NOUN

/ ˈmjuːsə ɛnˈsiːti / (AmE), / ˈmjuːsə ɛnˈsiːti / (BrE)
a large, evergreen, tree-like herb native to Africa, related to the banana, with huge paddle-shaped leaves, inedible banana-like fruit, but edible young flower shoots; also known as enset or false banana
species of African herb similar to banana plant

Examples

The farmers in Ethiopia cultivate Musa ensete as a vital staple crop.
Although Musa ensete resembles a banana plant, its fruit is inedible but its shoots are eaten.
The thick pseudostem of Musa ensete is used for various traditional purposes in some African communities.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: banana - the edible fruit of several large herbaceous plants of the genus Musa

Etymology

From the genus name Musa (Latin, from Arabic 'mauz' – banana) and species name ensete, modern Latin from Amharic 'ensat' (name for the plant in Ethiopia).
