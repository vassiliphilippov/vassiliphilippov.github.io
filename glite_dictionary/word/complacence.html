complacence

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈpleɪsəns / (AmE), / kəmˈpleɪsəns / (BrE)
A feeling of quiet pleasure or self-satisfaction, often while unaware of some potential danger or deficiency.
self-satisfied feeling; smugness

Examples

Her complacence prevented her from recognizing the problems in her team.
There was a sense of complacence among the employees, despite declining sales.
Critics accused the government of complacence in the face of rising crime rates.

Synset Examples

his complacency was absolutely disgusting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complacency - the feeling you have when you are satisfied with yourself
  • Antonym: dissatisfaction - a lack of satisfaction with oneself or circumstances

Etymology

From Latin complacentia (satisfaction, pleasing), from complacere (to please).
