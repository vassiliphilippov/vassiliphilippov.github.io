toyon

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊ.jɑn / (AmE), / ˈtəʊ.jɒn / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub native to the Pacific coast of the United States, with large white flowers and red berry-like fruits, often grown ornamentally
evergreen shrub with white flowers and red berries

Examples

The hillside was covered with toyon in full bloom, attracting birds with its bright red berries.
Gardeners often plant toyon for its drought resistance and ornamental appeal.
In winter, flocks of robins feed on the toyon berries along the California coast.

Etymology

From a Native American (Ohlone or Costanoan) term for the plant.
