barmbrack

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑrmˌbræk / (AmE), / ˈbɑːm.bræk / (BrE)
A traditional Irish fruit bread containing currants or raisins, often served sliced and buttered.
Irish fruit bread with currants or raisins

Examples

Every Halloween, my grandmother bakes a delicious barmbrack filled with sultanas and spices.
Sliced barmbrack was served with tea at the traditional Irish afternoon gathering.
The recipe for barmbrack has been passed down in our family for generations.

Etymology

From Irish *báirín breac* meaning 'speckled loaf'.
