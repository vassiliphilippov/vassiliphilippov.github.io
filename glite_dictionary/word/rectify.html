to rectify

1. VERB

/ ˈrɛktɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈrɛktɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to correct something that is wrong or to make something right
correct; make right

Examples

The company needs to rectify the errors in its financial statements before submitting them.
After realizing his mistake, he immediately took steps to rectify the situation.
Engineers were called in to rectify the faulty wiring in the building.

Synset Examples

Correct the mistakes
rectify the calculation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: correct - to remove errors or faults from something
  • Synonym: fix - to repair or mend something broken or wrong
  • Antonym: to worsen - to make something worse

Etymology

From Latin 'rectificare' (to make right), from 'rectus' (right) and 'facere' (to make).

/ ˈrɛk.təˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈrɛk.tɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
(in electricity) To convert alternating current into direct current.
convert AC to DC (electricity)

Examples

The circuit uses a diode bridge to rectify the incoming current.
This device can rectify alternating current to supply stable power for the equipment.
A transformer steps up the voltage, and then the system rectifies the AC to DC.
