nylons

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪˌlɑnz / (AmE), / ˈnaɪ.lɒnz / (BrE)
women's stockings made from a thin, sheer material such as nylon, rayon, or silk
sheer women's stockings

Examples

She put on a new pair of nylons before leaving for the party.
In the 1940s, nylons were considered a luxury and were often hard to find.
Her nylons had a small run, so she quickly changed before her meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stockings - close-fitting coverings for the foot and leg, typically made of nylon or silk

Etymology

From 'nylon,' the synthetic fiber invented in the 1930s, used to make stockings.
