timeserving

1. ADJ

/ ˈtaɪmˌsɝvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtaɪmˌsɜːvɪŋ / (BrE)
adapting one's principles or actions to suit the current situation for personal gain, often regarded as unprincipled or self-serving
serving one's own interests by adapting to circumstances

Examples

The politician's timeserving approach made him unpopular with those who valued principles over expediency.
Her timeserving attitude ensured she always sided with whoever had the most power.
Critics accused the CEO of timeserving rather than leading with integrity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opportunistic - taking advantage of opportunities as they arise, often without regard to principle

Etymology

From 'time' + 'serving', originally referring to serving the interests of the current moment rather than showing principle (17th century).
