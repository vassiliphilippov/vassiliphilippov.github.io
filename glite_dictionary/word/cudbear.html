cudbear

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌdˌbɛr / (AmE), / ˈkʌdˌbeə / (BrE)
a purplish dye obtained from certain lichens, especially orchil lichens, used in coloring textiles
purplish dye from lichens

Examples

The weaver used cudbear to produce a rich purple hue in the wool fabric.
In the 18th century, cudbear became a popular dye for coloring textiles in Scotland.
After extracting the cudbear, the artisan carefully applied the dye to achieve an even color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchil - a purple dye obtained from lichens

Etymology

Named after Dr. Cuthbert Gordon, who patented the process in the 18th century, with 'cudbear' derived from his first name.
