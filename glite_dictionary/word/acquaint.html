to acquaint

1. VERB

/ əˈkweɪnt / (AmE), / əˈkweɪnt / (BrE)
to make someone aware of or familiar with something; to inform or notify
to make someone aware; to inform

Examples

Please acquaint your team with the new procedures before next week.
She took a moment to acquaint him with the company policies.
I would appreciate it if you could acquaint me with the current situation.

Synset Examples

Please acquaint your colleagues of your plans to move

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inform - to give someone information; to tell
  • Synonym: to notify - to formally tell someone about something

Etymology

From Old French 'acointer' (to make known) and Medieval Latin 'accognitare'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00834439-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ əˈkweɪnt / (AmE), / əˈkweɪnt / (BrE)
to make someone familiar with something or to become familiar with it oneself
make familiar with something

Examples

It is important to acquaint new employees with the company's safety procedures.
You should acquaint yourself with the basic rules before starting the game.
The guide helped the tourists acquaint themselves with local customs and traditions.

Synset Examples

you should acquaint yourself with your new computer
We familiarized ourselves with the new surroundings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to familiarize - to make oneself or someone else familiar with something

Etymology

From Old French 'acointer' (make known), from Latin 'accognitare', frequentative of 'accognoscere' (to know).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00875857-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ əˈkweɪnt / (AmE), / əˈkweɪnt / (BrE)
to make someone aware of or familiar with; to introduce someone to another person or to information
make familiar or introduce

Examples

She took a moment to acquaint herself with the new software before starting work.
Allow me to acquaint you with the members of our team.
It took time for the students to acquaint themselves with university life.

Synset Examples

permit me to acquaint you with my son
introduce the new neighbors to the community

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to introduce - to bring (someone) into a group; to make known by name or sight

Etymology

From Old French *acquainter*, from Latin *accognitare* (to make known).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00902866-v
Synset POS: v
