to accumulate

1. VERB

/ əˈkjuːmjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / əˈkjuːmjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to gradually increase in number or amount over time by collecting or gathering things together
gather or collect over time

Examples

Dust can accumulate quickly if a room is not cleaned regularly.
Over the years, he managed to accumulate a vast collection of rare stamps.
Problems tend to accumulate when they are ignored for too long.

Synset Examples

Journals are accumulating in my office
The work keeps piling up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gather - to bring things together; to collect
  • Synonym: collect - to bring things together into one place or group
  • Antonym: to disperse - to scatter or cause to scatter in different directions

Etymology

From Latin accumulare, from ad- (to) + cumulare (to heap).

2. VERB

/ əˈkjumjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / əˈkjuːmjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to gather or collect a quantity of something over a period of time; to increase in amount gradually.
to gather or collect over time

Examples

Over the years, she managed to accumulate a substantial collection of rare stamps.
Dust tends to accumulate quickly on unused furniture.
He accumulated several awards during his outstanding career.

Synset Examples

I am accumulating evidence for the man's unfaithfulness to his wife
She is amassing a lot of data for her thesis
She rolled up a small fortune

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to amass - to gather or collect a large amount of something, especially over a period of time
  • Synonym: collect - to bring things together from different places or gather them
  • Antonym: to dissipate - to scatter or disperse, causing something to become less or disappear

Etymology

From Latin 'accumulare', from 'ad-' (to) + 'cumulare' (to heap).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02309962-v
Synset POS: v
