hadal

1. ADJ

/ ˈheɪ.dl̩ / (AmE), / ˈheɪ.dl̩ / (BrE)
relating to the deepest parts of the ocean, typically at depths below 6000 meters.
of the deep ocean below 6000 meters

Examples

Scientists are studying the unique organisms found in the hadal trenches of the Pacific Ocean.
The submersible was built to withstand the immense pressures of the hadal zone.
Biologists discovered a new species of fish living in hadal waters nearly 11,000 meters deep.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: abyssal - relating to ocean depths between about 2000 and 6000 meters

Etymology

From Hades, the Greek god of the underworld, referencing extreme ocean depth.
