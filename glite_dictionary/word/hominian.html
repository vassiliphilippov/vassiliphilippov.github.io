hominian

1. ADJ

/ ˈhoʊ.mɪ.ni.ən / (AmE), / ˈhɒ.mɪ.ni.ən / (BrE)
relating to the family Hominidae, which includes modern humans and extinct species of manlike creatures
relating to the family Hominidae

Examples

The fossil displayed several hominian features, linking it to early human ancestors.
Research into hominian evolution has reshaped our understanding of primate development.
Some scientists debate which fossils should be classified as hominian.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hominid - member of the family Hominidae, including humans and great apes

Etymology

From Latin 'homin-', meaning 'human being', plus English suffix '-ian'.
