rhinotracheitis

1. NOUN

/ ˌraɪnoʊtrəˌkiːˈaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌraɪnəʊtrəˌkiːˈaɪtɪs / (BrE)
an inflammation or infection of the nasal passages and trachea, especially in cattle, characterized by respiratory symptoms
respiratory infection of nose and throat in cattle
medical

Examples

The veterinarian confirmed that the calf was suffering from rhinotracheitis after observing its symptoms.
Preventative vaccination against rhinotracheitis is common on dairy farms to protect the herd.
Symptoms of rhinotracheitis in cattle include nasal discharge, coughing, and labored breathing.

Etymology

From rhino- (nose) + trachea (windpipe) + -itis (inflammation), from Greek roots.
