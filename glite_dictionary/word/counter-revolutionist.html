counter-revolutionist

1. NOUN

/ ˌkaʊntər rɪˈvɑːluˌʃənɪst / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntə rɪˈvjuːləˌʃənɪst / (BrE)
a person who seeks to reverse the changes brought about by a previous revolution
one who aims to undo a revolution

Examples

The counter-revolutionist led a campaign to restore the monarchy.
Many counter-revolutionists were exiled after the government was overthrown.
Accused of being a counter-revolutionist, he was forced to flee the country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: counterrevolutionary - a person who opposes and seeks to reverse a revolution

Etymology

Formed from 'counter-' (against) + 'revolutionist' (one who supports a revolution).
