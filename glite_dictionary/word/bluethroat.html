bluethroat

1. NOUN

/ ˈbluˌθroʊt / (AmE), / ˈbluːˌθrəʊt / (BrE)
a small Old World songbird (Luscinia svecica) of northern Europe and Asia, the male of which has a bright blue patch on its throat.
Old World songbird with blue throat

Examples

We spotted a rare bluethroat singing among the reeds this morning.
The distinctive blue patch makes the male bluethroat easy to identify during breeding season.
Birdwatchers travel from afar to catch a glimpse of the elusive bluethroat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nightingale - a small European songbird closely related to the bluethroat

Etymology

From blue + throat, referring to the blue coloration on the bird's throat.
