Piperaceae

1. NOUN

/ paɪpəˈreɪsiˌi / (AmE), / paɪpəˈreɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of tropical woody vines and herbaceous plants, noted for their aromatic parts and tiny flowers arranged in spikelets; includes the pepper plants.
family of aromatic tropical plants

Examples

The Piperaceae family includes the black pepper plant widely used as a spice.
Many species in the Piperaceae are native to tropical regions and valued for their aromatic qualities.
Researchers have studied the medicinal properties of several plants in the Piperaceae family.

Etymology

From Latin, based on the type genus Piper (pepper).

a family of tropical woody vines and herbaceous plants with aromatic foliage and tiny flowers borne in spikelets, including the pepper plants
a family of aromatic tropical plants (pepper family)

Examples

The Piperaceae family includes black pepper and kava among its many species.
Botanists have studied the unique flower structures found within the Piperaceae.
Most plants in the Piperaceae family are recognized by their aromatic leaves and clustered flowers.

Etymology

From Modern Latin Piperaceae, from Latin piper (pepper), the type genus Piper.
