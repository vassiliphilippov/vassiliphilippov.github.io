1-dodecanol

1. NOUN

/ ˌwʌn doʊˈdɛkəˌnɔl / (AmE), / ˌwʌn dəʊˈdɛkənɒl / (BrE)
a colorless, insoluble solid alcohol (C12H26O) used in making detergents and pharmaceuticals
solid alcohol used in detergents and drugs

Examples

The laboratory ordered more 1-dodecanol for use in their detergent formulations.
Pharmaceutical companies use 1-dodecanol as an intermediate in drug synthesis.
Because of its insolubility in water, 1-dodecanol is often used in specialized industrial processes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lauryl alcohol - another name for 1-dodecanol

Etymology

From the combination of '1-' (indicating position), 'dodeca-' (from Greek for twelve), and '-anol' (from alcohol).
