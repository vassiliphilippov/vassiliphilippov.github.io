to resemble

1. VERB

/ rɪˈzɛmbəl / (AmE), / rɪˈzɛmbəl / (BrE)
to look like or be similar to someone or something
be similar to; look like

Examples

The twins resemble each other so closely that people often confuse them.
Some mushrooms resemble edible varieties but are actually poisonous.
Her painting style resembles that of the Impressionist masters.

Synset Examples

She resembles her mother very much
This paper resembles my own work

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to differ - to be unlike or dissimilar
  • Synonym: to imitate - to copy the manner or appearance of

Etymology

From Old French 'resembler', from Latin 'similis' (like).
