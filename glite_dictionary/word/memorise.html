to memorise

1. VERB

/ ˈmɛm.əˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɛm.ə.raɪz / (BrE)
to commit something to memory; to learn by heart
commit to memory
british

Examples

She managed to memorise all the important dates for her history exam.
The actors need to memorise their scripts before rehearsals begin.
It took me hours to memorise the poem for class.

Synset Examples

Have you memorized your lines for the play yet?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to memorize - commit to memory (American spelling)
  • Antonym: to forget - fail to remember

Etymology

From Latin memoria (memory) + -ise/-ize suffix.
