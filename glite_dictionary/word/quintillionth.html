quintillionth

1. ADJ

/ kwɪnˈtɪljənθ / (AmE), / kwɪnˈtɪljənθ / (BrE)
The ordinal number matching one quintillion in a sequence; the position corresponding to number one quintillion.
ordinal number for one quintillionth place

Examples

The astronomer calculated the quintillionth star in the simulation universe.
She celebrated the quintillionth visitor to her science blog with a special post.
The computer processed its quintillionth instruction in less than a second.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quintillion - The cardinal number equal to 1 followed by 18 zeros (in US English) or 1 followed by 30 zeros (in British English)

Etymology

From 'quintillion' + '-th', formed using Latin-derived numerical prefix 'quinti-' (five) and English ordinal suffix.

2. NOUN

/ kwɪnˈtɪliənθ / (AmE), / kwɪnˈtɪliənθ / (BrE)
One part in a quintillion equal parts; the ordinal number corresponding to a quintillion.
one part in a quintillion; 1/1,000,000,000,000,000,000

Examples

A quintillionth of a second is an almost inconceivably small amount of time.
Even a quintillionth portion of the total would still be a large number of atoms.
She won’t accept even a quintillionth of the blame for the mistake.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: millionth - one part in a million equal parts
  • Degree Lower: billionth - one part in a billion equal parts
  • Degree Higher: sextillionth - one part in a sextillion equal parts

Etymology

Formed from 'quintillion' (from Latin 'quintus' meaning 'fifth', on the pattern of million, billion, etc.) and the ordinal suffix '-th'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
