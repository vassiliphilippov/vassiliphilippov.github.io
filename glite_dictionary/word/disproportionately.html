disproportionately

1. ADV

/ ˌdɪsprəˈpɔrʃənətli / (AmE), / ˌdɪsprəˈpɔːʃənətli / (BrE)
To a degree that is too large or too small in relation to something else.
to an unequal or inappropriate degree

Examples

The new law will disproportionately affect low-income families.
Women are disproportionately represented in leadership positions across the industry.
Resources were disproportionately allocated to certain departments, causing frustration among staff.

Synset Examples

his benefits were disproportionately generous

Related Terms

  • Antonym: proportionately - in a way that is in correct relation to something else

Etymology

From 'disproportionate' (from Latin dis- ‘apart, not’ + proportionatus ‘proportioned’), plus the adverbial suffix -ly.

2. ADV

/ ˌdɪs.prəˈpɔr.ʃən.ət.li / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.prəˈpɔː.ʃən.ət.li / (BrE)
in a way that is out of proportion to something else; excessively relative to what is normal or expected
excessively or unevenly in relation to something

Examples

The tax increase would disproportionately affect people on lower incomes.
The species has been disproportionately impacted by climate change compared to others in the region.
The funding was disproportionately allocated to urban rather than rural schools.

Synset Examples

this wall is disproportionately long

Related Terms

  • Antonym: proportionately - in a way that corresponds in amount or degree; in proportion

Etymology

From 'disproportion' + '-ately', patterned after 'proportionately'.
