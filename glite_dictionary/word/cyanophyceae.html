Cyanophyceae

1. NOUN

/ saɪˌænəˈfaɪsiː.i / (AmE), / saɪˌanəʊˈfɪsiː.iː / (BrE)
A class of photosynthetic bacteria, commonly called blue-green algae, found in freshwater and marine environments and characterized by the presence of chlorophyll a and phycobilins.
class of blue-green algae (photosynthetic bacteria)

Examples

Cyanophyceae play an important role in nitrogen fixation in aquatic ecosystems.
The bloom was caused mainly by Cyanophyceae populations in the lake.
Under the microscope, researchers identified Cyanophyceae based on their bluish pigment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue-green algae - common name for Cyanophyceae, photosynthetic bacteria

Etymology

From Greek 'kyanos' (dark blue) + 'phycos' (seaweed/alga) + '-eae' (a taxonomic suffix).

2. NOUN

/ saɪəˌnoʊfaɪˈsiː.i / (AmE), / saɪˌænə(ʊ)faɪˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
a class of photosynthetic bacteria, commonly known as blue-green algae, found in fresh and salt water and containing chlorophyll a and phycobilins; formerly considered algae
class of blue-green algae (cyanobacteria)

Examples

The bloom was caused by an overgrowth of Cyanophyceae in the lake.
Scientists study Cyanophyceae to understand early photosynthetic life on Earth.
Many species in the Cyanophyceae produce toxins that can affect aquatic ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cyanobacteria - photosynthetic bacteria formerly known as blue-green algae

Etymology

From Greek 'kyanos' (blue) + 'phyton' (plant) + '-eae' (taxonomic suffix).
