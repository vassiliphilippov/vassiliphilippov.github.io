to grizzle

1. VERB

/ ˈɡrɪzəl / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪzl / (BrE)
to complain or whine in a persistent or peevish manner, especially in a childish way
to complain or whine fretfully
british

Examples

Stop grizzling; things aren't that bad.
The child grizzled all afternoon because she missed her nap.
He tends to grizzle about the smallest inconveniences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whine - to complain in a feeble or petulant way
  • Synonym: moan - to complain or grumble, typically about something unimportant
  • Antonym: cheer - to be in a state of cheerfulness, contrasting with complaining

Etymology

From Middle English *grisen*, likely imitative of a child's whimpering sound.

2. VERB

/ ˈɡrɪzəl / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪzəl / (BrE)
to be sulky or sullen, often in silence; to pout or complain in a petulant manner
be sulky or sullen

Examples

Whenever Mark lost a game, he would just grizzle in the corner and refuse to talk.
She tends to grizzle for hours if something doesn't go her way.
Instead of expressing his feelings, John chose to grizzle quietly all afternoon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sulk - to be silent, morose, and bad-tempered out of annoyance or disappointment
  • Synonym: to complain - to express dissatisfaction or annoyance about something
  • Antonym: cheer - to shout for joy or in support (opposite of sullen silence)

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'grisel', meaning to lament or grumble, possibly of imitative origin.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
