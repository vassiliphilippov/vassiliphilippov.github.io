frangibility

1. NOUN

/ ˌfrændʒəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌfrændʒɪˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being easily broken, damaged, or destroyed.
the quality of being easily broken

Examples

The frangibility of the antique vase makes it unsuitable for everyday use.
Engineers tested the frangibility of the material to ensure it would break safely on impact.
Her sculptures explore the frangibility of beauty and the inevitability of decay.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fragility - the quality of being easily broken or damaged

Etymology

From Medieval Latin frangibilitas, from Latin frangibilis (breakable).
