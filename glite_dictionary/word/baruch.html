Baruch

1. NOUN

/ bəˈruːk / (AmE), / bəˈruːk / (BrE)
an Apocryphal book of the Old Testament, ascribed to Baruch, sometimes included in biblical canons of certain Christian traditions.
Apocryphal book of the Old Testament

Examples

The book of Baruch is considered canonical in the Catholic and Orthodox traditions.
Scholars debate whether Baruch was originally written in Hebrew or Greek.
He cited passages from Baruch in his theological essay.

Etymology

From Hebrew *Bārūkh*, meaning 'blessed'.
