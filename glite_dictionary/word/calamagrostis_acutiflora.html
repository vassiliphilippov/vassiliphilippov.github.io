Calamagrostis acutiflora

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæləməˈɡrɑstɪs ˌækjətəˈflɔrə / (AmE), / ˌkæləməˈɡrɒstɪs ˌækjʊtəˈflɔːrə / (BrE)
A species of tall, perennial reed grass native to Europe and Asia, commonly grown as an ornamental plant.
species of ornamental reed grass

Examples

Many landscapers plant Calamagrostis acutiflora to create dramatic vertical accents in gardens.
The tall plumes of Calamagrostis acutiflora appear in mid-summer and last into winter.
Several varieties of Calamagrostis acutiflora are popular for their resilience and graceful movement in the wind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reed grass - any of various tall grasses of the genus Calamagrostis

Etymology

From Latin: genus 'Calamagrostis' (from Greek 'kalamos' meaning reed and 'agrostis' meaning grass) and species 'acutiflora' meaning 'with pointed flowers'.
