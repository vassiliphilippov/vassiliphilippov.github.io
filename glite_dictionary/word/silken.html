silken

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪlkən / (AmE), / ˈsɪlkən / (BrE)
Having the smooth, soft, and lustrous quality or appearance of silk.
smooth and lustrous like silk

Examples

She wore a silken dress that shimmered under the lights.
His words had a silken charm that put everyone at ease.
The silken petals of the rose felt delicate to the touch.

Synset Examples

satiny gardenia petals
sleek black fur
silken eyelashes
silky skin
a silklike fabric
slick seals and otters
silky auburn hair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silky - having a smooth, soft texture like silk
  • Synonym: satiny - smooth and glossy like satin
  • Antonym: rough - having an uneven or coarse surface

Etymology

Formed from 'silk' + '-en' (adjectival suffix), Old English origins.
