crossbow

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔsˌboʊ / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsbəʊ / (BrE)
a weapon consisting of a bow mounted horizontally on a wooden stock, designed to shoot arrows or bolts
a weapon for shooting arrows or bolts

Examples

The medieval knight loaded his crossbow and aimed at the approaching enemy.
Hunters sometimes prefer a crossbow for its accuracy and power.
A crossbow can be more challenging to reload than a traditional bow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bow - a weapon for shooting arrows, consisting of a curved piece of wood with a taut cord

Etymology

From Old French 'arbaleste', from Medieval Latin 'arbalista', from Latin 'arcus' (bow) and 'ballista' (missile-throwing engine).
