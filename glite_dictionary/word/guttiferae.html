Guttiferae

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡʌtəˈfɪəriː / (AmE), / ˌɡʌtɪˈfɪəriː / (BrE)
A family of mainly tropical trees, shrubs, and vines, many of which produce oils, resins, or usable timber; also known as the family Clusiaceae.
plant family of tropical trees and shrubs

Examples

Many species in the Guttiferae family are prized for their medicinal resins and oils.
Some members of the Guttiferae are important sources of timber in tropical regions.
The botanist specialized in tropical plants, especially those from the Guttiferae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Clusiaceae - alternative modern family name for Guttiferae

Etymology

From Latin 'gutta' (drop, referring to resin drops) + '-ferae' (bearing).
