to unloose

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈlus / (AmE), / ʌnˈluːs / (BrE)
To set free or release from confinement or restraint.
free or release from restraint

Examples

The guard decided to unloose the prisoner after hearing his story.
Ancient myths often tell of heroes who unloose magical creatures from their bonds.
With a single gesture, she unloosed the chains and set the captive bird free.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: release - to set free from confinement; to allow to move freely
  • Antonym: to confine - to keep or restrict someone or something within certain limits

Etymology

From un- (a prefix meaning 'not' or 'reverse') + loose (Old English 'lōs').

2. VERB

/ ʌnˈluːs / (AmE), / ʌnˈluːs / (BrE)
to make something less tight or to release it from being fastened or bound
make less tight; loosen; release

Examples

Before taking off your shoes, remember to unloose the laces.
She carefully unloosed the ribbon wrapped around the present.
The storm unloosed several tiles from the old roof.

Synset Examples

unloose your sneakers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to loosen - make less tight; untie or relax
  • Antonym: to tighten - make tighter or more secure

Etymology

Formed by prefixing 'un-' to 'loose'; in English since Middle English.

Source: WordNet
