Cl

1. NOUN

/ siː ɛl / (AmE), / siː ɛl / (BrE)
the chemical symbol for chlorine, a common halogen element used as a disinfectant and bleaching agent
chemical symbol for chlorine

Examples

The pool was treated with Cl to keep the water clean and safe.
On the periodic table, Cl has the atomic number 17.
Engineers monitor Cl levels in drinking water to ensure effective disinfection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chlorine - a chemical element with symbol Cl, a yellow-green gas

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'chlorine', from Greek *khlōros* (greenish-yellow).
