drove

1. NOUN

/ droʊv / (AmE), / drəʊv / (BrE)
A stonemason's chisel with a broad edge, used for dressing and smoothing stone surfaces.
broad-edged stonemason's chisel

Examples

The stonemason selected a drove to smooth the surface of the granite block.
Each drove needs to be kept sharp for precise stonework.
He demonstrated the use of a drove during the restoration of the old cathedral.

2. NOUN

/ droʊv / (AmE), / drəʊv / (BrE)
a large group of animals, especially cattle, sheep, or pigs, moving together as a herd or flock
group of animals moving together

Examples

The shepherd guided the drove of sheep across the field at dawn.
A drove of cattle blocked the country road for several minutes.
Farmers moved their drove of pigs to the market every autumn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herd - a large group of animals, especially hoofed mammals, that live, feed, or migrate together
  • Synonym: flock - a group of birds or, in some cases, sheep or goats, assembled or moving together

Etymology

From Old English *drāf* (act of driving cattle), related to *drīfan* (to drive).

3. NOUN

/ droʊv / (AmE), / drəʊv / (BrE)
A large group or crowd of people or animals moving together.
large moving crowd

Examples

Tourists arrived in droves to see the newly opened gallery.
Farmers herded sheep in droves across the field during the spring migration.
Protesters gathered in droves at the city square demanding change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herd - a large group of animals, especially hoofed mammals, that live, feed, or migrate together
  • Synonym: flock - a group of birds, sheep, or goats kept or moving together

Etymology

From Old English *drāf* (act of driving).
