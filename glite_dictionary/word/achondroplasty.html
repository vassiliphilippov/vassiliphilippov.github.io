achondroplasty

1. NOUN

/ eɪˈkɑːndɹəˌplæsti / (AmE), / ˌeɪˌkɒndɹəˈplæsti / (BrE)
A rare inherited disorder in which cartilage is converted to bone, resulting in abnormal bone growth and dwarfism.
inherited disorder causing dwarfism
medical

Examples

The child was diagnosed with achondroplasty after showing signs of abnormal bone growth.
Medical experts are studying new treatments for achondroplasty to improve patients' quality of life.
People with achondroplasty may require surgery to manage complications related to their condition.

Etymology

From Greek a- (not) + chondros (cartilage) + -plasty (molding or forming).
