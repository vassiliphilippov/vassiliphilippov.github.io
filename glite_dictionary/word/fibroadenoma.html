fibroadenoma

1. NOUN

/ ˌfaɪbroʊˌædəˈnoʊmə / (AmE), / ˌfaɪbrəʊˌadɪˈnəʊmə / (BrE)
a benign, movable, firm, and non-tender tumor of the breast, commonly found in young women
benign breast tumor
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that the lump was a fibroadenoma and not cancerous.
Many young women discover a fibroadenoma during routine breast self-exams.
Surgery is not always required to remove a fibroadenoma unless it causes discomfort or grows rapidly.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: adenoma - a benign tumor of glandular tissue
  • Degree Higher: carcinoma - a malignant tumor that starts in epithelial tissue of the skin or internal organs

Etymology

From Latinized Greek 'fibra' (fiber) and 'adenoma' (glandular tumor).
