punning

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpʌnɪŋ / (BrE)
the humorous use of words that have more than one meaning, or words that sound similar but mean different things, to create a joke or witty expression
humorous play on words

Examples

His constant punning made the whole group groan and laugh.
She is known for her clever punning during conversations.
Some people appreciate skillful punning, while others find it annoying.

Synset Examples

I do it for the pun of it
his constant punning irritated her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pun - a joke exploiting the different possible meanings of a word

Etymology

From "pun" (to make a pun) + -ing, where "pun" is likely from Italian *puntiglio* (quibble), via French.
