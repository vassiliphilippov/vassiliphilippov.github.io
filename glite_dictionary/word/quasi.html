quasi

1. ADJ

/ ˈkwɑːzi / (AmE), / ˈkwɑːzaɪ / (BrE)
Seeming to be something but not really; having some but not all features of a certain kind.
seeming; having some resemblance

Examples

The committee reached a quasi decision, but nothing was finalized.
He held a quasi leadership role within the organization.
The contract was seen as a quasi agreement and not fully enforceable.

Synset Examples

a quasi success
a quasi contract

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pseudo - not genuine; sham; false
  • Antonym: genuine - truly what something is said to be

Etymology

From Latin quasi, meaning 'as if' or 'as though'.
