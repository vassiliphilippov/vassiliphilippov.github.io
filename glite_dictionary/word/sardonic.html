sardonic

1. ADJ

/ sɑrˈdɑnɪk / (AmE), / sɑːˈdɒnɪk / (BrE)
Displaying a cynical, mocking, or scornful sense of humor; disdainfully or ironically humorous.
scornfully mocking or cynical

Examples

Her sardonic comments made everyone laugh, although some found them a bit harsh.
He gave a sardonic smile as he dismissed their concerns.
The critic's sardonic review stung the director more than he cared to admit.

Synset Examples

his rebellion is the bitter, sardonic laughter of all great satirists

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sarcastic - using irony to mock or convey contempt
  • Synonym: ironic - using words to convey a meaning opposite to their literal meaning
  • Antonym: genuine - sincere and without pretense or irony

Etymology

From French 'sardonique', from Latin 'sardonius', from Greek 'sardonios (gelōs)' meaning 'bitter or scornful (laughter)'.
