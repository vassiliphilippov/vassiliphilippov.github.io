endarteritis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛn.dɑːr.təˈraɪ.tɪs / (AmE), / ˌɛn.dɑː.təˈraɪ.tɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of the innermost lining of an artery, usually caused by infection or autoimmune response
inflammation of artery's inner lining
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with endarteritis of the coronary artery.
Untreated endarteritis can lead to severe complications due to restricted blood flow.
Bacterial infections are a common cause of endarteritis in certain clinical cases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arteritis - inflammation of the walls of an artery

Etymology

From endo- (within) + artery + -itis (inflammation), patterned after similar medical terms.
