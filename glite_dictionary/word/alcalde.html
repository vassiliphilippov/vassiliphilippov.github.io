alcalde

1. NOUN

/ ælˈkɑldeɪ / (AmE), / ælˈkɑːldeɪ / (BrE)
the mayor or chief magistrate of a Spanish town or municipality, especially in historical or legal contexts
mayor/chief magistrate in Spanish town

Examples

The alcalde presided over the town council during the colonial era.
After winning the election, she became the first female alcalde of her hometown.
The alcalde worked to improve relations between local government and citizens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mayor - elected head of a city, town, or municipality

Etymology

From Spanish 'alcalde', from Arabic 'al-qāḍī' (the judge).
