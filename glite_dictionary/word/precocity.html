precocity

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈkɑːsəti / (AmE), / prɪˈkɒsəti / (BrE)
The state of having developed certain abilities or qualities, especially intelligence, at an unusually early age.
unusually early mental or physical development

Examples

Her precocity amazed her teachers, as she could read and write fluently at age four.
The child's musical precocity was evident from his first piano recital.
Many parents worry that precocity in one area might be accompanied by challenges in others.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: maturity - the state of being fully developed in body or mind

Etymology

From French 'précocité', from Latin 'praecocitas', from 'praecox' (early ripening).
