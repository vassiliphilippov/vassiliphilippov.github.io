nirvana

1. NOUN

/ nɪrˈvɑːnə / (AmE), / nəˈvɑːnə / (BrE)
(in Hinduism and Buddhism) a state of perfect happiness and peace, in which there is release from the cycle of birth, death, and rebirth, and suffering and individual consciousness end.
state of perfect peace in Hinduism and Buddhism

Examples

Many Buddhists strive for nirvana through meditation and moral living.
Achieving nirvana means being free from all desires and suffering.
The idea of nirvana offers hope for ultimate liberation in both Hindu and Buddhist traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moksha - liberation from the cycle of birth and rebirth in Hinduism

Etymology

From Sanskrit *nirvāṇa* (extinction, blowing out), referring to release from suffering and rebirth.

2. NOUN

/ nərˈvɑnə / (AmE), / nɪəˈvɑːnə / (BrE)
a place or state of perfect happiness, bliss, and peace; complete freedom from worry or pain
state of perfect happiness and peace

Examples

After weeks of stress at work, the spa felt like nirvana to her.
Some people imagine retirement as a kind of financial nirvana.
He described the quiet mountain cabin as his personal nirvana.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paradise - a place or state of perfect happiness; blissful place
  • Synonym: heaven - a place or condition of utmost happiness
  • Antonym: hell - a place or state of torment and suffering

Etymology

From Sanskrit *nirvāṇa* (extinction, liberation), from *nir-* (out) + *vāṇa* (blowing).
