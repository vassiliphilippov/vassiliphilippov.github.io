Onychogalea

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːnɪkoʊˈɡeɪliə / (AmE), / ˌɒnɪkəʊˈɡeɪlɪə / (BrE)
A genus of marsupials in the family Macropodidae, comprising the nail-tailed wallabies native to Australia.
genus of marsupials (nail-tailed wallabies)

Examples

The Onychogalea genus includes several unique Australian marsupials known as nail-tailed wallabies.
Researchers are working to protect threatened Onychogalea species from habitat loss.
The decline of Onychogalea populations highlights the need for conservation efforts in Australia.

Etymology

From Greek onyx (nail) + galea (weasel), referring to the distinctive nail-tipped tail.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑː.nɪ.koʊˈɡeɪ.li.ə / (AmE), / ˌɒ.nɪ.kəʊˈɡeɪ.li.ə / (BrE)
a genus of marsupials in the family Macropodidae, commonly known as nail-tail wallabies, found in Australia.
genus of nail-tail wallabies

Examples

The Onychogalea genus is distinguished by the unique horny spur on the tail of each species.
Scientists studied the decline of Onychogalea populations in their natural habitats.
Three species belong to the genus Onychogalea, all native to Australia.

Etymology

From Greek 'onycho-' (nail) + 'galea' (helmet or cap), referring to the characteristic tail spur.
