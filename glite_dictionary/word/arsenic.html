arsenic

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrsənɪk / (AmE), / ˈɑːs(ə)nɪk / (BrE)
a chemical element (symbol: As), a grey, very poisonous metalloid used in herbicides, insecticides, and various alloys
a poisonous chemical element (symbol As)

Examples

The factory was shut down after arsenic was found in the drinking water.
Some old pesticides contain dangerous levels of arsenic that can harm the environment.
Researchers detected traces of arsenic in the soil near the mining site.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antimony - a brittle, silvery-white metalloid element, related chemically to arsenic

Etymology

From Latin *arsenicum*, from Greek *arsenikon*, ultimately from Persian *zarniḵ* (yellow orpiment).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑːr.sə.nɪk / (AmE), / ˈɑː.sə.nɪk / (BrE)
a poisonous white powder (arsenic trioxide), used in making glass, pesticides, and rat poison
a poisonous chemical used in industry and pesticides

Examples

The criminal added arsenic to the victim’s drink, causing his untimely death.
Arsenic compounds have historically been used as rat poison and insecticide.
The glass manufacturing industry uses arsenic as a refining agent despite its toxicity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poison - a substance that causes harm or death when absorbed or ingested

Etymology

From Latin arsenicum, from Greek arsenikon (yellow orpiment), from Persian zarnik (gold-colored).
