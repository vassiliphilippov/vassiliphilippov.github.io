VAT

1. NOUN

/ viː eɪ tiː / (AmE), / viː eɪ tiː / (BrE)
a tax on the value added to goods and services at each stage of production or distribution, collected by the end retailer
value-added tax; sales tax on goods/services

Examples

Many European countries include VAT in the listed price of goods and services.
Companies must register for VAT if their turnover exceeds a certain threshold.
The government increased the VAT rate to boost public revenue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sales tax - a tax on sales or on the receipts from sales

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'value added tax'.
