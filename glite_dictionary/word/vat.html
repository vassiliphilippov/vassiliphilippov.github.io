vat

1. NOUN

/ væt / (AmE), / væt / (BrE)
a large container, typically an open vessel, used for storing or holding liquids, especially in industrial processes
large open container for liquids

Examples

The winery uses a large vat to ferment its grapes before bottling the wine.
Workers poured the dye into a wooden vat for the coloring process.
After cleaning, the tools were soaked in a disinfectant vat overnight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tank - a large container for storing liquid or gas

Etymology

From Old French *vat*, from Old English *fæt* (vessel, vat).
