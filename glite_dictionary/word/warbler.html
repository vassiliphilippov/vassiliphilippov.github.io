warbler

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔrb.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɔː.blə / (BrE)
A small, active songbird known for its musical or varied singing.
small songbird

Examples

The warbler perched on a branch, filling the early morning with its cheerful song.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot a rare species of warbler in the marsh.
Many warblers migrate thousands of miles each year to find suitable breeding grounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: songbird - a bird known for singing musical songs

Etymology

From 'warble', meaning to sing softly and melodiously, from Old French 'warbler'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔrb.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːb.lə / (BrE)
a singer, especially one who sings with embellishments or trills
singer who adds trills or embellishments

Examples

The young warbler captivated the audience with her soaring trills.
Critics praised the opera's lead warbler for his expressive voice.
As a skilled warbler, she brought a unique flair to every performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: singer - person who sings
  • Synonym: crooner - a singer, especially one who sings sentimental songs in a soft, low voice

Etymology

From 'warble', meaning to sing with trills or quavers, ultimately from Old French 'werbler' (to sing with trills).
