chrysarobin

1. NOUN

/ kraɪsəˌroʊbɪn / (AmE), / kraɪsəˈrəʊbɪn / (BrE)
a yellow, bitter powder obtained from certain plants and used in the treatment of skin diseases, particularly psoriasis and eczema
yellow powder for treating skin diseases
medical

Examples

Topical ointments containing chrysarobin were once common for treating stubborn cases of psoriasis.
The dermatologist recommended a cream with chrysarobin to relieve the patient's chronic skin irritation.
Allergic reactions to chrysarobin are rare but have been documented in medical literature.

Etymology

From Greek chrysos (gold) and Latin arbores (trees), referencing its source and color.
