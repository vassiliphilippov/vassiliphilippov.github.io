ulemorrhagia

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuːlɪməˈreɪdʒiə / (AmE), / ˌjuːlɪməˈreɪdʒɪə / (BrE)
Excessive or abnormal bleeding of the gums.
bleeding of the gums
medical

Examples

The dentist noted signs of ulemorrhagia during the patient's routine checkup.
Persistent ulemorrhagia may indicate underlying gum disease or vitamin deficiency.
Improper oral hygiene is a common cause of ulemorrhagia among adults.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gingival bleeding - bleeding from the gums, especially as a sign of disease

Etymology

From Greek 'ulē' (gum) and 'rhēgnynai' (to break, burst forth).
