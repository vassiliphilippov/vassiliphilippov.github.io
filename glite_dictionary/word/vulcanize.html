to vulcanize

1. VERB

/ ˈvʌlkəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvʌlkənaɪz / (BrE)
to treat (rubber or similar materials) with heat and sulfur or other agents to improve elasticity and strength
treat rubber with heat and chemicals

Examples

Modern tire factories routinely vulcanize rubber to ensure durability and safety.
Charles Goodyear discovered a way to vulcanize natural rubber, revolutionizing the industry.
Engineers must carefully monitor temperature when they vulcanize materials for industrial products.

Synset Examples

vulcanized rubber

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cure - to process rubber by heat to make it elastic and strong

Etymology

From Latin Vulcanus (the Roman god of fire), reflecting the use of heat in the process.

2. VERB

/ ˈvʌl.kəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvʌl.kə.naɪz / (BrE)
to treat rubber or similar materials with sulfur and heat to improve strength and elasticity
treat rubber with sulfur and heat

Examples

Charles Goodyear was the first to vulcanize rubber, revolutionizing the tire industry.
Modern factories vulcanize rubber to produce durable soles for shoes.
Without being vulcanized, natural rubber remains sticky and weak.

Synset Examples

vulcanize rubber

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cure - to treat rubber with heat (general term for hardening rubber)

Etymology

From Latin Vulcanus (the Roman god of fire), as the process involves heat.
