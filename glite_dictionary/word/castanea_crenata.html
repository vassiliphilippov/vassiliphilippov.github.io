Castanea crenata

1. NOUN

/ kæsˌteɪniə kriˈnɑːtə / (AmE), / kæsˌtiːniə krɛˈnɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of chestnut tree native to Japan and Korea, known for its short trunk and spreading form; commonly called the Japanese chestnut.
Japanese chestnut tree species

Examples

The Castanea crenata is widely cultivated in Japan for its edible nuts.
Researchers studied the disease resistance of Castanea crenata compared to other chestnut species.
Gardeners often plant Castanea crenata for its attractive spreading canopy and useful timber.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Japanese chestnut - the common name for Castanea crenata, a species of chestnut tree

Etymology

From Latin: 'Castanea' meaning chestnut, and 'crenata' meaning with scalloped edges, referring to the leaf margin.
