suctorial

1. ADJ

/ sʌkˈtɔriəl / (AmE), / sʌkˈtɔːriəl / (BrE)
(of an organ or part) adapted for sucking or clinging by means of suction.
adapted for sucking or clinging by suction

Examples

The leech attaches to its host using its suctorial mouthparts.
Certain frogs have developed suctorial disks on their toes to climb smooth surfaces.
The remora’s suctorial organ allows it to cling to larger marine animals for transportation.

Etymology

From Latin 'suctorius' (relating to sucking), from 'sugere' (to suck).
