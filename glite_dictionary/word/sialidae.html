Sialidae

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈælɪdiː / (AmE), / saɪˈalɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of insects in the order Megaloptera, commonly known as alderflies, found near water and characterized by soft bodies and long antennae.
family of insects (alderflies)

Examples

Members of the Sialidae family are often observed near streams and ponds.
The Sialidae are distinguished from related insect families by their broad wings and aquatic larvae.
Entomologists collected several specimens of Sialidae during their survey of the wetland ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alderfly - common name for insects of the family Sialidae

Etymology

From New Latin Sialidae, derived from Sialis (type genus) + -idae (taxonomic family suffix).
