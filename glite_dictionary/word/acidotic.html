acidotic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæs.ɪˈdoʊ.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌæs.ɪˈdɒt.ɪk / (BrE)
characterized by or pertaining to acidosis, a condition in which there is too much acid in the body fluids
affected by or relating to acidosis
medical

Examples

The patient became acidotic after several hours without treatment.
Doctors monitored his blood gas levels for signs of an acidotic state.
An acidotic crisis can be life-threatening if not managed promptly.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: alkalotic - characterized by or pertaining to alkalosis, an excess of base in the body fluids

Etymology

From 'acidosis' + '-tic' (adjectival suffix).
