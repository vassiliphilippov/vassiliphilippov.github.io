brit

1. NOUN

/ brɪt / (AmE), / brɪt / (BrE)
minute marine crustaceans that form an important food source for right whales
minute crustaceans eaten by whales

Examples

The blue whale feeds primarily on brit during its migration.
Scientists studied the abundance of brit in Antarctic waters.
Large swarms of brit attract many types of baleen whales.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: krill - small crustaceans that are a major food source for whales

2. NOUN

/ brɪt / (AmE), / brɪt / (BrE)
the young of a herring, sprat, or similar small fish
young herring or sprat

Examples

Large schools of brit can often be seen in coastal waters during spring.
Seabirds feed extensively on brit each nesting season.
The fisherman explained that brit are a vital part of the ocean's food chain.
