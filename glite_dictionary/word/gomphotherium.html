Gomphotherium

1. NOUN

/ ɡɑːmfəˈθɪriəm / (AmE), / ɡɒmfəˈθɪəriəm / (BrE)
An extinct genus of large, elephant-like mammals that lived during the Miocene and Pliocene epochs.
extinct genus of prehistoric elephant-like mammals

Examples

The fossils of Gomphotherium have been discovered in both Eurasia and North America.
Scientists believe that Gomphotherium had four tusks and lived in marshy areas.
Unlike modern elephants, Gomphotherium had a more elongated lower jaw.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mastodon - extinct genus of elephant-like mammals related to Gomphotherium
  • Degree Higher: elephant - large, living herbivorous mammal related to extinct gomphotheres

Etymology

From Greek gomphos (peg, bolt) + therion (beast), referring to its peg-like teeth.

2. NOUN

/ ɡɑmfəˈθɪəriəm / (AmE), / ɡɒmfəˈθɪəriəm / (BrE)
A genus of extinct prehistoric elephants that were the type genus of the family Gomphotheriidae.
genus of extinct prehistoric elephants

Examples

The fossils of Gomphotherium were discovered in both Eurasia and North America.
Scientists believe Gomphotherium had four tusks and a trunk similar to modern elephants.
Unlike modern elephants, Gomphotherium grazed near ancient lakes and rivers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mastodon - an extinct large-tusked mammal related to elephants

Etymology

From Greek gomphos (bolt, peg) + thērion (beast), referring to the animal's peg-like teeth.
