/ ˈjuːnəˌtɛri / (AmE), / ˈjuːnɪtəˌri / (BrE)
relating to or involving the use of units, especially as a method or standard in measurement or calculation.
relating to use of units in measurement or calculation

Examples

A unitary method was employed to solve the equations.
The researchers developed a unitary approach for calculating the results.
They agreed to standardize the measurement system by adopting a unitary scale.

Synset Examples

Etymology

From Latin unitarius, from 'unitas' (oneness, unity).

4. ADJ

/ ˈjuː.nə.ˌtɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈjuː.nɪ.təri / (BrE)
(of a country or system) characterized by a single central government in which most or all powers are held by the central authority, rather than divided among regional governments.
centralized government; not federal

Examples

France is governed as a unitary state, with power concentrated in the national government.
Unlike the United States, which is federal, the United Kingdom has traditionally been described as unitary.
A unitary system allows policies to be applied uniformly across the entire country.

Synset Examples

a unitary as opposed to a federal form of government

Related Terms

  • Antonym: federal - relating to a system in which power is shared between a central authority and constituent regions or states

Etymology

From Late Latin unitarius (of unity), from Latin unitas (oneness, unity).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a
