spectrograph

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɛktɹəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈspɛk.trə.ɡrɑːf / (BrE)
An instrument for recording and photographing spectra, used especially in scientific analysis.
instrument for recording spectra

Examples

The astronomer used a spectrograph to analyze the light from distant stars.
Modern laboratories often rely on a spectrograph for detailed molecular analysis.
Calibration of the spectrograph is essential for obtaining accurate measurement results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spectroscope - instrument for observing spectra

Etymology

From spectrum + -graph, indicating a device that records spectra.

2. NOUN

/ ˈspɛktɹəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈspɛktrəɡrɑːf / (BrE)
A photograph or other visual record produced by a spectroscope, showing the spectrum of light or other radiation from a source.
photographic record of a spectrum

Examples

The scientist examined the spectrograph to determine the composition of the unknown gas.
A clear spectrograph can reveal the unique spectral lines emitted by a star.
After running the experiment, they studied the spectrograph for evidence of new elements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spectrogram - a visual or photographic record of a spectrum of frequencies

Etymology

From spectrum + -graph, indicating an instrument for recording spectra.
