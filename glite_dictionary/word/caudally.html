caudally

1. ADV

/ ˈkɔdəli / (AmE), / ˈkɔːdəli / (BrE)
In a direction toward the posterior (tail) end of the body, especially in anatomy or zoology.
toward the tail or posterior end
medical

Examples

The spinal cord extends caudally from the brainstem.
Nerves branching caudally are responsible for lower limb movement.
The surgeon made an incision caudally to the previous scar.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cranially - toward the head or anterior end

Etymology

From Latin 'cauda' (tail) + '-al' + '-ly'.
