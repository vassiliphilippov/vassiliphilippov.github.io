to unitize

1. VERB

/ ˈjuːnɪˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈjuːnɪtʌɪz / (BrE)
to separate or classify something into individual units
separate or classify into units

Examples

The company decided to unitize its production process to increase efficiency.
In the new packaging system, products are unitized before shipping.
She suggested that we unitize the data to better analyze the results.

Synset Examples

The hospital was unitized for efficiency

Related Terms

  • Synonym: divide - to separate into parts or groups
  • Antonym: combine - to join or merge together to make a whole

Etymology

From 'unit' + '-ize', patterned after similar verbs formed with the suffix '-ize'.

2. VERB

/ ˈjuːnɪˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈjuːnɪtʌɪz / (BrE)
to divide bulk material and process it as separate units, especially for handling, shipping, or storage
divide and process as units

Examples

Companies often unitize goods before shipping to make handling easier.
The warehouse workers unitized the bulk flour into fifty-pound bags.
We are planning to unitize all incoming materials for more efficient storage.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to consolidate - to combine into a single whole

Etymology

From 'unit' + '-ize', forming a verb meaning to make into units.

Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈjuː.nəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈjuː.nɪ.taɪz / (BrE)
to make something into a single unit or group; to organize or package as a unit, especially for convenience or efficiency
make into a unit or organized group

Examples

The company decided to unitize its packaging process for efficiency.
Automotive engineers may unitize car bodies to reduce production costs.
We need to unitize these shipments to maximize storage space.

Synset Examples

unitize a car body

Related Terms

  • Antonym: divide - to separate into parts or groups
  • Synonym: combine - to join or merge into a single unit or group

Etymology

Formed from 'unit' + suffix '-ize', meaning to make or become.
