Priapus

1. PROPN

/ praɪˈeɪpəs / (AmE), / praɪˈeɪpəs / (BrE)
In classical mythology, a god associated with male procreative power and a guardian of gardens and vineyards.
god of male fertility and gardens (mythology)

Examples

The statue of Priapus stood at the entrance to the ancient vineyard.
Many Roman gardens featured images of Priapus to bless their crops with fertility.
References to Priapus in poetry often symbolize fertility and male virility.

Etymology

From Latin Priapus, from Greek Priapos, the name of the god.
