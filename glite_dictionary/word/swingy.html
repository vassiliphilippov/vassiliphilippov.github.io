swingy

1. ADJ

/ ˈswɪŋi / (AmE), / ˈswɪŋi / (BrE)
Having a lively or buoyant rhythm, especially in music or movement.
lively or buoyant rhythm

Examples

The jazz band's performance had a swingy feel that kept everyone tapping their feet.
She loved the swingy motion of her skirt as she danced across the stage.
The composer preferred a swingy rhythm in his latest musical piece.

Synset Examples

an easy lilting stride
the flute broke into a light lilting air
a swinging pace
a graceful swingy walk
a tripping singing measure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lilting - having a cheerful and rhythmic quality
  • Synonym: rhythmic - having or relating to rhythm

Etymology

From 'swing' + -y, patterned after adjectives describing qualities.
