spindle

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɪn.dl̩ / (AmE), / ˈspɪn.dl̩ / (BrE)
(biology) a structure formed of fibers during cell division, radiating from two poles and meeting at the equator, which helps distribute chromosomes.
cell-division structure in biology
medical

Examples

During mitosis, the spindle ensures that each daughter cell receives the correct number of chromosomes.
Under the microscope, scientists observed the spindle forming between opposite poles of the cell.
Errors in spindle formation can lead to problems in cell division.

Synset Examples

chromosomes are distributed by spindles in mitosis and meiosis

Etymology

From Old English spindel, related to spinning, due to its shape.

/ ˈspɪn.dl̩ / (AmE), / ˈspɪn.dl̩ / (BrE)

Examples

Ancient spinners commonly used a drop spindle to turn wool into yarn.
A wooden spindle lay beside the basket of freshly carded fibers.
