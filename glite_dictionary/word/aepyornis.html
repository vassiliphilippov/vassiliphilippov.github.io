aepyornis

1. NOUN

/ ˌiːpiˈɔrnɪs / (AmE), / ˌiːpɪˈɔːnɪs / (BrE)
an extinct, very large, flightless bird from Madagascar, known to have reached up to 9 feet in height
extinct giant flightless bird of Madagascar

Examples

The remains of an aepyornis egg were discovered on a remote beach in Madagascar.
Many scientists believe that the aepyornis went extinct around the 17th century.
The aepyornis was one of the largest birds ever to have lived.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elephant bird - another name for the aepyornis

Etymology

From Greek aipus (high) + ornis (bird), referring to its great size.
