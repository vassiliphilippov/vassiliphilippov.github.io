labetalol

1. NOUN

/ ləˈbeɪtəˌlɔl / (AmE), / ləˈbiːtəlɒl / (BrE)
a medication used to treat high blood pressure by blocking both alpha and beta-adrenergic receptors in the sympathetic nervous system
blood pressure-lowering medication
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed labetalol to help control her high blood pressure.
Labetalol is often administered intravenously in emergencies to quickly lower blood pressure.
After starting labetalol, he noticed fewer side effects compared to his previous medication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antihypertensive - a drug used to treat high blood pressure
