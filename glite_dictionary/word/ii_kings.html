II Kings

1. PROPN

/ ˌsɛkənd ˈkɪŋz / (AmE), / ˌsɛkənd ˈkɪŋz / (BrE)
the second book of Kings in the Old Testament, recounting the histories of the kingdoms of Judah and Israel
Old Testament book about Israelite kings

Examples

Many of the events in II Kings are central to the history of ancient Israel.
He quoted a passage from II Kings during his sermon.
My grandmother read stories from II Kings when I was a child.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: I Kings - the first book of Kings in the Old Testament

Etymology

From Latin 'Liber Regum Secundus' meaning 'Second Book of Kings', adopted in English Bible translations.
