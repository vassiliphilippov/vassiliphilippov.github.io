sardius

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑr.di.əs / (AmE), / ˈsɑː.dɪ.əs / (BrE)
a deep orange-red variety of the mineral chalcedony, used as a gemstone
deep orange-red chalcedony gemstone

Examples

The ancient ring was set with a sardius engraved with mysterious symbols.
Collectors prize the sardius for its striking orange-red hue and rarity.
She wore a necklace featuring a polished sardius pendant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carnelian - a reddish variety of chalcedony used as a gemstone

Etymology

From Latin 'sardius', from Greek 'sardios', related to Sardis, an ancient city in Asia Minor.
