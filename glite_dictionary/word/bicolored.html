bicolored

1. ADJ

/ ˈbaɪˌkʌlərd / (AmE), / ˈbʌɪˌkʌləd / (BrE)
Having two different colors; marked by two distinct colors.
having two colors

Examples

The rare parrot has a bicolored crest of red and blue feathers.
She admired the bicolored scarf, which was half green and half yellow.
The artist painted a stunning bicolored landscape for the exhibition.

Synset Examples

a bicolor flower
a bicolored postage stamp

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monochromatic - having only one color
  • Degree Higher: tricolored - having three colors
  • Degree Higher: multicolored - having many colors

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'bi-' (two) + 'colored' (having color).
