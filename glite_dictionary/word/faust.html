Faust

1. PROPN

/ faʊst / (AmE), / faʊst / (BrE)
A legendary German alchemist who makes a pact with the devil in exchange for knowledge and power; also, the central character in many literary works based on this legend.
legendary figure who sold his soul for knowledge

Examples

Many plays and books have been inspired by the story of Faust and his fateful bargain.
The term 'Faustian bargain' comes from the legend of Faust selling his soul for knowledge.
Goethe's Faust is considered one of the greatest works in German literature.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Mephistopheles - the devil in the Faust legend

Etymology

German, from the legendary character's name in German folklore.
