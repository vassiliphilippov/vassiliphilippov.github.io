burnability

1. NOUN

/ ˌbɜrnəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌbɜːnəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality or property of being capable of igniting and burning.
ability to ignite and burn

Examples

The burnability of the fabric is tested to ensure it meets safety standards.
Researchers are comparing the burnability of different types of wood for efficient fuel use.
Knowing the burnability of building materials is vital for fire prevention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flammability - the property of being easily set on fire
  • Antonym: nonflammability - the property of being not easily set on fire

Etymology

From 'burn' + '-ability', modeled on 'ability' suffix.
