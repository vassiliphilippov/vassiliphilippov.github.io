treated

1. ADJ

/ ˈtriːtɪd / (AmE), / ˈtriːtɪd / (BrE)
Given medical care or subjected to a medical procedure or treatment.
having received medical care

Examples

The wound was carefully treated to prevent infection.
A treated patient is more likely to recover quickly than an untreated one.
After being treated for her illness, she returned home feeling much better.

Synset Examples

a treated cold is usually gone in 14 days; if left untreated it lasts two weeks

Related Terms

  • Antonym: untreated - not having received medical care or treatment
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01962094-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈtritɪd / (AmE), / ˈtriːtɪd / (BrE)
having been subjected to a physical or chemical process to improve or alter its properties
altered by a process or agent

Examples

The treated wood can last decades even in harsh weather conditions.
All treated fabrics in the collection are resistant to stains and shrinkage.
After passing through the plant, the treated water is safe to drink.

Synset Examples

the sludge of treated sewage can be used as fertilizer
treated timbers resist rot
treated fabrics resist wrinkling

Related Terms

  • Antonym: untreated - not having been subjected to a process or agent
  • Synonym: processed - having undergone a special process, especially in industry
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01960457-a
Synset POS: a
