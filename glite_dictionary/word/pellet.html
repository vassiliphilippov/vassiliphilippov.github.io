pellet

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛl.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɛl.ɪt / (BrE)
A small, solid projectile fired from a firearm, often as part of a shotgun cartridge.
small solid missile fired from a gun

Examples

The hunter loaded his shotgun with pellets before heading into the woods.
A single pellet struck the tin can, denting it.
Several pellets embedded themselves in the tree trunk after the gun was fired.

Synset Examples

the shot buzzed past his ear

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bullet - a metal projectile for firearms, usually larger than a pellet

Etymology

From Old French *pelote* (small ball), from Vulgar Latin *pilotta*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛlɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɛlɪt / (BrE)
a small, rounded or cylindrical mass of a substance, especially one prepared by compressing material together
small rounded mass of a substance

Examples

The rabbit left a trail of pellets on the grass.
She loaded the air rifle with a single metal pellet.
The medication comes in the form of tiny pellets that dissolve under the tongue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: granule - a small, grain-like particle
  • Synonym: ball - a solid or hollow spherical or egg-shaped object
  • Synonym: lump - a compact mass of a substance

Etymology

From Middle French pelote (small ball), from Old French pelote.
