priestcraft

1. NOUN

/ ˈprist.kræft / (AmE), / ˈpriːs(t).krɑːft / (BrE)
The use of religious authority by priests to control secular or political affairs, often for personal or institutional gain (usually derogatory).
manipulation of secular affairs by priests (derogatory)

Examples

Some critics accused the church of priestcraft when it began to intervene in political matters.
The novel exposes the dangers of priestcraft and unchecked religious power.
Debates about priestcraft were common during the Enlightenment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clericalism - political influence or involvement of the clergy

Etymology

Formed from 'priest' + 'craft' indicating skill or artifice employed by priests.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpristˌkræft / (AmE), / ˈpriːs(t)ˌkrɑːft / (BrE)
the skills, methods, or practices associated with priests and their religious duties, often implying manipulation or secretiveness
skills and methods of priests

Examples

The villagers distrusted the old superstitions and the priestcraft that kept them alive.
Some writers accused the church leadership of using priestcraft to maintain power over the people.
The reformer denounced priestcraft and called for greater religious transparency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clergy - the body of people ordained for religious duties
  • Antonym: laity - lay people as distinguished from the clergy

Etymology

Formed from 'priest' + 'craft', modeled after similar compounds; first recorded in the 16th century.
