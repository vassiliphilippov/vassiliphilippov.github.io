artifact

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːrtəˌfækt / (AmE), / ˈɑːtɪfɑːkt / (BrE)
an object made by a human being, typically one of cultural or historical interest
a man-made object

Examples

The museum displayed an ancient artifact found during the excavation.
Each artifact tells a story about the culture that produced it.
Archaeologists carefully cleaned the fragile artifact before studying it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: object - a material thing that can be seen or touched
  • Antonym: natural object - an object occurring naturally, not made by humans

Etymology

From Latin arte factum (something made by skill), from ars (skill) + facere (to make).
