ravening

1. ADJ

/ ˈrævənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrævənɪŋ / (BrE)
extremely hungry or greedy; voracious, especially in a way that is aggressive or rapacious.
extremely greedy or voracious

Examples

The ravening wolves circled the herd, ready to attack at any moment.
She felt a ravening hunger after climbing the mountain all morning.
A ravening mob rushed into the store during the frantic sale.

Synset Examples

a rapacious divorcee on the prowl
ravening creditors
paying taxes to voracious governments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rapacious - aggressively greedy or grasping
  • Synonym: voracious - wanting or devouring great quantities of food

Etymology

From Middle English ravenen (to devour), from Old French raviner (to seize, plunder).

2. ADJ

/ ˈrævənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈreɪvənɪŋ / (BrE)
(of an animal or person) extremely hungry and hunting for food; devouring or eager to devour in great quantities.
devouring or searching for food hungrily

Examples

A pack of ravening wolves chased the frightened deer through the forest.
The ravening crowd emptied the buffet table in minutes.
He described the pirates as ravening beasts, desperate for plunder and food.

Synset Examples

edacious vultures
a rapacious appetite
ravenous as wolves
voracious sharks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ravenous - extremely hungry or greedy for food
  • Synonym: voracious - having a very eager approach to food or activity
  • Antonym: sated - having had more than enough of something, especially food

Etymology

From Middle English 'ravynen' (to take by force), from Old French 'raviner', from Latin 'rapina' (plunder).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00010745-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈrævənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrævənɪŋ / (BrE)
(of animals or people) living by preying on and devouring other animals; extremely hungry and hunting for food.
preying on other animals; extremely hungry

Examples

The pack of ravening wolves descended on the herd at dusk.
After days without food, his ravening hunger drove him to desperation.
The story described ravening beasts that roamed the forest at night.

Synset Examples

a predatory bird
the rapacious wolf
raptorial birds
ravening wolves
a vulturine taste for offal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predatory - living by preying on others
  • Synonym: rapacious - aggressively greedy or grasping
  • Antonym: herbivorous - feeding only on plants

Etymology

From Middle English raven, from Old French raviner (to seize by force), from Latin rapina (plunder).
