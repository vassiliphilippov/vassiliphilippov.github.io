nib

1. NOUN

/ nɪb / (AmE), / nɪb / (BrE)
the pointed metal or plastic part at the end of a pen that touches the paper when you write
writing point of a pen

Examples

He replaced the nib of his fountain pen after it became worn out.
Calligraphers often use pens with different nibs to achieve varied line thickness.
Make sure the nib is clean before you begin writing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tip - the pointed end of something, especially for writing

Etymology

Possibly from nibbe (Middle English), from Old Norse neb (beak, nose).

2. NOUN

/ nɪb / (AmE), / nɪb / (BrE)
the hard, projecting tip of a bird's beak, especially the horny part
horny beak tip of a bird

Examples

The hawk used its nib to tear into its prey.
The ornithologist examined the distinct curve of the eagle's nib.
Some birds have a specially adapted nib for cracking seeds.
