ungainliness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈɡeɪnlɪnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈɡeɪnlɪnɪs / (BrE)
awkwardness or lack of grace in movement or posture
awkwardness in movement or posture

Examples

Her ungainliness on the dance floor made her stand out among the graceful performers.
Despite his ungainliness, he managed to complete the obstacle course with determination.
The sculpture captured the ungainliness of a young foal taking its first steps.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: awkwardness - the quality of being awkward in movement or manner
  • Antonym: gracefulness - the quality of being smooth, elegant, and attractive in movement or manner

Etymology

From 'ungainly' + '-ness'; 'ungainly' from Middle English, formed with 'un-' (not) and 'gainly' (direct, convenient).
