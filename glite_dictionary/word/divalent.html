divalent

1. ADJ

/ daɪˈveɪlənt / (AmE), / daɪˈveɪlənt / (BrE)
(of an atom or ion) having a valence of two; capable of combining with two other atoms or ions.
having a valence of two (chemistry)
medical

Examples

Calcium is a divalent cation essential for many biological processes.
The chemist studied how the divalent ions interacted with the protein.
Some elements can exist in both divalent and trivalent forms depending on the chemical reaction.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: monovalent - having a valence of one
  • Degree Higher: trivalent - having a valence of three

Etymology

From Latin 'di-' (two) + 'valent' (from 'valentia', strength).
