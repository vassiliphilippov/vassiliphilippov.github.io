Ni-hard

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪˌhɑrd / (AmE), / ˈnaɪˌhɑːd / (BrE)
a type of cast iron alloy containing nickel, designed to resist wear and abrasion
wear-resistant nickel cast iron

Examples

The factory replaced standard castings with Ni-hard parts to improve machine durability.
Due to its superior wear resistance, Ni-hard is often used in mining equipment.
Engineers selected Ni-hard for the liners in the new processing plant.

Etymology

From chemical symbol ‘Ni’ for nickel + ‘hard’.
