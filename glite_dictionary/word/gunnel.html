gunnel

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌnəl / (AmE), / ˈɡʌnəl / (BrE)
A small, slender, eellike marine fish found commonly in shallow waters of the northern Atlantic.
small eellike fish of the northern Atlantic

Examples

We saw several gunnel darting between the rocks during our tide pool exploration.
A gunnel can easily be mistaken for a young eel because of its slim body.
The biologist collected a gunnel as part of her survey of local fish species.

Etymology

From Old Norse *gunnildi*, a name for certain fish.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌnəl / (AmE), / ˈɡʌnəl / (BrE)
The upper edge of the side of a boat or ship; the topmost plank or reinforcing band along the hull.
upper edge of a boat's side

Examples

The fisherman perched his rod on the gunnel as he waited for a bite.
When scrubbing down the sailboat, make sure to clean along the gunnel as well.
He secured the oars underneath the gunnel before they started rowing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gunwale - the upper edge of the side of a boat or ship

Etymology

Alteration of 'gunwale', from Middle English 'gonwale', originally meaning the wale (plank) at which guns were mounted.
