pitilessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪt̬.i.ləs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈpɪtɪlɪsnəs / (BrE)
The quality of lacking pity or compassion; mercilessness.
lack of pity or compassion

Examples

The pitilessness of the dictator's actions shocked the world.
In the novel, the harsh winter is described with a sense of pitilessness.
Her pitilessness toward her rivals made her feared in the business world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mercilessness - the state of having no mercy
  • Antonym: compassion - sympathetic pity and concern for the sufferings of others

Etymology

From 'pitiless' (without pity) + '-ness' (forming nouns denoting a state or quality).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɪt̬iˌlɪsnəs / (AmE), / ˈpɪtɪlɪsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of having no pity or compassion; extreme lack of sympathy or mercy for others
lack of pity or compassion

Examples

The villain's pitilessness shocked everyone in the courtroom.
History remembers dictators for their cruelty and pitilessness toward their own people.
Her gaze was filled with pitilessness as she rejected his plea for help.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruthlessness - lack of pity or compassion, often implying cruelty
  • Antonym: compassion - sympathetic pity and concern for the sufferings of others

Etymology

Formed from 'pitiless' + '-ness', denoting the state or quality.
