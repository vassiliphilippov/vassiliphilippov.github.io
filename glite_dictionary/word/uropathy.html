uropathy

1. NOUN

/ jʊˈrɑːpəθi / (AmE), / jʊəˈrɒpəθi / (BrE)
any disease or disorder affecting the urinary tract
disease of the urinary tract
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed him with a rare form of uropathy.
Chronic uropathy can lead to kidney failure if left untreated.
Researchers are developing new treatments to manage different types of uropathy.

Etymology

From Greek 'ouron' (urine) + '-pathy' (disease).
