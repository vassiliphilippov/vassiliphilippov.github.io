webbing

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛbɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɛbɪŋ / (BrE)
a membranous structure resembling a web, especially the skin connecting the toes of some animals, such as birds and amphibians
skin or membrane connecting animal toes

Examples

Many aquatic birds have webbing between their toes to help them swim efficiently.
The frog's webbing allows it to move quickly through water.
The duckling's webbing was clearly visible as it paddled in the pond.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: membrane - a thin layer of tissue covering a surface; a biological partition

Etymology

From 'web' + '-ing', referring to the formation of a web-like structure.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɛbɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɛbɪŋ / (BrE)
a strong, closely woven fabric made in narrow strips, used especially for straps and supports
strong woven fabric in strips

Examples

The backpack has reinforced webbing to support heavy loads.
Military belts are often made from durable webbing.
The craftsman used cotton webbing to repair the chair seat.

3. NOUN

/ ˈwɛbɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɛbɪŋ / (BrE)
a strong, narrow strip of closely woven fabric, often used in upholstery, harnesses, or for making seat belts
strong narrow woven tape

Examples

The backpack straps are reinforced with durable webbing for extra strength.
The seat belts are made from strong nylon webbing to ensure safety.
She learned how to attach the webbing to the wooden frame when reupholstering the chair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tape - a long, narrow strip of material used for binding or fastening
  • Synonym: strap - a strip of flexible material used for fastening, holding, or carrying

Etymology

From 'web' (Old English 'webb'), referring to woven fabric, plus '-ing'.
