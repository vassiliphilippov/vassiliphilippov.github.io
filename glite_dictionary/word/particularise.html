to particularise

1. VERB

/ pərˈtɪkjələˌraɪz / (AmE), / pəˈtɪkjʊləraɪz / (BrE)
to state or describe something in detail; to be specific about something
describe in detail; be specific
british

Examples

She asked the witness to particularise the events leading up to the incident.
In your application, please particularise any relevant work experience you have.
The report does not particularise how the funds were allocated.

Synset Examples

Could you please specify your criticism of my paper?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to specify - to state something clearly and exactly
  • Antonym: to generalise - to make something more general and less specific

Etymology

From particular + -ise, following the pattern of formation from the adjective 'particular' and the verb-forming suffix '-ise' (British English).
