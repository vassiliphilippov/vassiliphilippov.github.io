sensationalist

1. NOUN

/ sɛnˈseɪʃənəlɪst / (AmE), / sɛnˈseɪʃənəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who presents news or information in a way that is intended to provoke public interest or excitement, often by exaggerating or sensationalizing the facts.
person who sensationalizes information

Examples

The reporter was accused of being a sensationalist who cared more about ratings than the truth.
Many tabloids are run by sensationalists eager for dramatic headlines.
He dismissed the documentary as the work of a sensationalist rather than a serious journalist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensationalism - the use of exciting or shocking stories at the expense of accuracy

Etymology

From sensation + -alist, formed after sensationalism (late 19th century).
