Elijah

1. PROPN

/ ɪˈlaɪdʒə / (AmE), / ɪˈlaɪdʒə / (BrE)
(in the Bible) a Hebrew prophet who opposed idol worship in the kingdom of Israel and was taken to heaven in a chariot of fire.
Hebrew prophet in the Old Testament

Examples

Many artists have depicted Elijah being taken up to heaven in a chariot of fire.
The story of Elijah is recounted in the Book of Kings in the Old Testament.
Jews set a place for Elijah at the Passover Seder as part of tradition.

Etymology

From Hebrew ‘Ēlīyāhū meaning 'my God is Yahweh'.
