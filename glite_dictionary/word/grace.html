Grace

1. PROPN

/ ɡreɪs / (AmE), / ɡreɪs / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) one of the three minor goddesses, known as the Graces, who personify beauty, charm, and gracefulness.
one of the three goddesses of charm and beauty in Greek mythology

Examples

The sculptor created a masterpiece depicting the three Graces dancing together.
In classical art, the Graces are often shown bestowing beauty and charm on mortals.
The story centers on one of the Graces and her gift of gentleness to humanity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Charite - another name for one of the Graces in Greek mythology
  • Degree Lower: Muse - one of the inspirational goddesses of literature, science, and the arts in Greek mythology
  • Degree Higher: Aphrodite - Greek goddess of love and beauty, a higher-ranking deity of beauty

Etymology

From Latin 'Gratiae', from Greek 'Kharites', the goddesses of charm and beauty.
