out-of-pocket

1. ADJ

/ ˌaʊt əv ˈpɑːkət / (AmE), / ˌaʊt əv ˈpɒkɪt / (BrE)
(of expenses or costs) paid directly in cash by an individual rather than being reimbursed or covered by another party.
paid directly in cash by oneself

Examples

The company will cover your lodging, but meals are an out-of-pocket expense.
After the insurance claim, she still had some out-of-pocket costs for the repairs.
We try to minimize our out-of-pocket spending during business trips.

Synset Examples

his out-of-pocket costs were $10

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reimbursed - paid back or refunded by another party
