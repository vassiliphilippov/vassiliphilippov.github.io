tabular

1. ADJ

/ ˈtæbjələr / (AmE), / ˈtabjʊlə / (BrE)
arranged in or relating to the form of a table with rows and columns
arranged in table form

Examples

The results are presented in a tabular format for easy comparison.
Our finance department maintains all transactions in tabular spreadsheets.
She created a tabular summary of the survey responses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: table - an arrangement of data in rows and columns

Etymology

From Latin tabularis (pertaining to a board or table), from tabula (board, table).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03134301-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈtæbjələr / (AmE), / ˈtabjʊlə / (BrE)
having a flat, table-like surface; flat or planar in form
flat or table-like in form

Examples

The mountain has a tabular plateau at its summit.
Glaciers sometimes break off tabular icebergs that float out to sea.
She described the crystal's tabular shape as thin and flat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flat - having a level, even surface

Etymology

From Latin tabularis, from tabula (a board, a tablet).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00662804-s
Synset POS: s
