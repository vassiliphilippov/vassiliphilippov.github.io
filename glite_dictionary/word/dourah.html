dourah

1. NOUN

/ ˈduːrə / (AmE), / ˈdʊərə / (BrE)
A type of sorghum grown chiefly in dry regions of Asia and North Africa, used for food and forage.
a kind of sorghum grain

Examples

Farmers in Sudan rely on dourah as a staple crop in arid regions.
The market was filled with sacks of dourah ready to be sold for flour.
Drought-resistant dourah has long supported traditional diets in North Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorghum - a genus of grasses, some cultivated for grain

Etymology

From Arabic 'dura' (sorghum).
