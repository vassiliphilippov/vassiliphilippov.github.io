B.C.E.

1. ADV

/ ˌbiː ˌsiː ˈiː / (AmE), / ˌbiː ˌsiː ˈiː / (BrE)
An abbreviation for 'Before the Common Era,' used to mark years before the start of the Common Era, often preferred for its secular tone.
abbreviation for 'Before the Common Era' (dating system)

Examples

The temple was built in the third century B.C.E. and still stands today.
Historians believe the artifact dates back to 500 B.C.E..
Alexander the Great died in 323 B.C.E., marking the end of an era.

Synset Examples

in 200 BCE

Related Terms

  • Synonym: B.C. - abbreviation for 'Before Christ', traditional system to mark years before CE
  • Antonym: C.E. - abbreviation for 'Common Era', designating years after the start of the Common Era

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'Before the Common Era', coined as a secular alternative to 'B.C.' ('Before Christ').
