keratin

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛr.ə.tɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɛr.ə.tɪn / (BrE)
a tough, fibrous protein found in the outer layer of skin, and in hair, nails, feathers, and hooves
protein in skin, hair, nails, etc.
medical

Examples

The strength of fingernails comes from the presence of keratin in their structure.
Damaged hair often lacks sufficient keratin, leading to split ends and breakage.
Bird feathers are primarily made of keratin, giving them durability and flexibility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collagen - another major structural protein in the body, found in connective tissues

Etymology

From Greek 'kēratos' (horn) via German 'Keratin'.
