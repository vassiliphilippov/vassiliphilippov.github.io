elephantiasis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛlɪfənˈtaɪəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌɛlɪfənˈtaɪəsɪs / (BrE)
A condition characterized by gross enlargement and thickening of certain body parts, usually the legs and genitals, resulting from obstruction of the lymphatic system, often by parasitic infection.
disease causing swelling of limbs or genitals
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with elephantiasis after developing severe swelling in his legs.
In regions where filariasis is common, cases of elephantiasis remain a major health challenge.
Elephantiasis can lead to permanent disability and social stigma if left untreated.

Etymology

From Greek 'elephas, elephant-', meaning 'elephant', referring to the appearance of affected limbs.
