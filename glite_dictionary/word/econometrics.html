econometrics

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌkɑːnəˈmɛtrɪks / (AmE), / ɪˌkɒnəˈmɛtrɪks / (BrE)
the application of mathematics and statistical methods to analyze economic and financial data and problems
quantitative analysis of economic data

Examples

He specializes in econometrics, using advanced mathematical models to interpret market trends.
The field of econometrics has become essential for analyzing economic policy impacts.
Students are required to take a course in econometrics as part of their economics degree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: statistics - the science of collecting and analyzing numerical data

Etymology

From 'economic' + 'metrics', patterned after similar scientific terms and first used in the early 20th century.
