Baptisia

1. NOUN

/ bæpˈtɪziə / (AmE), / bæpˈtɪzɪə / (BrE)
A genus of North American plants in the pea family, known for their attractive flowers and inflated seed pods.
genus of flowering North American plants

Examples

The vibrant purple blooms of Baptisia add color to gardens in early summer.
Many native plant enthusiasts plant Baptisia for its hardiness and ornamental value.
The inflated seed pods on Baptisia plants rattle when shaken by the wind.

Etymology

From Greek baptisis (dipping, immersing), referring to the dye properties of some species.

2. NOUN

/ bæpˈtɪʃə / (AmE), / bæpˈtɪsɪə / (BrE)
A genus of North American plants in the pea family, known for their showy flowers and inflated pods.
genus of North American flowering plants

Examples

Several species in the genus Baptisia are commonly called wild indigo.
The garden featured a large clump of Baptisia in full bloom.
Farmers sometimes plant Baptisia to help enrich the soil with nitrogen.

Etymology

From Greek 'bapto' (to dip or dye), referring to the use of some species as dye plants.
