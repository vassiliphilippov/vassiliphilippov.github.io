mauve

1. NOUN

/ moʊv / (AmE), / məʊv / (BrE)
a pale purple color, typically with a bluish or pinkish tint
a pale purple color

Examples

She painted her bedroom walls a soft mauve to create a calming atmosphere.
The designer chose a dress in elegant mauve for the spring collection.
A vase of mauve flowers added a touch of color to the table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lavender - a pale purple color with a bluish tinge
  • Degree Higher: purple - a color intermediate between red and blue
  • Degree Lower: lilac - a pale purple color, lighter than mauve

Etymology

From French 'mauve', from Latin 'malva' (mallow, a plant with purple flowers).

2. ADJ

/ moʊv / (AmE), / məʊv / (BrE)
of a pale to moderate greyish violet color.
pale greyish violet color

Examples

She wore a mauve dress to the wedding, which complemented her complexion beautifully.
The evening sky was tinged with a soft mauve glow as the sun set.
They chose mauve curtains for the nursery to create a calm and serene atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: purple - a color intermediate between red and blue
  • Synonym: lavender - a pale purple color

Etymology

From French 'mauve', from Latin 'malva' (mallow), after the color of the mallow flower.
