sequence

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiː.kwəns / (AmE), / ˈsiː.kwəns / (BrE)
a passage in music in which a melodic phrase is repeated several times at successively higher or lower pitches
repetition of a melodic phrase in different keys (music)

Examples

The composer used a sequence to build tension in the piece.
In Baroque music, a descending sequence often appears in the bass line.
She recognized the familiar melodic sequence as it shifted to a higher key.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motif - a short musical idea or pattern repeated in a composition

Etymology

From Latin 'sequentia' (a following, succession).
