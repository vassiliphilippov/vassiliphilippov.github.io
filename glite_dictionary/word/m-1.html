M-1

1. NOUN

/ ɛm wʌn / (AmE), / ɛm wʌn / (BrE)
A semiautomatic rifle, especially the standard U.S. military rifle from 1936 to 1957, officially known as the M1 Garand.
U.S. semiautomatic military rifle

Examples

The soldier carefully cleaned his M-1 after a long day of training.
During World War II, the M-1 was the primary service rifle used by U.S. troops.
Collectors often seek out an original M-1 because of its historical significance.

Etymology

U.S. military designation: 'M' for 'Model' and '1' for the first in its series.
