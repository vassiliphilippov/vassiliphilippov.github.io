Capsicum annuum grossum

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæp.sɪ.kəm æˈnuː.əm ˈɡrɒs.əm / (AmE), / ˌkæp.sɪ.kəm əˈnjuː.əm ˈɡrɒs.əm / (BrE)
A cultivated variety of pepper plant bearing large, mild, thick-walled, usually bell-shaped fruits, commonly known as bell peppers or sweet peppers.
variety of pepper plant with bell-shaped fruits

Examples

The Capsicum annuum grossum is widely cultivated for its sweet, crunchy fruit used in salads and cooking.
Many gardeners choose Capsicum annuum grossum varieties for their large, brightly colored peppers.
Botanists classify bell peppers under the Capsicum annuum grossum group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bell pepper - a large, mild, usually bell-shaped variety of sweet pepper
  • Synonym: sweet pepper - any of several mild peppers of the variety Capsicum annuum grossum

Etymology

From Latin: Capsicum (pepper) + annuum (annual) + grossum (large).
