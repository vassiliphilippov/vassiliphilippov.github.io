Brassica oleracea italica

1. NOUN

/ brəˈsɪkə oʊˈlɛɹiə ɪˈtælɪkə / (AmE), / brəˈsɪkə əʊˈlɛːrɪə ɪˈtalɪkə / (BrE)
a plant species with dense clusters of tight green flower buds, cultivated as a vegetable; commonly known as broccoli
species of broccoli (vegetable plant)

Examples

The Brassica oleracea italica grown in this region is prized for its tender florets.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Brassica oleracea italica in varying soil conditions.
Farmers rotate crops each year, often planting Brassica oleracea italica after legumes to enrich the soil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broccoli - an edible green plant in the cabbage family, whose large flowering head is eaten as a vegetable

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Brassica' means cabbage, 'oleracea' means vegetable, and 'italica' refers to Italy.
