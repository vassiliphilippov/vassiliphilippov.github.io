metier

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪ.ti.eɪ / (AmE), / ˈmeɪ.tjeɪ / (BrE)
A person's area of expertise or strongest skill; something at which someone excels.
area of expertise; strong skill

Examples

Although John was good at many things, music was truly his metier.
She felt that teaching children was her real metier.
After years of searching, he finally found his metier in landscape photography.

Synset Examples

cooking is his forte

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forte - a person's strong point; something in which they excel

Etymology

From French métier (trade, profession), from Latin ministerium (service).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪ.tjeɪ / (AmE), / ˈmeɪ.tjeɪ / (BrE)
an occupation or activity for which someone is particularly well suited
field of special skill or suitability

Examples

Teaching has always been her true metier and passion.
He struggled in business but found his metier as a writer.
After years in finance, he realized that music was his real metier.

Synset Examples

in law he found his true metier

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vocation - a strong feeling of suitability for a particular career or occupation
  • Synonym: calling - a profession or occupation, especially one regarded as particularly worthy or requiring dedication

Etymology

From French métier (trade, profession), from Latin ministerium (service, employment).
