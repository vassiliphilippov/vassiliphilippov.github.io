sucralfate

1. NOUN

/ suːˈkrælˌfeɪt / (AmE), / suːˈkrælfɑːt / (BrE)
A medication used to treat and prevent ulcers in the digestive tract by forming a protective coating over the ulcer site.
ulcer-treating medication
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed sucralfate to help heal his stomach ulcer.
Patients are often advised to take sucralfate on an empty stomach for best results.
She experienced relief from her symptoms after starting sucralfate therapy.

Etymology

From sucrose and aluminum sulfate, reflecting its chemical composition.
