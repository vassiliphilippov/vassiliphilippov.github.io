Pistacia vera

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈsteɪʃə ˈvɪrə / (AmE), / pɪˈsteɪsɪə ˈvɪərə / (BrE)
A small tree native to southern Europe and Asia Minor that produces the edible pistachio nut.
a tree producing pistachio nuts

Examples

The Pistacia vera tree requires a hot, dry climate to thrive and produce quality nuts.
Farmers in Iran have cultivated Pistacia vera for centuries for its prized pistachios.
A mature Pistacia vera can yield dozens of kilograms of pistachio nuts each harvest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pistachio - the nut produced by Pistacia vera

Etymology

From Latin *Pistacia vera*, from Greek *pistákia* (pistachio tree) and *vera* (true).
