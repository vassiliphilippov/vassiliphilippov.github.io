rifling

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪflɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈraɪflɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of cutting spiral grooves into the inside of a firearm's barrel to make bullets spin and improve accuracy
spiral grooves inside a gun barrel

Examples

The rifling in the barrel increases the accuracy of the rifle.
Inspecting the rifling revealed the weapon had been fired several times.
Manufacturers use modern equipment to ensure precise rifling in each firearm.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smoothbore - a gun barrel without spiral grooves

Etymology

From rifle (a firearm), formed from French *rifler* (to scratch, groove).
