Seychelles

1. PROPN

/ seɪˈʃɛlz / (AmE), / seɪˈʃɛlz / (BrE)
an island country in the Indian Ocean, consisting of an archipelago of about 115 islands northeast of Madagascar, which became independent from the United Kingdom in 1976
island country in the Indian Ocean

Examples

She spent her honeymoon in Seychelles, enjoying the pristine beaches and turquoise waters.
Many rare species of birds are native to Seychelles.
The government of Seychelles is committed to preserving its rich biodiversity.

Etymology

From French (les) Seychelles, named after Jean Moreau de Séchelles, French finance minister in the 18th century.

2. PROPN

/ seɪˈʃɛlz / (AmE), / seɪˈʃɛlz / (BrE)
an island country consisting of about 90 islands in the western Indian Ocean, north of Madagascar
island country in the Indian Ocean

Examples

I have always wanted to visit the beautiful beaches of Seychelles.
The climate of Seychelles is tropical, making it a popular tourist destination.
Many rare species of plants and animals can be found only in Seychelles.

Etymology

From French S eychelles, named after Jean Moreau de Séchelles, Minister of Finance to Louis XV.
