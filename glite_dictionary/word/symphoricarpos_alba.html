Symphoricarpos alba

1. NOUN

/ sɪmˌfɔrɪˈkɑrpoʊs ˈælbə / (AmE), / sɪmˌfɒrɪˈkɑːpɒs ˈalbə / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub native to western North America, bearing spikes of pink flowers followed by round white berries; commonly known as snowberry.
a North American deciduous shrub with white berries

Examples

The Symphoricarpos alba is easily recognized by its clusters of white berries in late summer.
Hikers often spot Symphoricarpos alba growing along forest edges and trails in western Canada.
Gardeners value Symphoricarpos alba for its hardiness and attractive pale berries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snowberry - common name for Symphoricarpos alba and related species

Etymology

From Greek *symphorein* (to bear together) and Latin *alba* (white), referring to the white berries.
