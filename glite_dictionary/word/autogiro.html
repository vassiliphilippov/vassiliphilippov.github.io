autogiro

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔtoʊˈdʒaɪroʊ / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəʊˈdʒaɪrəʊ / (BrE)
A type of aircraft that flies using unpowered rotating horizontal blades for lift and a conventional propeller for forward motion.
aircraft with unpowered rotating blades

Examples

The autogiro was an important step in the development of rotary-wing aircraft.
He restored a vintage autogiro and took it to several airshows.
Unlike a helicopter, an autogiro cannot hover in place.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gyroplane - aircraft using lift from unpowered rotating blades

Etymology

From Spanish 'autogiro', coined by Juan de la Cierva, blending 'auto-' and 'giro' (turn).
