toughie

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌfi / (AmE), / ˈtʌfi / (BrE)
an aggressive and violent young person, typically involved in crime
aggressive young criminal
informal

Examples

After dark, the alley was the territory of the local toughies.
That toughie was finally caught by the police after months of investigation.
Growing up, he was a known toughie in his neighborhood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thug - a violent person, especially a criminal
  • Synonym: bully - a person who habitually seeks to harm or intimidate others

Etymology

Diminutive of 'tough', meaning a rough or violent person.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʌfi / (AmE), / ˈtʌfi / (BrE)
a difficult or puzzling question or problem
difficult question or problem
informal

Examples

That was a real toughie, but I managed to solve it in the end.
The final question on the test was a toughie, and many students got it wrong.
Whenever he faces a toughie at work, he asks his colleagues for advice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stumper - a question or problem that is very difficult to answer
  • Antonym: easy one - a simple question or problem
