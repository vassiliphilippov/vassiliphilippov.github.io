wagonwright

1. NOUN

/ ˈwæɡənˌraɪt / (AmE), / ˈwæɡənˌraɪt / (BrE)
a person who makes or repairs wagons
person who makes wagons

Examples

In the nineteenth century, every town had a skilled wagonwright to build and repair wagons.
The wagonwright crafted sturdy vehicles for farmers and merchants alike.
As a master wagonwright, he took great pride in his attention to detail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cartwright - a person who makes carts

Etymology

From 'wagon' + archaic 'wright' (maker, builder), from Old English 'wryhta'.
