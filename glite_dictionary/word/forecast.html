forecast

1. VERB

/ ˈfɔrˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈfɔːkɑːst / (BrE)
to predict what is likely to happen in the future, especially based on available information or analysis
predict future events, especially weather

Examples

The meteorologist will forecast heavy rain for tomorrow afternoon.
Economists often forecast growth or recession based on current data.
He forecast that technology would change the way people communicate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to predict - to say that something will happen in the future
  • Degree Lower: guess - to estimate or suppose without sufficient information

Etymology

From Old English 'fore-' (before) + 'casten' (to throw, reckon).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈfɔːkɑːst / (BrE)
a statement about what is likely to happen in the future, especially about the weather or a business situation
prediction of future events, esp. weather

Examples

The weather forecast predicts rain for the weekend.
Their financial forecast shows steady growth next year.
According to the latest forecast, temperatures will drop sharply tonight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prediction - an act of predicting future events
  • Antonym: retrospect - a review of past events

Etymology

From Middle English 'forcasten' (to anticipate), from 'fore-' (before) + 'casten' (to throw).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06762595-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈfɔrkæst / (AmE), / ˈfɔːkɑːst / (BrE)
To signal or indicate a future event or condition by means of a sign or omen.
indicate a future event by a sign

Examples

The early frosts forecast a harsh winter ahead for the farmers.
Rising unemployment often forecasts an economic downturn.
Traditionally, a red sky at morning is thought to forecast bad weather.

Synset Examples

These signs bode bad news

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to predict - to say that something will happen in the future
  • Synonym: to foretell - to tell beforehand; predict

Etymology

From Old English 'fore-' (before) and 'casten' (to throw), originally meaning to plan beforehand.

4. VERB

/ ˈfɔːr.kæst / (AmE), / ˈfɔː.kɑːst / (BrE)
to predict or estimate that something is likely to happen in the future
predict or estimate future events

Examples

Economists often forecast a rise in unemployment during economic downturns.
Meteorologists forecast heavy rainfall for the weekend.
Based on current trends, experts forecast a steady increase in global temperatures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to predict - to state that something will happen in the future
  • Synonym: to anticipate - to expect or look ahead to something
  • Antonym: retrospect - consider or review past events

Etymology

From Middle English 'forcasten', from 'fore-' (before) + 'casten' (to throw, to plan).
