downwards

1. ADV

/ ˈdaʊnwərdz / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnwədz / (BrE)
Towards or to a lower place, level, or position; from a higher to a lower point, either physically or figuratively.
towards a lower place or level

Examples

Temperatures are expected to move downwards throughout the night.
The leaves drifted downwards from the tall trees in the park.
Over the years, interest rates have shifted downwards significantly.

Synset Examples

don't fall down
rode the lift up and skied down
prices plunged downward

Related Terms

  • Antonym: upwards - towards a higher place or level

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old English 'dūneweard', meaning 'toward the hill' or 'downward'.
