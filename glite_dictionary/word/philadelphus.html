Philadelphus

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɪləˈdɛlfəs / (AmE), / ˌfɪləˈdɛlfəs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering shrubs, commonly known as mock orange, native to North America, Central America, Asia, and southeast Europe, valued for their fragrant white flowers.
genus of flowering shrubs (mock orange)

Examples

The garden was fragrant with blooming Philadelphus in early summer.
Several species of Philadelphus are grown as ornamental shrubs for their lovely flowers.
We planted a Philadelphus near the patio for its delightful scent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mock orange - common name for shrubs of the genus Philadelphus with fragrant white flowers

Etymology

From Greek *Philadelphos* (loving one's brother), applied to the genus by Linnaeus.
