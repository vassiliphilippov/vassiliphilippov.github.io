hudood

1. NOUN

/ huˈduːd / (AmE), / hʊˈduːd / (BrE)
In Islamic law, the set of boundaries or limits prescribed by Allah, including fixed punishments for certain serious crimes.
limits and punishments in Islamic law

Examples

Under the country's new legislation, certain crimes are subject to the hudood punishments.
Debates about the implementation of hudood laws often arise in legal and political contexts.
The concept of hudood is central to discussions of justice in Islamic jurisprudence.

Etymology

From Arabic ḥudūd, plural of ḥadd (limit, restriction).
