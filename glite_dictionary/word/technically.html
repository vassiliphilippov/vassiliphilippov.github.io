technically

1. ADV

/ ˈtɛknɪkli / (AmE), / ˈtɛknɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to technical skill, knowledge, or technology involved in a particular subject or activity.
with respect to technical skill or technology

Examples

The device was technically advanced compared to others on the market.
Her performance was technically flawless, but lacked emotional depth.
Although the plan was daring, it was technically feasible with current technology.

Synset Examples

a technically brilliant solution

Related Terms

  • Antonym: practically - in a way that relates to real situations rather than theory or pure skill

Etymology

From technical + -ly, from Greek tekhnikós ('of or pertaining to art or skill').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00127721-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ ˈtɛknɪkli / (AmE), / ˈtɛknɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to the exact meaning, rules, or facts of a situation, rather than informal or broader interpretations.
according to the exact meaning or facts

Examples

Technically, your contract starts on the first of the month.
Technically, the two words have different meanings.
Technically, he didn't break the rules, but he came very close.

Synset Examples

technically, a bank's reserves belong to the stockholders
technically, the term is no longer used by experts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strictly - In a rigid or exact manner, without deviation

Etymology

From technical + -ly, formed from Greek 'technikós' (of art or skill) via Latin and English adaptation.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00128014-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ ˈtɛknɪkli / (AmE), / ˈtɛknɪkli / (BrE)
With regard to technique; in a way that relates to the skills or methods used in a particular activity or field.
with regard to technique or skill

Examples

She is technically skilled but lacks emotional expressiveness in her music.
The architect’s design is technically impressive, featuring innovative construction methods.
He was technically correct, although his answer missed the main point.

Synset Examples

technically lagging behind the Japanese
a technically brilliant boxer

Related Terms

  • Antonym: practically - in a way that relates to actual practice rather than theory or technique

Etymology

From technical + -ly, where 'technical' is from Greek tekhnikos (of art or skill).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00127856-r
Synset POS: r
