fibrocalcific

1. ADJ

/ ˌfaɪbroʊkælˈsɪfɪk / (AmE), / ˌfʌɪbrəʊkalˈsɪfɪk / (BrE)
involving or resulting from the calcification of fibrous tissue
relating to calcified fibrous tissue
medical

Examples

The patient's echocardiogram revealed a fibrocalcific aortic valve with restricted motion.
Chronic inflammation can sometimes lead to fibrocalcific changes in the soft tissues.
A fibrocalcific plaque was identified during the coronary angiogram.

Etymology

From Latin 'fibra' (fiber) + Latin 'calx, calcis' (lime, limestone) + '-fic' (making or causing).
