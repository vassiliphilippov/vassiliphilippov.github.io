Asplenium trichomanes

1. NOUN

/ æsˈpliːniəm ˌtrɪkəˈmeɪniːz / (AmE), / æsˈpliːniəm ˌtrɪkəˈmeɪniːz / (BrE)
A small fern of the northern temperate zone and Hawaii, often found on rocks, with pinnate fronds; commonly known as maidenhair spleenwort.
a small rock-inhabiting fern

Examples

The delicate fronds of Asplenium trichomanes can often be found clinging to shaded limestone rocks.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate Asplenium trichomanes for its attractive, lace-like appearance.
During our hike, we discovered several patches of Asplenium trichomanes growing in the crevices of an old stone wall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maidenhair spleenwort - Common English name for Asplenium trichomanes

Etymology

From Latin, Asplenium: the genus name for spleenworts; trichomanes: from Greek trikhōmanes (a kind of fern).
