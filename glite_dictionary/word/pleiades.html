Pleiades

1. PROPN

/ ˈplaɪ.əˌdiz / (AmE), / ˈpliː.əˌdiːz / (BrE)
an open star cluster in the constellation Taurus, consisting of several bright stars visible to the naked eye and known since antiquity
star cluster in Taurus constellation

Examples

Through his telescope, he admired the sparkling beauty of the Pleiades on a clear winter night.
The Pleiades have inspired myths and legends in numerous cultures around the world.
From her backyard, she could easily spot the Pleiades, also called the Seven Sisters, near Taurus.

Etymology

From Greek Πλειάδες (Pleiádes), referring to the seven sisters in Greek mythology.

2. PROPN

/ ˈplaɪ.əˌdiz / (AmE), / ˈpliː.ə.diːz / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the seven daughters of Atlas who were placed among the stars as a constellation to escape Orion; also, the star cluster named after them.
star cluster; daughters of Atlas in myth

Examples

The constellation Pleiades is easily visible in the winter night sky.
In mythology, the Pleiades were turned into stars to escape Orion's pursuit.
Astronomers study the Pleiades to learn about the formation of young star clusters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hyades - a sister group to the Pleiades in Greek mythology; also a star cluster

Etymology

From Greek 'Pleiades', plural of 'Pleis', the name of the seven sisters.
