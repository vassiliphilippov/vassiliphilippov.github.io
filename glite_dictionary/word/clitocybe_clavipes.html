Clitocybe clavipes

1. NOUN

/ ˌklaɪtəˈsaɪbi ˈklævɪpiːz / (AmE), / ˌklɪtəˈsaɪbiː ˈklævɪpiːz / (BrE)
A species of mushroom with a flat cap that is greyish or yellowish brown, pale gills, and a stalk swollen at the base.
a mushroom species with a swollen base

Examples

The Clitocybe clavipes can be found in mixed woodlands during the autumn months.
Foragers should be cautious when collecting Clitocybe clavipes, as it can resemble some toxic species.
Botanical illustrations of Clitocybe clavipes highlight its distinctive bulbous stem.

Etymology

From Greek 'klitos' (sloping) + 'cybe' (head), and Latin 'clavipes' (club-footed).
