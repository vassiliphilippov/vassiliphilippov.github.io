spellbinder

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɛlˌbaɪndər / (AmE), / ˈspɛlˌbaɪndə / (BrE)
a person, especially a speaker, who is able to captivate and hold the attention of an audience.
captivating speaker or performer

Examples

The guest lecturer was a true spellbinder, keeping the entire audience mesmerized for two hours.
Her reputation as a spellbinder helped her attract crowds wherever she spoke.
Few politicians are natural spellbinders, able to inspire such passion in their listeners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orator - a public speaker, especially a skilled one
  • Antonym: bore - a person who is tedious or uninteresting

Etymology

From 'spell' (magical words) + 'binder' (one who binds), referring to someone who holds attention as if by magic.
