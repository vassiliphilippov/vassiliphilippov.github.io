depreciative

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈpriʃətɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈpriːʃətɪv / (BrE)
Tending to reduce or decrease the value of something.
causing a decrease in value

Examples

The company feared the depreciative impact of rising inflation on its investments.
A depreciative trend in the real estate market concerned many homeowners.
She warned about the depreciative effects of neglecting regular car maintenance.

Synset Examples

a depreciating currency
depreciatory effects on prices

Related Terms

  • Antonym: appreciative - showing or causing an increase in value

Etymology

From Latin depretiatus (to diminish in value), from de- (down) + pretium (price).

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈpriʃətɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈpriːʃətɪv / (BrE)
tending to diminish the value, worth, or importance of someone or something; expressing criticism or disapproval.
expressing criticism or making something seem less valuable

Examples

Her depreciative tone made the criticism sound harsher than it really was.
Many of the reviews took a depreciative view of the film's originality.
He was tired of her depreciative remarks about his achievements.

Synset Examples

belittling comments
managed a deprecating smile at the compliment
deprecatory remarks about the book
a slighting remark

Related Terms

  • Synonym: belittling - making something or someone seem unimportant
  • Synonym: deprecatory - expressing disapproval
  • Antonym: approving - showing approval or positive regard

Etymology

From Late Latin depreciativus (tending to depreciate), from depretiari (to depreciate).
