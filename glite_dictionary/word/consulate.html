consulate

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːnsjələt / (AmE), / ˈkɒnsjələt / (BrE)
A building where the official representative (consul) of a foreign government lives or works, typically providing assistance to citizens and facilitating diplomatic relations.
diplomatic building for a consul

Examples

The Italian consulate in New York helps Italian citizens with legal and travel issues.
Protesters gathered outside the Chinese consulate to demand political reforms.
She works at the Brazilian consulate, assisting travelers and expatriates daily.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embassy - the official residence or offices of an ambassador

Etymology

From Latin 'consulatus' (office of a consul), via Middle French 'consulat'.
