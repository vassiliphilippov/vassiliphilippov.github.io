machinery

1. NOUN

/ məˈʃinəri / (AmE), / məˈʃiːnəri / (BrE)
machines or equipment collectively, especially those used in industry or manufacturing
machines or mechanical equipment collectively

Examples

All of the machinery in the factory was inspected for safety last week.
Much of the farm's machinery was old and in need of repair.
The company invested heavily in new machinery to improve production efficiency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equipment - the necessary items for a particular purpose
  • Synonym: apparatus - the technical equipment or machinery needed for a particular activity or purpose
  • Synonym: hardware - tools, machinery, or other durable equipment

Etymology

From machine + -ery, patterned after French 'machinerie'.

2. NOUN

/ məˈʃinəri / (AmE), / məˈʃiːnəri / (BrE)
the systems and processes by which something, especially a social institution, operates or is organized
system of processes or organization

Examples

The machinery of government is designed to ensure stability and order.
The machinery for distributing aid must operate quickly in a crisis.
Reforming the outdated machinery of the organization took years of effort.

Synset Examples

the complex machinery of negotiation
the machinery of command labored and brought forth an order

Etymology

From machine + -ery, after French machinerie.
