adumbration

1. NOUN

/ ˌæd.əmˈbreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌæd.ʌmˈbreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of giving a vague or partial indication of something; a foreshadowing or outlining in a shadowy or unclear manner
vague foreshadowing or indication

Examples

The film’s early scenes offered a subtle adumbration of the tragic ending.
His theory gave only a faint adumbration of the discoveries that would follow.
Literature often relies on the adumbration of future conflicts to build suspense.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foreshadowing - the act of warning or indicating a future event

Etymology

From Latin adumbratio (a shadowing forth), from adumbrare ‘to shade, represent faintly’.

2. NOUN

/ ˌædəmˈbreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌædʌmˈbreɪʃən / (BrE)
a sketchy, imperfect, or faint outline or representation of something
a faint or partial representation

Examples

The historian provided only an adumbration of the ancient city's layout in his lecture.
Her early writings offered an adumbration of the ideas she would later develop in detail.
The report was little more than an adumbration of their long-term strategy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outline - a general description or plan giving the essential features but not the detail
  • Synonym: sketch - a brief or unfinished drawing or representation
  • Synonym: delineation - the act of representing something in outline or detail

Etymology

From Latin adumbratio, from adumbrare (to shadow, outline).

3. NOUN

/ ˌædəmˈbreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌadʌmˈbreɪʃən / (BrE)
a brief outline or summary of something, or an indication of what is to come
an outline or foreshadowing

Examples

The architect presented an adumbration of the planned building during the first meeting.
Her report offered an adumbration of the company’s new strategy.
There was a subtle adumbration of future developments in his speech.

Synset Examples

The adumbration of the project was shared at the conference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outline - a general description or plan that gives the main ideas
  • Synonym: summary - a brief statement of the main points
  • Synonym: foreshadowing - a warning or indication of a future event

Etymology

From Latin adumbratio (a sketch, outline), from adumbrare (to shadow forth, sketch).
