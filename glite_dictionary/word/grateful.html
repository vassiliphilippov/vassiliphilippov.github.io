grateful

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡreɪtfəl / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪtfəl / (BrE)
feeling or showing an appreciation for something done or received; thankful
feeling or showing thanks

Examples

She was grateful for the help her neighbors offered during the storm.
I am extremely grateful to everyone who supported me through difficult times.
He sent a grateful note to thank his teacher for her encouragement.

Synset Examples

a grateful heart
grateful for the tree's shade
a thankful smile

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thankful - feeling or expressing thanks
  • Antonym: ungrateful - not feeling or showing gratitude

Etymology

From Latin gratus (pleasing, thankful) via Old French gratefel.

WordNet ID: oewn-01149996-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡreɪtfəl / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪtf(ə)l / (BrE)
(of a thing) giving comfort or pleasure; agreeable or welcome.
giving comfort or pleasure

Examples

After hours in the cold, the grateful heat of the bath was all he wanted.
They welcomed the grateful shade provided by the trees at noon.
The aroma of baking bread filled the room with a grateful sense of home.

Synset Examples

the grateful warmth of the fire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pleasant - giving a sense of happy satisfaction or enjoyment
  • Synonym: comforting - providing physical or emotional comfort
  • Antonym: unpleasant - causing discomfort, displeasure, or unhappiness

Etymology

From Latin *gratus* (pleasing, thankful) via Old French *grateful*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01806331-s
Synset POS: s
