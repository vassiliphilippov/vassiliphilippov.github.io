Soissons

1. NOUN

/ swɑːˈsɒ̃ / (AmE), / swɑːˈsɒ̃ / (BrE)
a major battle during World War I in May 1918, where a German tactical victory ultimately led to strategic failure against Allied forces
World War I battle in May 1918

Examples

The Battle of Soissons marked a turning point in the German offensive during World War I.
Historians often analyze the consequences of Soissons when discussing the end of the war.
Many American troops fought bravely at Soissons, despite being newly arrived in Europe.

Etymology

Named after Soissons, a town in northern France near the battle site.
