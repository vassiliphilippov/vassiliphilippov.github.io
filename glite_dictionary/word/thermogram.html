thermogram

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɜːrməˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈθɜːməˌɡræm / (BrE)
A graphical record showing temperature variations over an area, produced by a thermograph or thermal imaging device.
record showing temperature variations

Examples

The doctor analyzed the thermogram to detect abnormal heat patterns in the patient's body.
A thermogram can reveal issues such as inflammation or tumors by highlighting areas of higher temperature.
Engineers studied the building's thermogram to identify spots where insulation was insufficient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thermograph - an instrument for recording temperature changes

Etymology

From thermo- (relating to heat) + -gram (something written or recorded).
