palfrey

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæl.fri / (AmE), / ˈpɔːl.fri / (BrE)
a light riding horse, especially one used by women in the Middle Ages
a light saddle horse, especially for a woman
archaic

Examples

In medieval times, a noblewoman would often ride a palfrey while traveling long distances.
The painting showed a lady in flowing robes astride her elegant palfrey.
Unlike a warhorse, a palfrey was prized for its gentle gait and comfortable ride.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courser - a swift horse, especially one used for hunting or in battle

Etymology

From Old French *palefrei*, from Medieval Latin *paraveredus* (a post horse, courier’s horse).
