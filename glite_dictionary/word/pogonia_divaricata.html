Pogonia divaricata

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈgoʊniə dɪˌvɛrɪˈkeɪtə / (AmE), / pəˈɡəʊniə dɪˌvɑːrɪˈkeɪtə / (BrE)
A species of orchid native to the northeastern United States, noted for its magenta-pink, funnel-shaped flowers.
a North American orchid species

Examples

The rare Pogonia divaricata can often be found in wet meadows and bogs in the Northeast.
Botanists are working to protect the declining habitats of Pogonia divaricata.
Visitors to the wildlife sanctuary were excited to see a blooming Pogonia divaricata among the native plants.

Etymology

From genus name Pogonia, from Greek 'pōgōn' (beard), and Latin 'divaricata' (spread apart).
