alimony

1. NOUN

/ ˈælɪˌmoʊni / (AmE), / ˈælɪməni / (BrE)
money that a court orders one spouse to pay to the other after separation or divorce, to provide financial support.
support payments after divorce or separation
legal

Examples

After the divorce was finalized, Mark was required to pay alimony to his ex-wife every month.
Many people misunderstand how alimony is calculated in court.
She used the alimony payments to help cover the mortgage on her house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maintenance - financial support paid to a spouse after separation or divorce, mainly used in British English

Etymology

From Latin alimentum (nourishment) via Late Latin alimonia (sustenance).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæl.əˌmoʊ.ni / (AmE), / ˈæl.ɪ.mə.ni / (BrE)
Money that a court orders someone to pay regularly to their former spouse after divorce or separation.
court-ordered support to ex-spouse
legal american

Examples

After their divorce, Mark was required to pay alimony every month.
She relied on alimony payments to cover her living expenses.
The couple negotiated the terms of alimony through their lawyers.

Synset Examples

After the divorce, she received alimony to support herself.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maintenance - court-ordered financial support to a spouse (British usage)

Etymology

From Latin 'alimonia' (nourishment, support), from 'alere' (to nourish).
