kerosine

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛrəsiːn / (AmE), / ˈkɛrəsiːn / (BrE)
a clear, flammable oil derived from petroleum, commonly used as fuel in lamps, heaters, and jet engines
flammable fuel oil

Examples

The old lantern was fueled by kerosine during the camping trip.
They stored containers of kerosine in a safe place for the winter.
A faint smell of kerosine lingered in the air after refilling the heater.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kerosene - a clear, flammable petroleum oil used as fuel

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'kerosene', from Greek 'keros' (wax) + '-ene', originally coined in mid-19th century Canada.
