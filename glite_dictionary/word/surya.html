Surya

1. PROPN

/ ˈsuːriə / (AmE), / ˈsʊəriə / (BrE)
In Hinduism, the sun god regarded as the source of light and warmth; an important deity worshipped as the personification of the sun.
Hindu sun god; personification of the sun

Examples

In Hindu mythology, Surya rides a chariot drawn by seven horses across the sky.
Many people offer prayers to Surya at sunrise to seek his blessings for health and prosperity.
The temple dedicated to Surya in Konark, India, is famous for its magnificent architecture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Helios - the personification of the sun in Greek mythology

Etymology

From Sanskrit सूर्य (sūrya) meaning 'sun'.
