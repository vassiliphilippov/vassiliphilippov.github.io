agranulocytic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæɡ.rəˌnu.ləˈsɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌaɡ.rəˌnjuː.ləˈsɪt.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to or affected by agranulocytosis, a lack of granulocytes in the blood
relating to agranulocytosis
medical

Examples

The patient developed an agranulocytic reaction after taking the medication.
People with agranulocytic conditions are especially vulnerable to infections.
Doctors monitored his white blood cell count for signs of an agranulocytic disorder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agranulocytosis - a severe reduction in the number of granulocytes in the blood

Etymology

From 'a-' meaning 'without' + 'granulocyte' + '-ic' (adjective suffix).
