cuspidor

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌs.pəˌdɔɹ / (AmE), / ˈkʌs.pɪ.dɔː / (BrE)
a bowl or container used as a receptacle for spitting into, especially one found in public places
spittoon; container for spit

Examples

A brass cuspidor stood in the corner of the old barroom.
The dentist placed a small cuspidor next to the examination chair.
In many nineteenth-century hotels, a cuspidor was a common sight in the lobby.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spittoon - a receptacle for spit, especially in public places

Etymology

From Portuguese 'cuspidor' (spittoon), from 'cuspir' (to spit).
