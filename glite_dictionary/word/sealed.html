sealed

1. ADJ

/ siːld / (AmE), / siːld / (BrE)
(of walls) finished or covered with a coat of plaster, often to make them smooth and ready for further treatment.
walls covered in plaster

Examples

The old brick wall was sealed before it could be painted.
Make sure all surfaces are sealed so the wallpaper sticks evenly.
These rooms have sealed walls to keep dust from penetrating the interior.

2. ADJ

/ siːld / (AmE), / siːld / (BrE)
closed or secured firmly, especially with a seal to prevent opening, tampering, or entry
closed or secured with a seal

Examples

The door remained sealed until the authorities arrived.
She handed him a sealed envelope containing confidential documents.
Make sure all jars are sealed tightly before storing them.

Synset Examples

the package is still sealed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: open - not sealed or closed; allowing access

Etymology

From the verb 'seal', ultimately from Old French 'sel', from Latin 'sigillum' (a small figure, a seal).

5. ADJ

/ siːld / (AmE), / siːld / (BrE)
kept secret or undisclosed for the time being; not to be revealed until a specified time or circumstance.
kept secret or undisclosed

Examples

The general handed her sealed orders that she could not open until reaching the destination.
After the sealed bid auction, the results were announced to the public.
In tournaments, a sealed move is made when a game is adjourned and play is to be resumed later.

Synset Examples

sealed orders
a sealed move in chess

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secret - kept hidden or not known; not disclosed
  • Antonym: open - not concealed or secret; accessible to all

Etymology

From Middle English 'selen', from Old French 'sceller', from Latin 'sigillare' (to seal), from 'sigillum' (seal).
