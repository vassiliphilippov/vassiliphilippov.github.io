sexist

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛksɪst / (AmE), / ˈsɛksɪst / (BrE)
characterized by or showing discrimination or prejudice based on sex, especially against women
discriminatory based on sex

Examples

The company faced criticism for its sexist hiring practices, which favored men over women.
She challenged the sexist assumptions made during the meeting and called for equal treatment.
Many people find jokes based on gender stereotypes to be sexist and offensive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misogynistic - hatred of, or prejudice against, women
  • Antonym: egalitarian - believing in or based on the principle that all people are equal

Etymology

From sex + -ist, patterned after terms like 'racist'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛksɪst / (AmE), / ˈsɛksɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that one gender, typically women, is inferior to the other; someone who practices or espouses sexism.
person with sexist beliefs

Examples

Many people were shocked when the politician was exposed as a sexist.
She stood up to the sexist who made derogatory remarks about women in the meeting.
A true sexist refuses to give women the same opportunities as men.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: misogynist - a person who dislikes, despises, or is prejudiced against women
  • Antonym: feminist - a person who supports equality for women

Etymology

Formed from 'sexism' + '-ist'. 'Sexism' from 'sex' (gender) + '-ism'.
