cytotoxin

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaɪtəˈtɑksɪn / (AmE), / ˌsaɪtəʊˈtɒksɪn / (BrE)
a substance that is toxic to cells
cell-toxic substance
medical

Examples

The scientist studied how the new cytotoxin affected cancer cells in the laboratory.
Some bacteria produce a cytotoxin that destroys white blood cells.
Exposure to the cytotoxin led to cell damage in multiple tissues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toxin - a poisonous substance produced by living cells or organisms

Etymology

From cyto- (relating to cells) + toxin (poison), from Greek 'kytos' (container, cell) and 'toxikon' (poison).
