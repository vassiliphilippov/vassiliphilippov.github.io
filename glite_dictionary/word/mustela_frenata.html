Mustela frenata

1. NOUN

/ mʌˈstelə frəˈnɑːtə / (AmE), / mʌˈstelə frɛˈnɑːtə / (BrE)
the long-tailed weasel, a common American weasel species with a large body and a black-tipped tail
species of American weasel

Examples

The Mustela frenata is known for its agility and swift hunting abilities.
Biologists studied the habitat preferences of Mustela frenata in the western United States.
Unlike other weasels, Mustela frenata can change the color of its fur with the seasons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: long-tailed weasel - common American weasel species with a black-tipped tail

Etymology

From Latin: 'Mustela' meaning 'weasel' and 'frenata' meaning 'bridled'.
