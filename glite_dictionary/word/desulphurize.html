to desulphurize

1. VERB

/ diˈsʌl.fjəˌraɪz / (AmE), / diˈsʌl.fjəˌraɪz / (BrE)
to remove sulfur from a substance, especially in industrial or chemical processes
remove sulfur from

Examples

Modern refineries desulphurize fuel to reduce air pollution caused by sulfur emissions.
After the gas was desulphurized, it met the strict environmental standards for release.
Some industrial plants are investing in equipment that efficiently desulphurizes their waste output.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to desulfurize - to remove sulfur from a substance (American spelling)

Etymology

From de- (removal) + sulphur (sulfur) + -ize (verb-forming suffix). Variant of 'desulfurize'.
