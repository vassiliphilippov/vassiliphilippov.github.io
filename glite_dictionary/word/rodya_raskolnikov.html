Rodya Raskolnikov

1. PROPN

/ ˈroʊdiə ræˈskɒlnɪkɒf / (AmE), / ˈrəʊdjə ræsˈkɒlnɪkɒf / (BrE)
the central character in Dostoevsky's novel 'Crime and Punishment', known for his complex moral reasoning and for murdering a pawnbroker
hero of Dostoevsky's 'Crime and Punishment'

Examples

Rodya Raskolnikov is tormented by guilt throughout Dostoevsky's 'Crime and Punishment'.
Many readers sympathize with Rodya Raskolnikov's internal struggles and philosophical conflicts.
The character of Rodya Raskolnikov has inspired countless discussions on morality and justice.

Etymology

From Russian Родион 'Rodion' (diminutive 'Rodya') and Раскольников 'Raskolnikov', a surname derived from 'raskolnik' (schismatic, dissenter).
