intercostal

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈkɑstl / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈkɒstl / (BrE)
Any of the muscles situated between the ribs, which contract during breathing to help expand and contract the chest cavity.
muscle between the ribs
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully examined the intercostal for signs of injury.
During inhalation, the intercostal muscles contract to expand the ribcage.
Damage to an intercostal can cause pain when breathing deeply.

Etymology

From Latin 'intercostalis', from 'inter-' (between) + 'costalis' (of the ribs), from 'costa' (rib).
