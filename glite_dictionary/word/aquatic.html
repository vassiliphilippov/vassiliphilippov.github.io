aquatic

1. ADJ

/ əˈkwɑːtɪk, æˈkwɑːtɪk / (AmE), / əˈkwætɪk, æˈkwætɪk / (BrE)
living, growing, or found in water; relating to water.
living or growing in water

Examples

The Amazon rainforest is home to many aquatic species.
She studies the effects of pollution on aquatic plants and animals.
The museum features an exhibit about aquatic ecosystems.

Synset Examples

boats are aquatic vehicles
water lilies are aquatic plants
fish are aquatic animals

Related Terms

  • Antonym: terrestrial - living or growing on land

Etymology

From Latin 'aquaticus', from 'aqua' meaning 'water'.

2. ADJ

/ əˈkwɑtɪk / (AmE), / əˈkwatɪk / (BrE)
relating to water; living in, happening in, or consisting of water.
relating to water or living in water

Examples

Many fish and plants have evolved to survive in aquatic environments.
The aquarium simulates aquatic habitats for various species.
Some mammals, such as dolphins and whales, are fully aquatic animals.

Synset Examples

an aquatic environment

Related Terms

  • Antonym: terrestrial - relating to the earth or land, as opposed to water

Etymology

From Latin 'aquaticus', from 'aqua' (water).

3. NOUN

/ əˈkwɑːtɪk / (AmE), / əˈkwætɪk / (BrE)
a plant or animal that lives in or on water, especially a water-dwelling plant
water-dwelling plant or animal

Examples

Many aquatics have adapted to life submerged beneath the surface of the pond.
The greenhouse specializes in rare aquatics for ornamental gardens.
Botanists collected several unusual aquatics from the Amazon river basin.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: terrestrial - a plant or animal that lives on land

Etymology

From Latin aquatica, feminine of aquaticus (of water), from aqua (water).
