mugger

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌɡər / (AmE), / ˈmʌɡə / (BrE)
a person who attacks and robs someone, usually in a public place, by threatening or using violence
street robber who uses violence or threats

Examples

The mugger approached his victim in a dark alley and demanded her purse.
Police arrested a mugger who had been targeting tourists in the park.
She felt lucky to escape unharmed after encountering a mugger last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robber - a person who steals, especially by force or threat
  • Antonym: burglar - a person who enters buildings illegally to steal

Etymology

From the verb 'mug' (to assault for robbery) + -er (agent noun suffix), 19th century slang.
