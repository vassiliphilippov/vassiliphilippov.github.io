pulpiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌlpinəs / (AmE), / ˈpʌlpɪnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being soft, mushy, and resembling pulp.
soft, mushy quality; pulp-like

Examples

The pulpiness of the ripe mango made it perfect for smoothies.
Some people dislike the pulpiness of orange juice and prefer it strained.
She was surprised by the pulpiness of the bread after soaking it in milk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pulpy - resembling or containing pulp; soft and mushy

Etymology

From 'pulpy' + '-ness', with 'pulpy' deriving from 'pulp' (from Latin 'pulpa', meaning 'flesh, pulp').
