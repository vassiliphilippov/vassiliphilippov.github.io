frail

1. ADJ

/ freɪl / (AmE), / freɪl / (BrE)
easily broken or damaged; physically weak or delicate.
easily broken or delicate

Examples

The antique vase was so frail that even a gentle touch could have shattered it.
After her illness, her body felt frail and easily fatigued.
They tried to repair the frail bridge before anyone else crossed it.

Synset Examples

a kite too delicate to fly safely
fragile porcelain plates
fragile old bones
a frail craft

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fragile - easily broken or damaged
  • Antonym: robust - strong and unlikely to break or weaken

Etymology

From Old French fraile, from Latin fragilis (fragile).

2. ADJ

/ freɪl / (AmE), / freɪl / (BrE)
physically weak and delicate; easily damaged or broken due to weakness or lack of strength
physically weak or delicate

Examples

After her long illness, she was still too frail to get out of bed unaided.
He helped his frail grandfather walk across the room.
The frail structure of the old bridge made it dangerous to cross.

Synset Examples

an invalid's frail body

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weak - lacking physical strength or power
  • Antonym: robust - strong and healthy; vigorous

Etymology

From Old French *fraile*, from Latin *fragilis* (fragile, easily broken).

3. NOUN

/ freɪl / (AmE), / freɪl / (BrE)
a basket, especially one made of rushes, used for holding dried fruit such as raisins or figs
basket for dried fruit
archaic

Examples

The worker filled a frail with freshly dried raisins from the sun.
Traders carried frails of figs to the market each morning in the old town.
A frail of dates sat in the corner, ready to be weighed and sold.

Etymology

From Middle English *frayle*, from Old French *fraile*, from Latin *frāgilis* (fragile), originally referring to a basket for fruit.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

4. ADJ

/ freɪl / (AmE), / freɪl / (BrE)
lacking moral strength, courage, or will; weak in character or resolve.
lacking moral strength or will

Examples

Even the most principled leaders sometimes reveal their frail human nature under pressure.
He confessed his frail character after struggling to resist temptation.
In moments of crisis, our frail resolve can lead us to make poor decisions.

Synset Examples

frail humanity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weak - lacking the power to resist or endure; not strong
  • Antonym: resolute - admirably purposeful, determined, and unwavering

Etymology

From Old French 'fraile', from Latin 'fragilis' (fragile, easily broken).
