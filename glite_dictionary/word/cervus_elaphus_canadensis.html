Cervus elaphus canadensis

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜrvəs ɪˈlæfəs kəˈnædɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɜːvəs ɪˈlæfəs kəˈnædɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A large subspecies of elk native to North America, with males distinguished by large, branched antlers.
North American elk subspecies

Examples

The Cervus elaphus canadensis roams the forests and grasslands of North America.
Wildlife biologists study the migration patterns of Cervus elaphus canadensis to better understand their behavior.
In some regions, Cervus elaphus canadensis is also referred to as the North American elk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elk - large species of deer also known as wapiti

Etymology

From Latin: 'cervus' meaning 'deer,' 'elaphus' meaning 'stag,' and 'canadensis' indicating origin from Canada.
