basidiomycete

1. NOUN

/ bəˌsɪdi.oʊˈmaɪsit / (AmE), / bəˌsɪdi.əʊˈmaɪsiːt / (BrE)
a fungus belonging to the subdivision Basidiomycota, characterized by producing spores on a basidium
fungus in Basidiomycota subdivision

Examples

The mushroom you found in the forest is a type of basidiomycete.
Many basidiomycetes play a crucial role in decomposing wood and recycling nutrients in ecosystems.
Rusts and smuts are examples of plant-pathogenic basidiomycetes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ascomycete - fungus in the subdivision Ascomycota

Etymology

From modern Latin Basidiomycetes, from 'basidium' (a small pedestal, from Latin) and Greek 'mykēs' (fungus).
