Griselinia

1. NOUN

/ ɡrɪˌsɛlɪˈniːə / (AmE), / ɡrɪˌsɛlɪˈniːə / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen shrubs native to New Zealand and South America, often grown as ornamental hedges.
genus of evergreen shrubs

Examples

Griselinia is often used to create dense, attractive hedges in gardens.
The branches of Griselinia remain green throughout the winter.
Several species of Griselinia are native to New Zealand's coastal regions.

Etymology

Formed from the name of Italian botanist Francesco Griselini (1717–1787) + Latin suffix -ia, denoting genera.
