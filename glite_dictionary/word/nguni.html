Nguni

1. NOUN

/ ənˈɡuːni / (AmE), / ənˈɡuːni / (BrE)
a group of closely related Bantu languages spoken in southern Africa, including Zulu, Xhosa, Swati, and Ndebele
group of southern African Bantu languages

Examples

The Nguni languages share many features in terms of grammar and vocabulary.
Zulu and Xhosa are both part of the Nguni language group.
Many researchers study the Nguni languages to understand linguistic diversity in southern Africa.

Etymology

From Zulu *iNguni*, referring to the Nguni people and their languages.
