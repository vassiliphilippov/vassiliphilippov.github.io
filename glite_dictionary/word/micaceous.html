micaceous

1. ADJ

/ maɪˈkeɪʃəs / (AmE), / maɪˈkeɪʃəs / (BrE)
containing, composed of, or resembling mica; having properties like those of mica, especially a tendency to split into thin layers
resembling or containing mica

Examples

The rocks in this area have a distinctly micaceous appearance due to embedded mica flakes.
She collected several micaceous minerals during her geology field trip.
The pottery shards were made with micaceous clay, giving them a slight shimmer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mica - a shiny silicate mineral that easily splits into thin sheets

Etymology

From mica (Latin, meaning 'crumb') + -aceous (Latin suffix meaning 'of the nature of').
