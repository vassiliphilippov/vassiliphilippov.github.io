to estrange

1. VERB

/ ɛˈstreɪndʒ / (AmE), / ɪˈstreɪndʒ / (BrE)
to cause someone to stop feeling friendly or affectionate towards another person or group, often resulting in alienation or separation
make someone feel alienated or distant

Examples

The constant arguments began to estrange him from his family.
Her decision to move abroad seemed to estrange her from her closest friends.
Years of neglect finally estranged the two brothers, who barely spoke after that.

Synset Examples

She alienated her friends when she became fanatically religious

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to alienate - to cause someone to feel isolated or estranged

Etymology

From Old French *estranger* ‘to treat as a stranger’, from Latin *extraneare* (from *extraneus*, ‘strange, foreign’).

2. VERB

/ ɪˈstreɪndʒ / (AmE), / ɪˈstreɪndʒ / (BrE)
to make someone feel no longer close to or affectionate towards someone or something; to cause someone to be alienated from a group or environment
make distant or alienated

Examples

Years of living abroad had estranged him from his family and friends.
Her political beliefs gradually estranged her from her colleagues at work.
Constant arguments can estrange even the closest of friends.

Synset Examples

years of boarding school estranged the child from her home

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to alienate - to cause someone to feel isolated or estranged

Etymology

From Old French 'estranger', from Latin 'extraneare' (to treat as a stranger), from 'extraneus' (foreign).

Synset POS: v
