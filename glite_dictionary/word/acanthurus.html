Acanthurus

1. NOUN

/ əˈkænθjʊrəs / (AmE), / əˈkænθjʊərəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Acanthuridae, comprising the doctorfishes or surgeonfishes found in tropical oceans.
genus of doctorfishes (surgeonfishes)

Examples

The Acanthurus genus includes many colorful reef-dwelling species.
Scientists observed the behavior of Acanthurus in their natural coral habitat.
Many aquarium enthusiasts keep Acanthurus species for their vibrant colors and unique shape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Acanthuridae - the family of marine fishes known as surgeonfishes or doctorfishes

Etymology

From Greek akantha (thorn, spine) + oura (tail), referring to the fish’s spiny tail.

2. NOUN

/ əˈkænθjʊrəs / (AmE), / əˈkænθjʊərəs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical marine fish in the family Acanthuridae, commonly known as doctorfishes or surgeonfishes.
genus of doctorfishes (marine fish)

Examples

Many aquarium enthusiasts keep Acanthurus species for their vibrant colors and algae-eating habits.
The Acanthurus genus includes several popular surgeonfish found in Indo-Pacific reefs.
Scientists have classified dozens of doctorfish species within the Acanthurus genus.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Acanthuridae - family of marine fish including surgeonfishes and tangs
  • Degree Lower: Zebrasoma - a related genus of surgeonfishes

Etymology

From Greek 'akantha' (thorn, spine) and 'oura' (tail), referring to the spine near the tail.
