Sarcoscyphaceae

1. NOUN

/ sɑːr.kəˌsaɪ.fəˈsiː.i / (AmE), / sɑː.kɒsˌsɪ.fəˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
A family of fungi in the order Pezizales, typically characterized by brightly colored, cup-shaped fruiting bodies.
family of cup fungi
medical

Examples

The Sarcoscyphaceae family includes several species known for their vivid red or orange cups.
Mycologists often study Sarcoscyphaceae to understand fungal biodiversity in forest ecosystems.
Members of the Sarcoscyphaceae can sometimes be found growing on decaying wood in damp environments.

Etymology

From New Latin, formed from 'Sarcoscypha' (a representative genus) + '-aceae' (family suffix).
