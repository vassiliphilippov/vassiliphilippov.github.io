sculpted

1. ADJ

/ ˈskʌlptɪd / (AmE), / ˈskʌlptɪd / (BrE)
shaped or carved into a desired form, especially by cutting or molding
shaped or carved into form

Examples

The museum displayed several sculpted marble statues from ancient Greece.
Her face had beautifully sculpted features that gave her a striking appearance.
The landscape was sculpted by millions of years of wind and water erosion.

Synset Examples

graven images
sculptured representations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carved - cut into a shape by carving, especially in wood or stone
  • Synonym: molded - shaped into a form using a mold or hands

Etymology

From the verb 'sculpt' (back-formation from 'sculptor'), from Latin 'sculptus' (carved), past participle of 'sculpere'.
