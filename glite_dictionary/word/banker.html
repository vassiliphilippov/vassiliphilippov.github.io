banker

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæŋkər / (AmE), / ˈbaŋkə / (BrE)
a person who owns, manages, or is an executive in a bank
a person who directs or runs a bank

Examples

After years of hard work, she became a successful banker at a leading international firm.
The banker approved their mortgage application after carefully reviewing their finances.
As a banker, he is often required to travel to meet with investors and clients around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: financier - a person skilled in large scale financial transactions
  • Degree Lower: teller - a bank employee who deals directly with customers
  • Synonym: executive - a person with senior managerial responsibility

Etymology

From 'bank' (financial institution) + '-er' (agent noun suffix)

2. NOUN

/ ˈbæŋkər / (AmE), / ˈbæŋkə / (BrE)
the person who manages the bank or funds in a gambling game, distributing or collecting bets
person in charge of funds in a gambling game

Examples

In baccarat, the banker is responsible for handling all bets and payouts.
Each round, the role of banker rotates among the players at the table.
The banker placed the chips in the center and began to deal the cards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dealer - person responsible for dealing cards in gambling games
