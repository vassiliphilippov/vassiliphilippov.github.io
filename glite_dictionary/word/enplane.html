to enplane

1. VERB

/ ɛnˈpleɪn / (AmE), / ɛnˈpleɪn / (BrE)
to board an aircraft; to go onto an airplane before a flight
to board an aircraft

Examples

All passengers must enplane at Gate 12 by 9:30 a.m.
After a brief delay, we were finally allowed to enplane for our flight to Chicago.
She quickly gathered her belongings and hurried to enplane before the doors closed.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to disembark - to leave an aircraft or other vehicle
  • Synonym: board - to get onto a vehicle, aircraft, or ship

Etymology

Formed from the prefix en- (to put into) + plane (airplane).
