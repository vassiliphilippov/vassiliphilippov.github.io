glossarist

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlɑsəˌrɪst / (AmE), / ˈɡlɒsəˌrɪst / (BrE)
a person who compiles glosses or glossaries, or explains difficult words in a text
person who compiles glossaries

Examples

The medieval glossarist contributed valuable notes to the study of ancient manuscripts.
Every major edition of the text benefited from the meticulous work of a skilled glossarist.
As a glossarist, she spent years clarifying obscure terms in old legal documents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lexicographer - a person who compiles dictionaries

Etymology

From 'glossary' + suffix '-ist', modeled after 'glossator' and similar scholarly terms.
