Phyllostachys bambusoides

1. NOUN

/ fɪl.oʊˈstæk.ɪs bæmˌbuːˈsɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / fɪl.əʊˈstæk.ɪs bamˌbjuːˈsɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A large species of bamboo with thick-walled stems, native to China and probably Japan, widely cultivated in many regions.
a large bamboo species with thick-walled stems

Examples

Phyllostachys bambusoides is often grown in botanical gardens for its impressive height and strength.
The hard culms of Phyllostachys bambusoides make it ideal for use in construction and furniture.
Gardeners prize Phyllostachys bambusoides for its rapid growth and elegant appearance.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'phyllon' (leaf) + 'stachys' (spike); species epithet relates to the genus Bambusa.
