athletic

1. ADJ

/ æθˈlɛtɪk / (AmE), / æθˈlɛtɪk / (BrE)
relating to athletics, sports, or athletes; physically strong, fit, or active in sports
relating to sports or athletes

Examples

The university has excellent athletic facilities for its students.
She has an athletic build and enjoys playing soccer regularly.
His athletic achievements earned him a scholarship.

Synset Examples

athletic facilities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sporty - fond of or good at sports
  • Antonym: unathletic - not good at or interested in sports

Etymology

From Greek 'athlētikos', from 'athlētēs' (athlete).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02918969-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ æθˈlɛtɪk / (AmE), / æθˈlɛtɪk / (BrE)
having a strong, fit, and well-proportioned body, typical of an athlete
physically strong and well-built

Examples

She has an athletic build and excels in many sports.
The actor trained hard to achieve an athletic appearance for his new role.
His athletic body was the result of years of dedicated exercise.

Synset Examples

an athletic build

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muscular - having well-developed muscles
  • Antonym: slender - gracefully thin or slim

Etymology

From Greek 'athlētikos' (pertaining to contests or athletes), from 'athlētēs' (contestant in the games).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00832200-s
Synset POS: s

/ əˈθlɛtɪk / (AmE), / əˈθlɛtɪk / (BrE)

physically strong or active

Examples

His athletic build made him well-suited for competitive sports.
