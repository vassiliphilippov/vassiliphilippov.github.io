lallation

1. NOUN

/ ləˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ləˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
speech that resembles the babbling sounds of a baby, often unintelligible or nonsensical
baby-like, babbling speech

Examples

The child's lallation filled the room with cheerful, nonsensical sounds.
During her performance, the actress imitated the lallation of an infant to amuse the audience.
The linguist studied the patterns of lallation in early language development.

Etymology

From Latin lallatio (babbling, prattling), from lallare (to sing a lullaby).

2. NOUN

/ læˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ləˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a speech defect in which the sound /l/ is incorrectly articulated or the phoneme /r/ is pronounced as /l/
defective pronunciation of l or r sounds
medical

Examples

The speech therapist worked with the child to correct his lallation.
Lallation is often noticed when young children are learning to speak and struggle with certain sounds.
An untreated lallation can affect a person's confidence when speaking in public.

Etymology

From Latin 'lallatio', from 'lallare' (to sing lullabies).
