spatulate

1. ADJ

/ ˈspætʃuˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈspatjʊlət / (BrE)
(of a leaf or other structure) having a broad, rounded apex and a narrow base; shaped like a spatula.
broad-rounded, narrow-based shape (e.g. leaf)

Examples

The plant's leaves are spatulate, widest at the tip and tapering toward the base.
Many succulents display spatulate foliage, which helps maximize sunlight absorption.
A botanist might describe certain rose petals as spatulate in shape.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: obovate - egg-shaped leaf with the broader end at the top
  • Synonym: spatula-shaped - having the shape of a spatula, broad at one end and narrow at the other

Etymology

From Latin spatulatus (‘shaped like a spatula’), from ‘spatula’ (a broad, flat tool).
