Pinus muricata

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪ.nəs mjuːˈrɪ.kə.tə / (AmE), / ˈpɪ.nəs mjuːˈrɪ.kə.tə / (BrE)
a species of pine tree native to the northwestern coast of California, often with two or three needles per cluster, commonly known as Bishop pine
species of pine tree (Bishop pine)

Examples

The Pinus muricata forests are an important habitat for local wildlife.
During our hike, we spotted several tall Pinus muricata along the coastal trail.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect Pinus muricata populations from disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bishop pine - common name for Pinus muricata

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Pinus' (pine) and species epithet 'muricata', meaning 'roughened' or 'prickly'.
