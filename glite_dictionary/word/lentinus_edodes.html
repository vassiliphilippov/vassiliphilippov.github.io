Lentinus edodes

1. NOUN

/ lɛnˈtaɪ.nəs ˌɛˈdoʊ.diz / (AmE), / lɛnˈtɪn.əs ˌiːˈdəʊ.diːz / (BrE)
an edible East Asian mushroom with a golden to dark brown cap and an inedible stipe, commonly cultivated for food
edible East Asian mushroom (shiitake)

Examples

The soup was garnished with sliced Lentinus edodes for added flavor.
Many Asian dishes feature Lentinus edodes as a key ingredient.
Farmers have begun cultivating Lentinus edodes on hardwood logs for commercial sale.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shiitake - another name for Lentinus edodes, the edible mushroom

Etymology

From New Latin taxonomic naming: genus Lentinus + specific epithet edodes, both Latinized from scientific nomenclature.
