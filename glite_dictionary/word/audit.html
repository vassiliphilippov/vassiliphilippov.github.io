audit

1. VERB

/ ˈɑdɪt / (AmE), / ˈɔːdɪt / (BrE)
to attend a class or course without receiving academic credit or a grade
attend a course without getting credit

Examples

Students can audit the class if they prefer not to take the final exam.
She decided to audit a philosophy course during her summer break.
Many retirees choose to audit university lectures to keep learning without worrying about grades.

Etymology

From Latin audīre (to hear), originally meaning to listen to lectures.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00601630-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑːdɪt / (AmE), / ˈɔːdɪt / (BrE)
a systematic inspection or review of a particular situation, process, or set of accounts
systematic inspection or review

Examples

The company is scheduled for an external audit next month.
We conducted a safety audit to identify any potential hazards in the building.
After the environmental audit, several recommendations for improvement were made.

Synset Examples

he made an audit of all the plants on his property
an energy efficiency audit
an email log audit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inspection - careful examination or scrutiny

Etymology

From Latin 'auditus' (a hearing), past participle of 'audire' (to hear).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00141996-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈɔdɪt / (AmE), / ˈɔːdɪt / (BrE)
an official inspection of an organization's financial accounts and records, typically by an independent body
official inspection of financial records

Examples

The company underwent an annual audit to ensure its financial statements were accurate.
A government audit revealed discrepancies in the public spending records.
Before applying for a loan, the business ordered an external audit of its accounts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inspection - careful examination or review

Etymology

From Latin 'auditus' (a hearing), from 'audire' (to hear).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13432330-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈɔdɪt / (AmE), / ˈɔːdɪt / (BrE)
to examine financial accounts or records carefully to check their accuracy and validity
to inspect financial records for accuracy

Examples

The company will audit its financial records before the annual report is published.
An external firm was hired to audit the charity's accounts for transparency.
He learned how to audit tax returns during his training as an accountant.

Synset Examples

audit accounts and tax returns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inspect - to look at something closely to check its condition
  • Synonym: review - to examine something with the possibility of making changes or improvements

Etymology

From Latin 'auditus' (a hearing), from 'audire' (to hear), originally referring to hearing accounts read aloud for verification.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00698828-v
Synset POS: v
