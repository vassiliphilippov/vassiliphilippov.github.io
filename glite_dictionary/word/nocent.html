nocent

1. ADJ

/ ˈnoʊsənt / (AmE), / ˈnəʊsənt / (BrE)
Having a tendency to cause harm; harmful or injurious.
harmful; injurious

Examples

The factory released nocent chemicals into the river, endangering local wildlife.
He argued that the defendant's actions, though reckless, were not nocent by legal standards.
Certain plants have nocent effects on livestock if ingested.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: innocuous - not harmful or offensive
  • Antonym: harmless - not able or not likely to cause harm
  • Synonym: pernicious - having a harmful effect, especially in a gradual or subtle way

Etymology

From Latin nocens, present participle of nocēre (to harm).
