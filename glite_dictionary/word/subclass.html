subclass

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌbˌklæs / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌklɑːs / (BrE)
(biology) A taxonomic category ranking below a class and above an order.
taxonomic rank below class

Examples

In biological classification, mammals are divided into several subclasses based on their reproductive traits.
The subclass Theria includes all mammals that give birth to live young.
Each subclass contains several different orders that share certain characteristics.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: class - taxonomic rank above subclass in biology
  • Degree Lower: order - taxonomic rank below subclass in biology

Etymology

From Latin sub- (under) + classis (class, division).
