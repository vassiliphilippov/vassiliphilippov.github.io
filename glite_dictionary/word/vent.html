vent

1. NOUN

/ vɛnt / (AmE), / vɛnt / (BrE)
A narrow slit or opening in a garment, especially in the back seam of a jacket, to allow ease of movement and improve fit.
slit in a garment, esp. in a jacket

Examples

The tailor added a vent to the back of my blazer for extra comfort.
Single or double vents are common features in men's suit jackets.
She preferred jackets without a vent for a sleeker silhouette.

2. NOUN

/ vɛnt / (AmE), / vent / (BrE)
the external opening of the urinary or genital system in lower vertebrates, such as fish, amphibians, or reptiles
external opening of urinary/genital system in lower vertebrates

Examples

The fish expels waste through its vent, located just behind the pelvic fins.
During spawning, eggs and sperm are released from the vent into the water.
The biologist examined the turtle's vent to determine its sex.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cloaca - common cavity into which the intestinal, urinary, and reproductive tracts open in some animals

Etymology

From Old French *vent*, from Latin *ventus* (wind), related to letting out air or fluids.
