eubacterium

1. NOUN

/ juː.bækˈtɪr.i.əm / (AmE), / juː.bækˈtɪə.ri.əm / (BrE)
a bacterium belonging to a large group characterized by rigid cell walls, including many common bacteria found in soil, water, and living organisms; some types are motile with flagella
a group of bacteria with rigid cell walls
medical

Examples

Some species of eubacterium are essential for digestion in the human gut.
The scientist identified the organism as a eubacterium under the microscope.
Unlike archaea, a eubacterium typically has a rigid cell wall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bacterium - single-celled microorganism

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'eu-' (true) and 'bacterium'.
