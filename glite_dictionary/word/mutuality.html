mutuality

1. NOUN

/ ˌmjuːtʃuˈæləti / (AmE), / ˌmjuːtʃuˈæləti / (BrE)
the fact of a feeling, action, or relationship being mutual or shared by two or more people
the state of being mutual or shared

Examples

The mutuality of their affection was clear to everyone who saw them together.
Lasting partnerships are built on trust and mutuality.
A sense of mutuality is essential for successful teamwork.

Synset Examples

the mutuality of their affection was obvious

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reciprocity - the practice of exchanging things with others for mutual benefit

Etymology

From mutual + -ity, indicating a state or condition.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmjuː.tʃuˈæl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌmjuː.tʃuˈæl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality or condition of being mutual; a reciprocal relationship or feeling between two or more people or groups.
reciprocal relationship or feeling

Examples

There was a strong sense of mutuality between the partners, allowing them to work efficiently together.
Successful relationships are built on trust and mutuality rather than selfishness.
Legal agreements often require mutuality in obligations to ensure fairness to both parties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reciprocity - the practice of exchanging things with others for mutual benefit
  • Antonym: unilateralism - the process of acting, reaching a decision, or espousing a principle unilaterally

Etymology

From French 'mutualité', from Latin 'mutuus' (borrowed, lent).
