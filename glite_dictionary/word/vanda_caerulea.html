Vanda caerulea

1. NOUN

/ ˈvændə siːˈruːliə / (AmE), / ˈvandə siːˈruːliə / (BrE)
a species of orchid native to northern India, known for its large pale to deep lilac-blue flowers
a blue-flowering orchid species from India

Examples

The Vanda caerulea is prized by horticulturists for its striking blue flowers.
Botanical gardens often showcase the Vanda caerulea among their rare plant collections.
She grew a Vanda caerulea in her greenhouse, delighting in its vibrant blossom each spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchid - any plant of the family Orchidaceae, often with showy flowers

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining the genus name 'Vanda' and Latin 'caerulea' meaning 'blue'.
