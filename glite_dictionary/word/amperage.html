amperage

1. NOUN

/ ˈæmpɚɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈæmpərɪdʒ / (BrE)
the strength of an electric current measured in amperes
strength of an electric current

Examples

The amperage of this circuit is too high for the wires to handle safely.
Technicians measured the amperage to ensure it met the required specifications.
Increasing the voltage without adjusting the amperage could damage the equipment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: current - the flow of electric charge
  • Degree Higher: voltage - electrical potential difference, related property
  • Degree Lower: resistance - opposition to the flow of an electric current

Etymology

From 'ampere' (a unit of electric current) + '-age' (forming nouns), after French 'ampérage'.
