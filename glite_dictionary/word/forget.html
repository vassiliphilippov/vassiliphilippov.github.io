to forget

1. VERB

/ fərˈɡɛt / (AmE), / fəˈɡɛt / (BrE)
to be unable to remember something
be unable to remember

Examples

I often forget where I left my keys in the morning.
She tried not to forget her friend's birthday, but it slipped her mind.
He sometimes forgets important details when he's stressed.

Synset Examples

I'm drawing a blank
You are blocking the name of your first wife!

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to remember - to keep something in mind or recall it
  • Antonym: recall - to bring a fact or event back into one's mind

Etymology

From Old English *forgietan* (to forget, neglect), related to Old High German *firgezzan*.

WordNet ID: oewn-00610654-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ fərˈɡɛt / (AmE), / fəˈɡɛt / (BrE)
to leave something behind unintentionally, especially because you did not remember to take it with you
leave something behind unintentionally

Examples

I always forget my lunch at home when I'm in a hurry.
She forgot her phone on the bus yesterday and had to go back for it.
If you forget your textbook in class, you can pick it up tomorrow.

Synset Examples

I forgot my umbrella in the restaurant
I left my keys inside the car and locked the doors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leave - to go away from a place, without taking something or someone

Etymology

From Old English forgietan, from for- (completely) + gietan (get, obtain).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00614532-v

3. VERB

/ fərˈɡɛt / (AmE), / fəˈɡɛt / (BrE)
to fail to remember to do something that you intended or were supposed to do
fail to do something as intended

Examples

Please don't forget to lock the door when you leave.
She always forgets to send birthday cards to her friends.
I forgot to bring my umbrella, so I got soaked in the rain.

Synset Examples

Don't forget to call the chairman of the board to the meeting!

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to remember - to keep something in mind or recall to do it

Etymology

From Old English 'forgietan', from for- (completely) + gietan (to get, grasp, understand).

4. VERB

/ fərˈɡɛt / (AmE), / fəˈɡɛt / (BrE)
To deliberately put something out of one's mind or stop thinking about it.
stop remembering or dismiss from the mind

Examples

She tries to forget the pain of her childhood by keeping busy.
Sometimes it's healthier to forget old arguments and move on.
He wished he could simply forget everything that had happened.

Synset Examples

I tried to bury these unpleasant memories

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to remember - to recall or keep in mind
  • Synonym: to repress - to subdue or push out of conscious memory

Etymology

From Old English *forgietan*, from *for-* (completely) + *gietan* (to get).
