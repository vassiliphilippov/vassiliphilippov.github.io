nitid

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈnɪtɪd / (BrE)
shining with a smooth, bright, or lustrous appearance; gleaming softly or with subdued brightness.
shining or lustrous; softly bright

Examples

The silverware was nitid after a thorough polishing.
Under the nitid glow of the lantern, the old painting looked almost new.
She admired the nitid surface of the river at sunrise.

Synset Examples

from the plane we saw the city below agleam with lights
the gleaming brass on the altar
Nereids beneath the nitid moon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gleaming - shining brightly, often with reflected light
  • Antonym: dull - not bright, shiny, or lustrous

Etymology

From Latin nitidus (shining, bright, polished).
