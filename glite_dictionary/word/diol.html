diol

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪˌɑl / (AmE), / ˈdaɪ.ɒl / (BrE)
a chemical compound containing two hydroxyl (–OH) groups attached to different carbon atoms in each molecule
alcohol with two hydroxyl groups
medical

Examples

A diol can be formed when ethylene glycol is produced from ethylene oxide.
The chemist synthesized a new diol for use in polymer manufacturing.
Glycerol is technically a triol, while ethylene glycol is a simple diol.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: alcohol - any compound with a hydroxyl group attached to a carbon atom

Etymology

From Greek 'di-' (two) and '-ol' (alcohol suffix).
