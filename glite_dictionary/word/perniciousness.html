perniciousness

1. NOUN

/ pərˈnɪʃəsnəs / (AmE), / pəˈnɪʃəsnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being highly harmful or deadly.
the quality of being very harmful

Examples

The perniciousness of the new drug was not realized until several patients fell ill.
Experts warned about the perniciousness of unchecked misinformation online.
He underestimated the perniciousness of the toxic chemical in the environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dangerousness - the quality of being dangerous
  • Antonym: safety - the condition of being protected from harm

Etymology

From Latin 'perniciosus' (destructive), via 'pernicies' (ruin, destruction).
