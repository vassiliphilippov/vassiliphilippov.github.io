sash

1. NOUN

/ sæʃ / (AmE), / sæʃ / (BrE)
A long strip of cloth worn around the waist or over the shoulder, typically as part of an outfit or uniform, to fasten or decorate clothing.
a band or strip of cloth worn as clothing

Examples

She wore a bright red sash over her dress at the ceremony.
The boy tied a blue sash tightly around his waist.
Traditional costumes often include a decorative sash to complete the look.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: belt - a strip of leather or other material worn around the waist to support or hold up clothing

Etymology

From Arabic 'shāsh' (muslin, a kind of cloth).

2. NOUN

/ sæʃ / (AmE), / sæʃ / (BrE)
a framework, often movable, that holds the panes of glass in a window and fits into the window frame
window frame holding the panes

Examples

The old house still had its original sash windows, complete with delicate wooden frames.
Each sash in the double-hung window can slide up and down independently.
He painted the sash to match the rest of the window frame.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: casement - a window that is attached to its frame by hinges

Etymology

From French *châssis* (frame), from Latin *capsa* (box, case).
