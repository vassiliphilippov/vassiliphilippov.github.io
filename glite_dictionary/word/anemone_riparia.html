Anemone riparia

1. NOUN

/ əˈnɛməni rɪˈpɛriə / (AmE), / əˈnɛməni rɪˈpeəriə / (BrE)
A species of thimbleweed native to northern North America, known for its distinctive white flowers.
a thimbleweed of northern North America

Examples

The riverbank was dotted with blooming Anemone riparia in early spring.
Botanists studied the habitat preferences of Anemone riparia alongside other native flora.
She photographed a delicate Anemone riparia during her hike through the northern woods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thimbleweed - another common name for various Anemone species

Etymology

From New Latin; 'Anemone' from Greek anemōnē (windflower), 'riparia' from Latin riparius (of the riverbank).
