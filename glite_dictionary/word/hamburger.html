hamburger

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæmˌbɝɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈhæmbəːɡə / (BrE)
beef that has been finely ground, typically used for making patties or as an ingredient in various dishes.
ground beef, especially for patties

Examples

The recipe calls for one pound of hamburger and several spices.
You can find frozen hamburger in most supermarkets.
She bought fresh hamburger to make her famous meatloaf.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ground beef - finely chopped or ground beef, used for cooking

Etymology

From German 'Hamburger', meaning 'from Hamburg', originally referring to a person from Hamburg, but in this context, it refers to the style of beef as prepared in Hamburg.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhæmˌbɝɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈhæmˌbɜːɡə / (BrE)
A sandwich consisting of a cooked patty of ground beef served in a split bun, typically with additional ingredients such as lettuce, tomato, cheese, and condiments.
sandwich with a ground beef patty in a bun

Examples

She ordered a hamburger with extra pickles at the new restaurant.
I like to grill hamburgers in the backyard during summer barbecues.
He topped his hamburger with lettuce, tomato, and onions.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: cheeseburger - hamburger with added cheese
  • Synonym: sandwich - two or more slices of bread with filling

Etymology

From German 'Hamburger' meaning 'from Hamburg', named after the German city of Hamburg.
