to barbarise

1. VERB

/ ˈbɑrbəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈbɑːbərʌɪz / (BrE)
(of a person or group) to become crude, savage, or barbaric in behavior or language.
become savage or crude

Examples

Left without guidance, the children began to barbarise until they could hardly recognize their own manners.
Some argue that violent media can barbarise society over time.
When civility breaks down, even the most cultured communities may barbarise.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to civilise - to bring out of a barbaric or savage state; to refine

Etymology

From Medieval Latin barbarizare, from barbarus ‘barbarous’.

2. VERB

/ ˈbɑrbəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈbɑːbəraɪz / (BrE)
to make or become crude, uncivilized, or savage in behavior or speech
make or become crude or uncivilized

Examples

The isolation tended to barbarise the small, neglected community.
Continuous warfare can barbarise even the most stable societies.
Some fear that violent media may barbarise young audiences over time.

Synset Examples

his years in prison have barbarized the young man

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to civilize - to bring out of a savage or uneducated state
  • Synonym: to brutalize - to make brutal or cruel

Etymology

From Latin barbarus (foreign, uncivilized), via French barbariser and medieval English.
