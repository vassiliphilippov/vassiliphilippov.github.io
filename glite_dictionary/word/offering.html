offering

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːf.ɚ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɒf.ər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
something that is presented or provided for consideration, sale, or acceptance, such as a product, service, or proposal.
something presented or provided for consideration

Examples

The company launched three new offerings to attract technology investors this year.
Her latest offering to the charity was a significant donation.
Several publishers introduced innovative offerings at the annual book fair.

Synset Examples

noteworthy new offerings for investors included several index funds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proposal - a suggestion or plan put forward for consideration
  • Synonym: bid - an offer of a price, especially at an auction
  • Synonym: auction - a public sale in which goods or property are sold to the highest bidder

Etymology

From Old English 'offrian' (to present, to sacrifice), from Latin 'offerre' (to present, to bring before).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔf.ɚ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɒf.ər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
a sum of money or other contribution given, especially to a church or charity, as an act of worship or charity
charitable or religious contribution

Examples

The congregation made a generous offering to support the building repairs.
Every Sunday, members place their offering in the collection plate.
The charity relies heavily on public offerings to fund its programs.

Synset Examples

oblations for aid to the poor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: donation - something given to a charity, especially money
  • Synonym: contribution - a gift or payment to a common fund or collection

Etymology

From Old English offrian (to offer up in sacrifice), from Latin offerre (to present, bring before).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01043530-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈɔːfərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɒfərɪŋ / (BrE)
a sum of money or items donated to a religious organization for religious purposes
donation given to a religious organization

Examples

The congregation placed their offering in the collection plate during the service.
He made a generous offering to support the church's new building fund.
The temple visitors left food and flowers as an offering to the gods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: donation - something that is given to a person or organization, especially money
  • Synonym: tithe - one tenth part of annual produce or earnings, formerly taken as a tax for the support of the Church

Etymology

From Old English 'offrian' (to offer), from Latin 'offerre' (to present, to bring before).

Synset POS: n
