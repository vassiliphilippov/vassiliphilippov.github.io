Chartres

1. PROPN

/ ʃɑrtrə / (AmE), / ˈʃɑːtrə / (BrE)
a town in northern France, famous for its Gothic cathedral, a masterpiece of medieval architecture
French town famous for its cathedral

Examples

The stunning cathedral in Chartres attracts visitors from around the world.
We spent a day exploring the medieval streets of Chartres and admiring its architecture.
Art historians often study the stained-glass windows of Chartres Cathedral.

Etymology

From French Chartres, name of the town; originally Latin 'Autricum', later 'Carnutes', tribe inhabiting the region.
