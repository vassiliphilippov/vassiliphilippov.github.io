archine

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrtʃin / (AmE), / ˈɑːtʃiːn / (BrE)
a historical Russian unit of length equal to approximately 71 centimeters
old Russian unit of length (71 cm)

Examples

The cloth was measured in archines at the market in 19th-century Russia.
He bought five archines of silk to make a dress for his wife.
Historians often convert archine measurements into meters to compare with modern data.

Etymology

From Russian аршин (arshin), possibly from Persian اَرْشِن‎ (aršin), meaning a measure of length.
