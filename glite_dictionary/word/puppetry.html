puppetry

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌpətri / (AmE), / ˈpʌpɪtri / (BrE)
the art or practice of creating and manipulating puppets, especially in theatrical performances
the art of making and using puppets

Examples

He specializes in puppetry and has performed shows across Europe.
The museum offers workshops on traditional puppetry for children.
Modern puppetry often incorporates new technology to enhance performances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puppet - a figure moved by hand, strings, or rods in performances

Etymology

From puppet + -ry (denoting occupation or practice).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpʌp.ɪ.tri / (AmE), / ˈpʌp.ɪ.tri / (BrE)
A style of dramatic performance that is stiff, artificial, or lacking in natural expression, as if performed by puppets.
stiff or artificial dramatic performance

Examples

The actor's puppetry made it hard for the audience to connect with the character.
Critics complained about the puppetry of the performances, noting the lack of emotion.
Her reading of the lines had a certain puppetry that detracted from the play's realism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wooden - lacking in natural expression, awkward
  • Antonym: naturalness - the quality of being natural or genuine in performance

Etymology

From puppet + -ry, formed after the pattern of other art or practice nouns.

Source: WordNet
