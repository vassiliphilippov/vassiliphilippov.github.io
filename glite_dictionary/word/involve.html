to involve

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈvɑlv / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɒlv / (BrE)
to include or contain something as a necessary or integral part
include as a part

Examples

The new policy will involve changes in how employees are trained.
Any renovation project is likely to involve additional costs.
Her research involves the study of rare plant species.

Synset Examples

Dinner at Joe's always involves at least six courses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to include - to comprise or contain as part of a whole
  • Antonym: to exclude - to deliberately leave out; to not include as a part

Etymology

From Latin 'involvere', meaning 'to roll in, wrap up'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02639299-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈvɑlv / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɒlv / (BrE)
to include or have as a necessary part or result; to require as a condition or consequence.
to include as a necessary part

Examples

The renovation will involve several major changes to the layout.
Achieving this goal involves both time and dedication from everyone on the team.
Adopting a pet often involves long-term commitment and responsibility.

Synset Examples

This decision involves many changes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entail - to involve as a necessary consequence
  • Antonym: to exclude - to leave out; to not include as a necessary part

Etymology

From Latin involvere (to enwrap, entangle), from in- (in) + volvere (to roll).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02642216-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɪnˈvɑlv / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɒlv / (BrE)
to make something complex or intricate; to make a situation or process complicated or difficult to understand
to make complicated or intricate

Examples

The legal proceedings involved a series of complicated steps that confused many participants.
Her explanation involved technical jargon and intricate details that made it hard to follow.
The project quickly involved more people and processes than originally anticipated.

Synset Examples

The situation was rather involved

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to complicate - to make something more difficult or complex
  • Antonym: to simplify - to make something simpler or easier to understand

Etymology

From Latin involvere (to roll in, enwrap) via Old French envolver.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00402444-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ɪnˈvɑlv / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɒlv / (BrE)
to occupy or engage the interest or attention of someone deeply
engage someone's interest or attention

Examples

The novel involved me so much that I lost track of time.
Her presentation involved the audience from the very first minute.
It was a complex story that involved him emotionally throughout the film.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to absorb - to hold all of someone’s attention or interest
  • Synonym: to engross - to completely occupy someone's attention
  • Antonym: bore - to cause to feel weary by dullness or tediousness

Etymology

From Latin involvere (to roll in, envelop).

Examples
