invocation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.voʊˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.vəʊˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of calling upon a spirit, devil, or supernatural force, especially through a ritual or prayer
calling upon a spirit or supernatural power

Examples

The priest began the invocation to summon ancient spirits from beyond.
During the ceremony, an invocation was used to call forth the demon.
Medieval grimoires often contain instructions for the invocation of supernatural beings.

Etymology

From Latin invocatio, from invocare (to call upon).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪnvoʊˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnvəˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
A prayer asking for God's help, guidance, or presence, especially at the beginning of a religious service.
prayer for divine help or guidance

Examples

The minister began the ceremony with an invocation to seek blessings for the couple.
Each meeting opens with an invocation led by a member of the clergy.
The choir stood quietly as the invocation was offered before the service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benediction - a short invocation for divine help, blessing, and guidance, usually at the end of worship service

Etymology

From Latin 'invocatio', from 'invocare' meaning 'to call upon'.

3. NOUN

The act of calling for help, support, or inspiration, especially from a higher power or authority.

Examples

During times of crisis, people often turn to invocation for strength and comfort.
Her speech ended with an invocation of unity and peace among all communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supplication - the action of asking or begging for something earnestly or humbly
  • Synonym: benediction - an utterance of blessing, especially at the beginning of a service

Etymology

From Latin 'invocatio', from 'invocare' meaning 'to call upon'.

4. NOUN

/ ˌɪnvəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnvəˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a spoken formula or incantation intended to summon a spirit or devil, especially in religious or magical contexts
spell or chant to summon a spirit or devil

Examples

The sorceress began her invocation to summon the demon from the underworld.
He recited an ancient invocation hoping to raise the dark spirit.
The ritual required a precise invocation to successfully conjure the entity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incantation - a series of words said as a magic spell
  • Antonym: banishment - a ritual or spell to send away a spirit

Etymology

From Latin invocatio (‘a calling upon’), from invocare (‘to call upon, invoke’).
