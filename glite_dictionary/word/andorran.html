Andorran

1. NOUN

/ ænˈdɔrən / (AmE), / anˈdɔːrən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Andorra
person from Andorra

Examples

The new student is an Andorran who recently moved to our town.
During the Olympics, the Andorran athletes proudly carried their nation's flag.
He struck up a conversation with an Andorran in a café in Barcelona.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Andorra - a small country in southwestern Europe

Etymology

From Andorra, the name of the country, plus -an (denoting inhabitant).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09709892-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ænˈdɔɹən / (AmE), / ænˈdɔːrən / (BrE)
of or relating to Andorra or its people.
of or from Andorra

Examples

The Andorran hills are famous for their beautiful views.
Many Andorran traditions have Spanish and French influences.
He is proud of his Andorran heritage.

Synset Examples

the Andorran hills

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Andorra - a small country in the Pyrenees between France and Spain

Etymology

From Andorra, the name of the country, with the suffix -an meaning 'of or relating to'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02989545-a
Synset POS: a
