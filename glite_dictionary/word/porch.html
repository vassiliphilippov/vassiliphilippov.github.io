porch

1. NOUN

/ pɔrtʃ / (AmE), / pɔːtʃ / (BrE)
A structure attached to the outside of a building, usually forming a covered entrance.
covered entrance attached to a building

Examples

We sat on the porch and watched the sun set over the hills.
She left her shoes on the front porch before coming inside.
The mailman left the package on the porch this morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: veranda - an open, roofed porch or balcony, often partly enclosed, along the outside of a building

Etymology

From Old French *porche*, from Latin *porticus* (colonnade, porch), from *porta* (gate).
