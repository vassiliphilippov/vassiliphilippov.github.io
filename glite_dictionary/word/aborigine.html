Aborigine

1. NOUN

/ ˌæbəˈrɪdʒəni / (AmE), / ˌabəˈrɪdʒəni / (BrE)
a member of the group of people who lived in Australia before European settlers arrived
native Australian person

Examples

Some Aborigines continue to practice traditional customs and storytelling.
The artwork in the museum was created by a renowned Aborigine.
Many Aborigines are working to preserve their languages and cultural heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Aboriginal - relating to the indigenous people of Australia

Etymology

From Latin 'aborigines' meaning original inhabitants.
