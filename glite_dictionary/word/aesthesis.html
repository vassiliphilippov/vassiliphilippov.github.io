aesthesis

1. NOUN

/ ɛsˈθiːsɪs / (AmE), / iːsˈθiːsɪs / (BrE)
A basic sensation or perception produced by external stimulation; an elementary form of awareness of being stimulated by the senses.
elementary sensory perception

Examples

The sudden aesthesis of cold air on her skin made her shiver.
Children often giggle at the aesthesis produced by tickling.
When the medication wore off, he became aware of every small aesthesis from his injured hand.

Synset Examples

a sensation of touch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensation - a physical feeling or perception resulting from something that happens to or comes into contact with the body

Etymology

From Greek aisthēsis (perception, sensation).
