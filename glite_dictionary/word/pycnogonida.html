Pycnogonida

1. NOUN

/ pɪkˌnɑːɡəˈnaɪdə / (AmE), / pɪkˌnɒɡəˈnaɪdə / (BrE)
A class of small, spider-like marine arthropods commonly known as sea spiders, found in oceans worldwide.
a class of sea spiders (marine arthropods)

Examples

The deep-sea expedition discovered several Pycnogonida near the hydrothermal vents.
Researchers are fascinated by the unique anatomy of Pycnogonida.
Unlike true spiders, Pycnogonida have their organs extended into their legs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea spider - common name for members of the class Pycnogonida

Etymology

From Greek 'pyknos' (dense, thick) + 'gonos' (knee, joint), denoting their jointed legs.
