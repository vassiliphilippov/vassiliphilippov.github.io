the Venerable Bede

1. PROPN

/ ði ˈvɛnərəbəl ˈbiːd / (AmE), / ðə ˈvɛnərəbəl ˈbiːd / (BrE)
(c. 672–735) An English monk, historian, and scholar, known for his influential works on early English history and Christian doctrine.
English monk and historian (672–735)

Examples

The Venerable Bede is often regarded as the father of English history.
Many medieval scholars were influenced by the writings of The Venerable Bede.
In his later years, The Venerable Bede completed his famous work, the 'Ecclesiastical History of the English People'.

Etymology

'Venerable' is a title of respect used in the Roman Catholic Church. 'Bede' is derived from Old English 'Bǣda.'
