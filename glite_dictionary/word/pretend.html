pretend

1. VERB

/ prɪˈtɛnd / (AmE), / prɪˈtɛnd / (BrE)
to act as if something is true in order to deceive someone; to feign or simulate a state or feeling
act as if something is true to deceive

Examples

She tried to pretend she wasn't disappointed, but her face gave her away.
The children love to pretend they are pirates searching for treasure.
He would often pretend to be asleep when his parents came in.

Synset Examples

He feigned that he was ill
He shammed a headache

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to feign - to pretend to be affected by a feeling, state, or injury
  • Synonym: sham - to pretend or fake something
  • Antonym: genuine - truly what something is said to be; authentic, not pretending

Etymology

From Old French 'pretendre', from Latin 'praetendere' (to stretch forth, allege as an excuse).

Synset POS: v

6. VERB

/ prɪˈtɛnd / (AmE), / prɪˈtɛnd / (BrE)
to behave in a way that is not genuine, often to create a false impression or feeling
to act insincerely or affectedly

Examples

She tried to pretend she was happy, but her smile was obviously forced.

  • Synonym: to simulate - to imitate or reproduce the appearance or character of something
  • Antonym: be genuine - to behave truly and sincerely, opposite of pretending

Etymology
