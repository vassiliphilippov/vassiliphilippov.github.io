Paradisaeidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌpær.ə.dɪˈsi.ə.daɪ.iː / (AmE), / ˌpar.ə.dɪˈsiː.ə.daɪ.iː / (BrE)
A family of passerine birds native to New Guinea and surrounding regions, known for the spectacular plumage and courtship displays of the males, commonly called birds of paradise.
family of birds of paradise

Examples

The Paradisaeidae family is renowned for the vibrant plumage of its male species.
Scientists discovered a new member of the Paradisaeidae in Papua New Guinea.
Many nature documentaries feature the elaborate courtship dances of birds from the Paradisaeidae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bird of paradise - any bird in the family Paradisaeidae, known for striking plumage

Etymology

From modern Latin Paradisaeidae, from Paradisaea (type genus) + -idae (zoological family suffix).
