chukka

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃʌkə / (AmE), / ˈtʃʌkə / (BrE)
In polo, one of the periods into which a match is divided, usually lasting seven and a half minutes.
period of play in a polo match

Examples

The third chukka saw the underdog team take the lead.
Each polo match is typically divided into six chukkas.
He was injured during the final chukka of the tournament.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: period - an interval of time during a sporting event

Etymology

From Hindi 'chakkar', meaning 'circle' or 'turn', via Urdu.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃʌkə / (AmE), / ˈtʃʌkə / (BrE)
An ankle-high boot or shoe, typically made of suede or leather, laced through two or three pairs of eyelets.
ankle-high suede/leather boot or shoe

Examples

He wore a pair of brown suede chukkas to the office with his jeans.
These chukkas are both stylish and comfortable for everyday wear.
Leather chukkas are a versatile choice for both casual and semi-formal outfits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: desert boot - a type of chukka boot with a crepe sole, originally worn by British soldiers in the desert

Etymology

Probably from 'chukka', a term used in polo for a period of play, indicating its sporting origin.
