Campanula rapunculoides

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæm.pəˈnjuː.lə rəˌpʊŋ.kjəˈlɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / ˌkæm.pəˈnjuː.lə rəˌpʌŋ.kjʊˈlɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
An erect European herb with creeping rootstocks and nodding spikelike racemes of blue to violet flowers, commonly known as creeping bellflower.
a European herb with blue-violet flowers

Examples

The gardener struggled to remove the persistent Campanula rapunculoides from her flowerbeds.
In some regions, Campanula rapunculoides is considered an invasive species due to its spreading roots.
The violet bells of Campanula rapunculoides added a splash of color to the woodland path.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creeping bellflower - Common name for Campanula rapunculoides

Etymology

From Latin: 'campanula' meaning 'little bell', referring to the bell-shaped flowers, and 'rapunculoides' indicating resemblance to the genus Rapunculus.
