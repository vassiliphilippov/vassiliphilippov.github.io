underwriter

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌndərˌraɪtər / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌraɪtə / (BrE)
a person or company that assesses and assumes some or all of the risk in an insurance policy, often by approving applications and selling insurance
person who assesses and sells insurance

Examples

The underwriter reviewed my application before approving my health insurance policy.
As an underwriter, she must assess risk carefully before selling insurance to clients.
Many underwriters work closely with agents to customize coverage for each customer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insurer - a person or company that provides insurance

Etymology

From underwrite (to sign one's name under), originally from Old English 'underwrītan', meaning to write under or subscribe.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʌndərˌraɪtər / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌraɪtə / (BrE)
a person or company, especially an insurance company, that assesses and accepts financial risk in exchange for a fee, typically by selling insurance policies or guaranteeing securities
person/company assuming financial risk (esp. insurance)

Examples

After reviewing the applicant's information, the underwriter decided to approve the life insurance policy.
Our company works closely with the underwriter to evaluate new insurance products.
The bank acted as the underwriter for several major corporate bonds this year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insurer - a person or company that provides insurance
  • Antonym: policyholder - a person who owns an insurance policy

Etymology

From 'underwrite,' originally meaning to write one's name under or at the end of a document as a signature, particularly when assuming responsibility, from Old English 'underwritan.'

3. NOUN

/ ˈʌndərˌraɪtər / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌraɪtə / (BrE)
a person or organization, especially a banker, that undertakes to finance or guarantee new securities or other financial ventures by taking on risk, often for a fee
person or organization that guarantees new securities

Examples

The investment bank acted as the principal underwriter for the company's initial public offering.
As an underwriter, she assessed the risks involved in issuing new corporate bonds.
Major underwriters often build relationships with large institutional investors to ensure the success of new securities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guarantor - a person or organization that guarantees something

Etymology

From the practice of writing one's name under the terms of a risk, particularly in insurance and banking.
