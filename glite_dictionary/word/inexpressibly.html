inexpressibly

1. ADV

/ ˌɪnɪkˈsprɛsəbli / (AmE), / ˌɪnɪkˈsprɛsəbli / (BrE)
to a degree that cannot be adequately described or expressed in words
to an indescribable degree

Examples

The view from the mountain was inexpressibly beautiful, leaving everyone speechless.
She felt inexpressibly grateful for the support of her friends during difficult times.
The loss was inexpressibly painful, and words failed to convey her sorrow.

Synset Examples

she was looking very young tonight, and, as usual, indescribably beautiful, in a simple strapless dress of a green and white silky cotton

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indescribably - to a degree that cannot be described

Etymology

Formed from 'inexpressible' (from in- 'not' + express + -ible) + -ly.
