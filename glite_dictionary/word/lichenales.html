Lichenales

1. NOUN

/ laɪkəˈneɪliːz / (AmE), / laɪkɪˈneɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of lichen-forming fungi, used especially in former biological classifications and now largely replaced by the division Lichenes
order of lichen-forming fungi (obsolete classification)

Examples

The Lichenales were commonly used in older taxonomic systems before being reclassified.
Many species formerly grouped under Lichenales are now included in the division Lichenes.
Research into Lichenales has revealed much about the evolution of lichen-forming fungi.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lichenes - the division of lichen-forming fungi (current classification)

Etymology

From New Latin, based on 'Lichen' (the genus) and the Latin suffix '-ales' (denoting an order).
