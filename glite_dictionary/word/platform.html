platform

1. NOUN

/ ˈplæt.fɔrm / (AmE), / ˈplætfɔːm / (BrE)
a woman's shoe with a very high, thick sole, often making the wearer appear taller
woman's shoe with a thick, high sole

Examples

She wore her new platform shoes to the party, gaining several inches in height.
Those red platforms are very stylish but can be hard to walk in.
The 1970s saw the return of the bold platform as a fashion statement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: high heel - a woman's shoe with a high, raised heel

2. NOUN

/ ˈplætfɔrm / (AmE), / ˈplætfɔːm / (BrE)
a long, flat, raised structure at a railway station, where passengers get on and off trains
raised area at railway station for boarding trains

Examples

She waited on the platform for her train to arrive.
The announcement asked all passengers to go to platform 3.
Crowds gathered on the platform as the morning train pulled in.

Etymology

From Old French platforme (flat form), from plat (flat) + forme (form, shape).
