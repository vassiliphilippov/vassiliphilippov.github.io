syncope

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋkəpi / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋkəpi / (BrE)
(Phonology) The omission of sounds or letters from the middle of a word, as in 'fo'c'sle' from 'forecastle'.
loss of sounds within a word (phonology)

Examples

In some dialects, the syncope of unstressed vowels is common.
The word 'chocolate' often undergoes syncope in casual speech, pronounced as 'choc'late'.
Historical texts provide many examples of syncope in English word forms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elision - the omission of a sound or syllable when speaking

Etymology

From Latin syncope, from Greek synkopē ‘cutting up’

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋkəpi / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋkəpi / (BrE)
a temporary loss of consciousness caused by a sudden decrease in blood flow to the brain
temporary loss of consciousness; fainting
medical

Examples

The patient experienced syncope after standing up too quickly from the hospital bed.
During the marathon, several runners suffered from syncope due to dehydration and heat.
If you have a history of syncope, consult a doctor for a thorough cardiovascular evaluation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fainting - losing consciousness for a short time due to lack of blood to the brain

Etymology

From Greek synkōpē (a cutting up or cutting short), from syn- (together) + kopē (a cutting).
