to crumple

1. VERB

/ ˈkrʌmpəl / (AmE), / ˈkrʌmpəl / (BrE)
to collapse or fall apart suddenly, especially as a result of external pressure or force
collapse or fall apart suddenly

Examples

The old bridge began to crumple after years of neglect and heavy traffic.
During the earthquake, several poorly built houses crumpled to the ground.
The cardboard box crumpled under the weight of the books.

Synset Examples

the building crumbled after the explosion
Negotiations broke down

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collapse - to fall down or cave in suddenly
  • Synonym: to disintegrate - to break into small parts or pieces

Etymology

From Middle English crumpelen, frequentative of 'crump', meaning 'bent, crooked.'

2. VERB

/ ˈkrʌmpəl / (AmE), / ˈkrʌmpəl / (BrE)
to become wrinkled, creased, or bent out of shape, especially in a way that is not smooth or flat
become wrinkled or creased

Examples

If you throw your shirt on the floor, it will crumple and look messy.
Leaves often crumple during a drought as they lose moisture.
The letter began to crumple after being stored carelessly in his pocket.

Synset Examples

This fabric won't wrinkle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crease - to become wrinkled or marked by lines; to make a line or fold in something, especially paper or fabric
  • Antonym: smooth - to make or become even, flat, or free of wrinkles

Etymology

From Middle English 'crumplen', frequentative form of 'crump' (bent); related to Old English 'crump' (bent, crooked).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00565934-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈkrʌmpəl / (AmE), / ˈkrʌmpəl / (BrE)
to crush or press something into wrinkles or folds; to become creased or wrinkled
to press into wrinkles or folds

Examples

She accidentally crumpled the letter before throwing it away.
He crumpled the paper into a ball and tossed it in the bin.
The fabric crumples easily if you don't hang it up right after washing.

Synset Examples

She puckered her lips

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crease - to make a line or ridge in cloth, paper, etc. by folding or crushing it
  • Antonym: smooth - to make something even or without wrinkles

Etymology

From Middle English 'crumpen' (to curl up), probably of Scandinavian origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01281420-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈkrʌmpəl / (AmE), / ˈkrʌmpəl / (BrE)
to suddenly fall down or collapse, especially due to weakness or exhaustion
fall down or collapse suddenly

Examples

The exhausted runner crumpled to the ground at the finish line.
She watched as the old building slowly crumpled during the demolition.
He crumpled under the pressure and fainted in front of the crowd.

Synset Examples

His knees buckled

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collapse - fall down or cave in suddenly
  • Synonym: buckled - to bend or give way under pressure

Etymology

From Middle English crumplen, related to Old English crumb (bent, crooked).
