vessel

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛsəl / (AmE), / ˈvɛsl̩ / (BrE)
a tube in the body through which a fluid such as blood or lymph circulates
tube for fluid circulation in the body
medical

Examples

An artery is a type of vessel that carries blood away from the heart.
Blockages in a blood vessel can cause serious medical problems.
The surgeon repaired the damaged vessel to restore proper circulation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artery - a blood vessel that carries blood away from the heart
  • Synonym: vein - a blood vessel that carries blood toward the heart
  • Synonym: capillary - a tiny blood vessel connecting arteries and veins

Etymology

From Latin 'vascellum', diminutive of 'vas' (vessel, dish).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɛsəl / (AmE), / ˈvɛsəl / (BrE)
a container used for holding liquids, such as a bowl, cup, or jug
container for liquids

Examples

The laboratory technician poured the solution into a clean vessel for analysis.
She displayed the antique vessel on a shelf in her living room.
A glass vessel is ideal for storing olive oil because it preserves the flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: container - a receptacle for holding something

Etymology

From Latin vasculum, diminutive of vas (vessel, dish, container).

3. NOUN

/ ˈvɛsəl / (AmE), / ˈvɛsəl / (BrE)
A ship or large boat designed for traveling on water.
ship or boat for water transport

Examples

The cargo was loaded onto the vessel before it set sail for the next port.
The coast guard inspected the fishing vessel for safety violations.
During the storm, the small vessel struggled to remain afloat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boat - a small vessel for travel on water
  • Synonym: ship - a large vessel for navigating deep waters

Etymology

From Old French 'vaissel' (ship, container), from Latin 'vascellum' (small vessel, container), diminutive of 'vas' (vessel, dish).
