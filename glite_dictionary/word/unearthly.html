unearthly

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɝθli / (AmE), / ʌnˈɜːθli / (BrE)
concerned with or affecting the spirit rather than the material world; spiritual or supernatural
spiritual; not of this world

Examples

The painting conveyed an unearthly sense of peace and tranquility.
She spoke about her unearthly devotion with a soft, reverent voice.
Their voices joined together in an unearthly harmony that seemed to fill the entire church.

Synset Examples

a spiritual approach to life
spiritual fulfillment
spiritual values
unearthly love

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiritual - relating to, or affecting the human spirit or soul
  • Antonym: earthly - relating to the material or physical world

Etymology

From un- (not) + earthly (of the earth), meaning not of this world.

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɝrθli / (AmE), / ʌnˈɜːθli / (BrE)
strange or unnatural in a way that suggests something supernatural; otherworldly.
strange, supernatural, or otherworldly

Examples

A strange, unearthly light filled the abandoned house, casting eerie shadows on the walls.
Her singing had an unearthly beauty that left the audience speechless.
We awoke in the night to an unearthly howl echoing through the empty fields.

Synset Examples

an eldritch screech
the three weird sisters
stumps … had uncanny shapes as of monstrous creatures
an unearthly light
he could hear the unearthly scream of some curlew piercing the din

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supernatural - attributed to forces beyond scientific understanding or the laws of nature
  • Antonym: earthly - relating to the world or human life (opposite sense)
