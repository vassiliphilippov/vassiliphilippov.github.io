to telepathize

1. VERB

/ tɛˈlɛpəˌθaɪz / (AmE), / tɛˈlɛpəˌθaɪz / (BrE)
to communicate with someone by means of telepathy rather than through normal sensory channels
communicate by telepathy

Examples

The twins claimed they could telepathize with each other over great distances.
Science fiction often features characters who can telepathize to share secrets instantly.
She wondered if it was possible to telepathize her thoughts to her friend during the experiment.

Synset Examples

some people believe they can telepathize with others around the world

Related Terms

  • Synonym: telepathy - the ability to communicate by transmitting thoughts directly without using sensory channels

Etymology

From 'telepathy' + '-ize', meaning to make or become telepathic.
