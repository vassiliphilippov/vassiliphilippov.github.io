nonchalantly

1. ADV

/ nɑnʃəˈlɑntli / (AmE), / nɒnʃəˈlɑːntli / (BrE)
in a manner that shows a lack of concern or interest; casually and unconcernedly.
in an unconcerned or casual manner

Examples

She nonchalantly tossed her coat onto the chair and began talking as if nothing had happened.
He strolled nonchalantly into the meeting, not worried about being late.
The student nonchalantly admitted he had forgotten his homework, showing no sign of embarrassment.

Synset Examples

glanced casually at the headlines

Related Terms

  • Synonym: casually - in a relaxed and informal way
  • Antonym: anxiously - in a manner showing worry or concern

Etymology

From the French 'nonchalant,' meaning 'indifferent' or 'careless,' from 'non-' (not) + 'chaloir' (to have concern for).

2. ADV

/ ˌnɑnʃəˈlɑntli / (AmE), / ˌnɒnʃəˈlɑːntli / (BrE)
in a calm and relaxed way that shows you are not worried or excited
in an unconcerned or relaxed manner

Examples

He walked into the meeting nonchalantly, as if nothing important was happening.
She shrugged nonchalantly when asked about the mistake, showing little concern.
Jake answered the difficult question nonchalantly, impressing everyone with his composure.

Synset Examples

without more ado Barker borrowed a knife from his brigade Major and honed it on a carborundum stone as coolly as a butcher

Related Terms

  • Synonym: casually - in a relaxed and informal way, without much thought or effort
  • Antonym: anxiously - in a worried or nervous way

Etymology

From French 'nonchalant', from Latin 'non' (not) + 'calēre' (to be warm, to care).
