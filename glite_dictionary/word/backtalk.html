backtalk

1. NOUN

/ ˈbækˌtɑk / (AmE), / ˈbækˌtɔːk / (BrE)
rude or disrespectful replies, especially to someone in authority
insolent or disrespectful reply
informal american

Examples

The teacher warned the students that she would not tolerate any more backtalk.
If you give your parents backtalk, you might lose your privileges.
She was punished for her constant backtalk during the meeting.

Synset Examples

don't give me any of your sass

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sass - impertinent or cheeky talk
  • Antonym: respect - polite or deferential speech, the opposite of backtalk

Etymology

From 'back' + 'talk', originally North American usage indicating talking back to someone in authority.
