to dialyse

1. VERB

/ daɪəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / daɪəˌlaɪz / (BrE)
to separate substances in a solution by means of dialysis, often to remove unwanted materials in medical procedures or laboratory processes
separate by dialysis
medical british

Examples

The laboratory technician used a membrane to dialyse the solution, separating small molecules from proteins.
Many patients with kidney failure need to dialyse regularly to remove toxins from their blood.
Researchers often dialyse cell extracts before further biochemical analysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dialyze - American English spelling of 'dialyse'

Etymology

From Greek 'dialusis' (dissolution), via French 'dialyse'.
