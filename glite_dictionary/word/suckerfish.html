suckerfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌkərˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈsʌkəˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a marine fish with a flattened, elongated body and a sucking disk on its head, used for attaching to larger animals or objects
marine fish with sucking disk on head

Examples

Some species of suckerfish attach themselves to sharks for transportation and protection.
The suckerfish uses its disk-like mouth to cling onto boats and swimming animals.
Researchers observed a group of suckerfish cleaning parasites off a whale's skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remora - a type of suckerfish known for attaching to sharks and other large marine animals
