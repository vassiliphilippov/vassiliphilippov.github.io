prudential

1. ADJ

/ pruːˈdɛnʃəl / (AmE), / pruːˈdɛnʃəl / (BrE)
Based on or showing careful judgment, especially in business or financial matters.
characterized by careful judgment

Examples

The board adopted a prudential approach to investing the company's assets.
Her prudential decisions helped the organization survive during difficult times.
Many banks implement prudential regulations to minimize financial risks.

Synset Examples

he abstained partly for prudential reasons

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prudent - showing careful and sensible judgment

Etymology

From Middle French 'prudentiel', from Latin 'prudentia' (prudence).
