Dromaius novaehollandiae

1. NOUN

/ droʊˈmeɪ.i.əs noʊ.viːˈhɑːlən.di.aɪ / (AmE), / drəʊˈmeɪ.i.əs nəʊ.viːˈhɒl.ən.dɪ.aɪ / (BrE)
a large Australian flightless bird, similar to the ostrich but smaller, commonly known as the emu
the emu, a large Australian flightless bird

Examples

The Dromaius novaehollandiae is the second-largest living bird by height, after the ostrich.
Farmers in Australia often encounter the Dromaius novaehollandiae roaming open plains and grasslands.
Unlike the ostrich, the Dromaius novaehollandiae is native to Australia and is well adapted to its climate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emu - common name for Dromaius novaehollandiae, large Australian flightless bird
  • Degree Higher: ostrich - the largest living bird, native to Africa, also flightless

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining Greek dromaios (runner) and Latin novae hollandiae (of New Holland, an early name for Australia).
