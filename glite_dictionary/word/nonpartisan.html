nonpartisan

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɑnˈpɑrtɪzən / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈpɑːtɪzən / (BrE)
a person who does not support or belong to any political party or group
person with no political party affiliation

Examples

As a nonpartisan, she prefers to evaluate policies on their merits rather than party loyalty.
The debate was moderated by a nonpartisan to ensure fairness for all candidates.
Many voters identify as nonpartisan because they do not feel represented by any major party.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: partisan - a strong supporter of a particular party, cause, or person

Etymology

From non- (not) + partisan (supporter of a party), first used in the early 20th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˌnɑːnˈpɑːrtɪzən / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈpɑːtɪzən / (BrE)
Not supporting or controlled by any political group; free from party affiliation or bias.
not biased toward any political party

Examples

The commission is a nonpartisan organization dedicated to fair elections.
We need a nonpartisan analysis to understand the facts objectively.
She was praised for her nonpartisan approach to policymaking.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: partisan - strong supporter of a party or cause

Etymology

From non- (not) + partisan, originally from French 'partisan' (supporter of a party).
