catharsis

1. NOUN

/ kəˈθɑrsɪs / (AmE), / kəˈθɑːsɪs / (BrE)
the process of releasing, and thereby providing relief from, strong or repressed emotions, especially through art or therapy
emotional release or cleansing

Examples

Writing poetry provided a much-needed catharsis after her difficult breakup.
Many people find that crying offers catharsis during stressful times.
The film's dramatic ending served as a powerful catharsis for the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emotional release - letting go of strong emotions for relief

Etymology

From Greek 'katharsis' (cleansing), from 'kathairein' (to purge).

2. NOUN

/ kəˈθɑrsɪs / (AmE), / kəˈθɑːsɪs / (BrE)
The process of purging the body, especially the bowels, by the use of a cathartic medicine.
purging of the bowels with medicine
medical

Examples

The physician recommended catharsis to relieve the patient's severe constipation.
In ancient medicine, catharsis was a common treatment for various illnesses.
Excessive use of drugs to induce catharsis can lead to dehydration and other complications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purgation - the act of cleansing or purging the bowels

Etymology

From Greek 'katharsis' (purging, cleansing), from 'kathairein' (to cleanse).
