charade

1. NOUN

/ ʃəˈreɪd / (AmE), / ʃəˈrɑːd / (BrE)
an act or event that is clearly false or intended to deceive; a false outward show or pretense
false outward show; pretense

Examples

The announcement was nothing but a charade meant to appease the public.
She grew tired of maintaining the charade of happiness at social gatherings.
Their supposed friendship was a charade to mask their rivalry.

Synset Examples

a beggar's masquerade of wealth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretense - an attempt to make something that is not the case appear true
  • Synonym: masquerade - a false show or pretense
  • Synonym: deceit - the action or practice of deceiving someone by concealing or misrepresenting the truth
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being honest and truthful

Etymology

From French 'charade', originally referring to a kind of riddle.

2. NOUN

/ ʃəˈreɪd / (AmE), / ʃəˈrɑːd / (BrE)
A word or phrase that is acted out during the game of charades for others to guess.
word acted out in charades

Examples

During the party, my favorite charade was pretending to be a famous actor.
He chose a difficult charade that no one could guess.
Each team took turns acting out a charade while the others tried to solve it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pantomime - the act of expressing meaning through gestures without speech

Etymology

From French 'charade,' originally a kind of riddle.
