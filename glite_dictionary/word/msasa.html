msasa

1. NOUN

/ məˈsɑːsə / (AmE), / məˈsɑːsə / (BrE)
A small shrubby African tree (Brachystegia spiciformis) with compound leaves and racemes of small, fragrant green flowers.
species of African tree

Examples

The msasa tree is admired for its striking foliage during the dry season.
Many animals seek shelter under the dense canopy of the msasa.
The msasa is common in the savannah woodlands of southern Africa.

Etymology

From Shona (an African language) *msasa*, the local name for the tree.
