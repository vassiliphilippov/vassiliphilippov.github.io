Rosa canina

1. NOUN

/ ˌroʊzə kəˈnaɪnə / (AmE), / ˌrəʊzə kəˈniːnə / (BrE)
a species of wild rose native to Europe, with pink or white scentless flowers and prickly stems
species of wild rose (dog rose) native to Europe

Examples

The hedgerows in England are often filled with Rosa canina in late spring.
Botanists studied the medicinal properties of Rosa canina hips.
Many birds feed on the bright red fruit of Rosa canina during autumn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dog rose - common name for Rosa canina

Etymology

Latin scientific name: rosa (rose) + canina (of the dog), referring to its common name 'dog rose'.
