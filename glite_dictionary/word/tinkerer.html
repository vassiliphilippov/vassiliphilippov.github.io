tinkerer

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪŋ.kɚ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɪŋ.kərə / (BrE)
a person who enjoys repairing, modifying, or inventing mechanical or electronic devices, often as a hobby
person who enjoys fixing or experimenting with devices

Examples

Ever since he was a child, Mark has been a tinkerer, always taking apart gadgets to see how they work.
The tinkerer spent hours in his garage inventing new tools for his workshop.
As a proud tinkerer, she enjoys fixing broken appliances instead of throwing them away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tinker - a person who makes a living by mending pots and pans

Etymology

From 'tinker', meaning a person who mends metal utensils, plus '-er'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɪŋ.kɚ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɪŋ.kər.ər / (BrE)
a person who enjoys repairing, adjusting, or experimenting with objects or devices, often without formal skill or training
person who fixes or experiments with things

Examples

As a child, he was always a tinkerer, taking apart radios and trying to put them back together.
The tinkerer in me couldn't resist opening up the old clock to see how it worked.
Although untrained, she became the neighborhood tinkerer, fixing toys and small appliances for friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tinker - person who repairs things in an unskilled or casual way

Etymology

From 'tinker' (one who mends pots and pans), with the agent suffix '-er'.
