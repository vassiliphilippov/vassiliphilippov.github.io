Gram-positive

1. ADJ

/ ɡræm ˈpɑzətɪv / (AmE), / ɡræm ˈpɒzətɪv / (BrE)
(of bacteria) retaining the violet stain during Gram's method of staining, indicating a thick peptidoglycan cell wall.
bacteria retaining Gram's violet stain
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that the infection was caused by a Gram-positive bacterium.
Certain antibiotics are more effective against Gram-positive bacteria than Gram-negative ones.
The laboratory test identified several Gram-positive cocci in the sample.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Gram-negative - (of bacteria) not retaining the violet stain during Gram's method

Etymology

Named after Danish bacteriologist Hans Christian Gram, who developed the Gram stain method in 1884.
