Pertusaria

1. NOUN

/ pərˈtjuːsəriə / (AmE), / pəˈtjuːsəriə / (BrE)
A genus of crustose lichens that are a source of the dyes archil and litmus.
genus of dye-producing lichens

Examples

Researchers studied the chemical properties of Pertusaria to develop new natural dyes.
The bark was covered in patches of Pertusaria, giving it a whitish appearance.
Pertusaria plays a significant role in the production of litmus, which is used for pH testing.

Etymology

From Modern Latin, based on Latin 'pertusus' meaning 'pierced', referring to the lichen's surface features.

2. NOUN

/ pərˌtuːˈsɛːriə / (AmE), / pɜːˌtjuːˈsɑːriə / (BrE)
A genus of crustose lichens that are sources of the dyes archil and litmus.
genus of crustose lichens

Examples

Scientists collected samples of Pertusaria from the forest for chemical analysis.
The dye litmus is traditionally extracted from certain species within the Pertusaria genus.
Many crustose lichens, including Pertusaria, play an important role in their ecosystems.

Etymology

From Latin 'pertusus' meaning 'bored through', referring to the small holes or pores in the lichen.
