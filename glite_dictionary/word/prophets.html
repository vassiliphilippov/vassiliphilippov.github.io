Prophets

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑːfɪts / (AmE), / ˈprɒfɪts / (BrE)
the second of the three main divisions of the Hebrew Scriptures, containing the books of the prophets.
second division of Hebrew Scriptures

Examples

Many scholars devote years to studying the Prophets and their historical context.
The Prophets include books such as Isaiah, Jeremiah, and Ezekiel.
Children at the synagogue learned about stories from the Prophets during religious classes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Torah - the first division of the Hebrew Scriptures, also known as the Law or the Pentateuch
  • Degree Higher: Writings - the third division of the Hebrew Scriptures, including Psalms and other books
