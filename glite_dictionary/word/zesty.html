zesty

1. ADJ

/ ˈzɛsti / (AmE), / ˈzɛsti / (BrE)
full of energy, enthusiasm, or excitement; lively and spirited.
full of energy or enthusiasm

Examples

Her zesty performance brought the whole crowd to its feet.
The novel's zesty humor kept readers thoroughly entertained.
He delivered his speech with a zesty enthusiasm that inspired everyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lively - full of life and energy
  • Antonym: dull - lacking interest or excitement

Etymology

Formed from 'zest' (enthusiasm, lively interest) plus the -y suffix.

2. ADJ

/ ˈzɛsti / (AmE), / ˈzɛsti / (BrE)
having a lively, pleasantly strong or slightly spicy flavor or character
having a lively or pungent flavor

Examples

The salsa has a zesty flavor that really wakes up your taste buds.
She prefers zesty salad dressings with lots of herbs and lemon.
This soup tasted rather dull until I added a zesty squeeze of lime.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tangy - having a sharp or strong flavor or smell
  • Antonym: bland - lacking strong flavor

Etymology

From 'zest' + '-y'; 'zest' from French 'zeste' (citrus peel).
