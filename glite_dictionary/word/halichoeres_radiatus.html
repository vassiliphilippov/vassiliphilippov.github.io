Halichoeres radiatus

1. NOUN

/ hæ.lɪˈkoʊ.iːriz ræd.iˈeɪ.təs / (AmE), / hæ.lɪˈkɔː.ɪəriːz ræd.iˈeɪ.təs / (BrE)
A species of bluish and bronze wrasse found in the western Atlantic, from the Florida Keys to Brazil.
species of wrasse fish

Examples

The Halichoeres radiatus is admired by divers for its striking coloration.
Researchers observed a group of Halichoeres radiatus near a coral reef in the Bahamas.
Aquarists sometimes keep Halichoeres radiatus in large saltwater tanks due to its unique appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrasse - a family of marine fish, many of which are brightly colored

Etymology

From genus name Halichoeres, from Greek 'halios' (of the sea) + 'choiros' (pig, referring to snout shape); Latin 'radiatus' meaning 'rayed' or 'striped'.
