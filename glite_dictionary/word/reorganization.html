reorganization

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːˌɔːrɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌɔːɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
an extensive change in the structure or operations of an organization, such as a corporation or government, often to improve efficiency or adapt to new conditions
major structural change in an organization

Examples

The company announced a major reorganization to streamline its management structure.
After the new law was passed, a complete reorganization of the agency took place.
The reorganization resulted in several new departments being created.

Synset Examples

after the takeover there was a thorough reorganization
the reorganization was prescribed by federal bankruptcy laws

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restructuring - the act of organizing differently, especially in business contexts

Etymology

From re- (again) + organization, modeled on French réorganisation.

2. NOUN

/ ˌriːˌɔːrɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌɔːɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of changing the structure or arrangement of something, especially in a thorough or drastic way
changing the structure of something

Examples

The company announced a major reorganization to improve efficiency and cut costs.
After the reorganization of the department, some employees were given new roles.
The school board approved the reorganization of several academic programs.

Synset Examples

a committee was appointed to oversee the reorganization of the curriculum
top officials were forced out in the cabinet shakeup

Related Terms

  • Synonym: organization - the arrangement or structure of something

Etymology

From re- (again) + organization, modeled on French réorganisation.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01140308-n
Synset POS: n
