utilitarian

1. ADJ

/ juːˌtɪlɪˈtɛriən / (AmE), / juːˌtɪlɪˈteəriən / (BrE)
Designed to be useful or practical rather than attractive or comfortable, often disregarding other values.
practical; focused on usefulness

Examples

The furniture in the office is strictly utilitarian, lacking any decorative features.
She preferred a utilitarian approach to kitchen design, choosing only what was necessary.
Many modern buildings have a utilitarian appearance, focusing on efficiency over style.

Synset Examples

plain utilitarian kitchenware

Related Terms

  • Synonym: functional - designed to be practical and useful rather than attractive
  • Antonym: aesthetic - concerned with beauty or the appreciation of beauty

Etymology

From Latin 'utilitas' (usefulness) + English '-arian', influenced by the philosophy of utility.

She chose a utilitarian backpack that prioritized comfort and durability over fashion.
His designs are more utilitarian than decorative, focusing on function above all else.

Related Terms

3. NOUN

/ juˌtɪlɪˈtɛriən / (AmE), / juːˌtɪlɪˈteəriən / (BrE)
a person who believes that the value of something depends on its usefulness or utility, especially in reference to the philosophy of utilitarianism
believer in utilitarianism

Examples

As a committed utilitarian, she tries to maximize happiness for the greatest number.
Many philosophers debate whether a true utilitarian could endorse such actions.
Jeremy Bentham is often cited as a pioneering utilitarian.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pragmatist - a person who is guided more by practical considerations than by ideals

Etymology

From utilitarian + -ian, coined in the 18th century from Latin utilitas (usefulness).
