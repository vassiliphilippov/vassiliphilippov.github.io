possessive

1. ADJ

/ pəˈzɛsɪv / (AmE), / pəˈzɛsɪv / (BrE)
(of a word or form) expressing or indicating possession or ownership.
expressing possession or ownership

Examples

In English, possessive pronouns include 'my', 'your', and 'her'.
The possessive form of 'dog' is 'dog's'.
Teachers often ask students to identify the possessive case in a sentence.

Synset Examples

possessive pronouns
the genitive endings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genitive - the grammatical case expressing possession

Etymology

From Latin possessivus, from possess-, stem of possidēre (to possess).

2. ADJ

/ pəˈzɛsɪv / (AmE), / pəˈzɛsɪv / (BrE)
wanting to own or control things or people, and not willing to share them with others
desirous of owning; unwilling to share

Examples

The little boy was so possessive of his toys that he refused to lend them to his friends.
Some people can become possessive in relationships, not wanting their partner to spend time with others.
Her possessive attitude made it difficult for her siblings to play with any of her belongings.

Synset Examples

small children are so possessive they will not let others play with their toys

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jealous - feeling or showing envy of someone or their achievements and advantages
  • Antonym: generous - willing to give more of something than is strictly necessary or expected

Etymology

From Latin possessivus, from possidere (to possess).

3. ADJ

/ pəˈzɛsɪv / (AmE), / pəˈzɛsɪv / (BrE)
having or showing a desire to own, control, or dominate, especially in relationships
tending to control or dominate

Examples

Her boyfriend can be quite possessive and dislikes her talking to other men.
He grew possessive of his friends in college, not wanting to share them with anyone.
Some parents become overly possessive of their children as they grow older.

Synset Examples

a possessive parent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: controlling - having a strong need to dominate or influence others
  • Synonym: jealous - feeling or showing resentment against a rival; possessive of one's rights
  • Antonym: detached - emotionally uninvolved or indifferent

Etymology

From Latin possessivus, from possessio (possession).

4. NOUN

/ pəˈzɛsɪv / (AmE), / pəˈzɛsɪv / (BrE)
the grammatical case or form used to indicate ownership or possession
grammatical case of ownership

Examples

In English, the possessive is often formed by adding 's to a noun.
The sentence uses the possessive to show that the book belongs to John.
Her understanding of the possessive helped her edit the essay for correct grammar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genitive - the grammatical case typically used to indicate possession
