Novaya Zemlya

1. PROPN

/ noʊˈvɑː.jə zɛmˈjɑː / (AmE), / nəʊˈvɑː.jə zɛmˈjɑː / (BrE)
A group of two large islands in the Arctic Ocean, belonging to Russia, known as a site for nuclear testing.
Russian Arctic island group, nuclear test site

Examples

The Soviet Union conducted several nuclear tests on Novaya Zemlya during the Cold War.
Scientists study the effects of climate change in the remote region of Novaya Zemlya.
Access to Novaya Zemlya is strictly controlled due to military restrictions.

Etymology

From Russian Новая Земля, literally 'new land'.
