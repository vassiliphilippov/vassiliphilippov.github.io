Ostracoda

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑː.strəˈkoʊ.də / (AmE), / ˌɒs.trəˈkəʊ.də / (BrE)
A class of small aquatic crustaceans, commonly known as seed shrimps, characterized by a bivalve-like shell covering the body.
a class of small aquatic crustaceans

Examples

Fossils of Ostracoda have helped scientists understand ancient aquatic environments.
Many species within the Ostracoda are important indicators of water quality.
The course included a section on the anatomy of Ostracoda and related crustaceans.

Etymology

From Greek ostrakon (shell) + -oda (suffix used in zoological taxonomy).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɒstrəˈkoʊdə / (AmE), / ˌɒstrəˈkəʊdə / (BrE)
a subclass of small aquatic crustaceans, commonly known as seed shrimps, with a bivalve-like shell enclosing the body
subclass of small aquatic crustaceans

Examples

Many fossils of Ostracoda have been discovered in sedimentary rocks around the world.
Because of their bivalve shells, Ostracoda are often mistaken for tiny clams.
Scientists study Ostracoda to understand changes in ancient aquatic environments.

Etymology

From Greek 'ostrakon' (shell).
