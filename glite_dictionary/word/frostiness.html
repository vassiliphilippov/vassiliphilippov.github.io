frostiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɔsti nəs / (AmE), / ˈfrɒstɪ nɪs / (BrE)
a silvery-white color resembling frost
silvery-white color like frost

Examples

The soft frostiness of the moonlight made the landscape appear enchanted.
Her hair had a delicate frostiness that shimmered in the morning sun.
They painted the room with a touch of frostiness to create a wintry atmosphere.

Etymology

From 'frosty' + '-ness', derived from Old English 'forst' (frost).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfrɑːstinəs / (AmE), / ˈfrɒstinɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being cold like frost; chilly temperature or atmosphere.
coldness like frost

Examples

The frostiness of the morning air made everyone shiver as they stepped outside.
There was a certain frostiness in the air after the sudden cold front passed through.
The frostiness of the landscape was visible in the sparkling ice on the trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chilliness - the quality or state of being noticeably cold
  • Antonym: warmth - the quality or state of being warm

Etymology

Formed from 'frosty' + '-ness'.
