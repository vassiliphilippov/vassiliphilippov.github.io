Swainsona grandiflora

1. NOUN

/ sweɪnˈsoʊnə ˌgrændɪˈflɔrə / (AmE), / sweɪnˈsəʊnə ˌgrændɪˈflɔːrə / (BrE)
A shrubby perennial plant native to southern Australia, with downy or woolly stems, hairy leaves, and clusters of red or pink flowers.
Australian perennial plant with red to pink flowers

Examples

The vibrant red flowers of the Swainsona grandiflora brighten the Australian landscape in spring.
Gardeners appreciate Swainsona grandiflora for its woolly stems and colorful racemes.
Botanists often study the habitat preferences of Swainsona grandiflora in southern Australia.

Etymology

Named after British botanist Isaac Swainson; 'grandiflora' from Latin meaning 'large-flowered'.
