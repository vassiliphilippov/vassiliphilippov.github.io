vena epigastrica inferior

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪnə ˌɛpɪˌɡæstrɪkə ɪnˈfɪriər / (AmE), / ˈviːnə ˌɛpɪˌɡæstrɪkə ɪnˈfɪəriə / (BrE)
a vein in the lower abdomen that drains into the external iliac vein
abdominal vein draining into external iliac vein
medical

Examples

The vena epigastrica inferior is often examined during abdominal surgeries to avoid accidental injury.
An enlarged vena epigastrica inferior may indicate abnormal blood flow in the pelvic area.
Blood from the lower anterior abdominal wall drains through the vena epigastrica inferior into the external iliac vein.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: external iliac vein - major vein in the pelvis receiving blood from the abdomen and leg

Etymology

From Latin: 'vena' meaning vein, 'epigastrica' referring to the area above the stomach, and 'inferior' meaning lower.
