tuille

1. NOUN

/ tuˈiːl / (AmE), / tjuˈiːl / (BrE)
A plate of armor that covers and protects the hip and thigh, used especially in medieval times.
armor plate protecting the hip and thigh

Examples

The knight's tuille was dented but still offered excellent protection during the joust.
Museums often display suits of armor complete with tuilles to demonstrate their full defensive design.
Artisans meticulously restored the tuille to match the original craftsmanship of the medieval armor.

Etymology

From French *tuille* (tile), referring to the tile-like plates.
