contumely

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːn.tʃəˌmi, ˈkɑːn.tjuː.mi / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.tjʊˌmɪli / (BrE)
insulting language or treatment; rude or contemptuous expression intended to offend or hurt
insulting or rude language

Examples

The politician responded to the contumely of his critics with calm composure.
She endured years of contumely from her harsh employer.
His speech was met with silence rather than the expected contumely.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insult - a disrespectful or scornful remark or action
  • Antonym: respect - politeness, consideration, or honor shown to someone or something

Etymology

From Latin contumelia (insult, affront).
