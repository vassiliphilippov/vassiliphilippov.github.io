ointment

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔɪntmənt / (AmE), / ˈɔɪnt.mənt / (BrE)
A smooth, thick substance applied to the skin for healing, soothing, or moisturizing purposes.
medicated or soothing skin preparation

Examples

The doctor recommended applying the ointment twice a day to help heal the cut.
She uses a soothing ointment to treat her dry skin during winter.
After the insect bite, he gently rubbed some ointment on the irritated area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salve - A medical ointment for healing or protection
  • Synonym: cream - A thick liquid cosmetic or medicinal preparation

Etymology

From Old French oignement, from Latin unguentum (ointment, salve).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔɪntmənt / (AmE), / ˈɔɪntmənt / (BrE)
a smooth, oily substance that is rubbed on the skin to heal a wound or sore, or to soothe irritation, often containing medicine
medicinal cream or salve for the skin

Examples

She applied the ointment to her ankle to reduce the swelling.
The doctor prescribed an antibiotic ointment for the wound.
Using a soothing ointment helped relieve the itching and redness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salve - an oily substance for healing or soothing the skin
  • Synonym: cream - a thick liquid or semi-solid cosmetic or medicinal preparation applied to the skin
  • Synonym: lotion - a liquid preparation for the skin, less thick than an ointment

Etymology

From Old French oignement, from Latin unguentum (ointment, salve), from unguere (to anoint).
