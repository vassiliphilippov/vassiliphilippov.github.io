sulfide

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌlfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈsʌlfaɪd / (BrE)
a chemical compound consisting of sulfur combined with another, more electropositive element
compound of sulfur with another element

Examples

Iron reacts with sulfur to form sulfide compounds.
Many minerals contain metal sulfide as an important component.
Hydrogen sulfide is well known for its rotten egg smell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sulphide - British spelling of 'sulfide'

Etymology

From French *sulfure* and Latin *sulphur* (sulfur), with the -ide suffix for chemical compounds.
