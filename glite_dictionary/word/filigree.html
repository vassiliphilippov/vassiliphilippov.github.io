filigree

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪlɪˌɡri / (AmE), / ˈfɪlɪɡriː / (BrE)
delicate and intricate ornamental work made from fine twisted wire, typically of gold or silver.
delicate wire ornamental work

Examples

The antique ring was decorated with beautiful filigree around the central ruby.
The artist used silver filigree to craft an intricate pair of earrings.
A delicate filigree adorned the lid of the ornate jewelry box.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arabesque - an ornamental design with intertwined flowing lines
  • Synonym: embossing - decoration with raised patterns

Etymology

From Italian 'filigrana', from Latin 'filum' (thread) + 'granum' (grain).

2. VERB

/ ˈfɪlɪˌɡri / (AmE), / ˈfɪlɪɡriː / (BrE)
to decorate or form with intricate ornamental work made of fine wire, especially of gold or silver
to decorate with fine wire work

Examples

The artisan carefully filigreed the silver brooch with delicate swirls and twists.
She loves to filigree small gold wires into intricate shapes for her jewelry designs.
As the master jeweler filigreeing the ring, he focused on every tiny detail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to embellish - to beautify by adding decorative details or features
  • Antonym: plain - without ornament; simple, undecorated

Etymology

From Italian 'filigrana', from Latin 'filum' (thread) and 'granum' (grain).
