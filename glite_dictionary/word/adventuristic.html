adventuristic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæd.vən.tʃəˈrɪs.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌæd.vɛn.tjʊˈrɪs.tɪk / (BrE)
characterized by or relating to adventurous or bold risk-taking, especially in politics or policy.
involving bold or risky actions

Examples

The government's adventuristic foreign policy led to international tensions.
Critics accused the leader of taking an adventuristic approach to economic reform.
Some argue that adventuristic actions can sometimes yield unexpected successes.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: adventurous - eager to take risks or try out new things

Etymology

From 'adventurism' + '-istic', modeled after 'adventurous'.
