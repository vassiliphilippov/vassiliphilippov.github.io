Cygnus atratus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪɡ.nəs əˈtreɪ.təs / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡ.nəs əˈtreɪ.təs / (BrE)
a species of large Australian swan with black plumage and a red bill
large black swan of Australia

Examples

The Cygnus atratus is native to Australia and is easily recognized by its striking black feathers.
Researchers observed a flock of Cygnus atratus near the wetlands during their migration season.
Compared to other swans, Cygnus atratus has a distinctive red bill and black plumage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black swan - large Australian swan with black feathers and a red bill

Etymology

From Latin: 'Cygnus' meaning 'swan'; 'atratus' meaning 'dressed in black'.
