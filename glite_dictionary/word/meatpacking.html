meatpacking

1. NOUN

/ ˈmiːtpækɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmiːtpakɪŋ / (BrE)
the commercial processing, packaging, and distribution of meat, including slaughtering animals for sale to retailers
processing and packaging of meat for sale

Examples

The city became famous for its meatpacking industry in the early twentieth century.
Modern meatpacking facilities have strict hygiene regulations to ensure food safety.
Thousands of workers are employed in the nation’s meatpacking plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butchery - the process of slaughtering and selling meat, often on a smaller scale
  • Synonym: slaughterhouse - place where animals are killed for meat
