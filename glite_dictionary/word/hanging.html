hanging

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæŋɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhæŋɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of suspending something from above so that it moves freely or is displayed
the act of suspending something

Examples

The hanging of the new chandelier transformed the dining room's appearance.
At the museum, there will be a hanging of contemporary art pieces next week.
They attended the hanging of the tapestry in the main hall.

Synset Examples

there was a small ceremony for the hanging of the portrait

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suspension - the act of hanging something from above

2. NOUN

/ ˈhæŋɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhæŋɪŋ / (BrE)
a decorative piece of fabric, such as a tapestry or curtain, that is hung on a wall or over a window
decorative fabric hung on walls or windows

Examples

He admired the medieval hangings that decorated the castle walls.
Richly colored hangings covered every window in the grand hall.
The museum displayed rare silk hangings from the eighteenth century.

Synset Examples

the cold castle walls were covered with hangings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tapestry - a thick textile fabric with pictures or designs used as a wall hanging or furniture covering
  • Synonym: curtain - a piece of material hung to cover a window or doorway

Etymology

From the verb 'hang' + -ing; use as a noun dates from the 16th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈhæŋɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhæŋɪŋ / (BrE)
a method of execution in which the condemned person is suspended by the neck from a gallows or similar structure until death occurs, typically as a form of capital punishment
execution by suspension from the neck

Examples

Public hanging was once considered a spectacle in many societies.
He was sentenced to death by hanging for his crimes.
The abolition of hanging marked a significant change in the country's legal system.

Synset Examples

in those days the hanging of criminals was a public entertainment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: execution - the carrying out of a death sentence
  • Synonym: beheading - execution by decapitation

Etymology

From Old English *hangian* (to hang), related to *hang* (verb).
