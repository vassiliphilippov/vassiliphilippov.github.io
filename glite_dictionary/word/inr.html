INR

1. PROPN

/ ˌaɪ ˌɛn ˈɑr / (AmE), / ˌaɪ ˌen ˈɑː / (BrE)
an agency within the U.S. Department of State responsible for providing interpretive analyses of global developments and serving as a focal point for policy issues within the Intelligence Community
U.S. State Department intelligence agency

Examples

The INR prepares daily intelligence briefs for State Department officials.
She worked in the INR for ten years, focusing on European affairs.
Collaboration between the CIA and INR is crucial for comprehensive policy analysis.

Etymology

Initialism of Bureau of Intelligence and Research.
