Mustelus

1. NOUN

/ mʌˈstɛləs / (AmE), / mʌˈstiːləs / (BrE)
A genus of small, mainly benthic sharks known as smooth dogfishes, found in coastal waters worldwide.
genus of smooth dogfishes (sharks)

Examples

The Mustelus are often called smooth dogfishes due to their sleek skin.
Scientists discovered a new species within the genus Mustelus off the coast of South America.
Unlike some sharks, Mustelus species pose little threat to humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dogfish - common name for several small sharks, including those of genus Mustelus

Etymology

From Latin 'mustela' meaning 'weasel', used in zoological Latin for the resemblance in shape.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmʌstɪləs / (AmE), / ˈmʌstɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of sharks commonly known as smooth dogfishes, found in temperate and tropical seas.
a genus of smooth dogfishes (sharks)

Examples

Several species within the genus Mustelus are widely studied by marine biologists.
The Mustelus sharks are better known as smooth dogfishes due to their skin texture.
Commercial fisheries often catch members of the genus Mustelus along temperate coastlines.

Etymology

From Latin 'mustela' (weasel), referencing the body's shape.
