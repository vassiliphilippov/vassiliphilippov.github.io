bonfire

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑnˌfaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈbɒnˌfaɪə / (BrE)
a large outdoor fire made for celebration, as a signal, or for burning things
large outdoor fire for celebration or signaling

Examples

The community gathered around the bonfire to celebrate the holiday.
They built a huge bonfire on the beach for the summer festival.
Smoke rose into the night sky from the bonfire at the center of the field.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: campfire - a small outdoor fire used for warmth or cooking
  • Synonym: beacon - a fire or light set up as a warning or signal

Etymology

From Middle English 'bonefire' (fire of bones), later generalized.
