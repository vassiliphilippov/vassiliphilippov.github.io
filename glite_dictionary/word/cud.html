cud

1. NOUN

/ kʌd / (AmE), / kʌd / (BrE)
partly digested food returned from the stomach to the mouth of a ruminant animal to be chewed again
regurgitated food chewed by ruminants

Examples

The cow chewed its cud lazily in the afternoon sun.
Farmers watch to ensure their cattle are bringing up cud as a sign of good health.
After grazing, sheep often rest and chew their cud in groups.

Etymology

Old English 'cudu', related to chewing.

2. NOUN

/ kʌd / (AmE), / kʌd / (BrE)
a wad of something chewable, such as tobacco, that is held in the mouth and chewed repeatedly
something chewable held in the mouth

Examples

He kept a cud of tobacco tucked in his cheek while he worked.
The old man spat out his cud before entering the house.
Chewing on a cud of mint leaves helped freshen her breath.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plug - a mass of tobacco for chewing
