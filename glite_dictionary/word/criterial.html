criterial

1. ADJ

/ kraɪˈtɪəriəl / (AmE), / kraɪˈtɪəriəl / (BrE)
serving as a basis for evaluation or judgment; establishing a criterion.
serving as a basis for evaluation

Examples

The professor outlined the criterial features that every essay must include.
Attention to criterial aspects of the diagnosis can improve accuracy.
Language tests use criterial items to determine proficiency levels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: criterional - relating to or serving as a criterion

Etymology

From 'criterion' + '-al', formed on the pattern of similar adjectives.
