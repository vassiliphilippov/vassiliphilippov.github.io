intumescency

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.tjuˈmɛs.ən.si / (AmE), / ˌɪn.tjʊˈmɛs.ən.si / (BrE)
The increase in volume of certain substances when they are heated, often accompanied by the release of water.
expansion of substances when heated

Examples

The fireproof coating relies on intumescency to swell and protect the steel structure beneath.
New materials with improved intumescency are being tested in construction for enhanced safety.
The effect of intumescency in certain clays can cause cracking in ceramic tiles during firing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intumescence - the process of swelling or expanding, especially when heated

Etymology

From Latin intumescere (to swell up).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.tuˈmɛs.ən.si / (AmE), / ˌɪn.tjuˈmɛs.ən.si / (BrE)
The swelling of a body part caused by the accumulation of blood or other fluids, especially as a result of congestion.
swelling caused by fluid or blood accumulation
medical

Examples

The presence of intumescency in the nasal membranes was noted during the medical examination.
Physicians treated the patient's abdominal intumescency caused by fluid retention.
Chronic congestion can result in persistent intumescency of the affected tissues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swelling - the condition of being larger or rounder than normal due to accumulation of fluid
  • Synonym: edema - an excessive accumulation of fluid in body tissues causing swelling

Etymology

From Latin 'intumescere' (to swell up), from 'in-' (in, into) + 'tumescere' (to begin to swell).
