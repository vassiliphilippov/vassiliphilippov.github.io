amphitheater

1. NOUN

/ ˈæmfɪˌθiːətər / (AmE), / ˈæmfɪˌθɪətə / (BrE)
A sloping gallery with seats arranged in tiers for spectators, often surrounding a central performance area, as in a theater or an operating room.
sloping, tiered gallery for spectators

Examples

The medical students watched the operation from the amphitheater above the surgery room.
The ancient Roman amphitheater could seat thousands of spectators at once.
During the play, the audience filled every seat in the modern amphitheater.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arena - a central area for performances or events, usually surrounded by seats

Etymology

From Greek amphitheatron, from amphi- 'on both sides' + theatron 'theatre'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæmfɪˌθiətɚ / (AmE), / ˈamfɪˌθɪətə / (BrE)
a large, round or oval building with tiers of seats around a central open area, used for sports, performances, or public events
oval or round stadium with tiered seating

Examples

The ancient Romans built many impressive amphitheaters for gladiator games and public spectacles.
We attended a concert under the stars at the outdoor amphitheater last summer.
The new amphitheater in the park hosts theater performances and music festivals throughout the year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arena - a central area surrounded by seats for sports or performances

Etymology

From Latin amphitheatrum, from Greek amphitheatron (theater with seats on all sides)
