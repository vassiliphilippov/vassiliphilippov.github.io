explorer

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈsplɔrɚ / (AmE), / ɪkˈsplɔːrə / (BrE)
a person who travels to unfamiliar or little known regions, especially for research or discovery
person who explores unknown places

Examples

The famous explorer mapped large areas of the Amazon rainforest.
As a child, she dreamed of becoming an explorer and discovering new lands.
Many early explorers risked their lives to expand our knowledge of the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adventurer - person who seeks adventure, often by taking risks
  • Antonym: settler - person who moves to a new area to live but not primarily to explore it

Etymology

From Latin 'explorator', meaning 'investigator, surveyor'.
