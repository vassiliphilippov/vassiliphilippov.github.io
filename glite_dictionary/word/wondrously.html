wondrously

1. ADV

/ ˈwʌndrəsli / (AmE), / ˈwʌndrəsli / (BrE)
In a way that excites wonder or admiration; excellently or remarkably.
in a way that inspires wonder

Examples

The garden was wondrously beautiful after the fresh morning rain.
The magician wondrously transformed the simple hat into a flutter of doves.
She wondrously described the landscape, captivating everyone in the room.

Synset Examples

her voice is superbly disciplined
the colors changed wondrously slowly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marvelously - in a wonderful or astonishing manner
  • Synonym: wonderfully - in a way that is extremely good or pleasing

Etymology

From 'wondrous' (from Middle English 'wondres', meaning 'wonderful') + '-ly' (forming adverbs).
