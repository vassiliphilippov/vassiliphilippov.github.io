Chlorococcum

1. NOUN

/ klɔːroʊˈkɒkəm / (AmE), / klɔːrəˈkɒkəm / (BrE)
A genus of unicellular green algae that occur singly or in layers on soil or damp rock.
genus of unicellular green algae

Examples

Scientists discovered a layer of Chlorococcum growing on the surface of old stone walls.
Chlorococcum is often found in moist soil where sunlight is abundant.
Recent studies have shown that Chlorococcum can survive in a range of environmental conditions.

Etymology

From Greek 'chlōros' (green) and 'kokkos' (grain, berry), referring to its green, single-celled structure.

2. NOUN

/ ˌklɔrəˈkɑkəm / (AmE), / ˌklɒrəˈkɒkəm / (BrE)
A genus of unicellular green algae found singly or forming a layer on soil or damp rock, serving as the type genus of the order Chlorococcales.
genus of unicellular green algae

Examples

The scientist identified the green growth as Chlorococcum covering the damp stone.
Samples from the pond revealed colonies of Chlorococcum among other algae.
Chlorococcum is studied for its potential in biofuel production.

Etymology

From Greek 'chloros' (green) and 'kokkos' (grain or berry), referring to the appearance of the algae.
