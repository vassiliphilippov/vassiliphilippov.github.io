Thomas Robert Malthus

1. PROPN

/ ˈtɑməs ˈrɑbərt ˈmælθəs / (AmE), / ˈtɒməs ˈrɒbət ˈmælθəs / (BrE)
(1766–1834) An English economist and demographer known for his theory that population growth would eventually outpace food supply, leading to societal strain.
English economist and demographer (1766–1834)

Examples

Thomas Robert Malthus is best known for his influential essay on the principle of population.
Many modern economists still reference Thomas Robert Malthus when discussing population growth and resources.
The theories of Thomas Robert Malthus had a lasting impact on economic and social policy.
