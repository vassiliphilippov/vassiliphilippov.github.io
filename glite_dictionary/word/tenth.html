tenth

1. NOUN

/ tɛnθ / (AmE), / tɛnθ / (BrE)
the position in a sequence that comes after the ninth and before the eleventh; number ten in a series
position ten in a sequence

Examples

She finished in tenth place in the race and was pleased with her effort.
This is the tenth time you've asked that question today.
On the tenth of every month, we hold a staff meeting.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: ninth - position nine in a sequence
  • Degree Higher: eleventh - position eleven in a sequence

Etymology

From Middle English 'tenthe', from Old English 'teogotha', related to 'ten'.

2. ADJ

/ tɛnθ / (AmE), / tɛnθ / (BrE)
Coming after the ninth and before the eleventh in position; being number ten in a sequence.
ordinal number: position ten

Examples

This is the tenth time I’ve tried to call her today.
He finished in tenth place in the marathon.
Their anniversary party was held on the tenth of October.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: ninth - ordinal number: position nine
  • Degree Higher: eleventh - ordinal number: position eleven

Etymology

From Old English teoþa (tenth), related to ten.

3. NOUN

/ tɛnθ / (AmE), / tɛnθ / (BrE)
one part in ten equal parts; 1/10 of a whole
one part in ten equal parts

Examples

She divided the cake into tenths so everyone could get an equal share.
A tenth of the money was donated to charity.
You must pay a tenth of your earnings as tax.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: one tenth - the same as 'tenth', a fractional part; 1/10
  • Degree Higher: quarter - one fourth; 1/4 of a whole

Etymology

Formed from "ten" + the ordinal suffix "-th"; pattern similar to "fourth," "fifth," etc.
