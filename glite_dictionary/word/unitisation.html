unitisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuːnɪtəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪtaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of converting an investment trust into a unit investment trust
conversion of an investment trust

Examples

The unitisation of the fund allowed investors greater flexibility in managing their holdings.
Following unitisation, the trust could be traded more easily on the stock exchange.
Financial advisers discussed the potential benefits of unitisation for long-term investors.

Etymology

From 'unit' + 'isation', modelled after 'unitise' and the suffix '-ation'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌjuː.nə.təˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌjuː.nɪ.taɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the joint development and operation of a petroleum resource that extends across territory controlled by different companies or entities, usually by agreement to maximize efficiency and recovery
joint development of shared petroleum resource

Examples

The government mandated unitisation of the oilfield to ensure efficient resource extraction.
Disputes over revenue sharing arose after the unitisation agreement was signed between the companies.
International guidelines were developed to manage unitisation of offshore petroleum resources.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unitization - the American spelling of 'unitisation'

Etymology

From 'unit' + '-isation', modelled on 'unitization' (US spelling), relating to the process of forming a single unit for development.

3. NOUN

the process of packaging cargo into standardized unit loads for easier handling and transportation
packaging cargo into unit loads

Examples

The unitisation of products has significantly reduced loading times at the port.
Advancements in unitisation have led to more efficient supply chain operations.
Proper unitisation of cargo helps minimize the risk of damage during transit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unitization - the American spelling of unitisation

Etymology

From 'unit' + 'isation', influenced by Latin 'unītās' (oneness) and the suffix '-isation', meaning process or result.

4. NOUN

/ ˌjuː.nɪ.təˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌjuː.nɪ.taɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
(psychology) the process of combining smaller units of information into larger, coordinated units to aid memory or understanding.
combining smaller units into larger ones (psychology)

Examples

Memory performance improved after the students practiced unitisation of the information.
Effective unitisation allows people to process complex information more efficiently.
In psychology, unitisation helps explain how we remember phone numbers as single chunks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chunking - grouping pieces of information for easier processing

Etymology

From unit + -isation, patterned after 'unitize', from 'unit' (single entity) and the suffix '-ize' (to make).
