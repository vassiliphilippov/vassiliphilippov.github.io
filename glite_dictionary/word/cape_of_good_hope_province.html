Cape of Good Hope Province

1. PROPN

/ ˌkeɪp əv ɡʊd ˈhoʊp ˈprɑvɪns / (AmE), / ˌkeɪp əv ɡʊd ˈhəʊp ˈprɒvɪns / (BrE)
a former province in southern South Africa, established by Dutch settlers in 1652, ceded to Great Britain in 1814, and divided into three new provinces in 1994
former province of South Africa

Examples

The Cape of Good Hope Province played a significant role in South Africa's colonial history.
After 1994, the Cape of Good Hope Province was divided into Western Cape, Eastern Cape, and Northern Cape.
She learned about the Cape of Good Hope Province during her studies of South African geography.
