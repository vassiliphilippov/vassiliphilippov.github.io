John Endecott

1. PROPN

/ dʒɑn ˈɛndɪˌkɑt / (AmE), / dʒɒn ˈɛndɪˌkɒt / (BrE)
(1588–1665) An English colonial administrator who was a founder and several times governor of the Massachusetts Bay Colony.
English colonial governor in Massachusetts

Examples

John Endecott was known for his strict leadership in early colonial Massachusetts.
The legacy of John Endecott remains significant in New England history.
Many documents from the seventeenth century bear the signature of John Endecott.
