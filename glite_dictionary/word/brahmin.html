Brahmin

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɑːmɪn / (AmE), / ˈbrɑːmɪn / (BrE)
a breed of large, greyish humped cattle originally from India, developed in the United States for resistance to heat and ticks, and commonly used for crossbreeding
a breed of heat-resistant humped cattle

Examples

The Texas rancher raised a herd of Brahmin cattle for their resilience in hot weather.
Brahmin cattle are noted for their distinctive humps and loose skin.
Farmers often use Brahmin bulls for crossbreeding with local cows to improve their stock.

Etymology

From Sanskrit *brāhmaṇa*, the Hindu priestly caste, reflecting the breed's Indian origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbrɑːmɪn / (AmE), / ˈbrɑːmɪn / (BrE)
a member of the highest caste in the traditional Hindu social hierarchy, historically priests and scholars
member of highest Hindu caste

Examples

He was born into a respected Brahmin family in South India.
Historically, the role of a Brahmin was closely linked to religious duties and scholarship.
Many Brahmins today pursue a variety of professions beyond their traditional roles.

Synset Examples

originally all brahmans were priests

Related Terms

  • Synonym: priest - a person who performs religious ceremonies in Hinduism

Etymology

From Sanskrit brāhmaṇa, meaning 'pertaining to Brahma' or 'priest.'
