Lophophora williamsii

1. NOUN

/ loʊˌfɑːfərə wɪlˈjæmsiaɪ / (AmE), / ləʊˌfɒfərə wɪlˈjæmsiaɪ / (BrE)
A small, spineless, globe-shaped cactus native to Mexico and southwestern United States, known as the source of peyote (mescal buttons).
species of spineless, globe-shaped cactus (peyote)

Examples

Lophophora williamsii is well known for its psychoactive properties, particularly in indigenous ceremonies.
Botanists have studied Lophophora williamsii to understand its ecological role in desert environments.
Growing Lophophora williamsii is regulated in many countries due to its use in producing mescal buttons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peyote - common name for Lophophora williamsii, a psychoactive cactus

Etymology

From Greek *lophos* (crest) and *phoros* (bearing), referring to physical features; the species name honors botanist J.A. Williams.
