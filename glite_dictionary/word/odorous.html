odorous

1. ADJ

/ ˈoʊdərəs / (AmE), / ˈəʊdərəs / (BrE)
having or giving off a distinctive or pleasant smell; fragrant.
having a noticeable or pleasant smell

Examples

The odorous blossoms filled the garden with a delightful scent each evening.
She opened the bag to find it filled with odorous herbs from the countryside.
Walking through the odorous market stalls, he noticed the fragrance of fresh spices and fruits.

Synset Examples

odoriferous spices
the odorous air of the orchard
the perfumed air of June
scented flowers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fragrant - having a pleasant or sweet smell
  • Antonym: odorless - having no smell

Etymology

From Latin 'odorosus' (fragrant, having a smell).

2. ADJ

/ ˈoʊ.dɚ.əs / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.dər.əs / (BrE)
having a noticeable or distinctive smell, especially one that is strong or unusual
having a noticeable smell

Examples

The odorous blossoms filled the room with their strong perfume.
After sitting out all night, the trash became quite odorous.
He remembered the odorous aroma of baked bread from his childhood.

Synset Examples

odorous jasmine flowers
odorous garbage
fresh odorous bread

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: fragrant - having a pleasant or sweet smell
  • Antonym: odorless - having no smell
  • Synonym: smelly - having a strong or unpleasant smell

Etymology

From Latin 'odorosus' (fragrant), from 'odor' (smell).

3. ADJ

/ ˈoʊdərəs / (AmE), / ˈəʊdərəs / (BrE)
having or emitting a noticeable smell, often one that is strong or distinctive
emitting a noticeable smell

Examples

The odorous kitchen trash needed to be taken out immediately.
She opened the box to find some odorous cheeses inside.
An odorous mist rose from the damp earth after the rain.

Synset Examples

odorous salt pork and weevily hardtack

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: fragrant - having a pleasant and sweet smell
  • Antonym: odorless - having no smell

Etymology

From Latin 'odorosus' (fragrant, odorous), from 'odor' (smell).
