niche

1. NOUN

/ nɪtʃ / (AmE), / niːʃ / (BrE)
A shallow recess, especially one in a wall to display a statue or other ornament.
shallow recess in a wall

Examples

A small marble statue stood in a niche in the corridor.
Shelves and a niche in the wall provided extra storage in the bathroom.
Ancient temples often featured a niche for a sacred image.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recess - a hollow space or indentation in a surface

Etymology

From French *niche*, from Italian *nicchia* (niche, nook), of uncertain origin.

2. NOUN

/ nɪtʃ, niːʃ / (AmE), / niːʃ, nɪtʃ / (BrE)
a shallow recess or hollow, especially in a wall, designed to display a statue, vase, or other object
recessed enclosure in a wall

Examples

A marble statue stood in a niche carved into the ancient temple wall.
She arranged fresh flowers in the niche above the fireplace.
The hallway featured a small niche perfect for displaying art.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alcove - a recess in a room, traditionally used for beds, bookshelves, or other features

Etymology

From French *niche*, from Italian *nicchia* (niche, recess), related to *nicchiare* (to make a nest).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04069081-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ nɪtʃ, niːʃ / (AmE), / niːʃ, nɪtʃ / (BrE)
(Ecology) The role or position of an organism or species within its environment and community, especially in relation to survival and reproduction.
ecological role of a species

Examples

Every species occupies a unique niche in the ecosystem.
The extinction of one animal can open up a niche for other organisms to fill.
Invasive species often disrupt the natural niche structure of local wildlife.

Etymology

From French 'niche', from Old French, from 'nicher' (to nest), from Latin 'nidus' (nest).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ niːʃ, nɪtʃ / (AmE), / niːʃ, nɪtʃ / (BrE)
a job, activity, or position that is especially suitable for someone and in which they can be successful or comfortable
suitable position or role

Examples

After years of searching, he finally found his niche as a graphic designer.
Many companies try to target a specific niche in the market to stand out.
She carved out a niche for herself in the world of independent filmmaking.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

From French 'niche' (recess), from Italian 'nicchia' (niche, recess in a wall).
