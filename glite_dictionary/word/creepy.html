creepy

1. ADJ

/ ˈkriːpi / (AmE), / ˈkriːpi / (BrE)
Annoying and unpleasant, often causing discomfort or unease.
annoying and unpleasant

Examples

She avoided the creepy man at the party because he made her uncomfortable.
The creepy atmosphere in the office made everyone want to leave early.
It was difficult to ignore the creepy feeling she got being around her new neighbor.

Synset Examples

some creepy kids were bothering her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unpleasant - not enjoyable, causing discomfort
  • Synonym: disturbing - causing worry or upset, unsettling
  • Antonym: appealing - pleasant or attractive

Etymology

From 'creep' + -y, originally meaning causing a creeping or shivering feeling.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkripi / (AmE), / ˈkriːpi / (BrE)
Making you feel nervous, frightened, or as if something unpleasant is crawling on your skin.
causing unease or a crawling sensation

Examples

That old abandoned house always gives me a creepy feeling when I walk past it at night.
She told a creepy story that made everyone shiver.
A creepy sensation ran down his spine as he entered the dark room.

Synset Examples

a creepy story
I had a creepy-crawly feeling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spooky - strangely frightening; ghostly
  • Synonym: eerie - strange and mysterious in an unsettling way
  • Antonym: comforting - making you feel less worried; reassuring

Etymology

From the verb 'creep' (to move slowly and quietly), with the adjective suffix '-y'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00196783-s
Synset POS: s
