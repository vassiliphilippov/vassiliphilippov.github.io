Euryale

1. NOUN

/ ˈjʊəriˌeɪli / (AmE), / ˈjʊərɪˌɑːli / (BrE)
A genus of basket stars, marine echinoderms characterized by their branched, flexible arms.
genus of basket stars (echinoderms)

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species within the Euryale genus in deep sea expeditions.
The delicate arms of Euryale allow it to catch plankton drifting in the current.
Euryale are distinguished from other brittle stars by their extensively branched arms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basket star - a type of brittle star with highly branched arms

Etymology

From Greek *Euryalē*, one of the Gorgons in mythology; used in scientific Latin as a genus name.

2. PROPN

/ jʊˈraɪ.əli / (AmE), / jʊˈraɪ.əli / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the second eldest of the three Gorgons, sisters to Medusa and Stheno.
second eldest Gorgon in Greek mythology

Examples

The myth describes Euryale and her sisters as immortal beings with deadly powers.
In ancient art, Euryale is often depicted alongside Medusa and Stheno.
Few myths focus solely on Euryale, but she plays a key role in the Gorgons' legend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Medusa - one of the three Gorgons, known for her snake-covered hair
  • Synonym: Stheno - eldest of the three Gorgons in Greek mythology

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Εὐρύαλη (Eurúalē), meaning 'broad-stepping' or 'wide sea.'
