sidewalk

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪdˌwɑk / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdwɔːk / (BrE)
A paved path for pedestrians, typically located alongside a street or roadway.
paved path beside a street for walking
american

Examples

Children were playing on the sidewalk outside their house.
The city cleared snow from every sidewalk after the storm.
Please stay on the sidewalk and do not walk in the street.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pavement - paved path beside a road; chiefly British English
  • Antonym: roadway - part of a road intended for vehicles

Etymology

Formed from 'side' and 'walk', first appeared in American English in the 18th century.
