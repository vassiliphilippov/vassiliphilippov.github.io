palatial

1. ADJ

/ pəˈleɪʃəl / (AmE), / pəˈleɪʃəl / (BrE)
(of a building or room) resembling a palace in being spacious and splendid.
resembling a palace; spacious and grand

Examples

They hosted a gala dinner in their palatial home.
The hotel offers palatial suites with stunning city views.
Despite its palatial appearance, the mansion was surprisingly affordable.

Synset Examples

the palatial residence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: luxurious - extremely comfortable or elegant, especially in a way that involves great expense
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance; (of place) simple or modest

Etymology

From Latin palatium (palace), via French palatial.

2. ADJ

/ pəˈleɪʃəl / (AmE), / pəˈleɪʃəl / (BrE)
Resembling a palace in being spacious and splendid; suitable for or like a palace.
resembling or suitable for a palace

Examples

They hosted the event in a palatial mansion overlooking the city.
The hotel lobby was so palatial that guests were awestruck on arrival.
Her palatial office was furnished with antique desks and crystal chandeliers.

Synset Examples

palatial furnishings
a palatial yacht

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnificent - impressively beautiful, elaborate, or extravagant
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's own importance; also, a modest dwelling

Etymology

From Latin palātium (palace), via French palatial.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01289097-s
Synset POS: s
