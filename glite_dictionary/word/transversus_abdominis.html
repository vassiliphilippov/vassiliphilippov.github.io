transversus abdominis

1. NOUN

/ trænzˈvɜrsəs æbˈdɑmɪnɪs / (AmE), / trænzˈvɜːsəs æbˈdɒmɪnɪs / (BrE)
A flat muscle with transverse fibers that forms the anterior and lateral walls of the abdominal cavity.
muscle in the abdominal wall
medical

Examples

Strengthening the transversus abdominis can help improve core stability and reduce back pain.
The transversus abdominis is engaged during many Pilates exercises for better support of the spine.
During deep breathing, the transversus abdominis contracts to help compress the abdominal contents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rectus abdominis - a long, flat muscle extending along the front of the abdomen

Etymology

From Latin 'transversus' (turned across) and 'abdominis' (of the abdomen).
