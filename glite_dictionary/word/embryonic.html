embryonic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛm.briˈɑ.nɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛm.brɪˈɒn.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to an embryo or the earliest stage of development before birth or hatching.
relating to embryos or early development

Examples

The scientist studied cells in their embryonic form to understand early development.
Her business idea was still at an embryonic stage, but showed great potential.
Researchers examined the embryonic tissue to detect genetic abnormalities.

Synset Examples

in the embryonic stage
embryologic development

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: fetal - relating to a fetus; later stage of development before birth

Etymology

From Greek embryon (embryo) + -ic (forming adjectives).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɛm.briˈɑ.nɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛm.brɪˈɒn.ɪk / (BrE)
In an early or undeveloped stage; just beginning to develop.
in an early stage of development

Examples

The embryonic project showed great promise, but there was still a long way to go.
Her embryonic understanding of the language improved rapidly with daily practice.
The company’s embryonic website barely offered more than a homepage at launch.

Synset Examples

the embryonic government staffed by survivors of the massacre
an embryonic nation, not yet self-governing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incipient - beginning to exist or appear
  • Synonym: nascent - just coming into existence and beginning to display signs of future potential
  • Antonym: mature - fully developed; grown to full potential

Etymology

From Greek embryon (fetus, unborn offspring).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00822150-s
Synset POS: s
