to stargaze

1. VERB

/ ˈstɑrˌɡeɪz / (AmE), / ˈstɑːˌɡeɪz / (BrE)
to indulge in daydreams or fantasies rather than paying attention to reality
to indulge in daydreams or fantasies

Examples

During boring meetings, Tom often stargazes about traveling the world.
She tends to stargaze instead of focusing on her studies.
If you stargaze all day, you'll miss what's happening around you.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daydream - to indulge in pleasant thoughts that distract from the present
  • Synonym: to fantasize - to imagine things, especially things that are unlikely or impossible

Etymology

From 'star' + 'gaze', originally referring to looking at stars, later extended figuratively.

2. VERB

/ ˈstɑrˌɡeɪz / (AmE), / ˈstɑːɡeɪz / (BrE)
to look at and study the stars in the night sky, often for enjoyment or scientific interest
to observe stars in the sky

Examples

We lay on the grass to stargaze after the sun went down.
Every summer, he would stargaze from the roof with his telescope.
Last night, we stargazed for hours and spotted several constellations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: starwatch - to observe stars, usually with intent

Etymology

From 'star' + 'gaze', first recorded in the 18th century.
