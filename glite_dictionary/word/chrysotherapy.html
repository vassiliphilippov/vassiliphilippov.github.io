chrysotherapy

1. NOUN

/ ˌkraɪsoʊˈθɛrəpi / (AmE), / ˌkrɪsəʊˈθɛrəpi / (BrE)
the use of gold-containing compounds in the treatment of certain diseases, especially rheumatoid arthritis
treatment with gold compounds
medical

Examples

Chrysotherapy is often considered when other treatments for rheumatoid arthritis have failed.
Doctors may recommend chrysotherapy as a last resort for managing chronic inflammation.
The risks and benefits of chrysotherapy should be carefully discussed with a rheumatologist.

Etymology

From Greek khrȳsós (gold) and therapy (treatment).
