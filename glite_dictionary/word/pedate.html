pedate

1. ADJ

/ ˈpiː.deɪt / (AmE), / ˈpiː.deɪt / (BrE)
(Botany) of a leaf, having radiating lobes, each of which is deeply cleft or divided, resembling a foot with toes.
leaf shape with radiating, deeply divided lobes

Examples

The plant has pedate leaves, each lobe spreading out like the toes of a foot.
Botanists easily distinguished the species by its unusual pedate leaf pattern.
The pedate structure of the foliage is characteristic of several woodland wildflowers.

Etymology

From Latin pedatus (footed), from pes (foot).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɛˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˈpiː.deɪt / (BrE)
having or resembling a foot; especially (in zoology and botany) having limbs or structures shaped like or divided similar to a foot.
having or resembling a foot

Examples

The leaves of some ferns are pedate, spreading out like a foot with several lobes.
The zoologist described the creature's pedate appendages, which allowed it to move swiftly across the sand.
The fossil exhibited pedate imprints, suggesting the animal had foot-like limbs.

Etymology

From Latin 'pes, pedis' meaning 'foot', with the suffix '-ate' indicating possession.
