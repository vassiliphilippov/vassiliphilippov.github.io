flavour

1. NOUN

/ ˈfleɪvɚ / (AmE), / ˈfleɪvə / (BrE)
the distinctive quality or atmosphere of a place, event, or situation that affects how people experience it
distinctive atmosphere or quality
british

Examples

The festival had a unique flavour that attracted visitors from across the country.
The concert hall retained the historic flavour of the original theater.
The new leadership gave the company a completely different flavour.

Synset Examples

the feel of the city excited him
a clergyman improved the tone of the meeting
it had the smell of treason

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tone - the general character or attitude of a place, piece of writing, situation, etc.
  • Synonym: feel - the general impression or atmosphere of a place, situation, or piece of work
  • Synonym: atmosphere - the pervading tone or mood of a place, situation, or creative work

Etymology

From Old French 'flaveur', from Latin 'flavor' (to smell, have a scent).

2. VERB

/ ˈfleɪvɚ / (AmE), / ˈfleɪvə / (BrE)
To add or enhance the taste of something by using seasonings or other substances.
add or enhance taste
british

Examples

The chef expertly flavoured the soup with fresh herbs and garlic.
You can flavour the yogurt with honey or fruit to make it sweeter.
The bread was lightly flavoured with rosemary, giving it a distinct aroma.

Synset Examples

Season the chicken breast after roasting it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: season - to add salt, herbs, or spices to food to enhance its flavor
  • Synonym: spice - to add spices to food

Etymology

From Old French 'flaur', from Vulgar Latin *flāvor, from Latin 'flāvus' (yellow, golden).

3. NOUN

/ ˈfleɪvɚ / (AmE), / ˈfleɪvə / (BrE)
the distinctive taste of a food or drink perceived in the mouth
distinctive taste of food or drink
british

Examples

This soup has a rich, spicy flavour that lingers on the tongue.
Adding a pinch of salt will enhance the flavour of the dish.
Each cheese offers a unique flavour profile for tasting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flavor - American English spelling of 'flavour', meaning the distinctive taste of food or drink
  • Synonym: taste - the sense by which the flavor of food or drink is perceived

Etymology

From Old French 'flavor', from Latin 'flāvor' (yellow color), later influenced in meaning by association with taste.

/ ˈfleɪvɚ / (AmE), / ˈfleɪvə / (BrE)
(physics) Any of the six types of quark: up, down, charm, strange, top, and bottom.
type of quark in physics

Examples

The up quark is one flavour among the six known in particle physics.
Physicists study how the properties of each quark flavour differ.
The experiment aimed to identify the presence of a rare flavour of quark.

Etymology

From 'flavour', used figuratively in particle physics to denote a distinguishing property, modeled after the culinary sense; adopted in the 1960s.
