to antagonize

1. VERB

/ ænˈtæɡəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ænˈtæɡənaɪz / (BrE)
to cause someone to become hostile or unfriendly; to provoke or annoy someone so that they react angrily
provoke hostility or anger

Examples

She warned him not to antagonize the neighbors with loud music late at night.
His sarcastic comments only served to antagonize the other members of the team.
Trying to antagonize your boss could hurt your career prospects.

Synset Examples

Don't antagonize your boss

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to provoke - to stimulate a reaction, often anger or annoyance
  • Antonym: to appease - to pacify or placate someone by acceding to their demands

Etymology

From Greek antagonizesthai, meaning 'to struggle against'.

2. VERB

/ ænˈtæɡəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ænˈtæɡənaɪz / (BrE)
to act in a way that makes someone hostile or unfriendly; to oppose or provoke anger in someone
provoke hostility or opposition

Examples

His harsh criticism only served to antagonize his colleagues further.
The company did not want to antagonize its customers with higher prices.
If you keep interrupting her, you'll only antagonize her and make things worse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to provoke - stimulate or incite someone to feel or do something, especially anger
  • Antonym: to appease - to calm or pacify someone, often by acceding to their demands

Etymology

From Greek antagōnizesthai (to struggle against), from anti- (against) + agōnizesthai (to contend for a prize).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
