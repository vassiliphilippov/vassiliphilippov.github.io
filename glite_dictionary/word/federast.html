federast

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛdəˌræst / (AmE), / ˈfɛdəˌræst / (BrE)
A derogatory term, used especially to insult someone perceived as supporting federalism or a federation, often with a mocking or contemptuous intent.
derogatory term for a federalism supporter
offensive slang

Examples

The politician was derided as a federast by his critics.
Online forums sometimes use federast as a way to insult advocates of a strong federation.
He dismissed the proposal as just another plan from a bunch of federasts.

Synset Examples

The term federast was used mockingly in their discussion.

Etymology

Blending of 'federation' and suffix '-ast', modeled after 'pederast'; first appeared in late 20th century political slang.
