bregma

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɛɡmə / (AmE), / ˈbrɛɡmə / (BrE)
the anatomical point on the top of the skull where the sagittal and coronal sutures meet
cranium suture junction point
medical

Examples

The surgeon marked the bregma before beginning the procedure to access the brain.
In infants, the bregma is covered by the anterior fontanelle, which gradually closes with age.
Precise measurements from the bregma are crucial in neuroanatomical research.

Etymology

From Greek *brégma* (forehead, front of the head).
