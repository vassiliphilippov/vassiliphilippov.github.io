Limburger

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪmbɜːrɡər / (AmE), / ˈlɪmbɜːɡə / (BrE)
A soft, white cheese known for its very strong, pungent odor and flavor.
strong-smelling soft cheese

Examples

She wrinkled her nose at the smell of Limburger in the fridge.
The sandwich was made with rye bread and a thick slice of Limburger.
Many people either love or hate the pungent taste of Limburger cheese.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: cheese - a food made from the pressed curds of milk

Etymology

From German 'Limburger Käse', named after Limburg, a region in Belgium.
