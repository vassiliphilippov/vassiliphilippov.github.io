tranquilizing

1. ADJ

/ ˈtræŋkwəˌlaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtræŋkwɪˌlaɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
having a calming or soothing effect; tending to make someone less anxious or more relaxed.
having a calming or soothing effect

Examples

The tranquilizing music helped her fall asleep quickly after a stressful day.
Some herbal teas are known for their tranquilizing properties and are popular evening drinks.
Doctors prescribed a tranquilizing medication to help manage his anxiety symptoms.

Synset Examples

valium has a tranquilizing effect
took a hot drink with sedative properties before going to bed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sedative - tending to calm or soothe; promoting relaxation or sleep
  • Antonym: stimulating - making someone feel more active or alert; having an invigorating effect

Etymology

From the verb 'tranquilize', which derives from Latin 'tranquillus' (quiet, calm).
