peripteral

1. ADJ

/ pəˈrɪp.tə.rəl / (AmE), / pəˈrɪp.tər.əl / (BrE)
(of a building, especially a temple) having a single row of columns on all sides.
having columns on all sides (architecture)

Examples

The Parthenon is a classic example of a peripteral temple in ancient Greece.
Archaeologists uncovered the remains of a peripteral structure at the site, notable for its uniform colonnade.
Many early Doric temples adopted the peripteral layout, providing symmetry and grandeur.

Etymology

From Greek peripteros, from peri- (around) + pteron (wing, but used architecturally for column arrangement).
