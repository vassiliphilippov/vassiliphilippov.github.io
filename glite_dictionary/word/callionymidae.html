Callionymidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæliəˈnɪmɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˌkaliəʊˈnɪmɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, colorful marine fishes commonly known as dragonets, characterized by elongated bodies and elaborate fins.
family of fish known as dragonets

Examples

Several species in the Callionymidae family are prized for their vibrant colors and striking patterns.
Researchers discovered a new member of the Callionymidae along the coral reefs of Indonesia.
The aquarium features several Callionymidae species, attracting many visitors interested in exotic marine life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dragonet - A small, brightly colored marine fish of the family Callionymidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'kallos' (beauty) and 'onyma' (name), referring to their ornate appearance.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkæliəˈnɪmɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌkalɪəˈnɪmɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, brightly colored marine fish known as dragonets, characterized by elongated bodies and often elaborate fin displays.
family of small marine fish (dragonets)
medical

Examples

The Callionymidae are admired for the vivid patterns displayed by many species of dragonets.
Researchers collected several specimens belonging to the Callionymidae from the coral reef.
Marine biologists study Callionymidae to understand coloration and courtship in small fishes.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek *kallionymos* (beautifully named), the type genus Callionymus.
