soapweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊpˌwid / (AmE), / ˈsəʊpˌwiːd / (BrE)
A tall, tree-like yucca native to the southwestern United States, known for its tough, sword-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers.
tall yucca plant of the southwestern US

Examples

Early settlers used the roots of soapweed to make soap for washing clothes.
The arid landscape was dotted with clusters of soapweed thriving under the harsh sun.
Botanists studied the blooming soapweed to better understand yucca pollination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yucca - a plant genus to which soapweed belongs

Etymology

Named for its traditional use; soap could be made from its roots or leaves.
