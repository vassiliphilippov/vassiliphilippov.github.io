Republic of Burundi

1. PROPN

/ rɪˈpʌblɪk əv bəˈrʊndi / (AmE), / rɪˈpʌblɪk əv bəˈrʊndi / (BrE)
a landlocked country in East-Central Africa, on the northeastern shore of Lake Tanganyika, officially known as Burundi
country in East-Central Africa

Examples

The Republic of Burundi gained independence from Belgium in 1962.
International aid has played a major role in rebuilding the Republic of Burundi after years of conflict.
The capital city of the Republic of Burundi is Gitega.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Burundi - country in East Africa, official short name

Etymology

From French 'République du Burundi', using the local name 'Burundi'.
