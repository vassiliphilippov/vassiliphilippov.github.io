seigniory

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːnjəri / (AmE), / ˈsiːnjəri / (BrE)
the authority, rank, or domain of a feudal lord; lordship
authority or domain of a feudal lord
archaic

Examples

The manor was part of his seigniory and subject to his laws.
Under the seigniory system, peasants owed service to their lord.
Her research focused on the history of feudal seigniory in medieval England.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lordship - the rank or authority of a lord

Etymology

From Old French seignorie (lordship), from seignor (lord), ultimately from Latin senior (elder).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsiːnjəri / (AmE), / ˈsiːnjəri / (BrE)
A district or estate governed by a feudal lord; the authority or domain of a seigneur.
estate or domain of a feudal lord

Examples

The castle stood at the center of his seigniory, overlooking the surrounding lands.
Throughout the Middle Ages, many villages fell under the control of a local seigniory.
After acquiring the estate, he became lord of the entire seigniory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lordship - authority or domain of a lord

Etymology

From Old French 'seigneurie' (lordship), from 'seigneur' (lord).
