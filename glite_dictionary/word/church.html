church

1. NOUN

/ tʃɝːtʃ / (AmE), / tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
A building used for public Christian worship.
Christian place of worship

Examples

The town's oldest church dates back to the 15th century.
On Sundays, the church is filled with families attending the morning service.
Lightning struck the steeple of the church during last night's storm.

Synset Examples

the church was empty

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: cathedral - the principal church of a diocese, containing the bishop's seat
  • Degree Lower: chapel - a small place of worship, often within a larger building or institution

Etymology

From Old English 'cirice', from Greek 'kyriakon' (the Lord's house).

2. NOUN

/ tʃɝtʃ / (AmE), / tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
