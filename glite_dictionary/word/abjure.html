to abjure

1. VERB

/ əbˈdʒʊr / (AmE), / əbˈdʒʊə / (BrE)
to formally renounce or reject a belief, cause, or claim, especially under pressure or on oath
formally renounce a belief or claim

Examples

Under public pressure, the politician finally abjured his radical views.
She was forced to abjure her former loyalty to the regime.
During the trial, he solemnly abjured any association with the rebel group.

Synset Examples

He retracted his earlier statements about his religion
She abjured her beliefs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to renounce - to formally declare abandonment of something
  • Synonym: to recant - to publicly withdraw a formerly held opinion

Etymology

From Latin abjurare (to deny on oath), from ab- (away) + jurare (to swear).
