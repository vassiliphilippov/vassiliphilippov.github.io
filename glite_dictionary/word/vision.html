vision

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪʒən / (AmE), / ˈvɪʒ.ən / (BrE)
the ability to see; the faculty or sense by which things are perceived visually
the ability to see

Examples

His vision has deteriorated over the past year due to cataracts.
The doctor tested her vision during the annual check-up.
Many animals have better night vision than humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sight - the faculty or power of seeing
  • Antonym: blindness - the condition of lacking sight

Etymology

From Latin 'visio', from 'videre' (to see).
