sequella

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈkwɛl.ə / (AmE), / sɪˈkwɛl.ə / (BrE)
A secondary consequence or result, especially one following a disease or injury.
secondary consequence or result
medical

Examples

The patient developed a chronic headache as a sequella of the injury.
Certain infections can lead to unexpected sequellae months after recovery.
Researchers are studying the sequella of long-term exposure to toxic chemicals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sequela - An abnormal condition resulting from a previous disease, injury, or attack

Etymology

From Latin 'sequela' (a follower, that which follows), patterned on 'sequela' in medical contexts.
