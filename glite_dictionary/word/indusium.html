indusium

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈduziəm / (AmE), / ɪnˈdjuːziəm / (BrE)
a thin membrane covering and protecting the developing spores, especially over the sori of a fern
membrane covering spores in ferns

Examples

The indusium protects the young sporangia on the underside of the fern frond.
Botanists examined the indusium to determine the maturity of the sori.
Not all ferns have an indusium covering their sori; in some species, it is absent.

Etymology

From Latin 'indusium' (a garment or covering).
