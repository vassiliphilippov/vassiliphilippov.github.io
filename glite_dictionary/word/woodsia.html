Woodsia

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊd.zi.ə / (AmE), / ˈwʊd.zi.ə / (BrE)
A genus of small to medium-sized ferns that typically grow on rocks in temperate and cold regions.
genus of rock-inhabiting ferns

Examples

Botanists discovered several rare Woodsia species growing among the cliffs.
The Woodsia genus is well-adapted to rocky and mountainous environments.
She specializes in the study of Woodsia and other rock-dwelling ferns.

Etymology

Named after English botanist Joseph Woods (1776–1864).
