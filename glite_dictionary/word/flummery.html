flummery

1. NOUN

/ ˈflʌməri / (AmE), / ˈflʌməri / (BrE)
A bland, sweet dessert made from stewed oatmeal or other ingredients, often formed into a custard or pudding.
sweet, bland oatmeal pudding

Examples

Traditional Welsh flummery is made by soaking oatmeal overnight and then straining and boiling it.
Her grandmother always served a pink strawberry flummery at holiday dinners.
Although flummery was once popular, few people make it from scratch today.

Etymology

From Welsh *llymru*, an oatmeal and water jelly.

2. NOUN

/ ˈflʌməri / (AmE), / ˈflʌməri / (BrE)
empty compliments or insincere, elaborate ceremonies meant to flatter or impress without substance.
empty flattery; insincere ceremony

Examples

His speech was full of flummery but had little real substance.
The awards ceremony turned into a display of political flummery.
She grew tired of the endless flummery and wanted honest feedback.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blandishment - flattering speech or action, often insincere
  • Antonym: genuine praise - honest, sincere commendation

Etymology

Originally a Welsh dish, later used figuratively in English for something insubstantial or silly.
