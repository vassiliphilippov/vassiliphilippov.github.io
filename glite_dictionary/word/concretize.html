to concretize

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑːŋ.krəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋ.krɪ.taɪz / (BrE)
To make something definite or specific; to give concrete form to an idea or concept.
make something definite or specific

Examples

The designer tried to concretize his vision through detailed sketches and models.
It can be difficult to concretize abstract ideas for young children.
The plan will help concretize our goals and set clear milestones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to actualize - make a reality of; realize
  • Antonym: abstract - consider something theoretically or separately from something else

Etymology

From French 'concrétiser', formed from 'concret' (concrete) and '-iser' (-ize).

2. VERB

/ ˈkɑːŋ.krəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋ.krɪ.taɪz / (BrE)
to make (something) specific, definite, or real rather than abstract
make something specific or definite

Examples

The concept finally concretized as a detailed plan after months of discussion.
Her vision for the project began to concretize with each draft of the proposal.
It took time for the vague idea to concretize into practical steps.

Synset Examples

the idea concretized in her mind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to materialize - to become real or actual
  • Antonym: abstract - to consider (something) in a general, non-specific form

Etymology

From French 'concrétiser', derived from Latin 'concretus' (hardened, condensed).
