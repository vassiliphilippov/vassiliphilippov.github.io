Loiseleuria

1. NOUN

/ loʊˌɪz.luˈɪə.ri.ə / (AmE), / ləʊˌɪz.ljʊˈɪə.ri.ə / (BrE)
A genus of small evergreen shrubs in the heath family, commonly known as alpine azalea, found in alpine and arctic regions.
genus of alpine evergreen shrubs

Examples

Loiseleuria is often found growing in rocky alpine soils across the Northern Hemisphere.
The tiny pink flowers of Loiseleuria are a welcome sight after the snow melts in spring.
Botanists have studied Loiseleuria to understand how plants survive harsh, cold environments.

Etymology

Named after French botanist Jean-Louis-Auguste Loiseleur-Deslongchamps.

2. NOUN

/ loʊ.ɪˌsɛl.jʊˈɪri.ə / (AmE), / lɔɪ.zəˌlɜː.riˈiː.ə / (BrE)
A genus of small evergreen shrubs in the heath family, with one species, commonly known as alpine azalea, found in alpine and arctic regions.
genus of alpine evergreen shrubs

Examples

The Loiseleuria genus is well adapted to survive in harsh arctic environments.
Botanists studied Loiseleuria to understand how plants cope with extreme cold.
The delicate pink flowers of Loiseleuria brighten the stony slopes in spring.

Etymology

Named after Jean-Louis-Auguste Loiseleur-Deslongchamps, French botanist.
