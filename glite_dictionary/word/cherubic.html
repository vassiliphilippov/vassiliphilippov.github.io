cherubic

1. ADJ

/ tʃəˈrubɪk / (AmE), / tʃəˈruːbɪk / (BrE)
having a sweet, innocent, and angelic appearance or nature, like a cherub
sweet and angelic in appearance or nature

Examples

The baby's cherubic smile melted everyone's heart in the room.
With her cherubic features and sparkling eyes, she looked like a living angel.
His cherubic expression made it hard for anyone to believe he was ever mischievous.

Synset Examples

an angelic smile
a cherubic face
looking so seraphic when he slept
a sweet disposition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angelic - having a nature or appearance like an angel
  • Synonym: seraphic - resembling a seraph; angelically pure or beautiful
  • Antonym: devilish - like a devil; evil or mischievous

Etymology

From cherub, ultimately via Latin from Hebrew kərūv, with -ic for the adjective form.
