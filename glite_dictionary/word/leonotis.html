Leonotis

1. NOUN

/ liːˈɒnətɪs / (AmE), / liːˈɒnətɪs / (BrE)
A small genus of tropical herbs and subshrubs native to South Africa, commonly known for their whorled orange flowers.
genus of tropical South African herbs and subshrubs

Examples

The vibrant flowers of Leonotis attract a variety of pollinators in South African gardens.
Some gardeners grow Leonotis for its showy, orange blossoms and drought tolerance.
Botanists study Leonotis to understand its adaptation to tropical climates.

Etymology

From Greek 'leon' (lion) and 'otis' (ear), referring to the shape of the flower clusters.

2. NOUN

/ liˈɑːnətɪs / (AmE), / liˈɒnətɪs / (BrE)
A small genus of tropical herbs and subshrubs native to South Africa, belonging to the mint family (Lamiaceae).
genus of tropical South African herbs

Examples

The bright orange flowers of Leonotis attract many pollinators in the wild.
Gardeners often cultivate Leonotis for its ornamental value and drought resistance.
The Leonotis species are well adapted to the climate of South Africa.

Etymology

From Greek 'leōn' (lion) and 'ous, ōtos' (ear), referring to the shape of the flowers.
