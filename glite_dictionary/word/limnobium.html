Limnobium

1. NOUN

/ lɪmˈnoʊbiəm / (AmE), / lɪmˈnəʊbiəm / (BrE)
A genus of aquatic plants in the family Hydrocharitaceae, commonly known as American frogbit, found in freshwater habitats of the Americas.
genus of aquatic plants (American frogbit)

Examples

Limnobium thrives in calm, freshwater ponds and slow-moving streams across much of North and South America.
The floating leaves of Limnobium help provide shelter for small aquatic animals.
Many gardeners add Limnobium to their aquatic plant collections due to its attractive, lily-like leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frogbit - a common name for Limnobium and related aquatic plants

Etymology

From Greek 'limnē' (lake) and 'bios' (life), meaning 'living in lakes'.
