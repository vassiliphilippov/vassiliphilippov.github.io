Spergula

1. NOUN

/ spɝˈɡjuːlə / (AmE), / spɜːˈɡjuːlə / (BrE)
a small genus of annual herbs native to the Old World, especially known for species like the corn spurry
genus of annual herbs (corn spurry)

Examples

The botanist identified the plant as Spergula in her field notes.
Farmers often regard Spergula as a weed in cereal fields.
Several species in the genus Spergula are found in temperate regions.

Etymology

From Latin 'spergula', diminutive of 'spargere' (to scatter), referring to seed dispersal.
