serendipitous

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛrənˈdɪpɪtəs / (AmE), / ˌsɛrənˈdɪpɪtəs / (BrE)
occurring or discovered by chance in a happy or beneficial way
occurring by chance in a beneficial way

Examples

Our meeting at the airport was completely serendipitous and led to a lasting friendship.
She considered the discovery of the old letter in her attic to be a serendipitous event.
The researchers made a serendipitous breakthrough while working on an unrelated project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lucky - having good luck
  • Antonym: unlucky - having or resulting from bad luck

Etymology

Formed from 'serendipity,' which was coined in 1754 by Horace Walpole after the fairy tale 'The Three Princes of Serendip.'
