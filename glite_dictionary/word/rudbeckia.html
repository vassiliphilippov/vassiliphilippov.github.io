Rudbeckia

1. NOUN

/ rʌdˈbɛkiə / (AmE), / rʊdˈbɛkɪə / (BrE)
A genus of North American perennial herbs with showy, cone-shaped yellow or orange flower heads, commonly known as coneflowers or black-eyed Susans.
a genus of North American flowering plants

Examples

The Rudbeckia in the garden added a bright splash of yellow throughout the summer months.
Many gardeners plant Rudbeckia to attract bees and butterflies to their flower beds.
She chose a bouquet featuring Rudbeckia for its striking color and long-lasting blooms.

Etymology

Named after Olof Rudbeck, Swedish botanist (1630–1702).

a genus of North American perennial herbs with showy, cone-shaped yellow, orange, or brown flower heads, commonly known as coneflowers or black-eyed Susans.
genus of North American flowering herbs

Examples

The garden was filled with bright yellow Rudbeckia, attracting bees and butterflies throughout the summer.
Many wildflower mixes include Rudbeckia because of its hardiness and showy blooms.

Related Terms
