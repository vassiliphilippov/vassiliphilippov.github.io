ablactation

1. NOUN

/ ˌæb.lækˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌablækˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of gradually substituting other food for a mother's milk in the diet of an infant or young mammal; weaning.
weaning from mother's milk
medical

Examples

Ablactation is an important milestone in a baby's development and should be approached gradually.
Veterinarians monitor the ablactation process in young animals to ensure healthy growth.
The practice of ablactation varies widely between cultures and families.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weaning - the process of accustoming an infant or young animal to food other than its mother's milk

Etymology

From Latin 'ablactatio' (weaning), from 'ablactare' (to wean), from 'ab-' (away) + 'lac, lact-' (milk).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæb.lækˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌab.lækˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of ending lactation; cessation of milk production in the mother.
cessation of lactation
medical

Examples

The doctor discussed the possible effects of ablactation with the mother.
Early ablactation may be necessary in certain medical situations.
Researchers are studying the hormonal changes that occur during ablactation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weaning - the process of gradually introducing an infant to food other than milk

Etymology

From Latin 'ablactatio', from 'ablactare' (to wean).
