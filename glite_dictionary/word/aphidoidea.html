Aphidoidea

1. NOUN

/ ˌæf.ɪˈdoʊ.ɪ.di.ə / (AmE), / ˌæf.ɪˈdəʊ.ɪ.dɪə / (BrE)
A superfamily of insects that includes most present-day families and species of aphids.
superfamily of aphids

Examples

The Aphidoidea superfamily contains a wide range of aphid species that are important crop pests.
Many entomologists study Aphidoidea to understand the evolution of plant-insect relationships.
Some members of Aphidoidea can transmit plant viruses as they feed on sap.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aphid - a small sap-sucking insect of the superfamily Aphidoidea

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Aphid and the suffix -oidea meaning 'like' or 'related to'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæfəˈdɔɪdiə / (AmE), / ˌæfɪˈdɔɪdɪə / (BrE)
A superfamily of insects that includes most of the existing families and species of aphids.
insect superfamily of aphids

Examples

The Aphidoidea includes a vast diversity of plant-feeding insects found worldwide.
Researchers studied members of the Aphidoidea to understand their impact on agriculture.
Most commonly known aphids are classified within the Aphidoidea superfamily.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aphid - small soft-bodied insect that feeds on plants

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Aphidoidea', based on 'Aphis', type genus of aphids.
