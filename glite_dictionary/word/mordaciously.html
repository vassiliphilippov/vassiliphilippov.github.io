mordaciously

1. ADV

/ mɔrˈdeɪʃəsli / (AmE), / mɔːˈdeɪʃəsli / (BrE)
In a biting, sharply critical, or caustic manner.
in a biting or sharply critical way

Examples

The critic wrote mordaciously about the novelist's latest work, sparing no detail.
She mordaciously remarked on his choice of outfit, making everyone at the table laugh.
His humor was mordaciously aimed at those in power, exposing their flaws.

Synset Examples

she taunted him mordaciously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sarcastically - in a sarcastic manner

Etymology

From Latin 'mordax' (biting), from 'mordere' (to bite) + English adverbial suffix '-ly'.
