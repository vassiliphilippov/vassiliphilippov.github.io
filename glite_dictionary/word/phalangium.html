Phalangium

1. NOUN

/ fəˈlændʒiəm / (AmE), / fəˈlændʒɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of arachnids in the family Phalangiidae, commonly known as harvestmen or daddy longlegs.
genus of arachnids (harvestmen)

Examples

The genus Phalangium includes several well-known species of harvestmen found across Europe.
Unlike spiders, Phalangium species have a single body segment and long, slender legs.
Many researchers have studied the reproductive behavior of Phalangium in natural habitats.

Etymology

From Latin 'phalangium', from Ancient Greek 'φάλαγγιον' (phalangion), meaning 'spider' or 'little phalanx'.

2. NOUN

/ fəˈlændʒiəm / (AmE), / fəˈlændʒɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of harvestmen, arachnids in the family Phalangiidae, commonly known as daddy longlegs.
genus of harvestmen arachnids

Examples

The species Phalangium opilio is widespread across Europe and North America.
Researchers found several Phalangium specimens living under the rocks.
Phalangium is distinguished by its long legs and globular body shape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harvestman - common name for arachnids of the order Opiliones, to which Phalangium belongs

Etymology

From Latin 'phalangium', from Greek 'phalangion', meaning 'venomous spider', diminutive of 'phalanx'.
