venae ciliares

1. NOUN

/ ˈviːnaɪ sɪlɪˈɛriz / (AmE), / ˈviːnaɪ sɪlɪˈɛəriːz / (BrE)
small veins that originate from the ciliary body in the eye and drain blood from this region
small veins of the eye's ciliary body
medical

Examples

The venae ciliares play an important role in draining blood from the ciliary body of the eye.
Damage to the venae ciliares can affect intraocular pressure and eye health.
Anatomists often refer to the venae ciliares when describing the blood supply around the lens and iris.

Etymology

From Latin 'vena' (vein) and 'ciliaris' (of the eyelash or ciliary body).
