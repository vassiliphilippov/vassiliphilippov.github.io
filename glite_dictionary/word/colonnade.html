colonnade

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑː.ləˈneɪd / (AmE), / ˌkɒl.əˈneɪd / (BrE)
A row of evenly spaced columns supporting a roof, an entablature, or arches, typically found in classical architecture.
a row of evenly spaced columns

Examples

The ancient temple features a colonnade that stretches along its entire length.
Visitors admired the colonnade that lined the front of the museum's grand façade.
A marble colonnade led the way from the garden into the palace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arcade - a covered passageway with arches along one or both sides

Etymology

From French 'colonnade', from Italian 'colonnata', from 'colonna' (column), from Latin 'columna'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkoʊləˈneɪd / (AmE), / ˌkɒləˈneɪd / (BrE)
a row of columns supporting a roof, an entablature, or arches, commonly found in classical architecture
row of columns supporting a roof or arches

Examples

The museum entrance was marked by a magnificent colonnade of marble pillars.
Visitors strolled along the colonnade, admiring the elegant arches above them.
The ancient temple featured a colonnade that surrounded its central courtyard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: portico - a structure consisting of a roof supported by columns at regular intervals, typically attached to a building as a porch

Etymology

From French 'colonnade', from Italian 'colonnata', from 'colonna' (column), from Latin 'columna'.
