scintigraph

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪntɪˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈsɪntɪˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
an apparatus used in nuclear medicine to make a scintigram by detecting radiation emitted from the body
medical imaging device for making scintigram
medical

Examples

The patient lay on the table while the scintigraph captured images of her thyroid gland.
A new digital scintigraph offers enhanced image resolution for bone scans.
Technicians calibrate the scintigraph daily to ensure accurate diagnostic images.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gamma camera - a device used to image gamma radiation emitting radioisotopes, especially in nuclear medicine

Etymology

From Latin 'scintilla' (spark) + Greek 'graphō' (to write), reflecting its function to produce images from radioactive tracers.
