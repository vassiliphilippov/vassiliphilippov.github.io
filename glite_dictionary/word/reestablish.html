to reestablish

1. VERB

/ ˌriː.ɪˈstæblɪʃ / (AmE), / ˌriː.ɪˈstæblɪʃ / (BrE)
to bring something back into existence, use, or position after it has ended or been lost
bring back into existence or use

Examples

After the flood, the local government worked hard to reestablish essential services for residents.
The company is trying to reestablish its reputation after a series of scandals.
They managed to reestablish communication with the base after the storm had passed.

Synset Examples

restore law and order
reestablish peace in the region
restore the emperor to the throne

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to restore - to bring back to a former condition or position

Etymology

From re- (again) + establish, after French réétablir.
