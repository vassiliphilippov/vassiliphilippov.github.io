sahib

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑː.hɪb, səˈhiːb / (AmE), / ˈsɑː.hɪb, səˈhiːb / (BrE)
(historical, chiefly British India) A term of respect used especially for important white Europeans in colonial India, commonly placed after the person's name.
term of respect for Europeans in colonial India
archaic british

Examples

The servants addressed the British officer as Captain Sahib during his stay in India.
In colonial tales, local people would often refer to European men as Sahib.
Even today, in some regions, the word Sahib is still used to show respect.

Etymology

From Hindi 'sāhib,' from Arabic 'ṣāḥib' (companion, master).
