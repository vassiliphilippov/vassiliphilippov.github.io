osteolysis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑstioʊˈlaɪsɪs / (AmE), / ˌɒstiəʊˈlaɪsɪs / (BrE)
the pathological destruction or dissolution of bone tissue, typically due to disease, infection, or insufficient blood supply
the breakdown or dissolution of bone tissue
medical

Examples

The X-ray revealed significant osteolysis near the site of the hip implant.
If untreated, chronic infection can lead to severe osteolysis in the affected bone.
Researchers are studying ways to prevent osteolysis following joint replacement surgery.

Etymology

From Greek osteon (bone) + lysis (a loosening or dissolution).
