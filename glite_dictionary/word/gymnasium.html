gymnasium

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪmˈneɪziəm / (AmE), / dʒɪmˈneɪziəm / (BrE)
A type of secondary school, particularly in some European countries, that prepares students for higher education, typically covering grades 9 to 12 or their equivalents.
secondary school preparing for higher education

Examples

After completing his studies at the gymnasium, he enrolled at the university.
The gymnasium in Germany is known for its rigorous curriculum.
She attended a gymnasium before pursuing medicine in college.

Etymology

From Latin 'gymnasium', from Greek 'gymnasion' (school for exercise or learning).

2. NOUN

/ dʒɪmˈneɪziəm / (AmE), / dʒɪmˈneɪziəm / (BrE)
a large room or building equipped for indoor sports, exercise, or physical training
sports or exercise facility

Examples

The school built a new gymnasium to provide students with better sports facilities.
During winter, our basketball team practices in the gymnasium instead of outdoors.
The gymnasium was filled with parents attending the annual sports day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gym - short form for gymnasium; also refers to a place for exercise

Etymology

From Latin 'gymnasium', from Greek 'gymnasion' (place for physical exercise).
