prospectiveness

1. NOUN

/ prɑːˈspɛktɪvnəs / (AmE), / prɒˈspɛktɪvnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of having potential or offering possibilities for future success, development, or discovery.
quality of having future potential

Examples

The prospectiveness of the site was evaluated before investing any additional resources.
Their report highlights the prospectiveness of newly discovered mineral deposits in the region.
Investors were attracted by the prospectiveness of the emerging technology sector.

Synset Examples

The well was intended to determine the prospectiveness of the eastern flank of the Kevin Dome and the information will be included in our revised geological model, which will determine our future geographic area of interest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potential - having or showing the capacity to develop into something in the future

Etymology

Formed from 'prospective' (from Latin 'prospectus', meaning 'view') + noun suffix '-ness'.
