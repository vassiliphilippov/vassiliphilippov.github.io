hare

1. NOUN

/ hɛr / (AmE), / heə / (BrE)
The flesh of a hare, used as food.
meat of a hare, used as food

Examples

The chef prepared a traditional stew using tender hare and root vegetables.
Game meats like hare are popular in some European cuisines, especially during autumn.
She had never tasted hare before and was surprised by its rich flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rabbit - flesh of a rabbit, used as food

Etymology

Old English *hara*; related to Dutch *haas* and German *Hase*.

2. VERB

/ hɛr / (AmE), / heə / (BrE)
to run or move quickly, especially in a sudden or energetic way
run or move quickly

Examples

As soon as the bell rang, the children hared out of the classroom.
He hared across the street to catch the bus.
She hared down the hallway when she heard the news.

Synset Examples

He hared down the hill

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rush - to move with urgent haste
  • Synonym: dash - to run or travel somewhere quickly
  • Antonym: amble - to walk at a slow, relaxed pace

Etymology

From the noun 'hare', referring to the animal noted for its speed.

3. NOUN

/ hɛr / (AmE), / heə / (BrE)
a fast, long-eared mammal resembling a large rabbit, with long hind legs and a divided upper lip, whose young are born with fur and open eyes
a fast, long-eared rabbit-like mammal

Examples

The startled hare shot across the field at remarkable speed.
She could distinguish a hare from a rabbit by its longer legs and ears.
As the sun set, a solitary hare grazed near the hedgerow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rabbit - a small burrowing mammal related to the hare

Etymology

From Old English hara (hare), of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
