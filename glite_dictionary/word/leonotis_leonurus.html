Leonotis leonurus

1. NOUN

/ liˈɑnətɪs liˈɒnərəs / (AmE), / liˈəʊnətɪs liˈɒnərəs / (BrE)
A South African plant of the mint family, with orange tubular flowers and leaves that are sometimes smoked as a tobacco substitute.
South African herb smoked like tobacco

Examples

Traditional healers in South Africa often use Leonotis leonurus for its medicinal properties.
Some people smoke the dried leaves of Leonotis leonurus as an herbal alternative to tobacco.
The bright orange flowers of Leonotis leonurus attract many pollinators to the garden.

Etymology

From Greek 'leon' (lion) + 'otis' (ear), referring to the shape of the flowers; species name 'leonurus' means 'lion's tail' in Latin.
