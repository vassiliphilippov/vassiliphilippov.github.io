ingrowth

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌɡroʊθ / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌɡrəʊθ / (BrE)
a growth or process in which tissue, material, or another substance develops inward or into something
something that grows inward

Examples

The surgeon observed ingrowth of tissue around the implant.
This material promotes bone ingrowth after joint replacement surgery.
The microscope revealed an ingrowth of new cells into the wound area.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: outgrowth - something that grows outward

Etymology

From in- (inward) + growth, modeled after similar compounds.
