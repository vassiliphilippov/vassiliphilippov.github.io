recollective

1. ADJ

/ ˌrɛkəˈlɛktɪv / (AmE), / ˌrɛkəˈlɛktɪv / (BrE)
able to remember things well; having a good memory.
having a good memory

Examples

She has a truly recollective mind, rarely forgetting any details from her childhood.
His recollective abilities impressed everyone during the trivia competition.
Being recollective is a valuable trait for historians and researchers.

Synset Examples

a retentive mind
tenacious memory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retentive - having the ability to remember facts easily
  • Antonym: forgetful - tending to forget things easily

Etymology

From Latin 'recollectus,' past participle of 'recollegere' (to recollect) + English suffix '-ive.'
