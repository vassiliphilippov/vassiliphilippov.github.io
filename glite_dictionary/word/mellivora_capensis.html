Mellivora capensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛlɪˈvɔrə kəˈpɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˌmɛlɪˈvɔːrə kəˈpɛnsɪs / (BrE)
The scientific name for the honey badger, a nocturnal, badger-like carnivorous mammal native to Africa and southern Asia.
scientific name for honey badger

Examples

The Mellivora capensis is renowned for its fearless nature in the wild.
Researchers observed the Mellivora capensis raiding a beehive at night.
Unlike other mustelids, Mellivora capensis often travels long distances in search of food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honey badger - a carnivorous mammal known for its strength and boldness

Etymology

From Latin: 'mellivora' meaning 'honey eater' and 'capensis' referring to the Cape of Good Hope region.
