Hunnemania fumariifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˌhʌnəˈmeɪniə fjuːˌmæriɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˌhʌnɪˈmiːniə fjuːˌmærɪˈfəʊliə / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to the highlands of Mexico, grown for its glossy, bright yellow flowers and finely divided blue-grey foliage.
Mexican perennial with yellow flowers

Examples

The Hunnemania fumariifolia thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.
Gardeners prize Hunnemania fumariifolia for its striking yellow flowers each summer.
The blue-grey foliage of Hunnemania fumariifolia creates a lovely contrast in mixed borders.

Etymology

Genus name from botanist John Hunneman; species name from Latin *fumaria* (smoky) and *folia* (leaves), referring to the foliage.
