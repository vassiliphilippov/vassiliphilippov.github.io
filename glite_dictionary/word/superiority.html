superiority

1. NOUN

/ suːˌpɪriˈɔrəti / (AmE), / suːˌpɪəriˈɒrəti / (BrE)
the state or quality of being better, more powerful, or more important than others
the state of being superior

Examples

Her sense of superiority made it difficult for her to work with others as equals.
The team's superiority was evident in every aspect of the competition.
He spoke with an air of superiority that irritated his colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inferiority - the state of being lower in status or quality
  • Synonym: dominance - power and influence over others

Etymology

From Latin superioritas, from superior (higher, upper) + -ity.

2. NOUN

/ suːˌpɪriˈɔrəti / (AmE), / suːˌpɪəriˈɒrəti / (BrE)
the attitude or belief that one is better than others; an air of self-importance or condescension
attitude of being better than others

Examples

Her tone was laced with superiority, making everyone around her feel small and unimportant.
Some people mask their insecurities behind a façade of superiority.
He spoke with such obvious superiority that no one wanted to argue with him.

Synset Examples

he hated the white man's superiority and condescension

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrogance - an attitude of superiority manifested in an overbearing manner
  • Antonym: humility - the quality of having a modest or low view of one's importance

Etymology

From Latin 'superioritas', from 'superior' meaning 'higher'.
