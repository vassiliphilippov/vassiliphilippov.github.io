deoxycytidine monophosphate

1. NOUN

/ diˌɑksɪˈsaɪtədin ˌmɑnəˌfɑsˌfeɪt / (AmE), / diˌɒksɪˈsɪtɪdiːn ˌmɒnəˌfɒsˌfeɪt / (BrE)
a nucleotide that is one of the four building blocks of DNA, consisting of a deoxycytidine molecule bound to a single phosphate group; also called deoxycytidylate or dCMP
a nucleotide in DNA, also called dCMP
medical

Examples

The enzyme catalyzes the conversion of deoxycytidine monophosphate to deoxycytidine diphosphate.
Researchers measured the levels of deoxycytidine monophosphate in different tissue samples.
A mutation can affect the synthesis of deoxycytidine monophosphate, potentially impacting DNA replication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nucleotide - a compound consisting of a nucleoside and a phosphate group, forming the basic structural unit of nucleic acids

Etymology

From 'deoxy-' (lacking oxygen), 'cytidine' (a nucleoside), and 'monophosphate' (one phosphate group).
