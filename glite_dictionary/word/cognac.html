Cognac

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊ.njæk / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.jæk / (BrE)
A high-quality grape brandy made in the Cognac region of France.
French brandy from Cognac region

Examples

After dinner, we enjoyed a glass of Cognac by the fireplace.
She prefers Cognac over other types of brandy for its rich flavor.
The restaurant offers an impressive selection of aged Cognacs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brandy - an alcoholic spirit distilled from wine or fermented fruit juice

Etymology

Named after Cognac, a town in southwestern France where the brandy is produced.
