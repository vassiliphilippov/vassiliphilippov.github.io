Monilia

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈnɪliə / (AmE), / məˈnɪlɪə / (BrE)
A genus of parasitic, yeast-like imperfect fungi that produce spherical or oval conidia in branched chains, with some species often classified under other genera, particularly Candida.
genus of parasitic yeastlike fungi
medical

Examples

Several species within Monilia are known to cause infections in both plants and humans.
Laboratory tests identified the fungal pathogen as Monilia rather than as a member of the genus Candida.
Researchers are studying the role of Monilia in agricultural crop diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Candida - a genus of yeastlike fungi, some species formerly placed in Monilia

Etymology

From Latin *monile* (necklace), likely referencing the chain-like appearance of spores.
