monogynist

1. NOUN

/ məˈnɑːdʒənɪst / (AmE), / məˈnɒdʒɪnɪst / (BrE)
A person who practices monogamy, having only one spouse at a time.
person with only one spouse at a time

Examples

She described herself as a proud monogynist who believes in lifelong commitment.
In his community, being a monogynist set him apart from many of his peers.
Despite popular trends, he remained a steadfast monogynist throughout his life.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: polygamist - a person who has more than one spouse at the same time

Etymology

From Greek 'monos' (single) + 'gynē' (woman, wife) + '-ist'.
