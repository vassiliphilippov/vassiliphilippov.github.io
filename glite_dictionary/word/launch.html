launch

1. NOUN

/ lɔntʃ / (AmE), / lɔːntʃ / (BrE)
the act of propelling or sending something with force, especially into motion or into the air
the act of sending with force

Examples

The launch of the new spacecraft was watched by millions around the world.
With a sudden launch, the ball soared over the fence.
The engineers prepared carefully for the launch of the missile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propulsion - the action of driving or pushing forward

Etymology

From Middle French *lancier* (to throw), itself from Late Latin *lanceare* (to hurl a lance).

Source: WordNet
