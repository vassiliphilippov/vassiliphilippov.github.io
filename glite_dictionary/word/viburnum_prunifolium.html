Viburnum prunifolium

1. NOUN

/ vaɪˈbɜrnəm pruːnɪˈfoʊliəm / (AmE), / vɪˈbɜːnəm pruːnɪˈfəʊlɪəm / (BrE)
a species of upright deciduous shrub, commonly known as blackhaw, native to eastern and east central North America, with frosted dark-blue fruit.
a North American shrub (blackhaw)

Examples

The gardener selected Viburnum prunifolium for its attractive spring flowers and autumn berries.
Native Americans are known to have used parts of Viburnum prunifolium for medicinal purposes.
In landscaping, Viburnum prunifolium is valued for its hardiness and wildlife-friendly fruit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackhaw - common English name for Viburnum prunifolium

Etymology

From Latin: 'viburnum' (a type of shrub) + 'prunifolium' (with leaves like a plum tree).
