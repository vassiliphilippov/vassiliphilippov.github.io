Manduca sexta

1. PROPN

/ mænˈduːkə ˈsɛkstə / (AmE), / manˈdjuːkə ˈsɛkstə / (BrE)
A species of moth native to the Americas, whose larvae are known as tobacco hornworms and are often studied in biology.
a species of moth; tobacco hornworm larvae

Examples

Researchers frequently study Manduca sexta to understand insect development and behavior.
The larvae of Manduca sexta are commonly called tobacco hornworms and feed on tobacco and tomato plants.
In the laboratory, Manduca sexta is a model organism for scientific experiments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tobacco hornworm - larval stage of Manduca sexta, known for feeding on tobacco plants

Etymology

From Latin, with Manduca meaning 'glutton' and sexta meaning 'sixth'.

2. NOUN

/ mænˈduːkə ˈsɛkstə / (AmE), / mænˈdjuːkə ˈsɛkstə / (BrE)
a large green larva of a hawkmoth species, commonly known as the tobacco hornworm, that feeds on tobacco and related plants
species of hawkmoth larva (tobacco hornworm)

Examples

The larvae of Manduca sexta are known for their voracious appetite for tobacco leaves.
Researchers use Manduca sexta as a model organism in studies of insect physiology.
Gardeners often mistake Manduca sexta for the similar-looking tomato hornworm.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: tomato hornworm - a similar green caterpillar feeding on tomato plants

Etymology

From Latin; Manduca (genus name from 'manducare', to chew) and sexta (Latin for 'sixth').
