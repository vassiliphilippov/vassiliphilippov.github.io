carabinier

1. NOUN

/ ˌkær.ə.bɪˈnɪr / (AmE), / ˌkær.ə.bɪˈnɪə / (BrE)
a soldier, historically a mounted one, armed with a carbine as a primary weapon
historical soldier armed with a carbine

Examples

The museum displayed the uniform of a carabinier from the Napoleonic Wars.
A regiment of carabiniers charged across the battlefield on horseback.
In the novel, the protagonist befriends a young carabinier stationed at the frontier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cavalryman - a soldier who fights mounted on horseback
  • Antonym: infantryman - a soldier who fights on foot

Etymology

From French carabinier, from carabine (carbine) + -ier (agent suffix).
