infirm

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈfɝm / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɜːm / (BrE)
lacking bodily or muscular strength, especially because of age or illness; weak or frail.
physically weak, especially from age or illness

Examples

After the fall, the infirm gentleman needed help with everyday tasks.
The nursing home provides special care for infirm elderly residents.
Years of illness left her feeling too infirm to go outside.

Synset Examples

a feeble old woman
her body looked sapless

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feeble - lacking physical strength, especially due to age or illness
  • Antonym: robust - strong and healthy; vigorous

Etymology

From Latin 'infirmitas' (weakness), from 'in-' (not) + 'firmus' (strong).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈfɝm / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɜːm / (BrE)
lacking strength or firmness of will, character, or purpose; weak or irresolute in spirit or determination.
lacking firmness of will or purpose

Examples

Despite good intentions, he proved too infirm to make the difficult decision.
Her infirm resolve made her easily swayed by others.
The leader's infirm character was apparent in times of crisis.

Synset Examples

Infirm of purpose! / Give me the daggers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weak-willed - lacking determination or resolve
  • Antonym: resolute - admirably purposeful, determined, and unwavering

Etymology

From Latin infirmus, meaning 'weak, feeble'.

Synset POS: s
