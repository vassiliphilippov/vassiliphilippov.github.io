caruncular

1. ADJ

/ kəˈrʌŋkjələr / (AmE), / kəˈrʌŋkjʊlə / (BrE)
Relating to or resembling a caruncle, a small, fleshy protuberance often found on animals or parts of the human body.
resembling or relating to a caruncle
medical

Examples

The surgeon noted a caruncular mass near the patient's lower eyelid.
In some birds, caruncular features on the head become more prominent during breeding season.
A caruncular swelling was observed on the animal's oral tissue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caruncle - a small, fleshy growth or protuberance

Etymology

From caruncle + -ar, modeled on Latin-derived adjectives.
