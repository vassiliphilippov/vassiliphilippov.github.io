Ethelred II

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛθəlˌrɛd səˈkənd / (AmE), / ˈiːθəlˌrɛd ðə ˈsɛkənd / (BrE)
King of England from 978 to 1016, known for his unsuccessful struggle against Danish invasions and called 'Ethelred the Unready.'
king of England (978–1016), 'the Unready'

Examples

During his reign, Ethelred II faced repeated invasions by Danish forces.
Historians often refer to Ethelred II as 'the Unready,' highlighting his difficulties as king.
The policies of Ethelred II had long-term effects on the English monarchy.

Etymology

From Old English Æþelræd (meaning 'noble counsel'), combined with 'II' as the regnal number.
