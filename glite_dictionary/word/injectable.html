injectable

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈdʒɛktəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʒɛktəbl̩ / (BrE)
(of a drug or substance) capable of being injected into the body, typically using a syringe or needle.
capable of being injected
medical

Examples

This vaccine is available as an injectable solution for adults and children.
The doctor prescribed an injectable medication for severe allergic reactions.
Some antibiotics can be taken orally, while others must be given in injectable form.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: oral - (of a medicine) taken by mouth rather than injection
  • Synonym: intravenous - (of a drug) administered directly into a vein

Etymology

From Latin 'injectus' (thrown in, injected) + English suffix '-able'.
