stonemason

1. NOUN

/ ˈstoʊnˌmeɪsən / (AmE), / ˈstəʊnˌmeɪsən / (BrE)
a skilled worker who builds or carves stone, especially as a trade
person who works with stone

Examples

The stonemason carefully shaped slabs for the cathedral's new facade.
Her grandfather worked as a stonemason in the local quarry.
They hired a skilled stonemason to restore the ancient fountain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bricklayer - person who builds with bricks

Etymology

From stone + mason, where mason comes from Old French 'maçon', meaning a builder with stone.
