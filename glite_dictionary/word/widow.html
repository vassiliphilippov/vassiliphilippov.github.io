widow

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪdoʊ / (AmE), / ˈwɪdəʊ / (BrE)
A woman whose husband has died, especially one who has not remarried.
woman whose husband has died

Examples

After her husband's passing, she became a widow at the age of 34.
The widow wore black as a sign of mourning during the ceremony.
Many charities offer support to the spouses and children of widows.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: widower - a man whose wife has died and has not remarried

Etymology

From Old English *widewe*, from Latin *vidua* (a widow), related to the verb *vidére* (to separate).

2. VERB

/ ˈwɪdoʊ / (AmE), / ˈwɪdəʊ / (BrE)
to make someone a widow or widower by causing the death of their spouse
to cause someone to lose a spouse by death

Examples

The civil war widowed thousands of families within a few years.
The sudden accident widowed her at only twenty-eight.
Many communities were devastated as the epidemic widowed numerous women.

Synset Examples

The war widowed many women in the former Yugoslavia

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orphan - to make someone an orphan by causing the death of their parents

Etymology

From Old English 'widewe', influenced by Old French 'veuve', both deriving from Latin 'vidua' (a woman deprived of a husband).
