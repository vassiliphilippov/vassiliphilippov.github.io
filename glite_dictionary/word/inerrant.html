inerrant

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈɛrənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈɛrənt / (BrE)
incapable of being wrong; free from error
incapable of error

Examples

Many believers consider their sacred texts to be inerrant and beyond question.
The judge was expected to be inerrant in his decisions, though mistakes happen.
Her inerrant intuition about people impressed everyone at the firm.

Synset Examples

the Church was … theoretically inerrant and omnicompetent
lack an inerrant literary sense
an unerring marksman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unerring - never failing to be right or accurate
  • Antonym: fallible - capable of making mistakes or being wrong

Etymology

From Latin inerrans, inerrant- (not erring), from 'in-' (not) + 'errare' (to stray, to err).
