thoroughness

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɜroʊnəs / (AmE), / ˈθʌrənəs / (BrE)
the quality of doing something with great care and attention to detail, making sure that every part is completed or considered
attention to detail; completeness

Examples

Her thoroughness in preparing the report impressed her supervisor.
The thoroughness of the cleaning made the house feel brand new.
He completed the project with remarkable thoroughness, checking every detail carefully.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meticulousness - the quality of paying close attention to detail
  • Antonym: carelessness - lack of attention or thoroughness

Etymology

From thorough + -ness, formed by adding the noun suffix -ness to 'thorough'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈθɝː.ə.nəs / (AmE), / ˈθʌr.ə.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of being complete and careful, showing attention to every detail.
attention to full detail; completeness

Examples

Her thoroughness in checking each report ensured that no errors were overlooked.
The thoroughness of the investigation impressed everyone involved.
Preparing for the audit required remarkable thoroughness from the entire team.

Synset Examples

Seitz had never heard of Wertham, but he recalls being struck by the precision and thoroughness of his testimony, which ran for more than an hour and took the form of a lecture to the court.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carefulness - the quality of being cautious and paying close attention
  • Antonym: sloppiness - the quality of being careless or lacking attention to detail

Etymology

From 'thorough' + '-ness', deriving from Middle English 'thurgh' (through).
