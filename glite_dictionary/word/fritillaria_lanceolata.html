Fritillaria lanceolata

1. NOUN

/ frɪˌtɪləriə ˌlænsiəˈleɪtə / (AmE), / frɪˌtɪlɪˈɑːrɪə ˌlænsiəˈleɪtə / (BrE)
a herbaceous plant native to northwestern America, with green-and-purple bell-shaped flowers
herbaceous plant with bell-shaped flowers

Examples

The rare Fritillaria lanceolata blooms in spring throughout the woodlands of Oregon and Washington.
Botanists were excited to find a cluster of Fritillaria lanceolata along the hiking trail.
Many gardeners admire Fritillaria lanceolata for its striking green-and-purple, bell-shaped flowers.

Etymology

From Latin 'Fritillaria' (a genus of flowering plants) and Latin 'lanceolata' (lance-shaped, referring to the leaves).
