disjunct

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈdʒʌŋkt / (AmE), / dɪsˈdʒʌŋkt / (BrE)
separated or disconnected from something with which it is usually joined or associated.
separate; disconnected

Examples

The two companies operated as disjunct entities with no shared resources.
Her disjunct ideas made the essay seem fragmented and hard to follow.
In the painting, the shapes appear disjunct rather than forming a continuous pattern.

Synset Examples

little isolated worlds, as abruptly disjunct and unexpected as a palm-shaded well in the Sahara

Related Terms

  • Antonym: connected - joined or linked together
  • Synonym: distinct - recognizably different from something else

Etymology

From Latin disjunctus, past participle of disjungere (to separate).

2. ADJ

/ dɪsˈdʒʌŋkt / (AmE), / dɪsˈdʒʌŋkt / (BrE)
(of an organism, especially an insect) having deep constrictions separating the head, thorax, and abdomen.
having head, thorax, and abdomen distinctly separated

Examples

Certain wasps are easily identified by their disjunct body segments.
Insects with disjunct morphology are distinguished by a narrow connection between the thorax and abdomen.
The disjunct structure is a key feature found across many ant species.

Etymology

From Latin disjunctus, past participle of disjungere (to separate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02491135-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈdɪs.dʒʌŋkt / (AmE), / ˈdɪs.dʒʌŋkt / (BrE)
(of a melody) moving by intervals larger than a major second, rather than by stepwise motion.
of a melody: moving by large intervals

Examples

The piece features a disjunct melody that leaps unpredictably between notes.
Compared to the smooth, stepwise motion, the disjunct passage challenges singers with its wide intervals.
Composers often use disjunct movement in modern music to create a sense of drama or tension.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conjunct - (of a melody) moving mainly by stepwise intervals

Etymology

From Latin disjunctus (separated, distinct).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00568227-a
Synset POS: a

4. ADJ

/ ˈdɪs.dʒʌŋkt / (AmE), / ˈdɪs.dʒʌŋkt / (BrE)
(of populations or distributions) separated from each other and not continuous; occurring in distinct and isolated groups.
separated or non-continuous (esp. in distributions)

Examples

The species are found in a disjunct pattern across three mountain ranges.
This bird has a disjunct distribution, with populations in both North America and Europe.
Fossil records reveal a disjunct presence on either side of the ocean.

Synset Examples

disjunct distribution of king crabs

Etymology

From Latin disjunctus, past participle of disjungere (to separate).
