to rewire

1. VERB

/ ˌriˈwaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈwaɪə / (BrE)
to provide (a building, device, etc.) with new electrical wiring
provide with new wiring

Examples

They had to rewire the entire house after the fire damaged the old cables.
The electrician will rewire the office to support more computers and equipment.
After renovating the kitchen, we decided it was best to rewire the whole floor.

Synset Examples

the university rewired the dormitories when most students brought computers and television sets

Etymology

From re- (again) + wire, meaning to wire again.
