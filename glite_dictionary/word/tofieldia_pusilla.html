Tofieldia pusilla

1. NOUN

/ toʊˈfɪldiə pjuːˈsɪlə / (AmE), / təʊˈfɪldiə pjuːˈsɪlə / (BrE)
A small perennial plant of mountainous regions of Europe, known as false asphodel, with spikes of white flowers.
a European mountain plant with white flowers

Examples

Tofieldia pusilla thrives in the cold, damp meadows of the Scandinavian mountains.
The delicate white flowers of Tofieldia pusilla appear in late spring above its grass-like leaves.
Botanists were excited to document a new population of Tofieldia pusilla in the Alps.

Etymology

From the genus name Tofieldia, honoring Thomas Tofield, an English botanist, and Latin pusilla meaning 'small'.
