Calidris canutus

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlaɪdrɪs kəˈnuːtəs / (AmE), / kəˈlaɪdrɪs kəˈnjuːtəs / (BrE)
A species of sandpiper, commonly known as the red knot, that breeds in the Arctic and migrates to the Southern Hemisphere during winter.
a species of sandpiper (red knot)

Examples

Every spring, thousands of Calidris canutus undertake one of the longest migrations of any bird species.
Researchers tracked the Calidris canutus as it traveled from the Arctic to South America.
The decline in horseshoe crab populations has affected the breeding success of Calidris canutus along the Atlantic coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red knot - common name for Calidris canutus, a migratory sandpiper

Etymology

From Latin: 'Calidris' (an ancient name for a gray-colored water bird) and 'canutus,' the specific epithet, possibly after King Canute.
