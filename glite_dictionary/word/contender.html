contender

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtɛndəɹ / (AmE), / kənˈtɛndə / (BrE)
a person or team that competes with others to win something, especially a championship or position of superiority
person or team competing to win

Examples

After a series of wins, she became a serious contender for the championship.
The team was seen as a strong contender in the playoffs this year.
Every contender trained hard in hopes of coming out on top.

Synset Examples

he had respect for his rivals
he wanted to know what the competition was doing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: competitor - a person who takes part in a competition
  • Synonym: opponent - a person who is against someone in a contest
  • Antonym: ally - a person who cooperates with another, usually for a common purpose

Etymology

From contend + -er, from Latin contendere (to strive, compete).
