economics

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛkəˈnɑmɪks, ˌikəˈnɑmɪks / (AmE), / ˌiːkəˈnɒmɪks, ˌɛkəˈnɒmɪks / (BrE)
The branch of social science concerned with the production, distribution, and consumption of goods and services, and the management of resources.
study of production and distribution of goods and services

Examples

She decided to major in economics at university because she was interested in understanding how markets work.
Many government policies are based on principles of economics.
The study of economics helps people make informed financial decisions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: economy - the system of production and distribution of goods and services in a country or area

Etymology

From Greek oikonomikos (practicing stewardship), from oikonomia (house management).
