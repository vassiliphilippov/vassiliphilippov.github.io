Catawba

1. NOUN

/ kəˈtɔbə / (AmE), / kəˈtɔːbə / (BrE)
the Siouan language historically spoken by the Catawba people of the southeastern United States
a Siouan language of the Catawba people

Examples

Linguists have worked to document the Catawba language before it disappeared.
He studied Catawba as part of his research on Siouan languages.
Very few speakers remain who are fluent in Catawba today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Siouan - a family of Native American languages including Catawba

Etymology

From the name of the Catawba people, possibly from their own Siouan term for themselves.

2. NOUN

/ kəˈtɔːbə / (AmE), / kəˈtɔːbə / (BrE)
A member of a Native American people originally living in the Carolinas.
a member of a Native American people

Examples

Many Catawba now live on a reservation in South Carolina.
The Catawba maintained distinct cultural traditions long after European settlers arrived.
He is a descendant of the Catawba who originally inhabited the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Siouan - a family of North American Indian languages including that of the Catawba

Etymology

From Catawba, the name given to the people by themselves, sometimes spelled Katapu in their own language.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09671242-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ kəˈtɔbə / (AmE), / kəˈtɔːbə / (BrE)
A variety of reddish American table grape with a slipskin, used for eating and making wine.
a reddish American grape variety

Examples

This vineyard grows several varieties, including the sweet Catawba grape.
I tried a glass of sparkling wine made from Catawba grapes at the festival.
The ripe Catawba has a pleasant, musky flavor that makes it popular for jams and jellies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Concord - a dark blue variety of American grape

Etymology

Named after the Catawba River and the Catawba people of the southeastern United States.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07775300-n
Synset POS: n
