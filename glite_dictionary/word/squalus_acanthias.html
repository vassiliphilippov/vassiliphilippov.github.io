Squalus acanthias

1. NOUN

/ ˈskwɑːləs əˈkænθiəs / (AmE), / ˈskwɑːləs əˈkanθɪas / (BrE)
A species of small shark, commonly known as the spiny dogfish, found in the coastal waters of the Atlantic and Pacific, often used in scientific studies.
a species of small shark (spiny dogfish)

Examples

The anatomy lab used a Squalus acanthias specimen to teach students about shark physiology.
Fisheries scientists are concerned about the declining population of Squalus acanthias in some regions.
Due to its abundance, the Squalus acanthias is often studied in marine biology courses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiny dogfish - common name for Squalus acanthias

Etymology

From Latin: 'Squalus' meaning shark, 'acanthias' from Greek 'akantha' meaning thorn or spine.
