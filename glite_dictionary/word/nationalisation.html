nationalisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌnæʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnæʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of bringing private assets, industries, or businesses under government ownership or control.
changing to state ownership or control

Examples

The nationalisation of the railway system was completed in 1948.
Many workers were uncertain about their futures after the sudden nationalisation of the factories.
He opposed the government's policy of nationalisation in key industries.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: privatisation - the transfer of ownership from the state to private individuals
  • Synonym: to nationalize - to transfer a business or industry from private to state ownership (verb)

Etymology

From French 'nationalisation', derived from 'nation' and the suffix '-alisation'; related to 'nationalise'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌnæʃ.ə.nəl.aɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌnæʃ.ə.nə.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something national in character; rendering it characteristic of or pertaining to a nation as a whole
making national in character

Examples

The nationalisation of traditional festivals was a deliberate move to foster national identity.
Many language reforms involved the nationalisation of vocabulary to reflect the country's heritage.
The committee supported the nationalisation of education policies to ensure uniformity across all regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nationalization - the process of a government taking control of private industry (economic sense)

Etymology

From national + -isation, by analogy with similar words formed in the 19th century.
