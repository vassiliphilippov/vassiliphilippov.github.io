Ruth

1. PROPN

/ ruːθ / (AmE), / ruːθ / (BrE)
(in the Bible) the great-grandmother of King David, whose story is told in the Book of Ruth in the Old Testament.
biblical ancestor of King David

Examples

Ruth is admired for her loyalty and devotion in the biblical narrative.
The Book of Ruth tells the story of her journey from Moab to Bethlehem.
Many people name their daughters Ruth in honor of her virtue.

Etymology

From Hebrew רוּת (Rut).

2. PROPN

/ ruːθ / (AmE), / ruːθ / (BrE)
A book of the Old Testament that tells the story of Ruth, a Moabite woman who remained with her Israelite mother-in-law Naomi after her husband's death.
book of the Old Testament about Ruth and Naomi

Examples

Many scholars have analyzed the narrative structure of Ruth in the Old Testament.
He delivered a sermon based on the story from Ruth about loyalty and faith.
The Book of Ruth is often read during the Jewish festival of Shavuot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Book of Ruth - Alternative full title for the same biblical book

Etymology

From Hebrew רוּת (Rut), Ruth, name of the main character.
