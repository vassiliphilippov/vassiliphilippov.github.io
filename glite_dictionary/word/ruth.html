ruth

1. NOUN

/ ruːθ / (AmE), / ruːθ / (BrE)
(Archaic) A feeling of pity, sympathy, or sorrow for the misfortune of others.
(archaic) pity or compassion
archaic

Examples

The old tale inspired a deep ruth in those who heard of its tragic end.
In his ruth, the judge showed unusual mercy toward the defendant.
She gazed at the orphaned children, her heart full of ruth.

Synset Examples

the blind are too often objects of pity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pity - the feeling of sorrow and compassion caused by the suffering of others
  • Synonym: compassion - sympathetic pity and concern for the sufferings of others
  • Antonym: cruelty - callous indifference to or pleasure in causing pain and suffering

Etymology

From Middle English *ruth*, from Old English *hrēow* (sorrow, grief, regret).
