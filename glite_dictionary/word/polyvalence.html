polyvalence

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːliˈveɪləns / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˈveɪləns / (BrE)
(Chemistry) The property of having a valence greater than two; the ability of an atom or ion to combine with more than two other atoms or ions.
having a valence greater than two (chemistry)
medical

Examples

Many transition metals are known for their polyvalence in chemical compounds.
The concept of polyvalence helps explain the diverse bonding patterns seen in certain elements.
Researchers studied the effect of polyvalence on the stability of complex ions.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: valence - the combining power of an element, especially as measured by the number of hydrogen atoms it can displace or combine with

Etymology

From French 'polyvalence', from 'poly-' (many) + 'valence' (capacity).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːliˈveɪləns / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈveɪləns / (BrE)
(toxicology, immunology) The state or quality of being capable of counteracting or acting against more than one toxin, antigen, or kind of microorganism.
ability to counteract multiple toxins or antigens
medical

Examples

The polyvalence of this antivenom makes it suitable for treating bites from several types of snakes.
Researchers are investigating vaccines with increased polyvalence to address rapidly mutating viruses.
A high degree of polyvalence is essential for effective broad-spectrum antibiotics.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monovalence - state of being effective against a single toxin or antigen

Etymology

From French 'polyvalence', from Greek 'poly' (many) + Latin 'valentia' (strength).
