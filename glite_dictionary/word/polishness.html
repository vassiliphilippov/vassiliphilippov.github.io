Polishness

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊlɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪʃnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being Polish, especially in terms of culture, identity, or national character.
the quality of being Polish

Examples

She always maintained a sense of Polishness in her traditions, even after living abroad for years.
His writing explores themes of identity and Polishness in contemporary society.
The festival was a joyful celebration of Polishness, featuring music, food, and folk dances.

Synset Examples

His second conclusion is that despite military feats, ethnic tolerance and constitutional advances, there is an aspect of Polishness that has, over the centuries, colluded in this oscillating fortune.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Englishness - the quality or state of being English
  • Synonym: Frenchness - the quality or state of being French

Etymology

From Polish + -ness, denoting the state or quality of being Polish.
