gee

1. VERB

/ dʒiː / (AmE), / dʒiː / (BrE)
(of a draft animal) to turn to the right or to command to turn right.
(of animals) turn to the right

Examples

The farmhand shouted for the mule to gee at the fork in the road.
When the lead dog refused to gee, the sled veered off course.
With a sharp command, she made the oxen gee into the next field.

Synset Examples

the horse geed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: haw - (of a draft animal) to turn to the left

Etymology

Imitative of the sound of the command 'gee' used to direct animals, originally in the 17th century.

2. NOUN

/ dʒi / (AmE), / dʒi / (BrE)
a unit of force equal to the force of gravity at the Earth's surface, used to indicate the acceleration experienced by a body
unit of force equal to Earth's gravity

Examples

The astronauts experienced three gees during liftoff.
At high speeds, fighter pilots may endure up to nine gees of acceleration.
Cars on this roller coaster can pull up to 4 gees in tight turns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: g-force - acceleration felt as weight; gravitational force equivalent

Etymology

From the letter 'G', representing 'gravity' or 'gravitational acceleration'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13670214-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ dʒi / (AmE), / dʒiː / (BrE)
to command a horse or other draft animal to turn to the right
command to a horse to turn right

Examples

The farmer shouted 'gee!' and the oxen turned right at the end of the field.
She had to gee the horse to avoid the muddy path on the left.
At his command to gee, the team of mules obediently swung to the right.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: haw - command to a horse to turn left

Etymology

Imitative of the spoken command 'gee!' used in driving horses.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00867328-v
Synset POS: v
