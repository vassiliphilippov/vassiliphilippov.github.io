ringworm

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪŋˌwɝm / (AmE), / ˈrɪŋˌwɜːm / (BrE)
A contagious fungal infection of the skin or nails characterized by itchy, circular patches.
fungal skin infection with circular patches
medical

Examples

The child developed ringworm on his arm after playing with a stray cat.
A doctor can prescribe ointment to treat ringworm effectively.
Ringworm often appears as a red, itchy, circular rash on the skin.

Etymology

From the characteristic ring-shaped appearance of the rash; 'worm' is a misnomer as no worm is involved.
