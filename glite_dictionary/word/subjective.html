subjective

1. ADJ

/ səbˈdʒɛktɪv / (AmE), / səbˈdʒɛktɪv / (BrE)
based on or influenced by personal feelings, tastes, or opinions rather than external facts
based on personal feelings or opinions

Examples

Her evaluation of the painting was highly subjective, influenced by her personal tastes.
It is difficult to eliminate subjective bias when making decisions.
Pain is a subjective experience that can vary greatly between individuals.

Synset Examples

a subjective judgment

Related Terms

  • Antonym: objective - not influenced by personal feelings or opinions; unbiased

Etymology

From Medieval Latin subjectiveus, from Latin subjectus (brought under).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01620077-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ səbˈdʒɛktɪv / (AmE), / səbˈdʒɛktɪv / (BrE)
(of a mental act or experience) occurring or existing only within the mind rather than produced by external objects.
existing within the mind

Examples

The perception of beauty can be considered a subjective experience, unique to each individual.
Her response was based on a purely subjective interpretation rather than observable facts.
Dreams are often described as subjective events that take place entirely within one's mind.

Synset Examples

a cognition is an immanent act of mind

Related Terms

  • Antonym: objective - existing outside the mind; not influenced by personal feelings

Etymology

From Medieval Latin subjectivus (of a subject), from Latin subjectus (subject).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01277020-a
Synset POS: a
