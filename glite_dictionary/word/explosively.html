explosively

1. ADV

/ ɪkˈsploʊsɪvli / (AmE), / ɪkˈspləʊsɪvli / (BrE)
In a way that involves or suggests an explosion; with sudden and intense force or effect.
in an explosive or intense manner

Examples

The chemicals reacted explosively when mixed together in the lab.
He shouted explosively after hearing the news, startling everyone in the room.
Tensions in the region have risen explosively over the last month.

Synset Examples

the political situation in Kashmir and Jammu is explosively unstable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violently - with strong, forceful, or intense force
  • Antonym: calmly - in a peaceful and composed manner

2. ADV

/ ɪkˈsploʊsɪvli / (AmE), / ɪkˈspləʊsɪvli / (BrE)
In a sudden and rapid manner; with extreme speed or intensity.
suddenly and rapidly

Examples

The company's growth accelerated explosively after the new product launch.
Tensions rose explosively during the heated debate.
Wildfires can spread explosively in dry, windy conditions.

Synset Examples

the population in Central America is growing explosively

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abruptly - suddenly, without warning
  • Antonym: gradually - in a slow, step-by-step manner

Etymology

From 'explosive' + '-ly'.
