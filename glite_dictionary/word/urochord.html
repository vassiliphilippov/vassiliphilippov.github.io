urochord

1. NOUN

/ ˈjʊr.oʊˌkɔrd / (AmE), / ˈjʊərəˌkɔːd / (BrE)
a notochord found in the tail of the larval stage of certain primitive marine animals, especially tunicates
notochord in the tail of some larval marine animals

Examples

The urochord is a key feature distinguishing the larvae of tunicates from adult forms.
Under the microscope, the urochord appears as a prominent structure running through the larva's tail.
Scientists study the urochord to better understand the development of chordate animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: notochord - a flexible rod made out of a material similar to cartilage found in the embryonic stage of all chordates

Etymology

From Greek oura (tail) + chordē (cord, string).

2. NOUN

/ ˈjʊrəˌkɔrd / (AmE), / ˈjʊərəˌkɔːd / (BrE)
a notochord found in the tail region of a larval tunicate
notochord in larval tunicate's tail

Examples

The urochord extends into the tail of the larval tunicate, supporting its swimming movements.
Scientists observed the degeneration of the urochord as the tunicate matured into its adult form.
The presence of a urochord is a key characteristic in the classification of tunicate larvae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: notochord - a flexible rod-like structure found in the embryos of all chordates

Etymology

From Greek 'oura' (tail) + 'chordē' (string, cord).
