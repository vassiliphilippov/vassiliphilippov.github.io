vibrion

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪbriˌɑn / (AmE), / ˈvɪbrɪɒn / (BrE)
a curved, rod-shaped, motile bacterium, especially of the genus Vibrio
curved motile bacterium
medical

Examples

The scientist identified a vibrion as the cause of the outbreak.
Some marine vibrions are known to cause foodborne illnesses in humans.
Under the microscope, the vibrion displayed a distinctive curved shape and rapid movement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vibrio - genus of curved, rod-shaped, motile bacteria

Etymology

From New Latin Vibrio; ultimately from Latin 'vibrare' (to vibrate).
