paperback

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪpərˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈpeɪpəbæk / (BrE)
a book with a flexible paper cover, rather than a hard one
book with a paper cover

Examples

She picked up a paperback novel to read on the train.
Most students bought the paperback edition because it was more affordable.
A new paperback version of the classic was released last month.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hardback - a book with a rigid protective cover

Etymology

From 'paper' + 'back', referring to the paper covers of the book.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03892129-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈpeɪ.pɚˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈpeɪ.pə.bæk / (BrE)
(of books) having a flexible cover made of thick paper or thin cardboard, not a hard cover.
having a flexible paper cover (of books)

Examples

The novel is available in both paperback and hardback formats.
She prefers paperback editions because they are lighter to carry.
Many students buy used paperback textbooks to save money.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hardback - (of books) having a hard, rigid cover

Etymology

Formed from 'paper' + 'back', referring to the paper cover replacing a hardback.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00258098-s
Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪ.pɚˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈpeɪ.pəˌbæk / (BrE)
A book with a flexible paper cover, rather than a hard cover.
book with a paper cover

Examples

She loves reading paperbacks because they are lightweight and easy to carry around.
The latest bestseller is now available in paperback at all major bookstores.
I prefer a paperback to a hardcover when traveling because it fits better in my bag.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hardback - book with a rigid or hard cover
  • Synonym: softcover - book with a flexible cover (synonym for paperback)

Etymology

From 'paper' + 'back', referencing the paper cover at the back (and front) of the book.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03892129-n
Synset POS: n
