opium

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ.pi.əm / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.pi.əm / (BrE)
A highly addictive narcotic drug obtained from the unripe seed capsules of the opium poppy, used medicinally and as an illegal drug.
addictive narcotic from opium poppy

Examples

The use of opium for pain relief dates back thousands of years.
He was arrested for smuggling opium across the border.
Many artists in the 19th century were rumored to have taken opium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heroin - a powerful, addictive drug derived from morphine, usually illegal
  • Synonym: morphine - a medicinal alkaloid derived from opium
  • Antonym: cocaine - a strong stimulant drug, sometimes compared with opium in narcotic discussions

Etymology

From Latin 'opium', from Greek 'opion' (poppy juice).
