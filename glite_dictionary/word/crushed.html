crushed

1. ADJ

/ krʌʃt / (AmE), / krʌʃt / (BrE)
(of a person or spirit) feeling extremely defeated, subdued, or brought low in condition or status.
subdued or brought low in spirit

Examples

After receiving the bad news, he felt completely crushed and could not face his friends.
She was crushed by the criticism from her mentor, losing all confidence in her abilities.
His crushed spirit was evident in the way he spoke and carried himself.

Synset Examples

brought low
a broken man
his broken spirit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broken - subdued or emotionally defeated
  • Antonym: elated - in high spirits or extremely happy
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ krʌʃt / (AmE), / krʌʃt / (BrE)
(of fabric) treated so as to have a permanently wrinkled or textured appearance, often for decorative effect.
fabric with a wrinkled or textured finish

Examples

She wore a luxurious jacket made of crushed velvet to the party.
The crushed fabric gave the curtains an elegant, textured look.
I prefer crushed materials because they don't show wrinkles as easily.

Synset Examples

crushed velvet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrinkled - having small lines or folds on the surface
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface, opposite of wrinkled or crushed
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02248412-s
Synset POS: s
