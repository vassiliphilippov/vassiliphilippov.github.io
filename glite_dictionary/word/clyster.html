clyster

1. NOUN

/ ˈklaɪstər / (AmE), / ˈklaɪstə / (BrE)
an injection of liquid into the rectum to stimulate evacuation of the bowels, especially for medical or diagnostic purposes; an enema
injection into rectum to evacuate bowels (enema)
archaic medical

Examples

In the 18th century, doctors often prescribed a clyster to relieve constipation.
The patient received a clyster before the surgical procedure commenced.
Historical records show that clysters were popular remedies in European medicine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enema - a procedure involving injection of liquid into the rectum to cause evacuation

Etymology

From Old French clister, from Latin clyster, from Ancient Greek klyster ('a syringe').
