catarrhine

1. ADJ

/ kəˈtær.aˌraɪn / (AmE), / kəˈtær.ə.raɪn / (BrE)
relating to Old World monkeys and apes that have close-set, downward-facing nostrils.
relating to Old World monkeys and apes

Examples

The catarrhine primates are native to Africa and Asia.
Scientists compared the nasal structure of catarrhine and platyrrhine monkeys.
A catarrhine skull typically shows nostrils close together, opening downward.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: platyrrhine - relating to New World monkeys with broad, sideways-facing nostrils

Etymology

From Greek 'katarrhin' meaning 'with downward-pointing nose', from 'kata-' (down) and 'rhis' (nose).

2. NOUN

/ kəˈtær.aˌraɪn / (AmE), / kəˈtar.ə.raɪn / (BrE)
an Old World monkey or ape of Africa or Asia, characterized by downward-facing nostrils and nonprehensile tails
Old World monkey or ape

Examples

Gibbons and baboons are examples of catarrhine primates found in Asia and Africa.
Unlike platyrrhines, catarrhines have downward-facing nostrils and nonprehensile tails.
Scientists compared the dental patterns of catarrhine and New World monkeys to study their evolution.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: platyrrhine - a New World monkey with broad, widely spaced nostrils

Etymology

From Greek 'katarrhin' (with a downward nose), from 'kata-' (down) + 'rhis, rhin-' (nose).
