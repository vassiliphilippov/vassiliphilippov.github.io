realization

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɪəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or result of making a plan, hope, or idea become real or concrete
making something real or concrete

Examples

The successful launch marked the realization of years of engineering effort.
His dream finally saw realization when the project was approved.
The realization of the plan required cooperation from every team member.

Synset Examples

the victory was the realization of a whole year's work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: achievement - something accomplished through effort

Etymology

From late Latin 'realis' (actual) + '-ation', via French 'réalisation'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00062737-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˌriələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɪəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
a musical composition that has been completed or enriched by someone other than the original composer, often by adding harmonies, instrumentation, or finishing unfinished sections
completed/enriched musical composition by another

Examples

The conductor presented a new realization of the unfinished symphony using the composer's original sketches.
Scholars praised her realization of the Baroque score for its sensitive use of period instruments.
His realization of the composer's notes brought the long-lost opera to life on stage.

Etymology

From realize + -ation; in musical sense, influenced by the practice of 'realizing' figured bass or unfinished works in music.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07052802-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˌriːələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɪəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of completing or elaborating a piece of music that was left sparsely notated by a composer, often by adding harmonies, dynamics, or instrumentation.
completion of a sparsely notated musical work

Examples

The pianist's realization of the Baroque manuscript added new melodic lines and harmony.
Her acclaimed realization of Bach's unfinished fugue brought the piece to life for modern audiences.
Composers often invite musicians to attempt their own realization of a figured bass.

Etymology

From Latin 'realizare' (to make real) via French 'réalisation'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00941859-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˌriːəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɪəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of becoming fully aware of something; an instance of understanding or perceiving something clearly
becoming fully aware or understanding

Examples

Her realization of the danger came too late to avoid the accident.
Winning the award was a realization of a lifelong dream for him.
A sudden realization struck her as she read the letter.

Synset Examples

a growing realization of the risk involved
a sudden recognition of the problem he faced
increasing recognition that diabetes frequently coexists with other chronic diseases

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recognition - the act of recognizing or being recognized, especially something previously known
  • Synonym: awareness - having knowledge or perception of a situation or fact
  • Antonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge or awareness

Etymology

From realize + -ation, ultimately from Latin rēs (thing) and -ationem (noun-forming suffix).

5. NOUN

/ ˌriːələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɪəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of converting assets into cash, especially by selling stocks or property, or the money obtained from such a sale
conversion of assets into cash; money obtained from sale
legal

Examples

The company's realization of its remaining assets helped pay off its debts.
After the realization of the estate, the creditors were finally paid.
A sudden market downturn forced the realization of several large stock holdings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liquidation - the process of dissolving a business and distributing its assets

Etymology

From late Latin 'realizatio', from 'realizare' (to make real).

6. NOUN

/ ˌriːəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɪəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making something real or giving it the appearance of being real
making something real or seeming real

Examples

Her realization of the character brought the story to life for the audience.
The sculptor's work is a remarkable realization of ancient myths.
Through detailed models, the architect achieved a striking realization of his vision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: actualization - the process of making something actual or real
  • Antonym: fantasy - the faculty or activity of imagining things, especially things that are impossible or improbable

Etymology

From Latin 'realis' (real) + '-ization' (forming nouns).
