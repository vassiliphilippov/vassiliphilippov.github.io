PFC

1. NOUN

/ pi ɛf si / (AmE), / piː ɛf siː / (BrE)
a perfluorocarbon; a powerful greenhouse gas emitted during aluminum production and some industrial processes
a greenhouse gas (perfluorocarbon)

Examples

The aluminum industry is working to lower its PFC emissions to reduce greenhouse gases.
Scientists measure PFC concentrations in the atmosphere to monitor climate change.
Reducing industrial PFC output is a key step toward environmental sustainability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perfluorocarbon - a compound of carbon and fluorine, often used refrigerant or greenhouse gas

Etymology

Acronym of 'perfluorocarbon'.
