Leucothoe racemosa

1. NOUN

/ luːˈkɑːθoi ˌræsɪˈmoʊsə / (AmE), / luːˈkɒθəʊi ˌræsɪˈməʊsə / (BrE)
A bushy deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States, with long clusters of pinkish flowers.
shrubs with pinkish flower clusters, eastern US

Examples

The garden featured several Leucothoe racemosa shrubs, which provided color in early spring.
Botanists studied the blooming pattern of Leucothoe racemosa across different habitats.
Many landscapers recommend Leucothoe racemosa for its attractive foliage and flowers.

Etymology

From Greek 'Leucothoe', a mythological figure, and Latin 'racemosa' meaning 'having racemes'.
