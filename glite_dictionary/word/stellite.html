Stellite

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɛlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈstɛl.aɪt / (BrE)
a very hard alloy mainly composed of cobalt and chromium, used for making cutting tools and surfaces subject to heavy wear
hard alloy of cobalt and chromium

Examples

The drill bit is made from Stellite to increase its durability and resistance to wear.
Dentists often use tools constructed of Stellite because of its toughness and anti-corrosive properties.
After years of heavy use, the Stellite blade still retained its sharp edge.

Etymology

Trade name coined in the early 20th century, possibly from 'stell-' (Latin 'stella', meaning 'star') for its luster, plus suffix '-ite'.
