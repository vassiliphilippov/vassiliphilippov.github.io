territorialization

1. NOUN

/ təˌrɪtɔːriəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / təˌrɪtəriəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or act of organizing an area as a territory, especially for administrative or political purposes.
process of organizing as a territory

Examples

The government's territorialization of the region resulted in the creation of new administrative districts.
Urban development often involves the territorialization of previously unregulated spaces.
Debates about territorialization arise when indigenous lands are formalized by national authorities.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: deterritorialization - the process of undoing territorial organization

Etymology

From 'territory' + '-alization', patterned after French 'territorialisation'.
