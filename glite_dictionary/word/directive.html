directive

1. ADJ

/ dəˈrɛktɪv, daɪˈrɛktɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛktɪv, daɪˈrɛktɪv / (BrE)
giving guidance or direction; showing the way by leading or guiding others
giving guidance or direction

Examples

His directive approach helped the team achieve its goals efficiently.
Teachers can choose a more directive role in the classroom or encourage student independence.
She placed a directive hand on his shoulder, gently guiding him forward.

Synset Examples

felt his mother's directing arm around him
the directional role of science on industrial progress

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guiding - providing guidance
  • Synonym: directive (noun) - an official instruction or order

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'directivus', from Latin 'dirigere' (to direct).

2. NOUN

/ dəˈrɛktɪv, daɪˈrɛktɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛktɪv, daɪˈrɛktɪv / (BrE)
an official instruction or order given by an authority
official instruction or order

Examples

The manager issued a new directive regarding workplace safety protocols.
All staff must comply with the latest directive from the health department.
Failure to follow the directive may result in disciplinary action.

Synset Examples

the boss loves to send us directives

Related Terms

  • Synonym: order - an authoritative command or instruction
  • Synonym: command - an instruction given by someone in authority
  • Antonym: request - an act of asking politely or formally for something

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'directivus', from Latin 'dirigere' (to direct).
