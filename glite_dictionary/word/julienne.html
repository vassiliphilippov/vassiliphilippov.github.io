julienne

1. NOUN

/ dʒuˈliːɛn / (AmE), / ˌʒuː.liˈɛn / (BrE)
A vegetable cut into thin, matchstick-like strips, commonly used in salads, soups, or as a garnish.
vegetable cut into thin strips

Examples

The chef added julienne carrots to the salad for extra flavor and texture.
Please cut the zucchini into julienne before adding them to the stir-fry.
Garnish the soup with a small mound of julienne leeks for a professional touch.

Etymology

From French 'julienne', originally denoting a soup with such vegetables, feminine form of the name Julien.

2. VERB

/ dʒuˈliːɛn / (AmE), / ˌʒuː.liˈen / (BrE)
to cut (food, especially vegetables) into long, thin strips
cut food into thin strips

Examples

To make the salad look attractive, julienne the carrots and arrange them on top.
The chef demonstrated how to julienne a bell pepper with precision.
She quickly julienned the vegetables for the stir-fry.

Synset Examples

julienne the potatoes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shred - to cut or tear something into thin strips; similar culinary technique

Etymology

From French 'julienne', meaning a preparation of vegetables cut into thin strips.
