invincibility

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌvɪnsəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪnˌvɪnsɪˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being impossible to defeat or overcome
quality of being impossible to defeat

Examples

The team's sense of invincibility faded after their surprising defeat.
Legends are filled with tales of heroes who possess invincibility in battle.
Some children believe in their own invincibility and take unnecessary risks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invincible - impossible to defeat or overcome
  • Antonym: vulnerability - the quality of being easily harmed or attacked

Etymology

From Latin 'invincibilis' (unconquerable) + '-ity' (noun suffix).
