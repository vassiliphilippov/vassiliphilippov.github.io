Polypodium vulgare

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑː.lɪˈpoʊ.di.əm vəlˈɡɛr.i / (AmE), / ˌpɒ.lɪˈpəʊ.di.əm vʌlˈɡɛə.ri / (BrE)
A species of fern with creeping rootstocks and divided fronds, found across North America, Europe, Africa, and East Asia.
species of mat-forming fern

Examples

Polypodium vulgare thrives in rocky or shady woodland environments.
Gardeners often use Polypodium vulgare for ground cover in shaded areas.
The fronds of Polypodium vulgare have a distinctive, feather-like appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fern - a non-flowering vascular plant with feathery leaves

Etymology

From Latin: 'polypodium' meaning 'many-footed' (referring to the rhizome), 'vulgare' meaning 'common'.
