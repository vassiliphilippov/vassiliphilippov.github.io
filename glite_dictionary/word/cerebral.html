cerebral

1. ADJ

/ səˈriː.brəl / (AmE), / səˈriː.brəl / (BrE)
Relating to the cerebrum or the brain.
relating to the brain
medical

Examples

The neurologist studied the cerebral cortex to understand the patient's symptoms.
A stroke can cause cerebral damage and lead to loss of function.
Research is ongoing into the effects of aging on cerebral blood flow.

Synset Examples

cerebral hemisphere
cerebral activity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brain - the organ in the head that controls thought, memory, feelings, and activity

Etymology

From Latin 'cerebrum' (brain).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02699711-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ səˈriː.brəl / (AmE), / səˈriː.brəl / (BrE)
Involving or requiring the use of the intellect rather than emotions or instinct.
intellectual or brainy

Examples

Her cerebral style of writing appeals most to readers who enjoy thoughtful analysis.
The debate became more cerebral as they shifted from personal stories to abstract theory.
He prefers cerebral films that challenge the mind instead of providing simple entertainment.

Synset Examples

a cerebral approach to the problem
cerebral drama

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intellectual - relating to the intellect
  • Antonym: emotional - involving emotion rather than intellect

Etymology

From Latin 'cerebrum' (brain), referencing the part of the brain associated with thought.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01933854-a
Synset POS: a
