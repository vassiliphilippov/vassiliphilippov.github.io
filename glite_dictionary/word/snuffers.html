snuffers

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnʌfərz / (AmE), / ˈsnʌfəz / (BrE)
A pair of scissors-like instruments used to trim and hold the burnt part of a candlewick.
instrument for trimming candlewicks
archaic

Examples

The butler used the snuffers to trim the candlewick during dinner.
Antique snuffers are now collectors' items found in museums.
She inherited a silver pair of snuffers from her grandmother.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candle snuffer - a tool used to extinguish the flame of a candle

Etymology

From verb 'snuff' (to trim a candlewick), from Middle English 'snuffen'.
