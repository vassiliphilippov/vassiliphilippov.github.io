taproot

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæpˌruːt / (AmE), / ˈtapˌruːt / (BrE)
an important central source or origin from which growth or development springs, especially in a figurative sense
central source or origin (figurative)

Examples

The taproot of the company’s success lies in its commitment to innovation.
Her passion for music served as the taproot of her lifelong career.
A family’s values often form the taproot of a child’s character.

Synset Examples

the taproot of his resentment
genius and insanity spring from the same taproot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: root - the origin or basis of something

Etymology

From 'taproot', the main root of a plant, used figuratively for something that is a central source.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtæpˌruːt / (AmE), / ˈtapˌruːt / (BrE)
The main root of a plant that grows vertically downward and gives off smaller lateral roots.
main root growing downward (botany)
medical

Examples

The carrot is an example of a plant with a thick taproot.
When the taproot is damaged, the tree may not grow as tall.
Many desert plants develop a deep taproot to reach underground water sources.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fibrous root - root system with many thin roots, no main root

Etymology

Formed from 'tap' (plug, spout) + 'root', likening the main root to a tap or stopper.

Source: WordNet
