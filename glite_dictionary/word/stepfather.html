stepfather

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɛpˌfɑðɚ / (AmE), / ˈstɛpˌfɑːðə / (BrE)
the husband of one's mother by a marriage after the divorce or death of one's father
husband of one's mother by a later marriage

Examples

My stepfather taught me how to ride a bicycle when I was young.
After her mother remarried, she had to adjust to living with a stepfather.
He gets along well with his stepfather despite their differences.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stepmother - the wife of one's father by a subsequent marriage

Etymology

From Old English 'steop-' (bereaved) + 'fæder' (father).
