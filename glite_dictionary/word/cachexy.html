cachexy

1. NOUN

/ kəˈkɛksi / (AmE), / kəˈkɛksi / (BrE)
A general reduction in vitality and strength of body and mind, often resulting from a debilitating chronic disease.
wasting of body due to chronic illness
medical

Examples

The patient showed signs of cachexy after several months of battling the disease.
Doctors were concerned about the rapid development of cachexy in the elderly resident.
Prolonged infection led to progressive cachexy and weakness despite nutritional support.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cachexia - wasting syndrome; severe loss of weight and muscle due to illness

Etymology

From Greek *kakhexia* (bad condition), from *kakos* (bad) + *hexis* (habit, state).
