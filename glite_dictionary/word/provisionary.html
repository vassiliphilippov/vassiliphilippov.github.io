provisionary

1. ADJ

/ prəˈvɪʒəˌnɛri / (AmE), / prəˈvɪʒənəri / (BrE)
not final; intended to be temporary or subject to further confirmation or approval
not final; temporary or provisional

Examples

The company's offer was purely provisionary and subject to change pending board approval.
She received a provisionary position while the hiring process continued.
The agreement is only provisionary until all parties have signed.

Synset Examples

probationary employees
a provisional government
just a tentative schedule

Related Terms

  • Synonym: provisional - intended to last for a limited time; temporary

Etymology

Formed from 'provision' + '-ary', modeled after 'provisional'
