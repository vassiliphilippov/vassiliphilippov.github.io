to tongue-tie

1. VERB

/ ˈtʌŋˌtaɪ / (AmE), / ˈtʌŋ.taɪ / (BrE)
to cause someone to be unable to speak easily or at all, typically due to nervousness, shyness, or surprise
to make someone unable to speak

Examples

The difficult question seemed to tongue-tie even the most experienced speaker.
Nervousness can tongue-tie a person during important interviews.
The teacher's harsh comments tongue-tied the shy student for the rest of the class.

Synset Examples

When he met his idol, the young man was tongue-tied

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silence - to make someone stop speaking or become quiet

Etymology

From the noun 'tongue-tie', originally referring to a physical condition restricting tongue movement.
