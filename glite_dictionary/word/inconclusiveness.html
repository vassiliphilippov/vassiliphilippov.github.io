inconclusiveness

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.kənˈkluː.sɪv.nəs / (AmE), / ˌɪn.kənˈkluː.sɪv.nəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of not leading to a definite or final result or decision
the quality of lacking a final result

Examples

The inconclusiveness of the study has left many experts demanding further research.
Political debates are sometimes plagued by inconclusiveness, frustrating those seeking clear answers.
He grew frustrated with the inconclusiveness of the investigation into the missing documents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inconclusive - not leading to a definite result or conclusion
  • Antonym: certainty - the quality of being certain or definite

Etymology

From 'inconclusive' + '-ness'. 'Inconclusive' derives from Late Latin 'inconclusivus'.
