buirdly

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɪrdli / (AmE), / ˈbɪədli / (BrE)
Muscular, sturdy, and heavily built; strong and robust in appearance (chiefly Scottish).
muscular and strongly built (Scottish)
british

Examples

The buirdly lad carried the heavy sacks with ease.
She admired the buirdly men competing in the Highland Games.
His buirdly appearance made him stand out in the crowd.

Synset Examples

a beefy wrestler
had a tall burly frame
clothing sizes for husky boys
a strapping boy of eighteen
‘buirdly’ is a Scottish term

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burly - large and heavily built
  • Synonym: strapping - tall, strong, and healthy
  • Antonym: slight - small and thin rather than strong or muscular

Etymology

From Scots, likely related to Scots 'buird' (table, in the sense of broadness) and influenced by Middle English.
