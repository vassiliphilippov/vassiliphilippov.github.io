lookout

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʊkˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈlʊkˌaʊt / (BrE)
a place, often elevated, from which a wide view is possible for purposes of observation or watchkeeping
elevated place for observing

Examples

The hikers stopped at the lookout to admire the breathtaking scenery below.
Park rangers use the mountain lookout during fire season to spot any signs of smoke.
From the wooden lookout, you could see for miles across the valley.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watchtower - a tower built to create an elevated observation point

Etymology

Compound of 'look' + 'out', dating from the late 17th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlʊk.aʊt / (AmE), / ˈlʊk.aʊt / (BrE)
a structure or place built to provide a wide view of the surrounding area, typically used for observation or keeping watch
observation structure with a wide view

Examples

We climbed to the top of the lookout to get a better view of the valley.
The park rangers built a wooden lookout at the highest point for fire spotting.
Hikers rested at the lookout, enjoying the panoramic scenery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watchtower - a tall structure built to observe or guard an area

Etymology

From the verb phrase 'look out', dating to the late 17th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈlʊk.aʊt / (AmE), / ˈlʊk.aʊt / (BrE)
a person whose job is to watch for something expected or warn others of danger or trouble
person who keeps watch for something

Examples

The gang posted a lookout at the corner to warn them of approaching police.
During the night, a lookout was stationed by the campfire to alert the group to any danger.
A sharp-eyed lookout spotted the ship on the horizon hours before anyone else.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sentinel - a person or thing that stands guard or watches
  • Synonym: watchman - a person who keeps guard or watches, especially at night

Etymology

From the phrase 'look out', referring to the act of keeping watch.

4. NOUN

/ ˈlʊkˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈlʊk.aʊt / (BrE)
the act of carefully observing something or keeping watch for danger
the act of observing or watching out

Examples

The sailor maintained a vigilant lookout throughout the night to ensure the ship stayed on course.
Good lookout is essential when hiking in areas where wildlife may appear unexpectedly.
She kept a careful lookout for her friend in the crowded station.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watch - the act of paying close attention to something
  • Antonym: neglect - the act of not paying attention or failing to watch carefully

Etymology

From the phrase 'look out,' dating to the early 18th century.
