Belemnitidae

1. NOUN

/ bɛlˌɛmˈnɪtɪˌdi / (AmE), / bɪˌlɛmˈnɪtɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of extinct cephalopods from the Mesozoic era, characterized by elongated, bullet-shaped internal shells.
family of extinct cephalopods

Examples

Fossils of Belemnitidae provide valuable insights into the marine life of the Jurassic period.
Scientists have classified several genera within the family Belemnitidae.
The Belemnitidae thrived in the oceans long before the first dinosaurs appeared.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: belemnite - an extinct group of squid-like cephalopods with bullet-shaped shells

Etymology

From New Latin, based on 'Belemnites', the type genus, and the suffix '-idae' used to form family names in zoological taxonomy.
