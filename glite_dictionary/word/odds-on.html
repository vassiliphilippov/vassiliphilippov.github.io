odds-on

1. ADJ

/ ɑdz ˈɑn / (AmE), / ɒdz ˈɒn / (BrE)
Very likely to succeed or win, especially having a greater than even chance; considered most probable.
very likely to succeed or win

Examples

She is the odds-on favorite to win the championship this year.
With his experience, it's odds-on that he'll get the promotion.
The film became the odds-on choice for the Best Picture award.

Synset Examples

the odds-on favorite

Related Terms

  • Synonym: favorite - person or thing most likely to win or succeed
  • Antonym: outsider - competitor with little chance of winning

Etymology

From betting terminology, referring to odds in favor of a competitor, indicating a higher probability of winning.

2. ADJ

/ ɑdz ˈɑn / (AmE), / ɒdz ˈɒn / (BrE)
very likely to happen or to be true; having a high probability, especially used in betting to indicate that the odds favor an outcome
very likely to happen

Examples

The weather forecast says it's odds-on we'll have rain this afternoon.
She was the odds-on favorite to win the tournament after her impressive performance in the semifinals.
It's odds-on that the new policy will face strong opposition from the public.

Synset Examples

It’s odds-on that they’ll announce the winner tonight.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unlikely - not likely to happen
  • Synonym: probable - likely to happen or be true
  • Degree Lower: possible - that may happen or be the case

Etymology

From the phrase 'odds on', originating in betting to indicate odds that favor a particular outcome.
