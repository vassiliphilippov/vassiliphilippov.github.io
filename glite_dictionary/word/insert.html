insert

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsɝt / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɜːt / (BrE)
A still image or a brief shot introduced into a film sequence that interrupts the main action, often to provide additional information or emphasis.
brief inserted shot in a film

Examples

The director decided to add an insert of the ticking clock for suspense.
An insert showing the letter's contents helped the audience follow the plot.
During editing, several inserts were placed to clarify details for viewers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cutaway - a shot that briefly interrupts the main action in a film

Etymology

From Latin insertus, past participle of inserere (to put in), via French insérer.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
