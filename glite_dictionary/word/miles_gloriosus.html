miles gloriosus

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪliːz ɡləˈrioʊsəs / (AmE), / ˈmiːleɪz ɡlɒrɪˈəʊsəs / (BrE)
A braggart or boastful soldier, especially a stock character in classical comedy who exaggerates his exploits.
boastful soldier archetype in comedy

Examples

In his performance, the actor parodied the miles gloriosus with exaggerated swagger and bravado.
The miles gloriosus is a classic figure in Roman comedy, always boasting of imaginary conquests.
Her play features a modern take on the miles gloriosus, casting him as an arrogant security guard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: braggart - a person who boasts about achievements or possessions

Etymology

Latin, literally ‘boastful soldier’; from Plautus's play ‘Miles Gloriosus’.
