penitential

1. ADJ

/ ˌpɛnɪˈtɛnʃəl / (AmE), / ˌpɛnɪˈtɛnʃəl / (BrE)
showing or expressing sorrow and regret for having done wrong; relating to or constituting penance
expressing penance or remorse

Examples

He wrote a penitential letter asking for forgiveness after his harsh remarks.
The atmosphere in the chapel was quiet and almost penitential during the service.
She shed penitential tears when confronted with the consequences of her actions.

Synset Examples

penitential tears
wrote a penitential letter apologizing for her hasty words

Related Terms

  • Synonym: penitent - feeling or showing sorrow and regret for having done wrong

Etymology

From Medieval Latin poenitentialis, from Latin poenitentia (repentance).
