breezy

1. ADJ

/ ˈbriːzi / (AmE), / ˈbriːzi / (BrE)
cheerful, lively, and refreshing in manner or style; having an easy and animated disposition.
cheerful and lively in manner

Examples

Her breezy manner made everyone feel at ease during the interview.
He delivered the presentation in a breezy and confident style.
The author's breezy writing makes even complex topics seem accessible.

Synset Examples

her breezy nature

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic; synonym for cheerful manner
  • Synonym: lively - full of life and energy; synonym for animated behavior

Etymology

From 'breeze' (a light wind) + '-y', originally meaning 'windy' but later extended metaphorically to liveliness of character.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbriːzi / (AmE), / ˈbriːzi / (BrE)
(of a place or weather) having a lot of light wind or breezes; exposed to the wind.
characterized by light wind or breezes

Examples

The breezy coastline made it a perfect place to fly a kite.
We enjoyed a breezy afternoon sitting on the porch.
It was too breezy for a picnic in the park that day.

Synset Examples

blowy weather
a windy bluff

Related Terms

  • Synonym: windy - characterized by or exposed to strong wind
  • Antonym: calm - characterized by little or no wind

Etymology

From breeze (light wind) + -y (adjectival suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00306024-s
Synset POS: s
