Macrotus californicus

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæk.roʊˈtʌs ˌkæl.ɪˈfɔr.nɪ.kəs / (AmE), / ˌmæk.rəʊˈtʌs ˌkæl.ɪˈfɔː.nɪ.kəs / (BrE)
A large-eared greyish bat native to southern California and northwestern Mexico.
large-eared bat of California and Mexico

Examples

The Macrotus californicus is easily recognized by its oversized ears and greyish fur.
Researchers in Baja California are studying the habitat of Macrotus californicus to help conserve its population.
Unlike most other bats in the region, the Macrotus californicus prefers rocky desert areas.

Etymology

From Latin: 'macrotus' meaning 'large-eared' and 'californicus' referring to California.
