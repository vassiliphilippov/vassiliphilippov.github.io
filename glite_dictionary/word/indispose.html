to indispose

1. VERB

/ ˌɪndɪsˈpoʊz / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪsˈpəʊz / (BrE)
to make someone unfit or unsuitable for something; to render unable or less able to do something
make unfit or unsuitable

Examples

A sudden illness can indispose her from attending the conference.
The long journey may indispose them to travel any farther today.
Financial troubles could indispose him for the responsibilities of leadership.

Synset Examples

Your income disqualifies you

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disqualify - to make ineligible or unsuitable for something

Etymology

From Latin *indispositus*, meaning 'not disposed, unprepared.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00302119-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˌɪndɪˈspoʊz / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈspəʊz / (BrE)
to make someone unwilling or disinclined to do something
make unwilling; disincline

Examples

They feared that the constant criticism would indispose him toward cooperation.
Her illness tended to indispose her to attend social gatherings.
The recent scandals may indispose investors to trust the company.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disincline - to make unwilling or reluctant
  • Antonym: to encourage - to give someone support or confidence to do something

Etymology

From French 'indisposer' and Medieval Latin 'indisponere' (to disarrange).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00683044-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˌɪndɪˈspoʊz / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈspəʊz / (BrE)
to make someone feel slightly ill or unwell; to disturb someone's health or ability to carry out activities
cause to feel unwell

Examples

A mild cold may indispose her and keep her from work for a day.
An overheated room can indispose many people and affect their concentration.
Heavy meals late at night sometimes indispose me the next morning.

Synset Examples

She was indisposed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disable - make unable to do something, often due to illness or injury

Etymology

From Latin 'indispositus' (disordered, unprepared), from 'in-' (not) + 'dispositus' (arranged).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00064660-v
Synset POS: v
