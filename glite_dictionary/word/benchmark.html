benchmark

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛn(t)ʃˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈbɛntʃˌmɑːk / (BrE)
a surveyor's mark made on a stationary object, indicating a known position and elevation and used as a reference point for measurements
surveyor's reference mark

Examples

The engineer used the benchmark on the bridge to determine the correct elevation for the new road.
Surveyors placed a benchmark near the old church to serve as a reference point for all future mapping.
Accurate measurements depend on starting from an established benchmark in the area.

Etymology

From 'bench' + 'mark', originally referring to a chiseled horizontal mark made in stone or other fixed object for surveyors' use.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɛn(t)ʃˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈbɛn(t)ʃˌmɑːk / (BrE)
a standard or point of reference against which things may be compared or assessed
standard for comparison

Examples

This new software sets a benchmark for ease of use in the industry.
Her test results served as a benchmark for the rest of the class.
The company uses international standards as its benchmark for quality control.

Synset Examples

his painting sets the benchmark of quality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yardstick - a standard used for comparison
  • Synonym: standard - an accepted model or level for comparison

Etymology

Originally from surveyor's term, from 'bench' (support) + 'mark'; meaning of standard since late 19th century.
