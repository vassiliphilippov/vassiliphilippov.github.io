sulfonate

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌl.fəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈsʌl.fə.neɪt / (BrE)
a salt or ester of a sulfonic acid, commonly used in chemistry and industrial processes
salt or ester of sulfonic acid

Examples

The detergent contains a type of sulfonate for improved cleaning performance.
During the reaction, the acid is converted into its sulfonate form.
Researchers studied the properties of this new sulfonate compound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sulfate - a salt or ester of sulfuric acid

Etymology

From sulfonic (acid) + -ate (denoting salt or ester)
