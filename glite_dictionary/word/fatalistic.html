fatalistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌfeɪtəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌfeɪtəˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
believing that all events are predetermined and inevitable, and that human actions cannot change the outcome; relating to fatalism
believing events are inevitable

Examples

In a fatalistic view, nothing you do can change your fate.
Her fatalistic attitude made it hard for her to plan for the future.
People became increasingly fatalistic after repeated natural disasters struck the region.

Synset Examples

a fatalist person
fatalistic thinking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resigned - accepting something as inevitable without resistance
  • Antonym: optimistic - believing the best will happen; confident about the future

Etymology

Formed from 'fatalism' (from Latin 'fatalis', meaning 'decreed by fate') plus the suffix '-ic'.
