palaeology

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪliˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌpæliˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the scientific study of ancient objects, especially those from prehistoric times
study of ancient, especially prehistoric, antiquities

Examples

Her passion for palaeology led her to uncover several important prehistoric artifacts.
Many universities offer courses in palaeology as part of their archaeology departments.
Advances in technology have greatly aided research in the field of palaeology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archaeology - the study of human history and prehistory through excavation of sites and analysis of artifacts

Etymology

From Greek 'palaios' (ancient) and 'logia' (study).
