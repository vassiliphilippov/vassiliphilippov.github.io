assistance

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɪstəns / (AmE), / əˈsɪstəns / (BrE)
the action of helping someone by providing support or resources to achieve a goal or meet a need
help or support given

Examples

The company offered assistance to customers affected by the outage.
Thanks to her assistance, I was able to finish the project on time.
Medical assistance arrived within minutes of the accident.

Synset Examples

he gave me an assist with the housework
could not walk without assistance
rescue party went to their aid
offered his help in unloading

Related Terms

  • Synonym: help - the act of assisting or aiding someone
  • Synonym: aid - assistance such as money or supplies
  • Antonym: obstruction - the act of hindering or preventing assistance

Etymology

From Old French 'assistance', from Latin 'assistentia' (an attending upon).

2. NOUN

/ əˈsɪstəns / (AmE), / əˈsɪstəns / (BrE)
the action of helping someone by making a task easier or possible; something that provides support or aid
help or support in doing something

Examples

The team provided vital assistance to the rescue workers during the flood.
She was grateful for his assistance in organizing the event.
Without financial assistance, many students would not be able to attend college.

Synset Examples

visual aids in teaching
his job was to give technical assistance over the phone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: help - the act of making it easier for someone to do something
  • Synonym: aid - assistance, such as money or supplies, provided to people in need
  • Antonym: obstruction - something that blocks or hinders progress

Etymology

From Latin 'assistentia' (attendance, presence), from 'assistere' (to stand by).

3. NOUN

/ əˈsɪstəns / (AmE), / əˈsɪstəns / (BrE)
help in the form of money or other material support given to a person or cause.
help or support, especially financial

Examples

The government provided assistance to families affected by the flood.
Many charities rely on donations and assistance from volunteers.
She applied for financial assistance to help pay her tuition fees.

Synset Examples

economic assistance to depressed areas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aid - help or support, especially in the form of money or resources
  • Synonym: support - financial or material help for a person or cause
  • Antonym: hindrance - something that provides resistance or obstruction

Etymology

From Old French 'assistance', from Latin 'assistentia' (attendance, presence).
