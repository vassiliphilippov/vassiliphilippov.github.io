Ebionite

1. ADJ

/ ˈiː.bi.əˌnaɪt / (AmE), / ˈiː.bɪ.ə.naɪt / (BrE)
of or relating to the Ebionites, an early Jewish-Christian sect and their religious practices or beliefs.
relating to the Ebionites or their religion

Examples

Some scholars have analyzed the Ebionite interpretation of Jesus and its differences from mainstream Christianity.
The Ebionite texts emphasized adherence to Jewish law and traditions.
Historians are fascinated by the beliefs of various Ebionite communities in the early centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ebionite - a member of the early Jewish-Christian sect called the Ebionites

Etymology

From Early Christian Latin Ebionitae, from Greek Ebionitai, from Hebrew ebyōnīm (the poor).

2. NOUN

/ ˈiː.bi.əˌnaɪt / (AmE), / ˈiː.bɪ.ə.naɪt / (BrE)
a member of an early Jewish Christian group who accepted Jesus as the Messiah, adhered to Jewish law, and rejected Pauline epistles, later deemed heretical by the Church.
member of an early Jewish Christian sect

Examples

Several ancient texts describe the beliefs of the Ebionites in detail.
The Ebionites maintained strict observance of Jewish law while accepting Jesus as the Messiah.
Modern scholars have debated the historical influence of the Ebionites on early Christianity.

Etymology

From Hebrew 'ebyōnīm' (the poor), by way of Greek 'Ebionitai'.

Synset POS: n
