postgraduate

1. ADJ

/ poʊstˈɡrædʒuət / (AmE), / pəʊstˈɡrædʒuət / (BrE)
relating to studies undertaken after earning a bachelor's degree, especially at a university or college
relating to education after a bachelor's degree

Examples

He is enrolled in a postgraduate program in molecular biology.
The university offers several postgraduate courses in law and engineering.
After completing my bachelor's, I'm considering postgraduate studies abroad.

Synset Examples

graduate courses

Related Terms

  • Antonym: graduate - relating to studies undertaken to earn a first university degree

Etymology

From Latin 'post-' (after) + 'graduatus' (graduated).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01214337-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊstˈɡrædʒuət / (AmE), / ˌpəʊstˈɡrædʒuət / (BrE)
a student who is studying for a degree or other qualification after having completed their first degree
a student studying after a first degree

Examples

After receiving her bachelor's degree, she became a postgraduate in environmental science.
Many postgraduate students are involved in groundbreaking research at the university.
He is a postgraduate looking to specialize in biomedical engineering.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: undergraduate - a university student who has not yet received a first degree

Etymology

From post- (meaning 'after') + graduate (one who has completed a degree).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10160629-n
Synset POS: n
