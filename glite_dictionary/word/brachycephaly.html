brachycephaly

1. NOUN

/ ˌbrækiˈsɛfəli / (AmE), / ˌbrækɪˈsɛfəli / (BrE)
A condition or quality in which the head is short and broad, having a cephalic index above 80.
short, broad head shape
medical

Examples

Brachycephaly is commonly seen in certain dog breeds like bulldogs and pugs.
The infant was diagnosed with mild brachycephaly due to premature fusion of cranial sutures.
Veterinarians monitor brachycephaly in cats to ensure their breathing is not adversely affected.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dolichocephaly - having a long, narrow head (low cephalic index)

Etymology

From Greek brakhus (short) + kephalē (head).
