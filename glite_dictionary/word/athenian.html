Athenian

1. NOUN

/ əˈθiniən / (AmE), / əˈθiːniən / (BrE)
a person who lives in or comes from Athens, the capital city of Greece
resident of Athens, Greece

Examples

As an Athenian, she took great pride in her city's rich history and culture.
The ancient Athenians are often credited with the development of democracy.
Many Athenians celebrate traditional festivals that date back centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Athens - the capital city of Greece

Etymology

From Latin Athenaeus, from Greek Athēnaios, from Athēnai (Athens).

2. ADJ

/ əˈθiniən / (AmE), / əˈθiːniən / (BrE)
of or relating to Athens or its inhabitants.
relating to Athens or its people

Examples

The ancient sculptures displayed an Athenian style that fascinated historians.
She admired the Athenian democracy for its influence on modern political systems.
The Athenian philosophers contributed greatly to Western thought.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Athens - the capital city of Greece

Etymology

From Latin Atheniensis, from Greek Athēnaios (‘of Athens’).
