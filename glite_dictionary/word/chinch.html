chinch

1. NOUN

/ tʃɪntʃ / (AmE), / tʃɪntʃ / (BrE)
a small bug found in temperate regions, especially known for infesting beds and feeding on human blood; a bedbug.
bedbug that infests beds and feeds on blood

Examples

The old inn was infested with chinches, making it hard for guests to sleep.
He discovered a chinch crawling on his bedsheet after returning from his trip.
Because chinches bite at night, visitors often woke up with itchy red marks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bedbug - an insect that feeds on blood and infests bedding

Etymology

From Spanish 'chinche' (bedbug), ultimately from Latin 'cimex.'
