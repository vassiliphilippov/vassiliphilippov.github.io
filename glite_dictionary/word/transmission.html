transmission

1. NOUN

/ trænzˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / trænzˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
the system of gears in a vehicle that transmits power from the engine to the wheels
system of gears transmitting engine power in vehicles

Examples

The mechanic replaced the faulty transmission to restore the car's performance.
Manual transmission vehicles offer more control over gear changes than automatics.
After 100,000 miles, her car's transmission started making strange noises.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gearbox - the mechanical casing containing the gears in a transmission system

Etymology

From Latin 'transmissio' (a sending across), from 'transmittere' (to send across).

2. NOUN

/ trænˈsmɪʃən / (AmE), / trænzˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of sending, passing on, or conveying something, especially a message or information, from one place or person to another
the act of sending something, especially a message

Examples

The transmission of confidential information over email must be done carefully.
Radio transmission was interrupted due to severe weather conditions.
Rapid transmission of news helped people prepare for the approaching storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transfer - the act of moving something from one place to another

Etymology

From Latin transmissio (a sending across), from transmittere (to send across).

Examples
