hemolysin

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈmɑːləsɪn / (AmE), / hɪˈməʊlɪsɪn / (BrE)
a substance that causes the destruction of red blood cells and the release of hemoglobin
substance that destroys red blood cells
medical

Examples

Some bacteria produce hemolysin as a way to attack red blood cells in the body.
The laboratory detected the presence of hemolysin in the patient's blood sample.
Doctors studied the effects of different hemolysins on animal tissues.

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) + -lysis (loosening, dissolving), via International Scientific Vocabulary.
