Drynaria

1. NOUN

/ draɪˈnɛəriə / (AmE), / draɪˈnɛəriə / (BrE)
a genus of large epiphytic ferns found in tropical forests and scrub in Africa, Asia, and Australia
a genus of tropical epiphytic ferns

Examples

Drynaria species are often cultivated for their attractive, oak-like fronds.
The Drynaria fern clings to the trunks of rainforest trees in tropical Asia.
Botanists recently discovered a new variety of Drynaria in northern Australia.

Etymology

From Greek 'dryas' (oak) + '-aria', referring to the oak-like leaves in some species.

2. NOUN

/ draɪˈnɛəriə / (AmE), / draɪˈnɛəriə / (BrE)
A genus of large, robust epiphytic ferns found in tropical forests and scrub, native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.
genus of large epiphytic ferns

Examples

The Drynaria genus includes several species known for their large and decorative fronds.
Botanists discovered a rare Drynaria growing high on the rainforest canopy.
Gardeners often cultivate Drynaria as ornamental plants for shaded outdoor spaces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fern - a nonflowering vascular plant with feathery leaves

Etymology

From Greek dryas (oak tree nymph), referencing the oak-like appearance of some species.
