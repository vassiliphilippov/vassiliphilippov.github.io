tutorial

1. ADJ

/ tuˈtɔriəl / (AmE), / tjuːˈtɔːriəl / (BrE)
relating to a tutor, tutoring, or instructional sessions designed to teach a particular subject or skill
relating to tutors or instruction sessions

Examples

She attended several tutorial classes to prepare for her exams.
The professor held a tutorial session to clarify difficult topics.
Many universities offer tutorial support for first-year students.

Synset Examples

tutorial sessions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: instructional - intended to teach or instruct
  • Synonym: didactic - intended to teach, particularly in a moral or instructive way

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'tutorialis', from Latin 'tutor' (protector, guardian).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03139103-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ tuˈtɔriəl, tjuˈtɔriəl / (AmE), / tjuːˈtɔː.ri.əl / (BrE)
a period of instruction given by a tutor to an individual or a small group, focusing on intensive learning or discussion
session of tuition with a tutor

Examples

Each student is expected to attend the weekly tutorial with their assigned tutor.
The professor scheduled an extra tutorial before the final exam to help us prepare.
I found the small-group tutorial much more helpful than the large lecture sessions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lecture - an educational talk to a large group

Etymology

From medieval Latin 'tutorialis', from 'tutor' (a guardian, teacher).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07162730-n
Synset POS: n
