frightfulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfraɪtfəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈfraɪtf(ʊ)lnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being frightful or causing great fear or horror
the state of being frightful

Examples

The frightfulness of the storm left the villagers seeking shelter for hours.
He could not shake off the frightfulness of the tragedy he had witnessed.
The novel captures the frightfulness of war with chilling detail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horror - a strong feeling of fear or shock
  • Antonym: delightfulness - the quality of being delightful

Etymology

From 'frightful' + '-ness', forming a noun indicating a state or quality.
