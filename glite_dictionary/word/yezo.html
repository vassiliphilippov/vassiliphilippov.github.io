Yezo

1. PROPN

/ ˈjeɪzoʊ / (AmE), / ˈjɛzəʊ / (BrE)
the former name for Hokkaido, the second largest of the four main islands of Japan, located north of Honshu
former name for Hokkaido, Japanese island

Examples

In the 19th century, explorers traveled to Yezo to map its rugged landscape.
Some older maps of Japan still label Hokkaido as Yezo.
The native Ainu people have long lived on the island once called Yezo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hokkaido - the northernmost of Japan's main islands, formerly called Yezo

Etymology

From Japanese 蝦夷 (Ezo), historically used for Hokkaido and surrounding regions.
