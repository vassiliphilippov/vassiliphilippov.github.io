Titanosaurus

1. PROPN

/ taɪˌtænəˈsɔːrəs / (AmE), / taɪˌtænəˈsɔːrəs / (BrE)
A genus of large, herbivorous dinosaurs that lived during the Cretaceous period in South America.
genus of Cretaceous herbivorous dinosaurs

Examples

The fossilized bones of Titanosaurus were discovered in Argentina by paleontologists.
Many scientists believe that Titanosaurus was one of the largest land animals of its time.
Children at the museum were fascinated by the life-sized model of Titanosaurus.

Etymology

From Greek 'titan' (giant) + 'sauros' (lizard).

2. NOUN

/ taɪˌtænəˈsɔrəs / (AmE), / taɪˌtænəˈsɔːrəs / (BrE)
A genus of large herbivorous sauropod dinosaurs that lived during the Cretaceous period in South America.
genus of Cretaceous herbivorous dinosaurs

Examples

Fossils of Titanosaurus have been found in various regions of South America.

The Titanosaurus was one of the giant sauropods that grazed on prehistoric vegetation.

Scientists believe Titanosaurus could reach lengths of up to 12 meters.

Etymology

From Greek 'Titan' (giant) + 'sauros' (lizard).
