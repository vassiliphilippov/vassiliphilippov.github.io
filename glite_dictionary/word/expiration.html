expiration

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛkspəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛkspəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the ending of a period for which something, such as a contract or document, is valid
end of validity period (contract, document)

Examples

You need to renew your passport before its expiration.
The expiration of the lease means we have to move out next month.
Make sure you check the expiration date on all your food products.

Synset Examples

the expiry of his driver's license

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expiry - the deadline or point when something legally ends or ceases to be valid
  • Antonym: renewal - the process of extending the validity of a contract or document

Etymology

From Latin 'expiratio' (exhalation, expiration), from 'expirare' (to expire).

A formal or euphemistic term for death; the act of dying.
euphemism for death

3. NOUN

/ ˌɛkspəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛkspɪˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of breathing out air from the lungs
breathing out; exhalation
medical

Examples

The doctor measured the patient's expiration to assess lung function.
During expiration, the diaphragm relaxes and air is expelled from the lungs.
Rapid expiration can indicate respiratory distress.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inspiration - the act of drawing air into the lungs
  • Synonym: exhalation - the act of breathing out

Etymology

From Latin expiratio (exhalation), from exspirare (to breathe out).
