fa

1. NOUN

/ fɑ / (AmE), / fɑː / (BrE)
the syllable used to name the fourth note (subdominant) of the diatonic scale in solfège.
musical syllable (fourth note in scale)

Examples

The choir practiced singing fa as part of their warm-up exercises.
In the key of C major, fa corresponds to the note F.
She learned to identify fa on the piano during her music lesson.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: sol - the syllable naming the fifth note of the diatonic scale in solfège
  • Degree Lower: mi - the syllable naming the third note of the diatonic scale in solfège

Etymology

From Italian 'fa', used in solfège to name the fourth note of the scale.
