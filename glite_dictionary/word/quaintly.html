quaintly

1. ADV

/ ˈkweɪntli / (AmE), / ˈkweɪntli / (BrE)
In a manner that is attractively unusual, old-fashioned, or charmingly picturesque.
in an attractively old-fashioned way

Examples

The cottage was quaintly decorated with antique furnishings and lace curtains.
She smiled quaintly at the old tradition, finding it charming.
A quaintly painted sign greeted visitors at the entrance to the village.

Synset Examples

the room was quaintly furnished

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quaint - attractively unusual or old-fashioned

2. ADV

/ ˈkweɪntli / (AmE), / ˈkweɪntli / (BrE)
in a manner that is attractively unusual or old-fashioned; in a strange but not unpleasant way
in an attractively odd or old-fashioned way

Examples

The cottage was decorated quaintly with vintage teacups and lace curtains.
She smiled quaintly, reminding him of someone from another era.
He described the tradition quaintly, adding a touch of nostalgia to his words.

Synset Examples

the old lady expressed herself somewhat quaintly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oddly - in a way that is strange or unusual
  • Synonym: charmingly - in a way that is pleasant and attractive

Etymology

Formed from 'quaint' (Middle English from Old French 'cointe', meaning clever, cunning, or unique) + '-ly' adverbial suffix.
