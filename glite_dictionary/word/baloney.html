baloney

1. NOUN

/ bəˈloʊni / (AmE), / bəˈləʊni / (BrE)
foolish or insincere talk; nonsense
nonsense; silly talk
informal american

Examples

Don't listen to his baloney; he just likes to impress people with big words.
That politician's promises turned out to be pure baloney.
She laughed off the rumors as complete baloney.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonsense - speech or writing that has no meaning or that is silly
  • Antonym: sense - a sensible or reasonable statement, opposite of nonsense

Etymology

Probably from alteration of 'bologna', as in the sausage, humorously extended to mean nonsense in American slang.
