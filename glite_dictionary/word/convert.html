2. NOUN

/ kənˈvɝt / (AmE), / ˈkɒnvɜːt / (BrE)
a person who has adopted a new religion, belief, or opinion, especially having changed from one to another
person who has changed religion or belief

Examples

After years of searching, he became a convert to Buddhism.
The new party attracted many converts from the opposition.
As a recent convert, she was eager to share her experiences with others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proselyte - a person who has converted from one religion or opinion to another
  • Antonym: apostate - a person who renounces a religious or political belief or principle

Etymology

From Old French *convertir*, from Latin *convertere* (to turn around, transform).
