blackened

1. ADJ

/ ˈblækənd / (AmE), / ˈblækənd / (BrE)
(of the face) darkened in color, especially as a result of suffused blood from strong emotion, injury, or other cause.
face darkened by blood, injury, or emotion

Examples

After the accident, his blackened eye was clearly visible to everyone.
Her face became blackened with rage as the argument escalated.
He wore sunglasses to hide his blackened cheek after falling down the steps.

Synset Examples

a face black with fury

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bruised - discolored from injury or impact
  • Antonym: pale - having less color, especially in the face

Etymology

Past participle of 'blacken', which is from Middle English 'blakenen', meaning to make black.

2. ADJ

/ ˈblækənd / (AmE), / ˈblækənd / (BrE)
darkened or made black, especially as a result of exposure to smoke, fire, or intense heat
darkened by smoke or fire

Examples

The old cottage had blackened beams after years of fires in the hearth.
Firefighters examined the blackened walls to determine the source of the blaze.
The chef served blackened fish, its skin crisp from the heat of the pan.

Synset Examples

blackened rafters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scorched - burned on the surface; slightly burned
  • Synonym: charred - burned or blackened by fire or heat
  • Antonym: bleached - made white or lighter in color
Source: WordNet
