to dignify

1. VERB

/ ˈdɪɡnɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈdɪɡnɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to confer dignity or honor upon (someone or something); to make worthy of respect or honor
to confer dignity or honor upon

Examples

The committee decided to dignify her achievements with a formal award ceremony.
He refused to dignify the rumor with a response, believing it unworthy of attention.
The newly renovated hall was dignified with elegant columns and classic artwork.

Synset Examples

He was dignified with a title

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honor - to show great respect or recognition; to treat with honor

Etymology

From Latin dignificare: dignus (worthy) + facere (to make).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02552573-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈdɪɡnɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈdɪɡnɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to make something or someone appear more important, valuable, or worthy of respect than they might deserve; to raise in status or dignity
make something seem more important or worthy

Examples

I refuse to dignify that silly rumor with a response.
He didn't want to dignify her rude behavior by acknowledging it.
The ceremony was meant to dignify the contributions of local volunteers.

Synset Examples

I shall not dignify this insensitive remark with an answer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to elevate - to raise to a higher position or rank
  • Antonym: to debase - to lower in status or quality

Etymology

From Latin 'dignificare', from 'dignus' (worthy) + 'facere' (to make).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00545870-v
Synset POS: v
