stricture

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrɪktʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈstrɪktʃə / (BrE)
an abnormal narrowing of a bodily canal or passageway, often due to disease or injury
abnormal narrowing of a body passage
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with a stricture of the esophagus, making swallowing difficult.
A urethral stricture may cause urinary retention and discomfort.
Surgery was required to treat the intestinal stricture that was causing a blockage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stenosis - narrowing or constriction of a passage in the body

Etymology

From Latin 'strictura' (a drawing together, constriction), from 'stringere' (to draw tight).

2. NOUN

/ ˈstrɪktʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈstrɪktʃə / (BrE)
a strong, formal criticism or censure
severe criticism or censure

Examples

The report contained several strictures against the government's policies.
He delivered a stricture on the lack of professionalism in the workplace.
Her speech was filled with strictures about modern education.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: censure - harsh criticism or disapproval
  • Antonym: praise - expression of approval or admiration

Etymology

From Latin 'strictura', meaning 'a drawing tight', from 'stringere' (to bind tight).
