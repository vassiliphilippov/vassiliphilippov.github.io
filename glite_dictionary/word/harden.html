to harden

1. VERB

/ ˈhɑrdən / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdən / (BrE)
to make or become hard or harder
make or become hard

Examples

The cement will harden as it dries overnight.
Repeated freezing and thawing can harden the surface of the soil.
Exposure to air caused the paint to harden quickly.

Synset Examples

The cold hardened the butter

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to soften - to make or become soft or softer
  • Synonym: to solidify - to become solid or make solid

Etymology

From Old English heardian, from heard (hard).

2. VERB

/ ˈhɑrdn̩ / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdn̩ / (BrE)
to become hard or harder; to undergo a process of becoming more solid or rigid
become hard or harder

Examples

Leave the clay sculpture out overnight and it will harden.
The concrete quickly hardened after being poured into the mold.
Wax begins to harden as it cools down.

Synset Examples

The wax hardened

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to soften - to become soft or softer

Etymology

From Old English heardian, from heard (hard).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00444071-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈhɑrdn̩ / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdn̩ / (BrE)
to make or become less sensitive or more used to something, especially something unpleasant, through repeated exposure
become less sensitive, get used to

Examples

Years of working in the emergency room had hardened her to the sight of injury.
His experiences in the military hardened him to the harsh realities of life.
Repeated losses gradually hardened the team to disappointment.

Synset Examples

He was inured to the cold

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inure - to accustom someone to something unpleasant
  • Antonym: to soften - to make or become less severe or less hard

Etymology

From Old English *heardian*, from *heard* (hard).
