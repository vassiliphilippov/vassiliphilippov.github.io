Clematis lasiantha

1. NOUN

/ kləˈmætɪs læsiˈænθə / (AmE), / klɪˈmɑːtɪs læsiˈanθə / (BrE)
a species of clematis native to California, known for its clusters of creamy-white flowers
clematis species native to California

Examples

The Clematis lasiantha blooms in spring along the hillsides of southern California.
Gardeners often choose Clematis lasiantha for its delicate, star-shaped flowers and climbing habit.
Hikers admired the clusters of Clematis lasiantha twining through the native chaparral.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Clematis' from Ancient Greek 'klēma' (vine branch), 'lasiantha' from Greek 'lasios' (hairy) + 'anthos' (flower).
