splatter

1. NOUN

/ ˈsplætər / (AmE), / ˈsplætə / (BrE)
a small amount of a liquid or moist substance that has been dropped or splashed onto a surface
small amount of a liquid splashed onto something

Examples

There was a splatter of tomato sauce on his shirt after lunch.
A sudden splatter of rain hit the window as we talked.
She noticed a splatter of paint on her shoes from the art project.

Synset Examples

a dab of paint
a splatter of mud
just a splash of whiskey

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splash - a small amount of liquid thrown or dropped onto a surface
  • Synonym: dab - a small amount of a substance applied or present
  • Synonym: spatter - a small amount of liquid scattered in drops

Etymology

Imitative of the sound made when a liquid hits a surface, likely influenced by 'splash' and 'spatter'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13783311-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈsplætər / (AmE), / ˈsplætə / (BrE)
to cause a liquid to fall or be thrown in drops, often in a scattered or messy way
make liquid fall in scattered drops

Examples

The rain began to splatter against the window panes as the storm moved in.
She accidentally splattered soup all over the stovetop while stirring too quickly.
Water splatters everywhere when you drop ice cubes into a hot pan.

Synset Examples

spill the milk
splatter water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spill - to accidentally or deliberately let liquid flow out of a container

Etymology

Imitative, possibly from splat (variant of splash) + -er.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01544989-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈsplætɚ / (AmE), / ˈsplætə / (BrE)
a sudden, sharp sound made when a liquid or small particles strike a surface explosively or forcefully
a sharp, explosive noise of something hitting a surface

Examples

A sudden splatter startled the crowd during the fireworks display.
She recognized the splatter of raindrops against the window pane.
The splatter from the kitchen made it clear dinner was being prepared.

Synset Examples

he heard a spatter of gunfire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spatter - a light, scattered noise of small drops hitting a surface
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07409414-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ˈsplæt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈsplætə / (BrE)
to splash or dash a liquid upon or against a surface, often in small drops or spots
to splash liquid onto a surface

Examples

Be careful not to splatter paint all over the floor while you work.
Hot oil can splatter if you cook at too high a temperature.
She accidentally splattered ketchup on her white shirt.

Synset Examples

The mother splashed the baby's face with water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spatter - to scatter small drops of liquid onto a surface
  • Synonym: splash - to cause liquid to fly about in drops

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound made by splashing liquid, influenced by 'splash' and 'spatter'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01376685-v
Synset POS: v
