John XXIII

1. PROPN

/ dʒɑn ðə ˌtwɛnti ˈθɜrd / (AmE), / dʒɒn ðə ˌtwɛn.ti ˈθɜːd / (BrE)
(1881–1963) The pope of the Roman Catholic Church from 1958 to 1963, known for initiating the Second Vatican Council.
Pope of the Roman Catholic Church, 1958–1963

Examples

John XXIII was widely praised for his humility and openness to reform.
The Second Vatican Council, called by John XXIII, brought major changes to the Church.
Many historians consider John XXIII one of the most influential popes of the twentieth century.
