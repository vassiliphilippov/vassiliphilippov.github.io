insular

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪn.sjələr / (AmE), / ˈɪn.sjʊ.lə / (BrE)
having a narrow or limited outlook; lacking openness or interest in ideas, cultures, or people outside one's own experience
narrow-minded or isolated in outlook

Examples

The company was criticized for its insular approach to global markets.
Growing up in a small village, his views remained quite insular for many years.
An insular community often struggles to adapt to change from the outside world.

Synset Examples

little sympathy with parochial mentality
insular attitudes toward foreigners

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parochial - having a limited or narrow outlook or scope
  • Antonym: open-minded - willing to consider new ideas; unprejudiced

Etymology

From Latin 'insularis' (of an island), from 'insula' (island).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɪn.sjələr / (AmE), / ˈɪn.sjʊ.lə / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of an island or islands.
of or relating to an island

Examples

The Galápagos is known for its insular wildlife, found nowhere else in the world.
He studied the insular customs of the island community for his research.
Several U.S. territories have insular governments with unique local laws.

Synset Examples

insular territories
Hawaii's insular culture

Etymology

From Latin 'insula' (island), via Late Latin 'insularis' (of an island).

3. ADJ

/ ˈɪn.sjələr / (AmE), / ˈɪn.sjʊ.lə / (BrE)
Characteristic of, or suggestive of the isolated life of an island; having a narrow or limited outlook.
isolated or narrow-minded in outlook

Examples

His insular attitude made it difficult for him to accept new ideas.
The community grew increasingly insular after decades without outside contact.
She criticized the organization's insular perspective and urged them to be more open-minded.

Synset Examples

an exceedingly insular man, so deeply private as to seem inaccessible to the scrutiny of a novelist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parochial - having a limited or narrow outlook or scope
  • Antonym: cosmopolitan - familiar with or at ease in many different countries and cultures

Etymology

From Latin 'insula' (island).
