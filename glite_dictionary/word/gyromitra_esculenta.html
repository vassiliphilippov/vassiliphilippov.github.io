Gyromitra esculenta

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒaɪrəˈmaɪtrə ˌɛskjʊˈlɛntə / (AmE), / ˌdʒaɪ.rəˈmʌɪ.trə ˌɛs.kjʊˈlɛn.tə / (BrE)
A species of poisonous fungus, known as the false morel, with a wrinkled, convoluted cap ranging in color from dull yellow to brown.
a poisonous species of false morel mushroom

Examples

Many mushroom foragers mistake Gyromitra esculenta for edible morels, which can lead to poisoning.
Despite its species name meaning 'edible,' Gyromitra esculenta is highly toxic and should not be consumed.
The wrinkled, brain-like appearance of Gyromitra esculenta distinguishes it from true morels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: false morel - A common name for several poisonous mushrooms resembling true morels
  • Antonym: Morchella - A genus of edible morel mushrooms, true morels contrasted with Gyromitra

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Gyromitra' (gyro- 'round' + mitra 'cap'), species name 'esculenta' meaning 'edible' in Latin, though it is poisonous.
