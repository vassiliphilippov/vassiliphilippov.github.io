noli-me-tangere

1. NOUN

/ ˌnoʊ.laɪ.miːˈtændʒəri / (AmE), / ˌnəʊ.li.meɪˈtændʒəri / (BrE)
an ulcerative cancer of soft tissue and bone, especially one considered difficult to treat or touch
cancerous ulcer of soft tissue and bone
medical archaic

Examples

Physicians in the past sometimes identified a particularly aggressive ulcer as noli-me-tangere.
The surgeon was concerned that the growth might be a noli-me-tangere due to its location and resilience.
Treatment options for noli-me-tangere were extremely limited before the advent of modern oncology.

Etymology

From Latin noli me tangere (‘do not touch me’), originally a biblical phrase; adopted for conditions sensitive to touch.
