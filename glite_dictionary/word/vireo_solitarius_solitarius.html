Vireo solitarius solitarius

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪrioʊ səˈlɪtɛriəs səˈlɪtɛriəs / (AmE), / ˈvɪəriəʊ səˈlɪtəriəs səˈlɪtəriəs / (BrE)
a common vireo of northeastern North America, recognized by its bluish slaty-grey head
a northeastern North American songbird subspecies

Examples

The Vireo solitarius solitarius is easily identified by its distinct bluish-grey head and white eye-ring.
During spring migration, birdwatchers in Maine often spot the Vireo solitarius solitarius in mixed woodlands.
Researchers studied the song patterns of Vireo solitarius solitarius compared to other vireo subspecies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blue-headed vireo - another common name for Vireo solitarius solitarius

Etymology

From New Latin, following taxonomic conventions; 'Vireo' is a genus of small birds, 'solitarius' means solitary in Latin.
