Atreus

1. PROPN

/ ˈeɪ.tri.əs / (AmE), / ˈæ.tri.əs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the king of Mycenae and father of Agamemnon and Menelaus.
mythical king of Mycenae in Greek legend

Examples

According to the legend, Atreus was betrayed by his brother Thyestes.
The curse of the house of Atreus is a recurring theme in ancient Greek tragedies.
Scholars often discuss the role of Atreus in shaping the fate of his descendants.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Agamemnon - son of Atreus, king of Mycenae in Greek myth
  • Degree Lower: Menelaus - son of Atreus, king of Sparta in Greek myth

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἀτρεύς (Atreús), a legendary name in classical literature.
