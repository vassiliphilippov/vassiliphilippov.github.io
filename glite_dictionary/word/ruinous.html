ruinous

1. ADJ

/ ˈruːnəs / (AmE), / ˈruːɪnəs / (BrE)
causing or likely to cause serious damage or destruction; disastrous.
causing great damage or destruction

Examples

The hurricane had a ruinous effect on the small coastal town.
Industry experts warned that such a policy could prove ruinous for small businesses.
A ruinous fire swept through the old mansion, leaving nothing but ashes.

Synset Examples

the blasting effects of the intense cold on the budding fruit
the blasting force of the wind blowing sharp needles of sleet in our faces
a ruinous war

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devastating - causing severe damage or destruction
  • Antonym: constructive - having a useful or beneficial effect, opposite of ruinous

Etymology

From Latin 'ruinosus' (full of ruin, destructive).

2. ADJ

/ ˈruː.nəs / (AmE), / ˈruː.ɪ.nəs / (BrE)
causing great harm or damage, especially in a way that brings physical or financial ruin
causing great harm or ruin

Examples

Taking on so much debt proved to be a ruinous decision for the company.
The hurricane left a ruinous path through the coastal town.
His ruinous gambling debts forced him to declare bankruptcy.

Synset Examples

a catastrophic depression
catastrophic illness
a ruinous course of action

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catastrophic - extremely harmful; involving great damage or suffering
  • Synonym: devastating - causing severe shock, distress, or destruction
  • Antonym: beneficial - producing good results or helpful effects

Etymology

From Old French *ruineus*, from Latin *ruinosus* (‘full of ruin’).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01165179-s
Synset POS: s
