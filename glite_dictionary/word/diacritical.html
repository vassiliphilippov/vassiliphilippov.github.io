diacritical

1. ADJ

/ ˌdaɪəˈkrɪtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌdʌɪəˈkrɪtɪk(ə)l / (BrE)
serving to distinguish between things; capable of showing differences or distinctions
serving to distinguish; distinguishing

Examples

The archaeologist relied on diacritical details to tell the artifacts apart.
Only a few diacritical features separate these two closely related plant species.
Her diacritical abilities made her an excellent wine taster.

Synset Examples

students having superior diacritic powers
the diacritic elements in culture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distinctive - serving to distinguish something from others; characteristic

Etymology

From Greek diakritikos (able to distinguish), from diakrinein (to distinguish).
