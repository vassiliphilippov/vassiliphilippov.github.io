cardiomegaly

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑrdioʊˈmɛɡəli / (AmE), / ˌkɑːdiəʊˈmɛɡəli / (BrE)
an abnormal enlargement of the heart, usually detected by imaging and associated with certain medical conditions or physical activity
abnormal enlargement of the heart
medical

Examples

After the chest X-ray, the doctor explained that cardiomegaly was present and needed further evaluation.
Athletes sometimes show mild cardiomegaly due to intense physical training.
Untreated high blood pressure can eventually lead to cardiomegaly and heart complications.

Synset Examples

mild cardiomegaly is common in athletes

Etymology

From Greek 'kardia' (heart) and 'megaly' (enlargement).
