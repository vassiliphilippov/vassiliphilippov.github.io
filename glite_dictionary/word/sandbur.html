sandbur

1. NOUN

/ ˈsændˌbɝ / (AmE), / ˈsændˌbɜː / (BrE)
a grass found in the eastern United States and tropical America, characterized by spikelets enclosed in sharp, prickly burs
a grass with prickly seed burs

Examples

Be careful where you walk barefoot, as there might be sandburs in the grass.
The dog's fur was full of sandburs after our hike through the field.
Many farmers try to eliminate sandbur from their pastures to protect livestock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bur - a prickly seed case or fruit

Etymology

Formed from "sand" + "bur," referring to its habitat and spiky seed cases.
