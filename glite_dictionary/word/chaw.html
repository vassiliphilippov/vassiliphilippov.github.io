chaw

1. NOUN

/ tʃɑ / (AmE), / tʃɔː / (BrE)
A wad of something that can be chewed, especially a piece of chewing tobacco.
wad of something to chew, esp. tobacco

Examples

He packed a fresh chaw of tobacco into his cheek before returning to work.
She found an old chaw of gum stuck under the table.
He offered me a chaw but I politely declined.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chew - a wad of something chewable, especially tobacco
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07594964-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ tʃɔ / (AmE), / tʃɔː / (BrE)
to chew something, especially tobacco, without swallowing
chew without swallowing (esp. tobacco)

Examples

He would often chaw a piece of tobacco while working in the fields.
You shouldn't chaw gum all day; it isn't good for your jaw.
The cowboy chawed on a stalk of grass as he watched the cattle.

Synset Examples

chaw tobacco

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chew - to use the teeth to break food down in the mouth

Etymology

Variant of 'chew', influenced by regional pronunciation.
