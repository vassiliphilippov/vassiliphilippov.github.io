Boraginaceae

1. NOUN

/ bɔːˌrædʒəˈneɪsiˌiː / (AmE), / bɒˌrædʒɪˈniːsiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants distinguished by spiral-shaped (circinate) flowers and nutlike fruits, including species such as borage and forget-me-not
family of flowering plants (borage family)

Examples

Many species in the Boraginaceae family have distinctive blue flowers and hairy leaves.
Gardeners often recognize members of the Boraginaceae by their spiral flower arrangements.
Plants from the Boraginaceae family, such as forget-me-nots, are popular in European gardens.

Etymology

From Modern Latin Boraginaceae, formed from the type genus Borago + -aceae (denoting plant families).

2. NOUN

/ bɔːˌrædʒɪˈneɪsiˌi / (AmE), / bɒˌrædʒɪˈneɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants, widely distributed, distinguished by circinate flowers and nutlike fruit; includes forget-me-nots and borage
family of flowering plants (Boraginaceae)

Examples

The Boraginaceae include familiar garden plants such as forget-me-nots and borage.
Many species in the Boraginaceae have hairy leaves and blue or violet flowers.
Botanists study the Boraginaceae for their unique nutlike fruit and spiral flower arrangement.

Etymology

From the Latin genus name Borago, after borage, plus the botanical suffix -aceae for plant families.
