YFLOPS

1. NOUN

/ waɪ flɑps / (AmE), / waɪ flɒps / (BrE)
(computer science) a unit of computing speed equal to one septillion (10²⁴) floating-point operations per second.
unit of computer processing speed (10²⁴ FLOPS)

Examples

The world's fastest supercomputers are approaching performance measured in YFLOPS.
Achieving one YFLOPS is a milestone in computational research and engineering.
Scientists hope to use YFLOPS systems to simulate complex global climate models.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: FLOPS - floating-point operations per second, unit of computer speed

Etymology

Acronym from 'yotta-' (SI prefix for 10²⁴) and 'FLOPS' (floating-point operations per second).
