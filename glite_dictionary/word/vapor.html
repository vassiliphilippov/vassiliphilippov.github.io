vapor

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪ.pɚ / (AmE), / ˈveɪ.pə / (BrE)
a visible suspension in the air of fine particles of a liquid or solid, such as mist, steam, or smoke
fine particles suspended in air

Examples

Clouds form when water vapor in the atmosphere cools and condenses.
The bathroom mirror fogged up due to vapor from the hot shower.
You could see the vapor rising from the kettle as the water boiled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mist - a thick, visible suspension of tiny water droplets in the air
  • Synonym: steam - water in the form of an invisible gas or as visible mist
  • Synonym: smoke - visible suspension of particles from burning

Etymology

From Latin 'vapor' (steam, exhalation).
