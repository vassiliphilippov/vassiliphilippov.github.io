popover

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːpˌoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈpɒpˌəʊvə / (BrE)
A light, hollow muffin made from a batter similar to that of Yorkshire pudding, baked in a deep muffin cup so that it puffs up and forms a crisp shell.
light hollow muffin baked in a cup

Examples

She served freshly baked popovers with butter for breakfast.
The popover rose so high in the oven that it nearly spilled over the pan.
I love the airy texture of a warm popover straight from the oven.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yorkshire pudding - a baked pudding made from batter, often served with roast beef

Etymology

From the notion that the batter 'pops over' the edge of the cup while baking.
