Picea mariana

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈsiːə məˈreɪniə / (AmE), / paɪˈsiːə məˈreɪniə / (BrE)
A species of small spruce tree native to boggy areas of northeastern North America, known for its spreading branches and dense foliage; its wood is considered inferior in quality.
species of small North American spruce

Examples

The dense growth of Picea mariana makes it a dominant species in many northern bogs.
Commercial logging rarely targets Picea mariana because its wood is considered less valuable.
Researchers studied how Picea mariana adapts to the harsh, waterlogged conditions of peatlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black spruce - common name for Picea mariana
  • Degree Higher: white spruce - species of spruce native to northern North America, different from Picea mariana

Etymology

From Latin: 'Picea' (spruce) and 'mariana', referring to St. Mary's Lake in Canada where it was first described.
