self-direction

1. NOUN

/ sɛlf dəˈrɛkʃən / (AmE), / sɛlf dɪˈrɛkʃən / (BrE)
the ability to direct one's own actions, decisions, or learning without outside control; personal independence
personal independence or autonomy

Examples

She showed remarkable self-direction by planning her own study schedule and sticking to it.
A job that encourages self-direction can lead to higher employee satisfaction.
Children benefit from developing self-direction in both learning and play.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autonomy - the right or condition of self-government
  • Antonym: dependence - reliance on others for support

Etymology

Formed from 'self-' (oneself) and 'direction' (guidance, management).
