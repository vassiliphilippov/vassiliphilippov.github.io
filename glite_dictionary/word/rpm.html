rpm

1. NOUN

/ ɑr pi ɛm / (AmE), / ɑː piː em / (BrE)
(abbreviation for revolutions per minute) The number of complete turns that an engine or other rotating device makes in one minute.
revolutions per minute; rotational speed

Examples

At high rpm, the engine produces more power but can wear out faster.
The tachometer shows the current rpm of the car's engine.
Keep the motor running at 3,000 rpm for optimal efficiency.

Synset Examples

the engine was doing 6000 revs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revolutions per minute - the full phrase for rpm; unit of rotational speed
  • Synonym: rev - informal abbreviation for revolution (complete turn), also used for engine speed

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'revolutions per minute'.
