to indorse

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈdɔrs / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɔːs / (BrE)
to guarantee that something meets a particular standard or quality; to certify officially
guarantee as meeting a standard

Examples

The association will only indorse products that meet strict quality guidelines.
Her signature served to indorse the certificate as genuine.
He refused to indorse any claims about the safety of the equipment.

Synset Examples

certified grade AAA meat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to endorse - to declare approval or support of something or someone

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'endorse', from Medieval Latin 'indorsare', from Latin 'in-' (on) + 'dorsum' (back).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02452658-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈdɔrs / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɔːs / (BrE)
to publicly show support or approval for someone or something
show support or approval

Examples

The senator chose to indorse the education reform bill after careful consideration.
Many celebrities indorse charitable organizations to help raise public awareness.
The company decided not to indorse any particular candidate in the upcoming election.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to endorse - to declare public approval or support
  • Antonym: to oppose - to disapprove or be against someone or something

Etymology

From Latin 'indorsare', meaning 'to write on the back (of)'. Variant spelling of 'endorse'.

3. VERB

/ ɪnˈdɔrs / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɔːs / (BrE)
to sign the back of a cheque, bill, or other document, typically to make it payable to someone other than the stated payee or to acknowledge the legal transfer of ownership
sign to transfer a cheque or document
legal

Examples

You must indorse the cheque before depositing it at the bank.
The lawyer asked him to indorse the title deed.
She accidentally indorsed the cheque on the wrong line.

Synset Examples

endorse cheques

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to endorse - to sign the back of a cheque or document to transfer ownership

Etymology

From Latin 'indorsare' via Medieval Latin, from 'in-' (on) + 'dorsum' (back).
