propenonitrile

1. NOUN

/ proʊˌpɛn.oʊˈnaɪ.tril / (AmE), / prəʊˌpɛn.əʊˈnaɪ.triːl / (BrE)
a colorless, liquid, unsaturated nitrile compound made from propene, used in chemical synthesis
chemical compound; unsaturated nitrile

Examples

Propenonitrile is used as an intermediate in the synthesis of various chemicals.
Because of its double bond, propenonitrile is more reactive than saturated nitriles.
Researchers handle propenonitrile in a fume hood due to its toxic vapors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acrylonitrile - a similar, more common unsaturated nitrile compound

Etymology

From 'propene' (the hydrocarbon) + 'nitrile' (organic compound containing the -C≡N group).
