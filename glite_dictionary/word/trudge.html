trudge

1. NOUN

/ trʌdʒ / (AmE), / trʌdʒ / (BrE)
A long and laborious walk, typically made with difficulty or weariness.
a long, weary or difficult walk

Examples

After several hours of their uphill trudge, the hikers finally reached the summit.
The daily trudge to the bus stop in the rain left him exhausted.
She was not looking forward to the long trudge through the snow to get home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trek - a long and difficult journey, especially on foot
  • Antonym: stroll - a leisurely and unhurried walk

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly imitative or related to Middle English 'trudgen', meaning to walk laboriously.

2. VERB

/ trʌdʒ / (AmE), / trʌdʒ / (BrE)
to walk slowly and with heavy steps, typically because of tiredness or harsh conditions
walk slowly and heavily, usually when tired

Examples

After hours of hiking, we had to trudge through thick, wet mud to reach the campsite.
Exhausted, she trudged home in the pouring rain after her late shift.
They were trudging along the snow-covered path, barely able to lift their tired feet.

Synset Examples

Mules plodded in a circle around a grindstone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plod - to walk doggedly and slowly with heavy steps
