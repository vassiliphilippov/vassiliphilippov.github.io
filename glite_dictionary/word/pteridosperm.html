pteridosperm

1. NOUN

/ tɛrɪˈdoʊspɝm / (AmE), / tɛrɪˈdəʊspɜːm / (BrE)
an extinct seed-producing fernlike plant of the order Cycadofilicales, considered among the earliest gymnosperms
extinct seed-producing fernlike plant

Examples

Fossils of pteridosperms provide important clues about the early evolution of seed plants.
Scientists believe that pteridosperms were widespread during the Paleozoic era.
The discovery of a new pteridosperm species shed light on ancient terrestrial ecosystems.

Etymology

From Greek pteris (fern) + sperma (seed).
