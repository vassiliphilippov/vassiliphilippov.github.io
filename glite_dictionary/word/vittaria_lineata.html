Vittaria lineata

1. NOUN

/ vɪˈtɛər.i.ə laɪˈneɪtə / (AmE), / vɪˈtɛːrɪə lɪˈniːətə / (BrE)
An epiphytic fern native to the lowland forests of tropical America, known for its slender, grass-like leaves.
epiphytic fern of tropical America

Examples

The delicate Vittaria lineata thrives high up on the trees in tropical rainforests.
Botanists discovered a rare colony of Vittaria lineata growing on mossy branches.
Due to deforestation, the habitat of Vittaria lineata has become increasingly threatened.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Vittaria', from Latin 'vitta' (band, ribbon) referring to the ribbon-like leaves, and Latin 'lineata' (lined, striped).
