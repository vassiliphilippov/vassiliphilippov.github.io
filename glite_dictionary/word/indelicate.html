indelicate

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈdɛlɪkət / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛlɪkət / (BrE)
lacking propriety or good taste; tactless or coarse in manners or conduct
lacking propriety or good taste

Examples

Some considered his joke to be indelicate and inappropriate for the occasion.
The article contained several indelicate comments that many readers found offensive.
She regretted making such an indelicate remark at the family dinner.

Synset Examples

indecorous behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indecorous - not in keeping with good taste and propriety
  • Antonym: delicate - showing fine or sensitive taste or tact

Etymology

Formed from prefix in- (not) + delicate (from Latin delicatus, pleasing, dainty).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈdɛlɪkət / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛlɪkət / (BrE)
showing a lack of sensitivity or good taste; offensively improper or almost indecent
lacking in good taste or propriety

Examples

He made an indelicate comment during the meeting, which embarrassed everyone present.
Some jokes cross the line from funny to indelicate and should be avoided in polite company.
The journalist's questions were considered indelicate and insensitive by the grieving family.

Synset Examples

an indelicate remark
an off-color joke

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tactless - lacking sensitivity in dealing with others
  • Degree Higher: indecent - not conforming with accepted standards of morality or decency
  • Antonym: delicate - showing sensitivity or carefulness; opposite of indelicate

Etymology

From Latin 'in-' (not) + 'delicatus' (dainty, pleasing).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02403360-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ɪnˈdɛlɪkət / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛlɪkət / (BrE)
Somewhat improper or offensive; verging on indecency or lacking sensitivity to social or moral standards.
improper or verging on indecency

Examples

His indelicate comments made several people in the room uncomfortable.
She found the joke too indelicate to repeat at the dinner table.
The actor apologized for his indelicate behavior during the interview.

Synset Examples

an indelicate proposition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: improper - not in accordance with accepted rules or standards
  • Antonym: decent - conforming with generally accepted standards of respectable or moral behavior

Etymology

From in- (not) + delicate, from Latin delicatus (dainty, sensitive).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00687331-s
Synset POS: s
