Parus

1. PROPN

/ ˈpɛər.əs / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.rəs / (BrE)
A genus of small passerine birds in the tit family, Paridae, including species such as the great tit and blue tit.
genus of birds in the tit family

Examples

The great tit, a member of the genus Parus, is common across Europe.
Researchers identified several new behaviors in Parus species during the study.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot a rare Parus in the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Paridae - the tit family; family of small insectivorous birds

Etymology

From Latin 'parus' meaning 'titmouse, tit'.
