rapeseed

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪpˌsid / (AmE), / ˈreɪpˌsiːd / (BrE)
the seed of the rape plant, used as a source of edible oil and animal feed
seed of rape plant; source of edible oil

Examples

Farmers in this region grow rapeseed primarily for its valuable oil content.
The price of rapeseed has increased due to rising demand for vegetable oils.
Biodiesel can be produced from rapeseed, making it an alternative energy source.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canola - an edible oil variety of rapeseed bred for low erucic acid

Etymology

From rape + seed; 'rape' from Latin 'rapum' meaning turnip.
