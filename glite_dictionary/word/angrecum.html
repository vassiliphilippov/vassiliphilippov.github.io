angrecum

1. NOUN

/ ænˈɡriːkəm / (AmE), / ænˈɡriːkəm / (BrE)
A genus of spectacular orchids having dark green leathery leaves and usually nocturnally scented white or ivory flowers.
a genus of nocturnally scented orchids

Examples

The greenhouse featured a rare angrecum in full bloom, emitting a delicate fragrance at dusk.
Botanists admire the unique star-shaped flowers of the angrecum genus.
She carefully tended her collection of angrecums, ensuring each orchid received the right amount of humidity.

Etymology

From New Latin Angraecum, from Latinized form of Malay 'angrek' (orchid).
