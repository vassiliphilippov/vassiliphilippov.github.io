amputation

1. NOUN

/ ˌæmpjəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌæmpjʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
The loss of a limb or part of a limb, typically as a result of surgery, trauma, or disease, resulting in a condition of disability.
loss of a limb or part of a limb
medical

Examples

After the accident, his amputation meant he had to adapt to a prosthetic leg.
The patient suffered an amputation below the knee following severe infection.
Support groups can help people cope with the challenges of life after an amputation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disability - a physical or mental condition that limits a person's movements, senses, or activities

Etymology

From Latin 'amputatio', from 'amputare' meaning 'to cut away'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæmpjəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌæmpjʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the surgical removal of all or part of a limb or other body part
surgical removal of a limb or part
medical

Examples

After the accident, the doctors had to perform an amputation of his right leg to save his life.
Advances in prosthetics have greatly improved the quality of life for people following amputation.
The risk of amputation increases in diabetic patients with untreated foot infections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disarticulation - surgical removal of a limb through a joint
  • Synonym: resection - surgical removal of part of an organ or structure

Etymology

From Latin amputatio (a cutting off), from amputare (to cut off).
