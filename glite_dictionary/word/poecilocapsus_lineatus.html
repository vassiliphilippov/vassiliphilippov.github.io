Poecilocapsus lineatus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊsɪloʊˈkæpsəs laɪˈneɪətəs / (AmE), / ˌpəʊsɪləkˈæpsəs lɪˈneɪətəs / (BrE)
A species of yellow or orange leaf bug with four black stripes on its back, commonly found in central and eastern North America.
a yellow or orange leaf bug with four black stripes

Examples

Gardeners often spot the Poecilocapsus lineatus feeding on the leaves of various plants in early summer.
Damage caused by the Poecilocapsus lineatus can be identified by small, round brown spots on foliage.
The bright yellow or orange color and four black stripes make the Poecilocapsus lineatus easy to distinguish from other bugs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: four-lined plant bug - common name for Poecilocapsus lineatus, a striped plant bug

Etymology

From modern Latin, where 'Poecilocapsus' means 'variegated covering' and 'lineatus' means 'lined' or 'striped.'
