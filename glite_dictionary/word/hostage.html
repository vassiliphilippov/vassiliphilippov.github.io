hostage

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑstɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈhɒstɪdʒ / (BrE)
a person who is seized or held as security for the fulfillment of certain conditions or terms by another party
person held as security for conditions

Examples

The government refused to negotiate with the terrorists who took a hostage.
After several days, the hostage was released unharmed by his captors.
Parents watched the news anxiously, hoping for the safe return of the hostage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: captive - a person who is confined or restrained, especially during war or by an enemy
  • Synonym: prisoner - a person deprived of liberty and kept under involuntary restraint
  • Antonym: kidnapper - a person who seizes or detains someone unlawfully, often for ransom

Etymology

From Old French *ostage*, from Latin *obsidāticum*, from *obses* (hostage).
