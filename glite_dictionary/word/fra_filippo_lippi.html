Fra Filippo Lippi

1. PROPN

/ frɑː fɪˈliːpoʊ ˈlɪpi / (AmE), / frɑː fɪˈliːpəʊ ˈlɪpi / (BrE)
(1406–1469) Italian Renaissance painter known for his three-dimensional style and influential religious works.
Italian painter (1406–1469)

Examples

The frescoes of Fra Filippo Lippi are admired for their delicate use of light and perspective.
Art historians often study Fra Filippo Lippi to understand the development of Renaissance techniques.
The influence of Fra Filippo Lippi can be seen in the work of his pupil, Sandro Botticelli.

Etymology

Italian, 'Fra' meaning 'Brother'; Filippo Lippi is the painter's full name.
