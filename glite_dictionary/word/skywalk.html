skywalk

1. NOUN

/ ˈskaɪˌwɔk / (AmE), / ˈskaɪˌwɔːk / (BrE)
an elevated, often enclosed, walkway connecting buildings above ground level, typically used by pedestrians
elevated walkway between buildings

Examples

A glass-enclosed skywalk links the hotel to the convention center.
Pedestrians use the skywalk to cross over busy streets safely.
The new office tower features a skywalk that offers stunning city views.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skybridge - a bridge-like elevated structure connecting buildings

Etymology

From 'sky' + 'walk', first used in the 20th century to describe elevated pedestrian walkways.
