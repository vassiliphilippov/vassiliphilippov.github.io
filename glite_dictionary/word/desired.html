desired

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈzaɪɚd / (AmE), / dɪˈzaɪəd / (BrE)
strongly wanted or wished for; greatly sought after
strongly wanted or wished for

Examples

The medication finally produced the desired result after several weeks of use.
A college education is still a highly desired goal for many students around the world.
She wore the dress she had always desired to impress her friends at the reunion.

Synset Examples

the child could no longer resist taking one of the craved cookies
it produced the desired effect

Related Terms

  • Antonym: undesired - not wanted or wished for
  • Synonym: desired effect - the effect that is intended or hoped for

Etymology

From the verb 'desire', from Old French 'desirer', from Latin 'desiderare' (to long for, wish for).
