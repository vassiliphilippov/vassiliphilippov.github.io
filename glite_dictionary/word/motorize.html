to motorize

1. VERB

/ ˈmoʊtəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈməʊtəraɪz / (BrE)
to equip with a motor, especially to make a vehicle or device powered by a motor rather than manual or animal power
equip with a motor

Examples

The company plans to motorize all its delivery carts next year.
With advances in technology, many bicycles are now easy to motorize.
The old farm equipment was eventually motorized to increase efficiency.

Synset Examples

motorized scooters are now the rage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mechanize - equip with machinery, usually to replace manual labor

Etymology

From 'motor' + '-ize', first used in the early 20th century.

2. VERB

/ ˈmoʊtəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈməʊtəraɪz / (BrE)
to equip (a vehicle, device, or organization) with a motor or motor vehicles
equip with a motor or motor vehicles

Examples

Many local police forces were motorized in the 1930s to improve response times.
They plan to motorize their entire fleet of delivery carts by next year.
The army began to motorize its infantry units after the invention of reliable trucks.

Synset Examples

The police around here are not motorized and patrol the streets on horseback

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mechanize - to equip with machinery, especially replacing human or animal labor

Etymology

From motor + -ize, first used in early 20th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02230954-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈmoʊtəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈməʊtəraɪz / (BrE)
To equip with motor vehicles, especially armed or armored vehicles for military use.
equip with motor vehicles (esp. military)

Examples

The government decided to motorize its infantry divisions for greater mobility.
During World War II, many armies sought to motorize their forces to enhance battlefield effectiveness.
To improve logistics, the unit was quickly motorized and supplied with new armored vehicles.

Synset Examples

mechanize armies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mechanize - to equip with machinery, often military vehicles

Etymology

From motor + -ize, formed in the early 20th century parallel with mechanize.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00481682-v
Synset POS: v
