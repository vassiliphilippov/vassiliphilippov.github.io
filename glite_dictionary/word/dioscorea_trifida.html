Dioscorea trifida

1. NOUN

/ diˌɑːskəˈriːə ˈtrɪfɪdə / (AmE), / diˌɒskəˈriːə ˈtrɪfɪdə / (BrE)
A tropical American yam plant, cultivated for its small yellow edible tubers.
tropical American yam with edible tubers

Examples

Farmers in the region often grow Dioscorea trifida because its tubers are highly valued in local cuisine.
The yellow flesh of Dioscorea trifida becomes creamy when cooked and is used in stews.
Researchers are studying Dioscorea trifida for its potential nutritional benefits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yam - Edible tuber of plants in the genus Dioscorea

Etymology

From genus name Dioscorea, after Greek physician Dioscorides, and trifida meaning 'three-lobed' in Latin.
