tailor

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪlər / (AmE), / ˈteɪlə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to make, alter, or repair clothes, especially suits, coats, and other outer garments
person who makes or alters clothes

Examples

The tailor took my measurements for a custom-fitted suit.
She went to a skilled tailor to have her dress altered.
A good tailor can make even inexpensive clothes look elegant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seamstress - a woman who sews, especially one who earns her living by sewing
  • Synonym: dressmaker - someone who makes custom dresses and clothing

Etymology

From Old French *taillour* (cutter), from *tailler* (to cut), from Latin *talea* (cutting, rod, stick).

2. VERB

/ ˈteɪlər / (AmE), / ˈteɪlə / (BrE)
to make (clothes) by cutting and sewing fabric to fit a particular person or purpose
make clothes from cloth

Examples

He decided to tailor his own suit for the job interview.
The dress was tailored specifically to fit her measurements.
They offer services to tailor clothing for all occasions.

Synset Examples

Can the seamstress sew me a suit by next week?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sew - to join or repair something by making stitches with a needle and thread
  • Antonym: manufacture - to make goods on a large scale using machinery

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman 'tailour', Old French 'taillour', from 'tailler' (to cut).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01670222-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈteɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈteɪlə / (BrE)
to make or adapt something, especially clothing, to fit a particular person or purpose
make or adapt to fit

Examples

She had her wedding dress tailored to fit her perfectly.
The program was tailored to meet the needs of young learners.
He can tailor a suit for any body shape.

Synset Examples

cut a dress

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to customize - to modify or build according to individual specifications
  • Synonym: to alter - to change or modify, especially clothing for fit

Etymology

From Old French *taillour* (cutter), from *tailler* (to cut), from Latin *taliare* (to cut).

4. VERB

/ ˈteɪlər / (AmE), / ˈteɪlə / (BrE)
to make or adapt something specifically to suit a particular purpose, need, or individual
adapt to suit a specific need

Examples

The company will tailor its services to meet the unique requirements of each client.
We can tailor the training program depending on your team's specific goals.
Educational content is often tailored to match the age and abilities of the students.

Synset Examples

a magazine oriented towards young people
tailor your needs to your surroundings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to customize - to make or change something according to the customer's needs
  • Antonym: to standardize - to make something conform to a standard

Etymology

From Old French 'tailleur' (cutter), from 'tailler' (to cut), ultimately from Latin 'taliare'.
