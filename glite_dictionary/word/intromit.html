to intromit

1. VERB

/ ˌɪntrəˈmɪt / (AmE), / ˌɪntrəˈmɪt / (BrE)
(archaic) To allow or admit to enter; to let in.
allow or admit to enter (archaic)
archaic

Examples

The old castle gates would intromit only those with a royal seal.
Air was intromitted into the chamber to equalize the pressure.
During the ceremony, only selected guests were intromitted into the inner hall.

Synset Examples

We cannot admit non-members into our club building
This pipe admits air

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to admit - to allow to enter; to grant entry to

Etymology

From Latin intromittere, from intro- (inward) + mittere (to send).
