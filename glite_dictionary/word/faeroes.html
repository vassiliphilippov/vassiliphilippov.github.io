Faeroes

1. PROPN

/ ˈfɛəroʊz / (AmE), / ˈfeərəʊz / (BrE)
a group of self-governing islands in the North Atlantic, officially a possession of Denmark, also known as the Faroe Islands.
group of self-governing North Atlantic islands; Danish possession

Examples

The Faeroes are known for their dramatic scenery and rugged coastlines.
Fishing is a major industry in the Faeroes, contributing significantly to the islands' economy.
The weather in the Faeroes can change rapidly even in summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Faroe Islands - Group of islands in the North Atlantic, also known as the Faeroes

Etymology

From Danish 'Færøerne', meaning 'the sheep islands'.

2. PROPN

/ ˈfɛr.oʊz / (AmE), / ˈfɛərəʊz / (BrE)
a group of 21 volcanic islands in the North Atlantic Ocean located between Iceland and the Shetland Islands; also known as the Faroe Islands.
North Atlantic volcanic island group

Examples

The Faeroes have a rich tradition of folk music and storytelling.
Fishing is the most important industry in the Faeroes.
Birdwatchers come from all over the world to visit the Faeroes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Faroe Islands - another common name for same island group

Etymology

From Danish 'Færøerne' meaning 'Sheep Islands'.
