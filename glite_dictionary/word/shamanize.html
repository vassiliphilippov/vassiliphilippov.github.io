to shamanize

1. VERB

/ ˈʃɑməˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈʃɑːmənaɪz / (BrE)
to practice shamanism or act as a shaman, especially by entering altered states to interact with the spirit world
practice shamanism; act as a shaman

Examples

The village elder was known to shamanize during important festivals.
Anthropologists observed several members of the community shamanizing to heal the sick.
She learned to shamanize as part of her people's spiritual tradition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shaman - a person regarded as having access to the spirit world

Etymology

From 'shaman' (a spiritual leader) plus '-ize' (a verb-forming suffix).
