high-yielding

1. ADJ

/ haɪ ˈjiːldɪŋ / (AmE), / haɪ ˈjiːldɪŋ / (BrE)
(of crops, plants, or livestock) producing a large amount or quantity, especially relative to area or effort.
producing a large amount or quantity

Examples

The new variety of rice is high-yielding and resistant to disease.
Farmers prefer high-yielding crops to maximize their profits.
Advances in breeding have resulted in high-yielding wheat strains that thrive in poor soil.

Synset Examples

The new seeds are designed to be famine resistant and high-yielding.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: low-yielding - producing a small amount of yield or output
