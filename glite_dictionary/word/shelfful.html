shelfful

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɛlf.fʊl / (AmE), / ˈʃɛlf.fʊl / (BrE)
An amount that fills a shelf; as many items as a shelf can hold.
amount that fills a shelf

Examples

He bought a shelfful of books at the sale.
There was a shelfful of trophies in his office.
She arranged a shelfful of flowerpots by the window.

Synset Examples

he bought a shelfful of books

Etymology

Formed from 'shelf' + suffixed '-ful' (denoting 'as much as can be held by').
