ABS

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ.biˈɛs / (AmE), / ˌeɪ.biˈɛs / (BrE)
a strong, tough plastic (acrylonitrile butadiene styrene) used to make car bodies, computer cases, and various other products
a type of tough plastic (acrylonitrile butadiene styrene)

Examples

The dashboard of the car was made from ABS to ensure durability.
Most modern LEGO bricks are constructed using ABS for its strength and versatility.
The casing of that laptop is designed from ABS plastic, making it resistant to impact.

Etymology

Acronym from acrylonitrile butadiene styrene, the polymers from which it is made.
