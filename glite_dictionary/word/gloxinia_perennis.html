Gloxinia perennis

1. NOUN

/ ɡlɑkˈsɪniə pəˈrɛnɪs / (AmE), / ɡlɒkˈsɪniə pəˈrɛnɪs / (BrE)
A tropical herb native to areas from Colombia to Peru, known for its pale purple flowers.
tropical herb with pale purple flowers

Examples

Some gardeners grow Gloxinia perennis for its attractive, bell-shaped flowers.
The pale purple blooms of Gloxinia perennis brighten shady corners in tropical gardens.
Researchers recorded the presence of Gloxinia perennis in several regions of northern Peru.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Gloxinia' (after Benjamin Peter Gloxin, German botanist) and Latin 'perennis' meaning 'perennial'.
