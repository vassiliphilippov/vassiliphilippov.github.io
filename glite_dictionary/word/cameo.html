cameo

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæmioʊ / (AmE), / ˈkæmiəʊ / (BrE)
A piece of jewelry, typically oval, consisting of a portrait or scene carved in relief on a background of a different color, often used in brooches or rings.
carved relief jewelry piece

Examples

She wore a beautiful Victorian cameo on her necklace.
The museum's collection includes several rare cameos from the eighteenth century.
The artisan crafted a delicate cameo from a piece of seashell.

Etymology

From Italian 'cammeo', perhaps from Medieval Latin 'camahūt-', possibly from Arabic 'qamāhūn' (amulet).
