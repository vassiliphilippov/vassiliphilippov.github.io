delighted

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈlaɪtɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈlaɪtɪd / (BrE)
very pleased; feeling or showing great pleasure or satisfaction.
very pleased; greatly satisfied

Examples

She was absolutely delighted with the surprise party her friends organized.
The children were delighted to see snow falling for the first time.
I'm delighted to announce that our school won the competition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pleased - feeling or showing satisfaction or happiness about something
  • Antonym: disappointed - feeling sad or displeased because expectations were not met

Etymology

Past participle of 'delight', from Old French 'deliter', from Latin 'delectare' (to please).

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈlaɪtɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈlaɪtɪd / (BrE)
very pleased; feeling or showing great pleasure and satisfaction.
very pleased or happy

Examples

She was delighted with the news of her promotion.
The children were delighted to see the puppies at the park.
I'm absolutely delighted that you could join us for dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pleased - feeling or showing satisfaction or pleasure
  • Antonym: disappointed - unhappy because something did not happen as expected
