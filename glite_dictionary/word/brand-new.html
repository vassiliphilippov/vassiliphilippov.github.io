brand-new

1. ADJ

/ brænd ˈnu / (AmE), / brænd ˈnjuː / (BrE)
Completely new and unused; in perfect condition as if fresh from the manufacturer.
completely new and unused

Examples

He drove away in a brand-new car that he had just purchased.
The company moved into a brand-new office building last month.
She wore a brand-new dress to the ceremony, and everyone admired her style.

Synset Examples

shiny brand-new shoes
a spick-and-span novelty

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: new - not existing before; made, introduced, or discovered recently

Etymology

From 'brand' (meaning a burning piece of wood) and 'new'—originally meaning 'fresh from the fire,' indicating an object newly made.
