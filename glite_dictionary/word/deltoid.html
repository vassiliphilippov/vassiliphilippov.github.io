deltoid

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɛl.tɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈdɛl.tɔɪd / (BrE)
Having a triangular shape or resembling the Greek letter delta (Δ), typically with a pointed apex.
triangular or delta-shaped

Examples

The architect designed a deltoid window to add geometric interest to the building.
Some leaves, such as those of the black poplar, have a deltoid shape.
The crystal displayed a distinct deltoid facet, tapering to a point at the top.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: triangular - having three sides and three angles

Etymology

From Greek *deltoeidēs*, from *delta* (Δ) + *-oid* (resembling).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɛl.tɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈdɛl.tɔɪd / (BrE)
a large triangular muscle covering the shoulder joint, responsible for raising, rotating, and moving the arm
shoulder muscle
medical

Examples

She injured her deltoid muscle while lifting weights at the gym.
The nurse administered the injection into his deltoid.
Strong deltoids are important for athletes who do overhead movements.

Etymology

From Greek 'deltoeidēs', meaning 'shaped like the letter delta (Δ)'.
