crucial

1. ADJ

/ ˈkruːʃəl / (AmE), / ˈkruːʃəl / (BrE)
Extremely important or decisive, especially in the success or failure of something.
extremely important; decisive

Examples

Her support was crucial to the team's victory in the tournament.
It is crucial that we meet this deadline to secure the contract.
Accurate information is crucial when making important decisions.

Synset Examples

a crucial moment in his career
a crucial election
a crucial issue for women

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vital - absolutely necessary or essential
  • Synonym: critical - involving an important or decisive moment
  • Antonym: trivial - of little value or importance

Etymology

From French *crucial* (decisive), from Latin *crux* (cross), originally referring to a cross-shaped arrangement marking a decisive point.

Examples

3. ADJ

/ ˈkruːʃəl / (AmE), / ˈkruːʃəl / (BrE)
Extremely important or necessary; having a decisive influence on the outcome of something.
extremely important or decisive

Examples

It is crucial that we finish this project before the deadline.
Choosing the right supplier is crucial for the success of our business.

Synset Examples

crucial to the case

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vital - absolutely necessary or essential
  • Synonym: critical - of great importance, especially in affecting the outcome
  • Antonym: trivial - of little value or importance; not crucial

From French 'crucial' (decisive), from Latin 'crux' (cross).
