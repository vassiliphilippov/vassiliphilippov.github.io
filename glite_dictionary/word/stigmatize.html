to stigmatize

1. VERB

/ ˈstɪɡ.mə.taɪz / (AmE), / ˈstɪɡ.mə.taɪz / (BrE)
to describe or treat someone or something as worthy of disgrace or disapproval; to mark with a stigma
to mark as disgraceful or disapproved

Examples

Society should not stigmatize people with mental health conditions.
He feared his background would stigmatize him in the new community.
The campaign aims to stop schools from stigmatizing students with learning difficulties.

Synset Examples

They wanted to stigmatize the adulteress

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disgrace - bring shame or discredit on
  • Antonym: honor - regard with great respect

Etymology

From Medieval Latin stigmatizare, from Greek stigma (mark, tattoo).

2. VERB

/ ˈstɪɡ.mə.taɪz / (AmE), / ˈstɪɡ.mə.taɪz / (BrE)
to describe or treat someone or something as socially unacceptable or disgraced
to brand as socially unacceptable

Examples

People often stigmatize those who struggle with addiction, making it harder for them to seek help.
It is unfair to stigmatize children because of their family's background.
The organization works to reduce the ways society stigmatizes mental health conditions.

Synset Examples

He denounced the government action
She was stigmatized by society because she had a child out of wedlock

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to denounce - to publicly declare to be wrong or evil
  • Synonym: brand - to describe someone or something as having a particular (usually bad) quality
  • Antonym: honor - to regard with great respect or esteem

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'stigmatizare', from Greek 'stigma' (mark, stain, or brand).

Synset POS: v
