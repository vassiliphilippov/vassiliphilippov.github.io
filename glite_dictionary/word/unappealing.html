unappealing

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.əˈpil.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌʌn.əˈpiː.lɪŋ / (BrE)
not attractive or interesting; tending to evoke dislike or a lack of interest.
not attractive or interesting

Examples

The movie was filled with unappealing characters that the audience struggled to care about.
He found the dish visually unappealing and decided not to try it.
The advertisement was so unappealing that it failed to attract any customers.

Synset Examples

all the characters were peculiarly unsympathetic

Related Terms

  • Antonym: appealing - attractive or interesting
  • Synonym: unsympathetic - not eliciting sympathy or approval

Etymology

From the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'appealing' (from appeal).

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.əˈpi.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌʌn.əˈpiː.lɪŋ / (BrE)
not attractive or pleasant; unlikely to evoke interest or approval.
not attractive or pleasant

Examples

The food looked unappealing after sitting out for hours.
His suggestion was so unappealing that nobody wanted to try it.
She found the color of the room unappealing and decided to repaint it.

Synset Examples

they have made the place as unappealing as possible
was forced to talk to his singularly unappealing hostess

Related Terms

  • Antonym: appealing - attractive or interesting
  • Synonym: unattractive - not pleasing to the eye or senses
  • Synonym: undesirable - not wanted or attractive

Etymology

Prefix un- (not) + appealing.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00172305-a
Synset POS: a
