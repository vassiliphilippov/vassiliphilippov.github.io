to induct

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈdʌkt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌkt / (BrE)
to formally admit someone to a position, office, or organization, especially with a ceremony
formally admit to a position or office

Examples

She was inducted as the new chairperson during Friday's ceremony.
The club will induct five new members at the annual dinner.
After winning the prize, he was inducted into the Hall of Fame.

Synset Examples

there was a ceremony to induct the president of the Academy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to install - to place someone in an official position in a formal manner
  • Antonym: to dismiss - to remove someone from an official position

Etymology

From Latin 'inducere' (to lead in).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02389417-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈdʌkt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌkt / (BrE)
to formally admit someone as a member of a group or organization, often with a special ceremony
formally admit to a group or organization

Examples

After years of hard work, she was inducted into the Hall of Fame.
New members are inducted to the society every spring.
The club inducted him in a formal ceremony attended by family and friends.

Synset Examples

African men are initiated when they reach puberty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: initiate - to admit someone into a group with a ritual or ceremony
  • Antonym: to expel - to formally remove someone from a group or organization

Etymology

From Latin 'inducere' meaning 'to lead in'.

3. VERB

/ ɪnˈdʌkt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌkt / (BrE)
to produce an electric current in a conductor by the influence of a changing magnetic or electric field
produce electric current by induction

Examples

A changing magnetic field can induct an electric current in a nearby coil.
Engineers designed the device to induct current as the magnetic field varies.
The oscillating circuit will induct voltage into the secondary winding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to induce - to produce (an electric current) by induction

Etymology

From Latin 'inducere' meaning 'to lead in', through French 'induire'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
