superhighway

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuːpərˈhaɪweɪ / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəˈhaɪweɪ / (BrE)
an extensive electronic network that enables rapid transfer of digital data, especially sound, video, and graphics; often used to refer to the internet
electronic data network; the internet

Examples

The information superhighway has transformed the way we share and access data worldwide.
Government policy aims to expand the superhighway to rural communities, ensuring high-speed internet for all.
As technology advanced, the superhighway became crucial in business, education, and entertainment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: internet - the global computer network providing information and communication

Etymology

Modelled after 'highway', indicating a major route for digital information.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsuː.pɚˌhaɪ.weɪ / (AmE), / ˈsuː.pəˌhaɪ.weɪ / (BrE)
A very wide and well-constructed road designed for high-speed traffic, often with multiple lanes and limited access points.
broad highway for high-speed traffic

Examples

The new superhighway connects the two cities and has greatly reduced travel time.
Plans are underway to expand the superhighway to include more lanes for increased capacity.
Driving on the superhighway at night requires extra caution due to higher speeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expressway - a major highway designed for fast traffic with few stops
  • Synonym: freeway - a highway without tolls or intersections, designed for fast traffic

Etymology

From 'super-' (above, very large or powerful) + 'highway'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03311555-n
Synset POS: n
