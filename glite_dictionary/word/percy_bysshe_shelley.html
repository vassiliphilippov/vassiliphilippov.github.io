Percy Bysshe Shelley

1. PROPN

/ ˈpɜːrsi ˈbɪʃ ˈʃɛli / (AmE), / ˈpɜːsi ˈbɪʃ ˈʃɛli / (BrE)
(1792–1822) English Romantic poet known for his radical views and influential lyrical poetry.
English Romantic poet (1792–1822)

Examples

Many scholars consider Percy Bysshe Shelley one of the greatest lyric poets in the English language.
Percy Bysshe Shelley wrote several influential poems, including "Ozymandias" and "To a Skylark".
The radical ideas of Percy Bysshe Shelley influenced both his contemporaries and later generations of writers.
