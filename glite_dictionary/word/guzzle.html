to guzzle

1. VERB

/ ˈɡʌz.əl / (AmE), / ˈɡʌz.əl / (BrE)
to drink something greedily or in large quantities, as if very thirsty
drink greedily or in large amounts

Examples

He managed to guzzle three cans of soda in under a minute.
At parties, she would often guzzle her drinks faster than anyone else.
The campers guzzled water after their long hike in the heat.

Synset Examples

The boys guzzled the cheap vodka

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gulp - to swallow quickly or in large mouthfuls
  • Antonym: sip - to drink in small amounts

Etymology

Possibly from Middle English *guslen* (to pour out in a mass, to squirt out), of imitative origin.
