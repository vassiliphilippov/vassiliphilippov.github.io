hateful

1. ADJ

/ ˈheɪt.fəl / (AmE), / ˈheɪt.fəl / (BrE)
full of hate; showing or causing strong dislike or malice.
full of hate, showing malice

Examples

She made a hateful remark that deeply offended her colleague.
The villain’s hateful actions made him despised by everyone in the story.
It was a hateful thing to spread such nasty rumors.

Synset Examples

a hateful thing to do
in a mean mood

Related Terms

  • Antonym: loving - showing love or great care
  • Synonym: hostile - very unfriendly or antagonistic

Etymology

From Middle English 'hatelful', from Old English 'hǣtful' (full of hate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01592194-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈheɪtfəl / (AmE), / ˈheɪtfʊl / (BrE)
arousing strong dislike, anger, or disgust; deserving to be hated
inspiring hate or strong dislike

Examples

His hateful comments caused outrage among the audience.
Many found the character so hateful that they wished he would leave the show.
It was a hateful crime that shocked the entire community.

Synset Examples

no vice is universally as hateful as ingratitude

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lovable - inspiring or deserving love or affection
  • Synonym: detestable - deserving intense dislike; abhorrent

Etymology

From Middle English *hatful*, from Old English *hatfull* (full of hate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01463343-a
Synset POS: a
