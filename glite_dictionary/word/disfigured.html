disfigured

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈfɪɡɚd / (AmE), / dɪsˈfɪɡəd / (BrE)
having the appearance spoiled or ruined; deformed or marred in appearance
having appearance spoiled or deformed

Examples

The accident left him disfigured and unable to recognize himself in the mirror.
Several statues in the park were badly disfigured by vandalism.
After the fire, the once beautiful painting was disfigured beyond repair.

Synset Examples

a disfigured face
strip mining left a disfigured landscape

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deformed - having a physical form that is misshapen or not normal
  • Synonym: marred - damaged or spoiled, especially in appearance
  • Antonym: unspoiled - not spoiled or damaged

Etymology

From disfigure + -ed, based on Old French desfigurer and Latin figurare (to form, shape).
