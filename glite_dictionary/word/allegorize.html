to allegorize

1. VERB

/ ˈæləˌɡɔːraɪz / (AmE), / ˈalɪɡəraɪz / (BrE)
to interpret or present something as an allegory; to explain or represent by means of allegory
interpret as allegory

Examples

The professor encouraged us to allegorize the poem to uncover its deeper meanings.
Some critics allegorize fairy tales to reveal their underlying moral messages.
In her essay, she allegorized the historical event as a struggle between light and darkness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to symbolize - to represent something by means of symbols

Etymology

From Medieval Latin allegorizare, from Greek allegoria (allegory).

2. VERB

/ ˈælɪɡəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈælɪɡəˌraɪz / (BrE)
to interpret or represent something as an allegory, or to make something into an allegory
interpret or make into an allegory

Examples

Some scholars tend to allegorize ancient myths to uncover hidden philosophical meanings.
The director chose to allegorize political conflict through his film's use of animal characters.
Over time, the historical events were increasingly allegorized in literature.

Synset Examples

The story was allegorized over time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to symbolize - to represent or express by symbols

Etymology

From Medieval Latin allegorizare, from allegoria (allegory).
