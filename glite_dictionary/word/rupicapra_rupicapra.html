Rupicapra rupicapra

1. NOUN

/ ˌruːpɪˈkæprə ˌruːpɪˈkæprə / (AmE), / ˌruːpɪˈkæprə ˌruːpɪˈkæprə / (BrE)
A species of goat-antelope, commonly known as the chamois, native to mountainous areas of Europe and western Asia, characterized by upright horns with backward-hooked tips.
species of goat-antelope (chamois)

Examples

The Rupicapra rupicapra is well adapted to the steep, rocky slopes of the Alps.
Researchers observed a herd of Rupicapra rupicapra in their natural mountainous habitat.
Hunting regulations protect the Rupicapra rupicapra in several European countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chamois - A species of goat-antelope native to European mountains

Etymology

From Latin, where 'rupicapra' means 'rock goat' ('rupes' = rock, 'capra' = goat).
