easiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːzi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈiːzi.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being easy, or free from difficulty, effort, or hardship
freedom from difficulty or effort

Examples

The easiness of the exam surprised all the students in the class.
Her easiness with public speaking makes her a great spokesperson for the company.
They admired the easiness with which he navigated complicated situations.

Synset Examples

he rose through the ranks with apparent ease
they put it into containers for ease of transportation
the very easiness of the deed held her back

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ease - absence of difficulty or effort
  • Antonym: difficulty - the state or condition of being hard to do or deal with

Etymology

From easy + -ness, forming the noun from the adjective 'easy'.

state of calmness; lack of worry

Examples

Synset Examples
