fondness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑnd.nəs / (AmE), / ˈfɒnd.nəs / (BrE)
a strong liking or affection for someone or something
strong liking or affection

Examples

His fondness for old books was obvious from his collection.
She had a special fondness for chocolate desserts.
Over the years, their fondness for each other grew stronger.

Synset Examples

he had a fondness for whiskey
she had dismissed him quite brutally, relegating him to the status of a passing fancy, or less

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affection - a gentle feeling of fondness or liking
  • Antonym: dislike - a feeling of not liking or approving of something or someone

Etymology

From 'fond' (Middle English, foolish, later affectionate) + '-ness' (forming nouns of quality or state).

3. NOUN

/ ˈfɑnd.nəs / (AmE), / ˈfɒnd.nəs / (BrE)
A feeling or quality of affection or liking for someone or something.
affection or liking

Examples

His fondness for classical music was evident in his collection of vinyl records.
She always showed a special fondness for her childhood home.
Over the years, my fondness for gardening has only grown stronger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affection - a gentle feeling of liking or love
  • Antonym: dislike - a feeling of distaste or aversion

Etymology

From 'fond' + '-ness' (suffix forming nouns from adjectives). 'Fond' traces to Middle English 'fon' meaning 'foolish'.
