Spica

1. PROPN

/ ˈspaɪkə / (AmE), / ˈspʌɪkə / (BrE)
the brightest star in the constellation Virgo, notable for its blue-white color and great luminosity
brightest star in Virgo

Examples

Astronomers use Spica to help locate other stars in the night sky.
The constellation Virgo is easily found by identifying Spica as its brightest point.
Spica shines with a distinct blue-white hue in the southern sky during spring.

Etymology

From Latin 'spīca' (ear of grain), referring to the star's position in the constellation Virgo, pictured as a maiden holding an ear of wheat.
