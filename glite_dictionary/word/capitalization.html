capitalization

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæpɪtl̩əˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkapɪtələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
an estimation of the total value of a business, especially as determined by the market value of its outstanding shares
estimation of a business's value

Examples

The company's capitalization has doubled in the past five years.
Analysts use capitalization to compare the size of different firms.
A sudden change in stock price can significantly affect a business's capitalization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: market capitalization - the total market value of a company's outstanding shares

Etymology

From capital + -ization; capital from Latin 'capitale' (stock, principal).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkæpɪtələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkæpɪtəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of selling shares of a company's capital stock to raise funds
sale of company stock to raise capital

Examples

The company's capitalization allowed them to expand into new markets.
Strong capitalization is essential for the long-term stability of a business.
After its initial capitalization, the startup attracted several major investors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: financing - the act of providing funds for business activities

Etymology

From capital + -ization, influenced by the process of turning capital into stock; originally from Latin capitālis (principal, chief).

3. NOUN

/ ˌkæpɪtələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkæpɪtələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of writing a word or text using capital letters
writing in capital letters

Examples

Proper capitalization is important when writing formal documents.
The logo features bold capitalization to make the brand name stand out.
Incorrect capitalization can change the meaning of a sentence.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lowercase - writing in small (non-capital) letters

Etymology

From 'capitalize' + '-ation', ultimately from Latin 'capitalis' (of the head) and '-atio' (action or process).
