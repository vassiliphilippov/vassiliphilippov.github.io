provocateur

1. NOUN

/ prəˌvɑkəˈtɜr / (AmE), / prəˌvɒkəˈtɜː / (BrE)
a person, especially a secret agent, who deliberately encourages others to commit illegal or rash acts to incriminate or discredit them.
person who incites others to commit illegal acts

Examples

The police planted a provocateur in the crowd to stir up trouble.
An undercover provocateur tried to get activists to commit acts of vandalism.
There were suspicions that a provocateur was responsible for the sudden outbreak of violence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agent provocateur - a person employed to entice others to break the law so that they can be convicted

Etymology

From French 'provocateur', from 'provoquer' (to provoke).
