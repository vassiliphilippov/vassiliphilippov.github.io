highway

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ.weɪ / (BrE)
A main road, especially one connecting major towns or cities, designed for use by motor vehicles.
major road for vehicles

Examples

The new highway will reduce travel time between the two cities by half.
They stopped at a rest area just off the highway to stretch their legs.
Heavy traffic is expected on the highway during the holiday weekend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: road - a long, hard surface built for vehicles to travel along

Etymology

From Old English heahweg, from heah (high) + weg (way, road). Originally referred to a public road maintained by the state.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ.weɪ / (BrE)
A main road, especially one connecting major towns or cities, used for long-distance travel.
major road for long-distance travel

Examples

Traffic moved quickly on the highway during the early morning hours.
They stopped at a rest area just off the highway for lunch.
The new highway reduced travel time between the cities by almost an hour.

Synset Examples

We took the highway to reach the beach faster.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: freeway - a major road usually with controlled access, especially in the United States
  • Synonym: motorway - a major road designed for fast traffic, especially in the UK
  • Antonym: back road - a minor or less-traveled road

Etymology

From Middle English 'heiwei', from Old English 'hēahweg' ('hēah' meaning 'high' + 'weg' meaning 'way, road').
