caviller

1. NOUN

/ ˈkævələr / (AmE), / ˈkævɪlə / (BrE)
A person who makes petty or unnecessary objections, often raising trivial criticisms.
one who makes petty objections

Examples

The committee grew impatient with the caviller who questioned every minor detail of the proposal.
As a habitual caviller, she managed to delay every decision with endless nitpicking.
Instead of addressing the real issues, he acted as a caviller, focusing on insignificant flaws.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quibbler - a person who raises trivial objections

Etymology

From the verb 'cavil', from Latin 'cavillari' (to jest, satirize).
