nighted

1. ADJ

/ ˈnaɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtɪd / (BrE)
(literary) Overtaken by night or enveloped in darkness.
overtaken by night; enveloped in darkness

Examples

The nighted landscape looked mysterious and foreboding under the clouded sky.
They hurried across the nighted fields, guided only by the faint glow of lanterns.
In the nighted hours, the city seems to belong to shadows and silence.

Synset Examples

benighted (or nighted) travelers hurrying toward home

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benighted - overtaken by darkness or ignorance

Etymology

Formed from 'night' + '-ed', meaning affected by or full of night.
