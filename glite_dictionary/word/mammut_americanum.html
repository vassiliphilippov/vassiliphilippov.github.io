Mammut americanum

1. NOUN

/ məˈmjuːt əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnəm / (AmE), / məˈmjuːt əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnəm / (BrE)
an extinct species of mastodon native to North America, often known as the American mastodon
extinct North American mastodon species

Examples

Fossils of the Mammut americanum have been found throughout North America, providing insight into prehistoric ecosystems.
The Mammut americanum roamed forests and woodlands until it became extinct around 10,000 years ago.
Scientists often study the teeth of Mammut americanum to understand its diet and environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mastodon - an extinct, elephant-like mammal related to Mammut americanum
  • Degree Higher: mammoth - a related genus of large extinct elephants, sometimes confused with mastodons

Etymology

From scientific Latin: genus name ‘Mammut’ and species Latin ‘americanum’ (American).
