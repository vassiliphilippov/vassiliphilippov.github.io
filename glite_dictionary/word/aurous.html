aurous

1. ADJ

/ ˈɔːrəs / (AmE), / ˈɔːrəs / (BrE)
(of a compound) containing gold, especially with a univalent oxidation state.
containing gold (chemistry)

Examples

The chemist explained how aurous chloride differs from auric chloride in its properties.
Researchers discovered a new aurous compound with potential medical applications.
Gold in the aurous state can participate in various types of chemical reactions.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: auric - containing gold with a trivalent (3+) oxidation state

Etymology

From Latin ‘aurum’ (gold).
