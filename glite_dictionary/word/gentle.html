gentle

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒɛntəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛntl̩ / (BrE)
having little force, strength, or impact; not harsh or severe
weak or mild in force or effect

Examples

The gentle rain tapped softly against the windowpane all afternoon.
She spoke in a gentle voice that put everyone at ease.
A gentle breeze rustled the leaves without disturbing the stillness of the park.

Synset Examples

an easy pat on the shoulder
gentle rain
a gentle breeze
a soft (or light) tapping at the window

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mild - not strong or severe; gentle in force or effect
  • Antonym: harsh - having a strong, unpleasant, or severe impact

Etymology

From Latin 'gentilis' (of the same clan, gentle), via Old French 'gentil'.

2. VERB

/ ˈdʒɛn.tl̩ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛn.tl̩ / (BrE)
(historical) To confer the status or title of a gentleman or noble upon someone.
to make someone a noble or gentleman
archaic

Examples

He was gentled after demonstrating great bravery on the battlefield.
It was considered a great honor to be gentled by royal decree.

Etymology

From Old French *gentil*, ultimately from Latin *gentilis* (of a clan, noble). Used as a verb historically to mean 'ennoble'.
