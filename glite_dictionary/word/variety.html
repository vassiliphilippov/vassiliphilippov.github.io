variety

1. NOUN

/ vəˈraɪəti / (AmE), / vəˈraɪəti / (BrE)
a type of entertainment show made up of a series of different, usually unrelated performances such as songs, dances, or comedy acts
show with mixed performances

Examples

The performers took turns on stage during the variety to showcase their many talents.
Every Saturday night, the theater hosts a hilarious variety featuring singers and comedians.
Television audiences once loved the weekly variety that brought magic, music, and humor into their homes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: variety show - a form of entertainment with a mix of acts

6. NOUN

/ vəˈraɪəti / (AmE), / vəˈraɪəti / (BrE)
(Biology) A taxonomic category ranking below species and subspecies, consisting of individuals of a species that differ from others in minor but heritable characteristics.
minor taxonomic category in biology

Examples

There is a rare variety of orchid found only on this island.
Botanists classified the plant as a distinct variety due to its unusual leaf pattern.
Several different varieties of apples grow in that region.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: subspecies - a taxonomic classification beneath species, typically a broader grouping than variety

Etymology

From Latin varietas (difference, diversity), from varius (varied).
