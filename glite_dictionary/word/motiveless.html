motiveless

1. ADJ

/ ˈmoʊtɪvləs / (AmE), / ˈməʊtɪvləs / (BrE)
(of an act or feeling) occurring or done without an apparent reason or motive.
without apparent motive or reason

Examples

The judge was baffled by the motiveless crime, which seemed to have no explanation.
Her motiveless cruelty shocked everyone at the animal shelter.
Investigators searched for evidence but found the shooting appeared motiveless.

Synset Examples

motiveless malignity
unprovoked and dastardly attack

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unprovoked - (of an action) done without cause or justification

Etymology

Formed from 'motive' + '-less', meaning 'without motive'.
