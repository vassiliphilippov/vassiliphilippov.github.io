weathercock

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛðɚˌkɑk / (AmE), / ˈwɛðəkɒk / (BrE)
a weathervane in the shape of a rooster, typically used to show the direction of the wind and often found on rooftops
weathervane shaped like a rooster

Examples

The old stone church was topped with a bronze weathercock that spun with every gust of wind.
He watched the weathercock turning, trying to predict whether the storm would pass.
From the garden, you can just make out the silhouette of the weathercock against the morning sky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weathervane - instrument for showing the direction of the wind

Etymology

From 'weather' + 'cock', meaning rooster; refers to the traditional rooster design of early weathervanes.
