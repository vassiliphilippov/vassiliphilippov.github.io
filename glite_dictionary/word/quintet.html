quintet

1. NOUN

/ kwɪnˈtɛt / (AmE), / kwɪnˈtɛt / (BrE)
A group or set of five similar things considered as a single unit.
group of five things

Examples

The chef arranged a quintet of desserts on the platter for the guests to sample.
A quintet of new policies will be implemented next year.
She received a quintet of letters from her friends all on the same day.

Etymology

From Italian 'quintetto', diminutive of 'quinto' (fifth), from Latin 'quintus' (fifth).

2. NOUN

/ kwɪnˈtɛt / (AmE), / kwɪnˈtɛt / (BrE)
A musical composition written for five performers or instruments.
musical composition for five performers

Examples

The string quintet by Mozart is one of his most celebrated chamber works.
Our ensemble will perform a new quintet at the festival next month.
She composed a jazz quintet for saxophone, trumpet, piano, bass, and drums.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: quartet - a musical composition for four performers
  • Degree Higher: sextet - a musical composition for six performers

Etymology

From Italian 'quintetto', from 'quinto' meaning 'fifth'.
