to imbibe

1. VERB

/ ɪmˈbaɪb / (AmE), / ɪmˈbaɪb / (BrE)
(of a substance) to absorb gas, light, or heat into a solution.
absorb gas, light, or heat into a solution

Examples

Certain types of charcoal can imbibe gases from the air.
The dye is able to imbibe light, making it useful for solar cells.
Some solutions imbibe heat when undergoing chemical reactions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to absorb - to take in or soak up (energy, liquid, or another substance)

Etymology

From Latin 'imbibere' (to drink in, absorb).

2. VERB

/ ɪmˈbaɪb / (AmE), / ɪmˈbaɪb / (BrE)
(formal) to absorb and retain ideas, knowledge, or principles in the mind.
absorb and retain (ideas, etc.) in the mind

Examples

Young students are encouraged to imbibe the values of honesty and respect from an early age.
She traveled the world to imbibe new cultures and perspectives.
It is important to imbibe the lessons of history and avoid repeating past mistakes.

Synset Examples

Imbibe ethical principles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to absorb - to take in and assimilate (ideas or information)

Etymology

From Latin 'imbibere', from 'in-' (in) + 'bibere' (to drink).
