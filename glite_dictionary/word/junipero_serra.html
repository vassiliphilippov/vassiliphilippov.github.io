Junipero Serra

1. PROPN

/ huːˈniː.pə.roʊ ˈsɛr.ə / (AmE), / ˌhjuː.nɪˈpeə.rəʊ ˈser.ə / (BrE)
(1713–1784) A Spanish Franciscan missionary who established missions in California and played a major role in its early European settlement.
Spanish missionary in California, 1713–1784

Examples

Many cities and landmarks in California are named after Junipero Serra.
Junipero Serra founded several missions along the coast that later became the basis for major cities.
The legacy of Junipero Serra is still debated by historians today.
