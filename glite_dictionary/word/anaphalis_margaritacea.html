Anaphalis margaritacea

1. NOUN

/ əˌnæf.əˈlaɪs ˌmɑr.ɡə.rɪˈteɪ.si.ə / (AmE), / əˌnaf.əˈlaɪs ˌmɑː.ɡər.ɪˈteɪ.si.ə / (BrE)
A North American plant of the daisy family, known as pearly everlasting, with woolly leaves and small white flower heads with pearly bracts.
North American plant with white flower heads

Examples

Anaphalis margaritacea is often planted in gardens for its attractive white blooms.
Native Americans traditionally used Anaphalis margaritacea for medicinal purposes.
The silver foliage of Anaphalis margaritacea makes it a popular choice for dried flower arrangements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pearly everlasting - Common English name for Anaphalis margaritacea

Etymology

From modern Latin; genus name from Greek 'anaphalis', and species 'margaritacea' from Latin 'margarita' (pearl), referring to the plant's pearly appearance.
