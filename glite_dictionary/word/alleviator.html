alleviator

1. NOUN

/ əˈliːviˌeɪtər / (AmE), / əˈliːvɪeɪtə / (BrE)
a person, especially a therapist, who makes suffering more bearable or less severe
person who makes suffering more bearable

Examples

The pain alleviator helped her manage her discomfort during recovery.
As a skilled alleviator of emotional distress, the counselor provided immense comfort to his patients.
Many see nurses as natural alleviators in times of crisis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reliever - a person or thing that relieves pain or distress

Etymology

From the verb 'alleviate', ultimately from Latin 'alleviare' (to lighten).

2. NOUN

/ əˈliː.vi.eɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / əˈliː.vi.eɪ.tə / (BrE)
a substance or treatment that reduces pain or discomfort without curing the underlying problem
remedy that relieves pain or discomfort

Examples

The medication acted as an alleviator of her chronic back pain.
He searched for a natural alleviator to help with his headaches.
Although an alleviator can provide relief, it does not address the underlying cause.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palliative - a remedy that relieves symptoms without curing the disease
  • Antonym: cure - a remedy that eliminates a disease or condition

Etymology

From Latin 'alleviare' (to lighten, relieve) + English '-or' (agent noun suffix).
