Seckel

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛkəl / (AmE), / ˈsɛkəl / (BrE)
A small, sweet variety of pear with yellowish to reddish-brown skin.
a type of small pear

Examples

The fruit basket included a ripe Seckel among the larger pears.
She prefers Seckel pears because they are especially sweet and juicy.
Farmers markets often sell Seckel pears during the autumn harvest.

Etymology

Named after the Pennsylvania farmer who first cultivated it in the early 19th century, originally Seckel's pear.
