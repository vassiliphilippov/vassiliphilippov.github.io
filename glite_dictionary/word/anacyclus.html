Anacyclus

1. NOUN

/ əˈnæsɪˌkləs / (AmE), / əˈnæsɪˌkləs / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the daisy family (Asteraceae), including species such as Spanish pellitory, native to the Mediterranean region.
genus of plants in the daisy family

Examples

The roots of Anacyclus have long been used in traditional medicine.
Botanists identified the specimen as belonging to the genus Anacyclus.
Anacyclus species are often found in dry, sandy soils across the Mediterranean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Spanish pellitory - Common name for several species in the Anacyclus genus

Etymology

From Greek anákyklos, meaning 'circle' or 'wheel', referring to the shape of the flower head.
