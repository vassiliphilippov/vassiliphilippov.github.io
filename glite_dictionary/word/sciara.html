Sciara

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈɛərə / (AmE), / saɪˈɑːrə / (BrE)
A genus of dark-winged fungus gnats belonging to the family Sciaridae, found in damp habitats and known for larvae that feed on fungi and decaying matter.
genus of fungus gnats

Examples

The larvae of Sciara can sometimes cause damage to greenhouse plants.
Researchers collected specimens from the Sciara genus to study their breeding habits.
Members of the Sciara genus are important decomposers in many ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sciaridae - the family of dark-winged fungus gnats to which Sciara belongs

Etymology

From New Latin Sciara, original genus name assigned in scientific taxonomy.
