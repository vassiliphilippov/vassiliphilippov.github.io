Mysticeti

1. NOUN

/ mɪˈstɪsətaɪ / (AmE), / mɪˈstɪsəti / (BrE)
A suborder of large marine mammals known as baleen whales, including the right whales, rorquals, blue whales, and humpbacks, characterized by the presence of baleen plates instead of teeth.
suborder of baleen whales

Examples

The Mysticeti suborder includes species such as the blue whale and the humpback whale.
Unlike their toothed relatives, Mysticeti possess baleen plates for filter feeding.
Researchers study the migratory patterns of Mysticeti to better understand ocean ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Odontoceti - suborder of toothed whales, including dolphins and sperm whales
  • Synonym: baleen whale - general term for whales in the suborder Mysticeti

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek mystikētēs (whalebone whale, from mystax, mystak- 'mustache', referring to baleen whiskers).

2. NOUN

/ mɪˈstɪsəti / (AmE), / mɪˈstɪsəti / (BrE)
a suborder of large marine mammals known as baleen whales, including right whales, rorquals, blue whales, and humpback whales
suborder of baleen whales

Examples

The Mysticeti include some of the largest animals on Earth, such as the blue whale.
Unlike toothed whales, members of Mysticeti use baleen plates to filter their food.
Scientists study the migration patterns of Mysticeti to understand changes in ocean ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baleen whale - a large whale of the suborder Mysticeti, which has baleen plates instead of teeth

Etymology

From Greek mystax (mustache) + kētos (whale), referring to the mustache-like baleen plates.
