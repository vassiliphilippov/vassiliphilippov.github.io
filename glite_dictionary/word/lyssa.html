lyssa

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪsə / (AmE), / ˈlɪsə / (BrE)
An acute viral disease of the nervous system in warm-blooded animals, commonly known as rabies, usually transmitted by the bite of an infected animal.
rabies; acute viral disease in animals
medical

Examples

Researchers studied outbreaks of lyssa in wild fox populations.
Vaccination is the most effective way to prevent lyssa in domestic animals.
If untreated, lyssa can lead to severe neurological symptoms and death.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rabies - a fatal viral disease causing inflammation of the brain in mammals

Etymology

From Greek 'lyssa' meaning 'madness' or 'rage'.
