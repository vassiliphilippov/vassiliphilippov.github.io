bodywork

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑdiˌwɜrk / (AmE), / ˈbɒdiˌwɜːk / (BrE)
the metal or fiberglass outer shell and panels of a motor vehicle, excluding the engine, frame, and interior
exterior shell of a vehicle

Examples

The car's bodywork was damaged in the collision but the engine was unharmed.
He spent hours polishing the bodywork to restore its shine.
Classic cars often require custom bodywork repairs to maintain their appearance.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːdiˌwɜrk / (AmE), / ˈbɒdiˌwɜːk / (BrE)
the making or repairing of the main structure of a vehicle, excluding the engine and chassis
making or repairing vehicle bodies

Examples

The bodywork of the car was damaged in the accident and needed extensive repairs.
She specializes in bodywork, fixing dents and restoring paint on vintage cars.
After the hailstorm, dozens of cars required new bodywork to fix the damage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coachwork - the body of a motor vehicle, especially historically or when custom-built
