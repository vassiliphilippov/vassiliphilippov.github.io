anus

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈeɪnəs / (BrE)
the opening at the end of the digestive tract through which solid waste leaves the body
the opening at the end of the digestive tract

Examples

Doctors recommend paying attention to symptoms such as pain or bleeding from the anus.
The human digestive tract ends at the anus.
Certain medical conditions can cause discomfort or irritation around the anus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rectum - final section of the large intestine, ending at the anus

Etymology

From Latin anus (ring, anus).
