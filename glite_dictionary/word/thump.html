thump

1. NOUN

/ θʌmp / (AmE), / θʌmp / (BrE)
a heavy, dull sound made by the impact of a heavy object
heavy dull sound from impact

Examples

There was a loud thump as the box hit the floor.
The ball landed with a thump on the roof of the car.
I heard a sudden thump coming from the attic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thud - a dull sound, as of a heavy object striking the ground

Etymology

Echoic formation imitating the sound of a blow or heavy fall.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07411545-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ θʌmp / (AmE), / θʌmp / (BrE)
a heavy blow, especially one made with the hand or a similar object
a heavy blow (esp. with the hand)

Examples

He landed a solid thump on his opponent's shoulder during the match.
There was a loud thump as the book fell to the floor.
She gave the table a thump to get everyone's attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: punch - a blow with the fist
  • Degree Lower: tap - a light or gentle hit
  • Degree Higher: bang - a sudden loud hit or impact

Etymology

Imitative of the sound produced by a heavy blow.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01177589-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ θʌmp / (AmE), / θʌmp / (BrE)
to make or cause to make a heavy, dull sound by striking something
make a heavy, dull sound

Examples

We heard his heart thump loudly in the quiet room.
The book thumped onto the floor when it slipped from her hands.
Heavy rain thumped against the window all night.

Synset Examples

the knocker thudded against the front door

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thud - to make a dull, heavy sound

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin, representing the sound.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02189056-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ θʌmp / (AmE), / θʌmp / (BrE)
to hit something or someone heavily, especially with your fist or a blunt object
hit or strike heavily

Examples

She thumped the table in frustration, making the glasses rattle.
The boxer thumped his opponent with a powerful right hook.
He could hear someone thumping on the wall next door.

Synset Examples

the salesman pounded the door knocker
a bible-thumping Southern Baptist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pound - to strike repeatedly with heavy blows
  • Degree Lower: tap - to strike lightly and quickly

Etymology

Imitative origin, echoing the sound of a heavy blow.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01417280-v
Synset POS: v

5. VERB

/ θʌmp / (AmE), / θʌmp / (BrE)
(of a heart or pulse) to beat or move with a steady, heavy, rhythmic sound.
beat rhythmically (heart, pulse)

Examples

Her heart thumped loudly in her chest as she waited for the results.
I could feel my pulse thumping through my veins after the intense workout.
His heart thumps faster whenever he gets nervous.

Synset Examples

Her heart was beating fast

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beat - to strike repeatedly; (of a heart) to pulsate

Etymology

Imitative origin, echoing the sound of a heavy blow.
