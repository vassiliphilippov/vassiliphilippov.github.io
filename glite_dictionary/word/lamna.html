Lamna

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæmnə / (AmE), / ˈlæmnə / (BrE)
a genus of mackerel sharks in the family Lamnidae, including the porbeagle and the salmon shark.
genus of mackerel sharks

Examples

Lamna includes well-known species such as the porbeagle and the salmon shark.
Biologists observed the migratory patterns of Lamna in the North Atlantic Ocean.
The teeth structure of Lamna differentiates it from other genera in the Lamnidae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lamnidae - the family of mackerel sharks encompassing Lamna and similar genera
  • Synonym: porbeagle - a species of shark in the genus Lamna

Etymology

From Greek lamna, possibly related to 'lamia' (a mythical sea-monster).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlæmnə / (AmE), / ˈlæmnə / (BrE)
A genus of mackerel sharks in the family Lamnidae, which includes the porbeagle and salmon shark.
genus of mackerel sharks

Examples

The porbeagle belongs to the Lamna genus, known for their streamlined bodies and swift swimming abilities.
Marine biologists study members of the Lamna genus to understand shark migration patterns.
The Lamna genus contains species found in both the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Lamnidae - family of large mackerel sharks including Lamna

Etymology

From Greek lamna (a kind of fish, possibly similar to a shark), used in scientific Latin.
