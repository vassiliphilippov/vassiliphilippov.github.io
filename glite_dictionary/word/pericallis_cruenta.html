Pericallis cruenta

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛrɪˈkælɪs kruːˈɛntə / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪˈkælɪs kruːˈɛntə / (BrE)
a herbaceous plant native to the Canary Islands, widely cultivated for its blue, purple, red, or variegated daisy-like flowers
a flowering herb from the Canary Islands

Examples

Many gardeners appreciate Pericallis cruenta for its dazzling display of colorful flowers each spring.
The botanical garden featured a vibrant bed of Pericallis cruenta in bloom.
Hybrid varieties of Pericallis cruenta are popular in ornamental horticulture.

Etymology

From genus name 'Pericallis' (Greek peri- 'around' + kallos 'beauty') and Latin 'cruenta' (bloody, referring to color).
