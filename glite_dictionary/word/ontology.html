ontology

1. NOUN

/ ɑnˈtɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ɒnˈtɒlədʒi / (BrE)
(in computer science) a structured framework that organizes knowledge within a specific domain, detailing entities, concepts, and relationships, typically in a hierarchical way.
knowledge organization system in computer science

Examples

The AI system was built using a complex ontology that mapped out medical concepts and their relationships.
Researchers developed an ontology to structure all the information for the digital library project.
Without a shared ontology, different systems have trouble exchanging and understanding data.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taxonomy - classification system, especially in science

Etymology

From Greek ontos (being) + -logy (study of); adapted in computer science to describe formal knowledge structures.

2. NOUN

/ ɑnˈtɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ɒnˈtɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The branch of metaphysics that studies the nature of being, existence, and reality.
study of being and existence

Examples

She is writing her thesis on ontology and the questions of existence.
The debate between scientists and philosophers often touches on issues of ontology.
Modern information science sometimes borrows the concept of ontology to organize complex data.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metaphysics - the branch of philosophy that deals with the fundamental nature of reality

Etymology

From Greek *ont-, on* (being) + *-logia* (study, discourse).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06172563-n
Synset POS: n
