readout

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːdˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈriːd.aʊt / (BrE)
information shown or recorded by an electronic device, often displayed on a screen or printed out.
information displayed by a device

Examples

The digital thermometer gave a clear readout of the patient's temperature.
The technician recorded the pressure readout from the gauge.
Please check the screen for the latest readout of the experiment's results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: display - visual presentation of information on a screen
  • Synonym: output - the information produced by a device or system

Etymology

From the verb 'read out' (to read something aloud), later applied to information electronically output for reading, especially in 20th-century technical contexts.

2. NOUN

/ ˈriːdˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈriːdˌaʊt / (BrE)
a display or report of data produced by a computer or other device, typically in a form that can be read easily
data output from a computer or device

Examples

The machine provides a readout of the patient's heart rate and blood pressure.
After the experiment, the scientist checked the readout on her computer.
A digital readout on the dashboard displays the car's speed and fuel level.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: output - information produced by a computer or process

Etymology

From 'read' + 'out,' referring to information being read out from a device.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07279857-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈriːdaʊt / (AmE), / ˈriːdaʊt / (BrE)
an electronic device or screen that displays information, especially data or measurements from an instrument
device or display showing electronic data

Examples

The digital readout showed that the temperature had reached 100 degrees.
She glanced at the readout on the monitor to check the patient's vital signs.
Engineers waited anxiously for the first results to appear on the readout.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: display - an electronic screen or device for showing information

Etymology

From 'read' + 'out', originally indicating the act of reading information out of a system.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04065706-n
Synset POS: n
