sawbill

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔːˌbɪl / (AmE), / ˈsɔːˌbɪl / (BrE)
A large diving duck with a slender, hooked bill that has serrated edges, adapted for catching fish; typically refers to merganser species.
a fish-eating duck with a serrated bill

Examples

A sawbill glided across the lake, its striking crest clearly visible in the morning sun.
Fishermen enjoy watching the sawbills dive for fish in this part of the river.
The distinctive bill of a sawbill helps it catch slippery prey with ease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: merganser - a type of sawbill duck known for its serrated bill

Etymology

From 'saw' (referring to serrated bill) + 'bill' (beak).
