primitivism

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪm.ɪ.tɪˌvɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈprɪm.ɪ.tɪ.vɪz.əm / (BrE)
the condition or quality of being wild, crude, or unrefined; a state lacking sophistication or modern development
a wild or unrefined state

Examples

The primitivism of the tribe's early rituals fascinated the visiting anthropologists.
Some artists sought inspiration in what they saw as the primitivism of nature untouched by technology.
He contrasted the primitivism of the ancient ways with the complexity of modern society.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: civilization - an advanced state of human society

Etymology

From French 'primitivisme', based on Latin 'primitivus' (first, original).

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɪm.ɪ.tɪˌvɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈprɪm.ɪ.tɪ.vɪ.zəm / (BrE)
a style or movement in art characterized by the imitation or emulation of the art of primitive peoples or children, often emphasizing simplicity and directness of expression
art style imitating primitive or child art

Examples

The painter adopted primitivism to evoke a sense of innocence in his landscapes.
Many modern artists were influenced by primitivism and incorporated bold lines into their work.
Her exhibit explores primitivism through sculptures reminiscent of ancient tribal art.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naïve art - art created by a person without formal training
  • Antonym: realism - art movement focused on accurate, detailed depiction of reality

Etymology

From French 'primitivisme', from 'primitif' (primitive) + '-isme' (-ism).
