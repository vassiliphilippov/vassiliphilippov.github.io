attosecond

1. NOUN

/ ˈætoʊˌsɛkənd / (AmE), / ˈatəʊˌsɛkənd / (BrE)
A unit of time equal to one quintillionth (10⁻¹⁸) of a second.
one quintillionth of a second

Examples

The laser produced pulses as short as a single attosecond.
An attosecond is to a second what a second is to the age of the universe.
Physicists use attoseconds to measure the movement of electrons in atoms.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: femtosecond - A unit of time equal to one quadrillionth (10⁻¹⁵) of a second
  • Degree Lower: zeptosecond - A unit of time equal to one sextillionth (10⁻²¹) of a second

Etymology

From 'atto-' (a metric prefix meaning 10⁻¹⁸, from Danish 'atten', meaning eighteen) + 'second'.
