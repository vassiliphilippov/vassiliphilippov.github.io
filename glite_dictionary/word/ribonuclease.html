ribonuclease

1. NOUN

/ ˌraɪbəˈnuːkliˌeɪs / (AmE), / ˌraɪbəˈnjuːkliˌeɪs / (BrE)
an enzyme that catalyzes the breakdown of ribonucleic acid (RNA) into smaller components
enzyme that degrades RNA
medical

Examples

The scientist added ribonuclease to the solution to degrade the RNA.
Some viruses evade destruction by producing inhibitors of ribonuclease.
Measurement of ribonuclease activity can help identify certain genetic disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enzyme - a substance produced by a living organism that acts as a catalyst to bring about a specific biochemical reaction

Etymology

From 'ribonucleic acid' + '-ase', a suffix used for enzymes.
