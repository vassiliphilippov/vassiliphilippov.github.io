hydrotherapy

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪ.droʊˈθɛr.ə.pi / (AmE), / ˌhaɪ.drəʊˈθɛr.ə.pi / (BrE)
the use of water, both internally and externally, for the treatment of disease or injury
water-based treatment for disease
medical

Examples

After her surgery, the doctor recommended hydrotherapy to aid her recovery.
He found relief from his arthritis pain through regular hydrotherapy sessions at the clinic.
Many spas now offer specialized hydrotherapy treatments designed to reduce stress and muscle tension.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: physiotherapy - the treatment of disease or injury by physical methods such as massage and exercise
  • Synonym: balneotherapy - the treatment of disease by bathing, especially in mineral springs

Etymology

From Greek hydor (water) + therapeia (healing, treatment).
