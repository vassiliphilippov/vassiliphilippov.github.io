intertrigo

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.tɚˈtraɪ.goʊ / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈtraɪ.gəʊ / (BrE)
inflammation or chafing of the skin at points where two skin surfaces are in close contact, commonly occurring in areas such as the armpits, under the breasts, or between the thighs
skin inflammation from chafing
medical

Examples

After running long distances in humid weather, she developed intertrigo in the folds of her skin.
Treating intertrigo often involves keeping the affected area dry and applying medicated creams.
Obese individuals are more susceptible to intertrigo due to increased skin-to-skin contact.

Etymology

From Latin intertrīgo (chafing), from inter- (between) and terere (to rub).
