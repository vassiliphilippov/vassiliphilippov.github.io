clad

1. ADJ

/ klæd / (AmE), / klæd / (BrE)
covered or encased with a particular material, especially a thin layer of metal.
covered, usually with metal

Examples

The museum displayed a clad statue, its bronze coating gleaming in the light.
The building's steel-clad exterior protects it from fire and harsh weather.
Their new armored van was clad in reinforced panels for added security.

Synset Examples

steel-clad
armor-clad

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coated - covered with a layer of something
  • Antonym: bare - without any covering or protection

Etymology

From Old English *clæthian* (to clothe), related to 'clothe'.

2. ADJ

/ klæd / (AmE), / klæd / (BrE)
wearing or covered with a particular type of clothing; sometimes used in combination
wearing or covered with clothing

Examples

The mountain was clad in a blanket of snow after the storm.
The knight arrived clad in shining armor at the castle gate.
The dancers were clad in colorful costumes for the festival.

Synset Examples

clothed and in his right mind
proud of her well-clothed family
nurses clad in white
white-clad nurses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clothed - wearing clothes
  • Antonym: naked - not wearing any clothes

Etymology

Past participle of 'clothe,' from Old English *clāþian* (to clothe).

Source: WordNet
