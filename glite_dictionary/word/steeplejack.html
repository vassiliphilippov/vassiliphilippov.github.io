steeplejack

1. NOUN

/ ˈstiːpəlˌdʒæk / (AmE), / ˈstiːpəlˌdʒæk / (BrE)
a person who builds, repairs, or maintains very tall structures such as steeples, chimneys, or towers
person who works on tall structures

Examples

The steeplejack scaled the church spire to repair loose tiles.
As a steeplejack, she often worked hundreds of feet above the ground.
A team of steeplejacks was hired to restore the historic clock tower.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roofer - person who constructs or repairs roofs

Etymology

From 'steeple' (a tall tower) + 'jack' (a common term for workman), first used in the 19th century.
