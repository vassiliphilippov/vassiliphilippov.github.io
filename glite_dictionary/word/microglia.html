microglia

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈkroʊɡliə / (AmE), / maɪˈkrəʊɡlɪə / (BrE)
Small glial cells in the central nervous system that act as the main form of active immune defense in the brain and spinal cord.
immune cells in the brain and spinal cord
medical

Examples

Activated microglia play a crucial role in the body's response to brain injuries.
Researchers are studying how microglia contribute to neurodegenerative diseases such as Alzheimer's.
The microglia responded rapidly to the infection, removing damaged cells and debris.

Etymology

From Greek 'mikros' (small) + 'glia' (glue).
