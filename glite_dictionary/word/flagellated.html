flagellated

1. ADJ

/ fləˈdʒɛl.eɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / fləˈdʒɛl.ə.tɪd / (BrE)
(of a cell or organism) having one or more flagella; resembling or equipped with a whip-like or lash-like appendage.
having a whip-like appendage (flagellum)

Examples

Many protozoa are flagellated, allowing them to move actively in water.
The flagellated sperm cells swim toward the egg using their whip-like tails.
Some bacteria are flagellated and can propel themselves through liquids easily.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonflagellated - lacking flagella (whip-like appendages)

Etymology

From Latin 'flagellum' (whip, scourge) plus '-ated' (possessing).
