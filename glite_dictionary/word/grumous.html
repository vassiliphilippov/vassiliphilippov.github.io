grumous

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡruːməs / (AmE), / ˈɡruːməs / (BrE)
(of a liquid) having been transformed into a soft semisolid or lumpy mass; coagulated or curdled.
coagulated or lumpy; curdled

Examples

The pathologist described the blood sample as grumous in appearance.
After standing for hours, the soup took on a strange, grumous texture.
The veterinarian noted grumous deposits in the animal's wound.

Synset Examples

coagulated blood
curdled milk
grumous blood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coagulated - (of a fluid) changed into a thickened, semi-solid mass
  • Synonym: curdled - (of a liquid, especially milk) separated into curds or lumps

Etymology

From Latin grumosus, from grumus (heap, little hill).
