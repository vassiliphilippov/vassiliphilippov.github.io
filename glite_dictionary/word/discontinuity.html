discontinuity

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪs.kənˈtuː.nə.t̬i / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.kənˈtjuː.nə.ti / (BrE)
A break or interruption in the normal course or continuity of something; a lack of connection or continuous sequence.
a break or interruption in continuity

Examples

The discontinuity in the data suggests an error occurred during collection.
Many scientists were puzzled by the sudden discontinuity in the geological record.
A social discontinuity often follows major political upheavals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: continuity - the uninterrupted connection, succession, or flow

Etymology

From Late Latin discontinuus (interrupted), from Latin dis- (apart) + continuus (continuous).
