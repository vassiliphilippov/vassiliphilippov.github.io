suasion

1. NOUN

/ swɛɪʒən / (AmE), / swɛɪʒən / (BrE)
the act of persuading someone to do or believe something; persuasive communication or influence
the act of persuading; persuasion

Examples

Despite his repeated suasion, she resisted the idea of moving abroad.
The government relied on moral suasion rather than strict regulation to reduce pollution.
Through gentle suasion, the teacher convinced the students to participate in the activity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: persuasion - the act of convincing someone to do or believe something

Etymology

From Latin suasio (persuasion), from suadere (to advise, persuade).
