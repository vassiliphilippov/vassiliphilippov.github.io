bastardization

1. NOUN

/ ˌbæstərdɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌbɑːstədʌɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of lowering the quality, value, or purity of something by introducing inferior elements or by corrupting its original form
act of debasing or corrupting

Examples

Many linguists lament the bastardization of certain words due to modern slang.
The bastardization of the classic recipe led to criticism from traditionalists.
He accused the developers of the bastardization of the historic neighborhood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corruption - the act or process of making something morally depraved or debased
  • Antonym: purification - the process of making something pure

Etymology

From 'bastard' + '-ization'; 'bastard' from Old French 'bastart', meaning illegitimate child.

2. NOUN

/ ˌbæ.stɚ.daɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌbɑː.stə.daɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of declaring someone illegitimate, especially as a child not legally recognized by both parents; the state of being rendered a bastard
action of declaring illegitimacy
archaic legal

Examples

After the court's decision, the bastardization of the heirs was officially recognized.
In earlier centuries, bastardization could have severe consequences for inheritance rights.
The annulment led to the bastardization of all children born within that union.

Synset Examples

the annulment of their marriage resulted in the bastardization of their children

Related Terms

  • Antonym: legitimation - the process of making legitimate or lawful, especially a child

Etymology

From 'bastard' (from Old French *bastard*) + '-ization' (forming nouns indicating action or process).

Source: WordNet
