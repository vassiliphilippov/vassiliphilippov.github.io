intercalation

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌtɝːkəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌtɜːkəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
The insertion of a day, week, or month into a calendar, often to correct its alignment with astronomical or seasonal events.
insertion into a calendar

Examples

The ancient Romans used intercalation to keep their calendar in sync with the solar year.
A leap day is a form of intercalation in the Gregorian calendar.
Intercalation was critical in ancient societies for planning agricultural festivals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leap year - a year with an extra day added to keep the calendar year synchronized with the astronomical year

Etymology

From Latin 'intercalatio' (insertion of a day), from 'intercalare' (to proclaim an extra day in the calendar).
