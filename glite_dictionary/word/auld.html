auld

1. ADJ

/ ɔld / (AmE), / ɔːld / (BrE)
(Scottish) old; used chiefly in Scottish English, especially in literary and traditional contexts.
Scottish term for 'old'
british

Examples

The villagers gathered in the auld church at the corner of the square.
They sang 'Auld Lang Syne' to remember auld times with friends.
He wore his auld coat, patched and faded from years of use.

Synset Examples

auld lang syne

Related Terms

  • Synonym: old - having lived for a long time; not young or new

Etymology

From Scots 'auld', variant of English 'old', from Old English 'eald'.
