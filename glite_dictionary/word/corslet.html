corslet

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrs.lət / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.slɪt / (BrE)
A piece of body armor covering the trunk, typically comprising a breastplate and back piece.
armor for the trunk of the body
archaic

Examples

The knight donned his corslet before heading into battle.
Historical reenactors often wear a replica corslet as part of their costumes.
The museum displayed a 16th-century corslet alongside other medieval armor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breastplate - a piece of armor covering the chest
  • Synonym: cuirass - a piece of armor consisting of breastplate and back piece

Etymology

From Old French corselet, diminutive of cors (body), from Latin corpus (body).
