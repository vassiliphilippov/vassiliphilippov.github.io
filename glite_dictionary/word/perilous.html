perilous

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɛrələs / (AmE), / ˈpɛrɪləs / (BrE)
Full of danger or risk; very hazardous.
full of danger or risk

Examples

Climbing the icy mountain was a perilous undertaking, but the team was well prepared.
The journey through the rainforest became increasingly perilous as night fell.
Ignoring the warnings and swimming in the rough sea was a perilous decision.

Synset Examples

a parlous journey on stormy seas
a perilous voyage across the Atlantic in a small boat
the precarious life of an undersea diver
dangerous surgery followed by a touch-and-go recovery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dangerous - likely to cause harm or injury
  • Antonym: safe - free from danger or risk

Etymology

From Old French 'perilleus', from Latin 'periculosus', from 'periculum' (danger).
