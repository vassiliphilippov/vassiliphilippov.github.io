tabi

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑː.bi / (AmE), / ˈtæb.i / (BrE)
A traditional Japanese sock with a separation between the big toe and other toes, typically worn with thong sandals.
Japanese split-toe sock

Examples

The kimono-clad performer wore white tabi with her traditional sandals.
You should remove your shoes and tabi before entering the tatami room.
She bought a new pair of tabi at the festival to match her yukata.

Etymology

From Japanese 足袋 (tabi), meaning 'foot bag'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɑːbi / (AmE), / ˈtæbi / (BrE)
A traditional Japanese sock that separates the big toe from the other toes, typically worn with thong sandals.
Japanese sock with big toe separation

Examples

He wore traditional tabi with his kimono during the ceremony.
The tabi made it easier to wear the wooden sandals known as geta.
Her white tabi contrasted with the dark color of her yukata.

Etymology

From Japanese 足袋 (tabi), meaning 'foot bag'.
