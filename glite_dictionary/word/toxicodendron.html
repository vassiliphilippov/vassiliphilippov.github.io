Toxicodendron

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɑksɪoʊˈdɛndrən / (AmE), / ˌtɒksɪəʊˈdɛndrən / (BrE)
a genus of shrubs and small trees, including poison ivy and poison oak, with foliage that is poisonous to the touch
genus of poisonous plants

Examples

Toxicodendron species include poison ivy, poison oak, and poison sumac.
Researchers found a new Toxicodendron shrub growing in the forest.
Avoid touching Toxicodendron leaves, as they can cause severe skin irritation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rhus - A genus of shrubs and trees, formerly including Toxicodendron

Etymology

From Greek toxikon (poison) + dendron (tree).

2. NOUN

/ ˌtɑksɪkoʊˈdɛndrən / (AmE), / ˌtɒksɪkəʊˈdɛndrən / (BrE)
a genus of shrubs and small trees, including poison ivy and poison oak, whose foliage can cause skin irritation on contact; native to North America and northern South America
genus of plants with poisonous foliage

Examples

Many people develop a rash after coming into contact with Toxicodendron species such as poison ivy.
The Toxicodendron genus includes both shrubs and small trees native to North America.
Botanists debated for years whether to classify poison oak in the Toxicodendron genus or elsewhere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poison ivy - a common plant in the genus Toxicodendron that causes skin irritation
  • Synonym: Rhus - a related genus to Toxicodendron, sometimes formerly considered the same

Etymology

From Greek toxikon (poison) + dendron (tree).
