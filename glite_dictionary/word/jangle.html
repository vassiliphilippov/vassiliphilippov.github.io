jangle

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒæŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
a harsh, discordant, metallic sound
harsh metallic sound

Examples

The jangle of keys startled the dog from its nap.
He listened to the jangle of bells outside the old shop.
The coins produced a sharp jangle as they dropped into the tin.

Synset Examples

the jingle of coins
the jangle of spurs

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: jingle - a light, ringing sound, usually pleasant and rhythmic

Etymology

Probably from Old French jangler (to chatter, prattle).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07400598-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈdʒæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒæŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
to make a harsh, discordant, ringing sound, typically of metal objects striking each other
make a harsh metallic ringing sound

Examples

Coins jangle in his pocket with every step he takes.
The wind caused the chimes to jangle noisily on the porch.
Her bracelets jangle whenever she moves her arm.

Synset Examples

The keys were jingling in his pocket

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jingle - to make a light, ringing sound like small bells
