widgeon

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪdʒən / (AmE), / ˈwɪdʒən / (BrE)
a freshwater duck of Eurasia and northern Africa, related to mallards and teals, known for its rounded head and distinctive whistling call.
a species of freshwater duck

Examples

A flock of widgeon landed on the lake at dawn, their distinctive whistles echoing across the water.
During the winter, large numbers of widgeon can be seen feeding in marshes and wetlands.
The birdwatchers were excited to spot a rare American widgeon mingling with local ducks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mallard - a common wild duck species, Anas platyrhynchos
  • Synonym: teal - any of several small freshwater ducks

Etymology

Origin unknown; recorded in English since the 16th century.
