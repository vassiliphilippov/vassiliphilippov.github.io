precentorship

1. NOUN

/ priˈsɛntərˌʃɪp / (AmE), / prɪˈsɛntəʃɪp / (BrE)
the office or position of a precentor, typically in a church or cathedral
position of a precentor (church singing leader)

Examples

After years of dedicated service, she was finally appointed to the precentorship at the cathedral.
The responsibilities of the precentorship include organizing choirs and leading hymnals.
He relinquished the precentorship when he retired from active church duties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: precentor - person who leads the singing in a church or cathedral

Etymology

Formed from 'precentor' + '-ship', denoting office or position. 'Precentor' from Latin 'praecentor', from 'praecinere' (to sing before).
