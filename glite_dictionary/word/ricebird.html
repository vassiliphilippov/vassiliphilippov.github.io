ricebird

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪsˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈraɪsˌbɜːd / (BrE)
A migratory American songbird, especially one known to feed in rice fields, such as the bobolink or certain weaverbirds.
migratory American songbird, e.g. bobolink

Examples

A flock of ricebirds landed in the wet rice fields at dawn.
Farmers often consider the ricebird a pest because it eats their crops.
The ricebird is admired for its striking plumage and melodic song.

2. NOUN

/ ˈraɪsˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈraɪsˌbɜːd / (BrE)
a small finch-like Indonesian weaverbird that is often found in rice fields
small weaverbird found in rice fields

Examples

The farmer noticed a flock of ricebirds eating the grains in his field.
Many ricebirds nest among the tall stalks during the growing season.
Locals often spot the ricebird darting between rice plants at dawn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weaverbird - a bird known for weaving intricate nests

Etymology

From 'rice' + 'bird', referring to its habit of feeding in rice fields.
