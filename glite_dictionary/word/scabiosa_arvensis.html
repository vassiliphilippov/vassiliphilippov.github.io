Scabiosa arvensis

1. NOUN

/ skəˈbaɪ.əsə ɑːrˈvɛnsɪs / (AmE), / skəˈbaɪ.əsə ɑːˈvɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A perennial plant with bluish-lilac flowers, commonly known as field scabious, introduced in the eastern United States.
a plant with bluish-lilac flowers

Examples

The meadow was dotted with Scabiosa arvensis blooming in the summer sun.
Botanists recorded the spread of Scabiosa arvensis in the eastern United States.
Gardeners prize Scabiosa arvensis for its delicate bluish-lilac flowers.

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Scabiosa' as the genus name and 'arvensis' meaning 'of the field'.
