chromatinic

1. ADJ

/ ˌkroʊməˈtɪnɪk / (AmE), / ˌkrəʊməˈtɪnɪk / (BrE)
(of a substance of a cell nucleus) readily stained or colored by dyes, especially in microscopic examination.
easily stained (biology, cell nucleus)
medical

Examples

The chromatinic material within the nucleus is easily observed with standard staining techniques.
Scientists used a special dye to highlight the chromatinic substances in the sample.
Under the microscope, the chromatinic regions appeared much darker than the surrounding areas.

Etymology

From chromatin + -ic, indicating relation to chromatin (a substance in cell nuclei).
