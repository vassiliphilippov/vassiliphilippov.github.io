transparent

1. ADJ

/ trænˈspɛrənt / (AmE), / trænˈspærənt / (BrE)
allowing light to pass through so that objects behind can be seen clearly; easily seen through.
allowing light to pass through clearly

Examples

The water in the mountain lake was so transparent that you could see every pebble on the bottom.
She chose a transparent glass vase to show off the colorful flowers inside.
The insect's wings are almost transparent, making them difficult to spot against the background.

Synset Examples

the cold crystalline water of melted snow
crystal clear skies
could see the sand on the bottom of the limpid pool
lucid air
a pellucid brook
transparent crystal

Related Terms

  • Antonym: opaque - not letting light pass through; not transparent or translucent
  • Degree Lower: translucent - allowing light to pass through diffusely

Etymology

From Latin 'transparent-', from 'trans-' (through) + 'parere' (to appear).

2. ADJ

/ trænˈspɛɹənt / (AmE), / trænˈspærənt / (BrE)
(of a material) so thin or finely woven as to allow light to pass through, making objects behind it visible; see-through.
allowing light to pass through; see-through

Examples

The dress was made of a transparent fabric that shimmered in the sunlight.
She hung transparent curtains in the living room to let in more light.
A transparent veil covered her face, softening her features.

Synset Examples

a hat with a diaphanous veil
filmy wings of a moth
gauzy clouds of dandelion down
gossamer cobwebs
sheer silk stockings
transparent chiffon
vaporous silks

Related Terms

  • Antonym: opaque - not able to be seen through; not transparent
  • Degree Lower: translucent - allowing some light, but not detailed shapes, to pass through

Etymology

From Latin 'trans-' (across) + 'parēre' (to appear).

3. ADJ

/ trænˈspɛrənt / (AmE), / trænˈspærənt / (BrE)
(of an action, statement, or process) easy to perceive or detect; obvious or readily understood, often because of a lack of subtlety or effort to conceal.
obvious or easy to understand

Examples

Her motives for volunteering were completely transparent to everyone in the room.
He gave a transparent excuse for being late, which nobody believed.
The company's attempts to hide the price increase were anything but transparent.

Synset Examples

a transparent explanation
a transparent lie

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obvious - easily perceived or understood; clear, self-evident
  • Synonym: clear - easy to perceive, understand, or interpret

Etymology

From Latin 'transparens', present participle of 'transparere' (to show through).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01623870-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ trænˈspɛrənt / (AmE), / trænˈspærənt / (BrE)
(of a person or process) open and honest, without attempting to deceive or hide information.
open and honest; not deceitful

Examples

The company's transparent accounting practices gained them the public's trust.
She appreciated his transparent honesty during their difficult conversation.
To ensure fair elections, the process must be completely transparent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honest - truthful; not intending to deceive
  • Antonym: deceitful - dishonest; misleading or lying

Etymology

From Medieval Latin transparentem, from Latin transparere (to show through), from trans- (through) + parere (to appear).
