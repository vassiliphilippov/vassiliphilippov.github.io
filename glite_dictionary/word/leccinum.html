Leccinum

1. NOUN

/ lɛˈsɪnəm / (AmE), / lɛˈsɪnəm / (BrE)
a genus of bolete fungi in the family Boletaceae, distinguished by small, rigid projections called scabers on their stems
a genus of bolete fungi

Examples

Many mushroom hunters seek Leccinum species for their edibility and distinctive appearance.
The stem of a Leccinum is often covered with tiny, rough scabers.
Scientists have discovered several new Leccinum species in northern forests.

Etymology

From Latin *leccinum*, meaning 'a boletus', from *leccinum* (a type of mushroom).

2. NOUN

/ ləˈsɪnəm / (AmE), / lɛˈsɪnəm / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Boletaceae, characterized by small, rigid projections (scabers) on the stem of their fruiting bodies.
a genus of fungi with scabers on stem

Examples

Several species of Leccinum are prized by mushroom foragers for their edible qualities.
The stems of Leccinum mushrooms are easily identified by their characteristic scabers.
Researchers discovered a new Leccinum species in the boreal forests of Canada.

Etymology

From Italian *leccino*, a type of olive, modern scientific Latin for the genus.
