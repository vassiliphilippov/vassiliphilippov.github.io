posthole

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊstˌhoʊl / (AmE), / ˈpəʊsthəʊl / (BrE)
a hole dug in the ground to hold a post, especially the upright of a fence
hole for setting a post

Examples

The workers used an auger to dig each posthole along the new fence line.
After marking where the gate would go, she started on the first posthole.
A properly dug posthole ensures your fence stays sturdy and upright.
