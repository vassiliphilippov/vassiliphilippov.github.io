Teredo

1. NOUN

/ təˈriːdoʊ / (AmE), / təˈriːdəʊ / (BrE)
A genus of marine bivalve molluscs in the family Teredinidae, commonly known as shipworms, that bore into and destroy submerged wood.
genus of wood-boring marine molluscs (shipworms)

Examples

The wooden hull of the old ship was damaged by Teredo infestations.
Researchers studied the destructive habits of Teredo species in tropical waters.
Shipbuilders must protect their vessels from the attacks of Teredo to prevent severe wood damage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shipworm - a common name for species in the genus Teredo, marine wood-boring molluscs

Etymology

From Latin *terēdō* (woodworm), ultimately from Greek *terēdōn*, meaning 'a gnawing worm'.
