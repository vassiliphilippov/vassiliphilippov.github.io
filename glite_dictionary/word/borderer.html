borderer

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔrdərər / (AmE), / ˈbɔːdərə / (BrE)
an inhabitant of a border area, especially the area between Scotland and England
inhabitant of a border area

Examples

Many borderers developed a unique culture shaped by centuries of conflict and cooperation.
During the 16th century, borderers were known for their cattle raiding on both sides of the divide.
Some families of borderers later settled in North America, bringing their traditions with them.

Etymology

From 'border' + '-er', denoting a person living near a border.
