foundress

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaʊndrɪs / (AmE), / ˈfaʊndrɪs / (BrE)
a woman who founds or establishes an institution, organization, or community, especially one of a religious nature
female founder, especially of an institution

Examples

Mother Teresa is often recognized as the foundress of the Missionaries of Charity.
As the foundress of the convent, she played a vital role in its development.
The university's foundress is honored annually for her pioneering work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: founder - a person who establishes an institution or organization

Etymology

From 'founder' + '-ess', a suffix forming female nouns from male or neutral ones; dating from Late Middle English.
