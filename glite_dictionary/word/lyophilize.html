to lyophilize

1. VERB

/ laɪˈɑːfəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / laɪˈɒfɪˌlaɪz / (BrE)
to dry (a substance such as blood, serum, or tissue) by freezing and then reducing the surrounding pressure to allow the frozen water to sublimate directly from the solid to the gas phase
to dry by freezing and vacuum (science)
medical

Examples

Scientists often lyophilize tissue samples to preserve them for future analysis.
The lab technician prepared to lyophilize the serum for long-term storage.
Some vaccines are lyophilized to ensure stability during transportation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to freeze-dry - to dry (especially food or biological material) by freezing and then vaporizing the ice in a vacuum

Etymology

From Greek lyo- (loosen, dissolve) + philein (to love); modelled after 'lyophile' and '-ize'.
