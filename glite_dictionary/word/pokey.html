pokey

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊki / (AmE), / ˈpəʊki / (BrE)
(slang) a jail or prison, especially a small one for people who are awaiting trial or serving a short sentence.
slang: jail or prison
slang informal american

Examples

He spent the night in the pokey after a minor traffic violation.
The old Western movies often show outlaws being thrown into the pokey.
She joked that if she kept parking illegally, she'd end up in the pokey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jail - a place for the confinement of people accused or convicted of a crime
  • Synonym: prison - a building where people are kept as a punishment for a crime

Etymology

Possibly from 'pogie', an American slang term for jail, origin uncertain.

3. ADJ

/ ˈpoʊki / (AmE), / ˈpəʊki / (BrE)
slow or sluggish, especially so as to waste time or cause delay.
slow; sluggish; wasting time
informal

Examples

The cashier was being so pokey that the line grew longer by the minute.
Most children get pokey when it's time to clean their rooms.
Don't be pokey—we'll be late if you don't hurry!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slow - moving or acting at a low speed
  • Synonym: sluggish - lacking energy; slow to move or respond
  • Antonym: brisk - quick and energetic
