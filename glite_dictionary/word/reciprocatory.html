reciprocatory

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈsɪprəkəˌtɔri / (AmE), / rɪˈsɪprəkəˌtɔːri / (BrE)
moving alternately backward and forward; characterized by a back-and-forth motion
moving back and forth

Examples

The reciprocatory movement of the engine piston helps power the vehicle.
A sewing machine uses a reciprocatory motion to move the needle up and down.
The experiment involved a reciprocatory arm oscillating between two fixed points.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reciprocating - moving alternately backward and forward

Etymology

From Latin reciprocus (returning the same way), with the suffix -tory forming adjectives.

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈsɪprəkəˌtɔri / (AmE), / rɪˈsɪprəkəˌtɔːri / (BrE)
given, done, or owed in return by each to the other; mutual.
mutual; given or owed to each other

Examples

Their reciprocatory gestures of respect helped create a strong partnership.
The agreement was based on reciprocatory obligations between the two parties.
They engaged in reciprocatory acts of kindness throughout their friendship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reciprocal - mutual; shared or done in return

Etymology

From Latin reciprocatus (past participle of reciprocāre, to move backwards and forwards) + -ory.
