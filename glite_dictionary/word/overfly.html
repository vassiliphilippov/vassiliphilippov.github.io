to overfly

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈflaɪ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈflaɪ / (BrE)
(of an aircraft) to fly over a place or area.
fly over (an area)

Examples

The aircraft was permitted to overfly several countries en route to its destination.
Fighter jets will overfly the stadium as part of the ceremony.
During the exercise, they overflew restricted airspace by mistake.

Synset Examples

The plane passed over Damascus

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fly over - to move through the air above something

Etymology

From 'over-' (above) and 'fly' (move through the air), first used in aviation contexts in early 20th century.
