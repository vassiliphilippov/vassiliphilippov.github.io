to reharmonize

1. VERB

/ riˈhɑːrməˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˌriːˈhɑːmənaɪz / (BrE)
to provide (a piece of music or a melody) with a new or different harmony
to give new harmony to music

Examples

The jazz pianist decided to reharmonize the standard song for a fresh arrangement.
Composers often reharmonize familiar tunes to create a unique sound.
For the upcoming concert, she will reharmonize the chorus to add more complexity.

Synset Examples

reharmonize the melody

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to harmonize - to add harmony to a melody

Etymology

Formed from re- (again) + harmonize, meaning to harmonize again.
