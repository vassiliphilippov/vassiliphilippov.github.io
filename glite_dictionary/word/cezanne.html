Cezanne

1. PROPN

/ seɪˈzæn / (AmE), / seɪˈzan / (BrE)
Paul Cézanne (1839–1906), a French Post-Impressionist painter noted for his influential role in the development of modern art, particularly by emphasizing the structural aspects found in nature and inspiring movements such as Cubism.
French Post-Impressionist painter

Examples

Many art historians consider Cezanne the father of modern painting.
The bold shapes and colors in Cezanne's still lifes distinguish his work from his contemporaries.
Young painters admired how Cezanne reinterpreted landscapes and inspired new art movements.

Etymology

From the French surname Cézanne.
