forthright

1. ADV

/ ˈfɔrθˌraɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɔːθˌraɪt / (BrE)
(adverb) Directly and without evasion; in a straightforward manner.
in a direct or straightforward way

Examples

She answered the question forthright, leaving no room for doubt.
The manager spoke forthright about the changes coming to the company.
When criticized, he always responds forthright, never avoiding tough topics.

Synset Examples

to face a problem squarely
the responsibility lies squarely with them
spoke forthright (or forthrightly) and to the point

Related Terms

  • Synonym: directly - in a direct manner; without deviation
  • Antonym: roundabout - not saying what is meant clearly and directly

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old English *forthriht* (straight forward).

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɔrθˌraɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɔːθˌraɪt / (BrE)
(of a person, speech, or manner) direct, honest, and outspoken; not attempting to hide anything or avoid the truth.
direct and honest in speech or manner

Examples

Her forthright comments during the meeting impressed everyone with their honesty.
It's refreshing to meet someone so forthright about their opinions.
He gave a forthright response, leaving no room for misunderstanding.

Synset Examples

blunt talking and straight shooting
a blunt New England farmer
I gave them my candid opinion
forthright criticism
a forthright approach to the problem
tell me what you think — and you may just as well be frank
it is possible to be outspoken without being rude
plainspoken and to the point
a point-blank accusation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candid - truthful and straightforward; frank
  • Synonym: blunt - uncompromisingly forthright; direct in speech or manner
  • Antonym: evasive - tending to avoid commitment or self-revelation, especially by responding only indirectly
  • Antonym: subtle - delicate or indirect in meaning or intent

Etymology

From Middle English 'forth right' (straight ahead), from Old English 'forðriht'.
