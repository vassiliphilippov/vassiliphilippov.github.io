bastardy

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæstɚdi / (AmE), / ˈbɑːstədi / (BrE)
the condition of being born to parents who were not married, especially as recognized by law or society
illegitimacy; being born out of wedlock
archaic legal

Examples

The laws regarding bastardy have changed significantly over the past century.
He brought a case to court to dispute the charge of bastardy.
In Victorian literature, bastardy was often associated with social stigma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illegitimacy - the state of being born to unmarried parents

Etymology

From Old French *bastardie*, from *bastard* (an illegitimate child).
