candent

1. ADJ

/ ˈkændənt / (AmE), / ˈkandənt / (BrE)
emitting light as a result of being heated; glowing with heat
glowing with heat; emitting light from heat

Examples

The candent metal rod glowed bright orange in the forge.
She admired the candent filaments inside the antique light bulb.
Molten lava flowed in candent streams down the side of the volcano.

Synset Examples

an incandescent bulb

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incandescent - emitting light as a result of being heated

Etymology

From Latin 'candēns', present participle of 'candēre' (to shine, to glow).
