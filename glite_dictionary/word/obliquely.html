obliquely

1. ADV

/ oʊˈbliːkli / (AmE), / əˈbliːkli / (BrE)
At an oblique angle; not directly or perpendicularly.
at an oblique angle

Examples

The late afternoon light fell obliquely across the dining room table.
She glanced obliquely at her supervisor, unsure how to respond.
The building's shadow stretched obliquely over the lawn as the sun set.

Synset Examples

the sun shone aslant into his face

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aslant - at a slant; not straight or level
  • Antonym: perpendicularly - at a right angle; straight up or down

Etymology

From Latin 'obliquus' (slanting, sideways) + English adverbial suffix '-ly'.

2. ADV

/ əˈbliːkli / (AmE), / ɒˈbliːkli / (BrE)
in a slanting or indirect manner; to, toward, or at one side.
in a slanting or indirect way

Examples

The sun shone obliquely through the window, casting long shadows across the floor.
She glanced obliquely at her colleague, unsure how to respond.
The road crossed the river obliquely, not at a right angle.

Synset Examples

darting eyes looking sidelong out of a wizened face

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sideways - to, toward, or at one side
  • Antonym: directly - in a straight or direct way

Etymology

From Latin obliquus (slanting, sideways) with the English adverbial suffix -ly.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r
