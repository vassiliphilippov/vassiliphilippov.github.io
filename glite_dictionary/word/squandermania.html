squandermania

1. NOUN

/ ˌskwɑːndərˈmeɪniə / (AmE), / ˌskwɒndəˈmeɪnɪə / (BrE)
Excessive or prodigious squandering, especially of public money or resources, typically by a government.
excessive government wastefulness

Examples

The opposition party accused the government of squandermania in its latest budget.
Years of squandermania have left the city facing severe financial difficulties.
Critics warn that unchecked squandermania could harm the nation's economic future.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extravagance - excessive spending or lack of restraint in using resources
  • Antonym: frugality - the quality of being economical with money or resources; opposite of squandermania

Etymology

Formed from 'squander' and '-mania' (Greek, meaning frenzy or obsession).
