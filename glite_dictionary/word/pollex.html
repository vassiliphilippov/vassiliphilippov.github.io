pollex

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːlɛks / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɛks / (BrE)
the thick, short, innermost digit of the hand or forelimb, such as the thumb in humans
the innermost digit, especially the thumb
medical

Examples

The opposable pollex gives humans the dexterity to grasp objects precisely.
In many mammals, the pollex is reduced or even absent.
The surgeon carefully examined the injury to the patient's pollex.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thumb - the short, thick first digit of the human hand

Etymology

From Latin 'pollex' meaning 'thumb'.
