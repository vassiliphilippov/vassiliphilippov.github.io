acme

1. NOUN

/ ˈæk.mi / (AmE), / ˈæk.mi / (BrE)
The highest point or stage of something; the peak of achievement, development, or excellence.
the highest point; peak

Examples

Winning the Nobel Prize was considered the acme of his scientific career.
Her performance reached the acme of artistic perfection.
The company was at the acme of its success before the market shifted.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zenith - the time at which something is most powerful or successful
  • Antonym: nadir - the lowest point in the fortunes of a person or organization
  • Synonym: apex - the top or highest part of something
  • Synonym: peak - the highest level or value of something

Etymology

From Greek akmē (point, peak, zenith).

2. NOUN

/ ˈækmi / (AmE), / ˈækmi / (BrE)
the highest point or stage of something; the peak or culmination
the highest point or peak

Examples

Winning the Nobel Prize was the acme of her scientific career.
The empire reached its acme during the 18th century before declining.
His performance at the concert was the acme of musical excellence.

Synset Examples

at the peak of the pyramid

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zenith - the highest point; the peak
  • Antonym: nadir - the lowest point; opposite of zenith or acme
  • Synonym: peak - the highest or most important point

Etymology
