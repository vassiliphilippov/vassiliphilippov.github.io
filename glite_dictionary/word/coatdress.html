coatdress

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊtˌdrɛs / (AmE), / ˈkəʊtˌdrɛs / (BrE)
A woman's dress designed like a tailored coat, usually with a front button fastening.
dress designed like a tailored coat

Examples

She wore a stylish coatdress to the luncheon, drawing many compliments.
The vintage coatdress featured elegant buttons running down the front.
For the reception, she chose a blue silk coatdress with a matching belt.

Etymology

Formed from 'coat' + 'dress', reflecting the garment's style.
