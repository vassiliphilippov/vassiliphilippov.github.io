slippage

1. NOUN

/ ˈslɪpɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈslɪpɪdʒ / (BrE)
a decrease in the amount of power transmitted by a mechanical system, caused by slipping between components
power loss caused by slipping (mechanical)

Examples

The engineers noticed significant slippage in the belt drive during the test.
Excessive slippage can reduce the efficiency of a car's transmission system.
Routine maintenance helped minimize slippage in the conveyor belt.

Etymology

From slip + -age, meaning the action or result of slipping.

2. NOUN

/ ˈslɪpɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈslɪpɪdʒ / (BrE)
the act or process of slipping or failing to stay in place or hold fast
failure to hold; slipping out of place

Examples

Due to excessive wear, there was some slippage in the transmission belt.
The mountain climber checked each knot to prevent any slippage during the ascent.
The engineer redesigned the fastener after noticing persistent slippage during safety tests.

Synset Examples

the knots allowed no slippage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slip - to slide accidentally or become loose and move out of place
  • Synonym: slide - to move smoothly along a surface while maintaining contact with it

Etymology

From slip + -age, first used in the 19th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈslɪpɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈslɪpɪdʒ / (BrE)
a gradual decline from an established standard of performance or achievement
decline from a standard level

Examples

The company experienced a noticeable slippage in sales last quarter.
There was some slippage in the team's performance after their coach left.
Teachers are concerned about slippage in students' test scores this year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decline - a gradual and continuous loss of strength, numbers, quality, or value
  • Antonym: improvement - the process of getting better or making something better

Etymology

From slip + -age, formed in English to mean the act or process of slipping.
