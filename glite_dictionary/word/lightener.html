lightener

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪtnər / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtənə / (BrE)
a substance used to make something lighter in color, especially hair, skin, or wood
substance making something lighter in color

Examples

She used a lightener on her hair to achieve a sun-kissed look.
The furniture was treated with a wood lightener to create a softer finish.
Many cosmetic products contain a lightener to reduce dark spots and brighten skin tone.

Synset Examples

Baking soda mixed with lemon juice makes a cheap and natural skin lightener.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bleach - a chemical used to make things lighter or whiter
  • Antonym: darkener - a substance that makes something darker

Etymology

From 'lighten' (to make lighter) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
