penetrating

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɛn.əˌtreɪ.t̬ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɛn.ɪˌtreɪ.tɪŋ / (BrE)
Able to enter, pierce, or affect deeply; having the power to penetrate.
able to pierce or affect deeply

Examples

The penetrating noise from the alarm woke everyone in the building.
She has a penetrating gaze that makes people uncomfortable.
A penetrating chill swept through the house when the door was left open.

Synset Examples

a toxic penetrative spray applied to the surface
a cold penetrating wind
a penetrating odor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sharp - able to cut or pierce easily
  • Antonym: blunt - not sharp or able to pierce

Etymology

From Latin 'penetratus', past participle of 'penetrare' (to enter, pierce).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɛnəˌtreɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɛnɪˌtreɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
Having or showing an ability to understand things quickly and clearly; keen or incisive.
showing keen or incisive insight

Examples

Her penetrating questions revealed the flaws in the argument.
The critic's penetrating analysis gave me a new perspective on the film.
He has a penetrating mind that quickly grasps complex issues.

Synset Examples

an acute observer of politics and politicians
incisive comments
icy knifelike reasoning
as sharp and incisive as the stroke of a fang
penetrating insight
frequent penetrative observations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incisive - clear and direct, especially in thought or expression
  • Synonym: acute - showing an ability to notice and understand things quickly
  • Antonym: dull - lacking sharpness or insight

Etymology

From Latin 'penetrare' (to enter, pierce).
