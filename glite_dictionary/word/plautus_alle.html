Plautus alle

1. NOUN

/ ˈplɔːtəs ˈæleɪ / (AmE), / ˈplɔːtəs ˈæliː / (BrE)
A small, short-billed auk abundant in Arctic regions, also known as the little auk.
small Arctic auk (little auk)

Examples

The Plautus alle is commonly seen nesting on rocky Arctic coasts.
Researchers observed a large colony of Plautus alle during their expedition to Greenland.
Unlike other auks, the Plautus alle prefers to feed on zooplankton in cold northern waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: little auk - small arctic seabird, another name for Plautus alle

Etymology

From Latin scientific name, "Plautus alle", used in taxonomy for the little auk species.
