saucy

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɔsi / (AmE), / ˈsɔːsi / (BrE)
Displaying a lively, boldly playful, or cheekily exuberant manner.
boldly playful or lively

Examples

Her saucy remarks made everyone laugh at the party.
The actor gave the audience a saucy wink as he left the stage.
She wore a saucy smile that suggested she was up to something clever.

Synset Examples

a certain irreverent gaiety and ease of manner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheeky - boldly rude or disrespectful, often in a lively or playful way
  • Synonym: impudent - rude and not showing respect for others
  • Antonym: respectful - showing politeness or deference toward others

Etymology

From sauce + -y, originally meaning 'having sauce' in the figurative sense of impudence.

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɔsi / (AmE), / ˈsɔːsi / (BrE)
improperly forward, bold, or disrespectful, especially in speech or manner
impertinently bold; cheeky

Examples

The child's saucy reply surprised the teacher.
She gave him a saucy wink as she left the room.
His saucy comments often get him into trouble.

Synset Examples

don't be fresh with me
impertinent of a child to lecture a grownup
an impudent boy given to insulting strangers
Don't get wise with me!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impertinent - not showing proper respect; rude
  • Synonym: cheeky - rudely bold or disrespectful, often in a playful way
  • Antonym: respectful - showing deference or proper respect

Etymology

From Middle English sausy, from Old French saussé (seasoned), from Latin salsa (salted). Shifted in sense from 'flavorful' to 'bold in manner'.
