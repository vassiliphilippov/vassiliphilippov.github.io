mukataa

1. NOUN

/ muːˈkɑː.tɑː / (AmE), / mʊˈkɑː.tɑː / (BrE)
A headquarters or administrative center, especially referring to government or official buildings in some Arabic-speaking regions.
administrative headquarters, esp. in Arabic-speaking regions

Examples

The president held an emergency meeting at the mukataa to address the crisis.
Journalists gathered outside the mukataa during the peace negotiations.
Security was tightened around the mukataa after the recent unrest.

Synset Examples

Arafat was holed up in the mukataa of his West Bank compound

Etymology

From Arabic مُقَاطَعَة‎ (muqāṭaʿa), meaning a district or administrative area.
