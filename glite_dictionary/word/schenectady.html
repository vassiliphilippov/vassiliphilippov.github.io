Schenectady

1. PROPN

/ skəˈnɛktədi / (AmE), / skəˈnɛktədi / (BrE)
A city in eastern New York State, located on the Mohawk River, known for its growth following the opening of the Erie Canal.
city in eastern New York State

Examples

She grew up in Schenectady before moving to New York City for college.
Many technological innovations were developed in Schenectady during the twentieth century.
The Mohawk River runs through Schenectady, adding to the city's scenic beauty.

Etymology

From the Mohawk word *Skanatenati*, meaning 'beyond the pines'.
