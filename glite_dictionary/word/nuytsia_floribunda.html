Nuytsia floribunda

1. NOUN

/ ˈnʌtsiə flɔːrɪˈbʌndə / (AmE), / ˈnʌtsiə flɒrɪˈbʌndə / (BrE)
a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Western Australia, known for its bright yellow-orange flowers and parasitic roots
evergreen tree of Western Australia with yellow-orange flowers

Examples

The Nuytsia floribunda is famous for its spectacular display of yellow-orange flowers during the Australian summer.
Many gardeners in Western Australia admire the Nuytsia floribunda for its unique roots and vibrant blossoms.
The Nuytsia floribunda is able to parasitize the roots of nearby grasses to obtain water and nutrients.

Etymology

From Nuytsia, the genus name, and the Latin floribunda (abundantly flowering).
