submaxilla

1. NOUN

/ sʌb.mækˈsɪl.ə / (AmE), / sʌb.mækˈsɪl.ə / (BrE)
the lower jawbone in vertebrates, which is hinged to open and close the mouth
lower jawbone in vertebrates

Examples

The snake's submaxilla allows it to open its mouth extremely wide when swallowing prey.
In many animals, the submaxilla is a highly flexible bone crucial for feeding.
The vet examined the injured submaxilla of the bird after its accident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mandible - the lower jawbone, especially in mammals
  • Antonym: maxilla - the upper jawbone in vertebrates

Etymology

From Latin sub- (under) + maxilla (jaw).
