Campanula trachelium

1. NOUN

/ kæmˈpæn.jələ trəˈkiː.li.əm / (AmE), / kamˈpan.juː.lə trəˈkiː.li.əm / (BrE)
A European species of bellflower with blue-purple to lilac flowers, formerly used to treat sore throat.
species of European bellflower

Examples

The woodland edge was dotted with clusters of Campanula trachelium in bloom.
Historically, Campanula trachelium was used in herbal remedies for sore throats.
Gardeners prize Campanula trachelium for its striking lilac-blue flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bellflower - plant of the genus Campanula with bell-shaped flowers

Etymology

From Latin: 'campanula' meaning 'little bell' and 'trachelium', referring to the neck (throat).
