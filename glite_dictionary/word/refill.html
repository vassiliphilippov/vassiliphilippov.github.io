refill

1. NOUN

/ ˈriˌfɪl / (AmE), / ˈriːfɪl / (BrE)
an amount of a prescription drug that is provided again after the initial supply has been used up
an additional supply of a prescription drug

Examples

Your doctor can authorize another refill if needed.
My pharmacy called to say my refill was ready for pickup.
The label indicates how many refills remain on this medication.

Synset Examples

he got a refill of his prescription
the prescription specified only one refill

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prescription - an instruction written by a medical practitioner for the dispensing of a medicine

Etymology

from re- (again) + fill, meaning to fill again

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04075373-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ riˈfɪl / (AmE), / ˌriːˈfɪl / (BrE)
to fill something again that has become empty or partially empty
fill something again

Examples

Could you please refill my water bottle before we leave?
The printer needs you to refill the paper tray every morning.
After finishing her tea, she asked the waitress to refill her cup.

Synset Examples

refill my glass, please

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fill - to make something full; to put as much as possible into
  • Antonym: empty - to remove the contents of something; to make empty

Etymology

From re- (again) + fill, both from Old English roots.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00454758-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈriːˌfɪl / (AmE), / ˈriːfɪl / (BrE)
a product or amount of something used to fill again a container with its original or suitable contents, especially when sold separately to replace what has been used
product for filling again

Examples

I need to buy a refill for my water filter before it stops working.
The store sells ink refills for all major ballpoint pen brands.
After using up the paper, she purchased a notebook refill to continue her notes.

Synset Examples

he got a refill for his ball-point pen
he got a refill for his notebook

Related Terms

  • Synonym: replacement - something that takes the place of another

Etymology

From re- (again) + fill (to make full).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04075167-n
Synset POS: n
