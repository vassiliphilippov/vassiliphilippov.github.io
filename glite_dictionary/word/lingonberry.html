lingonberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪŋ.ənˌbɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈlɪŋ.ənˌbɛr.i / (BrE)
A small red berry from a low evergreen shrub (Vaccinium vitis-idaea) native to northern Europe, Asia, and North America, often used in jams and sauces.
small red berry from a northern shrub

Examples

The sauce was made from fresh lingonberries picked in the northern forest.
Many Scandinavian recipes call for lingonberry jam as an accompaniment to meat dishes.
In Sweden, pancakes are often served with a generous spoonful of lingonberry preserve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cranberry - a similar tart red berry from a related plant

Etymology

From Swedish 'lingon' (the berry) + English 'berry'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɪŋ.ɡənˌbɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈlɪŋ.ənˌbɛr.i / (BrE)
A small, tart red berry resembling a cranberry, commonly found in northern regions and used in jams and sauces.
small, tart red berry similar to cranberry

Examples

Swedish pancakes are often served with lingonberry jam for a sweet and tart flavor.
The forest floor was covered with ripe lingonberries ready to be picked.
She added a handful of fresh lingonberries to the sauce for extra tang.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cranberry - a sour red berry closely related to the lingonberry

Etymology

From Swedish 'lingon' (cowberry) + English 'berry'.
