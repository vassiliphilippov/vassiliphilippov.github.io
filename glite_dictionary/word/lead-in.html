lead-in

1. NOUN

/ ˈlidˌɪn / (AmE), / ˈliːdˌɪn / (BrE)
A wire that connects an antenna to a receiver or a transmitter to a transmission line.
wire connecting antenna and receiver/transmitter

Examples

The technician checked the lead-in for any signs of wear before installing the new receiver.
A faulty lead-in can cause poor signal quality and lost channels.
They replaced the old antenna's lead-in with a more durable, insulated wire.

2. NOUN

/ ˈliːd ɪn / (AmE), / ˈliːd ɪn / (BrE)
an introductory section or phrase that precedes the main part of a story, broadcast, or presentation
introductory section before the main story

Examples

The reporter wrote a catchy lead-in to grab the readers' attention.
Her presentation started with a brief lead-in before she discussed the main topic.
That joke was an effective lead-in to a very serious conversation.

Synset Examples

it was an amusing lead-in to a very serious matter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: introduction - the beginning section of a text or speech
  • Antonym: conclusion - the final section of a text or speech

Etymology

From the verb 'lead' + preposition 'in', indicating something that leads into something else.
