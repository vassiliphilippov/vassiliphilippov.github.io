chincapin

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪŋkəˌpɪn / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪŋkəpɪn / (BrE)
A small edible nut from either of two species of small chestnut trees native to the southern United States, resembling a hazelnut.
edible nut from small US chestnut trees
american

Examples

The forest floor was covered with fallen chincapins after the autumn winds.
She collected a handful of chincapins to roast for a snack.
Many wildlife species rely on the chincapin as a seasonal food source.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chestnut - edible nut from the chestnut tree
  • Synonym: hazelnut - small edible nut from the hazel tree

Etymology

From Algonquian (Virginia) *chechinquamin*, meaning 'chestnut'.
