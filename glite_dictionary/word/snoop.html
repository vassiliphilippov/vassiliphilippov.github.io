snoop

1. NOUN

/ snup / (AmE), / snuːp / (BrE)
a person who secretly tries to find out private information about someone or something
person who pries into private matters

Examples

The neighbors complained about the constant presence of a snoop around their property.
She resented him acting like a snoop whenever she received phone calls.
A real snoop always knows when something unusual is going on.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: busybody - a person who meddles in others' affairs
  • Synonym: spy - a person who secretly collects information
  • Synonym: private investigator - someone hired to investigate matters secretly

Etymology

Perhaps imitative or from Dutch 'snoepen' (to pry).

2. VERB

/ snuːp / (AmE), / snuːp / (BrE)
to watch, observe, or try to find out about someone or something in a secret or sneaky way, especially about private matters
to secretly investigate or pry into

Examples

She tried to snoop on her brothers but got caught in the act.
Journalists were accused of snooping into private emails without permission.
I don't like it when people snoop around my desk when I'm not there.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pry - to inquire too closely into a person's private affairs
  • Synonym: spy - to watch someone secretly

Etymology

Probably imitative; first recorded in late 19th century American English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00787352-v
Synset POS: v
