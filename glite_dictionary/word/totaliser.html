totaliser

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊ.t̬əl.aɪ.zər / (AmE), / ˈtəʊ.təl.aɪ.zə / (BrE)
A computerized machine or system that records bets and calculates how winnings are shared among those who correctly placed their bets, especially at racecourses.
bet calculation machine at racetracks

Examples

The totaliser ensures that the winnings are accurately distributed among successful bettors.
After all bets were placed, the totaliser displayed the updated odds.
Advancements in technology have made the totaliser faster and more reliable at the racetrack.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: totalizer - alternative spelling for totaliser (machine for calculating bets)

Etymology

From 'totalize' + '-er', named for its function of totaling amounts.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊ.t̬l̩ˌaɪ.zɚ / (AmE), / ˈtəʊ.təl.aɪ.zə / (BrE)
a machine or device, especially a calculator, used to perform simple arithmetic operations such as addition and subtraction
simple arithmetic calculator/device
british

Examples

The totaliser made it easy to keep track of daily sales figures.
Early bookmakers relied on a mechanical totaliser to calculate bets quickly.
The museum displayed a vintage totaliser alongside other historical office equipment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calculator - an electronic or mechanical device for making mathematical calculations

Etymology

From 'total' + '-iser', indicating a device that totals amounts; 'totaliser' is the British spelling of 'totalizer'.
