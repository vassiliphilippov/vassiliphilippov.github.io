lcm

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛl.siːˈɛm / (AmE), / ˌɛl.siːˈɛm / (BrE)
the smallest number that is exactly divisible by each of a given set of numbers
smallest common multiple of numbers

Examples

To add fractions, you often need to find the lcm of the denominators.
The lcm of 8 and 14 is 56, which is useful for solving the equation.
When simplifying schedules, they calculated the lcm of the cycle times.

Synset Examples

the least common multiple of 12 and 18 is 36

Related Terms

  • Synonym: least common multiple - the smallest number divisible by each number in a set
  • Antonym: greatest common divisor - the largest number that divides each number in a set without remainder

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'least common multiple'.
