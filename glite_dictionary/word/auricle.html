auricle

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈɔːrɪkəl / (BrE)
the outer, visible, cartilaginous part of the ear that collects and directs sound waves into the ear canal
outer visible part of the ear
medical

Examples

She wore a new earring on her left auricle to match her necklace.
The doctor examined the patient's auricle for signs of infection.
A strong wind made his auricle turn red and cold.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pinna - outer, visible part of the external ear

Etymology

From Latin 'auricula' (little ear), diminutive of 'auris' (ear).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈɔːrɪkəl / (BrE)
a small conical pouch projecting from the upper anterior part of each atrium of the heart
heart atrium's small pouch
medical

Examples

The right auricle receives oxygen-poor blood from the body.
During surgery, the surgeon noted an abnormality in the left auricle.
The auricles of the heart help increase atrial volume.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atrium - an upper chamber of the heart

Etymology

From Latin 'auricula', diminutive of 'auris' (ear), due to its ear-like shape.
