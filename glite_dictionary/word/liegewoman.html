liegewoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːdʒˌwʊm.ən / (AmE), / ˈliːdʒˌwʊm.ən / (BrE)
(historical) A woman who holds land (a fief) from a feudal lord and owes fealty and service to that lord.
female feudal vassal

Examples

As a liegewoman, she was required to attend the court of her lord.
The king granted her land, making her his trusted liegewoman.
Few records of liegewomen survive, but some held significant power in medieval Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liegeman - a man who owes allegiance and service to a feudal lord

Etymology

From 'liege' (from Old French *lige*, feudal lord) + 'woman'.
