hydraulic

1. ADJ

/ haɪˈdrɔlɪk / (AmE), / haɪˈdrɒlɪk / (BrE)
operated, moved, or powered by the pressure or movement of a liquid such as water or oil
operated by liquid pressure or movement

Examples

The mechanic checked the hydraulic fluid levels before starting the repair.
They used a hydraulic press to shape the metal plates.
Modern elevators often rely on hydraulic systems for smooth operation.

Synset Examples

hydraulic erosion
hydraulic brakes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pneumatic - operated by air or gas pressure

Etymology

From Greek hydraulikos, from hydōr (water) + aulos (pipe).

WordNet ID: oewn-03085316-a

2. ADJ

/ haɪˈdrɔlɪk / (AmE), / haɪˈdrɒlɪk / (BrE)
relating to, operated by, or employing the movement of water or other fluids, especially under pressure.
relating to fluid movement or pressure

Examples

The construction crew used a hydraulic lift to reach the top of the building.
She specializes in hydraulic systems for industrial machinery.
A hydraulic engineer studies the flow and control of fluids.

Synset Examples

hydraulic engineer

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pneumatic - operated by air or gas under pressure

Etymology

From Greek hydraulikos, from hydor (water) + aulos (pipe).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03085179-a
Synset POS: a
