/ slæpˈbæŋ / (AmE), / slæpˈbaŋ / (BrE)

british

Examples

2. ADV

/ ˈslæpˌbæŋ / (AmE), / ˈslæpˌbæŋ / (BrE)
in a sudden, forceful, or noisy manner; with a loud or violent impact
suddenly or noisily, with force
informal british

Examples

She burst slap-bang into the middle of the meeting, startling everyone.
The ball hit the window slap-bang, cracking the glass instantly.
They arrived slap-bang on time, not a minute late.

Synset Examples

the pans fell slam-bang and woke the whole house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slam-bang - in a noisy, forceful, or haphazard manner

Etymology

Imitative; combines 'slap' (a sharp blow) and 'bang' (a loud noise).
