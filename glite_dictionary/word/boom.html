boom

1. NOUN

/ buːm / (AmE), / buːm / (BrE)

Examples

The tech industry experienced a boom due to new innovations.

2. NOUN

/ buːm / (AmE), / buːm / (BrE)
a long, horizontal pole or spar used to extend the foot of a sail on a boat, or for handling cargo and mooring
horizontal pole for sails or cargo

Examples

The mainsail is attached to the boom along its bottom edge.
The crew used a boom to lift the heavy cargo onto the ship.
Watch your head when you walk beneath the boom on the sailboat.

Etymology

Probably of Dutch origin, from *boom* (tree, pole, beam).
