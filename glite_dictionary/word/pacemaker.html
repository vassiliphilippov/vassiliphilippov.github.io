pacemaker

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪsˌmeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈpeɪsˌmeɪkə / (BrE)
a horse that is entered in a race to establish a fast pace for the benefit of another runner
horse setting pace in racing

Examples

The trainer assigned a pacemaker to help the favorite maintain a steady speed.
Without a pacemaker in the race, the early pace was unusually slow.
That horse has often served as a pacemaker for more talented stablemates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pacesetter - one that sets the pace in a race or competition
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02390795-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪsˌmeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈpeɪsˌmeɪkə / (BrE)
a group of specialized cells in the heart that regulate the heartbeat by generating electrical impulses
part of the heart regulating heartbeat
medical

Examples

The pacemaker of the heart ensures that each beat occurs at the right time.
Damage to the pacemaker can result in an irregular heartbeat.
Doctors monitored the patient's pacemaker activity during the procedure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sinoatrial node - the natural pacemaker of the heart, located in the right atrium
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05396803-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪsˌmeɪkɚ / (AmE), / ˈpeɪsˌmeɪkə / (BrE)
an organization, person, or thing that is the first to achieve something or sets the standard for others in its field
leader or standard-setter in a field

Examples

With its innovative design, the company became a pacemaker in the smartphone industry.
The artist was a pacemaker whose style influenced a generation of painters.
This technology serves as a pacemaker for sustainable development in the region.

Synset Examples

the new policy will be a pacesetter in community relations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pacesetter - a person or thing that leads others in a particular field

Etymology

From 'pace' + 'maker'; originally denoting one who sets the pace in a race.

4. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪsˌmeɪkər / (AmE), / ˈpeɪsˌmeɪkə / (BrE)
a small electronic device implanted in the body to regulate an abnormal heart rhythm by producing electrical impulses
an implanted electronic heart device
medical

Examples

After surgery, his heart was regulated by a pacemaker to ensure a steady rhythm.
The doctor explained that a pacemaker might be necessary for patients with severe arrhythmia.
Battery replacement is sometimes required for an implanted pacemaker.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defibrillator - a device that restores normal heartbeat by sending an electric pulse to the heart

Etymology

From 'pace' (rhythm or speed) + 'maker', describing a device that sets the rhythm for the heart.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
