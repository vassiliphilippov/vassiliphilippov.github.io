hardliner

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrdˌlaɪnər / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdˌlaɪnə / (BrE)
a person who is uncompromising and strict in their beliefs, especially in politics or ideology
an uncompromising person, especially in politics

Examples

The senator is considered a hardliner on immigration policy.
Negotiations broke down when hardliners refused to compromise.
He was labeled a hardliner by opponents for his strict stance on reforms.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: moderate - a person with less extreme or uncompromising views
  • Synonym: extremist - a person holding extreme political or religious views

Etymology

From 'hard line,' meaning an uncompromising approach, plus '-er' to denote a person.
