perverseness

1. NOUN

/ pərˈvɜrsnəs / (AmE), / pəˈvɜːsnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of deliberately and stubbornly opposing what is expected, proper, or reasonable
deliberate stubborn resistance

Examples

His perverseness made it nearly impossible for his teachers to discipline him effectively.
She admired his perverseness in refusing to follow the crowd, even when it cost him dearly.
The perverseness of the child's behavior surprised even the most patient of caregivers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstinacy - stubborn refusal to change one's opinion or action
  • Synonym: contrariness - the quality of being contrary or opposing

Etymology

From Latin perversus (turned away, contrary) via Old French pervers.

2. NOUN

/ pərˈvɜrsnəs / (AmE), / pəˈvɜːsnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of deliberately behaving in a way that is unreasonable or morally wrong
deliberate deviation from what is right

Examples

Some leaders act out of pure perverseness, ignoring the advice that would help their communities.
His perverseness was clear when he insisted on breaking every rule just for the sake of it.
The perverseness of his attitude shocked even his closest friends.

Synset Examples

there will always be a few people who, through macho perversity, gain satisfaction from bullying and terrorism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perversity - the state or quality of being perverse
  • Antonym: virtue - behavior showing high moral standards or goodness

Etymology

From Latin perversus (turned the wrong way).
