hundred

1. NUM

/ ˈhʌndrəd / (AmE), / ˈhʌndrɪd / (BrE)
the number equal to ten times ten; 100
the number 100

Examples

She received nearly hundred messages on her birthday.
It will take at least a hundred dollars to fix the car.
There were about a hundred students at the assembly.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: thousand - the number 1,000
  • Degree Lower: ninety - the number 90

Etymology

From Old English 'hundred', from Proto-Germanic *hundaradą.

2. NUM

/ ˈhʌndrəd / (AmE), / ˈhʌndrəd / (BrE)
the number 100; the quantity represented by one hundred units
the number 100

Examples

There are exactly one hundred pages in this book.
The company employs over five hundred people worldwide.
She scored a perfect hundred on her math test.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: thousand - the number 1,000
  • Degree Lower: ten - the number 10

Etymology

From Old English 'hundred', originally meaning a group of 100; related to Proto-Germanic 'hundaradą'.

Synset POS: n
