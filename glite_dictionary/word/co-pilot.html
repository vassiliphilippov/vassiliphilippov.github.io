co-pilot

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊˌpaɪlət / (AmE), / ˈkəʊˌpaɪlət / (BrE)
a pilot who assists the main pilot and can take over flying an aircraft if needed
assistant pilot on an aircraft

Examples

The co-pilot took control when the captain needed a break.
Every commercial flight requires both a pilot and a co-pilot for safety reasons.
Sarah was promoted to co-pilot after completing her advanced training.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pilot - the person who operates the controls of an aircraft
  • Degree Higher: captain - the main pilot in command of an aircraft

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'co-' (jointly) and 'pilot'.
