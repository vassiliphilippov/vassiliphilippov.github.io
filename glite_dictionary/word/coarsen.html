to coarsen

1. VERB

/ ˈkɔrsən / (AmE), / ˈkɔːsən / (BrE)
to make or become less refined, delicate, or subtle; to make or become rougher in quality or character
make or become less refined

Examples

As years passed, the harsh environment began to coarsen his manners.
Repeated exposure to crude language can coarsen a person's sense of humor.
Political debates often coarsen during election season.

Synset Examples

coarsen one's ideals

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to refine - to make more subtle or cultured

Etymology

Formed from 'coarse' + '-en' (verb-forming suffix).

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈkɔrsən / (AmE), / ˈkɔːsən / (BrE)
to make or become rougher, thicker, or less delicate in texture or quality
make or become rough or less delicate

Examples

Years of exposure to the sun have coarsened her skin.
The comedian's jokes seemed to coarsen as the night went on.
Repeated washing can coarsen the fabric over time.

Synset Examples

coarsen the surface
Their minds coarsened

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to refine - to make something more pure, subtle, or delicate

Etymology

From 'coarse' + '-en', a suffix forming verbs meaning 'to make or become'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01256032-v
Synset POS: v
