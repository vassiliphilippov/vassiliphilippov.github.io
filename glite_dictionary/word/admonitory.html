admonitory

1. ADJ

/ ædˈmɑnɪˌtɔri / (AmE), / ədˈmɒnɪtəri / (BrE)
expressing a warning or reprimand, especially as a corrective
expressing a warning or reprimand

Examples

Her tone was admonitory, reminding him to be careful with his words.
He received an admonitory glance from the teacher after speaking out of turn.
The manager's admonitory remarks prompted the team to improve their performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warning - statement or action that warns
  • Synonym: reproachful - expressing disapproval or disappointment

Etymology

From Latin admonitorius (serving to remind or warn).

2. ADJ

/ ædˈmɑnəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ədˈmɒnɪtəri / (BrE)
giving a warning or expressing reproof or caution.
serving to warn or caution

Examples

The teacher gave an admonitory glance to the noisy students.
The judge issued an admonitory statement before announcing the sentence.
Her tone was admonitory, cautioning him against making the same mistake again.

Synset Examples

shook a monitory finger at him
an exemplary jail sentence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warning - something serving to warn or caution
  • Synonym: cautionary - serving as a warning
  • Antonym: encouraging - giving support, confidence, or hope (opposite in tone to admonitory)

Etymology

From Latin admonitōrius, from admonere (to admonish).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01775648-s
Synset POS: s
