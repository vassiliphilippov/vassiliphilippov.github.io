rate

1. NOUN

/ ɹeɪt / (AmE), / ɹeɪt / (BrE)
the amount, frequency, or speed at which something occurs, measured in relation to another quantity, often time
frequency or amount per time unit

Examples

The rate of inflation has decreased significantly this year.
Doctors monitor the heart rate to assess cardiovascular health.
The plant grew at an impressive rate during the summer.

Synset Examples

they traveled at a rate of 55 miles per hour
the rate of change was faster than expected

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speed - the rate at which someone or something moves or operates
  • Synonym: pace - the rate of progress or movement

Etymology

From Old French *rate* (fixed allowance) and medieval Latin *rata* (proportion).

/ reɪt / (AmE), / reɪt / (BrE)

Examples

Related Terms
