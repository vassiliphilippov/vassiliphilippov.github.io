Diplopterygium longissimum

1. NOUN

/ daɪˌplɒptəˈrɪdʒiəm lɒŋˈɪsɪməm / (AmE), / dɪˌplɒptəˈrɪdʒiəm lɒŋˈɪsɪməm / (BrE)
A large scrambling fern species forming extensive patches up to 18 feet high, native to the Pacific region and China.
species of large scrambling fern

Examples

The Diplopterygium longissimum can form impressive dense thickets in subtropical forests.
Botanists studied the growth habits of Diplopterygium longissimum in its native habitat.
Gardeners value Diplopterygium longissimum for its ability to quickly cover large areas.

Etymology

From Greek roots: 'diplo-' (double), 'pterygium' (small wing/fin); Latin 'longissimum' (longest).
