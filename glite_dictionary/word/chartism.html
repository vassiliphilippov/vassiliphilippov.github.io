Chartism

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɑːrtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːtɪzəm / (BrE)
the political and social principles of a 19th-century English movement seeking improved rights and conditions for working people, notably through parliamentary reform
19th-century English workers' reform movement

Examples

Chartism emerged in response to harsh economic conditions facing Britain's working class during the Industrial Revolution.
The People's Charter was the document at the heart of Chartism's demands for parliamentary reform.
Historians debate the long-term political influence of Chartism on later social movements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chartist - a member or supporter of the Chartism movement

Etymology

From 'charter' referring to the people's charter of 1838, plus '-ism'.
