threat

1. NOUN

/ θrɛt / (AmE), / θrɛt / (BrE)
a statement or warning that something unpleasant or harmful is likely to happen
a warning of possible harm or danger

Examples

The employees ignored the threat of a pay cut and continued their protest.
A bomb threat caused the evacuation of the building.
She made a veiled threat to expose his secrets unless he cooperated.

Synset Examples

they were under threat of arrest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: menace - a person or thing that is likely to cause harm; a threat or danger
  • Antonym: promise - an assurance that one will do something or that something will happen (opposite of threat)

Etymology

From Old English threa (oppression, coercion), related to threaten.

2. NOUN

/ θrɛt / (AmE), / θrɛt / (BrE)
a statement expressing an intention to cause harm or punishment to someone
declaration of intent to cause harm

Examples

The senator received a threat after announcing her controversial decision.
Making a threat is a serious offense that could lead to legal consequences.
He dismissed the threat as an empty gesture, but the police took it seriously.

Synset Examples

his threat to kill me was quite explicit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: menace - a person or thing that is likely to cause harm; a threat or danger
  • Degree Lower: warning - an indication or statement of something impending, often as a caution
  • Antonym: promise - a declaration or assurance that one will do something, especially for another's benefit (opposite intent)

Etymology

From Old English 'threat' (oppression, coercion, menace); related to Old Norse 'thraut' (trouble, contest).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06746225-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ θrɛt / (AmE), / θrɛt / (BrE)
a person or thing likely to cause harm or danger
source of danger or harm

Examples

Climate change is now considered the biggest threat to global biodiversity.
The new virus represents a serious threat to public health.
Wildfires are a constant threat in dry, forested areas.

Synset Examples

earthquakes are a constant threat in Japan

Related Terms

  • Synonym: danger - the possibility of suffering harm or injury
  • Synonym: menace - a person or thing that is likely to cause harm
  • Antonym: protection - the action of keeping someone or something safe from harm

Etymology

From Old English 'þreat' (oppression, coercion, menace).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14566833-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ θrɛt / (AmE), / θrɛt / (BrE)
a person who causes others to feel fear or dread
person who causes fear or dread

Examples

The new gang leader quickly became a threat to everyone in the area.
Many saw her as a threat because of her ruthless reputation.
Once he moved away, the neighborhood felt safer without such a threat around.

Synset Examples

he was the terror of the neighborhood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: menace - a person or thing likely to cause harm; a threat
  • Synonym: danger - the possibility of suffering harm or injury
  • Antonym: protector - a person who protects others from harm or danger

Etymology

From Old English 'threat', related to Old Norse 'thraut' (violence, force).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
