to rumple

1. VERB

/ ˈrʌmpəl / (AmE), / ˈrʌmpəl / (BrE)
to make something wrinkled, creased, or disordered by compressing or manipulating it
make wrinkled or creased

Examples

Don't rumple your shirt before the meeting.
The dog jumped on the bed and rumpled the covers.
She hurriedly rumpled her hair to make it look less tidy.

Synset Examples

She puckered her lips

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to crumple - to crush something so that it becomes creased or wrinkled
  • Antonym: smooth - to make something flat or free from wrinkles or creases

Etymology

Probably from Middle Dutch 'rompelen' (to wrinkle).

2. VERB

/ ˈrʌmpəl / (AmE), / ˈrʌmpəl / (BrE)
to become wrinkled, creased, or untidy, especially in texture or appearance
become wrinkled or creased

Examples

If you don't fold your shirt neatly, it will rumple in the drawer.
Her dress began to rumple after she sat for a long time.
The paper can easily rumple if handled carelessly.

Synset Examples

This fabric won't wrinkle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrinkle - to form small creases or lines
  • Antonym: smooth - to make something flat or free from wrinkles
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00565934-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈrʌmpəl / (AmE), / ˈrʌmpəl / (BrE)
to make something, especially fabric or hair, less smooth or tidy by creasing or messing it up
make less smooth or tidy; crease

Examples

She tried not to rumple her dress while sitting down.
The wind began to rumple the pages of his notebook.
His hair was rumpled from sleeping on the train.

Synset Examples

ruffle the surface of the water

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smooth - to make or become smooth
  • Synonym: to crumple - to crush or wrinkle something into irregular folds
  • Synonym: ruffle - to disturb the smoothness or order of something

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle Dutch *rommelen* (to make uneven).
