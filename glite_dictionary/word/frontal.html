frontal

1. ADJ

/ ˈfrʌn.t̬əl / (AmE), / ˈfrʌn.təl / (BrE)
relating to or situated at the front part of something.
located at or relating to the front

Examples

The frontal lobe of the brain is responsible for decision making and problem solving.
The museum displayed a statue with a frontal view that emphasized the face.
Several animals have frontal appendages used for sensing their environment.

Synset Examples

a frontal appendage

Related Terms

  • Antonym: posterior - situated at the back part
  • Synonym: anterior - situated toward the front; preceding

Etymology

From Latin frontalis, from frons (forehead, front).

Ancient priests often included a golden frontal as part of their sacred vestments.
A silver frontal adorned the dancer's forehead, catching the light as she moved.

From Latin 'frontalis', from 'frons' (forehead).
