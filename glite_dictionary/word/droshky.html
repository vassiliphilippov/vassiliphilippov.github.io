droshky

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɑːski / (AmE), / ˈdrɒski / (BrE)
an open, four-wheeled horse-drawn carriage, formerly used in Poland and Russia
open horse-drawn carriage (Poland, Russia)

Examples

The wealthy merchant arrived at the estate in a splendid droshky.
Tourists admired the antique droshky displayed in the museum's transport section.
In nineteenth-century Russia, a ride in a droshky was common among city dwellers.

Etymology

From Russian *drozhki* (plural of *drozhka*, diminutive of *droga* 'carriage').
