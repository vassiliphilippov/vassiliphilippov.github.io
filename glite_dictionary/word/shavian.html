Shavian

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃeɪviən / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪvɪən / (BrE)
a person who admires or studies the works and ideas of George Bernard Shaw
admirer of George Bernard Shaw

Examples

He is a proud Shavian, having read all of Shaw’s plays.
Many Shavians gather annually to discuss the playwright's influence on modern theater.
As a devoted Shavian, she can quote lines from 'Pygmalion' by heart.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shavian - relating to the works or style of George Bernard Shaw

Etymology

From the name of George Bernard Shaw (1856–1950), British playwright and critic, with the suffix -ian.

2. ADJ

/ ˈʃeɪ.vi.ən / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪ.vi.ən / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of George Bernard Shaw or his works
relating to George Bernard Shaw

Examples

The director's new play revealed a distinctly Shavian wit and social criticism.
Many critics regard the dialogue as powerfully Shavian in both tone and structure.
The production adopted a Shavian approach to challenging societal norms.

Etymology

From the surname Shaw, with the suffix -ian meaning 'of or relating to', influenced by Latin formation patterns.
