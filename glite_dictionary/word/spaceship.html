spaceship

1. NOUN

/ ˈspeɪsˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈspeɪsʃɪp / (BrE)
a spacecraft designed to carry people, especially for travel between planets or stars, often in science fiction
crew-carrying spacecraft

Examples

The crew boarded the spaceship and prepared for launch.
In many science fiction movies, a spaceship travels between distant galaxies.
Designers are working on a real spaceship that could one day visit Mars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spacecraft - a vehicle designed for travel or operation in outer space

Etymology

Formed from 'space' + 'ship' in early 20th century, modeled on 'airship'.
