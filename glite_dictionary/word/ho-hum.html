ho-hum

1. ADJ

/ ˈhoʊˌhʌm / (AmE), / ˈhəʊˌhʌm / (BrE)
so dull or uninteresting as to cause boredom or mental weariness; monotonous or tedious.
dull or boring; uninteresting

Examples

After hearing the same speech for the third time, the audience found it rather ho-hum.
The film was so ho-hum that several people left before it ended.
Her presentation was criticized for being ho-hum and lacking new ideas.

Synset Examples

a boring evening with uninteresting people
the deadening effect of some routine tasks
a dull play
his competent but dull performance
a ho-hum speaker who couldn't capture their attention
what an irksome task the writing of long letters is
tedious days on the train
the tiresome chirping of a cricket
other people's dreams are dreadfully wearisome

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dull - lacking interest or excitement; boring
  • Antonym: exciting - causing great enthusiasm or eagerness

Etymology

Imitative of the sound of a bored or indifferent sigh, first recorded in the early 20th century.
