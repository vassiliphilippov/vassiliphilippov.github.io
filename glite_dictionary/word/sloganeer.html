sloganeer

1. VERB

/ ˌsloʊɡəˈnɪr / (AmE), / ˌsləʊɡəˈnɪə / (BrE)
to invent or promote slogans, especially for political or advertising purposes
to invent or promote slogans

Examples

The campaign team hired a writer to sloganeer for their new political movement.
Every election season, politicians sloganeer to capture the public's attention.
She spent the afternoon sloganeering for the environmental awareness program.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to propagandize - to promote or spread propaganda

Etymology

Formed from 'slogan' and the agent-forming suffix '-eer' by analogy with words like 'pioneer'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01702029-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˌsloʊɡəˈnɪr / (AmE), / ˌsləʊɡəˈnɪə / (BrE)
a person who invents and uses slogans, especially to promote a cause, idea, or product
person who creates and uses slogans

Examples

The political sloganeer came up with a catchy phrase that was repeated throughout the campaign.
He was accused of being a mere sloganeer, focusing on buzzwords instead of real solutions.
As a professional sloganeer, she crafted memorable lines for several social movements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propagandist - person who promotes a cause, often through persuasive means
  • Synonym: campaigner - person who takes part in organized activities to achieve a specific result

Etymology

Formed from 'slogan' + '-eer', by analogy to words like 'auctioneer'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10631889-n
Synset POS: n
