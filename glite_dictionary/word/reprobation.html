reprobation

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛprəˈbeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛprəˈbeɪʃən / (BrE)
strong disapproval or condemnation, especially on moral grounds
severe disapproval or condemnation

Examples

The preacher spoke with utter reprobation of the town's immoral behavior.
Her actions were met with universal reprobation from her colleagues.
He risked the reprobation of his family by choosing a different path.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disapproval - a feeling of having a negative opinion of someone or something
  • Antonym: approval - the belief that something or someone is good or acceptable

Etymology

From Late Latin 'reprobatio' (disapproval, condemnation) from 'reprobare' (to disapprove, condemn).

2. NOUN

/ ˌrɛprəˈbeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛprəˈbeɪʃən / (BrE)
(in Christian theology) Rejection by God, especially the state of being condemned to eternal damnation in Hell.
rejection by God; eternal damnation

Examples

Some theologians have debated the doctrine of reprobation for centuries.
The sermon warned sinners of the danger of reprobation and eternal punishment.
Belief in reprobation can significantly affect one's view of salvation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damnation - condemnation to eternal punishment

Etymology

From Late Latin reprobatio (rejection), from Latin reprobare (to disapprove, reject).
