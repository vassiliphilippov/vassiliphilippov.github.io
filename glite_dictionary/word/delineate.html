to delineate

1. VERB

/ dəˈlɪniˌeɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪnieɪt / (BrE)
to describe or explain something in detail, often clearly and precisely
describe in detail

Examples

The report delineates the steps involved in the project with remarkable clarity.
In her memoir, she delineates the hardships she faced during her childhood.
The document delineates the new regulations for environmental protection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to describe - to give a detailed account of something
  • Synonym: outline - to give a general description or summary of something

Etymology

From Latin delineare (to sketch out), from de- (completely) + linea (line).

2. VERB

/ dəˈlɪniˌeɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪnieɪt / (BrE)
to clearly show, outline, or trace the shape or form of something
to trace or outline the shape of

Examples

The architect used a pencil to delineate the boundaries of the new building on the blueprint.
The mountain range was sharply delineated against the morning sky.
With careful strokes, the artist delineated the contours of the model's face.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outline - to draw the outer edge or shape of something
  • Synonym: to describe - to give an account of the appearance or form of something

Etymology

From Latin delineare (to sketch out, draw), from de- (out) + linea (line).
