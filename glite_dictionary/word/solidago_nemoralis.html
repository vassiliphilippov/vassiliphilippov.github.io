Solidago nemoralis

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɑːlɪˈdeɪɡoʊ nɛˈmɔrəlɪs / (AmE), / ˌsɒlɪˈdeɪɡəʊ nɛˈmɒrəlɪs / (BrE)
A species of goldenrod native to Canada and the eastern United States, commonly known as dyer's weed, with yellow flowers sometimes used in dyeing.
a species of goldenrod with yellow flowers, used in dyeing

Examples

Solidago nemoralis can be found in open fields and along roadsides throughout its native range.
Textile artisans sometimes use Solidago nemoralis to produce natural yellow dyes.
The delicate yellow blossoms of Solidago nemoralis attract a variety of pollinators in late summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goldenrod - a common name for species in the genus Solidago, with yellow flowers

Etymology

From Latin: 'solidago' (goldenrod genus) + 'nemoralis' (of groves/woods).
