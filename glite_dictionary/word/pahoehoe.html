pahoehoe

1. NOUN

/ pəˈhoʊ.iˌhoʊ.i / (AmE), / pəˈhəʊ.iˌhəʊ.i / (BrE)
a type of lava flow with a smooth, ropelike surface, formed by the slow, steady movement of highly fluid lava
smooth, ropelike lava flow

Examples

The pahoehoe cooled into beautiful, ropelike patterns along the volcano’s slope.
Unlike rough, blocky aa lava, pahoehoe forms smooth and billowy surfaces.
Geologists studied the pahoehoe flow to learn more about the volcano’s recent eruption.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: aa - lava with a rough, jagged surface

Etymology

From Hawaiian 'pāhoehoe', meaning 'smooth, unbroken lava'.
