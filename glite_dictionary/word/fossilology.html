fossilology

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɑːsəˈlɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌfɒsɪˈlɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the scientific study of fossils and their relation to ancient life forms and environments
study of fossils
medical

Examples

He developed a keen interest in fossilology after discovering ancient shells in his backyard.
The university offers advanced degrees in fossilology for students focused on prehistoric life.
New findings in fossilology often reshape our understanding of Earth's history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paleontology - the scientific study of life in the geologic past, especially through fossils

Etymology

From Latin 'fossilis' (dug up) + Greek 'logia' (study).
