helminthiasis

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛlmɪnˈθaɪəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌhɛlmɪnˈθaɪəsɪs / (BrE)
Infestation of the body with parasitic worms.
parasitic worm infestation
medical

Examples

Poor sanitation can lead to widespread helminthiasis in rural communities.
The doctor diagnosed the child with helminthiasis after examining stool samples.
Mass deworming campaigns have helped reduce the prevalence of helminthiasis in many developing countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worm infection - the state of being infected with parasitic worms

Etymology

From Greek helmins, helminthos (worm) + -iasis (disease, condition).
