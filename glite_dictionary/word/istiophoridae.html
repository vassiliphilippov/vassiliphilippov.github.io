Istiophoridae

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪstioʊˈfɔːrəˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌɪstɪəʊˈfɒrɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large marine fishes including sailfishes, spearfishes, and marlins, noted for their elongated bills and fast swimming abilities.
family of fishes: marlins, sailfishes

Examples

The Istiophoridae are known for their remarkable speed and agility in the ocean.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of several species within the Istiophoridae family.
The marlin, a member of the Istiophoridae, is often a prized catch for sport fishermen.

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Greek istios (sail) and pherein (to bear).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪstioʊˈfɔrɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌɪstɪəʊˈfɒrɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, carnivorous marine fishes, including sailfishes, spearfishes, and marlins, known for their elongated bodies and spear-like upper jaws.
family of sailfishes, spearfishes, and marlins

Examples

The Istiophoridae are prized in sport fishing for their speed and strength.
Members of the Istiophoridae family have distinctive long bills used to slash at prey.
The biologist studied several specimens belonging to Istiophoridae in the marine laboratory.

Etymology

From New Latin Istiophorus (a genus within the family) and -idae (a taxonomic suffix from Greek -ιδαι, plural of -ιδης).
