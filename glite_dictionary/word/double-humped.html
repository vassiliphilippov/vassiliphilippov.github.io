double-humped

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʌb.əl hʌmpt / (AmE), / ˈdʌb.əl hʌmpt / (BrE)
having two humps, typically used to describe animals such as camels or physical features with two raised parts
having two humps

Examples

The double-humped camel is native to Central Asia.
We could see a double-humped sand dune rising in the distance.
Bactrian camels are the most famous double-humped animals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: single-humped - having one hump

Etymology

Formed from 'double' (from Latin 'duplus', meaning twofold) and 'humped' (from 'hump', meaning a rounded protuberance).
