familiarized

1. ADJ

/ fəˈmɪljəˌraɪzd / (AmE), / fəˈmɪliəraɪzd / (BrE)
having become comfortable or knowledgeable about a particular environment or subject through experience or study
having become comfortable with something

Examples

After a week at the new job, she felt familiarized with the office routines.
International students quickly became familiarized with the campus layout and resources.
The training session ensured all employees were familiarized with safety procedures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acquainted - having knowledge or experience of something

Etymology

From the verb 'familiarize', which is formed from 'familiar' from Latin 'familiaris' (of a household, familiar), with the suffix '-ize' and '-ed' for the past participle.
