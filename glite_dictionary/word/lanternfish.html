lanternfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæntɚnˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈlæntənˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a small deep-sea fish with rows of light-producing organs along each side of its body, some of which rise to the surface at night
small bioluminescent deep-sea fish

Examples

The lanternfish uses its glowing organs to attract prey in the dark ocean depths.
Schools of lanternfish migrate to the surface at night in search of food.
Scientists study the bioluminescence of the lanternfish to understand how deep-sea creatures communicate.

Etymology

Named for their bioluminescent organs, resembling lanterns.
