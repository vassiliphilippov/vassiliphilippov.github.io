rheometer

1. NOUN

/ riˈɑːmətər / (AmE), / riˈɒmɪtə / (BrE)
An instrument for measuring the flow or viscosity of liquids, especially in scientific or medical applications.
instrument for measuring liquid flow or viscosity
medical

Examples

The laboratory used a rheometer to analyze the blood samples for viscosity changes.
Engineers tested the new polymer's properties using a high-precision rheometer.
Regular calibration of the rheometer ensures accurate measurements in scientific studies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viscometer - instrument for measuring the viscosity of liquids

Etymology

From Greek 'rheos' (stream, flow) + '-meter' (measuring device).
