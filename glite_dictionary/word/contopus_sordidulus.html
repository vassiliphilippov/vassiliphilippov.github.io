Contopus sordidulus

1. NOUN

/ kɑnˈtoʊpəs sɔrˈdɪdʒələs / (AmE), / kɒnˈtəʊpəs sɔːˈdɪdjʊləs / (BrE)
A small flycatcher native to western North America, known for its slender build and distinctive call.
small North American flycatcher

Examples

Birdwatchers often spot the Contopus sordidulus in pine forests during the summer months.
The distinctive call of the Contopus sordidulus makes it easier to identify among other flycatchers.
Researchers are studying the migration patterns of Contopus sordidulus across North America.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Contopus' meaning 'shrunken foot,' and 'sordidulus' meaning 'rather dirty.'
