beebread

1. NOUN

/ ˈbiˌbrɛd / (AmE), / ˈbiːbrɛd / (BrE)
A substance made by worker bees from a mixture of pollen and nectar, used as food for developing larvae.
mixture of pollen and nectar fed to bee larvae

Examples

Worker bees store beebread in the honeycomb to feed their young.
The hive was filled with honey, wax, and beebread.
Scientists studied the nutritional value of beebread for developing larvae.

Etymology

Formed from 'bee' + 'bread', referring to its function as nourishment.
