incompressible

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪnkəmˈprɛsəbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪnkəmˈprɛsəbl / (BrE)
incapable of being compressed; resisting compression
not able to be compressed

Examples

Water is often treated as incompressible in fluid dynamics calculations for simplicity.
Plastic packaging protects fragile items with incompressible materials that absorb shocks.
The metal blocks used in testing were almost perfectly incompressible even under high pressure.

Synset Examples

mounds of incompressible garbage

Related Terms

  • Antonym: compressible - able to be pressed or squeezed into a smaller volume

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'in-' (not) + 'compressible,' from Latin 'comprimere' (to compress).
