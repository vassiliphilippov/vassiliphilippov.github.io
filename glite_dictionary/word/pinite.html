pinite

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪˌnaɪt / (AmE), / ˈpiːnʌɪt / (BrE)
a grey, green, or brown mineral resembling mica that contains aluminum and potassium sulphates, typically occurring in altered rocks
a mineral resembling mica

Examples

The geologist identified the shiny layer in the rock as pinite.
Samples of pinite were collected for analysis during the field trip.
Under the microscope, pinite displays properties similar to those of mica.

Etymology

From German 'Pinit', named after the mineralogist Johann F. L. Pina.
