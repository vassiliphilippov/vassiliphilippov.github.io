Montezuma

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːntəˈzuːmə / (AmE), / ˌmɒntɪˈzuːmə / (BrE)
An evergreen tree with large leathery leaves and large pink to orange flowers, considered a transitional species between the Bombacaceae and Sterculiaceae plant families.
evergreen tree with large pink to orange flowers

Examples

The Montezuma tree is prized for its striking pink and orange blossoms.
Botanists studied the Montezuma to understand the evolutionary links between plant families.
A rare Montezuma was discovered flourishing in the botanical garden.

Etymology

From Montezuma, the name of Aztec emperors; used in taxonomy for several plant and animal genera.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːn.təˈzuː.mə / (AmE), / ˌmɒn.tɪˈzjuː.mə / (BrE)
A genus of medium-sized evergreen trees native to Puerto Rico and Mexico.
genus of evergreen tree in the tropics

Examples

The Montezuma is prized for its hard wood and majestic canopy.
Researchers discovered a rare specimen of Montezuma in the forests of Puerto Rico.
Botanists are studying the unique leaf structure of the Montezuma tree.

Etymology

Named after Moctezuma (Montezuma), the Aztec emperor.
