Elector

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛktər / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktə / (BrE)
any of the German princes with the right to vote in the election of the Holy Roman Emperor
prince who elected the Holy Roman Emperor

Examples

The Elector of Saxony played a crucial role in the imperial election.
As an Elector, he held significant influence within the Holy Roman Empire.
The meeting of the Electors determined the next emperor.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'elector', from Latin 'eligere' (to choose).
