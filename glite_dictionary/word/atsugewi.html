Atsugewi

1. NOUN

/ ˌætsuˈɡeɪwi / (AmE), / ˌætsjuˈɡeɪwi / (BrE)
the Shastan language traditionally spoken by the Atsugewi people of northeastern California
a Shastan language of California

Examples

Only a handful of elders still remember how to speak Atsugewi.
Researchers have been working to document the vocabulary of Atsugewi before it disappears.
The Atsugewi language belongs to the Shastan family of native Californian languages.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑːtsuːˈɡeɪwi / (AmE), / ˌætsuːˈɡeɪwi / (BrE)
A member of a Native American people originally living in northern California.
member of Native American group of northern California

Examples

An anthropologist visited an Atsugewi village to study their traditional crafts.
The Atsugewi were known for their unique methods of basket weaving.
A proud Atsugewi elder shared stories about the tribe's history.

Etymology

From Atsugewi, the self-designation of the people in their own language.
