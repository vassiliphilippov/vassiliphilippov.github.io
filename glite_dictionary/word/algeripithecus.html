Algeripithecus

1. NOUN

/ ælˌdʒɛrɪˈpɪθɪkəs / (AmE), / ælˌdʒɛrɪˈpɪθɪkəs / (BrE)
an extinct genus of primates known from fossil remains in North Africa, once believed to belong to early apes.
extinct genus of North African primates

Examples

The discovery of Algeripithecus fossils in Algeria shed light on early primate evolution.
Some researchers debate whether Algeripithecus was more closely related to monkeys or apes.
Studies of Algeripithecus have influenced theories about primate migration in the Eocene.

Etymology

From Algeria (the country) + Greek 'pithēkos' (ape).

2. PROPN

/ ælˌdʒɪrɪˈpɪθɪkəs / (AmE), / ælˌdʒɪərɪˈpɪθɪkəs / (BrE)
an extinct genus of primates once classified among the Hominoidea, known from fossil remains found in Algeria
extinct genus of primates

Examples

The discovery of Algeripithecus fossils provided new insights into primate evolution.
Paleontologists debated the classification of Algeripithecus within the primate family tree.
The remains of Algeripithecus were unearthed in Eocene strata in Algeria.

Etymology

From Algeria, where its fossils were found, and Greek 'pithēkos' (ape).
