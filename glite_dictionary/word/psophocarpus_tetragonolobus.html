Psophocarpus tetragonolobus

1. NOUN

/ soʊfəˈkɑːrpəs ˌtɛtrəˌɡɑːnəˈloʊbəs / (AmE), / sɒfəˈkɑːpəs ˌtɛtrəˌɡænəˈləʊbəs / (BrE)
A tropical leguminous vine, commonly known as the winged bean, valued for its edible pods, seeds, tubers, and leaves.
species of tropical vine; winged bean

Examples

Farmers in Southeast Asia often cultivate Psophocarpus tetragonolobus for its high-protein beans and edible leaves.
The roots of Psophocarpus tetragonolobus are rich in nutrients and can be used as a food source.
Botanists are studying Psophocarpus tetragonolobus because it thrives in tropical climates and enriches soil fertility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winged bean - common name for Psophocarpus tetragonolobus

Etymology

Genus name from Greek *psophos* (a sound) and *karpos* (fruit); species name from Latin *tetragonus* (four-angled) and *lobus* (pod, lobe).
