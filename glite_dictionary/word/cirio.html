cirio

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːrioʊ / (AmE), / ˈsɪərɪəʊ / (BrE)
A tall columnar plant native to Mexico and southwestern California, known for its honey-scented creamy yellow flowers; also called candlewood.
a tall columnar plant of Mexico with yellow flowers

Examples

The cirio stands tall in the desert landscape, its flowers attracting bees from miles around.
During our trip to Baja California, we admired the strange beauty of the cirio forests.
The cirio is sometimes called the 'boojum tree' due to its unusual appearance.

Etymology

From Spanish 'cirio', meaning 'candle', referring to the plant's shape.
