Bidens

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪˌdɛnz / (AmE), / ˈbɪdɛnz / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, commonly known as bur marigolds, characterized by yellow, daisy-like flowers and barbed fruits that stick to clothing or fur.
a genus of plants (bur marigolds)

Examples

The fields were dotted with Bidens in full yellow bloom by late summer.
After walking through the meadow, seeds of Bidens clung to my socks.
Many gardeners plant Bidens to attract butterflies and bees to their gardens.

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'two-toothed', referring to the two barbed seeds of many species.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪdɛnz / (AmE), / ˈbaɪdɛnz / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the daisy family (Asteraceae), commonly known as bur marigolds, with species having barbed seeds that stick to animals and clothing.
a genus of bur marigolds (plants)

Examples

The Bidens genus includes several species commonly found in wetlands and along riverbanks.
Many gardeners appreciate Bidens for its bright yellow flowers and long blooming season.
Seeds from Bidens plants often cling to hikers' clothes due to their barbed structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bur marigold - common name for plants in the Bidens genus

Etymology

From Latin bidens (two-toothed), referring to the appearance of the seeds.
