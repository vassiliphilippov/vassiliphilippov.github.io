onrush

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑnˌrʌʃ / (AmE), / ˈɒnˌrʌʃ / (BrE)
a sudden and forceful attack or offensive, especially against an enemy in a military context
sudden and forceful attack

Examples

The enemy was caught off guard by the sudden onrush of troops at dawn.
The general prepared his forces to withstand the onrush from the north.
Their lines crumbled under the onrush of artillery and infantry.

Synset Examples

the attack began at dawn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assault - a violent attack
  • Antonym: defense - the act of resisting an attack

Etymology

From prefix 'on-' (toward) + 'rush' (to move forward quickly).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑnˌrʌʃ / (AmE), / ˈɒnˌrʌʃ / (BrE)
a sudden, powerful forward movement or flow of something
sudden, forceful forward movement

Examples

The river's onrush after the storm swept away the old bridge.
He was caught off guard by the onrush of memories triggered by the song.
The onrush of fans into the stadium was difficult to control.

Synset Examples

from the bow she stared at the mesmerising onrush of the sea where it split and foamed
the explosion interrupted the wild onrush of her thoughts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rush - a sudden movement forward; surge

Etymology

From prefix 'on-' (onward) + 'rush', dating from the early 19th century.
