sleaziness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsliːzinəs / (AmE), / ˈsliːzinɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being morally dishonorable, corrupt, or sordid
dishonorable or sordid quality

Examples

The sleaziness of the politician's actions shocked even his loyal supporters.
She was uncomfortable with the sleaziness that surrounded the nightclub business.
He tried to hide the sleaziness of his dealings, but the truth eventually came out.

Synset Examples

an embarrassing sleaziness that I hope will be corrected by the more ethical newspapers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corruption - dishonest or fraudulent conduct, especially by those in power
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being honest and free of deceit

Etymology

From 'sleazy' (of obscure origin), meaning sordid or corrupt, + '-ness' forming a noun.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsliːzinɪs / (AmE), / ˈsliːzinɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being shabby, dirty, or lacking in elegance, especially due to worn or untidy clothing
shabbiness or lack of elegance

Examples

The sleaziness of his appearance put off many potential employers.
Despite the sleaziness of the venue, the party was lively and fun.
She was determined to overcome the sleaziness of her surroundings and make a better life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shabbiness - the condition of being untidy or worn out
  • Antonym: elegance - the quality of being stylish and graceful

Etymology

From 'sleazy', of uncertain origin, possibly from Silesian (in Germany), known for producing inferior cloth in the 17th century, plus the suffix '-ness'.
