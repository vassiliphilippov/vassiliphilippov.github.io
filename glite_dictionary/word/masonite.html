Masonite

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪsəˌnaɪt / (AmE), / ˈmeɪsənaɪt / (BrE)
a type of hard, smooth fiberboard made from steam-cooked and pressure-molded wood fibers, commonly used in construction and furniture making
type of hard fiberboard

Examples

The doors were constructed from Masonite to provide a smooth and durable surface.
He used a sheet of Masonite as a backing for his painting.
Many theater sets are built using Masonite panels because they are easy to paint and cut.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fiberboard - a type of engineered wood product made from compressed wood fibers
  • Synonym: hardboard - a stiff board made from compressed and treated wood fibers

Etymology

Trademark; named after William H. Mason, the inventor, and -ite (indicating material).
