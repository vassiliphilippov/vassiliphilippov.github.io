Nimitz

1. PROPN

/ ˈnɪmɪts / (AmE), / ˈnɪmɪts / (BrE)
(1885–1966) United States admiral who commanded the Pacific fleet during World War II and played a key role in the defeat of the Japanese navy.
U.S. admiral, WWII Pacific fleet commander

Examples

Admiral Nimitz was instrumental in planning major naval battles in the Pacific during World War II.
Many naval strategies are still studied today because of Nimitz's innovative leadership.
The USS Nimitz aircraft carrier was named in honor of his significant military service.
