salade

1. NOUN

/ səˈlɑːd / (AmE), / səˈlɑːd / (BrE)
a type of light medieval helmet with a slit for vision, typically covering the back and sides of the head
light medieval helmet with vision slit

Examples

The knight wore a salade into battle for protection against sword strikes.
Museum visitors admired the exquisite salade displayed in the medieval armor section.
Artisans carefully crafted each salade to balance defense with visibility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: helmet - protective headgear, especially used in battle

Etymology

From Middle French salade, probably from Italian celata, ultimately from Latin celatus, past participle of celare (to hide).
