menacing

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɛnəsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɛnɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
suggesting the presence of danger or evil; threatening
threatening or suggesting harm

Examples

The dog gave the stranger a menacing growl as he approached the gate.
Dark, menacing clouds gathered on the horizon before the storm hit.
She shot him a menacing glance that made him feel increasingly uneasy.

Synset Examples

a baleful look
forbidding thunderclouds
his tone became menacing
ominous rumblings of discontent
sinister storm clouds
a sinister smile
his threatening behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: threatening - expressing a promise of harm or danger
  • Synonym: sinister - giving the impression that something harmful or evil is happening or will happen
  • Antonym: reassuring - making someone feel less worried or fearful

Etymology

From menace + -ing; 'menace' from Old French 'menacer', from Latin 'minacia' (threats).
