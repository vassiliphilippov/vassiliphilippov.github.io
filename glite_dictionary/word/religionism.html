religionism

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈlɪdʒənˌɪzəm / (AmE), / rɪˈlɪdʒənɪzəm / (BrE)
exaggerated or affected piety and religious zeal.
excessive religious zeal or piety

Examples

Her religionism was evident in every conversation, often making her colleagues uncomfortable.
Critics accused the leader of religionism when he imposed strict moral codes.
The novel explores the dangers of unchecked religionism within a divided community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zealotry - fanatical and uncompromising pursuit of religious or political ideals

Etymology

Formed from 'religion' + '-ism', indicating a characteristic or practice.

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈlɪdʒənˌɪzəm / (AmE), / rɪˈlɪdʒənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
exaggerated or excessive religious zeal or devotion, often characterized by intolerance or extreme practices.
excessive religious zeal or devotion

Examples

The leader's religionism made him intolerant of anyone with different beliefs.
Critics accused the group of religionism after they enforced strict codes of conduct on their members.
Some scholars argue that religionism can undermine social harmony when it turns divisive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zealotry - fanatical and uncompromising pursuit of religious or political ideals

Etymology

Formed from 'religion' + suffix '-ism', modeled on analogous terms indicating excessive or dogmatic behavior.
