ontogenesis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːntoʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌɒntə(ʊ)ˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (BrE)
the process by which an individual organism develops and changes from a simple to a more complex form, especially from fertilization to maturity
development of an organism from simple to complex
medical

Examples

The study focused on the ontogenesis of amphibians from their larval stage to adulthood.
Environmental factors can influence the ontogenesis of various traits in plants.
Understanding ontogenesis is crucial for developmental biology and medicine.

Synset Examples

he proposed an indicator of osseous development in children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ontogeny - the development or course of development, especially of an individual organism

Etymology

From Greek 'ontos' (being) + 'genesis' (origin, creation).
