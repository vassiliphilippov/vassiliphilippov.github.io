Kwajalein Atoll

1. PROPN

/ kwɑː.dʒəˌleɪ.ɪn əˈtɑl / (AmE), / kwɑː.dʒəˌleɪ.ɪn əˈtɒl / (BrE)
an atoll in the western Marshall Islands, notable for its use as a Japanese base during World War II and its continuing strategic importance
atoll in the western Marshall Islands

Examples

The United States captured Kwajalein Atoll from Japanese forces in 1944.
Today, Kwajalein Atoll hosts an important U.S. military installation and radar site.
Researchers studied coral reefs around Kwajalein Atoll to assess environmental changes.
