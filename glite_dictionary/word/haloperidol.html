haloperidol

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæloʊˈpɛrɪˌdɔl / (AmE), / ˌhaləʊˈpɛrɪdɒl / (BrE)
a powerful antipsychotic drug used to treat certain psychotic disorders and Tourette's syndrome
antipsychotic drug for psychotic disorders
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed haloperidol to help manage the patient's schizophrenia.
After taking haloperidol, her symptoms of agitation decreased significantly.
Some people experience side effects when using haloperidol for long-term treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antipsychotic - a drug used to treat psychosis

Etymology

From halo- (referring to halogen) + peridol (from piperidine, a chemical structure in the compound).
