eclectic

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈklɛktɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈklɛktɪk / (BrE)
deriving ideas, style, or taste from a diverse range of sources rather than following a single system
selecting from a variety of sources

Examples

Her eclectic taste in music ranges from classical to heavy metal.
The restaurant is known for its eclectic menu, offering dishes from around the world.
He decorated his apartment in an eclectic style, mixing vintage and modern pieces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: selective - choosing carefully from various options

Etymology

From Greek eklektikos (selective), from eklegein (to select).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00779045-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈklɛktɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈklɛktɪk / (BrE)
a person who selects ideas, styles, or tastes from a diverse range of sources rather than adhering to a single system or style
person choosing from various sources

Examples

The magazine editor was an eclectic, always curious to explore new trends and voices.
Among philosophers, he was regarded as an eclectic who embraced multiple schools of thought.
As an eclectic, she never limited herself to one genre in her art collection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eclectic - selecting ideas or styles from a broad range of sources (adjective)

Etymology

From Greek eklektikos (selective), from eklegein (to select) via Latin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10062832-n
Synset POS: n
