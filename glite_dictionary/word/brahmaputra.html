Brahmaputra

1. PROPN

/ ˌbrɑːməˈpuːtrə / (AmE), / ˌbrɑːməˈpuːtrə / (BrE)
a major river in South Asia, rising in Tibet and flowing through India and Bangladesh into the Bay of Bengal
major river in South Asia

Examples

The Brahmaputra often floods during the monsoon season, affecting millions of people in Bangladesh.
Scientists are studying the water quality of the Brahmaputra to assess environmental changes in the region.
The cruise along the Brahmaputra offers a unique view of Assam's rich wildlife and culture.

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'Brahmaputra', meaning 'son of Brahma'.
