Aleksandr Borodin

1. PROPN

/ əˌlɛkˈsɑndr ˌbɔrəˈdin / (AmE), / alɪkˈsandə ˌbɒrəˈdiːn / (BrE)
(1833–1887) A Russian composer and chemist, noted for his symphonies, operas, and chamber music, and as a leading member of the group known as The Five.
Russian composer and chemist

Examples

Many music historians regard Aleksandr Borodin as one of the leading Romantic composers in Russia.
The famous opera 'Prince Igor' was composed by Aleksandr Borodin.
In addition to his musical achievements, Aleksandr Borodin was also a distinguished chemist.
