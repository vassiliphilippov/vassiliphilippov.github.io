isoclinal

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪsəˈklaɪnəl / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəˈklaɪnəl / (BrE)
A line on a map or chart connecting points where the magnetic inclination is the same.
line connecting equal magnetic inclinations

Examples

The map showed an isoclinal running through the center of the country, indicating uniform magnetic inclination.
Scientists use isoclinals to study changes in the Earth’s magnetic field over large regions.
Each isoclinal represents a line of equal dip angle of the Earth’s magnetic field.

Etymology

From Greek iso- (equal) + klinein (to lean).

2. ADJ

/ ˌaɪ.səˈklaɪ.nəl / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.səˈklaɪ.nəl / (BrE)
Having equal magnetic inclinations; relating to or denoting lines on a map connecting points of equal magnetic dip.
having equal magnetic inclinations

Examples

The scientist drew isoclinal lines on the map to indicate areas of equal magnetic inclination.
Geologists often refer to isoclinal folds when discussing certain rock formations, but in geomagnetism, the term describes equal inclination.
Magnetic maps sometimes use isoclinal lines to help navigators understand changes in the Earth's magnetic field.

Etymology

From Greek 'isos' (equal) + 'klīnein' (to lean, incline).
