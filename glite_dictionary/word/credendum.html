credendum

1. NOUN

/ krəˈdɛndəm / (AmE), / krɛˈdɛndəm / (BrE)
(in Christian theology) any of the articles or sections into which a creed or doctrinal statement is divided, representing a tenet of faith to be believed.
doctrinal article in a creed

Examples

Each credendum of the creed was recited solemnly by the congregation.
The council debated the precise wording of every credendum.
He struggled with accepting every credendum required by his denomination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dogma - a principle or set of principles laid down by an authority as incontrovertibly true

Etymology

From Latin 'credendum', meaning 'that which is to be believed'.
