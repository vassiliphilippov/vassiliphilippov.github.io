to readjust

1. VERB

/ ˌriː.əˈdʒʌst / (AmE), / ˌriː.əˈdʒʌst / (BrE)
to adjust or adapt something again, especially after a previous attempt or change
adjust again; make new adjustments

Examples

After the new policy failed, the company had to readjust its strategy.
It took him several weeks to readjust to life after moving to a new city.
You may need to readjust your expectations if things don't go as planned.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to adjust - to change or alter something to make it suitable; the general verb

Etymology

From re- (again) + adjust, modeled on French réajuster.

2. VERB

/ ˌriˈəˌdʒʌst / (AmE), / ˌriːˈəˌdʒʌst / (BrE)
to adjust again, especially in order to adapt to new or changed conditions
adjust again or anew

Examples

After the major reorganization, employees had to readjust to their new roles.
It took her a while to readjust after returning home from abroad.
You may need to readjust your expectations when the situation changes.

Synset Examples

After moving back to America, he had to readjust

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to adjust - change or move something slightly to achieve the desired fit or result

Etymology

From re- (again) + adjust (from Old French 'ajuster', from Late Latin 'adjuxtare').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00150934-v
Synset POS: v
