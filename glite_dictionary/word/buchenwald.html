Buchenwald

1. PROPN

/ ˈbuːkənˌwɑld / (AmE), / ˈbuːkənˌvalt / (BrE)
A Nazi concentration camp for Jews and other prisoners during World War II, located in central Germany.
Nazi concentration camp in central Germany

Examples

Many prisoners perished at Buchenwald during the Holocaust.
The liberation of Buchenwald revealed the horrors of the Nazi regime to the world.
A memorial now stands at the site of Buchenwald to honor the victims.

Etymology

German, literally 'beech forest', named after its wooded location.
