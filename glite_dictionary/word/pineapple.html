pineapple

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪnˌæpəl / (AmE), / ˈpaɪnˌæpəl / (BrE)
a tropical plant with a large juicy edible fruit topped by stiff leaves, widely grown in warm climates
a tropical fruit with stiff leaves

Examples

He brought a fresh pineapple to the picnic for everyone to share.
The pineapple is rich in vitamin C and has a sweet, tangy flavor.
She used diced pineapple in the salsa to give it a tropical twist.

Etymology

From Spanish 'piña' (pine cone) and English 'apple', referring to the fruit's resemblance to a pine cone.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪnˌæpəl / (AmE), / ˈpaɪnˌæpəl / (BrE)
A large tropical fruit with sweet, juicy, yellow flesh and a tough, spiky skin, topped with a tuft of stiff leaves.
sweet tropical fruit with spiky skin

Examples

She cut the pineapple into slices and served them for dessert.
Fresh pineapple juice is my favorite drink on a hot day.
The pineapple plant has long, spiky leaves growing from the top of the fruit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ananas - another word for the pineapple fruit used in many languages
  • Degree Lower: banana - another tropical fruit, long and yellow

Etymology

From Spanish *piña* (pine cone) + English *apple* (fruit), due to resemblance.
