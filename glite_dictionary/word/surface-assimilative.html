surface-assimilative

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɝːfɪs əˌsɪməˈleɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈsɜːfɪs əˌsɪməˈleɪtɪv / (BrE)
having the ability or tendency to adsorb or cause substances to accumulate on a surface
tending to adsorb on a surface

Examples

The new filter uses a surface-assimilative material to trap contaminants effectively.
Researchers developed a surface-assimilative coating for medical devices to prevent bacterial buildup.
The surface-assimilative nature of the compound increases its efficiency in removing toxins from water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adsorptive - having the ability to adsorb substances onto a surface

Etymology

From 'surface' and 'assimilative', relating to absorbing or accumulating substances on surfaces.
