unmovable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈmuːvəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈmuːvəbəl / (BrE)
not able or intended to be moved; fixed in place
not able to be moved

Examples

The old concrete bench was so unmovable that no one even tried to lift it.
Her beliefs were unmovable despite the many arguments she heard.
The statue stood unmovable at the center of the plaza for over a century.

Synset Examples

the immovable hills

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immovable - not able to be moved; fixed in place
  • Antonym: movable - able to be moved

Etymology

From un- (not) + movable, based on move (from Latin movere).
