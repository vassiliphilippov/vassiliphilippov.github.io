unwarranted

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈwɔrən.tɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈwɒr.ən.tɪd / (BrE)
not having a good reason or justification; not deserved or justified
not justified or deserved

Examples

Her criticism was completely unwarranted and unfair.
The company issued an apology for the unwarranted delay in service.
He made unwarranted assumptions about her motives without evidence.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: justified - having a good reason or justification
  • Synonym: unjustified - not shown to be right or reasonable

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (not) + 'warranted' (from Old French 'garantir', to guarantee).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈwɔrən.tɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈwɒr.əntɪd / (BrE)
not justified or authorized; done without good reason or official permission.
not justified or authorized

Examples

The police conducted an unwarranted search of the premises, violating the residents' rights.
His criticism was entirely unwarranted and hurt her feelings unnecessarily.
Many felt the new regulations imposed unwarranted restrictions on businesses.

Synset Examples

desire for undue private profit
unwarranted limitations of personal freedom

Related Terms

  • Antonym: justified - having an acceptable or logical reason
  • Antonym: authorized - officially approved or permitted

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) and 'warranted,' from Old French *garantir* (to protect, guarantee).

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈwɔr.ən.tɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈwɒr.ən.tɪd / (BrE)
not justified or authorized; having no basis in fact or reason.
not justified; without reason or authorization

Examples

The manager's criticism of her work was completely unwarranted.
There was unwarranted fear about the safety of the new vaccine.
His arrest was unwarranted and later proved to be a mistake.

Synset Examples

baseless gossip
the allegations proved groundless
idle fears
unfounded suspicions
unwarranted jealousy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unfounded - having no basis in fact; not justified
  • Synonym: groundless - not based on any good reason
  • Antonym: justified - having a good reason for something

Etymology

From un- (not) + warranted, from warrant (guarantee, justify), ultimately from Old French *garant* (protector).
