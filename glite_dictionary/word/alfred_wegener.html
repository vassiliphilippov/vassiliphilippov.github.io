Alfred Wegener

1. PROPN

/ ˈælfɚd ˈveɪɡənɚ / (AmE), / ˈælfrəd ˈvɛɡənə / (BrE)
(1880–1930) German geophysicist who proposed the theory of continental drift.
German geophysicist; proposed continental drift

Examples

Alfred Wegener is best known for developing the theory that continents once formed a single landmass.
Many textbooks discuss the contributions of Alfred Wegener to modern geology.
Although initially controversial, Alfred Wegener's idea of continental drift revolutionized earth sciences.
