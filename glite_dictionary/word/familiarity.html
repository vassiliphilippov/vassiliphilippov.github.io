familiarity

1. NOUN

/ fəˌmɪl.jəˈrɛɾ.i / (AmE), / fəˌmɪl.iˈær.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being well known, recognized, or usual due to frequent occurrence or previous experience
the quality of being well known or usual

Examples

There was a sense of familiarity about the old café that made her feel comfortable.
Over time, the familiarity of his daily routine began to feel monotonous.
The familiarity of her voice reassured me despite the chaos around us.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: novelty - the quality of being new, original, or unusual

Etymology

From Latin familiaritas (close acquaintance or intimacy).

2. NOUN

/ fəˌmɪl.iˈær.ə.ti / (AmE), / fəˌmɪl.iˈær.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
personal knowledge or understanding of someone or something, usually gained by experience
personal knowledge or experience

Examples

Her familiarity with classical music made her an excellent piano teacher.
The manager's familiarity with the new software helped the team resolve technical issues quickly.
Over time, familiarity with the local customs allowed him to adjust easily to life abroad.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intimacy - close familiarity or friendship
  • Antonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge or information

Etymology

From Latin familiaritas (closeness, intimacy), from familiaris (familiar, of a household).

3. NOUN

/ fəˌmɪlˈjæɾɪti / (AmE), / fəˌmɪlˈjærɪti / (BrE)
a relaxed and informal way of behaving with others, often without strict respect for formality
casual or informal manner

Examples

His familiarity with the staff sometimes made new employees uncomfortable.
She approached the meeting with surprising familiarity, greeting everyone by their first names.
There's a danger in too much familiarity when dealing with clients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: informality - the quality of being relaxed and friendly without strict rules of behavior
  • Antonym: formality - strict adherence to established rules or conventions

Etymology

From Latin familiaritas (intimacy, friendship), from familiaris (of a household, familiar).
