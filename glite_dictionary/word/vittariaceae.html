Vittariaceae

1. NOUN

/ vɪˌtɛriˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / vɪˌtæriˈeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
A family of ferns, including the genus Vittaria, recognized in some plant classification systems.
family of ferns (includes Vittaria)
medical

Examples

The Vittariaceae are primarily tropical ferns found in various regions around the world.
Recent botanical studies have revised the classification of Vittariaceae.
Many species in the Vittariaceae family have distinctive ribbon-like leaves.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Vittaria and standard family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ vɪˌtɛriˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / vɪˌtɛəriˈeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of tropical ferns, including the genus Vittaria, classified in some systems as distinct from Polypodiaceae
family of tropical ferns

Examples

The Vittariaceae are known for their ribbon-like fronds in tropical forests.
Some botanists classify Vittariaceae as a separate family distinct from Polypodiaceae.
Members of the Vittariaceae can often be found growing on rocks and tree trunks.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Polypodiaceae - a larger family of ferns sometimes including Vittariaceae

Etymology

From New Latin, from genus Vittaria + standard botanical family suffix -aceae.
