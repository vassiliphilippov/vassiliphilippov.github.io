Phrynosoma

1. NOUN

/ fraɪˈnoʊsəmə / (AmE), / frɪˈnɒsəmə / (BrE)
A genus of lizards commonly known as horned lizards, characterized by short, squat bodies and spiny scales, native to North and Central America.
genus of horned lizards

Examples

The desert is home to several species of Phrynosoma, known for their spiky appearance.
Researchers studied a Phrynosoma to understand its unique defense mechanisms against predators.
Many people mistake a Phrynosoma for a toad because of its round body and spines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horned lizard - a lizard of the genus Phrynosoma, characterized by horn-like projections

Etymology

From Greek phrynos (toad) + soma (body), referring to the toad-like appearance of these lizards.
