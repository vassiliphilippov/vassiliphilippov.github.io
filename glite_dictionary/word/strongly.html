strongly

1. ADV

/ ˈstrɔŋli / (AmE), / ˈstrɒŋli / (BrE)
In a strong, forceful, or intense way.
in a forceful or intense manner

Examples

She strongly recommends trying the new restaurant downtown.
The evidence strongly suggests that they are telling the truth.
He strongly denied any involvement in the incident.

Synset Examples

argues very strongly for his proposal
he was strongly opposed to the government

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firmly - in a strong or secure way
  • Antonym: weakly - in a manner lacking strength

Etymology

From 'strong' + '-ly', with 'strong' from Old English 'strang'

2. ADV

/ ˈstrɔŋli / (AmE), / ˈstrɒŋli / (BrE)
In a powerful or forceful way; with great intensity or conviction.
in a powerful or forceful manner

Examples

She strongly opposed the proposed changes to the policy.
The flavors in this dish are strongly influenced by traditional spices.
Experts strongly recommend wearing sunscreen during long periods outdoors.

Synset Examples

the federal government replaced the powerfully pro-settler Sir Godfrey Huggins with the even tougher and more determined ex-trade unionist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: powerfully - in a powerful manner
  • Antonym: weakly - in a weak manner

Etymology

From strong + -ly, forming the adverb from the adjective 'strong'.
