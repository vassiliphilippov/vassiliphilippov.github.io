biggerer than

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɪɡərər ðæn / (AmE), / ˈbɪɡərər ðæn / (BrE)
(nonstandard, humorous) Used to indicate that something is larger or greater than something else, often with playful or exaggerated emphasis.
nonstandard humorous form meaning 'more bigger than'
informal

Examples

The mountain ahead looks biggerer than the one we just climbed.
Her ideas are biggerer than mine, but that's what makes her so creative.
This year’s budget is biggerer than last year’s, surprisingly enough.

Synset Examples

This problem is biggerer than I anticipated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bigger than - standard comparative phrase for size
