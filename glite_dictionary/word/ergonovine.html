ergonovine

1. NOUN

/ ˌɜːrɡəˈnoʊvin / (AmE), / ˌɜːɡəˈnəʊviːn / (BrE)
an alkaloid derived from ergot used to induce contraction of the uterus, especially after childbirth or abortion
alkaloid used to contract uterus
medical

Examples

The doctor administered ergonovine to control excessive bleeding after delivery.
Use of ergonovine is carefully monitored because of its strong effects on uterine muscles.
Researchers are studying alternatives to ergonovine for postpartum hemorrhage treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ergot - a fungus from which ergonovine is derived

Etymology

From 'ergo' (referring to ergot) and 'novine', a chemical suffix indicating an amide; named for its source and structure.
