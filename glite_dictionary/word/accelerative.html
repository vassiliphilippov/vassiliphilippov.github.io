accelerative

1. ADJ

/ əkˈsɛlɚˌeɪtɪv / (AmE), / əkˈsɛl.ər.ə.tɪv / (BrE)
tending to cause acceleration or increase in speed or rate; promoting acceleration.
increasing speed; promoting acceleration

Examples

The new drug has an accelerative effect on heart rate, making it useful in emergency situations.
Technological progress can have an accelerative impact on economic growth.
She studied the accelerative forces acting on the car during the test drive.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: decelerative - tending to decrease velocity

Etymology

From Latin 'accelerare' (to hasten, speed up) + '-ive' (forming adjectives).
