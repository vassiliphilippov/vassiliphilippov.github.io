multiprocessor

1. NOUN

/ ˌmʌltiˈproʊsɛsər / (AmE), / ˌmʌltɪˈprəʊsɛsə / (BrE)
A computer system that uses two or more central processing units (CPUs) under integrated control for increased performance or reliability.
computer with multiple processing units

Examples

Most modern servers are designed as multiprocessor systems to handle more tasks efficiently.
The company upgraded its infrastructure to include multiprocessor machines for faster data processing.
A multiprocessor can perform parallel computations that speed up complex calculations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uniprocessor - a computer with a single central processing unit

Etymology

Formed from 'multi-' (many) and 'processor'.
