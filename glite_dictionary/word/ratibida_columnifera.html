Ratibida columnifera

1. NOUN

/ rəˈtɪbɪdə kəˌlʌmɪˈfɪrə / (AmE), / rəˈtɪbɪdə kəˌlʌmɪˈfɪərə / (BrE)
A perennial North American wildflower with a distinctive tall central cone and drooping yellow or reddish petals, commonly called Mexican hat or prairie coneflower.
a wildflower with drooping petals and tall cone

Examples

The bright yellow flowers of the Ratibida columnifera waved gently in the prairie wind.
Gardeners often choose Ratibida columnifera for its hardiness and unique appearance.
Pollinators, such as bees and butterflies, are attracted to the Ratibida columnifera during the summer months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mexican hat - common name for Ratibida columnifera, a wildflower with a tall cone

Etymology

From Latin: 'columna' (column) and 'ferre' (to bear), referring to its column-like central disk.
