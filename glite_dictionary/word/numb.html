numb

1. ADJ

/ nʌm / (AmE), / nʌm / (BrE)
(of a person) so frightened, shocked, or overwhelmed that they are unable to move or respond; stunned or paralyzed with fear or emotion.
so shocked or afraid as to be unable to move

Examples

He was numb with terror as the shadow approached.
The terrible news left her numb and unable to speak.
I felt so numb with fear that I could not move a muscle.

Synset Examples

too numb with fear to move

Related Terms

  • Synonym: petrified - so frightened that one is unable to move; paralyzed with fear
  • Synonym: stunned - so shocked or surprised as to be temporarily unable to react
  • Antonym: alert - fully aware and able to respond quickly

Etymology

From Middle English 'nom', from Old English 'numen', past participle of 'niman' (to take). Original meaning referred to being taken or deprived of feeling.

2. ADJ

/ nʌm / (AmE), / nʌm / (BrE)
not showing feeling, emotion, or sensitivity; unresponsive or indifferent, especially to emotional experiences or suffering.
emotionally unresponsive or indifferent

Examples

After years of stressful work, she became numb to criticism from her boss.
He was so overwhelmed by tragedy that he felt numb to all emotion.
Many people become numb to reports of violence in the news.

Synset Examples

passersby were dead to our plea for help
numb to the cries for mercy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insensitive - not showing concern for others' feelings
  • Synonym: callous - showing or having an insensitive and cruel disregard for others
  • Antonym: empathetic - showing an ability to understand and share the feelings of others

Etymology

From Middle English 'nom', past participle of 'nimen' (to take), later influenced by 'dumb'.

3. VERB

/ nʌm / (AmE), / nʌm / (BrE)
to make a part of the body or the mind unable to feel sensation or emotion, typically due to shock, cold, or a drug
make unable to feel; deaden

Examples

The dentist injected a local anesthetic to numb the area before starting the procedure.
Years of disappointment had numbed his enthusiasm for the sport.
The icy wind quickly numbed my fingers as I waited for the bus.

Synset Examples

The shock numbed her senses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deaden - to make less sensitive or less intense; to dull
  • Antonym: to stimulate - to make more sensitive or responsive

Etymology

From Middle English *numme*, from Old English *numen*, past participle of *niman* (to take), originally meaning 'taken, seized'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

4. ADJ

/ nʌm / (AmE), / nʌm / (BrE)
deprived of the power of sensation; unable to feel, especially as a result of cold, shock, or anesthesia.
unable to feel; lacking sensation

Examples

After sitting in the snow, my hands became numb and difficult to move.
Her cheeks were numb from the cold wind blowing against her face.
The dentist used an anesthetic that left my mouth completely numb for hours.

Synset Examples

my foot is asleep
numb with cold

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insensible - unable to feel something physically; lacking physical sensation
  • Antonym: sensitive - quick to detect or respond to slight changes, signals, or influences

Etymology

From Middle English *nome*, past participle of *nim* (to take, seize); influenced by dumb.
