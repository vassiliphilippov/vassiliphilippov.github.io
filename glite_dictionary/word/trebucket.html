trebucket

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛbəkɪt / (AmE), / ˈtrɛb(j)ʊkɪt / (BrE)
a medieval catapult that uses a swinging arm to hurl a projectile
medieval catapult with swinging arm

Examples

The army constructed a massive trebucket to launch stones over the castle walls.
Historians debate the effectiveness of the trebucket compared to other siege weapons.
A replica trebucket was built for the museum exhibit and demonstrated for visitors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trebuchet - a medieval siege engine with a counterweight, used to hurl large stones
  • Synonym: catapult - a device for hurling objects

Etymology

Variant of 'trebuchet', from Old French *trebucher* (to overthrow, topple).
