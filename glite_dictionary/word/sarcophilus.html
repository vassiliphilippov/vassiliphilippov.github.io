Sarcophilus

1. NOUN

/ sɑːrˈkɑːfɪləs / (AmE), / sɑːˈkɒfɪləs / (BrE)
a genus of carnivorous marsupials, of which the Tasmanian devil is the only living member
genus including the Tasmanian devil

Examples

The genus Sarcophilus contains only one living species, the Tasmanian devil.
Researchers studied the evolution of Sarcophilus and its extinct relatives.
Fossils attributed to Sarcophilus have been discovered at several Australian sites.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tasmanian devil - a carnivorous marsupial native to Tasmania, species Sarcophilus harrisii

Etymology

From Greek sarkos (flesh) + philos (loving), meaning 'flesh-loving'.
