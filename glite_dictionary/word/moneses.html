Moneses

1. NOUN

/ məˈniːsiːz / (AmE), / məˈniːsiːz / (BrE)
A small genus of flowering plants in the heath family, commonly known as one-flowered wintergreen.
a genus of flowering plants (one-flowered wintergreen)

Examples

Moneses is often found in cool, moist woodland environments across the Northern Hemisphere.
The delicate white flower of Moneses makes it easy to distinguish from other wintergreens.
Some botanists include Moneses within the broader genus Pyrola.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pyrola - a related genus of flowering plants sometimes including Moneses

Etymology

From Greek 'monēsēs', meaning 'solitary, single' (referring to the plant's single flower).
