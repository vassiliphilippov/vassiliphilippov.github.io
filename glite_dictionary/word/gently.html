gently

1. ADV

/ ˈdʒɛntli / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛntli / (BrE)
In a manner that is soft, light, or with little force or pressure.
in a soft or light manner

Examples

She stroked the cat gently so it wouldn't get startled.
The doctor gently pressed on the injured area to check for pain.
He closed the door gently behind him, trying not to wake anyone.

Synset Examples

she kissed him lightly on the forehead

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lightly - in a way that uses little force or pressure
  • Antonym: firmly - with strength or steady force

Etymology

From Middle English gentil, from Old French, from Latin gentilis (of a clan, of noble birth).

2. ADV

/ ˈdʒɛn.tli / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛn.tli / (BrE)
in a gradual or moderate way, without sudden changes or steepness
in a gradual or moderate way

Examples

The hill rises gently from the river bank, making it easy to walk up.
The path descends gently through the forest, curving softly between the trees.
The road gently slopes upward, so cyclists can ascend without much effort.

Synset Examples

a gently sloping terrain

Related Terms

  • Antonym: steeply - at a sharp or abrupt angle; suddenly or sharply

Etymology

From gentle + -ly. Gentle from Old French gentil (kind, mild).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ ˈdʒɛntli / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛntli / (BrE)
In a way that is mild, kind, or not harsh or forceful.
in a mild or kind manner

Examples

She gently closed the door so as not to wake the baby.
The doctor spoke gently to reassure the frightened child.
He brushed her hair gently, careful not to pull too hard.

Synset Examples

he talked gently to the injured animal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: softly - in a gentle or quiet manner
  • Antonym: harshly - in a severe or rough manner

Etymology

From Middle English gentil (gentle) + -ly.
