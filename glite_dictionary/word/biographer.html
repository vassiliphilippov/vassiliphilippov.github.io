biographer

1. NOUN

/ baɪˈɑːɡrəfər / (AmE), / baɪˈɒɡrəfə / (BrE)
a person who writes a biography of someone, describing their life story and achievements
someone who writes another person's life story

Examples

The famous biographer spent years researching the life of the Nobel laureate.
A skilled biographer brings their subject to life with vivid details and careful analysis.
As a biographer, she interviewed dozens of people who knew her subject personally.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: autobiographer - a person who writes his or her own life story
  • Antonym: subject - the person whose life is described in a biography

Etymology

From Greek 'bios' (life) + 'graphein' (to write), via French 'biographe'.
