globule

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlɑb.jul / (AmE), / ˈɡlɒb.juːl / (BrE)
a small round mass or drop, especially of liquid
small round mass or drop

Examples

A single globule of mercury rolled slowly across the table.
The soup contained tiny globules of fat floating on its surface.
A globule of dew sparkled in the morning sunlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drop - a small quantity of liquid shaped like a sphere
  • Synonym: sphere - an object in the shape of a round ball

Etymology

From French 'globule', from Latin 'globulus', a diminutive of 'globus' (sphere, ball).
