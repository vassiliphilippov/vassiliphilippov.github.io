Centropus sinensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛnˈtroʊpəs sɪˈnɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˌsɛnˈtrəʊpʊs sɪˈnɛnsɪs / (BrE)
The scientific name for the greater coucal, a large, crow-like bird native to India and China.
scientific name for the greater coucal bird

Examples

The Centropus sinensis is often seen gliding through rice fields in India.
Birdwatchers were thrilled to spot a Centropus sinensis during their trip to southern China.
The deep call of the Centropus sinensis is easily recognized in the early morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greater coucal - large, crow-like bird found in Asia

Etymology

From Greek *kentron* (spur) and *pous* (foot), and Latin *sinensis* (Chinese).
