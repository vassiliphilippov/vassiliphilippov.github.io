Sesbania grandiflora

1. NOUN

/ sɛzˈbeɪni.ə ˌgræn.dɪˈflɔːrə / (AmE), / sɛzˈbeɪni.ə ˌgræn.dɪˈflɔː.rə / (BrE)
A tropical softwood tree with loose clusters of usually red or pink flowers, native to Australia and Asia and widely cultivated elsewhere.
species of tropical softwood tree

Examples

Farmers in Southeast Asia often grow Sesbania grandiflora both for its edible flowers and as animal fodder.
The showy, pendulous blooms of Sesbania grandiflora add vibrant color to tropical gardens.
In southern Florida, Sesbania grandiflora has become a common ornamental tree.

Etymology

From the genus name Sesbania, combined with Latin 'grandiflora' (large-flowered).
