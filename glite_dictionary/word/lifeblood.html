lifeblood

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪfˌblʌd / (AmE), / ˈlaɪfˌblʌd / (BrE)
the blood regarded as the source or seat of life and vitality in a living being
blood as the source of life

Examples

Many ancient cultures believed that lifeblood held a person's spirit and strength.
The vampire legend revolves around the consumption of human lifeblood.
Her injury was severe, and her lifeblood was draining away with every passing moment.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪfˌblʌd / (AmE), / ˈlaɪfˌblʌd / (BrE)
the thing that is most important for the continued success or existence of something
essential or vital force

Examples

Agriculture has always been the lifeblood of the region's economy.
Innovation is considered the lifeblood of the tech industry.
Tourism serves as the lifeblood for many small island communities.

Synset Examples

water is the lifeblood of India

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mainstay - a chief support or important element
  • Synonym: backbone - a central or important support
  • Synonym: essence - the most important quality or aspect of something

Etymology

Formed from 'life' + 'blood', referring to blood as vital for life.
