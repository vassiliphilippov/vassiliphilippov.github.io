ziggurat

1. NOUN

/ ˈzɪɡəˌræt / (AmE), / ˈzɪɡərat / (BrE)
a rectangular stepped temple tower or terraced structure, characteristic of ancient Assyrian and Babylonian architecture
rectangular stepped temple tower (ancient Mesopotamia)

Examples

The ancient city was dominated by a massive ziggurat rising above the flat landscape.
Archaeologists uncovered the ruins of a ziggurat dating back over four thousand years.
The ziggurat served as a religious center for the Babylonian people.

Etymology

From Akkadian *ziqqurratu* (pinnacle, temple tower).

2. NOUN

/ ˈzɪɡəˌræt / (AmE), / ˈzɪɡəræt / (BrE)
a rectangular, tiered temple or terraced mound built in ancient Mesopotamia by the Assyrians and Babylonians.
ancient tiered Mesopotamian temple

Examples

The ruins of a massive ziggurat still dominate the landscape of ancient Ur.
Historians believe each ziggurat served as a religious and administrative center.
The artists reconstructed what the great Babylonian ziggurat might have looked like.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pyramid - a monumental structure with a square or triangular base and sloping sides that meet in a point at the top, especially Egypt

Etymology

From Akkadian 'ziqqurratu' (temple tower).
