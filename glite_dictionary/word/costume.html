costume

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːstuːm / (AmE), / ˈkɒstjuːm / (BrE)
A set of clothes worn by an actor or participant to represent a particular character or theme in a play, performance, or at a fancy dress event.
special attire for plays or parties

Examples

She designed a remarkable costume for the school's Shakespeare production.
He wore a pirate costume to the Halloween party last year.
Her Cinderella costume won the competition at the fancy dress ball.

Synset Examples

he won the prize for best costume

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outfit - a set of clothes worn together, especially for a particular occasion

Etymology

From French 'costume', from Italian 'costume' (custom, fashion, dress).

/ ˈkɑːstuːm / (AmE), / ˈkɒstjuːm / (BrE)
a set of clothes worn to look like someone or something else, or clothes that are typical of a particular period, place, or activity and not part of everyday dress

Examples

The actor changed into his superhero costume backstage.
Children often dress up in fancy costumes for Halloween.
