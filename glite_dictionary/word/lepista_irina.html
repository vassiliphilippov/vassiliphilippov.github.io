Lepista irina

1. NOUN

/ ləˈpɪstə ɪˈriːnə / (AmE), / lɛˈpɪstə ɪˈriːnə / (BrE)
a species of edible mushroom with large silky white caps and thick stalks, belonging to the genus Lepista
species of edible mushroom

Examples

We found several Lepista irina growing in the forest after the rain.
The thick stalks of Lepista irina make it easy to identify among other wild mushrooms.
Many foragers prize Lepista irina for its delicate flavor and silky texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mushroom - a fleshy spore-bearing fruiting body of a fungus, typically eaten as food

Etymology

From modern Latin Lepista (genus name) and specific epithet irina.
