subatomic

1. ADJ

/ sʌbəˈtɑmɪk / (AmE), / sʌbəˈtɒmɪk / (BrE)
relating to the parts of an atom or the forces operating within an atom
relating to parts or forces inside an atom

Examples

The scientists studied subatomic particles to better understand the composition of matter.
Advances in subatomic physics have led to many technological innovations.
Researchers use powerful accelerators to observe subatomic interactions.

Synset Examples

subatomic particles
harnessing subatomic energy

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: atomic - relating to atoms; derived from or using atoms
  • Synonym: elementary - relating to the basic or fundamental parts

Etymology

From Latin 'sub-' (under, below) + 'atomic', referring to below or inside the atom.

2. ADJ

/ sʌb.əˈtɑː.mɪk / (AmE), / sʌb.əˈtɒm.ɪk / (BrE)
smaller than or occurring within an atom; relating to particles or phenomena at a scale smaller than atoms.
smaller than or within an atom

Examples

The discovery of subatomic particles revolutionized our understanding of matter.
Scientists use powerful accelerators to study subatomic phenomena.
Electrons, protons, and neutrons are common examples of subatomic particles.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: atomic - relating to atoms, the smallest units of matter

Etymology

Formed from Latin prefix 'sub-' (under, below) and 'atomic' (relating to atoms).
