Grifola frondosa

1. NOUN

/ ɡrɪˈfoʊlə frɒnˈdoʊsə / (AmE), / ɡrɪˈfəʊlə frɒnˈdəʊsə / (BrE)
A large, greyish-brown edible fungus with overlapping caps, resembling a hen, found at the base of trees; also known as 'hen of the woods' or 'maitake'.
an edible mushroom species

Examples

Chefs often use Grifola frondosa in gourmet dishes for its rich, earthy flavor.
While hiking, we found a huge cluster of Grifola frondosa at the base of an old oak tree.
Many health enthusiasts value Grifola frondosa for its potential medicinal properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hen of the woods - common name for Grifola frondosa mushroom
  • Synonym: maitake - Japanese name for Grifola frondosa

Etymology

From New Latin: 'Grifola,' a genus of fungi, + 'frondosa,' Latin for 'leafy,' referring to the leaf-like appearance of its caps.
