sieve

1. VERB

/ sɪv / (AmE), / sɪv / (BrE)
to examine something carefully in order to decide if it is suitable, especially by separating out unwanted items
to examine for suitability

Examples

The council will sieve all the proposals to find the most feasible ones.
Researchers sieved the data to identify relevant patterns for their study.
The committee is sieving applications to shortlist candidates for the scholarship.

Synset Examples

screen these samples
screen the job applicants

Related Terms

  • Synonym: screen - to examine in order to test suitability

Etymology

From Old English *sife* (sieve), related to Dutch *zeef* and German *Sieb*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02405520-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ sɪv / (AmE), / sɪv / (BrE)
to separate or strain a substance by passing it through a sieve to remove larger particles or lumps
strain or separate through a sieve

Examples

Carefully sieve the flour into the bowl to remove any lumps.
She sieved the soup before serving to make it perfectly smooth.
After gathering the soil sample, he sieved it to separate the fine grains from the rocks.

Synset Examples

sift the flour

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sift - to put a fine substance through a sieve to remove lumps or larger pieces

Etymology

From Old English *sife*, ultimately from Proto-Germanic *sibja* (sieve).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01462658-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ sɪv / (AmE), / sɪv / (BrE)
a device with a mesh or perforated bottom used for separating fine particles from coarser ones or for straining liquids from solids
mesh device for separating particles

Examples

She used a sieve to remove lumps from the flour before baking.
The laboratory technician passed the sand through a sieve to grade the particles.
After washing the pasta, she shook it in a fine sieve to drain the water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strainer - a device for separating liquid from solid food or for sifting dry ingredients

Etymology

From Old French 'sive', from Latin 'saepa' (a hedge or enclosure).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04223701-n
Synset POS: n

separate out by examining

Examples

Researchers must sieve the relevant data from this enormous dataset before analysis.
He sieves information from many sources to ensure accuracy in his reports.
