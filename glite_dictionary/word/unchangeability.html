unchangeability

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌnˌtʃeɪndʒəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌʌnˌtʃeɪndʒəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
The quality of being unchangeable; the tendency to remain unchanged.
quality of being unchangeable

Examples

The unchangeability of the law frustrated those who wanted reform.
Some philosophers ponder the unchangeability of human nature.
The company's policy is marked by its stubborn unchangeability despite changing markets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immutability - the quality of not changing; unchangeableness
  • Antonym: changeability - the quality of being changeable; ability to change

Etymology

From unchangeable + -ity, modeled after similar Latinate formations.
