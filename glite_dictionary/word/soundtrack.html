soundtrack

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaʊnˌtræk / (AmE), / ˈsaʊnˌtræk / (BrE)
the recorded music, speech, and sound effects that accompany a film, or the narrow strip on a film where this sound is stored
recorded music and sound for a film

Examples

The original soundtrack of the movie was restored for the new release.
A separate soundtrack accompanies each version of the film, including translated dialogues.
Film historians studied the soundtrack to analyze the use of sound effects in early cinema.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: score - music written specifically for a film or play

Etymology

Formed from 'sound' + 'track', referring to the strip on film where sound is recorded.
