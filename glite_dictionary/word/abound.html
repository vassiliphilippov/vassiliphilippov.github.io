to abound

1. VERB

/ əˈbaʊnd / (AmE), / əˈbaʊnd / (BrE)
to exist in large numbers or amounts; to be abundant or plentiful
exist in large quantities

Examples

Wildflowers abound in the valley during springtime, covering the hillsides in vibrant colors.
Rumors abound about the new CEO's plans for the company.
Opportunities to learn and grow abound at the university.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overflow - to be so full that contents spill over; synonym for 'abound' in the sense of abundance
  • Antonym: scarce - insufficient in quantity; antonym to 'abound'

Etymology

From Latin abundare (to overflow), from ab- (away) + undare (to flow), from unda (wave).

2. VERB

/ əˈbaʊnd / (AmE), / əˈbaʊnd / (BrE)
(of a place or situation) to be filled with a large number or amount of something; to be abundant
be plentiful, be filled with

Examples

During the summer, the fields abound with wildflowers of every color.
Rumors abounded after the unexpected resignation of the CEO.
Mysteries and legends abound in this ancient city, drawing curious tourists from around the world.

Synset Examples

The room abounded with screaming children
The garden bristled with toddlers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overflow - to be so full that the contents go over the edges
  • Synonym: to teem - to be full of or swarming with
  • Antonym: lack - to not have enough of something

Etymology

From Latin 'abundare' (to overflow).
