Satureja acinos

1. NOUN

/ sæ.tʃʊˈriː.ə əˈsaɪ.nəs / (AmE), / sætjʊˈriː.ə əˈsaɪ.nɒs / (BrE)
A fragrant European mint with clusters of small violet-and-white flowers, naturalized in parts of North America.
European mint with violet-and-white flowers

Examples

The hillside was dotted with the delicate blooms of Satureja acinos among the wild grasses.
Gardeners often plant Satureja acinos for its aromatic foliage and attractive flowers.
Botanists identified the specimen as Satureja acinos during their survey of the region.

Etymology

From Latin 'Satureja', the mint genus, and 'acinos', the species name from Greek 'akinos' (basil thyme).
