Gliridae

1. NOUN

/ ɡlɪˈrɪdiː / (AmE), / ɡlɪˈrɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of small rodents including dormice and other similar Old World forms
family of rodents: dormice

Examples

The Gliridae are known for their hibernation habits and long periods of inactivity.
Members of the Gliridae family are found throughout Europe, Asia, and Africa.
Unlike other rodents, Gliridae species typically have bushy tails and large eyes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dormouse - a small, nocturnal rodent found in Europe and Africa

Etymology

From New Latin Gliridae, from Glis (genus of dormouse) + -idae (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡlaɪrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈɡlɪərɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small rodents known as dormice, native to the Old World, noted for their long periods of hibernation.
the dormouse family of rodents

Examples

The Gliridae are distinguished by their large eyes and bushy tails.
Several species within the Gliridae family are known for hibernating for months at a time.
Researchers studied the distribution of Gliridae across Europe and Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dormouse - a small nocturnal rodent with a bushy tail

Etymology

From Latin *Gliris* (dormouse), plus the taxonomic suffix -idae indicating a family.
