Bauhaus

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaʊˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈbaʊ.haʊs / (BrE)
A German school and style of architecture, design, and art developed in the early 20th century, known for its emphasis on functionalism, simplicity, and integration of art and technology.
German architectural and design style (1919–33)

Examples

The Bauhaus movement revolutionized modern design in the early 20th century.
Many contemporary buildings incorporate elements of Bauhaus architecture in their design.
She studied the history of the Bauhaus and its influence on modern art.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: International Style - an architectural style influenced by Bauhaus principles

Etymology

From German 'Bauhaus' (building house), the name of a school of design founded by Walter Gropius in 1919.
