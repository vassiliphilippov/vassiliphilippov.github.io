wasteland

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪstˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈweɪstˌlænd / (BrE)
A large area of land that is barren or uncultivated and considered unsuitable for habitation or agriculture.
barren, uncultivated land

Examples

The abandoned factory stood in the middle of a vast wasteland outside the city.
After the fire, the once-green valley was reduced to a blackened wasteland.
The region remained a wasteland for decades, with no signs of life or cultivation.

Synset Examples

the barrens of central Africa
the trackless wastes of the desert

Related Terms

  • Synonym: desert - a dry, barren area of land, typically covered with sand
  • Synonym: wilderness - an uncultivated, uninhabited, and inhospitable region

Etymology

Formed from 'waste' (from Old French 'waste', Latin 'vastare' meaning to lay waste) + 'land'.
