gooseberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡusˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈɡuːzbəri / (BrE)
A small, round, edible berry with a tart flavor, or the spiny shrub that bears these fruits, native to Europe and parts of Asia.
a tart, round berry or its shrub

Examples

She picked a handful of gooseberries from the bush and added them to the pie.
The tart flavor of gooseberry makes it a favorite for jams and desserts.
They planted a gooseberry bush in their garden for its attractive fruit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: currant - type of small, edible berry related to the gooseberry

Etymology

From Middle English 'goosberry', probably influenced by the word 'goose' + 'berry', though the exact origin is uncertain.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡuːsˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈɡʊzb(ə)ri / (BrE)
a small, round, tart fruit, typically green or red, used chiefly in jams, jellies, and desserts; the plant that produces this berry
a tart, round berry used in food

Examples

The tart flavor of gooseberry makes it ideal for homemade jams and preserves.
She picked a handful of ripe gooseberries from the bush in her garden.
Gooseberry pie is a traditional dessert in many parts of England.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: currant - small, tart berry often used in cooking and baking

Etymology

From Middle English 'gosbery', of uncertain origin; possibly from 'goose' + 'berry', though no direct connection to geese.
