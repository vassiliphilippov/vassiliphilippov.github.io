eyesore

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ.sɔr / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.sɔː / (BrE)
an object, building, or scene that is extremely unattractive or offensive to look at
something very ugly to look at

Examples

That abandoned building has become a real eyesore in the neighborhood.
Many residents complained that the billboard was an eyesore and should be removed.
The new sculpture stands out as an eyesore among the historic architecture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blot - a disfiguring or unsightly mark or object
  • Antonym: beauty - an object or quality that is attractive or pleasing to look at

Etymology

From eye + sore, originally in Old English referring to something that hurts to look at.

2. NOUN

/ ˈaɪˌsɔr / (AmE), / ˈaɪˌsɔː / (BrE)
A person or thing that is very ugly or unpleasant to look at.
something ugly or unpleasant to see

Examples

The abandoned factory quickly became an eyesore in the town center.
Many residents complained that the new billboard was an eyesore along the highway.
The graffiti-covered wall is considered an eyesore by the local community.

Synset Examples

The derelict building was an eyesore in the neighborhood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blight - something that spoils or damages a place
  • Antonym: beauty - something beautiful or pleasing to look at

Etymology

From eye + sore, earliest use in the 16th century.
