Circumcision

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɜrkəmˈsɪʒən / (AmE), / ˌsɜːkəmˈsɪʒən / (BrE)
(in the Roman Catholic and Anglican Churches) A feast day celebrating the circumcision of Jesus, observed on January 1st.
Christian feast day celebrated on January 1st

Examples

Many churches hold special services on Circumcision to commemorate the life of Jesus.
The Feast of the Circumcision was historically observed with great solemnity in medieval Europe.
In the Anglican tradition, Circumcision is celebrated on the first day of the new year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Feast of the Circumcision - the Christian feast day marking the circumcision of Jesus

Etymology

From Latin 'circumcisio' (cutting around), from 'circumcidere'.
