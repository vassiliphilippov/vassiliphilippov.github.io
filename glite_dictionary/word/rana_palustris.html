Rana palustris

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪ.nə pəˈlʌs.trɪs / (AmE), / ˈrɑː.nə pəˈlʌs.trɪs / (BrE)
A species of meadow frog native to eastern North America, also known as the pickerel frog.
species of North American meadow frog

Examples

The Rana palustris is often found in wet meadows and streams throughout eastern North America.
Researchers studied the habitat of Rana palustris to better understand its ecological needs.
Unlike other frogs, the Rana palustris can secrete toxins that deter predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pickerel frog - another common name for Rana palustris

Etymology

From Latin: 'rana' meaning 'frog' and 'palustris' meaning 'of the marsh'.
