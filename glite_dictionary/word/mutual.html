mutual

1. ADJ

/ ˈmjutʃuəl / (AmE), / ˈmjuːtʃuəl / (BrE)
(of a feeling or action) experienced or done by each of two or more parties toward the other or others; shared in common.
shared by two or more parties

Examples

They have a mutual respect that makes their collaboration successful.
Trust is essential for any mutual agreement between companies.
After years of competition, the firms reached a mutual understanding.

Synset Examples

reciprocal aid
reciprocal trade
mutual respect
reciprocal privileges at other clubs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reciprocal - given, felt, or done in return; mutual

Etymology

From Latin mutuus (borrowed, lent, mutual), via Old French mutuel.

2. ADJ

/ ˈmju.tʃu.əl / (AmE), / ˈmjuː.tʃu.əl / (BrE)
(of a feeling or action) experienced or done by each of two or more parties towards the other or others; shared in common.
shared by two or more parties

Examples

They developed a mutual understanding after several discussions.
The agreement was based on mutual respect and trust.
Both companies have mutual interests in pursuing this partnership.

Synset Examples

a common friend
the mutual interests of management and labor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reciprocal - given or done in return; having the same relation each to the other
  • Antonym: individual - relating to a single person or thing rather than to a group

Etymology

From Latin 'mutuus' meaning 'borrowed, exchanged, reciprocal'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02159888-s
Synset POS: s
