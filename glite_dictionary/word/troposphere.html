troposphere

1. NOUN

/ ˈtroʊ.pəˌsfɪr / (AmE), / ˈtrɒp.əˌsfɪə / (BrE)
the lowest layer of Earth's atmosphere, extending from the surface up to about 4 to 11 miles (6 to 17 km) high, where most weather phenomena occur
lowest atmospheric layer of Earth

Examples

Most of the Earth's weather events occur within the troposphere.
As you climb higher in the mountains, you are still within the troposphere, although the air becomes thinner.
The troposphere contains almost all the atmospheric water vapor and clouds.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: stratosphere - the layer of the atmosphere above the troposphere
  • Degree Higher: atmosphere - the envelope of gases surrounding the Earth or another planet

Etymology

From French 'troposphère', from Greek 'tropos' (turn, change) + 'sphaira' (sphere).
