Samolus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæmələs / (AmE), / ˈsamələs / (BrE)
a genus of herbs, commonly known as water pimpernels, typically found in salt marshes and wet habitats
genus of water pimpernel herbs

Examples

Samolus species thrive in wet, saline soils near coastal marshes.
Botanists identified the plant as a member of the Samolus genus due to its small pink flowers.
Many wetlands conservationists track the presence of Samolus to assess habitat health.

Etymology

From Latin *samolus*, an ancient plant name.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsæmələs / (AmE), / ˈsæmələs / (BrE)
A genus of herbaceous plants that typically grow in salt marshes, commonly known as water pimpernels.
genus of marsh herbs; water pimpernel

Examples

The marsh was dotted with delicate Samolus plants along its edges.
Botanists were excited to find a rare species within the Samolus genus at the reserve.
Many coastal wetlands provide a perfect habitat for Samolus to thrive.

Etymology

From Latin Samolus, the classical name for the plant.
