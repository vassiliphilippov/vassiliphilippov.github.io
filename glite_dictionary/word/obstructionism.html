obstructionism

1. NOUN

/ əbˈstrʌkʃənˌɪzəm / (AmE), / əbˈstrʌkʃənɪzəm / (BrE)
The practice of deliberately delaying or preventing a process or change, especially in politics or organizations.
deliberate interference or hindrance

Examples

The senator was criticized for his repeated obstructionism during the legislative session.
Her obstructionism slowed the reform process to a standstill.
Some accused the committee of obstructionism instead of working towards a solution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstructionist - a person who practices obstructionism

Etymology

From 'obstruction' + '-ism', modeled after other '-ism' nouns indicating ideologies or practices.
