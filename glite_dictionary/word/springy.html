springy

1. ADJ

/ ˈsprɪŋ.i / (AmE), / ˈsprɪŋ.i / (BrE)
(of movements) light and buoyantly active; showing energy and confidence in the way one moves.
light and confidently active (of movements)

Examples

She walked with a springy step, full of energy and purpose.
The gymnast's springy movements impressed the judges.
After a month off, his run looked much less springy than before.

Synset Examples

he walked away with a springy step

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bouncy - having a lively, energetic, or resilient quality (of movement or impact)

Etymology

From 'spring' (to leap) + -y (adjective suffix).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsprɪŋi / (AmE), / ˈsprɪŋi / (BrE)
(of a material or surface) able to return quickly to its original shape after being pressed or stretched; elastic or resilient.
elastic; easily rebounds

Examples

The mattress felt springy and comfortable to sleep on.
She walked across the springy moss in the forest.
His hair was clean and springy after using the new shampoo.

Synset Examples

clean bouncy hair
a lively tennis ball
as resilient as seasoned hickory
springy turf

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elastic - able to resume normal shape after stretching or compression
  • Antonym: rigid - not able to be bent or changed; stiff
  • Synonym: bouncy - able to bounce readily; elastic

Etymology

From 'spring' + '-y', indicating the quality of springing.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00847134-s
Synset POS: s
