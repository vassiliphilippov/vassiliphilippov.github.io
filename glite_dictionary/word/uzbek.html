Uzbek

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌzbɛk / (AmE), / ˈʌzbɛk / (BrE)
the Turkic language spoken mainly in Uzbekistan and surrounding regions
Turkic language of Uzbekistan

Examples

She is fluent in both Russian and Uzbek.
The official documents were translated into Uzbek for local residents.
He took a course to improve his Uzbek language skills.

Etymology

From Russian 'Uzbek', ultimately of Turkic origin

2. NOUN

/ ˈuzˌbɛk / (AmE), / ˈʊzbɛk / (BrE)
a member of a Turkic ethnic group native to Uzbekistan and surrounding regions in Central Asia
member of a Central Asian ethnic group

Examples

The city is home to a large community of Uzbeks who celebrate their cultural traditions.
An Uzbek artist won international recognition for her contemporary paintings.
He learned several Turkic languages, including Uzbek, during his travels in Central Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Uzbekistan - a country in Central Asia, homeland of the Uzbek people

Etymology

From Russian 'uzbek', from Uzbek 'ozbek'.

3. PROPN

/ ˈʊzbɛk / (AmE), / ˈʊzbɛk / (BrE)
a landlocked country in Central Asia, officially known as the Republic of Uzbekistan
country in Central Asia

Examples

The capital city of Uzbek is Tashkent, which is known for its rich history.
After traveling across Central Asia, she spent two weeks exploring the stunning landscapes of Uzbek.
Natural resources are a major contributor to the economy of Uzbek.

Etymology

From Russian 'Uzbek', from Uzbek 'Oʻzbek'.

4. NOUN

/ ˈʌzˌbɛk / (AmE), / ˈʊzbɛk / (BrE)
the Turkic language spoken chiefly in Uzbekistan and by Uzbek communities in surrounding countries
Turkic language of Uzbekistan

Examples

She is fluent in both Russian and Uzbek.
The novel was originally written in Uzbek and later translated into English.
Many signs in Tashkent are written in both Cyrillic and Latin scripts for Uzbek.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Turkish - Oghuz Turkic language of Turkey
  • Synonym: Kazakh - Turkic language of Kazakhstan

Etymology

From Uzbek (self-designation of the people and language), possibly from the name of Khan Özbeg.
