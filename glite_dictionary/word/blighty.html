Blighty

1. NOUN

/ ˈblaɪti / (AmE), / ˈblaɪti / (BrE)
(informal, mainly historical) Britain or, more generally, the United Kingdom, especially as referred to by British people abroad or during wartime.
Britain or the UK (historical, informal)
informal british

Examples

Many soldiers longed to return to Blighty after years at the front.
She sent a letter home to Blighty every month during her travels.
Some still fondly refer to the UK as Blighty in nostalgic conversation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Britain - The island containing England, Scotland, and Wales; sometimes used for the United Kingdom
  • Synonym: home - A native country; often used to mean Britain by British people abroad

Etymology

From Hindi bilāyatī (foreign, European), via British India.
