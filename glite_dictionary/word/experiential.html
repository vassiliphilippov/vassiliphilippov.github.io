experiential

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˌspɪriˈɛnʃəl / (AmE), / ɪkˌspɪəriˈɛnʃəl / (BrE)
relating to or based on experience, especially as distinguished from theory or speculation
based on personal experience

Examples

The course takes an experiential approach to learning by involving students in real-world projects.
Her experiential knowledge of the culture came from years of traveling abroad.
They designed an experiential workshop that encouraged participants to learn by doing.

Synset Examples

a personal, experiential reality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: empirical - based on observation or experiment rather than theory

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'experientialis', from Latin 'experientia' (experience).

2. ADJ

/ ɪkˌspɪriˈɛnʃəl / (AmE), / ɪkˌspɪəriˈɛnʃəl / (BrE)
Relating to or derived from experience, especially as contrasted with theoretical knowledge.
relating to experience

Examples

The workshop offers an experiential approach to learning new skills.
Her experiential knowledge of the culture made her insights unique.
Some people prefer theoretical study, while others learn best through experiential methods.

Synset Examples

the rich experiential content of the teachings of the older philosophers
formal logicians are not concerned with existential matters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: empirical - based on observation or experience rather than theory

Etymology

From late Latin *experientialis*, from *experientia* (experience).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00862819-s
Synset POS: s
