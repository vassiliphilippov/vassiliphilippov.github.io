matchwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈmætʃˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈmatʃˌwʊd / (BrE)
small pieces or splinters of wood, especially as produced by smashing or shattering something wooden
small splinters or fragments of wood

Examples

The old chair collapsed into matchwood after years of neglect.
The violent storm reduced several garden sheds to matchwood.
The craftsmen were amazed at how quickly the machine turned the branch into matchwood.

Synset Examples

it was smashed into matchwood

Etymology

From 'match' (as in wooden matchstick) + 'wood', referring to the resemblance to thin sticks used for matches.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmætʃˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈmatʃˌwʊd / (BrE)
Wood that is suitable for making matchsticks, typically chosen for its ability to ignite easily and burn evenly.
wood suitable for matchsticks

Examples

The factory imported large quantities of matchwood from Scandinavia for its production lines.
Pine is often used as matchwood because it lights quickly and burns cleanly.
The remains of the burned cabin were reduced to splinters and matchwood.

Etymology

From 'match' (a strip of combustible material) + 'wood', referring to wood used for matches.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈmætʃˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈmatʃwʊd / (BrE)
wood broken or splintered into very small pieces, like that used for making matches
wood in small splinters or fragments

Examples

The boat smashed against the rocks and was reduced to matchwood within minutes.
After the hurricane, fences were left as nothing but matchwood.
The carpenter swept up the piles of matchwood from his workshop floor.

Synset Examples

the vessel was beaten to matchwood on the rocks

Etymology

Formed from 'match' (as in a small stick for lighting fire) and 'wood'.
