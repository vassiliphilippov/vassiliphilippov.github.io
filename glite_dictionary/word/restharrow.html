restharrow

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛsˌhæroʊ / (AmE), / ˈrɛsthærəʊ / (BrE)
a European woody plant of the genus Ononis, with pink flowers, unifoliate leaves, and long tough roots, often spreading by underground runners
European woody plant with pink flowers and tough roots

Examples

The farmer noticed a patch of restharrow growing along the edge of his field.
Gardeners value restharrow for its ability to prevent soil erosion.
The tough roots of restharrow once made it difficult to plow fields in rural Europe.

Etymology

From the practice of its tough roots stopping or 'arresting' the harrow (a farming implement), hence 'rest-harrow'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛstˌhæroʊ / (AmE), / ˈrɛstˌharəʊ / (BrE)
a Eurasian plant with spiny stems, tough roots, and loose clusters of pink or purple flowers, typically found in grassland or sandy soils
Eurasian plant with spiny stems and pink flowers

Examples

The roots of the restharrow are so tough that they can halt even the strongest plough.
Gardeners sometimes struggle to remove restharrow from sandy soil because of its deep roots.
The fields were dotted with clusters of pink restharrow flowers in late spring.

Etymology

From the plant's tough roots, which can 'arrest' (stop) the progress of a harrow; from Middle English 'rest' + 'harrow' (farming implement).
