authorisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔːθəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɔːθəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
official permission or approval to do something
official permission or approval

Examples

We received authorisation to access the restricted area.
Without proper authorisation, entry is strictly prohibited.
The manager must grant authorisation before you proceed with the purchase.

Synset Examples

authority for the program was renewed several times

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authorization - official permission or approval (US spelling)
  • Synonym: permit - an official document giving someone permission to do something
  • Antonym: prohibition - the action of forbidding something by law or authority

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'auctorisatio', from 'auctorizare' (to authorize), from 'auctor' (author, creator).

4. NOUN

/ ˌɔːθərəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɔːθəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the official act of giving permission or approval for something, often by a person or authority with the power to do so
official permission or approval
british

Examples

The manager's authorisation is required before you can access those files.
All transactions must have prior authorisation from the finance department.
She received formal authorisation to proceed with the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: authorization - official permission or approval (American spelling)
  • Synonym: approval - the act of agreeing to something or accepting it as satisfactory
  • Antonym: prohibition - the act of forbidding or not allowing something

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *auctorisatio*, from Latin *auctor* (author, originator).
