lobed

1. ADJ

/ loʊbd / (AmE), / ləʊbd / (BrE)
having margins that are deeply indented, forming rounded or pointed projections (lobes) that are not completely separate from each other.
having indented, projecting lobes

Examples

The maple leaf is easily recognized by its lobed shape.
Some fungi have lobed caps that resemble flower petals.
The liver is a lobed organ, divided into distinct sections.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unlobed - without lobes; having simple, unindented margins

Etymology

From 'lobe' + '-ed' suffix forming adjectives.
