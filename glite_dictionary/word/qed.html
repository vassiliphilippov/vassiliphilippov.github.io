QED

1. NOUN

/ ˌkjuː iː ˈdiː / (AmE), / ˌkjuː iː ˈdiː / (BrE)
a relativistic quantum theory that describes the electromagnetic interactions between photons, electrons, and muons
theory of electromagnetic interactions in quantum physics
medical

Examples

QED provides the framework scientists use to explain how light and matter interact on a quantum level.
Richard Feynman received a Nobel Prize for his contributions to QED in the 1960s.
Understanding QED is essential for advanced studies in particle physics and electrodynamics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quantum mechanics - branch of physics dealing with physical phenomena at nanoscopic scales
  • Degree Lower: electromagnetism - the physics of electric and magnetic fields

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'quantum electrodynamics', from quantum (Latin: quantity) and electrodynamics (from electro- and dynamics).
