Medicago falcata

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛdɪˈɡɑːkoʊ fælˈkeɪtə / (AmE), / ˌmɛdɪˈɡɑːkəʊ falˈkeɪtə / (BrE)
A European species of medic, naturalized in North America, distinguished by its yellow flowers and sickle-shaped seed pods.
a plant with yellow flowers and sickle-shaped pods

Examples

Medicago falcata is often found growing on roadsides and dry grasslands in Europe and North America.
The bright yellow flowers of Medicago falcata appear in mid-summer and attract numerous pollinators.
Botanists can distinguish Medicago falcata by its unique sickle-shaped seed pods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alfalfa - a related plant species, Medicago sativa, commonly cultivated as forage

Etymology

From Latin 'Medicago' (the genus, originally referring to a plant from Media) and Latin 'falcata' (sickle-shaped).
