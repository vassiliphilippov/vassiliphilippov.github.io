Thymus

1. NOUN

/ ˈθaɪ.məs / (AmE), / ˈtʌɪ.məs / (BrE)
A genus of aromatic plants in the mint family, including various species of thyme.
genus of aromatic mint plants (thyme)

Examples

The Thymus genus comprises several aromatic herbs used in cooking and medicine.
Botanists have identified over 350 species within the Thymus genus.
Gardeners often grow plants of the Thymus genus for their fragrant leaves and flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thyme - a common aromatic herb from the Thymus genus

Etymology

From Latin 'thymus', from Ancient Greek 'thýmos' (thyme).
