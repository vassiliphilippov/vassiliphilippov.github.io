to epitomize

1. VERB

/ ɪˈpɪtəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ɪˈpɪtəmaɪz / (BrE)
to be a perfect example of something; to embody the main characteristics of a particular quality or type
be a perfect example of

Examples

Her generosity truly epitomizes the spirit of the holiday season.
These paintings epitomize the style of the impressionist era.
He epitomized bravery during his years of service.

Synset Examples

The fugue typifies Bach's style of composition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to embody - to represent or express something in a visible or tangible way
  • Synonym: to exemplify - to be a typical example of something

Etymology

From Greek 'epitomēs' (abridgment) via Late Latin 'epitomizare'.
