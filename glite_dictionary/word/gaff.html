gaff

1. NOUN

/ ɡæf / (AmE), / ɡaf / (BrE)
a sharp metal spike or spur attached to the leg of a fighting cock
spur fixed to a gamecock's leg

Examples

The handler fastened a gaff to the rooster before the match began.
Authorities discovered illegal gaffs at the site of the cockfight.
A sharpened gaff can cause serious injuries in gamecocks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spur - a sharp projection attached to the leg of a fighting cock or to boots

2. NOUN

/ ɡæf / (AmE), / ɡaf / (BrE)
a large iron hook with a handle, used for landing large fish while fishing
hooked tool for landing fish

Examples

The angler used a gaff to haul the giant tuna onto the boat.
You must handle the gaff carefully to avoid injuring yourself or the fish.
After the fish was close enough, he reached out with his gaff to secure the catch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hook - a curved piece of metal or other material used for catching, holding, or pulling things

Etymology

Originates from Provençal *gafa* (hook), related to Old French *garf* (hook).

3. NOUN

/ ɡæf / (AmE), / ɡaf / (BrE)
a spar rising aft from a mast to support the upper edge of a fore-and-aft sail, especially a quadrilateral one
a spar supporting the top of a sail

Examples

The crew raised the sail using the gaff and tightened the ropes.
A traditional schooner often has a gaff rig for its main sails.
The gaff creaked as the wind caught the large quadrilateral sail.

Etymology

Probably from Dutch *gaff* (hook).
