to slam-dunk

1. VERB

/ ˈslæm ˌdʌŋk / (AmE), / ˈslæm ˌdʌŋk / (BrE)
to take aggressive action against someone or something in a way that leaves little chance of resistance or competition
forcefully overpower or outdo

Examples

The new smartphone company slam-dunked its competitors with innovative technology.
During the debate, she slam-dunked her opponent's arguments with a single, well-crafted response.
Streaming services have slam-dunked traditional television in terms of popularity among young audiences.

Synset Examples

the electronic travel market is slam-dunking traditional travel agencies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to outmaneuver - gain an advantage by skillful or clever action

Etymology

From the basketball term 'slam dunk', meaning a forceful shot made by thrusting the ball down through the hoop; used figuratively since the late 20th century.

2. VERB

/ slæm dʌŋk / (AmE), / slæm dʌŋk / (BrE)
to score by forcefully putting the ball directly through the basketball hoop with one or both hands
score in basketball by dunking forcefully

Examples

Rasheed slam-dunked the ball over two defenders in the final seconds.
Every time Jessica slam-dunks, the crowd goes wild.
He dreams of slam-dunking in a real basketball game someday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dunk - to score by putting the ball directly through the hoop in basketball

Etymology

Formed from 'slam' and 'dunk', first used in American basketball in the 1970s.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01600753-v
Synset POS: v
