Catherine I

1. PROPN

/ ˌkæθərˈin ðə fɜrst / (AmE), / ˌkæθərˈiːn ðə fɜːst / (BrE)
(1684–1727), empress of Russia who succeeded her husband Peter the Great; ruled as Empress from 1725 to 1727.
empress of Russia (ruled 1725–1727)

Examples

Catherine I became empress of Russia after the death of Peter the Great.
The brief reign of Catherine I marked an important period in Russian history.
Historians credit Catherine I with maintaining Peter the Great's reforms during her rule.
