party-crashing

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑr.ti ˌkræʃ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.ti ˌkræʃ.ɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of attending a party without an invitation, often to enjoy the festivities or get free food.
attending a party uninvited
informal

Examples

He made a habit of party-crashing whenever he heard about free drinks.
We caught her party-crashing at the annual Christmas celebration.
Some people consider party-crashing rude, while others find it thrilling.

Synset Examples

We ended up party-crashing at the neighbor's house.
She was notorious for party-crashing without an invite.
He loves party-crashing just for the free food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gatecrashing - attending an event without an invitation

Etymology

From 'party' (social gathering) + 'crashing' (informal sense: attending without invitation).
