dovecote

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌvˌkoʊt / (AmE), / ˈdʌvkɒt / (BrE)
A small building or structure intended to house pigeons or doves.
birdhouse for pigeons or doves

Examples

The old stone dovecote behind the manor house now shelters a family of pigeons.
Farmers built a dovecote to provide a safe place for their birds to nest.
She admired the intricate wooden design of the new dovecote in the garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pigeonhole - small compartments for pigeons in a dove house

Etymology

From Old French 'douve' (dove) + English 'cote' (shelter, hut).
