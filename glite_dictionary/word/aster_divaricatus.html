Aster divaricatus

1. NOUN

/ ˈæstər dɪˌvɛrɪˈkeɪtəs / (AmE), / ˈæstə dɪˌvɛrɪˈkeɪtəs / (BrE)
A species of perennial wood aster native to eastern North America, characterized by rhizomatous growth and white flowers.
species of wood aster with white flowers

Examples

The shady woodland was dotted with blooming Aster divaricatus in early autumn.
Gardeners often choose Aster divaricatus for its attractive white flowers and ability to thrive in partial shade.
Researchers noted that Aster divaricatus plays an important role in supporting local pollinators.

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name Aster from Greek 'astḗr' (star), divaricatus from Latin (spreading apart).
