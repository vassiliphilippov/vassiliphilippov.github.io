Ginglymostoma cirratum

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɪŋɡliːˈmɑːstəʊ ˈsɪrətəm / (AmE), / ˌɡɪŋɡlɪˌmɒstəmə sɪˈrætəm / (BrE)
a species of small, bottom-dwelling shark found in warm shallow waters of the Americas and the Indo-Pacific, commonly known as the nurse shark
a species of nurse shark

Examples

Divers often encounter the Ginglymostoma cirratum resting motionless on sandy sea floors during the day.
The Ginglymostoma cirratum is known for its docile nature and distinctive whisker-like barbels near its mouth.
Researchers tagged several Ginglymostoma cirratum individuals to study their migration patterns along the coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nurse shark - a common name for Ginglymostoma cirratum, a bottom-dwelling shark species

Etymology

From Greek ginglymos (hinge) and stoma (mouth); Latin cirratum (curled, ringed).
