Lashkar-e-Omar

1. PROPN

/ lʌʃ.kɑr eɪ ˈoʊ.mɑr / (AmE), / lʌʃ.kɑː eɪ ˈəʊ.mɑː / (BrE)
a terrorist organization formed in Pakistan in 2002 as a coalition of extremist Islamic militant groups including Lashkar-e-Taiba, Lashkar-e-Jhangvi, Jaish-e-Muhammad, and elements of al-Qaeda
Pakistani militant Islamist group (2002–)

Examples

The Pakistani government banned Lashkar-e-Omar due to its involvement in terrorist activities.
Intelligence agencies have linked several attacks in Karachi to Lashkar-e-Omar.
The formation of Lashkar-e-Omar brought together militants from various banned outfits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lashkar-e-Taiba - another Pakistan-based militant Islamist group
  • Synonym: Jaish-e-Muhammad - a Pakistan-based militant group
  • Synonym: al-Qaeda - international extremist Islamist organization

Etymology

From Urdu, meaning 'Army of Omar,' referencing the second caliph of Islam.
