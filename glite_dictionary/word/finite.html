finite

1. ADJ

/ ˈfaɪ.naɪt / (AmE), / ˈfaɪ.naɪt / (BrE)
(Of a verb form) marked by tense, person, and number; limited by features such as tense and agreement.
marked by tense, person, and number (grammar)

Examples

In the sentence 'She walks home,' 'walks' is a finite verb form.
Only finite verbs can function as the main verb of a sentence in English.
The distinction between finite and nonfinite verbs is important in syntactic analysis.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonfinite - (of a verb) not limited by tense, person, or number

Etymology

From Latin finitus (limited, bounded), past participle of finire (to limit, end).

2. ADJ

/ ˈfaɪ.naɪt / (AmE), / ˈfaɪ.naɪt / (BrE)
having limits or bounds; not infinite in extent, number, or duration
having limits or bounds

Examples

There are only a finite number of ways to solve this equation.
Because our resources are finite, we must use them wisely.
The lecture discussed whether the universe is finite or infinite.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: infinite - having no limits or bounds

Etymology

From Latin finitus, past participle of finire ‘to limit’.
