2. NOUN

/ biː / (AmE), / biː / (BrE)
A unit of area used in nuclear physics to measure the probability of interaction between particles, equal to 10⁻²⁸ square meters.
unit of area in nuclear physics
medical

Examples

The measured cross section was 3.2 b, indicating a high likelihood of interaction.
Scientists often use the b and its submultiples when discussing nuclear collisions.
A value of one b represents an area of 10⁻²⁸ square meters in nuclear physics.

Etymology

Shortened from 'barn', an informal name for the unit in nuclear physics.
