B

1. NOUN

/ bi / (AmE), / biː / (BrE)
the vitamin originally believed to be a single substance but now known to comprise several different B vitamins, such as B1, B2, and B12.
original vitamin B; now a group of B vitamins

Examples

Vitamin B was once considered a single nutrient before its components were identified.
Researchers later discovered that B was actually several different vitamins, not one.
The original concept of vitamin B led to the naming of the various B vitamins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: B vitamin - any of the group of water-soluble vitamins once regarded as a single vitamin B

Etymology

From the practice of using letters to label vitamins (early 20th century).

3. NOUN

/ biː / (AmE), / biː / (BrE)

Examples

B is an essential element in plant nutrition and is often found in fertilizers.
The hardness of B makes it useful for making abrasives and cutting tools.
When heated, powdered B can ignite and burn with a green flame.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boron - the chemical element with atomic number 5

Symbol for boron, derived from the element's name, which comes from Arabic 'būraq' (borax).

5. NOUN

/ bi / (AmE), / biː / (BrE)
the blood group whose red cells carry the B antigen and whose plasma contains anti-A antibodies
a blood group type
medical

Examples

She was relieved to learn that her blood type is B.
Donors with B blood group are compatible with both B and AB recipients for transfusions.
The hospital needed more B negative blood for their supplies.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: A - the blood group whose red cells carry the A antigen
  • Degree Lower: O - the blood group whose red cells carry neither A nor B antigen
  • Degree Higher: AB - the blood group whose red cells carry both A and B antigens

Etymology

Letter designation for the blood group by Karl Landsteiner (early 20th century).
