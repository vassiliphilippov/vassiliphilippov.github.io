sometimes

1. ADV

/ ˈsʌm.taɪmz / (AmE), / ˈsʌm.taɪmz / (BrE)
on some occasions but not always; now and then
occasionally; at certain times

Examples

I sometimes walk to work instead of taking the bus.
He sometimes forgets to call his parents when he's busy.
The weather in spring is unpredictable; sometimes it's warm, and other times it's quite cold.

Synset Examples

sometimes she wished she were back in England
sometimes her photography is breathtaking
sometimes they come for a month; at other times for six months

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: often - frequently; many times
  • Degree Lower: rarely - not often; seldom
  • Degree Higher: always - at all times; on every occasion
  • Degree Lower: never - at no time; not ever

Etymology

From Middle English som time (occasionally), from Old English sum + tīma (time).
