aspheric

1. ADJ

/ æˈsfɛrɪk / (AmE), / æsˈfɛrɪk / (BrE)
not perfectly spherical; having a surface that slightly deviates from being a perfect sphere, often used of lenses or mirrors.
not perfectly spherical (especially of lenses)

Examples

Modern camera lenses often contain at least one aspheric element to reduce image distortion.
By using an aspheric mirror, the telescope achieved much sharper focus than with a standard spherical one.
The optician explained that aspheric lenses are thinner and lighter than their spherical counterparts.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: spherical - having the shape of a sphere

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (not) + 'sphaira' (sphere), meaning not spherical.
