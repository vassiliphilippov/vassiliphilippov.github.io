to deprave

1. VERB

/ dɪˈpreɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈpreɪv / (BrE)
to make someone morally bad or wicked; to corrupt morally
corrupt morally; make wicked

Examples

Some critics believe that violent video games can deprave impressionable minds.
The judge argued that unchecked freedom of expression might deprave public morals.
She worried that the new friends would deprave her son with their reckless behavior.

Synset Examples

debauch the young people with wine and women
Socrates was accused of corrupting young men
Do school counselors subvert young children?
corrupt the morals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corrupt - to make someone or something morally bad; to cause to become dishonest or immoral

Etymology

From Latin depravare (to distort, pervert), from de- (thoroughly) + pravus (crooked, perverse).
