carpetbag

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːr.pət.bæɡ / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.pɪt.bæɡ / (BrE)
A large traveling bag made of carpet material, commonly used in the 19th century.
large bag made from carpet material

Examples

He packed his clothes into a battered old carpetbag before heading west.
The museum displayed a collection of 19th-century carpetbags owned by early settlers.
She carried a colorful carpetbag during her journey across the country.

Etymology

From 'carpet' + 'bag', referring to bags made from old pieces of carpet.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkɑːrpətˌbæɡ / (AmE), / ˈkɑːpɪtbæɡ / (BrE)
(of a person) seeking success or a position in a new locality, especially for personal gain and without local connections.
seeking advantage in a new locality

Examples

The senator was accused of being a carpetbag politician with no real ties to the state.
Voters were wary of the carpetbag candidate who had just moved into the district.
Historically, carpetbag opportunists arrived in towns after wars or upheavals.

Synset Examples

a carpetbag stranger
a carpetbag politician

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carpetbagger - a person who moves to a new place to seek personal gain, especially in politics

Etymology

From 'carpetbagger', originally referring to outsiders moving to the US South after the Civil War, carrying bags made from carpeting.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00935453-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈkɑːr.pətˌbæɡ / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.pɪtˌbæɡ / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of carpetbaggers, especially in being opportunistic or from outside a region, often for personal gain.
characteristic of carpetbaggers

Examples

The new administration was accused of forming a carpetbag government filled with outsiders.
Historians often debate the legacy of carpetbag policies during Reconstruction.
Many locals resented the arrival of carpetbag businessmen seeking quick profits.

Synset Examples

carpetbag adventurers
a carpetbag government

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carpetbagger - a person from the North who moved to the South after the American Civil War to profit from Reconstruction
  • Synonym: opportunistic - taking advantage of opportunities for personal gain, sometimes unethically

Etymology

From 'carpetbagger', historically referring to opportunists from the North who moved to the South after the American Civil War, named for the type of luggage they carried.
