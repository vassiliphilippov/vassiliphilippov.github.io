lycopodiate

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈkɑːpədiˌeɪt / (AmE), / laɪˈkɒpədiˌeɪt / (BrE)
A member of a group of lower vascular plants that includes club mosses and their relatives, such as Lycopodiales, Isoetales, and Selaginellales.
club moss and related plant group
medical

Examples

The ancient forests once teemed with lycopodiates that grew to massive sizes.
Researchers study lycopodiates to understand the evolution of vascular plants.
Some lycopodiates are still found in tropical and temperate regions today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: club moss - any of various primitive vascular plants of the family Lycopodiaceae

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Lycopodiatae', based on Greek 'lykos' (wolf) and 'pous, podos' (foot), referring to the genus Lycopodium.
