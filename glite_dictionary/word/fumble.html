fumble

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʌmbəl / (AmE), / ˈfʌmbəl / (BrE)
In sports, an act of accidentally dropping or failing to hold onto the ball, especially in American football.
sports mistake dropping the ball

Examples

The quarterback's fumble in the last quarter cost his team the game.
After recovering the fumble, the defense quickly turned the tide.
His first career touchdown came after he scooped up a fumble on the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turnover - Loss of possession to the other team in sports
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00076732-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈfʌmbəl / (AmE), / ˈfʌmbəl / (BrE)
to move or make one's way clumsily or blindly, typically by groping or searching awkwardly
move clumsily or blindly

Examples

In the dark, she fumbled along the wall searching for the light switch.
He fumbled through the crowded room, bumping into chairs and tables.
They fumbled their way down the narrow staircase, unable to see their feet.

Synset Examples

He fumbled towards the door

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grope - move or search blindly or uncertainly with the hands

Etymology

Probably from Middle English fumblen, of uncertain origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02055471-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈfʌm.bəl / (AmE), / ˈfʌm.bəl / (BrE)
to mishandle or fail to play cleanly a ball that is in play, especially by dropping or juggling it, typically in sports such as baseball or football
mishandle or drop a ball in play (sports)

Examples

The shortstop managed to fumble the grounder, allowing the runner to reach first base safely.
If you fumble the ball on a crucial play, it can cost your team the game.
During the ninth inning, he unexpectedly fumbled a routine ground ball.

Synset Examples

fumble a grounder

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bobble - to handle a ball clumsily, causing it to be dropped or nearly dropped

Etymology

Possibly of Scandinavian origin; compare Old Norse *fumla* (to fumble, grope).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01077720-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈfʌmbəl / (AmE), / ˈfʌmbəl / (BrE)
to use one's hands clumsily while handling or searching for something
handle clumsily or awkwardly

Examples

He began to fumble with the keys, unable to unlock the door in the dark.
She fumbled through her bag, looking for her ringing phone.
Don't fumble the ball during the game or you'll give the other team an advantage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bungle - to carry out a task clumsily or incompetently
  • Synonym: to mishandle - to manage or deal with something badly or incompetently
  • Antonym: to manage - to handle or control successfully

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English fumblen, possibly related to Old Norse fumla (to grope) or akin to German fummeln (to fumble).
