Amon

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑːmən / (AmE), / ˈɑːmɒn / (BrE)
An ancient Egyptian deity, regarded as the personification of air and breath, and especially worshipped at Thebes.
an ancient Egyptian god of air and breath

Examples

The temple of Amon at Karnak was one of the largest religious structures ever built.
Priests of Amon held significant power during parts of ancient Egypt's history.
Many ancient Egyptians believed Amon to be the king of the gods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Amun - alternative spelling or transliteration of Amon, an Egyptian god

Etymology

From Egyptian ‘Imn, meaning ‘the hidden one’.
