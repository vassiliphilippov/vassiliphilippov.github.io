Mitella

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈtɛlə / (AmE), / mɪˈtɛlə / (BrE)
A genus of low, slender herbaceous plants native to North America and northeastern Asia, with flowers that have deeply divided petals.
genus of herbaceous plants with divided petals

Examples

Mitella thrives in shady woodland environments and moist soils.
The delicate flowers of Mitella are a distinctive feature in spring gardens.
Botanists classify several species under the genus Mitella.

Etymology

From Latin *mitella*, meaning 'headband', referring to the shape of the plant's fruit.
