ditto

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdɪtəʊ / (BrE)
A mark (often ") indicating that the word or phrase above should be repeated.
mark indicating repetition

Examples

Please use the ditto mark if the answer in row two is the same as above.
In old documents, a ditto is often used instead of rewriting words.
She put a ditto in the column to indicate repetition of the address.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repeat - to say or write something again

Etymology

From Italian 'detto' (said), past participle of 'dire' (to say).

2. VERB

/ ˈdɪtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdɪtəʊ / (BrE)
to repeat or express agreement with an action or statement just made
to repeat or express agreement
informal

Examples

When asked for his opinion, Tom simply dittoed what Sarah had already said.
She dittoed the previous speaker’s thanks to the organizers.
During the meeting, several members dittoed her suggestion without adding any new points.

Synset Examples

The next speaker dittoed her argument

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repeat - to say or do something again; synonym as a verb

Etymology

From Italian 'detto', past participle of 'dire' (to say), used as 'the said' or 'already stated'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00961059-v
Synset POS: v
