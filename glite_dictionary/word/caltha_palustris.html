Caltha palustris

1. NOUN

/ ˈkælθə pəˈlʌstrɪs / (AmE), / ˈkælθə pəˈlʌstrɪs / (BrE)
A perennial swamp plant of Europe and North America, with bright yellow, buttercup-like flowers; also known as marsh marigold.
a swamp plant with yellow flowers (marsh marigold)

Examples

We found Caltha palustris blooming along the edges of the wet meadow in early spring.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate Caltha palustris in water gardens for its vivid yellow blossoms.
The presence of Caltha palustris often indicates nutrient-rich wetlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marsh marigold - common name for Caltha palustris

Etymology

From Latin: caltha (marigold), palustris (of the marsh).
