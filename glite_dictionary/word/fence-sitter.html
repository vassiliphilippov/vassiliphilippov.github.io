fence-sitter

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛns ˌsɪtər / (AmE), / ˈfɛns ˌsɪtə / (BrE)
a person who refuses to take sides in a dispute or controversy
person who avoids taking sides

Examples

During the heated debate, Tom remained a fence-sitter and declined to voice his opinion.
Elections are often decided by fence-sitters who wait until the last moment to choose a side.
As a manager, she tries not to be a fence-sitter and instead makes clear decisions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neutral - not supporting or helping either side in a conflict
  • Antonym: partisan - a strong supporter of a particular party, cause, or person

Etymology

From the image of sitting on a fence to avoid committing to either side; first recorded in late 19th-century American English.
