godchild

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑdˌtʃaɪld / (AmE), / ˈɡɒdˌtʃaɪld / (BrE)
A child who is sponsored by an adult (the godparent) at baptism, especially in certain Christian traditions.
child sponsored at baptism

Examples

After the ceremony, she was proud to be named her niece’s godchild.
Every Christmas, his godchild sends him a handmade card.
As a godchild, Lucy felt a special connection to her godparents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: godparent - adult who sponsors a child at baptism

Etymology

From 'god' + 'child'; modeled after the relationship described in Christian baptism.
