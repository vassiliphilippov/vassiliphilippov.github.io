Loxostege

1. NOUN

/ lɑkˈsɒstɪdʒi / (AmE), / lɒkˈsɒstɪdʒi / (BrE)
A genus of moths whose larvae are commonly known as garden webworms, pests of various crops and plants.
genus of moths (garden webworms)

Examples

Several types of Loxostege are considered agricultural pests due to their larvae's feeding habits.
The Loxostege moths lay eggs on the leaves of crops in the spring.
Gardeners often try to control Loxostege infestations to protect their vegetables.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: webworm - larval moth pest that spins webs on plants

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek elements: 'loxos' (slanting, oblique) and 'stege' (covering).

The Loxostege genus includes several species that affect crops worldwide.
