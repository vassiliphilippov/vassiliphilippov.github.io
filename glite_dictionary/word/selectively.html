selectively

1. ADV

/ səˈlɛktɪvli / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛktɪvli / (BrE)
In a selective way; by carefully choosing from among a group or set.
in a selective manner

Examples

The new medication targets cancer cells selectively, leaving healthy cells unharmed.
She applied to jobs very selectively, focusing only on companies she truly admired.
Resources were allocated selectively to the most promising research projects.

Synset Examples

we choose our students very selectively

Related Terms

  • Antonym: randomly - in a manner that is not selective; without method or conscious choice
  • Synonym: selective - characterized by careful choice; choosing only particular people or things

Etymology

From 'selective' + adverbial suffix '-ly'. Ultimately from Latin 'selectus' (chosen).
