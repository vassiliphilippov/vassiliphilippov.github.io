magneton

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæg.nəˌtɑn / (AmE), / ˈmæɡ.nɪ.tɒn / (BrE)
a unit of magnetic moment, especially of an atom, molecule, or subatomic particle
unit of magnetic moment

Examples

The Bohr magneton is a physical constant related to the electron's magnetic moment.
Physicists use the nuclear magneton as a standard unit for measuring magnetic moments.
The experiment measured the particle's moment in units of magnetons.

Etymology

From Greek 'magnet-', relating to magnetism, with scientific suffix '-on'.
