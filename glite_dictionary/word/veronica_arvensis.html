Veronica arvensis

1. NOUN

/ vəˈrɑnɪkə ɑrˈvɛnsɪs / (AmE), / vəˈrɒnɪkə ɑːˈvɛnsɪs / (BrE)
an annual plant with small blue flowers, commonly found in waste places in Europe and North America; also known as corn speedwell.
a blue-flowered annual plant (corn speedwell)

Examples

Veronica arvensis is often found growing alongside crops in European fields.
Botanists studied the spread of Veronica arvensis in disturbed, open habitats.
The small blue flowers of Veronica arvensis make it easy to identify in spring.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Veronica' (the genus) and 'arvensis' meaning 'of the field.'
