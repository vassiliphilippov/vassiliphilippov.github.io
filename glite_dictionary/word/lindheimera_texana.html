Lindheimera texana

1. NOUN

/ lɪndˈhaɪmərə tɛkˈsænə / (AmE), / lɪndˈhaɪmərə tɛkˈsænə / (BrE)
A species of annual plant native to Texas, with deeply lobed leaves and bright yellow, daisy-like flower heads, often grown for ornamental purposes.
Texas wildflower with yellow, daisy-like blooms

Examples

The bright yellow blooms of Lindheimera texana are a cheerful addition to any spring garden.
Botanists admire Lindheimera texana for its uniquely shaped leaves and vibrant flowers.
Lindheimera texana thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly seen in wildflower meadows across Texas.

Etymology

Named after Ferdinand Lindheimer, a German-American botanist, and the Latin 'texana' meaning 'from Texas'.
