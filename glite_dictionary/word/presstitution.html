presstitution

1. NOUN

/ prɛsˈstɪtuːʃən / (AmE), / prɛsˈstɪtjuːʃən / (BrE)
(slang) The practice of compromising journalistic integrity or ethics for profit or sensationalism, especially by reporting distorted or biased news.
journalistic corruption for profit; media bias for gain
slang offensive

Examples

Many critics argue that presstitution is undermining public trust in the media.
The rise of presstitution reflects the pursuit of ratings over accurate reporting.
He accused the network of blatant presstitution during the election coverage.

Synset Examples

The presstitution in media is alarming to many viewers.
She condemned the presstitution of journalism in today's world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yellow journalism - sensationalized or biased journalism
  • Antonym: journalistic integrity - the ethical standards upheld by journalists

Etymology

Blend of 'press' (news media) and 'prostitution' (selling oneself for money).
