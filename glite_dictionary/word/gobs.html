gobs

1. NOUN

/ ɡɑbz / (AmE), / ɡɒbz / (BrE)
a large quantity or number of something; a lot
large quantity or number
informal

Examples

She has gobs of experience in marketing and advertising.
After the sale, there were still gobs of clothes left on the racks.
He's made gobs of money from his new business venture.

Synset Examples

made lots of new friends
she amassed stacks of newspapers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loads - a large amount or number of something
  • Synonym: tons - a great deal; a large amount

Etymology

From 'gob', meaning a lump or mass; informal extension to mean 'a lot'.
