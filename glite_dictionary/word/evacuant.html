evacuant

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈvæk.ju.ənt / (AmE), / ɪˈvak.ju.ənt / (BrE)
(of a substance) causing evacuation of the bowels; strongly laxative.
strongly laxative; causing bowel evacuation
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an evacuant to relieve the patient's severe constipation.
Some herbal remedies are known for their evacuant effects on the digestive tract.
Before the procedure, she was given an evacuant to clear her intestines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laxative - a substance used to stimulate or facilitate bowel movements

Etymology

From Latin 'evacuare' (to empty).
