Indiaman

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪndiəmən / (AmE), / ˈɪndiəmən / (BrE)
a large sailing ship formerly used by European countries, especially Britain, for trade with India.
large sailing ship for India trade

Examples

The Indiaman docked in London after a long voyage from Calcutta.
Many fortunes were made from the trade carried by the Indiaman during the 18th century.
A model of a typical Indiaman is on display at the maritime museum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: East Indiaman - a specific type of Indiaman trading with the East Indies

Etymology

From 'India' + 'man', referring to ships trading with India.
