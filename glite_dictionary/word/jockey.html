jockey

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɑːki / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒki / (BrE)
a person who rides horses in races, especially as a professional occupation
professional horse racer

Examples

The experienced jockey guided the horse to victory in the Kentucky Derby.
Each jockey must maintain a strict weight limit to compete in major races.
He trained for years before becoming a professional jockey at age eighteen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rider - someone who rides, especially a horse or bike

Etymology

Probably from earlier Scots 'jocke' (boy, stable boy), diminutive of 'Jock' (a pet name for John).

3. VERB

/ ˈdʒɑki / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒki / (BrE)
to compete or struggle to gain an advantage or a better position, often by using clever or strategic moves
compete for advantage or position

Examples

The senators continued to jockey for position as the negotiations entered their final stages.
Several firms are jockeying to win the lucrative government contract.
She jockeyed herself into a key role on the committee through persistent effort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to vie - to compete eagerly with others for something
  • Synonym: struggle - to make forceful efforts to get free or achieve something

Etymology

From the noun 'jockey' (a person who rides horses in races), extended metaphorically to general competition.

4. VERB

/ ˈdʒɑki / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒki / (BrE)
to gain an advantage over someone, often by using unfair or deceptive methods
gain advantage by trickery or deceit

Examples

The companies attempted to jockey for position by spreading misleading information.
She managed to jockey her way into a higher-paying job ahead of her colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to outmaneuver - to gain an advantage by skill or cleverness
  • Synonym: cheat - to act dishonestly to gain an advantage

Etymology

From the noun 'jockey' (professional horse rider), alluding to the competitive and skillful maneuvering of riders.

Examples
