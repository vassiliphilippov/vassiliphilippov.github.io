wonted

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɔntəd / (AmE), / ˈwəʊntɪd / (BrE)
usual or customary; commonly found or practiced
usual; customary
archaic

Examples

She smiled with her wonted grace before addressing the audience.
He completed his duties with wonted efficiency, as expected.
Despite the crisis, they followed their wonted routine every morning.

Synset Examples

his accustomed thoroughness
took his customary morning walk
his habitual comment
with her wonted candor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: customary - according to the customs or usual practices
  • Antonym: unusual - not usual, uncommon

Etymology

From Middle English, past participle of 'wonen' (to be used to), ultimately from Old English 'wunian' (to dwell, be accustomed).
