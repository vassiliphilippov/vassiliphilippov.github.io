eruptive

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈrʌptɪv / (AmE), / ɪˈrʌptɪv / (BrE)
producing or characterized by eruptions, especially of a sudden or explosive nature.
producing or involving eruptions

Examples

The region has several eruptive volcanoes that pose a risk to nearby communities.
Some skin conditions, such as measles, are described as eruptive diseases.
The geologist gave a lecture about eruptive rocks formed by volcanic activity.

Synset Examples

an eruptive disease

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explosive - sudden and violent, especially with a loud noise

Etymology

From Latin 'eruptus', past participle of 'erumpere' (to break out).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02732923-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈrʌptɪv / (AmE), / ɪˈrʌptɪv / (BrE)
(of rocks or geological formations) formed by the action of fire or intense heat, especially during volcanic activity.
formed by fire or intense heat (geology)

Examples

The island was composed mainly of eruptive rocks, evidence of its volcanic origins.
Scientists studied the eruptive formations to better understand the area's geological history.
Some minerals are only found in eruptive environments where intense heat alters their structure.

Synset Examples

rocks formed by igneous agents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: igneous - (of rocks) formed through the cooling and solidification of magma or lava

Etymology

From Latin 'eruptivus', from 'erumpere' meaning 'to burst forth'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01357773-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ɪˈrʌptɪv / (AmE), / ɪˈrʌptɪv / (BrE)
actively spewing out or capable of spewing out lava, ash, or other material (especially of a volcano or geological feature)
actively spewing out lava or ash

Examples

Mount Etna is currently eruptive, sending columns of ash into the sky.
An eruptive volcano can pose significant risks to nearby communities.
The researchers studied patterns in eruptive geysers across the region.

Synset Examples

a geyser is an intermittently eruptive hot spring

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dormant - temporarily inactive (as of a volcano)
  • Synonym: active - currently active, not dormant or extinct (as of a volcano)

Etymology

From Latin 'eruptus', past participle of 'erumpere' (to burst forth).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00040757-s
Synset POS: s
