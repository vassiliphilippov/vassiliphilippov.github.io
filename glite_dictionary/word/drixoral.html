Drixoral

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɪksɔːrəl / (AmE), / ˈdrɪksɒrəl / (BrE)
A trademarked name for a drug formerly used to treat upper respiratory congestion, containing an antihistamine, bronchodilator, and vasoconstrictor.
trademarked decongestant drug

Examples

My doctor prescribed Drixoral to help relieve my sinus congestion during allergy season.
When Drixoral was discontinued, many patients had to switch to other decongestants.
He found that Drixoral worked more effectively than other over-the-counter remedies.
