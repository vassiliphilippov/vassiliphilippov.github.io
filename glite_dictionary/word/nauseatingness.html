nauseatingness

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɔziˌeɪtɪŋnəs / (AmE), / ˌnɔːziˌeɪtɪŋnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of causing nausea or intense disgust, especially due to extreme unpleasantness in taste or smell
quality of being nauseating

Examples

The nauseatingness of the spoiled food drove everyone out of the kitchen.
She couldn't bear the nauseatingness of the medicine's flavor.
The film captured the nauseatingness of the scene so effectively that some viewers felt ill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nausea - a feeling of sickness with an inclination to vomit
  • Synonym: disgust - a strong sense of revulsion or profound disapproval

Etymology

From 'nauseating' + '-ness'. 'Nauseating' from Latin 'nauseare' (to feel sickness).
