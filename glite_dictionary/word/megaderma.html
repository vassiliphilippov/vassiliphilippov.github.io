Megaderma

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛɡəˈdɜrmə / (AmE), / ˌmɛɡəˈdɜːmə / (BrE)
A genus of bats in the family Megadermatidae, known as false vampire bats, found in parts of Asia.
genus of bats (Megadermatidae family)

Examples

The Megaderma genus includes several species of large-eared bats native to South and Southeast Asia.
Researchers have long debated the hunting methods of Megaderma in their natural habitats.
The unique facial features of Megaderma distinguish it from other bat genera.

Etymology

From Greek mega- (large) + derma (skin), referring to the large ears or skin flaps of these bats.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛɡəˈdɜrmə / (AmE), / ˌmɛɡəˈdɜːmə / (BrE)
A genus of bats in the family Megadermatidae, commonly called false vampire bats, characterized by their large ears and predatory behavior.
genus of false vampire bats

Examples

The Megaderma genus includes bats that prey on small animals rather than insects.
Researchers discovered a new species belonging to the Megaderma genus in Southeast Asia.
Unlike most bats, members of Megaderma are known for their distinctive facial features and feeding habits.

Etymology

From Greek 'mega' (large) + 'derma' (skin), referring to the large ears and skin flaps.
