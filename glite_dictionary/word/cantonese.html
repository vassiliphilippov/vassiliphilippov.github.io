Cantonese

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæn.təˈniz / (AmE), / ˌkæn.təˈniːz / (BrE)
the variety of Chinese spoken in Guangdong province, Hong Kong, and various Chinese communities worldwide
variety of Chinese language

Examples

Many people in Hong Kong use Cantonese as their primary language at home and at work.
She is fluent in both Cantonese and Mandarin, which helps her communicate with a wide range of people.
The restaurant menu is written in both English and Cantonese to serve the local community.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Mandarin - the most widely spoken variety of Chinese
  • Synonym: Chinese - the group of related language varieties spoken in China

Etymology

From Canton, the former English name for Guangzhou, China, plus -ese denoting language, people, or origin.
