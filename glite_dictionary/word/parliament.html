parliament

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrləmənt / (AmE), / ˈpɑːləmənt / (BrE)
A card game in which players lay sevens and cards in sequence by suit; the aim is to be the first to play all your cards.
a card game involving sequences by suit

Examples

Every Friday night, the family gathers to play parliament together.
In parliament, it's crucial to keep track of which sevens have already been played.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrləˌmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈpɑːlɪmənt / (BrE)
the supreme legislative body in certain countries, responsible for making and passing laws
national legislative assembly

Examples

The British parliament convened to debate the new law.
Canada's parliament consists of two main chambers: the House of Commons and the Senate.
She was elected to parliament in her country's recent general election.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: congress - legislative body, especially in the United States
  • Synonym: senate - upper house of a legislature
  • Synonym: assembly - group of people gathered for legislative purposes

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman and Old French 'parlement' (discussion, assembly), from 'parler' (to speak).
