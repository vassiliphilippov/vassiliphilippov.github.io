Afrocarpus falcata

1. NOUN

/ ˌæfroʊˈkɑrpəs fælˈkeɪtə / (AmE), / ˌafrəʊˈkɑːpəs falˈkeɪtə / (BrE)
a medium-sized evergreen tree native to South Africa, belonging to the family Podocarpaceae
South African evergreen tree species

Examples

The majestic Afrocarpus falcata grows abundantly in South Africa's southern forests.
Many birds rely on the seeds of Afrocarpus falcata for food during the dry season.
Conservationists are working to protect the natural habitat of Afrocarpus falcata.

Etymology

Latinized botanical name; genus name combines 'Afro-' (Africa) and Greek 'karpos' (fruit), species name from Latin 'falcatus' (sickle-shaped, referring to leaf shape).
