hemipteran

1. NOUN

/ hɛˈmɪptərən / (AmE), / hɛˈmɪptərən / (BrE)
an insect of the order Hemiptera, characterized by sucking mouthparts and forewings that are thickened and leathery at the base; often undergo incomplete metamorphosis
insect with sucking mouthparts; true bug
medical

Examples

A scientist discovered a rare hemipteran species lurking beneath the leaves.
Certain hemipterans are known agricultural pests because they feed on plant sap.
The true bug is a familiar example of a hemipteran found in gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bug - an insect of the order Hemiptera; true bug

Etymology

From modern Latin Hemiptera, from Greek hemi- (half) + pteron (wing).
