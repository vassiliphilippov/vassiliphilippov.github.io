Spergularia

1. NOUN

/ spɚˌɡjʊˈlɛri.ə / (AmE), / spəˌɡjʊˈlɑːri.ə / (BrE)
A genus of chiefly maritime Eurasian herbs of the pink family, commonly known as sand spurry or sea spurry.
genus of maritime Eurasian herbs (sand spurry)

Examples

The sandy coastlines are often dotted with Spergularia growing among the pebbles.
Botanists recently discovered a new species within the Spergularia genus.
Spergularia is commonly referred to as sand spurry due to its preferred habitat.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the related genus Spergula, plus Latin suffix -aria.

2. NOUN

/ spərˌɡjʊˈlɛəriə / (AmE), / spəːˌɡjʊəˈlɑːrɪə / (BrE)
a genus of mostly coastal Eurasian herbs, commonly known as sand spurry or sea spurry, with small white or pink flowers
genus of coastal herbs (sand spurry)

Examples

The coastal dunes were covered with patches of Spergularia thriving in the sandy soil.
Several species within the Spergularia genus are adapted to saline environments.
Botanists identified the mysterious plant as a new member of the Spergularia genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sand spurry - a common name for herbs in the genus Spergularia
  • Synonym: sea spurry - a common name for some species in the genus Spergularia

Etymology

From Latin, diminutive of *Spergula*, a related plant genus.
