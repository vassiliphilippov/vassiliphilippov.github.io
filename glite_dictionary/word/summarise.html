to summarise

1. VERB

/ ˈsʌməˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈsʌməraɪz / (BrE)
to give a brief statement of the main points of something; to present a summary
give a summary of something
british

Examples

Can you summarise the meeting for those who couldn't attend?
She managed to summarise the entire novel in just a few sentences.
Before we finish, I'll summarise the main points of today's lecture.

Synset Examples

he summed up his results
I will now summarize

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to summarize - to give a summary (American spelling)
  • Antonym: detail - to describe something fully or with many details

Etymology

From French 'résumer', from Latin 'resumere' (to take up again). Spelling with 's' is British English; American English uses 'summarize'.

2. VERB

/ ˈsʌməˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈsʌməraɪz / (BrE)
to give a concise and comprehensive statement of the main points of something; to act as a summary of
give a concise statement of main points
british

Examples

The introduction summarises the key themes discussed in the book.
In the report, she summarised the challenges they faced over the year.
A chart at the end summarises all the major findings.

Synset Examples

The abstract summarizes the main ideas in the paper

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to summarize - American English spelling of 'summarise'
  • Degree Lower: paraphrase - express the meaning using different words
  • Synonym: to condense - make something more concise or concentrated

Etymology

From summary + -ise, modeled after summary.
