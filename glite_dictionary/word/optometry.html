optometry

1. NOUN

/ ɑpˈtɑmətɹi / (AmE), / ɒpˈtɒmətɹi / (BrE)
the professional practice or science of examining the eyes for visual defects and prescribing corrective lenses or other treatment
examination and treatment of eye vision
medical

Examples

She decided to study optometry after becoming fascinated by vision science.
Routine optometry exams can help detect eye diseases early.
The clinic specializes in pediatric optometry and fitting children with glasses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ophthalmology - branch of medicine dealing with eye diseases

Etymology

From Greek optos (seen, visible) + -metry (process of measuring).
