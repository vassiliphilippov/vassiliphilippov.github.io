host

1. NOUN

/ hoʊst / (AmE), / həʊst / (BrE)
A consecrated wafer representing the body of Christ and used in the sacrament of the Eucharist or Holy Communion.
consecrated bread in Christian communion

Examples

The priest raised the host during the celebration of Mass.
She received the host reverently during Communion.
The altar boys prepared the host before the ceremony began.

Etymology

From Latin 'hostia' meaning 'sacrificial victim'.
