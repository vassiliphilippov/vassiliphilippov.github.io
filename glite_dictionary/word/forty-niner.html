forty-niner

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɔr.tiˈnaɪ.nɚ / (AmE), / ˌfɔː.tiˈnaɪ.nə / (BrE)
a person, especially a miner, who took part in the California Gold Rush of 1849
participant in the 1849 California Gold Rush

Examples

Many forty-niners braved great hardships to travel to California in search of gold.
The museum displays artifacts that once belonged to a forty-niner.
Stories of forty-niners continue to capture the imagination of historians and adventure seekers.

Etymology

From 'forty-nine', referring to the year 1849, and the suffix '-er' for a person associated with an activity.
