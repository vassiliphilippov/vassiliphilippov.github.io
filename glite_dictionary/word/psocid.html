psocid

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊsɪd / (AmE), / ˈsəʊsɪd / (BrE)
A small winged insect found on the bark and leaves of trees, feeding on fungi and decaying plant matter; a member of the order Psocoptera.
small tree-dwelling insect

Examples

The scientist collected several psocids from the tree branches for examination.
Psocids are often found in damp, decaying leaves in wooded areas.
Tiny psocids can sometimes appear in homes where humidity is high.

Etymology

From modern Latin Psocidae, the family name, from Greek psōkhein (to grind, rub).
