wickup

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪk.ʌp / (AmE), / ˈwɪk.ʌp / (BrE)
A tall North American perennial plant (Chamerion angustifolium), with creeping rootstocks, narrow leaves, and spikes of pinkish-purple flowers, commonly found in burned-over areas or recent clearings; also known as fireweed.
North American perennial plant, also called fireweed

Examples

The slopes were covered with wickup after last summer's wildfire.
Bees are drawn to stands of wickup during its peak flowering season.
Locals often harvest wickup honey, prized for its delicate flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fireweed - another name for Chamerion angustifolium, a tall pink-flowered perennial found in burned-over areas
