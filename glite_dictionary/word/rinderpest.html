rinderpest

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪndɚˌpɛst / (AmE), / ˈrɪndəˌpɛst / (BrE)
an acute, highly contagious viral disease of cattle, often fatal, characterized by fever, diarrhea, and inflammation of the mucous membranes
infectious viral disease of cattle
medical

Examples

The outbreak of rinderpest devastated herds across several African countries in the nineteenth century.
Veterinarians worked tirelessly to contain the spread of rinderpest among local cattle.
The successful eradication of rinderpest is considered a major milestone in veterinary medicine.

Etymology

From German 'Rinderpest', from 'Rinder' (cattle) + 'Pest' (plague).
