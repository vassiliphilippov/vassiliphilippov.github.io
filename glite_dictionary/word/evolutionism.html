evolutionism

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛvəˈluːʃəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌiːvəˈluːʃənɪzəm / (BrE)
the scientific theory that species of plants and animals have developed and diversified from earlier forms during the history of the earth
theory of evolution in biology

Examples

Many scientists accept evolutionism as the best explanation for the diversity of life on Earth.
Debates between supporters of evolutionism and creationism have continued for decades.
Her textbook provides an introduction to evolutionism and its major proponents.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: creationism - the belief that life and the universe were created by a supernatural being

Etymology

From 'evolution' + '-ism', indicating adherence to the theory of evolution.
