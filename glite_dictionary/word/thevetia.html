Thevetia

1. NOUN

/ θəˈviːtiə / (AmE), / θəˈviːtiə / (BrE)
A genus of poisonous evergreen shrubs and trees native to tropical America, characterized by entire leaves and large cymose flowers.
genus of poisonous tropical American shrubs or trees

Examples

Thevetia is often grown for its attractive yellow flowers despite its toxicity.
All parts of the Thevetia plant are considered poisonous if ingested.
The garden featured several Thevetia shrubs among other tropical species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nerium - another genus of poisonous shrubs in the family Apocynaceae

Etymology

Named after French botanist André Thévet (1516–1592).

2. NOUN

/ θəˈviːʃiə / (AmE), / θɪˈviːtiə / (BrE)
A genus of poisonous tropical American evergreen shrubs and trees with entire leaves and large cymose flowers.
a genus of tropical American evergreen shrubs and trees

Examples

The poisonous seeds of Thevetia have been historically used as an arrow poison in some regions.
Gardeners should exercise caution when cultivating Thevetia because all parts of the plant are toxic.
The bright yellow flowers of Thevetia make it a popular ornamental shrub in tropical gardens.

Etymology

Named after André Thévet (1502–1590), French explorer and botanist.
