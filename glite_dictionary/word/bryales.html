Bryales

1. NOUN

/ braɪˈeɪliːz / (AmE), / braɪˈeɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of mosses characterized by a spore case separated from the capsule wall by a hollow intercellular space
an order of mosses

Examples

Scientists studied several species within the Bryales to understand moss diversity in the region.
Many members of the Bryales display unique structural features that differentiate them from other moss orders.
The forest floor was covered in a thick mat of Bryales, thriving in the damp environment.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on 'Bryum' (a genus of moss) + '-ales' (a standard suffix for botanical orders).

2. NOUN

/ braɪˈeɪliːz / (AmE), / braɪˈɑːliːz / (BrE)
an order of mosses characterized by a spore case separated from the capsule wall by a hollow intercellular space, as recognized in some classification systems
an order of mosses in plant taxonomy

Examples

The Bryales include many of the most common terrestrial mosses.
Some botanists classify mosses like Hypnum in the Bryales.
Research on Bryales has contributed to our understanding of moss evolution.

Etymology

From New Latin Bryales, formed from 'Bryum', a moss genus, plus the taxonomic suffix '-ales' indicating an order.
