kaury

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊ.ri / (AmE), / ˈkaʊ.ri / (BrE)
A tall timber tree of New Zealand with white, straight-grained wood, used mainly in carpentry and shipbuilding; also known as kauri.
New Zealand timber tree (variant of kauri)

Examples

The ancient kaury forests of New Zealand are renowned for their massive trees.
Shipbuilders prized kaury wood for its strength and straight grain.
A single fallen kaury log can yield exceptional lumber for furniture and flooring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kauri - Tall New Zealand tree valued for its wood, standard spelling

Etymology

From Māori 'kauri', name for the tree species Agathis australis.
