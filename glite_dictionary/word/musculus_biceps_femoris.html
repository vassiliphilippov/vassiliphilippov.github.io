musculus biceps femoris

1. NOUN

/ mʌsˈkjuːləs ˈbaɪsɛps fɛˈmɔrɪs / (AmE), / mʌsˈkjuːləs ˈbaɪsɛps fɛˈmɔːrɪs / (BrE)
the biceps muscle located at the back of the thigh, responsible for flexing the knee and rotating the leg laterally
muscle at the back of the thigh
medical

Examples

The musculus biceps femoris is easily strained during intense running or sudden movements.
After knee surgery, the strength of the musculus biceps femoris is crucial for proper rehabilitation.
Anatomy students carefully identify the musculus biceps femoris during their dissections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hamstring - muscle group at the back of the thigh
  • Antonym: quadriceps - the large four-part muscle at the front of the thigh

Etymology

From Latin: 'musculus' (muscle), 'biceps' (two-headed), and 'femoris' (of the femur or thigh).
