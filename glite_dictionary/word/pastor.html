Pastor

1. NOUN

/ ˈpæs.tər / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.stə / (BrE)
A genus of birds that includes only the rose-colored starling; sometimes classified as a separate genus from Sturnus.
genus of birds including the rose-colored starling

Examples

The rose-colored starling is the only species classified in the genus Pastor.
Some ornithologists debate whether Pastor should be considered distinct from Sturnus.
Flocks of birds in the genus Pastor migrate across Eurasia each year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sturnus - genus that includes starlings, closely related to Pastor

Etymology

From Latin *pastor* (shepherd), used in taxonomic naming.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpæstər / (AmE), / ˈpɑːstə / (BrE)
A subgenus of birds within the starling family, including the rose-colored starling; sometimes classified as its own genus.
taxonomic subgenus of some starlings

Examples

The rose-colored starling belongs to the subgenus Pastor, recognized for its distinctive plumage.
In some taxonomic schemes, Pastor is treated as a separate genus from Sturnus.
Researchers debate whether to classify the bird as Pastor or assign it to a broader group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sturnus - another genus of starlings

Etymology

From Latin *pastor* (shepherd), used in zoological nomenclature.
