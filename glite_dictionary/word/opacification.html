opacification

1. NOUN

/ oʊˌpæsɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / əʊˌpæsɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of becoming or making something cloudy or opaque, especially in medical or scientific contexts
process of becoming opaque or cloudy
medical

Examples

The opacification of the lens can lead to impaired vision known as cataracts.
During the X-ray, contrast dye was used to enhance the opacification of the blood vessels.
Researchers studied the causes of corneal opacification in laboratory animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opacity - the quality of being opaque

Etymology

From Latin 'opacitas' (shadiness, darkness) + English '-fication' (making or becoming).
