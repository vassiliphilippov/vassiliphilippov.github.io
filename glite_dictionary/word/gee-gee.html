gee-gee

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiˌdʒi / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːˌdʒiː / (BrE)
a horse, especially as called by children or used in informal slang
childish or slang term for horse
informal

Examples

As a child, she always wanted to ride a gee-gee around the park.
The little boy pointed excitedly at the gee-gee during the parade.
At the country fair, children queued up eagerly to pet the gee-gees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horse - a large domesticated ungulate animal used for riding or work

Etymology

Imitative formation based on the sound 'gee' used to urge horses onward.

2. NOUN

a child's or colloquial term for a horse
colloquial word for horse
informal british

Examples

The little girl was excited to ride the gee-gee at the fair.
He painted a picture of a gee-gee for his mother.
Many children ask for a toy gee-gee for their birthday.

Synset Examples

He took care of his gee-gee like a family member.
Her gee-gee has a unique personality that shines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horse - a large domesticated animal used for riding or carrying loads

Etymology

Imitative of the sound used to urge a horse on ('gee!') plus reduplication.
