peeved

1. ADJ

/ pivd / (AmE), / piÀêvd / (BrE)
annoyed or irritated, especially by something minor or petty
annoyed; irritated
informal

Examples

She seemed particularly peeved by the late delivery of her order.
He sounded peeved when I asked him about the unfinished report.
Maria was a bit peeved that no one remembered her birthday.

Synset Examples

made an irritated gesture
feeling nettled from the constant teasing
peeved about being left out
felt really pissed at her snootiness
riled no end by his lies
roiled by the delay

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritated - feeling slight anger or annoyance
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling anger or irritation

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'peeve' (to annoy), which is of uncertain origin, first recorded in the early 20th century.
