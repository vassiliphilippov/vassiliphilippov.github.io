to channelise

1. VERB

/ ˈtʃæn.ə.laɪz / (AmE), / ˈtʃæn.ə.laɪz / (BrE)
to cause (something, such as a stream) to form or take the course of a channel
cause to form a channel
british

Examples

The engineers worked to channelise the river to prevent flooding.
Efforts to channelise the stream altered the local ecosystem dramatically.
They proposed to channelise the water flow through concrete embankments.

Synset Examples

channelize a stream

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to channelize - American spelling; cause to form a channel

Etymology

Formed from 'channel' + '-ise', influenced by the French 'canaliser'.

2. VERB

/ ˈtʃæn.ə.laɪz / (AmE), / ˈtʃæn.ə.laɪz / (BrE)
to direct or guide something along a particular course or path
direct or guide along a course
british

Examples

She tried to channelise her energy into finishing her thesis.

3. VERB

/ ˈtʃænəlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈtʃænəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make or provide with a channel, especially for water, communication, or transportation
provide or make into a channel
british

Examples

Engineers decided to channelise the river to prevent flooding in the rainy season.
The government plans to channelise major waterways for improved cargo transport.
Urban development projects often channelise streams to control water flow and reduce erosion.

to send, direct, or guide something from one person or place to another; to transmit or convey along a particular route or channel
to direct, send, or convey

Examples

During emergencies, authorities quickly channelise aid to the most affected areas.
