angle

1. NOUN

/ ˈæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡl / (BrE)
The space, usually measured in degrees or radians, between two lines or planes that meet at a point, or the inclination of one line to another.
space between intersecting lines or planes

Examples

The two walls meet at a right angle of 90 degrees.
She measured the angle between the two lines using a protractor.
A triangle's internal angles always add up to 180 degrees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vertex - the point where two lines or planes meet
  • Synonym: degree - a unit of measurement for angles

Etymology

From Latin 'angulus' (corner, angle).

5. NOUN

/ ˈæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡəl / (BrE)
a particular way of considering, describing, or presenting a situation, especially one that shows bias or a specific point of view
a particular point of view or bias

Examples

The news article presented the story from a new angle to attract readers.
She always finds an interesting angle to approach her essays.
You need to consider the problem from every possible angle before making a decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perspective - a particular attitude or way of viewing something
  • Synonym: bias - tendency to present information with prejudice for or against something
  • Antonym: objectivity - the quality of being unbiased and impartial

Etymology

From Latin angulus (corner, angle), later adapted figuratively to mean perspective or viewpoint.
