curtal

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɜr.təl / (AmE), / ˈkɜː.təl / (BrE)
(archaic) Having something cut short or docked, especially a tail.
(archaic) docked or cut short
archaic

Examples

The old spaniel had a curtal tail from birth.
In medieval times, a curtal dog was commonly depicted in artwork.
He rode a horse with a curtal mane through the village.

Synset Examples

a dog with a curtal tail

Etymology

From Old French 'courtault', from 'court' (short).
