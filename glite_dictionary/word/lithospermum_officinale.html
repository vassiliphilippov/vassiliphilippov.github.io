Lithospermum officinale

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪθoʊˈspɜrməm ˌɒfɪˈsɪnəli / (AmE), / ˌlɪθəˈspɜːməm ˌɒfɪˈsɪnəli / (BrE)
A European perennial branching plant commonly found in hedgerows and at the edges of woodlands.
a European perennial plant species

Examples

Lithospermum officinale is often found growing at the edge of woodlands in Europe.
Traditionally, Lithospermum officinale was used in herbal remedies for kidney stones.
Gardeners appreciate Lithospermum officinale for its resilience and attractive branching structure.

Etymology

From Greek 'lithos' (stone) + 'sperma' (seed), referring to stone-like seeds; 'officinale' denotes historical use in medicine.
