rockrose

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑkˌroʊz / (AmE), / ˈrɒk.rəʊz / (BrE)
A low, bushy plant of the genus Helianthemum, with small, rose-like yellow, white, or reddish flowers, commonly grown in dry, rocky areas.
type of flowering shrub (Helianthemum)

Examples

The rockrose thrives in dry, rocky soils with plenty of sunlight.
Gardeners often plant rockrose for its attractive and resilient flowers.
A variety of rockrose with reddish petals brightened the stone border.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: helianthemum - genus of flowering plants related to the rockrose

Etymology

From 'rock' + 'rose', describing the plant's rocky habitat and rose-like flowers.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɑkˌroʊz / (AmE), / ˈrɒkˌrəʊz / (BrE)
A small shrub of southern Europe and North Africa, with showy flowers and evergreen, often aromatic foliage.
shrub with showy flowers and aromatic foliage

Examples

The rockrose thrives in dry, rocky soils where few other plants can survive.
Gardeners prize the rockrose for its delicate, papery flowers that bloom abundantly in spring.
A soft fragrance drifted from the rockrose bushes lining the Mediterranean path.

Etymology

Named for its rocky native habitat and rose-like flowers.
