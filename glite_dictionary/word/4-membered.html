4-membered

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɔrˌmɛmbɚd / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌmɛmbəd / (BrE)
(of a chemical compound) containing a ring structure consisting of four atoms or groups.
having a four-membered ring (chemistry)

Examples

The molecule cyclobutane features a 4-membered ring at its core.
Researchers studied several 4-membered heterocycles to understand their reactivity.
Many 4-membered rings display significant strain compared to larger ring systems.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: 5-membered - (of a chemical compound) containing a ring structure with five atoms or groups
  • Degree Lower: 3-membered - (of a chemical compound) containing a ring structure with three atoms or groups
