ruthful

1. ADJ

/ ˈruːθ.fəl / (AmE), / ˈruːθ.fəl / (BrE)
feeling or showing sorrow or pity; full of compassion or remorse, often for offenses or wrongdoing
full of sorrow or pity
archaic

Examples

He gave her a ruthful glance, regretting his harsh words.
Her ruthful expression betrayed a deep sense of guilt for her actions.
In a ruthful mood, he apologized sincerely to those he had wronged.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ruthless - having no pity or compassion

Etymology

From 'ruth' (pity, compassion), from Middle English, ultimately from Old Norse 'hrygð' (sorrow), + '-ful'.
