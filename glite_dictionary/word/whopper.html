whopper

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑːpər / (AmE), / ˈwɒpə / (BrE)
A gross or extravagant lie; a blatant untruth.
a blatant or extravagant lie
informal

Examples

He told a whopper about why he was late to work.
Everyone knew her story was a whopper and laughed out loud.
It's hard to believe he thought we'd fall for such a whopper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lie - a false statement made with deliberate intent to deceive

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɑːpər / (AmE), / ˈwɒpə / (BrE)
something unusually large or impressive of its kind
something very large or impressive

Examples

That fish he caught was a real whopper and impressed everyone on the boat.
Her story about winning the lottery turned out to be a whopper.
The ice cream sundae they served was a whopper that could feed three people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giant - something very large
  • Antonym: minnow - something very small
