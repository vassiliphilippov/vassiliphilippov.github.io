wayside

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈweɪ.saɪd / (BrE)
the edge or side of a road, path, or way
edge of a road or path

Examples

Wildflowers grew along the wayside as we walked through the countryside.
The old bicycle was abandoned by the wayside after it broke down.
Vendors set up small stalls along the wayside to sell fruit to passing travelers.

Synset Examples

flowers along the wayside

Etymology

From Middle English 'way' + 'side', meaning the side of a road.
