synchronization

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪŋ.krə.nəˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɪŋ.krə.naɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the state or process of events or things happening at the same time or moving at the same pace
things occurring at the same time

Examples

The synchronization of the dancers' movements was impressive during the performance.
Modern computers rely on the synchronization of multiple processes to function efficiently.
Proper audio and video synchronization is essential for a good viewing experience.

Synset Examples

the drug produces an increased synchrony of the brain waves

Related Terms

  • Antonym: asynchrony - lack of synchronization; events not happening at the same time
  • Synonym: simultaneity - the quality of two or more things happening at the same time

Etymology

From Greek 'syn-' (together) and 'chronos' (time), via French 'synchronisation'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɪŋ.krə.nəˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɪŋ.krə.naɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of causing two or more things to happen or operate at the same time, especially to indicate the same time
coordinating things to occur together in time

Examples

The synchronization of the dancers' movements created a stunning visual effect on stage.
Proper synchronization of audio and video signals is crucial in film production.
The mission required the precise synchronization of clocks at different research stations.

Synset Examples

the synchronization of their watches was an important preliminary

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coordination - organizing different elements so that they work together effectively
  • Antonym: desynchronization - the process of things becoming out of sync or not operating at the same time

Etymology

From Greek 'synchronos' (occurring at the same time), via French 'synchronisation'.

Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ ˌsɪŋ.krə.nəˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɪŋ.krə.naɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of causing events or actions to happen at the same time or in unison
the act of making things happen together

Examples

The synchronization of the dancers' movements made the performance especially impressive.
Accurate synchronization between the audio and video tracks is essential in film production.
There was a problem with the synchronization of the traffic lights, causing delays during rush hour.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coordination - the organization of different elements to enable them to work together

Etymology

From Greek 'synchronos' (happening at the same time), via French 'synchronisation'.
