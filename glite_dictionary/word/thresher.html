thresher

1. NOUN

/ ˈθrɛʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈθrɛʃə / (BrE)
a machine used on farms to separate seeds or grain from their husks and straw
a machine for separating grain from husks

Examples

The old barn still housed a thresher from the early twentieth century.
Farmers once relied on a steam-powered thresher to process their wheat.
The invention of the thresher revolutionized agriculture by making grain harvesting faster.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: threshing machine - a mechanical device for separating grain from plants

Etymology

From 'thresh' (to separate grain from husks) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈθrɛʃər / (AmE), / ˈθrɛʃə / (BrE)
a large shark of warm seas, known for its long, whiplike tail used to herd and stun small fish
a shark with a long tail

Examples

A thresher can use its powerful tail to stun schools of fish.
Divers were excited to spot a thresher near the coral reef.
Many fisheries try to protect the endangered thresher from overfishing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thresher shark - another term for the same large shark with a long tail

Etymology

From the verb 'thresh', referring to the motion similar to threshing grain.
