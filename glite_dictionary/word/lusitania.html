Lusitania

1. PROPN

/ luːsɪˈteɪniə / (AmE), / luːsɪˈteɪnɪə / (BrE)
an ancient region and former Roman province in the western Iberian Peninsula, corresponding roughly to present-day Portugal and parts of western Spain
ancient Roman province in Iberia

Examples

The Romans annexed Lusitania in the second century BC.
Many archaeological sites in modern Portugal can be traced back to Lusitania.
Ancient Lusitania played a crucial role in the expansion of the Roman Empire.

Etymology

From Latin Lusitania, of uncertain origin, possibly from Lusitani, the name of a local tribe.
