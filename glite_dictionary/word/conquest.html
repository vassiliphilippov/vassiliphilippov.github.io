conquest

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑŋ.kwɛst / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋ.kwɛst / (BrE)
the act of taking control over a place or people by use of military force or by overcoming resistance
the act of conquering

Examples

The conquest of the city marked a turning point in the war.
Centuries of conquest shaped the nation's borders.
Genghis Khan's conquest of vast territories changed the course of history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: victory - success in a contest or struggle over an opponent
  • Antonym: defeat - the act of overcoming or bringing down an opponent

Etymology

From Latin 'conquestus' (to seek, procure), via Old French 'conqueste'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːŋ.kwɛst / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋ.kwɛst / (BrE)
an act of winning someone's love or sexual favor, often implying skill or achievement in seduction
act of winning love or sexual favor

Examples

He often boasted about his latest conquest at the bar.
She was tired of being seen as just another conquest in his long list.
Stories of his romantic conquests quickly spread throughout the town.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seduction - the act of persuading someone into sexual activity

Etymology

From Latin 'conquaestus', past participle of 'conquĕrĕre' (to gain, win, collect).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɑŋ.kwɛst / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋ.kwɛst / (BrE)
the act of achieving success in overcoming something difficult or mastering a challenge
success in mastering something difficult

Examples

The conquest of Mount Everest requires courage, skill, and determination.
Her conquest of her fear of public speaking inspired the whole group.
Technological advances made the conquest of space a reality.

Synset Examples

the conquest of space

Related Terms

  • Synonym: victory - an act of defeating an opponent or overcoming difficulties

Etymology

From Latin 'conquaestus', past participle of 'conquĕrĕre' (to acquire, conquer), via Old French 'conqueste'.
