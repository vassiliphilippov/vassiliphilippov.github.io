Torreya

1. NOUN

/ tɔˈreɪə / (AmE), / tɒˈreɪə / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen trees and shrubs in the yew family, native to parts of Asia and North America, known as nutmeg-yews.
genus of nutmeg-yews (trees/shrubs)

Examples

The Torreya species produces seeds that resemble large nuts and are valued for their oil.
Several Torreya trees can be found in specialized arboretums across the United States.
The endangered Florida Torreya is being cultivated in conservation programs to prevent its extinction.

Etymology

Named after American botanist John Torrey (1796–1873).
