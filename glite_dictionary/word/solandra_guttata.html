Solandra guttata

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈlændrə ɡuːˈtɑːtə / (AmE), / səˈlændrə ɡʊˈtɑːtə / (BrE)
A Mexican evergreen climbing plant with large, solitary, funnel-shaped, fragrant yellow flowers marked with purple-brown ridges in the throat.
Mexican evergreen climbing plant with yellow funnel-shaped flowers

Examples

The large, sweet-scented flowers of Solandra guttata make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
Solandra guttata thrives in warm climates and can climb over trellises and fences.
Gardeners appreciate Solandra guttata for its striking yellow blossoms accented with purple-brown markings.

Etymology

From the genus name Solandra, honoring Swedish botanist Daniel C. Solander, and Latin guttata (spotted).
