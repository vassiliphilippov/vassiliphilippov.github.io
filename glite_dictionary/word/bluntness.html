bluntness

1. NOUN

/ ˈblʌnt.nəs / (AmE), / ˈblʌnt.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of lacking a sharp or clear edge or point.
the state of being dull or not sharp

Examples

Over time, the bluntness of the kitchen knife made it difficult to slice tomatoes.
The bluntness of the chisel resulted in messy carvings.
Repeated use led to the bluntness of his favorite drawing pencil.

Synset Examples

the dullness of the pencil made his writing illegible

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dullness - the state of being not sharp, as of an edge or point

Etymology

From 'blunt' + '-ness'. 'Blunt' derives from Middle English 'blont', probably of Scandinavian origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈblʌnt.nəs / (AmE), / ˈblʌnt.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being direct, frank, and uncompromising in speech or manner, sometimes to the point of rudeness
directness or frankness in speech

Examples

Her bluntness sometimes offended colleagues who preferred a more diplomatic approach.
He admired the bluntness of her answer, even if it stung a little.
Despite his bluntness, people respected his honesty and straightforwardness.

Synset Examples

the bluntness of a Yorkshireman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frankness - the quality of being open, honest, and direct in speech
  • Antonym: tact - skill and sensitivity in dealing with others or with difficult issues

Etymology

From 'blunt' (direct, not sharp) + '-ness' (forming nouns indicating a quality or state).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04853614-n
Synset POS: n
