mephobarbital

1. NOUN

/ ˌmiːfoʊˈbɑːrbɪtɔːl / (AmE), / ˌmiːfəʊˈbɑːbɪtɒl / (BrE)
a long-acting barbiturate drug used as a sedative and to treat epilepsy.
barbiturate drug used for epilepsy and sedation
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed mephobarbital to help control her seizure disorder.
Mephobarbital is less commonly used today due to the availability of safer medications.
Patients taking mephobarbital should be monitored for side effects such as drowsiness and dependence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barbiturate - a class of drugs acting as central nervous system depressants
  • Synonym: phenobarbital - another long-acting barbiturate used to treat epilepsy and as a sedative

Etymology

From 'methyl' and 'phenyl' plus 'barbital', indicating its chemical structure.
