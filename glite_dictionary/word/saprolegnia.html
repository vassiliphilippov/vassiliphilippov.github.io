Saprolegnia

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæprəˈlɛɡniə / (AmE), / ˌsæprəˈliːɡnɪə / (BrE)
a genus of aquatic fungi that typically grows on decaying plant material and animal remains
genus of aquatic fungi

Examples

Saprolegnia infections are commonly found on the skin of freshwater fish.
Microscopists observed the distinctive branching pattern of Saprolegnia under the microscope.
The decaying leaves in the pond provided a perfect habitat for Saprolegnia to grow.

Etymology

From New Latin Saprolegnia, formed from Greek sapro- (rotten) + lêgnion (small wooden vessel).
