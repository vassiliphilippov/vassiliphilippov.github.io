augmentative

1. ADJ

/ ɔgˈmɛntətɪv / (AmE), / ɔːgˈmɛntətɪv / (BrE)
serving to increase or enhance the size, value, or intensity of something
serving to increase or enhance

Examples

The linguist explained that some languages use an augmentative suffix to form words meaning 'large' or 'great'.
Her voice took on an augmentative tone as she emphasized the key points of her argument.
Adding a powerful amplifier had an augmentative effect on the sound system's output.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: diminutive - serving to reduce the size, value, or intensity of something

Etymology

From Latin augmentativus, from augmentare (to increase).

2. ADJ

/ ɔgˈmɛntətɪv / (AmE), / ɔːgˈmɛntətɪv / (BrE)
serving to increase size, amount, or degree; having the power to make something greater
having an increasing effect

Examples

The prefix 'super-' is often used in an augmentative sense, meaning 'greater' or 'above'.
Certain words in Spanish use augmentative endings to express a larger size or degree.
Her tone took on an augmentative quality, growing more forceful with every word.

Synset Examples

‘up’ is an augmentative word in ‘hurry up’

Related Terms

  • Antonym: diminutive - serving to make something smaller or lesser in size or importance

Etymology

From Latin 'augmentativus', from 'augmentare' (to increase).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
