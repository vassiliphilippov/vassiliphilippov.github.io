auriculate

1. ADJ

/ ɔːˈrɪkjələt / (AmE), / ɔːˈrɪkjʊlət / (BrE)
having ear-shaped appendages or lobes (auricles), especially as applied to leaves, petals, or anatomical structures
having ear-shaped lobes or appendages
medical

Examples

The plant's leaves are auriculate, forming small lobes at the base resembling ears.
Some mollusks have auriculate shells distinguished by ear-like extensions.
The botanist noted the auriculate structure at the leaf base in her report.

Etymology

From Latin 'auriculatus' (having ears or auricles), from 'auricula' (little ear).
