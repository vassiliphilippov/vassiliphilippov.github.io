peculiarly

1. ADV

/ pɪˈkjuljərli / (AmE), / pɪˈkjuːliəli / (BrE)
In a manner that is strange, unusual, or not typical.
in an unusual or strange manner

Examples

He always reacted peculiarly to stressful situations, often surprising everyone.
The room was decorated peculiarly, with mismatched furniture and bright colors.
She smiled peculiarly when asked about her weekend plans.

Synset Examples

had a curiously husky voice
he's behaving rather peculiarly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strangely - in a strange or unusual way
  • Antonym: normally - in a normal or expected way

Etymology

From 'peculiar' + '-ly', with 'peculiar' from Latin 'peculiaris' (of one's own).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00035878-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ pɪˈkjuljɚli / (AmE), / pɪˈkjuːliəli / (BrE)
in a way that is unique or characteristic of a particular person, place, or thing
in a unique or characteristic manner

Examples

The painting was executed in a peculiarly modern style that set it apart from earlier works.
She reacted in a peculiarly calm manner, considering the circumstances.
The festival has a peculiarly local charm that attracts visitors every year.

Synset Examples

these peculiarly cinematic elements
a peculiarly French phenomenon
everyone has a moment in history which belongs particularly to him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uniquely - in a way that is one of a kind
  • Synonym: characteristically - in a way that is typical of a particular person or thing

Etymology

Formed from 'peculiar' (from Latin 'peculiaris', meaning 'of private property') + '-ly' adverb suffix.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00249967-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ pɪˈkjuljɚli / (AmE), / pɪˈkjuːliəli / (BrE)
to a distinctly greater degree than is usual or expected; especially or particularly
to an unusual or special degree

Examples

The room was peculiarly quiet after everyone left.
She smiled peculiarly at the unexpected compliment.
The storm took a peculiarly destructive path through the town.

Synset Examples

he was particularly fussy about spelling
a particularly gruesome attack
under peculiarly tragic circumstances
an especially (or specially) cautious approach to the danger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: particularly - to a higher degree than is usual or expected
  • Synonym: especially - to a special degree; more than others

Etymology

From peculiar + -ly, ultimately from Latin 'peculiaris' (of private property).
