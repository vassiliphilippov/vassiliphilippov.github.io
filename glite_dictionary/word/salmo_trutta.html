Salmo trutta

1. NOUN

/ ˈsælmoʊ ˈtrʊtə / (AmE), / ˈsalməʊ ˈtrʊtə / (BrE)
A species of speckled brown trout native to European rivers and introduced to North America, prized for sport fishing.
species of brown trout

Examples

Anglers travel from afar hoping to catch a massive Salmo trutta in the river.
The Salmo trutta population has flourished since its introduction to North American streams.
Scientists are studying Salmo trutta to monitor the health of freshwater ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brown trout - common name for Salmo trutta, a species of trout

Etymology

From scientific Latin, genus 'Salmo' (salmon) and species 'trutta' (trout), both derived from Latin.
