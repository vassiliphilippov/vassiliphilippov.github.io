Apoidea

1. NOUN

/ ˌæpɔɪˈdiːə / (AmE), / ˌapɔɪˈdɪə / (BrE)
A superfamily of insects including bees and their close relatives within the order Hymenoptera.
insect superfamily including bees

Examples

The superfamily Apoidea includes all true bees and their closest relatives.
Scientists study the evolution of Apoidea to better understand pollinator diversity.
Most species within the Apoidea are known for their role in pollination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bee - common name for insects in superfamily Apoidea
  • Synonym: wasp - insect closely related to bees but not in all Apoidea members

Etymology

From Greek 'apo-' (bee) + 'eidos' (form, shape).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæpoʊˈaɪdiə / (AmE), / ˌapɔɪˈɪdɪə / (BrE)
A superfamily of insects within the order Hymenoptera that includes bees and some related wasps.
taxonomic superfamily of bees and relatives

Examples

Scientists have classified all true bees within the Apoidea superfamily.
Many members of Apoidea are important pollinators in natural ecosystems.
The evolutionary relationships within Apoidea have been studied extensively using genetic data.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bee - insect belonging to the superfamily Apoidea

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek apo- (bee) + -oidea (resembling, pertaining to).
