germander

1. NOUN

/ dʒərˈmændər / (AmE), / dʒəˈmændə / (BrE)
any of various plants of the genus Teucrium, often with aromatic leaves and sometimes used medicinally or ornamentally
plant of the genus Teucrium

Examples

The herbalist recommended using germander as part of the remedy for sore throats.
Gardeners often plant germander for its decorative flowers and pleasant scent.
Some species of germander are known to attract bees and butterflies to the garden.

Etymology

From Middle English germaunder, from Old French germandree, from Medieval Latin germandrea, from Greek khamaidrus, meaning 'ground oak'.
