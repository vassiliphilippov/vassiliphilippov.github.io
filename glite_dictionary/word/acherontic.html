Acherontic

1. ADJ

/ ˌækəˈrɒntɪk / (AmE), / ˌækəˈrɒntɪk / (BrE)
Dark, dismal, or gloomy, especially as reminiscent of the rivers Acheron and Styx in the Greek underworld.
gloomy like the rivers in Hades

Examples

The old castle had an Acherontic gloom that unsettled every visitor who entered its halls.
Legends spoke of Acherontic depths beneath the earth, where no light could ever reach.
His poetry often conjured an Acherontic atmosphere, filled with spectral shadows and mournful echoes.

Synset Examples

in the depths of an Acheronian forest
upon those roseate lips a Stygian hue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Stygian - dark and gloomy, especially as related to the river Styx
  • Synonym: infernal - relating to or characteristic of hell or the underworld

Etymology

From Acheron, a river in Greek mythology believed to lead to the underworld, plus the suffix '-ic'.
