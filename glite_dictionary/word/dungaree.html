dungaree

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʌŋɡəˈri / (AmE), / ˌdʌŋɡəˈriː / (BrE)
A coarse, durable twill-weave cotton fabric, typically used for work clothes and overalls.
coarse twill-weave cotton fabric

Examples

Workers preferred dungaree for its strength and resistance to wear in tough environments.
The overalls were made from blue dungaree, giving them a rugged appearance.
She chose dungaree fabric for the durability it would provide her homemade apron.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: denim - a sturdy cotton twill fabric, similar to dungaree

Etymology

From Hindi 'dūngarī', a type of coarse calico, originally from an area near Mumbai.
