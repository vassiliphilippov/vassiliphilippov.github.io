export

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈspɔrt / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɔːt / (BrE)
to transfer electronic data out of a database or document, typically into a format that can be used by other programs
transfer data out in a usable format

Examples

You can export your contacts from this app as a CSV file.
Before upgrading the system, remember to export all your important documents.
The data was exported from the database for analysis in another program.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: import - to bring data into a database or program from another format

Etymology

From Latin 'exportare' (to carry out).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02237659-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈɛksˌpɔrt / (AmE), / ˈɛk.spɔːt / (BrE)
to introduce or spread (an idea, activity, etc.) to another country or place
spread an idea to another country

Examples

The country tried to export its political system to neighboring states.
Hollywood movies have exported Western culture around the globe.
Religious groups sometimes export their beliefs to new regions.

Synset Examples

The Russians exported Marxism to Africa

Etymology

From Latin 'exportare', meaning 'to carry out'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01381762-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈɛkˌspɔrt / (AmE), / ˈɛkˌspɔːt / (BrE)
a commodity, product, or service sold and sent to another country
goods/services sent to another country for sale

Examples

Oil is one of the country's main exports.
The company increased its exports to Asia last year.
Wheat is a major export for Canada.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: import - goods or services bought from another country
  • Synonym: exportation - the act of exporting goods

Etymology

From Latin exportare, from ex- 'out' + portare 'to carry'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03311152-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ ɪkˈspɔrt / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɔːt / (BrE)
to send goods or services to another country for sale or trade
send goods abroad for sale

Examples

Last year, the company exported over a million tons of steel to Asia.
Many countries export agricultural products to generate foreign income.
They are planning to export their software to new international markets next year.

Synset Examples

we export less than we import and have a negative trade balance

Related Terms

  • Antonym: import - to bring goods or services into a country from abroad for sale
  • Synonym: ship - to send goods by a form of transport, especially by sea

Etymology

From Latin 'exportare', meaning 'to carry out'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02351405-v
Synset POS: v
