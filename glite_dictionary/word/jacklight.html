jacklight

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒækˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈdʒæk.laɪt / (BrE)
A portable light used at night to attract or locate animals when hunting or fishing.
night light used for hunting or fishing

Examples

Hunters often use a jacklight to spot deer in the dark woods.
The illegal use of a jacklight for night hunting is heavily fined in some states.
He fashioned a makeshift jacklight to attract fish to the surface of the lake.

Etymology

From 'jack' (a portable device) + 'light'; historically, 'jack' referred to various mechanical devices or tools.

2. VERB

/ ˈdʒæk.laɪt / (AmE), / ˈdʒæk.laɪt / (BrE)
to hunt animals at night by shining a bright light to dazzle and temporarily blind them, making them easier to shoot.
hunt animals at night with a bright light

Examples

It is illegal to jacklight deer in many states.
The hunters jacklighted rabbits in the woods last night.
They were arrested for jacklighting game out of season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spotlight - to shine a strong light on a person or animal, usually to make them visible for hunting or observation

Etymology

From 'jacklight', a portable light used for hunting at night.
