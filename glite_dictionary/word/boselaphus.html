Boselaphus

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈsɛl.ə.fəs / (AmE), / bəʊˈsɛl.ə.fəs / (BrE)
A genus of large antelopes native to South Asia, including the nilgai.
genus of South Asian antelopes

Examples

The Boselaphus genus is primarily represented by the nilgai in India.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Boselaphus populations in protected reserves.
Taxonomists classified the species under the genus Boselaphus due to its distinct characteristics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nilgai - a large antelope native to the Indian subcontinent

Etymology

From Greek 'bous' (ox) + 'elaphos' (deer).

2. NOUN

/ boʊsəˈlæfəs / (AmE), / bəʊsəˈlæfəs / (BrE)
A genus of large antelopes native to South Asia, including the nilgai.
a genus of South Asian antelopes

Examples

The Boselaphus genus is best known for its only living species, the nilgai.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of animals in the Boselaphus genus.
The Boselaphus is native to India and parts of Pakistan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nilgai - a large Asian antelope, the only species in the genus Boselaphus

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek bous (ox) and elaphos (deer).
