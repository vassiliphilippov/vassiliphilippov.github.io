to convince

1. VERB

/ kənˈvɪns / (AmE), / kənˈvɪns / (BrE)
to cause someone to believe that something is true or to persuade someone to do something
persuade someone of the truth

Examples

It took me several hours to convince her to join the team.
The lawyer tried to convince the jury of his client's innocence.
You will have to convince me that your plan is safe before I agree.

Synset Examples

He had finally convinced several customers of the advantages of his product

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to persuade - to cause someone to do or believe something by reasoning or urging
  • Antonym: to dissuade - to persuade someone not to take a particular course of action

Etymology

From Latin convincere (to overcome, to prove conclusively), from com- (together) + vincere (to conquer).
