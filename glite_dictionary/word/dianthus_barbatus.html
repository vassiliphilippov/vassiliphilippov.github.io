Dianthus barbatus

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈænθəs bɑːrˈbeɪtəs / (AmE), / daɪˈænθəs bɑːˈbeɪtəs / (BrE)
A species of Eurasian pink (commonly known as Sweet William), widely cultivated for its flat-topped dense clusters of multicolored flowers.
a flowering plant; Sweet William

Examples

The vibrant blooms of Dianthus barbatus make it a popular choice in cottage gardens.
She planted Dianthus barbatus along the walkway to add splashes of color.
Many gardeners appreciate the fragrance and variety of colors found in Dianthus barbatus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sweet William - common name for Dianthus barbatus, a cultivated garden flower

Etymology

From Latin: 'Dianthus', from Greek dios (divine) + anthos (flower); 'barbatus' meaning 'bearded' in Latin.
