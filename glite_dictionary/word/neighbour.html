neighbour

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪbər / (AmE), / ˈneɪbə / (BrE)
a person living near or next to another person; someone who lives close by
person living nearby
british

Examples

Our new neighbour brought us a cake to welcome us to the area.
He often helps his elderly neighbour with shopping and chores.
A dispute with a neighbour can sometimes turn into a long-lasting feud.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neighbor - American English spelling of 'neighbour'
  • Antonym: stranger - a person whom one does not know

Etymology

From Old English *nēahgebūr* (nēah ‘near’ + gebūr ‘dweller’).

2. NOUN

/ ˈneɪbər / (AmE), / ˈneɪbə / (BrE)
an object or entity that is located near another of the same kind
nearby object or entity
british

Examples

Venus is considered Earth's closest neighbour in the solar system.
The museum is a neighbour of the city library in the downtown area.
Saturn’s largest moon, Titan, is a neighbour to several smaller moons.

Synset Examples

Fort Worth is a neighbor of Dallas
what is the closest neighbor to the Earth?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neighbor - American spelling of 'neighbour'

Etymology

From Old English nēahgebūr, meaning 'near dweller'.

/ ˈneɪbər / (AmE), / ˈneɪbə / (BrE)

british

The school neighbours a quaint little bakery frequented by students.
