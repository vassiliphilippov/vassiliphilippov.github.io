brooch

1. NOUN

/ broʊtʃ / (AmE), / brəʊtʃ / (BrE)
A decorative jewelry pin, often worn on clothing, especially by women, as an ornament.
decorative jewelry pin

Examples

She fastened the silk scarf with a beautiful antique brooch.
For her birthday, he gave her a silver brooch shaped like a butterfly.
The museum exhibit included a Victorian-era brooch adorned with tiny pearls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pin - a small device, often with a sharp point, used to fasten things together

Etymology

From Old French 'broche' (a long needle), from Latin 'brocchus' (projecting, snaggle-toothed).
