kenotron

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛn.əˌtrɑn / (AmE), / ˈkɛn.əˌtrɒn / (BrE)
an early type of vacuum tube used as a high-voltage rectifier, especially in X-ray equipment and power supplies.
type of vacuum tube rectifier

Examples

The engineer replaced the faulty kenotron to restore the X-ray machine to working condition.
Early televisions sometimes relied on a kenotron in their high-voltage power supplies.
Because of advances in technology, the kenotron is now mostly a historical device.

Synset Examples

Vacuum rectifiers were made for very high voltages, such as the high voltage power supply for the cathode ray tube of television receivers, and the kenotron used for power supply in X-ray equipment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rectifier - a device that converts alternating current (AC) to direct current (DC)

Etymology

From Greek 'kenos' (empty) and 'tron' (device or instrument), indicating an evacuated tube device.
