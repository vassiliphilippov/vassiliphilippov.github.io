Phasmidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæzmɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæzmɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of insects commonly known as stick insects, characterized by their elongated bodies and camouflage that resembles twigs or sticks.
family of stick insects
medical

Examples

The Phasmidae have developed remarkable camouflage to avoid predators in their natural habitats.
Scientists studying Phasmidae have discovered new species in the rainforests of Southeast Asia.
Members of the Phasmidae family can sometimes regrow lost limbs during their development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stick insect - common name for insects of the family Phasmidae

Etymology

From New Latin 'Phasmidae', based on Greek 'phasma' (apparition, phantom).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfæzmɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæzmɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of insects commonly known as stick insects, characterized by their elongated bodies and resemblance to twigs or branches
family of stick insects

Examples

The Phasmidae are masters of camouflage, often remaining motionless to avoid predators.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Phasmidae family in the rainforests of Borneo.
Many children are fascinated by the unusual appearance of insects belonging to Phasmidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stick insect - insect with a long, thin body resembling a stick

Etymology

From modern Latin Phasmidae, based on Greek phasma (phantom or apparition), referring to their camouflage abilities.
