Artemisia vulgaris

1. NOUN

/ ɑːr.təˈmɪʒ.i.ə vʌlˈɡɛr.ɪs / (AmE), / ˌɑː.tɪˈmɪz.i.ə vʌlˈɡɑː.rɪs / (BrE)
A European perennial herb with hairy red or purple stems, dark green leaves white underneath, and red-brown florets; commonly known as mugwort.
European aromatic herb, known as mugwort

Examples

The herbalist recommended Artemisia vulgaris as a natural remedy for digestive issues.
You can find Artemisia vulgaris growing wild along the edges of European fields.
Traditional medicine often uses dried Artemisia vulgaris leaves in teas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mugwort - Common English name for Artemisia vulgaris

Etymology

From Latin, where Artemisia refers to a genus of plants and vulgaris means 'common'.
