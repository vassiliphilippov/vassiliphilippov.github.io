Anchusa capensis

1. NOUN

/ ænˈkuːsə kəˈpɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ænˈkjuːsə kəˈpɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A species of flowering plant native to southern Africa, known for its bright blue flowers with white centers.
species of flowering plant from southern Africa

Examples

Anchusa capensis is often grown in gardens for its striking blue flowers and easy maintenance.
The vibrant blooms of Anchusa capensis attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
Gardeners favor Anchusa capensis for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soils.

Etymology

From New Latin, where 'Anchusa' is the genus name (from Greek 'anchousa', a plant used as a cosmetic dye) and 'capensis' meaning 'of the Cape' (referring to the Cape of Good Hope).
