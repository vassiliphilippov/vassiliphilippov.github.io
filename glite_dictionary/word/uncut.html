uncut

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkʌt / (AmE), / ʌnˈkʌt / (BrE)
not trimmed, shortened, or cut; left in its original length or state.
not trimmed or shortened

Examples

He wore his hair uncut for several months, letting it grow long and wild.
The grass in the field remained uncut throughout autumn, swaying in the breeze.
She admired the natural look of uncut hedges along the old country road.

Synset Examples

shaggy untrimmed locks

Related Terms

  • Antonym: trimmed - cut to make neat or shorter

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'cut'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02464103-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkʌt / (AmE), / ʌnˈkʌt / (BrE)
(of the pages of a book) having some leaves still joined at the fore edge, not yet separated by cutting.
pages still joined in a book

Examples

The collector was thrilled to find a first edition with several uncut pages.
He used a letter opener to separate the uncut leaves of the old volume.
Early printings often arrived uncut, requiring the reader to slice open the edges by hand.

Synset Examples

a book with its leaves still uncut

Etymology

From un- (not) + cut; 'cut' in bookmaking refers to separating joined pages.
