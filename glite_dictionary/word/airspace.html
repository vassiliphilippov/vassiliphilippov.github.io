airspace

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛrˌspeɪs / (AmE), / ˈeəˌspeɪs / (BrE)
The part of the atmosphere above a particular area of the earth, especially that which is subject to the jurisdiction of a state.
space in the atmosphere above land or sea

Examples

All commercial flights must obtain permission before entering a country's airspace.
The government announced restrictions over the city’s airspace during the parade.
Birds sometimes fly at altitudes higher than the airspace used by planes.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛrˌspeɪs / (AmE), / ˈeəˌspeɪs / (BrE)
the part of the atmosphere above a particular country that is considered under its jurisdiction and control
atmosphere above a nation's territory

Examples

The pilot requested clearance before entering airspace controlled by another country.
Military jets were scrambled when an unknown aircraft entered national airspace.
There are strict regulations for foreign airlines flying over a country's airspace.

Synset Examples

the plane was refused permission to enter Chinese airspace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: territorial waters - the area of sea considered under a country's jurisdiction

3. NOUN

/ ˈɛrˌspeɪs / (AmE), / ˈeəˌspeɪs / (BrE)
the part of the atmosphere directly above a particular area of land, especially as controlled by a country
the atmosphere above a region; especially for aviation

Examples

A country's airspace is protected by international law and cannot be entered without permission.
Commercial planes must receive clearance before entering another nation's airspace.
Satellite images can be used to monitor activity within restricted airspace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: territorial waters - the waters under the jurisdiction of a state
