crippled

1. ADJ

/ ˈkrɪpəld / (AmE), / ˈkrɪpəld / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) disabled in the feet or legs, especially to the extent of being unable to walk.
disabled in the feet or legs
offensive

Examples

The crippled veteran moved slowly with the aid of a cane.
She cared deeply for abandoned and crippled animals at the shelter.
He was crippled after the accident and could no longer climb stairs.

Synset Examples

a crippled soldier
a game leg

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lame - unable to walk properly because of injury or disability in the leg or foot

Etymology

From Old English 'crypel' or 'cripel', related to 'creopan' (to creep).
