Tisiphone

1. PROPN

/ tɪˈsɪfəni / (AmE), / tɪˈsɪfəni / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, one of the three Furies (Erinyes), responsible for punishing crimes of murder.
mythological Fury punishing murder

Examples

In Greek tragedies, Tisiphone is invoked as a relentless pursuer of the guilty.
Literature often depicts Tisiphone as a symbol of unyielding vengeance for homicide.
The legend says Tisiphone haunted those who committed the crime of murder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Erinyes - the three goddesses of vengeance in Greek mythology
  • Synonym: Alecto - one of the three Furies in Greek mythology
  • Synonym: Megaera - one of the three Furies in Greek mythology

Etymology

From Greek Τισιφόνη (Tisiphonē), meaning 'avenging murder.'
