warrant

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔːrənt / (AmE), / ˈwɒrənt / (BrE)
a security issued by a company that gives the holder the right to purchase shares of stock at a specified price, usually within a certain time period
right to buy shares at set price

Examples

The company issued warrants with their new bonds to attract more investors.
She exercised her warrants to buy shares at a lower price than the current market value.
Many investors view warrants as a way to speculate on a company's future growth potential.

Synset Examples

as a sweetener they offered warrants along with the fixed-income securities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: option - a contract giving the right to buy or sell an asset at a future date at an agreed price
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13439392-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔɹənt / (AmE), / ˈwɒɹənt / (BrE)
formal or official authorization or approval for an action or policy.
formal authorization or approval

Examples

The committee's decision carries the official warrant for the project to proceed.
Her actions were taken with the full warrant of the university administration.
No employee should act without the proper warrant from management.

Synset Examples

a Democrat usually gets the union's endorsement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endorsement - formal support or approval for something or someone
  • Synonym: authorization - official permission or approval
  • Antonym: prohibition - the action of forbidding something by law or authority

Etymology

From Old French 'garant' (protector, defender), from Frankish 'warand' (warrant, safeguard).

/ ˈwɔːrənt / (AmE), / ˈwɒrənt / (BrE)

justify or make necessary

Examples

  • Antonym: to prohibit - to formally forbid by law, rule, or other authority

6. NOUN

/ ˈwɔːrənt / (AmE), / ˈwɒrənt / (BrE)
an official document issued by a court that authorizes police or other officials to perform certain actions, such as making an arrest, conducting a search, or carrying out other acts
official court order allowing police action
legal

Examples

The police obtained a warrant to search the suspect's house.
Without a warrant, the officers could not legally enter the premises.
A judge signed the warrant authorizing her arrest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subpoena - legal document ordering someone to attend court
  • Synonym: summons - official order to appear in court

Etymology

From Old French *garant* (protector, defender), from Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet
