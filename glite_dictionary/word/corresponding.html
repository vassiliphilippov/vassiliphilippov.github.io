corresponding

1. ADJ

/ ˌkɔːrəˈspɑːndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌkɒrəˈspɒndɪŋ / (BrE)
similar or matching in character, position, function, or purpose; analogous or equivalent
matching or analogous in position, function, etc.

Examples

The teacher pointed out the corresponding angles in the two triangles.
Each department moved to its corresponding location in the new building.
Her responsibilities are corresponding to those of her colleague in the main office.

Synset Examples

a number of corresponding diagonal points

Related Terms

  • Synonym: analogous - similar in some way; comparable
  • Antonym: different - not the same as another; dissimilar

Etymology

From Latin 'correspondere' (to answer together, to correspond).

2. ADJ

/ ˌkɔrəˈspɑndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌkɒrɪˈspɒndɪŋ / (BrE)
having a close similarity, match, or equivalence with something else; conforming in every detail.
having a close match or equivalence

Examples

The samples were placed in corresponding containers for analysis.
Students in each grade were given corresponding assignments to complete.
The map uses colors to highlight corresponding regions in each country.

Synset Examples

boxes with corresponding dimensions
the like period of the preceding year

Related Terms

  • Synonym: matching - having the same qualities or characteristics
  • Antonym: different - not the same as another or each other

Etymology

From Latin correspondere (to answer together), via Old French correspondre.

3. ADJ

/ ˌkɔr.əˈspɑn.dɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌkɒr.ɪˈspɒn.dɪŋ / (BrE)
matching, accompanying, or related to something else, especially so that one thing balances or is connected with another.
matching; accompanying; relating to another

Examples

All rights carry with them corresponding responsibilities.
Please check that the bills are filed in the corresponding folders.
The increase in temperature resulted in a corresponding rise in humidity.

Synset Examples

all rights carry with them corresponding responsibilities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: matching - being the same or suitable in some way
  • Antonym: contradictory - expressing the opposite or in conflict

Etymology

From Latin 'correspondere' (to answer together), via Old French and Middle English.
