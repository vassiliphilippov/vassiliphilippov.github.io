amuck

1. ADV

/ əˈmʌk / (AmE), / əˈmʌk / (BrE)
in a wildly violent or murderous frenzy; behaving uncontrollably and destructively, especially with sudden violence.
in a violent, uncontrollable frenzy

Examples

During the uprising, the crowd ran amuck through the narrow streets, breaking windows and overturning cars.
Rumors spread quickly, causing panic as people imagined thieves running amuck in the night.
Left unchecked, their emotions could easily run amuck in times of crisis.

Synset Examples

rioters running amuck and throwing sticks and bottles and stones

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amok - in a murderous or violent frenzy (alternative spelling)

Etymology

From Malay *amok*, meaning 'frenzied attack' or 'furious assault'.

/ əˈmʌk / (AmE), / əˈmʌk / (BrE)
in a state of wild frenzy and out of control; behaving in a violent or frenzied manner
wildly frenzied; out of control

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms
