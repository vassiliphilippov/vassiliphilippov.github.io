vocalisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌvoʊkələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvəʊkələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The sound produced when air is expelled through the vocal cords and shaped by the resonance of the vocal tract; the act or process of using the voice to make sounds.
sound produced by the voice

Examples

Birds use vocalisation to communicate with each other, especially during mating season.
The scientist recorded the dolphin's vocalisation for further analysis.
Loss of vocalisation is a symptom seen in some neurological disorders.

Synset Examples

a singer takes good care of his voice
the giraffe cannot make any vocalizations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voice - the sound produced in a person's larynx and uttered through the mouth
  • Antonym: silence - the absence of any sound or noise

Etymology

From Latin 'vocālis' (vocal) and the suffix '-ation', via French.
