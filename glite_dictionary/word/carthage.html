Carthage

1. PROPN

/ ˈkɑrθɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɑːθɪdʒ / (BrE)
an ancient city-state on the north African coast near present-day Tunis, founded by the Phoenicians and later destroyed and rebuilt by the Romans, eventually razed by the Arabs in 697 CE
ancient city-state near modern Tunis

Examples

Historians still study the rise and fall of Carthage as a powerful Mediterranean city-state.
The ruins of Carthage attract archaeologists and tourists from around the world.
After the Punic Wars, Carthage was completely destroyed by the Romans.

Etymology

From Latin Carthago, from Greek Karthago, from Phoenician Qart-ḥadašt (New City).
