swiz

1. NOUN

/ swɪz / (AmE), / swɪz / (BrE)
(British, slang) A deception, swindle, or trick; something that cheats someone out of what is fair.
British slang for a swindle or cheat
slang british

Examples

The concert being cancelled at the last minute felt like a real swiz.
I paid extra for priority shipping, but that turned out to be a swiz.
He warned me that the travel competition was probably a swiz.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swindle - An act of cheating or fraud, especially for financial gain
  • Synonym: scam - A dishonest scheme or fraud
