remilitarisation

1. NOUN

/ riːˌmɪlɪtəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / riːˌmɪlɪtəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of restoring military forces or capabilities to a region, country, or organization, especially after they have previously been removed or restricted
restoring military forces to a region

Examples

The remilitarisation of the region sparked international controversy.
After years of peace, the government announced the remilitarisation of the border area.
Historians debate the consequences of Germany's remilitarisation of the Rhineland in 1936.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: demilitarisation - the removal of military forces or capabilities from a region

Etymology

From re- (again) + militarisation, ultimately from Latin *militaris* (of soldiers).
