lawrencium

1. NOUN

/ lɔˈrɛnsiəm / (AmE), / lɔːˈrɛnsiəm / (BrE)
a radioactive chemical element with the symbol Lr and atomic number 103, artificially produced by bombarding californium with boron ions.
a synthetic radioactive element (Lr, atomic number 103)

Examples

The discovery of lawrencium expanded the list of known transuranic elements.
Researchers synthesized lawrencium by bombarding californium targets with boron ions.
Due to its radioactivity, lawrencium has no practical applications outside scientific research.

Etymology

Named after the American physicist Ernest O. Lawrence.
