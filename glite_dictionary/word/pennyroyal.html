pennyroyal

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛniˌrɔɪəl / (AmE), / ˈpɛniˌrɔɪəl / (BrE)
an aromatic herb of the mint family with small purple-blue flowers, used for its essential oil as an insect repellent and sometimes in traditional medicine
aromatic herb of the mint family

Examples

The essential oil of pennyroyal is often used to repel insects naturally.
She planted pennyroyal along the garden path for its pleasant scent and medicinal uses.
Some traditional remedies use pennyroyal leaves in herbal teas, though caution is advised.

Etymology

From Middle English *penyroyal*, from Old French *puliol real*, from Latin *puleium regium*, meaning royal flea herb.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛniˌrɔɪəl / (AmE), / ˈpɛniˌrɔɪəl / (BrE)
A small Eurasian perennial mint (Mentha pulegium) with ovate leaves and small lilac-blue flowers, yielding an aromatic oil.
aromatic mint plant and its oil

Examples

The gardener planted pennyroyal along the edge of the herb bed for its fragrant scent.
Some people use pennyroyal oil in traditional remedies, but it can be toxic if ingested.
Clusters of lilac-blue flowers made the pennyroyal stand out among the other herbs.

Etymology

Middle English, from Old English *puliol royal*, influenced by Anglo-Norman *puliol real* (royal puliol, the plant).
