incisure

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsɪʒər / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɪʃə / (BrE)
(Anatomy) A notch, cleft, or small indentation, particularly in the edge or surface of a structure, such as a bone or organ.
anatomical notch or small hollow
medical

Examples

The mandibular incisure is a small notch on the lower jawbone.
Several nerves pass through the scapular incisure in the shoulder blade.
The dental student pointed out the incisure on the anatomical model.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: notch - a V-shaped or U-shaped indentation

Etymology

From Latin incisura (a cutting into, notch), from incidere (to cut into).
