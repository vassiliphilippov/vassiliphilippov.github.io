to misgovern

1. VERB

/ mɪsˈɡʌvərn / (AmE), / mɪsˈɡʌvən / (BrE)
to govern or manage a country, organization, or people in a poor or improper way
govern badly

Examples

The regime continued to misgovern the country, leading to widespread discontent.
If leaders misgovern, it is the people who suffer the consequences most.
She accused the administration of having misgoverned for over a decade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to govern - exercise authority over; rule
  • Synonym: to mismanage - manage wrongly or badly
  • Antonym: govern well - to govern in a proper or effective manner

Etymology

From prefix 'mis-' (badly, wrongly) + 'govern', from Old French 'governer'.
