Kakatoe galerita

1. NOUN

/ kæˈkɑtoʊ ɡæləˈriːtə / (AmE), / kaˈkɑːtəʊ ˌɡæləˈriːtə / (BrE)
a large white cockatoo with a yellow, erectile crest, native to Australia and nearby regions; also known as the sulphur-crested cockatoo
white cockatoo with yellow crest

Examples

The Kakatoe galerita is well known for its loud screech and playful behavior.
In the wild, flocks of Kakatoe galerita can cause damage to crops in Australia.
Many bird enthusiasts keep the Kakatoe galerita as an intelligent and affectionate pet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sulphur-crested cockatoo - common name for Kakatoe galerita

Etymology

From Malay 'kakaktua' (cockatoo) and Neo-Latin genus name 'Kakatoe'.
