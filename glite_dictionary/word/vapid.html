vapid

1. ADJ

/ ˈvæpɪd / (AmE), / ˈvapɪd / (BrE)
lacking interest, liveliness, or spirit; dull or insipid.
lacking liveliness or interest

Examples

The movie was so vapid that I struggled to stay awake until the end.
She gave a vapid smile, betraying her lack of genuine interest.
Many critics found the novel vapid, with little to engage the reader.

Synset Examples

a vapid conversation
a vapid smile
a bunch of vapid schoolgirls

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insipid - lacking flavor, zest, or excitement
  • Antonym: lively - full of life and energy

Etymology

From Latin 'vapidus' (flat, insipid).

2. ADJ

/ ˈvæpɪd / (AmE), / ˈvapɪd / (BrE)
(of food or drink) lacking taste or flavor; insipid or bland.
lacking in taste or flavor

Examples

The vapid beer left a flat and disappointing impression on my palate.
After boiling for too long, the soup became vapid and unappetizing.
I prefer strong coffee to the vapid tea they served at the meeting.

Synset Examples

a bland diet
insipid hospital food
flavorless supermarket tomatoes
vapid beer
vapid tea

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insipid - lacking flavor; dull or uninteresting (of food or drink)
  • Synonym: bland - mild or lacking strong flavor
  • Antonym: flavorful - full of flavor; tasty

Etymology

From Latin 'vapidus' (flat, insipid).
