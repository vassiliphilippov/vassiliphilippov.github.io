balmy

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɑːmi / (AmE), / ˈbɑːmi / (BrE)
(of weather) pleasantly warm and mild.
pleasantly warm and mild (weather)

Examples

We enjoyed several balmy evenings on the beach during our vacation.
The city is known for its balmy climate year-round, attracting tourists from colder regions.
After a week of rain, the balmy weather was a welcome change.

Synset Examples

balmy days and nights
the climate was mild and conducive to life or growth
a soft breeze

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mild - not severe, gentle or moderate (weather)
  • Antonym: harsh - unpleasantly rough or severe (weather)

Etymology

From Middle English 'balmy', from 'balm' + '-y', referring to soothing, fragrant qualities.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbɑmi / (AmE), / ˈbɑːmi / (BrE)
(informal, chiefly British) Slightly crazy or foolish; eccentric.
slightly crazy; eccentric (informal)
informal british

Examples

Sarah thought her neighbor's plan was absolutely balmy and tried to talk him out of it.
It may sound balmy, but they really do believe the house is haunted.
He went a bit balmy after losing his keys for the fourth time this week.

Synset Examples

it used to drive my husband balmy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crazy - mentally deranged or extremely foolish
  • Antonym: sane - mentally sound; showing good judgment

Etymology

Extension of standard sense 'mild, soothing' with playful ironic use, chiefly British.
