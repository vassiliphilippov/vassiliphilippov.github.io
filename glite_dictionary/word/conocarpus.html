Conocarpus

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːnəˈkɑːrpəs / (AmE), / ˌkɒnəˈkɑːpəs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American trees known as buttonwood or button tree, typically found in coastal and mangrove habitats.
genus of tropical American trees

Examples

The mangrove forests along the coast are dominated by Conocarpus trees.
Botanists discovered a rare Conocarpus species during their expedition to the Caribbean islands.
The dense wood of Conocarpus is often used for making charcoal in tropical regions.

Etymology

From Greek 'kónos' (cone) and 'karpós' (fruit), referring to the cone-like fruit clusters.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkoʊ.nəˈkɑːr.pəs / (AmE), / ˌkəʊ.nəˈkɑː.pəs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American trees in the family Combretaceae, commonly known as the buttonwood or button tree.
a genus of tropical American trees

Examples

The Conocarpus is often planted in coastal parks for its salt tolerance.
Botanists have identified a new species belonging to the Conocarpus genus.
The dense wood of Conocarpus trees is used for making charcoal in some regions.

Etymology

From Greek 'kōnos' (cone) + 'karpos' (fruit), referring to the conical fruit.
