Lathyrus

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæθɪrəs / (AmE), / ˈlaθɪrəs / (BrE)
A genus of climbing herbs in the pea family, native to the Old World and temperate regions of North and South America, including vetchlings and wild peas.
genus of climbing herbs (pea family)

Examples

Several species of Lathyrus are cultivated for their attractive flowers.
The wild sweet pea, a member of the Lathyrus genus, thrives in temperate climates.
Researchers are studying Lathyrus plants for their nitrogen-fixing properties.

Etymology

From Latin Lathyrus, from Ancient Greek láthyros (‘pea’).
