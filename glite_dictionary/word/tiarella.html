Tiarella

1. NOUN

/ tiˈɑrələ / (AmE), / tɪˈarɪlə / (BrE)
A small genus of North American herbs with mainly basal leaves and slender clusters of delicate white flowers.
genus of North American flowering herbs

Examples

The woodland garden featured a patch of Tiarella blooming in the spring.
Botanists took samples of Tiarella to study its delicate flowers.
Many gardeners appreciate Tiarella for its attractive, lacy foliage and charming blossoms.

Etymology

From New Latin, coined by French botanist Antoine Laurent de Jussieu, diminutive of Latin 'tiara' (headdress), referring to the flower's shape.
