grime

1. NOUN

/ ɡraɪm / (AmE), / ɡraɪm / (BrE)
dirt or soot that makes something look dirty or unclean.
dirt or soot making things dirty

Examples

Years of grime had built up on the kitchen tiles.
He wiped away the grime from the window to let the sunlight in.
The workers were covered in grime after a long day at the factory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dirt - any substance that makes something unclean
  • Synonym: filth - thick, unpleasant dirt
  • Antonym: cleanliness - the state of being clean

2. VERB

/ ɡraɪm / (AmE), / ɡraɪm / (BrE)
to make something dirty or filthy by covering it with grime
make dirty or filthy

Examples

The children managed to grime the windows with their muddy hands.
Working in the garage will grime your clothes quickly if you're not careful.
Years of neglect had grimed the once-bright tiled floor.

Synset Examples

don't soil your clothes when you play outside!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soil - make something dirty or stained
  • Antonym: clean - remove dirt; make clean

Etymology

Related to Middle English *grim* (dirt), of uncertain origin.
