unworthiness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈwɝːði.nəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈwɜːðɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of not having merit, value, or worth
lack of merit or value

Examples

His constant sense of unworthiness prevented him from pursuing new opportunities.
The organization works to combat the feeling of unworthiness among disadvantaged youth.
Criticism can sometimes lead to a damaging belief in one’s own unworthiness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worthlessness - lack of worth, value, or usefulness
  • Antonym: worthiness - the quality of having worth or merit

Etymology

From 'unworthy' (from Old English 'unweorþ'), with the suffix '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ʌnˈwɝːðinəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈwɜːðɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of not being suitable, deserving, or appropriate for something
state of not being deserving or suitable

Examples

She struggled with feelings of unworthiness despite her many achievements.
His constant apologies betrayed a sense of unworthiness in the workplace.
They discussed the unworthiness of the proposal compared to other alternatives.

Synset Examples

he retracted nothing that he had said about the inappropriateness of either a corporeal God or a God who is a person
his praise released from her loud protestations of her unworthiness

Related Terms

  • Antonym: worthiness - the quality of being suitable or deserving
  • Synonym: inappropriateness - the quality of not being appropriate or fitting

Etymology

From un- (not) + worthy + -ness (forming nouns of state or quality).
