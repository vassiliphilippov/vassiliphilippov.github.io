Kinosternon

1. NOUN

/ ˌkaɪ.nəˈstɜr.nɒn / (AmE), / ˌkɪ.nɒsˈtɜː.nɒn / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Kinosternidae, comprising mud turtles found in North and Central America.
genus of mud turtles

Examples

The biologist studied the habitat of Kinosternon turtles in Mexico.
Several species within the genus Kinosternon are threatened due to habitat loss.
Members of Kinosternon are commonly referred to as mud turtles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kinosternidae - the family of mud turtles including Kinosternon

Etymology

From Greek 'kinein' (to move) + 'sternon' (chest).

2. NOUN

/ kɪˈnɑːstərˌnɑn / (AmE), / kɪˈnɒstənɒn / (BrE)
A genus of small aquatic turtles found primarily in North and Central America, commonly known as mud turtles.
genus of mud turtles

Examples

The Kinosternon genus includes species that are well adapted to muddy freshwater habitats.
Researchers found a new species within the Kinosternon genus in a remote region of Central America.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect several Kinosternon turtles threatened by habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Kinosternidae - Family of aquatic turtles containing the genus Kinosternon

Etymology

From Greek 'kinein' (to move) + 'sternon' (chest, breast), referring to the movable plastron (bottom shell) of these turtles.
