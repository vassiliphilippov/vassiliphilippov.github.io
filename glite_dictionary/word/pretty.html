pretty

1. ADJ

/ ˈprɪti / (AmE), / ˈprɪti / (BrE)
(used for emphasis, often ironically) used to describe something that is unexpectedly bad, awkward, or difficult.
emphatic, ironic: unexpectedly bad

Examples

Getting lost in the woods was a pretty problem to deal with after dark.
We've gotten ourselves into a pretty situation now, haven't we?
That was a pretty mess you made of the presentation.

Synset Examples

a pretty mess
a pretty kettle of fish

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrible - very bad; of poor quality
  • Synonym: awful - extremely bad or unpleasant
  • Antonym: nice - pleasant or agreeable (contrasting meaning, not an antonym in this sense)
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈprɪti / (AmE), / ˈprɪti / (BrE)
Attractive in a delicate or graceful way; pleasing without being striking or imposing.
attractive in a delicate or graceful way

Examples

She wore a pretty dress to the summer party.
The garden looked especially pretty in the morning light.
They enjoyed listening to a pretty song on the radio.

Synset Examples

pretty girl
pretty song
pretty room

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: beautiful - pleasing the senses or mind aesthetically
  • Degree Higher: lovely - beautiful or attractive, especially in a graceful way
  • Degree Lower: plain - not beautiful or attractive in appearance

Etymology

From Old English *prættig* (cunning, crafty), later shifting to 'attractive' in Middle English.

3. ADV

/ ˈprɪɾi / (AmE), / ˈprɪti / (BrE)
to some extent; fairly or moderately (used to qualify an adjective or another adverb)
to some extent; fairly

Examples

That movie was pretty entertaining, and I would watch it again.
This restaurant is pretty close to my office, so I come here a lot.
I'm pretty sure the meeting starts at 10 a.m., but I'll check.
