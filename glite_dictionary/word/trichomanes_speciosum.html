Trichomanes speciosum

1. NOUN

/ trɪˌkoʊməˈniːz spəˈsioʊsəm / (AmE), / trɪˌkɒməniːz spɛˈsiːəsəm / (BrE)
a large, sturdy fern native to extreme western Europe, notable for its delicate, translucent fronds
a large European fern species

Examples

Trichomanes speciosum can be found growing on damp, shaded rock faces in western Ireland.
The conservation efforts have helped increase the populations of Trichomanes speciosum in its native habitats.
Researchers are studying Trichomanes speciosum for its unique lifecycle and adaptability to humid environments.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; genus Trichomanes from Greek 'trikhos' (hair) + 'manes' (remaining), 'speciosum' from Latin 'speciosus' (showy, beautiful).
