carragheen

1. NOUN

/ ˌkærəˈɡin / (AmE), / ˌkærəˈɡiːn / (BrE)
a dark purple edible seaweed found along the Atlantic coasts of Europe and North America, often used as a thickening agent in food
edible Atlantic seaweed

Examples

The chef added carragheen to the soup for its thickening properties.
Traditional Irish desserts often include carragheen as a natural gelatin.
At the seaside market, several stalls sold dried carragheen for cooking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Irish moss - another term for carragheen, used especially in culinary contexts
  • Degree Higher: seaweed - general name for marine algae, including carragheen

Etymology

From Irish *carraigín* (little rock), reflecting where it grows.
