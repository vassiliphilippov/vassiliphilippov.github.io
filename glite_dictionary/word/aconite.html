aconite

1. NOUN

/ ˈæk.əˌnaɪt / (AmE), / ˈæk.ə.naɪt / (BrE)
a poisonous plant of the genus Aconitum, with tuberous roots and palmate leaves, often bearing blue or white flowers
poisonous plant of the genus Aconitum

Examples

The village herbalist warned that aconite is extremely poisonous if ingested.
Researchers studied the toxic effects of aconite on the nervous system.
Monkshood, also called aconite, was used in ancient times to make deadly poisons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wolfsbane - another name for the plant Aconitum, noted for its toxicity
  • Synonym: monkshood - common name for Aconitum plants with hood-shaped flowers

Etymology

From Latin aconitum, from Greek akoniton, possibly from akonē (stone) due to its habitat.
