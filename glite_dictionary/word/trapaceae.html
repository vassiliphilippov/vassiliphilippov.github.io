Trapaceae

1. NOUN

/ trəˈpeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / trəˈpeɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of aquatic flowering plants comprising only the genus Trapa, sometimes classified as a subfamily or tribe within the family Onagraceae.
family of aquatic plants (Trapa)

Examples

The water chestnut plant belongs to the Trapaceae family in some botanical classifications.
Recent studies have debated whether Trapaceae should remain distinct from Onagraceae.
Taxonomists reclassified several species after reviewing the characteristics of Trapaceae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Onagraceae - a family of flowering plants including evening primroses; sometimes including Trapaceae

Etymology

From the genus name Trapa + Latin family suffix -aceae, used in botanical nomenclature.

2. NOUN

/ trəˈpeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / trəˈpeɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of aquatic flowering plants comprising only the genus Trapa, sometimes classified as a subfamily or tribe of Onagraceae.
family of aquatic plants (water chestnuts)

Examples

Several aquatic species once classified under Trapaceae are now placed in the Lythraceae family by some botanists.
The genus Trapa is the sole member of the plant family Trapaceae.
The taxonomic status of Trapaceae has been revised in modern classifications.

Etymology

New Latin, from Trapa (the genus name) + -aceae (a standard suffix for plant families).
