Stanislavsky

1. PROPN

/ stəˈnɪslɑfski / (AmE), / stəˈnɪslɑfski / (BrE)
(1863–1938) Russian actor and theatre director known for developing a system of method acting emphasizing psychological motivation.
Russian actor and theatre director

Examples

Many actors credit Stanislavsky with revolutionizing their approach to character development.
The teachings of Stanislavsky continue to influence theatre schools around the world.
She studied the Stanislavsky method to improve her acting skills.

Etymology

Russian surname Станиславский (Stanislavsky), a stage name for Konstantin Sergeyevich Alekseyev.
