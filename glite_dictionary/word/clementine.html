clementine

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɛmənˌtaɪn / (AmE), / ˈklɛmənˌtiːn / (BrE)
a small, sweet variety of mandarin orange with a bright orange skin that is easy to peel, originally grown in the Mediterranean region and South Africa
small sweet mandarin orange

Examples

She peeled a clementine for a quick, healthy snack at her desk.
The trees in their orchard produce delicious clementines each winter.
I packed a clementine in my child's lunchbox because it's easy to eat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mandarin - a small citrus fruit similar to an orange
  • Synonym: tangerine - a variety of mandarin orange

Etymology

From French 'clémentine', named after Père Clément Rodier, a French missionary who is said to have developed this variety in Algeria.

2. NOUN

/ ˈklɛmənˌtaɪn, ˈklɛmənˌtiːn / (AmE), / ˈklɛmənˌtiːn, ˈklɛmənˌtaɪn / (BrE)
a small, sweet variety of mandarin orange, deep orange in color and usually seedless
a type of small, sweet orange

Examples

She peeled a clementine and offered some segments to her friend.
I prefer clementines to regular oranges because they are sweeter and easier to peel.
The salad was garnished with slices of fresh clementine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mandarin - a small, sweet citrus fruit with loose skin
  • Synonym: tangerine - a small citrus fruit closely related to the mandarin

Etymology

French, from the given name Clément; named after Father Clément Rodier, a French missionary said to have developed the variety.
