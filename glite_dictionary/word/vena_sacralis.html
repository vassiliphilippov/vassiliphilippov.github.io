vena sacralis

1. NOUN

/ ˌviːnə səˈkreɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˌviːnə səˈkreɪlɪs / (BrE)
A vein located in the sacral region of the body, draining blood from the lower spine area.
vein in the sacral (lower back) region
medical

Examples

The vena sacralis plays a crucial role in draining blood from the sacral vertebrae.
During surgery, care was taken to avoid injury to the vena sacralis.
Anatomists carefully traced the path of the vena sacralis in the human pelvis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sacral vein - another name for the vein of the sacral region
  • Degree Higher: vena cava - a large vein carrying blood to the heart

Etymology

From Latin 'vena' (vein) and 'sacralis' (of the sacrum).
