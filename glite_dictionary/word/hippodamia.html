Hippodamia

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɪpəˈdeɪmiə / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəˈdeɪmɪə / (BrE)
A genus of ladybugs, including several common species found in North America and elsewhere.
genus of ladybugs

Examples

The genus Hippodamia includes several species beneficial to gardens.
Researchers studied the feeding habits of Hippodamia in agricultural fields.
Hippodamia convergens is commonly used in biological pest control.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ladybug - a small, often red beetle with black spots

Etymology

From Greek Hippodámeia, a mythological name; adopted in 19th-century taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhɪpoʊˈdeɪmiə / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəˈdeɪmɪə / (BrE)
a genus of ladybugs (family Coccinellidae), including several widely distributed species such as the convergent lady beetle (Hippodamia convergens)
genus of ladybugs

Examples

The convergent lady beetle, a common North American species, belongs to the genus Hippodamia.
Researchers studied the different color patterns found in various Hippodamia species.
Gardeners value Hippodamia ladybugs for their effective control of aphid populations.

Etymology

From Greek hippodameia, a proper name; possibly also from hippos (horse) and damazein (to tame).
