purau

1. NOUN

/ ˈpuːraʊ / (AmE), / ˈpʊraʊ / (BrE)
A shrubby tree (Hibiscus tiliaceus) native to tropical coastal areas, valued for its light wood and strong fiber.
tropical shrubby tree (Hibiscus tiliaceus)

Examples

The lightweight wood of the purau is perfect for making canoe outriggers.
Traditional rope is often made from the strong fibers of the purau tree.
In village gardens, the purau is prized for its large yellow flowers and dense foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hibiscus - genus of flowering plants including Hibiscus tiliaceus

Etymology

From Tahitian *purau*, referring to the Hibiscus tiliaceus tree.
