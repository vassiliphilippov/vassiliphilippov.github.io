conacaste

1. NOUN

/ ˌkoʊ.nəˈkɑː.steɪ / (AmE), / ˌkɒ.nəˈkɑː.steɪ / (BrE)
A tropical South American tree with a wide-spreading crown of bipinnate leaves and coiled, ear-shaped fruits, grown for shade, ornament, and valuable timber.
tropical South American shade tree

Examples

The old conacaste in the plaza provided welcome shade during the hot afternoon.
Farmers use the timber from the conacaste for making furniture and tools.
Large, coiled fruits from the conacaste tree are a distinctive feature in the region's landscapes.

Etymology

From Spanish 'conacaste', originally from Nahuatl 'conácatl', a word for the tree.
