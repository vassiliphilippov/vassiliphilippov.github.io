damn

1. ADV

/ dæm / (AmE), / dam / (BrE)
(informal, sometimes vulgar) Used as an intensifier meaning 'extremely' or 'very'.
used as an intensifier; extremely
informal vulgar

Examples

It's damn cold outside tonight, so wear your warmest coat.
She worked damn hard to earn that promotion.
This cake tastes damn good!

Synset Examples

you are bloody right
Why are you so all-fired aggressive?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: very - to a high degree; used as an intensifier
  • Synonym: really - used to emphasize an adjective or adverb
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00025503-r
Synset POS: r

2. VERB

/ dæm / (AmE), / dam / (BrE)
to wish harm on someone or something, especially by calling upon a supernatural power to bring them misfortune
wish harm or invoke evil upon

Examples

The villagers believed the sorceress could damn anyone who crossed her.
He was furious and loudly damned his enemies for betraying him.
According to legend, a single word from the old wizard could damn a whole town.

Synset Examples

The bad witch cursed the child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curse - to invoke evil or misfortune upon

Etymology

From Latin 'damnare' (to condemn), from 'damnum' (damage, loss).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00867622-v
Synset POS: v

3. INTJ

/ dæm / (AmE), / dam / (BrE)
Used to express anger, annoyance, or surprise, often considered vulgar.
expletive to show anger or surprise
vulgar

Examples

Damn! I forgot my keys at home again.
Damn, that was a close call!
She whispered, 'damn, this is harder than I thought.'

Synset Examples

oh, damn (or goddamn)!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: darn - a milder expletive sometimes used in place of 'damn'

Etymology

From Latin 'damnare' (to condemn, pronounce judgment).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00673766-s
Synset POS: s

4. NOUN

/ dæm / (AmE), / dæm / (BrE)
a thing of little or no value (used mainly in negative expressions)
something worthless
informal vulgar

Examples

I don't give a damn about what they think of me.
His opinion isn't worth a damn to anyone here.
She realized her efforts didn't amount to a damn in the end.

Synset Examples

his promise is not worth a damn
not worth one red cent
not worth shucks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cent - a very small amount of money; often used in negative expressions ('not worth a cent')
  • Synonym: shuck - something worthless, especially in the phrase 'not worth shucks'
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05148040-n
Synset POS: n

5. ADJ

/ dæm / (AmE), / dam / (BrE)
Used informally to emphasize a quality, amount, or feeling, often to express annoyance, surprise, or intensity.
informal intensifier
informal

Examples

I don't give a damn what they think about my decision.
It was a damn cold night and nobody wanted to leave the house.
She's a damn good pianist for her age.

Synset Examples

he's a blasted idiot
it's a blamed shame
a blame cold winter
not a blessed dime
I'll be damned (or blessed or darned or goddamned) if I'll do any such thing
he's a damn (or goddam or goddamned) fool
a deuced idiot
an infernal nuisance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blasted - used informally as an intensifier
  • Synonym: darn - used as a mild informal intensifier
  • Synonym: blessed - used ironically as a mild intensifier
  • Synonym: goddamned - used for strong emphasis, often vulgar
  • Synonym: infernal - used informally as an intensifier, especially for annoyance

Etymology

From Old French *damner*, from Latin *damnare* (to condemn).
