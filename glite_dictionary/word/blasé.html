blasé

1. ADJ

/ blɑːˈzeɪ / (AmE), / blɑːˈzeɪ / (BrE)
unimpressed or indifferent to something because one has experienced or seen it so often before; sophisticated and unconcerned, typically from overexposure or excess
indifferent or sophisticated due to overexposure

Examples

After years of fine dining, she became quite blasé about gourmet cuisine.
The blasé attitude of the staff suggested they had seen it all before.
He regarded the celebrity guests with a blasé air, barely glancing up from his book.

Synset Examples

the blase traveler refers to the ocean he has crossed as ‘the pond’
the benefits of his worldly wisdom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: world-weary - feeling or showing fatigue from or boredom with the world
  • Synonym: sophisticated - having a lot of worldly experience and knowledge

Etymology

From French *blasé* (satiated, surfeited).

2. ADJ

/ blɑːˈzeɪ / (AmE), / blɑːˈzeɪ / (BrE)
unimpressed or indifferent to something because one has experienced or seen it so often before; showing a lack of concern or excitement, typically from overexposure or excess familiarity.
indifferent or unimpressed due to overfamiliarity

Examples

After years of traveling for work, she became blasé about flying.
He acted blasé during the ceremony, barely acknowledging the applause.
Despite the exciting news, Jack gave a blasé shrug and continued reading his book.

Synset Examples

a blase attitude about housecleaning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indifferent - having no particular interest or sympathy; unconcerned
  • Antonym: enthusiastic - having intense and eager enjoyment or interest

Etymology

From French 'blasé' (jaded, satiated), past participle of 'blaser' (to surfeit).
