to fructify

1. VERB

/ ˈfrʌktɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈfrʌktɪfaɪ / (BrE)
(of a plant or tree) to produce fruit; to bear fruit.
produce fruit (of plants or trees)

Examples

In a good season, the apple trees fructify abundantly.
With care and patience, the garden's vines will fructify by late summer.
Many plants fructify only after reaching a certain age or receiving proper nutrients.

Synset Examples

the apple trees fructify

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bear fruit - to produce fruit (of a plant or tree)

Etymology

From Latin fructificare, from fructus (fruit) + facere (to make).

2. VERB

/ ˈfrʌk.təˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈfrʌk.tɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to make productive or fruitful; to cause to bear fruit or yield results
make productive or fruitful

Examples

Innovative ideas can fructify a stagnant organization, leading to new success.
The abundant rains helped to fructify the valley, resulting in a record harvest.
Efforts to fructify the barren land finally paid off after years of hard work.

Synset Examples

The earth that he fructified

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fertilize - to make soil or land more productive; enrich
  • Antonym: barren - unable to produce offspring or results

Etymology

From Latin fructificare, from fructus (fruit).
