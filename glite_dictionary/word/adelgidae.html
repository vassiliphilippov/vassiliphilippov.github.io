Adelgidae

1. NOUN

/ əˈdɛl.dʒɪˌdi / (AmE), / əˈdɛl.dʒɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of small plant-sucking insects, commonly known as woolly aphids, pine aphids, or spruce aphids, that infest conifers
family of conifer-infesting aphids

Examples

The Adelgidae are notorious for damaging spruce and pine trees by feeding on their sap.
Entomologists study the Adelgidae to better understand their impact on conifer forests.
Several pest control methods target members of the Adelgidae family to protect commercial timber.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aphid - a small sap-sucking insect of the superfamily Aphidoidea

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Adelges, the type genus, itself from Greek 'adelos' (uncertain, possibly 'not clear' or 'not evident').

2. NOUN

/ əˈdɛl.dʒɪ.di / (AmE), / əˈdɛl.dʒɪ.diː / (BrE)
a family of small, sap-sucking insects, known as woolly conifer aphids, that infest conifers such as pines and spruces
family of woolly conifer aphids

Examples

Foresters monitor Adelgidae infestations to protect valuable spruce and pine trees.
The life cycle of Adelgidae includes complex alternation between host trees.
Adelgidae can cause significant damage to coniferous forests if left untreated.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Adelgidae, from the type genus Adelges.
