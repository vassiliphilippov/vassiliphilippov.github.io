citrus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪt.rəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪt.rəs / (BrE)
a fruit of the genus Citrus, such as an orange, lemon, lime, or grapefruit, characterized by a thick rind and juicy pulp, typically grown in warm regions.
a fruit like an orange or lemon

Examples

She served a fresh citrus salad with oranges and grapefruits for breakfast.
Vitamin C is abundant in citrus fruits like lemons and limes.
The trees in their garden yield many types of citrus every year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orange - a round, sweet citrus fruit with a thick orange skin
  • Synonym: lemon - a yellow, oval citrus fruit with sour juice

Etymology

From Latin Citrus, the name of the citron tree.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪtrəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪtrəs / (BrE)
a tropical or subtropical evergreen tree of the genus Citrus, cultivated for its juicy, edible fruits with aromatic rinds, such as oranges, lemons, and limes
tropical fruit tree of the Citrus genus

Examples

The climate in this region is ideal for growing citrus such as oranges and grapefruits.
Many citrus trees are in bloom, filling the air with a wonderful fragrance.
A healthy diet often includes fresh citrus fruits rich in vitamin C.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orange - a round, sweet citrus fruit with orange skin
  • Synonym: lemon - a yellow, acidic citrus fruit used in cooking and drinks
  • Synonym: lime - a small, green citrus fruit with acidic juice

Etymology

From Latin *citrus* (citron tree), originally from Greek *kitron*.
