Citrus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪtrəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪtrəs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the rue family, including orange, lemon, lime, and related fruit trees.
genus of fruit trees (orange, lemon, lime)

Examples

The Citrus genus includes popular fruits such as oranges, lemons, and grapefruits.
Researchers are studying various diseases that affect Citrus plants.
Farmers in warm climates often grow several Citrus species for commercial fruit production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orange - a round sweet citrus fruit with a tough bright reddish-yellow rind
  • Synonym: lemon - a yellow oval citrus fruit with thick skin and fragrant, acidic juice
  • Synonym: lime - a small green citrus fruit with acidic juice

Etymology

From Latin 'citrus' (citron tree), from Greek 'kítron'.
