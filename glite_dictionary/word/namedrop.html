to namedrop

1. VERB

/ ˈneɪmˌdrɑp / (AmE), / ˈneɪmˌdrɒp / (BrE)
to mention famous or important people in order to impress others, especially when they are not relevant to the conversation
mention famous people to impress
informal

Examples

During the interview, he tried to namedrop several celebrities to make himself seem more important.
She always namedrops politicians when talking about her work in government.
I found it annoying when he namedropped authors just to impress the group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boast - to talk with excessive pride about one's achievements or possessions
  • Synonym: humblebrag - to make a self-deprecating statement whose true purpose is to draw attention to something one is proud of

Etymology

Formed from 'name' + 'drop', referring to 'dropping' names into conversation (mid-20th century).
