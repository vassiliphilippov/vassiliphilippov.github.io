admission

1. NOUN

/ ədˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / ədˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
the fee required to enter a place, event, or institution
fee to enter a place or event

Examples

The admission to the museum is $10 per person.
Children under five receive free admission to the aquarium.
The concert's admission includes access to all exhibits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entry fee - a fee paid for entering a place or event

Etymology

From Latin 'admissio' (a letting in, admission).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13342517-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ədˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / ədˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
the right or permission to enter a place, organization, or event
right or permission to enter

Examples

Museum admission is free on Sundays for all residents.
You must buy a ticket for admission to the concert.
The admission process for the university is highly competitive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entry - the act of going into a place
  • Antonym: exclusion - the state of being denied entry

Etymology

From Latin 'admissio' (a letting in), from 'admittere' (to let in).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05183715-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ədˈmɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ədˈmɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
the act of allowing someone to enter a place, institution, or organization
the act of allowing entry

Examples

His first admission to the university was a moment of great pride for his family.
During her admission to the hospital, she underwent several tests.
The club reserves the right to refuse admission to anyone not following the dress code.

Synset Examples

the surgery was performed on his second admission to the clinic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entry - the act of entering or being allowed to enter a place

Etymology

From Latin admittere (to let in), via Old French admission.

4. NOUN

/ ədˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / ədˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of admitting or acknowledging the truth of something, especially something previously denied or concealed
acknowledgment of the truth of something

Examples

Her admission of the mistake helped restore trust within the team.
The politician's admission that he lied shocked the public.
He finally made an admission after months of denial.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confession - a formal statement admitting that one is guilty of a crime or wrong
  • Antonym: denial - a statement that something is not true or does not exist

Etymology

From Latin 'admissio', from 'admittere' (to let in, allow).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07230608-n
Synset POS: n
