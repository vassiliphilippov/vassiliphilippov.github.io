2. ADJ

/ rɛd / (AmE), / rɛd / (BrE)
of a color resembling that of blood, cherries, tomatoes, or rubies; situated at the end of the visible spectrum next to orange
of the color of blood or rubies

Examples

She wore a stunning red dress to the party.
The red apples looked fresh and delicious on the table.
His face turned red with embarrassment when he made a mistake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crimson - strong, deep red color
  • Synonym: scarlet - bright red color
  • Antonym: green - color at the other end of the spectrum from red
  • Degree Lower: pink - pale red color

Etymology

From Old English 'rēad', related to Dutch 'rood' and German 'rot'.

4. NOUN

/ ɹɛd / (AmE), / ɹɛd / (BrE)
a color resembling the hue of blood; one of the primary colors in the visible spectrum

Examples

She painted her bedroom walls a bold shade of red to create a lively atmosphere.
The artist mixed blue and yellow to make green, but red could not be created from other colors.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: pink - a pale red color
  • Antonym: blue - another primary color in the spectrum
  • Antonym: green - another primary color in the spectrum

Etymology

5. NOUN

/ rɛd / (AmE), / rɛd / (BrE)
an extreme radical or revolutionary person, especially a communist
radical or communist person

Examples

In the 1950s, many Americans feared that reds were infiltrating key positions in government.
The senator accused his opponents of being reds and undermining national security.
During the revolution, the term red was often used to label anyone suspected of leftist sympathies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: communist - a person who supports or believes in the principles of communism
  • Antonym: reactionary - a person opposing political or social progress or reform; conservative extremist

Etymology

From the association of the color red with left-wing political movements, particularly from the red flag symbolizing revolution and communism in 19th- and 20th-century Europe.
