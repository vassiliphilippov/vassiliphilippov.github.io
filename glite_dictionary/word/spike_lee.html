Spike Lee

1. PROPN

/ spaɪk liː / (AmE), / spaɪk liː / (BrE)
(born 1957) An American film director, producer, writer, and actor known for exploring the experiences of African Americans through his films.
American filmmaker and cultural commentator

Examples

Spike Lee received critical acclaim for his influential films about African American life.
The 1989 film 'Do the Right Thing' was directed and produced by Spike Lee.
Many students discussed how Spike Lee's movies address social issues in America.
