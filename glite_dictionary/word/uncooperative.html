uncooperative

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnkoʊˈɑpərətɪv / (AmE), / ˌʌnkəʊˈɒpərətɪv / (BrE)
unwilling to work with or help others; not cooperative.
not willing to cooperate

Examples

The manager found the employee uncooperative during the meeting.
Doctors described the patient as uncooperative, refusing to follow their instructions.
Negotiations stalled because one party remained uncooperative throughout the talks.

Synset Examples

an uncooperative witness

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cooperative - willing to help or work with others

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'cooperative'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.koʊˈɑː.pɚ.ə.tɪv / (AmE), / ˌʌn.kəʊˈɒp.ər.ə.tɪv / (BrE)
not willing to help or support others; deliberately not cooperative.
not willing to help or cooperate

Examples

The patient was uncooperative, making it difficult for the doctors to examine him.
Some students can be uncooperative when asked to follow classroom rules.
His uncooperative attitude slowed down the entire team's progress.

Synset Examples

the action was not offensive to him but proved somewhat disobliging

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cooperative - willing to help or work with others
  • Degree Lower: helpful - willing to assist or give support

Etymology

From un- (not) + cooperative, itself from Latin cooperari (to work together).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00021629-s
Synset POS: s
