premenopausal

1. ADJ

/ ˌpriːˌmɛnoʊˈpɔzəl / (AmE), / ˌpriːˌmɛnəʊˈpɔːzəl / (BrE)
(of a woman) relating to or occurring in the period before menopause.
before menopause (medical)
medical

Examples

Many premenopausal women experience irregular menstrual cycles from time to time.
The doctor explained that the risk factors differ for premenopausal and postmenopausal women.
Research has shown that breast cancer rates are lower in premenopausal women than in older age groups.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: postmenopausal - relating to the period after menopause
  • Degree Higher: perimenopausal - relating to the time around the onset of menopause

Etymology

Formed from 'pre-' (before) and 'menopausal', relating to menopause.
