conchoid

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑn.kɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋ.kɔɪd / (BrE)
A curve generated from a fixed point and a given curve, such that each point on the conchoid lies a constant distance from the given curve along a straight line going through the fixed point.
a mathematical curve defined by a locus

Examples

The mathematician drew a conchoid to illustrate the solution to the problem.
The limaçon of Pascal is a special case of a conchoid.
In geometry, the conchoid can be constructed using a fixed point and a given curve.

Synset Examples

If you consider a point on a radius of the rolling curve in generating a cardioid that is not on its circumference, the result is a conchoid called the limaçon of Pascal.

Etymology

From Greek *konchoeidēs* (like a shell), from *konchē* (shell).
