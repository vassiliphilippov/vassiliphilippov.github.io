griddlecake

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrɪd.əlˌkeɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪd.əl.keɪk / (BrE)
A flat cake made from a thin batter and cooked on a griddle, usually by frying on both sides.
flat cake fried on a griddle

Examples

He served the blueberry griddlecake with maple syrup and butter.
Each griddlecake was golden brown and perfectly crisp on the edges.
For breakfast, she made a stack of warm griddlecakes for the whole family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pancake - A flat cake made from batter and cooked on a pan or griddle

Etymology

From 'griddle' (a flat cooking surface) + 'cake'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡrɪd.əl.keɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪd.əl.keɪk / (BrE)
A small cake or scone made by dropping a spoonful of batter onto a hot griddle and cooking it on both sides.
small cake cooked on a griddle

Examples

She flipped the griddlecake until it was golden brown on both sides.
For breakfast, we had a fresh griddlecake with butter and syrup.
The old recipe calls for currants to be mixed into the griddlecake batter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pancake - a thin flat cake of batter cooked on both sides on a griddle or frying pan

Etymology

From 'griddle', a flat cooking surface, and 'cake'; indicating a cake made on a griddle.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07706836-n
Synset POS: n
