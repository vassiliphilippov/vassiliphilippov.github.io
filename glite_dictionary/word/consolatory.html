consolatory

1. ADJ

/ kənˈsɑːləˌtɔri / (AmE), / kənˈsɒlətəri / (BrE)
intended to provide comfort or solace in a time of distress or disappointment.
providing comfort or solace

Examples

She found his consolatory words helpful after her loss.
The letter was meant to be consolatory rather than hopeful.
A consolatory gift was given to the grieving family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comforting - giving comfort or reassurance
  • Antonym: distressing - causing unhappiness or pain

Etymology

From Latin consolatorius, from 'consolari' (to console).
