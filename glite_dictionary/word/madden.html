to madden

1. VERB

/ ˈmæd.ən / (AmE), / ˈmæd.ən / (BrE)
to make someone extremely annoyed or angry
make someone very angry or annoyed

Examples

The constant noise from the construction site would madden anyone trying to work from home.
His arrogant behaviour during the meeting maddened the entire team.
It maddens me when people chew loudly in a quiet room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enrage - to make extremely angry
  • Synonym: to infuriate - to make very angry
  • Antonym: calm - to make or become calm

Etymology

From Old English 'gemæddian' (to make mad) based on 'mad'.

2. VERB

/ ˈmædən / (AmE), / ˈmædn̩ / (BrE)
to make someone extremely angry or to drive someone insane
make extremely angry or insane

Examples

The constant noise would madden anyone after a few hours.
His stubborn refusal to listen only served to madden her.
It maddened him that he could not solve the puzzle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to infuriate - make extremely angry
  • Synonym: to enrage - make very angry
  • Antonym: calm - make less angry or agitated

Etymology

From Old English *gemæddian* (to make insane), from *mād* (mad).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01791641-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈmædən / (AmE), / ˈmad.ən / (BrE)
to make someone very angry, annoyed, or insane
make angry or drive insane

Examples

Her constant interruptions began to madden everyone in the room.
The relentless noise would madden even the calmest person.
His refusal to listen could madden his colleagues beyond endurance.

Synset Examples

His behavior is maddening

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enrage - to make someone extremely angry
  • Synonym: to infuriate - to make someone very angry
  • Antonym: to soothe - to calm someone down, to make less angry

Etymology

From Middle English maden, from Old English gemǣdan (to make insane), from 'mad'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01791419-v
Synset POS: v
