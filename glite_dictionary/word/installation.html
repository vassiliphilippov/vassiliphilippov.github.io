installation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnstəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnstəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a formal ceremony or act of placing someone in an official position, office, or organization
formal induction into office or organization

Examples

The mayor’s installation took place in a grand ceremony at the city hall.
Her installation as president was attended by dignitaries and family members.
The university held an official installation of its new chancellor.

Synset Examples

his initiation into the club
he was ordered to report for induction into the army
he gave a speech as part of his installation into the hall of fame

Related Terms

  • Synonym: induction - the formal introduction or admission into an office or position
  • Synonym: initiation - the act of being formally accepted into a group or organization

Etymology

From Medieval Latin installatio, from Latin in- (in) + stallum (place, stall).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪnstəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnstəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a building or structure that provides a particular service or is used for a specific industrial or operational purpose
building for a specific service or industry

Examples

The new military installation was operational by the end of the year.
That coastal installation supports the region's fishing industry.
Factory workers reported to the massive installation every morning.

Synset Examples

the assembly plant is an enormous facility

Related Terms

  • Synonym: facility - a place or building providing a particular service, especially one used for a specific purpose

Etymology

From French 'installation', from installer (to install), from Italian 'installare'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03319968-n
Synset POS: n
