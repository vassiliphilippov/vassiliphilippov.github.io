Calvert Vaux

1. PROPN

/ ˈkæl.vɚt vɔːks / (AmE), / ˈkæl.vət vɔːks / (BrE)
(1824–1895) United States landscape architect, born in England, known for co-designing Central Park in New York City.
US landscape architect, co-designer of Central Park

Examples

Calvert Vaux played a major role in designing many iconic parks in the United States.
Together with Frederick Law Olmsted, Calvert Vaux created the master plan for Central Park in New York City.
Modern city parks still follow many principles first established by Calvert Vaux in his work.
