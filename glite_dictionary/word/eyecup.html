eyecup

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪˌkʌp / (AmE), / ˈaɪˌkʌp / (BrE)
(embryology) A two-layered cuplike structure in the developing embryo that forms the pigmented and sensory layers of the retina.
embryonic structure forming retina layers
medical

Examples

During embryonic development, the eyecup gives rise to the retina's important layers.
Scientists observed the formation of the eyecup under a microscope in the early stages of eye development.
The inner and outer layers of the eyecup differentiate into the sensory and pigmented tissues of the retina.

Etymology

From 'eye' + 'cup', describing its shape and function.

2. NOUN

/ ˈaɪˌkʌp / (AmE), / ˈaɪˌkʌp / (BrE)
a small cup with a curved rim to fit the eye socket, used to apply medicated or cleansing solutions to the eyeball
vessel for washing or medicating the eye
medical

Examples

You should rinse the eyecup thoroughly before each use to avoid infection.
The doctor filled the eyecup with saline solution and placed it gently over my eye.
After splashing my eye with dust, I used an eyecup to wash it out.

Synset Examples

an eyecup is called ‘an eyebath’ in Britain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eyebath - British term for a vessel used to cleanse the eye
