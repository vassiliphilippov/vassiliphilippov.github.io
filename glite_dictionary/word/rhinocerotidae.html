Rhinocerotidae

1. NOUN

/ raɪˌnɑːsəˈrɑːtəˌdi / (AmE), / raɪˌnɒsəˈrɒtɪdiː / (BrE)
the biological family that includes all modern rhinoceroses, large, thick-skinned, herbivorous mammals native to Africa and Asia
the family of rhinoceroses

Examples

Members of the Rhinocerotidae family are recognized by their distinctive horns and massive bodies.
Conservationists are concerned about the declining numbers of Rhinocerotidae in the wild.
The Rhinocerotidae family includes several species, such as the white and black rhinoceros.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhinoceros - large, thick-skinned, horned mammal of Africa and Asia

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'rhin', nose + 'keras', horn, used in taxonomic classification.

The Rhinocerotidae family includes both the white and black rhinoceros species.
Scientists study the evolution of the Rhinocerotidae to understand how these animals adapted to their environments.
Members of the Rhinocerotidae are easily recognized by their characteristic horns and thick skin.

Related Terms

From New Latin, based on Greek 'rhin-' (nose) + 'keras' (horn).
