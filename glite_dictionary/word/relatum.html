relatum

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈleɪtəm / (AmE), / rɪˈleɪtəm / (BrE)
(Logic, philosophy) a term in a relation or proposition that is related to another term, known as the referent.
term in a relation or proposition

Examples

In the sentence 'Alice loves Bob', both 'Alice' and 'Bob' serve as relata in the relationship of loving.
The analysis of a proposition often involves identifying each relatum and the relation that connects them.
Every mathematical function consists of relata joined by a specific relation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: referent - the thing that a word or phrase denotes or stands for

Etymology

From Latin relatum, neuter past participle of referre (to bring back, report).
