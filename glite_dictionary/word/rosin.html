rosin

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːzɪn / (AmE), / ˈrɒzɪn / (BrE)
a solid or semisolid resin obtained from pine and some other plants, used especially to improve friction, as on the bows of stringed instruments
solid resin from pine used for friction

Examples

She applied rosin to her violin bow before the performance.
The carpenter used rosin to improve his grip on the tools.
Block rosin is often sold in music stores for string instrument players.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resin - a sticky substance produced by some trees and plants

Etymology

From Latin 'resina' meaning 'resin'.

2. VERB

/ ˈrɑː.zən / (AmE), / ˈrɒz.ɪn / (BrE)
to apply rosin to (especially a violin bow or similar musical instrument accessory)
apply rosin to something, esp. a bow

Examples

She carefully rosined her cello bow before the performance.
Make sure to rosin your bow regularly to get a clear sound.
He watched the violinist rosining her bow before playing.

Synset Examples

rosin the violin bow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resin - a sticky substance used to make rosin

Etymology

From the noun 'rosin', derived from Old French 'rosin', related to resin.
