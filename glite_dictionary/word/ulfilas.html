Ulfilas

1. PROPN

/ ʌlˈfaɪləs / (AmE), / ʌlˈfiːləs / (BrE)
(c. 311–383) A Christian bishop of the Visigoths who translated the Bible from Greek into Gothic and is traditionally credited with inventing the Gothic alphabet.
Christian bishop and Bible translator

Examples

Ulfilas played a crucial role in converting the Goths to Christianity.
The Gothic alphabet is traditionally attributed to Ulfilas.
Many scholars study the Bible translation work of Ulfilas when exploring early Germanic languages.

Etymology

From Latin Ulfilas, originally Gothic Wulfila, meaning 'little wolf'.
