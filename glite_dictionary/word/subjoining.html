subjoining

1. NOUN

/ səbˈdʒɔɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / sʌbˈdʒɔɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of adding or appending something, especially as a supplement at the end of a text or statement
the act of supplementing or appending

Examples

The subjoining of the references provides further support for the argument.
She made her point clearer by subjoining several examples to her statement.
In legal documents, the subjoining of additional clauses is a common practice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: addition - the process of adding something
  • Synonym: appending - the act of attaching or adding, especially at the end

Etymology

From subjoin + -ing; subjoin from Latin subjungere (to add beneath).
