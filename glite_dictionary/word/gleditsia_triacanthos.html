Gleditsia triacanthos

1. NOUN

/ ɡlɪˈdɪtsiə traɪəˈkænθəs / (AmE), / ɡlɛˈdɪtsiə traɪəˈkanθəs / (BrE)
a tall North American tree, commonly known as the honey locust, usually spiny, with small greenish-white flowers in drooping clusters and long twisting seed pods, valued for its hard reddish-brown wood
honey locust tree of North America

Examples

The Gleditsia triacanthos is often planted along city streets for its attractive, airy foliage.
Farmers use the tough wood of Gleditsia triacanthos to make fence posts and other durable structures.
The long seed pods of Gleditsia triacanthos hang from its branches well into winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honey locust - common name for Gleditsia triacanthos

Etymology

Gleditsia from the name of German botanist J. G. Gleditsch (1714–1786); triacanthos from Greek 'tri-' (three) and 'akantha' (thorn).
