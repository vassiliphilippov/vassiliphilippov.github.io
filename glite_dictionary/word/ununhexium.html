ununhexium

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuːnʌnˈhɛksiəm / (AmE), / ˌjuːnʌnˈhɛksiəm / (BrE)
the former systematic name for the chemical element with atomic number 116, now known as livermorium
former name of chemical element 116

Examples

Scientists first reported the synthesis of ununhexium in 2000 at the Joint Institute for Nuclear Research.
The name ununhexium was replaced by livermorium after the element was officially recognized.
Early papers on element 116 referred to it as ununhexium before a permanent name was adopted.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: livermorium - current official name for chemical element 116

Etymology

From Latin roots: un (one) + un (one) + hex (six) + ium (element suffix), denoting atomic number 116.
