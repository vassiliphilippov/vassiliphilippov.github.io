to carburize

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑrbjəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈkɑːbjʊrʌɪz / (BrE)
to infuse or combine a material, especially metal, with carbon, usually to harden its surface
infuse metal with carbon

Examples

The company decided to carburize the steel components to improve their durability.
Engineers often carburize gears to create a hard, wear-resistant surface.
Before assembly, the technician carburized the metal parts in a special furnace.

Synset Examples

carburize metal

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to decarburize - to remove carbon from a material, especially metal

Etymology

From French *carburer* (to combine with carbon), from *carbone* (carbon).
