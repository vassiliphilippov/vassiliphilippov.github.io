to retrace

1. VERB

/ riˈtreɪs / (AmE), / rɪˈtreɪs / (BrE)
to go back over the same route, path, or sequence that was previously taken
to go back over the same path

Examples

After realizing she had dropped her keys, Maria had to retrace her steps through the park.
The hiker decided to retrace the trail to find his lost backpack.
Detectives often retrace the victim's movements to gather clues about a crime.

Synset Examples

we retraced the route we took last summer
trace your path

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trace - to follow the course or trail of something

Etymology

From Latin retrahere (to draw back), influenced by trace (to follow a route).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02008366-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ riˈtreɪs / (AmE), / rɪˈtreɪs / (BrE)
to think back over or reconstruct something mentally, especially events or steps taken in the past
reconstruct mentally; recall past steps or events

Examples

She tried to retrace the events of that fateful night in her mind.
Detectives must retrace the steps that led to the crime.
During the interview, he struggled to retrace exactly what had happened years ago.

Synset Examples

reconstruct the events of 20 years ago

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reconstruct - form an image or idea of again, especially from evidence

Etymology

From Old French retraire, from Latin retrahere ‘draw back’ (re- ‘back’ + trahere ‘to pull’).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00635752-v
Synset POS: v
