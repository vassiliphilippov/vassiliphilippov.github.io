unmercenary

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈmɜrsəˌnɛri / (AmE), / ʌnˈmɜːsənəri / (BrE)
Not motivated by a desire for money; acting without regard for financial gain.
not motivated by money

Examples

Her unmercenary dedication to helping others earned her great respect in the community.
He was praised for his unmercenary attitude, always putting patients before profit.
The charity is run by unmercenary volunteers who expect nothing in return.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mercenary - motivated mainly by money; hired for pay

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) plus 'mercenary' (from Latin mercenarius, from merces: wages, fee).
