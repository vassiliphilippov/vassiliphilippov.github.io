contextual

1. ADJ

/ kənˈtɛkstʃuəl / (AmE), / kənˈtɛkstjʊəl / (BrE)
relating to or depending on the context in which something occurs or is used.
relating to context

Examples

The meaning of the word is highly contextual and depends on how it's used in a sentence.
Effective teaching often requires a contextual approach tailored to students' experiences.
She provided a contextual analysis of the literary work, considering its historical background.

Synset Examples

contextual information

Related Terms

  • Synonym: context - the circumstances or setting in which an event occurs

Etymology

From Latin 'contextus' (joining together, connection), from 'contexere' (to weave together) + '-al'.
