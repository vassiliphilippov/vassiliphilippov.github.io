Helenium

1. NOUN

/ həˈliːniəm / (AmE), / hɛˈliːniəm / (BrE)
A genus of American herbs in the daisy family, with yellow, daisy-like flowers; commonly known as sneezeweeds.
genus of flowering plants (sneezeweeds)

Examples

The garden was full of bright yellow Helenium blossoms in late summer.
Many gardeners plant Helenium to attract butterflies and bees.
Helenium species are often called sneezeweeds because their dried leaves were once used to make snuff.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sneezeweed - common name for plants in the Helenium genus

Etymology

From Greek Helene, the mythological name Helen; also linked to 'helene' meaning torch or bright in Greek.

2. NOUN

/ həˈliniəm / (AmE), / hɛˈliːniəm / (BrE)
A genus of American herbs in the daisy family, with yellow-rayed flowers; commonly known as sneezeweeds.
genus of American herbal plants (sneezeweeds)

Examples

Many gardeners choose to plant Helenium for its bright yellow flowers that attract pollinators.
The blooms of Helenium are often referred to as sneezeweeds because of their historical use in making snuff.
Native to North America, Helenium species flourish in sunny meadows and naturalized gardens.

Etymology

From New Latin Helenium, named after Helen of Troy in Greek mythology.
