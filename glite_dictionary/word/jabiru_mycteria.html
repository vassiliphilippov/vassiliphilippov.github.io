Jabiru mycteria

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒæbɪˈruː mɪkˈtɪəriə / (AmE), / ˌdʒabɪˈruː mɪkˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
a large white stork found in warm regions, especially in the Americas, recognized for its massive black bill and striking appearance
large white stork of the Americas

Examples

The Jabiru mycteria is often seen wading majestically along South American waterways.
Birdwatchers travel to the Pantanal to catch a glimpse of the impressive Jabiru mycteria.
With its bright white plumage and huge black bill, the Jabiru mycteria stands out among other wetland birds.

Etymology

From Portuguese 'jabiru', from Tupi 'jabirú', the native name for the bird; 'mycteria' from Greek 'mykter' (nose).
