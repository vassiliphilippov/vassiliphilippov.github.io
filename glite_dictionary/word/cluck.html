cluck

1. NOUN

/ klʌk / (AmE), / klʌk / (BrE)
the short, sharp sound made by a hen, especially when calling her chicks
the sound made by a hen

Examples

The mother hen gave a soft cluck to gather her chicks.
A sudden cluck came from the coop as the birds settled in for the night.
We heard the familiar cluck as we walked past the farmyard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cackle - the loud, harsh sound made by a hen after laying an egg

Etymology

Imitative of the sound made by a hen.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07395700-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ klʌk / (AmE), / klʌk / (BrE)
(of a hen) to make a series of short, low sounds; to utter a clucking sound.
make a clucking sound (as a hen)

Examples

The mother hen began to cluck loudly when a stranger approached her chicks.
At dawn, I could hear chickens clucking in the backyard.
She clucked her tongue disapprovingly at the mess in the kitchen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cackle - give a loud, harsh cry (as a hen)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01056721-v
Synset POS: v
