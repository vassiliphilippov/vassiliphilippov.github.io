rooftree

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːfˌtriː / (AmE), / ˈruːfˌtriː / (BrE)
A main beam laid along the ridge at the top of a roof where two sloping sides meet, supporting the upper ends of the rafters.
main beam at the top of a roof

Examples

The ancient cottage still had its original rooftree in place, supporting the entire roof structure.
During the restoration, workers discovered that the rooftree was infested with termites and needed replacement.
From the attic window, you could see sunlight streaming in where the rooftree joined the rafters.

Etymology

Old English 'hrōftrēow' (roof tree), from 'hrōf' (roof) + 'trēow' (tree, beam).
