arccotangent

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑrkˈoʊˌtæn.dʒənt / (AmE), / ˌɑːkˈɒ.tæn.dʒənt / (BrE)
the inverse function of the cotangent; the angle whose cotangent is a given number
inverse cotangent function (math)

Examples

The arccotangent function is commonly used in advanced calculus to solve equations involving cotangents.
To find the angle, take the arccotangent of the given value.
Many calculators do not have a dedicated button for arccotangent, so you may need to use other trigonometric identities.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cotangent - the trigonometric function cotangent

Etymology

Formed from 'arc' (referring to an angle) and 'cotangent' (a trigonometric function), modeled on similar terms like 'arcsine'.
