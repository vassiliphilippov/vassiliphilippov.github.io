bipartite

1. ADJ

/ baɪˈpɑːrtaɪt / (AmE), / baɪˈpɑːtaɪt / (BrE)
(especially of leaves or anatomical structures) divided into two distinct parts or sections, almost to the base.
divided into two parts nearly to the base

Examples

The leaf is bipartite, with each segment extending almost to the midrib.
Many fungi have bipartite fruiting bodies that are easily recognized.
The insect’s bipartite antennae help distinguish it from similar species.

Etymology

From Latin 'bipartitus' (divided into two), from 'bi-' (two) + 'partitus' (divided).

2. ADJ

/ baɪˈpɑːr.taɪt / (AmE), / bʌɪˈpɑː.tʌɪt / (BrE)
consisting of or involving two parts, elements, or parties
involving two parts or elements

Examples

The agreement was a bipartite contract signed by both companies.
A bipartite commission was established to resolve the dispute.
The bipartite structure of the document made its conditions clear to both parties.

Synset Examples

a bipartite document
a two-way treaty

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: multipartite - consisting of several or many parts

Etymology

From Latin bipartitus, past participle of bipartire (to divide into two parts), from bi- (two) + partire (to divide).
