seatbelt

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːtbɛlt / (AmE), / ˈsiːtbɛlt / (BrE)
A safety belt in a vehicle or aircraft designed to hold the occupant in place and reduce injury in the event of a crash or sudden stop.
safety belt in cars or planes

Examples

Please fasten your seatbelt before the plane takes off.
The driver forgot to wear his seatbelt, which is against the law.
Always check that children's seatbelts are securely fastened before starting the car.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: safety belt - belt designed to secure a person in a vehicle or aircraft

Etymology

Formed from 'seat' and 'belt', first attested mid-20th century in automotive context.
