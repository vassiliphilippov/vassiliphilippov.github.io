conchfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑntʃˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkɒntʃˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A small fish found in the West Indies that lives inside the mantle cavity of a living conch.
fish living in a conch's mantle cavity

Examples

The conchfish spends most of its life hidden within the shell of a live conch.
Marine biologists study the symbiotic relationship between the conchfish and its host.
We saw a rare conchfish while diving in the West Indies.

Etymology

From 'conch' (the marine mollusk) + 'fish', indicating its habitat.
