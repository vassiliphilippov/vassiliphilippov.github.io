ascesis

1. NOUN

/ əˈsiːsɪs / (AmE), / əˈsiːsɪs / (BrE)
The practice of rigorous self-denial and self-discipline, often for spiritual or religious purposes.
rigorous self-discipline or self-denial

Examples

Through years of ascesis, the monk achieved an inner calm few could imagine.
His commitment to ascesis was evident in his simple lifestyle and strict daily routines.
Some philosophies teach ascesis as a path to spiritual enlightenment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asceticism - severe self-discipline and avoidance of all forms of indulgence

Etymology

From Greek askēsis (exercise, training, practice), from askein (to exercise, train).
