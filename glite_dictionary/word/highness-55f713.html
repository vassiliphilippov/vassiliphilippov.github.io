highness

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈhaɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being high or lofty
quality of being high or lofty

Examples

The highness of the mountain made climbing it extremely challenging.
She admired the highness of his ideals and ambitions.
The highness of the shelf made it difficult to reach without a ladder.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lowness - the quality or state of being low

Etymology

From Middle English hignesse, from Old English hēannes (high position, superiority).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈhaɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being high in degree, amount, or intensity
the quality of being high in degree or amount

Examples

The highness of the mountain peaks amazed the climbers.
Many people complain about the highness of food prices these days.
The highness of his voice made it difficult to take him seriously.

Synset Examples

responsible for the highness of the rates

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lowness - the quality or state of being low in degree or amount

Etymology

From the adjective 'high' with the suffix '-ness', denoting quality or state.
