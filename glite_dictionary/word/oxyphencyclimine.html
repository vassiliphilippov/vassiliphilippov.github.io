oxyphencyclimine

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑksiˌfɛnsɪˈklaɪmin / (AmE), / ˌɒksɪˌfɛnsɪˈklɪmiːn / (BrE)
An anticholinergic drug used to treat peptic ulcers by reducing stomach acid secretion.
anticholinergic drug for peptic ulcers
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed oxyphencyclimine to help manage the patient's peptic ulcer symptoms.
Since starting oxyphencyclimine, she experienced fewer episodes of stomach pain.
Some patients may experience dry mouth as a side effect of oxyphencyclimine.

Etymology

Formed from chemical roots: oxy- (oxygen), phen- (phenyl group), cycl- (cycle), imine (a class of compounds).
