superintelligence

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuːpərɪnˈtɛlədʒəns / (AmE), / ˌsuːpərɪnˈtɛlɪdʒəns / (BrE)
The ability or quality of possessing an intelligence far exceeding that of the most gifted human minds; remarkable or exceptional intelligence, often used especially in the context of advanced artificial intelligence.
exceptionally high intelligence

Examples

Many scientists debate whether superintelligence would be a benefit or a threat to humanity.
Philosophers discuss the ethical implications of creating artificial superintelligence.
Some believe that a machine with superintelligence could solve complex global challenges.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: intelligence - the ability to acquire and apply knowledge and skills
  • Degree Lower: genius - exceptional intellectual or creative power or other natural ability

Etymology

Formed from 'super-' (above, beyond) and 'intelligence' (from Latin intelligentia, understanding).
