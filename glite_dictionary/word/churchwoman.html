churchwoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɝːtʃˌwʊm.ən / (AmE), / ˈtʃɜːtʃˌwʊm.ən / (BrE)
a woman who is a member of the clergy or actively involved in church activities
female clergy or active church member

Examples

The churchwoman led the community meeting with grace and compassion.
As a dedicated churchwoman, she volunteered her time to help the less fortunate.
Many people admired the churchwoman for her tireless devotion to the congregation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: churchman - a male clergyman or a man active in church activities

Etymology

Formed from 'church' + 'woman', modeled after 'churchman'.
