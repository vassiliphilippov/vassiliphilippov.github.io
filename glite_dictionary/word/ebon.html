ebon

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛb.ən / (AmE), / ˈɛb.ən / (BrE)
Very dark; deep black in color, often used in literary or poetic contexts.
very dark, deep black

Examples

The sky was an ebon expanse, dotted with countless stars.
She wore an ebon gown to the elegant evening ball.
The artist used ebon paint to create a sense of depth in the shadows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ebony - A dense black wood, or black in color

Etymology

Shortened form of 'ebony,' from Latin 'ebenus,' from Greek 'ébenos' (ebony tree).
