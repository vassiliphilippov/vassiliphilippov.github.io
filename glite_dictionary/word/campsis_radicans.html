Campsis radicans

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæmp.sɪs ˈræd.ɪ.kənz / (AmE), / ˈkæmp.sɪs ˈræd.ɪ.kənz / (BrE)
a North American woody climbing vine with pinnate leaves and large red trumpet-shaped flowers, often grown as an ornamental plant
North American woody vine with trumpet-shaped flowers

Examples

The Campsis radicans adds a vibrant splash of color to many American gardens each summer.
We planted a Campsis radicans along the fence to attract hummingbirds to our backyard.
The large trumpet-shaped flowers of Campsis radicans make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trumpet vine - common name for Campsis radicans, a climbing plant with trumpet-shaped flowers

Etymology

From Latin, 'Campsis' from Greek kampē (curvature) and 'radicans' meaning rooting.
