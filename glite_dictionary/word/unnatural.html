unnatural

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈnætʃərəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈnatʃ(ə)rəl / (BrE)
not in accordance with nature or what is expected in the natural world; contrary to nature or normal human behavior
contrary to nature or normal

Examples

His sudden outburst of anger seemed completely unnatural to everyone in the room.
The bright purple color of the river was distinctly unnatural and alarming.
Many believed that working all night was an unnatural way for humans to live.

Synset Examples

an unnatural death
the child's unnatural interest in death

Related Terms

  • Antonym: natural - existing in or caused by nature; not artificial

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) and 'natural'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01574863-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈnætʃɚəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈnætʃərəl / (BrE)
not typical, usual, or normal; deviating from what is expected or considered natural
not normal or typical

Examples

The scientist observed an unnatural pattern in the test results, which led her to repeat the experiment.
Her unnatural calmness during the crisis surprised everyone in the office.
Using too many filters made the photograph look unnatural and artificial.

Synset Examples

abnormal powers of concentration
abnormal amounts of rain
abnormal circumstances
an abnormal interest in food

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abnormal - deviating from what is normal or usual
  • Antonym: natural - existing in or caused by nature; not made or caused by humankind

Etymology

From un- (not) + natural, from Latin 'naturalis' (by birth, natural).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01600178-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈnætʃɚl / (AmE), / ʌnˈnætʃrəl / (BrE)
behaving or speaking in a way that is artificial or forced, rather than honest or genuine, often to impress others
artificial or forced in behavior or speech

Examples

Her attempt at friendliness came across as unnatural to everyone in the room.
He put on an unnatural accent in an effort to impress his audience.
There was an unnatural stiffness in his voice whenever he spoke to his boss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artificial - not natural, made or produced by humans
  • Antonym: genuine - truly what something is said to be; authentic and sincere

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'natural', from Latin 'natura' (nature).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00073656-a
Synset POS: a
