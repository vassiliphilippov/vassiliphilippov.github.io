sequenator

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːkwəˌneɪtər / (AmE), / ˈsiːkwɪˌneɪtə / (BrE)
an apparatus used in chemistry to determine the sequence of monomers in a polymer
apparatus for sequencing monomers in a polymer

Examples

The laboratory ordered a new sequenator for analyzing protein samples.
Using the sequenator, the scientists identified the order of amino acids in the polypeptide chain.
Proper maintenance of the sequenator is essential for accurate results in DNA analysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sequencer - device for determining the sequence of biological polymers

Etymology

Formed from 'sequence' + '-ator', following the pattern of 'sequencer'.
