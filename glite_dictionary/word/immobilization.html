immobilization

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌmoʊbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌməʊbɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of preventing movement or making someone or something unable to move
preventing or limiting movement

Examples

The doctor recommended immobilization of his injured leg to promote healing.
Extended immobilization can sometimes lead to muscle weakness in patients.
Due to the landslide, immobilization of all vehicles in the area was necessary.

Synset Examples

the storm caused complete immobilization of the rescue team

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restraint - the act of holding something back or limiting its movement
  • Antonym: freedom - the power or right to move freely

Etymology

From French 'immobilisation', from Latin 'im-' (not) + 'mobilis' (movable) + '-ation' (indicating action or process).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˌmoʊbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌməʊbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of preventing movement of a body part, usually by using a cast or splint, to promote proper healing
preventing movement to aid healing
medical

Examples

After the accident, immobilization of her arm was achieved with a splint.
Proper immobilization is crucial to ensure bones heal in the correct position.
The doctor recommended immediate immobilization of the ankle to prevent further injury.

Synset Examples

immobilization of the injured knee was necessary

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fixation - the process of making something immobile or fixed in position, especially in medical treatment

Etymology

From Latin 'immobilis' (immovable) and English '-ization' (action or process).
