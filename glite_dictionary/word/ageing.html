ageing

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈeɪdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
The process of acquiring desirable qualities or improving in character by being left undisturbed for some time, especially with reference to food, drink, or materials.
improving by being left over time

Examples

The ageing of cheese is crucial to its flavor development.
Many winemakers believe that careful ageing enhances wine's complexity.
The process of ageing beef results in more tender and flavorful meat.

2. NOUN

/ ˈeɪdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈeɪdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of becoming older and showing the signs or effects of advancing age
process of growing older

Examples

The ageing process can lead to changes in the skin, hair, and vision.
Researchers are studying ways to slow down ageing and increase health in later life.
Many societies face challenges related to their ageing population.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aging - alternate spelling, especially in American English

Etymology

From the verb 'age' with the suffix '-ing'.

3. ADJ

/ ˈeɪdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈeɪdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
becoming older; relating to or characteristic of the process of growing old.
growing old; becoming older

Examples

The company's products target the needs of an ageing population.
His ageing parents required more assistance around the house.
Researchers are studying ways to slow down the ageing process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elderly - old or advanced in years
  • Antonym: youthful - having the qualities of young people; not old

Etymology

From the verb 'age' + '-ing'.
