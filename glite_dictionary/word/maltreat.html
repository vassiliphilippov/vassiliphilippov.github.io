to maltreat

1. VERB

/ mælˈtrit / (AmE), / mælˈtriːt / (BrE)
to treat a person or animal cruelly or unfairly
treat cruelly or unfairly

Examples

The animal shelter rescues dogs that people maltreat or neglect.
It is illegal to maltreat prisoners under international law.
She was accused of having maltreated several children in her care.

Synset Examples

This boss abuses his workers
She is always stepping on others to get ahead

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abuse - to treat someone cruelly or violently
  • Synonym: to mistreat - to treat someone or something badly

Etymology

From Old French maltraiter, from mal (badly) + traiter (to treat).
