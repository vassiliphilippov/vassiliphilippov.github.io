Tetraclinis

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛtrəˈklaɪnɪs / (AmE), / ˌtɛtrəˈklɪnɪs / (BrE)
A small evergreen coniferous tree, native to North Africa, also known as the sandarac tree, valued for its resin and wood.
a small evergreen coniferous tree (sandarac tree)

Examples

The resin harvested from Tetraclinis is often used in varnishes and incense.
Tetraclinis forests can be found in parts of Morocco and Algeria.
Botanists studied the growth patterns of Tetraclinis in arid environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sandarac - resin obtained from Tetraclinis

Etymology

From Greek tetra- (four) and kline (bed), relating to the arrangement of cone scales.

2. NOUN

/ ˌtɛtrəˈklaɪnɪs / (AmE), / ˌtɛtrəˈklaɪnɪs / (BrE)
a genus of evergreen coniferous trees in the cypress family, containing the sandarac tree native to North Africa and southern Europe
a genus of trees (sandarac tree)

Examples

The Tetraclinis genus is native to North Africa and parts of the western Mediterranean.
Resin from the Tetraclinis tree, also known as sandarac, has been used in varnishes for centuries.
Botanists studied a rare specimen of Tetraclinis growing near the Spanish coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sandarac - resin derived from the Tetraclinis tree

Etymology

From Greek tetra- (four) + klinein (to incline), referring to the arrangement of leaves or branches.
