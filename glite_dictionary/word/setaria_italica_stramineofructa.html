Setaria italica stramineofructa

1. NOUN

/ sɛˈtɛəriə ɪˈtælɪkə strəˌmiːnoʊˈfrʌktə / (AmE), / sɛˈtɛːrɪə ɪˈtalɪkə stramiːnəʊˈfrʌkta / (BrE)
A variety of foxtail millet characterized by large, drooping spikes with yellow grains.
variety of millet with yellow grains

Examples

Farmers in the region cultivate Setaria italica stramineofructa for its high yield and drought resistance.
The golden hues of Setaria italica stramineofructa make it easy to distinguish at harvest.
Researchers are studying Setaria italica stramineofructa to improve millet crop resilience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foxtail millet - a type of millet, scientific name Setaria italica

Etymology

From Latin: Setaria (bristle grass genus), italica (Italian), stramineofructa (having straw-colored fruit).
