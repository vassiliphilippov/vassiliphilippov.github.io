dignity

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪɡ.nə.ti / (AmE), / ˈdɪɡ.nɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality of being worthy of respect or honor
worthiness of respect or honor

Examples

Even in defeat, she maintained her dignity and poise.
We must treat every person with dignity and compassion, regardless of their background.
Losing his job was difficult, but he faced the challenge with dignity.

Synset Examples

it was beneath his dignity to cheat
showed his true dignity when under pressure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honor - high respect or esteem
  • Antonym: humiliation - loss of self-respect or dignity

Etymology

From Latin 'dignitas' (worthiness), from 'dignus' (worthy).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɪɡ.nə.t̬i / (AmE), / ˈdɪɡ.nɪ.ti / (BrE)
A high rank, position, or title; an office or station of honor or authority.
high rank or official position

Examples

He ascended to a position of great dignity in the royal court.
The ambassadors carried themselves with the dignity of their high office.
The ceremony was attended by several men of dignity and influence.

Synset Examples

he respected the dignity of the emissaries

Etymology

From Latin 'dignitas' (worthiness, rank, dignity).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈdɪɡnəti / (AmE), / ˈdɪɡnəti / (BrE)
a composed or serious manner or style, often displayed in formal situations or through an appearance of self-respect and honor
a composed or formal manner or appearance

Examples

He carried himself with remarkable dignity during the ceremony.
Her dignity was evident in her calm and formal demeanor.
Even in difficult times, he maintained his dignity and composure.

Synset Examples

he behaved with great dignity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gravity - seriousness of manner or bearing
  • Synonym: solemnity - the state or quality of being serious and dignified
  • Antonym: informality - the lack of formality or dignity

Etymology

From Latin 'dignitas' (worthiness, honor, rank).
