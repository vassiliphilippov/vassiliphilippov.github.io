thwart

1. NOUN

/ θwɔrt / (AmE), / θwɔːt / (BrE)
a structural crosspiece extending between the sides of a boat, serving as a seat for rowers or to strengthen the hull
crosspiece seat in a rowboat

Examples

She sat on the thwart and prepared to row across the lake.
The old boat's thwart needed repairs before it could be safely used.
Extra gear was stored under the thwart to keep the deck clear.

Etymology

From Old Norse þvert (across).

2. VERB

/ θwɔrt / (AmE), / θwɔːt / (BrE)
to prevent someone from doing what they want to do; to hinder or oppose successfully
hinder or prevent the plans of

Examples

They tried to thwart the plan by leaking the details to the media.
The detective's quick thinking thwarted the thief's escape attempt.
Security measures are in place to thwart cyber attacks on the system.

Synset Examples

What ultimately frustrated every challenger was Ruth's amazing September surge
foil your opponent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to frustrate - to prevent someone from accomplishing a purpose
  • Synonym: foil - to prevent the success of someone or something
  • Antonym: aid - to help or support someone in achieving something

Etymology

From Old Norse 'thvert' (transverse), meaning across.
