pragmatics

1. NOUN

/ præɡˈmætɪks / (AmE), / præɡˈmatɪks / (BrE)
the branch of linguistics concerned with the study of language in use, particularly the ways context contributes to meaning
linguistics: study of language use in context
medical

Examples

She is an expert in pragmatics, focusing on how people interpret meaning in conversation.
Modern linguists often distinguish between pragmatics and semantics when analyzing language.
The field of pragmatics explores how context influences what speakers mean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: semantics - study of meaning in language
  • Synonym: syntax - study of arrangement of words in sentences

Etymology

From Greek pragma (deed, act) via modern formation.
