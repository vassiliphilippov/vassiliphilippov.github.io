purser

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrsər / (AmE), / ˈpɜːsə / (BrE)
an officer on a ship responsible for financial accounts and the welfare of passengers
ship officer managing accounts and passengers

Examples

The purser was responsible for recording all transactions during the voyage.
If you have any questions about your cabin, please contact the purser.
After dinner, the purser distributed information about the next port of call.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steward - a person whose job is to look after the passengers on a ship or aircraft
  • Antonym: captain - the person in command of a ship

Etymology

From Middle English 'porsere', from Anglo-Norman 'pursere', from 'purse'.
