Trionychidae

1. NOUN

/ traɪ.əˈnɪk.ɪˌdi / (AmE), / traɪ.əˈnɪk.ɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of soft-shelled freshwater turtles found in Africa, Asia, and North America.
family of soft-shelled turtles

Examples

The Trionychidae are well known for their leathery shells and flattened bodies.
Scientists have discovered new species within the Trionychidae family in Southeast Asia.
Unlike most turtles, members of the Trionychidae have a soft, flexible shell rather than a hard one.

Etymology

From modern Latin Trionychidae, based on Greek tri- (three) + onyx (claw, nail), referencing the three claws on each limb of these turtles.
