consolidative

1. ADJ

/ kənˈsɑlɪˌdeɪtɪv / (AmE), / kənˈsɒlɪˌdeɪtɪv / (BrE)
having the tendency or effect of combining things into a single unit
tending to combine into a single unit

Examples

The two companies entered into a consolidative merger to streamline their operations.
A consolidative approach was adopted to unify the various departments under one management.
Medical imaging can reveal consolidative changes in lung tissue characteristic of certain diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to consolidate - to make (something) physically stronger or more solid; to combine into a single unit

Etymology

From Latin 'consolidare' (to make firm) + English suffix '-ive'.

2. ADJ

/ kənˈsɑːlɪˌdeɪtɪv / (AmE), / kənˈsɒlɪˌdeɪtɪv / (BrE)
serving or tending to consolidate or bring together into a single, unified whole
tending to consolidate

Examples

The company pursued a consolidative strategy to combine its various business units.
In medicine, consolidative therapy may be used to prevent a disease from returning after initial treatment.
His approach was more consolidative than expansive, focusing on strengthening what was already achieved.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to consolidate - to make something physically stronger or more solid; to combine into a single whole

Etymology

From 'consolidate' plus the suffix '-ive', meaning 'having the nature of.'
