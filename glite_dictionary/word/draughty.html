draughty

1. ADJ

/ ˈdræf.ti / (AmE), / ˈdrɑːf.ti / (BrE)
(of a place) having currents of cold air blowing through it, typically because it is not airtight.
having cold air currents; not airtight
british

Examples

The old house was so draughty that we had to wear coats indoors.
Despite the heating, the room felt draughty in the winter.
We fitted new windows to make the cottage less draughty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drafty - having currents of cold air (American spelling)

Etymology

From 'draught' (British spelling of 'draft'), meaning a current of air.
