scoured

1. ADJ

/ ˈskaʊɚd / (AmE), / ˈskaʊəd / (BrE)
(of a surface) worn away or eroded, as by the action of water, ice, or wind.
worn away by natural forces

Examples

The scoured rocks along the riverbank showed signs of centuries of erosion.
After the glacier retreated, it left a scoured landscape dotted with boulders and bare earth.
High winds had scoured the desert surface, exposing ancient stones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eroded - gradually worn away by natural agents

Etymology

Past participle of 'scour', from Middle English 'scouren' (to clean, polish).
