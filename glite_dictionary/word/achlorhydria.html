achlorhydria

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪklɔrˈhaɪdriə / (AmE), / ˌeɪklɔːˈhɪdriə / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by an absence or deficiency of free hydrochloric acid in the gastric juice.
absence of stomach acid
medical

Examples

Patients suffering from achlorhydria may experience digestive problems due to the lack of stomach acid.
Doctors often diagnose achlorhydria in individuals with chronic gastritis or severe anemia.
The presence of achlorhydria increases the risk of certain gastrointestinal infections.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hypochlorhydria - reduced hydrochloric acid in the stomach, less severe than achlorhydria

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (without) + 'chloros' (green, referring to chlorine) + 'hydor' (water), relating to hydrochloric acid.
