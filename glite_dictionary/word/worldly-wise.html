worldly-wise

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɜːrldli ˌwaɪz / (AmE), / ˈwɜːldli ˌwaɪz / (BrE)
having experience and knowledge of the practical or social aspects of life, often with a sense of being shrewd or sophisticated
experienced and shrewd in worldly matters

Examples

As a worldly-wise traveler, she easily navigated the complexities of foreign cities.
The novel's protagonist grows from a naive youth into a worldly-wise adult.
His worldly-wise attitude impressed his colleagues during the business negotiations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: naive - lacking experience and sophistication
  • Synonym: sophisticated - having a lot of worldly experience and knowledge
