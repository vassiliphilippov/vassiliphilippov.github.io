to sequestrate

1. VERB

/ sɪˈkwɛstreɪt / (AmE), / sɪˈkwɛstreɪt / (BrE)
to set apart, isolate, or separate from others, especially by legal or formal means
to isolate or set apart

Examples

The committee decided to sequestrate the confidential documents for security reasons.
During the experiment, the scientist sequestrated the samples to prevent contamination.
In some medical procedures, it is necessary to sequestrate the affected tissue from the surrounding area.

Synset Examples

The dentist sequesters the tooth he is working on

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sequester - to isolate or set apart, especially for protection or secrecy

Etymology

From Latin sequestrare (to hand over to a trustee), from sequester (trustee, mediator).

2. VERB

/ sɪˈkwɛstreɪt / (AmE), / sɪˈkwɛstreɪt / (BrE)
to isolate or keep someone or something apart from others; to separate or set aside.
to isolate or set apart

Examples

During the trial, the judge decided to sequestrate the jury to ensure their impartiality.
He often sequestrates himself in his office when he needs to concentrate on writing.
After the chemical spill, authorities sequestrated the contaminated area to protect the public.

Synset Examples

He sequestered himself in his study to write a book

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sequester - to isolate or hide away; closely related meaning and usage

Etymology

From Latin sequestratus, past participle of sequestrare (to hand over to a trustee), from sequester (trustee, depositary).
