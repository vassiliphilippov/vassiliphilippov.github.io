hidebound

1. ADJ

/ ˈhaɪdˌbaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdˌbaʊnd / (BrE)
unwilling or unable to change because of tradition or convention; stubbornly conservative and narrow-minded
stubbornly conservative; narrow-minded

Examples

His hidebound attitude prevented the company from adopting new technologies.
Some institutions remain hidebound by outdated traditions and practices.
She found her supervisor too hidebound to consider creative solutions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conservative - favoring traditional views and values; tending to resist change
  • Antonym: open-minded - willing to consider new ideas; unprejudiced

Etymology

Originally referring to cattle with skin (hide) tightly bound to the body due to poor condition; figurative use from the 16th century.
