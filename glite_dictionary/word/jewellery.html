jewellery

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒuːəlri / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːəlri / (BrE)
Objects such as rings, necklaces, or bracelets that are made from precious metals and gems and worn for decoration.
decorative objects made from precious metals and gems

Examples

She wore her finest jewellery to the wedding ceremony.
The thief stole a box full of expensive jewellery during the night.
Antique jewellery can often be worth a great deal of money.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jewelry - Variant spelling used in American English

Etymology

From Old French 'jouel' (jewel) + '-ery'.
