agnate

1. NOUN

/ ˈæɡˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈaɡ.neɪt / (BrE)
a person related to another through the male line or father's side of the family
person related through the father's side

Examples

According to ancient Roman law, only the agnate heirs could inherit property.
Traditionally, titles would pass to the eldest male agnate in the family.
She discovered a distant agnate relative while researching her family tree.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cognate - person related on the mother's side or female line

Etymology

From Latin agnatus (relative on the father's side), from ad- (to) + gnatus (born).
