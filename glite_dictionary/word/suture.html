suture

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuːtʃər / (AmE), / ˈsjuːtʃə / (BrE)
a stitch or row of stitches used to close a wound or incision in surgery
stitch used in surgery
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully placed a suture to close the incision.
It is important to remove the suture after the wound has healed.
Dissolvable suture materials are often used for internal wounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stitch - a single loop of thread or yarn in sewing or medical procedures

Etymology

From Latin sutura (a seam, a sewing together), from suere (to sew).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsuːtʃər / (AmE), / ˈsuːtʃə / (BrE)
an immovable joint between two bones, especially between the bones of the skull
immovable joint between bones of the skull
medical

Examples

The coronal suture separates the frontal bone from the parietal bones in the human skull.
Growth can sometimes occur along the suture lines in children’s skulls.
Cranial suture patterns can be used to estimate age in forensic science.

Etymology

From Latin 'sutura' meaning 'a seam', from 'suere' (to sew).

3. NOUN

/ ˈsuːtʃər / (AmE), / ˈsjuːtʃə / (BrE)
a thread or wire used by surgeons to stitch tissues together after an injury or operation
surgical thread for stitching tissues
medical

Examples

The surgeon used a suture to close the wound after the operation.
After the stitches dissolved, no trace of the suture was visible on her skin.
The nurse carefully removed each suture from the patient's incision site.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stitch - a loop of thread or yarn resulting from a single pass or movement of the needle in sewing

Etymology

From Latin 'sutura' (a seam), from 'suere' (to sew).

4. VERB

/ ˈsutʃər / (AmE), / ˈsjuːtʃə / (BrE)
to join tissue or parts of the body together by sewing with a needle and thread, especially after surgery
to sew tissue together (medical)
medical

Examples

The surgeon will suture the incision to close the wound.
After the operation, the nurse helped to suture the deep cut.
Doctors frequently suture both internal tissues and the skin during procedures.
