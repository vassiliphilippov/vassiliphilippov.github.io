Channidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈkænɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈkanɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of predatory freshwater fish, commonly known as snakeheads, found in Africa and Asia.
family of fish known as snakeheads

Examples

The family Channidae includes several species known for their aggressive behavior and adaptability.
Conservationists are concerned about the invasive potential of Channidae in non-native habitats.
Several members of Channidae are prized in Asian cuisines for their firm, white flesh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snakehead - a predatory freshwater fish of the family Channidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'channe' (a type of fish) and the zoological family suffix '-idae'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkænəˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈkanɪdˌiː / (BrE)
A family of predatory freshwater fish, commonly known as snakeheads, native to Asia and Africa.
family of snakehead fish

Examples

Fossils suggest that members of the Channidae family have existed for millions of years.
The Channidae are known for their elongated bodies and air-breathing abilities.
Research on Channidae has increased as some species have become invasive in new habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snakehead - a predatory freshwater fish of the family Channidae

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Channidae', based on Greek 'channe' (a kind of fish) + '-idae' (family suffix).
