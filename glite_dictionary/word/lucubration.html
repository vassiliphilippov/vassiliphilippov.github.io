lucubration

1. NOUN

/ luːkjuˈbreɪʃən / (AmE), / luːkjuːˈbreɪʃən / (BrE)
serious or laborious study, especially when carried out late at night; elaborate or intensive meditation or writing
intense or painstaking study or thought

Examples

His latest novel was the result of years of solitary lucubration.
The philosopher’s lucubration impressed his peers with its depth and detail.
During long nights of lucubration, she composed her dissertation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: study - the act of devoting time and attention to acquiring knowledge

Etymology

From Latin lucubrationem (study by lamplight) from 'lucubrare' (to work by lamplight).

2. NOUN

/ ˌluː.kjuˈbreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌluː.kjʊˈbreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a literary work or piece of writing produced through intensive study and laborious thought, often with a solemn tone
a work born of laborious study or thought

Examples

The professor's latest lucubration was published in a prestigious academic journal.
After years of research, her lucubration offered a fresh perspective on the subject.
His dense lucubration required several readings to fully comprehend its arguments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treatise - a formal and systematic written discourse on a subject
  • Synonym: essay - a short piece of writing on a particular subject

Etymology

From Latin lucubrationem (nocturnal study), from lucubrare (to work by lamplight).
