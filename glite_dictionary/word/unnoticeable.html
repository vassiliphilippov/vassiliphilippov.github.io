unnoticeable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈnoʊtɪsəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈnəʊtɪsəbl̩ / (BrE)
so small or ordinary as not to attract attention or be easily seen; not noticeable
not easily seen or noticed

Examples

The stain on the carpet was so faint it was almost unnoticeable.
She wore makeup in such a subtle way that it was unnoticeable.
The security cameras were installed in unnoticeable corners of the room.

Synset Examples

her clothes were simple and unnoticeable

Related Terms

  • Antonym: noticeable - easy to see or recognize; attracting attention

Etymology

From un- (not) + noticeable (able to be noticed).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈnoʊtɪsəbl / (AmE), / ʌnˈnəʊtɪsəbl / (BrE)
not easily seen or attracting attention; difficult to notice.
not easily seen or attracting notice

Examples

The stain was so unnoticeable that no one mentioned it during the inspection.
He wore makeup to make the scar as unnoticeable as possible.
She slipped out of the room in an unnoticeable manner.

Synset Examples

an unnoticeable cigarette burn on the carpet
an obscure flaw

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invisible - not able to be seen
  • Antonym: conspicuous - easily seen or attracting notice

Etymology

From un- (not) + noticeable, formed from notice + -able.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00583988-s
Synset POS: s
