method

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛθ.əd / (AmE), / ˈmɛθ.əd / (BrE)
a particular way of doing something, especially in a systematic or organized manner involving logical steps
systematic way of doing something

Examples

She developed a new method for analyzing the chemical samples.
Different teachers often have their own methods for managing classrooms.
The scientific method requires careful observation and experimentation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: procedure - a series of actions conducted in a certain order or manner
  • Synonym: system - an organized way of doing something as a whole
  • Synonym: technique - a particular way of carrying out a task, especially one requiring skill
  • Antonym: chaos - complete disorder and lack of method

Etymology

From Latin 'methodus', from Greek 'methodos' meaning 'pursuit' or 'systematic course'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛθ.əd / (AmE), / ˈmeθ.əd / (BrE)
a technique of acting in which the actor draws on personal emotions and experiences to create a believable performance, especially as developed by Stanislavsky and popularized in American theatre and film
technique of acting using personal emotions

Examples

She studied method acting to better connect with her characters on stage.
Many famous actors credit the method for their most powerful performances.
The director encouraged the cast to use the method to bring authenticity to their roles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: method acting - a style of acting in which the actor uses personal experience and emotion to portray a role
