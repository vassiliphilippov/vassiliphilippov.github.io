rock-ribbed

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɑk ˌrɪbd / (AmE), / ˈrɒk ˌrɪbd / (BrE)
(of a place) abounding in or bordered by rocky cliffs or steep rock formations.
having many rocky cliffs or escarpments

Examples

The hikers marveled at the rock-ribbed coastline stretching for miles along the bay.
Her family has a cottage on a rock-ribbed peninsula where the sea crashes against granite cliffs.
Photographers are drawn to the rock-ribbed shores for their dramatic beauty.

Synset Examples

the rock-ribbed coast of Maine

2. ADJ

/ ˈrɑk ˌrɪbd / (AmE), / ˈrɒk ˌrɪbd / (BrE)
(of a person or their beliefs) very firm, uncompromising, or stubbornly conservative, especially in politics.
uncompromising or staunch (often in politics)

Examples

He was known as a rock-ribbed conservative who rarely strayed from his party's positions.
The senator's rock-ribbed beliefs made bipartisan cooperation challenging.
Her rock-ribbed commitment to tradition impressed some and frustrated others.

Synset Examples

an inflexible (or die-hard) conservative
rock-ribbed republican

Related Terms

  • Synonym: die-hard - resistant to change, stubbornly conservative
  • Synonym: inflexible - unwilling to change or compromise

Etymology

Figurative use, alluding to something as solid and unyielding as rock-ribbed geology.
