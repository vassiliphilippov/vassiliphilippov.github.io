nard

1. NOUN

/ nɑrd / (AmE), / nɑːd / (BrE)
A fragrant ointment made from the spikenard plant, used as a perfume or medicinal oil in ancient times.
aromatic ointment from spikenard plant

Examples

In ancient Rome, nard was prized for its pleasant fragrance and medicinal properties.
The Bible mentions nard as a precious substance used to anoint Jesus' feet.
Archaeologists discovered traces of nard in the burial chambers of Egyptian pharaohs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spikenard - an aromatic plant used to make nard

Etymology

From Latin nardus, from Greek nardos, ultimately from Sanskrit naladaḥ.
