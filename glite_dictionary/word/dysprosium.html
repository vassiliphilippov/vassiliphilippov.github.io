dysprosium

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈproʊziəm / (AmE), / dɪsˈprəʊziəm / (BrE)
a metallic element of the rare earth group, atomic number 66, used in alloys and magnetic materials
chemical element, rare earth metal

Examples

Magnets made with dysprosium are stronger and retain their strength at higher temperatures.
Researchers studied the effects of adding dysprosium to improve the stability of the alloy.
The periodic table lists dysprosium with the atomic number 66.

Etymology

From Greek dysprositos, meaning 'hard to get at,' due to its difficulty of isolation.

2. NOUN

/ dɪsˈproʊziəm / (AmE), / dɪsˈprəʊziəm / (BrE)
a chemical element (symbol Dy), a trivalent metallic element of the rare earth group, used in magnets and nuclear reactors
a rare earth metallic element (Dy)

Examples

Scientists use dysprosium in the manufacture of high-performance magnets for electronic devices.
The price of dysprosium has risen due to its critical role in green technologies.
A team of researchers isolated dysprosium from mineral samples using advanced extraction techniques.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: holmium - a trivalent metallic rare earth element, similar uses
  • Synonym: terbium - a related rare earth metallic element, used in electronics and magnets

Etymology

From Greek 'dysprositos' (hard to get at), named for its difficult extraction.
