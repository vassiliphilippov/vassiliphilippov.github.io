unilateralist

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuː.nəˈlæt̬.ə.rə.lɪst / (AmE), / ˌjuː.nɪˈlæt.ər.ə.lɪst / (BrE)
A person who supports or advocates for unilateral action, especially in international relations or politics, without seeking the agreement or cooperation of other parties.
advocate of unilateral action

Examples

The senator, known as a proud unilateralist, often pushed for independent national policies.
Critics argue that a unilateralist approach can damage long-term international relationships.
While some favor cooperation with allies, others in the administration are strong unilateralists.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: multilateralist - advocate of multilateral action or cooperation
  • Synonym: unilateralism - policy of taking unilateral action

Etymology

From unilateral + -ist, where 'unilateral' is from Latin 'unus' (one) and 'lateralis' (side).
