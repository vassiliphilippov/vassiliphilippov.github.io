Conyza

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.nə.zə / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.ɪ.zə / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the daisy family, commonly found as weeds or wildflowers in North America.
a weed or wildflower genus (daisy family)

Examples

Conyza can quickly spread and dominate disturbed soil in urban gardens.
Researchers studied the resistance of Conyza populations to common herbicides.
Some wildflower enthusiasts appreciate Conyza for its ecological value despite its weedy reputation.

Etymology

From Greek kōnyzē (a kind of plant).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːnɪzə / (AmE), / ˈkɒnɪzə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, commonly found as weeds or wildflowers in the Americas.
genus of American weeds or wildflowers

Examples

Many fields are overrun with Conyza during the summer months.
The botanist identified several species belonging to the genus Conyza on the prairie.
Gardeners often struggle to control Conyza because of its rapid spread.

Etymology

From Greek 'konyza', the ancient name for fleabane.
