Tongan

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɑːŋən / (AmE), / ˈtɒŋən / (BrE)
of or relating to Tonga, its people, or their language.
relating to Tonga or its people

Examples

The Tongan culture is rich in tradition and ceremony.
Many Tongan communities have settled in New Zealand and Australia.
We enjoyed delicious food at a Tongan festival last summer.

Synset Examples

Tongan beaches

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tonga - island country in the South Pacific Ocean

Etymology

From Tonga + -an, denoting origin or association.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɑːŋən / (AmE), / ˈtɒŋən / (BrE)
the Polynesian language spoken by the people of Tonga
Polynesian language of Tonga

Examples

She is currently studying Tongan at university as part of her linguistics degree.
The book provides an introduction to the grammar and vocabulary of Tongan.
He grew up speaking both English and Tongan at home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tonga - the Pacific island country

Etymology

From Tonga, the name of the Pacific island nation, plus English '-an'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈtɑŋɡən / (AmE), / ˈtɒŋɡən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Tonga, or a person of Tongan descent
a native or citizen of Tonga

Examples

Her father is a proud Tongan who teaches traditional music.
Several Tongans competed at the Olympic Games that year.
As a Tongan, he celebrates important cultural festivals with his family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Samoan - a native or inhabitant of Samoa
  • Synonym: Fijian - a native or inhabitant of Fiji

Etymology

From Tonga, the name of the country, plus English suffix -an.
