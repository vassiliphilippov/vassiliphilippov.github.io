Anser anser

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.sɚ ˈæn.sɚ / (AmE), / ˈansə ˈansə / (BrE)
A species of large grey wild goose native to Europe and Asia, commonly known as the greylag goose, and the ancestor of many domestic goose breeds.
species of wild grey goose

Examples

The migration patterns of Anser anser have been studied extensively by ornithologists.
Many domestic geese are descended from Anser anser.
Flocks of Anser anser can often be seen grazing in European wetlands during autumn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greylag goose - common name for Anser anser, a large wild goose species

Etymology

From Latin, where 'anser' means 'goose'.
