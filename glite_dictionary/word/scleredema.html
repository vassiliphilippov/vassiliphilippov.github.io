scleredema

1. NOUN

/ sklɛrəˈdimə / (AmE), / sklɛrɪˈdiːmə / (BrE)
A rare skin disease characterized by hardening and swelling of the skin, usually starting on the face.
skin disease with hard swelling
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with scleredema after developing swelling and stiffness in her facial skin.
Scleredema can sometimes spread to the neck and shoulders, making movement more difficult.
While scleredema is uncommon, prompt medical attention can help manage the symptoms.

Etymology

From Greek 'skleros' (hard) + 'oidēma' (swelling).
