bold

1. ADJ

/ boʊld / (AmE), / bəʊld / (BrE)
(of lines, shapes, or designs) clear, distinct, and easily noticeable; standing out strongly from its surroundings.
clear and distinct in appearance

Examples

The title was printed in bold letters at the top of the page.
She prefers bold patterns when decorating her apartment.
The artist used bold lines to define the shape of the figure.

Synset Examples

bold handwriting
a figure carved in bold relief
a bold design

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conspicuous - easily seen or noticed; attracting attention
  • Antonym: unobtrusive - not attracting attention; blending in
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ boʊld / (AmE), / bəʊld / (BrE)
a typeface or style of print in which the characters are thick and heavy, used for emphasis or distinction
heavy, thick style of typeface

Examples

Please highlight the key points in bold so they stand out.
The headline was printed in bold to grab readers' attention.
You can make your text bold by pressing the control and B keys together.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: italic - a typeface with characters slanted to the right
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06840269-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ boʊld / (AmE), / bəʊld / (BrE)
Willing to take risks; showing a willingness to face danger or act confidently without fear.
fearless and daring

Examples

The bold explorers set out to cross the icy wilderness despite the risks.
She made a bold decision to start her own business in a new country.
His bold remarks inspired others to speak up as well.

Synset Examples

bold settlers on some foreign shore
a bold speech
a bold adventure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brave - ready to face and endure danger or pain; showing courage
  • Synonym: courageous - not deterred by danger or pain; brave
  • Antonym: timid - showing a lack of courage or confidence; easily frightened

Etymology

From Old English 'beald' or 'bald' (bold, brave), related to Old High German 'bald' (bold, brave).

Source: WordNet

4. ADJ

/ boʊld / (AmE), / bəʊld / (BrE)
(of a rock face or cliff) very steep or prominent, often almost vertical.
very steep or prominent (rock/cliff)

Examples

The bold cliffs along the coast rose dramatically above the crashing waves.
A bold headland jutted into the sea, marking the edge of the peninsula.
Hikers admired the bold face of the mountain before beginning their ascent.

Synset Examples

a bluff headland
where the bold chalk cliffs of England rise
a sheer descent of rock

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sheer - (of a cliff or drop) very steep; almost vertical
  • Synonym: bluff - a steep cliff, bank, or promontory
