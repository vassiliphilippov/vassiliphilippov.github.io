comprehensiveness

1. NOUN

/ kəmˌprɛhənˈsɪvnəs / (AmE), / kəmˌprihɛnˈsɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of including or dealing with all or nearly all elements or aspects of something
quality of being comprehensive

Examples

The comprehensiveness of the report impressed everyone at the meeting.
A survey's accuracy often depends on the comprehensiveness of its questions.
She admired the comprehensiveness of the museum's collection.

Synset Examples

a teacher must have a breadth of knowledge of the subject
a man distinguished by the largeness and scope of his views

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breadth - wide scope or range; extent
  • Synonym: scope - extent of the area or subject matter that something deals with
  • Antonym: incompleteness - the state of not being complete or comprehensive

Etymology

From 'comprehensive' + '-ness', where 'comprehensive' derives from Latin 'comprehendere' (to seize, comprise, include).

2. NOUN

/ kəmˌprɛhənˈsɪvnəs / (AmE), / kəmˌprihɛnˈsɪvnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being complete and including all or nearly all elements or aspects of something.
quality of being thorough or complete

Examples

The comprehensiveness of the report impressed the entire management team.
She admired the comprehensiveness of his knowledge on the subject.
Our course design prioritizes comprehensiveness to ensure students are well prepared.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thoroughness - the quality of being very attentive to detail or complete
  • Antonym: superficiality - the quality of being shallow or lacking depth

Etymology

From 'comprehensive' + '-ness', with 'comprehensive' from Latin 'comprehendere' (to seize, include).
