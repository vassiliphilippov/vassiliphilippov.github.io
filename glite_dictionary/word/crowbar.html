crowbar

1. NOUN

/ ˈkroʊˌbɑr / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊˌbɑː / (BrE)
a heavy iron or steel bar with a curved end that is used as a lever for prying things apart or pulling out nails
heavy lever tool for prying

Examples

He used a crowbar to pry open the old wooden door.
The burglar broke in by forcing the window with a crowbar.
Workers often keep a crowbar in the truck for demolition jobs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lever - a rigid bar used to exert force

Etymology

From 'crow' (the resemblance of the curved end to a crow's beak) + 'bar'.
