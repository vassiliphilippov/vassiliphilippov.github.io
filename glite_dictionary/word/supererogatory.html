supererogatory

1. ADJ

/ ˌsuːpərɪˈrɑːɡəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˌsuːpərəˈrɒɡətəri / (BrE)
More than is required, needed, or expected; going beyond the call of duty.
more than required or necessary

Examples

Her donation was entirely supererogatory, given that the project was already fully funded.
Performing these supererogatory tasks will certainly impress your supervisor, though they aren't expected of you.
He made a supererogatory effort in helping his colleagues, beyond what was required by his job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redundant - not or no longer needed or useful
  • Synonym: superfluous - unnecessary, especially through being more than enough

Etymology

From Late Latin supererogatorius, from supererogare 'to pay over and above', from super- 'over' + erogare 'to pay out'.
