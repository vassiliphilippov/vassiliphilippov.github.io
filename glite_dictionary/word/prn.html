PRN

1. ADV

/ pi ɑr ɛn / (AmE), / pi ɑː ɛn / (BrE)
as needed; according to need (used especially in medical prescriptions to indicate that medication should be taken only when necessary)
as needed (medical term)
medical

Examples

The doctor advised taking the pain medication PRN for severe headaches.
Oxygen can be administered PRN if the patient experiences shortness of breath.
The nurse instructed him to use the inhaler PRN to manage asthma symptoms.

Synset Examples

add water as needed

Etymology

From Latin 'pro re nata' (for the thing born; as it arises).
