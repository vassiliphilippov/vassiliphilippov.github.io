integrated

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪntəˌɡreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈɪntɪˌɡreɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a place or organization) not segregated; open to people of all races or groups.
not segregated; open to all groups

Examples

The city worked hard to ensure its integrated public schools remained open to all students.
The company prides itself on being an integrated workplace welcoming diversity.
After decades of effort, the neighborhood became truly integrated.

Synset Examples

integrated schools

Related Terms

  • Antonym: segregated - restricted to a particular race or group; kept apart

Etymology

From Latin 'integratus', past participle of 'integrare' (to make whole).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01329100-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈɪn.təˌɡreɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈɪn.tɪ.ɡreɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
combined to form or function as a single, unified whole; having various parts or aspects working together effectively
combined into a unified whole

Examples

The company created an integrated marketing strategy to streamline its communications.
Our new software offers an integrated solution for project management and team collaboration.
The school is known for its integrated curriculum, bringing together science, art, and technology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unified - formed or united into a single entity
  • Antonym: fragmented - broken or separated into smaller parts

Etymology

From Latin 'integer' (whole, complete), via integrate + -ed.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02487032-s
Synset POS: s
