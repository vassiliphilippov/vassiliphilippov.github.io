Uranus

1. PROPN

/ ˈjʊr.ə.nəs, jʊˈreɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈjʊə.rə.nəs, jʊˈreɪ.nəs / (BrE)
the seventh planet from the Sun, a giant planet with bluish-green color, faint rings, and many moons
the seventh planet from the Sun

Examples

Uranus has at least 27 known moons orbiting its icy atmosphere.
Through a telescope, Uranus appears as a pale blue-green disk in the sky.
Voyager 2 provided stunning images of Uranus and its faint rings during its flyby in 1986.

Synset Examples

Uranus was discovered by William Herschel in 1781

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Neptune - the eighth planet from the Sun
  • Degree Lower: Saturn - the sixth planet from the Sun

Etymology

Named after Uranus, the ancient Greek god of the sky (Ouranos).

2. PROPN

/ ˈjʊrənəs, jʊˈreɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈjʊərənəs, jʊəˈreɪnəs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the god of the sky, husband of Gaea and father of the Titans.
Greek god of the sky

Examples

In ancient myths, Uranus was overthrown by his son Cronus.
Many artists have depicted the story of Uranus and his role in the creation of the Titans.
According to Greek mythology, Uranus was both the son and husband of Gaea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gaea - Greek goddess of the Earth, consort of Uranus
  • Synonym: Cronus - Greek Titan, son of Uranus and Gaea
  • Synonym: Titan - One of the children of Uranus and Gaea, a race of powerful deities

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Οὐρανός (Ouranos), meaning 'sky' or 'heaven'.
