grassroots

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡræsˌruːts / (AmE), / ˈɡrɑːsˌruːts / (BrE)
of or relating to ordinary people, especially as the fundamental source of support for a movement, organization, or campaign
originating among ordinary people

Examples

The candidate's grassroots campaign gained support from local communities across the country.
A grassroots organization led the effort to clean up the city's parks.
The new policy was shaped by grassroots activists demanding change.

Synset Examples

a grassroots movement for nuclear disarmament

Related Terms

  • Synonym: popular - carried out by or involving ordinary people of a society

Etymology

From the phrase 'grass roots', originally meaning the most basic level of an activity or organization.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡræsˌruts / (AmE), / ˈɡrɑːsˌruːts / (BrE)
Basic or fundamental; relating to the most essential level of an activity, organization, or movement.
basic; fundamental level

Examples

The committee made decisions based on grassroots concerns rather than top-down directives.
She emphasized the importance of grassroots support for the success of the campaign.
The reforms aim to address grassroots issues faced by ordinary citizens.

Synset Examples

the grassroots factor in making the decision

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fundamental - serving as an essential or central part

Etymology

From the noun 'grassroots', originally referring to the most basic level of society.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01862626-s
Synset POS: s
