sheathing

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃiðɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃiːðɪŋ / (BrE)
material or a layer applied to the outside of a structure, such as boards or panels, to protect, strengthen, and prepare it for further finishing or weatherproofing.
protective building covering

Examples

The builders installed sheathing on the exterior walls before applying the siding.
Plywood sheathing provides both strength and a base for other materials in modern construction.
Removing the old sheathing revealed signs of water damage underneath.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cladding - a covering or coating on a structure or material
  • Synonym: insulation - material used to protect, seal, or insulate buildings

Etymology

From 'sheathe', meaning to cover with a protective layer, plus -ing.
