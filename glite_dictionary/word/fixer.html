fixer

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪksər / (AmE), / ˈfɪksə / (BrE)
a person who arranges matters with authorities on behalf of someone, especially by underhand or illegal means, typically for payment
person who arranges illicit deals

Examples

The fixer promised to get the permits approved, but his fee was suspiciously high.
A seasoned fixer can make legal troubles disappear for the right price.
The journalist exposed a notorious fixer who manipulated city officials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broker - person who arranges transactions between others
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10113815-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɪksɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɪksə / (BrE)
a chemical compound used to make something permanent, such as fixing dyes to fabrics or stabilizing photographic images so they are no longer sensitive to light
a chemical for making something permanent, especially in photography or dyeing

Examples

The photographic fixer ensures that the image will not fade when exposed to light.
After dyeing the fabric, a fixer is applied to prevent the color from washing out.
Make sure to rinse out the fixer thoroughly during the development process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fixative - a substance used to fix or preserve something, especially in chemistry and art

Etymology

From 'fix', in the sense of making something stable or permanent, plus the agent suffix '-er'.

Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ ˈfɪksər / (AmE), / ˈfɪksə / (BrE)
a skilled worker whose job is to mend or repair things
person who repairs things

Examples

When the washing machine broke, we called a fixer to come and repair it.
The apartment building has a fixer who handles all maintenance issues.
As a fixer, she prides herself on being able to solve almost any household problem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repairman - a person whose job is to repair things (especially machinery)
  • Synonym: mechanic - a person who repairs and maintains machinery

Etymology

From the verb 'fix' (to repair) + -er (agent noun suffix).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
