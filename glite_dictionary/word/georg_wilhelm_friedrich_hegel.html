Georg Wilhelm Friedrich Hegel

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡeɪɔrɡ ˈwɪlhɛlm ˈfriːdrɪk ˈheɪɡəl / (AmE), / ˈɡeːɔːk ˈvɪlhɛlm ˈfʁiːdʁɪç ˈheːɡəl / (BrE)
(1770–1831) A German philosopher known for his development of dialectical reasoning and his influence on later thinkers, including Karl Marx.
German philosopher; developed dialectical reasoning

Examples

Many students find Georg Wilhelm Friedrich Hegel's writings both challenging and rewarding to study.
Karl Marx drew inspiration from the philosophies of Georg Wilhelm Friedrich Hegel.
The concept of dialectic is most famously associated with Georg Wilhelm Friedrich Hegel.

Etymology

From German personal name; 'Hegel' is a family name.
