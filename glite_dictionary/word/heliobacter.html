Heliobacter

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːli.oʊˈbæktər / (AmE), / ˌhiːlɪ.əʊˈbæktə / (BrE)
A genus of helical, curved, or straight aerobic bacteria with rounded ends and multiple flagella, found in the gastric mucosa of primates, including humans.
genus of bacteria found in gastric mucosa
medical

Examples

The discovery of Heliobacter in the stomach paved the way for new treatments of ulcers.
Researchers found that Heliobacter colonizes the stomach lining in many primates.
A biopsy revealed the presence of Heliobacter in the patient's gastric tissue.

Etymology

From Greek 'helix' (spiral) + 'bacter' (rod), referencing the shape of the bacteria.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhiːli.oʊˈbæktər / (AmE), / ˌhiːli.əˈbæktə / (BrE)
A genus of helical, curved, or straight aerobic bacteria with rounded ends and multiple flagella, found in the gastric mucosa of primates, including humans.
a genus of bacteria with helical shape
medical

Examples

Scientists isolated Heliobacter from the gastric tissue of several primate species.
The curved shape of Heliobacter can be observed under a high-powered microscope.
Some Heliobacter species are linked to stomach infections in humans.

Etymology

From Greek 'hēlios' meaning sun and 'baktērion' meaning little rod (bacterium).
