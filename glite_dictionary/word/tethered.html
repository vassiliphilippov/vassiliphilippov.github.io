tethered

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɛðərd / (AmE), / ˈtɛðəd / (BrE)
Confined or restricted by or as if by a rope or chain.
confined by a rope or chain

Examples

The goat was tethered to a post in the yard all day.
Her phone remained tethered to the charger because the battery was low.
The balloon was tethered to the ground to prevent it from floating away.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: untethered - not tied or confined by a rope or chain

Etymology

From the verb 'tether', which comes from Old Norse 'tjōthr' (tether, line).
