outrageous

1. ADJ

/ aʊtˈreɪdʒəs / (AmE), / aʊtˈreɪdʒəs / (BrE)
Shockingly bad, immoral, or offensive to accepted standards of decency or morality.
grossly offensive or shocking

Examples

The judge described the attack as an outrageous act of cruelty.
The living conditions in the prison were outrageous and inhumane.
She was shocked by the outrageous behavior of the corrupt officials.

Synset Examples

subjected to outrageous cruelty
a hideous pattern of injustice
horrific conditions in the mining industry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appalling - causing horror or dismay
  • Synonym: hideous - extremely unpleasant or shocking
  • Antonym: acceptable - regarded as normal or proper

Etymology

From French *outrageux*, from *outrage* (insult), from Old French *ultrage*, from Latin *ultra* (beyond).

2. ADJ

/ aʊtˈreɪdʒəs / (AmE), / aʊtˈreɪdʒəs / (BrE)
shockingly excessive or beyond what is considered reasonable or acceptable
shockingly excessive or unreasonable

Examples

The company charged an outrageous fee for late payments.
Some celebrities are known for their outrageous spending habits.
It's outrageous to expect anyone to pay that much for a concert ticket.

Synset Examples

exorbitant rent
extortionate prices
spends an outrageous amount on entertainment
usurious interest rate
unconscionable spending

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exorbitant - (of prices or amounts) unreasonably high
  • Antonym: reasonable - fair and moderate; not excessive

Etymology

From French 'outrageux', from 'outrage' (insult, offense), from Latin 'ultra' (beyond).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
