blackwater

1. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌwɔtɚ / (AmE), / ˈblækˌwɔːtə / (BrE)
any of several diseases in humans or animals marked by the passage of dark-colored urine due to rapid breakdown of red blood cells, especially as seen in severe forms of malaria (blackwater fever).
disease causing dark urine (esp. in malaria)
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with blackwater after presenting symptoms of fever and dark-colored urine.
Cases of blackwater were common among workers in tropical climates where malaria was prevalent.
He nearly died from blackwater complications during his expedition in Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackwater fever - a severe form of malaria causing dark urine and kidney failure

Etymology

From 'black' + 'water', referring to the dark colour of urine produced in the illness.
