Iberian

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈbɪriən / (AmE), / aɪˈbɪəriən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of the Iberian Peninsula, especially in ancient times
native of the Iberian Peninsula

Examples

The ancient Iberians played a crucial role in the early history of southwestern Europe.
Archaeological sites in Spain provide insights into the life of the Iberian people.
He traced his ancestry back to an Iberian tribe that once lived on the peninsula.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Spaniard - a native or inhabitant of Spain
  • Synonym: Portuguese - a native or inhabitant of Portugal

Etymology

From Latin Iberia, referring to the Iberian Peninsula.

2. ADJ

/ aɪˈbɪriən / (AmE), / aɪˈbɪəriən / (BrE)
of or relating to the Iberian Peninsula or its inhabitants, especially Spain and Portugal.
relating to the Iberian Peninsula or its people

Examples

Many Iberian cities blend Roman, Moorish, and modern influences.
The Iberian climate is known for its warm, dry summers and mild winters.
She specializes in Iberian history, particularly the cultures of Spain and Portugal.

Etymology

From Latin Iberia (the Iberian Peninsula), from Ancient Greek Ἰβηρία (Ibēría).

3. NOUN

/ aɪˈbɪəriən / (AmE), / aɪˈbɪəriən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of the ancient region of Iberia in the Caucasus, located in present-day Georgia.
person from ancient Iberia (Caucasus region)

Examples

Archaeologists have found artifacts dating back to the ancient Iberian civilization in modern-day Georgia.
The Iberian king played a crucial role in the region's early Christian history.
During the Roman Empire, trade routes connected Iberian merchants with neighboring territories.

Etymology

From Latin 'Iberia', referring to the ancient kingdom in the Caucasus, not to be confused with the Iberian Peninsula.
