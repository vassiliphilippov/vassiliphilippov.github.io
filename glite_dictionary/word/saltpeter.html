saltpeter

1. NOUN

/ sɔltˈpiːtər / (AmE), / sɒltˈpiːtə / (BrE)
a white crystalline compound (potassium nitrate), especially used in fertilizers, food preservation, and explosives
potassium nitrate compound

Examples

The miners discovered large deposits of saltpeter in the cave, used later for making gunpowder.
Saltpeter is often added to cured meats to preserve their color and prevent spoilage.
Farmers sometimes use saltpeter as a fertilizer to improve crop yields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potassium nitrate - the chemical compound KNO3

Etymology

From Medieval Latin sal petrae, meaning 'salt of rock.'
