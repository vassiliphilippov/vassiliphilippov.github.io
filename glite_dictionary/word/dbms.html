DBMS

1. NOUN

/ diː biː ɛm ɛs / (AmE), / diː biː ɛm ɛs / (BrE)
a software system that enables the creation, management, and use of electronic databases
database management system (software)

Examples

Many organizations use a DBMS to securely store and retrieve large volumes of data.
Choosing the right DBMS is critical for performance and scalability in enterprise applications.
Training is required to operate a complex DBMS effectively.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: database - a structured set of data held in a computer

Etymology

Acronym of Database Management System.
