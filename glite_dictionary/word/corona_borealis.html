Corona Borealis

1. PROPN

/ koʊˌroʊnə bɔːˈreɪəlɪs / (AmE), / kəˌrəʊnə bɔːˈreɪəlɪs / (BrE)
a small constellation in the Northern Hemisphere, located between Bootes and Hercules
constellation in the Northern Hemisphere

Examples

The constellation Corona Borealis is easily recognized by its semicircular shape.
Astronomers often point out Corona Borealis when teaching about the night sky in the Northern Hemisphere.
Ancient myths sometimes refer to Corona Borealis as the crown of Ariadne.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constellation - a group of stars forming a recognizable pattern

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'northern crown'.
