parnassia

1. NOUN

/ pɑrˈnæsiə / (AmE), / pɑːˈnasɪə / (BrE)
A genus of bog plants, usually evergreen, with broad basal leaves and a single pale flower that resembles a buttercup.
genus of evergreen bog plants with pale flowers

Examples

The wet meadow was dotted with the delicate white blossoms of parnassia.
Botanists discovered a rare species of parnassia thriving in the marshlands.
Her favorite wildflower is the parnassia, with its star-shaped petals and glossy leaves.

Etymology

From Latin Parnassia, referring to Mount Parnassus in Greece, where the plant was first found.
