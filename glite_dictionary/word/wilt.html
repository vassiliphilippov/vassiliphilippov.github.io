wilt

1. NOUN

/ wɪlt / (AmE), / wɪlt / (BrE)
A plant disease characterized by drooping and shriveling, usually caused by parasites attacking the roots.
plant disease causing drooping

Examples

The tomato crop was devastated by a severe case of wilt last summer.
Certain fungi in the soil can cause wilt in susceptible plants.
Farmers are searching for solutions to protect their fields from bacterial wilt.

2. VERB

/ wɪlt / (AmE), / wɪlt / (BrE)
(of a plant, flower, or leaf) to become limp through heat, loss of water, or disease; to droop and lose freshness.
become limp or droop (of plants)

Examples

If you don't water the plants, they will wilt in the afternoon sun.
After being left out all day, the lettuce began to wilt and lose its crispness.
The intense heat caused the flowers to wilt by evening.

Synset Examples

The flowers wilted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wither - (of a plant) to become dry and shriveled

Etymology

From Middle English 'welten' (to become soft or languid), of uncertain origin.

3. VERB

/ wɪlt / (AmE), / wɪlt / (BrE)
to become weak, tired, or less confident, often as a result of heat, exhaustion, or difficulty
become weak or tired

Examples

After running for an hour in the blazing sun, the athletes began to wilt from exhaustion.
She tried to remain cheerful, but her smile started to wilt as the meeting dragged on.
Even the strongest leaders can wilt under constant criticism and pressure.

Synset Examples

My opponent was wilting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to droop - to bend or hang down limply; to lose strength or spirit
  • Antonym: to perk up - to become more lively or cheerful again

Etymology

From Old English 'wyltan' (to become limp or wither).
