lead-colored

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɛd ˌkʌlɚd / (AmE), / ˈliːd ˌkʌləd / (BrE)
Having the dull grayish-blue color of lead.
of a dull grayish-blue color like lead

Examples

The sky turned a lead-colored shade before the storm rolled in.
Her dress was a striking lead-colored fabric that shimmered under the lights.
The artist chose a lead-colored background to convey a somber mood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gray - of a color between black and white
  • Synonym: bluish-gray - having a slight blue and gray color

Etymology

Formed from 'lead' (the metal) and 'colored', referring to the typical color of the metal.
