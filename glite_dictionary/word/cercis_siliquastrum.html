Cercis siliquastrum

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɜːrsɪs sɪlɪˈkwæstrəm / (AmE), / ˈsɜːsɪs sɪlɪˈkwæstrəm / (BrE)
A small tree native to the eastern Mediterranean, notable for its abundant purplish-red flowers that grow directly from old wood before the leaves, and widely cultivated in mild regions.
small Mediterranean tree with purplish-red flowers

Examples

The Cercis siliquastrum is often planted in gardens for its striking floral display each spring.
Wood from the Cercis siliquastrum is valued for its use in fine veneers.
Visitors admired the brilliant blossoms covering the old trunks of the Cercis siliquastrum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Judas tree - common name for Cercis siliquastrum

Etymology

From Latin *Cercis* (a genus of trees) and *siliquastrum* (bearing pods).
