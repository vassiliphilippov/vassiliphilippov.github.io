fare

1. NOUN

/ fɛr / (AmE), / feə / (BrE)
a passenger who pays for a trip in a taxi or public transport vehicle
paying taxi or bus passenger

Examples

The taxi waited outside, hoping for another fare before midnight.
He dropped his last fare off at the airport and headed home.
The driver refused to pick up the fare because it was too far out of town.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: passenger - a person traveling in a vehicle but not operating it
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10098423-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ fɛr / (AmE), / feə / (BrE)
the food and drink that are regularly provided or consumed, especially in a particular place or time
the food and drink usually served

Examples

The hotel offers traditional Irish fare in its cozy dining room.
After a long hike, we enjoyed simple but hearty fare around the campfire.
The restaurant is known for its vegetarian fare and organic ingredients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cuisine - a style or method of cooking, especially as characteristic of a particular country, region, or establishment

Etymology

From Old English 'faran' (to go, travel), later developing the sense of provisions for travel or meals.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07576217-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ fɛr / (AmE), / feə / (BrE)
to eat, especially to eat well or in a specified way
to eat, especially eat well

Examples

During their vacation, they fared sumptuously on local seafood and fresh fruit.
We fared very well at the banquet last night, enjoying several delicious courses.
In the countryside, travelers often fare better than in the city, as the food is fresher and more plentiful.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dine - to eat dinner or have a meal

Etymology

From Old English 'faran' (to travel, go, fare), with extended senses including to eat, especially in older or literary use.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01175266-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ fɛr / (AmE), / feə / (BrE)
the money paid for a journey on public transport such as a bus, train, or taxi
payment for public transportation

Examples

The bus fare increased last month, making commuting more expensive.
Please have the correct fare ready before you board the train.
She handed the taxi driver the fare at the end of the ride.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fee - a sum paid for a service
  • Synonym: ticket - a paper or digital document showing paid transport fare

Etymology

From Old English *fær* (journey, road, passage), related to the verb 'fare.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13329169-n
Synset POS: n

5. VERB

/ fɛr / (AmE), / feə / (BrE)
to proceed, perform, or get along in a particular situation, often regarding wellbeing or success
to get along; perform in a situation

Examples

After moving to the city, she often wondered how she would fare on her own.
Many small businesses did not fare well during the economic downturn.
We asked him at the reunion how he had fared since graduation.

Synset Examples

How is she doing in her new job?
How are you making out in graduate school?
He's come a long way

Related Terms

  • Synonym: do - to perform or carry out an action or task; in some contexts can be used as a synonym of 'fare', e.g., 'How did you do?'
  • Antonym: struggle - to proceed with difficulty or little success; often contrasted with 'fare well'
  • Degree Higher: to thrive - to do very well or prosper; a higher degree compared to 'fare'

Etymology

From Old English *faran* (to go, travel), related to Old Norse *fara* and German *fahren*.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
