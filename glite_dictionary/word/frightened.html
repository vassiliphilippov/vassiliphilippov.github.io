frightened

1. ADJ

/ ˈfraɪtənd / (AmE), / ˈfraɪtənd / (BrE)
feeling or showing fear; afraid, especially suddenly or intensely
afraid; feeling fear

Examples

She looked frightened when she heard the loud noise outside the window.
The frightened child clung to his mother during the thunderstorm.
He admitted feeling frightened by the prospect of starting a new job.

Synset Examples

became panicky as the snow deepened
felt panicked before each exam
trying to keep back the panic-stricken crowd
the terrified horse bolted

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: terrified - extremely afraid; intense fear
  • Synonym: afraid - feeling fear or apprehension
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other strong emotions

Etymology

From fright + -en (verb-forming suffix) + -ed (past participle suffix).

2. ADJ

/ ˈfraɪtənd / (AmE), / ˈfraɪtənd / (BrE)
feeling or showing fear; afraid or alarmed
afraid or alarmed

Examples

She looked frightened when she heard the strange noise outside.
The frightened kitten hid under the bed during the thunderstorm.
Many people feel frightened before giving a speech in public.

Synset Examples

the frightened child cowered in the corner
too shocked and scared to move

Related Terms

  • Synonym: afraid - feeling fear or anxiety
  • Synonym: scared - suddenly afraid or filled with fear
  • Antonym: brave - showing courage and not afraid
  • Antonym: fearless - not afraid or worried about dangerous things

Etymology

Past participle of 'frighten', from Middle English 'frighten', related to Old English 'fyrhtan' (to frighten).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00080219-s
Synset POS: s
