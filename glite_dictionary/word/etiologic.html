etiologic

1. ADJ

/ ˌitiəˈlɑdʒɪk / (AmE), / ˌiːtiəˈlɒdʒɪk / (BrE)
Relating to the cause or origin of a disease or condition.
relating to the cause of a disease
medical

Examples

The etiologic factors of the infection were not immediately apparent to the doctors.
Identifying the etiologic agent is crucial for effective disease management.
Her research focuses on the etiologic mechanisms underlying autoimmune disorders.

Synset Examples

etiological agent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: etiological - Alternate adjective form meaning 'relating to the cause (etiology) of a disease'

Etymology

From Greek aitia (cause) + -ology (study of) via Modern Latin etiology.

2. ADJ

/ ˌiːtiəˈlɑdʒɪk / (AmE), / ˌiːtiəˈlɒdʒɪk / (BrE)
Relating to the philosophical study of causation or the investigation of causes.
relating to the study of causation

Examples

Philosophers have long debated the etiologic basis of natural phenomena.
The etiologic perspective is essential in theories of causation.
His work focused on the etiologic aspects of historical events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: causal - relating to or involving a cause

Etymology

From Greek 'aitiología' (giving a reason for), from 'aitia' (cause) + '-logia' (study).
