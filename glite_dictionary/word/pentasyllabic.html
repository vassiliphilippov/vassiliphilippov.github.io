pentasyllabic

1. ADJ

/ ˌpɛntəˌsɪˈlæbɪk / (AmE), / ˌpɛntəˌsɪˈlæbɪk / (BrE)
having or consisting of five syllables.
having five syllables

Examples

The word 'banana' is not pentasyllabic, but 'California' is.
Poets sometimes use pentasyllabic lines to create a specific rhythm in their work.
Her name sounded pentasyllabic, making it stand out from the rest.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: trisyllabic - having or consisting of three syllables
  • Degree Lower: quadrisyllabic - having or consisting of four syllables
  • Degree Higher: hexasyllabic - having or consisting of six syllables

Etymology

From Greek 'pente' (five) + 'syllabē' (syllable).
