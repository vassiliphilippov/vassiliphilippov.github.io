ophthalmology

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑf.θəlˈmɑl.ə.dʒi / (AmE), / ˌɒf.θælˈmɒl.ə.dʒi / (BrE)
the branch of medicine concerned with the study and treatment of disorders and diseases of the eye
study and treatment of eye diseases
medical

Examples

She decided to specialize in ophthalmology after medical school.
Advances in ophthalmology have made many eye surgeries safer and more effective.
The annual ophthalmology conference attracted experts from around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: optometry - the practice or profession of examining the eyes for visual defects and prescribing corrective lenses

Etymology

From Greek 'ophthalmos' (eye) + 'logia' (study).
