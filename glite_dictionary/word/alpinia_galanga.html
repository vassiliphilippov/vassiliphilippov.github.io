Alpinia galanga

1. NOUN

/ ælˈpɪniə ɡəˈlæŋɡə / (AmE), / ælˈpɪniə ɡəˈlæŋɡə / (BrE)
A tropical Southeast Asian plant of the ginger family, with aromatic rhizomes used in cooking and medicine, also known as greater galangal.
tropical plant; aromatic root used in cooking

Examples

Traditional Thai soups often include slices of Alpinia galanga in their recipes.
The aromatic roots of Alpinia galanga have both culinary and medicinal uses.
Researchers studied the antibacterial properties of extracts from Alpinia galanga.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: galangal - common name for the rhizomes from Alpinia galanga and related plants

Etymology

From the genus name 'Alpinia,' after Italian botanist Prospero Alpini, and 'galanga', from Malay 'lengkuas'.
