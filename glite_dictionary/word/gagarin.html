Gagarin

1. PROPN

/ gəˈɡɑrɪn / (AmE), / ɡəˈɡɑːrɪn / (BrE)
(Yuri Gagarin) The Soviet cosmonaut who in 1961 became the first person to travel into outer space.
first human in space, Soviet cosmonaut

Examples

Yuri Gagarin made history as the first human to orbit the Earth in 1961.
Statues of Gagarin can be found in several Russian cities to honor his achievements.
The launch of Gagarin aboard Vostok 1 marked a major milestone in the Space Race.

Etymology

From the Russian surname Гагарин (Gagarin), notably Yuri Gagarin.
