to tattle

1. VERB

/ ˈtæt̬.əl / (AmE), / ˈtæt.əl / (BrE)
to talk rapidly and at length about trivial or unimportant matters, often in a gossipy way
talk rapidly about trivial matters

Examples

The children tattled on and on about their day at school.
She tends to tattle whenever she's nervous, filling the room with endless small talk.
He tattled cheerfully to his friends as they walked to the park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chatter - to talk quickly and continuously about trivial matters
  • Synonym: prattle - to talk at length in a foolish or inconsequential way

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'taten' (to talk, chatter), imitative of babbling speech.

2. VERB

/ ˈtætl̩ / (AmE), / ˈtætl̩ / (BrE)
to reveal confidential information or secrets, especially in a childish or petty way
reveal secrets, often in a childish manner

Examples

Children sometimes tattle on each other to their teachers.
She promised not to tattle about the surprise party.
It's considered rude to tattle on your coworkers without good reason.

Synset Examples

Be careful — his secretary talks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to blab - to reveal secrets or talk carelessly
  • Synonym: gossip - to talk about other people's private affairs
  • Antonym: to keep quiet - to not speak or reveal information

Etymology

Probably from Middle English tatelen (to chatter), of imitative origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00939238-v
Synset POS: v
