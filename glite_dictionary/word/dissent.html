dissent

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsɛnt / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛnt / (BrE)
(Law) A judge's opinion that disagrees with the majority decision of the court.
judicial disagreement with majority opinion
legal

Examples

The judge issued a strong dissent challenging the reasoning of the majority opinion.
Her dissent became a key reference in later court decisions.
The Supreme Court's ruling included one notable dissent from Justice Lee.

Synset Examples

he expressed his dissent in a contrary opinion

Related Terms

  • Antonym: concurrence - A statement by a judge agreeing with the majority but for different reasons
  • Antonym: majority opinion - The official decision agreed to by more than half the judges

Etymology

From Latin dissentire, from dis- (apart) + sentire (to feel, think).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈsɛnt / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛnt / (BrE)
the holding or expression of opinions that are different from those commonly or officially held; disagreement
difference of opinion; disagreement

Examples

The judge wrote a powerful dissent against the majority decision.
There was little room for dissent during the heated debate.
Honest dissent is essential in a healthy democracy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: agreement - harmony or accord in opinion or feeling
  • Synonym: disagreement - a lack of consensus or approval

Etymology

From Latin 'dissentire' meaning 'to differ in sentiment' (‘dis-’ apart + ‘sentire’ to feel, think).

/ dɪˈsɛnt / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛnt / (BrE)
to express or hold an opinion that differs from the majority or official position; to withhold assent
to disagree or withhold assent

Examples

The judge dissented from the majority opinion in the landmark case.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
