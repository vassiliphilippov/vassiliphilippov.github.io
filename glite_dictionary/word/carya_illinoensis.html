Carya illinoensis

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛəriə ɪˌlɪnoʊˈɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˈkeəriə ɪˌlɪnəʊˈensɪs / (BrE)
a tree native to the southern United States and Mexico, cultivated for its edible nuts known as pecans
pecan tree of southern US and Mexico

Examples

The Carya illinoensis is highly prized for the quality of its nuts, known as pecans.
Farmers in Texas extensively cultivate Carya illinoensis for commercial nut production.
Botanists study the genetics of Carya illinoensis to improve pecan yields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pecan - the edible nut of Carya illinoensis

Etymology

From Latinized form of genus Carya (from Greek káruon, 'nut') and specific epithet referencing Illinois.
