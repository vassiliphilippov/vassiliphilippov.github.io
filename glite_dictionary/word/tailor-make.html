to tailor-make

1. VERB

/ ˈteɪlər ˌmeɪk / (AmE), / ˈteɪlə ˌmeɪk / (BrE)
to make or produce something, especially clothing, specially to fit the needs or measurements of an individual
make specially to fit an individual

Examples

The designer will tailor-make a dress to match your exact measurements.
We can tailor-make a training program that suits your company's needs.
She had her wedding gown tailor-made by a famous couturier.

Synset Examples

Can the seamstress sew me a suit by next week?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to customize - to modify something according to individual requirements
  • Antonym: to mass-produce - to manufacture goods in large quantities, not tailored to individuals

Etymology

From 'tailor' (a person who makes clothes) and 'make' (to create); originally refers to making clothes to fit.

2. VERB

/ ˈteɪlərˌmeɪk / (AmE), / ˈteɪləˌmeɪk / (BrE)
to make or adapt something specifically for a particular person, purpose, or requirement
make something to fit specific requirements

Examples

They can tailor-make a training program to fit your company's unique needs.
The wedding dress was tailor-made for her and fit perfectly.
We offer services to tailor-make furniture according to your space and style preferences.

Synset Examples

I had this desk custom-made for me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to custom-make - to make something specifically for a person or requirement
  • Synonym: to adapt - to change something to suit a new use or situation

Etymology

Formed from 'tailor' (one who makes fitted clothes) and 'make'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01626526-v
Synset POS: v
