Feodor Dostoyevsky

1. PROPN

/ ˈfjodɔr ˌdɑstɔˈjɛfski / (AmE), / ˈfjɔːdɔː ˌdɒstɔɪˈɛfski / (BrE)
(1821–1881) A Russian novelist noted for exploring themes of human suffering with psychological depth and occasional humor.
Russian novelist (1821–1881)

Examples

Feodor Dostoyevsky's novels delve deep into the complexities of the human psyche.
Many critics consider Feodor Dostoyevsky one of the greatest writers in world literature.
Crime and Punishment, written by Feodor Dostoyevsky, remains a classic in psychological fiction.

Etymology

Transliteration of Russian Фёдор Достоевский (Fyodor Dostoyevsky).
