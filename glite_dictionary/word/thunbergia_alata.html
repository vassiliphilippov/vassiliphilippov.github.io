Thunbergia alata

1. NOUN

/ θʌnˈbɜrɡiə əˈlɑtə / (AmE), / θʌnˈbɜːɡɪə əˈlɑːtə / (BrE)
A tropical African climbing plant with yellow flowers and a dark purple center, commonly grown as an ornamental vine.
ornamental African climbing plant

Examples

We planted a Thunbergia alata near the fence, and its yellow blooms brightened the garden all summer.
The Thunbergia alata quickly covered the trellis with its lush green leaves and striking flowers.
Gardeners appreciate Thunbergia alata for its fast growth and decorative appeal in warm climates.

Etymology

Named after Swedish botanist Carl Peter Thunberg; 'alata' is Latin for 'winged', referring to the plant's winged stems.
