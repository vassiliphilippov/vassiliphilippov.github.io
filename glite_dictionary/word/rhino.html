rhino

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪnoʊ / (AmE), / ˈraɪnəʊ / (BrE)
a large, heavy, plant-eating mammal found in Asia and Africa, with very thick skin and one or two horns on its nose
large, thick-skinned horned mammal

Examples

The children were excited to see the rhino at the zoo.
Poachers hunt the rhino for its valuable horns.
A single rhino can weigh over two tons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhinoceros - the full term for the animal 'rhino', a large mammal with one or two horns

Etymology

Shortened form of 'rhinoceros', from Greek 'rhis' (nose) + 'keras' (horn).
