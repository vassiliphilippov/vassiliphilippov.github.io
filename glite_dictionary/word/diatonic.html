diatonic

1. ADJ

/ ˌdaɪ.əˈtɑn.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌdaɪ.əˈtɒn.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to or involving only the seven notes of a standard major or minor scale, without chromatic alterations
using standard major or minor scale notes

Examples

The melody was strictly diatonic, using only the notes of the C major scale.
Many folk songs are diatonic in nature and do not include chromatic notes.
She composed a piece with diatonic harmony, avoiding any accidentals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: chromatic - involving notes outside the standard major or minor scales

Etymology

From Greek 'diatonikos', from 'diateinein' (to stretch out).

2. ADJ

/ ˌdaɪ.əˈtɑ.nɪk / (AmE), / ˌdaɪ.əˈtɒ.nɪk / (BrE)
relating to musical scales that consist of five whole tones and two semitones without alteration by accidentals, especially the standard major or minor scales.
involving the standard major or minor scales in music

Examples

The melody was constructed using only diatonic tones from the C major scale.
Many folk songs are based on simple diatonic harmonies rather than chromatic ones.
She prefers the sound of diatonic music because of its clarity and simplicity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: chromatic - relating to or using notes not belonging to the standard scale of a key

Etymology

From Greek diatonikos, meaning 'stretching to the octave'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00410966-a
Synset POS: a
