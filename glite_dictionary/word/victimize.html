to victimize

1. VERB

/ ˈvɪktəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvɪktɪmaɪz / (BrE)
to treat someone cruelly or unfairly, especially by punishing them unjustly or making them the victim of wrongdoing
to treat someone unfairly or punish unjustly

Examples

The new law unfairly victimized small business owners who could not afford the extra fees.
Some children are victimized by bullies at school on a daily basis.
He felt victimized after being punished for something he did not do.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to persecute - to treat someone cruelly or unfairly, often persistently
  • Antonym: favor - to treat someone with preference and fairness, opposite of victimizing

Etymology

From 'victim' + '-ize'; 'victim' from Latin 'victima' (sacrificial animal).

2. VERB

/ ˈvɪktəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvɪktɪmaɪz / (BrE)
to deceive or cheat someone out of something; to deprive by trickery or fraud
to cheat or defraud by deception

Examples

The company used false promises to victimize hundreds of investors.
She was victimized by a scam artist pretending to represent a charity.
Elderly people are often victimized by telemarketers who employ deceptive tactics.

Synset Examples

He swindled me out of my inheritance
She defrauded the customers who trusted her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swindle - to cheat someone out of money or property using deception
  • Synonym: to defraud - to illegally deprive someone of money by deception

Etymology

From 'victim' + '-ize', first recorded in English in the 19th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02578034-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈvɪktɪˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvɪktɪmaɪz / (BrE)
to single out someone for cruel or unfair treatment, making them a victim
treat someone unfairly or cruelly

Examples

The new policy unfairly victimizes minority students.
She felt victimized by her coworkers after the mistake was made public.
Scammers often victimize elderly people by pretending to be officials.

Synset Examples

I was victimized by this con-man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to persecute - to treat someone cruelly or unfairly, especially because of their beliefs
  • Synonym: to harass - to subject someone to aggressive pressure or intimidation
  • Antonym: to protect - to keep someone safe from harm or injury

Etymology

From English 'victim' + '-ize'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02580280-v
Synset POS: v
