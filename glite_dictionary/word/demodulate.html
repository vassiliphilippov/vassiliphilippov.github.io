to demodulate

1. VERB

/ ˌdiːˈmɑːdʒəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˌdiːˈmɒdjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to extract the original information from a modulated carrier wave, typically in communications and electronics
extract information from a modulated signal

Examples

Modern radios can demodulate a wide range of signals, including FM and AM broadcasts.
The receiver circuit is designed to demodulate the incoming carrier wave and produce clear audio.
Engineers use special equipment to demodulate digital data from wireless transmissions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to modulate - to vary the amplitude, frequency, or phase of a carrier wave

Etymology

From de- (removal of) + modulate, based on Latin 'modulus' (measure).
