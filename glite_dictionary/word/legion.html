legion

1. NOUN

/ ˈliː.dʒən / (AmE), / ˈliː.dʒən / (BrE)
A large unit of the army, especially in ancient Rome, typically consisting of several thousand soldiers.
large military unit

Examples

The Roman legion was renowned for its discipline and training.
The general led his legion into battle with great confidence.
During ancient times, a single legion could control vast territories.

Synset Examples

the French Foreign Legion

Etymology

From Latin 'legio' (military levy, legion), from 'legere' (to levy, to choose).

an organization or association of ex-servicemen, especially one formed to support veterans' interests and camaraderie
association of ex-servicemen

Examples

He joined the local legion to stay connected with fellow veterans after leaving the army.
The legion organized a fundraising event for wounded soldiers and their families.
Many communities host annual parades sponsored by the legion, honoring those who have served.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: veterans' association - an organization for people who have served in the armed forces

Etymology

From Latin 'legio' (body of soldiers), evolving in English to refer to both military units and, by extension, associations of former soldiers.

Examples
