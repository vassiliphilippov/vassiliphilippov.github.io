crystallography

1. NOUN

/ ˌkrɪstəˈlɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌkrɪstəˈlɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the scientific study of the structure and formation of crystals
study of crystal structures

Examples

She specializes in crystallography, examining the atomic structure of minerals.
Advancements in crystallography have contributed greatly to modern chemistry and biology.
X-ray crystallography is a crucial tool for determining molecular structures.

Etymology

From Greek 'krystallos' (crystal) + 'graphia' (writing, description).
