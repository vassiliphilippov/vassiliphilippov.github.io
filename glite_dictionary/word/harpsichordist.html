harpsichordist

1. NOUN

/ hɑːrpsɪˌkɔːrdɪst / (AmE), / hɑːpsɪˌkɔːdɪst / (BrE)
a person who plays the harpsichord, especially as a professional occupation
person who plays the harpsichord

Examples

The renowned harpsichordist performed a Baroque concerto to a packed concert hall.
As a skilled harpsichordist, she specializes in early music and historical performance.
The orchestra hired a new harpsichordist for their Handel festival this year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pianist - person who plays the piano
  • Synonym: organist - person who plays the organ

Etymology

From 'harpsichord' + '-ist', denoting a person engaged in a particular activity.
