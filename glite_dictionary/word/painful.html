painful

1. ADJ

/ ˈpeɪnfəl / (AmE), / ˈpeɪnf(ə)l / (BrE)
Extremely bad or unpleasant; causing strong discomfort or displeasure.
very bad or unpleasant

Examples

Her singing was so painful that some people left the room.
The team's painful defeat left the fans in disbelief.
His attempt at humor was just painful to watch.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atrocious - extremely bad or unpleasant
  • Synonym: dreadful - very bad or causing fear or unease
  • Synonym: terrible - extremely bad or serious
  • Antonym: excellent - extremely good; outstanding

2. ADJ

/ ˈpeɪn.fəl / (AmE), / ˈpeɪn.fəl / (BrE)
causing physical discomfort or suffering.
causing physical discomfort

Examples

The nurse cleaned the wound, but it was still very painful to touch.
He described his recovery after surgery as the most painful experience of his life.
Wearing tight shoes for hours can become extremely painful.

Synset Examples

bites of black flies are more than irritating: they can be very painful

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sore - physically painful or sensitive
  • Antonym: comfortable - providing physical ease and relief from pain

Etymology

From Old French *peneful*, from Latin *poena* (penalty, punishment) + *-ful* (full of).

3. ADJ

/ ˈpeɪn.fəl / (AmE), / ˈpeɪn.fəl / (BrE)
Causing physical or emotional pain; distressing or unpleasant.
causing pain; distressing

Examples

The cut on his hand was extremely painful and made it hard for him to work.
She found the separation from her family to be emotionally painful.
It was a painful decision, but he knew it was the right one.

Synset Examples

worked with painful slowness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agonizing - causing extreme pain
  • Antonym: pleasant - giving a sense of happy satisfaction or enjoyment
  • Degree Lower: mild - not severe, less intense pain or discomfort
  • Degree Higher: excruciating - extremely painful

Etymology

From Middle English 'peynful', from Old French 'peneful', from 'peine' (pain) + '-ful'.

Source: WordNet

4. ADJ

/ ˈpeɪn.fəl / (AmE), / ˈpeɪn.fəl / (BrE)
causing physical pain, or emotional distress or suffering.
causing pain or distress

Examples

The recovery from the surgery was painful but necessary for his health.
It was a painful memory that she tried hard to forget.
The decision to say goodbye was painful for everyone involved.

Synset Examples

it was a sore trial to him
the painful process of growing up

Related Terms

  • Antonym: comfortable - free from pain or distress

Etymology
