Pagurus

1. NOUN

/ pəˈɡjʊrəs / (AmE), / pəˈɡjʊərəs / (BrE)
A genus of hermit crabs in the family Paguridae, widely found in marine environments.
genus of hermit crabs

Examples

The Pagurus genus includes many species commonly referred to as hermit crabs.
Marine biologists studied the mating habits of Pagurus in the coastal tide pools.
A specimen of Pagurus was displayed at the natural history museum's marine exhibit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hermit crab - common name for members of the family Paguridae

Etymology

From Latin *pagurus*, meaning 'sea crab', from Greek *pagouros* ('crab with a tail').

2. NOUN

/ pəˈɡʊrəs / (AmE), / pəˈɡjʊərəs / (BrE)
A genus of hermit crabs, the type genus of the family Paguridae.
genus of hermit crabs

Examples

Several species in the genus Pagurus are commonly found along Atlantic coasts.
Biologists discovered a new variety of hermit crab in the Pagurus genus.
The Pagurus genus includes many familiar hermit crabs kept in aquariums.

Etymology

From Latin 'pagurus', meaning a kind of crab, from Greek 'pagouros' (rock crab).
