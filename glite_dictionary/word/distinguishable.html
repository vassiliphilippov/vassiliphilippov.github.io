distinguishable

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈstɪŋɡwɪʃəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈstɪŋɡwɪʃəbl̩ / (BrE)
able to be recognized as different or distinct from others; capable of being distinguished
able to be recognized as different

Examples

The markings on each butterfly were clearly distinguishable from one another.
The two products are barely distinguishable in taste and appearance.
Her voice was not distinguishable among the crowd during the concert.

Synset Examples

only the shine of their metal was distinguishable in the gloom
a project distinguishable into four stages of progress
distinguishable differences between the twins

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indistinguishable - impossible to tell apart or separate by recognizing differences
  • Synonym: distinct - clearly separate and different from others

Etymology

From Latin distinguere (to separate, distinguish) + -able, meaning 'able to be distinguished'.

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈstɪŋɡwɪʃəbl̩ / (AmE), / dɪˈstɪŋɡwɪʃəbl̩ / (BrE)
able to be recognized as different; distinct or separate in nature or quality, often from something else
able to be recognized as different

Examples

The two species are easily distinguishable by the shape of their leaves.
Her accent was barely distinguishable from that of a native speaker.
The original painting was clearly distinguishable from the copy, even to an untrained eye.

Synset Examples

plants of several distinct types
the word ‘nationalism’ is used in at least two distinct senses
gold is distinct from iron
a tree related to but quite distinct from the European beech
management had interests quite distinct from those of their employees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distinct - clearly different or separate
  • Antonym: indistinguishable - not able to be recognized as different

Etymology

From distinguish + -able, meaning 'able to be distinguished'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02074467-s
Synset POS: s
