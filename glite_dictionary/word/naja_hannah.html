Naja hannah

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪ.dʒə ˈhæn.ə / (AmE), / ˈnɑː.dʒə ˈhæn.ə / (BrE)
a large, highly venomous snake species, known as the king cobra, found in southeastern Asia and the East Indies
the king cobra, a large venomous snake species

Examples

The Naja hannah, also known as the king cobra, can grow up to 5.5 meters long.
Snake handlers are particularly cautious when dealing with the venomous Naja hannah.
The habitat of the Naja hannah includes forests and plains across Southeast Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: king cobra - common name for Naja hannah, the world's largest venomous snake

Etymology

From Latinized genus 'Naja' (from Sanskrit 'nāga', meaning 'snake') and species 'hannah'.
