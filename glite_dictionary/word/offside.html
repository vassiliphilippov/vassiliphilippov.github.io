offside

1. ADJ

/ ˈɔfˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈɒfˌsaɪd / (BrE)
in a position unlawfully ahead of a prescribed line, area, or the ball or puck in certain sports, such as football or hockey
illegally ahead of ball or line in sports

Examples

The defender was clearly offside when the pass was made.
The referee called the forward offside during the second half.
If a player is offside, the play is stopped and a penalty is given.

Synset Examples

the touchdown was nullified because the left tackle was offside

Related Terms

  • Antonym: onside - in a legal or correct position according to the rules of the sport

Etymology

From the phrase 'off side,' referring to being on the wrong or prohibited side in early sports terminology.

2. ADV

/ ˈɔfˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈɒfˌsaɪd / (BrE)
In an illegal position ahead of the ball or puck, especially in sports such as soccer, hockey, or football.
illegally ahead of play in sports

Examples

The striker was clearly offside when the ball was passed to him.
If you move too quickly, you may find yourself offside and the goal won't count.
The referee blew the whistle for offside just before the shot was taken.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: onside - in a legal position with respect to play in sports

Etymology

From the phrase 'off side', originally used in sports contexts in the late 19th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00415483-r
Synset POS: r

3. NOUN

/ ˈɔf.saɪd / (AmE), / ˈɒf.saɪd / (BrE)
(in sports) a rule infringement in which a player occupies an illegal position on the field, especially in football (soccer) or hockey.
rule infringement; illegal position (sport)

Examples

The striker was called for offside just before scoring the goal.
Players must always be aware of the offside rule to avoid penalties.
Controversy erupted when the referee failed to notice an apparent offside during the match.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: onside - in a legal position as defined by the rules (sports)
