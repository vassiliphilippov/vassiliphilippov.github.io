centrally

1. ADV

/ ˈsɛntrəli / (AmE), / ˈsɛntrəli / (BrE)
in or near the center; in a way that is most important or principal
in or near the center; primarily

Examples

The new offices are centrally located in the city.
The heat is centrally controlled throughout the building.
She played a centrally important role in the negotiations.

Synset Examples

The theater is centrally located

Related Terms

  • Antonym: peripherally - in a manner relating to the edge or outskirts, not the center

Etymology

From central + -ly, where 'central' is from Latin 'centralis'.
