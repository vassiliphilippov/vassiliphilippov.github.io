tawny

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɔni / (AmE), / ˈtɔːni / (BrE)
of a light brown to brownish orange color, resembling the shade of tanned leather
light brown to brownish orange color

Examples

The lion's tawny mane glowed in the afternoon sun.
She wore a tawny scarf that matched the color of autumn leaves.
A tawny owl perched silently on the branch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tan - a pale brown color
  • Degree Lower: brown - of the color produced by mixing red, yellow, and black
  • Degree Higher: amber - a yellowish-brown color

Etymology

From Old French 'tanné' (tanned), from 'taner' (to tan leather).
