asarabacca

1. NOUN

/ ˌæsərəˈbækə / (AmE), / ˌæsərəˈbækə / (BrE)
A creeping evergreen herb (Asarum europaeum) native to western Europe, known for its round, shiny leaves and use in traditional medicine.
European evergreen herb (Asarum europaeum)

Examples

The leaves of asarabacca were once used as an herbal remedy.
You can find asarabacca growing wild across parts of western Europe.
Botanists study plants like asarabacca for their medicinal properties.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin asarabacca, combining asarum (the genus) and bacca (berry).
