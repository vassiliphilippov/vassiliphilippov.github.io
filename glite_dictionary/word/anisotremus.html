Anisotremus

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.ə.soʊˈtriː.məs / (AmE), / ˌæn.ɪ.səʊˈtriː.məs / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish in the family Haemulidae, known as grunts, native to the eastern Pacific and western Atlantic oceans.
a genus of marine grunts (fish)

Examples

The researchers studied the habitat of Anisotremus along the coral reefs.
Several species of Anisotremus are popular among aquarium enthusiasts.
The distinctive markings of Anisotremus help differentiate them from other grunts.

Etymology

From Greek 'anisos' (unequal) and 'tremus' (hole), referring to the fish's features.
