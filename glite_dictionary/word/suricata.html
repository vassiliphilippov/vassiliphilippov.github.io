Suricata

1. NOUN

/ sʊˈrɪkətə / (AmE), / sjuːrɪˈkɑːtə / (BrE)
A genus of small African mammals in the mongoose family, best known for including the meerkat.
genus of mammals including meerkats

Examples

The genus Suricata includes only one extant species: the meerkat.
Zoologists are studying the social structure of Suricata colonies in the Kalahari Desert.
Unlike other mongooses, Suricata species are highly social and live in large groups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meerkat - a small African mammal in the mongoose family

Etymology

From Latinized form of native Khoisan names, adopted into scientific taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ sʊˈrɪkətə / (AmE), / sjʊəˈrɪkətə / (BrE)
A genus of small mongooses native to southern Africa, of which the meerkat is the sole member.
genus of mongooses (includes meerkat)

Examples

The Suricata genus is represented by the well-known meerkat of southern Africa.
Scientists observed the social behavior of Suricata colonies in the Kalahari Desert.
Unlike other mongooses, Suricata species live in large family groups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meerkat - small mongoose native to southern Africa

Etymology

From Latin, used in zoological taxonomy to designate the genus.
