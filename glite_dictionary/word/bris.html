bris

1. NOUN

/ brɪs / (AmE), / brɪs / (BrE)
the Jewish religious ceremony of circumcision performed on a male child, typically on the eighth day after birth
Jewish circumcision ceremony

Examples

The family gathered for the bris of their newborn son.
He was honored to be chosen as the mohel for the bris.
Attending a bris is an important tradition in Jewish communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: circumcision - the act of removing the foreskin, often as a religious rite

Etymology

From Yiddish *bris*, from Hebrew *bərīṯ* (covenant).
