vineyard

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪn.jərd / (AmE), / ˈvɪn.jɑːd / (BrE)
A farm where grapevines are cultivated, especially for the production of wine.
farm growing wine grapes

Examples

They toured a famous vineyard in California’s Napa Valley during their vacation.
The family has been running the vineyard for several generations, producing award-winning wines.
Autumn is the busiest season at the vineyard as workers harvest the ripe grapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winery - a place where wine is made

Etymology

From Old French 'vignard', from 'vigne' (vine), from Latin 'vinea' (vineyard).
