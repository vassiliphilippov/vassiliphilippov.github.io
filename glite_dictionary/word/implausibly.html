implausibly

1. ADV

/ ɪmˈplɔːzəbli / (AmE), / ɪmˈplɔːzəbli / (BrE)
In a way that is difficult or impossible to believe; not seeming reasonable or probable.
in an unbelievable way

Examples

She won the lottery implausibly three times in a single year.
The witness explained the story implausibly, raising doubts about its truthfulness.
The plot twist in the novel was implausibly far-fetched for most readers.

Synset Examples

incredibly, she survived the crash

Related Terms

  • Antonym: believably - in a way that can be believed
  • Synonym: incredibly - in a way that is hard to believe

Etymology

From 'implausible' + '-ly', ultimately from Latin 'implausibilis'.
