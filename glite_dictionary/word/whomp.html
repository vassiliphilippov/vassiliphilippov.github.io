to whomp

1. VERB

/ wɑmp / (AmE), / wɒmp / (BrE)
to hit or strike someone or something with force, especially with the hand or a flat object
hit or strike forcefully
informal

Examples

He threatened to whomp anyone who touched his guitar.
The boxer whomped his opponent with a swift right hook.
She whomping the rug outside to get the dust out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smack - to hit someone or something with a flat object or with the hand
  • Synonym: slap - to hit with the palm of the hand or a flat object
  • Antonym: caress - to touch or stroke gently, often as a sign of affection

2. VERB

/ wɑmp / (AmE), / wɒmp / (BrE)
to defeat or beat someone decisively and overwhelmingly
defeat decisively
informal

Examples

The home team managed to whomp their rivals with a score of 6-0.
If you don't study, the test might really whomp you.
They whomped the competition and took home the trophy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrash - defeat decisively, especially in a competition or contest
  • Synonym: to trounce - defeat easily or thoroughly
  • Antonym: to lose - to be defeated in a contest or competition
