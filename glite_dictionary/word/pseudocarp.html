pseudocarp

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuːdoʊˌkɑːrp / (AmE), / ˈsjuːdəʊˌkɑːp / (BrE)
a fruit in which much of the fleshy tissue develops from parts of the flower other than the ovary, as in an apple or strawberry
fruit with flesh not derived from ovary
medical

Examples

The apple is considered a pseudocarp because much of its flesh develops from tissues outside the ovary.
Botanists often contrast pseudocarps with 'true fruits,' which form solely from the ovary.
A strawberry is a classic example of a pseudocarp due to its unique botanical structure.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: true fruit - a fruit derived entirely from the ovary of a flower

Etymology

From Greek pseudo- (false) + karpos (fruit).
