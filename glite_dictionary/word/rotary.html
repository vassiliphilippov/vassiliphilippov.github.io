rotary

1. ADJ

/ ˈroʊˌtɛri / (AmE), / ˈrəʊtəri / (BrE)
relating to or characterized by rotation or revolving movement, especially around an axis or center.
relating to rotation or revolving movement

Examples

The old phone featured a rotary dial instead of buttons.
A rotary engine works differently from a standard piston engine.
He used a rotary tool to carve the intricate design into the wood.

Synset Examples

rotary dial

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rotational - relating to rotation, especially as an abstract or mathematical property

Etymology

From Latin 'rotarius', from 'rota' meaning 'wheel'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03010069-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈroʊˌtɛri / (AmE), / ˈrəʊtəri / (BrE)
moving or able to move in a circle or around an axis; relating to rotation.
moving in a circle; rotating

Examples

The machine's rotary blade spins rapidly to cut through metal.
Wind turbines convert rotary motion into electricity.
The car's engine uses a unique rotary design instead of traditional pistons.

Synset Examples

the circular motion of the wheel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: circular - having the form of a circle
  • Antonym: linear - moving in or relating to a straight line

Etymology

From Latin 'rotarius' (of or relating to a wheel), from 'rota' (wheel).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00679539-s
Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ ˈroʊˌtɛri / (AmE), / ˈrəʊtəri / (BrE)
An electrical converter consisting of a synchronous machine that converts alternating current (AC) to direct current (DC) or vice versa.
electrical AC/DC converter (machine)

Examples

The old power plant still uses a rotary to supply DC electricity for the equipment.
Technicians inspected the rotary to ensure it was operating within safe parameters.
Replacing the outdated rotary improved the efficiency of the entire electrical system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rotary converter - a device for converting electrical current from AC to DC or vice versa

Etymology

From Latin 'rotarius' (of or belonging to a wheel), from 'rota' (wheel).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04382492-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈroʊˌtɛri / (AmE), / ˈrəʊtəri / (BrE)
a road junction where traffic moves in one direction around a central island; roundabout
circular road junction; roundabout
american

Examples

After the city installed a new rotary, traffic congestion in that area decreased significantly.
You must yield to traffic already in the rotary before entering it.
The rotary near the highway exit often confuses out-of-town drivers.

Synset Examples

the accident blocked all traffic at the rotary

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roundabout - a circular road junction with traffic moving one way around a central island

Etymology

From Latin *rota* (wheel), via Medieval Latin *rotarius* (pertaining to a wheel).
