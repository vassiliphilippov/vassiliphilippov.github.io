Dryopteris hexagonoptera

1. NOUN

/ draɪˈɒptərɪs ˌhɛksəˌgɒnˈɒptərə / (AmE), / draɪˈɒptərɪs ˌhɛksəˌgɒnˈɒptərə / (BrE)
A species of beech fern found in North American woodlands, characterized by its straw-colored stripes.
a North American beech fern species

Examples

Botanists discovered a thriving patch of Dryopteris hexagonoptera near the forest stream.
The straw-colored stripes of Dryopteris hexagonoptera make it easy to distinguish from similar ferns.
Hikers often overlook Dryopteris hexagonoptera despite its abundance in North American woodlands.

Etymology

From Greek: 'dryas' meaning oak tree and 'pteris' meaning fern; 'hexagonoptera' from 'hexagon' (six-angled) and 'pteron' (wing), referring to the shape of its fronds.
