prosecution

1. NOUN

/ ˌprɑːsɪˈkjuːʃən / (AmE), / ˌprɒsɪˈkjuːʃən / (BrE)
the legal team or lawyers who conduct the case against a defendant on behalf of the state in a criminal trial
lawyers representing the state in court
legal

Examples

The prosecution called several witnesses to testify against the accused.
After new evidence emerged, the prosecution decided to drop the charges.
During the trial, the judge listened carefully to the arguments made by the prosecution.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: defense - the lawyers acting for the accused in a court case

Etymology

From Latin 'prosecutio' meaning 'a following after, pursuit'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌprɑːsɪˈkjuːʃən / (AmE), / ˌprɒsɪˈkjuːʃən / (BrE)
the act of conducting legal proceedings against someone in respect of a criminal charge.
legal proceedings against a defendant
legal

Examples

The prosecution presented new evidence during the trial.
He faced prosecution for his involvement in the theft.
The case was dismissed after the prosecution failed to prove its case beyond a reasonable doubt.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: defense - the legal party defending the accused in court
  • Antonym: plaintiff - person who brings a case against another in a court of law

Etymology

From Latin prosecutio (a following, pursuit), from prosequi (to pursue).
