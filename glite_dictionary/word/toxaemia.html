toxaemia

1. NOUN

/ tɑkˈsiːmiə / (AmE), / tɒkˈsiːmiə / (BrE)
A medical condition in which the blood contains poisonous substances, especially those produced by bacteria.
blood poisoning by bacterial toxins
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with toxaemia after showing symptoms of severe infection.
Without prompt treatment, toxaemia can become life-threatening.
Doctors monitored her closely for signs of pre-eclampsia and toxaemia during pregnancy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: septicemia - blood poisoning caused by the presence of bacteria or their toxins in the blood

Etymology

From Greek toxikon (poisoned arrow) + -aemia (condition of the blood).

2. NOUN

/ tɑɡˈziːmiə / (AmE), / tɒkˈsiːmiə / (BrE)
A medical condition in pregnancy marked by high blood pressure, swelling (edema), and protein in the urine, also known as preeclampsia.
pregnancy disorder with high blood pressure and proteinuria
medical british

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with toxaemia after noticing high blood pressure and protein in her urine.
Untreated, toxaemia during pregnancy can lead to serious complications for both mother and child.
Swelling in her legs raised concerns about possible toxaemia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preeclampsia - a disorder of pregnancy characterized by high blood pressure and protein in the urine

Etymology

From Greek toxikon (poisoned arrow) + -aemia (from Greek haima, blood).
