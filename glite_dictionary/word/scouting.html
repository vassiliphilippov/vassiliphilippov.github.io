scouting

1. NOUN

/ ˈskaʊtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskaʊtɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity of exploring or gathering information about a place, especially for military or investigative purposes
exploring to gain information

Examples

Scouting in unfamiliar territory can reveal important details about the landscape.
The army relied on scouting missions to map out enemy positions.
His experience in scouting made him an invaluable asset during expeditions.

Synset Examples

scouting in enemy territory is very dangerous

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reconnaissance - the act of scouting or surveying, especially for military purposes
  • Synonym: surveillance - the act of closely observing a person or place

Etymology

Derived from 'scout', from Old French 'escouter' (to listen).
