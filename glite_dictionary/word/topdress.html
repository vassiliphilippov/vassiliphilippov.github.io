to topdress

1. VERB

/ ˈtɑpˌdrɛs / (AmE), / ˈtɒpˌdrɛs / (BrE)
to scatter manure, fertilizer, or other material over the surface of land without mixing it into the soil
apply fertilizer or manure on land surface

Examples

Farmers often topdress their fields in the spring to improve soil fertility.
It is advisable to topdress your lawn in early autumn for best results.
She topdressed the vegetable beds with compost before planting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fertilize - to add nutrients to soil to promote plant growth
  • Synonym: to cultivate - to prepare and use land for crops or gardening

Etymology

Formed from 'top' + 'dress', based on the sense of 'dress' meaning 'to treat or prepare (land, soil, etc.)'.
