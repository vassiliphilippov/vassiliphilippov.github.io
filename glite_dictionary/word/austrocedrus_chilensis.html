Austrocedrus chilensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔstroʊˈsiːdrəs tʃɪˈlɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˌɔːstrəʊˈsiːdrəs tʃɪˈlɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A small evergreen conifer native to South America, known for its copper-colored bark and attractive foliage.
South American evergreen conifer

Examples

The Austrocedrus chilensis is prized by landscapers for its ornamental bark and foliage.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect the forests where Austrocedrus chilensis grows naturally.
Botanists studied the ecological role of Austrocedrus chilensis in the Patagonian region.

Etymology

From Latinized botanical name; genus Austrocedrus (from Latin auster 'south' + cedrus 'cedar') and chilensis referring to Chile.
