Nothofagus obliqua

1. NOUN

/ noʊˌθoʊˈfeɪɡəs ɒbˈliːkwə / (AmE), / nɒˌθɒfəˈɡʌs ɒbˈliːkwə / (BrE)
A tall deciduous tree native to South America, valued for its timber and sometimes known as Patagonian oak.
South American deciduous tree species

Examples

The Nothofagus obliqua is often used in reforestation projects in Chile and Argentina.
Furniture made from Nothofagus obliqua is known for its strength and attractive grain.
During our hike, we passed a stand of ancient Nothofagus obliqua trees towering over the trail.

Etymology

From New Latin; genus name Nothofagus (from Greek 'nothos' meaning 'false' and 'phagos' meaning 'beech'), and specific epithet 'obliqua' (Latin for 'slanting' or 'oblique').
