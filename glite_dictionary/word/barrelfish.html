barrelfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈbærəlˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbærəlˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a large, deep-sea, blackish fish found in waters off New England and the North Atlantic, noted for its barrel-shaped body
a deep-sea blackish fish of the North Atlantic

Examples

The fisherman was delighted to catch a rare barrelfish during his deep-sea trip.
Biologists studied the unique features of the barrelfish found off the New England coast.
Local markets occasionally sell barrelfish, prized for their firm, mild-flavored flesh.
