roaring

/ ˈrɔrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɔːrɪŋ / (BrE)

3. NOUN

/ ˈrɔrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɔːrɪŋ / (BrE)
a deep, prolonged, and loud noise, often reminiscent of an animal's roar or a powerful natural force
deep, loud, prolonged noise

Examples

The roaring of the waterfall could be heard from miles away.
A sudden roaring filled the stadium when the home team scored.
He awoke to the roaring of thunder during the night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rumbling - a deep, continuous, rolling noise
  • Antonym: silence - complete absence of noise

Etymology

From the verb 'roar,' which comes from Old English 'rārian' (to roar, shout).
