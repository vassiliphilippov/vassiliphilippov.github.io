Cumbria

1. PROPN

/ ˈkʌmbriə / (AmE), / ˈkʌmbriə / (BrE)
a historic region and county in northwestern England, known for its hilly landscape, including the Lake District and the northern Pennines.
county/region in northwestern England

Examples

Many tourists visit Cumbria each year to explore the scenic Lake District.
The rugged mountains of Cumbria attract hikers from all over the world.
Cumbria has a rich history as a former Celtic kingdom.

Etymology

From Latinized form of the Welsh name 'Cymry', meaning 'fellow countrymen'.
