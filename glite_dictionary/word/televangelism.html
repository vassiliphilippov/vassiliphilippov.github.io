televangelism

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛlɪˈvændʒəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌtɛlɪˈvændʒəlɪzəm / (BrE)
The practice of conducting religious evangelism through televised broadcasts.
religious evangelism via television

Examples

Televangelism became a popular way to reach large audiences in the 1980s.
She studied the rise of televangelism in American religious history.
Some critics argue that televangelism blurs the line between faith and entertainment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evangelism - the spreading of the Christian gospel by public preaching or personal witness
  • Synonym: television ministry - religious service or preaching carried out via television broadcasts

Etymology

Blend of 'television' and 'evangelism'.
