cholecystitis

1. NOUN

/ ˌkoʊləsɪˈstaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌkɒləsɪˈstaɪtɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of the gallbladder, often caused by gallstones or infection
inflammation of the gallbladder
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with acute cholecystitis after reviewing her symptoms and ultrasound results.
Surgery may be required if cholecystitis does not respond to antibiotics.
Persistent abdominal pain could indicate a case of cholecystitis requiring immediate attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hepatitis - inflammation of the liver
  • Synonym: gallbladder - organ that stores bile

Etymology

From Greek *chole* (bile), *cystis* (bladder), and *-itis* (inflammation).
