Agamemnon

1. PROPN

/ ˌæɡəˈmɛmˌnɑn / (AmE), / ˌæɡəˈmɛm.nɒn / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the king of Mycenae who led the Greeks against Troy in the Trojan War.
king of Mycenae; Greek leader in the Trojan War

Examples

Agamemnon was a pivotal figure in many Greek tragedies and epic poems.
The murder of Agamemnon by his wife Clytemnestra is a famous story in classical literature.
During the Trojan War, Agamemnon commanded the united Greek forces against Troy.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Menelaus - king of Sparta; brother of Agamemnon in Greek mythology
  • Synonym: Achilles - Greek hero and warrior; key figure in the Trojan War
  • Antonym: Priam - king of Troy and enemy of Agamemnon

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἀγαμέμνων (Agamémnōn), meaning 'very resolute.'
