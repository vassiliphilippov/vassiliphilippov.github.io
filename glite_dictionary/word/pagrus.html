Pagrus

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪɡrəs / (AmE), / ˈpeɪɡrəs / (BrE)
a genus of marine fish in the family Sparidae, known as seabreams, including several commercially important species such as the red seabream and silver seabream
genus of seabream fish

Examples

The genus Pagrus includes several species of seabream harvested by commercial fisheries.
Scientists have observed a decline in Pagrus populations due to overfishing.
The Pagrus species are commonly found in the Mediterranean and eastern Atlantic oceans.

Etymology

From New Latin, coined from Greek 'págros', an old name for a kind of fish.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpæɡrəs / (AmE), / ˈpɑːɡrəs / (BrE)
a genus of marine fish in the family Sparidae, including species such as the southern common seabream and red seabream
a genus of seabream fish

Examples

The genus Pagrus includes several commercially important seabream species.
Many fishers hope to catch specimens from the Pagrus group due to their popularity as food.
Biologists are studying the population genetics of Pagrus in the Mediterranean Sea.

Etymology

From Latin Pagrus, a fish name, from Greek págrós (species of seabream).
