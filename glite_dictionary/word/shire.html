shire

1. NOUN

/ ʃaɪr / (AmE), / ʃaɪə / (BrE)
a former administrative district in England, equivalent to a county, especially in historical contexts
historic English administrative division
british archaic

Examples

Many of the traditional shires have names ending in "-shire" such as Lancashire and Gloucestershire.
The boundaries of the historic shire were often determined by natural features.
In medieval England, each shire was governed by a royal official known as a sheriff.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: county - an administrative division of a country, typically in the UK or US

Etymology

Old English *scīr* (official charge, district), related to Old High German *scira* (care).

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃaɪər / (AmE), / ˈʃaɪə / (BrE)
A British breed of large, strong horse bred for heavy draft work.
British breed of large draft horse
british

Examples

The farmer relied on his shire to pull the heavy plough across the fields.
A team of shires was used to transport the timber from the forest.
The shire is admired for its strength and gentle temperament.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Clydesdale - another breed of large draft horse
