sky-blue

1. ADJ

/ ˈskaɪ ˌblu / (AmE), / ˈskaɪ ˌbluː / (BrE)
of a deep, somewhat purplish blue color similar to that of a clear sky, especially in October
a deep purplish blue color

Examples

She wore a beautiful sky-blue dress to the wedding.
The artist mixed white and blue to create a perfect sky-blue shade for the painting.
On a clear autumn afternoon, the sky-blue sky stretched endlessly above the park.

Synset Examples

October's bright blue weather

Related Terms

  • Synonym: azure - bright blue in color, like a clear sky
  • Degree Lower: navy blue - a very dark shade of blue color

Etymology

From 'sky' (the upper atmosphere) + 'blue' (color).

2. NOUN

/ skaɪ bluː / (AmE), / skaɪ bluː / (BrE)
A light shade of blue, resembling the color of a clear daytime sky.
light shade of blue

Examples

She chose a dress in a delicate sky-blue for the summer party.
The artist mixed white and blue paint to get the perfect sky-blue for her canvas.
He looked up at the endless sky-blue and felt calm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: azure - a bright blue color, often likened to the sky
