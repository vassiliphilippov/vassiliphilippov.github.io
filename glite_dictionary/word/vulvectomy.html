vulvectomy

1. NOUN

/ vʌlˈvɛktəmi / (AmE), / vʌlˈvɛktəmi / (BrE)
a surgical operation to remove all or part of the vulva
surgical removal of the vulva
medical

Examples

The patient underwent a vulvectomy to treat her advanced vulvar cancer.
Doctors explained that a partial vulvectomy would preserve more tissue while removing the tumor.
Recovery after a vulvectomy varies depending on the extent of the surgery.

Etymology

From Latin 'vulva' (womb, covering) + Greek '-ectomy' (removal).
