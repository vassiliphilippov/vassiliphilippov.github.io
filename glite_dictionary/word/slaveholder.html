slaveholder

1. NOUN

/ ˈsleɪvˌhoʊldər / (AmE), / ˈsleɪvhəʊldə / (BrE)
a person who owns and keeps slaves
person who owns slaves

Examples

The wealthy slaveholder owned hundreds of people on his plantation.
Historians have studied the lives of slaveholders in the nineteenth century American South.
He was both a politician and a notorious slaveholder during that era.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slave-owner - a person who owns slaves
  • Antonym: abolitionist - a person who advocated for the abolition of slavery

Etymology

Formed from 'slave' + 'holder', indicating someone who holds or owns slaves.
