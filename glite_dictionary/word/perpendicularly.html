perpendicularly

1. ADV

/ pərˌpɛndɪˈkjʊlərli / (AmE), / pəˌpɛndɪˈkjʊːləli / (BrE)
In a manner that is at an angle of 90 degrees to a given line, plane, or surface; at right angles.
at a right angle; at 90 degrees

Examples

The two roads cross perpendicularly, forming a perfect intersection.
The flagpole rises perpendicularly from the ground.
To solve the problem, draw a line perpendicularly to the base of the triangle.

Synset Examples

this red line runs perpendicularly to the green line

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: vertically - in a vertical direction or position

Etymology

From 'perpendicular' + '-ly'. 'Perpendicular' from Latin 'perpendiculum' (plumb line), from 'per-' (thoroughly) + 'pendere' (to hang).

2. ADV

/ pərˌpɛndɪˈkjʊlərli / (AmE), / pəˌpɛndɪˈkjʊːləli / (BrE)
In a manner that is at a right angle to a given surface or line; straight up and down or straight across.
at a right angle; upright or straight across

Examples

The tower rose perpendicularly above the town, visible from miles away.
Ice crystals often form perpendicularly to the glass surface during freezing.
The cables must run perpendicularly along the wall to ensure safety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vertically - in a vertical direction or position
  • Antonym: horizontally - in a horizontal direction or position

Etymology

From Late Latin perpendicularis (perpendicular) + English suffix -ly.
