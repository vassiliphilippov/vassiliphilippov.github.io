Ipomoea nil

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪ.pəˈmiː.ə nɪl / (AmE), / ˌɪ.pəˈmiː.ə nɪl / (BrE)
an annual climbing plant native to the Old World tropics, known for its wide range of flower colors and frilled or double blossoms; commonly called the Japanese morning glory
a tropical climbing plant with colorful flowers

Examples

The Ipomoea nil in her garden produced vibrant blue and pink blossoms throughout the summer.
Botanists often study Ipomoea nil for its extensive range of flower colors and patterns.
She planted Ipomoea nil along the fence to enjoy its rapid growth and spectacular flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morning glory - any of several climbing plants with trumpet-shaped flowers, especially of the Ipomoea genus

Etymology

From Latin; 'Ipomoea' derives from Greek 'ips' (worm) + 'homoios' (resembling), referencing the plant's twining habit; 'nil' is Latin for 'nothing', of uncertain botanical relevance.
