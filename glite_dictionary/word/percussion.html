percussion

1. NOUN

/ pərˈkʌʃən / (AmE), / pəˈkʌʃ.ən / (BrE)
the act of tapping a part of the body as a diagnostic aid to assess the condition of underlying tissues or organs
tapping the body for medical diagnosis
medical

Examples

The doctor used percussion to check for fluid in the lungs.
By gently performing percussion on the abdomen, the physician detected an abnormal sound.
Percussion is a basic skill taught to medical students studying physical examination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palpation - examination by touch for medical diagnosis
  • Synonym: auscultation - listening to sounds within the body for diagnosis

Etymology

From Latin 'percussio' (a striking, percussion), from 'percutere' (to strike thoroughly)

2. NOUN

/ pərˈkʌʃən / (AmE), / pəˈkʌʃən / (BrE)
the act or art of playing percussion instruments, such as drums, cymbals, or xylophone, in music
playing percussion instruments

Examples

She specializes in percussion and often plays the drums in the orchestra.
During the performance, the percussion section added energy and rhythm to the music.
He took lessons in percussion to improve his timing and coordination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drumming - the act of playing drums, especially with sticks

Etymology

From Latin 'percussio' (a striking, percussion), from 'percutere' (to strike through).

3. NOUN

/ pərˈkʌʃ.ən / (AmE), / pəˈkʌʃ.ən / (BrE)
the act of detonating a percussion cap, especially in firearms or explosive devices
act of detonating a percussion cap

Examples

When the hammer struck, the percussion ignited the cartridge in the old revolver.
Early rifles relied on percussion rather than flintlock mechanisms to fire.
A misfire can occur if the percussion does not adequately ignite the powder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detonation - the action of causing something to explode
  • Synonym: ignition - the act of setting on fire or starting to burn

Etymology

From Latin percussio (a striking, a beating), from percutere (to strike, beat).

4. NOUN

/ pərˈkʌʃən / (AmE), / pəˈkʌʃ.ən / (BrE)
the section of a band or orchestra that consists of musicians who play percussion instruments, such as drums, cymbals, and xylophones
section of a band/orchestra playing percussion instruments

Examples

She joined the percussion in her school's orchestra, learning to play the snare drum and cymbals.
The percussion added energy and rhythm to the opening piece of the concert.
During rehearsal, the conductor asked the percussion to play more softly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: percussion instrument - a musical instrument sounded by striking, shaking, or scraping
  • Antonym: brass - the section of a band or orchestra that plays brass instruments
  • Antonym: strings - the section of a band or orchestra that plays string instruments

Etymology

From Latin percussio (a striking, beating), from percutere (to strike through).
