pompon

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑmpɒn / (AmE), / ˈpɒmpɒn / (BrE)
A dusky grey food fish found from Louisiana and Florida southward along the Atlantic and Gulf coasts of the United States.
a dusky grey fish of southern U.S. waters

Examples

Fishermen in the Gulf often catch pompon during the spring season.
The chef prepared a delicious dish featuring grilled pompon with fresh herbs.
Pompons are widely sought after by local anglers for their succulent meat.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑmˌpɑn / (AmE), / ˈpɒm.pɒn / (BrE)
a small ball of tufted wool, silk, or other material, used as a decoration on hats, garments, or accessories
tufted decorative ball for hats

Examples

She sewed a red pompon to the top of her winter hat for extra flair.
Traditional Scottish berets are often adorned with a woolen pompon in the center.
The children's craft project involved attaching a colorful pompon to each bookmark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pom-pom - a tufted decorative ball, especially used by cheerleaders or as an alternative spelling

Etymology

From French 'pompon', diminutive of 'pompe' (ball, knob).
