irredenta

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪrəˈdɛntə / (AmE), / ˌɪrəˈdɛntə / (BrE)
A region that is ethnically or historically related to one country but governed by another.
territory ethnically tied to another country

Examples

The region was considered an irredenta by its neighboring country due to a shared ethnic population.
After the war, the claim to the irredenta became a source of political tension.
Many nationalist movements focused on uniting their irredenta with the motherland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irredentism - the political movement advocating the reclamation of such territories

Etymology

From Italian 'irredenta', meaning 'unredeemed', referring to territories claimed by Italy in the 19th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪrəˈdɛntə / (AmE), / ˌɪrɛˈdɛntə / (BrE)
A region that is historically or ethnically related to one country but is politically controlled by another country.
region claimed by a country but ruled by another

Examples

Some politicians called for the annexation of the irredenta to restore historic borders.
Debates about the future of the irredenta have fueled nationalist sentiment in the region.
The treaty failed to address the status of the irredenta, leading to ongoing disputes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irredentism - the political movement advocating annexation of irredenta

Etymology

From Italian 'irredenta', meaning 'unredeemed', from 'irredento'.
