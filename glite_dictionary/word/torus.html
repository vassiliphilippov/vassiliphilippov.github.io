torus

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɔr.əs / (AmE), / ˈtɔː.rəs / (BrE)
A surface or solid having the shape of a ring, generated by rotating a circle around an axis outside the circle; commonly described as a doughnut shape in geometry and mathematics.
ring-shaped surface; doughnut shape

Examples

The inner tube of a bicycle is shaped like a torus and is filled with air.
In mathematics, a bagel is often used as an everyday example of a torus.
The topology class studied the properties of a torus and compared it with those of a sphere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ring - a circular band, often used as a synonym in geometry

Etymology

From Latin 'torus' meaning 'bulge' or 'swelling'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɔr.əs / (AmE), / ˈtɔː.rəs / (BrE)
A large, convex molding, typically semicircular in profile, that commonly forms the lowest part of the base of a classical column.
convex molding at base of a column

Examples

The ancient temple's columns were adorned with a distinctive torus at their bases.
The architect recommended a simple torus molding to enhance the column's appearance.
Restoration work revealed the original torus at the foot of each marble column.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: molding - decorative strip used in architecture

Etymology

From Latin *torus* (bulge, cushion).
