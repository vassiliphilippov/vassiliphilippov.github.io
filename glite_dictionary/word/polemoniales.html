Polemoniales

1. NOUN

/ poʊˌlɛməniˈeɪliˌiz / (AmE), / pəʊˌlɛməniˈeɪlɪiːz / (BrE)
a former order of flowering plants that included families such as Polemoniaceae, Solanaceae, Boraginaceae, Labiatae, Lentibulariaceae, and Pedaliaceae; in some classifications, it also included Scrophulariales.
former plant order including Polemoniaceae and others

Examples

Recent molecular studies have led to the reclassification of the Polemoniales.
The Polemoniales were traditionally distinguished by similarities in their floral structure.
Older botanical texts frequently reference the order Polemoniales when classifying plant families.

Etymology

From New Latin, after Polemoniaceae (a family in the order) + -ales (order suffix in botanical taxonomy).
