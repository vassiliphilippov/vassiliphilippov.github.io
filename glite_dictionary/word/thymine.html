thymine

1. NOUN

/ ˈθaɪmin / (AmE), / ˈθaɪmiːn / (BrE)
a compound that is one of the four bases of DNA, pairing with adenine; not present in RNA
a base in DNA, pairs with adenine
medical

Examples

The chemical thymine bonds with adenine in a DNA molecule.
A mutation that affects thymine can have serious consequences for cell replication.
Unlike DNA, RNA does not contain thymine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adenine - a base in DNA and RNA that pairs with thymine in DNA
  • Antonym: uracil - a base in RNA that replaces thymine

Etymology

From thymic, relating to the thymus gland, where it was first discovered, and -ine, a chemical suffix.
