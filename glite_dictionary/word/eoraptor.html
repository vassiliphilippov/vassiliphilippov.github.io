Eoraptor

1. NOUN

/ iˈoʊˌræptər / (AmE), / iˈəʊˌræptə / (BrE)
a small, lightly built early theropod dinosaur of the genus Eoraptor, which lived in the Late Triassic period
early theropod dinosaur genus

Examples

Fossils of the Eoraptor have provided valuable insights into early dinosaur evolution.
The Eoraptor was a swift, bipedal predator that inhabited South America around 230 million years ago.
Paleontologists consider Eoraptor to be one of the earliest known theropod dinosaurs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dinosaur - a group of extinct reptiles; dominant in the Mesozoic era

Etymology

From Latin 'eo' (dawn) + 'raptor' (thief), meaning 'dawn thief' due to its age and predatory nature.

2. PROPN

/ iː.oʊˈræptər / (AmE), / iː.əʊˈræptə / (BrE)
An extinct genus of small, primitive theropod dinosaur from the Early Triassic period, discovered in Argentina.
primitive theropod dinosaur genus

Examples

The discovery of Eoraptor provided valuable insights into the early evolution of dinosaurs.
Paleontologists believe Eoraptor hunted small animals in what is now Argentina.
Fossils of Eoraptor are among the oldest known theropod dinosaur remains.

Etymology

From Greek 'éōs' (dawn) and Latin 'raptor' (plunderer, thief); named 'dawn thief' to reflect its ancient period.
