Na

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛnˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˌɛnˈeɪ / (BrE)
the chemical symbol for sodium, a soft, silvery, highly reactive metallic element of the alkali metal group
chemical symbol for sodium

Examples

Table salt is composed of Na and chlorine.
When Na is exposed to water, it reacts violently.
The atomic number of Na is 11.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sodium - a soft, silvery, highly reactive metal; chemical element with symbol Na

Etymology

From New Latin natrium, from Arabic natrun (natron).
