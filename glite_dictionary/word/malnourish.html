to malnourish

1. VERB

/ mælˈnɜrɪʃ / (AmE), / mælˈnʌrɪʃ / (BrE)
to provide (a person or animal) with insufficient or inadequate nourishment, leading to poor health or development
to provide with inadequate nourishment

Examples

Poor diet and illness can malnourish children, affecting their growth and development.
If you malnourish a pet, it may develop severe health problems.
The drought continued to malnourish the entire population of the village.

Synset Examples

The stunted growth of these children shows that they are undernourished

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to undernourish - to provide with too little nourishment
  • Degree Higher: to starve - to suffer or die from lack of food

Etymology

Formed from 'mal-' (bad, inadequate) and 'nourish,' from Old French 'norir', from Latin 'nutrire' (to feed, nourish).
