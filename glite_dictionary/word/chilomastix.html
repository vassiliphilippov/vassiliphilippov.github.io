Chilomastix

1. NOUN

/ kaɪloʊˈmæstɪks / (AmE), / kɪləʊˈmæstɪks / (BrE)
A genus of flagellated protozoan parasites found in the intestines of vertebrates, including humans.
genus of intestinal protozoan parasites
medical

Examples

Chilomastix infection is usually asymptomatic but can sometimes cause digestive issues.
The laboratory identified Chilomastix in the stool sample during parasite screening.
Chilomastix is found worldwide, particularly in regions with poor sanitation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Giardia - a genus of intestinal protozoan parasites similar in habitat to Chilomastix

Etymology

From Greek 'cheilos' (lip) + 'mastix' (whip), referring to the appearance of its flagella.

medical

Examples

Chilomastix is often found in the intestines of humans in tropical regions.

Researchers are studying Chilomastix to understand how it adapts to its vertebrate hosts.

From Greek 'cheilos' (lip) and 'mastix' (whip), referring to its structure and flagella.
