acicula

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɪkjʊlə / (AmE), / əˈsɪkjʊlə / (BrE)
a needlelike part or structure, especially in plants, animals, or crystals, such as a spine or bristle
needlelike structure in plants, animals, or crystals

Examples

The worm's stiff acicula support its segmented body as it moves through the soil.
Microscopic examination revealed several crystal acicula in the mineral sample.
The plant's defense mechanism includes sharp acicula that deter grazing animals.

Etymology

From Latin *acicula* (little needle), diminutive of *acus* (needle).
