destructiveness

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈstrʌktɪvnəs / (AmE), / dɪˈstrʌktɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of causing damage or destruction
quality of causing destruction

Examples

The destructiveness of the storm left the entire coastline in ruins.
Some people worry about the destructiveness of new weapon technologies.
His anger sometimes reveals itself in surprising destructiveness toward his surroundings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: destructivity - the quality of being destructive
  • Antonym: constructiveness - the quality of serving to build up or improve rather than to destroy

Etymology

From 'destructive' with suffix '-ness', ultimately from Latin 'destruere' (to destroy).
