mongolism

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑŋɡəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈmɒŋɡəˌlɪzəm / (BrE)
(offensive, archaic) An outdated term for Down syndrome, a congenital disorder caused by an extra 21st chromosome, characterized by physical and intellectual disabilities.
outdated, offensive term for Down syndrome
offensive archaic medical

Examples

The term mongolism is now considered outdated and offensive in medical practice.
Older medical textbooks sometimes referred to Down syndrome as mongolism.
Use of the word mongolism has been replaced by the more accurate and respectful term, Down syndrome.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Down syndrome - congenital disorder caused by an extra 21st chromosome

Etymology

From 'Mongol', due to erroneous and offensive historical associations with physical features stereotypically attributed to Mongolian people, plus '-ism'.
