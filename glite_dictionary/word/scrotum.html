scrotum

1. NOUN

/ ˈskroʊ.təm / (AmE), / ˈskrəʊ.təm / (BrE)
the pouch of skin that contains and protects the testicles in most male mammals
external pouch containing testicles
medical

Examples

The scrotum changes position to help regulate the temperature of the testes.
During the exam, the doctor checked the patient's scrotum for any abnormalities.
Some animals have a visible scrotum, while in others it is less pronounced.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: testicle - either of the two oval organs within the scrotum that produce sperm and hormones

Etymology

From Latin 'scrotum', meaning 'quiver' or 'pouch'.
