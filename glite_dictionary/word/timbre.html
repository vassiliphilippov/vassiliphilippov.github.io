timbre

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæmbər / (AmE), / ˈtæmbə / (BrE)
the quality of a sound that makes it different from other sounds, even when they have the same pitch and loudness, especially in music or a voice
distinctive quality of a sound

Examples

The timbre of the violin made the piece sound rich and emotional.
Her speech had a warm timbre that was both calming and engaging.
Every instrument in the orchestra has its own unique timbre.

Synset Examples

the timbre of her soprano was rich and lovely
the muffled tones of the broken bell summoned them to meet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tone - a musical or vocal sound with reference to its pitch, quality, and strength

Etymology

From French 'timbre' (tone, quality of a sound).
