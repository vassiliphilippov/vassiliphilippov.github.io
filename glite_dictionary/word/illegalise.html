to illegalise

1. VERB

/ ɪˈliːɡəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ɪˈliːɡəlaɪz / (BrE)
To make something illegal by law; to outlaw.
make illegal by law
british

Examples

The government decided to illegalise the sale of certain pesticides.
Activists are campaigning to illegalise single-use plastics across the country.
If a substance is illegalised, strict penalties are put in place for possession or sale.

Synset Examples

Marijuana is criminalized in the U.S.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to legalise - to make something legal by law
  • Synonym: to criminalise - to make something a criminal offense by law

Etymology

From 'illegal' + '-ise', modelled on 'legalise'.
