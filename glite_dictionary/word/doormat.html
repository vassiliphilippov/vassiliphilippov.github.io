doormat

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɔrˌmæt / (AmE), / ˈdɔːˌmæt / (BrE)
A mat placed outside or just inside an exterior door for people to wipe their shoes on before entering.
mat for wiping shoes at a door

Examples

Please wipe your feet on the doormat before coming inside.
The doormat was muddy after the rainstorm yesterday.
I bought a new doormat with a cheerful welcome message on it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mat - a piece of material placed on a floor or other surface

Etymology

From 'door' + 'mat', reflecting its function and location.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɔːrˌmæt / (AmE), / ˈdɔːˌmæt / (BrE)
a person who is consistently weak, submissive, or easily taken advantage of by others
submissive or weak person

Examples

He let his coworkers treat him like a doormat, never standing up for himself.
She refused to be a doormat any longer and started asserting her opinions.
If you always say yes to everything, people may start seeing you as a doormat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pushover - a person who is easy to influence or defeat
  • Antonym: bully - a person who habitually seeks to harm or intimidate those whom they perceive as vulnerable

Etymology

From the literal meaning of 'doormat', a mat placed at a door, suggesting someone who gets walked over.

3. NOUN

/ ˈdoʊˌmæt / (AmE), / ˈdɔː.mæt / (BrE)
a person who allows others to treat them badly or take advantage of them, often by being excessively submissive or compliant.
person who is easily taken advantage of

Examples

She realized she had been a doormat in her last relationship and decided to stand up for herself.
He doesn't like being treated like a doormat by his coworkers anymore.
If you let people take advantage of you, they'll continue to see you as a doormat.

Synset Examples

He felt like a doormat, always letting others walk over him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pushover - a person who is easy to influence or persuade
  • Synonym: martyr - someone who suffers or allows themselves to be mistreated for others, often needlessly
  • Antonym: tyrant - a person who uses power or authority cruelly or unjustly
