vitalist

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪt̬əlɪst / (AmE), / ˈvaɪtəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes in vitalism, the doctrine that living organisms are fundamentally different from non-living entities because of a life force or vital principle
believer in vitalism

Examples

The biologist was labeled a vitalist due to his belief in a unique life force.
During the 19th century, many scientists were vitalists, holding that life could not be explained solely by chemistry and physics.
As a committed vitalist, she argued that living beings possess qualities beyond mere matter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vitalism - the doctrine that life depends on a vital principle distinct from physical forces

Etymology

From 'vitalism' (from Latin 'vita' meaning life) and '-ist' (person who believes in or practices).
