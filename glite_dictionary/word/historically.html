historically

1. ADV

/ hɪˈstɔrɪkli / (AmE), / hɪˈstɒrɪkli / (BrE)
In a manner relating to history or in terms of historical facts.
in relation to history

Examples

Historically, this region has been a center of trade and culture.
The building is historically significant due to its unique architecture.
Historically, women had fewer opportunities in higher education.

Synset Examples

this is historically interesting

Related Terms

  • Antonym: recently - in the near past; antonym in terms of time reference

Etymology

From 'historical' + '-ly.' 'Historical' from Latin 'historicus'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00110312-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ hɪˈstɔrɪkli / (AmE), / hɪˈstɒrɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to or is based on history; throughout history or in the past.
in relation to history; in the past

Examples

Historically, gold has been used as a store of value in many cultures.
The building is historically significant due to events that took place there centuries ago.
Historically, these two countries have maintained close diplomatic ties.

Synset Examples

historically they have never coexisted peacefully

Related Terms

  • Antonym: currently - at the present time

Etymology

Formed from 'historical' + '-ly'; from Greek 'historikos' via Latin and Old French.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00110430-r
Synset POS: r
