Aegypius

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈdʒɪpiəs / (AmE), / iˈdʒɪpɪəs / (BrE)
a genus of large Old World vultures in the family Accipitridae, including the cinereous vulture (Aegypius monachus)
genus of large Old World vultures

Examples

The Aegypius genus includes the impressive cinereous vulture found across parts of Europe and Asia.
Researchers observed the nesting habits of Aegypius vultures in the remote mountain regions.
The conservation status of Aegypius monachus has prompted international protection efforts.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek aigyps (vulture).

2. NOUN

/ iːˈdʒɪpiəs / (AmE), / iːˈdʒɪpɪəs / (BrE)
A genus of large Old World vultures in the family Accipitridae, notable for including the cinereous vulture.
genus of large Old World vultures

Examples

The Aegypius genus is represented by only one extant species, the cinereous vulture.
Researchers studied the habits of Aegypius in their native habitats across Eurasia.
Conservationists are working to protect Aegypius populations from further decline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cinereous vulture - a large vulture species (Aegypius monachus) found in Europe and Asia

Etymology

From Greek aigýpios (a kind of vulture).
