distortable

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈstɔrtəbl̩ / (AmE), / dɪsˈtɔːtəbəl / (BrE)
capable of being distorted; able to be altered, twisted, or misrepresented in form or meaning.
able to be distorted or twisted

Examples

Digital images are often distortable with editing software, making it easy to change their appearance.
Language is inherently distortable, allowing words to be twisted to fit various purposes.
Unlike rigid materials, rubber is highly distortable under pressure.

Synset Examples

our words are distortable things — as in a crooked mirror held up to nature

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to distort - to alter the shape or meaning of something
  • Antonym: undistortable - not capable of being distorted

Etymology

From 'distort' + '-able', meaning capable of being distorted.
