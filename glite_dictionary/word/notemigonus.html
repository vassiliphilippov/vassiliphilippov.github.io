Notemigonus

1. NOUN

/ noʊtəˈmɪɡənəs / (AmE), / nəʊtəˈmɪɡənəs / (BrE)
A genus of freshwater fish in the family Cyprinidae, commonly known as golden shiners, native to North America.
genus of freshwater fish (golden shiners)

Examples

The genus Notemigonus includes the species known as the golden shiner.
Biologists studied the distribution of Notemigonus populations in rivers across the Midwest.
Notemigonus is popular among anglers for use as bait fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golden shiner - a small, golden freshwater fish of North America

Etymology

Modern Latin, from Greek 'notos' (back) and 'mēkōn' (length); named for the elongated back.

2. NOUN

/ ˌnoʊtəˈmɪɡənəs / (AmE), / ˌnəʊtɛˈmɪɡənəs / (BrE)
A genus of freshwater fish in the family Cyprinidae, known especially for the golden shiner.
genus of freshwater fish (golden shiner)

Examples

The Notemigonus genus is easily recognizable by its slender body and silvery scales.
Aquarists often keep Notemigonus species, such as the golden shiner, in home tanks.
Research on Notemigonus has contributed to our understanding of freshwater fish behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golden shiner - a small freshwater fish of eastern North America, Notemigonus crysoleucas

Etymology

From Greek noton (back) + migo (to mix) + Latin -onus (suffix).
