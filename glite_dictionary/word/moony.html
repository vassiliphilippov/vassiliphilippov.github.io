moony

1. ADJ

/ ˈmuːni / (AmE), / ˈmuːni / (BrE)
having a dreamy, absent-minded, or distracted expression or mood
dreamy or absent-minded

Examples

She had a moony look on her face as she stared out the window, lost in thought.
His moony manner made it clear he wasn't paying attention to the meeting.
On warm summer nights, he would often become moony and drift into his own world.

Synset Examples

a woolgathering moment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dreamy - given to daydreaming; having a pleasantly vague quality
  • Synonym: woolgathering - indulging in aimless thought or dreamy daydreaming
  • Antonym: practical - concerned with actual use or practice; realistic

Etymology

From 'moon' + '-y', originally suggesting being affected by the moon or moonlight.

2. ADJ

/ ˈmuːni / (AmE), / ˈmuːni / (BrE)
Illuminated by moonlight; bathed in the light of the moon.
illuminated by moonlight

Examples

The garden looked mysterious beneath the moony sky.
We walked along the moony shoreline, listening to the waves.
The forest took on an ethereal glow in the moony darkness.

Synset Examples

the moonlit landscape

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moonlit - illuminated by moonlight

Etymology

From 'moon' + '-y', meaning 'of or pertaining to the moon'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00278872-a
Synset POS: a
