ubiquinone

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuːbɪˈkwɪnoʊn / (AmE), / ˌjuːbɪˈkwɪnəʊn / (BrE)
a quinone compound found in living cells, acting as a coenzyme in cellular respiration by transferring electrons between molecules
cellular respiration coenzyme (quinone)
medical

Examples

Ubiquinone plays a crucial role in the electron transport chain within mitochondria.
Certain supplements contain ubiquinone to support energy production in cells.
Researchers study how ubiquinone deficiencies can impact metabolic health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coenzyme Q10 - a form of ubiquinone commonly used as a dietary supplement

Etymology

From Latin 'ubique' (everywhere) + 'quinone', indicating its widespread occurrence in organisms.
