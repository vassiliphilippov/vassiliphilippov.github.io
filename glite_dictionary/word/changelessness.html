changelessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃeɪndʒləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪndʒlɪsnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of remaining unchanged or unaltered over time
state of not changing

Examples

The changelessness of the landscape made it feel like time had stood still.
Many people find comfort in the changelessness of their daily routines.
Religions sometimes promise a sense of changelessness in an ever-shifting world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immutability - the quality of being unchanging over time or unable to be changed
  • Antonym: change - the process or result of becoming different

Etymology

From 'changeless' + '-ness' (forming nouns denoting qualities or states).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃeɪndʒləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪndləsnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of remaining unchanged; lack of change or variation
the state of being unchanging

Examples

The changelessness of the mountain landscape amazed visitors every year.
Some people find comfort in the changelessness of daily routines.
Philosophers have debated the significance of changelessness in nature and society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constancy - the quality of being unchanging or unwavering
  • Synonym: immutability - the quality of being unchangeable
  • Antonym: change - the process of becoming different

Etymology

Formed from 'changeless' (unchanging) + '-ness' (forming nouns denoting a state or quality).
