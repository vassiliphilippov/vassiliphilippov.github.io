pinnate-leaved

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɪneɪt livd / (AmE), / ˈpɪneɪt liːvd / (BrE)
(of a plant) having leaves that are pinnate, with leaflets arranged on either side of a central stem.
having pinnate leaves

Examples

The pinnate-leaved ferns grew abundantly along the forest floor.
Many acacias are easily recognized by their pinnate-leaved foliage.
Some botanists specialize in studying pinnate-leaved species in tropical regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pinnate - having leaflets arranged on either side of a common axis
  • Antonym: palmate-leaved - having leaves shaped like an open hand, with lobes radiating from a center

Etymology

From Latin 'pinnatus' (feathered) + 'leaved'.
