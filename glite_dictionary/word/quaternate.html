quaternate

1. ADJ

/ kwəˈtɜr.neɪt / (AmE), / kwəˈtɜː.nət / (BrE)
(of parts, especially leaves) arranged or grouped in sets of four.
arranged in sets of four

Examples

The plant was easily identified by its quaternate leaf arrangement.
The mineral forms quaternate crystals under certain conditions.
Some shrubs exhibit quaternate branching at every node.

Synset Examples

quaternate leaves
a quaternary compound

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: ternate - arranged in groups of three
  • Degree Higher: quinary - arranged in groups of five

Etymology

From Latin 'quaternus' (containing four each).
