Monotropa uniflora

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑnəˈtroʊpə juːˈnɪflɔrə / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈtrəʊpə juːˈnɪflərə / (BrE)
a small, waxy, white or pinkish-white woodland plant with scalelike leaves and a nodding flower that turns black with age, commonly known as the ghost plant or Indian pipe
species of woodland plant (ghost plant/Indian pipe)

Examples

We found a patch of Monotropa uniflora growing in the shaded forest under the pine trees.
The delicate, ghostly appearance of Monotropa uniflora makes it easy to spot in dark woodlands.
Unlike most plants, Monotropa uniflora does not produce chlorophyll and gets its nutrients from decaying matter.

Etymology

From Greek 'monos' (single) and 'tropos' (turn), and Latin 'uniflora' (one-flowered).
