Nemophila aurita

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɛməˈfaɪlə ɔːˈriːtə / (AmE), / ˌniːməˈfaɪlə ɔːˈriːtə / (BrE)
A straggling annual herb native to California, bearing deep purple or violet flowers; sometimes classified within the genus Nemophila.
a Californian annual herb with purple flowers

Examples

The garden featured a bed of Nemophila aurita with striking violet blossoms.
Botanists have debated whether Nemophila aurita should be placed in its own genus.
During the spring, Nemophila aurita blooms add vibrant color to California meadows.

Etymology

From Greek 'nemos' (grove, glade) + 'philos' (loving).
