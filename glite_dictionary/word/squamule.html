squamule

1. NOUN

/ ˈskwæm.juːl / (AmE), / ˈskwɑː.mjuːl / (BrE)
a small or minute scale, especially as found on plants and lichens
minute scale, often on plants or lichens

Examples

Under the microscope, each lichen showed a distinct cluster of squamules along its surface.
The plant’s leaves are covered in tiny squamules that give them a powdery appearance.
Researchers noted the squamule size varied significantly between the different species of moss.

Etymology

From Latin *squamula*, diminutive of *squama* (scale).
