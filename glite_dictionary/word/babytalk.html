babytalk

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪbiˌtɔk / (AmE), / ˈbeɪbiˌtɔːk / (BrE)
the early and simplified form of speech used by very young children as they begin to learn language
speech of very young children

Examples

At eighteen months, her babytalk consisted mainly of single words and simple sounds.
Parents often find their child's babytalk both amusing and endearing.
As children grow older, their babytalk gradually evolves into more complex sentences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: childish speech - speech characteristic of children, especially in early development

2. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪ.biˌtɔk / (AmE), / ˈbeɪ.biˌtɔːk / (BrE)
Speech characterized by simple words, short sentences, and exaggerated sounds, typically used by adults imitating or communicating with young children.
simplified speech to or imitating young children

Examples

She used babytalk to make her infant smile during playtime.
The comedian's routine included a funny impression of babytalk.
Some parents worry that too much babytalk might slow their child's language development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infantile speech - the speech patterns actually used by infants and very young children

Etymology

From 'baby' + 'talk', referring to the way adults modify speech for or imitate babies.
