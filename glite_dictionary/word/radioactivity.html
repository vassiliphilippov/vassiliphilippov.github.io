radioactivity

1. NOUN

/ ˌreɪdioʊækˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəʊækˈtɪvɪti / (BrE)
the spontaneous emission of particles or electromagnetic radiation by unstable atomic nuclei as they decay into more stable forms
emission of particles/rays from decaying nuclei

Examples

The scientist measured the radioactivity emitted by the sample.
Concerns about radioactivity emerged after the nuclear plant accident.
Some rocks in nature have a low level of radioactivity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radiation - the emission of energy as electromagnetic waves or particles

Etymology

From 'radioactive' + '-ity', modeled on scientific terminology.
