gopherwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊfərˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊfəˌwʊd / (BrE)
a small deciduous tree with round-topped crown, showy white terminal flowers, and heavy hardwood used for yellow dye; also known from biblical references as the wood used to build Noah's Ark
type of deciduous tree, biblical wood

Examples

Biblical scholars have long debated the true identity of gopherwood mentioned in Genesis.
The craftsman prized the gopherwood for its density and beautiful yellow dye.
Researchers collected samples of gopherwood for their botanical studies.

Etymology

Of uncertain origin; known from the Bible (Genesis), where it is mentioned as the wood used to build Noah's Ark.
