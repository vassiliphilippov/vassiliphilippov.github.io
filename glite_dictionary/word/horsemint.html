horsemint

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrsˌmɪnt / (AmE), / ˈhɔːs.mɪnt / (BrE)
a tall, erect plant in the mint family with lance-shaped leaves and heads of purple-spotted creamy flowers, native to North America and including several subspecies
a North American mint plant with purple-spotted flowers

Examples

Native Americans have long used horsemint for its medicinal properties.
A patch of horsemint bloomed in the sunny corner of the garden.
Many pollinators, such as bees and butterflies, are attracted to horsemint flowers.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrsˌmɪnt / (AmE), / ˈhɔːs.mɪnt / (BrE)
an annual or perennial aromatic plant of the mint family, especially those of the genus Monarda, found in North America
aromatic mint plant, esp. Monarda species

Examples

The prairie was dotted with blooming horsemint in late summer.
Gardeners often plant horsemint for its attractive flowers and fragrance.
Bees are frequently attracted to horsemint when it is in full bloom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Monarda - a genus of aromatic plants commonly called bee balm or horsemint

Etymology

Named for its resemblance to true mint and former usage as feed for horses.

3. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrsˌmɪnt / (AmE), / ˈhɔːsˌmɪnt / (BrE)
a coarse mint plant with long leaves and spikelike clusters of flowers, native to the Old World and naturalized in parts of North America
a species of wild mint plant

Examples

The garden was filled with horsemint and other fragrant herbs.
Wild horsemint grows abundantly along the riverbanks in the eastern United States.
Bees are especially attracted to the purple flowers of horsemint during late summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mint - an aromatic plant, especially of the genus Mentha
