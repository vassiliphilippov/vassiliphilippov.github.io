Cornus amomum

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrnəs əˈmoʊməm / (AmE), / ˈkɔːnəs əˈməʊməm / (BrE)
A shrub native to eastern North America, characterized by purplish stems and blue fruit, also known as silky dogwood.
North American shrub with blue fruit

Examples

The wetland restoration project included planting Cornus amomum for erosion control.
Birds are attracted to the blue berries of Cornus amomum in late summer.
Gardeners value Cornus amomum for its striking purplish stems and hardiness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silky dogwood - another common name for Cornus amomum

Etymology

From Latin; 'Cornus' meaning dogwood, 'amomum' from a Greek word for a fragrant plant.
