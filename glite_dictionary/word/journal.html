journal

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɝːnəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːnəl / (BrE)
the part of a rotating shaft or axle that is supported by and turns within a bearing
shaft or axle part within a bearing

Examples

The journal must be carefully machined to ensure smooth rotation within the bearing.
Wear on the journal can lead to vibration and mechanical failure.
Engineers inspected the journal for any signs of scoring or pitting.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɝːnəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːnəl / (BrE)
a daily written record of personal experiences and observations
daily record of personal experiences

Examples

She kept a journal throughout her travels in Europe.
Writing in his journal every evening helped him process his thoughts.
Many people use a journal to record their goals and achievements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diary - a book in which you write down your experiences and thoughts each day

Etymology

From Old French 'journal' (daily), from Latin 'diurnalis' (daily).

3. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɝː.nəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜː.nəl / (BrE)
a record book in which financial transactions are entered in the order they occur, forming part of the accounting process
book for recording financial transactions

Examples

The accountant entered the expenses in the journal before updating the ledger.
Each sale was recorded in the journal on the day it occurred.
Careful maintenance of a journal is essential for accurate financial statements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ledger - a principal accounting book for final entry of transactions

Etymology

From Old French 'journal' (daily), from Latin 'diurnalis' (of the day).
