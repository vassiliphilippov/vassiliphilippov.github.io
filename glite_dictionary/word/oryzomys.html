Oryzomys

1. NOUN

/ ɔˈraɪzəˌmɪs / (AmE), / ɒˈraɪzəˌmɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small, semiaquatic rodents commonly known as rice rats, found in the Americas.
genus of rice rats

Examples

The marshy fields are home to several species of Oryzomys.
Researchers studied the behavior of Oryzomys in the Amazon basin.
Unlike many rodents, Oryzomys are adapted to wet environments.

Etymology

From Greek 'oryza' (rice) and 'mys' (mouse, rat).
