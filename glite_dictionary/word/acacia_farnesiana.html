Acacia farnesiana

1. NOUN

/ əˈkeɪʃə fɑrˈniːziənə / (AmE), / əˈkeɪʃə fɑːˈniːziənə / (BrE)
a tropical American thorny shrub or small tree, with fragrant yellow flowers used in perfumery
tropical shrub or tree; perfumery flowers

Examples

The fragrant yellow flowers of Acacia farnesiana are highly valued in the perfume industry.
In warm climates, Acacia farnesiana can be found growing as a thorny shrub along roadsides.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of extracts from Acacia farnesiana leaves.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Acacia' from Greek 'akakia', a name for a thorny Egyptian tree; 'farnesiana' honors Odoardo Farnese, an Italian nobleman.
