autodidactic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɔtoʊdaɪˈdæktɪk / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəʊdaɪˈdæktɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of an autodidact; self-taught.
relating to being self-taught

Examples

Her autodidactic approach to learning music allowed her to master several instruments without formal lessons.
The autodidactic nature of his studies impressed the admissions committee at the university.
Many inventors have an autodidactic streak, teaching themselves skills that schools do not offer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-taught - having learned by oneself rather than through formal instruction

Etymology

From Greek autodidaktos (self-taught), from autos (self) + didaktos (taught).
