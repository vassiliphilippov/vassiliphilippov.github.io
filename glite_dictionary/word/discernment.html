discernment

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsɜrn.mənt / (AmE), / dɪˈsɜːn.mənt / (BrE)
The ability to perceive or recognize things, especially those that are not immediately obvious or are difficult to detect.
ability to perceive hidden things

Examples

Her discernment allowed her to spot the subtle flaws in the plan that others missed.
A critic needs great discernment to appreciate truly original works of art.
Through careful discernment, the investigator uncovered evidence that had gone unnoticed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perception - the ability to see, hear, or become aware of something through the senses
  • Synonym: insight - the ability to understand people or situations accurately
  • Antonym: obtuseness - lack of perceptiveness or quickness to perceive

Etymology

From Latin discernere, meaning 'to separate, distinguish'.
