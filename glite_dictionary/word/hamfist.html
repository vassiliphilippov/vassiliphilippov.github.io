hamfist

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæmˌfɪst / (AmE), / ˈhæmˌfɪst / (BrE)
an approach or method that is clumsy or heavy-handed
clumsy or heavy-handed method
informal

Examples

His hamfist approach to the project caused several mistakes that could have been avoided.
Negotiators criticized her hamfist style for making discussions more difficult than necessary.
Using a hamfist technique in delicate matters often leads to poor results.

Synset Examples

His hamfist approach to the project caused several mistakes.
She often gets criticized for her hamfist style in negotiations.
I wish he wouldn’t use such a hamfist technique in delicate matters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ham-fisted - clumsy or inept, especially with one's hands

Etymology

From 'ham-fisted' (clumsy or heavy-handed), influenced by 'fist' and imagery of handling with a ham-like fist.
