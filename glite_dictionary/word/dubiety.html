dubiety

1. NOUN

/ duˈbaɪ.ə.ti / (AmE), / djuːˈbaɪ.ə.ti / (BrE)
the state or quality of being doubtful or uncertain; doubt or indecision
state of being doubtful or uncertain

Examples

There was a sense of dubiety in his voice as he answered the question.
The proposal was met with dubiety from the board members, who were unsure of its feasibility.
Her dubiety about the truth of the story made her hesitant to respond.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doubt - feeling of uncertainty or lack of conviction
  • Antonym: certainty - the state of being certain or confident

Etymology

From Latin 'dubietas', from 'dubius' (doubtful).
