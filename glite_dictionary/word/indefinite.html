indefinite

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈdɛfɪnət / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛfɪnət / (BrE)
not clearly defined, decided, or known; without fixed limits or exactness
not clearly defined or decided

Examples

The company gave an indefinite answer about when the repairs would be complete.
Her job contract was for an indefinite period, with no set end date.
The plans for the new project remain indefinite until further notice.

Synset Examples

were indefinite about their plans
plans are indefinite

Related Terms

  • Antonym: definite - clearly defined; certain or decided
  • Synonym: uncertain - not able to be relied on; not known or definite

Etymology

From Latin 'indefinitus', from in- (not) + definitus (defined).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈdɛfɪnət / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛfɪnət / (BrE)
not clear or fixed in meaning, limits, form, or duration; vague or not precisely defined
not clearly defined or stated

Examples

The instructions were so indefinite that no one knew what to do.
She gave an indefinite answer when asked about her future plans.
The boundaries of the park are rather indefinite and not clearly marked.

Synset Examples

must you be so indefinite?
amorphous blots of color having vague and indefinite edges
he would not answer so indefinite a proposal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vague - lacking clarity or definite shape or form
  • Antonym: definite - clearly defined or determined

Etymology

From Latin 'indefinitus' (not limited).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00704924-a
Synset POS: a
