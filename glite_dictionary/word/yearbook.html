yearbook

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɪrˌbʊk / (AmE), / ˈjɪəˌbʊk / (BrE)
A book published once every year, containing information, events, or records about the past year, especially for a school or organization.
annual book of yearly records or events

Examples

The school distributes a yearbook every spring, which is filled with photos and memories.
I found an old yearbook from 1985 in the attic last weekend.
Many students enjoy signing each other's yearbook at the end of the academic year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: almanac - an annual calendar with important dates and information

Etymology

Formed from 'year' + 'book', indicating a book released each year.

2. NOUN

/ ˈjɪrˌbʊk / (AmE), / ˈjɪəˌbʊk / (BrE)
A book published each year by the graduating class of a school or college, typically containing photographs of students and staff and a record of activities and events.
annual book with school photos and events

Examples

She flipped through her old yearbook and smiled at the memories.
The school's yearbook committee worked hard to meet the publishing deadline.
He asked his friends to sign his yearbook on the last day of school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annual - occurring once every year

Etymology

From 'year' + 'book', indicating a book for a particular year.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07297202-n
Synset POS: n
