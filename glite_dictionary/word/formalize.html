to formalize

1. VERB

/ ˈfɔːrməˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfɔːməlʌɪz / (BrE)
to make something official, especially by giving it a legal or formal status
make official or legally valid

Examples

The committee voted to formalize the agreement between the two companies.
They decided to formalize their partnership through a written contract.
After years of informal cooperation, the organizations finally formalized their relationship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to legalize - make something legal, especially by law
  • Antonym: to invalidate - make something not legally valid

Etymology

From French *formaliser*, from *formel* (formal), from Latin *formalis* (of form).

2. VERB

/ ˈfɔrməˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfɔːməlʌɪz / (BrE)
to make something become formal or official, especially by following established procedures or rules
make formal or official

Examples

The committee decided to formalize the agreement with a written contract.
We need to formalize the process to avoid any confusion in the future.
After months of negotiation, both countries formalized their partnership with an official ceremony.

Synset Examples

We formalized the appointment and gave him a title

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to institutionalize - to make into an accepted or established practice or custom
  • Synonym: to officialize - to make official; to give formal status to
  • Antonym: to abolish - to formally put an end to something, especially a system or practice

Etymology

From French 'formaliser', from Latin 'formalis' (formal).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02484525-v
Synset POS: v
