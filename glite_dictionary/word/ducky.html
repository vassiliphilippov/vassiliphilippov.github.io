ducky

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌki / (AmE), / ˈdʌki / (BrE)
A person who is especially loved or cherished; used as a term of endearment.
term of endearment for a loved one
informal british

Examples

Come here, my ducky, and let me give you a hug.
He always calls his wife ducky in letters.
'How are you today, ducky?' the shopkeeper asked kindly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: darling - a person who is very dear or loved
  • Synonym: sweetheart - a person loved by someone else

Etymology

Diminutive of 'duck', used in British English as a term of affection.
