ill-omened

1. ADJ

/ ɪl ˈoʊmənd / (AmE), / ɪl ˈəʊmənd / (BrE)
Regarded as a sign of bad luck or likely to lead to misfortune.
likely to bring bad luck

Examples

The expedition was considered ill-omened from the moment it set out in stormy weather.
Some saw the broken mirror as an ill-omened sign for the wedding day.
They abandoned the ill-omened plan after a series of mishaps.

Synset Examples

their business venture was doomed from the start
an ill-fated business venture
an ill-starred romance
the unlucky prisoner was again put in irons

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unlucky - having or bringing bad luck
  • Synonym: ill-fated - doomed to end badly or fail
  • Antonym: fortunate - bringing or marked by good luck

Etymology

Formed from 'ill-' (bad) + 'omened' (having an omen), reflecting the idea of an unfavorable sign.
