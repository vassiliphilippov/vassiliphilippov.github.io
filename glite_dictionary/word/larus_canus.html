Larus canus

1. PROPN

/ ˈlɛər.əs ˈkeɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈlɑː.rəs ˈka.nəs / (BrE)
A common gull species found in Eurasia and northeastern North America, characterized by grey wings and a white body.
a species of common gull

Examples

The Larus canus is often seen searching for food along coastal waters.
During migration season, large flocks of Larus canus can be observed near lakes and rivers.
The pale grey wings help distinguish Larus canus from other gull species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gull - a seabird with long wings and typically white or grey plumage

Etymology

From Latin: 'larus' meaning gull and 'canus' meaning grey or hoary.
