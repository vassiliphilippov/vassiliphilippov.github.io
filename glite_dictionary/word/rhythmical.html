rhythmical

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɪðmɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈrɪðmɪkəl / (BrE)
recurring with a regular, measured rhythm; having or characterized by rhythm
having or characterized by rhythm

Examples

The poem is notable for its rhythmical patterns and lyrical quality.
She danced with a rhythmical grace that captivated the audience.
The machine produced a rhythmical humming sound throughout the night.

Synset Examples

the rhythmic chiming of church bells
rhythmical prose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhythmic - having a regular repeated pattern of sounds or movements

Etymology

From rhythm + -ical, formed in analogy to other -ical adjectives.
