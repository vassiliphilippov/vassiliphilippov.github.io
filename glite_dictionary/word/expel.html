to expel

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈspɛl / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɛl / (BrE)
To officially force someone to leave a place, organization, or country.
force to leave (officially)

Examples

The student was expelled for violating school rules.
They threatened to expel anyone who broke the agreement.
After being found guilty, he was expelled from the country.

Synset Examples

He was expelled from his native country

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dismiss - to remove someone from a position or role
  • Synonym: to banish - to send someone away as a punishment
  • Antonym: to admit - to allow someone to enter

Etymology

From Latin 'expellere' (to drive out), from 'ex-' (out) + 'pellere' (to drive).

force to leave or drive out

Examples

They hoped loud noises would expel the birds from the warehouse.

4. VERB

/ ɪkˈspɛl / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɛl / (BrE)
to officially force someone to leave a position, office, or organization
to force someone out of a position or office

Examples

After an internal investigation, he was expelled from the board for misconduct.
The school decided to expel the principal after the scandal broke.
Several members were expelled from the organization due to unethical behavior.

Synset Examples

The chairman was ousted after he misappropriated funds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dismiss - to remove someone from their job or position
  • Synonym: to oust - to remove someone from a position or place of power or authority
  • Antonym: to reinstate - to restore someone to their former position or state

Etymology

From Latin expellere, from ex- (out) + pellere (to drive).
