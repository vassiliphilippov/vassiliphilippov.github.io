sol

1. NOUN

/ sɑl, soʊl / (AmE), / sɒl, səʊl / (BrE)
A type of colloid in which solid particles are dispersed in a liquid.
colloid with solid in liquid
medical

Examples

Milk is an example of a sol because fat particles are dispersed in liquid.
When heated, the gel turned into a sol and began to flow more easily.
In the laboratory, a sol can be created by combining powdered solid with water.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gel - a colloid in which the liquid phase is constrained within a solid network

Etymology

From French 'sol', from Latin 'solum' (ground, soil).

2. NOUN

/ soʊl / (AmE), / sɒl / (BrE)
the syllable used to name the fifth (dominant) note of a musical scale in the solfège (solmization) system; equivalent to the note G in fixed-do system.
syllable for fifth note of scale (solfège)

Examples

In the key of C major, sol corresponds to the note G.
She practiced singing the scale from do up to sol and back down again.
In solfège, sol is followed by la as the sixth note.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: so - alternate spelling for the same solfège note
  • Degree Lower: do - the first note of the scale in solfège
  • Degree Higher: la - the sixth note of the scale in solfège

Etymology

From Italian sol, part of the medieval solmization system.
