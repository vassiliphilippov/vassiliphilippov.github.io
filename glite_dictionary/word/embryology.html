embryology

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛm.briˈɑ.lə.dʒi / (AmE), / ˌɛm.brɪˈɒl.ə.dʒi / (BrE)
The branch of biology that studies the formation and early development of living organisms.
study of embryos and their development
medical

Examples

She decided to specialize in embryology after discovering her fascination with early development in animals.
Recent advances in embryology have improved our understanding of birth defects.
The textbook includes several chapters dedicated to the fundamentals of embryology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: developmental biology - the study of the process by which organisms grow and develop

Etymology

From Greek 'embryon' (fetus, embryo) + '-logy' (the study of).
