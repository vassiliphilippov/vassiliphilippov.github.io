M3

1. NOUN

/ ɛm θri / (AmE), / ɛm θri / (BrE)
an economic measure of the money supply that includes M2 plus deposits at institutions that are not banks, such as savings and loan associations.
broad measure of money supply

Examples

Many countries no longer report M3 data as part of their official statistics.
Economists often compare M3 to other money supply measures to analyze monetary trends.
A jump in M3 may indicate rising liquidity in the economy.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: M2 - narrower measure of money supply excluding some large deposits

Etymology

Abbreviation for 'Money 3' or 'Measure 3', indicating the third level of money supply classification.
