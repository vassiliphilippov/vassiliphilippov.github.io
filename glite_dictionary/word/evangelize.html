to evangelize

1. VERB

/ ɪˈvæn.dʒəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ɪˈvan.dʒɪ.laɪz / (BrE)
to try to convert people to Christianity by spreading its teachings
preach or spread the Christian faith

Examples

The missionaries traveled across Africa to evangelize remote communities.
He felt called to evangelize after his spiritual awakening.
They used radio broadcasts to evangelize to people in distant regions.

Synset Examples

The missionaries evangelized the Pacific Islanders

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to proselytize - to try to convert someone to a belief or religion

Etymology

From Medieval Latin evangelizare, from Greek euangelizesthai (to bring good news).

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪˈvæn.dʒəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ɪˈvan.dʒə.laɪz / (BrE)
to preach the Christian gospel or try to convert others to Christianity
preach or convert to Christianity

Examples

The missionaries traveled overseas to evangelize remote villages.
She felt called to evangelize among her local community through service and prayer.
Some television programs are designed to evangelize a wider audience about Christianity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to proselytize - to try to convert someone to one's faith or beliefs

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'evangelizare' (to preach the gospel), from Greek 'euangelizesthai' (to bring good news).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00829888-v
Synset POS: v
