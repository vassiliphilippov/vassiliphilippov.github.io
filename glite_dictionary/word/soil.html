soil

1. NOUN

/ sɔɪl / (AmE), / sɔɪl / (BrE)
The territory or land under the jurisdiction of a specific country or sovereign state.
territory or land of a state

Examples

After the agreement, foreign troops had to withdraw from national soil.
He dreams of returning to Irish soil after many years abroad.
The embassy is considered the sovereign soil of its home country.

Synset Examples

American troops were stationed on Japanese soil

2. NOUN

/ sɔɪl / (AmE), / sɔɪl / (BrE)
a stain or taint that makes something impure, dirty, or unclean
stain or impurity

Examples

The scandal left a soil on his otherwise unblemished reputation.
She felt that any lie would bring a soil to her good name.
No soil of suspicion ever attached itself to her honesty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stain - a mark or discoloration that spoils the appearance; something that tarnishes purity or honor
  • Antonym: purity - the state of being clean or free from contamination or sin

Etymology

From Latin 'solium' (seat, throne) or Old French 'soillier' (to make dirty).

3. NOUN

/ sɔɪl / (AmE), / sɔɪl / (BrE)
the top layer of the earth in which plants grow, consisting of a mixture of organic matter, minerals, gases, liquids, and organisms
top layer of earth for plant growth

Examples

The gardener tested the soil before planting the new flowers.
Rich, fertile soil is essential for growing healthy crops.
After years of farming, the soil became depleted of nutrients.

Synset Examples

the land had never been plowed
good agricultural soil

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dirt - loose earth or soil, often implying uncleanliness
  • Synonym: earth - the substance forming the surface of the world; soil

Etymology

From Old French 'soil' (ground), from Latin 'solium' (seat, foundation).

top layer of earth where plants grow

Examples

The rich soil in the valley produces excellent crops.
Farmers must protect the soil from erosion caused by wind and rain.
After digging through layers of soil, the archaeologists found ancient artifacts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dirt - loose earth or soil, often considered unclean

Etymology

From Old French *sol* (ground, earth), from Latin *solum* (bottom, ground, soil).

5. VERB

/ sɔɪl / (AmE), / sɔɪl / (BrE)
to make something dirty or stained, especially by contact with dirt, mud, or another substance
make dirty or stained

Examples

Please take off your shoes so you don't soil the carpet.
She accidentally soiled her dress when she dropped her lunch.
The dog's muddy paws soiled the freshly cleaned floor.

Synset Examples

don't soil your clothes when you play outside!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dirty - to make unclean or filthy
  • Antonym: clean - to make free from dirt; antonym of 'soil'

Etymology

From Old French 'soillier', from Latin 'suillāre', meaning to make dirty.
