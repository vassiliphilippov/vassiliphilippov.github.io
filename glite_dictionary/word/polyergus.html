Polyergus

1. NOUN

/ pəˈlaɪ.ɚ.ɡəs / (AmE), / pɒˈlaɪ.ə.ɡəs / (BrE)
A genus of ants known as Amazon ants, noted for their habit of raiding the nests of other ants to capture their pupae.
genus of Amazon ants

Examples

The Polyergus rely entirely on enslaved workers from other ant species to maintain their colonies.
Researchers observed that Polyergus conduct organized raids to kidnap pupae from nearby Formica colonies.
Because Polyergus cannot care for themselves, they depend on their captured slaves for food and brood care.

Etymology

From New Latin Polyergus, from Greek polyergos (very active, industrious): 'poly-' (many) + 'ergon' (work).

2. NOUN

/ pəˈlɪərɡəs / (AmE), / pɒlɪˈɜːɡəs / (BrE)
A genus of ants, commonly known as Amazon ants, noted for their habit of raiding the nests of other ant species and enslaving their workers.
genus of raiding Amazon ants

Examples

The Polyergus ants are notorious for enslaving workers from other colonies.
Scientists observed a dramatic raid by a Polyergus colony on a nearby ant nest.
Many researchers study Polyergus to understand social behavior in insects.

Etymology

From Greek polys (many) + ergon (work), referring to ant colonies’ division of labor.
