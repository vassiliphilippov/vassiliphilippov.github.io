Phylloscopus

1. NOUN

/ fɪˈlɒskəpəs / (AmE), / fɪˈlɒskəpəs / (BrE)
A genus of small insectivorous songbirds known as leaf warblers, native to Eurasia and Africa.
genus of leaf warblers (birds)

Examples

The Phylloscopus genus includes some of the most widespread leaf warblers in Eurasia.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot a Phylloscopus in the dense forest undergrowth.
Many species within Phylloscopus migrate thousands of miles each year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warbler - a small songbird, many of which belong to Phylloscopus

Etymology

From Greek 'phyllon' (leaf) and 'skopos' (seeker), referring to their habit of searching among leaves.
