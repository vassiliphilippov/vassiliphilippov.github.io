bionic

1. ADJ

/ baɪˈɑnɪk / (AmE), / baɪˈɒnɪk / (BrE)
Involving or relating to the application of electronic or mechanical devices to biological systems, especially for enhancing or replacing body parts or functions.
relating to artificial or enhanced body parts

Examples

After her accident, she received a bionic arm that allowed her to regain full movement.
The concept of bionic eyes has fascinated science fiction writers for decades.
Olympic authorities debated whether the use of certain bionic limbs created an unfair advantage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artificial - made by human skill; not natural

Etymology

Formed from 'bio-' (life) and 'electronic', used since the 1960s.

2. ADJ

/ baɪˈɑnɪk / (AmE), / baɪˈɒnɪk / (BrE)
Having physiological functions or parts enhanced or replaced by electronic or electromechanical devices.
enhanced by electronic devices

Examples

After her accident, she received a bionic arm that allowed her to regain movement.
Scientists are developing bionic eyes to help restore sight to the visually impaired.
The hero in the movie had several bionic enhancements that gave him superhuman abilities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cybernetic - relating to systems that use computer technology to control devices or processes

Etymology

Blend of 'biological' and 'electronic,' popularized in the 1970s by science fiction.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01576402-s
Synset POS: s
