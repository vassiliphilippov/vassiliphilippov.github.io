puss-puss

1. NOUN

/ pʊs pʊs / (AmE), / pʊs pʊs / (BrE)
Used as a term of endearment when addressing or referring to a cat or kitten.
affectionate term for a cat or kitten

Examples

She called out, "Here, puss-puss, dinner time!"
The children chased the playful puss-puss around the garden.
Whenever I pick up my puss-puss, she purrs contentedly in my arms.

Synset Examples

She gently stroked her puss-puss while watching TV.
The children giggled and called the kitten puss-puss.
My cat loves to be called puss-puss when she’s being affectionate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puss - a familiar or affectionate term for a cat

Etymology

Formed by reduplication of 'puss', a term for a cat, possibly of Scandinavian origin.
