two-dimensional

1. ADJ

/ tuː dɪˈmɛnʃənəl / (AmE), / tuː dɪˈmɛnʃənəl / (BrE)
lacking depth or substance; consisting only of width and height, not giving an illusion of depth
lacking depth; flat

Examples

The critics complained that the story's characters were two-dimensional and lacked complexity.
Modern art sometimes intentionally uses two-dimensional forms to challenge the viewer's perception.
Her description of the city felt two-dimensional, missing any sense of atmosphere or life.

Synset Examples

a film with two-dimensional characters
a flat two-dimensional painting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shallow - lacking depth or substance
  • Antonym: three-dimensional - having or appearing to have height, width, and depth

2. ADJ

/ tuː dɪˈmɛnʃənl / (AmE), / ˌtuː dɪˈmɛnʃənl / (BrE)
having or relating to two dimensions, such as length and width, but no depth
having length and width but no depth

Examples

The artist created a two-dimensional image using only pencil and paper.
In mathematics, we often work with two-dimensional shapes like squares and circles.
The movie's graphics appeared flat and two-dimensional compared to newer 3D films.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: three-dimensional - having or relating to three dimensions, such as length, width, and depth

3. ADJ

/ ˌtuː.dɪˈmɛn.ʃən.əl / (AmE), / ˌtjuː.dɪˈmɛn.ʃən.əl / (BrE)
Lacking depth or complexity; not exhibiting the qualities of three-dimensionality, either physically or in character and content.
lacking depth or complexity

Examples

The critics found the villain of the story to be too two-dimensional and predictable.
The scenery in the play was very two-dimensional, with simple painted backdrops.
He tends to write two-dimensional female characters who lack emotional depth.

Synset Examples

a film with two-dimensional characters
a flat two-dimensional painting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flat - lacking depth or three-dimensional quality

Etymology

Formed from 'two' and 'dimensional', originally referring to geometrical dimensions.

4. ADJ

/ tuː dɪˌmɛnʃəˈnəl / (AmE), / tjuː dɪˌmɛnʃəˈnəl / (BrE)
having only height and width, lacking depth
having height and width but not depth

Examples

The diagram is two-dimensional, so it does not show the depth of the object.
She created a two-dimensional map representing only the surface features of the area.
Unlike a real cube, a square is two-dimensional and has no volume.

Synset Examples

The artwork was strikingly two-dimensional in style.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: three-dimensional - having height, width, and depth

Etymology

Formed from 'two' + 'dimensional'; based on 'dimension' from Latin 'dimensio' (a measuring).
