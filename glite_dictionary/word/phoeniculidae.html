Phoeniculidae

1. NOUN

/ foʊˌniːnɪˈkɪlɪdiː / (AmE), / fəˌniːnɪˈkɪlɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of birds known as wood hoopoes, found in sub-Saharan Africa, characterized by their long, curved bills and iridescent plumage.
family of birds called wood hoopoes

Examples

The Phoeniculidae are easily recognized by their iridescent feathers and long, curved bills.
Researchers observed several species within the Phoeniculidae family feeding on insects in wooded areas.
Unlike true hoopoes, Phoeniculidae usually nest in tree cavities rather than on the ground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wood hoopoe - Common name for birds in the Phoeniculidae family

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Phoeniculus (type genus), from Greek 'phoinikous' (crimson) + '-idae' (family suffix in zoology).
