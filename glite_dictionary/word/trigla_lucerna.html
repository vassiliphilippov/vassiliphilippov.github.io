Trigla lucerna

1. NOUN

/ trɪɡ.lə luˈsɜːr.nə / (AmE), / trɪɡ.lə luˈsɜː.nə / (BrE)
A species of marine fish in the gurnard family, known commonly as the tub gurnard, found in the eastern Atlantic Ocean and Mediterranean Sea.
a species of gurnard fish

Examples

The Trigla lucerna is often found in shallow coastal waters across Europe.
Fishermen value the Trigla lucerna for its firm white flesh.
Marine biologists are studying the habitat preferences of Trigla lucerna in the Mediterranean Sea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gurnard - a group of marine fishes known for their armored heads and wing-like pectoral fins

Etymology

From Latin: 'Trigla', the genus of gurnards; 'lucerna', meaning 'lantern', possibly referring to the fish's appearance.
