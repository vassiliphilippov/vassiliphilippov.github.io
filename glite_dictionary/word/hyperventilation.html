hyperventilation

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpərˌvɛntɪˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəˌvɛntɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
excessively rapid or deep breathing beyond the body's needs, often leading to dizziness or tingling sensations
excessively rapid or deep breathing
medical

Examples

She began to experience hyperventilation during the panic attack.
The doctor explained that hyperventilation could lead to lightheadedness and numbness.
Practicing slow breathing can help prevent hyperventilation in anxious situations.

Etymology

With prefix hyper- (over, excessive) + ventilation, from Latin ventilare (to fan, agitate).
