unfaithful

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfeɪθfəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈfeɪθfəl / (BrE)
not remaining loyal to a commitment, promise, or duty; not faithful
not loyal or faithful

Examples

She felt betrayed when she discovered her partner had been unfaithful to her.
Employers expect their workers not to be unfaithful to their duties.
Rumors spread quickly about the celebrity's alleged unfaithful behavior.

Synset Examples

an unfaithful lover

Related Terms

  • Antonym: faithful - remaining loyal and steadfast to a person, cause, or belief

Etymology

From un- (prefix meaning 'not') + faithful, from faith + -ful.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00962920-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfeɪθfəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈfeɪθf(ə)l / (BrE)
behaving in a way that betrays trust or loyalty; having the character of a traitor or betrayer
betraying trust; traitorous

Examples

Many saw his actions as unfaithful to his country and comrades.
Her unfaithful conduct during the debate shocked even her closest supporters.
The agent was discovered to be unfaithful and quickly dismissed for betraying his organization.

Synset Examples

the faithless Benedict Arnold
a lying traitorous insurrectionist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faithless - lacking loyalty or trustworthiness
  • Antonym: loyal - showing firm and constant support or allegiance
  • Synonym: traitorous - having the character of a traitor; betraying trust

Etymology

From un- (not) + faithful, originally from Latin 'fidelis' (faithful).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00966128-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfeɪθfəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈfeɪθfəl / (BrE)
not true to the facts or original; not accurately representing the original or not trustworthy
not true to the facts or original

Examples

The movie was criticized as an unfaithful adaptation of the novel.
This translation is rather unfaithful to the original text.
Their copy was an unfaithful reproduction, missing many important details.

Synset Examples

an unfaithful reproduction

Related Terms

  • Antonym: faithful - accurately representing the original; trustworthy

Etymology

From un- (not) + faithful, from Old French feid (faith), from Latin fides.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00024180-s
Synset POS: s

4. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfeɪθfəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈfeɪθfəl / (BrE)
having sexual relations with someone other than your husband, wife, boyfriend, or girlfriend; not remaining loyal in a romantic relationship
not remaining loyal in a romantic relationship

Examples

After ten years of marriage, she discovered that her husband had been unfaithful to her.
Rumors spread quickly when it became clear he was unfaithful in their relationship.
She forgave him, even though he had been unfaithful more than once.

Synset Examples

her husband was unfaithful

Related Terms

  • Antonym: faithful - remaining loyal in a romantic relationship

Etymology

Formed with the prefix 'un-' (not) and 'faithful'. 'Faithful' from Middle English, from Old French 'feith' (faith) + '-ful'.
