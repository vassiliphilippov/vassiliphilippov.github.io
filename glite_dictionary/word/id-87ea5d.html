id

1. NOUN

/ ɪd / (AmE), / ɪd / (BrE)
(in psychoanalysis) the part of the mind containing instinctive, primitive impulses and desires, operating unconsciously according to the pleasure principle.
psychoanalytic part of the mind

Examples

Freud believed that the id was responsible for our most basic urges and desires.
According to psychoanalytic theory, the id operates unconsciously and seeks immediate gratification.
He argued that a strong id can make it difficult to control one's impulses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ego - part of the psyche that mediates between the id, reality, and the superego
  • Synonym: superego - part of the mind representing internalized ideals and morals

Etymology

From Latin 'id' (it); introduced by Sigmund Freud for the unconscious aspect of the psyche.
