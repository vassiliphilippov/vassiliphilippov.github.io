lipotropic

1. ADJ

/ ˌlɪpoʊˈtroʊpɪk, ˌlaɪp- / (AmE), / ˌlɪpəˈtrəʊpɪk, ˌlaɪp- / (BrE)
(of a substance) having an affinity for or promoting the breakdown of lipids (fats) in the body.
promoting the breakdown of fats
medical

Examples

Certain vitamins are considered lipotropic because they help the body process and remove fats.
Choline is classified as a lipotropic agent due to its role in fat metabolism.
Doctors may recommend lipotropic injections to support weight loss in some medical programs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lipid - any of a class of organic compounds that are fatty acids or their derivatives

Etymology

From Greek 'lipos' (fat) + 'tropos' (turn, direction).
