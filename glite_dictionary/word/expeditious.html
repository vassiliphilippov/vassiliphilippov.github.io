expeditious

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛk.spəˈdɪʃ.əs / (AmE), / ˌɛk.spɪˈdɪʃ.əs / (BrE)
done with speed and efficiency; quick and efficient.
quick and efficient

Examples

The company is known for its expeditious handling of customer requests.
She appreciated the expeditious resolution of her complaint.
An expeditious investigation helped authorities solve the case quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: efficient - achieving maximum productivity with minimum wasted effort or expense
  • Antonym: slow - moving or operating at a low speed; not quick

Etymology

From Latin 'expeditus' (unimpeded, swift), from 'expedire' (to free, set right).
