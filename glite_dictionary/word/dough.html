2. NOUN

/ doʊ / (AmE), / dəʊ / (BrE)
A mixture of flour and liquid, sometimes with other ingredients, that is stiff enough to knead or shape, used as a base for baked goods such as bread or pastries.
mixture of flour and liquid for baking

Examples

She kneaded the dough carefully before letting it rise.
The recipe calls for the dough to rest for an hour.
I rolled out the dough to make a fresh pizza crust.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: batter - a mixture of flour and liquid that is thinner and can be poured
  • Synonym: pastry - dough of flour, water, and shortening, used for pies and tarts

Etymology

From Middle English *dogh*, from Old English *dāg*, from Proto-Germanic *daigaz* (dough, kneaded mass).
