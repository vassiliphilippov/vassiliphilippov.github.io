Donnian

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʌniən / (AmE), / ˈdʌniən / (BrE)
Relating to, characteristic of, or in the style of John Donne, the English poet and cleric.
of or relating to John Donne or his style

Examples

Her poetry took a distinctly Donnian turn, full of wit and metaphysical conceits.
The critic admired the Donnian qualities in the novel’s introspective narrative voice.
Many imitators struggle to achieve the subtlety of Donnian language and imagery.

Etymology

Formed from the name John Donne (1572–1631), English poet, with the adjectival suffix -ian.
