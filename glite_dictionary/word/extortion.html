extortion

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstɔɹʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɔːʃən / (BrE)
the act of obtaining something, especially money, through force, threats, or the misuse of authority
the act of obtaining something by force or misuse of authority

Examples

The mayor was accused of extortion for demanding payments from local businesses.
Through extortion, the official collected illegal fees from citizens seeking permits.
The company's investigation revealed years of extortion by corrupt employees.

Synset Examples

the extortion by dishonest officials of fees for performing their sworn duty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackmail - the act of demanding money from someone by threatening to reveal compromising information
  • Synonym: coercion - the practice of persuading someone to do something by using force or threats

Etymology

From Latin extortionem (a twisting out, extortion), from extorquere (to wrench out).

2. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstɔɹʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɔːʃən / (BrE)
an exorbitant or excessively high charge or price demanded for something, especially one perceived as unfair or unreasonable
an exorbitant or unfair charge

Examples

The plumber's bill was pure extortion for such a simple job.
Ticket prices during the holidays amount to extortion when demand is high.
Many travelers complained about the extortion at local tourist shops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rip-off - an act of overcharging or swindling someone
  • Antonym: bargain - something bought or offered for sale at a favorable price

Etymology

From Latin extortio (a twisting out), from extorquere (to wrench out).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13328923-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ɪkˈstɔɹʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɔːʃən / (BrE)
the crime of obtaining money or something else of value from someone by using threats, force, or other unfair means
obtaining money by threats or force
legal

Examples

The businessman was found guilty of extortion after threatening his rival with violence.
Police arrested the gang members for their involvement in extortion schemes targeting shop owners.
Victims of extortion often feel trapped and afraid to report the crime.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blackmail - the crime of demanding money from someone by threatening to reveal damaging information about them

Etymology

From Latin extortio, from extorquere (to wrench out).
