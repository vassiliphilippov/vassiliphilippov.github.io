Nyssaceae

1. NOUN

/ nɪˈseɪsiˌi / (AmE), / nɪˈseɪsiːiː / (BrE)
a family of dicotyledonous trees in the order Myrtales, including the sour gum trees.
plant family including sour gum trees

Examples

The Nyssaceae family contains notable species such as the black tupelo and water tupelo.
Botanists recently reclassified certain trees into the Nyssaceae based on genetic research.
The fruit of some Nyssaceae members provides food for wildlife in North American wetlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sour gum - common name for trees in the genus Nyssa

Etymology

From the genus name Nyssa, named after a water nymph in Greek mythology, plus the standard botanical suffix -aceae for plant families.

2. NOUN

/ nɪˈseɪsi.iː / (AmE), / nɪˈseɪsi.iː / (BrE)
a family of dicotyledonous trees in the order Myrtales, which includes the sour gum trees
botanical family including sour gum trees

Examples

The Nyssaceae family contains trees commonly known as sour gums.
Botanists have recently revised the classification of Nyssaceae.
Some species in the Nyssaceae family are important in forestry and ecology.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Nyssa.
