moonshine

1. NOUN

/ ˈmuːnˌʃaɪn / (AmE), / ˈmuːnʃaɪn / (BrE)
the light that comes from the Moon, especially as seen at night
light from the Moon

Examples

The garden was bathed in moonshine that filtered through the trees.
They walked along the beach, guided only by soft moonshine.
The poem describes a landscape sparkling under mystic moonshine.

Synset Examples

moonlight is the smuggler's enemy
the Moon was bright enough to read by

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moonlight - the light of the Moon, especially at night

Etymology

Formed from 'moon' + 'shine', originally referring to the shining of the moon.

2. VERB

/ ˈmuːnˌʃaɪn / (AmE), / ˈmuːnʃaɪn / (BrE)
to distill alcohol illegally; to make illicit alcoholic liquor by distillation
distill alcohol illegally

Examples

They used to moonshine in the woods during Prohibition.
Authorities arrested several people suspected of moonshining in the rural county.
He learned to moonshine from his grandfather when he was a teenager.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bootleg - to make, distribute, or sell alcohol illegally

Etymology

From the noun 'moonshine', originally referring to illegally distilled liquor made at night by moonlight.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00229284-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈmuːnˌʃaɪn / (AmE), / ˈmuːnʃaɪn / (BrE)
illegally distilled or smuggled alcohol, especially homemade whiskey
illegally produced whiskey or liquor

Examples

Local farmers were caught selling moonshine during Prohibition.
He offered me a jar of homemade moonshine at the party last night.
Strict laws were enacted to combat the illegal production of moonshine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bootleg - illegally made, distributed, or sold alcohol
  • Synonym: whiskey - a strong alcoholic drink distilled from grain mash
  • Antonym: legal - permitted by law; opposite of illegal

Etymology

From 'moon' + 'shine', referring to illicit distillation done at night to avoid detection.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07918760-n
Synset POS: n
