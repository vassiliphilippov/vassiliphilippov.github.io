Haplopappus

1. PROPN

/ hæploʊˈpæpəs / (AmE), / hapləʊˈpapəs / (BrE)
A genus of perennial herbs or shrubs native to North and South America, typically with yellow flowers; certain classifications include species from related genera such as Hazardia.
genus of yellow-flowered American herbs or shrubs

Examples

Many species within Haplopappus are adapted to arid environments in western North America.
The bright yellow flowers of Haplopappus make it a striking addition to wildflower collections.
Taxonomists have debated which species should be included in Haplopappus and related genera.

Etymology

From Greek haplo- (single) + pappos (down), referring to the simple pappus (seed hair) structure in these plants.

2. NOUN

/ ˌhæpləˈpæpəs / (AmE), / ˌhæpləˈpæpəs / (BrE)
A genus of North and South American perennial herbs or shrubs with yellow flowers, some species of which are sometimes included in other genera such as Hazardia.
genus of American herbaceous plants or shrubs

Examples

The rare Haplopappus can be found in the arid regions of Chile.
Botanists are still debating which species truly belong to the genus Haplopappus.
The bright yellow flowers of Haplopappus attract a variety of pollinators during spring.

Etymology

From Greek haplo- 'single' and pappos 'down' (referring to the pappus, or seed tuft, in these plants).
