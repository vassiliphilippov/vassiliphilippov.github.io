contravallation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːntrəvəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒntrəvəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
A line of fortifications built by attackers around a besieged place, intended to protect the besiegers from counterattacks by the defenders.
siege fortifications built by attackers

Examples

The army constructed a contravallation to protect themselves from sorties by the city's defenders.
Historical accounts detail the contravallation surrounding the fortress during the lengthy siege.
Engineers were tasked with repairing the damaged contravallation after the defenders' night raid.

Etymology

From Latin 'contravallatio', from 'contra' (against) and 'vallum' (rampart).
