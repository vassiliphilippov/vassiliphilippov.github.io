to sadden

1. VERB

/ ˈsædən / (AmE), / ˈsæd(ə)n / (BrE)
to make or become sad; to cause to feel sorrow or unhappiness
to make or become sad

Examples

The news of his departure truly saddened everyone at the office.
It always saddens me to see animals being mistreated.
He tried not to let the gloomy weather sadden his spirits.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cheer - to make or become happy or more cheerful
  • Synonym: to depress - to make someone feel extremely unhappy or discouraged

Etymology

Formed from 'sad' + '-en' (verbal suffix).

2. VERB

/ ˈsæd.ən / (AmE), / ˈsæd.ən / (BrE)
to make someone feel unhappy or sorrowful
make someone feel unhappy

Examples

It greatly saddened her to hear about the accident.
The gloomy weather always seems to sadden me.
His harsh words saddened his friends more than he realized.

Synset Examples

The news of her death saddened me

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to cheer up - make someone feel happier
  • Synonym: to depress - make someone feel deeply unhappy or dejected

Etymology

From adjective 'sad' + suffix '-en', meaning to make or become.
