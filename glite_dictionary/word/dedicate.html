to dedicate

1. VERB

/ ˈdɛdɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛdɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to inscribe or address (a book, piece of music, etc.) as a compliment or tribute to a person
inscribe or address as a tribute

Examples

The author dedicated her first novel to her grandparents, who inspired her love of reading.
He dedicated his latest painting to his mentor, acknowledging her influence.
In the preface, the composer dedicated the symphony to the memory of his beloved friend.

Synset Examples

She dedicated her book to her parents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inscribe - to write or print words on something, especially as a formal or permanent record

Etymology

From Latin dedicare (to proclaim, to consecrate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00888663-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈdɛdəˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛdɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to set apart (a building or place), especially a church, for sacred use through formal ceremony or rites
formally set apart for sacred use

Examples

The bishop will dedicate the new chapel next Sunday.
The cathedral was dedicated in an elaborate ceremony attended by hundreds.
They are preparing to dedicate the site for the new monastery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consecrate - to make or declare something sacred, especially a church
  • Synonym: to bless - to make holy or sacred by religious rite

Etymology

From Latin dedicare (to proclaim, to dedicate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00679885-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈdɛdɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛdɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to give or devote something, such as time, effort, or oneself, completely to a person, activity, or cause
devote entirely to something or someone

Examples

She decided to dedicate her life to helping others through charity work.
After the ceremony, the team dedicated their victory to the late coach.
He spends hours every day dedicating himself to perfecting his craft.

Synset Examples

She committed herself to the work of God
give one's talents to a good cause
consecrate your life to the church

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to devote - to give most or all of one's time or resources to something

Etymology

From Latin dedicare (to proclaim, to devote).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00888984-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈdɛdəˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛdɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to formally declare something, such as a building, park, or monument, open to public use, often through a ceremony
formally declare open to public use

Examples

The mayor will dedicate the new library at the ceremony tomorrow.
Every year, the city council dedicates several new community parks to the public.
They invited local officials to dedicate the bridge and mark its official opening.

Synset Examples

The Beauty Queen spends her time dedicating parks and nursing homes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inaugurate - to formally open or introduce something, especially with a ceremony

Etymology

From Latin dedicare (to proclaim, to set apart); originally ‘to proclaim’ or ‘to consecrate’.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
