Allium cepa aggregatum

1. NOUN

/ ˈæliəm ˈsiːpə ˌægrəˈɡætəm / (AmE), / ˈæliəm ˈsiːpə ˌæɡrɪˈɡætəm / (BrE)
A cultivated variety of onion that produces small, clustered, mild-flavored bulbs, often used as a seasoning in cooking.
type of onion with clustered mild bulbs

Examples

The chef finely chopped Allium cepa aggregatum to add a subtle flavor to the stew.
Gardeners often plant Allium cepa aggregatum because of its clustered bulbs and easy harvest.
Many traditional recipes call for Allium cepa aggregatum as a key seasoning ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shallot - a similar small, mild-flavored bulb used as seasoning

Etymology

From Latin: 'Allium' (the onion genus), 'cepa' (onion), and 'aggregatum' (grouped together), referring to the clustered bulbs.
