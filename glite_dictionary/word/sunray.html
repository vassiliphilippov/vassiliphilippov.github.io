sunray

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌnˌreɪ / (AmE), / ˈsʌnreɪ / (BrE)
an artificial ultraviolet ray produced by a sunray lamp, used in medical or therapeutic treatments
ultraviolet ray from a sunray lamp

Examples

The doctor recommended exposure to a sunray from a special lamp to help treat her skin condition.
Older therapies often included the application of artificial sunray to promote healing.
Excessive use of a sunray lamp can cause skin irritation or burns.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsʌnˌreɪ / (AmE), / ˈsʌn.reɪ / (BrE)
a narrow beam of sunlight
a ray of sunlight

Examples

A single sunray broke through the clouds, lighting up the field.
She watched a sunray dance across the floor in the early morning.
Tiny dust motes glimmered in the sunray streaming through the window.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sunbeam - a ray or shaft of sunlight

3. NOUN

/ ˈsʌnˌreɪ / (AmE), / ˈsʌnˌreɪ / (BrE)
A herbaceous plant with a basal cluster of grey-green leaves and leafless stalks, each bearing a solitary broad yellow flower head; found in desert areas from Idaho to Arizona.
a desert plant with yellow flower heads

Examples

The sunray thrives in the arid landscapes of the American Southwest.
Hikers were delighted to spot a blooming sunray on the trail near Arizona.
Each stalk of the sunray plant supports a single, bright yellow blossom.
