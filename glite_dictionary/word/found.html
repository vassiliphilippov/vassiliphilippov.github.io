found

1. VERB

/ faʊnd / (AmE), / faʊnd / (BrE)
to establish or set up an institution, organization, or settlement; to bring into existence and provide the basis for its existence
establish or set up (organization, etc.)

Examples

She decided to found a non-profit organization to help underprivileged children.
The university was founded in the early eighteenth century.
He is known for founding several influential companies throughout his career.

Synset Examples

She set up a literacy program

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to establish - to set up or create (something, especially an organization) on a permanent basis
  • Antonym: dissolve - to close down or put an end to (an organization or assembly)

Etymology

From Latin fundāre (to lay the bottom, establish), from fundus (bottom, foundation).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

/ faʊnd / (AmE), / faʊnd / (BrE)

Examples

4. NOUN

/ faʊnd / (AmE), / faʊnd / (BrE)
food and lodging provided to an employee in addition to wages
food and lodging as part of wages
archaic

Examples

The servants received their found as part of their employment.
She worked in the house for little money, but her found was included.
In earlier centuries, farmhands were often paid with both wages and found.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

Etymology

From Old English 'fund', past participle of 'findan' (to find), referring to what is furnished or provided.
