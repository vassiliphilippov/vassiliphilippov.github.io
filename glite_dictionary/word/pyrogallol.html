pyrogallol

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪroʊˈɡæloʊl / (AmE), / ˌpʌɪərəˈɡaləʊl / (BrE)
a toxic white crystalline phenol used in the treatment of certain skin diseases and as a photographic developer
toxic crystalline phenol compound
medical

Examples

The dermatologist prescribed a cream containing pyrogallol for the stubborn skin condition.
Old photographic processes sometimes used pyrogallol as a developing agent.
Improper handling of pyrogallol can result in toxicity or severe skin irritation.

Etymology

From pyro- (relating to fire, heat) + gallol, from 'gallic acid', as it is formed by heating gallic acid.
