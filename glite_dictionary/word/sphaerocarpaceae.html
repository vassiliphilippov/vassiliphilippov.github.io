Sphaerocarpaceae

1. NOUN

/ sfɪrˌoʊkɑrˈpeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / sfɪərəʊkɑːˈpeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of liverworts characterized by bilaterally symmetrical gametophytes, sometimes placed in the order Jungermanniales
a family of liverworts (plants)

Examples

Members of the Sphaerocarpaceae family are primarily distributed in temperate regions.
The gametophytes of Sphaerocarpaceae are notable for their bilateral symmetry.
Botanists sometimes debate the classification of Sphaerocarpaceae within the order Jungermanniales.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liverwort - non-vascular plants in the division Marchantiophyta

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'sphaira' (sphere) and '-carp' (fruit), plus the family suffix '-aceae'.

2. NOUN

/ sfɪrˌoʊkɑːrˈpeɪʃiː / (AmE), / sfɪərəʊˌkɑːˈpeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of liverworts characterized by bilaterally symmetrical gametophytes, sometimes classified within the order Jungermanniales
family of liverworts with bilateral gametophytes

Examples

The Sphaerocarpaceae are distinguished from other liverwort families by the structure of their gametophytes.
Researchers found several new species within the Sphaerocarpaceae in tropical regions.
Members of the Sphaerocarpaceae are sometimes studied to understand plant evolution.

Etymology

From New Latin Sphaerocarpaceae, based on the genus Sphaerocarpos + -aceae (a botanical family suffix).
