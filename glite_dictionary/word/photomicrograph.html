photomicrograph

1. NOUN

/ ˌfoʊtoʊˈmaɪkrəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊˈmʌɪkrəˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
A photograph of a microscopic object taken using a microscope.
photograph taken through a microscope

Examples

The scientist showed a photomicrograph of the cell's nucleus during her lecture.
This photomicrograph reveals intricate details of the butterfly's wing scales.
He studied the bacteria using a photomicrograph taken at high magnification.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: microphotograph - a photograph reduced to a very small size for viewing under a microscope

Etymology

From photo- (light) + micro- (small) + -graph (writing or recording), reflecting its meaning of recording images of small objects using light.
