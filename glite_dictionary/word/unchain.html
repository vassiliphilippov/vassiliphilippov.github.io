to unchain

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈtʃeɪn / (AmE), / ʌnˈtʃeɪn / (BrE)
to remove chains from someone or something, setting them free
remove chains from

Examples

The guard finally decided to unchain the dog after seeing it was injured.
With a swift motion, she began to unchain the prisoner from the wall.
Protesters demanded that authorities unchain the captive elephants and release them to the wild.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: chain - to fasten or secure with chains

Etymology

From the prefix un- (removal, reversal) + chain.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01291411-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ʌnˈtʃeɪn / (AmE), / ʌnˈtʃeɪn / (BrE)
to set free from chains, restraints, or confinement
set free from chains or restraint

Examples

The guard decided to unchain the prisoners and let them out for fresh air.
She worked hard to unchain her mind from fears and doubts.
After years of captivity, rescuers managed to unchain the abused dogs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: release - to let go or set free
  • Antonym: to restrain - to keep under control or limit movement

Etymology

un- (a prefix meaning 'not' or 'reverse') + chain (from Old French *chaine*, from Latin *catena*)

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01291157-v
Synset POS: v
