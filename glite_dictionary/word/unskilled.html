unskilled

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈskɪld / (AmE), / ʌnˈskɪld / (BrE)
not having or showing any special skill, ability, or training; not requiring special skill or proficiency
lacking special skill or training

Examples

The factory was looking for unskilled laborers to fill several open positions.
She felt unskilled in using the new software at work.
Most of the unskilled tasks were assigned to temporary staff.

Synset Examples

unskilled in the art of rhetoric
an enthusiastic but unskillful mountain climber
unskilled labor
workers in unskilled occupations are finding fewer and fewer job opportunities
unskilled workmanship

Related Terms

  • Antonym: skilled - having or showing skill or proficiency
  • Synonym: inexperienced - lacking experience; not knowledgeable

Etymology

Formed by prefixing 'un-' (not) to 'skilled'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02236080-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈskɪld / (AmE), / ʌnˈskɪld / (BrE)
not having or showing the necessary ability, training, or qualification to do something well.
lacking necessary skill or competence

Examples

The company hired many unskilled workers for temporary positions.
He is still unskilled at playing chess and loses often.
The task was done in an unskilled manner, resulting in many mistakes.

Synset Examples

incompetent at chess

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incompetent - not having the necessary ability or skills
  • Antonym: skilled - having the necessary ability or experience

Etymology

From the prefix 'un-' meaning 'not' + 'skilled', from Middle English 'skil' (knowledge, discernment).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01131934-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈskɪld / (AmE), / ʌnˈskɪld / (BrE)
not having or showing professional skill, training, or experience in a particular job or activity
lacking professional skill or expertise

Examples

Many unskilled workers found it hard to get jobs after the factory closed.
He was given an unskilled task that required little training.
The repairs looked unskilled and had to be redone by a professional.

Synset Examples

a very amateurish job
inexpert but conscientious efforts
an unskilled painting

Related Terms

  • Antonym: skilled - having professional skill or expertise
  • Synonym: inexperienced - not having much experience or skill in something

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'un-' (not) + 'skilled'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01876586-s
Synset POS: s
