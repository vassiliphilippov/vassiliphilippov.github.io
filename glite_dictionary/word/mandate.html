mandate

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæn.deɪt / (AmE), / ˈmæn.deɪt / (BrE)
A territory assigned by the League of Nations to be governed by a foreign power following World War I, until considered able to govern itself.
League of Nations governed territory

Examples

The British administered the Palestinian mandate from 1920 until 1948.
After World War I, Germany lost several of its colonial possessions as League of Nations mandates.
French authorities governed the Syrian mandate until its independence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protectorate - a state controlled and protected by another

Etymology

From Latin mandatum (commission, order), through French mandat.

2. VERB

/ ˈmæn.deɪt / (AmE), / ˈmæn.deɪt / (BrE)
to officially require something to be done; to make mandatory by authority or law
require officially by authority

Examples

The government mandated the use of seat belts in all vehicles.
The school board mandates annual health screenings for students.
The company is mandating new security protocols for all employees.

5. NOUN

/ ˈmænˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˈmæn.deɪt / (BrE)
the authority given to a government and its policies as the result of an electoral victory
authority granted by electoral victory

Examples

The president interpreted his victory as a clear mandate from the voters.
A narrow win may make it difficult for a government to claim a mandate for radical change.
