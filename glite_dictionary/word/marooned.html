marooned

1. ADJ

/ məˈruːnd / (AmE), / məˈruːnd / (BrE)
stranded and left behind or isolated in an inaccessible place, especially as a result of being left there intentionally or by circumstance
stranded and left behind

Examples

After the storm damaged their boat, the sailors were marooned on a small uninhabited island for days.
She felt marooned in the rural village, far from her friends and family.
The hikers became marooned when the river flooded and the bridge collapsed.

Synset Examples

an isolated pawn
several stranded fish in a tide pool
travelers marooned by the blizzard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stranded - left without a way to leave a place
  • Synonym: isolated - separated from others; alone
  • Antonym: rescued - brought to safety from danger or isolation

Etymology

From the verb "maroon," meaning to put ashore on a deserted island, probably from "maroon" (noun, fugitive slave in the Caribbean).
