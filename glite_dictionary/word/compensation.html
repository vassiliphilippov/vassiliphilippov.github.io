compensation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːmpənˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒmpənˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
In psychiatry, a defense mechanism by which a person conceals perceived shortcomings by emphasizing desirable traits or behaviors.
concealing faults by emphasizing strengths (psychiatry)
medical

Examples

His constant boasting about his achievements could be seen as compensation for feelings of inadequacy.
Therapists recognized her perfectionism as a form of compensation for her lack of confidence.
In psychology, compensation often occurs unconsciously when individuals overemphasize positive attributes.

Etymology

From Latin compensatio (a weighing one thing against another).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːm.pənˈseɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒm.pənˈseɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
money or something else given to make up for loss, injury, or services rendered
money or payment for loss or services

Examples

The company offered compensation to employees who were laid off.
He received compensation for the injuries he suffered in the accident.
Victims of the flood are seeking compensation from the government.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restitution - the act of restoring or compensating for loss or injury
  • Synonym: damages - money claimed by, or ordered to be paid to, a person as compensation for loss or injury
  • Antonym: penalty - a punishment imposed for breaking a law, rule, or contract

Etymology

From Latin compensatio (a weighing against, balancing).

3. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːmpənˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒmpənˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of giving money or other benefit in return for work, services, damage, or loss
payment or benefit for service or loss

Examples

After the accident, she received compensation for her injuries from the insurance company.
The company offered generous compensation to attract top talent.
He demanded compensation for the extra hours he had worked.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restitution - returning something lost or stolen to its owner, or paying for loss or damage
  • Synonym: remuneration - money paid for work or a service
  • Antonym: penalty - punishment for breaking a law or rule

Etymology

From Latin compensatio (a balancing), from compensare (to counterbalance).
