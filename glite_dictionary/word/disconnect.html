disconnect

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪs.kəˈnɛkt / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.kəˈnɛkt / (BrE)
to separate or detach something from something else so that it is no longer joined or connected
to detach or remove a connection

Examples

Before fixing the appliance, always disconnect it from the power supply.
She disconnected the call by mistake when she pressed the wrong button.
Please disconnect the cables from the computer before moving it.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to connect - to join or link together
  • Synonym: to detach - to remove or separate something from something else

Etymology

From dis- (expressing reversal) + connect, from Latin connectere (to bind together).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01358741-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˌdɪs.kəˈnɛkt / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.kəˈnɛkt / (BrE)
to remove the supply of electricity, gas, water, etc. from a device or system by unplugging or detaching it, making it inoperable
to remove a device from power or service

Examples

Please disconnect the toaster after breakfast to save energy.
Make sure to disconnect your laptop before moving it to another room.
The technician had to disconnect the old washing machine before installing the new one.

Synset Examples

unplug the hair dryer after using it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to unplug - to remove an electrical plug from its socket

Etymology

From Latin dis- (apart) + connectere (to bind together).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01424071-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈdɪskəˌnɛkt / (AmE), / ˈdɪskəˌnɛkt / (BrE)
a lack of understanding or agreement between people or groups; a significant difference or gap between two things
lack of understanding or agreement

Examples

There is a clear disconnect between what the company promises and what it delivers.
The disconnect between parents and teenagers can lead to frequent misunderstandings.
Many analysts see a disconnect between stock prices and the real economy.

Synset Examples

he felt a gulf between himself and his former friends
there is a vast disconnect between public opinion and federal policy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gap - a difference or disparity between two things
  • Synonym: divide - a separation or disagreement between people or groups
  • Antonym: connection - a relationship or link between people or things

Etymology

From the verb 'disconnect,' based on the prefix dis- (expressing reversal) and 'connect.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04760315-n
Synset POS: n
