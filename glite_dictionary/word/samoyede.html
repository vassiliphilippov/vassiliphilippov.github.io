Samoyede

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæməˌjɛd / (AmE), / ˈsæməʊˌjɛd / (BrE)
A breed of large, white or cream-colored dog from Siberia, belonging to the spitz family, known for its thick, fluffy coat and friendly nature.
Siberian white or cream spitz dog breed

Examples

The Samoyede is prized for its gentle temperament and striking white fur.
She's owned several Samoyedes throughout her life, all equally friendly and energetic.
Early explorers relied on the Samoyede for sledging across icy terrains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Samoyed - A breed of large, white or cream-colored spitz dog

Etymology

From Russian 'Samoyed', relating to the Samoyedic peoples of Siberia, with whom the breed was associated.
