roseola

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈziːələ / (AmE), / rəʊˈziːələ / (BrE)
a condition marked by a red rash or eruption on the skin, especially in children
red rash or skin eruption
medical

Examples

The pediatrician diagnosed the child with roseola after noticing a sudden high fever followed by a red rash.
A characteristic sign of roseola is the appearance of pinkish spots that start on the trunk and spread outward.
Although usually mild, roseola can cause concern due to the abrupt onset of fever and rash.

Etymology

From Latin 'roseolus' (rosy), diminutive of 'roseus' (rosy, pink), from 'rosa' (rose).
