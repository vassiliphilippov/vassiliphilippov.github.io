immorality

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪməˈrælɪti / (AmE), / ˌɪməˈrælɪti / (BrE)
behavior that is considered to be morally wrong or evil.
morally wrong behavior

Examples

Some people believe that gambling is a form of immorality.
The novel explores the immorality of its main characters' actions.
He was criticized for promoting immorality in his films.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: morality - principles concerning the distinction between right and wrong
  • Synonym: vice - immoral or wicked behavior

Etymology

From Latin immoralitas (wickedness), from immoralis (not moral).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪməˈræləti / (AmE), / ˌɪməˈrælɪti / (BrE)
The state or quality of not conforming to accepted standards of morality; wickedness or unethical behavior.
lack of moral standards; wickedness

Examples

Many critics condemned the film for its immorality and explicit content.
Some people argued that the new law would encourage immorality in society.
The scandal revealed the depth of immorality among those in power.

Synset Examples

the immorality of basing the defense of the West on the threat of mutual assured destruction

Related Terms

  • Antonym: morality - Principles concerning the distinction between right and wrong or good and bad behavior
  • Synonym: wickedness - The quality of being evil or morally wrong

Etymology

From Latin immoralitas, from immoralis (not moral).
