abdominocentesis

1. NOUN

/ æbˌdɑmɪnoʊsɛnˈtisɪs / (AmE), / æbˌdɒmɪnəʊsenˈtiːsɪs / (BrE)
A medical procedure in which fluid is removed from the abdomen, usually for diagnostic purposes.
removal of abdominal fluid for diagnosis
medical

Examples

The doctor performed an abdominocentesis to determine the cause of the patient's abdominal swelling.
Fluid obtained from the abdominocentesis was sent to the laboratory for analysis.
An abdominocentesis is often required when ascites is suspected in patients with liver disease.

Etymology

From Latin abdomen (belly) + Greek kentesis (pricking, puncture).
