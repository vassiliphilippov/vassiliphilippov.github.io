intellection

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.t̬əlˈɛk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈlɛk.ʃən / (BrE)
The act or process of using the mind to think, understand, or consider something carefully.
the process of thinking or understanding

Examples

Her intellection on the topic impressed everyone in the seminar.
The essay demonstrated deep intellection and careful reasoning.
He prized intellection over intuition when making difficult decisions.

Synset Examples

thinking always made him frown
she paused for thought

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thought - the process of thinking; an idea or opinion produced by thinking

Etymology

From Latin 'intellectionem', from 'intelligere' (to understand).
