Hakea lissosperma

1. NOUN

/ ˈheɪ.ki.ə lɪˌsoʊˈspɜːr.mə / (AmE), / ˈheɪ.ki.ə lɪˌsɒˈspɜː.mə / (BrE)
A shrub native to eastern Australia, with pungent, rigid, needle-shaped leaves and white flowers.
Australian shrub with rigid needle leaves and white flowers

Examples

Hakea lissosperma thrives in rocky, mountainous regions of eastern Australia.
Botanists studied the adaptations of Hakea lissosperma to harsh alpine environments.
The spiky leaves of Hakea lissosperma make it easily distinguishable from other shrubs in its habitat.

Etymology

From genus Hakea (named after Baron Christian Ludwig von Hake) and Latin 'lissosperma' (meaning smooth-seeded).
