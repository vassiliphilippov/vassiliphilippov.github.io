call-in

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔlˌɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɔːlˌɪn / (BrE)
a situation in which a person telephones a radio or television program to participate or comment while it is being broadcast
telephone participation in media program

Examples

The late-night show hosts a popular call-in segment every Friday.
Listeners can join the discussion by making a call-in during the live broadcast.
Hundreds of people participated in the call-in to share their views on the topic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phone-in - radio or TV program where listeners/viewers call in to participate
