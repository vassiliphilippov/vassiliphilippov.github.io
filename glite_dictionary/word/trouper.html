trouper

1. NOUN

/ ˈtruːpər / (AmE), / ˈtruːpə / (BrE)
a person who is dependable, hardworking, and performs their duties without complaint, especially in difficult situations
reliable and hardworking person

Examples

Even after working all weekend, she was a real trouper and finished the project with a smile.
He's a trouper—he never complains about extra work and always helps his team.
Everyone agreed that Jack had been a trouper during the stressful months of the renovation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hard worker - a person who works with diligence and effort
  • Antonym: slacker - a person who avoids work or effort

Etymology

Originally from 'troupe' (a group of performers), referring to someone who, like a stage performer, keeps going no matter what.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtruːpər / (AmE), / ˈtruːpə / (BrE)
An actor or performer, especially one who is part of a touring company and travels to perform in different places.
touring actor or performer

Examples

He spent most of his youth as a trouper, performing Shakespeare in small towns.
As a seasoned trouper, she was used to life on the road with the theater company.
The old trouper shared stories of performing in makeshift venues across the country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: actor - a person who performs in plays, movies, or television

Etymology

From 'troupe', meaning a group of performers, from French 'troupe' (company, troop).
