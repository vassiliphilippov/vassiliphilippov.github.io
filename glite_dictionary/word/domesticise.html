to domesticise

1. VERB

/ dəˈmɛstəˌsaɪz / (AmE), / dəˈmɛstɪsaɪz / (BrE)
to make (an animal, plant, or person) docile or tractable; to tame or adapt for human use or companionship
make domesticated; tame

Examples

They worked hard to domesticise the wild horses on the farm.
Early humans began to domesticise wolves, leading to the dogs we know today.
Attempts to domesticise the stubborn animal were only partly successful.

Synset Examples

He tames lions for the circus
reclaim falcons

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to domesticate - tame or adapt an animal or plant for human use
  • Synonym: tame - make an animal docile

Etymology

Formed from 'domestic' + '-ise', parallel of American English 'domesticate'.
