antagonistic

1. ADJ

/ ænˌtæɡəˈnɪstɪk / (AmE), / ænˌtæɡəˈnɪstɪk / (BrE)
showing or feeling active opposition or hostility towards someone or something.
openly hostile or opposed

Examples

The two companies have always maintained an antagonistic relationship, competing fiercely for market share.
She became increasingly antagonistic toward her critics as the debate continued.
Despite his antagonistic attitude, the negotiations eventually succeeded.

Synset Examples

his antagonistic brusqueness
Europe was antagonistic to the United States

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hostile - showing or feeling opposition or dislike; unfriendly
  • Antonym: friendly - kind and pleasant toward someone or something

Etymology

From Greek antagōnistēs (opponent), from antagōnizesthai (to struggle against).

3. ADJ

/ ænˌtæɡəˈnɪstɪk / (AmE), / ænˌtæɡəˈnɪstɪk / (BrE)
(of drugs or muscles) counteracting or neutralizing each other's effects; acting in opposition to each other.
acting in opposition (esp. drugs or muscles)
medical

Examples

The two antibiotics are antagonistic when used together, reducing each other's effectiveness.
Flexor and extensor muscles can be antagonistic in their actions on a joint.
The combination of these drugs produces an antagonistic rather than a synergistic effect.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: synergistic - (of drugs or muscles) working together to produce a combined effect

Etymology

From French antagoniste, from Greek antagonistes (opponent).
