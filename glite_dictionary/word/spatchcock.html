spatchcock

1. NOUN

/ ˈspætʃ.kɑk / (AmE), / ˈspætʃ.kɒk / (BrE)
A chicken or game bird split open along the back and grilled or roasted, usually soon after being killed.
chicken or bird grilled after splitting along the back

Examples

The chef prepared a spatchcock for tonight's special.
They enjoyed grilled spatchcock with herbs and lemon at the barbecue.
A fresh spatchcock cooks more evenly and quickly than a whole bird.

Etymology

Probably from Irish *spadóg* (a spatula or wooden tool), referring to splitting the bird.

2. VERB

/ ˈspætʃ.kɑk / (AmE), / ˈspætʃ.kɒk / (BrE)
to prepare a bird, especially poultry, by splitting it open and flattening it for roasting or grilling
split and flatten poultry for cooking

Examples

The chef decided to spatchcock the chicken before grilling it over the open flame.
You should spatchcock your turkey; it cooks faster and more evenly that way.
She has never spatchcocked a bird before, but the instructions were easy to follow.

Synset Examples

spatchcock a guinea hen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butterfly - to cut meat, fish, or poultry almost in two and spread it out flat

Etymology

From Irish spadóg (a spatula or flat piece of wood), influenced by cock (rooster).

3. VERB

/ ˈspætʃˌkɑk / (AmE), / ˈspætʃˌkɒk / (BrE)
to interpolate or insert words, especially in a way that is awkward or hasty, into a sentence or story
insert words into a text

Examples

The author decided to spatchcock a few extra jokes into the final draft of the novel.
Trying to spatchcock an explanation into his report made it sound less convincing.
She spatchcocked several unnecessary comments into the middle of the discussion.

Etymology

Originally referred to preparing poultry, later used figuratively for inserting material; possibly from 'dispatch' + 'cock' (in cookery).
