Reformation

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛfəˈrmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛfəˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
the 16th-century religious movement that aimed to reform the Roman Catholic Church and led to the establishment of Protestant churches
16th-century Christian religious movement

Examples

The Reformation had a profound impact on European culture, politics, and religion.
Many historians study Martin Luther's role in the Reformation and its long-lasting outcomes.
The Reformation led to the creation of various Protestant denominations across Europe.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Counter-Reformation - the Catholic Church's response to the Reformation

Etymology

From Latin reformare (to form again, change), via Old French reformation.
