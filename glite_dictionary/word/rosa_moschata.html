Rosa moschata

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊzə mɒsˈkɑːtə / (AmE), / ˈrəʊzə mɒsˈkɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of rose native to the Mediterranean region, noted for its curved or climbing branches and loose clusters of musky-scented white flowers.
species of musky-scented Mediterranean rose

Examples

The garden was filled with the sweet aroma of Rosa moschata in full bloom.
Botanists are studying Rosa moschata for its unique musky fragrance and adaptability.
She planted a Rosa moschata near the arbor to enjoy its climbing branches and delicate flowers.

Etymology

From Latin, combining 'rosa' (rose) and 'moschata' (musk-scented).
