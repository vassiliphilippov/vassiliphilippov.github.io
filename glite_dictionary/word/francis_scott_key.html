Francis Scott Key

1. PROPN

/ ˈfræn.sɪs skɑt ki / (AmE), / ˈfræn.sɪs skɒt kiː / (BrE)
(1779–1843) An American lawyer and poet best known for writing the poem that became the lyrics of ‘The Star-Spangled Banner’, the national anthem of the United States.
U.S. lawyer and author of US national anthem lyrics

Examples

Francis Scott Key wrote the poem that became the lyrics of the American national anthem after witnessing the Battle of Baltimore.
The legacy of Francis Scott Key lives on every time 'The Star-Spangled Banner' is sung at sports events.
A statue of Francis Scott Key stands in Baltimore to honor his historic contribution.
