Capsella

1. NOUN

/ kæpˈsɛl.ə / (AmE), / kæpˈsel.ə / (BrE)
A genus of small, herbaceous plants in the mustard family, including shepherd's purse.
genus of weeds including shepherd's purse

Examples

Capsella is a genus in the mustard family, best known for the species shepherd's purse.
Researchers studied the distribution of Capsella species in different soils.
The common weed Capsella can often be seen blooming in early spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shepherd's purse - a small weed of the mustard family with heart-shaped seed pods

Etymology

From Latin *capsella* (little box), referring to the shape of the seed capsule.

2. NOUN

/ kæpˈsɛlə / (AmE), / kæpˈsɛlə / (BrE)
a genus of small annual and biennial herbs in the mustard family, including shepherd's purse, widely naturalized as a weed in many parts of the world.
genus of herbs including shepherd's purse

Examples

Capsella is a genus of plants commonly found in temperate regions around the world.
Shepherd's purse, known scientifically as Capsella bursa-pastoris, often grows along roadsides and fields.
Botanists have classified several weedy species under the genus Capsella.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shepherd's purse - a common weed with small white flowers, member of the genus Capsella

Etymology

From Latin 'capsella' (little box), referring to the shape of the seedpod.
