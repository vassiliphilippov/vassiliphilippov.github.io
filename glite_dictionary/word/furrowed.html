furrowed

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɜroʊd / (AmE), / ˈfʌr.əʊd / (BrE)
marked with long, narrow grooves or wrinkles on the surface
marked by grooves or wrinkles

Examples

The farmer's hands were rough and furrowed from years of hard work.
She gazed at his furrowed brow, worried about what he might say next.
After the plow passed, the field lay furrowed and ready for planting.

Synset Examples

furrowed fields
his furrowed face lit by a warming smile

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrinkled - having small lines or folds in the skin or surface

Etymology

From Middle English 'furwe', from Old English 'furh' (furrow), plus suffix '-ed'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɜːroʊd / (AmE), / ˈfʌrəʊd / (BrE)
(of a surface) marked by long, narrow, shallow grooves or wrinkles.
marked by grooves or wrinkles

Examples

The old farmer had a deeply furrowed brow from years of working in the sun.
The landscape was furrowed by winding streams and narrow valleys.
She ran her fingers across the furrowed texture of the ancient tree bark.

Synset Examples

furrowed fields
his furrowed face lit by a warming smile

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface; free from projections or indentations

Etymology

From Middle English *forowed*, from Old English *furh* (furrow) + -ed.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02251550-a
Synset POS: a
