to dialyze

1. VERB

/ ˈdaɪ.əˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈdaɪ.ə.laɪz / (BrE)
to separate substances in a solution by means of dialysis, especially using a semipermeable membrane
separate by dialysis
medical

Examples

The laboratory technician will dialyze the protein solution overnight to remove small molecules.
Doctors decided to dialyze the patient’s blood to eliminate toxins.
Researchers dialyzed the sample and then tested its purity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filter - to pass liquid through a device to remove unwanted material

Etymology

From 'dialysis', based on Greek 'dialusis' (dissolution); '-ize' forms verbs indicating process.
