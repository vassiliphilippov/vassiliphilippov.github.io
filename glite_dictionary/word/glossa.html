glossa

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlɑsə / (AmE), / ˈɡlɒsə / (BrE)
the tongue, especially as a structure of muscular tissue in the mouth, often used in zoology and anatomy
the tongue (anatomical term)
medical

Examples

The insect's glossa is adapted for feeding on nectar.
Damage to the glossa can impair both taste and speech functions.
In comparative anatomy, the structure of the glossa varies significantly among vertebrates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tongue - the muscular organ in the mouth used for tasting, licking, swallowing, and speech

Etymology

From Greek glôssa (tongue, language).
