expectancy

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspɛktənsi / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɛktənsi / (BrE)
the feeling or belief that something will happen; anticipation or expectation
anticipation or expectation

Examples

There was a sense of expectancy in the air as the concert was about to begin.
Her eyes shone with expectancy as she waited for the announcement.
The team's expectancy of winning grew after their recent victories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expectation - a strong belief that something will happen

Etymology

From Latin 'exspectantia' (expectation), from 'exspectare' (to look out for).

2. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspɛktənsi / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɛktənsi / (BrE)
the state of expecting something to happen, or something that is expected, especially based on a norm or standard
the state or fact of expecting; something expected

Examples

The team felt a sense of expectancy before the start of the championship match.
There is a general expectancy that graduates will find jobs within six months.
Her face was full of expectancy as she waited for the announcement.

Synset Examples

each of them had their own anticipations
an indicator of expectancy in development

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expectation - the strong belief that something will happen or be the case
  • Antonym: surprise - an unexpected or astonishing event, fact, etc.

Etymology

From Latin 'exspectantia' (expectation), from 'exspectare' (to look out for, expect).
