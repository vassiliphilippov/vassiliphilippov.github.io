czarina

1. NOUN

/ zɑːˈriːnə / (AmE), / zɑːˈriːnə / (BrE)
the wife or widow of a czar, especially in Russia before 1917
wife or widow of a czar

Examples

The czarina was known for her influence on Russian politics during the late 19th century.
After the death of the czar, the czarina lived in seclusion for several years.
A portrait of the last czarina of Russia is displayed in the museum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: czar - an emperor of Russia before 1917
  • Synonym: empress - a woman who is the sovereign of an empire or the wife of an emperor

Etymology

From Russian цари́ца (tsarítsa), feminine form of tsar.
