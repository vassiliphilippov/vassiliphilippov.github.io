endogamy

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˈdɑːɡəmi / (AmE), / ɛnˈdɒɡəmi / (BrE)
the custom of marrying only within one's own social group, tribe, or community, as prescribed by tradition or law
marriage within one's social group

Examples

Many traditional societies have practiced endogamy to preserve cultural identity.
Religious and caste-based endogamy is still observed in some parts of the world.
The shift from endogamy to exogamy changed social structures dramatically.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: exogamy - the custom of marrying outside one's social group

Etymology

From Greek endon (within) + gamos (marriage).
