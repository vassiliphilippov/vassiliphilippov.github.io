Aloe ferox

1. NOUN

/ əˌloʊ ˈfɛr.ɑks / (AmE), / əˌləʊ ˈfɪər.ɒks / (BrE)
A species of much-branched South African aloe with reddish, prickly, succulent leaves, known for its medicinal uses.
South African aloe plant species

Examples

Aloe ferox is widely cultivated for its medicinal properties and ornamental value.
The leaves of Aloe ferox are used to produce a bitter sap known for its laxative effects.
You can find Aloe ferox growing naturally in the dry regions of South Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aloe - a genus of succulent plants including Aloe vera and Aloe ferox

Etymology

From Latin; 'aloe' from Greek 'aloē', and 'ferox' meaning 'fierce' or 'ferocious'.
