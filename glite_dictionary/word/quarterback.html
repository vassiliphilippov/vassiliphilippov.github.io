quarterback

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɔrtərˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈkwɔːtəbæk / (BrE)
(in American football) the player who directs the team's offensive plays and is positioned behind the center; typically the leader of the offense.
player who leads offense in American football
american

Examples

The team's quarterback threw three touchdown passes in the first half.
Tom Brady is considered one of the greatest quarterbacks of all time.
Without a strong quarterback, the offense struggled to score points.

Synset Examples

quarterback is the most important position on the team

Related Terms

  • Synonym: running back - player who typically carries the ball on running plays in American football
  • Synonym: wide receiver - player who catches passes from the quarterback in American football

2. NOUN

/ ˈkwɔːrdɚˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈkwɔːtəbæk / (BrE)
(in American football and Canadian football) the player who directs the team's offensive play and is usually responsible for receiving the ball from the center at the start of a play.
team position in American football
american

Examples

The team's quarterback threw three touchdown passes during the game.
Every quarterback in the league dreams of leading his team to a championship.
The new quarterback showed great leadership on the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: halfback - player position in football backfield
  • Synonym: fullback - player position in football backfield

Etymology

Formed from 'quarter', possibly as a parallel to 'fullback' and 'halfback', indicating a specific backfield position in early football formations.

3. VERB

/ ˈkwɔːrdɚˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈkwɔːtəbæk / (BrE)
to play the position of quarterback in American football, or to lead and direct a project or activity
play as or act like a quarterback
american

Examples

He was chosen to quarterback the team in Sunday's crucial game.
She skillfully quarterbacked the negotiations between the two companies.
They asked her to quarterback the marketing campaign because of her experience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lead - to guide or direct a group or activity

Etymology

From 'quarterback', originally a noun referring to a position in American football.
