boundedness

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaʊndɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈbaʊndɪdnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or condition of being limited or finite in extent or scope.
the state of being finite or limited

Examples

The boundedness of the universe is a central question in cosmology.
In mathematics, boundedness describes a set with limits on its values.
Her argument focused on the boundedness of human knowledge and perception.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finiteness - the state of having bounds or limits
  • Antonym: infinity - the state of having no limits or end

Etymology

From English 'bounded' (having bounds or limits) + '-ness' (forming nouns of quality or state).
