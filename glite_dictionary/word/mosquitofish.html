mosquitofish

1. NOUN

/ məˈskiːtoʊˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / məˈskiːtəʊˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a small, silvery freshwater fish native to North America and the West Indies, noted for its effectiveness in controlling mosquito larvae
small fish used for mosquito control

Examples

The city introduced mosquitofish into local ponds to combat mosquito populations.
A single mosquitofish can eat hundreds of mosquito larvae each day.
Mosquitofish are often found in warm, shallow waters near the edges of lakes and streams.

Etymology

Named for its role in consuming mosquito larvae.
