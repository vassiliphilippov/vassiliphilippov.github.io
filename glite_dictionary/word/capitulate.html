to capitulate

1. VERB

/ kəˈpɪtʃəˌleɪt / (AmE), / kəˈpɪtjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to cease to resist an opponent or an unwelcome demand and surrender, especially on agreed terms
surrender under agreed conditions

Examples

After months of siege, the city finally capitulated to the invading army.
The company refused to capitulate to external pressure and continued its course of action.
When faced with overwhelming evidence, he had no choice but to capitulate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surrender - give up resistance to an enemy or opponent
  • Antonym: to resist - to withstand the action or effect of something

Etymology

From Late Latin capitulatus, past participle of capitulare (to draw up in chapters), from capitulum (chapter).
