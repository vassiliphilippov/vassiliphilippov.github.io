navy

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪvi / (AmE), / ˈneɪvi / (BrE)
a dark shade of blue, typically resembling the color of a naval uniform
a dark shade of blue

Examples

She wore a beautiful navy dress to the party.
The walls of his room were painted navy for a calm, sophisticated look.
He prefers navy over black when choosing a suit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: navy blue - a dark blue color similar to that of navy uniforms

Etymology

Originally from the color of uniforms worn by the British Royal Navy.

2. NOUN

/ ˈneɪvi / (AmE), / ˈneɪvi / (BrE)
the branch of a country's armed forces that conducts military operations at sea
a country's military sea force

Examples

The country increased funding for its navy to strengthen maritime defense.
After serving in the navy for five years, he returned home with many stories.
Britain's powerful navy played a crucial role in its history as a global empire.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: army - the branch of armed forces responsible for land-based military operations
  • Antonym: air force - the branch of a country's armed services responsible for aerial warfare

Etymology

From Latin 'navis' (ship) via Old French 'navee' (fleet)
