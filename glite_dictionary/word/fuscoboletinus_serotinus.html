Fuscoboletinus serotinus

1. NOUN

/ ˌfʌskoʊboʊˈlɛtɪnəs səˈrɑtɪnəs / (AmE), / ˌfʌskəʊbəʊˈlɛtɪnəs sɪˈrɒtɪnəs / (BrE)
a species of edible fungus with a broadly convex brown cap and a whitish pore surface and stalk
a species of edible mushroom

Examples

The forest floor was dotted with Fuscoboletinus serotinus after the autumn rains.
Many mushroom foragers prize Fuscoboletinus serotinus for its mild flavor and firm texture.
Careful identification is important because Fuscoboletinus serotinus closely resembles some toxic species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bolete - any mushroom of the family Boletaceae, often with pores rather than gills

Etymology

From Latin: 'fuscus' (dark), 'Boletinus' (a genus of mushrooms), and 'serotinus' (late, referring to late fruiting).
