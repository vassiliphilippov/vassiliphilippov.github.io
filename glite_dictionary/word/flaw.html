flaw

1. NOUN

/ flɔ / (AmE), / flɔː / (BrE)
a small fault or imperfection in an object or machine that makes it less perfect or less useful
imperfection or fault in an object

Examples

There was a tiny flaw in the glass that caused it to crack.
The engineer discovered a major flaw in the design of the engine.
Even the slightest flaw can affect the performance of the device.

Synset Examples

a flaw caused the crystal to shatter
if there are any defects, you should send it back to the manufacturer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defect - a fault or imperfection that makes something less valuable
  • Synonym: blemish - a small mark or flaw that spoils the appearance of something

Etymology

From Old Norse flaga (slab, spot on the skin, crack).

2. NOUN

/ flɔ / (AmE), / flɔː / (BrE)
An imperfection or weakness in a plan, theory, or legal document that causes it to fail or makes it less effective.
imperfection in a plan, theory, or document

Examples

There was a major flaw in the argument that made the conclusion unreliable.
The contract had a legal flaw that voided its terms.
Experts pointed out a fundamental flaw in the software design.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defect - a shortcoming or fault, especially one that lessens effectiveness
  • Antonym: strength - advantage or beneficial quality of something

Etymology

From Middle English flawe (a crack), of Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian flaga (slab, irregularity).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14486754-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ flɔ / (AmE), / flɔː / (BrE)
a defect or weakness in a person's character that makes them less perfect or effective
defect or weakness in character

Examples

Despite his many achievements, everyone knew he had a flaw in his judgment.
Her only serious flaw was her quick temper, which sometimes caused problems at work.
The hero's tragic flaw ultimately led to her downfall in the story.

Synset Examples

he had his flaws, but he was great nonetheless

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fault - a character defect or shortcoming
  • Antonym: virtue - a good quality or advantage in someone's character

Etymology

From Middle English flawe (flaw in metal), of uncertain origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14487410-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ flɔ / (AmE), / flɔː / (BrE)
to make imperfect or less effective by introducing a fault or defect
make imperfect by adding a defect

Examples

The discovery of a calculation error flawed the entire experiment.
One careless remark can flaw an otherwise perfect presentation.
Rough handling during delivery flawed the surface of the table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mar - to spoil the appearance or quality of something
  • Synonym: blemish - to spoil the appearance or integrity of something
