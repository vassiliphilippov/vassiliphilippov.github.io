Karl von Clausewitz

1. PROPN

/ kɑrl fɑn ˈklaʊzəvɪts / (AmE), / kɑːl fɒn ˈklaʊzəvɪts / (BrE)
(1780–1831) A Prussian general and military theorist known for his influential writings on the philosophy and strategy of war, including the doctrine that war is an extension of politics.
Prussian general and military theorist

Examples

Many military academies require students to study the theories of Karl von Clausewitz.
Karl von Clausewitz's famous book 'On War' remains influential in strategic studies.
The phrase 'war is the continuation of politics by other means' is often attributed to Karl von Clausewitz.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Napoleon Bonaparte - French military leader and emperor (1769–1821)
