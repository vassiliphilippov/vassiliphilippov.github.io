horse chestnut

1. NOUN

/ hɔrs ˈtʃɛs.nʌt / (AmE), / hɔːs ˈtʃes.nʌt / (BrE)
the inedible, nutlike seed of the horse chestnut tree, typically glossy brown and encased in a spiky husk
inedible seed of the horse chestnut tree

Examples

Children love to collect horse chestnuts in the autumn, even though they cannot be eaten.
The glossy brown horse chestnut is often mistaken for an edible chestnut, but it's poisonous.
A spiky green husk protects each horse chestnut as it matures on the tree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conker - the seed of the horse chestnut tree used in a children's game

Etymology

From 'horse' (possibly referring to inedibility for humans) + 'chestnut' (from Old French 'chastain', Latin 'castanea').
