arteria communicans

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈtɪəriə kəˌmjunɪˈkænz / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪəriə kəˌmjuːnɪˈkanz / (BrE)
any of three arteries in the brain that contribute to the formation of the circle of Willis
artery in the brain, part of circle of Willis
medical

Examples

The arteria communicans plays a crucial role in the brain's blood supply network.
If an arteria communicans is blocked, it can affect cerebral circulation.
During the operation, the surgeon carefully identified each arteria communicans.

Etymology

From Latin 'arteria' (artery) and 'communicans' (communicating).
