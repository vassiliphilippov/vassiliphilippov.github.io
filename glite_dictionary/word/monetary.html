monetary

1. ADJ

/ ˈmʌnəˌtɛri / (AmE), / ˈmʌnɪtəri / (BrE)
relating to or involving money, especially the supply of money in an economy
relating to money or currency

Examples

The government announced new monetary policies to control inflation.
She received a monetary award for her outstanding contributions to the project.
The loss was more emotional than monetary for the family.

Synset Examples

monetary rewards
he received thanks but no pecuniary compensation for his services

Related Terms

  • Synonym: financial - relating to finance or money

Etymology

From Late Latin monetarius (of money), from Latin moneta (money).
