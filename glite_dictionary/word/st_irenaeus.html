St. Irenaeus

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ɪrəˈneɪəs / (AmE), / seɪnt ɪrəˈneɪəs / (BrE)
A Greek theologian and bishop of Lyons (circa 130–200), known for his anti-heretical writings and recognized as a Doctor of the Church.
Greek theologian and saint, Bishop of Lyons

Examples

The writings of St. Irenaeus played a key role in shaping early Christian theology.
Many scholars study the legacy of St. Irenaeus to understand the history of the early Church.
As bishop of Lyons, St. Irenaeus opposed heresies and defended orthodox doctrine.
