introuvable

1. ADJ

/ ˌẽn.truːˈvɑː.bəl / (AmE), / ˌɒ̃.truːˈvɑː.blə / (BrE)
(of a book or object) impossible to find; extremely rare or unobtainable.
impossible to find

Examples

The first edition of the novel is considered introuvable among collectors.
Some manuscripts by the author remain introuvable, despite years of searching by scholars.
For decades, that jazz recording was thought to be introuvable outside private collections.

Synset Examples

the book is practically introuvable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unobtainable - not able to be obtained or acquired
  • Degree Lower: rare - uncommon or infrequently encountered
  • Antonym: ubiquitous - present or found everywhere

Etymology

From French 'introuvable' (not able to be found), from 'in-' (not) + 'trouver' (to find).
