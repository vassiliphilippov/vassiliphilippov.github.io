ZB

1. NOUN

/ ziː biː / (AmE), / ziː biː / (BrE)
an abbreviation for zettabyte, a unit of digital information equal to 1,024 exbibytes or 2^70 bytes
abbreviation for zettabyte (1,024 exbibytes)

Examples

Global data storage is expected to exceed 40 ZB by 2025.
A single ZB contains an almost unimaginable amount of digital information.
Cloud providers now offer storage capacities measured in ZB.

Etymology

Formed from 'zetta-' (a metric prefix meaning 10^21) and 'byte'.

2. NOUN

/ zi bi / (AmE), / ziː biː / (BrE)
an abbreviation for zettabyte, a unit of information equal to 1,000 exabytes or 10^21 bytes
abbreviation for zettabyte (data size unit)

Examples

Modern data centers can store more than one ZB of information.
By 2030, global internet traffic may reach several ZB annually.
The company's archive now exceeds 2 ZB, making it one of the largest in the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zettabyte - unit of digital information equal to 1,000 exabytes or 10^21 bytes
  • Degree Lower: EB - abbreviation for exabyte, 10^18 bytes
  • Degree Higher: YB - abbreviation for yottabyte, 10^24 bytes

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'zettabyte', from the SI prefix 'zetta-' (denoting a factor of 10^21) and 'byte'.
