Zb

1. NOUN

/ ˈzɛtəˌbɪt / (AmE), / ˈzɛtəˌbɪt / (BrE)
a unit of information equal to 1,000 exabits or 10^21 bits, known as a zettabit
zettabit; 10^21 bits

Examples

Data centers may soon need to handle volumes measured in Zb as global internet use increases.
Transferring one Zb of information would require highly advanced network infrastructure.
By 2030, it's possible that global data storage could surpass one Zb.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Eb - a unit of information equal to 10^18 bits (exabit)
  • Degree Higher: Yb - a unit of information equal to 10^24 bits (yottabit)

Etymology

From 'zetta-' (prefix denoting a factor of 10^21) + 'bit' (binary digit).
