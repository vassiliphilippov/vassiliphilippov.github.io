extrasystolic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛk.strə.sɪsˈtɑ.lɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛk.strə.sɪsˈtɒ.lɪk / (BrE)
relating to or occurring during an extrasystole, an additional or premature heartbeat
relating to an extrasystole (premature heartbeat)
medical

Examples

The doctor noted several extrasystolic beats during the patient's cardiac examination.
Extrasystolic activity may sometimes be detected on an electrocardiogram even in healthy individuals.
Treatment for extrasystolic arrhythmias depends on the underlying cause and frequency of the episodes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extrasystole - a premature contraction of the heart

Etymology

From 'extrasystole' (from Latin extra 'outside' + Greek systolē 'contraction') + '-ic'.
