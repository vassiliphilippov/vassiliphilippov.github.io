formication

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɔrmɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɔːmɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a sensation as if insects or small creatures are crawling on or under the skin, often with no physical cause
sensation of crawling on the skin
medical

Examples

The patient reported frequent episodes of formication, describing the sensation of bugs crawling on her arms.
Chronic amphetamine use can lead to hallucinations and formication.
Doctors recognized formication as a possible withdrawal symptom in certain cases.

Etymology

From Latin 'formica' (ant), referring to the crawling feeling.
