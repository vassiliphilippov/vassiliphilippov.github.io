frost-weed

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɑstˌwid / (AmE), / ˈfrɒstˌwiːd / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to the eastern United States, known for producing ice crystals on its stems during the first frosts and bearing early yellow flowers.
perennial plant; produces ice crystals in frost

Examples

In late autumn, the frost-weed revealed delicate ribbons of ice curled around its stems.
Wildlife photographers often search for frost-weed to capture the magical ice formations it creates.
Although its yellow flowers bloom early in the season, the frost-weed is most famous for its frosty appearance.

Etymology

From 'frost' and 'weed,' referring to the plant's tendency to form frost-like ice crystals on its stems.
