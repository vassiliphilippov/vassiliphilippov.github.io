Virgin

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɝːdʒən / (AmE), / ˈvɜːdʒɪn / (BrE)
the sixth sign of the zodiac, also known as Virgo, occurring when the sun is in this sign from about August 23 to September 22
the sixth sign of the zodiac (Virgo)
archaic

Examples

The sun enters the Virgin around the end of August each year.
Astrologers say those born under the Virgin are analytical and practical.
She always reads her horoscope for the Virgin at the start of September.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Virgo - the sixth sign of the zodiac, covering August 23–September 22

Etymology

From Latin 'virgo' meaning 'maiden'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɝːdʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈvɜːdʒɪn / (BrE)
(astrology) A person born while the sun is in the astrological sign Virgo, typically considered to fall between August 23 and September 22.
person born under Virgo (astrology)

Examples

My sister is a Virgin, so she reads her horoscope every morning.
At the party, she mentioned she was a Virgin and loved analyzing things.
Many Virgins are said to be practical and detail-oriented according to astrology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Virgo - The astrological sign representing the Virgin

Etymology

From Latin 'virgo' (maiden, virgin); used in astrology to name the zodiac sign.
