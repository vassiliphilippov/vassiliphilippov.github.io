virgin

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɝːdʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈvɜːdʒɪn / (BrE)
used to describe a material or substance that has not been used, worked, or processed before; new and original.
unprocessed; used for the first time

Examples

The company only manufactures clothing from virgin wool, ensuring high quality.
Researchers collected samples from virgin forest that had never been logged before.
The oil is extracted from virgin olives for a pure, superior flavor.

Synset Examples

virgin wool

Related Terms

  • Antonym: used - had already been put to use; not new

Etymology

From Latin 'virgo' (maiden, virgin), transferred sense to materials meaning untouched or unused.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɝːdʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈvɜːdʒɪn / (BrE)
a person, typically a woman, who has never had sexual intercourse
person who has never had sex

Examples

She remained a virgin throughout her life, dedicating herself to her work and family.
The story centers on a young virgin facing social pressures in her community.
He openly discussed his experiences as a virgin during the interview.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maiden - an unmarried girl or young woman, often used as a synonym for a virgin
  • Antonym: nonvirgin - a person who has had sexual intercourse, opposite of virgin

Etymology

From Latin 'virgo' (maiden, young woman).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10774925-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈvɝːdʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈvɜːdʒɪn / (BrE)
(of a person, especially a woman) not having had sexual intercourse.
not having had sexual intercourse

Examples

She remained a virgin into her thirties, by choice.
The legendary princess was described as a pure and virgin maiden.
The community held a celebration for girls deemed virgin by the elders.

Synset Examples

pure and vestal modesty
a spinster or virgin lady
men have decreed that their women must be pure and virginal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chaste - refraining from sexual intercourse or abstaining from all sexual activity

Etymology

From Latin virgo, virginis (maiden, young woman).
