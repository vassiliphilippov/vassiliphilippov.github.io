collaboration

1. NOUN

/ kəˌlæbəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / kəˌlæbəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of cooperating with an enemy occupying your country, especially in a way that is considered treasonous
traitorous cooperation with an occupying enemy

Examples

During the occupation, his collaboration with the enemy regime led to public outrage after the war.
Accusations of collaboration destroyed the reputations of several officials once liberation was achieved.
Trials for wartime collaboration were held to bring traitors to justice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treason - the crime of betraying one's country
  • Synonym: collaborator - a person who cooperates traitorously with an occupying enemy

Etymology

From Latin collaborare (to work together), but the sense of traitorous cooperation became prominent during World War II.

2. NOUN

/ kəˌlæbəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / kəˌlabəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of working with one or more people or groups to achieve a common goal
joint work or cooperation

Examples

The team achieved success through close collaboration on the project.
Scientific collaboration across borders has led to major discoveries.
There was a spirit of collaboration between the artists during the performance.

Synset Examples

they worked either in collaboration or independently

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cooperation - working together for a common purpose
  • Antonym: competition - the act of competing or striving against others

Etymology

From Latin collaboratio, from collaborare (to work with).
