gnathopod

1. NOUN

/ ˈnæθəˌpɑd / (AmE), / ˈnæθəˌpɒd / (BrE)
an appendage on the thorax of certain crustaceans, located just behind the maxillae, adapted for feeding
feeding appendage of some crustaceans
medical

Examples

The amphipod's gnathopods are specialized to grasp and handle food.
Scientists studied the gnathopods to understand their role in the crab's feeding behavior.
In some species, the size of the gnathopod differs between males and females.

Synset Examples

An appendage which is modified to assist in feeding is known as a maxilliped or gnathopod.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maxilliped - an appendage modified to assist in feeding, found in some arthropods

Etymology

From Greek 'gnathos' (jaw) + 'pous, podos' (foot).
