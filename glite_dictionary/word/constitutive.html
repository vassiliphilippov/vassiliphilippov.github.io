constitutive

1. ADJ

/ kənˈstɪtʃuˌtɪv / (AmE), / kənˈstɪtjuːtɪv / (BrE)
forming an essential part or element of something; having the power to establish or give existence to something.
forming an essential part or element

Examples

Genetic factors are constitutive in determining eye color.
A constitutive element of the theory is its emphasis on cooperation.
Water is constitutive of all living organisms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constitutional - relating to the structure or composition of something

Etymology

From Latin 'constitutivus', from 'constituere' (to establish).
