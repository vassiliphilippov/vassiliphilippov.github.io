rephrasing

1. NOUN

/ riˈfreɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / riˈfreɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of expressing something in a different way or using different words, especially to clarify or alter its meaning
expressing something differently

Examples

She suggested a rephrasing of the sentence to make it easier to understand.
After some rephrasing, the question became much clearer to the students.
Your report could benefit from a rephrasing of its main arguments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paraphrase - a restatement of a text or passage in other words

Etymology

From 'rephrase' (verb: to phrase again) + -ing, originally from the prefix 're-' (again) and 'phrase' (from French 'phraser')
