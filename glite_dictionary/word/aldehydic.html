aldehydic

1. ADJ

/ ˌældəˈhaɪdɪk / (AmE), / ˌældɪˈhʌɪdɪk / (BrE)
relating to, characteristic of, or containing aldehydes (a class of organic compounds)
relating to aldehydes

Examples

The aldehydic group is responsible for the compound's unique aroma.
This reaction produces an aldehydic intermediate before the final product forms.
Several essential oils have subtle aldehydic notes that enhance their fragrance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aldehyde - an organic compound containing a formyl group

Etymology

Formed from 'aldehyde' and the suffix '-ic', indicating relation or pertinence.
