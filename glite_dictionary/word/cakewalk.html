cakewalk

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪkˌwɑk / (AmE), / ˈkeɪkˌwɔːk / (BrE)
an extremely easy task or achievement
something very easy to do

Examples

For someone as experienced as her, the project was a total cakewalk.
They thought the exam would be a cakewalk, but it turned out to be quite challenging.
Securing the contract turned out to be a cakewalk thanks to our preparation.

Synset Examples

winning the tournament was a cakewalk for him
invading Iraq won't be a cakewalk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walkover - an easy victory or task
  • Synonym: pushover - something easily done or someone easily influenced
  • Antonym: struggle - something that is difficult to accomplish

Etymology

Originally a 19th-century dance contest in the southern United States, where the winner often received a cake as a prize.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪkˌwɔk / (AmE), / ˈkeɪkˌwɔːk / (BrE)
a lively, strutting dance developed in the southern United States, originally performed by Black dancers in competitions to win a cake, and later popularized in minstrel shows
strutting dance for a cake prize

Examples

The performers delighted the audience with an energetic cakewalk during the final act.
In the late 1800s, the cakewalk became a popular feature of minstrel shows.
Their cakewalk costumes were flamboyant, reflecting the dance’s origins in showmanship and competition.

Etymology

From the practice of awarding a cake as a prize in Black dance competitions in the southern US, 19th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00531911-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈkeɪkˌwɔk / (AmE), / ˈkeɪkˌwɔːk / (BrE)
to perform the cakewalk dance, a style characterized by high steps and strutting, originating in African American communities in the 19th century
perform the cakewalk dance

Examples

The performers delighted the audience as they cakewalked across the stage in elaborate costumes.
During the festival, children enthusiastically cakewalk to the lively tune played by the band.
In the old photograph, couples cakewalked with exaggerated elegance and comic grace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dance - to move rhythmically to music, typically following a set sequence of steps

Etymology

From the noun 'cakewalk', originally a dance contest in which the prize was a cake, popular in 19th century African American communities.
