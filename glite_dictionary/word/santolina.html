Santolina

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæn.təˈliː.nə / (AmE), / ˌsan.təˈliː.nə / (BrE)
A genus of aromatic Mediterranean subshrubs of the daisy family, characterized by rayless yellow flower heads, often grown as ornamentals.
genus of Mediterranean aromatic shrubs

Examples

The gardener planted Santolina along the borders to add texture to the landscape.
Santolina is valued for its silver-green foliage and aromatic scent.
Many Mediterranean gardens feature Santolina for its drought resistance and decorative appeal.

Etymology

From Latin Santolina, name of the plant genus.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsæn.təˈliː.nə / (AmE), / ˌsan.təˈliː.nə / (BrE)
A genus of Mediterranean subshrubs known for their aromatic foliage and rayless yellow flower heads, often used in gardens or for medicinal purposes.
genus of Mediterranean aromatic subshrubs

Examples

The gardener planted Santolina along the border to add texture and a pleasant scent.
Many Santolina varieties require well-drained soil and plenty of sunlight to thrive.
Dried Santolina leaves are sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.

Etymology

From Latin 'Santolina', derived from late Latin names, possibly relating to the plant's supposed medicinal uses.
