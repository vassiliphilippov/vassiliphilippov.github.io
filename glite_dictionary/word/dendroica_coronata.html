Dendroica coronata

1. NOUN

/ dɛnˈdroɪ.ɪkə kɔːˈroʊnətə / (AmE), / dɛnˈdrɔɪ.ɪkə kɒˈrəʊnətə / (BrE)
A North American songbird species known as the yellow-rumped warbler, notable for its yellow rump and variable plumage.
yellow-rumped warbler (bird species)

Examples

Every spring, birdwatchers eagerly search for the Dendroica coronata as it migrates north.
Recent studies confirmed that Dendroica coronata can thrive in both coniferous and deciduous forests.
The distinctive yellow patch makes Dendroica coronata easy to identify among other warblers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yellow-rumped warbler - Common name for Dendroica coronata, a North American songbird with distinctive yellow markings

Etymology

From New Latin, with 'Dendroica' from Greek dendron (tree) and oikos (house); 'coronata' from Latin corōnātus (crowned).
