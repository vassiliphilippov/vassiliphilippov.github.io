to colourise

1. VERB

/ ˈkʌlərˌaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkʌləraɪz / (BrE)
to add colour to something, especially a black-and-white photograph or film
add colour, especially to black-and-white images
british

Examples

Technology now allows us to colourise many classic black-and-white movies.
She spent weeks carefully colourising the family’s old photographs.
Documentaries often colourise historical footage to make the past feel more immediate.

Synset Examples

The child colored the drawings
Fall colored the trees
colorize black and white film

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to colorize - add color to something, especially a black-and-white film or image
  • Antonym: desaturate - remove color from an image, making it black-and-white

Etymology

Formed from 'colour' + '-ise', modeled after 'colorize' in American English.
