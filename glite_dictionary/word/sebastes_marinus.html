Sebastes marinus

1. NOUN

/ səˈbæstiz məˈraɪnəs / (AmE), / sɪˈbɑːstiːz məˈraɪnəs / (BrE)
a large marine fish of the North Atlantic, also called Atlantic redfish or ocean perch, found off the coasts of America and Europe
large North Atlantic marine fish; ocean perch

Examples

Fishermen caught several Sebastes marinus during their expedition in the North Atlantic.
The flesh of Sebastes marinus is often used for making fillets and is popular in European cuisine.
Scientists are studying the population decline of Sebastes marinus along European coasts.

Etymology

From Latin, with Sebastes from Greek sebastos (august) and marinus meaning 'of the sea'.
