phentolamine

1. NOUN

/ fɛnˈtɑləˌmeɪn / (AmE), / fɛnˈtɒləmeɪn / (BrE)
A drug used to treat erectile dysfunction in men, marketed under the trade name Vasomax.
drug for erectile dysfunction
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed phentolamine as part of his treatment for erectile dysfunction.
After taking phentolamine, he experienced improved blood flow as intended.
Some men may be given phentolamine when other treatments for impotence are ineffective.

Etymology

From pheno- (referring to the phenyl group) + toluidine + amine.
