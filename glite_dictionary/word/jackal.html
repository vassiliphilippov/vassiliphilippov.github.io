jackal

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒækəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒækəl / (BrE)
A wild dog-like animal of Africa and southern Asia, known for its slender build, pointed ears, and nocturnal scavenging habits.
wild dog-like animal of Africa and Asia

Examples

The jackal is known for its wily nature and distinctive howl at dusk.
A group of jackals scavenged near the edge of the village, searching for leftovers.
Unlike wolves, the jackal prefers to hunt alone or in pairs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wolf - large wild member of the dog family, related to jackals
  • Synonym: fox - smaller member of the dog family, with a bushy tail

Etymology

From Turkish *çakal*, from Persian *shaghāl*, from Sanskrit *śṛgālá*.

Related Terms

3. NOUN

/ ˈdʒæk.əl / (AmE), / ˈdʒæk.əl / (BrE)
A wild mammal of the dog family found in Africa and Asia, smaller than a wolf, usually hunting alone or in pairs.
wild dog-like mammal of Africa and Asia

Examples

A lone jackal prowled the edge of the village at dusk.
The jackal is known for its sharp senses and adaptability to survive in harsh environments.
Although usually solitary, a jackal may sometimes be seen hunting with a mate.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: wolf - a large wild carnivorous mammal of the dog family
  • Degree Lower: fox - a small to medium-sized member of the dog family with a bushy tail

Etymology

From Turkish 'çakal', via Persian 'shaghāl', from Sanskrit 'śṛgālaḥ' (jackal).

/ ˈdʒækəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒæk.əl / (BrE)

Examples

The black-backed jackal is commonly seen in southern African savannas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fox - a small, wild canine with a bushy tail

a wild dog native to Europe, Asia, and Africa

Examples

A jackal was spotted near the edge of the village last night.
Many species of jackal are scavengers, helping to keep the environment clean.

Related Terms
