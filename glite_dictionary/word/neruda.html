Neruda

1. PROPN

/ nəˈruːdə / (AmE), / nɛˈruːdə / (BrE)
(1904–1973) A Chilean poet and diplomat, renowned for his passionate love poems and political verse; Nobel Prize in Literature winner in 1971.
Chilean poet and Nobel laureate

Examples

Neruda is widely considered one of the most influential poets of the 20th century.
The poems of Neruda have been translated into dozens of languages.
Many scholars have studied Neruda's political activism as well as his literary work.
