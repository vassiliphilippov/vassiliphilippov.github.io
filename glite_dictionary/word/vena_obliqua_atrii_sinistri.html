vena obliqua atrii sinistri

1. NOUN

/ ˌveɪnə əˈbliːkwə ˈeɪtraɪ ˈsɪnɪstraɪ / (AmE), / ˌviːnə ɒˈblɪkwə ˈeɪtraɪ ˈsɪnɪstriː / (BrE)
A small vein on the posterior wall of the left atrium that drains into the coronary sinus.
vein on the left atrium draining to coronary sinus
medical

Examples

During the surgery, the vena obliqua atrii sinistri was carefully identified and preserved.
A thrombosis in the vena obliqua atrii sinistri is extremely rare and seldom reported.
The vena obliqua atrii sinistri is often studied in relation to congenital heart conditions.

Etymology

From Latin: 'vena' (vein), 'obliqua' (oblique), 'atrii sinistri' (of the left atrium).
