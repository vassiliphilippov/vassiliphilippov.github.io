copaiba

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈpaɪbə / (AmE), / kəˈpaɪbə / (BrE)
An oleoresin obtained from trees of the genus Copaifera, used in varnishes, ointments, and sometimes medicine.
an oleoresin from Copaifera used in varnishes and ointments

Examples

The craftsman applied a layer of copaiba to seal the wood.
Herbalists sometimes use copaiba in topical ointments to treat skin irritations.
The copaiba resin is harvested from South American trees.

Etymology

From Spanish 'copaiba', from Portuguese 'copaíba', ultimately from Tupi 'kupa'iwa'.
