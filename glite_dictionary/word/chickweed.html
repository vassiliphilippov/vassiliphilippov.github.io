chickweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪkˌwid / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪkwiːd / (BrE)
a small, low-growing plant of the genus Stellaria, with small white flowers, often found as a weed in gardens and fields
a low-growing plant, often a weed

Examples

Gardeners often struggle to remove chickweed from flower beds in the spring.
Many birds eat chickweed seeds as part of their diet.
The herbalist recommended chickweed for soothing minor skin irritations.

Etymology

From 'chick' + 'weed', named because it was often fed to poultry.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪkˌwid / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪk.wiːd / (BrE)
a small plant of the genus Stellaria, often found as a common weed in gardens and fields
a common small plant (Stellaria) often considered a weed

Examples

The garden was overrun with chickweed after the spring rains.
Many birds, especially chickens, are known to eat chickweed.
She pulled a handful of chickweed from between the paving stones.
