Agastache nepetoides

1. NOUN

/ əˌgæˈstækɪ nɛpəˈtɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / əˌɡæˈstæki nɛpɪˈtɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A species of erect perennial plant with stout stems and yellow-green flowers, native to southern Canada and the southeastern United States; also known as yellow giant hyssop.
a plant species with yellow-green flowers; yellow giant hyssop

Examples

The tall stalks of Agastache nepetoides attract bees and butterflies in the summer garden.
We spotted Agastache nepetoides growing wild along the woodland edge.
Gardeners often plant Agastache nepetoides for its unique, yellow-green flower spikes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yellow giant hyssop - common name for Agastache nepetoides, a plant with yellow-green flowers

Etymology

From the genus name 'Agastache' (from Greek 'aga' meaning much, and 'stachys' meaning ear of grain, referring to the flower spikes) and the species epithet 'nepetoides' (meaning 'resembling Nepeta', another plant genus).
