to uncork

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈkɔrk / (AmE), / ʌnˈkɔːk / (BrE)
to remove the cork from a bottle, especially a wine bottle
remove the cork from a bottle

Examples

He decided to uncork a bottle of champagne to celebrate the occasion.
The waiter expertly uncorked the vintage red wine at our table.
She uncorked the bottle and poured everyone a glass.

Synset Examples

uncork the French wine

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cork - to close with a cork; to stop up a bottle with a cork

Etymology

From un- (not, remove) + cork (a plug made of cork).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01426447-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ʌnˈkɔrk / (AmE), / ʌnˈkɔːk / (BrE)
to release or let out, especially something previously restrained, such as feelings or emotions
release or let out (figurative)

Examples

He tried not to uncork his frustration during the meeting, but eventually he spoke up.
The coach uncorked a passionate speech that inspired the whole team.
Sometimes, a good laugh uncorks all the tension you've been holding inside.

Synset Examples

uncork his anger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: release - to set free or allow to escape or move freely

Etymology

From un- (prefix meaning 'not, opposite of') + cork (from Spanish corcho, meaning 'bark of the cork oak').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01478367-v
Synset POS: v
