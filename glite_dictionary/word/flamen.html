flamen

1. NOUN

/ ˈfleɪ.mən / (AmE), / ˈfleɪ.mɛn / (BrE)
a priest in ancient Rome assigned to serve a particular deity, performing ritual duties and ceremonies
ancient Roman priest of a specific deity

Examples

The flamen led the sacrificial ceremony at the temple of Jupiter.
Each major Roman god had a dedicated flamen to oversee their rituals.
During the festival, the flamen wore distinctive ceremonial attire to distinguish himself from other priests.

Etymology

From Latin 'flamen', of uncertain origin, referring to a priest assigned to a specific god.
