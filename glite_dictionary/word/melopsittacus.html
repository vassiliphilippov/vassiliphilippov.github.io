Melopsittacus

1. NOUN

/ ˌmiːləpˈsɪtəkəs / (AmE), / ˌmiːləpˈsɪtəkəs / (BrE)
A genus of parrots in the family Psittacidae, containing the budgerigar (commonly kept as a pet parakeet).
genus of parrots; includes the budgerigar

Examples

The genus Melopsittacus contains only one living species, the familiar budgerigar.
Researchers studied vocal learning in Melopsittacus as part of their project on parrot intelligence.
As a pet owner, Jane was fascinated by the behavior unique to Melopsittacus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: budgerigar - small Australian parrot often kept as a pet

Etymology

From Greek ‘melos’ (song) + ‘psittakos’ (parrot).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛloʊˌsɪˈtækəs / (AmE), / ˌmɛləʊsɪˈtækəs / (BrE)
A genus of parrots that includes the budgerigar, an Australian parakeet popular as a pet.
genus of parrots including the budgerigar

Examples

The Melopsittacus genus contains only the budgerigar, a favorite pet bird worldwide.
Researchers studied the plumage patterns characteristic of Melopsittacus species.
The natural habitat of Melopsittacus is the open grasslands of Australia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: budgerigar - a small Australian parakeet of the species Melopsittacus undulatus

Etymology

From Greek 'melos' (song) + 'psittakos' (parrot).
