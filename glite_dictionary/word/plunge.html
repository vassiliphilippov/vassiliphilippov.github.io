plunge

1. NOUN

/ plʌndʒ / (AmE), / plʌndʒ / (BrE)
a brief swim or immersion in water, especially for refreshment or as a sudden action
brief swim or immersion in water

Examples

After a long hike, she enjoyed a refreshing plunge in the lake.
He took a quick plunge into the ocean before breakfast.
The cold weather didn't stop them from having a morning plunge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dip - a brief immersion in liquid, especially water
Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ plʌndʒ / (AmE), / plʌndʒ / (BrE)
to begin or engage in an activity suddenly and energetically, often with little hesitation or preparation
begin something suddenly or energetically

Examples

She decided to plunge into her new job without any hesitation.
After months of planning, they plunged into the project with great enthusiasm.
Sometimes it's best to plunge into a challenge rather than overthink your approach.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: launch - to start or set in motion with force or enthusiasm
  • Antonym: to hesitate - to pause before beginning something, often due to uncertainty

Etymology

From Late Latin *plumbicare* (to heave a lead), from Latin *plumbum* (lead).
