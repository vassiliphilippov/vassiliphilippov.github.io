soupfin

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuːpˌfɪn / (AmE), / ˈsuːpˌfɪn / (BrE)
A species of Pacific shark valued for its fins and vitamin A-rich liver, often used in Chinese cuisine.
a Pacific shark prized for fins and liver

Examples

The soupfin is commonly found off the California coast.
Demand for soupfin fins has led to strict fishing regulations.
The liver oil from a soupfin is exceptionally high in vitamin A.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shark - a large fish with a cartilaginous skeleton and sharp teeth
