draft

1. NOUN

/ dræft / (AmE), / drɑːft / (BrE)
a device in a fireplace, stove, or furnace used to control the flow of air into the fire
airflow regulator in a fireplace or stove

Examples

Adjust the draft to let more air reach the fire.
The old stove’s draft was stuck open, causing too much smoke.
If you forget to close the draft, the room will quickly fill with cold air.

2. NOUN

/ dræft / (AmE), / drɑːft / (BrE)
A system whereby people are required by law to serve in the armed forces; compulsory military service.
compulsory military service system
american

Examples

He was called up in the draft during the Vietnam War.
Some countries have abolished the draft and rely on volunteer soldiers.
Many young men tried to avoid the draft in the 1960s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conscription - the practice of ordering people by law to serve in the armed forces

Etymology

From Middle English 'draught', from Old English 'dræht', meaning the act of drawing or pulling; used since the 19th century in the military context.

/ dræft / (AmE), / drɑːft / (BrE)

Examples

From Old English *dreaft* (drinking), related to 'draw'. Nautical sense developed from the idea of drawing water.
