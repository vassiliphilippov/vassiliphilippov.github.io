Anas crecca

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəs ˈkrɛkə / (AmE), / ˌænəs ˈkrɛkə / (BrE)
A species of small dabbling duck, commonly known as the common teal, native to Eurasia and North America.
species of small teal duck

Examples

During migration season, birdwatchers often spot the Anas crecca along shallow lakes and marshes.
The Anas crecca is noted for its distinctive green eye patch and nimble flight.
Unlike many ducks, the Anas crecca forages in shallow waters for seeds and aquatic insects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teal - a small freshwater duck of the genus Anas

Etymology

From Latin: 'Anas' meaning duck and 'crecca,' a medieval Latin name for the teal.
