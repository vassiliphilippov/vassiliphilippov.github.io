sarsaparilla

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɑːrspəˈrɪlə / (AmE), / ˌsæspəˈrɪlə / (BrE)
Any of various prickly climbing plants of tropical America in the genus Smilax, having aromatic roots and heart-shaped leaves, sometimes used in traditional medicines and soft drinks.
prickly tropical climbing plant with aromatic roots

Examples

The herbalist recommended sarsaparilla for its traditional healing properties.
Many old-fashioned sodas were flavored with sarsaparilla root.
Sarsaparilla vines can often be found growing in tropical forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Smilax - genus of climbing plants related to sarsaparilla

Etymology

From Spanish 'zarzaparrilla', meaning 'bramble' (zarza) and 'little grapevine' (parrilla).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsæs.pəˈrɪl.ə / (AmE), / ˌsɑː.spəˈrɪl.ə / (BrE)
a sweet, carbonated soft drink traditionally flavored with extract from sarsaparilla root, or sometimes with birch oil and sassafras.
a sweet, root-flavored soft drink

Examples

He ordered a cold sarsaparilla at the old-fashioned soda fountain.
Some people say that sarsaparilla tastes similar to root beer but milder and less sweet.
The company still bottles their original sarsaparilla recipe from 1895.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: root beer - a sweet North American soft drink flavored with roots such as sassafras

Etymology

From Spanish 'zarzaparrilla', a diminutive of 'zarzaparra' (bramble).
