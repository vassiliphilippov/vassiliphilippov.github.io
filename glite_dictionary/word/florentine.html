Florentine

1. ADJ

/ ˈflɔːr.ənˌtin / (AmE), / ˈflɒr.ənˌtiːn / (BrE)
of or relating to Florence, Italy, or its people, culture, or art
from or related to Florence, Italy

Examples

The museum is famous for its collection of Florentine paintings.
She developed a taste for Florentine cuisine after visiting Italy.
The Florentine dialect influenced the Italian language as a whole.

Synset Examples

Florentine art

Etymology

From Latin Florentinus (of Florence), from Florence (Florentia), Italy.

2. NOUN

/ ˈflɔːrənˌtin / (AmE), / ˈflɒr.ənˌtiːn / (BrE)
a native or resident of Florence, Italy.
native or resident of Florence, Italy

Examples

Several famous artists were Florentine by birth or training.
As a proud Florentine, she celebrates the city's history every year.
The Florentines are known for their appreciation of art and architecture.

Etymology

From Latin Florentinus, meaning 'of or pertaining to Florence'.
