overweening

1. ADJ

/ ˌoʊvɚˈwiniŋ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈwiːnɪŋ / (BrE)
Having an excessively high opinion of oneself; arrogantly presumptuous or conceited.
excessively arrogant or presumptuous

Examples

His overweening pride made him unpopular among his colleagues.
She approached the task with overweening confidence, ignoring everyone's advice.
The company's overweening ambition eventually led to its downfall.

Synset Examples

had a witty but overweening manner
no idea how overweening he would be
getting a little uppity and needed to be slapped down

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrogant - having an exaggerated sense of one's own importance or abilities
  • Antonym: humble - having a modest or low view of one's own importance

Etymology

From Middle English ‘overwening’, from ‘over’ + ‘ween’ (to think, suppose).

2. ADJ

/ ˌoʊvərˈwinɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈwiːnɪŋ / (BrE)
Excessive or unrestrained, especially in terms of pride, ambition, or feelings.
excessive; unrestrained (pride, ambition, etc.)

Examples

His overweening ambition eventually led to his downfall.
The committee criticized her overweening pride in her achievements.
The manager's overweening confidence made him ignore valuable advice from his team.

Synset Examples

extravagant praise
exuberant compliments
overweening ambition
overweening greed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excessive - too much; more than is reasonable
  • Antonym: restrained - controlled; limited in degree or extent

Etymology

From Middle English 'overwening', from 'over' + 'ween' (to think, suppose).
