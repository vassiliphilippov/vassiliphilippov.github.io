bloodhound

1. NOUN

/ ˈblʌdˌhaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈblʌdˌhaʊnd / (BrE)
a large breed of dog of European origin, known for its keen sense of smell and often used for tracking.
breed of dog with keen sense of smell

Examples

The police used a bloodhound to follow the scent trail left by the missing child.
With its nose to the ground, the bloodhound quickly picked up the track of the fugitive.
She adopted a rescue bloodhound because of its gentle temperament and impressive tracking skills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hound - a type of hunting dog

Etymology

From 'blood' + 'hound', indicating pure-blooded lineage or tracking wounded game.
