serried

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛrid / (AmE), / ˈsɛrid / (BrE)
(especially of rows or objects) standing closely packed together, with little or no space between them.
closely packed together

Examples

The soldiers stood in serried ranks, awaiting inspection.
The serried trees formed an impenetrable barrier in the forest.
Fans gathered in serried rows at the front of the stage, eager for the concert to begin.

Synset Examples

in serried ranks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dense - closely compacted in substance
  • Antonym: spaced - placed with space between

Etymology

From Latin *serere* (to join, intertwine) via Old French *serrer* (to press closely).
