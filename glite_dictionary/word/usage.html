usage

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuːsɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈjuːsɪdʒ / (BrE)
the accepted or customary way in which something is done or used; habitual practice
accepted or habitual practice

Examples

The usage of cell phones in class is strictly prohibited.
Old English usage differs greatly from modern conventions.
It has become common usage to greet people with a handshake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: custom - a traditional and widely accepted way of behaving or doing something
  • Synonym: practice - the actual application or use of an idea, belief, or method
  • Antonym: innovation - the introduction of new ideas or methods, contrasting with established usage

Etymology

From Old French 'us/usage', from Latin 'usus' meaning 'use'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈjuːsɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈjuːsɪdʒ / (BrE)
the act or manner of using something; the way in which something is used
the act or manner of using

Examples

Recent data show that smartphone usage among teenagers has increased significantly in the past decade.
Limiting water usage is essential during drought conditions to conserve resources.
The software’s usage varies depending on the user’s needs and level of expertise.

Synset Examples

he warned against the use of narcotic drugs
skilled in the utilization of computers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: use - the act of using something
  • Synonym: utilization - the action of making practical use of something

Etymology

From Old French 'us, usage', from Latin 'usus' (use).

3. NOUN

/ ˈjuːsɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈjuːsɪdʒ / (BrE)
the way in which words or phrases are actually used in speech or writing, especially as accepted by a particular group or in a particular language
customary way language is used

Examples

The dictionary provides clear explanations of English usage.
Certain usage rules are different in American and British English.
He is interested in historical changes in word usage.

Synset Examples

English usage
a usage borrowed from French

Related Terms

  • Synonym: custom - a usual way of behaving or doing something

Etymology

From Old French *us, usage* (custom, practice), from Late Latin *usagium* (the act of using).
