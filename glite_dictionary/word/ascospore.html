ascospore

1. NOUN

/ ˈæs.kəˌspɔr / (AmE), / ˈæs.kə.spɔː / (BrE)
A sexually produced spore formed inside an ascus, typically found in certain fungi.
sexually produced fungal spore
medical

Examples

Each ascospore develops inside an ascus during the reproductive cycle of sac fungi.
The scientist observed eight ascospores aligned within a single ascus under the microscope.
Release of ascospores into the environment allows the fungus to disperse and colonize new substrates.

Etymology

From Greek askos (bag, sac) + spora (seed, spore).
