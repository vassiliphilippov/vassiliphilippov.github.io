lordolatry

1. NOUN

/ lɔrˈdɑlətri / (AmE), / lɔːˈdɒlətri / (BrE)
excessive admiration or worship of a lord because of his rank or title
worship or adulation of a lord

Examples

The historian criticized the era's rampant lordolatry, noting how titles commanded unearned respect.
His lordolatry blinded him to the failings of the aristocracy.
Many reformers sought to challenge the social effects of lordolatry in the old regime.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idolatry - the worship of idols; excessive devotion to something

Etymology

From 'lord' + 'idolatry', patterned after similar formations like 'angelolatry'.
