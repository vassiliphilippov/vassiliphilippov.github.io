stellar

1. ADJ

/ ˈstɛlɚ / (AmE), / ˈstelə / (BrE)
of or relating to stars; resembling or emanating from stars.
relating to stars

Examples

Astronomers discovered new stellar formations in the distant galaxy.
The photograph captured the stellar beauty of the night sky.
Scientists study stellar evolution to understand the life cycle of stars.

Synset Examples

an astral body
stellar light

Etymology

From Latin 'stellaris', from 'stella' meaning 'star'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈstɛlɚ / (AmE), / ˈstelə / (BrE)
exceptional or leading in performance or quality; outstanding, especially as a performer or role
outstanding or leading; star performer

Examples

Her stellar performance earned her a standing ovation from the audience.
He played a stellar role in the company's recent success.
The new production features a stellar cast of Broadway veterans.

Synset Examples

the leading man
prima ballerina
prima donna
a star figure skater
the starring role
a stellar role
a stellar performance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outstanding - extremely good or impressive
  • Antonym: mediocre - of only average quality; not outstanding

Etymology

From Latin 'stellaris', from 'stella' meaning 'star'.
