swirl

1. NOUN

/ swɝːl / (AmE), / swɜːl / (BrE)
a twisting or spiral shape formed by something moving or rotating rapidly
a spiral or twisting shape

Examples

A swirl of smoke rose from the burning log.
The artist painted a colorful swirl on the canvas.
Ice cream with a chocolate swirl is my favorite.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiral - a curve which winds around a central point getting progressively closer or farther away

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'swerlen', meaning to whirl or swirl.

2. VERB

/ swɝl / (AmE), / swɜːl / (BrE)
to move in a twisting or spinning motion, especially in circles or curves
move in a twisting spinning motion

Examples

A sudden breeze made the fallen leaves swirl along the pavement.
She watched the cream swirl into her coffee.
Snowflakes swirled around them as they hurried home.

Synset Examples

The leaves swirled in the autumn wind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whirl - to move or cause to move rapidly around and around
  • Synonym: spin - to turn around quickly or cause to turn around
  • Degree Lower: flow - to move steadily and continuously in a current or stream
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02052840-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ swɜrl / (AmE), / swɜːl / (BrE)
(of a liquid or other substance) to move in a twisting or spiraling pattern or to flow in a circular current.
move in a twisting or circular motion

Examples

The water began to swirl around the drain, forming a miniature whirlpool.
She watched the cream swirl into the coffee, creating delicate patterns.
Leaves swirled in the wind after the rainstorm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whirl - to move rapidly in a circle or spiral
  • Synonym: eddy - to move in a circular motion, often referring to water

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'swyrl' (to whirl), of Scandinavian origin; compare with Norwegian 'svirla' (to whirl).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02051599-v
Synset POS: v
