telecom

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛlɪˌkɑm / (AmE), / ˈtɛlɪˌkɒm / (BrE)
(often plural) A system or company providing telecommunications services such as phone, internet, or data transmission over long distances electronically.
telecommunications system or service

Examples

Several major telecom providers are expanding their 5G networks across the country.
The new startup aims to disrupt the telecom industry with affordable internet plans.
Many rural areas still lack access to reliable telecom services.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: telecommunications - systems for electronic long-distance communication

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'telecommunication', from Greek 'tele-' (far) + 'communication'.
