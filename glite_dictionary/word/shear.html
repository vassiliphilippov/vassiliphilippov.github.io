shear

1. NOUN

/ ʃɪr / (AmE), / ʃɪə / (BrE)
a heavy cutting tool or machine used to cut sheet metal by passing a blade through it
machine for cutting sheet metal

Examples

The worker used a shear to cut through the thick sheet of aluminum quickly.
Make sure the shear is sharp before attempting to cut metal plates.
They purchased a new hydraulic shear for the workshop to handle larger jobs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shears - scissors-like tool used for cutting; large scissors

/ ʃɪr / (AmE), / ʃɪə / (BrE)
to cut something, especially hair, wool, or foliage, using shears or a similar tool
cut with shears or scissors

3. NOUN

/ ʃɪr / (AmE), / ʃɪə / (BrE)
(Physics) A type of deformation in which parallel planes in a material slide past each other while remaining parallel.
a kind of deformation in materials (physics)

Examples

The engineer measured the shear experienced by the metal beam under stress.
During the earthquake, the building's foundation failed due to excessive shear.
One common example of shear is the sliding of decks in a stack of cards.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strain - deformation in a material resulting from stress
  • Antonym: compression - deformation caused by forces that press inward
  • Antonym: tension - deformation caused by forces that pull apart

4. VERB

/ ʃɪr / (AmE), / ʃɪə / (BrE)
to become deformed as a result of forces that cause layers or particles to slide past each other, especially in response to a shearing strain
to be deformed by shearing force

Examples

When subjected to extreme pressure, the metal can shear and lose its original shape.
During an earthquake, geological layers may shear along fault lines.
If the force is too great, the bolt will shear rather than bend.

Related Terms
