Pediculus

1. NOUN

/ pəˈdɪkjələs / (AmE), / pɪˈdɪkjʊləs / (BrE)
A genus of lice that infest humans, including species such as the human head louse and body louse.
genus of lice infesting humans
medical

Examples

The species Pediculus humanus is known to cause pediculosis in humans.
Researchers studied the life cycle of Pediculus to develop more effective treatments for lice infestations.
Medical professionals can identify Pediculus under a microscope by its distinct morphology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: louse - a small, wingless parasitic insect

Etymology

From Latin pediculus (louse, diminutive of pes, foot), used in taxonomy for lice.
