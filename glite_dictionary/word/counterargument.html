counterargument

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊntərˌɑːrɡjəmənt / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntərˌɑːɡjəmənt / (BrE)
An argument or set of reasons put forward to oppose or refute another argument.
argument opposing another

Examples

She presented a strong counterargument to challenge his claims.
Before making a decision, it is important to consider every counterargument.
His counterargument addressed the main points of the proposal directly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebuttal - A statement or response that refutes or disproves an argument

Etymology

Formed from 'counter-' (against) and 'argument'.
