lividity

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈvɪdəti / (AmE), / lɪˈvɪdəti / (BrE)
An unnatural lack of color in the skin, often resulting from bruising, illness, or intense emotional distress.
unnatural lack of skin color

Examples

The paramedic noted the lividity on the patient's arms, indicating possible circulation issues.
After hearing the tragic news, a look of lividity spread across her face.
There was obvious lividity around the injury, making it look much worse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pallor - paleness or loss of color in the skin

Etymology

From Latin 'lividitas', meaning 'bluish color'.

2. NOUN

/ lɪˈvɪdəti / (AmE), / lɪˈvɪdəti / (BrE)
A state of extreme anger or fury so intense that it causes the face to become discolored.
state of intense, face-discoloring anger

Examples

The lividity of the coach after the team's loss was impossible to ignore.
His face showed a lividity that startled even his closest friends.
She struggled to control the lividity that threatened to overwhelm her during the argument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fury - intense, violent anger
  • Antonym: calm - state of being free from strong emotions

Etymology

From Latin 'lividitas', from 'lividus' (bluish, lead-colored).
