cardboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːrdˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdˌbɔːd / (BrE)
a stiff, moderately thick paper-based material used for making boxes and signs
stiff, thick paper material

Examples

The books arrived in a large cardboard box to protect them during shipping.
He used cardboard to make a model of the building for his project.
Recycling cardboard helps reduce waste and conserve resources.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paperboard - thick, stiff paperboard used for packaging

2. ADJ

/ ˈkɑrdˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdbɔːd / (BrE)
(of a person, character, or thing) lacking realism, depth, or substance; insubstantial or unconvincing.
insubstantial; lacking depth or realism

Examples

Critics complained that the film's villain was a cardboard character with no real motivation.
She found the politician's promises to be cardboard and meaningless.
The novel’s hero felt like a cardboard figure, flat and unconvincing.

Synset Examples

cardboard caricatures of historical figures

Related Terms

  • Synonym: superficial - not thorough or deep; lacking depth
  • Antonym: lifelike - having a realistic or natural appearance or quality
