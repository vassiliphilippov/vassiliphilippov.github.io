to suburbanise

1. VERB

/ səˈbɜrbəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / səˈbɜːbənaɪz / (BrE)
to make (a place or area) more suburban in character or appearance, often by encouraging the development of residential suburbs
make suburban in character

Examples

The rapid growth of highways helped to suburbanise many rural areas around the city.
Recent policies have suburbanised what was once a vibrant urban district.
Developers are suburbanising farmland at an unprecedented pace, creating new housing estates.

Synset Examples

highly suburbanized cities

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to urbanise - to make urban in character

Etymology

From 'suburban' + '-ise', patterned after 'urbanise'.

2. VERB

/ səˈbɜrbəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / səˈbɜːbənaɪz / (BrE)
(of an area) to develop the characteristics of a suburb, especially as a result of urban expansion.
become or make suburban

Examples

The city began to rapidly suburbanise after the opening of the new highway.
Many rural areas have started to suburbanise in recent decades.
As industries moved outward, the whole northern district gradually suburbanised.

Synset Examples

the city suburbanized

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to urbanise - (of an area) to become more like a city

Etymology

Formed from 'suburban' + '-ise', modelled on 'urbanise'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00122826-v
Synset POS: v
