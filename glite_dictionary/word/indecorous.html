indecorous

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈdɛkərəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛkərəs / (BrE)
lacking propriety or good taste; improper or inappropriate in manners or conduct
improper; lacking good taste or manners

Examples

His indecorous remarks shocked many people at the formal dinner.
Laughing loudly during the ceremony was considered indecorous by the elders.
The teacher reprimanded the students for their indecorous conduct in the hallway.

Synset Examples

indecorous behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: improper - not conforming to accepted standards of behavior or morals
  • Antonym: decorous - in keeping with good taste and propriety; polite and restrained

Etymology

From Latin 'indecorosus' (unbecoming), from 'in-' (not) + 'decorus' (becoming, seemly).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈdɛkərəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛkərəs / (BrE)
not in keeping with accepted standards of propriety or good taste; improper or lacking decorum
improper; lacking decorum

Examples

His indecorous remarks shocked everyone at the formal dinner.
It was considered indecorous to interrupt the speaker during the ceremony.
The committee disapproved of the indecorous conduct displayed by some attendees.

Synset Examples

was buried with indecent haste
indecorous behavior
language unbecoming to a lady
unseemly to use profanity
moved to curb their untoward ribaldry

Related Terms

  • Antonym: decorous - in keeping with good taste and propriety; proper
  • Synonym: improper - not in accordance with accepted standards, especially of morality or honesty
  • Synonym: unseemly - not proper or appropriate; unbecoming

Etymology

From Latin 'indecorus' (unbecoming, unfitting), from 'in-' (not) + 'decorus' (becoming, seemly).
