sailor

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪlər / (AmE), / ˈseɪlə / (BrE)
a person who works as a member of a ship's crew, especially one who is not an officer
member of a ship's crew

Examples

My grandfather was a sailor in the navy during World War II.
As a sailor, he traveled to many exotic ports around the world.
The sailor climbed the mast to adjust the rigging.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seaman - a person who works on a ship, especially below the rank of officer

Etymology

From Middle English saylour, from Anglo-Norman sailer, based on sail (the act of sailing).

2. NOUN

/ ˈseɪlər / (AmE), / ˈseɪlə / (BrE)
a stiff straw hat with a flat crown, traditionally worn in summer and sometimes as part of a uniform
stiff straw hat with flat crown

Examples

She wore a white dress and a sailor to the garden party.
The school uniform included a navy sailor for special occasions.
Vintage shops sometimes sell the classic sailor alongside other hats from the 1920s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boater - a stiff straw hat with a flat crown and straight brim

Etymology

Refers to its association with naval uniforms and sailors.

3. NOUN

/ ˈseɪlər / (AmE), / ˈseɪlə / (BrE)
a person who works in the navy, especially as a member of the crew of a warship
navy serviceman or crew member

Examples

Her father was a sailor in the navy for over twenty years.
The young sailor stood proudly on the deck of the destroyer.
Many sailors participated in the rescue mission during the storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seaman - a person who works as a sailor, especially below the rank of officer
  • Antonym: soldier - a person serving in an army

Etymology

From Middle English *sailere*, from *sail* + *-er*, meaning one who sails.
