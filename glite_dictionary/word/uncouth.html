uncouth

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkuθ / (AmE), / ʌnˈkuːθ / (BrE)
lacking good manners, refinement, or grace; uncultured or coarse in behavior or appearance
lacking refinement or good manners

Examples

His uncouth behavior at dinner shocked the other guests.
The uncouth language used in the play was criticized by reviewers.
Despite his brilliance, some found his manners a bit uncouth.

Synset Examples

he had coarse manners but a first-rate mind
behavior that branded him as common
an untutored and uncouth human being
an uncouth soldier — a real tough guy
appealing to the vulgar taste for violence
the vulgar display of the newly rich

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coarse - rude or lacking in delicacy; rough or crude
  • Antonym: refined - having or showing well-bred feeling, taste, or manners

Etymology

From Old English uncūth (unknown, unfamiliar), from un- (not) + cūth (known, familiar).
