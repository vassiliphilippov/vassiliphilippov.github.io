nutrition

1. NOUN

/ nuˈtrɪʃən / (AmE), / njuˈtrɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of obtaining and using food substances needed for growth, health, and maintenance of life.
the process of nourishing or being nourished

Examples

Proper nutrition is essential for children's healthy development and growth.
She studied nutrition at university to become a dietitian.
Athletes pay close attention to their nutrition to enhance performance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: malnutrition - lack of proper nutrition
  • Synonym: nourishment - food or other substances necessary for growth, health, and good condition
  • Synonym: diet - the kinds of food a person or animal habitually eats

Etymology

From Late Latin nutritio (a nourishing), from Latin nutrire (to nourish).

2. NOUN

/ nuˈtrɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / njuˈtrɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
A source or supply of nourishment essential for the growth and maintenance of life and health.
food substances for health and growth

Examples

Children need proper nutrition to grow and develop healthily.
The soup provides excellent nutrition on a cold day.
Athletes pay close attention to their nutrition for peak performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nourishment - the food or other substances necessary for growth, health, and good condition
  • Antonym: malnutrition - lack of proper nutrition; insufficient or unbalanced diet

Etymology

From Latin 'nutritio' (nourishment), from 'nutrire' (to nourish).

3. NOUN

/ nuˈtrɪʃən / (AmE), / njuˈtrɪʃən / (BrE)
the scientific study of food and drink and how they affect health, especially in humans
science of food and health

Examples

She decided to study nutrition at university because she was interested in healthy eating.
Advances in nutrition have helped improve the quality of food we eat today.
Good nutrition is essential for growth and development in children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dietetics - the study of diet and its effects on health

Etymology

From Latin 'nutritio' (a nourishing), from 'nutrire' (to nourish).
