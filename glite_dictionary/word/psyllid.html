psyllid

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɪlɪd / (BrE)
A small jumping plant louse that resembles a tiny cicada, with hind legs adapted for leaping, which feeds on plant juices.
small jumping plant louse

Examples

Orchards can suffer extensive damage if psyllid populations are not properly managed.
The leaves curled and yellowed after being infested with psyllids.
Researchers are studying the behavior of the potato psyllid to control the spread of plant diseases.

Etymology

From New Latin Psylla (the genus name), from Greek psulla (flea), referencing the insect's shape and jumping ability.
