Tungusic

1. NOUN

/ tʌŋˈɡuːsɪk / (AmE), / tʌŋˈɡuːsɪk / (BrE)
a member of a people who speak languages belonging to the Tungusic family, native to parts of Siberia and northeastern China
member of a Tungusic-speaking people

Examples

The Tungusic peoples have rich traditions tied to Siberian geography.
Anthropologists have long studied the migration patterns of various Tungusic groups.
Many Tungusic now live in both Russia and northeastern China.

Etymology

From Russian Тунгусский (Tungusskiy), relating to Tungus people and their languages.

2. NOUN

/ tʌŋˈɡuːsɪk / (AmE), / tʌŋˈɡuːsɪk / (BrE)
a family of Altaic languages spoken in eastern Siberia and Manchuria, including Evenki, Manchu, and related languages
family of languages of eastern Siberia and Manchuria

Examples

The Tungusic languages are spoken across vast regions of Siberia and northeastern China.
Scholars debate the classification of Tungusic as part of the Altaic language family.
Manchu is one of the most prominent Tungusic languages, though it is now nearly extinct.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Altaic - a proposed language family of central and northern Asia

Etymology

From Russian 'tungus', of unknown origin, referring to the Evenki people.
