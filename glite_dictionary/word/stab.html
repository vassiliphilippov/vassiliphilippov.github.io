stab

1. NOUN

/ stæb / (AmE), / stæb / (BrE)
a sudden, forceful thrust with a knife or other sharp-pointed instrument
forceful thrust with a knife or sharp object

Examples

The stab in his arm was bleeding heavily.
She died from a single stab wound to the chest.
He was hospitalized after suffering a stab to his shoulder during the fight.

Synset Examples

one strong stab to the heart killed him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrust - a forceful forward movement, especially with a weapon

Etymology

From Middle English stabben, related to Middle Dutch stabben (to stab, thrust).

2. NOUN

/ stæb / (AmE), / stæb / (BrE)
An attempt or effort to do something, especially one that is not certain to succeed; a try.
attempt or effort
informal

Examples

He decided to take a stab at painting, even though he had no experience.
This is my first stab at baking bread, so it might not turn out perfect.
She made a stab at answering the difficult question, but wasn't completely sure.

Synset Examples

he gave it his best shot
he took a stab at forecasting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shot - an attempt to do something; a try
  • Synonym: try - an effort to do or accomplish something

Etymology

Originally from Middle English 'stabben', meaning to pierce; figurative use for 'attempt' attested from early 19th century.

/ stæb / (AmE), / stæb / (BrE)

She was stabbed in the arm during a robbery last night.
Police arrested the suspect for stabbing a man outside the bar.
