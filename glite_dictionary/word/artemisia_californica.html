Artemisia californica

1. NOUN

/ ɑːr.təˈmɪʒ.i.ə ˌkæl.ɪˈfɔːr.nɪ.kə / (AmE), / ˌɑː.tɪˌmɪz.i.ə ˌkæl.ɪˈfɒn.ɪ.kə / (BrE)
A low, ashy-grey shrub native to California, commonly known as California sagebrush.
California sagebrush shrub

Examples

Artemisia californica is often found on dry hillsides in Southern California.
Many gardeners use Artemisia californica to create drought-tolerant landscapes.
The aromatic leaves of Artemisia californica are a common feature in native plant gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sagebrush - A common name for aromatic shrubs of the genus Artemisia

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'Artemisia', after the goddess Artemis; 'californica' refers to California.
