casing

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkeɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
the outer layer or cover of a pneumatic tire, providing protection and structure
outer covering of a pneumatic tire

Examples

The mechanic inspected the casing of the tire for any cuts or damages.
A worn casing can increase the risk of tire failure on the road.
Modern tire casings are designed to be both durable and lightweight.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪ.sɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ.sɪŋ / (BrE)
the outer covering or housing of an object, designed to protect or contain it
protective outer covering or housing

Examples

The camera's casing is made of durable plastic to withstand drops.
She removed the metal casing to repair the broken fan inside.
Old clocks often feature a wooden casing with intricate designs.

Synset Examples

the clock has a walnut case

Related Terms

  • Synonym: housing - a protective covering or enclosure for machinery or equipment
  • Synonym: shell - a hard outer covering that protects something

Etymology

From 'case' (container) + '-ing' (forming nouns indicating action or result).
