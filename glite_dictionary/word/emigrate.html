to emigrate

1. VERB

/ ˈɛmɪˌɡreɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛmɪɡreɪt / (BrE)
to leave your own country in order to live permanently in another
leave one's country to settle elsewhere

Examples

Millions of people chose to emigrate during times of war and famine.
Her family decided to emigrate to Canada in search of new opportunities.
If you emigrate, you must adapt to a new culture and environment.

Synset Examples

Many people had to emigrate during the Nazi period

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to immigrate - to come to live permanently in a country from abroad
  • Synonym: to migrate - to move from one place to another, especially seasonally or temporarily

Etymology

From Latin 'emigrare' (to move out), from 'e-' (out) + 'migrare' (to migrate).
