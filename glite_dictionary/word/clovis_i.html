Clovis I

1. PROPN

/ ˈkloʊvɪs ðə ˈfɜrst / (AmE), / ˈkləʊvɪs ði ˈfɜːst / (BrE)
(c. 466–511) King of the Franks who unified Gaul, established his capital at Paris, and founded the Frankish monarchy.
first king of the united Franks

Examples

Clovis I is considered the founder of the Merovingian dynasty in France.
After converting to Christianity, Clovis I played a key role in spreading the religion among the Franks.
Historians regard Clovis I as a pivotal figure in the unification of Gaul.

Etymology

Latinized form of Frankish name *Chlodovechus*, later becoming 'Louis' in French.
