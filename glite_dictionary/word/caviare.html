caviare

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæv.i.ɑr / (AmE), / ˈkæv.i.ɑː / (BrE)
Salted roe of sturgeon or other large fish, typically served as a delicacy or hors d'oeuvre.
salted fish roe delicacy
british

Examples

She ordered a small dish of caviare with toast points for the appetizer.
The chef garnished the blinis with sour cream and a spoonful of caviare.
Many considered caviare a symbol of luxury at formal events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caviar - salted roe of sturgeon or other large fish, usually served as a delicacy

Etymology

From Italian 'caviaro' or Turkish 'havyar', both borrowed from Persian 'khāvyār'.
