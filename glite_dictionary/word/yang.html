yang

1. NOUN

/ jæŋ / (AmE), / jæŋ / (BrE)
(in Chinese philosophy) the active, bright, positive, and masculine principle in dualistic cosmology, often contrasted with yin.
the bright, positive principle in Chinese philosophy

Examples

The concept of yang is often associated with sunlight, warmth, and activity in Chinese philosophy.
Many traditional practices aim to balance yang and yin energies for health and harmony.
He explained how yang represents strength and assertiveness, complementing the soft power of yin.

Synset Examples

yin and yang together produce everything that comes into existence

Related Terms

  • Antonym: yin - the dark, passive, negative, and feminine principle in Chinese cosmology

Etymology

From Mandarin 陽 (yáng), referring to the 'sunny side' and the positive principle in Chinese cosmology.
