to phlebotomize

1. VERB

/ fləˈbɑtəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / flɪˈbɒtəˌmaɪz / (BrE)
to draw blood from a person's vein for medical purposes
draw blood from a vein
medical

Examples

The nurse will phlebotomize you to obtain a blood sample for the lab tests.
Years ago, doctors would frequently phlebotomize patients as a treatment for many diseases.
After being phlebotomized, the patient was asked to rest for a few minutes.

Synset Examples

In the old days, doctors routinely bled patients as part of the treatment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bleed - to draw blood, especially in older medical practice

Etymology

From Greek 'phlebotomizein', from 'phleps' (vein) + 'temnein' (to cut).
