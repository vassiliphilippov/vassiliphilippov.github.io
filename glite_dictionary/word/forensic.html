forensic

1. ADJ

/ fəˈrɛnsɪk / (AmE), / fəˈrɛnsɪk / (BrE)
relating to or used in the investigation and establishment of facts or evidence in a court of law
relating to legal evidence or investigation

Examples

The detective analyzed the forensic evidence collected at the crime scene.
Advances in forensic science have solved crimes that were once thought unsolvable.
She is a forensic specialist who often testifies in court cases.

Synset Examples

forensic photograph
forensic ballistics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legal - relating to the law

Etymology

From Latin 'forensis' (of a forum, public), referring to courts of law.

2. ADJ

/ fəˈrɛnsɪk / (AmE), / fəˈrɛnsɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or used in formal public debate, argument, or rhetorical discussion.
of public debate or argument

Examples

Her forensic skills made her a formidable opponent in the debate club.
The professor delivered a forensic analysis of the opposing viewpoints.
In ancient Rome, forensic oratory was a respected art in the public forum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhetorical - relating to rhetoric; the art of effective or persuasive speaking or writing

Etymology

From Latin 'forensis' (of the forum, public), relating to public discussion or debate.
