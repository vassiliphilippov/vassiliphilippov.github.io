regrettable

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈɡrɛdəbl̩ / (AmE), / rɪˈɡrɛtəbl̩ / (BrE)
Deserving regret; unfortunate or deplorable.
deserving regret; unfortunate

Examples

It was regrettable that the meeting had to be canceled at the last minute.
Her regrettable comment caused offense among several colleagues.
The loss of such valuable data is truly regrettable for the whole team.

Synset Examples

regrettable remarks
it's regrettable that she didn't go to college
it's too bad he had no feeling himself for church

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unfortunate - unlucky or causing sorrow; synonym for regrettable
  • Degree Higher: deplorable - deserving strong condemnation; even worse than regrettable
  • Antonym: fortunate - bringing good; opposite of regrettable

Etymology

From regret + -able, patterned after similar adjectives.
