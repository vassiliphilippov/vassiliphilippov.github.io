arcsecant

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrkˌsiːkænt / (AmE), / ˈɑːkˌsiːkənt / (BrE)
the inverse function of the secant; the angle whose secant is a given number
inverse of the secant function

Examples

The arcsecant of 2 is the angle whose secant is 2.
In this equation, you need to use the arcsecant function to find the value of x.
Some calculators do not have a direct arcsecant button, so alternate methods are required.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: secant - the trigonometric function equal to 1/cosine
  • Synonym: arccosine - the inverse function of the cosine
  • Synonym: arctangent - the inverse function of the tangent

Etymology

Formed from 'arc-' (as in 'arc sine', 'arc cosine') and 'secant', following mathematical naming conventions.
