surgeonfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝːdʒəˌnfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈsɜːdʒənˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a brightly colored tropical marine fish with sharp, knifelike spines on each side of the tail, commonly found among coral reefs
brightly colored coral-reef fish with tail spines

Examples

The surgeonfish darted quickly among the vibrant corals.
Aquariums often house species of surgeonfish due to their striking appearance.
Be careful when handling a surgeonfish, as its tail spines can inflict a painful wound.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tang - another name for surgeonfish, especially in aquariums

Etymology

Named for the sharp tail spines resembling a surgeon's scalpel.
