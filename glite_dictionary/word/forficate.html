forficate

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɔːr.fɪ.keɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɔː.fɪ.keɪt / (BrE)
(of a part or structure) shaped like a fork; divided or separated into two branches.
divided into two branches; forked

Examples

The insect's antennae were distinctly forficate, splitting into two delicate branches.
Several algae have forficate fronds that resemble the prongs of a fork.
The river follows a forficate course, dividing neatly around the central island.

Etymology

From Latin forficatus, past participle of forficare (to cut with scissors), from forfex (scissors).
