shenanigan

1. NOUN

/ ʃəˈnænɪɡən / (AmE), / ʃəˈnænɪɡən / (BrE)
a mischievous or tricky act; reckless or deceitful behavior that causes annoyance or trouble
mischievous or tricky act

Examples

The children were up to another shenanigan during recess, hiding the teacher’s chalk.
After all his financial shenanigans, the CEO was finally caught.
I'm tired of your shenanigans—just do your work properly!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mischief - behavior that causes annoyance or harm, especially by being playful or naughty
  • Synonym: prank - a playful trick or joke
  • Antonym: respectfulness - polite and considerate behavior, opposite of mischief or shenanigans

Etymology

Origin unknown; attested in American English in the mid-19th century.

2. NOUN

/ ʃəˈnænɪɡən / (AmE), / ʃɪˈnænɪɡən / (BrE)
a deceitful or mischievous trick, especially one intended to cheat or deceive someone
deceitful or mischievous trick

Examples

The company uncovered financial shenanigans that led to several employees being fired.
His constant shenanigans at school got him into a lot of trouble.
Don't try any shenanigan with my money, or you'll regret it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trick - a cunning or deceitful act
  • Synonym: prank - a mischievous act or trick

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from Irish slang or dialect.
