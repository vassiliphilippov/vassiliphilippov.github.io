theorizer

1. NOUN

/ ˈθiəˌraɪzər / (AmE), / ˈθɪəraɪzə / (BrE)
a person who forms or speculates about theories, especially in science or art
person who develops theories

Examples

As an ambitious theorizer, she constantly proposed new explanations for the mysterious phenomenon.
Many dismissed him as just a theorizer, but his ideas later revolutionized the field.
Artists and scientists alike benefit from the creative insights of a skilled theorizer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theorist - person who forms theories

Etymology

From 'theorize' + '-er'. Ultimately from Greek 'theōria' (contemplation).
