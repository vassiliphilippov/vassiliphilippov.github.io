to sheathe

1. VERB

/ ʃið / (AmE), / ʃiːð / (BrE)
to plunge or bury (a knife or sword) in flesh
plunge a blade into flesh

Examples

He sheathed his dagger in the traitor's chest without hesitation.
The assassin silently sheathed the blade into his victim.
With a cry, she sheathed her sword deeply into the beast's flank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stab - to thrust a pointed weapon into someone or something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01581563-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ʃið / (AmE), / ʃiːð / (BrE)
to put (a knife, sword, or other blade) into a sheath; to enclose or cover closely.
to put a blade into its sheath

Examples

After the ceremony, the knight carefully sheathed his sword.
Please sheathe the knife when you are finished using it.
Once the danger was over, she slowly sheathed the blade.

Synset Examples

sheathe a sword

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to unsheathe - to draw (a sword, knife, etc.) from its sheath

Etymology

From Middle English shethen, from Old English sceaþian (related to sheath).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01584211-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ʃið / (AmE), / ʃiːð / (BrE)
to cover or encase something, especially with a protective layer
cover or encase with a protective layer

Examples

The sword was sheathed in a leather scabbard at his side.
The mountain was sheathed in a layer of fresh snow after the storm.
She sheathed the cables in plastic tubing to protect them from damage.

Synset Examples

sheathe her face

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cover - to put something over something else to protect or hide it

Etymology

From Old English *sceathian* (to cover, protect), related to 'sheath'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01584484-v
Synset POS: v
