geophysics

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒiː.oʊˈfɪz.ɪks / (AmE), / ˌdʒiː.əʊˈfɪz.ɪks / (BrE)
The branch of earth sciences that applies physical principles and methods to study the Earth's properties and processes.
study of Earth's physical properties

Examples

He is a professor of geophysics at the university, specializing in seismic research.
Advancements in geophysics have improved our ability to predict earthquakes.
Modern geophysics uses satellites to study variations in the Earth's gravity field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geology - science dealing with the Earth's physical structure and substance
  • Synonym: physics - the science of matter and energy and their interactions

Etymology

From Greek geo- (earth) and physics (science of nature).
