remove

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈmuːv / (AmE), / rɪˈmuːv / (BrE)
a degree of separation or distance, especially figurative or genealogical; a step or stage away from something
degree of separation or distance (formal)

Examples

Though she was only one remove away, the truth felt utterly unreachable.
Historians believe modern customs are many removes from their ancient origins.
The tradition is at several removes from its original form.

Synset Examples

just one remove from madness
it imitates at many removes a Shakespearean tragedy

Etymology

From Latin *removēre* (to move back, remove).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05097462-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ rɪˈmuv / (AmE), / rɪˈmuːv / (BrE)
to dismiss someone from a position or office; to relieve someone of their duties

Examples
