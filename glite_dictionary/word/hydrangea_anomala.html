Hydrangea anomala

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈdreɪndʒə əˈnɑːmələ / (AmE), / hɪˈdrændʒɪə əˈnɒmələ / (BrE)
A species of deciduous climbing plant with aerial roots, bearing white to creamy flowers in relatively flat clusters.
species of climbing hydrangea plant

Examples

The old stone wall was covered in Hydrangea anomala, its creamy flowers standing out against the green leaves.
Gardeners often choose Hydrangea anomala for shady spots where it can climb fences or trellises.
In early summer, Hydrangea anomala produces large, flat clusters of delicate white blooms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydranggea - genus of flowering plants including hydrangeas

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Hydrangea' (the genus) and Latin 'anomala' (anomalous, irregular).
