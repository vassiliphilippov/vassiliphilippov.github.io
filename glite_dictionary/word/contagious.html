contagious

1. ADJ

/ kənˈteɪdʒəs / (AmE), / kənˈteɪdʒəs / (BrE)
(of a disease) able to be transmitted from one person or organism to another, typically by direct or indirect contact.
able to be spread by infection

Examples

The flu is highly contagious and can spread quickly through a school.
Chickenpox is a contagious disease, especially among young children.
She stayed home because her illness was still contagious and she didn't want to infect others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infectious - (of disease) capable of causing infection or spreading easily

Etymology

From Latin 'contagiosus', from 'contagio' (contact, contagion).

2. ADJ

/ kənˈteɪdʒəs / (AmE), / kənˈteɪ.dʒəs / (BrE)
(of a disease, feeling, or idea) able to be spread or passed quickly from one person or organism to another, especially through direct or close contact.
easily spread from person to person

Examples

Her contagious laughter soon had everyone in the room smiling.
The enthusiasm of the coach was truly contagious and inspired the whole team.
He caught a contagious cold at school and had to stay home for a week.

Synset Examples

a contagious grin

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infectious - (of diseases or emotions) likely to be transmitted to people, organisms, etc.
  • Antonym: noncontagious - not able to be spread from one person or organism to another

Etymology

From Latin 'contagiosus' meaning 'communicable by contact', from 'contagio' (contact, contagion).
