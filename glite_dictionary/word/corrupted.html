corrupted

1. ADJ

/ kəˈrʌptɪd / (AmE), / kəˈrʌptɪd / (BrE)
(of a person, system, or thing) having become morally or physically ruined, especially as a result of dishonesty or damage.
ruined in character or quality

Examples

The corrupted officials were removed from office after the scandal was exposed.
His corrupted values led him to make unethical decisions for personal gain.
The document became corrupted and could no longer be opened by the computer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immoral - not conforming to accepted standards of morality
  • Antonym: uncorrupted - not affected by corruption; pure

Etymology

Past participle of 'corrupt', from Latin 'corruptus', meaning spoiled or destroyed.

2. ADJ

/ kəˈrʌptɪd / (AmE), / kəˈrʌptɪd / (BrE)
Containing errors or alterations; changed from the original, often in a way that makes it less accurate or authentic.
containing errors or alterations

Examples

The corrupted file would not open due to data errors.
Over the years, a corrupted version of the legend spread through the village.
She realized she had received a corrupted text, full of mistakes and missing information.

Synset Examples

a corrupt text
spoke a corrupted version of the language

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corrupt - containing errors or dishonest alterations

Etymology

Past participle of 'corrupt', from Latin 'corruptus' (spoiled, ruined).
