Vaccinium macrocarpon

1. NOUN

/ vækˈsɪniəm ˌmækrəˈkɑrpən / (AmE), / vakˈsɪnɪəm ˌmakrəˈkɑːpɒn / (BrE)
the scientific name for the American cranberry, a prostrate North American plant with trailing stems and edible red fruit
American cranberry plant (scientific name)

Examples

Vaccinium macrocarpon is cultivated extensively for its tart, red berries used to make juice and sauce.
The wetlands of North America provide an ideal habitat for Vaccinium macrocarpon.
Many commercial cranberry products are derived from Vaccinium macrocarpon varieties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cranberry - edible red berry, particularly of Vaccinium macrocarpon

Etymology

From Latin: Vaccinium (a genus of shrubs) and macrocarpon (from Greek makros 'large' + karpos 'fruit').
