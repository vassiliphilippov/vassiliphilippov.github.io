to introspect

1. VERB

/ ˌɪn.trəˈspɛkt / (AmE), / ˌɪn.trəˈspɛkt / (BrE)
to examine or reflect on one's own thoughts and feelings
examine one's own thoughts or feelings

Examples

After the meeting, she took some time to introspect on her reaction to the criticism.
It's important to introspect before making major life decisions.
He often introspects about his goals and values while walking in the park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reflect - to think deeply or carefully about something
  • Synonym: to ruminate - to think deeply about something
  • Antonym: to ignore - to pay no attention to something

Etymology

From Latin 'introspicere' (to look inside), from 'intro-' (inward) + 'specere' (to look).
