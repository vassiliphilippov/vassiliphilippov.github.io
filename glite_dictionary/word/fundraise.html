to fundraise

1. VERB

/ ˈfʌndˌreɪz / (AmE), / ˈfʌndˌreɪz / (BrE)
to collect money for a cause, project, or organization, especially by organizing events or campaigns
collect money for a cause

Examples

The school plans to fundraise for new sports equipment this year.
Local artists volunteered their time to fundraise for the animal shelter.
She decided to fundraise by organizing a fun run in her neighborhood.

Synset Examples

We are fundraising for AIDS research

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raise money - to collect money for a cause or project

Etymology

From English 'fund' (money set aside for a purpose) + 'raise' (to collect or gather).

2. VERB

/ ˈfʌndˌreɪz / (AmE), / ˈfʌndreɪz / (BrE)
to collect money for a particular purpose, especially for a charity, cause, or project
collect money for a cause or project

Examples

Every year, the school organizes events to fundraise for new classroom equipment.
She volunteered to fundraise for the animal shelter over the summer.
The nonprofit has managed to fundraise millions for disaster relief efforts.

Synset Examples

We are fundraising for AIDS research

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collect - to gather or accumulate something, especially money or information

Etymology

From 'fund' (money) + 'raise' (to collect or gather), first attested in the mid-20th century.
