schizophrenic

1. ADJ

/ skɪt.səˈfrɛn.ɪk / (AmE), / skɪt.səʊˈfrɛn.ɪk / (BrE)
Suffering from schizophrenia or relating to this mental disorder.
having or relating to schizophrenia
medical

Examples

The study focused on schizophrenic patients and their treatment outcomes.
Her brother was diagnosed as schizophrenic at the age of twenty.
Doctors have developed new therapies for people with schizophrenic disorders.

Synset Examples

schizophrenic patients

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schizophrenia - a severe mental disorder involving a breakdown in the relation between thought, emotion, and behavior

Etymology

From schizophrenia + -ic, coined in early 20th century after schizophrenia was described in medical literature.

2. NOUN

/ ˌskɪtsəˈfrɛnɪk / (AmE), / ˌskɪtsəʊˈfriːnɪk / (BrE)
a person who has schizophrenia, a serious mental disorder characterized by distorted thinking, perceptions, emotions, and behavior
person diagnosed with schizophrenia
medical

Examples

The hospital provides specialized care for schizophrenics.
He was diagnosed as a schizophrenic during his early twenties.
Many schizophrenics benefit from a combination of medication and therapy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schizophrenia - a severe mental disorder characterized by distortions of reality, thought, and emotion

Etymology

From 'schizophrenia' (Greek skhizein 'to split' + phrēn 'mind') + -ic.

WordNet ID: oewn-10576132-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˌskɪtsəˈfrɛnɪk / (AmE), / ˌskɪtsəˈfrɛnɪk / (BrE)
of, relating to, or characteristic of schizophrenia, a severe mental disorder involving distorted thinking, perception, and emotions
relating to schizophrenia
medical

Examples

The patient displayed schizophrenic symptoms such as hallucinations and disorganized thinking.
This new drug is used to treat schizophrenic disorders in adults.
Her artwork is often described as schizophrenic, reflecting fragmented perspectives and themes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schizophrenia - a severe mental disorder characterized by loss of contact with reality

Etymology

Formed from "schizophrenia" + "-ic"; schizophrenia from Greek "schizein" (to split) and "phrēn" (mind).
