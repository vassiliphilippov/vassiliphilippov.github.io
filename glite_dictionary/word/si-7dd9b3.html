si

1. NOUN

/ si / (AmE), / si / (BrE)
the syllable used to name the seventh note of a musical scale in solmization, especially in the fixed-do system
musical note syllable (seventh note)

Examples

In solfège, singers recite the scale as do, re, mi, fa, sol, la, si, do.
The choir practiced the passage carefully, making sure to hit the si accurately before returning to do.
In some music education systems, si is used instead of 'ti' for the seventh scale degree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ti - the syllable naming the seventh note of the scale in the moveable-do system
  • Antonym: do - the syllable naming the first note of the scale in solmization

Etymology

From Italian solfège syllables, replacing 'ti' in some traditions.
