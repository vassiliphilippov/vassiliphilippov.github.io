penchant

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛn.tʃənt / (AmE), / ˈpɒ̃.ʃɒ̃ / (BrE)
a strong or habitual liking for something or tendency to do something
strong liking or preference

Examples

She has a penchant for collecting rare books and antiques.
His penchant for spicy food often surprises his friends.
The artist developed a penchant for abstract painting during his time in Paris.

Synset Examples

my own preference is for good literature
the Irish have a penchant for blarney

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preference - a greater liking for one alternative over another or others
  • Antonym: aversion - a strong dislike or disinclination

Etymology

From French 'penchant', present participle of 'pencher' (to incline, to lean), from Late Latin 'pendicare', from Latin 'pendēre' (to hang).
