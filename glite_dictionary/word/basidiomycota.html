Basidiomycota

1. NOUN

/ bəˌsɪdi.oʊˈmaɪkoʊtə / (AmE), / bəˌsɪdɪə(ʊ)ˈmʌɪkətə / (BrE)
a large division of fungi that produce spores on a basidium, including mushrooms, puffballs, rusts, and smuts
division of fungi including mushrooms and related species

Examples

Basidiomycota includes familiar fungi such as mushrooms, toadstools, and puffballs.
Scientists study Basidiomycota to better understand fungal biodiversity and the decomposition of wood.
Many edible mushrooms belong to the Basidiomycota, making them important for both ecology and cuisine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ascomycota - a division of fungi forming spores in a sac-like ascus

Etymology

From modern Latin, from Greek basidion (little base) + -mycota (pertaining to fungi).

2. NOUN

/ bəˌsɪdi.oʊˈmaɪkətə / (AmE), / bəˌsɪdi.əʊˈmaɪkətə / (BrE)
A large division of fungi characterized by bearing spores on a structure called a basidium, including mushrooms, puffballs, bracket fungi, and rusts.
division of fungi with spores on basidia
medical

Examples

Many edible mushrooms belong to the Basidiomycota, including popular varieties like shiitake and portobello.
Scientists study the Basidiomycota to better understand their role in forest ecosystems and decomposition.
Rust and smut fungi, both part of Basidiomycota, can cause significant diseases in agricultural crops.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: fungus - Any member of the kingdom Fungi, including mushrooms, molds, and yeasts
  • Antonym: ascomycota - A division of fungi characterized by producing spores in an ascus

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining 'basidium' (spore-bearing structure) and 'mycota' (relating to fungi).
