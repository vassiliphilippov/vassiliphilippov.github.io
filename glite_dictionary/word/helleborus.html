Helleborus

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛlɪˈbɔːrəs / (AmE), / ˌhɛlɪˈbɔːrəs / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants in the buttercup family (Ranunculaceae), commonly known as hellebores, with many species grown for their attractive winter-blooming flowers
genus of flowering plants called hellebores

Examples

The garden featured several varieties of Helleborus flowering in the late winter months.
Many horticulturists prize Helleborus for its ability to bloom when few other plants do.
Experts recommend planting Helleborus in shaded areas with well-drained soil.

Etymology

From Latin helleborus, from Greek helleboros, a kind of plant.
