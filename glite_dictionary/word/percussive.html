percussive

1. ADJ

/ pərˈkʌsɪv / (AmE), / pəˈkʌsɪv / (BrE)
relating to or produced by percussion; having the quality of being struck or involving percussive instruments.
relating to percussion

Examples

The rhythm of the dance was enhanced by percussive beats on the drums.
She prefers percussive music, especially with a strong emphasis on rhythm.
The artist used percussive sounds in her experimental performance.

Synset Examples

percussive music

Related Terms

  • Synonym: percussion - the striking of one solid object with or against another

Etymology

From Latin 'percussio' (a striking), via French 'percussif'.
