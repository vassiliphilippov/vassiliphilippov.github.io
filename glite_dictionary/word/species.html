species

1. NOUN

/ ˈspiːʃiːz / (AmE), / ˈspiːʃiːz / (BrE)
(biology) A group of living organisms consisting of similar individuals capable of exchanging genes or interbreeding.
biological group of interbreeding organisms

Examples

There are thousands of bird species found throughout the Amazon rainforest.
The panda is an endangered species due to habitat loss and low birth rates.
Scientists discovered a new species of frog in the remote mountains of Vietnam.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: genus - Taxonomic group above species, including similar species
  • Degree Lower: subspecies - Taxonomic group below species, identifiable variant within a species

Etymology

From Latin 'species' (appearance, form, kind).

2. NOUN

/ ˈspiːʃiz / (AmE), / ˈspiːʃiːz / (BrE)
a group of things or a category that share common characteristics; a specific kind or sort
a kind or sort of thing

Examples

Each species of problem requires a different solution.
They had never seen this species of antique furniture before.
A new species of corruption was uncovered in the investigation.

Synset Examples

a species of molecule
a species of villainy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: type - a group of things having common characteristics; a sort
  • Synonym: variety - a category of things distinguished by a particular characteristic
  • Synonym: category - a class or division of things regarded as having particular shared characteristics

Etymology

From Latin 'species' (appearance, kind, form).
