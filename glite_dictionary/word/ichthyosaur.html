ichthyosaur

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪkθioʊˌsɔr / (AmE), / ˈɪkθɪəˌsɔː / (BrE)
an extinct marine reptile of the Mesozoic era, resembling a dolphin with a fish-like body, dorsal and tail fins, and paddle-shaped limbs
extinct marine reptile of the Mesozoic era

Examples

The scientist carefully examined the fossil of the ichthyosaur discovered in the cliffs.
During the Jurassic period, the ichthyosaur was a dominant predator in the oceans.
Museum visitors are often amazed by the size and shape of the ichthyosaur skeleton.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plesiosaur - another type of extinct marine reptile from the Mesozoic era

Etymology

From Greek 'ichthys' (fish) and 'sauros' (lizard).
