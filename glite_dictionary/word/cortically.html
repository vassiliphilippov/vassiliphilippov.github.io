cortically

1. ADV

/ ˈkɔr.tɪ.kli / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.tɪ.kli / (BrE)
In a manner that relates to the cortex, especially of the brain.
in relation to the cortex
medical

Examples

The patient showed improvement after a cortically administered therapy.
Memory functions are often studied cortically in neuroscience research.
Certain types of learning can be cortically induced through direct stimulation.

Synset Examples

cortically induced arousal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cortical - relating to the cortex

Etymology

From 'cortical' (relating to the cortex) + '-ly' adverbial suffix. 'Cortex' from Latin 'cortex' meaning 'bark' or 'outer covering'.
