shopping

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɑːpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃɒpɪŋ / (BrE)
the things you have bought from shops; purchased goods
items bought from shops
british

Examples

She put all the shopping on the kitchen table before unpacking.
I forgot to bring my own bags for the shopping this morning.
The bus was crowded with people carrying their shopping home.

Synset Examples

she loaded her shopping into the car
women carrying home shopping didn't give me a second glance

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃɑːpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃɒpɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity of searching for or purchasing goods or services, especially as a regular or leisure activity
the activity of buying goods or services

Examples

Weekend afternoons are a popular time for shopping at the local mall.
She finished her shopping before the supermarket got too crowded.
Online shopping has become increasingly popular over the last decade.

Synset Examples

went shopping for a reliable plumber
does her shopping at the mall rather than down town

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buying - the act of purchasing goods or services
  • Antonym: selling - the act of offering goods or services for sale

Etymology

From the verb 'shop', ultimately from Old English 'sceoppa' (stall, booth); the noun form in this sense dates from the late 18th century.
