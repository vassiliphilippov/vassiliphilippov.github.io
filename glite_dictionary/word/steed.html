steed

1. NOUN

/ stid / (AmE), / stiːd / (BrE)
(literary) a spirited horse, especially one used for riding in battle.
spirited horse (literary, often for war)

Examples

The knight mounted his steed and rode into battle.
Legends often tell of valiant warriors and their loyal steeds.
With a swift motion, she climbed atop her white steed and galloped away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horse - a large domesticated animal used for riding and work

Etymology

From Old English *stēda* (stallion, stud-horse).
