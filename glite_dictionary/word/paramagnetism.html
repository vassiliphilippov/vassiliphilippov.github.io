paramagnetism

1. NOUN

/ ˌpær.əˈmæɡ.nəˌtɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˌpar.əˈmaɡ.nə.tɪz.əm / (BrE)
A physical property of certain materials which become magnetized temporarily when placed in a magnetic field, but lose their magnetism when the field is removed.
magnetism induced by a field, lost when removed

Examples

Aluminum is an example of a metal that exhibits paramagnetism under certain conditions.
Unlike ferromagnetism, paramagnetism does not remain once the external magnetic field is removed.
The study of paramagnetism has advanced our understanding of magnetic materials.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: ferromagnetism - a physical property where some materials become permanently magnetized
  • Antonym: diamagnetism - a property where materials are weakly repelled by a magnetic field

Etymology

From para- (beside, beyond) + magnetism; modeled after diamagnetism and ferromagnetism.
