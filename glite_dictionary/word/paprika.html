paprika

1. NOUN

/ pəˈprikə / (AmE), / pəˈpriːkə / (BrE)
A mild powdered spice made from dried red peppers, often used to add flavor and color to food.
mild powdered spice from red peppers

Examples

She sprinkled a pinch of paprika over the deviled eggs for color and flavor.
The recipe calls for smoked paprika to give the stew a deeper taste.
He loves to add paprika to his roasted potatoes for a mild, earthy kick.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chili powder - spicy powder made from ground chili peppers

Etymology

From Hungarian 'paprika', derived from Serbo-Croatian 'papar' (pepper).

2. NOUN

/ pəˈprikə, pæˈprikə / (AmE), / pəˈpriːkə, ˈpæprɪkə / (BrE)
A plant of the pepper family, bearing large, mild, thick-walled, often bell-shaped fruits, used chiefly in salads and for the production of the spice paprika.
mild pepper plant with large fruits

Examples

The paprika in her garden produced an abundance of bright red peppers by midsummer.
This variety of paprika is especially popular for making fresh salads and stir-fries.
Farmers often grow paprika not only for the spice but also for its sweet, flavorful fruits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bell pepper - a large, mild-tasting, bell-shaped pepper

Etymology

From Hungarian 'paprika', diminutive of 'papar' (pepper), ultimately from Latin 'piper'.
