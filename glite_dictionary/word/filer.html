filer

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪlər / (AmE), / ˈfaɪlə / (BrE)
a person or organization who officially submits a document or notice to a court or other authority
person who submits documents to a court
legal

Examples

The filer submitted all the required paperwork to the court before the deadline.
Each bankruptcy filer must disclose their full financial information to the judge.
The judge asked the filer to clarify certain details in the application.

Etymology

From the verb 'file' (to submit a document), with the agent -er suffix.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪlə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to organize and maintain files in an office or organization
person who maintains files

Examples

The filer ensured that all documents were organized and easy to find.
She was promoted from filer to office manager after two years of diligent work.
Each department has its own filer responsible for records management.
