kachina

1. NOUN

/ kəˈtʃiːnə / (AmE), / kəˈtʃiːnə / (BrE)
A spirit being in the religious beliefs of the Pueblo people, often represented by masked dancers or carved figures.
spirit being in Pueblo religion

Examples

The Pueblo people honor the kachina in ceremonies throughout the year.
A carved kachina doll was displayed on the shelf as a symbol of tradition.
Many tourists buy kachina dolls as souvenirs, unaware of their deep spiritual meaning.

Etymology

From Hopi kachina (spirit).

2. NOUN

/ kəˈtʃiːnə / (AmE), / kəˈtʃiːnə / (BrE)
A carved doll representing a Pueblo spirit, traditionally given as a gift to children in Pueblo communities.
carved doll of a Pueblo spirit

Examples

Each spring, the children are given a kachina to teach them about the spirits.
Collectors value authentic kachina dolls for their craftsmanship and cultural significance.
The museum exhibit features a variety of traditional kachina dolls from the Hopi tribe.

Etymology

From Hopi *katsina*, referring to spirit beings in Pueblo cosmology.
