poking

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpəʊkɪŋ / (BrE)
A sharp or quick hand gesture, especially resembling a jab or thrust with a finger or an object.
sharp, quick jab or thrust

Examples

He silenced his friend with a swift poking of the finger.
A gentle poking was all it took to get their attention.
The child’s constant poking annoyed everyone at the dinner table.

Synset Examples

he warned me with a jab with his finger
he made a thrusting motion with his fist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jab - a quick, sharp blow or poke
  • Synonym: thrust - a sudden or forceful pushing movement

Etymology

From the verb 'poke,' of uncertain origin, possibly related to Middle Dutch 'poken' (to poke).
