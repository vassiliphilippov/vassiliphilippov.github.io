Siskiyou lewisia

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪskiˌjuː luːˈɪziə / (AmE), / ˌsɪskjuː luːˈɪsiə / (BrE)
an evergreen perennial plant, Lewisia cotyledon, native to the mountains of southwestern Oregon and northern California, with a rosette of long, spatula-shaped leaves and clusters of pink or white-and-red-striped flowers
mountain perennial with rosette leaves and showy flowers

Examples

The rocky slopes were covered with blooming Siskiyou lewisia in the spring.
Gardeners often plant Siskiyou lewisia in rock gardens due to its attractive flowers and evergreen leaves.
On our hike, we spotted several clusters of Siskiyou lewisia growing in crevices along the mountain trail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bitterroot - a related plant in the same genus, Lewisia rediviva

Etymology

Named for the Siskiyou Mountains and A. M. Lewis, an American explorer and botanist.
