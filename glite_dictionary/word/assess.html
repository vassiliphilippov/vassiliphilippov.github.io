to assess

1. VERB

/ əˈsɛs / (AmE), / əˈsɛs / (BrE)
to estimate the value of property, especially for the purpose of taxation
estimate property value for taxes

Examples

The city will assess the new shopping center for property tax purposes this year.
An official came to assess the value of our property.
Before selling the house, we had it assessed by the county tax office.

Synset Examples

Our house hasn't been assessed in years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to appraise - to evaluate the worth of something, especially officially

Etymology

From Latin 'assessare' (to sit beside, judge), from 'assidere' (to sit by).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00684149-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ əˈsɛs / (AmE), / əˈsɛs / (BrE)
to officially charge (a person or property) with a payment, such as a tax, fee, or fine
officially charge with a payment or tax

Examples

The council will assess a fee for late payments.
Property owners were assessed additional taxes to fund the new library.
The state may assess a fine if you fail to file your return on time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: levy - impose (a tax, fee, or fine) officially
  • Antonym: refund - return money previously paid, often by official decision

Etymology

From Latin 'assessus', past participle of 'assidēre' (to sit beside, assist in office).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02313532-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ əˈsɛs / (AmE), / əˈsɛs / (BrE)
to officially determine the amount of (a payment such as a fine, tax, or fee)
determine the amount (of a fine, tax, etc.)

Examples

The city council voted to assess a $50 fine on parking violators.
After the audit, the authorities will assess additional taxes on the business.
The court may assess damages against the defendant for the losses incurred.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: levy - to impose (a tax, fee, or fine) officially

Etymology

From Old French 'assesser', from Medieval Latin 'assessare', from Latin 'assessus' (to sit by as an assistant).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02313369-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ əˈsɛs / (AmE), / əˈsɛs / (BrE)
to evaluate or estimate the nature, quality, ability, extent, or significance of something
evaluate or estimate something

Examples

The teacher will assess each student's progress at the end of the term.
Before buying the house, we must assess its overall condition.
Experts were called in to assess the damage after the flood.

Synset Examples

I will have the family jewels appraised by a professional
access all the factors when taking a risk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to evaluate - to judge or determine the value or condition of
  • Synonym: to appraise - to estimate the worth or quality of
  • Synonym: estimate - to roughly calculate or judge the value, number, or quantity of

Etymology

From Latin 'assessus', past participle of 'assidēre' (to sit beside, assist, assess).
