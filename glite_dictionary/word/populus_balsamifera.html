Populus balsamifera

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːpjʊləs bælˌsæməˈfɪrə / (AmE), / ˈpɒpjʊləs bælˌsæməˈfɪərə / (BrE)
A species of poplar tree native to northeastern North America, characterized by broad, heart-shaped leaves and aromatic resinous buds.
a North American poplar tree species

Examples

A row of Populus balsamifera trees lined the edge of the river, their broad leaves shimmering in the wind.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Populus balsamifera in response to climate change.
The sticky buds of Populus balsamifera are known for their distinctive resinous scent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: balsam poplar - common name for Populus balsamifera

Etymology

From Latin: 'populus' (poplar) + 'balsamum' (balsam, resin) + '-fera' (bearing).
