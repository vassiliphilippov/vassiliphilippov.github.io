tutelar

1. ADJ

/ ˈtuːtəˌlɑr / (AmE), / ˈtjuːtɪlə / (BrE)
serving as a guardian or providing protection; watching over and safeguarding
protective; acting as a guardian
archaic

Examples

The statue depicts a tutelar spirit watching over the ancient city.
She invoked the aid of the tutelar deities before the ceremony began.
The order was established to serve a tutelar function for orphaned children.

Synset Examples

daycare that is educational and not just custodial
tutelary gods

Related Terms

  • Synonym: custodial - relating to guardianship or protection
  • Synonym: protective - intended to protect someone or something

Etymology

From Latin tutelaris (pertaining to guardianship), from tutela (protection, guardianship).
