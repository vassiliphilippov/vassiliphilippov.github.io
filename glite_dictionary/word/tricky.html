tricky

1. ADJ

/ ˈtrɪki / (AmE), / ˈtrɪki / (BrE)
requiring care and skill because of hidden difficulties; difficult to deal with or handle
difficult; requiring special care or skill

Examples

Solving this puzzle is really tricky because it requires so much attention to detail.
The instructions looked simple, but assembling the furniture turned out to be tricky.
Managing multiple projects at once can be tricky if you're not well organized.

Synset Examples

a catchy question
a tricky recipe to follow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: difficult - requiring much effort or skill; not easy to do or achieve
  • Antonym: easy - not difficult; done or achieved without effort

Etymology

From trick + -y, first recorded in the 17th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˈtrɪki / (AmE), / ˈtrɪki / (BrE)
Deceitful or not to be trusted; likely to cheat or mislead.
not to be trusted; deceitful

Examples

You should be careful—he is a tricky businessman who bends the truth.
The contract was written by some tricky lawyers who added hidden fees.
It was a tricky operation that relied on deception and cunning.

Synset Examples

how extraordinarily slippery a liar the camera is

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dishonest - not honest; likely to cheat or lie
  • Antonym: trustworthy - reliable and can be trusted

Etymology

From English 'trick' + '-y'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02475957-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈtrɪki / (AmE), / ˈtrɪki / (BrE)
skilled in deception; adept at achieving aims by cunning or sly methods
deceptively clever; cunning

Examples

The tricky salesman convinced many people to buy things they didn't need.
She admired how tricky her opponent was during the debate, always turning questions back around.
He used a tricky move to outwit his rivals and win the game.

Synset Examples

cunning men often pass for wise
a foxy scheme
a slick evasive answer
sly as a fox
tricky Dick
a wily old attorney

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cunning - skilled in achieving one's ends by deceit or evasion
  • Synonym: sly - having or showing a cunning and deceitful nature
  • Antonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and sincere
