lavalava

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɑːvəˈlɑːvə / (AmE), / ˌlævəˈlævə / (BrE)
A garment worn in Polynesia, especially Samoa, consisting of a rectangular piece of cloth wrapped around the waist and worn as a skirt.
Polynesian wraparound skirt

Examples

He wore a colorful lavalava during the traditional Samoan ceremony.
Women in the village often make their own lavalavas with beautiful patterns.
Tourists sometimes buy a lavalava as a souvenir from Samoa.

Etymology

From Samoan lavalava (cloth, clothing).
