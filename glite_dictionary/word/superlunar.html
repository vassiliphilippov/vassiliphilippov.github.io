superlunar

1. ADJ

/ ˌsuː.pɚˈluː.nɚ / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˈluː.nə / (BrE)
Situated or occurring beyond the moon or its orbit around the earth.
beyond the moon or its orbit

Examples

Ancient philosophers believed that superlunar realms were subject to different laws than those on Earth.
The novel describes a superlunar civilization observing humanity from afar.
Scientists once speculated about superlunar objects in the early study of astronomy.

Synset Examples

who can imagine a translunary visitor in Times Square?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sublunar - situated beneath the moon or within its orbit; worldly, earthly
  • Synonym: translunary - existing or occurring beyond the moon; celestial

Etymology

From Latin 'super' (above, beyond) + 'luna' (moon).

2. ADJ

/ ˌsuː.pɚˈluː.nɚ / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˈluː.nə / (BrE)
relating to or situated beyond the moon; unworldly or ethereal, especially in the sense of not belonging to earthly experience
beyond the moon; ethereal, unworldly
archaic

Examples

The poet described her imagination as superlunar, untouched by everyday concerns.
During the ceremony, there was a superlunar atmosphere that seemed to lift everyone above earthly worries.
Some philosophers believed the heavens were composed of a superlunar substance distinct from anything on Earth.

Synset Examples

high translunary dreams

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sublunar - beneath the moon; worldly, mundane
  • Synonym: ethereal - extremely delicate or otherworldly

Etymology

From Latin 'super' (above) + 'lūnāris' (of the moon).
