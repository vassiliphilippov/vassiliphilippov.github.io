knish

1. NOUN

/ kəˈnɪʃ / (AmE), / kəˈnɪʃ / (BrE)
A baked or fried pastry with a filling of potato, meat, cheese, or other ingredients, commonly eaten as a snack.
filled pastry snack (often potato, meat, or cheese)

Examples

He bought a hot knish from the street vendor for lunch.
Potato knishes are a popular choice at Jewish delis in New York.
Grandma always made her own cheese knish for family gatherings.

Etymology

From Yiddish *knish*, from Ukrainian *knysh* (a type of pastry).
