amorphophallus

1. NOUN

/ əˌmɔrfəˈfæləs / (AmE), / əˌmɔːfəˈfaləs / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Amorphophallus, known for its large inflorescence and, in some species, a strong odor resembling rotting flesh.
a plant genus with large, pungent flowers

Examples

The amorphophallus attracted crowds when it bloomed at the botanical garden.
Some species of amorphophallus are cultivated for their unusual flowers and curious scent.
The corpse flower is a famous member of the amorphophallus genus.

Etymology

From Greek 'amorphos' (shapeless) + 'phallos' (phallus), referring to the shape of the flower's spadix.
