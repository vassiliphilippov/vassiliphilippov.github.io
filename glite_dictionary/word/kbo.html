KBO

1. NOUN

/ ˌkeɪ.biːˈoʊ / (AmE), / ˌkeɪ.biːˈəʊ / (BrE)
a minor planet or other small body that orbits the Sun in the Kuiper belt, beyond Neptune
minor planet in the Kuiper belt

Examples

Astronomers recently discovered a new KBO with an unusually elongated orbit.
Pluto was reclassified as a dwarf planet and is now considered a large KBO.
The study of KBOs helps scientists understand the early solar system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kuiper Belt - region of the solar system beyond Neptune containing many small icy bodies
  • Synonym: asteroid - small rocky body orbiting the sun, mainly between Mars and Jupiter

Etymology

Acronym from 'Kuiper Belt Object', named after Dutch-American astronomer Gerard Kuiper.
