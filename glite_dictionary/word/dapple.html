dapple

1. VERB

/ ˈdæp.əl / (AmE), / ˈdæp.əl / (BrE)
to mark or color with spots or patches of a different shade or color
to mark with spots or patches

Examples

Sunlight dappled the forest floor through the branches above.
Her dress was dappled with shades of gold and bronze.
Shadows dapples the path, creating a mosaic of light and dark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mottle - to mark with spots or smears of color
  • Synonym: spot - to mark with small round marks of a different color

Etymology

From Middle English 'daple', meaning spot or patch, of uncertain origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdæpəl / (AmE), / ˈdæpəl / (BrE)
a small area of color that is different from the color around it, often seen as a spot or patch
a small contrasting spot or patch

Examples

The horse’s coat was covered with dapples of grey and white.
In the morning light, dapples of sunlight danced across the forest floor.
A few dapples of color brightened the painter’s otherwise monochrome canvas.

Synset Examples

a bald spot
a leopard's spots
a patch of clouds
patches of thin ice
a fleck of red

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spot - a small round or roundish mark, differing in color or texture
  • Synonym: patch - a small area differing from what's around it

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *dappel* (spot, mark).
