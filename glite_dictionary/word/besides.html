besides

1. ADV

/ bɪˈsaɪdz / (AmE), / bɪˈsaɪdz / (BrE)
used to add another reason or point; in addition; anyway.
in addition; anyway

Examples

I'm not interested in going for a run; besides, it's raining outside.
He didn't want to join us, and besides, he was too busy with work.
The concert was expensive, and besides, it was sold out.

Synset Examples

I don't want to go to a restaurant; besides, we can't afford it
she couldn't shelter behind him all the time and in any case he wasn't always with her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anyway - used to add a point or return to a topic

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old English 'be sidan' (by the side).

2. ADV

/ bɪˈsaɪdz / (AmE), / bɪˈsaɪdz / (BrE)
In addition; as well; apart from the thing mentioned.
in addition; as well

Examples

She enjoys hiking and, besides, she volunteers at the local shelter.
Nobody wanted to leave early; besides, it was still raining outside.
The project is challenging and, besides, it pays well.

Synset Examples

he has a Mercedes, too

Related Terms

  • Synonym: also - in addition; too
  • Synonym: moreover - as a further matter; besides
  • Antonym: however - in contrast; on the other hand

Etymology

From Middle English, contraction of "by" + "sides".

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00048072-r
Synset POS: r
