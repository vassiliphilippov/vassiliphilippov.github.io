snakeblenny

1. NOUN

/ ˈsneɪkˌblɛni / (AmE), / ˈsneɪkˌblɛni / (BrE)
a small, elongated fish of cold northern waters, especially the Arctic and northern Atlantic, known for its snake-like appearance
a long, slender fish of cold northern waters

Examples

The snakeblenny is well adapted to the cold Arctic waters it inhabits.
Researchers found a snakeblenny swimming among the rocks near the coast.
Fishermen sometimes catch a snakeblenny by accident while searching for other species.

Etymology

Formed from 'snake' + 'blenny' due to its appearance resembling a snake and its belonging to the blenny family.
