Robinia viscosa

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈbɪniə vɪsˈkoʊsə / (AmE), / rəʊˈbɪniə vɪsˈkəʊsə / (BrE)
A small rough-barked tree native to the southeastern United States, with sticky branches and seeds and clusters of pink flowers; commonly called clammy locust.
species of locust tree with sticky branches

Examples

The Robinia viscosa in my garden attracts many bees with its pink flowers each spring.
Horticulturists recommend Robinia viscosa for difficult soils due to its adaptability and hardiness.
Sticky seeds from the Robinia viscosa can adhere to clothing during hikes in the southeastern U.S.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clammy locust - common name for Robinia viscosa

Etymology

From Latin; genus name 'Robinia' after Jean Robin, French botanist, and species name 'viscosa' meaning 'sticky' or 'viscous'.
