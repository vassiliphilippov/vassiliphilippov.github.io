bait

1. NOUN

/ beɪt / (AmE), / beɪt / (BrE)
something that is used to attract or entice someone to do something, often by offering a reward or advantage.
thing used to entice or attract

Examples

The free sample acted as bait to get shoppers into the store.
The politician offered tax cuts as bait for undecided voters.
He realized too late that the promise of instant fame was just bait to exploit his talent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lure - something that tempts or attracts, often deceitfully

Etymology

From Old Norse 'beita' (to cause to bite), related to Old English 'bǣtan'.

2. VERB

/ beɪt / (AmE), / beɪt / (BrE)
to deliberately harass or annoy someone with persistent criticism, teasing, or provocation
harass or provoke persistently

Examples

The tabloids continued to bait the celebrity with rumors and criticism.
He was tired of his classmates who constantly baited him about his accent.
Please stop baiting your sister—it’s clearly hurting her feelings.

Synset Examples

The children teased the new teacher
Don't ride me so hard over my failure
His fellow workers razzed him when he wore a jacket and tie

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taunt - to provoke or challenge someone with insulting remarks
  • Synonym: tease - to make fun of or try to provoke in a playful or unkind way

Etymology

From Old Norse *beita* (to cause to bite), later extended figuratively to provoking or harassing.

3. NOUN

/ beɪt / (AmE), / beɪt / (BrE)
food or other material used to attract fish or animals so that they can be caught or trapped.
food or material used to attract prey

Examples

She put fresh bait on the hook before casting her line again.
The hunter set out some bait to attract wild animals to the trap.
Worms are commonly used as bait when fishing in rivers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lure - something used to entice animals or fish to a trap or hook
  • Synonym: decoy - an object used to distract or lure animals into a trap

Etymology

From Old Norse 'beita' (food, bait), related to the verb 'to bite'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02779210-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ beɪt / (AmE), / beɪt / (BrE)
to attack or torment (an animal or person) with dogs or to set dogs upon; to harass with persistent attacks (historical or specialized use)
attack or torment using dogs

Examples

The cruel owner would bait the bear with fierce dogs for entertainment.
Centuries ago, villagers would bait bulls during festival events.
She was horrified to learn about the tradition of baiting animals with packs of dogs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torment - to cause severe physical or mental suffering
  • Synonym: tease - to make fun of or attempt to provoke, often repeatedly

Etymology

From Old Norse *beita* (to cause to bite, to bait), related to Old English *bǣtan*.

5. VERB

/ beɪt / (AmE), / beɪt / (BrE)
to use bait to lure, entice, or entrap someone or something
lure or entice using bait

Examples

He tried to bait the fish with a worm on his hook.
The detectives baited the suspect, hoping he would reveal more information.
She baited the trap with cheese to catch the mouse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lure - to tempt or attract, especially by offering something appealing
  • Synonym: to entice - to attract someone by offering pleasure or advantage
  • Antonym: to repel - to drive back or discourage from approaching

Etymology

From Old Norse *beita* (to cause to bite).
