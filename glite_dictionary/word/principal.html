principal

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪnsəpəl / (AmE), / ˈprɪnsɪpəl / (BrE)
the original sum of money borrowed or invested, on which interest is calculated
original sum of a loan or investment

Examples

You must repay the principal plus any accumulated interest by next year.
The principal on the mortgage is $200,000, which is separate from the interest.
If you pay more than the minimum, the extra goes toward the principal.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: interest - money paid regularly at a particular rate for the use of borrowed money
  • Synonym: capital - wealth in the form of money or assets

Etymology

From Latin 'principalis' (first, chief), from 'princeps' (principal, leader).

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɪnsəpəl / (AmE), / ˈprɪnsəpəl / (BrE)
the main participant in a financial transaction, especially one who buys or sells securities for their own account rather than for clients
main party in financial transaction

Examples

As a principal, the firm is trading securities for its own portfolio rather than on behalf of clients.
The broker acted as a principal in the stock transaction, assuming full risk and reward.
When buying equities, an individual investor can be classified as either an agent or a principal.

Etymology

From Latin *principalis* (first in importance), from *princeps* (chief, leader).

4. NOUN

/ ˈprɪnsəpəl / (AmE), / ˈprɪnsɪpəl / (BrE)
(Law) A person who is directly involved in committing a crime, regardless of whether they benefit from it.
person directly involved in a crime (legal)
legal

Examples

The court found that she was a principal in the burglary, not merely an accomplice.
Under criminal law, a principal is considered equally responsible for the offense regardless of profit.
Both the principal and the accessory faced criminal charges after the theft.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accomplice - a person who helps another commit a crime

Etymology

From Latin principalis (chief, primary), from principium (beginning, origin).
