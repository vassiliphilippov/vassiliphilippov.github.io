Asclepias

1. NOUN

/ əˈskliːpiəs / (AmE), / æsˈkliːpɪas / (BrE)
A genus of chiefly North American perennial herbs known as milkweeds, with milky sap and clusters of small flowers.
genus of milkweed plants

Examples

The monarch butterfly relies on Asclepias as a crucial host plant for its larvae.
Gardeners often plant Asclepias to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
Several species of Asclepias are valued for their ornamental flowers and ecological importance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milkweed - common name for plants in the genus Asclepias

Etymology

From Greek Asklēpias (Ασκληπιάς), named after Asclepius, the Greek god of healing.

2. NOUN

/ əˈskliːpiəs / (AmE), / æsˈkliːpiəs / (BrE)
a genus of chiefly North American perennial herbs, commonly known as milkweeds, including plants with milky sap and clusters of small flowers
genus of North American milkweeds

Examples

Many species in the genus Asclepias provide crucial habitat for monarch butterflies.
The botanist identified the tall plant as belonging to the genus Asclepias.
Some native prairies are home to several varieties of Asclepias, known for their milky sap.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milkweed - any plant of the genus Asclepias

Etymology

From Latin Asclepias, named after Asclepius, the Greek god of medicine.
