entrant

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛntrənt / (AmE), / ˈɛntrənt / (BrE)
a person who enters a competition or contest
person entering a competition

Examples

Each entrant must fill out the registration form before the race.
The judges were impressed by the skill of every entrant in the art competition.
Only one entrant from each school is allowed to participate in this category.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: competitor - someone who takes part in a contest or competition

Etymology

From French *entrant*, present participle of *entrer* (to enter), from Latin *intrare*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛntrənt / (AmE), / ˈɛntrənt / (BrE)
a new product or commodity that enters a market or competition, especially one competing with those already established
new product entering a market

Examples

A new entrant in the smartphone market quickly gained popularity among young professionals.
Many established brands felt threatened by the unique features of the entrant.
The company struggled to survive as an entrant against well-known household names.

Synset Examples

a well publicized entrant is the pocket computer

Related Terms

  • Antonym: incumbent - an established product already in the market

Etymology

From French *entrant*, present participle of *entrer* (to enter), from Latin *intrare*.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɛntrənt / (AmE), / ˈɛntrənt / (BrE)
a person or group that enters or takes part in a competition, activity, or organization, especially for the first time
new participant in an activity or competition

Examples

Each year, a new entrant is crowned champion of the marathon.
The contest is open to any entrant who registers before the deadline.
As a recent entrant to the field, she brought fresh ideas to the team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: participant - a person who takes part in an activity
  • Antonym: veteran - someone with long experience in an activity; opposite of novice or new entrant

Etymology

From Latin intrare (to enter) via French entrant (entering).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10375148-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈɛntrənt / (AmE), / ˈɛntrənt / (BrE)
a person who enters or takes part in a place, competition, or organization
person who enters or joins

Examples

All entrants must fill out an application form before the deadline.
The competition attracted hundreds of young entrants from around the world.
New entrants to the profession are required to undergo training.

Synset Examples

new entrants to the country must go though immigration procedures

Related Terms

  • Synonym: applicant - a person who applies for something, especially a job
  • Synonym: contestant - a person who takes part in a contest or competition

Etymology

From French 'entrant' (entering), present participle of 'entrer', from Latin 'intrare' (to enter).
