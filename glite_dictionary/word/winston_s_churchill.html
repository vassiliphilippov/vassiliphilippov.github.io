Winston S. Churchill

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɪnstən ɛs ˈtʃɜrtʃɪl / (AmE), / ˈwɪnstən ɛs ˈtʃɜːtʃɪl / (BrE)
(1874–1965) British statesman, writer, and prime minister, best known for leading the United Kingdom during World War II and receiving the Nobel Prize for Literature in 1953.
British leader during World War II

Examples

Winston S. Churchill delivered some of the most famous speeches in British history.
Many historians study the leadership style of Winston S. Churchill during World War II.
Winston S. Churchill was awarded the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1953.
