serialisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪriəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɪəriəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of publishing a story, book, or other work in separate parts at regular intervals in a magazine, newspaper, or other medium
publication of a work in installments
british

Examples

The novel quickly gained popularity following its serialisation in the Sunday newspaper.
His latest detective story is currently undergoing serialisation on national radio.
Serialisation of literary works was common in Victorian England.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serialization - publishing a work in parts; American English spelling

Etymology

From serial + -isation; chiefly used in British English, equivalent to American 'serialization'.
