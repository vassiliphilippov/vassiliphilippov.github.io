hymnary

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪmˌnɛri / (AmE), / ˈhɪm.nə.ri / (BrE)
a book containing a collection of hymns, typically used in religious services
book of hymns

Examples

The church introduced a new hymnary during the Sunday service.
She found her favorite hymn on page 47 of the old hymnary.
The choir director ordered updated hymnaries for the congregation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hymnal - a book of hymns or religious songs

Etymology

From Latin 'hymnarius', from 'hymnus' (hymn).
