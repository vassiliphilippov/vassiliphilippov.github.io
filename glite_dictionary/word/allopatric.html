allopatric

1. ADJ

/ ˌæləˈpætrɪk / (AmE), / ˌaləˈpatɹɪk / (BrE)
(of species or populations) occurring in different geographic areas; relating to or denoting groups that are isolated geographically from each other, especially as a mechanism of speciation.
occurring in different geographic areas (biology)
medical

Examples

Many closely related species are allopatric, occupying non-overlapping regions.
The Galápagos finches are a classic example of allopatric speciation.
Researchers found two allopatric populations that rarely interact in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sympatric - (of species or populations) occurring within the same geographic area

Etymology

From Greek 'allos' (other) + 'patra' (fatherland, native country).
