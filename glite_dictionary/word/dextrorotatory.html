dextrorotatory

1. ADJ

/ ˌdɛkstrəˈroʊtəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˌdɛkstrəʊˈrəʊtətəri / (BrE)
(of a substance) turning the plane of polarized light to the right, or clockwise, when viewed in the direction of the incoming light.
rotating polarized light to the right
medical

Examples

Glucose is a dextrorotatory sugar, meaning it rotates plane-polarized light to the right.
The chemist identified the compound as dextrorotatory based on its optical activity.
When testing the solution, the polarimeter showed a dextrorotatory effect.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: levorotatory - rotating plane-polarized light to the left

Etymology

From Latin 'dexter' (right) and 'rotatory' (turning).
