Salamandra

1. PROPN

/ sæləˈmændrə / (AmE), / sæləˈmændrə / (BrE)
A genus of amphibians in the family Salamandridae, including many typical European salamanders.
genus of European salamanders

Examples

The fire salamander is a well-known member of the genus Salamandra.
Many species in the Salamandra genus are native to central and southern Europe.
Researchers discovered a new population of Salamandra in the Carpathian Mountains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salamander - Any tailed amphibian of the order Caudata, related to Salamandra

Etymology

From Latin salamandra, from Greek salamandra (a kind of lizard or salamander).
