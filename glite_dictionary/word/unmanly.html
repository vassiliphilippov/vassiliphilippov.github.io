unmanly

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈmænli / (AmE), / ʌnˈmanli / (BrE)
Not having or showing qualities traditionally regarded as masculine, such as strength or courage.
not having masculine qualities

Examples

He felt his fear was unmanly and tried to hide it from his friends.
Some people still consider crying in public to be unmanly, though attitudes are changing.
Calling him unmanly for refusing to fight is both unfair and outdated.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: manly - having qualities traditionally regarded as masculine
  • Synonym: effeminate - having or displaying characteristics regarded as typical of a woman

Etymology

From un- (not) + manly (having qualities befitting a man).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈmænli / (AmE), / ʌnˈmanli / (BrE)
lacking qualities such as courage, strength, or resolve that are traditionally associated with men; weak or cowardly
lacking courage or strength; cowardly

Examples

His unmanly behavior during the crisis disappointed his friends.
She dismissed his fears as unmanly and urged him to be braver.
The film critic described the hero's retreat as cowardly and unmanly.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: manly - having qualities such as strength and courage traditionally associated with men
  • Synonym: cowardly - lacking courage; easily frightened

Etymology

From un- (not) + manly (having qualities attributed to men).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00267286-s
Synset POS: s
