Odin

1. PROPN

/ ˈoʊ.dɪn / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.dɪn / (BrE)
In Norse mythology, the chief god of the Aesir, associated with war, poetry, knowledge, and wisdom; husband of Frigg, and known for sacrificing one of his eyes for wisdom.
chief god in Norse mythology

Examples

In Norse legends, Odin sacrificed one of his eyes at Mimir's well to gain wisdom.
The followers of Odin believed he watched over them during times of war and hardship.
Many stories tell of Odin traveling the world in disguise to acquire knowledge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Wotan - chief god in Germanic mythology, associated with Odin
  • Synonym: Frigg - wife of Odin in Norse mythology

Etymology

From Old Norse Óðinn, related to Old English Wōden (Woden), both deriving from Proto-Germanic *Wōdanaz.
