semiaquatic

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛmiəˈkwætɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɛmɪəˈkwætɪk / (BrE)
(of an animal) having an aquatic early or larval form and a terrestrial adult form, or generally adapted to living partly on land and partly in water.
living partly in water and partly on land

Examples

Frogs are semiaquatic creatures, spending their early lives in water as tadpoles.
The beaver is well known for its semiaquatic lifestyle, building lodges in rivers and streams.
Some insects, such as dragonflies, are semiaquatic, with aquatic larvae and terrestrial adults.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: aquatic - living or growing in water
  • Antonym: terrestrial - living on or relating to land

Etymology

From Latin 'semi-' (half) + 'aquaticus' (relating to water).

2. ADJ

/ ˌsɛmiəˈkwɑtɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɛmɪəˈkwætɪk / (BrE)
Living or growing partly in water and partly on land.
partly aquatic; adapted to both land and water

Examples

The beaver is a well-known semiaquatic mammal that builds lodges in rivers and streams.
This semiaquatic plant thrives in marshy areas along the edge of the pond.
Many frogs are semiaquatic, spending part of their lives in water and part on land.

Synset Examples

a marginal subaquatic flora

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: aquatic - living or growing in water
  • Degree Lower: terrestrial - living or growing on land

Etymology

From Latin 'semi-' (half) + 'aquaticus' (relating to water).
