planoconvex

1. ADJ

/ ˌplænoʊˈkɑnvɛks / (AmE), / ˌplænəʊˈkɒnvɛks / (BrE)
having one surface that is flat and the other that is convex, typically used to describe lenses or objects in optics
flat on one side, convex on the other

Examples

A planoconvex lens is often used to focus light in optical instruments.
The glass was ground into a planoconvex shape to improve image clarity.
He studied the properties of planoconvex mirrors in physics class.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: biconvex - having both surfaces convex, often used for lenses
  • Antonym: plano-concave - flat on one side and concave on the other

Etymology

From Latin 'planus' (flat) + 'convexus' (arched, vaulted).
