Puerto Rico

1. PROPN

/ ˌpwɛrtoʊ ˈriːkoʊ / (AmE), / ˌpwɜːtəʊ ˈriːkəʊ / (BrE)
An island in the Caribbean that is a self-governing commonwealth in association with the United States.
Caribbean island; US commonwealth

Examples

Many tourists visit Puerto Rico for its beautiful beaches and vibrant culture.
After Hurricane Maria, Puerto Rico received international aid to help with recovery.
She was born in Puerto Rico but moved to the mainland United States as a child.

Etymology

Spanish for 'rich port', from puerto (port) + rico (rich).

2. PROPN

/ ˌpwɛrtoʊ ˈrikoʊ / (AmE), / ˌpwɛːtəʊ ˈriːkəʊ / (BrE)
an island and unincorporated territory of the United States in the Caribbean, and the smallest and easternmost of the Greater Antilles.
an island territory in the Caribbean

Examples

Many tourists visit Puerto Rico every year to enjoy its beaches and culture.
Hurricane Maria caused widespread damage in Puerto Rico in 2017.
Puerto Rico is the easternmost of the Greater Antilles in the Caribbean Sea.

Etymology

Spanish for 'rich port'.
