quirk

1. VERB

/ kwɝk / (AmE), / kwɜːk / (BrE)
to twist or move (especially a part of the body) suddenly or in a peculiar way
move or twist abruptly

Examples

He quirked an eyebrow in surprise at her suggestion.
She quirked her lips into a wry smile.
The puppy quirked its head to one side, watching him curiously.

Synset Examples

She quirked her head in a peculiar way

2. NOUN

/ kwɝk / (AmE), / kwɜːk / (BrE)
A narrow groove or channel, especially one formed beside a beading in woodwork or architecture.
narrow groove beside a beading

Examples

The cabinet doors were decorated with a delicate quirk running along each edge.
Our carpenter added a quirk to the moulding to give it more visual depth.
A classic column often features a quirk at the junction of the shaft and the base.
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04041873-n

3. NOUN

/ kwɝk / (AmE), / kwɜːk / (BrE)
a peculiar or unusual habit or aspect of a person's behavior; an idiosyncrasy or odd trait
a peculiar habit or trait

Examples

Her most endearing quirk is talking to her plants every morning.
Everyone has a quirk or two that makes them unique.
His habit of tapping his foot constantly is just one quirk of his personality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idiosyncrasy - a mode of behavior or way of thought peculiar to an individual
  • Synonym: eccentricity - an unusual or odd behavior or habit
  • Antonym: normality - the state of being usual, typical, or expected

Etymology

Perhaps from German Querkopf (cross-headed, eccentric person), or from obsolete quirk (trick, quibble).
