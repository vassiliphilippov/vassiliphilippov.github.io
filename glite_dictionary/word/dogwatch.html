dogwatch

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɔɡˌwɑtʃ / (AmE), / ˈdɒɡˌwɒtʃ / (BrE)
either of two short periods standing watch on a ship, usually from 4–6 pm or 6–8 pm
short ship's watch (4–6 or 6–8 pm)

Examples

The crew changed shifts at the beginning of the dogwatch.
He was assigned the first dogwatch and had to be on deck by 4 pm.
During the dogwatch, sailors often take a quick meal break.

Etymology

From nautical slang, perhaps from division of the traditional 'watch' or from Dutch 'dodenwacht' (dead man's watch); exact origin uncertain.
