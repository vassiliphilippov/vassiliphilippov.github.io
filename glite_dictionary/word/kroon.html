kroon

1. NOUN

/ kroʊn / (AmE), / kruːn / (BrE)
the former basic unit of currency in Estonia, replaced by the euro in 2011
former unit of currency in Estonia

Examples

The Estonian kroon was replaced by the euro in 2011.
I still have some old kroon notes from my trip to Tallinn.
Prices in shops were once listed in kroon before Estonia joined the Eurozone.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: euro - the official currency of the Eurozone, including Estonia since 2011

Etymology

From Estonian kroon (crown), from German Krone, ultimately from Latin corona (crown).
