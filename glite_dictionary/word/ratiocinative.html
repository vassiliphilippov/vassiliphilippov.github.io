ratiocinative

1. ADJ

/ ˌrætiˈɑːsəˌneɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˌrætiˈɒsɪnətɪv / (BrE)
Characterized by or based on exact or logical reasoning.
based on logical reasoning

Examples

Her ratiocinative approach to the problem impressed the panel of judges.
The detective admired his colleague's ratiocinative abilities during the complicated investigation.
A good scientist relies on ratiocinative methods rather than intuition alone.

Synset Examples

one's ratiocinative powers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: logical - relating to clear, sound reasoning

Etymology

From Latin ratiocinari (to reckon, reason) + -ive.
