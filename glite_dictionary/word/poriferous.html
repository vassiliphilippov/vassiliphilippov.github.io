poriferous

1. ADJ

/ pɔˈrɪfərəs / (AmE), / pɔːˈrɪfərəs / (BrE)
Having or containing pores, holes, or vessels; full of small openings.
full of pores or holes

Examples

The poriferous nature of the rock allows water to seep through easily.
Scientists studied the poriferous structure of sponges under a microscope.
A poriferous membrane can be used in various filtration systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porous - having many small holes that allow air or liquid to pass through

Etymology

From Latin 'porus' (pore) + '-ferous' (bearing).
