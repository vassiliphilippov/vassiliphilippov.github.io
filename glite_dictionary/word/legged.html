legged

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɛɡɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɛɡɪd / (BrE)
having legs of a specified kind or number, often used in combination (e.g., four-legged, long-legged)
having legs (used in compounds)

Examples

Many insects are described as six-legged creatures.
The restaurant had sturdy wooden three-legged stools for customers.
The long-legged bird waded through the shallow water.

Synset Examples

four-legged animals
a peg-legged man

Related Terms

  • Antonym: legless - having no legs
  • Synonym: footed - having feet of a specified kind or number
