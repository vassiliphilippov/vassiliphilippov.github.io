glutethimide

1. NOUN

/ ɡluˈtɛθɪˌmaɪd / (AmE), / ɡluːˈtiːθɪˌmaɪd / (BrE)
A sedative drug formerly used to treat sleep disorders, known by the trade name Doriden.
sedative drug for sleep disorders
medical

Examples

The doctor no longer prescribes glutethimide due to its side effects.
Researchers are studying the interaction of glutethimide with other medications.
Abuse of glutethimide led to its withdrawal in many countries.
