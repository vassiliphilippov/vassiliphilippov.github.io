equator

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈkweɪt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ɪˈkweɪtə / (BrE)
An imaginary line around the middle of the Earth, equidistant from the North and South Poles, dividing the Earth into the Northern and Southern Hemispheres.
imaginary line dividing Earth into hemispheres

Examples

The climate near the equator is typically hot and humid throughout the year.
Quito, in Ecuador, is one of the few capitals located almost directly on the equator.
Day and night are nearly the same length all year along the equator.

Synset Examples

the equator is the boundary between the northern and Southern Hemispheres

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: tropic - either of the two lines (Tropic of Cancer, Tropic of Capricorn) north and south of the equator
  • Antonym: pole - either of the two points (North Pole, South Pole) farthest from the equator on the globe

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'aequator' meaning 'equalizer', from Latin 'aequare' (to make equal).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈkweɪtər / (AmE), / ɪˈkweɪtə / (BrE)
An imaginary line around the middle of a sphere or planet, such as the Earth, dividing it into two equal and symmetrical parts.
imaginary dividing line around the middle of a sphere

Examples

The Earth's equator divides the planet into the Northern and Southern Hemispheres.
When drawing a sphere, the equator is often marked as a reference line.
Satellites in geostationary orbit move above the equator at a constant speed.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: meridian - an imaginary line, especially on the Earth's surface, from pole to pole

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'aequator' (equalizer), from Latin 'aequare' (to make equal).
