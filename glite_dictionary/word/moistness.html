moistness

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔɪst.nəs / (AmE), / ˈmɔɪst.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being slightly wet or damp
slight wetness or dampness

Examples

The moistness in the air made the room feel warmer than it actually was.
Check the cake for moistness to ensure it's not overbaked.
She brushed the leaves, feeling their cool moistness after the rain.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dryness - the state of being dry

Etymology

From 'moist' + '-ness'; 'moist' from Old French 'moiste' (damp, wet), from Latin 'muscidus' (musty, moldy).
