skit

1. NOUN

/ skɪt / (AmE), / skɪt / (BrE)
a short, informal performance or dramatic piece, often humorous, typically performed by a small group of people
short, informal dramatic performance

Examples

The students performed a hilarious skit about life in the library.
We wrote a short skit for the school talent show.
Their comedy group is known for clever political skits on television.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sketch - a short, humorous scene or play

Etymology

Possibly a variant of 'sketch', origin uncertain, first recorded in the 18th century.
