fictional

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɪkʃənl̩ / (AmE), / ˈfɪkʃənəl / (BrE)
related to or existing only in fiction; invented or imagined rather than real
imaginary; related to fiction

Examples

Many of the characters in this novel are entirely fictional and bear no resemblance to real people.
The movie is set in a fictional town in the American Midwest.
She prefers to read about fictional worlds instead of true stories.

Synset Examples

clever fictional devices
a fictional treatment of the train robbery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imaginary - existing only in the imagination
  • Antonym: real - actually existing, not imagined or invented

Etymology

From "fiction" + suffix -al, patterned after similar adjective forms.

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɪkʃənl / (AmE), / ˈfɪkʃənəl / (BrE)
invented for stories or not real; existing only in the imagination, especially in literature, film, or art
invented; not real; imaginary (esp. in stories)

Examples

The movie is based on a fictional city inspired by several real-world locations.
She prefers reading about fictional detectives rather than real-life cases.
That story about the talking cat is entirely fictional.

Synset Examples

a fabricated excuse for his absence
a fancied wrong
a fictional character

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imaginary - existing only in the mind; not real
  • Antonym: real - actually existing or happening; not imagined or supposed

Etymology

From 'fiction' + '-al', with 'fiction' from Latin 'fictio' (a fashioning, feigning).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01942527-s
Synset POS: s
