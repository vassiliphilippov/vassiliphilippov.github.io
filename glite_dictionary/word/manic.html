manic

1. ADJ

/ ˈmænɪk / (AmE), / ˈmænɪk / (BrE)
Showing wild, frenzied energy or activity, often as a result of mania or extreme excitement.
showing frenzied or manic energy

Examples

She cleaned the whole house with manic energy after hearing the good news.
He worked at a manic pace to meet the deadline.
The comedian's manic style kept the audience laughing throughout the show.

Synset Examples

a frenzied attack
a frenzied mob
the prosecutor's frenzied denunciation of the accused
outbursts of drunken violence and manic activity and creativity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frantic - in a state of wild panic or excitement
  • Antonym: sedate - calm, unhurried, or composed

Etymology

From Greek mania (madness, frenzy), via Late Latin maniacus.
