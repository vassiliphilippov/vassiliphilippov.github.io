rock-bottom

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɑkˌbɑtəm / (AmE), / ˈrɒkˌbɒtəm / (BrE)
Extremely low in amount, price, or level; as low as possible.
extremely low amount or price

Examples

Prices for winter coats are at rock-bottom during the summer sales.
The company was able to purchase the supplies at rock-bottom rates.
Due to the economic crisis, many used cars are now selling for rock-bottom prices.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: low - of less than average height or level
  • Synonym: bargain-basement - very low in price or cost

Etymology

From 'rock' (stone) + 'bottom'; originally used to describe the very lowest point, as if reaching the bedrock.
