joule

1. NOUN

/ dʒul / (AmE), / dʒuːl / (BrE)
a unit of energy equal to the work done when a force of one newton moves an object one metre; also used as a unit of electrical energy
unit of energy or work (SI)

Examples

The energy required to lift an apple one meter is about one joule.
Scientists measured the energy output of the reaction in joules.
One joule is equivalent to one watt-second in electrical terms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watt - unit of power in the SI system
  • Synonym: calorie - non-SI unit of energy commonly used in nutrition

Etymology

Named after James Prescott Joule (1818–1889), English physicist.
