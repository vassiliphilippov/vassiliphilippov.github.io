Achoerodus gouldii

1. NOUN

/ əˈkoʊ.rəˌdəs ˈɡuːl.di.aɪ / (AmE), / əˈkɒ.rəˌdəs ˈɡuːl.dɪ.aɪ / (BrE)
a large wrasse species found in the waters around the Great Barrier Reef and southern Australia, known for its blue coloring
a large blue wrasse fish species

Examples

The Achoerodus gouldii is commonly known as the Western Blue Groper in Australia.
Divers often spot Achoerodus gouldii in kelp beds along the southern Australian coast.
Conservation efforts are in place to protect populations of Achoerodus gouldii.

Etymology

From Greek 'achoerodus' (without pig-tooth) and honoring naturalist John Gould.
