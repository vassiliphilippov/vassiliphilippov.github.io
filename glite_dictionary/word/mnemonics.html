mnemonics

1. NOUN

/ nəˈmɑnɪks / (AmE), / nɪˈmɒnɪks / (BrE)
a method or system of improving memory, often using patterns, associations, or aids to help remember information
technique to improve memory

Examples

She used mnemonics to remember the order of the planets for her astronomy exam.
Teachers often teach children mnemonics to help them learn spelling rules.
Medical students rely heavily on mnemonics to memorize complex anatomical terms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: memory aid - a tool or device that helps someone remember information
  • Antonym: forgetfulness - the quality of being unable to remember

Etymology

From Greek mnēmonikos (mindful), from mnēmōn (mindful, remembering).
