Ceratitis

1. NOUN

/ sɛˈræ.tɪ.tɪs / (AmE), / sɛˈræ.tɪ.tɪs / (BrE)
A genus of fruit flies that includes the Mediterranean fruit fly, species known for damaging fruit crops.
genus of fruit flies

Examples

The Ceratitis genus includes species responsible for widespread damage to fruit crops.
Scientists are studying Ceratitis to develop better pest control methods.
Infestation by Ceratitis can lead to severe economic losses for farmers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mediterranean fruit fly - a species of fruit fly that is a major agricultural pest

Etymology

From Greek 'keras' (horn), referring to the horn-like processes on the head of some species.
