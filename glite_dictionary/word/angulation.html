angulation

1. NOUN

/ ˌæŋɡjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌaŋɡjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of forming an angle or angles, or of bending so as to create an angle
formation of an angle or angles

Examples

The angulation of the joint allowed for greater flexibility in movement.
Proper angulation of the camera lens is essential for capturing the perfect shot.
Abnormal angulation of bones can indicate a fracture or deformity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angle - the space between two intersecting lines or surfaces
  • Antonym: curvature - the state of being curved or bent

Etymology

From Latin *angulus* (angle) + English suffix -ation.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæŋɡjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌaŋɡjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or result of measuring or forming an angle, especially with precision; the degree of deviation from a straight line
precise measurement or formation of angles

Examples

Precise angulation of the X-ray beam is required for this dental procedure.
The angulation of the bones was assessed to plan corrective surgery.
The architect adjusted the angulation of the windows to maximize natural light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angle - the space between two intersecting lines or surfaces

Etymology

From Latin 'angulus' (angle) + '-ation' (action or process).
