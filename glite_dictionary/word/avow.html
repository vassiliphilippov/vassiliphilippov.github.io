to avow

1. VERB

/ əˈvaʊ / (AmE), / əˈvaʊ / (BrE)
to declare or assert something solemnly and openly as true, often in a formal or public context
declare or affirm solemnly as true

Examples

During the trial, he avowed his innocence before the entire court.
She avowed that she had no knowledge of the missing documents.
The leader publicly avowed his support for democratic principles.

Synset Examples

Before God I swear I am innocent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to affirm - to state something firmly as true
  • Antonym: to deny - to state that something is not true

Etymology

From Old French 'avouer', from Latin 'advocare' (to call to witness).

2. VERB

/ əˈvaʊ / (AmE), / əˈvaʊ / (BrE)
to state or admit something openly and publicly; to declare frankly or boldly
to assert or admit openly

Examples

She finally avowed her support for the new policy in front of the entire staff.
He did not hesitate to avow his love for her, even in public.
The politician was forced to avow his involvement in the scandal after evidence emerged.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confess - to admit to something, especially a fault or wrongdoing
  • Antonym: to deny - to state that something is not true

Etymology

From Old French avouer, from Latin *advocare* (to call upon), later developing the sense of 'acknowledge' or 'declare'.
