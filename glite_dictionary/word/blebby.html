blebby

1. ADJ

/ ˈblɛbi / (AmE), / ˈblɛbi / (BrE)
Covered with small blisters or bubble-like swellings.
covered with small blisters
medical

Examples

The patient's skin appeared blebby after exposure to the harsh chemical.
Under the microscope, the surface of the mineral showed a distinctly blebby texture.
Some marine organisms develop a blebby appearance as part of their natural defense mechanisms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blistered - covered with blisters
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even surface without blisters

Etymology

From 'bleb' (a small blister or bubble) + '-y' (adjectival suffix).

2. ADJ

/ ˈblɛbi / (AmE), / ˈblɛbi / (BrE)
(of glass or mineral) containing or marked by small bubbles or particles of foreign material.
containing small bubbles or particles (glass, minerals)

Examples

The geologist described the mineral sample as particularly blebby, due to the numerous tiny bubbles trapped inside.
A blebby appearance in glass can affect its clarity and overall strength.
Manufacturers aim to minimize blebby defects when producing high-quality quartz for electronics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bubbly - full of bubbles; containing bubbles
