Cheiranthus allionii

1. NOUN

/ ˌkaɪəˈrænθəs ˌæliˈoʊnaɪ / (AmE), / ˌkaɪəˈrænθəs ˌæliˈəʊniːaɪ / (BrE)
A showy erect biennial or short-lived perennial plant cultivated for its striking orange-yellow flowers, sometimes placed in the genus Cheiranthus.
a plant with orange-yellow flowers

Examples

The gardener planted Cheiranthus allionii along the walkway for its vibrant color.
Botanists often debate whether Cheiranthus allionii should be placed in a different genus.
Every summer, the bright blooms of Cheiranthus allionii attract butterflies to the garden.
