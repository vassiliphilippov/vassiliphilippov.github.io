avower

1. NOUN

/ əˈvaʊ.ər / (AmE), / əˈvaʊ.ə / (BrE)
a person who openly admits or declares something, especially boldly or without shame
person who openly admits or declares

Examples

The avower of his beliefs stood confidently before the crowd.
As an avower of unpopular views, she faced frequent criticism.
In court, the avower of the statement refused to retract his claim.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: admitter - a person who admits something is true

Etymology

From Old French 'avouer', based on Latin 'advocare' (to call to, summon).

2. NOUN

/ əˈvaʊ.ər / (AmE), / əˈvaʊ.ə / (BrE)
a person who openly declares or asserts something, especially the truth or their beliefs
a person who openly declares something

Examples

The avower of the policy spoke passionately at the meeting.
She described herself as an avower of honesty in all matters.
Despite the controversy, he remained an avower of his religious convictions.

Synset Examples

a bold asserter
a declarer of his intentions
affirmers of traditional doctrine
an asseverator of strong convictions
an avower of his own great intelligence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: declarer - a person who declares or states something
  • Antonym: denier - a person who denies something

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'avow' and the suffix '-er', meaning 'one who avows'; 'avow' from Old French 'avouer', from Latin 'advocare' (to call to).
