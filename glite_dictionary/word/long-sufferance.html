long-sufferance

1. NOUN

/ lɔŋˈsʌf.ɚ.əns / (AmE), / lɒŋˈsʌf.ər.əns / (BrE)
patient endurance of pain, suffering, or unhappiness over a prolonged period.
patient endurance of suffering

Examples

Her long-sufferance during years of hardship inspired those around her.
Acts of long-sufferance are often praised in classic literature.
His long-sufferance in the face of injustice became legendary among his peers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: long-suffering - having or showing patience in enduring pain or trouble

Etymology

Patterned after 'long-suffering', from 'long' + 'sufferance' (Middle English, from Old French, from Latin 'sufferentia').
