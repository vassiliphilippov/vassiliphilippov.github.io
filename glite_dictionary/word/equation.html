equation

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈkweɪʒən / (AmE), / ɪˈkweɪʃən / (BrE)
A statement in mathematics that shows that two expressions are equal, usually written with an equals sign (=) between them.
mathematical statement of equality

Examples

The teacher asked the students to solve the equation on the board.
Balancing a chemical equation is an important skill in chemistry class.
A simple equation like 2 + 3 = 5 helps children understand equality.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inequality - a mathematical statement that two expressions are not equal

Etymology

From Latin 'aequatio' (a making equal), from 'aequare' (to make equal).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈkweɪʒən / (AmE), / ɪˈkweɪʒ(ə)n / (BrE)
A state of being equal or equivalent; a condition in which different elements are equally balanced.
state of being equal or equivalent

Examples

The new policy aims to create an equation between work and family life.
There must be an equation among all departments for the project to succeed.
Seeking equation between quality and cost is a challenge for manufacturers.

Synset Examples

on a par with the best

Related Terms

  • Synonym: equality - the state of being equal, especially in status, rights, or opportunities
  • Antonym: imbalance - a lack of balance or symmetry

Etymology

From Latin aequatio (a making equal), from aequare (to make equal), from aequus (equal).

3. NOUN

/ ɪˈkweɪʒən / (AmE), / ɪˈkweɪʒən / (BrE)
the act of considering or treating things as equal or equivalent
act of regarding as equal

Examples

The equation of wealth with happiness is a common but mistaken belief.
Her argument relies on the equation of truth and opinion, which can be problematic.
The equation of these two concepts oversimplifies a complex issue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: identification - the act of treating or considering as the same
  • Antonym: differentiation - the act of distinguishing or making different

Etymology

From Latin 'aequationem', from 'aequare' (to make equal).
