Unitarianism

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuːnɪˈtɛriənɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪˈteəriənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A liberal religious movement that emphasizes individual freedom of belief, rejects the doctrine of the Trinity, and encourages reason and tolerance in matters of faith.
liberal religion rejecting the Trinity

Examples

The teachings of Unitarianism emphasize individual search for truth and meaning.
Many found Unitarianism appealing because of its open attitude towards different religious perspectives.
Modern Unitarianism often promotes social justice and inclusivity within its congregations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Trinitarianism - Christian doctrine affirming the Trinity (Father, Son, Holy Spirit) as one God in three persons
  • Synonym: Unitarian - A member or supporter of Unitarianism

Etymology

From 'Unitarian' + '-ism', originally indicating worship of a single unity, as opposed to the Christian doctrine of the Trinity.
