Suricata suricatta

1. NOUN

/ sʊˌrɪkətə sʊˈrɪkətə / (AmE), / sjuːˌrɪkətə sjuːˈrɪkətə / (BrE)
The scientific name for the meerkat, a small mongoose native to southern Africa, known for its slender body and elongated tail.
scientific name for meerkat

Examples

In the wild, Suricata suricatta lives in large social groups called mobs or gangs.
The behavior of Suricata suricatta has been studied extensively by zoologists.
A conservation program recently reintroduced Suricata suricatta to its native habitat in southern Africa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meerkat - a small burrowing mongoose native to southern Africa

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, following taxonomic conventions; 'Suricata' adopted from French 'suricate' (meerkat), ultimately of unknown origin.
