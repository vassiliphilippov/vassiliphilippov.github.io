carat

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛrət / (AmE), / ˈkærət / (BrE)
A unit of weight for precious stones, equal to 200 milligrams.
unit of weight for gems (200 mg)

Examples

The diamond weighed two carats and sparkled brilliantly in the sunlight.
This necklace features a pendant set with a one-carat ruby.
They bought her an engagement ring with a half-carat diamond.

Etymology

From French 'carat', from Italian 'carato', from Arabic 'qīrāṭ', from Greek 'kerátion' (carob seed, used as a standard of weight).

2. NOUN

/ ˈker.ət / (AmE), / ˈkær.ət / (BrE)
a unit used to measure the proportion of pure gold in an alloy, with 24 carats representing pure gold
unit of gold purity

Examples

The ring was made of 18-carat gold, giving it a rich yellow color.
Only pure gold is classified as 24-carat in the jewelry industry.
Prices increase with the carat value of the gold used.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: karat - alternate US spelling for the same unit of gold purity

Etymology

From Italian *carato* and Arabic *qīrāṭ*, ultimately from Greek *kerátion* (carob seed, a unit of weight).
