star-crossed

1. ADJ

/ stɑr krɔst / (AmE), / stɑː krɒst / (BrE)
(of a person or relationship, especially lovers) thwarted or doomed by fate; ill-fated.
ill-fated by destiny, especially of lovers

Examples

The young couple were star-crossed and faced obstacles at every turn.
Many tragic romances feature star-crossed lovers separated by circumstance.
Literature often portrays heroes as star-crossed individuals struggling against fate.

Synset Examples

The play tells the story of star-crossed teens in love.
Their star-crossed fate led to a tragic end.
They were star-crossed lovers, destined to be apart.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ill-fated - destined to fail or suffer misfortune
  • Antonym: fortunate - receiving good luck or favorable outcomes

Etymology

Popularised by Shakespeare's 'Romeo and Juliet'; from the notion that the stars (astrological fate) adversely influence one's destiny.
