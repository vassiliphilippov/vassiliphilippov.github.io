Connecticut

1. PROPN

/ kəˈnɛtɪkət / (AmE), / kəˈnɛtɪkət / (BrE)
A state in the northeastern United States, part of New England and one of the original thirteen colonies.
a northeastern US state; original colony

Examples

We traveled to Connecticut for a summer vacation last year.
Yale University is located in Connecticut.
Many colonial towns can be found throughout Connecticut.

Etymology

From the Mohegan-Pequot word 'quinetucket', meaning 'long tidal river'.

3. PROPN

/ kəˈnɛtɪkət / (AmE), / kəˈnɛtɪkət / (BrE)
a major river in the northeastern United States, flowing south from northern New Hampshire, along the border between New Hampshire and Vermont, and through Massachusetts and Connecticut, emptying into Long Island Sound.
major river in the northeastern US

Examples

The Connecticut River is the longest river in New England.
Many towns along the Connecticut developed because of its importance for shipping and trade.
Every spring, the Connecticut overflows its banks, nourishing the surrounding farmland.

Etymology

From the Mohegan-Pequot word *quinetucket*, meaning 'long tidal river'.
