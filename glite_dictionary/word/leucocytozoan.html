leucocytozoan

1. NOUN

/ luː.kəˌsaɪtəˈzoʊ.ən / (AmE), / luː.kəˌsɪtəʊˈzəʊ.ən / (BrE)
A parasitic protozoan organism that infects the blood cells of birds.
parasitic protozoan in birds
medical

Examples

Researchers discovered a new species of leucocytozoan infecting migratory songbirds in the Amazon rainforest.
The presence of leucocytozoan parasites can weaken birds and affect their survival rates.
Veterinarians study leucocytozoan infections to better understand avian diseases.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'leucocyte' (white blood cell) and '-zoan' (animal).
