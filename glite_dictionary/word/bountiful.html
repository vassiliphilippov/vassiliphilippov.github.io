bountiful

1. ADJ

/ ˈbaʊn.tɪ.fəl / (AmE), / ˈbaʊn.tɪ.fəl / (BrE)
Producing or providing in large amounts; abundant or generous.
abundant; producing in large amounts

Examples

The farmers celebrated a bountiful harvest after a season of heavy rains.
Her garden is always bountiful, filled with vegetables and flowers throughout the summer.
We were grateful for the bountiful meal that was prepared for the holiday.

Synset Examples

the bountiful earth
a plentiful year

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plentiful - existing or produced in large quantities
  • Antonym: scarce - insufficient for the demand; in short supply

Etymology

From Old French 'bountif', from 'bonté' meaning 'goodness', related to Latin 'bonitas'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbaʊntɪfəl / (AmE), / ˈbaʊntɪfəl / (BrE)
Giving generously or in large amounts; showing great generosity.
giving generously; generous

Examples

He is known for his bountiful donations to local charities every year.
The bountiful host made sure everyone left with plenty of food.
Her bountiful compliments boosted the team's morale.

Synset Examples

was a big tipper
the bounteous goodness of God
bountiful compliments
a freehanded host
a handsome allowance
Saturday's child is loving and giving
a liberal backer of the arts
a munificent gift
her fond and openhanded grandfather

Related Terms

  • Synonym: generous - willing to give more of something than is strictly necessary or expected
  • Antonym: stingy - unwilling to give or spend; ungenerous

Etymology

From Old French *bontif* (full of goodness) + English suffix *-ful*.
