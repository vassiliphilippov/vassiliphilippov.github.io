Clupea sprattus

1. NOUN

/ kluˈpiːə ˈsprætəs / (AmE), / kluːˈpiːə ˈsprætəs / (BrE)
a small, herring-like fish of European waters, often canned and eaten like a sardine; also known as European sprat
a small herring-like fish (European sprat)

Examples

The can contained Clupea sprattus packed in sunflower oil.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of Clupea sprattus in the Baltic Sea.
Many European recipes use Clupea sprattus as a savory ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sprat - small, herring-like fish found in European waters
  • Synonym: sardine - small, oily fish often canned for food
  • Synonym: herring - a common name for various species of small, silvery fish

Etymology

From Latin: Clupea (genus for herrings) + sprattus (species name for sprat).
