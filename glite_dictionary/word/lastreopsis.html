Lastreopsis

1. NOUN

/ læˈstriːɒpsɪs / (AmE), / læˈstriːɒpsɪs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical terrestrial shield ferns found mainly in the Southern Hemisphere.
a genus of tropical shield ferns

Examples

Botanists discovered a new species of Lastreopsis in the rainforest.
Many Lastreopsis ferns thrive in damp, shaded environments.
The delicate fronds of Lastreopsis are a hallmark of subtropical forests.

Etymology

From Greek roots: 'lastrion' (shield fern) and '-opsis' (appearance).

2. NOUN

/ læsˈtriːɒpsɪs / (AmE), / læˈstriːɒpsɪs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical terrestrial shield ferns found primarily in the Southern Hemisphere.
a genus of tropical shield ferns

Examples

Some species of Lastreopsis are commonly found in rainforests throughout Australia and New Zealand.
The Lastreopsis genus is known for its delicate fronds and distinctive shield-shaped sori.
Botanists conducted extensive studies on Lastreopsis to better understand fern evolution in the Southern Hemisphere.

Etymology

From Greek 'lastrion' (fern) and 'opsis' (appearance), indicating resemblance to ferns.
