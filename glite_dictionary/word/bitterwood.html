bitterwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪt̬ɚˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈbɪtəˌwʊd / (BrE)
A medium to large tropical American tree with odd-pinnate leaves, small pale yellow flowers in long clusters, and scarlet fruits, known for its bitter-tasting wood.
tropical American tree with bitter wood

Examples

The bitterwood thrives in the humid forests of South America.
Traditional healers often use extracts from bitterwood to prepare herbal remedies.
He identified the tree as a bitterwood because of its distinctive scarlet fruit and bitter taste.

Etymology

Refers to the tree's characteristically bitter wood.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɪtərˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈbɪtəˌwʊd / (BrE)
A West Indian tree (Picrasma excelsa) whose wood is known for its intensely bitter taste and is used to produce the medicinal drug Jamaica quassia.
West Indian tree yielding Jamaica quassia

Examples

The bark of the bitterwood tree is often used in herbal remedies for digestive issues.
Local healers extracted medicinal compounds from bitterwood to treat a variety of ailments.
Scientific research has explored the effectiveness of bitterwood against certain parasites.

Etymology

Named for its very bitter wood; 'bitter' + 'wood'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈbɪt̬ɚˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈbɪtəˌwʊd / (BrE)
A South American shrub or small tree with bright scarlet flowers, valued for its fine-grained yellowish wood and as a source of the bitter drug quassia.
South American tree; source of quassia

Examples

The extract from bitterwood is commonly used as a natural insect repellent.
Local artisans use bitterwood to craft fine furniture due to its attractive grain.
Herbalists have valued bitterwood for centuries because of its medicinal properties.
