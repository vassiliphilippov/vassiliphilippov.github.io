Rubus occidentalis

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːbəs ˌɑksɪˈdɛntælɪs / (AmE), / ˈruːbəs ˌɒksɪˈdɛntælɪs / (BrE)
A raspberry species native to eastern North America, known for its black, thimble-shaped fruit.
species of black raspberry

Examples

The forest clearing was thick with Rubus occidentalis, enticing hikers with its sweet black fruit.
Researchers are studying Rubus occidentalis for its potential health benefits due to high antioxidant content.
Many gardeners choose to cultivate Rubus occidentalis because it's well-adapted to the climate of eastern North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black raspberry - common name for Rubus occidentalis, a species with black fruit

Etymology

From Latin, 'rubus' (bramble) and 'occidentalis' (western, from the West).
