raising

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈreɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of lifting or increasing something to a higher position, level, or amount
the act of lifting or increasing something

Examples

The sudden raising of the flag signaled the start of the ceremony.
Their raising of prices led to complaints from many customers.
There was a noticeable raising of eyebrows when the decision was announced.

Synset Examples

an elevation of the temperature in the afternoon
a raising of the land resulting from volcanic activity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elevation - the process or result of raising something to a higher position

Etymology

From the verb 'raise', from Old Norse 'reisa' (to raise).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07385271-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈreɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈreɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
causing an increase in quantity or value; leading to something becoming higher, larger, or more intense.
causing an increase in amount or value

Examples

The board approved several raising proposals to boost the company's revenue.
A sudden raising trend in prices worried many consumers.
These raising taxes have made life more expensive for most citizens.

Synset Examples

a cost-raising increase in the basic wage rate

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lowering - causing to become less in quantity or value

3. NOUN

/ ˈreɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈreɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
the action or process of bringing up or nurturing a child to become a part of society
the upbringing or nurturing of a child

Examples

Many parents have different philosophies when it comes to the raising of their children.
She attributed her kindness to her strict but loving raising.
Debates about nature versus nurture often center on the raising of children.

Synset Examples

they debated whether nature or nurture was more important

Related Terms

  • Synonym: upbringing - the process of bringing up a child

Etymology

From the verb 'raise,' Middle English, from Old Norse 'reisa' (to raise).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈreɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈreɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
the way someone is cared for or trained as a child; upbringing
upbringing; manner of childhood care
american

Examples

Her strict raising influenced many of her decisions as an adult.
He credited his good manners to his Southern raising.
Despite her difficult raising, she became a compassionate person.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: upbringing - the treatment and instruction received by a child from its parents
  • Synonym: parenting - the process of raising and educating a child

Etymology

From the verb 'raise', ultimately from Old Norse 'reisa' (to raise, lift).
