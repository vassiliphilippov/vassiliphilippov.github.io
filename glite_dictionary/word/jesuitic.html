Jesuitic

1. ADJ

/ ˌdʒɛz.juˈɪ.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛz.juˈɪ.tɪk / (BrE)
Having qualities characteristic of Jesuits or Jesuitism, often implying subtlety, craftiness, or casuistic reasoning.
characteristic of Jesuits or Jesuitism

Examples

The priest's Jesuitic response left the congregation pondering his true beliefs.
Her Jesuitic approach to the dilemma was both clever and evasive.
The novel provides a Jesuitic exploration of morality and truth.

Synset Examples

Jesuitical education

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jesuitical - relating to Jesuits, sometimes with the sense of clever or ambiguous reasoning

Etymology

From Jesuit + -ic (forming adjectives)
