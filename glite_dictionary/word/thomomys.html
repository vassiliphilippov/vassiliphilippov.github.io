Thomomys

1. NOUN

/ θoʊˈmɑːmɪs / (AmE), / θəˈməʊmɪs / (BrE)
A genus of burrowing rodents, commonly called western pocket gophers, native to western North America.
genus of western pocket gophers

Examples

Thomomys species are well known for creating extensive tunnel systems underground.
Many farmers are familiar with Thomomys because of the damage they cause to crops.
Researchers tracked the population dynamics of local Thomomys over several years.

Etymology

From Greek 'thomos' (heap) and 'mys' (mouse), referring to their burrowing habits.

2. NOUN

/ θoʊˈmoʊmɪs / (AmE), / θəʊˈməʊmɪs / (BrE)
A genus of western pocket gophers native to North America, characterized by their external cheek pouches and burrowing habits.
genus of western pocket gophers

Examples

The Thomomys genus includes several species of pocket gophers found throughout western North America.
Biologists studied the ecological impact of Thomomys on local agriculture.
Members of the Thomomys genus are recognized by their large, fur-lined cheek pouches used for carrying food.

Etymology

From Greek *thōmōma* (a cut, socket) and scientific Latin usage for rodent genera.
