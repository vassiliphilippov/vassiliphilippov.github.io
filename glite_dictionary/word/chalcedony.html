chalcedony

1. NOUN

/ kælˈsɛdəni / (AmE), / kælˈsɛdəni / (BrE)
a translucent to transparent variety of quartz, typically milky or greyish in color, used as a gemstone
milky or greyish translucent quartz

Examples

The museum displayed a rare chalcedony carved with intricate designs.
Her ring featured a polished blue chalcedony stone set in silver.
Jewellers prize chalcedony for its ability to take a high polish and its pleasing translucency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agate - a variety of chalcedony with banded patterns, used as a gemstone

Etymology

From Latin 'chalcedonius', from Greek 'Khalkēdōn', an ancient city in Asia Minor.
