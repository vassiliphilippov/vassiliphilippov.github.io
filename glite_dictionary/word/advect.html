to advect

1. VERB

/ ædˈvɛkt / (AmE), / ædˈvɛkt / (BrE)
(of a fluid or its properties) to transport or convey by the horizontal movement of a fluid, especially in meteorology and fluid dynamics.
transport by horizontal fluid movement

Examples

Warm air masses may advect moisture from the ocean toward the land.
Meteorologists studied how pollutants were advected by prevailing winds across the region.
The model simulates how heat is advected through atmospheric currents.

Synset Examples

energy advected from the environment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advection - the process or act of transporting by horizontal movement of a fluid

Etymology

From Latin 'ad-' (to) + 'vehere' (to carry).
