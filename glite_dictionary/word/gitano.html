gitano

1. NOUN

/ hiˈtɑːnoʊ / (AmE), / hiˈtɑːnəʊ / (BrE)
A man of Romani (Gypsy) origin from Spain, especially one belonging to the traditional Romani communities in that country.
Spanish male Romani (Gypsy) person

Examples

He learned to play the guitar from a talented gitano in Seville.
Many gitanos have preserved their unique cultural traditions in southern Spain.
The gitano community is known for its contributions to flamenco music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gypsy - a member of the Romani people

Etymology

From Spanish 'gitano', from Latin 'Aegyptanus' (Egyptian), alluding to the mistaken belief that the Romani people came from Egypt.
