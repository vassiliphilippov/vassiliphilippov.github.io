Cajun

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪ.dʒən / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ.dʒən / (BrE)
A member of a group of people in Louisiana descended from French-speaking Acadian immigrants from Canada, especially Nova Scotia.
Louisianian descended from Acadian immigrants

Examples

Many Cajun families still speak a French dialect at home.
The Cajun community is known for its distinctive music and cuisine.
As a proud Cajun, she celebrates her heritage every year at local festivals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Acadian - A descendant of 17th-century French settlers in Acadia, Canada

Etymology

From French 'Cadien', a shortening of 'Acadien' (Acadian), referring to settlers from Acadia (now parts of eastern Canada).
