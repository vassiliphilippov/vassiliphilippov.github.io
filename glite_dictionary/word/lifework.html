lifework

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪfˌwɜrk / (AmE), / ˈlaɪfˌwɜːk / (BrE)
the principal or most significant work accomplished in a person's career or lifetime
main work of a person's career

Examples

Writing the series of novels was considered her lifework by critics and fans alike.
He dedicated his lifework to the fight against poverty and injustice.
The scientist viewed her research on renewable energy as her lifework.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: achievement - a thing done successfully, typically by effort or skill
  • Antonym: failure - lack of success in doing or achieving something significant

Etymology

Formed from 'life' + 'work'; attested since the 19th century.
