quadriplegia

1. NOUN

/ ˌkwɑːdrɪˈpliːdʒiə / (AmE), / ˌkwɒdrɪˈpliːdʒiə / (BrE)
Paralysis of all four limbs (both arms and both legs), usually caused by injury or disease affecting the spinal cord.
paralysis of all four limbs
medical

Examples

The accident left him with quadriplegia, requiring full-time medical care.
Research into spinal injuries offers hope to those living with quadriplegia.
She became an advocate for disability rights after developing quadriplegia at a young age.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: paraplegia - paralysis of both legs and lower body

Etymology

From Latin 'quadri-' meaning 'four' and Greek 'plēgē' meaning 'stroke'.
