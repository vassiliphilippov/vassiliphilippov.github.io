crystallised

1. ADJ

/ ˈkrɪstəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstəlaɪzd / (BrE)
(of fruits or other foods) preserved by coating with or allowing to absorb sugar, often resulting in a sweet, firm texture.
preserved with sugar (esp. fruits)
british

Examples

She decorated the cake with crystallised violets and orange peel.
You can buy crystallised ginger in most supermarkets.
The crystallised fruit added a sweet and festive touch to the dessert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candied - preserved or coated in sugar, especially relating to fruit
  • Synonym: crystallized - US spelling of crystallised

Etymology

From English 'crystal' and the suffix '-ise', indicating the process of forming crystals (in this case, of sugar).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkrɪstəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstəlaɪzd / (BrE)
(of an idea, opinion, or plan) having become clear, definite, and settled in form or character.
made fixed or definite; settled

Examples

Her crystallised views on the subject made her arguments very persuasive.
After weeks of discussion, their plan finally crystallised into a concrete proposal.
He found it hard to change his crystallised opinions about the new policy.

Synset Examples

distinguish between crystallized and uncrystallized opinion

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fluid - not fixed; likely to change
  • Antonym: uncrystallised - not fixed or definite in form

Etymology

From 'crystallise', from Greek 'krustallos' (ice, crystal).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00659885-a
Synset POS: a
