Poncirus trifoliata

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɒnˈsaɪrəs traɪˌfoʊliˈeɪtə / (AmE), / ˌpɒnˈsaɪrəs traɪˌfɒliˈɑːtə / (BrE)
A small, fast-growing, spiny deciduous tree native to China, bearing sweetly scented flowers and decorative but inedible fruit; often used as rootstock in citrus grafting and for hedges.
spiny deciduous Chinese orange tree, used in grafting

Examples

The nursery recommended Poncirus trifoliata as an effective hedge because of its dense, thorny branches.
Many citrus growers use Poncirus trifoliata as a rootstock to improve disease resistance in their orchards.
Although Poncirus trifoliata produces vibrant yellow fruit, it is not suitable for eating.

Etymology

From scientific Latin; 'Poncirus' is a genus name, 'trifoliata' means 'three-leaved' in Latin.
