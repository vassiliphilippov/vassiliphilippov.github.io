Dryopteris oreopteris

1. NOUN

/ draɪˈɑptərɪs ˌɔriˈɑptərɪs / (AmE), / draɪˈɒptərɪs ˌɔːriˈɒptərɪs / (BrE)
A species of fern native to European mountains, notable for its fragrant, lemon or balsam-scented fronds.
a European mountain fern species

Examples

The shady woodland trail was lined with lush Dryopteris oreopteris plants.
Botanists admire the distinctive scent of Dryopteris oreopteris when studying European mountain flora.
Gardeners sometimes plant Dryopteris oreopteris for its elegant fronds and pleasant fragrance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fern - a nonflowering vascular plant with feathery or leafy fronds

Etymology

From Greek 'drys' (oak) and 'pteris' (fern), with species name from Greek 'oros' (mountain) + 'pteris' (fern).
