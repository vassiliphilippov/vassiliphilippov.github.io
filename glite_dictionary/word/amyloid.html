amyloid

1. NOUN

/ ˈæm.i.lɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈam.ɪ.lɔɪd / (BrE)
A substance consisting mainly of starch or resembling starch, especially as found in some plants and foods.
substance resembling starch

Examples

The potato is rich in amyloid, making it a valuable source of energy.
Researchers studied the properties of plant-based amyloid under different conditions.
Certain foods contain a high percentage of amyloid and are recommended in starch-rich diets.

Etymology

From Greek 'amylon' (starch) + '-oid' (resembling).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæm.i.lɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈæm.ɪ.lɔɪd / (BrE)
a waxy, translucent protein substance that accumulates in tissues as the result of certain diseases, often causing organ dysfunction
abnormal protein deposit in tissues
medical

Examples

The accumulation of amyloid in the brain is a hallmark of Alzheimer's disease.
Doctors detected amyloid deposits in her liver tissue during the biopsy.
Scientists are researching new methods to prevent amyloid buildup in patients with neurodegenerative disorders.

Etymology

From German 'Amyloid', coined in the 19th century, from Latin 'amylum' (starch) based on its starch-like appearance under microscope.
