mousetrap

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊsˌtræp / (AmE), / ˈmaʊs.træp / (BrE)
a device designed to catch or kill mice
device for catching mice

Examples

The old farmhouse had several mousetraps hidden in the corners.
He set a mousetrap under the sink after spotting droppings.
They checked the mousetrap every morning to see if it had caught anything.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trap - a device for catching animals

2. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊsˌtræp / (AmE), / ˈmaʊsˌtræp / (BrE)
In American football, a play in which a defensive player is intentionally allowed to cross the line of scrimmage and is then blocked as the runner advances through the area the defender left open.
an American football play tricking a defender
american

Examples

The coach called a mousetrap to take advantage of the aggressiveness of the defensive tackle.
They executed a perfect mousetrap, allowing the runner to sprint through the gap.
Defensive players have to watch out for the mousetrap or risk opening a lane for the offense.
