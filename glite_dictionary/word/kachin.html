Kachin

1. NOUN

/ kɑˈtʃɪn / (AmE), / kəˈtʃɪn / (BrE)
a group of Tibeto-Burman languages spoken in the northernmost region of Myanmar (Burma) and adjacent areas of China and India
a Tibeto-Burman language group of northern Myanmar

Examples

The Kachin language is spoken by many indigenous people in northern Myanmar.
She studied the grammatical structure of Kachin for her linguistic research.
There are several dialects within the Kachin language family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jingpo - the principal language of the Kachin people

Etymology

From Burmese ကချင် (ka.ʧɪ̀ɴ), the name of the people and their language.
