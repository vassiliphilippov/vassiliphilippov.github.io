sequoia

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈkwoʊ.i.ə / (AmE), / sɪˈkwɔɪ.ə / (BrE)
The wood of either of two huge coniferous trees native to California, known for their great height and longevity.
wood of a giant California conifer

Examples

The house was built using rare sequoia for the beams and paneling.
Some furniture makers prize sequoia for its beautiful reddish color and grain.
Conservation efforts now restrict the commercial harvest of sequoia in California.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redwood - common name for several species of large coniferous trees, especially Sequoia sempervirens

Etymology

Named after Sequoyah, the Cherokee scholar who created the Cherokee syllabary.

2. NOUN

/ sɪˈk(w)ɔɪ.ə / (AmE), / sɪˈkwɔɪ.ə / (BrE)
A very large evergreen coniferous tree native to California, known for reaching great heights and for its massive size, including both the coast redwood and giant sequoia species.
large Californian coniferous tree

Examples

The ancient sequoia towers above the forest, its trunk thick and reddish-brown.
We visited a national park to see the massive sequoias that are hundreds of years old.
Scientists study the growth rings of sequoia trees to learn about historical climate patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redwood - large North American coniferous tree related to the sequoia
  • Synonym: giant sequoia - largest sequoia species; found in the Sierra Nevada
  • Degree Lower: pine - another type of coniferous tree

Etymology

Named for Sequoyah, a Cherokee leader; coined in the mid-19th century.
