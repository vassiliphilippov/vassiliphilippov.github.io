tensimeter

1. NOUN

/ tɛnˈsɪmɪtər / (AmE), / tɛnˈsɪmɪtə / (BrE)
an instrument (a type of manometer) used for measuring vapor pressure in liquids or gases
instrument for measuring vapor pressure

Examples

The laboratory technician used the tensimeter to determine the vapor pressure of the solvent.
A tensimeter is essential in experiments that require precise measurement of vapor pressure.
Modern tensimeters offer high accuracy and digital readouts for scientific research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manometer - an instrument for measuring the pressure of gases or liquids

Etymology

From Latin 'tensus' (stretched, tense) + Greek 'metron' (measure).
