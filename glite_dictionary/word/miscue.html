miscue

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪsˌkju / (AmE), / ˈmɪs.kjuː / (BrE)
a faulty shot in billiards or snooker in which the cue slips off the ball
faulty billiards/snooker shot

Examples

He groaned after his miscue cost him the winning shot in the game.
Her opponent capitalized on a critical miscue during the third frame.
The match was tense, but one unexpected miscue decided the outcome.

Etymology

From 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'cue' (stick used in billiards).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪsˌkju / (AmE), / ˈmɪs.kjuː / (BrE)
a minor and unintentional mistake, especially one made in speaking, writing, or performing an action
minor unintended mistake

Examples

The speaker made a small miscue during his presentation but quickly recovered.
She laughed off the miscue when she called her colleague by the wrong name.
His only miscue in the interview was forgetting one date, but overall he impressed the panel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mistake - an action or decision that is wrong or produces an unwanted result
  • Synonym: blunder - a careless or serious mistake

Etymology

From mis- (wrongly) + cue (signal, indication).
