Serranus

1. NOUN

/ səˈræ.nəs / (AmE), / sɛˈræ.nəs / (BrE)
A genus of chiefly small marine fishes in the family Serranidae, commonly known as sea basses, found mostly in the Pacific Ocean.
a genus of small Pacific sea basses

Examples

The Serranus genus includes several species of small but colorful fish.
Many Serranus species inhabit coral reefs in the Pacific Ocean.
Researchers studied the behavior of Serranus in their natural habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea bass - a type of marine fish belonging to several families, including Serranidae

Etymology

From Latin serranus, originally meaning 'of the mountains', later applied to this genus of fish.
