Wilhelm II

1. PROPN

/ ˈvɪlhaɪm səˈgænd / (AmE), / ˈvɪl.hɛlm ðə ˈsɛk.ənd / (BrE)
(1859–1941) The last German Emperor (Kaiser) and King of Prussia, reigning from 1888 until his abdication in 1918, notable for his role in World War I.
last German Emperor (Kaiser), 1888–1918

Examples

Wilhelm II played a critical role in the political developments leading up to World War I.
Historians debate to what extent Wilhelm II was responsible for the outbreak of the war.
After the 1918 revolution, Wilhelm II abdicated and went into exile in the Netherlands.

Etymology

German given name Wilhelm (William) and Roman numeral II, meaning 'the second'.
