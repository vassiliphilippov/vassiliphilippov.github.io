Corokia

1. NOUN

/ kəˈroʊkiə / (AmE), / kɒˈrəʊkiə / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen shrubs native to New Zealand, known for their twisted wiry stems and clusters of small yellow flowers.
genus of evergreen New Zealand shrubs

Examples

The Corokia hedge in their garden blooms with bright yellow flowers every spring.
Many New Zealand landscapes feature the unique twisted branches of the Corokia shrub.
Gardeners appreciate Corokia for its resilience and striking appearance throughout the year.
