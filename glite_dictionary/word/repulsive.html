repulsive

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈpʌlsɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈpʌlsɪv / (BrE)
(of a force or object) having the power or tendency to push away or drive apart something else.
having the power to repel

Examples

The north poles of two magnets exert a repulsive force on each other.
Electrons carry a negative charge and produce a repulsive electrical field around them.
Scientists studied the repulsive interactions between particles in the experiment.

Synset Examples

a repulsive force

Related Terms

  • Antonym: attractive - having the power to attract or pull toward

Etymology

From Latin repulsus (driven back, repelled), from repellere (to drive back).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00171417-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈpʌlsɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈpʌlsɪv / (BrE)
extremely unpleasant or ugly, causing strong dislike or disgust, sometimes to the point of being terrifying
extremely ugly or disgusting

Examples

The rotting food gave off a repulsive odor that filled the entire kitchen.
She recoiled from the sight of the repulsive mask in the shop window.
Many found the old mansion's repulsive appearance disturbing, especially at night.

Synset Examples

a hideous scar
a repulsive mask

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hideous - extremely ugly or horrifying
  • Antonym: attractive - pleasing or appealing to the senses
  • Synonym: disgusting - arousing revulsion or strong dislike

Etymology

From Latin repellere (to drive back), via French répulsif.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00223526-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ rɪˈpʌlsɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈpʌlsɪv / (BrE)
causing strong dislike or disgust; extremely offensive or objectionable to the mind or morals
causing strong disgust or offense

Examples

Many found his repulsive behavior at the meeting deeply upsetting.
The book described a repulsive character who delighted in cruelty.
Most people would consider such ideas to be utterly repulsive.

Synset Examples

an abhorrent deed
the obscene massacre at Wounded Knee
morally repugnant customs
repulsive behavior
the most repulsive character in recent novels

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abhorrent - inspiring disgust and loathing
  • Synonym: offensive - causing someone to feel deeply hurt, upset, or angry
  • Antonym: attractive - appealing or pleasing in appearance or character

Etymology

From Latin 'repulsivus', from 'repellere' (to drive back).
