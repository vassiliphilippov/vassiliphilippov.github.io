Niger-Kordofanian

1. NOUN

/ ˌnaɪdʒər kɔrˌdəˈfeɪniən / (AmE), / ˌniːdʒə ˌkɔːdəˈfeɪniən / (BrE)
a major family of languages spoken in sub-Saharan Africa, including most languages south of the Sahara; many are tonal, though some, like Swahili or Fula, are not
major sub-Saharan African language family

Examples

Many linguists consider Swahili part of the Niger-Kordofanian language family.
The Niger-Kordofanian languages are spoken across a vast region of sub-Saharan Africa.
Research into Niger-Kordofanian languages has greatly expanded our understanding of African linguistics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Niger-Congo - a large branch of the Niger-Kordofanian language family

Etymology

From Niger (referring to the Niger River) and Kordofan (a region in Sudan), indicating the geographical extent of the language family.
