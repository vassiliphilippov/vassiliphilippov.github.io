pup

1. NOUN

/ pʌp / (AmE), / pʌp / (BrE)
A young dog or the young of certain wild animals such as wolves or seals.
young dog or animal (e.g., wolf, seal)

Examples

The mother wolf protected her pup from danger.
Our neighbor brought over a litter of pups for us to meet.
Each newborn seal pup was carefully tagged by the researchers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puppy - a young dog

Etymology

Probably a shortening of 'puppy', originally from French 'poupée' (doll, toy), transferred to young dogs.

2. NOUN

/ pʌp / (AmE), / pʌp / (BrE)
an inexperienced or naive young person, especially one regarded with slight condescension
inexperienced young person
informal

Examples

He may act tough, but he's still just a pup in the business world.
When I started here, I was a young pup eager to learn from the older staff.
Don't underestimate her just because she's a pup; she's got real talent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: novice - a person new to and inexperienced in a job or situation
  • Synonym: rookie - a person new to an activity or organization
  • Antonym: veteran - a person with long experience in a field

Etymology

Originally from Latin 'pupus' meaning 'boy' or 'child'; the figurative sense for a young person comes from the literal sense of 'young animal'.

3. VERB

/ pʌp / (AmE), / pʌp / (BrE)
(Of a female dog or other certain mammals) to give birth to puppies.
give birth to puppies (animal)

Examples

Our golden retriever pupped last night and had six healthy puppies.
The vet monitored the fox as she pupped in her den.
Farmers often check on their sheepdogs during lambing season in case they begin pupping.

Synset Examples

the dog whelped

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whelp - (Of a female dog) to give birth to puppies
