Phalaris

1. NOUN

/ fəˈlɛrɪs / (AmE), / fəˈlɑːrɪs / (BrE)
a genus of grasses with broad leaves and dense, spike-like clusters of small flowers, found in temperate regions
a genus of grasses with dense flower spikes

Examples

The field was thickly covered with Phalaris swaying in the breeze.
Some species of Phalaris are cultivated as ornamental grasses in gardens.
Researchers studied the effects of Phalaris on local wildlife populations.

Etymology

From Greek phalaros (having a patch of white), referring to markings on some species.

2. NOUN

/ fəˈlɛərɪs / (AmE), / fəˈlɑːrɪs / (BrE)
A genus of grasses with broad leaves and a dense spike of flowers, commonly known as canary grasses.
a genus of grasses (canary grasses)

Examples

The grass species in the Phalaris genus are often cultivated as animal fodder.
Some Phalaris species are known for their attractive flower spikes in garden settings.
Researchers studied the alkaloid content of Phalaris plants to assess their effect on livestock.

Etymology

From Greek phalaris, a kind of grass.
