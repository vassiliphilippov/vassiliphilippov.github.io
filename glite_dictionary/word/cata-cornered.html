cata-cornered

1. ADJ

/ ˌkæt.əˈkɔr.nɚd / (AmE), / ˌkæt.əˈkɔː.nəd / (BrE)
Situated or positioned diagonally; placed at an angle across from something else, especially across an intersection or open area.
diagonally positioned or placed

Examples

She parked her car cata-cornered from the main entrance to the store.
The two houses sat cata-cornered across the busy intersection.
He ran cata-cornered through the playground, cutting across instead of following the path.

Synset Examples

set off in a catty-corner direction across the vacant lot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diagonal - slanting or positioned along a slanting line
  • Antonym: straight - not at an angle or slant, direct

Etymology

Alteration of dialectal 'catty-cornered,' from 'cater-corner,' with 'cater' from French 'quatre' (four), referring to the four corners of a square.
