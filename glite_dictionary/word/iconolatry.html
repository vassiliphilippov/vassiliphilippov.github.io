iconolatry

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪkəˈnɑːlətri / (AmE), / ˌaɪkəˈnɒlətri / (BrE)
the worship or adoration of sacred images or icons, especially in a religious context
worship of sacred images

Examples

The debate over iconolatry led to lasting divisions within the church.
Some religious traditions strictly prohibit iconolatry in their worship practices.
Art historians study the influence of iconolatry on early Christian mosaics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idolatry - the worship of idols or images as gods

Etymology

From Greek eikonolatreia: eikōn (image) + latreia (worship).
