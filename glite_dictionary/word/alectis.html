Alectis

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɛktɪs / (AmE), / əˈlɛktɪs / (BrE)
A genus of large marine fishes in the family Carangidae, including the threadfish and diamond trevallies.
a genus of marine fishes

Examples

The Alectis genus is known for its elongated fin filaments.
The diamond trevally, a member of Alectis, is prized by anglers.
Marine biologists studied the distribution of Alectis species in tropical waters.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'Alektis', but precise classical reference unclear.
