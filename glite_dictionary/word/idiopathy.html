idiopathy

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪdiˈɑpəθi / (AmE), / ˌɪdɪˈɒpəθi / (BrE)
a disease or medical condition that arises spontaneously or from an obscure or unknown cause
disease of unknown cause
medical

Examples

The cause of the patient's symptoms was determined to be an idiopathy after all other possibilities were excluded.
Some cases of epilepsy remain an idiopathy despite extensive testing.
Researchers are still investigating the origins of this rare cardiac idiopathy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idiopathic - arising from an unknown cause (adjective)

Etymology

From Greek idios (one's own) + pathos (suffering, disease).
