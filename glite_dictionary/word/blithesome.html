blithesome

1. ADJ

/ ˈblaɪð.səm / (AmE), / ˈblaɪð.səm / (BrE)
cheerful and carefree; showing a happy and lighthearted nature.
cheerful and carefree

Examples

She danced across the lawn in a blithesome mood, laughing at the sunlight.
His blithesome nature made him popular at every gathering.
On a blithesome afternoon, the park was filled with cheerful voices and song.

Synset Examples

was loved for her blithe spirit
a merry blithesome nature
her lighthearted nature
trilling songs with a lightsome heart

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blithe - cheerful and carefree
  • Synonym: merry - cheerful and lively
  • Antonym: gloomy - sad or depressed

Etymology

Formed from 'blithe' + '-some', modeled after similar adjectives. 'Blithe' comes from Old English 'blīðe' (joyous, kind).
