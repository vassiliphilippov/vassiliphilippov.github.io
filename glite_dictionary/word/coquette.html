coquette

1. VERB

/ koʊˈkɛt / (AmE), / kəʊˈkɛt / (BrE)
to behave flirtatiously or playfully, often without serious intentions
act flirtatiously without serious intent

Examples

She loved to coquette with her admirers at every party she attended.
He would often coquette with colleagues, making his intentions unclear.
Despite her marriage, she continued to coquette with others, enjoying the harmless attention.

Synset Examples

The guys always try to chat up the new secretaries
My husband never flirts with other women

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flirt - to behave as if attracted to someone, usually playfully

Etymology

From French 'coquetter', derived from 'coquette' (flirt, flirtatious woman).

2. NOUN

/ koʊˈkɛt / (AmE), / kɒˈkɛt / (BrE)
a woman who flirts or behaves in a teasingly seductive way to attract attention or admiration
a flirtatious or teasing woman

Examples

She was known as a coquette, charming everyone at the party with her playful smiles.
The writer described the character as a clever coquette who always got her way.
He suspected she was a coquette when she winked at him from across the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flirt - a person who behaves as if they are attracted to someone, without serious intent

Etymology

From French 'coquette', diminutive of 'coq' meaning ‘rooster, cock’; originally meaning a flirtatious woman.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09984664-n
Synset POS: n
