Eptatretus

1. NOUN

/ ɛp.təˈtriː.təs / (AmE), / ɛp.taˈtriː.təs / (BrE)
A genus of jawless fish in the family Myxinidae, commonly known as hagfish.
genus of jawless fish (hagfish)

Examples

The Eptatretus genus includes several species of hagfish found in deep-sea environments.
Marine biologists collected a specimen of Eptatretus during their deep-ocean survey expedition.
Research on Eptatretus has improved our understanding of primitive vertebrate anatomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Myxinidae - the family of jawless fish to which Eptatretus belongs
  • Synonym: hagfish - jawless marine fish belonging to family Myxinidae

Etymology

From Greek roots: 'hepta' (seven) and 'tretos' (perforated), referring to their gill openings.
