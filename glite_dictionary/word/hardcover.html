hardcover

1. ADJ

/ ˈhɑrdˌkʌvɚ / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdˌkʌv.ər / (BrE)
(of a book) having a rigid protective cover, as opposed to a paperback.
book with a rigid protective cover

Examples

The library just received a hardcover edition of the novel.
Most collectors prefer to buy hardcover books for their durability.
She gave him a beautiful hardcover biography for his birthday.

Synset Examples

hardback books

Related Terms

  • Antonym: paperback - a book bound in flexible paper covers
  • Synonym: hardback - a book with a hard cover
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00201850-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrdˌkʌvər / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdˌkʌvə / (BrE)
A book bound with rigid protective covers, typically made of cardboard, cloth, or leather.
book with rigid covers

Examples

The library has a pristine hardcover copy of the novel on display.
She prefers reading hardcover books because they are more durable and look elegant on a shelf.
The new release is available in both hardcover and paperback formats.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: paperback - a book with a flexible paper cover
