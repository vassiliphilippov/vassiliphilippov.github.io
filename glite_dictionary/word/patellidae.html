Patellidae

1. NOUN

/ pəˈtɛlɪˌdaɪ / (AmE), / pəˈtɛlɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of marine mollusks known as true limpets, characterized by their conical shells and found mostly in rocky intertidal zones
family of true limpets (marine mollusks)

Examples

Scientists discovered a previously unknown species belonging to the Patellidae family during their marine expedition.
Intertidal zones are often populated by various members of the Patellidae, which cling tightly to rocks.
The classification of Patellidae is based on both shell morphology and genetic analysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limpet - a type of marine mollusk with a conical shell

Etymology

From the Latin 'patella' (small pan or dish), used for limpets due to their shape, with '-idae' indicating a zoological family.

2. NOUN

/ pəˈtɛlɪˌdi / (AmE), / pəˈtɛlɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine gastropod mollusks known as true limpets, characterized by their simple, conical shells and found in intertidal zones worldwide.
family of marine true limpets

Examples

The Patellidae are distinguished by their cap-shaped shells and strong muscular feet.
Many intertidal rocky shores are home to different species of Patellidae.
Studies on Patellidae have contributed to understanding the evolution of marine gastropods.

Etymology

From modern Latin Patellidae, based on Patella (type genus), from Latin patella (a small pan, knee cap).
