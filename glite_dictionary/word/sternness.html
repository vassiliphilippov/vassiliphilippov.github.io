sternness

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɜrn.nəs / (AmE), / ˈstɜːn.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being firm, strict, or uncompromising in attitude or action
quality of being firm or strict

Examples

Her sternness made it clear she would not tolerate any disobedience.
There was a certain sternness in his voice when he warned the students.
The principal's sternness ensured discipline was maintained at the school.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: leniency - the quality of being more merciful or tolerant

Etymology

From Middle English 'sterne' (strict, severe) + '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɜrn.nəs / (AmE), / ˈstɜːn.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being grim, severe, or forbidding, especially in appearance, manner, or environment
quality of being grim or forbidding

Examples

The sternness of the deserted landscape made it feel unwelcoming.
Her father's sternness often intimidated her friends when they visited.
There was a certain sternness to the old castle that gave everyone chills.

Synset Examples

the sternness of his surroundings made him uncomfortable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: severity - the quality of being very harsh or strict
  • Antonym: gentleness - the quality of being soft or mild in manner or appearance

Etymology

From stern + -ness, forming a noun of quality.
