mart

1. NOUN

/ mɑrt / (AmE), / mɑːt / (BrE)
a place where goods are bought and sold; a marketplace or trading center, especially one established for public commerce
marketplace or trading center

Examples

The bustling mart was filled with vendors selling fresh produce and crafts.
They opened a new seafood mart on the edge of town.
In the center of the old city stood a grand mart where merchants gathered daily.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: market - a regular gathering for the sale of goods

Etymology

From Middle English 'mart', shortened from 'market', from Old French 'marché'.
