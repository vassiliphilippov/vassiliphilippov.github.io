borrelia

1. NOUN

/ bɔːrˈiːliə / (AmE), / bɒˈrɛliə / (BrE)
A genus of spiral-shaped bacteria, some species of which cause diseases such as relapsing fever in humans.
genus of spiral bacteria causing relapsing fever
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that borrelia bacteria are responsible for causing relapsing fever in some regions.
Researchers are studying how borrelia species are transmitted to humans by ticks.
Antibiotics are often effective against infections caused by borrelia in their early stages.

Etymology

Named after Amédée Borrel, French bacteriologist, with New Latin suffix -ia.
