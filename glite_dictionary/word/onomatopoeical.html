onomatopoeical

1. ADJ

/ ˌɑnəˌmætəˈpiɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌɒnəməˌtəʊˈpiːɪkəl / (BrE)
(of a word) formed by imitating the natural sound associated with the thing or action it refers to.
imitating a natural sound (of words)

Examples

The word 'buzz' is an onomatopoeical expression of the sound made by bees.
Many comic books use onomatopoeical words like 'bang' and 'crash' for sound effects.
Children find onomatopoeical language particularly engaging because it reflects familiar noises.

Synset Examples

onomatopoeic words are imitative of noises
it was independently developed in more than one place as an onomatopoetic term

Related Terms

  • Synonym: onomatopoeic - (of a word) imitative of natural sounds

Etymology

From onomatopoeia, from Greek onomatopoiia (making of a name or word), plus the adjective-forming suffix -al.
