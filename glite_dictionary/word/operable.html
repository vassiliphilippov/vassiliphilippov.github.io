operable

1. ADJ

/ ˈɑpərəbəl / (AmE), / ˈɒpərəbəl / (BrE)
(of a disease) able to be treated or removed by surgical operation.
treatable by surgery
medical

Examples

The tumor was found to be operable, giving hope to the patient.
Doctors explained that her condition was operable if detected early.
Fortunately, the cancer is operable and surgery is scheduled for next week.

Synset Examples

an operable cancer

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inoperable - not able to be treated or removed by surgery

Etymology

From Latin 'operari' (to work, to do) + '-able'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01173361-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈɑː.pɚ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈɒp.ər.ə.bəl / (BrE)
able to be used or put into operation for a particular purpose or under certain conditions
able to be used for a specific purpose

Examples

The new regulations make it difficult for factories to remain operable throughout the year.
The company presented an operable plan for reducing costs without layoffs.
Before launching the product, engineers ensured that every system was fully operable.

Synset Examples

an operable plan
a practicable solution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: practicable - able to be done or put into practice successfully
  • Antonym: inoperable - not able to be used or operated

Etymology

From Latin 'operari' (to work, to operate) + '-able' (capable of).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01841381-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈɑːpərəbəl / (AmE), / ˈɒpərəbəl / (BrE)
in a condition that allows something to be used or operated; fit or ready for use or service
fit or ready for use

Examples

The engine was repaired and is now fully operable.
Despite its age, the equipment remains operable and safe to use.
The hospital ensured that all critical systems were operable during the power outage.

Synset Examples

the toaster was still functional even after being dropped
the lawnmower is a bit rusty but still usable
an operational aircraft

Related Terms

  • Synonym: functional - working properly; able to operate
  • Synonym: operational - in working order; ready for use
  • Antonym: inoperable - not able to be used or operated

Etymology

From Latin 'operari' (to work) + '-able' (able to be).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02131634-s
Synset POS: s
