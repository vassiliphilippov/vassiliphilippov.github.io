litigant

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪtɪɡənt / (AmE), / ˈlɪtɪɡənt / (BrE)
a person or party involved in a lawsuit; someone who is either suing or being sued in a court of law
person involved in a lawsuit
legal

Examples

The litigant appeared in court to present his case against the corporation.
Both litigants agreed to settle the dispute out of court before the trial began.
As a seasoned litigant, she was familiar with the procedures of civil litigation.

Synset Examples

plaintiffs and defendants are both litigants

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plaintiff - the party who initiates a lawsuit
  • Antonym: defendant - the party who is sued in a lawsuit

Etymology

From Latin litigant-, present participle of litigare (to dispute, to sue), from litis (lawsuit) + agere (to drive, to do).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɪt̬.ɪ.ɡənt / (AmE), / ˈlɪt.ɪ.ɡənt / (BrE)
A person or party involved as a plaintiff or defendant in a lawsuit.
person involved in a lawsuit
legal

Examples

Both the plaintiff and the defendant are considered litigants in court.
As a litigant, she had to attend several hearings.
The judge instructed both litigants to present their evidence clearly.

Synset Examples

plaintiffs and defendants are both litigants

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plaintiff - the party who brings a lawsuit
  • Synonym: defendant - the party being sued or accused in a lawsuit

Etymology

From Latin 'litigant-', present participle of 'litigare' (to dispute, to sue).
