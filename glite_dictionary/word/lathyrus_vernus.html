Lathyrus vernus

1. NOUN

/ ləˈθaɪrəs ˈvɜrnəs / (AmE), / ləˈθaɪrəs ˈvɜːnəs / (BrE)
A bushy European perennial plant with nodding racemose violet-blue flowers, commonly known as the spring vetchling.
a European perennial plant with violet-blue flowers

Examples

The shaded woodland was dotted with clusters of Lathyrus vernus in full bloom.
Gardeners often plant Lathyrus vernus for its attractive violet-blue flowers in early spring.
Researchers studied how Lathyrus vernus adapts to different soil types in European forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spring vetchling - common name for Lathyrus vernus

Etymology

From the genus name Lathyrus (Latin, from Greek lathuros, a type of pea) and vernus, Latin for 'of the spring'.
