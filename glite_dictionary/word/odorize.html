to odorize

1. VERB

/ ˈoʊdəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈəʊdəˌraɪz / (BrE)
to give a particular smell to something, especially by adding a chemical or substance
give a smell to something

Examples

Natural gas companies odorize the gas to make leaks easier to detect.
The factory decided to odorize the product to create a more pleasant experience for users.
They odorized the air in the lobby with a subtle lavender scent.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to deodorize - to remove odors from; to eliminate smells from something

Etymology

From Latin 'odor' (smell) + English suffix '-ize'.
