four-lane

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɔrˌleɪn / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌleɪn / (BrE)
(of a road) having two lanes for traffic in each direction.
road with two lanes each way

Examples

The city recently expanded the old highway into a four-lane road to ease traffic congestion.
A new four-lane bridge was built over the river, improving connectivity between the towns.
Drivers prefer taking the four-lane expressway because it is faster and safer than the smaller roads.
