abstinent

1. ADJ

/ ˈæbstɪnənt / (AmE), / ˈæbstɪnənt / (BrE)
Deliberately avoiding or refraining from indulging in certain foods, drinks, or other pleasurable activities, especially for self-discipline or religious reasons.
refraining from indulgence, especially food or drink

Examples

She remained abstinent during the month, choosing not to drink any alcohol at events.
People who are abstinent from sugary foods often report increased energy and focus.
After many years of excess, he adopted an abstinent lifestyle for his health.

Synset Examples

not totally abstinent but abstemious

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abstemious - not allowing oneself much in the way of food or drink; moderate

Etymology

From Latin abstemius (sober; temperate), via Old French abstinent.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæbstənənt / (AmE), / ˈæbstɪnənt / (BrE)
A person who refrains from consuming alcoholic beverages.
a person who does not drink alcohol

Examples

He has been an abstinent for most of his adult life.
At the party, the abstinent sipped only water while others enjoyed cocktails.
She chose to become an abstinent after witnessing the negative effects of alcohol.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teetotaler - a person who never drinks alcohol
  • Antonym: drinker - a person who drinks alcoholic beverages

Etymology

From Latin abstinens, abstinentis, present participle of abstinere (to hold back).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09777275-n
Synset POS: n
