tibialis anterior

1. NOUN

/ tɪˌbaɪəlɪs ænˈtɪriər / (AmE), / tɪˌbeɪlɪs ænˈtɪərɪə / (BrE)
a muscle in the lower leg that runs from the tibia to the first metatarsal and cuneiform bones, responsible for dorsiflexing and inverting the foot
a lower leg muscle involved in foot movement
medical

Examples

The tibialis anterior helps lift your foot when you walk or run.
After his injury, she experienced pain along her tibialis anterior.
Stretching the tibialis anterior can help prevent shin splints.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tibialis posterior - a muscle located behind the tibia involved in foot movement

Etymology

From Latin: 'tibialis' meaning 'of the tibia' and 'anterior' meaning 'in front'.
