tomatillo

1. NOUN

/ toʊməˈtiːjoʊ / (AmE), / təˈmætɪəʊ / (BrE)
A small, edible fruit resembling a tomato, yellow to purple in color, enclosed in a papery husk, commonly used in Mexican cuisine.
small edible husked fruit, like a tomato

Examples

She added chopped tomatillo to the fresh salsa for a tangy flavor.
Tomatillo plants thrive in warm climates and produce fruit inside papery husks.
The recipe calls for roasting the tomatillos before blending them into the sauce.

Etymology

From Mexican Spanish, diminutive of 'tomate' (tomato), from Nahuatl *tomatl*.

2. NOUN

/ toʊ.məˈtiː.joʊ / (AmE), / təˈmæt.ɪ.jəʊ / (BrE)
A small, round, green or purplish fruit enclosed in a papery husk, native to Mexico and the southern United States, used in cooking and resembling a small tomato.
small green or purple fruit in a papery husk, used in cooking

Examples

The salsa was made with roasted tomatillo and fresh cilantro.
She added chopped tomatillos to the stew for a tart flavor.
You can find tomatillo plants at the local garden center.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tomato - large, red or yellow pulpy fruit used as a vegetable

Etymology

From Mexican Spanish 'tomatillo', diminutive of 'tomate' (tomato), from Nahuatl 'tomatl'.
