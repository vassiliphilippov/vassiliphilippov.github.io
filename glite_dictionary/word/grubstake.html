grubstake

1. VERB

/ ˈɡrʌbˌsteɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡrʌbsteɪk / (BrE)
to supply someone with funds or equipment in exchange for a promised share of any future profits or returns, especially in mining or prospecting.
to fund someone for a share of profits

Examples

He agreed to grubstake the prospector in exchange for a share of the gold found.
Many early miners were grubstaked by investors hoping for big returns.
The wealthy merchant grubstaked several expeditions during the gold rush.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finance - to provide funding for something or someone

Etymology

From 'grub' (food) and 'stake' (an interest in profit), originally US mining slang from the 19th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡrʌbˌsteɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡrʌbsteɪk / (BrE)
an amount of money or supplies given to a prospector or an entrepreneur in exchange for a share of the profits
funds supplied to a prospector or entrepreneur for a share of profits

Examples

The miner received a grubstake from a local businessman before heading into the mountains.
He promised her half the profits as a grubstake for her investment in his startup.
Without a grubstake, many prospectors would never have been able to begin their journeys.

Etymology

American English, originally from 'grub' (food) + 'stake' (share or investment), mid-19th century, referring to supplying necessities to prospectors.
