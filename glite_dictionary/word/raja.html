Raja

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɑːdʒə / (AmE), / ˈrɑːdʒə / (BrE)
A genus of rays in the family Rajidae, comprising several species of flat cartilaginous fish found in marine waters.
genus of rays (fish) in Rajidae family

Examples

The thornback skate belongs to the genus Raja and is common in European waters.
Scientists have identified several new species within the Raja genus based on their fin shapes and patterns.
The Raja are distinguished from other rays by the position of their dorsal fins.

Etymology

From Latin 'raja', meaning 'ray' (the fish).

2. NOUN

/ ˈreɪ.dʒə / (AmE), / ˈreɪ.dʒə / (BrE)
A genus of cartilaginous fish in the family Rajidae, commonly known as skates.
a genus of skates (fish)

Examples

Several species within the genus Raja are important for commercial fisheries worldwide.
The genus Raja is characterized by its flattened body and broad pectoral fins.
Biologists often study the distribution of Raja skates in coastal waters.

Etymology

From Latin 'raja' meaning 'ray' or 'skate' (type of fish).
