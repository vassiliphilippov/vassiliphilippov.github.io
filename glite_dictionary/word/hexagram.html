hexagram

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛk.səˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈhɛk.sə.ɡræm / (BrE)
a figure formed by extending the sides of a regular hexagon to produce two overlapping equilateral triangles, typically producing a six-pointed star
six-pointed star formed from two triangles

Examples

The mathematician drew a hexagram on the board to demonstrate the properties of symmetry.
The hexagram is an important symbol in several religious and cultural traditions.
Each side of the regular hexagon was extended to create a perfect hexagram on the paper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Star of David - the Jewish symbol consisting of a hexagram
  • Degree Lower: pentagram - a five-pointed star figure

Etymology

From Greek 'hexagrammon', from 'hex' (six) + 'gramma' (line, drawing).
