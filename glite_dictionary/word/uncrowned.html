uncrowned

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkraʊnd / (AmE), / ʌnˈkraʊnd / (BrE)
(of a tooth) not fitted with an artificial crown, especially referring to molars and bicuspids.
(of a tooth) without an artificial crown
medical

Examples

The dentist examined the uncrowned molar to assess the extent of decay.
Her uncrowned bicuspid was sensitive to cold foods.
Many patients have several uncrowned teeth that require restorative work.

Synset Examples

uncrowned teeth badly in need of attention

Related Terms

  • Antonym: crowned - (of a tooth) fitted with an artificial crown

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkraʊnd / (AmE), / ʌnˈkraʊnd / (BrE)
not officially given the title or status of a monarch, champion, or leader, especially though widely regarded as such.
not officially given a crown or title

Examples

He was often called the uncrowned king of jazz for his influence on the genre.
Despite her popularity, she remained the uncrowned queen of the competition.
The movement's uncrowned leader inspired thousands without ever holding an official position.

Synset Examples

the uncrowned king

Related Terms

  • Antonym: crowned - formally given a crown or official status as monarch or champion

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'crowned', from 'crown' (Middle English, from Anglo-Norman 'coroune', from Latin 'corona').
