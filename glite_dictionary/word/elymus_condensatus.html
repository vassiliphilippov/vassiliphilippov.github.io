Elymus condensatus

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːlɪməs kənˈdɛnsətəs / (AmE), / ˈiːlɪməs kənˈdɛnsətəs / (BrE)
A tall, stout perennial grass native to western North America, commonly known as giant wildrye.
a perennial grass of western North America

Examples

Elymus condensatus provides important habitat for wildlife throughout the western United States.
The tall stems of Elymus condensatus are often used for erosion control in native plant gardens.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Elymus condensatus in restored prairie ecosystems.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Elymus' (a genus of grass) and 'condensatus' (thick, condensed).
