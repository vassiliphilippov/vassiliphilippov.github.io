meanspirited

1. ADJ

/ ˈminˌspɪrɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈmiːnˌspɪrɪtɪd / (BrE)
having or showing a cruel, unkind, or spiteful nature; lacking generosity or magnanimity
unkind or spiteful in nature

Examples

His meanspirited remarks ruined the atmosphere at the dinner table.
She found his refusal to help out both petty and meanspirited.
A meanspirited attitude will only isolate you from others.

Synset Examples

it seems ungenerous to end this review of a splendid work of scholarship on a critical note
a meanspirited man unwilling to forgive

Related Terms

  • Antonym: generous - willing to give more help or money than is usual or expected
  • Antonym: kind-hearted - having a kind and sympathetic nature
  • Synonym: spiteful - showing or caused by malice

Etymology

From 'mean' (unkind, petty) + 'spirited' (having a particular temperament or attitude).

2. ADJ

/ ˈminˌspɪrɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈmiːnˌspɪrɪtɪd / (BrE)
Having or showing a cruel, petty, or unkind nature; lacking generosity or kindness.
cruel or unkind in spirit

Examples

Her meanspirited comments hurt the entire team.
It's meanspirited to mock someone for their mistakes.
The critic's meanspirited review did little to help the struggling artist.

Synset Examples

that liberal obedience without which your army would be a base rabble
taking a mean advantage
chok'd with ambition of the meaner sort
something essentially vulgar and meanspirited in politics

Related Terms

  • Antonym: generous - showing kindness and willingness to give
  • Antonym: kindhearted - having a kind and sympathetic nature
  • Synonym: malicious - intending to do harm

Etymology

From 'mean' (unkind, petty) + 'spirited' (having a specified kind of spirit).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01594057-s
Synset POS: s
