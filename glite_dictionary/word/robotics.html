robotics

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈbɑːtɪks / (AmE), / rəʊˈbɒtɪks / (BrE)
the branch of technology that deals with the design, construction, operation, and application of robots, especially for practical tasks
technology and use of robots

Examples

She decided to specialize in robotics because she was fascinated by intelligent machines.
Recent advances in robotics have made factory automation more efficient than ever before.
Universities around the world are offering new courses in robotics to keep up with industry demand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: automation - the use of automatic equipment in manufacturing or other processes
  • Synonym: robot - a machine capable of carrying out a complex series of actions automatically

Etymology

From 'robot' (invented by Czech writer Karel Čapek) combined with the English suffix '-ics', denoting fields of study.
