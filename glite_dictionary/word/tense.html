tense

1. NOUN

/ tɛns / (AmE), / tɛns / (BrE)
(Grammar) A category of verbs expressing time relations such as past, present, or future.
grammatical category indicating time

Examples

English verbs change their form to show the tense of an action or event.
The past tense is used to describe events that happened before now.
Teachers often explain the difference between present and future tense in grammar lessons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aspect - grammatical category expressing how an action extends over time

Etymology

From Latin 'tempus' (time, tense).

4. ADJ

/ tɛns / (AmE), / tɛns / (BrE)
(of a vowel) produced with greater tension of the tongue muscles and usually slightly higher tongue position and sometimes longer duration, as in the vowel sound of 'beat'.
(phonetics) vowel pronounced with tense tongue muscles

Examples

In English, the tense vowels are typically longer and require more tongue tension than lax vowels.
The tense vowel in 'beat' contrasts with the lax vowel in 'bit'.
Phonologists often distinguish between tense and lax vowels when analyzing language sounds.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lax - (of a vowel) produced with relatively less tension in the tongue muscles
