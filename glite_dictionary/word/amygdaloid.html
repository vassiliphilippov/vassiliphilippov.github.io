amygdaloid

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɪɡ.də.lɔɪd / (AmE), / əˈmɪɡ.də.lɔɪd / (BrE)
a volcanic rock characterized by rounded cavities filled with mineral deposits, formed by gases during solidification
volcanic rock with mineral-filled cavities

Examples

Geologists collected several samples of amygdaloid from the basalt outcrop.
The amygdaloid rock's cavities were later filled with quartz and calcite crystals.
Examining the amygdaloid in the laboratory revealed a fascinating pattern of mineral veins.

Etymology

From Latin 'amygdaloides', from Greek 'amygdaloeidēs' (like an almond), referring to the almond-shaped cavities.

2. ADJ

/ əˈmɪɡ.də.lɔɪd / (AmE), / əˈmɪɡ.də.lɔɪd / (BrE)
Shaped like an almond; having the form or outline of an almond.
shaped like an almond

Examples

The geologist identified an amygdaloid pattern in the volcanic rock formation.
Her eyes were described as amygdaloid by the painter, giving her a unique beauty.
An amygdaloid structure can be seen in certain cells under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: almond-shaped - having the shape of an almond

Etymology

From Greek amygdaloeidēs, from amygdalē (almond) + -oeidēs (-oid, form/likeness).
