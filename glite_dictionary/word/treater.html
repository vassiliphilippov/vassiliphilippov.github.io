treater

1. NOUN

/ ˈtriːtər / (AmE), / ˈtriːtə / (BrE)
a person who negotiates or participates in discussions to reach an agreement or settlement
person who negotiates or seeks agreement

Examples

The lead treater met with both parties to facilitate an agreement.
Each state appointed a treater to represent them at the peace conference.
As a skilled treater, she was able to resolve disputes quickly and fairly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: negotiator - a person who conducts negotiations to reach agreement

Etymology

From the verb 'treat', from Old French 'traitier', from Latin 'tractare' (to handle, manage, treat).
