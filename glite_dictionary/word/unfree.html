unfree

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfri / (AmE), / ʌnˈfriː / (BrE)
(especially of a person) not free; held in servitude or subject to the control of another.
not free; in servitude

Examples

Many people in history were born unfree and spent their lives in servitude.
The peasants were unfree, unable to leave the land they worked on.
Laws eventually changed to protect those who had once been considered unfree.

Synset Examples

he was born of unfree parents

Related Terms

  • Antonym: free - not under the control or in the power of another; able to act or be as one wishes

Etymology

Formed by prefixing un- (not) to free.

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfri / (AmE), / ʌnˈfriː / (BrE)
not free; restricted or hampered in action, rights, or choices; subject to the control or domination of others.
not free; constrained or controlled

Examples

History books describe many people who were unfree and denied basic rights.
In some societies, women remained largely unfree until the twentieth century.
He felt unfree working under such a strict and controlling manager.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: free - able to act or be done as one wishes; not under the control of another

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' to 'free', meaning 'not free'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01067975-a
Synset POS: a
