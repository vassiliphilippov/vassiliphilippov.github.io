crocus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkroʊ.kəs / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊ.kəs / (BrE)
a small, low-growing plant of the genus Crocus, with slender, grasslike leaves and showy white, yellow, or purple flowers, especially native to the Mediterranean region but widely cultivated as an ornamental.
a spring-flowering plant of the genus Crocus

Examples

Every spring, clusters of crocus bloom in the garden, adding vibrant color to the beds.
We planted several varieties of crocus along the front walkway.
The purple crocus is often the first flower to appear after winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saffron - an orange-yellow spice from the stigmas of Crocus sativus

Etymology

From Latin crocus, from Ancient Greek krokos (saffron, crocus).
