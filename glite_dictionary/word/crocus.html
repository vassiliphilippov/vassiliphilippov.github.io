Crocus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkroʊkəs / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊkəs / (BrE)
A genus of perennial flowering plants in the iris family (Iridaceae), known for species like the crocus, which bloom from underground corms.
genus of crocus-flowering plants

Examples

The Crocus genus includes both spring- and autumn-blooming species prized by gardeners.
Saffron is obtained from the stigmas of Crocus sativus flowers.
Many urban parks plant different species of Crocus to brighten the lawns in early spring.

Etymology

From Latin 'crocus', from Greek 'krokos', meaning saffron or crocus.
