lustrelessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌstərˌlɛsnəs / (AmE), / ˈlʌstəlɪsnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of lacking luster; having little or no shine, gloss, or brightness
lack of shine or gloss

Examples

The lustrelessness of the old painting became apparent after years of neglect.
Her hair had a certain lustrelessness that suggested illness or fatigue.
The documentary was criticized for the lustrelessness of its cinematography.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: luster - shine, gloss, or brightness
  • Synonym: dullness - the quality of lacking brightness or interest

Etymology

From 'lustreless' (without luster) + '-ness' (forming nouns denoting a state or quality).
