Lithophragma affinis

1. NOUN

/ lɪˌθɑːˈfræɡmə əˈfɪnɪs / (AmE), / lɪˌθɒˈfræɡmə əˈfɪnɪs / (BrE)
A perennial herb native to California, grown for its racemose white flowers with widely spreading petals; sometimes classified in the genus Tellima.
a California perennial herb with white racemose flowers

Examples

The native plant garden features several Lithophragma affinis blooming in early spring.
Botanists debate whether Lithophragma affinis should be placed in the genus Tellima.
Cultivating Lithophragma affinis requires well-drained soil and partial shade.
