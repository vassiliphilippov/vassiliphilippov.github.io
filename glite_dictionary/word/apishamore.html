apishamore

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪpɪˈʃæmɔr / (AmE), / ˌeɪpɪˈʃæmɔː / (BrE)
A saddle blanket made of buffalo hide, traditionally used by Native Americans or cowboys.
saddle blanket made of buffalo hide

Examples

The old trunk contained a beautifully decorated apishamore from the 19th century.
He spread the apishamore over his horse’s back before saddling up for the long ride.
Collectors of Western memorabilia treasure an authentic buffalo-hide apishamore for its craftsmanship and history.

Etymology

From American Spanish 'apishamó', likely from a Native American language.
