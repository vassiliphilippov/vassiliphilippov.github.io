Brassica oleracea

1. NOUN

/ brəˌsɪkə oʊˈlɛəriə / (AmE), / brəˌsɪkə əʊˈlɪərɪə / (BrE)
a plant species of the cabbage family that includes vegetables such as cabbage, broccoli, cauliflower, kale, and Brussels sprouts
species of plant including cabbage, broccoli, etc.

Examples

Many common vegetables, such as broccoli and cauliflower, come from Brassica oleracea.
Farmers have selectively bred Brassica oleracea to create a wide variety of edible plants.
The genetic diversity within Brassica oleracea allows for many types of cabbages and kales.

Etymology

From Latin: Brassica (cabbage) and oleracea (vegetable, of vegetables).

2. NOUN

/ brəˌsɪkə oʊˈlɛəriə / (AmE), / brəˌsɪkə əʊˈlɪəriə / (BrE)
a species of wild plant native to western coastal Europe, the wild ancestor of cultivated cabbages and related vegetables
species of wild cabbage

Examples

Modern cabbages, broccoli, and kale are all derived from Brassica oleracea.
Wild populations of Brassica oleracea grow naturally on the coastal cliffs of western Europe.
Scientists often study Brassica oleracea to understand the domestication of various vegetables.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cabbage - a leafy green vegetable derived from Brassica oleracea

Etymology

From Latin: 'brassica' (cabbage) and 'oleracea' (vegetable, garden plant).
