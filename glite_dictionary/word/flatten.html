to flatten

1. VERB

/ ˈflætən / (AmE), / ˈflætən / (BrE)
to make something flat or flatter by pressing or beating it down
make flat or flatter

Examples

Use a rolling pin to flatten the dough before baking.
Daily exercises can help you flatten your stomach over time.
The construction crew worked all day to flatten the uneven road surface.

Synset Examples

flatten a road
flatten your stomach with these exercises

Related Terms

  • Synonym: level - make or become level or even
  • Synonym: crush - press or squeeze with force to make flat or destroy shape
  • Antonym: to inflate - fill with air or gas; make something expand

Etymology

From Old English 'flett' (flat surface) + -en (verb-forming suffix).

2. VERB

/ ˈflætən / (AmE), / ˈflætən / (BrE)
to become flat or flatter; to lose height or unevenness and become level or smooth
become flat or flatter

Examples

Over time, the sand dunes flatten under the strong winds.
The batter will flatten as it bakes in the oven.
After the heavy rainfall, the field seemed to flatten and lose its natural curves.

Synset Examples

The landscape flattened

Related Terms

  • Synonym: level - to make or become level or even
  • Antonym: raise - to lift or move to a higher position

Etymology

Formed from the adjective 'flat' + the verb-forming suffix '-en' (Old English origin).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00464382-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈflætən / (AmE), / ˈflætən / (BrE)
(in music) to lower the pitch of (a note) by a semitone or half step.
lower (a musical note) by a semitone

Examples

The singer chose to flatten the note for dramatic effect in the chorus.
The composer instructs musicians to flatten every B in this section of the score.
You must flatten that G when you play this jazz standard.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to sharpen - raise the pitch of (a musical note) by a semitone
