Sirach

1. PROPN

/ ˈsaɪˌræk / (AmE), / ˈsɪəræk / (BrE)
an Apocryphal book of the Old Testament, mainly consisting of proverbs and maxims, also known as Ecclesiasticus.
Apocryphal book of proverbs and maxims

Examples

Many passages from Sirach closely resemble those in the book of Proverbs.
The wisdom literature of Sirach has influenced both Jewish and Christian traditions.
She cited Sirach when discussing ethical teachings in the seminar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ecclesiasticus - another name for the Book of Sirach

Etymology

From the Greek Seirach, after the traditional author Jesus, son of Sirach.
