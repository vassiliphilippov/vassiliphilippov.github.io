calamari

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæləˈmɑri / (AmE), / ˌkæləˈmɑːri / (BrE)
Squid, especially as prepared and served as food, often in Mediterranean or Italian cuisine.
squid served as food

Examples

We ordered a plate of calamari as an appetizer at the Italian restaurant.
The chef deep-fried the calamari until it was golden and crispy.
Some people prefer their calamari grilled rather than fried.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squid - a sea animal with a long body and ten arms

Etymology

From Italian 'calamari', plural of 'calamaro' (squid), from Late Latin 'calamarium' (ink pot, pen case).
