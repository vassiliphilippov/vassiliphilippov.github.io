political

1. ADJ

/ pəˈlɪdɪkəl / (AmE), / pəˈlɪtɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to ideas, beliefs, or opinions about power, authority, or social relationships in society
relating to power, authority, or social beliefs

Examples

She enjoys debating political issues with her friends over dinner.
His political views have changed over the years as he learned more about global affairs.
The article sparked a political controversy that lasted for weeks.

Synset Examples

political opinions

Related Terms

  • Antonym: apolitical - not interested or involved in politics or political affairs
  • Degree Higher: partisan - strongly supporting a particular political party or cause

Etymology

From Greek politikos (of citizens or the state), via Latin politicus.

2. ADJ

/ pəˈlɪtɪkəl / (AmE), / pəˈlɪtɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the government, public affairs, or the activities, ideas, or policies of politicians or political parties.
relating to politics or government

Examples

The mayor's decision to support the project was clearly a political move.
There are ongoing debates about the political implications of climate change.
He avoids discussing political issues in the workplace.

Synset Examples

calling a meeting is a political act in itself
political pressure
a political machine
political office
political policy

Related Terms

  • Antonym: apolitical - not interested or involved in politics

Etymology

From Greek politikos, from politēs (citizen), from polis (city).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01819670-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ pəˈlɪdɪkəl / (AmE), / pəˈlɪtɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to government, the profession of governing, or public affairs as distinguished from administration or business.
relating to government or governing

Examples

She has spent her entire life in political office.
Many students are considering political careers after graduation.
The country is facing a major political crisis this year.

Synset Examples

political career

Related Terms

  • Synonym: governmental - relating to a government or the act of governing
  • Antonym: apolitical - not interested or involved in politics

Etymology

From Latin politicus, from Greek politikos (of citizens, civil, civic).
