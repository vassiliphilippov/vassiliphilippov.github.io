scintilla

1. NOUN

/ sɪnˈtɪlə / (AmE), / sɪnˈtɪlə / (BrE)
A tiny or scarcely detectable amount; a very small trace or spark of something.
a tiny amount; trace

Examples

There was not a scintilla of doubt in her mind about the decision.
His accusations lacked a scintilla of evidence to support them.
The team showed not a scintilla of fear despite the tough competition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trace - a very small amount or sign of something
  • Synonym: shred - a very small piece or amount
  • Synonym: iota - an extremely small amount or part

Etymology

From Latin 'scintilla' (spark).

2. NOUN

/ sɪnˈtɪlə / (AmE), / sɪnˈtɪlə / (BrE)
A tiny spark or trace of a quality or feeling; a minute glittering particle.
a tiny spark or trace

Examples

There wasn't a scintilla of truth in his statement.
Not even a scintilla of light entered the dark room.
Her eyes held a scintilla of amusement as she listened.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trace - a very small amount
  • Synonym: iota - an extremely small amount

Etymology

From Latin 'scintilla' (spark).
