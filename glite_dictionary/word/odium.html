odium

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ.di.əm / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.di.əm / (BrE)
General or widespread hatred and disgust directed toward someone as a result of their actions.
widespread hatred and disgust

Examples

After the scandal, the politician became the subject of public odium and ridicule.
His dishonest actions brought lasting odium upon his family.
She endured much odium for her outspoken views on the subject.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contempt - the feeling that a person or a thing is beneath consideration; disdain
  • Synonym: disgrace - loss of reputation or respect as the result of a dishonorable action
  • Antonym: admiration - respect and warm approval

Etymology

From Latin 'odium' (hatred).

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊdiəm / (AmE), / ˈəʊdiəm / (BrE)
general or widespread hatred or disgust directed toward someone as a result of their shameful actions
general hatred or disgrace due to shameful behavior

Examples

The politician fell into odium after the scandal was made public.
Her betrayal brought considerable odium upon her family.
He struggled to rebuild his life, living under the odium of his past misdeeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disgrace - loss of respect or honor, especially as a result of bad behavior
  • Synonym: contempt - the feeling that a person or a thing is beneath consideration, worthless, or deserving scorn
  • Antonym: esteem - respect and admiration, typically for a person

Etymology

From Latin odium (hatred).
