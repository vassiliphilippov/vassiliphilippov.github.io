whizz

1. VERB

/ wɪz / (AmE), / wɪz / (BrE)
to move quickly through the air or along a path, often making a humming or buzzing sound
move quickly, often with a sound

Examples

The bullet whizzed past his head, narrowly missing him.
Children on bicycles whizz down the hill after school.
A red sports car whizzed by us on the highway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zoom - to move or travel very quickly
  • Synonym: rush - to move with urgent haste

Etymology

Imitative of a whizzing or buzzing sound; dates from the late 19th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02059445-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ wɪz / (AmE), / wɪz / (BrE)
a person who is extremely skilled or clever at something
a highly skilled person
informal

Examples

She's a real whizz at solving complex math problems.
Our new IT specialist is a whizz with computer networks.
If you need help with your taxes, ask Jason—he's a whizz in finance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expert - a person who has a comprehensive and authoritative knowledge of or skill in a particular area
  • Synonym: genius - a person with exceptional intellectual or creative power or other natural ability
  • Antonym: novice - a person new to and inexperienced in a job or situation
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09781932-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ wɪz / (AmE), / wɪz / (BrE)
to make a soft, continuous, swishing or hissing sound, often as something moves quickly through the air
make a soft swishing sound

Examples

The bike whizzed past us, leaving only a faint sound behind.
You could hear the arrow whizz through the air during the archery contest.
A bullet whizzed overhead, making everyone duck for cover.

Synset Examples

the motor whirred
the car engine purred

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whirr - make a low, continuous, regular sound
  • Synonym: buzz - make a low, humming or murmuring sound
  • Synonym: hum - make a low, steady, continuous sound

Etymology

Imitative origin, representing the sound itself.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02193033-v
Synset POS: v
