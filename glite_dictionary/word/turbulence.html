turbulence

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɜr.bjə.ləns / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.bjə.ləns / (BrE)
A state of violent disturbance and disorder, especially in politics or society.
violent disturbance or disorder

Examples

The collapse of the government plunged the country into political turbulence.
Periods of social turbulence often lead to major reforms.
The CEO was hired to lead the company through a time of economic turbulence.

Synset Examples

the industrial revolution was a period of great turbulence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unrest - a state of dissatisfaction, disturbance, and agitation in a group of people
  • Synonym: instability - lack of stability, especially in social or political situations
  • Antonym: calm - a state of peace and tranquility

Etymology

From Latin turbulentia (disorder, commotion), from turbulēns (full of commotion).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɜrbjələns / (AmE), / ˈtɜːbjʊləns / (BrE)
a state of instability or irregular motion in the atmosphere, often causing sudden changes in air movement and discomfort during flight
atmospheric instability or disturbance

Examples

The plane encountered severe turbulence as it flew through the storm clouds.
Passengers were advised to fasten their seatbelts because of unexpected turbulence.
Clear air turbulence can occur even when the sky looks calm and cloudless.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - absence of disturbance or movement in the atmosphere

Etymology

From Latin 'turbulentia' (disorder, commotion), from 'turbulentus' (full of commotion).

3. NOUN

/ ˈtɜrbjələns / (AmE), / ˈtɜːbjʊləns / (BrE)
Irregular, chaotic movement of a liquid or gas, often causing swirling or disturbance.
irregular, chaotic fluid movement

Examples

The airplane experienced severe turbulence as it flew through the storm.
Engineers study turbulence to improve the design of ships and aircraft.
Waves of turbulence made it difficult for the divers to see underwater.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: laminar flow - smooth, orderly flow of a fluid

Etymology

From Latin 'turbulentia', from 'turbulentus' meaning 'full of commotion'.
