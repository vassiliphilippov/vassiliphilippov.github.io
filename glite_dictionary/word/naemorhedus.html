Naemorhedus

1. NOUN

/ niːˈmɔːrhɛdəs / (AmE), / niːˈmɔːrɛdʊs / (BrE)
A genus of small goat-antelopes known as gorals, native to Asia.
genus of Asian goat-antelopes (gorals)

Examples

Biologists observed a group of Naemorhedus in the Himalayan foothills during the spring survey.
The Naemorhedus species is distinguished by its short horns and agile movements on rocky slopes.
Many conservation programs focus on protecting the habitat of the Naemorhedus due to its declining population.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goral - A small goat-antelope native to mountainous regions of Asia

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on Greek 'naemos' (glade, pasture) and 'hedos' (sitter), referring to habitat and stance.
