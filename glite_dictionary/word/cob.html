cob

1. NOUN

/ kɑb / (AmE), / kɒb / (BrE)
a stocky, short-legged horse, typically used for riding and driving
stocky short-legged horse

Examples

The young rider preferred a cob for her first lessons because of its steady temperament.
Many traditional carriage drivers choose a cob for both riding and harness work.
The cob stood patiently while the children brushed its thick mane.

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from Middle English 'cobbe' (leader, head).

2. NOUN

/ kɑb / (AmE), / kɒb / (BrE)
An adult male swan.
adult male swan

Examples

The elegant cob glided across the pond, leading his family of cygnets.
During mating season, the cob becomes very protective of the nest.
Wildlife photographers waited hours to capture the cob displaying his majestic wings.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pen - adult female swan

Etymology

Of uncertain origin; first recorded in English in the 15th century for various robust or prominent objects or animals, and later applied to swans.

3. NOUN

/ kɑb / (AmE), / kɒb / (BrE)
the edible nut of a hazel tree, especially in reference to certain varieties of Corylus
nut of a hazel tree

Examples

The children gathered cobs from the hazel trees in the autumn.
He enjoyed cracking open a cob and eating the sweet kernel inside.
In some regions, people roast cobs over an open fire as a seasonal treat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hazelnut - the nut of the hazel tree, edible and commonly used in cooking

Etymology

From Middle English 'cobbe', possibly of Old Norse origin, referring to a round object or lump.
