cockle-burr

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑkəlˌbɜr / (AmE), / ˈkɒkl̩ˌbɜː / (BrE)
a coarse weed of the genus Xanthium with spiny burrs that cling to animals or clothing
weed with spiny burrs (Xanthium genus)

Examples

Children often come home with cockle-burrs stuck to their clothes after playing in the fields.
The farmer cursed the stubborn cockle-burr plants taking over his pastures.
Removing cockle-burrs from a dog's fur can be a painful task.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burdock - a rough, weedy plant with burrs that cling to fur or clothing
