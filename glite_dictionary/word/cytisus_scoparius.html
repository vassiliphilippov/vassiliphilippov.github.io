Cytisus scoparius

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈtaɪsəs skoʊˈpɛəriəs / (AmE), / sɪˈtɪsəs skɒˈpɛəriəs / (BrE)
A species of deciduous, erect, spreading shrub native to western Europe, widely cultivated for its bright yellow flowers; commonly known as broom.
a European shrub with yellow flowers (broom)

Examples

The hillside was covered in bright yellow blooms of Cytisus scoparius.
Gardeners often plant Cytisus scoparius for its colorful appearance in spring.
Cytisus scoparius is considered invasive in some regions due to its rapid spread.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broom - a shrub with slender green stems and yellow flowers

Etymology

From Latin: 'Cytisus' is the genus name, 'scoparius' means 'sweeper', referring to its brush-like appearance.
