plica

1. NOUN

/ ˈplaɪkə / (AmE), / ˈpliːkə / (BrE)
a fold or ridge, especially of skin, membrane, or muscle, in the body
fold or ridge in the body
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully examined the plica in the patient's joint before proceeding.
An abnormal plica can sometimes cause pain in the knee.
The nasal plica was inflamed and required treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fold - a crease or bend in something, especially in tissue or fabric

Etymology

From Latin plica (a fold), from plicāre (to fold).
