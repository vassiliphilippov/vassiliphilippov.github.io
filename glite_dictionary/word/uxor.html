uxor

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌk.sɔr / (AmE), / ˈʌk.sɔː / (BrE)
(legal, rare) A wife; used especially in legal contexts or Latin phrases.
wife (legal/Latin term)
legal

Examples

In the legal document, her status was recorded simply as uxor.
He was accused of uxoricide, a crime referring to the killing of one's uxor.
The deed listed the land as owned by James Smith et uxor, meaning James Smith and his wife.

Etymology

From Latin uxor (wife).
