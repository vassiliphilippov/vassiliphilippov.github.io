Pulicaria dysenterica

1. NOUN

/ ˌpjuːlɪˈkɛəriə ˌdɪsən'tɛrɪkə / (AmE), / ˌpjuːlɪˈkeəriə ˌdɪsənˈtɛrɪkə / (BrE)
a hairy perennial Eurasian herb with yellow, daisylike flowers, traditionally believed to repel or destroy fleas
herb with yellow flowers, repels fleas

Examples

Pulicaria dysenterica is commonly found in damp meadows throughout Europe.
Some gardeners plant Pulicaria dysenterica to help keep fleas away from their homes.
The yellow flowers of Pulicaria dysenterica bloom from midsummer to early autumn.

Etymology

From Latin: Pulicaria refers to fleas, and dysenterica to 'dysentery', reflecting the plant's traditional medicinal uses.
