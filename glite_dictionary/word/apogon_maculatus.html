Apogon maculatus

1. NOUN

/ ˌæpəˌɡɑn ˌmækjəˈleɪtəs / (AmE), / ˌapəˌɡɒn ˌmakjʊˈleɪtəs / (BrE)
A species of cardinalfish found in tropical Atlantic coastal waters, known for its reddish coloration and nocturnal habits.
a species of cardinalfish

Examples

The Apogon maculatus is often seen hiding among rocks during the day.
Marine biologists studied the diet of Apogon maculatus along the coral reefs.
Aquarium enthusiasts prize Apogon maculatus for its vibrant color and peaceful nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cardinalfish - a family of small, brightly colored marine fishes

Etymology

From Latin: Apogon (genus of cardinalfish), maculatus meaning 'spotted'.
