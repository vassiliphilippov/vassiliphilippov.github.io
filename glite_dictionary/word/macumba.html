macumba

1. NOUN

/ məˈkʊm.bə / (AmE), / məˈkʌm.bə / (BrE)
A genre of popular Brazilian dance music, originally derived from the practices of the macumba religious cult.
Brazilian dance music with religious roots

Examples

The band played a lively set of macumba that had everyone dancing.
She grew up listening to macumba at family gatherings in Rio de Janeiro.
The rhythms of macumba reflect the influence of African spiritual traditions in Brazil.

Etymology

From Portuguese 'macumba', originally referring to Afro-Brazilian religious practices.

2. NOUN

/ məˈkumbə / (AmE), / məˈkʊmbə / (BrE)
A form of Afro-Brazilian religious practice involving rituals, beliefs, and ceremonies of African origin, often considered a folk or syncretic religion.
Afro-Brazilian religious practice

Examples

Many tourists attended a macumba ceremony during their stay in Brazil.
Anthropologists have studied the influence of macumba on local culture and traditions.
Some misconceptions surround the practices of macumba, often due to its syncretic nature.

Etymology

From Portuguese 'macumba', probably of Bantu origin.

3. NOUN

/ məˈkʊm.bə / (AmE), / məˈkʊm.bə / (BrE)
a Brazilian religious practice of African origin, combining elements such as voodoo, singing, chanting, and dancing
Brazilian religious cult of African origin

Examples

There are several groups in Brazil that still practice macumba today.
Anthropologists have studied the rituals of macumba in Rio de Janeiro.
The festival featured music and dancing inspired by macumba traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: voodoo - a religion practiced chiefly in Caribbean countries combining African, Roman Catholic, and indigenous traditions

Etymology

From Portuguese 'macumba', of African origin.
