detection

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈtɛkʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈtɛkʃən / (BrE)
the process of determining that a signal or message has been received, especially in electronics or communications.
recognizing that a signal is being received

Examples

The radar system's detection of the airplane triggered an automatic alert.
Signal detection is crucial in modern wireless communications.
Improved antenna design enhances the detection of weak signals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reception - the act of receiving a signal or message

Etymology

From Latin detectio, from detegere (to uncover, discover).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈtɛkʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈtɛkʃən / (BrE)
the act of noticing or discovering the presence or existence of something, especially something hidden or not immediately obvious
the act of discovering something hidden

Examples

Early detection of the disease greatly improves the chances of successful treatment.
The detection of smoke triggered the fire alarm.
Her sharp eyes led to the detection of the small, hidden camera.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discovery - the process of finding something for the first time
  • Antonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge or awareness about something detected

Etymology

From Latin detectio, from detegere (to uncover).

Examples

Thanks to new technology, detection of earthquakes has become more accurate and timely.
Airport security uses various methods for the detection of dangerous items in luggage.
