slasher

1. NOUN

/ ˈslæʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈslæʃə / (BrE)
a weapon, such as a sword or dagger, designed or used for slashing
a weapon used for slashing

Examples

The knight unsheathed his slasher and prepared for battle.
Archaeologists discovered an ancient slasher among the ruins.
The museum's display included a ceremonial slasher from the 12th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈslæʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈslæʃə / (BrE)
a person who attacks or injures others by slashing them with a sharp object
person who injures by slashing

Examples

The police are searching for the slasher who attacked three people last night.
The city was gripped by fear after reports of a slasher in the neighborhood.
The defendant denied being the slasher responsible for the series of assaults.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attacker - a person who attacks someone
  • Antonym: victim - a person harmed or injured by someone else

Etymology

From 'slash' (to cut with a sweeping motion) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
