attribution

1. NOUN

/ ˌætrɪˈbjuːʃən / (AmE), / ˌætrɪˈbjuːʃən / (BrE)
the act of regarding something as being caused by or belonging to a particular person or thing
assigning a cause or quality to something

Examples

The attribution of intelligence to animals varies among scientists.
Many religions involve the attribution of supernatural powers to certain individuals.
There was disagreement over the attribution of blame for the failed project.

Synset Examples

the attribution of language to birds
the ascription to me of honors I had not earned

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ascription - the act of assigning a quality to something

Etymology

From Latin attributio (assignment), from attribuere (to assign).

2. NOUN

/ ˌætrəˈbjuːʃən / (AmE), / ˌætrɪˈbjuːʃən / (BrE)
the act of regarding something as being caused by or the result of a particular person, thing, or event
assigning cause or source

Examples

The attribution of the quote to Shakespeare has been challenged by historians.
Scientists debated the attribution of the disease outbreak to contaminated water.
Her success was frequently given attribution to her hard work and determination.

Synset Examples

the attribution of lighting to an expression of God's wrath
he questioned the attribution of the painting to Picasso

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ascription - the act of assigning something to a particular cause, source, or author

Etymology

From Latin attribuere (to assign), via attributio (assignment).
