pelf

1. NOUN

/ pɛlf / (AmE), / pɛlf / (BrE)
money or wealth, especially when regarded with contempt or as ill-gotten
money, especially of dubious origin

Examples

He cared nothing for pelf and lived a simple life devoted to learning.
The corrupt official was driven by the lust for pelf rather than public service.
Novels of the era often warned against the pursuit of pelf at the expense of character.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lucre - money, especially when regarded as sordid or distasteful

Etymology

From Old French 'pelfre' (booty, stolen goods).

2. NOUN

/ pɛlf / (AmE), / pɛlf / (BrE)
money or wealth, especially when regarded as sordid or gained dishonestly
wealth or money, often with negative connotation
archaic

Examples

The nobleman filled his coffers with pelf from the peasants' taxes.
She cared only for pelf, not for honor or love.
Throughout history, many wars have been fought over pelf and power.

Synset Examples

He accumulated quite a bit of pelf over the years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wealth - abundance of valuable possessions or money
  • Synonym: riches - large amounts of money and valuable possessions
  • Antonym: poverty - state of being extremely poor

Etymology

From Old French *pelfre* (booty, stolen goods), related to pilfer.
