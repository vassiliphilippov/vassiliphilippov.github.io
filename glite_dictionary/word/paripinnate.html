paripinnate

1. ADJ

/ ˌpɛriˈpɪneɪt / (AmE), / ˌparɪˈpɪneɪt / (BrE)
(of a leaf) having leaflets arranged in pairs along the axis with a terminal pair at the apex, having no single terminal leaflet.
having paired leaflets at the apex (botanical)

Examples

The leaves of this species are paripinnate, ending in a pair of leaflets rather than a single one.
In botany class, we learned to distinguish paripinnate leaves from imparipinnate ones.
Acacia trees often have paripinnate foliage, with even pairs of leaflets along the stem.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: imparipinnate - (of a leaf) pinnate with a single terminal leaflet

Etymology

From Latin 'pari' (equal, paired) + 'pinnatus' (feathered, winged).
