scoffer

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɔfər / (AmE), / ˈskɒfə / (BrE)
A person who mocks, jeers at, or treats someone or something with contempt or derision.
person who mocks or jeers

Examples

The internet is full of scoffers who delight in mocking others anonymously.
He was known as a scoffer of traditional beliefs, constantly challenging those around him.
Despite the scoffers, she continued to pursue her unconventional ideas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mockery - ridicule or contempt; subject of laughter
  • Synonym: jeerer - a person who jeers or shouts derisively
  • Antonym: admirer - someone who looks at something or someone with approval or respect

Etymology

From the verb 'scoff', probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Old Norse skopa (mockery).

2. NOUN

/ ˈskɔfər / (AmE), / ˈskɒfə / (BrE)
a person who eats food rapidly and greedily
person who eats greedily or quickly

Examples

The children were such scoffers that the pie was gone in minutes.
You could always spot the scoffer at the party by the empty plate.
He was labeled a scoffer after devouring his meal before anyone else had finished their soup.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glutton - a person who eats excessively

Etymology

From verb 'scoff' (to eat greedily) + -er (agent noun suffix).
