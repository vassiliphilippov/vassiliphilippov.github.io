Tuareg

1. NOUN

/ ˈtwɑːrɛg / (AmE), / ˈtwɑːrɛɡ / (BrE)
the Berber language dialect spoken by the Tuareg people of the Sahara region
the Berber dialect of the Tuareg people

Examples

The Tuareg language is known for its unique script and phonetic structure.
He is fluent in Tuareg as well as several other Saharan languages.
Many traditional songs are preserved in the Tuareg dialect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Berber - the family of closely related languages indigenous to North Africa

Etymology

From French 'Touareg', from Arabic 'Tawāriq', referring to the Tuareg people.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtwɑːrɛɡ / (AmE), / ˈtwɑːrɛɡ / (BrE)
a member of a nomadic Berber people inhabiting the Sahara region of North Africa
member of a nomadic people of the Sahara

Examples

The Tuareg have a rich tradition of poetry and music passed down through generations.
A group of Tuareg traders crossed the Sahara with their camels and salt.
She learned about the customs and history of the Tuareg during her travels in Niger.

Etymology

From Arabic Tuwāreg (طوارق), plural of Tārgi, name for a member of this group.
