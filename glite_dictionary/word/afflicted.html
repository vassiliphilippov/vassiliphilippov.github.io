afflicted

1. ADJ

/ əˈflɪktɪd / (AmE), / əˈflɪktɪd / (BrE)
Suffering from pain, illness, or trouble, especially as a result of a disease or problem.
suffering from pain or illness

Examples

She has devoted her life to helping children afflicted by rare diseases.
After the disaster, many families were afflicted with grief and loss.
The small town was afflicted with a sudden outbreak of influenza.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unwell - not in good health; sick or ill
  • Antonym: healthy - in good health; not suffering from illness or injury

Etymology

From Latin afflictus, past participle of affligere (to strike down).

2. ADJ

/ əˈflɪktɪd / (AmE), / əˈflɪktɪd / (BrE)
severely affected by pain, suffering, or disease.
severely affected by illness or suffering

Examples

Many communities remain afflicted by poverty and lack of medical care.
The patient was afflicted with a rare genetic disorder from birth.
She felt compassion for the children afflicted by the earthquake.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unaffected - not influenced or harmed by something
  • Synonym: suffering - experiencing pain or hardship

Etymology

From Latin afflictus, past participle of affligere (to strike down).
