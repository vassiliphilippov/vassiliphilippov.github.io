Galatians

1. PROPN

/ ɡəˈleɪʃənz / (AmE), / ɡəˈleɪʃənz / (BrE)
A book of the New Testament containing the epistle from Saint Paul to the Christian community in Galatia.
New Testament epistle by Saint Paul

Examples

Many scholars study Galatians to understand Paul's theology and his relationship with early Christian communities.
The church is holding a Bible study series on the book of Galatians this month.
In Galatians, Paul emphasizes that faith in Christ is more important than observing the law.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ephesians - A New Testament book containing the epistle to the Ephesians
  • Synonym: Romans - A New Testament book containing the epistle to the Romans
  • Synonym: Philippians - A New Testament book containing the epistle to the Philippians

Etymology

From Latin 'Galatia', referring to a region in Asia Minor; the epistle was addressed to its people.
