Hydromyinae

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌdrɑːmiˈɪniː / (AmE), / haɪˌdrɒmiˈiːniː / (BrE)
A subfamily of rodents commonly known as water rats, native to Australia and New Guinea.
subfamily of water rats (rodents)

Examples

The Hydromyinae are adapted to a semi-aquatic lifestyle in the wetlands of New Guinea.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Hydromyinae subfamily last year.
Unlike many rodents, members of the Hydromyinae have webbed feet for swimming.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water rat - a common name for aquatic or semi-aquatic rodents
  • Degree Higher: rodent - a mammal of the order Rodentia

Etymology

From Greek 'hydro-' (water) + 'mys, myos' (mouse, rat), used in modern taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ haɪˌdrɒmiˈɪni / (AmE), / haɪˌdrɒmɪˈiːniː / (BrE)
A subfamily of rodents commonly known as the water rats, native to Australia and New Guinea.
rodent subfamily; water rats

Examples

Scientists have identified several new species within the Hydromyinae in New Guinea.
The Hydromyinae are uniquely adapted to aquatic life in Australia and nearby islands.
Conservationists are monitoring the habitats of Hydromyinae to protect them from extinction.

Etymology

From Greek 'hydro' (water) + 'mys' (mouse), with zoological Latin ending '-inae' (subfamily).
