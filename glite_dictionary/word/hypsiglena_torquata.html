Hypsiglena torquata

1. NOUN

/ hɪp.sɪˈɡliː.nə tɔːrˈkweɪ.tə / (AmE), / hɪp.sɪˈɡliː.nə tɔːˈkwɑː.tə / (BrE)
A small, nocturnal snake species native to the western United States and Mexico, commonly known as the night snake.
a species of nocturnal snake

Examples

The Hypsiglena torquata is most active at night, preying on small lizards and amphibians.
Researchers spotted a Hypsiglena torquata slithering across the desert just after sunset.
Many herpetologists study the habitat preferences of Hypsiglena torquata in the southwestern United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: night snake - common name for Hypsiglena torquata, a nocturnal snake species

Etymology

From Greek hypsi- (high, lofty) + glēna (socket of the eye), and Latin torquatus (collared), referring to distinctive markings.
