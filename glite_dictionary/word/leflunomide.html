leflunomide

1. NOUN

/ ləˈfluːnəˌmaɪd / (AmE), / lɛˈfluːnəˌmaɪd / (BrE)
a medication used to treat rheumatoid arthritis by inhibiting the proliferation of certain white blood cells, thereby reducing inflammation
drug for rheumatoid arthritis treatment
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed leflunomide to help manage her rheumatoid arthritis symptoms.
Patients taking leflunomide may require regular blood tests to monitor liver function.
Research shows that leflunomide can slow the progression of joint damage in certain autoimmune diseases.

Etymology

From le- (a French chemical prefix) + fluoro- (containing fluorine) + -nomide (from isoxazole carboxamide group); coined in scientific nomenclature.
