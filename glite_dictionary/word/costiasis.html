costiasis

1. NOUN

/ kɒsˈtaɪəsɪs / (AmE), / kɒsˈtaɪəsɪs / (BrE)
A fatal disease of freshwater fish caused by a flagellated protozoan that invades the skin.
disease of freshwater fish caused by a protozoan
medical

Examples

The outbreak of costiasis devastated the population of goldfish in the pond.
Fish infected with costiasis often show lesions and excessive mucus on their skin.
Prompt treatment is essential to prevent costiasis from spreading in an aquarium.

Etymology

From the genus name *Costia*, the causative protozoan, and the suffix -iasis (disease).
