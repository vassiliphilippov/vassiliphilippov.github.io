incompatibility

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnkəmˌpætəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌɪnkəmˌpætəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The state of being unable to exist or work together without conflict or difficulty.
state of being unable to coexist or work together

Examples

A high degree of incompatibility between the software programs caused frequent system crashes.
The couple separated due to fundamental incompatibility in their life goals and values.
Some materials have an incompatibility that makes it unsafe to store them together.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: compatibility - the quality of being able to exist or work together without conflict

Etymology

From Latin 'incompatibilis' meaning 'not compatible', from 'in-' (not) + 'compatibilis' (compatible).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪnkəmˌpætəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌɪnkəmˌpætəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
(Medical) The condition in which the immune system tries to reject foreign material, such as transfused blood or transplanted tissue, because it does not match the body's own tissues.
immune rejection of foreign material
medical

Examples

Incompatibility between donor and recipient blood can lead to dangerous transfusion reactions.
Doctors tested for tissue incompatibility before performing the organ transplant.
Immunological incompatibility was the main reason for the body's rejection of the graft.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: compatibility - the ability of things to exist or work together without conflict

Etymology

From Medieval Latin incompatibilitas (inability to exist together).

3. NOUN

/ ˌɪnkəmˌpætəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌɪnkəmˌpætəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the state of being such that two or more things, especially statements or propositions, cannot all be true or valid at the same time
state of things that cannot coexist or be true together

Examples

There is a clear incompatibility between the witness’s statement and the evidence presented.
Logical incompatibility makes it impossible for both claims to be correct simultaneously.
The incompatibility of these theories was the subject of much debate among philosophers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contradiction - a combination of statements, ideas, or features which are opposed to one another
  • Antonym: consistency - the quality of being able to exist or be true together

Etymology

From Medieval Latin incompatibilitas, from incompatible + -ity.
