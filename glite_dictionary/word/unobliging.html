unobliging

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.əˈblaɪ.dʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌʌn.əˈblaɪ.dʒɪŋ / (BrE)
not willing to help or do favors for others; uncooperative or unaccommodating.
not willing to help; uncooperative

Examples

The hotel staff were surprisingly unobliging when we asked for extra towels.
She found her new coworker unobliging and difficult to work with on group projects.
Despite my polite request, the clerk remained unobliging and refused to assist me.

Synset Examples

the unaccommodating bus driver pulled out while she was banging on the door

Related Terms

  • Antonym: obliging - willing to help or do favors for others
  • Synonym: unaccommodating - not helpful or accommodating

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'obliging' (willing to help).
