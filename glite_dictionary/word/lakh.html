lakh

1. NOUN

/ lɑk / (AmE), / læk / (BrE)
A unit in the numbering system of South Asia equal to one hundred thousand (100,000).
South Asian number: 100,000

Examples

His company sold more than two lakh phones last year.
The new stadium has a capacity of over one lakh spectators.
She donated one lakh rupees to charity.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: million - A cardinal number equal to one thousand thousands (1,000,000)
  • Degree Lower: thousand - A cardinal number equal to 1,000

Etymology

From Hindi 'lākh', from Sanskrit 'lakṣa' (a hundred thousand, mark, sign).
