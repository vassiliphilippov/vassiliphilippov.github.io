hackle

1. NOUN

/ ˈhækəl / (AmE), / ˈhækəl / (BrE)
a long, slender feather found on the necks of birds such as turkeys and pheasants
long neck feather on certain birds

Examples

The turkey's hackle feathers shimmered brightly in the sunlight.
Pheasants are recognized by the distinctive colored hackle feathers on their necks.
Fly tiers often use rooster hackle to craft realistic fishing lures.

Etymology

From Middle English *hakel*, of Germanic origin, related to Dutch *hak* (hook).

2. VERB

/ ˈhæk.əl / (AmE), / ˈhæk.əl / (BrE)
to comb (flax, hemp, or similar fibers) with a heckle in order to separate, straighten, or clean them
comb fibers such as flax or hemp

Examples

Before spinning, the workers would hackle the flax to remove impurities.
In traditional rope-making, hemp is often hackled several times for quality.
The artisan skillfully began to hackle the fibers, preparing them for weaving.

Synset Examples

heckle hemp or flax

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heckle - to comb (flax, hemp, etc.) with a heckle

Etymology

From Middle English *hekelen*, from heckle, a comb used for dressing flax or hemp.
