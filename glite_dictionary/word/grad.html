grad

1. NOUN

/ ɡræd / (AmE), / ɡræd / (BrE)
a person who has received a degree or diploma from a school, college, or university
a school or college graduate
informal

Examples

Many recent grads are searching for their first full-time job.
As a grad of this university, she was invited to the alumni dinner.
The company is looking to hire new grads with fresh ideas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: graduate - a person who has completed a course of study
  • Antonym: undergraduate - a student at a college or university who has not yet received a degree

Etymology

Shortened form of 'graduate', first attested in the 19th century.

2. NOUN

/ ɡræd / (AmE), / ɡræd / (BrE)
a unit of angle equal to one-hundredth of a right angle, or 0.9 degrees, used especially in surveying and navigation.
unit of angle (1/100 of a right angle)

Examples

The horizontal angle was measured as 50 grads by the surveyor.
One grad is equivalent to 0.9 degrees in the metric system of angular measurement.
Mapping systems sometimes use grad instead of degrees for precision.

Etymology

From French 'grade', from Latin 'gradus' meaning 'step, degree'.
