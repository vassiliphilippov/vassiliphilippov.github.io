to reactivate

1. VERB

/ riˈæktɪˌveɪt / (AmE), / riˈæktɪveɪt / (BrE)
to make something active again after it has been inactive or stopped
make active again

Examples

You will need to reactivate your account if you want to use the service again.
The company decided to reactivate its dormant website after several years offline.
He quickly reactivated the security system once he returned home from vacation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to activate - to make active; cause to function
  • Antonym: to deactivate - to make inactive or stop from working

Etymology

From re- (again) + activate (from Latin activare).
