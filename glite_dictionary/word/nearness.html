nearness

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɪr.nəs / (AmE), / ˈnɪə.nɪs / (BrE)
The state or quality of being close in space or distance.
quality of being close in space

Examples

The nearness of the river made the area popular for fishing.
She was comforted by the nearness of her friends during difficult times.
The nearness of the deadline made everyone work faster.

Synset Examples

the sudden closeness of the dock sent him into action

Related Terms

  • Antonym: distance - the amount of space between two points
  • Synonym: proximity - the state of being near or close

Etymology

From 'near' + '-ness', forming a noun indicating the state or quality.
