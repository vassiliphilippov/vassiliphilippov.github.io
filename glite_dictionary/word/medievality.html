medievality

1. NOUN

/ mɛˌdiːiˈvæləti / (AmE), / mɛˌdiːiˈvæləti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being characteristic of the Middle Ages.
medieval quality or character

Examples

The novel's attention to detail lends a distinct medievality to its setting and atmosphere.
Discussions about the medievality of certain traditions reveal their deep historical roots.
Some museums strive for authenticity in reproducing the sense of medievality within their exhibits.

Synset Examples

Also the increased archaeological interest in modernity has made the issue of medievality topical, since modernisation implies a certain conception of pre-modernity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: medievalism - the system of beliefs, customs, or style typical of the Middle Ages

Etymology

Formed from 'medieval' + '-ity', following the pattern of Latin-derived abstract nouns.
