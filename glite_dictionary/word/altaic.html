Altaic

1. ADJ

/ ælˈteɪɪk / (AmE), / ælˈteɪɪk / (BrE)
relating to the Altaic languages or to the peoples who speak them.
of the Altaic languages or peoples

Examples

Several languages in Central Asia are considered Altaic in origin.
The Altaic language family includes Turkish, Mongolic, and Tungusic languages.
Scholars debate the validity of the Altaic hypothesis in linguistics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Turkic - relating to the Turkic languages, a major group in the Altaic family
  • Antonym: Uralic - relating to the Uralic language family, sometimes discussed alongside Altaic

Etymology

From Altai, a mountain range in Central Asia, plus the suffix -ic.

2. NOUN

/ ælˈteɪɪk / (AmE), / ælˈteɪɪk / (BrE)
a hypothesized family of related languages spoken in Asia and southeastern Europe, including Turkic, Mongolic, and sometimes Tungusic languages
a hypothesized language family in Asia and Europe

Examples

Linguists once considered Turkish, Mongolian, and Manchu to belong to the Altaic language family.
Debate continues about whether Altaic should be classified as a true language family or an area of convergence.
She specializes in the study of Altaic languages and their historical development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Turkic - a major branch of the Altaic languages
  • Synonym: Mongolic - another branch of the proposed Altaic languages

Etymology

From French 'altaïque', from Altai (a mountain range in Central Asia).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06938989-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ælˈteɪɪk / (AmE), / ælˈteɪɪk / (BrE)
a member of any of the peoples who speak a language in the Altaic language group, such as Turkic, Mongolic, or Tungusic languages
member of a people speaking an Altaic language

Examples

The Altaic people have a rich tradition of nomadic lifestyle and horseback riding.
As an Altaic, she proudly preserves the language and customs of her ancestors.
Anthropologists have studied the migration patterns of various Altaic groups across Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Turkic - a member of the peoples speaking Turkic languages
  • Synonym: Mongolic - a member of the peoples speaking Mongolic languages

Etymology

From Altai, a mountain range in Central and East Asia, with -ic forming the adjective and noun.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09709604-n
Synset POS: n
