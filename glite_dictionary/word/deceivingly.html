deceivingly

1. ADV

/ dɪˈsivɪŋli / (AmE), / dɪˈsiːvɪŋli / (BrE)
In a way that gives a misleading impression; so as to deceive or mislead.
in a misleading or deceptive way

Examples

The task appeared deceivingly simple, but required significant skill to complete correctly.
The surface was deceivingly smooth, hiding many imperfections underneath.
Her words were deceivingly friendly, masking her true intentions.

Synset Examples

the exam looked deceptively easy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deceptively - in a way that misleads or gives a false impression

Etymology

Formed from 'deceiving' + adverbial suffix '-ly'; related to the verb 'deceive', from Old French *decevoir*.
