Hirundo rustica

1. NOUN

/ hɪˌrʌndoʊ ˈrʌstɪkə / (AmE), / hɪˌrʌnduː ˈrʌstɪkə / (BrE)
the scientific name for the barn swallow, a widely distributed species of swallow found in North America and Europe
scientific name for the barn swallow

Examples

The Hirundo rustica migrates thousands of miles between continents every year.
Researchers observed that Hirundo rustica prefers to build its nests in human structures like barns.
Many bird enthusiasts travel to wetlands to study the behavior of Hirundo rustica.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barn swallow - a common species of swallow found in barns and open country

Etymology

From Latin: 'hirundo' meaning 'swallow' and 'rustica' meaning 'of the country, rural'.
