to bask

1. VERB

/ bæsk / (AmE), / bɑːsk / (BrE)
to lie or be exposed to a pleasant warmth, especially from the sun
to lie and enjoy warmth, esp. sunlight

Examples

The seals basked in the afternoon sunlight on the rocks.
After a long hike, she basked in the gentle warmth of the spring sun.
They sat on the balcony, basking in the golden glow of the sunset.

Synset Examples

The seals were basking in the sun

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sunbathe - to sit or lie in the sun to get a suntan

Etymology

Perhaps from Old Norse *bask,* related to 'bathe.'

2. VERB

/ bæsk / (AmE), / bɑːsk / (BrE)
to take great pleasure or satisfaction from something, especially attention, praise, or success
take pleasure from praise or success

Examples

The singer basked in the applause of her adoring fans after the concert.
He likes to bask in the attention whenever he achieves something remarkable.
After winning the award, she basked in the admiration of her colleagues.

Synset Examples

She relished her fame and basked in her glory

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revel - to take intense pleasure or satisfaction
  • Synonym: to luxuriate - to enjoy oneself without restraint

Etymology

From Middle English *basken* (to wallow), possibly of Old Norse origin.
