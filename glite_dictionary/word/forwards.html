forwards

1. ADV

/ ˈfɔrwərdz / (AmE), / ˈfɔːwədz / (BrE)
In the direction that is in front; toward a place or position ahead.
toward the front; in a forward direction

Examples

The crowd began to move forwards as the doors opened.
She leaned forwards to get a better view of the painting.
The dog took a few cautious steps forwards before stopping.

Synset Examples

go ahead
the train moved ahead slowly
the boat lurched ahead
moved onward into the forest
they went slowly forward in the mud

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forward - toward the front; in a forward direction (main adverb form, more common in American English)

Etymology

From 'forward' + adverbial -s, by analogy with similar adverbs like 'towards.'

2. ADV

/ ˈfɔrwərdz / (AmE), / ˈfɔːwədz / (BrE)
towards the front; in the direction that is in front or ahead.
in the direction ahead; to the front
british

Examples

We began to walk forwards along the narrow path through the forest.
She had to lean forwards to reach the highest shelf in the cupboard.
The car rolled forwards a few inches before coming to a stop.

Synset Examples

he faced forward
step forward
she practiced sewing backward as well as frontward on her new sewing machine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forward - toward the front; in the direction that is in front or ahead (used in American English)
  • Antonym: backward - toward the rear or back; in the direction behind

Etymology

Formed from 'forward' + '-s', typical in British English for adverbs of direction.
