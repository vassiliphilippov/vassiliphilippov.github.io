pitchy

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɪtʃi / (AmE), / ˈpɪtʃi / (BrE)
Very black or dark, resembling pitch; as dark as jet or coal.
very black; like pitch or jet

Examples

The sky was pitchy after the storm, with no stars visible.
She wore a pitchy dress that stood out in the dimly lit room.
The cave plunged into pitchy darkness, swallowing every trace of light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jet-black - completely black, like jet
  • Synonym: ebony - deep black like ebony wood
  • Synonym: coal-black - black like coal

Etymology

From 'pitch' (a sticky, dark substance) + '-y' (adjective-forming suffix).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɪtʃi / (AmE), / ˈpɪtʃi / (BrE)
having the characteristics of pitch or tar; resembling or covered in pitch; sticky, dark, and thick.
resembling pitch or tar

Examples

The workers' clothes became pitchy after hours of repairing the old roof.
A pitchy substance oozed from the trunk of the pine tree.
His hands were left pitchy and black from handling the tar barrels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tar-like - resembling tar in appearance or texture

Etymology

From 'pitch' (sticky resin) + '-y' (adjective-forming suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00054604-s
Synset POS: s
