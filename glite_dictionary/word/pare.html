to pare

1. VERB

/ pɛr / (AmE), / peə / (BrE)
to remove the outer layer or edges from something, especially by cutting; to reduce or trim by cutting away unwanted parts
cut away outer layer or edges

Examples

She used a small knife to pare the apple before slicing it.
You need to pare your expenses if you want to save money.
The carpenter will pare the wood until it fits perfectly.

Synset Examples

pare one's fingernails
trim the photograph
trim lumber

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trim - to make something neat or of the required size by cutting away irregular or unwanted parts

Etymology

From Latin 'parare' (to prepare, trim).

2. VERB

/ pɛr / (AmE), / peə / (BrE)
to reduce something gradually by removing small amounts
reduce gradually by removing parts

Examples

The company had to pare its budget to stay profitable.
We need to pare down our belongings before moving to a smaller apartment.
The chef decided to pare the menu to focus on seasonal dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reduce - to make something smaller in amount or size

Etymology

From Latin 'parare' (to prepare, to trim) via Old French 'parer'.

3. VERB

/ pɛr / (AmE), / peə / (BrE)
to cut away the outer layer, edge, or a small part of something, often using a knife, especially to make it smaller or neater
trim or cut away outer parts

Examples

She used a small knife to pare the apple before eating it.
The carpenter began to pare thin shavings from the block of wood.
Carefully, he pared down his essay to fit the word limit.

Synset Examples

whittle a piece of wood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trim - to make something neat by cutting away irregular parts
  • Synonym: scrape - to remove something from a surface by rubbing it with a sharp or rough object

Etymology

From Latin 'parare' (to prepare, trim).

Source: WordNet

4. VERB

/ pɛr / (AmE), / peə / (BrE)
to remove the thin outer layer or skin from something, especially fruit or vegetables, with a knife or similar tool
remove the skin from something

Examples

She used a small knife to pare the apples for the pie.
It's easier to pare potatoes after boiling them briefly.
Carefully pare away the outer layer to avoid wasting any of the fruit.

Synset Examples

pare apples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peel - to remove the outer covering or skin from something, especially fruit or vegetables

Etymology

From Latin 'parare' (to prepare, trim).
