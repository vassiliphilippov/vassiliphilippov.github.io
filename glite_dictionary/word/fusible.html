fusible

1. ADJ

/ ˈfjuːzəbəl / (AmE), / ˈfjuːzɪbəl / (BrE)
capable of being melted or fused when heated
capable of being melted

Examples

This alloy is highly fusible, making it suitable for soldering electrical components.
Because the material is fusible, it can be shaped at relatively low temperatures.
The experiment required a fusible substance that liquefies under moderate heat.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: infusible - incapable of being melted

Etymology

From Medieval Latin fusibilis, from Latin fundere (to melt).
