to empathise

1. VERB

/ ˈɛmpəˌθaɪz / (AmE), / ɪmˈpæθaɪz / (BrE)
to understand and share the feelings of another person
show understanding of others' feelings
british

Examples

It's important for managers to empathise with their employees during difficult times.
She found it easy to empathise with the characters in the novel.
If you want to help effectively, you must first empathise with their situation.

Synset Examples

You don't need to explain — I understand!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to empathize - to understand and share the feelings of another person (American spelling)
  • Synonym: to sympathise - to feel or express sympathy or compassion for someone
  • Antonym: antipathy - a strong feeling of dislike

Etymology

From Greek 'empatheia' (passion, state of emotion), influenced by 'empathy'.
