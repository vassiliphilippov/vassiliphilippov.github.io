poetic

1. ADJ

/ poʊˈɛtɪk / (AmE), / pəʊˈɛtɪk / (BrE)
connected with poetry or having qualities typical of poetry, such as beauty, imagination, or emotional effect.
relating to poetry or having poetic qualities

Examples

The author’s new novel has a distinctly poetic style that captivates readers.
She read a poetic description of the sunset aloud to the class.
His praise was so poetic that it moved many listeners to tears.

Synset Examples

poetic works
a poetic romance

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prosaic - lacking poetic beauty; commonplace or unromantic

Etymology

From Greek poietikos (creative), via Latin poeticus.

2. ADJ

/ poʊˈɛtɪk / (AmE), / pəʊˈɛtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of poets or poetry; having qualities associated with poetry, such as beauty or emotional intensity.
relating to poets or poetry

Examples

The novel's language is often described as poetic and deeply moving.
Her poetic vision transformed everyday scenes into works of art.
The speaker's poetic insight captivated the audience with its vivid imagery.

Synset Examples

poetic insight

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prosaic - having the style or diction of prose; lacking poetic beauty

Etymology

From Greek *poētikos* (pertaining to poetry), via Latin *poeticus*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02868384-a
Synset POS: a

/ poʊˈɛtɪk / (AmE), / pəʊˈɛtɪk / (BrE)
having the qualities or style characteristic of poetry; expressing ideas in a beautiful or imaginative way.
having qualities of poetry

Examples

Her writing is admired for its poetic beauty and vivid imagery.
He described the sunset in such a poetic way that everyone was moved.
The filmmaker used poetic language to capture the mood of the city.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prosaic - lacking poetic beauty; commonplace or unromantic

Etymology

From French poétique, from Latin poëticus, from Greek poietikos (pertaining to creating or poetry).

4. ADJ

/ poʊˈɛtɪk / (AmE), / pəʊˈɛtɪk / (BrE)
Having or expressing qualities characteristic of poetry, especially romantic or imaginative imagery.
expressing romantic or imaginative imagery

Examples

The painter's work had a poetic quality that captivated everyone.
Her description of the sunrise was so poetic that it moved the audience to tears.
Even ordinary moments in life can feel poetic when seen through an artist's eyes.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prosaic - lacking poetic beauty; unimaginative

Etymology

From Latin poeticus (of poetry), from Greek ποιητικός (poētikos).
