pinealoma

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪniəˈloʊmə / (AmE), / ˌpɪniːəˈləʊmə / (BrE)
A tumor arising from the pineal gland in the brain.
tumor of the pineal gland
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with a pinealoma after exhibiting symptoms of increased intracranial pressure.
Surgical removal of a pinealoma can be complex due to its location in the brain.
A pinealoma may lead to hormonal disturbances if the pineal gland's function is affected.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tumor - an abnormal mass of tissue

Etymology

From pineal (relating to the pineal gland) + -oma (Greek, meaning tumor).
