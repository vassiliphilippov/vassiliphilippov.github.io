silkworm

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlkwɜrm / (AmE), / ˈsɪlkwəːm / (BrE)
a commercially bred caterpillar, especially Bombyx mori, that spins a silk cocoon and is the principal source of commercial silk
caterpillar that produces silk

Examples

The silkworm spins a cocoon of strong silk thread used in textiles.
Ancient Chinese people were the first to domesticate the silkworm for silk production.
Farmers carefully tend their silkworm crops each year during the harvesting season.

Etymology

From Middle English 'silke' (silk) + 'worm', referring to its silk-spinning behavior.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlkˌwɝm / (AmE), / ˈsɪlkˌwɜːm / (BrE)
the caterpillar of certain moths, especially the Asian moth Bombyx mori, which produces silk used in textiles as it spins its cocoon
caterpillar that produces silk

Examples

The silkworm spins a cocoon from a single thread of raw silk.
Farmers in China have been raising silkworms for thousands of years.
The children watched a silkworm eating mulberry leaves in their science class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caterpillar - the larval stage of a butterfly or moth
  • Synonym: moth - adult form of an insect whose larva is a silkworm

Etymology

From silk + worm, referring to the insect that produces silk.
