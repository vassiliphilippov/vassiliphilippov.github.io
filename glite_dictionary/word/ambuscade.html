ambuscade

1. VERB

/ ˌæmbəˈskeɪd / (AmE), / ˌæmbəˈskeɪd / (BrE)
to lie in wait in order to make a surprise attack; to ambush
to attack from hiding
archaic

Examples

The soldiers planned to ambuscade their enemy as they passed through the narrow pass.
Bandits would often ambuscade travelers along the deserted road at night.
They ambuscaded the approaching convoy, springing from their hiding places among the rocks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ambush - to attack by surprise from a hidden position

Etymology

From French *embuscade*, from Italian *imboscata* (meaning 'ambush', literally 'placed in the woods').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01140630-v

2. NOUN

/ ˌæmbəˈskeɪd / (AmE), / ˌambəˈskeɪd / (BrE)
An act of hiding and lying in wait to make a surprise attack; an ambush.
surprise attack from hiding

Examples

The soldiers fell into an ambuscade set by the enemy near the pass.
During the night, they prepared an ambuscade by the riverbank to surprise their pursuers.
The caravan was wary of a possible ambuscade as it entered the dense forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ambush - An act of attacking unexpectedly from a concealed position

Etymology

From French 'embuscade', from Italian 'imboscata' (an ambush).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01249473-n
Synset POS: n
