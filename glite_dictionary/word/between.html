between

1. ADV

/ bəˈtwin / (AmE), / bɪˈtwiːn / (BrE)
In or into the space or interval separating two points, objects, or places.
in the space separating two things

Examples

The park lies between the river and the road.
A cat squeezed between the bars of the gate.
He stood quietly between his two friends, waiting for the bus.

Synset Examples

two houses with a tree between

Etymology

Old English 'betwēonum', from 'be-' (by) + 'twēonum' (dative plural of 'twēon', meaning 'two').

2. ADV

/ bɪˈtwin / (AmE), / bɪˈtwiːn / (BrE)
in the interval separating two points in time, events, or things.
in the interval between

Examples

There was hardly any time between his arrival and his departure.
The classes are scheduled with a short break between.
She finished two meetings with only a few minutes between.

Synset Examples

dancing all the dances with little rest between
