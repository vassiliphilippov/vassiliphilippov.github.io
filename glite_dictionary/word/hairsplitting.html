hairsplitting

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛrˌsplɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈheəˌsplɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of making overly fine or trivial distinctions, especially in argument
making overly fine or trivial distinctions

Examples

The debate was bogged down by endless hairsplitting over minor details.
Her hairsplitting annoyed the rest of the committee, who wanted to make a decision.
Sometimes, legal arguments become too focused on hairsplitting instead of addressing the real issues.

Synset Examples

they didn't take his hairsplitting seriously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nitpicking - the practice of looking for small or unimportant errors or faults
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05757207-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈhɛrˌsplɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈheəˌsplɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
concerned with unreasonably or excessively fine details or distinctions, especially in argument or analysis.
paying undue attention to fine distinctions

Examples

The debate quickly descended into hairsplitting over trivial definitions.
She criticized his hairsplitting analysis as missing the main point.
Legal arguments can sometimes become unnecessarily hairsplitting and pedantic.

Synset Examples

finespun distinctions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nitpicking - giving too much attention to unimportant details; overly critical attention to minor details
  • Synonym: pedantic - overly concerned with minute details or formalisms, especially in teaching

Etymology

From the habit of literally splitting a hair, used figuratively since the 17th century for making overly fine distinctions.
