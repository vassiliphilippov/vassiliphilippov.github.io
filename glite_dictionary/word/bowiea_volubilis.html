Bowiea volubilis

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈiːə vəˈluːbɪlɪs / (AmE), / bəʊˈiːə vəˈluːbɪlɪs / (BrE)
A much-branched, leafless, twining South African plant cultivated as an ornamental for its bright green stems growing from large aboveground bulbs.
South African ornamental plant with green stems and bulbs

Examples

The greenhouse features a collection of rare species, including Bowiea volubilis with its unusual leafless stems.
Gardeners value Bowiea volubilis for its resilience and striking bulbs.
After two years, the Bowiea volubilis finally bloomed, revealing its delicate star-shaped flowers.

Etymology

Genus named after British botanist James Bowie; species from Latin 'volubilis' meaning 'twining'.
