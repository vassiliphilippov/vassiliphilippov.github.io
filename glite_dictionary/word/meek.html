meek

1. ADJ

/ mik / (AmE), / miːk / (BrE)
quiet, gentle, and easily imposed on; submissive or unwilling to assert oneself.
quiet, submissive, or gentle

Examples

Despite his accomplishments, he remained meek and never boasted about his success.
The meek child stood silently at the edge of the playground, watching the others play.
Her meek response surprised everyone in the heated meeting.

Synset Examples

meek and self-effacing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timid - showing a lack of courage or confidence
  • Antonym: assertive - confidently self-assured and forceful

Etymology

From Old Norse *mjǫkr* (gentle, soft).

2. ADJ

/ mik / (AmE), / miːk / (BrE)
quiet, gentle, and easily imposed on; submissive or lacking in spirit or courage.
overly submissive or gentle

Examples

Despite her meek appearance, she was very determined in her decisions.
He gave a meek apology, barely meeting her eyes.
In group discussions, Tom tends to remain meek and rarely shares his opinions.

Synset Examples

compliant and anxious to suit his opinions of those of others
a fine fiery blast against meek conformity
she looked meek but had the heart of a lion
was submissive and subservient

Related Terms

  • Synonym: submissive - ready to conform to the authority or will of others; meek
  • Antonym: assertive - having or showing a confident and forceful personality

Etymology

From Old Norse *mjúkr* (soft, gentle), related to Old English *meoce* (mild, meek).

3. ADJ

/ mik / (AmE), / miːk / (BrE)
quiet, gentle, and easily imposed on; submissive and docile.
submissive; very docile

Examples

He remained meek in the face of harsh criticism and never raised his voice.
Despite her meek appearance, she stood firm on her decision.
The meek puppy cowered behind the couch during the thunderstorm.

Synset Examples

tame obedience
meek as a mouse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: submissive - ready to conform to the authority or will of others; meek
  • Synonym: timid - showing a lack of courage or confidence; easily frightened
  • Antonym: assertive - having or showing a confident and forceful personality; the opposite of meek

Etymology

From Middle English 'meek', from Old Norse 'mjǫkr' (gentle, mild).
