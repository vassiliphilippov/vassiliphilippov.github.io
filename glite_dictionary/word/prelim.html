prelim

1. NOUN

/ ˈpriː.lɪm / (AmE), / ˈpriː.lɪm / (BrE)
an examination taken by graduate students, usually early in their program, to assess their suitability to continue their studies
graduate school examination

Examples

All PhD candidates must pass their prelim before starting their dissertation research.
She studied for months to ensure she would do well on her prelim.
Failure to pass the prelim can result in dismissal from the graduate program.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preliminary examination - an examination taken as a prerequisite to continue graduate studies

Etymology

Shortened from 'preliminary'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpriː.lɪm / (AmE), / ˈpriː.lɪm / (BrE)
a minor match, contest, or event that takes place before the main event, especially in sports or entertainment
minor match before main event
informal

Examples

The fighter won his prelim before the main card of the event.
We arrived early and watched the prelim matches before the headline bout.
Her brother is competing in the prelim at tonight's boxing tournament.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: preliminary - an event or contest serving as a preparation for something more important

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'preliminary'.
