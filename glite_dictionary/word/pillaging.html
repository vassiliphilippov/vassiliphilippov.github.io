pillaging

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪlɪdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɪlɪdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of stealing goods, especially during a war or in a violent or forceful way
the act of looting or plundering

Examples

The army's pillaging left the town in ruins and its people destitute.
Centuries of pillaging emptied the temple of its priceless treasures.
After the hurricane, reports of pillaging in abandoned stores began to surface.

Synset Examples

the plundering of the Parthenon
his plundering of the great authors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plundering - the act of stealing goods, usually by force
  • Synonym: looting - the act of stealing goods from a place during chaos

Etymology

From 'pillage' (French piller, meaning to plunder).
