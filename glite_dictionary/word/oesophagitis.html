oesophagitis

1. NOUN

/ iˌsɑfəˈdʒaɪtɪs / (AmE), / iˌsɒfəˈdʒaɪtɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of the esophagus, often caused by acid reflux or infection
inflammation of the esophagus
medical british

Examples

Chronic heartburn can eventually lead to oesophagitis if left untreated.
The doctor explained that her sore throat was caused by oesophagitis.
Treatment for oesophagitis may include medication to reduce stomach acid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: esophagitis - inflammation of the esophagus (American spelling)

Etymology

From Greek *oisophagos* (esophagus) + -itis (inflammation).
