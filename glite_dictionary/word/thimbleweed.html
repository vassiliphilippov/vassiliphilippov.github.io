thimbleweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɪmbəlˌwid / (AmE), / ˈθɪmbəlwiːd / (BrE)
a North American wildflower of the anemone genus, with cylindrical fruit clusters that resemble sewing thimbles
a North American anemone with thimble-shaped fruit

Examples

A patch of thimbleweed grew along the forest's edge, its delicate white flowers swaying in the breeze.
She photographed the distinctive fruit cluster of the thimbleweed for her botany project.
Gardeners often appreciate thimbleweed for its unique and attractive seed heads in late summer.

Etymology

Named for the thimble-like shape of its fruit clusters; 'thimble' (from Old English 'þȳmel') + 'weed'.
