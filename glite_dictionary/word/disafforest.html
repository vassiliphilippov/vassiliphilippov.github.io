to disafforest

1. VERB

/ ˌdɪs.əˈfɔːr.əst / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.əˈfɒr.ɪst / (BrE)
to remove trees from (an area), or to remove (land) from the legal status of being a forest
remove trees from; remove forest status

Examples

Plans to disafforest parts of the ancient woodland sparked considerable local opposition.
Within a decade, the government moved to disafforest the land for agricultural use.
The king issued an order to disafforest several royal parks, opening them for settlement.

Synset Examples

The landscape was deforested by the enemy attacks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deforest - to clear trees from an area

Etymology

From dis- (reverse) + afforest, ultimately from Old French 'forest' (wooded land).
