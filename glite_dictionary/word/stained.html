stained

1. ADJ

/ steɪnd / (AmE), / steɪnd / (BrE)
marked, dyed, or discolored by a substance, especially in a way that is difficult to remove
marked or discolored by a substance

Examples

The stained carpet in the hallway needed a professional cleaning.
After the accident, his shirt was stained with oil and dirt.
Her hands were stained blue from picking berries all morning.

Synset Examples

a badly stained tablecloth
tear-stained cheeks

Related Terms

  • Antonym: spotless - perfectly clean; without any stains or marks

Etymology

Past participle of 'stain', from Old French *teindre* (to dye) and Latin *tīngere* (to dye).

2. ADJ

/ steɪnd / (AmE), / steɪnd / (BrE)
(of wood, glass, etc.) having been coated or treated with a colored stain or varnish to alter its appearance or protect it.
coated with stain or varnish

Examples

The table was stained a rich mahogany to match the rest of the furniture.
He preferred stained wood over painted surfaces for a more natural look.
The stained finish on the floors helped protect them from water damage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: painted - coated with paint

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'stain', from Old French 'esteindre', ultimately from Latin 'tingere' (to dye, color).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01718166-s
Synset POS: s
