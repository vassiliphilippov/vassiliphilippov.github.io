poem

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊ.əm / (AmE), / ˈpəʊ.ɪm / (BrE)
a piece of writing in which the expression of feelings and ideas is given intensity by the use of distinctive style and rhythm; typically written in verse.
a written literary composition in verse

Examples

She read her favorite poem aloud to the class.
The young writer published his first poem in a magazine.
Each poem in the collection explores a different emotion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: verse - writing arranged with a metrical rhythm, typically having a rhyme
  • Antonym: prose - written or spoken language in its ordinary form, without metrical structure

Etymology

From Greek 'poiēma' (a thing made or created), via Latin 'poema'.
