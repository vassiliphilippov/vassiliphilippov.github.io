Lopholatilus

1. NOUN

/ loʊˌfoʊləˈtaɪləs / (AmE), / ləʊˌfɒləˈtaɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of large, brightly colored food fish found in deep waters of the Atlantic Ocean, including the golden tilefish.
genus of Atlantic food fish

Examples

Lopholatilus species are highly valued in commercial fishing for their tender, flavorful flesh.
The most famous member of the genus Lopholatilus is the golden tilefish, found along the Atlantic coast.
Marine biologists conducted a survey to estimate the population of Lopholatilus in deep-sea habitats.

Etymology

From Greek 'lophos' (crest) and 'latilus', related to fish genus Latilus.
