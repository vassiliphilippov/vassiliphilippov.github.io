isogram

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪsəˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈaɪsəʊɡram / (BrE)
a line on a map that connects points with the same numerical value of a particular variable, such as temperature or elevation
line connecting equal-value points on a map

Examples

The weather map showed several isograms indicating areas of equal pressure.
Cartographers carefully label each isogram to make the data easier to interpret.
An isogram can represent many different variables, including rainfall or population density.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contour line - a line joining points of equal elevation
  • Synonym: isotherm - a line connecting points of equal temperature

Etymology

From Greek 'isos' (equal) + 'gramma' (something written, line).
