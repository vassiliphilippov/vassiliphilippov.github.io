Dicentra canadensis

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈsɛntrə kəˈnædənsɪs / (AmE), / daɪˈsɛntrə kəˈnædənsɪs / (BrE)
a North American woodland plant with delicate, cream-colored flowers and tuberous roots resembling kernels of corn
plant with cream-colored flowers, corn-like roots

Examples

The delicate blooms of Dicentra canadensis appear in early spring in shady woodlands.
Botanists studied the unusual tuberous roots of Dicentra canadensis during their field survey.
Gardeners often refer to Dicentra canadensis as 'squirrel corn' due to its root shape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squirrel corn - common name for Dicentra canadensis, with similar meaning

Etymology

From New Latin: Dicentra from Greek di- (two) + kentron (spur), canadensis indicating origin from Canada.
