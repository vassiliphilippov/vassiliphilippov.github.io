overrating

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvərˈreɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈreɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
an act or instance of estimating or valuing something as greater or better than it really is
the act of overestimating

Examples

The overrating of his skills led to disappointment when he was given more responsibility.
They discovered an overrating of the company's assets during the audit.
Critics warned that the market's overrating of the product could lead to a sharp correction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overestimate - to estimate something as greater than it actually is
  • Antonym: underrating - an act or instance of estimating something as less than its true value

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'overrate' (from 'over-' + 'rate') and the noun-forming suffix '-ing'.
