apologia

1. NOUN

/ ˌæpəˈloʊdʒiə / (AmE), / ˌapəˈləʊdʒɪə / (BrE)
A formal written defense or justification of one's beliefs, actions, or opinion, especially in response to criticism.
formal written defense or justification

Examples

Her autobiography was not just a memoir but a carefully crafted apologia for her controversial decisions.
The author offered a lengthy apologia in the introduction, explaining the motivations behind the book.
He published an apologia to address the public's criticism of his policies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defense - the act of defending something against criticism or attack
  • Synonym: justification - an explanation or excuse for an action or belief

Etymology

From Latin apologia (a speech in defense), from Greek apologia (a speaking in defense).
