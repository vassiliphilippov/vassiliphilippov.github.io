Hassid

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑː.sɪd / (AmE), / ˈhɑː.sɪd / (BrE)
A member of a Jewish sect that observes strict Orthodox Judaism, typically associated with Hasidic Judaism.
member of strict Jewish sect

Examples

He became a Hassid after being inspired by the teachings of a local rabbi.
Many Hassids gather to celebrate religious festivals each year.
The community welcomed a new Hassid who recently moved from Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hasidic - relating to the Hasidic movement or its members

Etymology

From Hebrew ḥasīd (pious, devout).
