Esselen

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛsəˌlɛn / (AmE), / ˈɛsəˌlɛn / (BrE)
A member of a Native American people living on the California coast near Monterey.
member of a Native American people (California)

Examples

The Esselen were one of the smallest Native American groups in California.
Anthropologists have studied the traditions of the Esselen for insights into early coastal lifestyles.
Modern descendants of the Esselen are working to revive their language and culture.

Etymology

From the self-designation of the Esselen people; possibly related to the name of the Esselen language.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛsəˌlɛn / (AmE), / ˈɛsəˌlɛn / (BrE)
the extinct Hokan language formerly spoken by the Esselen people of California
the language of the Esselen people

Examples

Very few records exist of the Esselen language due to its early extinction.
Linguists have tried to reconstruct the vocabulary of Esselen from historical sources.
The Esselen language was once spoken along the central coast of California.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Esselen - a member of an Indigenous people of central California

Etymology

Named after the Esselen people, an indigenous group of California.
