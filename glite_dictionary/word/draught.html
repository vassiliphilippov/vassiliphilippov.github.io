draught

1. NOUN

/ dræft / (AmE), / drɑːft / (BrE)
A current of air moving through a room or other enclosed space, often felt as a cold breeze.
current of air in a room or space
british

Examples

A cold draught made everyone in the room reach for their sweaters.
She complained about the draught coming in through the old window.
The fire burned brighter when a draught blew down the chimney.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: draft - American spelling for a current of air in a room or chimney

Etymology

From Old English 'dræht', related to 'draw', meaning to pull or move something.

2. NOUN

/ dræft / (AmE), / drɑːft / (BrE)

Examples

Some riverboats are designed with a shallow draught for easier navigation in low waters.

Etymology

From Old English 'dræht', related to 'draw', referring to the amount drawn under water.
