to defy

1. VERB

/ dɪˈfaɪ / (AmE), / dɪˈfaɪ / (BrE)
to openly resist or refuse to obey; to confront with resistance or challenge authority.
resist or challenge authority

Examples

She chose to defy the unjust law, risking punishment for her beliefs.
The monument appears to defy the effects of time and weather.
He was admired for his courage to defy the expectations of his peers.

Synset Examples

The politician defied public opinion
The new material withstands even the greatest wear and tear
The bridge held

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to resist - to oppose or withstand something
  • Antonym: yield - to give way or submit under pressure

Etymology

From Old French 'desfier' (to challenge, defy), from Latin 'dis-' (away) + 'fidare' (to trust).

2. VERB

/ dɪˈfaɪ / (AmE), / dɪˈfaɪ / (BrE)
to openly challenge or resist someone or something, often refusing to obey or conform
challenge or resist openly

Examples

She chose to defy the new rules even though it risked punishment.
The group decided to defy the ban and hold the protest anyway.
He looked straight at his teacher and boldly defied her instructions.

Synset Examples

I dare you!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: challenge - to invite someone to contest or compete; to question authority
  • Antonym: to obey - to follow commands or rules

Etymology

From Old French *defier* (to challenge, renounce), from Latin *disfidare* (to mistrust).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00869761-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ dɪˈfaɪ / (AmE), / dɪˈfaɪ / (BrE)
to be impossible to explain, understand, or describe; to elude something, especially in a way that is puzzling.
elude or resist explanation or understanding

Examples

The beauty of the landscape defies description.
His sudden change in behavior defied all attempts at explanation.
This puzzle defies even the experts to solve it.

Synset Examples

This behavior defies explanation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to elude - to evade or escape from something, typically in a skillful or cunning way
  • Synonym: to resist - to withstand or oppose something

Etymology

From Old French defier (to challenge), from Vulgar Latin *disfidare (to mistrust).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
