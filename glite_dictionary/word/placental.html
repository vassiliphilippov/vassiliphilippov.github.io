placental

1. ADJ

/ pləˈsɛntəl / (AmE), / pləˈsɛntəl / (BrE)
relating to or having a placenta, especially in reference to mammals that develop a placenta during gestation.
of or relating to a placenta
medical

Examples

Some mammals, such as humans and whales, are classified as placental animals.
Research on placental development has improved our understanding of pregnancy complications.
Unlike marsupials, placental mammals nourish their young through an organ attached to the uterus.

Synset Examples

all mammals except monotremes and marsupials are placental mammals

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonplacental - not having a placenta

Etymology

From Latin 'placenta' (a flat cake, in medical use 'placenta'), with the suffix '-al' meaning 'pertaining to'.

2. NOUN

/ pləˈsɛntəl / (AmE), / pləˈsɛntəl / (BrE)
A mammal of the group Placentalia, characterized by the presence of a placenta, excluding monotremes and marsupials.
a mammal with a placenta

Examples

Most mammals, such as dogs and whales, are classified as placentals.
Unlike marsupials, placentals nourish their young through a placenta before birth.
The discovery of placental fossils helped scientists understand mammalian evolution.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: marsupial - a mammal whose young are born incompletely developed and are carried in a pouch
  • Antonym: monotreme - egg-laying mammal like the platypus or echidna

Etymology

From Latin 'placenta' (cake), referring to the organ's flat shape.
