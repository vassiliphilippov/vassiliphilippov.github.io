charmed

1. ADJ

/ tʃɑrmd / (AmE), / tʃɑːmd / (BrE)
filled with a sense of wonder and delight, as if under a spell or enchanted
filled with wonder and delight

Examples

The children were charmed by the magician's incredible performance.
She felt charmed by the beauty of the old city at sunset.
We listened, charmed, as the storyteller wove her magical tales.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enchanted - delighted as if under a spell
  • Synonym: delighted - very pleased and happy
  • Antonym: disenchanted - having lost one's sense of wonder or enchantment

Etymology

From charm + -ed, influenced by Middle English charme (spell, magical influence).

2. ADJ

/ tʃɑrmd / (AmE), / tʃɑːmd / (BrE)
feeling great attraction or delight; captivated or enchanted by someone or something
delighted or captivated

Examples

She was utterly charmed by his wit and good manners.
We were charmed by the beauty of the old town.
He looked charmed as he listened to the mesmerizing music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enchanted - delighted greatly or under a spell; fascinated
  • Synonym: captivated - strongly attracted by or interested in someone or something
  • Antonym: bored - feeling weary and uninterested

Etymology

From Latin 'carmen' (song, spell), via Old French 'charmer'.
