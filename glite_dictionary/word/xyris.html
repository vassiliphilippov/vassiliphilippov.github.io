Xyris

1. NOUN

/ ˈzaɪrɪs / (AmE), / ˈzaɪrɪs / (BrE)
a genus of chiefly American marsh plants, usually with yellow flowers
genus of marsh plants with yellow flowers

Examples

The marsh was covered with Xyris blooming in the summer sun.
Botanists discovered a new species of Xyris in the wetland reserve.
Many species of Xyris are important for supporting wildlife in their native habitats.

Etymology

From Greek xuron (razor), referring to the narrow leaves of some species.

2. NOUN

/ ˈzaɪrɪs / (AmE), / ˈzaɪrɪs / (BrE)
A genus of chiefly American marsh plants with usually yellow flowers, belonging to the family Xyridaceae.
a genus of marsh plants with yellow flowers

Examples

Several species in the Xyris genus are commonly found in wet, sandy soils across North America.
The botanist carefully documented the distinct yellow flowers characteristic of Xyris.
Marshlands in the southeastern United States are often home to Xyris plants.

Etymology

From New Latin Xyris, the genus name, likely from Greek xuron, meaning 'razor', referring to the shape of the leaves.
