uintathere

1. NOUN

/ juˈɪntəˌθɪr / (AmE), / juˈɪntəˌθɪə / (BrE)
an extinct genus of large, herbivorous mammals (order Dinocerata) from the Eocene epoch, characterized by three pairs of bony protuberances on the skull.
an extinct Eocene dinocerate mammal

Examples

The fossilized skull of the uintathere displayed its distinctive bony protrusions.
Paleontologists discovered a nearly complete uintathere skeleton in Wyoming.
Scientists believe uintatheres grazed on soft, aquatic vegetation along ancient riverbanks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dinocerate - an extinct order of large, horned herbivorous mammals from the Eocene

Etymology

Named after the Uinta Mountains (Utah) and -there (from Greek thēr, wild animal).
