Fraxinus ornus

1. NOUN

/ ˈfræksɪnəs ˈɔrnəs / (AmE), / ˈfraksɪnəs ˈɔːnəs / (BrE)
A species of ash tree native to the southern Mediterranean, known for its fragrant white flowers and for yielding manna.
species of Mediterranean ash tree

Examples

The Fraxinus ornus is prized in Mediterranean gardens for its ornamental flowers.
Manna extracted from the Fraxinus ornus has been used traditionally as a laxative.
Unlike other ashes, the Fraxinus ornus thrives in dry, rocky soils of southern Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ash tree - a tree of the genus Fraxinus

Etymology

From Latin: 'Fraxinus' meaning ash tree and 'ornus' referring to the manna ash.
