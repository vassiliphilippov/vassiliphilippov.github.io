liquidness

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪkwɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈlɪkwɪdnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being liquid; the property of a substance that allows it to flow easily and be relatively incompressible.
the state of being liquid

Examples

The liquidness of water allows it to adapt to the shape of its container.
Scientists studied the liquidness of different substances under varying temperatures.
She marveled at the liquidness of the metal when it was heated to high temperatures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liquidity - the quality of being liquid, especially in finance or substances

Etymology

From 'liquid' + '-ness', a suffix forming nouns denoting a state or quality.
