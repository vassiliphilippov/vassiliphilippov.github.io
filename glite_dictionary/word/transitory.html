transitory

1. ADJ

/ ˈtræn.səˌtɔr.i / (AmE), / ˈtræn.zɪ.tər.i / (BrE)
lasting for only a short time; not permanent
lasting only a short time

Examples

She knew that their happiness was transitory and would not last forever.
The beauty of the cherry blossoms is transitory, fading within a week.
His anger was transitory, and he soon apologized for his outburst.

Synset Examples

the ephemeral joys of childhood
a passing fancy
youth's transient beauty
love is transitory but it is eternal
fugacious blossoms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: temporary - lasting for a limited period; not permanent
  • Antonym: permanent - lasting or intended to last or remain unchanged indefinitely
  • Synonym: ephemeral - lasting for a very short time

Etymology

From Latin transitorius (passing away), from transire (to go across).
