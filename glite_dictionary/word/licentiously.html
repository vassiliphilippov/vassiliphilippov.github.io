licentiously

1. ADV

/ laɪˈsɛnʃəsli / (AmE), / laɪˈsɛnʃəsli / (BrE)
In a manner that shows a lack of moral restraint, especially regarding sexual conduct; promiscuously or immorally.
In a morally unrestrained way

Examples

The writer was known to describe decadent parties licentiously in his novels.
He behaved licentiously after hours, ignoring any sense of propriety.
Critics accused the film of portraying historical figures licentiously for sensationalism.

Synset Examples

this young girl has to share a room with her mother who lives promiscuously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: licentious - lacking legal or moral restraints, often in sexual context

Etymology

From 'licentious' + '-ly'; 'licentious' from Latin 'licentia' (freedom, license).
