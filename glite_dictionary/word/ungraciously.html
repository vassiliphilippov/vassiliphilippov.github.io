ungraciously

1. ADV

/ ʌnˈɡreɪʃəsli / (AmE), / ʌnˈɡreɪʃəsli / (BrE)
In a manner lacking grace, politeness, or charm; awkwardly or rudely.
in an ungraceful or rude manner

Examples

She accepted the apology ungraciously, barely managing a nod in response.
The host ungraciously dismissed his guests before the evening had ended.
He ungraciously snatched the package from the delivery person's hands.

Synset Examples

they moved woodenly

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gracefully - in an elegant or smooth manner
  • Synonym: awkwardly - in a clumsy or uncomfortable manner

Etymology

From 'ungracious' (from un- 'not' + gracious) and the adverbial suffix -ly.
