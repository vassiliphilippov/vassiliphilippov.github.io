Abronia

1. NOUN

/ əˈbroʊniə / (AmE), / əˈbrəʊniə / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants native to western North America, known for their showy flowers, commonly called sand-verbenas
genus of flowering plants (sand-verbenas)

Examples

The desert was dotted with blooming Abronia in vibrant pinks and yellows.
Many gardeners are drawn to Abronia for its clusters of fragrant flowers.
Several species of Abronia are adapted to sandy soils in arid regions.

Etymology

From New Latin, possibly derived from Greek 'abros' (delicate).

2. NOUN

/ əˈbroʊniə / (AmE), / əˈbrəʊniə / (BrE)
A genus of western North American herbs in the family Nyctaginaceae, known for their showy flowers, commonly called sand-verbenas.
genus of flowering herbs (sand-verbenas)

Examples

The Abronia species are especially noted for their brightly colored, fragrant blossoms.
Botanists are studying the distribution of Abronia in the deserts of California.
Many gardeners cultivate Abronia for its resilience and attractive flowers.

Etymology

From New Latin, probably from Greek 'abros' (delicate) referring to the delicate flowers.
