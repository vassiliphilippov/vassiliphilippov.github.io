glutamate

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡluːtəˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡluːtəmeɪt / (BrE)
a salt or ester of glutamic acid, commonly occurring in food and living organisms
a salt or ester of glutamic acid

Examples

Many processed foods contain glutamate as a flavor enhancer.
The researcher analyzed the concentration of glutamate in the sample.
Some people claim to be sensitive to monosodium glutamate in their diet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glutamic acid - an amino acid important in metabolism

Etymology

From glutamic acid, first identified in 19th century German chemistry.
