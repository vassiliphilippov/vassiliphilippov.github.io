disowning

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈoʊnɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪsˈəʊnɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of refusing to acknowledge or accept someone or something as one's own
act of refusing to acknowledge as one's own

Examples

Her disowning of her family shocked the entire community.
The disowning of responsibility was criticized by the public.
His disowning of his past actions seemed insincere.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disown - to refuse to acknowledge as one's own
