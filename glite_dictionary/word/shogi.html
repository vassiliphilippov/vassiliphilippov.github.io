shogi

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃoʊɡi / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊɡi / (BrE)
a Japanese board game similar to chess, played on a board with 81 squares, where each player has 20 pieces and captured pieces can be reused
Japanese chess game

Examples

Many people find shogi even more challenging than Western chess.
He spent years mastering the strategies of shogi before participating in tournaments.
The club meets weekly to play shogi and discuss advanced tactics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chess - a board game of strategic skill for two players, played on a checkered board

Etymology

From Japanese 将棋 (shōgi), meaning 'general's game'.
