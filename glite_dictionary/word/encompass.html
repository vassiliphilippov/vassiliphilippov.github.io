to encompass

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈkʌmpəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈkʌmpəs / (BrE)
to include a wide range of things, ideas, or people; to surround or cover something completely
include within a scope; surround

Examples

The festival encompasses music, film, and art from around the world.
Her interests encompass both science and literature.
The law encompasses all forms of discrimination.

Synset Examples

This group encompasses a wide range of people from different backgrounds
this should cover everyone in the group

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to include - to contain as part of a whole
  • Antonym: to exclude - to leave out; not include

Etymology

From Middle English 'encompassen', from Anglo-French 'encompasser' (en- 'in' + compasser 'to go round').
