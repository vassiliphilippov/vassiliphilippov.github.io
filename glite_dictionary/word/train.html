train

1. NOUN

/ treɪn / (AmE), / treɪn / (BrE)
a series of connected vehicles running on a railroad and used as public transport, typically drawn by a locomotive
railway vehicle for public transport

Examples

The train to London departs from platform four at 8:15 a.m.
We missed our train because of heavy traffic on the way to the station.
She commutes to work every day by train, enjoying the scenic views along the route.

Synset Examples

express trains don't stop at Princeton Junction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: locomotive - the vehicle that provides the power for a train
  • Synonym: subway - an underground railway system

Etymology

From Old French 'train' (trailing), from Latin 'trahere' (to pull, draw).
