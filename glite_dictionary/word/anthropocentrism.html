anthropocentrism

1. NOUN

/ ˌænθrəpoʊˈsɛntrɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌænθrəpəʊˈsɛntrɪzəm / (BrE)
The belief or practice of regarding humans and human experience as the central or most significant entities in the universe.
belief that humans are central or most important

Examples

Many environmentalists criticize anthropocentrism for placing human interests above those of other living beings.
The philosopher wrote extensively about the dangers of anthropocentrism in shaping ethical decisions.
Rejecting anthropocentrism can lead to a more respectful relationship with nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: egocentrism - focus on oneself as the center of all experience
  • Antonym: biocentrism - belief that all living things are equally valuable

Etymology

From Greek anthrōpos (human being) + -centrism (centered on).
