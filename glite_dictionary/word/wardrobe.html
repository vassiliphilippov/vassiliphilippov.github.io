wardrobe

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔːrˌdroʊb / (AmE), / ˈwɔː.drəʊb / (BrE)
the collection of clothes that someone owns
collection of a person's clothing

Examples

After her promotion, she updated her entire wardrobe with new business attire.
His summer wardrobe consists mainly of shorts and t-shirts.
She has a separate wardrobe for formal events and special occasions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clothing - items worn to cover the body

Etymology

From Old French 'warderobe', from warder (to guard) + robe (garment).

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔrˌdroʊb / (AmE), / ˈwɔːdrəʊb / (BrE)
the collection of costumes and clothing owned by a theatrical company or used in a performance
collection of theatrical costumes

Examples

The theater's wardrobe was filled with elaborate period costumes for the upcoming play.
After years of performances, the company's wardrobe contained outfits from dozens of productions.
She was responsible for organizing the wardrobe before each rehearsal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: costume - clothing worn by an actor or performer for a particular role

Etymology

From Old French *garderobe* (wardrobe, cloakroom), from garder (to guard) + robe (garment).

3. NOUN

/ ˈwɔːrˌdroʊb / (AmE), / ˈwɔːdrəʊb / (BrE)
A large, tall piece of furniture with doors, used for storing clothes by hanging or shelving them.
tall clothes storage furniture

Examples

Emma kept her favorite dresses neatly in the wardrobe beside her bed.
We bought a new wardrobe for the guest room last weekend.
He opened the wardrobe to choose a suit for the wedding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: closet - a small room or enclosed recess for storing clothes or other items
  • Synonym: cupboard - a cabinet with shelves for storing dishes, food, or clothes

Etymology

From Old French 'warderobe', from 'warder' (to guard) + 'robe' (garment).
