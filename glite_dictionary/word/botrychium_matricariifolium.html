Botrychium matricariifolium

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈtrɪkiəm ˌmætrɪˌkæriɪˈfoʊliəm / (AmE), / bəʊˈtrɪkiəm ˌmætrɪˌkæriɪˈfəʊliəm / (BrE)
A species of grape-fern found in North America and Eurasia, known for its finely divided, daisy-like leaves.
a species of grape-fern

Examples

The rare Botrychium matricariifolium was discovered growing along the forest stream.
Botanists conducted a survey to estimate the population of Botrychium matricariifolium in the region.
Due to habitat loss, Botrychium matricariifolium is considered threatened in some areas.

Etymology

From Latin: genus 'Botrychium' (from Greek 'botrys' meaning 'bunch of grapes') and species 'matricariifolium' (with leaves like those of Matricaria, a daisy genus, from Latin 'folium' meaning 'leaf').
