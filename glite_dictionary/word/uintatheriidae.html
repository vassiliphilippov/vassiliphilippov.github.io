Uintatheriidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuːˌaɪntəˌθɪriˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌjuːˌɪntəˌθɪəriˈaɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, extinct herbivorous mammals of the order Dinocerata that lived during the Eocene epoch.
extinct family of Eocene mammals

Examples

Fossils of the Uintatheriidae have been discovered across North America and Asia.
Members of the Uintatheriidae were notable for their large size and distinct cranial projections.
The extinction of the Uintatheriidae marked the end of a unique branch of early mammalian evolution.

Etymology

From New Latin, after the genus Uintatherium, itself named after the Uinta Mountains and -therium (Greek for 'beast'), with the taxonomic suffix -idae.

2. NOUN

/ ˌjuː.ɪn.təˌθɪər.iˈaɪ.diː / (AmE), / ˌjuː.ɪn.təˌθɪə.riˈaɪ.diː / (BrE)
an extinct family of large, herbivorous mammals belonging to the order Dinocerata, known from the Eocene epoch
extinct family of large prehistoric mammals

Examples

Fossils from the Uintatheriidae provide important information about early mammalian evolution.
The Uintatheriidae were among the largest terrestrial mammals of their time.
Paleontologists studied the distinctive skulls of several Uintatheriidae specimens found in Wyoming.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Uintatherium, named after the Uinta Mountains in Utah, USA, and Greek thērion (beast), with -idae denoting a zoological family.
