Eurypterida

1. NOUN

/ jʊˌrɪptəˈrɪdə / (AmE), / jʊəˌrɪptəˈrɪdə / (BrE)
an order of extinct aquatic arthropods from the Paleozoic era, commonly known as sea scorpions
an order of extinct aquatic arthropods (sea scorpions)

Examples

Fossils of Eurypterida have been found on every continent except Antarctica.
The Eurypterida, or sea scorpions, flourished in the Silurian period.
Scientists believe that Eurypterida were among the largest arthropods to have ever lived.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'eury-' (wide) + 'pteron' (wing, fin) + '-ida' (suffix for zoological taxa).

2. NOUN

/ jʊˌrɪptəˈraɪdə / (AmE), / jʊərəptəˈrɪdə / (BrE)
an extinct order of aquatic arthropods from the Paleozoic era, commonly known as sea scorpions
extinct order of ancient aquatic arthropods

Examples

Fossils of Eurypterida reveal that these creatures were among the dominant predators of the Paleozoic seas.
The Eurypterida are sometimes called sea scorpions because of their resemblance to modern scorpions.
Scientists have discovered several giant species within Eurypterida that reached lengths of over two meters.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'eurys' (broad) and 'pteron' (wing, fin), referring to their broad swimming appendages.
