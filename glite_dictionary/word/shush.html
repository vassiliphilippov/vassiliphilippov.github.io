to shush

1. VERB

/ ʃʊʃ / (AmE), / ʃʊʃ / (BrE)
to tell someone to be quiet by saying 'shush' or making a similar sound
tell (someone) to be quiet by saying 'shush'

Examples

She shushed the children when the movie started.
He quickly shushed his friend in the library to avoid disturbing others.
The teacher shushed the class to get their attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hush - to make silence or become quiet
  • Synonym: silence - to make someone stop talking or making noise
