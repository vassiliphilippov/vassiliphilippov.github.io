automotive

1. ADJ

/ ˌɔː.təˈmoʊ.tɪv / (AmE), / ˌɔː.təˈməʊ.tɪv / (BrE)
relating to or containing within itself the means of propulsion or movement; self-propelled, especially referring to motor vehicles.
relating to self-propelled vehicles

Examples

The automotive industry has seen major technological advancements in the last decade.
With his interest in automotive engineering, he decided to design a new kind of self-driving car.
Many automotive companies are shifting toward electric vehicles to reduce emissions.

Synset Examples

a self-propelled vehicle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-propelled - capable of moving by itself, without external force

Etymology

From French 'automotive', from Greek 'autos' (self) + Latin 'motivus' (capable of moving).

2. ADJ

/ ˌɑːtəˈmoʊtɪv / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəˈməʊtɪv / (BrE)
relating to or concerned with motor vehicles or the automobile industry.
relating to motor vehicles

Examples

She works as an engineer in the automotive industry.
The company specializes in automotive parts and accessories.
Advances in automotive technology have made cars safer and more efficient.

Synset Examples

automotive supplies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: automobile - a car; a road vehicle, typically with four wheels, powered by an internal combustion engine

Etymology

From French *automotive* or directly from automobile + -ive (adjective suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02938249-a
Synset POS: a
