agitated

1. ADJ

/ ˈædʒɪˌteɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈædʒɪteɪtɪd / (BrE)
feeling or appearing troubled and nervous, often because of anxiety or excitement
emotionally troubled or nervous

Examples

She became agitated when she heard the unexpected news.
The agitated crowd grew louder as the announcement was delayed.
His hands were trembling and his voice was agitated during the meeting.

Synset Examples

agitated parents

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness or agitation
  • Synonym: disturbed - showing signs of emotional or mental agitation

Etymology

From Latin agitatus, past participle of agitare (to set in motion, disturb), via French agiter (to agitate).

2. ADJ

/ ˈædʒɪˌteɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈadʒ.ɪ.teɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
(of a liquid or substance) physically disturbed or set in motion by shaking or stirring.
physically disturbed or set in motion

Examples

The agitated water in the washing machine helped remove the stains quickly.
After being shaken vigorously, the agitated paint was ready to use.
The chemical reaction increased when the solution was agitated with a glass rod.

Synset Examples

the agitated mixture foamed and bubbled

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stirred - mixed or agitated by stirring
  • Antonym: settled - not disturbed; remaining still or at rest

Etymology

From Latin agitatus, past participle of agitare 'to shake, move'.

WordNet ID: oewn-00088039-a
Synset POS: a
