predicative

1. ADJ

/ prɛˈdɪkətɪv / (AmE), / prɪˈdɪkətɪv / (BrE)
(of an adjective or a phrase) used in the predicate of a sentence, especially after a verb such as 'be', and typically describing the subject (as in 'the sky is blue').
(grammar) used in the predicate of a sentence

Examples

In the sentence "The cake looks delicious," "delicious" is a predicative adjective.
Most adjectives can be used in both attributive and predicative positions.
The teacher explained the difference between an attributive and a predicative use of adjectives.

Synset Examples

‘red’ is a predicative adjective in ‘the apple is red’

Related Terms

  • Antonym: attributive - (of an adjective) preceding and modifying a noun directly

Etymology

From Latin praedicativus, from praedicare (to say publicly, to assert).
