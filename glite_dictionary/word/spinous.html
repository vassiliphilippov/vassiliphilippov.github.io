spinous

1. ADJ

/ ˈspaɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈspaɪ.nəs / (BrE)
shaped like a spine or thorn; having spines or thorn-like projections, especially in anatomical or botanical contexts
shaped like a spine or thorn

Examples

The cactus is covered in spinous outgrowths to deter animals from eating it.
Several spinous processes extend from each vertebra in the human spine.
The marine creature's spinous appendages make it well protected from predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiny - having many spines or thorns

Etymology

From Latin 'spinosus' (full of thorns), from 'spina' (thorn, spine).

2. ADJ

/ ˈspaɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈspaɪnəs / (BrE)
having spines or spine-like projections; covered with or bearing spines.
having spines or spine-like projections

Examples

The spinous dorsal fin of the fish helps protect it from predators.
Several plants have spinous leaves to deter herbivores.
The vertebra features a prominent spinous process visible in X-ray images.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiny - covered with or full of spines

Etymology

From Latin 'spinosus', from 'spina' meaning 'thorn or spine'.
