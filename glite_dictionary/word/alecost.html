alecost

1. NOUN

/ ˈæliˌkɔst / (AmE), / ˈæliˌkɒst / (BrE)
A Eurasian perennial herb (Tanacetum balsamita) with a scent resembling tansy, yellow buttonlike flowers, and leaves used as a salad green, flavoring, or for potpourri.
Eurasian herb used for flavoring and potpourri

Examples

She sprinkled chopped alecost leaves into the salad for a subtle, aromatic flavor.
The alecost in the garden thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.
Historically, brewers added alecost to ale for its pleasant, herbal taste.

Etymology

From 'ale' + 'cost', alluding to its historical use for flavoring ale; 'cost' meaning a kind of aromatic plant.
