foolery

1. NOUN

/ ˈfuːləri / (AmE), / ˈfuːləri / (BrE)
foolish or senseless behavior; silly or ridiculous actions or conduct.
foolish or silly behavior

Examples

He was scolded for his constant foolery during the meeting.
A bit of harmless foolery can lighten the mood of a serious discussion.
Their foolery at the party made everyone laugh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: folly - lack of good sense; foolish action or idea
  • Antonym: wisdom - the quality of being wise; good judgement

Etymology

From Middle English *folerye*, from Old French *folerie*, from *fol* ('fool').
