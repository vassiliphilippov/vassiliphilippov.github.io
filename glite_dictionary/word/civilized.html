civilized

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪvəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈsɪvəlaɪzd / (BrE)
having an advanced level of social, cultural, and technological development; marked by refinement and order
having advanced cultural and social development

Examples

New laws were introduced to ensure a more civilized society.
Many historians study the rise and fall of civilized societies throughout history.
The conference promoted the sharing of ideas in a truly civilized atmosphere.

Synset Examples

terrorist acts that shocked the civilized world

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uncivilized - lacking advanced culture and development; rude or primitive
  • Synonym: cultured - characterized by refined taste and manners

Etymology

Formed from "civilize" (from French "civiliser", from Latin "civilis" meaning "relating to a citizen").

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɪvəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈsɪv.ɪ.laɪzd / (BrE)
having or showing polite manners, refined taste, and good education; marked by sophistication and courtesy.
refined in taste and manners

Examples

Despite their wealth, the family was known for their civilized manners and hospitality.
A civilized conversation requires both parties to listen and respect differing opinions.
His civilized taste in art and music set him apart from his peers.

Synset Examples

cultivated speech
cultured Bostonians
cultured tastes
a genteel old lady
polite society

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cultured - having refined taste and manners
  • Synonym: genteel - polite, refined, and respectable in manner
  • Synonym: polite - having or showing behavior that is respectful and considerate of others
  • Antonym: uncivilized - lacking refinement, manners, or culture

Etymology

From Latin civilis (relating to citizens), via French civilisé.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01954376-s
Synset POS: s
