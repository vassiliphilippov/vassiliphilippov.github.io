eubacteria

1. NOUN

/ juː.bækˈtɪər.i.ə / (AmE), / juː.bækˈtɪə.ri.ə / (BrE)
a large group of bacteria characterized by rigid cell walls, some of which are motile due to the presence of flagella; includes most common bacteria except archaea
a group of bacteria with rigid cell walls
medical

Examples

Many eubacteria are beneficial and play a crucial role in digestion.
The scientist used a microscope to observe the structure of eubacteria in the sample.
Unlike archaea, most eubacteria have a rigid cell wall made of peptidoglycan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bacteria - microscopic single-celled organisms
  • Antonym: archaea - microorganisms similar to bacteria but genetically distinct

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'eu-' (true) and 'bacteria' (bacteria).
