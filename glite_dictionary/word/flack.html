flack

1. NOUN

/ flæk / (AmE), / flæk / (BrE)
intense and often public criticism or censure.
intense criticism or censure

Examples

The company received a lot of flack over its decision to raise prices.
She took some serious flack from the media after her comments.
Despite the flack, he stood by his controversial proposal.

Synset Examples

Clinton directed his fire at the Republican Party
the government has come under attack
don't give me any flak

Related Terms

  • Synonym: criticism - the expression of disapproval of someone or something; critique
  • Antonym: praise - the expression of approval or admiration

Etymology

Variant of 'flak' (anti-aircraft fire), which was adopted in English from German.

2. NOUN

/ flæk / (AmE), / flæk / (BrE)
a person who acts as a spokesperson or publicist, especially one who promotes a positive image and deflects criticism for their employer or client
spokesperson/publicist who defends employer

Examples

The company hired a flack to handle its public relations during the crisis.
Whenever there was bad press, their chief flack spun a positive story for the media.
As a seasoned flack, she knew exactly how to deflect tough questions from reporters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: publicist - a person responsible for publicizing a product, person, or company

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from the name of American press agent Gene Flack, or a variant of 'flak' as criticism.
