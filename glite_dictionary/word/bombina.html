Bombina

1. NOUN

/ bɒmˈbiːnə / (AmE), / bɒmˈbiːnə / (BrE)
A genus of small, brightly colored frogs known as fire-bellied toads, found in Europe and Asia.
genus of fire-bellied toads

Examples

The vivid colors of the Bombina serve as a warning to predators.
Several species within the genus Bombina are popular in the pet trade.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Bombina in Eastern Europe.

Etymology

From Latin 'bombina', meaning 'a buzzing or humming', used as a genus name in taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ bɑmˈbiːnə / (AmE), / bɒmˈbiːnə / (BrE)
A genus of small, brightly colored toads known as fire-bellied toads, found in Europe and Asia.
genus of fire-bellied toads

Examples

The Bombina genus includes several strikingly colored species found in wetlands of Europe and Asia.
Researchers studied the mating calls of the Bombina to better understand their communication.
The Bombina is well known for the bright red or orange pattern on its underside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fire-bellied toad - Common name for toads of the genus Bombina

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Italian 'bombina' (diminutive of 'bombo', meaning 'bumblebee'), possibly referring to the toads' coloration.
