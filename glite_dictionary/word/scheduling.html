scheduling

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɛdʒʊlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃedjuːlɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of arranging or planning events to take place at specific times or in a particular order
arranging planned events in time

Examples

Scheduling a meeting with everyone was more difficult than anticipated.
Project scheduling is essential for efficient time management.
Good scheduling helped the team finish the project ahead of time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: planning - the process of making plans for something
  • Degree Lower: rescheduling - changing the time of a planned event

Etymology

From 'schedule' + '-ing'; 'schedule' from Medieval Latin 'schedula' (slip of paper).
