hypolipoproteinemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpoʊˌlɪpoʊˌproʊtiˈniːmiə / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəʊˌlɪpəʊˌprəʊtiˈniːmiə / (BrE)
a medical condition characterized by abnormally low levels of lipoproteins and cholesterol in the blood
condition of low blood lipoproteins and cholesterol
medical

Examples

Routine screening revealed that the patient had hypolipoproteinemia after months of unexplained fatigue.
Certain genetic conditions can cause hypolipoproteinemia in children, leading to developmental problems.
The physician discussed treatment options for managing hypolipoproteinemia with the patient's family.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hyperlipoproteinemia - an excess of lipoproteins in the blood

Etymology

From hypo- (under, less) + lipoprotein + -emia (blood condition).
