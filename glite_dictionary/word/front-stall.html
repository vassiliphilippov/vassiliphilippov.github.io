front-stall

1. NOUN

/ frʌnt stɔl / (AmE), / frʌnt stɔːl / (BrE)
A piece of medieval plate armor designed to protect a horse's head in battle.
medieval horse head armor

Examples

The knight fitted a front-stall to his horse before entering the tournament.
Many museums display ornate front-stalls from the late medieval period.
A damaged front-stall was discovered during the castle excavation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chanfron - plate armor for a horse's head
