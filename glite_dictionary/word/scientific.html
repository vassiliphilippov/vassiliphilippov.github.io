scientific

1. ADJ

/ saɪənˈtɪfɪk / (AmE), / saɪənˈtɪfɪk / (BrE)
relating to or involving the principles, methods, or practices of science; based on or characterized by the methods and standards of science.
relating to science or scientific methods

Examples

She used a scientific approach to solve the complex problem.
All claims must be supported by scientific evidence.
The research was conducted in a thoroughly scientific manner.

Synset Examples

a scientific approach

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unscientific - not based on or conforming to scientific methods

Etymology

From Latin 'scientia' (knowledge) + '-ficus' (making, doing).

2. ADJ

/ ˌsaɪənˈtɪfɪk / (AmE), / ˌsaɪənˈtɪfɪk / (BrE)
relating to or based on the practice or principles of science.
relating to science

Examples

The results were published in a scientific journal after peer review.
Her scientific approach ensures that experiments are conducted accurately and fairly.
He made several scientific discoveries during his career as a researcher.

Synset Examples

scientific journals

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unscientific - not based on or in accordance with the methods or principles of science

Etymology

From Latin 'scientia' (knowledge) and the suffix '-fic' (making, doing).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02804097-a
