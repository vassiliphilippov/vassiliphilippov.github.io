otter

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːtər / (AmE), / ˈɒtə / (BrE)
the fur of an otter, especially when used for making garments
fur of an otter (used for garments)

Examples

The trader showed us a coat lined with otter that felt incredibly soft.
In the past, hats made from otter were considered a luxury.
He inherited a set of vintage gloves crafted from otter and mink.

Etymology

From Old English *otor*, from Proto-Germanic *otraz*, ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *udrós* (water animal).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑːtər / (AmE), / ˈɒtə / (BrE)
a semi-aquatic carnivorous mammal with webbed and clawed feet and usually dark brown fur, found in rivers, lakes, and coastal areas.
semi-aquatic carnivorous mammal

Examples

We watched an otter swimming gracefully in the river near our cabin.
The otter built its den along the muddy riverbank.
Conservation efforts have helped increase the local otter population in recent years.

Etymology

From Old English *otor*, of Germanic origin, related to German *Otter* and Latin *lutra*.
