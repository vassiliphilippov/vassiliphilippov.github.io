Peneidae

1. NOUN

/ pəˈniː.ɪˌdiː / (AmE), / pɛˈniː.ɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of tropical marine prawns, many of which are important as food and in fisheries.
family of tropical prawns

Examples

The Peneidae family includes many prawns that are farmed commercially.
Researchers studied the distribution of Peneidae along the tropical coastline.
Shrimp in the Peneidae family are an important source of seafood worldwide.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus name Penaeus.
