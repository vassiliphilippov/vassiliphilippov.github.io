to requite

1. VERB

/ rɪˈkwaɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈkwaɪt / (BrE)
to make repayment for or return (a favor, service, wrongdoing, love, etc.)
to repay or return (a favor or feeling)

Examples

She hoped to requite his kindness with a thoughtful gift.
Their loyalty was requited by generous rewards from the company.
He longed to requite her love, but could not find the right words.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to repay - to pay back money or a favor
  • Synonym: to retaliate - to take revenge or return an attack
  • Antonym: to forgive - to stop feeling resentment towards someone

Etymology

From Latin 'requītāre' (to repay), from 're-' (again) + 'quītāre' (to settle, pay).
