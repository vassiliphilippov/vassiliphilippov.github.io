Pierre Corneille

1. PROPN

/ ˌpjɛər kɔrˈneɪ / (AmE), / ˌpjɛː kɔːˈneɪ / (BrE)
(1606–1684) A French tragic playwright renowned for his plays that address grand moral themes in elegant verse.
French tragic playwright (1606–1684)

Examples

Pierre Corneille is considered one of the founders of French classical tragedy.
Many students study the works of Pierre Corneille alongside those of Racine and Molière.
The influence of Pierre Corneille on European drama has been profound and long-lasting.
