to complexify

1. VERB

/ kəmˈplɛksɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / kəmˈplɛksɪˌfaɪ / (BrE)
to make or become more complex; to cause something to have more complicated characteristics or consequences
make or become more complex

Examples

New regulations will only complexify the tax process.
Attempts to solve one problem may sometimes complexify another.
Their interactions tended to complexify the project rather than simplify it.

Synset Examples

These actions will ramify

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to complicate - to make something more difficult or involved

Etymology

Formed from 'complex' plus the suffix '-ify', meaning 'to make'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00402279-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kəmˈplɛksɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / kəmˈplɛksɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to make or become more complex; to increase the complexity of something
make or become more complex

Examples

The new regulations only complexify the process instead of making it simpler.
She tends to complexify simple tasks by adding unnecessary steps.
Instead of solving the problem, his suggestions seemed to complexify it further.

Synset Examples

he unnecessarily complexified every problem

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to complicate - to make more difficult or complex

Etymology

Formed from 'complex' + '-ify', following the pattern of verbs such as 'simplify'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00402108-v
Synset POS: v
