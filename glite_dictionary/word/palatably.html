palatably

1. ADV

/ ˈpælətəbli / (AmE), / ˈpælətəbli / (BrE)
in a way that is pleasant to the taste or acceptable to the mind or feelings
in a pleasant or acceptable way

Examples

The chef prepared the vegetables so palatably that even the children enjoyed them.
The book explains complex theories palatably, making them accessible to a wider audience.
She expressed her criticism palatably, ensuring that no one felt offended.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unpalatably - in an unpleasant or unacceptable way
  • Synonym: tastily - in a tasty or flavorful way

Etymology

From 'palatable' + '-ly', with 'palatable' from French 'palatable', from Latin 'palatum' (palate).
