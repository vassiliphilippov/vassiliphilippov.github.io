Plumeria

1. NOUN

/ pluːˈmɪriə / (AmE), / pluːˈmɪəriə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American deciduous shrubs and small trees with candelabra-like branches and fragrant white or pink flowers.
tropical flowering plant genus

Examples

The Plumeria tree is famous for its sweetly scented blossoms.
She planted a Plumeria in her garden for its beautiful pink flowers.
Hawaiian leis are often made using Plumeria flowers.

Etymology

Named after Charles Plumier (1646–1704), French botanist.
