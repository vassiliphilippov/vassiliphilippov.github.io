springboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈsprɪŋˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈsprɪŋˌbɔːd / (BrE)
a flexible board from which someone jumps upward, especially in diving or gymnastics
flexible board for jumping

Examples

The diver ran down the pool and leaped off the springboard with perfect form.
Gymnasts often use a springboard to get extra height for their vaults.
She bounced on the springboard before executing a flawless dive.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: platform - a raised level surface on which people or things can stand

Etymology

From 'spring' (to leap) + 'board', indicating a board that allows springing upward.

an idea, event, or starting point that provides a basis for further development or activity
starting point for further development

He used his previous job experience as a springboard to start his own business.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsprɪŋˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈsprɪŋˌbɔːd / (BrE)
a thing or event that helps initiate or further progress in an activity, development, or career; a starting point for advancement
starting point for advancement

Examples

Her first book became a springboard for her career as a novelist.
The training program acted as a springboard to new job opportunities.
Winning the competition provided a valuable springboard for his career.

Synset Examples

Her internship served as a springboard for her career.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: launchpad - a platform or starting point for launching, especially as a metaphor for beginnings

Etymology

From spring + board, originally referring to a flexible board used to gain height in diving or gymnastics; used figuratively since the early 20th century.
