dicynodont

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈsaɪnəˌdɑnt / (AmE), / daɪˈsɪnədɒnt / (BrE)
An extinct group of herbivorous therapsid reptiles characterized by two tusks and beak-like jaws, dominant during the late Permian and Triassic periods.
extinct tusked therapsid reptile

Examples

The fossilized remains of a dicynodont were discovered in the South African desert.
Scientists believe the dicynodont was one of the most successful herbivores of its time.
A child's curiosity was piqued by a museum display featuring a life-size model of a dicynodont.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: therapsid - any member of the group of mammal-like reptiles from which mammals evolved

Etymology

From Greek di- (two) + kýōn (dog) + odont- (tooth), referring to their paired tusks.
