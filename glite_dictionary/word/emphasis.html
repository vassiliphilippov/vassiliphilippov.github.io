emphasis

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛmfəsɪs / (AmE), / ˈɛm.fə.sɪs / (BrE)
special importance, value, or prominence given to something, or the intensity or forcefulness with which something is expressed
special importance or intensity in expression

Examples

She spoke with great emphasis, making her point clear to everyone in the room.
The teacher placed strong emphasis on the importance of regular study habits.
His denial carried an unusual emphasis that made everyone believe him.

Synset Examples

the vehemence of his denial
his emphasis on civil rights

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stress - special attention or importance attached to something
  • Antonym: to understate - to present or express with less force, emphasis, or importance than is necessary

Etymology

From Greek *emphasis* (appearance, outward sign), from *emphainein* (to present, exhibit).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛm.fə.sɪs / (AmE), / ˈɛm.fə.sɪs / (BrE)
Special importance or attention given to something, often made clear by position or repetition in speech or writing.
special importance or stress

Examples

The teacher placed emphasis on the importance of good study habits.
By repeating the word, she gave extra emphasis to her point.
In English, word order can change the emphasis of a sentence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stress - greater force given to a word or syllable in speech
  • Antonym: deemphasize - to reduce or minimize the importance of something

Etymology

From Greek *emphásis* (appearance, significance), from *emphaínein* (to exhibit, indicate).

4. NOUN

/ ˈɛm.fə.sɪs / (AmE), / ˈɛm.fə.sɪs / (BrE)
special importance or prominence given to a syllable or musical note, especially by means of stress or pitch.
prominence given to a syllable or note

Examples

In the word 'record', the emphasis changes depending on whether it's a noun or a verb.
The conductor asked for more emphasis on the downbeat in the next movement.
When learning English, it's important to listen for the emphasis on different syllables.

Synset Examples

he put the stress on the wrong syllable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stress - relative emphasis given to a syllable or word in speech

Etymology

From Latin 'emphasis', from Greek 'emphasis' (significance, display), from 'emphainein' (to present, display).
