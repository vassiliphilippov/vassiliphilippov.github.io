autacoid

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːtəˌkɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈɔːtəkɔɪd / (BrE)
a physiologically active substance produced within the body, often with a local effect, whose classification is sometimes uncertain
body's internal secretion of local action
medical

Examples

Histamine is a well-known autacoid involved in allergic responses.
Researchers studied the effects of an autacoid on local tissue inflammation.
Some autacoids act quickly and are rapidly broken down at the site of release.

Etymology

From Greek 'autos' (self) + 'akos' (remedy).
