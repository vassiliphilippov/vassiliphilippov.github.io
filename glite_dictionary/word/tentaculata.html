Tentaculata

1. NOUN

/ tɛnˌtækjuˈleɪtə / (AmE), / tɛnˌtækjʊˈleɪtə / (BrE)
a class of marine animals (ctenophores) characterized by having retractile tentacles
class of marine ctenophores with retractile tentacles

Examples

Scientists observed the unique feeding behavior of Tentaculata in the open ocean.
The class Tentaculata is distinguished by its retractile tentacles.
Many species within Tentaculata play important roles in marine ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ctenophore - marine invertebrate with comb-like cilia

Etymology

From Latin 'tentaculum' (feeler, tentacle), referring to the tentacles of these animals.

2. NOUN

/ tɛnˌtækjuˈleɪtə / (AmE), / tɛnˌtækjʊˈlɑːtə / (BrE)
a class of ctenophores characterized by possessing retractile tentacles.
ctenophore class with retractile tentacles

Examples

The majority of ctenophores found in shallow waters belong to the Tentaculata.
Researchers discovered a new species within the class Tentaculata during the deep-sea expedition.
Unlike Nuda, members of Tentaculata possess two well-developed retractile tentacles.

Etymology

From Latin 'tentaculum' (feeler or tentacle), with the Neo-Latin suffix '-ata' for taxonomic classes.
