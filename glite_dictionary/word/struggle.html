struggle

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrʌɡəl / (AmE), / ˈstrʌɡl / (BrE)
a determined effort to achieve or overcome something, often involving difficulty or opposition
a determined effort to achieve something

Examples

Completing the marathon was a real struggle for her, but she never gave up.
He faced many struggles while building his business from scratch.
Finding affordable housing in the city has become a struggle for many families.

Synset Examples

getting through the crowd was a real struggle
he fought a battle for recognition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: battle - a sustained fight or effort to achieve or defend something
  • Synonym: effort - a vigorous or determined attempt
  • Antonym: to succeed - to achieve the desired aim or result

Etymology

From Old French *strugler* (to wriggle, to struggle), probably of Germanic origin.
