audacious

1. ADJ

/ ɔˈdeɪʃəs / (AmE), / ɔːˈdeɪʃəs / (BrE)
showing a willingness to take bold risks or to behave in a way that is shockingly unconventional or disrespectful of rules and propriety
bold; unrestrained by convention

Examples

The audacious plan surprised even the most seasoned executives at the company.
Her audacious performance challenged the norms of classical theatre.
He made an audacious move by questioning the established procedures during the meeting.

Synset Examples

an audacious trick to pull
a barefaced hypocrite
the most bodacious display of tourism this side of Anaheim
bald-faced lies
brazen arrogance
the modern world with its quick material successes and insolent belief in the boundless possibilities of progress

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brazen - bold and without shame
  • Antonym: timid - showing a lack of courage or confidence

Etymology

From Latin audax (bold, daring), from audere (to dare).

2. ADJ

/ ɔˈdeɪʃəs / (AmE), / ɔːˈdeɪʃəs / (BrE)
willing to take bold risks; showing a readiness to venture or challenge norms
willing to take risks; daring

Examples

The young entrepreneur made an audacious decision to launch her startup in a crowded market.
He presented an audacious plan to revolutionize public transportation.
Their audacious climb of the remote mountain impressed even the most seasoned adventurers.

Synset Examples

audacious visions of the total conquest of space
an audacious interpretation of two Jacobean dramas
the most daring of contemporary fiction writers
a venturesome investor
a venturous spirit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daring - willing to take risks; bold
  • Antonym: cautious - careful to avoid risk or danger

Etymology

From Latin 'audacia' (boldness), from 'audax'.

Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ɔˈdeɪʃəs / (AmE), / ɔːˈdeɪʃəs / (BrE)
showing a willingness to take surprisingly bold risks; not afraid of danger or difficulty
fearless; daring; bold

Examples

The audacious climber reached the summit despite the terrible weather conditions.
She made an audacious decision to travel alone through dangerous regions.
His audacious spirit inspired others to take risks and face their fears.

Synset Examples

audacious explorers
fearless reporters and photographers
intrepid pioneers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fearless - not afraid; without fear
  • Synonym: intrepid - very brave; fearless
  • Antonym: timid - showing a lack of courage or confidence

Etymology

From Latin 'audacia' (boldness), from 'audax' (bold).
