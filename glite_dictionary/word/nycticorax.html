Nycticorax

1. NOUN

/ nɪkˈtɪkəˌræks / (AmE), / nɪkˈtɪkəræks / (BrE)
A genus of medium-sized night herons found in the Old World, known for their nocturnal habits.
genus of Old World night herons

Examples

The black-crowned Nycticorax is often seen hunting for fish at dusk.
Several species within the genus Nycticorax inhabit wetlands across Asia and Africa.
Researchers observed the nesting behavior of the Nycticorax in the mangrove forests.

Etymology

From Greek *nykt-*, 'night', and *korax*, 'raven', referring to their nocturnal habits.

2. NOUN

/ nɪkˈtɪkəˌrɑks / (AmE), / nɪkˈtɪkəˌræks / (BrE)
A genus of night herons found mainly in the Old World, known for their nocturnal habits.
genus of Old World night herons

Examples

The Nycticorax is well known for its shy nature and crepuscular feeding habits.
Several species within the genus Nycticorax are found in wetlands across Asia, Africa, and Europe.
Naturalists often observe Nycticorax roosting quietly in mangrove swamps during the day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: night heron - a type of heron active mainly at night, genus Nycticorax and others

Etymology

From Greek nuktíkorax, meaning 'night raven'.
