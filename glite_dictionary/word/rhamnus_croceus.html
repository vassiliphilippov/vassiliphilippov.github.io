Rhamnus croceus

1. NOUN

/ ˈræmnəs ˈkroʊsiəs / (AmE), / ˈræmnəs ˈkrəʊsiəs / (BrE)
A small, spiny, evergreen shrub native to the western United States and Mexico, with tiny flowers and bright red berries.
spiny shrub with red berries, native to W U.S. and Mexico

Examples

The Rhamnus croceus is commonly found in dry, rocky soils in the southwestern United States.
Birds are attracted to the bright red berries of the Rhamnus croceus shrub.
Gardeners appreciate Rhamnus croceus for its evergreen foliage and ornamental appeal.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Rhamnus' (buckthorn) and 'croceus' (saffron-colored, referring to the berries).
