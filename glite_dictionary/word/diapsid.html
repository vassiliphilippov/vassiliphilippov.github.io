diapsid

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈæp.sɪd / (AmE), / daɪˈap.sɪd / (BrE)
A reptile having a pair of openings in the skull behind each eye, characteristic of a large group that includes most modern reptiles and their extinct relatives.
reptile with two skull openings

Examples

The tuatara is a living diapsid that has remained largely unchanged for millions of years.
Fossil evidence shows that dinosaurs were diapsids, sharing this skull feature with modern birds.
Paleontologists use the presence of two temporal openings to classify extinct reptiles as diapsids.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: synapsid - reptile-like vertebrate with a single skull opening behind each eye

Etymology

From Greek di- (two) + apsis (arch, loop), referring to the two skull openings.
