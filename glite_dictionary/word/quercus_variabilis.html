Quercus variabilis

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɜːrkəs ˌvær.iˈæb.ɪ.lɪs / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs ˌvær.iˈæb.ɪ.lɪs / (BrE)
a medium to large deciduous oak tree native to China, Japan, and Korea, known for its thick, corky bark
East Asian oak tree with corky bark

Examples

The Quercus variabilis is prized for its corky bark, which is sometimes harvested.
Botanists studied the distribution of Quercus variabilis across the mountainous regions of China.
In traditional landscaping, Quercus variabilis is valued for its resilience and shade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oak - any tree or shrub in the genus Quercus

Etymology

From Latin: 'Quercus' meaning 'oak', and 'variabilis' meaning 'variable' or 'changeable'.
