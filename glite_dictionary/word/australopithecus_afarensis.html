Australopithecus afarensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑː.strə.lə.pɪˈθiː.kəs ˌæfəˈrɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˌɒ.strələ.pɪˈθiː.kəs ˌæfəˈrɛnsɪs / (BrE)
an extinct species of early hominin known from fossils found in Ethiopia, dating from about 3.5 to 4 million years ago
early extinct hominin species

Examples

Fossils of Australopithecus afarensis have provided important insights into human evolution.
The famous 'Lucy' skeleton belongs to the species Australopithecus afarensis.
Researchers continue to debate the social behavior of Australopithecus afarensis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Australopithecus - genus of extinct hominins to which A. afarensis belongs

Etymology

From Latinized Greek: 'Australopithecus' (southern ape) and 'afarensis', referring to the Afar region in Ethiopia where fossils were found.
