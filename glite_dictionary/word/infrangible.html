infrangible

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈfrændʒəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈfrændʒɪbəl / (BrE)
impossible or very difficult to break or separate into parts; unbreakable or indivisible.
impossible to break or divide

Examples

The treaty formed an infrangible bond between the two nations.
Her infrangible spirit helped her endure many hardships.
Philosophers have debated the existence of an infrangible truth.

Synset Examples

an infrangible series

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fragile - easily broken or damaged
  • Synonym: indestructible - impossible to destroy or break

Etymology

From Latin 'infrangibilis', from 'in-' (not) + 'frangere' (to break).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈfrændʒəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈfrændʒɪbəl / (BrE)
impossible to break or violate; not able to be infringed upon
impossible to violate or break

Examples

The constitution guarantees infrangible rights to all its citizens.
They believe the code of honor is infrangible and must be respected by everyone.
The treaty established infrangible boundaries between the two nations.

Synset Examples

infrangible human rights

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inviolable - never to be broken or infringed

Etymology

From Latin 'infrangibilis', from 'in-' (not) + 'frangere' (to break).
