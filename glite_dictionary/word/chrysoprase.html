chrysoprase

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪsəˌpreɪz / (AmE), / ˈkrɪsəˌpreɪz / (BrE)
A green variety of chalcedony used as a semi-precious gemstone.
green gemstone variety of chalcedony

Examples

She wore a beautiful pendant set with a shimmering chrysoprase.
Ancient Greeks prized chrysoprase for its vivid green color and rarity.
The jeweler displayed several rings featuring polished chrysoprase stones.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: chalcedony - a microcrystalline variety of quartz used as a gemstone

Etymology

From Greek *khrūsoprasos*, from *khrūso-* (gold) + *prason* (leek), referring to its color.
