spasmodically

1. ADV

/ spæzˈmɑːdɪkli / (AmE), / spæzˈmɒdɪkli / (BrE)
In sudden, brief, or irregular bursts; occurring intermittently or unevenly.
in sudden, irregular bursts

Examples

She worked spasmodically, sometimes making great progress and other times being idle for weeks.
Thunder rumbled spasmodically in the distance throughout the night.
The old car engine started spasmodically before finally stalling.

Synset Examples

I began to write intermittently and spasmodically

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intermittently - at irregular intervals; from time to time

Etymology

From 'spasmodic' + '-ally'; 'spasmodic' from French 'spasmodique', from Greek 'spasmos' (spasm).

2. ADV

/ spæzˈmɑdɪkli / (AmE), / spæzˈmɒdɪkli / (BrE)
In a manner characterized by sudden, brief, or irregular bursts or movements; in spasms.
in spasms or irregular bursts

Examples

He sobbed spasmodically as he recalled the traumatic memory.
Lights flickered spasmodically during the thunderstorm, causing the room to alternately brighten and darken.
Her hand twitched spasmodically as the effects of the medication wore off.

Synset Examples

the mouth was slightly open, and jerked violently and spasmodically at one corner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intermittently - in a manner that starts and stops at intervals
  • Antonym: steadily - in a continuous, regular, and even manner

Etymology

From 'spasmodic' (relating to a spasm) + '-ally'. 'Spasmodic' from New Latin 'spasmodicus', from Greek 'spasmos' (spasm).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00465509-r
Synset POS: r
