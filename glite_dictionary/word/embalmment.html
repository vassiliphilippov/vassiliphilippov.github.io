embalmment

1. NOUN

/ ɛmˈbɑlm.mənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɑːlm.mənt / (BrE)
The process of preserving a dead body by treating it with balsams, drugs, or other chemicals to delay decomposition.
preservation of a dead body with chemicals

Examples

The embalmment of pharaohs was a crucial part of ancient Egyptian funerary rituals.
Due to the embalmment process, the body was preserved for the funeral service days later.
Modern embalmment techniques involve advanced chemicals to slow decay.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to embalm - to preserve a dead body with chemicals
  • Antonym: burial - the act of placing a dead body in the ground

Etymology

From Old French *embaumer* (to embalm), from Latin *in balma*, referring to use of balsam.
