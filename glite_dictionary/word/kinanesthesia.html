kinanesthesia

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɪn.æn.əsˈθi.ʒə / (AmE), / ˌkɪn.æn.ɪsˈθiː.zi.ə / (BrE)
the inability to perceive or sense movement of the body or its parts
inability to sense movement
medical

Examples

The patient with kinanesthesia was unable to detect the movement of her own limbs.
Diagnosis of kinanesthesia often requires specialized neurological testing.
Researchers are studying kinanesthesia to understand its impact on motor control.

Etymology

From Greek 'kinein' (to move) and 'aisthēsis' (sensation).
