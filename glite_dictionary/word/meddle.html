to meddle

1. VERB

/ ˈmɛdəl / (AmE), / ˈmɛdəl / (BrE)
to become involved in something that is not your concern, especially in a way that is annoying or unwanted
interfere unwantedly in others' affairs

Examples

You shouldn't meddle in other people's arguments if you don't know the full story.
The government has promised not to meddle with the independence of the courts.
He was always meddling in his sister’s personal life, much to her annoyance.

Synset Examples

Don't meddle in my affairs!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to interfere - to become involved in something that does not concern you
  • Synonym: to butt in - to interrupt or involve oneself unwantedly in others' concerns

Etymology

From Middle English 'medlen', from Old French 'mesler', meaning 'to mix', from Vulgar Latin *misculare.
