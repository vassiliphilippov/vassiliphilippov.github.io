Botaurus stellaris

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈtɔːrəs stɛˈlæɹɪs / (AmE), / bəʊˈtɔːrəs stɛˈlɑːrɪs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the Eurasian bittern, a large, secretive wading bird found in marshes across Europe and Asia, known for its distinctive booming call
Eurasian bittern, bird species

Examples

The Botaurus stellaris is rarely seen but often heard in the reed beds of European wetlands.
During breeding season, the distinctive booming call of the Botaurus stellaris echoes across the marshes.
Conservation efforts have helped increase the population of Botaurus stellaris in several regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: great bittern - common English name for Botaurus stellaris

Etymology

From Latin: 'Botaurus' (from Medieval Latin for 'bittern'), 'stellaris' meaning 'starry' or 'of the stars'.
