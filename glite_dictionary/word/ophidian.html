ophidian

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈfɪdiən / (AmE), / əʊˈfɪdɪən / (BrE)
A reptile of the suborder Ophidia, comprising snakes; a snake.
a snake or member of the snake suborder

Examples

The museum features a rare fossil of an extinct ophidian from the late Cretaceous period.
Several ophidian species have developed unique methods for delivering venom to their prey.
Researchers observed the camouflage patterns of the ophidian in the dense rainforest undergrowth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snake - A legless, scaly, elongate reptile; member of suborder Ophidia

Etymology

From Greek 'ophidion', diminutive of 'ophis' (snake).
