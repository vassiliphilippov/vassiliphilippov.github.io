Nycticebus

1. NOUN

/ nɪkˈtɪsɪbəs / (AmE), / nɪkˈtɪsɪbəs / (BrE)
a genus of primates in the family Lorisidae, comprising the slow lorises of Southeast Asia
genus of slow lorises (primates)

Examples

The Nycticebus genus includes all species of slow lorises found in Southeast Asia.
Conservationists are concerned about the decline in Nycticebus populations due to illegal wildlife trade.
Researchers observed that Nycticebus species are primarily nocturnal and arboreal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slow loris - a small, nocturnal primate of genus Nycticebus

Etymology

From Greek nyx, nyktos (night) + kepos (monkey); refers to nocturnal habits.

2. NOUN

/ nɪkˈtɪsɪbəs / (AmE), / nɪkˈtɪsɪbəs / (BrE)
A genus of slow-moving, nocturnal primates known as slow lorises, native to Southeast Asia.
genus of slow lorises (primates)

Examples

The Nycticebus genus includes several species of slow lorises found in Southeast Asia.
Researchers studied the behavior of Nycticebus in their natural habitat.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect Nycticebus from illegal wildlife trade.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek 'nykt-' meaning 'night' and '-cebus' meaning 'monkey'.
