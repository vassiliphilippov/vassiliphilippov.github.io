nonporous

1. ADJ

/ nɑnˈpɔrəs / (AmE), / nɒnˈpɔːrəs / (BrE)
not allowing liquid or air to pass through; not having pores or openings
not allowing liquid or air through

Examples

The laboratory requires nonporous surfaces for proper sanitation.
He bought a nonporous raincoat that keeps him completely dry.
Nonporous tiles are ideal for bathrooms because they resist water absorption.

Synset Examples

nonporous wood

Related Terms

  • Antonym: porous - having small holes that allow liquid or air to pass through

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'non-' (not) and 'porous' (having pores or openings), from Latin 'porus'.
