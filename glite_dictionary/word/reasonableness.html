reasonableness

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːzənəblnəs / (AmE), / ˈriːzənəblnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being fair, sensible, and based on good judgment
quality of being sensible or fair

Examples

The reasonableness of her proposal convinced the whole committee to support the project.
Courts are expected to assess the reasonableness of actions taken by officials.
We questioned the reasonableness of the new company policy during the staff meeting.

Synset Examples

the judiciary is built on the reasonableness of judges

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rationality - the quality of being logical and reasonable
  • Antonym: irrationality - the state of being unreasonable or lacking sense

Etymology

From Middle English 'resonable', from Old French 'raisonnable', from Latin 'rationabilis' (reasonable) + -ness.
