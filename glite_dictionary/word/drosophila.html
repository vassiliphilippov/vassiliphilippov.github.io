drosophila

1. NOUN

/ drəˈsɑːfɪlə / (AmE), / drəˈsɒfɪlə / (BrE)
A small fruit fly of the genus Drosophila, widely used in genetic research due to its simple genetics and rapid life cycle.
genetic research fruit fly
medical

Examples

The drosophila has played a crucial role in the field of genetics.
Researchers use drosophila to study gene inheritance patterns and mutations.
Many discoveries about chromosomes were first made using drosophila in laboratory experiments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fruit fly - a small fly that feeds on fruit, notably Drosophila species

Etymology

From New Latin Drosophila, genus name, from Greek drosos (dew) + phila (loving).
