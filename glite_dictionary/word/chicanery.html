chicanery

1. NOUN

/ ʃɪˈkeɪnəri / (AmE), / ʃɪˈkeɪnəri / (BrE)
the use of clever but dishonest tricks or deception, especially to achieve a financial or legal advantage
deceptive trickery or fraud

Examples

The company was accused of chicanery in its accounting practices.
Through legal chicanery, the contract favored the seller more than the buyer realized.
Voters resent the political chicanery that undermines their trust in the system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deceit - the act of deceiving or misleading
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and fair

Etymology

From French 'chicanerie', derived from 'chicaner' (to quibble, to trick).
