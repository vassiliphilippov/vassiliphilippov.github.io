sheepshearing

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃipˌʃɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃiːpˌʃɪə.rɪŋ / (BrE)
the time or season when sheep are sheared for their wool
season or event when sheep are shorn

Examples

The annual sheepshearing attracted visitors from neighboring villages.
Many farmers prepare for sheepshearing during the late spring.
After sheepshearing, the wool is collected and sent to the mill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shearing - the act of cutting the wool off sheep

Etymology

From sheep + shearing, denoting the act of shearing sheep.

3. NOUN

/ ˈʃipˌʃɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃiːpˌʃɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
A traditional festival held when sheep are sheared, often featuring celebrations, competitions, and gatherings.
festival for sheep shearing

Examples

The village hosts an annual sheepshearing each summer, drawing visitors from across the region.
During the sheepshearing, locals demonstrate traditional shearing techniques and celebrate with music and food.
Many rural communities preserve their heritage by organizing a sheepshearing as a community event.
