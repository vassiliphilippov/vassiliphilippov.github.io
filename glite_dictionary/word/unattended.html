unattended

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.əˈtɛn.dɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.əˈtɛn.dɪd / (BrE)
not being watched or cared for; lacking supervision or attention.
not being watched or cared for

Examples

Please do not leave your bags unattended at any time in the airport.
The injured man lay unattended on the roadside for several minutes before help arrived.
Young children must not be left unattended near the swimming pool.

Synset Examples

a neglected child
many casualties were lying unattended

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neglected - not receiving proper care or attention

Etymology

From un- (not) + attended (past participle of 'attend', from Latin 'attendere' meaning 'to give heed to').

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.əˈtɛn.dɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.əˈtɛn.dɪd / (BrE)
not being watched, looked after, or cared for; left without supervision.
not watched or cared for

Examples

Never leave your luggage unattended in public places.
The child wandered into the street while his mother was unattended.
A fire that is unattended can quickly become dangerous.

Synset Examples

she dashed out leaving the bar unattended
a fire left unattended

Related Terms

  • Antonym: attended - being watched, looked after, or cared for

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'attended' (past participle of 'attend').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02359129-s

3. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.əˈtɛn.dɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.əˈtɛn.dɪd / (BrE)
not being watched, cared for, or accompanied by someone; without supervision, escort, or protection.
not watched or accompanied

Examples

The package was left unattended at the airport for over an hour.
Children should not be allowed to play unattended near the pool.
Several unattended cars were parked in the lot overnight.

Synset Examples

unattended women
problems unattended with danger

Related Terms

  • Antonym: attended - having someone present to look after or accompany; being supervised or accompanied

Etymology

From un- (not) + attended (past participle of attend).
