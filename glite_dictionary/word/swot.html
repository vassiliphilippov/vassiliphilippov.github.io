swot

1. VERB

/ swɑt / (AmE), / swɒt / (BrE)
to study intensively, especially before an examination
study intensively before an exam
british informal

Examples

I had to swot all night for my biology exam.
She often swots in the library until late in the evening.
He was busy swotting for his finals and barely had time to relax.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

2. NOUN

/ swɑt / (AmE), / swɒt / (BrE)
(British, informal) a student who is considered to be excessively studious or boringly academic, often ridiculed by peers.
excessively studious student (British, informal)
informal british

Examples

She was labeled a swot by her classmates for always getting top marks.
Nobody wanted to sit next to the swot during lunch breaks.
Even though he was teased as a swot, he was proud of his academic achievements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nerd - a person devoted to academic pursuits, often considered socially awkward
  • Antonym: dunce - a person who is slow to learn or stupid, often the opposite social role in a school context

Etymology

British slang, possibly from 'to swot' (to study hard), of uncertain origin; compare Scottish 'swat' (to sweat).
