hemimetaboly

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛmɪˈmɛtəˌboʊli / (AmE), / ˌhɛmɪˈmɛtəbəli / (BrE)
incomplete metamorphosis in insects, where development proceeds through distinct nymphal stages without a pupal stage
incomplete metamorphosis in insects
medical

Examples

Grasshoppers undergo hemimetaboly, skipping the pupal stage seen in butterflies.
Mayflies are classic examples of insects displaying hemimetaboly.
Students studied hemimetaboly to compare it with complete metamorphosis in their biology class.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: holometaboly - complete metamorphosis in insects, including a pupal stage

Etymology

From Greek hemi- (half) + metabole (change).
