adrenosterone

1. NOUN

/ əˌdrɛnəˈstɜrˌoʊn / (AmE), / əˌdrɛnəʊˈstɜːrəʊn / (BrE)
a steroid hormone with androgenic activity produced by the adrenal cortex
steroid hormone from adrenal cortex
medical

Examples

Researchers discovered that adrenosterone may play a role in regulating certain metabolic processes.
The production of adrenosterone in the adrenal cortex varies depending on physiological conditions.
Some supplements falsely claim to increase adrenosterone levels for athletic performance.

Etymology

From 'adrenal' + 'testosterone', indicating its origin and function.
