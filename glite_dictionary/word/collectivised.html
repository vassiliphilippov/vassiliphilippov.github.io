collectivised

1. ADJ

/ kəˈlɛktɪˌvaɪzd / (AmE), / kəˈlɛktɪvaɪzd / (BrE)
(of property, industry, or agriculture) organized on the principle of collective ownership by the state or the people.
organized on principle of collective ownership
british

Examples

After the revolution, all farms were collectivised to increase agricultural output.
In a collectivised economy, private ownership of factories is eliminated.
The collectivised system faced criticism for reducing individual incentive among workers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collectivized - organized on the principle of collective ownership (American spelling)

Etymology

From 'collectivise', from French collectiviser, based on 'collective'.

2. ADJ

/ kəˈlɛktɪˌvaɪzd / (AmE), / kəˈlɛktɪvaɪzd / (BrE)
(British spelling) subscribing to or characterized by the socialistic doctrine of ownership by the people collectively, especially regarding property or means of production.
adhering to collective social ownership
british

Examples

Many industries in the Soviet Union were collectivised during the 20th century.
Her political beliefs were firmly collectivised, favoring shared ownership over private property.
After the revolution, farmland was rapidly collectivised under the new regime.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collectivized - subscribing to the socialistic doctrine of ownership by the people collectively (U.S. spelling)
  • Antonym: individualistic - characterized by independence and self-reliance, the opposite of collective ownership

Etymology

From British English 'collectivise', itself from 'collective' + '-ise', patterned after the Russian concept of collective farming (kolkhoz).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00299373-s
Synset POS: s
