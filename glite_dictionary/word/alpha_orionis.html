Alpha Orionis

1. PROPN

/ ˈælfə ɔːˈraɪ.ə.nɪs / (AmE), / ˈælfə ɔːˈraɪ.ən.ɪs / (BrE)
the second brightest star in the constellation Orion, also known as Betelgeuse
the second brightest star in Orion

Examples

Many amateur astronomers easily spot Alpha Orionis in the night sky during winter.
The brightness of Alpha Orionis can vary dramatically due to its size and age.
Ancient cultures often used Alpha Orionis as a reference point for navigation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Betelgeuse - bright red supergiant star in Orion; another name for Alpha Orionis
  • Degree Higher: Rigel - the brightest star in Orion

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'the alpha (brightest) star of Orion', although Beta Orionis is slightly brighter.
