amazing

1. ADJ

/ əˈmeɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈmeɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
causing great surprise or wonder; astonishing
causing great surprise or wonder

Examples

The magician performed an amazing trick that left everyone speechless.
She showed amazing strength during the difficult ordeal.
The view from the top of the mountain was absolutely amazing.

Synset Examples

she does an amazing amount of work
the dog was capable of astonishing tricks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astonishing - causing a feeling of great surprise or wonder
  • Antonym: ordinary - not unusual or surprising

Etymology

From amaze + -ing, first used in the 16th century.

2. ADJ

/ əˈmeɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈmeɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
causing great surprise or wonder; inspiring awe or admiration
inspiring awe or admiration

Examples

The museum's new exhibit is absolutely amazing and should not be missed.
We had an amazing time traveling through Japan last summer.
Her performance in the play was truly amazing and impressed the entire audience.

Synset Examples

New York is an amazing city
the Grand Canyon is an awe-inspiring sight
the awesome complexity of the universe
this sea, whose gently awful stirrings seem to speak of some hidden soul beneath
Westminster Hall's awing majesty, so vast, so high, so silent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astonishing - causing a feeling of great surprise or wonder
  • Antonym: unimpressive - not evoking admiration or wonder
  • Synonym: awesome - inspiring awe or admiration

Etymology

From amaze + -ing. 'Amaze' from Middle English 'amasen', from Old English 'amasian' (to confound, stupefy).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01285602-s
Synset POS: s
