impeccability

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˌpɛkəˈbɪlədi / (AmE), / ɪmˌpɛkəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality of being flawless or free from error or fault
flawlessness; absence of error

Examples

The impeccability of her research earned her international acclaim.
No one doubted the impeccability of his character.
Their work is a model of impeccability in both accuracy and style.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perfection - the state or quality of being perfect
  • Antonym: fallibility - the tendency to make mistakes or be wrong

Etymology

From impeccable + -ity, based on Latin impeccabilis.

2. NOUN

/ ɪmˌpɛkəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪmˌpɛkəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality of being incapable of sinning or being exempt from sin; perfect moral integrity or faultlessness, especially in a religious context
incapability of sinning or moral faultlessness

Examples

The doctrine of Christ's impeccability has been debated by theologians for centuries.
Her reputation for moral impeccability earned her the respect of everyone in the community.
Some claim the leader's public image projects an air of impeccability that few can actually live up to.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sinlessness - the state of being without sin
  • Antonym: fallibility - the tendency to make errors; capability of being wrong or sinful

Etymology

From Latin impeccābilis (not liable to sin), from in- (not) + peccare (to sin) + -ity (quality).
