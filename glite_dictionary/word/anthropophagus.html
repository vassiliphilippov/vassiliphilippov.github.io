anthropophagus

1. NOUN

/ ænˌθrɑːpəˈfeɪɡəs / (AmE), / ænˌθrɒpəˈfeɪɡəs / (BrE)
a person who eats human flesh; a cannibal
person who eats human flesh

Examples

In ancient myths, the land was said to be inhabited by a fearsome anthropophagus.
Explorers once feared encounters with an anthropophagus tribe in remote regions.
Stories of the anthropophagus chilled audiences in Elizabethan theatre.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cannibal - a person who eats the flesh of other humans

Etymology

From Greek anthrōpophagos, from anthrōpos (human) + -phagos (eater).
