anti-establishment

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.ti.ɪˈstæb.lɪʃ.mənt / (AmE), / ˌæn.ti.ɪˈstæb.lɪʃ.mənt / (BrE)
Opposed to or hostile toward conventional social, political, or economic institutions or authority.
opposed to the established order or authority

Examples

She became known for her anti-establishment views, often criticizing government policies in her speeches.
An anti-establishment movement gained momentum among young voters leading up to the election.
His music and lyrics reflect a strongly anti-establishment attitude, challenging traditional norms.

Synset Examples

Young and new faces emerged in the new council members, as veteran politicians stepped down in both the anti-establishment and pro-Beijing campus.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: establishment - the dominant group or accepted authority in a society
  • Synonym: countercultural - opposed to or rejecting the dominant culture or values

Etymology

Formed from 'anti-' (against) and 'establishment' (the dominant group or authority).
