Flavius Josephus

1. PROPN

/ ˈfleɪviəs dʒoʊˈsiːfəs / (AmE), / ˈfleɪvɪəs dʒəʊˈsiːfəs / (BrE)
(37–c. 100), Jewish historian and military leader who took part in the Jewish revolt against Rome and authored influential works on Jewish history.
Jewish historian and leader (37–c. 100)

Examples

Flavius Josephus is often cited by scholars studying the history of the Second Temple period.
The writings of Flavius Josephus provide insight into Jewish life during Roman rule.
Many historians rely on the accounts of Flavius Josephus for information about the Jewish revolt.

Etymology

Latinized name from Roman gens Flavia and Greek Ιωσήπος (Iōsēpos), his adopted Roman name.
