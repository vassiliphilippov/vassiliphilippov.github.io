to sensitize

1. VERB

/ ˈsɛn.sɪ.taɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsɪtaɪz / (BrE)
to make a material sensitive to light, usually of a particular color, by coating it with a photographic emulsion
make sensitive to light (photography)

Examples

Before use, you need to sensitize the film so that it can capture images in low light.
The technician sensitized the photographic plates with a special emulsion to increase their responsiveness to blue light.
To create color prints, the paper must be sensitized to specific wavelengths of light.

Synset Examples

sensitize the photographic film

Etymology

From French sensibiliser, ultimately from Latin sensibilis (perceptible by the senses).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00574748-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈsɛnsɪˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsɪtaɪz / (BrE)
to make someone or something sensitive or aware of something, especially to an issue, effect, or stimulus
make sensitive or aware

Examples

The campaign aims to sensitize people to the importance of recycling.
Repeated exposure to allergens can sensitize the immune system.
Teachers often try to sensitize students to cultural differences.

Synset Examples

He was not sensitized to her emotional needs

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to desensitize - make less sensitive or less aware
  • Synonym: alert - to make someone aware or warn about something

Etymology

From French 'sensibiliser', from 'sensible', ultimately from Latin 'sensibilis' (sensitive).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00273190-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈsɛn.sə.taɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.sɪ.taɪz / (BrE)
to cause (someone or something) to become sensitive or responsive, especially to a drug, allergen, or other substance
make sensitive to a substance
medical

Examples

Certain chemicals can sensitize the skin to sunlight, causing irritation.
Repeated contact with latex may sensitize some people, leading to allergic reactions.
Doctors warn that overuse of antibiotics may sensitize patients to drug allergies.

Synset Examples

Long-term exposure to this medicine may sensitize you to the allergen

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to desensitize - to make less sensitive or responsive to a substance

Etymology

From French sensibiliser, from sensible (sensitive).

4. VERB

/ ˈsɛn.səˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsɪtaɪz / (BrE)
to make someone or something sensitive to a particular drug, allergen, or other agent, usually so that a reaction will occur upon further exposure
make sensitive to a drug or allergen
medical

Examples

Frequent exposure to certain chemicals can sensitize the skin, making it react more strongly over time.
Long-term use of this antibiotic may sensitize some patients to its effects.
Certain vaccines are designed to sensitize the immune system to specific pathogens.

Synset Examples

Long-term exposure to this medicine may sensitize you to the allergen

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to desensitize - to make less sensitive, especially to a drug or allergen

Etymology

From French *sensitiser*, from Latin *sensibilis* (sensitive).

5. VERB

/ ˈsɛn.sɪˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsɪtaɪz / (BrE)
to make someone or something sensitive to something, or more aware of it; to cause a heightened response to a stimulus
make sensitive; cause awareness

Examples

The graphic images sensitized the public to the dangers of smoking.
Teachers hope to sensitize children to the importance of cultural diversity.
Repeated exposure to the allergen can sensitize the immune system.

Synset Examples

My tongue became sensitized to good wine

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to desensitize - to make less sensitive or less aware
  • Synonym: alert - to make someone aware of something

Etymology

From French 'sensitiser,' from 'sensible' (sensitive); based on 'sensitive.'
