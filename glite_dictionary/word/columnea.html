columnea

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈlʌmniə / (AmE), / kəˈlʌmniə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical plants with thick, hairy, toothed leaves and showy yellow to scarlet flowers, often grown for their ornamental value.
genus of tropical flowering plants

Examples

The columnea is prized for its vibrant flowers that brighten up any conservatory.
Many gardeners collect different columnea varieties for their uniquely shaped foliage and blooms.
A hanging basket of columnea can make a stunning centerpiece in indoor gardens.

Etymology

From Latin 'columnea', after Fabio Colonna (Latinized as Columna), an Italian botanist.
