blackout

1. NOUN

/ ˈblækaʊt / (AmE), / ˈblækaʊt / (BrE)
A period of darkness caused by the deliberate extinction of lights, especially in a city, to prevent it from being seen by enemy aircraft during wartime.
period of total darkness (for safety)

Examples

During World War II, strict blackout rules were enforced in the city.
The blackout made it impossible for enemy bombers to find their targets.
Residents covered their windows to comply with the nightly blackout orders.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: brownout - a reduction in or restriction of electrical power

2. NOUN

/ ˈblækaʊt / (AmE), / ˈblækaʊt / (BrE)
a period when the electrical power supply to a particular area is interrupted or cut off
electrical power failure in an area

Examples

We experienced a blackout in the middle of the storm last night.
The sudden blackout left the entire city in darkness for hours.
Businesses suffered losses due to the unexpected blackout yesterday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: power outage - interruption in electric power supply

Etymology

From 'black' + 'out'; originally referring to blackout curtains during air raids in World War II, later extended to power failures.

3. NOUN

/ ˈblækaʊt / (AmE), / ˈblækaʊt / (BrE)
a temporary loss of consciousness or memory, often caused by a medical condition or extreme stress
temporary loss of consciousness

Examples

She suffered a blackout right after the accident and didn’t remember what happened.
After standing up too quickly, he experienced a brief blackout and had to sit down immediately.
The stress caused several blackouts during his college exams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fainting - loss of consciousness for a short time because of temporarily insufficient blood flow to the brain

5. NOUN

/ ˈblækaʊt / (AmE), / ˈblækaʊt / (BrE)
a temporary suspension of radio or television broadcasting, often for security or legal reasons
temporary halt of broadcasting

Examples

The government declared a blackout on all news about the incident until the investigation was complete.
Due to technical problems, there was a brief blackout of the television signal last night.
During wartime, a radio blackout was imposed to prevent sensitive information from being broadcast.
