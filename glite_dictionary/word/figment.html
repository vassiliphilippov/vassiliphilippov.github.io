figment

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪɡ.mənt / (AmE), / ˈfɪɡ.mənt / (BrE)
Something invented, imagined, or made up, especially something unreal or fictitious.
something imagined or invented

Examples

Many believe the monster in the lake is just a figment of local legend and not real.
Her story about being chased by aliens was dismissed as a figment of her imagination.
The rumor was nothing more than a figment created to stir up excitement.

Synset Examples

a figment of the imagination

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fiction - prose literature involving imaginary events or people; something invented for entertainment or deception
  • Antonym: reality - the quality or state of being actual or true

Etymology

From Latin 'figmentum' (an invention, creation), from 'fingere' (to form, shape).
