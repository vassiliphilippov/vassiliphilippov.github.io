quartic

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɔrtɪk / (AmE), / ˈkwɔːtɪk / (BrE)
an algebraic equation or function of the fourth degree
fourth-degree equation or function

Examples

He solved the complicated quartic on his final math exam.
A general quartic can have up to four real roots.
Factoring a quartic is more challenging than solving a quadratic equation.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: quadratic - an equation of the second degree
  • Degree Lower: cubic - an equation of the third degree

Etymology

From Latin 'quartus' meaning 'fourth', referring to the degree of the equation.
