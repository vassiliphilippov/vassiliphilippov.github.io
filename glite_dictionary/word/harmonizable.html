harmonizable

1. ADJ

/ ˌhɑːrməˈnaɪzəbəl / (AmE), / ˌhɑːməˈnaɪzəbəl / (BrE)
able to be made harmonious or consistent; capable of being brought into agreement.
able to be made harmonious or consistent

Examples

The company found that its various systems were not immediately harmonizable without extensive changes.
These cultures have values that may not be easily harmonizable with our own.
Economic policies across regions must be harmonizable to ensure a stable currency union.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: harmonious - forming a pleasing or consistent whole
  • Antonym: discordant - not harmonious; conflicting

Etymology

From 'harmonize' + suffix '-able', meaning 'capable of'.
