Tanakh

1. NOUN

/ tɑˈnɑx / (AmE), / tɑˈnɑx / (BrE)
the canonical collection of Jewish scriptures, comprising the Torah, the Prophets, and the Writings
the Hebrew Bible; Jewish scriptures

Examples

The Tanakh is central to Jewish religious tradition and study.
Many scholars analyze the Tanakh to understand ancient Hebrew culture.
He read passages from the Tanakh during the ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Torah - the first five books of the Hebrew Bible
  • Synonym: Bible - the Christian scriptures, including the Old and New Testaments

Etymology

From Hebrew tənakh, an acronym formed from Torah (Law), Nevi'im (Prophets), and Ketuvim (Writings).
