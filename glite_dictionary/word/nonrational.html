nonrational

1. ADJ

/ nɑnˈræʃənəl / (AmE), / nɒnˈræʃənəl / (BrE)
not based on or guided by reason; lacking a rational basis
not based on reason

Examples

Much of human behavior can be described as nonrational rather than strictly logical.
The artist was interested in exploring nonrational aspects of the mind.
Superstitions often stem from nonrational beliefs and traditions.

Synset Examples

there is a great deal that is nonrational in modern culture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irrational - not logical or reasonable; contrary to reason

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'non-' (not) and 'rational', from Latin rationalis (of reason).

2. ADJ

/ nɑnˈræʃənəl / (AmE), / nɒnˈræʃənəl / (BrE)
Not based on reasoning, logic, or observation; obtained through intuition or other means beyond rational thought.
not based on reasoning or logic

Examples

Some artists rely on nonrational inspiration to create their work.
His decision seemed nonrational, guided more by instinct than by logic.
Many cultural rituals are rooted in nonrational beliefs and traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irrational - not logical or reasonable; lacking reason
  • Antonym: rational - based on logic or reason

Etymology

From prefix non- (not) + rational, from Latin rationalis (of reason).
