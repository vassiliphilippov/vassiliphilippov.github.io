breadstuff

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɛdˌstʌf / (AmE), / ˈbrɛdˌstʌf / (BrE)
flour, meal, or grain used in making bread
ingredient for breadmaking

Examples

During the war, the supply of breadstuff became severely limited.
Wheat is the most common type of breadstuff in many cultures.
The farmer sold his breadstuff at the local market.

Etymology

Formed from 'bread' and 'stuff' to mean material for bread.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbrɛd.stʌf / (AmE), / ˈbrɛd.stʌf / (BrE)
A food prepared by baking dough made from flour or meal, usually leavened.
food made from baked dough

Examples

The bakery displays a wide variety of breadstuff every morning.
Wheat is a common grain used in the production of breadstuff.
War shortages led to rationing of breadstuff in many cities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bread - A staple food made from baked dough of flour or meal, usually leavened.

Etymology

Formed from 'bread' + 'stuff', meaning things made from bread.
