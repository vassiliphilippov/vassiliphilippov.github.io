Mormon

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔːrmən / (AmE), / ˈmɔːmən / (BrE)
a member of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints, a Christian religious movement founded in the United States in the 19th century
member of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints

Examples

Sarah was raised as a Mormon in Utah.
The Mormon community is known for its commitment to family values.
Many Mormons serve as missionaries around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Latter-Day Saint - a member or adherent of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints

Etymology

From 'The Book of Mormon', a sacred text of the Latter-Day Saint movement, published by Joseph Smith in 1830.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10268239-n
Synset POS: n

2. PROPN

/ ˈmɔr.mən / (AmE), / ˈmɔː.mən / (BrE)
(in the beliefs of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints) The ancient prophet whose writings form part of the Book of Mormon and were revealed to Joseph Smith.
ancient prophet in Latter-Day Saint belief

Examples

According to Latter-Day Saint scriptures, Mormon compiled the records of his people into what became the Book of Mormon.
The Church teaches that Mormon was a prophet and historian who lived in the ancient Americas.
Many missionaries explain the story of Mormon when introducing their faith to others.

Etymology

From the name Mormon as used in the Book of Mormon, written by Joseph Smith in the 19th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-11211465-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈmɔːrmən / (AmE), / ˈmɔːmən / (BrE)
relating to the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints or its members
of or relating to the Mormon Church

Examples

The Mormon faith emphasizes family and community values.
She attended a Mormon wedding in Salt Lake City last summer.
Many Mormon missionaries travel abroad to share their beliefs.

Synset Examples

Mormon leaders
the former Mormon practice of polygamy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Latter-day Saint - member of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints

Etymology

From the name Mormon, a prophet in the Book of Mormon, central to the Latter-day Saints faith.
