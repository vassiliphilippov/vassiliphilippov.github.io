accessory

1. NOUN

/ əkˈsɛsəri / (AmE), / əkˈsɛsəri / (BrE)
a person who helps another to commit a crime without directly taking part in it
person aiding a crime
legal

Examples

He was arrested as an accessory in the robbery case.
The court found her guilty of being an accessory to the theft.
If you hide a fugitive, you could be charged as an accessory.

Synset Examples

he was charged with being accessory to the crime

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accomplice - a person who helps another commit a crime

Etymology

From late Latin accessorius (additional), from Latin accessus (approach).

2. NOUN

/ əkˈsɛsəri / (AmE), / əkˈsɛsəri / (BrE)
a person who helps another to commit a crime but does not take part directly
person aiding a crime
legal

Examples

The police arrested her as an accessory to the robbery.
He was charged as an accessory after the fact for hiding the stolen goods.
Without the accessory's assistance, the crime might have failed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accomplice - a person who actively participates in the commission of a crime

Etymology

From Latin accessorius (additional, assisting), from accedere (to approach).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09779206-n

3. NOUN

/ əkˈsɛsəri / (AmE), / əkˈsɛsəri / (BrE)
An item, such as a piece of jewelry, scarf, or bag, that is worn or carried to complement or enhance an outfit.
item worn to complement clothing

Examples

She chose a gold necklace as the perfect accessory to her evening gown.
Hats can be both a practical and stylish accessory.
A colorful scarf is my favorite winter accessory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adornment - something that decorates or adds beauty, especially in clothing

Etymology

From Medieval Latin accessorius (additional), from Latin accessus (approach, access).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02674410-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ əkˈsɛsəri / (AmE), / əkˈsɛsəri / (BrE)
A supplementary item or component that improves the usefulness, convenience, or appearance of something.
supplementary component or item

Examples

She bought a phone accessory to protect and decorate her device.
The kitchen mixer came with several useful accessories for different cooking tasks.
This camera accessory lets you take better pictures at night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attachment - an extra part added to equipment for a particular function
  • Antonym: essential - something that is absolutely necessary

Etymology

From Latin 'accessorius' (additional), from 'accedere' (to approach, to enter).

providing additional support or help, but not essential; supplementary.
supplementary or additional support

Examples
