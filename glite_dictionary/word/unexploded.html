unexploded

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnɪkˈsploʊdɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪkˈspləʊdɪd / (BrE)
(of a bomb, shell, or similar explosive device) remaining without having exploded.
not yet exploded (of bomb or shell)

Examples

The children found an unexploded shell buried in the garden.
Authorities evacuated the area after discovering an unexploded bomb.
During construction, workers uncovered several unexploded grenades from the war.

Synset Examples

undischarged ammunition
an unexploded bomb

Related Terms

  • Antonym: exploded - having already burst or blown up

Etymology

From prefix un- (not) + past participle of explode.
