chatterer

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃætərər / (AmE), / ˈtʃætərə / (BrE)
A passerine bird found in the tropical regions of the New World, known for its noisy calls; member of the family Cotingidae or related families.
tropical New World songbird

Examples

The rainforests of South America are home to many species of chatterer with striking plumage.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot a rare chatterer during their expedition to the Amazon.
The distinctive calls of the chatterer echoed through the dense tropical canopy.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæt̬.ɚ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈtʃæt.ər.ər / (BrE)
a person who talks a lot, especially in a trivial or irritating way
a person who chats incessantly

Examples

The meeting was delayed because one chatterer wouldn't stop interrupting everyone.
Even among friends, a constant chatterer can become quite tiresome.
Every class seems to have at least one chatterer who distracts the others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chatterbox - a person who talks a lot, often in a lively or persistent way
  • Synonym: babble - to talk rapidly and continuously in a foolish way
  • Antonym: listener - a person who listens rather than speaks

Etymology

From 'chatter' (to talk rapidly or incessantly), with agent suffix '-er'.
