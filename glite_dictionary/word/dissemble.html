to dissemble

1. VERB

/ dɪˈsɛm.bəl / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛm.bəl / (BrE)
to conceal one's true motives, feelings, or beliefs; to pretend or act falsely, often with the intent to deceive
conceal true motives or feelings; pretend

Examples

He tried to dissemble his true intentions behind a smile.
Politicians sometimes dissemble to avoid answering difficult questions directly.
She was skilled at dissembling her emotions, even in stressful situations.

Synset Examples

He feigned that he was ill
He shammed a headache

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretend - to behave as if something is true when it is not
  • Synonym: to feign - to give a false appearance or impression
  • Antonym: to reveal - to show or make known something that was hidden

Etymology

From Latin 'dissimulo', meaning 'to disguise, conceal'.

2. VERB

/ dɪˈsɛmbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛmbəl / (BrE)
to behave in an insincere or affected manner, hiding one's true feelings or motives
to hide true feelings; act insincerely

Examples

He tried to dissemble his disappointment, but his expression gave him away.
Politicians sometimes dissemble to avoid answering difficult questions directly.
Despite her calm demeanor, she was unable to dissemble her anger for long.

Synset Examples

She's just acting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretend - to act as if something is true when it is not
  • Synonym: act - to behave in a way that is not genuine
  • Antonym: to reveal - to make something known that was hidden

Etymology

From Old French *dissembler*, from Latin *dissimulare* (to disguise, conceal).

3. VERB

/ dɪˈsɛm.bəl / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛm.bəl / (BrE)
to conceal one's true motives, feelings, or beliefs; to hide under a false appearance
to conceal true feelings or motives

Examples

She tried to dissemble her nervousness during the interview but her hands trembled slightly.
It is hard to dissemble genuine excitement when you get such good news.
Politicians sometimes dissemble their true intentions to win public approval.

Synset Examples

He masked his disappointment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretend - act so as to appear to be something that is not the case
  • Synonym: mask - to conceal feelings or truth by putting on a false appearance
  • Antonym: to reveal - to make something known or visible

Etymology

From Latin dissimulāre (to disguise, conceal).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02152033-v
Synset POS: v
