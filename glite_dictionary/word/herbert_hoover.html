Herbert Hoover

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɝːbɚt ˈhuːvɚ / (AmE), / ˈhɜːbət ˈhuːvə / (BrE)
(1874–1964) The 31st President of the United States (1929–1933), whose presidency was marked by the onset of the Great Depression.
31st U.S. President, 1929–1933

Examples

Herbert Hoover was President when the stock market crashed in 1929.
Many historians debate the policies implemented by Herbert Hoover during the Great Depression.
The Hoover Dam was named after Herbert Hoover in recognition of his contributions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Franklin Roosevelt - 32nd President of the United States; Hoover's successor
