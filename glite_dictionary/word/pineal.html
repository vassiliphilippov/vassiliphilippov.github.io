pineal

1. ADJ

/ ˈpaɪniəl / (AmE), / ˈpɪniəl / (BrE)
Having the shape or form of a pine cone.
shaped like a pine cone

Examples

The pineal gland is so named because of its shape resembling a pine cone.
Certain sea urchins have a pineal structure on their outer shells.
The botanist described the rare plant’s fruit as pineal in appearance.

Etymology

From Latin 'pinealis', from 'pinus' (pine tree).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpaɪniəl / (AmE), / ˈpɪniəl / (BrE)
relating to the pineal gland, a small endocrine gland in the brain that produces melatonin.
relating to the pineal gland
medical

Examples

The pineal gland is responsible for producing melatonin.
Doctors studied the pineal region of the brain to understand its functions.
A pineal tumor can affect sleep patterns due to hormone disruption.

Synset Examples

pineal hormone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pineal gland - small endocrine gland in the brain producing melatonin

Etymology

From Latin 'pinea' (pine cone), from the shape of the gland.
