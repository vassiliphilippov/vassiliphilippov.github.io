squatting

1. NOUN

/ ˈskwɑtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskwɒtɪŋ / (BrE)
an exercise in which a person repeatedly bends their knees to lower their body into a crouching position and then stands up again, typically to strengthen the leg muscles
a leg-strengthening exercise

Examples

He incorporates squatting into his fitness routine to build stronger thighs.
The trainer demonstrated proper squatting technique to avoid knee injuries.
Many athletes use weighted squatting for increased strength and power.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squat - to exercise by bending the knees and lowering the body

2. NOUN

/ ˈskwɑːtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskwɒtɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of assuming or maintaining a crouching position with the knees bent and the buttocks close to the heels.
crouching with knees bent near heels

Examples

He injured his knees from too much squatting during weightlifting practice.
Extended squatting can be difficult if you are not used to it.
Farmers use squatting while working in the fields to stay close to the crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crouching - the act of bending your knees and bringing your body close to the ground

Etymology

From 'squat' + '-ing'; 'squat' from Old French 'esquatir' (to press down), from Vulgar Latin *excoactare.
