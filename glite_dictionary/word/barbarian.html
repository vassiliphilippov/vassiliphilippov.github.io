barbarian

1. NOUN

/ bɑrˈbɛriən / (AmE), / bɑːˈbɛəriən / (BrE)
A person who is crude, uncouth, or lacking in culture or refinement.
uncultured, crude person

Examples

He called his roommate a barbarian for eating with his hands at the formal dinner.
Only a barbarian would ignore such beautiful works of art.
Her friends joked that she was a barbarian because she had never been to a museum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: philistine - a person who is hostile or indifferent to culture and the arts

Etymology

From Latin barbarus (foreign, barbaric), from Greek barbaros (foreign, non-Greek)

2. NOUN

/ bɑrˈbɛriən / (AmE), / bɑːˈbɛːriən / (BrE)
a member of a community or tribe not belonging to one of the great civilizations; historically, a person regarded as uncivilized, primitive, or culturally inferior
a person regarded as uncivilized or primitive

Examples

The Romans considered anyone outside their empire to be a barbarian.
Some historians argue that the label barbarian was used to justify conquest and colonization.
In popular culture, a barbarian often appears as a fierce, sword-wielding warrior.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: civilized - marked by well-organized laws and rules, and considered more advanced culturally

Etymology

From Latin *barbarus* (foreigner, uncivilized person), from Greek *barbaros* (foreigner, one who babbles).

3. ADJ

/ bɑːrˈbɛriən / (AmE), / bɑːˈbɛːriən / (BrE)
(of people, customs, or behavior) uncivilized, lacking refinement or civilized influences.
uncivilized; lacking refinement

Examples

The barbarian tribes showed little respect for the traditions of the conquered city.
Some regarded the unfamiliar customs as barbarian and refused to accept them.
Centuries ago, Roman writers often described their enemies as barbarian peoples.

Synset Examples

barbarian invaders
barbaric practices
a savage people
fighting is crude and uncivilized especially if the weapons are efficient
wild tribes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: civilized - marked by refinement and good manners
  • Synonym: barbaric - savagely cruel; exceedingly brutal; uncivilized
  • Synonym: savage - (of a person or action) fierce, violent, and uncontrolled; uncivilized

Etymology

From Latin barbarus, from Greek barbaros (foreign, uncivilized).
