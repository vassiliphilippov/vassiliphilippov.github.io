transferrable

1. ADJ

/ trænˈsfɜːrəbəl / (AmE), / trɑːnsˈfɜːrəbl / (BrE)
able to be legally or officially transferred to the ownership or possession of another person or entity.
able to be legally transferred to another

Examples

Tickets to the event are transferrable provided you inform the organizers in advance.
Ownership of the property is transferrable upon completion of all legal requirements.
Some airline miles are not transferrable to another person.

Synset Examples

negotiable bonds

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nontransferable - not able to be transferred to another person or entity
  • Synonym: negotiable - able to be transferred or assigned, especially in finance

Etymology

From the verb 'transfer' (from Latin transferre), with the suffix '-able' indicating capability.

2. ADJ

/ trænsˈfɝːəbəl / (AmE), / trænsˈfɜːrəbl / (BrE)
able to be transferred or moved from one place, person, or situation to another.
able to be transferred or moved

Examples

The airline ticket is transferrable, so you may give it to someone else if you can't travel.
Ownership of the software license is transferrable under certain conditions.
Many skills learned in this job are transferrable to other industries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transferable - also able to be transferred or moved; more common variant spelling
  • Antonym: nontransferable - not able to be transferred or moved

Etymology

From Latin 'transferre' (to carry across) via English 'transfer' + '-able'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01527843-s
Synset POS: s
