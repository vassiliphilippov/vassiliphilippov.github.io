cysteine

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪstiˌin / (AmE), / ˈsɪstiːiːn / (BrE)
an amino acid containing sulfur that is present in many proteins and can oxidize to form cystine
a sulfur-containing amino acid
medical

Examples

The body uses cysteine to produce important antioxidants.
A diet rich in cysteine can support healthy skin and hair.
On exposure to air, cysteine readily oxidizes to form cystine.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: cystine - an amino acid formed by the oxidation of two cysteine molecules

Etymology

From Greek kystis (bladder) + -eine, modeled after chemical names such as 'guanine'.
