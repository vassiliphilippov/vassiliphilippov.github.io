coextensive

1. ADJ

/ ˌkoʊ.ɪkˈstɛn.sɪv / (AmE), / ˌkəʊ.ɪkˈstɛn.sɪv / (BrE)
having the same extent, scope, or duration as something else; exactly coinciding in range or coverage
having the same extent or scope

Examples

The boundaries of the two districts are coextensive, creating a seamless administrative region.
Their partnership was coextensive with the entire launch phase of the project.
The rights are coextensive with the period specified in the contract.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coterminous - having the same boundaries or extent in space, time, or meaning

Etymology

From Latin 'co-' (together) + 'extensivus' (capable of extension).
