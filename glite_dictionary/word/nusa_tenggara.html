Nusa Tenggara

1. PROPN

/ ˌnuːsə tɛnˈgɑːrə / (AmE), / ˌnuːsə tɛnˈɡɑːrə / (BrE)
a chain of islands and a region in Indonesia, lying east of Java, which includes Bali, Lombok, Sumbawa, Flores, Sumba, and Timor
island region of Indonesia east of Java

Examples

Tourists are increasingly drawn to Nusa Tenggara for its beautiful beaches and diverse culture.
The island of Flores, located in Nusa Tenggara, is famous for the Komodo dragons.
Bali is part of the Nusa Tenggara island chain in Indonesia.

Etymology

From Indonesian, literally 'Southeast Islands' (nusa 'island' + tenggara 'southeast').
