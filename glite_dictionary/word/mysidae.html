Mysidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈmɪsɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, shrimp-like crustaceans found in marine and fresh waters.
family of small shrimp-like crustaceans

Examples

Scientists collected several Mysidae species from the brackish waters of the estuary.
The diet of some fish includes a variety of Mysidae found in freshwater lakes.
Members of the Mysidae family play an important role in aquatic food webs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mysis - a genus of small shrimp-like crustaceans

Etymology

From New Latin Mysidae, based on the genus Mysis + -idae (standard zoological suffix for animal families).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈmɪsɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, shrimp-like crustaceans found mostly in marine and brackish waters.
family of small shrimp-like crustaceans

Examples

The Mysidae are an important food source for many fish species.
Scientists discovered a new member of the Mysidae in coastal waters.
Several species within the Mysidae family inhabit both fresh and brackish environments.

Etymology

From Greek 'mysid', name of the type genus *Mysis*, with Latin taxonomic suffix -idae.
