offset

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑfˌsɛt / (AmE), / ˈɒf.sɛt / (BrE)
a consequence or result arising naturally from the development of something.
resulting consequence of development

Examples

The creation of new industries is an offset of technological progress.
Increased pollution may be an unavoidable offset of rapid urban development.
Urban sprawl is often an offset of economic growth in large cities.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔfˌsɛt / (AmE), / ˈɒfˌsɛt / (BrE)

ledge where wall narrows abruptly

Examples

The architect designed an offset to provide visual interest along the exterior wall.
At the offset, the thickness of the wall decreases to support the upper floors.
Restoration revealed an original offset buried beneath later additions to the building.

3. VERB

/ ˈɑfˌsɛt / (AmE), / ˈɒfˌsɛt / (BrE)
(of printed matter) to transfer or smear ink onto another surface unintentionally.
transfer ink to another surface (printing)

Examples

If wet ink is not allowed to dry properly, it can offset onto the next page.
Be careful when stacking these brochures, as the images might offset and leave marks.
The printer used special drying techniques to prevent the text from offsetting onto other sheets.
