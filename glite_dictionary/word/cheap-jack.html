cheap-jack

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃiːp ˌdʒæk / (AmE), / ˈtʃiːp ˌdʒæk / (BrE)
a person who sells inferior or shoddy goods, often in a loud or showy manner, typically at markets or public places
seller of inferior goods
archaic

Examples

The fair was full of cheap-jack stalls selling everything from toys to fake jewelry.
He earned a reputation as a cheap-jack peddler, hawking goods of questionable quality.
The cheap-jack's loud sales pitch drew a skeptical crowd.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: huckster - a person who sells small items, often in a flashy manner

Etymology

Probably from the use of 'Jack' as a generic name, implying an unscrupulous or common man, combined with 'cheap'.
