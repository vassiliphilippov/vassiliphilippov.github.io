Leitneria floridana

1. NOUN

/ laɪtˈnɪriə flɔːˈrɪdənə / (AmE), / laɪtˈnɪəriə flɒˈrɪdənə / (BrE)
A very small deciduous tree or shrub native to damp habitats in the southeastern United States, known for its extremely light wood.
a small southeastern US tree or shrub with light wood

Examples

The wood of Leitneria floridana is so light it can float on water easily.
Botanists discovered a rare stand of Leitneria floridana in the swampy area.
Leitneria floridana is sometimes cultivated for ornamental planting in wetlands.

Etymology

Genus named after Austrian botanist E. F. Leitner; species epithet refers to Florida.
