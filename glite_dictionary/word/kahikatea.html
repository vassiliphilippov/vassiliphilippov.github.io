kahikatea

1. NOUN

/ kaˈhikəˌtiːə / (AmE), / kaˌhɪkəˈtiːə / (BrE)
A tall New Zealand evergreen tree (Dacrycarpus dacrydioides) valued for its light, easily worked wood.
New Zealand evergreen tree

Examples

The ancient kahikatea towers high above the surrounding forest canopy.
Furniture makers prize kahikatea for its durable and lightweight timber.
Many birds nest in the branches of the kahikatea during spring.

Etymology

From Māori 'kahikatea', the common name for the species.
