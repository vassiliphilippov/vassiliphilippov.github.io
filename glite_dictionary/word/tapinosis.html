tapinosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌtæpɪˈnoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌtæpɪˈnəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A rhetorical figure of speech in which something is deliberately described as less important or serious than it actually is.
figure of speech minimizing importance

Examples

The politician's use of tapinosis downplayed the significance of the economic crisis.
Literary critics often identify tapinosis in works that use understatement for effect.
Her speech employed tapinosis by referring to a catastrophic event as 'a slight inconvenience.'

Synset Examples

The last and perhaps the most important of the ridiculing figures according to these writers is tapinosis.

Etymology

From Greek *tapínōsis* (humbling, making low), from *tapinoō* (to humble).
