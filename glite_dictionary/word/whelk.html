whelk

1. NOUN

/ wɛlk / (AmE), / wɛlk / (BrE)
a large, carnivorous marine snail found in coastal waters, with a strong spiral shell
large marine snail with spiral shell

Examples

The fisherman collected several whelks from the rocky tidal pools.
Many chefs prize the tender meat of the whelk in seafood dishes.
A broken whelk shell washed ashore after the storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whelk - a pimple or pustule (archaic meaning)

Etymology

From Middle English "welke", from Old English "weoloc" or "wiloc" (a kind of mollusc).

2. NOUN

/ wɛlk / (AmE), / wɛlk / (BrE)
a large, edible marine snail with a spiral shell, common in European waters and often used as food
large edible marine snail

Examples

We collected several whelks from the rocky shoreline for our dinner.
In France, whelk is often served cold with garlic mayonnaise.
Fishermen harvest whelks using pots set on the sea floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conch - a marine mollusc with a spiral shell, sometimes used for food or ornament

Etymology

From Middle English welk, from Old English weoloc (snail shell), related to Dutch welk and Old High German welk (gastropod).
