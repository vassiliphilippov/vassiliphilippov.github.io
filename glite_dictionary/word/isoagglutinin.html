isoagglutinin

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪsoʊəˈɡluːtənɪn / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəʊəˈɡluːtɪnɪn / (BrE)
an antibody produced by one individual that causes agglutination of red blood cells in other individuals of the same species
antibody that agglutinates same-species red blood cells
medical

Examples

The presence of isoagglutinin in the blood can cause transfusion reactions if not properly matched.
Researchers studied the effect of isoagglutinin antibodies on blood compatibility.
Detection of isoagglutinin is a key step in determining safe blood donors.

Etymology

From iso- (equal, same) + agglutinin (from Latin agglutinare, to glue to).
