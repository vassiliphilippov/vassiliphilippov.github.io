splutter

1. NOUN

/ ˈsplʌtər / (AmE), / ˈsplʌtə / (BrE)
A series of short, explosive, spattering noises, typically made by something malfunctioning, choking, or struggling to work.
explosive, spattering noise

Examples

The engine gave a loud splutter before coming to a stop.
There was a splutter of laughter from the back of the room.
We heard the splutter of rain against the metal roof.

Synset Examples

he heard a spatter of gunfire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sputter - A series of soft explosive or spitting sounds (noun)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07409414-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈsplʌtɚ / (AmE), / ˈsplʌtə / (BrE)
an utterance produced with spitting or popping sounds, especially when speaking angrily or in a flustered way
spitting or popping-sounding utterance

Examples

His angry splutter made everyone in the room fall silent.
With a splutter of indignation, she tried to defend herself.
The teacher was met with a confused splutter from the back of the class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stammer - speak with involuntary pauses or repetitions
  • Synonym: spit - to eject saliva forcibly from one's mouth

Etymology

Imitative of the sound produced; related to 'splutter' (verb), probably of imitative origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07144891-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈsplʌtɚ / (AmE), / ˈsplʌtə / (BrE)
to speak or utter something in a rushed or hasty way, often with spitting sounds, especially due to anger or confusion
speak hastily with spitting sounds

Examples

"I... I never said that!" he spluttered in protest.
She spluttered with anger when accused of lying.
The teacher spluttered out instructions amidst the chaos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stammer - to speak with sudden pauses and repeated sounds or words, often due to nervousness
  • Synonym: stutter - to speak with involuntary repetition of sounds, usually due to a speech disorder or nervousness
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00988929-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈsplʌt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈsplʌtə / (BrE)
To spit out or speak with short, explosive sounds, often due to strong emotion or while struggling to speak clearly.
to speak or spit with explosive sounds

Examples

He began to splutter angrily when accused of lying.
She spluttered her tea in surprise at the news.
The engine spluttered and stalled in the cold morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stammer - to speak with sudden involuntary pauses
  • Synonym: spit - to eject saliva forcefully from the mouth
  • Synonym: sputter - to make a series of soft explosive sounds

Etymology

Imitative; related to 'splutter' as an expressive formation from 'splutter' and 'splatter'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00102057-v
Synset POS: v
