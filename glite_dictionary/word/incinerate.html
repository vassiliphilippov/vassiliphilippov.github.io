to incinerate

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈsɪnəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɪnəreɪt / (BrE)
to burn something completely, especially until it is reduced to ashes
burn or reduce to ashes

Examples

The landfill operators incinerate large amounts of waste every week.
Some medical facilities incinerate hazardous materials to prevent contamination.
Old confidential documents are often incinerated for security reasons.

Synset Examples

burn garbage
The car burns only Diesel oil

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burn - to consume by fire
  • Antonym: to extinguish - to put out or cause to stop burning

Etymology

From Latin incinerare (to turn to ashes), from in- (into) + cinis (ashes).

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈsɪnəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɪnəreɪt / (BrE)
to become reduced to ashes, usually as a result of burning completely
become reduced to ashes by burning

Examples

The old letter incinerated within seconds in the fireplace.
After hours in the furnace, the metal scraps finally incinerated.
If you place dried leaves in a strong enough fire, they will quickly incinerate.

Synset Examples

The paper incinerated quickly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burn - to undergo combustion

Etymology

From Medieval Latin incinerare, from Latin cinis (ashes).
