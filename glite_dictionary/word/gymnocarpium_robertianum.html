Gymnocarpium robertianum

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪm.nəˈkɑːr.pi.əm ˌroʊ.bɚˈtiː.nəm / (AmE), / dʒɪm.nəˈkɑː.pi.əm ˌrəʊ.bəˈtiː.nəm / (BrE)
A small yellow-green fern commonly found in rocky areas of the Northern Hemisphere.
yellow-green fern of rocky regions

Examples

Gymnocarpium robertianum often grows in the crevices of limestone cliffs.
Botanists discovered a patch of Gymnocarpium robertianum near the riverbank.
The delicate leaves of Gymnocarpium robertianum set it apart from other ferns in the region.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Gymnocarpium' (a genus of ferns) and 'robertianum', often in reference to Saint Robert or the herb Robert.
