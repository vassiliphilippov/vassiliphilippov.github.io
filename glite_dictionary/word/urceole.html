urceole

1. NOUN

/ ˈɜːr.si.oʊl / (AmE), / ˈɜː.si.əʊl / (BrE)
a vessel used to hold water for washing the hands, especially in religious or ritual settings
ritual vessel for handwashing

Examples

The priest carefully poured water from the urceole during the ceremony.
An ornate urceole stood on the altar, filled with fresh water.
Ancient rituals often required an urceole for washing hands before prayer.

Etymology

From Latin urceolus, a diminutive of urceus (pitcher, jug).
