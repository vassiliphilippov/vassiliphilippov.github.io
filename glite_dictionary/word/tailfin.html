tailfin

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪlˌfɪn / (AmE), / ˈteɪl.fɪn / (BrE)
a vertical stabilizing fin located at the rear of an aircraft, forming part of the tail structure
vertical stabilizing fin on an aircraft's tail

Examples

The tailfin helps keep the airplane flying straight, especially in crosswinds.
After the accident, the engineers examined the damage to the tailfin.
Modern jetliners often feature a logo painted on their tailfin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vertical stabilizer - a fixed or adjustable stabilizing surface on the tail of an aircraft

Etymology

From 'tail' + 'fin', referring to a fin at the tail end of an object, especially an aircraft.

2. NOUN

/ ˈteɪlˌfɪn / (AmE), / ˈteɪl.fɪn / (BrE)
a thin, vertical projection at the rear of a car, especially as a decorative feature extending above and beyond the rear fenders, popular in the 1950s and 1960s.
decorative rear projection on a car

Examples

The classic Cadillac's tailfin design became an icon of American car culture.
Restoring the vintage car involved repairing its damaged tailfins.
Many automobile enthusiasts admire the dramatic tailfins on cars from the 1950s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fender - the part of a car over the wheel to protect against splashes

Etymology

Formed from 'tail' + 'fin', referring to the resemblance to a fish's fin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04391493-n
Synset POS: n
