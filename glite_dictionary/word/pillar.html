pillar

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈpɪlə / (BrE)
A tall, upright, cylindrical structure that stands alone and does not support anything, often serving as a monument or decorative feature.
upright cylindrical monument or structure

Examples

The ancient city square is marked by a single marble pillar that honors a forgotten king.
A stone pillar stood in the middle of the park, engraved with the town's founding date.
Tourists gathered around the tall bronze pillar to take photographs.

Etymology

From Latin 'pila' (pillar, column).

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈpɪlə / (BrE)
a tall, narrow structure or formation that resembles a column or tower in shape, especially one that is not architectural
something shaped like a column or tower

Examples

A thin pillar of smoke could be seen rising from the chimney.
A pillar of light shone through the clouds after the storm.
The tornado left a pillar of dust in its wake.

Synset Examples

the test tube held a column of white powder
a tower of dust rose above the horizon
a thin pillar of smoke betrayed their campsite

Related Terms

  • Synonym: column - a vertical structure shaped like a post or pillar
  • Synonym: tower - a tall, narrow structure, either natural or built

Etymology

From Latin pila (pillar, pier).

5. NOUN

/ ˈpɪlɚ / (AmE), / ˈpɪlə / (BrE)
a tall, vertical, usually cylindrical structure that is used to support a building or other construction
upright supporting column

Examples

The ancient temple was supported by massive stone pillars on all sides.
A marble pillar rose from the floor to the ornate ceiling of the hall.
They decorated each pillar with intricate carvings representing the city's history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: column - a vertical structural element, typically cylindrical, that supports weight

Etymology

From Latin pila (pillar, pier, column, stone barrier).
