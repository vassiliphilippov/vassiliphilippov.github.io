shtick

1. NOUN

/ ʃtɪk / (AmE), / ʃtɪk / (BrE)
A prank, piece of clowning, or a characteristic comic routine; something done for comedic effect.
comic bit or routine; prank
informal

Examples

Her comedy shtick always gets the audience laughing.
He relies on the same shtick in every one of his shows.
Their new shtick involves juggling and singing at the same time.

Synset Examples

his shtik made us laugh

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gag - a joke or a comic act
  • Synonym: routine - a regular or characteristic comedic act

Etymology

From Yiddish shtik (piece, trick), ultimately from German Stück (piece).

2. NOUN

/ ʃtɪk / (AmE), / ʃtɪk / (BrE)
a routine, gimmick, or comic act that is repeatedly used by a performer to attract attention
performer's recurring gimmick or routine

Examples

The comedian's signature shtick always gets a laugh from the audience.
He tried a new shtick during his act, but it didn't land as well as usual.
Her interview was full of the same old shtick she uses in every public appearance.

Synset Examples

play it straight with no shtik

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gimmick - a trick or device intended to attract attention
  • Synonym: routine - a regular or habitual performance, especially on stage

Etymology

From Yiddish שטיק (shtik), meaning 'piece' or 'thing', itself from German Stück.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00551582-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ʃtɪk / (AmE), / ʃtɪk / (BrE)
a devious trick or bit of cheating, especially one meant to deceive or mislead
devious trick; bit of cheating

Examples

I can't believe you almost fell for his shtick during the negotiations.
That whole lottery was just a shtick to get people to buy more tickets.
She pulled her usual shtick to get out of doing any work.

Synset Examples

how did you ever fall for a shtik like that?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trick - a cunning or skillful act intended to deceive or outwit someone
  • Synonym: ruse - an action intended to deceive; a trick

Etymology

From Yiddish *shtik* (piece, trick), ultimately from German *Stück* (piece).
