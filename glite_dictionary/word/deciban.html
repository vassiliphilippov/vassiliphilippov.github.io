deciban

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛsɪˌbæn / (AmE), / ˈdɛsɪˌbæn / (BrE)
a unit of information equal to one tenth of a ban, used to express weights of evidence or log-odds in information theory and statistics
unit of information (one tenth of a ban)

Examples

The scientist calculated the evidence for the hypothesis in decibans to compare competing models.
A difference of 10 decibans corresponds to approximately doubling the odds in Bayesian inference.
In statistical analysis, the weight of evidence is often measured in decibans for clarity and precision.

Synset Examples

The deciban is a particularly useful unit for log-odds, notably as a measure of information in Bayes factors, odds ratios (ratio of odds, so log is difference of log-odds), or weights of evidence.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: ban - a unit of information, used in Bayesian statistics

Etymology

Formed by analogy with 'decibel', from 'deci-' (ten) and 'ban' (information unit).
