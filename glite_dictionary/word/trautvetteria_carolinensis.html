Trautvetteria carolinensis

1. NOUN

/ traʊtˌvɛtəriə ˌkæroʊlɪˈnɛnsɪs / (AmE), / traʊtˌvɛtəriə ˌkarəlɪˈnɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A tall perennial plant native to the eastern United States, with large basal leaves and clusters of white flowers blooming in summer.
a tall perennial plant with white summer flowers

Examples

We spotted Trautvetteria carolinensis blooming along the shaded woodland trail in June.
The large leaves of Trautvetteria carolinensis make it easy to distinguish from other wildflowers.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate Trautvetteria carolinensis for its unique white summer flowers.

Etymology

Named after Russian botanist Ernst Rudolf von Trautvetter, with the species name referring to Carolina.
