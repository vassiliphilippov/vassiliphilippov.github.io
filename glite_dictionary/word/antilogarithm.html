antilogarithm

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.tiˈlɔ.ɡəˌrɪð.əm / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈlɒ.ɡə.rɪð.əm / (BrE)
The number for which a given number is the logarithm; the inverse operation of a logarithm.
the inverse of a logarithm

Examples

To solve the equation, you need to find the antilogarithm of each side.
Using tables, scientists could quickly determine the antilogarithm before calculators were common.
The antilogarithm of 2 is 100 when working with base 10.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: logarithm - the exponent by which a base must be raised to produce a given number

Etymology

From 'anti-' (opposite) and 'logarithm', first used in mathematics in the mid-19th century.
