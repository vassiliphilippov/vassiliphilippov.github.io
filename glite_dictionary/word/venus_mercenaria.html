Venus mercenaria

1. NOUN

/ ˈviːnəs mərˌsɛˈnɛəriə / (AmE), / ˈviːnəs məˌsɛnˈeərɪə / (BrE)
an edible American clam, also known as the hard clam or quahog; its heavy shells were historically used as money by some Native American tribes
an edible American clam species

Examples

Some Native American tribes used the shells of Venus mercenaria as wampum, a form of currency.
The Venus mercenaria is often found on the Atlantic coast of North America.
Chefs prize the tender meat of Venus mercenaria for use in chowders and other seafood dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quahog - common name for Venus mercenaria, an edible clam

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Venus' (goddess of love) and species name 'mercenaria' meaning 'of wages' or 'mercenary', referring to its use as money.
