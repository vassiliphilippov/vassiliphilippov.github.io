inanimacy

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈnænɪməsi / (AmE), / ɪˈnænɪməsi / (BrE)
(Linguistics) The grammatical property of being inanimate, as applied to nouns in some languages, affecting their inflection or syntactic behavior.
property of being grammatically inanimate
medical

Examples

In Russian, nouns are marked for inanimacy as part of their declension.
The inanimacy of the word 'rock' means it follows certain grammatical rules in Polish.
Researchers examine how inanimacy affects subject–verb agreement in different languages.
