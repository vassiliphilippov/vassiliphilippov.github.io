Melopsittacus undulatus

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛləpˌsɪtəkəs ˌʌndjəˈleɪtəs / (AmE), / ˌmɛləpˌsɪtəkəs ˌʌndjʊˈleɪtəs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the budgerigar, a small Australian parakeet commonly kept as a pet and noted for its bright colors and cheerful disposition
scientific name for budgerigar (parakeet)

Examples

Breeders of Melopsittacus undulatus have created a stunning array of feather colors over the years.
The Melopsittacus undulatus is native to Australia but can now be found as a pet worldwide.
Veterinarians often refer to the budgerigar by its scientific name, Melopsittacus undulatus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: budgerigar - a small Australian parakeet, also called a budgie

Etymology

From Greek 'melos' (song) + 'psittakos' (parrot), and Latin 'undulatus' (wavy, referring to markings).
