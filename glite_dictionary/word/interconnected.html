interconnected

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪntərkəˈnɛktɪd / (AmE), / ˌɪntəkəˈnɛktɪd / (BrE)
having all parts or things connected with each other so that each part affects or depends on the others
mutually connected

Examples

The internet is a global network of interconnected computers.
The two issues are deeply interconnected and cannot be solved separately.
These ecosystems are interconnected, so a change in one affects the other.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: connected - joined or linked together
  • Antonym: isolated - separate; not connected

Etymology

From inter- (between) + connected, past participle of connect.

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪntərkəˈnɛktɪd / (AmE), / ˌɪntəkəˈnɛktɪd / (BrE)
Connected with each other so that things operate together as a unit or are mutually dependent.
connected and operating as a unit

Examples

The new software allows all the devices to be interconnected for better efficiency.
Global economies have become increasingly interconnected over the past few decades.
The nervous system is a complex network of interconnected cells.

Synset Examples

a unified utility system
a coordinated program

Related Terms

  • Synonym: connected - joined or linked together
  • Antonym: disconnected - not connected or joined together

Etymology

From inter- (between) and connected (from Latin connectere, to bind together).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01331181-s
Synset POS: s
