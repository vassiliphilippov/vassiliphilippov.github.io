gayfeather

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡeɪˌfɛðɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪˌfɛðə / (BrE)
A North American plant of the genus Liatris, with tall spikes of small, fluffy, usually purple or white flowers.
tall North American flowering plant (Liatris genus)

Examples

The gayfeather brightened the prairie with its spikes of purple blooms each summer.
Gardeners often plant gayfeather to attract butterflies to their yards.
Among the wildflowers, the gayfeather stood out for its vibrant color and unique texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blazing star - another common name for plants of the genus Liatris

Etymology

Named for its showy, feathery flower spikes.
