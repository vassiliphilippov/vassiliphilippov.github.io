Ribes sanguineum

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪ.biːz sæŋˈɡwɪniəm / (AmE), / ˈraɪ.biːz sæŋˈɡwiːniəm / (BrE)
A species of flowering shrub, commonly known as the flowering currant or red-flowering currant, native to western North America and grown for its ornamental flowers.
a species of flowering shrub (flowering currant)

Examples

Ribes sanguineum is popular in gardens for its showy clusters of pink to red flowers in early spring.
The deep green leaves of Ribes sanguineum have a distinctive, currant-like aroma when crushed.
Gardeners often recommend Ribes sanguineum for attracting hummingbirds and bees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flowering currant - another common name; a shrub with ornamental pink to red flowers

Etymology

From Latin: 'Ribes' (currant genus) and 'sanguineum' (blood-red), referring to the reddish flowers.
