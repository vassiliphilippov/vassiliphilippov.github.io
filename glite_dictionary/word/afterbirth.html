afterbirth

1. NOUN

/ ˈæf.tɚˌbɝθ / (AmE), / ˈɑːf.təˌbɜːθ / (BrE)
the placenta and fetal membranes that are expelled from the uterus after a baby is born
placenta and membranes expelled after birth

Examples

The nurse examined the afterbirth to ensure that nothing was left inside the uterus.
Complications can occur if the afterbirth is not completely expelled after delivery.
The doctor explained the importance of delivering the afterbirth safely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: placenta - the organ that nourishes the fetus in the womb

Etymology

Formed from 'after' + 'birth'.
