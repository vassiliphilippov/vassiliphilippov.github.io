travesty

1. VERB

/ ˈtræv.ə.sti / (AmE), / ˈtræv.ɪsti / (BrE)
to represent or treat something in a way that makes it appear absurd, grotesque, or distorted; to parody or mock falsely or unfairly.
parody or distort falsely

Examples

The film completely travesties the historical events on which it is based.
Some critics argue that the play travestied Shakespeare's original intent.
It is dangerous when the media travesties the truth for sensational headlines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parody - to imitate with deliberate exaggeration for comic effect
  • Synonym: to distort - to twist or misrepresent, usually in a negative way

Etymology

From French *travesti* (disguised), past participle of *travestir* (to disguise), from Italian *travestire*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtræv.ɪ.sti / (AmE), / ˈtræv.ɪ.sti / (BrE)
a work or performance that humorously imitates and distorts the style of someone or something; a grotesque or absurd misrepresentation
humorous imitation; distorted version

Examples

The comedian's travesty of the politician had the audience in stitches.
That movie adaptation was a complete travesty of the original novel's spirit.
His speech was considered a travesty of the scientific method.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parody - a humorous or satirical imitation of a serious piece of literature or writing
  • Synonym: caricature - a picture or description that exaggerates certain features for comic effect
  • Synonym: imitation - something that copies the appearance or character of something else

Etymology

From French *travestir* (to disguise), from Italian *travestire* (to disguise).

3. NOUN

/ ˈtræv.ə.sti / (AmE), / ˈtræv.ə.sti / (BrE)
A comic work or performance characterized by broad satire and exaggerated, improbable situations, often parodying a serious subject.
comic satire with exaggerated situations

Examples

The playwright's new production is a travesty that lampoons the original tragedy with slapstick and absurd plot twists.
Many early nineteenth-century novels were adapted into stage travesties that exaggerated their melodramatic moments.
The film is a bold travesty of the detective genre, filled with outrageous situations and biting satire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parody - an imitation of the style of a particular writer or genre with deliberate exaggeration for comic effect

Etymology

From French 'travestir' (to disguise), from Italian 'travestire', from Latin 'trans-' (across) + 'vestire' (to clothe).
