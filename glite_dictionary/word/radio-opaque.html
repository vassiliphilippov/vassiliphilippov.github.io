radio-opaque

1. ADJ

/ ˌreɪdioʊ oʊˈpeɪk / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəʊ əʊˈpeɪk / (BrE)
not allowing X-rays or similar forms of radiation to pass through; appearing white or light on radiographic images
not transparent to X-rays
medical

Examples

The surgeon used a radio-opaque dye to highlight the blood vessels during the procedure.
You can clearly see the radio-opaque marker on the X-ray.
Some medical implants are made from radio-opaque materials for easy detection in imaging scans.

Synset Examples

barium sulfate is radiopaque

Related Terms

  • Antonym: radiolucent - allowing X-rays or similar radiation to pass through; opposite of radio-opaque

Etymology

Formed from 'radio-' (relating to radiation) + 'opaque' (not transmitting light).
