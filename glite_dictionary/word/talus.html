talus

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪləs / (AmE), / ˈtæl.əs / (BrE)
the bone in the ankle that connects with the leg bones to form the ankle joint
ankle bone forming ankle joint
medical

Examples

The surgeon repaired the fracture in the talus after the patient's car accident.
A dislocation of the talus can cause long-term mobility issues if left untreated.
Pain in the talus area often requires a detailed CT scan for accurate diagnosis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ankle - the joint connecting the foot with the leg

Etymology

From Latin talus (ankle bone, knuckle bone).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtæləs / (AmE), / ˈtaləs / (BrE)
a sloping mass of loose rocks accumulated at the base of a cliff or slope, formed by the falling of rock fragments
slope of loose rocks at a cliff's base

Examples

The climbers carefully navigated the talus at the bottom of the cliff.
Heavy rain can cause a talus slope to shift unexpectedly.
Vegetation rarely grows on a fresh talus because of the instability of the rocks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scree - a mass of small loose stones covering a slope or cliff base

Etymology

From French *talus* (sloping bank), of unknown origin.

Synset POS: n
