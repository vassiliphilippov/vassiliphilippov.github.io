pollotarianism

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊ.loʊˈtɛəri.əˌnɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˌpɒl.əˈteə.ri.ə.nɪz.əm / (BrE)
the practice of eating poultry but not meat from mammals as part of one's diet
diet including poultry but not mammalian meat

Examples

Many people choose pollotarianism for health or environmental reasons rather than personal taste.
After learning about animal agriculture, she decided to switch to pollotarianism and only eat poultry.
His doctor recommended pollotarianism as a healthier alternative to his previous meat-heavy diet.

Synset Examples

Vegetarianism, pescetarianism and pollotarianism diets are all strict diets that regulate consumption — the most strict diet of all is a vegan diet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vegetarianism - the practice of abstaining from consumption of meat
  • Synonym: pescetarianism - the practice of eating fish but not other meat

Etymology

From Spanish 'pollo' (chicken) + 'vegetarianism'.
