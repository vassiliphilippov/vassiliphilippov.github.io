dehydrated

1. ADJ

/ diˈhaɪ.dɹeɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / diˈhaɪ.dɹeɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
suffering from an excessive loss of water from the body; lacking sufficient fluids to function normally
lacking body water or fluids

Examples

After hiking for hours without enough water, she became dehydrated and dizzy.
Athletes are at risk of becoming dehydrated if they don't drink during long workouts.
The nurse checked whether the patient was dehydrated after the illness.

Synset Examples

fever resulted from becoming dehydrated
was dehydrated after the marathon

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hydrated - having enough water in the body
  • Synonym: parched - extremely dry or thirsty due to lack of water

Etymology

From 'dehydrate' (remove water) + '-ed'. 'Dehydrate' is from French 'déshydrater', from Greek 'hydor' (water).

2. ADJ

/ diˈhaɪdreɪtɪd / (AmE), / diˈhaɪdreɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of food or other substances) preserved by removing natural moisture; dried in order to extend shelf life or concentrate flavor.
preserved by removing moisture

Examples

The campers packed dehydrated meals for their hiking trip to save weight and space.
Dehydrated fruit snacks are a convenient option for school lunches.
To make instant soup, just add hot water to the dehydrated vegetables and seasonings.

Synset Examples

dried beef
dried fruit
dehydrated eggs
shredded and desiccated coconut meat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dried - preserved by removing moisture
  • Synonym: desiccated - dried out; lacking moisture, often referring to food

Etymology

From dehydrate + -ed, ultimately from Greek hydor (water).
