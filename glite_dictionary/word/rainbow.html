rainbow

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪnˌboʊ / (AmE), / ˈreɪnbəʊ / (BrE)
An arc of different colors visible in the sky, caused by the refraction and dispersion of sunlight by rain.
arc of colors in the sky after rain

Examples

After the storm, a beautiful rainbow appeared across the sky.
Children ran outside to admire the bright rainbow after the rain stopped.
The legend says that there is a pot of gold at the end of every rainbow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rainbow - multicolored arc caused by light refraction after rain

Etymology

From Old English 'regnboga', literally 'rain bow' (bow as in arch), from 'regn' (rain) + 'boga' (bow).

2. NOUN

/ ˈreɪnˌboʊ / (AmE), / ˈreɪnbəʊ / (BrE)
An illusory hope or something that is impossible or unlikely to achieve.
illusory or impossible hope

Examples

Some people spend their entire lives chasing rainbows that simply don't exist.
Believing he could win the lottery was just another rainbow in his mind.
The promise of quick riches turned out to be a rainbow that vanished as soon as reality set in.

Synset Examples

chasing rainbows

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fantasy - something imagined or unrealistic
  • Antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist
