unobservable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnəbˈzɝːvəbl̩ / (AmE), / ʌnəbˈzɜːvəbl̩ / (BrE)
not able to be seen or detected by direct observation.
not able to be directly observed

Examples

The particles are so small that they are unobservable to the naked eye.
Many aspects of the distant universe remain unobservable with current technology.
Her motivations were largely unobservable, hidden beneath a calm exterior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invisible - unable to be seen; not visible

Etymology

From un- (not) + observable (able to be observed).
