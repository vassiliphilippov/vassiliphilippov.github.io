lyrate

1. ADJ

/ ˈlaɪˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈlʌɪ.reɪt / (BrE)
(of a leaf) shaped like a lyre, with a large rounded end and smaller lobes towards the base.
lyre-shaped leaf

Examples

The lyrate leaves of this plant make it easy to identify in the field.
Some species in the mustard family display distinctive lyrate leaf margins.
Botanists noted the unusual lyrate shape among the seedling foliage.

Etymology

From Latin lȳrātus, from lȳra (lyre).
