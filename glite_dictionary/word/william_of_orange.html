William of Orange

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɪljəm əv ɔːrɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈwɪljəm əv ˈɒrɪndʒ / (BrE)
(1650–1702) King of England, Scotland and Ireland, also known as William III; became joint monarch with Mary II after the Glorious Revolution.
King of England, Scotland, and Ireland (1650–1702)

Examples

William of Orange and his wife Mary II ruled as joint sovereigns after the Glorious Revolution.
The invitation to William of Orange marked a turning point in British constitutional history.
As William of Orange ascended the throne, he established Protestant rule in England.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mary II - Co-monarch with William III after the Glorious Revolution

Etymology

From Dutch Willem van Oranje, reflecting his title as Prince of Orange, a principality in southern France.
