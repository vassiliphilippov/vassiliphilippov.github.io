dystrophy

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪstrəfi / (AmE), / ˈdɪstrəfi / (BrE)
any of a group of hereditary diseases characterized by progressive weakness and wasting of the muscles
hereditary muscle-wasting disease
medical

Examples

Researchers continue to search for a cure for dystrophy and related muscular diseases.
Some forms of dystrophy only affect certain muscle groups in the body.
Early diagnosis of dystrophy is crucial for managing symptoms and improving quality of life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muscular dystrophy - a specific hereditary disease causing progressive weakness and wasting of skeletal muscles
  • Degree Lower: atrophy - gradual decline or wasting of muscle tissue

Etymology

From Greek *dys-* (bad, difficult) + *-trophē* (nourishment).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɪstrəfi / (AmE), / ˈdɪstrəfi / (BrE)
A disorder in which an organ or tissue degenerates because of inadequate or defective nutrition.
degenerative disorder from poor nutrition
medical

Examples

Muscular dystrophy causes progressive weakness in the muscles.
Doctors are researching new treatments for various forms of dystrophy.
Nutritional dystrophy can develop if a child does not receive essential vitamins and minerals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atrophy - the wasting away or decrease in size of an organ or tissue
  • Antonym: hypertrophy - an abnormal enlargement of an organ or tissue

Etymology

From Greek 'dys-' (bad, difficult) + 'trophē' (nutrition, nourishment).
