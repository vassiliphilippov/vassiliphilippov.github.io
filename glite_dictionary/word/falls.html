falls

1. NOUN

/ fɔlz / (AmE), / fɔːlz / (BrE)
the outer petals or sepals of a flower, especially of an iris, that bend downward
downward-bending outer petals (iris)

Examples

The characteristic purple falls of the iris add elegance to the garden.
Each iris flower displays three upright standards and three drooping falls.
Bees often land on the colorful falls to access the nectar.

2. NOUN

/ fɔlz / (AmE), / fɔːlz / (BrE)
a place where water descends steeply over a cliff or rocks in a river; a waterfall, usually used in the plural for named sites
steep descent of river water; waterfall

Examples

We visited the majestic falls during our trip to the national park.
The falls are especially beautiful after heavy rains.
Many tourists hike to the falls to take photographs of the rushing water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waterfall - a cascade of water falling from a height

Etymology

Plural of 'fall,' in this sense meaning to drop or descend; used for geographical features since Middle English.
