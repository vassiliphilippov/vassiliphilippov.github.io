reminiscence

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛmɪˈnɪsəns / (AmE), / ˌrɛmɪˈnɪsns / (BrE)
a memory or recollection of something from the past
a memory from the past

Examples

Her story was filled with reminiscence of childhood summers at the lake.
Looking at old photographs often brings a wave of reminiscence.
During the reunion, conversations were dominated by reminiscence about days gone by.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: memory - the faculty by which the mind stores and remembers information

Etymology

From Latin reminiscentia (remembrance, recollection).

2. NOUN

/ ˌrɛm.əˈnɪs.əns / (AmE), / ˌrɛm.ɪˈnɪs.əns / (BrE)
the act or process of recalling past experiences or events, especially through deliberate mental effort
the act of remembering past experiences

Examples

Her reminiscence of childhood holidays brought a smile to her face.
The evening was filled with reminiscence as old friends shared stories from the past.
Through careful reminiscence, he was able to piece together what happened that night.

Synset Examples

he has total recall of the episode

Related Terms

  • Synonym: memory - the mental capacity for storing and recalling facts, events, and impressions

Etymology

From Latin reminisci (to remember).
