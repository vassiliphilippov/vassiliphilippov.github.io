Antennaria

1. NOUN

/ ænˌtɛˈnɛriə / (AmE), / ænˌtɛnˈnɛːriə / (BrE)
A genus of small perennial woolly herbs with whitish discoid flowers surrounded by a ring of club-shaped bristles, commonly known as pussytoes.
genus of small woolly herbs (pussytoes)

Examples

Many gardeners plant Antennaria for its unique woolly foliage and drought tolerance.
The small white flowers of Antennaria bloom in late spring across alpine meadows.
Researchers studied several species within the Antennaria genus to understand their adaptation to cold climates.

Etymology

From Latin 'antenna' (feeler), referring to the bristle-like structures resembling insect antennae.
