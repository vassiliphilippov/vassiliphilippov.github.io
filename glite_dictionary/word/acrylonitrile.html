acrylonitrile

1. NOUN

/ əˌkɹiːloʊˈnaɪtɹəl / (AmE), / əˌkɹiːləʊˈnaɪtɹaɪl / (BrE)
a colorless, volatile, and flammable liquid chemical (C3H3N) used in making plastics, synthetic rubber, and fibers
colorless liquid chemical used in plastics

Examples

Workers at the chemical plant handle acrylonitrile with care due to its flammability.
The production of acrylic fibers involves the polymerization of acrylonitrile.
Prolonged exposure to acrylonitrile vapors can pose health risks if safety precautions are not followed.

Etymology

From 'acrylic' (related to acrolein, from Latin 'acer' sharp) + 'nitrile' (from nitro and -ile) indicating a nitrile group attached to an acryl group.
