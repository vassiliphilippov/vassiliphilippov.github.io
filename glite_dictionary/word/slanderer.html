slanderer

1. NOUN

/ ˈslæn.dər.ər / (AmE), / ˈslæn.dər.ə / (BrE)
a person who makes false and damaging statements about someone; a person who slanders others
a person who makes false, damaging statements

Examples

The newspaper was sued by the politician for calling him a slanderer in its editorial.
Her reputation was unfairly ruined by a persistent slanderer in the community.
Laws are in place to protect individuals from a slanderer who spreads false rumors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defamer - a person who damages another's reputation by making false statements
  • Synonym: libeler - a person who publishes false written statements damaging to a person's reputation
  • Antonym: praiser - a person who expresses approval or admiration

Etymology

From Middle English slaundrer, from Old French esclandre (see slander).
