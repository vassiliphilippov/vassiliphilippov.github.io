duplicitous

1. ADJ

/ duˈplɪs.ɪ.təs / (AmE), / djuːˈplɪs.ɪ.təs / (BrE)
Deliberately deceptive, especially by pretending to have one set of feelings or intentions while acting on another.
deceitful or two-faced

Examples

People accused the politician of being duplicitous in his promises to different groups.
She felt betrayed by her friend's duplicitous behavior during the negotiations.
His duplicitous nature made it hard for anyone to trust him.

Synset Examples

she was a deceitful scheming little thing
a double-dealing double agent
a double-faced infernal traitor and schemer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deceitful - intending to deceive or mislead
  • Antonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and sincere

Etymology

From Latin 'duplex' (double), referring to doubleness or deceit.
