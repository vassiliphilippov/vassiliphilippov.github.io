Potamogetonaceae

1. NOUN

/ poʊˌtæməˌdʒiːtoʊˈneɪsiː / (AmE), / pəʊˌtæməˌdʒiːtəˈniːsiː / (BrE)
a family of aquatic flowering plants, commonly known as pondweeds, that grow in ponds and slow-moving streams
family of aquatic plants (pondweeds)
medical

Examples

Many species in the Potamogetonaceae family provide important habitat for aquatic wildlife.
Biologists surveyed the Potamogetonaceae in several lakes to assess water quality.
The Potamogetonaceae are easily recognized by their ribbon-like submerged leaves.

Etymology

From the genus name Potamogeton (from Greek potamos 'river' + geiton 'neighbor') plus Latin family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊ.tə.moʊˌdʒiː.təˈneɪ.siː / (AmE), / ˌpɒt.ə.məˌdʒiː.təˈneɪ.siː / (BrE)
a family of aquatic plants that grow in ponds and slow streams, sometimes including the family Zosteraceae
aquatic plant family (pondweeds)
medical

Examples

Many freshwater ecologists study members of the Potamogetonaceae because they play an important role in aquatic ecosystems.
The Potamogetonaceae include a variety of pondweed species found worldwide.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Potamogetonaceae family in a remote lake.

Etymology

From genus name Potamogeton (pondweed), from Greek potamos (river) + geiton (neighbor), with botanical family suffix -aceae.
