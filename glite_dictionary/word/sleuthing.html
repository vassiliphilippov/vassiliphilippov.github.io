sleuthing

1. NOUN

/ ˈsluːθɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsluːθɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of investigating a crime or suspicious activity, especially as done by police or detectives
investigative work by a detective or police

Examples

The journalist spent months sleuthing to uncover the truth behind the political scandal.
Thanks to modern technology, sleuthing has become more sophisticated than ever before.
Her passion for sleuthing led her to join the police force as a detective.

Synset Examples

detection is hard on the feet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detective work - the activity of investigating crimes and mysteries
  • Synonym: investigation - the process of examining a crime or problem to discover facts

Etymology

From 'sleuth' (detective), itself from Old Norse 'slóð' (track, trail).
