Roger Brooke Taney

1. PROPN

/ ˈrɑdʒər broʊk ˈtɑːni / (AmE), / ˈrɒdʒə brʊk ˈtɔːni / (BrE)
(1777–1864) United States jurist who served as Chief Justice of the Supreme Court, known for the Dred Scott decision declaring that slaves and their descendants could not be citizens.
U.S. Chief Justice famous for Dred Scott decision

Examples

Many historians consider Roger Brooke Taney’s legacy to be shaped by the controversial Dred Scott ruling.
Abraham Lincoln publicly criticized Chief Justice Roger Brooke Taney for his decision on the citizenship of slaves.
The Supreme Court under Roger Brooke Taney made several landmark decisions in the mid-19th century.
