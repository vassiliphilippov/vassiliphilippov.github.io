Chaeronea

1. PROPN

/ kɛrəˈniːə / (AmE), / kiːərəˈniːə / (BrE)
an ancient town in Greece, notable as the site of important battles, especially in 338 BC when Philip II of Macedon defeated the Athenians and Thebans, and in 86 BC when Sulla defeated Mithridates' forces.
site of ancient battles in Greece

Examples

Historians often cite the Battle of Chaeronea as a pivotal moment in Greek history.
Sulla's victory at Chaeronea marked the decline of Mithridates' power in Greece.
The strategic alliances leading up to Chaeronea had lasting consequences for the region.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Χαιρώνεια (Chairṓneia), the name of the town.
