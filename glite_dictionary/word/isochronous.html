isochronous

1. ADJ

/ ˌaɪˈsɑkrənəs / (AmE), / ˌaɪˈsɒkrənəs / (BrE)
occurring at equal intervals of time; having the same duration or period
equal in duration or interval

Examples

The clock mechanism ensures isochronous movement of the pendulum.
The data packets must be transmitted in an isochronous fashion to maintain signal integrity.
Their heartbeats became isochronous during the experiment.

Synset Examples

the oscillations were isochronal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: synchronous - occurring at the same time; simultaneous
  • Synonym: periodic - occurring or recurring at regular intervals

Etymology

From Greek 'iso-' (equal) + 'chronos' (time).
