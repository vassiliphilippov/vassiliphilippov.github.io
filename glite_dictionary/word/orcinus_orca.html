Orcinus orca

1. NOUN

/ ɔrˈsaɪnəs ˈɔrkə / (AmE), / ɔːˈsɪnəs ˈɔːkə / (BrE)
A large, predatory black-and-white toothed whale with a tall dorsal fin, found especially in cold seas; also called the killer whale.
killer whale species; large predatory marine mammal

Examples

The Orcinus orca is famous for its intelligence and complex social behaviors.
Scientists observed a pod of Orcinus orca hunting seals near the Antarctic ice.
Marine parks sometimes have Orcinus orca as one of their main attractions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: killer whale - common name for Orcinus orca

Etymology

From Latin, genus name 'Orcinus' (meaning relating to Orcus, the underworld) and species name 'orca' (large whale).
