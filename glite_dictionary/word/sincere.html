sincere

1. ADJ

/ sɪnˈsɪr / (AmE), / sɪnˈsɪə / (BrE)
showing genuine feelings or beliefs, without pretending or deceit; earnest and honest in expression or action.
genuine; not pretending or deceitful

Examples

Her apology sounded sincere, and I believed she truly regretted her mistake.
You can always count on him for sincere advice, even if it's hard to hear.
The author's sincere passion for the subject is evident throughout the book.

Synset Examples

both sides were deeply in earnest, even passionate
an entirely sincere and cruel tyrant
a film with a solemn social message

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honest - truthful and free of deceit; synonym of 'sincere' in intent
  • Antonym: insincere - not expressing genuine feelings; antonym of 'sincere'

Etymology

From Latin sincerus (clean, pure, sound).

2. ADJ

/ sɪnˈsɪr / (AmE), / sɪnˈsɪə / (BrE)
(of a person, feeling, or action) genuine and honest, not pretending or deceitful.
genuine and honest

Examples

He offered a sincere apology for his mistake, and everyone could tell he meant it.
Her sincere smile made a lasting impression on everyone she met.
It is important to give sincere feedback so people know you truly care.

Synset Examples

he was a good man, decent and sincere
felt sincere regret that they were leaving
sincere friendship

Related Terms

  • Antonym: insincere - not expressing genuine feelings; pretending
  • Synonym: genuine - truly what something is said to be; authentic

Etymology

From Latin sincerus (clean, pure, sound).
