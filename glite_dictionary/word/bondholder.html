bondholder

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːndˌhoʊldər / (AmE), / ˈbɒndˌhəʊldə / (BrE)
a person or organization that owns bonds issued by a government or corporation, and is entitled to receive interest and repayment of principal.
owner of government or corporate bonds

Examples

The government promised to pay all bondholders back in full at the maturity date.
Many bondholders were concerned about the company's ability to meet its debt obligations.
In the event of bankruptcy, bondholders are paid before shareholders.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: shareholder - a person who owns shares in a company

Etymology

From 'bond' (meaning a debt security) + 'holder' (one who possesses).
