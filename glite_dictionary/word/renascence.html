Renascence

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈneɪsəns / (AmE), / rɪˈneɪs(ə)ns / (BrE)
the period in European history at the end of the Middle Ages, marked by a cultural renewal spanning from the 14th to the mid-17th centuries; a revival of art, literature, and learning
the European cultural revival after the Middle Ages

Examples

The Renascence brought a new appreciation for classical learning and human achievement in Europe.
Many historians debate the exact beginning and end of the Renascence period.
The artistic developments during the Renascence influenced generations of painters and writers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Renaissance - the revival of art and learning in Europe from the 14th to the 17th centuries

Etymology

From Latin 'renascentia' meaning 'rebirth'; related to 'Renaissance'.
