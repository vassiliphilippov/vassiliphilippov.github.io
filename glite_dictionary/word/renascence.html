renascence

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈnæsəns / (AmE), / rɪˈneɪsəns / (BrE)
a revival or renewed interest in learning and culture, especially after a period of decline.
revival of learning and culture
archaic

Examples

The late 19th century saw a renascence of classical architecture in public buildings.
After years of neglect, the town enjoyed a renascence in arts and literature.
Her essay explores the renascence of folk music in the digital age.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: renaissance - the period of revival of art, literature, and learning in Europe in the 14th–17th centuries

Etymology

From Latin 'renascentia', meaning 'being born again'.

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈnæsəns / (AmE), / rɪˈnasəns / (BrE)
A new birth, revival, or renewal of something, especially of interest, culture, or activity.
revival or rebirth; renewal

Examples

The city experienced a renascence of art and culture after years of decline.
A renascence in scientific interest led to several important discoveries.
After recovering from hardship, she felt a personal renascence and embraced new opportunities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: renaissance - a period of revival, especially cultural or artistic; rebirth

Etymology

From Latin 'renascentia' (being born again), from 'renasci' (to be born again).
