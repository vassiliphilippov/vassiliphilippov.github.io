detergent

1. NOUN

/ dəˈtɝːdʒənt / (AmE), / dɪˈtɜːdʒənt / (BrE)
A synthetic cleaning substance that removes dirt and grease, especially one that is not made from natural fats or oils and differs from soap in chemical composition.
synthetic cleaning substance

Examples

Be sure to use a mild detergent when washing delicate clothes.
The stain came out easily after soaking the shirt in liquid detergent for an hour.
Many people prefer eco-friendly detergent to reduce their environmental impact.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: soap - a substance used for washing, made from natural fats and lye

Etymology

From Latin 'detergere' meaning 'to wipe away'.

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈtɝːdʒənt / (AmE), / dɪˈtɜːdʒənt / (BrE)
a chemical substance containing surfactants, used for cleaning, especially in laundering and industrial processes
cleaning chemical containing surfactants

Examples

This new detergent works wonders on stubborn grass stains.
You should check if the detergent is suitable for use in high-efficiency washing machines.
Industrial cleaning often requires a stronger detergent to remove oil and grease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soap - a substance used for washing and cleaning, typically made from natural fats and alkali
  • Antonym: contaminant - a substance that makes something impure or dirty

Etymology

From Latin 'detergere' (to wipe away, cleanse).
