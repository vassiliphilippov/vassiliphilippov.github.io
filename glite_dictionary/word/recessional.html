recessional

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈsɛʃənəl / (AmE), / rɪˈsɛʃənəl / (BrE)
the ceremonial withdrawal of the clergy and choir from the chancel to the vestry at the end of a church service
ceremonial church procession at end of service

Examples

The congregation stood quietly as the recessional made its way down the aisle.
After the final hymn, the recessional marked the conclusion of the Sunday service.
The choir rehearsed the recessional to ensure a smooth and dignified exit.

Etymology

From Latin recessus (a going back, retreat) via English 'recession' + '-al'.

/ rɪˈsɛʃ.ə.nəl / (AmE), / rɪˈsɛʃ.ə.nəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of receding or moving backward.
relating to moving back or receding

Examples

The glacier is exhibiting recessional behavior as it slowly retreats up the valley.
The recessional phase of the ceremony was accompanied by soft organ music.
Scientists studied the recessional movement of the shoreline after the storm.

Etymology

From recession (from Latin recessio, withdrawal) + -al.

3. NOUN

/ rɪˈsɛʃənəl / (AmE), / rɪˈsɛʃənəl / (BrE)
a hymn or piece of music sung or played at the end of a religious service while the clergy and choir leave
hymn sung as clergy and choir exit

Examples

The choir sang a beautiful recessional as the congregation prepared to leave the church.
After the service, the organist played a solemn recessional while the clergy walked down the aisle.
At weddings, couples often select a special recessional to celebrate their exit.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: processional - a hymn or music played at the start of a service as clergy proceed to the altar

Etymology

From Latin 'recessio' (a going back, withdrawal).
