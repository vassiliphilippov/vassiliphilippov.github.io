to ingratiate

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈɡreɪʃiˌeɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡreɪʃieɪt / (BrE)
to try to gain favor with someone by deliberate efforts, often through flattery or pleasing behavior
gain favor with someone by deliberate effort

Examples

He tried to ingratiate himself with his new colleagues by bringing in homemade cookies.
Politicians often ingratiate themselves with the public through promises and friendly gestures.
She attempted to ingratiate herself with her boss by volunteering for extra projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flatter - to praise excessively, often insincerely, to please someone
  • Antonym: to alienate - to cause someone to feel isolated or unfriendly

Etymology

From Italian 'ingraziare', from 'in-' (in, on) + 'grazia' (grace), ultimately from Latin 'gratia'.
