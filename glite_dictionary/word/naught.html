naught

1. NOUN

/ nɔt / (AmE), / nɔːt / (BrE)
nothing; a quantity of no importance or value.
nothing; zero; nothing of significance

Examples

After hours of work, their efforts came to naught when the machine broke down.
His apologies meant naught to her after what had happened.
Compared to her achievements, my success seems like naught.

Synset Examples

it looked like nothing I had ever seen before
reduced to nil all the work we had done
we racked up a pathetic goose egg
it was all for naught
I didn't hear zilch about it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nothing - not anything; no single thing
  • Synonym: zero - the number 0; nothing at all
  • Antonym: aught - anything; something (archaic/poetic)

Etymology

From Old English nāwiht, meaning 'no thing'.

2. NOUN

/ nɔt / (AmE), / nɔːt / (BrE)
nothing; the state of having no result, value, or success; complete failure.
nothing, complete failure
archaic

Examples

Despite his best efforts, it all came to naught in the end.
Their plans for a new business were brought to naught by unforeseen difficulties.
She worked tirelessly, but her hopes proved to be for naught.

Synset Examples

all my efforts led to naught

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nothing - the absence of anything; nothingness
  • Synonym: zero - the number 0; nothing at all

Etymology

From Old English 'nāwiht' (no thing), from 'nā' (no) + 'wiht' (thing).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00067973-n
Synset POS: n
