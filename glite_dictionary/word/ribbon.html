ribbon

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪbən / (AmE), / ˈrɪbən / (BrE)
a long, narrow strip of fabric, usually inked, used in typewriters and some printers to transfer characters onto paper
strip of inked fabric in typewriters or printers

Examples

The typewriter could not print clearly because the ribbon was nearly dry.
Before sending the letter, she replaced the ribbon in her typewriter to improve the print quality.
Many old printers still use an ink ribbon instead of toner or cartridges.

Etymology

From Old French 'ribane', of Germanic origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɪb.ən / (AmE), / ˈrɪb.ən / (BrE)
a long, narrow strip or line of something, especially one that resembles a strip of fabric.
long, narrow strip or line

Examples

A ribbon of smoke curled from the chimney into the morning sky.
At dusk, a ribbon of car lights wound through the mountain pass.

3. NOUN

/ ˈrɪb.ən / (AmE), / ˈrɪb.ən / (BrE)
A long, narrow strip of fabric, typically used for tying something or as a decorative trimming.
narrow strip of fabric for decoration or tying

Examples

She tied her hair back with a blue ribbon.
The gift was wrapped in colorful paper and finished with a silk ribbon.
The winner received a gold ribbon for first place.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: band - a flat strip or loop of material used as a fastener, for reinforcement, or as decoration
  • Synonym: tape - a narrow strip of material, typically used to hold or fasten something

Etymology

From Middle English riban, from Old French ruban, of Germanic origin.
