palm

1. NOUN

/ pɑm / (AmE), / pɑːm / (BrE)
an ancient unit of length based on the width or length of the human hand, typically about 7.5 to 10 cm (3 to 4 inches)
ancient hand-based unit of length

Examples

In ancient Egypt, a palm was used as a standard unit of measurement for building.
Historical texts sometimes refer to the palm when describing the dimensions of old buildings.
A sword blade four palms long was considered ideal in some medieval societies.

Etymology

From Latin 'palma' (palm of the hand), referencing its use as a measurement.

2. NOUN

/ pɑm / (AmE), / pɑːm / (BrE)
a mark of honor or award, especially given for winning a championship or to commemorate a notable event
honor or award for achievement

Examples

After his outstanding performance, he was presented with the palm at the award ceremony.
Winning the championship earned her the coveted palm among her peers.
The palm is often given to those who show exceptional skill or bravery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: award - a prize or mark of recognition for achievement
  • Synonym: trophy - an object awarded as a prize for victory or achievement
  • Synonym: medal - a metal disk given as a prize, especially in sports

Etymology

From Latin palma (palm of the hand, palm branch as symbol of victory).

3. NOUN

/ pɑm / (AmE), / pɑːm / (BrE)
the inner surface of the hand from the wrist to the base of the fingers
inner surface of the hand

Examples

She placed the coin in the palm of my hand.
His palms were sweaty as he waited for the results.
The fortune teller traced a line on her palm and smiled.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: back of the hand - the outer surface of the hand opposite the palm

Etymology

From Latin palma (flat of the hand, palm tree), originally related to the shape resemblance.

4. NOUN

/ pɑm / (AmE), / pɑːm / (BrE)
A tropical plant with a long, unbranched trunk, topped by large feather-like or fan-shaped leaves, belonging to the family Arecaceae.
tropical tree with large leaves

Examples

Towering palms lined the white sandy beach, providing much-needed shade.
The palm is known for its distinctive fan-shaped leaves and tall, slender trunk.
Dates are the fruit of a species of palm native to the Middle East.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palmetto - a small palm tree with fan-shaped leaves

Etymology

From Latin palma (palm tree, palm of the hand), referring to the hand-like spread of the leaves.
