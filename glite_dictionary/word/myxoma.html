myxoma

1. NOUN

/ mɪkˈsoʊmə / (AmE), / mɪkˈsəʊmə / (BrE)
A benign tumor of connective tissue containing a gelatinous or jellylike substance.
benign tumor with jellylike tissue
medical

Examples

The surgeon removed the myxoma from the patient's left atrium.
A myxoma often presents as a slow-growing, painless mass.
Cardiac myxoma is the most common primary tumor of the heart.

Etymology

From Greek myxa (mucus) + -oma (tumor).
