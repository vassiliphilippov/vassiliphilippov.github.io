intercalary

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈtɜrkəˌlɛri / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːkələri / (BrE)
(of a day or month) inserted in the calendar to harmonize the year with the solar year, as in a leap year.
inserted day or month in calendar

Examples

The calendar includes an intercalary month every few years to correct the alignment with the seasons.
February 29 is the only intercalary day in the Gregorian calendar.
In some ancient calendars, an intercalary period was occasionally added to maintain accuracy.

Synset Examples

a leap year is an intercalary year

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leap year - a year with an extra day added to keep the calendar year synchronized with the astronomical year

Etymology

From Latin intercalarius, from intercalare (to proclaim an extra day).
