precipitous

1. ADJ

/ prɪˈsɪpɪtəs / (AmE), / prɪˈsɪpɪtəs / (BrE)
(of a slope, drop, or rise) extremely steep and often dangerous.
dangerously steep (of slope, drop, etc.)

Examples

The trail to the summit was blocked by a precipitous cliff face.
They carefully descended the precipitous mountain road as thick fog rolled in.
A precipitous drop in the path made hiking treacherous during the rain.

Synset Examples

an abrupt canyon
the precipitous rapids of the upper river
the precipitous hills of Chinese paintings
a sharp drop

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steep - having a sharp inclination or slope

Etymology

From Latin 'praeceps, praecipitis' meaning 'steep, headlong, precipitous'.

2. ADJ

/ prɪˈsɪp.ɪ.təs / (AmE), / prɪˈsɪp.ɪ.təs / (BrE)
Done suddenly or with very great haste and without careful consideration; rash or impulsive.
done hastily or without thought

Examples

The company made a precipitous decision to close the branch without consulting the staff.
She regretted her precipitous move to another city, feeling she hadn't thought it through.
His precipitous resignation left the team scrambling for leadership.

Synset Examples

hasty marriage seldom proveth well
hasty makeshifts take the place of planning
rejected what was regarded as an overhasty plan for reconversion
wondered whether they had been rather precipitate in deposing the king

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hasty - done or made very quickly, often without enough thought
  • Synonym: impulsive - acting quickly without careful thought
  • Antonym: deliberate - done consciously and intentionally, with careful thought

Etymology

From Latin praecipitōsus, from praeceps (headlong, steep).
