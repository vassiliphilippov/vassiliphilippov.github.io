prizefight

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪzˌfaɪt / (AmE), / ˈpraɪzfaɪt / (BrE)
A boxing match between professional boxers, especially one in which a cash prize is awarded to the winner.
professional boxing match for a prize

Examples

The two heavyweights faced off in a sold-out prizefight last night.
Winning the prizefight brought him both fame and a substantial amount of money.
He trained for months to prepare for his first professional prizefight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boxing match - a match or contest between boxers

Etymology

From 'prize' (reward) + 'fight', originally referring to contests for a prize.

2. VERB

/ ˈpraɪzˌfaɪt / (AmE), / ˈpraɪz.faɪt / (BrE)
(of two people) to box each other for a prize or money, especially in a professional contest.
box for a prize or money

Examples

The two champions agreed to prizefight in Las Vegas next month.
He once prizefought under the name "The Tiger" for quick cash.
Many young men dreamed of making a living by prizefighting in the city's underground clubs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: box - to fight with fists as a sport (verb)

Etymology

Formed from 'prize' (reward) + 'fight' (combat), reflecting the practice of fighting for a monetary reward.

Synset POS: v
