mentor

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛn.tɔr / (AmE), / ˈmɛn.tɔː / (BrE)
an experienced and trusted advisor who guides and supports a less experienced person
trusted advisor and guide

Examples

She became a mentor to many young artists starting their careers.
A good mentor offers both guidance and constructive criticism.
The internship program matched each student with an experienced mentor in their field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advisor - a person who gives advice, especially professionally
  • Antonym: protégé - a person who is guided and supported by an older and more experienced person

Etymology

From Greek Mentor, the name of the advisor of Odysseus's son Telemachus in the Odyssey.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10329163-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈmɛn.tɔɹ / (AmE), / ˈmɛn.tɔː / (BrE)
to act as a trusted adviser or guide, especially in a professional or educational context
act as a trusted adviser or guide

Examples

After joining the company, she was mentored by a senior engineer during her first year.
The university offers programs where alumni mentor current students in their chosen fields.
He has been mentoring young entrepreneurs to help them launch their own businesses.

Synset Examples

The famous professor mentored him during his years in graduate school
She is a fine lecturer but she doesn't like mentoring

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coach - to train or give instruction to someone in a particular area
  • Synonym: to advise - to offer suggestions about the best course of action
  • Antonym: to follow - to accept the guidance or leadership of

Etymology

From Mentor, the name of a character in Greek mythology who was the adviser of Telemachus.
