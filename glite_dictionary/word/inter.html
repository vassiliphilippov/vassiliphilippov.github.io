to inter

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈtɜr / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜː / (BrE)
to place a dead body in a grave or tomb; to bury
to bury a dead body

Examples

The family chose to inter their loved one in a peaceful countryside cemetery.
The poet's remains were interred beneath the old oak tree in the village churchyard.
After the ceremony, he was interred alongside his ancestors in the family mausoleum.

Synset Examples

Stalin was buried behind the Kremlin wall on Red Square
The pharaohs were entombed in the pyramids
My grandfather was laid to rest last Sunday

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bury - to put a dead body in the ground
  • Antonym: to disinter - to dig up or remove a body from a grave

Etymology

From Latin *interrare* (to bury), from *in-* (in) + *terra* (earth).
