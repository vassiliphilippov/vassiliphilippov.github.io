hyrax

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪˌræks / (AmE), / ˈhʌɪ.raks / (BrE)
A small, herbivorous mammal native to Africa and Asia, with rodent-like front teeth and hoof-like toes.
small African or Asian mammal

Examples

The hyrax is often found basking on rocks in the midday sun.
Although it looks like a rodent, the hyrax is more closely related to elephants.
Researchers observed a group of hyraxes communicating with complex vocalizations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dassie - Another name for the rock hyrax

Etymology

From Greek hýrax (shrew-mouse), used in scientific Latin.
