arenavirus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛrəˈnəvaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˌɑːrəˈneɪvɪrəs / (BrE)
any of a group of viruses belonging to the family Arenaviridae, often causing disease in animals and humans
virus of the family Arenaviridae
medical

Examples

Scientists are studying the arenavirus to better understand how it spreads among rodents.
Infection with an arenavirus can lead to severe hemorrhagic fever in humans.
The lab confirmed the diagnosis of an arenavirus after running specialized tests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: virus - a microscopic infectious agent

Etymology

From Latin 'arena' (sand), referencing the sandy appearance of the virus particles under an electron microscope.
