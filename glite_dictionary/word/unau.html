unau

1. NOUN

/ uːˈnɑːu / (AmE), / uːˈnɑːu / (BrE)
A small, fast-moving, two-toed sloth native to Central and South America, with two long claws on each front foot.
a two-toed sloth of the Americas

Examples

The unau spends most of its life hanging from tree branches in tropical forests.
Unlike its three-toed relative, the unau is known for being relatively quick and active.
Researchers observed several unaus feeding on leaves high in the rainforest canopy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: two-toed sloth - a sloth with two claws on each front foot

Etymology

From Portuguese 'unau', from Tupi 'unaú', referring to sloths.

2. NOUN

/ ˈjuːnɑːˌuː / (AmE), / ˈjuːnɑːˌuː / (BrE)
A sloth native to Central and South America, especially known as the two-toed sloth, having two long claws on each forefoot and three long claws on each hindfoot.
two-toed sloth native to Central and South America

Examples

The unau spends most of its life hanging from trees in the rainforest canopy.
Unlike the three-toed sloth, the unau has only two long claws on each forefoot.
Researchers observed the unau moving slowly through the branches in search of leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: two-toed sloth - a sloth species with two claws on each forefoot

Etymology

From Portuguese *unau*, from Tupi *unâu* (sloth).
