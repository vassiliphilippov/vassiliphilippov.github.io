scrawniness

1. NOUN

/ ˈskrɔːni.nəs / (AmE), / ˈskrɔːni.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality or condition of being very thin, lean, or underdeveloped in size or appearance
state of being very thin or underdeveloped

Examples

His scrawniness made him look younger than he really was.
The scrawniness of the stray dog was obvious to everyone who passed by.
Despite her scrawniness, she was stronger than she appeared.

Synset Examples

the scrawniness of sickly trees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leanness - the condition of being thin but healthy
  • Antonym: robustness - the quality of being strong and healthy

Etymology

Formed from 'scrawny' (of unclear origin, possibly related to Scottish 'scranny' or 'scraunie').

2. NOUN

/ ˈskrɑːni nəs / (AmE), / ˈskrɔːni nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being very thin or lacking flesh.
thinness; lack of flesh

Examples

His scrawniness was apparent when he took off his jacket.
Despite her scrawniness, she was surprisingly strong.
The doctor was concerned about the boy’s scrawniness and prescribed a better diet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thinness - the quality of being thin
  • Antonym: plumpness - the quality of being pleasantly fat or rounded

Etymology

From 'scrawny' + -ness; 'scrawny' likely of imitative origin.
