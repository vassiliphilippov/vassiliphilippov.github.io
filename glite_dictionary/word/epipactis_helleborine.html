Epipactis helleborine

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈpæktɪs ˌhɛlɪˈbɔːraɪn / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈpæktɪs ˌhɛlɪˈbɔːriːn / (BrE)
A Eurasian orchid species bearing spikes of green and pinkish or purplish flowers, commonly found in woodlands.
a Eurasian woodland orchid species

Examples

The woodland path was lined with Epipactis helleborine in full bloom, adding delicate color to the greenery.
Many gardeners value Epipactis helleborine for its resilience and ornamental flower spikes.
Researchers studied the pollination habits of Epipactis helleborine to better understand orchid adaptation.

Etymology

Epipactis from Greek 'epipaktis' (an orchid); helleborine from New Latin, relating to Helleborus.
