Chaetognatha

1. NOUN

/ kiːtəɡˈnæθə / (AmE), / kiːtəɡˈneɪθə / (BrE)
a phylum of small, active, transparent marine worms known as arrowworms, found in oceans worldwide
phylum of marine arrowworms

Examples

Chaetognatha are important predators in planktonic ecosystems, feeding on smaller zooplankton.
Marine biologists discovered a new species within Chaetognatha in the Pacific Ocean.
Because of their transparency, Chaetognatha are difficult to spot without careful observation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrowworm - common name for members of the phylum Chaetognatha

Etymology

From Greek khaite (long hair) + gnathos (jaw), referring to their bristle-like jaws.
