reamer

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːmɚ / (AmE), / ˈriːmə / (BrE)
A kitchen tool with a conical, ridged center used for extracting juice from citrus fruits by pressing and twisting the fruit against it.
citrus-juice extraction tool

Examples

She used a reamer to extract the lemon juice for the recipe.
The reamer fits comfortably in the hand, making it easy to squeeze oranges.
A simple glass reamer can efficiently juice grapefruits and limes.

Etymology

From 'ream', originally meaning to widen or enlarge a hole, reflecting the tool's similar motion in use.

2. NOUN

/ ˈriːmər / (AmE), / ˈriːmə / (BrE)
a tool with a cylindrical, often fluted, cutting edge used to enlarge or finish holes in metal, wood, or other materials
tool for enlarging or finishing holes

Examples

The mechanic used a reamer to enlarge the hole for the new bolt.
A reamer is essential in precision engineering for finishing drilled holes.
After drilling, he ran a reamer through the metal to smooth the edges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drill - a tool or machine with a rotating cutting tip used for making holes

Etymology

From 'ream' (to enlarge a hole) + '-er' (agent noun suffix); ultimately from Old English 'rēman' (to widen).
