Lepiotaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛpiˌoʊˈteɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌlɛpɪˌəʊˈteɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of fungi characterized by free gills and a cap that is easily separable from the stalk
family of fungi with free gills and separable caps
medical

Examples

Members of the Lepiotaceae family are often found in leaf litter in forests.
Some varieties within Lepiotaceae are edible, while others are highly toxic.
The cap and stalk of mushrooms in Lepiotaceae are usually easily separated.

Etymology

From the genus Lepiota, based on Greek 'lepis' (scale) and scientific Latin '-aceae' (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌlɛpiˌɑːtəˈsiː.i / (AmE), / ˌlɛpɪ.əˈteɪ.siː / (BrE)
a family of fungi characterized by free gills and a cap that can be easily separated from the stalk; includes various mushrooms
family of mushrooms with free gills and separable caps

Examples

The Lepiotaceae are studied for their diversity of mushroom types in many ecosystems.
Some edible mushrooms belong to the Lepiotaceae, but others in the family are poisonous.
Under the microscope, features of Lepiotaceae help distinguish them from similar fungal families.

Etymology

From genus Lepiota and standard botanical family suffix -aceae, from Greek lepis (scale).
