to senesce

1. VERB

/ səˈnɛs / (AmE), / sɪˈnɛs / (BrE)
to grow old; to undergo the process of aging
to grow old; to age

Examples

Many species of trees senesce at a predictable age in the wild.
The scientist studied how cells senesce over time in culture.
As humans senesce, their cells become less efficient at repair.

Synset Examples

She aged gracefully
we age every day — what a depressing thought!
Young men senesce

Related Terms

  • Synonym: age - to grow older; to show signs of aging
  • Antonym: to rejuvenate - to make or become young again

Etymology

From Latin 'senescere' meaning 'to grow old', from 'senex' meaning 'old'.
