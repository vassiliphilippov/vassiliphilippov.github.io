Grampus

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡræmpəs / (AmE), / ˈɡræmpəs / (BrE)
A genus of marine mammals in the dolphin family, including the species Risso's dolphin (Grampus griseus).
a genus of dolphins (Risso's dolphin)

Examples

The Grampus genus is represented today by only a single living species.
Marine biologists observed a pod of Grampus while conducting research in the Pacific Ocean.
Unlike other dolphins, Grampus griseus is known for its blunt head and unique body markings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Risso's dolphin - A species within the genus Grampus, known for its distinctive scarred appearance.

Etymology

From Latin 'grampus', used in taxonomy for marine mammals known as Risso's dolphin.
