muckraker

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌkˌreɪkər / (AmE), / ˈmʌkˌreɪkə / (BrE)
a journalist or writer who seeks out and publishes scandalous or damaging information about public figures, often for political motives
one who exposes scandals, often in politics

Examples

The newspaper hired a well-known muckraker to investigate the mayor's office.
As a muckraker, she uncovered several cases of political corruption during her career.
Many early 20th-century muckrakers played a key role in exposing corporate abuses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: investigative journalist - a journalist who deeply investigates issues of public interest
  • Degree Lower: sensationalist - one who presents stories in a way that is intended to provoke public interest or excitement
  • Synonym: scandal-monger - a person who spreads scandal

Etymology

Formed from 'muckrake,' popularized in the early 20th century after U.S. President Theodore Roosevelt used the term in 1906 to criticize journalists exposing corruption.
