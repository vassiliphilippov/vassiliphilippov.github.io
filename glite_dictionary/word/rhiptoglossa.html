Rhiptoglossa

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɪptəˈɡlɒsə / (AmE), / ˌrɪptəˈɡlɒsə / (BrE)
A group of Old World chameleons; in some classifications treated as a superfamily within the order Sauria.
group of Old World chameleons

Examples

The chameleons of the genus Rhiptoglossa are known for their zygodactylous feet and independently mobile eyes.
In some classifications, Rhiptoglossa is considered a distinct superfamily of lizards.
Herpetologists study the evolutionary relationships within Rhiptoglossa to better understand chameleon diversity.

Etymology

From Greek 'rhipto' (to throw) and 'glossa' (tongue), referring to the chameleons' projecting tongues.
