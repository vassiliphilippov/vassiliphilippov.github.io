to deglycerolise

1. VERB

/ diˈɡlɪsəˌroʊlaɪz / (AmE), / diˈɡlɪsərəlaɪz / (BrE)
to remove glycerol from a substance, particularly from preserved red blood cells or other materials
remove glycerol from something
medical

Examples

After thawing, it is necessary to deglycerolise the red blood cells before transfusion.
The samples were carefully deglycerolised to prepare them for further analysis.
A special filter is used to deglycerolise stored blood units in the laboratory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glycerol - a simple polyol compound often used as a preserving agent

Etymology

From the prefix 'de-' (removal) + 'glycerol' + '-ise' (verb-forming suffix).
