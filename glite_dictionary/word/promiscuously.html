promiscuously

1. ADV

/ prəˈmɪskjuəsli / (AmE), / prəˈmɪskjuːəsli / (BrE)
in a way that involves having casual sexual relationships with many different partners, or in a disordered or indiscriminate manner
in a promiscuous or indiscriminate manner

Examples

The novel describes a group of people living promiscuously in a chaotic flat.
He was rumored to behave promiscuously at every social gathering.
It was frowned upon in her community to date promiscuously without serious intentions.

Synset Examples

this young girl has to share a room with her mother who lives promiscuously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: promiscuous - having or involving many sexual partners or relationships

Etymology

From 'promiscuous' + '-ly'; ultimately from Latin 'promiscuus' (mixed, indiscriminate).

2. ADV

/ prəˈmɪskjuəsli / (AmE), / prəˈmɪskjʊəsli / (BrE)
in an indiscriminate or unselective manner; without careful choice or order
in an indiscriminate or unselective manner

Examples

The books were stacked promiscuously on the shelves, with no apparent order.
He made friends promiscuously, never caring about their backgrounds or interests.
She sampled cuisines promiscuously, eager to try a bit of everything at the festival.

Synset Examples

she reads promiscuously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indiscriminately - in a random or haphazard way, without careful choice

Etymology

From Latin 'promiscuus' (mixed, indiscriminate) + English adverbial suffix '-ly'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00435256-r
Synset POS: r
