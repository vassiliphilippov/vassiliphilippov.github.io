Reithrodontomys

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːθroʊˈdɒntəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˌriːθrəʊˈdɒntəˌmɪz / (BrE)
A genus of small rodents commonly known as New World harvest mice, native to North and Central America.
genus of New World harvest mice

Examples

The biologist spent years studying populations of Reithrodontomys in the grasslands of Mexico.
Several species within the genus Reithrodontomys have adapted to different habitats.
The field guide provides detailed descriptions of all known Reithrodontomys species.

Etymology

From Greek 'reithron' (channel, groove), 'odous' (tooth), and 'mys' (mouse), referring to grooved teeth of the genus.
