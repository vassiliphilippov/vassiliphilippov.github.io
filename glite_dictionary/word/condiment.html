condiment

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑn.dɪ.mənt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.dɪ.mənt / (BrE)
A substance such as a sauce, relish, or spice added to food to enhance its flavor.
substance added to enhance food flavor

Examples

Please put all the condiments on the table before dinner is served.
Ketchup is the most popular condiment in American households.
She enjoys trying different condiments with her fries, such as aioli and sriracha.

Synset Examples

mustard and ketchup are condiments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seasoning - a substance used to add flavor to food
  • Synonym: spice - an aromatic or pungent vegetable substance used to flavor food

Etymology

From Latin 'condīmentum' (spice, seasoning), from 'condīre' (to preserve, season).
