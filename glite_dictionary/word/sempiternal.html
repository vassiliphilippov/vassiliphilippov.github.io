sempiternal

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛmpɪˈtɜrnəl / (AmE), / ˌsɛmpɪˈtɜːnəl / (BrE)
lasting forever; having no known beginning or end; eternal.
lasting forever; eternal

Examples

Some philosophers believe in a sempiternal truth beyond human understanding.
The sea's sempiternal rhythm calms her mind.
Ancient myths often refer to the gods as sempiternal beings.

Synset Examples

the dateless rise and fall of the tides
time is endless
sempiternal truth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eternal - lasting forever, without beginning or end
  • Antonym: temporary - lasting for only a limited period

Etymology

From Latin 'sempiternus', from 'semper' (always).
