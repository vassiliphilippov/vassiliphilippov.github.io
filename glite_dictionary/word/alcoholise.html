to alcoholise

1. VERB

/ ˈæl.kə.hə.laɪz / (AmE), / ˈal.kə.lɪz / (BrE)
to treat or infuse (something) with alcohol
treat or infuse with alcohol

Examples

Some chefs alcoholise fruit to enhance its flavor in desserts.
You should alcoholise the specimen before preservation to prevent decay.
They alcoholised the cherries and stored them for the holidays.

Synset Examples

alcoholize the fruit and let them sit in the refrigerator

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to alcoholize - to treat or infuse with alcohol (American spelling)

Etymology

From alcohol + -ise, patterned after similar English verbs.

2. VERB

/ ˈæl.kə.hə.laɪz / (AmE), / ˈæl.kə.hɒ.laɪz / (BrE)
to make something alcoholic, especially by fermentation
make alcoholic by fermentation

Examples

The manufacturer decided to alcoholise the fruit to create a unique dessert.
Before serving, they alcoholised the punch with a generous splash of rum.
Some traditional recipes call for you to alcoholise cherries before using them in cakes.

Synset Examples

alcoholize prunes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ferment - to undergo or cause fermentation, especially to produce alcohol
  • Synonym: to alcoholize - an alternative spelling of 'alcoholise' (make alcoholic, as by fermenting)

Etymology

From alcohol + -ise, patterned after similar English verbs.
