to except

1. VERB

/ ɪkˈsɛpt / (AmE), / ɪkˈsɛpt / (BrE)
to prevent (someone or something) from being included, considered, or accepted; to exclude
prevent from being included; exclude

Examples

All international flights are excepted from the restriction.
Children under 12 are excepted from taking the test.
They decided to except certain items from the final bill.

Synset Examples

The bad results were excluded from the report
Leave off the top piece

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exclude - to leave out; to prevent from being part of something
  • Antonym: to include - to make someone or something part of a group or collection

Etymology

From Latin 'excipere' meaning 'to take out, exclude'.
