farrow

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæroʊ / (AmE), / ˈfarəʊ / (BrE)
the production or birth of a litter of pigs
birth of piglets

Examples

The sow had a large farrow this spring, with ten healthy piglets.
Farmers keep detailed records of each farrow to monitor breeding success.
Proper nutrition is important to ensure a healthy farrow in pigs.

Etymology

From Old English *fearh* ('young pig') and related Germanic terms.

2. VERB

/ ˈfæroʊ / (AmE), / ˈfarəʊ / (BrE)
(of a sow) to give birth to piglets.
(of a sow) give birth to piglets

Examples

The sow farrowed early in the morning, delivering eight healthy piglets.
Farmers monitor their pigs closely when they are about to farrow.
If a sow does not farrow on schedule, it may indicate a problem.

sows farrow
