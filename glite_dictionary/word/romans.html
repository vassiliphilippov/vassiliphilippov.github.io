Romans

1. PROPN

/ ˈroʊmənz / (AmE), / ˈrəʊmənz / (BrE)
a book of the New Testament, written by Saint Paul, which presents his teachings on Christian doctrine; also known as the Epistle to the Romans
book of the New Testament by Saint Paul

Examples

Many scholars consider Romans to be the most theologically significant book in the New Testament.
During the service, the pastor read a passage from Romans about faith and grace.
She studied Romans in her Bible study group to better understand Paul's teachings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Galatians - another New Testament epistle by Saint Paul
  • Synonym: Corinthians - epistles by Saint Paul to the Corinthians

Etymology

Named after the recipients of the epistle, the Christians in Rome (from Latin 'Romanus' meaning 'Roman').
