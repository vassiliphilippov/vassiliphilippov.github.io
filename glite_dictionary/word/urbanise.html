to urbanise

1. VERB

/ ˈɝː.bə.naɪz / (AmE), / ˈɜː.bə.naɪz / (BrE)
make or become more like a city, usually by increasing buildings, industry, or population density
make more city-like or industrial

Examples

The government plans to urbanise several villages near the capital.
Rapid migration can urbanise a region in just a few decades.
Once the area was urbanised, property values increased significantly.

Synset Examples

The area was urbanized after many people moved in

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to urbanize - alternative spelling, especially American English
  • Antonym: ruralise - make more rural or country-like

Etymology

From Latin 'urbanus' (of a city), via French 'urbaniser'.

2. VERB

/ ˈɝː.bə.naɪz / (AmE), / ˈɜː.bə.naɪz / (BrE)
to impart urban habits, ways of life, or responsibilities to a person, group, or thing
to impart urban habits or lifestyle

Examples

Efforts to urbanise the rural population have met both support and resistance.
Tourism can gradually urbanise wildlife as they adapt to human presence.
Educators aim to urbanise young students by exposing them to city culture and technology.

Synset Examples

Birds are being urbanized by people in outdoor cafes feeding them

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to urbanize - alternative spelling, especially American English
  • Antonym: ruralize - to impart rural habits or ways of life

Etymology

From French 'urbaniser', from Latin 'urbanus' (of a city).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
