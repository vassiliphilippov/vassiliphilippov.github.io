to cark

1. VERB

/ kɑrk / (AmE), / kɑːk / (BrE)
(archaic) To disturb, worry, or cause unease in someone's mind.
disturb or worry (archaic)
archaic

Examples

It did cark her heart to hear such dreadful news.
Financial troubles cark him night and day, leaving him restless.
The burden of secrecy began to cark the young squire's conscience.

Synset Examples

She was rather perturbed by the news that her father was seriously ill

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worry - to cause someone to feel anxious or concerned
  • Synonym: to perturb - to disturb or trouble mentally
  • Antonym: to reassure - to say or do something to remove the doubts or fears of someone

Etymology

From Middle English *carken* (to burden), from Old Northern French *carquier* (to load, charge).
