branch

1. NOUN

/ bræntʃ / (AmE), / brɑːntʃ / (BrE)
a part of a tree or plant that grows out from the main stem and has leaves, flowers, or fruit growing on it
a part growing from the main stem of a plant or tree

Examples

The children climbed out onto a branch to pick some apples.
After the storm, several branches were scattered across the lawn.
A bird built its nest at the end of a branch high in the oak tree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: limb - a large branch of a tree

Etymology

From Old French *branche*, from Late Latin *branca* (paw, claw, branch).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13184148-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ bræntʃ / (AmE), / brɑːntʃ / (BrE)

Examples
