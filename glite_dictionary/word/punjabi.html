Punjabi

1. NOUN

/ pʊnˈdʒɑːbi / (AmE), / pʊnˈdʒɑːbi / (BrE)
the Indo-Aryan language spoken mainly in the Punjab region of Pakistan and India
Indo-Aryan language of Punjab region

Examples

She is fluent in Punjabi as well as in English and Hindi.
They watched a film dubbed in Punjabi last night.
Many songs in Punjabi have become popular worldwide.

Etymology

From Punjabi Panjābī, meaning 'of Punjab.'

2. NOUN

/ pʌnˈdʒɑːbi / (AmE), / pʌnˈdʒɑːbi / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of the Punjab region of Pakistan or India, or a member of the Punjabi people
a person from Punjab (Pakistan or India)

Examples

My best friend is a Punjabi who grew up in Lahore.
Many Punjabis celebrate both religious and regional festivals with great enthusiasm.
The Punjabi was proud to share stories about his heritage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sikh - a member of a religious community originating in the Punjab region

Etymology

From Hindi Panjābī, derived from Panjāb, the name of the region and its people.

3. NOUN

/ pʌnˈdʒɑːbi / (AmE), / pʌnˈdʒɑːbi / (BrE)
the Indo-Aryan language spoken mainly in the Punjab region of Pakistan and India
Indo-Aryan language of Punjab region

Examples

She speaks Punjabi fluently with her grandparents at home.
The Punjabi language has a rich tradition of poetry and music.
Many signs in that area are written in both English and Punjabi.

Etymology

From Hindi 'Pañjābī', meaning 'of Punjab', from Persian 'panj' (five) + 'āb' (water), referring to the five rivers of Punjab.
