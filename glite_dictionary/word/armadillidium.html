Armadillidium

1. NOUN

/ ɑːr.məˈdɪl.i.ɜːm / (AmE), / ˌɑː.mə.dɪˈlɪd.i.əm / (BrE)
A genus of small, terrestrial crustaceans in the family Armadillidiidae, known for their ability to roll into a ball when threatened.
genus of pill bugs (crustaceans)

Examples

Armadillidium species can often be found hiding under logs and stones in damp environments.
Scientists studied the behavior of Armadillidium to understand how they react to predation threats.
My children love collecting Armadillidium in the garden and watching them roll into tiny balls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pill bug - common name for small, terrestrial isopods that roll into a ball
  • Synonym: woodlouse - terrestrial isopod crustacean, broader category including Armadillidium

Etymology

From Latin 'armadillo' (little armored one) with scientific suffix '-idium'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrməˌdɪlɪˈdiːəm / (AmE), / ˌɑːməˌdɪlɪˈdiːəm / (BrE)
A genus of woodlice in the family Armadillidiidae, known for their ability to roll into a ball when disturbed.
genus of woodlice

Examples

The most common Armadillidium species in Europe is Armadillidium vulgare, also known as the common pill bug.
Researchers collected several Armadillidium specimens from under the garden stones for their study.
Many Armadillidium species can roll up tightly when threatened, just like miniature armadillos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodlouse - small terrestrial crustacean with a rigid, segmented exoskeleton

Etymology

From New Latin, based on 'armadillo', referring to the animal's ability to roll into a ball.
