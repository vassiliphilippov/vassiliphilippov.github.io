Helicidae

1. NOUN

/ hɛˈlɪsəˌdiː / (AmE), / hɛˈlɪsɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of land snails that includes the common edible snail and certain pest species
family of land snails

Examples

Members of the Helicidae family are commonly found in gardens throughout Europe.
The Helicidae include the well-known edible garden snail used in French cuisine.
Some species within Helicidae can become agricultural pests due to their feeding habits.

Etymology

From modern Latin Helicidae, based on Greek helix (spiral, snail shell).

2. NOUN

/ ˌhɛlɪˈsɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌhɛlɪˈsɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of land snails including the common edible snail and some species regarded as pests
family of land snails

Examples

The Helicidae family includes many common land snails found in gardens across Europe.
Certain Helicidae species are considered pests because they damage crops and ornamental plants.
Escargot, a popular delicacy, is often prepared using snails of the Helicidae family.

Etymology

From Modern Latin Helicidae, based on Greek helix (spiral, snail shell).
