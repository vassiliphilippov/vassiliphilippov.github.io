Virgil

1. PROPN

/ ˈvɜrdʒəl / (AmE), / ˈvɜːdʒɪl / (BrE)
(70–19 BC) A famous Roman poet known for writing the epic poem 'Aeneid'.
Roman poet, author of the 'Aeneid'

Examples

Virgil is often studied in Latin classes for his influence on Western literature.
The 'Aeneid', written by Virgil, remains a cornerstone of classical epic poetry.
Many scholars consider Virgil second only to Homer among ancient epic poets.

Etymology

From Latin 'Vergilius', the poet's family name.
