Epinephelus fulvus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪnɪˈfiːləs ˈfʊlvəs / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪnɪˈfiːləs ˈfʊlvəs / (BrE)
a species of grouper fish, usually dusky-colored with black spots and reddish fins, found in tropical and subtropical waters
species of dusky-colored, black-spotted grouper fish

Examples

The Epinephelus fulvus is often found near reefs in the western Atlantic Ocean.
Divers occasionally spot the elusive Epinephelus fulvus among the coral formations.
Researchers studied the diet of Epinephelus fulvus as part of a marine ecology survey.

Etymology

From New Latin; 'Epinephelus' is a genus name from Greek epi- (upon) + nephelē (cloud), and 'fulvus' is Latin for tawny or yellowish-brown.
