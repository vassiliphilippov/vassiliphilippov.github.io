fergusonite

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɝːɡəsˌnaɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɜːɡəsˌnaɪt / (BrE)
A dark mineral composed chiefly of oxides of yttrium, erbium, tantalum, and other rare elements.
a mineral of rare earth oxides

Examples

The geologist collected a specimen of fergusonite from the granite outcrop.
Rare earth metals are often extracted from minerals like fergusonite in specialized mines.
The presence of fergusonite indicated that the area might be rich in tantalum and yttrium.

Etymology

Named after Scottish physicist Robert Fergusson.
