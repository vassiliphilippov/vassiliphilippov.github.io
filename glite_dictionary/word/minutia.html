minutia

1. NOUN

/ məˈnuːʃiə / (AmE), / mɪˈnjuːʃɪə / (BrE)
In biometrics and forensics, any of the distinct features of a fingerprint that allow different prints to be compared.
fingerprint feature used for identification
medical

Examples

Fingerprint analysts examine the minutia to establish a match between two prints.
Each minutia in a fingerprint forms a unique pattern for identification.
The software detected multiple minutia during the scan for biometric verification.

Synset Examples

The major minutia features of fingerprint ridges are ridge ending, bifurcation, and short ridge (or dot).

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ridge ending - point where a fingerprint ridge terminates
  • Synonym: bifurcation - point where a fingerprint ridge splits into two

Etymology

From Latin *minutia* (smallness, trifles), derivative of *minutus* (small).
