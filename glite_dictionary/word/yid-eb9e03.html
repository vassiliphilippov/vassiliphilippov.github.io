Yid

1. NOUN

/ jid / (AmE), / jɪd / (BrE)
(offensive or informal) A Jewish person, sometimes used as a self-identifying term within some Jewish communities, but generally considered offensive in English usage.
offensive term for a Jewish person
offensive

Examples

He proudly identified himself as a Yid at the gathering.
Some people in the crowd chanted 'Come on, you Yids!' as a show of solidarity, although it's controversial.
She explained that, for her, calling herself a Yid was a way to reclaim the term.

Synset Examples

Etymology

From Yiddish ייִד (yid), meaning 'Jew', originating from Middle High German 'Jüde'.
