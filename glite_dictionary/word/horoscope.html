horoscope

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔr.əˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈhɒr.əˌskəʊp / (BrE)
A diagram or chart showing the positions of the planets and signs of the zodiac at a particular time and place, used in astrology to predict a person's future or character.
astrological chart of planets and zodiac signs

Examples

She created a detailed horoscope for her friend's birthday using their exact place and time of birth.
In ancient times, kings often consulted their horoscope before making important decisions.
The astrologer explained how the horoscope reflects the alignment of the planets at the moment you were born.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astrology - the study of how the positions of celestial bodies supposedly influence human affairs

Etymology

From Greek hōroskopos, meaning 'observer of the hour'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɔːrəˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈhɒrəsˌkəʊp / (BrE)
A prediction of a person's future, typically based on the positions of the stars and planets at the time of their birth.
astrological prediction of the future

Examples

She read her horoscope every morning before leaving the house.
According to my horoscope, today is a good day for new beginnings.
He doesn't believe in horoscopes, but sometimes checks them for fun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astrology - the study of movements and relative positions of celestial bodies interpreted as having an influence on human affairs
  • Synonym: prediction - a statement about what will happen in the future

Etymology

From Greek hōroskopos, from hōra (time) + skopos (observer).
