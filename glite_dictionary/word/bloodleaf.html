bloodleaf

1. NOUN

/ ˈblʌdˌlif / (AmE), / ˈblʌdˌliːf / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Iresine, known for its brightly colored foliage, often red or purple.
plant of the genus Iresine with colored foliage

Examples

The vibrant red leaves of the bloodleaf make it a popular choice for decorative gardens.
Many gardeners use bloodleaf as a colorful accent in shaded areas.
Some species of bloodleaf are also grown indoors as attractive houseplants.

Etymology

From 'blood' (referring to red coloration) and 'leaf'.
