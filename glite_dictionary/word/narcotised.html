narcotised

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɑrkəˌtaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈnɑːkətaɪzd / (BrE)
affected by or under the influence of a narcotic drug, especially to the point of diminished awareness or consciousness.
under the influence of narcotics
medical

Examples

She appeared narcotised after receiving her pain medication.
The patient was found in a deeply narcotised state in the hospital room.
After the injection, he lay narcotised and unresponsive to questions.

Synset Examples

knocked out by doped wine
a drugged sleep
were under the effect of the drugged sweets
in a stuperous narcotized state

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drugged - affected by drugs or narcotics

Etymology

From narcotic + -ise/-ize (to make or become).
