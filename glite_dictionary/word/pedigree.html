pedigree

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛd.ɪˌɡri / (AmE), / ˈpɛd.ɪˌɡriː / (BrE)
the recorded ancestry or line of descent of a purebred animal, showing it is purebred
ancestry of a purebred animal

Examples

The dog’s pedigree proves that it comes from an award-winning line.
Official documents confirmed the horse’s pedigree before it was allowed to race.
She paid a high price for the cat because of its excellent pedigree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ancestry - family lineage or descent
  • Synonym: lineage - direct descent from an ancestor
  • Antonym: mongrel - an animal of mixed or unknown breed; not purebred

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman 'pé de grue' (crane's foot), referring to the shape of lines on genealogical charts.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛdɪˌɡri / (AmE), / ˈpɛdɪɡriː / (BrE)
the recorded ancestry or lineage of a purebred animal, especially as proof of its breeding
recorded ancestry of a purebred animal

Examples

The dog's pedigree showed that it was descended from several champion show dogs.
Only animals with a verified pedigree can be entered into this prestigious competition.
The breeder proudly displayed the pedigree certifying the horse's pure lineage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lineage - direct descent from an ancestor; ancestry
  • Synonym: ancestry - one's family or ethnic descent
  • Antonym: mongrel - an animal, especially a dog, of no definable type or breed (opposite of pedigree)

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman 'pé de grue' (crane's foot), referring to the branch lines in genealogical charts.

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɛdɪˌɡri / (AmE), / ˈpɛdɪɡriː / (BrE)
the recorded ancestry or lineage of a person or animal, especially when considered to be pure or distinguished
recorded ancestry or lineage

Examples

The horse's pedigree was carefully traced back several generations.
She came from a family with an impressive pedigree in the arts.
Some dog breeds require a documented pedigree for participation in competitions.

Synset Examples

his entire lineage has been warriors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lineage - direct descent from an ancestor
  • Synonym: ancestry - family or ancestral descent
  • Degree Lower: progeny - descendants or offspring

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *pé de grue* (‘crane’s foot’), referring to a symbol used in genealogical trees.

4. ADJ

/ ˈpɛdəˌɡri / (AmE), / ˈpɛdɪɡriː / (BrE)
(of an animal) having a recorded line of ancestors, especially as proof of being purebred.
(of an animal) purebred with recorded ancestry

Examples

The kennel club only accepts pedigree dogs for official registration.
Her farm specializes in raising pedigree sheep for breeding competitions.
A pedigree cat can often fetch a much higher price than a mixed-breed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purebred - bred from parents of the same breed or variety
  • Antonym: mongrel - an animal of mixed or indeterminate breed

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman 'pé de grue' (crane’s foot), referring to a mark used on family trees.
