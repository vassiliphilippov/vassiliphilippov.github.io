fountainhead

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaʊntənˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈfaʊntənˌhɛd / (BrE)
the original source of a stream or river; the point from which a flow of water originates
source of a stream or river

Examples

The mountain spring is the fountainhead of the river that winds through the valley.
They hiked for hours to reach the hidden fountainhead of the stream.
Protecting the fountainhead ensures clean water for all the towns downstream.

Synset Examples

they tracked him back toward the head of the stream

Related Terms

  • Synonym: source - a place, person, or thing from which something originates
  • Antonym: mouth - the place where a river flows into another body of water

Etymology

From 'fountain' + 'head', dating to the 16th century, denoting the head or origin of a water fountain.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfaʊntənˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈfaʊntənˌhɛd / (BrE)
an original and abundant source of something, especially knowledge, ideas, or inspiration
abundant original source

Examples

The city became a fountainhead of innovation and creativity during the Renaissance.
He is regarded as the fountainhead of new ideas in the company.
Ancient Greece was the fountainhead of Western philosophy and science.

Synset Examples

she was a well of information

Related Terms

  • Synonym: source - a place, person, or thing from which something originates

Etymology

From 'fountain' + 'head'; originally referring to the principal source of a stream.

Synset POS: n
