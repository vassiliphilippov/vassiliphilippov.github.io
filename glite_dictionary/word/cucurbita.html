Cucurbita

1. NOUN

/ kuːˈkɜːrbɪtə / (AmE), / kjuːˈkɜːbɪtə / (BrE)
a genus of plants in the gourd family (Cucurbitaceae), including squashes, pumpkins, and gourds
genus of gourds and squashes

Examples

The genus Cucurbita includes plants that produce squashes and pumpkins.
Researchers are studying the domestication process of Cucurbita species in the Americas.
Many varieties of Cucurbita are grown worldwide for their edible fruits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gourd - a hard-shelled fruit used as a vessel or ornament
  • Synonym: pumpkin - a large, round, orange fruit of the plant Cucurbita pepo

Etymology

From Latin cucurbita (gourd, pumpkin).

2. NOUN

/ kuːˈkɝːbɪtə / (AmE), / kjuːˈkɜːbɪtə / (BrE)
A genus of herbaceous vines in the gourd family (Cucurbitaceae), which includes squashes, pumpkins, and gourds.
genus of squash and gourds

Examples

The Cucurbita genus includes familiar vegetables such as pumpkins and zucchini.
Researchers studied the genetic diversity within Cucurbita species to improve crop resilience.
Many varieties of Cucurbita are cultivated for their edible fruits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cucurbitaceae - the plant family containing gourds, squashes, and pumpkins

Etymology

From Latin cucurbita (gourd, pumpkin, squash).
