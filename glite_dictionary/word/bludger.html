bludger

1. NOUN

/ ˈblʌdʒər / (AmE), / ˈblʌdʒə / (BrE)
in the fictional sport of Quidditch, a ball that is hit by beaters and attempts to knock players off their brooms; in real-life Quidditch, a ball used similarly, often a slightly deflated dodgeball.
Quidditch ball hit by beaters

Examples

The beater swung his bat and sent the bludger flying toward the opposing team's seeker.
Three bludgers are used in Muggle Quidditch, and they are typically dodgeballs.
During practice, Alice narrowly avoided being hit by a speeding bludger.

Synset Examples

Beaters can try hitting the bludgers around in the air with a shortened hockey sticks or a short bat.
This guide suggests ways to play Harry Potter's game of Quidditch in real life (Muggle Quidditch), by optionally riding on brooms, using three deflated dodge balls as bludgers for beaters to bat at players, a volleyball as a quaffle for chasers to throw into goal hoops, and a coin, ball or a running person as the snitch, which possibly blindfolded seekers must find.
Place the quaffle and three bludgers in the middle of the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quaffle - ball used in Quidditch for scoring goals
  • Synonym: snitch - small ball in Quidditch that must be caught by seekers

Etymology

Popularized by J.K. Rowling's Harry Potter series (late 20th c.) as the name of a Quidditch ball; unrelated to Australian slang 'bludger'.
