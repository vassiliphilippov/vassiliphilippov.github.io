compressible

1. ADJ

/ kəmˈprɛsəbəl / (AmE), / kəmˈprɛsɪbəl / (BrE)
Able to be compressed or reduced in volume by pressure.
able to be compressed

Examples

Foam is highly compressible, making it ideal for cushioning material.
Air is a compressible fluid, unlike water which is nearly incompressible.
These materials remain compressible even under significant pressure.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: incompressible - not able to be compressed

Etymology

From 'compress' + '-ible', from Latin 'comprimere' (to press together).

2. ADJ

/ kəmˈprɛsəbəl / (AmE), / kəmˈprɛsəbəl / (BrE)
Capable of being compressed or made more compact.
able to be compressed

Examples

The foam is highly compressible and fits easily into small spaces.
Gases are generally more compressible than liquids.
She chose compressible materials for her travel pillow.

Synset Examples

compressible packing materials
a compressible box

Related Terms

  • Antonym: incompressible - not able to be compressed or made more compact

Etymology

From 'compress' + '-ible', from Latin 'comprimere' (to press together) and Latin '-ibilis' (able to be).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00516321-a
Synset POS: a
