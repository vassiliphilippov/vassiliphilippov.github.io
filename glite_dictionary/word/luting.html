luting

1. NOUN

/ ˈluːtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈluːtɪŋ / (BrE)
a substance used for packing a joint or coating a porous surface to make it impervious to gas or liquid
substance for sealing joints or surfaces

Examples

The engineer applied luting to the pipe joints to prevent any gas from leaking out.
Dental luting cements are used to fix crowns and bridges to teeth securely.
A layer of luting was spread over the surface to make it watertight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sealant - a substance used to block the passage of fluids

Etymology

From Latin 'lutum' (mud, clay), related to the act of sealing or coating with clay.
