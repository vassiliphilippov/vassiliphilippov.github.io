conglutination

1. NOUN

/ kɑnˌgluːtəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / kɒnˌgluːtɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the process in which the edges of a wound or broken bones grow together and heal
the growing together in healing of a wound or bones
medical

Examples

The surgeon observed the natural conglutination of the wound after several days.
In the case of minor fractures, conglutination may occur without surgical intervention.
Successful conglutination is essential to proper bone healing.

Etymology

From Latin 'conglutinatio', from 'conglutinare' meaning 'to glue together'.

2. NOUN

/ kənˌɡluːtəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / kɒnˌɡluːtɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of uniting or sticking together different things into a single body or group; the growing together of separate parts, especially in biology or medicine
union by sticking or growing together

Examples

Doctors studied the conglutination of tissues after the surgery.
Biologists observed the conglutination of single cells to form larger structures.
The team's success was a result of the conglutination of diverse talents and ideas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fusion - the process or result of joining two or more things together to form a single entity

Etymology

From Latin conglutinatio, from conglutinare 'to glue together', from con- 'together' + glutinare 'to glue'.
