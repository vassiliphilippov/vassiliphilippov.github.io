amiable

1. ADJ

/ ˈeɪmiəbəl / (AmE), / ˈeɪmiəbl / (BrE)
having or displaying a friendly and pleasant manner
friendly and pleasant in manner

Examples

Despite the tense situation, she remained amiable toward everyone in the room.
His amiable nature made him well-liked by colleagues and friends alike.
The host greeted each guest with an amiable smile that set everyone at ease.

Synset Examples

an affable smile
an amiable gathering
cordial relations
a cordial greeting
a genial host

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affable - friendly, easy to talk to
  • Synonym: cordial - warm and friendly
  • Antonym: hostile - unfriendly, antagonistic

Etymology

From Old French amiable, from Late Latin amicabilis (friendly), from Latin amicus (friend).

2. ADJ

/ ˈeɪmiəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈeɪmiəbl̩ / (BrE)
having a friendly and pleasant manner; easy to like
friendly and pleasant

Examples

Everyone enjoyed working with the amiable new manager.
Her amiable personality made her very popular among her classmates.
Even as an amiable rival, he was fiercely competitive on the field.

Synset Examples

an amiable villain with a cocky sidelong grin

Related Terms

  • Synonym: friendly - kind and pleasant in manner
  • Antonym: unfriendly - not friendly or pleasant; hostile

Etymology

From Late Latin amicabilis (friendly), from Latin amicus (friend).
