to specialise

1. VERB

/ ˈspɛʃəlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃəlaɪz / (BrE)
to concentrate on and become expert in a particular area of work, study, or activity
focus on a specific area or activity
british

Examples

After law school, she decided to specialise in environmental law.
Many doctors specialise in a particular branch of medicine, such as cardiology.
This company specialises in the restoration of historic buildings.

Synset Examples

She specializes in Near Eastern history

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to specialize - to focus on a particular area (American spelling)

Etymology

From Latin 'specialis' (particular), via Old French, related to 'special'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00438402-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈspɛʃəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃəlaɪz / (BrE)
(of an organism) to evolve or adapt so as to become particularly suited to a specific environment, role, or function.
evolve or adapt to a specific environment or role

Examples

Some insects specialise in feeding on a single type of plant.
Over many generations, the finches specialised for different food sources on the islands.
As the climate changed, the mammal population gradually specialised to survive in the new environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to adapt - to change so as to become suited to new conditions

Etymology

From Latin 'specialis' (particular, belonging to a kind or species), via French 'spécialiser'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00119074-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈspɛʃəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃəlaɪz / (BrE)
to adapt or concentrate on a specific purpose, area, or field; to make or become suited to a particular function or activity
focus or adapt to a special purpose

Examples

After her internship, she decided to specialise in environmental law.
Certain insects have specialised mouthparts for feeding on specific plants.
Our company specialises in custom software solutions for small businesses.

Synset Examples

specialize one's research
this kind of beak has become specialized in certain Galapagos finches

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to generalise - to make less specific, or broaden application rather than focus sharply

Etymology

From Latin specialis (particular), via Old French spécialiser (to make specific).
