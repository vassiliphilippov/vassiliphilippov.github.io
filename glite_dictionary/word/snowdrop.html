snowdrop

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnoʊˌdrɑp / (AmE), / ˈsnəʊˌdrɒp / (BrE)
A common anemone of eastern North America with solitary white flowers often tinged with pink.
North American anemone with white, pink-tinged flowers

Examples

In the woods, we found a single snowdrop blooming among the fallen leaves.
The snowdrop is cherished for its delicate, pink-tinged white petals.
Gardeners often plant snowdrops to add early spring color to their landscapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Anemone quinquefolia - The scientific name for the North American snowdrop anemone
  • Synonym: snowdrop (Galanthus) - European bulbous plant with drooping white flowers, also called snowdrop
