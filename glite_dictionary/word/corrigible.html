corrigible

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɔr.ɪ.dʒə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈkɒr.ɪ.dʒɪ.bəl / (BrE)
Capable of being corrected, improved, or set right.
able to be corrected or reformed

Examples

The committee considered the error a corrigible mistake that could be addressed in the next report.
Even the most corrigible behaviors may require time and patience to change.
The company assured customers that the software’s flaws were corrigible through a future update.

Synset Examples

a corrigible defect
a corrigible prisoner

Related Terms

  • Antonym: incorrigible - not able to be corrected or reformed
  • Synonym: rectifiable - able to be put right or corrected

Etymology

From Latin corrigibilis (that can be corrected), from corrigere (to correct).
