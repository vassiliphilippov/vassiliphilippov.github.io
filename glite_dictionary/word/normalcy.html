normalcy

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔrməlsi / (AmE), / ˈnɔːməlsi / (BrE)
the condition of being normal; the state of being within the usual range of functioning or circumstances
state of being normal

Examples

After months of chaos, the city finally returned to a sense of normalcy.
Many people are hoping for normalcy after the disruption caused by the pandemic.
The new medication helped restore normalcy to his daily life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: normality - the state of being normal
  • Antonym: abnormality - the state of being not normal

Etymology

Formed in the 19th century from 'normal' + '-cy', popularized by US President Warren G. Harding.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɔrməlsi / (AmE), / ˈnɔːməlsi / (BrE)
The state of being normal, usual, or expected; ordinariness or regularity.
state of being normal or usual

Examples

After the chaos, the community was eager to restore normalcy as quickly as possible.
Travel restrictions were lifted when normalcy returned to the country.
Many people longed for the normalcy of their pre-pandemic routines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: normality - the state of being normal; usual; expected

Etymology

Formed from 'normal' + '-cy', popularized in the early 20th century, especially by U.S. President Warren G. Harding.
