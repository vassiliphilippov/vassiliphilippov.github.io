oilfield

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔɪlˌfiːld / (AmE), / ˈɔɪlˌfiːld / (BrE)
an area of land or seabed that contains abundant petroleum deposits, especially one with active oil wells producing crude oil
area with oil deposits and wells

Examples

The company invested millions to redevelop the oilfield after new reserves were discovered.
Many communities in Texas have grown up around the oilfield industry.
After years of drilling, the once-prosperous oilfield began to show signs of decline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oil well - a well drilled to extract petroleum from underground
