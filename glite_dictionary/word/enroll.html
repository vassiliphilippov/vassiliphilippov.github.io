to enroll

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈroʊl / (AmE), / ɪnˈrəʊl / (BrE)
to officially register as a member or participant in a course, organization, or activity
register as a member or participant

Examples

I decided to enroll in a painting class this spring.
International students must enroll before the start of the term.
She enrolled her son in the local soccer league.

Synset Examples

The party recruited many new members

Related Terms

  • Synonym: register - to record or enroll formally
  • Antonym: to withdraw - to remove oneself from enrollment or participation

Etymology

From Old French 'enroller', from 'en-' (in) + 'rolle' (list).
