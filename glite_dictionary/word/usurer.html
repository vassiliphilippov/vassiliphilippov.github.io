usurer

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuːʒərər / (AmE), / ˈjuːʒərə / (BrE)
A person who lends money at unreasonably high rates of interest, often illegally.
person lending at extortionate interest

Examples

The desperate farmer borrowed money from a local usurer to save his crops.
In many stories, the usurer is a villain who exploits the poor.
Laws were enacted to limit the power of usurers in medieval Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moneylender - person who lends money at interest

Etymology

From Old French usurier, from Latin usurarius (interest-taker), from usura (interest).
