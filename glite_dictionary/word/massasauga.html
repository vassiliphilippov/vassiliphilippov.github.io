massasauga

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæsəˈsɔɡə / (AmE), / ˌmasəˈsɔːɡə / (BrE)
a small venomous rattlesnake native to North America, distinguished by its gray or light brown coloring with darker bands or spots
a small North American rattlesnake

Examples

The massasauga is most often found in wetlands and marshy areas.
Wildlife experts are working to protect the endangered massasauga from habitat loss.
I spotted a massasauga while hiking in Michigan last summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rattlesnake - a venomous American snake with a conspicuous rattle at the end of its tail

Etymology

From Ojibwa *mashkode:zaagi*, meaning 'great river-mouth'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmæsəˈsɔɡə / (AmE), / ˌmæsəˈsɔːɡə / (BrE)
a small venomous rattlesnake (Sistrurus catenatus) native to moist areas of North America, especially from the Great Lakes region to Mexico
North American pygmy rattlesnake

Examples

The massasauga prefers to live in wetlands and grasslands near the Great Lakes.
People are advised to be cautious when hiking in areas known to be inhabited by the massasauga.
The conservation program aims to protect the endangered massasauga and its habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rattlesnake - a venomous American snake with a rattling tail

Etymology

From Ojibwa (Anishinaabe) *massa'ki:sag*, meaning 'great river-mouth'.
