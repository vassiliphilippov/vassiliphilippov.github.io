painfully

1. ADV

/ ˈpeɪnfəli / (AmE), / ˈpeɪnfəli / (BrE)
In a way that involves or suggests pain; with physical or emotional pain.
with or as if with pain

Examples

She walked painfully across the room after her injury.
He spoke painfully about his difficult childhood.
The decision to resign was painfully obvious to everyone involved.

Synset Examples

she moved painfully forward
sorely wounded

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sorely - in a severe or distressing way; very much

Etymology

From 'painful' + '-ly'. 'Painful' from Middle English, from 'pain' + '-ful'.

2. ADV

/ ˈpeɪnfəli / (AmE), / ˈpeɪnfəli / (BrE)
In a way that is extremely unpleasant, distressing, or causing discomfort.
in an extremely unpleasant or distressing way

Examples

Her mistake was painfully obvious to everyone in the room.
The meeting dragged on painfully for hours without any progress.
He was painfully aware of his lack of experience in the new job.

Synset Examples

his ignorance was painfully obvious

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pleasantly - in a pleasant or enjoyable way
  • Degree Higher: excruciatingly - to an unbearably intense or severe degree
  • Degree Lower: slightly - to a small degree

Etymology

From 'painful' + '-ly'. 'Painful' from Old French *peine* (pain, punishment) and Latin *poena* (penalty).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00115516-r
Synset POS: r
