to excavate

1. VERB

/ ˈɛk.sə.veɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛk.sə.veɪt / (BrE)
to recover or remove something from the ground by digging
to recover by digging

Examples

Archaeologists plan to excavate the ancient temple site next summer.
The team hopes to excavate precious artifacts buried for centuries under the sand.
Miners excavate gold from deep beneath the mountain.

Synset Examples

Schliemann excavated Troy
excavate gold

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dig up - to remove something from the earth by digging

Etymology

From Latin excavare, from ex- (out) + cavare (to make hollow), related to cavus (hollow).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02148002-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈɛkskəˌveɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛkskəveɪt / (BrE)
to find or uncover something by digging in the ground, especially during an archaeological investigation
find by digging in the ground

Examples

The team hopes to excavate ancient tools at the new site.
Archaeologists were able to excavate several priceless artifacts from the ruins.
They plan to excavate a small area of the backyard in search of old bottles.

Synset Examples

I dug up an old box in the garden

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dig up - to find something by digging in the ground

Etymology

From Latin excavare, from ex- (out) + cavare (to make hollow).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01316583-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈɛkskəˌveɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛkskəveɪt / (BrE)
to form a space or hole by hollowing out; to remove earth or material to create a cavity or depression
to form by hollowing out

Examples

The workers used heavy machinery to excavate a large pit for the new building foundation.
Over centuries, the river gradually excavated a deep gorge through the landscape.
They plan to excavate a tunnel through the mountains to improve transportation.

Synset Examples

Carnegie had a lake excavated for Princeton University's rowing team
excavate a cavity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dig - to break up and move earth, similar process to excavate

Etymology

From Latin excavare, from ex- (out) + cavare (to make hollow), from cavus (hollow).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01314038-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈɛk.səˌveɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛk.skəˌveɪt / (BrE)
to remove the inner part or core of something, often by digging or hollowing out
remove or dig out the core

Examples

The engineers will excavate a tunnel through the mountain to build the new railway.
They needed to excavate the hillside before they could lay the foundation for the road.
Archaeologists carefully excavate ancient sites to discover hidden artifacts.

Synset Examples

the mining company wants to excavate the hillside

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dig - to break up and move earth, or to make a hole by doing this

Etymology

From Latin excavatus, past participle of excavare (‘to hollow out’), from ex- (‘out’) + cavare (‘to make hollow’)
