to suffocate

1. VERB

/ ˈsʌfəˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsʌfəˌkeɪt / (BrE)
to die or cause someone to die from lack of air or inability to breathe
to prevent breathing; cause to suffocate

Examples

The smoke from the burning building began to suffocate the trapped residents.
He tried to suffocate the fire with a blanket to cut off its air supply.
If you block their airways, fish can easily suffocate out of water.

Synset Examples

The foul air was slowly suffocating the children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to smother - to kill by covering the nose and mouth, preventing breathing

Etymology

From Latin suffocare (to choke, stifle), from sub- (below) + fauces (throat).
