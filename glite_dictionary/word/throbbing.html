throbbing

1. NOUN

/ ˈθrɑːbɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθrɒbɪŋ / (BrE)
a strong, rhythmic, vibrating sound or beat
strong rhythmic sound or beat

Examples

The throbbing of drums filled the concert hall.
She was lulled to sleep by the gentle throbbing of the boat's engine.
We listened to the distant throbbing of the city late at night.

Synset Examples

the throbbing of the engines

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pulsation - a rhythmic, throbbing or vibrating motion or sound

2. NOUN

/ ˈθrɑbɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθrɒbɪŋ / (BrE)
a rapid, strong, and repeated pulsing or beating, especially as felt in the body
rapid, strong pulsing or beating

Examples

He woke up with a dull throbbing in his temples after the long night.
The sudden throbbing in her wrist made her realize she had sprained it.
A persistent throbbing behind his eyes warned him of an approaching migraine.

Synset Examples

he felt a throbbing in his head

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pulse - the regular beating of the heart or arteries
  • Antonym: ache - a continuous or prolonged pain

Etymology

From the verb 'throb,' of imitative origin, referring to pulsating movement or sensation.

3. ADJ

/ ˈθrɑbɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθrɒbɪŋ / (BrE)
pounding or beating with a strong, regular rhythm, especially so as to be felt or heard; pulsating intensely.
pounding or pulsating strongly

Examples

She felt a throbbing pain in her temples after the long meeting.
The throbbing bass from the speakers filled the room with energy.
He pressed his hand to his throbbing wound as he waited for help.

Synset Examples

a throbbing pain
the throbbing engine of the boat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pulsating - expanding and contracting with a strong regular movement
  • Synonym: beating - striking repeatedly with a regular rhythm

Etymology

Present participle of 'throb', which likely derives from Middle English 'throbben'.
