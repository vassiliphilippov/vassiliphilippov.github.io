Soleirolia soleirolii

1. NOUN

/ soʊˌleɪroʊliə soʊˌleɪroʊlaɪ / (AmE), / səʊˌleɪərəʊliːə səʊˌleɪərəʊliːaɪ / (BrE)
A creeping perennial plant from Corsica with small, round, moss-like leaves, often grown as an ornamental ground cover; also known as baby's tears.
creeping ornamental plant; baby's tears

Examples

The delicate foliage of Soleirolia soleirolii makes it a popular choice for terrariums and indoor gardens.
Gardeners often use Soleirolia soleirolii as ground cover due to its fast-growing, moss-like appearance.
I planted Soleirolia soleirolii around the edges of the patio for a lush, green border.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baby's tears - Common name for Soleirolia soleirolii, a creeping ornamental plant

Etymology

Named after French army engineer Joseph François Soleirol, who collected the species.
