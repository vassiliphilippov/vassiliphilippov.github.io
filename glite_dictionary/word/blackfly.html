blackfly

1. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈblækˌflʌɪ / (BrE)
a small, dark aphid that infests plants such as beans and sugar beets, often causing damage to crops
a dark aphid that infests crops

Examples

The bean plants were covered with blackfly by mid-summer, stunting their growth.
Gardeners tried various methods to control the blackfly infestation on their sugar beets.
A severe outbreak of blackfly can significantly reduce crop yields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aphid - a small sap-sucking insect

2. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈblækˌflaɪ / (BrE)
A small, dark-colored biting fly with aquatic larvae, known for sucking the blood of birds and mammals, including humans.
biting fly with aquatic larvae

Examples

In early summer, hikers often complain about being bitten by the blackfly near rivers and streams.
The blackfly population increased after several weeks of heavy rain flooded the area.
Birds sometimes suffer from blackfly bites, just like humans do.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mosquito - a small insect that feeds on blood and can carry disease
  • Synonym: gnat - a small biting fly
