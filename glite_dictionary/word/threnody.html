threnody

1. NOUN

/ ˈθrɛnədi / (AmE), / ˈθrɛnədi / (BrE)
a song, poem, or piece of music expressing mourning or lamentation for the dead
song or poem of mourning for the dead

Examples

The orchestra performed a moving threnody in memory of the composer.
Her latest poem is a poignant threnody for her lost childhood friend.
As the funeral ended, a solemn threnody echoed through the hall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elegy - a poem of serious reflection, often for the dead
  • Synonym: dirge - a lament for the dead, especially one forming part of a funeral rite

Etymology

From Greek 'threnōidia' (funeral song), from 'threnos' (lament) + 'oide' (song).
