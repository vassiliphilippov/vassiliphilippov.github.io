mudcat

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌd.kæt / (AmE), / ˈmʌd.kæt / (BrE)
A large catfish found in the central United States, known for its broad, flattened head and protruding lower jaw.
large catfish of central US

Examples

Many anglers come to the river hoping to catch a giant mudcat during the summer months.
A single mudcat can weigh over fifty pounds in this lake.
The mudcat is known for its whiskered face and muddy habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catfish - a type of freshwater fish with whisker-like barbels around the mouth

Etymology

From 'mud' and 'cat', referencing the fish's habitat and resemblance to a cat (catfish).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmʌdˌkæt / (AmE), / ˈmʌdˌkat / (BrE)
the flesh of a scaleless food fish, often farmed, found especially in the southern United States and commonly used as food; another name for certain types of catfish
edible catfish of the southern US

Examples

The restaurant specializes in fried mudcat, prepared with traditional southern spices.
Local fishermen supply fresh mudcat to the market every morning.
I ordered grilled mudcat and hush puppies for dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catfish - any of numerous mostly freshwater fish with whisker-like barbels around the mouth

Etymology

From 'mud' + 'cat', alluding to the fish's habitat and catfish family.
