Linanthus dichotomus

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈnænθəs daɪˈkɒtəməs / (AmE), / lɪˈnanθəs daɪˈkɒtəməs / (BrE)
A small annual plant native to California, bearing white flowers; commonly known as evening snow or fragrant linanthus.
California annual plant with white flowers

Examples

The delicate blooms of Linanthus dichotomus are a common sight in California in late spring.
Gardeners appreciate Linanthus dichotomus for its fragrant white flowers and ease of cultivation.
On our hike, we spotted several patches of Linanthus dichotomus along the trail.

Etymology

From Latinized botanical naming: genus 'Linanthus' from Greek 'linon' (flax) + 'anthos' (flower); species 'dichotomus' meaning 'forked' or 'divided in two'.
