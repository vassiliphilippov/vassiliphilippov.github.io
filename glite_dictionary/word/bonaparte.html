Bonaparte

1. PROPN

/ ˈboʊnəˌpɑrt / (AmE), / ˈbəʊnəˌpɑːt / (BrE)
Napoleon Bonaparte (1769–1821), French general and emperor, famed for his military campaigns and rule over France.
French general and emperor Napoleon (1769–1821)

Examples

Many historians have debated whether Bonaparte was a hero or a tyrant.
The defeat of Bonaparte at Waterloo marked the end of his reign.
A biography of Bonaparte reveals much about European history.

Etymology

From Italian Bonaparte, meaning ‘good part’.
