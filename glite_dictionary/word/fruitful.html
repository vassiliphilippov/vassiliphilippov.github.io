fruitful

1. ADJ

/ ˈfrut.fəl / (AmE), / ˈfruːt.f(ə)l / (BrE)
producing good results; productive or conducive to producing in abundance.
productive; yielding results or abundance

Examples

The meeting was very fruitful and led to several new initiatives.
Her years of fruitful research changed the field of medicine.
We hope the discussion will prove fruitful for both parties.

Synset Examples

be fruitful and multiply

Related Terms

  • Synonym: productive - producing or able to produce large amounts or results
  • Antonym: barren - not productive or unable to produce results

Etymology

From Old English 'frȳt' (fruit) + '-ful'.
