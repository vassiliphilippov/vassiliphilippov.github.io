proliferation

1. NOUN

/ prəˌlɪfəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / prəˌlɪfəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a rapid increase in the number or amount of something, especially something undesirable or harmful
rapid increase in number or amount

Examples

The proliferation of nuclear weapons is a major concern for global security.
There has been a proliferation of online news sources in recent years.
Scientists are studying the proliferation of invasive species in the ecosystem.

Synset Examples

the proliferation of nuclear weapons

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expansion - the act of becoming greater in size, number, or importance
  • Antonym: reduction - the act of making something smaller or less in amount

Etymology

From French 'prolifération', from 'proliférer' (to proliferate), from Latin 'proles' (offspring) + 'ferre' (to bear).

2. NOUN

/ prəˌlɪfəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / prəˌlɪfəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
rapid increase in number or amount, especially through quick multiplication or spreading
rapid increase or multiplication

Examples

The proliferation of new technologies has transformed the way we communicate.
There has been a proliferation of coffee shops in the downtown area over the past decade.
Experts are concerned about the proliferation of nuclear weapons in unstable regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: increase - an instance of growing in number or size
  • Antonym: decline - a decrease or reduction in the number or amount

Etymology

From French prolifération, from proliférer (to proliferate), ultimately from Latin proles (offspring) + -fer (bearing).
