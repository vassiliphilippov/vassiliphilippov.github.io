oak

1. NOUN

/ oʊk / (AmE), / əʊk / (BrE)
A large deciduous or evergreen tree of the genus Quercus, typically having lobed leaves and bearing acorns.
a tree with lobed leaves and acorns

Examples

The old oak in the garden provides shade during the summer months.
Many traditional barrels are made from oak wood, prized for its strength and flavor.
The forest was composed mainly of towering oak and beech trees.

Synset Examples

great oaks grow from little acorns

Related Terms

  • Antonym: maple - a deciduous tree with lobed leaves, known for its sap and wood
  • Antonym: ash - a deciduous tree with pinnate leaves and winged seeds

Etymology

Old English *āc*, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch *eik* and German *Eiche*.

2. NOUN

/ oʊk / (AmE), / əʊk / (BrE)
the hard, durable wood of the oak tree, commonly used for furniture and flooring
hard wood from the oak tree

Examples

The table is made of solid oak and has lasted for generations.
They chose oak flooring for its durability and rich color.
A bookshelf crafted from oak gives the room a classic look.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maple - wood from the maple tree, used for furniture and flooring
  • Synonym: pine - wood from the pine tree, used for building and carpentry

Etymology

From Old English *āc*, related to other Germanic languages (e.g., Dutch *eik*, German *Eiche*).
