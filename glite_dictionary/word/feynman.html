Feynman

1. PROPN

/ ˈfaɪnmən / (AmE), / ˈfaɪnmən / (BrE)
(1918–1988) US physicist known for his work on quantum electrodynamics and for popularizing science.
US physicist; Nobel laureate

Examples

Richard Feynman won the Nobel Prize in Physics for his pioneering work in quantum electrodynamics.
Many students have been inspired by Feynman's engaging lecturing style and curiosity.
The Feynman diagrams are widely used tools in particle physics.
