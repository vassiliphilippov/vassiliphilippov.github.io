gateway

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡeɪtˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪt.weɪ / (BrE)
An entrance, typically one that can be closed with a gate, often marking the access to a particular area.
entrance that can be closed by a gate

Examples

They walked through the old stone gateway into the town square.
The large wooden gateway at the entrance of the estate was locked at night.
A beautiful archway served as a gateway to the botanical gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gate - a movable barrier for closing an opening in a fence, wall, or passage

Etymology

From Middle English gatewaye, formed from 'gate' + 'way'.
