Monocotyledones

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːnoʊˌkɑːtɪlɪˈdoʊniːz / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəʊˌkɒtɪlɪˈdəʊniːz / (BrE)
A major group of flowering plants whose seeds have a single cotyledon (seed leaf), typically with parallel-veined leaves; includes grasses, lilies, palms, and orchids.
group of flowering plants with a single seed leaf

Examples

All grasses belong to the Monocotyledones, distinguished by their single seed leaf and parallel leaf veins.
Unlike the dicots, Monocotyledones display scattered vascular bundles within their stems.
Orchid flowers are a prime example of the diversity found within the Monocotyledones.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dicotyledon - plant with two cotyledons in the seed

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'monos' (single) + 'kotyledon' (cup-shaped hollow, seed leaf).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːnoʊˌkɑːtɪlɪˈdoʊniːz / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəkɒtɪlɪˈdəʊniːz / (BrE)
A large group of flowering plants whose seeds contain a single embryonic leaf or cotyledon, typically having parallel-veined leaves, including grasses, lilies, palms, and orchids.
group of flowering plants with single cotyledon
medical

Examples

Many common crops, such as wheat and rice, belong to the Monocotyledones.
The distinctive leaf patterns are a hallmark of the Monocotyledones.
Palms, orchids, and lilies are all included in the Monocotyledones.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dicotyledon - flowering plant with two embryonic seed leaves

Etymology

From Greek 'mono-' (single) + 'kotyledon' (cup-shaped cavity, seed leaf).
