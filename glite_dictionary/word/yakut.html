Yakut

1. NOUN

/ jɑːˈkut / (AmE), / jæˈkuːt / (BrE)
the Turkic language spoken by the Yakut people of northeastern Siberia
Turkic language of the Yakut people

Examples

She is fluent in several languages, including Yakut.
The Yakut language has many unique sounds not found in English.
Linguists have studied Yakut to trace the development of Turkic languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sakha - another name for the Yakut language and the Yakut people

Etymology

From Russian Якут (Yakut), the native name is Sakha.

2. NOUN

/ jɑːˈkuːt / (AmE), / jæˈkuːt / (BrE)
a member of a Turkic ethnic group native to northeastern Siberia, chiefly in the Lena river basin.
a Turkic ethnic group of Siberia

Examples

The Yakut are known for their traditional reindeer herding in the harsh Siberian climate.
She has spent years studying the folklore of the Yakut people.
A Yakut elder shared stories about life along the Lena river.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sakha - another name for the Yakut people and their language

Etymology

From Russian Якут (Yakut), ultimately from their self-designation саха (Sakha).
