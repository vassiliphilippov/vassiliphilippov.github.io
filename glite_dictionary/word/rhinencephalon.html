rhinencephalon

1. NOUN

/ raɪˌnɛnˈsɛfəlɒn / (AmE), / raɪˌnɛnˈsɛfəlɒn / (BrE)
a part of the brain within the cerebral hemispheres associated primarily with the sense of smell, especially developed in some animals and linked to emotional behavior in humans
part of brain linked to smell and emotion
medical

Examples

The rhinencephalon is more prominent in animals that rely heavily on their sense of smell.
Damage to the rhinencephalon can affect both olfactory perception and emotional responses.
Researchers studied the rhinencephalon to better understand its role in human emotion.

Etymology

From Greek 'rhinos' (nose) + 'enkephalos' (brain).
