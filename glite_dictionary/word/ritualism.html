ritualism

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪtʃuəlˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈrɪtʃuəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the study or use of religious or magical rites and ceremonies, often with excessive emphasis on prescribed forms
study or use of religious or magical rites

Examples

The anthropologist devoted his life to the study of ritualism in ancient cultures.
Some critics argue that organized religion is often reduced to mere ritualism without genuine faith.
Her fascination with ritualism led her to participate in ceremonies from many different traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ritual - a religious or solemn ceremony consisting of a series of actions performed according to a prescribed order

Etymology

From ritual + -ism, modeled after similar English formations such as 'spiritualism'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɪtʃuəlɪz(ə)m / (AmE), / ˈrɪtjʊəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
excessive or undue emphasis on rituals or formal procedures, especially in religious worship
excessive emphasis on rituals in worship

Examples

Some churches have been criticized for their ritualism and lack of genuine spiritual engagement.
The ceremony was filled with so much ritualism that the original meaning seemed lost.
Opponents argued that ritualism in the church distracted from its moral teachings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: formalism - strict adherence to prescribed forms or procedures

Etymology

From 'ritual' + '-ism', first used in the mid-19th century.
