immobilizing

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmoʊbəˌlaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪˈməʊbəˌlaɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of preventing movement or making something incapable of movement
the act of making something immobile

Examples

The patient's injury required immediate immobilizing to prevent further damage.
Effective immobilizing of the vehicle was crucial after the accident.
During surgery, immobilizing the area is essential for precision.

Synset Examples

the storm caused complete immobilization of the rescue team

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immobilization - the process of making immobile
  • Antonym: motion - the act of changing location or position

Etymology

From the verb 'immobilize' + -ing, formed from Latin 'immobilis' (not movable).
