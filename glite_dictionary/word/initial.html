initial

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈnɪʃəl / (AmE), / ɪˈnɪʃəl / (BrE)
the first letter of a word, especially used to refer to the first letter of a person's name.
first letter of a word or name

Examples

Please write your initial at the top of each page.
Her middle initial is M, for Marie.
Many documents require you to sign your full name, not just your initial.

Synset Examples

he refused to put the initials FRS after his name

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monogram - a motif made by overlapping or combining two or more letters, usually a person's initials

Etymology

From Latin initialis, from initium (beginning).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06844047-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈnɪʃəl / (AmE), / ɪˈnɪʃəl / (BrE)
existing or occurring at the beginning; first or earliest in a sequence or process.
occurring at the beginning

Examples

She made an initial investment before deciding to expand the business further.
The initial findings suggest that the project is feasible.
After the initial shock, he quickly regained his composure.

Synset Examples

took the initial step toward reconciliation

Related Terms

  • Antonym: final - coming at the end; last in a sequence
  • Degree Lower: preliminary - preceding or done in preparation for something fuller or more important

Etymology

From Latin initialis (of the beginning), from initium (beginning).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01015570-s
Synset POS: s

3. VERB

/ ɪˈnɪʃ.əl / (AmE), / ɪˈnɪʃ.əl / (BrE)
to mark or sign something with one's initials, typically to show approval or authorization
mark or sign with initials

Examples

Please initial each page of the contract to confirm you have read it.
All employees must initial the attendance sheet every morning.
She initialed the correction to indicate her approval of the change.

Etymology

From the noun 'initial', ultimately from Latin 'initialis' (of a beginning).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00999352-v
Synset POS: v
