Lasthenia chrysostoma

1. NOUN

/ læsˈθiːniə kraɪˈsɒstəˌmə / (AmE), / lasˈθiːniə kraɪˈsɒstəmə / (BrE)
A small, slender, woolly annual plant with very narrow opposite leaves and branches bearing solitary golden-yellow flower heads, native to southwestern Oregon to Baja California and Arizona and often cultivated.
a small annual plant with golden-yellow flowers

Examples

Lasthenia chrysostoma is commonly found in grasslands through California and nearby states.
The golden blooms of Lasthenia chrysostoma add a splash of color to meadows in the spring.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate Lasthenia chrysostoma for its delicate appearance and drought tolerance.

Etymology

Genus name from Ancient Greek 'Lasthenia' (a woman's name); species from Greek 'chrysos' (gold) and 'stoma' (mouth).
