edible

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛdəbəl / (AmE), / ˈɛdɪbəl / (BrE)
a substance that can be eaten, especially something regarded as suitable or safe to eat
something that can be eaten

Examples

Wild mushrooms should never be eaten unless you are certain they are edibles.
The shop sells several types of cannabis edibles, including brownies and gummies.
During the survival course, we learned how to identify safety and prepare forest edibles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: food - any nutritious substance that people or animals eat or drink
  • Antonym: poison - a substance that is harmful or fatal if eaten

Etymology

From Latin *edibilis* (edible, eatable), from *edere* (to eat).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɛdəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈɛdəbl̩ / (BrE)
safe or suitable to be eaten; fit for human consumption
suitable to be eaten

Examples

The berries in this forest are not edible and can make you sick if you eat them.
Are these mushrooms edible, or should we avoid them?
Some flowers are actually edible and can be used to decorate salads.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inedible - not fit to be eaten
  • Synonym: eatable - able to be eaten, palatable but not necessarily good

Etymology

From Latin edibilis (‘eatable’), from edere (‘to eat’).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a
