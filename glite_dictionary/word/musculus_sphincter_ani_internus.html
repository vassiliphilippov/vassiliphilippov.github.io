musculus sphincter ani internus

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌskjələs ˈsfɪŋktər ˈænaɪ ɪnˈtɜrnəs / (AmE), / ˈmʌskjʊləs ˈsfɪŋktə ˈani ɪnˈtɜːnəs / (BrE)
the internal ring of smooth muscle formed by circular fibers at the end of the rectum, responsible for involuntary control of defecation
internal anal sphincter muscle
medical

Examples

The musculus sphincter ani internus is responsible for maintaining resting tone in the anal canal.
During rectal surgery, care must be taken not to damage the musculus sphincter ani internus.
Disorders affecting the musculus sphincter ani internus can lead to problems with involuntary release of stool.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: external anal sphincter - the muscle surrounding the outside of the anal canal, under voluntary control

Etymology

From Latin: musculus (muscle), sphincter (binder), ani (of the anus), internus (internal).
