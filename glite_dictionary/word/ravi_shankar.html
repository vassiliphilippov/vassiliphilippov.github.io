Ravi Shankar

1. PROPN

/ ˌrɑːvi ˈʃɑːŋkɑːr / (AmE), / ˌrɑːvi ˈʃæŋkɑː / (BrE)
(1920–2012) An Indian sitar virtuoso and composer who played a key role in popularizing classical Indian music in the West.
Indian sitar player and composer

Examples

Ravi Shankar collaborated with George Harrison to bring Indian music to a global audience.
Many Western musicians cite Ravi Shankar as an inspiration for their own work.
A performance by Ravi Shankar is often credited with introducing the sitar to the mainstream West.
