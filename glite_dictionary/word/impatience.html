impatience

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpeɪʃəns / (AmE), / ɪmˈpeɪʃəns / (BrE)
a feeling of annoyance or restlessness caused by having to wait or by a delay
annoyance or restlessness due to delay

Examples

Her impatience was obvious as she tapped her foot and glanced at her watch repeatedly.
During the long meeting, his impatience started to show in his tone of voice.
The children showed their impatience as they waited for the movie to begin.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: patience - the capacity to accept or tolerate delay without getting angry or upset

Etymology

From Latin 'impatientia' (lack of patience), from 'im-' (not) + 'patientia' (patience).

2. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpeɪʃəns / (AmE), / ɪmˈpeɪʃəns / (BrE)
a restless desire for change or excitement, especially when unable to wait calmly for something to happen
restless desire for change or excitement

Examples

The impatience in his voice revealed his eagerness for something new to happen.
After months of routine, a sense of impatience with daily life set in.
Their impatience made them constantly seek out new adventures and experiences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restlessness - the inability to remain at rest, a need for movement or change

Etymology

From Old French 'impacience', from Latin 'impatientia' (impatience), from 'im-' (not) + 'patientia' (patience).

3. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpeɪʃəns / (AmE), / ɪmˈpeɪʃəns / (BrE)
the feeling of being unable or unwilling to wait for something or tolerate delay, often resulting in irritation or restlessness
lack of patience; irritation at delay

Examples

She tapped her fingers with visible impatience while waiting for the bus.
His impatience with slow service was obvious to everyone at the table.
Children often show impatience when they have to wait for a treat.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: patience - the ability to wait calmly for something without frustration
  • Synonym: irritation - the state of being annoyed or slightly angry

Etymology

From Latin 'impatientia' (impatience), from 'im-' (not) + 'patientia' (patience).
