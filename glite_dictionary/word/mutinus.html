Mutinus

1. NOUN

/ mjuˈtaɪnəs / (AmE), / mjuːˈtɪnəs / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Phallaceae, characterized by their phallic fruiting bodies.
genus of fungi in Phallaceae

Examples

The rare Mutinus fungus can be found in decaying woodlands during certain months.
Researchers documented several species within the Mutinus genus in the forest survey.
The distinctive shape of Mutinus fruiting bodies makes them easy to identify.

Etymology

New Latin; genus name formed according to scientific naming conventions.

2. NOUN

/ mjuːˈtaɪnəs / (AmE), / mjuːˈtaɪnəs / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Phallaceae, known for their often foul-smelling, finger-like fruiting bodies.
a genus of stinkhorn fungi

Examples

The species Mutinus caninus is commonly found in woodlands across Europe.
Several mushrooms in the genus Mutinus emit a strong odor to attract insects for spore dispersal.
Researchers recently discovered a new member of the Mutinus genus in South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Phallaceae - the family of fungi to which Mutinus belongs

Etymology

From Latin 'mutinus', a minor Roman god associated with fertility, due to the phallic shape of the fungi.
