to anaesthetize

1. VERB

/ əˈnɛsθəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / əˈniːsθətaɪz / (BrE)
to administer an anesthetic drug to someone in order to induce a loss of sensation or consciousness, typically before a medical procedure
induce loss of sensation (medically)
medical british

Examples

The dentist will anaesthetize your jaw before beginning the tooth extraction.
Doctors must carefully anaesthetize patients to ensure they feel no pain during surgery.
She was anaesthetized for the knee operation to prevent discomfort.

Synset Examples

The patient must be anesthetized before the operation
anesthetize the gum before extracting the teeth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to anesthetize - American spelling of 'anaesthetize'

Etymology

From Greek an- (without) + aisthēsis (sensation), via French and medical Latin.
