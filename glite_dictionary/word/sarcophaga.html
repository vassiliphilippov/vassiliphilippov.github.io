Sarcophaga

1. NOUN

/ sɑrˈkɑfəɡə / (AmE), / sɑːˈkɒfəɡə / (BrE)
a genus of flesh flies, known for their larvae that feed on decaying organic matter or carrion.
a genus of flesh flies

Examples

The larvae of many Sarcophaga species are important in forensic investigations.
Researchers studied the life cycle of Sarcophaga to understand decomposition rates.
You can often identify a Sarcophaga by the characteristic stripes on its thorax.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flesh fly - a common name for flies of the genus Sarcophaga and related genera

Etymology

From Greek 'sarkos' (flesh) + 'phagein' (to eat).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɑːrkəˈfeɪɡə / (AmE), / ˌsɑːkəˈfeɪɡə / (BrE)
A genus of flesh flies, characterized by their association with decaying matter and known for larvae that feed on carrion.
a genus of flesh flies

Examples

Researchers discovered that Sarcophaga larvae played a vital role in decomposing animal remains.
The specimen was identified as a member of the Sarcophaga genus due to its distinct striped thorax.
Some species within Sarcophaga are used in forensic entomology to estimate time of death.

Etymology

From Greek sarkophagos, meaning 'flesh-eating'.
