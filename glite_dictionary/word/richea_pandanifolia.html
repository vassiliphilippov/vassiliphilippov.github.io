Richea pandanifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːkiə ˌpændəniˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˈriːkiə ˌpændəniˈfəʊliə / (BrE)
A tall, slender evergreen shrub or tree native to Tasmania, with long, narrow, tapering leaves measuring 3 to 5 feet in length.
Tasmanian shrubby tree with long tapering leaves

Examples

The dense undergrowth included the unusual Richea pandanifolia with its sword-like foliage.
Botanists admired the towering Richea pandanifolia in the Tasmanian highlands.
Hikers often spot Richea pandanifolia growing along the wet slopes of the island's mountains.

Etymology

From genus name 'Richea', honoring Claude Antoine Gaspard Riche; species name from Latin 'pandanus' (referring to screw pine) and 'folia' (leaves).
