Pistia stratiotes

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɪstiə strætiˈoʊtiːz / (AmE), / ˌpɪstiə strætiˈəʊtiːz / (BrE)
A pantropical floating aquatic plant, also known as water lettuce, with rosettes of wedge-shaped leaves; often found as a weed in rivers and lakes.
floating aquatic plant (water lettuce)

Examples

Pistia stratiotes can rapidly cover the surface of ponds, often choking out native species.
Botanists studied the spread of Pistia stratiotes in tropical waterways.
The removal of Pistia stratiotes is necessary to maintain healthy aquatic ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water lettuce - common name for Pistia stratiotes, a floating aquatic plant

Etymology

From Greek 'pistia' (water plant) and 'stratiotes' (soldier), referring to the plant's clustered appearance.

a pantropical floating aquatic plant, also known as water lettuce, with rosettes of wedge-shaped leaves, often found as a weed in rivers and lakes
pantropical floating aquatic plant (water lettuce)

Examples

The rapid growth of Pistia stratiotes can choke waterways and disrupt aquatic ecosystems.
We found Pistia stratiotes covering the surface of the local lake during our field survey.
Efforts to remove Pistia stratiotes have increased in areas where it is considered an invasive species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water lettuce - common name for Pistia stratiotes, floating aquatic plant

Etymology

From Latin; genus name 'Pistia', species 'stratiotes' meaning 'soldier' or 'of a soldier', referring to the plant's rosette formation.
