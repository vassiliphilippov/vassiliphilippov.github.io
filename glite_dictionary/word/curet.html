curet

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjʊrɛt / (AmE), / ˈkjʊərɛt / (BrE)
A surgical instrument with a scoop-shaped end, used to remove tissue from a bodily cavity.
surgical instrument for scraping tissue
medical

Examples

The surgeon used a curet to carefully remove the abnormal tissue.
A dental curet is often employed to clean plaque from beneath the gums.
After the biopsy, the doctor cleaned the area with a curet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curette - alternative spelling of curet, surgical instrument for scraping tissue

Etymology

From French *curette*, diminutive of *curer* (to clean).
