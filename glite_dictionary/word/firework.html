firework

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪɚˌwɝk / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəwɜːk / (BrE)
A device containing combustible chemicals that produces a display of light, color, and sound when ignited, often used for entertainment or signaling.
explosive device for light and color display

Examples

The city organized a spectacular firework show for New Year's Eve.
Children watched in awe as colorful fireworks lit up the night sky.
Each firework exploded with a unique pattern of lights and sounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pyrotechnic - relating to fireworks or the art of making them

Etymology

From early modern English 'fire' + 'work', meaning 'pyrotechnic display'.
