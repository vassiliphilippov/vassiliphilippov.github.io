bloated

1. ADJ

/ ˈbloʊ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈbləʊ.tɪd / (BrE)
(of a part of the body) abnormally swollen with fluid or gas.
abnormally swollen, especially from fluid or gas

Examples

After the large meal, Maria felt bloated and uncomfortable all evening.
The vet said the dog's stomach was dangerously bloated and needed immediate attention.
Pictures from the famine showed children with bloated bellies due to malnutrition.

Synset Examples

hungry children with bloated stomachs
he had a grossly distended stomach
eyes with puffed (or puffy) lids
swollen hands
tumescent tissue
puffy tumid flesh

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swollen - enlarged or puffed up, often due to fluid
  • Synonym: distended - enlarged or expanded from internal pressure
  • Antonym: flat - not swollen or distended; normal in size

Etymology

From the past participle of 'bloat', probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Old Norse 'blautr' (soft, soaked).
