Pygopodidae

1. NOUN

/ paɪˌɡɑːpəˈdɪdiː / (AmE), / paɪˌɡɒpəˈdɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of legless lizards native to Australia and Tasmania, resembling snakes but distinguished by their internal anatomy and certain external features.
family of legless Australian lizards

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species within the Pygopodidae family in Western Australia.
Unlike snakes, members of Pygopodidae have remnants of hind limbs and external ear openings.
The Pygopodidae family demonstrates remarkable adaptation to their arid environments.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Pygopodidae', based on Greek 'pygē' (rump) + 'pous, podos' (foot), referring to their vestigial limbs.
