sob

1. NOUN

/ sɑb / (AmE), / sɒb / (BrE)
a convulsive catching of the breath when crying or weeping
convulsive gasp during crying

Examples

She let out a loud sob as the news sank in.
A quiet sob escaped from the back of the room during the speech.
He tried to hold back a sob but couldn't control his emotions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cry - to shed tears, especially as an expression of emotion
  • Antonym: laugh - to make sounds and movements of the face and body that are expressions of amusement or happiness

Etymology

From Middle English sobben, possibly imitative in origin.

2. VERB

/ sɑb / (AmE), / sɒb / (BrE)
to cry noisily, taking sudden, sharp breaths, especially because of strong emotion
cry noisily with deep breaths

Examples

She began to sob uncontrollably when she heard the news.
He sobbing quietly in the corner, tears streaming down his face.
After the movie ended, many people in the theater were sobbing openly.

Synset Examples

He was sobbing inconsolably

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to weep - to shed tears; to cry, especially quietly
  • Antonym: laugh - to make the spontaneous sounds and movements of the face and body that are the instinctive expressions of lively amusement

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin, echoing the sound made while sobbing.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
