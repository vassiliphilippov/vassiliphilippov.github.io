encyclopaedist

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˌsaɪkləˈpidɪst / (AmE), / ɪnˌsʌɪklɪəˈpiːdɪst / (BrE)
a person who compiles, writes, or edits encyclopedias
person who writes/complies encyclopedias

Examples

Denis Diderot was a renowned encyclopaedist in eighteenth-century France.
A modern encyclopaedist must be skilled in digital research and organization.
She aspired to become an encyclopaedist, contributing articles on science and history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: encyclopedist - alternative (US) spelling for encyclopaedist

Etymology

From French 'encyclopédiste', based on 'encyclopedia'.
