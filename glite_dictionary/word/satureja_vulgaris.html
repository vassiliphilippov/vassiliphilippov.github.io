Satureja vulgaris

1. NOUN

/ ˌsætjʊˈriːə vʌlˈgɛrɪs / (AmE), / ˌsatjʊˈrɪə vʌlˈɡɑːrɪs / (BrE)
An aromatic herb with heads of small pink or whitish flowers, widely distributed in the United States, Europe, and Asia.
species of aromatic herb

Examples

Satureja vulgaris is valued in some cuisines for its aromatic leaves and flowers.
Botanists have identified Satureja vulgaris growing along the riverbanks in eastern Europe.
The herbal tea was infused with Satureja vulgaris to give it a delicate flavor.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Satureja' and species 'vulgaris' meaning 'common'.
