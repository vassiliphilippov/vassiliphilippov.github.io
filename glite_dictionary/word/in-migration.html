in-migration

1. NOUN

/ ɪn maɪˈɡreɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪn maɪˈɡreɪʃən / (BrE)
the movement of people into a place, especially into a country or region, to live there permanently
movement of people into a place to settle

Examples

The rapid in-migration of workers transformed the city's demographics within a decade.
Economic opportunities in urban areas often drive in-migration from rural regions.
Policies are being developed to better manage in-migration and support new residents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immigration - the action of coming to live permanently in a foreign country
  • Antonym: out-migration - the movement of people out of a place, especially to settle elsewhere

Etymology

Formed from 'in-' (into) and 'migration', from Latin 'migratio' (removal, migration).
