new-mown

1. ADJ

/ ˈnuː ˌmoʊn / (AmE), / ˈnjuː ˌməʊn / (BrE)
(of grass, hay, etc.) that has been recently cut or mown.
recently cut (grass or hay)

Examples

The scent of new-mown grass filled the summer air.
He lay back on the new-mown lawn and looked up at the sky.
The villagers gathered the new-mown hay into neat stacks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fresh-cut - recently cut; particularly used for grass, flowers, or timber

Etymology

Formed from 'new' + 'mown', past participle of 'mow'.
