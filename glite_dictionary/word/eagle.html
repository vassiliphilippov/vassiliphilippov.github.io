eagle

1. NOUN

/ ˈiɡəl / (AmE), / ˈiːɡl̩ / (BrE)
a former gold coin of the United States with a value of ten dollars, issued from the late 18th to the 20th century
former U.S. gold coin worth ten dollars

Examples

The collector was thrilled to find a rare 1804 eagle at the auction.
He inherited a small pouch of old eagles from his great-grandfather.
In the nineteenth century, a ten-dollar gold piece was commonly called an eagle.

Etymology

Named for the depiction of an eagle on the coin's design.

2. NOUN

/ ˈiːɡəl / (AmE), / ˈiːɡl̩ / (BrE)
(in golf) a score of two strokes under par on a single hole.
golf score: two under par on a hole

Examples

She was thrilled to make an eagle on the par 5.
Scoring an eagle at the last hole allowed him to win the tournament.
It's rare for amateurs to get an eagle during a round.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: birdie - a golf score of one stroke under par on a hole
  • Degree Higher: albatross - a golf score of three strokes under par on a hole

Etymology

Named after the bird 'eagle', following the use of 'birdie' in golf scoring.

6. NOUN

/ ˈiɡəl / (AmE), / ˈiːɡl̩ / (BrE)
a large bird of prey with a powerful beak, keen eyesight, and broad wings, known for its soaring flight
large bird of prey with broad wings

Examples

The eagle soared high above the mountains, scanning the ground for prey.
An American eagle is featured on the back of the quarter dollar coin.
With its sharp talons, the eagle caught a fish from the river.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawk - a bird of prey similar to but typically smaller than an eagle
  • Synonym: vulture - a large bird of prey that feeds chiefly on carrion

Etymology

From Old French 'aigle', from Latin 'aquila' (eagle).
