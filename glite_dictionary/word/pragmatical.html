pragmatical

1. ADJ

/ præɡˈmætɪkəl / (AmE), / præɡˈmatɪkəl / (BrE)
Relating to, or concerned with, the theory or principles of pragmatism.
concerning pragmatism theory

Examples

Researchers debated the pragmatical implications of the theory in their publications.
The pragmatical approach to philosophy emphasizes practical application over abstract speculation.
Her arguments were firmly rooted in pragmatical principles derived from everyday experience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pragmatic - dealing with things sensibly and realistically; practical
  • Synonym: pragmatism - the philosophical doctrine focusing on practical consequences

Etymology

From Medieval Latin pragmaticus (practical), with the English suffix -al, and related to 'pragmatism'.

2. ADJ

/ præɡˈmætɪkəl / (AmE), / præɡˈmatɪkəl / (BrE)
Dealing with things sensibly and realistically, in a way that is based on practical rather than theoretical considerations.
concerned with practical matters
archaic

Examples

Her pragmatical attitude helped the team solve problems quickly and efficiently.
The manager preferred a pragmatical approach over lengthy theoretical discussions.
Throughout history, pragmatical thinkers have often found practical solutions when others could not.

Synset Examples

a matter-of-fact (or pragmatic) approach to the problem
a matter-of-fact account of the trip

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pragmatic - dealing with things sensibly and realistically

Etymology

From Medieval Latin pragmaticus, from Greek pragmatikos (of action, practical). Related to 'pragmatic'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01841127-s
Synset POS: s
