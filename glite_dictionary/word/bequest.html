bequest

1. NOUN

/ bəˈkwɛst / (AmE), / bɪˈkwɛst / (BrE)
(law) a gift of personal property left to someone in a will.
gift left in a will
legal

Examples

The lawyer explained that the bequest would go to her eldest son after her passing.
He received a generous bequest of antique jewelry from his late aunt's will.
The museum was funded largely through a bequest left by an anonymous donor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legacy - a gift of money or property left to someone in a will
  • Synonym: inheritance - assets received from someone after their death

Etymology

From Old French 'biquest', variant of 'bequist', from be- + *quedan* (to say), related to English 'bequeath'.
