impecunious

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪmpɪˈkjuːniəs / (AmE), / ˌɪmpɪˈkjuːniəs / (BrE)
having little or no money; lacking funds to pay for basic needs
having little or no money

Examples

After losing his job, he found himself impecunious and struggling to pay the rent.
Despite his impecunious background, he managed to put himself through college.
The impecunious artist survived by selling sketches on the street.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poor - lacking sufficient money to live comfortably
  • Antonym: wealthy - having a large amount of money and possessions

Etymology

From Latin 'impecuniosus', from 'in-' (not) + 'pecunia' (money).
