leper

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛpər / (AmE), / ˈlɛpə / (BrE)
a person who is suffering from leprosy
person with leprosy

Examples

In the past, a leper often lived in isolation from the rest of society.
The villagers shunned him after rumors spread that he was a leper.
Many ancient texts mention the difficulties faced by a leper in finding acceptance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leprosy - a chronic infectious disease; the condition that afflicts a leper

Etymology

From Middle English lepre, from Old French lepre, from Late Latin lepra, from Greek lepra meaning 'leprosy'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɛpər / (AmE), / ˈlɛpə / (BrE)
A person who is avoided or shunned by others; an outcast.
a person who is shunned or outcast

Examples

After the scandal, he became a leper in his own community.
She was treated like a leper because of her unconventional beliefs.
Refusing to follow tradition made him a social leper among his peers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pariah - a person who is despised or avoided
  • Synonym: outcast - a person who is rejected by society

Etymology

From Middle English lepre, leper, from Old French lepre (leprosy), from Latin lepra.
