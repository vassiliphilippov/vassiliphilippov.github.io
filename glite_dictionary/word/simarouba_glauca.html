Simarouba glauca

1. NOUN

/ sɪməˈruːbə ˈɡlɔkə / (AmE), / sɪˌmaruːbə ˈɡlɔːkə / (BrE)
A medium to large evergreen tree native to tropical regions of the Americas, known for its pinnate leaves, panicles of small pale yellow flowers, and scarlet fruits.
a tropical American evergreen tree species

Examples

The seeds of the Simarouba glauca tree are used to extract edible oil in some regions.
Farmers are increasingly cultivating Simarouba glauca for its drought resistance and various ecological benefits.
A cluster of Simarouba glauca trees stands at the edge of the rainforest, their scarlet fruits attracting birds.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Simarouba' (from French, originally from Arawak), and the species name 'glauca', Latin for 'bluish-green'.
