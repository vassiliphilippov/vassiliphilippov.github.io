Amaranthus

1. PROPN

/ ˌæməˈrænθəs / (AmE), / ˌaməˈranθəs / (BrE)
a large genus of mostly coarse annual herbs known for their brightly colored flowers and edible seeds, commonly called amaranth
a genus of annual herbs; amaranth

Examples

Amaranthus species are grown worldwide for their edible leaves and seeds.
Many gardeners cultivate ornamental varieties of Amaranthus for their strikingly colorful plumes.
Scientists study Amaranthus to understand its potential as a drought-resistant crop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amaranth - the common name for plants of the genus Amaranthus

Etymology

From Greek 'amáranthos', meaning unfading, referring to the long-lasting flowers.
