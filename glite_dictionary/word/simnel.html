simnel

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪmnəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪmnəl / (BrE)
A light fruitcake, sometimes covered with a layer of almond paste or marzipan, traditionally eaten at mid-Lent, Easter, or Christmas.
fruitcake with almond paste for Easter or Lent
british

Examples

A traditional simnel is decorated with eleven marzipan balls representing the apostles, excluding Judas.
She baked a delicious simnel for the family to enjoy on Easter Sunday.
Many British bakeries offer simnel during the spring festival season.

Etymology

From Old French *simenel*, from Latin *simila* (fine flour).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪmnəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪmnəl / (BrE)
a light, fruit-filled cake, often covered with a layer of marzipan, traditionally eaten during Lent or at Easter in Britain
a traditional fruit cake with marzipan, eaten at Easter
british

Examples

Traditionally, a simnel was made by daughters for their mothers on Mothering Sunday.
The bakery sold out of every simnel before Easter morning.
He baked a rich simnel with layers of marzipan and sweet dried fruit.

Etymology

From Latin simila (fine flour), via Old French simenel and Medieval Latin simenellus.
