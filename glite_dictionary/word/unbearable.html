unbearable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈbɛrəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈbeərəbl / (BrE)
too painful, unpleasant, or extreme to be tolerated or endured
too difficult to endure or tolerate

Examples

The pain in his knee became unbearable after the accident.
She found the heat inside the car simply unbearable during the summer.
Their constant arguing made living together unbearable for everyone.

Synset Examples

an intolerable degree of sentimentality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intolerable - too bad or harsh to endure or tolerate
  • Antonym: bearable - able to be tolerated or endured

Etymology

From un- (not) + bearable, from bear (to tolerate) + -able (capable of).
