nocturnal

1. ADJ

/ nɑkˈtɝnəl / (AmE), / nɒkˈtɜːnəl / (BrE)
active, occurring, or relating to the night rather than the day.
active or happening at night

Examples

Owls are nocturnal creatures that hunt when it is dark.
The museum organized a nocturnal tour to showcase its exhibits at night.
Some flowers only bloom during their nocturnal cycle.

Synset Examples

nocturnal darkness

Related Terms

  • Antonym: diurnal - active or occurring during the daytime

Etymology

From Latin 'nocturnus' (of the night), from 'nox' (night).

2. ADJ

/ nɑkˈtɜrnəl / (AmE), / nɒkˈtɜːnəl / (BrE)
Active, occurring, or existing during the night rather than during the day.
active or occurring at night

Examples

Owls are nocturnal birds that hunt for food after sunset.
Many flowers that bloom at night are adapted to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
Because bats are nocturnal, they rest in caves during the day and emerge after dusk.

Synset Examples

nocturnal animals are active at night
nocturnal plants have flowers that open at night and close by day

Related Terms

  • Antonym: diurnal - active or occurring during the daytime

Etymology

From Latin nocturnus (of the night), from nox (night).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00682190-a
Synset POS: a
