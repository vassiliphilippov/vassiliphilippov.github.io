adenomegaly

1. NOUN

/ ˌædɪnoʊˈmɛɡəli / (AmE), / ˌædɪnəʊˈmɛɡəli / (BrE)
abnormal enlargement of a gland, especially a lymph node, in the body
enlargement of a gland
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the patient with adenomegaly after noticing the swollen lymph nodes.
Adenomegaly can be a sign of infection or an underlying systemic disease.
Routine examinations often help in the early detection of adenomegaly in children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lymphadenopathy - disease of the lymph nodes, often involving enlargement

Etymology

From Greek adēn (gland) + -megaly (enlargement).
