Cardamine douglasii

1. NOUN

/ kɑrˈdæmɪni ˌduːˈɡlæsiaɪ / (AmE), / kɑːˈdamɪni ˌduːˈɡlasɪaɪ / (BrE)
A small perennial herb native to cooler regions of North America, having racemose clusters of purple flowers.
a North American perennial herb with purple flowers

Examples

Cardamine douglasii is among the earliest wildflowers to bloom in spring in eastern North America.
The woodland floor was dotted with the delicate purple flowers of Cardamine douglasii.
Botanists often study Cardamine douglasii to understand early spring pollination.

Etymology

Named after the Scottish botanist David Douglas; Cardamine from Latin 'cardamine', a genus in the mustard family.
