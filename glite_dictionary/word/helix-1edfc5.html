helix

1. NOUN

/ ˈhiːlɪks / (AmE), / ˈhiːlɪks / (BrE)
a curve that spirals around a cylinder or cone at a constant angle, especially in mathematics and science
spiral-shaped curve

Examples

The staircase was designed in the shape of a helix winding upward.
DNA is famous for its double helix structure.
He traced the helix drawn on the paper with his finger.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiral - a curve winding around a central point or axis

Etymology

From Greek helix (something twisted, spiral).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhiːlɪks / (AmE), / ˈhiːlɪks / (BrE)
a shape formed by something wound in a continuous spiral or series of loops, like a coil
spiral or coiled shape

Examples

The DNA molecule is structured as a double helix with two strands winding around each other.
We admired the elegant helix of the spiral staircase in the old mansion.
The craftsman bent the wire into a helix to form a decorative spring.

Synset Examples

a coil of rope

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiral - a curve that winds around a fixed center point at a continuously increasing (or decreasing) distance
  • Synonym: coil - a series of loops formed by winding

Etymology

From Greek 'helix' (spiral, whirl).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03069428-n
Synset POS: n
