to repine

1. VERB

/ rɪˈpaɪn / (AmE), / rɪˈpaɪn / (BrE)
to express dissatisfaction or a sense of longing or complaint, often about something out of one's control
express discontent or complain
archaic

Examples

Though she missed her family, she would not repine but made the best of her new home.
It is useless to repine over circumstances that cannot be changed.
He repined for years about the life he could have had.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to complain - to express dissatisfaction or annoyance

Etymology

From Middle English *repinen* (to regret, be discontented), from Old French *repiner*, from re- (again) + *piner* (to pine).
