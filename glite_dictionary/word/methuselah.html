Methuselah

1. NOUN

/ məˈθuːzələ / (AmE), / mɪˈθjuːzələ / (BrE)
an extremely old person; someone of great age, alluding to the biblical figure Methuselah, said to have lived 969 years.
an extremely old person

Examples

After celebrating his hundredth birthday, everyone called Grandpa a Methuselah.
Despite his Methuselah reputation at work, he remained sharp and energetic.
The tortoise, a true Methuselah among reptiles, had been alive for over a century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ancient - very old, belonging to the distant past
  • Antonym: youth - the quality or state of being young

Etymology

From the biblical Methuselah, a patriarch said to have lived 969 years (Genesis 5:27).

2. PROPN

/ məˈθuːzələ / (AmE), / mɪˈθjuːzɪlə / (BrE)
In the Bible, a patriarch known as the grandfather of Noah, reputed to have lived 969 years and often cited as a symbol of longevity.
biblical patriarch known for extreme longevity

Examples

The old man was as old as Methuselah, or so it seemed to everyone in the village.
The wine was stored in a Methuselah, a large bottle named after the biblical figure.
She often joked that her ancient cat was a real Methuselah among felines.

Etymology

From Hebrew 'məthūšélaḥ', name of a biblical patriarch.
