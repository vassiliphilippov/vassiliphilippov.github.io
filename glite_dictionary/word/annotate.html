to annotate

1. VERB

/ ˈænəˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈænəteɪt / (BrE)
to add notes or explanations to a text or diagram, often to clarify or comment
add notes or explanations

Examples

The editor annotated the manuscript, clarifying difficult passages with brief explanations.
Students are often required to annotate primary texts in their literature classes.
She annotated each page with her observations and cross-references to related works.

Synset Examples

He annotated on what his teacher had written

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comment - to make explanatory remarks, often in writing

Etymology

From Latin annotatus, past participle of annotare (to note down), from ad- (to) + notare (to note).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00963361-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈænəˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈænəteɪt / (BrE)
to add notes to a text or diagram explaining or commenting on parts of it
add explanatory notes

Examples

The biology student had to annotate each diagram in the lab report.
Editors often annotate manuscripts to clarify difficult passages for readers.
You are required to annotate your sources in this bibliography.

Synset Examples

The scholar annotated the early edition of a famous novel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comment - to express an opinion or give an explanation; to remark on something
  • Synonym: gloss - to provide an explanation or translation of a word or phrase in a text

Etymology

From Latin 'annotare' (to mark, note down), from 'ad-' (to) + 'notare' (to note).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01708812-v
Synset POS: v
