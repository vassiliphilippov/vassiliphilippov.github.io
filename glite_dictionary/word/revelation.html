Revelation

1. PROPN

/ ˌrɛvəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛvəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the last book of the New Testament in the Bible, containing visions of the end of the world and the ultimate victory of good over evil, traditionally attributed to Saint John the Apostle
the last book of the New Testament

Examples

Many Christians study Revelation to understand biblical prophecies about the end times.
The imagery in Revelation has inspired countless works of art and literature.
Scholars debate the authorship and interpretation of Revelation to this day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Apocalypse - another name for the Book of Revelation or for prophetic visions of the end times

Etymology

From Latin 'revelatio' (a revealing), from 'revelare' (to unveil).
