alundum

1. NOUN

/ əˈlʌndəm / (AmE), / əˈlʌndəm / (BrE)
a substance made of fused alumina, often used as an abrasive or in refractory products
fused alumina used as an abrasive

Examples

Grinding wheels are often made from alundum because of its durability.
The laboratory uses alundum crucibles for high-temperature experiments.
He bought a block of alundum to sharpen his tools.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alumina - an oxide of aluminum, Al2O3

Etymology

From alumina and corundum, indicating its composition and relation to the mineral corundum.
