argentine

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrdʒənˌtaɪn / (AmE), / ˈɑːdʒəntiːn / (BrE)
any of various small, silver-scaled, salmon-like marine fishes of the family Argentinidae, found in deep ocean waters.
small silver-scaled marine fish

Examples

The scientist collected an argentine from the ocean trench for further study.
Several species of argentine are prized for their shiny, silver scales.
Fishing boats often encounter schools of argentine deep beneath the Atlantic.

Etymology

From Latin 'argentum' (silver), referring to the fish’s silvery appearance.
