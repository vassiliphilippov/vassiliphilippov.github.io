Polyporus

1. NOUN

/ pəˈlaɪpərəs / (AmE), / pɒˈlɪpərəs / (BrE)
A genus of bracket fungi in the family Polyporaceae, some of which are important pathogens of trees such as birches and conifers.
genus of bracket fungi
medical

Examples

Several species within the genus Polyporus are known to cause decay in hardwood trees.
Scientists studied the unique structure of a Polyporus fungus collected from a fallen birch.
The fruiting bodies of Polyporus can often be found on the trunks and branches of dead trees.

Etymology

From Greek 'polýs' (many) + 'póros' (pore), referring to the many pores on the underside of the fruiting body.

2. NOUN

/ pəˈlɪpərəs / (AmE), / pɒˈlɪpərəs / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Polyporaceae, which includes important pathogenic species affecting trees such as birches and conifers.
genus of pathogenic polypore fungi

Examples

Several species of Polyporus are known to cause decay in the trunks of old birch trees.
Researchers studied the life cycle of Polyporus to better understand its impact on forest ecosystems.
The shelf-like fruiting bodies produced by Polyporus are commonly found on dead or dying wood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Polyporaceae - The family to which Polyporus belongs

Etymology

From Greek 'polys' (many) + 'poros' (pore), referring to the fungi's many pores.
