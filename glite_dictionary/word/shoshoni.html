Shoshoni

1. NOUN

/ ʃoʊˈʃoʊni / (AmE), / ʃəˈʃəʊni / (BrE)
A member of a Native American people of the western United States, primarily living in areas of Idaho, Utah, and Nevada.
member of a Native American people of the western US

Examples

A group of Shoshoni gathered for the annual cultural festival.
The Shoshoni were known for their skilled horsemanship and hunting abilities.
She traced her ancestry to the Shoshoni people who once lived in northern Utah.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Shoshone - alternative form or spelling, same Native American people

Etymology

From Shoshoni self-designation, possibly from a word meaning 'high-growing grasses.'
