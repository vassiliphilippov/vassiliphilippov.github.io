Griselinia littoralis

1. NOUN

/ ɡrɪˌsɛlɪˈniːə lɪtəˈrɑːlɪs / (AmE), / ɡrɪˌsɛlɪˈniːə lɪtəˈrɑːlɪs / (BrE)
a small evergreen tree native to New Zealand, often grown in warm regions as an ornamental plant
New Zealand evergreen ornamental tree

Examples

The glossy leaves of Griselinia littoralis make it a popular choice for coastal gardens.
Gardeners often plant Griselinia littoralis as a hedge due to its dense foliage.
Griselinia littoralis thrives in mild climates and requires little maintenance.

Etymology

From the genus name Griselinia (honoring Italian botanist Francesco Griselini) and the Latin 'littoralis' meaning 'of the seashore'.
