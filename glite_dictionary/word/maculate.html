maculate

1. ADJ

/ ˈmækjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈmækjʊleɪt / (BrE)
marked with spots or blotches; stained or blemished
spotted or blotched

Examples

The butterfly's maculate wings helped it blend into the dappled sunlight.
The archaeologist was fascinated by the maculate patterns on the ancient pottery.
Her dress featured a unique, maculate design that attracted compliments from everyone.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: immaculate - perfectly clean or free from stains or spots

Etymology

From Latin maculatus, past participle of maculare (to spot), from macula (spot).

2. VERB

/ ˈmækjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈmækjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to mark with spots or stains; to soil, pollute, or defile
mark with spots or pollute

Examples

The painters accidentally maculated the canvas with drops of blue paint.
Centuries of pollution had maculated the once pristine lake.
He feared that a single mistake could maculate his honor forever.

Synset Examples

The townspeople defiled the river by emptying raw sewage into it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defile - to make dirty or impure
  • Synonym: taint - to contaminate or pollute
  • Antonym: to cleanse - to make clean or free from impurities

Etymology

From Latin 'maculatus', past participle of 'maculare' meaning 'to spot' or 'to stain'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00493346-v
Synset POS: v

/ ˈmæk.jə.lət / (AmE), / ˈmak.jʊ.lət / (BrE)
morally stained or impure; having a blemish on one’s moral character
morally stained or impure

Examples

The judge considered him maculate after the corruption allegations were proven.
Many saw the leader’s once spotless reputation become maculate following the scandal.
She tried to rebuild trust, but her maculate past haunted her career.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: immaculate - entirely pure; free from moral stain or blemish

Etymology

From Latin 'maculatus', past participle of 'maculare' (to stain).

4. VERB

/ ˈmækjʊˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈmakjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to stain or make dirty; to spoil or tarnish, especially in a moral or reputational sense
to stain or make dirty

Examples

Her white dress was maculated by mud from the garden path.
The scandal maculated his previously unblemished reputation.
Years of neglect had maculated the walls with stains and mold.

Synset Examples

The silver was tarnished by the long exposure to the air
Her reputation was sullied after the affair with a married man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tarnish - to lose or cause to lose luster, especially due to exposure; to sully or spoil
  • Synonym: to sully - to damage or soil the purity, integrity, or condition of something

Etymology

From Latin maculatus (spotted, defiled), from macula (spot, stain).
