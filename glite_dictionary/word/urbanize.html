to urbanize

1. VERB

/ ˈɝː.bə.naɪz / (AmE), / ˈɜː.bə.naɪz / (BrE)
to make an area or place more characteristic of a city, often through increased development, building, and population density
make more city-like; develop as urban area

Examples

Many small towns have begun to urbanize rapidly due to economic growth.
Government policies can urbanize remote areas by improving infrastructure and attracting businesses.
The decision to urbanize the farmland was controversial among local residents.

Synset Examples

The area was urbanized after many people moved in

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ruralize - to make more rural in character
  • Synonym: urban - relating to a city or town

Etymology

From French 'urbaniser', from Latin 'urbanus' (city, city-dweller).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00500893-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈɝː.bə.naɪz / (AmE), / ˈɜː.bə.naɪz / (BrE)
To cause someone or something to acquire urban habits, ways of life, or attitudes.
impart urban habits or ways of life

Examples

Wild animals can sometimes be urbanized through ongoing exposure to city environments.
Efforts to urbanize rural communities often involve social and cultural changes.
Pigeons have become highly urbanized, thriving in metropolitan areas worldwide.

Synset Examples

Birds are being urbanized by people in outdoor cafes feeding them

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to civilize - to bring to a stage of social, cultural, and moral development

Etymology

From French 'urbaniser', from Latin 'urbanus' (of a city).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00501136-v
Synset POS: v
