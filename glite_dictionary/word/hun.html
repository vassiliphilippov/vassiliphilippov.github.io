Hun

1. NOUN

/ hʌn / (AmE), / hʌn / (BrE)
An offensive term for a person of German descent, especially used during periods of conflict.
offensive term for Germans
offensive

Examples

During World War I, British propaganda often referred to German soldiers as Huns.
The use of the word Hun to describe Germans is now considered highly offensive.
Some old newspapers used the term Hun in articles about the war.

Etymology

Originally from medieval Latin 'Hunni', referring to the historical people led by Attila; later used as a derogatory term for Germans, especially in World Wars.

2. NOUN

/ hʌn / (AmE), / hʌn / (BrE)
a member of a warlike nomadic people from Central Asia who invaded and settled in parts of Europe in the 4th and 5th centuries
member of nomadic people who invaded Europe in the 4th century

Examples

The Huns played a significant role in the collapse of the Western Roman Empire.
Attila the Hun became a legendary figure in both European history and folklore.
Archaeological sites reveal much about the way of life of the Huns and their migrations.

Etymology

From Latin 'Hunni', of uncertain origin, referring to the people known as the Huns.
