astragal

1. NOUN

/ ˈæstrəɡəl / (AmE), / ˈastrəɡəl / (BrE)
A narrow molded strip, often with a beaded profile, used as an edging or decorative element on furniture or architectural features.
beaded molding for decorating furniture

Examples

The cabinet maker added an astragal to the edge of the shelves for an elegant finish.
Restorers replaced the damaged astragals on the antique doors to preserve their original appearance.
A decorative astragal runs along the top of the window frame, enhancing its classical style.

Etymology

From Latin 'astragalus', meaning 'ankle bone' or 'molding bead', via Greek 'astragalos'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæstrəɡəl / (AmE), / ˈæstrəɡəl / (BrE)
the bone in the ankle that connects the leg bones to form the ankle joint
ankle bone (talus)
medical

Examples

The surgeon explained that the fracture involved the astragal and required specialized treatment.
Archaeological digs have uncovered animal astragals used as ancient dice.
Damage to the astragal can severely limit ankle movement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: talus - the large bone in the ankle that articulates with the tibia and fibula

Etymology

From Greek astragalos (ankle bone, knucklebone).
