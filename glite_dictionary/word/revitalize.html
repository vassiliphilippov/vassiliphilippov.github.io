to revitalize

1. VERB

/ riˈvaɪtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / rɪˈvʌɪtəlʌɪz / (BrE)
to give new life, energy, or strength to something; to make something active or effective again
give new life or energy to

Examples

The government hopes to revitalize the city's economy with new investments.
After the long break, fresh ideas helped revitalize the project.
Drinking a glass of cold water in the afternoon can revitalize you.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to invigorate - to give strength or energy to
  • Antonym: to weaken - to make or become weaker or less strong

Etymology

From re- (again) + vital + -ize, meaning 'to make vital again'.

2. VERB

/ riˈvaɪtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / rɪˈvɪtəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something or someone strong, active, or healthy again; to give new life, energy, or strength to something
to restore strength or energy

Examples

The government hopes to revitalize the city by investing in infrastructure and new businesses.
Fresh air and sunlight can revitalize your mood after a long week indoors.
The coach's pep talk revitalized the team before the final game.

Synset Examples

This food revitalized the patient

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reinvigorate - to give new energy or strength to something
  • Antonym: to weaken - to make or become less strong

Etymology

From re- (again) + vitalize (give life to), formed from Latin 'vita' (life).
