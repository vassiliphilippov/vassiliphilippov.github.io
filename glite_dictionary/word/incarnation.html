incarnation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnˌkɑːrˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnˌkɑːˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
a period of existence in a bodily form, especially as believed in some religions and philosophies involving rebirth or life after death
existence in a particular bodily form

Examples

In his second incarnation, the philosopher believed he would become a wiser person.
According to tradition, the god returned in a new incarnation every millennium.
She wondered what her next incarnation might hold if she believed in rebirth.

Synset Examples

he believes that his life will be better in his next incarnation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reincarnation - the belief that the soul is reborn in another body after death

Etymology

From Late Latin incarnatio (embodiment), from Latin incarnare (to make flesh).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.kɑrˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.kɑːˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a person or thing that embodies or personifies a particular quality, idea, or concept
personification or embodiment of an idea

Examples

Some fans saw her as the very incarnation of honesty and intelligence.
The villain in the story is the incarnation of pure evil.
His generosity was seen as the incarnation of kindness among his peers.

Synset Examples

the embodiment of hope
the incarnation of evil
the very avatar of cunning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embodiment - the tangible or visible form of an idea, quality, or feeling
  • Synonym: personification - the representation of a quality or concept in human form

Etymology

From Latin incarnatio (embodiment in flesh), from incarnare (to make flesh).
