maternalism

1. NOUN

/ məˈtɝː.nə.lɪz.əm / (AmE), / məˈtɜː.nə.lɪz.əm / (BrE)
Behavior or attitudes similar to those of a caring mother, especially in providing nurture and protection.
motherly care or behavior

Examples

The teacher's maternalism made her students feel cared for in the classroom.
Some social programs are inspired by a belief in maternalism and protection of the vulnerable.
Her gentle maternalism was evident in the way she supported her friends during difficult times.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: paternalism - practice of managing others in a fatherly or controlling manner
  • Synonym: maternal - relating to a mother; motherly

Etymology

From 'maternal' + '-ism', based on Latin 'mater' (mother).

2. NOUN

/ məˈtɝːnəlɪzəm / (AmE), / məˈtɜːnəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the quality or practice of showing care, affection, and protection characteristic of a mother
motherly care or affection

Examples

Her maternalism made her a natural caregiver for the young children.
The teacher's maternalism was evident in the way she nurtured her students.
He was comforted by the gentle maternalism of his aunt after a difficult day.

Synset Examples

the girl's motherliness made her invaluable in caring for the children

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motherliness - the quality of being like a mother, showing warmth and care
  • Antonym: paternalism - the practice of managing individuals or groups in a fatherly way

Etymology

From Latin 'mater' (mother) + '-al' + '-ism'.
