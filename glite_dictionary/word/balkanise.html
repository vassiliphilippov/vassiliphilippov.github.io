to balkanise

1. VERB

/ ˈbɑːlkəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈbɔːlkənaɪz / (BrE)
to divide (a region, country, or group) into smaller, often hostile units or states, especially along ethnic or territorial lines
divide into hostile smaller units

Examples

Some observers fear that the country could balkanise if tensions continue to rise.
Politicians warned that sudden autonomy could balkanise the region and lead to conflict.
The civil war threatened to balkanise the nation into several unstable territories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fragment - break or cause to break into pieces

Etymology

From the Balkan Peninsula, which was fragmented into smaller, often hostile states in the early 20th century.
