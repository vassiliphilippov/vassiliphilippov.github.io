Voltarian

1. ADJ

/ voʊlˈtɛəriən / (AmE), / vɒlˈtɛəriən / (BrE)
In the manner or style of Voltaire, especially characterized by wit, irony, and sharp criticism.
in the style of Voltaire; witty, ironic

Examples

Her Voltarian wit made her essays both entertaining and thought-provoking.
The novel features a Voltarian critique of organized religion and authority.
His speeches, Voltarian in tone, challenged the status quo with biting irony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Voltairean - in the manner of Voltaire

Etymology

From the name Voltaire, the French Enlightenment writer and philosopher (1694–1778), plus the suffix -an.
