insurance

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈʃʊrəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈʃʊərəns / (BrE)
An arrangement in which a company agrees to provide compensation for specified loss, damage, illness, or death in return for payment of a premium.
arrangement for compensation against loss or damage

Examples

I bought insurance to protect my house against fire and theft.
She works for a large insurance company in New York.
Travelers are advised to purchase health insurance before going abroad.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assurance - a positive declaration intended to give confidence; in UK, also refers to certain kinds of insurance policies

Etymology

From French *assurance* (assurance), from assurer (to assure), ultimately from Latin *ad* (to) + *securus* (secure).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈʃʊr.əns / (AmE), / ɪnˈʃɔː.rəns / (BrE)
An arrangement in which a company or state provides a guarantee of compensation for specified loss, damage, illness, or death in return for payment of a premium.
coverage against financial loss

Examples

It is important to have insurance in case of an accident or theft.
She bought travel insurance before going abroad to cover any medical emergencies.
Many homeowners consider insurance against natural disasters essential for peace of mind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assurance - A pledge or guarantee of compensation for loss or damage; often used in British English for life insurance
  • Antonym: risk - Possibility of loss or damage

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman 'enseurance', from Old French 'enseurer' (to assure).

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈʃʊrəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈʃʊərəns / (BrE)
A document or contract that provides a written guarantee of compensation for specified loss, damage, illness, or death in return for payment of a premium.
written contract guaranteeing compensation for loss

Examples

Please make sure you bring your insurance to the hospital.
The insurance was renewed every year to keep the building protected.
She kept her insurance papers safe in a locked drawer.

Synset Examples

you should have read the small print on your policy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: policy - the document containing an insurance contract

Etymology

From late Middle English, from Old French *ensurer* (assure).
