ferociousness

1. NOUN

/ fəˈroʊʃəsnəs / (AmE), / fəˈrəʊʃəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being extremely fierce, violent, or cruel
quality of being fierce or cruel

Examples

The ferociousness of the storm left the villagers in fear for their lives.
She was shocked by the ferociousness displayed during the heated argument.
The lion's ferociousness made it a feared predator in the savannah.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ferocity - the state or quality of being ferocious
  • Antonym: gentleness - the quality of being gentle, mild, or kind

Etymology

From ferocious + -ness; originally from Latin 'ferox' meaning fierce.
