spouter

1. NOUN

/ ˈspaʊtər / (AmE), / ˈspaʊtə / (BrE)
a whale that blows water and air out of its blowhole; a spouting whale.
a whale that spouts water

Examples

The sailors watched as a spouter broke the surface near the ship.
In the distance, a spouter could be seen releasing a column of mist.
The naturalist pointed out a spouter among the waves during the tour.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whale - a large marine mammal of the order Cetacea

Etymology

From 'spout' (to emit in a stream) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈspaʊtər / (AmE), / ˈspaʊtə / (BrE)
an oil well that forcefully ejects oil to the surface; a well that is spouting
an oil well that is spouting

Examples

The first spouter in the region brought instant prosperity to the town.
Photographs from the 1920s show massive oil plumes erupting from a spouter.
After weeks of drilling, they finally hit a spouter and oil gushed skyward.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gusher - an oil well that comes in with a violent flow

Etymology

From the verb 'spout' (to gush forth), by analogy to objects or places that spout.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04294489-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈspaʊtər / (AmE), / ˈspaʊtə / (BrE)
a person who talks excessively and in a foolish or showy manner, often in an obnoxious way
obnoxious, foolish talker

Examples

Everyone at the meeting was annoyed by the constant interruptions from the spouter in the back row.
The office spouter never misses a chance to share his outrageous opinions with anyone who will listen.
Instead of joining the conversation, he became a spouter, dominating the discussion with his loud and foolish remarks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: windbag - a person who talks too much, especially in a boring way
  • Synonym: babbler - a person who talks foolishly or makes meaningless sounds
  • Antonym: listener - a person who listens rather than talks

Etymology

From the verb 'spout' meaning to speak at length in a self-important way, + '-er'

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
