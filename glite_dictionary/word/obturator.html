obturator

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑb.tʃəˌreɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈɒb.tjʊˌreɪ.tə / (BrE)
a medical device or prosthesis used to close an opening, especially in the hard palate, such as in cases of cleft palate
prosthesis to close an anatomical opening
medical

Examples

The dentist designed an obturator to help the patient speak more clearly after surgery.
An obturator may be required for individuals born with a cleft palate.
The surgeon fitted an obturator to close the defect in the patient's hard palate.

Etymology

From Latin 'obturator', from 'obturare' (to stop up, to close).
