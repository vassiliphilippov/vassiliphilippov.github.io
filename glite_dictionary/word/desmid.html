desmid

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛzmɪd / (AmE), / ˈdɛzmɪd / (BrE)
A type of single-celled freshwater green algae with symmetrical and often intricately shaped cells, belonging to the family Desmidiaceae.
freshwater green algae

Examples

The pond was teeming with various species of desmid visible under the microscope.
Scientists study desmids to better understand freshwater ecosystems.
A single desmid can display intricate cell shapes and incredible symmetries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alga - a simple non-flowering plant of a large group that includes the seaweeds and many single-celled forms

Etymology

From Greek desmos (bond, chain) and eidos (form, shape), referring to their appearance.
