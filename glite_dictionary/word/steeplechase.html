steeplechase

1. NOUN

/ ˈstiːpəlˌtʃeɪs / (AmE), / ˈstiːpəlˌtʃeɪs / (BrE)
a race for horses in which competitors jump over fences, water jumps, and other obstacles along the course
horse race with obstacles

Examples

The annual steeplechase drew crowds from all over the region.
He trained his horse specifically for the challenging steeplechase course.
Winning the steeplechase is considered a major achievement in the equestrian world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hurdle race - a horse or foot race in which runners or horses jump over hurdles

Etymology

From the practice of racing from one church steeple to another, using steeples as distant markers.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstiːpəlˌtʃeɪs / (AmE), / ˈstiːpəlˌtʃeɪs / (BrE)
An athletics race, usually 3000 meters, run over a track with hurdles and a water jump.
race with hurdles and water jump

Examples

She trained for months to compete in the steeplechase at the national championships.
The steeplechase is known for its challenging water jump that tests every runner's agility.
He won a gold medal in the 3,000-meter steeplechase at the Olympics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hurdles - races in which runners must leap over barriers

Etymology

From the practice of racing on horseback from one church steeple to another, which was visible in the landscape.
