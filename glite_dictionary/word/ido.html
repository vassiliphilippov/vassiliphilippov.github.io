Ido

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːdoʊ / (AmE), / ˈiːdəʊ / (BrE)
an artificial language that is a revision and simplification of Esperanto, created as an alternative international auxiliary language
artificial language, simplified form of Esperanto

Examples

Many language enthusiasts study Ido because of its regular grammar and simplified vocabulary.
The meeting was attended by speakers of both Esperanto and Ido.
He wrote a short story in Ido to demonstrate its clarity and expressiveness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Esperanto - the most widely spoken constructed international auxiliary language

Etymology

From Esperanto 'ido' (offspring), chosen as a name to signify its origin from Esperanto.
