khanate

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɑːneɪt / (BrE)
the position or office of a khan; or a territory ruled by a khan, especially in Central Asian or Mongol history
territory or position of a khan

Examples

The Mongol Empire was divided into several khanates after Genghis Khan’s death.
Each khanate was ruled independently but recognized the authority of the Great Khan.
He studied the history of the Golden Horde khanate for his dissertation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: khan - a title for a ruler or official, especially in central Asia

Etymology

From Turkish 'hanlık', from Persian 'khān' (khan) + '-ate' (denoting office or jurisdiction).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɑːn.eɪt / (BrE)
A territory or realm ruled by a khan, especially in Central Asia, the Middle East, and some historical empires.
territory ruled by a khan

Examples

The Mongol Empire was divided into several khanates after Genghis Khan's death.
Each khanate operated independently under its own ruler.
The Russian conquest of Central Asia led to the end of many ancient khanates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kingdom - country, state, or territory ruled by a king or queen
  • Synonym: emirate - territory ruled by an emir

Etymology

From Turkish *han* (khan, ruler) + English suffix *-ate*, modeled after Latinate forms.
