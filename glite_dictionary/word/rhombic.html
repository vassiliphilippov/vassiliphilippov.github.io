rhombic

1. ADJ

/ rɑm.bɪk / (AmE), / ˈrɒm.bɪk / (BrE)
having the shape or characteristics of a rhombus; diamond-shaped.
having the shape of a rhombus

Examples

The crystal has a rhombic structure that is easily visible under magnification.
Tiles with a rhombic pattern were used to decorate the hallway floor.
She drew a rhombic shape to demonstrate the geometric concept.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhomboid - a shape similar to a rhombus but with adjacent sides of unequal lengths

Etymology

From Greek *rhombos* (spinning top, parallelogram), with '-ic' forming an adjective.
