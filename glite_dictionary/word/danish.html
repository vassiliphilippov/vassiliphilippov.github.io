Danish

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪ.nɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈdeɪ.nɪʃ / (BrE)
the Scandinavian language spoken mainly in Denmark, and the official language of that country
the official language of Denmark

Examples

He studied Danish at university to improve his communication skills during his trip to Copenhagen.
Many words in Danish resemble those in other Scandinavian languages.
She speaks fluent Danish as her mother grew up in Denmark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Swedish - the North Germanic language spoken in Sweden
  • Synonym: Norwegian - the North Germanic language spoken in Norway

Etymology

From Danish dansk (of Denmark), from Old Norse Danir (the Danes).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06966788-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈdænɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈdænɪʃ / (BrE)
of or relating to Denmark, its people, or its language
relating to Denmark or its people

Examples

The museum has a stunning collection of Danish paintings from the 19th century.
She loves the clean, minimalist look of Danish design.
Many Danish words are similar to German words in pronunciation and meaning.

Synset Examples

Danish furniture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dane - a native or inhabitant of Denmark
  • Antonym: Norwegian - of or relating to Norway, its people, or its language

Etymology

From Middle English Danis, from Old Norse Danask, meaning 'of the Danes'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02971884-a
Synset POS: a
