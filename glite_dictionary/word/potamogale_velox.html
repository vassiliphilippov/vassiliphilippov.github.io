Potamogale velox

1. NOUN

/ poʊˌtæməˈɡeɪli ˈviːlɑks / (AmE), / pəʊˌtæməˈɡɑːli ˈviːlɒks / (BrE)
A species of amphibious African insectivorous mammal resembling an otter, also known as the giant otter shrew.
African amphibious mammal species

Examples

The Potamogale velox uses its long, flat tail to swim swiftly through African rivers.
Researchers observed the Potamogale velox hunting for aquatic insects at dusk.
Unlike otters, Potamogale velox prefers to remain hidden in dense riverbank vegetation.

Etymology

From Greek 'potamos' (river) + 'gale' (weasel), with 'velox' meaning 'swift' in Latin.
