crottle

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɑː.təl / (AmE), / ˈkrɒt.əl / (BrE)
any of several lichens, especially of the genus Parmelia, from which reddish-brown or purple dyes are made
lichen used to make reddish dyes

Examples

The traditional tartans of Scotland were once dyed using crottle gathered from rocky surfaces.
She collected crottle to extract its deep red color for her weaving project.
In the Highlands, crottle was valued for its use in making natural dyes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lichen - a composite organism formed from a fungus and an alga or cyanobacterium

Etymology

From Scottish Gaelic 'crotal', meaning lichen or scurf.
