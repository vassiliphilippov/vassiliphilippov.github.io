downcast

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊnˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnkɑːst / (BrE)
A ventilation shaft through which fresh air enters a mine.
mine ventilation shaft

Examples

The miners descended near the downcast to ensure proper airflow throughout the tunnels.
Fresh air is supplied through the downcast during every shift in the coal mine.
Regular inspections of the downcast are essential for maintaining a safe environment underground.

2. ADJ

/ ˈdaʊnˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnkɑːst / (BrE)
(of eyes, look, or glance) directed downward, especially as a sign of sadness or modesty.
eyes or glance directed downward

Examples

She avoided my gaze, her downcast eyes revealing her discomfort.
With a downcast glance, he admitted his mistake to the group.
The child's downcast look made it clear that he was feeling ashamed.

Synset Examples

Etymology

From Middle English down + cast, literally 'cast downward'.
