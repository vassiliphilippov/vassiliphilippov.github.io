crenelle

1. NOUN

/ krəˈnɛl / (AmE), / krəˈnɛl / (BrE)
a rounded projection or the notch between such projections, formed by curves along the edge of an object, such as a leaf, shell, or cell
rounded projection or notch along an edge

Examples

The leaf's edge was decorated with a series of crenelles that gave it a distinctive appearance.
Under the microscope, the cell membrane showed several crenelles resulting from shrinkage in a hypertonic solution.
The seamstress added delicate crenelles to the border of the fabric to create a decorative effect.

Etymology

From French *crenelle* (notch), diminutive of *cran* (notch, gap).

2. NOUN

/ krəˈnɛl / (AmE), / krəˈnɛl / (BrE)
A notch or open space between two merlons in a crenellated battlement, used originally for shooting or observation.
notch in a battlement

Examples

The archers fired arrows through each crenelle of the stone walls.
Restorers carefully repaired the broken crenelles on the medieval castle.
From the crenelle, the guards had a clear view of the approaching enemy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embrasure - a small opening in a battlement for shooting through
  • Antonym: merlon - the solid upright section of a battlement between crenelles

Etymology

From Old French 'crenel', diminutive of 'cren' (notch), related to 'crenellated'.
