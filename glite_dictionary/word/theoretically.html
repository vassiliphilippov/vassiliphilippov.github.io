theoretically

1. ADV

/ ˌθiəˈrɛtɪkli / (AmE), / ˌθɪəˈrɛtɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to the theory of a subject rather than its practical application.
in a theoretical manner

Examples

Theoretically, anyone can learn a new language with enough practice.
The plan works theoretically, but there are issues in implementation.
Theoretically, this method should produce faster results than the traditional approach.

Synset Examples

he worked the problem out theoretically

Related Terms

  • Antonym: practically - in a practical manner; relating to real situations or actions

Etymology

From 'theoretical' + '-ly'. 'Theoretical' from Late Latin 'theoreticus' (contemplative, speculative).

2. ADV

/ ˌθiəˈrɛtɪkli / (AmE), / ˌθɪəˈrɛtɪkli / (BrE)
according to theory or in principle, rather than in practice; based on hypothetical or assumed facts
in theory; in principle

Examples

Theoretically, this plan should save us a lot of money, but there may be hidden costs.
Theoretically, light can travel forever in a vacuum without losing speed.
Theoretically, anyone can win the lottery, but the odds are extremely low.

Synset Examples

on paper the candidate seems promising

Related Terms

  • Antonym: practically - in practice; in reality

Etymology

From theory + -ical + -ly; ultimately from Greek theōria (contemplation).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00171356-r
Synset POS: r
