forest

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔːrɪst / (AmE), / ˈfɒrɪst / (BrE)
A large area of land covered chiefly with trees and undergrowth.
large area covered with trees

Examples

The Amazon rainforest is the largest forest in the world and is home to diverse wildlife.
We took a hike through the dense forest to enjoy the peaceful surroundings.
Several species of birds nest only in old-growth forests where the canopy is thick and undisturbed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodland - land covered with trees and shrubs, usually smaller than a forest
  • Antonym: desert - area with little vegetation, especially few or no trees

Etymology

From Old French *forest*, from Late Latin *foresta* (open wood).

2. VERB

/ ˈfɔr.əst / (AmE), / ˈfɒr.ɪst / (BrE)
to plant trees or establish a forest on land that previously did not have one
to plant trees on new land

Examples

The government plans to forest the area to combat soil erosion.
Volunteers helped forest the barren hillside last spring.
Many countries are working to forest land that was previously used for agriculture.

Synset Examples

afforest the mountains

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to afforest - to convert land into a forest, especially by planting trees
  • Antonym: to deforest - to clear trees or forests from land

Etymology

From Old French *forest*, from Medieval Latin *foresta* (open wood).

3. NOUN

/ ˈfɔr.ɪst / (AmE), / ˈfɒr.ɪst / (BrE)
a large area covered chiefly with trees and undergrowth, typically larger and denser than woods
large area of dense trees and plants

Examples

We hiked through the ancient forest, listening to the sounds of birds and rustling leaves.
Much of the country's forest has been cleared for farmland.
A variety of animals, from deer to foxes, make their home in the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodland - land covered with trees, less extensive than a forest
  • Synonym: jungle - land densely covered with tropical vegetation
  • Antonym: desert - arid land with sparse vegetation, opposite environment to a forest

Etymology

From Old French *forest*, from Medieval Latin *foresta* (open wood), originally meaning ‘outside’ (Latin *foris*).
