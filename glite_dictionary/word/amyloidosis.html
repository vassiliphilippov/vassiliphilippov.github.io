amyloidosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌæm.ə.lɔɪˈdoʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌam.ɪ.lɔɪˈdəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
a rare medical condition in which abnormal protein (amyloid) deposits build up in organs or tissues, disrupting their normal function
disease: abnormal amyloid protein deposits
medical

Examples

The diagnosis of amyloidosis was confirmed after a biopsy of his kidney.
Amyloidosis can affect various organs, including the heart and liver.
Early treatment of underlying conditions may help prevent the development of amyloidosis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amyloid - abnormal protein that accumulates in amyloidosis

Etymology

From amyloid + -osis, denoting a disease or abnormal condition.
