pit-a-pat

1. ADV

/ ˈpɪt̬.əˌpæt / (AmE), / ˈpɪt.əˌpæt / (BrE)
With a quick, light, and repeated rhythm, as of footsteps or a heartbeat.
with a quick, light rhythm

Examples

Raindrops fell pit-a-pat on the roof during the night.
Her heart went pit-a-pat as she waited for the results.
Little feet raced pit-a-pat across the wooden floor.

Synset Examples

he came running pit-a-pat down the hall

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patter - make a quick succession of light tapping sounds

Etymology

Imitative; from the sound produced by quick, light beats.

2. ADV

/ ˈpɪt̬.əˌpæt / (AmE), / ˈpɪt.əˌpæt / (BrE)
with a light, repeated, and rhythmic beating or tapping sound; in a manner resembling rapid heartbeats or footsteps.
with a rhythmic beating sound

Examples

Raindrops fell pit-a-pat on the roof all night long.
Her heart beat pit-a-pat as she waited for the results.
The children's feet ran pit-a-pat down the hallway early in the morning.

Synset Examples

his heart went pit-a-pat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitter-patter - a rapid series of light sounds, as of rain or small feet

Etymology

Imitative; an onomatopoeia representing rhythmic tapping sounds, such as a heartbeat or footsteps.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00426498-r
Synset POS: r
