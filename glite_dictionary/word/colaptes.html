Colaptes

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈlæptiːz / (AmE), / kəʊˈlæptiːz / (BrE)
A genus of woodpeckers, commonly known as flickers, found in the Americas.
genus of flickers (woodpeckers)

Examples

The Colaptes genus includes several species of flickers found across North and South America.
Scientists observed a Colaptes foraging for ants on the forest floor.
Many birdwatchers are excited to spot a member of the Colaptes genus during migration season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flicker - a type of woodpecker in the genus Colaptes
  • Degree Higher: woodpecker - a bird of the family Picidae

Etymology

From Ancient Greek 'kolaptēs', meaning 'chiseller' or 'pecker', referring to the bird's wood-pecking habits.
