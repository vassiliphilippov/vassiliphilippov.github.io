oceanic

1. ADJ

/ ˌoʊʃiˈænɪk / (AmE), / ˌəʊsiˈænɪk / (BrE)
relating to, occurring in, or living in the open ocean, especially far from land.
of or pertaining to the open ocean

Examples

The Galápagos Islands are known for their unique oceanic wildlife.
Scientists study oceanic currents to understand climate patterns.
Many seabirds have adapted to a predominantly oceanic lifestyle.

Synset Examples

oceanic islands like Bermuda
oceanic currents
oceanic birds
pelagic organisms
pelagic whaling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pelagic - relating to the open sea rather than coastal or inland waters

Etymology

From French 'océanique', from Latin 'Oceanus' (Ocean).

2. ADJ

/ ˌoʊʃiˈænɪk / (AmE), / ˌəʊʃɪˈænɪk / (BrE)
Extremely vast or limitless in extent, degree, or amount; resembling the ocean in size or scale.
vast or limitless like the ocean

Examples

Her oceanic grief could not be comforted by anyone around her.
The writer described the oceanic silence of the empty desert at night.
He felt an oceanic sense of happiness after hearing the news.

Synset Examples

the oceanic violence of his rage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boundless - having no boundaries; unlimited
  • Synonym: limitless - without any limit or end

Etymology

From ocean + -ic, patterned after similar English adjectives.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01418932-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ oʊˈʃæniɪk / (AmE), / ˌəʊsiˈænɪk / (BrE)
relating to or living in the open sea, especially away from the coast or continental shelf
of or living in the open sea

Examples

Marine biologists study many oceanic species that never come near the shore.
The oceanic climate is typically mild with frequent rainfall.
She is fascinated by oceanic currents and their effect on global weather.

Synset Examples

oceanic waters
oceanic life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pelagic - relating to the open sea rather than near the coast
  • Antonym: coastal - relating to or situated on the coast

Etymology

From Latin oceanicus, from Oceanus, the god and personification of the sea in Greek mythology.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01384122-s
Synset POS: s
