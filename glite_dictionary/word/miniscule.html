miniscule

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɪnɪˌskjul / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪˌskjuːl / (BrE)
Extremely small; tiny.
extremely small

Examples

The miniscule details in the artwork are easy to miss at first glance.
They found a miniscule crack in the foundation that required immediate repair.
Even a miniscule error could ruin the entire experiment.

Synset Examples

a minuscule kitchen
a minuscule amount of rain fell

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tiny - very small in size
  • Antonym: huge - very large in size

Etymology

From Latin 'minusculus' meaning 'rather small', via French 'minuscule'. Often spelled 'minuscule'.
