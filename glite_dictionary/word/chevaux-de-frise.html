chevaux-de-frise

1. NOUN

/ ʃəˌvoʊ də ˈfriz / (AmE), / ʃəˌvəʊ də ˈfriːz / (BrE)
a defensive barrier consisting of a movable wooden frame studded with spikes or barbed wire, used to obstruct cavalry or enemy troops
movable defensive barrier with spikes

Examples

During the siege, soldiers erected chevaux-de-frise along the outer walls to slow the enemy advance.
The museum's courtyard displayed a reconstructed chevaux-de-frise as an example of historical military engineering.
In the open field, the cavalry found their way blocked by a series of chevaux-de-frise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barricade - barrier to block passage
  • Synonym: obstacle - physical object blocking movement

Etymology

From French, literally 'horses of Friesland', referring to origin in Friesland.
