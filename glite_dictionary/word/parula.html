Parula

1. NOUN

/ pəˈruːlə / (AmE), / pəˈruːlə / (BrE)
A genus of small, brightly colored American wood warblers, formerly regarded as the type genus of the Parulidae family.
genus of American wood warblers

Examples

The Parula is known for its vibrant plumage and melodic songs.
Some birdwatchers travel specifically to observe members of the Parula genus in their natural habitat.
Scientists have reclassified several species that once belonged to the Parula group.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Spanish 'paraulata', an old name for this type of bird.
