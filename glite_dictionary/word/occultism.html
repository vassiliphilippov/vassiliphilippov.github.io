occultism

1. NOUN

/ əˈkʌlˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / əˈkʌltɪzəm / (BrE)
the study and practice of supernatural, mystical, or magical beliefs, powers, or phenomena
study or practice of the supernatural

Examples

She became interested in occultism after reading a book about ancient rituals.
Some people study occultism to learn more about supernatural forces.
The rise of occultism in the nineteenth century influenced art and literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mysticism - belief that direct knowledge of spiritual truth can be attained through meditation or insight

Etymology

From Latin 'occultus' (hidden, secret) + '-ism'.

2. NOUN

/ əˈkʌlˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / əˈkʌltɪzəm / (BrE)
The belief in supernatural powers and the practice of attempting to control or understand them through mystic or esoteric means.
belief in and study of supernatural powers

Examples

Many people became interested in occultism after reading about ancient mystical traditions.
The novelist's fascination with occultism is evident in her depiction of secret rituals.
He devoted his life to the study and practice of occultism in search of hidden knowledge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiritualism - belief that spirits of the dead can communicate with the living
  • Antonym: rationalism - the practice of basing opinions and actions on reason and knowledge rather than religious belief or emotion

Etymology

From Latin 'occultus' meaning 'hidden, secret', with the suffix '-ism' denoting a belief or practice.
