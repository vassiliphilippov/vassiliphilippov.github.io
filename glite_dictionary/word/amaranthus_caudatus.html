Amaranthus caudatus

1. NOUN

/ ˌæməˈrænθəs kɔːˈdeɪtəs / (AmE), / ˌaməˈranθəs kɔːˈdɑːtəs / (BrE)
A species of amaranth plant, commonly cultivated for its edible leaves and seeds, which are used as vegetables and cereals.
species of amaranth used for food

Examples

The leaves of Amaranthus caudatus are a nutritious addition to many traditional dishes.
Farmers in South America often grow Amaranthus caudatus for its grain-like seeds.
Researchers are studying the potential health benefits of consuming Amaranthus caudatus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amaranth - a group of flowering plants with edible leaves and seeds

Etymology

From New Latin, with 'Amaranthus' from Greek 'amarantos' (unfading) and 'caudatus' from Latin (tailed), referring to the plant's long, drooping flower clusters.
