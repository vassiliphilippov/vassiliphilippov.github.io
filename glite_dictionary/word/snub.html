snub

1. ADJ

/ snʌb / (AmE), / snʌb / (BrE)
(of a nose or similar feature) unusually short, turned up, or blunt in shape.
short or turned-up (usually of a nose)

Examples

She always wished she had a slightly less snub nose.
The bulldog's snub muzzle gives it a distinctive look.
The statue's snub features suggested a blunt, youthful face.

Synset Examples

a snub nose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blunt - having a worn-down edge or point; not sharp (can refer to shapes/features)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01440142-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ snʌb / (AmE), / snʌb / (BrE)
an act of ignoring or refusing to acknowledge someone, especially as a social slight
an act of ignoring someone

Examples

After years of friendship, his snub at the reunion was deeply hurtful.
The actress took the public snub from the committee with surprising grace.
Everyone noticed the obvious snub when she walked past her old colleague without saying hello.

Synset Examples

the snub was clearly intentional

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slight - an insult made by treating someone as unimportant
  • Synonym: affront - an action or remark that causes outrage or offense

Etymology

From Old Norse *snubba* (to scold, reprove).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01228486-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ snʌb / (AmE), / snʌb / (BrE)
to deliberately ignore or reject someone or something in a blunt or disrespectful way
ignore or reject bluntly

Examples

After not receiving an invitation, she felt deeply hurt that her friends had snubbed her.
The actor snubbed reporters outside the theater, refusing to answer any questions.
He snubbed our offer without even considering it.

Synset Examples

She snubbed his proposal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to someone or something

Etymology

Originates from a Scandinavian source; related to Old Norse 'snubba' meaning 'to scold, rebuke'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00800201-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ snʌb / (AmE), / snʌb / (BrE)
to deliberately ignore or refuse to acknowledge someone, especially as a way of showing disapproval or disdain
deliberately ignore or refuse to acknowledge

Examples

She felt hurt when her colleague snubbed her in front of the entire team.
He snubbed his old friend at the party by pretending not to recognize him.
Despite being greeted warmly, the celebrity snubbed the fans waiting outside the theatre.

Synset Examples

She cut him dead at the meeting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ignore - to pay no attention to someone or something
  • Antonym: to acknowledge - to recognize or admit the existence of someone

Etymology

Probably from Old Norse *snubba* (to scold, reproach).

Source: WordNet
