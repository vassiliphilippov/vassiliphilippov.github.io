possibly

1. ADV

/ ˈpɑːsəbli / (AmE), / ˈpɒsəbli / (BrE)
Perhaps; used to indicate that something might happen or be true.
perhaps; by chance

Examples

She will possibly join us for dinner if she finishes work early.
Could you possibly help me carry these boxes upstairs?
He possibly forgot to send the email, which is why we haven't heard back.

Synset Examples

perhaps she will call tomorrow
we may possibly run into them at the concert
it may peradventure be thought that there never was such a time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perhaps - used to express uncertainty or possibility
  • Antonym: certainly - without doubt; definitely
  • Antonym: definitely - without any doubt; certainly

Etymology

From Latin possibilis (able to be done) via Old French possible and Middle English.

2. ADV

/ ˈpɑːsəbli / (AmE), / ˈpɒsəbli / (BrE)
Used to indicate that something may happen or be true, or to emphasize that something cannot or could not happen.
perhaps; by any chance

Examples

Do you think it would possibly be ready by Friday?
We can't possibly finish this project in just two days.
Is there any way he could possibly know about our plans?

Synset Examples

they can't possibly get here in time for the funeral?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: probably - more likely than not; very likely
  • Synonym: perhaps - something is possible but not certain

Etymology

From Latin 'possibilis' (that can be done).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00301868-r
Synset POS: r
