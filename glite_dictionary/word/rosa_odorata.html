Rosa odorata

1. NOUN

/ ˌroʊzə oʊˈdɔrə̩tə / (AmE), / ˌrəʊzə əʊˈdɔːrətə / (BrE)
A species of tea-scented Chinese rose with pink or yellow flowers, often used in the development of hybrid bush roses.
species of tea-scented Chinese rose

Examples

The garden's centerpiece was a beautiful Rosa odorata with delicate pink blossoms and a sweet fragrance.
Many modern hybrid teas have been bred from Rosa odorata to enhance their scent and color.
Botanists value Rosa odorata for its contribution to rose cultivation and breeding.

Etymology

From Latin: 'rosa' (rose) and 'odorata' (fragrant).
