habitual

1. ADJ

/ həˈbɪtʃuəl / (AmE), / həˈbɪtjʊəl / (BrE)
done or experienced regularly; usual or customary.
regular; usual or customary

Examples

He nodded in his habitual way when he agreed with something.
The restaurant is busy at its habitual lunch hour.
Her habitual kindness made her popular among her classmates.

Synset Examples

his accustomed thoroughness
took his customary morning walk
his habitual comment
with her wonted candor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: customary - usual, according to custom
  • Antonym: occasional - not occurring often; infrequent

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'habitualis', from Latin 'habitus' (condition, character).
