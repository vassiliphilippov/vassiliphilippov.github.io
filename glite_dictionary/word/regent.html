regent

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈriːdʒənt / (BrE)
A member of a governing board, particularly in certain universities or institutions, who is responsible for overseeing policy and administration.
member of a governing board

Examples

She was elected as a regent of the university to help guide its future direction.
The board of regents met to discuss changes to the college curriculum.
Serving as a regent requires a commitment to the institution's long-term goals.

Etymology

From Latin regens (ruling), present participle of regere (to rule).

2. NOUN

/ ˈriːdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈriːdʒənt / (BrE)
a person who governs a country in place of a monarch who is absent, incapacitated, or too young to rule
one who governs in a monarch's absence

Examples

The council appointed a regent to rule until the prince came of age.
Queen Sophia served as regent during her son's minority.
When the king fell seriously ill, a trusted noble acted as regent for the realm.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monarch - a sovereign head of state, especially a king, queen, or emperor

Etymology

From Latin 'regent-', present participle of 'regere' (to rule).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10535710-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈriːdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈriːdʒənt / (BrE)
Acting as a regent; temporarily ruling in place of a monarch or leader.
temporarily ruling in place of a monarch

Examples

The regent government implemented new policies during the king's absence.
During her son's minority, she served as the regent queen of the country.
A regent authority managed all royal affairs until the rightful heir came of age.

Synset Examples

prince-regent

Etymology

From Latin regens (ruling), from regere (to rule).
