toque

1. NOUN

/ toʊk / (AmE), / təʊk / (BrE)
A small, round, close-fitting woman's hat, often without a brim.
small round woman's hat

Examples

She wore a stylish toque to the wedding.
In the 1920s, the toque became popular among fashionable women.
Her grandmother gave her a velvet toque as a gift.

Etymology

From French 'toque', originally meaning a kind of cap.

2. NOUN

/ toʊk / (AmE), / təʊk / (BrE)
a tall, white, pleated hat with a pouched crown, traditionally worn by chefs
chef's tall white hat

Examples

The head chef wore a crisp white toque as he prepared the evening specials.
All of the cooks in the kitchen adjusted their toques before service began.
She recognized the famous chef instantly by his trademark toque and confident demeanor.

Etymology

From French 'toque' (hat), possibly from Spanish, originally denoting a type of hat.
