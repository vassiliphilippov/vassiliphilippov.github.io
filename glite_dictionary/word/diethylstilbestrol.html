diethylstilbestrol

1. NOUN

/ daɪˌɛθəlstɪlˈbɛstrɔl / (AmE), / daɪˌiːθɛlˌstɪlˈbɛstrɒl / (BrE)
a synthetic, potent estrogen formerly used in medicine and as a growth promoter in livestock and poultry feed
synthetic potent estrogen hormone
medical

Examples

The physician explained that diethylstilbestrol was once widely prescribed to pregnant women.
Researchers have studied the long-term effects of diethylstilbestrol exposure on offspring.
Diethylstilbestrol use in animal feed has been banned in many countries due to health concerns.

Etymology

From di- (two), ethyl (a chemical group), and stilbene (type of hydrocarbon), referring to its chemical structure.
