damosel

1. NOUN

/ ˈdæməzəl / (AmE), / ˈdaməzl / (BrE)
An archaic term for a young unmarried woman; a damsel.
archaic: young unmarried woman
archaic

Examples

The knight pledged to rescue the damosel from peril.
In the old tale, a fair damosel awaited her champion.
Poets have often sung of a damosel with golden hair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damsel - a young unmarried woman

Etymology

From Old French 'demoiselle' meaning 'young lady'.
