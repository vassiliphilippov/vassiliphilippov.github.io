flintstone

1. NOUN

/ ˈflɪntˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈflɪntˌstəʊn / (BrE)
A small piece of flint, especially one used in masonry construction or as a building stone.
small piece of flint used in construction

Examples

The old church walls were built using local flintstone.
Masons sometimes prefer flintstone for its durability and unique appearance.
We collected several pieces of flintstone from the riverbank for our garden wall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flint - a hard gray or black mineral used for tools and fire-starting

Etymology

From 'flint' (a hard type of quartz) + 'stone'.
