ken

1. NOUN

/ kɛn / (AmE), / kɛn / (BrE)
the range of what can be seen or known; sight or perception
the range of vision or perception
archaic

Examples

The ship slowly disappeared beyond my ken as it sailed toward the horizon.
These distant mountains are just within the ken of our lookout.
The island lay far outside the ken of most sailors.

Synset Examples

out of sight of land

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sight - the range of vision
  • Synonym: perception - the ability to see, hear, or become aware of something

Etymology

From Scots, originally from Old English 'cennan' (to make known) and Old Norse 'kenna' (to know, recognize).

2. NOUN

/ kɛn / (AmE), / kɛn / (BrE)
the range of knowledge or understanding that someone has
range of knowledge or understanding

Examples

The science behind quantum mechanics is truly beyond my ken.
She spoke of things outside the ken of ordinary people.
Problems of such complexity are sometimes beyond the ken of even the smartest minds.

Synset Examples

beyond my ken

Etymology

From Scots and Middle English 'ken' (to know), from Old English 'cennan', related to Old Norse 'kenna' (to know, perceive).
