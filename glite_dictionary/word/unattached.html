unattached

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.əˈtætʃt / (AmE), / ˌʌn.əˈtætʃt / (BrE)
not involved in a romantic or exclusive sexual relationship; single.
not in a romantic relationship

Examples

After her breakup, she enjoyed being unattached for a while.
He prefers to stay unattached so he can travel without commitments.
Are you unattached at the moment, or seeing someone?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: single - not married or in a romantic relationship
  • Antonym: attached - in a romantic or exclusive relationship

Etymology

Prefix un- (not) + attached.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00159672-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.əˈtætʃt / (AmE), / ˌʌn.əˈtætʃt / (BrE)
not fastened, fixed, or joined to anything; free or able to move about.
not attached; free to move

Examples

The unattached larvae drifted with the ocean currents.
Without any roots, the unattached plants floated freely on the water's surface.
After breaking free from the reef, the coral polyps became unattached and mobile.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: attached - fastened or joined to something

Etymology

Formed from un- (not) + attached (from Old French *atachier*, 'to fasten').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00161553-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˌʌnəˈtætʃt / (AmE), / ˌʌnəˈtætʃt / (BrE)
not fastened or joined to anything; separate or loose.
not fastened or joined together

Examples

The wires were left unattached and posed a safety hazard.
Make sure no papers are unattached before submitting your application.
The painting hung unattached on the wall, resting on a shelf.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: attached - fastened or joined to something

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (not) + 'attached', from Old French attacher (to attach).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00571145-s
Synset POS: s
