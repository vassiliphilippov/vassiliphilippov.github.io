blunderer

1. NOUN

/ ˈblʌndəɹɚ / (AmE), / ˈblʌndərə / (BrE)
a person who often makes serious mistakes, especially through clumsiness or incompetence
person prone to serious mistakes

Examples

Despite his best intentions, James was known as a blunderer in the office.
The project failed because a single blunderer was left in charge of key details.
She tried to overcome her reputation as a blunderer by double-checking her work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bungler - a person who habitually does things clumsily or incompetently
  • Antonym: expert - a person with a high degree of skill or knowledge

Etymology

From 'blunder' (to make a serious mistake) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
