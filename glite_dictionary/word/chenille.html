chenille

1. NOUN

/ ʃəˈnil / (AmE), / ʃəˈniːl / (BrE)
a soft, tufted cord with a velvety texture, used in embroidery and for trimming fabrics
soft, tufted cord for embroidery

Examples

The pillow was edged with chenille to give it a luxurious feel.
She chose blue chenille for her embroidery project because of its softness.
The blanket was woven with chenille for extra warmth and texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: velvet - a soft, closely woven fabric with a thick pile

Etymology

From French 'chenille' meaning 'caterpillar', referring to its furry appearance.

2. NOUN

/ ʃəˈnil / (AmE), / ʃəˈniːl / (BrE)
A velvety, heavy fabric made with chenille yarn, commonly used for bedspreads and rugs.
soft, tufted fabric for furnishings

Examples

The antique armchair was reupholstered in a luxurious chenille.
She bought a pink chenille bedspread for the guest room.
Soft chenille rugs add warmth and color to the hardwood floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: velvet - a soft, closely woven fabric

Etymology

From French chenille (caterpillar), referring to the yarn's furry appearance.
