Felidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːlɪˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈfiːlɪdiː / (BrE)
The biological family of mammals that includes all modern and extinct cats, such as domestic cats, wildcats, lions, leopards, and cheetahs.
the cat family of mammals

Examples

All members of the Felidae family are carnivorous and share many physical characteristics.
The cheetah, as part of Felidae, is known for being the fastest land animal.
Conservationists are concerned about the declining populations of many species within Felidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cat - small domesticated carnivorous mammal, member of Felidae
  • Antonym: Canidae - the dog family of mammals; wild dogs, wolves, foxes

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Latin 'felis' (cat) and scientific suffix '-idae' (family).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfi·lɪˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈfiː.lɪ.diː / (BrE)
a family of mammals that includes all cats, both big and small, such as lions, tigers, leopards, cheetahs, and domestic cats
the cat family of mammals

Examples

The Felidae family includes both the domestic cat and large wild species like the tiger.
Scientists study Felidae to understand the evolution of carnivorous mammals.
Many members of the Felidae family are endangered due to habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cat - small domesticated mammal; member of Felidae
  • Synonym: lion - large wild member of Felidae, often called the king of the jungle

Etymology

From Latin *felis* (cat) plus taxonomic suffix *-idae* used for animal families.
