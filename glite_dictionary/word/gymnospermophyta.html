Gymnospermophyta

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪm.nəˌspɜr.məˈfaɪ.tə / (AmE), / dʒɪm.nəˌspɜː.məˈfaɪ.tə / (BrE)
A taxonomic division of plants characterized by having seeds that are not enclosed in an ovary, including conifers, cycads, ginkgo, and gnetophytes.
division of plants with naked seeds

Examples

Gymnospermophyta include conifers such as pines and firs, as well as cycads and ginkgo.
The evolutionary history of Gymnospermophyta provides insight into how plants adapted to terrestrial life.
Researchers classified the rare specimen as a member of the Gymnospermophyta division.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: angiosperm - plants with seeds enclosed in an ovary (flowering plants)

Etymology

From Greek 'gymnos' (naked) + 'sperma' (seed) + 'phyta' (plants).

2. NOUN

/ dʒɪmˌnoʊspɚˈmɑfɪtə / (AmE), / dʒɪmˌnɒspəˈmɒfɪtə / (BrE)
a division of plants, known as gymnosperms, that have seeds not enclosed in an ovary, including conifers, cycads, ginkgoes, and gnetophytes
division of seed-producing plants without ovaries

Examples

The Gymnospermophyta include familiar trees such as pines, firs, and spruces.
Researchers identified ancient fossils belonging to the Gymnospermophyta in the rock strata.
Unlike flowering plants, members of Gymnospermophyta do not produce fruits.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: angiosperm - a plant that has seeds enclosed within an ovary (fruit)

Etymology

From Greek 'gymnos' (naked) + 'sperma' (seed) + '-phyta' (plants).
