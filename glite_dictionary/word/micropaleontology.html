micropaleontology

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪ.kroʊˌpeɪ.li.ɑnˈtɑl.ə.dʒi / (AmE), / ˌmaɪ.krəʊˌpæ.lɪ.ɒnˈtɒl.ə.dʒi / (BrE)
The branch of paleontology that studies microfossils, which are fossils of microscopic organisms.
study of microfossils in paleontology
medical

Examples

She published a groundbreaking paper in micropaleontology focusing on the diversity of foraminifera.
Advances in micropaleontology have helped researchers understand ancient climate changes.
During the lecture, the professor highlighted the importance of micropaleontology in oil exploration.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: paleontology - the scientific study of the history of life on Earth through fossils

Etymology

From micro- (small) + paleontology, meaning the study of ancient life.
