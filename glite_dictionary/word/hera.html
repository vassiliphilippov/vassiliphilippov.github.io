Hera

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɛrə / (AmE), / ˈhɪərə / (BrE)
the queen of the Olympian gods in Greek mythology, sister and wife of Zeus, known for her jealousy and identified with the Roman goddess Juno
queen of the Greek gods; wife of Zeus

Examples

In Greek mythology, Hera is often depicted as a powerful and vengeful goddess.
Many ancient temples were dedicated to Hera across the Greek world.
Hera was famous for punishing the lovers and offspring of Zeus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Juno - Roman goddess identified with Hera; queen of the gods

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἥρα (Hēra), the Greek goddess.

2. PROPN

/ ˈhɛr.ə / (AmE), / ˈhɪə.rə / (BrE)
in Greek mythology, the queen of the Olympian gods, sister and wife of Zeus, known for her jealousy and association with marriage and family; identified with the Roman goddess Juno
queen of the Olympian gods (Greek mythology)

Examples

Ancient Greeks worshipped Hera as the protector of marriage and women.
In many myths, Hera's jealousy leads her to punish Zeus's lovers.
The peacock is often seen as a sacred animal of Hera in classical art.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Juno - Roman goddess equivalent to Hera, queen of the gods

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἥρα (Hēra), the name of the goddess.
