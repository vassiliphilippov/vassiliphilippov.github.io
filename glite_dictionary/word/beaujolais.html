Beaujolais

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈʒoʊleɪ / (AmE), / bəʊˈʒɒleɪ / (BrE)
A dry, fruity, light red wine, typically consumed soon after production, made from grapes in the Beaujolais district of southeastern France.
light, fruity red wine from Beaujolais, France

Examples

We celebrated the harvest with a bottle of Beaujolais and a hearty meal.
The fresh and lively flavors of Beaujolais pair well with roast chicken.
This year's Beaujolais is especially aromatic and easy to drink.

Etymology

From French Beaujolais, the name of the wine-producing district.
