phrase

1. NOUN

/ freɪz / (AmE), / freɪz / (BrE)
a short musical passage that acts as a unit within a longer composition
short musical passage

Examples

The violinist played a delicate phrase that lingered in the air.
Each phrase in the symphony builds upon the previous musical idea.
He struggled to memorize the closing phrase of the piano piece.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motif - a recurring musical idea or figure

Etymology

From Greek 'phrasis' (speech, way of speaking) via Latin 'phrasis' and French 'phrase'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07059411-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ freɪz / (AmE), / freɪz / (BrE)
to divide or mark into phrases, especially in music or speech, by grouping words, notes, or passages in a way that makes sense and flows naturally
to divide or mark into phrases

Examples

You should phrase the melody carefully to enhance the musical expression.
The teacher asked the student to phrase the sentence more clearly for the class.
He phrased each passage beautifully during his piano recital.

Synset Examples

phrase a musical passage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: articulate - to express or pronounce clearly
  • Synonym: segment - to divide into separate parts
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00963146-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ freɪz / (AmE), / freɪz / (BrE)
A small group of words that forms a constituent within a sentence and has a particular meaning, especially one that does not contain both a subject and a verb.
group of words forming a unit

Examples

The phrase 'on the table' tells you where the book is.
She used the phrase 'time flies' in her presentation.
Can you explain what this phrase means in the text?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clause - a group of words containing a subject and a verb
  • Synonym: expression - a word or group of words with a particular meaning

Etymology

From French 'phrase', from Latin 'phrasis', from Greek 'phrasis' (speech, way of speaking).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06327041-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ freɪz / (AmE), / freɪz / (BrE)
to express (something) in words, often choosing particular wording or style
express in words; word a message

Examples

She carefully phrased her apology to avoid hurting anyone's feelings.
Can you phrase your question more clearly?
The president phrased his statement to emphasize unity.

Synset Examples

He formulated his concerns to the board of trustees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to formulate - to express precisely and systematically
  • Synonym: to rephrase - to express differently or in other words
  • Antonym: to misstate - to state wrongly or inaccurately

Etymology

From Greek 'phrasis' (speech, way of speaking), via Latin and Old French.

Source: WordNet
