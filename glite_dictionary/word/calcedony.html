calcedony

1. NOUN

/ kælˈsɛdəni / (AmE), / kælˈsɛdəni / (BrE)
a milky or greyish translucent to transparent form of quartz, often used as a gemstone
translucent variety of quartz

Examples

The museum's collection includes a rare calcedony carving from ancient Rome.
She admired the pale blue calcedony pendant set in silver.
Geologists often study the formation and properties of calcedony in volcanic rock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agate - a banded variety of chalcedony
  • Synonym: jasper - an opaque variety of chalcedony

Etymology

From Latin 'chalcedonius', from Greek 'Khalkēdōn', the city of Chalcedon in Asia Minor.
