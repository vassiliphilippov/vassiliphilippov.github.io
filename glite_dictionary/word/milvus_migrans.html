Milvus migrans

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪlvəs ˈmaɪɡrænz / (AmE), / ˈmɪlvəs ˈmɪɡrans / (BrE)
A dark-colored bird of prey of the Old World, commonly known as the black kite, which feeds chiefly on carrion.
Old World bird of prey, black kite

Examples

The Milvus migrans is easily identified by its forked tail and agile flight.
Ecologists observed that Milvus migrans often scavenge in urban areas.
During migration season, large flocks of Milvus migrans can be seen soaring over the countryside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: black kite - common name for Milvus migrans, a bird of prey

Etymology

From Latin: Milvus (kite) and migrans (migrating, wandering).
