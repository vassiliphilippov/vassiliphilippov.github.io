Gyromitra

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒaɪrəˈmaɪtrə / (AmE), / ˌdʒaɪrəʊˈmaɪtrə / (BrE)
a genus of fungi in the family Helvellaceae, characterized by irregularly shaped, tan to brown fruiting bodies, some species of which are known as false morels and can be toxic.
a genus of fungi (false morels)

Examples

Some species in the genus Gyromitra are poisonous and can be mistaken for edible morels.
The distinctive lobed appearance of Gyromitra makes it recognizable among wild mushrooms.
Researchers studied the toxins present in Gyromitra to understand their effects on human health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: false morel - a common name for several species of Gyromitra, distinguishable from true morels

Etymology

From Greek 'gyros' (circle) and 'mitra' (headband or head), referring to the fungi's convoluted shape.
