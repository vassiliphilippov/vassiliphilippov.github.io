Ariidae

1. NOUN

/ əˈraɪɪˌdiː / (AmE), / əˈraɪɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of catfish, commonly called sea catfishes, found mainly in marine and brackish waters.
family of sea catfishes

Examples

The Ariidae are distributed in warm and temperate coastal waters worldwide.
Biologists have documented more than 150 species within the Ariidae family.
Many members of Ariidae are important to local fisheries due to their abundance and size.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea catfish - type of catfish from the Ariidae family

Etymology

From modern Latin Ariidae, based on the genus Arius.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛə.riˈaɪ.diː / (AmE), / ˌæ.rɪˈaɪ.diː / (BrE)
a family of sea catfishes found in tropical and subtropical marine and brackish waters, noted for their whiskered appearance
family of sea catfishes

Examples

The Ariidae family includes many species known as sea catfishes.
Members of the Ariidae are often found in estuarine and coastal waters worldwide.
Researchers studied the reproductive habits of Ariidae in the Indian Ocean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catfish - a diverse group of ray-finned fish with whisker-like barbels

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Arius.
