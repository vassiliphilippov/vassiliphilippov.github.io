gentlewoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛn.tl̩ˌwʊ.mən / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛn.tl̩ˌwʊ.mən / (BrE)
A woman of high social standing, refinement, or good breeding, especially in past times.
refined or high-status woman

Examples

The queen was attended by her loyal gentlewoman during the royal procession.
Emma admired the grace with which the gentlewoman greeted her guests.
In Shakespeare's play, the gentlewoman witnessed Lady Macbeth's sleepwalking.

Synset Examples

a chauffeur opened the door of the limousine for the grand lady

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lady - a woman of high social position or refinement

Etymology

Formed in Middle English from 'gentle' (well-born) and 'woman'.
