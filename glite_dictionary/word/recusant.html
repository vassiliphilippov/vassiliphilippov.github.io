recusant

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɛk.jə.zənt / (AmE), / rɪˈkjuː.zənt / (BrE)
(historical) (of Catholics) refusing to attend services of the Church of England, especially during the period after the English Reformation.
Catholic refusing Anglican church attendance (historical)
archaic

Examples

During Elizabeth I's reign, many families remained openly recusant despite the risk of heavy fines.
The government kept a list of known recusant Catholics in the parish.
Her recusant status brought suspicion from local authorities in seventeenth-century England.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conformist - one who conforms to the practices of the established church

Etymology

From Latin recusare (to refuse); used in post-Reformation England for those refusing Anglican services.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛkjʊzənt / (AmE), / rɪˈkjuːzənt / (BrE)
A person who refuses to comply with established authority, customs, or laws, especially one who does not conform to the practices of an established church.
person who refuses to conform

Examples

During the reign of Elizabeth I, many Catholics were labeled as recusants for refusing to attend Anglican services.
Even today, the term recusant can describe someone who resists societal norms or official regulations.
Historical records detail the penalties imposed on recusants who defied civil or religious authorities.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conformist - someone who conforms to established standards
  • Synonym: nonconformist - person who does not conform to established church or customs

Etymology

From Latin recusant-, present participle of recusare (to refuse).

3. ADJ

/ ˈrɛk.jə.zənt / (AmE), / rɪˈkjuː.zənt / (BrE)
Refusing to submit to authority; not complying with established rules or governing powers.
refusing to submit to authority

Examples

The recusant group refused to accept the new regulations imposed by the city council.
During the trial, several recusant witnesses declined to follow the judge's instructions.
He admired her recusant attitude when she challenged the company's unethical practices.

Synset Examples

the recusant electors … cooperated in electing a new Senate

Related Terms

  • Antonym: compliant - willing to comply or submit to authority
  • Synonym: rebellious - resisting authority or control

Etymology

From Latin recusant-, recusans, present participle of recūsare (to refuse).
