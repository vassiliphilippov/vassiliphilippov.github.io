assuredness

1. NOUN

/ əˈʃʊrd.nəs / (AmE), / əˈʃʊəd.nɪs / (BrE)
confidence and composure, especially in difficult situations; the state of being sure of oneself or calm under pressure.
confidence and composure under stress

Examples

Her assuredness during the interview impressed the hiring committee.
He faced the crisis with remarkable assuredness and clarity.
Public speaking requires a certain level of assuredness that comes from experience.

Synset Examples

keep your cool

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confidence - a feeling or belief that you can do something well
  • Synonym: composure - the state of being calm and in control of oneself
  • Antonym: nervousness - the state of being worried or afraid

Etymology

From 'assured' + '-ness'; 'assured' from Old French 'asseurer', based on Latin 'securus' (secure).
