gollop

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑl.əp / (AmE), / ˈɡɒl.əp / (BrE)
an amount of a substance, usually liquid or semi-liquid, typically added in cooking; similar to a dollop
a small amount, esp. in cooking
informal

Examples

She poured a gollop of cream into her coffee.
The recipe calls for just a gollop of honey to sweeten the dressing.
He couldn't resist adding another gollop of syrup to his pancakes.

Synset Examples

Just give me a gollop of that delicious sauce.
She added a gollop of oil to the frying pan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dollop - a lump or small amount of something, especially food

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑl.əp / (AmE), / ˈɡɒl.əp / (BrE)
a large mouthful, spoonful, or serving of food or drink, taken quickly or greedily
large mouthful or spoonful of food
informal

Examples

She took a gollop of tea before rushing out the door.
He finished the milk in one big gollop.
After hiking for hours, they each enjoyed a hearty gollop of stew.

Synset Examples

He took a big gollop of soup and slurped it down.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gulp - a large and hurried swallow
  • Antonym: sip - a small mouthful of liquid
