to recomp

1. VERB

/ ˈriːkɑmp / (AmE), / ˈriːkɒmp / (BrE)
(informal) To compensate or reward, especially in return for work, service, or effort.
to compensate or reward
informal

Examples

The charity decided to recomp the volunteer for extra travel expenses.
After months of unpaid work, the organization finally agreed to recomp the volunteers.
If we want more help, we should consider how to adequately recomp those who contribute their time.

Synset Examples

We need to recomp the volunteers for their hard work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recompense - to make amends or pay compensation

Etymology

Shortened form of 'recompense,' from Old French 'recompensier.'
