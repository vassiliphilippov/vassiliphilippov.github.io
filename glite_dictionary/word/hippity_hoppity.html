hippity hoppity

1. ADV

/ ˈhɪpɪti ˈhɑpɪti / (AmE), / ˈhɪpɪti ˈhɒpɪti / (BrE)
moving with quick, bouncy, and playful jumps; often used in a lighthearted or childlike context.
moving with playful, bouncy jumps
informal

Examples

The children went hippity hoppity across the lawn, giggling with glee.
A little frog moved hippity hoppity through the wet grass.
She skipped hippity hoppity down the path in her new shoes.

Synset Examples

The kids were hippity hoppity all over the playground.
The rabbit was hippity hoppity through the garden.
She laughed when he said, 'Hippity hoppity get off my property!'
She danced in a hippity hoppity manner at the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bounce - to move quickly up and down in a lively way

Etymology

Reduplicative form from 'hip' and 'hop', imitating a bouncing sound.
