whydah

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪdə / (AmE), / ˈwɪdə / (BrE)
A small African bird of the weaverbird family, typically with mainly black plumage and, in males, long tail feathers during the breeding season.
African weaverbird with long tail feathers

Examples

The male whydah displays its striking tail feathers during courtship.
Many birdwatchers travel to Africa to see the colorful whydah in its natural habitat.
A flock of whydahs was feeding among the tall grasses near the river.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: widowbird - another name for whydah, African weaverbird with long tail feathers

Etymology

From Whydah, a former kingdom and port in present-day Benin, West Africa, from where the bird was first described.
