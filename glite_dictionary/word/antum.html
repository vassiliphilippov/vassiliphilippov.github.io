Antum

1. PROPN

/ ˈæn.tʊm / (AmE), / ˈæn.tʊm / (BrE)
A goddess in Babylonian mythology, known as the consort of Anu, the sky god.
Babylonian goddess, consort of Anu

Examples

In ancient texts, Antum is often invoked alongside Anu.
Scholars believe that Antum's role evolved over time in Babylonian religion.
The temple dedicated to Antum was discovered in the ruins of a Babylonian city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Anu - Babylonian sky god, consort of Antum

Etymology

From Akkadian Antu, female counterpart to Anu.
