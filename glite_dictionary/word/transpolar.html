transpolar

1. ADJ

/ trænsˈpoʊlər / (AmE), / tranzˈpəʊlə / (BrE)
extending across or crossing one of the Earth's poles, especially the North or South Pole.
crossing one of Earth's poles

Examples

The airline introduced a new transpolar flight path to reduce travel time between continents.
A transpolar expedition requires careful planning and specialized equipment for the harsh polar environment.
Scientists monitored transpolar drift of sea ice in the Arctic Ocean.

Synset Examples

transpolar air routes

Etymology

From Latin 'trans' (across) + 'polar' (pertaining to the pole).
