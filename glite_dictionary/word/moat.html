moat

1. NOUN

/ moʊt / (AmE), / məʊt / (BrE)
A deep, wide ditch surrounding a castle, fort, or town, typically filled with water and intended as a defense against attack.
defensive ditch, often water-filled

Examples

The ancient castle was surrounded by a wide moat to keep out invaders.
During the renovation, archaeologists discovered remnants of the original moat.
Some zoos use a moat instead of fences to separate animals from visitors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ditch - a long narrow excavation in the ground

Etymology

From Old French *mote* (mound, embankment, moat).
