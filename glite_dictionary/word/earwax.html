earwax

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪrˌwæks / (AmE), / ˈɪəˌwaks / (BrE)
a yellowish, waxy substance secreted in the ear canal to protect and lubricate the ear
waxy substance in the ear canal

Examples

Make sure not to push earwax further into your ear with cotton swabs.
Excess earwax can sometimes cause difficulty hearing.
The doctor removed a large piece of earwax from the patient's ear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cerumen - the medical term for earwax

Etymology

From 'ear' + 'wax', both of Old English origin.
