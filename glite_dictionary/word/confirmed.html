confirmed

1. ADJ

/ kənˈfɜrmd / (AmE), / kənˈfɜːmd / (BrE)
(of a person or their habit) having a particular habit or belief that is firmly established and unlikely to change.
firmly established in habit or status

Examples

Despite his friends' encouragement, Mark remained a confirmed bachelor well into his fifties.
She is a confirmed skeptic who never accepts anything at face value.
After years of illness, he became a confirmed invalid and rarely left his room.

Synset Examples

a confirmed bachelor
a confirmed invalid

Related Terms

  • Synonym: habitual - done as a habit; usual or typical
  • Synonym: incorrigible - (of a person or their tendencies) not able to be corrected or reformed

Etymology

From Medieval Latin confirmare (to strengthen, make firm).

2. ADJ

/ kənˈfɝːmd / (AmE), / kənˈfɜːmd / (BrE)
(of a fact, arrangement, report, or status) established as true or definite; (of a person) having received the Christian rite of confirmation.
established as true, or having received confirmation

Examples

We received confirmed reports that the event would proceed as planned.
She is a confirmed member of the church and attends every Sunday.
The hotel sent an email with my confirmed reservation details.

Synset Examples

confirmed reservations
received confirmed reports of casualties
a confirmed Catholic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: established - having been in existence for a long time and therefore recognized and generally accepted
  • Antonym: tentative - not certain or fixed; provisional

Etymology

Past participle of 'confirm', from Latin confirmare (to strengthen, establish).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01638538-a
Synset POS: a
