to manducate

1. VERB

/ ˈmæn.dʒuˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈmandjʊkeɪt / (BrE)
to chew (food); to bite and grind with the teeth, especially in a formal or scientific context
to chew food

Examples

Certain reptiles will manducate their prey slowly to aid in digestion.
The scientist observed how the insect would manducate small leaves with its mandibles.
At the ancient feast, guests were expected to manducate politely and not speak with their mouths full.

Synset Examples

He jawed his bubble gum
Chew your food and don't swallow it!
The cows were masticating the grass

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chew - to bite and grind with the teeth
  • Synonym: to masticate - to chew food; grind with the teeth

Etymology

From Latin 'manducare' (to chew).
