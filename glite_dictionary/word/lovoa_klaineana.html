Lovoa klaineana

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈvoʊ.ə kleɪˈniː.ənə / (AmE), / ləʊˈvəʊ.ə kleɪˈniː.ənə / (BrE)
a tropical African timber tree with wood that closely resembles mahogany, valued for its quality and appearance
African timber tree resembling mahogany

Examples

The wood of Lovoa klaineana is prized for its durability and beauty in fine furniture making.
Due to overharvesting, Lovoa klaineana has become less common in some African forests.
A shipment of Lovoa klaineana logs was inspected before export for quality assurance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mahogany - hard reddish-brown timber from several tropical trees

Etymology

Scientific name; genus 'Lovoa' and species commemorating French botanist Auguste Jean Baptiste Chevalier's collaborator, E. Klaine.
