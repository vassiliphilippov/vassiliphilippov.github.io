to intrust

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈtrʌst / (AmE), / ɪnˈtrʌst / (BrE)
to assign responsibility for something to someone; to place trust or something valuable in someone's care
assign responsibility; place trust in
archaic

Examples

He decided to intrust the business to his loyal assistant before departing.
Many people intrust their valuables to banks for safekeeping.
I would not intrust such a delicate matter to anyone but you.

Synset Examples

The messenger was entrusted with the general's secret
I commit my soul to God

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to entrust - to put something into someone's care; assign responsibility

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'entrust', from Middle English 'intrusten', from 'en-' or 'in-' + 'trust'.
