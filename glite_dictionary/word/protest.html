protest

1. VERB

/ proʊˈtɛst / (AmE), / prəˈtɛst / (BrE)
to express strong disagreement or disapproval, especially publicly or formally, through action or words
express strong opposition or objection

Examples

Many students plan to protest the university’s new tuition policy in front of the administration building.
The workers protested against unsafe conditions by staging a walkout.
Citizens are protesting the proposed changes to the environmental law.

Synset Examples

dissent to the laws of the country

Related Terms

  • Synonym: object - to express or feel opposition or disapproval
  • Antonym: support - to give approval or encouragement to something

Etymology

From Latin 'protestari', meaning 'to declare publicly or testify'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈproʊ.tɛst / (AmE), / ˈprəʊ.tɛst / (BrE)
a public demonstration expressing strong objection or disapproval of something, often organized to show dissent or demand change
public demonstration of dissent

Examples

Hundreds of people attended the protest against the new policy in the city square.
The protest turned peaceful after organizers spoke with the police.
Thousands marched in a protest demanding action on climate change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demonstration - a public gathering to express views, often political
  • Synonym: rally - a mass meeting of people showing support or protest
  • Antonym: support - agreement or approval, opposite of protest

Etymology

From Latin 'protestari' (to declare publicly, testify), from 'pro-' (publicly) + 'testari' (to bear witness).
