traditionalist

1. ADJ

/ trəˈdɪʃənəlɪst / (AmE), / trəˈdɪʃənəlɪst / (BrE)
Having a strong adherence to traditional views, especially in a way that is conservative and resistant to change.
strongly conservative, resistant to change

Examples

The committee remained traditionalist in its approach, refusing to consider any new proposals.
Her traditionalist attitude often put her at odds with the more progressive members of the group.
Despite growing calls for reform, the school's traditionalist policies did not change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conservative - favoring traditional views and values; resistant to change
  • Antonym: progressive - favoring innovation and change

Etymology

From 'tradition' + '-alist' by analogy with nouns like 'journalist', subsequently used adjectivally.

2. NOUN

/ trəˈdɪʃənəlɪst / (AmE), / trəˈdɪʃ(ə)n(ə)lɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports or believes in traditional ideas, customs, or values, and resists change or innovation
person who favors traditional views

Examples

As a proud traditionalist, he opposed any reforms to the ceremony.
In debates, traditionalists often argue against modernizing old customs.
The organization is led by a traditionalist who values long-standing practices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conservative - person who is averse to change and holds to traditional values
  • Antonym: progressive - person favoring reform and new ideas

Etymology

From 'tradition' + '-alist', modeled after 'traditional'.

Synset POS: n
