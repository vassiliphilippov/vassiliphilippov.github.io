indecisiveness

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.dɪˈsaɪ.sɪv.nəs / (AmE), / ˌɪn.dɪˈsaɪ.sɪv.nəs / (BrE)
the state of being unable to make decisions quickly or effectively
inability to make decisions

Examples

Her indecisiveness made it difficult for the team to choose a final design.
After hours of indecisiveness, he finally picked a restaurant for dinner.
The company's indecisiveness caused them to miss the best opportunity for investment.

Synset Examples

his indecision was only momentary but the opportunity was lost

Related Terms

  • Antonym: decisiveness - the ability to make decisions quickly and effectively
  • Synonym: indecision - the inability to decide

Etymology

From independent + decisive + -ness, after 'indecisive'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪndɪˈsaɪsɪvnəs / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈsʌɪsɪvnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being unable to make decisions quickly or firmly; a lack of determination or resolve.
lack of decisiveness or resolve

Examples

Her indecisiveness about which job to take frustrated her family and friends.
The team's indecisiveness during the meeting led to missed opportunities.
Leaders must overcome indecisiveness to earn the trust of their followers.

Synset Examples

the king's incurable indecisiveness caused turmoil in his court

Related Terms

  • Antonym: decisiveness - the ability to make decisions quickly and confidently
  • Synonym: irresolution - lack of resolution or determination

Etymology

From in- (not) + decisiveness, based on Latin decisus (determined).
