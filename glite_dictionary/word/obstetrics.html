obstetrics

1. NOUN

/ əbˈstɛtrɪks / (AmE), / əbˈstɛtrɪks / (BrE)
the branch of medicine concerned with childbirth and the care of women giving birth
branch of medicine for childbirth
medical

Examples

She decided to specialize in obstetrics during her medical training.
Advances in obstetrics have greatly improved maternal and infant health.
The hospital has a separate wing dedicated to obstetrics and newborn care.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gynecology - the branch of medicine dealing with women's reproductive systems

Etymology

From Latin 'obstetricius' (relating to a midwife), from 'obstetrix' (midwife).
