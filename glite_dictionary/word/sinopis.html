sinopis

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈnoʊ.pɪs / (AmE), / sɪˈnəʊ.pɪs / (BrE)
a reddish-brown natural earth pigment, formerly used as a red ocher by artists and artisans
a red ocher pigment

Examples

The ancient mural was painted with pigments such as sinopis and lapis lazuli.
Artists in antiquity often relied on sinopis for its rich, reddish tones.
The discovery of sinopis traces in the fresco confirmed the use of natural earth pigments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ocher - an earthy pigment containing ferric oxide, typically with clay, varying from light yellow to brown or red

Etymology

From Latin *sinopis*, from Greek *sinōpis* (red earth from Sinope, a city on the Black Sea).
