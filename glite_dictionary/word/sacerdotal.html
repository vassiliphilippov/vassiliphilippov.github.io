sacerdotal

1. ADJ

/ ˌsæsərˈdoʊtəl / (AmE), / ˌsasəˈdəʊtəl / (BrE)
relating to priests or the priesthood, especially to the belief that priests have special powers or authority.
relating to priests or the priesthood

Examples

The church maintained a sacerdotal structure, emphasizing the authority of ordained priests.
Some theologians rejected sacerdotal claims about the necessity of a priest for valid sacraments.
Her writings critiqued the sacerdotal traditions within the religious community.

Synset Examples

sacerdotal emphasis on the authority of priests

Etymology

From Latin sacerdotalis, from sacerdos meaning 'priest'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌsæsɚˈdoʊtəl / (AmE), / ˌsæsəˈdəʊtəl / (BrE)
relating to priests or the priesthood; priestly.
relating to priests or the priesthood

Examples

The bishop donned his sacerdotal robes for the ceremony.
Their chanting had a distinctly sacerdotal quality, as if invoking sanctity.
The ancient text describes sacerdotal duties in elaborate detail.

Synset Examples

priestly (or sacerdotal) vestments
hieratic gestures

Related Terms

  • Synonym: priestly - relating to priests or priesthood
  • Antonym: secular - not connected with religious or spiritual matters

Etymology

From Latin sacerdotalis, from sacerdos meaning 'priest'.
