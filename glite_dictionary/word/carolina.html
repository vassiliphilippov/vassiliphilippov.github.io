Carolina

1. PROPN

/ ˌkɛrəˈlaɪnə / (AmE), / ˌkarəˈlʌɪnə / (BrE)
the region consisting of the states of North Carolina and South Carolina in the United States, often referred to collectively for historical or cultural reasons
region of North and South Carolina, U.S.

Examples

Many tourists visit the beaches of Carolina each summer, enjoying both the northern and southern coasts.
Barbecue styles in Carolina are famous for their unique flavors and regional differences.
Several species of birds can only be found in the forests of Carolina.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: North Carolina - a state in the southeastern United States
  • Synonym: South Carolina - a state in the southeastern United States

Etymology

From the Latinized form of the name Carolus, referring to King Charles I of England. The territory was named in his honor.
