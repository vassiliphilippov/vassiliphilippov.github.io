linearity

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪniˈærəti / (AmE), / ˌlɪniˈærɪti / (BrE)
the property of having only one dimension, like a line; the state or quality of being linear
the property of being one-dimensional or linear

Examples

The linearity of the thread made it easy to measure its length.
In geometry, a line exhibits perfect linearity as it extends only in one direction.
Scientists study linearity when analyzing the simplest possible forms in space.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: planarity - the property of being planar or two-dimensional

Etymology

From linear + -ity, from Latin 'linearis' (of a line) and '-itas' (state or condition).
