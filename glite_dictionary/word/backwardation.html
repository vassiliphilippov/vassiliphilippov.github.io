backwardation

1. NOUN

/ ˌbækwɚˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌbækwəˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
A situation in the futures or forward markets where the price of a contract is lower than the expected spot price at the contract's maturity.
lower futures price vs. expected spot price

Examples

Periods of backwardation can indicate a shortage of the underlying commodity in the market.
Oil markets experienced backwardation last year as prompt supplies became more desirable than futures contracts.
Traders often monitor backwardation and contango to inform their investment strategies.

Synset Examples

In Treatise on Money (1930, chapter 29), economist John Maynard Keynes argued that in commodity markets, backwardation is not an abnormal market situation, but rather arises naturally as ‘normal backwardation’ from the fact that producers of commodities are more prone to hedge their price risk than consumers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: contango - a market condition where futures prices are higher than the expected spot price

Etymology

From 'backward' + '-ation', originally referring to a fee paid for deferring the delivery of stock in 19th-century London.
