keen-sighted

1. ADJ

/ ˈkin ˌsaɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈkiːn ˌsaɪtɪd / (BrE)
Having very sharp or acute vision; able to see extremely well or clearly.
having very sharp vision

Examples

The keen-sighted hawk spotted its prey from high in the sky.
He is so keen-sighted that he can read road signs from half a mile away.
Thanks to her keen-sighted nature, she quickly noticed the subtle details others missed.

Synset Examples

quick-sighted as a cat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sharp-sighted - having very acute vision
  • Antonym: short-sighted - unable to see distant objects clearly

Etymology

Formed from 'keen' (sharp) and 'sighted' (having sight), meaning one who sees sharply.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkin ˌsaɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈkiːn ˌsaɪtɪd / (BrE)
able to see very well or to a great distance; having sharp eyesight.
having sharp or excellent eyesight

Examples

The keen-sighted hawk circled high above the fields, searching for prey.
Even from a distance, her keen-sighted grandfather could spot tiny details that others missed.
Guides in the desert are often keen-sighted, able to notice wildlife far away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sharp-sighted - having very good eyesight
  • Antonym: short-sighted - unable to see things clearly that are far away

Etymology

From 'keen' (sharp) + 'sighted' (having sight), meaning sharp of sight.

WordNet ID: oewn-02165009-s
Synset POS: s
