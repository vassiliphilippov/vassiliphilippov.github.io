ant

1. NOUN

/ ænt / (AmE), / ænt / (BrE)
a small insect that lives in highly organized colonies, with workers, soldiers, and reproductive individuals such as queens and males, often known for their industrious behavior
small social insect living in colonies

Examples

An ant colony can contain thousands of individuals cooperating in complex ways.
Sarah watched a line of ants carrying food back to their nest.
The ant bit him on the foot as he was walking barefoot in the garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: termite - another social insect often confused with ants

Etymology

From Old English 'ǣmete', related to German 'Ameise' and Dutch 'mier'.
