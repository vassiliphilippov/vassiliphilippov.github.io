handcar

1. NOUN

/ ˈhændˌkɑr / (AmE), / ˈhændˌkɑː / (BrE)
a small, lightweight railroad vehicle that is operated by hand power or a small motor, traditionally used by railway workers for maintenance purposes.
small manually-powered railroad car

Examples

The workers used a handcar to inspect the tracks early in the morning.
Tourists enjoyed a short ride on the vintage handcar at the railway museum.
Maintaining balance on the moving handcar required teamwork and strong arms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: draisine - a light auxiliary rail vehicle propelled by hand or pedals
