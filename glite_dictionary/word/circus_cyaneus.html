Circus cyaneus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝːkəs saɪˈæniəs / (AmE), / ˈsɜːkəs saɪˈeɪniəs / (BrE)
A medium-sized bird of prey, also known as the hen harrier, found in North America and Europe, typically nesting in marshes and open fields.
species of bird of prey (hen harrier)

Examples

We spotted a Circus cyaneus gliding low over the marshes during our birdwatching trip.
The Circus cyaneus is easily recognized by its long wings and white rump patch.
Conservationists are making efforts to protect the habitat of Circus cyaneus in the open fields of Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hen harrier - a medium-sized harrier, the common name for Circus cyaneus

Etymology

From Latin: 'circus' meaning 'circle, hawk', and 'cyaneus' meaning 'bluish'.
