Lycopodium

1. NOUN

/ ˌlaɪ.kəˈpoʊ.di.əm / (AmE), / ˌlaɪ.kəˈpəʊ.di.əm / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen clubmoss plants in the family Lycopodiaceae, sometimes used for Christmas decorations.
a genus of evergreen clubmoss plants

Examples

Lycopodium is commonly used as a unique accent in holiday wreaths and floral arrangements.
The spores of Lycopodium are sometimes collected for use in science demonstrations.
Several species within the Lycopodium genus thrive in temperate forest environments.

Etymology

From Greek 'lykos' meaning 'wolf' and 'pous, podos' meaning 'foot', referring to the resemblance of the plant's rootstock to a wolf's paw.

2. NOUN

/ laɪˈkɑːpədiəm / (AmE), / laɪˈkɒpədiəm / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen plants in the family Lycopodiaceae, some species of which are used for Christmas decorations.
a genus of evergreen clubmosses

Examples

Lycopodium is often gathered for use in traditional Christmas wreaths and garlands.
Scientists studied several species within the Lycopodium genus to better understand fern evolution.
The spores of Lycopodium were once used in early photography and stage lighting.

Etymology

From Greek lykos (wolf) + podion (small foot), referring to the appearance of the plant's shoots.
