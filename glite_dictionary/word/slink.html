to slink

1. VERB

/ slɪŋk / (AmE), / slɪŋk / (BrE)
to move quietly and cautiously, often with the intention of avoiding notice, especially because of guilt or fear
move quietly and cautiously

Examples

The cat tried to slink past the sleeping dog without making a sound.
She would slink out of the office every day hoping no one would notice her.
He slinked away after realizing he had been caught in a lie.

Synset Examples

I saw a cougar slinking toward its prey

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creep - to move slowly and carefully in order to avoid being heard or noticed
  • Synonym: sneak - to move in a secretive, stealthy manner

Etymology

Old English slincan (to creep, crawl).
