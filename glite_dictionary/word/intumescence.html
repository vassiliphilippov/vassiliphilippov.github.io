intumescence

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.tuˈmɛs.əns / (AmE), / ˌɪn.tjuːˈmɛs.əns / (BrE)
the increase in volume of a substance when heated, often with the release of water, especially as seen in certain chemicals or materials
increase in volume of a substance when heated

Examples

The fire safety coating relied on intumescence to expand and protect the underlying surface.
Upon heating, the mineral underwent intumescence and released steam.
The process of intumescence is critical for certain fire-retardant materials.

Etymology

From Latin intumescere (to swell up), from in- (in) + tumescere (to begin to swell).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.tuˈmɛs.əns / (AmE), / ˌɪn.tjuˈmɛs.əns / (BrE)
A swelling or enlargement of a part, especially due to accumulation of blood or other fluids.
swelling caused by accumulation of fluids
medical

Examples

The doctor noted an intumescence on the patient's ankle after the injury.
Excessive fluid retention can lead to the intumescence of internal organs.
After the allergic reaction, a visible intumescence developed at the site of the insect bite.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swelling - an abnormal enlargement, especially from injury or inflammation

Etymology

From Latin intumescere, from in- (in) + tumescere (to swell).
