foliose

1. ADJ

/ ˈfoʊ.li.oʊs / (AmE), / ˈfəʊ.li.əʊs / (BrE)
(of plants or lichens) having many leaves or leaf-like parts.
having many leaves or leaf-like parts

Examples

The foliose lichens hung thickly from the old oak branches, creating a lush appearance.
Botanists identified the specimen as a foliose plant due to its many broad leaves.
In damp forests, various foliose algae cling to submerged rocks.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fruticose - having a shrubby or bush-like form (applied to lichens and some plants)
  • Antonym: crustose - having a crust-like form, closely attached to the surface (applied to lichens)

Etymology

From Latin 'foliosus', from 'folium' (leaf).
