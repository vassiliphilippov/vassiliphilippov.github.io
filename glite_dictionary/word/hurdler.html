hurdler

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɜrdlər / (AmE), / ˈhɜːdlə / (BrE)
an athlete who competes in hurdle races, running and jumping over barriers in track and field events
athlete who races over hurdles

Examples

The hurdler cleared every barrier with remarkable speed and precision.
She trained for months to become a competitive hurdler in the national championships.
Injuries are a common risk for a hurdler during intense track meets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sprinter - an athlete who runs short-distance races at high speed
  • Synonym: jumper - an athlete who competes in jumping events

Etymology

From 'hurdle' (a barrier in a race) + '-er' (person who does an activity).
