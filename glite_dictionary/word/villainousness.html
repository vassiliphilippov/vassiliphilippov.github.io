villainousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪlənəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈvɪlənəsnəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being wicked or villainous
quality of being villainous

Examples

The villainousness of the main character shocked the entire audience.
He tried to conceal his villainousness behind a charming smile, but his true nature soon showed.
Stories often exaggerate the villainousness of their antagonists to make them more memorable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: villainy - wicked or criminal behavior
  • Antonym: virtue - behavior showing high moral standards

Etymology

From 'villainous' + '-ness'.
