Lygodium

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈɡoʊdiəm / (AmE), / lɪˈɡəʊdiəm / (BrE)
A genus of chiefly tropical climbing ferns with leaves that unroll and grow continuously from the tip.
genus of climbing ferns

Examples

Many species in the genus Lygodium are known for their unique climbing habit.
The delicate fronds of Lygodium can be seen winding up tree trunks in tropical forests.
Researchers studied how Lygodium spreads and its potential as an invasive plant.

Etymology

From Greek *lygodes* ('flexible, like a willow'); used as a botanical genus name.

2. NOUN

/ laɪˈɡoʊdiəm / (AmE), / lɪˈɡəʊdiəm / (BrE)
A genus of chiefly tropical climbing ferns known for their long, twining fronds.
genus of tropical climbing ferns

Examples

Many species in the Lygodium genus are valued for their ornamental climbing fronds.
The creeping stems of Lygodium allow it to cover large areas quickly in tropical forests.
Botanists studied the unique reproduction methods of several Lygodium ferns.

Etymology

From Greek lygos (flexible twig, withe) + -odium (diminutive suffix).
