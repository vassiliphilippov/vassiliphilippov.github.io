transalpine

1. ADJ

/ trænˈsælpaɪn / (AmE), / trænˈsælpaɪn / (BrE)
relating to or situated on the far side of the Alps; especially north or west of the Alps as seen from Italy.
on the far side of the Alps (from Italy)

Examples

The Roman army marched into transalpine Gaul to secure the region.
He specialized in the art of transalpine painting from the Renaissance era.
Many transalpine goods were traded at the Italian markets.

Synset Examples

ancient transalpine Gaul was an area northwest of the Alps and included modern France and Belgium
Cracow was a transalpine university

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cisalpine - on the near side of the Alps (from Italy)

Etymology

From Latin 'transalpinus', from 'trans-' (across) + 'Alpī' (the Alps).

2. NOUN

/ trænzˈælpaɪn / (AmE), / tranzˈalpaɪn / (BrE)
a person living on or coming from the far side of the Alps from the perspective of Italy, especially in historical contexts
person from the other side of the Alps (from Italy)

Examples

Julius Caesar described the Gauls as transalpine peoples in his Commentaries.
Trade with the transalpine was crucial for the Roman Empire's economy.
The transalpine armies were known for their fierce resistance to Roman rule.

Etymology

From Latin transalpinus, from trans- (across) + Alpes (the Alps).
