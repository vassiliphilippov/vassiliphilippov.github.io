Abudefduf saxatilis

1. NOUN

/ ˌæbjudɛfˈdʌf sækˈsætɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˌæbjudɛfˈdʌf sækˈsætɪlɪs / (BrE)
a large blue-grey damselfish with black stripes, found in warm seas and known as the sergeant major
a black-striped marine damselfish

Examples

The Abudefduf saxatilis is often spotted swimming near coral reefs in tropical waters.
Marine biologists observed a school of Abudefduf saxatilis feeding close to the shore.
Many aquariums feature the Abudefduf saxatilis because of its striking black stripes and vibrant colors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sergeant major - common name for Abudefduf saxatilis, a black-striped damselfish

Etymology

From Arabic roots; genus name from Arabic abu (‘father’) + defdef (‘side’ or ‘flank’).
