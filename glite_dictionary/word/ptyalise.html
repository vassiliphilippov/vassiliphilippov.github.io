to ptyalise

1. VERB

/ ˈtaɪ.əˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈtɪə.laɪz / (BrE)
to expel or eject saliva from the mouth; to spit
expel saliva from the mouth
medical

Examples

During the dental procedure, the patient began to ptyalise excessively.
Certain medications may cause people to ptyalise more than usual.
Veterinarians observed the dog ptyalising after eating the toxic plant.

Synset Examples

The father of the victim spat at the alleged murderer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spit - to force saliva, phlegm, etc., from the mouth

Etymology

From Greek ptyalon (saliva), from ptyein (to spit).
