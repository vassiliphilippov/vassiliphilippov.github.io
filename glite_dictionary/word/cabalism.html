Cabalism

1. NOUN

/ kəˈbɑːlɪzəm / (AmE), / kəˈbælɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the mystical and esoteric doctrines or practices of the Kabbalah, a Jewish tradition of interpreting the Scriptures
doctrines of the Kabbalah

Examples

He devoted his studies to Cabalism and the mystical interpretation of Jewish texts.
Some Renaissance philosophers were deeply influenced by Cabalism in their search for spiritual truths.
The exhibition features rare manuscripts on Cabalism and other esoteric traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kabbalah - Jewish mystical tradition focused on esoteric interpretation of the Scriptures

Etymology

From Medieval Latin Cabala (Kabbalah) + -ism.
