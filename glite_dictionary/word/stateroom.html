stateroom

1. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪtˌruːm / (AmE), / ˈsteɪtˌruːm / (BrE)
a private cabin or compartment for passengers on a ship or train, typically with sleeping and washing facilities
private cabin on a ship or train

Examples

She was delighted to find her stateroom spacious and comfortable for the journey.
Every stateroom on the cruise ship has its own bathroom and window.
They returned to their stateroom after dinner to relax and enjoy the ocean view.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cabin - a small private room or compartment on a ship or train

Etymology

From 'state' (suggesting grandeur or dignity) + 'room'.
