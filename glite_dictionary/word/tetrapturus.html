Tetrapturus

1. NOUN

/ tɛˈtræptʃərəs / (AmE), / tɛˈtraptjʊərəs / (BrE)
A genus of billfish in the family Istiophoridae, comprising certain species of marlins and spearfish.
a genus of billfish

Examples

The Tetrapturus genus includes several fast-swimming species prized by sport fishermen.
Scientists identified the fish as a member of the Tetrapturus genus based on its long bill and dorsal fin.
Research on Tetrapturus has helped marine biologists understand migration patterns of billfish.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Istiophoridae - the family of billfishes, including marlins and spearfish

Etymology

From Greek tetra- (four) + pteron (fin or wing), referring to features of the fin structure in these fish.

2. NOUN

/ tɛˈtræptʃərəs / (AmE), / tɛˈtræptjʊərəs / (BrE)
A genus of large marine fishes in the family Istiophoridae, commonly known as spearfish.
a genus of spearfish

Examples

The Tetrapturus genus includes several species of spearfish found in tropical and subtropical waters.
Marine biologists recently identified a new species within the Tetrapturus genus.
The elongated bill is characteristic of fishes in the Tetrapturus genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Istiophoridae - the family of billfishes including marlins and spearfish

Etymology

From Greek 'tetra-' meaning four and 'pteron' meaning fin or wing, referring to fin structure.
