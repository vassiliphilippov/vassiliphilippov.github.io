shameful

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃeɪm.fəl / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪm.fəl / (BrE)
(of conduct or character) deserving or bringing disgrace or shame.
deserving disgrace or shame

Examples

The company’s shameful treatment of its workers was revealed in the investigation.
He made a shameful mistake that cost him his reputation.
It is shameful to cheat during an exam.

Synset Examples

Man … has written one of his blackest records as a destroyer on the oceanic islands
an ignominious retreat
inglorious defeat
an opprobrious monument to human greed
a shameful display of cowardice

Related Terms

  • Antonym: shameless - having no shame; not showing guilt or embarrassment
  • Synonym: disgraceful - causing shame or loss of respect
  • Synonym: ignominious - deserving or causing public disgrace or shame

Etymology

From Old English *sceamful* (“full of shame”).

2. ADJ

/ ˈʃeɪmfəl / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪmfəl / (BrE)
causing shame or disgrace; offensive to moral sensibilities or reputation
causing shame or disgrace

Examples

His shameful actions ruined the company's reputation.
The treatment of the prisoners was truly shameful and inhumane.
She apologized for her shameful behavior at the party.

Synset Examples

scandalous behavior
the wicked rascally shameful conduct of the bankrupt
the most shocking book of its time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scandalous - causing general public outrage by a perceived offense against morality
  • Antonym: honorable - deserving honor and respect; honest and fair

Etymology

From Old English *scamful*, from *scamu* (shame) + *-ful* (full of).
