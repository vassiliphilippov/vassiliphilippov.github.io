to resuspend

1. VERB

/ ˌriː.səˈspɛnd / (AmE), / ˌriː.səˈspɛnd / (BrE)
to put something, especially particles or cells, back into a suspended state within a liquid after it has settled or precipitated
put back into suspension (liquid)

Examples

Gently resuspend the cells in fresh buffer before proceeding with the experiment.
After centrifugation, the technician had to resuspend the pellet in a saline solution.
If the particles settle at the bottom, simply vortex the tube to resuspend them.

Synset Examples

resuspend particles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to suspend - to cause particles or cells to be held within a liquid so that they do not settle

Etymology

From the prefix 're-' (again) and 'suspend', from Latin 'suspendere' (to hang up).
