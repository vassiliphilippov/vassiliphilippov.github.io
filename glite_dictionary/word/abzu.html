Abzu

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑːbzuː / (AmE), / ˈabzuː / (BrE)
in Mesopotamian mythology, the primeval sea; the father of the gods and consort of Tiamat
Mesopotamian primeval sea deity

Examples

In ancient mythology, Abzu was revered as the source of all freshwater.
The epic describes how Abzu and Tiamat gave birth to the first gods.
Scholars often debate the symbolic meaning of Abzu in Sumerian religion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tiamat - primordial goddess of the salt sea in Mesopotamian mythology

Etymology

From Akkadian *apsû*, related to Sumerian *abzu* ('deep water, abyss').
