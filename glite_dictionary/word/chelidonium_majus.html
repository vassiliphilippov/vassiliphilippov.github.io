Chelidonium majus

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɛlɪˈdoʊniəm ˈmeɪdʒəs / (AmE), / ˌkɛlɪˈdəʊniəm ˈmeɪdʒəs / (BrE)
A perennial herb with a branched woody base and bright yellow flowers, native to Europe and western Asia, also known as greater celandine.
perennial herb with yellow flowers (greater celandine)

Examples

Chelidonium majus has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its supposed healing properties.
The bright yellow flowers of Chelidonium majus can be spotted along old stone walls in the countryside.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate Chelidonium majus for its ornamental value and historical significance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greater celandine - common name for Chelidonium majus

Etymology

From Latin *chelidonium* (swallow, the bird) and *majus* (greater), referring to its blooming time and distinction from lesser celandine.
