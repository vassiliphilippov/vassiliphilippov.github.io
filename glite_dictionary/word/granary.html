granary

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡræ.nə.ri / (AmE), / ˈɡran.əri / (BrE)
a building or place where grain is stored, especially after it has been threshed
storehouse for grain

Examples

The farmer filled the granary with the season’s wheat harvest.
Ancient civilizations often built large granaries to ensure a stable food supply.
After the grain was threshed, it was carried to the granary for storage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silo - a tall tower or pit on a farm used to store grain or silage

Etymology

From Latin 'granarium', from 'granum' meaning 'grain'.
