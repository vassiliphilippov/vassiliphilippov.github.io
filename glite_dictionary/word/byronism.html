Byronism

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪrənˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈbaɪrənɪzəm / (BrE)
the qualities or style characteristic of the poet Byron, especially romanticism, deep melancholy, and dramatic energy
qualities or style of Byron's poetry

Examples

The hero of the novel is so steeped in Byronism that his actions seem shaped by poetry rather than reality.
Her fascination with Byronism is evident in her collection of melancholic and passionate verse.
Critics have noted a persistent Byronism in his writing, especially in its romantic idealism.

Synset Examples

The Byronism of these passages is of course odd, except that it is a Byronism of desire and not of deed.

Etymology

From the name of the English poet Lord Byron (1788–1824) + -ism (indicating a distinctive practice or system).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪrənˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈbaɪərənɪzm / (BrE)
Admiration for or imitation of the style, behavior, or spirit of the poet Lord Byron.
admiration of or emulation of Lord Byron

Examples

His poetry was heavily influenced by Byronism, both in style and in outlook.
You can see a touch of Byronism in the brooding, rebellious hero of the novel.
Throughout the nineteenth century, Byronism fascinated European writers and artists.

Synset Examples

Lermontov's Byronism apparently wasn't merely a kinship of contemporarality and random similarity in soul with Byron.

Etymology

From Byron (surname of George Gordon Byron, 6th Baron Byron, English Romantic poet) + -ism.

3. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪərənˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈbaɪərənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The imitation of or adherence to the ideals, attitudes, or lifestyle of Lord Byron, especially as characterized by romanticism, rebelliousness, and individualism.
imitation of Lord Byron's ideals/lifestyle

Examples

The poet's fascination with Byronism is evident in his rebellious verses and melancholic tone.
In the nineteenth century, Byronism swept through European intellectual circles, influencing artists and thinkers alike.
Her novel explores themes of Byronism, featuring a brooding hero who defies societal norms.

Synset Examples

Modern Greece was associated with a political Byronism of a certain kind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: romanticism - artistic and literary movement emphasizing emotion and individualism

Etymology

Formed from the name Byron (George Gordon, Lord Byron) + -ism, denoting a characteristic or movement.
