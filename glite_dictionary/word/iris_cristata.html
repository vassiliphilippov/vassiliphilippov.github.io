Iris cristata

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪ.rɪs krɪˈsteɪ.tə / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.rɪs krɪˈstɑː.tə / (BrE)
A low-growing iris species native to the northeastern United States, known for its summer flowers and distinctive crested petals.
species of low-growing iris with crested petals

Examples

The Iris cristata adds a splash of purple to shaded woodland gardens in spring.
Gardeners admire the delicate, crested blooms of Iris cristata for ground cover.
We planted several Iris cristata along the path to enjoy their unique flowers each year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iris - a genus of plants with showy flowers

Etymology

From modern Latin, where 'Iris' is the genus and 'cristata' means 'crested' in reference to the flower's shape.
