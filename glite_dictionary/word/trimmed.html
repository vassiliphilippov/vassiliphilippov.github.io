trimmed

1. ADJ

/ trɪmd / (AmE), / trɪmd / (BrE)
(of hair, grass, etc.) made neat and tidy by cutting away irregular or unwanted parts.
made neat and tidy by trimming

Examples

He always keeps his beard trimmed for a professional look.
The hedges were neatly trimmed before the party.
Her trimmed nails looked clean and polished.

Synset Examples

his neatly trimmed hair

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unkempt - not neat or tidy
  • Antonym: shaggy - untidy or scruffy in appearance, especially hair

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'trim', originally from Old English 'trymman' (to strengthen, arrange).
