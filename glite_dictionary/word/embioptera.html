Embioptera

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛm.biˈɑp.tə.rə / (AmE), / ˌɛm.bɪˈɒp.tər.ə / (BrE)
an order of small, elongated insects known as web spinners, noted for their ability to spin silken tunnels or galleries
order of insects called web spinners

Examples

Embioptera are unique for their ability to spin silk with glands located in their front legs.
Scientists discovered several new species of Embioptera in the Amazon rainforest.
Unlike most insects, members of Embioptera create elaborate silk tunnels as shelters.

Etymology

From Greek *embios* (living) + *pteron* (wing).
