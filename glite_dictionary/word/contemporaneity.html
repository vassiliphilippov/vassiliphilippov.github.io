contemporaneity

1. NOUN

/ kənˌtɛm.pəˌreɪˈni.ə.ti / (AmE), / kənˌtɛm.pəˈreɪ.ni.ə.ti / (BrE)
the quality of belonging to the same period or being current; the state of existing or happening at the same time as something else
state of being current or of the same time

Examples

The contemporaneity of the exhibition's artworks makes them relevant for today's audience.
Her writing is valued for its contemporaneity as well as its insight.
The film captures the contemporaneity of urban life with remarkable accuracy.

Synset Examples

a shopping mall would instill a spirit of modernity into this village

Related Terms

  • Synonym: modernity - the quality or condition of being modern

Etymology

From Latin 'contemporaneus' (contemporary) + '-ity'

2. NOUN

/ kənˌtɛm.pəˈreɪ.ni.ɾi / (AmE), / kənˌtɛm.pəˈreɪ.ni.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality or state of existing or occurring in the same period of time as something else.
the state of being contemporary in time

Examples

The contemporaneity of the two artworks has fueled debate among historians.
Many social movements are distinguished by their contemporaneity with major political events.
He was struck by the contemporaneity of the discoveries in different scientific fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contemporaneousness - the quality of being contemporaneous
  • Antonym: anachronism - something belonging to a different time period

Etymology

From Latin 'contemporaneus' (contemporary) + '-ity'.
