Old Italian

1. NOUN

/ oʊld ɪˈtæliən / (AmE), / əʊld ɪˈtæliən / (BrE)
the stage of the Italian language spoken and written up to the middle of the 16th century
historical stage of Italian (to mid-16th century)

Examples

Many medieval texts were originally written in Old Italian.
Dante's 'Divine Comedy' is a celebrated example of literature in Old Italian.
Linguists study Old Italian to trace the evolution of modern Italian.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Italian - the Romance language currently spoken in Italy and other regions
  • Degree Lower: Latin - the classical language from which Italian developed

Etymology

Old + Italian, denoting the earlier form of the Italian language.
