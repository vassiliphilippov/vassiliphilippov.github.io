squabble

1. VERB

/ ˈskwɑːbəl / (AmE), / ˈskwɒbəl / (BrE)
to argue noisily over trivial or petty matters
argue noisily over petty matters

Examples

The children began to squabble over who would sit by the window.
Siblings often squabble about the smallest things during road trips.
We shouldn't squabble about chores when we could just work together.

Synset Examples

Let's not quibble over pennies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quarrel - to argue angrily or loudly, especially for a long time or about something unimportant
  • Synonym: fight - to argue or struggle in conflict

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly imitative or from a dialectal Scandinavian word.

Source: WordNet

2. NOUN

/ ˈskwɑːbəl / (AmE), / ˈskwɒbəl / (BrE)
a noisy or petty argument, especially about trivial matters
a minor quarrel or argument

Examples

The siblings had a squabble over who would sit in the front seat.
Their squabble about the remote control lasted only a few minutes.
Even the smallest issue can spark a squabble between coworkers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quarrel - an angry disagreement or argument
  • Degree Higher: fight - a violent struggle or argument
  • Degree Lower: disagreement - a lack of consensus or approval

Etymology

Probably from a dialectal word meaning 'to quarrel', of uncertain origin, first recorded in the late 17th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07199395-n
Synset POS: n
