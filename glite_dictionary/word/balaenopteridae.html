Balaenopteridae

1. NOUN

/ bəˌliːnoʊpˈtɛrɪdiː / (AmE), / bəˌliːnɒpˈtɛrɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large baleen whales that includes rorquals and blue whales, characterized by pleated throat grooves and streamlined bodies.
family of large baleen whales (rorquals, blue whales)

Examples

The Balaenopteridae are distinguished by their pleated throat grooves, which allow expansion during feeding.
Scientists have classified the blue whale, the largest animal ever known, within the family Balaenopteridae.
Many species in the Balaenopteridae family are migratory, traveling thousands of miles between feeding and breeding grounds.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Latin balaena (whale) and Greek pteron (fin, wing).

2. NOUN

/ bəˌlinɑːpˈtɛrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / bəˌliːnɒpˈtɛrɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of large whales, including rorquals and blue whales, characterized by elongated bodies and pleated throat grooves
family of large whales (rorquals, blue whales)

Examples

The Balaenopteridae include some of the largest animals on the planet, such as the blue whale.
Research on Balaenopteridae has provided insight into the evolution of baleen whales.
Unlike other cetacean families, Balaenopteridae are characterized by their throat pleats and feeding techniques.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whale - large marine mammal belonging to the order Cetacea
  • Degree Higher: blue whale - the largest animal on Earth, a species within Balaenopteridae

Etymology

From New Latin Balaenopteridae, from genus Balaenoptera ('fin whale') + -idae (standard zoological family suffix).
