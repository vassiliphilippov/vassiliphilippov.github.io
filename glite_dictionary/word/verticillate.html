verticillate

1. ADJ

/ vərˈtɪsəˌleɪt / (AmE), / vəˈtɪsɪleɪt / (BrE)
(of leaves or other organs) arranged in one or more whorls around an axis, especially a stem.
arranged in whorls (botany)

Examples

The leaves of the plant are verticillate, forming a circle at each node along the stem.
Some aquatic plants display verticillate branching patterns that distinguish them from others.
In botany exams, students often have to identify verticillate leaf arrangements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whorled - arranged in a whorl, especially leaves or petals

Etymology

From Latin verticillatus, from verticillus 'whorl'.
