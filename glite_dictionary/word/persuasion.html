persuasion

1. NOUN

/ pərˈsweɪʒən / (AmE), / pəˈsweɪʒn / (BrE)
the act of convincing someone to do or believe something through reasoning or argument
the act of convincing someone

Examples

Her skill in persuasion helped her win the debate against more experienced opponents.
Advertising often uses subtle persuasion to influence consumer choices.
It took some persuasion, but he finally agreed to attend the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convincing - the action of making someone believe something
  • Antonym: dissuasion - the act of persuading someone not to do something

Etymology

From Latin 'persuasio', from 'persuadere' meaning 'to persuade'.

2. NOUN

/ pərˈsweɪʒən / (AmE), / pəˈsweɪʒən / (BrE)
A personal belief or opinion, especially one that is held without proof or certainty.
a personal belief or opinion

Examples

Despite belonging to a different persuasion, they remained good friends.
He is a man of liberal persuasion and strong convictions.
Among people of every persuasion, there was agreement on the need for change.

Synset Examples

my opinion differs from yours
I am not of your persuasion
what are your thoughts on Haiti?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opinion - a belief or judgment not based on absolute certainty
  • Synonym: conviction - a firmly held belief or opinion

Etymology

From Latin persuasio (conviction, belief), from persuadere (to persuade).
