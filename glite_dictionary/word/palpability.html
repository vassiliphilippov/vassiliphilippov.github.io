palpability

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæl.pəˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌpal.pəˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality of being easily felt or touched; the property of being perceivable by touch.
quality of being felt by touch

Examples

The palpability of the swelling made the diagnosis easier for the doctor.
Despite the darkness, the palpability of the wall was reassuring to him as he felt his way forward.
The artist aimed to give her sculptures a sense of palpability unmatched by any other medium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tangibility - the quality of being perceptible by touch
  • Antonym: intangibility - the quality of not being able to be touched or felt

Etymology

From Latin 'palpabilis' (that may be touched or felt) + '-ity' (a suffix forming nouns).
