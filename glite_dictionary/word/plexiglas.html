Plexiglas

1. NOUN

/ ˈplɛksɪˌɡlæs / (AmE), / ˈplɛksɪˌɡlɑːs / (BrE)
a lightweight, transparent, weather-resistant thermoplastic material often used as a substitute for glass
transparent thermoplastic material

Examples

Many aquariums use Plexiglas to construct large, clear tanks for viewing marine life.
The protective barrier was made from Plexiglas to ensure visibility and safety.
He replaced the broken window with a sheet of Plexiglas because it was lighter and more durable than glass.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acrylic - a synthetic resin used in paints and plastics
  • Synonym: poly(methyl methacrylate) - chemical name for the plastic known commercially as Plexiglas

Etymology

Trade name, from 'plexi-' (short for polymer) and 'glass'; originally a trademark for acrylic sheets.
