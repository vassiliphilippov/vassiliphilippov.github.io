rosebud

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊzˌbʌd / (AmE), / ˈrəʊzˌbʌd / (BrE)
the unopened flower of a rose; the bud stage before the rose opens
the unopened flower of a rose

Examples

She placed a single rosebud in the small glass vase on her desk.
The rosebud slowly opened over the next few days, revealing delicate pink petals.
Each rosebud in the garden promised to bloom into a beautiful flower.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bud - a young, undeveloped or unopened flower or leaf

2. NOUN

/ ˈroʊz.bʌd / (AmE), / ˈrəʊzbʌd / (BrE)
a pretty young girl, especially as a poetic or literary metaphor
pretty young girl (literary/poetic)

Examples

In the poem, the innocent rosebud symbolized youthful beauty and fragile innocence.
She was the rosebud of the village, adored for her charm and grace.
Victorian novels often describe the heroine as a delicate rosebud on the brink of womanhood.

Etymology

From 'rose' (the flower) + 'bud', referring to a young, unopened flower; used metaphorically from the 18th century.
