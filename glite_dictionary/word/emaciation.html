emaciation

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌmeɪsiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌmeɪsiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the state of being abnormally thin or weak, usually due to lack of food or disease
extreme thinness due to starvation or illness

Examples

The doctor was shocked by the emaciation of the famine victims.
Chronic illness led to severe emaciation in the patient.
Signs of emaciation were evident in the neglected animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wasting - gradual loss of weight and muscle
  • Synonym: malnutrition - poor health from lack of proper nutrients
  • Antonym: obesity - the state of having too much body fat

Etymology

From Latin emaciatio, from emaciare (to make thin).
