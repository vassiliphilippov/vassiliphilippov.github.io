rigging

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪɡɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɪɡɪŋ / (BrE)
the system of ropes, cables, and chains used to support a ship's masts and to control or set the sails
system of ropes and masts on a ship

Examples

The storm damaged the rigging and left the ship adrift at sea.
Sailors inspected the rigging before setting off on their voyage.
Restoring the old yacht included replacing much of its original rigging.

Etymology

From Middle English 'riggen' (to rig, fit out), of Scandinavian origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɪɡɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɪɡɪŋ / (BrE)
the system of ropes, cables, and chains used to support a ship's masts and to control or set the sails
ropes and wires supporting ship's masts and sails

Examples

The sailors repaired the rigging before setting out to sea.
Storms can easily damage the rigging on even the strongest ships.
She climbed the rigging to untangle a rope near the top of the mast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tackle - equipment consisting of ropes and pulleys used for lifting and pulling

Etymology

From Middle English 'riggen' (to fit out a ship), of Scandinavian origin.
