wedlock

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛd.lɑk / (AmE), / ˈwɛd.lɒk / (BrE)
the state of being married; the condition of marriage
the state of being married

Examples

The couple celebrated twenty-five years of wedlock with a big party.
They had three children out of wedlock, before deciding to marry.
Some cultures place great value on remaining in wedlock for life.

Synset Examples

a long and happy marriage
God bless this union

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marriage - the formal union of two people as partners in a personal relationship

Etymology

From Old English 'wedlāc' (state of being married), from 'wed' (pledge, agreement) + 'lāc' (action, proceeding).
