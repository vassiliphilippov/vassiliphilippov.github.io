Prunus domestica

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruː.nəs doʊˈmɛs.tɪ.kə / (AmE), / ˈpruː.nəs dəˈmɛs.tɪ.kə / (BrE)
a species of plum tree, widely grown in temperate regions for its edible fruit
a species of plum tree

Examples

The orchard was filled with rows of Prunus domestica heavy with ripe, purple fruit.
European Prunus domestica varieties are prized for their sweet, juicy plums.
Farmers often graft new Prunus domestica cultivars to improve yield and disease resistance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plum - the edible fruit produced by Prunus species

Etymology

From Latin: 'prunus' (plum tree) + 'domestica' (domesticated).
