Allium sativum

1. NOUN

/ ˈæliəm səˈtiːvəm / (AmE), / ˈalɪəm səˈtiːvəm / (BrE)
the scientific name for garlic, a bulbous plant with strong-flavored cloves used in cooking and medicine
scientific name for garlic plant

Examples

Allium sativum is prized for its medicinal properties as well as its flavor in cuisines worldwide.
Many gardeners choose to grow Allium sativum for its easy cultivation and health benefits.
The study focused on extracts from Allium sativum to assess their antibacterial effects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garlic - a plant with a strong taste and smell, used in cooking

Etymology

From Latin: 'allium' meaning garlic, and 'sativum' meaning cultivated.
