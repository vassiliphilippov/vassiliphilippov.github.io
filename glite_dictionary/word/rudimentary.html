rudimentary

1. ADJ

/ ˌruː.dəˈmɛn.tə.ri / (AmE), / ˌruː.dɪˈmen.tər.i / (BrE)
involving only basic or elementary facts or principles; simple and undeveloped
basic or elementary; undeveloped

Examples

Children learn rudimentary math concepts before moving on to more complex problems.
Her knowledge of French is rudimentary, limited to a few basic phrases.
The museum displayed rudimentary tools made by early humans.

Synset Examples

the fundamental laws of the universe
a fundamental incompatibility between them
these rudimentary truths
underlying principles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basic - forming an essential foundation or starting point; fundamental
  • Synonym: elementary - simple or basic in form, dealing with the simplest facts or principles
  • Antonym: advanced - far ahead in progress, complexity, or knowledge

Etymology

From Latin rudimentarius, from rudimentum (first principle, beginning).

2. ADJ

/ ˌruːdəˈmɛnˌtɛri / (AmE), / ˌruːdɪˈmɛntəri / (BrE)
(of a body part or organ) not fully developed or only present in a basic form.
not fully developed; basic form

Examples

The bird's rudimentary wings made it unable to fly.
Some snakes have rudimentary legs hidden within their bodies.
The fossil showed a creature with only rudimentary limbs.

Synset Examples

rudimentary wings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: undeveloped - not fully grown or matured
  • Antonym: developed - having reached a full state of growth or maturity

Etymology

From Latin rudimentarius, from rudimentum (early training, beginning).

3. ADJ

/ ˌruːdəˈmɛn.tə.ri / (AmE), / ˌruː.dɪˈmen.tər.i / (BrE)
existing at a very basic or early stage of development; not fully developed or formed.
basic; undeveloped

Examples

The species still possesses a rudimentary tail from its evolutionary ancestors.
We had only a rudimentary understanding of the problem at the time.
His drawing skills were rudimentary, but he enjoyed the creative process.

Synset Examples

rudimentary plans

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basic - forming an essential foundation; fundamental
  • Antonym: developed - fully grown or advanced; matured

Etymology

From Latin rudimentarius (early, undeveloped), from rudimentum (first attempt, beginning).
