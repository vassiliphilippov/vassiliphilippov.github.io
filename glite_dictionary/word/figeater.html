figeater

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪɡˌiːtər / (AmE), / ˈfɪɡˌiːtə / (BrE)
a large, greenish beetle (Cotinis mutabilis) found in the southern United States, often seen feeding on fruit such as figs and grapes
large green beetle of southern US

Examples

A figeater beetle landed on the ripe figs in my backyard.
Figeaters are easily recognized by their metallic green shells.
Many gardeners in the Southwest try to keep figeaters away from their fruit trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: June beetle - a beetle of the genus Phyllophaga, known for its emergence in June
