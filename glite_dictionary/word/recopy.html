to recopy

1. VERB

/ riˈkɑpi / (AmE), / riˈkɒpi / (BrE)
to make a new copy of something; to copy again, especially after a mistake
copy again after a mistake

Examples

If your essay is too messy, you may need to recopy it before handing it in.
She forgot to save her work and had to recopy the entire report.
The teacher asked him to recopy the answers neatly on a new sheet.

Synset Examples

The child had to recopy the homework

Related Terms

  • Synonym: copy - to produce something that is the same as the original

Etymology

From the prefix re- (again) + copy.
