Anton Chekhov

1. PROPN

/ ˈæn.tɑn ˈtʃɛ.kɑf / (AmE), / ˈæn.tɒn ˈtʃɛ.kɒf / (BrE)
(1860–1904) A Russian dramatist and short story writer known for plays exploring the complexity of human relationships and communication.
Russian playwright and short story writer

Examples

Many actors aspire to perform in a Chekhov play at least once in their careers.
The subtlety of Anton Chekhov's writing has influenced generations of playwrights and authors.
We discussed the themes of isolation in Anton Chekhov's short stories during literature class.

Etymology

Transliteration of Russian Антон Чехов (Anton Chekhov), his full name being Anton Pavlovich Chekhov.
