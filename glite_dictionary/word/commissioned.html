commissioned

1. ADJ

/ kəˈmɪʃ.ənd / (AmE), / kəˈmɪʃ.ənd / (BrE)
(of a military officer) holding rank by virtue of a formal commission, usually starting at second lieutenant or ensign or above.
holding formal military rank by commission

Examples

He served as a commissioned officer in the United States Army for over twenty years.
Only commissioned officers have the authority to lead a battalion into battle.
After graduating from the academy, she became a commissioned second lieutenant.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: noncommissioned - (of military personnel) not holding a formal commission; below officer rank

Etymology

From the verb 'commission', ultimately from Latin 'committere' (to entrust), via Old French and Middle English.

2. ADJ

/ kəˈmɪʃ.ənd / (AmE), / kəˈmɪʃ.ənd / (BrE)
officially appointed or given authority to perform a particular role, duty, or function
having official authority or approval

Examples

He worked as a commissioned officer in the navy for over twenty years.
As a commissioned broker, she was legally allowed to complete major transactions.
Only commissioned representatives can approve these types of contracts.

Synset Examples

an accredited college
commissioned broker
licensed pharmacist
authorized representative

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appointed - officially assigned to a role or position
  • Synonym: authorized - having official permission or approval
  • Antonym: uncommissioned - not given official approval or authority

Etymology

Past participle of 'commission', from Latin 'commissio' (delegation, entrusting).
