self-referent

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛlfˈrɛfərənt / (AmE), / ˌsɛlfˈrɛfərənt / (BrE)
(of a statement or expression) referring back to itself; self-referential.
referring to itself

Examples

The statement 'this sentence is false' is a classic example of a self-referent paradox.
Some jokes are self-referent, commenting on their own structure or delivery.
A self-referent remark can often reveal underlying assumptions about the conversation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-referential - making reference to itself

Etymology

Based on "self-" (referring to itself) + "referent" (something that refers to another).
