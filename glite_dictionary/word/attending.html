attending

1. NOUN

/ əˈtɛn.dɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈtɛn.dɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of being present at a meeting, event, or activity
the act of being present at an event

Examples

Her attending of the conference helped her make important professional connections.
Regular attending of staff meetings is mandatory in this company.
The award was given for perfect attending throughout the school year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attendance - the action or state of being present at a place or event

2. NOUN

/ əˈtɛn.dɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈtɛn.dɪŋ / (BrE)
The act or process of concentrating on specific features of the environment while ignoring others.
focusing attention on something

Examples

Effective attending is essential for successful learning in noisy classrooms.
The therapist practiced active attending to better understand her client's concerns.
Mindful attending helps reduce stress and improve well-being.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attention - the mental focus on specific stimuli
  • Antonym: distraction - a thing that prevents someone from giving full attention

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'attend', from Latin 'attendere' (give heed to).

3. ADJ

/ əˈtɛndɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈtɛndɪŋ / (BrE)
actively giving practical help or services, especially as part of one's professional duties
giving practical help or service

Examples

The attending nurse checked on her patient every hour throughout the night.
At the conference, the attending physician was responsible for assessing all the new arrivals.
Only attending staff members are permitted in the operating theatre during surgery.
