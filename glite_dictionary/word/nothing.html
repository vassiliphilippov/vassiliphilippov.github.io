nothing

1. ADV

/ ˈnʌθɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈnʌθɪŋ / (BrE)
not at all; in no way or to no degree.
not at all; in no way

Examples

Despite all the hype, the movie was nothing like I expected.
This recipe tastes nothing like the original.
She is nothing if not determined to succeed.

Synset Examples

he looks nothing like his father

Related Terms

  • Synonym: not at all - to no extent or degree; not in any way
  • Antonym: something - to some degree or extent; the opposite of nothing in this context
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r

2. PRON

/ ˈnʌθɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈnʌθɪŋ / (BrE)
not anything; no single thing; the absence or lack of anything of importance or value.
absence of anything; not anything

Examples

After searching the room, I found nothing of interest.
He said nothing during the entire meeting, just listened in silence.
There was nothing left to do but go home.

Synset Examples

it looked like nothing I had ever seen before
reduced to nil all the work we had done
we racked up a pathetic goose egg
it was all for naught
I didn't hear zilch about it

Related Terms

  • Antonym: something - an unspecified or unknown thing
  • Antonym: everything - all things; the whole quantity or extent
  • Antonym: anything - any thing whatever

Etymology

From Old English nāthing (no thing).

Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ ˈnʌθɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈnʌθɪŋ / (BrE)
not anything; no single thing; a quantity of no importance or zero value.
not anything; zero, none

Examples

After all that effort, we ended up with nothing to show for it.
There is nothing in the box—it's completely empty.
She said nothing during the entire meeting.

Synset Examples

it looked like nothing I had ever seen before
reduced to nil all the work we had done
we racked up a pathetic goose egg
it was all for naught
I didn't hear zilch about it

Related Terms

  • Antonym: something - an unspecific thing; a certain amount or thing
  • Antonym: anything - any object or thing, no matter which
  • Synonym: nil - zero, nothing; especially in scores
  • Synonym: naught - nothing; zero

Etymology

From Old English nān þing (‘not a thing’), later contracted.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13762308-n
Synset POS: n
