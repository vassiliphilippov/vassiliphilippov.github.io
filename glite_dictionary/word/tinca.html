Tinca

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪŋkə / (AmE), / ˈtɪŋkə / (BrE)
A genus of freshwater fish in the family Cyprinidae, including the tench.
genus of freshwater fish (tench)

Examples

The genus Tinca is best known for its single species, the tench.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Tinca in European lakes.
Fish enthusiasts often keep Tinca in ornamental ponds due to their interesting behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tench - a freshwater fish, Tinca tinca, of the carp family

Etymology

From Latin *tinca* (tench).
