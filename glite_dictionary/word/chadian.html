Chadian

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃædiən / (AmE), / ˈtʃædiən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Chad, a country in north-central Africa
person from Chad

Examples

There are many Chadians living in the capital city, N'Djamena.
As a Chadian, she is proud of her cultural heritage.
The film was directed by a renowned Chadian filmmaker.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chadian - relating to Chad, its people, or language (adjective use)

Etymology

From Chad (the country) + English suffix -ian.

2. ADJ

/ ˈtʃædiən / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːdiən / (BrE)
of, relating to, or characteristic of the Republic of Chad, its people, or its language.
of or relating to Chad or its people

Examples

The Chadian government announced new infrastructure projects this year.
Many Chadian dishes include millet and beans as staple ingredients.
The author collected traditional Chadian stories during her travels in northern Africa.

Synset Examples

the Chadian desert
Chadian folktales
Chadian soldiers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chad - landlocked country in north-central Africa

Etymology

From Chad + -ian, forming demonyms and adjectives for nationality.
