laurels

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔrəlz / (AmE), / ˈlɔːrəlz / (BrE)
honors or recognition for achievement, especially in a particular field
honors or accolades for achievement

Examples

She earned her laurels as a scientist after years of dedicated research.
The director did not rest on his laurels after winning the award; he continued to work hard.
Athletes strive for laurels at every major competition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honors - public recognition or awards for achievement

Etymology

From the tradition of crowning victors with laurel wreaths in ancient Greece and Rome.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɔrəlz / (AmE), / ˈlɒːrəlz / (BrE)
awards, honors, or symbols of achievement given as recognition for success or distinction
symbols of achievement or honor

Examples

She won laurels for her groundbreaking research in physics.
He rested on his laurels after winning several prestigious awards.
Athletes often compete fiercely to earn laurels at international competitions.

Synset Examples

an award for bravery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honor - public recognition or distinction for achievement
  • Synonym: award - a prize or other mark of recognition given in honor of achievement
  • Antonym: disgrace - loss of respect, honor, or esteem

Etymology

From Latin 'laurea' (laurel wreath), awarded to victors in ancient Greece and Rome.
