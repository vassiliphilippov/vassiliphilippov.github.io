formyl

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔr.mɪl / (AmE), / ˈfɔː.mɪl / (BrE)
(Chemistry) The univalent acyl group or radical –CHO, found in aldehydes and related compounds.
chemical group –CHO in aldehydes
medical

Examples

The formyl group is essential in the structure of many aldehydes.
During synthesis, we introduced a formyl substituent to the benzene ring.
Removal of the formyl radical alters the chemical properties of the molecule.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aldehyde - An organic compound containing the –CHO group

Etymology

From French formyle, from formique (formic) and -yle (radical, group).
