ciprofloxacin

1. NOUN

/ sɪpɹoʊˈflɑksəˌsɪn / (AmE), / sɪˌprɒfləˈksæsɪn / (BrE)
an antibiotic medicine used to treat various serious bacterial infections, such as those affecting the skin, respiratory tract, urinary tract, bones, or joints
antibiotic for serious bacterial infections
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed ciprofloxacin to treat her urinary tract infection.
Patients allergic to penicillin may be given ciprofloxacin instead.
Long-term use of ciprofloxacin can sometimes lead to side effects such as tendon pain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antibiotic - a medicine that inhibits the growth of or destroys microorganisms

Etymology

From chemical name, based on 'cyclo-' (circle), 'piperazin' (piperazine ring), and 'fluoroquinolone' (its antibiotic class).
