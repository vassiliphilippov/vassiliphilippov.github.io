zoom

1. NOUN

/ zuːm / (AmE), / zuːm / (BrE)
a rapid and significant increase, especially in price, level, or activity
a rapid increase or rise

Examples

There was a sudden zoom in stock market prices after the announcement.
We have witnessed a zoom in demand for electric cars this year.
The past decade saw a remarkable zoom in the city’s population.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surge - a sudden powerful forward or upward movement, especially by a crowd or by a natural force

2. VERB

/ zuːm / (AmE), / zuːm / (BrE)
to rise rapidly and suddenly, especially in amount, value, or level
rise rapidly and suddenly

Examples

House prices are expected to zoom in the next quarter as demand increases.
The company's stock zoomed after the announcement of the merger.
Interest rates could zoom if inflation continues to rise.

Synset Examples

the dollar soared against the yen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to soar - to rise quickly to a high level
  • Antonym: plummet - to fall or drop suddenly and quickly in amount or value

3. NOUN

/ zuːm / (AmE), / zuːm / (BrE)
A rapid rise or upward movement, especially into the air.
a rapid upward movement

Examples

The pilot executed a steep zoom shortly after takeoff.
With a sudden zoom, the rocket shot into the sky.
During the airshow, the jet performed an impressive zoom maneuver.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: climb - the act of moving upward, especially in aviation
  • Antonym: dive - a steep downward movement in flight

Etymology

Imitative; from the sound produced by something moving swiftly (early 20th century).

4. VERB

/ zuːm / (AmE), / zuːm / (BrE)
to move quickly with a low humming or buzzing sound
move quickly with a humming sound

Examples

The motorcycle zoomed past us on the highway, leaving only a trail of sound behind.
Children zoom down the hill on their sleds every winter.
A dragonfly suddenly zoomed over the pond as we walked by.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whiz - to move quickly with a whistling or buzzing sound
  • Synonym: buzz - to make a low continuous humming sound while moving
  • Antonym: creep - to move slowly and quietly
Source: WordNet

5. VERB

/ zuːm / (AmE), / zuːm / (BrE)
to move or travel very quickly, often with a loud sound
move very quickly

Examples

The red car zoomed past us on the highway.
Children zoom down the slide at the playground every afternoon.
The ambulance zoomed through the busy streets to reach the hospital.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speed - to move quickly
  • Synonym: rush - to move with urgent haste
  • Antonym: crawl - to move very slowly

Etymology

Imitative of the sound of something moving swiftly; first attested in the early 20th century.
