likeable

1. ADJ

/ ˈlaɪkəbəl / (AmE), / ˈlaɪkəbl / (BrE)
pleasant and easy to like; having qualities that attract affection or approval.
pleasant and easy to like

Examples

Sarah is a likeable character who quickly wins everyone's trust.
The new manager turned out to be much more likeable than we expected.
His likeable personality makes him popular among his colleagues.

Synset Examples

an attractive and likable young man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agreeable - pleasant or easy to like
  • Synonym: pleasant - giving a sense of happy satisfaction or enjoyment
  • Antonym: unpleasant - not enjoyable, disagreeable

Etymology

From 'like' + '-able', indicating capability or suitability.

2. ADJ

/ ˈlaɪkəbəl / (AmE), / ˈlaɪkəbl / (BrE)
(of a person or character) easy to like; pleasant, attractive, or appealing to others.
easy to like; appealing

Examples

The main character in the novel is extremely likeable and relatable.
Her likeable personality made her popular with her classmates and teachers alike.
Even villains can sometimes be unexpectedly likeable in certain stories.

Synset Examples

the sympathetic characters in the play

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unlikeable - not easy to like; unpleasant or unattractive
  • Degree Higher: adorable - extremely attractive or lovable
  • Synonym: pleasant - giving a sense of happy satisfaction or enjoyment

Etymology

From 'like' + '-able', meaning capable of being liked.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02384845-a
Synset POS: a
