pearlweed

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɝːlˌwid / (AmE), / ˈpɜːlˌwiːd / (BrE)
any low-growing plant of the genus Sagina, with small spherical flowers that resemble pearls
a plant of the genus Sagina

Examples

The moist garden beds were dotted with clusters of pearlweed near the stepping stones.
Botanists often study pearlweed for its resilience in damp, sandy soils.
After the rain, the tiny flowers of pearlweed glistened in the sunlight.

Etymology

Formed from 'pearl' and 'weed', referring to the pearl-like flowers and weedy growth habit.
