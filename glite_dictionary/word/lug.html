Lug

1. PROPN

/ luːg / (AmE), / luːg / (BrE)
An ancient Celtic god associated with the arts, crafts, and light, revered especially in Irish mythology.
ancient Celtic god

Examples

The festival of Lughnasadh is named after the Celtic god Lug.
Many legends describe Lug as a master of all arts and skills.
Ancient druids held ceremonies in honor of Lug during mid-summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lugh - Irish form of the name for the same Celtic god

Etymology

From Irish *Lugh*, a prominent figure in Celtic mythology.
