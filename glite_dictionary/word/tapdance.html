to tapdance

1. VERB

/ ˈtæpˌdæns / (AmE), / ˈtæpˌdɑːns / (BrE)
to perform a dance in which rhythmic tapping sounds are made with the feet by means of metal plates fixed to the soles of special shoes
to dance rhythmically with tap shoes

Examples

She learned to tapdance when she was only seven years old.
The star of the show tapdanced across the stage to thunderous applause.
I always wanted to tapdance on Broadway someday.

Synset Examples

Glover tapdances better than anybody

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clog dance - a dance performed with heavy, wooden-soled shoes, producing percussive sounds
  • Antonym: ballet - a classical dance form characterized by grace and precision of movement

Etymology

From 'tap' (referring to tapping sound) + 'dance'.
