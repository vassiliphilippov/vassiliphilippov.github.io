commonness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑmənnəs / (AmE), / ˈkɒmənnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of sharing features or attributes with others; the condition of being common
state of being common or shared

Examples

The commonness of certain behaviors makes them seem normal in society.
There was a commonness among the paintings that suggested a shared influence.
Scientists look for the commonness of traits within a species to understand its evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: similarity - the state of being similar
  • Antonym: distinctiveness - the quality of being distinct or different

Etymology

From 'common' + suffix '-ness.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmənnəs / (AmE), / ˈkɒmənnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being common or frequently occurring.
the state of being common

Examples

The commonness of the name made it difficult to identify the correct person.
We were surprised by the commonness of this species in the region.
Discussing the commonness of certain illnesses can help reduce stigma.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rarity - the state of being rare or uncommon
  • Synonym: prevalence - the fact or condition of being widespread or common

Etymology

From 'common' + '-ness', denoting the state or quality of being common.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.mən.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.ən.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of lacking taste, sophistication, or refinement; vulgarity or coarseness.
lack of taste or refinement; vulgarity

Examples

Some critics dismissed the painting for its commonness, saying it lacked any sense of elegance.
The novel was criticized for the commonness of its language and settings.
He regretted the commonness in his manners when dining with such sophisticated company.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vulgarity - rudeness or lack of refinement or taste
  • Antonym: refinement - elegance or cultivated taste; the opposite of coarseness

4. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmənnəs / (AmE), / ˈkɒmənnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being common, usual, or frequent.
the quality of being common or frequent

Examples

The commonness of the species makes it easy to spot in local parks.
He disliked the commonness of mass-produced furniture and sought something unique for his home.
Some find comfort in the commonness of familiar traditions and routines.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rarity - the quality of being rare or uncommon
  • Synonym: ordinariness - the state or quality of being ordinary or unremarkable

Etymology

From 'common' (Latin communis, meaning 'shared by all or many') + '-ness' suffix forming nouns indicating a state or quality.
