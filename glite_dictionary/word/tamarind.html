tamarind

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæmərɪnd / (AmE), / ˈtamərɪnd / (BrE)
the fruit or seed pod of a tropical tree, containing tangy edible pulp often used in cooking and making chutneys
tangy tropical fruit pod used in cooking

Examples

The chef added tamarind to the sauce to give it a distinct tangy flavor.
She prepared a refreshing drink using fresh tamarind pulp for her guests.
Dried tamarind is a key ingredient in many South Asian and Central American dishes.

Etymology

From Arabic *tamar hindī* (Indian date).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtæmərɪnd / (AmE), / ˈtæmərɪnd / (BrE)
a tropical evergreen tree (Tamarindus indica) with feathery leaves, fragrant flowers, and long pods containing edible, acidic, brown pulp; also, the fruit or pulp produced by this tree, used in cooking and beverages.
tropical tree with edible acidic fruit

Examples

She added tamarind to the curry for its unique sour flavor.
The tamarind tree in the garden provides shade and tasty fruit pods every summer.
Traditional drinks in many countries use tamarind as a key ingredient for a refreshing taste.

Etymology

From Arabic 'tamr hindī' (Indian date).
