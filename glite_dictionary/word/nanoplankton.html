nanoplankton

1. NOUN

/ ˌnænoʊˈplæŋktən / (AmE), / ˌnanəʊˈplaŋktən / (BrE)
Microscopic planktonic organisms, usually between 2 and 20 micrometers in size, found in aquatic environments.
microscopic organisms in plankton
medical

Examples

Scientists studied the role of nanoplankton in the ocean's carbon cycle.
The researcher collected water samples to analyze the nanoplankton population.
Many marine food webs begin with nanoplankton as a crucial source of nutrition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phytoplankton - microscopic plant-like plankton
  • Synonym: zooplankton - microscopic animal-like plankton
  • Degree Higher: plankton - small and microscopic organisms drifting in water

Etymology

From nano- (meaning 'dwarf,' from Greek 'nanos') + plankton (from Greek 'planktos,' meaning 'wandering').
