Arachis

1. NOUN

/ əˈrækɪs / (AmE), / əˈrækɪs / (BrE)
A genus of leguminous plants whose pods mature underground, including the peanut.
plant genus including peanut

Examples

The genus Arachis contains several species, the most famous being the peanut.
Researchers studied Arachis plants to understand how their pods develop underground.
Many legumes, including Arachis, are important sources of protein.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peanut - the most well-known species of the Arachis genus, grown for its edible seeds

Etymology

From Greek arakis (kind of legume).

2. NOUN

/ əˈrækɪs / (AmE), / əˈrækɪs / (BrE)
A genus of leguminous plants whose pods ripen underground, including the peanut.
a genus of plants including the peanut

Examples

The genus Arachis includes several species native to South America.
Scientists have studied Arachis to improve peanut crop yields.
Many wild relatives of Arachis are valuable for breeding disease resistance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peanut - the well-known edible seed, also called groundnut

Etymology

From Greek *arachís* (a kind of leguminous plant).
