Alopecurus

1. NOUN

/ æ.ləˈpiː.kjʊ.rəs / (AmE), / ˌal.əˈpiː.kjʊ.rəs / (BrE)
A genus of grasses, known as foxtail, comprising annual or perennial species found in meadows and sometimes considered agricultural weeds.
a genus of grasses (foxtail)

Examples

Some species of Alopecurus are valued as ornamental grasses in garden landscapes.
The field was overrun by Alopecurus, making it difficult to grow crops.
Researchers studied the impact of Alopecurus on native meadow ecosystems.

Etymology

From Greek alōpēkouros, meaning 'foxtail', from alōpēx (fox) and oura (tail).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæləˈpikjərəs / (AmE), / ˌaləˈpiːkjʊrəs / (BrE)
A genus of annual or perennial grasses, including species used as ornamental plants, meadow grasses, and some that are agricultural weeds.
genus of grasses, some ornamental or weedy

Examples

Several species in the Alopecurus genus are valued as ornamental grasses in garden design.
Farmers often struggle to control Alopecurus populations because some species are persistent agricultural weeds.
The meadow was dotted with tall stems of Alopecurus, swaying gently in the spring breeze.

Etymology

From Greek 'alopekourus', meaning 'foxtail', from 'alopex' (fox) + 'oura' (tail).
