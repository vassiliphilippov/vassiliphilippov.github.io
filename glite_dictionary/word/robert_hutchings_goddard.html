Robert Hutchings Goddard

1. PROPN

/ ˈroʊbɚt ˈhʌtʃɪŋz ˈɡɑdɚd / (AmE), / ˈrɒbət ˈhʌtʃɪŋz ˈɡɒdəd / (BrE)
(1882–1945) United States physicist who developed the first successful liquid-fueled rocket, regarded as a pioneer of modern rocketry.
U.S. physicist and rocket pioneer

Examples

Many consider Robert Hutchings Goddard the father of modern rocketry for his pioneering innovations.
The first liquid-fueled rocket was successfully launched by Robert Hutchings Goddard in 1926.
NASA often honors Robert Hutchings Goddard for his significant contributions to space exploration.
