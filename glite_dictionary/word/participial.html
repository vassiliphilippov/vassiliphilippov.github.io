participial

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑrtɪˈsɪpiəl / (AmE), / ˌpɑːtɪˈsɪpɪəl / (BrE)
A non-finite verb form, such as the present or past participle, used mainly as an adjective or in forming compound verb tenses.
non-finite verb form used as adjective or tense

Examples

The phrase 'running late' contains a participial form of the verb 'run'.
In the sentence, 'Baked fresh daily,' the word 'baked' is a participial.
Some English learners struggle to identify the correct participial in complex sentences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: participle - a non-finite verb form with adjectival uses

Etymology

From Latin participialis, from participium (participle).

relating to or consisting of participles; of or characteristic of a participle.
relating to a participle

Examples

The report included several participial phrases that made the text complex.
He struggled to identify the participial clauses in the sentence.
Teachers often emphasize recognizing participial forms when analyzing grammar.
