Sistrurus miliaris

1. NOUN

/ sɪsˈtruːrəs mɪlˈjɛərɪs / (AmE), / sɪsˈtruːrəs mɪlˈjɑːrɪs / (BrE)
A small species of pygmy rattlesnake native to the southeastern United States.
small pygmy rattlesnake species

Examples

The Sistrurus miliaris is often found in sandy areas of the southeastern United States.
Venom from the Sistrurus miliaris is less potent than that of larger rattlesnake species.
Wildlife researchers are studying the habitat preferences of Sistrurus miliaris.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rattlesnake - a venomous American snake with a rattle at the end of its tail

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Sistrurus' and species name 'miliaris'.
