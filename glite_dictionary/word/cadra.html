Cadra

1. NOUN

/ ˈkædrə / (AmE), / ˈkædrə / (BrE)
A genus of small moths in the family Pyralidae, some species of which are pests of stored products.
a genus of small moths (Pyralidae)

Examples

The larvae of Cadra species can cause significant damage to stored grain products.
Entomologists studied the spread of Cadra moths in agricultural storage facilities.
A particular Cadra species is known as the almond moth due to its preference for nuts.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Cadra', used in entomological classification.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæd.rə / (AmE), / ˈkæd.rə / (BrE)
A genus of small moths in the family Pyralidae, several species of which are pests of stored products.
genus of small moths (Pyralidae)

Examples

The almond moth belongs to the genus Cadra, which includes several pest species.
Researchers identified a new species in the Cadra genus affecting stored food grains.
Many of the Cadra moths are considered agricultural pests worldwide.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, created for scientific classification.
