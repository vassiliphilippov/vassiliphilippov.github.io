Ursulines

1. NOUN

/ ˈɜr.sjəˌlaɪnz / (AmE), / ˈɜː.sjʊˌlaɪnz / (BrE)
A Roman Catholic religious order of women founded in 1535 by Saint Angela Merici, dedicated mainly to education and charitable works.
Roman Catholic order of women

Examples

The Ursulines established many schools for girls throughout Europe and North America.
Several members of the Ursulines dedicated their lives to teaching and charitable work.
In the eighteenth century, the Ursulines played a key role in the education of young women.

Etymology

From the Latin 'Ursulinae', named after Saint Ursula.
