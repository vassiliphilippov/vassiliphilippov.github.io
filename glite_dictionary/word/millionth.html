millionth

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪljənθ / (AmE), / ˈmɪljənθ / (BrE)
one part out of a million equal parts
one part in a million

Examples

A millionth of a second is called a microsecond in science.
Only a millionth of the solution is made up of this rare element.
He inherited just a millionth of his ancestor's vast fortune.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: thousandth - one part in a thousand equal parts
  • Degree Higher: billionth - one part in a billion equal parts

Etymology

Formed from "million" plus the ordinal suffix "-th".

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɪljənθ / (AmE), / ˈmɪljənθ / (BrE)
the ordinal number corresponding to one million in a sequence; the position marked as number one million.
ordinal number: position one millionth

Examples

She was the millionth visitor to the museum and won a special prize.
For the millionth time, I reminded him to lock the door before leaving.
Their company celebrated its millionth sale with a big party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: million - the number 1,000,000

Etymology

From "million" + the ordinal suffix "-th."

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02220260-s
Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɪljənθ / (AmE), / ˈmɪljənθ / (BrE)
the position of someone or something that is number 1,000,000 in a sequence; the item in a series that comes after nine hundred and ninety-nine thousand, nine hundred and ninety-nine others
the 1,000,000th item in a sequence

Examples

She proudly accepted the award for being the millionth customer at the store.
The millionth visitor was greeted with confetti and cheers.
He never imagined he would become the millionth follower on her social media page.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: thousandth - the position of number 1,000 in a sequence

Etymology

Formed from "million" + ordinal suffix "-th".

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13872110-n
Synset POS: n
