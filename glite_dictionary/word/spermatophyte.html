spermatophyte

1. NOUN

/ spɚˈmætəˌfaɪt / (AmE), / spəˈmatəˌfaɪt / (BrE)
a plant that reproduces by means of seeds rather than spores
seed-producing plant

Examples

Ferns and mosses are not spermatophytes because they reproduce by spores rather than seeds.
The appearance of spermatophytes marked a significant step in the evolution of land plants.
Scientists classify flowering plants and conifers as spermatophyte groups.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cryptogam - a plant that reproduces by spores, not seeds

Etymology

From Greek 'sperma' (seed) + 'phyton' (plant).
