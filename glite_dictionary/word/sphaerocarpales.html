Sphaerocarpales

1. NOUN

/ sfɪəroʊˌkɑrˈpeɪliːz / (AmE), / sfɪərəʊˌkɑːˈpɑːliːz / (BrE)
a small order of liverworts, sometimes included in the order Jungermanniales, comprising simple thalloid plants
order of simple liverworts
medical

Examples

The Sphaerocarpales are distinguished by their simple, ribbon-like thalli and unique reproductive structures.
Botanists debated whether to classify Sphaerocarpales as a separate order or within Jungermanniales.
Recent studies have shed light on the phylogenetic relationships within the Sphaerocarpales.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Jungermanniales - an order of leafy liverworts; sometimes includes Sphaerocarpales

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'sphaira' (sphere) and 'karpos' (fruit), referencing the rounded capsules of some species.
