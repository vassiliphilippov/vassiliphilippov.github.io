to begrime

1. VERB

/ bɪˈɡraɪm / (AmE), / bɪˈɡraɪm / (BrE)
to make dirty or cover with grime; to soil or blacken with dirt
make dirty or filthy

Examples

The workers' clothes were begrimed with oil and soot after their shift.
Years of pollution had begrimed the old statues in the city square.
Her hands became begrimed while planting flowers in the garden.

Synset Examples

don't soil your clothes when you play outside!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soil - to make dirty, especially with earth or mud
  • Antonym: clean - to remove dirt from something

Etymology

From be- (intensive prefix) + grime, Middle English *grim* (dirt).
