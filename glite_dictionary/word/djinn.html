djinn

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪn / (AmE), / dʒɪn / (BrE)
(in Islamic belief) an invisible spirit or supernatural being, often believed to influence humans by appearing as people or animals.
supernatural spirit in Islamic belief

Examples

According to legend, a powerful djinn was trapped inside the ancient lamp.
Many stories describe djinn interfering in the lives of humans, sometimes bringing luck or disaster.
The villagers believed a mischievous djinn was responsible for the unexplained events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genie - a supernatural spirit or being in Arabic and Islamic mythology

Etymology

From Arabic 'jinn', plural of 'jinni', meaning 'spirit' or 'genie'.
