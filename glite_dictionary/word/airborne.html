airborne

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛrˌbɔrn / (AmE), / ˈeəˌbɔːn / (BrE)
carried or transported by the air; moved or conveyed through the air.
carried by or through the air

Examples

Many diseases are airborne and can be spread by coughing or sneezing.
The pollen became airborne and caused allergic reactions in the city.
After the explosion, particles of dust were airborne for several hours.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: waterborne - carried or transmitted by water

Etymology

Formed from 'air' + 'borne', past participle of 'bear' (to carry).
