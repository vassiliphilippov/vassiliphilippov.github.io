snow

1. VERB

/ snoʊ / (AmE), / snəʊ / (BrE)
(of snow) to fall from the sky as flakes in cold weather.
fall as snow

Examples

It is expected to snow this weekend in the mountains.
Last night, it snowed so much that schools were closed.
The skies grew dark, and soon it began snowing steadily.

Synset Examples

It was snowing all night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rain - (of rain) to fall as drops of water from clouds
  • Synonym: hail - (of hail) to fall as pellets of frozen rain

Etymology

Old English 'snāwan', from Proto-Germanic *snaiwan.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02764935-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ snoʊ / (AmE), / snəʊ / (BrE)
A layer of white, frozen crystals of water that forms on the ground when temperatures are cold.
frozen white layer covering the ground

Examples

Fresh snow blanketed the entire landscape this morning.
The children built a snowman out of the fallen snow in the yard.
After a night of heavy snow, the roads were closed until midday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ice - frozen water, solid state of water
  • Antonym: rain - precipitation in the form of water drops from clouds

Etymology

Old English *snāw*, from Proto-Germanic *snaiwaz*; related to Dutch *sneeuw* and German *Schnee*.

/ snoʊ / (AmE), / snəʊ / (BrE)
to mislead or deceive someone, especially by pretending good intentions or by being persuasive

informal american

Examples

5. NOUN

/ snoʊ / (AmE), / snəʊ / (BrE)
Atmospheric water vapor frozen into ice crystals and falling in light white flakes or lying on the ground as a white layer.
frozen precipitation as flakes

Examples

Heavy snow fell overnight, covering the roads and trees in white.
The children ran outside to play in the fresh snow after school.
Travel delays are common when a lot of snow accumulates quickly.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rain - precipitation in liquid form
  • Synonym: hail - precipitation in the form of hard pellets of ice
  • Synonym: sleet - a form of precipitation consisting of ice pellets, often mixed with rain or snow

Etymology

Old English 'snāw', from Proto-Germanic '*snaiwaz', related to Dutch 'sneeuw' and German 'Schnee'.

Synset POS: n
