carminative

1. ADJ

/ kɑrˈmɪnətɪv / (AmE), / kɑːˈmɪnətɪv / (BrE)
(of a medicine or substance) relieving flatulence or colic by expelling gas from the stomach or intestines.
relieving gas in the digestive tract
medical

Examples

Peppermint oil is well known for its carminative properties, helping to soothe digestive discomfort.
Doctors often recommend carminative herbs to relieve excessive gas and bloating.
The carminative effect of ginger makes it popular in traditional remedies for stomach upset.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antiflatulent - a substance that prevents or relieves flatulence

Etymology

From Latin carminativus (relieving flatulence), from carminare (to card, to cleanse).

2. NOUN

/ kɑrˈmɪnətɪv / (AmE), / kɑːˈmɪnətɪv / (BrE)
A substance or medicine that prevents the formation of gas in the digestive tract or relieves flatulence by aiding its expulsion.
drug that relieves or prevents gas in the digestive tract
medical

Examples

Ginger is sometimes used as a carminative to relieve indigestion and bloating.
Your doctor may recommend a carminative if you frequently experience uncomfortable gas.
Many herbal teas have mild carminative properties, helping soothe the digestive system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antiflatulent - a substance that reduces or prevents flatulence
  • Degree Higher: laxative - a substance that promotes bowel movements

Etymology

From Latin 'carminativus', from 'carminare' (to card wool, clean out), used in Medieval Latin to mean 'expel gas'.
