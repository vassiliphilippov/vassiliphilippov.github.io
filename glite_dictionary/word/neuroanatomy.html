neuroanatomy

1. NOUN

/ ˌnʊroʊəˈnætəmi / (AmE), / ˌnjʊərəʊəˈnætəmi / (BrE)
the study of the structure and organization of the nervous system, especially the brain and spinal cord
anatomy of the nervous system
medical

Examples

She decided to specialize in neuroanatomy during her medical education.
A clear understanding of neuroanatomy is essential for brain surgeons.
The textbook includes detailed diagrams to support the study of neuroanatomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anatomy - the study of the structure of living things

Etymology

From neuro- (relating to nerves or the nervous system) + anatomy (from Greek anatomē, dissection).
