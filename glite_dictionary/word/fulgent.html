fulgent

1. ADJ

/ ˈfʊl.dʒənt / (AmE), / ˈfʊl.dʒənt / (BrE)
Shining brightly; radiating intense light.
shining intensely; radiant

Examples

The fulgent chandelier filled the room with radiant light.
Her dress sparkled with a fulgent array of sequins under the stage lights.
They admired the fulgent sunset as it cast golden hues across the ocean.

Synset Examples

the blazing sun
blinding headlights
dazzling snow
fulgent patterns of sunlight
the glaring sun

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brilliant - extremely bright or radiant
  • Synonym: dazzling - extremely bright; blinding to the eyes
  • Antonym: dim - not bright; lacking in light

Etymology

From Latin *fulgens*, present participle of *fulgēre* (to shine).
