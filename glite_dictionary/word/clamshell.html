clamshell

1. NOUN

/ ˈklæmˌʃɛl / (AmE), / ˈklæmʃɛl / (BrE)
a mechanical bucket with hinged halves that open and close like a clam's shell, used mainly for dredging or handling bulk materials
hinged mechanical bucket for dredging

Examples

The crane operator lowered the clamshell into the river to collect sediment from the bottom.
We used a clamshell to remove debris from the construction site.
A heavy-duty clamshell is essential for many dredging operations in ports and harbors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grab bucket - a similar mechanical bucket for lifting bulk materials

Etymology

From clam + shell, named for its resemblance to a clam's shell.

2. NOUN

/ ˈklæmˌʃɛl / (AmE), / ˈklæmʃɛl / (BrE)
the hinged shell of a clam, consisting of two parts that open and close
the shell of a clam

Examples

He found a beautiful clamshell on the beach and took it home as a souvenir.
The artist used a clamshell as a mold for her pottery.
Children love searching for clamshells along the shoreline in summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shell - the hard protective outer case of a mollusk or crustacean
