niobium

1. NOUN

/ naɪˈoʊbiəm / (AmE), / naɪˈəʊbiəm / (BrE)
a soft, grey, ductile metallic element used mainly in alloys and superconducting materials; chemical symbol Nb.
a metallic chemical element (Nb)

Examples

The company is researching ways to use niobium in high-strength steel alloys.
Pure niobium is rarely found in nature and is usually extracted from minerals.
Superconducting magnets often rely on niobium for their performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tantalum - a rare, hard, blue-grey metallic element; chemical element closely related to niobium

Etymology

From New Latin 'niobium', named after Niobe, daughter of Tantalus in Greek mythology, because of its similarity to tantalum.
