adoption

1. NOUN

/ əˈdɑpʃən / (AmE), / əˈdɒpʃən / (BrE)
the act of accepting or starting to use something new or different, especially after careful consideration or approval
the act of accepting or approving something

Examples

The rapid adoption of new technology transformed the industry's productivity.
Her proposal met with immediate adoption by the board of directors.
The adoption of his ideas was crucial to the project's success.

Synset Examples

its adoption by society
the proposal found wide acceptance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acceptance - the act of agreeing or consenting to something

Etymology

From Latin adoptio (a taking by choice).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00181262-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ əˈdɑpʃən / (AmE), / əˈdɒpʃən / (BrE)
the act of taking up, using, or appropriating ideas, words, or practices from another source
appropriation of ideas or words

Examples

The adoption of European design trends transformed the city's architecture.
Her novel shows the adoption of several foreign literary techniques.
Cultural adoption of new words is common in globalized societies.

Synset Examples

the borrowing of ancient motifs was very apparent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: borrowing - the act of taking and using something from another source
  • Synonym: appropriation - the act of taking something for oneself, often without permission

Etymology

From Latin adoptio, from adoptare (to choose for oneself, take by choice).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00085191-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ əˈdɑpʃən / (AmE), / əˈdɒpʃən / (BrE)
the legal process by which a person takes another, usually a child, into their family and assumes parental rights and responsibilities for them
legal process of becoming a parent to a child not born to you

Examples

After years of waiting, the couple celebrated the finalization of the adoption of their daughter.
The agency handles all the legal paperwork required for adoption.
International adoption can be a complicated and lengthy process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to adopt - to take a child legally into one's family and bring it up as one's own

Etymology

From Latin 'adoptio', from 'adoptare' (to choose for oneself, to adopt).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01187782-n
Synset POS: n
