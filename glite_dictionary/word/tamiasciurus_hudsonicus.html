Tamiasciurus hudsonicus

1. NOUN

/ ˌtæmiəˌsaɪˈjʊərəs hʌdˈsoʊnɪkəs / (AmE), / ˌtæmiəsɪˈjʊərəs hʌdˈsəʊnɪkəs / (BrE)
A species of red squirrel native to forests in the northern United States and Canada.
North American red squirrel species

Examples

The Tamiasciurus hudsonicus is known for its reddish fur and energetic behavior.
Researchers tracked the diet of the Tamiasciurus hudsonicus in the boreal forests.
Many birders are delighted to spot a Tamiasciurus hudsonicus during their hikes in Canada.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red squirrel - the common name of Tamiasciurus hudsonicus, a small, reddish-brown tree squirrel

Etymology

From the genus name Tamiasciurus and species epithet hudsonicus, referring to Hudson Bay.
