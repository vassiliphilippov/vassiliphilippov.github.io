Smilodon

1. NOUN

/ ˈsmaɪlədɒn / (AmE), / ˈsmʌɪlədɒn / (BrE)
an extinct genus of large, saber-toothed cats that lived in the Americas during the Pleistocene epoch, commonly known as saber-toothed tigers
extinct saber-toothed cat genus

Examples

The Smilodon is often depicted with enormous canine teeth used to seize prey.
Fossils of Smilodon have been found in the La Brea Tar Pits in California.
Unlike modern big cats, the Smilodon had a more robust build and shorter tail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saber-toothed tiger - common name for Smilodon or similar prehistoric cats

Etymology

From Greek 'smilē' (chisel, knife) and 'odous' (tooth), referring to its long canine teeth.
