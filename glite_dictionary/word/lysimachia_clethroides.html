Lysimachia clethroides

1. NOUN

/ laɪsɪˈmeɪkiə klɛˈθrɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / lɪˌsɪməkɪə klɛˈθrɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A species of plant in the loosestrife family, native to East Asia, known for its arching flower spikes and white blooms; commonly called gooseneck loosestrife.
gooseneck loosestrife plant species

Examples

Many gardeners appreciate the elegant arch of the Lysimachia clethroides flower spikes in summer borders.
The Lysimachia clethroides spreads quickly, so be mindful of its growth habit in your garden.
She planted Lysimachia clethroides alongside roses to add texture and interest to the flower bed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gooseneck loosestrife - common name for Lysimachia clethroides
  • Degree Higher: loosestrife - any plant of the genus Lysimachia

Etymology

Genus name from Ancient Greek Λυσιμάχεια (Lysimachia), referring to King Lysimachus; species name from Greek 'klēthra' (alder) + '-oides' (resembling).
