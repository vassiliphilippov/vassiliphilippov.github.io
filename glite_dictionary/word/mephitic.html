mephitic

1. ADJ

/ məˈfɪtɪk / (AmE), / mɪˈfɪtɪk / (BrE)
(of a gas or vapor) having a foul, poisonous, or noxious smell, especially from pollution or decay.
foul-smelling or poisonous (gas/vapor)

Examples

The cave was filled with a mephitic vapor that made it hard to breathe.
Factories released mephitic fumes into the air, endangering the health of nearby residents.
The sewer's mephitic odors lingered in the early morning fog.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malodorous - having an unpleasant smell
  • Antonym: fragrant - having a pleasant or sweet smell

Etymology

From Latin mephiticus, from mephitis (noxious vapor).
