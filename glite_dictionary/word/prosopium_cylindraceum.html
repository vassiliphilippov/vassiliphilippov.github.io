Prosopium cylindraceum

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈsoʊpiəm sɪˌlɪndrɪˈsiəm / (AmE), / prəˈsəʊpiəm sɪˌlɪndrɪˈsiːəm / (BrE)
A species of whitefish with a bronze back, found in northern North America and Siberia.
a whitefish of northern North America and Siberia

Examples

Many anglers in Canada prize the Prosopium cylindraceum for its delicate flavor.
The Prosopium cylindraceum is an important part of the Arctic freshwater ecosystem.
Researchers studied the spawning patterns of Prosopium cylindraceum in northern lakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whitefish - a common name for several species of freshwater fish

Etymology

From New Latin; genus 'Prosopium' + species 'cylindraceum', meaning 'cylindrical'.
