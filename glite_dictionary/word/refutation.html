refutation

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛfjuˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛfjʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of proving a statement or theory to be wrong or false
the act of proving something false

Examples

Her detailed refutation of the claims silenced all her critics.
The scientist presented a strong refutation of the outdated theory.
He wrote an article that serves as a clear refutation of the rumor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebuttal - the act of countering an argument; synonym to 'refutation'
  • Antonym: confirmation - the act of establishing or supporting the truth; antonym to 'refutation'

Etymology

From Latin refutatio (refuting, rebuttal).

3. NOUN

/ ˌrɛfjəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛfjʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of proving a statement, argument, or theory to be wrong or false; evidence or reasoning that demonstrates falsity.
evidence or argument disproving something

Examples

Her detailed refutation of the allegations convinced the committee of her innocence.
The scientist presented a refutation supported by new experimental data.
His article offers a strong refutation of common misconceptions about climate change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebuttal - a statement that contradicts or denies another statement
  • Antonym: confirmation - evidence that supports the truth of something

Etymology

From Latin refutatio (a rebuttal or disproof), from refutare (to repel, refute).
