sunburn

1. VERB

/ ˈsʌnˌbɝn / (AmE), / ˈsʌnˌbɜːn / (BrE)
to get a reddened or blistered skin as a result of excessive exposure to the sun's ultraviolet rays
get reddened skin from too much sun

Examples

If you don't use sunscreen at the beach, you might sunburn quickly.
She sunburned her shoulders while hiking without a hat.
Children are especially likely to sunburn after swimming for hours.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tan - to become brown or bronzed from exposure to the sun

Etymology

Formed from 'sun' + 'burn', modeled after the noun 'sunburn'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsʌnˌbɝn / (AmE), / ˈsʌnˌbɜːn / (BrE)
A condition in which skin becomes red and sore from excessive exposure to the sun's ultraviolet rays.
redness of skin from sun exposure

Examples

She forgot to wear sunscreen and ended up with a painful sunburn on her shoulders.
After a long day at the beach, his face was red from sunburn.
Severe sunburn can cause peeling and sensitivity for several days.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: tan - darker skin color after sun exposure, usually without soreness

Etymology

Formed from 'sun' + 'burn', indicating skin burned by the sun.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14313867-n
Synset POS: n
