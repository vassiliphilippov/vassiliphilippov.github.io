mental defectiveness

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛntəl dɪˈfɛktɪvnəs / (AmE), / ˈmɛntəl dɪˈfɛktɪvnəs / (BrE)
a condition of intellectual disability marked by intelligence that falls significantly below the normal range, historically referred to in medical and psychological contexts
intellectual disability; below-average intelligence
medical archaic offensive

Examples

The institution provided care for children with mental defectiveness throughout the twentieth century.
Doctors once used the term mental defectiveness to describe various levels of intellectual impairment.
Modern psychiatry rejects the label mental defectiveness in favor of more respectful terminology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intellectual disability - significantly impaired cognitive functioning and adaptive behavior
  • Synonym: mental retardation - impairment of intellectual and adaptive functioning
