simmering

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪmɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɪmərɪŋ / (BrE)
The process of cooking food gently in liquid kept just below the boiling point.
cooking just below boiling point

Examples

Constant simmering is ideal for making rich stocks and broths.
The recipe calls for the simmering of the sauce for 30 minutes to enhance the flavor.
Her method involves the careful simmering of the vegetables in a fragrant broth.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: boiling - cooking in a liquid at its highest temperature (boiling point)
  • Degree Lower: poaching - cooking food gently in a small amount of liquid below simmering

Etymology

From the verb 'simmer', likely imitative, first recorded in the 17th century.
