bogy

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊɡi / (AmE), / ˈbəʊɡi / (BrE)
An unidentified aircraft, especially one that may be enemy.
unidentified (possibly enemy) aircraft (military)

Examples

Radar picked up a bogy approaching our airspace at high speed.
Pilots were ordered to intercept the bogy detected on the western flank.
The squadron received an alert about a possible bogy during their training exercise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bogey - unidentified aircraft; alternative spelling

Etymology

Possibly derived from 'bogey', an older term for something frightening or unknown.

2. NOUN

/ ˈboʊ.ɡi / (AmE), / ˈbəʊ.ɡi / (BrE)
An imaginary evil being or spirit, typically used to frighten children; a bogle or goblin. Sometimes, when used as a proper name, refers to the Devil.
imaginary evil being; goblin or bogle

Examples

The old house was said to be haunted by a terrifying bogy.
As a child, she feared the bogy lurking under her bed.
In some folklore, the bogy is considered another name for the Devil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bogey - another word for a goblin or ghost used to frighten
  • Synonym: goblin - a mischievous, ugly, or evil spirit or sprite
  • Synonym: devil - the supreme spirit of evil in Christian and Jewish belief

Etymology

Probably from Scots 'bogle', influenced by 'bogey'.
