granitic

1. ADJ

/ ɡræˈnɪtɪk / (AmE), / ɡrəˈnɪtɪk / (BrE)
Extremely hard or unyielding, like granite; resembling granite in toughness or firmness.
extremely hard or unyielding; like granite

Examples

She approached the judge with a granitic determination that would not be shaken.
The granitic resolve of the team helped them overcome all obstacles.
His granitic stare made it clear he would not change his mind.

Synset Examples

a granitic fist

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: granite - a very hard, granular, crystalline, igneous rock

Etymology

From granite + -ic, modeled after the rock granite.

2. ADJ

/ ɡræˈnɪtɪk / (AmE), / ɡrəˈnɪtɪk / (BrE)
(of a person or attitude) extremely hard, unyielding, or showing no empathy or tenderness.
emotionally hard or unyielding

Examples

Her granitic expression showed no trace of sympathy for the child's plight.
He faced criticism with a granitic indifference that frustrated his opponents.
Despite their pleas, she remained granitic and unmoved by their arguments.

Synset Examples

his flinty gaze
the child's misery would move even the most obdurate heart

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unfeeling - lacking emotion or sympathy
  • Antonym: tender - showing gentleness or sympathy

Etymology

From granite (the hard igneous rock) + -ic (forming adjectives).
