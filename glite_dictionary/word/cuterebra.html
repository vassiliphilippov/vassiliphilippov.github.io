Cuterebra

1. NOUN

/ kjuːtəˈriːbrə / (AmE), / kjuːtəˈriːbrə / (BrE)
a genus of large botflies whose larvae infest rodents and other mammals; the type genus of the family Cuterebridae.
genus of botflies (family Cuterebridae)
medical

Examples

The larvae of Cuterebra often develop beneath the skin of wild rodents.
Veterinarians may treat animals for infestations caused by Cuterebra species.
A Cuterebra infection typically results in a swelling with a breathing hole on the host's skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: botfly - a type of fly whose larvae are parasitic in mammals

Etymology

From New Latin, likely formed from Latin words; used as a taxonomic name in entomology.

2. NOUN

/ kjuːˈtɛrəbrə / (AmE), / kjuːˈtɛrɪbrə / (BrE)
A genus of large parasitic flies of the family Cuterebridae, whose larvae infest rodents and other mammals.
genus of parasitic flies

Examples

The Cuterebra genus is known for its parasitic larvae that infect small mammals.
Biologists studied the life cycle of Cuterebra to better understand host-parasite relationships.
Several species within Cuterebra are notable for their unique parasitic adaptations.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Cuterebridae - family of parasitic flies related to Cuterebra

Etymology

From New Latin, coined as a genus name in scientific taxonomy.
