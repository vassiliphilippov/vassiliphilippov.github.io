confident

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɑːn.fɪ.dənt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.fɪ.dənt / (BrE)
Certain in one's judgment or actions; not likely to make a mistake.
certain in judgment or actions

Examples

The lawyer offered a confident opinion that left no room for doubt.
Her confident handling of the crisis reassured everyone in the company.
He made a confident decision that proved to be correct.

Synset Examples

most surefooted of the statesmen who dealt with the depression
demonstrates a surefooted storytelling talent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surefooted - not likely to make mistakes; confident and reliable
  • Antonym: uncertain - not confident or unsure in judgment or action

Etymology

From Latin 'confidens', present participle of 'confidere' (to trust).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00307707-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈkɑːn.fɪ.dənt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.fɪ.dənt / (BrE)
certain about something or convinced that something is true or will happen; having strong belief or assurance.
sure or certain; convinced

Examples

She felt confident that her answer was correct after checking it twice.
The team is confident they will win the championship this year.
He spoke in a confident tone that reassured everyone in the room.

Synset Examples

were convinced that it would be to their advantage to join
I am positive he is lying
was confident he would win

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unsure - not certain or convinced about something
  • Synonym: certain - completely sure; having no doubt

Etymology

From Latin 'confidere' (to have full trust), from 'con-' (with) + 'fidere' (to trust).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00338070-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈkɑn.fə.dənt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.fɪ.dənt / (BrE)
feeling or showing certainty in oneself or in a situation; sure of one's abilities or success
sure of oneself; certain

Examples

She gave a confident answer during the job interview, impressing the panel.
After months of practice, he felt more confident on stage than ever before.
You need to be confident in your decision and stand by it.

Synset Examples

a confident speaker
a confident reply
his manner is more confident these days
confident of fulfillment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-assured - having confidence in one's own abilities or worth
  • Synonym: certain - feeling sure that something is true or will happen
  • Antonym: unsure - not certain or confident
  • Antonym: insecure - lacking confidence or assurance

Etymology

From Latin 'confidens', present participle of 'confidere' (to trust).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00339715-a
