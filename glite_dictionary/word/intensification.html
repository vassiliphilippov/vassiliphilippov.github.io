intensification

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌtɛnsəfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌtɛnsɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of increasing the contrast or density of a photographic film or image, especially to improve its appearance or clarity
increasing contrast in photographic film

Examples

The intensification of the old film restored its original detail and depth.
Photographers sometimes use intensification to enhance the quality of underexposed negatives.
After intensification, the image revealed much finer features than before.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reduction - the process of decreasing the contrast of photographic film

Etymology

From Latin 'intensus' (intense) + English suffix '-fication', indicating the action or process.

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˌtɛnsəfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌtɛnsɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the action of making something stronger, more intense, or more extreme
making something stronger or more intense

Examples

The intensification of the storm forced residents to seek shelter.
There has been an intensification of efforts to reduce air pollution.
Critics warn against the intensification of military conflict in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intensity - the quality of being intense
  • Antonym: weakening - the process of making something weaker

Etymology

From Latin 'intensificatio', from 'intensus' (intense) + '-ficare' (to make).
