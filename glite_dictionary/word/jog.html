jog

1. NOUN

/ dʒɑɡ / (AmE), / dʒɒɡ / (BrE)
a sharp or sudden change in direction, especially in a road, path, or line
a sharp change in direction

Examples

The trail took a sudden jog to the left near the old oak tree.
We almost missed the sign at the jog in the road.
The fence follows the field's edge but makes a slight jog around the pond.

4. VERB

/ dʒɑg / (AmE), / dʒɒg / (BrE)
to give a slight push or nudge to someone or something, especially to attract attention or remind
give a slight push to

Examples

Could you jog my elbow if I start to doze off during the meeting?
He jogged her arm to get her attention as they walked through the crowd.
A gentle breeze jogged the door open just a little.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nudge - to push gently or poke
  • Synonym: prod - to poke with a finger or pointed object

Etymology

Probably from Middle English joggen, of uncertain origin, possibly imitative.
