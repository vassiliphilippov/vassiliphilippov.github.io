Gymnadenia conopsea

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪmˌnædəˈniːə kəˈnɒpsiə / (AmE), / dʒɪmˌnædəˈniːə kəˈnɒpsiə / (BrE)
A European orchid with dense spikes of fragrant pink, lilac, or red flowers, each flower having a conspicuous spur.
European orchid with fragrant spiked flowers

Examples

The meadows were dotted with the delicate Gymnadenia conopsea, filling the air with a sweet fragrance.
Botanists were excited to find a thriving population of Gymnadenia conopsea in the protected nature reserve.
The distinctive pink flowers of Gymnadenia conopsea make it a favorite among wild orchid enthusiasts.

Etymology

From Greek 'gymnos' (naked) and 'aden' (gland), referring to floral structures; 'conopsea' from Greek 'kónops' (mosquito), alluding to the flower's shape.
