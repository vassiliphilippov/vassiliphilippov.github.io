Bath

1. PROPN

/ bæθ / (AmE), / bɑːθ / (BrE)
a historic city in southwestern England, known for its Roman baths and Georgian architecture
historic city in England

Examples

Many tourists visit Bath each year to see its ancient Roman baths.
The architecture in Bath is predominantly Georgian, reflecting its 18th-century heritage.
Jane Austen lived in Bath for several years, and the city influenced some of her novels.

Etymology

Named for the Roman baths established in the area during Roman Britain.
