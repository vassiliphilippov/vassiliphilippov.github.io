confinement

1. NOUN

/ kənˈfaɪnmənt / (AmE), / kənˈfaɪnmənt / (BrE)
the state of being kept in a limited space or restricted from freedom; imprisonment or physical limitation
state of being confined or imprisoned

Examples

After the incident, he spent several months in confinement awaiting trial.
Many people found the prolonged confinement during quarantine difficult to endure.
Her illness required a period of strict confinement to her bedroom.

Synset Examples

he was held in confinement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imprisonment - the state of being imprisoned
  • Antonym: liberty - freedom from confinement or restraint

Etymology

From French *confinement*, from confiner ('to confine').

2. NOUN

/ kənˈfaɪnmənt / (AmE), / kənˈfaɪnmənt / (BrE)
the act of keeping someone within limits or restricting their liberty, usually by imprisoning or detaining them
act of restraining someone's liberty

Examples

The court ordered the confinement of the suspect until the trial began.
After several years of confinement, the prisoner was finally released.
His unjust confinement attracted international attention and protests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imprisonment - the state of being held in prison as a legal punishment
  • Antonym: liberation - the act of setting someone free from imprisonment or confinement

Etymology

From French *confinement*, from confiner (to confine), from Latin *confinare* (to border, to confine).

3. NOUN

/ kənˈfaɪnmənt / (AmE), / kənˈfaɪnmənt / (BrE)
the act of keeping someone or something within limits or bounds, especially by force
the act of restricting within boundaries

Examples

Strict confinement of the animals is required to prevent the spread of disease.
After his arrest, he faced six months of confinement in a small cell.
The scientist described the confinement of the experiment to a controlled environment.

Synset Examples

the restriction of the infection to a focal area

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restriction - the act of limiting or controlling something
  • Synonym: imprisonment - the state of being kept in prison
  • Antonym: freedom - the state of not being imprisoned or restricted

Etymology

From French *confinement*, from confiner (to confine).

4. NOUN

/ kənˈfaɪnmənt / (AmE), / kənˈfaɪnmənt / (BrE)
the period during which a woman is in labor, from the onset of contractions until the birth of the child
labor and childbirth period

Examples

After a long confinement, she gave birth to a healthy baby boy.
In Victorian times, women were often secluded during their confinement.
Her confinement was shorter and less difficult than she had anticipated.

Synset Examples

she was in labor for six hours

Related Terms

  • Synonym: labor - the process of childbirth, especially the period of contractions leading to delivery
  • Synonym: delivery - the act of giving birth to a child

Etymology

From Middle French *confinement*, from *confiner* (to confine).
