Stropharia hornemannii

1. NOUN

/ stroʊˈfɛəriə hɔr.nəˈmæn.i.aɪ / (AmE), / strɒˈfɛəriə hɔː.nɪˈmæn.ɪ.aɪ / (BrE)
A large gilled mushroom with a slimy purple or olive cap, purple-black gills as it matures, and a long stalk marked with remnants of a white sheath up to a ring.
species of large gilled mushroom

Examples

The forest floor was dotted with several Stropharia hornemannii, their caps shining in the damp shade.
Mycology students identified the large, slimy-capped mushroom as Stropharia hornemannii during their field trip.
It's important not to confuse Stropharia hornemannii with similar-looking edible fungi, as it is considered inedible.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'strophos' (belt), referring to the ring on the stalk; species named after Jens Wilken Hornemann, Danish botanist.
