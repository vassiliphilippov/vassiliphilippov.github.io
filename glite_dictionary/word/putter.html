putter

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌtər / (AmE), / ˈpʌtə / (BrE)
A golfer who is making putts, especially during the act of putting on the green.
golfer making putts

Examples

The putter studied the green carefully before making his shot.
As an inexperienced putter, she often missed short-distance strokes.
Each putter in the tournament competed for precision on the tricky greens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: putt - to hit a golf ball gently on the green towards the hole
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10515666-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈpʌt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈpʌtə / (BrE)
a golf club with a flat face, designed for making short, low-speed strokes on the putting green
golf club for putting

Examples

He selected his putter to finish the hole from just a few feet away.
Every golfer knows the importance of choosing the right putter for their game.
She practiced with her new putter every evening to improve her putting skills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: putt - to strike a golf ball gently, especially on the green

Etymology

From 'putt' (to strike gently) + -er (agent noun suffix).

3. VERB

/ ˈpʌtɚ / (AmE), / ˈpʌtə / (BrE)
to occupy oneself in a leisurely or aimless way, often doing small or unimportant tasks; to spend time idly without real purpose
do small, aimless tasks or pass time idly
informal

Examples

On weekends, my grandfather likes to putter around the garden.
She spent the afternoon puttering in the kitchen, making little snacks and tidying up.
I didn't accomplish much today except putter about in the garage.

Synset Examples

The old lady is usually mucking about in her little house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potter - to busy oneself in a leisurely, casual way
