hummus

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʊməs, ˈhʌməs / (AmE), / ˈhʊm.əs, ˈhʌm.əs / (BrE)
a thick, creamy spread made from mashed chickpeas, tahini, lemon juice, and garlic, eaten especially as a dip
Middle Eastern chickpea dip

Examples

She brought a bowl of hummus and fresh vegetables to the party.
I like to spread hummus on warm pita bread for a quick snack.
Supermarkets now offer several varieties of hummus, including roasted red pepper and garlic flavors.

Etymology

From Arabic ḥummuṣ (chickpeas).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhʊməs, ˈhʌməs / (AmE), / ˈhʊm.əs, ˈhʌm.əs / (BrE)
a creamy Middle Eastern dip or spread made from mashed chickpeas, tahini, lemon juice, and garlic, often served with pita bread
Middle Eastern chickpea dip

Examples

We ordered a plate of hummus with warm pita bread at the restaurant.
She likes to add hummus to her sandwiches for extra flavor and creaminess.
Fresh vegetables go perfectly with a bowl of homemade hummus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baba ghanoush - a Middle Eastern dip made from roasted eggplant, tahini, garlic, and lemon juice

Etymology

From Arabic ḥummuṣ (chickpeas).
