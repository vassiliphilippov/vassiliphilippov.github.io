blackhead

1. NOUN

/ ˈblækˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈblakˌhɛd / (BrE)
a small dark spot on the skin caused by a blocked pore, typically filled with excess oil and dead skin cells, with the surface appearing black
small dark spot on skin from clogged pore

Examples

She noticed a blackhead on her nose and used a cleanser to remove it.
Teenagers are often concerned about blackheads appearing on their skin during puberty.
The dermatologist explained how to prevent blackheads by keeping pores clean.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: whitehead - a small white bump on the skin caused by a blocked pore, with the surface remaining closed
