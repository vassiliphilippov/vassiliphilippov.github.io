stoker

1. NOUN

/ ˈstoʊkər / (AmE), / ˈstəʊkə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to tend and fuel the fires in a furnace, especially on a coal-fired train or steamship
person who tends fires in steam engines

Examples

The stoker worked tirelessly to keep the steamship running through the night.
As a young man, he found work as a stoker on a locomotive.
Being a stoker was a hot and grueling job during the age of steam engines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fireman - a person in charge of tending fires, especially on a steam engine

Etymology

From the Dutch 'stoken' (to stoke, to feed a fire).

2. NOUN

/ ˈstoʊkər / (AmE), / ˈstəʊkə / (BrE)
a mechanical device for supplying fuel to a furnace automatically
mechanical device for feeding a furnace

Examples

The coal-fired plant installed a new stoker to increase efficiency.
The stoker jammed, cutting off the fuel supply to the boiler.
Automatic stokers revolutionized steam locomotives by reducing manual labor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stoke - to feed or add fuel to a fire or furnace

Etymology

From the verb 'stoke', meaning to feed or fuel a fire, of Dutch origin 'stoken' (to stoke, to poke).
