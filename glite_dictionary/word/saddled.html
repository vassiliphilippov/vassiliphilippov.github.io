saddled

1. ADJ

/ ˈsædld / (AmE), / ˈsædld / (BrE)
(of a horse or other animal) having a saddle on; equipped for riding.
having a saddle on

Examples

The horses were saddled and tied to the post, ready for the long trek ahead.
He approached a saddled mare grazing quietly in the field.
By dawn, the riders had saddled their mounts and set out toward the valley.

Synset Examples

saddled and spurred and ready to ride

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unsaddled - not having a saddle on

Etymology

Past participle of 'saddle', from Old English 'sadol' (seat for a rider).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsædld / (AmE), / ˈsædld / (BrE)
Burdened or encumbered with an unwanted responsibility, duty, or problem.
burdened with responsibility or duty

Examples

After the sudden resignation of her boss, she was saddled with extra duties at work.
He found himself saddled with debt after the failed investment.
Many students feel saddled with loans after graduating from university.

Synset Examples

left me saddled with the bill
found himself saddled with more responsibility than power

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burdened - carrying a heavy load or responsibility
  • Antonym: unburdened - not burdened or encumbered; free from a load or responsibility

Etymology

From Middle English 'sadelen', from 'saddle' (seat for a rider), used figuratively for being burdened.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00873435-s
Synset POS: s
