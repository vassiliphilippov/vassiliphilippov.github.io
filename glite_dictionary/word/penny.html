penny

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛni / (AmE), / ˈpɛni / (BrE)
a small unit of currency in the United Kingdom and formerly in Ireland, equal to one hundredth of a pound
UK/Ireland currency unit; 1/100 of a pound

Examples

He found a shiny penny on the street and put it in his pocket.
The item costs fifty pence, so she handed over a one-pound coin and got two pennies in change.
The collection includes rare Victorian pennies that are highly valued by coin enthusiasts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cent - one hundredth of the basic monetary unit in many countries, e.g., US dollar, euro

Etymology

From Old English *pening*, related to Old High German *pfennig*; original use as a coin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛni / (AmE), / ˈpɛni / (BrE)
a coin worth one-hundredth of a currency unit, used in various monetary systems such as the British pound and the US dollar
a coin worth one-hundredth of a currency unit

Examples

She found a penny lying on the sidewalk.
In the United States, a penny is made of copper-plated zinc.
He saved every penny he earned from his summer job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cent - a coin worth one-hundredth of a US dollar or other units

Etymology

From Old English 'penig', related to Old High German 'pfennig'.
