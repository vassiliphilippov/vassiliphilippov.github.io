adoptive

1. ADJ

/ əˈdɑptɪv / (AmE), / əˈdɒptɪv / (BrE)
Acquired or chosen as one's own, not by birth but by free choice or legal means.
acquired by choice, not birth

Examples

After moving to France, Paris became her adoptive city.
He felt a deep loyalty to his adoptive country.
She considers her adoptive family as close as any biological relatives.

Synset Examples

my adopted state
an adoptive country

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adopted - legally taken as one's own (child, homeland, etc.)

Etymology

From Latin adoptivus (fit for adoption), from adoptare (to adopt).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01037978-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ əˈdɑptɪv / (AmE), / əˈdɒptɪv / (BrE)
(of parents or relationships) related or connected by the process of adoption rather than by birth.
related by adoption

Examples

Her adoptive mother attended every school event and cheered her on from the stands.
The twins have both biological and adoptive siblings in their family.
He searched for his birth parents but was always grateful to his adoptive family.

Synset Examples

adoptive parents

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: adopted - legally taken as one's own child

Etymology

From Medieval Latin adoptivus, from Latin adoptare (to choose for oneself, to adopt).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01409235-a
Synset POS: a
