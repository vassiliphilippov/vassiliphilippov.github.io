nowhere

1. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊˌwɛr / (AmE), / ˈnəʊˌwɛə / (BrE)
an imaginary or insignificant place that is considered to be remote or unimportant
an imaginary or unimportant place

Examples

After years living in nowhere, he moved to the city to pursue his dreams.
The town was so small, it felt like nowhere to most visitors.
Suddenly, a new competitor appeared out of nowhere and surprised everyone.

Synset Examples

he came out of nowhere

2. ADV

/ ˈnoʊˌwɛr / (AmE), / ˈnəʊˌwɛə / (BrE)
not in or at any place; in no place whatsoever.
not in or at any place

Examples

After hours of driving, we found ourselves nowhere near the hotel.
He looked for his keys, but they were nowhere to be found.
With no clear plan, their business was going nowhere fast.

Synset Examples

I am going nowhere

Related Terms

  • Antonym: everywhere - in or to all places
  • Antonym: somewhere - in or at an unspecified place
  • Antonym: anywhere - in or at any place

Etymology

From Old English 'nāhwǣr', from 'nā' (no, not) + 'hwǣr' (where).
