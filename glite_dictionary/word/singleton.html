singleton

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋɡəlˌtən / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡ(ə)ltən / (BrE)
a set containing exactly one element, especially in mathematics and computer science
a set with one member

Examples

In set theory, a singleton is a set that contains only one element.
The function returns a singleton containing the result.
Mathematicians often use a singleton to represent a unique value in a set.

Etymology

From single + -ton, patterned after similar set-theoretic terms.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋɡəlˌtən / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡltən / (BrE)
a single person, thing, or occurrence, especially one that is distinct as being single out of a usual pair or set
a single object or individual

Examples

After the drawing, Maria was left with only a singleton in the game.
In bridge, having a singleton in a suit can affect your strategy.
The crate contained just one singleton bottle, instead of the usual set.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pair - two similar or matching items considered together
  • Degree Higher: set - a collection of distinct objects

Etymology

From Latin 'singulus' (single, one by one), with the suffix '-ton'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋɡəltən / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡ(ə)ltən / (BrE)
A playing card that is the only card of its suit in a player's hand, especially in bridge.
the only card of a suit in a hand (cards)

Examples

She opened the bidding with a spade, holding a singleton in hearts.
In bridge, having a singleton often influences your strategy for discarding cards.
Her singleton club helped her partner read the distribution of the hand.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: void - the absence of any cards in a suit in a hand (cards)

Etymology

From single + -ton, modeled after words like 'simpleton' in early 19th-century card terminology.
