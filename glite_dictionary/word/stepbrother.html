stepbrother

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɛpˌbrʌðɚ / (AmE), / ˈstɛpˌbrʌðə / (BrE)
a male child of one's step-parent by a previous marriage, who is not a biological sibling
male child of a step-parent; not a biological brother

Examples

After his father remarried, Tom gained a stepbrother who was the same age as him.
Although my stepbrother and I are not related by blood, we grew up in the same house.
She invited both her biological brother and her stepbrother to the family reunion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stepsister - a female child of one's step-parent, not a biological sibling
  • Degree Higher: half-brother - a brother who shares one biological parent
  • Degree Higher: brother - a male sibling sharing both biological parents

Etymology

Formed from 'step-' (from Old English 'stēop-' indicating a relationship by marriage) + 'brother'.
