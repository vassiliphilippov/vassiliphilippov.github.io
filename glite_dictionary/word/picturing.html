picturing

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪktʃɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɪktʃərɪŋ / (BrE)
The act or process of creating mental images or representations in the mind; visual imagery.
the act of creating mental images

Examples

Her picturing of the story's setting helped immerse the readers in the scene.
Effective meditation often relies on the picturing of calming images or landscapes.
The artist's process includes the picturing of concepts before committing them to canvas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imagination - the faculty of forming new ideas or images in the mind
  • Synonym: visualization - the process of forming a mental picture of something

Etymology

From the verb 'picture,' which comes from Latin 'pictura' (painting).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɪktʃərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɪktʃərɪŋ / (BrE)
a visual representation, especially by photography or painting
visual representation by art or photography

Examples

The exhibition features the picturing of landscapes from across the country.
Early picturing of scientific phenomena helped people understand complex concepts.
Her picturing of urban life captured the vibrancy of the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depiction - the action or result of depicting something, especially in art
  • Synonym: representation - the description or portrayal of someone or something in a particular way
  • Antonym: abstraction - visual art that does not attempt to represent reality directly

Etymology

From the verb 'picture' + -ing, denoting the act or result of picturing.
