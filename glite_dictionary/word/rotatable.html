rotatable

1. ADJ

/ ˈroʊt̬əˌbleɪ̯ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊtətəbl / (BrE)
able to be rotated or turned around a central point or axis.
capable of being rotated

Examples

The new monitor has a rotatable screen, making it easier to share content with others.
Many security cameras are rotatable for wider surveillance coverage.
The rotatable display allowed the artist to paint from different angles.

Synset Examples

the theater had a rotatable stage

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fixed - not able to be moved or rotated
  • Synonym: rotary - turning around a central point; relating to rotation

Etymology

From rotate + -able, formed by adding the suffix -able ('able to be') to the verb 'rotate'.
