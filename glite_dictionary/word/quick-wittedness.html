quick-wittedness

1. NOUN

/ kwɪkˈwɪtɪdnəs / (AmE), / kwɪkˈwɪtɪdnəs / (BrE)
the ability to think and respond quickly and intelligently
ability to respond quickly and intelligently

Examples

Her quick-wittedness impressed the interviewers during the challenging job interview.
He relied on his quick-wittedness to get out of awkward situations with amusing remarks.
In debates, quick-wittedness is often just as important as thorough preparation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wit - keen intelligence or mental sharpness
  • Antonym: slowness - lack of speed in understanding or response

Etymology

From 'quick-witted' (quick + witted), formed from Old English 'cwic' (alive, alert) and 'wit' (understanding).
