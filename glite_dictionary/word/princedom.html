princedom

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪns.dəm / (AmE), / ˈprɪns.dəm / (BrE)
the position, authority, or territory held by a prince; the rank or domain of a prince
rank or domain of a prince

Examples

The small princedom maintained its independence for centuries despite its powerful neighbors.
He inherited the princedom at a young age when his father passed away.
Throughout history, each princedom had its own unique customs and government.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: principality - the territory ruled by a prince

Etymology

Formed from 'prince' + '-dom', modeled after kingdom.

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɪns.dəm / (AmE), / ˈprɪns.dəm / (BrE)
A territory or state ruled by a prince.
territory ruled by a prince

Examples

The small European princedom maintained its independence for centuries.
During the 18th century, the region was divided into several princedoms.
He inherited the princedom after the unexpected death of his father.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: principality - a territory ruled by a prince

Etymology

From prince + -dom, Old French 'prince' and -dom denoting domain or jurisdiction.
