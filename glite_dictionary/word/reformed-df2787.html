reformed

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈfɔrmd / (AmE), / rɪˈfɔːmd / (BrE)
having changed and abandoned a former, undesirable way of life or behavior, especially for the better
having given up a bad or undesirable practice

Examples

After years in prison, he became a reformed man dedicated to helping others.
She is a reformed gambler who now speaks about the dangers of addiction.
The program helps reformed offenders reintegrate into society.

Synset Examples

a reformed drunkard

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rehabilitated - restored to normal life or health, especially after imprisonment or addiction

Etymology

From reform + -ed, meaning 'changed for the better'.
