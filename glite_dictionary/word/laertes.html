Laertes

1. PROPN

/ leɪˈɜrtiz / (AmE), / leɪˈɜːtiːz / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the father of Odysseus and a king of Ithaca.
father of Odysseus in Greek mythology

Examples

In Homer's epic, Laertes mourns the disappearance of his son Odysseus.
The old king Laertes was eventually reunited with Odysseus after many years.
Greek myths often depict Laertes as a symbol of patience and endurance.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Λαέρτης (Laértēs).
