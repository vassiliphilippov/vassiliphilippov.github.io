turmoil

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝː.mɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.mɔɪl / (BrE)
A state of great disturbance, confusion, or uncertainty, especially as the result of protest or unrest.
a state of disturbance or confusion

Examples

The country was thrown into turmoil after the unexpected resignation of the president.
His sudden departure left the department in a state of turmoil.
Widespread protests caused turmoil in the streets for several days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chaos - complete disorder and confusion
  • Antonym: tranquility - a state of peace and calm

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɜrˌmɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.mɔɪl / (BrE)
A state of great disturbance, confusion, or uncertainty, often involving violent disorder.
a state of disturbance or confusion

Examples

Political turmoil led to widespread protests in the capital.
The company faced financial turmoil after losing several major clients.
She remained calm despite the turmoil around her.

Synset Examples

the convulsions of the stock market

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commotion - a state of confused and noisy disturbance
  • Antonym: calm - the absence of disturbance or agitation

Etymology

Of uncertain origin, possibly from Middle French *tourmouil* (disorder, uproar).

3. NOUN

/ tɜrˌmɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.mɔɪl / (BrE)
a state of great disturbance, confusion, or agitation, often involving disorder or unrest
state of violent disturbance or confusion

Examples

The country was thrown into turmoil after the election results were announced.
After the scandal broke, the company was in a state of turmoil for months.
His sudden resignation caused considerable turmoil among the staff.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: upheaval - a violent or sudden change or disruption
  • Antonym: calm - a state of peace and tranquility

Etymology

Probably from Middle French 'tourmoil' (disorder, commotion).
