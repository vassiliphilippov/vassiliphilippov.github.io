Aton

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑː.tɒn / (AmE), / ˈɑː.tɒn / (BrE)
the sun god worshipped as the sole deity of the monotheistic religion established by Pharaoh Akhenaten in ancient Egypt
ancient Egyptian sun god

Examples

Pharaoh Akhenaten declared that Aton was the only god and built a new capital city to honor him.
Scholars debate the influence of Aton worship on later monotheistic religions.
Temples dedicated to Aton were constructed with open roofs to allow sunlight to reach the altar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ra - principal sun god in ancient Egyptian religion

Etymology

From Egyptian 'jtn' (disk [of the sun]), adopted in historical and religious contexts.
