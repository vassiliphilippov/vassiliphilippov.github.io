Db

1. NOUN

/ diː biː / (AmE), / diː biː / (BrE)
the chemical symbol for dubnium, a synthetic transuranic element with atomic number 105
chemical element; symbol for dubnium

Examples

The element Db was first synthesized in 1967 in Dubna, Russia.
Db is a highly radioactive element found in Group 5 of the periodic table.
Scientists use only minute amounts of Db in experimental research due to its instability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dubnium - a synthetic transuranic chemical element with the symbol Db and atomic number 105

Etymology

From Latin 'Dubna', the Russian town near which the element was first produced.
