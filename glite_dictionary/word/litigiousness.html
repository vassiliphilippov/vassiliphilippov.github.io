litigiousness

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈtɪdʒəsnəs / (AmE), / lɪˈtɪdʒəsnəs / (BrE)
a tendency to engage in lawsuits or legal disputes frequently and often unnecessarily
tendency to engage in lawsuits

Examples

The company's litigiousness has earned it a reputation for constantly suing competitors.
Her litigiousness discouraged neighbors from even minor disagreements, fearing legal action.
Many critics blame the rise in healthcare costs on the litigiousness of modern society.

Synset Examples

charges of litigiousness and widespread perjury

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quarrelsomeness - a tendency to argue or fight frequently
  • Antonym: peacefulness - a disposition to avoid conflict or litigation

Etymology

From Latin litigiosus (fond of lawsuits), from litigium (dispute, lawsuit).
