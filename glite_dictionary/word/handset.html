handset

1. NOUN

/ ˈhændˌsɛt / (AmE), / ˈhændˌsɛt / (BrE)
the part of a telephone that you hold in your hand and speak into and listen from, with both the mouthpiece and earpiece mounted on a single handle
telephone part with mouthpiece and earpiece

Examples

She picked up the handset and quickly dialed her friend’s number.
He wiped the handset with a cloth after every use in the office.
The handset was heavy and old-fashioned compared to today's mobile phones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: receiver - the part of a telephone that you listen and speak into
