to asperse

1. VERB

/ əˈspɝs / (AmE), / əˈspɜːs / (BrE)
to make false or malicious statements about someone; to attack the reputation of another person.
attack someone's reputation with falsehoods

Examples

It is unfair to asperse someone's character without any evidence.
Political opponents often asperse each other during heated campaigns.
She resented those who would asperse her integrity for personal gain.

Synset Examples

The journalists have defamed me!
The article in the paper sullied my reputation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to defame - to damage the good reputation of someone by making false statements
  • Synonym: slander - to make false and damaging statements about someone
  • Antonym: praise - to express warm approval or admiration of someone

Etymology

From Latin 'aspersus', past participle of 'aspergere' meaning 'to sprinkle', later used metaphorically for casting slander.
