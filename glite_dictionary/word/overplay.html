to overplay

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈpleɪ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈpleɪ / (BrE)
(of an actor) to exaggerate or overdo one's performance in a way that lacks subtlety or realism.
exaggerate acting or performance

Examples

The actor tended to overplay his emotions in dramatic scenes.
Some critics felt she overplayed the character's anger, making it seem unnatural.
Directors often advise new performers not to overplay their roles on stage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overact - to act a part with exaggerated emotion or expression
  • Antonym: to underplay - to act or present with restraint or subtlety

Etymology

From 'over-' (excessively) + 'play' (perform a role or part).
