blazonry

1. NOUN

/ ˈbleɪ.zən.ɹi / (AmE), / ˈbleɪ.zən.ri / (BrE)
the official symbols or heraldic devices of a family, state, or organization, especially coats of arms and their display
official heraldic symbols (e.g. coats of arms)

Examples

The ancient castle was adorned with the blazonry of its former noble occupants.
He studied the blazonry on the shield, trying to identify the knight's lineage.
Medieval manuscripts often depict elaborate blazonry to record noble families' histories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heraldry - the study or art of armorial bearings
  • Synonym: arms - heraldic symbols representing a family or organization

Etymology

From French *blason* (coat of arms) + -ry (forming nouns).
