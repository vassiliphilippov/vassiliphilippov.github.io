whang

1. VERB

/ wæŋ / (AmE), / wæŋ / (BrE)
to attack or strike forcefully, especially in a vigorous or verbal manner
attack or strike forcefully

Examples

The senator began to whang away at the proposed legislation with sharp criticism.
He whanged the door shut and stormed out of the room.
The newspaper editorial whanged at the government's decision for days.

Synset Examples

whang away at the school reform plan

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bash - to hit something very hard
  • Synonym: to pummel - to strike repeatedly with the fists
  • Antonym: to defend - to protect from attack; to resist

Etymology

Origin unknown; possibly imitative of a loud striking sound.

2. VERB

/ wæŋ / (AmE), / wæŋ / (BrE)
to hit or propel something with great force, usually with a loud sound
to hit or propel forcefully
informal

Examples

He whanged the ball over the fence with one powerful swing.
She whanged the lid onto the pot, startling everyone in the kitchen.
During the storm, branches whanged against the window all night.

Synset Examples

whang the ball

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slam - to strike with great force or loud noise
  • Synonym: smack - to strike something with a loud, sharp noise
  • Degree Lower: tap - to hit lightly or gently
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01405666-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ wæŋ / (AmE), / wæŋ / (BrE)
a vigorous blow; the act of hitting something forcefully
a forceful hit or blow

Examples

He gave the table a whang that made the dishes rattle.
With a loud whang, she slammed the door shut.
Every whang of the hammer echoed through the workshop.

Synset Examples

he gave the table a whack

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whack - a sharp or resounding blow
  • Synonym: smack - a sharp slap or blow
  • Degree Lower: tap - a light touch or hit

Etymology

Probably imitative in origin, echoic of the sound made by a blow.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ wæŋ / (AmE), / wæŋ / (BrE)
to strike or beat with great force
strike or beat with force

Examples

He whanged the old gate with a stick, making a loud metallic noise.
Lightning whanged down from the sky, shaking the house.
The drummer whangs on his kit with wild abandon during the solo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strike - to hit forcibly and deliberately
  • Synonym: hit - bring one object into contact with another quickly and forcefully

Etymology

Possibly imitative of the sound of a heavy blow; related to Scandinavian words such as Norwegian "vang" (draft, blow).
