shiny

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃaɪni / (AmE), / ˈʃaɪni / (BrE)
having a bright surface that reflects light; glossy or lustrous
having a surface that reflects light

Examples

The car looked brand new with its shiny red paint.
She brushed her hair until it was shiny and smooth.
I love wearing shiny shoes to special occasions.

Synset Examples

glistening bodies of swimmers
the horse's glossy coat
lustrous auburn hair
saw the moon like a shiny dime on a deep blue velvet carpet
shining white enamel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glossy - having a smooth, shiny surface
  • Antonym: dull - not shiny; lacking brightness or sheen

Etymology

From shine + -y.

2. ADJ

/ ˈʃaɪni / (AmE), / ˈʃaɪni / (BrE)
smooth and bright as a result of reflecting light, often because of being polished or new.
smooth and reflecting light, glossy

Examples

She wore shiny black boots that caught everyone's attention.
The car looked almost new after it was washed and became shiny again.
His shiny hair gleamed under the bright lights.

Synset Examples

bright silver candlesticks
a burnished brass knocker
she brushed her hair until it fell in lustrous auburn waves
rows of shining glasses
shiny black patents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glossy - having a surface that is smooth and shiny
  • Antonym: dull - not bright, shiny, or reflecting light

Etymology

From shine + -y, formed in English.

3. ADJ

/ ˈʃaɪni / (AmE), / ˈʃaɪni / (BrE)
having a bright or glossy surface that reflects light; covered with a shiny coating
bright or glossy in appearance

Examples

The new car looked especially shiny in the sunlight after a thorough wash.
She wore shiny patent leather shoes to the party.
The shiny surface of the table made the whole room seem brighter.

Synset Examples

glazed fabrics
glazed doughnuts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glossy - having a smooth, shiny, and lustrous surface
  • Antonym: dull - lacking brightness or sheen; not shiny

Etymology

From shine + -y, originally Middle English 'shyni.'
