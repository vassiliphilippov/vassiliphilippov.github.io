Thylogale

1. NOUN

/ θaɪˈloʊɡəli / (AmE), / θaɪˈlɒɡəli / (BrE)
A genus of small to medium-sized marsupials, known as pademelons, native to Australia, New Guinea, and nearby islands.
genus of marsupials (pademelons)

Examples

The Thylogale species are notable for their compact size and secretive behavior.
Researchers observed several Thylogale individuals feeding at dusk in the Tasmanian forest.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitat of the endangered Thylogale in New Guinea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pademelon - A small, forest-dwelling marsupial of the genus Thylogale

Etymology

From Greek 'thylakos' (pouch) and 'gale' (weasel).
