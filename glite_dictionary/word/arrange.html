to arrange

1. VERB

/ əˈreɪndʒ / (AmE), / əˈreɪndʒ / (BrE)
to set or prepare printed matter into a specific format for presentation or publication
set printed text into a format

Examples

Can you arrange this report so it fits on two pages?
The editor will arrange the chapters for the book layout.
She needs to arrange the text for printing by tomorrow.

Synset Examples

Format this letter so it can be printed out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: format - to arrange or organize written text or data into a particular layout
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01748949-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ əˈreɪndʒ / (AmE), / əˈreɪndʒ / (BrE)
to organize or make plans for something to happen, such as a meeting or event
organize or make plans for

Examples

Let me arrange a taxi for you to the airport.
They managed to arrange the conference in just two weeks.
Could you arrange dinner reservations for tomorrow night?

Synset Examples

Can you arrange a meeting with the President?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to organize - to arrange or coordinate systematically
  • Antonym: cancel - to decide that something planned will not happen

Etymology

From Old French *arangier* (to put in order), from *a-* (to) + *rangier* (to arrange).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01023638-v
Synset POS: v
