averting

1. NOUN

/ əˈvɝːtɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈvɜːtɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of preventing something from happening or avoiding an undesirable outcome
the act of preventing something

Examples

His quick thinking led to the averting of a serious accident.
The committee focused its efforts on the averting of potential financial crises.
Effective communication can aid in the averting of misunderstandings.

Synset Examples

averting danger was his responsibility

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prevention - the act of stopping something from happening

Etymology

From the verb 'avert', from Latin 'aversus', past participle of 'avertere' (to turn away).

2. NOUN

/ əˈvɜrtɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈvɜːtɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of turning away, especially one's eyes, face, or attention from something
the act of turning away

Examples

The averting of her eyes made it clear she did not want to answer the question.
His averting of attention whenever the subject came up was noticeable.
There was a moment of averting their gazes when the embarrassing truth was revealed.

Synset Examples

averting her gaze meant that she was angry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aversion - a strong dislike or disinclination
  • Antonym: facing - the act of looking toward or confronting something
