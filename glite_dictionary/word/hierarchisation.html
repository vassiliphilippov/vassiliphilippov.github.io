hierarchisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪ(ə)rɑrkəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌhaɪ(ə)ˈrɑːkɪˌzeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
The act or process of arranging things or people into a hierarchy or ordered levels of importance.
act of creating a hierarchy

Examples

The company underwent a complete hierarchisation of its management structure last year.
Academic disciplines often debate the hierarchisation of knowledge and its implications.
A new software tool was introduced to assist in the hierarchisation of complex datasets.

Synset Examples

We consider a new hierarchization algorithm for sparse grids of high dimension and low level.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hierarchy - a system in which people or things are ranked one above the other

Etymology

From French 'hiérarchisation', based on 'hierarchy' and the suffix '-isation' (equivalent to English '-ization').
