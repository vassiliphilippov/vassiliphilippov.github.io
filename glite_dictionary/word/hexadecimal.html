hexadecimal

1. ADJ

/ ˌhɛksəˈdɛsɪməl / (AmE), / ˌhɛksəˈdɛsɪməl / (BrE)
relating to or using a number system with sixteen as its base, especially used in mathematics and computing
relating to base-16 numeral system

Examples

Many color codes in web design are written in hexadecimal notation.
The programmer converted the binary value into its hexadecimal equivalent.
Memory addresses in computers are often displayed in hexadecimal form.

Etymology

From Greek 'hex', meaning 'six', and Latin 'decimus', meaning 'tenth' (implying sixteen in total).
