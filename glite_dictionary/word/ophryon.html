ophryon

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːfriˌɑːn / (AmE), / ˈɒfriːɒn / (BrE)
the anatomical point in the midline of the forehead just above the eye sockets, used as a reference in craniometry.
craniometric point above the eye sockets
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully measured from the ophryon to the nasion to assess facial symmetry.
In craniometric studies, the ophryon is an important landmark for anthropological analysis.
A small scar was noted just above the patient's ophryon after the accident.

Etymology

From Greek ophryon (brow, eyebrow).
