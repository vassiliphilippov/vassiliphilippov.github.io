to abate

1. VERB

/ əˈbeɪt / (AmE), / əˈbeɪt / (BrE)
to become less strong or decrease in amount, intensity, or force
become less intense or strong

Examples

As the fever began to abate, the patient started to feel better.
The strong winds did not abate until late in the evening.
Public anger over the scandal failed to abate even after the politician apologized.

Synset Examples

The storm abated
The rain let up after a few hours

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to diminish - to become or make less
  • Antonym: to intensify - to become or make more intense

Etymology

From Old French abatre (to beat down), from Latin abattere, from ab- (away) + battuere (to beat).

2. VERB

/ əˈbeɪt / (AmE), / əˈbeɪt / (BrE)
to make something become less strong, intense, or severe; to diminish in intensity or amount
make or become less intense

Examples

After several hours, the storm finally began to abate and the skies started to clear.
The city took measures to abate air pollution during summer months.
She waited for his anger to abate before speaking to him again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to diminish - become or make less; lessen in intensity or amount
  • Antonym: to intensify - become or make greater in strength, intensity, or amount

Etymology

From Old French abatre (to beat down), from Latin abattere, a- (from) + battere (to beat).
