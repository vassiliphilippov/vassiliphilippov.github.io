momentarily

1. ADV

/ ˌmoʊ.mənˈter.ə.li / (AmE), / ˌməʊ.mənˈteə.rɪ.li / (BrE)
for a very short time; for an instant or moment
for a very short time

Examples

The power went out momentarily during the storm.
She closed her eyes momentarily, trying to collect her thoughts.
The pain subsided momentarily, but soon returned.

Synset Examples

we paused momentarily before proceeding
a cardinal perched momently on the dogwood branch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: briefly - for a short time
  • Antonym: permanently - lasting forever or for a long time

Etymology

From Latin 'momentarius' (momentary) + English adverbial suffix '-ly'.

2. ADV

/ ˌmoʊ.mənˈter.ə.li / (AmE), / ˌməʊ.mənˈteə.rɪ.li / (BrE)
Very soon; in a very short time.
very soon; in a short time

Examples

The train will arrive momentarily, so please stand back from the edge.
She said she would return momentarily with the documents.
The pilot announced that we would be landing momentarily.

Synset Examples

she will be with you momently

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shortly - in a short time; soon
  • Degree Higher: immediately - at once; without delay

Etymology

From Latin 'momentarius' (momentary), from 'momentum.'
