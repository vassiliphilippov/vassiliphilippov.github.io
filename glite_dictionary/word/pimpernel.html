pimpernel

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪmpərˌnɛl / (AmE), / ˈpɪmpənɛl / (BrE)
a low-growing plant of the genus Anagallis, typically with small, brightly colored flowers, found in grassland and open habitats
a plant of the genus Anagallis

Examples

The common pimpernel is known for its delicate, star-shaped flowers.
Botanists often study the water pimpernel in wetland areas.
Red pimpernel can be found growing wild in English meadows.

Etymology

From Middle English 'pympernella', from Old French, from Medieval Latin 'piperinella'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɪmpərˌnɛl / (AmE), / ˈpɪmpəˌnɛl / (BrE)
A small European herbaceous plant of the genus Anagallis, with purple-tinged flowers, whose leaves are sometimes used in salads.
a European herb with purple-tinged flowers

Examples

The wild pimpernel brightened the edges of the meadow with its delicate blooms.
Gardeners sometimes grow pimpernel both for its beauty and for its edible leaves.
She added fresh pimpernel to the salad for a hint of color and flavor.

Etymology

From Old French 'pimprenelle', ultimately from Latin 'piperinella' (name for the plant).
