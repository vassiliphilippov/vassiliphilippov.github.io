fixity

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪksɪti / (AmE), / ˈfɪksɪti / (BrE)
The state or quality of being fixed, stable, or firmly attached in place.
quality of being fixed or stable

Examples

The fixity of the statue ensured it remained upright despite the strong winds.
His gaze had an intense fixity that made others uncomfortable.
Architects considered the fixity of the building's foundation critical to its stability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stability - state of being stable or steady
  • Antonym: mobility - the ability to move easily

Etymology

From Latin 'fixus' (fixed) + English suffix '-ity'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɪksəti / (AmE), / ˈfɪksɪti / (BrE)
the state or quality of being fixed, stable, or unchanging
state of being fixed or unchanging

Examples

The fixity of her beliefs made her difficult to persuade.
Scientists once believed in the fixity of celestial orbits.
The fixity of traditions can sometimes hinder social progress.

Synset Examples

Darwin challenged the fixity of species

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stability - the state of being steady or not changing
  • Synonym: immutability - the quality of being unchangeable
  • Antonym: changeability - the quality of being able to change

Etymology

From Latin 'fixus' (fixed) via French 'fixité'.
