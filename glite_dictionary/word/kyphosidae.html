Kyphosidae

1. NOUN

/ kaɪˈfɑːsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / kɪˈfɒsɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of marine fish known as sea chubs, found mostly in tropical and subtropical waters
family of marine fish (sea chubs)

Examples

Members of the Kyphosidae family are commonly known as sea chubs.
Many Kyphosidae inhabit coral reefs in warm coastal waters.
Marine biologists study the diet and behavior of the Kyphosidae to understand their role in marine ecosystems.

Etymology

From Greek kyphos (hump, curvature) + -idae (zoological family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ kaɪˈfɒsɪdiː / (AmE), / kɪˈfɒsɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of mainly marine fish known as sea chubs, found in warm and temperate seas worldwide
family of marine fish (sea chubs)

Examples

The Kyphosidae are commonly found in tropical and temperate coastal waters.
Researchers studied the feeding habits of several species in the Kyphosidae family.
Many aquariums feature Kyphosidae due to their hardy nature and social behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea chub - a common name for fish of the family Kyphosidae

Etymology

From New Latin *Kyphosidae*, based on the genus *Kyphosus* and the suffix *-idae* (denoting animal families).
