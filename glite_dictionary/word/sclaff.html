sclaff

1. VERB

/ sklæf / (AmE), / sklæf / (BrE)
(in golf or similar sports) to strike the ground with the club or stick in making a shot, causing a poor or mishit stroke.
hit ground in golf shot

Examples

He managed to sclaff his shot on the fairway, sending the ball only a few yards.
She groaned as she sclaffed the approach, and the ball barely left the ground.
Golfers often lose distance when they accidentally sclaff the ball during their swing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duff - to mishit a golf ball by striking the ground

Etymology

Scottish origin, imitative of the sound made when the club strikes the ground.

2. VERB

/ sklæf / (AmE), / sklɑːf / (BrE)
(in golf) to hit the ground before striking the ball, causing a badly played shot.
to hit ground before ball (golf)
british

Examples

He accidentally sclaffed his approach shot, and the ball barely made it onto the green.
If you sclaff the ball, you'll lose both distance and accuracy.
She has worked hard on her swing to avoid sclaffing during important rounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duff - to mishit a golf ball, typically by hitting the ground first

Etymology

Scottish origin, possibly imitative of the sound produced.

3. NOUN

/ sklæf / (AmE), / sklɑːf / (BrE)
In golf, a poor stroke where the club head strikes the ground before making contact with the ball.
golf stroke where club hits ground first

Examples

He ruined his approach shot with a sclaff just short of the green.
A sclaff like that can add several strokes to your round.
She blamed the wet grass for her unexpected sclaff on the last hole.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duff - a poor golf stroke, especially one where the ball is badly hit
