lopsided

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɑpˌsaɪdɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɒpˌsaɪdɪd / (BrE)
having one side lower or smaller than the other; turned or twisted toward one side; not evenly balanced.
uneven or tilted to one side

Examples

His smile was distinctly lopsided after the dental procedure.
They noticed the picture was lopsided and straightened it on the wall.
The jury delivered a lopsided verdict in favor of the prosecution.

Synset Examples

a … youth with a gorgeous red necktie all awry
his wig was, as the British say, skew-whiff

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uneven - not level or equal; not uniform
  • Antonym: symmetrical - having the same shape, size, and position on both sides of a dividing line

Etymology

From 'lop' (hang loosely) + 'sided', first recorded in the mid-18th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˈlɑpˌsaɪdɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɒpˌsaɪdɪd / (BrE)
having one side lower, smaller, or heavier than the other; uneven or unbalanced in shape or appearance
uneven or unbalanced in shape

Examples

The picture on the wall looks lopsided and needs to be straightened.
She wore a lopsided smile after hearing the unexpected compliment.
The team suffered a lopsided defeat, losing 8-1 to their rivals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: symmetrical - having both sides exactly the same
  • Antonym: balanced - equal in weight or importance on both sides
  • Synonym: uneven - not level or equal; irregular
