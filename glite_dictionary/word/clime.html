clime

1. NOUN

/ klaɪm / (AmE), / klaɪm / (BrE)
a region considered with reference to its typical weather or climate, especially used in literary contexts
region defined by its climate

Examples

Many poets sought inspiration in warmer climes during the winter months.
Migrating birds instinctively seek out suitable climes for breeding.
She longed to travel to distant climes with gentler breezes and sunny skies.

Synset Examples

the dank climate of southern Wales
plants from a cold clime travel best in winter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: climate - the average weather conditions in an area over a long period

Etymology

From Latin 'clima' (region, zone), from Greek 'klima' (slope, region).

2. NOUN

/ klaɪm / (AmE), / klaɪm / (BrE)
a region defined by its climate; the typical weather conditions of a particular area
region defined by its climate
archaic

Examples

The lush vineyards thrive in this fertile clime.
Many poets write about distant and exotic climes.
Birds migrate to warmer climes during the winter months.

Synset Examples

The clime here is perfect for growing grapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: climate - the weather conditions typical of a region over a long period

Etymology

From Latin 'clima' (region, slope of the earth), from Greek 'klīma' (slope, latitude).
