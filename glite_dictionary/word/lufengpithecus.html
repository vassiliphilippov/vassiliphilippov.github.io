Lufengpithecus

1. NOUN

/ luːˌfɛŋ.pəˈθiː.kəs / (AmE), / luːˌfɛŋ.pɪˈθiː.kəs / (BrE)
A genus of extinct hominids from the Miocene and Pliocene epochs, known from fossils found in East Asia, including three species.
genus of extinct Asian hominids

Examples

Fossils of Lufengpithecus have been discovered in the Yunnan Province of China.
Many paleontologists study Lufengpithecus to better understand early ape evolution.
The skull of Lufengpithecus displays features intermediate between apes and early humans.

Etymology

From Lufeng, a region in China, combined with Greek pithēkos (ape).

2. NOUN

/ luˈfʌŋ.pɪˌθi.kəs / (AmE), / luːˈfɛŋ.pɪˌθiː.kəs / (BrE)
a genus of extinct great apes in the family Hominidae, known from fossil remains found in China and including species such as Lufengpithecus lufengensis.
a genus of extinct hominid apes

Examples

Fossils of Lufengpithecus have provided important insights into hominid evolution in East Asia.
The genus Lufengpithecus includes several species discovered in Yunnan Province, China.
Scientists debate where Lufengpithecus fits in the family tree of great apes.

Etymology

From Lufeng, a county in Yunnan, China, where fossils were discovered, and Greek 'pithēkos' (ape).
