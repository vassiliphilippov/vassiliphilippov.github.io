spoiling

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɔɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspɔɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of damaging or ruining something or making it less enjoyable or valuable
the act of damaging or ruining

Examples

The spoiling of the food made it unsafe to eat.
Her deliberate spoiling of my painting was upsetting.
Repeated spoiling of the surprise ruined the party for everyone.

Synset Examples

her spoiling my dress was deliberate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damage - physical harm that impairs the value or function of something
  • Antonym: preservation - the act of keeping something safe from harm or damage

2. NOUN

/ ˈspɔɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspɔɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of becoming spoiled, damaged, or ruined, especially by decay or improper care
the process of becoming spoiled

Examples

The spoiling of fresh fruit can occur quickly if it is not kept cool.
Prolonged exposure to warm temperatures accelerates the spoiling of milk.
Proper packaging helps prevent the spoiling of perishable goods during shipping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decay - the process of rotting or decomposing
  • Antonym: preservation - the act of keeping something from being spoiled
