retch

1. NOUN

/ rɛtʃ / (AmE), / rɛtʃ / (BrE)
an involuntary spasm or attempt to vomit, often without bringing anything up
involuntary attempt to vomit

Examples

A loud retch echoed from the bathroom, alarming everyone at the party.
He suppressed a retch as the unpleasant smell filled the room.
Each morning, she awoke with a painful retch that left her weak.

Synset Examples

a bad case of the heaves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heave - an act of retching or vomiting

Etymology

Probably imitative in origin; related to the verb 'retch'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00227818-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ rɛtʃ / (AmE), / rɛtʃ / (BrE)
to make a strong, involuntary effort to vomit, often without bringing anything up
attempt to vomit without success

Examples

The smell made him retch repeatedly, though nothing came up.
She covered her mouth and began to retch over the sink.
He tried not to retch when cleaning up the mess.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vomit - to eject the contents of the stomach through the mouth
  • Synonym: gag - to experience a reflex that almost leads to vomiting

Etymology

Probably imitative in origin; resembles the sound made when retching.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00077211-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ rɛtʃ / (AmE), / rɛtʃ / (BrE)
to make the sounds and movements of vomiting, sometimes bringing up stomach contents
make vomiting movements or sounds

Examples

The unpleasant smell made him retch in the hallway.
She began to retch after seeing the graphic scene in the movie.
Trying to clean the old fridge, I couldn't help but retch from the stench.

Synset Examples

After drinking too much, the students vomited
He purged continuously
The patient regurgitated the food we gave him last night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vomit - to eject the contents of the stomach through the mouth
  • Synonym: to regurgitate - to bring food up from the stomach into the mouth

Etymology

Perhaps of imitative origin, influenced by Middle English reche (to reach) in the sense of straining.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00076153-v
Synset POS: v
