Gastrophryne carolinensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡæstrəˈfraɪniː ˌkærəˌlɪnˈɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˌɡastrəʊˈfraɪniː ˌkarəˌlɪnˈɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A small species of narrow-mouthed toad found in the southeastern United States, known for its secretive habits.
species of narrow-mouthed toad

Examples

The Gastrophryne carolinensis can often be heard but rarely seen due to its secretive lifestyle.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Gastrophryne carolinensis in coastal wetlands.
Unlike other toads, the Gastrophryne carolinensis has a distinctive, narrow mouth adapted for eating ants.

Etymology

From Greek 'gastro-' (stomach) + 'phryne' (toad), and Neo-Latin for 'of Carolina'.
