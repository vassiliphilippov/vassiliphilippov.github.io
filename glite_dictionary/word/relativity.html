relativity

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛləˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / ˌrɛləˈtɪvɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being relative and meaningful only in relation to something else.
quality of being relative

Examples

The concept of relativity can apply to beauty, as it often depends on cultural standards.
Values often have a certain relativity, differing from one society to another.
She argued that moral relativity makes it difficult to judge actions universally.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: absoluteness - the quality of being absolute, not relative
  • Degree Higher: relativity theory - the scientific theory by Einstein about space, time, and gravity

Etymology

From Latin 'relativus' (having reference or relation) + '-ity'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌrɛləˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / ˌrɛləˈtɪvɪti / (BrE)
(physics) The scientific theory that space and time are not absolute but are relative to the motion of the observer, as formulated notably by Albert Einstein.
theory that space and time are relative

Examples

Einstein's theory of relativity revolutionized our understanding of space and time.
The concept of relativity fundamentally changed the laws of physics.
She studied relativity in her advanced physics course.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relativism - the doctrine that knowledge, truth, and morality exist in relation to culture or context

Etymology

From late Latin relativitas, from Latin relativus (having reference or relation).
