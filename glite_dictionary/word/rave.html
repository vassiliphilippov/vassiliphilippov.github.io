rave

1. VERB

/ reɪv / (AmE), / reɪv / (BrE)
to praise something or someone enthusiastically and unreservedly
praise enthusiastically

Examples

The critics raved about the new film, calling it a masterpiece.
She raved about the restaurant's delicious desserts to everyone she met.
After reading the novel, he couldn't stop raving about its beautiful prose.

Synset Examples

She raved about that new restaurant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enthuse - to show or express enthusiasm, especially verbally
  • Antonym: to criticize - to express disapproval or point out faults

Etymology

From Middle English raven (to speak wildly), of uncertain origin; this sense developed later.

2. NOUN

/ reɪv / (AmE), / reɪv / (BrE)
an all-night dance party featuring electronically synthesized music, especially of the electronic dance music genre
all-night electronic music dance party

Examples

We're going to a rave in the warehouse district this Saturday night.
She met her best friends at a rave during the summer music festival.
The city cracked down on illegal rave parties held in abandoned buildings.

Synset Examples

raves are very popular in Berlin

Related Terms

  • Synonym: party - a social gathering of invited guests
  • Degree Higher: festival - a day or period of celebration, often with music and entertainment

Etymology

Originally from English verb 'rave' meaning 'to talk wildly', this noun sense emerged in late 20th-century British club culture.

Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ reɪv / (AmE), / reɪv / (BrE)
an extremely enthusiastic review or approval, especially in the context of art, performances, or products
enthusiastic review or approval

Examples

The movie received a rave from every major critic.
His latest play opened to raves across the country.
She wrote a rave about the new restaurant in the magazine.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pan - a negative or critical review, especially in the arts
  • Synonym: praise - expression of approval, admiration or commendation

4. VERB

/ reɪv / (AmE), / reɪv / (BrE)
to participate in an all-night event featuring electronic dance music and dancing, often with light shows and DJs
attend an all-night dance party

Examples

We used to rave every weekend when we were in college.
Hundreds of people raved under the stars until sunrise at the festival.
Are you going to rave with us at the underground club tonight?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: party - to attend or participate in a social gathering

Etymology

Originally from Middle English 'raven' (to behave wildly), modern sense derived from the noun 'rave' meaning a dance party.

5. VERB

/ reɪv / (AmE), / reɪv / (BrE)
to speak in a wild, loud, or enthusiastic way, often showing strong emotions or excitement
talk loudly and excitedly

Examples

He began to rave about the injustice of the decision, attracting a crowd in the process.
The actor raved so passionately during the interview that it left everyone speechless.
She was raving about the new restaurant all night, barely letting anyone else talk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rant - to speak or shout in a loud, wild, or angry way
  • Antonym: mumble - to speak quietly and not clearly, often in a low voice

Etymology

From Middle English 'raven' (to wander, be delirious), probably from Old French 'raver' (to rave), altered from 'resver' (to dream, be delirious).
