to outrun

1. VERB

/ aʊtˈrʌn / (AmE), / aʊtˈrʌn / (BrE)
to run faster than someone or something; to move more quickly on foot than another.
run faster than someone or something

Examples

Despite his best efforts, he could not outrun the speedy greyhound.
She trained hard so she could outrun her competitors in the marathon.
The villagers had to outrun the rising floodwaters to reach safety.

Synset Examples

in this race, I managed to outrun everybody else

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to outpace - to move faster than someone or something
  • Synonym: to overtake - to catch up with and pass while traveling in the same direction
  • Antonym: lag behind - to fall behind in movement or progress

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'out-' (meaning 'beyond', 'better than') + 'run'.
