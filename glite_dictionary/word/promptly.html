promptly

1. ADV

/ ˈprɑmptli / (AmE), / ˈprɒmptli / (BrE)
Without delay; at once or punctually.
without delay; punctually

Examples

Please reply promptly to ensure your reservation is held.
The train departed promptly at 8 a.m. as scheduled.
She finished her assignment promptly after receiving the instructions.

Synset Examples

he did his homework promptly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immediately - without any delay
  • Antonym: slowly - in a slow manner, taking time

Etymology

From the adjective 'prompt,' itself from Latin 'promptus' (at hand, ready).

2. ADV

/ ˈprɑmptli / (AmE), / ˈprɒmptli / (BrE)
immediately; without delay.
immediately; without delay

Examples

The manager promptly addressed the customer’s complaint as soon as it was received.
He realized his mistake and promptly apologized to everyone involved.
When the alarm rang, the fire department responded promptly and contained the situation.

Synset Examples

he promptly forgot the address

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immediately - without any delay
  • Synonym: instantly - at once; immediately
  • Antonym: slowly - at a slow speed; not quickly

Etymology

From Middle English *promptly*, from Old French *prompt*, from Latin *promptus* (ready).

3. ADV

/ ˈprɑmpt.li / (AmE), / ˈprɒmpt.li / (BrE)
without delay; immediately or at the scheduled time
immediately; without delay

Examples

The meeting will start promptly at 10 a.m., so please be on time.
She responded promptly to my email with all the information I needed.
If you pay promptly, you may qualify for a discount.

Synset Examples

the rescue squad arrived promptly
come here, quick!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immediately - at once; without delay
  • Degree Lower: quickly - at a fast speed; rapidly
  • Antonym: tardily - with delay; late

Etymology

From 'prompt' + '-ly', where 'prompt' comes from Latin 'promptus' (brought forth, ready).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00106290-r
Synset POS: r
