sensationally

1. ADV

/ sɛnˈseɪʃənəli / (AmE), / sɛnˈseɪʃənəli / (BrE)
in a manner that causes great public interest or excitement; exceptionally or remarkably.
in an exciting or remarkable way

Examples

The movie was sensationally popular, breaking box office records for months.
She was sensationally dressed, attracting everyone's attention at the gala.
The team sensationally defeated their rivals in the final seconds of the match.

Synset Examples

in the summer of 1958 the pianist had a sensationally triumphant return

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remarkably - in a way that is worthy of attention; strikingly
  • Antonym: ordinarily - in a usual or normal way

Etymology

From 'sensational' + adverbial suffix '-ly'.
