casuarina

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæʒuˈæriːnə / (AmE), / ˌkazjʊˈɑːrɪnə / (BrE)
A tree or shrub of the genus Casuarina, characterized by jointed stems and whorled, scalelike leaves; some species yield heavy hardwood.
tree or shrub of the genus Casuarina

Examples

The beach was lined with tall casuarina trees swaying gently in the breeze.
Some species of casuarina are valued for their strong and durable wood.
After the storm, many fallen casuarinas blocked the coastal road.

Etymology

From Latinized form of Malay 'kasuari' (cassowary), referring to the resemblance of the leaves to cassowary feathers.
