Casuarina

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæʒuˈɑːrɪnə / (AmE), / ˌkazjʊəˈriːnə / (BrE)
a genus of trees and shrubs, commonly known as she-oaks, widely naturalized in southern United States and the West Indies, belonging to the family Casuarinaceae.
genus of trees and shrubs (she-oaks)

Examples

The Casuarina trees lined the coastal road, their feathery foliage swaying in the breeze.
Many birds nest in Casuarina groves found throughout the region.
Scientists are studying how Casuarina adapts to different soil types in the southern United States.

Etymology

From modern Latin Casuarina, named for the resemblance of branchlets to the feathers of the cassowary (genus Casuarius).
