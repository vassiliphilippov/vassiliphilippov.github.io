Bismarck

1. PROPN

/ ˈbɪzmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈbɪzmɑːk / (BrE)
the capital city of North Dakota, located in the south-central part of the state on the Missouri River
capital city of North Dakota, USA

Examples

The government offices in Bismarck serve all of North Dakota.
She traveled to Bismarck to attend a statewide conference.
Bismarck sits on the banks of the Missouri River.

Etymology

Named after Otto von Bismarck, the first Chancellor of the German Empire.

2. PROPN

/ ˈbɪzˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈbɪzˌmɑːk / (BrE)
(1815–1898) A German statesman who unified Germany and served as its first Chancellor.
German statesman; unifier of Germany (1815–1898)

Examples

Historians credit Bismarck with masterminding the unification of Germany in the 19th century.
Bismarck's policies reshaped European politics for decades after his death.
The military strategies of Bismarck are still studied by political scientists today.

Etymology

From Otto von Bismarck, the family name of the statesman.
