obsequy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑb.sɪ.kwi / (AmE), / ˈɒb.sɪ.kwi / (BrE)
a funeral rite or ceremony honoring a deceased person
funeral ceremony or rite

Examples

The obsequy was attended by family, friends, and colleagues from around the world.
Traditional obsequies often include prayers and the singing of hymns.
The city held an elaborate obsequy in honor of the late leader.

Synset Examples

hundreds of people attended his funeral

Related Terms

  • Synonym: funeral - a ceremony for burying or cremating a dead person
  • Synonym: rite - a ceremonial act or procedure

Etymology

From Latin 'obsequiae' (funeral rites), plural of 'obsequium' (compliance, service).
