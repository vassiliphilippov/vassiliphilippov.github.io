bursar

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɝːsər / (AmE), / ˈbɜːsə / (BrE)
an official in a college or university who is responsible for managing financial affairs, especially payments and accounts
college or university financial officer

Examples

The bursar explained the new tuition payment plan to the students.
Each year, the bursar reviews all financial records and accounts for the university.
If you have a problem with your bill, you should contact the bursar's office immediately.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treasurer - a person in charge of the money in an organization

Etymology

From Middle English *bursar*, from Medieval Latin *bursarius* (purse-bearer), from Latin *bursa* (purse).
