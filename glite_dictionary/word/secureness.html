secureness

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈkjʊrnəs / (AmE), / sɪˈkjʊənɪs / (BrE)
the state of being secure, especially freedom from fear or danger
state of being secure; safety

Examples

He felt a deep sense of secureness in his new home.
The secureness of the job allowed her to make long-term plans.
Children thrive in an environment where secureness and stability are present.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: security - the state of being free from danger or threat
  • Antonym: insecurity - uncertainty or anxiety about oneself; lack of confidence

Etymology

From secure + -ness, with 'secure' from Latin securus (free from care).

2. NOUN

/ sɪˈkjʊrnəs / (AmE), / sɪˈkjʊənəs / (BrE)
the quality of being firmly fixed in place; the state of being securely attached or fastened
the state of being firmly fixed

Examples

The secureness of the bolts ensured the fence would withstand strong winds.
After the earthquake, engineers checked the secureness of the building's foundations.
She tested the secureness of the baby's car seat before driving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: security - the state of being free from danger or threat
  • Antonym: instability - the quality or state of being likely to change or fail; lack of stability

Etymology

From secure + -ness, from Latin securus (safe, free from care).
