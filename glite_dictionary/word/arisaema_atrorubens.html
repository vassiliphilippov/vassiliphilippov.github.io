Arisaema atrorubens

1. NOUN

/ ˌærɪˈsiːmə ˌætrəˈruːbɛnz / (AmE), / ˌærɪˈsiːmə ˌætrəˈruːbɛnz / (BrE)
A North American spring-flowering woodland plant with sheathing leaves and a club-shaped spadix, topped by a green and purple spathe, and producing scarlet berries.
North American spring-flowering woodland herb

Examples

In early May, clusters of Arisaema atrorubens brighten the forest floor with their striking flowers.
Gardeners appreciate Arisaema atrorubens for its unusual shape and vibrant scarlet berries.
During our hike, we spotted several Arisaema atrorubens nestled among the undergrowth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jack-in-the-pulpit - Common name for Arisaema atrorubens, a woodland plant

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining genus Arisaema and species epithet atrorubens (Latin for 'dark red').
