anticipation

1. NOUN

/ ænˌtɪsɪˈpeɪʃən / (AmE), / ænˌtɪsɪˈpeɪʃən / (BrE)
A feeling of expectation and excitement about something that is going to happen, especially with confidence that it will occur.
expectation or excitement for something forthcoming

Examples

She waited in anticipation for the announcement of the winner.
There was a sense of anticipation in the air before the concert began.
His eyes lit up with anticipation as he opened the gift.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expectation - a strong belief that something will happen
  • Antonym: surprise - an unexpected or astonishing event

Etymology

From Latin anticipatio (a taking beforehand), from anticipare (to take before).
