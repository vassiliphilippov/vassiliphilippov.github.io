hemothorax

1. NOUN

/ himoʊˈθɔːræks / (AmE), / hɛməʊˈθɔːræks / (BrE)
the accumulation of blood in the pleural cavity, the space between the lungs and the chest wall
blood accumulation in the pleural cavity
medical

Examples

The patient developed a hemothorax after sustaining a chest injury in the accident.
Doctors drained the hemothorax to prevent respiratory complications.
A severe hemothorax can be life-threatening if not treated promptly.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pneumothorax - accumulation of air in the pleural cavity

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) + thorax (chest).
