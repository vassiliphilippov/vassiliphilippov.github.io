Myrmecobius

1. NOUN

/ mɜːrˌmiːˈkoʊbiəs / (AmE), / mɜːˌmiːˈkəʊbiəs / (BrE)
A small Australian marsupial, also known as the numbat or banded anteater, which feeds mainly on termites.
Australian marsupial, the numbat

Examples

The Myrmecobius is recognizable by its distinctive stripes and long tongue adapted for termite hunting.
The Myrmecobius, or numbat, is native to southwestern Australia and is an endangered species.
Researchers observed a Myrmecobius searching for termites among fallen logs in the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: numbat - Another name for the banded anteater, Myrmecobius fasciatus

Etymology

From Greek 'myrmex' (ant) and 'bios' (life), referring to its ant-eating habits.

2. NOUN

/ mɜrˌmiːˈkoʊbi.əs / (AmE), / mɜːˌmiːˈkəʊbi.əs / (BrE)
A genus of small marsupials native to Australia, including the banded anteater (numbat).
genus of marsupials (banded anteater)

Examples

The Myrmecobius is notable for its distinctive stripes and long, sticky tongue used to catch ants and termites.
Researchers have studied the habitat requirements of Myrmecobius to improve conservation efforts.
The only living species in the genus Myrmecobius is commonly known as the numbat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: numbat - a small Australian marsupial, also called the banded anteater

Etymology

From Greek 'myrmex' (ant) + 'bios' (life), referring to its ant-eating habits.
