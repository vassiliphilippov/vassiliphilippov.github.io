Callinectes

1. NOUN

/ ˌkælɪˈnɛkˌtiːz / (AmE), / ˌkælɪˈnɛkˌtiːz / (BrE)
A genus of New World blue crabs, including several important edible species found in coastal waters of the Americas.
genus of New World blue crabs

Examples

The Atlantic blue crab, a member of the genus Callinectes, is highly prized by seafood lovers.
Researchers studied the behavior of various Callinectes species in estuarine environments.
Several species of Callinectes are important for both commercial and recreational fisheries.

Etymology

From Greek 'kallos' (beauty) + 'nectes' (swimmer).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkælɪˈnɛktiːz / (AmE), / ˌkælɪˈnɛktiːz / (BrE)
A genus of blue crabs native to the Americas, including the commonly known blue crab.
genus of New World blue crabs

Examples

The genus Callinectes includes the widely known Atlantic blue crab.
Researchers are studying the population dynamics of Callinectes in coastal waters.
Several species in the genus Callinectes are important for commercial fisheries.

Etymology

From Greek 'kallos' (beauty) and 'nectes' (swimmer), meaning 'beautiful swimmer'.
