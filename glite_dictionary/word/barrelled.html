barrelled

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɛr.əld / (AmE), / ˈbær.əld / (BrE)
(of an arrow or similar object) tapered toward both ends, usually to reduce weight and improve aerodynamics.
tapered toward both ends (esp. of an arrow)

Examples

The barrelled shafts of these arrows allow for greater stability in flight.
He prefers using barrelled arrows for target archery due to their improved performance.
The artisan painstakingly shaped each barrelled arrow by hand.

Etymology

From 'barrel', referencing the shape's resemblance to a barrel's gentle curve; -ed forms an adjective.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈbærəld / (AmE), / ˈbærəld / (BrE)
(of a liquid or other substance) put in or stored in a barrel, especially as part of a process such as aging.
stored in a barrel

Examples

The brewery offers several barrelled ales that have been aged to perfection.
Most traditional Scotch whiskies are barrelled for years before bottling.
The wine's flavor developed while it was barrelled in oak casks.

Synset Examples

barreled beer

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bottled - (of a liquid) put into bottles for storage or sale

Etymology

From the noun 'barrel' (Middle English from Old French 'baril'), with the suffix '-ed' forming the adjective.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a
