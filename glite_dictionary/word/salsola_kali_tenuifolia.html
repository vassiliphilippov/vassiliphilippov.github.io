Salsola kali tenuifolia

1. NOUN

/ sælˈsoʊlə ˈkælaɪ ˌtɛnjuəˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / sælˈsəʊlə ˈkæli ˌtɛnjʊɪˈfəʊliə / (BrE)
A prickly, bushy Eurasian plant of the amaranth family, commonly known as Russian thistle or tumbleweed, often considered a noxious weed in the United States.
prickly Eurasian weed (Russian thistle)

Examples

Dry regions in the West are often covered with Salsola kali tenuifolia, better known as Russian thistle.
The spread of Salsola kali tenuifolia has created significant problems for local agriculture.
Researchers studied how Salsola kali tenuifolia adapts to different soil types and climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Russian thistle - Common name for Salsola kali, a noxious weed and tumbleweed
  • Synonym: tumbleweed - Any of several plants that break away from their roots and are tumbled by the wind

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Salsola' from Latin 'salsus' (salty), and 'kali' from Arabic 'al-qali', referring to alkaline plants.
