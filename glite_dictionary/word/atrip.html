atrip

1. ADJ

/ əˈtrɪp / (AmE), / əˈtrɪp / (BrE)
(of an anchor) just clear of the bottom; no longer holding the vessel to the seabed.
anchor just clear of the bottom (nautical)

Examples

The captain announced that the anchor was atrip and they were ready to sail.
Checking the depth, the crew made sure the anchor was atrip before starting the engines.
With the anchor atrip, the ship drifted slowly forward.

Etymology

Probably from Middle English on trippe (on the move). Nautical usage from the 17th century.
