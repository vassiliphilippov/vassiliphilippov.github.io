denticle

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛn.tɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈdɛn.tɪ.kəl / (BrE)
a small, pointed ridge or projection, especially one on the exoskeleton of an arthropod
small pointed ridge or projection

Examples

The surface of the beetle's wing is covered in tiny denticles that provide extra grip.
Some species of spiders have distinct denticles along their leg joints.
Under the microscope, the exoskeleton reveals hundreds of minute denticles forming a protective texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tooth - a hard, bony structure in the mouth used for biting and chewing
  • Synonym: spine - a sharp, pointed projection on an organism, such as a plant thorn or animal spine

Etymology

From Latin denticulus (small tooth), diminutive of dens (tooth).
