Ichthyosauridae

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪkθioʊˈsɔrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌɪkθi.əˌsɔːˈrɪ.diː / (BrE)
a family of extinct marine reptiles from the Jurassic and Cretaceous periods, widely distributed in both hemispheres
family of extinct marine reptiles (Jurassic/Cretaceous)

Examples

Fossils of the Ichthyosauridae have been found on every continent.
The Ichthyosauridae thrived during the Jurassic and Cretaceous periods before going extinct.
Researchers believe that climate change played a role in the extinction of the Ichthyosauridae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ichthyosaur - an extinct marine reptile resembling a fish, part of Ichthyosauridae

Etymology

From Greek 'ikhthys' (fish) + 'sauros' (lizard), with scientific Latin family suffix '-idae.'

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪkθiˌoʊsɔːˈrɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌɪkθiˌəʊsɔːˈrɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of extinct marine reptiles that lived during the Jurassic and Cretaceous periods, known for their fish-like bodies and wide distribution across both hemispheres
family of extinct Jurassic and Cretaceous marine reptiles

Examples

The fossils of Ichthyosauridae have been discovered on every continent, indicating a wide distribution.
Many members of Ichthyosauridae were well-adapted to life in open oceans during the Jurassic period.
Paleontologists often classify new ichthyosaur fossils within Ichthyosauridae based on distinctive skeletal features.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ichthyosaur - an extinct marine reptile of the Mesozoic era

Etymology

From Greek 'ichthys' (fish) + 'sauros' (lizard) + taxonomic suffix '-idae'.
