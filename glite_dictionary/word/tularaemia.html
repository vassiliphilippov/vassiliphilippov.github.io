tularaemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌtuːləˈriːmiə / (AmE), / ˌtjuːləˈriːmiə / (BrE)
A highly infectious bacterial disease mainly affecting rodents, especially rabbits and squirrels, that can be transmitted to humans by ticks, flies, or contact with infected animals.
infectious disease transmitted from rodents to humans
medical

Examples

Cases of tularaemia have been reported after people handled wild rabbits.
Public health officials warn of increased tularaemia risk during the summer months.
He contracted tularaemia after being bitten by an infected tick while hiking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rabbit fever - another name for tularaemia, especially in lay usage

Etymology

From New Latin, after Tulare County, California, where the disease was first identified.
