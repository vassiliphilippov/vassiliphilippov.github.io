NCDC

1. NOUN

/ ɛn si di si / (AmE), / ɛn siː diː siː / (BrE)
the part of the U.S. National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA) that maintains the world's largest active archive of weather data
U.S. weather data archive agency

Examples

Researchers often rely on data from the NCDC for climate studies.
The NCDC plays a crucial role in preserving historical weather records.
Many meteorologists access the NCDC archive for accurate temperature data.

Etymology

Acronym of National Climatic Data Center.
