Rosa banksia

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊzə ˈbæŋksiə / (AmE), / ˈrəʊzə ˈbaŋksɪə / (BrE)
A Chinese evergreen climbing rose with yellow or white single flowers, often grown as an ornamental plant.
a species of climbing rose with yellow or white flowers

Examples

The garden trellis was covered with blooming Rosa banksia in early spring.
She planted Rosa banksia beside the stone wall for its delicate yellow flowers.
Many botanists admire Rosa banksia for its resilience and attractive climbing habit.

Etymology

Named after Lady Banks, wife of English botanist Sir Joseph Banks.
