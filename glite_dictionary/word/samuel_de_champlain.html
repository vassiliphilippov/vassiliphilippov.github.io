Samuel de Champlain

1. PROPN

/ ˈsæmjuəl də ʃæmˈpleɪn / (AmE), / ˈsæmjʊəl də ʃæmˈpleɪn / (BrE)
(1567–1635) A French explorer and cartographer who founded Quebec and played a major role in the early exploration and settlement of Canada.
French explorer; founder of Quebec

Examples

Samuel de Champlain is often called the "Father of New France" due to his role in establishing Quebec.
The voyages of Samuel de Champlain greatly expanded European knowledge of northeastern North America.
Many Canadian landmarks are named after Samuel de Champlain to honor his exploration achievements.
