bunfight

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌnˌfaɪt / (AmE), / ˈbʌn.faɪt / (BrE)
(British) A grand or formal party, often celebrating an important occasion.
grand formal party (British)
british informal

Examples

We were invited to a bunfight at the embassy last Saturday.
The annual awards ceremony is always a lavish bunfight attended by celebrities.
After the formal speeches, the bunfight began, complete with champagne and canapés.

Etymology

British informal; originally used humorously to refer to school or club gatherings with refreshments, especially where buns were served.
