stabilisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌsteɪbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌstəbɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act or process of making something more stable, especially by preventing it from overturning or becoming unsteady.
the act of making more stable

Examples

The addition of a keel greatly improved the stabilisation of the sailing boat.
Modern aircraft rely on advanced technology for automatic stabilisation during turbulent flights.
Efforts toward stabilisation prevented the cargo ship from capsizing in rough waters.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: destabilisation - the act or process of making something less stable

Etymology

From French 'stabilisation', from Latin 'stabilis' (firm, stable).
