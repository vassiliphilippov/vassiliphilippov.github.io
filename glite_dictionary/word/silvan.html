silvan

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪl.vən / (AmE), / ˈsɪl.vən / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of woods or forest regions; wooded or covered in trees.
relating to woods or forests

Examples

The cottage was nestled in a silvan landscape, surrounded by towering oaks and whispering pines.
Many poets praise the peacefulness of a silvan glade in their verses.
The painting depicts a silvan scene, with sunlight filtering through dense foliage.

Synset Examples

a shady sylvan glade

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sylvan - relating to or characteristic of wooded regions

Etymology

From Latin *silva* (forest, wood), via French *sylvain*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪl.vən / (AmE), / ˈsɪl.vən / (BrE)
(in mythology or literature) a spirit or deity associated with the woods or forests.
woodland spirit or deity

Examples

The old legends tell of a silvan who protects the heart of the forest.
As dusk fell, she fancied she caught a glimpse of a silvan slipping among the ancient trees.
Poets often describe the mysterious dances of silvans on moonlit woodland nights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faun - a mythical creature, part human and part goat, associated with forests
  • Synonym: dryad - a tree nymph or tree spirit in Greek mythology

Etymology

From Latin 'silvanus', meaning 'of the woods' or the Roman god of forests.
