Grindelia squarrosa

1. NOUN

/ ɡrɪnˈdɛliə skwəˈrɒsə / (AmE), / ɡrɪnˈdiːliə skwəˈrɒsə / (BrE)
A perennial gumweed native to western and central North America, known for its yellow flower heads and sticky resin.
a North American perennial gumweed plant

Examples

Grindelia squarrosa grows abundantly in prairies and along roadsides throughout the Midwest.
Native Americans traditionally used Grindelia squarrosa for its medicinal properties.
Botanists recently discovered several new populations of Grindelia squarrosa in Colorado.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gumweed - any of various plants with sticky resinous heads, including Grindelia species

Etymology

From Latin; 'Grindelia' named after Italian botanist David Hieronymus Grindel, 'squarrosa' meaning having rough or spreading scales.
