married

1. NOUN

/ ˈmærid / (AmE), / ˈmærid / (BrE)
a person who is married, especially when referring to members of a couple considered together with others in the same situation
a married person (noun)

Examples

On the guest list were several marrieds and a handful of singles.
The resort offers special packages for young marrieds on their honeymoon.
In the past, clubs often held separate events for singles and marrieds.

Synset Examples

we invited several young marrieds

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bachelor - an unmarried man
  • Antonym: single - an unmarried person
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10315480-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈmærid / (AmE), / ˈmærid / (BrE)
Relating to the state of being married or connected to marriage.
relating to marriage

Examples

They enjoyed several years of married life before starting a family.
She found great comfort in their strong married partnership.
Their married relationship faced challenges but grew stronger over time.

Synset Examples

marital status
marital fidelity
married bliss

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unmarried - not married; single
  • Antonym: single - not married or in a romantic relationship

Etymology

From Middle English 'maried', past participle of 'marien' (to marry), from Old French 'marier', from Latin 'maritare'.

3. ADJ

/ ˈmærid / (AmE), / ˈmærid / (BrE)
having a husband or wife; joined in matrimony.
having a husband or wife

Examples

They have been happily married for twenty years.
Is he married or single?
The law gives certain rights to married couples.

Synset Examples

a married man
a married couple

Related Terms

  • Antonym: single - not married; without a spouse
  • Antonym: unmarried - not having a spouse; not married
  • Synonym: to wed - having entered into marriage

Etymology

Past participle of 'marry,' from Middle English 'marien,' from Old French 'marier,' from Latin 'maritare' (to wed, to marry).
