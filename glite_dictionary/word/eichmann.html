Eichmann

1. PROPN

/ ˈaɪkmən / (AmE), / ˈaɪkmən / (BrE)
(1906–1962) An Austrian Nazi official responsible for organizing and overseeing the transport of Jews to concentration camps during the Holocaust.
Nazi official in charge of Holocaust deportations

Examples

The trial of Eichmann in Jerusalem was a pivotal moment in Holocaust history.
Many books examine the psychological motives behind Eichmann's actions during World War II.
Discussions of responsibility during the Holocaust often mention Eichmann as a central figure.
