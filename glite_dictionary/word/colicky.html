colicky

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɑː.lɪ.ki / (AmE), / ˈkɒl.ɪ.ki / (BrE)
suffering from or pertaining to colic, especially marked by periods of severe abdominal pain due to intestinal gas or obstruction, often used in reference to infants.
suffering from colic or abdominal pain
medical

Examples

The colicky baby cried for hours each night despite being well-fed and changed.
She brought her colicky infant to the pediatrician for advice on soothing his discomfort.
After the meal, he felt colicky pains and had to lie down for a while.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colic - severe, often fluctuating pain in the abdomen

Etymology

From colic + -y (suffix forming adjectives).
