mobilization

1. NOUN

/ ˌmoʊbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌməʊbɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of organizing and preparing resources, people, or activities so they are ready for use or action, often in response to a need or crisis
organizing and preparing for action

Examples

The government ordered the mobilization of emergency services after the hurricane struck.
Successful mobilization of support from all departments ensured the project finished on time.
Rapid mobilization of medical personnel was vital during the outbreak.

Synset Examples

mobilization of the country's economic resources

Related Terms

  • Antonym: demobilization - the process of standing down or disbanding organized resources or troops

Etymology

From French 'mobilisation', from 'mobiliser' (to make mobile), based on 'mobile' (capable of movement).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmoʊbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌməʊbɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of assembling and preparing troops, resources, or people for war or emergency action.
the process of preparing for war or emergency

Examples

The government began the mobilization of reserve troops in response to the growing threat.
Rapid mobilization of medical teams saved many lives after the earthquake.
The country’s economic mobilization ensured supplies reached those in need during the crisis.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: demobilization - the process of disbanding or standing down organized forces

Etymology

From French mobilisation, from mobiliser (to make mobile) + -ation.
