Rattus

1. NOUN

/ ˈræ.təs / (AmE), / ˈræt.əs / (BrE)
a genus of rodents that includes the common house rats, characterized by incisors with a beveled edge
genus of common house rats

Examples

Several species in the genus Rattus have adapted well to urban environments.
The laboratory experimented with Rattus norvegicus as a model organism.
Rattus populations can spread disease if left uncontrolled in cities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rat - a medium-sized rodent, typically with a pointed snout and long tail
  • Degree Lower: mouse - a small rodent, typically smaller than a rat

Etymology

From Latin 'rattus' meaning 'rat', used as the genus name in scientific classification.
