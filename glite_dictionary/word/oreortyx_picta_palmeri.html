Oreortyx picta palmeri

1. NOUN

/ ɔːriːˈɔːrtɪks ˈpɪktə ˈpɑːlməri / (AmE), / ˌɔːrɪˈɒrtɪks ˈpɪktə ˈpɑːlməri / (BrE)
A subspecies of mountain quail found in California, known as Palmer's mountain quail, slightly larger than the California quail.
California bird; Palmer's mountain quail subspecies

Examples

The Oreortyx picta palmeri prefers dense mountain brush for habitat in California.
Birdwatchers documented a flock of Oreortyx picta palmeri during their survey of the Sierra Nevada.
Unlike the California quail, the Oreortyx picta palmeri has a more pronounced crest and larger body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quail - a small, short-tailed Old World game bird
  • Degree Lower: Oreortyx picta - mountain quail, the species to which Oreortyx picta palmeri belongs

Etymology

From scientific Latin, combining the genus name Oreortyx, species picta ('painted'), and subspecies palmeri (honoring T.S. Palmer, American ornithologist).
