Rus

1. PROPN

/ ruːs / (AmE), / ruːs / (BrE)
the medieval East Slavic state established in the 9th century, with its capital initially at Novgorod and later at Kiev.
medieval East Slavic state (9th–13th centuries)

Examples

Historians believe that Rus was shaped by a blending of Slavic and Scandinavian influences.
The rise and decline of Rus had a profound impact on Eastern European history.
Novgorod was one of the earliest centers of power in Rus.

Etymology

From Old East Slavic Русь (Rusʹ); possibly related to Old Norse 'roðr' (rowing) or the people called the 'Rus' (Scandinavian traders and settlers).
