participation

1. NOUN

/ pɑːrˌtɪsɪˈpeɪʃn̩ / (AmE), / pɑːˌtɪsɪˈpeɪʃən / (BrE)
the action of taking part in an activity or event with other people
the act of taking part in something

Examples

Her active participation in the discussion made the meeting more productive.
Employee participation in the training program is strongly encouraged.
The success of the festival relied on broad community participation.

Synset Examples

the teacher tried to increase his students' engagement in class activities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: involvement - the fact of being involved in something
  • Antonym: withdrawal - the act of ceasing to take part in something

Etymology

From Latin participatio, from participare (to share in, take part).

2. NOUN

/ pɑrˌtɪsɪˈpeɪʃən / (AmE), / pɑːˌtɪsɪˈpeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of taking part in or becoming involved with others in an activity or event
the act of taking part in something

Examples

Participation in the annual meeting is required for all members.
The teacher encouraged greater participation in classroom discussions.
Their participation helped the event run smoothly and successfully.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: involvement - the state of participating in something
  • Antonym: exclusion - the state of not being allowed or included

Etymology

From Latin participatio (sharing in, partaking), from participare (to share).
