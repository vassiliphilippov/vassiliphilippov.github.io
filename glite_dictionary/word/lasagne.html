lasagne

1. NOUN

/ ləˈzɑːnjə / (AmE), / ləˈzanjə / (BrE)
A baked Italian dish consisting of layers of pasta, sauce, cheese, and often meat or vegetables.
Italian baked pasta dish

Examples

She made a delicious lasagne for the family dinner last night.
The restaurant’s vegetarian lasagne is their most popular dish.
After baking, let the lasagne rest before serving so it keeps its shape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lasagna - alternate spelling, especially in American English

Etymology

From Italian 'lasagne', plural of 'lasagna', borrowed from Latin 'lasanum' (cooking pot).

2. NOUN

/ ləˈzɑːnjə / (AmE), / ləˈzanjə / (BrE)
Very wide, flat strips of pasta, often used in layered baked dishes.
wide, flat pasta sheets

Examples

She layered the sauce, cheese, and lasagne noodles to make a classic Italian dish.
Fresh lasagne sheets cook more quickly than dried ones.
Many recipes call for egg-based lasagne for a richer flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pasta - dough made of wheat and water, shaped in various forms

Etymology

From Italian 'lasagne', plural of 'lasagna', from Latin 'lasanum' (cooking pot).
