sulkiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌlkinəs / (AmE), / ˈsʌlkinɪs / (BrE)
a mood or state of being sulky, resentful, or silently sullen
the state of being sulky or resentful

Examples

Her sulkiness was obvious when she refused to join the group for dinner.
A cloud of sulkiness settled over him after the argument with his friend.
Despite his sulkiness, his sister managed to make him smile by the end of the day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sulky - moody and silent due to resentment; showing sulkiness
  • Antonym: cheerfulness - state of being happy and contented; antonym of sulkiness

Etymology

From 'sulky' + '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsʌlkinəs / (AmE), / ˈsʌlkinɪs / (BrE)
a state or quality of being sullen, moody, and resentful; the tendency to sulk
sullen or moody disposition

Examples

Her sulkiness was obvious after the argument with her friend.
The child’s persistent sulkiness made it difficult to enjoy the family outing.
He tried to hide his sulkiness behind a forced smile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sullenness - a gloomy and resentful mood or attitude
  • Antonym: cheerfulness - the quality of being noticeably happy and optimistic

Etymology

From 'sulk' (to be sullen or silently resentful) + '-iness' (forming nouns indicating a state or condition).

3. NOUN

/ ˈsʌlkinəs / (AmE), / ˈsʌlkɪnɪs / (BrE)
a mood or state of being sullen, withdrawn, or silently resentful
state of being sulky or withdrawn

Examples

There was a palpable sulkiness in his tone after the argument.
Her persistent sulkiness made it difficult for the team to move forward.
He retreated to his room in sulkiness after being scolded.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sulky - moody and silently resentful
  • Antonym: cheerfulness - state of being noticeably happy and optimistic

Etymology

From 'sulky' (mid 18th cent.) + '-ness', forming nouns denoting a state or condition.
