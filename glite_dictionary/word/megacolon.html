megacolon

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛɡəˌkoʊlɑn / (AmE), / ˈmɛɡəkəʊlɒn / (BrE)
an abnormal enlargement of the colon, which may be present from birth or develop later in life
abnormal enlargement of the colon
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the child with megacolon after noticing severe abdominal swelling.
Untreated megacolon can lead to serious complications if not managed promptly.
Certain congenital disorders, such as Hirschsprung's disease, commonly result in megacolon.

Etymology

From Greek 'mega' (large) and 'colon' (the large intestine).
