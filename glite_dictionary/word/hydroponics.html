hydroponics

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪdrəˈpɑnɪks / (AmE), / ˌhaɪdrəˈpɒnɪks / (BrE)
a method of growing plants without soil, using nutrient-rich water instead
soil-less plant growing method

Examples

Many commercial greenhouses use hydroponics to maximize crop yield with minimal land use.
With hydroponics, you can grow lettuce indoors year-round, regardless of the weather outside.
He explained how hydroponics could help feed a growing population in urban areas.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: aquaponics - a system combining hydroponics with fish farming

Etymology

From Greek hydor (water) + ponos (labor, work).
