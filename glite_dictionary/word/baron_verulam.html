Baron Verulam

1. PROPN

/ bɛˈrɑn vɜːˈluːləm / (AmE), / ˈbar.ən vəˈlʊː.ləm / (BrE)
Title used for Francis Bacon (1561–1626), English statesman and philosopher known for advocating inductive reasoning and as a precursor of British empiricism.
title for Francis Bacon, philosopher and statesman

Examples

As Baron Verulam, Francis Bacon made significant contributions to philosophy and science.
Many scholars credit Baron Verulam with shaping the foundation of modern empirical methods.
The writings of Baron Verulam are still studied by philosophy students today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Francis Bacon - English philosopher and statesman, often called Baron Verulam

Etymology

Baron from Old French, 'Verulam' from the Roman name for St Albans, England (Verulamium).
