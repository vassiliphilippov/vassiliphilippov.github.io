arteria digitalis

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈtɪəriə dɪˈdʒɪtəlɪs / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪəriə dɪˈdʒɪtælɪs / (BrE)
Any of the arteries in the hand or foot that supply blood to the fingers or toes.
artery supplying fingers or toes
medical

Examples

Injury to the arteria digitalis may compromise blood flow to the affected finger.
During surgery, the arteria digitalis was carefully preserved to ensure proper healing.
The doctor explained that a blockage in the arteria digitalis can cause numbness in the toes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: digital artery - artery supplying the fingers or toes

Etymology

From Latin: 'arteria' (artery) + 'digitalis' (of the finger or toe, from 'digitus', finger or toe).
