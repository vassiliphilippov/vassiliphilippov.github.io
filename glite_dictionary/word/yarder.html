yarder

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɑrdər / (AmE), / ˈjɑːdə / (BrE)
a powered machine using one or more winches for hauling logs from the forest to a collection point in logging operations
machine for hauling logs in logging

Examples

The logging crew used a yarder to move the felled trees up the steep hillside.
Modern yarders are often equipped with computerized controls for precise operation.
After cutting, the logs were attached to cables and hauled away by the yarder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winch - a machine for lifting or pulling heavy objects

Etymology

From 'yard' (to gather or haul) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈjɑrdər / (AmE), / ˈjɑːdə / (BrE)
(chiefly in combination) something that measures a specified number of yards in length, height, or distance, especially a shot, object, or event related to that measure.
something of a specified yard length (in combinations)

Examples

The golfer hit a massive 300-yarder down the fairway.
He completed a 40-yarder pass to score a touchdown.
That field goal was a tough 50-yarder in windy conditions.

Synset Examples

the golfer hit a 300-yarder to the green

Related Terms

  • Synonym: footer - something of a specified number of feet in length or height (in combinations)

Etymology

Formed from 'yard' (unit of length) plus the agent suffix '-er'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13672353-n
Synset POS: n
