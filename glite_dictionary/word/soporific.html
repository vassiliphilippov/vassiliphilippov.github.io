soporific

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɑːpəˈrɪfɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɒpəˈrɪfɪk / (BrE)
A drug or other substance that induces sleep.
sleep-inducing drug or substance
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a soporific to help the patient sleep through the night.
Because of his insomnia, he often turned to herbal soporifics before bedtime.
Some over-the-counter medications contain mild soporific effects that can cause drowsiness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sedative - a drug or substance that calms or relaxes
  • Antonym: stimulant - a substance that raises levels of physiological or nervous activity

Etymology

From Latin sopor (sleep) + -ific (making, causing).

Examples

His voice was so dull and soporific that I struggled to stay awake.

3. ADJ

/ sɑːpəˈrɪfɪk / (AmE), / sɒpəˈrɪfɪk / (BrE)
Tending to induce sleep; making someone feel sleepy.
sleep-inducing; causing sleep

Examples

The professor's soporific lecture caused several students to nod off before noon.
This medication has a soporific effect, so avoid driving after taking it.
The gentle sound of rain at night is highly soporific for many people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sedative - a substance that calms or soothes; often causing sleep
  • Antonym: stimulant - a substance that raises levels of nervous activity in the body

Etymology

From Latin sopor (deep sleep) + -ificus (making), meaning 'sleep-inducing'.
