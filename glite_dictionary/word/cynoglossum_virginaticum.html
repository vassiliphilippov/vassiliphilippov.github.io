Cynoglossum virginaticum

1. NOUN

/ saɪnəˌɡlɒsəm vɜrdʒɪˈnætɪkəm / (AmE), / sɪnəˌɡlɒsəm vɜːdʒɪˈnætɪkəm / (BrE)
A perennial shrub native to North America, with coarse, tongue-shaped leaves and pale-blue to purple flowers.
North American perennial plant species

Examples

The forest floor was dotted with patches of Cynoglossum virginaticum in bloom.
Gardeners often admire Cynoglossum virginaticum for its attractive purple flowers.
Botanists study Cynoglossum virginaticum to understand its ecological role in North American woodlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hound's-tongue - Common name for plants in the genus Cynoglossum

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus name 'Cynoglossum' (from Greek kyon, 'dog' + glossa, 'tongue', referring to the leaves), and the species epithet referencing Virginia (in North America).
