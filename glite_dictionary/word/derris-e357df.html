Derris

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛrɪs / (AmE), / ˈdɛrɪs / (BrE)
a genus of tropical shrubs and woody vines native to the Old World, some species of which are used as sources of rotenone
genus of tropical shrubs and vines

Examples

The roots of Derris are known for their insecticidal properties.
Several species in the Derris genus are important in traditional agriculture.
Scientists discovered a new compound in Derris plants.

Etymology

From modern Latin, named after John Der, an 18th-century Dutch botanist.
