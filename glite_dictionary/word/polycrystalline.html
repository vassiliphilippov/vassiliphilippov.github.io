polycrystalline

1. ADJ

/ ˌpɑliˈkrɪstəˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˈkrɪstəlʌɪn / (BrE)
Consisting of or formed from many small crystals or grains, rather than a single continuous crystal.
composed of multiple crystals

Examples

The laboratory produced a polycrystalline sample of silicon for testing solar cell efficiency.
Structural properties of polycrystalline metals differ from those of single crystals.
Researchers observed grain boundaries in the polycrystalline film under an electron microscope.

Synset Examples

polycrystalline metals

Related Terms

  • Antonym: crystalline - having the structure and form of a crystal
  • Antonym: amorphous - lacking a clearly defined shape or crystalline structure

Etymology

From 'poly-' (many) and 'crystalline' (having the structure of a crystal).
