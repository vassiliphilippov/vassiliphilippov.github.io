Bryonia alba

1. NOUN

/ braɪˈoʊni.ə ˈæl.bə / (AmE), / brɪˈəʊnɪə ˈal.bə / (BrE)
A climbing plant with white flowers, thick roots, and small black berries, native to Europe and western Asia.
climbing plant with white flowers and black berries

Examples

Bryonia alba is considered invasive in some regions of North America.
The roots of Bryonia alba have been used in traditional remedies, though they can be toxic.
Gardeners sometimes try to eradicate Bryonia alba due to its aggressive growth.

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Bryonia' is the genus name and 'alba' means 'white.'
