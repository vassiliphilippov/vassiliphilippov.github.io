railbird

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪlˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈreɪlˌbɜːd / (BrE)
A fan who watches horse races, especially from near the outer rail of the racetrack.
racing fan near the outer rail

Examples

Many railbirds arrive hours before the start to claim a prime spot along the fence.
After the big race, the railbirds exchanged tips about the horses and jockeys.
He considered himself a true railbird, never missing a Saturday at the track.
