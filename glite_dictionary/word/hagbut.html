hagbut

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæɡ.bʌt / (AmE), / ˈhæɡ.bʌt / (BrE)
an early type of firearm with a long barrel, used especially in the 15th and 16th centuries; also known as an arquebus
obsolete long-barreled firearm
archaic

Examples

The museum displays a rare hagbut from the sixteenth century.
In battle, soldiers armed with a hagbut could fire from a distance.
The hagbut required a match to ignite the gunpowder.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arquebus - an early portable long gun with a matchlock

Etymology

From Middle French 'harquebuse' (arquebus), later altered in English.
