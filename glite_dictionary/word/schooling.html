schooling

1. NOUN

/ ˈskuːlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskuːlɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of being taught, especially at a school
the process of being taught at school

Examples

Many children in remote areas have limited access to schooling.
His schooling was interrupted by the war.
She received her early schooling in France.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: education - the process of receiving or giving systematic instruction

Etymology

From the verb 'school' + -ing, originally from Old English 'scol', from Latin 'schola'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈskuːlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskuːlɪŋ / (BrE)
the training of an animal, especially a horse, in order to develop particular skills or behaviors, such as for dressage
training of an animal, esp. a horse

Examples

The horse underwent months of schooling before it was ready for competition.
Proper schooling is essential for a show jumper to perform at its best.
After years of schooling, the stallion excelled in dressage events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: training - teaching animals or people particular skills or behaviors
Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ ˈskuːlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskuːlɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of receiving formal education, especially at a school
formal education at a school

Examples

His parents made sure he received the best schooling available in the city.
After completing her schooling, she decided to travel before starting university.
In many countries, schooling is compulsory up to a certain age.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: education - the process of receiving or giving systematic instruction

Etymology

From 'school' + '-ing', formed in English.
