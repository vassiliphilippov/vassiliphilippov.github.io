hedged

1. ADJ

/ hɛdʒd / (AmE), / hɛdʒd / (BrE)
(of a statement or response) carefully worded to avoid making a definite or clear commitment; evasive or non-committal.
carefully evasive or non-committal (of statements)

Examples

Her hedged answer left everyone unsure about her true opinion.
The CEO gave a hedged statement to the press, avoiding any direct commitment.
Political speeches are often hedged to minimize the risk of criticism.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unequivocal - leaving no doubt; unambiguous

Etymology

Past participle of 'hedge', meaning to limit or qualify, from the original sense of surrounding something with a hedge.
