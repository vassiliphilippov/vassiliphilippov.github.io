afterward

1. ADV

/ ˈæftərwərd / (AmE), / ˈɑːftəwəd / (BrE)
At a later time; subsequently.
at a later time

Examples

We went for coffee afterward to discuss the meeting.
She felt a great sense of relief afterward.
There was a celebration at the park afterward.

Synset Examples

he apologized subsequently
he's going to the store but he'll be back here later
it didn't happen until afterward
two hours after that

Related Terms

  • Synonym: later - at a time in the future or after the expected time
  • Antonym: beforehand - in advance; ahead of time

Etymology

From Middle English, from after + -ward.
