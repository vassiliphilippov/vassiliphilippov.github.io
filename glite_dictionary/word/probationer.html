probationer

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈbeɪʃənər / (AmE), / prəˈbeɪʃənə / (BrE)
a person who has been released from custody on probation or parole and is under supervision instead of serving time in prison
person on probation or parole

Examples

The probationer must report weekly to their probation officer.
Several probationers attended a rehabilitation program as part of their release conditions.
If a probationer violates the terms, they may be sent back to prison.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parolee - a person who is released from prison on parole

Etymology

From 'probation' (trial period or supervised freedom), from Latin 'probatio' (testing), with the suffix '-er' denoting a person.

2. NOUN

/ proʊˈbeɪʃənər / (AmE), / prəˈbeɪʃənə / (BrE)
a nurse in training who is undergoing a trial or evaluation period before being fully qualified
nurse in training on trial period

Examples

After six months as a probationer, she was accepted into the full nursing staff.
Each probationer is assigned a mentor to guide them during their initial placement.
As a probationer, he worked long hours and learned from experienced nurses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trainee nurse - a person training to be a nurse

Etymology

From Latin 'probationem' (examination, trial) via Old French and English 'probation'.
