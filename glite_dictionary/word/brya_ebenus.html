Brya ebenus

1. NOUN

/ ˈbraɪ.ə ˈɛb.ən.əs / (AmE), / ˈbraɪ.ə ˈiː.bə.nəs / (BrE)
a tropical West Indian tree that yields a fine grade of green ebony used in cabinetmaking and inlays
West Indian tree yielding green ebony

Examples

Luthiers highly prize the dense wood of Brya ebenus for its use in musical instruments.
The furniture in the colonial house was crafted from Brya ebenus, giving it a unique greenish hue.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect wild populations of Brya ebenus in Jamaica.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Brya' (New Latin, used in botanical nomenclature) and the species name 'ebenus' (Latin, relating to ebony).
