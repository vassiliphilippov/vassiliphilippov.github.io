quiescency

1. NOUN

/ kwiˈɛsənsi / (AmE), / kwɪˈɛsənsi / (BrE)
A state of quietness or inactivity, especially one that is temporary or intermediate.
state of quiet inaction

Examples

The lake returned to a state of quiescency after the storm passed.
For years, the project remained in quiescency before it was finally resumed.
After an initial burst of activity, the colony entered a period of quiescency.

Synset Examples

the volcano erupted after centuries of dormancy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dormancy - a period of inactivity, especially in plants or volcanoes
  • Antonym: activity - the state of being active, doing things

Etymology

From Latin 'quiescentia', from 'quiescens' (being at rest).

2. NOUN

/ kwaɪˈɛsənsi / (AmE), / kwɪˈɛsənsi / (BrE)
A state of being quiet and inactive; restfulness or dormancy.
quiet and inactive state

Examples

After the excitement subsided, the forest returned to its natural quiescency.
Periods of quiescency can be essential for creative thinking and reflection.
The animal remained in quiescency, barely moving for hours.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dormancy - state of being inactive or asleep
  • Antonym: activity - state of being active or engaged

Etymology

From Latin 'quiescentia' (restfulness), from 'quiescere' (to rest).
