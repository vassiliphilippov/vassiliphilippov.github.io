continuing

1. ADJ

/ kənˈtɪn.ju.ɪŋ / (AmE), / kənˈtɪn.juː.ɪŋ / (BrE)
persisting or lasting for a long time; ongoing
lasting for a long time

Examples

The country faces continuing political unrest despite recent reforms.
He struggled with continuing health issues throughout the year.
They are working to resolve the continuing shortage of affordable housing.

Synset Examples

chronic money problems

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chronic - continuing for a long time or recurring frequently
  • Synonym: persistent - continuing firmly or obstinately in a course of action
  • Antonym: temporary - lasting for only a limited period

2. ADJ

/ kənˈtɪnjuɪŋ / (AmE), / kənˈtɪnjʊɪŋ / (BrE)
remaining in force or lasting over a period of time without interruption; ongoing or persistent.
lasting or ongoing without interruption

Examples

She is taking a continuing education course to improve her skills.
The city faces a continuing shortage of affordable housing.
Our continuing partnership has brought many benefits to both companies.

Synset Examples

the act provided a continuing annual appropriation
the continuing struggle to put food on the table

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ongoing - continuous, still in progress
  • Antonym: ceasing - coming to an end or stopping

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'continue,' from Latin 'continuare' (to join together, make continuous).
