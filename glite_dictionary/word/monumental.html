monumental

1. ADJ

/ ˌmɑːn.jəˈmen.t̬əl / (AmE), / ˌmɒn.jʊˈmen.tl̩ / (BrE)
relating to or serving as a monument, especially having the characteristics of a monument in size, design, or enduring importance.
relating to or serving as a monument

Examples

The museum displayed several monumental sculptures carved from solid marble.
They admired the monumental gates at the entrance to the ancient city.
Her thesis focused on monumental architecture in nineteenth-century Europe.

Synset Examples

the use of the arch in monumental architecture
monumental sculptures

Etymology

From Latin monumentalis, from monumentum (monument).

2. ADJ

/ ˌmɑːn.jəˈmen.t̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˌmɒn.jʊˈmen.təl / (BrE)
extremely important or having great significance; outstanding in size, scope, or achievement
of great significance or importance

Examples

Her novel is considered a monumental work in modern literature.
The scientists achieved monumental progress in their research this year.
The decision to expand the company was a monumental step for the entire industry.

Synset Examples

Einstein's monumental contributions to physics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: significant - important, with notable influence or effect
  • Antonym: trivial - of little value or importance

Etymology

From Latin 'monumentalis', from 'monumentum' (monument).

3. ADJ

/ ˌmɑː.njuˈmen.tl̩ / (AmE), / ˌmɒn.juˈmen.tl̩ / (BrE)
extremely large, impressive, or solid in size, scale, or importance; massive or imposing.
massive or imposing in size or scale

Examples

The museum's entrance is guarded by monumental stone lions.
The project required a monumental amount of planning and effort.
We stood in awe before the monumental cathedral that towered over the city.

Synset Examples

massive oak doors

the monolithic proportions of Stalinist architecture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: massive - large and heavy or solid
  • Synonym: colossal - extremely large
  • Antonym: petite - small or diminutive

Etymology

From Latin 'monumentalis', from 'monumentum' (monument).
