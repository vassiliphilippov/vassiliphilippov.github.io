chacma

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃækmə / (AmE), / ˈtʃækmə / (BrE)
a large, greyish baboon native to southern and eastern Africa, known for its dog-like face and social behavior
large baboon of southern Africa

Examples

The chacma is the largest species of baboon found in Africa.
We watched a troop of chacma baboons crossing the road in Kruger National Park.
Researchers study the social structure of the chacma in their natural habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baboon - a large Old World monkey with a dog-like snout

Etymology

From South African Dutch 'chacma', of Khoikhoi origin.
