eventful

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈvɛntfəl / (AmE), / ɪˈvɛntf(ʊ)l / (BrE)
full of important or interesting events; marked by significant happenings or results.
full of significant events

Examples

It had been an eventful year, with many unexpected changes and challenges.
The journey became eventful when their train broke down in the middle of nowhere.
After an eventful meeting, the board finally reached a decision.

Synset Examples

the year's only really consequential legislation
an eventful decision

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uneventful - not marked by important or interesting events
  • Synonym: consequential - having important consequences or results

Etymology

From 'event' + '-ful', meaning 'full of events'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01280863-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ɪˈvɛntfl̩ / (AmE), / ɪˈvɛntfʊl / (BrE)
marked by a lot of important, interesting, or unusual events or incidents
full of important or interesting events

Examples

Our trip to the city turned out to be quite eventful after we missed our train.
It has been an eventful year for the company, with several major product launches.
The play’s eventful plot kept the audience engaged from start to finish.

Synset Examples

the most exhausting and eventful day of my life

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uneventful - having nothing interesting or significant happening

Etymology

From "event" + "-ful" (having the qualities of).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00807790-a
Synset POS: a
