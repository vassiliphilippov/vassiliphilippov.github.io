Sisymbrium barbarea

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈsɪm.bri.əm bɑːrˈbɛr.i.ə / (AmE), / sɪˈsɪm.bri.əm bɑːˈbɛər.ɪ.ə / (BrE)
A noxious plant with yellow flowers, sometimes classified under the genus Sisymbrium; also known as winter cress or Barbarea vulgaris.
plant species, also called winter cress

Examples

Farmers often encounter Sisymbrium barbarea invading their fields in early spring.
The bright yellow flowers of Sisymbrium barbarea can easily be spotted along riverbanks.
Botanists sometimes debate whether Sisymbrium barbarea belongs in its own genus or in Sisymbrium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winter cress - common name for Sisymbrium barbarea

Etymology

From Latin genus name Sisymbrium and species name barbarea, the latter from St. Barbara, the plant's eponym.
