Dodonaea

1. NOUN

/ ˌdoʊdoʊˈniːə / (AmE), / ˌdəʊdəˈniːə / (BrE)
a genus of tropical or subtropical shrubs and small trees, including species commonly known as hopbushes
genus of tropical shrubs or trees (hopbushes)

Examples

Several species within the genus Dodonaea are valued for their ornamental foliage.
Dodonaea grows well in arid and semi-arid environments, making it suitable for drought-resistant landscaping.
Botanists recently discovered a new hybrid in the Dodonaea genus in northern Australia.

Etymology

Named after Dodona, an ancient Greek city, referencing the oracle of Zeus.
