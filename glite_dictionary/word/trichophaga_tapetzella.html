Trichophaga tapetzella

1. NOUN

/ trɪˌkɑfəɡə tæˌpɛtˈzɛlə / (AmE), / trɪˌkɒfəɡə tæˌpɛtˈzɛlə / (BrE)
a species of moth whose larvae feed on carpets and other woolen materials, commonly known as the carpet moth
species of moth known as the carpet moth

Examples

Damage to the rug was traced back to an infestation of Trichophaga tapetzella larvae.
Pest control experts identified the culprit as Trichophaga tapetzella, commonly known as the carpet moth.
Textiles stored in humid environments are especially vulnerable to Trichophaga tapetzella infestations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carpet moth - common name for Trichophaga tapetzella

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'tricho-' (hair) and '-phaga' (eater), and Latin 'tapetzella' (from 'tapete', carpet).
