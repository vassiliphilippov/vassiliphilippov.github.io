unstinted

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈstɪntɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈstɪntɪd / (BrE)
Given or done freely and generously, without holding back or limitation.
given generously; not limited

Examples

The queen was known for her unstinted generosity toward her staff and the townspeople.
After months of unstinted support from his friends, he finally achieved his goal.
We received unstinted praise from our teachers for our hard work.

Synset Examples

distributed gifts with a lavish hand
the critics were lavish in their praise
a munificent gift
his father gave him a half-dollar and his mother a quarter and he thought them munificent
unsparing generosity
his unstinted devotion
called for unstinting aid to Britain
lavish praise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lavish - given in large amounts, very generous
  • Synonym: unstinting - not holding back; very generous
  • Antonym: stingy - not generous, unwilling to give or spend

Etymology

From 'un-' (not) + 'stinted', from Old English 'styntan' (to blunt, make dull), later meaning to restrict or limit.
