dolichocephalic

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɑːlɪkoʊsəˈfælɪk / (AmE), / ˌdɒlɪkəʊsɪˈfælɪk / (BrE)
An adult whose head is long and narrow in proportion to its width.
person with a long, narrow head
medical

Examples

The anthropologist identified several dolichocephalic individuals among the ancient skulls.
Most of the population in that region was described as dolichocephalic in cranial studies.
A dolichocephalic shape was once thought to indicate certain ancestral traits.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: brachycephalic - an adult whose head is short and broad in proportion to its width

Etymology

From Greek dolikhos (long) + kephalē (head).

2. ADJ

/ ˌdoʊlɪkoʊsəˈfælɪk / (AmE), / ˌdɒlɪkəʊsɪˈfælɪk / (BrE)
Having a relatively long head, specifically with a cephalic index (the ratio of head width to length) under 75.
having a relatively long head (cephalic index under 75)
medical

Examples

Some breeds of dogs, like the Greyhound, are known for their dolichocephalic skulls.
In anthropology, dolichocephalic features are often used to describe ancient populations.
The radiologist noted that the infant's head was dolichocephalic in shape on the scan.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: brachycephalic - having a relatively broad, short head (cephalic index over 80)

Etymology

From Greek dolikhos (long) + kephalē (head).
