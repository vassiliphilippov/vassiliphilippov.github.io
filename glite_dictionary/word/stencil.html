stencil

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɛnsl̩ / (AmE), / ˈstɛnsəl / (BrE)
a thin sheet of material with a design cut out, used to produce a pattern on a surface by allowing ink, paint, or other substance to pass through the holes
sheet with cut-out design for painting/printing

Examples

He used a stencil to paint numbers on the parking spaces.
The artist created a beautiful mural with the help of a stencil.
You can buy a stencil set to decorate your walls easily.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: template - a shaped piece used as a pattern to make consistent copies
  • Synonym: mask - a covering used to protect or define an area when painting or printing

Etymology

From Latin *stilus* (writing instrument), via French *estancille* and Old French *estenceler* (to sparkle, make a pattern).

2. VERB

/ ˈstɛn.səl / (AmE), / ˈstɛn.səl / (BrE)
to mark or print a design, letters, or patterns by using a stencil
to mark using a stencil

Examples

The artist carefully stenciled the pattern onto the wall above the fireplace.
We spent the afternoon stenciling holiday designs onto plain T-shirts for the children.
Each box was stenciled with a unique identification number for inventory purposes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trace - to copy a design or drawing by drawing over its lines
  • Synonym: paint - to apply color to a surface using a brush, etc.

Etymology

From the noun 'stencil', which comes from Old French 'estenceler' meaning 'to sparkle, decorate'.
