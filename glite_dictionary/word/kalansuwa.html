kalansuwa

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɑːnswə / (AmE), / kəˈlæn.swə / (BrE)
a cap wrapped by a turban, traditionally worn by Muslim religious elders
Muslim elders' cap under a turban

Examples

The imam adjusted his kalansuwa before donning his turban for the Friday prayer.
During the ceremony, each elder wore a white kalansuwa beneath ornate headwraps.
The museum displayed historic kalansuwas alongside other traditional Muslim garments.

Etymology

From Arabic قَلَنْسُوَة‎ (qalansuwa), meaning 'cap' or 'head covering'.
