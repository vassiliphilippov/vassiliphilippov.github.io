to inhabit

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈhæbɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈhabɪt / (BrE)
to live in or occupy a place or environment, especially as a permanent resident
to live in or occupy a place

Examples

Polar bears inhabit the icy regions of the Arctic.
Many rare species inhabit the rainforests of South America.
In the past, nomadic tribes inhabited these deserts.

Synset Examples

People lived in Africa millions of years ago
The people inhabited the islands that are now deserted
this kind of fish dwells near the bottom of the ocean
deer are populating the woods

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to occupy - to reside in or take possession of a place
  • Antonym: desert - to leave a place so that it is no longer inhabited

Etymology

From Latin 'inhabitare' (to dwell in), from 'in-' (in) + 'habitare' (to dwell).

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈhæbɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈhæbɪt / (BrE)
(formal or literary) To exist or be present in something, typically in a metaphorical sense; to occupy or fill.
exist or be present in (something)

Examples

A sense of nostalgia seemed to inhabit the old house.
Strange ideas inhabit her imagination at night.
Doubt began to inhabit his thoughts after the incident.

Synset Examples

Strange notions inhabited her mind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to occupy - to fill or take up (a space, position, etc.)
  • Synonym: to reside - to live or exist in a place

Etymology

From Latin 'inhabitare', from 'in-' (in) + 'habitare' (to dwell).

3. VERB

/ ɪnˈhæbɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈhæbɪt / (BrE)
(of a person, animal, or thing) to be present in or occupy a place, especially over a period of time; to live or exist in.
to live in or occupy a place

Examples

Sweet memories inhabit this house, making it feel alive with the past.
Lively traditions inhabit every corner of the ancient town.
Dreams and hopes inhabit the hearts of young people everywhere.

  • Synonym: to occupy - to be present in or fill a space
