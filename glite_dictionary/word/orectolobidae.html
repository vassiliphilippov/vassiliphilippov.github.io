Orectolobidae

1. NOUN

/ əˌrɛk.təˈlɒ.bɪˌdi / (AmE), / ɒˌrɛk.təˈlɒ.bɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of sharks known as nurse sharks and carpet sharks, native primarily to the Indo-Pacific region, characterized by their ornate patterns and broad, flattened bodies.
family of nurse and carpet sharks
medical

Examples

The Orectolobidae family includes the distinctive wobbegongs found along Australian coasts.
Researchers studied several species belonging to the Orectolobidae to understand their camouflage adaptations.
Conservationists are concerned about declining populations within the Orectolobidae family due to overfishing.

Etymology

From Greek 'orektos' (stretched out) and 'lobos' (lobe), referring to the shape of the fins, plus the zoological family suffix '-idae'.
