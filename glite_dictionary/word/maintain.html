to maintain

1. VERB

/ meɪnˈteɪn / (AmE), / meɪnˈteɪn / (BrE)
to have and exercise (power, control, authority, etc.)
to have and exercise power or authority

Examples

The president continued to maintain control over the country's foreign policy.
She struggled to maintain her influence after the leadership change.
Despite criticism, he managed to maintain authority within the organization.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to wield - to have and exercise (power, authority, etc.)

Etymology

From Latin 'manu tenere' (to hold in the hand), via Old French 'maintenir'.
