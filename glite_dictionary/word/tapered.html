tapered

1. ADJ

/ ˈteɪpɚd / (AmE), / ˈteɪpəd / (BrE)
gradually becoming narrower toward one end or along its length
gradually narrower

Examples

She wore jeans with tapered ankles that matched her boots perfectly.
The architect designed tapered columns for the entrance hall.
The artist's brush had a tapered tip for fine detail work.

Synset Examples

long tapering fingers
trousers with tapered legs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tapering - becoming gradually narrower; in the process of tapering

Etymology

From the verb 'taper', from Middle French 'taper', meaning to diminish.
