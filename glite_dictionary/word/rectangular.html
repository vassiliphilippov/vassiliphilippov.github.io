rectangular

1. ADJ

/ rɛkˈtæŋɡjələr / (AmE), / rɛkˈtæŋɡjʊlə / (BrE)
(of a shape or system) having sides or axes at right angles to each other; relating to or shaped like a rectangle.
having sides or axes at right angles

Examples

The engineer designed a rectangular frame to ensure structural stability.
In three-dimensional space, movement can be described using rectangular coordinates.
Please arrange the tables in a neat rectangular formation for the conference.

Synset Examples

wind and sea may displace the ship's center of gravity along three orthogonal axes
a rectangular Cartesian coordinate system

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orthogonal - at right angles, especially in mathematics or geometry

Etymology

From Latin 'rectangulus' (right-angled), from 'rectus' (right) + 'angulus' (angle).

2. ADJ

/ rɛkˈtæŋɡjələr / (AmE), / rɛkˈtæŋɡjʊlə / (BrE)
having four right angles; shaped like a rectangle.
having four right angles; like a rectangle

Examples

The students drew a rectangular shape on their graph paper for the math exercise.
Her new desk has a rectangular surface large enough to fit all her books and a computer.
The garden was divided into neat, rectangular plots for different types of vegetables.

Synset Examples

a rectangular figure twice as long as it is wide

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: square - a plane figure with four equal sides and four right angles

Etymology

From Latin 'rectangulus' (right-angled), from 'rectus' (right) + 'angulus' (angle).
