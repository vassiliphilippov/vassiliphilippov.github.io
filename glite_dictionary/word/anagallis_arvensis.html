Anagallis arvensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈɡælɪs ɑrˈvɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˌænəˈɡælɪs ɑːˈvɛnsɪs / (BrE)
a low-growing herb with scarlet, white, or purple flowers that close before rain, commonly known as the scarlet pimpernel
a flowering herb (scarlet pimpernel)

Examples

Anagallis arvensis is often found growing in cultivated fields and gardens.
Gardeners sometimes call Anagallis arvensis the 'poor man's weather glass' because its flowers close before rain.
The small red flowers of Anagallis arvensis add a splash of color to meadows during summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scarlet pimpernel - common name for Anagallis arvensis, a flowering plant

Etymology

From Latin: 'Anagallis' (a plant genus), 'arvensis' meaning 'of the field'.
