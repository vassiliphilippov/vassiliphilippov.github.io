pitted

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈpɪtɪd / (BrE)
Marked with many small holes or cavities on the surface, resembling a honeycomb.
having small cavities or holes

Examples

The ancient fossil was pitted with hundreds of tiny holes formed by marine organisms.
Some types of volcanic rock are highly pitted, giving them a rough texture.
After years of weathering, the statue's face became pitted and worn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honeycombed - filled with or resembling a honeycomb with many cavities
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even surface without holes or bumps

Etymology

From the verb 'pit' (to mark with depressions), derived from 'pit' (hole).
