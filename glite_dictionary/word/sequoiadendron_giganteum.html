Sequoiadendron giganteum

1. NOUN

/ səˌkwɔɪ.əˈdɛndrən ʤaɪˈgiː.tʃi.əm / (AmE), / sɪˌkwɔɪ.əˈdɛndrən dʒɪˈgiː.ti.əm / (BrE)
A species of extremely tall evergreen tree native to the western slopes of the Sierra Nevada in California, known as the giant sequoia and recognized as the largest living organism by volume.
species of giant evergreen tree (giant sequoia)

Examples

The Sequoiadendron giganteum can live for over 3,000 years in its natural habitat.
Scientists traveled to California to study the ecology of Sequoiadendron giganteum forests.
Tourists flock to see the massive trunks of Sequoiadendron giganteum in national parks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: giant sequoia - common name for Sequoiadendron giganteum

Etymology

From Latin; genus name formed from 'Sequoia' (in honor of Sequoyah, a Native American polymath) and Greek 'dendron' (tree); species name from Latin 'giganteum' (gigantic).
