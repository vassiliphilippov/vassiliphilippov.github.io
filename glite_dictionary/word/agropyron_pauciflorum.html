Agropyron pauciflorum

1. NOUN

/ ˌæɡroʊˈpaɪrɒn ˌpɔːsɪˈflɔrəm / (AmE), / ˌaɡrəˈpaɪərɒn ˌpɔːsɪˈflɔːrəm / (BrE)
A North American grass species cultivated in the western United States as an excellent forage crop.
forage grass of western North America

Examples

Farmers in the western United States grow Agropyron pauciflorum for its value as livestock forage.
The drought resistance of Agropyron pauciflorum makes it popular among ranchers.
Researchers are studying Agropyron pauciflorum to improve the yield of forage crops in arid regions.

Etymology

From Greek *agros* (field) + *pyros* (wheat); species name from Latin *pauciflorus* (few-flowered).
