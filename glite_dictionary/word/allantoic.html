allantoic

1. ADJ

/ əˌlænˈtoʊ.ɪk / (AmE), / əˌlanˈtəʊ.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characterized by an allantois, especially in embryonic development of reptiles, birds, and mammals.
relating to an allantois (embryonic sac)
medical

Examples

Researchers studied the allantoic fluid to understand the embryo's immune response.
Certain viruses are cultured in the allantoic cavity of chicken eggs for vaccine development.
The allantoic membrane assists in the transfer of waste and gases in bird embryos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allantois - the fetal membrane involved in embryonic development in reptiles, birds, and mammals

Etymology

From New Latin allantois + -ic, indicating relation; allantois itself from Greek allas (sausage) due to shape.
