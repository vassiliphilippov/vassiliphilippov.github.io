irrevocability

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌrɛvəˌkəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪˌrɛvəkəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being impossible to change, reverse, or retract; finality.
the state of being impossible to reverse

Examples

The irrevocability of her decision weighed heavily on her mind.
In many legal systems, the irrevocability of certain contracts ensures finality in business dealings.
They discussed the irrevocability of changes made to the environment.

Synset Examples

It would have to show that the irrevocability of execution is a sufficient reason to prohibit the penalty even in the absence of acceptable penalties that achieve similar deterrent and retributive effects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irreversibility - the quality of being impossible to reverse

Etymology

From 'irrevocable', from Latin 'irrevocabilis' (not able to be recalled), plus suffix '-ity'.
