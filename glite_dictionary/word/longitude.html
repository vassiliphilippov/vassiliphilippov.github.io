longitude

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑːndʒəˌtuːd / (AmE), / ˈlɒndʒɪtjuːd / (BrE)
The angular distance of a place east or west of the prime meridian at Greenwich, usually expressed in degrees and minutes.
angular distance from prime meridian

Examples

The city lies at a longitude of 73 degrees east.
Early navigators had difficulty determining their exact longitude at sea.
Maps indicate both the latitude and longitude of a location.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: latitude - angular distance north or south of the equator

Etymology

From Latin 'longitudo' (length), from 'longus' (long).
