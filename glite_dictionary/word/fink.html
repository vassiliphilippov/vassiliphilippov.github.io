fink

1. VERB

/ fɪŋk / (AmE), / fɪŋk / (BrE)
(of a person) to take over the job of a worker who is on strike, often considered an act of betrayal by other workers.
take over a striker's job
informal american

Examples

During the union strike, he was criticized for finking on his coworkers by taking their shifts.
Some workers refused to fink, showing solidarity with those on the picket line.
She lost friends after she finked during the factory walkout.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scab - a person who works or replaces striking workers

Etymology

Originally American slang, possibly from Yiddish *fnk* (to squeal, betray).

2. NOUN

/ fɪŋk / (AmE), / fɪŋk / (BrE)
a person who acts as an informer, especially one who betrays colleagues or associates to the police
informer; betrayer to the police
informal

Examples

He was considered a fink after he gave information to the police.
Nobody trusts a fink in this neighborhood.
The gang ousted him, calling him a fink for cooperating with the authorities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snitch - a person who informs on someone; informer
  • Synonym: rat - a person who betrays others, especially by informing to the authorities

Etymology

Of unknown origin, possibly from Yiddish 'fink' (finch, a type of bird, with a slang association to prying or tattling).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10110781-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ fɪŋk / (AmE), / fɪŋk / (BrE)
(informal) To confess to a punishable or disapproved act, especially by informing on others, usually under pressure.
confess or inform on someone (informal)
informal american

Examples

He refused to fink on his friends, despite the police questioning him for hours.
Some worry that a coworker might fink if the boss applies enough pressure.
She vowed never to fink, no matter what threats they made.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inform - to disclose information about someone, especially to authorities

Etymology

Originally US slang; probably from the noun 'fink,' meaning informer or unpleasant person, itself of uncertain origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00821169-v
Synset POS: v
