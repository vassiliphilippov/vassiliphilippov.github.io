contumeliously

1. ADV

/ ˌkɑːn.təˈmiː.li.əs.li / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.tjʊˈmiː.li.əs.li / (BrE)
In a scornful, insulting, or contemptuous manner; showing or expressing disrespect.
in a scornfully disrespectful way

Examples

She glared at the judge and spoke contumeliously, shocking everyone in the courtroom.
The official replied contumeliously to the protestors, refusing to consider their concerns.
His remarks were delivered contumeliously, leaving no doubt about his contempt.

Synset Examples

she spoke of him contemptuously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contemptuously - in a scornful or disdainful way
  • Antonym: respectfully - in a respectful manner

Etymology

From Latin contumeliosus (insulting, reproachful), from contumelia (abuse, affront).
