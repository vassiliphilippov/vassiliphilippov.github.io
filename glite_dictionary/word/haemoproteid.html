haemoproteid

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːməˈproʊtiːd / (AmE), / ˌhiːməˈprəʊtiːd / (BrE)
a parasitic protozoan of the genus Haemoproteus, typically infecting the blood and internal organs of birds and sometimes associated with malaria-like symptoms
a parasitic protozoan of birds (genus Haemoproteus)
medical

Examples

Many species of birds can be infected by haemoproteids transmitted by biting insects.
The researcher identified a new haemoproteid in the blood samples of migratory swallows.
Haemoproteids typically have part of their life cycle within the internal organs of their avian hosts.

Etymology

From modern Latin Haemoproteus (genus name), from Greek 'haima' (blood) + 'proteios' (primary; relating to the first) + scientific suffix -id.
