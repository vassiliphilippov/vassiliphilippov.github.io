Abkhazian

1. NOUN

/ æbˈkɑːziən / (AmE), / æbˈkɑːziən / (BrE)
a member of an ethnic group native to Abkhazia, located to the east of the Black Sea
member of an ethnic group from Abkhazia

Examples

There is a large community of Abkhazians living on the eastern shore of the Black Sea.
The traditional music of the Abkhazian people is known for its unique harmonies.
She is proud of her Abkhazian heritage and often shares stories about her culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Circassian - a member of a North Caucasian ethnic group; related regional ethnic group

Etymology

From Abkhazia, the name of the region inhabited by the Abkhaz people.

3. NOUN

/ æbˈkɑːziən / (AmE), / æbˈkɑːziən / (BrE)
the language of the Abkhaz people, belonging to the Northwest Caucasian language family
language of the Abkhaz people

Examples

Many people in Abkhazia speak Abkhazian as their first language.
The grammar of Abkhazian is known to be quite complex.
She decided to study Abkhazian at university to learn more about the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Abkhaz - another name for the Abkhazian language
  • Synonym: Circassian - a closely related Northwest Caucasian language

Etymology

From Abkhaz, the name of the ethnic group plus English '-ian.'
