Heritiera littoralis

1. NOUN

/ hɛˌrɪtiˈɛrə lɪtəˈrælɪs / (AmE), / hɛˌrɪtiˈɛəriə lɪtɒˈrɑːlɪs / (BrE)
A small tree native to coastal regions of the Old World tropics, distinguished by its silvery undersides of the leaves.
tropical coastal tree with silvery leaves beneath

Examples

The leaves of Heritiera littoralis are easily recognized by their silvery undersides.
Heritiera littoralis is often found along the muddy banks of tropical coastlines.
Researchers studied how Heritiera littoralis adapts to saline environments.

Etymology

From the genus name Heritiera (named after French botanist Charles Louis L'Héritier de Brutelle) and Latin 'littoralis', meaning 'of the shore'.
