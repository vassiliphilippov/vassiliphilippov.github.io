Ka

1. PROPN

/ kɑː / (AmE), / kɑː / (BrE)
an epithet for Prajapati and Brahma, used as the name of an unknown god in Hindu mythology
epithet for a Hindu deity (Brahma, Prajapati)

Examples

Many hymns of the Rigveda refer to Ka as the creator of the universe.
Scholars debate the significance of Ka in early Vedic religion.
The name Ka highlights the mysterious origins of the Hindu god Brahma.

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'ka', meaning 'who?', used as a name for the creator god in Vedic texts.
