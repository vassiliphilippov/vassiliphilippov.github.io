ire

1. NOUN

/ aɪɚ / (AmE), / aɪə / (BrE)
anger; intense displeasure or indignation
anger; intense displeasure

Examples

His careless remarks only increased her ire.
The company's decision provoked widespread ire among its employees.
She struggled to contain her ire during the heated debate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anger - strong feeling of displeasure or hostility
  • Degree Higher: rage - violent, uncontrollable anger
  • Degree Lower: annoyance - a mild feeling of irritation

Etymology

From Latin 'ira' meaning 'anger'.

2. NOUN

/ aɪɚ / (AmE), / aɪə / (BrE)
intense anger; wrath, often aroused by a real or perceived wrong
intense anger; wrath

Examples

Her criticism sparked his ire and led to a heated argument.
The new tax law has drawn public ire across the country.
He tried to hide his ire, but his clenched fists gave him away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anger - a strong feeling of displeasure or hostility
  • Synonym: wrath - extreme anger, chiefly used in literary contexts
  • Antonym: calm - a state of being free from anger or strong emotions

Etymology

From Latin ira (anger, wrath).
