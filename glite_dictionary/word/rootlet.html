rootlet

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːtlət / (AmE), / ˈruːtlɪt / (BrE)
a small root or a subdivision of a root, especially one of the finer branches in a plant's root system
a small root

Examples

The orchid's rootlets help it absorb moisture from the air.
Tiny rootlets spread out from the main root, anchoring the plant in the soil.
Scientists examined the rootlets under a microscope to study their structure.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: root - the main part of a plant below the ground that absorbs water and nutrients

Etymology

Formed from 'root' + diminutive suffix '-let', indicating smallness.
