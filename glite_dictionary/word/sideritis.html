Sideritis

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈdɛr.ɪ.tɪs / (AmE), / sɪˈdɛr.ɪ.tɪs / (BrE)
a genus of woolly, aromatic herbs or shrubs native to the Mediterranean region, commonly known as ironwort
Mediterranean genus of aromatic herbs

Examples

Many herbal teas in southern Europe are made from the Sideritis plant.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of Sideritis species.
The hillsides were covered with aromatic shrubs of Sideritis.

Etymology

From Greek σιδηρίτις (sidēritis), meaning 'plant that contains iron'; used in classical authors for various plants.

2. NOUN

/ saɪˈdɛrɪtɪs / (AmE), / sɪˈdɛrɪtɪs / (BrE)
a genus of woolly, aromatic herbs or shrubs native to the Mediterranean region, commonly known as mountain tea or ironwort
genus of aromatic Mediterranean herbs or shrubs

Examples

Researchers studied several species from the Sideritis genus for their medicinal properties.
Traditional mountain tea made from Sideritis is popular in Greece and the Balkans.
Some Sideritis plants have been used in herbal remedies since ancient times.

Etymology

From Greek sideritis, meaning 'ironwort', referring to plants used in ancient times to heal wounds caused by iron weapons.
