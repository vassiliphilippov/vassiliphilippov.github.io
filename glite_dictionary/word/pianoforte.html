pianoforte

1. NOUN

/ ˌpiː.ə.noʊˈfɔr.teɪ / (AmE), / ˌpjɑː.nəʊˈfɔː.teɪ / (BrE)
A large keyboard musical instrument in which keys cause hammers to strike strings, producing musical sounds; an early form of the modern piano.
early form of the piano

Examples

The museum features a beautifully restored pianoforte from the early nineteenth century.
Unlike the harpsichord, the pianoforte allows for dynamic expression through touch.
She studied classical compositions on an authentic pianoforte to better understand period performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piano - a keyboard instrument evolved from the pianoforte

Etymology

From Italian 'pianoforte' (soft-loud); combining 'piano' (soft) and 'forte' (loud), referring to dynamic range.
