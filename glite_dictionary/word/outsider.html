outsider

1. NOUN

/ ˌaʊtˈsaɪ.dɚ / (AmE), / ˌaʊtˈsaɪ.də / (BrE)
a person who is not accepted as a member of a group, organization, or community
person not part of a group

Examples

As an outsider, she found it hard to understand their traditions and customs.
The company hired an outsider to bring a fresh perspective to the team.
He always felt like an outsider at school because he moved so often.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: insider - a person who is accepted as a member of a group or organization

Etymology

From out + side, first used in early 18th century to mean someone not belonging to an inner circle.

2. NOUN

/ aʊtˈsaɪdɚ / (AmE), / aʊtˈsaɪdə / (BrE)
a competitor in a contest, event, or race who is not expected to win or do well
competitor not expected to win

Examples

The horse was considered an outsider, but it surprised everyone by finishing first.
Many people root for the outsider in major tournaments, hoping for an upset.
As an outsider, she had odds of 50 to 1 to win the marathon.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: favorite - competitor expected to win
  • Synonym: underdog - competitor with little chance of winning

Etymology

From out + side, originally meaning 'one who is not part of a group' and extended to competitions.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10407891-n
Synset POS: n
