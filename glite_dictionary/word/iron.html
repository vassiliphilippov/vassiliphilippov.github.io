/ ˈaɪɚn / (AmE), / ˈaɪən / (BrE)

/ ˈaɪɚn / (AmE), / ˈaɪən / (BrE)

type of golf club with a metal head

Examples

He chose a seven iron to hit the ball onto the green.
Many beginner golfers find it easier to use an iron rather than a wood.
She kept a full set of irons in her golf bag for different shots.

6. NOUN

/ ˈaɪɚn / (AmE), / ˈaɪən / (BrE)
a heavy, magnetic, silver-white metallic element (chemical symbol Fe) that rusts easily and is widely used in construction, manufacturing, and essential for transporting oxygen in the blood.
a strong, magnetic metallic element

Examples

Blood contains iron, which is crucial for carrying oxygen throughout the body.
Ancient swords were often made from iron due to its strength and availability.
The old bridge is constructed from solid iron beams.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: steel - an alloy made primarily of iron and carbon
  • Antonym: copper - another common metallic element, reddish in color

Etymology

From Old English *īsern*, related to Old Norse *ísarn* and Latin *ferrum* (iron).
