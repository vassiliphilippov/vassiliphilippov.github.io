Obadiah

1. PROPN

/ ˌoʊbəˈdaɪə / (AmE), / ˌəʊbəˈdaɪə / (BrE)
A book of the Old Testament containing the prophecies of Obadiah; the shortest book in the Christian Bible.
book of prophecy in the Old Testament

Examples

The Book of Obadiah contains only one chapter and is rich in prophetic imagery.
Many scholars debate the historical context behind Obadiah and his message to Edom.
In Bible study groups, Obadiah is often read alongside Amos and other minor prophets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Amos - another prophetic book in the Old Testament

Etymology

From Hebrew *‘Ōḇaḏyāh* meaning 'servant of Yahweh'.

2. PROPN

/ ˌoʊbəˈdaɪə / (AmE), / ˌəʊbəˈdaɪə / (BrE)
A Hebrew minor prophet in the Old Testament who prophesied against Edom; also, the name of the biblical book attributed to him.
a Hebrew prophet and biblical book

Examples

Obadiah is one of the twelve minor prophets whose book appears in the Old Testament.
The prophecy of Obadiah focuses on the judgment of Edom for its actions against Israel.
Many scholars have debated the historical context in which Obadiah delivered his message.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Book of Obadiah - the biblical book traditionally attributed to the prophet Obadiah

Etymology

From Hebrew ‘Oḇadyāh (‘servant of Yahweh’), via Greek Obdios and Latin Obadia.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
