convolution

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːn.vəˈluː.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.vəˈluː.ʃən / (BrE)
a fold or ridge in the surface of the brain, especially one of the rounded prominences on the cerebral cortex
fold or ridge on the brain's surface

Examples

The intricate convolutions of the brain help increase its surface area and cognitive capacity.
Damage to certain convolutions of the cerebral cortex can affect memory and language abilities.
Neurosurgeons must navigate the complex convolutions of the brain during delicate procedures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gyrus - a ridge or fold between two clefts on the cerebral surface
  • Antonym: sulcus - a groove or furrow on the surface of the brain

Etymology

From Latin 'convolutio' (a rolling together), from 'convolvere' (to roll together).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːn.vəˈluː.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.vəˈluː.ʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of coiling, twisting, or winding together; or the state of being so twisted or coiled.
a coil or twist; act of twisting

Examples

The convolution of vines around the fence created a beautiful natural pattern.
His explanation got lost in the convolution of details and digressions.
She marveled at the convolution of the shell's intricate design.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coil - a length of something wound or arranged in a spiral or sequence of rings
  • Synonym: twist - the act of turning or winding something

Etymology

From Latin 'convolutio' (a rolling together), from 'convolvere' (to roll together).
