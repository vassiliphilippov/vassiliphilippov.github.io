bloody-minded

1. ADJ

/ ˈblʌdi ˌmaɪndɪd / (AmE), / ˈblʌdi ˌmaɪndɪd / (BrE)
Eager to engage in violence or bloodshed; showing a cruel or ruthless disposition.
eager for violence or bloodshed

Examples

The dictator's bloody-minded policies left the country in constant turmoil.
His bloody-minded cruelty shocked even his most hardened allies.
During the rebellion, a bloody-minded attitude prevailed among the warring factions.

Synset Examples

bloody-minded tyrants
bloodthirsty yells
went after the collaborators with a sanguinary fury that drenched the land with blood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bloodthirsty - eager to shed blood or commit violence
  • Synonym: ruthless - having no pity or compassion

Etymology

Formed from 'bloody' + 'minded', meaning having a mind set on blood (violence).

2. ADJ

/ ˈblʌdi ˌmaɪndɪd / (AmE), / ˈblʌdi ˌmaɪndɪd / (BrE)
deliberately difficult and uncooperative; stubbornly obstructive, especially out of spite.
deliberately obstructive or uncooperative
british

Examples

The negotiations stalled when one side became bloody-minded and refused any compromise.
His bloody-minded attitude made teamwork nearly impossible during the project.
She faced the changes with a bloody-minded determination not to give in.

Synset Examples

unions … have never been as bloody-minded about demarcation as the shipbuilders

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstinate - stubbornly refusing to change one's opinion or chosen course of action
  • Antonym: cooperative - willing to help or do what people ask

Etymology

Formed from 'bloody' (as an intensifier) and 'minded' (having a specified attitude).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02335070-s
Synset POS: s
