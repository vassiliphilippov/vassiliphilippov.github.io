Sikh

1. NOUN

/ siːk / (AmE), / siːk / (BrE)
A follower of Sikhism, a monotheistic religion founded in Punjab in the 15th century.
follower of Sikhism

Examples

My friend is a proud Sikh who attends the gurdwara every week.
Many Sikhs celebrate Vaisakhi as an important religious festival.
The Sikh community is known for its charitable work and commitment to equality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sikhism - a monotheistic religion founded in Punjab in the 15th century

Etymology

From Punjabi ਸਿੱਖ (sikh), meaning 'disciple, learner'.

2. ADJ

/ siːk / (AmE), / siːk / (BrE)
of or relating to the Sikhs or their religious beliefs and customs
relating to Sikhs or Sikhism

Examples

Many Sikh temples, known as gurdwaras, are open to everyone regardless of background.
She wore traditional Sikh attire for the religious festival.
The Sikh community celebrates Vaisakhi as a major holiday.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Hindu - of or relating to Hinduism or Hindus
  • Antonym: Muslim - of or relating to Muslims or Islam

Etymology

From Punjabi ਸਿੱਖ (sikh), meaning 'disciple'.
