vanadate

1. NOUN

/ vəˈneɪdeɪt / (AmE), / vəˈneɪdeɪt / (BrE)
a salt or ester of vanadic acid, containing an anion with pentavalent vanadium
a salt or ester of vanadic acid
medical

Examples

The scientist analyzed the crystal structure of the vanadate compound.
Certain enzymes are inhibited by vanadate ions in solution.
The laboratory experiment involved the synthesis of sodium vanadate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vanadic acid - an acid containing vanadium in its pentavalent state

Etymology

From vanadium + -ate, following chemical nomenclature conventions.
