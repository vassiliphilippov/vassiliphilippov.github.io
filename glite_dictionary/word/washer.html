washer

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑːʃər / (AmE), / ˈwɒʃə / (BrE)
a machine used for washing clothes and linens automatically
automatic machine for washing clothes

Examples

Our old washing machine broke down, so we bought a new washer last weekend.
She loaded the clothes into the washer and pressed the start button.
The washer makes doing laundry much easier and quicker.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: washing machine - an automatic machine for washing clothes

Etymology

From Old English 'wæscere', one who washes.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɑʃər / (AmE), / ˈwɒʃə / (BrE)
A flat disc, typically of metal, rubber, or plastic, placed between two surfaces to prevent leakage, distribute pressure, or reduce vibration.
flat disc used to prevent leakage

Examples

The plumber replaced the worn washer to stop the faucet from dripping.
Before tightening the bolt, make sure to place a washer between the surfaces.
A rubber washer provides a watertight seal in the pipe joint.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gasket - a seal of rubber or other material used to prevent leakage

Etymology

From Middle English wascher, related to 'wash' + '-er', originally referring to a person or thing that washes; later extended to the device’s cleaning or sealing function.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04562157-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈwɑʃər / (AmE), / ˈwɒʃə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to wash clothes or other items
person who washes items for a living

Examples

She worked as a washer at the local laundry for over ten years.
The hotel employs several washers to keep the linens spotless.
After arriving in the city, he found a job as a washer to support his family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: launderer - a person whose job is to launder clothes; synonym for washer (person)
  • Synonym: cleaner - a person whose job is to clean; related occupation

Etymology

From Old English 'wæscan' (to wash) + agent suffix '-er'.
