vinblastine

1. NOUN

/ vɪnˈblæstiːn / (AmE), / vɪnˈblɑːstiːn / (BrE)
an antineoplastic drug derived from the periwinkle plant, used to treat certain types of cancer by inhibiting cell division
anticancer drug from periwinkle plant
medical

Examples

The oncologist prescribed vinblastine as part of the chemotherapy regimen for lymphoma.
Side effects of vinblastine may include hair loss and a lowered white blood cell count.
Researchers studied the effectiveness of vinblastine in treating various childhood cancers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vincristine - another antineoplastic drug derived from periwinkle plants

Etymology

Blend of 'vinca' (genus name of the periwinkle plant) and 'blast' (relating to cell growth), with suffix '-ine'.
