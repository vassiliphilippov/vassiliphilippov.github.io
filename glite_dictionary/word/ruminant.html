ruminant

1. NOUN

/ ˈruː.mə.nənt / (AmE), / ˈruː.mɪ.nənt / (BrE)
any hoofed mammal that chews cud and has a stomach divided into four (occasionally three) compartments, such as cattle, sheep, and deer
cud-chewing hoofed mammal with multi-chambered stomach

Examples

Cows, sheep, and deer are all examples of ruminant animals.
The digestive system of a ruminant is specially adapted for fermenting plant material.
Unlike humans, a ruminant regurgitates and rechews its food.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonruminant - an animal that does not chew cud or have a multi-chambered stomach

Etymology

From Latin 'ruminant-', from 'ruminare' (to chew the cud).

2. ADJ

/ ˈruːmɪnənt / (AmE), / ˈruːmɪnənt / (BrE)
relating to animals that chew the cud, especially those of the suborder Ruminantia, such as cattle, sheep, and deer.
relating to cud-chewing animals

Examples

The cow is a typical ruminant species, adapted to digest tough plant material.
Some ruminant mammals, such as giraffes, have specialized stomachs for regurgitating and rechewing food.
Veterinarians study ruminant physiology to better understand the digestive processes of livestock.

Synset Examples

ruminant mammals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herbivorous - feeding only on plants; relating to plant-eating animals

Etymology

From Latin 'ruminant-', present participle of 'ruminare' (to chew the cud).
