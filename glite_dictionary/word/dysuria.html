dysuria

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈjʊriə / (AmE), / dɪsˈjʊəriə / (BrE)
pain or discomfort when urinating
painful or difficult urination
medical

Examples

The patient complained of dysuria and lower abdominal pain during the examination.
Dysuria can be a symptom of a urinary tract infection or other bladder issues.
If you experience persistent dysuria, it is important to consult a healthcare provider.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: urgency - a sudden, compelling urge to urinate
  • Synonym: nocturia - frequent urination at night

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'dys-' (bad, difficult) + 'ouron' (urine).
