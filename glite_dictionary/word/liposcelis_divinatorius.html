Liposcelis divinatorius

1. NOUN

/ lɪˌpɒsəˌsaɪlɪs ˌdɪvəˌneɪtɔːriəs / (AmE), / lɪˌpɒsəˌsiːlɪs ˌdɪvəˈneɪtəriəs / (BrE)
A small, wingless insect of the booklouse family, known for damaging books and papers.
a species of booklouse damaging books and papers

Examples

Old libraries often struggle with infestations of Liposcelis divinatorius, which can damage valuable manuscripts.
Proper humidity control helps prevent Liposcelis divinatorius from thriving among archived documents.
Conservators discovered traces of Liposcelis divinatorius among the pages of the rare collection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: booklouse - a small insect that feeds on books and paper

Etymology

From Latin: genus Liposcelis (booklouse) and species name divinatorius.
