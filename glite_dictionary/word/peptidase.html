peptidase

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛp.tɪˌdeɪs / (AmE), / ˈpɛp.tɪˌdeɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that catalyzes the breakdown of proteins into peptides and amino acids by proteolysis
enzyme that breaks down proteins
medical

Examples

The enzyme peptidase plays a vital role in protein digestion within the body.
Researchers discovered a new type of peptidase that acts specifically on dietary proteins.
Certain medicines can inhibit peptidase activity to help treat particular diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protease - an enzyme that performs proteolysis, protein breakdown
  • Synonym: proteinase - another term for an enzyme that breaks down proteins

Etymology

From peptide + -ase (enzyme suffix indicating catalytic action).
