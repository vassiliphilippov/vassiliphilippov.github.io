chlorothiazide

1. NOUN

/ ˌklɔːrəˈθaɪəzaɪd / (AmE), / ˌklɔːrəˈθaɪəzaɪd / (BrE)
a diuretic drug used to treat hypertension and fluid retention (edema)
a diuretic drug for hypertension and edema
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed chlorothiazide to help reduce her high blood pressure.
Patients taking chlorothiazide should be monitored for changes in electrolyte levels.
Chlorothiazide is often used alongside other medications for managing heart failure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diuretic - a substance promoting urine production

Etymology

From 'chloro-' (referring to chlorine) and 'thiazide' (a class of diuretic compounds).
