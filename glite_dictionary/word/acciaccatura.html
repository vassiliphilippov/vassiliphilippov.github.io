acciaccatura

1. NOUN

/ əˌtʃɑːkəˈtʊrə / (AmE), / əˌtʃakəˈtjʊərə / (BrE)
A short, ornamental note played quickly before a main note, usually written in small print.
ornamental musical note played quickly

Examples

The pianist added an acciaccatura before the main melody to create a lively effect.
In Baroque music, the acciaccatura often appears just before a strong beat.
She struggled at first to play the acciaccatura smoothly in her piano piece.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grace note - an extra note added as ornamentation in music

Etymology

From Italian acciaccatura, from acciaccare (to crush).
