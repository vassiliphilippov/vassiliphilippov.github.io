gleeful

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡliː.fəl / (AmE), / ˈɡliː.fəl / (BrE)
feeling or showing great delight or joy; full of high-spirited happiness
full of joy or delight

Examples

The children were gleeful as they opened their birthday presents.
She let out a gleeful laugh when she heard the good news.
His gleeful reaction showed just how much he enjoyed the surprise.

Synset Examples

a joyful heart

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joyful - feeling, expressing, or causing great pleasure and happiness
  • Antonym: miserable - feeling very unhappy or uncomfortable

Etymology

From Middle English 'glee' (mirth, joy) + '-ful' (full of).
