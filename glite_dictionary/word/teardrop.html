teardrop

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪr.drɑp / (AmE), / ˈtɪə.drɒp / (BrE)
a shape that is rounded at one end and pointed at the other, resembling a falling drop of liquid; often used to describe ornaments or designs.
shape like a falling drop

Examples

She wore a delicate necklace with a single teardrop pendant.
The artist painted flowers with teardrop-shaped petals.
The chandelier was decorated with crystal teardrops that sparkled in the light.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oval - an elongated round shape, egg-shaped

Etymology

Formed from 'tear' (as in weeping) and 'drop', referring to the shape of a falling tear.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɪrˌdrɑp / (AmE), / ˈtɪəˌdrɒp / (BrE)
A single drop of the salty liquid that forms in the eye as a result of emotion or irritation and runs down the cheek when a person cries.
a single drop of a tear

Examples

A single teardrop slid down her cheek as she listened to the sad news.
He noticed a teardrop clinging to her eyelashes just before she turned away.
The artist painted a portrait with a brilliant blue teardrop on the subject's face.

Synset Examples

his story brought tears to her eyes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tear - the liquid secreted by glands in the eye

Etymology

Formed from 'tear' (from Old English 'tēar') + 'drop'.

Source: WordNet
