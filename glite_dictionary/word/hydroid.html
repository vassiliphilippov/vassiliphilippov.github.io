hydroid

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪ.drɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ.drɔɪd / (BrE)
a colonial aquatic animal of the class Hydrozoa, typically having a life stage dominated by the polyp form
colonial aquatic animal, polyp-dominant
medical

Examples

Marine biologists discovered a colony of hydroid growing on the rocks near the shore.
The life cycle of a hydroid typically includes both polyp and medusa forms.
Many species of hydroid provide shelter for small marine creatures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrozoan - an animal belonging to the class Hydrozoa, which includes hydroids

Etymology

From Greek hydra (water serpent) + -oid (form, resembling).
