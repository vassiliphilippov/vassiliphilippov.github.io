Bromeliaceae

1. NOUN

/ broʊˌmiliˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / brəʊˌmiːlɪˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of tropical American plants that includes the pineapple and several ornamental species
family of tropical American plants

Examples

Many members of the Bromeliaceae family are popular houseplants due to their striking appearance.
The pineapple, a well-known fruit, belongs to the Bromeliaceae family.
Researchers are studying the adaptation mechanisms of Bromeliaceae in different rainforest environments.

Etymology

From New Latin, after the type genus Bromelia, named for Swedish botanist Olof Bromelius.

2. NOUN

/ broʊˌmiːliˈeɪsiːˌiː / (AmE), / brəʊˌmiːlɪˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of tropical American flowering plants, including the pineapple and many ornamental species, belonging to the order Poales.
family of tropical American flowering plants

Examples

The Bromeliaceae family includes a wide range of colorful and unique plants such as bromeliads and the pineapple.
Many members of the Bromeliaceae are popular as ornamental houseplants due to their striking appearance.
Biologists are studying the adaptation strategies of the Bromeliaceae in different tropical environments.

Etymology

From New Latin Bromeliaceae, based on the genus Bromelia (named after Swedish botanist Olaf Bromelius).
