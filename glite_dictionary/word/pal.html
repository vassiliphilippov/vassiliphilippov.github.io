pal

1. NOUN

/ pæl / (AmE), / pæl / (BrE)
a close friend or companion; someone with whom one spends time and shares activities
close friend or companion
informal

Examples

He's been my best pal since we were kids.
After work, she met up with her pals at the pub for a drink.
I can always count on my pal to cheer me up when I'm down.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: friend - a person whom one knows and with whom one has a bond of mutual affection

Etymology

From Romani *phral* (brother) or shortening of 'pal'ly' (associate), 17th-19th century slang.

2. VERB

/ pæl / (AmE), / pæl / (BrE)
to become friends or act in a friendly way towards someone
to become friends or act friendly
informal

Examples

Ever since they started working together, the two colleagues have palled around a lot more.
She tried to pal up with the new student to make him feel welcome.
During the summer camp, the kids quickly palled around with each other.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to befriend - to act as a friend to someone

Etymology

Originally from the noun 'pal' (friend), which comes from Romani 'phral' (brother).
