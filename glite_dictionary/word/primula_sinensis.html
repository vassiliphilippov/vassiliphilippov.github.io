Primula sinensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌprɪmjələ sɪˈnɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˌprɪmjʊlə sɪˈnɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A species of primrose native to China, cultivated for its attractive flowers.
a species of primrose native to China

Examples

The Primula sinensis is prized for its delicate, colorful blooms in early spring.
Gardeners often grow Primula sinensis in pots to brighten up their homes.
She studied the growth patterns of Primula sinensis for her botany project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: primrose - a plant with colorful flowers, in the genus Primula

Etymology

From Latin: 'primula' meaning 'little first one' (primrose), and 'sinensis' meaning 'from China'.
