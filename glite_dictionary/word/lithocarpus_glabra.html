Lithocarpus glabra

1. NOUN

/ lɪθoʊˈkɑrpəs ˈɡleɪbrə / (AmE), / lɪθəˈkɑːpəs ˈɡleɪbrə / (BrE)
A small evergreen tree native to China and Japan, belonging to the beech family.
small evergreen tree of China and Japan

Examples

The leaves of Lithocarpus glabra are glossy and remain green throughout the year.
Botanists collected samples of Lithocarpus glabra from a forest in central Japan.
Many gardens in China feature Lithocarpus glabra for its attractive evergreen foliage.

Etymology

From New Latin: 'Lithocarpus' from Greek 'lithos' (stone) + 'karpos' (fruit); 'glabra' from Latin meaning 'smooth'.
