Emilia

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmɪliə / (AmE), / ɪˈmiːliə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical African herbs in the daisy family, known for their bright orange or red flower heads.
genus of tropical African herbs

Examples

Emilia is commonly found in open fields and along roadsides in tropical Africa.
Several species in the Emilia genus are grown for their ornamental flower heads.
Botanists have documented the medicinal uses of Emilia in traditional African practices.

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈmɪliə / (AmE), / ɪˈmɪliə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical African herbs with daisy-like flowers, commonly cultivated for ornamental purposes.
genus of tropical African herbs

Examples

The vibrant flowers of Emilia make it a popular choice in ornamental gardens.
Several species within the genus Emilia are native to tropical Africa.
Botanists recently discovered a new Emilia plant in the savanna.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herb - a plant valued for flavor, scent, or medicinal properties

Etymology

Named after the Italian botanist Emilio Zanoni.
