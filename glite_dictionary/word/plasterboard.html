plasterboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈplæstərˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈplɑːstəˌbɔːd / (BrE)
a building material consisting of a rigid board with a gypsum plaster core sandwiched between layers of paper or fiberboard, used for making interior walls and ceilings
gypsum-based board for interior walls

Examples

The builders installed plasterboard to create smooth interior walls throughout the house.
After the flood, we had to replace the damaged plasterboard in the basement.
Using plasterboard is a quick and efficient way to finish room partitions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drywall - a similar board material used for interior walls
  • Synonym: wallboard - general term for panels used in building interior walls

Etymology

From 'plaster' + 'board', named for composition and function.
