Euphorbia dentata

1. NOUN

/ juːˈfɔːrbiə dɛnˈteɪtə / (AmE), / juːˈfɔːbɪə dɛnˈtɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of annual weed native to northeastern North America, characterized by toothed leaves.
a North American annual weed with toothed leaves

Examples

Euphorbia dentata often grows in disturbed soils along roadsides and fields.
The leaves of Euphorbia dentata have a distinctively toothed margin.
Farmers try to control the spread of Euphorbia dentata because it competes with crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toothed - having a jagged or serrated edge or margin

Etymology

From New Latin: Euphorbia (the plant genus, named after the Greek physician Euphorbus) and Latin dentata (toothed), referring to its serrated leaves.
