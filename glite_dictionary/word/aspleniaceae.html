Aspleniaceae

1. NOUN

/ æˌspliːniˈeɪsiːˌi / (AmE), / æˌspliːniˈeɪsiːˌi / (BrE)
A family of ferns that includes the genera Asplenium, Pleurosorus, and Schaffneria, sometimes regarded as a subdivision of Polypodiaceae in certain botanical classification systems.
family of ferns including Asplenium

Examples

The Aspleniaceae family contains many common species of ferns found in temperate regions.
Botanists recently revised the classification of Aspleniaceae based on new genetic evidence.
Several ornamental ferns in gardens belong to the Aspleniaceae family.

Etymology

From the genus name Asplenium, ultimately from Greek 'asplēnon' (spleenwort), and the Latin suffix '-aceae' denoting families.

2. NOUN

/ æˌspliːniˈeɪsiːˌiː / (AmE), / æˌspliːniˈeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of ferns, traditionally including the genera Asplenium, Pleurosorus, and Schaffneria, classified in some systems as separate from Polypodiaceae
a family of ferns (plants)

Examples

The Aspleniaceae include many species commonly known as spleenworts.
Botanists have reclassified some ferns into the Aspleniaceae family based on genetic studies.
Members of the Aspleniaceae are often found in temperate and tropical forests.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Polypodiaceae - a large family of ferns to which Aspleniaceae has sometimes been assigned or split from
  • Synonym: Asplenium - a genus of ferns commonly referred to as spleenworts; the type genus of Aspleniaceae

Etymology

From the genus name Asplenium + Latin -aceae (a family suffix in taxonomy).
