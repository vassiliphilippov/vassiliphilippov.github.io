booyong

1. NOUN

/ ˈbuː.jɒŋ / (AmE), / ˈbuː.jɒŋ / (BrE)
A large tree native to Australasia, known for its strong timber and spreading canopy.
large Australasian tree

Examples

The booyong is valued for its durable wood and shade in parks.
Many native birds build their nests in the branches of the booyong.
The booyongs along the river provide important habitat for local wildlife.

Etymology

From a native Australian Aboriginal language name for the tree.
