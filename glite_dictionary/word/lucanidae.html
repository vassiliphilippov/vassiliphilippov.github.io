Lucanidae

1. NOUN

/ luːˈkænɪˌdiː / (AmE), / luːˈkænɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of beetles known as stag beetles, characterized by large mandibles and typically robust bodies.
family of stag beetles
medical

Examples

Scientists collected several Lucanidae specimens from the forest for further study.
The Lucanidae family includes some of the largest beetles in the world.
Enthusiasts prize members of the Lucanidae for their impressive jaws and appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stag beetle - a large beetle with prominent mandibles, often resembling antlers

Etymology

From New Latin 'Lucanus' (a type of beetle) + '-idae' (a zoological family suffix).
