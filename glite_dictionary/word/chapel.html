chapel

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæpəl / (AmE), / ˈtʃæpəl / (BrE)
a religious service held in a place of worship, typically one with its own altar, especially as part of a school or institution
religious service in a chapel

Examples

Every student was required to attend chapel every morning.
He missed chapel and had to explain himself to the headmaster.
After chapel, the school gathered for announcements in the main hall.

Synset Examples

he was late for chapel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: service - a meeting for public worship

Etymology

From Old French 'chapelle', from Medieval Latin 'cappella' (little cloak), referring to the relic cloak of St. Martin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃæpəl / (AmE), / ˈtʃæpəl / (BrE)
A small building or room used for Christian worship, typically with its own altar, and often attached to a larger church or institution.
small place of Christian worship

Examples

The hospital has a small chapel where patients and staff can pray or reflect.
They held their wedding ceremony in a centuries-old chapel overlooking the valley.
Each morning, the students gather in the school chapel for assembly and prayer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: church - a building used for public Christian worship
  • Degree Higher: cathedral - the principal church of a diocese

Etymology

From Old French 'chapel', from Medieval Latin 'capella' (little cloak, chapel).
