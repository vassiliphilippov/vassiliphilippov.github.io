bleary

1. ADJ

/ ˈblɪri / (AmE), / ˈblɪəri / (BrE)
(of the eyes, vision, or a person) looking or feeling unfocused and tired, especially from lack of sleep or exhaustion.
tired or unfocused in appearance

Examples

After staying up all night to finish the project, she arrived at work with bleary eyes.
He rubbed his bleary face, struggling to wake up on Monday morning.
The students looked bleary after cramming for their exams until sunrise.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clear - not blurred; easy to see or understand

2. ADJ

/ ˈblɪri / (AmE), / ˈblɪəri / (BrE)
(of eyes or vision) blurred or indistinct, typically from tiredness or strain.
indistinct or blurred (as vision or eyes)

Examples

When he woke up, his bleary eyes struggled to focus on the clock.
After staring at the screen for hours, everything looked a bit bleary to her.
Through the rain-soaked window, the city lights appeared bleary and distant.

Synset Examples

a landscape of blurred outlines
the trees were just blurry shapes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blurry - unclear or unfocused; not sharply defined
  • Antonym: clear - easy to perceive, hear, or see; unobstructed or sharp

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English *bleren* (to blur, to dim the sight).
