Hindi

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪn.di / (AmE), / ˈhɪn.di / (BrE)
the most widely spoken modern Indic language in northern India, one of the official languages of India, usually written in Devanagari script
official language of India; modern Indic language

Examples

She is fluent in both English and Hindi.
Many Bollywood films are produced in the Hindi language.
Hindi is written in the Devanagari script.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Urdu - a language of southern Asia closely related to Hindi but written in Persian script and with many loanwords from Persian and Arabic
  • Degree Lower: Sanskrit - ancient Indic language from which Hindi descended

Etymology

From Hindi (हिन्दी, hindī), meaning 'of Hind or India', from Persian هندی‎ (hendī).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɪndi / (AmE), / ˈhɪndi / (BrE)
the most widely spoken modern Indic language, used mainly in northern India and written in Devanagari script; one of the official languages of India
major Indic language of northern India

Examples

She is fluent in Hindi and English, which allows her to communicate with people across India.
Many Bollywood movies are produced in Hindi and subtitled in other languages.
My friend offered to teach me how to read and write Hindi.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Urdu - another Indo-Aryan language closely related to Hindi

Etymology

From Persian 'Hindi' meaning 'of India', derived from 'Hind' (India).
