knotty

1. ADJ

/ ˈnɑti / (AmE), / ˈnɒti / (BrE)
(of a tree, piece of wood, or old person) covered with hard lumps or knobs; full of knots.
covered with knobs or knots

Examples

The knotty trunk of the ancient oak was rough to the touch.
Her knotty fingers showed years of hard labor in the fields.
They sat on a knotty wooden bench carved from an old tree.

Synset Examples

gnarled and knotted hands
a knobbed stick

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gnarled - rough and twisted, especially with age (often used of trees or hands)
  • Synonym: knobby - having or covered with small knobs

Etymology

From knot + -y, meaning having knots.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02320915-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈnɑti / (AmE), / ˈnɒti / (BrE)
(of a problem, issue, or situation) extremely complicated or difficult to solve.
very difficult or intricate (problem, issue)

Examples

The lawyer was faced with a knotty legal case that required weeks of research.
Trying to agree on a new policy proved to be a knotty process for the committee.
They spent hours discussing the knotty problems facing the company.

Synset Examples

the Byzantine tax structure
Byzantine methods for holding on to his chairmanship
convoluted legal language
convoluted reasoning
the plot was too involved
a knotty problem
got his way by labyrinthine maneuvering
Oh, what a tangled web we weave
tortuous legal procedures
tortuous negotiations lasting for months

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complex - not easy to analyze or understand; complicated
  • Antonym: simple - easily understood or done; not complicated

Etymology

From Middle English *knotty*, from *knot* (a tied loop or tangle) + *-y*.

3. ADJ

/ ˈnɑti / (AmE), / ˈnɒti / (BrE)
tangled and twisted into knots or snarls; full of knots.
tangled or full of knots

Examples

The knotty string was impossible to unravel without scissors.
She spent hours working out the knotty mess in her shoelaces.
A knotty mass of hair formed after several days without brushing.

Synset Examples

a mass of knotted string
snarled thread

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tangled - twisted together untidily; matted
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface; not tangled

Etymology

From knot + -y, Old English cnotta (knot).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00256586-s

4. ADJ

/ ˈnɑːti / (AmE), / ˈnɒti / (BrE)
(of a problem or situation) difficult to solve or understand; complex and troublesome.
difficult or complicated to solve

Examples

The committee struggled for hours with the knotty questions about funding.
She found herself confronted with a knotty dilemma that had no easy answers.
Resolving the inheritance dispute proved more knotty than anyone expected.

Synset Examples

a baffling problem
I faced the knotty problem of what to have for breakfast
a problematic situation at home

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complex - consisting of many different and connected parts; not easy to analyze or understand
  • Antonym: simple - easily understood or done; presenting no difficulty

Etymology

From knot + -y, referring metaphorically to the complexity of a knot.
