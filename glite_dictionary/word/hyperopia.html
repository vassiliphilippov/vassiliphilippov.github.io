hyperopia

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪ.pəˈroʊ.pi.ə / (AmE), / ˌhaɪ.pəˈrəʊ.pi.ə / (BrE)
a condition of the eye in which distant objects are seen more clearly than near objects, commonly known as farsightedness
farsightedness; clearer vision for distant objects
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with hyperopia after she complained of trouble reading small print.
Children with hyperopia may not notice issues until school tasks require close vision.
Corrective lenses can greatly improve quality of life for people with hyperopia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: farsightedness - the ability to see distant objects more clearly than those near at hand; another term for hyperopia
  • Antonym: myopia - a condition where close objects are seen clearly but distant objects appear blurred; nearsightedness

Etymology

From Greek hyper- (over) + ōps, ōp- (eye).
