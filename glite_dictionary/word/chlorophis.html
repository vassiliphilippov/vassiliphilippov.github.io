Chlorophis

1. NOUN

/ klɔːˈrɑːfɪs / (AmE), / klɔːˈrɒfɪs / (BrE)
A genus of African green snakes belonging to the family Colubridae.
genus of African green snakes

Examples

The Chlorophis genus includes several strikingly green African snakes.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the Chlorophis genus in Central Africa.
Many Chlorophis snakes are arboreal, living primarily in forested regions.

Etymology

From Greek 'chloros' (green) and 'ophis' (snake), reflecting their coloration and family.
