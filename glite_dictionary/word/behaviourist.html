behaviourist

1. NOUN

/ bəˈheɪvjərɪst / (AmE), / bɪˈheɪvjərɪst / (BrE)
A psychologist who studies and applies behaviorism, focusing on observable behaviors rather than internal mental states.
psychologist subscribing to behaviorism

Examples

The behaviourist argued that only observable actions should be studied in psychology.
As a behaviourist, she focused on reward and punishment in shaping children's habits.
Many behaviourists reject introspection as a valid scientific method.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: behaviorist - psychologist subscribing to behaviorism (American spelling)

Etymology

From 'behaviour' (British spelling of 'behavior') + '-ist' (person who practices).

2. ADJ

/ bɪˈheɪvjɚrɪst / (AmE), / bɪˈheɪvjərɪst / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of behaviorism, a theory in psychology that focuses on observable behaviors rather than internal mental processes.
relating to behaviorism theory

Examples

The new therapy program takes a strictly behaviourist approach to treating phobias.
Her research is grounded in behaviourist principles and rejects introspection.
Critics argue that behaviourist models overlook important mental processes.

Synset Examples

behavioristic psychology

Related Terms

  • Synonym: behavioristic - relating to behaviorism or its principles

Etymology

Formed from 'behaviour' + '-ist', referring to the psychological theory of behaviorism.
