Tripleurospermum

1. NOUN

/ trɪpˌljuːroʊˈspɜrməm / (AmE), / trɪpˌljuːərəˈspɜːməm / (BrE)
A small genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, often included in the genus Matricaria.
genus of flowering plants (daisies)

Examples

Tripleurospermum species are commonly found in temperate regions across Europe and Asia.
The white petals of Tripleurospermum flowers resemble those of camomile.
Botanists sometimes classify Tripleurospermum under the genus Matricaria due to their similarities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Matricaria - genus of flowering plants related to Tripleurospermum

Etymology

From Greek 'tri-' (three), 'pleura' (side), and 'sperma' (seed), referring to seed shape.

2. NOUN

/ ˌtrɪpliˌʊroʊˈspɜːrməm / (AmE), / ˌtrɪpljʊərəˈspɜːməm / (BrE)
A small genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, often included in the genus Matricaria, known for their daisy-like appearance.
genus of daisy-like flowering plants

Examples

Tripleurospermum is commonly known as false chamomile in many regions.
Several species within the Tripleurospermum genus display white petals and yellow centers.
Botanists sometimes classify Tripleurospermum together with Matricaria due to their similarities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Matricaria - related genus of daisy-like plants

Etymology

From Greek 'triplous' (triple) + 'oura' (tail) + 'sperma' (seed), referring to the seed's structure.
