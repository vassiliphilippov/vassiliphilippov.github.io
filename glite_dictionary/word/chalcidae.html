Chalcidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈkælsɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˈkalkɪdi / (BrE)
A family of small parasitic wasps known as chalcidflies, many of which lay eggs in or on other insects.
family of chalcidflies (wasps)

Examples

The Chalcidae are known for their role in controlling pest populations as natural parasites.
Many species within the Chalcidae family have metallic coloring.
Biologists collected samples from the Chalcidae to study their parasitic behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chalcidfly - a small wasp of the family Chalcidae

Etymology

From New Latin Chalcididae, from genus Chalcis, used in taxonomic classification.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkælsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈkalkɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small parasitic wasps, commonly known as chalcidflies, within the order Hymenoptera.
family of small parasitic wasps

Examples

The Chalcidae are known for their diverse parasitic behavior among insects.
Many species within the Chalcidae are important for biological pest control.
Researchers discovered a new member of the Chalcidae family in the tropical rainforest.

Etymology

From Greek khalkís (a bronze-colored insect), referring to the metallic appearance of some species.
