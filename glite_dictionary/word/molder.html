to molder

1. VERB

/ ˈmoʊldər / (AmE), / ˈməʊldə / (BrE)
to decay or disintegrate gradually, especially through the effects of moisture, time, or neglect
decay or disintegrate gradually

Examples

If neglected, the old wooden fence will molder in the rain.
The abandoned house had started to molder after years of disuse.
Books left in the damp basement began to molder and fall apart.

Synset Examples

The bodies decomposed in the heat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to decompose - to break down into basic components through decay
  • Synonym: decay - to undergo decomposition or rot
  • Antonym: preserve - to prevent decay or decomposition

Etymology

From Middle English 'molderen', probably of Scandinavian origin; compare Danish 'mulde' (to turn to dust).
