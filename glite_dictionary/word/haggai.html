Haggai

1. PROPN

/ ˈhæɡaɪ / (AmE), / ˈhæɡaɪ / (BrE)
A Hebrew minor prophet of the 6th century BCE, the first of three post-exile prophets, and the author of the Book of Haggai in the Hebrew Bible.
Hebrew prophet and biblical book author

Examples

The Book of Haggai encourages the people of Judah to rebuild the Temple in Jerusalem.
Haggai prophesied during the reign of King Darius of Persia.
Many scholars study the messages of Haggai alongside other post-exilic prophets.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Zechariah - A Hebrew prophet and second of the three post-exile prophets
  • Degree Higher: Malachi - A Hebrew prophet and third of the three post-exile prophets

Etymology

From Hebrew Ḥaggay, meaning 'festive' or 'my feast'.

2. PROPN

/ ˈhæɡ.aɪ / (AmE), / ˈhæɡ.aɪ / (BrE)
A book of the Old Testament containing the prophecies of Haggai, focusing on the rebuilding of the Temple after the Babylonian Captivity.
Old Testament book of prophecies

Examples

Many scholars have studied the Book of Haggai to understand its historical context.
In his sermon, the pastor referenced Haggai to emphasize the importance of fulfilling one's promises.
Haggai is one of the shortest books in the Old Testament, containing only two chapters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Zechariah - Book of the Old Testament containing prophecies contemporaneous with Haggai
  • Synonym: Malachi - Book of the Old Testament that follows Haggai and Zechariah

Etymology

From Hebrew Ḥaggay, meaning 'festive' or 'my feast'.
