phenylpropanolamine

1. NOUN

/ ˌfiːnəlˌproʊpəˈnæləˌmiːn / (AmE), / ˌfiːnɪlˌprəʊpənˈaləˌmiːn / (BrE)
A synthetic compound used as a decongestant and appetite suppressant, which acts on adrenergic receptors; formerly used to relieve allergies and respiratory conditions.
decongestant and appetite suppressant drug
medical

Examples

The over-the-counter medication was recalled because it contained phenylpropanolamine.
Several cold remedies once relied on phenylpropanolamine to relieve nasal congestion.
The use of phenylpropanolamine has declined due to concerns about possible side effects.

Synset Examples

drugs containing phenylpropanolamine are being recalled

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decongestant - a medicine that relieves nasal congestion
  • Synonym: pseudoephedrine - a similar drug used as a decongestant

Etymology

From 'phenyl-' (a chemical group derived from benzene) + 'propanol' (an alcohol) + 'amine' (an organic compound containing a nitrogen atom).
