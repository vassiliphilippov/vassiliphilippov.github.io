Carpinaceae

1. NOUN

/ kɑr.pɪˈneɪ.si.i / (AmE), / kɑː.pɪˈneɪ.sɪ.iː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants that includes the genera Carpinus and Ostryopsis, used in some classification systems
a plant family including hornbeams and allies

Examples

The genus Carpinus belongs to the Carpinaceae family in certain botanical systems.
Some older texts refer to the hornbeams under Carpinaceae rather than Betulaceae.
Ostryopsis is often placed in the Carpinaceae when classified according to traditional systems.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Carpinus (the genus hornbeam) + -aceae (a standard suffix for plant families).
