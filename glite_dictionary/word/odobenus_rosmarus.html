Odobenus rosmarus

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊdəˈbiːnəs rɒsˈmeɪrəs / (AmE), / ˌəʊdəˈbiːnəs rɒzˈmarəs / (BrE)
The scientific name for the walrus, a large marine mammal native to northern Atlantic and Arctic waters, known for its long tusks and whiskers.
the scientific name for the walrus

Examples

Scientists have studied Odobenus rosmarus to learn more about Arctic marine ecosystems.
The thick blubber of Odobenus rosmarus provides insulation in freezing waters.
Conservation efforts focus on protecting the habitat of Odobenus rosmarus from climate change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walrus - a large Arctic marine mammal with tusks and whiskers

Etymology

From Latinized New Latin; 'Odobenus' from Greek 'odous, odontos' (tooth) and 'baino' (to walk), 'rosmarus' Latinized form of Scandinavian 'rosmhvalr' (walrus).
