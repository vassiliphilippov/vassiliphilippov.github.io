Neuroptera

1. NOUN

/ nuˈrɑptərə / (AmE), / njʊˈrɒptərə / (BrE)
an order of insects characterized by net-veined wings, including lacewings, antlions, dobsonflies, and their relatives
order of insects with net-veined wings

Examples

The Neuroptera are easily recognized by their delicate, lace-like wings.
Entomologists studied various species within the order Neuroptera for their ecological importance.
Some members of the Neuroptera are beneficial predators in garden ecosystems.

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'neuron' (nerve) + 'pteron' (wing), referring to the veined wings.

2. NOUN

/ nuˈrɑptərə / (AmE), / njʊˈrɒptərə / (BrE)
an order of insects including lacewings, antlions, dobsonflies, alderflies, fish flies, mantispids, and spongeflies, characterized by net-veined wings and a predatory larval stage
insect order: lacewings, antlions, etc.
medical

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species within the Neuroptera in the Amazon rainforest.
The delicate wings of insects in the Neuroptera are often compared to lace.
Entomology students are required to study the life cycle of Neuroptera for their final exam.

Etymology

From Greek neuron (nerve) + pteron (wing), referring to the network of veins in the wings.
