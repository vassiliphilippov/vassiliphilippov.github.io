Hygrophorus kauffmanii

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌɡrɑːfərəs kɔːfˈmænaɪ / (AmE), / haɪˌɡrɒfərəs kɔːfˈmænaɪ / (BrE)
A species of fungus with a slightly sticky cap; both the cap and gills are reddish brown, while the stalk is grey.
a species of reddish-brown fungus

Examples

Mushroom foragers identified the specimen as Hygrophorus kauffmanii due to its characteristic reddish-brown cap.
The stalk of Hygrophorus kauffmanii is typically grey, helping to distinguish it from similar species.
During autumn, Hygrophorus kauffmanii can often be found growing in damp woodland areas.

Etymology

Named after the mycologist Calvin H. Kauffman; genus name from Greek 'hygros' (moist) and 'phoros' (bearing).
