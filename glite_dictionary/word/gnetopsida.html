Gnetopsida

1. NOUN

/ niːˌtɑːpˈsaɪdə / (AmE), / nɛˌtɒpˈsɪdə / (BrE)
A class of gymnospermous flowering plants, considered to be an evolutionary link between conifers and angiosperms; classified as a class or subdivision in different botanical systems.
a class of gymnospermous plants

Examples

Gnetopsida are a unique group of seed plants that have long puzzled botanists regarding their evolutionary relationships.
Some botanists argue that Gnetopsida represent an important transitional form between conifers and flowering plants.
The differences in vessel structure make Gnetopsida distinct from other gymnosperms.

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on the genus Gnetum + Greek 'opsis' (appearance) + -ida (taxonomic suffix).
