nonviable

1. ADJ

/ nɑnˈvaɪəbəl / (AmE), / nɒnˈvaɪəbl / (BrE)
not capable of living, developing, or succeeding; unable to survive or function as intended
not able to survive or succeed

Examples

The experiment was halted when the embryo was found to be nonviable.
Without sufficient funding, the project became nonviable within a few months.
They decided to close the factory because it was financially nonviable.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: viable - capable of living or developing successfully

Etymology

From the prefix 'non-' (not) and 'viable', ultimately from Latin 'vita' (life).
