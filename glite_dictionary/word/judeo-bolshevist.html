Judeo-Bolshevist

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒuːdioʊˈboʊlʃəvɪst / (AmE), / ˌdʒuːdiəʊˈbɒlʃəvɪst / (BrE)
A person or an ideology purportedly representing a blend of Jewish and Bolshevik (communist) influence, often used in an anti-Semitic or anti-communist context.
anti-Semitic, anti-communist label
offensive

Examples

The propaganda campaign labeled political opponents as Judeo-Bolshevists to spread fear and paranoia.
Historical documents often reference the supposed threat of Judeo-Bolshevists in the early 20th century.
Using the term Judeo-Bolshevist reveals the speaker's adherence to extremist and prejudiced ideologies.

Synset Examples

The term Judeo-Bolshevist is often used in extremist rhetoric.
He was labeled a Judeo-Bolshevist for his radical views.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bolshevik - a member of the Russian Communist Party
  • Synonym: anti-Semitic - hostile to or prejudiced against Jewish people

Etymology

Formed from 'Judeo-' (Jewish) and 'Bolshevist' (supporter of Bolshevism, from Russian 'bol'shevik', majority).
