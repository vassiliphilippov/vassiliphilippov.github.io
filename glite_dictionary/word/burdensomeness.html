burdensomeness

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɝːdənˌsəmnəs / (AmE), / ˈbɜːdənˌsəmnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being burdensome; the state of causing hardship or difficulty.
the quality of being burdensome

Examples

The burdensomeness of the new regulations discouraged small businesses from expanding.
She was frustrated by the burdensomeness of her daily commute.
Many students complained about the burdensomeness of excessive homework assignments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burden - a heavy load or responsibility
  • Antonym: ease - the state of being comfortable or free from difficulty

Etymology

From 'burdensome' + '-ness', denoting the state or quality of being burdensome.
