to disband

1. VERB

/ dɪsˈbænd / (AmE), / dɪsˈbænd / (BrE)
(of an organization, group, or team) to stop functioning or existing as a unit; to break up formally.
to break up or cease to function (as a group)

Examples

After ten successful years, the band decided to disband and pursue solo careers.
The committee will disband once its final report is published next month.
Several activist groups were forced to disband due to a lack of funding.

Synset Examples

The political wing of the party dissolved after much internal fighting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dissolve - to officially end (an organization or body)
  • Synonym: to break up - to separate into parts; to end a group or organization

Etymology

From Middle French 'desbander' (to disband, break up), from 'des-' (dis-) + 'bander' (to bind).

2. VERB

/ dɪsˈbænd / (AmE), / dɪsˈbænd / (BrE)
to cause (an organization or group) to break up or stop functioning; to dissolve or dismiss
cause a group to break up

Examples

The city council decided to disband the committee after it finished its work.
After many years of service, the charity was officially disbanded.
The coach announced that he would disband the team due to lack of funding.

Synset Examples

the principal disbanded the political student organization

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dissolve - to officially end a group, organization, or legal entity
  • Antonym: to assemble - to come together as a group

Etymology

From dis- (apart) + band (a group of people).
