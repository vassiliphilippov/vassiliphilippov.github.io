Centrolobium robustum

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛntrəˈloʊbiəm roʊˈbʌstəm / (AmE), / ˌsɛntrəˈləʊbiəm rəʊˈbʌstəm / (BrE)
a Brazilian tree valued for its attractively marked wood, used in furniture and fine woodworking
Brazilian tree with attractive wood

Examples

The richly colored wood of Centrolobium robustum is prized by cabinetmakers.
Conservationists are concerned about overharvesting Centrolobium robustum in Brazilian forests.
Furniture crafted from Centrolobium robustum displays a striking and unique grain pattern.

Etymology

From Latin: 'centro-' (center) + 'lobium' (pod/lobe), and 'robustum' meaning 'strong'.
