Scleranthus annuus

1. NOUN

/ sklɪˈɹænθəs əˈnuː.əs / (AmE), / sklɪəˈɹænθəs əˈnjuː.əs / (BrE)
A widely distributed low-growing Eurasian herb with narrow leaves and inconspicuous green flowers, commonly found in dry, sandy soils.
species of low-growing Eurasian herb

Examples

Scleranthus annuus thrives in dry, sandy soils across much of Europe and Asia.
The tiny green flowers of Scleranthus annuus are often overlooked by casual observers.
Botanists observed that Scleranthus annuus is well-adapted to nutrient-poor environments.

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Scleranthus' (from Greek 'skleros' meaning hard and 'anthos' meaning flower) and Latin 'annuus' meaning annual.
