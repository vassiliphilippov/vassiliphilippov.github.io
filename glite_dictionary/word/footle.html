to footle

1. VERB

/ ˈfuːtəl / (AmE), / ˈfuːt(ə)l / (BrE)
to act or talk in a silly or foolish way; to waste time with trivial or nonsensical behavior
act or talk foolishly

Examples

Stop footling around and help me with this task.
He tends to footle when he's nervous, talking about nothing in particular.
They spent the afternoon footling about in the park instead of working.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fool around - to behave in a silly way; to waste time
  • Synonym: trifle - to treat something without seriousness or respect; act frivolously

Etymology

Probably from 'footy' (foolish) + -le (frequentative suffix), late 19th century.

2. VERB

/ ˈfuːtl̩ / (AmE), / ˈfuːtl̩ / (BrE)
to move or wander about a place aimlessly or without any clear purpose
move aimlessly without purpose

Examples

The boys decided to footle around the park instead of going home after school.
She spent the afternoon footling about the city, not really doing anything productive.
You can't just footle here all day; you need to find something useful to do.

Synset Examples

The high school students like to loiter in the Central Square
Who is this man that is hanging around the department?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to loiter - to stand or wait around idly or without apparent purpose
  • Synonym: to wander - to walk or move in a leisurely or aimless way

Etymology

Origin uncertain; related to the noun 'footle', meaning nonsense, possibly influenced by 'foot'.
