rosaceous

1. ADJ

/ roʊˈzeɪʃəs / (AmE), / rəʊˈzeɪʃəs / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of plants of the rose family (Rosaceae).
relating to the rose family (Rosaceae)

Examples

Many fruits we eat, such as apples and cherries, come from rosaceous plants.
The garden's rosaceous shrubs bloomed beautifully in early summer.
Some rosaceous species are prized for their ornamental flowers.

Etymology

From Latin 'rosaceus' (of roses) + '-ous'.

2. ADJ

/ roʊˈzeɪʃəs / (AmE), / rəʊˈzeɪʃəs / (BrE)
having a dusty purplish pink color, similar to that of some roses.
dusty purplish pink color

Examples

The evening sky turned a rosaceous hue as the sun began to set.
She chose a rosaceous fabric for the curtains to brighten the living room.
The artist blended rosaceous tones into the portrait's background.

Synset Examples

the roseate glow of dawn

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roseate - having a pink or rose-like color

Etymology

From Latin 'rosaceus', meaning 'of a rose'.
