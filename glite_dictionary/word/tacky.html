tacky

1. ADJ

/ ˈtæki / (AmE), / ˈtaki / (BrE)
showy in a cheap or tasteless way; lacking style or good taste
cheaply showy; tasteless

Examples

Her outfit was so tacky that it turned heads for all the wrong reasons.
They decorated the room with tacky plastic flowers and glitter everywhere.
His new sports car looked tacky with its bright neon stripes and oversized spoiler.

Synset Examples

a flash car
a flashy ring
garish colors
a gaudy costume
loud sport shirts
a meretricious yet stylish book
tawdry ornaments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gaudy - excessively bright or showy, typically so as to be tasteless
  • Synonym: flashy - ostentatiously attractive or impressive, often tastelessly so
  • Antonym: tasteful - showing good aesthetic judgment or appropriate style

2. ADJ

/ ˈtæki / (AmE), / ˈtæki / (BrE)
(of a substance, especially paint or varnish) not completely dry and slightly sticky to the touch.
not fully dry and slightly sticky

Examples

Avoid touching the tacky surface until the paint has fully dried.
The glue remained tacky for several hours after application.
He accidentally left fingerprints in the tacky varnish on the newly finished table.

Synset Examples

tacky varnish

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sticky - having the property of adhering or sticking to a surface
  • Antonym: dry - free from moisture or liquid; not wet or sticky

Synset POS: s
