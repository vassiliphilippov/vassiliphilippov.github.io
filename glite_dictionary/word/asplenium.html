Asplenium

1. NOUN

/ æˈspliːniəm / (AmE), / æˈspliːniəm / (BrE)
a genus of about 700 species of ferns, some commonly called spleenworts, in the family Aspleniaceae.
genus of ferns (spleenworts)

Examples

The Asplenium genus includes the popular bird's nest fern found in many homes.
Several species of Asplenium thrive in the moist understory of temperate forests.
She studied the unique spores produced by Asplenium ferns for her research project.

Etymology

From Greek asplēnion, a name for the spleenwort fern.

2. NOUN

/ āsˈpliːniəm / (AmE), / æsˈpliːniəm / (BrE)
A genus of about 700 species of ferns, commonly known as spleenworts, in the family Aspleniaceae.
a genus of ferns (spleenworts)

Examples

The fern species Asplenium nidus is often grown as a houseplant for its graceful, arching fronds.
Researchers discovered a new Asplenium species in the tropical rainforest.
Many members of the Asplenium genus are known as spleenworts because of their historical medicinal uses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spleenwort - common name for ferns of the genus Asplenium

Etymology

From Greek *asplēnion*, a kind of spleenwort, from *splēn* (spleen), due to former medicinal use.
