4. ADV

/ ˈrɪli / (AmE), / ˈriːli / (BrE)
in a way that reflects the truth or facts; actually or genuinely
actually; in fact; truthfully

Examples

Do you really believe those rumors about him?
She was surprised to find the city really beautiful in the spring.
I'm really not interested in going to that concert.

Synset Examples

she was now truly American
a genuinely open society
they don't really listen to us

Related Terms

  • Synonym: truly - in a truthful or genuine way
  • Synonym: actually - as the truth or facts of a situation
  • Antonym: falsely - not in accordance with truth or fact

Etymology

From real + -ly, after Middle English realli, from Old French real, from Latin realis (actual, real).
