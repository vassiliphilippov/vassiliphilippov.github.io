acmeism

1. NOUN

/ ˈæk.miˌɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈak.miˌɪz.əm / (BrE)
A Russian school of poetry from the early 20th century that emphasized clarity, concrete imagery, and precise language, in contrast to Symbolism.
Russian 20th-century poetic movement emphasizing clarity

Examples

The rise of acmeism transformed Russian poetry by focusing on concrete images and direct expression.
Anna Akhmatova was a leading figure in the acmeism movement of the early twentieth century.
Many modern critics compare acmeism with the Imagist movement in English poetry.

Synset Examples

Amongst the major acmeist poets, each interpreted acmeism in a different stylistic light, from Akhmatova's intimate poems on topics of love and relationships to Gumilev's narrative verse.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: symbolism - A late 19th-century poetry movement emphasizing suggestion and mood
  • Synonym: imagism - An early 20th-century English/American poetic movement emphasizing clarity and imagery

Etymology

From Russian акмеизм (akmeism), from Greek akmē (the highest point, peak).
