hereditary

1. ADJ

/ həˈrɛdəˌtɛri / (AmE), / hɪˈrɛdɪtəri / (BrE)
(of a characteristic, right, or title) passed from parent to offspring, especially by legal or established means of inheritance.
passed down by inheritance

Examples

In a hereditary monarchy, power is passed down from one generation to the next.
She was diagnosed with a hereditary condition that affected several members of her family.
Ownership of the estate remained a hereditary right for centuries.

Synset Examples

ancestral home
ancestral lore
hereditary monarchy
patrimonial estate
transmissible tradition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inherited - received from someone after their death, especially property or money
  • Antonym: acquired - gained by one's own efforts rather than inherited

Etymology

From Latin hereditarius, from hereditas (inheritance).

2. ADJ

/ həˈrɛdəˌtɛri / (AmE), / hɪˈrɛdɪtəri / (BrE)
(of a trait, disease, or characteristic) passed from parent to offspring through the genes.
passed down by genes

Examples

Many forms of cancer are considered hereditary and can run in families.
The disease has a hereditary pattern, often affecting several generations.
Researchers are exploring how hereditary factors influence the risk of heart disease.

Synset Examples

an inherited disease
familial traits
genetically transmitted features

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inherited - (of a trait or disease) received by genetic transmission
  • Degree Lower: congenital - (of a disease or physical abnormality) present from birth, not necessarily inherited
  • Antonym: acquired - (of a trait or disease) not inherited but developed after birth

Etymology

From Latin hereditarius (heir, inheritance), from hereditas (inheritance).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01317491-s
Synset POS: s
