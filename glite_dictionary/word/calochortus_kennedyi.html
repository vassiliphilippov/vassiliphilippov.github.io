Calochortus kennedyi

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæləˈkɔrtəs ˈkɛnədaɪ / (AmE), / ˌkæləˈkɔːtəs ˈkɛnɪdi / (BrE)
A species of mariposa lily native to southern California, Arizona, and Mexico, known for clusters of bell-shaped vermilion, orange, or yellow flowers on short stems.
a species of mariposa lily with vivid flowers

Examples

The desert was ablaze with blooming Calochortus kennedyi after the spring rains.
Gardeners prize Calochortus kennedyi for its striking orange and red blossoms.
We spotted clusters of Calochortus kennedyi while hiking in southern Arizona.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mariposa lily - a group of flowering plants in the genus Calochortus

Etymology

From Greek kalos (beautiful) + chortos (grass), genus name; kennedyi honors William L. Kennedy.
