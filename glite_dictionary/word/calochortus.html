Calochortus

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæləˈkɔrtəs / (AmE), / ˌkæləʊˈkɔːtəs / (BrE)
A genus of leafy-stemmed bulbous herbs native to western North America, known for their showy, often tulip-like flowers.
genus of western North American flowering plants

Examples

Many species in the Calochortus genus are commonly called mariposa lilies due to their butterfly-like petals.
During our hike in California, we were lucky to spot several blooming Calochortus along the trail.
Botanists study Calochortus to understand the diversity of wildflowers in western North America.

Etymology

From Greek kalos (beautiful) + chortos (grass), referencing the plant's attractive appearance.

2. NOUN

/ ˌkæləˈkɔrtəs / (AmE), / ˌkæləkˈɔːtəs / (BrE)
A genus of western North American leafy-stemmed bulbous herbs in the lily family, including various species known as mariposa lilies.
genus of western North American herbs

Examples

Several species in the Calochortus genus are commonly called mariposa lilies.
The Calochortus plants thrive in dry, open habitats across western North America.
Botanists have discovered new variations within the Calochortus genus in recent years.

Etymology

From Greek 'kalos' (beautiful) + 'chortos' (grass); name given because of the plants' attractive appearance.
