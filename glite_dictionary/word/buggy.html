buggy

1. ADJ

/ ˈbʌɡi / (AmE), / ˈbʌɡi / (BrE)

infested with bugs or insects

Examples

2. ADJ

/ ˈbʌɡi / (AmE), / ˈbʌɡi / (BrE)
(informal) Mentally unstable, eccentric, or slightly crazy.
informal; crazy or eccentric
informal

Examples

After three sleepless nights, she started acting a bit buggy around the office.
His buggy ideas always make the team laugh, even if they rarely make sense.
Don't pay attention to his buggy behavior—he's always been a little odd.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crazy - mentally deranged or wildly foolish
  • Antonym: sane - of sound mind; not mentally ill

Etymology

Possibly from 'bug', an old slang term for crazy, combined with the suffix '-y'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈbʌɡi / (AmE), / ˈbʌɡi / (BrE)
A small, light carriage for one or two people, typically drawn by a single horse.
small horse-drawn carriage

Examples

The old farm kept an antique buggy in the barn, still in good condition.
They rode through the countryside in a horse-drawn buggy.
In the museum, you can see a restored buggy from the 19th century.
