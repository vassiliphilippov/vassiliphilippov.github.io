to vowelise

1. VERB

/ ˈvaʊ.əˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvaʊ.ə.laɪz / (BrE)
to pronounce or treat as a vowel, especially in linguistics and phonetics
pronounce as a vowel (linguistics)

Examples

Linguists study how certain consonants can vowelise in rapid speech.
The liquid 'r' sometimes vowelises between two other consonants.
In this dialect, the consonant may be vowelised depending on its position in the word.

Synset Examples

between two consonants, this liquid is vowelized

Related Terms

  • Antonym: consonantalize - to pronounce or treat as a consonant

Etymology

From 'vowel' + '-ise', modelled after similar English formation with the suffix '-ise' (British spelling of '-ize').
