significantly

1. ADV

/ sɪɡˈnɪfɪkəntli / (AmE), / sɪɡˈnɪfɪkəntli / (BrE)
In a way that is important, large, or noticeable enough to have an effect.
in an important or noticeable way

Examples

The company's profits increased significantly last year despite the economic downturn.
Her mood improved significantly after she received the good news.
The medication significantly reduces the risk of heart attack.

Synset Examples

our budget will be significantly affected by these new cuts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substantially - to a great extent; considerably

Etymology

Formed from 'significant' + '-ly'; 'significant' from Latin significare (to signify).

2. ADV

/ sɪɡˈnɪfɪkəntli / (AmE), / sɪɡˈnɪfɪkəntli / (BrE)
In a way that is statistically significant; to an extent that is unlikely to have occurred by chance.
in a statistically significant way

Examples

The treatment group improved significantly compared to the control group.
The test results differ significantly from previous studies.
There was no significantly higher risk found in the analysis.

Synset Examples

the two groups differed significantly

Related Terms

  • Antonym: insignificantly - in a way that lacks statistical significance

Etymology

From "significant" (meaning important or meaningful) + "-ly".

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ sɪɡˈnɪfɪkəntli / (AmE), / sɪɡˈnɪfɪkəntli / (BrE)
In a way that is important or large enough to have an effect; to a notable degree.
in an important or notable way

Examples

The company's profits increased significantly after the new product launch.
Her health has improved significantly since she started the new medication.
There were significantly fewer errors in the revised report.

Synset Examples

more importantly, Weber held that the manifold meaning attached to the event by the social scientist could alter his definition of the concrete event itself

Related Terms

  • Synonym: substantially - to a large degree or extent
  • Antonym: marginally - in a minor or insignificant way

Etymology

From 'significant' + '-ly'; 'significant' from Latin significare (to signify).
