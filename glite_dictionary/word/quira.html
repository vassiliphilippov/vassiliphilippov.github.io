quira

1. NOUN

/ ˈkiː.rə / (AmE), / ˈkwiː.rə / (BrE)
Any of several tropical American trees, some of which produce economically important timber.
tropical American tree

Examples

The dense wood of the quira is highly valued in furniture making.
Many local communities rely on quira trees for timber and shade.
Botanists documented several species of quira in the rainforest expedition.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkiːrə / (AmE), / ˈkiːrə / (BrE)
A hard, heavy red wood obtained from the quira tree, used especially in carpentry and construction.
hard, heavy red wood from the quira tree

Examples

The furniture was crafted from solid quira, giving it a distinctive reddish hue.
Many builders prize quira for its durability and weight in construction projects.
Artisans often choose quira when they need wood that will last for generations.
