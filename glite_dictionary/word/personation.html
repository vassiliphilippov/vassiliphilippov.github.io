personation

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɜːrsəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɜːsəˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of portraying or representing a character on stage through speech, action, and gesture
acting or portraying a character on stage

Examples

Her personation of Lady Macbeth was praised for its intensity and depth.
The actor's skilled personation brought the historical figure vividly to life on stage.
Classical theatre valued precise personation as essential to a convincing performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impersonation - the act of pretending to be another person, especially for entertainment or fraud

Etymology

From Latin 'persona' (mask, character played by an actor) + English '-ation'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɜr.səˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɜː.səˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of imitating the behaviour or mannerisms of another person, often in order to deceive or entertain
imitating another person's mannerisms

Examples

His personation of the famous actor was so accurate that the audience burst into laughter.
The magician was skilled in the personation of various historical figures.
Legal charges were filed for the personation of a police officer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impersonation - the act of pretending to be another person to fool others
  • Synonym: mimicry - the act of imitating someone or something

Etymology

From Latin persona (character, person) + -ation (forming nouns indicating action or process).
