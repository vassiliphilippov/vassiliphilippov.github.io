ruffled

1. ADJ

/ ˈrʌfəld / (AmE), / ˈrʌfəld / (BrE)
(of fabric or clothing) having decorative ruffles or frills, typically along the edges.
having decorative ruffles or frills

Examples

She wore a beautiful dress with ruffled sleeves to the party.
The curtains were made of ruffled white linen that added elegance to the room.
He gave his sister a ruffled apron as a birthday gift.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frilled - decorated with a narrow strip of gathered or pleated material
  • Antonym: plain - without decoration or ornamentation

Etymology

From the verb 'ruffle', meaning to ornament with frills or gathers.

2. ADJ

/ ˈrʌfəld / (AmE), / ˈrʌfəld / (BrE)
(of a surface or material) made uneven or wavy, typically by the wind or movement.
having a wavy or uneven surface

Examples

The strong breeze left the pond's surface ruffled and sparkling in the sunlight.
She admired the ruffled edges of the silk scarf blowing in the wind.
After the storm, the ruffled banners hung loosely from the flagpole.

Synset Examples

the rippled surface of the pond
with ruffled flags flying

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rippling - forming small waves on the surface, especially of water or fabric
  • Antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface; free from waves or projections
