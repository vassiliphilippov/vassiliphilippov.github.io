arteria intercostalis

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈtɪəriə ˌɪntərkɒˈsteɪlɪs / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪəriə ˌɪntəkɒˈstɑːlɪs / (BrE)
Any of several arteries that supply blood to the intercostal spaces between the ribs.
artery supplying spaces between ribs
medical

Examples

The arteria intercostalis provides oxygenated blood to the muscles between the ribs.
Damage to an arteria intercostalis can result in significant bleeding during chest injuries.
During surgery, care must be taken not to cut the arteria intercostalis.

Etymology

From Latin: 'arteria' (artery) + 'intercostalis' (between the ribs).
