scaphopod

1. NOUN

/ ˈskæfəˌpɑd / (AmE), / ˈskafəpɒd / (BrE)
A burrowing marine mollusk of the class Scaphopoda, having a tubular shell open at both ends, commonly known as a tusk shell.
marine mollusk with a tubular shell
medical

Examples

The scaphopod uses its long, tubular shell to burrow into sandy sea bottoms.
Scientists collected several scaphopods while studying marine sediment samples.
A scaphopod is easily identified by its tusk-like shell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tusk shell - another common name for scaphopod mollusks

Etymology

From Greek 'skaphē' (boat) + 'pous, podos' (foot), referring to the boat-shaped foot of the mollusk.
