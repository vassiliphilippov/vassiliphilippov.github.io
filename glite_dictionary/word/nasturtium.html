Nasturtium

1. NOUN

/ nəˈstɜrʃəm / (AmE), / nəˈstɜːʃəm / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the family Brassicaceae, including watercress and related species.
genus of herbs in the Brassicaceae family

Examples

The stream was lined with wild Nasturtium plants, their green leaves trailing in the water.
Botanists debated whether this variety belonged to the Nasturtium genus or a related group.
The salad included leaves from the Nasturtium, prized for their peppery taste.

Etymology

From Latin *nasturtium*, meaning 'nose twist', referring to its pungent smell.

2. NOUN

/ næˈstɜːrʃəm / (AmE), / næˈstɜː.tjʊm / (BrE)
A small genus of plants in the family Brassicaceae, including several species of aquatic or semi-aquatic herbs known for their pungent, edible leaves and stems.
genus of pungent, edible plants

Examples

Nasturtium species are often found growing along streams and in wet meadows.
Cooks use the young leaves and stems of Nasturtium as a peppery addition to salads.
The botanist collected several samples of Nasturtium for analysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watercress - an aquatic plant in the genus Nasturtium, known for edible leaves

Etymology

From Latin *nasturtium*, meaning 'nose-twist,' referring to the pungent smell of the plant.
