cedar

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːdər / (AmE), / ˈsiːdə / (BrE)
the durable, fragrant wood from any of several types of cedar trees, especially valued for its resistance to decay and insects
aromatic, durable wood from cedar trees

Examples

The closet was lined with cedar to protect clothes from moths.
Furniture made of cedar is both attractive and long-lasting.
The carpenter chose cedar for its pleasant aroma and resistance to decay.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: red cedar - a type of cedar tree or its wood, especially valued for reddish hue and scent
  • Degree Lower: white cedar - a type of cedar tree or its lighter, pale-colored wood

Etymology

From Old French cedre, from Latin cedrus, from Greek kedros (cedar tree).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsiːdɚ / (AmE), / ˈsiːdə / (BrE)
any of several coniferous trees of the family Cupressaceae that resemble true cedars, often with aromatic wood used in construction and furniture
coniferous tree resembling a cedar

Examples

The aromatic wood of cedar trees is highly valued for making furniture.
Many parks in the region are planted with cedar species from the Cupressaceae family.
The scent of cedar reminds me of old closets and mountain forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: juniper - coniferous tree or shrub of the genus Juniperus, related to cedars
  • Antonym: true cedar - trees of the genus Cedrus, the typical or original cedars

Etymology

From Old French cedre, from Latin cedrus, from Ancient Greek kedros (κέδρος).

3. NOUN

/ ˈsiːdər / (AmE), / ˈsiːdə / (BrE)
A large, evergreen coniferous tree of the genus Cedrus, native to mountainous regions and valued for its aromatic wood.
evergreen tree of the genus Cedrus

Examples

The ancient cedar stood tall on the mountainside, its scent filling the crisp air.
A piece of furniture made from cedar is prized for its beauty and durability.
Several cedars grow in the park, offering shade and a pleasant aroma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cedarwood - aromatic wood from cedar trees
  • Antonym: pine - another type of coniferous tree, different genus

Etymology

From Old French cedre, from Latin cedrus, from Greek kedros (kind of tree).
