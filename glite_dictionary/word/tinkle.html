tinkle

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪŋkəl / (AmE), / ˈtɪŋkəl / (BrE)
a light, clear, metallic sound, like that produced by a small bell
light, clear ringing sound

Examples

The tinkle of wind chimes was soothing as the breeze picked up.
A faint tinkle echoed down the hallway, catching her attention.
We heard the tinkle of tiny bells as the cat entered the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chime - a clear ringing sound, often from a bell
  • Synonym: clink - a short, sharp ringing sound

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound itself.

WordNet ID: oewn-07412697-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈtɪŋkəl / (AmE), / ˈtɪŋ.kəl / (BrE)
to make or emit a light, high, ringing sound, like that of small bells or glass
emit a high, light ringing sound

Examples

The little bell began to tinkle softly in the breeze.
I heard wind chimes tinkling outside the window as I fell asleep.
Coins tinkled as she dropped them into the glass jar.

Synset Examples

tinkling bells

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jingle - to make light ringing sounds, often repeatedly, like small metal objects knocking together
  • Synonym: chime - to make a series of melodious ringing sounds, especially by bells

Etymology

Imitative origin, referring to the sound produced by small bells or glass.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02190952-v
Synset POS: v
