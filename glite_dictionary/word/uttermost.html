uttermost

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌt̬ɚˌmoʊst / (AmE), / ˈʌtəməʊst / (BrE)
the greatest or highest possible degree or amount of something
the greatest possible degree

Examples

She devoted the uttermost of her energy to the project.
In the face of danger, he summoned the uttermost of his courage.
Achieving that goal required the uttermost in determination and effort.

Synset Examples

he tried his utmost

Related Terms

  • Synonym: utmost - the greatest possible degree or amount

Etymology

From Middle English 'uttermest', superlative of 'utter' (outermost), from Old English 'ūtermest'.

WordNet ID: oewn-05132193-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈʌt̬.ɚˌmoʊst / (AmE), / ˈʌt.ə.məʊst / (BrE)
Situated at the greatest distance or most extreme point in space, time, or order.
the most distant or extreme

Examples

They reached the uttermost regions of the Arctic after weeks of travel.
The explorers pushed on to the uttermost boundary of the desert.
The uttermost branch of the river was barely visible from where they stood.

Synset Examples

explored the furthest reaches of space
the utmost tip of the peninsula

Related Terms

  • Synonym: utmost - most extreme; greatest
  • Antonym: innermost - nearest to the centre or most inward

Etymology

From Middle English 'utermost', from Old English 'ūtermest', superlative of 'ūt' (out).

greatest possible extent; most extreme

Examples
