Pterocles alchata

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛrəˈkliz ælˈkɑːtə / (AmE), / ˌtɛrəˈkliːz ælˈkɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of sandgrouse found in Europe and Africa, having elongated middle tail feathers.
species of sandgrouse native to Europe and Africa

Examples

The Pterocles alchata is well adapted to dry habitats across Europe and Africa.
Birdwatchers spotted a flock of Pterocles alchata near the salt flats at dawn.
Due to its distinctive markings, the Pterocles alchata is easy to identify among sandgrouse species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sandgrouse - a family of birds adapted to arid environments

Etymology

From Greek *pteron* (feather, wing) and Latinized specific epithet; binomial coined in scientific classification.
