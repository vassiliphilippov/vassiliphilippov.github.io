Cyclamen purpurascens

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪkləmən pɜrˌpjʊrəˈsɛnz / (AmE), / ˈsɪkləmən pɜːˌpjʊərəˈsɛnz / (BrE)
A Mediterranean plant species widely grown as a houseplant, noted for its dark green leaves marked with silver and nodding white, pink, or reddish flowers with reflexed petals.
a cultivated Mediterranean flowering plant species

Examples

Cyclamen purpurascens thrives in moist, shaded woodland gardens where its flowers brighten the ground in late summer.
Many collectors prize Cyclamen purpurascens for its attractive silver-patterned foliage and delicate pink blossoms.
The florist recommended Cyclamen purpurascens as a long-lasting houseplant that blooms even in the winter months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cyclamen - a genus of flowering plants in the primrose family, including Cyclamen purpurascens

Etymology

From Latin 'cyclamen' (from Greek 'kyklaminos'), and Latin 'purpurascens' meaning 'becoming purple'.
