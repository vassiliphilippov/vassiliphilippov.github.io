obbligato

1. NOUN

/ ɑːblɪˈɡɑːtoʊ / (AmE), / ˌɒblɪˈɡɑːtəʊ / (BrE)
(in music) a musical part that is essential and must be performed as written, usually accompanying the main part.
essential musical part in a score

Examples

The violin obbligato added a brilliant texture to the symphony.
She played the flute obbligato with flawless precision.
The composer wrote an obbligato for solo cello in the final movement.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ad libitum - a musical part that is optional or may be omitted

Etymology

From Italian 'obbligato' (obliged, required), from Latin 'obligatus'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑːbliˈɡɑːtoʊ / (AmE), / ˌɒblɪˈɡɑːtəʊ / (BrE)
a musical passage or accompaniment, especially one that is persistent but subordinate to the main melody
persistent subordinate musical motif

Examples

The flute obbligato added a delicate layer beneath the soprano's voice.
In the trio, a violin obbligato weaves around the main piano theme.
The piece features a persistent cello obbligato supporting the choir.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ostinato - a continually repeated musical phrase or rhythm

Etymology

From Italian 'obbligato' (obliged, indispensable), past participle of 'obbligare' (to oblige).
