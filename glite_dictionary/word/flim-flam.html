to flim-flam

1. VERB

/ ˈflɪm.flæm / (AmE), / ˈflɪm.flæm / (BrE)
to trick, cheat, or deceive someone, especially by using clever talk or a dishonest scheme
to trick or cheat someone
informal

Examples

The con artist tried to flim-flam us out of our money with a fake investment scheme.
She realized too late that the salesman had flim-flammed her into buying a useless warranty.
He warned his friends not to let anyone flim-flam them with unbelievable offers.

Synset Examples

We tricked the teacher into thinking that class would be cancelled next week

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trick - to deceive or cheat someone
  • Synonym: to deceive - to make someone believe something that is not true

Etymology

Originated in the 16th century as a reduplicative nonsense word for deception or trickery.
