microbar

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪkroʊˌbɑr / (AmE), / ˈmʌɪkrəʊˌbɑː / (BrE)
A unit of pressure equal to one millionth of a bar, or one dyne per square centimeter.
unit of pressure (one millionth of a bar)

Examples

The atmospheric pressure on Venus can reach up to 9,200,000 microbars, far higher than Earth's.
Scientists measured a sound wave that fluctuated by only a few microbars.
Sensitive instruments detected changes in pressure down to a single microbar.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: bar - a metric unit of pressure, equal to 100,000 pascals

Etymology

From micro- (small) and bar (unit of pressure), bar from Greek 'baros' meaning weight.
