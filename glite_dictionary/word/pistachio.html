pistachio

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈstæʃioʊ / (AmE), / pɪˈstæʃiəʊ / (BrE)
a small, pale green edible nut from a Mediterranean tree, often eaten roasted or used in cooking and baking
edible green nut from the pistachio tree

Examples

She sprinkled chopped pistachios over the top of the cake before serving.
A bag of roasted pistachios makes a healthy snack for the afternoon.
His favorite ice cream flavor is pistachio because of its unique taste and color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nut - an edible seed, usually encased in a hard shell

Etymology

From Italian 'pistacchio', from Latin 'pistacium', from Greek 'pistákion' (the nut), ultimately from Persian 'pista'.

2. NOUN

/ pɪˈstæʃioʊ / (AmE), / pɪˈstæʃiəʊ / (BrE)
A small tree native to southern Europe and Asia Minor that produces small hard-shelled nuts, commonly known as pistachios.
tree producing pistachio nuts

Examples

The pistachio thrives in dry, warm climates typical of southern Europe.
Farmers planted a grove of pistachio trees on the hillside.
A mature pistachio can yield hundreds of nuts each season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pistachio nut - the edible nut produced by the pistachio tree

Etymology

From Italian 'pistacchio', from Latin 'pistacium', from Greek 'pistákion', from Persian 'pesteh'.
