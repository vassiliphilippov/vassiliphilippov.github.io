rondeau

1. NOUN

/ rɒnˈdoʊ / (AmE), / ˈrɒndəʊ / (BrE)
a musical form consisting of a recurring refrain, often used as the last movement of a sonata or other classical piece
musical form with recurring refrain

Examples

The final movement of the sonata was a lively rondeau that delighted the audience.
Many Baroque composers favored the rondeau structure in their keyboard works.
The pianist skillfully interpreted the playful character of the rondeau.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rondo - a similar musical form with a recurring principal theme

Etymology

From French 'rondeau' (round), derived from 'rond' (round).

2. NOUN

/ rɒnˈdoʊ / (AmE), / rɒnˈdəʊ / (BrE)
a French verse form of 10 or 13 lines with two rhymes, in which the opening phrase is repeated as the refrain of the second and third stanzas
a French poetic verse form with repeated refrain

Examples

The poet composed a rondeau to capture the cyclical nature of the seasons.
Her favorite form was the rondeau, which allowed her to play with repetition and rhyme.
In literature class, we studied the structure of a rondeau and wrote our own versions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: villanelle - a 19-line poetic form with two rhymes and repeating lines
  • Synonym: ballade - a form of verse, often narrative, set to music

Etymology

Borrowed from French rondeau, diminutive of rond (round), referencing the poem's cyclic structure.
