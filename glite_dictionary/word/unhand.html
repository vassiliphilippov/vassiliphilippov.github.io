to unhand

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈhænd / (AmE), / ʌnˈhænd / (BrE)
to release (someone or something) from one's grasp; to remove one's hand or hands from.
remove one's hand from; let go of

Examples

The guard ordered the man to unhand the prisoner at once.
She shouted at him to unhand her immediately.
He refused to unhand the package, even as the train pulled away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: release - to let go of or set free
  • Antonym: grasp - to seize or hold firmly

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (reverse action) and 'hand'.
