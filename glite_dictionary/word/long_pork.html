long pork

1. NOUN

/ lɔŋ pɔrk / (AmE), / lɒŋ pɔːk / (BrE)
a euphemism for human flesh, especially as food, used particularly in contexts involving cannibalism
euphemism for human flesh (cannibalism)

Examples

In some historical accounts, the term long pork was used to mask the reality of cannibalism.
The explorers were horrified to discover what the locals meant by long pork.
Discussions about long pork appear in certain anthropological studies of Pacific Island cultures.

Synset Examples

The documentary covered the history of long pork.
Long pork is a term often used in dark humor.
Long pork was mentioned in the folklore of the island.

Etymology

Said to arise from the comparison of human flesh to pork in taste and texture, noted in 19th-century writings about the South Pacific.
