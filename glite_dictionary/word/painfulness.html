painfulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪnfəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈpeɪnfəlnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of causing physical or emotional pain
the quality of causing pain

Examples

Despite the painfulness of her injury, she managed to stay calm and positive.
He described the painfulness of losing a close friend as overwhelming.
The painfulness of the procedure was less than he had anticipated.

Synset Examples

she feared the painfulness of childbirth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pain - physical or emotional suffering
  • Antonym: pleasure - a feeling of happy satisfaction and enjoyment

Etymology

From 'painful' + '-ness' (forming nouns denoting a state or quality).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪnfəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈpeɪnfʊlnəs / (BrE)

quality of causing pain or distress

Examples

Sometimes, the painfulness of a situation teaches us important lessons.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pain - physical or emotional suffering or discomfort
  • Antonym: pleasure - a feeling of happy satisfaction and enjoyment
