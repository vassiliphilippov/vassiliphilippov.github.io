Siracusa

1. PROPN

/ ˌsirəˈkuːzə / (AmE), / ˌsɪrəˈkuːzə / (BrE)
A city in southeastern Sicily, Italy, founded by Corinthians in the 8th century BC and notable for its classical history.
historic city in southeastern Sicily

Examples

Many tourists visit Siracusa to see its ancient Greek theater and archaeological sites.
Archimedes, the famous mathematician, was born in Siracusa.
The coastline near Siracusa is known for its crystal-clear waters and beautiful scenery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Syracuse - Anglicized name for the city of Siracusa in Sicily, Italy

Etymology

From Italian 'Siracusa', from Latin 'Syracusae', from Ancient Greek 'Συράκουσαι' (Syrákousai).
