self-consciousness

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlfˈkɑn.ʃəs.nəs / (AmE), / ˌsɛlfˈkɒn.ʃəs.nəs / (BrE)
Embarrassment or discomfort arising from the belief that others are critically watching or judging you.
feeling of embarrassment from being observed

Examples

He spoke with self-consciousness in front of the large audience, glancing nervously at the crowd.
Her self-consciousness made her hesitant to join the conversation at the party.
Wearing the unusual costume triggered intense self-consciousness in him.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: confidence - the feeling or belief that you can rely on someone or something; firm trust
  • Synonym: shyness - the quality of being reserved or timid in the company of others

Etymology

From self- + consciousness, patterned on 18th-century psychological use.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlfˈkɑn.ʃəs.nəs / (AmE), / ˌselfˈkɒn.ʃəs.nəs / (BrE)
Awareness of oneself as an individual, especially with the realization that others are also aware of you, often resulting in a heightened sense of being observed or evaluated.
awareness of oneself, especially in relation to others' perception

Examples

Her acute self-consciousness made it hard for her to speak in front of the crowd.
Adolescents often struggle with self-consciousness as they become more aware of how others perceive them.
He overcame his self-consciousness and started to express his ideas more openly in meetings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-awareness - conscious knowledge of one's own character, feelings, or existence

Etymology

From 'self-conscious' + '-ness'; 'self-conscious' attested since the 18th century, combining 'self' and 'conscious.'
