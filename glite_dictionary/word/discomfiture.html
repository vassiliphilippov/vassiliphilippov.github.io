discomfiture

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈkʌmfɪtʃər / (AmE), / dɪsˈkʌmfɪtʃə / (BrE)
A feeling of uneasy embarrassment or confusion; the state of being disconcerted.
anxious embarrassment or confusion

Examples

To her discomfiture, she realized everyone was staring at her.
He tried to hide his discomfiture after forgetting his lines on stage.
A moment of discomfiture passed before he regained his confidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embarrassment - a feeling of self-consciousness and shame
  • Antonym: composure - the state of being calm and in control of oneself

Etymology

From Old French 'desconfiture' (defeat), from 'desconfit' (defeated), past participle of 'desconfire' (to defeat).
