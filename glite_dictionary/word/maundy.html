Maundy

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɔːndi / (AmE), / ˈmɔːndi / (BrE)
A public ceremony held on Maundy Thursday in which the British monarch distributes Maundy money to selected recipients.
ceremony of distributing Maundy money
british

Examples

The Queen performed the annual Maundy ceremony at the cathedral.
Each recipient at the Maundy service received special coins from the monarch.
Tourists were fascinated by the tradition of the Maundy ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Maundy Thursday - the Thursday before Easter commemorating the Last Supper

Etymology

From Old French 'mandé', from Latin 'mandatum' (commandment), referring to Jesus’ commandment of love commemorated on Maundy Thursday.
