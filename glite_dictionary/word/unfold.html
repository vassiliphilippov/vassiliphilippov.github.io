to unfold

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈfoʊld / (AmE), / ʌnˈfəʊld / (BrE)
to open or spread out something that has been folded, so that it is flat or extended
open or spread out (something folded)

Examples

She carefully unfolded the letter to read its contents.
Please unfold the map so we can see the route clearly.
He unfolded his umbrella as the rain started to fall.

Synset Examples

Unfold the newspaper
stretch out that piece of cloth
extend the TV antenna

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fold - to bend something, especially paper or cloth, so that one part covers another

Etymology

From un- (not) + fold, meaning to open out what has been folded.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02058913-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ʌnˈfoʊld / (AmE), / ʌnˈfəʊld / (BrE)
to develop or progress gradually so that something becomes clearer, fuller, or reaches a promising stage
develop or become clearer/promising

Examples

As the project continued, its true potential began to unfold before our eyes.
The story unfolded over several months, keeping readers engaged until the very end.
New opportunities may unfold as you gain experience in your career.

Synset Examples

Youth blossomed into maturity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to evolve - to develop gradually over time
  • Synonym: blossom - to develop successfully or come to a promising stage

Etymology

From Old English 'un-' (reversal) + 'fealdan' (to fold).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00294522-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ʌnˈfoʊld / (AmE), / ʌnˈfəʊld / (BrE)
to be revealed gradually or made visible to view
become revealed or visible

Examples

As the curtain rose, the beautiful set began to unfold before the audience.
Her life story would slowly unfold as the documentary progressed.
A stunning mountain vista unfolded as we turned the corner.

Synset Examples

A walk through town will unfold many interesting buildings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reveal - make (something) visible or known
  • Antonym: to conceal - to keep from sight; to hide

Etymology

From Old English 'anfealdan', from 'un-' (reversal) + 'foldan' (to fold).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02148564-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ʌnˈfoʊld / (AmE), / ʌnˈfəʊld / (BrE)
to open or spread out something that has been folded; to make or become open from a closed state
open from a folded state

Examples

He carefully unfolded the old letter and began to read it.
As the plane took off, the flight attendant unfolded the safety instruction card.
The flower buds will unfold in the warm morning sunlight.

Synset Examples

open the map
spread your arms

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fold - to bend something over onto itself so that one part covers another

Etymology

From Old English 'onfealdan', from 'on-' (un-) and 'fealdan' (to fold).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01582662-v
Synset POS: v
