polysomy

1. NOUN

/ pəˈlɪsəmi / (AmE), / pɒˈlɪsəmi / (BrE)
A condition in which an organism has one or more extra chromosomes in its somatic cells, leading to a genetic abnormality.
having extra chromosomes in cells
medical

Examples

The presence of polysomy in genetic testing can indicate certain chromosomal disorders.
Researchers discovered polysomy in several tumor samples, suggesting its role in cancer progression.
A diagnosis of polysomy often prompts further investigation into possible congenital health issues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aneuploidy - presence of an abnormal number of chromosomes in a cell
  • Antonym: monosomy - condition of having one less chromosome than normal
  • Degree Lower: trisomy - form of polysomy with three copies of a chromosome

Etymology

From Greek 'poly-' (many) + '-somy' from 'sōma' (body).
