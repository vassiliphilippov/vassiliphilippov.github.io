PFLOPS

1. NOUN

/ piːˈflɑps / (AmE), / piːˈflɒps / (BrE)
A unit of computing speed equal to one quadrillion (10^15) floating-point operations per second.
one quadrillion floating-point operations per second

Examples

Modern supercomputers can now achieve over 100 PFLOPS of processing power.
Reaching one PFLOPS was once considered a significant milestone in high-performance computing.
The laboratory announced that their new system surpassed 1.5 PFLOPS during the benchmark tests.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: FLOPS - floating-point operations per second; unit of computer speed

Etymology

Acronym from 'peta-' (prefix for quadrillion) and 'FLOPS' (floating-point operations per second).
