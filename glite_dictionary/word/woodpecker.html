woodpecker

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊdˌpɛkər / (AmE), / ˈwʊdˌpɛkə / (BrE)
A bird with strong claws, a stiff tail for climbing, and a hard, chisel-like bill for boring into wood to find insects.
bird that bores into wood for insects

Examples

The woodpecker hammered loudly on the old oak tree in search of insects.
Several woodpeckers visit our garden each spring, drawn by the tall pines.
A red-headed woodpecker drilled a perfect hole in the fence post.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flicker - type of woodpecker with a distinctive call and coloring
  • Synonym: sapsucker - type of woodpecker that feeds on tree sap

Etymology

From Middle English 'wodepec' (woodpecker), from 'wood' + 'pecker' (one who pecks).
