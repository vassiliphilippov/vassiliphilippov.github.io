debauched

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈbɔtʃt / (AmE), / dɪˈbɔːtʃt / (BrE)
Indulging in or characterized by excessive sensual pleasures; unrestrained by morals or convention.
morally unrestrained; indulgent

Examples

The novel depicts a debauched lifestyle filled with parties and indulgence.
Some critics saw the film as a portrayal of a debauched celebrity culture.
He was notorious for his debauched behavior during his time in Paris.

Synset Examples

Congreve draws a debauched aristocratic society
deplorably dissipated and degraded
riotous living
fast women

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dissolute - lacking in morals or restraint
  • Antonym: virtuous - having or showing high moral standards
  • Synonym: corrupt - morally depraved; wicked

Etymology

From French 'débaucher' (to entice away from work or duty), from Old French, perhaps from 'des-' (away) + vulgar Latin '*baucare' (to entice).
