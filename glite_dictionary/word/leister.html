leister

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪstər / (AmE), / ˈliːstə / (BrE)
a spear with three or more prongs, traditionally used for spearing fish, especially salmon
spear with prongs for fishing

Examples

The fisherman used a leister to catch salmon in the shallow river.
Ancient tribes crafted intricate leisters for spearing fish during seasonal migrations.
He displayed an old leister at the local maritime museum as part of the fishing exhibit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trident - a three-pronged spear used as a weapon or tool

Etymology

From Old Norse *ljóstr* (spear), related to spear-fishing tools in Norse culture.
