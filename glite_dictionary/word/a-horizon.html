A-horizon

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ həˌraɪzən / (AmE), / ˈeɪ həˌraɪz(ə)n / (BrE)
the uppermost layer of soil, typically rich in organic material and humus, where most plant roots grow
upper layer of soil rich in organic matter

Examples

The A-horizon is often the most fertile part of the soil, supporting lush plant growth.
Erosion of the A-horizon can severely impact agriculture productivity in a region.
Farmers test the A-horizon to understand nutrient levels before planting crops.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: B-horizon - the soil layer beneath the A-horizon, often having less organic material

Etymology

Formed from the letter 'A' (denoting the top layer) and 'horizon' (soil layer), used in soil science classification since the early 20th century.
