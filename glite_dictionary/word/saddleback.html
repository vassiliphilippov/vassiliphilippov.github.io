saddleback

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæd.əlˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈsæd.əlˌbæk / (BrE)
a low pass or ridge between two peaks, shaped like a saddle
a ridge or pass between two peaks

Examples

The hikers reached the saddleback before stopping for lunch.
From the summit, you could see a distinct saddleback connecting the two mountains.
The trail follows the saddleback between the higher ridges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: col - a low point on a ridge between two peaks

Etymology

From 'saddle' + 'back', referring to the resemblance in shape to a horse's saddle.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsædəlˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈsæd.əl.bæk / (BrE)
A roof having two slopes that meet at a central ridge, with gables at each end.
double sloping roof with gables at each end

Examples

The old barn was topped with a prominent saddleback roof, giving it a traditional appearance.
Rainwater runs off easily from a saddleback because of its double slopes and central ridge.
Many historic churches feature a saddleback tower, distinct from flat-roofed designs.

Etymology

From 'saddle' (the seat for a rider) + 'back', referring to the shape's resemblance to a saddle.
