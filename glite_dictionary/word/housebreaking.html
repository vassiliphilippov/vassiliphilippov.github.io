housebreaking

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaʊsˌbreɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhaʊsˌbreɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of illegally entering a building, especially a house, with the intent to commit a crime.
illegal entry into a building with criminal intent
legal

Examples

The police arrested him for housebreaking after he was caught inside the building at midnight.
Laws regarding housebreaking can differ depending on the country or state.
After a rise in housebreaking cases, residents were urged to upgrade their home security.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burglary - illegal entry into a building to commit a crime, especially theft
  • Degree Lower: trespassing - entering someone's property without permission

Etymology

Formed from 'house' + 'breaking', referring to breaking into a house.
