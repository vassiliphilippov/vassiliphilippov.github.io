bitewing

1. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪtˌwɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbaɪtˌwɪŋ / (BrE)
A dental X-ray film or radiograph designed to be held between the teeth, used for detecting cavities and bone loss.
dental X-ray film held by teeth
medical

Examples

The dentist took a bitewing to check for cavities between my back teeth.
Routine bitewing X-rays help monitor bone loss caused by gum disease.
He needed to reposition the bitewing film for a clearer view of the molars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: periapical - a type of dental X-ray showing the whole tooth

Etymology

From 'bite' + 'wing', referring to the tab or wing that is bitten to hold the film in place.
