Testudines

1. NOUN

/ tɛˈstjuːdɪniːz / (AmE), / tɛˈstjuːdɪniːz / (BrE)
the order of reptiles that includes turtles and tortoises, characterized by a bony or cartilaginous shell
order of reptiles: turtles and tortoises

Examples

Scientists classify all modern turtles and tortoises as members of the order Testudines.
The evolutionary history of Testudines dates back more than 200 million years.
Researchers study the anatomy of Testudines to understand how their shells evolved.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turtle - reptile with a hard shell covering its body
  • Synonym: tortoise - land-dwelling reptile with a domed shell

Etymology

From Latin 'testudo' (tortoise).
