serfhood

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝf.hʊd / (AmE), / ˈsɜːf.hʊd / (BrE)
the condition or state of being a serf, especially in a feudal society
state of being a serf

Examples

Peasants under serfhood had very few rights and lived under the control of their lords.
The practice of serfhood was officially abolished in Russia in the 19th century.
Escaping from serfhood was nearly impossible for most medieval Europeans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serfdom - state of being a serf; synonym for serfhood

Etymology

From 'serf' (from Latin 'servus', slave) + '-hood' (denoting condition or state).
