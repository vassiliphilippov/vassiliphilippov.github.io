Standard & Poor's

1. NOUN

/ ˌstændərd ən ˈpɔrz / (AmE), / ˌstændəd ænd ˈpɔːz / (BrE)
a major financial services company known for producing market indices, especially the S&P 500, a broadly based stock market index.
major financial market index and company

Examples

Many investors use the Standard & Poor's index to gauge the performance of U.S. stocks.
After the news broke, the Standard & Poor's 500 experienced significant volatility.
She checks the Standard & Poor's every morning for market trends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: S&P 500 - an index of 500 leading US companies as compiled by Standard & Poor's

Etymology

Named after founders Henry Varnum Poor and Henry William Poor, and after Standard Statistics Company.
