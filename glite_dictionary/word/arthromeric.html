arthromeric

1. ADJ

/ ˌɑːr.θrəˈmɛr.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌɑː.θrəʊˈmɛr.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to a body segment of jointed animals, such as arthropods.
of a jointed animal's body segment

Examples

The arthromeric structure of the millipede was clearly visible under the microscope.
Researchers studied the development of arthromeric divisions in various insect larvae.
The fossil showed distinct arthromeric segmentation, suggesting it belonged to an ancient arthropod.

Etymology

From Greek 'arthron' (joint) + 'meros' (part, segment) + '-ic'.
