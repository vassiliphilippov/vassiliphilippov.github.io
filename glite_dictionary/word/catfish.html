catfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈkætˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkatˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
The flesh of a scaleless freshwater fish, often eaten as food, especially in the southern United States.
flesh of a freshwater fish used as food

Examples

We ordered fried catfish with hushpuppies at the roadside diner.
Grilled catfish is a popular dish in southern cuisine.
The market sells fresh catfish caught from local rivers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catfish - a scaleless freshwater fish with whiskerlike barbels

Etymology

From 'cat' and 'fish,' so called because of the fish's long barbels resembling a cat's whiskers.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkætˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkatˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A large predatory deep-sea fish with strong teeth and no pelvic fins, valued as food.
large toothy deep-sea fish

Examples

The chef prepared a delicious dish using fresh catfish caught from the icy waters.
Fishermen often compete to catch the largest catfish during the annual competition.
Unlike most fish, this species of catfish lacks pelvic fins and has powerful jaws.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wolffish - large predatory marine fish with strong teeth

Etymology

Named for the prominent whisker-like barbels around its mouth, resembling a cat's whiskers.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02619481-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈkætˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkatˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A freshwater fish with whisker-like barbels around the mouth, found chiefly in Eurasia and North America.
freshwater fish with whisker-like barbels

Examples

The catfish lurked at the bottom of the muddy river.
She caught a large catfish while fishing in the lake yesterday.
Many restaurants in the South serve fried catfish as a specialty.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: cat - domestic feline animal
  • Degree Higher: fish - cold-blooded aquatic animal

Etymology

Named for the resemblance of their barbels to a cat's whiskers.

4. VERB

/ ˈkætˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkat.fɪʃ / (BrE)
To deceive someone by pretending to be another person online, often by using a fake identity to pursue a romantic relationship.
deceive with a fake online identity (esp. romantically)

Examples

He discovered he had been catfished when the person he was chatting with online refused to video call.
Many people have been catfished on dating apps by someone using fake photos.
She warned her friends about the dangers of catfishing after her own experience online.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: imposter - a person who pretends to be someone else to deceive others
  • Synonym: scam - a dishonest scheme or fraud, especially on the internet

Etymology

Originally from the noun 'catfish'. The verb sense is derived from internet culture, popularized by the 2010 documentary 'Catfish'.
