tangerine

1. NOUN

/ ˌtæn.dʒəˈrin / (AmE), / ˌtæn.dʒəˈriːn / (BrE)
A small, sweet citrus fruit with a deep orange skin that is easy to peel, originating from various varieties of mandarin oranges.
a small, sweet orange citrus fruit

Examples

She peeled the tangerine and shared the juicy segments with her friends.
A bowl of tangerines brightened up the kitchen table during the holidays.
The tangerine is a popular snack because it is sweet and easy to eat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mandarin - a small, sweet citrus fruit with loose skin

Etymology

From Tangier, a port in Morocco, from where tangerines were originally shipped to Europe.

2. ADJ

/ ˌtæn.dʒəˈrin / (AmE), / ˌtæn.dʒəˈriːn / (BrE)
Of a strong reddish-orange color, similar to the skin of a ripe tangerine fruit.
strong reddish-orange color

Examples

She wore a tangerine dress that stood out at the party.
The sunset glowed with deep tangerine light over the horizon.
The artist used a bold tangerine shade to highlight the painting.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: orange - a bright reddish-yellow color
  • Degree Higher: vermilion - a vivid red to reddish-orange color

Etymology

From the name of the tangerine fruit, itself from Tangier, Morocco, an early source of the fruit.
