Vulpes

1. NOUN

/ ˈvʌlˌpiːz / (AmE), / ˈvʌl.piːz / (BrE)
A genus of mammals that comprises the true foxes, distinguished by their pointed muzzles and bushy tails.
genus of true foxes
medical

Examples

The red fox, classified as Vulpes vulpes, is widespread across the Northern Hemisphere.
Several species in the genus Vulpes show remarkable adaptations to different habitats.
Scientists studied the evolutionary history of Vulpes to better understand the origins of modern foxes.

Etymology

From Latin 'vulpes' meaning 'fox'.
