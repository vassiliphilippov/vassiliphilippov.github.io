syncretistic

1. ADJ

/ sɪnkrəˈtɪstɪk / (AmE), / sɪŋkrɪˈtɪstɪk / (BrE)
relating to the process or tendency in a language to simplify or reduce the use of inflections, blending different inflectional forms.
relating to the reduction of inflections in a language

Examples

Many linguists consider Modern English to be a syncretistic language.
The syncretistic changes in grammar simplified many English verb endings.
As Latin evolved into Romance languages, they became progressively more syncretistic.

Synset Examples

modern English is a syncretic language

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fusional - relating to a type of language with inflectional word forms
  • Synonym: analytic - relating to a language with little or no inflection

Etymology

From Greek 'synkretismos' (union of different beliefs or practices), via French 'syncrétisme' and modern Latin 'syncretismus'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌsɪŋkrəˈtɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɪŋkrɪˈtɪstɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characterized by syncretism, especially the combination of different beliefs or practices.
characterized by syncretism

Examples

The syncretistic nature of the religion allowed it to incorporate elements from several older traditions.
Her research explores syncretistic approaches in modern spiritual movements.
Many festivals in the region display a syncretistic blending of indigenous and colonial customs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: syncretic - combining different forms of belief or practice

Etymology

From syncretism, from Greek 'synkretismos' (union of communities).
