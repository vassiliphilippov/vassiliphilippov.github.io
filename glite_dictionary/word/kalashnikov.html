Kalashnikov

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlæʃnɪˌkɑv / (AmE), / kəˈlæʃnɪˌkɒf / (BrE)
A type of automatic rifle, especially the AK-47, originally developed in the Soviet Union by Mikhail Kalashnikov.
Russian automatic rifle, especially AK-47

Examples

The Kalashnikov is renowned for its durability and ease of use in harsh conditions.
Rebels armed themselves with Kalashnikovs during the conflict.
He recognized the distinctive sound of a Kalashnikov being fired in the distance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: AK-47 - A specific model of Kalashnikov assault rifle

Etymology

Named after Mikhail Kalashnikov (1919–2013), Russian general and inventor.
