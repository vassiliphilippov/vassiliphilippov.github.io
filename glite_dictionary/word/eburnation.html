eburnation

1. NOUN

/ ˌiːbərˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌiːbɜːˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
a process in which bone becomes dense and smooth, resembling ivory, often occurring in joints affected by degenerative disease
ivory-like transformation of bone in joint disease
medical

Examples

The pathologist noted significant eburnation on the surface of the femoral head.
Radiographic images revealed areas of eburnation consistent with advanced osteoarthritis.
Joint pain can be aggravated when eburnation leads to increased friction between bone surfaces.

Etymology

From Latin eburneus (of ivory), from ebur (ivory), with the suffix -ation.
