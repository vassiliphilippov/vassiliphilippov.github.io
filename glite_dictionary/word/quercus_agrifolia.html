Quercus agrifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɜrkəs ˌæɡrɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs ˌaɡrɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A species of evergreen oak native to the coastal areas of western North America, with small, thick, usually spiny-toothed dark-green leaves.
a species of evergreen oak tree

Examples

The Quercus agrifolia provides essential habitat for many native animal species in California.
Botanists studied the leaves of Quercus agrifolia to understand its adaptation to coastal conditions.
Landscapers often recommend Quercus agrifolia due to its drought tolerance and evergreen foliage.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Quercus' (oak) and 'agrifolia' (meaning 'sharp-leaved').
