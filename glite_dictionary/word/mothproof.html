mothproof

1. VERB

/ ˈmɔθˌpruf / (AmE), / ˈmɒθˌpruːf / (BrE)
to treat or protect (an article, especially of clothing or fabric) so that it is safe from damage by moths
treat to protect from moths

Examples

We always mothproof our sweaters before packing them away for summer.
It is important to mothproof wool carpets so they last longer.
She spent the afternoon mothproofing blankets and coats in the attic.

Synset Examples

mothproof woollen clothes during the summer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disinfect - to clean so as to destroy or prevent the growth of disease-carrying microorganisms

Etymology

Formed from 'moth', the insect, and 'proof', meaning resistant to.

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɑːθˌpruːf / (AmE), / ˈmɒθˌpruːf / (BrE)
Resistant to damage or destruction by moths, especially in reference to fabrics or materials.
resistant to moth damage

Examples

The company sells mothproof wool carpets that last for years.
I prefer buying mothproof clothing to avoid any unwanted holes.
After treatment, the blanket became completely mothproof and safe to store.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waterproof - impervious to water
  • Antonym: moth-eaten - damaged or worn out by moths

Etymology

Formed from 'moth' + 'proof', similar to words like 'waterproof'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01779164-s
Synset POS: s
