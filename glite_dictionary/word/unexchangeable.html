unexchangeable

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnɪkˈstʃeɪndʒəbəl / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪkˈstʃeɪndʒəbl / (BrE)
not able to be exchanged for something else; not suitable or permitted for exchange.
not able to be exchanged

Examples

Gift cards marked as unexchangeable cannot be returned or swapped for cash.
The airline ticket was unexchangeable, so any changes would incur a full charge.
Due to hygiene reasons, all undergarments are unexchangeable after purchase.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: exchangeable - able to be exchanged for something else
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00921605-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnɪksˈtʃeɪndʒəbəl / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪksˈtʃeɪndʒəbəl / (BrE)
(of a currency or asset) not able to be exchanged for or replaced by another currency or item of equal value.
not able to be exchanged for equal value

Examples

After the government introduced strict controls, the local currency became unexchangeable on international markets.
Gift cards marked as unexchangeable cannot be traded for cash or other vouchers.
Many collectors value coins that are unexchangeable because they are harder to obtain.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: exchangeable - able to be exchanged for something else of equal value

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' to 'exchangeable'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00920321-a
Synset POS: a
