Pediocactus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpiːdi.oʊˈkæktəs / (AmE), / ˌpiːdi.əˈkæktəs / (BrE)
a genus of low-growing cacti native to the Great Plains and western regions of North America
genus of low-growing North American cacti

Examples

The Pediocactus is well adapted to the harsh, dry climate of the Great Plains.
Botanists discovered a rare Pediocactus flower blooming in the wild this spring.
Many Pediocactus species are now considered endangered due to habitat loss.

Etymology

From Greek 'pedion' (plain, field) and 'cactus', referring to cacti typical of open plains.
