horsewhip

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrsˌwɪp / (AmE), / ˈhɔːswɪp / (BrE)
a whip used for controlling or urging horses
whip used to control horses

Examples

The coachman raised his horsewhip to direct the team of horses.
She purchased a new horsewhip before starting her riding lessons.
In the old painting, a horsewhip hangs beside the stable door.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whip - a flexible instrument for striking or urging animals
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03544934-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈhɔrsˌwɪp / (AmE), / ˈhɔːswɪp / (BrE)
to strike or flog someone or something with a whip intended for horses
strike with a horsewhip

Examples

The cruel trainer threatened to horsewhip the animal if it disobeyed.
He claimed he would horsewhip anyone who insulted his honor.
She was shocked to see the farmer horsewhipping the stubborn mule in the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whip - to strike with a flexible instrument like a whip

Etymology

Formed from 'horse' and 'whip', describing the act of whipping with a horsewhip.
