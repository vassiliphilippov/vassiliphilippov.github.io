eldership

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛldɚˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈɛldəʃɪp / (BrE)
the office or position of being an elder, especially in a church or community
the role or office of an elder

Examples

He was elected to the eldership of the church for a three-year term.
The village respected the decisions of the eldership in all important matters.
Her father’s long service in the eldership impressed the congregation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elder - a person of greater age, wisdom, or authority, especially in a church or community

Etymology

From 'elder' + '-ship' (forming nouns denoting office or status).
