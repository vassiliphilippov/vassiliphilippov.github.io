strategics

1. NOUN

/ strəˈdʒiːtɪks / (AmE), / strəˈdʒiːtɪks / (BrE)
the science or art of planning and directing overall military operations and movements in a war or battle; the study or skill of strategy.
science/art of strategy

Examples

He spent decades studying strategics at military academies around the world.
Modern strategics incorporates concepts from economics, politics, and information technology.
A firm grasp of strategics is essential for senior officers in the armed forces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strategy - a plan of action designed to achieve a long-term or overall aim

Etymology

From 'strategic', after 'strategy'; patterned like 'logistics', from Greek 'strategikos' (of a general, relating to a commander).
