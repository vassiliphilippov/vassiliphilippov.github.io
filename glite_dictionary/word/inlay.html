inlay

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌleɪ / (AmE), / ˈɪn.leɪ / (BrE)
(Dentistry) A solid filling, usually of gold, porcelain, or a composite, made to fit a cavity in a tooth and cemented into place.
solid dental filling fitted into a tooth
medical

Examples

The dentist recommended an inlay rather than a standard filling to restore the damaged tooth.
Gold inlays are known for their strength and durability in dental treatments.
After the cavity was cleaned, the technician prepared an inlay to perfectly fit the space.

Etymology

From in- + lay, referring to something laid or set into a surface.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌleɪ / (AmE), / ˈɪn.leɪ / (BrE)
a decorative pattern formed by fitting pieces of wood, metal, or other materials into slots cut in a surface
decorative pattern fitted into a surface

Examples

The antique desk was embellished with a beautiful inlay of rosewood and ivory.
The artisan spent hours perfecting the delicate inlay around the tabletop.
Museum visitors admired the intricate inlay work on the cabinet's surface.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marquetry - the art of applying pieces of veneer to form decorative patterns or pictures

Etymology

From the verb 'inlay', which comes from Middle English inlaien (to lay in).

3. VERB

/ ɪnˈleɪ / (AmE), / ɪnˈleɪ / (BrE)
to decorate a surface by embedding pieces of a different material such as wood, stone, or metal
decorate by embedding contrasting material

Examples

The artist decided to inlay the tabletop with pieces of colored glass.
They inlaid the wooden floor with a beautiful geometric pattern.
She is inlaying silver threads into the jewelry box lid as a finishing touch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to embellish - to make something more attractive by adding decorative details
  • Synonym: to carve - to cut into the surface of something to create a design

Etymology

From Old English 'inlaegdan' ('inlaid'), from 'in-' + 'lay'.
