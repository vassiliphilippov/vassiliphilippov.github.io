testimonial

1. ADJ

/ ˌtɛstəˈmoʊniəl / (AmE), / ˌtɛstəˈməʊniəl / (BrE)
(of an action or event) expressing admiration, gratitude, or appreciation toward someone or something.
expressing admiration or appreciation

Examples

She was honored at a testimonial banquet celebrating her decades of service.
The charity hosted a testimonial event to thank its longtime supporters.
He gave a brief speech at the testimonial dinner in honor of his retiring colleague.

Synset Examples

testimonial dinner

Etymology

From Medieval Latin testimonialis, from testimonium (testimony).

WordNet ID: oewn-02824101-a

2. ADJ

/ ˌtɛstəˈmoʊniəl / (AmE), / ˌtɛstəˈməʊniəl / (BrE)
(adj.) Of or relating to testimony, or serving as evidence or proof in support of something.
relating to testimony or evidence

Examples

The lawyer submitted several testimonial documents to support her client's case.
Her testimonial statements were crucial in the investigation.
The judge requested any testimonial evidence that could clarify the events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: testimony - a formal written or spoken statement, especially in a court of law
  • Synonym: testimonial - a statement testifying to someone's character or qualifications (noun)

Etymology

From Medieval Latin testimonialis, from Latin testimonium (evidence, witness).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02823928-a
Synset POS: a

3. NOUN

/ ˌtɛstəˈmoʊniəl / (AmE), / ˌtɛstəˈməʊniəl / (BrE)
something given or done as an expression of appreciation, esteem, or admiration; a tribute or statement honoring a person or achievement
a tribute or statement of esteem

Examples

At the retirement party, his colleagues presented him with a testimonial in recognition of his decades of service.
The award ceremony concluded with a moving testimonial to the late director's legacy.
They wrote a heartfelt testimonial expressing their gratitude for her unwavering support.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tribute - an act, statement, or gift that is intended to show gratitude, respect, or admiration

Etymology

From Latin testimonium (evidence, proof, witness).

4. NOUN

/ ˌtɛstəˈmoʊniəl / (AmE), / ˌtɛstɪˈməʊniəl / (BrE)
a formal written or spoken statement of praise or recommendation, especially attesting to someone's character, qualifications, or the quality of a product
statement of praise or recommendation

Examples

She wrote a glowing testimonial describing his dedication and professionalism.
The company displayed customer testimonials on its website to attract new clients.
Before hiring him, they asked for a testimonial from his previous employer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recommendation - a suggestion or endorsement that someone or something is good or suitable

Etymology

From Medieval Latin testimonialis, from Latin testimonium (evidence, witness).

5. NOUN

/ ˌtɛstəˈmoʊniəl / (AmE), / ˌtɛstɪˈməʊniəl / (BrE)
A statement, event, or object that provides evidence or proof of something, especially a person's ability or character.
evidence or proof of something

Examples

The glowing testimonial from her former boss helped her secure the job.
Each award acts as a testimonial to his commitment and skill.
The worn-out shoes are a testimonial to the miles she has run.

Synset Examples

his effort was testimony to his devotion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: testimony - a formal statement serving as evidence
  • Synonym: proof - evidence or argument establishing a fact or truth
  • Antonym: refutation - the act of proving a statement or theory to be wrong

Etymology

From Medieval Latin testimonialis (certificate of character), from Latin testimonium (testimony).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06660952-n
Synset POS: n
