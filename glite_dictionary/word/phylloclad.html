phylloclad

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪləˌklæd / (AmE), / ˈfɪləkˌlæd / (BrE)
A flattened stem that resembles and functions as a leaf, found in certain plants.
flattened, leaf-like stem

Examples

The cactus relies on its phylloclad for photosynthesis instead of true leaves.
Botanists observed that the phylloclad allowed the plant to survive in arid conditions.
Some plants develop a phylloclad as an adaptation to reduce water loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cladode - a branch resembling a leaf

Etymology

From Greek phyllon (leaf) + klados (branch).
