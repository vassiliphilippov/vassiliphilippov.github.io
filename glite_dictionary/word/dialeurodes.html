Dialeurodes

1. NOUN

/ daɪ.əˈlʊə.roʊˌdiːz / (AmE), / daɪ.əˈljʊərəʊdiːz / (BrE)
A genus of whiteflies in the family Aleyrodidae, some species of which are known agricultural pests.
genus of whiteflies

Examples

Dialeurodes includes several species of whiteflies harmful to citrus crops.
Scientists are studying the life cycle of Dialeurodes to improve pest management strategies.
A severe infestation of Dialeurodes can drastically reduce fruit yields.

Etymology

From Greek 'dia' (through) and 'aleurodes' (floury, mealy; a reference to their powdered wings).

2. PROPN

/ daɪəˈlʊəroʊˌdiːz / (AmE), / daɪəˈljʊərəʊˌdiːz / (BrE)
A genus of small whiteflies in the family Aleyrodidae, including several species that are plant pests.
genus of whiteflies (insects)

Examples

The citrus whitefly, a member of the genus Dialeurodes, often infests orange groves.
Researchers are studying Dialeurodes populations to develop more effective pest control.
Several species in the genus Dialeurodes are known to damage ornamental plants.

Etymology

From Greek 'dia-' (through, across) + 'aleuron' (flour), referring to the floury appearance, used in modern taxonomic naming.
