epiphysis

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈpɪfɪsɪs / (AmE), / ɪˈpɪfɪsɪs / (BrE)
a small endocrine gland located in the brain beneath the back part of the corpus callosum that secretes melatonin; also known as the pineal gland
pineal gland in the brain
medical

Examples

Scientists have studied the epiphysis to understand its role in regulating circadian rhythms.
Damage to the epiphysis may disrupt normal sleep patterns in some patients.
The epiphysis secretes melatonin, which helps control the sleep-wake cycle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pineal gland - the endocrine gland in the brain that secretes melatonin

Etymology

From Greek 'epiphusis' meaning 'growth upon', from 'epi-' (upon) and 'physis' (growth).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈpɪfɪsɪs / (AmE), / ɪˈpɪfɪsɪs / (BrE)
the rounded end part of a long bone, initially separated from the main bone by cartilage, which later fuses with the shaft as the bone matures
end part of a long bone
medical

Examples

The growth of the epiphysis is crucial for the lengthening of long bones during childhood.
A fracture involving the epiphysis may affect bone growth in young people.
Ossification of the epiphysis usually completes by late adolescence.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: diaphysis - the shaft or central part of a long bone

Etymology

From Greek 'epiphusis' (growth upon), from 'epi' (upon) + 'phusis' (growth).
