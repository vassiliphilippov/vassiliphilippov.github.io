to eruct

1. VERB

/ ɪˈrʌkt / (AmE), / ɪˈrʌkt / (BrE)
to eject or send out something in large quantities, often explosively or forcefully; to emit, especially metaphorically
eject or emit forcibly, often in large amounts

Examples

The volcano began to eruct clouds of ash into the sky during the eruption.
In a fit of rage, he seemed to eruct insults without restraint.
The faulty exhaust system would occasionally eruct thick black smoke into the air.

Synset Examples

the volcano spews out molten rocks every day
The editors of the paper spew out hostile articles about the Presidential candidate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to spew - to pour out or eject forcibly

Etymology

From Latin eructare (to belch forth), from ex- (out) + ructare (to belch).

2. VERB

/ ɪˈrʌkt / (AmE), / ɪˈrʌkt / (BrE)
to expel gas noisily from the stomach through the mouth; to belch
expel gas from the stomach (belch)
medical

Examples

It is considered impolite to eruct loudly during a formal dinner.
Some digestive disorders may cause patients to eruct frequently throughout the day.
He tried to suppress a sudden urge to eruct in the middle of the meeting.

Synset Examples

Please don't burp at the table

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burp - expel gas from the stomach noisily through the mouth
  • Synonym: belch - emit gas from the stomach noisily through the mouth

Etymology

From Latin 'eructare' (to belch forth).
