alkalinuria

1. NOUN

/ ˌæl.kə.lɪˈnjʊr.i.ə / (AmE), / ˌal.kə.lɪˈnjʊə.rɪ.ə / (BrE)
a condition in which the urine is abnormally alkaline, rather than slightly acidic as is normal
abnormally alkaline urine condition
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed alkalinuria after analyzing the pH of the patient's urine.
Certain medications can cause temporary alkalinuria as a side effect.
Persistent alkalinuria may point to problems with kidney function.

Etymology

From alkali (a base or basic compound) + -uria (urine condition), from Greek ouron (urine).
