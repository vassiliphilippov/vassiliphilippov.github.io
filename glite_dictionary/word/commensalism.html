commensalism

1. NOUN

/ kəˈmɛnsəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / kəˈmɛnsəlɪzəm / (BrE)
a relationship between two different organisms in which one benefits while the other is neither helped nor harmed
biology: relationship where one organism benefits, the other is unaffected

Examples

Barnacles attached to whales are a classic example of commensalism in marine environments.
In commensalism, one organism gains food or shelter without affecting the host species.
Scientists studied the commensalism between tiny insects and large mammals in the savanna.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: mutualism - a relationship between two species in which both benefit
  • Degree Higher: parasitism - a relationship where one organism benefits at the expense of another

Etymology

From Latin 'commensalis' (sharing a table), from 'com-' (together) + 'mensa' (table).
