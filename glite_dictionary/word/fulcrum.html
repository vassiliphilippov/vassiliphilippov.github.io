fulcrum

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʊl.krəm / (AmE), / ˈfʊl.krəm / (BrE)
the point or support about which a lever pivots or turns
pivot point of a lever

Examples

The seesaw tipped when its fulcrum shifted to one side.
A crowbar uses a rock as its fulcrum to lift a heavy object.
Understanding where the fulcrum is placed changes how easily a lever moves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pivot - central point on which something turns
  • Synonym: lever - rigid bar that moves around a fulcrum

Etymology

From Latin *fulcrum* (bedpost, support), from *fulcire* (to prop up).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfʊlkrəm / (AmE), / ˈfʌlkrəm / (BrE)
the central or most important point in an activity, event, or situation; a pivotal or crucial element in a relationship or discussion
central or pivotal point

Examples

She became the fulcrum of the team's efforts to win the championship.
Trust is often the fulcrum on which strong relationships are balanced.
In the novel, the secret meeting acts as a fulcrum for the entire plot.

Synset Examples

He was the fulcrum in their partnership, balancing ideas.
The fulcrum of the debate was about climate change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pivot - the central point, pin, or shaft on which a mechanism turns or oscillates; a key person or thing

Etymology

From Latin 'fulcrum' (bedpost, support), from 'fulcire' (to prop up).
