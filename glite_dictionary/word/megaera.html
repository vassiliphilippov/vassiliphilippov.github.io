Megaera

1. PROPN

/ məˈɡɪrə / (AmE), / mɪˈdʒɪərə / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) one of the three Furies, responsible for punishing crimes involving marital infidelity.
one of the Furies in Greek mythology

Examples

In ancient tales, Megaera was dreaded as the punisher of marital betrayal.
The painting in the museum depicts Megaera unleashing her wrath on a faithless husband.
According to myth, Megaera was relentless in pursuing those who broke their marriage vows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fury - any of the three goddesses of vengeance in Greek mythology
  • Synonym: Alecto - one of the three Furies in Greek mythology, sister of Megaera
  • Synonym: Tisiphone - one of the three Furies in Greek mythology, sister of Megaera

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Μέγαιρα (Mégaira), meaning 'grudge' or 'jealous rage'.
