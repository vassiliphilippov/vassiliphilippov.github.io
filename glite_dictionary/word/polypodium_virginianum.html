Polypodium virginianum

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑlɪˈpoʊdiəm vɚˌdʒɪniˈænəm / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈpəʊdiəm vɜːˌdʒɪniˈænəm / (BrE)
A chiefly lithophytic or epiphytic fern native to North America and eastern Asia, also known as the rock polypody.
North American and East Asian fern species

Examples

The Polypodium virginianum fern grows on mossy rocks in forested areas.
Botanists studied Polypodium virginianum to understand its adaptation to epiphytic life.
Many gardeners appreciate Polypodium virginianum for its delicate, evergreen fronds.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Polypodium' (many-footed) and species modifier 'virginianum' (of Virginia).
