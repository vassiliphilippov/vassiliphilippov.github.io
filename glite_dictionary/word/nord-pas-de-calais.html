Nord-Pas-de-Calais

1. PROPN

/ nɔrd ˌpɑ də kælˈeɪ / (AmE), / nɔːd ˌpɑː də kalˈeɪ / (BrE)
a former administrative region of northeastern France, on the border with Belgium, known for its industrial heritage and coastal areas
region in northeastern France

Examples

Many tourists visit Nord-Pas-de-Calais to explore its beaches and World War battlefields.
The climate in Nord-Pas-de-Calais is influenced by its proximity to the North Sea.
Historically, Nord-Pas-de-Calais was a major center for coal mining and industry.

Etymology

French, from 'Nord' (north) and 'Pas-de-Calais' (strait of Calais)
