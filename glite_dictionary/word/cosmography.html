cosmography

1. NOUN

/ kɑzˈmɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / kɒzˈmɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the science or practice of mapping or describing the general features of the earth or the universe
mapping or description of earth or universe

Examples

Ancient scholars contributed greatly to the cosmography of the known world.
The study of medieval cosmography reveals how people understood the universe.
Ptolemy's works are considered monumental in the field of cosmography.

Synset Examples

the cosmography of Ptolemy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cartography - the science or practice of drawing maps

Etymology

From Greek kosmographia, from kosmos (world, universe) + -graphia (writing).

2. NOUN

/ kɑzˈmɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / kɒzˈmɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the science that describes and maps the general features of the universe, including both heaven and earth, but distinct from geography or astronomy
science of mapping the universe

Examples

During the Renaissance, cosmography flourished as scholars sought to map both the earth and the heavens.
Her thesis explored the history of cosmography and its impact on scientific thought.
Many early explorers relied on cosmography to guide their voyages and understand the world.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: astronomy - the scientific study of celestial bodies
  • Degree Lower: geography - the study of the earth's physical features

Etymology

From Greek kosmographia, from kosmos (world, universe) + -graphia (writing, description).
