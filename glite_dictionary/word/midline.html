midline

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪdˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈmɪd.laɪn / (BrE)
an imaginary line or plane dividing the body or a part of the body into left and right halves
median plane of the body
medical

Examples

The surgeon made an incision along the midline of the abdomen.
During the scan, the doctor checked if the heart was shifted from the midline.
In anatomy class, students learned to identify the body's midline.

Etymology

From 'mid' + 'line', indicating a line in the middle.
