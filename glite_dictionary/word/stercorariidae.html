Stercorariidae

1. NOUN

/ stɛrˌkɔrəˈraɪɪdi / (AmE), / stɜːˌkɒrəˈraɪɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of seabirds comprising the skuas and jaegers, known for their aggressive behavior and predatory habits.
family of seabirds (skuas and jaegers)

Examples

Members of the Stercorariidae are well known for their aggressive kleptoparasitic behavior.
Birdwatchers in the Arctic islands often spot several species within the Stercorariidae family.
The conservation status of Stercorariidae varies depending on the particular species and region.

Etymology

From modern Latin Stercorariidae, based on genus Stercorarius, from Latin stercorarius (of dung), referring to a feeding habit once mistakenly attributed to these birds.
