paediatrics

1. NOUN

/ piˈædɪˌætrɪks / (AmE), / piːˈdiːətrɪks / (BrE)
The branch of medicine concerned with the treatment of infants, children, and adolescents.
medical care of children
medical british

Examples

She decided to specialize in paediatrics after finishing medical school.
Advances in paediatrics have greatly improved childhood survival rates.
The hospital is known for its outstanding paediatrics department.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pediatrics - The branch of medicine concerned with the treatment of children (American spelling).

Etymology

From Greek pais, paidos (child) + -iatric (from iatros, physician).
