anoxemic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.ɑkˈsiː.mɪk / (AmE), / ˌæn.ɒkˈsiː.mɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or marked by an abnormally low level of oxygen in arterial blood.
with too little oxygen in the blood
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed as anoxemic after laboratory tests revealed a dangerously low oxygen level.
During the rescue, several miners became anoxemic due to insufficient ventilation in the shaft.
Doctors monitored the anoxemic symptoms to prevent further complications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypoxemic - relating to or marked by insufficient oxygen in the blood

Etymology

From 'anoxemia' (lack of oxygen in blood) + '-ic' (forming adjectives).
