Maghrib

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːɡrɪb / (AmE), / ˈmæɡrɪb / (BrE)
the region of northwest Africa including Morocco, Algeria, and Tunisia, known for its shared cultural and historical heritage
region of northwest Africa

Examples

The traditional foods of the Maghrib reflect a blend of Berber, Arab, and Mediterranean influences.
He traveled across the Maghrib to study its diverse languages and cultures.
The Atlas Mountains are a significant geographical feature of the Maghrib.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: North Africa - the northernmost region of the African continent

Etymology

From Arabic 'al-Maghrib' (the west), referring to the westernmost part of the Arab world.
