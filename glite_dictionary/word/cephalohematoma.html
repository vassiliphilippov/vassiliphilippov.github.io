cephalohematoma

1. NOUN

/ sɛf-ə-loʊ-hiː-mə-toʊ-mə / (AmE), / sɛf-ə-ləʊ-hiː-mə-təʊ-mə / (BrE)
A localized collection of blood beneath the scalp of a newborn baby, usually caused by pressure during birth.
blood collection under newborn's scalp
medical

Examples

The doctors monitored the baby's cephalohematoma to ensure it did not increase in size.
A cephalohematoma may take several weeks to resolve without intervention.
Parents were reassured that the cephalohematoma was a common birth-related injury and would heal on its own.

Etymology

From Greek kephalē (head) + haima (blood) + -oma (tumor or swelling).
