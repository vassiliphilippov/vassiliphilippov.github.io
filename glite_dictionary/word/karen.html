Karen

1. NOUN

/ kəˈrɛn / (AmE), / kəˈrɛn / (BrE)
the Tibeto-Burman language spoken in the border regions of Thailand and Myanmar (Burma), mostly by the Karen people
Tibeto-Burman language of Southeast Asia

Examples

Many people in the region speak Karen as their native language.
She is taking classes to learn Karen to communicate with local villagers.
The Karen language has several distinct dialects.

Etymology

From Burmese ကရင် (kăyañ), the ethnonym for the Karen people.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɛrən / (AmE), / ˈkærən / (BrE)
A mocking or pejorative term for a woman regarded as entitled, demanding, or exhibiting a sense of privilege, typically in public settings or towards service workers.
entitled or demanding woman (stereotype)
informal slang

Examples

The barista rolled her eyes as a Karen complained loudly about her coffee order.
On social media, people often post videos of a Karen arguing with customer service staff.
It's unfair to label every dissatisfied customer as a Karen.

Synset Examples

Everyone dreaded the day a Karen showed up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boomer - older person who is perceived as out of touch or condescending, often used pejoratively

Etymology

Expanded from the given name 'Karen,' adopted in internet culture during the 2010s to denote this stereotype.
