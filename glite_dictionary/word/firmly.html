firmly

1. ADV

/ ˈfɝːmli / (AmE), / ˈfɜːmli / (BrE)
In a secure or strong manner; in a way that is fixed and unlikely to change or be moved.
in a secure or strong manner

Examples

She gripped the railing firmly as she climbed the stairs.
You must close the lid firmly so that it seals properly.
He held my hand firmly to reassure me.

Synset Examples

she held the child securely

Related Terms

  • Synonym: securely - in a safe or secure manner

Etymology

From 'firm' (adjective) + '-ly' (adverb suffix), from Latin 'firmus' (strong, stable).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00226317-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ ˈfɝːmli / (AmE), / ˈfɜːmli / (BrE)
In a strong, secure, or definite way; with firmness or determination.
in a strong, determined way

Examples

She held the handlebars firmly as she rode down the hill.
The manager firmly rejected the proposal during the meeting.
Make sure the lid is firmly attached before you shake the bottle.

Synset Examples

held hard to the railing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: securely - in a safe or stable way, without risk of falling or shifting
  • Antonym: loosely - in a way that is not tight or firmly fixed

Etymology

From 'firm' + '-ly', with 'firm' from Latin 'firmus' (strong, stable).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00092514-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ ˈfɝːmli / (AmE), / ˈfɜːmli / (BrE)
in a way that shows strong determination or resolve
with strong determination

Examples

She firmly refused to change her decision despite the pressure.
He firmly believes that hard work will pay off in the end.
You need to speak firmly if you want to be taken seriously.

Synset Examples

we firmly believed it
you must stand firm

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resolutely - in an admirably determined way
  • Antonym: weakly - in a way that lacks strength or determination

Etymology

From 'firm' (adjective) + '-ly' (adverbial suffix).
