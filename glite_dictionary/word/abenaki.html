Abenaki

1. NOUN

/ ˌæbəˈnɑːki / (AmE), / ˌabəˈnɑːki / (BrE)
a member of an Indigenous Algonquian people of northern New England and southern Quebec
Indigenous group of New England and Quebec

Examples

The Abenaki have inhabited the region for centuries, preserving their unique cultural traditions.
An Abenaki elder shared stories about his ancestors with the students.
During the festival, several Abenaki participated in traditional dances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Algonquian - a family of Indigenous peoples and languages in North America

Etymology

From Western Abenaki *wαbənakiiak* (meaning 'people of the dawn land').
