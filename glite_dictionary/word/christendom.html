Christendom

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪs.tən.dəm / (AmE), / ˈkrɪs.tən.dəm / (BrE)
all Christians collectively, or the parts of the world where Christianity is the predominant religion, especially considered in a historical or cultural context.
the worldwide community of Christians

Examples

Medieval Europe was widely considered the heart of Christendom.
He argued that the values of Christendom have shaped Western civilization.
Pilgrims from across Christendom traveled to visit the holy sites.

Synset Examples

for a thousand years the Roman Catholic Church was the principal church of Christendom

Etymology

From Old English cristendōm (Christianity, Christians collectively), based on Christ + -dom (domain, state).
