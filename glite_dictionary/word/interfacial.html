interfacial

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪntərˈfeɪʃəl / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈfeɪʃəl / (BrE)
Relating to or situated at the boundary between two different substances, phases, or regions.
at a boundary between substances

Examples

The scientist studied the interfacial tension between oil and water.
Coatings can improve the interfacial bonding between metal and composite materials.
They observed unusual chemical reactions at the interfacial region where the two liquids met.

Synset Examples

an interfacial layer
interfacial tension is the surface tension at the interface between two liquids

Etymology

From interface + -al, indicating 'pertaining to an interface.'
