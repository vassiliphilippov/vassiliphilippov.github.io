rectum

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛktəm / (AmE), / ˈrɛktəm / (BrE)
the final section of the large intestine, ending at the anus
final part of the large intestine
medical

Examples

A colonoscopy allows the doctor to examine the rectum and entire colon for signs of disease.
During digestion, waste passes through the rectum before leaving the body.
He experienced discomfort in his rectum and decided to consult a gastroenterologist.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: anus - the opening at the end of the digestive tract through which waste leaves the body

Etymology

From Latin 'rectum intestinum' (straight intestine).
