Ozonium

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈzoʊniəm / (AmE), / əʊˈzəʊniəm / (BrE)
A genus of imperfect fungi characterized by their threadlike vegetative bodies, often found in soil or decayed plant matter.
genus of imperfect fungi

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species within the genus Ozonium in the forest soil.
The presence of Ozonium can indicate high levels of decaying organic material in an area.
Researchers studied the effects of temperature on the growth of Ozonium cultures.

Etymology

From Greek 'ozōn' (smelling) and Latin '-ium' (suffix for genera), indicating the odor associated with some species.

2. NOUN

/ oʊˈzoʊniəm / (AmE), / əʊˈzəʊniəm / (BrE)
a genus of imperfect fungi characterized by threadlike, often orange-brown mycelium, typically found on decaying material.
genus of imperfect fungi

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species within the Ozonium genus in the forest soil sample.
The orange strands indicated the presence of Ozonium growing on the decaying wood.
Identifying fungi like Ozonium often requires microscopic examination of the mycelium.

Etymology

From Greek 'ozōn' (smell) + New Latin suffix '-ium' used in taxonomy.
