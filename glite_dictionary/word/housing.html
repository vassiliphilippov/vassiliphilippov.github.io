housing

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaʊzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhaʊzɪŋ / (BrE)
a protective outer case or cover designed to contain or support a mechanical part or component
protective casing for a mechanical part

Examples

The engine's housing protects its internal components from dust and moisture.
He removed the metal housing to access the gears inside the machine.
A plastic housing was fitted over the wiring to prevent accidents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: case - a container or cover for protecting something mechanical
  • Synonym: chassis - the frame or casing for machinery or electronics

2. NOUN

/ ˈhaʊzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhaʊzɪŋ / (BrE)
A decorative covering for a horse, especially used for a warhorse or on ceremonial occasions.
decorative covering for a horse

Examples

The knight’s horse wore an elaborate housing embroidered with his family crest.
During the parade, each steed was draped in a brightly colored housing.
Historical paintings often depict warhorses adorned in ornate housings for battle.

Etymology

From Middle English 'housynge', meaning shelter or covering, related to 'house'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈhaʊzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhaʊzɪŋ / (BrE)
buildings or structures where people live, considered collectively; accommodation provided for people
buildings where people live collectively

Examples

Affordable housing is a major concern in big cities.
The government is planning to build new housing for low-income families.
There is an urgent need for student housing near the university.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accommodation - a place where people can live, stay, or work
  • Synonym: shelter - a place giving protection; a basic form of housing
