Muscari comosum

1. NOUN

/ mʌsˈkɛəri koʊˈmoʊsəm / (AmE), / mʌsˈkɑːri kəˈməʊsəm / (BrE)
A Mediterranean plant species, also known as tassel hyacinth, with bluish-violet sterile flowers in a tuft above the fertile flowers.
Mediterranean plant, tassel hyacinth

Examples

Muscari comosum blooms in late spring with elegant tassel-like flowers.
Gardeners often plant Muscari comosum for its vivid bluish-violet display.
The unique structure of Muscari comosum makes it a favorite among Mediterranean wildflowers enthusiasts.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Muscari', species epithet 'comosum' meaning 'hairy' or 'tufted'.
