Kalimantan

1. PROPN

/ ˌkɑːlɪˈmɑːntɑːn / (AmE), / ˌkælimænˈtɑːn / (BrE)
the Indonesian portion of the island of Borneo, occupying the southern and central parts of the island
Indonesian region on Borneo

Examples

She spent two years conducting research in Kalimantan on rainforest biodiversity.
Major rivers in Kalimantan are vital for transportation and local livelihoods.
Wildlife conservation is increasingly important in Kalimantan due to deforestation.

Etymology

From Indonesian 'Kalimantan', the name for the whole island of Borneo in that language.

2. PROPN

/ ˌkɑːlɪˈmɑːntɑːn / (AmE), / ˌkælɪˈmæntæn / (BrE)
the Indonesian portion of the island of Borneo, known for its extensive rainforests and natural resources
Indonesian part of Borneo island

Examples

The untouched rainforests of Kalimantan are home to many endangered species.
Many scientists travel to Kalimantan to research its unique biodiversity.
Illegal logging remains a major concern in Kalimantan's dense jungles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Borneo - the third largest island in the world, shared by Indonesia, Malaysia, and Brunei

Etymology

From Indonesian/Malay 'Kalimantan', the name for Borneo.
