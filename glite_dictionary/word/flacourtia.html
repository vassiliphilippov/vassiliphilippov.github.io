Flacourtia

1. NOUN

/ fləˈkɔrtiə / (AmE), / fləˈkɔːtiə / (BrE)
a genus of often spiny trees or shrubs native to tropical Asia and Africa, some species of which are cultivated for their fruit or as ornamentals
genus of tropical spiny trees or shrubs

Examples

Several species of Flacourtia are cultivated in gardens for their attractive foliage and fruit.
The fruit of the Flacourtia tree is eaten in some parts of Africa and Asia.
Botanists discovered a new species belonging to the Flacourtia genus in tropical India.

Etymology

Named after Étienne de Flacourt, a 17th-century French colonial governor and naturalist.
