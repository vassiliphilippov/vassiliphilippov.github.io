Macowanites

1. NOUN

/ ˌmækoʊˈwaɪnaɪts / (AmE), / ˌmakəʊˈwaɪnaɪts / (BrE)
A genus of stout-stemmed fungi in the family Secotiaceae, characterized by fruiting bodies that never expand completely.
genus of stout-stemmed fungi

Examples

The species in the genus Macowanites are notable for their unique fruiting bodies.
Researchers collected several samples of Macowanites from the forest floor.
Unlike related fungi, Macowanites does not fully expand its fruiting structures.

Etymology

Named after Scottish-South African botanist Peter MacOwan (1830–1909) with the standard suffix for fungal genera.
