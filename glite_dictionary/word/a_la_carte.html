a la carte

1. NOUN

/ ɑ lɑ ˈkɑrt / (AmE), / a la ˈkaːt / (BrE)
A menu or system in which each dish is ordered and priced separately, rather than as part of a set meal.
menu with individually priced dishes

Examples

The restaurant offers an a la carte option for those who prefer to choose individual dishes.
We decided to order from the a la carte menu rather than the fixed-price set.
Their new a la carte selection includes a variety of appetizers, main courses, and desserts.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: table d'hôte - a menu with a fixed meal and set price

Etymology

From French 'à la carte' meaning 'according to the menu.'

2. ADV

/ ɑ lɑ ˈkɑrt / (AmE), / a la ˈkɑːt / (BrE)
(of a menu or restaurant) allowing customers to choose individual dishes from a menu rather than having a fixed set meal.
ordering individual menu items

Examples

Many people prefer to dine a la carte so they can choose exactly what they want.
The restaurant offers both set menus and a la carte options for its customers.
She decided to order a la carte instead of choosing the three-course meal.

Synset Examples

we ate a la carte

Related Terms

  • Antonym: table d'hôte - meal where a fixed menu is offered at a set price

Etymology

From French 'à la carte' (according to the menu).

3. ADJ

/ ˌɑ lə ˈkɑrt / (AmE), / ˌæ lə ˈkɑːt / (BrE)
(of a menu or meal) allowing customers to choose individual dishes, each priced separately, rather than offering a set meal.
individually priced menu items

Examples

The restaurant offers an a la carte menu as well as several set menus.
We decided to order a la carte so we could try a variety of different dishes.
Unlike the buffet, everything on the a la carte menu was prepared fresh to order.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: table d'hôte - fixed-price meal with a set menu

Etymology

From French, meaning 'according to the menu'.
