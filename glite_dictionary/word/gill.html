gill

1. NOUN

/ ɡɪl / (AmE), / ɡɪl / (BrE)
a unit of liquid or dry capacity in the British Imperial system, equal to 5 fluid ounces or about 142 milliliters
British imperial capacity unit (5 fluid ounces)
british

Examples

The recipe calls for half a gill of milk.
A gill of ale was traditionally served in English taverns.
He measured the spirits precisely, pouring a gill into the jug.

Etymology

From Old French *gile*, possibly a diminutive based on Latin *gillo*, meaning a measure.

2. NOUN

/ ɡɪl / (AmE), / ɡɪl / (BrE)
a respiratory organ found in many aquatic animals, used to extract oxygen from water
respiratory organ in aquatic animals

Examples

Fish use their gills to absorb oxygen from the water.
The biologist examined the gills of the newly discovered species for signs of disease.
Unlike mammals, most aquatic animals rely on gills for respiration.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lung - respiratory organ in air-breathing animals

Etymology

From Old Norse 'gill' (a fish's gill).

3. NOUN

/ ɡɪl / (AmE), / ɡɪl / (BrE)
any of the thin, radiating, leaflike structures on the underside of the cap of a mushroom or similar fungus, where spores are produced
radiating structure under mushroom cap

Examples

Careful examination revealed the gills beneath the mushroom cap were lined with tiny spores.
The identification key uses the color of the gills to help distinguish between edible and poisonous species.
Moisture tends to collect on the gills, aiding the dispersal of spores in humid conditions.

4. NOUN

/ ɡɪl / (AmE), / ɡɪl / (BrE)
A United States unit of liquid measurement equal to 4 fluid ounces, or one quarter of a pint.
US liquid measure (4 fluid ounces)

Examples

The recipe calls for one gill of milk, which is the same as four ounces.
He poured out half a gill of brandy for each of his guests.
A gill is not a commonly used measure in modern American cooking, but it can still be found in old cookbooks.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: gill - a British liquid unit equal to 5 fluid ounces
  • Degree Lower: fluid ounce - a unit of liquid volume

Etymology

From Old French *guille* or Middle English *gille*, meaning vessel, possibly of Germanic origin.
