Volapuk

1. NOUN

/ ˌvoʊləˈpuːk / (AmE), / ˌvɒləˈpʊk / (BrE)
an artificial language created in the late 19th century for international communication, drawing on English, German, French, and Latin roots
constructed international auxiliary language

Examples

Few people today speak Volapuk, but it played a major role in the history of constructed languages.
The creator of Volapuk hoped it would be adopted for global communication.
Interest in Volapuk declined after Esperanto became more popular.

Etymology

From German 'Volapük', coined by Johann Martin Schleyer from 'vol' (world) + 'pük' (speak).
