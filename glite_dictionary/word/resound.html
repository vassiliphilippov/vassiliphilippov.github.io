to resound

1. VERB

/ rɪˈsaʊnd / (AmE), / rɪˈzaʊnd / (BrE)
(of a place or thing) to be filled with or echo with a sound or sounds; to ring out loudly.
echo or ring loudly with sound

Examples

The concert hall resounded with thunderous applause as the orchestra finished playing.
Her voice resounded through the empty church, filling the silence.
The mountain valley resounds with the calls of birds every morning.

Synset Examples

the hall resounded with laughter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: echo - to reflect or repeat a sound; a similar verb meaning

Etymology

From Old French resoner, from Latin resonare (to sound again, echo).

2. VERB

/ rɪˈsaʊnd / (AmE), / rɪˈzaʊnd / (BrE)
(of a place or object) to be filled with sound; to echo or emit a loud or prolonged noise.
to echo or be filled with sound

Examples

The cathedral walls resound with the choir’s singing every Sunday morning.
Cheers and applause resounded through the stadium after the final goal.
Her laughter resounded in the empty hallway, making everyone smile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: echo - to repeat or reflect a sound
  • Synonym: to reverberate - to be filled with or repeat a loud sound

Etymology

From Latin 'resonare' (to sound again), from 're-' (again) + 'sonare' (to sound).
