vanda

1. NOUN

/ ˈvæn.də / (AmE), / ˈvan.də / (BrE)
A tropical orchid of the genus Vanda, noted for its large, showy flowers arranged in loose clusters.
a tropical orchid with large, showy flowers

Examples

The gardener proudly displayed his rare vanda in the greenhouse during the flower show.
Many collectors appreciate the vivid colors of the vanda orchid.
She carefully watered her vandas every morning to ensure they thrived.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: orchid - any plant of the family Orchidaceae, typically with showy flowers

Etymology

From modern Latin Vanda, the genus name, derived from Sanskrit वन्द vāndā, the name for Vanda tessellata.
