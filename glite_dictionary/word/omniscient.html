omniscient

1. ADJ

/ ɑmˈnɪʃənt / (AmE), / ɒmˈnɪʃənt / (BrE)
having complete or unlimited knowledge, awareness, or understanding; infinitely wise.
having infinite knowledge or wisdom

Examples

Many religions describe their gods as omniscient beings who know everything in the universe.
The narrator in the novel is omniscient and understands the thoughts of every character.
Sometimes, teenagers think their parents are omniscient when it comes to family secrets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: all-knowing - having or appearing to have complete or unlimited knowledge
  • Antonym: ignorant - lacking knowledge or awareness

Etymology

From Latin 'omniscient-', from 'omnis' (all) + 'scire' (to know).
