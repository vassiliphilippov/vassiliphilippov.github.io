pyroxyline

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈrɑksəˌliːn / (AmE), / paɪˈrɒksɪliːn / (BrE)
a highly flammable form of nitrocellulose used in making collodion, plastics, and lacquers
flammable nitrocellulose used in plastics and lacquers
medical

Examples

Early photographic films were coated with pyroxyline to create a sensitive surface for image capture.
The factory stored barrels of pyroxyline, which made fire safety measures especially important.
Collodion made from pyroxyline was commonly used in 19th-century medical dressings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nitrocellulose - a highly flammable compound made by nitrating cellulose, used in explosives and plastics
  • Degree Higher: celluloid - a tough, highly flammable material made from nitrocellulose and camphor, used in early film and plastics

Etymology

From Greek pyr (fire) + oxy- (sharp, acid) + -line (suffix, as in cellulose derivatives).
