hepatotoxic

1. ADJ

/ ˌhɛpətoʊˈtɑksɪk / (AmE), / ˌhɛpətəʊˈtɒksɪk / (BrE)
harmful or toxic to the liver
toxic to the liver
medical

Examples

Some medications are discontinued if they prove to be hepatotoxic in clinical trials.
Researchers screened the compound for potential hepatotoxic effects before further development.
Long-term use of the drug can be hepatotoxic, so regular liver tests are recommended.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hepatotoxicity - the quality of being toxic to the liver
  • Antonym: hepatoprotective - serving to protect the liver

Etymology

From Greek hēpar (liver) + toxic.
