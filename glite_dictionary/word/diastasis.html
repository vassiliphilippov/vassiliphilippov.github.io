diastasis

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈæstəsɪs / (AmE), / daɪˈæstəsɪs / (BrE)
separation of parts of a bone, especially the epiphysis from the long bone to which it is attached, without a fracture
medical: bone separation without fracture
medical

Examples

A diastasis of the pubic symphysis can occur after childbirth.
The X-ray showed a diastasis between the epiphysis and the femoral shaft.
Prompt treatment is necessary to address diastasis in growing children.

Etymology

From Greek diastasis (separation), from diistanai (to separate).
