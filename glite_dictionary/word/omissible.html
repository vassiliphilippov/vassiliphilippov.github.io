omissible

1. ADJ

/ əˈmɪsəbəl / (AmE), / əˈmɪsɪbəl / (BrE)
Capable of being left out or omitted without loss or damage.
able to be omitted

Examples

Participation in the meeting is omissible for those not directly involved.
The author argued that several details were omissible without altering the main message.
Certain sections of the form are omissible if they do not apply to you.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: optional - left to personal choice or preference
  • Antonym: indispensable - absolutely necessary, cannot be omitted

Etymology

From Latin 'omittere' (to let go, omit) with the suffix '-ible' meaning 'able to be'.
