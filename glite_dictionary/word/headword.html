headword

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdˌwɝd / (AmE), / ˈhɛdˌwɜːd / (BrE)
A word or phrase placed at the beginning of a dictionary entry, often serving as the entry's title or subject.
main word in a dictionary entry

Examples

Each headword in the dictionary is printed in bold type.
Scholars debated which spelling should be used as the headword of the entry.
The glossary lists headwords alphabetically followed by their definitions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entry word - a word that is the subject of a dictionary or glossary entry

Etymology

From 'head' (meaning top or chief) + 'word'.

Source: WordNet

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdˌwɝd / (AmE), / ˈhɛdˌwɜːd / (BrE)
A word that functions as the main word in a phrase or entry, especially one that can be modified by other words or appears in a dictionary as the entry to which explanations and additional forms are attached.
main word in a phrase or dictionary entry

Examples

In the phrase 'red apple', 'apple' is the headword and 'red' is the modifier.
Each dictionary entry begins with a headword in bold type.
The headword determines the grammatical properties of the entire phrase.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lemma - base form of a word as used in dictionaries

Etymology

From 'head' + 'word', indicating the leading or primary word in a phrase or entry.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06302934-n
Synset POS: n
