procurer

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈkjʊrər / (AmE), / prəˈkjʊərə / (BrE)
a person who obtains or acquires something, often on behalf of others
someone who obtains or acquires

Examples

As the procurer of rare books, she had access to many exclusive libraries.
The company hired a procurer to source the finest ingredients from around the world.
He acted as a procurer of information for journalists during the investigation.

Synset Examples

the procurer of opera tickets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: provider - someone who supplies or makes available
  • Synonym: supplier - person or organization that provides goods or services

Etymology

From Middle French 'procureur', from Latin 'procurator' (manager, agent).

2. NOUN

/ prəˈkjʊrər / (AmE), / prəˈkjʊərə / (BrE)
a person who procures clients for prostitutes; a pimp
someone who finds clients for prostitutes

Examples

The police arrested the procurer during a raid on the brothel.
As a procurer, he was responsible for arranging meetings between clients and sex workers.
She accused her former employer of acting as a procurer for prostitutes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pimp - a person who manages prostitutes, finding clients for them

Etymology

From French 'procurer' (to procure), ultimately from Latin 'procurare' (to take care of, manage).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10453501-n
Synset POS: n
