full-grown

1. ADJ

/ fʊlˈɡroʊn / (AmE), / fʊlˈɡrəʊn / (BrE)
(of an animal, plant, or person) having reached full size or maturity; fully developed.
having reached full size or maturity

Examples

By the end of the year, the puppy was full-grown and much calmer.
A full-grown oak tree can provide shelter for hundreds of animals.
It is dangerous to approach a full-grown lion in the wild.

Synset Examples

an adult animal
a grown woman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mature - fully developed physically; full-grown
  • Antonym: immature - not fully developed or mature

Etymology

Formed from 'full' + 'grown', meaning fully grown or mature.
