permissibility

1. NOUN

/ pərˌmɪsəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / pəˌmɪsɪˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being permitted or allowable; the extent to which something is permitted by law or by rules
quality of being permitted or allowable

Examples

The permissibility of certain drugs varies greatly from country to country.
Questions were raised about the permissibility of using cell phones during the exam.
The court debated the permissibility of the newly introduced evidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: admissibility - the quality of being acceptable or allowed, especially in a court of law
  • Antonym: prohibition - the act of forbidding something by law or authority

Etymology

From Latin 'permissibilis' (allowable), from 'permittere' (to let through, permit), with '-ity' forming a noun of quality.
