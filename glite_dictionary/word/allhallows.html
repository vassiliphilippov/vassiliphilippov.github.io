Allhallows

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔlˌhæloʊz / (AmE), / ˈɔːlˌhæləz / (BrE)
An old name for All Saints' Day, a Christian feast day honoring all the saints, observed on November 1st.
old name for All Saints' Day
archaic

Examples

Many churches hold special services on Allhallows to commemorate the saints.
In medieval England, people would attend mass on Allhallows as part of the holy observance.
The tradition of Allhallows dates back to the 9th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: All Saints' Day - a Christian festival honoring all the saints on November 1st

Etymology

From Middle English 'al halwes', meaning 'all saints', from Old English 'ealra halgena'.
