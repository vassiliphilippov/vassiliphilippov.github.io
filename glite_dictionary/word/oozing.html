oozing

1. NOUN

/ ˈuːzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈuːzɪŋ / (BrE)
the slow, steady movement of a liquid or substance as it seeps out through small openings
the process of seeping out

Examples

The oozing of sap from the tree attracted a swarm of insects.
There was a slow oozing of water from the cracked pipe.
The oozing of lava from the volcano continued for several days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seepage - the process by which a liquid flows slowly through small holes or spaces
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07446719-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈuːzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈuːzɪŋ / (BrE)
Leaking or seeping out slowly, especially as a thick liquid.
slowly leaking out

Examples

The wound was oozing blood despite being bandaged.
Thick syrup was oozing from the sides of the pancake stack.
Her shoes were oozing mud after walking through the marsh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to seeping - flowing or leaking slowly through small openings

Etymology

Present participle of 'ooze', from Old English 'wōs' (juice, sap).
