baronetage

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːr.ə.nɪ.tɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈbær.ə.nɪ.tɪdʒ / (BrE)
the rank or dignity of a baronet; the collective body or institution of baronets
rank of a baronet; body of baronets
british

Examples

He inherited the baronetage after his father's death in 1885.
The institution of the baronetage played a unique role in British society.
Titles of the baronetage are often passed down through generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baronet - a hereditary title awarded by the British Crown, below a baron

Etymology

From 'baronet' + '-age', coined in English in the early 17th century following introduction of the baronetcy by James I.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbær.ə.nɪt.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈbær.ə.nɪt.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
The whole class or collective body of baronets; the rank or dignity of holding a baronetcy.
the collective body of baronets
british

Examples

The history of the baronetage is closely tied to the British honor system.
He inherited his title as the latest addition to the baronetage.
Many notable families are listed in the official registry of the baronetage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peerage - the collective body of peers

Etymology

From baronet + -age, following the pattern of words like 'peerage'.
