Lycosa tarentula

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈkoʊsə təˈrɛntjʊlə / (AmE), / lɪˈkəʊsə tʌˈrɛntjʊlə / (BrE)
A large wolf spider native to southern Europe, once believed to cause tarantism, a condition of uncontrollable movement.
large southern European spider

Examples

The bite of the Lycosa tarentula was once mistakenly thought to cause wild dancing, known as tarantism.
Researchers found a Lycosa tarentula in the fields near southern Italy.
Although the Lycosa tarentula looks menacing, it is generally not dangerous to humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tarantula - large hairy spider; unrelated but often confused with Lycosa tarentula

Etymology

From Latin: Lycosa (a genus of wolf spiders) + tarentula (referring to Taranto, Italy, where the spider is common).
