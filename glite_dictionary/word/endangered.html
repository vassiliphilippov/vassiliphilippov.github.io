endangered

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈdeɪndʒɚd / (AmE), / ɪnˈdeɪndʒəd / (BrE)
(of plants or animals) at serious risk of extinction; likely to disappear soon unless protected.
at risk of extinction (plants or animals)

Examples

Many countries are working to protect endangered species in their natural habitats.
The giant panda is one of the most well-known endangered animals in the world.
Illegal poaching has pushed several rhino species to become critically endangered.

Synset Examples

an endangered species

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: extinct - no longer in existence (of a species)
  • Degree Lower: threatened - at risk of becoming endangered, but less severe

Etymology

From prefix 'en-' meaning 'in' or 'put in' and 'dangered', from 'danger'.
