Llew Llaw Gyffes

1. PROPN

/ ɬjuː ɬaʊ ˈɡɪfɛs / (AmE), / ɬjuː ɬaʊ ˈɡɪfɛs / (BrE)
A heroic figure in Welsh mythology, the son of Gwydion and Arianrhod, known for overcoming curses and supported by magical powers.
hero of Welsh mythology

Examples

The legend of Llew Llaw Gyffes is an important part of Welsh folklore.
Many stories recount how Llew Llaw Gyffes overcame the curse placed on him by his mother.
Scholars debate the symbolic meaning behind the feats of Llew Llaw Gyffes in the Mabinogi.

Etymology

From Welsh myth, name meaning 'the bright one with the skillful hand.'
