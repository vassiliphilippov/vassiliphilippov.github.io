Menuridae

1. NOUN

/ məˈnʊrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / mɛˈnjʊərɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of Australian birds known as lyrebirds, notable for the males' elaborate tail feathers and remarkable ability to mimic sounds.
family of birds (lyrebirds)
medical

Examples

The family Menuridae consists of two species known as lyrebirds, native to Australia.
Scientists have long studied the mimicry skills unique to the Menuridae.
Within Menuridae, the superb lyrebird is particularly famous for its impressive tail display.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lyrebird - Australian bird with a tail shaped like a lyre, known for its mimicry

Etymology

From modern Latin Menura (the type genus) + -idae (zoological family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ mɛˈnʊrəˌdaɪ / (AmE), / mɛˈnjʊərɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of birds native to Australia, comprising the lyrebirds, known for their elaborate tail feathers and remarkable ability to mimic sounds.
family of Australian lyrebirds

Examples

The Menuridae family is renowned for the males' spectacular tail plumage displayed during courtship.
Scientists classify lyrebirds under the family Menuridae.
Unique to Australia, the Menuridae are famous for their complex vocal mimicry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lyrebird - Australian bird noted for ornate tail and sound mimicry

Etymology

From New Latin Menura (type genus) + -idae, a zoological family suffix.
