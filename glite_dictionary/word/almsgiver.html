almsgiver

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːlmzˌɡɪvər / (AmE), / ˈɑːlmzˌɡɪvə / (BrE)
a person who gives money, food, or other help to people in need, especially as a charitable act
person who gives alms

Examples

The almsgiver distributed loaves of bread to the homeless every Sunday.
Many religious traditions praise the almsgiver for their generosity and compassion.
She became known in the village as a quiet almsgiver who never sought recognition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benefactor - a person who gives help, especially financial help, to a person or cause

Etymology

From Middle English, from 'alms' (charity) + 'giver' (one who gives).
