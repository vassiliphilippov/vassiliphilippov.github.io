chute

1. NOUN

/ tʃuːt / (AmE), / tʃuːt / (BrE)
A piece of equipment, such as a parachute, that fills with air and slows the fall of a person or object from a height.
rescue device that slows a fall

Examples

The pilot relied on his chute to land safely after ejecting from the plane.
All skydivers should check their chute before every jump to ensure it works properly.
Emergency chutes were deployed when the aircraft encountered engine failure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parachute - device for slowing a fall by creating air resistance, typically used for rescue

Etymology

From French 'chute' meaning 'fall'.

2. NOUN

/ ʃuːt / (AmE), / ʃuːt / (BrE)
A sloping channel or passage down which people or things can descend or slide.
a sloping passage for sliding things down

Examples

The laundry was dropped down the chute to the basement.
Children took turns going down the water chute at the park.
Emergency exits included a special chute for rapid evacuation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slide - a structure with a smooth sloping surface for sliding down

Etymology

From French 'chute' meaning 'fall', from Old French 'cheoir' (to fall).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03033305-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ʃuːt / (AmE), / ʃuːt / (BrE)
to jump from an aircraft and descend to the ground using a parachute.
descend from an aircraft with a parachute

Examples

The skydivers prepared to chute from the plane at 10,000 feet.
He had never chuted before but was eager to try skydiving for the first time.
Several soldiers chuted into the drop zone during the military exercise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parachute - to descend from an aircraft with a parachute

Etymology

Shortened from 'parachute', from French *parachute*, from Italian *para-* (protect) + *scudo* (shield).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
