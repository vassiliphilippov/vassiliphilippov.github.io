Piaget

1. PROPN

/ piˈɑʒeɪ / (AmE), / piˈɑːʒeɪ / (BrE)
(1896–1980) Swiss psychologist known for his pioneering studies on cognitive development in children.
Swiss psychologist; child development theory

Examples

Piaget developed theories that transformed our understanding of how children learn and think.
Many educational programs are influenced by the work of Piaget.
Psychology students often study Piaget's stages of cognitive development.
