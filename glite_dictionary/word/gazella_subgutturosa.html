Gazella subgutturosa

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈzɛl.ə ˌsʌb.ɡʌˈtʊr.oʊ.sə / (AmE), / ɡəˈzɛl.ə ˌsʌb.ɡʌˈtʊər.ə.sə / (BrE)
A species of gazelle native to parts of western and central Asia, known as the goitered gazelle.
a species of gazelle in Asia

Examples

The Gazella subgutturosa is often found in dry grasslands and deserts of Asia.
Researchers tagged several Gazella subgutturosa to monitor their migration patterns.
Unlike some other gazelles, the Gazella subgutturosa prefers open plains to wooded areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goitered gazelle - alternative common name for Gazella subgutturosa

Etymology

From Latin: 'Gazella' for gazelle, and 'subgutturosa' meaning 'with a somewhat swollen throat'.
