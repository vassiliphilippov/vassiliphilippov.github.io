to acquit

1. VERB

/ əˈkwɪt / (AmE), / əˈkwɪt / (BrE)
to officially declare someone not guilty of a criminal charge in a court of law
declare not guilty (legal)
legal

Examples

After a lengthy trial, the jury decided to acquit the defendant of all charges.
The judge explained the reasons for his decision to acquit the accused.
Despite the evidence, the court chose to acquit him, citing insufficient proof.

Synset Examples

The suspect was cleared of the murder charges

Related Terms

  • Antonym: convict - to find or declare someone guilty of a crime

Etymology

From Old French *aquiter*, from Late Latin *acquitare* (to settle or pay a debt).
