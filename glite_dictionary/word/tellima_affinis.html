Tellima affinis

1. NOUN

/ tɛˈlɪmə əˈfɪnɪs / (AmE), / tɛˈlɪmə əˈfɪnɪs / (BrE)
A perennial herb native to California, cultivated for its racemose white flowers with widely spreading petals; sometimes placed in the genus Tellima.
a California perennial herb with white flowers

Examples

The shady woodland garden featured several Tellima affinis plants blooming in the spring.
Botanists often classify Tellima affinis within the saxifrage family.
Gardeners value Tellima affinis for its delicate, widely spreading petals and ease of cultivation.

Etymology

Tellima: New Latin, anagram of 'mitella'; affinis: Latin meaning 'related' or 'similar'.
