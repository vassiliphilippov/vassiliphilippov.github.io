saleratus

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæləˈreɪtəs / (AmE), / ˌsæləˈreɪtəs / (BrE)
A white, soluble compound (sodium bicarbonate) formerly used in baking powders and effervescent drinks, and as an antacid.
historical term for sodium bicarbonate

Examples

Early recipes for biscuits often called for saleratus as a leavening agent.
The chemist explained that saleratus was the precursor to modern baking soda.
Pioneers carried saleratus on their journeys to use in cooking and cleaning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baking soda - sodium bicarbonate, used in baking and as an antacid

Etymology

From Latin 'sal aeratus' meaning 'aerated salt'.
