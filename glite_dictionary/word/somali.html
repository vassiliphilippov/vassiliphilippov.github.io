Somali

1. ADJ

/ soʊˈmɑːli / (AmE), / səʊˈmɑːli / (BrE)
relating to Somalia, its people, language, or culture
of Somalia or its people/language/culture

Examples

The Somali coastline is the longest on the mainland of Africa.
Traditional Somali music combines poetry and dancing.
She prepared a Somali dish for the international festival.

Synset Examples

Somali coffee is excellent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Somalia - country in eastern Africa

Etymology

From Somali Soomaali, the name of the people and language.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03130850-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ soʊˈmɑːli / (AmE), / səʊˈmɑːli / (BrE)
a member of a people native to Somalia, typically tall and of East African descent, most of whom are Muslim
a person from Somalia or Somali ethnicity

Examples

Many Somali have settled in cities across Europe and North America.
The Somali are known for their rich tradition of oral poetry.
A group of Somali gathered to celebrate their country's independence day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Somalian - a person from Somalia

Etymology

From Somali *Soomaali*, the native name of the people and language.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09769211-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ soʊˈmɑli / (AmE), / səˈmɑːli / (BrE)
the Cushitic language spoken in Somalia and neighboring regions, mainly by the Somali people
the main language of Somalia

Examples

Many documents were translated into Somali to reach a wider audience in East Africa.
He is fluent in both Somali and English, often acting as an interpreter.
The Somali language uses a Latin-based script for official purposes.

Etymology

From Somali *Soomaali*, the name for the people and language.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07003664-n
Synset POS: n
