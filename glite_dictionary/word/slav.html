Slav

1. NOUN

/ slæv / (AmE), / slɑːv / (BrE)
a member of a group of peoples of eastern Europe and Asian Russia who speak a Slavic (Slavonic) language, such as Russian, Polish, Czech, or Serbian
member of a Slavic-speaking people

Examples

Poland, Russia, and the Czech Republic are countries where many Slavs live.
The migration of the Slavs into Europe had a significant impact on its cultural landscape.
She is a proud Slav, deeply connected to her family's heritage and language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Slavic - relating to the Slavs or their languages

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *Slavus*, from Old Church Slavonic *slovĕne* (Slavs).

2. ADJ

/ slæv / (AmE), / slɑːv / (BrE)
relating to people who speak a Slavic language or to their languages and cultures
relating to Slavic people or languages

Examples

The Slav communities have preserved many traditional customs.
Researchers are studying Slav languages to trace their origins.
The festival showcased Slav music and dance from several countries.

Synset Examples

the Slav population of Georgia

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Slavic - relating to the Slavs or their languages

Etymology

From Medieval Latin Slavus, from Byzantine Greek Sklabos, referring to Slavic people.
