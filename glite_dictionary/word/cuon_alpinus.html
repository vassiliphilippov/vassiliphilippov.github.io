Cuon alpinus

1. NOUN

/ kuːˌɑn ælˈpaɪnəs / (AmE), / kjuːˌɒn ælˈpiːnəs / (BrE)
A fierce wild dog native to the forests of central and southeast Asia that hunts in packs; also known as the dhole.
Asian wild dog species (dhole)

Examples

The Cuon alpinus is known for its exceptional teamwork when hunting prey.
Researchers are studying the habitat loss threatening the survival of Cuon alpinus across Asia.
Unlike wolves, the Cuon alpinus often forms larger, more cooperative packs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dhole - common name for Cuon alpinus, an Asian wild dog

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Cuon' from Greek 'kuōn' (dog); species 'alpinus' meaning 'of the mountains'.
