Tragopogon porrifolius

1. NOUN

/ ˌtræɡəˈpoʊɡən pɔːˈrɪfoʊliəs / (AmE), / ˌtræɡəˈpɒɡən pɒːˈrɪfəliəs / (BrE)
A Mediterranean biennial herb with purple ray flowers and long edible root, often called salsify or oyster plant.
Mediterranean herb with edible root (salsify)

Examples

Tragopogon porrifolius is often cultivated for its edible root, which has a mild flavor reminiscent of oysters.
Gardeners in the United States sometimes grow Tragopogon porrifolius as a biennial vegetable for its nutritional value.
The long, slender root of Tragopogon porrifolius can be roasted or boiled and added to soups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salsify - Common name for Tragopogon porrifolius, edible root vegetable
  • Synonym: oyster plant - Another common name for Tragopogon porrifolius

Etymology

From Latin, combining Greek 'tragos' (goat) and 'pogon' (beard) for the genus, and Latin 'porrum' (leek) and 'folium' (leaf) for species.
