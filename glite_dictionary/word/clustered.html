clustered

1. ADJ

/ ˈklʌstɚd / (AmE), / ˈklʌstəd / (BrE)
Grouped closely together, often without forming a single coherent structure.
grouped closely together

Examples

The wildflowers were clustered at the edge of the meadow, creating a patch of color.
Data points appeared clustered in the lower left corner of the graph.
Several cottages are clustered around the old village church.

Synset Examples

an agglomerated flower head

Related Terms

  • Antonym: scattered - spread widely apart rather than grouped closely

Etymology

From the noun 'cluster', ultimately from Old English 'clyster' (bunch, cluster).

2. ADJ

/ ˈklʌstɚd / (AmE), / ˈklʌstəd / (BrE)
growing or grouped close together, but not packed densely
growing or grouped closely together

Examples

The wildflowers were clustered together in small patches across the field.
Mussels can often be found clustered on rocks near the shoreline.
Several villages are clustered along the riverbank, each within walking distance of the next.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: scattered - spread over a wide area, not grouped together

Etymology

From 'cluster' (Middle English, from Old English 'clyster', meaning 'bunch') plus '-ed' suffix forming past participle/adjective.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02261292-s
Synset POS: s
