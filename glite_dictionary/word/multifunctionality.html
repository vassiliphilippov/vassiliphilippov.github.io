multifunctionality

1. NOUN

/ ˌmʌltiˌfʌŋkʃəˈnæləti / (AmE), / ˌmʌltɪˌfʌŋkʃəˈnæləti / (BrE)
The quality or state of having multiple functions or purposes.
the state of having multiple functions

Examples

The multifunctionality of smartphones makes them essential in modern life.
The architect praised the building's multifunctionality, noting it could host both meetings and exhibitions.
Many kitchen tools now feature multifunctionality to save space and increase convenience.

Synset Examples

There is some controversy concerning the appropriateness of including food security and rural employment as aspects of the multifunctionality of agriculture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: versatility - ability to adapt or be used for many different purposes
  • Antonym: specialization - the process of concentrating on a single function or activity

Etymology

From multi- (many) + function + -ality (noun suffix forming nouns of quality or state).
