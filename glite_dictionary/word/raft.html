raft

1. VERB

/ ræft / (AmE), / rɑːft / (BrE)
to bind or assemble (logs, planks, etc.) together to form a raft
to assemble into a raft

Examples

They decided to raft the fallen logs to transport them downstream.
Local workers rafted bamboo poles together to make a temporary bridge across the river.
He spent the morning rafting planks for the raft-building contest.

7. NOUN

/ ræft / (AmE), / rɑːft / (BrE)
a flat structure, typically made of logs or planks, fastened together for use as a boat or floating platform
flat floating structure for transport or swimming

Examples

After the bridge collapsed, the group built a raft to cross the river.
Many swimmers rested on the floating raft in the middle of the lake.
They lashed logs together to create a sturdy raft for their supplies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barge - a flat-bottomed boat for carrying freight
  • Degree Higher: boat - a small vessel for traveling on water

Etymology

From Middle English 'rafte', from Old Norse 'raptr' (log).
