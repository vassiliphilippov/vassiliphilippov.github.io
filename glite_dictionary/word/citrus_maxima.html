Citrus maxima

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪtrəs ˈmæksɪmə / (AmE), / ˈsɪtrəs ˈmæksɪmə / (BrE)
A large, southeastern Asian tree of the citrus family that produces pomelo fruits, which resemble large grapefruits.
southeastern Asian citrus tree; the pomelo

Examples

The Citrus maxima is commonly grown throughout Southeast Asia for its sweet, juicy fruit.
Botanists classify the pomelo as Citrus maxima, distinguishing it from grapefruits and other citrus species.
Farmers cultivate Citrus maxima both for its fruit and ornamental value in tropical regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pomelo - the large citrus fruit produced by Citrus maxima
  • Degree Lower: grapefruit - a related citrus fruit, smaller and more bitter than pomelo

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Citrus' meaning the citrus genus and 'maxima' meaning largest.
