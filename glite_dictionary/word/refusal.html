refusal

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈfjuːzəl / (AmE), / rɪˈfjuːzəl / (BrE)
an act of or a message stating that something offered or requested is not accepted; a rejection
an act or statement of rejecting something

Examples

His refusal to join the team surprised everyone.
The company sent a formal refusal to the applicant.
Despite several offers, her refusal remained firm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rejection - the act of dismissing or refusing an offer, proposal, or application
  • Antonym: acceptance - the act of consenting to receive or undertake something offered

Etymology

From Old French *refuser* (to refuse) via Latin *refusare* (to reject, refuse).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈfjuːzəl / (AmE), / rɪˈfjuːzəl / (BrE)
The act of saying or showing that you will not do, accept, or allow something.
the act of refusing

Examples

Her refusal to apologize only made the situation worse.
Despite repeated offers, his refusal remained firm.
The manager expressed disappointment at the client's refusal to sign the contract.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acceptance - the act of agreeing or consenting

Etymology

From Old French *refus* (refusal), from Latin *refūsus*, past participle of *refundere* (to pour back, reject).
