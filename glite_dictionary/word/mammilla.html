mammilla

1. NOUN

/ məˈmɪlə / (AmE), / məˈmɪlə / (BrE)
a small, rounded projection of a mammary gland, especially the nipple
small projection of a mammary gland; nipple
medical

Examples

The doctor examined the patient's mammilla for signs of infection.
In mammals, the mammilla plays a crucial role in nursing the young.
The textbook included a detailed diagram of the human mammilla and surrounding tissues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nipple - the protruding part of the breast through which milk can be drawn

Etymology

From Latin 'mammilla', diminutive of 'mamma' (breast, udder).
