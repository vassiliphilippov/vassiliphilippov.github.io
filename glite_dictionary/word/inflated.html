inflated

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈfleɪtɪd / (AmE), / ɪnˈfleɪtɪd / (BrE)
exaggerated or enlarged beyond what is reasonable, true, or normal; made to seem greater or more important than it really is
exaggerated or excessive

Examples

The company made inflated claims about the effectiveness of its product.
Many people believe the housing market has inflated prices at the moment.
His inflated ego made him difficult to work with.

Synset Examples

a hyperbolic style

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exaggerated - Represented as greater than is true or reasonable
  • Antonym: deflated - Made smaller, less important, or less confident

Etymology

From Latin inflatus, past participle of inflare (to blow into, puff up).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈfleɪtəd / (AmE), / ɪnˈfleɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of language, ideas, or claims) pretentious or excessively grand in style or tone.
pretentious or grandiose (especially of language)

Examples

The politician's inflated promises were met with skepticism by the public.
She was known for her inflated speeches, filled with complicated words and grandiose ideas.
His inflated claims about his achievements made him sound arrogant and untrustworthy.

Synset Examples

high-flown talk of preserving the moral tone of the school
a high-sounding dissertation on the means to attain social revolution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretentious - attempting to impress by affecting greater importance or merit than is actually possessed
  • Synonym: bombastic - high-sounding but with little meaning; inflated
  • Antonym: modest - not excessively grand or boastful; humble

Etymology

From Latin inflatus, past participle of 'inflāre' (to blow into, inflate).
