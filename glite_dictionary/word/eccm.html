ECCM

1. NOUN

/ iː siː siː ɛm / (AmE), / iː siː siː ɛm / (BrE)
electronic counter-countermeasures; techniques used in electronic warfare to ensure effective use of the electromagnetic spectrum by friendly forces despite enemy countermeasures.
techniques countering enemy electronic warfare

Examples

The military invested in advanced ECCM systems to protect their communications during the operation.
Modern aircraft are equipped with both ECM and ECCM capabilities for superior defense.
The success of the mission depended on effective ECCM to prevent jamming by enemy forces.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ECM - electronic countermeasures; techniques to impair enemy electronic systems

Etymology

Acronym for Electronic Counter-Countermeasures.
