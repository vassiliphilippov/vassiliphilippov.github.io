physa

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪsə / (AmE), / ˈfʌɪsə / (BrE)
A freshwater snail belonging to the genus Physa, typically found in ponds and slow-moving water.
a freshwater snail of the genus Physa

Examples

Biologists collected several physa specimens from the pond for study.
The shell shape of a physa helps distinguish it from other freshwater snails.
In some ecosystems, physa play an essential role in controlling algae growth.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: snail - a gastropod mollusk with a coiled shell

Etymology

From New Latin Physa, the genus name, derived from Greek physa (bladder, bubble).
