overlay

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvərˌleɪ / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəˌleɪ / (BrE)
a protective covering, such as a layer of boards placed over the basic framework of a building, used to strengthen it and provide a base for weatherproofing
protective covering used in building construction

Examples

The builders installed an overlay of plywood to reinforce the floor.
A moisture barrier was added beneath the overlay for extra protection.
This type of overlay is essential before applying exterior siding.

Etymology

From over- + lay, meaning 'laid over the top'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04194276-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvərˌleɪ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈleɪ / (BrE)
a layer of decorative material such as gold leaf or veneer that is applied to the surface of something
decorative layer on a surface

Examples

The antique table featured an intricate overlay of gold leaf around the edges.
A decorative wood overlay added elegance to the cabinet doors.
Restorers carefully reapplied the missing overlay to match the original design.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inlay - a decoration set into the surface of something
  • Synonym: veneer - a thin decorative covering of fine wood applied to a coarser wood

Etymology

From Old English 'oferlecgan', meaning to lay over.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03870372-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈleɪ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈleɪ / (BrE)
to cover the surface of something with a layer of another material
cover with a layer on top

Examples

Before serving, overlay the cake with a thin layer of chocolate ganache.
A transparent map can overlay the satellite view to show the city's expansion.
He likes to overlay his photos with digital effects for a dramatic look.

Synset Examples

cover the meat with a lot of gravy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cover - to place something upon or over, so as to protect or conceal

Etymology

From Old English 'oferlecgan', from 'ofer' (over) + 'lecgan' (to lay).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
