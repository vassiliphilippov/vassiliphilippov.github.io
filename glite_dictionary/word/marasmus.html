marasmus

1. NOUN

/ məˈræz.məs / (AmE), / məˈraz.məs / (BrE)
Severe malnutrition and wasting, especially in children, resulting from inadequate intake or absorption of nutrients.
severe malnutrition, especially in children
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the child with marasmus after observing severe weight loss and muscle wasting.
Poor nutrition and famine can lead to marasmus in vulnerable populations.
Early intervention is crucial in treating marasmus to prevent long-term health consequences.

Etymology

From Greek 'marasmos' (withering), from 'marainnein' (to waste away).
