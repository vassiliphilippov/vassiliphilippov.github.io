venture

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛntʃər / (AmE), / ˈvɛntʃə / (BrE)
a risky or daring project, business, or activity, especially one with an uncertain outcome
a risky or daring undertaking

Examples

Starting a new business is always a venture filled with both opportunity and risk.
The expedition to the Arctic was a dangerous venture for the scientists involved.
They invested heavily in the joint venture between the two companies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enterprise - a project or undertaking, especially one that is difficult or requires effort
  • Synonym: risk - the possibility of loss or danger
  • Antonym: certainty - something that is sure to occur or be true

Etymology

From Old French 'aventure' (chance, accident) via Latin 'adventura' (about to happen).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɛntʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈvɛntʃə / (BrE)
An investment or business involving considerable risk of loss but also the possibility of substantial profit.
risky investment or business

Examples

The new tech startup was a bold venture with the potential for huge returns.
Many investors lost money in the risky venture when the market crashed.
Starting a restaurant is often considered a high-risk venture in the business world.

Synset Examples

he knew the stock was a speculation when he bought it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speculation - investment involving risk for the chance of profit
  • Synonym: enterprise - a project or business, especially one that is new and involves risk
  • Antonym: sure thing - an investment with little or no risk

Etymology

From Old French 'aventure', from Latin 'adventura' (thing about to happen).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13363707-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈvɛntʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈvɛntʃə / (BrE)
to go somewhere or do something that involves risk or danger
go in spite of risk or danger

Examples

They decided to venture into the abandoned building despite the warnings.
Few tourists venture into the remote parts of the forest after dark.
She was nervous but chose to venture outside her comfort zone and try skydiving.

Synset Examples

We ventured into the world of high-tech and bought a supercomputer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dare - to have the courage to do something risky
  • Synonym: risk - to expose oneself to danger or loss

Etymology

From Old French 'aventure' (adventure, chance), from Latin 'adventura' (a thing about to happen).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02378427-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈvɛn.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈvɛn.tʃə / (BrE)
to expose to the risk of loss or harm; to undertake something that may result in danger or failure
put at risk; undertake risk

Examples

She decided to venture her savings on a new business opportunity.
He would never venture his reputation by making unsubstantiated claims.
Despite the risks, the company chose to venture into new markets.

Synset Examples

I will stake my good reputation for this

Related Terms

  • Synonym: risk - to expose to danger or harm
  • Antonym: to protect - to keep safe from harm or risk

Etymology

From Old French 'aventure' (chance, risk) via Latin 'adventura' (what must happen), related to 'advenire' (to arrive).

5. NOUN

/ ˈvɛntʃər / (AmE), / ˈventʃə / (BrE)
a business or commercial project, especially one that involves risk or uncertainty
risky business project

Examples

They started a joint venture to develop renewable energy solutions.
Her latest venture in the fashion industry has proven very successful.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enterprise - a project or activity, especially one that is difficult or requires effort; often used as a synonym for a business venture

Etymology

From Old French aventure (a happening, risk) and Latin adventura (a thing about to happen).

6. VERB

/ ˈvɛntʃər / (AmE), / ˈvɛntʃə / (BrE)
to say or suggest something, such as a guess or opinion, even though you may be wrong or criticized for it
say or suggest something despite risk of being wrong

Examples

I hesitate to venture an opinion in such a complex situation.
She ventured a guess that the meeting would end early.
He ventured that the new policy might not succeed as intended.

Synset Examples

I am guessing that the price of real estate will rise again
I cannot pretend to say that you are wrong

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to suggest - to mention or introduce (an idea, proposition, plan, etc.) for consideration or possible action
  • Synonym: to assert - to state a fact or belief confidently and forcefully
  • Antonym: to withdraw - to take back or retract (a statement, opinion, or proposal)

Etymology

From Old French 'aventure' (chance, accident, occurrence), from Latin 'adventura' (a thing about to happen).
