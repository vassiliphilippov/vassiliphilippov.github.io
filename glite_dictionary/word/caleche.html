caleche

1. NOUN

/ kɑːˈleɪʃ / (AmE), / kæˈleɪʃ / (BrE)
A large, folded, hooped hood worn by women in the 18th century.
18th-century woman's hooped hood
archaic

Examples

The portrait depicts the lady in a velvet gown and a distinctive caleche.
To protect her elaborate hairstyle, she placed the caleche over her head before stepping outside.
During the 18th century, the caleche was a fashionable accessory among aristocratic women.

Etymology

From French 'calèche', originally meaning a type of carriage, transferred to the hood due to its similar shape.

2. NOUN

/ kəˈleɪʃ / (AmE), / kaˈlɛʃ / (BrE)
the folding hood of a horse-drawn carriage, designed to protect passengers from the weather
folding hood of a carriage

Examples

The caleche was raised quickly when the rain began to fall.
Resting beneath the caleche, the passengers remained dry and comfortable throughout the journey.
The old carriage had a beautifully restored caleche that could still fold back smoothly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carriage - a horse-drawn vehicle

Etymology

From French 'calèche', originally from Italian 'calessa' (carriage).
