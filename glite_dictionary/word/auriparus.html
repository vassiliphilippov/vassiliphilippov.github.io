Auriparus

1. NOUN

/ ɔːˈrɪpərʌs / (AmE), / ɔːˈrɪpərəs / (BrE)
A genus of small passerine birds in the family Paridae, containing the verdin.
genus of birds (verdins)

Examples

The Auriparus is best known for its only species, the verdin.
Ornithologists have classified the verdin under the genus Auriparus.
Unlike most Paridae, the Auriparus is found mainly in arid regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: verdin - A small songbird (Auriparus flaviceps) of southwestern North America

Etymology

From Latin 'aurum' (gold) + 'parus' (titmouse).

2. NOUN

/ ɔːˈrɪpərəs / (AmE), / ɔːˈrɪpərəs / (BrE)
A genus of small songbirds in the family Paridae, containing only the verdin species, found in arid regions of North America.
bird genus; contains the verdin

Examples

The Auriparus genus is represented solely by the verdin bird in North America.
Birdwatchers in the desert eagerly search for members of the Auriparus genus.
Some ornithologists have studied the nesting behavior of Auriparus species extensively.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: verdin - a small, grayish songbird found in southwestern North America, the sole member of Auriparus
