milliequivalent

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪliɪˈkwɪvələnt / (AmE), / ˌmɪliɪˈkwɪvələnt / (BrE)
A unit equal to one-thousandth of a chemical equivalent, used to express the amount of a substance based on its chemical activity or combining capacity.
one-thousandth of a chemical equivalent
medical

Examples

The doctor calculated the patient's potassium level in milliequivalents per liter.
You may see the dose expressed as milliequivalent on some medication labels.
Accurate measurement in milliequivalents is essential in electrolyte balance studies.

Etymology

From milli- (meaning 'one thousandth') + equivalent.
