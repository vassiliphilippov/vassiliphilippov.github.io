outwardness

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊt.wɚd.nəs / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.wəd.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being concerned with or responsive to external things, especially material objects rather than abstract ideas
focus on external or material things

Examples

Her outwardness made her popular at social gatherings, as she was very engaging and approachable.
The artist's outwardness was evident in the way he drew inspiration from his surroundings rather than his imagination.
In contrast to his brother's introspection, Tim was known for his outwardness and enthusiasm for new experiences.

Synset Examples

hearty showmanship and all-round outwardness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: materialism - the tendency to consider material possessions and physical comfort as more important than spiritual values
  • Antonym: inwardness - the quality of being concerned with inner thoughts or spiritual matters

2. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtwɚdnəs / (AmE), / ˈaʊtwədnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being outside, or directed toward or relating to the exterior or external aspects
quality of being outside or external

Examples

The outwardness of the design draws attention from all angles.
Many philosophers debate the outwardness versus inwardness of human perception.
The garden's outwardness contrasts sharply with the intimacy of the indoor spaces.

Synset Examples

the outwardness of the world

Related Terms

  • Synonym: externality - the state or quality of being external or outside

Etymology

From 'outward' + '-ness', denoting the state or quality of being outward.

3. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtwɚdnəs / (AmE), / ˈaʊtwədnəs / (BrE)
concern with external or material things, as opposed to mental or spiritual aspects
focus on material or external matters

Examples

The philosopher emphasized outwardness as a distraction from true self-understanding.
Her outwardness made her more interested in appearances than in personal values.
The debate centered around the outwardness of sensory experience versus internal thought.

Synset Examples

what is the origin of the outwardness of our sensations of sound, smell, or taste
an abstract conception with feelings of reality and spatial outwardness attached to it

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inwardness - concern with inner or spiritual aspects as opposed to external things

Etymology

Formed from 'outward' + '-ness', denoting the quality or state of being outward.
