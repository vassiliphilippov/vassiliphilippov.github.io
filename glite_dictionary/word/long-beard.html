long-beard

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔŋˌbɪrd / (AmE), / ˈlɒŋˌbɪəd / (BrE)
A stoneware drinking jug with a long neck, often decorated with a caricature of Cardinal Bellarmine; popular in the 17th century.
17th-century stoneware drinking jug

Examples

The museum displayed a beautiful long-beard among its collection of antique jugs.
Collectors prize the long-beard for its distinctive bearded face and historical value.
She restored a 17th-century long-beard to its former glory for the exhibition.

Etymology

From the jug's characteristic decoration featuring a long-bearded face, often of Cardinal Bellarmine.
