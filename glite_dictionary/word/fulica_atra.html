Fulica atra

1. NOUN

/ fəˈlaɪkə ˈɑːtrə / (AmE), / fjʊˈliːkə ˈɑːtrə / (BrE)
A species of coot found in Eurasia, identifiable by its black plumage and white bill; commonly known as the Eurasian coot.
Eurasian species of coot (bird)

Examples

The lake was dotted with pairs of Fulica atra among the reeds.
As winter approaches, flocks of Fulica atra gather on inland waters.
Researchers observed several Fulica atra building nests near the riverbank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coot - a medium-sized waterbird with a white bill and forehead shield

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Fulica' meaning coot and 'atra' meaning black or dark.
