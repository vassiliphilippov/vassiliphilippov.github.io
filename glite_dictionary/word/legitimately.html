legitimately

1. ADV

/ lɪˈdʒɪt.ə.mɪt.li / (AmE), / lɪˈdʒɪt.ɪ.mət.li / (BrE)
In a manner that is in accordance with the law or is recognized as lawful or valid.
in a lawful or legal manner

Examples

She became the CEO legitimately, after years of hard work and dedication.
Property rights can only be acquired legitimately through legal channels.
The dispute was settled legitimately in court, following all necessary procedures.

Synset Examples

let's get married so our child can be born legitimately

Related Terms

  • Antonym: illegitimately - in a manner that is not allowed by law or not recognized as lawful

Etymology

From legitimate + -ly, ultimately from Latin legitimus (lawful, according to law).

2. ADV

/ lɪˈdʒɪtəmətli / (AmE), / lɪˈdʒɪtɪmətli / (BrE)
in a manner that conforms to the law or to accepted rules or standards; legally or properly
in a lawful or proper manner

Examples

The company acted legitimately and followed all required regulations.
He inherited the property legitimately through his father's will.
You must acquire permission legitimately before using these materials.

Synset Examples

you cannot do this legitimately!

Related Terms

  • Antonym: illegitimately - in a manner not in accordance with accepted standards or the law

Etymology

From legitimate + -ly; ultimately from Latin legitimus (lawful).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00365014-r
Synset POS: r
