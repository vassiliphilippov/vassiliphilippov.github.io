incendiary

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsɛndiˌɛri / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɛndiəri / (BrE)
A person who illegally sets fire to property; an arsonist.
person who sets property on fire illegally

Examples

The police arrested the incendiary who caused the warehouse fire.
Firefighters quickly extinguished the blaze started by an incendiary in the old factory.
The authorities are searching for the incendiary responsible for the recent string of arson attacks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arsonist - a person who commits arson (deliberately sets fire to property)

Etymology

From Latin 'incendiarius', from 'incendere' (to set fire to).

Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈsɛndiˌɛri / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɛndiəri / (BrE)
(of a device, attack, or act) deliberately causing or designed to cause fires, especially for destructive purposes.
deliberately causing fires

Examples

The police confirmed that the warehouse was destroyed by an incendiary device.
Investigators are looking into the origins of the incendiary fire that consumed the building.
The suspect was charged for his role in the incendiary attack on the church.

Synset Examples

an incendiary fire

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arson - the criminal act of deliberately setting fire to property

Etymology

From Latin 'incendium' (conflagration), from 'incendere' (to set on fire).

WordNet ID: oewn-02758869-a

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˈsɛndiˌɛri / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɛndiəri / (BrE)
a bomb or device designed to cause fires, especially effective against flammable targets
fire-starting bomb or device

Examples

The military used an incendiary to destroy the enemy's fuel depot.
The factory was badly damaged after an incendiary was dropped during the air raid.
During World War II, incendiaries caused widespread fires in many cities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firebomb - a bomb designed to start fires

Etymology

From Latin incendiarius (setting on fire), from incendium (a fire).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03571171-n
Synset POS: n

Able to cause fires or easily catch fire; designed to ignite and burn rapidly.
capable of causing fire

Examples

5. ADJ

/ ɪnˈsɛndiˌɛri / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɛndɪəri / (BrE)
Tending to stir up conflict, provoke strong feelings, or incite rebellion.
provoking conflict or rebellion

Examples

The leader's incendiary rhetoric inspired many to challenge the authorities.
Publishing such an incendiary article was bound to spark controversy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: provocative - Causing annoyance, anger, or another strong reaction, especially deliberately
  • Antonym: soothing - Having a calming or tranquilizing effect
