Physalis philadelphica

1. NOUN

/ fɪˈseɪlɪs fɪləˌdɛlfɪkə / (AmE), / fɪˈseɪlɪs fɪləˌdɛlfɪkə / (BrE)
A Mexican annual plant of the nightshade family, naturalized in North America, bearing yellow to purple edible fruit similar to small tomatoes, commonly known as tomatillo.
Mexican plant bearing tomatillo fruit

Examples

The culinary uses of Physalis philadelphica are particularly popular in Mexican salsas and sauces.
Farmers grow Physalis philadelphica both for its fruit and its ornamental value.
Botanists classify the tomatillo under the scientific name Physalis philadelphica.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tomatillo - The edible fruit of Physalis philadelphica

Etymology

Physalis from Greek phusallis (bladder), referring to the inflated fruit husk; philadelphica is Latinized after Philadelphia, USA, where the species was identified.
