billfold

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪl.foʊld / (AmE), / ˈbɪl.fəʊld / (BrE)
a small, flat case for carrying paper money and other small personal documents, designed to fit in a pocket
pocket-sized case for money and papers

Examples

He pulled a worn billfold from his back pocket and counted the bills inside.
She received a new leather billfold as a birthday gift from her father.
After losing his billfold, he had to replace all of his identification cards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wallet - a flat folding case for holding money and cards

Etymology

From 'bill' (paper money) + 'fold', referring to an object for folding paper money.
