reconstructive

1. ADJ

/ ˌriːkənˈstrʌktɪv / (AmE), / ˌriːkənˈstrʌktɪv / (BrE)
serving to restore something, especially a body part or structure, to a good or former condition
serving to restore to former condition
medical

Examples

After the accident, she underwent reconstructive surgery on her face.
The hospital specializes in reconstructive procedures for burn victims.
Recent advances make reconstructive treatments more effective and less invasive.

Synset Examples

reconstructive surgery
rehabilitative exercises

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restorative - having the ability to restore health, strength, or well-being

Etymology

From re- (again) + constructive, based on Latin roots meaning 'to build again.'
