Acanthocereus pentagonus

1. NOUN

/ əˌkænθoʊsɪˈriːəs pɛnˈtæɡənəs / (AmE), / əˌkanθəʊsɪˈriːəs pɛnˈtaɡənəs / (BrE)
A species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, known for its edible juicy fruit.
a cactus species with edible fruit

Examples

The Acanthocereus pentagonus is often cultivated for its sweet, edible fruit in Mexico.
Wild Acanthocereus pentagonus plants thrive in arid regions of the southwestern United States.
Researchers studied the water storage adaptations of Acanthocereus pentagonus in desert environments.

Etymology

From Greek 'akantha' (thorn) + genus 'Cereus'; species name from Latin 'pentagonus' (five-angled).
