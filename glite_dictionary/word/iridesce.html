to iridesce

1. VERB

/ ˌɪrəˈdɛs / (AmE), / ˌɪrɪˈdɛs / (BrE)
(of a surface or object) to display a play of lustrous, rainbowlike colors that appear to change when seen from different angles.
display iridescent colors

Examples

The soap bubble began to iridesce in the sunlight, shimmering with every color of the spectrum.
The artist created a glaze that would iridesce on ceramic surfaces, adding depth to her work.
Beneath the shallow water, fish scales iridesced as they darted through the light.

Synset Examples

The corals iridesced under the surface of the clear water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iridescent - showing luminous colors that seem to change when seen from different angles

Etymology

From iridescent, ultimately from Latin 'iris' (rainbow).
