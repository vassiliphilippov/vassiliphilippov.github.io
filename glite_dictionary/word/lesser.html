lesser

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɛsər / (AmE), / ˈlɛsə / (BrE)
of a smaller size, amount, or value; not as important or significant as something else of the same kind
smaller or less important

Examples

The company focused on its lesser expenses to save money.
He played a lesser role in the new film compared to his last appearance.
Several lesser species share the same habitat as the giant panda.

Synset Examples

the lesser powers of Europe
the lesser anteater

Related Terms

  • Antonym: greater - of a larger size, amount, or degree
  • Synonym: less - to a smaller extent; not as much

Etymology

From Middle English 'lesse' (less), from Old English 'lǣssa'; 'lesser' formed with a comparative suffix for emphasis.

2. ADJ

/ ˈlɛsər / (AmE), / ˈlɛsə / (BrE)
of smaller size, degree, or importance; not as great as something else in comparison
of smaller size or importance

Examples

The lesser panda is also known as the red panda.
He saw his efforts as the lesser of the two achievements.
Many people choose the lesser evil when faced with two bad options.

Synset Examples

the lesser anteater
the lesser of two evils

Related Terms

  • Antonym: greater - of larger size, degree, or importance
  • Synonym: minor - lesser in importance, seriousness, or significance

Etymology

Comparative form of 'less', from Old English 'læssa' (less).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01398493-a
Synset POS: a
