foil

1. NOUN

/ fɔɪl / (AmE), / fɔɪl / (BrE)
a transparent sheet bearing a positive photograph or drawing, intended to be viewed with a projector
transparent sheet for projection

Examples

The teacher placed a foil on the overhead projector to display the diagram to the class.
Vintage photos are sometimes preserved as a foil for use in slide projectors.
He carefully mounted the map onto a foil to ensure clarity during his presentation.

2. NOUN

/ fɔɪl / (AmE), / fɔɪl / (BrE)
a person or thing that contrasts with and so emphasizes and enhances the qualities of another.
a contrasting person or thing that highlights another's qualities

Examples

The villain in the movie acted as a perfect foil to the charming hero.
Her reserved personality made him an ideal foil for her outgoing nature.
In the painting, the dark background serves as a foil to the vibrant colors in the foreground.

Synset Examples

pretty girls like plain friends as foils

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contrast - the state of being strikingly different from something else

Etymology

From Old French *foille* (leaf), influenced by the practice of setting a gem on a thin sheet of metal to enhance its brightness.
