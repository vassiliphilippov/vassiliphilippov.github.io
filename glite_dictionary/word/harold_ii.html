Harold II

1. PROPN

/ ˈhærəld səˈkənd / (AmE), / ˈhærəld ðə ˈsɛkənd / (BrE)
(c. 1022–1066) The last Anglo-Saxon King of England, who succeeded Edward the Confessor in 1066 and was killed at the Battle of Hastings by forces led by William the Conqueror.
last Anglo-Saxon King of England (1066)

Examples

Harold II was crowned King of England in January 1066.
The death of Harold II marked the end of Anglo-Saxon rule in England.
Historians often debate whether Harold II could have won the Battle of Hastings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Edward the Confessor - King of England before Harold II, died in 1066
  • Antonym: William the Conqueror - Invader who defeated Harold II at the Battle of Hastings
