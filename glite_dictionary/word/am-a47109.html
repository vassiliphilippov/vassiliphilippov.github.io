Am

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ ˈɛm / (AmE), / ˌeɪ ˈɛm / (BrE)
The chemical symbol for americium, a synthetic radioactive metallic element with atomic number 95, produced by bombarding uranium with helium atoms.
chemical symbol for americium (element)

Examples

The smoke detector contains a small amount of Am to sense the presence of smoke.
Scientists discovered Am by bombarding uranium with alpha particles in 1944.
Handling Am requires strict safety precautions due to its radioactivity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: americium - a synthetic radioactive metallic element, atomic number 95

Etymology

From American (United States of America), after which the element was named.
