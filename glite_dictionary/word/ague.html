ague

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪɡjuː / (AmE), / ˈeɪɡjuː / (BrE)
a fever or shivering fit, especially one involving chills and sweating, formerly associated with malaria and similar illnesses
fever with shivering fits
archaic

Examples

He was seized by an ague that left him shivering uncontrollably.
In the old days, many suffered from ague during the winter months.
Her sudden ague was followed by a persistent fever.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fever - an abnormally high body temperature due to illness

Etymology

From Middle English *ague* (fever), from Old French *ague* (sharp, acute), from Latin *acutus* (sharp, pointed).

2. NOUN

/ ˈeɪɡjuː / (AmE), / ˈeɪɡjuː / (BrE)
An illness characterized by recurring chills, fever, and sweating, especially as a symptom of malaria.
recurring fever with chills (malaria symptom)
archaic

Examples

The patient suffered a violent ague with shaking chills every evening.
During the 19th century, many in the region were plagued by ague and other fevers.
His strength was sapped by recurring bouts of ague each spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malaria - a disease characterized by periodic fevers caused by parasites transmitted by mosquitoes

Etymology

From Middle English ague, from Old French aiguë (ague, sharp fever), from Latin acuta (sharp, acute).
