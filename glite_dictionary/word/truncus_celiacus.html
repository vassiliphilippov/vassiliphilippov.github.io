truncus celiacus

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrʌŋ.kəs siːˈlaɪ.ək.əs / (AmE), / ˈtrʌŋ.kəs siːˈliː.ə.kəs / (BrE)
a major artery arising from the abdominal aorta just below the diaphragm, which branches into the left gastric, common hepatic, and splenic arteries
main abdominal artery below diaphragm
medical

Examples

The truncus celiacus supplies blood to several vital organs in the upper abdomen.
A blockage in the truncus celiacus can cause serious digestive problems.
During surgery, the truncus celiacus must be carefully identified to prevent accidental injury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celiac artery - main artery supplying the stomach, liver, and spleen

Etymology

From Latin 'truncus' (trunk) and 'celiacus' (relating to the abdomen).
