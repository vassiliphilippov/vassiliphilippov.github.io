self-service

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlfˈsɝːvɪs / (AmE), / ˌsɛlfˈsɜːvɪs / (BrE)
A system or practice in which customers serve themselves, especially in shops or restaurants, rather than being served by an employee.
practice of serving oneself

Examples

Many supermarkets now use a self-service checkout system to speed up shopping.
The hotel offers a self-service breakfast buffet every morning.
At the self-service gas station, customers pump their own fuel.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: full-service - a service system where staff assist customers directly

Etymology

From 'self-' (oneself) + 'service' (the act of serving), first used in the early 20th century in retail.

2. ADJ

/ ˌsɛlf ˈsɝːvɪs / (AmE), / ˌself ˈsɜːvɪs / (BrE)
(of a shop, restaurant, etc.) in which customers serve themselves rather than being served by staff.
where customers serve themselves

Examples

The hotel offers a self-service breakfast bar with a variety of options.
Many laundromats operate on a self-service basis, letting customers use machines independently.
This supermarket was one of the first to introduce self-service checkouts in the city.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: counter-service - service in which staff serve customers from behind a counter
  • Antonym: full service - a service in which staff serve customers at their tables or personally; not self-service
