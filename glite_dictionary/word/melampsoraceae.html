Melampsoraceae

1. NOUN

/ məˌlæmp.səˈreɪ.si.i / (AmE), / mɛˌlæmp.səˈreɪ.siː.iː / (BrE)
A family of rust fungi that are plant pathogens, often affecting trees and causing disease.
family of rust fungi (plant pathogens)

Examples

Several species in the Melampsoraceae family are known for causing leaf rust in poplar trees.
The forest was severely impacted by a Melampsoraceae outbreak, resulting in widespread defoliation.
Researchers are studying Melampsoraceae fungi to develop better methods for controlling plant diseases.

Etymology

From the genus name Melampsora, itself from Greek 'melas' (black) and 'psora' (itch, scab), referring to the appearance of the rust.

2. NOUN

/ məˌlæmpsoʊˈreɪsiaː / (AmE), / mɪˌlæmpsɒˈreɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of rust fungi known for infecting plants, particularly trees and crops
family of rust fungi

Examples

The Melampsoraceae are responsible for several plant diseases affecting forests.
Researchers are studying the life cycle of the Melampsoraceae to better control rust outbreaks.
Many agricultural problems are linked to fungi from the Melampsoraceae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pucciniaceae - another family of rust fungi

Etymology

From the type genus Melampsora, from Greek melas (black) + psora (itch, scab).
