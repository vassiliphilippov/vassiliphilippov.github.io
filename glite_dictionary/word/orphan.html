orphan

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔr.fən / (AmE), / ˈɔː.fən / (BrE)
In typesetting, the first line of a paragraph that appears alone at the bottom of a page or column, separated from the rest of the paragraph.
first line of a paragraph at bottom of a page (typography)

Examples

In print design, a single orphan at the end of a page can disrupt the visual flow of the text.
The book's layout program automatically detects and fixes any orphan lines to improve readability.
Editors often adjust spacing to prevent an orphan from appearing at the bottom of a column.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: widow - the last line of a paragraph appearing alone at the top of a page or column (in typography)

Etymology

From Greek ὀρφανός (orphanos, 'bereaved, parentless'). Typographic use by analogy to a child separated from its family.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔr.fən / (AmE), / ˈɔː.fən / (BrE)
A young animal whose mother has died or is otherwise absent.
a young animal without its mother

Examples

The rescue center took in the tiny orphan and hand-fed it until it grew stronger.
After a storm destroyed the nest, a wildlife volunteer found an orphan owl chick.
Many animal shelters have special programs for caring for each orphan until it can survive alone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orphan - a child whose parents have died

Etymology

From Late Latin orphanus (orphan), from Greek orphanos (parentless).

3. NOUN

/ ˈɔɹ.fən / (AmE), / ˈɔː.fən / (BrE)
a person or thing that lacks support, care, or supervision
person or thing without support or care

Examples

After the department shut down, several projects were left as orphans with no one to maintain them.
The abandoned kitten became an orphan when its mother disappeared.
In the absence of supervision, the server was an orphan in the network.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waif - a homeless, neglected person, especially a child

Etymology

From Late Latin orphānus, from Greek orphanós (bereaved).

4. VERB

/ ˈɔr.fən / (AmE), / ˈɔː.fən / (BrE)
to deprive (a child) of one or both parents through death
deprive of parents by death

Examples

The war threatened to orphan thousands of children.
A sudden accident orphaned her at a young age.
Disease outbreaks continue to orphan families in some regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: widow - a woman whose spouse has died

From Late Latin orphanus (orphan), from Greek orphanos (bereaved).
