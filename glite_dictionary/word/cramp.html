cramp

1. NOUN

/ kræmp / (AmE), / kramp / (BrE)
a sudden, painful, and involuntary contraction of a muscle or group of muscles
painful involuntary muscle contraction

Examples

He stopped running because he got a terrible cramp in his leg.
Swimming after eating can sometimes give you a cramp.
She massaged her foot to relieve the cramp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spasm - a sudden involuntary muscular contraction

Etymology

From Middle English crampe, from Old French crampe (spasm), from Frankish *kramp (bent, crooked).

6. VERB

/ kræmp / (AmE), / kramp / (BrE)
(of a muscle or person) to experience or be affected by a sudden painful contraction or tightening of a muscle.
to suffer a sudden muscle contraction

Examples

My leg suddenly began to cramp during the marathon and I had to stop running.
Swimmers often cramp if they don't stretch properly before getting into the pool.
After sitting for too long, his back muscles started to cramp painfully.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spasm - a sudden involuntary contraction of a muscle or group of muscles

Etymology

From Middle English crampe, from Old French crampe, from Medieval Latin crampa (bend, hook); related to German Krampf.
