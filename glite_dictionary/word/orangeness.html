orangeness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔrɪndʒnəs / (AmE), / ˈɒrɪndʒnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being orange in color; the characteristic of having a hue between red and yellow
the quality of being orange in color

Examples

The orangeness of the sunset filled the sky with warmth.
She admired the orangeness of the ripe pumpkins in the field.
The artist mixed paints to achieve the perfect orangeness for her autumn landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orange - a color between red and yellow; the fruit

Etymology

From 'orange' (the color) + '-ness' (a noun-forming suffix).
