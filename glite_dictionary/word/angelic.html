angelic

1. ADJ

/ ænˈdʒɛlɪk / (AmE), / ænˈdʒelɪk / (BrE)
Exceptionally kind, pure, or beautiful; resembling an angel in character or appearance.
like an angel; exceptionally kind or pure

Examples

Her angelic smile could calm anyone, even on their worst days.
The choir sang with an angelic purity that captivated the entire audience.
Despite facing hardship, his angelic kindness never faltered.

Synset Examples

angelic beneficence
a beatific smile
a saintly concern for his fellow men
my sainted mother

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saintly - very holy or morally good
  • Antonym: demonic - resembling or characteristic of a demon; evil

Etymology

From Medieval Latin angelicus, from Latin angelus (angel).

2. ADJ

/ ænˈdʒɛlɪk / (AmE), / ænˈdʒelɪk / (BrE)
having the qualities of an angel, especially being very kind, innocent, or beautiful
like or befitting an angel; innocent or kind

Examples

Her angelic smile could brighten anyone's day.
The choirboy looked angelic in his white robes during the ceremony.
Despite his mischievous behavior, he wore an angelic expression when caught.

Synset Examples

an angelic smile
a cherubic face
looking so seraphic when he slept
a sweet disposition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seraphic - having a serene, angel-like quality
  • Synonym: cherubic - innocently or sweetly angelic in appearance
  • Antonym: devilish - having the qualities of a devil; mischievous or wicked

Etymology

From Late Latin angelicus, from Greek angelikos (of or relating to angels).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01462871-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ænˈdʒɛlɪk / (AmE), / ænˈdʒɛlɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of angels
relating to angels

Examples

The choir's angelic voices filled the cathedral with music.
She smiled with an angelic expression that melted everyone's heart.
The artist painted an angelic figure surrounded by clouds and light.

Synset Examples

angelic messenger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heavenly - like or belonging to heaven; divine

Etymology

From Medieval Latin angelicus, from Latin angelus (angel), from Greek angelos (messenger).
