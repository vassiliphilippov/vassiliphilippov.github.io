distinguishability

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌstɪŋɡwɪʃəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / dɪˌstɪŋɡwɪʃəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being able to be recognized as different or distinct from others.
ability to be distinguished

Examples

The distinguishability of the twins depends on their clothing and hairstyles.
In quantum mechanics, distinguishability of particles has important consequences for their behavior.
The designer improved the distinguishability of buttons by using contrasting colors.

Synset Examples

In this article we consider the distinguishability of segments by investigating first their equioptics in detail.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indistinguishability - the state of not being able to be distinguished

Etymology

From distinguish + -ability, modeled on 'distinguishable.'
