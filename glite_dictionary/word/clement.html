clement

1. ADJ

/ ˈklɛmənt / (AmE), / ˈklɛmənt / (BrE)
(of weather or climate) mild and pleasant; not severe.
mild and pleasant (of weather)

Examples

The forecast promised clement weather for the outdoor wedding this weekend.
After weeks of storms, the region finally enjoyed several days of clement conditions.
Travelers prefer to visit coastal towns during the more clement months of spring and autumn.

Synset Examples

clement weather

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mild - not severe or extreme, especially of weather
  • Antonym: harsh - unpleasantly severe (of weather)

Etymology

From Latin clemens, clementis (mild, gentle).

2. ADJ

/ ˈklɛmənt / (AmE), / ˈklɛmənt / (BrE)
(of a person or their actions) inclined to be merciful or lenient.
merciful; lenient

Examples

Despite the seriousness of the offense, the judge remained clement and reduced the penalty.
The principal was known for her clement approach to discipline, preferring guidance over punishment.
In times of crisis, a clement leader can restore hope and forgiveness to a community.

Synset Examples

a more clement judge reduced the sentence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: merciful - showing mercy or compassion
  • Synonym: lenient - showing tolerance or gentleness, not strict
  • Antonym: harsh - strict, severe, or unkind in treatment or judgment

Etymology

From Latin *clemens* (merciful, mild).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00439279-a
Synset POS: a
