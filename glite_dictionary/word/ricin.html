ricin

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪsɪn / (AmE), / ˈraɪsɪn / (BrE)
a highly toxic protein obtained from castor beans, used in scientific research and sometimes as a bioweapon
highly toxic protein from castor beans
medical

Examples

Scientists handle ricin with extreme care due to its deadly properties.
Reports of ricin being used as a weapon have appeared in recent news.
Just a small amount of ricin can be fatal if ingested or inhaled.

Synset Examples

one milligram of ricin can kill an adult

Etymology

From Latin 'ricinus' (castor-oil plant).
