prostate

1. ADJ

/ ˈprɑːsteɪt / (AmE), / ˈprɒsteɪt / (BrE)
Relating to the prostate gland.
relating to the prostate gland
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended a prostate examination during the annual check-up.
He was diagnosed with prostate cancer after experiencing urinary difficulties.
Many men over 50 are advised to have regular prostate screenings.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin prostata, from Greek prostatēs (prostate gland), from proistānai (to set before).

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɑːsteɪt / (AmE), / ˈprɒsteɪt / (BrE)
a firm, partly muscular gland in male mammals, situated at the base of the bladder and surrounding the urethra, which produces a fluid that forms part of semen
male gland producing seminal fluid
medical

Examples

Many men experience problems with the prostate as they age.
The doctor recommended a test to check for any abnormalities in the prostate.
Regular check-ups can help detect prostate issues early.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin prostata, from Ancient Greek prostatēs (one who stands before, guardian), referring to its position.
