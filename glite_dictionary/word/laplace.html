Laplace

1. PROPN

/ ləˈplɑs / (AmE), / laˈplas / (BrE)
Pierre-Simon Laplace (1749–1827), French mathematician and astronomer, known for his work on probability and the nebular hypothesis of the solar system.
French mathematician and astronomer

Examples

Laplace's equations are fundamental to mathematical physics.
Many students study the theory of probability developed by Laplace.
The nebular hypothesis was first proposed by Laplace in the 18th century.

Etymology

French surname; Pierre-Simon Laplace.
