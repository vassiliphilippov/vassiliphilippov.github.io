intro

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪntroʊ / (AmE), / ˈɪntrəʊ / (BrE)
a short introduction, especially when formally making someone known to others or opening an event, speech, or piece of music
short introduction; formal presentation
informal

Examples

The host began the show with a brief intro for the guest speaker.
After a short intro, the band played their most popular song.
Could you give me a quick intro to the team before the meeting?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: introduction - the act of formally presenting or making someone known

Etymology

Shortened form of 'introduction'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07232009-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪntroʊ / (AmE), / ˈɪntrəʊ / (BrE)
a short introductory passage at the beginning of a piece of popular music
introductory section of a song
informal

Examples

The band's new album features a catchy intro before the first verse starts.
She learned how to play the intro on her guitar in just one day.
The intro sets the mood for the entire track.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: introduction - the opening section of a piece of music

Etymology

Shortened form of 'introduction'.
