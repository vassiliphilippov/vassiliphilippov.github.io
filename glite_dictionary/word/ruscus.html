Ruscus

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌs.kəs / (AmE), / ˈrʌs.kəs / (BrE)
a genus of European evergreen shrubs, formerly in the lily family and sometimes placed in Asparagaceae, known for their tough, spiny leaves and small flowers
genus of European evergreen shrubs

Examples

The florist used Ruscus branches to add greenery to the bouquet.
Ruscus plants are often grown for their ornamental leaves and berries.
Because Ruscus is evergreen, it provides year-round interest in gardens.

Etymology

From Latin 'ruscus', meaning butcher's broom.
