sepulchre

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛp.əl.kɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɛp.əl.kə / (BrE)
a small room or monument, often made of stone, in which a dead person is buried
burial chamber or tomb

Examples

The ancient kings were laid to rest in a grand sepulchre deep within the mountains.
Many visitors come to see the famous sepulchre where the saint is believed to be buried.
The old stone sepulchre was covered in vines and moss, untouched for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tomb - a large grave, typically an underground one for the dead

Etymology

From Latin 'sepulcrum' (tomb), from 'sepelire' (to bury).
