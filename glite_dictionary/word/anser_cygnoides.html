Anser cygnoides

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.sɚ sɪgˈnɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / ˈæn.sə sɪgˈnɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
a large wild goose native to northeast Asia, also known as the swan goose, which interbreeds with the greylag goose
large wild goose of northeast Asia (swan goose)

Examples

The Anser cygnoides population has declined in some habitats due to overhunting and habitat destruction.
In zoos, the Anser cygnoides is often displayed alongside other species of geese.
Hybrid offspring occur where the Anser cygnoides freely interbreeds with the greylag goose in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: goose - a large waterbird with a long neck, short legs, and webbed feet
  • Synonym: greylag - a large species of goose native to Europe and Asia, Anser anser
  • Degree Higher: swan - large waterbird with a long neck, mostly white plumage

Etymology

From Latin: 'anser' (goose) + 'cygnoides' (swan-like, from 'cygnus' meaning swan).
