drawers

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɔrz / (AmE), / ˈdrɔːəz / (BrE)
(old-fashioned) women's underpants, especially with legs and often made of cotton or linen.
old-fashioned women's underpants
archaic

Examples

In Victorian times, women commonly wore drawers under their skirts.
She laughed, recalling the lace-trimmed drawers her grandmother once showed her.
Antique drawers made of fine cotton are now sought after by collectors.

Synset Examples

she was afraid that her bloomers might have been showing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bloomers - loose-fitting women's underpants, especially from the late 19th and early 20th century

Etymology

From the verb 'draw' (to pull), referring to clothing that can be drawn on or up; 16th century usage for undergarments.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdrɔrz / (AmE), / ˈdrɔːz / (BrE)
underpants worn by men, especially long ones that cover part of the legs; an old-fashioned term for men's underwear
men's underpants (old-fashioned)
archaic

Examples

He reached for a clean pair of drawers from his dresser.
In those days, men wore woolen drawers during the winter.
The tailor sold vests and drawers in all sizes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: underpants - an article of clothing worn under outer clothes, covering the lower part of the body
  • Degree Lower: boxers - loose-fitting men's underpants
  • Degree Higher: long johns - long, warm underpants covering the legs

Etymology

From the verb 'draw' (to pull), referring to clothing drawn on; originally (16th century) meaning articles of clothing drawn up the legs.
