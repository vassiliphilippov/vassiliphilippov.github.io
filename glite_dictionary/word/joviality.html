joviality

1. NOUN

/ ˌdoʊviˈæləti / (AmE), / ˌdʒəʊviˈælɪti / (BrE)
the state or quality of being cheerful and full of good humor
cheerful good humor

Examples

The joviality of the holiday party put everyone in a good mood.
Even during stressful meetings, her joviality helped lighten the atmosphere.
His natural joviality made him popular among his colleagues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheerfulness - the state of being noticeably happy and optimistic
  • Antonym: gloominess - a state of depression or low spirits

Etymology

From Latin *Jovialis* (of Jupiter), referring to the cheerful disposition thought to be influenced by the planet Jupiter.

2. NOUN

/ ˌdʒoʊ.viˈæ.lə.ti / (AmE), / ˌdʒəʊ.viˈæl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the state or quality of being cheerful and friendly; good-humored liveliness
cheerful and friendly state

Examples

Her joviality made everyone around her feel welcome and at ease.
Despite the long hours, the team's joviality never faded during the project.
He was known for his joviality at social gatherings, always spreading laughter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheerfulness - the quality of being noticeably happy and optimistic
  • Antonym: gloom - a state of partial or total darkness; a feeling of depression or despondency

Etymology

From Latin 'jovialis' (of Jupiter, merry), from 'Jovis' (Jupiter).
