inhuman

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈhjuːmən / (AmE), / ɪnˈhjuːmən / (BrE)
lacking compassion, pity, or human feeling; cruel or brutal.
lacking human feeling; cruel

Examples

The dictator was known for his inhuman treatment of political prisoners.
Such inhuman acts shocked the entire community.
It was an inhuman and senseless attack on innocent people.

Synset Examples

in cold blood
cold-blooded killing
insensate destruction

Related Terms

  • Antonym: humane - showing compassion and benevolence

Etymology

From Latin inhumanus (not human, cruel, barbarous).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈhjuːmən / (AmE), / ɪnˈhjuːmən / (BrE)
not belonging to or resembling a human or human qualities; alien or strange in form or nature.
not human in form or quality

Examples

A strange, inhuman light flickered deep within the cavern.
The scream was so inhuman that everyone in the room fell silent.
The creature made an inhuman sound that sent chills down my spine.

Synset Examples

something dark and inhuman in form
a babel of inhuman noises

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonhuman - not human; not consisting of humans or not derived from humans

Etymology

From Latin inhumanus (not human).
