estrangement

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈstreɪndʒmənt / (AmE), / ɪˈstreɪndʒmənt / (BrE)
the state of no longer being friendly or close to someone, often as a result of hostility or conflict
state of alienation or separation

Examples

The years of conflict led to a deep estrangement between the two brothers.
Many couples experience estrangement after repeated arguments and misunderstandings.
His estrangement from his family became evident during the holidays.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reconciliation - restoration of friendly relations
  • Synonym: alienation - the state or experience of being isolated from a group or activity

Etymology

From Old French *estranger* (to treat as a stranger), from Latin *extraneare* (to treat as a stranger).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈstreɪndʒmənt / (AmE), / ɪˈstreɪndʒmənt / (BrE)
a feeling of being alienated or separated from other people, often resulting in a sense of emotional distance or isolation
feeling of being alienated or separated

Examples

Her years abroad led to a deep estrangement from her family.
Many people feel a sense of estrangement in large, impersonal cities.
The novel explores the estrangement between parents and children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alienation - the state or experience of being isolated or estranged from something
  • Antonym: reconciliation - the restoration of friendly relations

Etymology

From Old French *estranger* (to treat as a stranger), from Latin *extraneare*, from *extraneus* (foreign, external).
