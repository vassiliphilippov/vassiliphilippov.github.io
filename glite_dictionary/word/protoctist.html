protoctist

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈtɑktɪst / (AmE), / prəˈtɒktɪst / (BrE)
any of various single-celled or simple multicellular organisms, such as algae and protozoans, regarded as protists.
unicellular or simple multicellular organism (protist)
medical

Examples

Many protoctists can only be observed with a microscope due to their small size.
Scientists study protoctists to understand the evolution of complex life.
Some protoctists play vital roles in aquatic ecosystems as primary producers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protist - any member of the group of mostly unicellular eukaryotic organisms

Etymology

From Greek proto- (first) + ktistos (established, created).
