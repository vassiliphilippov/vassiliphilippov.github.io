Gaius Petronius

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡaɪ.əs pɛˈtroʊ.ni.əs / (AmE), / ˈɡaɪ.əs pɛˈtrəʊ.ni.əs / (BrE)
A Roman satirist and author, best known for the work 'Satyricon'; died in AD 66.
Roman satirist and author (died 66)

Examples

Scholars regard Gaius Petronius as the likely author of the 'Satyricon.'
During Nero's reign, Gaius Petronius was famed for his wit and literary style.
Some historians call Gaius Petronius the 'arbiter of elegance' in the Roman court.

Etymology

Latin name; 'Gaius' a Roman praenomen, 'Petronius' a family name.
