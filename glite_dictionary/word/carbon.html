carbon

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːr.bən / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.bən / (BrE)
a chemical element, a nonmetal present in all known life forms, found in forms such as graphite, diamond, and amorphous carbon
a chemical element; basis of organic life

Examples

Carbon is a crucial component of all living organisms.
Diamond and graphite are both forms of carbon.
The greenhouse gas carbon dioxide contains one atom of carbon and two atoms of oxygen.

Etymology

From Latin carbo (charcoal, coal).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrbən / (AmE), / ˈkɑːbən / (BrE)
A copy of a written or typed document made by using carbon paper.
a copy made with carbon paper

Examples

Please send me a carbon of the letter for my records.
In the days before photocopiers, making a carbon was common practice in offices.
He signed both the original contract and the carbon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duplicate - an exact copy of something

Etymology

From Latin 'carbo' (charcoal), reflecting the carbon content of the original paper.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrbən / (AmE), / ˈkɑːbən / (BrE)
a chemical element, a nonmetal that occurs naturally in forms such as graphite and diamond, and is present in all organic compounds
chemical element; nonmetal; found in all organic matter

Examples

The element carbon is essential for all living things on Earth.
Graphite and diamond are both forms of carbon with very different properties.
Burning fossil fuels releases carbon into the atmosphere as carbon dioxide.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: graphite - a soft, black form of carbon used in pencils and as a lubricant
  • Degree Higher: diamond - a very hard, transparent form of carbon often used as a gemstone

Etymology

From Latin 'carbonem' (charcoal).

4. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrbən / (AmE), / ˈkɑːbən / (BrE)
a thin sheet coated on one side with a dark waxy substance, used for making copies of written or typed documents by placing it between two sheets of paper
thin sheet for copying documents

Examples

She placed a carbon between two sheets to make a copy of the letter.
The old typewriter came with a stack of carbon sheets for duplicating documents.
He accidentally got ink on his hands from the carbon while preparing forms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carbon paper - thin paper coated with a pigment for making copies

Etymology

From French 'carbone', from Latin 'carbo' (charcoal), referring to its original carbon-based content.
