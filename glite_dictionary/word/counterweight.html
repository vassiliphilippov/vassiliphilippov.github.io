counterweight

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊntərˌweɪt / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌweɪt / (BrE)
a weight used to balance another weight, especially in mechanisms or machines
a balancing weight

Examples

A heavy counterweight was attached to the crane to keep it stable during lifting operations.
The elevator system relies on a counterweight to offset the weight of the cabin.
Adding a counterweight to the seesaw helped the children balance perfectly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: counterbalance - a weight or force that balances another

Etymology

Formed from 'counter-' (against, opposite) + 'weight'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03123190-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈkaʊntɚˌweɪt / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌweɪt / (BrE)
to act as a counterbalance to something; to offset or neutralize the effect of something else
to act as a counterbalance

Examples

The company's efforts to expand abroad helped to counterweight losses in the domestic market.
Rising exports may counterweight the negative impact of higher fuel prices.
We need new policies to counterweight the growing influence of large corporations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: counterbalance - to offset or balance the weight of something

Etymology

From 'counterweight' (noun), from counter- (against) + weight.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02672750-v
Synset POS: v
