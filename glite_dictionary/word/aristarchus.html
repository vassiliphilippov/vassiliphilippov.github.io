Aristarchus

1. PROPN

/ ˌærɪˈstɑrkəs / (AmE), / ˌærɪˈstɑːkəs / (BrE)
a prominent and bright crater located on the northwest part of the near side of the Moon
bright lunar crater

Examples

The Aristarchus crater is one of the brightest features visible on the lunar surface.
Astronomers often study Aristarchus to understand the geology of the Moon.
Under certain lighting, Aristarchus appears to glow, making it easy to spot through telescopes.

Etymology

Named after Aristarchus of Samos, an ancient Greek astronomer.

2. PROPN

/ ˌærɪˈstɑrkəs / (AmE), / ˌærɪˈstɑːkəs / (BrE)
an ancient Greek grammarian (c. 217–145 BC) known for his influential commentaries on the Iliad and the Odyssey
ancient Greek grammarian and commentator

Examples

Aristarchus devoted much of his life to analyzing Homeric poetry.
Many modern scholars still study the critical methods developed by Aristarchus.
The commentaries of Aristarchus are considered vital resources for understanding ancient Greek literature.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἀρίσταρχος (Arístarchos), a personal name meaning 'best ruler'.
