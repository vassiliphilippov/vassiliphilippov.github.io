hypotension

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪ.poʊˈtɛn.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪ.pəʊˈtɛn.ʃən / (BrE)
Abnormally low blood pressure, especially in the arteries.
abnormally low blood pressure
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with hypotension after experiencing dizziness and fainting.
Severe hypotension may require immediate medical attention to prevent organ damage.
Some medications can cause hypotension as a side effect, especially in elderly people.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hypertension - abnormally high blood pressure
  • Synonym: low blood pressure - blood pressure lower than normal

Etymology

From hypo- (under) + tension (pressure).
