scores

1. NOUN

/ skɔrz / (AmE), / skɔːz / (BrE)
large numbers or amounts of people or things.
large number or amount

Examples

There were scores of people waiting outside the theater.
She received scores of letters congratulating her on her achievement.
After the announcement, scores of complaints flooded in from customers.

Synset Examples

made lots of new friends
she amassed stacks of newspapers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lots - a large number or amount
  • Synonym: stacks - large amounts or a great quantity

Etymology

Plural of "score," originally from Old Norse "skor" meaning a notch, tally, or group of twenty; extended to mean many.
