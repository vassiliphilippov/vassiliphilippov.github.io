Lepiota cepaestipes

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛpiˈoʊtə ˌsɛpiˈɛstaɪps / (AmE), / ˌlɛpɪˈəʊtə ˌsɛpɪˈiːstɪpiːz / (BrE)
A species of white mushroom with a tendency to grow in clusters and a club-shaped base.
a white mushroom species

Examples

The cluster of Lepiota cepaestipes was growing at the base of the old oak tree.
Foragers should be cautious when identifying Lepiota cepaestipes in the wild due to its similarity to other species.
The club-shaped base makes Lepiota cepaestipes distinctive among white mushrooms.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining genus Lepiota and species epithet cepaestipes (from Latin 'cepae' meaning 'of onions' and 'stipes' meaning 'stem'), referencing the shape of its stalk.
