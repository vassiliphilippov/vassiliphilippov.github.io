imagism

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪmədʒˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈɪmɪdʒɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
a movement in early 20th-century American and English poetry that rejected Victorian sentimentality and advocated free verse and clear, concrete imagery in common speech
poetic movement favoring clear, concrete imagery

Examples

Ezra Pound was a leading figure in imagism, helping to define its principles and promote its poets.
Imagism sought to create poetry that was direct and precise, free from excess ornamentation.
The influence of imagism can be seen in many modernist poets' focus on clear, concrete images.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: symbolism - a literary movement emphasizing symbolic representation

Etymology

From image + -ism, modeled after French imagisme, which described the movement.
