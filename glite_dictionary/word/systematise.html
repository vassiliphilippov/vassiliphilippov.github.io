to systematise

1. VERB

/ ˈsɪs.tə.mə.taɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪs.tə.mə.taɪz / (BrE)
to arrange or organize something according to a system; to make systematic.
organize according to a system
british

Examples

We need to systematise the procedures to ensure consistency across departments.
The scientist worked to systematise the data collected over several years.
Plans are underway to systematise public transportation routes in the city.

Synset Examples

systematize our scientific knowledge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to systematize - arrange according to a system (American spelling)
  • Synonym: to organize - to arrange systematically; to put in order

Etymology

From Greek "systēmatizein" via French and late Latin; British variant spelling of "systematize".
