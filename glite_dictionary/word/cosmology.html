cosmology

1. NOUN

/ kɑzˈmɑlədʒi / (AmE), / kɒzˈmɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the scientific or metaphysical study of the origin, structure, and nature of the universe
study of the universe's origin and nature

Examples

She decided to pursue a graduate degree in cosmology to understand the origins of the universe.
Modern cosmology relies heavily on data from space telescopes.
Ancient cultures developed their own forms of cosmology to explain the night sky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astronomy - the scientific study of celestial objects and phenomena

Etymology

From Greek kosmos (world, universe) + -logia (study of).

2. NOUN

/ kɑːzˈmɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / kɒzˈmɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of science that deals with the origin, structure, and development of the universe
science of the universe's origin and structure

Examples

She has a deep interest in cosmology and often reads about the early universe.
Modern cosmology seeks to explain how galaxies and stars formed over billions of years.
The lecture on cosmology covered theories about the universe's expansion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astronomy - the scientific study of celestial objects, space, and the universe as a whole
  • Synonym: astrophysics - the branch of astronomy dealing with the physics of celestial objects and phenomena

Etymology

From Greek kosmos (universe) + -logia (study).
