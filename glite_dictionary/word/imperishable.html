imperishable

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɛr.ɪʃ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛr.ɪʃ.ə.bəl / (BrE)
Not able to be destroyed, decayed, or worn out; enduring forever.
not able to perish; everlasting

Examples

The legend of King Arthur is considered an imperishable part of British folklore.
Her imperishable spirit inspired generations of activists and leaders.
Many believe that true art is imperishable and endures through the ages.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: perishable - liable to decay or spoil
  • Synonym: eternal - lasting forever; without end

Etymology

From Latin 'imperibilis', from 'in-' (not) + 'perire' (to perish).

2. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɛrɪʃəbəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛrɪʃəbəl / (BrE)
lasting forever or for a very long time; not subject to decay or destruction.
not able to decay or be destroyed

Examples

Some values are considered imperishable throughout generations and cultures.
The monument was built to stand as an imperishable symbol of their achievements.
Her imperishable optimism inspired everyone she met.

Synset Examples

an abiding belief
imperishable truths

Related Terms

  • Antonym: perishable - liable to decay or spoil
  • Synonym: eternal - lasting or existing forever; without end

Etymology

From Latin 'imperishabilis', from in- 'not' + perire 'to perish'.
