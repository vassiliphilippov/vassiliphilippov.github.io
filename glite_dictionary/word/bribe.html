bribe

1. VERB

/ braɪb / (AmE), / braɪb / (BrE)
to illegally give money or gifts to someone in authority to persuade them to do something dishonest or favorable to you
make illegal payments for influence

Examples

She attempted to bribe the official to gain a construction permit.
The company was accused of bribing foreign politicians to secure contracts.
He denied that he had ever bribed a public servant.

Synset Examples

This judge can be bought

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corrupt - to make someone act dishonestly in return for money or gifts
  • Antonym: to extort - to obtain something by force, threats, or other unfair means

Etymology

From Middle English *bryben* (to beg, to steal), later shifting to its current meaning in the 16th century.

2. NOUN

/ braɪb / (AmE), / braɪb / (BrE)
a sum of money or something valuable given to someone in a position of trust to influence their actions or decisions dishonestly
payment to corrupt someone's judgment

Examples

The official accepted a bribe in exchange for issuing the permit.
She refused to pay a bribe, even though it would have made the process easier.
The company was fined heavily after it was discovered they offered a bribe to secure the contract.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kickback - a secret payment for facilitating a deal
  • Antonym: reward - something given in recognition of service or achievement

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French *briber* (to beg, cheat), later 'bribe' meaning 'alms, theft, or fraud'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
