unfairly

1. ADV

/ ʌnˈfɛrli / (AmE), / ʌnˈfɛəli / (BrE)
In a way that is not fair or just.
in an unjust or inequitable manner

Examples

He believed he had been unfairly treated by his employer.
The referee penalized our team unfairly during the match.
Critics argued that the policy unfairly targets low-income families.

Synset Examples

they dealt with him unfairly
their accusations hit below the belt

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fairly - in a fair and just manner

Etymology

From 'unfair' + '-ly', with 'unfair' formed from prefix 'un-' (not) + 'fair.'
