spangled

1. ADJ

/ ˈspæŋɡəld / (AmE), / ˈspaŋɡəld / (BrE)
decorated or covered with small, shiny objects such as beads, jewels, or sequins, especially for ornamental effect
decorated with shiny objects or sequins

Examples

The performer's dress was spangled with shimmering sequins that sparkled under the lights.
Children stared at the spangled banners waving above the parade.
Her shoes were spangled, catching everyone's attention at the party.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sparkling - shining with bright flashes of light
  • Antonym: plain - without decoration or ornamentation

Etymology

From the noun 'spangle' (small sparkly piece) + -ed (past participle/adj. forming suffix).
