attar

1. NOUN

/ ˈæt ɑr / (AmE), / ˈæt ɑː / (BrE)
A natural perfume or essential oil obtained by distilling flowers, especially in traditional South Asian and Middle Eastern perfumery.
flower-derived essential perfume

Examples

The merchant sold exquisite attar made from rose petals in ornate bottles.
She applied a drop of attar to her wrist before attending the celebration.
Traditional ceremonies often include the use of attar as a fragrant offering.

Etymology

From Persian 'ʿitr' (perfume), via Hindi-Urdu 'itr','attar'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈætɑr / (AmE), / ˈætɑː / (BrE)
a fragrant essential oil or perfume obtained from flowers, especially rose petals
perfumed oil from flowers

Examples

The delicate fragrance of attar filled the room during the traditional ceremony.
She purchased a small bottle of rose attar as a gift from the bazaar.
Artisans carefully extract attar from flower petals using ancient distillation methods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: otto - essential oil from flowers (variant spelling of attar)

Etymology

From Persian *ʿaṭr* (perfume, scent) via Hindi and Urdu.
