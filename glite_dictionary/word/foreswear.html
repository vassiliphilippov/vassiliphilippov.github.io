to foreswear

1. VERB

/ fɔrˈswɛr / (AmE), / fɔːˈswɛə / (BrE)
Formally renounce, abstain from, or give up something, especially a belief, practice, or habit.
formally renounce or give up
archaic

Examples

After years of indulgence, he decided to foreswear alcohol entirely.
The king was forced to foreswear his claim to the throne.
She publicly foreswore her association with the group.

Synset Examples

We are dispensing with formalities
relinquish the old ideas

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to renounce - Formally declare one's abandonment of something
  • Synonym: to relinquish - Voluntarily cease to keep or claim; give up

Etymology

From Old English forswearan (to perjure oneself), from for- (away, off) + swerian (to swear).

2. VERB

/ fɔrˈswɛr / (AmE), / fɔːˈswɛə / (BrE)
to renounce or give up something, especially formally or under oath
formally give up; renounce

Examples

After years of indulgence, he decided to foreswear all unhealthy foods for good health.
She has foresworn her allegiance to the organization, choosing a new path in life.
Many athletes temporarily foreswear certain pleasures before a competition to remain focused.

Synset Examples

I am foreswearing women forever

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to renounce - to formally declare abandonment of a claim or right
  • Synonym: to relinquish - to voluntarily give up or surrender

Etymology

From Old English forswerian, from for- (denoting prohibition) + swerian (to swear).

Synset POS: v
