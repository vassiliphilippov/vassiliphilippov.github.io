megalocyte

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛɡəloʊˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɛɡələʊˌsaɪt / (BrE)
an abnormally large red blood cell, often associated with certain types of anemia
abnormally large red blood cell
medical

Examples

The doctor identified a megalocyte while examining the patient's blood smear.
A high number of megalocytes can be a sign of pernicious anemia.
Researchers study megalocyte formation to better understand certain blood disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: macrocyte - an abnormally large red blood cell
  • Antonym: microcyte - an abnormally small red blood cell

Etymology

From Greek 'megas' (large) + 'kytos' (cell).
