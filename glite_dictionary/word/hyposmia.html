hyposmia

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈpɑz.mi.ə / (AmE), / haɪˈpɒz.mi.ə / (BrE)
A reduced or lessened sensitivity to odors; a decreased ability to smell.
reduced sense of smell
medical

Examples

After his cold, he noticed he had hyposmia and could barely detect the scent of flowers.
Hyposmia is sometimes an early symptom of neurological diseases like Parkinson's.
The doctor diagnosed her with hyposmia after she reported changes in her sense of smell.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: anosmia - loss or absence of the sense of smell
  • Degree Lower: normosmia - normal sense of smell

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'hypo-' (under) and 'osme' (smell).
