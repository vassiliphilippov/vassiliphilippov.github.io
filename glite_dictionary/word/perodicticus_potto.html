Perodicticus potto

1. NOUN

/ pɛˌroʊˈdɪktɪkəs ˈpɑtoʊ / (AmE), / pɛˌrəʊˈdɪktɪkəs ˈpɒtəʊ / (BrE)
A species of slow-moving, nocturnal primate native to tropical Africa, notable for its gray-brown fur and vestigial index finger.
a species of slow-moving African primate

Examples

The Perodicticus potto spends most of its life in the trees, rarely coming down to the ground.
Researchers observed the Perodicticus potto using its specially adapted hands to grip branches tightly.
Deforestation poses a significant threat to the survival of the Perodicticus potto in its natural habitat.

Etymology

From scientific Latin 'Perodicticus' (genus name) + 'potto' (a name of West African origin for this animal).
