agglomerator

1. NOUN

/ əˈɡlɑm.əˌreɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / əˈɡlɒm.əˌreɪ.tə / (BrE)
a machine or device that gathers small particles or materials into larger, rounded masses or balls, often by using heat, pressure, or agitation
device that gathers material into balls

Examples

The factory installed a new agglomerator to improve the efficiency of pellet production.
Fine powders are processed in the agglomerator to prevent dust and improve handling.
A sonic agglomerator uses sound waves to bind particles together.

Synset Examples

a sonic agglomerator

Etymology

From Latin 'agglomerare' (to gather into a ball) + English '-or' (agent noun suffix).
