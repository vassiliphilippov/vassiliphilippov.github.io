archosaur

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑr.kəˌsɔr / (AmE), / ˈɑː.kə.sɔː / (BrE)
any of a group of extinct reptiles that includes the ancestors and relatives of dinosaurs, pterosaurs, and crocodilians
group of extinct reptiles including dinosaurs

Examples

Most scientists believe birds are descended from archosaurs.
The discovery of the fossil filled an important gap in our understanding of archosaur evolution.
Early archosaurs dominated terrestrial ecosystems before the rise of true dinosaurs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dinosaur - large, extinct terrestrial reptile
  • Degree Higher: reptile - cold-blooded, usually egg-laying vertebrate of the class Reptilia

Etymology

From Greek arkhos (ruler) + sauros (lizard).
