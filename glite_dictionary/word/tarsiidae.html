Tarsiidae

1. NOUN

/ tɑːrˈsiɪˌdiː / (AmE), / tɑːˈsɪɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, arboreal primates comprising the tarsiers, found mainly in Southeast Asia.
family of tarsiers (primates)

Examples

Researchers have long debated the evolutionary history of Tarsiidae.
The unusual anatomy of Tarsiidae distinguishes them from other primate families.
Members of the Tarsiidae family are known for their elongated tarsal bones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tarsius - genus of primates known as tarsiers
  • Synonym: tarsier - small nocturnal primates with large eyes, native to Southeast Asia

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus name Tarsius and the family suffix -idae.
