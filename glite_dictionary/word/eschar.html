eschar

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛskɑr / (AmE), / ˈɛskɑː / (BrE)
a dry, dark scab or dead patch of skin formed as a result of a burn, cauterization, or infection
dry scab after burn or cauterization
medical

Examples

After the burn healed, an eschar formed over the affected area.
The surgeon removed the eschar to assess the underlying tissue damage.
A thick, black eschar is often seen in certain skin infections.

Etymology

From Greek 'escharā' (scab formed after a burn), through Latin 'eschar' (scab).
