meningioma

1. NOUN

/ məˌnɪndʒiˈoʊmə / (AmE), / mɛˌnɪndʒiˈəʊmə / (BrE)
a tumor that develops from the meninges, the membranes that surround the brain and spinal cord, usually slow-growing and sometimes malignant
tumor of the meninges in the brain or spinal cord
medical

Examples

The surgeon removed the meningioma after careful evaluation of the MRI results.
Although most meningiomas are benign, they can still cause serious symptoms depending on their location.
Symptoms of a meningioma may include headaches, vision problems, and seizures.

Etymology

From New Latin, from 'meninges' (the membranes) + '-oma' (tumor).
