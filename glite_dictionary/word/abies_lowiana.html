Abies lowiana

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.bi.iːz loʊˈwaɪ.ə.nə / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.bi.iːz ləʊˈwaɪ.ənə / (BrE)
A medium to tall species of fir tree native to central and western United States, characterized by a narrow, upright crown and soft wood.
species of North American fir tree

Examples

The Sierra Nevada forests are home to the majestic Abies lowiana.
Lumber from Abies lowiana is valued for its softness and workability.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Abies lowiana in different elevations.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: fir - a type of evergreen coniferous tree in the genus Abies

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Abies' (fir) and the species epithet honors John Low, an American botanist.
