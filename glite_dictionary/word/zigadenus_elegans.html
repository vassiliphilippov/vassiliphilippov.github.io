Zigadenus elegans

1. NOUN

/ zɪɡəˈdiːnəs ˈɛlɪɡənz / (AmE), / zɪɡəˈdiːnəs ˈɛlɪɡənz / (BrE)
A plant of western North America, with grasslike leaves and greenish-white flowers, also known as elegant death camas.
a western North American flowering plant

Examples

The poisonous bulbs of Zigadenus elegans are easily mistaken for edible wild onions.
We found a cluster of Zigadenus elegans blooming along the rocky hillside.
Cattle are sometimes poisoned after grazing in areas where Zigadenus elegans grows abundantly.

Etymology

From genus name Zigadenus (Greek zygos 'yoke' + aden 'gland') and Latin elegans 'elegant, fine'.
