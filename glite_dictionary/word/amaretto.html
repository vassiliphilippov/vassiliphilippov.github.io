amaretto

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːməˈrɛtoʊ / (AmE), / ˌaməˈrɛtəʊ / (BrE)
A sweet Italian liqueur flavored with almonds or the kernels of stone fruit, often used in cocktails and desserts.
Italian almond-flavored liqueur

Examples

He ordered an amaretto on the rocks after dinner.
This cake recipe calls for a splash of amaretto to enhance the flavor.
She added a little amaretto to her coffee for a sweet, nutty taste.

Etymology

Italian, diminutive of 'amaro' meaning 'bitter'.
