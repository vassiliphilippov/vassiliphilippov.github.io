stalagmometer

1. NOUN

/ stəˌlæɡˈmɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / stælˌæɡˈmɒmɪtə / (BrE)
an instrument used to measure the surface tension of liquids by counting the number of drops formed as the liquid flows out of a tube
instrument for measuring surface tension
medical

Examples

The scientist carefully calibrated the stalagmometer before beginning her experiment with the new liquid sample.
Using a stalagmometer, he measured the surface tension of several unknown solutions in the laboratory.
Training included a demonstration of handling a stalagmometer and interpreting its results.

Synset Examples

The part of the bottom of the stalagmometer is narrowed down to let the fluid fall out from the tube in a shape of drop.

Etymology

From Greek 'stalagma' (drop) + '-meter' (measuring device).
