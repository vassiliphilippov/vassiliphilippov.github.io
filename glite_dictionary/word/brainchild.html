brainchild

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪnˌtʃaɪld / (AmE), / ˈbreɪntʃaɪld / (BrE)
an idea, invention, or project that is the result of someone's creativity or ingenuity
product of someone's creative work

Examples

The new mobile app is the brainchild of a group of university students.
Her latest novel is the brainchild of many years of hard work and dedication.
That impressive sculpture in the park is the brainchild of a local artist.

Synset Examples

he had little respect for the inspirations of other artists
after years of work his brainchild was a tangible reality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invention - something that has been created or designed, especially a new device or process
  • Synonym: creation - the act of bringing something into existence; something that has been made
  • Antonym: copy - something made to be like something else

Etymology

Formed from 'brain' (the mind) + 'child' (offspring), first used in the late 19th century.
