baneful

1. ADJ

/ ˈbeɪnfəl / (AmE), / ˈbeɪnf(ə)l / (BrE)
Causing great harm or destruction; exceedingly harmful or destructive.
exceedingly harmful; deadly

Examples

The baneful effects of pollution on marine life are well documented.
His baneful influence led many students astray over the years.
Exposure to the chemical had a baneful impact on the environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harmful - causing or capable of causing harm
  • Antonym: beneficial - producing good or helpful results

Etymology

From Middle English bane (poison, destruction) + -ful (full of).

2. ADJ

/ ˈbeɪnfəl / (AmE), / ˈbeɪnfəl / (BrE)
causing harm, ruin, or death; seriously harmful or sinister
harmful or deadly

Examples

The baneful effects of pollution are evident in the declining health of the local wildlife.
She regarded him with a baneful glare that chilled him to the core.
Rumors had a baneful influence on the community, sowing distrust among friends.

Synset Examples

the Florida eagles have a fierce baleful look

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baleful - threatening harm; menacing or deadly

Etymology

From late Middle English, from bane (a cause of death or destruction) + -ful.
