cargo-200

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑrgoʊ tuː ˈhʌndrəd / (AmE), / ˈkɑːɡəʊ tuː ˈhʌndrəd / (BrE)
a military term for the transportation of the bodies of deceased soldiers, especially in sealed coffins; the deceased themselves
military term for transporting deceased soldiers

Examples

The government confirmed the arrival of a new cargo-200 flight from the war zone.
Families waited anxiously for news, fearing their sons might be among the cargo-200.
International media reported a rise in cargo-200 transports after the recent escalation.

Synset Examples

Families gathered to grieve for their loved ones in the cargo-200.
The cargo-200 flight brought back fallen soldiers.

Etymology

From Russian 'Груз 200' (Gruz 200), a military code name for the transport of bodies in zinc coffins.
