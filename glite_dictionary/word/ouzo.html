ouzo

1. NOUN

/ ˈuːzoʊ / (AmE), / ˈuːzəʊ / (BrE)
a clear, strong Greek alcoholic drink flavored with aniseed, often served as an aperitif
Greek anise-flavored alcoholic drink

Examples

After dinner, we enjoyed a glass of ouzo on the patio overlooking the sea.
The distinctive aroma of ouzo reminded him of his holiday in Greece.
She brought a bottle of ouzo back from her trip as a souvenir.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sambuca - an Italian anise-flavored liqueur
  • Synonym: anisette - a sweet, anise-flavored liqueur

Etymology

From Modern Greek ούζο (oúzo).
