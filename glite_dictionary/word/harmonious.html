harmonious

1. ADJ

/ hɑrˈmoʊniəs / (AmE), / hɑːˈməʊniəs / (BrE)
Having parts that are well matched and blend together in a pleasing or consistent way.
well-matched; blending nicely

Examples

The architect designed a harmonious blend of old and new in the building.
Their voices were harmonious, creating a beautiful choir performance.
All the elements in this room are harmonious and create a cozy atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: discordant - lacking harmony or agreement

Etymology

From Latin 'harmoniosus', from Greek 'harmonios' (harmonious).

2. ADJ

/ hɑːrˈmoʊniəs / (AmE), / hɑːˈmɒniəs / (BrE)
suitable or fitting together in a pleasing or appropriate way; forming a consistent or agreeable whole.
suitable or fitting together

Examples

The design of the new extension was harmonious with the existing building.
Her outfit was harmonious with the event's elegant theme.
The colors in the living room were carefully chosen to be harmonious.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compatible - able to exist or work together without conflict
  • Antonym: conflicting - incompatible or in disagreement

Etymology

From Latin 'harmoniosus', from Greek 'harmonios' (in harmony), relating to fitting together.

3. ADJ

/ hɑrˈmoʊniəs / (AmE), / hɑːˈməʊniəs / (BrE)
(of music or sound) having a pleasant and agreeable combination of tones; musically pleasing or well blended.
musically pleasing; well blended sound

Examples

The choir produced a harmonious blend of voices during the performance.
We listened to the harmonious notes of the orchestra as they filled the hall.
Their instruments created a harmonious sound that was soothing and pleasant to the ear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melodious - having a pleasant melody; tuneful
  • Antonym: discordant - lacking harmony; harsh or jarring in sound

Etymology

From Latin 'harmoniosus', from Greek 'harmonios' (harmonious, fitted together).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01166627-a

4. ADJ

/ hɑrˈmoʊniəs / (AmE), / hɑːˈməʊniəs / (BrE)
(of people, groups, or things) living or existing together in a peaceful or agreeable way.
peaceful and in agreement

Examples

They strive to create a harmonious working environment where everyone feels welcome.
The colors in the painting blend in a harmonious way.
Despite their different backgrounds, the members live together in a harmonious community.

Synset Examples

harmonious family relationships

Related Terms

  • Antonym: discordant - lacking harmony or agreement
  • Synonym: concordant - in agreement; consistent or harmonious

Etymology

From Latin harmoniosus, from harmonia (harmony).
