Mongolian

1. NOUN

/ mɑŋˈɡoʊliən / (AmE), / mɒŋˈɡəʊliən / (BrE)
a member of the nomadic people native to Mongolia, especially those belonging to the dominant ethnic group of the country
a person from Mongolia; member of the Mongol people

Examples

Many Mongolian herders continue to live in traditional yurts on the steppe.
The Mongolian people are known for their rich history and unique culture.
She became friends with a group of Mongolian students at the university.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mongol - member of the Mongol ethnic group
  • Antonym: Chinese - a person from China

Etymology

From Mongolia + -an, denoting relation to Mongolia or its people.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09664219-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ mɑnˈɡoʊliən / (AmE), / mɒŋˈɡəʊliən / (BrE)
the language or family of closely related languages spoken mainly in Mongolia and surrounding regions
Altaic language spoken in Mongolia

Examples

Many students in Ulaanbaatar speak fluent Mongolian in their daily lives.
He is studying the history and structure of Mongolian at university.
The traditional script for Mongolian differs markedly from the Cyrillic alphabet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mongol - native or inhabitant of Mongolia; Mongolian language (less common usage)

Etymology

From Mongolia, with the suffix -an indicating belonging or relating to.

Source: WordNet
