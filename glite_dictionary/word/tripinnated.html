tripinnated

1. ADJ

/ traɪˈpɪneɪtɪd / (AmE), / trɪˈpɪneɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a leaf) divided pinnately three times; thrice pinnate.
thrice pinnate leaf (botany)

Examples

The fern's tripinnated fronds are an example of complex leaf division seen in some plant species.
Under the microscope, the botanist confirmed the specimen had tripinnated leaves.
Many members of the carrot family exhibit tripinnated leaf structures, making them easily identifiable.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bipinnated - (of a leaf) twice pinnate; divided pinnately two times
  • Degree Higher: quadripinnated - (of a leaf) four times pinnate; divided pinnately four times

Etymology

From Latin tri- (three) + pinnatus (feathered, winged).
