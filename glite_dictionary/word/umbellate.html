umbellate

1. ADJ

/ ʌmˈbɛlət / (AmE), / ʌmˈbɛlət / (BrE)
(of a plant or flower cluster) bearing or forming umbels, or resembling an umbel in appearance.
having or resembling umbels (botany)

Examples

The plant's umbellate flowers attracted a variety of pollinators throughout the summer.
Some species in the carrot family are easily recognized by their umbellate arrangement of blossoms.
The umbellate inflorescence made the wildflowers stand out in the meadow.

Etymology

From Latin umbellatus, from umbella (a little shadow, parasol, or umbel).

2. ADJ

/ ʌmˈbɛlət / (AmE), / ʌmˈbɛlət / (BrE)
(of a plant or flower cluster) arranged like or resembling an umbel, with stalks of nearly equal length spreading from a common point.
arranged or shaped like an umbel

Examples

The umbellate inflorescence was typical of many members in the carrot family.
Several species display umbellate flower clusters at the top of their stems.
The plant's umbellate arrangement made identifying it much easier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: umbel - a flower cluster with stalks of roughly equal length that spread from a common point, resembling an umbrella

Etymology

From Latin umbellatus, from umbella (a parasol, umbel).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
