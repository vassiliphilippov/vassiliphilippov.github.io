modicum

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːdɪkəm / (AmE), / ˈmɒdɪkəm / (BrE)
a small quantity of something, especially something desirable or valuable
small quantity of something

Examples

She felt a modicum of relief after hearing the good news.
Even a modicum of respect would have made a difference.
With only a modicum of effort, you can improve your results.

Synset Examples

England still expects a modicum of eccentricity in its artists

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bit - a small amount or portion
  • Degree Lower: trace - a very small quantity
  • Antonym: abundance - a very large quantity

Etymology

From Latin 'modicum' (a little), diminutive of 'modicus' (moderate), from 'modus' (measure).
