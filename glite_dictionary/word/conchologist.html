conchologist

1. NOUN

/ kɑnˈkɑːlədʒɪst / (AmE), / kɒŋˈkɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
a person who collects and studies the shells of mollusks
collector and student of mollusc shells

Examples

The conchologist spent years cataloguing rare shells from beaches around the world.
As a passionate conchologist, she could identify dozens of species by their shells alone.
The museum hosted a talk by a renowned conchologist on the diversity of marine mollusks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malacologist - a scientist who studies mollusks in general

Etymology

From Greek konkhē (shell) + -logist (one who studies).
