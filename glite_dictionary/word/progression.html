progression

1. NOUN

/ prəˈɡrɛʃən / (AmE), / prəˈɡrɛʃən / (BrE)
a sequence of things that follow one another according to a particular rule or pattern, especially in mathematics or music
a sequence with a patterned advance

Examples

The numbers 2, 4, 6, and 8 form an arithmetic progression.
A musical progression can create a sense of movement in a composition.
His career followed a steady progression from researcher to department head.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sequence - a set of related events or things that follow each other in a particular order
  • Synonym: series - a number of things, events, or people of a similar kind or related nature coming one after another

Etymology

From Latin progressio (an advance), from progredi (to go forward).

2. NOUN

/ prəˈɡrɛʃən / (AmE), / prəˈɡrɛʃən / (BrE)
the process of moving forward or developing towards a more advanced state
a movement forward or development

Examples

The scientists observed a steady progression of the disease over several weeks.
His career has followed a natural progression from entry-level roles to management.
The progression of the parade down the street was watched by hundreds of spectators.

Synset Examples

he listened for the progress of the troops

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advance - a forward movement or development
  • Antonym: regression - a return to a previous or less developed state

Etymology

From Latin 'progressio' meaning an advance, from 'progredi' (to go forward).

3. NOUN

/ prəˈɡrɛʃən / (AmE), / prəˈɡrɛʃən / (BrE)
the process of developing or moving gradually towards a more advanced state or achieving a particular goal
gradual development or forward movement

Examples

The progression of the disease was slower than the doctors had initially feared.
She was pleased with her progression from beginner to advanced level in just one year.
The scientists monitored the progression of the experiment over several weeks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advance - forward movement towards a goal
  • Antonym: regression - a return to a previous or less developed state

Etymology

From Latin progressio (an advance), from progredi (to go forth, advance).
