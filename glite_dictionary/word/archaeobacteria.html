archaeobacteria

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrkioʊˌbækˈtɪriə / (AmE), / ˌɑːkɪəʊbækˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
Microorganisms regarded as ancient life forms that evolved separately from bacteria and blue-green algae, now classified as archaea.
ancient group of microorganisms (archaea)

Examples

Scientists once classified archaeobacteria as a type of bacteria before recognizing their distinct characteristics.
Recent studies suggest that archaeobacteria thrive in extreme environments such as hot springs and salt lakes.
The unique cell membranes of archaeobacteria distinguish them from both bacteria and eukaryotes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archaea - domain of single-celled microorganisms distinct from bacteria
  • Antonym: bacteria - large domain of prokaryotic microorganisms

Etymology

From Greek arkhaīos (ancient) + bacterium (bacterium).
