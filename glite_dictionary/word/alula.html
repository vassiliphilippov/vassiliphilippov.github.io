alula

1. NOUN

/ əˈluːlə / (AmE), / əˈluːlə / (BrE)
A scalelike structure located between the base of the wing and the halter in certain two-winged flies.
scalelike structure on a two-winged fly

Examples

The scientist carefully examined the alula under a microscope to study its texture.
Damage to the alula can affect the flight capabilities of some flies.
Entomologists use characteristics of the alula to differentiate between species of flies.

Etymology

From Latin 'alula', diminutive of 'ala' meaning 'wing'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæljʊlə / (AmE), / ˈæl.jʊ.lə / (BrE)
A small projection on the front edge of a bird's wing, consisting of a tuft of short, stiff feathers attached to the first digit, that aids in flight control.
projection of feathers on a bird's wing

Examples

The bird spread its alula as it prepared to land gently on the branch.
Damage to the alula can seriously affect a bird's ability to maneuver in flight.
Birdwatchers often notice the alula when studying a bird's wing structure up close.

Etymology

From Latin 'alula', diminutive of 'ala' meaning 'wing'.
