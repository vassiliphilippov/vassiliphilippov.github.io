Fucaceae

1. NOUN

/ fjuːˈkeɪsiˌiː / (AmE), / fjuːˈkeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of brown algae that includes gulfweeds and rockweeds, common in marine environments
family of brown algae

Examples

The Fucaceae are abundant along rocky shorelines where they form dense algae beds.
Marine biologists often study the reproductive cycles of the Fucaceae.
Several genera within the Fucaceae are important components of intertidal ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fucus - a genus of brown algae within the Fucaceae family

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus Fucus and the family suffix -aceae.

2. NOUN

/ fjuˈkeɪsiˌaɪ / (AmE), / fjuːˈkeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of brown algae comprising gulfweeds and rockweeds, typically found in marine environments
family of brown algae (gulfweeds, rockweeds)

Examples

The Fucaceae are commonly found along rocky northern seashores.
Members of the Fucaceae include the familiar rockweed and gulfweed.
Scientists study the Fucaceae to understand their adaptations to intertidal environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fucus - a genus of brown algae in the family Fucaceae
  • Degree Higher: Sargassaceae - another family of brown algae, related to Fucaceae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Fucus (a genus of algae) and -aceae (a standard family suffix in botanical nomenclature).
