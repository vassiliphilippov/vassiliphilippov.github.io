Guanyin

1. PROPN

/ ˈgwɑnˌjɪn / (AmE), / ˈgwæn.jɪn / (BrE)
in Buddhism, a female Bodhisattva known as the Goddess of Mercy, revered especially in East Asia and identified as an aspect of the Bodhisattva Avalokiteśvara.
Buddhist Goddess of Mercy

Examples

Many people pray to Guanyin for compassion in times of distress.
In Chinese temples, statues of Guanyin are often found at the entrance.
The story of Guanyin is told throughout East Asia as a symbol of mercy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Avalokiteśvara - Bodhisattva of compassion in Buddhism, of which Guanyin is an aspect
  • Synonym: Kwannon - Japanese name for Guanyin, the Bodhisattva of Mercy

Etymology

From Chinese 观音 (Guānyīn), meaning 'Perceiver of Sounds (or Cries)', referring to perceived compassion.

2. PROPN

/ ɡwɑnˈjin / (AmE), / ɡwænˈjɪn / (BrE)
(in Chinese Buddhism) the bodhisattva of compassion, venerated as the goddess of mercy.
bodhisattva of compassion in Chinese Buddhism

Examples

Many temples in China are dedicated to Guanyin, the goddess of mercy.
The legend of Guanyin is an important part of East Asian Buddhist tradition.
Visitors pray to Guanyin for compassion and protection during difficult times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kannon - Japanese Buddhist bodhisattva of compassion
  • Synonym: Avalokiteshvara - the Sanskrit name for the bodhisattva of compassion

Etymology

From Chinese 观音 (Guānyīn), meaning 'Perceiver of Sounds (of the World)'.
