classicist

1. NOUN

/ ˈklæsɪsɪst / (AmE), / ˈklæsɪsɪst / (BrE)
an artist or thinker who practices or supports classicism, especially in art, literature, or architecture
advocate or practitioner of classicism

Examples

She is a renowned classicist whose paintings evoke the harmony of ancient Greece.
As a classicist, he rejects modern styles in favor of traditional forms and ideals.
Many classicists admire the balance and clarity found in Renaissance art.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: romanticist - an artist or thinker who practices or supports romanticism

Etymology

From 'classicism' + -ist (denoting an adherent or practitioner).

2. NOUN

/ ˈklæsəsɪst / (AmE), / ˈklæsɪsɪst / (BrE)
A person who studies or is an expert in the languages, literature, and culture of ancient Greece and Rome.
student of ancient Greek and Latin

Examples

She is a renowned classicist who has published many articles on ancient Greek poetry.
As a classicist, he spends much of his time translating Latin texts into English.
The university hired a new classicist to expand its ancient studies program.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classic - a work of ancient Greek or Roman literature; or something judged to be outstanding

Etymology

From 'classics', referring to classical studies, plus '-ist' (denoting a person who practices or is concerned with something).
