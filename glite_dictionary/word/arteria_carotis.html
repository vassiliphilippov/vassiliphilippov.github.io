arteria carotis

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈtɪriə kəˈroʊtɪs / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪəriə kəˈrəʊtɪs / (BrE)
Either of two major arteries in the neck that supply blood to the head and brain; also called the carotid artery.
major artery of the neck and head
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully examined the arteria carotis before proceeding with the operation.
A blockage in the arteria carotis can lead to serious medical emergencies such as a stroke.
Medical students learn to locate the arteria carotis during their anatomy classes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carotid artery - either of the two main arteries that supply blood to the head and neck

Etymology

From Latin 'arteria carotis', from Greek 'karōtis', meaning 'causing stupor' (as blockage can cause fainting).
