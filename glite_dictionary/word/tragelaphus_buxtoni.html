Tragelaphus buxtoni

1. NOUN

/ træɡəˌlæfəs ˈbʌkstəni / (AmE), / trəˈɡɛlɪfəs ˈbʌkstəni / (BrE)
a large, shaggy antelope native to the mountainous regions of Ethiopia, also known as the mountain nyala
Ethiopian mountain-dwelling antelope

Examples

The endangered Tragelaphus buxtoni is found only in Ethiopia's highland forests.
Wildlife conservationists track Tragelaphus buxtoni to monitor its dwindling population.
Photographers travel to the Bale Mountains to catch a glimpse of the elusive Tragelaphus buxtoni.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mountain nyala - common name for Tragelaphus buxtoni, an Ethiopian antelope

Etymology

Tragelaphus from Greek 'tragos' (goat) + 'elaphos' (deer); 'buxtoni' honours British naturalist Arnold Buxton.
