Magnificat

1. NOUN

/ mæɡˈnɪfɪˌkæt / (AmE), / mæɡˈnɪfɪˌkæt / (BrE)
a hymn of praise sung by the Virgin Mary in the Gospel of Luke, beginning with 'My soul magnifies the Lord'; often used in Christian worship
the canticle of the Virgin Mary in Luke 1:46–55

Examples

The choir sang the Magnificat beautifully during the evening service.
Many composers have set the Magnificat to music over the centuries.
She recited the words of the Magnificat as part of her daily prayers.

Etymology

From Latin 'Magnificat anima mea Dominum' (My soul magnifies the Lord), the opening words of the hymn.
