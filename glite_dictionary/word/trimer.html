trimer

1. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪ.mɚ / (AmE), / ˈtraɪ.mə / (BrE)
a compound or polymer consisting of three identical monomer units chemically bonded together
molecule of three identical monomers

Examples

A trimer is formed when three identical molecules join together in a chemical reaction.
Collagen often exists as a trimer in connective tissues.
Scientists studied the structure of the viral protein trimer to understand how it binds to cells.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: dimer - a molecule composed of two identical monomers
  • Degree Higher: tetramer - a molecule consisting of four identical monomers

Etymology

From Greek 'tri-' (three) and '-meros' (part).
