Silesia

1. PROPN

/ saɪˈliːʒə / (AmE), / saɪˈliːziə / (BrE)
a historical region in central Europe, known for its rich coal and iron ore deposits, which was annexed by Prussia in 1742 and is now mostly part of Poland
historical region in central Europe

Examples

She was born in Silesia when it was part of Germany, but moved to Poland after the borders changed.
The industrial potential of Silesia attracted significant investment throughout the nineteenth century.
Modern-day Silesia is known for its cultural heritage and diverse population.

Etymology

From Latin Silesia, from German Schlesien, of uncertain origin.
