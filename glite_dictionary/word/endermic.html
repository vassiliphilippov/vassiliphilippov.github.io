endermic

1. ADJ

/ ɛnˈdɜrmɪk / (AmE), / ɛnˈdɜːmɪk / (BrE)
(of a substance or treatment) acting by absorption through the skin.
acting by absorption through the skin
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an endermic cream for the patient's rash.
Some medications are administered in endermic form to avoid digestive side effects.
The endermic route is often preferred for controlled drug delivery.

Synset Examples

endermic ointment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transdermal - relating to or denoting the application of a medicine or drug through the skin, typically by using an adhesive patch

Etymology

From Greek 'endermis' (in the skin), from 'en-' (in) + 'derma' (skin).
