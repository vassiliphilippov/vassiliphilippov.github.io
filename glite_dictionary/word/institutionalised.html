institutionalised

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪnstəˈtuːʃənəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˌɪnstɪˈtjuːʃənəlaɪzd / (BrE)
officially placed in or committed to a specialized institution such as a hospital, prison, or care facility.
committed to a specialized institution

Examples

Many institutionalised patients struggle to adapt to life outside the facility.
The institutionalised children were provided with structured routines every day.
Some people fear becoming institutionalised after spending years in prison.

Synset Examples

had hopes of rehabilitating the institutionalized juvenile delinquents

Etymology

From 'institution' + '-alised', meaning made or rendered into an institution; the suffix '-ise'/-'ize' from Latin '-izare'.

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˌstɪtuˈʃənəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ɪnˌstɪtjuˈʃənaɪzd / (BrE)
(Of a practice, custom, or behavior) established as a norm or integrated into a structured and well-established system or institution.
incorporated into an established system

Examples

The country struggled with institutionalised corruption that affected every level of government.
Many forms of discrimination have become institutionalised over time, making change difficult.
He campaigned against institutionalised racism in schools and workplaces.

Synset Examples

institutionalized graft
institutionalized suicide as practiced in Japan

Related Terms

  • Synonym: institutionalized - US spelling; given the character of an institution
