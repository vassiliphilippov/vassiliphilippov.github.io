Guarneri

1. PROPN

/ ɡwɑrˈnɛr.i / (AmE), / ɡwɑːˈnɛə.ri / (BrE)
A member of a renowned family of Italian violin makers from Cremona, especially noted for instruments crafted by Giuseppe Guarneri.
famous Italian family of violin makers

Examples

He owns a rare Guarneri violin valued at over a million dollars.
Some musicians prefer the deep, rich sound of a Guarneri over other instruments.
The orchestra featured a soloist performing on a Guarneri from the 18th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Stradivarius - Famous Italian family of violin makers, especially Antonio Stradivari

Etymology

From the Italian surname Guarneri, renowned violin makers of Cremona, Italy.
