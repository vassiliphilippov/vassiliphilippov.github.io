Medoc

1. NOUN

/ meɪˈdɑk / (AmE), / meɪˈdɒk / (BrE)
A red Bordeaux wine produced in the Medoc district of southwestern France, noted for its deep flavor and complexity.
red Bordeaux wine from Medoc, France

Examples

The sommelier recommended a Medoc to accompany our steak dinner.
She collects vintage bottles of Medoc from renowned chateaux.
Last night, we enjoyed a glass of Medoc with our roasted lamb.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Bordeaux - red wine from the Bordeaux region of France

Etymology

From French Médoc, the region's name in southwestern France.
