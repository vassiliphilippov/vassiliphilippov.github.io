fortuneteller

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrtʃəntɛlɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːtʃənˌtɛlə / (BrE)
a person who claims to be able to predict someone’s future, often using cards, palm reading, or other methods
person who predicts the future

Examples

The fortuneteller gazed into her crystal ball and began to speak of my destiny.
At the carnival, we visited a fortuneteller who read our palms for fun.
The novel's mysterious fortuneteller warned the hero about a difficult journey ahead.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seer - a person who is supposed to be able to foresee the future
  • Synonym: prophet - a person regarded as an inspired teacher or proclaimer of the will of God
  • Antonym: skeptic - a person inclined to question or doubt accepted opinions

Etymology

Formed from 'fortune' (fate, destiny) and 'teller' (one who tells or relates).
