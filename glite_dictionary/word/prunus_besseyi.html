Prunus besseyi

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruːnəs bəˈsaɪ.i / (AmE), / ˈpruːnəs bəˈsiː.aɪ / (BrE)
A dwarf ornamental shrub native to the western United States, known for its large, sweet, edible fruits ranging in color from black to red and yellow.
dwarf shrub with sweet edible fruit

Examples

The Prunus besseyi shrub produces fruit that is prized for making jams and preserves.
Many gardeners plant Prunus besseyi for its attractive flowers and edible berries.
The sweet fruit of Prunus besseyi attracts birds and wildlife in the spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sand cherry - common name for Prunus besseyi, a fruit-bearing shrub

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Prunus' (plum, cherry) and specific epithet in honor of Charles E. Bessey, American botanist.
