behaviour

1. NOUN

/ bəˈheɪvjər / (AmE), / bɪˈheɪvjə / (BrE)
The observable actions, responses, or reactions of a person, animal, or organism, especially as studied in psychology.
observable actions or responses

Examples

Psychologists study human behaviour to understand how people react in different situations.
Animal behaviour can often be predicted by observing environmental changes.
The teacher noticed a sudden change in the student's behaviour during the semester.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conduct - the manner in which a person behaves, especially on a particular occasion or in a particular context
  • Antonym: misconduct - unacceptable or improper behavior

Etymology

From Old French *behaviour* (modern French *comportement*), from *behave*, ultimately from Middle English.

2. NOUN

/ bɪˈheɪvjər / (AmE), / bɪˈheɪvjə / (BrE)
the way in which a machine, substance, or system acts or responds under particular conditions
conduct or response of a machine or substance

Examples

The behaviour of the new engine was carefully monitored during the test run.
Researchers are studying the behaviour of certain chemicals at high temperatures.
The behaviour of the computer system under heavy load was remarkably stable.

Synset Examples

the behavior of small particles can be studied in experiments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: behavior - alternative US spelling of 'behaviour'

Etymology

From Middle English 'behave', influenced by Old French 'be-' (about) + 'haver' (to have, to hold).

3. NOUN

/ bɪˈheɪvjər / (AmE), / bɪˈheɪvjə / (BrE)
the way in which a person acts or conducts themselves, especially towards others or in response to particular situations
manner of acting or conduct
british

Examples

Her polite behaviour towards the guests impressed everyone at the party.
The teacher praised the student's behaviour in class after he helped his classmates.
Good behaviour is expected from all children on the school trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: behavior - American English spelling with identical meaning
  • Synonym: conduct - the manner in which a person behaves
  • Antonym: misconduct - improper or unacceptable behaviour

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *behaviour*, from Old French *behaviour*, based on *be- + havour* (to have, to hold oneself); related to 'have'.

4. NOUN

/ bɪˈheɪvjər / (AmE), / bɪˈheɪvjə / (BrE)
the way in which a person acts, especially toward other people or in response to a particular situation
the way someone acts, especially to others
british

Examples

The teacher praised the student for his excellent behaviour in class.
His rude behaviour at the dinner table embarrassed his parents.
Good behaviour is expected from everyone during the ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: behavior - the way in which a person acts, US spelling
  • Synonym: conduct - the manner in which someone behaves, especially on a particular occasion or in a particular context
  • Antonym: misbehaviour - bad or improper behaviour, especially by children

Etymology

From Middle English 'behaviour', from Old French 'behaviour', from 'behave' + '-our'.
