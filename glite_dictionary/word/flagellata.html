Flagellata

1. NOUN

/ fləˌdʒɛˈleɪtə / (AmE), / fləˌdʒɛˈlɑːtə / (BrE)
A group of protozoa characterized by the presence of one or more flagella used for movement.
group of protozoa with flagella
medical

Examples

Many species within the Flagellata use their flagella to propel themselves through water.
Researchers observed several Flagellata under the microscope during the biology lab session.
The classification of Flagellata has changed as scientists learn more about protozoan diversity.

Etymology

From Latin flagellum (whip, lash), referring to the whip-like appendages.

2. NOUN

/ fləˌdʒɛləˈteɪtə / (AmE), / fləˌdʒɛləˈtɑːtə / (BrE)
A former class of protozoa characterized by the presence of one or more flagella for locomotion.
class of protozoa with flagella

Examples

The Flagellata are distinguished by their whip-like flagella used for movement.
Some scientists believe the Flagellata do not form a natural group.
The study of Flagellata has contributed to our understanding of evolutionary biology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protozoa - single-celled eukaryotic organisms, formerly regarded as animals

Etymology

From Latin 'flagellum' (whip, lash), referring to the whip-like flagella.
