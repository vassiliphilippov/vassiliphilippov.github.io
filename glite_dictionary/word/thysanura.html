Thysanura

1. NOUN

/ θaɪsəˈnjʊrə / (AmE), / θɪsəˈnjʊərə / (BrE)
an order of small, primitive, wingless insects including silverfish and firebrats; now largely obsolete as a taxonomic term
order of primitive, wingless insects

Examples

The order Thysanura includes primitive insects such as silverfish and firebrats.
Scientists once classified both silverfish and firebrats within Thysanura.
Textbooks now often refer to Thysanura as an obsolete group, replaced by more precise classifications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silverfish - a small, wingless insect with a silvery appearance
  • Synonym: firebrat - a wingless insect related to the silverfish, common in warm areas

Etymology

From Greek thysanos (tassel, fringe) + oura (tail), referring to the insects' tail filaments.
