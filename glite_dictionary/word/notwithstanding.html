notwithstanding

1. ADV

/ ˌnɑtwɪðˈstændɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌnɒtwɪðˈstændɪŋ / (BrE)
Despite what has just been said or referred to; nevertheless.
in spite of; nevertheless

Examples

Notwithstanding the bad weather, they decided to go ahead with the picnic.
The defendant claimed innocence, notwithstanding the overwhelming evidence against him.
Her explanation was clear, notwithstanding its complexity.

Synset Examples

although I'm a little afraid, however I'd like to try it
while we disliked each other, nevertheless we agreed
he was a stern yet fair master
granted that it is dangerous, all the same I still want to go

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nevertheless - in spite of that; however
  • Synonym: despite - without being affected by; in spite of

Etymology

From Middle English 'notwithstondyng', from 'not' + 'withstanding', meaning 'not standing in the way'.
