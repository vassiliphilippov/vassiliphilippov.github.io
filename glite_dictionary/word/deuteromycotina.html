Deuteromycotina

1. NOUN

/ ˌduːt̬əroʊmaɪˈkoʊtə nə / (AmE), / ˌdjuːtəˌrəʊmaɪˈkəʊtɪnə / (BrE)
a large and diverse group of fungi for which no sexually reproductive stage has been observed
group of fungi with no known sexual stage

Examples

Many plant diseases are caused by fungi classified in the Deuteromycotina.
The Deuteromycotina are often referred to as imperfect fungi due to their lack of a known sexual cycle.
Advances in molecular biology have led to the reclassification of some species previously placed in the Deuteromycotina.

Etymology

From Greek 'deuteros' (second) + 'mykes' (fungus), indicating a secondary grouping in fungal classification.

2. NOUN

/ duːˌtɛrəˌmaɪkəˈtaɪnə / (AmE), / djuːˌtɛərəˌmʌɪkəˈtʌɪnə / (BrE)
a large and diverse former subdivision of fungi comprising species with no known sexual reproductive stage, also known as 'fungi imperfecti'
former fungal subdivision lacking sexual stage
medical

Examples

Modern classification has largely replaced Deuteromycotina with more genetically accurate groups.
Some plant pathogens, formerly classified in Deuteromycotina, are crucial for agricultural research.
Only asexual reproduction is observed in the fungal species grouped as Deuteromycotina.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fungi imperfecti - group of fungi with no known sexual reproductive stage

Etymology

From Greek deuteros (second) + mykes (fungus), referring to a secondary classification of fungi.
