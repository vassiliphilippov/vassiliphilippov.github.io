Thessaly

1. PROPN

/ ˈθɛsəli / (AmE), / ˈθɛsəli / (BrE)
a region in east central Greece, known in ancient times for its fertile plains and key historical significance
region of Greece, ancient and modern

Examples

Many ancient myths and legends are set in Thessaly, making it a place of great historical interest.
The plains of Thessaly were famous for their fertility in the ancient world.
During our trip to Greece, we spent several days exploring Thessaly and its archaeological sites.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Θεσσαλία (Thessalía), name of the region.
