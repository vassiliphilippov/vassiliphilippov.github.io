to vacate

1. VERB

/ ˈveɪkeɪt, veɪˈkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈveɪkeɪt, veɪˈkeɪt / (BrE)
to leave a job, position, or office, especially so that it is available for someone else
leave a job or position

Examples

The CEO decided to vacate her position after five years of service.
Several members of the board were forced to vacate their offices due to the scandal.
He will vacate his post at the end of the fiscal year.

Synset Examples

She vacated the position when she got pregnant
The chairman resigned when he was found to have misappropriated funds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to resign - to formally give up a job or position

Etymology

From Latin vacare (to be empty, free).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02372031-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈveɪkeɪt, veɪˈkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈveɪkeɪt, veɪˈkeɪt / (BrE)
to cancel or annul (an official decision, order, or judgment) so that it is no longer in effect
to cancel or annul officially
legal

Examples

The judge decided to vacate the previous ruling after new evidence came to light.
The court may vacate an injunction if the circumstances change.
If the appeal is successful, the conviction will be vacated.

Synset Examples

He revoked the ban on smoking
lift an embargo
vacate a death sentence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revoke - to officially cancel (a decree, decision, or permission)
  • Synonym: to annul - to officially declare invalid (an order, contract, or marriage)
  • Antonym: to uphold - to maintain or support (an original decision or judgment)

Etymology

From Latin vacātus, past participle of vacāre 'to be empty, free'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00801460-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈveɪ.keɪt / (AmE), / ˈveɪ.keɪt / (BrE)
to leave a place, building, or position so that it is empty or available for someone else
leave or move out of a place

Examples

All employees must vacate the premises before the alarm is set.
The family plans to vacate their apartment by the end of the month.
Once the show is over, please vacate your seats promptly.

Synset Examples

You must vacate your office by tonight

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to occupy - to fill or take possession of a place or space

Etymology

From Latin vacare (to be empty, free).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02080923-v
Synset POS: v
