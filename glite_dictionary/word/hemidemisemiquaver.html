hemidemisemiquaver

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛmiˌdɛmɪˈsɛmiˌkweɪvər / (AmE), / ˌhɛmɪˌdɛmɪˈsɛmɪˌkweɪvə / (BrE)
A musical note played for one sixty-fourth the duration of a whole note; the sixty-fourth note.
sixty-fourth musical note
british

Examples

The pianist played a rapid series of hemidemisemiquavers in the final cadenza.
It's rare to see a hemidemisemiquaver in beginner sheet music because of its short duration.
The composer's modern works are filled with complex rhythms and hemidemisemiquavers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sixty-fourth note - A musical note with the same value as a hemidemisemiquaver
  • Degree Lower: demisemiquaver - A musical note of one thirty-second the duration of a whole note

Etymology

From hemi- (half) + demi- (half) + semi- (half) + quaver ‘eighth note’, reflecting the successive halving of note value.
