insufficiency

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.səˈfɪʃ.ən.si / (AmE), / ˌɪn.səˈfɪʃ.ən.si / (BrE)
The state of not having enough of something necessary, especially competence, skill, or ability.
lack of competence or adequacy

Examples

Her insufficiency as a manager became clear when the team failed to meet its goals.
Many applicants were rejected due to an insufficiency of relevant experience.
He felt a sense of insufficiency in dealing with complex technical problems.

Synset Examples

pointed out the insufficiencies in my report
juvenile offenses often reflect an inadequacy in the parents

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inadequacy - the quality of being insufficient or not good enough
  • Antonym: competence - the ability to do something successfully or efficiently

Etymology

From Latin insufficientia (inability, incapacity), from 'in-' (not) + 'sufficere' (to suffice).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪnsəˈfɪʃənsi / (AmE), / ˌɪnsəˈfɪʃənsi / (BrE)
The state of not having enough of something; lack of adequate quantity or number.
lack of enough; inadequacy

Examples

There was an obvious insufficiency of medical supplies in the clinic.
The insufficiency of evidence made it difficult to convict the suspect.
Financial insufficiency prevented them from renovating the entire building.

Synset Examples

the inadequacy of unemployment benefits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inadequacy - the state of being insufficient or not enough
  • Antonym: sufficiency - state of having enough

Etymology

From Latin 'insufficientia' ('not sufficient').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05120433-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˌɪnsəˈfɪʃənsi / (AmE), / ˌɪnsəˈfɪʃənsi / (BrE)
(medical) Inability of a bodily organ or part to function normally or adequately.
organ or body part not functioning properly
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with adrenal insufficiency after showing symptoms of fatigue and weight loss.
Cardiac insufficiency can lead to decreased blood flow throughout the body.
Renal insufficiency often requires close monitoring and medical treatment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: failure - Collapse or cessation of normal function, especially in organs
  • Synonym: deficiency - Lack or shortage of something necessary in the body

Etymology

From Latin insufficientia, from insufficiens meaning 'not sufficient'.
