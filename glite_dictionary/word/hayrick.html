hayrick

1. NOUN

/ ˈheɪˌrɪk / (AmE), / ˈheɪ.rɪk / (BrE)
a large pile or stack of hay, usually outdoors and often shaped to shed water
a stack of hay

Examples

After the harvest, the farmer built a massive hayrick in the corner of the field.
Children played hide-and-seek around the old hayrick on summer afternoons.
Crows perched atop the hayrick, searching for leftover grains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: haystack - a large pile of hay, typically in a field

Etymology

From 'hay' (grass cut and dried for fodder) + 'rick' (a stack or pile), both of Old English origin.
