Ephedraceae

1. NOUN

/ ɛˌfɛdrəˈsiː.iː / (AmE), / ˌɛfɪdrəˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
A family of gymnosperm plants that includes the ephedras, sometimes classified within the Gnetaceae family.
family of gymnosperm plants (ephedras)

Examples

The Ephedraceae family consists of shrubs known for their medicinal properties.
Botanists often debate whether Ephedraceae should be placed within the Gnetaceae family.
Many species within Ephedraceae thrive in arid and desert environments.

Etymology

From ‘Ephedra’ (the type genus) + Latin ‘-aceae’ (denoting a plant family).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛfəˈdreɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ˌɛfəˈdreɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of shrubs and small plants, commonly known as ephedras, sometimes included in the Gnetaceae in certain classifications
a family of plants known as ephedras

Examples

The Ephedraceae are well known for their medicinal uses, especially species of Ephedra.
In some systems, the Ephedraceae are classified within the Gnetaceae family.
The desert is home to a variety of Ephedraceae species that have adapted to harsh conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ephedra - a genus of shrubs in the family Ephedraceae

Etymology

From the genus name Ephedra, with the standard botanical Latin suffix -aceae for plant families.
