Tettigoniidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛtɪˈgoʊniːɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌtɛtɪˈgəʊniːɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of insects commonly known as katydids or long-horned grasshoppers, characterized by long antennae and resembling crickets.
family of long-horned grasshoppers (katydids)

Examples

The Tettigoniidae family includes a diverse range of katydids with distinct mating calls.
Researchers often study Tettigoniidae to better understand acoustic communication in insects.
Many tropical forests are home to numerous species within the Tettigoniidae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: katydid - an individual insect belonging to the Tettigoniidae family
  • Degree Lower: grasshopper - a related but distinct insect in the family Acrididae

Etymology

From scientific Latin Tettigoniidae, based on genus Tettigonia (from Greek tettigonion, a diminutive of tettix, cicada).

2. NOUN

/ tɛtɪˈgoʊniɪˌdi / (AmE), / tɛtɪˌgəʊnɪˈaɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of insects commonly known as long-horned grasshoppers or katydids, characterized by very long antennae and leaf-like wings.
family of long-horned grasshoppers/katydids

Examples

Many species within the Tettigoniidae family can mimic leaves for camouflage.
Researchers collected several Tettigoniidae specimens from the rainforest to study their behavior.
Members of the Tettigoniidae are often heard at night due to their distinctive calls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: katydid - insect of the family Tettigoniidae, known for distinctive song
  • Antonym: grasshopper - insect in the suborder Caelifera, related to katydids but with shorter antennae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Tettigonia.
