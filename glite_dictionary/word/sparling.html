sparling

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɑrlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspɑːlɪŋ / (BrE)
a small silvery fish, the European smelt (Osmerus eperlanus), found in coastal waters and rivers of northern Europe
the European smelt fish

Examples

Local fishermen still catch sparling during the early spring runs in the estuary.
The sparling is prized for its delicate flavor and traditional use in European cuisine.
Populations of sparling have declined in some rivers due to pollution and habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smelt - any of various small silvery fishes of the family Osmeridae

2. NOUN

/ ˈspɑrlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspɑːlɪŋ / (BrE)
a small, silvery fish (Osmerus eperlanus), also known as the smelt, found in the coastal waters and rivers of Europe
a European smelt fish

Examples

Fishermen often catch sparling in estuaries during the spring migration.
The river once teemed with sparling, but their numbers have declined in recent years.
Grilled sparling is considered a traditional delicacy in some European regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smelt - a small silvery fish found in northern waters

Etymology

Possibly related to Old French esperlan (smelt) and Middle Dutch spierling.
