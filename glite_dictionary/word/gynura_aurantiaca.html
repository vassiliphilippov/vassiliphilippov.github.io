Gynura aurantiaca

1. NOUN

/ dʒaɪˈnjʊrə ɔːˌrænˈtaɪəkə / (AmE), / dʒaɪˈnjʊərə ɔːˌranˈtʌɪəkə / (BrE)
A tropical plant native to Java, grown as an ornamental for its velvety leaves covered with violet-purple hairs.
ornamental plant with purple-haired leaves

Examples

The Gynura aurantiaca brightened the living room with its vibrant purple leaves.
She propagated her Gynura aurantiaca by taking stem cuttings in spring.
Gardeners prize Gynura aurantiaca for its unique velvety texture and bold color.

Etymology

From botanical Latin; Gynura from Greek 'gynē' (woman) and 'oura' (tail), and aurantiaca meaning 'orange-colored'.
