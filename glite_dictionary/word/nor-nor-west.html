nor'-nor'-west

1. ADV

/ nɔr nɔr wɛst / (AmE), / nɔː nɔː wɛst / (BrE)
To, toward, or in the direction of north-northwest.
toward north-northwest (compass direction)

Examples

The ship was heading nor'-nor'-west to avoid the storm.
We set our course nor'-nor'-west at daybreak.
The wind shifted nor'-nor'-west overnight, bringing cooler air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: north-northwest - direction halfway between north and northwest

2. NOUN

/ nɔr nɔr wɛst / (AmE), / nɔː nɔː west / (BrE)
the compass point halfway between north and northwest, corresponding to north-northwest (NNW) on a compass
compass point between north and northwest

Examples

The ship altered course to nor'-nor'-west to avoid the island.
The wind was blowing steadily from nor'-nor'-west throughout the morning.
They sighted the coastline at nor'-nor'-west, just as the captain had predicted.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: north-northwest - the compass point between north and northwest, abbreviated as NNW
  • Degree Lower: northwest - the compass point between north and west
  • Degree Higher: north - the compass point opposite south, one of the four cardinal points

Etymology

Abbreviated from 'north-northwest,' using contracted forms typical in nautical language.
