Artemisia stelleriana

1. NOUN

/ ɑr.tɪˈmiː.zi.ə stɛl.əˈraɪ.ə.nə / (AmE), / ɑː.tɪˈmiː.zi.ə stel.əˈraɪ.ə.nə / (BrE)
a herbaceous plant with silvery-grey leaves, native to the east coast of North America and grown as an ornamental plant
ornamental herb with silvery-grey leaves

Examples

The Artemisia stelleriana in my garden thrives in dry, sandy soil and adds a silvery touch to the border.
Many landscapers choose Artemisia stelleriana for its drought tolerance and attractive foliage.
Known for its soft, felt-like leaves, Artemisia stelleriana is often planted along coastal areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dusty miller - common name for several plants with silvery-grey foliage, including Artemisia stelleriana

Etymology

From scientific Latin; named after the genus Artemisia and the botanist Georg Wilhelm Steller.
