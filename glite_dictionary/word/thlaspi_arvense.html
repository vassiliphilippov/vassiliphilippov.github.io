Thlaspi arvense

1. NOUN

/ ˌθlæspi ɑrˈvɛnsi / (AmE), / ˌθlaspi ɑːˈvɛnsiː / (BrE)
A foul-smelling Eurasian weed, also known as field pennycress, with round flat seed pods; widespread as a weed in North America.
weed with round flat pods (field pennycress)

Examples

Thlaspi arvense spreads easily in disturbed soils, often outcompeting native plants.
Farmers try to control Thlaspi arvense because it can become a serious agricultural weed.
The distinctive flat seed pods make Thlaspi arvense easy to identify in fields and gardens.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Thlaspi' (Greek) and the species 'arvense' (Latin 'of the field').
