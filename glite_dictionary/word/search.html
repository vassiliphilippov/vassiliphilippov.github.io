search

1. NOUN

/ sɝːtʃ / (AmE), / sɜːtʃ / (BrE)
an act or process of examining a collection of items to find one or more with a specific property, especially by means of a computer program
act of trying to find an item in a set, esp. computing

Examples

The new algorithm significantly improved the efficiency of the database search.
A binary search can quickly find an item in a sorted list.
He customized the program to perform a more advanced search for matching records.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lookup - the act of finding specific data in a table or database

Etymology

From Old French cerchier, from Latin circare (go about, wander), from circus (circle).
