Ctenophora

1. NOUN

/ təˈnɑfərə / (AmE), / tɛˈnɒfərə / (BrE)
a small phylum of marine animals known as comb jellies, characterized by eight rows of ciliary plates used for locomotion
phylum of marine comb jellies

Examples

Biologists discovered several new species within the phylum Ctenophora during the deep-sea expedition.
Members of Ctenophora are distinguished from jellyfish by their eight rows of comb-like cilia.
The unique iridescent movements of Ctenophora have fascinated marine researchers for decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comb jelly - common name for members of the phylum Ctenophora

Etymology

From Greek 'ktenos' (comb) + 'phoros' (bearing), referring to their comb-like cilia.
