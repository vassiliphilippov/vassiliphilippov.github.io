boldly

1. ADV

/ ˈboʊldli / (AmE), / ˈbəʊldli / (BrE)
in a confident and courageous way; without hesitation or fear
in a confident or courageous manner

Examples

She stepped boldly onto the stage to deliver her speech.
The hikers boldly ventured into the dense forest despite the warnings.
He boldly questioned the decision, surprising everyone in the meeting.

Synset Examples

we must tackle these tasks boldly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bravely - in a brave way, showing courage
  • Antonym: timidly - in a timid or shy manner

Etymology

From 'bold' + -ly, with 'bold' from Old English 'beald' (brave, confident).
