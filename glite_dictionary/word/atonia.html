atonia

1. NOUN

/ əˈtoʊniə / (AmE), / əˈtəʊnɪə / (BrE)
A lack of normal muscle tone or tension.
absence of normal muscle tone
medical

Examples

The patient exhibited atonia in his limbs after the surgery.
During REM sleep, atonia prevents most voluntary muscles from moving.
Atonic seizures are characterized by sudden atonia and loss of muscle strength.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flaccidity - the condition of being soft and limp; lack of firmness or muscle tone
  • Antonym: hypertonia - increased muscle tension or tone

Etymology

From Greek atonia, from a- (without) + tonos (tone).
