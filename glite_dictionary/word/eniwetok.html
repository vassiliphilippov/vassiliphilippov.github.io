Eniwetok

1. PROPN

/ ˌɛnɪˈwiːtɒk / (AmE), / ˌɛniːˈwiːtɒk / (BrE)
An atoll in the Marshall Islands, noted as the site of a major World War II battle where American forces captured a Japanese stronghold in February 1944.
atoll; WWII battle site in Marshall Islands

Examples

During World War II, American forces fought fiercely to capture Eniwetok from the Japanese.
The island of Eniwetok later became a site for nuclear testing after the war.
Historians often cite the battle of Eniwetok as a turning point in the Pacific campaign.

Etymology

From Marshallese 'Ānewetak', the indigenous name for the atoll.

2. PROPN

/ ˌɛniˈwiːtɑk / (AmE), / ˌɛniˈwiːtɒk / (BrE)
An atoll in the Marshall Islands, notable as a World War II battle site and a location for U.S. atomic bomb testing.
atoll in the Marshall Islands, WWII battle and nuclear test site

Examples

The United States conducted several atomic tests at Eniwetok in the 1940s and 1950s.
Historians have studied the fierce fighting that took place on Eniwetok during World War II.
Today, Eniwetok remains a remote atoll, visited mainly by researchers and former residents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bikini Atoll - another Marshall Islands atoll used for nuclear testing
  • Synonym: Kwajalein - another major atoll in the Marshall Islands, site of WWII battle
