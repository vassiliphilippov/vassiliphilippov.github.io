part-timer

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrt ˌtaɪmər / (AmE), / ˈpɑːt ˌtaɪmə / (BrE)
a person who works for only part of the usual working time, typically less than full time
person working less than full time

Examples

Many college students work as part-timers to support themselves while studying.
The company is hiring several part-timers for the busy holiday season.
As a part-timer, she only works three days a week.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: full-timer - a person who works the customary or standard full working hours

Etymology

Formed from 'part-time' + '-er', indicating a person who does something.
