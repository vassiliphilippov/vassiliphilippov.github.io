niobite

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪ.əˌbaɪt / (AmE), / ˈnaɪ.əˌbaɪt / (BrE)
A black mineral consisting primarily of niobium and tantalum oxides, serving as an ore for these elements.
black mineral ore of niobium and tantalum

Examples

The geologist identified the black rock as niobite during the mineral survey.
Extraction of niobium often involves processing niobite ores from mines in Canada.
Samples of niobite revealed traces of tantalum alongside the predominant niobium content.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: columbite - a mineral containing niobium and iron, similar to niobite

Etymology

From niobium, with suffix -ite used for minerals.
