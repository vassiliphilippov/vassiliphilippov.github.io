Hermann

1. PROPN

/ hɛrˈmɑːn / (AmE), / hɛˈmɑːn / (BrE)
A German chieftain who led the defeat of the Romans at the Battle of the Teutoburg Forest in AD 9.
German leader who defeated Romans in AD 9

Examples

Many historians credit Hermann with halting the Roman expansion into Germania.
The statue of Hermann in Detmold commemorates his victory over the Romans.
Hermann is often regarded as a national hero in Germany.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arminius - Another name for Hermann, leader of the Germanic tribes against Rome

Etymology

From the German personal name Hermann.
