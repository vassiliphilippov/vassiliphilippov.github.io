inexorable

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈɛksərəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈɛksərəbəl / (BrE)
impossible to stop, persuade, or change by any means; relentless and unyielding.
impossible to persuade or stop

Examples

The judge remained inexorable, refusing to reconsider the sentence despite emotional appeals.
Time marches forward with inexorable certainty, unaffected by human desires.
Her inexorable opposition made any compromise impossible.

Synset Examples

he is adamant in his refusal to change his mind
Cynthia was inexorable: she would have none of him
an intransigent conservative opposed to every liberal tendency

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relentless - showing or promising no abatement of severity, intensity, strength, or pace
  • Synonym: unyielding - not giving way under pressure; firm or inflexible
  • Antonym: flexible - willing to change or compromise
  • Antonym: yielding - giving way under pressure; not strict or harsh

Etymology

From Latin inexorabilis, from in- ('not') + exorabilis ('able to be entreated').

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈɛksərəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈɛksərəbəl / (BrE)
impossible to stop, persuade, or prevent; not able to be appeased or moved by request.
impossible to stop or persuade

Examples

The inexorable advance of new technology has changed every aspect of our lives.
Despite their pleas, the judge remained inexorable and refused to grant bail.
Time marches on with inexorable certainty, unaffected by human wishes.

Synset Examples

grim determination
grim necessity
Russia's final hour, it seemed, approached with inexorable certainty
relentless persecution
the stern demands of parenthood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relentless - never stopping or becoming less intense
  • Synonym: unforgiving - not allowing any kind of leniency or mercy
  • Antonym: merciful - showing mercy or compassion

Etymology

From Latin inexorabilis (not to be entreated), from in- (not) + exorabilis (capable of being entreated).
