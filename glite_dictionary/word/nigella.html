Nigella

1. NOUN

/ naɪˈdʒɛlə / (AmE), / nɪˈdʒɛlə / (BrE)
A genus of erect annual herbs native to Eurasia, including species grown for their ornamental flowers and aromatic seeds.
a genus of Eurasian annual herbs

Examples

The garden was filled with blooming Nigella plants, their feathery foliage adding a delicate touch.
Some cooks use the seeds of Nigella as a spice in traditional dishes.
Botanists studied several varieties within the Nigella genus for their medicinal properties.

Etymology

From Latin nigella, diminutive of niger (black), referencing the dark seeds.
