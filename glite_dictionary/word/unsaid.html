unsaid

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsɛd / (AmE), / ʌnˈsɛd / (BrE)
(of words or ideas) not spoken or expressed aloud; not explicitly stated.
not spoken or expressed aloud

Examples

There was a tension in the room, full of things left unsaid.
She appreciated his support, even if much remained unsaid between them.
The contract contained an unsaid understanding that both parties would act in good faith.

Synset Examples

the unexpressed terms of the agreement
things left unsaid
some kind of unspoken agreement
his action is clear but his reason remains unstated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unspoken - not spoken; not verbalized
  • Antonym: expressed - made explicit or stated clearly

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'said', past participle of 'say'.
