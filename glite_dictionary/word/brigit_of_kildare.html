Brigit of Kildare

1. PROPN

/ ˈbrɪdʒɪt əv kɪlˈdɛr / (AmE), / ˈbrɪdɪt əv kɪlˈdeə / (BrE)
A prominent Irish abbess and a patron saint of Ireland, believed to have lived from 453 to 523.
Irish abbess and patron saint

Examples

Many churches in Ireland are named after Brigit of Kildare in honor of her legacy.
Feast Day celebrations for Brigit of Kildare are held each year on February 1st.
Legends about Brigit of Kildare emphasize her generosity and leadership among early Christian communities.

Etymology

Brigit from Old Irish Brigit, and Kildare from Cill Dara, meaning 'church of the oak'.
