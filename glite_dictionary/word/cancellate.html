cancellate

1. ADJ

/ ˈkæn.səˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈkæn.sə.leɪt / (BrE)
having an open, latticed, or porous structure, often referring to a network of intersecting spaces, especially in biology or anatomy
having a porous, latticed structure

Examples

The sponge's skeleton has a distinctly cancellate structure, making it lightweight yet strong.
Under the microscope, the bone tissue appeared cancellate with numerous small spaces.
Certain shells display a beautifully cancellate surface, resembling delicate latticework.

Etymology

From Latin 'cancellatus', past participle of 'cancellare' (to make like a lattice), from 'cancelli' (lattice, grating).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkæn.sə.leɪt / (AmE), / ˈkan.sɪ.leɪt / (BrE)
having a latticelike structure with openings or spaces, as found in some biological tissues or materials
latticelike, porous structure

Examples

The cancellate structure of the bone makes it both strong and lightweight.
This sponge has a cancellate appearance with its intricate network of holes.
Researchers examined the cancellate patterns in the shell's surface under a microscope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porous - having small holes that allow air or liquid to pass through

Etymology

From Latin 'cancellatus' (having crossbars, latticed), from 'cancelli' (lattice).
