to jubilate

1. VERB

/ ˈdʒuːbɪˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːbɪleɪt / (BrE)
to feel or express great joy; to celebrate, especially in connection with a jubilee or significant occasion
express great joy or celebrate

Examples

The whole community jubilated at the news of liberation.
Fans jubilated after their team won the championship.
They will jubilate during the city's centennial jubilee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rejoice - to feel or show great joy or delight

Etymology

From Latin jubilare (to shout for joy).

2. VERB

/ ˈdʒuːbəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːbɪleɪt / (BrE)
to show or express great joy or delight; to rejoice greatly
express great joy; rejoice

Examples

Fans began to jubilate as their team scored the winning goal.
She could hardly contain herself and started to jubilate at the good news.
The whole village seemed to jubilate when the harvest was finally brought in.

Synset Examples

Who cannot exult in Spring?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rejoice - to feel or show great joy or delight
  • Antonym: lament - to express sorrow or regret

Etymology

From Latin jubilatus, past participle of jubilare (to shout for joy).
