schizophrenia

1. NOUN

/ skɪt͡səˈfriːniə / (AmE), / skɪt.səˈfriː.ni.ə / (BrE)
a serious mental disorder characterized by distortions of reality, disturbances in thought and language, and withdrawal from social interaction
a severe mental disorder
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed him with schizophrenia after several months of observation.
People living with schizophrenia may have difficulty distinguishing what is real from what is not.
Advances in medication have improved the quality of life for those affected by schizophrenia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychosis - a severe mental disorder with disconnection from reality

Etymology

From Greek 'schizo' (split) + 'phren' (mind).
