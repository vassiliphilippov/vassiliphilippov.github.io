zonula

1. NOUN

/ ˈzoʊ.njʊ.lə / (AmE), / ˈzɒn.jʊ.lə / (BrE)
A small beltlike zone or band, especially in biological tissues such as at cell junctions.
beltlike zone in biology
medical

Examples

The zonula occludens plays a crucial role in maintaining the integrity of epithelial layers.
Under the microscope, a distinct zonula appeared at the junction between the two cells.
Researchers studied the proteins associated with the zonula in order to understand its function.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zona - a beltlike or girdle-shaped region, especially in anatomy

Etymology

From Latin *zonula*, diminutive of *zona* (belt, girdle).
