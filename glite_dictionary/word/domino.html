domino

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑːmɪˌnoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdɒmɪnəʊ / (BrE)
A loose, hooded cloak with a half mask, worn as part of a masquerade costume, especially in the past.
cloak and mask for masquerade

Examples

At the ball, she wore a black domino that concealed her identity.
The mysterious figure in the domino slipped silently through the crowd.
In eighteenth-century Venice, a domino was a common choice for masquerade attire.

Etymology

From French domino, perhaps from Latin dominus (lord, master), referring to black cloaks worn by priests.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɑːməˌnoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdɒmɪnəʊ / (BrE)
a mask covering the upper part of the face, usually with holes for the eyes, often worn at masquerades
mask covering upper part of the face

Examples

Guests at the ball wore elaborate costumes and a domino to conceal their identities.
He lifted his domino to reveal a mischievous smile beneath the mask.
In many Venetian carnivals, the classic black domino is a popular choice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mask - a covering for all or part of the face

Etymology

From French 'domino', originally referring to a black hood worn by priests, later extended to the mask.

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈdoʊməˌnoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdɒmɪnəʊ / (BrE)
a small rectangular tile marked with zero to six dots on each of its two ends, used in playing the game of dominoes
rectangular gaming tile with dots

Examples

He picked up a domino and placed it at the end of the row.
The children were entertained by making a tower out of each domino.
Each domino in the set is marked with a unique combination of dots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dominoes - game played with domino tiles

Etymology

From French *domino* (black hood worn by priests), possibly referring to the appearance of the tiles.
