midnight

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪdˌnaɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɪdˌnaɪt / (BrE)
12 o'clock at night; the middle of the night.
12 o'clock at night

Examples

The clock struck midnight as the fireworks began.
She promised to be home before midnight.
The new law will take effect at midnight tonight.

Synset Examples

young children should not be allowed to stay up until midnight

Related Terms

  • Antonym: noon - 12 o'clock in the day; midday

Etymology

From Middle English 'midnight', from Old English 'midniht', literally 'middle night'.
