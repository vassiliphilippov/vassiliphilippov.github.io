Kabbalist

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæbəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈkæbəlɪst / (BrE)
A person who studies or practices the Jewish mystical tradition known as Kabbalah.
student or practitioner of Kabbalah

Examples

As a devoted Kabbalist, she spent years studying ancient mystical texts.
The Kabbalist explained the esoteric significance of Hebrew letters to his students.
Many modern Kabbalists seek to reconcile tradition with contemporary spiritual needs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Kabbalah - Jewish mystical tradition, teachings about the nature of God and creation

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'cabbalista', from Hebrew 'qabbālah' (tradition, received lore).
