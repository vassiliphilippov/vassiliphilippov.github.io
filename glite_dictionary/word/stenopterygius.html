Stenopterygius

1. NOUN

/ stɛˌnɑːp.təˈrɪdʒ.i.əs / (AmE), / stɛnˌɒp.təˈrɪdʒ.i.əs / (BrE)
A genus of extinct ichthyosaurs from the Jurassic period, known for their fish-like bodies.
genus of extinct ichthyosaurs

Examples

The fossils of Stenopterygius have been found in Jurassic marine deposits in Europe.
Scientific studies show that Stenopterygius had a streamlined body adapted for fast swimming.
Paleontologists often compare Stenopterygius to other ichthyosaurs to study evolutionary relationships.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Ichthyosauridae - family of large marine reptiles resembling fish

Etymology

From Greek 'stenos' (narrow) and 'pteryx' (fin or wing), referring to the narrow fins.

2. NOUN

/ stɛˌnɒptəˈrɪdʒiəs / (AmE), / stɛˌnɒptəˈrɪdʒɪəs / (BrE)
A genus of extinct marine reptiles (ichthyosaurs) that lived during the Early Jurassic period, characterized by a streamlined body and long, paddle-like limbs.
extinct ichthyosaur genus

Examples

The fossilized remains of Stenopterygius are among the best preserved of all ichthyosaurs.
Scientists believe that Stenopterygius gave birth to live young, similar to modern dolphins.
A complete skeleton of Stenopterygius was discovered in the limestone deposits of Germany.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ichthyosaur - extinct marine reptile resembling a dolphin

Etymology

From Greek 'stenos' (narrow) + 'pteryx' (fin or wing), referring to its slender fins.
