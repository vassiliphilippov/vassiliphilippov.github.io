turnround

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːn.raʊnd / (AmE), / ˈtɜːn.raʊnd / (BrE)
the act or process of unloading, loading, and servicing a vessel or aircraft to prepare it for a return trip
servicing for return of vessel or aircraft

Examples

The airport reduced turnround times to increase the number of daily flights.
Efficient turnround is essential for maintaining a tight schedule in shipping.
The airline improved their turnround procedures, minimizing delays for passengers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turnaround - the act or process of unloading, loading, and servicing a vessel or aircraft to prepare for return (American English spelling)

Etymology

From 'turn' + 'round', referring to turning a vehicle or vessel around for a return journey.
