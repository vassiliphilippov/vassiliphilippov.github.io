heathen

1. NOUN

/ ˈhiðən / (AmE), / ˈhiːð(ə)n / (BrE)
a person who does not belong to a widely held religion, especially someone who is not a Christian, Jew, or Muslim as regarded by those groups
person not belonging to a major religion

Examples

The missionaries tried to convert the local heathen population to Christianity.
He accused his neighbors of acting like heathens because they never went to church.
During the Middle Ages, many European texts referred to non-Christians as heathen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infidel - a person who does not believe in a particular religion
  • Antonym: believer - a person who believes in a particular religion or set of religious ideas

Etymology

From Old English hǣthen (not Christian or Jew), probably akin to heath (open country), referring to rural pagans.

2. ADJ

/ ˈhiðən / (AmE), / ˈhiːðən / (BrE)
(of a person or people) not acknowledging or believing in the God of Christianity, Judaism, or Islam; pagan.
not acknowledging Abrahamic religions
archaic

Examples

The missionaries sought to convert the heathen populations in foreign lands.
In medieval Europe, those who did not follow Christianity were often called heathen.
The tribe practiced rituals that were considered heathen by neighboring Christian communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pagan - not belonging to any major world religion, especially Christianity, Judaism, or Islam

Etymology

From Old English hǣthen (not Christian or Jewish), related to heath (open uncultivated land), possibly implying rural pagans.
