Naticidae

1. NOUN

/ nəˈtɪsɪˌdiː / (AmE), / nəˈtɪsɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of predatory sea snails known as moonshells, with smooth, rounded shells, found in marine environments worldwide
a family of predatory sea snails (moonshells)

Examples

The Naticidae are known for their smooth, rounded shells and predatory habits.
Many beachcombers collect the attractive shells of Naticidae found along the shoreline.
Marine biologists study Naticidae to understand their role in coastal ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moon snail - a common name for members of the Naticidae family, a predatory sea snail

Etymology

From Latin 'Natic-' (type genus Natica) + '-idae' (family suffix in zoological taxonomy).
