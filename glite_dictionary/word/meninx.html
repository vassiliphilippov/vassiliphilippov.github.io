meninx

1. NOUN

/ ˈmiːnɪŋks / (AmE), / ˈmiːnɪŋks / (BrE)
a membrane, one of the three, that covers and protects the brain and spinal cord
membrane around brain and spinal cord
medical

Examples

Inflammation of a meninx can result in serious neurological symptoms.
Each meninx serves to protect the brain from physical impacts and infection.
During surgery, special care is taken not to damage any meninx.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meninges - the three membranes covering the brain and spinal cord (plural of meninx)

Etymology

From Greek mēninx (membrane).
