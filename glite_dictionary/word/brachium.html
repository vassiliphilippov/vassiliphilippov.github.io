brachium

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪkiəm / (AmE), / ˈbrækɪəm / (BrE)
(biology) An armlike or branching part of an animal, such as the upper arm or a limb segment.
armlike part of an animal
medical

Examples

The scientist observed how the starfish extended its brachium to capture prey.
Damage to the brachium can affect the mobility of certain limbs.
Some cephalopods have a specialized brachium used for reproduction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arm - the upper limb of the human body

Etymology

From Latin 'bracchium' (arm).
