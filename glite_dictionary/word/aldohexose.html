aldohexose

1. NOUN

/ ˌæl.doʊˈhɛk.soʊs / (AmE), / ˌal.dəʊˈhɛk.səʊs / (BrE)
a monosaccharide sugar containing six carbon atoms and an aldehyde group
a six-carbon aldose sugar

Examples

Glucose is the most well-known aldohexose found in nature.
Researchers studied the reaction mechanisms of several aldohexose sugars.
The structural formula of an aldohexose shows an aldehyde group attached to the first carbon atom.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ketose - a monosaccharide with a ketone group
  • Synonym: hexose - a monosaccharide sugar with six carbon atoms

Etymology

From 'aldo-' (referring to an aldehyde group) and 'hexose' (a six-carbon sugar).
