Apterygidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌæptəˈrɪdʒɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˌæptəˈrɪdʒɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of flightless birds native to New Zealand, comprising the kiwis.
family of birds: kiwis

Examples

The Apterygidae are endemic to New Zealand and are best represented by the kiwi.
Scientists study the Apterygidae to understand the evolution of flightlessness in birds.
The conservation of Apterygidae species is a priority due to their unique ecological role.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kiwi - A flightless bird native to New Zealand
  • Synonym: Apterygiformes - Order of birds containing kiwis

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (without) + 'pterygion' (small wing), referring to their lack of wings, with the family-name suffix '-idae'.
