hardy

1. ADJ

/ ˈhɑːrdi / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdi / (BrE)
strong and able to endure difficult conditions; tough and resilient, especially physically.
strong and able to withstand hardship

Examples

The hardy explorers survived weeks in the Arctic without shelter.
Only hardy plants can thrive in such a harsh climate.
She admired the hardy determination of the mountain climbers.

Synset Examples

hardy explorers of northern Canada
proud of her tall stalwart son
stout seamen
sturdy young athletes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robust - strong and healthy; vigorous
  • Antonym: frail - weak and delicate; easily broken

Etymology

From Old French *hardi* (bold, daring), from *hardir* (to make hard), of Germanic origin.

2. ADJ

/ ˈhɑːrdi / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdi / (BrE)
able to withstand difficult or unfavorable conditions, especially weather or environment; strong and resilient
able to survive tough conditions

Examples

These winter vegetables are particularly hardy and can be left outside all season.
Only the most hardy adventurers attempt the climb during winter.
The hardy breed of sheep thrives in the mountainous region.

Synset Examples

strawberries are hardy and easy to grow
camels are tough and hardy creatures

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tough - strong enough to withstand adverse conditions or rough handling
  • Synonym: robust - strong and able to withstand hardship or adverse conditions
  • Antonym: delicate - easily damaged or susceptible to adverse conditions

Etymology

From Middle English hardi, from Old French hardi (bold, daring), from hardir (to make hard or bold).

3. ADJ

/ ˈhɑr.di / (AmE), / ˈhɑː.di / (BrE)
willing to take risks and undeterred by fear or intimidation; brave and courageous.
courageous; not easily frightened

Examples

The hardy volunteers braved the harsh weather to complete the rescue mission.
Despite the threats, she remained hardy and stood up for what she believed in.
Only the most hardy climbers attempt to reach the mountain's summit in winter.

Synset Examples

audacious explorers
fearless reporters and photographers
intrepid pioneers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intrepid - fearless; showing great courage
  • Antonym: timid - showing a lack of courage or confidence; easily frightened

Etymology

From Old French *hardi* (bold, daring), from Frankish *hardjan* (to make hard), from Proto-Germanic *harduz* (hard, strong).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
