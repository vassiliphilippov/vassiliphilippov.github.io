excisewoman

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈsaɪzˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ɪkˈsaɪzˌwʊmən / (BrE)
A woman employed by a government to collect excise taxes.
female tax collector (excise)

Examples

As the only excisewoman in the district, she was responsible for overseeing all alcohol-related taxes.
The government appointed its first excisewoman in the early twentieth century.
Stories about a fearless excisewoman circulated widely among the local smugglers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exciseman - a person (historically, usually a man) who collects excise taxes for the government

Etymology

Formed by analogy with 'exciseman': excise (tax) + woman.
