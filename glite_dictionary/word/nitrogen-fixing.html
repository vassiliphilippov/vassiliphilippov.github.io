nitrogen-fixing

1. ADJ

/ ˈnaɪtrə.dʒən ˌfɪksɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈnʌɪtrədʒ(ə)n ˌfɪksɪŋ / (BrE)
(of an organism or process) able to convert atmospheric nitrogen into a form usable by plants, typically through nitrogen fixation.
able to convert atmospheric nitrogen

Examples

Certain plants, such as beans and peas, are known as nitrogen-fixing because they help enrich the soil.
The introduction of nitrogen-fixing crops improved agricultural yields dramatically.
Legume roots often host nitrogen-fixing bacteria within specialized nodules.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nitrogen-fixing bacteria - bacteria that carry out nitrogen fixation

Etymology

From 'nitrogen' + 'fixing', referring to the process of nitrogen fixation.
