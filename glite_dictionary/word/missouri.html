Missouri

1. NOUN

/ məˈzʊri / (AmE), / məˈzʊəri / (BrE)
A member of the Native American people formerly inhabiting the valley of the Missouri River in what is now Missouri, United States.
member of a Native American people

Examples

Archaeologists have studied the ancient villages built by the Missouri in the river valley.
After European contact, many Missouri joined with the Otoe tribe for survival.
Descendants of the Missouri still preserve their traditions today.

Etymology

From the Missouri River, which is named from an Algonquian language meaning 'people with wooden canoes'.

3. PROPN

/ mɪˈzʊr.i, məˈzɜr.i / (AmE), / mɪˈzʊəri / (BrE)
a state in the central United States, located in the Midwest, known for the Missouri River and as a border state during the American Civil War
a state in the central United States

Examples

The Mississippi and Missouri Rivers meet near St. Louis, making the city a historic crossroads of America.
She moved from Missouri to California after finishing college.
Missouri is famous for its Gateway Arch and barbecue cuisine.

Etymology

Named after the Missouri River, which in turn derives from an Illinois language term meaning 'people with wooden canoes'.
