Gentiana villosa

1. NOUN

/ dʒɛnˈʃænə vɪˈloʊsə / (AmE), / dʒɛnˈʃɑːnɪə vɪˈləʊsə / (BrE)
A perennial species of marsh gentian native to eastern North America, with yellowish or pale blue flowers.
a marsh gentian plant of eastern North America

Examples

The rare Gentiana villosa can be found in wet meadows throughout the Appalachian region.
Botanists are studying the habitat requirements of Gentiana villosa to aid conservation efforts.
In autumn, the pale blooms of Gentiana villosa add a delicate beauty to marshy landscapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gentian - any plant of the genus Gentiana, typically with blue flowers

Etymology

From Latin: 'gentiana' (gentian) and 'villosa' (hairy).
