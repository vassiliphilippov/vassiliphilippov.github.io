to interlard

1. VERB

/ ˌɪntərˈlɑrd / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈlɑːd / (BrE)
to insert words, phrases, or elements into speech or writing, often in a way that interrupts or adds variety
to insert words into speech or writing

Examples

She likes to interlard her lectures with jokes to keep students engaged.
The report was interlarded with technical jargon that made it difficult for non-experts to follow.
Throughout his story, he would interlard personal anecdotes and witty remarks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to interpolate - to insert something, especially into a text or written work

Etymology

From French 'entrelarder', from 'entre-' (between) + 'lard' (bacon); originally to insert strips of fat into meat.
