echinococcus

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌkaɪnəˈkɑkəs / (AmE), / ˌɛkɪnəˈkɒkəs / (BrE)
a genus of tapeworms whose larvae are parasitic in humans and domestic animals, known for causing hydatid disease
a genus of parasitic tapeworms
medical

Examples

The liver cyst was caused by an echinococcus infection.
Farmers are at risk of contracting diseases from echinococcus larvae in livestock.
Doctors identified the presence of echinococcus during the ultrasound examination.

Etymology

From Greek ekhinos (hedgehog, sea urchin) + kokkos (berry, seed), referring to the appearance of the cysts.
