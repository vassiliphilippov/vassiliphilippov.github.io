Mahdist

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːdɪst / (AmE), / ˈmɑːdɪst / (BrE)
A follower of Mahdism, especially a supporter of the self-proclaimed Mahdi in Sudan in the late 19th century.
adherent of Mahdism

Examples

The British forces clashed with the Mahdists during the campaign in Sudan.
He studied the beliefs and history of the Mahdists of the late 19th century.
The Mahdist uprising had a significant impact on the region's future.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mahdism - the religious movement believing in the coming of the Mahdi

Etymology

From Arabic 'Mahdī' (the guided one), the prophesied redeemer in Islamic tradition, with English suffix '-ist'.
