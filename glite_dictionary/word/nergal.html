Nergal

1. PROPN

/ ˈnɜr ɡəl / (AmE), / ˈnɜː ɡəl / (BrE)
An Akkadian god who, together with his consort Ereshkigal, ruled over the world of the dead.
Akkadian god of the underworld

Examples

The legend describes how Nergal descended into the underworld to rule by Ereshkigal's side.
Priests in ancient Mesopotamia offered sacrifices to Nergal to appease his wrath.
In many myths, Nergal was associated with war, plague, and the dead.

Etymology

From Akkadian Nergal, a principal deity in Mesopotamian religion.
