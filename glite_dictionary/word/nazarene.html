Nazarene

1. ADJ

/ ˌnæzəˈrin / (AmE), / ˌnazəˈriːn / (BrE)
of or relating to the Nazarenes, an early Christian sect, or their religious beliefs and practices
relating to the Nazarenes or their religion

Examples

The Nazarene tradition emphasized strict adherence to Jewish law.
A Nazarene approach can be seen in the community’s practices.
This church follows Nazarene teachings in both belief and ritual.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nazarene - a member of the Nazarenes, a religious group

Etymology

From Latin Nazarenus, from Greek Nazōraios (of Nazareth), originally referring to Jesus or his followers.

3. NOUN

/ ˌnæzəˈrin / (AmE), / ˌnazəˈriːn / (BrE)
an early term used to refer to a Christian, especially in the context of the early followers of Jesus of Nazareth
early name for a Christian

Examples

In the earliest decades after Jesus’s death, supporters of his teachings were often called Nazarenes by others.
Some Roman officials distinguished between Jews and Nazarenes in their records of religious sects.
The Acts of the Apostles refers to Paul as a ringleader of the sect of the Nazarenes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Christian - a person who follows Christianity

Etymology

From Latin Nazarēnus, from Ancient Greek Ναζαρηνός (Nazarēnos), from Nazareth, the town associated with Jesus.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˌnæzəˈrin / (AmE), / ˌnazəˈriːn / (BrE)
a member of a sect of Jews in the early Christian Church who accepted Jesus as the Messiah, followed Jewish law, and were later regarded as heretics by the Church of Rome
early Christian Jewish sect member

Examples

Historians believe that the Nazarenes maintained most traditional Jewish customs while worshipping Jesus.
The early Nazarenes were distinct in rejecting the Epistles of St. Paul.
Some scholars argue that the beliefs of the Nazarenes influenced later Christian sects.

Etymology

From Nazareth, the town associated with Jesus, via Late Latin Nazarēnus and Greek Nazarēnos.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

5. NOUN

/ ˌnæzəˈriːn / (AmE), / ˌnæzəˈriːn / (BrE)
A native or inhabitant of Nazareth, an ancient town in Galilee, especially in historical or biblical contexts.
person from Nazareth

Examples

Many early Christians were referred to as Nazarene because they followed Jesus of Nazareth.
The village once known as Nazareth is the birthplace of the Nazarene.
He identified himself as a Nazarene in the historical documents.

Etymology

From Late Latin Nazarēnus, from Greek Nazarēnos, relating to Nazareth.
