cup

1. NOUN

/ kʌp / (AmE), / kʌp / (BrE)
A small open container, usually with a handle, used for drinking liquids.
small drinking container with handle

Examples

She sipped her tea slowly from her favorite cup.
The cup slipped from his hand and broke on the kitchen floor.
Could you please bring me a cup of water?

Synset Examples

he put the cup back in the saucer
the handle of the cup was missing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mug - a larger drinking vessel, often cylindrical and without a saucer
  • Synonym: glass - a container for drinking, typically made of glass and without a handle

Etymology

From Old English 'cuppe', from Late Latin 'cuppa'.

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ kʌp / (AmE), / kʌp / (BrE)
A unit of liquid measurement in the United States equal to 8 fluid ounces (approximately 237 milliliters).
US liquid measure (8 fl oz)

Examples

The recipe calls for one cup of milk.
She measured out two cups of water for the soup.
A standard cup in the US holds 8 fluid ounces.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: pint - a US liquid unit equal to 16 fluid ounces
  • Degree Lower: fluid ounce - a US or British unit of liquid volume (approximately 29.6 ml US, 28.4 ml UK)
