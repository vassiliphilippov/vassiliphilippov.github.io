broadloom

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɔːdˌlum / (AmE), / ˈbrɔːdˌluːm / (BrE)
A carpet woven on a wide loom, typically sold in large rolls and designed to cover a floor with minimal seams.
carpet woven on a wide loom

Examples

The entire living room was covered in plush broadloom.
They chose a neutral-colored broadloom carpet for the hotel suite.
Replacing the old area rugs with broadloom gave the office a seamless look.

Etymology

From 'broad' + 'loom', indicating a loom of wide width.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ ˈbrɔdˌlum / (AmE), / ˈbrɔːd.luːm / (BrE)
(of rugs or carpets) woven in a single, wide piece, usually intended for wall-to-wall installation rather than as individual rugs.
carpet woven in a wide piece

Examples

They decided to install broadloom in the living room for a seamless look.
The hotel corridors are covered with a plush, red broadloom carpet.
This store specializes in fitted broadloom as well as traditional area rugs.

Synset Examples

a broadloom rug

Etymology

Formed from 'broad' + 'loom', indicating a wide weaving loom.

WordNet ID: oewn-02262190-s
Synset POS: s
