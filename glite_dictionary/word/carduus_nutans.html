Carduus nutans

1. NOUN

/ karˈduː.əs ˈnuː.tænz / (AmE), / kɑːˈdjuː.əs ˈnjuː.tænz / (BrE)
a spiny Eurasian perennial plant with nodding musky crimson flower heads, naturalized in eastern North America, known as the nodding thistle
species of spiny Eurasian perennial plant (nodding thistle)

Examples

The field was filled with Carduus nutans in full bloom, attracting bees and butterflies.
Gardeners in North America sometimes try to control Carduus nutans because it spreads quickly in pastures.
You can identify Carduus nutans by its nodding crimson flower heads and spiny leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nodding thistle - common English name for Carduus nutans

Etymology

From Latin: 'carduus' meaning 'thistle' and 'nutans' meaning 'nodding'.
