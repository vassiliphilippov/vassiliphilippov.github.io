decongestant

1. NOUN

/ ˌdiːkənˈdʒɛstənt / (AmE), / ˌdiːkənˈdʒɛstənt / (BrE)
a drug or substance that relieves congestion, especially in the nasal passages or lungs
drug that relieves nasal or lung congestion
medical

Examples

You should take a decongestant to help clear your stuffy nose.
The pharmacist recommended an over-the-counter decongestant for my sinus congestion.
Many cold remedies contain a nasal decongestant to relieve symptoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antihistamine - drug used to counteract allergic reactions

Etymology

From de- (removal of) + congestant (that causes congestion).
