thermion

1. NOUN

/ ˈθɜːr.mi.ˌɑn / (AmE), / ˈθɜː.mi.ɒn / (BrE)
an electrically charged particle, such as an electron or ion, emitted from a substance when it is heated to a high temperature
charged particle emitted at high temperature

Examples

The heated filament in a vacuum tube releases thermions that enable current to flow.
At high temperatures, metals emit thermions due to increased energy of their electrons.
The study focused on how thermion emission is affected by the type of material used.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electron - a subatomic particle with a negative charge
  • Synonym: ion - an atom or molecule with an electric charge

Etymology

From Greek 'thermos' (hot) + 'ion' (going, ion).
