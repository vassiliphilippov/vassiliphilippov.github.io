irredentism

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪrəˈdɛntɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌɪrəˈdɛntɪzm̩ / (BrE)
The political principle or policy advocating the restoration to a country of any territory formerly belonging to it, typically based on ethnic or historical grounds.
policy of reclaiming lost territory

Examples

After the war, the rise of irredentism in the region led to numerous border disputes.
His policies were fueled by irredentism, demanding the return of lands lost decades before.
Debates over irredentism can threaten stability in areas with mixed populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irredentist - a person who supports the doctrine of irredentism

Etymology

From Italian 'irredentismo', from 'Italia irredenta' (unredeemed Italy).
