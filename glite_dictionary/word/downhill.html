downhill

1. ADJ

/ ˈdaʊnˌhɪl / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnˌhɪl / (BrE)
(of a slope or surface) sloping downwards, especially at a steep angle.
sloping downwards steeply

Examples

The downhill trail was much faster but required extra caution.
They built their house on a downhill street that flooded easily during rainstorms.
After climbing the hill, we enjoyed a quick downhill walk back to the village.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uphill - sloping upward, opposite of downhill
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊnˌhɪl / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnˌhɪl / (BrE)
a downward slope of a hill or road; the part of a hill that descends.
the downward slope of a hill

Examples

The skiers gathered at the top of the downhill before racing each other to the bottom.
After a long climb, the cyclists enjoyed coasting down the downhill.
Loose rocks made the downhill treacherous for hikers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ascent - an upward slope or climb

3. ADV

/ ˌdaʊnˈhɪl / (AmE), / ˌdaʊnˈhɪl / (BrE)
(of a situation or condition) towards a worse or less successful state.
towards a worse state

Examples

After his injury, his health started to go downhill.
The team's performance went downhill after the coach left.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

5. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊnˌhɪl / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnˌhɪl / (BrE)
A ski race in which competitors race down a slope, usually at high speed, following a set course.
high-speed ski race down a slope

Examples

He won the gold medal in the Olympic downhill last year.
The downhill is considered one of the most challenging events in alpine skiing.
She trained for months to improve her speed in the downhill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slalom - a ski race in which competitors zigzag through a course marked by flags or poles
