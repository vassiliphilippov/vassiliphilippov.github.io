estivation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛstəˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌiːstɪˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
A state of dormancy or greatly reduced activity in some animals during hot or dry periods, especially in summer.
dormancy in animals during summer heat
medical

Examples

Many snails enter estivation to survive the hot, dry climate of the desert.
During severe droughts, some amphibians rely on estivation to conserve water and energy.
The scientist explained how estivation helps certain reptiles survive extreme summer temperatures.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hibernation - state of inactivity during winter in animals

Etymology

From Latin aestivatio (spending the summer), from aestivare (to spend the summer), from aestivus (of summer).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛstəˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌiːstɪˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
(Botany) The arrangement of the sepals and petals in a flower bud before it opens.
botanical: arrangement of sepals and petals in a bud
medical

Examples

The estivation of the rose petals is easily observed in unopened buds.
Botanists study the pattern of estivation to classify different species of plants.
Spiral estivation can be seen in certain tropical flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vernation - the arrangement of young leaves in a bud

Etymology

From Latin aestīvātiō (summer pasturing), reinterpreted in botanical usage.
