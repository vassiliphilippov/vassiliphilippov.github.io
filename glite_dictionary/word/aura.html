aura

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔɹə / (AmE), / ˈɔːrə / (BrE)
a radiant light or luminous glow seen or depicted as surrounding the head or body of a holy figure, such as a saint, in religious art
radiant light shown around a saint

Examples

The painter depicted a golden aura encircling the saint's head.
In many icons, the aura signifies holiness and divinity.
A soft, glowing aura was illustrated to indicate the angel's purity.

Etymology

From Latin 'aura' (breeze, air), from Greek 'aura' (breeze, air).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔrə / (AmE), / ˈɔːrə / (BrE)
A sensation, such as a cold breeze or bright light, experienced before the onset of certain disorders, especially migraines or epileptic seizures.
preliminary sensation before migraine or seizure
medical

Examples

Some people experience a visual aura before the headache of a migraine begins.
She recognized the tingling aura as a warning of an approaching seizure.
Doctors advise patients to rest immediately if they notice their aura.

Etymology

From Latin 'aura' (breeze, air), from Greek 'aura'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɔrə / (AmE), / ˈɔːrə / (BrE)
A distinctive atmosphere or quality that seems to surround a person, place, or thing and is often intangible or subtle.
distinctive atmosphere or quality

Examples

The old library had an aura of mystery that captivated every visitor.
She carried herself with an aura of confidence that was contagious to everyone around her.
The painting gave the room an aura of elegance and tranquility.

Synset Examples

an air of mystery
the house had a neglected air
an atmosphere of defeat pervaded the candidate's headquarters
the place had an aura of romance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atmosphere - the pervading tone or mood of a place, situation, or creative work

Etymology

From Latin aura (breeze, air), from Greek aúra (breeze, air).
