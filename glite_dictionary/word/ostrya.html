Ostrya

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑstriə / (AmE), / ˈɒstriə / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous trees, known as hop-hornbeams, native to Europe, Asia, and America, with catkin flowers and hard wood.
genus of hop-hornbeam trees

Examples

The wood of the Ostrya is valued for its strength and resistance to wear.
Several species of Ostrya can be found in temperate forests of North America.
Gardeners often plant Ostrya for its attractive bark and catkins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hop-hornbeam - common name for trees in the genus Ostrya
  • Synonym: Carpinus - genus of hornbeam trees, closely related to Ostrya

Etymology

From Greek ostrua (hop-hornbeam), a kind of tree.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑstriə / (AmE), / ˈɒstriə / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous trees known as hop-hornbeams, native to Europe, Asia, and North America, sometimes classified in the birch family.
a genus of deciduous trees (hop-hornbeams)

Examples

The leaves of the Ostrya are finely serrated and resemble those of a birch tree.
Several species of Ostrya thrive in temperate forests across North America.
Botanists recently discovered a new variety within the Ostrya genus in China.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hop-hornbeam - common name for trees in the genus Ostrya

Etymology

From Ancient Greek ὀστρῦα (ostrya), meaning 'hop-hornbeam'.
