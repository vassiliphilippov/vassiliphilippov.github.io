de-Stalinisation

1. NOUN

/ di ˌstɑləˌnaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / di ˌstælɪnaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of eradicating the influence of Joseph Stalin by revising his policies, removing symbols and monuments, and renaming places associated with him.
removal of Stalin's influence in society

Examples

After the leader's death, a wave of de-Stalinisation swept through the country, changing many aspects of public life.
The removal of statues was a prominent feature of de-Stalinisation in Eastern Europe.
Historians often debate the extent to which de-Stalinisation achieved real political reform.

Synset Examples

his statue was demolished as part of destalinization

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rehabilitation - restoration of someone's reputation after political repression
  • Antonym: Stalinisation - process of adopting Stalin's policies and methods

Etymology

From prefix de- (removal) + Stalin + -isation, modelled after Russian десталинизация (destalinizatsiya).
