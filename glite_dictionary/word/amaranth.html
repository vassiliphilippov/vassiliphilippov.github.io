amaranth

1. NOUN

/ ˈæm.əˌrænθ / (AmE), / ˈæm.ərænθ / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Amaranthus, with dense clusters of small green or red flowers, often cultivated for its edible leaves and seeds
a plant with edible leaves and seeds

Examples

Ancient civilizations valued amaranth as a vital staple crop for its nutritious seeds.
The vibrant red flowers of the amaranth brightened the garden in late summer.
She added boiled amaranth leaves to the salad for extra flavor and color.

Etymology

From Greek 'amaranthos' meaning 'unfading flower.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈæm.əˌrænθ / (AmE), / ˈæm.ərænθ / (BrE)
The small seeds of certain amaranth plants, used as a grain or cereal, especially in Central and South America.
seeds of amaranth plant used as grain

Examples

Ancient civilizations such as the Aztecs cultivated amaranth as a staple food.
The chef added amaranth to the salad for an extra boost of protein.
Many people use amaranth flour as a gluten-free alternative in baking.

Etymology

From Greek amaranthos (unfading flower).
