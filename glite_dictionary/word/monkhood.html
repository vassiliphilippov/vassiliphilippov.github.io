monkhood

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌŋkˌhʊd / (AmE), / ˈmʌŋk.hʊd / (BrE)
The state or condition of being a monk, or monks collectively as a group.
the state or body of monks

Examples

The monkhood played a vital role in preserving ancient texts during the Middle Ages.
He aspired to join the monkhood after years of spiritual searching.
Throughout history, the monkhood has often provided education and charity to local communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clergy - the body of people ordained for religious duties

Etymology

Formed from 'monk' + '-hood,' an Old English noun-forming suffix indicating state or condition.
