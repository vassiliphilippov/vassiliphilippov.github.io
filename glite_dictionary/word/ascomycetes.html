Ascomycetes

1. NOUN

/ ˌæskəˈmaɪsitiːz / (AmE), / ˌæskəʊˈmaɪsiːtiːz / (BrE)
A large class of fungi, characterized by producing spores in sac-like structures called asci; also known as sac fungi.
class of sac fungi

Examples

Many species in the Ascomycetes are important decomposers in forest ecosystems.
Penicillium, a genus within the Ascomycetes, is known for producing the antibiotic penicillin.
Lichen symbioses often involve a fungal partner from the Ascomycetes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ascomycota - division of fungi containing the ascomycetes; sac fungi
  • Antonym: Basidiomycetes - a class of higher fungi with spores borne on basidia; includes mushrooms

Etymology

From Greek askos (sac) + -mycetes (fungi).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæskoʊˈmaɪsitiːz / (AmE), / ˌæskəʊˈmaɪsiːtiːz / (BrE)
a large class of higher fungi, known as sac fungi, which produce spores in special sac-like structures called asci; coextensive with the division Ascomycota
a large class of sac fungi

Examples

More than 75% of known fungi belong to the Ascomycetes, making it the largest fungal class.
Members of the Ascomycetes include yeasts, molds, and morels.
The scientist specialized in the study of the Ascomycetes due to their unique reproductive structures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ascomycota - division of sac fungi related to Ascomycetes; higher taxonomic rank
  • Antonym: Basidiomycetes - class of fungi characterized by producing spores on basidia

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'askos' (sac) and 'mykēs' (fungus).
