estradiol

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛstrəˈdaɪˌɒl / (AmE), / ˌɛstrəˈdaɪ.ɒl / (BrE)
the principal and most potent naturally occurring estrogen hormone in women, used medically to treat hormone deficiencies and certain cancers
potent female estrogen hormone
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed estradiol to help manage symptoms of menopause.
Researchers studied the effects of estradiol on bone density in postmenopausal women.
Some breast cancers are treated with therapies that lower estradiol levels in the body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: estrogen - a group of steroid hormones promoting female characteristics

Etymology

From estrane (parent structure of estrogens) + di- (two) + -ol (alcohol).
