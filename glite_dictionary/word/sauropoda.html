Sauropoda

1. NOUN

/ sɔːˈrɑːpədə / (AmE), / sɔːˈrɒpədə / (BrE)
A suborder of large, herbivorous dinosaurs with long necks, long tails, and pillar-like legs, including the largest land animals ever to live.
suborder of large long-necked dinosaurs

Examples

The Sauropoda are famous for their enormous size and distinctively long necks.
Many of the fossils found in this region belong to the Sauropoda suborder.
Researchers believe that Sauropoda roamed in herds across prehistoric landscapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sauropod - a member of the Sauropoda suborder

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining Greek saura (lizard) and pous, podos (foot).

2. NOUN

/ sɔːˈrɑːpədə / (AmE), / sɔːˈrɒpədə / (BrE)
a suborder of large, herbivorous, long-necked, quadrupedal dinosaurs including Brachiosaurus and Diplodocus, known for their enormous size
suborder of large, long-necked dinosaurs

Examples

The Sauropoda include some of the largest land animals to have ever lived.
Scientists estimate that members of the Sauropoda could reach lengths of over 30 meters.
Fossils of the Sauropoda have been discovered on every continent, revealing a diverse group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sauropod - a member of the Sauropoda suborder of dinosaurs

Etymology

From Greek sauros (lizard) + pous, pod- (foot), referring to lizard-footed form.
