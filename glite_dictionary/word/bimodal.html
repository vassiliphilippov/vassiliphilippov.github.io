bimodal

1. ADJ

/ baɪˈmoʊ.dəl / (AmE), / ˌbaɪˈməʊ.dəl / (BrE)
(Of a distribution, set, or phenomenon) having or characterized by two distinct modes or peaks.
having two modes; with two peaks

Examples

The data showed a bimodal distribution, with two distinct peaks in frequency.
Some natural phenomena exhibit bimodal patterns during certain times of the year.
If a histogram is bimodal, it suggests there may be two underlying groups in the data.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unimodal - having only one mode or peak

Etymology

From bi- (meaning 'two') + modal (relating to mode).
