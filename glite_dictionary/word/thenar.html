thenar

1. NOUN

/ ˈθiːnɑr / (AmE), / ˈθiːnɑː / (BrE)
the inner surface of the hand between the wrist and the base of the fingers, especially the fleshy mound at the base of the thumb
fleshy part of palm near thumb
medical

Examples

The surgeon examined the patient's thenar for signs of nerve damage.
After the injury, swelling appeared on the thenar area of his right hand.
Weakness in the thenar muscles may indicate carpal tunnel syndrome.

Etymology

From Greek thenar (palm of the hand, sole of the foot).
