unsynchronous

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsɪŋkrənəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈsɪŋkrənəs / (BrE)
Not occurring, happening, or existing at the same time; not simultaneous.
not occurring at the same time

Examples

The two machines operated in an unsynchronous manner, causing delays in production.
An unsynchronous event can lead to coordination problems in distributed systems.
Their unsynchronous conversation made it difficult to follow the discussion.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: synchronous - occurring at the same time
  • Synonym: asynchronous - not occurring at the same time (used more commonly)

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) and 'synchronous' (occurring at the same time), from Greek 'syn-' (together) and 'chronos' (time).
