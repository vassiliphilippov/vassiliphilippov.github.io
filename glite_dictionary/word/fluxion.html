fluxion

1. NOUN

/ ˈflʌk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈflʌk.ʃən / (BrE)
a flow, discharge, or movement of fluid, often used in scientific or historical contexts
a flow or discharge
archaic

Examples

The scientist observed the fluxion of water from the broken pipe.
In ancient medical texts, fluxion referred to any abnormal discharge from the body.
The term fluxion is now rarely used outside of historical or specialized scientific contexts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flow - the action or fact of moving along in a steady, continuous stream
  • Synonym: discharge - the action of releasing or sending out

Etymology

From Latin 'fluxio' (a flowing), from 'fluere' (to flow).
