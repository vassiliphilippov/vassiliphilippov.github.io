Achaian

1. NOUN

/ əˈkeɪən / (AmE), / əˈkeɪi.ən / (BrE)
a member of an ancient Greek people who formed one of the four main linguistic divisions of the Greeks, living on the Greek mainland, Crete, and Cyprus during the 16th to 12th centuries BC
member of an ancient Greek people

Examples

The Achaian kings played a central role in Homeric legends and Greek mythology.
Archaeologists have discovered several Achaian settlements on the Greek mainland.
Scholars debate the influence of the Achaian dialect on later forms of Greek.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mycenaean - member of another prehistoric Greek group

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἀχαιοί (Akhaioí), referring to a mythic and historical people of Greece.
