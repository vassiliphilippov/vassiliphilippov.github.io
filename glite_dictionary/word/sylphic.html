sylphic

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɪlfɪk / (AmE), / ˈsɪlfɪk / (BrE)
(of a woman or girl) slender and graceful in a way that suggests a sylph; delicately attractive and light in movement.
slender and graceful (like a sylph)

Examples

The ballet dancer was admired for her sylphic beauty and lightness on stage.
With her sylphic figure, she seemed to float across the crowded room.
The artist captured a sylphic elegance in the graceful posture of his subject.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sylphlike - resembling a sylph; slender and graceful

Etymology

From sylph (a slender, graceful being in folklore) + -ic (forming adjectives).
