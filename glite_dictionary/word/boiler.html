boiler

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔɪlər / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪlə / (BrE)
a sealed vessel in which water is heated and converted to steam, typically for heating or power generation
sealed vessel for producing steam

Examples

The old boiler in the basement supplies steam to the radiators throughout the house.
A broken boiler can leave an entire building without heat during winter.
Engineers inspected the boiler regularly to ensure it operated safely and efficiently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steam generator - device that produces steam for power or heating

Etymology

From 'boil' + '-er', denoting a device that boils something.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɔɪlər / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪlə / (BrE)
a metal container or pot with a lid used for stewing or boiling food
metal pot for boiling or stewing

Examples

She lifted the heavy boiler off the stove and poured out the stew.
Every Sunday, he washed the laundry in a large boiler in the backyard.
The recipe called for all the ingredients to be simmered together in a boiler for two hours.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pot - a container, typically round, used for cooking
  • Synonym: kettle - a container for boiling liquids, especially water

Etymology

From English 'boil' + '-er', denoting an object that boils.
