Pallas Athene

1. PROPN

/ ˈpæləs əˈθini / (AmE), / ˈpæləs æˈθiːni / (BrE)
in Greek mythology, the goddess of wisdom, crafts, and strategic warfare, and protector of Athens, equivalent to the Roman Minerva
Greek goddess of wisdom and war

Examples

The Parthenon in Athens was devoted to Pallas Athene, the city's guardian deity.
In classical artwork, Pallas Athene is often depicted wearing a helmet and holding a shield.
Many philosophers regarded Pallas Athene as a symbol of wisdom and rational thought.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Minerva - Roman goddess of wisdom, equivalent to Pallas Athene

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Παλλὰς Ἀθηνᾶ (Pallás Athēnâ), an epithet for Athena.
