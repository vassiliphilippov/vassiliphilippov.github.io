largess

1. NOUN

/ lɑrˈʤɛs / (AmE), / lɑːˈdʒɛs / (BrE)
generous gifts or money given to others, often in a showy manner
generous or showy giving of gifts or money

Examples

The billionaire was known for his largess, donating millions to charity every year.
After the mayor’s largess during the festival, many citizens applauded his generosity.
She distributed her largess among the staff, rewarding them for their hard work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: generosity - quality of being kind and generous
  • Antonym: stinginess - reluctance to give or spend; meanness

Etymology

From Old French 'largece', from 'large' (generous), from Latin 'largus' (abundant).

2. NOUN

/ lɑrˈdʒɛs / (AmE), / lɑːˈdʒɛs / (BrE)
generous giving of money or gifts, especially to an inferior or to a group; generosity of spirit or liberality in bestowing gifts.
generosity in giving gifts or money

Examples

The billionaire was admired for his largess in supporting educational causes across the country.
Every holiday season, her largess brought joy to the children in the neighborhood.
The museum was built thanks to the largess of a local businessman.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: generosity - willingness to give money, help, or time freely
  • Antonym: stinginess - unwillingness to spend money or give gifts

Etymology

From Old French 'largece', from 'large' (generous), ultimately from Latin 'largus' (abundant, plentiful).
