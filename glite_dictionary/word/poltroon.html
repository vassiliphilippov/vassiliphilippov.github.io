poltroon

1. NOUN

/ pɑlˈtrun / (AmE), / pɒlˈtruːn / (BrE)
a person who is extremely cowardly; an abject coward
an extreme coward

Examples

The commander called him a poltroon for fleeing the battlefield.
She refused to be labeled a poltroon despite her fear of public speaking.
Years of bullying had turned him into a secret poltroon who avoided all conflict.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coward - someone who lacks courage
  • Antonym: hero - brave or courageous person

Etymology

From French 'poltron' and Italian 'poltrone' (coward).

2. ADJ

/ pɑlˈtrun / (AmE), / pɒlˈtruːn / (BrE)
(literary) Utterly cowardly; showing complete lack of courage.
completely cowardly (literary)

Examples

The general's poltroon behavior shocked his soldiers.
He gave a poltroon response instead of standing up for what was right.
Such poltroon actions have no place in our organization.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowardly - lacking courage; easily frightened
  • Antonym: brave - showing courage; not afraid

Etymology

From French *poltron* (coward), from Italian *poltrone* (lazy person).
