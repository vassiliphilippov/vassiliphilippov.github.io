cautionary

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɔʃəˌnɛri / (AmE), / ˈkɔːʃənəri / (BrE)
Serving as a warning or intended to warn or advise against danger or undesirable outcomes.
serving as a warning

Examples

The film ended with a cautionary message about the dangers of texting while driving.
Her failure served as a cautionary tale for her classmates.
The teacher gave a cautionary lecture about the consequences of plagiarism.

Synset Examples

shook a monitory finger at him
an exemplary jail sentence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advisory - giving advice or a warning; cautionary
  • Synonym: exhortatory - acting as encouragement or urging; providing warning or guidance

Etymology

From caution + -ary, modeled on Latin 'cautio' (a taking care, a caution).

2. ADJ

/ ˈkɔʃəˌneri / (AmE), / ˈkɔːʃənəri / (BrE)
Serving to ward off danger or evil; giving protection by warning.
serving to warn against or prevent harm

Examples

The old tales acted as cautionary legends to keep children from wandering into the woods.
A cautionary symbol was placed on the fence to deter trespassers from entering the property.
Some ancient charms were believed to have cautionary powers, protecting families from misfortune.

Synset Examples

the swastika … a very ancient prophylactic symbol occurring among all peoples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prophylactic - serving to prevent or ward off disease or danger
  • Synonym: warning - giving notice of danger

Etymology

From caution + -ary, modeled on similar Latin-derived adjectives.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01893737-s
Synset POS: s
