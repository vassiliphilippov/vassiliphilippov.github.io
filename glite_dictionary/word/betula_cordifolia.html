Betula cordifolia

1. NOUN

/ bəˈtjuːlə kɔrˈdɪfoʊliə / (AmE), / bɛˈtjuːlə kɔːˈdɪfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
a small North American birch tree with peeling white bark, often used for making baskets or toy canoes
a birch tree species with white peeling bark

Examples

The white bark of the Betula cordifolia peels easily, making it ideal for traditional crafts.
Many indigenous people used Betula cordifolia bark to weave baskets and fashion small canoes.
The forest was dotted with graceful Betula cordifolia trees shining in the sunlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: birch - a tree of the genus Betula

Etymology

From Latin, genus name 'Betula' (birch) and 'cordifolia' (heart-leaved).
