jaw

1. VERB

/ dʒɔ / (AmE), / dʒɔː / (BrE)
to talk at length in a tedious or tiresome way, especially when scolding or lecturing
talk tediously or at length

Examples

My grandmother would jaw for hours about the old days.
He jawed at me endlessly after I broke the vase.
They sat around jawing about politics well into the night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lecture - to give a long talk or scold someone tediously
  • Synonym: harangue - to speak to someone forcefully and tediously
  • Antonym: to be quiet - to stop talking or speaking

2. NOUN

/ dʒɑ / (AmE), / dʒɔː / (BrE)
either of the two parts of a tool or device that move together to grip, hold, or clamp an object firmly
a part of a tool for gripping objects

Examples

The machinist adjusted the jaw of the vise to secure the metal rod.
Be careful not to pinch your fingers between the jaws of the pliers.
The jaw of the clamp closed tightly around the wooden board.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clamp - a tool for fastening or holding things together tightly
  • Synonym: vise - a mechanical screw apparatus used for holding or clamping a workpiece

3. NOUN

/ dʒɑ / (AmE), / dʒɔː / (BrE)
either of the two bony structures in the head that frame the mouth and hold the teeth, especially the lower movable part
bony structure that frames the mouth

Examples

He broke his jaw during the football match and needed surgery.
The dentist examined her upper and lower jaw for any signs of infection.
Snakes can unhinge their jaw to swallow prey much larger than their heads.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mandible - the lower jawbone, especially in vertebrates
  • Synonym: maxilla - the upper jawbone in vertebrates

Etymology

From Old French 'chau' (jaw), of uncertain origin.
