leishmaniosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌliːʃˌmæniˈoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌliːʃˌmænɪˈəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A disease caused by infection with protozoa of the genus Leishmania, transmitted by sandflies, resulting in sores on the skin and sometimes affecting internal organs.
disease caused by Leishmania protozoa
medical

Examples

Doctors in the region are studying cases of leishmaniosis brought by travelers returning from tropical areas.
A sandfly bite can transmit the parasite responsible for leishmaniosis, making prevention essential in endemic zones.
The patient was diagnosed with cutaneous leishmaniosis after developing persistent skin sores.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leishmaniasis - disease caused by Leishmania protozoa; alternative spelling and term

Etymology

From genus name Leishmania, named after Scottish pathologist William B. Leishman, plus the medical suffix -osis (denoting a disease).
