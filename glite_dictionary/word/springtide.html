springtide

1. NOUN

/ ˈsprɪŋ.taɪd / (AmE), / ˈsprɪŋ.taɪd / (BrE)
A swelling or rising surge of a particular quality, event, or phenomenon, especially used figuratively.
a swelling or rising surge (figurative)

Examples

His optimism soared on the springtide of recent successes.
The revolution began amid a springtide of hope and fervor.
She was carried away by a springtide of emotion after winning the award.

Synset Examples

he rose on the springtide of prosperity

Etymology

Formed from 'spring' (to rise) + 'tide' (period or surge), originally referring to the rising high tide.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsprɪŋ.taɪd / (AmE), / ˈsprɪŋ.taɪd / (BrE)
a tide in which the difference between high and low water is greatest, occurring at the new and full moons
tide with greatest difference; occurs at new/full moon

Examples

During a springtide, coastal flooding is more likely because of the unusually high water levels.
Fishermen often plan their trips around the springtide for optimal catches.
The difference between high and low water is most pronounced at springtide each month.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: neap tide - a tide with the least difference between high and low water

Etymology

From 'spring' (to rise) + 'tide' (rise and fall of the sea).
