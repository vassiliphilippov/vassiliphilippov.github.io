overprint

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊ.vər.prɪnt / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.və.prɪnt / (BrE)
an additional mark, text, or design printed on top of something that has already been printed, especially on postage stamps or currency.
something added by printing over an existing print

Examples

The stamp featured an overprint that indicated its new value.
Collectors highly prize rare overprints created during historical events.
The overprint on the old currency was clear and easy to read.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surcharge - an additional mark or charge added, especially on stamps

Etymology

From over- (above, on top of) + print, referring to printing over an existing print.

WordNet ID: oewn-03871382-n

2. VERB

/ ˌoʊ.vɚˈprɪnt / (AmE), / ˌəʊ.vəˈprɪnt / (BrE)
to print additional text, images, or colors onto material that has already been printed upon
print over already printed material

Examples

The printer will overprint the new date on all unsold tickets.
They decided to overprint the product with a warning label after the recall.
You should not overprint dark colors over light backgrounds, as the details may be lost.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: print - to produce text or images on paper by pressing ink through a machine

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' (above, in addition) + 'print'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01752081-v
Synset POS: v
