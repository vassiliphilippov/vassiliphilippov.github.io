Triplochiton scleroxylon

1. NOUN

/ trɪp.ləˈkaɪ.tɑn sklɛ.rɒkˈsaɪ.lɒn / (AmE), / trɪp.ləˈkaɪ.tɒn sklɛ.rɒkˈsaɪ.lən / (BrE)
A large West African tree with palmately lobed leaves, small white flowers in cymose clusters, and wood that is soft, white to pale yellow; commonly known as obeche and used in timber production.
West African timber tree (obeche)

Examples

The wood of the Triplochiton scleroxylon tree is highly valued for its light weight and workability.
Many West African forests are home to the Triplochiton scleroxylon, also known as the obeche tree.
Panels and veneers made from Triplochiton scleroxylon are commonly used in furniture and interior design.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obeche - Another name for Triplochiton scleroxylon

Etymology

From Greek roots: 'triploos' (threefold), 'chiton' (tunic), and 'skleros' (hard) + 'xylon' (wood).
