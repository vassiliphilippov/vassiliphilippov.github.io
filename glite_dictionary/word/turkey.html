2. NOUN

/ ˈtɜr.ki / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.ki / (BrE)
a large bird native to North America, with a fan-shaped tail, widely domesticated for its meat.
large domesticated bird for food

Examples

We roasted a turkey for Thanksgiving dinner and invited the whole family.
The farm raises turkeys and sells them to local markets every autumn.
A wild turkey strutted across the backyard early in the morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chicken - a common domestic fowl kept for its eggs and meat
  • Synonym: goose - a large waterbird closely related to the duck and swan

Etymology

From Turkish 'tavuk' (chicken) via early mistaken association with the country Turkey.

3. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːki / (AmE), / ˈtɜːki / (BrE)
the flesh of a large domesticated bird, often roasted and eaten as food
meat of a domesticated bird

Examples

We had roast turkey for Thanksgiving dinner this year.
She prefers turkey sandwiches to those made with ham or beef.
A slice of turkey with cranberry sauce is a holiday tradition in our family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chicken - flesh of a domesticated bird used as food
  • Synonym: goose - flesh of a domesticated waterfowl used as food
  • Synonym: duck - flesh of a domesticated duck used as food

Etymology

From the country name 'Turkey', via mistaken belief that the guinea fowl, and later the turkey, originated from Turkey.
