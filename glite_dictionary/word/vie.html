to vie

1. VERB

/ vaɪ / (AmE), / vaɪ / (BrE)
to compete eagerly with someone in order to achieve or win something
compete to achieve or win

Examples

Several teams will vie for the championship title this year.
The candidates are vying for attention in the crowded marketplace.
Writers vied with one another for the prestigious award.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to compete - to strive to gain or win something by defeating or establishing superiority over others
  • Antonym: to cooperate - to work jointly towards the same end

Etymology

From Old French 'envier' (to compete), based on Latin 'invitare' (to invite, challenge).
