mass

1. NOUN

/ mæs / (AmE), / mæs / (BrE)
A body of matter, often large and without a definite shape.
body of matter without definite shape

Examples

A dense mass of fog rolled over the city.
The avalanche left a huge mass of snow at the foot of the mountain.
She pointed to the mass of tangled wires behind the TV.

Synset Examples

a huge ice mass

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lump - a compact mass of a substance
  • Synonym: bulk - the mass or magnitude of something large

Etymology

From Latin massa (lump, dough), from Greek maza (barley cake).

5. NOUN

/ mæs / (AmE), / mæs / (BrE)
the property of a physical body that gives it weight in a gravitational field and resistance to acceleration.
property of matter, causes weight

Examples

The mass of an object determines how much force is needed to move it.
Physicists carefully measured the mass of each sample in the laboratory.
Despite being the same size, lead has a greater mass than wood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weight - the force with which a body is attracted towards the center of the earth
  • Degree Lower: volume - the amount of space that a substance or object occupies

Etymology

From Latin massa (lump, bulk).
