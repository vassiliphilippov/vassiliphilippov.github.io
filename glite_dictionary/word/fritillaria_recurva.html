Fritillaria recurva

1. NOUN

/ frɪˌtɪləriə rɪˈkɝːvə / (AmE), / frɪˌtɪləˈrɪə rɪˈkɜːvə / (BrE)
A species of lily native to the western United States, with scarlet and yellow, narrow, bell-shaped flowers.
species of lily with bell-shaped scarlet flowers

Examples

The slopes were dotted with blooming Fritillaria recurva, their red bells nodding in the breeze.
Botanists traveled to observe Fritillaria recurva in its native habitat of California woodlands.
Gardeners admire Fritillaria recurva for its striking scarlet and yellow flowers.

Etymology

From Latin: 'fritillaria', a genus of lilies, and 'recurva', meaning 'curved back', referring to the shape of the flower petals.
