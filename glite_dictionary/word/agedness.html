agedness

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪdʒɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈeɪdʒɪdnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being old; the characteristic of old age
state or quality of being old

Examples

The agedness of the building was apparent in its crumbling stone walls.
As the population increases in agedness, society must adapt to new needs.
Her agedness did not stop her from leading an active and fulfilling life.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: youthfulness - the quality or state of being young

Etymology

From 'aged' (having lived for many years) + '-ness' (state or quality).
