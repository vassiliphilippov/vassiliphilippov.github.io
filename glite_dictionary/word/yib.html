YiB

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪ.biː / (AmE), / ˈwiː.biː / (BrE)
a unit of digital information equal to 1,024 zebibytes or 2^80 bytes (1,208,925,819,614,629,174,706,176 bytes)
unit of digital information (2^80 bytes)

Examples

The storage array has a maximum capacity of one YiB, which is unusually large for current technology.
Researchers discussed the theoretical use of a YiB in future data centers.
Most consumer devices cannot handle data sizes anywhere near a single YiB.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yobibyte - a unit of digital information equal to 2^80 bytes
  • Degree Lower: zebibyte - a unit of digital information equal to 2^70 bytes

Etymology

Acronym formed from 'yobi-' (from 'yotta binary') and 'byte'.
