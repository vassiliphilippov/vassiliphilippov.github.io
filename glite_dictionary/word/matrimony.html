matrimony

1. NOUN

/ ˈmætrəˌmoʊni / (AmE), / ˈmat.rɪ.mə.ni / (BrE)
the state of being married; marriage, especially as a formal or religious institution
the state of being married

Examples

They celebrated their matrimony with a beautiful church ceremony.
Entering into matrimony is a significant decision for many people.
They have enjoyed thirty years of happy matrimony.

Synset Examples

a long and happy marriage
God bless this union

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marriage - the legal or formal union of two people as partners in a relationship
  • Antonym: divorce - the legal dissolution of a marriage

Etymology

From Latin matrimonium (marriage), from mater (mother).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmætrəˌmoʊni / (AmE), / ˈmætrɪməni / (BrE)
the formal ceremony or sacrament in which two people are joined in marriage, especially in a religious context
marriage ceremony or sacrament

Examples

The couple exchanged vows during the sacred matrimony at the cathedral.
She believes that matrimony is a sacred bond that should be respected by all.
They invited all their friends and family to witness their matrimony last summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marriage - the state of being married; the relationship between spouses

Etymology

From Latin matrimonium (marriage, wedlock), from mater (mother).
