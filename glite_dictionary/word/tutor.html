tutor

1. NOUN

/ ˈtuːtər / (AmE), / ˈtjuːtə / (BrE)
a person who teaches one student or a small group privately, especially for a specific subject or skill
private teacher or instructor

Examples

After school, a tutor helped James improve his mathematics skills.
She hired a tutor to prepare for her piano examination.
As a language tutor, he gives private lessons to international students.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teacher - a person who teaches, especially in a school
  • Antonym: student - a person who is being taught, especially by a tutor or teacher

Etymology

From Latin 'tutor' (guardian, watcher), from 'tuērī' (to look after, protect).

2. VERB

/ ˈtuːtər / (AmE), / ˈtjuːtə / (BrE)
to act as a legal guardian of a child or minor, especially supervising their welfare and interests
to act as a legal guardian

Examples

Under the law, his uncle was appointed to tutor him until he reached adulthood.
After her parents died, a relative was chosen to tutor the young heiress and oversee her estate.
The court may tutor a minor to ensure his or her rights are protected.

Etymology

From Latin tutor (guardian, protector), from tueri (to watch, protect).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02598823-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈtuːtər / (AmE), / ˈtjuːtə / (BrE)
to teach or give individual instruction to someone, especially as a private teacher
give individual instruction or teach privately

Examples

She tutored me in Spanish every weekend before the exams.
He has tutored high school students in mathematics for several years.
This semester I am tutoring three children who need help with reading.

Synset Examples

She tutored me in Spanish

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to instruct - to teach or educate someone
  • Synonym: coach - to train or tutor a person in a subject or skill
