Austrocedrus

1. NOUN

/ ɔːstroʊˈsiːdrəs / (AmE), / ɒstrəʊˈsiːdrəs / (BrE)
a genus of evergreen coniferous tree in the cypress family, native to South America, comprising one species formerly classified under Libocedrus.
genus of South American coniferous tree

Examples

The forests of Patagonia are home to the rare Austrocedrus tree.
Researchers studied the growth rings of Austrocedrus to analyze climate changes.
Austrocedrus populations have declined due to disease and logging.

Etymology

From Latin 'auster' (south) and 'cedrus' (cedar), indicating a southern cedar.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɔːstroʊˈsiːdrəs / (AmE), / ˌɒstrəʊˈsiːdrəs / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen coniferous trees in the cypress family, native to southern South America, now containing a single species.
genus of evergreen coniferous trees

Examples

Researchers discovered a rare stand of Austrocedrus in the Patagonian Andes.
The Austrocedrus is often called the Chilean cedar, though it is not a true cedar.
Dendrochronologists study Austrocedrus trees to understand historical climate patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Libocedrus - a related genus of coniferous trees formerly including Austrocedrus

Etymology

From Greek 'austro-' (south) and Latin 'cedrus' (cedar).
