bilinear

1. ADJ

/ baɪˈlɪn.i.ɚ / (AmE), / baɪˈlɪn.i.ə / (BrE)
(of a function, form, or mapping) linear with respect to each of two variables or arguments when the other is held constant.
linear in two variables or arguments

Examples

A bilinear transformation is used to map one plane onto another in mathematics.
The system is modeled using a bilinear equation involving both variables.
Bilinear interpolation is commonly applied in image processing for rescaling pixels.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: linear - involving a single variable without exponents or powers greater than one

Etymology

From Latin 'bi-' (two) + 'linear' (from Latin 'linearis', meaning 'of a line').
