Rhincodontidae

1. NOUN

/ raɪnˌkoʊdɛnˈtɪdiː / (AmE), / rɪŋˌkɒdɒnˈtɪdɪiː / (BrE)
A family of sharks consisting of only one extant species, the whale shark, known for having small teeth.
family of sharks (whale shark)

Examples

The Rhincodontidae family is represented today only by the whale shark.
Researchers study Rhincodontidae fossils to learn about prehistoric sharks.
The Rhincodontidae are known for their distinctive small teeth and massive size.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whale shark - large filter-feeding shark, only extant species in Rhincodontidae

Etymology

From New Latin Rhincodon (the genus name) + -idae (a zoological family suffix).
