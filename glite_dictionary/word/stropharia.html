Stropharia

1. NOUN

/ stroʊˈfɛəriə / (AmE), / strɒˈfɛəriə / (BrE)
A genus of gilled fungi with brown spores, closely related to Agaricus and classified in the family Strophariaceae.
a genus of gilled fungi

Examples

The vibrant cap of the Stropharia mushroom makes it popular among foragers and mycologists.
Several species within the Stropharia genus are commonly found in wooded areas throughout the spring.
The biologist identified the mushroom as a member of the Stropharia genus due to its distinctive ring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Agaricus - a related genus of mushrooms

Etymology

From Greek 'strophos' (a band or twisted cord), referring to the ring on the mushroom's stem.
