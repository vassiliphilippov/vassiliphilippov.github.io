YHVH

1. NOUN

/ jiː eɪtʃ viː eɪtʃ / (AmE), / wiː eɪtʃ viː eɪtʃ viː eɪtʃ / (BrE)
A name for God in the Old Testament, rendered from the Hebrew consonants YHVH.
Hebrew name for God in the Old Testament

Examples

The sacred name YHVH appears many times in the scriptures.
Scholars debate the correct pronunciation of YHVH due to the absence of vowels in ancient Hebrew.
Some translations of the Bible replace YHVH with 'the LORD' for reverence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yahweh - Alternative transliteration of the Hebrew name for God (יהוה)
  • Synonym: Jehovah - Latinized form of the Hebrew name for God (יהוה)

Etymology

Transliteration of the Hebrew Tetragrammaton (יהוה), traditionally rendered as YHWH or Jehovah.
