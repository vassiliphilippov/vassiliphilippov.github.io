cession

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˈsɛʃ.ən / (BrE)
the formal giving up of rights, property, or territory by a state or person, usually by treaty or agreement
the act of giving up rights or territory

Examples

The treaty resulted in the cession of large tracts of land to the neighboring country.
After lengthy negotiations, the cession of the disputed territory was finalized.
The cession of power was a major turning point in the nation's history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relinquishment - the act of giving up or abandoning something
  • Antonym: acquisition - the act of gaining or acquiring something, especially territory or property

Etymology

From Latin 'cessio' (a yielding), from 'cedere' (to yield).
