caulescent

1. ADJ

/ kɔːˈlɛsənt / (AmE), / kɔːˈlɛsənt / (BrE)
(of plants) having a well-developed stem above ground.
having a well-developed stem (botany)

Examples

Many herbaceous plants are described as caulescent because their stems are visible above the soil.
The daisy is a caulescent plant, easily identified by its upright stem.
Unlike rosette plants, caulescent species grow vertical stalks above ground.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acaulescent - lacking a visible stem above ground (in plants)

Etymology

From Latin 'caulis' (stem) plus '-escent' (becoming).
