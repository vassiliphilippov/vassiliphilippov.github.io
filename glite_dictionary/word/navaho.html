Navaho

1. NOUN

/ ˈnævəˌhoʊ / (AmE), / ˈnavəhəʊ / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people originally from Arizona, New Mexico, and Utah, known for their rich culture and traditions
member of a Native American people

Examples

Many Navahos still live on reservations in the Southwest.
The Navaho are renowned for their woven blankets and rugs.
He traced his ancestry back to the Navaho people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Navajo - member of a Native American people from the southwestern US

Etymology

Variant spelling of Navajo, from Tewa (a Tanoan language): Navahu 'large area of cultivated fields'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnævəˌhoʊ / (AmE), / ˈnavəhəʊ / (BrE)
the Athabaskan language spoken by the Navajo people of the southwestern United States
language of the Navajo people

Examples

Many elders continue to speak Navaho as their first language at home.
The study of the Navaho language provides insights into indigenous American cultures.
He took evening classes to learn Navaho from a fluent speaker.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Navajo - the people of a Native American tribe in the southwestern United States

Etymology

From Navajo (self-designation: Diné), sometimes spelled 'Navaho' in older sources.
