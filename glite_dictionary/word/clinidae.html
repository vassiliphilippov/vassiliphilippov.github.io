Clinidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈklaɪnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈklɪnɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of small marine fish (viviparous blennies) found in temperate and tropical seas, known for live-bearing reproduction
a family of viviparous marine fish

Examples

The Clinidae are a group of fishes commonly called viviparous blennies.
Marine biologists study the reproductive behavior of Clinidae in coastal ecosystems.
Several species within the Clinidae family are popular in saltwater aquariums due to their unique appearance.

Etymology

From New Latin Clinidae, from Greek 'klinein' (to slope), referring to the sloping heads of some species.
