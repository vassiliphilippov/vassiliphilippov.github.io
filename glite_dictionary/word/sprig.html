sprig

1. NOUN

/ sprɪɡ / (AmE), / sprɪɡ / (BrE)
an ornament shaped like a small spray of leaves or flowers, often used in decoration
ornament shaped like a spray of leaves or flowers

Examples

The dress was decorated with a delicate sprig of blue flowers at the collar.
She wore a silver sprig in her hair for the wedding ceremony.
The pottery was adorned with painted sprigs of leaves and blossoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spray - a small branch with leaves or flowers

2. NOUN

/ sprɪɡ / (AmE), / sprɪɡ / (BrE)
a small stem or branch, especially one that is freshly cut or used for decoration or cooking
small stem or branch

Examples

She placed a sprig of mint on top of the dessert for decoration.
A single sprig of rosemary can add a lot of flavor to a roast.
He wore a sprig of holly in his buttonhole during the holidays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: twig - a small thin branch of a tree or bush

Etymology

From Middle English sprigge, of unknown origin.
