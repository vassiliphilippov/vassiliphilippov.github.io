unexpended

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnɪkˈspɛndɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪkˈspɛndɪd / (BrE)
(of money or resources) not yet spent or used.
not yet spent or used (money/resources)

Examples

The committee allocated the unexpended funds to new projects.
At the end of the year, a significant portion of the budget remained unexpended.
Any unexpended grants must be returned to the funding agency.

Synset Examples

unexpended funds
his unspent allowance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unspent - not spent; remaining

Etymology

From un- (not) + expended, past participle of expend, from Latin expendere (to pay out).

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.ɪkˈspɛn.dɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.ɪkˈspen.dɪd / (BrE)
not used up; not yet spent or consumed
not used up

Examples

The army reported that several unexpended shells were recovered from the training area.
At the end of the fiscal year, there was a significant amount of unexpended grant money.
He carefully stored the unexpended supplies for future emergencies.

Synset Examples

leftover meatloaf
she had a little money left over so she went to a movie
some odd dollars left
saved the remaining sandwiches for supper
unexpended provisions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unused - not being used or not yet used
  • Antonym: spent - used up; consumed
  • Antonym: expended - used up or spent

Etymology

From the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'expended', past participle of 'expend' (to spend or use up), from Latin 'expendere' (to weigh out, pay).
