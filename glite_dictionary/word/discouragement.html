discouragement

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈkʊr.ɪdʒ.mənt / (AmE), / dɪsˈkʌr.ɪdʒ.mənt / (BrE)
the act of expressing opposition or disapproval, especially to deter someone from doing something
expression of opposition or disapproval

Examples

She spoke with clear discouragement about his plans to quit school.
Their constant discouragement made it difficult for him to pursue his dream.
Despite the discouragement from critics, she continued with her project.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: encouragement - the act of giving support, confidence, or hope to someone
  • Synonym: disapproval - the act of not approving; condemnatory feeling or expression

Etymology

From Old French 'descouragement', from 'descourager' (to discourage) and '-ment'.

2. NOUN

/ dɪsˈkɝːrɪdʒmənt / (AmE), / dɪsˈkʌrɪdʒmənt / (BrE)
the act of making someone less willing to do something; the process of deterring or dissuading
the act of deterring or dissuading

Examples

The government's new policy is aimed at the discouragement of tax evasion.
Effective discouragement of underage drinking requires both education and enforcement.
There was a noticeable discouragement of risky investments in the new regulations.

Synset Examples

the discouragement of petty theft

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deterrence - the act of discouraging an action through fear or doubt

Etymology

From Old French *descouragement*, from *descourager* (to discourage).

3. NOUN

/ dɪsˈkɝːrɪdʒmənt / (AmE), / dɪsˈkʌrɪdʒmənt / (BrE)
A feeling of having lost confidence or enthusiasm, especially when faced with difficulties or setbacks.
loss of confidence or hope

Examples

After several job rejections, she felt a wave of discouragement wash over her.
Despite his discouragement, his friends urged him to keep trying.
Persistent discouragement can make it difficult to achieve personal goals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: encouragement - the act of giving support, confidence, or hope
  • Synonym: despair - the complete loss or absence of hope

Etymology

From Old French 'descoragier' (discourage), from Latin 'cor' (heart).
