venomousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛn.ə.məs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈvɛn.ə.məs.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being venomous; the possession of venom or venom-producing glands.
quality of being venomous

Examples

Scientists studied the venomousness of the newly discovered species of snake.
The degree of venomousness varies greatly among different types of spiders.
He was fascinated by the venomousness displayed by certain insects as a defense mechanism.

Synset Examples

Black-tailed prairie dogs also adjust their antisnake behavior to apparent risk, as indicated by the size and venomousness of the snake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: venomous - having the ability to produce and deliver venom
  • Antonym: harmlessness - the quality of being harmless

Etymology

From 'venomous' + '-ness', where 'venomous' comes from Old French 'venimeux', from Latin 'venenosus'.
