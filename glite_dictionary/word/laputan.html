Laputan

1. ADJ

/ ləˈpjutən / (AmE), / ləˈpjuːtən / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of Laputa, the imaginary flying island in Jonathan Swift's 'Gulliver's Travels', or its people; often implying fanciful, visionary, or absurdly impractical ideas.
of or relating to Laputa; fanciful or impractical

Examples

His proposal for solving the energy crisis was so Laputan that no one could take it seriously.
The novel is filled with Laputan inventions that defy logic and practicality.
Critics dismissed the project as a Laputan fantasy detached from reality.

Etymology

From Laputa, the imaginary flying island in Jonathan Swift's 'Gulliver's Travels' (1726).

2. ADJ

/ ləˈpjuːtən / (AmE), / ləˈpjuːtən / (BrE)
Impractical, fanciful, or visionary; characterized by speculative ideas with little connection to reality.
impractical or visionary; speculative

Examples

Some critics dismissed the proposal as a Laputan fantasy, insisting it could never work in practice.
He was fond of inventing Laputan schemes that ignored basic realities.
Their Laputan plans for urban renewal impressed no one who knew the city's real problems.

Synset Examples

airy theories about socioeconomic improvement
visionary schemes for getting rich

Related Terms

  • Synonym: visionary - having or showing clear ideas about what should happen or be done in the future, often unrealistic
  • Antonym: practical - concerned with actual use or practice, opposed to speculative or imaginative

Etymology

From Laputa, the flying island in Jonathan Swift's 'Gulliver's Travels,' whose inhabitants were preoccupied with impractical schemes.
