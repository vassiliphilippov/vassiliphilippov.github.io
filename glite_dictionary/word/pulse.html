pulse

1. NOUN

/ pʌls / (AmE), / pʌls / (BrE)
a brief, sudden change in an electrical signal, often repeated periodically, as used in electronics or telecommunications
brief change in electrical signal

Examples

The circuit generates a pulse every second to regulate the timing.
A digital signal consists of a series of pulses representing binary information.
The technician measured the length of each pulse using an oscilloscope.

Synset Examples

the pulsations seemed to be coming from a star

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waveform - shape of an oscillating signal, especially in electronics

2. NOUN

/ pʌls / (AmE), / pʌls / (BrE)
the regular throbbing of arteries caused by the beating of the heart, especially as felt at the wrist or neck
heart's rhythmic beat in arteries

Examples

The nurse checked his pulse to monitor his heart rate.
After running up the stairs, her pulse was racing.
She placed two fingers on his wrist to feel his pulse.

Synset Examples

he could feel the beat of her heart

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heartbeat - the regular movement or sound of the heart as it pumps blood
  • Degree Higher: palpitation - an irregular, rapid beating of the heart

Etymology

From Latin 'pulsus' (beating, striking), from 'pellere' (to push, strike).
