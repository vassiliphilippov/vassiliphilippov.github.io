Ballistite

1. NOUN

/ bəˈlɪstaɪt / (AmE), / bəˈlɪstaɪt / (BrE)
A smokeless explosive compound, containing nitrocellulose and nitroglycerin, used as a propellant in firearms and artillery.
smokeless explosive and propellant

Examples

The new artillery shells were filled with Ballistite for improved range and reduced smoke.
Alfred Nobel invented Ballistite as a more stable propellant for firearms.
Because Ballistite burns with little smoke, it offered a tactical advantage in military operations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cordite - a smokeless propellant similar to Ballistite, used in ammunition

Etymology

From Italian *ballistite*, from *ballista* (war engine for hurling stones or missiles), named by Swedish chemist Alfred Nobel.
