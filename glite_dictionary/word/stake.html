stake

1. VERB

/ steɪk / (AmE), / steɪk / (BrE)
to mark or indicate a place or boundary by driving stakes into the ground
mark with stakes

Examples

They staked the garden to show where the new path would go.
Surveyors stake the corners of the property before construction begins.
Workers staked out a section of the forest for tree planting.

Synset Examples

stake out the path

Etymology

From Old English *staca* (stake, post), of Germanic origin.
