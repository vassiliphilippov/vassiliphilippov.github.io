Lycopus americanus

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈkoʊpəs əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnəs / (AmE), / lɪˈkəʊpəs əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnəs / (BrE)
An aromatic perennial herb native to the United States, commonly known as American water horehound, often found in wetlands.
aromatic wetland herb of the U.S.

Examples

The wetland was filled with Lycopus americanus and other native plants.
Botanists documented the spread of Lycopus americanus along the riverbanks last summer.
Herbalists sometimes use extracts from Lycopus americanus in traditional remedies.

Etymology

From genus name Lycopus (Greek lykos, wolf + pous, foot) and Latin americanus, meaning 'of America'.
