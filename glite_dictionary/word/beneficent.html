beneficent

1. ADJ

/ bɪˈnɛfəsənt / (AmE), / bɪˈnɛfɪsənt / (BrE)
generous or doing good, especially by giving help to those in need.
generous; doing good or charitable acts

Examples

The foundation was established by a beneficent couple who wanted to support local families in need.
His beneficent actions brought hope to many homeless people during the harsh winter months.
She is known for her beneficent donations to educational charities across the country.

Synset Examples

a benevolent contributor
eleemosynary relief
philanthropic contributions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benevolent - well meaning and kindly
  • Antonym: malevolent - having or showing a wish to do evil to others

Etymology

From Latin beneficent-, present participle of benefacere (to do good).

2. ADJ

/ bəˈnɛfɪsənt / (AmE), / bɪˈnɛfɪsənt / (BrE)
(of a person or action) doing or producing good; resulting in benefit or advantage, especially through acts of kindness or charity.
doing or producing good; charitable

Examples

The foundation is known for its beneficent activities throughout the region.
Many considered her reign to be a period of beneficent leadership.
He made a beneficent donation that helped build the new hospital.

Synset Examples

the most beneficent regime in history

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benevolent - well meaning and kindly, showing goodwill
  • Antonym: maleficent - causing harm or destruction, especially by supernatural means

Etymology

From Latin beneficent-, from beneficus 'favorable, generous', from bene 'well' + facere 'to do'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00225394-a
Synset POS: a
