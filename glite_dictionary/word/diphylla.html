Diphylla

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈfɪlə / (AmE), / daɪˈfɪlə / (BrE)
A genus of vampire bats native to the Americas, containing the hairy-legged vampire bat (Diphylla ecaudata).
genus of vampire bats

Examples

The Diphylla genus includes the rarely encountered hairy-legged vampire bat.
Researchers studied the feeding habits of Diphylla in Central America.
Unlike Desmodus, Diphylla bats feed mostly on the blood of birds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Desmodus - genus of common vampire bats

Etymology

From Greek di- (two) + phyllon (leaf), referring to leaf-like structures on the nose.
