to yearn

1. VERB

/ jɝːn / (AmE), / jɜːn / (BrE)
to have a strong, persistent desire or longing for something, especially something that is difficult to obtain or unattainable
desire strongly or persistently

Examples

She would often yearn for her childhood home during difficult times.
As the years passed, he began to yearn for adventure beyond his small town.
Many people yearn for a sense of purpose in their lives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: long - to have a strong desire for something
  • Synonym: to crave - to feel a powerful desire for something
  • Antonym: to loathe - to feel intense dislike or aversion to

Etymology

From Old English 'giernan' (to desire, beg, demand); related to German 'gären' (to ferment).

2. VERB

/ jɝn / (AmE), / jɜːn / (BrE)
to have a strong and intense desire or longing for something or someone, especially when they are absent or unattainable
to strongly desire or long for something or someone

Examples

After moving abroad, she would often yearn for the comforts of home.
He could not help but yearn for the days of his youth.
Despite her busy life, she sometimes yearned for quiet moments of solitude.

Synset Examples

She ached for a cigarette
I am pining for my lover

Related Terms

  • Synonym: long - to have a strong wish or desire for something
  • Synonym: to crave - to want intensely
  • Synonym: desire - to want something strongly

Etymology

From Old English *giernan* (to desire), related to Old High German *gerōn*.
