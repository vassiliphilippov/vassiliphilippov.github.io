Sambucus racemosa

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæm.bjʊˈkoʊs ræˈsiː.moʊ.sə / (AmE), / ˌsæm.bjuːˈkəʊs rəˈsiː.mə.sə / (BrE)
A species of elder shrub native to Eurasia, known for its clusters of red berries.
Eurasian shrub with red berries

Examples

The Sambucus racemosa produces bright red berries that attract a variety of birds.
Gardeners often plant Sambucus racemosa for its ornamental value and tolerance of cold climates.
Berries from the Sambucus racemosa should not be eaten raw, as they can be toxic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elderberry - Fruit of the elder shrub (genus Sambucus)

Etymology

From Latin: 'Sambucus' meaning 'elder tree' and 'racemosa' meaning 'having clusters' (referring to the berry arrangement).
