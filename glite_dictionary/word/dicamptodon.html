Dicamptodon

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈkæmptədɒn / (AmE), / daɪˈkæmptədɒn / (BrE)
A genus of salamanders found near cold streams, native to western North America.
genus of salamanders in North America

Examples

The Pacific giant Dicamptodon is known for its large size among salamanders.
Researchers observed several Dicamptodon larvae hiding under rocks in the cold stream.
Many species in the genus Dicamptodon play important roles in forest ecosystems.

Etymology

From Greek 'di-' (two) and 'kamptos' (flexed/bent) plus '-odon' (tooth), referring to tooth structure.

2. NOUN

/ daɪˈkæmptədɒn / (AmE), / daɪˈkæmptədɒn / (BrE)
A genus of large salamanders native to western North America, constituting the type genus of the family Dicamptodontidae.
a genus of North American salamanders

Examples

The Dicamptodon is often referred to as the Pacific giant salamander in scientific literature.
Her research focused on the habitat preferences of Dicamptodon in old-growth forests.
Several species within the Dicamptodon genus exhibit unique vocalizations compared to other salamanders.

Etymology

From Greek ‘di-’ (two) and ‘kamptos’ (bent), used in scientific Latin nomenclature.
