beaming

1. ADJ

/ ˈbiːmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbiːmɪŋ / (BrE)
smiling broadly with pleasure and pride; radiantly pleased and proud
smiling broadly, radiantly pleased

Examples

Her beaming smile lit up the entire room.
He stood next to his award, beaming with pride.
The beaming parents watched their child perform on stage.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radiant - showing great happiness, especially in one's face
  • Antonym: gloomy - looking sad or depressed

2. ADJ

/ ˈbiːmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbiːmɪŋ / (BrE)
radiating light or brightness; shining vividly
radiating light; shining

Examples

The children looked up at the beaming sun with delight.
She walked into the room with a beaming smile that brightened everyone's mood.
The garden was filled with beaming daffodils on that spring morning.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radiant - emitting light or heat; shining brightly
  • Antonym: dim - not bright; giving off little light

Etymology

3. ADJ

/ ˈbiːmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbiːmɪŋ / (BrE)
smiling broadly with great pleasure or happiness; radiantly cheerful and bright.
radiantly cheerful or smiling

Examples

She greeted her old friend with a beaming smile that lit up the room.
The proud parents stood beaming at their daughter's graduation ceremony.
A beaming child waved enthusiastically as the parade passed by.

Synset Examples

a beaming smile
a glad May morning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radiant - shining or glowing brightly, especially with happiness
  • Antonym: gloomy - looking sad or depressed; lacking cheerfulness or brightness

Etymology

Present participle of 'beam', meaning to emit rays of light or to smile radiantly.
