Bignonia

1. NOUN

/ bɪɡˈnoʊniə / (AmE), / bɪɡˈnəʊniə / (BrE)
a genus of tropical American climbing plants in the family Bignoniaceae, known for their large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers; sometimes used more specifically for the cross vine.
a genus of tropical climbing plants

Examples

The Bignonia is prized in gardens for its spectacular orange blossoms.
A mature Bignonia can climb high walls and trellises with ease.
Botanists recently discovered a new species related to the common Bignonia.

Etymology

Named after Jean-Paul Bignon, a French ecclesiastic and librarian (1662–1743).

2. NOUN

/ bɪɡˈnoʊniə / (AmE), / bɪɡˈnəʊniə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Bignoniaceae, with one species, the cross vine.
genus of flowering plants (Bignoniaceae)

Examples

The Bignonia genus is best known for its beautiful trumpet-shaped flowers.
Botanists classify the cross vine under the genus Bignonia.
Bignonia plants are often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Bignoniaceae - the trumpet creeper family of flowering plants
  • Degree Lower: cross vine - a climbing vine, the only species in Bignonia

Etymology

Named after Jean-Paul Bignon, a French librarian and scholar.
