nob

1. NOUN

/ nɑb / (AmE), / nɒb / (BrE)
(informal) An upper-class or wealthy person, especially one perceived as privileged or snobbish.
upper-class or wealthy person (informal)
informal british

Examples

The exclusive club was full of nobs in expensive suits.
He acted like a real nob at the party, looking down on everyone else.
Some say the old village is now just a playground for London nobs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toff - an upper-class person in Britain, especially one who is well dressed
  • Antonym: commoner - a person who is not a member of the nobility or aristocracy

Etymology

Origin uncertain, possibly from 'noble' or 'nobleman'.
