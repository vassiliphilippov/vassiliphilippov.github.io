reducible

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈduːsəbəl / (AmE), / rɪˈdjuːsəbəl / (BrE)
able to be reduced in size, amount, degree, or complexity; capable of being brought to a simpler, smaller, or more basic form.
able to be reduced

Examples

The complex equation is reducible to a much simpler form.
Her symptoms were reducible through medication and therapy.
Some materials are not reducible by ordinary chemical means.

Synset Examples

reducible to a set of principles of human nature

Related Terms

  • Antonym: irreducible - not able to be reduced

Etymology

From Latin 'reducibilis', from 'reducere' meaning 'to lead back, bring back'.
