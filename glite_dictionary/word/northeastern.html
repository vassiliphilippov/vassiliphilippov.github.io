northeastern

1. ADJ

/ nɔrtˈiːstərn / (AmE), / nɔːθˈiːstən / (BrE)
relating to or situated in the northeast, especially the region of the United States generally including the New England states, New York, and sometimes New Jersey and Pennsylvania
of or from the northeastern U.S.

Examples

He grew up in a northeastern city surrounded by historic universities.
The northeastern United States experiences cold winters and colorful autumns.
Many northeastern towns are known for their colonial architecture.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: southeastern - relating to or situated in the southeast region, especially of the U.S.
  • Degree Lower: northwestern - relating to or situated in the northwest region, especially of the U.S.
  • Degree Lower: eastern - relating to the east or eastern part of a region

Etymology

From north + eastern, indicating direction or region.

2. ADJ

/ ˌnɔrtˈiːstərn / (AmE), / ˌnɔːθˈiːstən / (BrE)
Situated in or oriented toward the northeast.
situated toward the northeast

Examples

The northeastern corner of the city receives the most snowfall each winter.
A powerful storm struck the northeastern region of the country last night.
They enjoy hiking in the northeastern part of the national park.

Synset Examples

the northeasterly part of the island

Related Terms

  • Synonym: northeast - the direction between north and east
  • Antonym: southeastern - situated in or oriented toward the southeast
  • Antonym: northwestern - situated in or oriented toward the northwest

Etymology

From 'north' + 'eastern', meaning toward the northeast direction.
