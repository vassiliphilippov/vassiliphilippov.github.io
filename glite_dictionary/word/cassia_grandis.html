Cassia grandis

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæsiə ˈɡrændɪs / (AmE), / ˈkæsiə ˈɡrændɪs / (BrE)
A tropical American semi-evergreen tree with erect clusters of pink or rose-colored flowers, often grown as an ornamental.
ornamental tropical tree with pink flowers

Examples

The Cassia grandis in the botanical garden draws attention with its striking pink blossoms.
Many homeowners plant Cassia grandis for its ornamental value and shade.
In spring, the Cassia grandis bursts into color, transforming streets and parks.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Cassia' is a genus name, 'grandis' means 'large' in Latin.
