capote

1. NOUN

/ kəˈpoʊt / (AmE), / kəˈpəʊt / (BrE)
A long cloak with a hood that can be pulled over the head, traditionally worn as outerwear.
long cloak with a hood

Examples

The old photograph showed a woman in a capote strolling through the market.
Her grandmother's capote was made of thick wool and beautifully embroidered.
During the festival, many participants dressed in traditional clothing, including the iconic capote.

Etymology

From French 'capote', meaning cloak or hood.

2. NOUN

/ kəˈpoʊt / (AmE), / kəˈpəʊt / (BrE)
a long overcoat with a hood, especially one worn in the past
a long hooded overcoat

Examples

The soldier wore a thick capote during the harsh winter months.
In the museum, a 19th-century capote was displayed among the military uniforms.
She draped the capote over her shoulders before heading outside into the snow.

Etymology

From French *capote* (hooded cloak or coat), ultimately from late Latin *cappa* (cloak).
