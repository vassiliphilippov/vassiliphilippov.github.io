acidophilous

1. ADJ

/ ˌæs.ɪˈdɑ.fə.ləs / (AmE), / ˌæs.ɪˈdɒf.ɪ.ləs / (BrE)
(of organisms, especially bacteria) thriving or growing well in an acidic environment.
growing well in an acidic environment

Examples

Some acidophilous bacteria are essential in the fermentation industry.
The soil is too alkaline for acidophilous plants to flourish.
Researchers discovered a new acidophilous species thriving in the hot springs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acidophilic - preferring or thriving in acidic conditions
  • Antonym: acidophobic - not tolerating acidic environments

Etymology

From Latin 'acidum' (sour, acid) + Greek 'philos' (loving).
