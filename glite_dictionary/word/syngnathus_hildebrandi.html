Syngnathus hildebrandi

1. NOUN

/ sɪŋˈnæθəs hɪl.dəˈbræn.di / (AmE), / sɪŋˈnæθəs hɪl.dəˈbran.di / (BrE)
A small species of pipefish, about 4 inches long, found off the Florida Gulf Coast.
species of small Gulf Coast pipefish

Examples

The Syngnathus hildebrandi is well-camouflaged among the seagrasses off the Florida Gulf Coast.
Marine biologists discovered a population of Syngnathus hildebrandi thriving in coastal waters.
Because of its small size, the Syngnathus hildebrandi can be difficult to spot in its natural habitat.

Etymology

From modern Latin; genus name Syngnathus from Greek syn- (together) + gnathos (jaw); species named after Samuel F. Hildebrand, American ichthyologist.
