Monocanthus

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːnəˈkænθəs / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈkænθəs / (BrE)
The type genus of the fish family Monocanthidae, comprising filefishes with distinctive rough skin and spiny dorsal fins.
genus of filefishes

Examples

The genus Monocanthus is characterized by a single spine on its dorsal fin.
Marine biologists study Monocanthus to understand filefish evolution.
Several species within Monocanthus inhabit coral reefs in tropical waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filefish - common name for members of the Monocanthidae family

Etymology

From Greek 'monos' (single) + 'akantha' (thorn, spine), referring to the prominent spine on these fishes.
