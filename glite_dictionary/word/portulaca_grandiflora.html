Portulaca grandiflora

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɔːr.t͡ʃəˈleɪ.kə ˌɡræn.dɪˈflɔːr.ə / (AmE), / ˌpɔː.tjʊˈlɑː.kə ˌɡræn.dɪˈflɔː.rə / (BrE)
a species of flowering plant, also called moss rose, cultivated for its fleshy, moss-like leaves and brightly colored flowers
a cultivated flowering plant (moss rose)

Examples

Portulaca grandiflora adds vibrant colors to summer gardens thanks to its profusion of blooms.
Many gardeners choose Portulaca grandiflora for its drought tolerance and easy care.
The fleshy leaves of Portulaca grandiflora make it a unique addition to rock gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moss rose - another common name for Portulaca grandiflora

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Portulaca' as the genus name and 'grandiflora' meaning 'large-flowered'.
