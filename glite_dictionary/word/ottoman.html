Ottoman

1. ADJ

/ ˈɑː.t̬ə.mən / (AmE), / ˈɒt.ə.mən / (BrE)
relating to the Ottoman Empire, its people, or its culture
relating to the Ottoman Empire

Examples

The Ottoman architecture of the palace impressed visitors from around the world.
Many traditions from the Ottoman period still influence modern Turkish culture.
She wrote her thesis on the effects of Ottoman rule in the Balkans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Turkish - relating to Turkey, its people, or its language/culture

Etymology

From French ‘ottoman’, from Italian ‘ottomano’, from Arabic ‘ʿUthmānī’, from ‘Uthmān’ (Osman I, founder of the Ottoman dynasty).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑːtəˌmɑːn / (AmE), / ˈɒtəmən / (BrE)
the Turkish dynasty that governed the Ottoman Empire from the 13th century until the early 20th century
Turkish ruling dynasty, 13th–20th centuries

Examples

The rise of the Ottoman dynasty marked a new era in Middle Eastern history.
For centuries, the Ottoman sultans controlled vast territories across three continents.
After World War I, the reign of the Ottoman family came to an end.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ottoman Empire - the empire ruled by the Ottoman dynasty, spanning Southeast Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa

Etymology

From Arabic 'ʿUthmān', name of the dynasty's founder Osman I.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08173404-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈɑːt̬əˌmɑːn / (AmE), / ˈɒtəmən / (BrE)
A member of the Turkish dynasty founded by Osman I, or, more broadly, a Turk belonging to the Ottoman Empire.
member of Osman I's Turkish dynasty or empire

Examples

Many historians study the rise of the Ottoman dynasty in Anatolia.
The conquest of Constantinople was led by an Ottoman sultan.
He traced his ancestry back to the early Ottoman Turks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Turk - a native or inhabitant of Turkey, or a person of Turkish descent

Etymology

From French 'ottoman', from Italian 'ottomano', from Arabic 'ʿUthmānī' (related to Osman I).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
