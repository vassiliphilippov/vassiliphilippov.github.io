ottoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑː.t̬ə.mən / (AmE), / ˈɒt.ə.mən / (BrE)
a padded, upholstered seat or bench, often without a back or arms, typically used as a footstool or occasional seat
padded seat or footstool

Examples

She placed her feet on the ottoman while reading her book.
We bought a new leather ottoman to match the sofa.
The ottoman also doubles as a storage unit for blankets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: footstool - a low stool for resting the feet

Etymology

From French *ottomane*, after the Ottoman Empire, where such furniture originated.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑː.t̬ə.mən / (AmE), / ˈɒt.ə.mən / (BrE)
a low seat with a thick, padded cushion, often without a back or arms, sometimes used as a footrest or for storage
padded seat or footrest, often without a back

Examples

She placed her feet on the ottoman while reading a book by the fire.
The living room is furnished with a large ottoman that doubles as a coffee table.
He bought a velvet ottoman to add extra seating to the small apartment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: footstool - a low stool used to support the feet

Etymology

From French 'ottomane', ultimately from the Ottoman Empire, reflecting the style's origin.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
