townsman

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaʊnzmən / (AmE), / ˈtaʊnzmən / (BrE)
a man who lives in the same town as someone else or who is a resident of a particular town
male resident of the same town

Examples

He greeted his fellow townsman warmly after many years apart.
A townsman approached the mayor to discuss the new park project.
During the festival, each townsman dressed in traditional clothing.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: townswoman - a female resident of a town

Etymology

From Middle English 'townsman', from 'town' + 'man'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtaʊnzmən / (AmE), / ˈtaʊnzmən / (BrE)
a man who lives in a town or city
male resident of a town or city

Examples

The old townsman gathered with his friends in the village square every evening.
As a lifelong townsman, he took pride in the city's vibrant culture.
The townsman offered directions to the lost travelers with a warm smile.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: townswoman - female resident of a town or city
  • Synonym: citizen - an inhabitant of a town or city; especially one entitled to its privileges

Etymology

From town + man, dating from Middle English.
