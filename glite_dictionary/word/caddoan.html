Caddoan

1. NOUN

/ ˈkædoʊən / (AmE), / ˈkædəʊən / (BrE)
a family of North American indigenous languages spoken mainly in the Midwest, especially by the Caddo people
family of North American indigenous languages

Examples

Linguists have studied the grammar and vocabulary of several Caddoan languages.
The Caddoan family includes languages such as Caddo, Wichita, and Pawnee.
Many Caddoan languages are now endangered or have very few speakers.

Etymology

From the name 'Caddo', an indigenous people of the southern Great Plains, plus '-an', denoting relation.
