stoppage

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɑːpɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈstɒpɪdʒ / (BrE)
a period or state in which an activity, process, or movement is brought to a halt or is inactive, usually after an interruption
a period when something is stopped

Examples

There was a brief stoppage in play while the injured player received treatment.
A water stoppage left the entire neighborhood without running water for hours.
The factory faced a stoppage due to a power outage yesterday.

Synset Examples

the negotiations were in arrest
held them in check
during the halt he got some lunch
the momentary stay enabled him to escape the blow
he spent the entire stop in his seat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interruption - a break in continuity or a period when activity stops
  • Antonym: continuation - the state of continuing without stopping

Etymology

From stop + -age, first recorded in the late 15th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɑːpɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈstɒpɪdʒ / (BrE)
an obstruction that prevents the normal flow of liquid or gas in a pipe or tube
obstruction in a pipe or tube

Examples

The stoppage in the sink was caused by grease buildup.
A plumber found a stoppage deep within the main sewer pipe.
Heavy rainfall sometimes leads to a stoppage in old drainage systems.

Synset Examples

we had to call a plumber to clear out the blockage in the drainpipe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blockage - something that blocks a pipe or tube
  • Synonym: obstruction - something that obstructs or blocks passage

Etymology

From 'stop' + '-age', first attested in the 17th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈstɑːpɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈstɒpɪdʒ / (BrE)
the act or process of stopping something; a halt or interruption in movement or activity
the act of stopping something

Examples

There was a brief stoppage of play while the injured player was treated.
The engineer cleared the stoppage in the fuel line quickly.
Unexpected stoppages have caused delays in the schedule.

Synset Examples

the third baseman made some remarkable stops
his stoppage of the flow resulted in a flood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interruption - the action of stopping something for a short period
  • Antonym: continuation - the act of continuing or resuming

Etymology

From stop + -age, first recorded in the late 15th century.
