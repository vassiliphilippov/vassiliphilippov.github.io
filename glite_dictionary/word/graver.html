graver

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪvər / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪvə / (BrE)
a small cutting tool with a sharp point, used by engravers for incising lines into metal, wood, or other materials
engraving tool with a sharp point

Examples

The artisan selected a fine graver to etch the intricate patterns into the silver plate.
Careful pressure with the graver allows the engraver to create precise lines and shading.
Each graver in the set is designed for different types of cuts and finishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burin - a steel engraving tool with a distinctive V-shaped point

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *graveer* (to engrave), related to 'grave', meaning to carve.
