notebook

1. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊtˌbʊk / (AmE), / ˈnəʊtˌbʊk / (BrE)
a small, lightweight, and portable personal computer, typically with a thin profile and battery power, designed for mobile use
portable personal computer (laptop)

Examples

She carried her notebook to the café to finish some work.
The company issued new notebooks to all employees for remote work.
My old notebook finally stopped working after five years of daily use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laptop - a portable personal computer that is small enough to use on one's lap
Source: WordNet

2. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊtˌbʊk / (AmE), / ˈnəʊtˌbʊk / (BrE)
A book with blank or lined pages used for writing notes, memoranda, or other information.
book for writing notes

Examples

She filled her notebook with ideas for her new novel.
Please bring a notebook and a pen to the meeting.
He found an old notebook from college in his desk drawer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pad - a stack of blank or lined sheets for writing or drawing

Etymology

From note + book.
