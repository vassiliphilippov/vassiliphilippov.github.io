irons

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪɚnz / (AmE), / ˈaɪənz / (BrE)
metal shackles used for restraining the hands or legs, especially of prisoners
metal shackles for restraint

Examples

The prisoner was led away in irons by the guards.
Old sailing ships often carried irons to punish mutinous crew members.
After years in irons, he was finally released from captivity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shackles - metal fastenings for securing the wrists or ankles
  • Synonym: handcuffs - metal restraints for securing a person's wrists

Etymology

From Old English 'īren', meaning iron (the metal), later applied to restraints made from iron.
