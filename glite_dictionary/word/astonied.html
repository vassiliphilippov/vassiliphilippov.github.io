astonied

1. ADJ

/ əˈstoʊnid / (AmE), / əˈstəʊnɪd / (BrE)
overcome with overwhelming surprise or shock; astonished or stunned
overwhelmed by shock or surprise (archaic)
archaic

Examples

She stood astonied at the sudden and unexpected news.
The knight looked astonied upon seeing the dragon soaring above the castle.
They were left astonied, unable to utter a single word in their disbelief.

Synset Examples

an amazed audience gave the magician a standing ovation
I stood enthralled, astonished by the vastness and majesty of the cathedral
astounded viewers wept at the pictures from the Oklahoma City bombing
stood in stunned silence
stunned scientists found not one but at least three viruses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astonished - feeling great surprise or amazement
  • Synonym: astounded - extremely surprised or shocked
  • Synonym: stunned - shocked or greatly surprised

Etymology

From Middle English 'astonied', past participle of 'astonien', from Old French 'estoner', from Latin 'extonare' (to thunder out).
