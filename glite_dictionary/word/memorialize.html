to memorialize

1. VERB

/ məˈmɔːriəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / mɪˈmɔːrɪəlaɪz / (BrE)
To do or create something that keeps the memory of a person or event alive, especially by making a public memorial or in a formal ceremony.
honor or commemorate a person or event

Examples

The city plans to memorialize the fallen heroes with a new monument downtown.
Every year, the community memorializes the tragic event with a candlelight vigil.
They memorialized their friend through a scholarship fund in his name.

Synset Examples

This sculpture commemorates the victims of the concentration camps
We memorialized the Dead

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to commemorate - to remember and show respect for someone or something in a ceremony

Etymology

From memorial + -ize, meaning to make into a memorial.

2. VERB

/ məˈmɔriəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / mɪˈmɔːrɪəlaɪz / (BrE)
to honor or commemorate someone or something in a lasting or formal way, especially by addressing or recording in a memorial
commemorate or honor in a formal way

Examples

The city decided to memorialize the fallen soldiers with a new monument.
She memorialized her grandmother by writing a heartfelt tribute.
An annual ceremony is held to memorialize the victims of the tragedy.

Synset Examples

The President memorialized the heroes of the battle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to commemorate - to honor the memory of someone or something, especially with a ceremony

Etymology

From memorial + -ize, meaning to make into a memorial or to record as a memorial.
