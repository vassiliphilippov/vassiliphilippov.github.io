Canna generalis

1. NOUN

/ ˈkænə dʒɛnˈɛrəlɪs / (AmE), / ˈkanə dʒɛnˈɪərəlɪs / (BrE)
A group of hybrid canna plants cultivated for their large, brightly colored yellow to red flowers.
hybrid garden canna plant

Examples

The Canna generalis planted along the driveway add a splash of vibrant color in summer.
Gardeners often choose Canna generalis for its striking, tropical-looking flowers and lush foliage.
During the festival, the park's flower beds were filled with blooming Canna generalis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canna - any plant of the genus Canna, grown for showy flowers or foliage

Etymology

From Latin 'canna' (reed) and Neo-Latin 'generalis' (general, of a group); used in horticulture to describe hybrid cannas.
