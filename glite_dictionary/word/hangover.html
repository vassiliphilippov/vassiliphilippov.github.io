hangover

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæŋˌoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈhæŋˌəʊvə / (BrE)
A feeling of illness or discomfort following the use of alcohol or certain drugs, typically involving headache, nausea, and sensitivity to light and sound.
aftereffects of alcohol or drug use

Examples

She had a terrible hangover after the party last night.
Drinking plenty of water can help with a hangover.
He called in sick because his hangover was so bad.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intoxication - the state of being affected by alcohol or drugs
  • Degree Higher: withdrawal - symptoms following the cessation of drug or alcohol use

Etymology

From 'hang' + 'over', originally meaning something that remains from a previous time; current sense from 19th century English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhæŋˌoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈhæŋˌəʊvə / (BrE)
something that remains from an earlier time or that has survived from the past
remaining thing from the past

Examples

That law is a hangover from a time when society was very different.
Many of the city's traditions are actually hangovers from colonial days.
The ceremony felt like a hangover from the empire's glory years.

Synset Examples

a holdover from the sixties
hangovers from the 19th century

Related Terms

  • Synonym: holdover - something that remains from an earlier time
  • Synonym: remnant - a small remaining part from something larger
