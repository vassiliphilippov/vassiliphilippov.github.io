to blaspheme

1. VERB

/ blæsˈfim / (AmE), / blɑːsˈfiːm / (BrE)
to speak about God or sacred things in a disrespectful or irreverent way; to utter profanities or obscene language
speak irreverently or profanely

Examples

Many considered it shocking when he would blaspheme during his speeches.
During the argument, she began to blaspheme loudly, shocking those around her.
In some countries, to blaspheme can be a criminal offense punishable by law.

Synset Examples

The drunken men were cursing loudly in the street

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curse - to use profane or offensive language
  • Synonym: profane - to treat something sacred with disrespect

Etymology

From Greek *blasphemein* (to speak evil of), via Late Latin *blasphemare*.

2. VERB

/ blæsˈfim / (AmE), / blæsˈfiːm / (BrE)
to speak about God or sacred things in a way that shows disrespect or irreverence
speak irreverently about sacred things

Examples

He was accused of blaspheming against the church during his speech.
To blaspheme in some countries can be a criminal offense.
She would never dare to blaspheme in front of her deeply religious family.

Synset Examples

blaspheme God

Related Terms

  • Synonym: profane - treat (something sacred) with irreverence or disrespect
  • Antonym: revere - regard with deep respect or awe

Etymology

From Greek blasphēmein (to speak evil of), via Late Latin blasphemare.
