flavone

1. NOUN

/ ˈfleɪˌvoʊn / (AmE), / ˈflāvəʊn / (BrE)
a colorless crystalline compound found in many plants, from which numerous plant pigments are derived
a plant-derived crystalline compound

Examples

Many yellow flowers contain flavone as a key pigment component.
Researchers isolated flavone from the leaves to study its antioxidant properties.
The structure of flavone provides a basis for understanding related plant compounds.

Etymology

From Latin 'flavus' (yellow), referring to the yellow pigments derived from flavone.
