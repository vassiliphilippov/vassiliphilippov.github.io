Tajik

1. NOUN

/ tɑːˈdʒiːk / (AmE), / tɑːˈdʒiːk / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Tajikistan or the surrounding regions, especially in Uzbekistan, Afghanistan, and China
a person from Tajikistan or nearby regions

Examples

Many Tajiks speak both Tajik and Russian fluently.
The mountainous regions are mainly inhabited by Tajiks and Uzbeks.
She met a group of friendly Tajiks while traveling through Central Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Uzbek - a native or inhabitant of Uzbekistan
  • Synonym: Afghan - a native or inhabitant of Afghanistan

Etymology

From Persian تاجیک (tājīk), referring to people of Persian origin.

2. NOUN

/ tɑːˈdʒik / (AmE), / tæˈdʒiːk / (BrE)
the Iranian language spoken mainly in Tajikistan and parts of Iran, closely related to Farsi
Iranian language of Tajikistan

Examples

She is fluent in both Tajik and Russian, making her an excellent interpreter.
The official documents were translated from Tajik into English for the conference.
Many schools in Tajikistan teach their classes in Tajik rather than Russian.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Farsi - the Persian language, especially as spoken in Iran

Etymology

From Persian تاجیک (Tājīk), referring to a Persian-speaking people of Central Asia.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06987050-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ tɑˈdʒik / (AmE), / ˈtɑːdʒiːk / (BrE)
a member of a mainly Muslim ethnic group living primarily in Tajikistan and neighboring regions of Uzbekistan, Afghanistan, and China
member of a Central Asian ethnic group

Examples

The Tajik people have a rich tradition of poetry and music.
Many Tajik communities speak a dialect of Persian called Tajik.
During the festival, a group of Tajik dancers performed traditional routines.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Uzbek - member of another Central Asian ethnic group, primarily in Uzbekistan

Etymology

From Persian تاجیک (Tājīk), meaning 'Tajik'.
