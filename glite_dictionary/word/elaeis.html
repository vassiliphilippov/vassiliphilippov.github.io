Elaeis

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈliːɪs / (AmE), / ɪˈliːɪs / (BrE)
A genus of tropical palms, including species widely cultivated for their oil-rich fruit, such as the African oil palm.
genus of oil palms

Examples

Elaeis species are the primary source of palm oil produced worldwide.
The Elaeis genus includes the commercially important oil palm trees.
Scientists are studying Elaeis to improve oil yield and sustainability.

Etymology

From Greek elaia (olive), referring to the oil-rich fruit.

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈliː.ɪs / (AmE), / ɪˈliː.ɪs / (BrE)
a genus of tropical palms known as oil palms, cultivated for their edible oil-rich fruit
genus of oil palms

Examples

The Elaeis genus is essential for producing palm oil used worldwide.
Researchers study Elaeis to improve oil yield and disease resistance.
Plantations of Elaeis are a major part of the agricultural landscape in Malaysia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oil palm - a palm tree of the genus Elaeis that produces oil-rich fruit

Etymology

From Greek elaia (olive), due to the oil-producing nature of the palms.
