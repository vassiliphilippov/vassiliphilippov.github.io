Jamesian

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒeɪmziən / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪmzɪən / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of Henry James or his style of writing, often involving psychological insight and intricate prose.
of or relating to Henry James or his style

Examples

The novel's subtle psychological complexity is distinctly Jamesian in style.
Critics praised the author's Jamesian attention to the nuances of social interaction.
Her writing has a Jamesian quality, marked by detailed character exploration.

Etymology

From the name Henry James (1843–1916), American-born writer, with the suffix -ian.

2. ADJ

/ ˈdʒeɪmziən / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪmziən / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of William James, his philosophy, or his teachings.
relating to William James or his philosophy

Examples

The Jamesian approach to psychology emphasized the importance of subjective experience.
Her thesis presents a Jamesian analysis of religious belief.
In the seminar, we discussed the impact of Jamesian pragmatism on modern philosophy.

Etymology

Formed from the name William James, American philosopher and psychologist, with the adjectival suffix '-ian'.
