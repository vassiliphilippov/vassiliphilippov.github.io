Pomacentrus

1. NOUN

/ poʊməˈsɛntrəs / (AmE), / pɒməˈsɛntrəs / (BrE)
A genus of small, brightly colored marine damselfishes found in tropical and subtropical waters, especially in the Indo-Pacific region.
genus of damselfishes

Examples

Many species within the genus Pomacentrus are popular in marine aquariums due to their bright colors.
Researchers discovered a new species of Pomacentrus off the coast of Indonesia.
The coral reefs are home to several varieties of Pomacentrus that play a crucial role in the ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damselfish - small, brightly colored marine fish of the family Pomacentridae

Etymology

From Greek 'poma' (cover) + 'kentron' (sting), referring to structures on the fish.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊməˈsɛntrəs / (AmE), / ˌpɒməˈsɛntrəs / (BrE)
A genus of damselfishes in the family Pomacentridae, found mainly in tropical marine waters.
a genus of damselfish

Examples

The reef was teeming with Pomacentrus among the corals.
Researchers studied the behavior of Pomacentrus in their natural habitat.
A young aquarist recently added a few Pomacentrus to her marine aquarium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damselfish - any small, brightly colored marine fish of the family Pomacentridae

Etymology

From Greek 'poma' (cover, lid) and 'kentron' (sting, spur), referring to features of the fish.
