discontinuance

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪskənˈtinuəns / (AmE), / ˌdɪskənˈtɪnjuəns / (BrE)
the act of ending something or stopping it from continuing; an interruption, either temporary or permanent.
the act of ending or stopping

Examples

The sudden discontinuance of the service caused inconvenience to many customers.
After years of losses, the company announced the discontinuance of its magazine.
Weather conditions led to the temporary discontinuance of public transportation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cessation - the process of ending or stopping something
  • Synonym: interruption - the act of stopping something for a period of time

Etymology

From Old French 'descontinuance', based on Latin 'continuare' (to continue) with the prefix 'dis-' indicating reversal.
