lovage

1. NOUN

/ ˈlʌvɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈlʌvɪdʒ / (BrE)
a tall, perennial herb native to southern Europe, with aromatic leaves, stalks, and seeds used as food or seasoning
aromatic edible herb

Examples

Fresh lovage leaves can be chopped and added to salads for a unique flavor.
The chef used lovage stalks as a crunchy garnish for the soup.
Gardeners in Mediterranean climates often grow lovage for its edible parts and aromatic qualities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celery - a similar but more commonly used culinary herb and vegetable

Etymology

From Old French 'lovache', from Late Latin 'levisticum', influenced by Latin 'Ligusticum' (from Liguria, Italy).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlʌvɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈlʌvɪdʒ / (BrE)
a tall perennial herb (Levisticum officinale) of the parsley family, with aromatic leaves and stems used in cooking and its seeds used as a spice
an aromatic herb used in cooking

Examples

She added chopped lovage to the soup for a distinct, celery-like flavor.
Fresh lovage leaves can be used in salads, much like parsley.
Some artisanal cheeses are seasoned with lovage seeds for extra aroma.

Etymology

From Old French 'lovache', from Latin 'levisticum' (the plant name).
