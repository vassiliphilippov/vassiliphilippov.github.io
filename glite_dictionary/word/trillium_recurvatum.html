Trillium recurvatum

1. NOUN

/ trɪl.i.əm rɪˌkɝː.vəˈtəm / (AmE), / trɪl.i.əm rɪˌkɜː.vəˈtʌm / (BrE)
A species of trillium native to central United States, characterized by dark purple, stalkless flowers.
plant with dark purple, sessile flowers

Examples

The woodland was dotted with Trillium recurvatum, their deep purple petals standing out among the greenery.
Botanists conducted a study on the habitat preferences of Trillium recurvatum in central Illinois.
Gardeners admire Trillium recurvatum for its unusual, sessile flowers and attractive foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trillium - a genus of perennial flowering plants with three leaves and three petals

Etymology

From Latin: 'trillium' (a genus of plants), 'recurvatum' (curved back).
