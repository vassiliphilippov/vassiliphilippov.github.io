syncategoreme

1. NOUN

/ sɪnˌkætəɡəˈriːm / (AmE), / sɪnˌkætɪɡəˈriːm / (BrE)
A word or expression that does not function as a term on its own in a logical proposition, such as a quantifier, adverb, preposition, or conjunction.
word not usable alone in logic

Examples

Logical quantifiers are typical examples of syncategoremes in formal systems.
Unlike nouns and verbs, syncategoremes such as prepositions require other terms to complete their meaning.
The analysis focused on how syncategoremes function within the structure of logical propositions.

Synset Examples

logical quantifiers, adverbs, prepositions, and conjunctions are called syncategoremes

Etymology

From Medieval Latin syncategorema (something not predicable by itself), from Greek syn- (together) + katēgorēma (predicate).
