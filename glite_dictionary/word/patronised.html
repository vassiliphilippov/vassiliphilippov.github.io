patronised

1. ADJ

/ ˈpeɪ.trə.naɪzd / (AmE), / ˈpæ.trə.naɪzd / (BrE)
(of a business or service) having frequent customers or clients; regularly visited or supported by patrons.
having customers; regularly visited (British spelling)
british

Examples

The restaurant is one of the most patronised spots in town, especially on weekends.
A well-patronised café means steady business throughout the year.
The new library is now heavily patronised by local students.

Synset Examples

street full of flourishing well-patronized shops

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patronized - American spelling: having frequent customers or clients
  • Synonym: popular - liked or supported by many people; frequented

Etymology

From 'patron' (customer, supporter) + '-ise/-ize', meaning to act as a patron to; in this sense, referring to being the object of patronage.
