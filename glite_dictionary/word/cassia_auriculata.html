Cassia auriculata

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæsiə ɔːˌrɪkjʊˈleɪtə / (AmE), / ˌkæsiə ɔːˌrɪkjʊˈleɪtə / (BrE)
an evergreen Indian shrub with bright yellow flowers, whose bark is used in tanning; sometimes placed in the genus Cassia
Indian shrub with yellow flowers, used in tanning

Examples

The bark of Cassia auriculata is traditionally used in the leather tanning process in India.
Herbalists often recommend remedies made from Cassia auriculata for its medicinal properties.
In the wild, Cassia auriculata grows abundantly and displays striking yellow flowers throughout the year.

Etymology

From Latin 'Cassia', a genus name, and 'auriculata', meaning 'eared' (referring to the shape of the leaf base).
