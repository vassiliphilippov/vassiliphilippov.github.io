lenity

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛn.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˈlɛn.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
mercifulness or gentleness; the quality of being mild or tolerant in judgment or punishment
mercifulness or gentleness

Examples

The judge showed lenity by giving a reduced sentence to the defendant.
A spirit of lenity and forgiveness prevailed after the conflict ended.
Despite her authority, she ruled with remarkable lenity and kindness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mercy - compassion or forgiveness shown toward someone
  • Antonym: severity - the fact or condition of being severe

Etymology

From Latin 'lenitas' (gentleness, mildness), from 'lenis' (gentle, soft).
