Le Carre

1. PROPN

/ lə kɑːˈreɪ / (AmE), / lə ˈkaː.reɪ / (BrE)
the pen name of John le Carré (1931–2020), an English novelist known for his espionage fiction.
English author of espionage novels

Examples

Many critics consider Le Carre to be a master of the modern spy novel.
I have read every book by Le Carre and admire his intricate plots.
The works of Le Carre often explore moral ambiguity in the world of espionage.

Etymology

French for 'the square'; pen name adopted by David Cornwell.
