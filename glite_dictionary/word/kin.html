kin

1. NOUN

/ kɪn / (AmE), / kɪn / (BrE)
a group of people who are related to each other by blood or marriage; relatives
relatives; family group

Examples

Many people traveled from far away to attend the family reunion and see their kin.
She felt a deep sense of connection with her kin despite living in another country.
After the tragedy, neighbors and kin gathered to offer support to the family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relative - a person connected by blood or marriage
  • Antonym: unrelated - not connected by family or blood ties

Etymology

Old English cyn (family, race, kind), related to German Kind (child) and kin (race, family).

/ kɪn / (AmE), / kɪn / (BrE)

relatives; family members

Examples

It is important to respect your elders and your kin.

3. ADJ

/ kɪn / (AmE), / kɪn / (BrE)
Related by blood; sharing a common ancestry.
related by blood

Examples

Only kin are permitted to attend the private ceremony.
She felt a strong bond with her kin despite living far away from them.
Hospitals may grant visiting rights to close kin in emergencies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relative - a person related to another by blood or marriage
  • Antonym: unrelated - not related by blood or family

Etymology

From Old English cyn (family, race, kind), related to Old Norse kyn (family, race), Proto-Germanic *kunją.
