Boccaccio

1. PROPN

/ boʊˈkɑːtʃioʊ / (AmE), / bəˈkætʃiəʊ / (BrE)
Giovanni Boccaccio (1313–1375), an Italian poet and writer, best known for his work 'The Decameron'.
Italian poet and writer (1313–1375)

Examples

Boccaccio is celebrated for his influential collection of stories, 'The Decameron'.
During the Renaissance, Boccaccio played a key role in shaping Italian literature.
Many scholars have studied Boccaccio's works to understand medieval European society.

Etymology

Italian surname; Giovanni Boccaccio was a major figure of the Italian Renaissance.
