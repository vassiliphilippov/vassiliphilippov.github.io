city

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪti / (AmE), / ˈsɪti / (BrE)
A large and densely populated urban area, often with its own government and administrative systems.
large, densely populated urban area

Examples

New York is the largest city in the United States.
They moved from a small village to a bustling city for better job opportunities.
Each city has its unique culture and attractions.

Synset Examples

Ancient Troy was a great city

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: town - a human settlement smaller than a city
  • Degree Higher: metropolis - a very large and important city

Etymology

From Old French 'cite', from Latin 'civitas' (community, city, citizenship).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪti / (AmE), / ˈsɪti / (BrE)
An incorporated urban area with its own local government, typically larger than a town.
an incorporated urban area with local government

Examples

The city council approved funding for a new park downtown.
Living in a city offers many cultural opportunities and amenities.
The city recently upgraded its public transportation system.

Synset Examples

the city raised the tax rate

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: town - an urban area that is smaller than a city
  • Degree Higher: metropolis - a very large and densely populated city

Etymology

From Old French cité (city, town), from Latin civitas (citizenship, community).

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɪti / (AmE), / ˈsɪti / (BrE)
A large town; a place with a large population and particular legal status, often with its own local government.
large town or urban area

Examples

New York is the largest city in the United States.
After moving to the city, she missed the quiet of the countryside.
The city offers many cultural attractions and entertainment options.

Synset Examples

the city voted for Republicans in 1994

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: town - a smaller urban area than a city
  • Degree Higher: metropolis - a very large and important city

Etymology

From Old French *cite*, from Latin *civitas* (community, city, citizenship).
