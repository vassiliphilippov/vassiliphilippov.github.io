astatine

1. NOUN

/ ˈæstəˌtin / (AmE), / ˈastətiːn / (BrE)
a highly unstable radioactive chemical element (the heaviest of the halogen series), produced as a decay product of uranium and thorium; symbol: At
a highly unstable radioactive chemical element

Examples

Scientists produce astatine in minute quantities for research due to its extreme instability.
Because astatine is radioactive, it is rarely found in nature in significant amounts.
The element astatine is classified as the heaviest halogen on the periodic table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: halogen - any element of group 17 in the periodic table

Etymology

From Greek 'astatos' meaning 'unstable', plus the chemical suffix '-ine'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈæs.təˌtin / (AmE), / ˈast.ətiːn / (BrE)
a highly unstable radioactive chemical element of the halogen group, the heaviest known halogen, atomic number 85.
a radioactive chemical element (halogen, atomic number 85)

Examples

Astatine is so rare that only tiny amounts are found in nature.
Scientists have studied the properties of astatine to understand its behavior within the halogen group.
Unlike other halogens, astatine is highly radioactive and decays rapidly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: halogen - any of the elements in group 17 of the periodic table

Etymology

From Greek 'astatos' (unstable) + chemical suffix '-ine'.
