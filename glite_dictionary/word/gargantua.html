Gargantua

1. PROPN

/ ɡɑrˈɡæn.tʃu.ə / (AmE), / ɡɑːˈɡæn.tjuː.ə / (BrE)
A voracious giant, the main character in François Rabelais' satirical novel 'Gargantua', known for his enormous appetite and size.
a giant in Rabelais' novel

Examples

The novel tells the absurd adventures of Gargantua and his family.
His appetite rivaled that of Gargantua from the famous Renaissance satire.
Literary scholars often discuss Gargantua as a symbol of excess and humor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gargantuan - enormously large; gigantic; derived from Gargantua

Etymology

From the title character of French writer François Rabelais' work (1534), itself a playful formation suggesting 'gargantuan'.
