chirpy

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃɜːrpi / (AmE), / ˈtʃɜːpi / (BrE)
(of birds or insects) making short, high-pitched sounds; characterized by frequent chirping.
making high-pitched chirping sounds

Examples

The chirpy sparrows woke me up at dawn with their singing.
Outside, a chirpy cricket filled the garden with its music.
All morning, the forest was alive with chirpy birds in the trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheerful - happy and showing positive feelings

Etymology

From 'chirp' + '-y', 19th century formation.

2. ADJ

/ ˈtʃɜːrpi / (AmE), / ˈtʃɜːpi / (BrE)
cheerful and lively; noticeably happy and energetic.
cheerful and lively

Examples

She sounded chirpy on the phone this morning, full of excitement for her new job.
After a good night's sleep, he felt much more chirpy and optimistic about the day.
The children were chirpy as they got ready for the school field trip.

Synset Examples

buoyant spirits
his quick wit and chirpy humor
looking bright and well and chirpy
a perky little widow in her 70s

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheerful - happy and showing it
  • Antonym: gloomy - unhappy and without hope

Etymology

Imitative, from 'chirp' (sound made by small birds) plus the adjectival suffix '-y'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00364701-s
Synset POS: s
