Portunus puber

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔːr.tʃu.nəs ˈpjuː.bər / (AmE), / ˈpɔː.tjuː.nəs ˈpjuː.bə / (BrE)
a species of swimming crab found along the coasts of the British Isles and western Europe, known for its velvety shell and aggressive behavior
velvet swimming crab species

Examples

The Portunus puber is easily identified by the velvety texture of its shell.
Marine biologists conducted a study on the habitat of Portunus puber along the Cornish coast.
Fishermen often encounter Portunus puber when setting traps near rocky shorelines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: velvet crab - common English name for Portunus puber

Etymology

From Latin: genus Portunus (a kind of crab) + puber (hairy, downy).
