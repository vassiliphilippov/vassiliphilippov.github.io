Cyamopsis psoraloides

1. NOUN

/ saɪ.əˈmɑp.sɪs soʊˌrælˈɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / saɪ.əˈmɒp.sɪs səʊˌraːlˈɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A drought-tolerant herbaceous plant cultivated for animal forage and for its seeds, which yield guar gum used as a thickening agent or sizing material.
plant species producing guar gum

Examples

Cyamopsis psoraloides is widely cultivated in India for its valuable guar gum.
The seeds of Cyamopsis psoraloides are processed to make a popular thickening agent in the food industry.
Farmers appreciate Cyamopsis psoraloides for its ability to thrive in dry, arid soils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guar - alternative name for Cyamopsis psoraloides

Etymology

From botanical Latin; genus name 'Cyamopsis' and species epithet 'psoraloides', meaning 'resembling Psoralea'.
