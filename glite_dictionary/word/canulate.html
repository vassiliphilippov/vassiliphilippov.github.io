to canulate

1. VERB

/ ˈkæn.jə.leɪt / (AmE), / ˈkan.ju.leɪt / (BrE)
to insert a cannula or tube into a body cavity, duct, or vessel
insert a cannula or tube medically
medical

Examples

The nurse carefully canulated the patient's arm to start the IV drip.
It is important to canulate the vein with precision to avoid complications.
After the doctor successfully canulated the artery, the blood flow was monitored closely.

Synset Examples

Cannulate the blood vessel in the neck

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cannulate - alternative spelling: insert a cannula or tube into

Etymology

From Latin 'cannula' (small reed, tube), diminutive of 'canna' (reed).
