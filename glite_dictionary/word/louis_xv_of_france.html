Louis XV of France

1. PROPN

/ luːˈiː ðə ˈfɪfθ əv ˈfræns / (AmE), / luːˈiː ðə ˈfɪfθ əv ˈfrɑːns / (BrE)
the king of France from 1715 to 1774, grandson of Louis XIV, known for leading France during the War of the Austrian Succession and the Seven Years' War
King of France (1715–1774)

Examples

During his reign, Louis XV of France faced mounting financial problems that weakened the monarchy.
Historians debate whether Louis XV of France's foreign policies led to France's later difficulties.
The court of Louis XV of France was renowned for its luxury and intrigue.
