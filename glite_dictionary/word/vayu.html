Vayu

1. PROPN

/ ˈvɑːjuː / (AmE), / ˈvɑːjuː / (BrE)
In Hinduism, the god of wind, regarded as an important deity in the Vedic tradition.
Hindu god of wind

Examples

According to ancient scriptures, Vayu is responsible for the movement of the winds.
Many temples in India are dedicated to Vayu, the Hindu god of wind.
In Hindu mythology, Vayu plays a crucial role as the father of Hanuman.

Etymology

From Sanskrit वायु (vāyu), meaning 'wind' or 'air.'
