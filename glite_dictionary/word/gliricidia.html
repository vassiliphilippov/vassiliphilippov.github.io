gliricidia

1. NOUN

/ ɡlɪˌrɪsɪˈdiːə / (AmE), / ɡlɪˌrɪsɪˈdiːə / (BrE)
A genus of small deciduous trees native to Central America, valued for their durable dark wood and dense clusters of pink, nectar-rich flowers; all plant parts are poisonous.
genus of small poisonous flowering trees

Examples

Farmers often plant gliricidia along field edges as a living fence and to provide shade.
The dense racemes of pink flowers make gliricidia a favorite ornamental tree in tropical gardens.
All parts of the gliricidia tree are poisonous and should be handled with care.

Etymology

From Latinized form of Spanish 'gliricidia', from 'glires' (dormouse) and 'cida' (killer), referring to its rodenticidal properties.
