groundberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡraʊndˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊndˌbɛri / (BrE)
A small, low-growing shrub with scarlet flowers and succulent fruit resembling cranberries; sometimes classified in the genus Styphelia.
a low shrub with cranberry-like fruit

Examples

The bright fruit of the groundberry attracts many birds during the summer months.
She knelt to inspect the groundberry shrubs that grew wild throughout the sandy heathland.
Researchers are studying the medicinal properties of the succulent groundberry fruit.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡraʊndˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊndˌbɛri / (BrE)
A creeping shrub native to eastern North America, with white bell-shaped flowers, spicy red fruit, and aromatic leaves that produce wintergreen oil.
creeping North American shrub yielding wintergreen oil

Examples

The groundberry is valued for its aromatic leaves that are used to extract wintergreen oil.
Hikers came across a patch of groundberry plants with their distinctive red fruit.
Some indigenous peoples used groundberry leaves to make traditional herbal teas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wintergreen - a plant with aromatic leaves producing wintergreen oil
