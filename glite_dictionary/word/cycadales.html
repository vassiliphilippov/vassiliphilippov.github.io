Cycadales

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈkædəˌliz / (AmE), / sɪˈkædeɪliːz / (BrE)
An order of primitive tropical gymnosperm plants, once widespread during the Mesozoic era, now represented by a few species mainly in tropical regions.
order of ancient tropical gymnosperm plants

Examples

Many species within the Cycadales order are now endangered due to habitat loss.
Fossils show that Cycadales were abundant during the age of dinosaurs.
Some living members of Cycadales are prized as ornamental plants in tropical gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cycad - any plant of the family Cycadaceae, individual plants within Cycadales

Etymology

From New Latin, based on 'Cycad-' (genus Cycas) + '-ales' (denoting orders in botanical Latin).
