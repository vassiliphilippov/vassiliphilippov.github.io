teletypewriter

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛləˈtaɪpˌraɪtər / (AmE), / ˌtɛlɪˈtaɪpˌraɪtə / (BrE)
a device that prints typed messages transmitted over telegraph or telephone lines, operating much like a typewriter
telegraph-connected character printer

Examples

The message arrived on the teletypewriter just minutes after it was sent.
Before computers became common, news agencies used teletypewriter networks to communicate.
Repairing an old teletypewriter requires knowledge of both mechanical and electrical systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: teletype - a brand or shortened term for teletypewriter

Etymology

Formed from 'tele-' (distance), 'type', and 'writer', describing its function as a remote typewriter.
