erosiveness

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈroʊsɪvnəs / (AmE), / ɪˈrəʊsɪvnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of causing or tending to wear away or erode by gradual action.
quality of causing erosion

Examples

Scientists measure the erosiveness of different soil types to understand their vulnerability to rainfall.
The erosiveness of ocean waves has dramatically altered the coastline over the past century.
Reducing the erosiveness of water runoff is essential for protecting agricultural land from degradation.

Synset Examples

The erosiveness of rainfall is proportional to the energy of the falling drops and is influenced by the total amount of rain, the size of the drops, and their velocity of fall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: erosion - the process of being gradually worn away
  • Synonym: corrosiveness - quality of causing gradual destruction, especially by chemical action

Etymology

From 'erosive' + '-ness'. 'Erosive' from Latin 'erodere' (to gnaw away).
