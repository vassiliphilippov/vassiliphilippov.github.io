Soma

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊmə / (AmE), / ˈsəʊmə / (BrE)
the personification of a sacred intoxicating drink used in ancient Vedic rituals, regarded as a deity in Vedic religion
Vedic deity; sacred ritual drink

Examples

According to the Vedas, Soma granted immortality to the gods who drank it.
The ritual of offering Soma played a central role in ancient Vedic ceremonies.
Some scholars debate the botanical origins of Soma described in sacred Hindu texts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ambrosia - the food or drink of the gods in Greek mythology

Etymology

From Sanskrit सोम (soma), meaning 'extract' or 'pressed', referring to the drink.
