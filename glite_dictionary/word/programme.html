programme

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊɡræm / (AmE), / ˈprəʊɡræm / (BrE)
a set of planned activities or steps to achieve a particular aim or result
a set of planned steps or activities
british

Examples

The government announced a new programme to support small businesses.
Participants in the training programme will learn important job skills.
The cleanup programme includes several steps to reduce pollution in the river.

Synset Examples

they drew up a six-step plan
they discussed plans for a new bond issue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plan - a detailed proposal for achieving something
  • Synonym: scheme - a large-scale systematic plan or arrangement

Etymology

From French programme, from Latin programma (proclamation, edict), from Greek programma (proclamation, public notice).
