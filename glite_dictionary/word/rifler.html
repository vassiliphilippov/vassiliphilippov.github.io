rifler

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪflər / (AmE), / ˈraɪflə / (BrE)
a person skilled in the use of a rifle
person skilled with a rifle

Examples

The army recruited an experienced rifler to help train new soldiers.
He became a champion rifler after years of practice at the shooting range.
The rifler spotted his target from a considerable distance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rifleman - a soldier or person armed with a rifle

2. NOUN

/ ˈraɪfələr / (AmE), / ˈraɪflə / (BrE)
a soldier armed with a rifle, especially in military contexts
soldier armed with a rifle

Examples

The unit sent a skilled rifler ahead to scout the area.
During the Napoleonic Wars, the British army deployed several companies of riflers.
Each rifler carried special ammunition for his weapon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rifleman - a soldier armed with a rifle

Etymology

From 'rifle' + '-er', denoting a person who uses a rifle.
