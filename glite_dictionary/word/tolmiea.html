Tolmiea

1. NOUN

/ tɑlˈmiːə / (AmE), / tɒlˈmiːə / (BrE)
A genus of North American perennial herbs in the saxifrage family, containing a single species known as the pickaback plant.
genus of perennial herbs, pickaback plant

Examples

The Tolmiea thrives best in a cool, shaded greenhouse.
She placed a Tolmiea in her living room because of its interesting foliage.
Botanists studied the unique reproductive method of Tolmiea leaves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pickaback plant - a perennial herbaceous plant, sole species of Tolmiea

Etymology

Named after William Fraser Tolmie (1812–1886), a Scottish-born Canadian botanist.

2. NOUN

/ tɑlˈmiːə / (AmE), / tɒlˈmiːə / (BrE)
A genus of North American plants in the family Saxifragaceae, containing the pickaback plant.
genus of plants including pickaback plant

Examples

The Tolmiea genus is native to western North America.
Gardeners often cultivate Tolmiea for its unusual leaf structure.
Only one species is classified under the Tolmiea genus, the pickaback plant.

Etymology

Named after William Fraser Tolmie (1812–1886), a Scottish-Canadian physician and botanist.
