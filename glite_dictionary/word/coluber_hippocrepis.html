Coluber hippocrepis

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈluːbər ˌhɪp.oʊˈkrɛpɪs / (AmE), / kɒˈluːbə ˌhɪp.əˈkrɛpɪs / (BrE)
A slender, fast-moving snake found in Eurasia, also known as the horseshoe whip snake.
Eurasian horseshoe whip snake species

Examples

The Coluber hippocrepis is known for its remarkable speed and agility in the wild.
Scientists studied the habitat preferences of Coluber hippocrepis across southern Europe.
The distinctive horseshoe marks make Coluber hippocrepis easy to identify among similar snakes.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Coluber' meaning 'snake', 'hippocrepis' from Greek 'hippos' (horse) and 'krepis' (shoe), referring to the horseshoe-shaped markings.
