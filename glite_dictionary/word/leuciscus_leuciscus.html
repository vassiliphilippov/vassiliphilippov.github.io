Leuciscus leuciscus

1. NOUN

/ luˈsɪskəs luˈsɪskəs / (AmE), / ljuˈsɪskəs ljuˈsɪskəs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the dace, a small European freshwater fish with a slender bluish-green body
a small European freshwater fish (dace)

Examples

The river is home to a large population of Leuciscus leuciscus, commonly known as the dace.
Scientists observed the spawning behavior of Leuciscus leuciscus in the clear streams.
Anglers often seek out Leuciscus leuciscus because of its lively fight on the line.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dace - a small freshwater fish closely related to carp, common in Europe and Asia

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Leuciscus', from Greek 'leukiskos' (dace), diminutive of 'leukos' (white).
