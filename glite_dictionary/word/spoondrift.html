spoondrift

1. NOUN

/ ˈspunˌdrɪft / (AmE), / ˈspuːnˌdrɪft / (BrE)
Spray or fine mist blown from the surface of the sea by the wind.
sea spray blown by wind

Examples

The spoondrift sparkled in the morning sunlight as the waves crashed against the shore.
Sailors braced themselves as the spoondrift whipped over the deck during the storm.
The cliffs were veiled in spoondrift, making it hard to see the beach below.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spindrift - sea spray blown by wind

Etymology

From 'spoon' (to paddle or move swiftly) and 'drift' (driving or being driven), originally used in reference to sea spray.
