quidnunc

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɪd.nʌŋk / (AmE), / ˈkwɪd.nʌŋk / (BrE)
a person who is eager to know the latest news or gossip, often meddling in other people's affairs
an inquisitive gossip or busybody
archaic

Examples

The village quidnunc was always the first to spread rumors about her neighbors.
Every office seems to have a quidnunc who can't resist peeking into everyone's business.
Tired of the local quidnunc, she kept her personal matters private.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: busybody - a person who meddles in other people's affairs
  • Synonym: gossip - a person who likes talking about others' private lives

Etymology

From Latin 'quid nunc' meaning 'what now?'.
