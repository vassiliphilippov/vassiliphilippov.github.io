mead

1. NOUN

/ mid / (AmE), / miːd / (BrE)
An alcoholic drink made by fermenting honey with water, sometimes with added spices or fruit.
honey-based alcoholic drink

Examples

He poured himself a glass of mead to celebrate the festival.
Artisanal mead has become popular among craft beverage enthusiasts in recent years.
The banquet featured roast meats and mead served in wooden cups.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: beer - alcoholic drink made from barley, hops, and water
  • Degree Higher: wine - alcoholic drink made from fermented grape juice
  • Degree Lower: cider - alcoholic drink made from fermented apple juice

Etymology

Old English *medu*, from Proto-Germanic *meduz*, from Proto-Indo-European *médʰu* ('honey').
