genet

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛnɪt / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnɪt / (BrE)
A small, agile carnivorous mammal of the Old World with a spotted coat and a long, ringed tail, belonging to the family Viverridae.
small Old World carnivorous mammal

Examples

The genet slipped silently through the undergrowth, its spotted coat barely visible in the moonlight.
Several genets inhabit the rocky hillsides of southern Europe and northern Africa.
A genet uses its long, ringed tail for balance as it leaps between tree branches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: civet - a similar small, nocturnal mammal from the family Viverridae

Etymology

From French *genette*, from Medieval Latin *genetta*, likely from Catalan *geneta*.
