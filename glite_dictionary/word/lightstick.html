lightstick

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪtˌstɪk / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtˌstɪk / (BrE)
A small, portable stick that emits light, often used for illumination at concerts, parties, or as emergency lighting.
portable rod that emits light

Examples

She waved her lightstick during the concert, making a bright pattern in the dark crowd.
Emergency responders used a lightstick to mark the entrance in the darkness.
After cracking the lightstick, it glowed brightly for several hours.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glow stick - a similar item containing chemicals that produce light when mixed

Etymology

From 'light' and 'stick', referring to its shape and function.
