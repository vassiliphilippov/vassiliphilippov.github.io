ventilator

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɛn.t̬əˌleɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈvɛn.tɪ.leɪ.tə / (BrE)
a machine that helps a person breathe by moving air in and out of the lungs, typically used when they are unable to do so unaided
breathing-assistance machine
medical

Examples

The patient was placed on a ventilator to help him breathe after the surgery.
During the pandemic, hospitals faced a shortage of ventilators for critically ill patients.
She recovered well enough to be taken off the ventilator after several days.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: respirator - a device used to assist breathing, often used interchangeably with 'ventilator' in medical contexts

Etymology

From Latin 'ventilare' (to fan, agitate the air).

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɛn.tɪˌleɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈvɛn.tɪ.leɪ.tə / (BrE)
A device or machine designed to circulate air, especially by introducing fresh air or removing stale air from a space.
device for air circulation

Examples

The hospital installed a new ventilator system to improve air quality in the wards.
Please open the ventilator to let in some fresh air during the meeting.
The old warehouse relied on a large ventilator mounted in the ceiling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fan - device for producing a current of air
  • Synonym: air conditioner - device for cooling and circulating air

Etymology

From Latin 'ventilare' (to fan, agitate), via French 'ventilateur'.
