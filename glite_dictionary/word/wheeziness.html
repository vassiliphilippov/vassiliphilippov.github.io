wheeziness

1. NOUN

/ ˈwiːzi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈwiːzi.nəs / (BrE)
the condition of producing high-pitched, whistling sounds when breathing, typically due to narrowed or obstructed airways
high-pitched breathing sound
medical

Examples

The doctor noted the patient's wheeziness during the physical examination.
If you experience persistent wheeziness, you should seek medical attention.
Her asthma attacks were often preceded by a period of mild wheeziness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wheeze - a high-pitched whistling sound during breathing

Etymology

From 'wheeze' (to breathe with a whistling sound) + '-iness' (suffix forming nouns denoting states or qualities).
