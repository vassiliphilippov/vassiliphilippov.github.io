dishonourable

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈɑnərəbəl / (AmE), / dɪsˈɒnərəbəl / (BrE)
Lacking honor or integrity; deserving of shame or disgrace.
lacking honor or integrity
british

Examples

He was dismissed from his position for his dishonourable conduct.
Many considered her actions dishonourable and unworthy of her title.
Cheating in the competition was seen as a deeply dishonourable act.

Synset Examples

dishonorable in thought and deed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: honourable - having or showing honor and integrity
  • Synonym: dishonorable - lacking honor or integrity (American spelling)

Etymology

From Old French 'deshonorable', from Latin 'dishonorabilis' (disgraceful, shameful).
