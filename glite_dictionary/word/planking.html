planking

1. NOUN

/ ˈplæŋkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈplæŋkɪŋ / (BrE)
(nautical) A structure or surface consisting of planks, especially as a covering or flooring on a ship.
planks forming a ship's surface or flooring

Examples

The old ship's planking had rotted and needed to be replaced.
Workers spent weeks repairing the planking along the hull.
Water seeped through the gaps in the planking after the storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decking - planks forming the deck of a ship or a platform

Etymology

From 'plank' + '-ing', denoting material made of planks.

2. NOUN

/ ˈplæŋkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈplæŋkɪŋ / (BrE)
a collection or quantity of planks, especially when used as a material in construction or carpentry
a collection of planks

Examples

The old ship's deck was made entirely of planking.
We need more planking for the new floor in the hallway.
After the renovation began, piles of planking were stacked beside the house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plank - a long, flat piece of timber or board

3. NOUN

/ ˈplæŋkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈplæŋkɪŋ / (BrE)
the process or work of covering an area, such as a floor or the sides of a ship, with planks of wood
work of covering with planks

Examples

The carpenters finished the planking of the ship's hull last week.
Proper planking ensures the floor remains stable and even.
Restoring the old house required extensive planking of the damaged floorboards.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decking - the process of covering a surface with planks, especially as a floor or outdoor feature

Etymology

From 'plank' + '-ing', indicating the action or process of using planks.
