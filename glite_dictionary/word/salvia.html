salvia

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæl.vi.ə / (AmE), / ˈsal.vɪ.ə / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Salvia, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs, some used for medicinal or culinary purposes.
an aromatic herb or shrub of the genus Salvia

Examples

Salvia is often grown in gardens for its colorful flowers and aromatic leaves.
Some species of salvia are used in traditional medicine.
The gardener recommended planting salvia to attract bees and butterflies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sage - an aromatic herb, Salvia officinalis, used for culinary and medicinal purposes

Etymology

From Latin *salvia* (sage), related to *salvus* (safe, healthy).
