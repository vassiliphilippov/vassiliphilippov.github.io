Gerardia

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈrɑrdiə / (AmE), / dʒəˈrɑːdɪə / (BrE)
a genus of annual or perennial herbs with showy pink, purple, or yellow flowers, often reclassified under the genera Aureolaria or Agalinis
genus of flowering herbs

Examples

Several species formerly classified as Gerardia are now placed in the genus Agalinis.
The Gerardia is known for its vibrant pink and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.
Botanists used to collect Gerardia along the riverbanks for study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Aureolaria - a genus to which some Gerardia species are reassigned
  • Synonym: Agalinis - a genus to which some Gerardia species are reassigned

Etymology

Named after John Gerard (1545–1612), English botanist.
