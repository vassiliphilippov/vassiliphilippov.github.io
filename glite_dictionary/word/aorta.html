aorta

1. NOUN

/ eɪˈɔrtə / (AmE), / eɪˈɔːtə / (BrE)
the main artery in the body, carrying blood from the heart's left ventricle to the rest of the body
main artery carrying blood from the heart
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully repaired the damaged aorta during the operation.
A blockage in the aorta can be life-threatening and requires immediate attention.
The aorta is the largest artery in the human body.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artery - a blood vessel that carries blood away from the heart

Etymology

From Latin *aorta*, from Ancient Greek *aortē* (main artery).
