ignominy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪɡ.nəˌmɪn.i / (AmE), / ɪɡˈnɒm.ɪ.ni / (BrE)
public shame or disgrace; a state of dishonor or humiliation
public shame or disgrace

Examples

He feared the ignominy that would follow if his secret were revealed.
After the scandal, she lived in ignominy for many years.
The team's defeat brought ignominy to a proud city.

Synset Examples

one mistake brought shame to all his family
suffered the ignominy of being sent to prison

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disgrace - loss of reputation or respect; shame
  • Antonym: honor - high respect; esteem or privilege

Etymology

From Latin ignominia (disgrace, dishonor), from in- (not) + nomen (name, reputation).
