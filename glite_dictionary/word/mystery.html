mystery

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪstəri / (AmE), / ˈmɪstəri / (BrE)
something that is difficult or impossible to understand or explain
something hard to explain or understand

Examples

The cause of the sudden illness remains a mystery to the doctors.
For years, the disappearance of the ship was shrouded in mystery.
It’s a mystery to me how he managed to escape unnoticed.

Synset Examples

how it got out is a mystery
it remains one of nature's secrets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enigma - a person or thing that is mysterious or difficult to understand
  • Synonym: secret - something kept hidden or unexplained
  • Antonym: solution - an answer that explains or makes something clear

Etymology

From Latin 'mysterium' (secret rite), from Greek 'mystērion'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪstəri / (AmE), / ˈmɪstəri / (BrE)
a novel, play, or film whose plot involves solving a crime or uncovering secrets, especially a murder
crime story, novel, or film

Examples

She loves reading mystery novels late at night.
The new film is a gripping mystery about the disappearance of a famous painter.
Agatha Christie wrote many classic mystery stories featuring Hercule Poirot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detective story - a story about a detective solving a crime
  • Degree Lower: thriller - a suspenseful novel, play, or film
