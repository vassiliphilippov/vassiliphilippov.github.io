Megatherium

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛɡəˈθɪriəm / (AmE), / ˌmiːɡəˈθɪəriəm / (BrE)
an extinct genus of giant ground sloths that lived in South America during the Pleistocene epoch
extinct genus of giant ground sloths

Examples

The Megatherium was one of the largest land mammals of its time, weighing up to four tonnes.
Fossils of Megatherium have been unearthed in Argentina, providing insight into ancient ecosystems.
Scientists believe that Megatherium became extinct due to climate change and human activity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sloth - a slow-moving mammal found in Central and South America

Etymology

From Greek mega- (great) + therion (beast)

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛɡəˈθɪriəm / (AmE), / ˌmɛɡəˈθɪəriəm / (BrE)
a genus of extinct giant ground sloths that lived from the Early Pliocene to the end of the Pleistocene in South America
extinct genus of giant ground sloths

Examples

Fossils of Megatherium have been discovered throughout South America.
Scientists estimate that Megatherium could reach a length of up to six meters.
The extinction of Megatherium likely coincided with the arrival of humans in the region.

Etymology

From Greek mega (great) + thērion (beast).
