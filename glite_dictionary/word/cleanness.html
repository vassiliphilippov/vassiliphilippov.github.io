cleanness

1. NOUN

/ ˈklin.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkliːn.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being clean, free from dirt, impurities, or contamination
the state of being clean

Examples

The cleanness of the hotel room impressed the guests immediately.
Personal cleanness is important for good health and well-being.
He took pride in the cleanness of his workspace, making sure everything was spotless.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cleanliness - the practice or state of being clean
  • Antonym: dirtiness - the state of being dirty or unclean

Etymology

From Old English 'clænnys', from 'clæne' (clean) + '-ness' (state, quality).

2. NOUN

/ ˈklin.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkliːn.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of being free from moral defects or impurity; moral purity.
moral purity; freedom from moral defects

Examples

He admired her cleanness of heart and sincerity in all matters.
The preacher spoke of the importance of cleanness in one's thoughts and actions.
Many ancient texts praise the cleanness of a soul unmarked by dishonesty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purity - the quality of being morally pure or without sin
  • Antonym: sinfulness - the quality of being morally wrong or wicked

Etymology

From Old English 'clǣnnes', from 'clǣne' meaning clean + '-ness'.
