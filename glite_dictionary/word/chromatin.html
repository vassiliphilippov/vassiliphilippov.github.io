chromatin

1. NOUN

/ ˈkroʊ.mə.tɪn / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊ.mə.tɪn / (BrE)
the substance within a cell nucleus, consisting of DNA, RNA, and proteins, that condenses to form chromosomes during cell division
substance in cell nucleus of DNA, RNA, and proteins
medical

Examples

The chromatin in the cell nucleus becomes visible under a microscope during mitosis.
Researchers studied how chemicals can affect chromatin structure and gene expression.
Changes in chromatin organization can influence cellular development and disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chromosome - thread-like structure of nucleic acids and protein carrying genetic information

Etymology

From Greek 'chroma' (color) + '-in' (chemical suffix), referring to its staining properties.
