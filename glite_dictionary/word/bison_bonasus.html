Bison bonasus

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪsən boʊˈneɪsəs / (AmE), / ˌbaɪsən bəˈneɪsəs / (BrE)
The European bison, a large wild bovine native to Europe, distinguished by its smaller and higher-set head compared to the North American bison.
European bison species

Examples

The Bison bonasus once roamed freely across European forests but is now mainly found in reserves.
Conservationists are working to increase the population of Bison bonasus in Eastern Europe.
Visitors to the national park were excited to spot a herd of Bison bonasus grazing in the meadow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bison - A large, humpbacked wild ox, including both European and North American species
  • Antonym: American bison - The North American species of bison

Etymology

From Latin, with 'bison' meaning wild ox and 'bonasus', an ancient term for the European bison.
