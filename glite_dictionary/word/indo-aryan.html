Indo-Aryan

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪndoʊˈɛriən / (AmE), / ˌɪndəʊˈɛəriən / (BrE)
of or relating to the Indo-Aryan branch of the Indo-European peoples, languages, or cultures, especially those originating in South Asia.
relating to Indo-Aryan peoples or languages

Examples

The Indo-Aryan languages are spoken by millions across South Asia.
Ancient texts were written in early Indo-Aryan dialects.
Scholars study Indo-Aryan migration patterns to understand the region's history.

Synset Examples

Indo-European migrations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Indo-European - relating to the Indo-European language family
  • Antonym: Dravidian - relating to the Dravidian peoples or languages of South India

Etymology

From Indo- (India) + Aryan (from Sanskrit ārya, 'noble').

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪndoʊˈɛriən / (AmE), / ˌɪndəʊˈɛəriən / (BrE)
a branch of the Indo-Iranian family of languages, including Hindi, Bengali, Punjabi, and others spoken mainly in South Asia
branch of Indo-Iranian languages

Examples

Hindi and Bengali are both Indo-Aryan languages spoken by millions of people.
The Indo-Aryan branch is one of the largest subgroups of the Indo-European language family.
Many Indo-Aryan tongues share common grammatical structures and vocabulary.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Indo-Iranian - the larger language family to which Indo-Aryan belongs

Etymology

From 'Indo-' (relating to India) + 'Aryan', a linguistic and ethnic designation.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06984750-n
Synset POS: n
