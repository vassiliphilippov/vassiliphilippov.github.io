uphill

1. ADJ

/ ʌpˈhɪl / (AmE), / ʌpˈhɪl / (BrE)
(of a road, path, or surface) sloping upward.
sloping upward

Examples

The runners struggled along the uphill path to the finish line.
Cycling became much harder when the road turned uphill.
She prefers the uphill climb because it's more of a challenge.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: downhill - sloping downward
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02494208-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˈʌpˌhɪl / (AmE), / ˈʌp.hɪl / (BrE)
An upward slope or incline, especially of a hill or road.
an upward slope of a hill or road

Examples

The cyclists struggled as they rode up the uphill toward the summit.
After a long uphill, we finally reached the top of the trail.
That section of the race includes a steep uphill that challenges even experienced runners.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: downhill - the downward slope of a hill
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09489808-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADV

/ ˈʌpˌhɪl / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌhɪl / (BrE)
in an upward direction on a hill or slope
upward on a hill or slope

Examples

We had to walk uphill for nearly an hour to reach the waterfall.
The road winds uphill through dense forest before reaching the summit.
Cyclists find the route especially challenging when heading uphill.

Synset Examples

this street lay uphill

Related Terms

  • Antonym: downhill - in a downward direction on a hill or slope
