Inula helenium

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnjuˈleɪniəm / (AmE), / ˌɪnjuːlə ˈhɛliːniəm / (BrE)
A tall, coarse Eurasian herb with yellow daisylike flowers, whose roots are used medicinally; also called elecampane.
a tall medicinal Eurasian herb

Examples

The roots of Inula helenium have been used in traditional herbal medicine for centuries.
Gardeners appreciate Inula helenium for its tall stature and showy yellow flowers.
Researchers are studying the health benefits of extracts from Inula helenium roots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elecampane - common name for Inula helenium, a medicinal plant

Etymology

From the Latin 'inula' (the plant name) and 'helenium' (thought to be named after Helen of Troy).
