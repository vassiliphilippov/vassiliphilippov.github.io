arum

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛərəm / (AmE), / ˈɑːrəm / (BrE)
A starch similar to sago, obtained from the root of the cuckoopint plant.
starch from cuckoopint root

Examples

In some regions, arum was traditionally used as a thickener in soups and stews.
The chef experimented with arum to create a unique texture in his dessert.
People once collected arum from wild plants before commercial starches became widely available.

Etymology

From Latin 'arum', from Greek 'aron', plant name.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛr.əm / (AmE), / ˈɑː.rəm / (BrE)
A plant of the family Araceae, typically having small flowers arranged on a spadix and surrounded by a large, often colorful spathe.
plant of the arum family (Araceae)

Examples

The woodland arum is known for its distinctive, hood-like spathe.
She planted several arums in the shade garden to add exotic foliage.
Many species in the arum family are prized for their unusual flowers.

Etymology

From Latin 'arum', from Greek 'aron', referring to the arum lily.
