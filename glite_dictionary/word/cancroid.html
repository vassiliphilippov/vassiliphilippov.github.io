cancroid

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæŋ.krɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈkan.krɔɪd / (BrE)
A carcinoma of the skin; the most common form of skin cancer, typically appearing as a malignant ulcer or growth.
a type of skin cancer
medical

Examples

The dermatologist diagnosed the lesion as a cancroid after a biopsy.
Early detection of cancroid increases the likelihood of successful treatment.
He underwent surgery to remove a cancroid from his scalp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carcinoma - a malignant tumor arising from epithelial tissue

Etymology

From Greek 'kankrōdes' meaning 'cancer-like'.

/ ˈkæŋ.krɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈkaŋ.krɔɪd / (BrE)
relating to or resembling a cancroid, especially in medical contexts.
resembling a cancroid (cancer-like)
medical

Examples

The lesion was examined and found to have cancroid characteristics under the microscope.
A cancroid growth must be diagnosed and treated quickly to prevent metastasis.
