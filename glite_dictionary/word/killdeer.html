killdeer

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪldɪr / (AmE), / ˈkɪldɪə / (BrE)
a North American plover (Charadrius vociferus) commonly found near inland waters and fields, known for its distinctive cry
North American plover with a distinctive cry

Examples

We saw a killdeer running across the gravel driveway, calling loudly as it tried to distract us from its nest.
The high-pitched cry of the killdeer is often heard near lakes and open fields in spring.
A flock of killdeer landed by the pond, their brown and white plumage blending with the shoreline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plover - a type of wading bird with a rounded head, short bill, and long legs

Etymology

Imitative of its call, 'kill-deer'.
