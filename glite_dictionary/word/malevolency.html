malevolency

1. NOUN

/ məˈlɛv.ə.lən.si / (AmE), / məˈlɛv.əl.ən.si / (BrE)
the state or quality of having or showing a desire to cause harm or evil to others
quality of wishing harm or evil

Examples

Her malevolency was evident in the way she plotted against her rivals.
They were driven away by the malevolency that poisoned the atmosphere of the meeting.
Legends described the old castle as haunted by a spirit of pure malevolency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: malevolence - the quality of wishing evil or harm to others
  • Antonym: benevolence - the quality of being well-meaning or kindly

Etymology

Formed from 'malevolent' (from Latin malevolus: 'ill-wishing') + '-cy' suffix.
