CD

1. NOUN

/ siː ˈdiː / (AmE), / siː ˈdiː / (BrE)
a small, round optical disc on which digital audio, video, or other data is stored and that is played or read using a laser
compact disc for digital data or audio

Examples

She put the new CD into the player and listened to her favorite songs.
Many artists now release their albums both as digital downloads and on CD.
My collection includes over a hundred CDs from various genres.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compact disc - synonym for 'CD'; an optical disc for digital storage
  • Antonym: vinyl record - an older analog audio storage medium

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'compact disc', introduced in the early 1980s by Philips and Sony.

2. NOUN

/ siː ˈdiː / (AmE), / siː ˈdiː / (BrE)
a certificate of deposit; a financial product offered by banks that pays interest on money deposited for a fixed period
certificate of deposit (banking)

Examples

She invested her savings in a five-year CD to earn higher interest.
The bank's new CD rates are very competitive this year.
When the CD matures, you can withdraw the money along with the interest earned.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: certificate of deposit - a savings certificate with a fixed maturity date and interest rate

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'certificate of deposit'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13386310-n
Synset POS: n
