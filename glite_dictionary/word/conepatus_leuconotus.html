Conepatus leuconotus

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈnɛp.ə.təs luːˈkɒn.ə.təs / (AmE), / kəˈnɛp.ətəs ljuː.kəˈnəʊ.təs / (BrE)
A large hog-nosed skunk with a white back and tail, native to southwestern North America and Mexico.
a large white-backed hog-nosed skunk

Examples

The Conepatus leuconotus is often spotted in desert regions at night.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of the Conepatus leuconotus for conservation purposes.
Farmers sometimes encounter the Conepatus leuconotus digging for insects in their fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hog-nosed skunk - a skunk species with a pig-like snout

Etymology

From Latin; 'Conepatus' is the genus name, 'leuconotus' comes from Greek 'leukos' (white) and 'notos' (back).
