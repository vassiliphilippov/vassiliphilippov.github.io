periarteritis

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛriˌɑːrˈtɛrɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌpɛriˌɑːˈtɛr.ɪ.tɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of the outer layer of an artery
inflammation of outer artery layer
medical

Examples

The biopsy confirmed the presence of periarteritis in the affected tissue.
Chronic periarteritis can lead to significant complications if left untreated.
Doctors found signs of periarteritis during the examination of the patient's artery.

Etymology

From Greek 'peri-' (around) + 'arteria' (artery) + '-itis' (inflammation).
