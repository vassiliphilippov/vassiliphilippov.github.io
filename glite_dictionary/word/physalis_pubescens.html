Physalis pubescens

1. NOUN

/ fɪˈseɪlɪs pjuˈbɛsəns / (AmE), / fɪˈseɪlɪs pjuˈbɛsns / (BrE)
A species of annual plant native to the Americas, having round, fleshy yellow berries enclosed in a bladder-like husk; grown for decoration or fruit.
annual plant with yellow berries in husk

Examples

The Physalis pubescens added a splash of color to her summer garden.
Chefs sometimes use Physalis pubescens berries as a garnish for desserts.
He studied the unique husk that surrounds the fruit of Physalis pubescens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ground cherry - plant with edible berries enclosed in a papery husk
  • Synonym: Physalis peruviana - closely related species often called Cape gooseberry

Etymology

From Latin: Physalis (a plant genus) + pubescens, meaning 'downy' or 'covered with fine hairs'.
