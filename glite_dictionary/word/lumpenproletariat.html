lumpenproletariat

1. NOUN

/ ˌlʌmpənproʊləˈtɛriət / (AmE), / ˌlʌmpənprəʊlɪˈtɛːrɪət / (BrE)
(in Marxist theory) the lowest stratum of the working class, comprising individuals who are unemployed or marginalised and are not interested in or involved in revolutionary activity.
the lowest stratum of the working class

Examples

In his analysis, Marx argued that the lumpenproletariat lacked the class consciousness necessary for revolution.
Some regimes have used the lumpenproletariat as a tool to suppress political dissent.
The novel depicts the struggles of the lumpenproletariat living on the fringes of society.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: proletariat - the working class, especially in Marxist theory

Etymology

From German *Lumpenproletariat*, combining 'Lumpen' (rag, rogue) and 'Proletariat' (working class).
