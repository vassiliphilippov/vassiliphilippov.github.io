Eastertide

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːstərˌtaɪd / (AmE), / ˈiːstətaɪd / (BrE)
the period of the Christian year beginning with Easter and lasting until Pentecost, celebrating the resurrection of Jesus Christ.
the Easter season in Christianity

Examples

During Eastertide, many churches hold special services and celebrations.
The choir prepared a beautiful anthem to perform throughout Eastertide.
Pilgrims traveled to sacred sites during Eastertide to mark the holy season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Easter - the festival celebrating the resurrection of Jesus Christ

Etymology

From 'Easter' + 'tide' (Old English 'tīd' meaning 'time, season').
