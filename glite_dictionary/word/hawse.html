hawse

1. NOUN

/ hɔz / (AmE), / hɔːz / (BrE)
the opening in a ship's bow through which the anchor cable passes
hole for anchor cable on a ship

Examples

After the anchor was raised, seawater still dripped from the hawse at the bow.
An obstruction in the hawse can prevent the anchor chain from running smoothly.
Before setting sail, the crew checked the hawse for signs of rust or damage.

Etymology

From Middle English 'halse', from Old Norse 'hálss' (neck).
