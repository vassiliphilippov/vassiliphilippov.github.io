octavo

1. NOUN

/ ɑkˈteɪvoʊ / (AmE), / ɒkˈtɑːvəʊ / (BrE)
a book or page size made by folding a standard printing sheet three times to form eight leaves (sixteen pages)
book size made by folding a sheet into eight leaves

Examples

The rare book collection includes several first-edition octavos from the eighteenth century.
She prefers the compact size of an octavo for reading during her commute.
This volume was printed in octavo, making it smaller and easier to handle than a folio.

Etymology

From Latin 'octavus' meaning 'eighth', referring to the eight leaves produced by folding.
