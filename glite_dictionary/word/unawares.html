unawares

1. ADV

/ ˌʌnəˈwɛrz / (AmE), / ˌʌnəˈwɛəz / (BrE)
without warning; suddenly and unexpectedly
suddenly and unexpectedly

Examples

The heavy snowstorm took the hikers unawares as they crossed the mountain pass.
We were all caught unawares by the abrupt change in policy.
The announcement arrived unawares, leaving everyone in the office surprised.

Synset Examples

rain caught them unawares
sorrow comes to all, and to the young it comes with bittered agony because it takes them unawares

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unexpectedly - in a way not anticipated or predicted
  • Synonym: suddenly - quickly and without warning

Etymology

From 'unaware' (not aware) + adverbial -s, patterned after older English usage.

2. ADV

/ ˌʌnəˈwɛrz / (AmE), / ˌʌnəˈwɛəz / (BrE)
without being aware of something; unexpectedly or by surprise
unexpectedly; by surprise

Examples

She stumbled unawares into the middle of the heated debate.
The storm caught the hikers unawares while they were setting up camp.
He was taken unawares by the surprise birthday party his friends organized.

Synset Examples

came upon the diamond unawares

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unexpectedly - in a way that is not expected

Etymology

From un- (not) + aware, patterned after Old French en awarés (unawares, off one's guard), with the adverbial -s ending.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00486012-r
Synset POS: r
