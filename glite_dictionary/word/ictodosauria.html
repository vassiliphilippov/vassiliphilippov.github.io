Ictodosauria

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˌtɑːdoʊˈsɔːriə / (AmE), / ɪkˌtəʊdəʊˈsɔːrɪə / (BrE)
an order or suborder of extinct reptiles from the Late Triassic period, characterized by certain primitive features and considered significant in the evolution of early reptiles
order of extinct Late Triassic reptiles

Examples

Fossil remains of Ictodosauria provide clues about reptile evolution during the Triassic period.
Scientists believe that Ictodosauria filled a unique ecological niche in prehistoric aquatic environments.
The study of Ictodosauria has helped paleontologists understand the diversity of Triassic reptiles.

Etymology

From Greek 'ichthys' (fish) and 'sauros' (lizard), reflecting their lizard-like appearance and aquatic adaptations.
