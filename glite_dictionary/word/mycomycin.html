mycomycin

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪkəˈmaɪsɪn / (AmE), / ˌmʌɪkəˈmʌɪsɪn / (BrE)
a highly unsaturated antibiotic acid obtained from a species of actinomycete, used in certain medical applications
antibiotic acid from an actinomycete
medical

Examples

Researchers discovered that mycomycin is effective against several bacterial strains.
The production of mycomycin relies on fermentation by actinomycetes in a controlled environment.
Doctors once prescribed mycomycin for certain infections before newer antibiotics became available.

Etymology

From 'myco-' (relating to fungi) + 'mycin' (antibiotic suffix, from Greek 'mykes' meaning fungus).
