Hagiographa

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæɡiˈɑːɡrəfə / (AmE), / ˌhæɡiˈɒɡrəfə / (BrE)
the third division of the Hebrew Scriptures, comprising books such as Psalms, Proverbs, and Job, also known as the Writings.
third division of the Hebrew Scriptures

Examples

The Hagiographa contain some of the most poetic books in the Hebrew Scriptures.
Scholars often analyze the structure and influence of the Hagiographa in biblical studies.
She studied the Psalms, Proverbs, and Job, all part of the Hagiographa.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ketuvim - the Hebrew term for the Writings, the third part of the Hebrew Bible

Etymology

From Greek *hagiographa* (sacred writings), from *hagio-* (holy) + *graphein* (to write).
