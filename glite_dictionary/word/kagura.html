kagura

1. NOUN

/ kɑːˈɡuːrə / (AmE), / kəˈɡuːrə / (BrE)
A traditional Japanese ceremonial dance associated with the Shinto religion, often performed at festivals to honor the gods.
Japanese Shinto ceremonial dance

Examples

The shrine's celebration featured a graceful kagura performance in honor of the local deities.
Many Japanese festivals include kagura to invoke blessings and good harvests.
He studied the symbolic movements of kagura to better understand Shinto rituals.

Synset Examples

Kagura, in particular those forms that involve storytelling or reenactment of fables, is also one of the primary influences on the Noh theatre.

Etymology

Japanese 神楽 (kagura), from 神 (kami, 'god') + 楽 (gaku, 'music').
