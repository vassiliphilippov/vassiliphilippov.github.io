Phalacrocoracidae

1. NOUN

/ fælækɹoʊˈkɔɹəsɪˌdi / (AmE), / fæləkɹəʊˈkɒɹəsɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of aquatic birds commonly known as cormorants, characterized by their long necks, hooked bills, and diving abilities.
the cormorant bird family

Examples

The Phalacrocoracidae family includes several species found in coastal regions around the world.
Many ornithologists study the diving techniques unique to Phalacrocoracidae.
A new field guide details the global diversity of Phalacrocoracidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cormorant - a large diving bird of the family Phalacrocoracidae

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek phalakros (bald) and korax (raven), referring to the birds' dark plumage.
