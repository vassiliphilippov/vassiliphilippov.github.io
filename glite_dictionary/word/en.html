en

1. NOUN

/ ɛn / (AmE), / ɛn / (BrE)
A unit of measurement in typography equal to half the width of an em.
typographic unit equal to half an em

Examples

The width of the space was set to one en for clarity in the text.
Traditional typographers often use en dashes to indicate ranges.
Print designers must understand the difference between an em and an en.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: em - a typographic unit equal to the point size of the type

Etymology

From the name of the letter 'N', patterned after 'em', the name of the letter 'M', used in typographic measurement.
