Biu-Mandara

1. NOUN

/ ˌbiːuː mænˈdɑːrə / (AmE), / ˌbiːuː mænˈdɑːrə / (BrE)
a group of Chadic languages spoken in the border region between Cameroon and Nigeria, south of Lake Chad
group of Chadic languages in Cameroon and Nigeria

Examples

The Biu-Mandara languages are spoken by several ethnic groups in northern Cameroon.
Linguists have studied the Biu-Mandara branch to understand the history of Chadic languages.
She is fluent in one of the Biu-Mandara dialects spoken near Lake Chad.

Etymology

From the names of central languages in the group: Biu and Mandara.
