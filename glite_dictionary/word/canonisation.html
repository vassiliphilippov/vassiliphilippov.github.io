canonisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæn.ə.nəˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkan.ə.naɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
(in the Roman Catholic and Eastern Orthodox Churches) the official process of declaring that a deceased person is a saint.
official declaration of sainthood

Examples

After decades of advocacy, Mother Teresa's canonisation was celebrated by thousands at the Vatican.
The process of canonisation can take many years and requires careful investigation of miracles.
Many Christians around the world rejoiced at the canonisation of the beloved bishop.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: beatification - declaration by the Pope that a deceased person is blessed and may be venerated

Etymology

From Medieval Latin canonizatio, from canonizare (to canonise).
