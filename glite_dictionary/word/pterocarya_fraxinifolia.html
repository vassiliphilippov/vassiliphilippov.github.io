Pterocarya fraxinifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛrəˈkɛəriə ˌfræksɪnɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˌtɛrəˈkeəriə ˌfræksɪnɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
a medium-sized, much-branched tree native to the Caucasus, distinguished by its winged fruit, also known as the Caucasian wingnut
Caucasian wingnut tree species

Examples

The Pterocarya fraxinifolia is often planted in parks for its distinctive winged seeds.
Botanists studied the leaf patterns of Pterocarya fraxinifolia in the Caucasus region.
A mature Pterocarya fraxinifolia can provide ample shade due to its spreading branches.

Etymology

From Greek pteron (wing) + karyon (nut) and Latin fraxinifolia (ash-leaved).
