scanty

1. NOUN

/ ˈskænti / (AmE), / ˈskænti / (BrE)
Short underpants for women or children.
short underpants; women's or children's

Examples

She packed her favorite scanty in her suitcase for the trip.
The shop displayed colorful scanties in the lingerie section.
As a child, she wore cotton scanty during the summer months.

Etymology

From the adjective 'scanty', meaning 'meager' or 'bare', applied to clothing due to its minimal coverage.

2. ADJ

/ ˈskænti / (AmE), / ˈskænti / (BrE)
insufficient in quantity or amount; barely enough
insufficient; barely enough

Examples

After a long, hot summer, the farmers were disappointed by the scanty harvest.
Her scanty savings were barely enough to pay the bills.
The room was furnished in a scanty manner, with only a bed and a chair.

Synset Examples

a bare livelihood
a scanty harvest
a spare diet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meager - lacking in quantity or quality
  • Antonym: abundant - present in large quantities; more than enough
  • Synonym: sparse - thinly dispersed or scattered; not dense

Etymology

From Old Norse 'skamt', meaning 'short' or 'scant'.

3. ADJ

/ ˈskæn.ti / (AmE), / ˈskæn.ti / (BrE)
(of clothing) barely sufficient to cover the body; revealing more of the body than is usual or considered appropriate.
revealing or insufficient clothing

Examples

She wore a scanty bikini to the pool, turning many heads.
The actor's scanty costume was considered too daring for television.
Some schools have strict dress codes that prohibit overly scanty outfits.

Synset Examples

her dress was scanty and revealing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skimpy - (of clothing) very small and revealing
  • Antonym: modest - (of clothing) not revealing or indecent

Etymology

From Latin 'scantus', past participle of 'scandere' (to climb); via Old Norse 'skamt', meaning 'shortness'.
