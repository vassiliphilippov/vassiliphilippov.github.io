to vary

1. VERB

/ ˈvɛri / (AmE), / ˈveəri / (BrE)
to change something so that it is more diverse or different; to introduce variety; to modify or alter slightly
make or become more diverse or different

Examples

The chef likes to vary the menu each week to keep things interesting.
Teachers often vary their lessons to maintain student engagement.
You can vary the recipe by adding different spices or vegetables.

Synset Examples

Vary the menu

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to diversify - to make more varied or diverse
  • Antonym: to standardize - to make things uniform or the same

Etymology

From Latin 'variare' (to change, make different), from 'varius' (varied).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈvɛri / (AmE), / ˈveəri / (BrE)
to change or differ depending on the circumstances or the variable involved
to change or differ based on variables

Examples

Temperatures vary greatly between summer and winter in this region.
The amount of rainfall can vary from year to year.
His interests vary depending on his mood.

Synset Examples

Prices vary
His moods vary depending on the weather

Related Terms

  • Synonym: change - to become different; to undergo alteration
  • Antonym: to remain - to stay the same, to not change

Etymology

From Latin variare (to change), from varius (different).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02668375-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈvɛri / (AmE), / ˈveəri / (BrE)
to differ or be at variance with something; to not conform or be out of line with
be at variance with; differ from

Examples

His actions vary from what was expected by the team.
These results vary significantly from those in previous studies.
The latest figures vary with the official estimates provided earlier.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to differ - to be unlike or distinct in nature, form, or characteristics
  • Antonym: to conform - to comply with rules, standards, or laws; to be in line with

Etymology

From Latin variāre (to change), from varius (different, various).

Source: WordNet

4. VERB

/ ˈvɛri / (AmE), / ˈveəri / (BrE)
to change or alter in some way, without losing essential characteristics; to differ or fluctuate within limits
to change or differ without losing essence

Examples

The menu at this restaurant can vary depending on the season and availability of ingredients.
Her opinions vary from day to day, depending on her mood.
The prices of flights vary greatly over the course of the year.
