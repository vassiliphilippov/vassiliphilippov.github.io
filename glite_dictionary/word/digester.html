digester

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈdʒɛstər / (AmE), / daɪˈdʒɛstə / (BrE)
a strong container, often a pressure vessel, used for digesting plant or animal material through chemical or biological processes
vessel for digesting materials

Examples

The factory installed a new digester to process agricultural waste efficiently.
Bacteria inside the digester help break down organic matter into biogas.
Researchers monitored the temperature of the digester to optimize methane production.

Etymology

From Latin 'digestor', from 'digestus', meaning 'arranged' or 'separated'.
