wino

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪnoʊ / (AmE), / ˈwaɪnəʊ / (BrE)
a person who habitually drinks excessive amounts of alcohol, especially inexpensive or fortified wine
habitual heavy drinker, especially of cheap wine
slang

Examples

The alley was home to more than one wino seeking shelter for the night.
He was mistaken for a wino when he walked out of the bar in ragged clothes.
The neighborhood association discussed how best to help the winos who gathered in the park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alcoholic - a person addicted to alcohol
  • Antonym: teetotaler - a person who abstains completely from alcoholic drinks

Etymology

From 'wine' + '-o', a common slang ending; first attested in the early 20th century in American English.
