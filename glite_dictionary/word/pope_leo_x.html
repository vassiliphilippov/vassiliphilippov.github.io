Pope Leo X

1. PROPN

/ poʊp ˈliː.oʊ ˌtɛnθ / (AmE), / pəʊp ˈlɪəʊ ˌtɛnθ / (BrE)
(1475–1521) The pope of the Roman Catholic Church from 1513 to 1521, known for excommunicating Martin Luther and granting Henry VIII the title 'Defender of the Faith'.
pope (1513–1521), excommunicated Luther

Examples

Pope Leo X played a pivotal role in the religious conflicts of the early 16th century.
It was Pope Leo X who excommunicated Martin Luther, marking a turning point in church history.
The title 'Defender of the Faith' was first granted to Henry VIII by Pope Leo X in 1521.
