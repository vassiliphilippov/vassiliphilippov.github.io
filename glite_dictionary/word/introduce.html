to introduce

1. VERB

/ ˌɪn.trəˈdus / (AmE), / ˌɪn.trəˈdjuːs / (BrE)
to make someone known by name to another person, especially in a formal situation
present someone to another

Examples

Let me introduce you to my friends from college.
She was nervous to introduce her boyfriend to her parents.
At the meeting, the manager introduced the new team members to everyone.

Synset Examples

permit me to acquaint you with my son
introduce the new neighbors to the community

Related Terms

  • Synonym: present - to formally make someone known to another

Etymology

From Latin introducere, from intro- (inward) + ducere (to lead).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00902866-v
Synset POS: v
