trundle

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrʌndəl / (AmE), / ˈtrʌndəl / (BrE)
a low bed on wheels or casters that is stored under a higher bed and can be pulled out for use
a low bed stored under another bed

Examples

The guest room has a trundle that can be pulled out for extra sleeping space.
We bought a trundle for our daughter's friend to use during sleepovers.
The trundle fits neatly under the main bed when not in use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trundle bed - a bed that can be rolled under another

Etymology

From Middle English 'trundel' meaning small wheel or roller.

2. VERB

/ ˈtrʌndl̩ / (AmE), / ˈtrʌndl̩ / (BrE)
to move slowly and heavily, often making a rumbling or clattering noise, typically on wheels.
move slowly and heavily, often on wheels

Examples

The old cart trundled along the bumpy country road, its contents rattling with every jolt.
He watched as the delivery van trundled down the narrow street, barely fitting between the parked cars.
The child trundled her toy wagon across the backyard, laughing as it bumped over the grass.

Synset Examples

the streetcar trundled down the avenue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roll - to move by turning over and over on a surface

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'trendle' (small wheel), from Old English 'tryndel' (wheel, circle).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01872125-v
Synset POS: v
