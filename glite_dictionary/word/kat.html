kat

1. NOUN

/ kæt / (AmE), / kæt / (BrE)
the leaves of the shrub Catha edulis, which are chewed as a stimulant or used to make tea, especially in East Africa and the Arabian Peninsula
leaves of Catha edulis, chewed for stimulation

Examples

Many people in East Africa chew kat to experience its stimulating effects.
The government has debated whether to ban the import of kat due to its psychoactive properties.
She brewed a tea from kat leaves that her relatives brought from Yemen.

Synset Examples

in Yemen, kat is used daily by 85% of adults

Related Terms

  • Synonym: khat - alternate spelling for the leaves of Catha edulis chewed as a stimulant

Etymology

From Arabic 'qāt', referring to the plant Catha edulis.
