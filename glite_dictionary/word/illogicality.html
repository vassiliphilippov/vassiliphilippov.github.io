illogicality

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlɑdʒəˈkæləti / (AmE), / ɪˌlɒdʒɪˈkælɪti / (BrE)
The quality of lacking logic or sound reasoning; invalid or incorrect reasoning.
lack of logic or sound reasoning

Examples

His argument was dismissed due to its clear illogicality and lack of evidence.
The film’s plot suffered from frequent illogicalities that confused the audience.
She pointed out the illogicality of his conclusion during the debate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: illogical - not logical; lacking clear, sound reasoning
  • Antonym: logic - sound reasoning; principles of valid inference

Etymology

From 'illogical' plus the noun suffix '-ity', ultimately from Latin 'illogicus'.
