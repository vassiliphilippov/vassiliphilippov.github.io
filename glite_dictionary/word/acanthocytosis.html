acanthocytosis

1. NOUN

/ əˌkænθoʊsaɪˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / əˌkanθəʊsaɪˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A medical condition in which abnormally shaped, spiny red blood cells (acanthocytes) are present in the blood, as seen in disorders such as abetalipoproteinemia.
presence of acanthocytes in the blood
medical

Examples

The blood test revealed a high level of acanthocytosis in the patient.
Acanthocytosis can be a diagnostic clue for certain genetic disorders.
Physicians observed acanthocytosis while examining the peripheral blood smear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acanthocyte - a red blood cell with spiky projections

Etymology

From Greek akantha (thorn, spine) + -cytosis (condition of cells).
