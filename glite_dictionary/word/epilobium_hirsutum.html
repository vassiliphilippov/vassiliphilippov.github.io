Epilobium hirsutum

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpəˌloʊbiəm hɪrˈsuːtəm / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˌləʊbiəm hɪəˈsuːtəm / (BrE)
A species of plant native to Europe and Asia, with purplish-red flowers and hairy stems and leaves, commonly known as great willowherb; also found in North America.
species of flowering plant (great willowherb)

Examples

The banks of the river were lined with Epilobium hirsutum, their vibrant flowers standing out against the green.
Gardeners sometimes remove Epilobium hirsutum as it can spread quickly in damp soil.
Botanists observed that Epilobium hirsutum thrives in wetlands across much of Europe.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Epilobium' (genus name), from Greek 'epi-' (upon) + 'lobos' (pod), and 'hirsutum' (hairy).
