materialism

1. NOUN

/ məˈtɪriəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / məˈtɪəriəlɪzəm / (BrE)
a tendency to consider material possessions and physical comfort as more important than ethical values or spiritual well-being
focus on material possessions over values

Examples

Many people criticize materialism as a shallow pursuit that ignores deeper values.
The rise of consumer culture has often been linked to increasing materialism in society.
He rejected materialism in favor of a more spiritual way of life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greed - intense and selfish desire for something, especially wealth or power
  • Antonym: asceticism - severe self-discipline and avoidance of all forms of indulgence

Etymology

From Latin 'materialis' (of matter), via French 'matérialisme'.

2. NOUN

/ məˈtɪriəlɪzəm / (AmE), / məˈtɪəriəlɪzəm / (BrE)
(Philosophy) The belief or theory that physical matter is the only or fundamental reality, and that all phenomena, including mental phenomena and consciousness, are the result of material interactions.
doctrine that only physical matter is real

Examples

The philosopher defended materialism as the foundation for understanding consciousness.
In contrast to dualism, materialism claims that only matter truly exists.
Modern science often operates under assumptions similar to those of materialism.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: idealism - the philosophical doctrine that reality is fundamentally mental or spiritual

Etymology

From French matérialisme, from Latin materia (matter).
