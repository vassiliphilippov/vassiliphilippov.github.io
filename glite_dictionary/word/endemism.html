endemism

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛndəˌmɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈɛndɪˌmɪzəm / (BrE)
The condition of being native to and restricted to a particular geographic area.
the state of being native to a specific place

Examples

The endemism of wildlife on Madagascar makes it a unique hotspot for biodiversity.
Botanists study areas of high endemism to understand evolutionary processes.
Marine endemism in this region results from long periods of isolation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: endemic - native and restricted to a certain place

Etymology

From Greek endēmos (native, dwelling in a place), from en- (in) + dēmos (people, district).
