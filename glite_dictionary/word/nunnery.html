nunnery

1. NOUN

/ ˈnʌn.ə.ri / (AmE), / ˈnʌn.əri / (BrE)
a convent or religious community where nuns live and work
convent for nuns

Examples

The old stone nunnery stood at the edge of the village, surrounded by gardens.
She decided to join a nunnery after feeling called to a life of religious devotion.
Historians studied the daily routines of women living in the medieval nunnery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convent - a community or building for religious members, especially nuns

Etymology

From Middle English nunnery, from nun + -ery; nun from Old English nunne (nun), from Late Latin nonna.
