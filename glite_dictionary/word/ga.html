2. NOUN

/ ˌdʒiː ˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˌdʒiː ˈeɪ / (BrE)
A highly toxic, combustible nerve agent (tabun) developed in Germany in 1936, used as a chemical weapon.
toxic nerve agent; tabun

Examples

The military discovered a small quantity of GA hidden in an abandoned facility.
Exposure to GA can result in serious neurological damage or death within minutes.
International treaties strictly prohibit the production and use of GA in warfare.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tabun - a highly toxic nerve agent developed in Germany; also known as GA
  • Degree Higher: sarin - a highly toxic nerve agent (GB) used as a chemical weapon

Etymology

Abbreviation for 'German Agent', indicating its origin in German chemical weapons research.
