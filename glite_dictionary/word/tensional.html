tensional

1. ADJ

/ ˈtɛnʃənəl / (AmE), / ˈtɛnʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to or caused by tension.
relating to tension

Examples

The engineer examined the tensional forces acting on the bridge cables.
An MRI can show tensional changes in soft tissues after an injury.
Geologists study tensional stress to understand how earthquakes occur.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tension - the state of being stretched tight; mental or emotional strain

Etymology

From Latin 'tensio' (a stretching) + '-al' (adjective forming suffix).
