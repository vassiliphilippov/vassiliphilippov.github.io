infantilism

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfæntɪˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪnˈfæntɪlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The exhibition of childlike or immature behavior by an adult.
childlike behavior in adults

Examples

His constant refusal to take responsibility was seen as a form of infantilism by his colleagues.
The therapist noted signs of infantilism in the patient's reactions to stressful situations.
Critics argued that modern advertising sometimes encourages infantilism in adult consumers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immaturity - the state of being immature; not fully developed emotionally

Etymology

From Latin 'infans' (child) + '-ism' (practice or condition).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfæntəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪnˈfæntɪlɪzəm / (BrE)
a condition in which a child or adult retains infantile physical or psychological characteristics beyond the usual age
retention of infantile characteristics
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the patient with infantilism after observing persistent childlike behaviors.
Certain hormonal disorders can lead to physical infantilism in adults.
Psychologists study infantilism to understand developmental delays and emotional immaturity.

Etymology

From French 'infantalisme', from Latin 'infans' (infant, child).
