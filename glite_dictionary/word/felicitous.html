felicitous

1. ADJ

/ fəˈlɪsɪtəs / (AmE), / fəˈlɪsɪtəs / (BrE)
marked by good fortune; resulting in or characterized by happiness and success
marked by good fortune

Examples

She considered herself to have led a felicitous life, full of unexpected joys.
Their meeting at the café was a felicitous coincidence that changed everything.
Thanks to some felicitous investments, he retired earlier than he expected.

Synset Examples

a felicitous life
a happy outcome

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unfortunate - marked by bad luck
  • Synonym: fortunate - having good fortune
  • Synonym: happy - feeling or showing pleasure or contentment

Etymology

From Latin 'felicitas' (happiness, luck), from 'felix' (happy, fortunate).

2. ADJ

/ fəˈlɪsɪtəs / (AmE), / fəˈlɪsɪtəs / (BrE)
pleasantly and appropriately expressed or suited to the occasion; well-chosen or apt in manner or style.
aptly or suitably expressed; appropriate

Examples

Her felicitous choice of words made the speech memorable for everyone.
The decor was felicitous and matched the joyful mood of the event perfectly.
His felicitous remarks eased the tension in the meeting.

Synset Examples

a felicitous speaker

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appropriate - suitable for a particular situation or purpose
  • Antonym: inept - not suitable or appropriate; awkward

Etymology

From Latin felicitas (happiness, luck), via French félicité.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01003236-a
Synset POS: a
