checkbook

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛkˌbʊk / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛkˌbʊk / (BrE)
a book containing preprinted checks, issued by a bank for use with a checking account
book of bank checks

Examples

She opened her purse and took out her checkbook to pay for the groceries.
The bank manager issued a new checkbook after his old one was depleted.
Please make sure to record every transaction in your checkbook to avoid any overdrafts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chequebook - British spelling of checkbook

Etymology

From 'check' (bank order) + 'book'.
