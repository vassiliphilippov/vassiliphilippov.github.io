escudo

1. NOUN

/ ɛˈskuːdoʊ / (AmE), / ɛˈskuːdəʊ / (BrE)
the former basic monetary unit of Portugal, equal to 100 centavos, used until the adoption of the euro in 2002
former Portuguese currency unit

Examples

Portugal replaced the escudo with the euro in 2002.
He found a collection of old escudo coins in his grandfather's drawer.
The price was listed in both escudos and euros during the transition period.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: euro - currency that replaced the escudo in Portugal

Etymology

From Spanish and Portuguese 'escudo' meaning 'shield', originally referring to the coin's design.

2. NOUN

/ ɛˈskudoʊ / (AmE), / ɛsˈkuːdəʊ / (BrE)
the basic unit of currency in Cape Verde, equal to 100 centavos
Cape Verdean unit of currency

Examples

Tourists exchanged their dollars for escudos upon arriving in Cape Verde.
The restaurant bill came to 2,000 escudos for the entire meal.
Cape Verde adopted the escudo as its national currency after gaining independence.

Etymology

From Portuguese 'escudo' meaning 'shield', used for various historical and modern currencies.
