format

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrˌmæt / (AmE), / ˈfɔːmæt / (BrE)
the general arrangement, appearance, or layout of a publication or document
general arrangement or layout of a publication

Examples

The magazine changed its format to attract a younger audience.
What format does the publisher require for manuscripts?
The new format made the company's brochure easier to read.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: layout - the way in which text or pictures are set out on a page

Etymology

From French 'format' (size, form), from Latin 'forma' (form, shape).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04684742-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈfɔːrˌmæt / (AmE), / ˈfɔːmæt / (BrE)
to arrange or organize data in a particular way for storage or display, especially in computing.
arrange data for storage/display (computing)

Examples

Make sure you format the disk before copying any files to it.
The program can format your data for easier analysis.
Please format the document according to the company's style guide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: structure - to organize or arrange parts in a particular way

Etymology

From French 'format', from Latin 'forma' (form).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00702102-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈfɔːr.mæt / (AmE), / ˈfɔː.mæt / (BrE)
the way in which information is arranged or organized, especially according to a set of rules or specifications for computer processing
arrangement or organization of information

Examples

Please make sure the document is saved in the correct format for our system.
The data was corrupted because it was not in a readable format.
You can export the file in several different formats, such as PDF or DOCX.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: layout - the way in which elements are arranged, especially on a page or screen

Etymology

Borrowed from French format, from Latin formātus (formed, shaped), from formāre (to shape).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06649331-n
Synset POS: n

/ ˈfɔːrˌmæt / (AmE), / ˈfɔːmæt / (BrE)
to prepare a disk or other storage medium for use by dividing it into marked sectors to store data
prepare disk for data storage

Examples

You must format the new hard drive before installing the operating system.

She is formatting the USB stick to ensure it is compatible with the computer.

/ ˈfɔrˌmæt / (AmE), / ˈfɔːmæt / (BrE)
to arrange or prepare text, images, or data into a particular layout or structure, especially for printing or display
arrange text or data into a layout

Examples

She formatted the report to make it easier to read.
The computer can automatically format your text based on the selected style.

Synset Examples

Format this letter so it can be printed out

Related Terms

Etymology

From French *format* (form, shape, size), from Latin *forma* (form, mold).
