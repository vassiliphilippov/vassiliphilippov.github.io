fanlight

1. NOUN

/ ˈfænˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈfanˌlʌɪt / (BrE)
a small window above a door, often semicircular or rectangular, sometimes hinged to a horizontal crosspiece
window above a door

Examples

The afternoon sun streamed through the fanlight, casting colorful patterns on the hallway floor.
She glanced up at the fanlight to see if anyone was home before knocking on the door.
The old house was known for its arched fanlight above the main entrance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transom - a horizontal crosspiece over a door or a window above such a crosspiece

Etymology

From 'fan' (from the shape of the glass panes) + 'light' (window).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfænˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈfan.laɪt / (BrE)
A window, often semicircular or fan-shaped, set over a door or in a roof to admit daylight.
window over door or in roof for light

Examples

A decorative fanlight above the front door allowed sunlight to brighten the hallway.
They installed a new fanlight in the attic to bring in more natural light.
The architect designed the entryway with an ornate fanlight to give the home a classic touch.

Etymology

From 'fan' (referring to its typical shape) + 'light' (window pane), first used in the late 18th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04239909-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈfænˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈfanˌlaɪt / (BrE)
A semicircular or semi-elliptical window above a door or another window, often with bars radiating like the ribs of a fan.
semicircular window above a door

Examples

The house had a beautiful stained-glass fanlight above the front door.
Sunlight streamed through the fanlight, casting patterns on the entryway floor.
The architect included a traditional fanlight to add charm and brightness to the entrance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transom - a horizontal crosspiece over a door or window, often supporting a window above the door
