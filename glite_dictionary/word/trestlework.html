trestlework

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛsəlˌwɝk / (AmE), / ˈtrɛsəlˌwɜːk / (BrE)
a supporting framework made of a series of connected trestles, used especially in bridges, piers, or scaffolds
framework made of connected trestles

Examples

The wooden trestlework supported the railway bridge across the river.
Engineers inspected the trestlework for signs of wear and damage after the storm.
A delicate trestlework was used to hold the scaffolding in position during construction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trestle - a horizontal beam supported by two pairs of sloping legs, used as a support

Etymology

Formed from 'trestle' (from Old French 'trestel', from Latin 'transtellum') and 'work'.
