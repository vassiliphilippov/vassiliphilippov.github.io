thereinafter

1. ADV

/ ˌðɛrɪnˈæftər / (AmE), / ˌðeərɪnˈɑːftə / (BrE)
In the following part of a document, text, or speech; later in the same document or context.
later in the same document or context
legal

Examples

Any person referred to as the ‘borrower’ thereinafter shall be bound by the following terms.
The contract specifies damages in Section 4 and, thereinafter, outlines remedies for breach.
All parties thereinafter agree to comply with the listed obligations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hereinafter - later in this document or statement

Etymology

Formed from 'therein' + 'after', modeled after legal and formal document conventions.
