salpingectomy

1. NOUN

/ sæl.pɪŋˈɛk.tə.mi / (AmE), / sæl.pɪŋˈɛk.tə.mi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure to remove one or both Fallopian tubes
removal of Fallopian tube(s)
medical

Examples

The patient underwent a salpingectomy to treat the ectopic pregnancy.
A bilateral salpingectomy is sometimes performed to reduce the risk of ovarian cancer.
After her salpingectomy, she discussed fertility options with her doctor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oophorectomy - surgical removal of one or both ovaries

Etymology

From Greek 'salpinx' (trumpet, Fallopian tube) + '-ectomy' (removal).
