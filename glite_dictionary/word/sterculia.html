Sterculia

1. NOUN

/ stərˈkjuːliə / (AmE), / stəːˈkjuːlɪə / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous or evergreen trees found in tropical and subtropical regions of the Old and New World, belonging to the Sterculiaceae family.
a genus of tropical and subtropical trees

Examples

The bark of the Sterculia tree is often used in traditional medicine.
Several species of Sterculia can be found growing in the tropical rainforests of Africa.
The large, star-shaped fruit of Sterculia trees is distinctive among tropical flora.

Etymology

From Latin *Sterculia*, the name of the genus, from *stercus* meaning 'dung', due to the unpleasant smell of some species.

2. NOUN

/ stərˈkuːliə / (AmE), / stɜːˈkjuːlɪə / (BrE)
a genus of tropical trees and shrubs, many of which produce useful fibers or gums
genus of tropical trees and shrubs

Examples

The Sterculia tree is valued for its strong fibers used in making ropes.
Several species of Sterculia are grown for their ornamental appeal in tropical gardens.
The resin from Sterculia is often used as a natural gum in the food industry.

Etymology

From Latin 'Sterculia', named after Sterculius, the Roman god of manure, due to the unpleasant odor of some species.
