obtrusive

1. ADJ

/ əbˈtruːsɪv / (AmE), / əbˈtruːsɪv / (BrE)
Noticeably sticking out or projecting; prominent in a way that is unwelcome or intrusive.
sticking out; prominent

Examples

The camera's obtrusive lens ruined the subtle design of the phone.
His obtrusive behavior at the event made everyone uncomfortable.
The sculpture was placed in an obtrusive spot, blocking the hallway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protruding - sticking out; projecting

Etymology

From Latin 'obtrudere' (to thrust upon).

2. ADJ

/ əbˈtruːsɪv / (AmE), / əbˈtruːsɪv / (BrE)
Very noticeable in an unpleasant or unwelcome way; attracting attention in a way that is not wanted.
noticeable in an unpleasant way

Examples

The sign's obtrusive colors drew the attention of everyone passing by.
Her obtrusive questions made the guests feel uncomfortable.
The new building was criticized for its obtrusive design in the historic neighborhood.

Synset Examples

the obtrusive behavior of a spoiled child
equally obtrusive was the graffiti

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inconspicuous - not easily seen or attracting attention
  • Synonym: conspicuous - easily seen or noticed

Etymology

From Latin 'obtrudere' (to thrust upon).

Synset POS: s
