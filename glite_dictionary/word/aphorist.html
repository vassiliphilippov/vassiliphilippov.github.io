aphorist

1. NOUN

/ ˈæf.əˌrɪst / (AmE), / ˈæf.ər.ɪst / (BrE)
A person who composes or frequently quotes aphorisms.
a person who creates or quotes aphorisms

Examples

The essay was full of witty remarks, suggesting its author was quite an aphorist.
Many regard Oscar Wilde as a master aphorist of his age.
She gained a reputation as an aphorist, known for her sharp and memorable sayings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aphorism - a concise statement expressing a general truth or principle

Etymology

From 'aphorism' + '-ist', modeled on similar agent nouns.
