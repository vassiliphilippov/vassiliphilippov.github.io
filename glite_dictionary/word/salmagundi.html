salmagundi

1. NOUN

/ ˌsælməˈɡʌndi / (AmE), / ˌsælməˈɡʌndi / (BrE)
a dish consisting of chopped cooked meats, eggs, and vegetables, usually arranged attractively and dressed with a salad dressing
dish of mixed meats, eggs, and vegetables

Examples

The chef prepared a colorful salmagundi for the luncheon, arranging everything in neat rows on a large platter.
She served salmagundi as a centerpiece at the holiday table, impressing all her guests.
Historically, sailors enjoyed salmagundi aboard ships because it was easy to make from various available ingredients.

Etymology

From French salmigondis (hodgepodge), of uncertain origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsæl.məˈɡʌn.di / (AmE), / ˌsæl.məˈɡʌn.di / (BrE)
A miscellaneous assortment or mixture of different things.
mixture; assortment of various things

Examples

The exhibition showcases a salmagundi of artistic styles from around the globe.
Her bookshelf is a salmagundi of genres, from mystery novels to ancient history.
The market was a salmagundi of scents, colors, and sounds.

Synset Examples

a great assortment of cars was on display

a veritable smorgasbord of religions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: medley - a varied mixture of people or things
  • Synonym: hodgepodge - a confusing mixture or jumble

Etymology

From French *salmigondis* (mixed food dish, hotchpotch).
