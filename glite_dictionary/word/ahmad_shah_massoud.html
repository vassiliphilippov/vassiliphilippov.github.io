Ahmad Shah Massoud

1. PROPN

/ ɑːˈmɑːd ʃɑː məˈsuːd / (AmE), / əˈmɑːd ʃɑː məˈsuːd / (BrE)
(1953–2001) Afghan military leader and politician known for leading resistance against the Soviet invasion in the 1980s and against the Taliban, earning the title 'Lion of Panjshir'. He was assassinated in 2001.
Afghan military leader and resistance figure

Examples

Ahmad Shah Massoud played a crucial role in resisting both Soviet and Taliban forces in Afghanistan.
Many Afghans revere Ahmad Shah Massoud as a national hero and symbol of freedom.
Documentaries often highlight the leadership and strategy of Ahmad Shah Massoud during the Afghan conflict.
