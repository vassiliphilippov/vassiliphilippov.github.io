to persist

1. VERB

/ pərˈsɪst / (AmE), / pəˈsɪst / (BrE)
to continue to exist, especially for longer than expected; to last or endure
continue to exist; last

Examples

Despite the storm, the tradition continued to persist in the village.
Fears about the disease persist even after years without any new cases.
The belief that the building is haunted has persisted for generations.

Synset Examples

These stories die hard
The legend of Elvis endures

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to endure - to continue to exist; last; remain
  • Antonym: to disappear - to cease to exist or be visible

Etymology

From Latin persistere, meaning 'to stand firm, endure'.

2. VERB

/ pərˈsɪst / (AmE), / pəˈsɪst / (BrE)
to continue firmly or obstinately in an opinion or a course of action despite difficulty, opposition, or failure
continue firmly despite opposition or difficulty

Examples

Despite facing several setbacks, she chose to persist with her studies.
The pain may persist for a few days after the operation.
He would persist in asking the same question, even after being told the answer.

Synset Examples

he persisted to call me every night
The child persisted and kept asking questions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to continue - to keep going without interruption
  • Antonym: to give up - to stop trying or abandon effort

Etymology

From Latin 'persistere', from 'per-' (through) + 'sistere' (to stand).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00351115-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ pərˈsɪst / (AmE), / pəˈsɪst / (BrE)
(formal) To remain in a place and not leave; to stay for an extended period.
remain in a place; not leave

Examples

Several protesters chose to persist inside the building even after they were asked to leave.
If symptoms persist, consult your doctor before continuing with any activities.
The fog persisted over the hills for hours, refusing to lift.

Synset Examples

We are staying in Detroit; we are not moving to Cincinnati
Stay put in the corner here!
Stick around and you will learn something!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stay - to remain in a place instead of leaving
  • Antonym: to depart - to leave a place

Etymology

From Latin persistĕre, from per- (thoroughly) + sistere (to stand).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01861378-v
Synset POS: v
