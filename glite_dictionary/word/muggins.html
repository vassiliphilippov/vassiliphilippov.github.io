muggins

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌɡɪnz / (AmE), / ˈmʌɡɪnz / (BrE)
a person who is easily deceived or taken advantage of, especially due to a lack of good judgment; a fool.
a person who is easily deceived; a fool
informal british

Examples

Don't ask muggins here to organize your party, or everything will go wrong.
They played a prank on muggins, and he didn’t even notice.
Who ended up doing all the work? Why, muggins as usual!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fool - a person lacking sense or judgement
  • Synonym: dupe - someone who is easily tricked or deceived
  • Antonym: wise - someone having or showing experience, knowledge, and good judgment

Etymology

Possibly from the British dialect term 'muggins', meaning a simpleton or fool, of uncertain origin.
