John Jay

1. PROPN

/ dʒɑn dʒeɪ / (AmE), / dʒɒn dʒeɪ / (BrE)
(1745–1829) American diplomat, jurist, and statesman who helped negotiate peace with Britain and served as the first Chief Justice of the United States Supreme Court.
first Chief Justice of the U.S. Supreme Court

Examples

Few figures in American history are as influential as John Jay, the nation’s first Chief Justice.
The peace treaty with Britain was largely negotiated by John Jay and his colleagues.
Students discussed the legacy of John Jay in their constitutional law class.
