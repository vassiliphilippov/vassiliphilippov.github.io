conformance

1. NOUN

/ kənˈfɔrməns / (AmE), / kənˈfɔːməns / (BrE)
the state of corresponding in form, appearance, or character to a standard or specified set of rules
correspondence in form or appearance

Examples

All materials used in the project must be in conformance with safety regulations.
The product failed inspection due to its lack of conformance to industry standards.
His design was praised for its conformance to classic architectural principles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compliance - the act of conforming to a rule or standard
  • Antonym: nonconformity - failure to conform to a standard or rule

Etymology

From conform + -ance, influenced by Late Latin conformantia (agreement).
