Cystopteris

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈstɑp.tə.rɪs / (AmE), / sɪˈstɒp.tə.rɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small, usually perennial ferns commonly known as bladder ferns, often found growing among rocks; sometimes classified in the Polypodiaceae family.
genus of small rock ferns

Examples

The Cystopteris can often be found growing between rocks in shaded woodlands.
Several species of Cystopteris are known for their delicate, lacy fronds.
Botanists collected samples of Cystopteris to study its adaptations to rocky environments.

Etymology

From Greek 'kystis' (bladder) + 'pteris' (fern), referring to the bladder-like structures in these ferns.

2. NOUN

/ saɪsˈtɑptərɪs / (AmE), / sɪsˈtɒptərɪs / (BrE)
a genus of chiefly small perennial rock ferns, commonly known as bladder ferns
genus of small bladder ferns

Examples

The Cystopteris genus includes several ferns found on rocky hillsides.
Botanists recently identified a new species within the Cystopteris group.
Gardeners often cultivate Cystopteris for its delicate and lacy fronds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bladder fern - a common name for ferns in the genus Cystopteris

Etymology

From Greek 'kystis' (bladder) + 'pteris' (fern), referring to the bladder-like structures on the ferns.
