Marcus Junius Brutus the Younger

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɑːrkəs ˈdʒuːniəs ˈbruːtəs ði ˈjʌŋɡər / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkəs ˈdʒuːnɪəs ˈbruːtəs ði ˈjʌŋɡə / (BrE)
(85–42 BC) A Roman statesman famous for leading, along with Cassius, the conspiracy to assassinate Julius Caesar.
Roman statesman, Caesar's assassin

Examples

Many historians debate the motives of Marcus Junius Brutus the Younger in the assassination of Caesar.
Shakespeare’s play 'Julius Caesar' dramatizes the role of Marcus Junius Brutus the Younger in Roman history.
The legacy of Marcus Junius Brutus the Younger remains controversial among scholars of classical antiquity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Julius Caesar - Roman dictator assassinated by Brutus and others
