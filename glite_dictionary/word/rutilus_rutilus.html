Rutilus rutilus

1. NOUN

/ ˈruː.tɪ.ləs ˈruː.tɪ.ləs / (AmE), / ˈruː.tɪ.ləs ˈruː.tɪ.ləs / (BrE)
A European freshwater fish, commonly known as the roach, with a greenish back and silvery sides, found in rivers and lakes.
European freshwater fish (roach)

Examples

Anglers often target Rutilus rutilus in European rivers during the summer months.
The diet of Rutilus rutilus consists mainly of plant material and small invertebrates.
Researchers noted an increase in the population of Rutilus rutilus due to improved water quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roach - common European freshwater fish, Rutilus rutilus

Etymology

From Latin rutilus (‘reddish, golden-red’), describing the fish's coloration.
