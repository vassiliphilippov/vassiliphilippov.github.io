spoil

1. NOUN

/ spɔɪl / (AmE), / spɔɪl / (BrE)
items of value taken by force, especially in war; loot or plunder, usually referred to in plural as 'spoils'.
goods taken by force, especially in war

Examples

The victors divided the spoils among themselves after the battle.
The soldiers returned home with the spoils of war.
For centuries, conquerors claimed the spoils as their reward.

Synset Examples

to the victor belong the spoils of the enemy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loot - goods stolen or taken by force, especially in war
  • Synonym: booty - valuables seized during war or piratical activity

Etymology

From Old French *espoille* (booty), from Latin *spolium* (booty, arms stripped from an enemy).
