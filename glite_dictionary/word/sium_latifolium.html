Sium latifolium

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪəm læˈtɪˌfoʊliəm / (AmE), / ˈsɪʊm latɪˈfɔːliəm / (BrE)
A large, robust perennial plant with white flowers, found wild in shallow freshwater areas of Europe; also known as greater water-parsnip.
European aquatic perennial plant

Examples

Sium latifolium thrives in wetlands and along the banks of European rivers.
Botanists studied how Sium latifolium adapts to freshwater habitats.
Some conservation programs aim to protect Sium latifolium due to its declining populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greater water-parsnip - common name for Sium latifolium

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Sium' from Ancient Greek 'síon' (water plant), and 'latifolium' meaning 'broad-leaved.'
