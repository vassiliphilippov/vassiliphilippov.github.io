purulence

1. NOUN

/ ˈpjʊrələns / (AmE), / ˈpjʊərəlns / (BrE)
the presence or production of pus, especially as a result of infection or inflammation.
presence of pus
medical

Examples

The doctor noted significant purulence in the wound, indicating a serious infection.
An abscess typically contains purulence that must be drained for proper healing.
The presence of purulence may require antibiotic treatment to resolve the underlying infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pus - a thick yellowish or greenish fluid produced in infected tissue

Etymology

From Latin purulentus (full of pus), from pus (pus, matter).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpjʊrələns / (AmE), / ˈpjʊərəlns / (BrE)
the quality or state of containing or producing pus
the presence of pus
medical

Examples

The wound showed increased purulence, indicating a possible infection.
Doctors monitor surgical sites for signs of redness and purulence.
A yellow-green discharge is a classic sign of purulence in an infected area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suppuration - the process of forming or discharging pus

Etymology

From Latin 'purulentus' (full of pus), from 'pus, puris' (pus).
