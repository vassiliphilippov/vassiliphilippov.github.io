to befriend

1. VERB

/ bɪˈfrɛnd / (AmE), / bɪˈfrɛnd / (BrE)
to act as a friend to someone, especially by offering help or support
act as a friend to

Examples

She tried to befriend the new student in her class.
Wild animals sometimes befriend each other despite their differences.
After moving to the city, he quickly befriended his neighbors.

Synset Examples

John and Eric soon became friends
Have you made friends yet in your new environment?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to befriend - act as a friend to someone
  • Synonym: help - to give assistance to someone
  • Antonym: to betray - to be disloyal to someone, opposite of befriending

Etymology

From be- (a prefix forming verbs) + friend (noun), first attested in Middle English.
