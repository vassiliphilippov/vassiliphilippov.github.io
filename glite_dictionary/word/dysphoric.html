dysphoric

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈfɔrɪk / (AmE), / dɪsˈfɒrɪk / (BrE)
feeling a general sense of unease, dissatisfaction, or distress, often of a psychological nature.
feeling general distress or unease
medical

Examples

The patient felt increasingly dysphoric during the evaluation, expressing deep unease.
After the medication change, she reported feeling dysphoric and restless most days.
A dysphoric mood is a common symptom of several mood disorders.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: euphoric - experiencing intense excitement or happiness

Etymology

From Greek dysphoros (hard to bear), from dys- (bad, difficult) + pherein (to bear).
