battlefield

1. NOUN

/ ˈbætlˌfiːld / (AmE), / ˈbæt.əl.fiːld / (BrE)
an area where a battle is being or has been fought
area where a battle took place

Examples

The soldiers marched bravely across the battlefield as the sun rose.
Many historians have visited the battlefield to study the events that unfolded there.
The documentary featured aerial views of the famous battlefield at Gettysburg.

Synset Examples

they made a tour of Civil War battlefields

Related Terms

  • Synonym: battleground - an area where a battle takes place

Etymology

Formed from 'battle' (from Old French 'bataille') + 'field' (from Old English 'feld', open land).
