pseud

1. NOUN

/ suːd / (AmE), / sjuːd / (BrE)
a person who pretends to be knowledgeable or cultured, often to impress others
a pretentious or false person
british informal

Examples

Everyone in the art scene knows he's just a pseud trying to sound profound.
She dismisses his opinions, believing he’s nothing more than a pseud.
The club was full of pseuds discussing things they barely understood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poser - a person who pretends to be something they are not, to impress others
  • Synonym: fake - a person who is not genuine
  • Antonym: genuine person - someone who is authentic and not pretending

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'pseudo-', from Greek 'pseudes' (false).
