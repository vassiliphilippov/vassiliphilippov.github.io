symphysion

1. NOUN

/ sɪmˈfɪziˌɒn / (AmE), / sɪmˈfɪzɪən / (BrE)
the most forward point of the alveolar process of the lower jaw (mandible), used as a reference in anatomy and dentistry
anterior point of lower jaw (mandible)
medical

Examples

In orthodontics, the symphysion is used as a reference landmark during cephalometric analysis.
The measurement was taken from the symphysion to the anterior nasal spine.
The surgeon made an incision near the symphysion to access the bone.

Etymology

From Greek 'symphysis' (growing together) + '-ion' (diminutive suffix).
