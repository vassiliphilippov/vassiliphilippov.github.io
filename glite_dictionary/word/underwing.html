underwing

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌndɚˌwɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌwɪŋ / (BrE)
A moth of a group characterized by dull-colored forewings and brightly colored hind wings, especially of the genus Catocala.
a moth with dull front wings, bright hind wings

Examples

The underwing reveals its bright colors only when threatened, startling predators.
Some collectors specialize in rare underwing species from North America.
An underwing moth perched camouflaged on the tree trunk during the day.

Etymology

From 'under' + 'wing', referring to the often hidden, vividly colored hind wings.
