spiel

1. NOUN

/ spiﾋ人 / (AmE), / spiﾋ人 / (BrE)
a lengthy or glib talk or speech, especially one intended to persuade or promote something
glib sales talk or persuasive speech

Examples

The salesman launched into his usual spiel about the benefits of the product.
After listening to her long spiel, I still wasn't convinced to buy anything.
I quickly recognized his pitch as just another marketing spiel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitch - a persuasive sales talk or argument

Etymology

From German 'Spiel' meaning 'play' or 'game'.

WordNet ID: oewn-07026516-n

2. VERB

/ spiﾋ人 / (AmE), / spiﾋ人 / (BrE)
to talk at length or flamboyantly, typically to persuade or advertise; to deliver a lengthy or extravagant speech.
talk at length, often persuasively or extravagantly

Examples

He spieled for nearly an hour about the virtues of his new product.
She tends to spiel whenever she gets excited about her favorite topic.
The salesman spieled his pitch so quickly that I barely understood a word.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ramble - to talk or write at length in a confused or inconsequential way
  • Synonym: drone - to speak tediously in a dull, monotonous tone
  • Antonym: to summarize - to give a brief statement of the main points

Etymology

From German *Spiel* (play, game, performance).
