insufferable

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈsʌfərəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈsʌfərəbl / (BrE)
Too extreme or unpleasant to endure; intolerable.
too extreme or unbearable

Examples

The insufferable noise from the construction site made it impossible to concentrate.
She found his arrogance truly insufferable and avoided him whenever she could.
The humidity was insufferable during the summer heatwave.

Synset Examples

the insufferable heat of August in a New York apartment with no air conditioning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unbearable - so unpleasant or overwhelming as to be impossible to endure
  • Antonym: tolerable - able to be endured or tolerated

Etymology

From Latin 'insūfferabilis', formed from 'in-' (not) + 'sūfferre' (to bear).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈsʌfərəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈsʌfərəbəl / (BrE)
Extremely unpleasant, arrogant, or conceited to the point of being unbearable.
unbearably arrogant or unpleasant

Examples

Her insufferable arrogance made it impossible to work with her.
He found the constant boasting absolutely insufferable.
The meeting dragged on, dominated by his insufferable condescension.

Synset Examples

insufferable insolence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unbearable - too unpleasant or difficult to endure
  • Antonym: tolerable - able to be endured or tolerated

Etymology

From Latin insufferabilis, from in- 'not' + sufferre 'to bear'.
