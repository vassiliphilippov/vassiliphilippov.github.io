fetter

1. VERB

/ ˈfɛtər / (AmE), / ˈfɛtə / (BrE)
to restrain or shackle someone with chains around the ankles; to limit someone's freedom or movement
to restrain with chains or shackles

Examples

The prison guard fettered the inmate's feet before escorting him down the hall.
Heavy chains fettered the prisoners, making escape impossible.
She felt fettered by the strict rules of her household.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shackle - to restrain with chains or manacles
  • Antonym: free - to release from confinement or restraint

Etymology

From Old English *feter* (a chain or shackle for the feet), related to Old High German *fezzera*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɛt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɛtə / (BrE)
a chain or shackle placed on the ankles or feet, typically to restrain movement.
ankle shackle; chain for feet

Examples

The prisoner struggled against the heavy fetters locked around his ankles.
Iron fetters were used to restrain slaves during transport.
He felt as if invisible fetters were holding him back from freedom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shackle - a device for restraining movement by locking around the wrists or ankles
  • Synonym: manacle - a metal band for fastening someone's arms or hands

Etymology

From Old English *fetter* (chain or shackle), related to Old Norse *föturr* (fetter).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03335892-n
Synset POS: n
