roarer

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɔrər / (AmE), / ˈrɔːrə / (BrE)
a person who speaks or cries out very loudly
person who speaks or cries out loudly

Examples

The roarer in the back row disrupted the entire performance with his booming voice.
Even among a noisy crowd, the roarer could always be heard above everyone else.
She was known as a roarer for her habit of calling out orders across the factory floor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shouter - person who shouts loudly
  • Antonym: whisperer - person who speaks in a whisper

Etymology

From 'roar' (to make a loud, deep sound) + '-er' (agent suffix).
