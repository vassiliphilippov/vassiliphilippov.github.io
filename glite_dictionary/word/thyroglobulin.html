thyroglobulin

1. NOUN

/ ˌθaɪroʊˈɡlɑːbjəlɪn / (AmE), / ˌθaɪərəʊˈɡlɒbjʊlɪn / (BrE)
an iodine-containing protein produced by the thyroid gland, serving as a precursor for thyroid hormones
thyroid gland protein precursor
medical

Examples

Blood tests for thyroglobulin can help monitor certain thyroid disorders.
The surgeon explained that thyroglobulin levels are often checked after thyroid cancer treatment.
Scientists are studying how thyroglobulin is processed inside the thyroid gland.

Etymology

From 'thyroid' + 'globulin', indicating a globulin protein from the thyroid gland.
