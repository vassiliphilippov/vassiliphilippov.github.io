monstrous

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɑːnstrəs / (AmE), / ˈmɒnstrəs / (BrE)
distorted and unnatural in shape or size; abnormal and hideous
unnaturally large or hideous in form

Examples

The abandoned mansion was filled with monstrous statues that frightened anyone who entered.
A monstrous wave crashed over the boat, nearly tipping it over in the storm.
The artist's painting depicted a monstrous creature with three heads and glowing red eyes.

Synset Examples

tales of grotesque serpents eight fathoms long that churned the seas
twisted into monstrous shapes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grotesque - distorted, bizarre, or unnatural in shape or appearance
  • Synonym: hideous - extremely ugly or unpleasant to look at
  • Antonym: normal - conforming to a standard; usual, typical, or expected

Etymology

From Old French *monstrueux*, from Latin *monstruosus* (unnatural, strange).

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɑːnstrəs / (AmE), / ˈmɒnstrəs / (BrE)
Extremely and abnormally large in size, amount, or degree.
abnormally large or huge

Examples

The company reported a monstrous increase in profits last quarter.
A monstrous wave crashed over the seawall during the storm.
They discovered a monstrous pumpkin at the county fair, weighing over a thousand pounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: huge - very large in size or amount
  • Antonym: tiny - very small in size

Etymology

From Latin 'monstruosus', from 'monstrum' (monster, omen).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01392672-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈmɑnstrəs / (AmE), / ˈmɒnstrəs / (BrE)
Extremely cruel, brutal, or shocking in a way that evokes horror or outrage; exceptionally evil.
shockingly brutal or cruel

Examples

His crimes were so monstrous that even his allies were horrified.
The dictator's monstrous acts drew condemnation from the entire world.
She could not forgive such a monstrous betrayal by someone she trusted.

Synset Examples

murder is an atrocious crime
a grievous offense against morality
a grievous crime
no excess was too monstrous for them to commit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atrocious - shockingly wicked or cruel
  • Synonym: heinous - utterly wicked or evil
  • Antonym: merciful - marked by compassion or forgiveness

Etymology

From Latin monstrum (portent, monster) via Old French monstre and Middle English.
