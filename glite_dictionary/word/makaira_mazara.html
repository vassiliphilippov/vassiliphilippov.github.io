Makaira mazara

1. NOUN

/ məˈkaɪrə məˈzɑrə / (AmE), / məˈkaɪərə məˈzɑːrə / (BrE)
A large marlin species of the Pacific Ocean, also known as the Indo-Pacific blue marlin, which can reach up to 1,000 pounds in weight.
Indo-Pacific blue marlin species

Examples

Anglers consider the Makaira mazara to be one of the most prized catches in deep-sea fishing.
The Makaira mazara is capable of powerful bursts of speed when hunting smaller fish.
Scientists are studying the population decline of the Makaira mazara in the western Pacific.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marlin - a large game fish with a long pointed snout

Etymology

From New Latin, Makaira (genus name) and mazara (species name), used in scientific taxonomy.
