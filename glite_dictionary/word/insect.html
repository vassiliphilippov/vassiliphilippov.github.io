insect

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌsɛkt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.sɛkt / (BrE)
A person regarded as contemptible, insignificant, or despicable.
a contemptible or insignificant person

Examples

He treated his opponents as mere insects, unworthy of notice.
She felt crushed by his words, as if she were an insect under his heel.
The dictator referred to dissenters as insects to justify harsh measures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vermin - a person perceived as despicable or loathsome
  • Synonym: nobody - a person of no importance or influence

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌsɛkt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.sekt / (BrE)
a small air-breathing arthropod that typically has a segmented body, three pairs of legs, and, usually, two pairs of wings
small air-breathing arthropod

Examples

The insect landed on the window sill and buzzed softly.
Many farmers use pesticides to control harmful insects in their crops.
A butterfly is a beautiful type of insect found in gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bug - an informal term for an insect
  • Antonym: arachnid - a related arthropod group including spiders and scorpions, but not insects

Etymology

From Latin insectum, meaning 'cut into' (referring to the segmented body).
