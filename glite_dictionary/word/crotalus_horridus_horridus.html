Crotalus horridus horridus

1. NOUN

/ kroʊˈteɪləs hɒˈrɪdəs hɒˈrɪdəs / (AmE), / krəʊˈteɪləs hɒˈrɪdəs hɒˈrɪdəs / (BrE)
A subspecies of timber rattlesnake native to rugged areas of the eastern United States.
a subspecies of timber rattlesnake

Examples

The Crotalus horridus horridus is seldom seen except by experienced hikers in remote forests.
Venom from Crotalus horridus horridus is being researched for potential medical uses.
Conservationists are working to protect the habitats of Crotalus horridus horridus in the Appalachian Mountains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: timber rattlesnake - a species of venomous pit viper found in North America

Etymology

From Latin: *crotalus* (rattle) and *horridus* (rough, bristly).
