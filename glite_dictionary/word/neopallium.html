neopallium

1. NOUN

/ niː.oʊˈpæli.əm / (AmE), / niː.əʊˈpælɪ.əm / (BrE)
the most recently evolved part of the cerebral cortex, involved in higher brain functions such as sensory perception, cognition, and motor commands
part of the cerebral cortex
medical

Examples

The neopallium is responsible for advanced cognitive processes in mammals.
Researchers studied the evolution of the neopallium to understand brain complexity.
Damage to the neopallium can severely affect learning and memory functions.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: archipallium - the oldest part of the cerebral cortex
  • Degree Lower: paleopallium - an older part of the cerebral cortex than the neopallium but newer than the archipallium

Etymology

From Greek neo- (new) and Latin pallium (cloak, mantle), referring to new layers of the cortex.
