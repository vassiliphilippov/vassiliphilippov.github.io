cross-stitch

1. NOUN

/ krɔs stɪtʃ / (AmE), / krɒs stɪtʃ / (BrE)
A stitch made by crossing two stitches to form an X, often used in embroidery.
embroidery stitch forming an X

Examples

She decorated the pillowcase with a delicate cross-stitch pattern.
Each cross-stitch should be neat and evenly spaced for best results.
He learned to make a cross-stitch by watching online tutorials.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embroidery - decorative needlework sewn on fabric

Etymology

From 'cross' meaning to go across and 'stitch' (a loop of thread), referring to the crossed pattern.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔsˌstɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌstɪtʃ / (BrE)
a type of embroidery in which pairs of stitches cross each other to form an X shape
embroidery stitch forming an X shape

Examples

She spent the afternoon working on the cross-stitch pattern for her aunt's gift.
The pillowcases were decorated with delicate blue cross-stitch along the edges.
He learned how to do cross-stitch when he was a child.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embroidery - the art or process of decorating fabric with needle and thread

Etymology

From 'cross' (to go across) + 'stitch' (a loop of thread in sewing or knitting).
