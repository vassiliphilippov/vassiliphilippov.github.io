aboveboard

1. ADJ

/ əˈbʌvˌbɔrd / (AmE), / əˈbʌvˌbɔːd / (BrE)
honest and open; not secretive or deceptive
honest and open

Examples

We want to ensure all our business dealings are aboveboard and transparent.
The negotiations were completely aboveboard, with no hidden agendas.
If you keep everything aboveboard, you'll gain the trust of your colleagues.

Synset Examples

their business was open and aboveboard
straightforward in all his business affairs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honest - always telling the truth and never stealing or cheating
  • Antonym: deceptive - giving an appearance or impression different from the true one; misleading

Etymology

Originally from 17th-century gambling, meaning 'on or above the board (table),' referring to keeping cards visible.

2. ADV

/ əˈbʌvˌbɔrd / (AmE), / əˈbʌvˌbɔːd / (BrE)
In an honest and open way; without deception or concealment.
honestly; openly and fairly

Examples

All the negotiations were handled aboveboard and in full view of the public.
She made sure her business dealings remained aboveboard at all times.
If you keep everything aboveboard, you have nothing to fear from an audit.

Synset Examples

if he can't get it honestly, he is willing to steal it
was known for dealing aboveboard in everything

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honestly - in an honest manner
  • Antonym: deceptively - in a misleading or dishonest manner

Etymology

Probably from card playing, where keeping one's cards 'above board' (table) ensured honesty.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00315777-r
Synset POS: r
