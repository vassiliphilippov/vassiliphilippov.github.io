sousaphone

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuːzəˌfoʊn / (AmE), / ˈsuːzəfəʊn / (BrE)
a large brass wind instrument of the tuba family, designed to wrap around the body for ease of carrying while marching
a large brass marching instrument

Examples

The marching band featured a gleaming sousaphone that stood out during the parade.
She learned to play the sousaphone for her high school band.
A sousaphone is easier to carry than a concert tuba when marching.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tuba - a large brass wind instrument producing the lowest pitch in the brass family

Etymology

Named after John Philip Sousa, the American bandmaster who promoted its use.
