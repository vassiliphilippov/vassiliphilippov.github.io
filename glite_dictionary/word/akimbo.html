akimbo

1. ADJ

/ əˈkɪmboʊ / (AmE), / əˈkɪmbəʊ / (BrE)
(of limbs) bent outward with the joint away from the body, especially with hands on the hips and elbows turned outward.
with limbs bent outward at the joint

Examples

She walked in with her arms akimbo, ready to argue her case.
The children sat on the grass, legs akimbo, laughing together.
He glanced at the mirror and noticed his awkward stance, one leg akimbo against the chair.

Synset Examples

a tailor sitting with legs akimbo
stood with arms akimbo

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English 'in kenebowe' (in a keen bow), of uncertain origin, possibly influenced by Old Norse or Old French forms.

2. ADV

/ əˈkɪmboʊ / (AmE), / əˈkɪmbəʊ / (BrE)
with the hands on the hips and the elbows turned outward, typically to convey confidence or stance
with hands on hips and elbows out

Examples

She stood with her arms akimbo, glaring at the mess on the floor.
The children paused, hands akimbo, trying to catch their breath after the race.
With her elbows akimbo, she asked a question that silenced the room.

Synset Examples

she stood there akimbo

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English in kenebowe (in a sharp bend).
