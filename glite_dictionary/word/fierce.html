fierce

1. ADJ

/ fɪrs / (AmE), / fɪəs / (BrE)
Having or showing intense and violent force, energy, or aggressiveness.
intense and violent in force or energy

Examples

The fierce storm uprooted trees and damaged several houses along the coast.
She gave a fierce glare that silenced the noisy classroom instantly.
The debate became fierce as both sides defended their opinions passionately.

Synset Examples

a ferocious beating
fierce fighting
a furious battle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ferocious - marked by extreme and violent energy
  • Antonym: gentle - having or showing a mild, kind, or tender temperament

Etymology

From Middle English 'fierse', from Old French 'fers', from Latin 'ferus' (wild, untamed).
