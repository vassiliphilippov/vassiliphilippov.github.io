North Dakota

1. PROPN

/ nɔrθ dəˈkoʊtə / (AmE), / nɔːθ dəˈkəʊtə / (BrE)
A state in the north-central region of the United States, bordering Canada.
a north-central U.S. state

Examples

She grew up in North Dakota before moving to California for college.
The winters in North Dakota can be extremely cold and snowy.
Oil production has significantly boosted North Dakota's economy in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: South Dakota - a state directly south of North Dakota, also named after the Dakota people

Etymology

Named after the Dakota people, an Indigenous tribe of the northern Great Plains.
