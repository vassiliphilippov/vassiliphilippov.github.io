Anglo-Catholicism

1. NOUN

/ ˌæŋɡloʊ kəˈθɒlɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌæŋɡləʊ kəˈθɒlɪˌsɪzəm / (BrE)
A tradition and movement within the Church of England that emphasizes continuity with Catholic doctrine, liturgy, and church practices.
Church of England movement stressing Catholic traditions

Examples

As a follower of Anglo-Catholicism, he appreciated the Church's traditional rituals and symbolism.
The Oxford Movement played a key role in the revival of Anglo-Catholicism during the 19th century.
Many parishes embracing Anglo-Catholicism incorporate incense and elaborate vestments into their services.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: High Church - Favouring traditional rituals and liturgy in the Anglican Church
  • Antonym: Evangelicalism - Emphasis on the Protestant aspects of the Church of England

Etymology

Formed from 'Anglo-' (English) and 'Catholicism'.
