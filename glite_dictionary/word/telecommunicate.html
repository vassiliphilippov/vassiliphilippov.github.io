to telecommunicate

1. VERB

/ ˌtɛlɪkəˈmjunɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˌtɛlɪkəˈmjuːnɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to communicate with someone over a long distance using electronic means, such as telephone or email
communicate electronically over a distance

Examples

Many employees now telecommunicate with clients across the world rather than meet in person.
As technology advanced, people started to telecommunicate more frequently through video calls and emails.
Because of the storm, the team decided to telecommunicate instead of gathering at the office.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to communicate - to share or exchange information

Etymology

From 'tele-' (Greek for 'far') + 'communicate'.
