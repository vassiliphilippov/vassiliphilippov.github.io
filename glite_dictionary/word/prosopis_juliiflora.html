Prosopis juliiflora

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈsoʊpɪs ˌdʒuːliɪˈflɔːrə / (AmE), / prəʊˈsəʊpɪs ˌdʒuːliɪˈflɔːrə / (BrE)
a species of mesquite tree native to the Gulf Coast and Caribbean Islands from Mexico to Venezuela, known for its hardiness and invasive potential
a species of mesquite tree

Examples

The dense thickets of Prosopis juliiflora have altered the landscape in many parts of Africa.
Farmers often struggle to control the spread of Prosopis juliiflora on their land.
The wood of Prosopis juliiflora is frequently used as fuel in rural communities.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Prosopis' is the genus name, and 'juliiflora' means 'having catkin-like flowers'.
