Cucurbitaceae

1. NOUN

/ kuːˌkɝːbɪˈteɪsiˌiː / (AmE), / kjuːˌkɜːbɪˈteɪsiːiː / (BrE)
a family of herbaceous vines that includes cucumbers, melons, squashes, and pumpkins
family of vines (cucumber, melon, squash)

Examples

The Cucurbitaceae family includes many familiar fruits such as watermelon and pumpkin.
Scientists study Cucurbitaceae to learn about vine growth and fruit development.
Many gardeners grow members of the Cucurbitaceae family in their vegetable patches.

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Latin cucurbita (gourd, pumpkin).

2. NOUN

/ kuːˌkɝːbɪˈteɪʃiˌaɪ / (AmE), / kjuːˌkɜːbɪˈteɪʃɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of herbaceous vine plants that includes cucumbers, melons, squashes, and pumpkins
plant family of vines including cucumbers and melons

Examples

Many edible plants, such as melons and squashes, belong to the Cucurbitaceae family.
Botanists study the Cucurbitaceae for their agricultural importance worldwide.
Pumpkins, a member of the Cucurbitaceae, are used in a variety of dishes.

Etymology

From Latin 'cucurbita' (gourd, pumpkin) + botanical suffix '-aceae'.
