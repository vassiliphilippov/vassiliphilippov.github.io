Xyris operculata

1. NOUN

/ ˈzaɪrɪs ɒpərˈkjuːlɑːtə / (AmE), / ˈzaɪrɪs ɒpəˈkjuːlɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of yellow-eyed grass native to Australia, belonging to the family Xyridaceae.
Australian species of yellow-eyed grass

Examples

The wetlands are home to Xyris operculata, which blooms in late summer.
Botanists discovered a rare population of Xyris operculata during their field survey.
Xyris operculata can be distinguished from similar species by its unique flower structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yellow-eyed grass - Any plant of the genus Xyris, with grass-like leaves and yellow flowers

Etymology

From genus name Xyris (Greek: xuron, razor, alluding to the leaves), and Latin operculata (covered with a lid or cap).
