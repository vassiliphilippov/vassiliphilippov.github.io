ascription

1. NOUN

/ əˈskrɪpʃən / (AmE), / əˈskrɪpʃən / (BrE)
the act of attributing something to a particular cause, source, or author
attributing a cause, source, or author

Examples

The ascription of the novel to that author has been disputed by scholars.
Some cultures differ in their ascription of natural events to supernatural causes.
His sudden success led to the ascription of many talents he did not possess.

Synset Examples

the attribution of lighting to an expression of God's wrath
he questioned the attribution of the painting to Picasso

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attribution - the act of regarding something as being caused by a person or thing

Etymology

From Latin ascriptio (an enrolling, assignment), from ascribere (to assign).

2. NOUN

/ əˈskrɪp.ʃən / (AmE), / əˈskrɪp.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of assigning a quality, characteristic, or attribution to a person or thing
act of assigning a quality or attribution

Examples

Many scholars disagree with the ascription of this poem to Shakespeare.
The ascription of blame was unfair given the circumstances.
His ascription of genius to her surprised the entire committee.

Synset Examples

the attribution of language to birds
the ascription to me of honors I had not earned

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attribution - the act of regarding something as being caused by a person or thing

Etymology

From Latin 'ascriptio' (a writing in, assignment).
