aquatint

1. NOUN

/ ˈækwəˌtɪnt / (AmE), / ˈakwətɪnt / (BrE)
An etching produced by a printmaking process that creates tonal effects resembling a watercolor.
an etching with watercolor-like tones

Examples

Her latest exhibition includes several beautiful aquatints inspired by urban landscapes.
The artist used the aquatint technique to achieve rich, subtle gradations of tone in his prints.
We admired the delicate colors in the aquatint hanging in the front hall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: etching - a print produced from a metal plate etched with acid

Etymology

From Italian *acqua tinta* (dyed water), referring to the use of acid and water in the process.

2. VERB

/ ˈækwəˌtɪnt / (AmE), / ˈakwətɪnt / (BrE)
to etch a design onto a metal plate using the aquatint technique, producing areas of tone and shading.
etch using the aquatint process

Examples

The artist carefully aquatinted the copper plate to achieve subtle gradations of tone.
She spent hours aquatinting her print, adding depth to the composition.
Several famous prints were aquatinted in the 18th century using this delicate process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to etch - to engrave a design with acid on a surface

Etymology

From Italian *acqua tinta* (dyed water), referring to the method's use of acid and resin.

3. NOUN

/ ˈæk.wəˌtɪnt / (AmE), / ˈakwətɪnt / (BrE)
a method of etching that produces areas of tone resembling watercolor washes, commonly used in printmaking.
printmaking technique resembling watercolor washes

Examples

She specializes in making fine aquatint prints that capture the subtle nuances of light and shadow.
The artist used an aquatint technique to achieve the rich tonal textures in her landscape.
We admired the museum’s collection of eighteenth-century aquatint engravings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: etching - the practice of using acid to cut into a metal surface to create a design in intaglio in printmaking

Etymology

From Italian 'acqua tinta' (dyed water), from 'acqua' (water) + 'tinta' (dyed).
