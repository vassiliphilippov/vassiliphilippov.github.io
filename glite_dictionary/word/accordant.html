accordant

1. ADJ

/ əˈkɔrdənt / (AmE), / əˈkɔːdənt / (BrE)
in agreement or harmony; consistent with or conforming to something else
in agreement; consistent

Examples

His actions were accordant with his stated beliefs.
The policy is accordant with international regulations.
Their decisions were not accordant with company values.

Synset Examples

salaries agreeable with current trends
plans conformable with your wishes
expressed views concordant with his background

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concordant - being in agreement or harmony
  • Antonym: discordant - not in agreement or harmony

Etymology

From Old French 'acordant', from Latin 'accordare' (to agree).

2. ADJ

/ əˈkɔrdənt / (AmE), / əˈkɔːdənt / (BrE)
being in agreement or harmony with something or someone, often used with 'with' to show alignment or conformity.
in agreement or harmony

Examples

His decision was accordant with the values of the company.
The music played in the background was accordant with the peaceful atmosphere of the garden.
Their views on climate policy were completely accordant, leading to a successful partnership.

Synset Examples

a place perfectly accordant with man's nature

Related Terms

  • Antonym: discordant - not in agreement or harmony
  • Synonym: consonant - in agreement or harmony, consistent

Etymology

From Old French 'acordant', from 'acorder' (to agree), from Latin 'accordare' (to be of one heart).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00555061-a
Synset POS: a
