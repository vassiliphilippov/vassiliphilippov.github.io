Trichophaga

1. NOUN

/ trɪˈkɑfəgə / (AmE), / trɪˈkɒfəgə / (BrE)
A genus of small moths whose larvae are known for feeding on natural fibers, including carpets and clothing.
genus of carpet moths

Examples

The larvae of Trichophaga can cause extensive damage to wool carpets in homes.
Museum conservators are wary of Trichophaga infestations in historic textile collections.
You might spot cases of Trichophaga moths near stored clothing made from natural fibers.

Etymology

From Greek 'trichos' (hair) + 'phagein' (to eat).

2. NOUN

/ trɪˈkɑfəɡə / (AmE), / trɪˈkɒfəɡə / (BrE)
A genus of small moths in the family Tineidae, commonly known as carpet moths, whose larvae feed on textiles such as wool and carpets.
genus of carpet moths

Examples

The larvae of Trichophaga can cause considerable damage to woolen carpets and clothing.
A pest control specialist identified the infestation as being due to Trichophaga species.
Researchers are studying the diet preferences of Trichophaga moths in urban environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carpet moth - A moth whose larvae feed on wool and carpets

Etymology

From Greek 'tricho-' meaning 'hair' and 'phaga' meaning 'eater'.
