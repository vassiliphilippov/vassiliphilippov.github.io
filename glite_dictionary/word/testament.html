testament

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛstəmənt / (AmE), / ˈtɛstəmənt / (BrE)
either of the two main divisions of the Christian Bible, the Old Testament and the New Testament.
part of the Christian Bible

Examples

Many Christians read the Testament every day as part of their devotional practice.
The New Testament contains the Gospels and letters to the early churches.
Scholars often compare stories from the Old Testament and the New Testament.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Old Testament - the first main part of the Christian Bible, based on the Hebrew Scriptures
  • Degree Higher: New Testament - the second main part of the Christian Bible, concerning the life and teachings of Jesus

Etymology

From Latin 'testamentum' (will, covenant), from 'testari' (to bear witness).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛstəmənt / (AmE), / ˈtɛstəmənt / (BrE)
Something that serves as strong evidence or proof of a particular fact, event, or quality.
strong evidence or proof

Examples

Her success is a testament to her dedication and hard work.
The museum itself stands as a testament to the city’s rich history.
The restored painting is a testament to the skill of the conservators.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proof - evidence or argument establishing a fact or the truth of a statement
  • Synonym: evidence - the available body of facts indicating whether a belief is true

Etymology

From Latin testamentum (will, testament), from testari (to testify).

Examples
