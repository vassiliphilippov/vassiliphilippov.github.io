cataphatism

1. NOUN

/ ˌkætəˈfeɪtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌkatəˈfeɪtɪzəm / (BrE)
The doctrine that God can be known and described by positive statements or affirmations, rather than solely by negation.
doctrine of positive knowledge of God

Examples

The theologian emphasized cataphatism as a way to understand divine attributes through affirmative statements.
Many religious traditions utilize both cataphatism and apophatism to discuss the nature of the divine.
In his sermon, the priest favored cataphatism by describing God in terms of love and wisdom.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: apophatism - the doctrine that God can only be described in terms of what He is not (negative theology)

Etymology

From Greek kataphasis (affirmation), from kata- (down, according to) + phanai (to speak).
