exoticism

1. NOUN

/ ɪɡˈzɑtɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɒtɪsɪzəm / (BrE)
the quality or charm of being unusual and strikingly different from what is familiar or customary, especially due to foreignness
quality of being exotic or foreign

Examples

The exoticism of the island made it a popular destination for adventurous travelers.
Some artists use elements of exoticism to create a sense of mystery in their work.
He was fascinated by the exoticism of the spices and fabrics found in the marketplace.

Synset Examples

he loved the exoticism of Egypt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exotic - strikingly unusual or different, often due to being foreign

Etymology

From French exotisme, from exotique (exotic), ultimately from Greek exōtikos (from the outside).

2. NOUN

/ ɪɡˈzɑː.tɪ.sɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɒt.ɪ.sɪzəm / (BrE)
The quality of being unusual and interesting because it is from a distant or foreign place or culture.
the quality of being exotic

Examples

She was fascinated by the exoticism of the traditional music she heard overseas.
The restaurant's décor adds an air of exoticism to the dining experience.
Some critics argue that exoticism in art often leads to stereotypes.

Synset Examples

he loved the exoticism of Egypt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foreignness - the quality of being foreign
  • Antonym: familiarity - the quality of being familiar

Etymology

From French exotisme, based on exotic, from Latin exoticus (foreign).
