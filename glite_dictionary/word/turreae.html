Turreae

1. NOUN

/ tʊˈriː.i / (AmE), / tʊˈriː.i / (BrE)
A group of trees and shrubs, chiefly grown for their glossy leaves and sweetly fragrant, star-shaped flowers, often found in tropical regions.
group of trees and shrubs with fragrant starry flowers

Examples

Many botanical gardens cultivate Turreae species for their ornamental value.
The vivid foliage of Turreae plants is a highlight among tropical collections.
Flower arrangers appreciate Turreae flowers for their lasting fragrance and beauty.
