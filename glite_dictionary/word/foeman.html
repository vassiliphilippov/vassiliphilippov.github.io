foeman

1. NOUN

/ ˈfoʊ.mən / (AmE), / ˈfəʊ.mən / (BrE)
a male enemy, especially a soldier or armed adversary in battle; chiefly literary or archaic
male enemy or adversary, especially in war
archaic

Examples

The knight faced his foeman with unwavering courage.
In ancient tales, the hero always defeats his foeman in single combat.
The foeman retreated, defeated and humbled by the unexpected strategy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enemy - a person who is actively opposed or hostile
  • Antonym: friend - a person with whom one has a bond of mutual affection

Etymology

From Old English fāmǣn, compound of 'foe' and 'man'.
