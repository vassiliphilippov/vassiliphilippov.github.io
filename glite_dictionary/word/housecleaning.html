housecleaning

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaʊsˌkliːnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhaʊsˌkliːnɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of cleaning the rooms and furnishings of a house
cleaning the interior of a house

Examples

We devoted our Saturday to housecleaning after a busy week.
Spring housecleaning is a tradition in many households.
She hired a service to help with the heavy housecleaning.

Synset Examples

efficient housecleaning should proceed one room at a time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cleaning - the act of making something clean

Etymology

Formed from 'house' + 'cleaning' (clean + -ing suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhaʊsˌkliːnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhaʊsˌkliːnɪŋ / (BrE)
(figurative) the process of removing unwanted people, practices, or conditions in order to reform an organization or system.
removal of unwanted elements for reform

Examples

After the merger, the new management began a thorough housecleaning of the staff and procedures.
The scandal prompted a much-needed housecleaning throughout the entire department.
Many outdated practices were swept away during the recent housecleaning at city hall.

Synset Examples

more housecleaning is in store at other accounting firms
many employees were discharged in a general housecleaning by the new owners

Related Terms

  • Synonym: purge - an abrupt or systematic removal of people, often for political reasons

Etymology

Figurative use based on the literal sense of cleaning a house.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00581602-n
Synset POS: n
