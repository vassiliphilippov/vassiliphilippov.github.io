croup

1. NOUN

/ kruːp / (AmE), / kruːp / (BrE)
A disease of infants and young children characterized by a harsh cough, hoarseness, fever, and difficulty breathing due to inflammation of the air passages.
respiratory disease in young children
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the child with croup after hearing his barking cough and examining his throat.
Parents should seek medical attention if their child with croup has trouble breathing or stridor.
During the winter, cases of croup often increase among infants and toddlers.

Etymology

From Old French 'croupe' (swelling, lump), later used in medical Latin as 'croupus' for this respiratory illness.

2. NOUN

/ kruːp / (AmE), / kruːp / (BrE)
the part of an animal, especially a horse, that corresponds to the human buttocks, located at the top of the hindquarters
hindquarters region of an animal

Examples

The horse had an injury on its croup after the jump.
Proper grooming of the croup is essential for show horses.
The stallion's croup was well-muscled and sloped gently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rump - the hind part of the body of an animal, especially a quadruped

Etymology

From French croupe (rump), ultimately from Old French croup (hump, swelling).
