gamble

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæm.bəl / (AmE), / ˈɡæm.bəl / (BrE)
an act of risking money on a game or event for the chance of monetary gain
money risked in hopes of gain

Examples

He placed a gamble on the horse in the third race.
The lottery ticket was just a small gamble for him.
She lost her entire week's wages on a single gamble.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bet - an amount of money staked in a gamble

Etymology

Perhaps from English 'game' + '-ble', possibly influenced by Middle English 'gamel' (to play, sport).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13364002-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈɡæm.bəl / (AmE), / ˈɡæm.bəl / (BrE)
to play games of chance for money; to bet money on uncertain outcomes
play games of chance for money

Examples

She loves to gamble in Las Vegas on the slot machines.
You should never gamble more money than you can afford to lose.
Many people gamble online, attracted by the hope of big winnings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bet - risk something, usually money, on the outcome of a game or event

Etymology

Probably from the obsolete 'gamble' (to play games), a frequentative of 'game', from Old English 'gamen' (joy, sport, game).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01140949-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈɡæmbəl / (AmE), / ˈɡæmbəl / (BrE)
to take a risk in the hope of a favorable or advantageous outcome, especially in situations involving uncertainty
take a risk in hopes of success

Examples

Investing in a startup can be a risky move, but many entrepreneurs are willing to gamble on success.
She decided to gamble her savings on a new business idea.
Every time you make a bold career change, you gamble with your future.

Synset Examples

When you buy these stocks you are gambling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: risk - to expose oneself to danger or loss; to take a chance
  • Synonym: bet - to stake money or something of value on the outcome of an event

Etymology

From Middle English gamelen (to play, jest), from Old English gamol (game, play).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02550045-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ ˈɡæmbəl / (AmE), / ˈɡæmbəl / (BrE)
a risky act or undertaking in which the outcome is uncertain and success depends largely on chance
risky act or undertaking

Examples

Starting your own business can be a big gamble if you don't have a clear plan.
Investing in that startup was a huge gamble, but it paid off.
Taking the mountain road at night was a dangerous gamble given the weather conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: risk - the possibility or chance of loss, danger, or injury
  • Antonym: certainty - something that is completely predictable or certain

Etymology

From the verb 'gamble', derived from 'game' with the frequentative suffix '-le'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00804623-n
Synset POS: n
