pyroligneous

1. ADJ

/ ˌpaɪroʊˈlɪdʒəniəs / (AmE), / ˌpɪrəˈlɪdʒɪniəs / (BrE)
(of a substance) produced by the effect of heat on wood, especially by destructive distillation.
produced by heating wood; from wood distillation

Examples

The chemical was identified as a pyroligneous acid derived from the destructive distillation of timber.
Early industrial processes often used pyroligneous liquids in the production of acetic acid.
A smoky flavor can result from traces of pyroligneous substances left in the food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pyrolytic - relating to decomposition by heat

Etymology

From French *pyroligineux*, from Greek *pyr* (fire) + Latin *lignum* (wood).
