scrimpy

1. ADJ

/ ˈskrɪmpi / (AmE), / ˈskrɪmpi / (BrE)
insufficient in amount, quality, or extent; scanty or meager
insufficient; scanty or meager

Examples

His scrimpy lunch left him still feeling hungry by afternoon.
The scrimpy fabric barely covered the window, letting in too much light.
Guests complained about the hotel's scrimpy breakfast portions.

Synset Examples

meager resources
meager fare

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meager - lacking in quantity or quality
  • Synonym: scanty - small or insufficient in amount

Etymology

From 'scrimp' (to be frugal or sparing), with the adjectival suffix '-y'.
