one-member

1. ADJ

/ wʌn ˈmɛmbɚ / (AmE), / wʌn ˈmɛmbə / (BrE)
(of a legislative district or similar body) having only one representative or member elected from each constituency.
having only one member (e.g., per district)

Examples

Under the new law, each district will have a one-member representation in the assembly.
Proponents of the one-member system argue it increases accountability to constituents.
The country switched from a multi-member to a one-member electoral district model last year.

Synset Examples

a uninominal electoral system

Related Terms

  • Antonym: multi-member - having more than one representative per district
  • Synonym: single-member - having a single representative per legislative district
  • Synonym: uninominal - based on the system of electing one member per district
