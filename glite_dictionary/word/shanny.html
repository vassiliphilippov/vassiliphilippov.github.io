shanny

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃæni / (AmE), / ˈʃæni / (BrE)
A small European blenny (Lipophrys pholis) commonly found in rocky coastal waters, notable for its lack of scales.
a European blenny fish

Examples

We spotted a shanny hiding among the rocks at low tide.
The shanny is well adapted to life in shallow pools along the European coast.
Fishermen sometimes see shannies darting between crevices near the shoreline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blenny - a small marine fish of the family Blenniidae
