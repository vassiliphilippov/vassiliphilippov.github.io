lordliness

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɔrdlinəs / (AmE), / ˈlɔːdlɪnəs / (BrE)
an overbearing or superior manner arising from excessive pride or a sense of superiority
overbearing pride; superior manner

Examples

His lordliness was apparent in the way he dismissed everyone else's opinions without a second thought.
The lordliness of the old aristocrat made others in the room uneasy.
She resented the lordliness with which her superior treated the staff.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrogance - an attitude of superiority manifested in overbearing manner
  • Antonym: humility - a modest or low view of one's own importance; humbleness

Etymology

From 'lordly' + '-ness'; 'lordly' ultimately from Old English 'hlaford' (lord).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɔrdli.nəs / (AmE), / ˈlɔːdli.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of behaving or appearing in a dignified, commanding, or stately manner
dignified and commanding manner or appearance

Examples

He entered the room with an air of lordliness that captured everyone’s attention.
Despite his humble origins, his lordliness was apparent in every word and gesture.
Some admired her lordliness, while others thought it seemed arrogant.

Synset Examples

he behaved with great dignity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dignity - the state or quality of being worthy of honor or respect

Etymology

From 'lordly' + '-ness', formed on the model of 'lord' (Old English hlāford).
