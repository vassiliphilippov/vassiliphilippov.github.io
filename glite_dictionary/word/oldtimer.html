oldtimer

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊldˌtaɪmər / (AmE), / ˈəʊl(d)ˌtaɪmə / (BrE)
A person who has had long experience in a particular job, organization, or activity; someone who has been involved for a long time.
experienced person; veteran

Examples

The oldtimer shared stories from decades of working at the factory.
Every company needs an oldtimer to show newcomers the ropes.
As an oldtimer, she helped everyone navigate tough times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: veteran - a person with long experience in a particular field

Etymology

From 'old' + 'timer', originally U.S. colloquial, late 19th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊldˌtaɪmɚ / (AmE), / ˈəʊldˌtaɪmə / (BrE)
an elderly man, especially one who is respected or experienced
elderly man; experienced elder
informal

Examples

The small town was full of oldtimers who remembered its early days.
That oldtimer at the store always has a story to tell.
After fifty years at the company, he's respected as the resident oldtimer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: senior citizen - an elderly person, typically someone over 65
  • Antonym: youngster - a young person or child

Etymology

From 'old' + 'timer', originally American English slang for an old or veteran person.
