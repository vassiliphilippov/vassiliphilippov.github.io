Thamnophis sauritus

1. NOUN

/ θæmˈnɑːfɪs sɔːˈraɪtəs / (AmE), / θamˈnɒfɪs sɔːˈrɪtəs / (BrE)
a slender, yellow-striped North American garter snake, commonly found near water and known as the ribbon snake
a yellow-striped North American ribbon snake

Examples

The Thamnophis sauritus is often seen swimming in marshes and ponds.
Herpetologists observe Thamnophis sauritus to study its unique yellow stripes.
Unlike some other garter snakes, Thamnophis sauritus prefers wetter habitats.

Etymology

From Greek 'thamnos' (bush) + 'ophis' (snake); Latin species epithet 'sauritus' referring to lizard-like features.
