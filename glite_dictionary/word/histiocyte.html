histiocyte

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɪstioʊˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈhɪstɪə(ʊ)sʌɪt / (BrE)
a type of macrophage found in connective tissue, involved in the immune response by engulfing foreign particles and debris
macrophage in connective tissue
medical

Examples

A histiocyte can engulf bacteria and dead cells as part of the immune response.
The pathologist identified an increased number of histiocytes in the tissue sample.
Some rare diseases are characterized by an abnormal proliferation of histiocyte populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: macrophage - a large phagocytic cell found in tissues or as a mobile white blood cell

Etymology

From Greek 'histion' (web, tissue) + '-cyte' (cell) from Greek 'kytos' (container, cell).
