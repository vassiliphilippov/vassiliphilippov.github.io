innumerableness

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈnuːmərəblnəs / (AmE), / ɪˈnjuːmərəblnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being too many to be counted; countlessness
the state of being countless

Examples

The innumerableness of the stars in the sky left her in awe.
Historians often remark on the innumerableness of cultural influences seen in ancient cities.
He marveled at the innumerableness of grains of sand on the beach.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: countlessness - the state of being countless
  • Antonym: finiteness - the state of having limits or bounds

Etymology

From 'innumerable' + '-ness', with 'innumerable' from Latin innumerabilis (countless).
