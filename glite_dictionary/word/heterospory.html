heterospory

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛtəˈrɑːspəri / (AmE), / ˌhɛtəˈrɒspəri / (BrE)
the production of both microspores and megaspores by the same plant, leading to separate male and female gametophytes
production of two types of spores in plants
medical

Examples

The evolution of heterospory marked a significant event in the diversification of land plants.
Ferns exhibit either heterospory or homospory depending on the species.
The presence of heterospory allows for the separation of male and female reproductive roles in plants.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: homospory - production of only one type of spore in plants

Etymology

From Greek hetero- (different) + spora (seed, sowing).
