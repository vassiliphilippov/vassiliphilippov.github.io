grandmaster

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrændˌmæstər / (AmE), / ˈɡrɑːnˌmɑːstə / (BrE)
a player of exceptional or world-class skill in chess or bridge, officially recognized as holding the highest title awarded in these games
world-class chess or bridge player

Examples

She became a chess grandmaster at the age of fifteen, breaking several records.
The bridge grandmaster is known for his brilliant strategies and calm demeanor under pressure.
Many aspire to earn the title of grandmaster in the competitive world of chess.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: international master - a chess title ranking below grandmaster
  • Degree Lower: master - a skilled player, but lower than grandmaster in chess

Etymology

From 'grand' (great, important) + 'master' (expert at a skill); used for high-ranking chess and bridge titles from the 20th century.
