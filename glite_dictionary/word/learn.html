to learn

1. VERB

/ lɝn / (AmE), / lɜːn / (BrE)
to be a student of a particular subject, or to acquire knowledge by studying it
be a student of a subject

Examples

She decided to learn French at university.
He wants to learn physics to become an engineer.
Many students travel abroad to learn new subjects.

Synset Examples

She is reading for the bar exam

Related Terms

  • Synonym: study - to devote time and attention to acquiring knowledge of a subject

Etymology

Old English *leornian* (to learn); related to German *lernen* and Dutch *leren*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00601387-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ lɝn / (AmE), / lɜːn / (BrE)
to gain knowledge or skill in a subject by study, experience, or teaching
acquire knowledge or skill

Examples

She hopes to learn French before her trip to Paris.
Children learn about dinosaurs in their science class.
It takes time to learn how to play the guitar well.

Synset Examples

She learned dancing from her sister
I learned Sanskrit
Children acquire language at an amazing rate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to memorize - to commit something to memory
  • Antonym: to forget - to fail to remember

Etymology

From Old English 'leornian' (to get knowledge, be cultivated), of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00599310-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ lɝn / (AmE), / lɜːn / (BrE)
to discover or find out about something, often by chance rather than deliberate study
discover or become aware of

Examples

I learned that the meeting was rescheduled only after I arrived at the office.
She learned about the surprise party from a mutual friend.
He learns of his promotion through a company-wide email.

Synset Examples

I learned that she has two grown-up children
I see that you have been promoted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to find out - to discover information, especially by making an effort
  • Synonym: to discover - to become aware of something not previously known

Etymology

From Old English 'leornian' (to get knowledge), related to German 'lernen', Dutch 'leren'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00600349-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ lɜrn / (AmE), / lɜːn / (BrE)
To commit information to memory so that it can be recalled later; to memorize.
commit to memory, memorize

Examples

She had to learn all the vocabulary words for the test.
Actors must learn their lines before rehearsals begin.
It took me a week to learn the poem by heart.

Synset Examples

Have you memorized your lines for the play yet?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to memorize - to commit something to memory

Etymology

Old English *leornian* (to get knowledge), related to German *lernen*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00605971-v
Synset POS: v
