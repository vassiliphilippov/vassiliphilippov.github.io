preformation

1. NOUN

/ ˌpriːfɔrˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːfɔːˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
The now obsolete biological theory that an individual organism develops by enlargement of a fully formed miniature version of itself that already exists in the germ cell.
discredited theory of embryonic development

Examples

The theory of preformation was widely accepted before advances in microscopy challenged its assumptions.
Modern biology has rejected preformation in favor of the concept of epigenesis.
During the 18th century, debates over preformation and epigenesis shaped the study of embryology.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: epigenesis - theory that an organism develops from the successive formation of new parts

Etymology

From Latin praeformare (to form beforehand), via French préformation.
