myxomycete

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪksoʊˈmaɪsiːt / (AmE), / ˌmɪksəʊˈmʌɪsiːt / (BrE)
a slime mold belonging to the class Myxomycetes, a group of fungus-like organisms found in moist environments
a member of the class Myxomycetes; a slime mold

Examples

Many myxomycetes can be found on decaying logs in damp forests.
Scientists study myxomycetes to better understand their unique life cycles.
One species of myxomycete produced strikingly colorful fruiting bodies in the laboratory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slime mold - a general term for eukaryotic organisms belonging to several different groups, including Myxomycetes

Etymology

From Greek myxa (mucus, slime) + mykēs (fungus) + scientific suffix -ete.
