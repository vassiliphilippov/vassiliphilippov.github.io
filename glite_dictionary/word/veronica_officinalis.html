Veronica officinalis

1. NOUN

/ vəˈrɑnɪkə ˌɑfɪˈnɑlɪs / (AmE), / vəˈrɒnɪkə ˌɒfɪˈnælɪs / (BrE)
A common perennial European plant with hairy stems and pale blue or lilac flowers borne in axillary clusters, also known as common speedwell.
species of speedwell with pale blue flowers

Examples

The leaves of Veronica officinalis are sometimes used to make herbal tea.
We found Veronica officinalis growing wild in the meadow during our walk.
Gardeners appreciate Veronica officinalis for its attractive, pale blue flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speedwell - common name for plants in the genus Veronica

Etymology

From Latin: genus name Veronica and specific epithet officinalis, meaning 'used in medicine'.
