staining

1. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsteɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of marking or discoloring a surface with a stain
act of marking with a stain

Examples

The staining of the carpet made it look old and dirty.
Proper staining of wood can enhance its natural beauty.
She was worried that the red wine would cause permanent staining on the tablecloth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spotting - the act of making spots or marks
  • Antonym: cleaning - the act of removing stains or dirt

2. NOUN

/ ˈsteɪ.nɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsteɪ.nɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of applying a dye to biological specimens to enhance their visibility under a microscope
dyeing specimens for microscopic study
medical

Examples

The pathologist used a special staining technique to highlight abnormal cells in the tissue sample.
Accurate staining is essential for distinguishing different types of bacteria under the microscope.
After staining, the slides were examined to identify the presence of specific proteins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dyeing - the process of coloring materials with a dye
  • Antonym: bleaching - the process of removing color from something

Etymology

From the verb 'stain', ultimately from Old French 'esteindre' (to quench, extinguish), influenced by Late Latin 'distinguere' (to separate).
