reentry

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːˈɛntri / (AmE), / ˌriːˈɛntri / (BrE)
the act of entering again, especially after having left or exited before
the act of entering again

Examples

The astronaut prepared for reentry into Earth's atmosphere.
After the fire drill, the students waited outside until reentry was permitted.
A parole violation can prevent reentry into society after release from prison.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entry - the act of going or coming in

Etymology

From Latin re- (again) + entry.
