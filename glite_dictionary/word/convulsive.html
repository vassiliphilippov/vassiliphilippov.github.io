convulsive

1. ADJ

/ kənˈvʌl.sɪv / (AmE), / kənˈvʌl.sɪv / (BrE)
(of movements or actions) characterized by sudden, violent, and involuntary muscle contractions; resembling or causing convulsions.
characterized by sudden, involuntary muscle movements

Examples

The patient experienced convulsive movements after the medication was administered.
Her laughter became convulsive, shaking her entire body uncontrollably.
He was taken to the hospital after a convulsive fit left him unable to stand.

Synset Examples

convulsive motions
his body made a spasmodic jerk
spastic movements

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spasmodic - occurring in sudden brief bursts, like a spasm
  • Synonym: involuntary - not under conscious control

Etymology

From Latin convulsivus, from convellere (to wrench).

2. ADJ

/ kənˈvʌlsɪv / (AmE), / kənˈvʌlsɪv / (BrE)
Sudden, violent, and uncontrollable, resembling a convulsion.
sudden and violent, like a convulsion

Examples

He erupted into convulsive laughter at the comedian's joke.
The patient was seized with convulsive movements during the episode.
Her convulsive sobs echoed through the empty house.

Synset Examples

a convulsive rage
convulsive laughter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spasmodic - occurring in bursts or spasms, similar to convulsive

Etymology

From Latin 'convulsivus', from 'convulsio' (a convulsion).

Synset POS: s
