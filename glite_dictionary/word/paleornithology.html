paleornithology

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪliɔːrnɪˈθɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌpalɪɔːnɪˈθɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The scientific study of ancient or fossil birds and their evolutionary history.
study of ancient or fossil birds
medical

Examples

She devoted her career to paleornithology, focusing on the evolution of prehistoric birds.
New fossil discoveries have significantly advanced the field of paleornithology.
A background in geology and biology is helpful for research in paleornithology.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: ornithology - the scientific study of birds

Etymology

From Greek 'palaio-' (ancient) + 'ornithology' (study of birds).
