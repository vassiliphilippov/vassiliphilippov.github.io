pea-souper

1. NOUN

/ piːˈsuːpər / (AmE), / piːˈsuːpə / (BrE)
a very thick, yellowish fog, especially one characteristic of industrial cities in Britain in the past
thick yellowish fog, esp. historic Britain
british

Examples

By morning, the entire city was shrouded in a pea-souper that made driving impossible.
Old London was notorious for the pea-soupers caused by coal smoke and damp air.
The detective novel opens on a night of thick pea-souper settling over the murky streets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fog - a thick cloud of tiny water droplets near the ground

Etymology

Refers to the color and thickness resembling pea soup; originated in Britain during industrial times.

2. NOUN

/ piˈsuːpər / (AmE), / piːˈsuːpə / (BrE)
a very thick, yellowish fog, especially one caused by air pollution; formerly common in British cities
thick yellowish fog
informal british

Examples

London was notorious for its pea-soupers during the early twentieth century.
The thick pea-souper made it nearly impossible to see the street ahead.
We had to wait until the pea-souper lifted before continuing our journey.

Synset Examples

We got lost in the pea-souper on our way home.
The city was shrouded in a pea-souper fog this morning.
Driving was challenging in the thick pea-souper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fog - a thick cloud of tiny water droplets suspended in the atmosphere
  • Synonym: smog - fog combined with smoke or air pollution

Etymology

From 'pea soup' (for color and thickness) + '-er', British slang from the 19th century.
