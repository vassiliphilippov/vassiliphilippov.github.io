coracle

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrəkəl / (AmE), / ˈkɒrəkl / (BrE)
a small, round, lightweight boat made by stretching animal hides or canvas over a wicker frame, traditionally used in Wales and parts of western Britain
small round boat of hides on a wicker frame

Examples

The fishermen paddled the coracle quietly across the river.
A display at the museum showed how a traditional coracle is constructed.
Tourists were fascinated by the unusual shape of the old coracle on the riverbank.

Etymology

From Welsh 'corwg', meaning a boat of wickerwork and hides.
