gaga

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡɑː.ɡə / (AmE), / ˈɡɑː.ɡə / (BrE)
extremely enthusiastic, excited, or infatuated with someone or something; preoccupied or obsessed.
extremely enthusiastic or infatuated
informal

Examples

Ever since she met him, she's been gaga over him.
The crowd went gaga when the band took the stage.
He's absolutely gaga about vintage cars.

Synset Examples

crazy about cars and racing
they are dotty about each other
gaga over the rock group's new album

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crazy - extremely enthusiastic or very interested in something
  • Synonym: dotty - infatuated or obsessed with someone or something

Etymology

Probably from French 'gaga' (senile, crazy), of imitative origin.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡɑːɡɑː / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːɡɑː / (BrE)
No longer able to think or behave normally because of old age; feeble-minded or mentally confused, especially due to age.
mentally weakened with age

Examples

After turning ninety, her great-uncle became a bit gaga and often forgot familiar faces.
Don't mind her odd stories—she's gone gaga in her old age.
The children were amused by their gaga grandfather's eccentric behavior.

Synset Examples

his mother was doddering and frail

Related Terms

  • Synonym: senile - showing weaknesses of old age, especially mental decline
  • Antonym: vigorous - strong and healthy, especially mentally or physically

Etymology

French, reduplicative of 'gaga' meaning nearly senile.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01649738-s
Synset POS: s
