Cupressus macrocarpa

1. NOUN

/ kuˈprɛsəs ˌmækroʊˈkɑrpə / (AmE), / kjuːˈprɛsəs ˌmakrəʊˈkɑːpə / (BrE)
A tall cypress tree native to the Monterey Bay area in California, commonly planted for ornament, reforestation, and shelterbelts.
a tall Californian cypress tree species

Examples

The Cupressus macrocarpa thrives along the rugged coast of Monterey Bay.
Many parks have planted Cupressus macrocarpa for windbreaks and landscaping.
Botanists are working to preserve wild populations of Cupressus macrocarpa in California.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Monterey cypress - common name for Cupressus macrocarpa, a tall Californian cypress tree

Etymology

From Latin: Cupressus (cypress) + macrocarpa (large-fruited).
