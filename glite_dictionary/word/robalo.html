robalo

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈbɑːloʊ / (AmE), / rəʊˈbɑːləʊ / (BrE)
A percoid fish found in coastal waters, especially in the Americas, prized as a game and food fish.
a percoid fish, often a game fish

Examples

The chef prepared grilled robalo with lime and fresh herbs for the special.
Many anglers travel to the Gulf coast each year hoping to catch a large robalo.
The robalo is highly valued for both its sporting quality and its delicate flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snook - a common name for several species of fish, including the robalo

Etymology

From Spanish 'róbalo', meaning 'snook' or a type of percoid fish.
