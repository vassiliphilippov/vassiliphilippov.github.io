Peridiniidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛrəˌdɪnɪˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪˌdɪnɪˈaɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of single-celled, often planktonic, dinoflagellates found in marine and freshwater environments.
family of aquatic dinoflagellates

Examples

Scientists collected samples of Peridiniidae from both the river and the lake for their research.
Blooms of Peridiniidae can significantly change the composition of plankton communities.
Under the microscope, various species within Peridiniidae display unique cell wall structures.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Peridinium.

2. NOUN

/ pɛrəˌdɪniˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / pɛrɪˌdɪnɪˈaɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of mainly marine and freshwater dinoflagellates distinguished by the unique structure of their cell coverings
a family of dinoflagellates (protists)

Examples

Members of the Peridiniidae family are important contributors to freshwater plankton communities.
Certain Peridiniidae species can be identified by their distinctive armored plates.
Researchers studied the seasonal abundance of Peridiniidae in the coastal lagoon.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Peridinium + -idae (family suffix in taxonomy).
