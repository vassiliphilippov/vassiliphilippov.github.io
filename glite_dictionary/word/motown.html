Motown

1. PROPN

/ ˈmoʊˌtaʊn / (AmE), / ˈməʊˌtaʊn / (BrE)
A nickname for Detroit, Michigan, especially known as the center of the United States automobile industry and for its distinctive musical heritage.
nickname for Detroit, Michigan

Examples

Many famous musicians started their careers in Motown during the 1960s.
After moving away, she often missed the vibrant culture of Motown.
The legacy of Motown music continues to attract tourists to Detroit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Detroit - the largest city in Michigan; center of the U.S. automobile industry

Etymology

Blend of 'motor' and 'town', originally from Detroit's role in the automobile industry and popularized by the Motown record label.
