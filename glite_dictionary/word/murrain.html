murrain

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɜːr.ɪn / (AmE), / ˈmjʊə.rən / (BrE)
A contagious disease affecting cattle or other domestic animals, often causing widespread death.
contagious livestock disease
archaic

Examples

In the 18th century, a murrain devastated many herds in the region.
Farmers took great precautions to prevent the spread of murrain among their animals.
Historical records often mention murrain as a cause of famine due to the loss of livestock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plague - a contagious bacterial disease causing high mortality
  • Synonym: epizootic - a disease that appears as new cases in animal populations

Etymology

From Old French morine, from morir (to die), from Latin mori.
