Notropis atherinoides

1. NOUN

/ noʊˈtroʊpɪs æθəˌrɪnɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / nəʊˈtrəʊpɪs æθəˌrɪnɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
a small freshwater fish of the minnow family, native to the Great Lakes and Mississippi Valley, with a greenish metallic sheen; also known as the emerald shiner
species of small greenish freshwater fish

Examples

Fishermen often use Notropis atherinoides as bait in the Great Lakes region.
Biologists conducted a population study of Notropis atherinoides in the Mississippi River valley.
The shimmering green coloration of Notropis atherinoides makes it easy to identify among other minnows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emerald shiner - common name for Notropis atherinoides

Etymology

From Greek 'notos' (back) + 'tropis' (keel), referring to body shape; 'atherinoides' means 'like Atherina' (a genus of silverside fish) in Latinized Greek.
