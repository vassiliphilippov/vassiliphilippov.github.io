isotonic

1. ADJ

/ ˌaɪsəˈtɑnɪk / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəˈtɒnɪk / (BrE)
(of two or more muscles) having equal tension or tone.
having equal tension (muscles)
medical

Examples

When both biceps and triceps are isotonic, the arm remains steady during a particular motion.
Physical therapists may test whether paired muscles are isotonic to assess coordination.
Maintaining isotonic conditions can be important for balanced muscle development during rehabilitation.

Etymology

From Greek 'isos' (equal) + 'tonos' (tone).

WordNet ID: oewn-02415586-s

2. ADJ

/ ˌaɪ.səˈtɑ.nɪk / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.səˈtɒn.ɪk / (BrE)
(of solutions) having the same or equal osmotic pressure, especially as applied to biological fluids.
having equal osmotic pressure (in solutions)
medical

Examples

Saline used in medical treatments is usually isotonic with human blood.
The experiment compared the effects of isotonic and hypertonic solutions on red blood cells.
An isotonic drink helps maintain fluid balance during intense exercise.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hypotonic - (of a solution) having a lower osmotic pressure than another solution
  • Degree Higher: hypertonic - (of a solution) having a higher osmotic pressure than another solution

Etymology

From Greek 'isos' (equal) + 'tonos' (tension, tone).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00898273-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˌaɪsəˈtɑnɪk / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəˈtɒnɪk / (BrE)
(of muscular contraction) involving constant tension while the length of the muscle changes.
of muscle contraction: constant tension, changing length
medical

Examples

During an isotonic contraction, the muscle shortens as it lifts a constant load.
Physical therapists often recommend isotonic exercises to improve joint mobility and muscle strength.
The biceps undergoes an isotonic contraction when you lift a dumbbell.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: isometric - (of muscular contraction) involving equal muscle length with varying tension

Etymology

From Greek 'iso-' (equal) + 'tonos' (tension).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00357061-a
Synset POS: a
