to digitise

1. VERB

/ ˈdɪdʒɪˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈdɪdʒɪtʌɪz / (BrE)
to convert information or data into a digital format that can be used by a computer
convert into digital format
british

Examples

The library began to digitise rare manuscripts to make them accessible online.
We need to digitise these paper records for easier storage and retrieval.
The museum has digitised its entire photo collection to preserve it for future generations.

Synset Examples

he bought a device to digitize the data

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to digitize - to convert into digital format (American spelling)

Etymology

From 'digit' (number, numerical symbol), influenced by 'digital', plus the verb-forming suffix '-ise'.
