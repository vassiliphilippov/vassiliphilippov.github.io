Tragelaphus angasi

1. NOUN

/ ˌtrædʒɪˈlæfəs ænˈɡɑːsi / (AmE), / ˌtrædʒɪˈlæfəs æŋˈɡɑːsi / (BrE)
A spiral-horned South African antelope, known as the nyala, with a fringe of white hairs along its back and neck.
species of spiral-horned South African antelope

Examples

The Tragelaphus angasi is commonly found in the dense woodlands of southern Africa.
Male Tragelaphus angasi can be easily identified by their striking spiral horns and shaggy coats.
Wildlife photographers often seek out Tragelaphus angasi for its distinctive appearance and elusive nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nyala - common name for Tragelaphus angasi, a spiral-horned antelope

Etymology

From Latinized Greek 'tragelaphos' (goat-stag) and the species name honoring George French Angas, an explorer.
