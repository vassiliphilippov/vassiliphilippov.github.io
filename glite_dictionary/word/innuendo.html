innuendo

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnjuˈɛndoʊ / (AmE), / ˌɪnjuːˈɛndəʊ / (BrE)
an indirect remark or hint, typically of a suggestive or disparaging nature
indirect, often suggestive remark

Examples

Despite his denial, the article was full of innuendo about his private life.
She didn't accuse him directly, but her innuendo was clear to everyone.
Office gossip often spreads through sly innuendo rather than open statements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: implication - something suggested without being stated directly
  • Synonym: insinuation - an unpleasant hint or suggestion of something bad
  • Antonym: compliment - a polite expression of praise or admiration

Etymology

From Latin 'innuere' (to nod at, hint), via Italian 'innuendo'.
