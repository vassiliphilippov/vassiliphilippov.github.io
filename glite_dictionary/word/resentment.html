resentment

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈzɛntmənt / (AmE), / rɪˈzɛntmənt / (BrE)
a feeling of deep and bitter anger or ill-will, often caused by being treated unfairly or insulted
feeling of bitter anger or ill-will

Examples

He spoke with obvious resentment about being overlooked for the promotion.
Years of resentment had built up between the two siblings, making reconciliation difficult.
Her resentment grew as she realized her efforts were unappreciated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bitterness - anger and disappointment at being treated unfairly; synonym for resentment
  • Antonym: forgiveness - the action or process of forgiving; opposite of resentment

Etymology

From French *ressentiment* (animosity), from Latin *sentire* (to feel).
