dideoxyinosine

1. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪ.diˌɑːk.siˈɪn.oʊˌsin / (AmE), / ˌdaɪ.diˌɒk.siˈɪn.əʊˌsiːn / (BrE)
an antiviral drug used to treat HIV infection by inhibiting reverse transcriptase
antiviral drug treating HIV
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed dideoxyinosine as part of his antiretroviral therapy regimen.
Researchers continue to study the effectiveness of dideoxyinosine in combination with other HIV medications.
Some patients may experience side effects when taking dideoxyinosine for an extended period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: didanosine - another name for dideoxyinosine, used as an antiviral agent
  • Synonym: reverse transcriptase inhibitor - category of drugs that prevent viral replication by inhibiting reverse transcriptase

Etymology

From 'di-' (two) + 'deoxy' (lacking oxygen) + 'inosine' (a nucleoside).
