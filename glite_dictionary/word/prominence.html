prominence

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑː.mə.nəns / (AmE), / ˈprɒm.ɪ.nəns / (BrE)
the state of being important or well-known
importance; being well-known

Examples

Her sudden prominence in the industry surprised everyone.
The newspaper gave prominence to the story on its front page.
He achieved prominence as a leading environmental activist.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: obscurity - the state of being unknown or unimportant

Etymology

From Latin prominentia (a jutting out, prominence), from prominere (to project, stand out).

/ ˈprɑː.mə.nəns / (AmE), / ˈprɒm.ɪ.nəns / (BrE)

Examples

Despite his early prominence, he faded from public view after retirement.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: obscurity - the state of being unknown or unimportant

Etymology

From Latin 'prominentia' (a projecting part, prominence), from 'prominere' (to jut out).

3. NOUN

/ ˈprɑː.mə.nəns / (AmE), / ˈprɒm.ɪ.nəns / (BrE)
a part that sticks out or protrudes from its surroundings, such as a hill or anatomical bulge
a part that juts out; a projection

Examples

The explorer climbed to the top of the rocky prominence to survey the landscape.
A slight prominence could be felt on the surface of the bone.
From the valley, the prominence of the hill was clearly visible against the skyline.

From Latin prominentia (a jutting out, projection), from prominere (to jut out).
