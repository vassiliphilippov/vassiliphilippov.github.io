to misconceive

1. VERB

/ ˌmɪskənˈsiv / (AmE), / ˌmɪskənˈsiːv / (BrE)
to interpret or understand something incorrectly
interpret wrongly

Examples

He tends to misconceive people’s intentions, thinking they are being rude when they are just shy.
It’s easy to misconceive the message if you don’t pay attention to the context.
Many people misconceive statistics as absolute facts rather than interpretations.

Synset Examples

Don't misinterpret my comments as criticism
She misconstrued my remarks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to misunderstand - to fail to understand correctly
  • Synonym: to misinterpret - to interpret something wrongly
  • Antonym: to understand - to grasp the meaning or significance of something

Etymology

From mis- (wrongly) + conceive (from Latin concipere, to take in, conceive).
