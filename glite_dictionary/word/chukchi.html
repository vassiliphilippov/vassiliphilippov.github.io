Chukchi

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃʊktʃi / (AmE), / ˈtʃʊktʃi / (BrE)
a member of an indigenous people living on the Chukchi Peninsula in northeastern Siberia.
member of an indigenous Siberian people

Examples

The Chukchi have traditionally relied on reindeer herding for their livelihood.
A Chukchi elder shared stories of his people's history and customs.
Many Chukchi still live in remote villages along the coast of the Chukchi Sea.

Etymology

From Russian Чукчи (Chukchi), plural of Чукча (Chukcha), the people’s self-designation pronounced as /ʔtʃawtʃew/ in their own language.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃʊk.tʃi / (AmE), / ˈtʃʊk.tʃi / (BrE)
the language of the Chukchi people, an indigenous and isolated language of unknown origin spoken in northeastern Siberia, noted for differences in pronunciation between men and women
language of the Chukchi people

Examples

Linguists have studied the Chukchi language for its unusual grammatical structures.
She is fluent in both Russian and Chukchi.
The Chukchi language features pronunciation differences based on the speaker's gender.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chukchi - a member of the indigenous people of northeastern Siberia

Etymology

From Russian Чукчи (Chukchi), from the Chukchi people's own name for themselves.
