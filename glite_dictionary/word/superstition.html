superstition

1. NOUN

/ ˌsuː.pɚˈstɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˈstɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
A belief or practice resulting from ignorance, fear of the unknown, or trust in magic or chance, rather than reason or knowledge.
belief based on fear or ignorance

Examples

Many people consider walking under a ladder to be a superstition.
Her grandmother had a superstition about opening umbrellas indoors bringing bad luck.
Some athletes have a superstition that wearing the same socks will help them win.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: myth - A traditional story or widely held but false belief
  • Antonym: rationality - The quality of being based on or in accordance with reason or logic

Etymology

From Latin superstitio, from superstes (standing over, surviving).
