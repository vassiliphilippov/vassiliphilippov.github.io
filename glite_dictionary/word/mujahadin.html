mujahadin

1. NOUN

/ muːˌdʒɑː.hɑːˈdiːn / (AmE), / muːˌdʒɑː.haːˈdiːn / (BrE)
A member of a group of Muslim guerrilla fighters engaged in a jihad, especially in Afghanistan in the 20th century.
Muslim guerrilla fighter in a jihad

Examples

The Afghan mujahadin opposed the Soviet invasion during the 1980s.
Many young men joined the mujahadin to fight for their beliefs.
International support was given to the mujahadin during the war.

Synset Examples

some call the mujahidin international warriors but others just call them terrorists

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jihadist - A person involved in a jihad, often a synonym in modern contexts

Etymology

From Arabic *mujāhidīn* (fighters in a jihad), plural of *mujāhid* (fighter).
