materiality

1. NOUN

/ məˌtɪriˈæləti / (AmE), / məˌtɪəriˈæləti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being physical or composed of matter; physical existence.
physical existence or substance

Examples

The materiality of the sculpture makes it strikingly real and tangible.
Philosophers have long debated the materiality of the human soul.
Modern art often challenges our understanding of materiality and form.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: immateriality - the quality of not consisting of matter; lack of physical substance

Etymology

From Medieval Latin materialitas, from Latin materia (matter, substance).

2. NOUN

/ məˌtɪriˈæləti / (AmE), / məˌtɪəriˈæləti / (BrE)
the quality of being relevant and significant, especially in a way that warrants careful attention or consideration
importance or relevance requiring attention

Examples

The auditor questioned the materiality of the minor accounting errors in the report.
Before making her decision, she assessed the materiality of each argument presented.
Courts often debate the materiality of facts when determining if a case should proceed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: importance - the state or fact of being of great significance or value
  • Antonym: insignificance - lack of importance or relevance

Etymology

From Late Latin materialitas, from Latin materia (matter, substance).
