Livonian

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈvoʊniən / (AmE), / lɪˈvəʊniən / (BrE)
the Finnic language formerly spoken by the Livonian people of Latvia and Estonia
Finnic language of Latvia and Estonia

Examples

The last native speaker of Livonian passed away in the 21st century.
She is studying endangered languages, including Livonian and Votic.
Few people today can understand the ancient texts written in Livonian.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Finnic - relating to the group of Finno-Ugric languages that includes Finnish and Estonian

Etymology

From Livonia, the historical region inhabited by the Livonian people, + -ian.

2. NOUN

/ lɪˈvoʊniən / (AmE), / lɪˈvəʊniən / (BrE)
a member of the Livonian-speaking people indigenous to northern Latvia
member of the Livonian-speaking people of Latvia

Examples

Only a small number of Livonians remain in modern Latvia today.
The Livonian community has worked to preserve its unique language and traditions.
Historians study the ancient settlements of the Livonian people along the Latvian coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Latvian - a person from Latvia or of Latvian descent

Etymology

From Livonia, a historical region in the Baltic, combined with the suffix -ian.
