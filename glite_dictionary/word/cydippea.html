Cydippea

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈdɪpiə / (AmE), / sɪˈdɪpiːə / (BrE)
A group of ctenophores characterized by having two long, feather-like tentacles.
a group of ctenophores with two long tentacles

Examples

The Cydippea are easily recognized by their two long, feathery tentacles.
Many marine biologists study the feeding habits of Cydippea in their natural habitat.
Unlike other comb jellies, Cydippea species use their tentacles to capture plankton.
