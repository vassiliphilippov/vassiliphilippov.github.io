scarabaeus

1. NOUN

/ ˌskærəˈbiːəs / (AmE), / ˌskarəˈbiː.əs / (BrE)
A large dung beetle, especially one considered sacred by the ancient Egyptians.
sacred Egyptian dung beetle

Examples

The ancient Egyptians regarded the scarabaeus as a symbol of rebirth and transformation.
A carving of a scarabaeus was found among the royal tombs.
The museum displays several scarabaei amulets used by priests in ceremonies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scarab - A type of beetle regarded as sacred in ancient Egypt

Etymology

From Latin 'scarabaeus', from Greek 'skarabeios', referring to a type of beetle.
