Strobilomyces

1. NOUN

/ stroʊˌbɪləˈmaɪsiːz / (AmE), / strəʊˌbɪləˈmaɪsiːz / (BrE)
A genus of fungi similar to boletes, characterized by a shaggy, scaly cap.
genus of fungi with shaggy, scaly caps

Examples

The Strobilomyces mushrooms in this forest are easily identified by their dark, shaggy caps.
Mycologists often seek out Strobilomyces species for their distinctive appearance and ecological importance.
During the hike, we photographed a rare Strobilomyces growing on the damp woodland floor.

Etymology

From Greek 'strobilos' (pine cone) and 'myces' (fungus).
