diocesan

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈɑsəsən / (AmE), / daɪˈɒsəsən / (BrE)
a bishop having jurisdiction over a diocese
a bishop with authority over a diocese

Examples

The newly appointed diocesan visited every parish in his territory during his first year in office.
The diocesan issued pastoral letters to guide local clergy and congregations.
A former diocesan was commemorated for his dedication to community service.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin diocesanus, from diocesis (diocese).

2. ADJ

/ daɪˈɑːsən / (AmE), / daɪˈɒsɪsən / (BrE)
relating to or belonging to a diocese, or governed by a bishop or other church leader of a diocese
relating to a diocese or its leader

Examples

The diocesan authorities issued new guidelines for the parish.
He attended a diocesan conference last week.
Her role was to coordinate diocesan activities throughout the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: episcopal - relating to a bishop or bishops

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *diocesanus*, from *dioecesis* (diocese).
