facile

1. ADJ

/ ˈfæsl / (AmE), / ˈfæs.aɪl / (BrE)
able to express oneself easily, clearly, and effectively, especially in speech or writing; fluent or eloquent.
fluent and effective in expression

Examples

Her facile manner of speaking won over the audience immediately.
With his facile tongue, he could persuade almost anyone to see his point of view.
The politician delivered a facile speech that captivated the entire room.

Synset Examples

able to dazzle with his facile tongue
silver speech

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fluent - able to express oneself easily and articulately
  • Synonym: eloquent - fluent or persuasive in speaking or writing

Etymology

From Latin facilis (easy), from facere (to do, make).

Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈfæsl̩ / (AmE), / ˈfæs.aɪl / (BrE)
(of an idea, explanation, or solution) appearing neat and comprehensive only by ignoring the true complexities; superficial or simplistic.
superficially simple or effortless

Examples

The committee criticized the report for its facile analysis of complex issues.
Her facile explanations failed to address the underlying causes of the problem.
It is tempting to offer facile answers to difficult social questions, but real solutions require depth.

Synset Examples

too facile a solution for so complex a problem
it was a neat plan, but bound to fail
a slick advertising campaign

Related Terms

  • Synonym: superficial - lacking thoroughness or depth
  • Synonym: shallow - not showing deep understanding or insight
  • Antonym: thorough - showing careful attention to detail and depth

Etymology

From Latin facilis (easy to do), from facere (to do, make).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01880529-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈfæ.səl / (AmE), / ˈfæ.sɑɪl / (BrE)
performing or achieved with ease; effortless or adroit, especially in a way that appears without difficulty
performed with effortless skill

Examples

With his facile touch, the pianist made even the hardest pieces sound simple.
Her facile handling of the negotiations impressed everyone in the boardroom.
The artist's facile brushwork gave the painting a lively, spontaneous quality.

Synset Examples

a facile hand

Related Terms

  • Synonym: effortless - achieved with little or no effort
  • Antonym: labored - done with great effort and difficulty

Etymology

From Latin 'facilis' (easy to do), from 'facere' (to do).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00842072-s
Synset POS: s
