elf

1. NOUN

/ ɛlf / (AmE), / ɛlf / (BrE)
A small, often mischievous creature from folklore, typically depicted as having pointed ears and magical powers.
mythical mischievous creature

Examples

The mischievous elf hid all the children's shoes before sunrise.
In ancient tales, an elf is said to dance by moonlight in the forest.
She dressed as an elf for the holiday party, complete with pointed ears and a green tunic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fairy - a small imaginary being with magical powers
  • Degree Lower: goblin - a mischievous, often malicious folklore creature
  • Synonym: sprite - a small or elusive supernatural being

Etymology

From Old English 'ælf', related to Old Norse 'álfr'.
