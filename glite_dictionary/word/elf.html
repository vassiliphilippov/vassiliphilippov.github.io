ELF

1. NOUN

/ i ɛl ɛf / (AmE), / iː ɛl ɛf / (BrE)
a radio frequency in the electromagnetic spectrum with a frequency below 3 kilohertz (kHz), known as extremely low frequency
extremely low frequency (below 3 kHz)
medical

Examples

The submarine communicated using ELF signals, which can penetrate deep below the ocean surface.
Scientists monitored ELF waves to study their effect on the Earth's magnetic field.
ELF frequencies are often used for long-range communication with submerged vessels.

Etymology

Acronym of 'Extremely Low Frequency.'
