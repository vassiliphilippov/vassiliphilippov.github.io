auxin

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔksɪn / (AmE), / ˈɔːksɪn / (BrE)
a plant hormone that controls growth by promoting cell elongation, root formation, and bud development
plant hormone regulating growth
medical

Examples

The scientist measured the concentration of auxin in the plant's stem.
Applying auxin to cuttings can encourage them to grow new roots.
High levels of auxin stimulate cell elongation in young shoots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hormone - a regulatory substance produced in organisms to stimulate specific cells or tissues

Etymology

From Latin 'auxin-' (stem of 'auxere', to increase), coined in the early 20th century.
