overbusy

1. ADJ

/ ˌoʊvərˈbɪzi / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈbɪzi / (BrE)
Too busy; excessively occupied or engaged.
excessively busy

Examples

He felt overbusy at work, unable to take a single break all day.
Many parents become overbusy juggling their children's schedules and their own responsibilities.
The overbusy manager rarely found time to sit down for lunch.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: busy - actively engaged or occupied; having a lot to do
  • Degree Higher: overwhelmed - feeling unable to cope due to the amount of work or emotion

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'over-' meaning 'excessively' and 'busy.'
