to convey

1. VERB

/ kənˈveɪ / (AmE), / kənˈveɪ / (BrE)
to transmit or serve as a medium for transmission (such as information, sound, or heat) from one place or person to another
transmit or serve as medium for transmission

Examples

Wires are used to convey electricity throughout the building.
The pipes convey water from the reservoir to the city.
Radio waves convey information over great distances.

Synset Examples

Sound carries well over water
The airwaves carry the sound
Many metals conduct heat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to transmit - to send or pass from one person or place to another
  • Synonym: transfer - to move from one place to another

Etymology

From Old French *conveier*, from Vulgar Latin *conviare* (to escort, accompany), from Latin *com-* (together) + *via* (way).
