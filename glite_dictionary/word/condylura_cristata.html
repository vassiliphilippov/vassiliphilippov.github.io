Condylura cristata

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑn.dɪˈlʊr.ə krɪˈsteɪ.tə / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.dɪˈljʊə.rə krɪˈsteɪ.tə / (BrE)
a species of mole native to eastern North America, notable for the pink, fleshy tentacles around its nose; also known as the star-nosed mole
species of mole with star-shaped nose

Examples

The Condylura cristata is easily recognized by the star-shaped appendages on its snout.
Biologists studied the unique sensory abilities of the Condylura cristata in swampy areas.
A Condylura cristata can detect and eat prey with remarkable speed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: star-nosed mole - common name for Condylura cristata, a mole with a star-shaped nose

Etymology

From Latin: 'condylura' (knobbed tail); 'cristata' (crested, tufted).
