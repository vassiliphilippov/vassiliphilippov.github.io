transplanting

1. NOUN

/ trænsˈplæntɪŋ / (AmE), / trænsˈplɑːntɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of removing something from one place and introducing it to another, especially a plant or organ
the act of relocating something

Examples

Frequent transplanting can stress young seedlings and hinder their growth.
Many patients owe their lives to the successful transplanting of organs.
The transplanting of traditions from one culture to another can be challenging.

Synset Examples

the transplant did not flower until the second year
too frequent transplanting is not good for families
she returned to Alabama because she could not bear transplantation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transplantation - the act of transplanting, especially with organs or tissues
  • Degree Lower: removal - the act of taking something away or off
  • Synonym: relocation - the act of moving something to a new place

Etymology

From Latin 'transplantare' (to plant across or beyond).
