Ummah

1. NOUN

/ ʊˈmɑː / (AmE), / ˈʊmɑː / (BrE)
The whole community of Muslims worldwide, regarded as a single group linked by religion.
global Muslim community

Examples

Many Muslims feel a strong sense of solidarity with the Ummah across national borders.
Leaders called on the Ummah to unite during times of crisis.
Discussions about charity often focus on the needs of the global Ummah.

Synset Examples

moderate Muslims urge the Ummah to reject the terrorism of radical Muslims

Etymology

From Arabic أمة‎ (ummah), meaning 'community' or 'nation'.
