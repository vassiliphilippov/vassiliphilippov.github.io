Aleyrodidae

1. NOUN

/ əˌlɛɪəˈrɒdəˌdi / (AmE), / əˌlɛɪəˈrɒdɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, sap-sucking insects known as whiteflies, many of which are pests of crops.
family of whiteflies

Examples

The family Aleyrodidae includes many species that cause significant damage to agricultural crops.
Scientists collected samples from several Aleyrodidae populations in the greenhouse study.
Effective management of Aleyrodidae requires monitoring and early intervention in affected fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whitefly - a small, pale insect that feeds on plants; member of Aleyrodidae

Etymology

From New Latin Aleyrodidae, based on Aleyrodes (a genus of whiteflies) and suffix -idae (used in zoological family names).
