foolscap

1. NOUN

/ ˈfuːlˌskæp / (AmE), / ˈfuːlskæp / (BrE)
a size of writing paper, especially in Britain, traditionally measuring about 13 by 8 inches (330 by 203 mm)
a British paper size
british

Examples

The contract was written on several sheets of foolscap paper.
He found an old ledger filled with notes on foolscap.
In British schools, students used to write essays on foolscap before A4 became standard.

Etymology

From the watermark of a fool's cap on such paper in England in the 17th century.
