Huygens

1. PROPN

/ ˈhaɪɡənz / (AmE), / ˈhɔɪɡənz / (BrE)
Christiaan Huygens (1629–1695), Dutch physicist, mathematician, and astronomer known for formulating the wave theory of light.
Dutch physicist and mathematician (1629–1695)

Examples

Huygens was instrumental in advancing the wave theory of light.
The discoveries of Huygens greatly influenced later scientists such as Newton.
Many optical phenomena were first explained by Huygens in the seventeenth century.

Etymology

Dutch surname, notably borne by Christiaan Huygens.
