Palometa simillima

1. NOUN

/ pæloʊˈmɛtə sɪˈmɪlɪmə / (AmE), / ˌpæləˈmiːtə sɪˈmɪlɪmə / (BrE)
A species of small fish related to the Florida pompano, found commonly in the West Indies.
a small West Indies fish species

Examples

The Palometa simillima is often caught by local fishers in the West Indies.
Unlike the Florida pompano, the Palometa simillima is smaller and more abundant near coral reefs.
Marine biologists documented several Palometa simillima specimens along the Caribbean coast.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Florida pompano - a closely related and larger fish species

Etymology

From Spanish 'palometa' (type of fish) and Latin 'simillima' (most similar).
