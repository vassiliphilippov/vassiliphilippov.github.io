landfill

1. NOUN

/ ˈlændˌfɪl / (AmE), / ˈlænd.fɪl / (BrE)
An area of land where waste is buried under layers of soil to reclaim or elevate land.
area where waste is buried to fill land

Examples

The city decided to convert the old quarry into a landfill for municipal waste.
After years of use, the landfill was transformed into a public park.
Strict regulations are enforced to prevent contamination around the landfill site.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dump - site for disposing of waste
  • Synonym: landfill site - site used for burial of waste material

Etymology

Formed from 'land' + 'fill'.
