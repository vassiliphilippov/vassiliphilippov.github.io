administrator

1. NOUN

/ ədˈmɪn.əˌstreɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ədˈmɪn.ɪ.streɪ.tə / (BrE)
a person appointed by a court to manage and distribute the estate of someone who has died intestate or without naming an executor
court-appointed estate manager (probate)
legal

Examples

The administrator of the estate was responsible for settling debts and distributing assets.
When someone dies without a will, the court appoints an administrator to handle the estate.
As administrator, she was required to file regular reports with the probate court.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: executor - person named to carry out the terms of a will

Etymology

From Latin administrātor (manager, steward), from administrare (to manage).

2. NOUN

/ ədˈmɪnɪˌstreɪtər / (AmE), / ədˈmɪnɪstreɪtə / (BrE)
a person responsible for managing a government agency or department, or for directing an organization or institution.
manager of a government agency or department

Examples

The new administrator implemented several reforms within the department.
Each government agency appoints an administrator to oversee daily operations.
The administrator was praised for improving efficiency in public services.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manager - a person responsible for controlling or administering an organization or group of staff
  • Synonym: executive - a person with senior managerial responsibility in a business or organization

Etymology

From Latin administrātor (one who manages affairs), from administrare (to manage).

3. NOUN

/ ədˈmɪn.əˌstreɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ədˈmɪn.ɪ.streɪ.tə / (BrE)
a person responsible for running a business, organization, or institution
person who manages an organization

Examples

The school's administrator organized the annual budget meeting for all staff members.
She was hired as the office administrator to ensure daily operations ran smoothly.
Our new administrator has already improved the efficiency of several departments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manager - a person responsible for controlling or administering an organization or group of staff
  • Synonym: executive - a person with senior managerial responsibility in a business organization

Etymology

From Latin administrare, meaning 'to manage' or 'to direct'.
