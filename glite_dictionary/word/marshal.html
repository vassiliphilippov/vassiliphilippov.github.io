marshal

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːrʃəl / (AmE), / ˈmɑːʃəl / (BrE)
an official responsible for enforcing the orders of a court of law, with duties similar to those of a sheriff
law officer enforcing court orders
legal

Examples

The U.S. marshal ensured the safe transport of the prisoner to the courthouse.
After the trial, a marshal served the eviction notice as ordered by the judge.
She called the marshal for assistance when court proceedings became disorderly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sheriff - a law officer with similar duties to a marshal

Etymology

From Old French 'mareschal', meaning 'stable keeper', later 'commander', from Frankish 'marhskalk' (horse servant).

/ ˈmɑrʃəl / (AmE), / ˈmɑːʃəl / (BrE)
to arrange or organize people or things systematically, especially to assemble soldiers in order for a ceremony, parade, or battle
arrange or organize systematically

Examples

We need to marshal all available resources for the upcoming project.
She skillfully marshaled the volunteers into teams for the event.

7. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrʃəl / (AmE), / ˈmɑːʃəl / (BrE)
(in some countries) a military officer of the highest rank, often commanding an army or air force.

Examples
