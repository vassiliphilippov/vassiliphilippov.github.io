cangue

1. NOUN

/ kæŋ / (AmE), / kæŋ / (BrE)
a heavy wooden collar formerly used as a punishment in China, confining the neck and sometimes the arms of a criminal
a wooden punishment collar from China

Examples

The prisoner was forced to wear a cangue for several days as part of his punishment.
Historians have studied the use of the cangue in traditional Chinese legal systems.
A wooden cangue hung on display in the museum's section on early justice.

Etymology

From French 'cangue', from Portuguese 'canga' (yoke), ultimately from Malay or Chinese.
