abjection

1. NOUN

/ æbˈdʒɛk.ʃən / (AmE), / æbˈdʒɛk.ʃən / (BrE)
A state of being cast down or brought low; extreme degradation or humiliation.
state of extreme degradation or humiliation

Examples

After losing everything in the recession, the family endured a period of utter abjection.
The novel depicts the abjection of those forced to live on the margins of society.
His feeling of abjection was evident in the way he avoided meeting anyone's eyes.

Synset Examples

each confession brought her into an attitude of abasement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abasement - the act of being lowered in rank, status, or esteem

Etymology

From Latin abjectio (a casting down), from abicere (to cast down).
