Pulsatilla occidentalis

1. NOUN

/ ˌpʌlsəˈtɪlə ɒkˌsɪdəntˈælɪs / (AmE), / ˌpʌlsəˈtɪlə ɒkˌsɪdəntˈælɪs / (BrE)
A species of herbaceous perennial plant native to western North America, also known as western pasqueflower, belonging to the buttercup family (Ranunculaceae).
herbaceous plant of western North America

Examples

The meadows were covered in blooming Pulsatilla occidentalis during the early spring.
Many hikers admire the unique fuzzy appearance of Pulsatilla occidentalis along mountain trails.
Researchers are studying the distribution of Pulsatilla occidentalis in relation to climate change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Anemone occidentalis - Alternate scientific name for Pulsatilla occidentalis

Etymology

Genus name from Latin 'pulsatilla' (diminutive of 'pulsare', to beat or strike, referring to movement in the wind); 'occidentalis' means 'western' in Latin.
