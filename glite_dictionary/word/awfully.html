awfully

1. ADV

/ ˈɔfli / (AmE), / ˈɔːfli / (BrE)
In a way that is extremely bad or unpleasant; dreadfully.
extremely or dreadfully bad

Examples

The movie was awfully boring, and I left before it was over.
She felt awfully sorry for upsetting her friend.
It was awfully cold last night, and the pipes froze.

Synset Examples

there was a dreadfully bloody accident on the road this morning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dreadfully - in a way that causes fear or is extremely bad
  • Synonym: terribly - in an extremely bad or serious way
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ ˈɔfli / (AmE), / ˈɔːfli / (BrE)
In a very bad or unpleasant manner.
in a terrible manner

Examples

She played the violin awfully at the recital, making everyone cringe.
He behaved awfully toward his guests, showing no manners at all.
The cake turned out awfully, so we couldn't serve it to anyone.

Synset Examples

she sings terribly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terribly - in a terrible manner; very badly
  • Synonym: horribly - in a horrible or very unpleasant way

Etymology

From 'awful' + '-ly', originally meaning 'inspiring awe' but later developing negative senses.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00055639-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ ˈɔːfli, ˈɑːfli / (AmE), / ˈɔːfli / (BrE)
To a very great or excessive degree; used to add emphasis.
to a very great degree; very

Examples

She looked awfully tired after her long journey.
I'm awfully sorry for the mistake.
It was awfully nice of you to help.

Synset Examples

terribly interesting
I'm awful sorry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terribly - used as an intensifier meaning 'very' or 'extremely'
  • Synonym: dreadfully - used as an intensifier for emphasis
  • Antonym: slightly - to a small degree; the opposite in intensity

Etymology

From 'awful' + '-ly', originally meaning 'in an awe-inspiring manner', later developing the intensifying sense.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00055488-r
Synset POS: r
