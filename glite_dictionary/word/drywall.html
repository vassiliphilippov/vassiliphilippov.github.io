drywall

1. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪˌwɔl / (AmE), / ˈdraɪˌwɔːl / (BrE)
A rigid panel made of plaster, wood pulp, or other materials, used to form the interior walls and ceilings of buildings.
panel for walls and ceilings

Examples

He spent the afternoon installing new drywall in the living room.
A hole in the drywall needs to be patched before we can paint.
The contractor recommended using thicker drywall for better sound insulation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plasterboard - panel made of plaster used for interior walls

Etymology

Formed from 'dry' + 'wall', contrasting with wet plastering methods.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdraɪˌwɔl / (AmE), / ˈdraɪˌwɔːl / (BrE)
A building material consisting of large, flat boards made of plaster, wood pulp, or similar materials, used to form the interior walls and ceilings of buildings.
interior building material for walls/ceilings

Examples

The contractor installed new drywall in the renovated kitchen.
After a leak, the drywall had to be replaced to prevent mold.
She learned how to patch a hole in the drywall herself.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plasterboard - Building material similar to drywall, mainly used in British English

Etymology

Formed from 'dry' + 'wall', indicating a wall material installed dry (without mortar).
