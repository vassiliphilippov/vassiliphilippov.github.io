Neoceratodus forsteri

1. NOUN

/ nioʊˌsɛrəˌtoʊdəs ˈfɔrstəri / (AmE), / niːəʊˌsɛrəˈtəʊdəs ˈfɔːstəraɪ / (BrE)
an endangered species of freshwater lungfish native to rivers in Queensland, Australia, known for its primitive features and ability to breathe air
Australian lungfish species

Examples

The Neoceratodus forsteri is often referred to as a living fossil because of its ancient lineage.
Researchers are studying the Neoceratodus forsteri to learn more about the evolution of lungfish.
Conservation efforts have increased to protect the endangered Neoceratodus forsteri in Queensland rivers.

Etymology

From Greek neos (new), keras (horn), odous (tooth), and named for naturalist J. R. Forster.
