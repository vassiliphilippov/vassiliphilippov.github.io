Acanthocybium solandri

1. NOUN

/ əˌkænθoʊˈsaɪbiəm soʊˈlændri / (AmE), / əˌkanθəˈsɪbɪəm səʊˈlandri / (BrE)
A large, fast-swimming predatory fish (also known as wahoo), found in tropical and subtropical seas worldwide, valued as a food and game fish.
species of large predatory fish (wahoo)

Examples

Anglers often prize the Acanthocybium solandri for its speed and fighting ability.
Commercial fishers harvest Acanthocybium solandri in many tropical waters worldwide.
The culinary value of Acanthocybium solandri makes it popular in coastal cuisines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wahoo - common name for Acanthocybium solandri, a large predatory fish

Etymology

From Greek akantha (thorn/spine) + kybion (a small cube, fish), species name honoring Daniel Solander, Swedish naturalist.
