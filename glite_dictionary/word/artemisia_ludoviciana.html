Artemisia ludoviciana

1. NOUN

/ ɑr.təˈmɪzi.ə lʊˌdoʊ.vɪˈʃaɪ.ni.ə / (AmE), / ˌɑː.təˈmɪz.i.ə ˌluː.dəʊ.vɪˈkiː.ni.ə / (BrE)
a perennial herb of the southwestern United States with cottony-white foliage, commonly known as white sage or prairie sage
a cottony-white perennial herb of the US Southwest

Examples

Artemisia ludoviciana is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and attractive silver leaves.
Indigenous peoples have traditionally used Artemisia ludoviciana for medicinal purposes.
The cottony texture of Artemisia ludoviciana makes it easily recognizable among native prairie plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white sage - a common name for Artemisia ludoviciana

Etymology

From the genus name Artemisia, named after the Greek goddess Artemis, and the species epithet 'ludoviciana', referring to Louisiana or the Louisiana Territory.
