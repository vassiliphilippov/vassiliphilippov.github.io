heliolatry

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːliˈɑːlətri / (AmE), / ˌhiːliˈalətri / (BrE)
the worship of the sun, especially as practiced in ancient religions
worship of the sun

Examples

Many ancient civilizations practiced heliolatry, constructing temples dedicated to the sun god.
The ruins of the temple suggest a culture steeped in heliolatry and ritual sun worship.
Historians trace the spread of heliolatry from Egypt to other parts of the ancient world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idolatry - the worship of idols
  • Synonym: solar - relating to or determined by the sun

Etymology

From Greek hēlios (sun) + latreia (worship).
