Pternohyla

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɜːr.noʊˈhaɪ.lə / (AmE), / ˌtəː.nəʊˈhʌɪ.lə / (BrE)
A genus of burrowing tree frogs found in Central America.
a genus of burrowing tree frogs

Examples

The Pternohyla are known for their unique adaptation to burrowing in soft soil.
Researchers discovered a new species within the genus Pternohyla in southern Mexico.
Unlike many other tree frogs, Pternohyla species spend much of their lives underground.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɜr.noʊˈhaɪ.lə / (AmE), / ˌpɜː.nəʊˈhaɪ.lə / (BrE)
a genus of burrowing tree frogs found in Central America, known for their distinctive adaptations for digging.
a genus of burrowing tree frogs

Examples

Scientists found a new species within the Pternohyla genus during their expedition to southern Mexico.
The unique webbed feet help Pternohyla frogs adapt to both burrowing and climbing.
Conservationists are studying the habitat requirements of Pternohyla to protect these rare tree frogs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tree frog - a frog that lives mostly in trees

Etymology

From Greek 'pternon' (heel) and 'hylē' (forest, wood), used in scientific Latin classification.
