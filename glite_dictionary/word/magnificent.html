magnificent

1. ADJ

/ mæɡˈnɪfəsənt / (AmE), / mæɡˈnɪfɪsənt / (BrE)
impressive in appearance or style; grand or splendid
impressively grand or splendid

Examples

The palace features a magnificent view of the lake from every window.
Her performance in the lead role was absolutely magnificent and drew a standing ovation.
They attended a magnificent banquet held in the grand ballroom.

Synset Examples

the brilliant court life at Versailles
a glorious work of art
magnificent cathedrals
the splendid coronation ceremony

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splendid - very impressive; magnificent
  • Synonym: glorious - having great beauty and splendor
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance; not grand or magnificent

Etymology

From Latin 'magnificus', from 'magnus' (great) + 'facere' (to make).
