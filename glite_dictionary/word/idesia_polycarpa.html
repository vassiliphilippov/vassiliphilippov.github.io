Idesia polycarpa

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈdiːziə ˌpɒliˈkɑːrpə / (AmE), / ɪˈdiːzɪə ˌpɒlɪˈkɑːpə / (BrE)
A deciduous, round-headed Asiatic tree grown in mild climates for its heart-shaped leaves, fragrant yellow-green flowers, and hanging clusters of orange-red berries.
ornamental Asiatic tree with colorful berries

Examples

The botanical garden features a beautiful Idesia polycarpa near its entrance, displaying clusters of bright berries in autumn.
Gardeners value Idesia polycarpa for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in mild climates.
Birds are drawn to the orange-red berries produced by the Idesia polycarpa each fall.

Etymology

From the genus name Idesia and species name polycarpa, from Greek 'poly-' (many) and 'karpos' (fruit).
