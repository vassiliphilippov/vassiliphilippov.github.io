to summon

1. VERB

/ ˈsʌmən / (AmE), / ˈsʌmən / (BrE)
to officially order someone to appear, especially in a law court or for an official duty
officially order to appear (esp. in court)
legal

Examples

The lawyer was summoned to appear before the judge on Monday morning.
She summoned the witness to testify during the trial.
You may be summoned to court if you ignore the jury notice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subpoena - a written order requiring someone to attend court
  • Antonym: to dismiss - to order someone to leave or not to appear

Etymology

From Latin 'summonere' (to call, remind), via Old French 'somoner'.

6. VERB

/ ˈsʌmən / (AmE), / ˈsʌmən / (BrE)
to call or order someone to be present, especially for a particular purpose or to ask someone to come and help
call or order someone to be present

Examples

The principal decided to summon the parents to discuss their child's progress.
She quickly summoned a taxi when it started to rain heavily.
We may need to summon extra staff for the busy weekend.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invite - to ask someone to come to an event or place
  • Antonym: to dismiss - to send someone away or to allow them to leave

Etymology

From Old French *somoner* or Latin *summonere* (to call, remind), from *sub-* (under) + *monere* (to warn).
