Leptopteris superba

1. NOUN

/ lɛpˈtɑptərɪs suːˈpɜrbə / (AmE), / lɛpˈtɒptərɪs suːˈpɜːbə / (BrE)
A species of fern native to New Zealand, with pinnate fronds and densely woolly stalks; sometimes classified under the genus Todea.
species of fern native to New Zealand

Examples

The Leptopteris superba thrives in damp, shaded forests of New Zealand's South Island.
Botanists often admire the distinctive woolly stems of Leptopteris superba.
Some classifications include Leptopteris superba in the genus Todea due to its similar features.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'leptos' (slender) + 'pteris' (fern); species name from Latin 'superbus' (splendid).
