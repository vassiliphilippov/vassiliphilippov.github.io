editorial

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛdəˈtɔriəl / (AmE), / ˌɛdɪˈtɔːriəl / (BrE)
An article in a newspaper or magazine that gives the opinions of the editors or publishers on a particular topic.
opinion article in a publication

Examples

The newspaper published an editorial criticizing the government's new policy.
Her editorial explained the reasons behind the company's recent changes.
Many people eagerly read the Sunday editorial for insight into the editor's views.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: op-ed - an article expressing the opinion of a named author, typically not from the editorial staff
  • Synonym: column - a regular feature or article by a particular writer, often expressing opinions

Etymology

From editor plus the suffix -ial, indicating relation to editing or editors.

2. ADJ

/ ˌɛdəˈtɔriəl / (AmE), / ˌɛdɪˈtɔːriəl / (BrE)
relating to the writing or content of an article that expresses opinions or perspectives, especially in a newspaper or magazine.
relating to opinion articles

Examples

The newspaper published an editorial piece criticizing government policy.
She is responsible for the editorial decisions at the magazine.
An editorial column often reflects the views of the publication's editors.

Synset Examples

editorial column

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opinionated - expressing strong personal opinions
  • Antonym: objective - not influenced by personal feelings or opinions

Etymology

From Latin 'editorius' (of an editor) via French 'éditorial'.

3. ADJ

/ ˌɛdəˈtɔriəl / (AmE), / ˌɛdɪˈtɔːriəl / (BrE)
relating to editing or to the work or duties of an editor
relating to editing or editors

Examples

Her editorial responsibilities include reviewing and approving submitted articles.
The magazine maintains strict editorial standards to ensure quality and accuracy.
Many editorial decisions are made during the final stages of publication.

Synset Examples

editorial duties

Related Terms

  • Synonym: editor - a person who is in charge of and determines the final content of a text

Etymology

From Latin 'editor' (publisher) + English suffix '-ial'.
