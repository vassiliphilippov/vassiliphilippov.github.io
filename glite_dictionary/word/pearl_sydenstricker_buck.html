Pearl Sydenstricker Buck

1. PROPN

/ pɝl ˈsaɪdənˌstrɪkər bʌk / (AmE), / pɜːl ˈsaɪdənˌstrɪkə bʌk / (BrE)
(1892–1973) United States author best known for her novels about China, drawing on her experiences as a missionary there.
US author, novels set in China

Examples

Pearl Sydenstricker Buck won the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1938 for her rich and truly epic descriptions of peasant life in China.
Many high school students read 'Pearl Sydenstricker Buck''s novel 'The Good Earth' as part of their curriculum.
As a child of missionaries in China, Pearl Sydenstricker Buck brought a unique perspective to American literature.
