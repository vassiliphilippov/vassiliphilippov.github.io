nelfinavir

1. NOUN

/ nɛlˈfɪnəˌvɪr / (AmE), / nɛlˈfɪnəˌvɪə / (BrE)
a protease inhibitor drug used in the treatment of HIV infections, often in combination with other antiretroviral medications
an HIV protease inhibitor drug
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed nelfinavir as part of the patient's antiretroviral therapy.
Many patients taking nelfinavir notice improvements in their immune system over time.
Researchers are studying the effects of nelfinavir in combination with other HIV medications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protease inhibitor - a drug that blocks the activity of protease enzymes, used especially in treating HIV/AIDS

Etymology

From the chemical name; trade name Viracept.
