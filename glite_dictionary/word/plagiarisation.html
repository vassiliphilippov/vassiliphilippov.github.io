plagiarisation

1. NOUN

/ pleɪdʒəˌraɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / pladʒəˌraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of copying someone else's words or ideas and presenting them as your own
the act of copying someone else's work
british

Examples

The university has strict policies to prevent plagiarisation in academic work.
She was accused of plagiarisation after copying several paragraphs without citation.
Teachers use special software to detect plagiarisation in student essays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plagiarism - the act of using someone else's work as your own

Etymology

From the verb 'plagiarise' + suffix '-ation', modeled on 'plagiarism'.
