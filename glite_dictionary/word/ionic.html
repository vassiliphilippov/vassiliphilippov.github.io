Ionic

1. ADJ

/ aɪˈɑnɪk / (AmE), / aɪˈɒnɪk / (BrE)
of or relating to Ionia, its people, or its ancient Greek dialect.
relating to Ionia or its people/language

Examples

The ancient city displayed many Ionic influences in its architecture and culture.
Scholars have studied the Ionic dialect to learn more about classical Greek literature.
Several temples were dedicated by Ionic settlers along the western coast of Asia Minor.

Etymology

From Latin 'Ionicus', from Greek 'Iōnikos', relating to Ionia.

2. NOUN

/ aɪˈɑnɪk / (AmE), / aɪˈɒnɪk / (BrE)
the form of Ancient Greek spoken and written in the region of Ionia and, with some variation, in Attica and Athens
Ancient Greek dialect of Ionia and Attica

Examples

The Ionic dialect differs significantly from Doric and Aeolic in certain grammatical features.
Many of the poets from ancient Ionia wrote in the Ionic dialect.
Her studies focus on the evolution of Ionic as used in pre-classical Greek literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Doric - another major dialect of Ancient Greek
  • Synonym: Attic - the Ancient Greek dialect of the region around Athens

Etymology

From Latin Ionicus, from Greek Iōnikos (of Ionia).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06990270-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ aɪˈɑnɪk / (AmE), / aɪˈɒnɪk / (BrE)
relating to the Ionic order, one of the classical orders of ancient Greek architecture characterized by scroll-shaped ornaments (volutes) on the capital of columns.
of the Ionic order in Greek architecture

Examples

The temple features beautiful Ionic columns with distinctive capitals.
Architects often study the proportions and decorations of the Ionic order when designing neoclassical buildings.
The Ionic style is easily recognized by the scroll-like volutes on top of its columns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Doric - relating to the Doric order of classical Greek architecture
  • Synonym: Corinthian - relating to the Corinthian order of classical Greek architecture

Etymology

From Latin Ionicus, from Greek Iōnikos, referring to Ionia, a region of ancient Greece.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: a
