epicycloid

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈsaɪklɔɪd / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈsʌɪklɔɪd / (BrE)
A curve described by a point on the circumference of a circle as it rolls around the outside of another fixed circle.
curve traced by point on rolling circle

Examples

The shape of the gear teeth can be based on the epicycloid curve.
By varying the radii, different epicycloid patterns can be created.
An epicycloid is generated by tracing a point as a circle rolls around another circle.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hypocycloid - curve traced by a point on a circle rolling inside another circle

Etymology

From Greek epi- (upon, on) and kyklos (circle).
