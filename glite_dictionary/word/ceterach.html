Ceterach

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːtəˌræk / (AmE), / ˈsiːtərak / (BrE)
A small genus of Old World ferns, now often included within other genera such as Asplenium or Pleurosorus.
genus of Old World ferns

Examples

The genus Ceterach is known for its resilience in dry environments.
Botanists have reclassified many species formerly placed within Ceterach.
The rustyback fern was once categorized under Ceterach but is now often listed as Asplenium ceterach.

Etymology

From New Latin *Ceterach*, the genus name, derived from Arabic *shatruḥ*, a fern.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛtərˌæk / (AmE), / ˈsiːtəræk / (BrE)
A small genus of Old World ferns, sometimes classified under other genera such as Asplenium or Pleurosorus.
genus of Old World ferns

Examples

Many botanists now classify Ceterach as part of the genus Asplenium.
The distinctive leaves of Ceterach are often found growing in rocky crevices.
Some ferns initially identified under Ceterach have since been reclassified.

Etymology

From Greek *kēterax*, the ancient name for this fern.
