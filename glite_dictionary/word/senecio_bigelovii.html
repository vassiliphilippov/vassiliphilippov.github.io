Senecio bigelovii

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈniːʃioʊ bɪˌɡɛlˈoʊviːaɪ / (AmE), / sɪˈniːsiəʊ bɪˌɡɛlˈəʊviːaɪ / (BrE)
A species of plant with upright leafy stems and clusters of yellow, rayless flower heads on curved stalks, native to moist areas of the southwestern United States.
species of plant native to southwestern US

Examples

Senecio bigelovii grows in moist mountain regions and is easily recognized by its yellow flower heads.
Botanists often study Senecio bigelovii to understand its role in southwestern U.S. plant communities.
The bent stalks of Senecio bigelovii make it stand out among other wildflowers.

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Senecio' meaning 'old man,' and honoring American botanist J.M. Bigelow.
