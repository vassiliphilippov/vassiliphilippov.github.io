Hypericum

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈpɛr.ɪ.kəm / (AmE), / hɪˈpɪər.ɪ.kəm / (BrE)
A genus of mostly evergreen or deciduous shrubs and herbs, commonly known for their showy yellow flowers; includes species found almost worldwide except in tropical lowlands, Arctic, high altitudes, and deserts.
genus of flowering plants (St. John’s worts)

Examples

The garden was filled with blooming Hypericum during the summer months.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of several Hypericum species.
Many gardeners choose Hypericum for its attractive and long-lasting yellow flowers.

Etymology

From Greek hyperikon, the ancient name for St. John’s wort.

2. NOUN

/ haɪˈpɛrɪkəm / (AmE), / hɪˈpɪərɪkəm / (BrE)
a large genus of flowering plants, commonly known as St. John's worts, comprising evergreen or deciduous shrubs and herbs, widely found except in tropical lowlands, Arctic, high altitudes, and deserts
genus of St. John's wort plants

Examples

Many herbal remedies use Hypericum extracts for their supposed mood-lifting properties.
The bright yellow flowers of Hypericum can be found in gardens around the world.
Researchers are studying several species within the Hypericum genus for their medicinal potential.

Etymology

From Latin *Hypericum*, from Ancient Greek ὑπέρεικος (hupéreikos), a name for the plant.
