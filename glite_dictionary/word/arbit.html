to arbit

1. VERB

/ ˈɑːrbɪt / (AmE), / ˈɑːbɪt / (BrE)
to resolve a dispute by acting as an arbitrator; to arbitrate
resolve as an arbitrator
legal

Examples

They agreed to arbit the contract disagreement rather than file a lawsuit.
An independent panel was chosen to arbit the labor dispute between workers and management.
The two companies decided to arbit instead of allowing the courts to settle their differences.

Synset Examples

They decided to arbit the dispute instead of going to court.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to arbitrate - act as an arbitrator to decide a dispute

Etymology

Back-formation from 'arbitration' or 'arbitrator'; related to Latin 'arbitrari' (to judge, consider).
