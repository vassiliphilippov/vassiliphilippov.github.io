shove-halfpenny

1. NOUN

/ ʃʌv ˈhæfˌpɛni / (AmE), / ʃʌv ˈhɑːfpni / (BrE)
A traditional British game in which coins or discs are slid by hand across a board toward a mark.
traditional British coin-sliding game
british

Examples

The children gathered in the pub's back room to play shove-halfpenny on a rainy afternoon.
He demonstrated remarkable skill at shove-halfpenny, winning nearly every round.
A battered shove-halfpenny board was found among the old bar games.

Etymology

From British English 'shove' (to push) and 'halfpenny' (a small coin), reflecting the gameplay and equipment.
