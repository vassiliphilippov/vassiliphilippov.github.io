upholstery

1. NOUN

/ ʌpˈhoʊlstəri / (AmE), / ʌpˈhəʊlstəri / (BrE)
the soft, padded covering, including fabric, padding, springs, and webbing, on furniture such as chairs and sofas
padded covering of furniture

Examples

The upholstery on the old armchair was worn and torn in several places.
He chose a deep blue fabric for the new upholstery of his sofa.
Cleaning the upholstery regularly helps to maintain the furniture's appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: furniture - large movable objects used to make a room suitable for living or working

Etymology

From Middle English upholder (upholsterer), from the verb uphold (to furnish).

2. NOUN

/ ʌpˈhoʊlstəri / (AmE), / ʌpˈhɒlstəri / (BrE)
the work or craft of covering furniture with padding, springs, webbing, and fabric or leather
the craft of covering furniture

Examples

She studied upholstery for two years before starting her own business.
The museum offered workshops on traditional upholstery techniques.
Modern upholstery often uses synthetic materials for durability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: upholsterer - a person whose job is upholstering furniture

Etymology

From Middle English *upholdester* (upholder), from 'uphold' + '-ster', later influenced by Old French *hous* (cover, housing).
