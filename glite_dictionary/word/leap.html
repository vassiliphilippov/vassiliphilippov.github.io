leap

1. NOUN

/ lip / (AmE), / liːp / (BrE)
a sudden and decisive increase in the level or amount of something
sudden and decisive increase

Examples

There has been a significant leap in sales since the new product launched.
The company reported a leap in profits this quarter.
Advances in technology led to a huge leap in efficiency.

Synset Examples

a jump in attendance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jump - a sudden increase

Etymology

From Old English hleapan (to leap, jump).
