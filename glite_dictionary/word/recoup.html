to recoup

1. VERB

/ rɪˈkup / (AmE), / rɪˈkuːp / (BrE)
to reimburse or compensate (someone) for a loss; to regain something that was lost
reimburse or recover a loss

Examples

The insurance policy helped recoup her financial losses after the accident.
He hopes to recoup his investment within two years of starting the business.
The company managed to recoup some of its expenses through a government grant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reimburse - to pay back money to someone

Etymology

From French 'recouper', from 're-' (again) + 'couper' (to cut).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02254399-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ rɪˈkup / (AmE), / rɪˈkuːp / (BrE)
to regain or recover something lost, especially money or expenses
regain or recover lost money or resources

Examples

The company hopes to recoup its losses by launching a new product line.
She managed to recoup her investment after a year of hard work.
It may take time to recoup the costs spent on repairs.

Synset Examples

recuperate one's losses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to recover - to regain possession of something lost or stolen
  • Antonym: to lose - to be deprived of or cease to have something

Etymology

From French 'recouper' (to cut back, regain), from Latin 'recupare'.
