Elizabeth

1. PROPN

/ ɪˈlɪzəbəθ / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪzəbəθ / (BrE)
The queen of the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland from 1952 until her death in 2022, daughter of George VI.
Queen Elizabeth II, British monarch (1926–2022)

Examples

Elizabeth became queen in 1952 after the death of her father, King George VI.
Many people around the world admired Elizabeth for her dedication and sense of duty.
During her reign, Elizabeth witnessed significant historical events and changes to the monarchy.

Synset Examples

Elizabeth II is the head of state in Great Britain

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Queen Elizabeth I - Queen of England and Ireland from 1558 to 1603

Etymology

From Hebrew Elisheva, meaning 'God is my oath.'
