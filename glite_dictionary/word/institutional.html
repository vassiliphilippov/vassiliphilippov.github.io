institutional

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.stɪˈtuː.ʃə.nəl / (AmE), / ˌɪn.stɪˈtjuː.ʃə.nəl / (BrE)
relating to or involving an institution, especially a large and important organization such as a university, church, or bank.
relating to an institution or organization

Examples

The company changed its institutional policy to emphasize sustainability.
There are concerns about institutional racism within some organizations.
He focused on the institutional structure of the health care system in his report.

Synset Examples

institutional policy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: organizational - relating to the structure or management of an organization

Etymology

From institution + -al, first used in the early 19th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.stɪˈtuː.ʃən.əl / (AmE), / ˌɪn.stɪˈtjuː.ʃən.əl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of an institution, especially being organized or established as an institution.
relating to an institution or organized system

Examples

The policy changes affected all institutional procedures at the university.
Many people are critical of institutional responses to mental health issues.
He preferred an institutional approach to reform, relying on established organizations.

Synset Examples

institutional religion

Related Terms

  • Antonym: individual - single, not part of an institution or collective

Etymology

From institution + -al, formed in English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01234064-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.stɪˈtu.ʃə.nəl / (AmE), / ˌɪn.stɪˈtjuː.ʃən.əl / (BrE)
(especially of places, products, or services) Characteristic of a large organization, especially by being uniform, dull, or lacking individuality.
uniform or dull, like a large institution

Examples

People often complain about the institutional feel of hospitals.
The cafeteria served bland, institutional food to hundreds of students every day.
Her office had an institutional atmosphere, with gray walls and identical furniture.

Synset Examples

institutional food

Related Terms

  • Synonym: institutionalized - established as a norm or practice within an institution, often suggesting rigidity
  • Antonym: individual - distinct or unique, not uniform or institutional

Etymology

From institution + -al, denoting characteristics of an institution.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01234511-s
Synset POS: s
