dimple

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪmpəl / (AmE), / ˈdɪmpəl / (BrE)
A small natural hollow that forms in the cheek or chin, especially when a person smiles.
small hollow in cheek or chin

Examples

Her cheeks showed a deep dimple every time she laughed.
The baby was born with a cute dimple on her chin.
He admired her dimple as she smiled across the table.

Synset Examples

His dimple appeared whenever he smiled

Etymology

From Middle English *dympel*, of Germanic origin; related to Low German *dimpel* and Old High German *tumpil* (small hollow).

3. NOUN

/ ˈdɪmpəl / (AmE), / ˈdɪmpəl / (BrE)
a small, rounded indentation or slight depression on a surface
slight depression or indentation

Examples

The surface of the golf ball is covered in tiny dimples to improve aerodynamics.
A shallow dimple formed in the metal after it was lightly struck.
She noticed a small dimple in the sand where the pebble had rested.

Synset Examples

there are approximately 336 dimples on a golf ball

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dent - a slight hollow in a surface made by a blow or pressure

Etymology

From Middle English dympel, of Scandinavian origin; compare Norwegian dimpil (small depression).
