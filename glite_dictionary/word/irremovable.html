irremovable

1. ADJ

/ ɪrɪˈmuːvəbl / (AmE), / ɪrɪˈmuːvəbl / (BrE)
impossible to remove, dismiss, or get rid of
impossible to remove or dismiss

Examples

The judge has an irremovable appointment to the bench.
Those stains are virtually irremovable, no matter what cleaner we use.
An irremovable sense of guilt haunted him for years.

Synset Examples

irremovable boulders
irremovable obstacles

Related Terms

  • Antonym: removable - able to be removed or taken away

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'ir-' (not) + 'removable' (from 'remove' + '-able').
