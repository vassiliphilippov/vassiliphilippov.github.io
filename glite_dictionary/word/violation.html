violation

1. NOUN

/ ˌvaɪ.əˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌvaɪ.əˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The act of entering another person's property without right or permission; an instance of trespassing.
entry onto property without permission
legal

Examples

The security cameras caught footage of the violation last night when someone entered the yard.
Entering the premises without the owner's consent constitutes a violation of property rights.
Police were called to investigate a suspected violation of the restricted area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trespass - entering someone's land or property without permission

Etymology

From Latin violatio(n-), from violare 'treat with violence'.

an act that shows a lack of respect for a rule, law, or the rights or dignity of others
disrespectful or unlawful act

Examples

She was upset by the violation of her privacy when her diary was read without permission.
Such a violation of trust can permanently damage a friendship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: breach - an act of breaking a law or rule

From Latin 'violatio', from 'violare' (to violate).

4. NOUN

/ ˌvaɪəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvaɪəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
(Law) A minor offense or crime less serious than a felony, often punishable by a fine or other non-jail penalties.
minor legal offense; less serious than a felony
legal

Examples

Parking in a no-parking zone is considered a violation and usually results in a fine.
The officer issued a ticket for the traffic violation committed by the driver.
She had a clean record with no criminal charges, only one minor violation for littering.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: misdemeanor - a crime less serious than a felony but more serious than an infraction
