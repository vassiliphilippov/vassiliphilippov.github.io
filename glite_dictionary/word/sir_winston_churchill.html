Sir Winston Churchill

1. PROPN

/ wɪn.stən ˈtʃɝ.tʃɪl / (AmE), / ˈwɪn.stən ˈtʃɜː.tʃɪl / (BrE)
(1874–1965) British statesman, Prime Minister during World War II, renowned for his leadership and oratory; awarded the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1953.
British Prime Minister and WWII leader

Examples

Sir Winston Churchill delivered some of the most memorable speeches in British history.
Many historians credit Sir Winston Churchill with inspiring the British people during World War II.
Sir Winston Churchill was awarded the Nobel Prize in Literature for his historical writings.
