bacteriophagous

1. ADJ

/ bækˌtɪri.oʊˈfeɪɡəs / (AmE), / bækˌtɪəri.əˈfəːɡəs / (BrE)
Relating to or involving bacteriophages; pertaining to organisms or agents that attack or feed on bacteria.
relating to bacteriophages
medical

Examples

Some viruses display bacteriophagous behavior, targeting specific bacterial populations for destruction.
Scientists are studying bacteriophagous enzymes as a possible alternative to antibiotics.
The bacteriophagous properties of this compound make it valuable in controlling bacterial infections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bacteriophage - a virus that infects and destroys bacteria

Etymology

From 'bacteriophage', itself from 'bacteria' and Greek 'phagein' (to eat), plus the adjective suffix '-ous'.
