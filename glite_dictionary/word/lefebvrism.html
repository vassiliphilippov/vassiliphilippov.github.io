Lefebvrism

1. NOUN

/ ləˈfɛbvɪˌrɪzəm / (AmE), / lɛˈfɛvbɪˌrɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
Support for or membership in the Society of Saint Pius X, an international traditionalist Catholic organization, or adherence to its doctrines.
support for Society of Saint Pius X

Examples

The bishop's outspoken advocacy of Lefebvrism created significant controversy within the Catholic community.
Many young priests have shown renewed interest in Lefebvrism and traditional liturgical practices.
As debates about Vatican II continued, Lefebvrism remained influential among some conservative Catholics.

Synset Examples

They will carry the torch of Lefebvrism, of an impossible Church theology, of having a foot in both religions, Catholic and modernist, of sifting Vatican documents and decrees.

Etymology

From the name of French Archbishop Marcel Lefebvre (1905–1991), founder of the Society of Saint Pius X, + -ism.
