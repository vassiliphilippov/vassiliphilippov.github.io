scarab

1. NOUN

/ ˈskær əb / (AmE), / ˈskær əb / (BrE)
a large dung beetle, particularly regarded as sacred in ancient Egyptian religion and art
a sacred beetle in ancient Egypt

Examples

The ancient Egyptians regarded the scarab as a powerful symbol of rebirth and immortality.
He wore a necklace with a carved scarab as a good luck charm.
The museum's collection includes a rare golden scarab from the New Kingdom period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scarabaeus - Latin term for the sacred scarab beetle
  • Degree Higher: beetle - an insect with a hard wing case

Etymology

From Latin *scarabaeus*, from Greek *karabos* (beetle).
