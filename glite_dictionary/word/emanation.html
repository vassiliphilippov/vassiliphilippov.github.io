emanation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛm.əˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛm.əˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a substance or form of energy that is emitted or radiated from a source, such as a gas, odor, or light
something that is emitted or radiated

Examples

A faint emanation of perfume filled the air as she entered the room.
Scientists measured the radioactive emanation coming from the rocks.
The emanation of light from the lamp brightened the dark hallway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emission - the act of sending out something such as gas or radiation
  • Antonym: absorption - the process of taking in or soaking up something

Etymology

From Latin emanatio (a flowing out), from emanare (to flow out).

3. NOUN

/ ˌɛm.əˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛm.əˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
(theology) The origination or process by which the Holy Spirit proceeds or flows from the Father (and the Son) in Christian theology, especially as described at Pentecost.
theological process of the Holy Spirit's origination

Examples

The doctrine of the emanation of the Holy Spirit is central to some Christian traditions.
Early theologians debated the nature and significance of the emanation at Pentecost.
The emanation is often discussed in studies of the Trinity.

Synset Examples

the emanation of the Holy Spirit
the rising of the Holy Ghost
the doctrine of the procession of the Holy Spirit from the Father and the Son

Related Terms

  • Synonym: procession - the theological doctrine concerning the Holy Spirit's origination

Etymology

From Latin emanatio (a flowing forth), from emanare (to flow out).
