to aerosolise

1. VERB

/ ˈɛr.əˌsɑːl.aɪz / (AmE), / ˈɛər.əˌsɒl.aɪz / (BrE)
to disperse (a substance) as a fine spray or mist, especially in the form of an aerosol
to disperse as an aerosol

Examples

Some medical devices are designed to aerosolise medications for patients with respiratory diseases.
Accidentally aerosolising cleaning chemicals can pose inhalation risks in confined spaces.
The laboratory procedure aerosolised the virus for further study in controlled environments.

Synset Examples

The bacteria suspension was aerosolized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to vaporize - to convert into vapor

Etymology

From 'aerosol' + '-ise'; 'aerosol' from Greek 'aēr' (air) and Latin 'solutio' (solution).

2. VERB

/ ˈɛrəsɒˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɛərəʊsəlaɪz / (BrE)
(of a substance) to become dispersed as tiny particles suspended in air, forming an aerosol.
to become dispersed as an aerosol

Examples

The chemicals can aerosolise during the process, posing a health risk.
Certain cleaning agents may aerosolise when sprayed onto surfaces.
If the virus aerosolises, it can spread more easily through the air.

Synset Examples

the bacteria quickly aerosolised

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to aerosolize - to disperse or become dispersed as an aerosol (American spelling)

Etymology

From 'aerosol' + '-ise'; 'aerosol' from Greek 'aēr' (air) and Latin 'solutio' (solution).
