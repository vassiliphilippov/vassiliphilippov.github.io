ceibo

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪboʊ / (AmE), / ˈseɪbəʊ / (BrE)
A small spiny tree native to South America, known for its dark crimson or scarlet flowers that grow solitary or in clusters.
South American tree with crimson flowers

Examples

The ceibo is celebrated as the national flower of Argentina.
Brightly colored ceibo blossoms covered the tree during the summer.
Many birds are attracted to the nectar of the ceibo in bloom.

Etymology

From Spanish 'ceibo', used in South America to describe this tree.
