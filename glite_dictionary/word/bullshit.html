bullshit

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊlˌʃɪt / (AmE), / ˈbʊlʃɪt / (BrE)
Nonsense or deceptive, unacceptable behavior; something considered false, insincere, or worthless, often said with strong disapproval.
nonsense or unacceptable behavior (vulgar)
vulgar slang informal american

Examples

I've heard enough of your bullshit—just tell me what really happened.
No one believed his story because it sounded like complete bullshit.
I'm not in the mood to deal with office bullshit today.

Synset Examples

I put up with a lot of bullshit from that jerk
what he said was mostly bull

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonsense - spoken or written words that have no meaning or make no sense
  • Antonym: truth - the quality of being true or accurate, opposite of lies or deception

Etymology

Formed from 'bull' and the vulgar 'shit', first attested in the early 20th century in American English.

2. VERB

/ ˈbʊlˌʃɪt / (AmE), / ˈbʊlʃɪt / (BrE)
to talk nonsense, especially by speaking insincerely or without regard for the truth, often with the intention to deceive or impress
talk nonsense or insincerely
slang vulgar informal

Examples

You can't just bullshit your way through the interview; they'll see right through you.
He tried to bullshit the professor when he hadn't read the book, but it didn't work.
Stop bullshitting and tell me what really happened.

Synset Examples

The politician was not well prepared for the debate and faked it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lie - to make a false statement with deliberate intent to deceive
  • Synonym: bluff - to mislead or deceive, especially by a false display of confidence
  • Antonym: to come clean - to be honest or tell the truth

Etymology

Originally U.S. slang, from 'bull' (nonsense) + 'shit' (excrement), early 20th century.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
