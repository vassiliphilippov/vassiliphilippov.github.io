Pentecostal

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛn.tɪˈkɑː.stəl / (AmE), / ˌpɛn.tɪˈkɒs.təl / (BrE)
a member of a Christian religious group that emphasizes the direct experience of the Holy Spirit, especially through speaking in tongues, healing, and prophecy
member of a Pentecostal Christian group

Examples

He was raised as a Pentecostal and attended church several times a week.
Many Pentecostals believe in the power of divine healing and speaking in tongues.
As a Pentecostal, she emphasized personal experiences of faith over ritual.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Evangelical - member of an Evangelical Christian movement
  • Antonym: Catholic - member of the Catholic Church
  • Synonym: Pentecostalism - the beliefs and practices of Pentecostal religious groups

Etymology

From Pentecost, the Christian festival commemorating the descent of the Holy Spirit, from Ancient Greek 'Pentēkostē' (fiftieth [day]).

2. ADJ

/ ˌpɛntəˈkɑstəl / (AmE), / ˌpɛntɪˈkɒstəl / (BrE)
relating to any of the Christian churches or movements that emphasize the work of the Holy Spirit and the direct experience of the presence of God, often through speaking in tongues
of or relating to Pentecostal churches or members

Examples

She joined a Pentecostal church known for its lively worship services.
Many Pentecostal believers emphasize the role of spiritual gifts in daily life.
The Pentecostal movement spread rapidly throughout the twentieth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charismatic - of or relating to charismatic Christians or movements emphasizing gifts of the Spirit

Etymology

From Pentecost, the Christian festival, referencing events described in the Acts of the Apostles.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03110085-a
Synset POS: a
