Preakness

1. PROPN

/ ˈpriːknɪs / (AmE), / ˈpriːknɪs / (BrE)
An annual horse race for three-year-old Thoroughbreds, held at Pimlico Race Course in Baltimore, Maryland.
major annual horse race in Maryland, US

Examples

The Preakness is the second jewel in the US Triple Crown of horse racing.
Spectators gathered at Pimlico Race Course to watch the Preakness this year.
Winning the Preakness is considered a significant achievement in American horse racing.

Etymology

Named after the horse 'Preakness,' winner of the first Dinner Party Stakes at Pimlico in 1870.
