Dactylopiidae

1. NOUN

/ dækˌtɪloʊˈpaɪɪdiː / (AmE), / dækˌtɪlɒˈpɪɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of scale insects commonly known as cochineal insects, some of which are used for producing carmine dye.
a family of cochineal insects

Examples

Researchers found new species within the Dactylopiidae family in South America.
Some Dactylopiidae are vital for producing natural red dye from cochineal insects.
The distribution of Dactylopiidae is closely tied to cactus plants, their primary hosts.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Coccoidea - superfamily of scale insects
  • Degree Lower: Dactylopius - genus of cochineal insects; source of carmine dye

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Dactylopius and the suffix -idae (family).

2. NOUN

/ dækˌtɪloʊˈpaɪɪdiː / (AmE), / dækˌtɪləʊˈpɪɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of scale insects known as cochineal insects, some of which are used as natural dye sources.
family of cochineal insects

Examples

The Dactylopiidae family includes species essential for producing natural cochineal dye.
Researchers are studying how Dactylopiidae insects affect prickly pear crops.
Only female Dactylopiidae are harvested for their valuable carminic acid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cochineal - a red dye made from insects of this family

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Dactylopiidae, from the genus name Dactylopius.
