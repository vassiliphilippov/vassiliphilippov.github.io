descriptive

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈskrɪptɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈskrɪptɪv / (BrE)
relating to an approach in linguistics that aims to objectively record and analyze the forms, structures, and usage of a language, without imposing rules or standards.
relating to language description in linguistics

Examples

The textbook's explanation follows a descriptive approach, simply outlining how people actually use the language.
A descriptive grammar documents real-world speech patterns, not idealized rules.
Linguists often distinguish between descriptive and prescriptive analyses when discussing grammar.

Synset Examples

descriptive grammar

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prescriptive - relating to laying down rules or norms for language use

Etymology

From Latin descriptivus (serving to describe), from describere (to copy, sketch, describe).

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈskrɪptɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈskrɪptɪv / (BrE)
Giving details about someone or something; serving to describe or to provide information.
giving details; serving to describe

Examples

The author used descriptive language to create vivid images in the reader's mind.
A descriptive essay paints a picture using sensory details.
The report included several descriptive statistics about the survey results.

Synset Examples

the descriptive variable
a descriptive passage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explanatory - intended to explain or make clear
  • Synonym: narrative - telling a story or relating events
  • Antonym: prescriptive - giving rules or directions

Etymology

From Latin descriptivus, from describere (to describe).
