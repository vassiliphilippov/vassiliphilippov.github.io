encainide

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˈkeɪˌnaɪd / (AmE), / ɛnˈkeɪnʌɪd / (BrE)
a drug used to treat serious irregular heart rhythms; it can increase the risk of sudden death in heart attack patients.
antiarrhythmic drug for heart conditions
medical

Examples

Doctors prescribed encainide to control his life-threatening arrhythmia.
Research showed that encainide increases the risk of sudden death in some heart attack survivors.
Many hospitals stopped using encainide after concerns about its safety emerged.
