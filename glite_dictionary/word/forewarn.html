to forewarn

1. VERB

/ fɔrˈwɔrn / (AmE), / fɔːˈwɔːn / (BrE)
to warn someone in advance of possible danger or trouble
warn in advance

Examples

Police attempted to forewarn residents about the upcoming storm.
If only someone had forewarned me about the risks, I would have been more careful.
She forewarns her students that the exam will be challenging.

Synset Examples

I forewarned him of the trouble that would arise if he showed up at his ex-wife's house

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to warn - to advise someone of danger or problems

Etymology

From Old English 'fore-' (before) + 'warn.'
