risky

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɪski / (AmE), / ˈrɪski / (BrE)
involving the possibility of loss, harm, or danger; hazardous
involving danger or hazard

Examples

Investing in that company was a risky decision, but it paid off in the end.
Climbing alone in bad weather can be extremely risky.
He avoided taking on any risky projects at work so close to his retirement.

Synset Examples

skydiving is a hazardous sport
extremely risky going out in the tide and fog
a wild financial scheme

Related Terms

  • Antonym: safe - not likely to cause harm or risk; secure
  • Synonym: hazardous - dangerous, involving risk or danger

Etymology

From risk + -y, first attested in the late 19th century.

2. ADJ

/ ˈrɪski / (AmE), / ˈrɪski / (BrE)
involving the possibility of loss or danger, especially lacking financial safety or security
involving possibility of danger or financial loss

Examples

Investing in that startup would be a risky move for our company.
Some people enjoy making risky bets on the stock market.
He lost most of his money on a risky real estate deal.

Synset Examples

a bad investment
high risk investments
anything that promises to pay too much can't help being risky
speculative business enterprises

Related Terms

  • Antonym: safe - not in danger; financially secure
  • Synonym: speculative - involving a high degree of risk, especially financially

Etymology

From risk + -y, formed in English in the 19th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02282286-s
Synset POS: s
