Chu Kiang

1. PROPN

/ tʃuː ˈkjɑːŋ / (AmE), / tʃuː ˈkjɑːŋ / (BrE)
a river in southeast China that flows into the South China Sea; also known as the Pearl River
major river in southeast China

Examples

The Chu Kiang delta is one of the most densely urbanized regions in the world.
Many shipping routes follow the Chu Kiang through southern China.
The Chu Kiang is crucial for the region's agriculture and trade.

Etymology

From Mandarin 珠江 (Zhū Jiāng), meaning 'Pearl River'.
