Anopheles

1. NOUN

/ əˈnɑː.fəˌliːz / (AmE), / əˈnɒ.fəˌliːz / (BrE)
A genus of mosquitoes that transmits malaria and is characterized by adults resting with their heads downward and larvae lacking breathing tubes.
genus of malaria mosquitoes
medical

Examples

Scientists studied the life cycle of Anopheles to understand how malaria spreads.
Control of Anopheles populations is crucial in combating malaria in tropical regions.
The larvae of Anopheles mosquitoes are found in clean, still water.

Etymology

From Greek anōphelēs (useless, harmful), from an- (not) + ophelos (benefit).

2. NOUN

/ əˈnɑfəˌliz / (AmE), / əˈnɒfəˌliːz / (BrE)
A genus of mosquitoes distinguished by the adult's head-downward stance and by larvae lacking breathing tubes, several species of which transmit malaria.
a genus of malaria mosquitoes
medical

Examples

The spread of malaria is largely due to female Anopheles mosquitoes.
Scientists are studying Anopheles populations to better control disease transmission.
Larvae of the Anopheles genus develop in water without using breathing tubes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mosquito - a small flying insect, some species of which bite and transmit diseases
  • Synonym: Aedes - another genus of mosquitoes, some species of which transmit diseases

Etymology

From Greek 'anō' (up, upward) + 'phelēs' (useless), originally named for their supposed insignificance before their role in malaria was known.
