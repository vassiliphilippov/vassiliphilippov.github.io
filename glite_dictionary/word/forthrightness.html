forthrightness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrθˌraɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈfɔːθˌraɪtnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being honest and direct in attitude or speech
honesty; directness in speech

Examples

Her forthrightness impressed everyone during the meeting, as she answered every question honestly.
Many people appreciate his forthrightness even when his opinions are unpopular.
You can always count on Sarah's forthrightness when you need sincere advice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: candor - the quality of being open and honest; frankness
  • Antonym: evasion - the practice of avoiding honesty or directness

Etymology

From 'forthright' (direct, straightforward), itself from Middle English 'forth right' (straight ahead).
