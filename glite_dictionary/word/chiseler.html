chiseler

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɪzələr / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪzələ / (BrE)
a person who cheats others by using deception or fraud; a swindler.
someone who cheats others by fraud

Examples

The crooked accountant turned out to be a chiseler who had stolen thousands.
Nobody in the office trusted Mark after he was exposed as a chiseler.
She warned her friends not to fall for the chiseler's convincing lies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swindler - a person who cheats others, especially for money
  • Synonym: fraudster - a person who commits fraud
  • Antonym: honest person - someone who does not cheat or deceive

Etymology

From 'chisel' (to cheat), metaphorically extended to someone who commits petty fraud.
