gantlet

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæntlət / (AmE), / ˈɡæntlɪt / (BrE)
a protective glove with a long cuff covering part of the forearm, often worn as part of a suit of armor or for safety.
protective glove with a long cuff

Examples

The knight wore a steel gantlet to protect his hand and forearm in battle.
Welders often use a leather gantlet when working with hot metals.
She admired the intricate design on the old gantlet displayed in the museum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gauntlet - alternative spelling for a protective glove with a long cuff

Etymology

From Old French *gantelet*, diminutive of *gant* (glove).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡæntlət / (AmE), / ˈɡæntlɪt / (BrE)
A form of punishment in which a person is forced to run between two rows of people who strike at them as they pass.
punishment: run between rows of assailants

Examples

In the 18th century, sailors might be forced to run the gantlet as punishment for disobedience.
The prisoner dreaded the moment he would have to face the gantlet set up by the guards.
To teach a lesson, the captain ordered the accused to run the gantlet before the entire crew.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gauntlet - a form of punishment: to run between two lines of people who beat you

Etymology

From Swedish 'gatlopp', from 'gata' (lane) + 'lopp' (course, running).

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡænt.lət / (AmE), / ˈɡɔːnt.lɪt / (BrE)
a glove made of armored leather, used to protect the hand, especially as part of a suit of armor
armored glove (hand protection)

Examples

The knight slipped his gantlet back on before mounting his horse.
During the duel, her opponent lost a gantlet, exposing his hand.
The museum displayed a medieval gantlet alongside other pieces of armor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gauntlet - an armored glove worn with a suit of armor

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'gauntlet', from Old French 'gantelet', diminutive of 'gant' (glove).

4. NOUN

/ ˈɡæntlət / (AmE), / ˈɡæntlɪt / (BrE)
A place where two parallel railroad tracks converge closely, with the inner rails crossing and running parallel before diverging, so each train stays on its own track.
converging railroad tracks with overlapping rails

Examples

A section of the line includes a gantlet to allow trains to pass through the narrow tunnel.
Engineers installed a gantlet track near the station to accommodate both freight and passenger trains.
The train slows down as it approaches the complicated gantlet section.
