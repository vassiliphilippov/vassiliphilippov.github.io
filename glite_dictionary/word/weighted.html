weighted

1. ADJ

/ ˈweɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈweɪtɪd / (BrE)
adjusted by giving different values, importance, or factors more influence than others
adjusted by importance or value

Examples

The weighted scores ensure that more important tasks influence the final result more heavily.
Our club uses a weighted voting system to give larger groups more say in decisions.
The report includes both simple and weighted averages for comparison.

Synset Examples

votes weighted according to the size of constituencies
a law weighted in favor of landlords
a weighted average

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unweighted - not adjusted by importance or value

Etymology

From the verb 'weight' + -ed, meaning provided with a weight or influenced by weighting.

2. ADJ

/ ˈweɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈweɪtɪd / (BrE)
Made heavy or burdened, either physically or metaphorically; pressed down by weight or weariness.
made heavy or burdened

Examples

After the long hike, his weighted arms hung limply by his sides.
She tried to keep her eyes open, but her weighted eyelids betrayed her exhaustion.
The thick coat left him feeling weighted and sluggish during the winter walk.

Synset Examples

his leaden arms
weighted eyelids

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leaden - heavy and inert; oppressively weighty
  • Antonym: light - not heavy; having little weight

Etymology

From the verb 'weight', derived from Middle English 'weyte', from Old North French 'weité', based on 'weight'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01193396-s
Synset POS: s
