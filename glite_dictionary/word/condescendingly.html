condescendingly

1. ADV

/ ˌkɑːn.dɪˈsɛnd.ɪŋ.li / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.dɪˈsend.ɪŋ.li / (BrE)
In a way that shows that you think you are more important or intelligent than other people; in a patronizing manner.
in a patronizing way

Examples

She spoke condescendingly to the interns, assuming they knew nothing about the project.
He nodded condescendingly, making it clear he did not truly value her opinion.
When he explained the instructions condescendingly, it made everyone in the room uncomfortable.

Synset Examples

he treats his secretary condescendingly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patronizingly - in a condescending or superior manner

Etymology

From 'condescending' + '-ly'; 'condescending' from Latin 'condescendere' (to stoop together, yield), from 'con-' (together) + 'descendere' (to descend).
