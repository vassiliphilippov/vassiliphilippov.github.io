drink

1. NOUN

/ drɪŋk / (AmE), / drɪŋk / (BrE)
A single serving of a beverage, especially an alcoholic one.
single serving of a beverage

Examples

She ordered a cold drink to cool off after her walk.
Would you like a drink with your meal?
He poured himself a stiff drink after a long day.

Synset Examples

I asked for a hot drink
likes a drink before dinner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beverage - a drink, especially one other than water

Etymology

From Old English 'drincan' (to drink), related to Dutch 'drinken' and German 'trinken'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
