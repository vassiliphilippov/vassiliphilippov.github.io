make-believe

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪk bəˌliv / (AmE), / ˈmeɪk bɪˌliːv / (BrE)
the action or practice of pretending; imaginative play, especially as engaged in by children.
imaginative pretending or play

Examples

Children often engage in make-believe games, creating imaginary worlds and characters.
The playroom was filled with costumes and props for endless hours of make-believe.
For a few moments, the actor's performance blurred the lines between reality and make-believe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretending - the act of making believe or imagining something is true
  • Synonym: fantasy - the faculty or activity of imagining things, especially things that are impossible or improbable
  • Antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist

Etymology

Formed from the phrase 'make believe', meaning to pretend, dating from the 19th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪk bəˌliv / (AmE), / ˈmeɪk bəˌliːv / (BrE)
the act of pretending or imagining, especially as part of children's play or in fiction
imaginative play; pretense

Examples

Children love playing make-believe with their toys, pretending to be superheroes or explorers.
The story takes place in a world of make-believe, filled with magic and talking animals.
Sometimes adults indulge in a little make-believe to escape the stresses of everyday life.

Synset Examples

it was just pretend

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretend - the act of pretending or feigning
  • Antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist

Etymology

Formed from the phrase 'make believe', first recorded in the 19th century, meaning 'to pretend'.
