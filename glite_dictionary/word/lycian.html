Lycian

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪʃiən / (AmE), / ˈlɪsɪən / (BrE)
an extinct Anatolian language that was spoken by the ancient Lycians in what is now southwestern Turkey
ancient Anatolian language

Examples

Ancient inscriptions in Lycian have helped linguists better understand Indo-European languages.
The Lycian language was once spoken along the Mediterranean coast of what is now Turkey.
Few people today can read Lycian, but its script has been partially deciphered by scholars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Luwian - another extinct Anatolian language related to Lycian
  • Synonym: Hittite - an ancient Anatolian language of the Indo-European family

Etymology

From Lycia, the historical region in Anatolia, with English suffix -an.
