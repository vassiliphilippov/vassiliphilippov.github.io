lash-up

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæʃ ʌp / (AmE), / ˈlæʃ ʌp / (BrE)
an arrangement or structure put together quickly or temporarily, often using whatever materials are at hand
a temporary, improvised arrangement

Examples

The engineers created a lash-up to keep the system running until a proper fix was installed.
After the power outage, we had to make a quick lash-up to restore communication.
Their tent was really just a lash-up made from tarps and sticks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contraption - a machine or device that appears strange or unnecessarily complicated; a gadget
  • Synonym: jury-rig - to make a temporary repair or arrangement, typically in an improvised manner
  • Antonym: permanent - lasting or intended to last indefinitely; not temporary

Etymology

From the verb 'lash' (to tie or bind with rope or cord), suggesting something tied together hastily.
