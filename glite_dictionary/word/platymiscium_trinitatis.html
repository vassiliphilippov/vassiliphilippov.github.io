Platymiscium trinitatis

1. NOUN

/ ˌplætɪˌmɪs.siˈʌm trɪ.nɪˈteɪ.tɪs / (AmE), / ˌplætɪˌmɪs.siˈʌm trɪ.nɪˈteɪ.tɪs / (BrE)
A large tropical tree native to Trinidad and Guyana, with odd-pinnate leaves, violet-scented yellow flowers in racemes, and long smooth pods; often cultivated in parks and large gardens.
tropical South American tree species

Examples

The Platymiscium trinitatis tree is prized for its beautiful yellow blossoms and fragrant scent in botanical gardens.
In Trinidad, Platymiscium trinitatis can be found as a specimen tree in many large parks and estates.
Due to its attractive foliage and smooth pods, Platymiscium trinitatis is often used for ornamental planting.

Etymology

From Latinized genus name 'Platymiscium' and species epithet referring to Trinidad.
