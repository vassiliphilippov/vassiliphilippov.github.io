intensely

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈtɛnsli / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛnsli / (BrE)
In an intense manner; to a very high degree or with great force or focus.
to a high or extreme degree

Examples

The room was intensely quiet after everyone left.
She studied intensely for her final exams, barely taking breaks.
The flavors in this dish are intensely rich and satisfying.

Synset Examples

he worked intensely
it was intensely hot
the sky was intensely blue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extremely - to a very great degree; very much
  • Antonym: mildly - to a slight degree; not strongly

Etymology

From the adjective 'intense' + '-ly'. 'Intense' from Latin 'intensus' (stretched, strained).

2. ADV

/ ɪnˈtɛn(s)li / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛn(t)sli / (BrE)
in a way that is very strong, extreme, or shows great depth of feeling or concentration.
in a very strong or extreme way

Examples

She stared intensely at the painting, trying to understand its meaning.
He felt intensely nervous before the big performance.
They argued intensely about the best approach to the problem.

Synset Examples

They felt the loss deeply
she loved him intensely

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deeply - to a great depth or degree (emotionally or physically)
  • Antonym: mildly - in a gentle or moderate way

Etymology

From Latin intensus (stretched, strained) + -ly.
