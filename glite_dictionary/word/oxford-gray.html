Oxford-gray

1. ADJ

/ ˈɑːksfərd ɡreɪ / (AmE), / ˈɒksfəd ɡreɪ / (BrE)
of a dark, bluish-grey color, typically resembling the shade associated with Oxford uniforms or cloth.
dark, bluish-grey color

Examples

He wore an Oxford-gray suit to the interview, looking both professional and stylish.
The room was decorated with Oxford-gray curtains that gave it a sophisticated feel.
They chose Oxford-gray paint for the feature wall in their new apartment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gray - the color between black and white; grey

Etymology

Named after Oxford, referencing the color of traditional Oxford University uniforms or cloth.
