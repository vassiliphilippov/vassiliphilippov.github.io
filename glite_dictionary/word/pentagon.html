Pentagon

1. PROPN

/ ˈpɛn.tə.ɡɑn / (AmE), / ˈpɛn.tə.ɡən / (BrE)
the main building in Washington, D.C., with five sides, that serves as the headquarters of the United States Department of Defense
U.S. Defense Department headquarters building
american

Examples

The Pentagon announced new military spending plans this morning.
Security around the Pentagon was heightened following the recent incident.
Many important decisions about national defense are made at the Pentagon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: White House - the official residence and workplace of the President of the United States

Etymology

From Greek 'pentagonon' meaning 'five-angled', referring to the building's shape.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛn.tə.ɡɑn / (AmE), / ˈpɛn.tə.ɡən / (BrE)
the building in Washington, D.C. that serves as the headquarters of the United States Department of Defense; also used to refer to the U.S. military leadership or establishment collectively
U.S. Department of Defense headquarters; U.S. military leadership
american

Examples

Officials from the Pentagon announced new military operations overseas.
The Pentagon released a statement regarding the security situation.
Budget cuts have been a major concern for the Pentagon this year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Department of Defense - the executive department of the U.S. government responsible for the armed forces

Etymology

From the five-sided shape (Greek 'pentagonon'); the building was named for its shape.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08224016-n
Synset POS: n
