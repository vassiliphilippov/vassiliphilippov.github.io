conurbation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːnɚˈbeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒnəˈbeɪʃən / (BrE)
A large area made up of several towns or cities that have grown and merged together to form one continuous urban region.
large merged urban area

Examples

The conurbation stretches for miles, blending once separate towns into a single urban landscape.
Industrial development has transformed the region into a bustling conurbation.
As the city grew, smaller villages were absorbed into the expanding conurbation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metropolis - a very large and densely populated industrial and commercial city
  • Synonym: megalopolis - a very large, heavily populated urban complex, often including several cities

Etymology

From Latin 'con-' (together) + 'urb' (city) + '-ation'. Coined by Patrick Geddes in 1915.
