to embroil

1. VERB

/ ɪmˈbrɔɪl / (AmE), / ɪmˈbrɔɪl / (BrE)
to involve someone deeply in an argument, conflict, or difficult situation
involve in conflict or difficulty

Examples

Several politicians became embroiled in the scandal following the investigation.
He didn't want to get embroiled in their family dispute, so he kept his distance.
The company was embroiling itself in a lengthy legal battle over the new patents.

Synset Examples

They were swept up by the events
don't drag me into this business

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to entangle - to involve in a complicated or difficult situation
  • Antonym: to extricate - to free from an entanglement or difficulty

Etymology

From French 'embrouiller', from 'em-' (in) + 'brouiller' (to tangle, confuse).
