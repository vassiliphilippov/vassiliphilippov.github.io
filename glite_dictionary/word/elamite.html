Elamite

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛləˌmaɪt / (AmE), / ˈiːləˌmaɪt / (BrE)
an extinct ancient language once spoken by the Elamites in what is now southwestern Iran; its linguistic affiliations are not well understood
extinct ancient language of the Elamites

Examples

Ancient records in the Elamite language provide valuable insight into early Iranian history.
Researchers struggle to decipher Elamite because of its unique structure and unknown relations.
The oldest surviving Elamite inscriptions date back more than four thousand years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sumerian - an extinct language of ancient Sumer, unrelated to known language families

Etymology

From Elam, an ancient civilization in southwest Iran, plus the English suffix -ite.

2. NOUN

/ ˈiː.ləˌmaɪt / (AmE), / ˈiː.lə.maɪt / (BrE)
a member of an ancient people who lived in Elam, east of Babylonia, as early as 3000 BC
member of ancient Elamite people

Examples

Archaeologists discovered artifacts believed to belong to early Elamite settlers in the region.
The Elamite army often clashed with their Babylonian neighbors.
Ancient Elamite rulers built ziggurats that still fascinate historians today.

Etymology

From Elam, the name of the ancient region, + -ite (denoting people or things associated with a place).
