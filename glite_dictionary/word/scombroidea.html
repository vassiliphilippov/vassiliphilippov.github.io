Scombroidea

1. NOUN

/ skɒmˈbrɔɪdiə / (AmE), / skɒmˈbrɔɪdɪə / (BrE)
A superfamily of marine fish that includes mackerels, tunas, bonitos, swordfishes, and sailfishes.
superfamily of marine fish (mackerels, tunas, etc.)

Examples

The family of Scombroidea includes some of the fastest swimmers in the ocean.
Researchers are studying how climate change affects distribution within the Scombroidea superfamily.
Swordfish, a member of the Scombroidea, are known for their long, flat bills.

Etymology

From New Latin Scombroidea, based on Latin scomber (mackerel) and Greek -oeidēs (like).
