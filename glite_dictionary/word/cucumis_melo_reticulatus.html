Cucumis melo reticulatus

1. NOUN

/ kuˈkjuː.mɪs ˈmɛloʊ rɛˌtɪk.jʊˈleɪ.təs / (AmE), / kjuːˈkjuː.mɪs ˈmiː.ləʊ rɛˌtɪk.jʊˈleɪ.təs / (BrE)
A variety of muskmelon plant with a thin netted rind and sweet green flesh, cultivated for its edible fruit.
a muskmelon with netted rind and green flesh

Examples

The Cucumis melo reticulatus thrives in warm climates and produces sweet, succulent fruit.
Botanists often use Cucumis melo reticulatus to refer to certain varieties of muskmelons.
He prefers the taste of Cucumis melo reticulatus to other types of melons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muskmelon - a melon variety with sweet fragrant flesh
  • Degree Lower: cantaloupe - a type of muskmelon with orange flesh

Etymology

From Latin: genus Cucumis (cucumber/melon) and species melo (melon), with 'reticulatus' referring to the net-like rind.
