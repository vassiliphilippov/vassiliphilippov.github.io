impeccant

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɛkənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛkənt / (BrE)
Free from sin; morally faultless.
free from sin

Examples

Despite the temptations that surrounded her, she remained impeccant throughout her life.
The story described an impeccant monk who inspired the entire village.
He aspired to live an impeccant existence, free from even the smallest transgression.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sinless - without sin or moral fault
  • Synonym: impeccable - flawless, without fault
  • Antonym: sinful - full of sin; wicked

Etymology

From Latin 'impeccans', from 'im-' (not) + 'peccare' (to sin).
