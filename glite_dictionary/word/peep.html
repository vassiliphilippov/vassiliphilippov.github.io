peep

1. VERB

/ pip / (AmE), / piːp / (BrE)
to look quickly and secretly, especially through a narrow opening or from a hidden place
look quickly and secretly

Examples

She tried to peep through the keyhole without being noticed.
The children peeped behind the curtain to watch the magician set up.
He couldn't resist peeping at his birthday presents early.

Synset Examples

He peeped at the woman through the window

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peek - to look quickly or furtively
  • Synonym: glance - to take a brief look

Etymology

From Middle English pepen, probably imitative of the sound of a slight, sharp look or chirp.

3. NOUN

/ pip / (AmE), / piːp / (BrE)
a short, soft, high-pitched cry made by a young bird
a young bird's weak cry

Examples

The tiny chick let out a soft peep as it hatched from its egg.
From the nest, the mother heard the constant peep of her hungry offspring.
Not a single peep was heard after the ducklings fell asleep.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chirp - a short, sharp sound made by small birds or insects

Etymology

Imitative origin, echoing the sound made by chicks.
