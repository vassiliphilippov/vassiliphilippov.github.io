to goose step

1. VERB

/ ɡus stɛp / (AmE), / ɡuːs stɛp / (BrE)
(of soldiers) to march with stiff, straight legs, swinging them high, in a military fashion typical of some armies.
march stiffly in military style

Examples

The soldiers began to goose step across the square during the national parade.
Recruits practiced for hours each day learning to goose step in perfect unison.
He watched as the ceremonial guard goose stepped past the grandstand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: march - walk with regular steps in a formal group, especially as part of a military parade

Etymology

From 'goose' (the bird) + 'step' (stride), referring to the characteristic marching style resembling the gait of a goose.
