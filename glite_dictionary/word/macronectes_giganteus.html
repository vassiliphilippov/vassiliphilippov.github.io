Macronectes giganteus

1. NOUN

/ ˌmækroʊˈnɛktiːz ʤaɪˈgænˌtiːəs / (AmE), / ˌmækrəʊˈnɛktiːz dʒɪˈgæn.tɪ.əs / (BrE)
A large brownish petrel found chiefly in Antarctic seas, also known as the southern giant petrel.
Antarctic large brownish petrel

Examples

The Macronectes giganteus is one of the largest seabirds nesting in Antarctica.
Researchers observed a flock of Macronectes giganteus feeding on the shoreline.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect the habitats of Macronectes giganteus in the Southern Ocean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: southern giant petrel - common name for Macronectes giganteus

Etymology

From Greek makros (large) + nektes (swimmer); species epithet from Latin giganteus (gigantic).
