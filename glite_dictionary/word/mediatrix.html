mediatrix

1. NOUN

/ ˌmiːdiˈeɪtrɪks / (AmE), / ˌmiːdiˈætrɪks / (BrE)
a woman who mediates; a female mediator
female mediator

Examples

The queen was regarded as a mediatrix between the king and his subjects.
Mary is often referred to as the mediatrix in Catholic theology.
As a skilled mediatrix, she resolved conflicts within the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mediator - a person who mediates between parties

Etymology

From Latin mediātrix (female mediator), feminine of mediator.
