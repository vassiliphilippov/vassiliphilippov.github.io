phycomycosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌfaɪkoʊmaɪˈkoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌfʌɪkəʊmʌɪˈkəʊsɪs / (BrE)
a fungal infection caused by fungi of the Phycomycetes group
fungal infection from Phycomycetes
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the skin lesions as phycomycosis after microscopic examination.
Veterinarians may encounter phycomycosis in livestock exposed to contaminated environments.
Treatment of phycomycosis often requires antifungal medication and sometimes surgical intervention.

Etymology

From Greek phykos (seaweed, fungus) + mycosis (fungal infection).
