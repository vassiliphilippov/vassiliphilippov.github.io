Pinter

1. PROPN

/ ˈpɪntər / (AmE), / ˈpɪntə / (BrE)
Harold Pinter (1930–2008), an English dramatist and Nobel laureate known for his plays marked by silences, pauses, and the use of inaction.
English dramatist and Nobel laureate

Examples

Pinter's plays are famous for their use of silences and underlying tension.
The works of Pinter have had a profound influence on modern theater.
Everyone in the class analyzed how Pinter used pauses to create drama.
