Penutian

1. NOUN

/ pəˈnuːtiən / (AmE), / pəˈnjuːtiən / (BrE)
a member of any of various North American indigenous peoples who speak one of the Penutian languages
member of a Penutian-speaking indigenous people

Examples

The anthropologist spent years studying the culture of the Penutian people in the Pacific Northwest.
Artifacts from ancient Penutian settlements have provided valuable insights into their way of life.
Several tribes classified as Penutian share similar linguistic and cultural traits.

Etymology

From Penuti, the name of a language, combined with -an, denoting people or pertaining to.

2. NOUN

/ pəˈnuːtiən / (AmE), / pɛˈnjuːtiən / (BrE)
A family of Native American languages spoken originally in the interior valley of California and surrounding regions.
Native American language family

Examples

The Penutian language family includes several languages once spoken in California.
Linguists debate whether Penutian is a genetically unified language group.
Researchers have uncovered new evidence about the origins of the Penutian peoples.

Etymology

Formed from 'Pene' and 'Utian', the names of two constituent languages; coined by linguist Roland B. Dixon in 1913.
