chokepoint

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃoʊkˌpɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈtʃəʊk.pɔɪnt / (BrE)
A narrow passage or area where movement or flow is restricted or slowed down.
narrow passage restricting flow

Examples

The Strait of Hormuz is a critical chokepoint for global oil shipments.
During rush hour, the intersection becomes a chokepoint for city traffic.
Inspectors identified a chokepoint in the assembly line that was causing delays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bottleneck - a point of congestion or blockage

Etymology

From 'choke' (to block) + 'point', referring to a location where flow is impeded.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃoʊkˌpɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈtʃəʊkˌpɔɪnt / (BrE)
a place where movement is slowed down or blocked by a bottleneck or congestion
place of critical congestion or blockage

Examples

During the morning commute, the tunnel becomes a major chokepoint for city traffic.
In military strategy, controlling a chokepoint can change the outcome of a campaign.
Supply lines slowed at a chokepoint near the border, delaying deliveries for days.

Synset Examples

the bridge is always a chokepoint at rush hour

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bottleneck - a narrow or critical point that slows traffic or progress

Etymology

From 'choke' meaning to block and 'point' referring to a specific place; developed in mid-20th-century English.
