Apennines

1. PROPN

/ əˈpɛnˌaɪnz / (AmE), / əˈpɛn.aɪnz / (BrE)
a mountain range that extends along the length of the Italian peninsula, forming its backbone
mountain range in Italy

Examples

The Apennines are home to a variety of unique wildlife and plant species.
Many picturesque villages are nestled within the foothills of the Apennines.
Heavy snowfall made traveling through the Apennines difficult that winter.

Etymology

From Latin Apenninus, the classical name for the mountain range.
