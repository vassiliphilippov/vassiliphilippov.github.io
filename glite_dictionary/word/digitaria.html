Digitaria

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈdʒɪtəriə / (AmE), / dɪˈdʒɪtəriə / (BrE)
A genus of grasses that includes several weedy species commonly known as crab grass or finger grass.
genus of grasses (crab grass)

Examples

Several species of Digitaria are known as invasive crab grasses in North America.
Farmers often struggle to control Digitaria in their wheat and corn fields.
The characteristic finger-like seed heads make Digitaria easy to identify among other grasses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crabgrass - common name for weedy grasses of the genus Digitaria
  • Synonym: finger grass - another common name for species of Digitaria

Etymology

From Latin 'digitus' (finger), referring to the finger-like arrangement of the flower spikes.

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈdʒɪtəriə / (AmE), / dɪˈdʒɪtəriə / (BrE)
A genus of grasses in the family Poaceae, commonly known as crab grass or finger grass.
genus of grasses (crab grass)

Examples

The invasive species of Digitaria can quickly take over lawns in warm climates.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of several Digitaria species to control weed spread in agriculture.
Some birds feed on the seeds produced by plants of the Digitaria genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crabgrass - a common name for grasses in the genus Digitaria

Etymology

From Latin 'digitus' (finger), referring to the finger-like arrangement of the flower clusters.
