cartridge

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːrtrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɑːtrɪdʒ / (BrE)
the part of a record player arm that holds the needle and converts vibrations into an electrical signal
removable holder for a record player's needle

Examples

The sound quality improved dramatically after I replaced the old cartridge on my turntable.
He carefully aligned the new cartridge to ensure the needle tracked the grooves accurately.
Many audiophiles invest in a high-end cartridge to get the best possible playback from their vinyl records.

Etymology

From French cartouche, from Italian cartuccia, originally meaning a cartridge for ammunition.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑr.trɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.trɪdʒ / (BrE)
a small container or module designed to be inserted into a larger device, typically to provide a replaceable medium, substance, or component such as ink, tape, or data storage
replaceable module for a device

Examples

I need to replace the ink cartridge in my printer before I can print more documents.
The video game console requires you to insert a cartridge to play different games.
He carefully slid the new tape cartridge into the player and pressed play.

Synset Examples

he loaded a cartridge of fresh tape into the tape deck

Etymology

From French 'cartouche', from Italian 'cartuccia', meaning a case for charges.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɑr.trɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.trɪdʒ / (BrE)
A light-tight container that holds photographic film and supplies it for exposure in a camera.
container holding photographic film

Examples

The photographer loaded a new cartridge into his camera before heading outside.
You need to rewind the cartridge before removing it from the camera.
Most modern cameras no longer require a film cartridge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: film canister - small container for holding and protecting film, especially 35mm

4. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːrtrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɑːtrɪdʒ / (BrE)
A metal or plastic case containing a charge and a bullet or shot, used as ammunition in firearms.
ammunition case for firearms

Examples

The hunter loaded a new cartridge into his rifle before aiming at the target.
She found a spent cartridge on the forest floor after the shooting practice.
Always use the correct type of cartridge for your handgun to ensure safety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shell - casing containing a charge and projectile, for firearms

Etymology

From French 'cartouche', from Italian 'cartuccia', diminutive of 'carta' (paper).
