Bambuseae

1. NOUN

/ bæmˈbuːsi.i / (AmE), / bæmˈbuːsi.i / (BrE)
a tribe of tall, woody grasses known as bamboos, found mainly in tropical and subtropical regions
tribe of bamboos (plants)

Examples

The Bambuseae are widely cultivated for their fast growth and versatile uses.
Botanists classify many common bamboos under the tribe Bambuseae.
Members of the Bambuseae can reach impressive heights in a short period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bamboo - a giant woody grass of the subfamily Bambusoideae

Etymology

From Latin 'bambusa' (bamboo), used in botanical taxonomy.
