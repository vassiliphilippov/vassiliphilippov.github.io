to purvey

1. VERB

/ pərˈveɪ / (AmE), / pəˈveɪ / (BrE)
to supply or provide, especially food or other essential goods
supply or provide goods, especially food

Examples

The local farm continues to purvey fresh vegetables to restaurants across the city.
For decades, that merchant has purveyed fine wines to discerning customers.
Several companies purvey organic foods to health-conscious consumers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supply - to provide something that is needed
  • Synonym: to provide - to make available what is needed
  • Antonym: to withhold - to refuse to give or supply something

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *purveier*, from Old French *porveoir* (to provide), from Latin *providere* (to foresee, provide).
