algorism

1. NOUN

/ ˈælɡəˌrɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈalɡərɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the Arabic or decimal system of numeration, using ten digits including zero
Arabic (decimal) system of numeration

Examples

The adoption of algorism in Europe greatly improved commercial arithmetic.
He wrote a paper on the history of algorism and its influence on mathematics.
Before algorism, calculations were far more complex using Roman numerals.

Etymology

From Old French 'algorisme', from Medieval Latin 'algorismus', named after the Persian mathematician al-Khwarizmi.

2. NOUN

/ ˈælɡərɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈælɡərɪzəm / (BrE)
the method of performing arithmetic calculations using Arabic numerals; the decimal number system as opposed to Roman numerals
arithmetic with Arabic numerals

Examples

Before the widespread use of algorism, calculations were performed with Roman numerals or on counting boards.
The development of algorism greatly simplified arithmetic in medieval Europe.
She studied the origins of algorism for her thesis on the history of mathematics.

Etymology

From Old French *algorisme*, from Medieval Latin *algorismus*, from Arabic al-Khwarizmi, referring to the Persian mathematician al-Khwarizmi.
