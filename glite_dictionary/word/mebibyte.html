mebibyte

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛbɪˌbaɪt / (AmE), / ˈmiːbɪˌbaɪt / (BrE)
a unit of information equal to 1,048,576 (2^20) bytes, commonly used in computing to specify data size
unit of 1,048,576 bytes (computer data)

Examples

My hard drive has a capacity of 500 mebibytes for backup files.
The file size was exactly one mebibyte when I checked my computer.
Some software installations require at least 300 mebibytes of free space.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: megabyte - unit of information equal to one million bytes; often used less precisely than 'mebibyte'

Etymology

Formed from 'mega binary byte', with 'bi' inserted to indicate binary multiples, as standardized by the International Electrotechnical Commission (IEC) in 1998.
