meshing

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɛʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of two or more parts, especially gears or mechanical components, fitting or coming into contact by engaging with each other
engagement or fitting of mechanical parts

Examples

The precise meshing of the gears ensured smooth operation of the machine.
Improper meshing can cause excessive wear or failure in mechanical systems.
He examined the meshing of the cogs to diagnose the timing issue.

Synset Examples

the engagement of the clutch
the meshing of gears

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engagement - the state of being interlocked or in contact (in mechanics)
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07352714-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɛʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of interlocking or fitting together, especially of gears or similar parts
the act of interlocking or joining together

Examples

The careful meshing of gears ensures that the machine runs smoothly.
Proper meshing of ideas from both teams led to a successful project.
Engineers inspected the meshing of the cogs to prevent mechanical failure.

Synset Examples

an interlocking of arms by the police held the crowd in check

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engagement - the act of being engaged or interlocked (especially of gears)
  • Antonym: disengagement - the act of separating or no longer interlocking

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'mesh' (to engage or interlock), with the suffix '-ing' denoting the action or process.

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɛʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɛʃɪŋ / (BrE)
an open fabric of string, rope, or wire woven together at regular intervals to form a net-like material
open net-like woven material

Examples

The beekeeper wore a veil made of fine meshing to protect his face.
Chicken wire is a type of meshing used to build animal enclosures.
The tent windows were covered with meshing to keep out insects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mesh - an open space created by the threads of a net or fabric; the material itself

Etymology

From 'mesh' (Old English *mǣsce*, net, mesh) + '-ing', forming a noun of action or result.
