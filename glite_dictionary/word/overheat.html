to overheat

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊ.vɚˈhiːt / (AmE), / ˌəʊ.vəˈhiːt / (BrE)
to make or become excessively hot, especially to a point where it may cause damage or discomfort
make or become excessively hot

Examples

If you overheat the soup, it might lose its flavor.
The engine can overheat on a hot day if not checked regularly.
Don’t let your computer overheat during long gaming sessions.

Synset Examples

The room was overheated

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cool - to make or become less hot

Etymology

From over- (excessively) + heat.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00373154-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈhit / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈhiːt / (BrE)
(of a machine, device, or organism) to become excessively and undesirably hot, often causing malfunction or danger.
become excessively hot

Examples

If you leave your laptop running all day, it may overheat and shut down unexpectedly.
The engine began to overheat after the coolant leaked out onto the road.
During the race, his car overheated and forced him to stop.

Synset Examples

The car engines overheated

Related Terms

  • Antonym: overcool - to become or make excessively cold
  • Degree Lower: heat - to make or become hot

Etymology

From over- (excessively) + heat (from Old English hǣtan, to make hot).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00370916-v
Synset POS: v
