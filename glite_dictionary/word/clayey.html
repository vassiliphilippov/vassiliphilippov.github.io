clayey

1. ADJ

/ ˈkleɪ.i / (AmE), / ˈkleɪ.i / (BrE)
containing or resembling clay; having the characteristics of clay, especially in texture or composition.
containing or resembling clay

Examples

The soil in this part of the field is very clayey and hard to work with.
They found several fossils embedded in clayey layers beneath the sand.
The vineyard’s clayey ground retains moisture well during dry summers.

Synset Examples

argillaceous rocks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: argillaceous - containing or composed of clay

Etymology

From 'clay' + '-ey', meaning 'like or full of'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkleɪ.i / (AmE), / ˈkleɪ.i / (BrE)
(of soil) containing or resembling clay; compact and fine-grained.
containing or resembling clay (of soil)

Examples

The plants struggled to grow in the clayey field after heavy rain.
Gardeners often dislike clayey ground because it drains poorly.
The area is known for its clayey texture, making it ideal for pottery production.

Synset Examples

the clayey soil was heavy and easily saturated

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sandy - (of soil) containing or resembling sand

Etymology

From 'clay' + '-ey', with '-ey' forming adjectives meaning 'characteristic of'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00504399-s
Synset POS: s
