Polypodiaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊləˌpoʊdiˈeɪsiˌaɪ / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪpəʊdɪˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a large family of ferns that has sometimes been divided into several separate families, including Aspleniaceae, Blechnaceae, and others
a family of ferns in plant taxonomy

Examples

The Polypodiaceae include many species that are popular as ornamental plants.
Botanists have debated the exact classification of the Polypodiaceae for decades.
Ferns in the Polypodiaceae can be found in a wide range of habitats around the world.

Etymology

From New Latin Polypodiaceae, from Polypodium (the type genus) + -aceae (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːlɪˌpoʊdiˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˌpəʊdiˈeɪsɪiː / (BrE)
a large family of ferns, commonly known as the polypody family, which has sometimes been divided into several smaller families in botanical classification.
a family of ferns (polypody family)

Examples

The Polypodiaceae are widely distributed in tropical and temperate regions around the world.
Many houseplant enthusiasts grow ferns that belong to the Polypodiaceae family.
Scientists debated the classification of Polypodiaceae, splitting it into separate families in some systems.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the genus Polypodium, from Greek polypodion 'little many-footed one'.
