mixed

1. ADJ

/ mɪkst / (AmE), / mɪkst / (BrE)
consisting of or involving people of different races; interracial.
involving people of different races

Examples

The school has a mixed student population, reflecting the diversity of the community.
They moved into a mixed neighborhood where families from different backgrounds live together.
Many colleges promote mixed housing to encourage interaction among students of various races.

Synset Examples

interracial schools
a mixed neighborhood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interracial - involving or composed of different races

2. ADJ

/ mɪkst / (AmE), / mɪkst / (BrE)
consisting of different kinds or elements, especially arranged in a random or varied way; composed of an assortment of types.
consisting of various kinds; assorted

Examples

The festival offered a mixed program of music, featuring jazz, rock, and classical performances.
She prepared a mixed bouquet containing roses, lilies, and daisies for the centerpiece.
The team is composed of a mixed group of experts from several different fields.

Synset Examples

an arrangement of assorted spring flowers
assorted sizes
miscellaneous accessories
a mixed program of baroque and contemporary music
a motley crew
sundry sciences commonly known as social

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assorted - of various sorts put together; varied
  • Synonym: miscellaneous - consisting of many different kinds; mixed
  • Antonym: uniform - all the same; not mixed or varied

Etymology

From the verb 'mix', from Middle English 'mixen', from Old English 'miscian', from Latin 'miscēre' (to mix).
