radially

1. ADV

/ ˈreɪdiəli / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiəli / (BrE)
In a manner that proceeds outward from a central point or axis; in the direction of a radius or radii.
in a direction from the center outward

Examples

The seeds are dispersed radially from the center of the flower head.
The spokes extended radially from the hub of the bicycle wheel.
Heat is transferred radially outward in a circular metal plate.

Synset Examples

an imaginative dispersal of the pews radially from the central focus of the pulpit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: circularly - in a circular manner
  • Antonym: axially - along or relating to an axis

Etymology

From 'radial' + '-ly'; 'radial' from Latin 'radius' (ray, spoke of a wheel).
