South Platte

1. PROPN

/ saʊθ ˈplæt / (AmE), / saʊθ ˈplæt / (BrE)
a river in the central United States, a tributary of the Platte River, flowing mainly through Colorado and Nebraska
tributary river in U.S. (Colorado, Nebraska)

Examples

The South Platte flows through Denver before joining the main Platte River.
Many towns along the South Platte rely on its water for agriculture and industry.
Heavy rains caused the South Platte to flood several nearby fields this spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Platte River - river in the central United States into which the South Platte flows
