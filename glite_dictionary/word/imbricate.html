imbricate

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪm.brɪ.kət / (AmE), / ˈɪm.brɪ.kət / (BrE)
(esp. of leaves, scales, or similar elements) overlapping each other like tiles or shingles.
overlapping in a tile-like pattern

Examples

The imbricate arrangement of the pine cone's scales helps protect its seeds.
The rose petals are imbricate, giving the flower its tightly packed structure.
Under the microscope, the fish's gills appeared imbricate, each layer neatly overlapping the next.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overlapping - partly covering something else
  • Antonym: adjacent - next to or adjoining something else

Etymology

From Latin imbricatus, past participle of imbricare, from imbrex (roof tile).

2. VERB

/ ˈɪm.brɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪm.brɪ.keɪt / (BrE)
to arrange or lay so as to overlap, especially in a regular pattern like roof tiles or scales
arrange to overlap (like tiles or scales)

Examples

The ceramic shingles were imbricated to ensure the roof was watertight.
The architect carefully imbricated the glass panels for both function and beauty.
Certain leaves on this plant imbricate naturally, forming a pleasing pattern.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overlap - to extend over and cover a part of
  • Antonym: separate - to move or be apart; not touching or overlapping

Etymology

From Latin imbricatus, past participle of imbricare (to cover with tiles), from imbrex (roof tile).

3. VERB

/ ˈɪm.brɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪm.brɪ.keɪt / (BrE)
(of things such as tiles, scales, or leaves) to overlap in a regular, often slanted, pattern, like roof tiles.
to overlap in a regular pattern

Examples

The feathers on the bird's wing imbricate to form a protective surface.
Some plant leaves imbricate along the stem, creating a layered effect.
Roof tiles are designed to imbricate so that rainwater runs off smoothly.

Synset Examples

The roof tiles imbricate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overlap - to partly cover the surface of something else

Etymology

From Latin imbricatus, past participle of imbricare (to cover with tiles), from imbrex (roof tile).
