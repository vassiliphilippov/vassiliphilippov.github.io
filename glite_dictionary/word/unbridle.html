to unbridle

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈbraɪdəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈbraɪdəl / (BrE)
to remove the bridle from (a horse, mule, etc.)
remove the bridle from an animal

Examples

After the long ride, she carefully unbridled her horse and led it to the stable.
The groom unbridled the mule before letting it graze in the pasture.
Please unbridle your pony as soon as you get back from the trail.

Etymology

From un- (prefix meaning 'not' or 'remove') + bridle (from Old English 'bridel').
