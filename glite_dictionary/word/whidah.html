whidah

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪdə / (AmE), / ˈwɪdə / (BrE)
A small African songbird of the weaver family, especially known for the long tail feathers of the male.
African songbird with long tail feathers

Examples

The male whidah displays its long, flowing tail feathers during the breeding season.
We spotted a flock of whidahs near the riverbank in Ghana.
Many bird enthusiasts are fascinated by the striking appearance of the whidah.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: widowbird - African bird with a long tail, also known as whydah

Etymology

From Whydah, a former kingdom and port in Benin, West Africa, where the birds are found.
