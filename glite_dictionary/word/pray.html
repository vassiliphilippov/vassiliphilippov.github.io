to pray

1. VERB

/ preɪ / (AmE), / preɪ / (BrE)
to address a deity, prophet, saint, or object of worship in words or thought, especially in order to ask for help or express thanks
address a deity or worship in prayer

Examples

Every night before bed, she would pray for her family's safety.
Many people pray in times of trouble, seeking comfort and guidance.
He knelt in the chapel to pray for forgiveness and strength.

Synset Examples

pray to the Lord

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worship - to show reverence and adoration for a deity

Etymology

From Old French *preier*, from Latin *precari* (to ask, entreat, beg).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00761989-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ preɪ / (AmE), / preɪ / (BrE)
to ask someone for something earnestly or humbly; to entreat or beg
to ask or beg earnestly

Examples

I pray you, listen to my explanation before deciding.
She prayed him not to leave at midnight.
You must pray their forgiveness if you hope to make amends.

Synset Examples

I beg you to stop!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to beg - to ask someone earnestly or humbly for something
  • Synonym: to implore - to beg someone earnestly or desperately to do something
  • Antonym: demand - to ask for something forcefully, not humbly

Etymology

From Latin *precari* (to beg, entreat) via Old French *preier*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00761314-v
Synset POS: v
