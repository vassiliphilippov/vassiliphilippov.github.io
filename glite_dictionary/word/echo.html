Echo

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɛkəʊ / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, a mountain nymph who was rejected by Narcissus and faded away until only her voice remained.
nymph in Greek mythology; pined away for Narcissus

Examples

The story of Echo and Narcissus is a popular myth explaining the origin of echoes.
In the legend, Echo could only repeat the last words she heard from others.
Poets often reference Echo to symbolize unrequited love and longing.

Etymology

From Greek *Ēkhō* (Ἠχώ), name of the mythological nymph.
