calf

1. NOUN

/ kæf / (AmE), / kɑːf / (BrE)
fine leather made from the skin of a young cow, valued for its softness and smooth finish
fine leather from young cow's skin

Examples

These shoes are made of the finest calf.
He bought a wallet crafted from black calf.
The book was bound in rich brown calf.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calfskin - leather made from the skin of a calf

2. NOUN

/ kæf / (AmE), / kɑːf / (BrE)
the muscular back part of the lower leg below the knee
muscular back part of lower leg

Examples

After running up the hill, my calf muscles were sore for days.
She pulled a muscle in her calf during the soccer match.
The physical therapist massaged his injured calf to reduce the swelling.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: shin - the front part of the lower leg

Etymology

From Old English 'cealf' (calf of leg), origin uncertain but distinct from 'calf' (young cow).

3. NOUN

/ kæf / (AmE), / kɑːf / (BrE)
the young of certain large mammals such as whales, giraffes, elephants, or buffalo
young of large mammals (e.g. whale, elephant)

Examples

The elephant calf stayed close to its mother in the herd.
We saw a giraffe calf learning to walk for the first time.
A blue whale calf can weigh more than two tons at birth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cub - young of certain large carnivorous mammals (e.g. lion, bear, fox)

Etymology

Old English 'cealf' (young cow); also applied to other large mammals by extension.

4. NOUN

/ kæf / (AmE), / kɑːf / (BrE)
a young domestic cow or bull, especially one that is still being fed with its mother's milk
young of domestic cattle

Examples

The farmer checked on the newborn calf early in the morning.
A mother cow licked her calf clean after it was born.
Children at the petting zoo fed milk to the calf.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: cow - an adult female of domestic cattle
  • Degree Higher: bull - an adult male of domestic cattle

Etymology

Old English 'cealf', of Germanic origin; related to Dutch 'kalf' and German 'Kalb'.
