diversionary

1. ADJ

/ dəˈvɝːʒəˌnɛri / (AmE), / dɪˈvɜːʃənəri / (BrE)
Intended to distract attention or mislead, especially as a tactic.
designed to distract or mislead

Examples

The army launched a diversionary attack to draw enemy forces away from the main target.
Her comments were purely diversionary, meant to shift the focus from the real issue.
He created a diversionary event to keep the media occupied.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distracting - causing someone to turn their attention away

Etymology

From diversion + -ary, meaning 'pertaining to diversion'.
