Mah-Jongg

1. NOUN

/ mɑːˈʒɑŋ / (AmE), / mɑːˈʒɒŋ / (BrE)
A Chinese game for four players, played with a set of 144 tiles marked in suits and honors, typically involving skill, strategy, and calculation as well as chance.
Chinese four-player tile game

Examples

We gathered on Saturday evening to play Mah-Jongg with friends.
Her grandmother taught her how to play Mah-Jongg when she was a child.
The club organizes a monthly Mah-Jongg tournament for enthusiasts.

Etymology

From Chinese (Mandarin) májiàng, the name of the game.
