Aureolaria virginica

1. NOUN

/ ɔːriəˌlɛəriə vɜrˈdʒɪnɪkə / (AmE), / ɔːriəʊˌlɛːriə vɜːˈdʒɪnɪkə / (BrE)
A species of North American perennial plant with sparse branches and terminal racemes of bright yellow, foxglove-like flowers; sometimes classified in the genus Gerardia.
a North American yellow-flowered perennial plant

Examples

Aureolaria virginica grows best in partial shade and well-drained soil.
The bright yellow flowers of Aureolaria virginica make it easy to identify in the wild.
Botanists often debate whether Aureolaria virginica should be classified under Gerardia.

Etymology

From modern Latin, combining 'aureolus' (golden) with 'virginica', referring to Virginia or the region's flora.
