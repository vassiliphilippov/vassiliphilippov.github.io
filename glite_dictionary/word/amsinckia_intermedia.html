Amsinckia intermedia

1. NOUN

/ æmˈsɪŋkiə ˌɪntərˈmiːdiə / (AmE), / æmˈsɪŋkiə ˌɪntəˈmiːdiə / (BrE)
An annual plant native to the western United States, producing coiled spikes of yellow-orange flowers; commonly known as common fiddleneck.
a plant with coiled yellow-orange flower spikes

Examples

The hills were covered in Amsinckia intermedia, their vivid orange flowers brightening the landscape.
Hikers often spot Amsinckia intermedia along the trails in springtime.
Botanists collected samples of Amsinckia intermedia to study its distinctive coiled flower spikes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fiddleneck - common name for plants of the genus Amsinckia

Etymology

Genus name from botanist Wilhelm Amsinck; species name from Latin meaning 'intermediate'.
