to deoxygenate

1. VERB

/ diˈɑksɪdʒəˌneɪt / (AmE), / diːˈɒksɪdʒəneɪt / (BrE)
to remove oxygen from (a substance, especially water or blood)
remove oxygen from something

Examples

The machine is designed to deoxygenate the blood before it is stored for transfusion.
Industrial processes sometimes deoxygenate water, affecting aquatic life.
They used nitrogen gas to deoxygenate the solution during the experiment.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to oxygenate - to supply, treat, or infuse with oxygen

Etymology

From Latin prefix de- (removal) + oxygen + -ate (verb-forming suffix).
