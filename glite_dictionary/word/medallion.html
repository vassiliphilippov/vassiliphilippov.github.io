medallion

1. NOUN

/ məˈdæliən / (AmE), / mɪˈdalɪən / (BrE)
a small, round portion of food, especially a boneless cut of meat or fish
small, round portion of food

Examples

The chef served tender medallions of beef in a rich mushroom sauce.
She arranged the salmon medallions neatly on each plate before adding the garnish.
For dinner, we ordered medallions of pork with a side of roasted vegetables.

Synset Examples

medallions of veal

Etymology

From French 'médaillon', diminutive of 'médaillé' (medal), referring to the shape.

3. NOUN

/ məˈdæliən / (AmE), / mɪˈdalɪən / (BrE)
an official emblem or badge granted by authorities to indicate that a taxicab is licensed to operate
official taxi license emblem

Examples

Only cabs with a medallion are legally allowed to pick up passengers in the city.
The price of a taxi medallion soared before ride-sharing services became popular.
He proudly displayed his medallion on the dashboard of his yellow cab.

Etymology

From French médaillon, diminutive of médaille (medal), originally from Italian medaglia.
