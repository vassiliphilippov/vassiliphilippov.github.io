anthology

1. NOUN

/ ænˈθɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / ænˈθɒlədʒi / (BrE)
a published collection of poems, stories, or other pieces of writing, often by various authors
a collection of literary works

Examples

The latest anthology features short stories by emerging writers from around the world.
She contributed a poem to an anthology celebrating contemporary women poets.
Our professor assigned us to read a science fiction anthology for next week's class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collection - a group of things gathered together

Etymology

From Greek anthologia, from anthos (flower) + logia (collection), originally meaning 'a collection of flowers.'
