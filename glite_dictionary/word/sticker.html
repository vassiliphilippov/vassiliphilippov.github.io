sticker

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪkər / (AmE), / ˈstɪkə / (BrE)
a small piece of paper, plastic, or similar material with an adhesive back, used for attaching to surfaces to provide information, decoration, or identification
an adhesive label

Examples

She put a sticker on her laptop to show her favorite band.
Each box was marked with a colored sticker for identification purposes.
Children love collecting stickers to decorate their notebooks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: label - a piece of paper or other material that is attached to something and gives information about it

Etymology

From 'stick' (verb) + '-er' (agentive suffix), indicating something that sticks.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɪkər / (AmE), / ˈstɪkə / (BrE)
a short knife with a pointed blade designed for piercing or stabbing
a short pointed knife

Examples

The hunter carried a sticker for protection as he moved through the dense forest.
In the old days, a sticker was often used in close combat situations.
The museum displayed a collection of antique stickers from the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dagger - a short knife with a pointed and edged blade for stabbing
  • Degree Higher: sword - a weapon with a long metal blade and a hilt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poser - a question that is difficult to answer

4. NOUN

/ ˈstɪkər / (AmE), / ˈstɪkə / (BrE)
a small, sharp-pointed projection, resembling a spike or thorn, found on a plant stem or leaf
sharp-pointed projection on a plant

Examples

Be careful of the sticker on that cactus; it can prick your finger.
The dog's fur was full of stickers after running through the field.
Some weeds in the garden produce stickers that can get stuck in clothing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thorn - a stiff, sharp-pointed projection on a plant
  • Synonym: prickle - a small, sharp outgrowth on a plant stem or leaf
