heft

1. VERB

/ hɛft / (AmE), / hɛft / (BrE)
to lift something in order to estimate or test its weight
estimate weight by lifting

Examples

She hefted the bag in her hands to guess how heavy it was.
He hefted the hammer before starting work to make sure it felt right.
Before buying the melon, she hefted it to judge its ripeness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to weigh - to measure how heavy something is

Etymology

Probably related to Middle English *heften* (to lift), from Old English *heftan* (to lift, raise).

2. NOUN

/ hɛft / (AmE), / hɛft / (BrE)
the quality of being heavy or having great weight; heaviness
heaviness or weight

Examples

The heft of the book surprised me when I picked it up.
She felt the heft of the suitcase before deciding to carry it herself.
The sculpture has a pleasing heft that gives it presence on the table.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weight - the measure of how heavy something is
  • Antonym: lightness - the property of being light in weight

Etymology

From Middle English *heften* (to lift, heave).

3. VERB

/ hɛft / (AmE), / hɛft / (BrE)
to lift or raise something, especially to test its weight
to lift or raise, often to weigh

Examples

She hefted the box to see how heavy it was before trying to carry it.
He hefted the bag onto his shoulder with a grunt.
Before buying the melon, she hefted it in her hands to judge its ripeness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lift - to raise something to a higher position
  • Synonym: hoist - to raise or lift, especially with mechanical help

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *heften* (to lift), related to heft (noun).
