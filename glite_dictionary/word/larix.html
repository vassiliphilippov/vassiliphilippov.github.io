Larix

1. NOUN

/ ˈlær.ɪks / (AmE), / ˈlær.ɪks / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous coniferous trees known as larches, native to cooler regions of the Northern Hemisphere, with needle-like leaves that turn yellow and fall in autumn.
genus of larch trees

Examples

Several species in the Larix genus are valued for their timber and ornamental qualities.
The needles of Larix trees change color and drop every autumn, unlike most other conifers.
Scientists studied the adaptation mechanisms of Larix in cold boreal forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: larch - a type of deciduous coniferous tree of the genus Larix

Etymology

From Latin 'larix', referring to the larch tree.
