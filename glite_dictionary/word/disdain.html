disdain

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈdeɪn / (AmE), / dɪsˈdeɪn / (BrE)
the feeling that someone or something is unworthy of one's consideration or respect; contempt
contempt; lack of respect and strong dislike

Examples

Her obvious disdain for their traditions made her unpopular with the villagers.
The manager spoke with disdain about the competitor's products.
He looked at the messy room with undisguised disdain.

Synset Examples

he was held in contempt
the despite in which outsiders were held is legendary

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contempt - the feeling that a person or thing is beneath consideration or deserving scorn

Etymology

From Old French desdaign, from Latin dedignari (to scorn), from de- (down from) + dignari (to consider worthy).

2. NOUN

/ dɪsˈdeɪn / (AmE), / dɪsˈdeɪn / (BrE)
a communication or expression that shows a lack of respect or contempt for someone or something
expression of contempt or lack of respect

Examples

She spoke to him with barely concealed disdain, making her contempt clear.
His disdain for their ideas was obvious from his dismissive tone.
With a look of disdain, she turned her back on the proposal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contempt - the feeling that someone or something is worthless or beneath consideration
  • Antonym: respect - a feeling of deep admiration for someone or something elicited by their abilities, qualities, or achievements

Etymology

From Old French 'desdaign', from Latin 'dedignari' (to scorn).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06732153-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ dɪsˈdeɪn / (AmE), / dɪsˈdeɪn / (BrE)
to regard or treat with contempt; to consider unworthy of one's notice or respect
to treat with contempt

Examples

Despite his achievements, he seemed to disdain the recognition that others sought.
She would often disdain those who failed to meet her high standards.
The critic disdained the new film, calling it uninspired and predictable.

Synset Examples

He despises the people he has to work for
The professor scorns the students who don't catch on immediately

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scorn - to treat or regard with contempt or derision
  • Synonym: to despise - to feel contempt or a deep repugnance for
  • Antonym: respect - to have a high regard for; to esteem

Etymology

From Old French *desdeignier*, from Latin *dedignari* (to scorn, to disdain).

Source: WordNet

4. VERB

/ dɪsˈdeɪn / (AmE), / dɪsˈdeɪn / (BrE)
to refuse or reject something or someone because they are considered unworthy or beneath respect; to treat with scorn
reject with contempt

Examples

She disdained his offer of help, believing she could manage on her own.
The artist disdains commercialism, insisting on creative freedom above all.
He disdained to respond to the insults hurled at him during the debate.

Synset Examples

She spurned his advances

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scorn - to feel or express contempt or derision for someone or something
  • Antonym: respect - to admire someone or something deeply, as a result of their abilities or qualities

Etymology

From Old French 'desdaigner', based on Latin 'dedignari' (to scorn), from 'de-' (expressing reversal) + 'dignari' (to deem worthy).
