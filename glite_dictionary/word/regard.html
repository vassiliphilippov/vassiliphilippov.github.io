regard

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈɡɑrd / (AmE), / rɪˈɡɑːd / (BrE)
a long, attentive, or fixed look at someone or something
a long, fixed look

Examples

She met his regard with a steady expression, refusing to look away.
He held her in a thoughtful regard before finally speaking.
The teacher's stern regard silenced the noisy students.

Synset Examples

he fixed his paternal gaze on me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gaze - a long steady look
  • Antonym: glance - a quick or brief look
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00880326-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈɡɑrd / (AmE), / rɪˈɡɑːd / (BrE)
a feeling of admiration or respect for someone or something
admiration or respect

Examples

She has great regard for her professor's dedication and hard work.
He spoke of her achievements with genuine regard.
The company is held in high regard by its customers.

Synset Examples

she lost all respect for him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: respect - a feeling of deep admiration for someone or something
  • Synonym: esteem - respect and admiration for a person
  • Antonym: disregard - lack of consideration or respect

Etymology

From Old French *regarder* (to look at), from re- (again) + garder (to watch).
