shave

1. NOUN

/ ʃeɪv / (AmE), / ʃeɪv / (BrE)
an act of removing hair from the face or body using a razor
an act of removing hair with a razor

Examples

After his morning shave, he felt refreshed and ready for the day.
She bought a new razor for a closer shave.
Before the interview, he made sure to get a quick shave.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shaving - the action or process of shaving

Etymology

From Old English 'sceafan' (to scrape, shave), of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ ʃeɪv / (AmE), / ʃeɪv / (BrE)
to pass very close to and lightly touch the surface of something without significant contact
touch lightly or graze while passing

Examples

The cyclist shaved the edge of the curb as he turned the corner.
Her new dress barely shaved the floor as she walked by.
The plane's wing shaved the treetops during its emergency landing.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: graze - to scrape or touch lightly in passing
  • Synonym: brush - to touch lightly in passing
  • Antonym: miss - to fail to make contact, the opposite of touching

/ ʃeɪv / (AmE), / ʃeɪv / (BrE)
to remove hair from the body, especially the face or legs, using a razor or similar tool

Examples

Many athletes shave their legs to reduce drag during competitions.
She usually shaves under her arms as part of her daily routine.

Etymology
