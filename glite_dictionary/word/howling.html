howling

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaʊlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhaʊlɪŋ / (BrE)
a long, loud, emotional cry or sound, especially one expressing pain, amusement, or strong feeling
loud emotional cry or sound

Examples

A howling of anguish echoed through the dark hallways.
Their howling grew louder as the jokes became more outrageous.
The eerie howling from the woods frightened the children.

Synset Examples

he gave a howl of pain
howls of laughter
their howling had no effect

Related Terms

  • Synonym: howl - a long, loud, doleful cry or wail

2. ADJ

/ ˈhaʊlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhaʊlɪŋ / (BrE)
Used to emphasize that something is exceptionally successful or impressive.
exceptionally successful or impressive

Examples

The new product launch was a howling success and exceeded all expectations.
Critics called the play a howling triumph of modern theater.
Their latest album turned out to be a howling hit with young audiences.

Synset Examples
