planchet

1. NOUN

/ ˈplæn.tʃɪt / (AmE), / ˈplæn.tʃɪt / (BrE)
a flat metal disk prepared to be stamped or struck as a coin
coin blank; unstruck metal disk for coins

Examples

The mint worker inspected each planchet before it was stamped with a design.
Collectors sometimes value coins with errors that result from a defective planchet.
Before becoming legal tender, every coin begins as a simple planchet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blank - a flat metal disk to be stamped as a coin

Etymology

From French 'planchet', diminutive of 'planche' meaning 'plank'.
