systematics

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪstəˈmætɪks / (AmE), / ˌsɪstəˈmætɪks / (BrE)
the scientific study of the classification and relationships of living organisms
science of biological classification

Examples

She specializes in systematics and has published several papers on the classification of flowering plants.
Advances in molecular biology have greatly influenced modern systematics.
A deep understanding of systematics helps explain the evolutionary relationships among species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taxonomy - the science of classifying organisms

Etymology

From Greek 'systema' (an organized whole) and the suffix '-ics', denoting fields of study.
