strident

1. ADJ

/ ˈstraɪdənt / (AmE), / ˈstraɪdnt / (BrE)
expressing opinions or demands in a loud, forceful, or determined way, often in a way that is unpleasant or aggressive
forceful and unpleasantly loud or insistent

Examples

The union issued strident demands for better working conditions during the negotiations.
Her strident voice made it difficult for others to express their opinions.
The editorial took a strident tone, criticizing the government's new policy harshly.

Synset Examples

strident demands
shrill criticism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrill - having a loud, high-pitched, and penetrating quality

Etymology

From Latin 'strident-', past participle of 'stridere' (to make a harsh noise).

4. ADJ

/ ˈstraɪ.dənt / (AmE), / ˈstraɪ.dənt / (BrE)
(of certain speech sounds) produced by forcing air through a constricted passage, resulting in a hissing or buzzing quality, as in the sounds of 'f', 's', 'z', or 'th'.
describing hissing or buzzing speech sounds

Examples

In linguistics, the strident consonants include sounds like 's' and 'z'.
The teacher explained how strident fricatives are produced by directing air through a narrow channel.
Languages differ in which strident sounds they use and how frequently they occur.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sibilant - a speech sound characterized by a hissing sound, such as 's' or 'sh'

Etymology

From Latin 'strident-', present participle of 'stridere' meaning 'to make a harsh noise'.
