whimsical

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɪmzɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈwɪmzɪkəl / (BrE)
characterized by or resulting from unpredictable whims, impulses, or caprice rather than necessity or reason; fanciful or unpredictable.
unpredictable or fanciful; subject to whims

Examples

Her whimsical decisions kept the team guessing about what she would do next.
The artist's studio was filled with whimsical sculptures and bizarre creations.
Children are often drawn to whimsical stories full of imagination and surprises.

Synset Examples

a capricious refusal
authoritarian rulers are frequently capricious
the victim of whimsical persecutions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: capricious - given to sudden and unaccountable changes of mood or behavior
  • Synonym: arbitrary - based on random choice or personal whim
  • Antonym: predictable - able to be predicted; not whimsical or capricious

Etymology

From whimsy + -ical; 'whimsy' derives from 'whim', possibly from 'whim-wham'.
