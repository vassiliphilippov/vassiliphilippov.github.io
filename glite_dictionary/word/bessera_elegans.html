Bessera elegans

1. NOUN

/ bəˈsɛrə ɛˈlɛɡənz / (AmE), / bɛˈsɛərə ɛˈlɛɡənz / (BrE)
A half-hardy Mexican herb cultivated for its drooping terminal clusters of showy red and white flowers.
Mexican plant with drooping red-and-white flowers

Examples

Gardeners often plant Bessera elegans for its striking red-and-white blossoms.
The drooping umbels of Bessera elegans make it a favorite in ornamental gardens.
Native to Mexico, Bessera elegans thrives in warm, sunny locations.

Etymology

Genus named after Wilibald Swibert Joseph Gottlieb von Besser, Austrian botanist (1784–1842); 'elegans' from Latin meaning 'elegant, fine'.
