malodorousness

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæˈloʊdərəsnəs / (AmE), / ˌmæˈləʊdərəsnɪs / (BrE)
the characteristic or state of having a strong, unpleasant smell
quality of having a bad smell

Examples

The malodorousness of the garbage made it difficult to breathe in the alley.
Their shoes added an unexpected malodorousness to the otherwise pleasant room.
Complaints about the malodorousness of the chemicals led to stricter regulations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fragrance - pleasing or sweet smell

Etymology

From 'malodorous' (from Latin 'malus' meaning bad and Middle English 'odour' from Latin 'odor') + '-ness'.
