topos

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊ.pɑs / (AmE), / ˈtəʊ.pɒs / (BrE)
A traditional theme, motif, or convention used in literature or rhetoric.
traditional literary motif or theme

Examples

The author employed the topos of the journey in his coming-of-age novel.
Medieval poetry often revisits the topos of unrequited love.
In her essay, she analyzes the use of the exile topos in modern literature.

Synset Examples

James Joyce uses the topos of the Wandering Jew in his Ulysses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motif - A recurring theme, subject, or idea in artistic or literary work
  • Synonym: convention - An established technique or device in literature or art

Etymology

From Greek 'topos' meaning 'place', referring to a common theme or 'place' in literature.
