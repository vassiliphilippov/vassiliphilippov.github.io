exordium

1. NOUN

/ ɪɡˈzɔrdiəm / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɔːdɪəm / (BrE)
the introductory part of a speech or written discourse, especially in classical rhetoric, intended to prepare the audience and introduce the subject
introduction of a speech or discourse

Examples

The speaker devoted his exordium to establishing rapport with the audience.
In her essay, the exordium skillfully outlined the historical context of the debate.
Cicero’s exordium was carefully crafted to engage and persuade his listeners from the outset.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: introduction - the beginning section of a piece of writing or speech

Etymology

From Latin exordium (a beginning), from exordiri (to begin).
