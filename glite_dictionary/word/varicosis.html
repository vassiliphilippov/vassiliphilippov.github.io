varicosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌværɪˈkoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌværɪˈkəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A pathological condition characterized by the presence of varicose veins.
pathological condition with varicose veins
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the patient with varicosis after a careful examination of her legs.
Chronic varicosis can lead to discomfort and swelling over time.
Advances in treatment have made managing varicosis much more effective in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: varicose veins - enlarged, twisted veins, usually appearing in the legs

Etymology

From Latin 'varicosus' (full of dilated veins) and medical suffix '-osis' (condition).
