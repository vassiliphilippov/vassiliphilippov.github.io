Melampsora

1. NOUN

/ mɛˌlæmpˈsɔrə / (AmE), / mɛˌlæmpˈsɔːrə / (BrE)
A genus of rust fungi characterized by sessile one-celled teliospores arranged in a single layer, often found on plants.
genus of rust fungi

Examples

The leaves were infected with Melampsora, causing yellow spots to form along the veins.
Plant pathologists identified the rust as a species of Melampsora affecting the local poplar trees.
Research on Melampsora has provided insights into plant-fungus interactions in forest ecosystems.

Etymology

From Greek 'melas' (black) + 'psora' (itch, scab), referring to the appearance of the spores.

2. NOUN

/ məˌlæmpˈsɔrə / (AmE), / mɪˌlæmpˈsɔːrə / (BrE)
a genus of rust fungi characterized by having sessile, one-celled teliospores arranged in a single layer
genus of rust fungi

Examples

Many species of Melampsora are important plant pathogens affecting crops and trees.
Scientists are studying Melampsora to understand how rust fungi spread and infect their hosts.
The leaves were spotted with the characteristic rust caused by Melampsora species.

Etymology

From Greek 'melas, melanos' (black) + 'psora' (itch, scab), referring to the appearance of the spores.
