Castanopsis

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæstəˈnɑpsɪs / (AmE), / ˌkɑːstəˈnɒpsɪs / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen trees and shrubs found in warm regions, valued for their attractive foliage, native to parts of southeastern United States, eastern Australia, and northern New Zealand.
genus of evergreen trees and shrubs

Examples

The lush leaves of the Castanopsis tree make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens in subtropical regions.
Several species of Castanopsis are important for timber and reforestation projects in Australia.
In botanical studies, Castanopsis is often compared with oaks and chestnuts due to their similar foliage.

Etymology

From Greek 'kastanon' (chestnut) and '-opsis' (appearance), referring to its resemblance to chestnut trees.
