tormenter

1. NOUN

/ tɔːrˈmɛntər / (AmE), / tɔːˈmɛntə / (BrE)
A tall, flat piece of scenery placed at each side of a theater stage to prevent the audience from seeing into the wings.
stage flat hiding the wings

Examples

The tormenter on stage left was painted to blend seamlessly with the backdrop.
Technicians quickly adjusted the tormenters before the next scene change.
Without a tormenter, the actors waiting in the wings could easily be seen by the audience.

2. NOUN

/ tɔrˈmɛntər / (AmE), / tɔːˈmɛntə / (BrE)
a person who deliberately causes pain or distress to another
someone who causes pain or distress

Examples

The playground tormenter made recess miserable for several children.
He finally confronted his longtime tormenter and demanded an apology.
Childhood memories of a cruel tormenter can linger for years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bully - a person who habitually seeks to harm or intimidate those whom they perceive as vulnerable
  • Antonym: victim - a person harmed or tormented by others

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman tormentour, from Old French tormenter, from Latin tormentum (instrument of torture).
