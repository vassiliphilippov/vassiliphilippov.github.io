abulic

1. ADJ

/ əˈbjuːlɪk / (AmE), / əˈbjuːlɪk / (BrE)
Suffering from or characterized by abulia; showing abnormal inability to act or make decisions.
exhibiting abnormal inability to act or decide
medical

Examples

The patient appeared abulic, unable to choose even simple meals for himself.
Her abulic state left her sitting motionless for hours at a time.
Doctors noted that some individuals become abulic after certain types of brain injury.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: decisive - able to make decisions quickly and effectively
  • Synonym: apathetic - showing or feeling no interest, enthusiasm, or concern

Etymology

From abulia (from Greek a- 'without' + boulē 'will').
