mythologisation

1. NOUN

/ mɪˌθɑːlədʒəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / mɪˌθɒlədʒaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of turning a story, event, or person into a myth or representing it in mythical terms
the process of creating or shaping a myth

Examples

The mythologisation of historical figures can often obscure the reality of their lives.
Recent films have contributed to the mythologisation of the space race era.
There is ongoing debate about the mythologisation of national events in textbooks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mythologization - the American English spelling of 'mythologisation'

Etymology

From 'mythology' + '-isation', ultimately from Greek 'mythologia'.
