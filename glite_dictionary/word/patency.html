patency

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪtənsi / (AmE), / ˈpeɪtənsi / (BrE)
The state or quality of being easily observed, recognized, or understood.
the quality of being easy to see or understand

Examples

The patency of her explanation helped everyone grasp the concept quickly.
The patency of the road sign ensured drivers understood the warning without confusion.
In legal documents, patency is valued to prevent misinterpretation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: obscurity - the state of being difficult to understand or see

Etymology

From Latin 'patens', present participle of 'patere' (to be open).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪtənsi / (AmE), / ˈpætənsi / (BrE)
the state of being open or unobstructed, especially in reference to a bodily passage or duct
state of being open (medical)
medical

Examples

The surgeon confirmed the patency of the coronary artery after the procedure.
Maintaining airway patency is crucial during emergency care.
Regular scans monitor the patency of the patient's stents to detect any problems early.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: occlusion - the blockage or closing of a bodily passage or duct

Etymology

From Latin 'patere' meaning 'to lie open'.
