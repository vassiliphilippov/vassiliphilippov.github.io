unfathomable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfæðəməbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈfæðəməbəl / (BrE)
impossible to understand completely; difficult or impossible to explain or comprehend
impossible to understand or explain

Examples

The mystery of the ancient ruins remains unfathomable to researchers even after decades of study.
Her decision to leave everything behind seemed unfathomable to her friends.
There are unfathomable depths in the ocean that no explorer has yet reached.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incomprehensible - impossible to understand
  • Antonym: understandable - able to be understood

Etymology

From un- (not) + fathom (to understand, originally to measure depth) + -able (capable of).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfæð.əm.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈfæð.əm.ə.bəl / (BrE)
too deep to be measured, or impossible to understand completely.
impossible to measure or understand

Examples

The unfathomable depths of the ocean have always inspired awe and mystery.
Her grief was unfathomable, leaving those around her at a loss for words.
Scientists are still trying to understand the unfathomable complexities of the human brain.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fathomable - able to be measured or understood
  • Synonym: incomprehensible - impossible to understand

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'fathom' (to measure depth) + '-able'.

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfæð.ə.mə.bəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈfæð.ə.mə.bəl / (BrE)
too deep to be measured or impossible to understand; resembling an abyss in depth or extent.
too deep or impossible to measure/understand

Examples

The trench was so unfathomable that scientists could not reach its bottom.
She stared into the unfathomable darkness, feeling a shiver run down her spine.
The depth of his sorrow seemed unfathomable to those around him.

Synset Examples

the abyssal depths of the ocean

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immeasurable - too large, extensive, or extreme to be measured

Etymology

From un- (not) + fathomable, from fathom (a measure of depth) + -able.
