robotic

1. ADJ

/ roʊˈbɑtɪk / (AmE), / rəʊˈbɒtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or resembling a robot, especially in being mechanical or automated.
relating to robots; mechanical or automated

Examples

The factory relies on robotic arms to assemble cars quickly and accurately.
Her movements were stiff and robotic, as if she were performing a rehearsed routine.
Many households now use robotic vacuum cleaners to keep their floors clean.

Synset Examples

among our robotic devices is a vacuum cleaner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mechanical - operated by or relating to a machine or machinery
  • Synonym: automated - operated automatically, especially by machines or computers

Etymology

From 'robot' (first used in Czech play 'R.U.R.' by Karel Čapek, 1920) + '-ic' (forming adjectives).

2. ADJ

/ roʊˈbɑtɪk / (AmE), / rəʊˈbɒtɪk / (BrE)
behaving in a mechanical, unfeeling, or automatic way, as if controlled by a machine rather than by thought or emotion
mechanical or unfeeling in manner

Examples

Her robotic responses made it hard to know how she really felt.
The customer service agent's robotic tone frustrated the caller.
He moved through his daily routine in a robotic manner, showing no enthusiasm.

Synset Examples

an automatic ‘thank you’
machinelike efficiency

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mechanical - operating like a machine, lacking emotion or spontaneity
  • Synonym: automatic - done without conscious thought, by reflex
  • Antonym: human - relating to or characteristic of people, especially showing emotion or warmth

Etymology

From robot + -ic, originally from Czech robota (forced labor) via 'robot'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01503678-s
Synset POS: s
