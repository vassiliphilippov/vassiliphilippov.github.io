fruitwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈfruːt.wʊd / (AmE), / ˈfruːt.wʊd / (BrE)
Wood from a fruit-bearing tree, such as apple, cherry, or pear, especially used in making furniture or decorative objects.
wood from a fruit-bearing tree used in crafts

Examples

The cabinet was made of fine fruitwood, giving it a warm, rich appearance.
Many artisans prefer fruitwood for carving delicate patterns into their work.
The restoration involved replacing the damaged panels with matching fruitwood.

Etymology

Formed from 'fruit' + 'wood', directly indicating wood from fruit trees.
