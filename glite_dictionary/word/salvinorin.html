salvinorin

1. NOUN

/ sælˈvɪnərɪn / (AmE), / sælˈvɪnərɪn / (BrE)
a hallucinogenic compound found in the plant Salvia divinorum, known for producing potent psychoactive effects
hallucinogen from Salvia divinorum
medical

Examples

Research into salvinorin has revealed its unique properties among hallucinogenic substances.
The psychoactive effects of salvinorin are distinct from those of LSD or psilocybin.
After ingesting salvinorin, participants reported intense but short-lived alterations in perception.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hallucinogen - a substance that causes hallucinations

Etymology

From the scientific genus name Salvia with the suffix -in, used in chemistry for substances.
