neology

1. NOUN

/ niˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / niˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
a newly coined word, phrase, or expression, or the practice of creating such new terms
newly coined word or expression

Examples

The author's use of neology made the novel feel contemporary and fresh.
In linguistics, the study of neology helps explain how languages evolve over time.
Recent technological advances have fueled a surge in neology as new concepts arise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neologism - a newly coined word or expression

Etymology

From French 'néologie', from Greek neos (new) + -logia (study, discourse).

2. NOUN

/ niˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / niːˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the practice or act of creating new words or expressions
creation of new words

Examples

The rapid technological advances of the 21st century have led to an unprecedented wave of neology.
Some writers are well known for their flair in neology, constantly crafting original expressions.
Academic debates often center on the impact of neology on language evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coinage - the invention of a new word or phrase
  • Synonym: neologism - a newly coined word or expression

Etymology

From French 'néologie' and Late Latin 'neologia' (new word formation), from Greek 'neos' (new) + 'logia' (study).
