borer

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔɹər / (AmE), / ˈbɔːrə / (BrE)
a machine or tool used for drilling holes, especially in rock or earth
drilling tool or machine, especially for rock

Examples

The workers used a powerful borer to tunnel through the limestone.
A new borer was brought in to speed up the mining project.
This type of borer is designed to withstand intense pressure deep underground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drill - a tool or machine used for making holes in hard materials

Etymology

From 'bore' (to make a hole) + '-er' (agent suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɔrɚ / (AmE), / ˈbɔːrə / (BrE)
an insect, larva, or mollusk that bores into wood, often causing damage to trees and timber
wood-boring insect or larva

Examples

The fallen tree was riddled with holes made by a borer.
Farmers often worry about crop losses due to borers infesting their fields.
An infestation of borer larvae can weaken wooden beams in old houses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodworm - a larva that bores into wood, especially in furniture
  • Synonym: termite - a social insect whose colonies feed on wood

Etymology

From 'bore' (to make a hole) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01925666-n
Synset POS: n
