Coelophysis

1. NOUN

/ siːˈlɑːfɪsɪs / (AmE), / siːˈlɒfɪsɪs / (BrE)
a genus of small, slender, carnivorous dinosaurs from the Late Triassic period, known as one of the oldest dinosaurs
Triassic carnivorous dinosaur genus

Examples

Fossils of Coelophysis have been found in large groups, suggesting social behavior or mass death events.
The distinctive hollow bones of Coelophysis made it both lightweight and agile.
Some paleontologists believe Coelophysis may have engaged in cannibalism based on fossil evidence.

Etymology

From Greek 'koilos' (hollow) and 'physis' (form or nature), referring to its hollow bones.

2. NOUN

/ siːˈlɑfɪsɪs / (AmE), / siːˈlɒfɪsɪs / (BrE)
A genus of late Triassic carnivorous saurischian dinosaurs known for their slender bodies and hollow bones.
genus of late Triassic carnivorous dinosaurs

Examples

The fossils of Coelophysis have been discovered in the southwestern United States.
Scientists believe Coelophysis hunted in packs due to fossil evidence of group behavior.
The slender build of Coelophysis made it agile and quick for a dinosaur of its era.

Etymology

From Greek 'koilos' (hollow) + 'physis' (form, nature), referring to the hollow bones.
