crosspiece

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɑsˌpis / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌpiːs / (BrE)
A piece of material, often wood or metal, set or fixed across another as a support or brace.
transverse support or brace

Examples

The carpenter installed a crosspiece to strengthen the frame of the table.
A metal crosspiece runs between the legs of the ladder for added stability.
You must nail the crosspiece tightly if you want the structure to be secure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brace - a support that steadies or strengthens structure

Etymology

Formed from 'cross' + 'piece', indicating a piece placed across something.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrɔsˌpis / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌpiːs / (BrE)
A horizontal beam or bar placed across a structure to provide support or connect other parts.
horizontal beam across a structure

Examples

The carpenter attached a crosspiece to stabilize the wooden frame.
Each window was reinforced with a metal crosspiece for added strength.
He measured the length before cutting the crosspiece to fit across the beams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beam - a long, sturdy piece of timber or metal used to support weight
  • Synonym: brace - a device that clamps things tightly together or holds something in place

Etymology

Formed from 'cross' + 'piece', indicating a piece that crosses.
