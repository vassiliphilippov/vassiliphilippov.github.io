manure

1. VERB

/ məˈnʊr / (AmE), / məˈnjʊə / (BrE)
to spread manure on land in order to fertilize it
to fertilize land with manure

Examples

Farmers often manure their fields in the spring to improve the soil's fertility.
The gardener manured the vegetable patch before planting the seeds.
They spent the afternoon manuring the orchard to ensure a good harvest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fertilize - to add substances to soil to increase its fertility

Etymology

From Old French 'manoeuvre' (manual work), later specialized to fertilization.

2. NOUN

/ məˈnʊr / (AmE), / məˈnjʊə / (BrE)
Animal or plant material, especially animal excreta mixed with litter, used to fertilize soil.
organic fertilizer, especially animal excreta

Examples

Farmers spread manure on their fields each spring to improve the soil.
Composted manure is an excellent source of nutrients for vegetable gardens.
The strong smell of manure was noticeable near the barn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fertilizer - a substance added to soil to enhance plant growth

Etymology

From Middle English, from Anglo-Norman manovrer, from Old French manoeuvrer (to work land).
