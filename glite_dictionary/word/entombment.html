entombment

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtuːm.mənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtuːm.mənt / (BrE)
the act or process of placing a dead body in a tomb or grave, especially as part of a funeral rite
being placed in a tomb or grave

Examples

The entombment of the queen was attended by dignitaries from around the world.
Ancient cultures practiced entombment as a central part of their burial rituals.
The artist painted a scene depicting the entombment of Christ.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burial - the act of placing a dead body in the ground
  • Antonym: cremation - the act of burning a dead body, especially as part of a funeral

Etymology

From French *entomber* (to bury in a tomb), from en- (in) + tombe (tomb).
