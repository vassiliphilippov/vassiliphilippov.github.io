os lunatum

1. NOUN

/ ɑs luˈneɪtəm / (AmE), / ɒs luːˈneɪtəm / (BrE)
one of the eight small bones in the wrist, situated in the center of the proximal row of the carpus
a carpal bone in the wrist
medical

Examples

The os lunatum is often involved in certain wrist disorders.
A fracture of the os lunatum can cause significant pain and limited movement.
On the X-ray, the doctor pointed out the os lunatum among the other carpal bones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lunate bone - the crescent-shaped carpal bone in the wrist

Etymology

From Latin os (bone) + lunatum (crescent-shaped), referring to its shape.
