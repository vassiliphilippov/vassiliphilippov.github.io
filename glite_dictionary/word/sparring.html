sparring

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɑːrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspɑːrɪŋ / (BrE)
The practice of making simulated attacks and defenses with the fists and arms, especially as a form of training for a boxer.
boxing or martial arts practice fighting

Examples

The two fighters engaged in sparring to prepare for their upcoming bout.
She improved her technique considerably through regular sparring sessions at the gym.
During sparring, safety equipment is always required to prevent injuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boxing - the sport of fighting with fists

Etymology

From the verb 'spar,' meaning to engage in mock fighting, of unknown origin, first recorded in the 16th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈspɑrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspɑːrɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of engaging in a heated or competitive argument, often as an attempt to gain some advantage over another
argument or exchange to gain advantage

Examples

The two politicians engaged in sparring throughout the televised debate.
There was plenty of sparring between the lawyers during the trial.
Their meeting quickly devolved into verbal sparring over policy differences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debate - a formal discussion or argument in which opposing arguments are put forward
  • Synonym: disagreement - a situation in which people have different opinions

Etymology

From 'spar' (to box lightly, to argue), originally from Old Norse 'sparra' (to flap, quarrel).
