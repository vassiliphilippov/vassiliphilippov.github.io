haranguer

1. NOUN

/ həˈræŋər / (AmE), / həˈræŋə / (BrE)
a person who delivers a forceful, loud, or angry speech in public
person giving a forceful public speech

Examples

The haranguer paced back and forth, shouting his grievances to the gathered crowd.
As a seasoned haranguer, she could always draw attention with her fiery words.
Reporters noted how the haranguer's passionate speech swayed public opinion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orator - a public speaker, especially a skilled one

Etymology

From harangue (French 'harangue', meaning a noisy speech), plus '-er' denoting an agent noun.
