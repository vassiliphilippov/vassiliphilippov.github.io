Clitoria ternatea

1. NOUN

/ klɪˈtɔriə tərˈneɪtiə / (AmE), / klɪˈtɔːrɪə tɜːˈniːtɪə / (BrE)
A tropical Asian vine with pinnate leaves and vivid blue flowers with yellow centers, also known as butterfly pea.
vine with blue and yellow flowers (butterfly pea)

Examples

The vivid blue flowers of the Clitoria ternatea are often used to make herbal tea.
Gardeners admire Clitoria ternatea for its rapid growth and vibrant blossoms.
Researchers are studying compounds found in Clitoria ternatea for possible health benefits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butterfly pea - Common name for Clitoria ternatea, a vine with blue flowers

Etymology

From the Latin genus name Clitoria, named after its flower shape, and species name ternatea, from Ternate in the Moluccas.
