faggot

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæɡət / (AmE), / ˈfæɡət / (BrE)
A bundle of sticks or branches, especially for use as fuel.
bundle of sticks or branches

Examples

The farmer carried a faggot of wood back to his cottage.
They gathered faggots to start the evening fire.
Collections of faggot were often sold as kindling in rural areas.

Etymology

From Old French *fagot*, perhaps of Germanic origin.

3. VERB

/ ˈfæɡət / (AmE), / ˈfaɡət / (BrE)
(historical, metallurgy) To fasten together rods of iron so they can be heated or welded into a single piece.
fasten iron rods to heat/weld

Examples

The blacksmith faggoted several iron rods before placing them in the forge.
To prepare for welding, the apprentices faggoted the metal strips tightly together.
Traditional ironworkers often faggot bundles of rods to form strong bars.

Etymology

From Middle English *fagot* (a bundle of sticks), likely by extension to iron rods bundled for welding.

4. VERB

/ ˈfæɡət / (AmE), / ˈfæɡət / (BrE)
to ornament or join pieces of fabric together using a decorative openwork stitch known as faggoting
join fabric by decorative stitch

Examples

She carefully faggoted the edges of the dress to create a delicate pattern.
The blouse was expertly faggoted at the seams, giving it an elegant finish.
After learning the technique, he faggoted a scarf to match the handmade blouse.

Synset Examples

He fagotted the blouse for his wife

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faggoting - the process or result of joining fabric with an openwork stitch

Etymology

From the noun 'faggoting', a needlework term, ultimately from Old French 'fagot' (bundle of sticks), referring to the stitch's appearance.
