acquitted

1. ADJ

/ əˈkwɪtɪd / (AmE), / əˈkwɪtɪd / (BrE)
Having been declared not guilty of a specific offense or crime by a court of law.
declared not guilty in court

Examples

After a lengthy trial, the defendant was acquitted of all charges.
Despite strong evidence, the jury acquitted her and she walked free from court.
He always claimed innocence and was finally acquitted last week.

Synset Examples

he stands acquitted on all charges
the jury found him not guilty by reason of insanity

Related Terms

  • Antonym: convicted - having been found guilty of a crime

Etymology

Past participle of 'acquit,' from Old French 'acquiter' (to pay, settle, clear), from Medieval Latin 'acquitare'.
