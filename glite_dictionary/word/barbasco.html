barbasco

1. NOUN

/ bɑːrˈbæskoʊ / (AmE), / bɑːˈbæskəʊ / (BrE)
A West Indian shrub or small tree with leathery, saponaceous leaves and extremely hard wood.
West Indian shrub or small tree

Examples

The wood from the barbasco is valued for making durable tools.
Local communities use barbasco for traditional medicinal purposes.
Botanists are studying the unique properties of barbasco leaves.

Etymology

From Spanish 'barbasco', used for certain poisonous plants in Latin America.
