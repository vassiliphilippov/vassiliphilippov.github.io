amaurotic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæmɔːˈrɑːtɪk / (AmE), / ˌamjʊəˈrɒtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or affected by amaurosis (blindness, typically due to disease of the optic nerve or retina).
relating to amaurosis (blindness)
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with amaurotic blindness after presenting with sudden vision loss.
In rare cases, children may develop amaurotic idiocy, a degenerative condition of the central nervous system.
The doctor noted several amaurotic symptoms during the neurological examination.

Etymology

From Greek amauros (dim, obscure) + -otic (forming adjectives) via Late Latin amauroticus.
