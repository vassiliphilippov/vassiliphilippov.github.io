speleology

1. NOUN

/ ˌspiːliˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌspiːliˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the scientific study and exploration of caves
study and exploration of caves

Examples

My interest in speleology began after I visited a limestone cave on a school trip.
Sarah joined a speleology club to learn more about subterranean environments.
Courses in speleology cover both the geological and biological aspects of caves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caving - recreational activity involving exploring caves

Etymology

From French 'spéléologie', based on Greek 'spēlaion' (cave) and '-logia' (study).
