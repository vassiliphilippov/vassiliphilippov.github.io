elicitation

1. NOUN

/ əˌlɪsɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌlɪsɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of drawing out or provoking a response, information, or a particular behavior from someone
the act of drawing out a response or information

Examples

The research focused on the elicitation of emotional responses from survey participants.
The elicitation of detailed memories required gentle and patient questioning.
In language teaching, teachers use various techniques for the elicitation of vocabulary from students.

Synset Examples

the elicitation of his testimony was not easy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evocation - the act of bringing something into the mind or memory
  • Antonym: suppression - the act of preventing or restraining a response or behavior

Etymology

From Latin elicitare, from elicitus, past participle of elicere (to draw out).
