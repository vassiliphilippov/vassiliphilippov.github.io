Hibiscus moschatus

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈbɪskəs mɒsˈkeɪtəs / (AmE), / hɪˈbɪskəs mɒsˈkɑːtəs / (BrE)
a bushy tropical Asian herb cultivated for its yellow, pink, or scarlet hibiscus-like flowers
Asian herb with hibiscus-like flowers

Examples

The Hibiscus moschatus blooms beautifully in the humid summers of Southeast Asia.
Gardeners often choose Hibiscus moschatus for its vibrant pink and yellow flowers.
In traditional medicine, extracts from Hibiscus moschatus have been used for various remedies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hibiscus - a genus of flowering plants with large, showy flowers

Etymology

From Latin genus name Hibiscus and species name moschatus, meaning 'musky'.
