Proboscidea

1. NOUN

/ proʊˌbɑːsɪˈdiːə / (AmE), / prəˌbɒsɪˈdiːə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Martyniaceae, commonly known as unicorn plants, native to the Americas.
genus of unicorn plants

Examples

The genus Proboscidea includes several species known for their distinctive hooked seed pods.
Botanists studied Proboscidea to learn more about plant adaptations in arid climates.
The seeds of Proboscidea are often called "devil's claws" because of their unique appearance.

Etymology

From Latin 'proboscis' (trunk, snout), referring to the curved seed pods resembling a trunk.

2. NOUN

/ proʊˌbɑːsɪˈdiːə / (AmE), / prəˌbɒsɪˈdiːə / (BrE)
a biological order of large mammals that includes elephants and extinct relatives such as mammoths and mastodons
order of mammals: elephants, mammoths

Examples

Scientists study the evolutionary history of Proboscidea to learn more about extinct species like the woolly mammoth.
The Proboscidea order is characterized by animals with long trunks and tusks.
Fossils belonging to Proboscidea have been discovered on several continents, indicating a wide prehistoric distribution.

Etymology

From modern Latin, from Latin 'proboscis' (elephant's trunk), from Greek 'proboskis'.

3. NOUN

/ prəˌbɑːsɪˈdiːə / (AmE), / prəˌbɒsɪˈdiːə / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants, sometimes called unicorn plants, that includes species with distinctive curved seed pods; sometimes merged with the genus Martynia in certain classifications
a genus of unicorn plants

Examples

The Proboscidea genus includes several species commonly known as devil's claw or unicorn plant.
Botanists sometimes classify certain unicorn plants under the Proboscidea rather than Martynia.
The seed pods of Proboscidea are known for their curious hooked shapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Martynia - a closely related genus sometimes combined with Proboscidea

Etymology

From Latin 'proboscis' (trunk, snout), referring to the shape of the seed pods.

4. NOUN

/ proʊˌbɑːsɪˈdiːə / (AmE), / prəˌbɒsɪˈdiːə / (BrE)
an order of large mammals that includes elephants and extinct relatives such as mammoths, characterized by a long trunk.
order of elephants and mammoths

Examples

The order Proboscidea includes both modern elephants and their extinct relatives, such as mammoths.
Paleontologists have discovered fossils belonging to the Proboscidea in various parts of the world.
Members of Proboscidea are known for their distinctive elongated trunks.

Etymology

From Modern Latin, based on Latin 'proboscis' (trunk or snout), from Greek 'proboskis'.
