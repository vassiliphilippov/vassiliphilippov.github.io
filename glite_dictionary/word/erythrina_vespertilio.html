Erythrina vespertilio

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌrɪθrɪnə vəˌspɜːrˈtɪlioʊ / (AmE), / ɪˌrɪθrɪnə vɛspəˈtɪlɪəʊ / (BrE)
A species of prickly Australian coral tree with soft, spongy wood, native to Australia.
species of Australian coral tree

Examples

The Erythrina vespertilio is often grown as an ornamental tree in Australian gardens.
Indigenous Australians have used the wood of the Erythrina vespertilio for making tools and shields.
During spring, the Erythrina vespertilio produces striking red flowers that attract local wildlife.

Etymology

From Latin: Erythrina (from Greek 'erythros', meaning red, referring to the flower color), vespertilio (Latin for bat, alluding to the shape of the leaves).
