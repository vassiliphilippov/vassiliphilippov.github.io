ironing

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪərnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈaɪənɪŋ / (BrE)
clothes or linens that are to be ironed or have been ironed.
clothes or linens for ironing

Examples

She had a pile of ironing waiting for her after work.
All the ironing is neatly folded and put away.
I need to finish the ironing before the guests arrive.

Synset Examples

there was a basketful of ironing to do

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laundry - clothes or linens that need to be washed or have been washed

Etymology

From the verb "iron" + -ing, denoting the result or product of ironing.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03591044-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈaɪərnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈaɪənɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity of using a heated iron to smooth and remove wrinkles from washed clothes
the activity of pressing clothes with heat

Examples

She spent the afternoon doing the ironing while listening to music.
There is still some ironing left to do before the guests arrive.
He dislikes ironing but can't stand wrinkled shirts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pressing - the act of smoothing clothes with pressure and heat

Etymology

From the verb 'iron' + -ing.

Synset POS: n
