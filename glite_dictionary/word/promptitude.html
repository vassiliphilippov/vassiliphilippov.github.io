promptitude

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɑːmp.təˌtud / (AmE), / ˈprɒmp.tɪˌtjuːd / (BrE)
the quality of acting quickly or without delay
readiness, quickness to act

Examples

His promptitude in responding to urgent emails impressed his boss.
The rescue team's promptitude saved several lives during the disaster.
She replied with admirable promptitude to every inquiry she received.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: promptness - the quality of being done without delay
  • Antonym: tardiness - the quality of being late or delayed

Etymology

From French 'promptitude', from Latin 'promptitudo' (readiness), from 'promptus' (ready).
