Corydalus

1. NOUN

/ kɔːˈrɪdələs / (AmE), / kɒˈrɪdələs / (BrE)
a genus of large aquatic insects in the family Corydalidae, commonly known as dobsonflies.
genus of aquatic insects (dobsonflies)

Examples

The larvae of Corydalus are known as hellgrammites, an important bait for fishing.
Researchers discovered a new species within the genus Corydalus in South America.
Adult Corydalus are notable for their large size and impressive mandibles.

Etymology

From Greek kōrydalos (a lark), used in scientific Latin classification.

2. NOUN

/ kɔɹəˈdeɪləs / (AmE), / kɒɹɪˈdeɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of large, aquatic insects in the family Corydalidae, commonly known as dobsonflies.
genus of aquatic insects (dobsonflies)

Examples

The larvae of Corydalus are often referred to as hellgrammites by anglers.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Corydalus genus in South America.
Adult Corydalus are notable for their large mandibles and impressive wingspans.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'korýdallos' (lark), used in taxonomy.
