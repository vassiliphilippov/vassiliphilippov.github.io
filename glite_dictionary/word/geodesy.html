geodesy

1. NOUN

/ dʒiˈɑːdəsi / (AmE), / dʒiˈɒdəsi / (BrE)
the scientific study of the Earth's shape, size, and the exact positions of geographical points
study of Earth's shape and position

Examples

Geodesy provides the measurements needed for accurate global navigation systems.
Advancements in geodesy have improved our understanding of sea level change.
She chose to specialize in geodesy because of her interest in mapping and Earth sciences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cartography - the science or practice of drawing maps
  • Synonym: topography - the arrangement of the natural and artificial physical features of an area
  • Antonym: astronomy - the science that deals with the universe beyond Earth's atmosphere

Etymology

From Greek geōdaisia (division of the earth): geō- (earth) + daiein (to divide).
