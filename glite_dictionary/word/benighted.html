benighted

1. ADJ

/ bɪˈnaɪtɪd / (AmE), / bɪˈnaɪtɪd / (BrE)
Overtaken by night or darkness while traveling or outdoors.
overtaken by night or darkness

Examples

The benighted hikers lost their way as darkness suddenly fell in the mountains.
As the path became less visible, we realized we were truly benighted in the dense forest.
The benighted travelers hurried along the road, hoping to reach shelter before it was completely dark.

Synset Examples

benighted (or nighted) travelers hurrying toward home

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nighted - overtaken by night; enveloped in darkness

Etymology

From Middle English, past participle of 'benight' meaning to be overtaken by night.

2. ADJ

/ bəˈnaɪtɪd / (AmE), / bɪˈnaɪtɪd / (BrE)
lacking knowledge, enlightenment, or culture; existing in a state of moral or intellectual darkness
lacking enlightenment or knowledge

Examples

The villagers lived in a benighted state, unaware of the advances happening in the world beyond.
For centuries, superstition and fear dominated the benighted era known as the Dark Ages.
The documentary criticized colonial attitudes toward what was perceived as benighted cultures.

Synset Examples

this benighted country
benighted ages of barbarism and superstition
the dark ages
a dark age in the history of education

Related Terms

  • Antonym: enlightened - having or showing a rational, modern, and well-informed outlook
  • Synonym: ignorant - lacking knowledge or awareness in general

Etymology

From Middle English, past participle of 'benight' meaning 'to overtake by darkness'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00887743-s
Synset POS: s
