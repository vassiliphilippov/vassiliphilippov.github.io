Adventism

1. NOUN

/ ˈæd.vənˌtɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈæd.vən.tɪz.əm / (BrE)
A branch of Christianity that believes in the imminent second coming of Christ, especially as taught by various denominations such as the Seventh-day Adventists.
Christian belief in Christ's imminent return

Examples

The growth of Adventism in the nineteenth century led to the formation of several new denominations.
Many people are attracted to Adventism because of its emphasis on the imminent return of Christ.
Historical studies of Adventism often focus on the origins of the Seventh-day Adventist Church.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: millenarianism - belief in a coming transformation of society, especially the second coming of Christ

Etymology

From Latin 'adventus' (coming) + '-ism', referring to the anticipated coming of Christ.
