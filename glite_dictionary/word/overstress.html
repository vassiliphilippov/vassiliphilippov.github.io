to overstress

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvɚˈstrɛs / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈstrɛs / (BrE)
to place special or excessive emphasis on something
place excessive emphasis on

Examples

The report tends to overstress minor details at the expense of the main issue.
It is possible to overstress the benefits of technology without considering its drawbacks.
Some teachers overstress the importance of test scores in education.

Synset Examples

I cannot overemphasize the importance of this book

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overemphasize - to place too much emphasis on something
  • Degree Lower: stress - to lay emphasis on

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' (too much) + 'stress' (emphasize).
