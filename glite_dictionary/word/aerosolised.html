aerosolised

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛr.oʊ.səˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈɛː.rə.səˌlaɪzd / (BrE)
(of a substance) converted into ultramicroscopic solid or liquid particles dispersed or suspended in air or gas.
converted to fine airborne particles
medical

Examples

Many medications can be aerosolised to allow patients to inhale them directly into their lungs.
A virus may be aerosolised during certain medical procedures, increasing the risk of transmission.
The aerosolised particles remained suspended in the laboratory air for several hours.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aerosolized - American spelling of 'aerosolised'

Etymology

From 'aerosol' + '-ised', based on Latin 'aer' (air) and solutum (dissolved).
