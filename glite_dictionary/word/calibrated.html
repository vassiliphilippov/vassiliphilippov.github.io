calibrated

1. ADJ

/ ˈkæləˌbreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈkælɪbreɪtɪd / (BrE)
marked or adjusted so that measurements or readings are accurate, often by being divided into degrees or units.
marked or adjusted for accurate measurement

Examples

We need a calibrated scale to ensure all the measurements are precise.
The lab always uses calibrated instruments for their experiments.
Only a calibrated thermometer can give us an accurate temperature reading.

Synset Examples

a calibrated thermometer

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uncalibrated - not calibrated; not adjusted or marked for accuracy

Etymology

From the verb 'calibrate,' from French 'calibrer', probably from 'calibre' (caliber, gauge).
