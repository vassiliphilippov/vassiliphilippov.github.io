nosh

1. NOUN

/ nɑʃ / (AmE), / nɒʃ / (BrE)
A small snack or light meal, often eaten between main meals.
snack or light meal

Examples

I grabbed a nosh from the kitchen before heading out.
We enjoyed a quick nosh during the break.
A little nosh in the afternoon keeps me going until dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snack - a small amount of food eaten between meals

Etymology

From Yiddish *nashn* (to nibble or snack), related to German *naschen* (to nibble).

2. VERB

/ nɑʃ / (AmE), / nɒʃ / (BrE)
to eat a snack or eat lightly, especially between meals
eat a snack; eat lightly
informal

Examples

She likes to nosh on fruit while watching television in the evening.
We stopped at a café to nosh before the show started.
He tends to nosh throughout the day instead of having big meals.

Synset Examples

She never loses weight because she snacks between meals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snack - to eat a small amount of food between meals
  • Antonym: feast - to eat a large or elaborate meal

Etymology

From Yiddish *nashn* (to nibble), related to German *naschen*.
