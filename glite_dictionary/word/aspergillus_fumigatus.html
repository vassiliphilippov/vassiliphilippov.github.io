Aspergillus fumigatus

1. NOUN

/ ˌæspərˈdʒɪləs ˌfjuːmɪˈɡeɪtəs / (AmE), / ˌaspəˈdʒɪləs ˌfjuːmɪˈɡɑːtəs / (BrE)
a species of mold that can cause aspergillosis, especially in birds and humans
species of mold causing aspergillosis
medical

Examples

Aspergillus fumigatus is commonly found in decaying organic matter, such as compost heaps.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with an infection caused by Aspergillus fumigatus.
Wild birds are especially susceptible to respiratory diseases caused by Aspergillus fumigatus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aspergillosis - an infection or allergy caused by fungi of the genus Aspergillus

Etymology

From Latin 'aspergillus', a genus name, and 'fumigatus', meaning 'smoky' or 'fumigated'.
