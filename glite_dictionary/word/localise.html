to localise

1. VERB

/ ˈloʊkəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈləʊkəlaɪz / (BrE)
to restrict or confine something to a particular area or place
restrict to a particular area
british

Examples

Doctors were able to localise the infection to a single organ.
Security measures were introduced to localise the disturbance and prevent it from spreading.
The company hopes to localise production in order to better serve regional markets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confine - to keep or restrict someone or something within certain limits
  • Antonym: spread - to extend over a larger area or range

Etymology

From Latin 'localis' (pertaining to a place) + '-ize'.

2. VERB

/ ˈloʊkəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈləʊkəlaɪz / (BrE)
to identify the specific location or place of something
identify the location of something
british

Examples

The doctors managed to localise the infection to one area of the lung.
Engineers worked all night to localise the fault in the network.
It often takes time to localise the origin of persistent noise in old buildings.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to identify - to recognize or determine the particular person or thing
  • Synonym: to locate - to discover or mark the position of something
  • Antonym: to misplace - to put in the wrong place or lose temporarily

Etymology

From French 'localiser', based on 'local' from Latin 'localis'.
