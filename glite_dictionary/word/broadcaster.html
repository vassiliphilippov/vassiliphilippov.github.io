broadcaster

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɔːdˌkæstər / (AmE), / ˈbrɔːdˌkɑːstə / (BrE)
a person who speaks or presents on radio or television
radio or television presenter

Examples

The famous broadcaster interviewed politicians from around the world.
Many young people dream of becoming a television broadcaster.
As a radio broadcaster, she has a distinctive and engaging voice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: announcer - a person who introduces or gives information on radio or TV
  • Synonym: reporter - a person who reports news or conducts interviews on television or radio
  • Antonym: listener - someone who listens to radio or watches TV, not producing content

Etymology

From 'broadcast' (to transmit), with agentive suffix '-er'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbrɔdˌkæstər / (AmE), / ˈbrɔːdˌkɑːstə / (BrE)
a mechanical device for scattering seeds, fertilizer, sand, or similar materials evenly over a wide area
device for scattering material

Examples

The farmer used a broadcaster to distribute fertilizer evenly across the field.
After filling the broadcaster with grass seed, she walked slowly to cover the entire lawn.
A handheld broadcaster is ideal for spreading salt on icy sidewalks during winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spreader - a device used for spreading things such as fertilizer or seeds

Etymology

From 'broadcast' (to scatter widely), referring to the scattering of seeds, plus the agent suffix '-er'.
