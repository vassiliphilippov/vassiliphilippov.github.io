French

1. NOUN

/ frɛntʃ / (AmE), / frɛntʃ / (BrE)
the people of France collectively; the citizens or natives of France
the people of France

Examples

The French are renowned for their cuisine and culture.
During the World Cup, many French traveled to support their team.
The French have made significant contributions to art and philosophy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Frenchman - a man from France
  • Synonym: Frenchwoman - a woman from France

Etymology

From Old French 'franc', from Medieval Latin 'Franciscus', relating to the Franks.

3. NOUN

/ frɛntʃ / (AmE), / frɛntʃ / (BrE)
the Romance language spoken in France and in many countries around the world, especially those formerly colonized by France
the French language

Examples

Many students at our school study French as a second language.
She speaks French fluently after living in Paris for a year.
The film was originally made in French and later dubbed into English.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: English - the West Germanic language spoken in England and many countries worldwide
  • Synonym: Spanish - the Romance language spoken in Spain and many American countries

Etymology

From Old French *franceis*, from Latin *Franciscus* (Frankish).
