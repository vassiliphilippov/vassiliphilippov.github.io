dinar

1. NOUN

/ diːˈnɑr / (AmE), / dɪˈnɑː / (BrE)
the basic unit of currency in Algeria, equal to 100 centimes
Algerian currency unit

Examples

The price was listed as 500 dinar for a cup of coffee in Algeria.
She exchanged her euros for Algerian dinar at the airport.
Inflation caused the value of the dinar to fluctuate throughout the year.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: centime - a subunit of currency in various countries, including Algeria

Etymology

From Arabic 'dīnār', derived from Latin 'denarius', an ancient Roman coin.
