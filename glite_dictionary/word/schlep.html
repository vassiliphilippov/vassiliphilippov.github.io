schlep

1. NOUN

/ ʃlɛp / (AmE), / ʃlɛp / (BrE)
A tedious or difficult journey, especially one that requires considerable effort.
tedious or difficult journey
informal

Examples

After missing the bus, the two-mile walk home was quite a schlep.
It was a real schlep to get all the way to their house in the snow.
No one wanted to make the schlep across town for a ten-minute meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trek - a long and difficult journey, especially on foot
  • Antonym: jaunt - a short journey for pleasure

Etymology

From Yiddish *shlepn* (to drag, haul).

2. VERB

/ ʃlɛp / (AmE), / ʃlɛp / (BrE)
to carry or drag something with effort, especially something heavy or awkward
carry or drag with effort
informal

Examples

I had to schlep all my groceries home from the store.
She didn't want to schlep her suitcase up the long flight of stairs.
After the picnic, we schlepped our chairs and coolers back to the car.

Synset Examples

Can you shlep this bag of potatoes upstairs?
She pulled along a large trunk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lug - to carry or pull something heavy with effort
  • Synonym: drag - to pull something along the ground with effort

Etymology

From Yiddish *shlepn* (to drag, pull), from Middle High German *schleppen* (to drag).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01456329-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ʃlɛp / (AmE), / ʃlɛp / (BrE)
An awkward, clumsy, or inept person, often regarded as foolish or lacking in intelligence (informal).
awkward or foolish person (informal)
informal

Examples

I can't believe I let that schlep fix my sink; now it's leaking worse than before.
He always acts like a total schlep at parties, spilling drinks everywhere.
Don't be such a schlep—pay attention to where you're going!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doofus - a foolish or stupid person
  • Synonym: klutz - a clumsy person

Etymology

From Yiddish *shlep* (to drag, pull), extended in English to mean a person who moves or acts clumsily.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
