tone-deaf

1. ADJ

/ ˈtoʊnˌdɛf / (AmE), / ˈtəʊnˌdɛf / (BrE)
Unable to perceive or reproduce differences in musical pitch accurately; unable to appreciate music.
unable to distinguish musical pitch

Examples

Although he loves singing, Jake is completely tone-deaf and can't carry a tune.
Some children are naturally talented at music, while others seem almost tone-deaf.
Even if you're tone-deaf, you can still enjoy listening to music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tuneless - lacking musical ability or unable to carry a tune

2. ADJ

/ ˈtoʊnˌdɛf / (AmE), / ˈtəʊnˌdɛf / (BrE)
Insensitive to or unaware of emotional nuances or social cues, often resulting in inappropriate or unperceptive responses.
insensitive to social or emotional cues

Examples

His tone-deaf remarks during the meeting upset several colleagues.
The company's tone-deaf advertisement was widely criticized for ignoring public sentiment.
She is sometimes tone-deaf to the mood of the room, making jokes at the wrong moment.

Synset Examples

Her tone-deaf approach to the issue frustrated everyone.
His tone-deaf comments offended many people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insensitive - lacking sensitivity to others' feelings or situations
  • Antonym: tactful - showing sensitivity and consideration in dealing with others

Etymology

Metaphorical extension from the original musical sense, in which someone is unable to distinguish musical pitches.
