hike

1. NOUN

/ haɪk / (AmE), / haɪk / (BrE)
a long walk, especially in the countryside, taken for exercise or pleasure
long walk for exercise or pleasure

Examples

We planned a hike in the mountains this weekend.
The group paused for lunch during their five-mile hike.
A sudden rainstorm forced us to end our hike early.

Synset Examples

she enjoys a hike in her spare time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walk - an act of travelling on foot, especially for recreation
  • Degree Lower: stroll - a slow, relaxed walk, especially for pleasure
  • Degree Higher: trek - a long and often difficult journey on foot

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *hiken* (to move vigorously), of uncertain origin.

2. VERB

/ haɪk / (AmE), / haɪk / (BrE)
to take a long walk, especially in the countryside or wilderness, for pleasure or physical exercise
walk for pleasure or exercise

Examples

We decided to hike up the mountain trail early in the morning.
They spent the afternoon hiking through the national park.
Last summer, she hiked across several states on the Appalachian Trail.

Synset Examples

We were hiking in Colorado
hike the Rockies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trek - to make a long or arduous journey, especially by foot
  • Synonym: ramble - to walk for pleasure in the countryside

Etymology

Perhaps a variant of earlier 'hitch' (to move, jerk), origin uncertain.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ haɪk / (AmE), / haɪk / (BrE)
a sudden or significant increase in the cost, amount, or level of something, especially prices, rates, or wages
sudden or large increase (especially in prices)

Examples

The government announced a 5% hike in fuel prices last month.
A sudden hike in tuition fees led to student protests across the country.
There was an unexpected hike in electricity bills this winter.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: increase - an instance of becoming greater in size, amount, or degree
  • Antonym: cut - a reduction or decrease, the opposite of an increase
