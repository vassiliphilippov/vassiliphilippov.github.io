handless

1. ADJ

/ ˈhænd.ləs / (AmE), / ˈhænd.ləs / (BrE)
lacking a hand or hands; having no hand(s)
without a hand or hands

Examples

The handless clock sat silently on the mantel.
After the accident, he became handless and had to relearn daily tasks.
The sculptor created a handless statue to symbolize loss.

Synset Examples

a handless war veteran

Etymology

Formed from 'hand' + suffix '-less', meaning 'without'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02035641-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈhænd.ləs / (AmE), / ˈhænd.ləs / (BrE)
Lacking physical skill or coordination, especially with the hands; clumsy or inept.
lacking manual skill or coordination

Examples

Despite years of practice, he remained handless when it came to pottery.
Her handless attempt to fix the clock only made things worse.
As a cook, John was completely handless, unable to even boil water properly.

Synset Examples

a bumbling mechanic
a bungling performance
ham-handed governmental interference
could scarcely empty a scuttle of ashes, so handless was the poor creature

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clumsy - awkward in movement or handling
  • Antonym: dexterous - skilful with the hands

Etymology

Formed from 'hand' + '-less' meaning 'without.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00063822-s
Synset POS: s
