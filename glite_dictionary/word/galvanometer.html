galvanometer

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡælvəˈnɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / ˌɡælvəˈnɒmɪtə / (BrE)
an instrument used to detect, measure, or compare small electric currents
instrument for measuring small electric currents

Examples

The scientist used a galvanometer to detect the faint current in the wire.
Careful calibration of the galvanometer ensures accurate measurements in the experiment.
Older laboratory equipment often includes an analog galvanometer for checking circuits.

Etymology

From French 'galvanomètre', after Luigi Galvani, Italian scientist.
