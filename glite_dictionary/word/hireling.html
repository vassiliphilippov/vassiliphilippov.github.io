hireling

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪərlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪəlɪŋ / (BrE)
a person who works solely for money, often implying lack of loyalty or personal commitment
a person paid to do work without personal loyalty

Examples

The politician was accused of serving the interests of wealthy hirelings rather than the public.
During the strike, management brought in hirelings to keep production running.
He dismissed her as a mere hireling who cared nothing for the company's values.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mercenary - a person who works or acts for money rather than principle

Etymology

From Old English hyr(e)ling (hired servant), from hyran (to hire) + -ling (diminutive or agent suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪɚlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪəlɪŋ / (BrE)
a person who works purely for pay, especially one who lacks integrity or devotion
person working only for pay, lacking integrity

Examples

The politician's hirelings spread rumors about his opponent to gain favor.
He was dismissed as a hireling who cared little for the truth.
Some view corporate lobbyists as mere hirelings with no principles.

Synset Examples

The journalist was dismissed as a hireling for his bias.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mercenary - a person primarily concerned with making money at the expense of ethics

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old English *hýrian* (to hire) + *-ling* (a diminutive or person suffix).
