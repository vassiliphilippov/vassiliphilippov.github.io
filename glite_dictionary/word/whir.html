whir

1. NOUN

/ wɝː / (AmE), / wɜː / (BrE)
a continuous soft, rapid sound made by something moving quickly or rotating
soft continuous sound of rapid motion

Examples

The only sound in the room was the gentle whir of the ceiling fan.
A sudden whir erupted as the old computer started up.
He listened to the whir of insect wings outside the window.

Synset Examples

whir of a bird's wings
the whir of the propellers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whirr - variant spelling of 'whir', same meaning
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07414517-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ wɜr / (AmE), / wɜː / (BrE)
(of an object or machine) to make a continuous, soft, swishing or buzzing sound, especially one that is rapid or low in pitch.
make a soft, continuous swishing sound

Examples

The old fan began to whir quietly as she turned it on.
You could hear the sewing machine whir from the next room.
Helicopter blades whir overhead as they prepare to land.

Synset Examples

the motor whirred
the car engine purred

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buzz - to make a continuous humming sound
  • Synonym: hum - to make a low, steady continuous sound

Etymology

Imitative of the sound made by something spinning or rapidly moving.
