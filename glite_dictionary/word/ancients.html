ancients

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪnʃənts / (AmE), / ˈeɪnʃənts / (BrE)
people who lived in ancient times, especially before the fall of the Roman Empire in western Europe.
people of ancient times

Examples

Historians often refer to the ancients when discussing early scientific discoveries.
Many philosophies developed by the ancients still influence modern thought.
The ancients built impressive monuments that have survived for thousands of years.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: moderns - people living in modern times
