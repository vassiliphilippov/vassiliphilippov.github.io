consistory

1. NOUN

/ kənˈsɪstəri / (AmE), / kənˈsɪstəri / (BrE)
an official council or tribunal, especially within certain Christian churches, that acts as a governing body or court
church tribunal or governing council

Examples

The bishop convened a consistory to address matters of church discipline.
The decisions made by the consistory are binding for the entire parish.
A new member was elected to serve on the consistory of the reformed church.

Etymology

From Latin consistorium (assembly, council), from consisto (to stand together)
