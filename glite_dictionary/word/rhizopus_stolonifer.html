Rhizopus stolonifer

1. NOUN

/ raɪˈzoʊpəs stoʊˈlɑnɪfər / (AmE), / rʌɪˈzəʊpəs stɒˈlɒnɪfə / (BrE)
a species of fungus that causes soft, watery rot in fruits and vegetables, and dry rot in the roots of sweet potatoes
fungus causing rot in produce
medical

Examples

Fruits left in damp conditions are often ruined by Rhizopus stolonifer infections.
Rhizopus stolonifer is commonly called black bread mold and frequently spoils stored produce.
Sweet potatoes can develop dry rot due to infection by Rhizopus stolonifer.

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Rhizopus' from Greek 'rhiza' (root) + 'pous' (foot); species name 'stolonifer' means 'bearing stolons' in Latin.
