condign

1. ADJ

/ kənˈdaɪn / (AmE), / kənˈdaɪn / (BrE)
(of punishment or retribution) fitting, appropriate, and deserved; suitable to the crime or wrongdoing.
fitting and deserved, esp. of punishment

Examples

The judge imposed a condign punishment on the defendant for his reckless actions.
Her dismissal was considered a condign consequence of years of negligence.
A condign sentence restored public faith in the justice system.

Synset Examples

condign censure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appropriate - suitable or proper in the circumstances
  • Synonym: just - morally right or fair, especially of punishment
  • Antonym: undue - not appropriate or justified

Etymology

From Latin 'condignus' (highly worthy), from 'con-' (altogether) + 'dignus' (worthy).
