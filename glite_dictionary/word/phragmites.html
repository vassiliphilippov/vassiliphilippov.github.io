Phragmites

1. NOUN

/ fræɡˈmaɪtiːz / (AmE), / fræɡˈmaɪtiːz / (BrE)
A genus of tall perennial grasses (reeds) commonly found in marshes and along riversides, especially in tropical and temperate regions.
genus of tall marsh reeds

Examples

The wetlands are dominated by dense stands of Phragmites that provide habitat for birds and fish.
Ecologists are concerned about the rapid spread of Phragmites in North American marshes.
We saw tall Phragmites swaying in the breeze along the riverbank.

Etymology

From Greek *phragmitēs* (a kind of reed), from *phragma* (fence, hedge).

2. NOUN

/ fræɡˈmaɪtiːz / (AmE), / fræɡˈmaɪtiːz / (BrE)
A genus of tall perennial grasses commonly known as reeds, found in marshes and along rivers in tropical and temperate regions.
genus of tall marsh grasses (reeds)

Examples

Phragmites often dominate wetlands and can rapidly spread in favorable conditions.
The invasive nature of Phragmites is a concern for many conservationists.
Large stands of Phragmites provide habitat for various bird species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reed - tall, slender-leaved plant of wetlands

Etymology

From Ancient Greek *phragmites* (growing in hedges), from *phragma* (fence, hedge).
