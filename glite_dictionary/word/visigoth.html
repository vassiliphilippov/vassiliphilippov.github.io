Visigoth

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪzɪˌɡɑθ / (AmE), / ˈvɪzɪˌɡɒθ / (BrE)
a member of the western branch of the Goths who invaded Rome and established a kingdom in what is now Spain and southern France during the early Middle Ages
member of a western Gothic people (history)

Examples

The Visigoth invasion marked a turning point in the decline of the Western Roman Empire.
Many artifacts found in the region once ruled by the Visigoths reveal their craftsmanship and culture.
A Visigoth leader, Alaric I, is famous for leading the sacking of Rome in 410 AD.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Ostrogoth - a member of the eastern branch of the Goths
  • Synonym: Goth - a member of an ancient Germanic people

Etymology

From Latin 'Visigothi', meaning 'western Goths', from 'visi' (west) and 'Gothi' (Goths).
