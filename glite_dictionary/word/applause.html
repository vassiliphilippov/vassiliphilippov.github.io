applause

1. NOUN

/ əˈplɔz / (AmE), / əˈplɔːz / (BrE)
approval or enjoyment expressed by the clapping of hands, usually after a performance or speech
clapping to show approval or enjoyment

Examples

The audience erupted in applause after the final note was played.
She received a round of applause for her moving speech.
As the curtain fell, thunderous applause filled the theater.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acclaim - public praise or applause for someone or something
  • Antonym: boos - shouts or noises expressing disapproval, the opposite of applause

Etymology

From Latin applausus, past participle of applaudere (to clap, to praise).
