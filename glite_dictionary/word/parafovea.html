parafovea

1. NOUN

/ ˌpær.əˈfoʊ.vi.ə / (AmE), / ˌpar.əˈfəʊ.vi.ə / (BrE)
the area of the retina immediately surrounding the fovea, involved in high-acuity vision
area surrounding the fovea in the retina
medical

Examples

The parafovea contains a high density of photoreceptor cells involved in detailed vision.
Damage to the parafovea can significantly affect one's ability to read small print.
Researchers studied the thickness of the parafovea in patients with retinal diseases.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: fovea - small depression in the retina where visual acuity is highest
  • Degree Lower: perifovea - region of the retina surrounding the parafovea

Etymology

From Greek 'para' (beside) + 'fovea' (pit, depression).
