sunshine

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌnˌʃaɪn / (AmE), / ˈsʌnʃaɪn / (BrE)
pleasant weather marked by moderate temperatures and clear, sunny skies, suitable for outdoor activities
pleasant, sunny weather

Examples

The picnic was a great success thanks to the beautiful sunshine.
We decided to go hiking because the sunshine made the weather perfect.
After a week of rain, the return of sunshine lifted everyone's spirits.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rain - precipitation; wet weather, often considered opposite to sunshine
  • Antonym: cloudiness - state of being cloudy, lacking sunshine

Etymology

From Old English sunne (sun) + scīn (shine, light).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsʌnˌʃaɪn / (AmE), / ˈsʌnʃʌɪn / (BrE)
a cheerful quality that dispels gloom or lifts spirits
cheerful quality; uplifting spirit

Examples

Her sunshine brightened the whole office on a gloomy Monday morning.
He brought a touch of sunshine to our lives with his constant jokes and smiles.
Their laughter was pure sunshine in the midst of a difficult day.

Synset Examples

flowers added a note of cheerfulness to the drab room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheerfulness - the state of being noticeably happy and optimistic

3. NOUN

/ ˈsʌnˌʃaɪn / (AmE), / ˈsʌnʃaɪn / (BrE)
the light and heat that come from the sun
rays of the sun; sunlight

Examples

After days of rain, the sunshine made everyone feel cheerful again.
The garden needs plenty of sunshine to thrive.
We sat outside, enjoying the warm sunshine on our faces.

Synset Examples

the shingles were weathered by the sun and wind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sunlight - the light that comes from the sun
  • Antonym: shade - an area where direct sunlight is blocked from reaching

Etymology

From Middle English 'sunshyne', from 'sun' + 'shine'.
