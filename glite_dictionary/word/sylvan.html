sylvan

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvən / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvən / (BrE)
A spirit or deity that inhabits or frequents the woods, especially in mythology or folklore.
woodland spirit or deity

Examples

Legends spoke of a sylvan who guarded the ancient grove from intruders.
The poet imagined a sylvan watching silently amid the mossy trees.
In the story, a sylvan lured travelers deeper into the enchanted forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dryad - a tree or forest nymph in Greek mythology
  • Synonym: nymph - a mythological spirit of nature imagined as a beautiful maiden

Etymology

From Latin 'sylvanus', from 'silva' (forest, wood).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɪlvən / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvən / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of woods or forest regions; wooded or rural.
relating to wooded or forest areas

Examples

The couple enjoyed a picnic in the sylvan setting by the lake.
The sylvan pathways are especially beautiful in autumn when the leaves change color.
Poets often describe magical creatures living in sylvan landscapes.

Synset Examples

a shady sylvan glade

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wooded - covered with trees or woods
  • Synonym: rural - relating to the countryside rather than the town

Etymology

From Latin 'silvanus', from 'silva' (forest, wood).
