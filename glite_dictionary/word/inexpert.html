inexpert

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈekspɚt / (AmE), / ɪnˈekspɜːt / (BrE)
lacking skill or expertise in a particular area or activity.
not skilled or experienced

Examples

Her inexpert handling of the tools caused several minor accidents.
The report was clearly written by an inexpert author unfamiliar with the topic.
Our team was inexpert, but we learned quickly through practice.

Synset Examples

a very amateurish job
inexpert but conscientious efforts
an unskilled painting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unskilled - lacking skill or training
  • Synonym: amateurish - not done with professional skill, lacking polish
  • Antonym: expert - having a high level of skill or knowledge

Etymology

From Latin inexpertus (untried, inexperienced), from in- (not) + expertus (experienced, tried).
