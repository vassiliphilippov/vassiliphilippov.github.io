overspill

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvərˌspɪl / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəˌspɪl / (BrE)
the situation in which liquid, especially water, goes beyond the limits of a container or place because there is too much of it
excess liquid exceeding a boundary

Examples

The heavy rain caused an overspill from the river onto the nearby fields.
Engineers installed barriers to control the overspill from the reservoir during storms.
A sudden overspill of water flooded the basement after the tank was filled too quickly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overflow - the flow of liquid over the limits of a container

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvərˌspɪl / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəˌspɪl / (BrE)
the relocation of people from overcrowded cities to new housing in smaller towns, typically as part of an organized plan to reduce urban congestion
relocation of people from overcrowded cities

Examples

The city faced a significant overspill of residents after World War II, prompting the development of new towns.
Government policy encouraged the overspill population to settle in suburban areas.
Housing projects were built to accommodate the overspill from the capital's crowded neighborhoods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: urban sprawl - spread of a city into surrounding areas

Etymology

From over- (excess) + spill (to flow over), referring to the overflow of population.
