unreformable

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnrɪˈfɔrməbəl / (AmE), / ˌʌnrɪˈfɔːməbəl / (BrE)
incapable of being reformed or improved in behavior or character
incapable of being reformed

Examples

Despite years of counseling, he remained unreformable in his criminal ways.
The judge called her an unreformable offender with no hope for rehabilitation.
Some argue that certain beliefs are unreformable, no matter how much evidence is presented.

Synset Examples

an unregenerate criminal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incorrigible - (of a person or their behavior) not able to be corrected, improved, or reformed

Etymology

From un- (not) + reformable (capable of being reformed).

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnrɪˈfɔrməbəl / (AmE), / ˌʌnrɪˈfɔːməbəl / (BrE)
incapable of being reformed or improved in conduct, character, or structure
incapable of being reformed

Examples

The judge saw him as an unreformable criminal with no hope of rehabilitation.
Some believe that certain systems are simply unreformable and must be replaced entirely.
The organization concluded the policy was unreformable and recommended a complete overhaul.

Synset Examples

vicious irreclaimable boys
irredeemable sinners

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irredeemable - cannot be corrected or saved
  • Antonym: reformable - capable of being reformed

Etymology

From un- (not) + reformable (able to be reformed).
