expectable

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈspɛktəbəl / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɛktəbl̩ / (BrE)
able to be expected; likely to happen or occur.
able to be expected

Examples

Given his reputation, his victory was entirely expectable.
Disagreements in such a complex project are expectable.
Some delays are expectable during periods of bad weather.

Synset Examples

differences of opinion are quite expectable given the present information

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expected - regarded as likely to happen; anticipated
  • Antonym: unexpected - not regarded as likely to happen; surprising

Etymology

From the verb 'expect' + suffix '-able', from Latin 'exspectare' (to look out for, await).
