lengthily

1. ADV

/ ˈlɛŋθɪli / (AmE), / ˈleŋθɪli / (BrE)
In a lengthy, prolonged, or overly detailed manner.
in a lengthy manner

Examples

The professor explained the theory lengthily, leaving some students confused.
He wrote lengthily about his travels, filling several notebooks.
During the meeting, they discussed the proposal lengthily before reaching a decision.

Synset Examples

the argument went on lengthily
she talked at length about the problem

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lengthy - taking a long time; prolonged
  • Antonym: briefly - for a short time or in few words

Etymology

From 'lengthy' (English) + '-ly' (adverb suffix).
