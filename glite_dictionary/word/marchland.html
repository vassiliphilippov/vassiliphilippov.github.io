marchland

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑrtʃ.lænd / (AmE), / ˈmɑːtʃ.lænd / (BrE)
A region of land on or near the border between two countries or territories; a border district.
border region or district
archaic

Examples

The ancient marchland was fiercely contested by rival lords.
Trade flourished in the marchlands bordering the two kingdoms.
Settlements on the marchland often had to defend themselves from raids.

Synset Examples

the Welsh marches between England and Wales

Related Terms

  • Synonym: borderland - region or district near a border
  • Synonym: frontier - a border between two countries, especially where unsettled

Etymology

From Old English *mearc* (boundary) and *land* (land).
