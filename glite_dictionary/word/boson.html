boson

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊˌsɑn / (AmE), / ˈbəʊzɒn / (BrE)
A subatomic particle that obeys Bose-Einstein statistics, not the Pauli exclusion principle; includes particles, such as photons and all nuclei with even mass numbers.
particle obeying Bose-Einstein statistics

Examples

The photon is a classic example of a boson in quantum mechanics.
Higgs boson discovery was a groundbreaking event in particle physics.
Because helium-4 atoms are bosons, they can form a superfluid at low temperatures.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fermion - a particle that obeys the Pauli exclusion principle

Etymology

Named after Indian physicist Satyendra Nath Bose and the suffix -on (as in electron).
