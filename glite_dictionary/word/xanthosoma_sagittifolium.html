Xanthosoma sagittifolium

1. NOUN

/ zænˌθoʊˌsoʊmə sæˌɡɪtəˈfoʊliəm / (AmE), / zanˌθəʊˌsəʊmə sæˌɡɪtɪˈfəʊliəm / (BrE)
A tropical American plant of the arum family with edible tubers, often cooked and eaten like yams or potatoes.
tropical plant with edible tubers

Examples

Xanthosoma sagittifolium is cultivated in many tropical countries as a root vegetable.
The tubers of Xanthosoma sagittifolium can be prepared in much the same way as potatoes.
Many Caribbean dishes use Xanthosoma sagittifolium as a staple ingredient.

Etymology

From Greek xanthos (yellow) + soma (body) and Latin sagittifolium (arrow-leafed).
