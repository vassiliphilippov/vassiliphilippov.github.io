shellflower

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɛlˌflaʊər / (AmE), / ˈʃɛlflaʊə / (BrE)
A showy perennial plant of marshlands in eastern and central North America, having waxy lance-shaped leaves and flowers with lower parts creamy white and upper parts pale pink to deep purple.
perennial marsh plant with showy flowers

Examples

The shellflower blooms in late summer, adding a splash of color to the marshlands.
Gardeners admire the shellflower for its delicate pink and purple petals.
Botanists are studying the shellflower to learn more about its adaptation to wet environments.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃɛlˌflaʊ.ər / (AmE), / ˈʃɛlˌflaʊ.ə / (BrE)
An ornamental plant with shiny oblong leaves and arching clusters of white flowers tinged with pink, yellow, and magenta.
ornamental plant with colorful flowers

Examples

The shellflower added a tropical touch to their garden with its bright, arching clusters.
She admired the delicate pink and yellow streaks on the petals of the shellflower.
Many gardeners prize the shellflower for its striking foliage as well as its blooms.
