clarification

1. NOUN

/ ˌklɛr.ə.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌklær.ɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of removing solid particles from a liquid to make it clear
removal of solids from liquid

Examples

The water plant uses clarification to remove impurities before distribution.
After clarification, the juice looked much clearer and brighter.
Industrial waste treatment often involves clarification to separate solid sludge from liquid.

Etymology

From French clarifier (to make clear), from Latin clarificare.

2. NOUN

/ ˌklɛrəfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌklærɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
an explanation or interpretation that makes something clearer or easier to understand
the act of making something clear

Examples

The manager provided a helpful clarification during the meeting, resolving the team’s confusion.
If you need any clarification on the new policy, please ask.
After reading the instructions again, Maria sought clarification from her supervisor.

Synset Examples

the professor's clarification helped her to understand the textbook

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explanation - a statement or account that makes something clear
  • Antonym: confusion - lack of understanding; uncertainty

Etymology

From French 'clarification' and Medieval Latin 'clarificatio', from Latin 'clarificare' (to make clear).
