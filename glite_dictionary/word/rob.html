to rob

1. VERB

/ rɑb / (AmE), / rɒb / (BrE)
(informal) To charge (someone) an unreasonably high price for something; to overcharge or swindle.
overcharge; charge too much
informal

Examples

The taxi driver tried to rob us by charging double the usual fare.
I felt like the restaurant robbed me with their ridiculously high prices.
They know tourists will pay, so they rob them every chance they get.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overcharge - to charge someone too high a price

2. VERB

/ rɑb / (AmE), / rɒb / (BrE)
to take property or money from a person or place illegally, often using force or threats
take property by force or illegally

Examples

The thieves planned to rob the jewelry store at night.
He felt violated after being robbed of his wallet on the street.
They tried to rob the bank, but were caught by the police.

Synset Examples

The burglars robbed him of all his money

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steal - to take another person's property without permission or legal right
  • Synonym: to burgle - to enter a building illegally and steal things (mainly British)

Etymology

From Old French 'rober' (to rob), from Germanic origins.
