barbarity

1. NOUN

/ bɑrˈbærɪti / (AmE), / bɑːˈbærɪti / (BrE)
cruel and brutal behavior; a brutal or savage act
cruel, brutal act or behavior

Examples

The barbarity of the attack shocked the entire community.
They condemned the dictator's barbarity against his own people.
History is full of stories of both kindness and barbarity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cruelty - deliberate infliction of pain or suffering
  • Antonym: civilization - the stage of human social development regarded as more advanced and humane

Etymology

From Medieval Latin barbaritas (foreignness, cruel nature), from Latin barbarus (foreign, barbaric).

2. NOUN

/ bɑɹˈbæɹɪti / (AmE), / bɑːˈbærɪti / (BrE)
Extreme cruelty or brutality; the quality of being shockingly cruel and inhumane.
extreme cruelty or inhumanity

Examples

The barbarity of the attack shocked everyone who heard about it.
History books are filled with tales of barbarity during wartime.
Many condemned the regime for its barbarity towards prisoners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cruelty - callous indifference to or pleasure in causing pain or suffering
  • Synonym: inhumanity - devoid of compassion or kindness; extreme cruelty
  • Antonym: kindness - the quality of being friendly, generous, or considerate

Etymology

From Latin 'barbaritas' (foreignness, cruelty) via Old French 'barbarie'.
