Alma-Ata

1. PROPN

/ ˌɑːl.məˈɑː.tə / (AmE), / ˌælməˈɑːtə / (BrE)
the largest city in Kazakhstan, formerly the capital until 1998, now known as Almaty
largest city in Kazakhstan, former capital

Examples

She traveled to Alma-Ata to visit her relatives during the summer holidays.
In 1997, the capital was moved from Alma-Ata to Astana.
Many international conferences were held in Alma-Ata during the Soviet era.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Almaty - modern name for the city formerly known as Alma-Ata

Etymology

Russian form of Kazakh Almaty, formerly Alma-Ata meaning 'Father of Apples.'
