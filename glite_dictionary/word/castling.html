castling

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæs.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɑːs.lɪŋ / (BrE)
in chess, a special move in which the king and a rook are moved at the same time, interchanging their positions according to specific rules
special chess move involving king and rook

Examples

After several moves, he decided that castling would protect his king.
The opportunity for castling was lost after the king had already moved.
In chess strategy, early castling is often advised to safeguard the king.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: castle - to make the castling move in chess

Etymology

From 'castle' (a rook in chess) + -ing, referring to the action involving the rook (castle).
