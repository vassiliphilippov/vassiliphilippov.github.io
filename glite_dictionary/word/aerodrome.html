aerodrome

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛrəˌdroʊm / (AmE), / ˈeərəˌdrəʊm / (BrE)
an airfield equipped with a control tower, hangars, and facilities for passengers and cargo
airfield with control tower and facilities

Examples

The new aerodrome was built to accommodate increased air traffic near the city.
Several small aircraft are stored in hangars at the local aerodrome.
Plans are underway to modernize the aerodrome with upgraded passenger facilities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: airport - a place where aircraft regularly take off and land, with buildings for passengers

Etymology

From French 'aérodrome', from Greek 'aēr' (air) + 'dromos' (course, running).
