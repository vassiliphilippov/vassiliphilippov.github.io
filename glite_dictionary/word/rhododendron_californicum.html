Rhododendron californicum

1. NOUN

/ ˌroʊdəˈdɛndrən ˌkælɪˈfɔrnɪkəm / (AmE), / ˌrəʊdəˈdɛndrən ˌkælɪˈfɔːnɪkəm / (BrE)
a medium-sized rhododendron native to the Pacific coast of North America, bearing large rosy flowers with brown spots
Pacific coast rhododendron species

Examples

The Rhododendron californicum thrives in the moist forests along the Pacific coast.
Gardeners admire Rhododendron californicum for its striking rosy flowers and attractive foliage.
During spring, clusters of Rhododendron californicum brighten up the shaded woodland gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rhododendron - a genus of shrubs with large clusters of bell-shaped flowers

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus name 'Rhododendron' and the specific epithet 'californicum' (of California).
