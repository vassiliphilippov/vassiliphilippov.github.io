tither

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪðər / (AmE), / ˈtaɪðə / (BrE)
A person who pays tithes, especially as part of religious duties.
person who pays tithes

Examples

As a faithful tither, she contributed a portion of her income to the church every month.
The community recognized him as the most generous tither in the congregation.
Historically, being a tither was almost mandatory in some rural villages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tithe-payer - a person who pays tithes
  • Antonym: nonpayer - a person who does not pay tithes or taxes

Etymology

From 'tithe' + '-er', indicating a person who performs the action of tithing.
