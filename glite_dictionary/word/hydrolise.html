to hydrolise

1. VERB

/ ˈhaɪdrəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdrəˌlaɪz / (BrE)
to cause (a compound) to react with water and undergo hydrolysis
to subject to hydrolysis

Examples

Enzymes can hydrolise proteins into their constituent amino acids.
Industrial processes often hydrolise fats to produce soap.
Certain acids are used to hydrolise cellulose into glucose.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hydrolyze - to cause a compound to react with water (US spelling)

Etymology

From hydro- (water) and -lyse (to loosen), after hydrolysis.
