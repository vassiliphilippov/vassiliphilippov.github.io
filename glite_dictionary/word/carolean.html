Carolean

1. ADJ

/ ˌkɛroʊˈliːən / (AmE), / ˌkærəˈliːən / (BrE)
relating to the period or style of the reigns of Charles I or Charles II of England
of or relating to the reigns of Charles I or II

Examples

The architecture of the manor is purely Carolean in design.
She admires the Carolean furniture for its ornate carvings and bold lines.
The exhibition showcases Carolean art from the 17th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Elizabethan - relating to the period or style of Queen Elizabeth I's reign

Etymology

From Latin Carolus (Charles), with English -ean suffix.
