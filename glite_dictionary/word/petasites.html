Petasites

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛtəˌsaɪtiːz / (AmE), / ˈpɛtəˌsaɪtiːz / (BrE)
A genus of rhizomatous perennial herbs native to north temperate regions, commonly known as butterburs or sweet coltsfoots.
a genus of butterburs (plants)

Examples

The broad leaves of Petasites plants provide excellent ground cover in damp areas.
Many gardeners cultivate Petasites for its impressive size and decorative foliage.
Petasites is well known for its use in traditional remedies for headaches and allergies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butterbur - common name for plants in the genus Petasites

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on Greek 'petasos' (a broad-brimmed hat), referring to the large leaves.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɛtəˈsaɪtiːz / (AmE), / ˌpɛtəˈsaɪtiːz / (BrE)
A genus of rhizomatous perennial herbs in the daisy family (Asteraceae), found in northern temperate regions, commonly known as butterbur or sweet coltsfoot.
a genus of perennial herbs (butterbur)

Examples

Petasites plants are valued in gardens for their large, dramatic leaves.
In spring, Petasites produces clusters of pale pink or white flowers before the leaves emerge.
Researchers have studied Petasites extracts for their potential use in treating migraines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butterbur - a common name for some plants in the genus Petasites
  • Synonym: coltsfoot - a related plant in the daisy family resembling butterbur

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, from Greek petasos (wide-brimmed hat), referencing the large leaves.
