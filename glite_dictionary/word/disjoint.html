disjoint

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈdʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / dɪsˈdʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
(of sets) having no elements in common; mutually exclusive.
(math) with nothing in common

Examples

The two sets are disjoint, meaning they share no members at all.
If two groups are disjoint, there is no overlap between their members.
Events A and B are disjoint if they cannot happen at the same time.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: overlapping - (of sets) sharing one or more elements

Etymology

From Latin dis- (apart) + iungere (to join).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02118273-s
Synset POS: s

2. VERB

/ dɪsˈdʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / dɪsˈdʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
To become separated, disconnected, or to lose a joint or connection.
become separated or disconnected

Examples

When the puzzle pieces disjoint, it is difficult to see the image clearly.
After the accident, the train cars disjointed and came to rest apart from each other.
If the system components disjoint, the network will fail to operate as a whole.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: separate - to move or be apart
  • Antonym: to connect - to join or link together

Etymology

From Latin disjunctus, past participle of disjungere (to separate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01296785-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ dɪsˈdʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / dɪsˈdʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
to separate (meat or a body) at the joints; to cut apart at the joints
separate at the joints

Examples

Before roasting the bird, you should disjoint it into manageable pieces.
The butcher expertly disjointed the carcass in just a few minutes.
It can be tricky to disjoint a rabbit without practice.

Synset Examples

disjoint the chicken before cooking it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joint - to cut (meat) into joints

Etymology

From Latin disjungere (to unyoke, separate), via Old French desjoindre.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01297235-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ dɪsˈdʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / dɪsˈdʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
to separate or disconnect parts; to undo the joining of something
to separate or disconnect

Examples

The mechanic had to disjoint several parts of the engine to find the source of the leak.
Carefully disjoint the limbs from the turkey before serving.
The puzzle pieces had to be disjointed and rearranged to solve the picture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: separate - to set or keep apart
  • Antonym: join - to put or bring two or more things together

Etymology

From Old French 'desjoint', based on Latin 'disiungere' (to unyoke, separate), from 'dis-' (apart) + 'iungere' (to join).
