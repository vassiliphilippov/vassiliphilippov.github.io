septectomy

1. NOUN

/ sɛpˈtɛktəmi / (AmE), / sɛpˈtɛktəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure involving the removal of all or part of a septum, especially the nasal or atrial septum
removal of a septum by surgery
medical

Examples

The surgeon recommended a septectomy to correct the patient’s chronic nasal obstruction.
After undergoing a septectomy, the patient reported improved breathing and fewer sinus infections.
Complications from an incomplete septectomy may require further medical intervention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: septoplasty - surgical repair of a septum, especially the nasal septum

Etymology

From septum (wall dividing two cavities) + -ectomy (surgical removal)
