sistership

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪstərˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈsɪstəʃɪp / (BrE)
the relationship between a sister and her siblings
relationship between a sister and her siblings

Examples

The close sistership between the three girls was evident in their daily lives.
Research on sistership explores how sisters interact within the family structure.
She treasured the unique sistership she shared with her younger siblings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kinship - family relationship; blood relationship

Etymology

Formed from 'sister' + '-ship' (denoting state or condition), following the model of 'kinship' and 'friendship'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪstərˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈsɪstəʃɪp / (BrE)
an association or society of women united by a common religion, trade, or interest
association of women with shared interest

Examples

The charitable sistership welcomed new members from various professions.
They formed a sistership dedicated to promoting women's rights in the workplace.
The sistership held monthly meetings to support women in their community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sisterhood - the relationship between sisters or a society of women associated for a common purpose

Etymology

Formed from 'sister' + 'ship', following the pattern of 'brotherhood'.
