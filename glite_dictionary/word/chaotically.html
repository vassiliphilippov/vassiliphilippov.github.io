chaotically

1. ADV

/ keɪˈɑtɪkli / (AmE), / keɪˈɒtɪkli / (BrE)
In a wild, disordered, or confused manner.
in a wild or confused way

Examples

The papers were scattered chaotically across the desk, making it impossible to find anything quickly.
Children ran chaotically around the playground after the bell rang.
During the blackout, people exited the building chaotically, ignoring the usual orderly procedure.

Synset Examples

the drugged man was talking chaotically

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chaotic - utterly disordered or confused

Etymology

From 'chaotic' (from chaos, ultimately from Greek khaos) + -ally.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00285172-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ kiˈɑːtɪkli / (AmE), / keɪˈɒtɪkli / (BrE)
In a very disordered or confused way, suggesting chaos.
in a very disordered way

Examples

The children ran chaotically through the garden, ignoring any sense of order.
Papers and books were piled chaotically on his desk, making it hard to find anything.
Traffic moved chaotically during the storm, with drivers ignoring most of the rules.

Synset Examples

the room was chaotically disorganized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chaotic - completely disordered or confused
  • Antonym: orderly - arranged or organized in a neat, methodical way

Etymology

From 'chaotic' + '-ally', from Greek 'khaos' (disorder, confusion).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00285042-r
Synset POS: r
