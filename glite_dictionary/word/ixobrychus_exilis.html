Ixobrychus exilis

1. NOUN

/ aɪkˈsɑːbɹɪkəs ɪɡˈzɪlɪs / (AmE), / ɪkˈsɒbrɪkəs ɪɡˈzɪlɪs / (BrE)
A small species of American bittern, commonly known as the least bittern, found in marshes across North and Central America.
species of small American bittern

Examples

The Ixobrychus exilis is rarely seen because it hides in dense reed beds.
Wetlands conservation is vital for the survival of the Ixobrychus exilis population.
Ornithologists observed several Ixobrychus exilis individuals nesting along the marsh's edge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: least bittern - common name for Ixobrychus exilis

Etymology

From Greek 'ixos' (mistletoe, used for birdlime) and 'brychein' (to bellow), with Latin 'exilis' (slender, small).
