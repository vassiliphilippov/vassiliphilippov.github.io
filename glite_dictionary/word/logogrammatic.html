logogrammatic

1. ADJ

/ ˌlɑːɡəɡræˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌlɒɡəɡræˈmætɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of logograms or logographs (written symbols that represent words or morphemes)
relating to logograms or logographs

Examples

The logogrammatic system of Chinese writing allows complex ideas to be represented by single characters.
Scholars compared logogrammatic scripts to phonetic writing systems in their research.
Her thesis focused on the historical development of logogrammatic writing in ancient civilizations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: logographic - relating to logographs; using symbols for entire words

Etymology

From 'logogram' (from Greek 'logos' meaning word + 'gramma' meaning letter) + '-matic' (adjectival suffix).
