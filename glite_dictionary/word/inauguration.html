inauguration

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌnɔɡjəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌnɔːɡjʊˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of beginning or introducing a new system, policy, or operation
beginning of a new system or operation

Examples

The inauguration of the new recycling program brought excitement to the city council.
Management delayed the inauguration of the updated software platform until testing was complete.
The hospital's inauguration of night-time emergency services greatly improved patient care.

Synset Examples

he opposed the inauguration of fluoridation
the startup of the new factory was delayed by strikes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commencement - the act or process of beginning something
  • Antonym: termination - the act of ending something

Etymology

From Latin inauguratio, from inaugurare (to consecrate by augury).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˌnɔɡjəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌnɔːɡjʊˈreɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
a formal ceremony to mark the beginning of a leader's term or the opening of something important, especially the official induction of someone into office
formal ceremony of induction into office

Examples

The inauguration of the new mayor drew a large crowd from across the city.
Her speech at the inauguration set the tone for her presidency.
The museum will hold its official inauguration next weekend.

Synset Examples

the new president obviously enjoyed his inauguration

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swearing-in - the official ceremony in which someone takes an oath of office

Etymology

From Latin 'inauguratio', from 'inaugurare' (to consecrate by augury).
