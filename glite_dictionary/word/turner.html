turner

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːnər / (AmE), / ˈtɜːnə / (BrE)
a person who swings the ends of the ropes in the game of jump rope while others jump
one who swings ropes in jump rope

Examples

During recess, Ella was the turner while her friends took turns jumping rope.
The game requires two skilled turners to keep the ropes moving smoothly.
He started as a turner, but soon joined the jumpers when he got the rhythm.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɜrnər / (AmE), / ˈtɜːnə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to shape wood, metal, or other materials on a lathe
lathe operator (craftsperson)

Examples

He apprenticed as a turner and quickly mastered working with precision tools.
The turner shaped the wooden legs for the new table with great skill.
A skilled turner can produce intricate patterns on metal or wood using a lathe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lathe operator - a person who operates a lathe machine

Etymology

From Middle English turnour, from Old French turneor, from tourner (to turn).

3. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːnər / (AmE), / ˈtɜːnə / (BrE)
A cooking utensil with a flat, flexible blade and a long handle, used to turn or serve food.
flat kitchen utensil for turning food

Examples

She used a turner to flip the pancakes on the hot griddle.
The silicone turner won't scratch your nonstick pans.
After cooking the fish, he carefully lifted it from the pan with a turner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spatula - a kitchen utensil with a flat, broad blade used for lifting or flipping food

4. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːnɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɜːnə / (BrE)
A gymnast or tumbler who is a member of a turnverein (a German gymnastic society).
a gymnast in a turnverein

Examples

As a skilled turner, he competed in several national gymnastic events organized by the local club.
The city hosted a convention of turners from turnvereins across the country.
Her great-grandfather was a prominent turner who promoted physical fitness in the community.

Etymology

From German Turner, meaning 'gymnast', derived from 'turnen' (to do gymnastics).
