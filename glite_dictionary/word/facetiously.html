facetiously

1. ADV

/ fəˈsiːʃəsli / (AmE), / fəˈsiːʃəsli / (BrE)
in a manner that is not meant to be taken seriously; jokingly or sarcastically
in a joking or unserious manner

Examples

Please don't take my comment facetiously; I was only trying to lighten the mood.
When he suggested we skip work and go to the beach, he was speaking facetiously.
She apologized after realizing her facetiously intended remark had upset him.

Synset Examples

I meant it facetiously

Related Terms

  • Antonym: seriously - in a serious or earnest manner

Etymology

From 'facetious' (from French facétieux, from Latin facetia 'jest'), plus the adverbial suffix '-ly'.
