countercurrent

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊn.tɚˌkɝ.ənt / (AmE), / ˈkaʊn.təˌkʌr.ənt / (BrE)
a tendency, movement, or action that runs counter to the prevailing direction or main activity, especially within a group or society
opposing tendency; movement contrary to the main flow

Examples

There was a strong countercurrent of dissent among the younger members of the organization.
Although most of the team supported the merger, a countercurrent of opposition emerged during discussions.
Social reform often faces a countercurrent of traditional values and resistance.

Synset Examples

political crosscurrents disrupted the conference

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crosscurrent - a conflicting tendency or movement (especially within a group); similar to 'countercurrent'

Etymology

Formed from 'counter-' (against) + 'current' (flow).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊntərˌkɝrənt / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəkʌrənt / (BrE)
A stretch of turbulent water in a river or the sea where one current flows into or across another current, often resulting in swirling motion or disruption.
a turbulent area where two water currents meet

Examples

The boat struggled against the countercurrent where the river met the incoming tide.
Swimmers should be careful of the strong countercurrent near the rocks.
A countercurrent developed as the ocean's main flow collided with a coastal stream.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: current - A continuous flow of water in a particular direction

Etymology

From 'counter-' (opposing) + 'current' (flow of water), indicating opposing flows.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
