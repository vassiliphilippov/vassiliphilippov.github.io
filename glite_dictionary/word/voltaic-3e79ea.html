voltaic

1. ADJ

/ voʊˈteɪɪk / (AmE), / vɒlˈteɪɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or producing electric current by chemical action, especially in a battery or cell.
producing electric current by chemical action

Examples

The scientist demonstrated a voltaic cell to produce electricity from chemicals.
This battery operates on a voltaic principle, generating power through chemical reactions.
A voltaic pile is considered the first modern electric battery.

Synset Examples

a galvanic cell
a voltaic (or galvanic) couple

Related Terms

  • Synonym: galvanic - relating to or producing a direct electric current, especially by chemical action

Etymology

From the name of Alessandro Volta, Italian physicist who invented the voltaic pile.
