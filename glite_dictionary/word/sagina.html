Sagina

1. NOUN

/ səˈɡaɪnə / (AmE), / səˈɡiːnə / (BrE)
A genus of small, low-growing annual or perennial herbs found in temperate and cool regions, commonly known as pearlworts.
genus of small herbaceous plants

Examples

The botanist identified the tiny plant as a species of Sagina.
Several varieties of Sagina are cultivated as ground cover in gardens.
Sagina is often found in moist, grassy meadows across northern Europe.

Etymology

From Latin *sagina* (fodder), referring to plants used as animal feed.

2. NOUN

/ səˈdʒaɪnə / (AmE), / səˈdʒiːnə / (BrE)
A genus of small, low-growing annual or perennial herbs found in temperate and cool regions, belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.
a genus of small temperate herbs

Examples

The tiny flowers of Sagina are often found in rocky alpine regions.
Several species within Sagina are commonly known as pearlworts.
Gardeners sometimes use Sagina as a low-maintenance ground cover.

Etymology

From Latin sagina (fattening, feeding), also used historically for certain plants.
