Lucullus

1. PROPN

/ luˈkʌləs / (AmE), / luˈkʌləs / (BrE)
A Roman general (circa 110–57 BC) renowned for his wealth, luxurious lifestyle, and lavish banquets.
Roman general known for luxury and banquets

Examples

The senator's dining room was reminiscent of the tables of Lucullus.
Gourmets sometimes speak of enjoying a true Lucullus experience.
Historians often cite Lucullus as a symbol of ancient Roman luxury.

2. PROPN

/ luˈkʌl.əs / (AmE), / luˈkʌl.əs / (BrE)
(c. 110–57 BC) A Roman general renowned for his wealth, self-indulgence, and lavish banquets.
Roman general known for lavish banquets

Examples

The extravagant feasts of Lucullus became legendary in ancient Rome.
Historians often cite Lucullus as a symbol of Roman luxury and excess.
The term 'a Lucullus feast' refers to meals as sumptuous as those enjoyed by the Roman general.

Etymology

From Latin 'Lucullus', name of the Roman general Lucius Licinius Lucullus.
