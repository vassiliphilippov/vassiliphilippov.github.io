homogeneity

1. NOUN

/ ˌhoʊməˌdʒiˈneɪɾi / (AmE), / ˌhɒməˌdʒiːˈniːɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being uniform throughout in composition or structure
uniformity in composition or structure

Examples

The homogeneity of the materials ensured that the product had consistent quality throughout.
Cultural homogeneity can sometimes lead to a lack of diversity in ideas and perspectives.
Scientists tested the homogeneity of the solution to ensure every sample was identical.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: heterogeneity - the quality or state of being diverse in composition or structure

Etymology

From Greek 'homogenēs' (of the same kind), from 'homos' (same) + 'genos' (kind).

2. NOUN

/ ˌhoʊməˌdʒiːˈneɪəti / (AmE), / ˌhɒməˌdʒiːˈneɪəti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being all the same or of a similar kind or nature; uniformity.
uniformity; similarity throughout

Examples

The research group values homogeneity in its experimental procedures to ensure consistent results.
Urban planning often encourages architectural homogeneity to create visually harmonious neighborhoods.
Cultural homogeneity can make it easier for communities to reach consensus on important issues.

Synset Examples

there is a remarkable homogeneity between the two companies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uniformity - the quality or state of being uniform; overall consistency; synonym for 'homogeneity'
  • Antonym: heterogeneity - the quality of being diverse or different in kind; antonym of 'homogeneity'

Etymology

From Greek homogeneia, from homos (same) + genos (kind).
