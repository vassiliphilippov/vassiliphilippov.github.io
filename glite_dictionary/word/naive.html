naive

1. ADJ

/ naɪˈiv / (AmE), / naɪˈiːv / (BrE)
lacking experience, wisdom, or judgment; not initiated or lacking relevant experience.
lacking experience or judgment

Examples

As a naive newcomer, she made many honest mistakes at her first job.
His naive optimism led him to believe success would be easy.
Many people are naive about the risks involved in starting a new business.

Synset Examples

it seemed a bizarre ceremony to uninitiated western eyes
he took part in the experiment as a naive subject

Related Terms

  • Synonym: innocent - lacking experience or sophistication
  • Antonym: sophisticated - having much worldly experience and knowledge

Etymology

From French 'naïf' (naive), from Latin 'nativus' (native, natural).

lacking experience or sophistication

4. ADJ

/ naɪˈiv / (AmE), / naɪˈiːv / (BrE)

inexperienced or innocent

Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sophisticated - having a great deal of worldly experience and knowledge

5. ADJ

/ naɪˈiv / (AmE), / naɪˈiːv / (BrE)
(of art or style) produced by someone without formal training, characterized by simplicity or a childlike quality.
art/style by untrained creator, simple in form

Examples

The exhibition features several naive paintings by local, self-taught artists.
Her naive sculptures possess a raw, honest charm often missing from academic works.
Critics praised the naive style of his drawings for their freshness and sincerity.

Synset Examples

primitive art such as that by Grandma Moses is often colorful and striking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: primitive - art or style produced in a simple, unsophisticated manner, often by self-taught artists

Etymology

From French 'naïve', from Latin 'nativus' (innate, natural).
