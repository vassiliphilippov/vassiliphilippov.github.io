Gizeh

1. PROPN

/ ɡɪˈzeɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡɪzi / (BrE)
an ancient city in Egypt, on the west bank of the Nile near Cairo, famous as the site of the Great Pyramids and the Sphinx
ancient Egyptian city; site of the Pyramids

Examples

The Great Pyramid at Gizeh is one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World.
Many tourists visit Gizeh each year to see the Sphinx and the pyramids.
Excavations at Gizeh have revealed important artifacts from ancient Egypt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Giza - alternative English spelling of the same city; ancient Egyptian city with pyramids

Etymology

From Arabic الجيزة (al-Gīzah), meaning 'the plateau'.
