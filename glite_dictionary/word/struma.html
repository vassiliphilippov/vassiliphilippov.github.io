struma

1. NOUN

/ ˈstruːmə / (AmE), / ˈstruːmə / (BrE)
A swelling of the thyroid gland, commonly known as a goiter, caused by various factors such as hormonal imbalance or iodine deficiency.
enlarged thyroid gland
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with a struma after observing the swelling in her neck.
A struma can develop if the body does not get enough iodine from the diet.
Treatment for struma depends on whether the thyroid is underactive, overactive, or normal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goiter - swelling of the neck due to thyroid gland enlargement

Etymology

From Latin 'struma' (swelling, tumor).

2. NOUN

/ ˈstruːmə / (AmE), / ˈstruːmə / (BrE)
A form of tuberculosis characterized by swelling of the lymphatic glands, especially in the neck; also used to refer generally to goiter.
tuberculosis of lymphatic glands; also goiter
medical

Examples

The child was diagnosed with struma after doctors observed swelling in her neck lymph glands.
Historians note that struma was once a common manifestation of tuberculosis in Europe.
Successful treatment of struma depends on early medical intervention and appropriate antibiotics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goiter - an enlargement of the thyroid gland
  • Synonym: scrofula - tuberculosis of the lymph nodes, especially in the neck

Etymology

From Latin struma (scrofulous tumor, swelling).
