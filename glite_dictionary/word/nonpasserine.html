nonpasserine

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɑnˈpæsəˌraɪn / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈpæsəraɪn / (BrE)
relating to birds that are not perching birds, especially those not belonging to the order Passeriformes
not a perching bird (non-passerine)

Examples

Owls, penguins, and hummingbirds are considered nonpasserine species within the bird classification system.
Unlike most songbirds, eagles are nonpasserine and have different anatomical features.
The exhibition showcased both nonpasserine and passerine birds from around the world.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: passerine - relating to perching birds or birds of the order Passeriformes

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'non-' (not) + 'Passerinae' (a subgroup of perching birds), referring to birds outside that group.
