Fijian

1. ADJ

/ fiˈdʒiən / (AmE), / fɪˈdʒiːən / (BrE)
of or relating to Fiji, its people, language, or culture
of or relating to Fiji or its people

Examples

The Fijian islands attract tourists from around the world for their beautiful beaches.
She wore a traditional Fijian dress to the cultural festival.
Many Fijian dishes include fresh seafood and tropical fruits.

Synset Examples

the Fijian population
Fijian folktales

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fiji - an island country in the South Pacific Ocean

Etymology

2. NOUN

/ fɪˈdʒiːən / (AmE), / fɪˈdʒiːən / (BrE)
the Austronesian language spoken chiefly on the islands of Fiji
language spoken in Fiji

Examples

Many people in Fiji are fluent in both Fijian and English.
She is studying Fijian to communicate better with her family.
The dictionary includes translations between English and Fijian.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Samoan - the Austronesian language spoken in Samoa
  • Antonym: English - the Germanic language spoken in the United Kingdom, the United States, and many other countries

Etymology

From Fiji, the name of the Pacific island country, plus -an.
