Taricha

1. NOUN

/ təˈriːkə / (AmE), / təˈriːkə / (BrE)
A genus of Pacific newts found in western North America, known for their toxic skin secretions.
genus of Pacific newts

Examples

The genus Taricha includes several species of newts native to the Pacific Coast of North America.
Herpetologists are studying the toxins produced by Taricha to understand their defensive mechanisms.
Unlike other newts, Taricha are particularly resistant to certain predators due to their potent neurotoxins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: newt - a small amphibian with a lizard-like appearance

Etymology

From Greek 'tarichos' meaning 'pickled fish', referring to the preserved appearance of some newts.

2. NOUN

/ təˈriːkə / (AmE), / təˈriːkə / (BrE)
A genus of newts native to western North America, commonly known as Pacific newts.
a genus of Pacific newts

Examples

The Taricha genus includes species that produce a potent neurotoxin as a defense mechanism.
Researchers studied the habitats of several Taricha species along the Pacific coastline.
Many herpetologists consider Taricha an important group for understanding amphibian evolution in North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: newt - a small semiaquatic amphibian

Etymology

From New Latin, possibly derived from Greek 'tarichos' meaning 'dried fish' or 'pickled fish', referring to the ancient practice of preserving animals.
