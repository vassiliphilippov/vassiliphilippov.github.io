Sturnella neglecta

1. NOUN

/ stɜrˈnɛl.ə nəˈɡlɛk.tə / (AmE), / stɜːˈnɛl.ə nɪˈɡlɛk.tə / (BrE)
A species of meadowlark native to western North America, known for its distinctive song and yellow underparts.
a North American meadowlark species

Examples

I spotted a Sturnella neglecta singing from a fence post during my trip to Colorado.
The song of the Sturnella neglecta is often heard across the grasslands in spring.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of Sturnella neglecta throughout the western United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: western meadowlark - common name for Sturnella neglecta
  • Degree Lower: Sturnella magna - the eastern meadowlark, a related species

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, where 'Sturnella' is a diminutive of 'Sturnus' (starling), and 'neglecta' means 'neglected' in Latin.
