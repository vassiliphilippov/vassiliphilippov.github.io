stillness

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪlnəs / (AmE), / ˈstɪlnəs / (BrE)
a deep or tranquil silence; absence of movement or sound, especially suggesting calm
deep silence; tranquil calm

Examples

The stillness of the early morning was broken only by birdsong.
He meditated in complete stillness, undisturbed by the outside world.
A sudden stillness fell over the crowd as the lights dimmed.

Synset Examples

the still of the night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silence - complete absence of sound
  • Synonym: quiet - state of low noise or calm
  • Antonym: noise - unwanted or disturbing sound

Etymology

From Old English stilnes, from stil (still) + -ness (noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɪlnəs / (AmE), / ˈstɪlnəs / (BrE)
a state in which there is no movement; complete absence of motion or sound
absence of movement or sound

Examples

The stillness of the early morning was broken only by birdsong.
He appreciated the stillness in the art gallery after a noisy day.
She meditated in stillness, feeling at peace with the world.

Synset Examples

the utter motionlessness of a marble statue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motionlessness - absence of movement
  • Antonym: movement - the act of moving or changing place

Etymology

From Old English 'stilnes', from 'stille' (still) + '-ness'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈstɪlnəs / (AmE), / ˈstɪlnɪs / (BrE)
a state or quality of being calm and untroubled by wind; complete absence of movement in the air
absence of wind; calm air

Examples

The stillness of the air made the lake's surface look like glass.
After the storm passed, an eerie stillness descended on the valley.
They waited in the stillness before dawn, listening for any sign of wind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calm - absence of wind or disturbance in the air

Etymology

From Middle English stilnesse, from still (calm, motionless) + -ness.
