sogginess

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑɡi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsɒɡi.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being very wet or saturated with water.
the condition of being very wet

Examples

The sogginess of the bread ruined the sandwich for me.
After days of rain, the sogginess in the backyard made it impossible to mow the lawn.
She complained about the sogginess of her socks after walking through the puddles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wetness - the state of being wet; moisture
  • Antonym: dryness - the state or quality of being dry

Etymology

From 'soggy' + '-ness', with 'soggy' likely from Middle English 'sogged' (soaked).
