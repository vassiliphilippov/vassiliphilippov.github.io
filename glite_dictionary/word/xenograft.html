xenograft

1. NOUN

/ ˈziːnoʊˌɡræft / (AmE), / ˈzɛːnəʊˌɡrɑːft / (BrE)
Tissue taken from one species transplanted into a member of another species, typically used temporarily for medical treatment such as severe burns.
tissue graft from a different species
medical

Examples

The doctors used a xenograft to temporarily cover the patient's burn wounds.
Research into xenograft rejection has led to advances in transplant medicine.
The laboratory tested the effectiveness of the xenograft in promoting skin regeneration.

Etymology

From Greek xenos (stranger, foreigner) + graft (from Old French grafe).
