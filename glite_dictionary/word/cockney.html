Cockney

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑkni / (AmE), / ˈkɒkni / (BrE)
A native of the East End of London, especially one who speaks the traditional dialect associated with that area.
native of the East End of London
british

Examples

Many famous comedians are proud to be called Cockney by birth.
He spoke with a distinctive Cockney accent that charmed everyone in the pub.
The Cockney community has its own rich traditions and rhyming slang.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Londoner - a person from London

Etymology

Originally a pejorative from Middle English 'cockenay' meaning 'milksop', later used for Londoners.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkɑkni / (AmE), / ˈkɒkni / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the working-class natives of East London or their dialect.
of or relating to Cockneys or their dialect

Examples

She spoke with a distinct Cockney accent that everyone could recognize.
The old market was full of Cockney charm and lively banter.
The film captured the humor and spirit of traditional Cockney communities.

Synset Examples

Cockney street urchins

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Londoner - a native or inhabitant of London
  • Synonym: Estuary English - an accent from the southeast of England influenced by Cockney

Etymology

From Middle English 'cockeney', meaning a pampered child; later used to denote city-dwellers, especially in East London.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɑk.ni / (AmE), / ˈkɒk.ni / (BrE)
the nonstandard dialect traditionally spoken by natives of the East End of London, characterized by distinctive pronunciation and vocabulary
London East End dialect
british

Examples

She spoke in a strong Cockney accent that was hard to mistake.
Some of the most famous rhyming slang terms come from Cockney English.
He enjoyed listening to the friendly chatter of the local Cockney vendors at the market.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Londoner - a native or inhabitant of London
  • Antonym: Received Pronunciation - the standard form of British English pronunciation

Etymology

From Middle English *cokeney* (pampered child), later associated with Londoners.
