pointillist

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɔɪn.təl.ɪst / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪn.tɪ.lɪst / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of pointillism, a painting technique using small dots of color to create an image
relating to pointillism (painting technique)

Examples

The artist's pointillist approach gave the landscape a shimmering effect.
She wrote her thesis on the techniques of pointillist painters in the 19th century.
His use of color was distinctly pointillist, with each hue applied in tiny, deliberate dots.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pointillism - a painting technique using dots of color

Etymology

From French pointilliste, from pointiller (to mark with dots), related to point (dot).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɔɪn.təl.ɪst / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪn.tɪ.lɪst / (BrE)
an artist who creates paintings using the technique of pointillism, applying small distinct dots of color to form an image
artist using pointillism technique

Examples

The famous pointillist painted scenes that seemed to shimmer with light and color.
As a pointillist, she carefully applied tiny dots of paint to create a detailed portrait.
The museum hosted an exhibition of works by 19th-century French pointillists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pointillism - the technique of painting using small dots of color

Etymology

From French 'pointilliste', from 'pointiller' (to mark with dots) + '-iste' (-ist).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10467498-n
Synset POS: n
