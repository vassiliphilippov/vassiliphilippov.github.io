Paiwanic

1. NOUN

/ paɪˈwɑːnɪk / (AmE), / paɪˈwænɪk / (BrE)
the Formosan language spoken by the Paiwan people of southern Taiwan
language of the Paiwan people, Taiwan

Examples

The Paiwanic language has a rich oral tradition passed down through generations.
She is working on a research project about Paiwanic grammar and vocabulary.
Efforts to revitalize Paiwanic have increased among the younger generation in Taiwan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Paiwan - the indigenous people of southern Taiwan who speak Paiwanic

Etymology

From Paiwan, an indigenous people of Taiwan, with the suffix -ic denoting relation.
