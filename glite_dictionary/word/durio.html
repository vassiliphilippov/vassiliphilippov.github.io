Durio

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʊriˌoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdjʊəriəʊ / (BrE)
A genus of tall tropical Asian trees belonging to the family Malvaceae, known for bearing the durian fruit.
genus of tropical Asian trees (source of durian fruit)

Examples

The genus Durio includes several species that produce the famous durian fruit.
Botanists have classified the spiky-fruited trees as part of the Durio genus.
Research on Durio has increased due to the fruit's economic importance in Southeast Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: durian - the large, spiky fruit produced by trees of the genus Durio

Etymology

From Malay 'durian', referring to the durian fruit, itself from 'duri' (thorn). Used in botanical Latin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʊrioʊ / (AmE), / ˈdjʊəriəʊ / (BrE)
a genus of tall tropical Asian trees in the family Malvaceae, best known for producing the durian fruit
genus of Asian trees producing durian

Examples

The Durio genus is native to Southeast Asia and is famed for its unique fruits.
Scientists are conducting research on the genetic diversity within Durio species.
The best-known member of the Durio genus is the durian tree, prized for its edible fruit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: durian - the edible fruit produced by trees of the genus Durio

Etymology

From Malay 'duri' (thorn), referring to the spiny fruit of the plants in this genus.
