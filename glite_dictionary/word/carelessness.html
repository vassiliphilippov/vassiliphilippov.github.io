carelessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛr.lɪs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkeə.lɪs.nəs / (BrE)
lack of attention or thought when doing something, resulting in mistakes or accidents.
lack of attention; negligence

Examples

His carelessness led to a costly mistake in the report.
The accident was due to carelessness rather than bad luck.
Repeated carelessness can harm your reputation at work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: negligence - failure to take proper care in doing something
  • Antonym: attentiveness - the quality of paying close attention

Etymology

From 'careless' + '-ness'; careless from Middle English, from care + -less.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɛr.ləs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈkeə.ləs.nəs / (BrE)
lack of attention or consideration, resulting in mistakes or accidents; the quality of not being careful.
lack of care or attention

Examples

Her carelessness while driving almost caused an accident.
A moment of carelessness can sometimes lead to lifelong regrets.
The report blamed the spill on the carelessness of the workers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: negligence - failure to take proper care in doing something
  • Antonym: attentiveness - the quality of being attentive or paying close attention

Etymology

From 'careless' + '-ness', modelled after Old English patterns for forming nouns from adjectives.
