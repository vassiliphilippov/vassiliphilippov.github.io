Merrimac

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɛrɪˌmæk / (AmE), / ˈmɛrɪˌmæk / (BrE)
the ironclad warship built by the Confederate forces during the American Civil War to break the Union blockade, originally the USS Merrimack and later renamed CSS Virginia
Confederate ironclad warship (Civil War)

Examples

The battle between the Merrimac and the Monitor marked a new era of naval warfare.
Historians often debate the strategic impact of the Merrimac's engagement in Hampton Roads.
Visitors to the museum can view a model of the famous Merrimac.

Etymology

Originally USS Merrimack, named after the Merrimack River in New Hampshire and Massachusetts.
