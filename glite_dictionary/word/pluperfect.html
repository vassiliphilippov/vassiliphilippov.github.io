pluperfect

1. NOUN

/ ˌpluˈpɝː.fɪkt / (AmE), / ˌpluːˈpɜː.fɪkt / (BrE)
a verb tense used to express an action that was completed before another past action or time
grammatical tense for completed action in the past

Examples

The pluperfect is often introduced in advanced grammar lessons.
In the sentence 'She had left', the verb is in the pluperfect.
French verbs require a pluperfect form in certain subordinate clauses.

Synset Examples

‘I had finished’ is an example of the past perfect

Related Terms

  • Synonym: past perfect - grammatical tense indicating completed action before another past event

Etymology

From Latin 'plus quam perfectum' meaning 'more than perfect'.

2. ADJ

/ pluˈpɝː.fɛkt / (AmE), / pluːˈpɜː.fɪkt / (BrE)
exceeding perfection; more than perfect, often used to emphasize an extreme degree of accuracy or excellence.
more than perfect; extremely precise or excellent

Examples

Her pluperfect manners impressed even the most critical guests at the table.
The report was written with pluperfect attention to every detail.
He delivered his lines with pluperfect confidence, leaving the audience captivated.

Synset Examples

he spoke with pluperfect precision

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: perfect - completely free from faults or defects; as good as possible
  • Antonym: imperfect - not perfect; having faults or errors

Etymology

From Latin 'plus quam perfectum' (more than perfect).
