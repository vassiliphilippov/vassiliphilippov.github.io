Scolymus hispanicus

1. NOUN

/ skoʊˈlaɪməs hɪˈspænɪkəs / (AmE), / skɒˈlɪməs hɪˈspanɪkəs / (BrE)
a golden thistle native to southwestern Europe, cultivated for its edible sweet roots, leaves, and stalks, and sometimes used as a saffron substitute
a golden thistle with edible parts, used like saffron

Examples

In parts of Spain, Scolymus hispanicus is gathered wild and added to traditional stews.
The yellow flowers of Scolymus hispanicus are a popular local substitute for saffron in some cuisines.
Gardeners cultivate Scolymus hispanicus for its edible stalks and sweet roots.

Etymology

From Latin 'Scolymus' (a kind of thistle) and 'hispanicus' (Spanish) indicating its origin.
