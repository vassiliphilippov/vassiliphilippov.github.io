myositis

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪ.əˈsaɪ.tɪs / (AmE), / ˌmaɪ.əˈsaɪ.tɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of muscle tissue, often causing pain, swelling, and weakness
inflammation of muscle tissue
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with myositis after experiencing persistent muscle pain and weakness.
Certain infections can lead to myositis, causing inflammation in various muscle groups.
Doctors prescribed medication to help reduce the myositis and relieve the patient's symptoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muscle - a tissue composed of fibers capable of contracting
  • Degree Lower: tendinitis - inflammation of a tendon

Etymology

From Greek 'mys' (muscle) and '-itis' (inflammation).
