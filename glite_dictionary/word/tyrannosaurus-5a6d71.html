tyrannosaurus

1. NOUN

/ tɪˌrænəˈsɔːrəs / (AmE), / tɪˌrænəˈsɔːrəs / (BrE)
a large carnivorous bipedal dinosaur with massive teeth, native to late Cretaceous North America
large carnivorous dinosaur

Examples

The fossilized skull of a tyrannosaurus was unearthed by paleontologists in Montana.
Many children are fascinated by the fearsome appearance of the tyrannosaurus in museum exhibits.
The movie depicted a fierce tyrannosaurus chasing a herd of smaller dinosaurs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tyrannosaurus rex - the species Tyrannosaurus rex, the largest and most famous of the tyrannosauruses

Etymology

From Greek ‘tyrannos’ (tyrant) + ‘sauros’ (lizard).
