swoon

1. NOUN

/ swun / (AmE), / swuːn / (BrE)
a temporary loss of consciousness; a fainting spell, often caused by insufficient blood to the brain
a brief fainting or loss of consciousness

Examples

She fell into a swoon after hearing the shocking news.
The sight of blood made him collapse in a swoon on the floor.
A sudden swoon overtook her during the hot afternoon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faint - a sudden, brief loss of consciousness
  • Synonym: collapse - to fall down or suddenly lose strength or consciousness

Etymology

Middle English 'swounen', from Old English 'swōgan' (to sound, ring, grow faint).

2. VERB

/ swun / (AmE), / swuːn / (BrE)
to faint, especially from extreme emotion or physical weakness
to faint from emotion or weakness

Examples

She was so overcome with joy that she began to swoon in his arms.
The sight of blood made him swoon unexpectedly during the procedure.
As the heat rose in the crowded hall, several people started swooning and needed assistance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faint - to lose consciousness for a short time because of a temporarily insufficient supply of oxygen to the brain

Etymology

From Middle English *swounen* (to faint), related to Old English *swōgan* (to make a noise, swoon).
