geartrain

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪr.treɪn / (AmE), / ˈɡɪə.treɪn / (BrE)
a set of interconnected gears through which force, motion, or torque is transmitted or modified
set of interconnected gears

Examples

The mechanic inspected the geartrain to identify the source of the noise.
A malfunction in the geartrain caused the machine to stop working suddenly.
Modern bicycles often use a complex geartrain to improve efficiency and speed.

Synset Examples

the fool got his tie caught in the geartrain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gearbox - a casing containing a set of gears; a transmission system

Etymology

From 'gear' (mechanical part) + 'train' (series of connected parts).
