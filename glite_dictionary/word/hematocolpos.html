hematocolpos

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːmətoʊˈkɑlpəs / (AmE), / ˌhiːmətəʊˈkɒlpɒs / (BrE)
the accumulation of menstrual blood in the vagina, usually caused by an imperforate hymen or other blockage
accumulation of menstrual blood in the vagina
medical

Examples

The ultrasound revealed a hematocolpos as the cause of the patient's abdominal pain.
A hematocolpos is often diagnosed in adolescent girls presenting with primary amenorrhea and cyclic pelvic pain.
Surgical intervention was needed to relieve the obstruction caused by hematocolpos.

Etymology

From Greek haima (blood) + kolpos (vagina, hollow).
