to distil

1. VERB

/ dɪˈstɪl / (AmE), / dɪˈstɪl / (BrE)
to purify a liquid by heating it until it becomes a vapor and then cooling it so that it condenses, separating it from less volatile substances
purify a liquid by distillation

Examples

The laboratory technician carefully distilled the sample to remove any impurities.
Rainwater can be distilled to make it safe for drinking in emergencies.
The company distils essential oils from fresh herbs using specialized equipment.

Synset Examples

purify the water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to purify - to remove contaminants from a substance, making it pure
  • Synonym: to refine - to remove impurities or unwanted elements from a substance

Etymology

From Latin 'destillare' (to drip down), via Old French 'destiller'; modern spelling influenced by Latin.

2. VERB

/ dɪˈstɪl / (AmE), / dɪˈstɪl / (BrE)
(of a vapor or gas) to undergo condensation and change into liquid drops, especially as part of a purification process.
condense from vapor to liquid

Examples

During the laboratory process, the ethanol begins to distil as the temperature rises.
Rain forms when water vapor in the atmosphere cools and distils into droplets.
The chemist demonstrated how to distil pure water from a contaminated source.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to condense - change from a gas to a liquid

Etymology

3. VERB

/ dɪˈstɪl / (AmE), / dɪˈstɪl / (BrE)
to produce or give off a liquid, often drop by drop, especially as a result of condensation or exudation
to give off or emit a liquid

Examples

After hours in the humid room, water began to distil from the cold metal pipes.
Drops of sap distil slowly from the bark of wounded trees.
The ceiling seemed to distil moisture during the rainy season, making the room damp.

4. VERB

/ dɪˈstɪl / (AmE), / dɪˈstɪl / (BrE)
to undergo the process of distillation; to be purified or concentrated by distillation

Examples

During the process, impurities separate while pure substances slowly distil into the collection vessel.
