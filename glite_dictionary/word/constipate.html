to constipate

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑn.stəˌpeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.stɪ.peɪt / (BrE)
to cause someone to become unable to easily pass stools or to become constipated
cause to be constipated

Examples

Some medications can constipate patients, so doctors often recommend a high-fiber diet.
Eating too much cheese may constipate you if you're not careful.
The lack of water in his diet began to constipate him after a few days.

Synset Examples

These foods tend to constipate you

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to relieve - to cause the opposite effect, to ease constipation

Etymology

From Latin 'constipatus', past participle of 'constipare' (to crowd together, to press).

2. VERB

/ ˈkɑnstəˌpeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒnstɪpeɪt / (BrE)
to impede or block the normal movement or flow of something, especially to clog or obstruct; also used figuratively to mean to render sluggish or inactive
impede or clog (literal or figurative)

Examples

Traffic is often constipated by construction on this busy street.
Too much paperwork can constipate the decision-making process in a company.
His anxiety sometimes constipates his ability to speak clearly in public.

Synset Examples

The market is being clogged by these operations
My mind is constipated today

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clog - to block or become blocked so as to slow movement
  • Antonym: to unclog - to remove a blockage or obstruction

Etymology

From Latin 'constipare' (to crowd together, to press).

Synset POS: v
