pitfall

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪtˌfɔl / (AmE), / ˈpɪtˌfɔːl / (BrE)
a hidden or camouflaged hole designed to trap someone or something, often used as a trap for animals or people
concealed trap in the form of a hole

Examples

The hunter covered the pitfall with leaves and branches to disguise it from animals.
Many wild animals have been captured using a pitfall hidden along their usual paths.
The explorer narrowly avoided falling into a deep pitfall in the dense jungle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trap - a device or enclosure designed to catch and retain animals or people

Etymology

From Middle English pitfall, from pit (“hole”) + fall (“to cause to fall”).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɪtˌfɔl / (AmE), / ˈpɪt.fɔːl / (BrE)
an unexpected difficulty or problem, especially one that is not immediately obvious
an unexpected problem or difficulty

Examples

When starting a new business, it's important to be aware of every possible pitfall.
She navigated the project successfully, avoiding every pitfall along the way.
One common pitfall in dieting is expecting quick results without consistent effort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hazard - a danger or risk
  • Synonym: snag - an unexpected or hidden obstacle or drawback
  • Synonym: obstacle - something that blocks progress or makes things difficult

Etymology

Originally denoting a trap for catching animals; from Middle English 'pit' + 'fall'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
