clepsydra

1. NOUN

/ klɛpˈsɪdrə / (AmE), / klɪpˈsɪdrə / (BrE)
an ancient timekeeping device that measures time by the flow of water, also called a water clock
ancient water clock

Examples

The museum displayed an ancient clepsydra used for measuring time.
Researchers reconstructed a clepsydra to study early timekeeping techniques.
A clepsydra works by allowing water to escape at a constant rate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water clock - a clock that measures time by the flow of water

Etymology

From Greek klepsydra, from kleptein (to steal) + hydor (water), meaning 'water thief'.
