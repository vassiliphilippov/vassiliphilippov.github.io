worldliness

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɝːldlinɪs / (AmE), / ˈwɜːldlinɪs / (BrE)
Concern with material or worldly affairs, often to the neglect of spiritual or moral values.
concern with worldly or material affairs

Examples

She rejected the worldliness of city life and sought spiritual fulfillment.
His sermons warned against excessive worldliness and encouraged a focus on faith.
Monks often take vows to renounce worldliness and dedicate themselves to prayer.

Synset Examples

he disliked the worldliness of many bishops around him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: materialism - focus on material possessions and physical comfort over spiritual values
  • Antonym: spirituality - concern with the soul, spirit, or religious values over material matters

Etymology

From 'worldly' + '-ness', patterned after 'worldly' (relating to the material world).

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɝːld.li.nəs / (AmE), / ˈwɜːld.li.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being experienced, sophisticated, and knowledgeable about life and worldly matters, often as a result of education or experience
sophistication and practical experience in worldly affairs

Examples

Her worldliness made her an insightful advisor to those less experienced.
The novel captures the worldliness of someone who has traveled extensively and seen much of life.
Despite his youth, his worldliness was evident in how he handled complex situations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sophistication - having a refined knowledge of the world
  • Antonym: naivety - lack of experience, wisdom, or judgment

Etymology

From 'worldly' + '-ness', denoting the state or quality of being worldly.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04887468-n
Synset POS: n
