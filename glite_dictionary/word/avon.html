Avon

1. PROPN

/ ˈeɪ.vɑn / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.vən / (BrE)
A river in southwestern England that rises in Gloucestershire, flows through Bristol, and empties into the estuary of the Severn.
river in southwestern England

Examples

The city of Bristol is situated on the banks of the Avon.
After heavy rain, the Avon often threatens to burst its banks.
The Avon is popular with boaters and tourists exploring southwest England.

Etymology

From the Welsh word 'afon', meaning 'river'.

2. PROPN

/ ˈeɪ.vɑn / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.vən / (BrE)
a river in central England that flows through Stratford-upon-Avon and empties into the River Severn
river in central England

Examples

The Avon is famous for passing through Shakespeare's birthplace, Stratford-upon-Avon.
Many boats can be seen floating on the Avon during the summer months.
The River Avon eventually joins the Severn near Tewkesbury.

Etymology

From Welsh afon (river), used as a river name in Britain.

3. PROPN

/ ˈeɪvɒn / (AmE), / ˈeɪvən / (BrE)
A former county in southwestern England, named after the river Avon, that existed from 1974 to 1996.
former county in southwestern England
british

Examples

The city of Bristol was once part of Avon county before administrative changes.
Many historical documents still reference Avon as the county for local towns.
She grew up in Avon, close to the banks of the river.

Etymology

From Old Welsh *afon* (river), related to the Welsh word for river.
