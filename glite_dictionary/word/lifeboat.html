lifeboat

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪfˌboʊt / (AmE), / ˈlaɪf.bəʊt / (BrE)
a strong boat kept on a ship or at the shore for rescuing people in danger at sea, especially from sinking ships
rescue boat at sea

Examples

The lifeboat was quickly lowered into the rough sea to save the stranded crew.
All passengers were instructed to proceed calmly to the nearest lifeboat.
The coastguard sent a lifeboat to assist the fishermen caught in the storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rescue boat - a boat used for rescuing people from danger at sea

Etymology

Formed from 'life' + 'boat'; attested since the late 18th century in English.
