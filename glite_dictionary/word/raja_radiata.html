Raja radiata

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪdʒə rædiˈɑːtə / (AmE), / ˈrɑːdʒə reɪdɪˈɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of cold-water bottom-dwelling fish with spines on its back, reaching up to 40 inches in length, also known as the thorny skate.
a cold-water bottom-dwelling fish with back spines

Examples

The Raja radiata is frequently studied by marine biologists due to its unique spiny back.
Fishermen sometimes catch the Raja radiata by accident while trawling for other species.
The diet of Raja radiata consists mainly of small fish and crustaceans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thorny skate - common English name for Raja radiata

Etymology

Latin: 'Raja' (ray, a type of fish) and 'radiata' (radiated, referring to star-shaped markings).
