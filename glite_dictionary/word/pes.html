pes

1. NOUN

/ pɛs / (AmE), / pɛs / (BrE)
the part of the leg of a human or animal below the ankle joint; the foot, especially in anatomical contexts
anatomical term for the foot
medical

Examples

The doctor examined the patient's pes for signs of infection.
Fractures of the pes can impede normal walking and require specialized treatment.
In comparative anatomy, the structure of the pes varies greatly among mammals.

Synset Examples

his bare feet projected from his trousers
armored from head to foot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foot - the lower extremity of the leg below the ankle, on which a person stands or walks

Etymology

From Latin 'pēs' (foot).
