photoretinitis

1. NOUN

/ ˌfoʊtoʊˌrɛtəˈnaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊˌrɛtɪˈnaɪtɪs / (BrE)
A condition in which exposure to sunlight or very bright light causes damage to the retina of the eye.
retinal damage from bright light
medical

Examples

Looking directly at the solar eclipse without protection can cause photoretinitis.
Doctors warn that ultraviolet rays may lead to photoretinitis if eyes are not properly shielded.
Cases of photoretinitis often occur after people stare at the sun for prolonged periods.

Etymology

From Greek phōt- (light) + retinitis (inflammation of the retina).
