vomitus

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɑːmɪtəs / (AmE), / ˈvɒmɪtəs / (BrE)
the material that is expelled from the stomach through the mouth during vomiting
material expelled by vomiting
medical

Examples

The doctor examined the vomitus for signs of blood.
Nurses are trained to record the color and amount of vomitus from patients.
If any unusual odor is noticed in the vomitus, inform the physician immediately.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vomit - to eject matter from the stomach through the mouth

Etymology

From Latin 'vomitus' (vomit, act of vomiting).
