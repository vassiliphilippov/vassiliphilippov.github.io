hysteric

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈstɛrɪk / (AmE), / hɪˈstɛrɪk / (BrE)
a person suffering from hysteria, especially with uncontrollable emotional excess
person with uncontrollable emotional excess
archaic

Examples

In Victorian times, women diagnosed as hysterics were often misunderstood and mistreated.
The doctor suspected that the patient was a hysteric rather than physically ill.
Some novels depict hysterics as being overwhelmed by emotion, regardless of the true nature of their condition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hysterical - affected by or prone to uncontrollable emotion

Etymology

From Greek *hystera* (womb), via Latin and French, reflecting a historical association with hysteria.

2. ADJ

/ hɪˈstɛrɪk / (AmE), / hɪˈstɛrɪk / (BrE)
Characterized by or arising from hysteria; affected by uncontrolled or exaggerated emotion, often as a psychological disorder.
affected by hysteria
medical

Examples

The doctor noted several hysteric reactions among the patients during the crisis.
His hysteric laughter alarmed everyone in the room.
Certain memory disorders, such as hysteric amnesia, can result from extreme psychological stress.

Synset Examples

during hysterical conditions various functions of the human body are disordered
hysterical amnesia

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hysterical - affected by or deriving from wildly uncontrolled emotion

Etymology

From Greek 'hystera' (womb), through Latin 'hystericus,' originally relating to hysteria.
