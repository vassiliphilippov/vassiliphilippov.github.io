2. NOUN

/ ˈmæpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmæpɪŋ / (BrE)
(Mathematics) A function or relation that associates each element of a given set (the domain) with an element of another set (the range).
mathematics: function or association between sets
medical

Examples

A continuous mapping may have a wide range of applications in analysis.
Any linear transformation is a type of mapping between vector spaces.
The teacher explained how a mapping can be used to describe the relationship between two sets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: function - a mathematical relation that assigns exactly one output to each input

Etymology

From 'map' (to correspond or associate) + '-ing'. Derived from mathematical usage in the 20th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈmæpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmæpɪŋ / (BrE)
In genetics, the process of determining the position of genes on a chromosome.
locating genes on a chromosome
medical

Examples

Mapping of the human genome has advanced rapidly over the last two decades.
Gene mapping techniques help scientists trace inherited diseases.
The research team specializes in mapping genes responsible for cancer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gene mapping - identifying the location of genes on chromosomes
  • Synonym: sequencing - determining the order of nucleotides in DNA or RNA

Etymology

From 'map' + -ing; scientific sense developed in mid-20th century.
