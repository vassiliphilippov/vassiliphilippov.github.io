potsherd

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑtˌʃɜrd / (AmE), / ˈpɒtˌʃɜːd / (BrE)
a broken piece or fragment of pottery, often found at archaeological sites
fragment of pottery

Examples

During the excavation, the archaeologists uncovered a potsherd with intricate patterns.
A single potsherd can tell us much about ancient civilizations.
She carefully examined each potsherd in search of clues about the vessel's original shape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shard - a broken piece of ceramic, glass, or metal

Etymology

From Old English *pot* (pot) + *sċeard* (a cut, notch, fragment).
