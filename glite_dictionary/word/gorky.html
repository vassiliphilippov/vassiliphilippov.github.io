Gorky

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡɔrki / (AmE), / ˈɡɔːki / (BrE)
Maxim Gorky (1868–1936), Russian writer known for his plays, novels, and short stories depicting social outcasts.
Russian writer and playwright

Examples

Gorky was a leading figure in the development of Russian literature in the early 20th century.
The works of Gorky often explore themes of poverty and social injustice.
Many critics consider Gorky's play 'The Lower Depths' a masterpiece of modern drama.

Etymology

From Russian Горький, pseudonym of Alexei Maximovich Peshkov.
