sugarberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʊɡɚˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈʃʊɡəbɛri / (BrE)
A small, sweet, edible dark purple to black berry with a large pit, found chiefly in the southern United States.
sweet edible berry of the southern US

Examples

We picked a bucket of sugarberries during our hike in Louisiana.
The sugarberry isn’t sold in most supermarkets because it is delicate and perishable.
Birds love to feed on the ripe sugarberries that fall to the ground in late summer.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsʌɡɚˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈsʌɡəˌbɛri / (BrE)
A deciduous tree of the southern United States with soft yellowish wood and small, sweet, dark berries.
North American tree with small sweet berries

Examples

The sugarberry grows well in sandy soils throughout the lower Mississippi valley.
Birds love eating the ripe sugarberry fruits in late summer.
The furniture was crafted from sugarberry wood, giving it a light golden hue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hackberry - A related tree of the same genus, Celtis, bearing similar berries

Etymology

From 'sugar' + 'berry', referring to the sweet fruit of the tree.
