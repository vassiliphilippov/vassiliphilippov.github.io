generative

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒɛn.əˌreɪ.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛn.ər.ə.tɪv / (BrE)
having the ability to produce new life, offspring, or new things; capable of generating or causing something to develop.
producing new life or offspring

Examples

The generative organs in plants are responsible for producing seeds and fruit.
Her research focuses on the generative processes that lead to the development of new species.
The artist described his work as a generative force, constantly bringing new ideas to life.

Synset Examples

the reproductive potential of a species is its relative capacity to reproduce itself under optimal conditions
the reproductive or generative organs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reproductive - relating to or capable of reproduction

Etymology

From Latin generativus, from generare (to beget, produce).

2. ADJ

/ ˈdʒɛnərətɪv / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnərətɪv / (BrE)
having the power or ability to produce or originate something; able to generate.
having power to produce or originate

Examples

Creativity is a highly generative quality in artists and thinkers.
The generative process of language acquisition fascinates linguists.
New technologies can have a powerful generative impact on society.

Synset Examples

generative power
generative forces

Related Terms

  • Synonym: productive - having the ability to produce large amounts or results
  • Antonym: sterile - incapable of producing offspring or results

Etymology

From Latin generativus, from generare (to beget, produce).
