lecherousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛtʃɚrəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈlɛtʃərəsnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of having or showing strong and excessive sexual desire, often in an inappropriate or lustful way
excessive sexual desire

Examples

The character’s lecherousness made him unpopular among his peers.
She was disturbed by his open lecherousness at the office party.
Novels often portray kings whose lecherousness leads to scandal and downfall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lust - strong sexual desire
  • Antonym: prudery - excessive propriety or modesty in sexual matters

Etymology

From Old French *lecheros* (lustful), from lecher (to lick, indulge in sexual activity).
