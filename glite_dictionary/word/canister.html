canister

1. NOUN

/ ˈkænɪstɚ / (AmE), / ˈkænɪstə / (BrE)
A metallic cylinder filled with shot and used as a type of ammunition in a firearm, especially in historical artillery.
metal cylinder of shot for ammunition

Examples

The cannon was loaded with a canister to maximize damage at close range.
During the battle, soldiers fired canister to break up enemy formations.
A single canister blast was devastating to advancing troops in open fields.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkænɪstər / (AmE), / ˈkænɪstə / (BrE)
A metal container used for storing dry foods such as tea, flour, or sugar.
metal container for storing dry foods

Examples

She keeps the coffee in a large canister on the kitchen counter.
I bought a decorative canister to store my tea leaves.
The flour canister was nearly empty, so I needed to refill it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tin - a metal container used especially for food storage

Etymology

From Latin 'canistrum' (wicker basket), via Greek 'kanastron'.
