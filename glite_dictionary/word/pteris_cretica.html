Pteris cretica

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛrɪs krɪˈtiːkə / (AmE), / ˈtɛrɪs krɛˈtiːkə / (BrE)
A species of fern native to Europe and Asia, commonly cultivated in many varieties as a houseplant.
a fern species grown as a houseplant

Examples

The elegant fronds of the Pteris cretica add a touch of greenery to any living room.
She preferred to grow Pteris cretica in hanging baskets for its decorative foliage.
Botanists recommend Pteris cretica as a low-maintenance indoor plant for beginners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fern - nonflowering vascular plant with fronds and spores

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: genus name 'Pteris' from Greek 'pteris' (fern); species 'cretica' referring to Crete.
