convoy

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːn.vɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.vɔɪ / (BrE)
a group of vehicles, typically motor vehicles, traveling together for support or protection
group of vehicles traveling together

Examples

The supply convoy was escorted by several military vehicles for safety.
A long convoy of trucks stretched along the highway, delivering relief aid to the city.
During the snowstorm, the buses traveled in a convoy to ensure none got stranded.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caravan - a group of people, especially traders or pilgrims, traveling together; mainly used of vehicles or animals

Etymology

From French 'convoi', from Medieval Latin 'convoiare' (to escort).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːnˌvɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.vɔɪ / (BrE)
the act or process of accompanying and protecting vehicles, ships, or people while they are in transit, especially to guard them from danger or attack
group escorting for protection

Examples

A military convoy guarded the trucks as they traveled through the conflict zone.
The ships sailed together in a convoy to ensure their safety.
During wartime, merchant vessels often moved in convoy to avoid submarine attacks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escort - the act of accompanying someone or something for protection or guidance

Etymology

From French 'convoyer', from Medieval Latin 'conviare', meaning 'to escort'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɑnˌvɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.vɔɪ / (BrE)
A group of merchant ships or vehicles traveling together with an escort, especially for protection.
group of ships or vehicles with escort

Examples

During wartime, a convoy of merchant ships set sail under the protection of several destroyers.
The navy organized a convoy to ensure vital supplies reached the mainland safely.
Pirates attacked the convoy at dawn, hoping to capture the valuable cargo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caravan - a group of people or vehicles traveling together, often across a desert
  • Synonym: fleet - a group of ships sailing together; large group of ships

Etymology

From French 'convoi', from Medieval Latin 'conviare' (to escort), from Latin 'com-' (together) + 'via' (way).

/ ˈkɑːn.vɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.vɔɪ / (BrE)
to accompany and protect vehicles, ships, or people as they travel, especially during times of danger
