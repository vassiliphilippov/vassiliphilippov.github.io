Enterobacteriaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛntəroʊˌbæktəriˈeɪsiˌaɪ / (AmE), / ˌɛntərəʊˌbæktəriˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a large family of Gram-negative, rod-shaped bacteria that includes many pathogenic and non-pathogenic species, such as Escherichia coli and Salmonella
family of Gram-negative, rod-shaped bacteria
medical

Examples

Many intestinal infections are caused by members of the Enterobacteriaceae family.
Researchers discovered a new antibiotic-resistant strain within the Enterobacteriaceae.
Doctors routinely test for Enterobacteriaceae when diagnosing urinary tract infections.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'entero-' (intestine), 'bacterium', and Latin '-aceae' (indicating a family).
