to confront

1. VERB

/ kənˈfrʌnt / (AmE), / kənˈfrʌnt / (BrE)
to present someone with something, especially in a way that challenges, accuses, or criticizes
present someone with a challenge or accusation

Examples

The detective confronted the suspect with new evidence during the interview.
She confronted her manager about the unfair treatment she received at work.
When confronted with the facts, he finally admitted his mistake.

Synset Examples

We confronted him with the evidence
He was faced with all the evidence and could no longer deny his actions
An enormous dilemma faces us

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to accuse - to state that someone has done something wrong
  • Synonym: challenge - to invite someone to contest or dispute something
  • Antonym: to ignore - to refuse to acknowledge or deal with something

Etymology

From Latin 'confrontare', from 'com-' (together) + 'frons, front-' (forehead, front).

2. VERB

/ kənˈfrʌnt / (AmE), / kənˈfrʌnt / (BrE)
to face someone or something, especially in a hostile or opposing manner
to face in hostility or competition

Examples

The manager had to confront the employee about repeated lateness.
During the debate, the two candidates confronted each other over policy differences.
She is preparing to confront her fears and speak in public.

Synset Examples

You must confront your opponent
Jackson faced Smith in the boxing ring
The two enemies finally confronted each other

Related Terms

  • Synonym: face - to meet or deal with directly
  • Antonym: to avoid - to keep away from or not confront

Etymology

From Latin confrontare, from com- (together) + frons (forehead, front).

3. VERB

/ kənˈfrʌnt / (AmE), / kənˈfrʌnt / (BrE)
to face or deal with a difficult situation, person, or problem directly and boldly
face or deal with directly

Examples

It's important to confront your fears rather than run from them.
The manager had to confront the team about their poor performance.
She finally confronted the issue that had been troubling her for months.

Synset Examples

You must confront your problems
He faced the terrible consequences of his mistakes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: face - to deal with or accept something unpleasant
  • Antonym: to avoid - to keep away from or not face something difficult

Etymology

From Latin confrontare (to border on, face), from com- (together) + frontem (forehead, front)
