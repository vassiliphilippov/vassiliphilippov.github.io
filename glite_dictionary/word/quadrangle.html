quadrangle

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑːˌdræŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈkwɒˌdræŋɡl / (BrE)
A four-sided polygon; a shape with four straight sides and four angles.
four-sided polygon

Examples

The playground was shaped like a quadrangle with four clearly defined corners.
Any quadrangle can be divided into two triangles by drawing a diagonal line.
A square is a special type of quadrangle with all sides equal and all angles right.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quadrilateral - a four-sided polygon
  • Degree Lower: triangle - a three-sided polygon
  • Degree Higher: pentagon - a five-sided polygon

Etymology

From Latin 'quadrangulum' (four-cornered).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑːˌdræŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈkwɒˌdræŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
a rectangular courtyard or open space surrounded on all four sides by buildings, especially in a school or college
rectangular courtyard enclosed by buildings

Examples

The students gathered in the quadrangle between classes to socialize and enjoy the sunshine.
Our dormitory rooms overlook the college quadrangle, giving us a beautiful view of the lawns.
A statue stands in the center of the ancient quadrangle, surrounded by ivy-covered walls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courtyard - an open space surrounded by walls or buildings

Etymology

From Latin 'quadrangulum' (having four angles), from 'quattuor' (four) and 'angulus' (angle).
