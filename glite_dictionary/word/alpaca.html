alpaca

1. NOUN

/ ælˈpækə / (AmE), / ælˈpækə / (BrE)
a domesticated South American mammal with long silky fleece, related to the llama and valued for its wool
South American mammal valued for its wool

Examples

The farm raises alpacas for their luxurious wool.
She wore a sweater made from pure alpaca.
Many travelers visit Peru to see wild alpaca herds grazing in the Andes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: llama - a domesticated South American mammal related to the alpaca

Etymology

From Spanish 'alpaca', from Aymara or Quechua 'allpaca', referring to the animal.

2. NOUN

/ ælˈpækə / (AmE), / ælˈpækə / (BrE)
the fine, soft wool obtained from the alpaca, used especially for making high-quality textiles and garments
fine wool from the alpaca

Examples

The luxurious scarf was made from pure alpaca and felt incredibly soft against my skin.
Traditional Peruvian garments often feature distinctive patterns woven with dyed alpaca.
Because alpaca is warmer than sheep's wool, it's popular in winter clothing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alpaca - a South American mammal valued for its wool
  • Synonym: wool - the soft, thick hair of sheep and some other animals, or the textile fiber made from it

Etymology

From Spanish 'alpaca', borrowed from Aymara or Quechua 'allpaca', referring to the animal.

3. NOUN

/ ælˈpækə / (AmE), / ælˈpækə / (BrE)
A thin, glossy fabric made from the wool of the alpaca or from a rayon or cotton imitation of such wool.
thin, glossy fabric from alpaca wool or imitation

Examples

She wore a stylish jacket made of alpaca for the evening party.
The store specializes in luxury textiles like alpaca and cashmere.
His suit was tailored from fine black alpaca, giving it a subtle sheen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alpaca - a domesticated South American mammal valued for its wool
  • Synonym: mohair - fabric made from the hair of the Angora goat

Etymology

From Spanish 'alpaca', from Aymara or Quechua 'allpaca', originally referring to the animal.
