knitwork

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɪtˌwɝk / (AmE), / ˈnɪtˌwɜːk / (BrE)
Needlework made by interlacing yarn into a series of connected loops, typically using straight, eyeless needles or by machine.
fabric made by interlacing yarn loops

Examples

She admired the intricate knitwork of her grandmother's handmade scarf.
Modern machines can produce knitwork much faster than by hand.
The gallery displayed several pieces of traditional knitwork from different cultures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: knitting - the craft or process of making fabric by interlocking yarn with needles
  • Antonym: crochet - needlework made by looping yarn with a hooked needle

Etymology

Compound of 'knit' + 'work'; 'knit' from Old English 'cnyttan' (to tie in a knot).
