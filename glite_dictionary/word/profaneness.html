profaneness

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈfeɪn.nəs / (AmE), / prəˈfeɪn.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being profane; irreverence or lack of holiness
state of being profane; irreverence

Examples

The profaneness of his remarks shocked the deeply religious audience.
Many feared that the temple would be tainted by profaneness and disrespect.
The novel explores the tension between sacredness and profaneness in modern society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: profanity - language or behavior showing disrespect for sacred things
  • Antonym: sacredness - the quality of being sacred or holy

Etymology

From Latin profanus (outside the temple, not sacred) via Middle English, from profane + -ness.

2. NOUN

/ prəˈfeɪn.nəs / (AmE), / prəˈfeɪn.nɪs / (BrE)
lack of reverence or respect, especially toward sacred things or a divinity; irreverence.
irreverence toward the sacred

Examples

Many were disturbed by the profaneness of his jokes about religious traditions.
The profaneness in her remarks shocked the deeply religious audience.
He was reprimanded for the profaneness he displayed during the sacred ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irreverence - lack of respect for people or things that are generally taken seriously
  • Synonym: blasphemy - the act of insulting or showing contempt for God or sacred things
  • Antonym: reverence - deep respect for someone or something, especially the sacred

Etymology

From Latin 'profanus' (not sacred, outside the temple), via Old French 'profane'.
