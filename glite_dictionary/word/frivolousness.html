frivolousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrɪvələsnəs / (AmE), / ˈfrɪvələsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being frivolous; a lack of seriousness or sense
lack of seriousness; silliness

Examples

Her frivolousness during the meeting annoyed her more serious colleagues.
The judge rebuked the lawyer for the frivolousness of his arguments.
A moment of frivolousness can brighten a stressful day.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: seriousness - the quality of being serious
  • Synonym: levity - lightness of manner or speech, especially when inappropriate

Etymology

From frivolous + -ness, with 'frivolous' from Latin 'frivolus' (worthless, trifling).
