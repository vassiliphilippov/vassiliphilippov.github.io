Passeriformes

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæs.ə.rɪˈfɔr.miːz / (AmE), / ˌpæs.ər.ɪˈfɔː.miːz / (BrE)
the largest order of birds, including perching birds such as sparrows, finches, robins, and swallows
order of perching birds

Examples

The Passeriformes include more than half of all bird species.
Scientists study the vocalization patterns in Passeriformes to learn about animal communication.
Robins, wrens, and swallows are all classified within the order Passeriformes.

Etymology

From Latin 'passer' (sparrow) + '-iformes' (having the form of), used for taxonomic orders.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpæsərɪˈfɔrmiːz / (AmE), / ˌpæsərɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
the largest order of birds, comprising about half of all known bird species, including rooks, finches, sparrows, tits, warblers, robins, wrens, and swallows
largest order of birds; perching birds

Examples

Over half of all bird species belong to the Passeriformes, making them the largest avian order.
Sparrows and swallows are both members of the Passeriformes order.
Scientists study Passeriformes to understand the diversity and evolution of songbirds.

Etymology

From Latin *passer* (sparrow) + *-iformes* (having the form of), used in taxonomy.
