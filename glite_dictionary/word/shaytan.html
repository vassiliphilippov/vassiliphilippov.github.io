shaytan

1. NOUN

/ ʃaɪˈtɑn / (AmE), / ʃeɪˈtɑːn / (BrE)
(in Islam) a rebellious jinni who leads humans astray; an evil spirit or demon.
evil jinni in Islamic belief

Examples

In the story, a cunning shaytan tried to tempt the man away from his faith.
Some believe that whisperings from a shaytan can lead people to make poor decisions.
The imam spoke about the ways a shaytan can try to influence believers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devil - (in various religions) an evil spirit or supernatural being
  • Synonym: jinni - a spirit or supernatural being in Islamic and Arabic tradition

Etymology

From Arabic *shayṭān* (devil, demon).
