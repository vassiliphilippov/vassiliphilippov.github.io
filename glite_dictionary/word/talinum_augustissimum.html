Talinum augustissimum

1. NOUN

/ təˈlaɪnəm ɔːˌɡʌstəˈsɪməm / (AmE), / təˈlaɪnəm ɔːˌɡʌstəˈsɪməm / (BrE)
A species of flowering plant found in the southwestern United States, similar to Talinum aurantiacum but with narrower leaves and yellow-orange flowers.
a southwestern US plant with narrow leaves and yellow-orange flowers

Examples

Talinum augustissimum is native to arid regions in the southwestern United States.
Botanists distinguish Talinum augustissimum from related species by its narrower leaves and flower color.
Gardeners value Talinum augustissimum for its drought tolerance and distinctive yellow-orange blossoms.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Talinum aurantiacum - a closely related species of Talinum with broader leaves and orange flowers

Etymology

From Latin; 'Talinum' is the genus name, 'augustissimum' meaning 'most august' or 'most venerable.'
