unconventional

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.kənˈvɛn.ʃə.nəl / (AmE), / ˌʌnkənˈvɛnʃənl / (BrE)
not based on or conforming to what is generally done, accepted, or considered legal, moral, or normal.
not conforming to accepted customs or rules

Examples

The artist was known for his unconventional approach to modern sculpture.
She wore an unconventional dress that caught everyone's attention at the gala.
Their unconventional lifestyle often sparked curiosity and sometimes criticism among their neighbors.

Synset Examples

an unconventional marriage
improper banking practices

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conventional - following traditional or accepted norms or standards

Etymology

From un- (not) + conventional; based on Latin 'conventionem' (assembly, agreement).
