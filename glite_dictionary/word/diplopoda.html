Diplopoda

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪpləˈpoʊdə / (AmE), / ˌdɪpləˈpəʊdə / (BrE)
a class of arthropods characterized by elongated bodies composed of numerous double segments, each bearing two pairs of legs; millipedes
class of arthropods, the millipedes

Examples

The class Diplopoda includes all species commonly known as millipedes.
Scientists observed several unique species within Diplopoda in the tropical rainforest.
Unlike centipedes, members of Diplopoda have two pairs of legs on each body segment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: millipede - a myriapod arthropod with many body segments and two pairs of legs per segment

Etymology

From New Latin Diplopoda, from Greek diploos (double) + pous, podos (foot).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈplɑːpədə / (AmE), / dɪˈplɒpədə / (BrE)
A class of arthropods known as millipedes, characterized by bodies with numerous double body segments, each bearing two pairs of legs.
class of arthropods: millipedes

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species within the Diplopoda in the South American rainforest.
The Diplopoda have a unique defense mechanism that involves secreting chemicals when threatened.
Students studied the anatomy of Diplopoda during their zoology lab.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: millipede - a type of arthropod with many legs, member of class Diplopoda

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Greek diplos (double) and pous, pod- (foot).
