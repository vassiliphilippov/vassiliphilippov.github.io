Scolytus

1. NOUN

/ skoʊˈlaɪtəs / (AmE), / skɒˈlɪtəs / (BrE)
A genus of small bark beetles, many of which are important pests of trees.
genus of bark beetles

Examples

The Scolytus genus includes several beetle species that attack elm trees.
Researchers found evidence of Scolytus activity in the infested wood samples.
Several forests have suffered damage due to Scolytus populations increasing rapidly.

Etymology

From New Latin, derived from Greek *skolytos* (a kind of worm).

2. NOUN

/ ˈskoʊ.lɪ.təs / (AmE), / ˈskɒl.ɪ.təs / (BrE)
A genus of small bark beetles, which is the type genus of the family Scolytidae.
a genus of small bark beetles

Examples

Several species within the Scolytus genus are known to attack elm trees.
The Scolytus beetle has been studied for its role in spreading plant diseases.
Forestry experts monitor populations of Scolytus to prevent widespread tree damage.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Scolytus', based on Greek 'skolytos' (gnawed or chewed), referencing the beetles' wood-boring habit.
