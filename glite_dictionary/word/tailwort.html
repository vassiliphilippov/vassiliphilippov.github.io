tailwort

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪlˌwɜrt / (AmE), / ˈteɪlˌwɜːt / (BrE)
A hairy, blue-flowered European annual herb, historically used in herbal medicine and eaten raw as salad greens or cooked like spinach.
European herb used as salad green

Examples

In her garden, she grew tailwort to add a fresh, herbal note to her salads.
Traditional recipes sometimes called for tailwort to be cooked like spinach.
Herbalists valued tailwort for its supposed medicinal properties throughout Europe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: borage - another name for this plant
