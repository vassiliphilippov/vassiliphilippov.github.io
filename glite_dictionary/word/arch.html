arch

1. NOUN

/ ɑrtʃ / (AmE), / ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
a curved structure of masonry or stone built to span an opening and support the weight above it
curved structure supporting weight over an opening

Examples

The stone arch above the doorway has stood for centuries.
Many bridges are supported by a series of arches that distribute their weight evenly.
Roman engineers perfected the use of the arch in their vast buildings and aqueducts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vault - arched structure forming a roof or ceiling

Etymology

From Latin arcus (bow, arch).
