forking

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːkɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of dividing into two or more branches or parts
the act of branching or dividing

Examples

The forking of the river created several small islands.
Computer scientists often discuss the forking of processes in operating systems.
The forking pattern in tree branches was evident as spring arrived.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: branching - division into branches; a similar act of splitting

Etymology

From the verb 'fork' (to divide into branches) + -ing.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːkɪŋ / (BrE)
a place where something, such as a path, river, or branch, divides into two or more parts
a place where something splits into branches

Examples

The forking of the river created a small island in the middle of the water.
They stood at the forking of the mountain trail, unsure which direction to take.
The unusual tree had a prominent forking just above the ground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: junction - a place where things join or meet

Etymology

From the verb 'fork,' ultimately from Old English 'forc' (pitchfork), influenced by Latin 'furca' (fork).
