Ictalurus

1. NOUN

/ aɪkˈtæljʊrəs / (AmE), / ɪkˈtaljʊərəs / (BrE)
A genus of North American freshwater catfishes, including the channel catfish, widely found in lakes and rivers.
genus of North American catfishes

Examples

The genus Ictalurus includes several species of catfish native to North America.
Scientists studied the habitat preferences of Ictalurus in the Mississippi River basin.
The popular channel catfish is classified under the genus Ictalurus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catfish - a freshwater or marine fish with whisker-like barbels around the mouth

Etymology

From Greek 'ichthys' (fish) and 'ailouros' (cat), referring to catfish.
