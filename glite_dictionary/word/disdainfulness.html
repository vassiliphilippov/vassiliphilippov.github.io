disdainfulness

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈdeɪn.fəl.nəs / (AmE), / dɪsˈdeɪn.fəl.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of showing contempt or arrogance toward others considered inferior
arrogant or contemptuous attitude

Examples

Her disdainfulness was evident in the way she addressed her coworkers as if they were beneath her.
The teacher's disdainfulness made the students reluctant to participate in class discussions.
His disdainfulness toward the less experienced team members alienated him from the group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contemptuousness - the quality of showing contempt
  • Synonym: arrogance - an attitude of superiority manifested in an overbearing manner
  • Antonym: respectfulness - the quality of showing respect

Etymology

From 'disdain' + '-fulness'; 'disdain' from Old French 'desdaing', from Latin 'dignari' (to deem worthy).
