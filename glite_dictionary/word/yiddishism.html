Yiddishism

1. NOUN

/ ˈjɪdɪʃˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈjɪdɪʃɪzəm / (BrE)
A word, phrase, or linguistic feature characteristic of Yiddish, especially one adopted into another language.
word or feature from Yiddish used in another language

Examples

The word schmooze is a common Yiddishism in English speaking communities.
Many New York newspapers feature at least one Yiddishism in their headlines every week.
His use of Yiddishism adds a colorful, expressive quality to his writing.

Synset Examples

‘schmooze’ is an example of a Yiddishism.

Etymology

Formed from 'Yiddish' + '-ism', following patterns for naming linguistic borrowings.
