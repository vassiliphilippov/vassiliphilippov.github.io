apical

1. ADJ

/ ˈeɪ.pɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈæp.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
relating to or situated at the apex or tip of something, especially in anatomical or botanical contexts.
at or relating to an apex or tip

Examples

The apical bud of the plant controls its vertical growth.
Damage to the apical region of the heart affected its function.
In phonetics, an apical sound is made with the tip of the tongue.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: basal - relating to or forming the base, especially in biological contexts

Etymology

From Latin apex (apex, summit) + -al (adjectival suffix).
