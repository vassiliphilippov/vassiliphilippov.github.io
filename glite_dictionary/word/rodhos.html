Rodhos

1. PROPN

/ ˈroʊdoʊs / (AmE), / ˈrəʊdɒs / (BrE)
A Greek island in the southeast Aegean Sea, near the Turkish coast, known as the largest of the Dodecanese and site of the Colossus of Rhodes.
Greek island in the Aegean Sea

Examples

Many tourists visit Rodhos each year for its beaches and historical sites.
Rodhos was once the location of the famous Colossus, one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World.
The Dodecanese islands include Rodhos, which has a rich blend of Greek and Ottoman influences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rhodes - the more common English name for the Greek island Rodhos

Etymology

From Greek Ρόδος (Ródhos), the Greek name for Rhodes.
