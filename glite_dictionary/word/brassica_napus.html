Brassica napus

1. NOUN

/ brəˈsɪkə ˈneɪpəs / (AmE), / brəˈsɪkə ˈneɪpəs / (BrE)
A Eurasian plant cultivated for its seeds, which are used to produce rapeseed oil, and also grown as a forage crop.
plant grown for seeds and forage

Examples

Brassica napus is commonly grown for the production of canola oil.
Farmers rotate wheat with Brassica napus to improve soil health.
Extensive fields of Brassica napus bloom with bright yellow flowers in the spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rapeseed - seed of Brassica napus, used to produce oil

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Brassica' (cabbage) and species name 'napus' (turnip).
