to cinematize

1. VERB

/ ˈsɪnəməˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪnəmətaɪz / (BrE)
to make a film of something or adapt it so as to produce a film
make or adapt into a film

Examples

Many classic novels have been cinematized over the past century.
The director hopes to cinematize the best-selling historical saga next year.
Efforts to cinematize complex philosophical ideas often meet with mixed success at the box office.

Synset Examples

cinematize history

Related Terms

  • Synonym: film - to make a movie of something
  • Synonym: to adapt - to change something for a new use, especially for film or television

Etymology

From cinema + -ize, meaning to convert into or employ as cinema.
