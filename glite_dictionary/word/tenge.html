tenge

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛŋgeɪ / (AmE), / ˈtɛŋgeɪ / (BrE)
the basic unit of currency in Kazakhstan
currency of Kazakhstan

Examples

The price of a loaf of bread in Almaty is around 200 tenge.
She exchanged her dollars for tenge before traveling to Kazakhstan.
The value of the tenge fell sharply against the dollar last year.

Etymology

From Kazakh теңге (teńge), ultimately from Turkic languages meaning 'balance' or 'scales'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛnɡeɪ / (AmE), / ˈtɛŋɡɛ / (BrE)
the basic monetary unit of Kazakhstan, equal to 100 tiyn.
currency of Kazakhstan

Examples

I exchanged my dollars for tenge when I arrived in Almaty.
The price of the hotel room was listed in tenge rather than US dollars.
She collected coins from around the world, including a few Kazakh tenge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruble - the basic unit of money in Russia and some other former Soviet states
  • Synonym: dollar - the basic unit of money in the United States, Canada, Australia, and some other countries

Etymology

From Kazakh *teńge*, meaning 'balance' or 'scale,' related to Russian *ten'ga*, an old Turkic word for money.
