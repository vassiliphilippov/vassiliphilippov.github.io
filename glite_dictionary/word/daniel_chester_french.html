Daniel Chester French

1. PROPN

/ ˈdænjəl ˈtʃɛstər frɛntʃ / (AmE), / ˈdænjəl ˈtʃɛstə frɛntʃ / (BrE)
(1850–1931) An American sculptor best known for creating the seated marble statue of Abraham Lincoln in the Lincoln Memorial, Washington, D.C.
American sculptor, created Lincoln Memorial statue

Examples

Daniel Chester French designed many famous monuments during his prolific career.
The iconic Lincoln Memorial statue was sculpted by Daniel Chester French in the early twentieth century.
Art historians often study the works of Daniel Chester French for their historical and artistic significance.
