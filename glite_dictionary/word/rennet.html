rennet

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛnət / (AmE), / ˈrɛnɪt / (BrE)
A substance, often obtained from the stomachs of young mammals, that causes milk to coagulate and is used in making cheese and junket.
substance that curdles milk for cheese

Examples

The cheese maker added rennet to the warm milk to start the cheese-making process.
Vegetarian cheeses often use microbial rennet instead of animal-based enzymes.
Traditional junket desserts require a small amount of rennet to achieve the right texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coagulate - to thicken or clot, especially by curdling

Etymology

From Old English 'rynet', related to runian (to coagulate).
