octogenarian

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑktədʒəˈnɛriən / (AmE), / ˌɒktədʒəˈneəriən / (BrE)
a person who is between 80 and 89 years old
person aged 80–89 years

Examples

The retirement home organized a special celebration for its oldest octogenarian.
Despite being an octogenarian, she still runs in local charity races every year.
The mayor congratulated the city’s octogenarian residents during the anniversary parade.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: nonagenarian - person aged 90–99 years
  • Degree Lower: septuagenarian - person aged 70–79 years

Etymology

From Latin octogenarius (containing eighty), from octogeni (eighty each), from octoginta (eighty).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑktədʒəˈnɛriən / (AmE), / ˌɒktədʒəˈneəriən / (BrE)
A person who is between 80 and 89 years old; relating to people in their eighties.
person aged 80–89 years

Examples

The retirement home hosts several lively octogenarians who participate in daily activities.
Despite being an octogenarian, she still enjoys hiking in the mountains.
The author's latest novel recounts the adventures of an octogenarian exploring the world.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: nonagenarian - person aged 90 to 99 years
  • Degree Lower: septuagenarian - person aged 70 to 79 years

Etymology

From Latin 'octogenarius' (containing eighty), from 'octogeni' (eighty each), from 'octoginta' (eighty).
