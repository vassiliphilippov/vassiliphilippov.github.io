anisotropically

1. ADV

/ ˌænɪˌsaɪˈtrɑpɪkli / (AmE), / ˌænɪˌsɒˈtrɒpɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that shows different properties or behaviors in different directions.
in an anisotropic manner

Examples

Light is absorbed anisotropically by certain crystals, depending on the direction of the incident beam.
The material expands anisotropically when exposed to heat, resulting in a change in shape.
Magnetic properties change anisotropically with respect to the orientation of the external field.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: isotropically - in an isotropic (identical in all directions) manner

Etymology

From 'anisotropic' (from Greek 'an-' not + 'isos' equal + 'tropos' turn) + '-ally'.
