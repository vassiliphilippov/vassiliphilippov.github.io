Hippotragus

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈpɑːtrəɡəs / (AmE), / hɪˈpɒtrəɡəs / (BrE)
A genus of large African antelopes that includes the sable antelope and related species.
genus of African antelopes

Examples

The Hippotragus genus includes notable species such as the sable antelope and the roan antelope.
Researchers observed a herd of Hippotragus in the savanna during their field study.
The conservation status of Hippotragus species has become a concern in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sable antelope - a large African antelope with long, curved horns

Etymology

From Greek 'hippos' (horse) + 'tragos' (he-goat), referring to the animal's appearance.
