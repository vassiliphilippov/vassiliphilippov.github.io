breakfast

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɛkfəst / (AmE), / ˈbrɛkfəst / (BrE)
the first meal of the day, typically eaten in the morning
first meal of the day

Examples

We met for breakfast at a little café near the station.
She skipped breakfast because she was running late for work.
Having a healthy breakfast can help you feel energized throughout the day.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: brunch - a meal eaten late in the morning, between breakfast and lunch
  • Degree Higher: lunch - the meal eaten in the middle of the day
  • Degree Higher: dinner - the main meal of the day, usually eaten in the evening

Etymology

From Middle English ‘brekfast’, literally ‘breaking the fast’.

2. VERB

/ ˈbrɛkfəst / (AmE), / ˈbrɛkfəst / (BrE)
to eat breakfast in the morning; to have the first meal of the day
to eat breakfast

Examples

We usually breakfast together before leaving for work.
She breakfasted on toast and coffee every day during her vacation.
Tomorrow, they will breakfast at the hotel before the conference begins.

Synset Examples

We breakfast at seven

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dine - to eat dinner or have the main meal of the day

Etymology

From Middle English *brekfast*, literally ‘to break the fast’.
