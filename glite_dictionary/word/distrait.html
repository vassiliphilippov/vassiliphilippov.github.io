distrait

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈstreɪ / (AmE), / dɪˈstreɪ / (BrE)
Absent-minded, distracted, or inattentive, especially because of anxiety or worry.
distracted or absent-minded

Examples

She seemed rather distrait during the meeting, barely responding to the questions asked.
His distrait expression made it clear he was lost in thought.
Though he tried to focus, Tom remained distrait after receiving the troubling news.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distracted - Unable to concentrate because of worry or other preoccupation
  • Antonym: attentive - Paying close attention to something

Etymology

From French 'distrait', past participle of 'distraire' (to distract), from Latin 'distrahere' (to pull apart, distract).
