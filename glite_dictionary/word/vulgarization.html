vulgarization

1. NOUN

/ ˌvʌlɡərəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvʌlɡərʌɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making something coarse, unrefined, or less sophisticated
making something coarse or less refined

Examples

The vulgarization of popular culture worries some critics who cherish classic standards.
She opposed the vulgarization of the language, believing it deserved more respect.
Many claim that mass media contributes to the vulgarization of artistic traditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coarsening - the process of becoming less refined or more crude

Etymology

From Latin 'vulgaris' (common, ordinary) via French 'vulgarisation'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌvʌlɡərəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvʌlɡəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something more accessible or appealing to the general public, often by simplifying or popularizing it
making something accessible to the public

Examples

The vulgarization of scientific ideas can help non-experts appreciate complex discoveries.
Some critics argue that too much vulgarization reduces the accuracy of the original material.
The museum's exhibits underwent vulgarization to attract a larger audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: popularization - the act of making something popular or easily understood by many people

Etymology

From Latin 'vulgus' (the common people), via French 'vulgarisation'.
