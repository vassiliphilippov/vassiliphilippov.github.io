expectorator

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspɛktəˌreɪtər / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɛktəreɪtə / (BrE)
a medicine that promotes the discharge of mucus from the respiratory tract
medicine that clears mucus from airways
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed an expectorator to help relieve her persistent cough.
Many over-the-counter syrups contain an expectorator to aid in clearing chest congestion.
Using an expectorator can make it easier for patients to expel phlegm from their lungs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expectorant - a medicine that causes the removal of mucus from the lungs and air passages

Etymology

From Latin 'expectorare' (to expel from the chest), via 'expectorate'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspɛktəˌreɪtər / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɛktəreɪtə / (BrE)
a person who spits out saliva or phlegm from the mouth
a person who spits out saliva or phlegm
medical

Examples

The dentist reminded the expectorator to use a tissue when clearing his throat.
In some cultures, being an open expectorator in public is considered rude.
The coach scolded the young player for being an expectorator on the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spitter - a person who spits

Etymology

From Latin expectorare (to expel from the chest); related to 'expectorate'.
