terrene

1. ADJ

/ təˈriːn / (AmE), / təˈriːn / (BrE)
relating to, or inhabiting the land as opposed to the sea or air; earthly.
of the land; earthly

Examples

The explorers documented many terrene species living far from the coast.
She preferred terrene habitats over aquatic ones for her research.
The novel described a mythical conflict between terrene and marine creatures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrestrial - of, on, or relating to the earth or land
  • Antonym: marine - relating to the sea
  • Antonym: aerial - relating to the air

Etymology

From Latin terrenus (of the earth), from terra (earth).

2. ADJ

/ təˈrin / (AmE), / təˈriːn / (BrE)
of or relating to the earth; earthly, as opposed to heavenly or ideal
relating to the earth; earthly
archaic

Examples

Many poets contrast the terrene with the celestial in their verses.
The philosopher questioned the value of terrene pleasures.
He regarded all his terrene possessions as fleeting and unimportant.

Synset Examples

not a fairy palace; yet a mundane wonder of unimagined kind
so terrene a being as himself

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earthly - relating to this world as opposed to a spiritual one
  • Antonym: celestial - relating to the sky or heaven; heavenly, divine

Etymology

From Latin terrenus (of earth), from terra (earth).
