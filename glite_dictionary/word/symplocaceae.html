Symplocaceae

1. NOUN

/ sɪmˌploʊˈkeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / sɪmˌpləʊˈkeɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of dicotyledonous flowering plants, known commonly as the sweetleaf family, found mainly in tropical and subtropical regions.
a family of flowering plants (sweetleaf family)

Examples

Many species in the Symplocaceae family are evergreen trees or shrubs found in moist forests.
The Symplocaceae are notable for their simple leaves and fragrant flowers.
Botanists have revised the classification of Symplocaceae several times over the years.

Etymology

From the genus Symplocos, which is derived from Greek *symplokē* (intertwining).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɪmpləˈkeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌsɪmpləˈkeɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of dicotyledonous flowering plants within the order Ebenales, which includes shrubs and trees found mainly in tropical and subtropical regions.
a family of flowering plants (Ebenales)

Examples

The Symplocaceae family includes many evergreen species native to Asia and the Americas.
Researchers have identified several medicinal compounds in plants belonging to the Symplocaceae.
Ecologists study the distribution of Symplocaceae species in subtropical forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ebenaceae - another family in the order Ebenales

Etymology

From the genus name Symplocos, based on Greek symplokē (intertwined), + the standard botanical suffix -aceae for plant families.
