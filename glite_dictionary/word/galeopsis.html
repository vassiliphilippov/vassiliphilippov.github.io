Galeopsis

1. NOUN

/ ɡəˈliːəpsɪs / (AmE), / ɡaɪˈliːəpsɪs / (BrE)
A genus of erect annual European herbs in the mint family, commonly known as hemp-nettle.
a genus of European annual herbs

Examples

Several species of Galeopsis can be found growing wild in the European countryside.
The medicinal properties of Galeopsis have been studied since the 18th century.
Galeopsis is commonly referred to as hemp-nettle due to its resemblance to stinging nettles.

Etymology

From Greek gale (weasel) + opsis (appearance), referring to the shape of the flower.

2. NOUN

/ ɡeɪˈlɒp.sɪs / (AmE), / ɡeɪˈlɒp.sɪs / (BrE)
a genus of erect annual herbs native to Europe, some species known as hemp-nettles.
a genus of European annual herbs

Examples

Several species of Galeopsis grow wild in European meadows and disturbed ground.
The flowers of Galeopsis are often pink or purple and attract many insects.
Botanists studied the medicinal properties of Galeopsis plants in the laboratory.

Etymology

From Greek gale (weasel) and opsis (appearance), referring to the shape of the flower.
