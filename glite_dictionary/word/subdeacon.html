subdeacon

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˈdiːkən / (AmE), / sʌbˈdiːkən / (BrE)
a member of a former order of clergy ranking below a deacon, historically part of the Western Christian church and some Eastern Catholic Churches
historical church official below a deacon

Examples

In medieval times, the subdeacon played a key role during religious ceremonies.
He was ordained as a subdeacon before advancing to the higher orders.
The duties of a subdeacon have remained largely ceremonial in some Eastern Catholic Churches.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: deacon - a member of the clerical order immediately above subdeacon

Etymology

From Latin subdiaconus, from sub- (under) + diaconus (deacon).
