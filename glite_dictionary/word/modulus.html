modulus

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːdʒələs / (AmE), / ˈmɒdjʊləs / (BrE)
the absolute value of a complex number, equal to the square root of the sum of the squares of its real and imaginary parts
absolute value of a complex number

Examples

To find the modulus of a complex number, you calculate the square root of the sum of the squares of its parts.
The modulus of 3 + 4i is 5.
Engineers often use the modulus when analyzing alternating current circuits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absolute value - the non-negative value of a number without regard to its sign

Etymology

From Latin 'modulus' (a small measure, standard), diminutive of 'modus' (measure).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːd.jʊ.ləs / (AmE), / ˈmɒd.jʊ.ləs / (BrE)
an integer that divides exactly into the difference between two other integers, with no remainder
integer dividing a difference exactly

Examples

The number 3 is a modulus of 7 and 10, since 10 minus 7 is 3.
If a modulus divides evenly into the difference, the numbers are said to be congruent modulo that modulus.
In this case, 6 acts as a modulus for the difference between 12 and 18.

Synset Examples

2 is a modulus of 5 and 9

Related Terms

  • Synonym: divisor - a number by which another number is divided

Etymology

From Latin modulus (small measure), diminutive of modus (measure).

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːdʒələs / (AmE), / ˈmɒdjʊləs / (BrE)
(physics) A numerical value or coefficient that indicates how much of a particular property, such as elasticity or rigidity, is exhibited by a specified material or substance.
physics coefficient of a property in a material
medical

Examples

Scientists measured the modulus of elasticity for the new composite material.
The steel's modulus of rigidity determines its resistance to deformation.
A high bulk modulus means the substance is relatively incompressible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moduli - plural of modulus, used for more than one coefficient or value

Etymology

From Latin modulus (a small measure), diminutive of modus (measure).
