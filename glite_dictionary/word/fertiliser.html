fertiliser

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɝːtɪlaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈfɜːtɪlaɪzə / (BrE)
a substance added to soil to make it more fertile, especially by supplying essential nutrients for plants
substance to make soil more fertile
british

Examples

The farmer spread fertiliser over the fields to improve crop yields.
Many gardeners use organic fertiliser made from compost.
Runoff from excess fertiliser can contaminate nearby rivers and lakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fertilizer - American English spelling: substance for making soil more fertile

Etymology

From 'fertile' + '-iser' (British spelling of 'fertilizer'); 'fertile' from Latin fertilis (fruitful).
