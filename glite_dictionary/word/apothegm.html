apothegm

1. NOUN

/ ˈæpəˌθɛm / (AmE), / ˈapəθɛm / (BrE)
A short, pithy, and instructive saying or maxim.
short instructive saying

Examples

His speech was filled with clever apothegms that made the audience think.
The teacher shared an ancient apothegm to illustrate the lesson.
She admired how her grandmother always had an apothegm for every situation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aphorism - a concise statement of a principle
  • Synonym: maxim - a short statement expressing a general truth or rule of conduct
  • Synonym: proverb - a simple, traditional saying expressing perceived truth

Etymology

From Greek apophthegma (a terse, pointed saying) via Latin apophthegma.
