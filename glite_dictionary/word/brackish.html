brackish

1. ADJ

/ ˈbrækɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbrækɪʃ / (BrE)
(of food, drink, or an experience) distasteful and unpleasant; spoiled by mixture.
distasteful or unpleasant (figurative)

Examples

The medicine had a brackish aftertaste that lingered in his mouth.
Her comments left a brackish feeling in the meeting, spoiling the friendly atmosphere.
The soup was a brackish concoction that no one wanted to finish.

Synset Examples

a thin brackish gruel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unpalatable - not pleasant to taste
  • Antonym: pleasant - enjoyable or satisfying

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *brac* (salty).

2. ADJ

/ ˈbræk.ɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbræk.ɪʃ / (BrE)
(of water) slightly salty, typically because it is a mixture of seawater and fresh water.
slightly salty (of water)

Examples

The estuary contains brackish water where the river meets the sea.
Fish that thrive in brackish environments can often adapt to both saltwater and freshwater.
They built a pier over the brackish lagoon, which was teeming with unique plant life.

Synset Examples

a brackish lagoon
the briny deep

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saline - containing or impregnated with salt
  • Antonym: fresh - not salty, especially of water

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *brac* (salty).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01077750-s
Synset POS: s
