Hulsea nana

1. NOUN

/ hʌlˈsiːə ˈnænə / (AmE), / hʌlˈsiːə ˈnænə / (BrE)
A species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, commonly known as dwarf hulsea, that is similar to but smaller than the alpine hulsea.
a species of flowering plant, dwarf hulsea

Examples

The rocky slopes are covered with Hulsea nana, a hardy plant adapted to high altitudes.
Researchers studied the distribution of Hulsea nana in the mountainous region.
Unlike its relative, Hulsea nana thrives in smaller, more sheltered areas.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Hulsea alpina - a related larger species, alpine hulsea

Etymology

From the genus name Hulsea + Latin nana meaning 'dwarf, small'.
