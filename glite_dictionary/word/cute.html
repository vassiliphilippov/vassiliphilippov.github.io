cute

1. ADJ

/ kjuːt / (AmE), / kjuːt / (BrE)
attractive in a charming, pretty, or endearing way, especially because of smallness or youthfulness
attractive in a pretty or endearing way

Examples

Everyone thought the puppy was cute with its big eyes and floppy ears.
She wore a cute dress to the party last night.
Their new apartment is small but really cute and cozy.

Synset Examples

a cute kid with pigtails
a cute little apartment
cunning kittens
a cunning baby

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretty - pleasing or attractive to the eye in a delicate way
  • Synonym: adorable - inspiring great affection; delightful; charming
  • Antonym: ugly - unpleasant or repulsive in appearance

Etymology

Originally shortened from 'acute', first used in American English in the 18th century.

2. ADJ

/ kjut / (AmE), / kjuːt / (BrE)
(of behaviour or style) deliberately or insincerely designed to be charming or appealing, often in a way that seems artificial or affected.
artificially charming or affected

Examples

Some critics found the film's dialogue cute rather than genuinely funny.
The singer's cute gestures on stage felt forced to many in the audience.
The ad's cute tone put off viewers who preferred something more sincere.

Synset Examples

an insufferably precious performance
a child with intolerably cute mannerisms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: precious - affectedly or overly charming in a way that seems contrived
  • Synonym: affected - artificial, pretended, or insincere in manner
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00149910-s
Synset POS: s
