emesis

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛməsɪs / (AmE), / ˈɛməsɪs / (BrE)
the act or process of vomiting; the reflex of ejecting the contents of the stomach through the mouth
medical term for vomiting
medical

Examples

Nausea and emesis are common side effects of chemotherapy.
The physician administered medication to control the patient's emesis.
Chronic emesis may lead to dehydration if left untreated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vomiting - the act of ejecting stomach contents through the mouth

Etymology

From Greek emesis (vomiting), from emein (to vomit).
