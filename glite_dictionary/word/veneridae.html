Veneridae

1. NOUN

/ vəˈnɛr.ɪˌdi / (AmE), / vəˈnɛr.ɪ.diː / (BrE)
A family of marine bivalve mollusks commonly known as hard-shell clams, found in various coastal regions.
family of hard-shell clams

Examples

The Veneridae are commonly known as hard-shell clams and are widely distributed in coastal waters.
Scientists studied the Veneridae family to understand their role in marine ecosystems.
Many commercially important shellfish species belong to the Veneridae.

Etymology

From New Latin Veneridae, from Venus (genus of clams) + -idae (family suffix in zoology).
