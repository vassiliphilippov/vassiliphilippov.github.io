Prohibition

1. NOUN

/ prəˌhɪˈbɪʃən / (AmE), / prəˌhɪˈbɪʃən / (BrE)
the period from 1920 to 1933 in the United States when the sale, production, and transport of alcoholic beverages was banned by law
U.S. period (1920–33) banning alcohol
american

Examples

The crime rate soared in Chicago during Prohibition.
Many secret bars, known as speakeasies, opened during Prohibition.
The end of Prohibition was marked by the ratification of the Twenty-first Amendment.

Etymology

From Latin 'prohibitus' (forbidden); capitalized for the historical U.S. period.
