prohibition

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊ.hɪˈbɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌprəʊ.ɪˈbɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
the act of officially forbidding something by law, rule, or authority
the act of forbidding something

Examples

The city council voted for a prohibition on smoking in public parks.
Religious prohibition of certain foods is strictly followed in their community.
A prohibition on cell phones in the classroom helped improve students' concentration.

Synset Examples

they were restrained by a prohibition in their charter
a medical inhibition of alcoholic beverages
he ignored his parents' forbiddance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ban - an official or legal prohibition
  • Antonym: allowance - the act of allowing or permitting

Etymology

From Latin 'prohibitio' (a forbidding), from 'prohibere' (to forbid, prevent).

2. NOUN

/ ˌproʊ.hɪˈbɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌprəʊ.hɪˈbɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
an official order that forbids something; the act of formally banning or forbidding an activity by authority
official ban or forbidding of something

Examples

The city council issued a prohibition against smoking in all public parks.
During the early 20th century, the prohibition of alcohol led to the rise of illegal speakeasies.
A national prohibition on ivory trade has helped protect endangered elephants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ban - an official or legal prohibition
  • Antonym: permission - the act of allowing someone to do something

Etymology

From Latin 'prohibitio', from 'prohibere' (to forbid).

4. NOUN

/ ˌproʊ.hɪˈbɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌprəʊ.ɪˈbɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
the period during which the manufacture, sale, and transportation of alcoholic beverages was legally prohibited, especially in the United States from 1920 to 1933; or a law forbidding such practices
law or period forbidding sale of alcohol

Examples

During prohibition, many illegal bars known as speakeasies were established across the United States.
The temperance movement led to the enactment of prohibition in the early twentieth century.
He studied the social consequences of prohibition for his history thesis.

Synset Examples

in 1920 the 18th amendment to the Constitution established prohibition in the US

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ban - an official or legal prohibition

Etymology

From Latin prohibitiōn-, prohibitiō (a forbidding), from prohibēre (to keep off, prevent).
