girth

1. NOUN

/ ɡɝːθ / (AmE), / ɡɜːθ / (BrE)
the measurement around the middle of a person's body, especially the waist; circumference
the measurement around a body; circumference

Examples

Her doctor told her to measure the girth of her waist every week.
The wrestler's impressive girth made him a formidable opponent.
As he gained weight, his girth increased noticeably.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: circumference - the continuous line forming the boundary of a closed geometric figure, esp. a circle

Etymology

From Old Norse 'gyrðr', related to gird (to encircle with a belt).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05108669-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ɡɜrθ / (AmE), / ɡɜːθ / (BrE)
a strap that passes under a horse's belly, fastening a saddle or pack in place
strap holding a horse's saddle

Examples

Before the ride, she checked that the girth was tight enough to keep the saddle secure.
The leather girth broke during the competition, causing the saddle to slip.
It's important to clean the girth regularly to prevent chafing on the horse's skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cinch - a strap used for the same purpose, especially in Western riding

Etymology

From Old Norse *gjörð* (girdle, band).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03035741-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ɡɜrθ / (AmE), / ɡɜːθ / (BrE)
to fasten a saddle on a horse by tying a band (a girth) around its belly
fasten a saddle by tying a band

Examples

The stable hand carefully girthed the horse before the ride.
Make sure to girth the saddle tightly so it doesn't slip.
After girthing the mare, she led it out to the paddock.

Synset Examples

cinch horses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cinch - to fasten a saddle with a strap underneath the horse

Etymology

From Old Norse *gjǫrð* (a girdle, band), related to 'gird.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01305642-v
Synset POS: v
