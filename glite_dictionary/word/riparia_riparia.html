Riparia riparia

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈpɛəriə rɪˈpɛəriə / (AmE), / rɪˈpɛːrɪə rɪˈpɛːrɪə / (BrE)
A small swallow of the Northern Hemisphere, also known as the sand martin or bank swallow, that nests in tunnels dug in clay or sand banks.
species of swallow; sand martin or bank swallow

Examples

A flock of Riparia riparia swooped low over the river, catching insects in flight.
The sandy cliffs along the bank provided perfect nesting sites for Riparia riparia.
During migration season, large numbers of Riparia riparia can be observed gathering near wetlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sand martin - a small migratory swallow; alternative common name for Riparia riparia
  • Synonym: bank swallow - North American common name for Riparia riparia

Etymology

From Latin: 'riparia' meaning 'of the riverbank,' referring to its preferred nesting habitat.
