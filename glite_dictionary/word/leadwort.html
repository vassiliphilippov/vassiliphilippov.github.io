leadwort

1. NOUN

/ ˈlidˌwɝt / (AmE), / ˈliːdˌwɜːt / (BrE)
any of several plants of the genus Plumbago, typically with blue flowers and sometimes cultivated as ornamentals.
plant of the genus Plumbago

Examples

The gardener planted a row of leadwort along the garden path for their striking blue flowers.
Many species of leadwort thrive in sunny, well-drained soil.
Some gardeners prefer leadwort as ground cover due to its attractive foliage and blooms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plumbago - the genus of flowering plants commonly known as leadworts

Etymology

From 'lead' and 'wort' (meaning 'plant' or 'herb'); possibly in reference to ancient beliefs about its medicinal uses with lead poisoning.
