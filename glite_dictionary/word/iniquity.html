iniquity

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈnɪkwəti / (AmE), / ɪˈnɪkwɪti / (BrE)
immoral or grossly unfair behavior
immoral or unfair behavior

Examples

The dictator was known for his iniquity and disregard for human rights.
Religious texts often warn against acts of iniquity committed by individuals or societies.
The citizens united to oppose the iniquity present in the corrupt legal system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wickedness - evil or morally wrong behavior
  • Antonym: virtue - behavior showing high moral standards

Etymology

From Latin 'iniquitas' (unevenness, injustice), from 'iniquus' (unequal, unjust).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈnɪkwəti / (AmE), / ɪˈnɪkwɪti / (BrE)
An act that is morally wrong or grossly unfair; great injustice or wickedness.
unjust or wicked act

Examples

The corruption in the government was seen as a great iniquity by the public.
They fought against every form of social iniquity in their community.
Historians have written about the iniquity of slavery throughout the ages.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: justice - fairness; just behaviour or treatment
  • Synonym: wickedness - the quality of being evil or morally wrong
  • Synonym: sin - an act regarded as a transgression against divine law

Etymology

From Latin 'iniquitas' (unequal, unfair), from 'in-' (not) + 'aequus' (equal).
