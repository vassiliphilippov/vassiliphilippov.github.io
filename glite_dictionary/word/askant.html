askant

1. ADJ

/ əˈskænt / (AmE), / əˈskɑːnt / (BrE)
(of a glance or look) directed to one side, often expressing doubt, suspicion, or envy.
to one side; with suspicion or doubt
archaic

Examples

She gazed askant at the stranger, uncertain whether to trust him.
They cast askant looks at each other when the secret was revealed.
His askant expression made his doubt about the plan obvious.

Synset Examples

her eyes with their misted askance look
sidelong glances

Related Terms

  • Synonym: askance - to one side; with suspicion or doubt
  • Synonym: sidelong - (of a glance) directed sideways or obliquely

Etymology

From Middle English 'askant', of uncertain origin, perhaps related to 'askance'.
