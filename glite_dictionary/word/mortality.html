mortality

1. NOUN

/ mɔrˈtæləti / (AmE), / mɔːˈtælɪti / (BrE)
The condition or fact of being subject to death or the inevitability of dying.
condition of being subject to death

Examples

Many religions grapple with the nature of mortality and what, if anything, comes after death.
The novel explores the mortality of its characters, forcing them to confront the limits of human life.
Despite medical advances, mortality remains an unavoidable aspect of the human experience.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: immortality - the state of being exempt from death; eternal life

Etymology

From Latin mortalitas (mortality, death), from mortalis (mortal).

2. NOUN

/ mɔrˈtæləti / (AmE), / mɔːˈtælɪti / (BrE)
The number of deaths in a given population, scaled to the size of that population, typically expressed per thousand individuals per year.
death rate in a population

Examples

The infant mortality rate has decreased significantly over the past decade.
Researchers are studying the causes of high mortality in certain regions.
Public health initiatives aim to lower mortality through better access to healthcare.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: morbidity - the frequency of disease in a population

Etymology

From Latin 'mortalitas', from 'mortalis' (mortal).
