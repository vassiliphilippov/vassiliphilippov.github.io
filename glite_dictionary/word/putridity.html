putridity

1. NOUN

/ pjuˈtrɪdəti / (AmE), / pjuˈtrɪdəti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being putrid; rottenness or foul decay
state of being putrid or rotten

Examples

The putridity of the decaying garbage was overwhelming.
He recoiled at the putridity coming from the abandoned cellar.
Over time, the putridity of the carcass attracted swarms of flies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: putrescence - the state of undergoing putrefaction
  • Antonym: freshness - the state of being fresh, not decayed

Etymology

From Latin putriditas (rottenness), from putridus (rotten).
