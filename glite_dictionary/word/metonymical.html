metonymical

1. ADJ

/ məˌtɑːnɪˈmɪkəl / (AmE), / mɪˌtɒnɪˈmɪkəl / (BrE)
(of a word or phrase) relating to or characterized by the use of the name of one thing for that of another with which it is closely associated.
relating to metonymy; substituting one thing's name for another's

Examples

The phrase 'the White House issued a statement' is a metonymical expression, standing for the U.S. president or administration.
His writing style is rich in metonymical references, which invite deeper interpretation.
Using 'the crown' as a metonymical term for the monarchy is common in British journalism.

Synset Examples

to say ‘he spent the evening reading Shakespeare’ is metonymic because it substitutes the author himself for the author's works

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metonymic - having the characteristics of metonymy
  • Degree Higher: metaphorical - using figurative language; involving a metaphor

Etymology

From Greek metōnymia (change of name), via Late Latin metonymia and French métonymique.
