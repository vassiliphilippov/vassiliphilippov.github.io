modelled

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɑːdəld / (AmE), / ˈmɒdld / (BrE)
having features, form, or shape that resemble those created by a sculptor; shaped or contoured as if crafted with artistic skill.
sculpted or artistically shaped in form

Examples

Her face had modelled cheekbones that caught the light beautifully.
The statue is admired for its modelled limbs and lifelike expression.
He possessed a physique so modelled it looked as if it had been carved from marble.

Synset Examples

her finely modeled features
rendered with … vivid sculptural effect
the sculpturesque beauty of the athletes' bodies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sculpted - shaped or formed as if by sculpture
  • Degree Lower: angular - having clear, sharply defined outlines or features, especially in a person's face

Etymology

From 'model' (verb) + '-ed'; 'model' from Italian modello, from Latin modulus (measure, standard).
