Picris echioides

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪkrɪs ˌɛkiˈɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / ˈpɪkrɪs ˌiːkɪˈɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A European plant species in the daisy family, known for its spiny, tongue-shaped leaves and yellow flowers; commonly found as a weed and naturalized in the United States.
species of spiny-leaved yellow-flowered weed

Examples

Picris echioides often colonizes roadsides and abandoned fields throughout southern Europe.
Gardeners may find Picris echioides difficult to remove due to its spiny foliage and deep roots.
The yellow flowers of Picris echioides resemble those of the common dandelion.

Etymology

From the genus name Picris (from Greek *pikris*, 'bitter') and species name echioides ('Echium-like'), referring to leaf resemblance.
