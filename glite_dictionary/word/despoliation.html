despoliation

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌspoʊliˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌspəʊliˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of violently stripping or plundering something, especially of valuables or resources.
act of violent stripping or plundering

Examples

The conquering army was notorious for its despoliation of villages along the coast.
Centuries of despoliation left the ancient temples in ruins and stripped of their treasures.
Environmentalists condemned the despoliation of forests by illegal logging operations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plundering - the act of stealing goods, typically using force and in a time of war or civil disorder
  • Antonym: restoration - the act of returning something to a former owner, place, or condition

Etymology

From Latin despoliare (to strip, plunder).
