Tetrahymena

1. NOUN

/ tɛtrəˈhaɪmɪnə / (AmE), / tɛtrəˈhɪmɪnə / (BrE)
A genus of single-celled protozoa characterized by having four membranous ciliary organelles, commonly used in biological research.
genus of single-celled protozoa
medical

Examples

The biologist observed Tetrahymena under the microscope to study its unique ciliary structures.
Tetrahymena has become a valuable model organism in molecular biology research.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Tetrahymena genus during their field study.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'tetra-' (four) and Greek 'hymēn' (membrane).
