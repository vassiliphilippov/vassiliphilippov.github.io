tetrahymena

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛtrəˈhaɪmɪnə / (AmE), / ˌtɛtrəˈhʌɪmɪnə / (BrE)
A genus of single-celled freshwater protozoans related to paramecium, often used as a model organism in genetics and cell biology research.
genus of freshwater protozoans used in research

Examples

Tetrahymena has been instrumental in the study of cilia and cell movement.
Many genetic experiments use tetrahymena because of its simple structure and rapid reproduction.
Researchers examined the life cycle of tetrahymena under a microscope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paramecium - a genus of single-celled ciliated protozoa

Etymology

From Greek tetra- (four) + hymen (membrane), referring to structures observed in the organism.
