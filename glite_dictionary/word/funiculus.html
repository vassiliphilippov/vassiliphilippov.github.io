funiculus

1. NOUN

/ fəˈnɪkjələs / (AmE), / fjʊˈnɪkjʊləs / (BrE)
A cord-like structure in the body, especially one made up of bundles of nerve fibers, vessels, or other tissues.
cord-like anatomical structure
medical

Examples

The spinal cord is enclosed by a funiculus made up of white matter.
A funiculus can carry nerve impulses between different parts of the nervous system.
Her research focused on the dorsal funiculus of the spinal cord.

Etymology

From Latin 'funiculus', meaning 'small rope' (diminutive of 'funis', rope).

2. NOUN

/ fəˈnɪkjələs / (AmE), / fjʊˈnɪkjʊləs / (BrE)
the slender stalk attaching an ovule or seed to the ovary wall in plants
stalk attaching ovule/seed in plants

Examples

The funiculus supplies the developing seed with nutrients from the parent plant.
Botanists observed that the funiculus was unusually thick in this species of flower.
If the funiculus is damaged, the seed may not receive enough nourishment to grow.

Etymology

From Latin 'funiculus' (a slender rope or cord), diminutive of 'funis' (rope).
