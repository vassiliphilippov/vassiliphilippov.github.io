Ziziphus lotus

1. NOUN

/ ˈzɪzɪfəs ˈloʊtəs / (AmE), / ˈzɪzɪfəs ˈləʊtəs / (BrE)
a shrubby deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region, with thorny branches and edible fruit
Mediterranean shrubby tree with edible fruit

Examples

The fruit of the Ziziphus lotus is used in traditional Mediterranean cuisine.
Botanists studied the adaptation of Ziziphus lotus to arid environments.
A dense thicket of Ziziphus lotus provided shelter for small animals.

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Ziziphus' is the genus name and 'lotus' means 'lotus tree'.
