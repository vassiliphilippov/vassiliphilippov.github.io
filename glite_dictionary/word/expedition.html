expedition

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.spəˈdɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌɛk.spɪˈdɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
an organized journey undertaken by a group of people for a particular purpose, especially exploration, research, or war
organized journey for a purpose

Examples

The research team launched an expedition to the Arctic to study climate change.
She joined an expedition in search of ancient ruins deep in the jungle.
The army sent an expedition across the border to secure the territory.

Synset Examples

an expedition was sent to explore Mars

Related Terms

  • Synonym: journey - an act of traveling from one place to another
  • Synonym: trip - a journey or excursion, especially for pleasure
  • Synonym: mission - an important assignment, often involving travel

Etymology

From Latin 'expeditio' (an expedition, a military campaign), from 'expedire' (to free, to prepare).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.spəˈdɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌɛk.spɪˈdɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
A journey undertaken for a specific reason, especially one that is carefully organized and involves a group of people.
journey with a specific purpose

Examples

The team prepared for months before setting out on their expedition to Antarctica.
Scientists launched an expedition into the Amazon rainforest to study rare plants.
Her first expedition was a challenging trek across the Sahara Desert.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exploration - the act of traveling for the purpose of discovery
  • Synonym: mission - an important assignment typically involving travel
  • Antonym: retreat - a withdrawal (opposite in direction or intent to expedition)

Etymology

From Latin expeditio (a removing of impediments, military campaign), from expedire (to make ready).

3. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.spəˈdɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌɛk.spɪˈdɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
A military operation undertaken to achieve a specific goal, especially in a foreign country.
military operation in a foreign land

Examples

The army launched an expedition to secure the coastal region.
Napoleon's Egyptian expedition changed the course of Middle Eastern history.
They planned a military expedition to capture the enemy outpost.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: campaign - a series of military operations intended to achieve a goal

Etymology

From Latin expeditio (an expedition, a setting forth), from expedire (to make ready).

4. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.spəˈdɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌɛk.spɪˈdɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
a journey, often organized, taken for pleasure or recreation
pleasure journey or trip

Examples

Over the summer, the family planned an expedition to the coast for relaxation.
We set out on an expedition to explore the beautiful lakes nearby.
A weekend expedition with friends is a great way to unwind after a busy week.

Synset Examples

many summer excursions to the shore
it was merely a pleasure trip
after cautious sashays into the field

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excursion - a short journey or trip, especially one taken for leisure
  • Synonym: outing - a short trip taken for pleasure or education
  • Antonym: business trip - a journey taken for work-related purposes, not pleasure

Etymology

From Latin expeditio (a military campaign or journey), from expedire (to make ready).
