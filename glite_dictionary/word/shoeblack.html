shoeblack

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃuˌblæk / (AmE), / ˈʃuːblæk / (BrE)
a person whose job is to clean and polish shoes and boots, typically in public places
person who polishes shoes
archaic

Examples

A shoeblack set up his stand near the busy train station.
In Victorian London, a shoeblack could often be found on almost every street corner.
The young boy worked as a shoeblack to earn money for his family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bootblack - a person who polishes boots and shoes for money
