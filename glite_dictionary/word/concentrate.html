concentrate

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑːnsənˌtreɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒnsəntreɪt / (BrE)
(of a liquid or sauce) to be cooked until much of the liquid has evaporated, making the flavor or substance stronger.
to cook until liquid is reduced

Examples

Simmer the mixture gently until the sauce concentrates and thickens.
As the stock concentrates, its flavor becomes much more intense.
The chef allowed the soup to concentrate before serving it.

Synset Examples

The sauce should reduce to one cup

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reduce - to cook a liquid so that it becomes thicker and more concentrated
