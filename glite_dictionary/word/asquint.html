asquint

1. ADJ

/ əˈskwɪnt / (AmE), / əˈskwɪnt / (BrE)
(especially of glances) looking or directed to one side, often with doubt, suspicion, or envy.
directed to one side, often doubtfully or suspiciously

Examples

She glanced asquint at him, uncertain whether she could trust his words.
He fixed me with an asquint look that made me feel uneasy.
The child looked asquint at the new toy, not sure if he liked it.

Synset Examples

her eyes with their misted askance look
sidelong glances

Related Terms

  • Synonym: askance - with a look or glance that is sideways or distrustful
  • Synonym: sidelong - directed sideways or obliquely

Etymology

From Middle English, possibly from 'a-' (on) + 'squint' (to look obliquely).
