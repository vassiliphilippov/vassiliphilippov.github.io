to mistranslate

1. VERB

/ ˌmɪs.trænzˈleɪt / (AmE), / ˌmɪs.trænzˈleɪt / (BrE)
to translate words or text incorrectly, so that the meaning is changed or lost
translate incorrectly

Examples

If you mistranslate this sentence, the entire meaning could be lost.
The manual was mistranslated, leading to confusion among users.
It's important not to mistranslate cultural references in literature.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to translate - to express the meaning of words or text in another language
  • Synonym: to misinterpret - to understand or explain something incorrectly

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'mis-' (wrongly) and 'translate'.
