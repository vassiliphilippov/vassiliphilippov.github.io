vaudevillian

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɔdəˈvɪliən / (AmE), / ˌvɔːdəˈvɪliən / (BrE)
an entertainer who performs in vaudeville shows, typically involving a variety of acts such as comedy, music, or dance
vaudeville performer

Examples

The famous vaudevillian captivated audiences with his slapstick humor and dance routines.
As a skilled vaudevillian, she seamlessly transitioned from singing to performing magic tricks on stage.
Many early film stars began their careers as vaudevillians before moving to Hollywood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entertainer - a person who entertains others, especially professionally on stage or television

Etymology

From 'vaudeville' (a type of theatrical variety show) + '-ian' (denoting association or belonging).
