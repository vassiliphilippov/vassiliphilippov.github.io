Proteles cristata

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈtiːlɛs krɪˈsteɪtə / (AmE), / prəʊˈtiːlɛs krɪˈsteɪtə / (BrE)
a small, insectivorous mammal of Southeast Africa, also known as the aardwolf, related to the hyena and characterized by its striped coat
insect-eating striped mammal of SE Africa

Examples

The Proteles cristata is distinguished from other hyenas by its thin muzzle and diet of termites.
A Proteles cristata typically avoids confrontation and relies on its ability to consume thousands of insects each night.
Researchers tracked several Proteles cristata across savannah habitats to study their feeding behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aardwolf - another common name for Proteles cristata

Etymology

From Greek protēles (complete before) and Latin cristata (crested), referring to physical characteristics.
