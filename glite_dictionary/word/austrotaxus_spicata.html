Austrotaxus spicata

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔstroʊˌtæksəs spɪˈkeɪtə / (AmE), / ˌɔːstrəʊˌtæksəs spɪˈkɑːtə / (BrE)
a large yew tree native to New Caledonia, also cultivated in eastern Australia, New Zealand, and Hawaii
a large yew tree native to New Caledonia

Examples

The Austrotaxus spicata is highly valued for its ornamental foliage in gardens.
Botanists noted that Austrotaxus spicata thrives in the humid climate of New Caledonia.
Several parks in New Zealand now feature the striking Austrotaxus spicata among their exotic tree collections.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yew - any of various evergreen trees and shrubs of the genus Taxus

Etymology

From Latinized New Latin: 'Austro-' meaning southern, and 'Taxus' meaning yew; 'spicata' refers to the spiked arrangement of its leaves.
