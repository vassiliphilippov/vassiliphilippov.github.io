impossible

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɑːsəbəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɒsəbl / (BrE)
(of a person or their behavior) very difficult or unreasonable to deal with; not acceptable or reasonable.
very difficult or unreasonable (person/behavior)

Examples

His impossible behavior at the party embarrassed everyone.
She's being absolutely impossible to work with today.
The children were so impossible last night that we had to leave early.

Synset Examples

impossible behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unreasonable - not guided by or based on good sense
  • Synonym: unmanageable - difficult or impossible to control or deal with
  • Degree Lower: difficult - not easy; requiring effort

Etymology

From Latin 'impossibilis', from 'in-' (not) + 'possibilis' (able to be done).

WordNet ID: oewn-02445119-s

2. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɑːsəbəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɒsəbl / (BrE)
not able to happen, exist, or be done; totally unlikely or not at all possible.
not able to happen or exist

Examples

Winning the lottery twice is almost impossible for the average person.
It seemed impossible to finish the project on time, but the team persevered.
The mountain looked impossible to climb, but she reached the summit after days of effort.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: possible - able to be done or occur
  • Synonym: unlikely - not likely to happen or be true

Etymology

From Latin 'impossibilis', from 'in-' (not) + 'possibilis' (able to be done).
