reproduction

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːprəˈdʌkʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːprəˈdʌkʃən / (BrE)
the process by which living organisms produce offspring, especially through sexual activity
biological process of producing offspring

Examples

Sexual reproduction allows for genetic variation in the offspring.
Scientists study the reproduction of endangered species in captivity.
Education about human reproduction is part of the biology curriculum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: procreation - the act or process of producing offspring

Etymology

From Latin 're-' (again) + 'producere' (to bring forth).

3. NOUN

/ ˌriːprəˈdʌkʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːprəˈdʌkʃən / (BrE)
the process by which living organisms produce new individuals, or offspring, typically involving the transmission of genetic material
the process of producing offspring

Examples

Sexual reproduction in plants often involves pollination by insects or the wind.
Scientists study cell division as part of the broader process of reproduction.
Through reproduction, animals ensure the continuation of their species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: procreation - the act or process of producing offspring; synonym for reproduction
  • Antonym: sterility - the inability to produce offspring; antonym for reproduction

Etymology

From Latin 'reproductio', from 're-' (again) + 'producere' (to produce).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13571521-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˌriːprəˈdʌkʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːprəˈdʌkʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of recalling information by reproducing the original stimulus as it was initially perceived or learned, especially in memory research
recall by replicating original stimulus

Examples

In psychology, reproduction of learned material is tested by asking subjects to restate exactly what they studied.
The experiment examines whether reproduction or recognition is more effective for memorizing complex figures.
His reproduction of the poem from memory was almost word-perfect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recall - the act of remembering or recollecting information

Etymology

From Latin 're-' (again) + 'producere' (to produce).

copy of an original work or object

Examples

She bought a reproduction of the antique vase for her living room.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: original - the first form or version from which copies are made
