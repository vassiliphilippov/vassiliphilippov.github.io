chamfron

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃæm.frɒn / (AmE), / ˈʃæm.frɒn / (BrE)
A piece of medieval plate armor designed to protect a horse's head in battle.
medieval horse's head armor

Examples

The knight ensured his charger was fully equipped with a chamfron before heading into battle.
Museum visitors admired the ornate chamfron displayed alongside other medieval armor.
The chamfron protected the warhorse's face during cavalry charges.

Etymology

From French 'chanfrein', meaning 'forehead' or 'front of a horse's head'.
