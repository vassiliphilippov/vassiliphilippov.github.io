rupestral

1. ADJ

/ ruˈpɛstrəl / (AmE), / ruˈpɛstrəl / (BrE)
(of art or inscriptions) composed of or inscribed on rock, especially referring to prehistoric cave paintings or carvings.
composed of or inscribed on rock

Examples

The ancient cave contained magnificent rupestral drawings that revealed details of early human life.
Archaeologists study rupestral art to better understand prehistoric cultures.
Many rupestral inscriptions have survived for thousands of years due to the durability of stone.

Synset Examples

rupestral drawings in the caves of Northern Spain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: petroglyph - a rock carving, especially a prehistoric one
  • Synonym: rock art - human-made markings placed on natural stone

Etymology

From Latin rupestris, from rupes (rock, cliff).
