free-spoken

1. ADJ

/ ˈfriːˌspoʊkən / (AmE), / ˈfriːˌspəʊkən / (BrE)
speaking in a direct and unreserved way, without subtlety or evasion; frank or outspoken.
direct and unreserved in speech

Examples

Her free-spoken comments surprised the committee members who were used to more guarded language.
The free-spoken journalist quickly gained a reputation for honest and direct reporting.
He has always been free-spoken, never hesitating to share his true thoughts and opinions.

Synset Examples

blunt talking and straight shooting
a blunt New England farmer
I gave them my candid opinion
forthright criticism
a forthright approach to the problem
tell me what you think — and you may just as well be frank
it is possible to be outspoken without being rude
plainspoken and to the point
a point-blank accusation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outspoken - expressing opinions directly and honestly
  • Synonym: blunt - speaking in a direct and sometimes rude way
  • Antonym: reticent - not revealing thoughts or feelings readily

Etymology

Formed from 'free' (open, unrestrained) + 'spoken' (past participle of 'speak').
