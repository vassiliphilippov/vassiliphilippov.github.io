idocrase

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪdəˌkreɪs / (AmE), / ˈɪdəʊˌkreɪs / (BrE)
a green, yellow, or brown mineral consisting of a hydrated silicate, occurring as crystals in limestone and used as a gemstone
a mineral gemstone found in limestone

Examples

The jeweller showed me a beautiful ring set with a rare idocrase stone.
Some geologists discovered crystals of idocrase embedded in the ancient limestone.
The greenish-yellow hue of the idocrase made it highly prized among gemstone collectors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vesuvianite - another name for idocrase, a hydrated silicate mineral

Etymology

From Greek eidos (form) + krasis (mixture), a reference to its varied crystal shapes.
