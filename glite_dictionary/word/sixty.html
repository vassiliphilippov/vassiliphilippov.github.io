sixty

1. NUM

/ ˈsɪksti / (AmE), / ˈsɪksti / (BrE)
The number equivalent to six tens; one more than fifty-nine and one less than sixty-one.
the number 60

Examples

She celebrated her sixty birthday with a big party for friends and family.
There are sixty minutes in an hour.
The package weighed about sixty kilograms.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: fifty-nine - the number 59; one less than sixty
  • Degree Higher: sixty-one - the number 61; one more than sixty

Etymology

From Old English 'sixtig' (group of sixty), from 'six' plus the Old English suffix '-tig' (group of ten).

3. NUM

/ ˈsɪksti / (AmE), / ˈsɪksti / (BrE)
the number 60; the cardinal number representing six times ten
cardinal number 60

Examples

She celebrated her sixtyth birthday with a big party.
There are sixty students in this class.
He counted exactly sixty dollars in his wallet.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: fifty - the number 50; the cardinal number representing five times ten
  • Degree Higher: seventy - the number 70; the cardinal number representing seven times ten

Etymology

From Old English 'sixtig', from 'six' + '-tig' (group of ten).
