Alexandria

1. PROPN

/ ˌælɪɡˈzændriə / (AmE), / ˌælɪɡˈzændriə / (BrE)
A major port city in northern Egypt on the Mediterranean Sea, founded by Alexander the Great and historically the capital of ancient Egypt.
port city in northern Egypt

Examples

Many tourists visit Alexandria to see its ancient landmarks and beautiful Mediterranean coastline.
The famous Library of Alexandria was once the largest in the ancient world.
She learned about the history of Alexandria during her studies of ancient civilizations.

Etymology

Named after Alexander the Great, who founded the city in 331 BCE.
