accordingly

1. ADV

/ əˈkɔrdɪŋli / (AmE), / əˈkɔːdɪŋli / (BrE)
in a way that is appropriate to what has been done or said; in accordance with something mentioned
in a manner fitting circumstances

Examples

If the weather is bad, we will change our plans accordingly.
He received new instructions and acted accordingly.
She was warned about the risks and prepared accordingly.

Synset Examples

she acted accordingly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consequently - as a result; therefore
  • Synonym: therefore - for that reason; consequently

Etymology

From 'accord' + '-ing' + '-ly', formed on the pattern of similar adverbs.

2. ADV

/ əˈkɔrdɪŋli / (AmE), / əˈkɔːdɪŋli / (BrE)
for the reason stated; as a result or consequence
as a result; consequently

Examples

The project was delayed, and accordingly, the launch date was moved back.
He received a warning and acted accordingly to avoid further trouble.
The weather forecast predicted rain, so she packed her umbrella accordingly.

Synset Examples

consequently, he didn't do it
continued to have severe headaches and accordingly returned to the doctor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consequently - as a result; therefore
  • Synonym: therefore - for that reason, as a result
  • Antonym: nevertheless - in spite of that; however

Etymology

From 'according' + '-ly', ultimately from Old French 'acorder', from Latin 'accordare' (to bring into harmony).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00063395-r
Synset POS: r
