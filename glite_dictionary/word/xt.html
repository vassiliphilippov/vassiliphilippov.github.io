XT

1. NOUN

/ ɛks ti / (AmE), / ɛks ti / (BrE)
unwanted signal in a system caused by accidental coupling, known as crosstalk, especially in telecommunications or electronic circuits
unwanted signal crosstalk (telecommunications)
medical

Examples

The engineer identified XT as the source of interference between the two circuits.
Testing revealed that XT was causing audio distortion on the communication line.
Minimizing XT is crucial when designing high-frequency electronic systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crosstalk - unwanted transfer of signals between communication channels

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'crosstalk', technical jargon in electronics and telecommunications.
