bodega

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈdeɪɡə / (AmE), / bəʊˈdeɪɡə / (BrE)
a small grocery store, especially one in a Spanish-speaking neighborhood or urban area, often selling wine and everyday items
small grocery store, esp. in Hispanic neighborhoods
american

Examples

I stopped by the bodega on my way home to pick up some milk and bread.
Many New Yorkers rely on their local bodega for quick snacks and groceries.
The bodega on the corner is always open late and sells fresh sandwiches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corner shop - a small shop on a street corner selling groceries and household goods
  • Antonym: supermarket - a large self-service store selling foods and household goods

Etymology

From Spanish 'bodega' meaning 'wine shop' or 'cellar', originally from Latin 'apotheca' (storehouse).
