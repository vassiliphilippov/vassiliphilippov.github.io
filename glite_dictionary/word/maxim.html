maxim

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæk.sɪm / (AmE), / ˈmæk.sɪm / (BrE)
A short, well-known statement expressing a general truth or rule of conduct.
concise statement of a general truth or rule

Examples

Her favorite maxim is 'Actions speak louder than words.'
He lived his life according to the maxim 'Honesty is the best policy.'
The old maxim about patience proved true once again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proverb - a simple and concrete saying popularly known and repeated
  • Synonym: aphorism - a concise statement of a principle or truth, often witty

Etymology

From Latin 'maxima' (short for 'maxima sententia', meaning 'greatest proposition').
