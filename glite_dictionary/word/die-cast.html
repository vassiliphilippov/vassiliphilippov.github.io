die-cast

1. ADJ

/ ˈdaɪˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈdaɪˌkɑːst / (BrE)
formed by forcing molten metal into a mold (die), especially under high pressure; made by die casting.
made by forced casting in a mold

Examples

The model car was a die-cast replica with remarkable detail.
Collectors prize rare die-cast toys from the 1950s for their craftsmanship.
Many machine parts are die-cast to ensure consistent strength and precision.

Synset Examples

a die-cast seal

Etymology

From 'die' (industrial mold) + 'cast' (to shape by pouring in a mold).
