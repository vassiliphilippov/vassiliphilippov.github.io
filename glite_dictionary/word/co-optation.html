co-optation

1. NOUN

/ koʊ.ɑpˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / kəʊ.ɒpˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of appointing someone to a position, especially in a group or organization, often without their consent or usual formal process
the act of appointing summarily

Examples

The board's decision for co-optation of new members surprised the entire committee.
In some organizations, co-optation is used to quickly fill vacated positions.
He objected to the co-optation process, arguing it bypassed the usual democratic procedures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: appointment - the act of assigning a job or position to someone

Etymology

From Latin 'cooptatio', from 'cooptare' (to choose together).

2. NOUN

/ koʊˌɑpˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / kəʊˌɒpˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of appointing a new member to a group, often by vote of existing members rather than by general election
the process of appointing a new group member

Examples

The board used co-optation to fill the vacant seat quickly and smoothly.
Some clubs rely on co-optation rather than public elections to choose new members.
Critics argued that the practice of co-optation undermined the organization's democratic values.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to co-opt - to appoint as a member of a group by invitation of existing members

Etymology

From Latin cooptatio (admission by choice), from cooptare (to choose together).
