fungus

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʌŋ.ɡəs / (AmE), / ˈfʌŋ.ɡəs / (BrE)
A member of a large group of organisms (kingdom Fungi) that lack chlorophyll and feed on organic matter, including molds, yeast, mushrooms, and toadstools.
organism of the fungi kingdom

Examples

The biologist discovered a new species of fungus in the tropical rainforest.
Mushrooms are a familiar type of fungus found in many environments.
Athlete's foot is caused by a fungus that grows on the skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mold - a type of fungus that grows in the form of multicellular filaments
  • Synonym: yeast - a type of single-celled fungus

Etymology

From Latin 'fungus' (mushroom).
