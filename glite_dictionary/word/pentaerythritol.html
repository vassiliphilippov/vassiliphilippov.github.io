pentaerythritol

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛntəɪˈrɪθɪˌtɔl / (AmE), / ˌpɛntəɪˈrɪθɪtɒl / (BrE)
A white crystalline polyhydric alcohol used in the manufacture of explosives, resins, and drugs, and as a coronary vasodilator to treat angina pectoris.
chemical compound; coronary vasodilator
medical

Examples

The medication contains pentaerythritol to help dilate blood vessels in patients with angina.
Industrial processes use pentaerythritol as a building block for synthetic resins and explosives.
Researchers are studying new applications for pentaerythritol in pharmaceutical formulations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: erythritol - a related polyhydric alcohol found in some foods

Etymology

From penta- (five) + erythritol (a related sugar alcohol), referring to its structure.
