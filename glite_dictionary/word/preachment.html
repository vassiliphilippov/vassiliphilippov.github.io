preachment

1. NOUN

/ ˈpritʃmənt / (AmE), / ˈpriːtʃmənt / (BrE)
a sermon or earnest speech intended to give moral or religious advice
moral or religious sermon

Examples

The minister's preachment focused on the importance of kindness in daily life.
She ignored his lengthy preachment about the virtues of hard work.
He delivered a passionate preachment that moved the entire congregation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sermon - a speech given as part of a religious service, usually offering moral teaching

Etymology

From preach + -ment, patterning after French *prêchement* (sermon).
