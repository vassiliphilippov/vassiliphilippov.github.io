regal

1. ADJ

/ ˈriːɡəl / (AmE), / ˈriːɡəl / (BrE)
of, relating to, or suitable for a king or queen; royal or magnificent in appearance or manner.
fit for or characteristic of royalty

Examples

She entered the hall with a regal bearing that commanded everyone's attention.
The hotel suite was decorated in a regal style, with velvet drapes and golden chandeliers.
His deep voice and regal manner made him perfect for the role of the king in the play.

Synset Examples

golden age of imperial splendor
purple tyrant
regal attire
treated with royal acclaim
the royal carriage of a stag's head

Related Terms

  • Synonym: royal - of or relating to a king or queen or the royal family
  • Synonym: majestic - having or showing impressive beauty or dignity
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance; not proud or arrogant

Etymology

From Latin regalis (royal), from rex (king).
