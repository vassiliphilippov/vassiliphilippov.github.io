Chlorophyllum

1. NOUN

/ ˌklɔːrəˈfɪləm / (AmE), / ˌklɒrəˈfɪləm / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Agaricaceae, known for including some large, white-spored mushrooms such as the shaggy parasol.
genus of fungi including shaggy parasols

Examples

The mushroom she found was identified as a species of Chlorophyllum.
Mycologists study different varieties within the Chlorophyllum genus for their unique characteristics.
Some edible mushrooms in the genus Chlorophyllum are commonly known as shaggy parasols.

Etymology

From Greek 'chloros' (green) + 'phyllon' (leaf), originally referring to the greenish tint of some species.
