pessimum

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɛsɪməm / (AmE), / ˈpɛsɪməm / (BrE)
(of an organism's environment) being the least favorable conditions for survival or growth.
least favorable environmental conditions

Examples

Temperatures at the pessimum can significantly decrease metabolic activity in cold-blooded animals.
Seedlings exposed to pessimum light levels often fail to thrive.
Growth slowed dramatically when the fungus was kept at environmental pessimum rather than optimum conditions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: optimum - most favorable conditions for survival or growth
  • Degree Higher: maximum - greatest possible amount; most favorable in some contexts
  • Degree Lower: minimum - least possible amount

Etymology

From Latin 'pessimus' (worst) plus the suffix '-um', modeled after 'optimum'.
