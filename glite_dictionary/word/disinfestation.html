disinfestation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪs.ɪn.fɛˈsteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.ɪn.fɛˈsteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of removing or eliminating unwanted insects or vermin, especially from buildings, crops, or stored products
removal of pests or vermin

Examples

After the disinfestation of the warehouse, no signs of insects remained.
Regular disinfestation is essential in the food industry to keep products safe.
The team scheduled a disinfestation to tackle the rat problem in the building.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pest control - the management or regulation of pests
  • Antonym: disinfection - the process of cleaning to destroy bacteria

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'dis-' (expressing removal) and 'infestation' (from Latin 'infestare', to attack), meaning the act of removing infestation.
