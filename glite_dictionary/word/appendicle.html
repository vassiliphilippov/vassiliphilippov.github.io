appendicle

1. NOUN

/ əˈpɛndɪkəl / (AmE), / əˈpɛndɪkəl / (BrE)
a small appendage or outgrowth, especially in biological or anatomical contexts
small appendage or outgrowth

Examples

The insect's antennae are each tipped with a tiny appendicle that aids in sensing the environment.
Some plants develop a small appendicle at the base of each petal.
The researchers noted the presence of an unusual appendicle extending from the organism's shell.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: appendage - a thing that is added or attached; an addition or accessory

Etymology

From Latin 'appendicula', diminutive of 'appendix' (something attached).
