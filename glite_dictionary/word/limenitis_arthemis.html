Limenitis arthemis

1. PROPN

/ lɪˌmɛnətɪs ɑrˈθɛmɪs / (AmE), / lɪˌmɛnɪtɪs ɑːˈθɛmɪs / (BrE)
A North American butterfly with blue-black wings marked by a broad white band, commonly known as the white admiral or red-spotted purple.
a North American butterfly species

Examples

The Limenitis arthemis is often spotted in woodland areas during the summer months.
Many photographers seek out the Limenitis arthemis for its striking blue-black and white wing pattern.
I identified a butterfly in my garden as Limenitis arthemis using my field guide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white admiral - common name for Limenitis arthemis in northern range
  • Synonym: red-spotted purple - common name for Limenitis arthemis in southern range

Etymology

From the genus name Limenitis (Greek: 'of the harbour, port', used for butterfly genus) and the species name arthemis (possibly from Artemis, Greek goddess).
