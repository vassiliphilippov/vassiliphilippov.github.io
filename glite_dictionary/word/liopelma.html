Liopelma

1. NOUN

/ laɪ.oʊˈpɛl.mə / (AmE), / laɪ.əʊˈpɛl.mə / (BrE)
A genus of primitive frogs native to New Zealand, constituting the sole genus of the family Leiopelmatidae.
genus of primitive New Zealand frogs

Examples

The endangered Liopelma frogs are unique to New Zealand’s forests.
Scientists are studying the habitat of the Liopelma genus to aid conservation efforts.
Unlike other frogs, Liopelma species retain primitive characteristics.

Etymology

From Greek 'leios' (smooth) + 'pelma' (sole of the foot).
