achromatinic

1. ADJ

/ ˌeɪ.kroʊ.məˈtɪn.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌeɪ.krəʊ.məˈtɪn.ɪk / (BrE)
(of cell nuclear substance) not readily colored by stains; lacking affinity for dyes during microscopic examination.
not readily stained cell nucleus substance
medical

Examples

The achromatinic regions of the nucleus were difficult to observe under standard staining methods.
Some cancerous cells exhibit a highly achromatinic appearance, making diagnosis more challenging.
The microscopist identified both chromatic and achromatinic substances in the tissue sample.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: chromatic - easily colored by dyes during microscopic examination

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (not) + 'chroma' (color) + '-inic' (relating to).
