wellspring

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɛlˌsprɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɛlˌsprɪŋ / (BrE)
a source of water, especially the one that supplies a well
source of water for a well

Examples

The ancient village was built near a wellspring that provided fresh water year-round.
After days of searching, the explorers finally found the hidden wellspring deep in the forest.
Local legends claimed that the magical wellspring never ran dry, even during the harshest drought.

Etymology

From Middle English welspring, from Old English wellspring (source of water, spring).

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɛlˌsprɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɛlˌsprɪŋ / (BrE)
A source that supplies something abundantly, especially as the origin or starting point of an idea, emotion, or supply.
abundant source or origin

Examples

Her creativity was a wellspring of inspiration for the entire team.
The tradition is a wellspring of community pride and identity.
Education can be a wellspring for future opportunities.

Synset Examples

she was a well of information

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fount - a source of a desirable quality or commodity
  • Synonym: source - the place where something begins or arises

Etymology

Formed from 'well' (a source of water) and 'spring' (a place where water emerges from the ground).
