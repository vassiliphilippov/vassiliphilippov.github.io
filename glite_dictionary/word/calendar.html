calendar

1. NOUN

/ ˈkælɪndər / (AmE), / ˈkælɪndə / (BrE)
A chart or series of pages showing the days, weeks, and months of a particular year, usually arranged in a table or grid.
table of days in a year

Examples

She marked all her important appointments on the kitchen calendar.
The school year is divided into terms on the academic calendar.
I bought a new calendar for my office desk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: almanac - annual publication listing a set of events

Etymology

From Latin 'calendarium' (account book), from 'calendae' (the first day of the month).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkælənˌdɚ / (AmE), / ˈkæl.ɪn.də / (BrE)
A system for organizing days for social, religious, commercial, or administrative purposes, defining the beginning, length, and divisions of the year.
system for organizing and dividing the year

Examples

The Gregorian calendar is the most widely used system of timekeeping in the world.
Ancient civilizations each developed their own calendar to track the seasons and agricultural cycles.
Because of differences in the lunar and solar calendar, some holidays fall on varying dates each year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: almanac - an annual calendar containing important dates and information

Etymology

From Latin 'calendarium' (account book), from 'calendae' (the first day of the month).
