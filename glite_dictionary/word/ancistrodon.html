Ancistrodon

1. NOUN

/ ænˌsɪstrəˈdɑn / (AmE), / anˌsɪstrəˈdɒn / (BrE)
a genus of venomous snakes native to Asia and North America, commonly known as copperheads or moccasins
genus of venomous snakes

Examples

The Ancistrodon genus includes snakes known for their distinctive copper-colored heads.
Herpetologists often study Ancistrodon species to understand venom evolution.
Several species in the Ancistrodon genus are native to the southern United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Agkistrodon - alternative genus name for the same group of snakes

Etymology

From Greek 'ankistron' (hook) and 'odōn' (tooth), referring to the hooked fangs.

2. NOUN

/ ænˈsɪstrədɒn / (AmE), / ænˈsɪstrədɒn / (BrE)
A genus of venomous snakes commonly known as copperheads or moccasins, native to Asia and the Americas.
a genus of venomous snakes (copperheads, moccasins)

Examples

The Ancistrodon genus includes several species commonly referred to as moccasins.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Ancistrodon genus in Southeast Asia.
Venom from Ancistrodon snakes is studied for its potential medical applications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Agkistrodon - genus name now often used for the same group of snakes

Etymology

From Greek 'ankistron' (hook) and 'odous' (tooth), referring to hooked fangs.
