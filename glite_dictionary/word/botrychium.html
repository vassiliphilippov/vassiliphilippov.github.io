Botrychium

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈtrɪkiəm / (AmE), / bəˈtrɪkɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of small ferns in the family Ophioglossaceae, commonly known as grape ferns or moonworts.
genus of small ferns (grape ferns)

Examples

The Botrychium genus is easily recognized by its grape-like clusters of spores.
Rare species of Botrychium can be found growing in undisturbed meadows.
Scientists are studying Botrychium populations to understand their ecological requirements.

Etymology

From Greek 'botrys' (a bunch of grapes), referring to the shape of the spore clusters.

2. NOUN

/ boʊˈtrɪkiːəm / (AmE), / bəʊˈtrɪkɪəm / (BrE)
a genus of small ferns known as grape ferns or moonworts, characterized by spore-bearing clusters resembling grapes.
a genus of small grape ferns (moonworts)

Examples

The Botrychium genus includes several species commonly known as moonworts.
Researchers discovered a rare Botrychium in the alpine meadows during their botanical survey.
Some ferns in the Botrychium genus are difficult to distinguish from one another.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moonwort - a common name for several plants in the genus Botrychium
  • Synonym: grape fern - another common name for plants in the genus Botrychium

Etymology

From Greek 'botrys' (bunch of grapes), referring to the shape of the spore clusters.
