bok choy

1. NOUN

/ bɑk tʃɔɪ / (AmE), / bɒk tʃɔɪ / (BrE)
A Chinese cabbage with white stalks and dark green leaves, commonly used in Asian cuisine.
Chinese cabbage with white stalks

Examples

I like to add fresh bok choy to my stir-fry for a crunchy texture.
The recipe calls for a bunch of bok choy, washed and chopped.
She grows bok choy in her backyard garden each spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pak choi - A similar Chinese leafy vegetable, sometimes considered the same as bok choy

Etymology

From Cantonese 白菜 (baahk choi), meaning 'white vegetable'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌbɑk ˈtʃɔɪ / (AmE), / ˌbɒk ˈtʃɔɪ / (BrE)
A type of Chinese cabbage with an elongated head, dark green leaves, and thick white stalks, commonly used in Asian cooking.
Chinese cabbage with green leaves and white stalks

Examples

She stir-fried some bok choy with garlic and ginger for dinner.
The market had fresh bok choy stacked next to the carrots and broccoli.
I like adding steamed bok choy to my noodle soup for extra flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pak choi - A variety of Chinese cabbage with similar characteristics

Etymology

From Cantonese 白菜 (baak choi), meaning 'white vegetable'.
