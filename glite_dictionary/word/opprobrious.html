opprobrious

1. ADJ

/ əˈproʊbriəs / (AmE), / əˈprəʊbrɪəs / (BrE)
(of language or behavior) expressing strong criticism, scorn, or contempt; conveying insult or disgrace.
expressing scorn or contempt

Examples

The politician faced opprobrious comments after his controversial statement.
Her opprobrious remarks left everyone in stunned silence at the meeting.
He was subjected to opprobrious criticism in the tabloids.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insulting - speaking or behaving in a way that is rude or offensive
  • Antonym: complimentary - expressing praise or approval

Etymology

From Latin opprobriosus, from opprobrum (disgrace, reproach).

/ əˈproʊbriəs / (AmE), / əˈprəʊbrɪəs / (BrE)
deserving or causing public disgrace or shame; expressing scorn or criticism
deserving disgrace or shame

Examples

The scandal left the company with an opprobrious reputation that was hard to repair.
