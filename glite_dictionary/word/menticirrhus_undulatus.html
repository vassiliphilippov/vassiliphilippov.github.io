Menticirrhus undulatus

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛntɪˈsɪrəs ʌnˈdjuːleɪtəs / (AmE), / ˌmɛntɪˈsɪrəs ʌnˈdjuːlətəs / (BrE)
a bluish-grey species of whiting found along the California coast, also called California kingcroaker
bluish-grey whiting of California coast

Examples

The Menticirrhus undulatus is often found in shallow waters along the sandy beaches of California.
Anglers sometimes catch Menticirrhus undulatus while fishing for surfperch in the Pacific.
The bluish-grey coloring of Menticirrhus undulatus helps it blend into its coastal environment.

Etymology

From New Latin; genus Menticirrhus and Latin undulatus meaning 'wavy', referring to the fish's appearance.
