macrophage

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæk.rəˌfeɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmak.rəˌfeɪdʒ / (BrE)
A large white blood cell that engulfs and digests cellular debris, foreign substances, microbes, and cancer cells.
type of large phagocytic white blood cell
medical

Examples

Macrophages play a key role in defending the body against infections.
The scientist discovered that macrophages help remove dead cells from tissues.
Certain diseases can affect how macrophages function in the immune system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phagocyte - a cell that engulfs and digests particles and microorganisms

Etymology

From Greek makros (large) + phagein (to eat).
