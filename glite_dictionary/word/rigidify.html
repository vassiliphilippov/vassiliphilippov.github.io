to rigidify

1. VERB

/ rɪˈdʒɪdəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / rɪˈdʒɪdɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to become or make rigid; to become stiff or inflexible
become or make rigid

Examples

Under the intense cold, the metal quickly began to rigidify.
The artist's new technique helps colors stand out but may also rigidify the canvas.
In fear, her muscles seemed to rigidify and she couldn't move.

Synset Examples

The body rigidified

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to harden - to become or make hard or harder
  • Antonym: to soften - to become or make soft or softer

Etymology

From Latin rigidus (stiff, hard) + English -ify (verb-forming suffix).

2. VERB

/ ˈrɪdʒ.ə.dəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈrɪdʒ.ɪ.dɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to make or become rigid, stiff, or inflexible; to set into a fixed or conventional pattern
make or become rigid or inflexible

Examples

The company decided to rigidify its safety protocols after the recent incidents.
If you rigidify your approach, you may miss out on creative solutions.
The lawyer warned that new regulations could rigidify business practices throughout the industry.

Synset Examples

rigidify the training schedule
ossified teaching methods
slogans petrify our thinking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ossify - to turn into bone or become rigid; to become inflexible
  • Synonym: to petrify - to make or become stone-like; to make rigid or motionless
  • Antonym: to soften - to make or become softer, less hard or less rigid

Etymology

From 'rigid' + '-ify', patterned after Latin roots meaning 'to make rigid'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00419035-v
Synset POS: v
