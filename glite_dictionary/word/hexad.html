hexad

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛk.sæd / (AmE), / ˈhɛk.sæd / (BrE)
A group or set of six; the cardinal number six.
group or number of six

Examples

The scientist grouped the samples into a hexad for analysis.
Each musical piece was played by a hexad of performers, creating a rich harmony.
In chemistry, a hexad can refer to an element with a valency of six.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: six - the number 6
  • Degree Higher: septet - a group of seven
  • Degree Lower: pentad - a group of five

Etymology

From Greek hexas, hexad- (group of six), from hex (six).
