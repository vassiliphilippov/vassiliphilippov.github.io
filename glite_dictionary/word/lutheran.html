Lutheran

1. ADJ

/ ˈluːθərən / (AmE), / ˈluːθərən / (BrE)
of or relating to the branch of the Protestant Church following the teachings of Martin Luther or to its members, beliefs, or institutions
relating to the Lutheran Church or its teachings

Examples

The Lutheran Church celebrates Reformation Day every October 31st.
Her grandparents were devout Lutheran believers who attended church every Sunday.
The seminary offers courses in Lutheran theology and liturgy.

Synset Examples

Lutheran doctrines

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Protestant - relating to the branch of Christianity that originated with the Reformation

Etymology

From Martin Luther (1483–1546), German Protestant reformer, + -an.

2. NOUN

/ ˈluːθərən / (AmE), / ˈluːθərən / (BrE)
a person who follows the teachings of Martin Luther or belongs to the Lutheran Church
follower of Lutheranism

Examples

Her grandfather was a devoted Lutheran who attended church every Sunday.
Many Lutherans celebrate Reformation Day on October 31st.
The community built a new school with support from the local Lutheran congregation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Protestant - a member of a Christian church founded on the Reformation
  • Antonym: Catholic - a member of the Roman Catholic Church

Etymology

From the name of Martin Luther (1483–1546), German theologian and religious reformer.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10297512-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈluːθərən / (AmE), / ˈluːθərən / (BrE)
Relating to Martin Luther, his teachings, or the religious movement and churches based on them.
of or relating to Martin Luther or his teachings

Examples

The Lutheran Reformation had a profound impact on European Christianity.
She attended a Lutheran church service every Sunday morning.
The council discussed the differences between Lutheran and Catholic doctrines.

Synset Examples

the Lutheran doctrine of justification by faith alone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Protestant - Relating to any of the Western Christian churches that are separate from the Roman Catholic Church and follow the principles of the Reformation.
  • Antonym: Catholic - Relating to the Roman Catholic Church, especially as distinct from Protestant churches.

Etymology

From Martin Luther (1483–1546), German theologian and reformer, with the suffix -an.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03046255-a
Synset POS: a
