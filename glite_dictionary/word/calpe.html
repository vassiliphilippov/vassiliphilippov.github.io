Calpe

1. PROPN

/ ˈkæl.pi / (AmE), / ˈkæl.pi / (BrE)
an ancient name for Gibraltar, the limestone promontory at the southern tip of Spain, known as one of the Pillars of Hercules.
ancient name for Gibraltar

Examples

In ancient times, Calpe was considered one of the mythical Pillars of Hercules.
The strategic position of Calpe made it valuable for controlling naval passage.
Historians often refer to Gibraltar by its ancient name, Calpe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gibraltar - a British Overseas Territory at the southern tip of Spain

Etymology

From Latin Calpe, an ancient designation for Gibraltar.
