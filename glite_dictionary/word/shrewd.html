shrewd

1. ADJ

/ ʃruːd / (AmE), / ʃruːd / (BrE)
Having or showing clear understanding and good judgment, often with practical or strategic intent; astute or clever in practical matters.
astute; clever in practical matters

Examples

She made a shrewd investment that doubled her money in just a year.
His shrewd negotiating skills helped the company secure a major contract.
It takes a shrewd mind to spot such a rare opportunity.

Synset Examples

the most calculating and selfish men in the community

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astute - having or showing an ability to accurately assess situations or people
  • Antonym: naive - lacking experience, wisdom, or judgment

Etymology

From Middle English 'shrewed', originally meaning 'wicked', from 'shrew', an evil or mischievous person.

2. ADJ

/ ʃrud / (AmE), / ʃruːd / (BrE)
having or showing sharp powers of judgment; marked by practical and astute intelligence, especially in business or practical affairs.
having sharp practical judgment

Examples

She offered a shrewd analysis of the company's financial situation.
His shrewd negotiating skills helped him close the deal quickly.
Only a shrewd investor would have spotted the hidden value in that property.

Synset Examples

a smart businessman
an astute tenant always reads the small print in a lease
he was too shrewd to go along with them on a road that could lead only to their overthrow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: astute - having or showing an ability to notice and understand things clearly; mentally sharp or clever
  • Antonym: naive - lacking experience or sophistication; easily fooled

Etymology

From Middle English 'shrewed' (wicked, mischievous), from 'shrew' (a spiteful person).
