Shiloh

1. PROPN

/ ˈʃaɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˈʃaɪləʊ / (BrE)
a major battle fought in 1862 during the American Civil War in southwestern Tennessee, notable for its high casualties and strategic consequences
Civil War battle in Tennessee, 1862

Examples

Many historians believe the Battle of Shiloh marked a turning point in the Civil War.
Thousands of soldiers lost their lives during Shiloh, making it one of the war's bloodiest engagements.
Students visited the Shiloh National Military Park to learn about Civil War history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gettysburg - Civil War battle in Pennsylvania, 1863
  • Synonym: Antietam - Civil War battle in Maryland, 1862

Etymology

From the name of a small community and church near the battle site in Tennessee, originally from Hebrew meaning 'place of peace'.
