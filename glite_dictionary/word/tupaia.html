Tupaia

1. PROPN

/ tuˈpaɪə / (AmE), / tjuˈpaɪə / (BrE)
A genus of small, arboreal mammals known as tree shrews, native to Southeast Asia.
genus of tree shrews

Examples

The genus Tupaia includes several species of tree shrews found in Southeast Asia.
Scientists often study Tupaia to understand the evolution of primates.
A Tupaia was observed building a nest in the rainforest canopy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tree shrew - small, squirrel-like mammal native to Southeast Asia

Etymology

From Modern Latin, derived from Malay 'tupai' meaning 'tree shrew'.
