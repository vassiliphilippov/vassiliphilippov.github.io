diversified

1. ADJ

/ daɪˈvɜrsɪˌfaɪd / (AmE), / daɪˈvɜːsɪfaɪd / (BrE)
having a variety of different forms, types, or components; made more varied or diverse
having increased variety or diversity

Examples

The company's diversified portfolio helped it withstand economic downturns.
Many farmers have adopted diversified crops to reduce risk and improve income.
Her interests are quite diversified, ranging from music to mountain climbing.

Synset Examples

a diversified musical program ranging from classical to modern
diversified farming
diversified manufacturing
diversified scenery
diversified investments

Related Terms

  • Synonym: varied - showing variety; different kinds or types present
  • Antonym: homogeneous - of the same or similar kind; lacking diversity

Etymology

Past participle of 'diversify', from Latin 'diversificare', meaning 'to make diverse'.
