Anastatica

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.əˈstæt.ɪ.kə / (AmE), / ˌæn.əˈstæt.ɪ.kə / (BrE)
A genus of plant in the mustard family (Brassicaceae), known for the species Anastatica hierochuntica, commonly called the rose of Jericho or resurrection plant, which can survive extreme dehydration and revive when watered.
genus of plant known as the rose of Jericho

Examples

The Anastatica can survive for years without water, appearing to come back to life when hydrated.
Botanists are fascinated by the unique adaptations of the Anastatica to desert conditions.
Pilgrims often carried Anastatica as a symbol of hope and renewal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rose of Jericho - Common name for Anastatica hierochuntica, the resurrection plant
  • Synonym: resurrection plant - Plants that can survive desiccation and recover when rehydrated

Etymology

From Greek anastatikē, meaning 'resurrection' or 'restoration'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌænəˈstætɪkə / (AmE), / ˌanəˈstatɪkə / (BrE)
a genus of plants in the mustard family (Brassicaceae), consisting of a single species, commonly known as the rose of Jericho, famous for its ability to survive extreme desiccation by curling up and reviving when watered.
a plant genus including the rose of Jericho

Examples

The Anastatica, or rose of Jericho, is often used as a symbol of resurrection.
Researchers studied how Anastatica survives in arid deserts by curling its leaves.
Collectors value the Anastatica for its unusual ability to revive when placed in water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rose of Jericho - a plant known for appearing to come back to life after drying out

Etymology

From Greek *anastatikē* (restorative), referencing the plant's ability to revive after desiccation.
