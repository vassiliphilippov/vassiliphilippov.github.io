Cyrano de Bergerac

1. PROPN

/ ˌsɪrəˌnoʊ də bərˈʒɛræk / (AmE), / ˌsɪərəˌnəʊ də bɛəˈʒɛəræk / (BrE)
a French soldier and writer (1619–1655), primarily remembered as the larger-than-life hero with a prominent nose in Edmond Rostand's 1897 play of the same name.
French soldier; hero of Rostand's play

Examples

The play about Cyrano de Bergerac has captivated audiences for generations.
He admired the wit and bravery of Cyrano de Bergerac, both as a real person and as a character.
Many actors dream of performing as Cyrano de Bergerac on stage.

Etymology

From the name of the real 17th-century French writer and soldier Hercule Savinien Cyrano de Bergerac, popularized by Rostand's play.
