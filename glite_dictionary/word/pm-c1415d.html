Pm

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiː ˈɛm / (AmE), / ˈpiː ˈɛm / (BrE)
a soft, silvery metallic chemical element of the rare earth group, with no stable isotopes, symbol Pm
chemical element, symbol Pm

Examples

Scientists used Pm as a radioactive tracer in their experiments.
There are no stable isotopes of Pm found in nature.
The element Pm was discovered as a product of uranium fission.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: promethium - soft silvery radioactive rare-earth metal, atomic number 61

Etymology

From Prometheus, a figure in Greek mythology, after whom the element was named.
