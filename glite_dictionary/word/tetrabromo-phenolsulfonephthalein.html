tetrabromo-phenolsulfonephthalein

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛtrəˌbroʊmoʊ fiːˌnɒl sʌlˌfoʊn ɛfˈθæliːn / (AmE), / ˌtɛtrəˌbrəʊməʊ fiːˌnɒl sʌlˌfəʊn ɛfˈθæliːn / (BrE)
a dye used as an acid-base indicator, especially in laboratories, with a useful range between pH 3.0 and 4.6
a chemical acid-base indicator dye
medical

Examples

In the experiment, tetrabromo-phenolsulfonephthalein changed color as the solution became more acidic.
The laboratory technician added tetrabromo-phenolsulfonephthalein to determine the pH of the sample.
Because of its sharp transition range, tetrabromo-phenolsulfonephthalein is often preferred for precise titrations.

Etymology

From tetra- (four) + bromo (bromine) + phenol + sulfone + phthalein; describing the chemical structure.
