Lama pacos

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑːmə ˈpeɪkoʊs / (AmE), / ˈlɑːmə ˈpækɒs / (BrE)
a domesticated South American mammal with long silky fleece, commonly known as the alpaca, bred for its wool
species of domesticated alpaca

Examples

The Lama pacos is prized for its exceptionally soft and warm wool.
Farmers in the Andes have bred the Lama pacos for thousands of years.
Scientists classified the Lama pacos as a domesticated species distinct from the llama.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alpaca - domesticated South American camelid bred for its wool

Etymology

From Latin, scientific name given in taxonomy; 'Lama' from Spanish 'llama', 'pacos' of unknown origin.
