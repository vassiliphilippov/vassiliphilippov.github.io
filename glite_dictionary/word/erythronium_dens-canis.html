Erythronium dens-canis

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌrɪθroʊniəm ˌdɛnsˈkeɪnɪs / (AmE), / ɪˌrɪθrəʊniəm ˌdɛnsˈkanɪs / (BrE)
a species of European dogtooth violet with rose to mauve flowers, widely cultivated in gardens
European dogtooth violet species

Examples

Erythronium dens-canis thrives in moist woodland soil and is prized for its vibrant blooms.
Gardeners plant Erythronium dens-canis in shaded borders for early spring color.
The delicate petals of Erythronium dens-canis range from soft pink to deep mauve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dogtooth violet - common name for several Erythronium species

Etymology

From Latin: 'erythronium' from Greek 'erythros' (red); 'dens-canis' means 'dog's tooth' in Latin, referring to the shape of the bulb.
