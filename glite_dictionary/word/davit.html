davit

1. NOUN

/ ˈdævɪt / (AmE), / ˈdævɪt / (BrE)
A small crane-like device, typically one of a pair, used to suspend, lower, and raise equipment such as lifeboats on a ship.
crane for lowering/raising equipment (lifeboats)

Examples

The crew quickly operated the davit to lower the lifeboat into the sea.
Maintenance of each davit is crucial for the safety of passengers and crew.
On modern ships, the davits are hydraulically powered for smoother operation.

Etymology

From Middle English *davytt*, possibly from Old French *davide*, of uncertain origin.
