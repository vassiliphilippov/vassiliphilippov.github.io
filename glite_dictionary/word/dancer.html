dancer

1. NOUN

/ ˈdæn.sɚ / (AmE), / ˈdɑːn.sə / (BrE)
a person who participates in a social dance or attends a dance event
person attending a social dance

Examples

At the annual ball, every dancer wore formal attire and enjoyed the music.
The room filled with dancers as the band started to play a waltz.
She was not a trained dancer, but she loved attending community dances.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: dancer - a person who dances professionally or as a performer

Etymology

From Old French 'danser' (to dance), from Frankish 'dansōn'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdænsər / (AmE), / ˈdɑːnsə / (BrE)
A person who performs dances as a profession or for entertainment.
professional performer of dance

Examples

The dancer captivated the audience with her graceful movements.
He trained for years to become a professional dancer.
Several talented dancers auditioned for the lead role in the musical.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: choreographer - a person who composes the sequence of steps and moves for dances
  • Antonym: singer - a person who sings, especially professionally

Etymology

From Old French *dancier*, related to the verb 'to dance'.
