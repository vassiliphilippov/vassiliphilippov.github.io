self-reproof

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlf.rɪˈpruf / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf.rɪˈpruːf / (BrE)
the act of criticizing or blaming oneself for a fault or mistake
the act of blaming oneself

Examples

His constant self-reproof made it difficult for him to move on from his mistakes.
After the argument, she sat in self-reproof, reflecting on her harsh words.
A moment of self-reproof passed across his face as he admitted fault.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-blame - the act or state of blaming oneself
  • Synonym: remorse - deep regret or guilt for a wrong committed
  • Synonym: self-criticism - criticism of oneself or one's own actions

Etymology

Formed from 'self-' + 'reproof' (from Old French 'reprover', from Latin 'reprobare', meaning 'disapprove').
