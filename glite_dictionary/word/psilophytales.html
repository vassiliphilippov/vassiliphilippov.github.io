Psilophytales

1. NOUN

/ saɪləˈfaɪteɪlz / (AmE), / saɪləˈfaɪteɪliːz / (BrE)
an extinct order of Paleozoic vascular plants with simple, dichotomously branched stems, known from fossils in Europe and eastern Canada, including the earliest vascular land plants
extinct order of ancient vascular plants

Examples

Fossils of the Psilophytales provide valuable insights into early plant evolution.
The Psilophytales are considered among the first vascular plants to colonize land.
Many paleobotanists study Psilophytales to understand prehistoric terrestrial ecosystems.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'Psilophyton' (a genus) and '-ales' (order suffix in botanical nomenclature).
