hemogenesis

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːmoʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌhiːməˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (BrE)
the formation of blood cells within the living body, especially in the bone marrow
formation of blood cells
medical

Examples

Recent studies have provided insights into how hemogenesis is regulated by various genetic factors.
Disorders of hemogenesis can result in anemia or other blood-related illnesses.
The stimulation of hemogenesis is essential in the treatment of patients with bone marrow failure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hematopoiesis - the formation of the cellular components of blood

Etymology

From Greek 'haima' (blood) + 'genesis' (origin, creation).
