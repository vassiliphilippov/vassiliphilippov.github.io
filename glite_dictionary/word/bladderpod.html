bladderpod

1. NOUN

/ ˈblædərˌpɑd / (AmE), / ˈblæd.ə.pɒd / (BrE)
any of several annual or perennial herbs with inflated seed pods, belonging to genera such as Lesquerella and Physaria.
herb with inflated seed pods

Several species of bladderpod are native to the western United States.

2. NOUN

/ ˈblædərˌpɑd / (AmE), / ˈblædərˌpɒd / (BrE)
A North American wild plant of the lobelia family, having small blue flowers and inflated seed capsules, formerly used as an antispasmodic.
wild lobelia plant with inflated capsules

Examples

The bladderpod thrives in open fields across much of North America.
Native Americans once used bladderpod in traditional medicine for its antispasmodic properties.
Clusters of small blue flowers distinguish the bladderpod from other wildflowers.

Etymology

From 'bladder' (referring to the shape of the seed pod) + 'pod' (seed vessel).
