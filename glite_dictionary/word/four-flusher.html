four-flusher

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔr ˌflʌʃər / (AmE), / ˈfɔː ˌflʌʃə / (BrE)
a person who pretends to have qualities, abilities, or resources they do not possess, especially to deceive others; a bluffer or pretender
person who bluffs or pretends
informal

Examples

Don’t trust him—he’s just a four-flusher trying to impress everyone.
Everyone thought she was an expert, but she turned out to be a four-flusher.
Calling someone a four-flusher in that town was a serious insult.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bluffer - someone who tries to deceive by pretending confidence or capability
  • Synonym: faker - a person who pretends to be something they are not
  • Antonym: straight shooter - a person who is honest and direct

Etymology

From the poker term 'four flush', referring to a hand with four cards of the same suit, which is not a winning hand; used metaphorically for someone pretending to have more than they do.
