pismire

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪsˌmaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈpɪsˌmaɪə / (BrE)
An ant, especially when referred to in older or literary contexts.
an ant (old/literary)
archaic

Examples

In old poetry, the pismire is often mentioned as a symbol of industry.
She watched a single pismire carry a crumb across the garden path.
The ancient text described fields buzzing with the activity of pismires.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ant - a small social insect living in colonies

Etymology

From Middle English 'pismire,' from Scandinavian, with 'pis' meaning urine (from the smell) and 'mire' meaning ant.
