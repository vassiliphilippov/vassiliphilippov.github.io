clientage

1. NOUN

/ ˈklaɪ.ən.tɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈklaɪ.ən.tɪdʒ / (BrE)
the relationship between a client and a patron, especially in a historical or social context where protection, support, or services are exchanged for loyalty or services
relationship between client and patron

Examples

The system of clientage was essential to the structure of Roman society.
During the Middle Ages, clientage often determined loyalty and social status.
Many ancient landlords maintained clientage networks that ensured their power and influence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patronage - support or sponsorship by a patron

Etymology

From Latin 'clientela' (body of clients), via Old French 'clientage'.
