achromatic

1. ADJ

/ ˌækrəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌakrəˈmatɪk / (BrE)
having no hue; relating to colors such as black, white, or gray that do not have any hue
lacking hue; colorless

Examples

The artist preferred achromatic tones to create a sense of simplicity in his painting.
Black, white, and all shades of gray are considered achromatic colors.
The designer used an achromatic palette to give the space a modern, minimalist look.

Synset Examples

neutral colors like black or white

Related Terms

  • Antonym: chromatic - having color or related to color

Etymology

From Greek a- (not) + khrōma (color).
