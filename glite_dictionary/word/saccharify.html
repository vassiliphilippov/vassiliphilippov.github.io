to saccharify

1. VERB

/ ˈsæk.ə.rəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈsak.ə.rɪˌfaɪ / (BrE)
to convert (a substance) into a simple, soluble, fermentable sugar by hydrolyzing a sugar derivative or complex carbohydrate
convert to a simple sugar

Examples

Enzymes are used to saccharify starch during the brewing process.
Cellulose in the plant material can be saccharified to produce bioethanol.
Scientists found an efficient way to saccharify complex carbohydrates in agricultural waste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hydrolyze - to break down a compound through reaction with water

Etymology

From Latin 'saccharum' (sugar) + '-ify' (make or become).

2. VERB

/ sæˈkæ.rəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / sæˈkæ.rɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to convert (a substance) into sugar or make sweet by adding sugar, especially in a chemical or technical process
convert into sugar; make sweet with sugar

Examples

Certain enzymes can saccharify starches to produce glucose.
The brewer explained how to saccharify the grains during the mashing process.
Chemists saccharify cellulose to obtain fermentable sugars for biofuel production.

Synset Examples

sugar your tea

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sweeten - make sweeter by adding sugar or a sweetener

Etymology

From French *saccharifier*, from Latin *saccharum* (sugar) + *-fy* (make).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02200397-v
Synset POS: v
