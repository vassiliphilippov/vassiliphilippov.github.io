abhorrent

1. ADJ

/ əbˈhɔːrənt / (AmE), / əbˈhɒrənt / (BrE)
causing a strong feeling of dislike or disgust; morally offensive or repugnant
causing strong dislike or disgust

Examples

The community found the politician’s remarks utterly abhorrent.
Animal cruelty is abhorrent to most people around the world.
She refused to participate in any abhorrent practices, no matter the cost.

Synset Examples

an abhorrent deed
the obscene massacre at Wounded Knee
morally repugnant customs
repulsive behavior
the most repulsive character in recent novels

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repugnant - causing intense disgust or aversion
  • Synonym: repulsive - causing strong dislike or disgust
  • Antonym: attractive - pleasing or appealing to the senses

Etymology

From Latin 'abhorrens', present participle of 'abhorrēre' (to shrink back from, shudder at).
