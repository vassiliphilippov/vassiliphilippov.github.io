procurator

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈkjʊrˌeɪtər / (AmE), / prəˈkjʊərətə / (BrE)
an official in ancient Rome appointed by the Emperor to manage financial affairs and collect taxes in a province or district
Roman official managing finance and taxes

Examples

The procurator was responsible for gathering taxes in the province.
During the Roman Empire, the procurator acted as the emperor's financial agent.
Archaeologists discovered a document bearing the name of a procurator from Judaea.

Etymology

From Latin 'procurator', meaning 'manager, agent'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈproʊkjəˌreɪtər / (AmE), / prəˈkjʊərətə / (BrE)
an official or person who is authorized to act on behalf of another, especially in legal or administrative matters
person authorized to act for another

Examples

The Roman Empire appointed a procurator to govern the province.
In the court, the procurator presented evidence on behalf of the claimant.
As a procurator, she had the authority to sign documents for the company.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: agent - a person who acts on behalf of another

Etymology

From Latin procurator (manager, agent), from procurare (to manage, take care of).
