Casuariiformes

1. NOUN

/ kæzjuˌɛriˈɪfɔrˌmiːz / (AmE), / kazuːəˌrɪɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
An order of large, flightless birds including cassowaries and emus, native to Australasia.
order of flightless birds: cassowaries and emus

Examples

The Casuariiformes include some of the heaviest birds on Earth, such as the emu and cassowary.
Scientists study the Casuariiformes to understand ratite evolution and adaptation.
Australia and New Guinea are home to all living members of the Casuariiformes.

Etymology

From modern Latin, combining 'Casuarius' (cassowary) + '-formes' (having the form of).
