latterly

1. ADV

/ ˈlætərli / (AmE), / ˈlatəli / (BrE)
in the recent past; recently or during the later period under consideration
in the recent past; recently

Examples

Economic conditions have improved latterly, with more jobs becoming available.
He had supported the policy at first, but latterly became one of its critics.
The author's style has changed latterly, becoming more experimental in recent works.

Synset Examples

he was in Paris recently
lately the rules have been enforced
as late as yesterday she was fine
feeling better of late
the spelling was first affected, but latterly the meaning also

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recently - not long ago; in the near past
  • Antonym: formerly - in the past; in earlier times

Etymology

From 'latter' + '-ly', modeled after words like 'formerly'.
