to implode

1. VERB

/ ɪmˈploʊd / (AmE), / ɪmˈpləʊd / (BrE)
to burst or collapse inward with force, especially as a result of external pressure
burst or collapse inward

Examples

The old building was set to implode when the support structures were removed.
If the glass is cracked, it may implode under the pressure.
The submarine began to implode as it descended too deep.

Synset Examples

The bottle imploded

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to explode - to burst outward with force

Etymology

From Latin 'implodere' meaning 'to burst inward', modeled after 'explode'.
