fundamentally

1. ADV

/ ˌfʌndəˈmɛntəli / (AmE), / ˌfʌndəˈmɛntəli / (BrE)
In a basic or essential way; relating to the most important or central aspects.
in a basic or essential way

Examples

Fundamentally, their disagreement was about morals rather than facts.
The two proposals are fundamentally different in their approach to the problem.
She remains fundamentally optimistic despite recent setbacks.

Synset Examples

He is basically dishonest
the argument was essentially a technical one

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basically - in an essential or fundamental way
  • Synonym: essentially - in an essential or basic manner

Etymology

From Latin fundamentum (foundation) via Late Latin fundamental-is (serving as a foundation) + English -ly.
