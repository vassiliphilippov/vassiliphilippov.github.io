stellate

1. ADJ

/ ˈstɛl.eɪt / (AmE), / ˈstɛl.eɪt / (BrE)
arranged like rays or having a star-like pattern; radiating from a common center.
star-shaped; radiating from a center

Examples

The flower's petals are stellate, radiating outward from the center.
Some types of crystal grow in a stellate formation.
Under the microscope, the cells appeared stellate, with multiple arms extending from the center.

Synset Examples

radial symmetry
a starlike or stellate arrangement of petals
many cities show a radial pattern of main highways

Related Terms

  • Synonym: star-shaped - having the shape of a star
  • Synonym: radial - arranged like rays or radii

Etymology

From Latin stellatus, past participle of stellare (to star), from stella (star).
