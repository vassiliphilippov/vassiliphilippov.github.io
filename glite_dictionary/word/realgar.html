realgar

1. NOUN

/ riˈælˌɡɑr / (AmE), / riˈælˌɡɑː / (BrE)
a rare soft orange-red mineral consisting of arsenic sulfide (As₄S₄), used as an important ore of arsenic
soft orange-red arsenic sulphide mineral

Examples

The miners collected samples of realgar from the caves for further analysis.
During the festival, traditional fireworks contained traces of realgar.
The vivid color of realgar makes it recognizable to mineral collectors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orpiment - a yellow mineral also consisting of arsenic sulfide

Etymology

From Medieval Latin realgar, from Arabic rahj al-ġār (powder of the mine or cave).
