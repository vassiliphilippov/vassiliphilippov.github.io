Liparidae

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈpærəˌdiː / (AmE), / lɪˈparɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine fishes, known as snailfishes, characterized by their soft, elongated bodies and found in cold waters worldwide.
family of marine fishes (snailfishes)

Examples

The Liparidae are found at great depths in oceans around the world.
Researchers recently discovered a new species in the Liparidae family in the Pacific trench.
Due to their unique adaptations, Liparidae can survive in extremely cold environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snailfish - Common name for fishes of the family Liparidae

Etymology

From Greek 'liparos' (fat, oily), referring to the fish's soft, oily texture, combined with the taxonomic suffix '-idae' for animal families.

2. NOUN

/ lɪˈpærɪˌdi / (AmE), / lɪˈpærɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine fishes known as snailfishes, characterized by soft, scaleless bodies and commonly found in cold, deep waters.
family of marine fishes (snailfishes)

Examples

The deep-sea biologists discovered a new species within the Liparidae family.
Researchers are studying the unique adaptations of Liparidae to high-pressure environments.
Many Liparidae species have a gelatinous body that helps them survive in deep ocean waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snailfish - a small, soft-bodied marine fish of the family Liparidae

Etymology

From Greek *liparos* (fat, shining, oily), from which the type genus Liparis is derived.
