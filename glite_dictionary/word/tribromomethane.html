tribromomethane

1. NOUN

/ traɪˌbroʊmoʊˈmɛθeɪn / (AmE), / traɪˌbrəʊməˈmiːθeɪn / (BrE)
A colorless, heavy liquid haloform compound (CHBr₃), used in chemical synthesis and as a laboratory reagent.
a colorless liquid chemical compound (CHBr₃)

Examples

The laboratory ordered a fresh supply of tribromomethane for the organic chemistry experiments.
Due to its density, tribromomethane sinks quickly when poured into water.
Proper ventilation is required when handling tribromomethane because of its volatile nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bromoform - another name for tribromomethane (CHBr₃)

Etymology

From 'tri-' meaning three, 'bromo-' referring to bromine atoms, and 'methane'.
