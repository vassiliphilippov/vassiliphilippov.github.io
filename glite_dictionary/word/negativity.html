negativity

1. NOUN

/ ˌnɛɡəˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / ˌnɛɡəˈtɪvɪti / (BrE)
a tendency to be skeptical, disagreeable, or resistant to suggestions or commands, often characterized by pessimism or denial
habitual skepticism or disagreeableness

Examples

Her constant negativity made it difficult for the team to stay motivated.
You need to let go of negativity if you want to enjoy life more.
His negativity about every proposal was wearing down their enthusiasm.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: positivity - the practice of being optimistic or positive in attitude

Etymology

From negative + -ity, modeled after French négativité.

2. NOUN

/ ˌneɡəˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / ˌneɡəˈtɪvɪti / (BrE)
the property or character of being negatively charged, especially in relation to the negative pole of an electric cell.
state of being negatively charged

Examples

The negativity of the cathode enables it to attract positive ions.
Scientists measured the negativity at the electrode during the experiment.
Batteries function due to the differences in negativity and positivity between their terminals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: positivity - the property or character of being positively charged

Etymology

From negative + -ity, after Latin 'negativitas'.
