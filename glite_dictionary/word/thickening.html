thickening

1. ADJ

/ ˈθɪkənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθɪkənɪŋ / (BrE)
becoming denser or more intense, especially in a gradual way; increasing in accumulation or intensity.
becoming denser or more intense

Examples

The thickening fog made it impossible to see the road ahead.
As the evening went on, the thickening tension between the two became obvious to everyone.
We walked home through the thickening twilight after the sun had set.

Synset Examples

the thickening dusk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deepening - becoming deeper or more intense

2. NOUN

/ ˈθɪkənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθɪkənɪŋ / (BrE)
a substance added to make a liquid thicker or less runny, especially in cooking or manufacturing
substance used to make liquids thicker

Examples

Cornstarch is commonly used as a thickening in soups and sauces.
Egg yolk can serve as a natural thickening in custards.
Gelatin is sometimes added as a thickening for desserts and jellies.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thickener - a substance used to make a liquid thicker

Etymology

From 'thicken' + '-ing', denoting the action or material that thickens.

/ ˈθɪkənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθɪkənɪŋ / (BrE)

the process of becoming thicker
