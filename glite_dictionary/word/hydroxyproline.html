hydroxyproline

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌdrɑksiˈproʊlin / (AmE), / haɪˌdrɒksɪˈprəʊliːn / (BrE)
a crystalline amino acid, formed by hydroxylation of proline, commonly found in collagen and gelatin
an amino acid found in collagen
medical

Examples

Hydroxyproline is an important component of collagen, helping stabilize its triple-helical structure.
Laboratory analysis of urine can detect elevated hydroxyproline levels in certain bone diseases.
Gelatin contains a high concentration of hydroxyproline, which distinguishes it from many other proteins.

Etymology

From hydroxy- (indicating an -OH group) + proline (the parent amino acid).
