cytosine

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪ.təˌsin / (AmE), / ˈsaɪ.tə.siːn / (BrE)
a nitrogenous base found in DNA and RNA, paired with guanine, and derived from pyrimidine
a DNA/RNA base paired with guanine
medical

Examples

Cytosine is one of the four main bases found in DNA, along with adenine, guanine, and thymine.
The structure of cytosine enables it to pair specifically with guanine through hydrogen bonds.
Mutations involving cytosine can lead to changes in the genetic code.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guanine - a base found in DNA and RNA, pairs with cytosine

Etymology

From Greek kytos (hollow, cell) + -ine (chemical suffix).
