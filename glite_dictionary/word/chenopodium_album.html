Chenopodium album

1. NOUN

/ ˌkiːnoʊˈpoʊdiəm ˈælbəm / (AmE), / ˌkiːnəˈpəʊdiəm ˈælbəm / (BrE)
A common weedy plant native to Europe, often used as a potherb, and naturalized in North America; also known as lamb's quarters.
a weedy European plant; edible potherb

Examples

Many gardeners remove Chenopodium album from their vegetable patches to prevent overcrowding.
The leaves of Chenopodium album can be cooked and eaten like spinach.
Some herbalists value Chenopodium album for its nutritional properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lamb's quarters - Another name for Chenopodium album, a weedy edible plant

Etymology

From Latin: 'chenopodium' (from Greek 'khēn' meaning 'goose' and 'pous' meaning 'foot', referring to the shape of the leaves) and 'album' meaning 'white'.
