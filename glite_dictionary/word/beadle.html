beadle

1. NOUN

/ ˈbiːdəl / (AmE), / ˈbiːdəl / (BrE)
a minor parish official, often responsible for ceremonial duties and various administrative tasks in a church or synagogue
minor parish official with ceremonial duties

Examples

The beadle led the procession through the church at the start of the service.
As a beadle, he was responsible for maintaining order during parish meetings.
The old beadle had served the cathedral for over thirty years, always in his traditional uniform.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: verger - church official who looks after the interior, grounds, and sometimes leads processions

Etymology

From Old French *bedel*, from Medieval Latin *bedellus* (summoner, messenger), from *biddan* (to bid, command).
