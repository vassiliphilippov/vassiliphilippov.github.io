sorry

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɑːri / (AmE), / ˈsɒri / (BrE)
feeling or showing regret or sorrow for something that has happened or been done, especially a mistake or wrong
feeling regret or sorrow

Examples

He was sorry for the harsh words he had spoken.
I'm truly sorry that I missed your call last night.
She looked sorry as she apologized for the mistake.

Synset Examples

felt regretful over his vanished youth
regretful over mistakes she had made
he felt bad about breaking the vase

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regretful - feeling regret or sorrow about something past
  • Synonym: remorseful - feeling deep regret or guilt for a wrong committed
  • Antonym: proud - feeling deep pleasure or satisfaction as a contrast to feeling sorry

Etymology

From Old English sarig (distressed, grieved), related to 'sore.'

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɑri / (AmE), / ˈsɒri / (BrE)
Of poor quality or little worth; inadequate or pitiable.
of poor quality or little worth

Examples

He made a sorry attempt at an apology that convinced no one.
They were living in a sorry state after the storm destroyed their home.
That was a sorry excuse for a meal; I’m still hungry.

Synset Examples

a sorry horse
a sorry excuse
a lazy no-count, good-for-nothing goldbrick
the car was a no-good piece of junk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: worthless - having no value or use; completely without worth
  • Antonym: good - of high quality; valuable or useful
  • Degree Higher: excellent - of exceptionally high quality

3. ADJ

/ ˈsɑːri / (AmE), / ˈsɒri / (BrE)
(of a state, situation, or condition) poor in quality or unfortunate; deserving of pity or regret.
poor or unfortunate in quality or condition

Examples

After the storm, the garden was in a sorry state, with plants knocked over and debris everywhere.
The team's sorry performance disappointed their loyal fans.
He tried fixing the bike, but it was in such sorry condition that it barely worked.

Synset Examples

my finances were in a deplorable state
a lamentable decision
her clothes were in sad shape
a sorry state of affairs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deplorable - deserving strong condemnation; shockingly bad
  • Synonym: lamentable - regrettable; unfortunate
  • Antonym: excellent - exceptionally good; outstanding

Etymology

From Old English sārlic (distressing, grievous), related to sār (sore, pain).
