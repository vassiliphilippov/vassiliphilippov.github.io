to ambass

1. VERB

/ æmˈbæs / (AmE), / æmˈbæs / (BrE)
to serve as an official representative, advocate, or spokesperson for an organization, especially a charity or brand.
act as an official representative or spokesperson
informal

Examples

She was asked to ambass the local animal shelter for their new campaign.
Many celebrities ambass charities to raise awareness for important causes.
He loves to ambass the brand at events across the country.

Synset Examples

As a former athlete, he felt honored to ambass the charity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to represent - to act or speak on behalf of someone or something
  • Synonym: ambassador - an official representative of an organization or country

Etymology

Back-formation from 'ambassador.'
