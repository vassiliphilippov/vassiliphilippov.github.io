solicitorship

1. NOUN

/ səˈlɪsətɚˌʃɪp / (AmE), / səˈlɪsɪtəʃɪp / (BrE)
the office, position, or status of a solicitor, especially in a legal context
position or office of a solicitor
legal

Examples

After years of hard work, she finally obtained the solicitorship at the prestigious law firm.
He resigned from his solicitorship to pursue a career as a barrister.
Competition for the solicitorship was intense among recent law graduates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solicitor - a type of lawyer qualified to represent clients in some legal matters

Etymology

From 'solicitor' + '-ship', denoting the condition or office.
