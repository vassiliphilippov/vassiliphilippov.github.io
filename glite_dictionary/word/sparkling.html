sparkling

1. ADJ

/ ˈspɑrklɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspɑːklɪŋ / (BrE)
(of wine or water) containing bubbles of gas, either naturally or added artificially.
(of drink) containing bubbles of gas

Examples

She ordered a glass of sparkling water with lemon at the restaurant.
Many people prefer sparkling wine for celebrations and special occasions.
Would you like your water still or sparkling?

Synset Examples

sparkling wines
sparkling water

Related Terms

  • Antonym: still - (of drinks) not containing bubbles of gas; not sparkling
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02285028-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈspɑrk.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspɑːklɪŋ / (BrE)
shining with flashes of light; bright and lively in appearance.
shining with flashes of light

Examples

The sparkling snow covered the entire landscape, making it look magical.
She greeted us with sparkling eyes and a warm smile.
Champagne is known for its sparkling appearance and lively bubbles.

Synset Examples

sparkling snow
sparkling eyes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glittering - shining with a bright, shimmering reflected light
  • Antonym: dull - not bright or shiny; lacking luster

Etymology

From the verb 'sparkle' + -ing; 'sparkle' from Middle English 'sparklen', frequentative of 'spark'.
