lefty

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛfti / (AmE), / ˈlɛfti / (BrE)
(informal) a left-handed person, especially a baseball pitcher who throws with the left hand.
left-handed person or pitcher
informal american

Examples

The team brought in a veteran lefty to face the tough hitters in the ninth inning.
As a lefty, he has a natural curveball that confuses most batters.
Scouts are keeping an eye on the high school lefty with a fast pitch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: southpaw - a left-handed pitcher in baseball

Etymology

From 'left' + diminutive suffix '-y', used for informal or affectionate forms.
