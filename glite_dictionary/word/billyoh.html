billyoh

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɪlioʊ / (AmE), / ˈbɪliəʊ / (BrE)
(informal, British) An unimaginably large amount or to an extreme degree, commonly used in expressions such as 'like billyoh'.
unimaginably large amount (British informal)
informal british

Examples

When the storm hit, it rained billyoh and flooded the whole street.
He ran like billyoh to catch the last train home.
That old engine smokes billyoh when you start it up in the morning.

Synset Examples

British say ‘it rained like billyo’ where Americans say ‘it rained like all get out’

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly an alteration of the phrase 'like Billy-O', 19th-century British slang, the exact origin unknown.
