hyperlipemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpərlɪˈpiːmiə / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəlɪˈpiːmiə / (BrE)
the presence of an abnormally high concentration of lipids in the blood
excess lipids in the blood (medical)
medical

Examples

A diagnosis of hyperlipemia often leads to lifestyle changes and medication to reduce cardiovascular risk.
Her routine blood test revealed hyperlipemia, prompting further investigation by her doctor.
Doctors monitor patients with diabetes for signs of hyperlipemia to prevent complications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyperlipidemia - the presence of excess lipids in the blood (alternate spelling/term)
  • Antonym: hypolipidemia - abnormally low levels of lipids in the blood

Etymology

From Greek 'hyper-' (over, excessive) + 'lipos' (fat) + '-emia' (condition of the blood).
