sawbuck

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɔːˌbʌk / (AmE), / ˈsɔːˌbʌk / (BrE)
a frame, typically in the shape of an X, used to hold wood in place while it is being sawed
wood-holding frame for sawing

Examples

We placed the log on the sawbuck before starting to cut it.
Using a sawbuck made sawing the firewood much easier and safer.
The carpenter adjusted the sawbuck to hold larger pieces of timber.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sawhorse - a frame used to support wood for sawing

Etymology

Probably from the resemblance to the X-shaped legs of a sawhorse ('saw' + 'buck').
