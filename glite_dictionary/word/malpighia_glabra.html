Malpighia glabra

1. NOUN

/ mælˈpɪdʒiə ˈɡleɪbrə / (AmE), / malˈpɪɡɪə ˈɡleɪbrə / (BrE)
A tropical American shrub bearing edible, acidic red fruit that resembles cherries, also known as Barbados cherry.
tropical shrub with cherry-like fruit

Examples

The Malpighia glabra thrives in warm climates and produces an abundance of fruit each year.
Researchers studied the vitamin C content in Malpighia glabra compared to other fruits.
Gardeners often cultivate Malpighia glabra for its attractive, cherry-like berries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Barbados cherry - common name for Malpighia glabra

Etymology

From Latin; 'Malpighia' named after Marcello Malpighi, an Italian biologist, and 'glabra' meaning 'smooth' in Latin, referring to the plant's smooth leaves.
