to vesicate

1. VERB

/ ˈvɛsɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈvɛsɪkeɪt / (BrE)
(of skin) to form blisters or cause blisters to appear.
to form blisters
medical

Examples

Some chemical agents can vesicate the skin upon contact.
Prolonged exposure to the sun may vesicate sensitive areas of your body.
Doctors observed that the new ointment did not vesicate the patients' skin.

Synset Examples

Her feet blistered during the long hike

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blister - a small pocket of fluid within the upper layers of the skin, typically caused by friction or burn

Etymology

From Latin vesica (bladder, blister).
