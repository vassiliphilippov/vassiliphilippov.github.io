Corregidor

1. PROPN

/ kəˈrɛɡɪˌdɔr / (AmE), / kəˈrɛɡɪˌdɔː / (BrE)
an island at the entrance of Manila Bay in the Philippines, site of major battles during World War II between Japanese and American forces
island at entrance of Manila Bay, Philippines; WWII battle site

Examples

Many tourists visit Corregidor to learn about its pivotal role in World War II history.
The siege of Corregidor was a defining moment in the Pacific theater during 1942.
After months of resistance, Allied forces surrendered Corregidor to the Japanese army.

Etymology

From Spanish 'corregidor', meaning 'magistrate' or 'governor', reflecting the island's historical role during Spanish rule.
