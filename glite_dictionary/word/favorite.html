favorite

1. VERB

/ ˈfeɪv(ə)rɪt / (AmE), / ˈfeɪv(ə)rɪt / (BrE)
To indicate approval or mark an item as a personal favorite, especially by clicking a designated icon on social media platforms.
to mark as a favorite online
informal

Examples

I often favorite tweets that I want to read again later.
She favorited my photo on the app last night.
Have you favorited any new playlists this week?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: like - to express approval of online content
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-90000601-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪvərət / (AmE), / ˈfeɪvərɪt / (BrE)
A person or competitor who is considered most likely to win a competition or contest.
person likely to win; expected winner

Examples

After his impressive record this season, he is the clear favorite to win the tournament.
The horse named Thunder was the favorite according to most betting odds.
Despite being the favorite, she remained humble and focused throughout the competition.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: underdog - a competitor thought unlikely to win

Etymology

From Middle English, via Old French 'favorit', from Latin 'favorem' (favor).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10133522-n
Synset POS: n
