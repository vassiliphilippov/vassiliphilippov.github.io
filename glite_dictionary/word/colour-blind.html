colour-blind

1. ADJ

/ ˈkʌlər blaɪnd / (AmE), / ˈkʌlə blaɪnd / (BrE)
Unable to distinguish certain colors or to perceive color differences.
unable to distinguish certain colors
british medical

Examples

My grandfather is colour-blind and cannot tell the difference between red and green traffic lights.
As a colour-blind artist, she uses labels on her paint tubes to help her.
Certain professions may not be suitable for someone who is colour-blind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: color-blind - Unable to distinguish certain colors (American spelling)

2. ADJ

/ ˈkʌlɚ blaɪnd / (AmE), / ˈkʌlə blaɪnd / (BrE)
Treating people equally without regard to their race or ethnic background; unprejudiced about race.
unprejudiced about race
british

Examples

The company takes pride in its colour-blind hiring policies.
She was raised to be colour-blind and to treat everyone equally.
Some argue that a truly colour-blind society does not ignore the existence of racism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: color-blind - US spelling: unprejudiced about race
