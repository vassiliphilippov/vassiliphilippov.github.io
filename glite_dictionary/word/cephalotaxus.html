Cephalotaxus

1. PROPN

/ sɛfəloʊˈtæksəs / (AmE), / sɛfəˌləʊˈtæksəs / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen shrubs and small trees known as plum-yews, native to East Asia.

The leaves of Cephalotaxus are soft and needle-like, resembling those of yews.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛfəloʊˈtæksəs / (AmE), / ˌsɛfəʊləˈtæksəs / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen shrubs and small trees, commonly known as plum-yews, native to eastern Asia.
genus of evergreen shrubs (plum-yews)

Examples

Many gardeners appreciate the hardiness and unique foliage of Cephalotaxus in shady landscapes.
The Cephalotaxus species are resistant to deer and thrive in acidic soils.
Botanists have classified several varieties within the genus Cephalotaxus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plum-yew - common name for plants in the genus Cephalotaxus

Etymology

From Greek kephalē (head) and taxos (yew), indicating a yew-like plant with clustered fruits.
