mestranol

1. NOUN

/ ˈmestrəˌnɔl / (AmE), / ˈmestrənɒl / (BrE)
a synthetic estrogen used in combination with a progestin as an ingredient in oral contraceptives
synthetic estrogen in oral contraceptives
medical

Examples

Some early birth control pills contained mestranol as their primary estrogen component.
Researchers studied the effects of mestranol on hormone levels in women.
The dosage of mestranol in oral contraceptives is carefully regulated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethinylestradiol - a synthetic estrogen used in oral contraceptives
  • Degree Lower: estradiol - a natural estrogen hormone
  • Degree Lower: progestin - a synthetic form of progesterone used with estrogens in contraceptives

Etymology

From 'methyl' and 'estradiol', chemical elements in its synthesis.
