heliacal

1. ADJ

/ hiˈlaɪ.ə.kəl / (AmE), / hɪˈlaɪ.ə.kəl / (BrE)
relating to or occurring near the sun; especially describing the first visible rising or last visible setting of a star as it becomes lost in, or emerges from, the sun's glare.
relating to the sun, esp. star's first rising/setting

Examples

The ancient Egyptians based their calendar on the heliacal rising of Sirius.
Astronomers track the heliacal risings of bright stars for calendrical purposes.
The festival coincides with the heliacal rising of the constellation Orion.

Synset Examples

the heliacal rising of the Dog Star
the heliacal or Sothic year is determined by the heliacal rising of Sothis (the Egyptian name for the Dog Star)

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solar - relating to or determined by the sun

Etymology

From Greek hēliakos, from hēlios (sun).

2. ADJ

/ hɪˈlaɪəkəl / (AmE), / hɪˈlaɪək(ə)l / (BrE)
relating to or occurring near the sun; especially describing the first visible rising of a star after a period of invisibility due to its proximity to the sun.
relating to the sun or a star's visible rising near the sun

Examples

The ancient Egyptians marked the New Year by the heliacal rising of Sirius.
Many ancient calendars were based on heliacal phenomena.
Observers waited eagerly for the heliacal appearance of the planet, signaling the start of a new season.

Synset Examples

the heliacal rising of the Dog Star
the heliacal or Sothic year is determined by the heliacal rising of Sothis (the Egyptian name for the Dog Star)

Etymology

From Greek hēliakos, from hēlios (sun).
