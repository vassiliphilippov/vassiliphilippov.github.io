Montia chamissoi

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɒnti.ə kəˈmɪsoʊi / (AmE), / ˌmɒnti.ə kəˈmɪsɔɪ / (BrE)
A perennial aquatic or semi-aquatic plant of western North America, with floating or creeping stems and clusters of pale rose to white flowers.
a North American aquatic flowering plant

Examples

Montia chamissoi thrives in the shallow waters of mountain streams.
Many hikers admire the delicate flowers of Montia chamissoi during late spring.
Restoration efforts have helped reestablish Montia chamissoi in some protected wetlands.

Etymology

Named after Adelbert von Chamisso, German naturalist, and the genus Montia.
