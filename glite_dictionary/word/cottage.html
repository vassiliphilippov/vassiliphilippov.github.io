cottage

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.t̬ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɒt.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
A small house, typically one in the countryside and usually of a simple, single-story design.
small house, often in the countryside

Examples

They spent their summer holidays in a delightful cottage by the lake.
The artist painted a picture of the old stone cottage nestled in the hills.
She dreams of retiring to a quiet cottage in the countryside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cabin - a small, simple house or shelter, often made of wood

Etymology

From Old French *cote* (hut), later *cotage*; related to medieval Latin *cotagium*.
