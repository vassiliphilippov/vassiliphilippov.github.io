fastener

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæsənər / (AmE), / ˈfɑːsənə / (BrE)
a person who fastens or secures something
a person who fastens

Examples

The fastener of the gate must have been here recently, as the latch is still warm.
Detectives searched for the mysterious fastener who had locked the briefcase shut.
Who was the fastener responsible for shutting all the windows last night?

Synset Examples

he found the door fastened and wondered who the fastener was

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attacher - a person who attaches something
  • Antonym: unfastener - a person who unfastens or loosens something

Etymology

From the verb 'fasten' + -er (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfæsnər / (AmE), / ˈfɑːsnə / (BrE)
A device or mechanism used to attach or secure two or more objects together or to hold something in place.
device that holds or attaches things

Examples

She used a fastener to keep the bag closed during her flight.
The jacket's fastener broke, so he couldn't zip it up.
Many children's shoes have Velcro fasteners instead of laces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clasp - a device with interlocking parts used for fastening things together
  • Synonym: fastening - something that attaches or holds things together

Etymology

From 'fasten' + '-er', indicating something that fastens.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03328648-n
Synset POS: n
