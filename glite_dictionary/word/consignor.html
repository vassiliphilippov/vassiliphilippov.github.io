consignor

1. NOUN

/ kənˈsaɪnər / (AmE), / kənˈsʌɪnə / (BrE)
a person or company that sends goods to another, especially for sale or shipment
person who sends goods

Examples

The consignor must provide all necessary documentation before the shipment can be processed.
As the consignor, you are responsible for ensuring the goods are properly packed.
The contract specifies the rights and obligations of both the consignor and the consignee.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: consignee - person or company to whom goods are shipped

Etymology

From consign + -or, modeled on legal/Latin agent nouns.
