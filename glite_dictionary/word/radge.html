to radge

1. VERB

/ rædʒ / (AmE), / rædʒ / (BrE)
(informal, chiefly Scottish) To become extremely upset or angry.
become very angry (Scottish slang)
informal slang british

Examples

He started to radge when he lost his favorite game.
My brother will radge if he hears about the broken lamp.
Don’t radge just because things didn’t go your way.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rage - to become very angry

Etymology

Originally Scottish dialect, probably a variant of 'rage'.
