candlepin

1. NOUN

/ ˈkændlˌpɪn / (AmE), / ˈkændlˌpɪn / (BrE)
A thin, cylindrical bowling pin used in candlepin bowling, slimmer than a standard tenpin.
thin bowling pin used in candlepin bowling

Examples

She bowled a strike by knocking down every candlepin on her first try.
The shape of a candlepin makes the game more challenging than standard bowling.
Unlike a tenpin, a candlepin does not have a wide base.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tenpin - a larger, wider bowling pin used in tenpin bowling

Etymology

From the resemblance of the pin's shape to that of a candle; formed from 'candle' + 'pin'.
