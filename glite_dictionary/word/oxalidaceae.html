Oxalidaceae

1. NOUN

/ ɑˌksælɪˈdeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ɒkˌsælɪˈdeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
A large family of widely distributed herbaceous plants with compound leaves and five-parted flowers, including the wood sorrels.
a family of flowering plants (wood sorrels, etc.)

Examples

Several species in the Oxalidaceae family are cultivated as ornamentals because of their attractive foliage and flowers.
The Oxalidaceae are easily recognized by their characteristic trifoliate leaves and yellow or pink flowers.
Taxonomists debated the classification of Oxalidaceae within the order Geraniales for many years.

Etymology

From Modern Latin Oxalidaceae, based on the genus Oxalis (wood sorrel), from Greek 'oxalis' (sour, sharp).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑksəˌlɪdəˈsiː / (AmE), / ˌɒksəlɪdəˈsiː / (BrE)
a family of widely distributed herbaceous plants, commonly known as the wood-sorrel family, characterized by compound leaves and five-parted flowers.
herbaceous plant family (wood-sorrels)

Examples

The Oxalidaceae family includes many species known for their sour-tasting leaves.
Several ornamental plants belong to the Oxalidaceae family, making them popular in gardens.
Botanists often study the Oxalidaceae to learn about leaf structure and flower morphology.

Etymology

From the genus name Oxalis, from Greek oxys (sharp, acidic), referring to the sour taste of the leaves, with Latin botanical family suffix -aceae.
