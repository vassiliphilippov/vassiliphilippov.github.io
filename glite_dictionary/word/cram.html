to cram

1. VERB

/ kræm / (AmE), / kræm / (BrE)
to study intensely and quickly for an examination, usually in a short period of time
study intensively before an exam

Examples

I had to cram for my chemistry exam last night because I didn't study earlier.
Many students cram the night before finals instead of spreading out their studying.
She crammed all weekend to prepare for her medical school entrance test.

Synset Examples

I had to bone up on my Latin verbs before the final exam

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swot - to study very hard, especially for exams
  • Synonym: to bone up - to study intensively or review material quickly
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00607178-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kræm / (AmE), / kræm / (BrE)
to fill something completely by forcing things or people into it, often so that there is no space left
to pack tightly or completely

Examples

Tourists tried to cram onto the last bus leaving the station.
She managed to cram all her clothes into a single suitcase.
They crammed the tiny room with balloons for the party.

Synset Examples

the theater was jampacked

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stuff - to fill tightly with something
  • Synonym: jam - to squeeze or pack tightly into a space

Etymology

Probably from Middle English crammen (to press, stuff), from Old English crammian.

Source: WordNet

3. VERB

/ kræm / (AmE), / kræm / (BrE)
to fill a space completely by forcing things into it tightly
fill tightly; stuff into space

Examples

He tried to cram all his clothes into the small backpack.
Children crammed sweets into their pockets before leaving the party.
The suitcase was crammed with shoes and souvenirs from her trip.

Synset Examples

cram books into the suitcase

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stuff - to pack or fill tightly
  • Synonym: jam - to squeeze or pack tightly into a space

Etymology

Possibly from Middle English *crammen*, from Old English *crammian* (to stuff, press).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ kræm / (AmE), / kræm / (BrE)
to prepare hastily for an exam, especially by trying to learn a lot of information quickly before it
prepare hastily for an exam

Examples

We stayed up late to cram for the biology test.
He always tries to cram the night before his exams, but it rarely works well.
The teacher offered extra sessions to help students cram for the finals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swot - to study intensively, especially right before an exam (British)

Etymology

Probably from Middle English crammen, from Old English crammian (to stuff, cram).

Source: WordNet
