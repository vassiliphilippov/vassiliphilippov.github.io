Argiope aurantia

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈdʒaɪ.əpi ɔːrˈæn.ʃə / (AmE), / ɑːˈdʒaɪ.əpi ɔːˈræntiə / (BrE)
A large, brightly colored North American garden spider known for its distinctive yellow and black markings and orb-shaped webs.
a North American garden spider

Examples

Many gardeners are pleased to find a Argiope aurantia weaving its intricate web among their plants.
The striking yellow and black markings of the Argiope aurantia make it easy to identify.
I watched a Argiope aurantia capture and wrap a grasshopper in its web.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garden spider - common name for various orb-weaving spiders often found in gardens

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Argiope' and species name 'aurantia', meaning 'golden'.
