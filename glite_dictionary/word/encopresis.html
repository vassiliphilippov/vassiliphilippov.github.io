encopresis

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˌkɑːˈprisɪs / (AmE), / ɛnˌkɒˈpriːsɪs / (BrE)
involuntary passage of feces, especially in children, not caused by physical defects or illness
involuntary defecation (medical)
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the child with encopresis after several episodes of involuntary bowel movements.
Treatment for encopresis often involves behavioral therapy and changes in diet.
Parents of children with encopresis may need support and education about the condition.

Etymology

From Greek en- (in) + kopros (feces).
