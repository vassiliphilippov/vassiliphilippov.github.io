Coccoidea

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈkɔɪdiə / (AmE), / kɒˈkɔɪdiə / (BrE)
A superfamily of scale insects and mealybugs, comprising about 8,000 species, many of which are plant pests.
superfamily of scale insects and mealybugs

Examples

Several members of the Coccoidea cause significant damage to citrus crops worldwide.
Researchers are studying the biology of Coccoidea to develop better pest control methods.
The Coccoidea superfamily includes both scale insects and mealybugs.

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Greek kokkos (grain, berry), referring to the insects' appearance.

2. NOUN

/ koʊˈkɔɪdiə / (AmE), / kəˈkɔɪdiə / (BrE)
a superfamily of scale insects and mealybugs, containing around 8,000 species, many of which are pests of plants
superfamily of scale insects and mealybugs

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species within the Coccoidea superfamily during the rainforest expedition.
Many members of the Coccoidea are considered agricultural pests due to their sap-sucking habits.
The evolutionary relationships among families of the Coccoidea have been studied using genetic analysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scale insect - small insect that sucks plant sap and often secretes a waxy coating

Etymology

From modern Latin Coccoidea, based on Greek kokkos (berry, referring to the shape of scale insects) and the suffix -oidea (resembling, used in taxonomy).
