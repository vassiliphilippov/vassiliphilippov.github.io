Bibos frontalis

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪbɒs frɒnˈteɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˌbaɪbɒs frɒnˈtɑːlɪs / (BrE)
a large ox native to Southeast Asia, sometimes regarded as a domesticated form of the gaur
a domesticated Southeast Asian ox

Examples

The Bibos frontalis is known for its impressive size and powerful build.
Farmers in parts of Southeast Asia still use the Bibos frontalis as a working animal.
Unlike the wild gaur, the Bibos frontalis is sometimes kept in domesticated herds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gaur - a large wild ox native to South and Southeast Asia

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining the genus name Bibos (from Latin bibere, to drink) and specific epithet frontalis (Latin meaning 'of the forehead').
