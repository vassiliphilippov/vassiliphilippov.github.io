altered

1. ADJ

/ ˈɔltɚd / (AmE), / ˈɔːltəd / (BrE)
changed in order to improve something or make it suitable for a particular purpose
changed or modified

Examples

The altered schedule allowed students more time for assignments.
These pants have been altered to fit me better.
The altered document now contains the updated information.

Synset Examples

seeds precisely adapted to the area
instructions altered to suit the children's different ages

Related Terms

  • Synonym: modified - changed in some way, especially to improve or make more suitable
  • Antonym: unchanged - remaining the same as before, not changed

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'alter', from Latin alterare (to change).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɔltɚd / (AmE), / ˈɔːltəd / (BrE)
changed in form or character without becoming something different
changed in form or character

Examples

The doctor prescribed medication for the patient's altered mental state.
We noticed the altered design of the building when we walked by.
She returned from her trip with an altered perspective on life.

Synset Examples

the altered policy promised success
following an altered course we soon found ourselves back in civilization
he looked … with clouded eyes and with an altered manner of breathing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: changed - made different in some way
  • Antonym: unaltered - remaining in the original condition; not changed

Etymology

Past participle of "alter"; from Latin "alterare" (to change).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00353228-a

3. ADJ

/ ˈɔltɚd / (AmE), / ˈɔːltəd / (BrE)
(of an animal) having had the testicles or ovaries surgically removed, usually to prevent reproduction.
having testicles or ovaries removed (animals)

Examples

The animal shelter only adopts out altered dogs and cats to help control the pet population.
After she was altered, the cat became much calmer around other animals.
All of their pets are altered and up to date on vaccinations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: neutered - (of an animal) having reproductive organs removed, preventing reproduction
  • Synonym: spayed - (of a female animal) having the ovaries removed
  • Antonym: intact - (of an animal) not having had reproductive organs removed
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02144382-s
Synset POS: s
