sedation

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / sɪˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
the state of calm or sleepiness produced by the administration of a sedative drug
state induced by sedative drug
medical

Examples

The dentist will use sedation to help you relax during the procedure.
After taking the medication, a feeling of sedation quickly set in.
For minor surgeries, light sedation is often preferred over general anesthesia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anesthesia - insensitivity to pain, especially as induced for medical procedures
  • Antonym: stimulation - a state of heightened activity or arousal

Etymology

From Latin 'sedatio' (a calming), from 'sedare' (to calm, settle).

2. NOUN

/ səˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / sɪˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
the administration of a drug to produce a state of calm or sleep, especially before or during a medical procedure
administration of a sedative drug
medical

Examples

The dentist recommended sedation to help the anxious patient remain calm during the procedure.
After receiving sedation, the child was relaxed and ready for the minor surgery.
Patients often require sedation before undergoing an endoscopy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anesthesia - loss of sensation induced by drugs, often to allow medical procedures
  • Antonym: stimulation - encouragement of activity or increased alertness, often in contrast to calming or sedating

Etymology

From Latin sedatio (calming, soothing), from sedare (to calm).
