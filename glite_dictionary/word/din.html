din

1. VERB

/ dɪn / (AmE), / dɪn / (BrE)
to make someone learn or remember something by repeating it many times
instill by constant repetition

Examples

His parents dinned the importance of honesty into him every day.
The coach dinned the new strategy into the players until they memorized it.
She patiently dinned the safety rules into the children at the swimming pool.

Synset Examples

he dinned the lessons into his students

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drum - to repeat something persistently to make someone remember it
  • Synonym: hammer - to emphasize or repeat something forcefully or persistently

Etymology

From Old English "dyne" (loud noise), related to the noun "din."

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00608245-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ dɪn / (AmE), / dɪn / (BrE)
a loud, unpleasant, and prolonged noise, especially one caused by many people or things all making sound at once
a loud, continuous noise

Examples

The constant din from the construction site made it impossible to concentrate.
She could barely hear her phone over the din of the crowded café.
As the crowd cheered, the din rose to a deafening level.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clamor - a loud and confused noise, especially that of people shouting
  • Antonym: silence - complete absence of sound

Etymology

From Old English dyne (loud noise, clatter), related to Old Norse dynr (noise, roaring).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00554860-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ dɪn / (AmE), / dɪn / (BrE)
to make a loud, continuous, and often repeated noise
make a loud, continuous noise

Examples

The church bells dinned in the early morning, waking everyone in the village.
Artillery fire dinned across the landscape during the battle.
Her criticism dinned in his ears long after the argument had ended.

Synset Examples

His deep voice boomed through the hall

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boom - make a deep, resonant sound
  • Synonym: thunder - produce a loud, rumbling sound

Etymology

From Old English 'dynian' (to make a noise), related to 'dyn' (noise).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02191956-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ dɪn / (AmE), / dɪn / (BrE)
A loud, unpleasant, and prolonged noise, especially one that is harsh or confusing.
loud, harsh noise

Examples

The children made such a din in the hallway that the teacher had to intervene.
She could barely concentrate on her work because of the constant din from the street below.
After the fireworks ended, the sudden absence of the din was almost startling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clamor - a loud and confused noise, especially that of people shouting
  • Synonym: racket - a loud unpleasant noise
  • Antonym: silence - the absence of sound

Etymology

From Old English 'dyn' (noise) and related to Old Norse 'dynr' (noisy sound).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07392073-n
Synset POS: n
