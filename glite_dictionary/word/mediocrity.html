mediocrity

1. NOUN

/ ˌmiːdiˈɑːkrəti / (AmE), / ˌmiːdiˈɒkrəti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being average or unremarkable; lack of distinction or excellence
the state of being average or unremarkable

Examples

She was frustrated by the mediocrity that seemed to pervade the company's culture.
The film's mediocrity left critics unimpressed and audiences indifferent.
Despite his efforts, he resigned himself to a life of mediocrity.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: excellence - the quality of being outstanding or extremely good
  • Synonym: averageness - the quality of being average in quality or standard

Etymology

From Latin 'mediocritas', from 'mediocris' (moderate, ordinary).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmiːdiˈɑːkrəti / (AmE), / ˌmiːdiˈɒkrəti / (BrE)
a person who has only average or ordinary ability or value, not distinguished or outstanding in any way
person of only average ability or value

Examples

Despite years in the profession, he remained a mediocrity among rising stars.
She feared becoming just another mediocrity in her field, easily overlooked.
His achievements were limited, and his colleagues dismissed him as a mere mediocrity.

Synset Examples

a team of aging second-raters
shone among the mediocrities who surrounded him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonentity - a person of no importance or influence

Etymology

From Latin mediocritas (moderateness, mediocrity), from mediocris (moderate, ordinary).
