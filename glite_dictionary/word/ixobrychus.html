Ixobrychus

1. PROPN

/ ˌaɪksoʊˈbraɪkəs / (AmE), / ˌɪksəʊˈbrɪkəs / (BrE)
a genus of small, mostly secretive bittern birds found worldwide
genus of small bittern birds

Examples

The least bittern is a member of the Ixobrychus genus.
Researchers observed several Ixobrychus species nesting in the marshland.
The distribution of Ixobrychus spans multiple continents.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek 'ixos' (mistletoe, birdlime) + 'brychein' (to bellow).

2. NOUN

/ aɪkˈsɒbrɪkəs / (AmE), / ɪkˈsɒbrɪkəs / (BrE)
A genus of small bitterns in the heron family, found in wetland areas worldwide.
a genus of small bitterns (bird)

Examples

The least bittern belongs to the genus Ixobrychus, which includes several small heron species.
Researchers observed that birds in the Ixobrychus genus typically inhabit reeds and marshes.
Members of the Ixobrychus genus are noted for their secretive behavior and distinctive calls.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining Greek 'ixos' (mistletoe, birdlime) and 'brykhō' (to bellow).
