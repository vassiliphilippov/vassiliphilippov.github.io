to ablate

1. VERB

/ əˈbleɪt / (AmE), / əˈbleɪt / (BrE)
to remove material from the surface of something by erosion, melting, vaporization, or other processes
remove by erosion or vaporization

Examples

The surgeon used a laser to ablate the tumor tissue with precision.
During re-entry, the spacecraft's heat shield began to ablate due to intense friction.
Wind and water gradually ablate the mountain's rocky surface over centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to erode - to gradually wear away the surface of something

Etymology

From Latin ablat-, past participle of ablāre (to carry away).

2. VERB

/ əˈbleɪt / (AmE), / əˈbleɪt / (BrE)
to remove an organ, tissue, or bodily structure, typically by surgery or other medical procedures
to remove bodily tissue or organ
medical

Examples

The surgeon decided to ablate the tumor to prevent further complications.
Radiofrequency energy is often used to ablate abnormal heart tissue in arrhythmia patients.
In some cases, doctors may ablate part of the thyroid gland to treat hyperthyroidism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excise - to remove completely, especially by cutting

Etymology

From Latin ablat-, past participle of auferre (to carry away).
