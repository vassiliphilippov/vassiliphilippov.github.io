Erinaceus europaeus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛr.ɪˈneɪ.si.əs ˌjʊə.roʊˈpiː.əs / (AmE), / ˌɛr.ɪˈneɪ.si.əs ˌjʊə.rəˈpiː.əs / (BrE)
a small, nocturnal mammal of Europe, commonly known as the European hedgehog, covered with hair and short, sharp spines
European hedgehog species

Examples

The Erinaceus europaeus is commonly found in parks and gardens throughout Europe.
Gardeners often welcome Erinaceus europaeus because it feeds on insects and slugs.
Conservation groups are working to protect the natural habitat of Erinaceus europaeus across Britain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hedgehog - a small spiny mammal found in Europe, Asia, and Africa

Etymology

From Latin: Erinaceus (hedgehog) + europaeus (European).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛrɪˈneɪsiəs juːˌroʊˈpiːəs / (AmE), / ˌɛrɪˈneɪsiəs juːˌrəʊˈpiːəs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the European hedgehog, a small nocturnal mammal covered with hair and protective spines, native to Europe
European hedgehog (scientific name)

Examples

The Erinaceus europaeus is a common sight in European gardens during the evening.
Wildlife photographers often search for the elusive Erinaceus europaeus to capture its nocturnal activities.
Unlike some other hedgehog species, Erinaceus europaeus prefers habitats with dense undergrowth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hedgehog - a small nocturnal mammal covered with spines

Etymology

From Latin: 'erinaceus' meaning hedgehog and 'europaeus' meaning European.
