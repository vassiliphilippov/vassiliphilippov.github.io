apostolic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæpəˈstɑlɪk / (AmE), / ˌapəˈstɒlɪk / (BrE)
Relating to the Apostles or their teachings, especially those of the early Christian church.
relating to the Apostles or their teachings

Examples

The apostolic teachings played a central role in shaping early Christian doctrine.
He is known for his apostolic zeal in spreading the faith.
The church claims to preserve an apostolic tradition dating back to the first disciples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apostle - each of the twelve chief disciples of Jesus Christ

Etymology

From Late Latin apostolicus, from Greek apostolikos, from apostolos (apostle).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02870589-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˌæpəˈstɑːlɪk / (AmE), / ˌapəˈstɒlɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or ordered by the pope or the papacy, regarded as the successor of the Apostles.
of or pertaining to the authority of the pope

Examples

The order was given in an apostolic letter signed by the pope himself.
Certain privileges may only be granted through apostolic authority within the Catholic Church.
He requested an apostolic blessing for the new cathedral.

Synset Examples

papal dispensation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: papal - relating to the pope

Etymology

From Late Latin apostolicus (of an apostle), from Greek apostolikos.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02890942-a
Synset POS: a
