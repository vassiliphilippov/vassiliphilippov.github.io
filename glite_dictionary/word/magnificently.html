magnificently

1. ADV

/ mæɡˈnɪfəsəntli / (AmE), / mæɡˈnɪfɪsəntli / (BrE)
in an impressive, splendid, or very beautiful manner
in an impressive or splendid manner

Examples

The cathedral was magnificently illuminated for the festival.
She performed magnificently on stage, captivating the entire audience.
The dinner was magnificently prepared, with every detail carefully considered.

Synset Examples

he did splendidly in the exam
we got along famously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splendidly - in a magnificent or very impressive manner
  • Synonym: wonderfully - in a way that inspires delight or admiration

Etymology

From 'magnificent' + '-ly'; 'magnificent' from Latin 'magnificus' (grand, noble).

2. ADV

/ mæɡˈnɪfəsəntli / (AmE), / mæɡˈnɪfɪsəntli / (BrE)
in a way that is impressively beautiful, grand, or splendid
in a splendid or impressive manner

Examples

The palace was decorated magnificently for the royal wedding.
She performed magnificently on stage, captivating the entire audience.
The mountains rose magnificently against the evening sky.

Synset Examples

the Princess was gorgeously dressed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: splendidly - in a splendid or impressive way
  • Synonym: grandly - in a grand or impressive manner
  • Antonym: poorly - in a way that is not impressive or beautiful

Etymology

From Latin 'magnificentia' (greatness, grandeur), via Old French 'magnificence'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00351959-r
Synset POS: r
