reflexology

1. NOUN

/ ˌriːflɛkˈsɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌriːflɛkˈsɒlədʒi / (BrE)
A therapeutic method involving massage and finger pressure on specific points of the feet, hands, or head, believed to correspond to different areas of the body and relieve tension or illness.
massage-based therapy using pressure points

Examples

Many people use reflexology to alleviate stress and promote relaxation.
He booked a reflexology session to address his chronic headaches.
The spa offers a range of therapies, including reflexology and aromatherapy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: massage - manual manipulation of soft tissues for relaxation or health

Etymology

From 'reflex' and '-logy', meaning the study of reflexes.

2. NOUN

/ ˌriː.flɛkˈsɑː.lə.dʒi / (AmE), / ˌriː.flɛkˈsɒl.ə.dʒi / (BrE)
the study of reflex action and its influence on the behavior of organisms
study of reflex actions in organisms

Examples

She published a paper on reflexology and animal behavior.
The field of reflexology explores how reflexes shape an organism's responses to stimuli.
Modern reflexology began with early research into the nervous systems of simple organisms.

Etymology

From 'reflex' + '-ology' (the study of).
