Prunus serrulata

1. NOUN

/ ˈpruː.nəs səˈruː.lə.tə / (AmE), / ˈpruː.nəs səˈrʌl.ə.tə / (BrE)
A species of ornamental cherry tree, native to East Asia, widely cultivated for its clusters of showy white or pink blossoms; also known as the Japanese cherry.
ornamental cherry tree with showy blossoms

Examples

Every spring, the park is filled with tourists eager to see the Prunus serrulata in full bloom.
Gardeners often plant Prunus serrulata for its spectacular springtime flowers.
The delicate petals of Prunus serrulata are a symbol of renewal in Japanese culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cherry blossom - flowers of the cherry tree, especially Prunus serrulata

Etymology

From Latin 'prunus' (plum tree) + 'serrulata' (finely saw-toothed), referring to the leaf edges.
