impureness

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpjʊrnəs / (AmE), / ɪmˈpjʊənəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being impure; lack of purity.
state of being impure

Examples

The impureness of the water made it unsafe to drink.
Religious practices often emphasize ritual cleansing to remove impureness.
Any impureness in the sample could affect the experiment's results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impurity - the state or quality of being impure

Etymology

From Latin 'impurus' (unclean, impure) + English noun-forming suffix '-ness'.
