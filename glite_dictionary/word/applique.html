to applique

1. VERB

/ ˌæplɪˈkeɪ / (AmE), / ˌæplɪˈkeɪ / (BrE)
to sew or attach a decorative piece of fabric onto another fabric as decoration.
sew fabric decoration onto another

Examples

She decided to applique a butterfly onto her daughter's dress.
The artisan carefully appliqued velvet shapes onto the quilt.
He enjoys appliqueing colorful designs on bags as a hobby.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to embroider - to decorate fabric with needlework designs

Etymology

From French 'appliqué', past participle of 'appliquer' (to apply), from Latin 'applicare'.
