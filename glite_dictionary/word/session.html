session

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛʃən / (AmE), / ˈsɛʃən / (BrE)
a period during which classes are held at a school or institution
school term or class period

Examples

The new academic year will start with the first session next Monday.
Due to snowstorms, the morning session at the school was canceled.
Each session lasts fifty minutes, followed by a ten-minute break.

Synset Examples

they had to shorten the school term

Etymology

From Latin 'sessio' (a sitting), from 'sedere' (to sit).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛʃən / (AmE), / ˈsɛʃən / (BrE)
a period of time dedicated to a particular activity, especially a meeting, class, or event
period devoted to a specific activity or meeting

Examples

The committee held a special session to discuss the new policy.
She booked a session with her personal trainer for Friday morning.
We had a productive brainstorming session that resulted in some great ideas.

Synset Examples

a filming session
a gossip session

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meeting - an act of coming together for a discussion or activity

Etymology

From Latin 'sessio' (sitting), from 'sedere' (to sit).

3. NOUN

/ ˈsɛʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˈseʃ.ən / (BrE)
a period during which a group, such as a legislature or court, meets to conduct its business
meeting period of a group or organization

Examples

The annual budget is discussed during the first session of parliament.
The court resumed its session after a short break.
Each session of the conference will focus on a different theme.

Synset Examples

it was the opening session of the legislature

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meeting - an assembly of people for a particular purpose

Etymology

From Latin 'sessio' (a sitting), from 'sedere' (to sit).
