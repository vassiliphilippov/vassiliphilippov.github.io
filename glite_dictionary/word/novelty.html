novelty

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑːvəlti / (AmE), / ˈnɒvəlti / (BrE)
the quality of being new, original, or unusual, often in a way that is interesting or surprising
the quality of being new or unusual

Examples

The novelty of the new smartphone quickly attracted attention from tech enthusiasts.
Although the novelty of working from home wore off, many still enjoy its flexibility.
The exhibition's main appeal lay in the sheer novelty of its interactive displays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: originality - the quality of being novel or unusual

Etymology

From Old French 'novelty', from Latin 'novellitas', from 'novellus' (new).

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɑːvəlti / (AmE), / ˈnɒvəlti / (BrE)
a small inexpensive mass-produced article, often bought as a gift or souvenir
small inexpensive mass-produced item

Examples

The shop sells all sorts of novelties for tourists, including keychains and magnets.
Every year, the company gives away holiday novelties to its employees as a token of appreciation.
He bought a silly novelty hat for the party, which made everyone laugh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trinket - a small ornament or item of jewelry that is of little value
  • Synonym: souvenir - an object kept as a reminder of a person, place, or event

Etymology

From Latin novellitas (newness), via Old French nouveleté, referring to new or unusual things.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03628657-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈnɑːvəlti / (AmE), / ˈnɒvəlti / (BrE)
cheap and showy jewelry or an ornament worn on clothing, often considered tasteless or of little value
cheap showy jewelry or ornament

Examples

She loved to decorate her bag with every new novelty she could find at the market.
The stall was filled with colorful novelties and trinkets for children.
Some people enjoy collecting novelty items, even if they are cheap and flashy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bauble - a small, showy trinket or decoration
  • Synonym: trinket - a small ornament, typically of little value

Etymology

From Latin 'novellitas' via Old French 'novellet', meaning 'newness'; this sense refers to small items made for their novelty value.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈnɑːvəlti / (AmE), / ˈnɒvəlti / (BrE)
the quality of being new, original, or refreshingly different
originality; newness or freshness

Examples

The novelty of their approach caught the judge's attention.
Traveling to a new country brings a sense of novelty to everyday life.
The product's novelty soon wore off, and sales declined.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: originality - the ability to think or create in an independent and fresh way
  • Antonym: conventionality - the quality of being traditional and lacking originality

Etymology

From Latin 'novellus' (new, young), diminutive of 'novus' (new).
