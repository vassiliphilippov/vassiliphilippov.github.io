disparateness

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈspɛr.ət.nəs / (AmE), / dɪˈspær.ət.nɪs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being completely different or dissimilar
the state of being very different

Examples

The disparateness of their backgrounds made collaboration a challenge at first.
There was a striking disparateness in the data gathered from different regions.
Critics pointed out the disparateness of the styles in the artist's earlier and later works.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: similarity - the state of being similar or alike
  • Synonym: difference - the quality or state of being different

Etymology

From the adjective 'disparate' (from Latin disparātus, 'separated, distinct'), plus the suffix '-ness'.
