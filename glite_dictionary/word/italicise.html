to italicise

1. VERB

/ ɪˈtælɪsaɪz / (AmE), / ɪˈtælɪsaɪz / (BrE)
to print or write (text) in italics for emphasis or distinction
print in italics
british

Examples

The author chose to italicise the foreign words for emphasis.
Please italicise the book titles in your essay.
She italicised the key points to make them stand out.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to italicize - to print in italics (American spelling)

Etymology

From 'italic' (typeface) + -ise (verb-forming suffix).
