stroller

1. NOUN

/ ˈstroʊlɚ / (AmE), / ˈstrəʊlə / (BrE)
a small, folding vehicle with four wheels in which a baby or young child can be pushed around while seated
pushchair for babies or young children

Examples

She put her baby in the stroller and went for a walk in the park.
The stroller folds easily and fits in the trunk of the car.
Parents pushed their children in strollers along the busy sidewalk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pushchair - a small chair on wheels for pushing a baby or young child
  • Synonym: pram - a baby carriage, especially in British English

2. NOUN

/ ˈstroʊlɚ / (AmE), / ˈstrəʊlə / (BrE)
a person who walks at a slow, relaxed pace, typically for pleasure rather than necessity
someone who walks at a leisurely pace

Examples

The park was filled with strollers enjoying the sunny afternoon.
As a habitual stroller, he loved to take his time along the riverbank.
Each morning, a group of elderly strollers gathered on the main street.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wanderer - someone who travels without a fixed path or destination
  • Antonym: hiker - someone who walks long distances in the countryside

Etymology

From the verb 'stroll,' of imitative origin, plus agent suffix '-er.'
