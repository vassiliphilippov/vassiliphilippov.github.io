to deaminize

1. VERB

/ diˈæməˌnaɪz / (AmE), / diˈæməˌnaɪz / (BrE)
to remove the amino group from a compound, usually by hydrolysis
remove amino group from a compound
medical

Examples

The enzyme acts to deaminize certain amino acids during metabolism.
Researchers can deaminize the compound to study the resulting chemical changes.
It is possible to deaminize cytosine residues in DNA using specific reagents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deaminate - to remove an amino group from a chemical compound

Etymology

From 'de-' (removal) and 'amine' + '-ize' (to make or become).
