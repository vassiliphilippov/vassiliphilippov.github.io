nitrobenzene

1. NOUN

/ ˌnaɪtroʊˈbɛnziːn / (AmE), / ˌnɪtrəʊˈbɛnziːn / (BrE)
a poisonous yellow oily liquid with the chemical formula C6H5NO2, used as a solvent and in the manufacture of aniline and other chemicals
poisonous oily chemical liquid, solvent, used in manufacturing

Examples

The chemical plant uses nitrobenzene as an intermediate in the production of dyes.
Exposure to nitrobenzene can be hazardous, so workers wear protective gear.
Researchers analyzed the effects of nitrobenzene contamination in groundwater.

Etymology

From nitro- (relating to nitrogen compounds) + benzene (the parent aromatic hydrocarbon).
