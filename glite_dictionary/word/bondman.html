bondman

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑnd.mæn / (AmE), / ˈbɒnd.mən / (BrE)
a male slave, especially one bound to work for a master
male slave; serf
archaic

Examples

In medieval times, a bondman had little hope of gaining his freedom.
The law declared every bondman on the estate belonged to its lord.
He worked as a bondman, toiling in the fields from sunrise to sunset.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slave - a person who is the legal property of another and forced to obey them
  • Antonym: freeman - a person not in slavery or serfdom

Etymology

From Middle English bondeman, from Old English bonda (householder, tenant farmer) + man.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːndˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈbɒndmən / (BrE)
a male slave or serf; a man who is bound to serve another without wages
male slave or serf
archaic

Examples

In the feudal era, a bondman worked the land at his lord’s command.
He was no longer a free citizen but had become his master’s bondman.
The law emancipated every bondman within the kingdom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slave - a person who is the legal property of another and forced to obey them
  • Antonym: freeman - a person who is not a slave or serf; a person with full civil rights

Etymology

From Middle English 'bondeman', from Old Norse 'bondi' (farmer, householder) + 'man'.
