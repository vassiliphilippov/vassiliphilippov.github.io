jerky

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒɝːki / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːki / (BrE)
characterized by abrupt, uneven, or sudden movements or transitions.
abrupt or uneven in movement or change

Examples

His handwriting was so jerky that it was hard to read.
The car made a jerky stop at the traffic light.
The film's editing was criticized for its jerky scene transitions.

Synset Examples

choppy prose

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smooth - having an even, continuous movement or transition
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01147235-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈdʒɝki / (AmE), / ˈdʒəːki / (BrE)
moving in sudden, uneven, or irregular motions; lacking a steady rhythm.
moving with sudden, uneven motions

Examples

The bus came to a jerky stop, making everyone sway in their seats.
His jerky handwriting made the note hard to read.
The film was difficult to watch because of its jerky animation.

Synset Examples

an arrhythmic heartbeat

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smooth - having an even and regular movement or surface

Etymology

Probably from 'jerk' (to move with a sudden motion) + -y.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02311256-s
Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɝːki / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːki / (BrE)
meat, especially beef, that is cut into strips and dried, often by sun or low heat, to preserve it.
strips of dried meat

Examples

We packed some jerky for our hiking trip because it lasts a long time without refrigeration.
She offered me a piece of homemade jerky seasoned with pepper and garlic.
Beef jerky is a popular snack among travelers and campers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: biltong - a South African dried, cured meat snack

Etymology

Perhaps from North American English, possibly related to Quechua ch'arki (dried, salted meat).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07680515-n
Synset POS: n
