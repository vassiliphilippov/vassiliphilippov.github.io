hippopotamus

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɪpəˈpɑtəməs / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəˈpɒtəməs / (BrE)
a large, thick-skinned, mostly herbivorous mammal that lives in or near rivers in tropical Africa
large African river mammal

Examples

During their safari, the tourists saw a huge hippopotamus lounging by the riverbank.
A hippopotamus can spend several hours submerged in water to keep cool.
Despite its bulky appearance, the hippopotamus can run surprisingly fast on land.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hippo - informal abbreviation of 'hippopotamus'

Etymology

From Greek 'hippos' (horse) + 'potamos' (river), meaning 'river horse'.

2. NOUN

/ hɪpəˈpɑtəməs / (AmE), / hɪpəˈpɒtəməs / (BrE)
a large, mostly herbivorous mammal with thick skin that lives in or near rivers in tropical Africa
large African river-dwelling mammal

Examples

The hippopotamus spends much of the day submerged in river water to keep cool.
At the zoo, we watched a hippopotamus yawn and reveal its giant teeth.
A fully grown hippopotamus can weigh more than three thousand kilograms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hippo - shortened informal form of 'hippopotamus'

Etymology

From Greek 'hippos' (horse) and 'potamos' (river), meaning 'river horse'.
