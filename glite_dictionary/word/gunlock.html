gunlock

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡʌnˌlɑk / (AmE), / ˈɡʌn.lɒk / (BrE)
a mechanism on a firearm that ignites the charge, setting off the gunpowder to fire the projectile
mechanism that ignites a firearm's charge

Examples

The antique musket's gunlock was carefully restored by the collector.
An effective gunlock made the difference in early firearms technology.
She studied the gunlock to understand how flintlock rifles were fired.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firing mechanism - mechanism that initiates gunfire

Etymology

From 'gun' + 'lock', where 'lock' refers to a firing mechanism.
