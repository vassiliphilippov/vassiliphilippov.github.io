brigand

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɪɡənd / (AmE), / ˈbrɪɡənd / (BrE)
an armed thief, especially one of a group that lives by plundering or robbing people in remote or wild regions
armed thief, member of a band

Examples

Legends tell of the notorious brigand who terrorized travelers crossing the old mountain pass.
The police arrested a group of brigands hiding in the forest after a series of robberies.
He was mistaken for a brigand because of his rugged appearance and the weapon he carried.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bandit - an outlaw who lives by plundering, especially in a band
  • Synonym: robber - a person who steals, especially using force or threats
  • Antonym: lawman - a law officer, such as a sheriff or marshal

Etymology

From Old French brigand (armed foot soldier, bandit), from Italian brigante (skirmisher, bandit), from 'brigare' (to fight, brawl).
