Gerbillus

1. NOUN

/ dʒərˈbɪləs / (AmE), / dʒəˈbɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of rodents comprising the typical gerbils, native to Africa and Asia.
genus of typical gerbils

Examples

The genus Gerbillus includes many species of gerbils found in desert regions.
Scientists discovered a new species belonging to Gerbillus in northern Africa.
Most laboratory gerbils are derived from the Mongolian species within Gerbillus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gerbil - a small burrowing rodent kept as a pet or found in the wild

Etymology

From modern Latin, adopted for scientific classification.

2. NOUN

/ dʒərˈbɪləs / (AmE), / dʒəˈbɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of small rodents in the subfamily Gerbillinae, comprising the typical gerbils found in Africa and Asia.
genus of typical gerbils

Examples

The Gerbillus genus includes many species adapted to arid environments.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Gerbillus genus in North Africa.
Members of the Gerbillus genus are popular in laboratory research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gerbil - a small, burrowing rodent often kept as a pet

Etymology

From New Latin 'Gerbillus', diminutive of Latin 'gerbillus', related to 'gerbo', meaning gerbil.
