gagwoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæɡˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈɡaɡˌwʊmən / (BrE)
A woman who writes comic material, such as jokes or sketches, for public performers.
female comic material writer

Examples

The famous comedian hired a talented gagwoman to freshen up her material.
Many television shows rely on a skilled gagwoman to generate laughs behind the scenes.
As a pioneering gagwoman, she broke barriers in a male-dominated comedy writing industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gagman - a man who writes comic material for public performers

Etymology

Formed from 'gag' (joke, comic line) + 'woman', by analogy with 'gagman'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡæɡˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈɡaɡˌwʊmən / (BrE)
A female comedian who performs gags or comedic routines.
female comedian performing gags

Examples

The popular gagwoman delivered hilarious sketches on last night's show.
As a pioneering gagwoman, she broke many barriers in the comedy industry.
Several gagwomen have gained fame on variety television programs in recent years.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: gagman - a male comedian who performs gags
  • Synonym: comedian - a person whose profession is to entertain others by making them laugh

Etymology

From 'gag' (a joke or prank) + 'woman'. Formed by analogy with 'gagman', mainly in entertainment industry contexts.
