to demilitarise

1. VERB

/ diˈmɪlɪtəˌraɪz / (AmE), / diˈmɪlɪtəraɪz / (BrE)
to remove military forces or weapons from (an area) or to deprive of military capability.
remove military forces/weapons

Examples

Following the treaty, both countries agreed to demilitarise the border region.
The government decided to demilitarise the area to promote peace and stability.
Several European powers pushed to demilitarise the conflict zone after years of unrest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to demilitarize - Alternative spelling, chiefly American
  • Antonym: to militarise - to equip or supply with military forces

Etymology

From de- (removal) + militarise, based on military.

2. VERB

/ diːˈmɪlɪtəˌraɪz / (AmE), / diːˈmɪlɪtəraɪz / (BrE)
to remove or prohibit military forces or installations from (an area) or to do away with its military capability
remove military forces or potential

Examples

The countries agreed to demilitarise the border region after years of conflict.
After the peace treaty, the government began to demilitarise its coastal cities.
The United Nations sent observers to ensure both sides would demilitarise the disputed area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to demilitarize - American spelling meaning to remove military capability from an area
  • Antonym: to militarise - to equip or prepare for war or military action

Etymology

From de- (removal) + militarise, based on Latin 'militaris' (of soldiers).
