Saint Petersburg

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈpiːtərzbɚɡ / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈpiːtəzbɜːɡ / (BrE)
a port city in northwestern Russia, formerly the capital, situated at the head of the Gulf of Finland; Russia's second largest city.
city in northwestern Russia; former capital

Examples

Many tourists visit Saint Petersburg to experience its famous White Nights and grand palaces.
The Hermitage Museum in Saint Petersburg houses one of the world's largest art collections.
After the Russian Revolution, Saint Petersburg was renamed Leningrad for several decades.

Etymology

Named after Saint Peter and modeled after Western European cities; earlier known as Petrograd and Leningrad.

2. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈpiːtɚzˌbɝɡ / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈpiːtəzbɜːɡ / (BrE)
A city in western Florida, situated on Tampa Bay, known as a popular winter resort.
city in western Florida, winter resort

Examples

Many retirees choose to spend their winters in Saint Petersburg for its mild climate and beautiful beaches.
The Saint Petersburg waterfront area is known for its vibrant arts scene and museums.
Every year, tourists flock to Saint Petersburg to enjoy festivals and outdoor activities.

Etymology

Named after Saint Petersburg, Russia.
