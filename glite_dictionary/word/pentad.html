pentad

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛn.tæd / (AmE), / ˈpɛn.tæd / (BrE)
A group or set of five; the number five.
group of five; the number five

Examples

The scientist noted that the element formed a stable pentad in the compound.
In ancient mythology, the pentad often symbolized balance and harmony.
The panel was composed of a pentad of distinguished judges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quintet - a group of five people or things
  • Degree Lower: quartet - a group of four people or things
  • Degree Higher: hexad - a group of six people or things

Etymology

From Greek 'pentas' (group of five), from 'pente' (five).
