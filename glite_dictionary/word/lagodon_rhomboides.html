Lagodon rhomboides

1. NOUN

/ ləˈɡoʊdən roʊmˈbɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / ləˈɡəʊdən rɒmˈbɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A species of small spiny-finned fish, commonly known as the pinfish, found in bays along the southeastern coast of the United States.
species of small spiny-finned fish (pinfish)

Examples

The Lagodon rhomboides is a common catch for anglers along the Florida coast.
Marine biologists study Lagodon rhomboides to monitor the health of bay ecosystems.
Young Lagodon rhomboides often inhabit seagrass beds for protection from predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pinfish - Common name for Lagodon rhomboides

Etymology

From Greek 'lagōs' (hare) and 'odous' (tooth), and 'rhomboides' (rhomboid-shaped).
