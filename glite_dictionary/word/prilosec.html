Prilosec

1. NOUN

/ ˈpraɪloʊˌsɛk / (AmE), / ˈpraɪləsɛk / (BrE)
A trademarked antacid medication used to decrease the amount of acid produced in the stomach, commonly prescribed for acid reflux and ulcers.
antacid medication (brand name)
medical american

Examples

My doctor recommended Prilosec to help manage my chronic heartburn.
After taking Prilosec for two weeks, her symptoms improved significantly.
It’s important to take Prilosec before meals for the best results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: omeprazole - the generic name for Prilosec, a proton pump inhibitor
