betrothal

1. NOUN

/ bɪˈtroʊθəl / (AmE), / bɪˈtrəʊθəl / (BrE)
the formal act or state of being engaged to be married
engagement to be married

Examples

Their betrothal was announced to the family during a special dinner.
The custom of arranging a formal betrothal still exists in some cultures.
She wore the ring her grandmother received at her betrothal decades ago.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engagement - a formal agreement to get married

Etymology

From Old English 'betrothian' (to pledge), from 'be-' (about) + 'troth' (truth, loyalty).

2. NOUN

/ bɪˈtroʊθəl / (AmE), / bɪˈtrəʊθəl / (BrE)
a formal mutual promise or agreement to marry
formal promise to marry

Examples

Their betrothal was announced at a grand family gathering last summer.
The novel focuses on the complicated betrothal between the main characters.
In some cultures, a formal betrothal ceremony takes place before the wedding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engagement - a formal agreement to marry
  • Degree Higher: marriage - the legally or formally recognized union of two people as partners
  • Antonym: separation - ending or withdrawing from a previously agreed union

Etymology

From Old English 'betrothian' (to pledge to marry), based on 'be-' (about) + 'troth' (truth, fidelity).
