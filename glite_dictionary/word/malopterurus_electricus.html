Malopterurus electricus

1. NOUN

/ məˌlɑptəˈrʊərəs ɪˌlɛkˈtrɪkəs / (AmE), / məˌlæptəˈrʊərəs ɪˌlɛkˈtrɪkəs / (BrE)
a species of freshwater catfish found in the Nile and central Africa, notable for possessing an electric organ used to generate electric shocks
an electric catfish species of Nile and central Africa

Examples

The Malopterurus electricus is capable of delivering strong electric shocks to defend itself from predators.
Researchers studied the behavior of Malopterurus electricus in the freshwater habitats of central Africa.
Aquariums housing a Malopterurus electricus must be specially equipped due to its electric organ.

Etymology

From Greek 'malos' (soft), 'pteron' (fin), and Latin 'electricus' (producing electricity).
