ternion

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɝːniən / (AmE), / ˈtɜːnɪən / (BrE)
A group or set of three; the number three.
a group or set of three

Examples

A ternion of knights rode into the courtyard, their armor shining in the sunlight.
The artist completed a ternion of paintings to explore the theme in depth.
In ancient texts, a ternion often symbolizes balance and harmony among three forces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trio - a group or set of three
  • Synonym: trinity - a group of three closely related things or people

Etymology

From Latin 'ternion-', from 'terni' (three each).
