scoliosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌskoʊliˈoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌskəʊliˈəʊsɪs / (BrE)
an abnormal lateral curvature of the spine
abnormal spinal curvature
medical

Examples

Her doctor diagnosed her with scoliosis after noticing her uneven shoulders.
Physical therapy can be part of the treatment plan for children with scoliosis.
Severe cases of scoliosis sometimes require surgery to correct spinal curvature.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: kyphosis - abnormal forward curvature of the spine
  • Degree Higher: lordosis - excessive inward curvature of the spine

Etymology

From Greek skoliosis (crookedness), from skolios (bent, crooked).
