nakedwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪkɪdwʊd / (AmE), / ˈneɪkɪdwʊd / (BrE)
A tree of extreme southern Florida and the West Indies with thin scaly bark, aromatic fruits and seeds, and yielding hard, heavy, close-grained zebrawood.
a tropical tree producing zebrawood

Examples

The furniture was crafted from rare nakedwood imported from the West Indies.
Botanists are studying the nakedwood for its aromatic seeds and potential medicinal uses.
The scaly bark of the nakedwood helps distinguish it from other trees in southern Florida.

2. NOUN

/ ˈneɪkɪdˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈneɪkɪdˌwʊd / (BrE)
Any of several small to medium-sized trees native to Florida and the West Indies, characterized by thin scaly bark and heavy dark heartwood.
tree with scaly bark and dark heartwood

Examples

The nakedwood is highly valued for its durable heartwood in local carpentry.
Botanists conducted a study on the growth patterns of nakedwood in the West Indies.
Many species of nakedwood are important for maintaining native bird habitats.
