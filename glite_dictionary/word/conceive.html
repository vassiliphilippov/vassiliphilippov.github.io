to conceive

1. VERB

/ kənˈsiv / (AmE), / kənˈsiːv / (BrE)
to form or develop an idea, plan, or intention in the mind
form or create an idea in the mind

Examples

The architect conceived an innovative design for the new museum.
She conceived the idea while traveling abroad last year.
A solution to the problem was finally conceived after hours of discussion.

Synset Examples

He conceived of a robot that would help paralyzed patients
This library was well conceived

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devise - to plan or invent (a complex procedure, system, or mechanism)
  • Synonym: to imagine - to form a mental image or concept of
  • Antonym: to forget - to fail to remember, to not bring to mind

Etymology

From Latin 'concipere' (to take in, conceive), from 'com-' (together) + 'capere' (to take).

2. VERB

/ kənˈsiv / (AmE), / kənˈsiːv / (BrE)
to become pregnant; to cause a child to begin to form in the womb
become pregnant

Examples

After years of trying, they were overjoyed when she finally conceived.
Some couples find it difficult to conceive naturally.
Their second child was conceived during their vacation in Italy.

Synset Examples

She cannot conceive
My daughter was conceived on Christmas Day

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to miscarry - to lose a pregnancy
  • Synonym: to fertilize - to cause an egg or ovum to develop by joining with a sperm

Etymology

From Latin concipere (to take in, become pregnant), from com- (together) + capere (to take).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00054002-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ kənˈsiːv / (AmE), / kənˈsiːv / (BrE)
to form an idea, opinion, or belief about something; to regard or consider in a particular way
form an idea or opinion about

Examples

Many people conceive art to be a reflection of society.
He could not conceive of anyone disagreeing with his opinion.
The law conceives of corporations as legal persons.

Synset Examples

I think he is very smart
I believe her to be very smart
I think that he is her boyfriend
The racist conceives such people to be inferior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to imagine - to form a mental image or concept of something
  • Synonym: view - to regard or consider in a specified way
  • Antonym: disregard - to pay no attention to; ignore

Etymology

From Latin concipere (to take in, conceive), from com- (together) + capere (to take).
