Latinesce

1. NOUN

/ ˌlætɪˈnɛs / (AmE), / ˌlatɪˈnɛs / (BrE)
an artificial language created as a blend of Esperanto and Latin.
constructed language based on Esperanto and Latin

Examples

He is fluent in Latinesce as well as several other constructed languages.
The grammar of Latinesce draws heavily from both Esperanto and Latin.
A small online community regularly discusses rules and vocabulary changes in Latinesce.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Esperanto - a widely used constructed international auxiliary language
  • Synonym: Latino sine Flexione - a simplified Latin-based constructed language

Etymology

Formed from 'Latin' and the Esperanto suffix '-esce' (to become).
