propagation

1. NOUN

/ ˌprɑːpəˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌprɒpəˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a wave, such as sound or light, travels through a medium from one place to another
movement of a wave through a medium

Examples

The propagation of sound waves requires a material medium such as air or water.
Fiber optic cables allow the efficient propagation of light signals over long distances.
Atmospheric conditions can affect the propagation of radio waves.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transmission - the act of passing something from one place to another, especially energy or information

Etymology

From Latin 'propagatio' (a breeding, propagation), from 'propagare' (to reproduce, spread).

2. NOUN

/ ˌprɑː.pəˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌprɒp.əˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of producing offspring or increasing in number, especially by reproduction or multiplication
producing offspring or multiplying

Examples

The propagation of certain plants can be achieved by taking cuttings from the parent.
In biology class, we studied the propagation of bacteria under different conditions.
Successful propagation of livestock is vital for sustainable agriculture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reproduction - the process of producing offspring
  • Synonym: multiplication - increase in number

Etymology

From Latin propagatio (extension, reproduction), from propagare (to propagate).

3. NOUN

/ ˌprɑːpəˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌprɒpəˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of spreading ideas, beliefs, or practices so that they are adopted in new areas or among more people
spreading of ideas or practices

Examples

The propagation of new technologies depends on effective communication and public acceptance.
Religious propagation was a central goal of the missionaries during that era.
He studied the propagation of cultural practices through migration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dissemination - the act of spreading something, especially information, widely
  • Synonym: spread - the act or process of spreading or extending something

Etymology

From Latin 'propagatio' (a setting; propagation), from 'propagare' (to set forward, extend).
