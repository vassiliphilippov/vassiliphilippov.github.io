phoebe

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːbi / (AmE), / ˈfiːbi / (BrE)
a small, brownish-gray North American flycatcher bird of the genus Sayornis, known for its soft calls and bobbing tail
a small North American flycatcher bird

Examples

A phoebe perched on the fence, wagging its tail up and down.
Eastern phoebes are among the first migratory birds to return in spring.
The phoebe built its nest under the eaves of our porch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flycatcher - a type of bird that catches insects in the air

Etymology

Possibly imitative of its call, or from the feminine given name 'Phoebe'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfiːbi / (AmE), / ˈfiːbi / (BrE)
a small North American flycatcher with dull brown or grayish plumage, belonging to the genus Sayornis
North American flycatcher bird

Examples

A phoebe perched on the wire, calling out its name in a sharp, clear note.
We spotted a phoebe building its nest under the eaves last spring.
Unlike other songbirds, the phoebe returns early in the spring, braving chilly weather.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flycatcher - a bird that catches insects in flight

Etymology

Named after 'Phoebe', a figure in Greek mythology. Used as an onomatopoeia for the bird's call.
