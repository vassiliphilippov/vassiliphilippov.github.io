Callophis

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæl.ə.fɪs / (AmE), / ˈkæl.ə.fɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small, brightly colored venomous snakes native to Asia, commonly known as Asian coral snakes.
genus of Asian coral snakes

Examples

Callophis species are known for their distinctive red and black banding.
Researchers discovered a new Callophis in the forests of India.
Unlike other snakes, Callophis are rarely encountered by humans due to their secretive nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coral snake - venomous snake with brightly colored bands

Etymology

From Greek 'kallos' (beauty) and 'ophis' (snake), meaning 'beautiful snake'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæl.əˌfɪs / (AmE), / ˈkæl.əˌfɪs / (BrE)
A genus of venomous Asian coral snakes in the family Elapidae, known for their bright colors and potent venom.
genus of Asian coral snakes

Examples

The Callophis are known for their highly potent venom and distinctive coloration.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the Callophis genus in Southeast Asia.
Unlike other related snakes, members of Callophis prefer forested habitats.

Etymology

From Greek 'kallos' (beauty) and 'ophis' (snake), referring to the brightly colored appearance of the snakes.
