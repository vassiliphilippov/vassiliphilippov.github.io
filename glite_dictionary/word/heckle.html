heckle

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛkəl / (AmE), / ˈhɛkəl / (BrE)
A comb with sharp teeth used for separating the fibers of flax or hemp during processing.
comb for separating flax fibers

Examples

The workers used a heckle to process the flax before spinning it into thread.
She examined the old wooden heckle in the museum's collection of textile tools.
After passing the stalks through the heckle, the fibers became soft and separated.

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *hekel* (comb with hooked teeth).

2. VERB

/ ˈhɛkəl / (AmE), / ˈhɛkəl / (BrE)
to comb (hemp or flax) with a heckle in order to separate the fibers
comb hemp or flax with a heckle

Examples

Traditional linen makers would heckle the flax to prepare it for spinning.
She watched her grandfather heckle hemp fibers by hand in the old workshop.
Before spinning, the workers carefully heckled the flax to remove impurities.

Synset Examples

heckle hemp or flax

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *hekele* (a comb for flax).

3. VERB

/ ˈhɛkəl / (AmE), / ˈhɛk(ə)l / (BrE)
to interrupt or challenge a speaker with aggressive or critical comments, especially from an audience
interrupt with aggressive comments

Examples

The comedian tried to ignore the man who continued to heckle him during the performance.
Politicians are sometimes heckled by members of the public at rallies.
She was nervous that someone in the crowd might heckle her while she gave her speech.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jeer - to make rude and mocking remarks
  • Antonym: to applaud - to show approval by clapping or praise

Etymology

Originated in the 18th century; earlier 'to comb flax or hemp', from Middle English *hekelen*, from Old English *hecel* (a comb). Sense shifted to its current usage via the notion of persistently harassing someone with sharp remarks.
