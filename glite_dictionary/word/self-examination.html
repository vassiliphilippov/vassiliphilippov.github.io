self-examination

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlf ɪɡˌzæməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌself ɪɡˌzæmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of carefully considering your own thoughts, feelings, and behavior to gain self-understanding or personal insight
careful consideration of one's own thoughts or behavior

Examples

After several days of self-examination, she realized what she truly wanted from her career.
The therapist encouraged regular self-examination as a way to understand his emotions.
His period of intense self-examination led to a positive change in his behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: introspection - the examination of one's own mental and emotional processes

Etymology

Formed from 'self' + 'examination'.
