bridesmaid

1. NOUN

/ ˈbraɪdzˌmeɪd / (AmE), / ˈbraɪdz.meɪd / (BrE)
A woman, typically unmarried, who attends and assists the bride at her wedding.
woman attending a bride at a wedding

Examples

She asked her sister to be her bridesmaid at the wedding.
All the bridesmaids wore matching dresses in pale blue.
The bridesmaid helped the bride adjust her veil before the ceremony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maid of honor - chief bridesmaid assisting the bride
  • Antonym: groomsman - man who attends the groom at a wedding

Etymology

Formed from 'bride' and 'maid' (unmarried woman) in English.
