St. Polycarp

1. PROPN

/ sənt ˈpɑliˌkɑrp / (AmE), / sənt ˈpɒlɪˌkɑːp / (BrE)
A Greek bishop of Smyrna (circa 69–155 AD), known for his refusal to renounce his Christian faith and his martyrdom by burning.
Greek bishop and early Christian martyr

Examples

Many early Christian writings record the martyrdom of St. Polycarp at Smyrna.
The steadfast faith of St. Polycarp inspired generations of believers throughout history.
Churches around the world commemorate St. Polycarp on February 23 each year.

Etymology

From Greek Polykarpos, meaning 'much fruit'; 'Saint' from Latin sanctus (holy).
