Chalcedon

1. PROPN

/ kælˈsidən / (AmE), / kælˈsiːdən / (BrE)
an ancient town on the Bosporus, now part of Istanbul, notable as the site of the Council of Chalcedon in 451 AD
ancient town in Istanbul, site of Council of Chalcedon

Examples

The Council of Chalcedon played a pivotal role in early Christian history.
Archaeologists have discovered artifacts from Chalcedon dating back to the classical period.
Today, the site of ancient Chalcedon forms part of modern Istanbul.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Χαλκηδών (Khalkēdōn), name of the settlement.

2. NOUN

/ kælˈsiːdən / (AmE), / kælˈsiːdən / (BrE)
the fourth ecumenical council held in AD 451 that defined the doctrine of the two natures (human and divine) in Christ
the fourth ecumenical council of 451 defining Christ's two natures

Examples

The Council of Chalcedon played a crucial role in shaping Christian doctrine.
Debates at Chalcedon centered on the nature of Christ and his divinity.
Many historical theologians reference the outcomes of Chalcedon in their writings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Council of Nicaea - the first ecumenical council in 325 AD that defined the nature of Christ and produced the Nicene Creed
  • Synonym: ecumenical council - a general council of the Christian church to decide doctrine

Etymology

Named after Chalcedon, an ancient city in Asia Minor (now part of Istanbul, Turkey).
