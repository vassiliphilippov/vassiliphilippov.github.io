captive

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæp.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈkæp.tɪv / (BrE)
an animal that is kept in confinement and not allowed to live freely in its natural environment
an animal kept in confinement

Examples

The zoo works to improve the living conditions of each captive in its care.
Researchers studied the behavior of captives compared to animals in the wild.
Many captive animals are unable to survive if released into nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prisoner - a person who is confined and kept under guard

Etymology

From Latin captivus (taken prisoner).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæp.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈkæp.tɪv / (BrE)
A person who is strongly influenced or controlled by a powerful emotion or passion.
person dominated by emotion or passion

Examples

He became a captive of his own ambition, unable to let go of his dreams.
She was a willing captive to the thrill of adventure.
Too often, people are captives of their fears and cannot move forward.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slave - a person who is excessively influenced or controlled by something
  • Antonym: free - not controlled or constrained by emotion or passion

Etymology

From Latin captivus (one taken prisoner), from capere (to seize).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkæp.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈkæp.tɪv / (BrE)
a person who has been taken prisoner or kept confined, especially during war
a person held as a prisoner

Examples

The soldiers released the captive after weeks of negotiations.
He felt like a captive in his own home, unable to step outside.
During the war, many civilians were taken captive and held in camps.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prisoner - a person who is kept in prison as a punishment or while awaiting trial
  • Antonym: jailer - a person in charge of a prison or of prisoners

Etymology

From Latin captivus (taken prisoner), from capere (to seize, take).
