seductive

1. ADJ

/ səˈdʌktɪv / (AmE), / sɪˈdʌktɪv / (BrE)
Tending to attract or tempt someone into a desired action or state; alluring or enticing.
alluring; tempting

Examples

The seductive aroma of freshly baked bread drew customers into the bakery.
Her seductive smile made it difficult for anyone to refuse her requests.
The advertisement used seductive images to attract potential buyers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alluring - powerfully attractive or charming; tempting
  • Synonym: tempting - something that entices or attracts you to do something, often something wrong or unwise
  • Antonym: repellent - able to repel or drive away; unappealing

Etymology

From Latin seductivus (tending to lead astray), from seducere (to lead away).
