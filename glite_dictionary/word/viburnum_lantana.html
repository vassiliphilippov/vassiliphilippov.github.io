Viburnum lantana

1. NOUN

/ vaɪˈbɜrnəm lænˈteɪnə / (AmE), / vaɪˈbɜːnəm lænˈtɑːnə / (BrE)
A vigorous deciduous European shrub, commonly found along roadsides, with red berries that turn black; also known as the wayfaring tree.
European shrub with berries; the wayfaring tree

Examples

The Viburnum lantana is often seen growing along rural English lanes.
Gardeners prize Viburnum lantana for its attractive clusters of berries and spring flowers.
Birds are drawn to the Viburnum lantana when its berries ripen in late summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wayfaring tree - common name for Viburnum lantana, a deciduous European shrub with berries

Etymology

From Latin, with 'Viburnum' being the genus name and 'lantana' as the species epithet.
