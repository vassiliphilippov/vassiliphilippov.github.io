cashmere

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæʒmɪr / (AmE), / ˈkæʃmɪə / (BrE)
A soft, luxurious fabric made from the wool of the Cashmere goat, prized for its warmth and softness.
soft fabric from Cashmere goat wool

Examples

She wore a cashmere scarf to keep warm on chilly evenings.
The sweater is made from 100% pure cashmere and feels incredibly soft.
Many luxury brands offer cashmere coats as part of their winter collections.

Etymology

From the region of Kashmir (anglicized as Cashmere), where the goats originate.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæʒ.mɪr / (AmE), / ˈkæʒ.mɪə / (BrE)
the fine, soft wool obtained from the Kashmir goat, used especially for making high-quality knitwear
fine wool from the Kashmir goat

Examples

This luxurious scarf is made from pure cashmere.
He bought a cashmere sweater because of its warmth and softness.
High-quality cashmere comes from goats raised in cold mountainous regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wool - the soft, thick hair of sheep or similar animals, used as a textile fiber

Etymology

From Kashmir, a region in the Indian subcontinent, where the Kashmir goat originates.
