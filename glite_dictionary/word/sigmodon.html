Sigmodon

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪɡ.məˌdɑn / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡ.məˌdɒn / (BrE)
a genus of rodents known as American cotton rats, native to the Americas and commonly found in grasslands and fields
genus of American cotton rats

Examples

Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Sigmodon in the Texas grasslands.
The population of Sigmodon increased noticeably after the rainy season.
Sigmodon species are frequently used in laboratory studies of disease transmission.

Etymology

From Greek 'sigma' and 'odous' (tooth), referring to the S-shaped molar teeth.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪɡ.məˌdɒn / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡ.məˌdɒn / (BrE)
a genus of American cotton rats found mainly in warm regions of North and South America
genus of American cotton rats

Examples

The North American Sigmodon is a common resident of grassy fields and agricultural areas.
Biologists studied the behavior of several Sigmodon species in their natural habitat.
Evidence of Sigmodon activity was found in the abandoned barn.

Etymology

From Greek 'sigma' (the letter, resembling an S shape) and 'odous, odontos' (tooth), referring to the S-shaped tooth pattern in these rodents.
