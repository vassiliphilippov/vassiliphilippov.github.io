condominium

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːn dəˈmɪni əm / (AmE), / ˌkɒn dəˈmɪni əm / (BrE)
an apartment or unit in a building or complex owned by an individual, while common areas are owned jointly with others in the building
privately owned apartment/unit in shared building

Examples

She bought a condominium near the beach to use as a vacation home.
My friend lives in a spacious condominium downtown, close to her office.
The new condominium complex includes a gym and a swimming pool.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apartment - a set of rooms forming an individual residence, typically in a building containing several such residences

Etymology

From Latin 'con-' (together) + 'dominium' (ownership, right of ownership).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːn.dəˈmɪn.i.əm / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.dəˈmɪn.i.əm / (BrE)
an apartment or unit in a building or complex in which each residence is individually owned, while common areas are jointly owned
individually owned apartment or unit

Examples

They bought a new condominium near the city center for their retirement.
Each owner in the condominium is responsible for maintaining their own unit.
The building is managed by a condominium association that oversees the shared spaces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apartment - a set of rooms for living in, usually on one floor of a building
  • Degree Lower: cooperative - a jointly owned and democratically controlled housing corporation

Etymology

From Latin 'con-' (together) + 'dominium' (ownership), meaning joint sovereignty or ownership.
