chine

1. NOUN

/ tʃaɪn / (AmE), / tʃaɪn / (BrE)
a cut of meat or fish that includes at least part of the backbone
cut of meat or fish with backbone

Examples

The chef prepared a chine of pork for the special evening menu.
They ordered a roasted lamb chine with herbs and garlic.
Traditional recipes often feature a smoked chine served with seasonal vegetables.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loin - cut of meat from the sides of the backbone

Etymology

From Old French *eschine* (back, spine), of Germanic origin.

2. VERB

/ tʃaɪn / (AmE), / tʃaɪn / (BrE)
to cut through the backbone of an animal, especially when butchering meat
cut through an animal's backbone

Examples

The butcher expertly chined the lamb before preparing the rack.
Some recipes call for you to chine the pork to make carving easier.
He has learned how to chine a side of beef during his apprenticeship.

Etymology

From Old French *eschine* (backbone), of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ tʃaɪn / (AmE), / tʃaɪn / (BrE)
the backbone of an animal, especially when used as food or in butchery
animal's backbone (culinary/anatomical)

Examples

The chef expertly cut along the chine to separate the rack of lamb.
He roasted a chine of pork for the family dinner.
The butcher trimmed the chine before preparing the ribs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spine - the backbone or vertebral column of a vertebrate animal
  • Synonym: backbone - the vertebral column; the main support structure of the body

Etymology

From Old French 'eschine' (spine, back) and Old English 'scinu' (spine).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
