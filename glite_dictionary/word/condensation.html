condensation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑn.dənˈseɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.dɛnˈseɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a gas changes into a liquid or solid state, usually as a result of cooling.
change from gas to liquid or solid

Examples

Condensation formed on the windows as the warm air met the cold glass.
The condensation of water vapor into droplets is what creates clouds.
You can see condensation on a cold drink on a hot day.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: evaporation - the process of a liquid changing into a vapor

Etymology

From Latin 'condensatio', from 'condensare' (to make dense).
