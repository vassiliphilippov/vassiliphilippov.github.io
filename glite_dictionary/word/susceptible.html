susceptible

1. ADJ

/ səˈsɛptəbəl / (AmE), / səˈsɛptəbl̩ / (BrE)
Easily influenced or affected emotionally; likely to be moved or touched by the feelings of others.
emotionally easily influenced

Examples

As a child, she was particularly susceptible to stories that had happy endings.
His susceptible nature made him an easy target for emotional appeals.
People who are susceptible to flattery often fall for insincere compliments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensitive - easily affected by the feelings of others
  • Antonym: resistant - not easily influenced or affected emotionally

Etymology

From Late Latin susceptibilis (capable of receiving), from Latin suscipere (to take up).

2. ADJ

/ səˈsɛptəbl̩ / (AmE), / səˈsɛptəbl̩ / (BrE)
easily influenced or harmed by something; capable of being affected or admitting a specified treatment or impression (often followed by 'to' or 'of').
easily affected or influenced

Examples

Children are especially susceptible to certain infectious diseases.
Some metals are susceptible of being shaped at high temperatures.
Because she lacked experience, she was susceptible to being misled by others.

Synset Examples

susceptible to colds
susceptible of proof

Related Terms

  • Antonym: immune - not affected or influenced by something

Etymology

From Late Latin susceptibilis, from Latin susceptus, past participle of suscipere (to take up).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02369003-a
Synset POS: a
