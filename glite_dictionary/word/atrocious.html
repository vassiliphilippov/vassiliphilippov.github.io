atrocious

1. ADJ

/ əˈtroʊʃəs / (AmE), / əˈtrəʊʃəs / (BrE)
extremely bad or unpleasant; of very poor quality
extremely bad or unpleasant

Examples

The movie's atrocious acting made it almost impossible to sit through.
His spelling was so atrocious that the teacher could barely read his essay.
The restaurant received atrocious reviews due to its poor service and bland food.

Synset Examples

atrocious taste

dreadful manners
a painful performance
terrible handwriting
an unspeakable odor came sweeping into the room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: execrable - extremely bad or unpleasant
  • Antonym: excellent - extremely good or outstanding

Etymology

From Latin atrocitas (cruelty, fierceness), from atrox (fierce, cruel).

2. ADJ

/ əˈtroʊʃəs / (AmE), / əˈtrəʊʃəs / (BrE)
extremely cruel, brutal, or wicked; shockingly bad or unpleasant.
extremely cruel or brutal

Examples

The prisoners suffered under atrocious conditions during the war.
Many people were shocked by the atrocious acts committed by the regime.
The judge called the crime atrocious and sentenced the defendant to life in prison.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monstrous - shockingly evil or brutal
  • Synonym: horrific - causing horror, extremely bad or cruel
  • Antonym: kind - having a gentle nature; opposite of atrocious

Etymology

From Latin atrocis (fierce, cruel), via French atroce.

/ əˈtroʊ.ʃəs / (AmE), / əˈtrəʊ.ʃəs / (BrE)
extremely bad, shocking, or provoking horror; horrifying or gruesome.
shockingly bad or horrifying

Examples

The aftermath of the accident was an atrocious scene that shocked everyone.
That was an atrocious crime which left the community reeling.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: delightful - highly pleasing, giving great pleasure

Etymology

From Latin 'atrox', 'atrocis' (fierce, cruel).
