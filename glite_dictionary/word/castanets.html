castanets

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæstəˈnɛts / (AmE), / ˌkæstəˈnɛts / (BrE)
A percussion instrument consisting of a pair of hollow pieces of wood or bone, clicked together in the hand, especially used in Spanish dancing.
percussion instrument; pair of clappers used in Spanish dance

Examples

The dancer held a pair of castanets in her hands, clicking them in time with the music.
Children enjoyed trying the castanets during the music lesson.
He bought authentic wooden castanets as a souvenir from his trip to Spain.

Etymology

From Spanish 'castañeta', diminutive of 'castaña' (chestnut), referring to their shape.
