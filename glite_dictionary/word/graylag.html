graylag

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪˌlæɡ / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪ.læɡ / (BrE)
a common large grey wild goose of Europe, believed to be the ancestor of many domestic breeds
a large grey European wild goose

Examples

A flock of graylag geese landed on the lake at dawn.
Many domestic geese are descended from the wild graylag.
We spotted a rare graylag during our birdwatching trip.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greylag - alternative spelling for the European grey wild goose

Etymology

From Middle English 'greylek', probably from its gray color and 'lag' (to lag behind), referring to its late migration.
