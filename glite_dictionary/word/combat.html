combat

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmˌbæt / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.bæt / (BrE)
a fight or battle between two armed forces, especially during a war
a fight between military forces

Examples

The soldiers prepared for combat as the enemy approached their position.
He was wounded in combat during the final days of the war.
Close combat requires both skill and bravery on the battlefield.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: battle - a fight between large organized armed forces

Etymology

From Old French *combatre* (to fight), from Late Latin *combattere*, from Latin *com-* (together) + *battere* (to beat).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːmˌbæt, kəmˈbæt / (AmE), / ˈkɒmbæt, kəmˈbæt / (BrE)
a fight or struggle, especially a violent one between opposing forces
fighting or conflict, especially violent

Examples

The soldiers returned from combat deeply changed by their experiences.
The government is funding new programs to prevent violent combat in city neighborhoods.
During the tournament, the two teams engaged in fierce combat for the championship.

Synset Examples

a fight broke out at the hockey game
there was fighting in the streets
the unhappy couple got into a terrible scrap

Related Terms

  • Synonym: battle - a fight between opposing armed forces
  • Synonym: conflict - a serious disagreement or argument, often a prolonged fight
  • Antonym: peace - a state of tranquility or absence of war

Etymology

From Old French 'combatre' (to fight), from Late Latin 'combattere'.

3. VERB

/ kəmˈbæt / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.bæt / (BrE)
to fight or try to stop something harmful or dangerous; to battle against someone or something
to fight or struggle against

Examples

The government launched a campaign to combat drug abuse among teenagers.
Doctors around the world are working together to combat the spread of infectious diseases.
She made it her life's mission to combat injustice and discrimination.

Synset Examples

The Kurds are combating Iraqi troops in Northern Iraq
We must combat the prejudices against other races
they battled over the budget

Related Terms

  • Synonym: battle - to fight against; contend in battle
  • Synonym: to confront - to face up to and deal with a problem
  • Antonym: yield - to give way to pressure or force; to submit

Etymology

From Old French *combatre* (to fight with), from Late Latin *combattere* (to fight with).
