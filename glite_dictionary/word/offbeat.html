offbeat

1. ADJ

/ ˈɔfˌbit / (AmE), / ˈɒfˌbiːt / (BrE)
Strikingly unconventional or unusual; not conforming to the ordinary or expected.
unconventional or unusual
informal

Examples

The director is known for his offbeat sense of humor in his films.
She decorated her apartment in an offbeat style with vintage furniture and quirky artwork.
He enjoys reading offbeat novels that challenge traditional storytelling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eccentric - unconventional or slightly strange
  • Antonym: conventional - following accepted customs and proprieties

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔfˌbit / (AmE), / ˈɒfˌbiːt / (BrE)
(in music) an unaccented beat, especially the last beat of a measure, opposite to the main strong beat.
unaccented beat in music

Examples

In reggae music, the guitarist often plays on the offbeat to create a syncopated rhythm.
The drummer emphasized the offbeat, giving the song its catchy groove.
Dancers listened for the offbeat to time their movements with the music.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: downbeat - the first, usually accented, beat of a measure in music
