roadworthiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊdˌwɜːrðinəs / (AmE), / ˈrəʊdˌwɜːðɪnəs / (BrE)
the condition of being fit and safe to be driven on public roads, especially for motor vehicles
fitness of a vehicle for road use

Examples

The annual inspection will assess the roadworthiness of your vehicle.
A car's roadworthiness can be compromised by worn brakes or faulty lights.
Regular maintenance is essential to ensure the roadworthiness of any automobile.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roadworthy - fit to be used or driven on the road

Etymology

From 'roadworthy' + '-ness', where 'roadworthy' means suitable for use on the road.
