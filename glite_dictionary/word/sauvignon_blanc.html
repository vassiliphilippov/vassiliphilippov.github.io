Sauvignon blanc

1. NOUN

/ ˌsoʊviːˈjoʊ̃ ˈblɑːŋk / (AmE), / ˌsɔːvɪˈnjɒ̃ ˈblæŋk / (BrE)
a variety of white grape, and the dry white wine made from it, originally from the Bordeaux region of France but now grown worldwide
a white grape and the wine made from it

Examples

The waiter recommended a chilled bottle of Sauvignon blanc to complement the seafood.
Many California vineyards are famous for their Sauvignon blanc wines.
I prefer the crisp, citrus notes of Sauvignon blanc over other white wines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chardonnay - another popular type of white wine grape

Etymology

French, from sauvage ('wild') and blanc ('white')
