deferent

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɛfərənt / (AmE), / ˈdɛfərənt / (BrE)
showing respect or yielding to the opinions or wishes of others; respectful or submissive
showing respect or deference

Examples

The students were deferent to their professor's expertise during the seminar.
She adopted a deferent attitude when addressing the board of directors, showing respect for their authority.
His deferent manner put others at ease, as he always listened attentively to their suggestions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: respectful - showing respect
  • Antonym: defiant - showing open resistance; bold disobedience

Etymology

From Latin deferens, present participle of deferre (to yield, bring down).
