to vitaminise

1. VERB

/ vaɪˈtæmənaɪz / (AmE), / vɪˈtæmɪnaɪz / (BrE)
to add vitamins to something, especially as a supplement to food
add vitamins as a supplement
british

Examples

Some breakfast cereals are vitaminised to improve their nutritional value.
The company decided to vitaminise its juice products to appeal to health-conscious consumers.
After processing, the milk is often vitaminised before packaging.

Synset Examples

vitaminized processed foods

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fortify - to strengthen, especially by adding nutrients
  • Synonym: to enrich - to improve the quality of something by adding nutrients or other substances

Etymology

Formed from 'vitamin' + suffix '-ise' (British spelling, equivalent to American '-ize').
