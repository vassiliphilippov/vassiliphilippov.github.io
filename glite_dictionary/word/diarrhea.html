diarrhea

1. NOUN

/ daɪəˈriə / (AmE), / dɪəˈrɪə / (BrE)
Frequent and watery bowel movements, often as a symptom of infection or digestive disorder.
frequent, watery bowel movements
medical

Examples

The patient developed diarrhea after eating contaminated food.
Travelers often experience diarrhea when visiting countries with different water standards.
Young children are more vulnerable to dehydration caused by severe diarrhea.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: constipation - a condition in which there is difficulty in emptying the bowels
  • Degree Higher: dysentery - inflammatory disorder of the intestine causing severe diarrhea with blood

Etymology

From Greek 'diarrhoia' (a flowing through), via Late Latin 'diarrhoea'.
