Nyctereutes procyonides

1. NOUN

/ nɪk.təˈruːtiːz ˌproʊ.siˈoʊ.nɪdiːz / (AmE), / nɪk.təˈruːtiːz ˌprəʊ.sɪˈəʊ.nɪdiːz / (BrE)
A small wild canid of eastern Asia, also known as the raccoon dog, with facial markings resembling those of a raccoon.
small wild canid, raccoon dog

Examples

The Nyctereutes procyonides is often referred to as the raccoon dog due to its distinctive facial markings.
Researchers have observed Nyctereutes procyonides in both wild and urban environments across eastern Asia.
Unlike true raccoons, Nyctereutes procyonides belongs to the canine family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raccoon dog - common name for Nyctereutes procyonides

Etymology

From Greek nykt-, nyktos (night) + ereutes (wanderer) and Greek prokyon (raccoon) + eides (like).
