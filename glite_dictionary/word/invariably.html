invariably

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈvɛriəbli / (AmE), / ɪnˈveəriəbli / (BrE)
always; in every case or on every occasion, without exception or change.
always, without exception

Examples

He invariably takes a walk after dinner, regardless of the weather.
The trains in this country are invariably late during winter.
She invariably brings her own lunch to the office.

Synset Examples

constantly kind and gracious
he always arrives on time

Related Terms

  • Synonym: always - at all times; on every occasion
  • Antonym: never - not at any time; not ever

Etymology

From Latin 'invariabilis' (unchangeable), from 'in-' (not) + 'variabilis' (varying).
