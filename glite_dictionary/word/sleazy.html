sleazy

1. ADJ

/ ˈsliːzi / (AmE), / ˈsliːzi / (BrE)
of very poor quality or construction; flimsy or cheap
of poor quality; flimsy

Examples

The hotel room was sleazy, with peeling wallpaper and a stained carpet.
He bought a sleazy jacket that fell apart after one wash.
The market is filled with sleazy knockoffs that break easily.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shoddy - of poor quality, especially in workmanship
  • Synonym: substandard - below an acceptable standard of quality
  • Antonym: sturdy - strongly and solidly built

Etymology

Perhaps from German 'schlissig' (flimsy, sleazy), originally referring to thin or loosely woven fabric.

2. ADJ

/ ˈsliːzi / (AmE), / ˈsliːzi / (BrE)
morally low, dishonest, or sordid, especially in appearance, behavior, or atmosphere.
morally corrupt or sordid

Examples

The nightclub had a sleazy reputation and attracted all kinds of shady people.
He refused to work with such sleazy business partners after learning about their past scams.
She avoided the sleazy motels along the highway and found a cleaner place to stay.

Synset Examples

a seedy district
the seamy side of life
sleazy characters hanging around casinos
sleazy storefronts with … dirt on the walls
the sordid details of his orgies stank under his very nostrils
the squalid atmosphere of intrigue and betrayal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sordid - involving immoral or dishonorable actions or motives
  • Synonym: seedy - having an unpleasant, disreputable, or sordid quality
  • Antonym: respectable - considered to be good, proper, or correct by society

Etymology

Originated in the 17th century from Silesia, a region known for producing thin, flimsy textiles.

3. ADJ

/ ˈsliːzi / (AmE), / ˈsliːzi / (BrE)
(of cloth) thin and loosely woven, often of poor quality or shabbily made.
thin and loosely woven (of cloth)

Examples

The jacket was made of sleazy material that tore easily.
Some old books have a sleazy fabric covering the spine, which wears out quickly.
The sleazy curtains barely blocked any light from the room.

Synset Examples

the coat has a sleazy lining

Etymology

Perhaps from German 'schlissig' (flimsy), originally applied to inferior textiles.
