disorganization

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsˌɔrɡənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪsˌɔːɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
A state in which order or system has been disrupted or lost.
loss or lack of order

Examples

After months of neglect, there was noticeable disorganization in the office, with files stacked everywhere.
The event was marked by disorganization, causing confusion among attendees.
His life descended into disorganization after he lost his job.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: organization - an orderly system or arrangement
  • Synonym: chaos - complete disorder and confusion
  • Synonym: mess - a situation of disorder or untidiness

Etymology

From French désorganisation, from dés- (dis-) + organisation (organization).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsˌɔrɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪsˌɔːɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the state of being untidy, confused, or lacking an orderly structure; disturbance of systematic arrangement
lack of order or system

Examples

The disorganization in the office led to missed deadlines and unhappy clients.
After the festival, there was visible disorganization in the streets as crowds dispersed.
Sudden changes in leadership resulted in the disorganization of the company's operations.

Synset Examples

the disorganization of the enemy troops by a flank attack

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disorder - lack of order or regularity
  • Antonym: order - a condition in which everything is in its correct place

Etymology

From dis- (opposite of) + organization, after French désorganisation.
