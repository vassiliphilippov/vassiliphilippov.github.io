dredger

1. NOUN

/ ˈdrɛdʒər / (AmE), / ˈdrɛdʒə / (BrE)
a boat or vessel equipped for removing mud, sand, or other material from the bottom of a body of water to keep waterways navigable
a vessel used for dredging waterways

Examples

The harbour was cleared of silt by a dredger working through the night.
A large dredger sailed up the river to maintain its depth for shipping.
Engineers deployed a dredger to remove debris after the flooding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barge - a flat-bottomed boat for carrying freight

Etymology

From 'dredge' (to clear the bed of a waterway by scooping out mud) + '-er' (agent noun).
