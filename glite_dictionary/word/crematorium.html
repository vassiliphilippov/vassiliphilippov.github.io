crematorium

1. NOUN

/ ˌkriːməˈtɔriəm / (AmE), / ˌkriːməˈtɔːrɪəm / (BrE)
a building or place containing a furnace for the cremation of dead bodies
building for cremating dead bodies

Examples

The family gathered at the crematorium to pay their last respects.
The new crematorium was built on the outskirts of the city.
Flowers lined the entrance to the crematorium during the service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crematory - a place where bodies are cremated
  • Antonym: cemetery - a place where dead bodies are buried

Etymology

From Latin 'cremare' (to burn) + '-orium' (place for).

2. NOUN

/ kɹiːməˈtɔɹiəm / (AmE), / kɹɛməˈtɔːɹiəm / (BrE)
A building or facility where dead bodies are cremated.
place where bodies are cremated

Examples

The city's new crematorium offers modern facilities and a peaceful environment for families.
After the funeral service, her remains were taken to the crematorium for cremation.
Many people attended his memorial at the local crematorium to pay their respects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crematory - a place where bodies are cremated (alternative form, especially American English)
  • Synonym: mortuary - a room or building where dead bodies are kept before burial or cremation
  • Antonym: cemetery - an area of land for burying the dead

Etymology

From Latin 'crematorium', from 'cremare' (to burn).
