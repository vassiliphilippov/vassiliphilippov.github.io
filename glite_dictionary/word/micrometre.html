micrometre

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪkroʊˌmiːtər / (AmE), / maɪˈkrɒmɪtə / (BrE)
a metric unit of length equal to one millionth of a meter (0.000001 meter), commonly used in science and engineering
one millionth of a meter

Examples

The width of a human hair is typically about seventy micrometres.
Bacteria are often just a few micrometres in size.
Scientists measured the particle diameter to within one micrometre.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: micron - another name for micrometre (esp. in older usage)

Etymology

From Greek micro- 'small' + -metre, meaning 'measure'.
