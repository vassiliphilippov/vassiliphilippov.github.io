collimation

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑː.ləˈmeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒl.ɪˈmeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the precise adjustment of the line of sight in a telescope or optical instrument to ensure accuracy of alignment
adjustment of a telescope's line of sight

Examples

Proper collimation of your telescope ensures you get the sharpest possible images of stars.
After transportation, the collimation of the optical equipment must be checked before use.
The astronomer explained that poor collimation could cause blurry or distorted views through the eyepiece.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alignment - arrangement in a straight line or correct relative position

Etymology

From Latin 'collimare', an alteration of 'collineare' (to make straight), from 'com-' (together) + 'linea' (line).
