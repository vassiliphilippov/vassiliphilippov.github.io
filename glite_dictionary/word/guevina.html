Guevina

1. NOUN

/ ɡeɪˈviːnə / (AmE), / ɡeɪˈviːnə / (BrE)
A genus of trees in the family Proteaceae, native to Chile, including the Chilean nut tree.
genus of Chilean nut trees

Examples

The Guevina genus is native to the temperate forests of southern Chile.
Botanists have studied the Guevina tree for its edible nuts and ecological importance.
Farmers in Chile sometimes cultivate Guevina for its nut harvest.

/ ɡeɪˈviːnə / (AmE), / ɡeɪˈviːnə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Proteaceae, consisting of a single species, the Chilean nut.
genus of Chilean nut plants

Examples

The Guevina genus contains only one living species native to South America.
Botanists collected seeds from Guevina for their research in the Andes.
