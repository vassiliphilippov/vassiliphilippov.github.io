geometrically

1. ADV

/ ˌdʒiː.əˈmɛ.trɪ.kli / (AmE), / ˌdʒiː.əˈmɛ.trɪ.kli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to geometry or geometric principles; in a pattern or progression characterized by geometric properties.
in a geometric manner; by geometric progression

Examples

The number of infections increased geometrically during the first weeks of the outbreak.
Her designs use shapes that are arranged geometrically for aesthetic effect.
Profits have grown geometrically thanks to their new marketing strategy.

Synset Examples

it grew geometrically

Related Terms

  • Antonym: arithmetically - in a way relating to arithmetic or an arithmetic progression

Etymology

Formed from 'geometric' + '-ally', with roots in Greek 'geōmetrikos' (geometric).

2. ADV

/ dʒiˌɑː.məˈtrɪk.li / (AmE), / dʒɪˈɒ.mɪ.trɪ.kəl.i / (BrE)
in a way that relates to or is based on geometry or geometric principles
in relation to geometry

Examples

The building was designed geometrically, with perfect symmetry and precision.
He analyzed the pattern geometrically to understand its structure.
The artist arranged the objects geometrically to create a sense of balance.

Synset Examples

this shape is geometrically interesting

Related Terms

  • Antonym: arithmetically - in a way that relates to arithmetic or numbers

Etymology

From 'geometric' + '-ally', forming the adverb from the adjective 'geometric'.
