pillaged

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɪlɪdʒd / (AmE), / ˈpɪlɪdʒd / (BrE)
robbed and destroyed by force and violence, especially in war
robbed and destroyed by force

Examples

Many villages were pillaged during the invasion, leaving nothing but ruins behind.
The museum displayed artifacts recovered from pillaged ancient cities.
After the army retreated, the once-thriving town lay pillaged and deserted.

Synset Examples

the raped countryside

Related Terms

  • Synonym: looted - robbed of valuables by force, especially during disorder
  • Synonym: ravaged - severely damaged or devastated, especially by war or natural disaster

Etymology

From the verb 'pillage', from French *pillage* (looting), ultimately from Old French *piller* (to plunder).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɪlɪdʒd / (AmE), / ˈpɪlɪdʒd / (BrE)
(of a place or object) wrongfully emptied or stripped of anything valuable, typically as a result of theft or violence.
stripped of valuables by theft or violence

Examples

The pillaged museum was left in ruins after the burglars broke in.
After the war, families returned to find their pillaged homes empty and destroyed.
Historical records describe the city as having been pillaged by invading forces.

Synset Examples

the robbers left the looted train
people returned to the plundered village

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plundered - wrongfully taken or stripped of valuables, especially by force
  • Synonym: looted - robbed or stripped of goods, especially during unrest

Etymology

From Old French *piller* (to plunder, pillage), related to Latin *pilare* (to pluck, strip).
