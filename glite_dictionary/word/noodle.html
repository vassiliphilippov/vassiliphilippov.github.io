2. NOUN

/ ˈnuːdəl / (AmE), / ˈnuːdl̩ / (BrE)
A ribbon-like strip of pasta, often cooked in boiling water and used in various dishes.
a type of ribbon-shaped pasta

Examples

She added egg noodles to the chicken soup for extra flavor.
The chef made fresh noodles from scratch for the dish.
Stir-fried noodles are a popular option in many Asian cuisines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pasta - Italian food made from wheat dough formed into various shapes, often including noodles
  • Degree Lower: spaghetti - a long, thin, cylindrical form of pasta

Etymology

From German 'Nudel' (noodle, pasta), of uncertain origin.
