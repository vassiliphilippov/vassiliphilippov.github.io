Gitche Gumee

1. PROPN

/ ˈɡɪtʃi ɡuːˈmi / (AmE), / ˈɡɪtʃi ɡuːˈmi / (BrE)
a traditional Ojibwe name for Lake Superior, often featured in legends and literature
Ojibwe name for Lake Superior

Examples

The waves crashed against the rocky shore of Gitche Gumee during the storm.
Henry Wadsworth Longfellow immortalized Gitche Gumee in his poem 'The Song of Hiawatha'.
Many travelers are drawn to the mysterious beauty of Gitche Gumee.

Synset Examples

The sunset over Gitche Gumee was breathtaking.
Legends say Gitche Gumee is home to many mystical creatures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lake Superior - the largest of the Great Lakes of North America

Etymology

From Ojibwe gichi-gami (great sea or great lake).
