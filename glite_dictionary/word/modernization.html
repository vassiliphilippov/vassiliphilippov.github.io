modernization

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːdərnəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɒdənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
A modern version or adaptation of something, especially a literary or artistic work, updated to fit contemporary styles or ideas.
updated or modern version of something

Examples

The director's modernization of the classic play brought new energy to the story.
This modernization features updated language and contemporary costumes.
Critics praised the modernization for making the themes accessible to today's audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adaptation - a version revised for a new use or audience

Etymology

From modern + -ization, based on Latin modernus (modern) and the suffix -ization indicating action or process.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɑːdərnəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɒdənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something more modern in style, methods, or attitude
the process of making modern

Examples

The government is investing heavily in the modernization of public transportation.
Economic modernization often requires significant changes in infrastructure and education.
The modernization of the library included digital catalogs and new computers.

Synset Examples

the modernization of Nigeria will be a long process

Related Terms

  • Synonym: update - to make something more modern, usually with new information or equipment
  • Synonym: innovation - the introduction of something new
  • Antonym: preservation - the act of keeping something in its original state

Etymology

From modern + -ization, patterned after French 'modernisation'.
