cuneus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjuːniəs / (AmE), / ˈkjuːnɪəs / (BrE)
A wedge-shaped structure or any shape that is triangular in cross section, especially as used in anatomy or geometry.
wedge-shaped structure; triangular in cross section

Examples

The parietal lobe of the brain contains a region known as the cuneus.
The architect designed support beams in the form of a cuneus for added strength.
Any cross section of the channel revealed a distinct cuneus shape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wedge - a piece of material with a thick end and a thin edge, used to split things apart

Etymology

From Latin *cuneus* (wedge).
