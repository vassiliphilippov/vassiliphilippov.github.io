genteel

1. ADJ

/ dʒɛnˈtil / (AmE), / dʒɛnˈtiːl / (BrE)
polite, refined, or respectable, often in an affected or ostentatious way
refined and polite in manners or taste

Examples

Her genteel appearance made her stand out at the informal gathering.
He spoke with a genteel accent that impressed his colleagues.
The house was decorated in a genteel style, with elegant furnishings and delicate china.

Synset Examples

cultivated speech
cultured Bostonians
cultured tastes
a genteel old lady
polite society

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refined - showing elegant manners, taste, or style
  • Synonym: cultured - characterized by refined taste and manners
  • Antonym: vulgar - lacking sophistication or good taste; unrefined

Etymology

From French 'gentil' (well-born, noble), from Latin 'gentilis' (of the same clan, of noble birth).
