anabiosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌænəbaɪˈoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌænəbaɪˈəʊsɪs / (BrE)
a temporary state in which an organism's life processes are suspended, especially during extreme dryness, with revival upon the return of moisture
suspended animation during drought in organisms

Examples

Some desert-dwelling animals survive long dry periods through anabiosis.
During anabiosis, the organism appears lifeless but can revive when moisture returns.
Scientists have studied anabiosis to understand how certain bacteria withstand harsh environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cryptobiosis - a state in which metabolic activity is reduced to an undetectable level

Etymology

From Greek ana- (again) + biosis (manner of life), meaning 'return to life'.
