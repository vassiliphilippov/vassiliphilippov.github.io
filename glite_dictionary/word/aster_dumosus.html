Aster dumosus

1. NOUN

/ ˈæstər duːˈmoʊsəs / (AmE), / ˈæstə djuːˈməʊsəs / (BrE)
A stiff perennial plant native to the eastern United States, with small linear leaves and many tiny white flower heads.
a perennial plant of eastern US with tiny white flowers

Examples

The garden was full of Aster dumosus, adding delicate white blooms in late summer.
Botanists often identify Aster dumosus by its stiff stems and abundance of tiny flowers.
Aster dumosus thrives in well-drained soil and sunny locations.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus name 'Aster' (from Greek 'astēr', meaning 'star') and the species Latin 'dumosus' ('bushy').
