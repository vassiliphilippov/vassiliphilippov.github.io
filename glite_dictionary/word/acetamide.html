acetamide

1. NOUN

/ əˈsitəˌmaɪd, ˌæsɪˈtæmɪd / (AmE), / əˈsiːtəˌmʌɪd, ˌasɪˈtamɪd / (BrE)
A colorless, crystalline solid amide derived from acetic acid, used as a solvent and in the synthesis of organic compounds.
chemical compound; amide of acetic acid
medical

Examples

Acetamide is commonly used as a solvent in organic chemistry laboratories.
Researchers found that acetamide can be produced by the hydrolysis of acetonitrile.
As a colorless solid, acetamide has a wide range of applications in chemical synthesis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acetamide - ethanamide; an alternative systematic name for acetamide

Etymology

From acetic (acid) + amide, reflecting its structure as the amide of acetic acid.
