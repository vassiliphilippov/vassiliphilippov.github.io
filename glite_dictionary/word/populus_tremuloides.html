Populus tremuloides

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːpjələs trɛˌmjuloʊˈaɪdiːz / (AmE), / ˈpɒpjʊləs trɛmjʊləʊˈiːdiːz / (BrE)
A slender species of aspen native to North America, commonly called the quaking aspen, known for its trembling leaves.
North American quaking aspen tree

Examples

The leaves of Populus tremuloides shimmer in the slightest breeze, creating a distinctive sound in the forest.
Researchers studied the genetic diversity of Populus tremuloides across different regions of North America.
In autumn, the golden foliage of Populus tremuloides transforms mountain landscapes into a brilliant tapestry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aspen - A type of tree with fluttering leaves, in the genus Populus
  • Synonym: quaking aspen - Common name for Populus tremuloides; tree with trembling leaves

Etymology

From Latin: 'populus' (poplar) + 'tremuloides' (resembling tremulous, i.e., trembling), referring to its distinctive quivering leaves.
