Ursus americanus

1. NOUN

/ ˈɝː.səs əˌmeɹ.ɪˈkeɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈɜː.səs əˌmɛr.ɪˈkeɪ.nəs / (BrE)
the American black bear, a North American bear species typically brown to black in color and smaller and less aggressive than the brown bear
American black bear species

Examples

The Ursus americanus inhabits forests across North America and is known for its adaptability to different environments.
Unlike the brown bear, the Ursus americanus is generally less aggressive and somewhat smaller.
Researchers tagged several Ursus americanus individuals to monitor their migration and feeding habits.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: brown bear - large bear species, Ursus arctos, related species, more aggressive
  • Synonym: bear - large heavy mammals of the family Ursidae

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Ursus' means 'bear' and 'americanus' means 'of America'.
