to fry

1. VERB

/ fraɪ / (AmE), / fraɪ / (BrE)
to execute (someone) by electrocution, especially using the electric chair
execute by electrocution
informal

Examples

The judge sentenced the criminal to be fried for his crimes.
Rumors spread that the prisoner would be fried at midnight in the electric chair.
They threatened to fry him if he didn't confess to the charges.

Synset Examples

The serial killer was electrocuted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to electrocute - to kill by electric shock

2. VERB

/ fraɪ / (AmE), / fraɪ / (BrE)
to cook (food) in hot fat or oil, typically in a shallow pan
cook in hot fat or oil

Examples

I like to fry eggs for breakfast on weekends.
The chef will fry the fish until it turns golden brown.
Heat some oil in the pan before you fry the vegetables.

Synset Examples

fry the pancakes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sauté - to cook food quickly in a small amount of fat
  • Antonym: boil - to cook in hot water or other liquid
  • Antonym: to bake - to cook food by dry heat in an oven

Etymology

From Old French *frire*, from Latin *frigere* (to roast, fry).

3. VERB

/ fraɪ / (AmE), / fraɪ / (BrE)
(informal) To become very hot, especially due to high temperatures or exposure to strong sunlight.
become very hot or overheated
informal

Examples

We were starting to fry in the midday sun before we found some shade.
If you forget your hat at the festival, you’ll fry out there all afternoon.
She felt like she was going to fry during the heatwave last July.

Synset Examples

If the children stay out on the beach for another hour, they'll be fried

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bake - to be or become very hot, especially due to weather
  • Synonym: roast - to become very hot, especially from sun or heat
  • Antonym: freeze - to become very cold

4. VERB

/ fraɪ / (AmE), / fraɪ / (BrE)
to cook food in hot oil or fat, typically in a shallow pan
cook food in hot oil

Examples

She likes to fry eggs for breakfast every morning.
When you fry onions, they become sweet and golden brown.
Please make sure not to fry the fish for too long, or it will dry out.

Synset Examples

Make sure to fry the chicken until it's golden.
He likes to fry vegetables in olive oil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sauté - to cook food quickly in a small amount of oil or fat over high heat
  • Antonym: boil - to cook food in water or other liquid at or near boiling point
  • Antonym: to bake - to cook food using dry heat without direct exposure to a flame, typically in an oven

Etymology

From Old French *frire*, from Latin *frīgere* (to roast or fry).
