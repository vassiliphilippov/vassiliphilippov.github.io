erudition

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛr.juˈdɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌɛr.jʊˈdɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
the quality of having or showing great knowledge gained from study and reading
deep scholarly knowledge

Examples

Her erudition was evident in every lecture she delivered to her students.
The book is admired for its remarkable erudition and thoughtful analysis of ancient texts.
Despite his erudition, he remained humble and approachable to everyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: learning - general knowledge and education acquired by learning
  • Antonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge or awareness

Etymology

From Latin 'eruditio' (instruction, learning), from 'erudire' (to teach).
