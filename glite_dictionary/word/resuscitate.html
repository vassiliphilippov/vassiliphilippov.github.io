to resuscitate

1. VERB

/ rɪˈsʌsɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈsʌsɪteɪt / (BrE)
to revive someone from unconsciousness or apparent death
to revive from unconsciousness
medical

Examples

The paramedics managed to resuscitate the victim after he stopped breathing.
Doctors did everything possible to resuscitate her when she collapsed at work.
Quick action by the lifeguard helped resuscitate the drowning swimmer.

Synset Examples

The doctors revived the comatose man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to revive - to restore to life or consciousness

Etymology

From Latin resuscitare (to revive), from re- (again) + suscitare (to rouse).

2. VERB

/ rɪˈsʌsɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈsʌsɪteɪt / (BrE)
to revive someone from unconsciousness or apparent death, especially after they have stopped breathing
revive from unconsciousness or apparent death

Examples

The lifeguard managed to resuscitate the child who had nearly drowned.
Doctors tried desperately to resuscitate her after she collapsed on the street.
The paramedics resuscitated him using CPR and a defibrillator.

Synset Examples

The patient came to quickly
She revived after the doctor gave her an injection

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to revive - to bring back to consciousness or life
  • Antonym: faint - to lose consciousness temporarily

Etymology

From Latin resuscitare, from re- (again) + suscitare (to rouse, awaken).
