showman

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃoʊˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊˌmæn / (BrE)
a man who organizes, presents, or sponsors public entertainment events, such as shows, fairs, or performances
man who presents or organizes entertainment

Examples

The famous showman brought a circus to town every summer.
As a skilled showman, he knew exactly how to draw large crowds.
That showman always had a flair for dramatic performances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impresario - a person who organizes and often finances concerts, plays, or operas

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃoʊˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊˌmæn / (BrE)
a person who is skilled at entertaining an audience or making effective presentations, often in a theatrical or engaging manner
person skilled at entertaining audiences

Examples

The politician proved himself to be a real showman during the televised debate.
With his flair for drama, he is as much a showman as he is a scientist.
The circus hired a talented showman to host the evening performances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: performer - a person who entertains an audience

Etymology

From 'show' + 'man', dating from the late 17th century, originally referring to those who staged shows or spectacles.
