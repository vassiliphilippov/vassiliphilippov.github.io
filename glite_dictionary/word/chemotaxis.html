chemotaxis

1. NOUN

/ ˌkiːməˈtæksɪs / (AmE), / ˌkiːməʊˈtaksɪs / (BrE)
the movement of a cell or organism in response to a chemical stimulus, typically towards or away from certain chemicals
movement in response to chemical stimulus
medical

Examples

The bacteria use chemotaxis to move toward higher concentrations of nutrients.
Immune cells rely on chemotaxis to reach sites of infection efficiently.
Scientists studied how chemotaxis helps sperm cells locate an egg.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phototaxis - movement in response to light stimulus
  • Synonym: thermotaxis - movement in response to temperature stimulus

Etymology

From German 'Chemotaxis', from 'chemo-' (chemical) + 'Taxis' (arrangement, ordering), based on Greek 'taxis'.
