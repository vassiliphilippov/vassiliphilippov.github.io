booger

1. NOUN

/ ˈbuːɡər / (AmE), / ˈbʊɡə / (BrE)
an imaginary monster or bogeyman used to frighten children
imaginary monster to scare children

Examples

As a child, he was terrified that the booger would come out at night if he misbehaved.
The old tales warned of a booger hiding in the closet to scare naughty kids.
Parents used stories about the booger to make their children go to bed early.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bogeyman - an imaginary evil spirit used to frighten children

Etymology

Alteration of 'bogey' or 'bugbear', influenced by regional American dialects.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbuɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈbʊɡə / (BrE)
An informal term for a piece of dried nasal mucus found inside the nose.
piece of dried nasal mucus
informal american

Examples

As a child, he was always picking a booger out of his nose.
She was embarrassed when she noticed a booger on her friend's face.
Parents often tell kids not to eat their boogers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bogey - British term for dried nasal mucus
