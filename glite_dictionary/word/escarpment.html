escarpment

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈskɑːrpmənt / (AmE), / ɪˈskɑːpmənt / (BrE)
a steep artificial slope built in front of a fortification as a defensive measure
steep defensive slope in front of a fortification

Examples

The castle was protected by a massive escarpment that deterred invaders.
Engineers constructed an escarpment around the fortress to strengthen its defenses.
Soldiers struggled to scale the steep escarpment during the siege.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rampart - a defensive wall or bank around a fort; synonym in fortification
  • Synonym: moat - a deep, wide ditch surrounding a fort

Etymology

From French 'escarpement', from Italian 'scarpa' (slope of a fortification wall).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈskɑːrmənt / (AmE), / ɪˈskɑːpmənt / (BrE)
a long, steep slope or cliff at the edge of a plateau or separating areas of land at different heights, typically formed by erosion
steep slope or cliff at plateau edge

Examples

The hikers paused to admire the view from the top of the escarpment.
A dramatic escarpment separates the highland from the vast plains below.
Erosion gradually shaped the limestone escarpment over millions of years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cliff - steep rock face, especially at the edge of the sea

Etymology

From French 'escarpement', from 'escarper' meaning 'to cut steeply', ultimately from Italian 'scarpa' (slope or embankment).
