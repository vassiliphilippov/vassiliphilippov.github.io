atop

1. ADV

/ əˈtɑp / (AmE), / əˈtɒp / (BrE)
on, to, or at the top of something; on top.
on top of

Examples

The old mansion sat atop the highest hill in the valley.
She balanced the vase delicately atop the narrow shelf.
A layer of fresh strawberries was placed atop the cake before serving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: above - at a higher level or position than something
  • Antonym: underneath - directly below something

Etymology

From Middle English, formed as a- (on) + top.
