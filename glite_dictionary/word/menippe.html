Menippe

1. NOUN

/ məˈnɪpi / (AmE), / mɪˈnɪpi / (BrE)
A genus of large, robust marine crabs commonly known as stone crabs, found in the western Atlantic and Gulf of Mexico.
genus of stone crabs

Examples

The Menippe genus includes several economically important stone crab species.
Fishermen along the Gulf coast often catch Menippe for their prized claws.
Researchers are studying Menippe to understand their role in coastal ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stone crab - a common name for species of crabs in the genus Menippe

Etymology

From Greek Μενίππη (Menippe), a name from classical mythology, used in scientific Latin.

2. NOUN

/ mɛˈnɪpi / (AmE), / mɛˈnɪpiː / (BrE)
A genus of stone crabs found in the Atlantic and Gulf coasts, known for their large, strong claws.
a genus of stone crabs

Examples

The genus Menippe includes several species of edible stone crabs prized for their meat.
Researchers collected samples from Menippe populations along the Florida coast.
The powerful claws of crabs in the genus Menippe are adapted for breaking shells.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stone crab - a common name for crabs of the genus Menippe

Etymology

From Greek 'mēnippē', a personal name in classical antiquity.
