yellow-bellied

1. ADJ

/ ˈjɛloʊˌbɛlid / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊˌbɛlid / (BrE)
Easily frightened; cowardly; lacking courage.
cowardly; easily frightened

Examples

He was too yellow-bellied to stand up to the bully.
Don't be yellow-bellied; just tell them the truth.
Many accused the politicians of being yellow-bellied when they refused to address the issue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowardly - lacking courage
  • Antonym: brave - showing courage

Etymology

Formed from 'yellow' (color symbolizing cowardice) and 'belly' (body part); in use since the late 19th century in American English.

2. ADJ

/ ˈjɛloʊ ˌbɛlid / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ ˌbɛlɪd / (BrE)
cowardly or easily frightened; lacking courage
cowardly or timid
informal

Examples

He was too yellow-bellied to admit his mistake in front of everyone.
The journalist called the government yellow-bellied for not taking a stand on the issue.
She hated feeling yellow-bellied whenever she avoided difficult conversations.

Synset Examples

They considered him yellow-bellied for not standing up.
He was called yellow-bellied for backing out of the debate.
She felt yellow-bellied for avoiding confrontation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowardly - lacking courage; easily frightened
  • Antonym: brave - showing courage; not afraid

Etymology

Originally from the comparison to an animal or bird with a pale or yellow underside, symbolizing fear or weakness.
