to permeate

1. VERB

/ ˈpɜr.mi.eɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.mi.eɪt / (BrE)
to spread or pass through every part of something; to be present and apparent throughout
spread through; fill every part

Examples

A strong scent of lavender permeated the entire room as soon as we opened the door.
A feeling of excitement permeated the crowd as the band took the stage.
The spirit of generosity permeates every aspect of the community's work.

Synset Examples

An atmosphere of distrust has permeated this administration
music penetrated the entire building
His campaign was riddled with accusations and personal attacks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to penetrate - to pass into or through; to enter forcibly
  • Synonym: to saturate - to fill completely so that no more can be absorbed

Etymology

From Latin permeatus, past participle of permeare (to pass through), from per- (through) + meare (to go, pass).

2. VERB

/ ˈpɝː.miˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.mi.eɪt / (BrE)
(of two or more things) to penetrate each other mutually, so as to be interlocked or intertwined.
penetrate mutually; be interlocked

Examples

Cultural traditions often permeate each other when diverse communities live closely together.
In metaphysics, some philosophers argue that mind and body permeate one another.
The boundaries between art and technology increasingly permeate in modern design.

Synset Examples

The territories of two married people interpenetrate a lot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to interpenetrate - to penetrate each other mutually

Etymology

From Latin permeatus, past participle of permeare (to pass through).

3. VERB

/ ˈpɝː.mi.eɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.mi.eɪt / (BrE)
(of a liquid, gas, idea, etc.) to pass through or spread throughout something, especially gradually or completely.
pass or spread through (something)

Examples

The smell of fresh bread permeated the kitchen, making everyone hungry.
During the rainy season, water can permeate the soil and reach the roots of plants.
New ideas about sustainability have gradually permeated the company's culture.

Synset Examples

Water permeates sand easily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to penetrate - enter or pass into or through something

Etymology

From Latin permeare (to pass through), from per- (through) + meare (to pass, go).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01460583-v
Synset POS: v
