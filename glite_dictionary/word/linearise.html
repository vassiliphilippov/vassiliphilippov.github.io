to linearise

1. VERB

/ ˈlɪniəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈlɪniəraɪz / (BrE)
to make linear or transform into a linear form, especially in mathematics or science
make linear; transform to linear form
british

Examples

The software can linearise complex equations to simplify calculations.
Scientists often linearise data so it can be graphed more easily.
A catalyst is used to linearise polyethylene during the manufacturing process.

Synset Examples

a catalyst linearizes polyethylene

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to linearize - to make linear or get into a linear form (US spelling)

Etymology

From linear + -ise, patterned after the adjective 'linear'.
