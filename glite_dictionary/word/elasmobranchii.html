Elasmobranchii

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlæzməˈbræŋkiː / (AmE), / ɪˌlæzməˈbraŋkɪaɪ / (BrE)
A subclass of cartilaginous fishes that includes sharks, rays, skates, and related species.
subclass of cartilaginous fishes
medical

Examples

The fossil record shows that Elasmobranchii have existed for over 400 million years.
Marine biologists are studying the unique cartilage structure found in Elasmobranchii.
Members of the Elasmobranchii, such as sharks and rays, have specialized senses for detecting prey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shark - large predatory cartilaginous fish
  • Synonym: ray - cartilaginous fish with a flattened body and enlarged pectoral fins
  • Synonym: skate - cartilaginous fish similar to rays

Etymology

From Greek 'elasmo-' (plate) + 'branchia' (gills), referring to their plate-like gill structures.

2. NOUN

/ ɪˌlæzməˈbræŋki.aɪ / (AmE), / ɪˌlæzməˈbræŋki.aɪ / (BrE)
A large subclass of cartilaginous fishes including sharks, rays, dogfishes, and skates.
subclass of cartilaginous fishes

Examples

The Elasmobranchii include a remarkable diversity of species such as sharks and rays.
Scientists study the Elasmobranchii to understand the evolution of cartilaginous fishes.
Conservation efforts focus on many members of the Elasmobranchii threatened by overfishing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shark - large predatory fish of the sea
  • Synonym: ray - flat-bodied cartilaginous fish

Etymology

From Greek elasma (metal plate) + branchia (gills), referring to their plate-like gill structures.
