amount

1. NOUN

/ əˈmaʊnt / (AmE), / əˈmaʊnt / (BrE)
a quantity of money; a sum of money
quantity of money

Examples

She paid a large amount for the new car.
A small amount was withdrawn from his savings account.
The charity raised an impressive amount of money last year.

Synset Examples

he borrowed a large sum
the amount he had in cash was insufficient

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sum - a quantity of money; an amount of money

Etymology

From Old French 'amonter' (to go up), from 'à monter' (to mount).

Source: WordNet

2. NOUN

/ əˈmaʊnt / (AmE), / əˈmaʊnt / (BrE)
a quantity of something, especially when measured in relation to a particular criterion or need
quantity or total of something

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quantity - an amount or number of something; how much or how many
  • Synonym: sum - the total amount resulting from addition
  • Synonym: volume - the amount of space that a substance or object occupies

Etymology

From Old French 'amunter', from Latin 'ad montem' meaning 'to the mountain, heap up'.

3. NOUN

/ əˈmaʊnt / (AmE), / əˈmaʊnt / (BrE)
a total quantity produced by adding together several numbers, things, or quantities
quantity obtained by addition

Examples

The final amount was calculated by adding all the expenses together.
She paid the full amount at the end of the month.
After counting the votes, the amount was higher than expected.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sum - the total amount resulting from the addition of numbers
  • Synonym: total - the whole amount; the resulting quantity from addition
  • Antonym: difference - the result of subtracting one amount from another

Etymology

From Old French 'amont' (upward, to the top), from Latin 'ad montem' (to the mountain).

6. VERB

/ əˈmaʊnt / (AmE), / əˈmaʊnt / (BrE)
(of a total or sum) to add up to a particular number or quantity.
add up to a total

Examples

The final bill amounted to over $3,000, which was more than we expected.
Her yearly expenses amount to only a few hundred dollars.
All the evidence amounts to a strong case against him.

Synset Examples

The bills amounted to $2,000
The bill came to $2,000

Related Terms

  • Synonym: total - to add up to a particular total; be equivalent to a total
  • Synonym: to come to - to amount to a total; to reach a particular number

Etymology

From Old French amonter (‘to go up, ascend, increase’), from Late Latin *admontare (‘to amount to, to go up’).
