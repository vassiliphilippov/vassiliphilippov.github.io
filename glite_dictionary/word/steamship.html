steamship

1. NOUN

/ ˈstiːmˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈstiːmʃɪp / (BrE)
a ship that is powered by one or more steam engines
ship powered by steam engines

Examples

The steamship crossed the Atlantic in just fourteen days.
He worked as an engineer aboard a steamship in the late nineteenth century.
The museum features a restored steamship among its many exhibits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steamboat - a boat powered by a steam engine, usually smaller than a steamship

Etymology

From 'steam' (referring to steam power) + 'ship'.
