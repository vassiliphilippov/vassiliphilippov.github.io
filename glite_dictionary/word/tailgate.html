tailgate

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪlˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈteɪlɡeɪt / (BrE)
a gate at the downstream end of a lock or canal, used to control the flow of water at the lower end
gate at canal or lock's lower end

Examples

When the water level drops, the lock keeper opens the tailgate to let out more water.
The engineers checked the tailgate of the canal for any signs of damage after the storm.
Failure to properly secure the tailgate can cause flooding at the lower end of the lock.

2. NOUN

/ ˈteɪlˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈteɪlɡeɪt / (BrE)
a hinged gate at the back of a vehicle, such as a truck, that can be lowered for loading or unloading
gate at rear of a vehicle

Examples

The truck's tailgate was lowered to unload the heavy boxes.
He leaned against the tailgate and watched the scenery go by.
After the picnic, they cleaned off the muddy tailgate before heading home.

Etymology

From 'tail' + 'gate', referring to positioning at the tail (rear) of a vehicle.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04391680-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈteɪlˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈteɪlɡeɪt / (BrE)
to drive too closely behind another vehicle, especially in a way that is dangerous
drive too closely behind another vehicle

Examples

If you tailgate on the highway, you increase the risk of an accident.
The police issued a fine to the driver for tailgating another car.
I always slow down when someone starts to tailgate me.

Synset Examples

it is dangerous to tailgate another vehicle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rear-end - to crash into the back of another vehicle
  • Antonym: to overtake - to pass another vehicle moving in the same direction

Etymology

From 'tail' (the back) + 'gate'; originally referred to the gate at the rear of a vehicle, then developed the verb sense in motoring.
