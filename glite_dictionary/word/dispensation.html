dispensation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪspənˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪspɛnˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
official permission to not follow a particular rule or obligation, especially granted by an authority.
official exemption from a rule or duty

Examples

The church granted him a dispensation from fasting during his illness.
She received a special dispensation allowing her to work from home.
A dispensation was required to get married without the usual waiting period.

Etymology

From Latin dispensatio (distribution, management), from dispensare (to distribute, administer).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɪspənˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪspɛnˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of distributing or supplying something, especially in portions or according to a system
the act of giving out or distributing

Examples

The dispensation of food supplies was organized by local volunteers during the emergency.
Her job involved the dispensation of medicines to patients at the clinic.
Government regulations strictly control the dispensation of certain drugs to ensure safety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distribution - the action of sharing something out among a number of recipients

Etymology

From Latin 'dispensatio', from 'dispensare' (to distribute, manage).

3. NOUN

/ ˌdɪspənˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪspɛnˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
a portion or share that is distributed or given out; something that has been provided or allocated to people
a share that has been distributed

Examples

Each community received its dispensation of food supplies after the storm.
His inheritance was just one small dispensation from his family's vast fortune.
The annual report outlined the dispensation of resources to different projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allocation - an amount or portion of a resource assigned to someone
  • Synonym: portion - a part or share of the whole

Etymology

From Latin dispensatio (distribution, management), from dispensare (to distribute, manage).
