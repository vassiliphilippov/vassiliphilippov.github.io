plowland

1. NOUN

/ ˈplaʊˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈplaʊˌland / (BrE)
Land that has been plowed and is used for growing crops.
land plowed for crops

Examples

The farmer spent all spring preparing his plowland for the new season's crops.
Vast stretches of plowland bordered the village, ripe with wheat and barley.
After the storm, much of the plowland required repairs before it could be planted again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arable land - land suitable for growing crops
