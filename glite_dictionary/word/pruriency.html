pruriency

1. NOUN

/ ˈprʊr.i.ən.si / (AmE), / ˈprʊə.ri.ən.si / (BrE)
An excessive or morbid sexual desire; a tendency toward lewd or lascivious thoughts or behavior.
excessive sexual desire; lewdness

Examples

The scandal was fueled by the public's pruriency and curiosity about the intimate details.
His writing was criticized for appealing too much to pruriency rather than genuine artistic expression.
Some tabloids thrive on the pruriency of their readers, offering sensational stories full of innuendo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lust - intense sexual desire
  • Synonym: lewdness - the quality of being obscene or indecent
  • Antonym: modesty - the quality of being modest, not overly sexual

Etymology

From Latin 'prurientia' (itching, longing), from 'prurire' (to itch, to long for).
