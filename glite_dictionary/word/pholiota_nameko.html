Pholiota nameko

1. NOUN

/ foʊˈliː.ə.tə nɑːˈmeɪ.koʊ / (AmE), / fəˈliː.ə.tə nəˈmeɪ.kəʊ / (BrE)
a cultivated edible mushroom with a slightly gelatinous cap, widely grown and used in Japanese cuisine
cultivated edible Japanese mushroom

Examples

Many Japanese recipes use Pholiota nameko to add a unique flavor and texture to soups and noodle dishes.
The slightly slippery texture of Pholiota nameko makes it a sought-after ingredient in Asian cuisine.
Cultivators often export Pholiota nameko mushrooms to specialty markets around the world.

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Pholiota' (from Greek 'pholis' meaning 'scale', referring to the scaly cap), species 'nameko' from Japanese ナメコ (nameko).
