dessiatine

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛsiˈɑtiːn / (AmE), / ˌdɛsiˈɑtiːn / (BrE)
a former Russian unit of area, equivalent to approximately 2.7 acres (10,925 square meters)
old Russian area unit (2.7 acres)

Examples

The estate covered nearly ten dessiatines of fertile land.
Historical records often list rural properties in dessiatines rather than hectares or acres.
Under Russian law, peasants could own a certain number of dessiatines.

Etymology

From Russian 'десятина' (desyatina), meaning 'tenth part'.
