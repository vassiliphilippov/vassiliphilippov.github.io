decagon

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛkəˌgɑn / (AmE), / ˈdɛkəɡən / (BrE)
A polygon with ten sides and ten angles.
ten-sided polygon

Examples

The students learned how to draw a regular decagon using only a ruler and compass.
Each side of the decagon measured exactly five centimeters in length.
Architects sometimes use a decagon shape to create unique and visually striking buildings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polygon - a geometric figure with three or more straight sides and angles
  • Degree Lower: pentagon - a polygon with five sides and five angles
  • Degree Higher: dodecagon - a polygon with twelve sides and twelve angles

Etymology

From Greek deka (ten) + gōnia (angle).
