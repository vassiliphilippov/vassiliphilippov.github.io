swap

1. VERB

/ swɑp / (AmE), / swɒp / (BrE)
to exchange one thing for another
exchange one thing for another

Examples

Let's swap seats so you can see the stage better.
She swapped her red sweater for a blue one at the shop.
People often swap recipes with each other at cooking classes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exchange - to give something and receive something else in return
  • Synonym: trade - to give something in return for something received

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin, related to 'swop' in British English.

Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ swɑp / (AmE), / swɒp / (BrE)
an act of exchanging one thing for another, especially of equal value
an act of exchange

Examples

They made a swap of baseball cards at the playground.
Our school organized a clothing swap to promote sustainability.
We did a book swap with our neighbors last weekend.

Synset Examples

we had no money so we had to live by barter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exchange - the act of giving one thing and receiving another of the same kind
  • Synonym: trade - the action of buying, selling, or exchanging goods and services

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'swappen' (to strike, exchange), related to Low German 'swappen' (to swap, exchange).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01111592-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ swɑp / (AmE), / swɒp / (BrE)
(in computing) To move a program or a part of it into memory so it can be executed, typically by exchanging it with another part already in memory.
move program part into memory (computing)
medical

Examples

When the operating system needs more RAM, it can swap inactive program segments into memory.
The process scheduler may swap a task back into memory when it is ready to run.
Older systems often had to swap large parts of programs to minimize memory usage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: page - move a block of memory from disk to RAM (computing)
Source: WordNet
