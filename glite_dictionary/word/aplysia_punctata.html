Aplysia punctata

1. NOUN

/ əˈplɪziə pʌŋkˈteɪtə / (AmE), / əˈplɪzɪə pʌŋkˈtɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of naked marine gastropod, also known as the sea hare, with a soft body, reduced internal shell, and two pairs of ear-like tentacles.
a species of sea hare (marine gastropod)

Examples

Researchers observed the nervous system of the Aplysia punctata to gain insights into memory formation.
The Aplysia punctata can release a purple ink when threatened by predators.
In tide pools, you might spot an Aplysia punctata grazing on algae along the rocks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sea hare - marine gastropod mollusk of the family Aplysiidae

Etymology

From Latin genus name *Aplysia* (‘unwashed’) and species epithet *punctata* (‘spotted’).
