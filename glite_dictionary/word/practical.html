practical

1. ADJ

/ ˈpræktɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈpræktɪkəl / (BrE)
Almost or nearly; being such in most respects though not strictly or officially so.
almost; nearly (not strictly but essentially)

Examples

After the storm, the town was in practical ruin, though a few buildings remained standing.
With no money left, their trip was a practical failure before it even began.
Given the delays, finishing on time is practically impossible.

Synset Examples

a practical failure
the once elegant temple lay in virtual ruin

Related Terms

  • Synonym: virtual - being such in essence or effect though not in name or form

Etymology

From Late Latin practicalis, from practicus (practical).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpræktɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈpræktɪkəl / (BrE)
concerned with actual use or practice, guided by experience and observation rather than theory
guided by experience, not just theory

Examples

She prefers a practical solution instead of endless discussion about theories.
His practical experience in construction makes him a valuable team member.
We need practical advice that can actually be applied in real life.

Synset Examples

a hardheaded appraisal of our position
a hard-nosed labor leader
completely practical in his approach to business
not ideology but pragmatic politics

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pragmatic - dealing with things sensibly and realistically
  • Antonym: theoretical - concerned with theory rather than practical application

Etymology

From late Latin practicus (practical), from Greek praktikos (concerned with action or doing).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01947268-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈpræktɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈpræktɪkəl / (BrE)
concerned with or involving the actual doing or use of something rather than with theory or ideas
concerned with real use, not theory

Examples

Students often struggle to see the practical benefits of mathematical concepts in everyday life.
The engineer focused on practical solutions rather than abstract theories.
We need a practical approach to reduce energy consumption in the office.

Synset Examples

practical applications of calculus

Related Terms

  • Antonym: theoretical - concerned with or involving the theory of a subject rather than its practical use

Etymology

From Late Latin practicus (practical), from Greek praktikos (concerned with action).

4. ADJ

/ ˈpræk.tɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈpræk.tɪ.kəl / (BrE)
concerned with or suited to real situations, rather than theory or speculation; useful and effective in actual use.
concerned with real use, not theory

Examples

She has a very practical approach to solving problems at work.
Learning to cook is a practical skill that will always be useful.
The course offered practical experience, not just theory.

Synset Examples

he is a very practical person
the idea had no practical application
a practical knowledge of Japanese
woodworking is a practical art

Related Terms

  • Antonym: theoretical - concerned with theory rather than practical application
  • Synonym: useful - able to be used for a practical purpose in real life

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French *practique*, from Medieval Latin *practicus* (practical), from Greek *praktikos* (concerned with action).
