unionization

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuːniənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌjuːniənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of forming or organizing workers into a labor union
the process of forming a labor union

Examples

The company resisted unionization efforts by its employees for many years.
Rapid unionization of factories led to better working conditions for many laborers.
Concerns about unionization surfaced during contract negotiations with management.

Synset Examples

the issue underlying the strike was unionization

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to unionize - to organize workers into a labor union
  • Synonym: collectivization - the process of organizing people or things into a collective group, often in agriculture or labor
  • Antonym: deunionization - the process of dissolving or reducing the influence of labor unions

Etymology

From 'union' (Latin 'unio', meaning unity) and '-ization' (forming nouns of action or process).
