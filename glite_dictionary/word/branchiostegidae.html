Branchiostegidae

1. NOUN

/ brænkiˌɑːstəˈdʒɪdiː / (AmE), / braŋkɪˌɒstəˈdʒɪdiː / (BrE)
A small family of marine fishes characterized by covered gills, belonging to the order Perciformes.
family of marine fishes with covered gills

Examples

The Branchiostegidae are distinguished by the presence of covered gills.
Marine biologists have discovered a new species within the Branchiostegidae family.
The anatomy of Branchiostegidae offers insights into the adaptation of fishes to coastal waters.

Etymology

From Modern Latin Branchiostegidae, from Greek branchia (gills) + stegē (covering).

2. NOUN

/ ˌbræŋ.kioʊ.stəˈdʒiː.di / (AmE), / ˌbraŋ.kɪ.ɒ.steˈdʒiː.diː / (BrE)
a small family of marine fishes characterized by covered gills, including certain tilefishes
family of marine fishes with covered gills

Examples

The Branchiostegidae are commonly found in deep marine waters in tropical regions.
Scientists discovered a new species belonging to the Branchiostegidae family.
Because of their covered gills, members of the Branchiostegidae are easily distinguished from related fish families.

Etymology

From Greek 'branchia' (gills) + 'stegos' (covering), referring to the covered gills.
