Notropis

1. NOUN

/ noʊˈtroʊpɪs / (AmE), / nəʊˈtrəʊpɪs / (BrE)
a genus of small freshwater fish in the family Cyprinidae, commonly known as shiners, found in North America
genus of North American freshwater fish (shiners)

Examples

The biologist studied the river's population of Notropis to understand its ecological health.
Several species within the Notropis genus are popular among freshwater aquarium enthusiasts.
You can find many types of Notropis in clear streams throughout North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shiner - a small, silvery freshwater fish; common name for Notropis species

Etymology

From Greek 'notos' (back) and 'tropis' (keel), referring to the shape of the fish's body.
