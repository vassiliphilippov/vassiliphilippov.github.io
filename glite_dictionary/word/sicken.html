to sicken

1. VERB

/ ˈsɪkən / (AmE), / ˈsɪkən / (BrE)
to cause strong feelings of aversion, disgust, or moral offense in someone
cause strong aversion or disgust

Examples

The gruesome details of the crime scene sickened everyone present.
Her blatant dishonesty truly sickens me.
Many people were sickened by the corruption revealed in the report.

Synset Examples

The pornographic pictures sickened us

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disgust - to cause a strong feeling of dislike or revulsion
  • Synonym: to offend - to cause someone to feel upset or annoyed, often due to moral reasons
  • Antonym: delight - to give great pleasure or joy to someone
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01812341-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈsɪkən / (AmE), / ˈsɪkən / (BrE)
to make someone feel sick, upset, or disgusted, especially by causing nausea
cause to feel sick or nauseated

Examples

The foul odor of the garbage would sicken anyone with a sensitive stomach.
The cruelty described in the report genuinely sickened her.
Seeing the moldy food on the table began to sicken the guests.

Synset Examples

The smell of the food turned the pregnant woman's stomach
The mold on the food sickened the diners

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to nauseate - to make someone feel as if they are going to vomit
  • Synonym: disgust - to cause someone to feel revulsion or profound disapproval

Etymology

From Old English 'siccan', related to 'sick'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02199637-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈsɪk.ən / (AmE), / ˈsɪk.ən / (BrE)
to make someone sick or ill, either physically or mentally
make sick; cause illness

Examples

The spoiled meat sickened everyone at the picnic.
The graphic images in the documentary sicken many viewers.
The smell of chemicals in the factory can sicken workers over time.

Synset Examples

This kind of food sickens me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to nauseate - to make someone feel sick or disgusted
  • Antonym: to heal - to make healthy or well again

Etymology

From Old English *siccian* (to become sick).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00087961-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈsɪkən / (AmE), / ˈsɪkən / (BrE)
to become sick or ill; to fall ill
to become sick or ill

Examples

After eating the spoiled food, he began to sicken within hours.
Several people may sicken if the flu continues to spread through the community.
She seemed fine all day, but suddenly started to sicken that evening.

Synset Examples

She fell sick last Friday, and now she is in the hospital

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fall ill - to become ill or sick
  • Antonym: to recover - to get well after being sick

Etymology

From Old English 'siccan', related to 'sick'.
