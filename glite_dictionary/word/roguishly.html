roguishly

1. ADV

/ ˈroʊɡɪʃli / (AmE), / ˈrəʊɡɪʃli / (BrE)
In a way that is characteristic of a rogue; playfully mischievous or slightly dishonest.
in a mischievous or playful manner

Examples

She smiled roguishly and winked at her friend.
He glanced roguishly at the audience before delivering his punchline.
The child roguishly hid his mother's keys as a prank.

Synset Examples

he roguishly intended to keep the money

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mischievously - in a way that shows a fondness for causing trouble playfully
  • Antonym: honestly - in a truthful or sincere way

Etymology

From 'rogue' + '-ish' + '-ly'.

2. ADV

/ ˈroʊɡɪʃli / (AmE), / ˈrəʊɡɪʃli / (BrE)
In a playfully mischievous or cheeky manner.
in a playful or mischievous way

Examples

He smiled roguishly as he revealed the surprise.
She roguishly suggested they sneak out for dessert before dinner.
The child glanced roguishly at his mother after hiding her keys.

Synset Examples

he winked at her roguishly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mischievously - in a way showing playful desire to cause trouble
  • Antonym: innocently - in a way that shows no desire to cause harm or trouble

Etymology

From 'roguish' + '-ly'. 'Roguish' derives from 'rogue', possibly from Latin 'rogare' (to beg).
