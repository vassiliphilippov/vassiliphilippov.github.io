Lanius excubitor

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪni.əs ɛkˈskjuːbɪtər / (AmE), / ˈleɪnɪ.əs ɛkˈskjuːbɪtə / (BrE)
the scientific name for the great grey shrike, a common European butcherbird known for impaling its prey
scientific name for the great grey shrike

Examples

Lanius excubitor is notable for its behavior of impaling prey on thorns or barbed wire.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot a Lanius excubitor during their excursion in eastern Europe.
The diet of Lanius excubitor includes insects, small birds, and rodents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: great grey shrike - common European butcherbird; English name for Lanius excubitor

Etymology

From Latin: 'lanius' meaning 'butcher' and 'excubitor' meaning 'sentinel'.
