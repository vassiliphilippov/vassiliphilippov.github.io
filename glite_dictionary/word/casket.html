casket

1. VERB

/ ˈkæskɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɑːskɪt / (BrE)
to place or enclose (something, especially a dead body) in a casket
enclose or place in a casket

Examples

The family decided to casket their loved one's remains in an oak coffin.
After the ceremony, the undertakers carefully casketed the body for burial.
It is a tradition to casket the deceased with personal mementos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to entomb - to place a dead body in a tomb

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæskɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɑːskɪt / (BrE)
A small, often ornate box for storing jewels, valuables, or precious items.
ornate box for valuables or jewels

Examples

She kept her grandmother's pearls in a velvet-lined casket on her dresser.
The ornate casket was passed down through generations as a family heirloom.
Each casket in the museum display was decorated with intricate designs and precious stones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jewel box - A small box for storing jewelry

Etymology

From Old French 'cassette', diminutive of 'casse' (box), from Latin 'capsa' (box).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02981514-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈkæskɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɑːskɪt / (BrE)
A box in which a dead body is buried or cremated.
coffin or burial box

Examples

The family gathered around the casket to say their final goodbyes.
She placed a single rose on the casket during the funeral service.
The polished casket was carefully lowered into the ground as everyone watched in silence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coffin - a box in which a dead body is buried

Etymology

From Old French casse (box), from Latin capsa (box, case).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
