Photinia arbutifolia

1. NOUN

/ foʊˈtɪniə ɑːrˌbjuːtəˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / fəʊˈtɪniə ɑːˌbjuːtɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
An ornamental evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Pacific coast of the United States, with large white flowers and red berry-like fruits, sometimes classified within the genus Photinia.
evergreen shrub native to the Pacific US

Examples

Gardeners often plant Photinia arbutifolia for its attractive red berries and glossy foliage.
The landscape design featured a row of Photinia arbutifolia along the southern fence.
In spring, Photinia arbutifolia produces clusters of white flowers that attract pollinators.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek photeinos (shining), referring to the glossy leaves; species name from Latin arbutus (strawberry tree) and folium (leaf).
