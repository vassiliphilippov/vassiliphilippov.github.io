thymidine

1. NOUN

/ ˈθaɪməˌdiːn / (AmE), / ˈθɪmɪdiːn / (BrE)
A nucleoside consisting of thymine linked to deoxyribose, found as a component of DNA.
a nucleoside in DNA
medical

Examples

The researchers measured the amount of thymidine incorporated into the cells to assess DNA synthesis.
An absence of thymidine can inhibit cell division and slow growth.
Radioactive thymidine is often used to label newly synthesized DNA in laboratory experiments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cytidine - a nucleoside component of RNA and DNA
  • Synonym: uridine - a nucleoside component of RNA

Etymology

From thymine + –idine, a common chemical suffix for nucleosides.
