goof

1. NOUN

/ ɡuf / (AmE), / ɡuːf / (BrE)
a person who is regarded as silly, foolish, or incompetent, especially a man
a silly or incompetent person
informal

Examples

Stop being such a goof and take this seriously.
Everyone laughed when the goof tripped over his own feet on stage.
He admitted he was a goof for forgetting his own birthday.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fool - a person who acts unwisely or imprudently
  • Synonym: idiot - a person of low intelligence or who acts very stupidly
  • Antonym: genius - a person of exceptional intellect or talent

Etymology

Probably from American English slang, possibly imitative, first recorded in early 20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ɡuːf / (AmE), / ɡuːf / (BrE)
a person who amuses others by acting in a silly or ridiculous way; a fool
person acting in a silly manner
informal

Examples

Everyone thinks Jake is a goof, but his jokes always lighten the mood.
Don't be such a goof—try to act seriously during the meeting.
Her brother is a real goof who dances around the kitchen making funny faces.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clown - a person who amuses others by playful or comic behavior
  • Synonym: fool - a person who acts unwisely or imprudently; a silly person
  • Antonym: genius - a person with exceptional intellectual or creative power

Etymology

Probably from earlier 'goff' (fool, simpleton), of uncertain origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09950334-n

3. VERB

/ ɡuːf / (AmE), / ɡuːf / (BrE)
to make a careless or silly mistake
make a silly mistake
informal

Examples

I can't believe I goofed on such an easy question during the test.
He really goofed when he forgot to save his work before closing the program.
Try not to goof this time; double-check your calculations.

Synset Examples

I blundered during the job interview

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blunder - to make a serious mistake, usually due to carelessness or confusion
  • Synonym: to err - to make an error or go astray in conduct, thought, or belief

Etymology

Probably an alteration of 'goofus', of uncertain origin, possibly related to 'goof' meaning a foolish person.
