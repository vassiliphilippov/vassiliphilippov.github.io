Pempheridae

1. NOUN

/ pɛmˈfɛrɪˌdi / (AmE), / pɛmˈfɛrɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, tropical marine fish known as sweepers, found mainly in the Indo-Pacific region.
family of tropical marine fish (sweepers)

Examples

The Pempheridae are commonly found near coral reefs in the Indian and Pacific Oceans.
Scientists have identified over 25 species within the Pempheridae family.
At night, Pempheridae emerge from their hiding places to feed on plankton.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweeper - a small tropical marine fish of the family Pempheridae

Etymology

From New Latin Pempheridae, based on the genus name Pempheris.

2. NOUN

/ pɛmˈfɛrɪˌdi / (AmE), / pɛmˈfɛrɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, nocturnal marine fishes known as sweepers, found primarily in tropical and subtropical waters; characterized by compressed bodies and large eyes.
a family of marine fishes called sweepers

Examples

The Pempheridae are commonly found in coral reef environments in the Indo-Pacific region.
Members of the Pempheridae family are noted for their reflective scales and nighttime feeding habits.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Pempheridae near the Australian coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweeper - a small marine fish of the family Pempheridae

Etymology

From Greek *pempherēs* (puffed, glaring) + taxonomic suffix -idae, denoting a family.
