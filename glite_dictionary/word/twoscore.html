twoscore

1. ADJ

/ ˈtuˌskɔr / (AmE), / ˈtuːˌskɔː / (BrE)
amounting to forty; being two times twenty.
forty; two times twenty
archaic

Examples

The army boasted twoscore men ready for battle.
He had waited twoscore years to return to his homeland.
The document was written nearly twoscore generations ago.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: score - twenty; a group or set of twenty items
  • Degree Higher: threescore - sixty; being three times twenty

Etymology

Formed from 'two' + 'score', where 'score' refers to twenty, from Old Norse 'skor' (mark, tally).
