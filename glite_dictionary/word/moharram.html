Moharram

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈhɑːrəm / (AmE), / məˈhærəm / (BrE)
the first month of the Islamic calendar, observed as a holy month in Islam, particularly notable for the Day of Ashura.
first month of the Islamic calendar

Examples

The Islamic New Year begins with Moharram, a month of reflection and mourning for many Muslims.
The Day of Ashura is observed on the tenth day of Moharram each year.
Many communities hold processions during Moharram to commemorate significant events in Islamic history.

Etymology

From Arabic 'Muḥarram', meaning 'forbidden' or 'sacred'.
