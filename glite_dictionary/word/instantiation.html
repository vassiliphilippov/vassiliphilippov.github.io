instantiation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪnˌstænʃiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnˌstænʃɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of representing an idea or concept by a specific example or instance of it
concrete example of a concept or type

Examples

The architect provided a striking instantiation of modern design in her latest building.
Each object in the program is an instantiation of a specific class.
Philosophers often debate whether morality has any real-world instantiation.

Synset Examples

how many instantiations were found?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: instance - an example or single occurrence of something

Etymology

From Latin 'instantia' (example, presence), influenced by 'instance' and 'instantiate'.
