after

1. ADV

/ ˈæf.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈɑːf.tə / (BrE)
at a later time than a specified or implied event or point in time
at a later time

Examples

We finished dinner, and an hour after we went for a walk.
The meeting will start at 3 PM, so please arrive any time after that.
He returned home after he finished his work.

Synset Examples

he apologized subsequently
he's going to the store but he'll be back here later
it didn't happen until afterward
two hours after that

Related Terms

  • Antonym: before - at an earlier time than a specified event
  • Synonym: later - at a time following the present or a specified time

Etymology

From Old English æfter, related to Old Norse eptir.

2. ADV

/ ˈæftər / (AmE), / ˈɑːftə / (BrE)
Following or at the rear of someone or something in space or order.
at the rear or behind

Examples

He followed closely after the leader during the march.
The parade moved down the street, with the clowns coming after the marching band.
She glanced after her friend as he hurried out the door.

Synset Examples

and Jill came tumbling after

Related Terms

  • Synonym: behind - at or toward the rear of
  • Antonym: ahead - in front or further forward

Etymology

From Old English 'æfter' (after, behind), related to Old Frisian and Old High German forms.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00510603-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADJ

/ ˈæf.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈɑːf.tə / (BrE)
Situated closer to the stern or rear of a ship or aircraft.
near the stern or rear (ship or aircraft)

Examples

The after cabins of the ship offer more privacy than the forward ones.
Repairs were being carried out in the after section of the aircraft.
Crew members gathered near the after deck as the sun set.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: forward - situated nearer to the bow, front or ahead (ship or aircraft)

Etymology

Old English æfter, meaning 'after, behind, later'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01037224-s
Synset POS: s
