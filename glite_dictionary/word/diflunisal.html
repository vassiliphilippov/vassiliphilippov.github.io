diflunisal

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈfluːnɪsæl / (AmE), / dɪˈfluːnɪsæl / (BrE)
A nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug used to treat arthritis and other inflammatory conditions.
an anti-inflammatory drug for arthritis
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed diflunisal to help manage her chronic arthritis pain.
Taking diflunisal regularly reduced his joint swelling and stiffness.
Some patients experience fewer stomach problems with diflunisal compared to other NSAIDs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ibuprofen - a common nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug
  • Antonym: acetaminophen - a pain reliever not classed as an NSAID

Etymology

Blend of 'di-' (two), 'fluoro' (containing fluorine), and 'salicylic acid.'
