enfeeblement

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfibəlmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈfiːblmənt / (BrE)
the process or condition of becoming physically or mentally weak; the state of being enfeebled
the state of being weakened

Examples

The long illness resulted in a gradual enfeeblement of his body and mind.
Economic crisis often leads to the enfeeblement of national power.
After weeks of little food, she struggled with the enfeeblement caused by malnutrition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weakness - the state of lacking strength or energy
  • Antonym: strength - the quality or state of being physically strong

Etymology

From Middle French 'enfeebler', from Old French 'en-' (cause to be) + 'feble' (weak).
