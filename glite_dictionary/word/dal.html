dal

1. NOUN

/ dæl / (AmE), / dæl / (BrE)
a metric unit of volume or capacity equal to 10 liters
metric unit (10 liters)

Examples

The tank can hold up to 5 dal of water.
Wine production is often measured in dals for larger quantities.
The recipe called for one dal of milk, which is equal to 10 liters.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: liter - a metric unit of capacity equal to one cubic decimeter
  • Degree Higher: hectoliter - a metric unit of capacity equal to 100 liters

Etymology

From the International System of Units, combining 'deca-' (ten) and 'liter'.
