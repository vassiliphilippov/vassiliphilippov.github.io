investigation

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌvɛstəˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌvɛstɪˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
A formal or systematic inquiry to discover and examine the facts about something, typically to uncover the truth or gather information about unfamiliar or questionable activities.
systematic inquiry or examination

Examples

The police launched an investigation into the cause of the fire.
A thorough investigation revealed several irregularities in the accounting records.
The committee's investigation will take several months to complete.

Synset Examples

there was a congressional probe into the scandal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: probe - a thorough investigation, especially in a formal context
  • Synonym: inquiry - an act of asking for information, often in a formal context

Etymology

From Latin 'investigatio', from 'investigare' meaning 'to trace out, search after'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˌvɛstɪˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌvɛstɪˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of examining a situation, event, or problem thoroughly and systematically to discover the facts or truth.
thorough and systematic inquiry or examination

Examples

The police launched an investigation into the cause of the fire.
After months of investigation, the truth was finally uncovered.
A scientific investigation revealed new information about the disease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: probe - a thorough investigation, often to discover the cause of something
  • Synonym: examination - a detailed inspection or investigation
  • Synonym: inquiry - an act of asking for information or an official investigation

Etymology

From Latin 'investigatio' (a searching into), from 'investigare' (to track, trace).

Synset POS: n
