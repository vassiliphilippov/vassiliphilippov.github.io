storehouse

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɔːrˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈstɔː.haʊs / (BrE)
a building or place where goods are stored for future use
building for storing goods

Examples

The grain was kept in a large storehouse on the edge of town.
After the harvest, workers moved the produce into the storehouse for safekeeping.
Old ledgers were discovered inside an abandoned storehouse.

Synset Examples

storehouses were built close to the docks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warehouse - a large building for storing goods before distribution
  • Synonym: depository - a place where things are stored safely

Etymology

Formed from 'store' + 'house'; in use since Middle English period.
