embassy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛm.bə.si / (AmE), / ˈɛm.bə.si / (BrE)
the group of people, including the ambassador and their staff, who represent their country at a foreign diplomatic mission
the ambassador and staff representing a country

Examples

The American embassy worked tirelessly to assist citizens during the crisis.
She was invited to a reception hosted by the French embassy.
The embassy organized cultural exchange programs in the host country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consulate - the office or staff of a consul, a lower rank diplomatic official

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'ambassia', influenced by 'ambassador'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛmbəsi / (AmE), / ˈɛmbəsi / (BrE)
The official residence or offices of an ambassador and their staff in a foreign country.
official building for diplomats

Examples

The American embassy in Paris is located near the river Seine.
She went to the embassy to renew her visa before it expired.
Security has increased around the embassy following recent political unrest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consulate - a building where a consul's duties are carried out

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *ambassia*, from Old French *ambassade* (mission, embassy).
