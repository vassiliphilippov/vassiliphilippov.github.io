wakeless

1. ADJ

/ ˈweɪk.ləs / (AmE), / ˈweɪk.lɪs / (BrE)
(of sleep) deep and without interruption; so sound that one does not awaken easily.
deep, uninterrupted sleep

Examples

After the long hike, I collapsed into a wakeless slumber that lasted till morning.
The child’s wakeless sleep was disturbed by a loud thunderstorm.
Lost in wakeless rest, he did not hear the alarm clock at all.

Synset Examples

a heavy sleep
fell into a profound sleep
a sound sleeper
deep wakeless sleep

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sound - (of sleep) deep and peaceful
  • Antonym: restless - unable to relax or rest; often waking or disturbed

Etymology

Formed from 'wake' + '-less', meaning 'without waking.'
