notary

1. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊ.tə.ri / (AmE), / ˈnəʊ.tə.ri / (BrE)
a person legally authorized to witness the signing of documents, certify their validity, and take depositions
person who certifies documents
legal

Examples

A notary must witness the signing of these legal documents.
Before submitting the contract, we had it certified by a notary.
She visited a notary to have her signature verified.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: notary public - an official notary licensed to perform functions for the public

Etymology

From Latin 'notarius' (clerk, secretary).
