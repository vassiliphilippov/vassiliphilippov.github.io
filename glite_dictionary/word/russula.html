Russula

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌs.jʊ.lə / (AmE), / ˈrʌs.jʊ.lə / (BrE)
A large genus of fungi characterized by stout stems, brittle caps of various colors, and white spores, lacking both a ring and a volva.
a genus of fungi with brittle, colorful caps

Examples

The woods were carpeted with Russula mushrooms in every shade of red and green.
Scientists identified the specimen as a member of the Russula genus due to its brittle cap and lack of milky juice.
Foragers collected several Russula species, but only some were suitable for eating.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Lactarius - a related genus of fungi that exudes milky juice when cut

Etymology

From Latin 'russula', diminutive of 'russa' (red), referring to the color of some species.

2. NOUN

/ ruˈsuːlə / (AmE), / rʌˈsjuːlə / (BrE)
A large genus of stout-stemmed mushrooms with brittle caps of various colors and white spores, lacking both an annulus and volva, and not exuding milky juice.
genus of brittle-capped mushrooms

Examples

Several species of Russula can be found growing beneath birch trees in the autumn.
The distinctive brittle cap is a key feature that helps identify mushrooms of the genus Russula.
Unlike some toxic fungi, many Russula mushrooms are safe to handle, though not all are edible.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Lactarius - genus of milk-cap mushrooms, similar but exuding milky juice when cut

Etymology

From Latin *russulus* (reddish), referencing the common cap color in some species.
