moment

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊmənt / (AmE), / ˈməʊmənt / (BrE)
A very short period of time; a particular point in time when something happens.
a particular point in time

Examples

There was a pause, and then, in a single moment, the decision was made.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: instant - a particular point in time
  • Degree Lower: second - a short and precise time unit

Etymology

From Latin 'momentum' (movement, motion, instant).

3. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊmənt / (AmE), / ˈməʊmənt / (BrE)
A turning force produced by a force acting at a distance from a pivot point, or a measure of this force.
turning force; measure of torque

Examples

The engineer calculated the moment needed to rotate the beam.
A small force applied far from the hinge can produce a large moment.
To prevent the structure from tipping, the resisting moment must exceed the overturning force.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torque - the measure of the force that can cause an object to rotate about an axis

6. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊmənt / (AmE), / ˈməʊmənt / (BrE)
In mathematics and statistics, a quantitative measure expressing the expected value of a specified power of the deviations of a random variable from a fixed point, usually its mean.
statistical measure of deviation (math)

Examples

The variance is the second moment about the mean in a probability distribution.
Calculating the third moment can provide information on the skewness of a dataset.
The concept of a moment is fundamental in probability and statistics.

Etymology

From Latin momentum (movement, importance), in mathematics from use in physics for a measure of force times distance.
