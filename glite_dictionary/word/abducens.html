abducens

1. NOUN

/ æbˈduː.sənz / (AmE), / æbˈdjuː.sənz / (BrE)
the sixth cranial nerve, responsible for controlling the lateral rectus muscle of the eye, which moves the eyeball outward
sixth cranial nerve controlling eye movement
medical

Examples

Damage to the abducens can cause double vision and difficulty moving the eye outward.
Your doctor may test your abducens function during a neurological exam.
The abducens nerve is essential for coordinated horizontal eye movement.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: oculomotor - the third cranial nerve, controlling most eye muscles
  • Degree Lower: trochlear - the fourth cranial nerve, controlling the superior oblique muscle of the eye

Etymology

From Latin 'abducens', present participle of 'abducere' (to lead away), referring to the nerve's function in moving the eye outward.
