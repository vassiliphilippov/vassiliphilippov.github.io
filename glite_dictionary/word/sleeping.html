sleeping

1. NOUN

/ ˈsliːpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsliːpɪŋ / (BrE)
The state of being asleep, marked by a suspension of consciousness and a decrease in metabolic activity.
the state of being asleep

Examples

Sleeping is essential for good health and proper functioning of the body.
We heard a strange noise during the middle of our sleeping hours.
Babies spend most of their time sleeping in the first few months.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wakefulness - the state of being awake and conscious
  • Synonym: sleep - a natural periodic suspension of consciousness

3. NOUN

/ ˈsliːpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsliːpɪŋ / (BrE)
the state of being asleep or at rest, especially when characterized by quietness and inactivity
the state of being asleep

Examples

Sleeping is essential for physical and mental health.
The quiet sleeping of the village was disturbed by the storm.
He was startled out of a deep sleeping by a loud noise.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wakefulness - the state of being awake and alert
  • Synonym: sleep - a natural state of rest during which the body is inactive and the mind is unconscious

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'sleep' + -ing suffix, denoting the act or process.
