Juniperus flaccida

1. NOUN

/ dʒuːˈnɪpərəs flækˈsiːdə / (AmE), / dʒuːˈnɪpərəs flækˈsɪdə / (BrE)
a small tree native to western Texas and the mountains of Mexico, with spreading branches and drooping branchlets; also known as weeping juniper
species of juniper tree

Examples

The Juniperus flaccida can be identified by its distinctive drooping branchlets.
Researchers observed that Juniperus flaccida thrives in the mountainous regions of northern Mexico.
Many birds use the Juniperus flaccida as shelter due to its dense, spreading branches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: juniper - a type of evergreen shrub or tree of the genus Juniperus

Etymology

From Latin: 'Juniperus' (juniper) and 'flaccida' (drooping, weak), referring to its drooping branches.
