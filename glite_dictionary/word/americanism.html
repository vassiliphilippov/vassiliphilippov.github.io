Americanism

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛr.ɪ.kə.nɪz.əm / (AmE), / əˈmɛr.ɪ.kə.nɪz.əm / (BrE)
A word, phrase, or feature that is characteristic of American English, especially as distinguished from other varieties of English.
word or feature unique to American English

Examples

The word 'sidewalk' is an Americanism for what the British call a 'pavement'.
Many Americanisms have found their way into international English.
He compiled a dictionary of Americanism to help non-native speakers understand U.S. usage.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anglicism - a word or phrase characteristic of English, especially as borrowed into another language

Etymology

From American + -ism, first recorded in the 18th century to denote words or usages peculiar to the United States.

2. NOUN

/ əˈmɛr.ɪ.kəˌnɪz.əm / (AmE), / əˈmɛr.ɪ.kəˌnɪz.əm / (BrE)
a custom, practice, or way of life that is characteristic of the United States or its people
a custom peculiar to the U.S.

Examples

Halloween is an Americanism that has spread to many other countries.
Some critics see tipping in restaurants as a needless Americanism.
Fast food culture is often cited as a modern Americanism influencing global eating habits.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Anglicism - a custom or expression characteristic of England or English people

Etymology

From American + -ism, denoting characteristics or customs of the United States.

3. NOUN

/ əˈmɛrɪkəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
Loyalty to the United States and its institutions.
loyalty to the United States

Examples

Her strong sense of Americanism was evident in her dedication to civic duty.
The politician's speech promoted Americanism and national unity.
Some critics argue that excessive Americanism can lead to a disregard for global perspectives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patriotism - Love for or devotion to one's country
  • Antonym: anti-Americanism - Opposition to or hostility toward the United States

Etymology

Formed from 'American' + '-ism', following standard derivational patterns in English.
