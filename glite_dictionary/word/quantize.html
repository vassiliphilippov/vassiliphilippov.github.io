to quantize

1. VERB

/ ˈkwɑn.taɪz / (AmE), / ˈkwɒn.taɪz / (BrE)
to apply the principles of quantum theory to something so that its possible values or states are restricted to discrete amounts, usually integral multiples of a fundamental unit
restrict to discrete quantum values

Examples

Scientists are working on ways to quantize gravity within a unified physical theory.
The engineer needed to quantize the electrical signal for digital processing.
Quantum theory was used to quantize energy levels in the atom.

Synset Examples

Quantize gravity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discretize - to make continuous data discrete by dividing into intervals or separate values

Etymology

From quantum + -ize, meaning to make or treat as in quantum theory.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00639856-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈkwɑːn.taɪz / (AmE), / ˈkwɒn.taɪz / (BrE)
to approximate a continuously varying signal by restricting its amplitude to a set of discrete values
approximate by discrete values (in signals)

Examples

Audio engineers often quantize signals during digital recording to reduce file size.
Modern image processing algorithms quantize colors to simplify complex images.
If you quantize the input, small variations in signal strength are ignored.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to digitize - convert information into a digital form

Etymology

From 'quantum' in Latin meaning 'how much,' plus the suffix '-ize.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00675014-v
Synset POS: v
