Argonautidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑr.ɡəˈnɑ.tɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˌɑː.ɡəˈnæ.tɪ.diː / (BrE)
A family of pelagic octopuses represented solely by the genus Argonauta, commonly known as paper nautiluses.
family of pelagic octopuses (paper nautiluses)

Examples

The Argonautidae family includes only the delicate paper nautiluses.
Scientists studied the reproductive habits of Argonautidae in their oceanic habitat.
Unlike other octopuses, members of the Argonautidae family create thin, shell-like egg cases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Argonauta - genus of pelagic octopuses called paper nautiluses

Etymology

From New Latin, from Argonauta (the genus name) + -idae (a standard zoological suffix for family names).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrɡəˌnɔːtəˈdiː / (AmE), / ˌɑːɡəˌnɔːtɪˈdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine cephalopods, represented solely by the genus Argonauta, commonly known as paper nautiluses.
family of cephalopods (paper nautiluses)

Examples

The Argonautidae are known for their delicate, papery eggcases.
Scientists often study Argonautidae to learn more about cephalopod behavior.
Unlike true nautiluses, members of the Argonautidae family produce their own eggcases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Argonauta - Genus of paper nautiluses; the only genus in Argonautidae

Etymology

From New Latin Argonautidae, based on Argonauta (the type genus) and the suffix -idae used for animal families.
