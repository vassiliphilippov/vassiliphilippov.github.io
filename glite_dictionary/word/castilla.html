Castilla

1. PROPN

/ kæˈstiːjə / (AmE), / kaˈstiːjə / (BrE)
A region of central Spain; formerly a kingdom that unified with Aragon to form modern Spain in 1479.
historical region and former kingdom in Spain

Examples

During the Middle Ages, Castilla played a major role in the Reconquista.
The unification of Castilla and Aragon paved the way for the birth of modern Spain.
Many Spanish customs and traditions have their roots in Castilla.

Etymology

From Spanish 'Castilla', derived from 'castillo' (castle), itself from Latin 'castellum'.
