Caretta

1. NOUN

/ kəˈrɛtə / (AmE), / kəˈrɛtə / (BrE)
A genus of sea turtles that includes the loggerhead turtle, known for its large head and widespread distribution in subtropical and temperate oceans.
genus of loggerhead turtles

Examples

The Caretta is studied by marine biologists due to its threatened status.
Conservation efforts focus on protecting Caretta nesting sites along the coast.
Young Caretta hatchlings face many dangers during their journey to the sea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loggerhead turtle - a large marine turtle with a broad head (Caretta caretta)

Etymology

From Latinized form of Italian *caretta* (loggerhead turtle), of uncertain origin.
