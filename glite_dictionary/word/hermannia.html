Hermannia

1. NOUN

/ hərˈmeɪniə / (AmE), / həˈmɑːnɪə / (BrE)
a genus of African herbs and subshrubs known for their honey-scented, bell-shaped flowers
genus of African herbs with bell-shaped flowers

Examples

The vibrant yellow flowers of Hermannia attract many pollinators in the South African veld.
Gardeners prize Hermannia for its delicate, honey-scented blossoms and low-growing habit.
Researchers studied several species within the Hermannia genus for their ecological adaptations.

Etymology

Named after Paul Hermann (1646–1695), German botanist.

Several species in the Hermannia genus are prized for their ornamental value in gardens.
