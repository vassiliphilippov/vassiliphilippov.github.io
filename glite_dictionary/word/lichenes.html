Lichenes

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈkiːniːz / (AmE), / lɪˈkiːniːz / (BrE)
A former taxonomic group comprising the lichens, organisms that grow symbiotically with algae, sometimes considered an independent group separate from algae and fungi.
former taxonomic group of lichens

Examples

The classification of Lichenes has been revised as scientific understanding evolved.
Many specimens in the herbarium were labeled under Lichenes according to the old system.
Researchers once debated whether Lichenes deserved their own category apart from fungi and algae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lichen - a composite organism from algae and fungi living together
  • Degree Higher: fungus - a kingdom of spore-producing organisms
  • Degree Higher: alga - a simple non-flowering plant living mostly in water

Etymology

From Latin plural of lichen, used in taxonomy.
