to overcapitalize

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈkæpɪtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈkæpɪtəlaɪz / (BrE)
to supply or record more capital in a business than is warranted by its actual value or earning prospects, often resulting in financial difficulties
to provide or record excessive capital in a business

Examples

The company made a critical mistake when it chose to overcapitalize during its early expansion.
If you overcapitalize a business, you risk reducing its long-term profitability.
Banks are cautious not to overcapitalize start-ups with uncertain futures.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: undercapitalize - to provide insufficient capital to a business

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' meaning 'excessively' and 'capitalize'.

2. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈkæpɪtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈkæpɪtəlaɪz / (BrE)
to estimate the value of something, especially a business or property, as being higher than its real market worth
estimate value as higher than market worth

Examples

The company was accused of overcapitalizing its real estate assets to attract investors.
If you overcapitalize your property, you may struggle to sell it at the expected price.
Some small firms unintentionally overcapitalize their business during periods of rapid growth.

Synset Examples

overcapitalize a property

Related Terms

  • Antonym: undercapitalize - to estimate the value of something as being lower than its real worth

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' (excessively) + 'capitalize'.

4. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈkæpɪtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈkæpɪtəlaɪz / (BrE)
to estimate the capital value of a business at an unreasonably or unlawfully high level
estimate company capital too highly

Examples

The accounting firm was accused of trying to overcapitalize the company to attract investors.
If you overcapitalize your business, it may become difficult to deliver expected returns.
Some startups overcapitalize their assets, making their financial statements appear stronger than they are.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: undercapitalize - estimate the capital value of a company at an unreasonably low level

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' (in excess) and 'capitalize' (to provide with capital).
