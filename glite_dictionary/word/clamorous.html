clamorous

1. ADJ

/ ˈklæmərəs / (AmE), / ˈklæmərəs / (BrE)
making a loud and confused noise; characterized by vehement shouting or outcry.
making a loud, noisy outcry

Examples

The protesters were clamorous outside the government building, demanding change.
A clamorous crowd filled the stadium after the winning goal was scored.
His clamorous objection interrupted the entire meeting and drew everyone's attention.

Synset Examples

blatant radios
a clamorous uproar
strident demands
a vociferous mob

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vociferous - expressing opinions or feelings loudly and forcefully
  • Synonym: blatant - (of bad behavior) done openly and unashamedly; glaringly obvious; offensively loud
  • Antonym: quiet - making little or no noise

Etymology

From Latin 'clamorosus' (full of noise or shouting), from 'clamor' (a shout, cry).
