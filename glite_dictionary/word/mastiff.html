mastiff

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæs.tɪf / (AmE), / ˈmɑː.stɪf / (BrE)
a large, powerful dog breed with a deep chest and smooth coat, traditionally used for guarding and protection
a large powerful guard dog breed

Examples

The family kept a mastiff to guard their country house.
A full-grown mastiff can weigh over 200 pounds.
The mastiff slept quietly at the foot of the gate, alert for strangers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bulldog - a muscular, heavy-set dog breed, also used as a guard dog

Etymology

From Middle English mastif, from Old French mastin, from Vulgar Latin *mānsuētīnus (tame, gentle).
