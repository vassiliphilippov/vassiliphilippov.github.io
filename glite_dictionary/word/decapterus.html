Decapterus

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈkæptəˌrəs / (AmE), / dɪˈkæptərəs / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish in the family Carangidae, commonly known as scads or mackerel scad, found in oceans worldwide.

A grilled Decapterus is a popular dish in many coastal communities.

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈkæptərəs / (AmE), / dɪˈkæptərəs / (BrE)
A genus of fish in the Carangidae family, commonly known as scads or mackerel scads, found worldwide in marine waters.
genus of scad fish

Examples

The mackerel scad, belonging to the Decapterus genus, is popular among fishermen in tropical regions.
Species within Decapterus are often found schooling near coastal reefs and offshore waters.
Scientists recently described a new Decapterus species from the western Pacific Ocean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scad - common name for small marine fish in the Carangidae family

Etymology

From Greek deka- (ten) + pteron (fin or wing), referring to fin structure.
