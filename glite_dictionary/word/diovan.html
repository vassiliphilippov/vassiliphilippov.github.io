Diovan

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪ.oʊˌvæn / (AmE), / ˈdaɪ.əʊˌvæn / (BrE)
A proprietary name for an angiotensin II receptor blocker (valsartan) used to treat high blood pressure and heart failure.
brand name for blood pressure medicine
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Diovan to help control her hypertension.
If you experience dizziness while taking Diovan, inform your healthcare provider.
Millions of patients worldwide rely on Diovan for managing high blood pressure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: valsartan - an angiotensin II receptor blocker used to treat high blood pressure
