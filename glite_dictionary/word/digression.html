digression

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈɡrɛʃən / (AmE), / daɪˈɡrɛʃən / (BrE)
a temporary departure from the main subject in speech or writing
deviation from main subject

Examples

Please forgive my digression, but I felt this story was important to share.
The professor often goes off on a digression before returning to the main topic.
Her essay was clear, with only a brief digression about her personal experience.

Synset Examples

a diversion from the main highway
a digression into irrelevant details
a deflection from his goal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diversion - an instance of turning aside from a course or activity
  • Synonym: tangent - a completely different line of thought or action
  • Antonym: focus - concentration on the main point

Etymology

From Latin 'digressio' (a going aside), from 'digredi' (to step aside).

3. NOUN

/ daɪˈɡrɛʃ.ən / (AmE), / daɪˈɡrɛʃ.ən / (BrE)
a temporary departure from the main path or route during a journey
deviation from main path or route

Examples

After a brief digression from the main trail, the hikers soon found their way back on course.
The tour included an intentional digression to visit a hidden waterfall off the main road.
We took a small digression through the woods to avoid the heavy traffic on the highway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detour - an alternative route or deviation from a direct route

Etymology

From Latin 'digressio' (a going away, departure), from 'digredi' (to go apart).
