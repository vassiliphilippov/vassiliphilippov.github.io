cockleshell

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑkəlˌʃɛl / (AmE), / ˈkɒkəlˌʃɛl / (BrE)
a small, light, and fragile boat, typically regarded as insubstantial or incapable of withstanding rough conditions
small, flimsy boat

Examples

The fishermen paddled their cockleshell bravely through the choppy waters.
In such a storm, that tiny boat looked like a cockleshell at the mercy of the sea.
She laughed at the idea of crossing the channel in a mere cockleshell.

Etymology

From 'cockle' (a type of shell) + 'shell,' likening the boat's fragile structure to that of a shell.
