rabbit-weed

1. NOUN

/ ˈræb.ɪtˌwid / (AmE), / ˈræb.ɪtˌwiːd / (BrE)
A low-growing, sticky subshrub native to the southwestern United States, characterized by narrow linear leaves and numerous tiny yellow flower heads.
low-growing southwestern US subshrub with yellow flowers

Examples

The dry plains were dotted with patches of rabbit-weed in full bloom.
Local wildlife often seek shelter among the dense branches of rabbit-weed.
During late summer, the rabbit-weed adds a splash of yellow to the desert landscape.
