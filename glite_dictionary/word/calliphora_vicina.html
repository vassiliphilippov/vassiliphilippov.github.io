Calliphora vicina

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɪfəˌrɔː vəˈsaɪnə / (AmE), / kəˈlɪfəˌrɔː vɪˈsiːnə / (BrE)
A species of blowfly with an iridescent blue body, known for its loud buzzing flight.
species of blue blowfly

Examples

Calliphora vicina is commonly found near decaying organic matter in urban areas.
The wings of Calliphora vicina produce a distinctive buzzing sound.
Forensic entomologists often study Calliphora vicina to estimate the time of death.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blowfly - a type of fly in the family Calliphoridae

Etymology

From New Latin, where 'Calliphora' derives from Greek 'kalliphora' meaning 'bearing beauty', and 'vicina' is Latin for 'neighboring'.
