tailback

1. NOUN

/ ˈteɪlˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈteɪlbæk / (BrE)
(in American football) a player positioned farthest from the line of scrimmage, typically the main ball-carrier in offensive plays.
American football running position
american

Examples

The team's starting tailback rushed for over 100 yards in the season opener.
As a tailback, he needs both speed and agility to break through defensive lines.
She was the first woman to play tailback on her college football team.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: running back - A player who primarily runs with the football; broader category including tailbacks and fullbacks

2. NOUN

/ ˈteɪlˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈteɪlˌbæk / (BrE)
(American football) the offensive back who lines up farthest from the line of scrimmage, typically serving as the team's main ball-carrier.
offensive football position farthest from the line
american

Examples

The team's star tailback rushed for over 150 yards in the playoff game.
As a tailback, he needs both speed and agility to break through the defense.
The coach moved him to the tailback position to take advantage of his running ability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: halfback - an offensive back who lines up behind the quarterback

Etymology

From 'tail' (hind part) + 'back', referring to the player's position at the back of the formation.
