naprapathy

1. NOUN

/ nəˈpræpəθi / (AmE), / nəˈprapəθi / (BrE)
A system of treatment that does not use drugs, based on the belief that symptoms of disease are caused by disorders of ligaments and connective tissues.
system of drugless therapy for connective tissues
medical

Examples

After several unsuccessful treatments, she decided to try naprapathy for her chronic back pain.
He studied naprapathy at a specialized institute before opening his clinic.
Some people choose naprapathy as an alternative to chiropractic care.

Etymology

From Czech 'napravit' (to correct) + Greek 'pathos' (suffering).
