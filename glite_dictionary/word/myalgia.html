myalgia

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈældʒə / (AmE), / maɪˈaldʒɪə / (BrE)
Pain in a muscle or group of muscles.
muscle pain
medical

Examples

Many patients with viral infections report experiencing myalgia during the course of their illness.
After the intense workout, she suffered from severe myalgia in her legs.
Some medications can cause myalgia as a side effect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muscle pain - pain localized in the muscles

Etymology

From Greek 'my-' (muscle) + 'algos' (pain).
