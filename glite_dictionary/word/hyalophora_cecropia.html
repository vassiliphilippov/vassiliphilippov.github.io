Hyalophora cecropia

1. NOUN

/ haɪəˈlɑfərə səˈkroʊpiə / (AmE), / haɪəˈlɒfərə sɪˈkrəʊpiə / (BrE)
a large North American silkworm moth whose larvae feed on the leaves of various forest trees
North American silkworm moth species

Examples

The Hyalophora cecropia is the largest native moth in North America, with a wingspan of up to six inches.
I saw a Hyalophora cecropia cocoon attached to the branch of a maple tree in the park.
Scientists study Hyalophora cecropia to learn about insect development and metamorphosis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cecropia moth - common name for Hyalophora cecropia, large North American moth

Etymology

From Greek 'hyalos' (glass) and 'phoros' (bearing), used in scientific Latin; 'cecropia' refers to Cecrops, a mythical king of Athens.
