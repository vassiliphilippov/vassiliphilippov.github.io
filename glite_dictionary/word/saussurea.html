Saussurea

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈsjʊəriə / (AmE), / sɔːˈsjʊəriə / (BrE)
A genus of herbs native to temperate and cool regions of Eurasia, known for their woolly leaves and often purple or blue flowers.
genus of Eurasian herbs

Examples

The Himalayan variety of Saussurea is prized in traditional medicine.
Many species of Saussurea thrive in high-altitude environments.
Botanists discovered a new Saussurea species in Central Asia.

Etymology

Named after Swiss botanist Horace-Bénédict de Saussure (1740–1799).

2. NOUN

/ soʊˈsjʊəriə / (AmE), / sɔːˈsjʊəriə / (BrE)
A genus of perennial herbs found in temperate and cool regions of Eurasia, including many species used in traditional medicine.
genus of Eurasian perennial herbs

Examples

The rare Himalayan Saussurea is prized for its medicinal properties.
Several species of Saussurea are adapted to grow in alpine environments.
Researchers studied the chemical compounds found in Saussurea leaves.

Etymology

Named after Swiss botanist H.-B. de Saussure (1740–1799).
