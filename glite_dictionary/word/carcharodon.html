Carcharodon

1. NOUN

/ kɑːrˈkærəˌdɑn / (AmE), / kɑːˈkærədɒn / (BrE)
a genus of large predatory sharks, including the great white shark.
genus of large predatory sharks

Examples

The Carcharodon is recognized as one of the ocean's top predators.
Scientists discovered a new fossil belonging to the Carcharodon genus.
Many documentaries showcase the hunting behavior of Carcharodon in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: great white shark - a large predatory shark, Carcharodon carcharias

Etymology

From Greek *karcharos* (sharp, jagged) + *odous* (tooth).
