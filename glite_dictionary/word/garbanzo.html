garbanzo

1. NOUN

/ ɡɑːrˈbɑnzoʊ / (AmE), / ɡɑːˈbænzəʊ / (BrE)
the edible seed of the chickpea plant, used especially in Mediterranean and Middle Eastern cooking
edible seed of the chickpea plant

Examples

The salad was topped with garbanzo beans, tomatoes, and olives.
She added a cup of cooked garbanzos to the stew for extra protein.
Dishes like hummus are made from mashed garbanzo beans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chickpea - the edible round seed of a plant, used in cooking

Etymology

From Spanish 'garbanzo', meaning chickpea.

2. NOUN

/ ɡɑrˈbɑnzoʊ / (AmE), / ɡɑːˈbænzəʊ / (BrE)
a large, round, white legume, usually dried, commonly known as the chickpea and widely used in cooking
large round legume; chickpea

Examples

She added garbanzo beans to the salad for extra protein.
Hummus is traditionally made from mashed garbanzo beans, also known as chickpeas.
The store sells both canned and dried garbanzo beans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chickpea - another common name for the garbanzo legume

Etymology

From Spanish 'garbanzo', referring to the chickpea.
