synchronism

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋ.krəˌnɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋ.krə.nɪ.zəm / (BrE)
the state or fact of events or actions occurring at the same time
coincidence in time; simultaneous occurrence

Examples

The film's director used clever editing to maintain synchronism between the music and the action.
The two clocks are checked regularly to ensure their synchronism.
Perfect synchronism between dancers made the performance truly impressive.

Synset Examples

the drug produces an increased synchrony of the brain waves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: synchrony - the state of being synchronous or simultaneous
  • Antonym: asynchrony - lack of synchrony; not occurring at the same time

Etymology

From Greek 'synchronismos', from 'synchronos' (contemporary, simultaneous).
