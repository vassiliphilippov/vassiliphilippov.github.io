to scry

1. VERB

/ skraɪ / (AmE), / skraɪ / (BrE)
to predict the future or discover hidden knowledge by gazing into a crystal or other reflective surface
predict by gazing into crystals

Examples

In old legends, wizards would scry into crystal balls to uncover hidden truths.
She claimed she could scry her future using a bowl of water.
The fortune teller scried for answers before giving her prediction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: divine - to discover or foretell by supernatural means
  • Synonym: to foretell - to tell what will happen in the future

Etymology

From Old English *scrīan* (to reveal, make manifest); influenced by 'descry'.
