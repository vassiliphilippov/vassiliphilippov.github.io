entrecote

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːntrəˈkoʊt / (AmE), / ˈɒntrɛkəʊt / (BrE)
A boneless steak cut from the rib area of beef, particularly popular in French cuisine.
rib steak cut (culinary)

Examples

He ordered the entrecote cooked medium-rare for dinner at the French restaurant.
The chef recommended the grilled entrecote served with a red wine sauce.
I prefer an entrecote because it is both tender and full of flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ribeye - a tender and flavorful steak cut from the rib section of beef

Etymology

From French entrecôte, from entre (between) + côte (rib).
