lavender

1. NOUN

/ ˈlævəndər / (AmE), / ˈlævəndə / (BrE)
a pale purple colour, like that of the lavender flower
a pale purple color

Examples

She wore a beautiful lavender dress to the wedding.
The sky turned a soft shade of lavender at sunset.
He painted the bedroom walls a calming lavender to create a peaceful atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: violet - a bluish-purple color
  • Degree Lower: lilac - a color that is pale purple with a bluish tinge

Etymology

Named after the lavender flower, from Old French 'lavandre', from Medieval Latin 'lavandula'.

/ ˈlævəndɚ / (AmE), / ˈlævəndə / (BrE)
of a pale purple color, like that of the lavender flower.
pale purple in color

Examples

As the sun set, the sky turned a beautiful lavender hue.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: violet - of a bluish-purple color

3. NOUN

/ ˈlævəndər / (AmE), / ˈlævəndə / (BrE)
a small aromatic shrub or subshrub of the mint family, native to the Old World, with narrow leaves and pale purple or blue flowers, often grown for its fragrance or ornamental use.
aromatic shrub with pale purple flowers

Examples

She planted lavender along the garden path for its lovely scent.
The soothing aroma of lavender is often used in essential oils and perfumes.
Bundles of dried lavender hung from the beams in the old country kitchen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lavandula - the genus of aromatic plants including lavender

Etymology

From Old French lavandre, from Medieval Latin lavandula.
