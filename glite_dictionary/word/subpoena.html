subpoena

1. NOUN

/ səˈpinə / (AmE), / sʌˈpiːnə / (BrE)
a written order issued by a court requiring a person to attend as a witness at a legal proceeding; failure to comply may result in punishment for contempt of court
court order to appear as a witness
legal

Examples

The court issued a subpoena requiring her to testify in the trial next week.
Ignoring a subpoena can result in being held in contempt of court.
The lawyer requested a subpoena to obtain the key witness's attendance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: summons - a legal order to appear in court

Etymology

From Latin 'sub poena' (under penalty).

2. VERB

/ səˈpinə / (AmE), / səˈpiːnə / (BrE)
(Law) To officially order someone to appear in court or to produce documents, by serving them with a subpoena.
order someone to appear in court
legal

Examples

The prosecution subpoenaed several witnesses to testify during the trial.
She was subpoenaed to provide the documents requested by the court.
The company is subpoenaing records as part of its legal defense.

Synset Examples

The witness and her records were subpoenaed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to summon - to order someone to appear in court

Etymology

From Latin sub poena (under penalty).
