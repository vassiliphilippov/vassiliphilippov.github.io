to itemise

1. VERB

/ ˈaɪtəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈaɪtəmaɪz / (BrE)
to list or mention things separately or individually, often in order to make them clear or organized
to list things separately
british

Examples

Please itemise all your expenses for the trip in the report.
They asked the contractor to itemise every cost in the proposal for transparency.
The accountant will itemise each deduction on your tax return this year.

Synset Examples

She enumerated the many obstacles she had encountered
The doctor recited the list of possible side effects of the drug

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to itemize - to list or mention items separately (American spelling)
  • Synonym: to enumerate - to mention (a number of things) one by one

Etymology

From 'item', influenced by the verb-forming suffix '-ise'. Variant spelling of 'itemize', with 'ise' used in British English.

2. VERB

/ ˈaɪtəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈaɪtəmaɪz / (BrE)
to list things separately, especially by providing details for each item
list things separately
british

Examples

The accountant helped me itemise all of my business expenses for the year.
Please itemise each product on the invoice with its corresponding price.
She carefully itemised the damages caused during the move.

Synset Examples

itemize one's tax deductions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to itemize - to list things separately, especially by providing details for each item (US spelling)
  • Antonym: to summarise - to present the main points without detailed listing

Etymology

From 'item' + '-ise', modeled after the noun 'item'; compare American English 'itemize'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00948620-v
Synset POS: v
