Liriodendron

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɪri.oʊˈdɛndrɑn / (AmE), / ˌlɪri.əʊˈdɛndrən / (BrE)
A small genus of tall, deciduous trees known as tulip trees, native to North America and eastern Asia, recognized for their distinctive, tulip-shaped flowers and hardwood.
genus of tulip trees

Examples

The Liriodendron in our garden is covered with yellow-green flowers every spring.
Botanists identify the Liriodendron by its unique leaf shape and tulip-like blooms.
Several species within the Liriodendron genus are valued for their strong, light-colored wood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tulip tree - a common name for species in the genus Liriodendron

Etymology

From Greek leirion (lily) and dendron (tree).
