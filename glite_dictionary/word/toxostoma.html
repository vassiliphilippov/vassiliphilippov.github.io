Toxostoma

1. NOUN

/ tɑkˈsɑstəˌmə / (AmE), / tɒkˈsɒstəˌmə / (BrE)
A genus of North American birds in the family Mimidae, known as thrashers, characterized by their long bills and song-like calls.
genus of North American thrashers

Examples

The Toxostoma genus includes several striking birds with distinctive curved bills.
Researchers observed a Toxostoma in the chaparral, singing loudly from a bush.
Many birdwatchers travel to the Southwest to spot rare species of Toxostoma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrasher - a type of songbird in the family Mimidae

Etymology

From Greek toxon (bow) + stoma (mouth), referring to the curved bill shape.

2. NOUN

/ toʊkˈsɒstəˌmə / (AmE), / tɒkˈsɒstəmə / (BrE)
A genus of North and Central American birds in the family Mimidae, known as thrashers, characterized by their long, curved bills and foraging behavior.
a genus of thrashers (birds)

Examples

The Toxostoma genus includes birds commonly known as thrashers, which are native to North America.
Several species in the Toxostoma genus are noted for their distinctive curved bills.
Ornithologists have studied the foraging habits of Toxostoma in arid environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrasher - a songbird of the family Mimidae with a long tail and curved bill

Etymology

From Greek toxon (bow) + stoma (mouth), referring to the curved bill.
