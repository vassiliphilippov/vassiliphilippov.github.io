hypercholesteremia

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpərkəˌlɛstərˈiːmiə / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəkɒˌlɛstərˈiːmɪə / (BrE)
an abnormally high level of cholesterol in the blood, increasing the risk of heart disease and atherosclerosis
high cholesterol level in the blood
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with hypercholesteremia after reviewing her blood test results.
Untreated hypercholesteremia can lead to serious cardiovascular complications.
Diet and exercise are important in managing hypercholesteremia effectively.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypercholesterolemia - abnormally high cholesterol level in the blood (alternate common medical spelling)

Etymology

From Greek hyper- (over, excessive) + cholesterol + -emia (blood condition).
