globeflower

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡloʊbˌflaʊər / (AmE), / ˈɡləʊbˌflaʊə / (BrE)
any of several perennial plants of the genus Trollius, having rounded, usually yellow flowers resembling a globe
plant with globe-shaped yellow flowers

Examples

The globeflower blooms in late spring and brightens up the shade garden with its vivid yellow petals.
We planted several globeflowers along the edge of the pond.
The globeflower prefers moist soil and partial sunlight for optimal growth.

Etymology

From 'globe' (referring to the spherical shape of the flower) + 'flower'.
