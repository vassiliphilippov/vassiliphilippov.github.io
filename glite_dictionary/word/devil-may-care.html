devil-may-care

1. ADJ

/ ˌdɛvəl meɪ ˈkɛr / (AmE), / ˌdɛvəl meɪ ˈkeə / (BrE)
cheerfully unconcerned about consequences; carefree and irresponsible in attitude or behavior
cheerfully irresponsible; carefree

Examples

She has a devil-may-care attitude towards her finances, spending freely without worry.
His devil-may-care approach to life often gets him into trouble, but he never seems concerned.
With a devil-may-care grin, he signed up for the most dangerous challenge at the festival.

Synset Examples

carefree with his money
freewheeling urban youths
had a harum-scarum youth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carefree - untroubled and unconcerned
  • Degree Higher: reckless - without regard for danger or consequences

Etymology

From the expression 'the devil may care,' meaning a person acts as if only the devil would care, indicating disregard for consequences.

2. ADJ

/ ˌdɛvəl meɪ ˈkɛr / (AmE), / ˌdɛvəl meɪ ˈkɛə / (BrE)
showing a carefree, reckless, or unconcerned attitude toward risk or convention
carefree or recklessly unconcerned

Examples

With his devil-may-care grin, he tackled every challenge as if nothing could go wrong.
Her devil-may-care attitude made her popular at parties but sometimes got her into trouble at work.
They admired his devil-may-care style, refusing to play by anyone else's rules.

Synset Examples

a cocktail party given by some … raffish bachelors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carefree - free from worries or responsibility
  • Synonym: reckless - without regard for danger or consequences
  • Antonym: cautious - showing care and attention, especially to avoid danger

Etymology

First recorded in early 19th century; from the phrase 'the devil may care,' meaning one who acts without concern for the consequences.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00611718-s
Synset POS: s
