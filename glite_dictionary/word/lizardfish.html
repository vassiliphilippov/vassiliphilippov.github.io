lizardfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪzərdˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈlɪzədˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A tropical marine fish with a lizard-like head and a large mouth, found in warm seas worldwide.
tropical marine fish with a lizard-like head

Examples

The lizardfish rests on the sandy seabed, waiting to ambush passing prey.
Divers spotted a camouflaged lizardfish among the coral reefs.
Many lizardfish species are masters of disguise in their marine environments.

Etymology

From 'lizard' + 'fish', referring to the reptilian appearance of its head.
