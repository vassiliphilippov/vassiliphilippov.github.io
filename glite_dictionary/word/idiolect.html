idiolect

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪdiəˌlɛkt / (AmE), / ˈɪdɪəˌlɛkt / (BrE)
The language or speech pattern unique to an individual at a particular time.
an individual's unique speech or language

Examples

Every person's idiolect is shaped by their experiences and social environment.
Linguists may analyze a writer's idiolect to identify authorship of anonymous texts.
Her unique expressions and slang became part of her recognizable idiolect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dialect - the form of language spoken by people in a particular region or group

Etymology

From Greek idio- (own, personal) + -lect (from 'dialectos', dialect).
