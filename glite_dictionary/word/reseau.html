reseau

1. NOUN

/ reɪˈzoʊ / (AmE), / reɪˈzəʊ / (BrE)
a net or mesh that forms the foundation of lace
mesh foundation for lace

Examples

The delicate lace was supported by a fine reseau that gave it structure.
She admired the intricate patterns woven into the reseau of the antique shawl.
Mastering the art of creating a reseau is essential for traditional lace-makers.

Etymology

From French 'réseau' (net, network), from Latin 'retis' (net).

2. NOUN

/ reɪˈzoʊ / (AmE), / reɪˈzəʊ / (BrE)
A network of fine lines, usually on glass, used by astronomers as a reference grid for making precise measurements on photographic plates or images of stars.
network of reference lines used in astronomy

Examples

The astronomer overlaid a reseau on the photographic plate to improve measurement accuracy.
Specially designed reseaux are essential for mapping star positions precisely.
Before digital imaging, scientists used a glass reseau for calibrating film-based observations.

Etymology

From French 'réseau' (network, grid), from Old French 'resel' (net), from Latin 'rete' (net).
