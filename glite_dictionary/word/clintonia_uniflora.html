Clintonia uniflora

1. NOUN

/ klɪnˈtoʊniə ˌjunəˈflɔrə / (AmE), / klɪnˈtəʊniə ˌjuːnɪˈflɔːrə / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to western North America, bearing 1 or 2 white star-shaped flowers on short, leafless stalks.
mountain wildflower of western North America

Examples

Clintonia uniflora is often found in moist, shaded forests across the Pacific Northwest.
The delicate white flowers of Clintonia uniflora bloom in late spring at higher elevations.
Hikers in the mountains sometimes encounter Clintonia uniflora growing beside streams and under conifers.

Etymology

From the genus name Clintonia and Latin uniflora, meaning 'one-flowered'.
