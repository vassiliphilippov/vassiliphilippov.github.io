cymbid

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪmbɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɪmbɪd / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Cymbidium, known for its long drooping clusters of colorful, boat-shaped flowers, widely cultivated as houseplants and florists' flowers.
a type of orchid (Cymbidium genus)

Examples

She placed a pink cymbid in a glass vase on the windowsill.
Many florists favor the cymbid for its long-lasting and colorful blooms.
The greenhouse features several rare species of cymbids in full flower.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cymbidium - a genus of orchids with boat-shaped flowers

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Cymbidium', derived from Greek 'kymbídion' (a small boat), referring to the shape of the flower lip.
