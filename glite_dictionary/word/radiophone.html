radiophone

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪdiˌoʊfoʊn / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiəʊfəʊn / (BrE)
A telephone that transmits voice or data using radio waves instead of wires or cables.
telephone using radio waves

Examples

The emergency team communicated with headquarters using a radiophone during the operation.
Before cellphones, field workers relied heavily on the radiophone for remote communication.
He demonstrated how the radiophone could connect people across great distances without wires.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cellphone - a portable telephone that uses cellular network technology

Etymology

From radio + phone, combining 'radio' (wireless transmission) and 'telephone'.
