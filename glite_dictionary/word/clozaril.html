Clozaril

1. NOUN

/ ˈkloʊzəˌrɪl / (AmE), / ˈkləʊzərɪl / (BrE)
A trademarked antipsychotic drug (clozapine) used as a sedative and for treatment-resistant schizophrenia.
antipsychotic drug (trade name)
medical

Examples

The psychiatrist prescribed Clozaril to help control his schizophrenia symptoms.
Some patients respond well to Clozaril when other medications have failed.
Due to its possible side effects, regular blood tests are required for those taking Clozaril.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clozapine - the generic drug of which Clozaril is the trade name, used as an antipsychotic
