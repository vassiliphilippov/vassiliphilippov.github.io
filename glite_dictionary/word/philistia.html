Philistia

1. PROPN

/ fəˈlɪstiə / (AmE), / fɪˈlɪstiə / (BrE)
an ancient region on the southwestern coast of Palestine, inhabited by the Philistines and significant in biblical times
ancient region in southwestern Palestine

Examples

The cities of Philistia played a vital role in ancient trade routes.
Many stories in the Old Testament involve conflicts with Philistia.
Archaeologists have uncovered artifacts from Philistia dating back to the Iron Age.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Philistine - member of an ancient people of Philistia

Etymology

From Latin Philistia, from Hebrew Pĕlēšet (land of the Philistines).
