sloe

1. NOUN

/ sloʊ / (AmE), / sləʊ / (BrE)
a small, dark purple fruit from a wild plum tree (Prunus spinosa), typically found in Europe and parts of North America, known for its tart flavor
small, tart, dark purple fruit of a wild plum tree

Examples

Blackthorn bushes produce sloe berries, which are often used to make sloe gin.
The tartness of a fresh sloe makes it unsuitable for eating raw.
We gathered a basket of sloes during our walk through the countryside.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plum - the edible fruit of various trees of the genus Prunus

Etymology

From Old English *slāh*, related to Proto-Germanic *slaihō (sloe).

2. NOUN

/ sloʊ / (AmE), / sləʊ / (BrE)
a small, dark purple, sour fruit that grows on the blackthorn bush, often used in preserves and in making sloe gin
small sour dark purple fruit

Examples

The hedgerows were dotted with ripe sloes ready to be picked for gin making.
She bit into a sloe and made a face at its tartness.
In autumn, we gather sloes to make homemade jelly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plum - a soft, round fruit with sweet flesh and a pit, related to sloe

Etymology

From Old English *slāh*, related to Dutch *slee* and German *Schlehe*.

3. NOUN

/ sloʊ / (AmE), / sləʊ / (BrE)
a small thorny Eurasian shrub, Prunus spinosa, bearing small, sour, dark purple plumlike fruits known as sloes
thorny Eurasian shrub with sour fruits

Examples

Wild sloe bushes grow along the hedgerows in the English countryside.
The tart sloe berries are often used to make sloe gin.
In autumn, birds feast on the ripe sloe fruits.

Etymology

From Old English *slāh*, related to German *Schlehe*.
