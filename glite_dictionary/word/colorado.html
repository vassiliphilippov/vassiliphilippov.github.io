Colorado

1. PROPN

/ ˌkɑː.ləˈræd.oʊ / (AmE), / ˌkɒl.əˈræd.əʊ / (BrE)
A state in the west-central United States, located in the Rocky Mountains, known for its mountainous landscapes and outdoor recreation.
U.S. state in the Rocky Mountains

Examples

I visited Colorado last summer and went hiking in the Rocky Mountains.
The weather in Colorado can change very quickly, especially in the mountains.
Colorado is famous for its beautiful ski resorts and national parks.

Etymology

From Spanish 'colorado' meaning 'colored red', referring to the reddish color of parts of the region.
