monas

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊ.næs / (AmE), / ˈməʊ.næs / (BrE)
A singular metaphysical entity or unit, considered the ultimate source or foundation from which all material properties derive, especially in philosophical contexts.
singular metaphysical entity in philosophy

Examples

Some philosophers argue that the universe is ultimately composed of a single monas.
Leibniz's theory posits the existence of the monas as the basic substance of reality.
In metaphysical discourse, the monas serves as an indivisible origin of all things.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monad - a single unit or entity, especially in metaphysics or philosophy

Etymology

From Greek μονάς (monás, unit), from μόνος (mónos, alone, single).
