phaeton

1. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪ.tən / (AmE), / ˈfeɪ.ətən / (BrE)
a large, open automobile seating four people, often with a folding top, popular in the early 20th century
large open car with folding top

Examples

The museum displayed a beautifully restored phaeton from the 1920s.
Riding in a classic phaeton with the top down was an exhilarating experience.
He collects vintage cars, and his favorite is a red phaeton.

Etymology

From French *phaéton*, from Greek Phaethōn, the mythological son of Helios, referencing speed and recklessness.
