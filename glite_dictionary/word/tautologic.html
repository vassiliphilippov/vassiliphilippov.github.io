tautologic

1. ADJ

/ tɔːt.əˈlɑː.dʒɪk / (AmE), / tɔː.təˈlɒ.dʒɪk / (BrE)
expressing the same idea twice in different words; redundant or tautological.
redundant; involving needless repetition

Examples

His explanation was unnecessarily tautologic, restating the same point in several ways.
The report became tautologic when it referred to an 'unexpected surprise'.
Avoiding tautologic phrases can help make your writing clearer and more concise.

Synset Examples

‘a true fact’ and ‘a free gift’ are pleonastic expressions
the phrase ‘a beginner who has just started’ is tautological
at the risk of being redundant I return to my original proposition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tautological - repeating the same idea in different words; redundant

Etymology

From Greek tautologia (saying the same), via Medieval Latin tautologicus.
