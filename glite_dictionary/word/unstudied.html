unstudied

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈstʌd.id / (AmE), / ʌnˈstʌd.id / (BrE)
lacking knowledge or familiarity gained through study, often in a particular subject or field
lacking knowledge from study

Examples

He remains unstudied in the classics despite years at university.
Her opinions on astronomy are quite unstudied and lack depth.
The applicant appeared unstudied in recent technological developments.

Synset Examples

is unstudied in Latin as he is in may other matters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ignorant - lacking knowledge or awareness
  • Antonym: learned - having acquired much knowledge through study

Etymology

un- (not) + studied (past participle of 'study').

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈstʌdid / (AmE), / ʌnˈstʌdid / (BrE)
(of an action, appearance, etc.) not planned or rehearsed; natural and spontaneous.
not planned or rehearsed; spontaneous

Examples

Her unstudied remarks charmed everyone at the meeting.
He admired the unstudied elegance of her movements.
The photograph captured an unstudied moment between friends.

Synset Examples

an air of unstudied spontaneous utterance is apt to be painstakingly achieved
simple unstudied charm

Related Terms

  • Synonym: natural - existing in or caused by nature; not artificial
  • Synonym: spontaneous - performed or occurring as a result of a sudden impulse
  • Antonym: affected - artificially created or contrived

Etymology

From prefix un- (not) + studied (planned, considered).
