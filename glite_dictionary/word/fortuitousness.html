fortuitousness

1. NOUN

/ fɔːrˈtuːɪtəsnəs / (AmE), / fɔːˈtjuːɪtəsnəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of happening by accident and producing a fortunate result
quality of being lucky by chance

Examples

The fortuitousness of their meeting led to a lifelong friendship.
Her career success was partly due to the fortuitousness of being in the right place at the right time.
He marveled at the fortuitousness of discovering the lost manuscript on his first day at the library.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serendipity - the occurrence of events by chance in a happy or beneficial way
  • Synonym: luck - success or failure apparently brought by chance rather than through one's own actions
  • Antonym: misfortune - bad luck; an unfortunate condition or event

Etymology

From Latin fortuitus (by chance) + English suffix -ness.
