unadjusted

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.əˈdʒʌs.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.əˈdʒʌs.tɪd / (BrE)
not having adapted or changed to fit new or different conditions
not adapted to new conditions

Examples

The unadjusted students struggled to keep up with the fast pace of the new school.
After several months, some refugees remained unadjusted and found daily life challenging.
The company hired a counselor to help unadjusted employees adapt to the new management style.

Synset Examples

several unadjusted refugees

Related Terms

  • Antonym: adapted - having adjusted to new conditions

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'adjusted', from Latin 'ad-' (to) + 'iustus' (right, proper).

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.əˈdʒʌs.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.ədˈdʒʌs.tɪd / (BrE)
not changed or modified to account for specific requirements, differences, or influences
not changed to fit requirements

Examples

The report included both unadjusted and adjusted earnings figures for the year.
The data are unadjusted for seasonal variation, so comparisons may be misleading.
She realized the unadjusted chair was too low for her to work comfortably.

Synset Examples

an unadjusted figure of 8.5 percent
the unadjusted clock is running fast

Related Terms

  • Antonym: adjusted - changed to fit specific requirements or circumstances

Etymology

From un- (not) + adjusted (from adjust, ultimately from Latin adjustare)

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00352328-a
Synset POS: a
