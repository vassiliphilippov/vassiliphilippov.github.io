metalanguage

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛtəˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmɛtəˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A language or system of terms used to describe, analyze, or discuss another language.
language describing other languages

Examples

Linguists use metalanguage to clarify the structure and rules of grammar.
A dictionary often relies on metalanguage to explain word meanings.
Understanding metalanguage helps teachers describe how language works to their students.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: language - system of communication used by a community

Etymology

From meta- (beyond, about) + language, first used in linguistics in the 20th century.
