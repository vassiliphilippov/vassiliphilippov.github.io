gripes

1. NOUN

/ ɡraɪps / (AmE), / ɡraɪps / (BrE)
severe, cramp-like abdominal pain, especially in infants
acute abdominal pain in infants
archaic medical

Examples

The baby was crying constantly due to gripes after feeding.
Many old remedies were used to soothe infants suffering from gripes.
Doctors would often diagnose gripes when a child had persistent stomach pain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colic - severe, often fluctuating pain in the abdomen caused by intestinal gas or obstruction

Etymology

From Middle English 'grypen' (to seize or clutch), related to the sensation of being gripped by pain.
