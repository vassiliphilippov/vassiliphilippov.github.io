settlor

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛt.lɔr / (AmE), / ˈsɛt.lɔː / (BrE)
(law) a person who creates a trust by transferring property to a trustee for the benefit of a beneficiary.
person who creates a trust
legal

Examples

The settlor must have legal capacity to transfer property into the trust.
After the settlor died, the trustee managed the estate for the grandchildren.
Both the settlor and the trustee signed the trust deed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trustor - person who creates a trust (especially in American usage)
  • Antonym: trustee - person who administers a trust
  • Antonym: beneficiary - person who benefits from a trust

Etymology

From 'settle' + '-or', meaning one who settles property in trust.
