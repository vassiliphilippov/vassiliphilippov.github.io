lockstep

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑkˌstɛp / (AmE), / ˈlɒk.stɛp / (BrE)
a way of marching in which each person's step matches and follows exactly behind the step of the person ahead
marching in exact synchronization

Examples

The soldiers marched in lockstep during the parade, their movements perfectly synchronized.
Because of the chains, the prisoners shuffled forward in lockstep down the narrow hall.
The ceremony began with a procession of students walking in lockstep toward the stage.

Synset Examples

the prisoner's ankles were so chained together that they could only march in lockstep

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɑkˌstɛp / (AmE), / ˈlɒkˌstɛp / (BrE)
a way of doing something in which people act or think in exactly the same way, often without questioning it
rigid conformity or procedure

Examples

Many companies innovate instead of working in lockstep with industry traditions.
The two political parties often move in lockstep when passing budget bills.
Critics say that the board acted in lockstep, ignoring alternative proposals.

Synset Examples

the union's support had been in lockstep for years

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conformity - compliance with standards, rules, or laws
  • Antonym: independence - freedom from outside control; self-governance
  • Synonym: uniformity - the quality of being the same in all cases

Etymology

From the literal sense of marching in step, originally military term in the 19th century U.S.
