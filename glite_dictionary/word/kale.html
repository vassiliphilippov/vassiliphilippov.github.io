2. NOUN

/ keɪl / (AmE), / keɪl / (BrE)
a hardy cabbage with coarse, curly leaves that do not form a compact head, often grown as a vegetable
a leafy green vegetable (curly cabbage)

Examples

Fresh kale can be sautéed with garlic for a healthy side dish.
She added chopped kale to her morning smoothie for extra nutrition.
Farmers harvest kale in late autumn because it tolerates frost well.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collard - a type of leafy green similar to kale but with smooth leaves
  • Degree Higher: cabbage - a round-headed leafy vegetable related to kale

Etymology

From Middle English cale, from Old English cāwel, from Latin caulis (cabbage, stem).

3. NOUN

/ keɪl / (AmE), / keɪl / (BrE)
a dark green, coarse, curly-leafed cabbage, eaten as a vegetable
curly-leafed green cabbage

Examples

She made a healthy salad with kale, chickpeas, and lemon dressing.
Farmers often grow kale because it thrives in colder weather.
He added chopped kale to the soup for extra flavor and nutrition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cabbage - a round, green or purple vegetable with thick leaves

Etymology

From Middle English 'cāle', from Old English 'cāl', from Latin 'caulis' (stem, cabbage).
