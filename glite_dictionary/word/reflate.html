to reflate

1. VERB

/ riˈfleɪt / (AmE), / riˈfleɪt / (BrE)
to inflate something again; to cause something to become filled with air or gas after deflation
inflate again

Examples

After the tire went flat, the mechanic had to reflate it before we could continue driving.
Sarah used a pump to reflate the air mattress before the guests arrived.
If you accidentally let out too much air, it's easy to reflate the balloon.

Synset Examples

reflate the balloon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inflate - to fill with air or gas

Etymology

From re- (again) + inflate.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00265171-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ riˈfleɪt / (AmE), / riˈfleɪt / (BrE)
to become inflated again, especially referring to an economy or prices after a period of contraction or deflation
to become inflated again

Examples

Experts predict the economy will reflate as consumer confidence returns.
After a period of falling prices, the market began to reflate due to new government policies.
When demand increases, prices in the affected sectors may reflate quickly.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to deflate - to reduce or become reduced in price, value, or volume, especially economically

Etymology

From re- (again) + inflate, first used in economic contexts in the early 20th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00257169-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ riˈfleɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈfleɪt / (BrE)
to stimulate the economy by increasing demand, expanding the money supply, or raising prices after a period of deflation
stimulate economy after deflation

Examples

The government hopes that tax cuts will reflate the sluggish economy.
Central banks can reflate the economy by lowering interest rates.
Policymakers are debating how best to reflate demand after a long period of deflation.

Synset Examples

These measures reflated the economy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inflate - to increase prices or the amount of currency in circulation
  • Antonym: to deflate - to reduce the level of prices or economic activity

Etymology

From re- (again) + inflate, patterned after 'inflate' (mid 19th century, from Latin 'inflare').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00564177-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ riˈfleɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈfleɪt / (BrE)
to restore economic growth or price levels, especially after a period of deflation, typically by government or central bank action
restore economic activity after deflation

Examples

After a severe recession, the government intervened to reflate the economy.
Central banks sometimes lower interest rates to help reflate growth and prevent prolonged downturns.
Analysts predicted the market would reflate in response to the new fiscal policies.

Synset Examples

The economy reflated after the Fed took extreme measures

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inflate - to increase prices or economic activity, especially to an excessive degree
  • Antonym: to deflate - to reduce the level of prices or economic activity

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 're-' (again) and 'inflate', modeled on 'inflate/deflate' in economic usage.
