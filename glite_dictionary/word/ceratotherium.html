Ceratotherium

1. NOUN

/ sɛrəˌtoʊˈθɪriəm / (AmE), / sɛrəˌtəʊˈθɪəriəm / (BrE)
a genus of African rhinoceroses, including the white rhinoceros
genus of African rhinoceroses

Examples

The Ceratotherium genus is best known for the white rhinoceros.
Paleontologists discovered fossils belonging to the Ceratotherium in East Africa.
Conservation efforts have focused on protecting all species within Ceratotherium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white rhinoceros - large African rhinoceros, member of the genus Ceratotherium

Etymology

From Greek keras (horn) + therion (beast).

2. NOUN

/ sɛˌrætəˈθɪriəm / (AmE), / sɛˌrætəˈθɪəriəm / (BrE)
A genus of African rhinoceroses, including the white rhinoceros.
genus of African rhinoceroses

Examples

The white rhinoceros belongs to the genus Ceratotherium.
Researchers observed several members of Ceratotherium in the savannah.
Conservation efforts have focused particularly on Ceratotherium species facing habitat loss.

Etymology

From Greek 'keras' (horn) + 'therion' (beast), referring to the horned animal.
