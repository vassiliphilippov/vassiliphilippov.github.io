Ferdinand I

1. PROPN

/ ˌfɜrdɪˌnænd ðə ˈfɜrst / (AmE), / ˌfɜːdɪˌnænd ði ˈfɜːst / (BrE)
(1503–1564) Holy Roman Emperor and king of Hungary and Bohemia; a major European monarch of the 16th century.
Holy Roman Emperor (1503–1564)

Examples

Ferdinand I was crowned Holy Roman Emperor in 1558 after the abdication of his brother Charles V.
Historians credit Ferdinand I with shaping the Habsburg monarchy's future in Central Europe.
The Treaty of Augsburg in 1555 was signed during the reign of Ferdinand I.
