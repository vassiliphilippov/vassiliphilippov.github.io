scrutiniser

1. NOUN

/ skruːˈtɪnəɪzər / (AmE), / skruːˈtɪnʌɪzə / (BrE)
a person who examines or inspects something with great care and attention to detail
a careful examiner or inspector
british

Examples

The election scrutiniser monitored the counting process to ensure fairness.
Every detail was noted by the scrutiniser during the inspection.
As a financial scrutiniser, her reports were always thorough and precise.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scrutinizer - a person who examines carefully (American spelling)

Etymology

From 'scrutinise' (British spelling), ultimately from Latin 'scrutari' (to search, examine).
