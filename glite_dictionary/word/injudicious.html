injudicious

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪn.dʒuˈdɪʃ.əs / (AmE), / ˌɪn.dʒuˈdɪʃ.əs / (BrE)
lacking judgment or discretion; unwise
unwise; lacking good judgment

Examples

It would be injudicious to invest so much money without proper research.
Her injudicious remarks caused unnecessary tension in the meeting.
He later regretted his injudicious choice of words.

Synset Examples

an injudicious measure
the result of an injudicious decision

Related Terms

  • Antonym: judicious - having or showing good judgment; wise
  • Synonym: unwise - not wise; lacking in judgment or discretion

Etymology

From Latin 'in-' (not) + 'judicium' (judgment).
