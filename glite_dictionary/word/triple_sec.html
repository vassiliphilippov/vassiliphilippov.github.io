triple sec

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪpəl sɛk / (AmE), / ˈtrɪp.əl sɛk / (BrE)
a strong, clear, orange-flavored liqueur of French origin, often used in cocktails
orange-flavored liqueur

Examples

The bartender added a splash of triple sec to the cocktail for extra flavor.
Classic margarita recipes usually call for tequila, lime juice, and triple sec.
She likes her cosmopolitans slightly sweeter, so she adds a little more triple sec.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curaçao - orange-flavored liqueur from the island of Curaçao

Etymology

French, literally meaning 'triple dry', from 'triple' (triple) and 'sec' (dry).
