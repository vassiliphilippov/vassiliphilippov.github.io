skylark

1. NOUN

/ ˈskaɪˌlɑrk / (AmE), / ˈskaɪˌlɑːk / (BrE)
a small brown-speckled European songbird (Alauda arvensis) known for singing while hovering high in the sky
a European songbird known for high hovering song

Examples

We watched a skylark rise above the fields, filling the morning with its song.
The skylark's cheerful melody is often heard on summer days in the countryside.
Poets have long been inspired by the skylark and its soaring music.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lark - a small songbird of the family Alaudidae

Etymology

From 'sky' + 'lark', describing the bird's habit of singing high in the air.

2. VERB

/ ˈskaɪˌlɑrk / (AmE), / ˈskaɪˌlɑːk / (BrE)
to frolic or play boisterously, generally in a lively and carefree way
frolic or play boisterously

Examples

The children skylarked around the playground, laughing and shouting with joy.
We watched the puppies skylarking in the grassy field under the summer sun.
Instead of working, the sailors spent the afternoon skylarking on deck.

Synset Examples

The children frolicked in the garden
the gamboling lambs in the meadows
The toddlers romped in the playroom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frolic - to play and move about cheerfully, excitedly, or energetically
  • Synonym: romp - to play roughly and energetically

Etymology

From the noun 'skylark' (the songbird), referencing the bird's exuberant flight and song.
