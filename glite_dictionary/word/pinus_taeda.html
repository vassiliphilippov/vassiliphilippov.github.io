Pinus taeda

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪnəs ˈtiːdə / (AmE), / ˈpʌɪnəs ˈtiːdə / (BrE)
a tall, spreading pine tree of the southeastern United States, with reddish-brown fissured bark and bushy foliage, commonly called loblolly pine
species of pine tree (loblolly pine)

Examples

The timber industry relies heavily on Pinus taeda for its fast growth and quality wood.
In southeastern forests, Pinus taeda is often the most common pine species.
Researchers study Pinus taeda to understand how forests respond to climate change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: loblolly pine - common name for Pinus taeda, a pine species of the US southeast

Etymology

From Latin: Pinus (pine) + taeda (pine torch or pine wood, referring to its resinous wood).
