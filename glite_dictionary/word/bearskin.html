bearskin

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛrˌskɪn / (AmE), / ˈbeəˌskɪn / (BrE)
a tall fur hat, traditionally made of bearskin, worn as part of the ceremonial uniform by certain British soldiers, such as the Guards regiments
tall ceremonial fur hat worn by British soldiers

Examples

The Queen's Guard stood motionless in their impressive bearskins outside Buckingham Palace.
During the parade, the soldiers marched in perfect formation wearing their traditional red tunics and bearskins.
The tall bearskin made the ceremonial uniform even more imposing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: busby - a tall military fur hat, similar in style to the bearskin, but usually smaller

Etymology

From bear + skin, originally referring to the material used to make the hat.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɛrˌskɪn / (AmE), / ˈbɛəˌskɪn / (BrE)
the skin and fur of a bear, especially when used as a material
pelt of a bear

Examples

The hunter proudly displayed the bearskin he had brought back from his trip.
A thick bearskin rug covered the cabin floor, adding both warmth and decoration.
Early settlers often used bearskin to make coats and hats for winter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pelt - the skin of an animal with the hair, wool, or fur still on it
