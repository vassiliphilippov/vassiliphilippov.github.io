Canis anthus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkeɪnɪs ˈænθəs / (AmE), / ˈkeɪnɪs ˈænθəs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the African golden wolf, a canid native to the deserts and savannas of Northern Africa, closely related to the golden jackal and the grey wolf
African golden wolf (canid species)

Examples

Recent genetic studies have revealed that Canis anthus is a separate species from the golden jackal.
The deserts and savannas of Africa are home to Canis anthus.
Researchers are studying the diet and behavior of Canis anthus to better understand its role in the ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golden jackal - a closely related canid species found in Eurasia
  • Synonym: grey wolf - a related larger canid species, Canis lupus

Etymology

From Latin: 'Canis' meaning 'dog', 'anthus' from Greek 'anthos' (flower), possibly referencing color.
