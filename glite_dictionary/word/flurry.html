flurry

1. NOUN

/ ˈflɝːi / (AmE), / ˈflʌri / (BrE)
a sudden burst of activity or commotion
sudden activity or commotion

Examples

A sudden flurry of emails kept the office busy all morning.
The announcement caused a flurry of excitement among the students.
There was a flurry of activity as the guests began to arrive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bustle - busy or energetic activity
  • Antonym: calm - the absence of disturbance or noise

Etymology

Probably from Old French 'florée' (a sudden outburst), related to 'fleurir' (to blossom).

2. NOUN

/ ˈflɝː.i / (AmE), / ˈflʌr.i / (BrE)
A small, sudden burst of activity, wind, or snowfall, often brief and light.
sudden burst of wind, snow, or activity

Examples

A sudden flurry of snow made the streets look white within minutes.
There was a flurry of excitement when the famous actor entered the room.
The chickens scattered in a flurry of feathers.

Synset Examples

he had to close the window against the flurries
there was a flurry of chicken feathers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gust - a sudden strong rush of wind
  • Antonym: calm - a period of no wind or activity

3. VERB

/ ˈflɜri / (AmE), / ˈflʌri / (BrE)
to move in an agitated, hurried, or confused manner
move in an agitated or confused way

Examples

The secretary began to flurry about the office as the deadline approached.
He flurried through his notes, trying to find the right page.
She was flurrying from room to room, looking for her misplaced keys.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hurry - to move or act with haste
  • Synonym: rush - to move with urgent haste
