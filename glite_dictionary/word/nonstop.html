nonstop

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑnˌstɑp / (AmE), / ˈnɒnˌstɒp / (BrE)
a flight or journey made without any stops between the starting point and the final destination
direct flight with no stops

Examples

I prefer to book a nonstop whenever I fly cross-country.
The airline added two new nonstops to Florida this season.
Are there any nonstops available to San Francisco tomorrow?

Synset Examples

how many nonstops are there to Dallas?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: direct - a flight that goes straight to its destination, sometimes with stops but no change of planes
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00302827-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ nɑnˈstɑp / (AmE), / nɒnˈstɒp / (BrE)
(of a journey, especially a flight) occurring without any stops or interruptions along the way.
without intermediate stops

Examples

We booked a nonstop flight from New York to Los Angeles.
Their train journey was nonstop, saving them hours of travel time.
The athlete completed the marathon in a nonstop run without resting.

Synset Examples

a nonstop flight to Atlanta

Related Terms

  • Synonym: direct - (of a train, bus, or flight) going to a destination without breaking the journey
  • Antonym: connecting - (of flights or trains) requiring a change or stop before reaching the final destination
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00764992-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADV

/ ˈnɑnˌstɑp / (AmE), / ˈnɒnˌstɒp / (BrE)
without stopping; continuously or without interruption, especially relating to travel routes.
without stopping or interruption

Examples

The flight from San Francisco to Paris goes nonstop, which saves travelers several hours.
Buses now run nonstop between the two cities during rush hour.
They drove nonstop through the night to reach their destination by morning.

Synset Examples

we are flying nonstop from New York to Tokyo

Related Terms

  • Synonym: direct - (of a journey) with no intermediate stops or changes

4. ADJ

/ ˈnɑnˌstɑp / (AmE), / ˈnɒnˌstɒp / (BrE)
(of an activity or action) continuing without interruption or pause; unceasing.
continuing without stopping

Examples

The nonstop rain made it difficult to go outside all week.
She works at a nonstop pace to finish all her assignments on time.
The hospital provides nonstop care for its patients, day and night.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
