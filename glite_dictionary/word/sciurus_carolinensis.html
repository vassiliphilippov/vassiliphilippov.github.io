Sciurus carolinensis

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈjʊərəs ˌkæ.rə.lɪˈnɛn.sɪs / (AmE), / sɪˈjʊərəs ˌkæ.rə.lɪˈnɛn.sɪs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the Eastern gray squirrel, a common squirrel native to eastern North America and introduced to other regions, such as England
scientific name for the Eastern gray squirrel

Examples

The Sciurus carolinensis has adapted well to urban environments.
Researchers studied the population growth of Sciurus carolinensis after its introduction to England.
Sciurus carolinensis often outcompetes native squirrel species for resources.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eastern gray squirrel - common name for Sciurus carolinensis, a medium-large gray squirrel species

Etymology

From Latin: 'Sciurus' (squirrel) and 'carolinensis' (of Carolina, referring to the Carolinas in the United States).
