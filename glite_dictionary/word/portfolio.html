portfolio

1. NOUN

/ pɔrˈtfoʊlioʊ / (AmE), / pɔːtˈfəʊliəʊ / (BrE)
a large, flat, thin case for carrying loose papers, drawings, or maps, often made of leather
case for carrying papers or drawings

Examples

The artist carried her sketches in a black portfolio to the gallery.
He bought a leather portfolio to keep his important documents organized.
After the meeting, she slipped the contracts carefully back into her portfolio.

Synset Examples

he remembered her because she was carrying a large portfolio

Etymology

From Italian 'portafoglio', from 'portare' (to carry) + 'foglio' (sheet, leaf).

2. NOUN

/ pɔrˈtfoʊlioʊ / (AmE), / pɔːtˈfəʊliəʊ / (BrE)
a collection of financial assets, such as stocks and bonds, held by an individual, organization, or institution
list of financial assets held

Examples

She diversified her portfolio to reduce the risk of heavy losses.
A balanced portfolio typically includes stocks, bonds, and real estate.
The fund manager reviews each client's portfolio regularly to adjust their investments.

Synset Examples

they were disappointed by the poor returns on their stock portfolio

Related Terms

  • Synonym: investment - the action or process of investing money for profit
  • Synonym: asset - an item of value owned

Etymology

From Italian 'portafoglio', from 'portare' (to carry) + 'foglio' (sheet, leaf).

4. NOUN

/ pɔrˈtfoʊlioʊ / (AmE), / pɔːtˈfəʊliəʊ / (BrE)
A collection of examples of a person's creative work, compiled to demonstrate their ability and shown to potential employers or clients.
collection of creative works for display

Examples

She spent weeks assembling her portfolio before applying to art school.
The designer's portfolio impressed the interviewers with its variety and creativity.
Photographers often update their portfolio to showcase their latest projects.

Synset Examples

the artist had put together a portfolio of his work
every actor has a portfolio of photographs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: work sample - an individual example of someone's work given for evaluation
  • Degree Higher: portfolio career - a career constructed from multiple jobs or projects, rather than a single full-time role

Etymology

From Italian 'portafoglio', from 'portare' (to carry) and 'foglio' (sheet, leaf).
