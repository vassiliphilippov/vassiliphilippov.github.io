Christopher Marlowe

1. PROPN

/ ˌkrɪstəfɚ ˈmɑrloʊ / (AmE), / ˌkrɪstəfə ˈmɑːləʊ / (BrE)
(1564–1593) An English poet and playwright noted for introducing blank verse to English drama, and known for works such as "Doctor Faustus."
English poet and playwright (1564–1593)

Examples

Christopher Marlowe was a pioneering figure in Elizabethan drama, influencing generations of playwrights.
Many scholars debate whether Christopher Marlowe's works had an impact on William Shakespeare's writing.
The mysterious death of Christopher Marlowe has been the subject of much speculation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: William Shakespeare - English playwright and poet, contemporary of Marlowe
