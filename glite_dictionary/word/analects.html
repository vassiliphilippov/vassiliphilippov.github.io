analects

1. NOUN

/ ˈæn.əˌlɛkts / (AmE), / ˈæn.ə.lɛkts / (BrE)
a collection of selected passages or excerpts from a literary work or works
selected passages from a literary work

Examples

The Confucian analects have been studied for centuries by scholars around the world.
A new edition of Shakespeare's analects was released by the university press.
Her thesis focused on the philosophical themes present in ancient Greek analects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anthology - a published collection of written works, often poems or stories

Etymology

From Greek 'analekta' (selected things), from 'analegein' (to gather up).
