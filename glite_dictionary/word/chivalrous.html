chivalrous

1. ADJ

/ ˈʃɪvəlrəs / (AmE), / ˈʃɪvəlrəs / (BrE)
(of a man) courteous and attentive to women, showing the qualities of an ideal knight.
courteous and attentive, esp. to women

Examples

He held the door open in a chivalrous gesture that made her smile.
It was chivalrous of him to offer his seat to the elderly lady.
Many see his old-fashioned manners as chivalrous rather than outdated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gallant - showing special attention or courtesy to women
  • Degree Lower: polite - having or showing good manners and respect

Etymology

From Old French 'chevalerous', from 'chevalier' (knight), from Late Latin 'caballarius' (horseman).
