paramagnetic

1. ADJ

/ ˌpær.ə.mæɡˈnɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌpar.ə.maɡˈnɛt.ɪk / (BrE)
(of a material or substance) having the property of being attracted by an externally applied magnetic field, due to the presence of unpaired electrons.
attracted to a magnetic field (physics, chemistry)

Examples

Oxygen is a paramagnetic substance, meaning it is attracted by magnetic fields.
Research has shown that certain salts exhibit paramagnetic behavior at room temperature.
The paramagnetic properties of the sample were measured using a sensitive magnetometer.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: diamagnetic - not attracted to a magnetic field; repelled slightly instead
  • Degree Higher: ferromagnetic - having strong attraction to a magnetic field, like in iron

Etymology

From Greek 'para-' (beside) and 'magnet', referring to its relation to magnetism.
