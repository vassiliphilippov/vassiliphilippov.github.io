Senate

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnət / (AmE), / ˈsɛnət / (BrE)
the upper chamber of the United States Congress, consisting of elected representatives from each state
upper house of the U.S. Congress

Examples

The Senate passed the bill after a lengthy debate.
She was elected to serve in the United States Senate.
The power to confirm presidential appointments rests with the Senate.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: House of Representatives - the lower chamber of the United States Congress

Etymology

From Latin 'senatus', meaning 'council of elders', from 'senex', meaning 'old man'.
