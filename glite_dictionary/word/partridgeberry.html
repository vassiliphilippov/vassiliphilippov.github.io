partridgeberry

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːrtrɪdʒˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈpɑːtrɪdʒˌbɛri / (BrE)
a small, creeping woody plant native to eastern North America, with glossy evergreen leaves and scarlet berries
creeping plant with scarlet berries

Examples

The partridgeberry grows close to the ground in shady forests across eastern North America.
Bright red partridgeberries dotted the forest floor amid the glossy green leaves.
Some people use partridgeberry plants as decorative ground cover in shaded gardens.

Etymology

From 'partridge' (the bird) and 'berry', named for the plant's bright berries which are sometimes eaten by partridges.
