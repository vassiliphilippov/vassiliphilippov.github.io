to hyperventilate

1. VERB

/ ˌhaɪpərˈvɛntɪˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəˈvɛntɪleɪt / (BrE)
to cause someone to breathe abnormally fast or deeply, typically in a medical context
cause abnormal rapid breathing (someone else)
medical

Examples

The nurse had to hyperventilate the patient during the procedure.
Doctors may hyperventilate a patient to help expel excess carbon dioxide.
Under certain conditions, medical staff might intentionally hyperventilate someone to control their blood gases.

Synset Examples

The nurses had to hyperventilate the patient

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ventilate - to provide with fresh air or to cause air to circulate

Etymology

From hyper- (over) + ventilate, after medical terminology for rapid breathing.

2. VERB

/ ˌhaɪpərˈvɛntɪleɪt / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəˈvɛntɪleɪt / (BrE)
to breathe abnormally fast and deeply, often leading to a reduction of carbon dioxide in the blood
breathe extremely fast and deeply
medical

Examples

He began to hyperventilate when he saw the crowd gathering.
If you hyperventilate, try to slow your breathing and stay calm.
During a panic attack, it is common for people to hyperventilate.

Synset Examples

The mountain climber started to hyperventilate

Etymology

From Greek 'hyper-' (above, beyond) + 'ventilate' (to aerate or expose to air).
