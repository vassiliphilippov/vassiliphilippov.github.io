Doodia

1. NOUN

/ ˈduːdiə / (AmE), / ˈduːdiə / (BrE)
A genus of small, colony-forming terrestrial ferns native to Australasia, sometimes classified in the family Polypodiaceae.
genus of Australasian ferns

Examples

The forest floor was covered in lush Doodia ferns, spreading in vibrant, green colonies.
Several species of Doodia thrive in the damp, shaded areas of eastern Australia.
Gardeners often cultivate Doodia for its attractive foliage and adaptability.

2. NOUN

/ ˈduːdiə / (AmE), / ˈduːdiə / (BrE)
A genus of small, colony-forming terrestrial ferns native to Australasia, sometimes classified in the family Polypodiaceae.
genus of Australasian ferns

Examples

The Doodia is commonly found in Australian forests, thriving in damp and shaded environments.
Botanists recently discovered a new species within the genus Doodia during their expedition in New Zealand.
Gardeners often include doodias in shaded rock gardens for their unique foliage.

Etymology

Named after Samuel Doody, an 18th-century English botanist.
