deputy

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛp.ju.t̬i / (AmE), / ˈdɛp.ju.ti / (BrE)
a person who is appointed to represent or act on behalf of others, especially one who assists or substitutes for a superior.
person acting as an assistant or representative

Examples

She served as the deputy during his absence and managed the office efficiently.
The mayor appointed a new deputy to help oversee city operations.
As a deputy, he had the authority to make decisions when his superior was unavailable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: representative - someone who acts or speaks for others
  • Antonym: subordinate - a person under the authority of another; antonym in terms of status, not exactly role

Etymology

From Old French 'deputé', past participle of 'deputer' (to assign, appoint), from Latin 'deputare' (to assign, allot).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɛpjəti / (AmE), / ˈdepjʊti / (BrE)
a person authorized to act as a substitute or assistant for someone in a position of authority, especially a law officer such as a sheriff
assistant or substitute, especially for a sheriff

Examples

The deputy responded to the emergency call when the sheriff was unavailable.
Serving as a deputy, he upheld the law in the absence of the sheriff.
When the town faced a crisis, the deputy quickly took charge.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: sheriff - an elected or appointed official in charge of law enforcement in a county

Etymology

From Old French 'deputé' (appointed, assigned), from Latin 'deputatus' (appointed).

3. NOUN

/ ˈdɛpjəti / (AmE), / ˈdɛpjʊti / (BrE)
a member of the lower house of the legislative assembly in certain countries, especially France
member of lower house (legislature, esp. France)

Examples

The French deputy addressed the National Assembly during a heated debate.
Each deputy is elected to represent a specific constituency in the lower house.
Several deputies proposed amendments to the new education bill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: member of parliament - an elected member of a parliament (general legislative body)
  • Antonym: senator - a member of a senate, usually the upper chamber in a bicameral legislature

Etymology

From Old French 'deputé', past participle of 'deputer' (to appoint), from Latin 'deputare' (to assign, allot).

4. NOUN

/ ˈdɛpjəti / (AmE), / ˈdɛpjʊti / (BrE)
A person appointed as an assistant with the authority to act on behalf of their superior, especially when the superior is absent.
assistant with authority to act in place of superior

Examples

The deputy signed the documents while the manager was away on business.
If the sheriff is unavailable, the deputy steps in to maintain order.
She was promoted to deputy director after years of dedicated service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assistant - a person who helps someone in a job or task
  • Synonym: subordinate - a person under the authority of another within an organization
  • Antonym: chief - a leader or person with the highest rank

Etymology

From Middle French 'député', past participle of 'députer' (to appoint), from Latin 'deputare' (to assign).
