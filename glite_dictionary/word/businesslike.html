businesslike

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɪznɪsˌlaɪk / (AmE), / ˈbɪznɪsˌlaɪk / (BrE)
showing efficiency, professionalism, and a systematic or practical approach, especially appropriate for business or work settings.
efficient and professional in manner

Examples

Our team adopted a businesslike approach to solving the problem.
She answered the questions in a calm and businesslike manner.
Despite the stressful situation, he remained efficient and businesslike throughout the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: efficient - working in a well-organized and competent way
  • Antonym: casual - relaxed and unconcerned; not formal or methodical

Etymology

Formed from 'business' + 'like', meaning 'in the manner of business'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbɪznɪsˌlaɪk / (AmE), / ˈbɪznɪsˌlaɪk / (BrE)
efficient, practical, and focused on the task or goals without distractions or unnecessary emotion.
efficient and goal-focused

Examples

She gave a businesslike presentation that impressed everyone with its clarity and focus.
His tone was businesslike, making it clear he wanted to get straight to the point.
Despite the chaos around her, Caroline remained calm and businesslike throughout the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: efficient - working in a well-organized and productive way
  • Antonym: unfocused - not concentrating or paying attention to the main objective

Etymology

From 'business' + 'like', meaning 'in the manner of business'.
