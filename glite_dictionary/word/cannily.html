cannily

1. ADV

/ ˈkænɪli / (AmE), / ˈkanɪli / (BrE)
in a shrewd or clever manner; with foresight or prudence.
in a clever or shrewd manner

Examples

She handled the negotiations cannily, securing the best deal for her company.
The thief cannily avoided all the cameras as he moved through the gallery.
He invested his money cannily, choosing stocks that steadily grew over time.

Synset Examples

more presciently than they superiors, these workers grasped the economic situation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canny - having or showing shrewdness and good judgment, especially in practical matters

Etymology

From 'canny' + '-ly'. 'Canny' is from Scots, possibly from Old English 'cunnan' (to know).
