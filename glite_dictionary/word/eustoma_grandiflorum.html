Eustoma grandiflorum

1. NOUN

/ juˈstoʊmə ɡrændɪˈflɔrəm / (AmE), / juːˈstɒmə ɡrændɪˈflɔːrəm / (BrE)
A species of prairie wildflower native to North America, noted for its large, upright, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers, commonly found in moist grasslands.
prairie wildflower with showy blue flowers

Examples

The florist arranged Eustoma grandiflorum with roses to create a stunning bouquet.
Eustoma grandiflorum thrives in moist prairies from Colorado to Texas.
Gardeners prize Eustoma grandiflorum for its elegant, bell-shaped flowers.

Etymology

From Greek 'eu' (good) + 'stoma' (mouth), referring to the flower's shape. Species name from Latin meaning 'large-flowered.'
