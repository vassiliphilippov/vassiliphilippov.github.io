oxaloacetate

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑksəˌloʊˈæsɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˌɒksələʊˈæsɪteɪt / (BrE)
a salt or ester of oxaloacetic acid, commonly found as an intermediate in metabolic pathways such as the citric acid cycle
a salt or ester of oxalacetic acid
medical

Examples

Oxaloacetate plays a crucial role in the citric acid cycle during cellular respiration.
The conversion of malate to oxaloacetate occurs in the mitochondria.
Researchers measured the concentration of oxaloacetate in muscle tissue samples.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oxaloacetic acid - a dicarboxylic acid; key metabolic intermediate

Etymology

Formed from 'oxaloacetic', deriving from 'oxalic acid' and 'acetic acid' plus '-ate' as a salt/ester suffix.
