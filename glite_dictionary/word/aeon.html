aeon

1. NOUN

/ ˈiː.ɑn / (AmE), / ˈiː.ɒn / (BrE)
The longest division of geological time, comprising several eras.
the longest geological time division

Examples

Geologists believe the Earth formed during the Hadean aeon.
The Precambrian aeon accounts for most of Earth's history.
Each aeon is further subdivided into smaller units such as eras and periods.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: epoch - a division of geological time smaller than an era
  • Degree Lower: era - a major division of geological time, smaller than an aeon

Etymology

From Greek aiōn (age, vital force, eternity).

2. NOUN

/ ˈiːˌɑn, ˈeɪˌɑn / (AmE), / ˈiːən, ˈiːɒn / (BrE)
(In Gnosticism) A divine being or power emanating from the Supreme Being, each playing a role in the structure and operation of the universe.
divine being or power in Gnosticism

Examples

In Gnostic belief, each aeon represents a stage in the process of universal creation.
According to the text, the last aeon was responsible for shaping the material world.
Scholars often debate the number and hierarchy of aeons within different Gnostic sects.

Etymology

From Greek aiōn (age, vital force, eternity), adopted into Latin as aeon.

3. NOUN

/ ˈiː.ɑn / (AmE), / ˈiː.ɒn / (BrE)
An immeasurably or indefinitely long period of time; an age.
an immeasurably long period of time

Examples

It seemed like an aeon had passed before the results were announced.
Some believe the universe has existed for countless aeons.
The ancient ruins have stood silent for aeons, shrouded in mystery.

Synset Examples

oh, that happened eons ago

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epoch - a distinctive period in history or a person's life; era
  • Synonym: era - a long and distinct period of history
  • Antonym: moment - a very brief period of time

Etymology

From Greek aiōn (age, eternity), Latin aevum (age).
