esurience

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈsjʊriəns / (AmE), / ɪˈsjʊəriəns / (BrE)
An excessive or insatiable desire to eat; extreme hunger or greed for food.
excessive desire to eat

Examples

His esurience at the banquet was noticeable to everyone present.
The novel described the villain's esurience for extravagant meals in vivid detail.
Driven by esurience, she ordered nearly everything on the menu.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gluttony - habitual greed or excess in eating
  • Degree Lower: hunger - the feeling of needing or wanting to eat

Etymology

From Latin 'esurientia' (hunger), from 'esurire' (to be hungry).

2. NOUN

/ iˈsjʊriəns / (AmE), / ɪˈsjʊəriəns / (BrE)
an intense hunger or greed, especially for food
extreme hunger or greed for food

Examples

The esurience of the guests was evident as they devoured every dish on the table.
His esurience was not limited to food; he was greedy for wealth as well.
A sense of esurience gripped him after days without a proper meal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gluttony - habitual greed or excess in eating
  • Degree Higher: starvation - suffering or death caused by lack of food
  • Degree Lower: hunger - the feeling of needing or wanting food

Etymology

From Latin esurientia (hunger), from esurire (to be hungry).
