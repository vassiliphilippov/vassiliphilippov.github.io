acorea

1. NOUN

/ əˈkɔriə / (AmE), / əˈkɔːriə / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by the absence of the pupil in the eye.
absence of the pupil in the eye
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the child with acorea after observing the complete absence of a visible pupil.
Cases of acorea are extremely rare but require specialized ophthalmological care.
Because of her congenital acorea, she experienced significant vision problems from birth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corectopia - displacement of the pupil from its normal position
  • Synonym: aniridia - absence of the iris in the eye

Etymology

From Greek a- (not, without) + kore (pupil of the eye).
