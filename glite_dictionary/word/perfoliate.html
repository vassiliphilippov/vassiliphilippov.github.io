perfoliate

1. ADJ

/ pərˈfoʊliət / (AmE), / pəˈfəʊlieɪt / (BrE)
(of a leaf) having the base with edges united around and apparently pierced by the stem so that the stem appears to pass through the leaf.
(leaf) base united around stem
medical

Examples

The plant is easily identified by its perfoliate leaves, where the stem seems to pass directly through each blade.
Botanists often refer to this structure as perfoliate when describing certain wildflowers.
Unlike most leaves, the perfoliate leaves embrace the stem completely.

Etymology

From Latin perfoliatus, from per- (through) + folium (leaf).
