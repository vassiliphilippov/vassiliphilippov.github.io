Geococcyx

1. NOUN

/ dʒiː.əˈkɑk.sɪks / (AmE), / dʒiː.əˈkɒk.sɪks / (BrE)
a genus of ground-dwelling birds in the cuckoo family, commonly known as roadrunners, native to the southwestern United States and Central America
genus of birds called roadrunners

Examples

The Geococcyx is well known for its remarkable speed on the ground.
Several species within the Geococcyx genus are found in arid regions.
The cartoon character Road Runner is inspired by the real-life Geococcyx.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roadrunner - a fast-running bird, especially of North America, of the genus Geococcyx

Etymology

From Greek 'gē' meaning 'earth' and 'kokkyx' meaning 'cuckoo'.

2. NOUN

/ dʒiːoʊˈkɑksiːks / (AmE), / dʒiːəˈkɒksiːks / (BrE)
a genus of birds in the cuckoo family, known commonly as roadrunners, native to the deserts of North and Central America
genus of birds known as roadrunners

Examples

The Geococcyx is famous for its remarkable running speed across the desert sands.
Scientists observed a Geococcyx building its nest in a cactus.
Members of the genus Geococcyx are well adapted to arid environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roadrunner - a fast-running bird of the Geococcyx genus, native to North America

Etymology

From Greek 'geō-' (earth) + 'kokkyx' (cuckoo), referencing the bird's terrestrial habits.
