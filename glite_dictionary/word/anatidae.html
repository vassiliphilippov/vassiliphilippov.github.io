Anatidae

1. NOUN

/ əˈnætɪˌdeɪ / (AmE), / əˈnætɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of swimming birds with heavy, short-legged bodies and bills with a horny tip, including swans, geese, and ducks
family of swimming birds: swans, geese, ducks

Examples

The Anatidae family encompasses swans, geese, and a variety of duck species found worldwide.
Scientists study the migration patterns of birds within the Anatidae to better understand environmental changes.
Unlike other birds, members of the Anatidae are specially adapted for swimming and feeding in aquatic habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duck - a waterbird with a broad blunt bill, short legs, and webbed feet
  • Synonym: goose - a large waterbird with a long neck and webbed feet
  • Synonym: swan - a large waterbird with a long slender neck and usually white plumage

Etymology

From New Latin Anatidae, based on Latin anas (duck) + -idae (a zoological family suffix).
