Orcinus

1. NOUN

/ ɔrˈsaɪnəs / (AmE), / ɔːˈsaɪnəs / (BrE)
A genus of marine mammals containing the killer whale (Orcinus orca).
genus of the killer whale

Examples

The killer whale is scientifically classified under the genus Orcinus.
Marine biologists studied the behavior of Orcinus orca in their natural habitat.
There is only one living species currently recognized in the genus Orcinus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orca - a large, black-and-white predatory whale; killer whale

Etymology

From Latin 'orcīnus', meaning 'of or belonging to an orca'.
