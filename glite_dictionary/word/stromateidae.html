Stromateidae

1. NOUN

/ stroʊˌmæt.iˈidaɪ / (AmE), / strəʊˌmat.iˈiːdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, deep-bodied marine fishes, commonly known as butterfishes or dollar fishes, found in warm and temperate seas.
family of marine fishes (butterfishes)

Examples

Several species in the Stromateidae family are widely consumed as food in coastal regions.
The Stromateidae are known for their compressed, silvery bodies and delicate flavor.
Researchers discovered a new member of the Stromateidae off the coast of South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butterfish - common name for several fishes in the Stromateidae family

Etymology

From New Latin Stromateidae, based on the genus Stromateus, from Greek strōmat-, strōma (bed, covering).

2. NOUN

/ stroʊˌmætiˈaɪdi / (AmE), / strəʊˌmætiˈiːdiː / (BrE)
a family of marine fishes known as butterfishes, including harvest fishes and dollar fishes, characterized by their deep, laterally compressed bodies.
family of marine butterfishes

Examples

The Stromateidae are commonly found in temperate and tropical waters worldwide.
Many species in the Stromateidae family are valued for their mild flavor and tender flesh.
Members of Stromateidae, such as butterfish and harvestfish, are important in commercial fisheries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: butterfish - a small, laterally compressed marine fish of the family Stromateidae

Etymology

From genus Stromateus (type genus), from Greek *strōma* (bed, cover).
