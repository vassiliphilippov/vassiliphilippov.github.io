ownership

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊnərˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈəʊnəʃɪp / (BrE)
the state or fact of possessing something, especially the right to possess, use, and transfer property or assets
possession or right to possess

Examples

The ownership of the house was transferred after the sale.
Questions of ownership often arise during divorce proceedings.
He took great pride in his company’s ownership of several patents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: possession - the state of having or owning something
  • Degree Lower: tenancy - possession of land or property as a tenant
  • Degree Higher: proprietorship - the state or right of owning a business or holding property as an owner

Etymology

Formed from 'owner' + '-ship', denoting state or condition.

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊnərˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈəʊnəʃɪp / (BrE)
the state or fact of possessing something; the legal right to possess and control property, goods, or assets.
the state of possessing something

Examples

They disputed the ownership of the land in court.
The museum has ownership of several rare paintings.
New policies make it easier for citizens to achieve home ownership.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: possession - the state of having or owning something
  • Degree Lower: tenancy - the possession or occupancy of land, property, or offices

Etymology

From own + -ship, modeled after similar nouns in English.

3. NOUN

/ ˈoʊnərˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈəʊnəʃɪp / (BrE)
the state or fact of having legal right and control over something
having legal right and control over something

Examples

The ownership of the land has been disputed for decades.
He finally took ownership of the car after making the last payment.
Increasing ownership rates among young adults is a sign of economic growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: possession - the state of having or holding something
  • Degree Lower: tenancy - the possession or occupancy of land or property by rental
  • Degree Lower: usufruct - the right to use and enjoy property of another

Etymology

From 'owner' + '-ship', modeled after similar noun formations in English.
