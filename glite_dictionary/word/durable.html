durable

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʊrəbl / (AmE), / ˈdjʊərəbl / (BrE)
Able to withstand wear, pressure, or damage; very long-lasting.
long-lasting; resistant to wear

Examples

This brand of suitcase is designed to be especially durable for frequent travelers.
The company chose a durable material to reduce replacement costs over time.
Their friendship proved durable, lasting through many ups and downs.

Synset Examples

less durable rocks were gradually worn away to form valleys
the perdurable granite of the ancient Appalachian spine of the continent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perdurable - lasting through a long period of time; enduring
  • Antonym: fragile - easily broken or damaged

Etymology

From Latin durabilis, from durare (to last).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʊrəbəl / (AmE), / ˈdjʊərəbəl / (BrE)
A product or good designed to last for a long time and not be quickly worn out or consumed, especially consumer goods that are not destroyed by use.
long-lasting consumer good

Examples

Many households invested in durables such as refrigerators and washing machines after the war.
Sales of durables tend to increase when consumers feel confident about the economy.
Unlike food or fuel, durables can be used for several years before needing replacement.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: consumable - goods that are intended to be used up quickly and replaced

Etymology

From Latin durabilis, from durare (to last, endure).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03262580-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈdʊrəbəl / (AmE), / ˈdjʊərəbəl / (BrE)
able to exist or be used for a long time without significant deterioration; long-lasting
lasting for a long time

Examples

They invested in durable materials to ensure the building would stand for decades.
His reputation as a durable leader survived many political storms.
A durable peace agreement is essential for the region's long-term stability.

Synset Examples

hopes for a durable peace
a long-lasting friendship

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: permanent - lasting or intended to last indefinitely
  • Antonym: temporary - lasting for only a limited period of time
  • Synonym: lasting - continuing for a long time

Etymology

From Latin 'durabilis' (able to last), from 'durare' (to last).
