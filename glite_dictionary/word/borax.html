borax

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔːrˌæks / (AmE), / ˈbɔː.ræks / (BrE)
a white mineral consisting of hydrated sodium borate, used as a cleaning agent or in making glass and ceramics
a mineral used as a cleaning agent

Examples

She added a spoonful of borax to the laundry to boost cleaning power.
Borax is commonly used in making glass and ceramics.
Some people use borax as a natural cleaning agent around the house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sodium borate - another name for borax, a compound used in cleaning
  • Degree Lower: boron - a chemical element, borax contains boron

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'borax', from Arabic 'būraq', from Persian 'burah' (borax).
