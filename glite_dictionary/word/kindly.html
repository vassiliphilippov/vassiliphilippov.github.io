kindly

1. ADV

/ ˈkaɪndli / (AmE), / ˈkaɪndli / (BrE)
In a kind, gentle, or benevolent manner; out of kindness.
in a kind or gentle manner

Examples

She always speaks kindly to her students, even when they make mistakes.
He kindly offered to help carry the heavy boxes upstairs.
The nurse kindly explained the procedure to the frightened patient.

Synset Examples

He spoke kindly to the boy
she kindly overlooked the mistake

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kindly - having or showing a friendly, generous, or considerate nature (adjective)
  • Antonym: unkindly - in an unkind or harsh manner

2. ADJ

/ ˈkaɪndli / (AmE), / ˈkaɪndli / (BrE)
(of weather or environments) pleasant and agreeable; producing positive or comfortable feelings.
pleasant or agreeable (of weather or things)

Examples

The island's kindly climate attracted tourists all year round.
A kindly wind helped blow the sailboat into the bay.
We enjoyed the kindly sunshine during our afternoon walk.

Synset Examples

a kindly climate
kindly breeze

Related Terms

  • Antonym: harsh - unpleasant or severe (of weather, conditions)
  • Synonym: pleasant - giving a sense of happy satisfaction or enjoyment

Etymology

From Old English 'cyndelic' (natural, native; kind, gentle), from 'cynd' (nature, kind).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00228483-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈkaɪndli / (AmE), / ˈkaɪndli / (BrE)
showing a gentle, caring, and generous attitude toward others; motivated by sympathy or goodwill
having a gentle and generous nature

Examples

Her kindly words comforted the anxious student after his disappointing exam results.
He always offered kindly advice to those who needed help in the neighborhood.
The elderly man received a kindly smile from the nurse every morning.

Synset Examples

was charitable in his opinions of others
kindly criticism
a kindly act
sympathetic words
a large-hearted mentor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kind - having or showing a friendly, generous, and considerate nature
  • Synonym: benevolent - well meaning and kindly
  • Antonym: unkind - not friendly or considerate toward others

Etymology

From Middle English kindeli, from kind (nature, goodwill) + -ly (adjectival suffix).
