Strelitziaceae

1. NOUN

/ stɹɛˌlɪtsiˈeɪsiˌaɪ / (AmE), / stɹɛˌlɪtsiˈeɪsiˌaɪ / (BrE)
A family of woody, usually tropical plants with erect stems, native to South America, South Africa, and Madagascar, sometimes included in the family Musaceae.
a family of tropical woody plants

Examples

The Strelitziaceae family includes the well-known bird of paradise flower.
Botanists often debate whether Strelitziaceae should be classified separately from Musaceae.
Rare members of the Strelitziaceae can be found in the wilds of Madagascar.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Musaceae - the banana family, sometimes considered to include Strelitziaceae

Etymology

From the genus name Strelitzia, after Queen Charlotte of Mecklenburg-Strelitz, combined with the botanical family suffix -aceae.

a family of woody plants with erect stems, native to tropical South America, South Africa, and Madagascar, sometimes classified within the Musaceae family

Examples

The Strelitziaceae family includes the well-known bird-of-paradise plants.

Several ornamental species in parks belong to the Strelitziaceae family.

Related Terms

From the genus name Strelitzia, itself named after Queen Charlotte of Mecklenburg-Strelitz.
