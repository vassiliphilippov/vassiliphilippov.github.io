royalism

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɔɪ.ə.lɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈrɔɪ.ə.lɪzəm / (BrE)
support for or loyalty to a monarchy or the principle of monarchical government.
support for monarchy

Examples

His strong belief in royalism made him support the return of the exiled monarch.
Debates over royalism versus republicanism shaped the nation's political landscape.
Books on royalism often explore the historical roots of monarchy in Europe.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: republicanism - the support for a republic rather than a monarchy

Etymology

From 'royal' (relating to a king or queen) + '-ism' (a belief or doctrine).
