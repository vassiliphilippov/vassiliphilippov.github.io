Mercenaria

1. NOUN

/ mɜr.sɪˈnɛr.i.ə / (AmE), / mɜː.sɪˈnɛə.ri.ə / (BrE)
A genus of edible marine clams in the family Veneridae, including species such as the hard clam or quahog.
genus of edible clams

Examples

The genus Mercenaria includes the well-known hard clam, commonly harvested along the Atlantic coast.
Because Mercenaria species are edible, they are important in both commercial and recreational fisheries.
Researchers studied the shell morphology of several Mercenaria specimens collected from the bay.

Etymology

From Latin 'mercenarius' (hired, for wages), possibly referring to the value of the clams.

2. NOUN

/ mɜr.sɪˈnɛr.i.ə / (AmE), / mɜː.sɪˈnɛə.ri.ə / (BrE)
A genus of edible marine bivalve mollusks in the family Veneridae, which includes hard clams such as the quahog.
a genus of edible bivalve mollusks

Examples

The Mercenaria genus includes species commonly harvested as hard clams or quahogs.
Shells of Mercenaria are often found along the eastern coast of North America.
Biologists studied the life cycle of Mercenaria to better understand their ecological role.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quahog - a common edible clam, Mercenaria mercenaria

Etymology

From Latin 'mercenarius' (hired, for wages), referring to their commercial value.
