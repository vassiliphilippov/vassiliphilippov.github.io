Begonia erythrophylla

1. NOUN

/ bɪˈɡoʊniə ˌɛrɪθroʊˈfɪlə / (AmE), / bɪˈɡəʊniə ˌɛrɪθrəˈfɪlə / (BrE)
A species of rhizomatous begonia with round, fleshy leaves that are reddish underneath, often grown as a houseplant.
species of begonia with red-tinged leaves

Examples

The Begonia erythrophylla is prized for its ornamental leaves in many indoor gardens.
She placed her Begonia erythrophylla near the window to catch the morning sunlight.
Botanists admire the distinctive foliage of Begonia erythrophylla when studying houseplants.

Etymology

From genus name 'Begonia', honoring Michel Bégon, and species name from Greek 'erythros' (red) and 'phyllon' (leaf).
