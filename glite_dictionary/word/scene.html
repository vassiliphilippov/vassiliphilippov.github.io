scene

1. NOUN

/ siːn / (AmE), / siːn / (BrE)
a subdivision of an act in a play or performance, usually marked by a change of setting or characters
part of a play or performance

Examples

The play opened with a dramatic scene on the battlefield.
Each scene in the second act moves the story forward.
They rehearsed their lines for the final scene of the performance.

Synset Examples

the first act has three scenes

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: act - a major division in a play

Etymology

From Latin 'scēna' (stage, scene), from Greek 'skēnē' (tent, stage).
