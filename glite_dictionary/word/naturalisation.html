naturalisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌnætʃərələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnatʃ(ə)rələˈzeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
The process of introducing animals or plants to regions where they are not native but become able to thrive as if they were.
establishment of non-native species in new regions

Examples

The naturalisation of rabbits in Australia has had significant ecological impacts.
Many plants have undergone naturalisation after being introduced by humans to new continents.
Efforts to control the naturalisation of invasive species are ongoing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: introduction - the act of bringing a species into a new environment

Etymology

From French 'naturalisation', from 'naturaliser' (to naturalize), from 'naturel' (natural).

2. NOUN

/ ˌnætʃərələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnatʃərəlʌɪˈzeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the legal process by which someone who is not a citizen of a country is granted citizenship
granting of citizenship to a foreigner
legal british

Examples

After years of living in the country, she finally completed her naturalisation process.
The ceremony marked the naturalisation of dozens of new citizens from around the world.
Changes in immigration law affected the naturalisation requirements for applicants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: naturalization - the legal process by which someone is granted citizenship (American spelling)

Etymology

From French 'naturalisation', from 'naturalisé' (naturalised), ultimately from Latin 'natura' (nature).

3. NOUN

/ ˌnætʃərələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnatʃ(ə)rələˈzeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
The process by which a borrowed word is adapted in pronunciation to fit the phonological system of the borrowing language.
adaptation of borrowed word's pronunciation

Examples

The naturalisation of 'piano' led to its current English pronunciation.
Over time, naturalisation has made many foreign words sound completely native in English.
Linguists study the patterns of naturalisation in different languages to understand their phonological rules.

Synset Examples

the naturalization in English of many Italian words

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assimilation - process by which sounds become similar to neighboring sounds

Etymology

From French 'naturalisation', from 'naturaliser' (make natural).
