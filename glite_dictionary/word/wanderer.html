wanderer

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑːndərər / (AmE), / ˈwɒndərə / (BrE)
A computer program that automatically travels across the internet to locate publicly accessible resources for inclusion in a searchable database.
internet program that gathers resources

Examples

The early wanderer programs laid the foundation for today's complex search engines.
A wanderer continuously scans the internet for new sites to include in its database.
The system relies on a wanderer to update its index of web pages automatically.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: web crawler - An automated program that scans the web to index content for search engines
  • Synonym: spider - Computer program that browses the web to collect data

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɑːndərər / (AmE), / ˈwɒndərə / (BrE)
a person who travels from place to place without a fixed home or purpose
person who moves from place to place

Examples

After years as a wanderer, he decided to settle down in a small village.
Legends spoke of a mysterious wanderer who brought news from distant lands.
She lived as a wanderer, never staying in one place for more than a few months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nomad - a member of a people who move from place to place rather than settling permanently
  • Antonym: settler - someone who settles in a new region and does not move regularly

Etymology

From Old English 'wandrian' (to wander) + '-er' (agent suffix).
