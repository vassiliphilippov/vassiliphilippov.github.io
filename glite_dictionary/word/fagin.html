Fagin

1. PROPN

/ ˈfeɪɡɪn / (AmE), / ˈfeɪɡɪn / (BrE)
a villainous character in Charles Dickens' novel 'Oliver Twist', known for leading a gang of child thieves.
villain in 'Oliver Twist' by Dickens

Examples

The cunning Fagin manipulates the boys into stealing for him in the novel.
In literary discussions, Fagin is often cited as one of Dickens' most memorable villains.
Some critics view Fagin as a symbol of corruption and exploitation in Victorian London.

Synset Examples

Fagin was a fence who trained boys as pickpockets

Etymology

From the name of the character created by Charles Dickens in 'Oliver Twist' (1837-39).
