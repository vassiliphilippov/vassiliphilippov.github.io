Lycopodium alopecuroides

1. NOUN

/ ˌlaɪkəˈpoʊdiəm ælˌoʊpɪˈkjʊəraɪdiːz / (AmE), / ˌlɪkəˈpəʊdiəm ælˌəʊpɪˈkjʊəraɪdiːz / (BrE)
A species of ground pine covered with bristly leaves, found in sandy or peaty moist coastal regions of eastern and southeastern United States.
a species of ground pine with bristly leaves

Examples

Lycopodium alopecuroides is often found in sandy, coastal habitats along the eastern United States.
Botanists identified the specimen as Lycopodium alopecuroides due to its dense, bristly foliage.
The bristly leaves of Lycopodium alopecuroides help distinguish it from other types of ground pine.

Etymology

From Greek 'lykos' (wolf) and 'pous, podos' (foot); 'alopecuroides' from Greek 'alopex' (fox) and 'eidos' (resembling), referring to fox-tail likeness.
