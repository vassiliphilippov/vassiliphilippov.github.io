sprag

1. NOUN

/ spræɡ / (AmE), / spræɡ / (BrE)
a wooden or metal prop used to support the roof of a mine
prop supporting a mine roof

Examples

The workers placed a sprag under the weak section of the mine roof for additional support.
Old coal mines often relied on wooden sprags to prevent cave-ins.
A broken sprag can be very dangerous in underground mining operations.

2. NOUN

/ spræɡ / (AmE), / spræɡ / (BrE)
a chock or bar wedged under a wheel or inserted between spokes to prevent a vehicle or machinery from rolling or moving
wedge or bar to stop rolling

Examples

The mechanic placed a sprag under the wheel to keep the cart from slipping.
Rail workers often used a sprag to secure mining trucks on steep tracks.
He forgot to remove the sprag before trying to move the wagon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chock - a wedge or block placed against a wheel to prevent movement

Etymology

Of uncertain origin; possibly related to Norwegian *sprag* (a stick or shaft).
