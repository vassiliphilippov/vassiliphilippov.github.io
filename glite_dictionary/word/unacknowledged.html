unacknowledged

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.əkˈnɑ.lɪdʒd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.ækˈnɒl.ɪdʒd / (BrE)
not recognized, admitted, or publicly acknowledged
not recognized or admitted

Examples

Her contributions to the project often went unacknowledged by her colleagues.
There are many unacknowledged influences in his writing style.
They suffered from unacknowledged grievances for years before speaking out.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acknowledged - recognized or admitted

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix 'un-' (not) to 'acknowledged', meaning not acknowledged.

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnəkˈnɑlɪdʒd / (AmE), / ˌʌnəkˈnɒlɪdʒd / (BrE)
not recognized, admitted, or openly accepted as existing or valid.
not recognized or admitted

Examples

The manager's contributions often went unacknowledged by the company.
There was an unacknowledged tension in the room during the meeting.
Her help with the project remained unacknowledged, even though it was crucial.

Synset Examples

an unacknowledged emergency

Related Terms

  • Antonym: acknowledged - recognized or openly admitted

Etymology

Prefix un- + acknowledged; from acknowledge (from Old French acknoer, variant of 'recognize').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00692017-s
Synset POS: s
