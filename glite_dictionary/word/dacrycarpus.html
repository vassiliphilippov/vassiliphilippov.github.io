Dacrycarpus

1. NOUN

/ ˌdækriˈkɑrpəs / (AmE), / ˌdakrɪˈkɑːpəs / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen coniferous trees and shrubs native to regions from New Zealand to Malaysia and the Philippines.
genus of evergreen coniferous trees/shrubs

Examples

The Dacrycarpus species are popular among botanists for their unique fruit and foliage.
Some Dacrycarpus trees can reach significant heights in the forests of New Zealand.
Gardeners in tropical regions sometimes cultivate Dacrycarpus for ornamental purposes.

Etymology

From Greek dakryon (tear) + karpos (fruit), referring to the shape of the fruit.

2. NOUN

/ ˌdækriˈkɑrpəs / (AmE), / ˌdakrɪˈkɑːpəs / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen coniferous shrubs or trees native to regions from New Zealand to Malaysia and the Philippines, belonging to the family Podocarpaceae.
genus of coniferous trees from New Zealand to Southeast Asia

Examples

The Dacrycarpus species are important components of mountain forests in New Zealand.
Botanists discovered a rare Dacrycarpus tree growing on a remote island in Malaysia.
Members of the Dacrycarpus genus are known for their attractive, narrowly conical shapes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: podocarpus - another genus of evergreen coniferous trees in the family Podocarpaceae

Etymology

From Greek 'dakryon' (tear) and 'karpos' (fruit), referring to the fruit shape.
