conspicuous

1. ADJ

/ kənˈspɪkjuəs / (AmE), / kənˈspɪkjuəs / (BrE)
Easily seen or noticed; attracting attention because of being unusual, remarkable, or clearly visible.
easily seen or noticeable

Examples

The bright red car was especially conspicuous in the parking lot.
She made a conspicuous effort to arrive late and draw attention to herself.
The error was so conspicuous that anyone could spot it immediately.

Synset Examples

a tower conspicuous at a great distance
wore conspicuous neckties
made herself conspicuous by her exhibitionistic preening

Related Terms

  • Synonym: noticeable - Easily seen or detected; attracting attention
  • Antonym: inconspicuous - Not easily seen or noticed; not prominent

Etymology

From Latin conspicuus (visible), from conspicere (to look at).

2. ADJ

/ kənˈspɪkjuəs / (AmE), / kənˈspɪkjuəs / (BrE)
Easily seen or noticed; attracting attention because of being obvious or without any attempt at concealment.
easily seen or noticed; obvious

Examples

The bright red coat made her conspicuous in the crowd.
His conspicuous generosity quickly earned him a reputation in the community.
The error was so conspicuous that everyone noticed it immediately.

Synset Examples

blatant disregard of the law
a blatant appeal to vanity
a blazing indiscretion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obvious - Easily perceived or understood; clear, self-evident
  • Synonym: blatant - Completely lacking in subtlety; very obvious
  • Antonym: hidden - Kept out of sight; concealed

Etymology

From Latin conspicuus (visible, remarkable), from conspicere (to look at, observe).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02097713-s
Synset POS: s
