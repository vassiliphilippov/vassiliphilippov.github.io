disadvantageous

1. ADJ

/ ˌdɪsˌædvənˈteɪdʒəs / (AmE), / ˌdɪsˌædvənˈteɪdʒəs / (BrE)
causing difficulty, loss, or harm; creating an unfavorable or detrimental situation
unfavorable or detrimental

Examples

His comments put the company in a disadvantageous position during negotiations.
It would be disadvantageous for us to accept these terms without further discussion.
Students from rural areas often face disadvantageous conditions compared to their urban peers.

Synset Examples

a disadvantageous outcome
a well-known study from the 1970's showed that gender stereotyping placed women in a disadvantageous position
made an unfavorable impression

Related Terms

  • Antonym: advantageous - creating favorable or beneficial circumstances

Etymology

From 'disadvantage' (Middle English, from Old French 'desavantage') + '-ous'.
