roof

1. NOUN

/ ruːf / (AmE), / ruːf / (BrE)
the inner top surface of a covered area or hollow space, such as a cave or the mouth
inner top surface, as in a cave or mouth

Examples

The explorer pointed to the roof of the cave where bats were hanging upside down.
There was a crack in the roof of the tunnel letting in daylight.
He injured the roof of his mouth while eating crispy bread.

Synset Examples

the roof of the cave was very high
I could see the roof of the bear's mouth

Etymology

From Old English *hrōf* (roof, ceiling, top covering).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09439768-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ruːf / (AmE), / ruːf / (BrE)
to provide or cover (a building) with a roof.
cover with a roof

Examples

They hired a contractor to roof their new house before winter arrived.
It took the workers only two days to roof the entire barn.
After the storm, we had to roof the shed again to keep out the rain.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unroof - to remove the roof from a building

Etymology

From Old English 'hrōf' (roof, cover).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01236557-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ruf / (AmE), / ruːf / (BrE)
an upper limit or maximum allowed for something, such as spending or salaries
maximum permitted limit; upper cap

Examples

They set a roof on the monthly expenses to control the budget.
A roof on salaries was imposed to reduce costs across the company.
The new law establishes a roof for political campaign donations.

Synset Examples

he put a ceiling on the number of women who worked for him
there was a roof on salaries
they established a cap for prices

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ceiling - an upper limit, especially an imposed one
  • Synonym: cap - an upper limit or restriction

4. NOUN

/ ruf / (AmE), / ruːf / (BrE)
the fixed, usually solid covering forming the uppermost part of a motor vehicle, protecting passengers and interior from weather
top covering of a motor vehicle

Examples

The sunroof lets you enjoy the sky without removing the roof completely.
After the hailstorm, there were dents on the car's roof.
They strapped the luggage to the roof of the van before leaving.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hood - the hinged cover over the engine of a car (American English)

Etymology

Old English *hrōf* (roof, covering); extended to vehicles in the early 20th century.

Source: WordNet

5. NOUN

/ ruːf / (AmE), / ruːf / (BrE)
the structure that covers the top of a building and provides protection from the weather
structure covering a building

Examples

Heavy snow collected on the roof during the storm.
We climbed up to the roof to watch the sunset over the city.
The old roof was replaced with new tiles last year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ceiling - the upper interior surface of a room
  • Antonym: floor - the lower surface of a room, on which one stands

Etymology

From Old English 'hrōf' (roof, ceiling, sky).
