to describe

1. VERB

/ dɪˈskraɪb / (AmE), / dɪˈskraɪb / (BrE)
(in science) To identify, name, and formally record a new species in a field such as botany or biology.
formally identify and record a species

Examples

The botanist was the first to describe this rare orchid as a new species.
Hundreds of new insects are described each year by entomologists.
She spent her career in the Amazon, describing plants that were previously unknown to science.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to classify - to arrange in categories or groups

Etymology

From Latin describere (to write down, copy, sketch).

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ dɪˈskraɪb / (AmE), / dɪˈskraɪb / (BrE)
to give a detailed account or explanation of something, often using words to convey what it is like
give a detailed account

Examples

Can you describe the suspect to the police officer?
She struggled to describe the beauty of the sunset.
The manual describes every part of the machine in detail.

Synset Examples

He drew an elaborate plan of attack

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to depict - to show or represent by a drawing, painting, or other art form
  • Synonym: to narrate - to tell a story or describe a series of events

Etymology

From Latin 'describere' (to write down, copy, sketch), from 'de-' (down) + 'scribere' (to write).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00989103-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ dɪˈskraɪb / (AmE), / dɪˈskraɪb / (BrE)
to mark out or trace the outline of (a shape, figure, or path) on a surface, especially in geometry or technical contexts
to trace or mark out a shape or line

Examples

Using a compass, the student described a perfect circle on the paper.
The architect described an arc to show the curve of the ceiling.
As the stick moved through the sand, it described a winding path.

Synset Examples

draw a line
trace the outline of a figure in the sand

Etymology

From Latin describere (to write down, copy, sketch, represent).
