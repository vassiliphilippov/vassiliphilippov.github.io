Erythrocin

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈrɪθrəˌsɪn / (AmE), / ɪˈrɪθrəˌsɪn / (BrE)
A trademarked antibiotic medication, containing erythromycin, used to treat infections caused by various bacteria.
brand name of antibiotic erythromycin
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Erythrocin to treat the patient's respiratory infection.
Children who are allergic to penicillin may be given Erythrocin as an alternative.
It is important to take Erythrocin exactly as directed to ensure effectiveness.

Related Terms
