swop

1. NOUN

/ swɑp / (AmE), / swɒp / (BrE)
an act of exchanging one thing for another of approximately equal value
an act of exchanging things

Examples

Let's do a swop—I'll give you my book for your DVD.
The children arranged a swop of trading cards at lunchtime.
After some negotiation, they agreed on a swop of seats for the journey.

Synset Examples

we had no money so we had to live by barter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swap - an act of exchanging one thing for another
  • Synonym: barter - exchange goods or services without using money

Etymology

Variant spelling of 'swap', probably of imitative origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01111592-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ swɑp / (AmE), / swɒp / (BrE)
to exchange or give (something) in exchange for something else
exchange one thing for another
british

Examples

If you like my book, I'll swop it for your DVD.
We decided to swop seats for a better view of the stage.
She often swops clothes with her friends before parties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swap - to exchange one thing for another

Etymology

Variant of 'swap', chiefly British.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02263812-v
Synset POS: v
