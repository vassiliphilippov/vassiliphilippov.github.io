polecat

1. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊlˌkæt / (AmE), / ˈpəʊlˌkæt / (BrE)
a small, dark brown mammal of the weasel family found in Eurasian woodlands, known for releasing a strong, unpleasant odor when threatened
Eurasian weasel-like mammal with strong odor

Examples

The polecat is rarely seen during the day because it is mostly nocturnal.
When threatened, a polecat can emit a pungent smell to deter predators.
Farmers often blamed the polecat for disturbances in their chicken coops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ferret - domesticated form of the European polecat
  • Synonym: skunk - American mammal known for its foul-smelling defensive spray

Etymology

From Middle French 'polcat', likely from 'poule' (hen) and 'cat', referring to its reputation for raiding henhouses.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpoʊlˌkæt / (AmE), / ˈpəʊlkæt / (BrE)
An American mammal related to weasels and skunks, known for ejecting a strong-smelling fluid as a defense when startled.
American mammal, sprays foul odor

Examples

When threatened, a polecat can spray a noxious fluid to ward off predators.
Many people mistake a polecat for other small carnivorous mammals in North America.
The smell of polecat spray can linger for days after it has been released.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skunk - American mammal known for its pungent defensive spray

Etymology

From Middle French *poulcat*, probably from Old French *poul* (chicken) + *cat* (cat), perhaps referring to its predation on poultry.
