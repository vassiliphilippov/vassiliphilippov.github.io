St. Brigid

1. PROPN

/ seɪnt ˈbrɪdʒɪd / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈbrɪdʒɪd / (BrE)
An Irish abbess and one of the patron saints of Ireland, renowned for her piety and leadership; lived circa 453–523.
Irish patron saint and abbess (c. 453–523)

Examples

Many Irish people celebrate St. Brigid's Day on the first of February.
The legends of St. Brigid emphasize her generosity and compassion.
Schools and churches across Ireland are named after St. Brigid in her honor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: St. Patrick - Another patron saint of Ireland

Etymology

Brigid is from Old Irish 'Brigit', related to the Celtic goddess Brigid.
