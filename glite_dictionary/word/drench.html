to drench

1. VERB

/ drɛntʃ / (AmE), / drɛntʃ / (BrE)
to make someone or something completely wet by covering them with a large amount of liquid
make completely wet; soak

Examples

The sudden storm drenched us before we reached the shelter.
She accidentally drenched her notes with coffee.
Firefighters drenched the building with water to prevent the flames from spreading.

Synset Examples

souse water on his hot face

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soak - to make completely wet by immersing in liquid
  • Antonym: dry - to remove moisture; to make not wet

Etymology

From Old English *drenchan* (to cause to drink, to drown).

2. VERB

/ drɛntʃ / (AmE), / drɛntʃ / (BrE)
to force (a person or animal) to drink a liquid, especially as a medical treatment
force (a person or animal) to drink

Examples

The veterinarian had to drench the sheep with medicine to treat the parasites.
In older times, farmers would drench their cattle with herbal remedies before modern drugs existed.
It can be difficult to drench a stubborn animal without assistance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to administer - to give (medicine or treatment) to someone

Etymology

From Old English *drencan* (to cause to drink), related to 'drink'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01171869-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ drɛntʃ / (AmE), / drɛntʃ / (BrE)
to make someone or something extremely wet, especially by soaking or submerging in liquid
make thoroughly wet or soak

Examples

They were completely drenched after the unexpected downpour.
The firefighter drenched the burning house with water to extinguish the flames.
The waves crashed over the rocks and drenched the people standing nearby.

Synset Examples

The tsunami swamped every boat in the harbor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soak - to make thoroughly wet by immersing in liquid
  • Synonym: to submerge - to cause to be under the surface of water or liquid
  • Antonym: dry - to remove moisture or become dry

Etymology

From Old English *drencan* (to make drink, soak, drown), related to drink.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02777522-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ drɛntʃ / (AmE), / drɛntʃ / (BrE)
to fill or permeate something completely, especially with a liquid or strong feeling
to permeate or soak thoroughly

Examples

The entire town was drenched in a sense of despair after the tragedy.
Her white dress was drenched in sunlight as she walked through the meadow.
The writer drenched his novel in historical detail, making the era come alive for readers.

Synset Examples

The war drenched the country in blood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soak - to make thoroughly wet by immersion or saturation
  • Antonym: dry - to remove moisture or become free from liquid

Etymology

From Old English drencan (to cause to drink, make wet), related to 'drink'.

Source: WordNet
