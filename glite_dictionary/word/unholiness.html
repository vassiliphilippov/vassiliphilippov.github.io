unholiness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈhoʊlinəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈhəʊlinəs / (BrE)
The state or quality of being unholy; lack of holiness or sanctity.
lack of holiness or sanctity

Examples

The community was concerned about the unholiness of recent events.
He tried to hide his unholiness behind pious words.
The book discusses the unholiness that can creep into religious practices.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: holiness - the quality of being holy

Etymology

Formed from 'unholy' (not holy) and the suffix '-ness'.
