citrine

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪˌtrin / (AmE), / ˈsɪtriːn / (BrE)
A semiprecious gemstone that is a yellow variety of quartz, often used in jewelry and resembling topaz.
yellow variety of quartz; semiprecious stone

Examples

She wore a delicate necklace with a citrine pendant that sparkled in the sunlight.
The jeweler recommended citrine as a more affordable alternative to topaz.
Collectors often prize citrine for its rich golden-yellow hue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: topaz - a precious yellow or brownish gemstone
  • Degree Lower: amethyst - a purple variety of quartz used as a gemstone

Etymology

From Latin *citrinus* (lemon-colored), ultimately from *citrus* (citron).
