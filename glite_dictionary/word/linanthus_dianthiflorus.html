Linanthus dianthiflorus

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈnænθəs daɪˌænθɪˈflɔrəs / (AmE), / lɪˈnanθəs daɪˌanθɪˈflɔːrəs / (BrE)
A low, wiry-stemmed branching herb native to southern California, known for its fringed pink flowers.
a flowering herb of southern California

Examples

The delicate pink blooms of Linanthus dianthiflorus brightened the rocky hillside in early spring.
Researchers surveyed populations of Linanthus dianthiflorus to monitor its response to climate change.
Gardeners admire Linanthus dianthiflorus for its ability to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

Etymology

Linanthus from Greek 'linon' (flax) and 'anthos' (flower); species name from 'Dianthus' (pinks or carnations) and Latin 'flos' (flower).
