unsanctification

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌnˌsæŋktəfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌʌnˌsæŋktɨfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
The state or process of being made unholy or losing sanctity; the quality of being profane.
loss of holiness; becoming profane

Examples

The ritual's improper performance led to the unsanctification of the sacred space.
Some viewed the abandoned church as a symbol of unsanctification in the community.
The desecration represented the unsanctification of ancient traditions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sanctification - the action or process of making or declaring something holy
  • Synonym: profanation - the act of treating something sacred with disrespect

Etymology

From un- (not) + sanctification (from Latin sanctificare, to make holy).
