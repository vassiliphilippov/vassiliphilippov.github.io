pachycephalosaur

1. NOUN

/ ˌpækiˈsɛfəloʊˌsɔr / (AmE), / ˌpakɪˈsɛfələsɔː / (BrE)
a large bipedal herbivorous dinosaur with a thick domed skull, known for having up to 10 inches of bone atop its head
a dome-headed dinosaur genus

Examples

The pachycephalosaur is famous for its extraordinarily thick skull, which scientists think was used for head-butting behaviors.
Fossils of a pachycephalosaur were discovered in North America, providing insight into Cretaceous period fauna.
Children visiting the museum were fascinated by the life-size model of a pachycephalosaur.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dinosaur - prehistoric reptile group including extinct species

Etymology

From Greek *pakhus* (thick) + *kephalē* (head) + *sauros* (lizard).
