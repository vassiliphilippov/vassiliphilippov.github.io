to bestialise

1. VERB

/ ˈbɛstiəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈbɛstɪəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make brutal, depraved, or animal-like; to deprive of human qualities or sensibilities
make brutal or animal-like

Examples

The harsh conditions of the prison served only to bestialise its most vulnerable inmates.
War can bestialise even the most civilized societies, stripping away compassion and empathy.
Some critics argue that violent media may inadvertently bestialise its audience over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dehumanize - to deprive of human qualities, make less human

Etymology

From Latin 'bestia' (beast) plus the suffix '-ise'.
