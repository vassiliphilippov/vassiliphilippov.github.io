vivisectionist

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɪvəˈsɛkʃənɪst / (AmE), / ˌvɪvɪˈsɛkʃənɪst / (BrE)
a person, especially a biologist, who performs operations on live animals for experimental research
person who performs vivisection

Examples

The vivisectionist defended his research by emphasizing its contribution to medical science.
As a student, she was appalled to see a vivisectionist at work in the laboratory.
Many animal rights activists protest against the practices of vivisectionists worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vivisection - the practice of performing operations on live animals for research

Etymology

From 'vivisection' + '-ist'; 'vivisection' from Latin 'vivus' (alive) and 'sectio' (cutting).
