extrapolation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛkstrəˌpəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛkstrəpəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of estimating or calculating a value by extending known information or data beyond its original range, especially in mathematics.
calculation beyond known data range

Examples

We used extrapolation to predict the population growth for the next decade.
By applying extrapolation, the scientist estimated future temperature increases beyond the observed data.
Accurate extrapolation requires careful analysis to avoid misleading results.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: interpolation - calculation of a value within the range of known values

Etymology

From Latin extra (outside) + English interpolation.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛkˌstræpəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛkˌstræpəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of estimating or inferring information about the future or about an unobserved situation based on known facts or data
inference based on existing facts

Examples

Scientists use extrapolation to predict future climate change based on current trends.
His investment advice involved careful extrapolation from past market behavior.
Any extrapolation from these results should be made with caution.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: interpolation - the process of estimating values within the range of known data points

Etymology

From Latin extra (outside) + English interpolation.
