stepwise

1. ADV

/ ˈstɛpˌwaɪz / (AmE), / ˈstɛpwaɪz / (BrE)
In a series of distinct stages or steps; proceeding gradually from one stage to the next.
in steps or distinct stages

Examples

The treatment should be administered in a stepwise manner to minimize side effects.
The manager introduced the new policy stepwise rather than all at once.
The researchers increased the pressure stepwise to observe the material's response.

Synset Examples

the voltage was increased stepwise

2. ADJ

/ ˈstɛpˌwaɪz / (AmE), / ˈstɛpˌwaɪz / (BrE)
happening or progressing in distinct steps or stages, rather than all at once
in distinct steps or stages

Examples

The chemical reaction proceeds in a stepwise manner rather than happening all at once.
Learning to play the piano is a stepwise process, requiring practice at each stage.
The policy changes were implemented stepwise to minimize disruption.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gradual - taking place or progressing slowly or by degrees
  • Antonym: abrupt - sudden and not gradual

Etymology

From 'step' + '-wise', used to indicate 'in the manner or direction of steps'.
