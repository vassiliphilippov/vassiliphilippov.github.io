perfection

1. NOUN

/ pərˈfɛkʃən / (AmE), / pəˈfɛkʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of improving something so it becomes perfect
making something perfect

Examples

The designer spent months on the perfection of every detail in the dress.
His dedication to the perfection of his craft is admirable.
Years of research led to the perfection of the new vaccine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: improvement - the process of making something better

Etymology

From Latin 'perfectio', from 'perficere' meaning 'to complete, accomplish'.

2. NOUN

/ pərˈfɛkʃən / (AmE), / pəˈfɛkʃən / (BrE)
An ideal example or embodiment of something in its most excellent or flawless form.
ideal or perfect example

Examples

Michelangelo's David is often regarded as the perfection of Renaissance sculpture.
Her performance in the play was the very perfection of acting, captivating the entire audience.
The garden was a perfection of color and fragrance during the spring bloom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exemplar - a person or thing serving as a typical example or excellent model
  • Antonym: imperfection - the state of being imperfect or incomplete

Etymology

From Latin perfectio, from perficere (to complete, finish, bring to full development).

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ pərˈfɛkʃən / (AmE), / pəˈfɛkʃən / (BrE)
the condition or state of being entirely free from flaws or defects; complete excellence
state of being flawless

Examples

Her voice achieved a level of perfection that captivated the entire audience.
He spent years refining his technique in search of perfection.
The cake's appearance and taste were close to perfection.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: imperfection - the state of having flaws or defects
  • Synonym: flawlessness - the quality of being without mistakes or defects

Etymology

From Latin perfectio (completion, accomplishment), from perficere (to complete, to finish).
