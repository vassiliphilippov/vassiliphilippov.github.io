to devour

1. VERB

/ dɪˈvaʊɚ / (AmE), / dɪˈvaʊə / (BrE)
to eat something eagerly and in large amounts so that nothing is left
eat eagerly in large amounts

Examples

The children devoured the chocolate cake within minutes.
After a long hike, she devoured her lunch hungrily.
He devours books as quickly as he can get them.

Synset Examples

Some people can down a pound of meat in the course of one meal
The teenagers demolished four pizzas among them

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gobble - to eat something quickly and noisily
  • Synonym: to consume - to eat or drink something
  • Antonym: nibble - to eat something by taking small bites

Etymology

From Latin 'devorare', from 'de-' (down) + 'vorare' (to swallow).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01199565-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ dɪˈvaʊɚ / (AmE), / dɪˈvaʊə / (BrE)
To read, watch, or listen to something with great enthusiasm and eagerness.
enjoy something intensely or eagerly

Examples

As a child, she devoured every fantasy novel she could find.
He devoured the film series in just a weekend, unable to stop watching.
They devour podcasts about science with endless fascination.

Synset Examples

She devoured his novels

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to consume - to eat, drink, or ingest; to use up enthusiastically

Etymology

From Old French *devorer*, from Latin *devorare* (to swallow down).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01824736-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ dɪˈvaʊɚ / (AmE), / dɪˈvaʊə / (BrE)
To eat hungrily or quickly; to consume or destroy something eagerly or completely.
eat or consume eagerly or completely

Examples

The hungry wolves devoured their prey within minutes.
She devoured the novel in a single sitting, unable to put it down.
Flames quickly devoured the old wooden barn, leaving only ashes behind.

Synset Examples

Fire had devoured our home

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to consume - to eat or use up; devour
  • Synonym: to destroy - to put an end to the existence of; ruin

Etymology

From Old French *devorer*, from Latin *devorare* ('to swallow down, gulp'), from *de-* ('down') + *vorare* ('to eat greedily').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01568141-v
Synset POS: v
