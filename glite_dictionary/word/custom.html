custom

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌstəm / (AmE), / ˈkʌstəm / (BrE)
a traditional and widely accepted way of behaving or doing something that is specific to a particular society, place, or time
tradition or long-established practice

Examples

It is a custom in some cultures to remove your shoes before entering a home.
The annual festival is a custom dating back hundreds of years.
Giving gifts on birthdays is a common custom around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tradition - a belief or behavior passed down within a society
  • Antonym: innovation - the introduction of something new

Etymology

From Latin 'consuetudo' (habit, usage), via Old French 'costume'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05675248-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʌstəm / (AmE), / ˈkʌstəm / (BrE)
money collected by a government on imported or exported goods; a duty imposed under a tariff.
money collected on imports or exports

Examples

All custom collected at the border was recorded in detailed ledgers.
The government increased the custom on imported luxury goods last year.
Revenue from custom supports the country's public services.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duty - a tax on imports or exports
  • Synonym: tariff - a tax or duty to be paid on a particular class of imports or exports
  • Synonym: excise - a tax levied on certain goods

Etymology

From Old French *costume* and Latin *consuetudo* (custom, usage), in this sense originally referring to customary payments or dues.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13338024-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈkʌstəm / (AmE), / ˈkʌstəm / (BrE)
(of goods or services) made or done to fit the needs or requirements of a particular person; customized.
made for a particular individual

Examples

She ordered a custom dress for her wedding, designed to her exact measurements.
Many companies offer custom software solutions for specialized business needs.
He drives a custom motorcycle built to reflect his personality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: customized - made or changed according to the buyer's requirements
  • Antonym: standard - not made to individual requirements; regular or typical

Etymology

From Old French 'costume' (custom, usage), from Latin 'consuetudo' (habit, custom).

4. NOUN

/ ˈkʌstəm / (AmE), / ˈkʌstəm / (BrE)
A traditional and widely accepted way of behaving or doing something that is specific to a particular society, place, or time.
traditional practice or convention

Examples

It is a custom in many countries to shake hands when greeting someone.
The family has a custom of gathering for dinner every Sunday.
Removing your shoes before entering the house is a common custom in Japan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tradition - A custom or belief passed down from generation to generation
  • Synonym: habit - A regular practice or routine, especially one that is hard to give up
  • Antonym: law - A system of rules recognized by a particular country or community

Etymology

5. NOUN

regular patronage of a business

Examples
