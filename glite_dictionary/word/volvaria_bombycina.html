Volvaria bombycina

1. NOUN

/ voʊlˈvɛəriə bɒmˈbɪsɪnə / (AmE), / vɒlˈvɑːrɪə bɒmˈbɪsɪnə / (BrE)
A species of fungus, known as the silky rosegill, that is a parasite on various trees.
parasitic fungus species (silky rosegill)

Examples

The Volvaria bombycina can often be seen growing on dead poplar trees in late summer.
Mycology students studied the lifecycle of Volvaria bombycina as part of their fieldwork.
Because Volvaria bombycina prefers decaying wood, it plays an important role in forest ecosystems.

Etymology

From New Latin: Volvaria (genus name, from Latin 'volva' meaning 'wrapper') + bombycina (from Latin 'bombycinus', 'silken').
