gimmick

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪmɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡɪmɪk / (BrE)
A clever or unusual trick or device, especially one intended to attract attention, publicity, or business.
trick or device to attract attention

Examples

The new packaging was just a gimmick to catch the eyes of shoppers.
The magician's act relied on a clever gimmick that amazed the audience.
Some people saw the free giveaway as a marketing gimmick rather than a real offer.

Synset Examples

he would stoop to any device to win a point
it was a great sales gimmick
a cheap promotions gimmick for greedy businessmen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trick - an action intended to deceive or outwit
  • Synonym: device - a plan, scheme, or trick for achieving some end

Etymology

Origin uncertain, possibly from early 20th-century American slang, perhaps alteration of 'gimcrack'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪmɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡɪmɪk / (BrE)
an object whose name is forgotten or not known; a thingamajig or gadget
unspecified object or gadget
informal

Examples

He handed me a strange gimmick and asked if I knew what it was.
We've lost the gimmick that attaches this old lamp to the stand.
Can you pass me that gimmick over there, the one next to the coffee mug?

Synset Examples

she eased the ball-shaped doodad back into its socket
there may be some great new gizmo around the corner that you will want to use

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gizmo - an object whose name is unknown or forgotten
  • Synonym: thingamajig - an object or device whose name is unknown or forgotten
  • Synonym: widget - an unnamed device or gadget

Etymology

Original use (1920s) in U.S. slang, possibly a fanciful alteration of 'gimcrack' or related to 'gadget'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03223248-n
Synset POS: n
