aloe

1. NOUN

/ ˈæloʊ / (AmE), / ˈaləʊ / (BrE)
a succulent plant with thick, fleshy leaves arranged in a rosette, often with spiky flowers, found mainly in Africa and known for its medicinal properties
a succulent plant, often medicinal

Examples

The aloe in her garden produced a tall stalk of yellow flowers this spring.
Some skincare products contain extracts from the aloe plant to soothe sunburn.
African landscapes are often dotted with wild aloe thriving in the arid climate.

Etymology

From Greek aloē, referring to the plant; possibly via Arabic alwā or Hebrew 'ahāl.
