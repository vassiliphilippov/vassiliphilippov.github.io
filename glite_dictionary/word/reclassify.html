to reclassify

1. VERB

/ riˈklæsɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈklæsɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to assign to a different category or class; to classify again, especially after new information becomes available
assign to a different category or class

Examples

After reviewing the data, the committee decided to reclassify some of the endangered species.
Due to recent changes in legislation, the government may reclassify certain chemicals as hazardous.
The school had to reclassify several courses as advanced-level classes.

Synset Examples

The zoologists had to reclassify the mollusks after they found new species

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to classify - to arrange or organize into categories

Etymology

From re- (again) + classify, ultimately from French classifier, from Latin classis (class).
