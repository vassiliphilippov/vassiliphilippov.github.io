Bruchus

1. PROPN

/ ˈbruːkəs / (AmE), / ˈbruːkəs / (BrE)
a genus of small beetles, commonly known as seed beetles, in the family Bruchidae, many of which are pests of legumes
genus of seed beetles

Examples

The scientist discovered a new species in the genus Bruchus while studying agricultural pests.
Several types of Bruchus beetles are known to damage stored beans and lentils.
Farmers are concerned about infestations caused by Bruchus in their crops.

Etymology

From New Latin, used as a genus name, from Greek brúkhos (a kind of insect eating figs and wheat).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbruːkəs / (AmE), / ˈbruːkəs / (BrE)
A genus of beetles that is the type genus of the family Bruchidae, commonly known as seed beetles.
genus of seed beetles

Examples

The Bruchus genus includes several species that damage stored beans and peas.
Researchers identified the pest as a member of the Bruchus genus.
Understanding Bruchus is important for controlling legume crop infestations.

Etymology

From Latin Bruchus, meaning a kind of beetle.
