amenableness

1. NOUN

/ əˈmɛn.ə.bəl.nəs / (AmE), / əˈmiː.nə.bəl.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being willing to cooperate or be responsive to suggestions or authority
quality of being cooperative or responsive

Examples

Her amenableness made her an excellent team leader who could resolve conflicts easily.
The manager appreciated the amenableness of her staff members during the busy season.
Their amenableness to new ideas contributed greatly to the project's success.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cooperativeness - the quality of being cooperative

Etymology

From "amenable" + "-ness"; "amenable" from Old French "amener" (to bring to), from Latin "ad" (to) + "minare" (to lead).
