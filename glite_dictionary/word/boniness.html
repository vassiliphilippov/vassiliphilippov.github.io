boniness

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊni.nəs / (AmE), / ˈbəʊni.nəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being very thin, with bones prominently showing, often due to lack of flesh from disease or starvation
extreme thinness with prominent bones

Examples

Her boniness became more apparent after months of illness and poor nutrition.
The boniness of the stray dogs alarmed the animal rescue workers.
Despite his boniness, he remained surprisingly strong for his size.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leanness - the quality of lacking excess flesh; being thin
  • Synonym: emaciation - the state of being abnormally thin or weak, especially due to illness or lack of food
  • Antonym: plumpness - the quality of being full or rounded in form, especially with flesh

Etymology

From 'bony' + '-ness', with 'bony' derived from 'bone'.
