defeasible

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈfiːsəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈfiːsɪbəl / (BrE)
(of a right, interest, or claim) capable of being annulled, voided, or terminated under certain conditions.
capable of being annulled or terminated
legal

Examples

The contract was defeasible if either party failed to meet the set conditions.
His right to the property is defeasible should he breach the terms of the will.
A defeasible interest may end if certain circumstances arise.

Synset Examples

a claim to an estate may be defeasible so long as the claimant is under 21 and unmarried

Etymology

From Old French *defaisable* (able to be undone), from *défaire* (to undo), from Latin *dis-* (apart) + *facere* (to do).
