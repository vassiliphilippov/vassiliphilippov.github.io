Verpa conica

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɜr.pə kəˈnaɪ.kə / (AmE), / ˈvɜː.pə kəˈniː.kə / (BrE)
A species of morel mushroom with a smooth, conical cap and a fragile stalk, found in temperate regions.
species of morel mushroom

Examples

The delicate Verpa conica can be mistaken for true morels by inexperienced foragers.
We spotted several Verpa conica mushrooms growing along the riverbank this spring.
Unlike more robust morels, the stem of Verpa conica is notably fragile and easily broken.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morel - an edible fungus with a honeycombed cap

Etymology

From Latin: 'verpa' meaning 'rod' and 'conica' meaning 'conical', referring to the shape of the mushroom cap.
