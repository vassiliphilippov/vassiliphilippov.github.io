reckoner

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛk.ən.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈrɛk.ən.ə / (BrE)
a person who is skilled at calculating, especially with figures or calculating machines
expert at calculation

Examples

As the chief reckoner in the office, she quickly solved even the most complex equations.
He was known as a human reckoner before electronic calculators became popular.
The company's success depended heavily on its talented reckoner and his precise bookkeeping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calculator - a person or device that performs mathematical calculations

Etymology

From Middle English 'rekken' (to reckon, count), ultimately from Old English 'gerecenian'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛkənər / (AmE), / ˈrɛkənə / (BrE)
a handbook or set of tables designed to help with calculating or converting numbers
handbook of calculation tables

Examples

The engineer referred to a reckoner to convert inches to millimeters quickly.
Before calculators, people often used a reckoner for complex arithmetic.
My grandfather had a well-worn reckoner he used for financial estimates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calculator - a device or person that performs mathematical calculations
  • Synonym: logarithm table - a table for finding the logarithms of numbers to simplify calculations

Etymology

From Middle English 'rekener', from 'rekenen' (to reckon, count).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06435139-n
Synset POS: n
