colewort

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊlˌwɜrt / (AmE), / ˈkəʊlˌwɜːt / (BrE)
A hardy cabbage with coarse, curly leaves that do not form a head, often grown as a vegetable.
hardy cabbage with loose curly leaves

Examples

The chilly climate is perfect for growing colewort in the winter months.
She picked fresh colewort from the garden to use in her soup recipe.
Unlike regular cabbages, colewort does not form a tight head and its leaves remain open.

Etymology

From Old English 'cāl' (cabbage) + 'wort' (plant, herb).
