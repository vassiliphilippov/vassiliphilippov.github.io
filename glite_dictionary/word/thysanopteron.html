thysanopteron

1. NOUN

/ θaɪˌsænəpˈtɛrɑn / (AmE), / θaɪˌsanɒpˈtɛrən / (BrE)
An insect of the order Thysanoptera, characterized by slender bodies and fringed wings; commonly known as a thrips.
insect of the Thysanoptera order

Examples

The thysanopteron is often found feeding on plant sap in greenhouses.
Scientists studied the lifecycle of a thysanopteron under controlled laboratory conditions.
A severe infestation of thysanoptera can cause damage to crops and ornamental plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrips - small insect of Thysanoptera, common name

Etymology

From Greek thysanos (fringe) + pteron (wing), referring to the fringed wings of the insect.
