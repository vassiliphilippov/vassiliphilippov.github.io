Romeo

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊmioʊ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊmiəʊ / (BrE)
A passionate or ardent male lover, often used to refer to a man who is infatuated or very attentive to a romantic partner.
an ardent male lover

Examples

He fancied himself a Romeo after falling head over heels for his coworker.
The tabloids called the actor a real-life Romeo due to his many love affairs.
At school, he tried to play the Romeo by writing love poems to his crush.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suitor - a man who pursues a romantic relationship with someone
  • Synonym: Casanova - a man with a reputation for having many romantic relationships
  • Antonym: Juliet - a female lover, the partner of Romeo in Shakespeare's play

Etymology

From the name of the male protagonist in Shakespeare's play 'Romeo and Juliet'.
