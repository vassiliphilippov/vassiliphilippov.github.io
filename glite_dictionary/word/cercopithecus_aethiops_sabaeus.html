Cercopithecus aethiops sabaeus

1. NOUN

/ sɜːr.kəˈpɪθ.ɪ.kəs iːˈθaɪ.ɑps səˈbiː.əs / (AmE), / sɜː.kəˈpɪθ.ɪ.kəs iːˈθaɪ.ɒps səˈbiː.əs / (BrE)
A common savannah monkey of West Africa, characterized by a greenish-grey back and a yellow tail; commonly known as the green monkey.
West African green savannah monkey

Examples

The Cercopithecus aethiops sabaeus is often found in large groups on the West African savannah.
Researchers observed several Cercopithecus aethiops sabaeus individuals foraging for food near the river.
Local folklore features the Cercopithecus aethiops sabaeus as a clever and adaptable animal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: green monkey - common name for Cercopithecus aethiops sabaeus

Etymology

From Modern Latin: genus Cercopithecus (Greek kerkos, 'tail' + pithekos, 'ape'); species aethiops, 'Ethiopian'; subspecies sabaeus, 'of Saba'.
