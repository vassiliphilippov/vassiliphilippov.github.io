carnelian

1. NOUN

/ kɑːrˈniːliən / (AmE), / kɑːˈniːliən / (BrE)
a translucent red or orange variety of chalcedony, used as a semi-precious gemstone
a reddish-orange semi-precious gemstone

Examples

She wore a necklace adorned with polished carnelian beads.
The carnelian stone glowed warmly in the sunlight.
Ancient Egyptians often used carnelian for jewelry and amulets.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'cornelianus', from 'cornum' (cornel cherry), referring to the stone's color.
