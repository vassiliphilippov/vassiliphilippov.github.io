detail

1. NOUN

/ ˈdiː.teɪl, dɪˈteɪl / (AmE), / ˈdiː.teɪl, dɪˈteɪl / (BrE)
an individual fact, item, or element, especially one considered separately from the whole
individual fact or item

Examples

She remembered every little detail of the conversation.
The police are interested in even the smallest detail of the crime scene.
Could you provide more detail about what happened?

Synset Examples

several of the details are similar
a point of information

Related Terms

  • Synonym: particular - a specific detail or item
  • Antonym: generalization - a general statement, as opposed to a specific detail

Etymology

From French détail (a small item), from détailler 'to cut up'.

/ ˈdiː.teɪl / (AmE), / ˈdiː.teɪl / (BrE)
a group of people, especially soldiers, assigned to carry out a specific task or duty
group assigned to a specific task

Examples

A detail was assigned to guard the entrance throughout the night.
The cleanup detail finished the work ahead of schedule.
Each detail was given clear instructions before starting the project.

Synset Examples

Etymology

From French détail (itemized list, small part); sense of 'assigned group' arose in military usage.
