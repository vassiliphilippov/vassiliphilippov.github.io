citation

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / saɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
an official statement recognizing a person’s bravery or service, often given as a public award
official award or commendation

Examples

The soldier received a citation for his acts of bravery during the mission.
Her citation was presented at a special ceremony in recognition of her outstanding service.
He proudly displayed the citation he earned for exceptional performance in the line of duty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: commendation - an official praise or award for achievement

Etymology

From Latin 'citare' (to summon), via Old French 'citer'.

2. NOUN

/ saɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / saɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a passage or expression that is quoted, especially as an example or evidence
a quoted passage or expression

Examples

The article included a citation from a renowned scientist to support its claims.
Proper use of citation is essential in academic writing to give credit to original authors.
The judge referenced a legal citation during the trial.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quotation - a passage or phrase taken from a book, speech, or another text and repeated by someone other than the original author

Etymology

From Latin 'citare' (to summon, to call forward).

/ saɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / saɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
an official summons requiring a person to appear in court or at a legal proceeding
official summons to appear in court
legal

Examples

The judge issued a citation ordering the defendant to appear in court next week.
Failure to respond to the citation could result in a warrant for her arrest.
He was served with a citation after violating the city ordinance.

Etymology

From Latin 'citare' (to summon, call).

4. NOUN

/ saɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / saɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a reference to a source of information, especially in academic writing, acknowledging where information, ideas, or quotations originate
reference to a source of information

Examples

The essay contained a citation for every source the student used.
She forgot to include a citation for the statistics in her report.
Proper citation is required in all academic papers.

Synset Examples

the student's essay failed to list several important citations

the article includes mention of similar clinical cases

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reference - mention of a source of information

Etymology

From Latin 'citare' (to summon, to cite), via Old French 'citation'.

5. NOUN

/ saɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / saɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of quoting or referring to a spoken or written source, such as a legal precedent, passage, or statement
the act of quoting or referring to a source

Examples

The lawyer included a citation from a recent Supreme Court case to support her argument.
In academic writing, every citation must correspond to a source in the bibliography.
The judge dismissed the claim due to the lack of proper legal citations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quote - to repeat or copy out (a passage or statement)
  • Synonym: reference - the act of mentioning or alluding to something
  • Antonym: paraphrase - to restate a text or passage in other words

Etymology

From Latin 'citare' (to summon, to cite), past participle 'citatus'.
