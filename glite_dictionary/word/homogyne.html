Homogyne

1. NOUN

/ hoʊˈmɑːdʒiːn / (AmE), / həʊˈmɒdʒiːn / (BrE)
A small genus of perennial herbs native to mountainous regions of Europe, sometimes classified within the genus Tussilago.
genus of European perennial herbs

Examples

The Homogyne genus includes several species commonly found in alpine meadows.
Botanists debated whether Homogyne should be classified within the Tussilago genus.
You can identify Homogyne by its low growth and distinctive leaves.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining Greek 'homo-' (same) and 'gyne' (woman, female), referring to botanical characteristics.

2. NOUN

/ hoʊˈmɑːdʒiːn / (AmE), / hɒˈmɒdʒiːn / (BrE)
A small genus of low, perennial herbs native to montane regions of Europe, sometimes classified within the genus Tussilago.
genus of European montane herbs

Examples

The Homogyne genus is distinguished by its small, ground-hugging growth in alpine meadows.
Botanists debated whether Homogyne should be treated as part of the genus Tussilago.
Several species in the Homogyne genus are adapted to harsh, mountainous climates.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin 'Homogyne', formed from Greek 'homo-' (same) and 'gyne' (female), referring to botanical features.
