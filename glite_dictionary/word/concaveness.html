concaveness

1. NOUN

/ kənˈkeɪvnəs / (AmE), / kɒnˈkeɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being concave; the property of curving inward like the interior of a bowl
the state of being concave

Examples

The concaveness of the lens allows it to focus light efficiently.
Architects often use concaveness in modern structures to create dramatic visual effects.
The slight concaveness of the bowl helps prevent spills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concavity - the state or quality of being concave
  • Antonym: convexity - the state or quality of being convex (curving outward)

Etymology

From concave + -ness (forming nouns expressing a state or quality).
