pipistrelle

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɪpɪˈstrɛl / (AmE), / ˌpɪpɪˈstrɛl / (BrE)
a small, brown bat native to Europe and parts of Asia, known for its swift, agile flight, especially at dusk
small European brown bat

Examples

We saw a pipistrelle darting among the trees at sunset.
The pipistrelle uses echolocation to hunt insects during the night.
Conservationists are monitoring pipistrelle populations to ensure their habitats are protected.

Etymology

From French *pipistrelle*, from Italian *pipistrello* (bat), ultimately from Latin *vespertilio* (bat).
