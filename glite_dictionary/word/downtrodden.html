downtrodden

1. ADJ

/ ˈdaʊnˌtrɑdən / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnˌtrɒdn̩ / (BrE)
oppressed or treated badly by people in authority or those with power
oppressed; treated badly by those in power

Examples

The charity works to help the downtrodden and give them a voice in society.
Stories about the downtrodden often highlight issues of injustice and inequality.
He never forgot his roots or lost sympathy for the downtrodden around him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oppressed - treated in a cruel or unfair way, especially by a government or authority

Etymology

From 'down' + past participle of 'tread' (Old English 'tredan', to step).
