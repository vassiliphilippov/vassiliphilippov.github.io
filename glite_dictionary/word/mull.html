Mull

1. PROPN

/ mʌl / (AmE), / mʌl / (BrE)
an island in western Scotland, part of the Inner Hebrides, known for its rugged scenery and wildlife
island in western Scotland

Examples

She spent her summer holiday hiking across Mull and exploring its coastline.
The ferry to Mull departs from Oban several times a day.
Wildlife enthusiasts visit Mull to see eagles and otters in their natural habitat.

Etymology

From Scottish Gaelic 'Muile', the name for the island.
