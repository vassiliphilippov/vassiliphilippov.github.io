investigatory

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈvɛstəˌɡeɪˌtɔri / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɛstɪɡətəri / (BrE)
intended or designed to find information, examine facts, or conduct an investigation.
intended to find information or examine facts

Examples

The police opened an investigatory file to look into the allegations.
The committee was granted investigatory powers to question witnesses and demand documents.
Journalists carried out an investigatory report into the company's financial dealings.

Synset Examples

a fact-finding committee
investigative reporting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: investigative - relating to or designed to investigate; synonym for 'investigatory'

Etymology

From Latin investigare (to examine into, search for) through English 'investigate' + -ory (forming adjectives meaning 'relating to').
