granadilla

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡræn.əˈdɪl.ə / (AmE), / ˌɡræn.əˈdɪl.ə / (BrE)
A type of passionflower native to Brazil, cultivated for its edible deep purple fruit.
Brazilian passionflower with purple fruit

Examples

The farmer exported crates of ripe granadilla to markets across the country.
The sweet pulp of the granadilla is often used in desserts and drinks.
Although less well-known than other passionfruits, the granadilla is prized for its refreshing taste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: passionfruit - the fruit of the passionflower, especially Passiflora edulis

Etymology

From Spanish 'granadilla', diminutive of 'granada' meaning 'pomegranate'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɡrænəˈdɪlə / (AmE), / ˌɡranəˈdɪlə / (BrE)
A tropical climbing plant of the passionflower family that bears a large, edible fruit with sweet pulp, considered among the best of its kind.
tropical fruit of certain passionflowers

Examples

The sweet, aromatic pulp of the granadilla is prized in many tropical cuisines.
She cut open a ripe granadilla and served it chilled for dessert.
Farmers in the region grow granadillas for both local consumption and export.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: passion fruit - fruit of various passionflower species, closely related or often confused with granadilla

Etymology

From Spanish 'granadilla', diminutive of 'granada' (pomegranate), referring to the fruit's appearance.

3. NOUN

/ ˌɡrænəˈdɪliə / (AmE), / ˌɡranəˈdɪlɪə / (BrE)
An egg-shaped edible fruit of certain tropical American vines related to the passionflower, often with a sweet or tart flavor and a seedy pulp.
edible tropical fruit related to passionflower

Examples

The refreshing juice of the granadilla made it a popular drink in tropical countries.
She cut open the granadilla to scoop out its sweet, aromatic pulp.
Farmers in South America often grow granadilla alongside coffee and bananas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: passion fruit - the fruit of another species in the passionflower family, also edible and similar in use

Etymology

From Spanish 'granadilla', diminutive of 'granada' (pomegranate).

4. NOUN

/ ˌɡrænəˈdɪliə / (AmE), / ˌɡranəˈdɪlɪə / (BrE)
A tropical American passionflower plant that produces a large, edible fruit with a sweet, aromatic pulp.
tropical American fruit-bearing passionflower

Examples

The ripe granadilla is prized for its sweet, aromatic pulp.
We tasted granadilla for the first time on our trip to South America.
The vines of the granadilla plant spread quickly in warm climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: passionfruit - the fruit of several plants of the genus Passiflora, similar to granadilla

From Spanish 'granadilla', diminutive of 'granada' (pomegranate).
