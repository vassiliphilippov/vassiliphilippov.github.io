spoonbill

1. NOUN

/ ˈspuːnˌbɪl / (AmE), / ˈspuːnˌbɪl / (BrE)
a large wading bird with a long, flat bill that has a broad, spoon-shaped tip, found in warm regions worldwide
large wading bird with a spoon-shaped bill

Examples

The spoonbill swept its unusual beak through the shallow water, searching for food.
Birdwatchers spotted a rare Eurasian spoonbill in the wetlands yesterday.
The long, flat bill of a spoonbill makes it easy for the bird to catch small fish and crustaceans.

Etymology

From 'spoon' + 'bill', referring to the bird's distinctive bill shape.
