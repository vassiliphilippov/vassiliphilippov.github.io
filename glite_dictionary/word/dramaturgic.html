dramaturgic

1. ADJ

/ ˌdræməˈtɜrdʒɪk / (AmE), / ˌdrɑːməˈtɜːdʒɪk / (BrE)
relating to the technical or compositional aspects of drama or dramatic art.
concerning drama or theatrical composition

Examples

She offered dramaturgic advice to improve the pacing and structure of the play.
His dramaturgic expertise made the production more coherent and engaging for the audience.
The class analyzed the dramaturgic elements of Shakespearean tragedy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dramaturgical - relating to the art or technique of dramatic composition; alternative adjectival form

Etymology

From Greek dramatourgikos, from drama (action, play) + -ourgikos (relating to work or production).
