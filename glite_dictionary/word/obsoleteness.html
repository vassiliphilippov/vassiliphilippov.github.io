obsoleteness

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑb.səˈliːt.nəs / (AmE), / ˌɒb.səˈliːt.nəs / (BrE)
The state or quality of being obsolete or out of date.
state of being obsolete

Examples

The obsoleteness of the equipment became clear when it failed during the critical operation.
Many companies are working to avoid the obsoleteness of their products through continual innovation.
Historians often study the obsoleteness of certain customs in order to understand societal change.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obsolescence - the process of becoming obsolete or outdated

Etymology

From obsolete + -ness, formed on the pattern of English abstract nouns.
