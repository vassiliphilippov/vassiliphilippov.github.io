Caltha

1. PROPN

/ ˈkælθə / (AmE), / ˈkælθə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the buttercup family (Ranunculaceae), known for species such as the marsh marigold.
genus of flowering plants (buttercup family)

Examples

The Caltha grows naturally in wetlands and damp meadows across the Northern Hemisphere.
Gardeners admire the bright yellow flowers of Caltha during early spring.
Botanists often study Caltha for its adaptation to waterlogged soils.

Etymology

From Latin 'caltha', meaning marigold, originally from ancient Greek 'kálthē' (a yellow flower).
