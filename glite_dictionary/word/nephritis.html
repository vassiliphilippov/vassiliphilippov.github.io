nephritis

1. NOUN

/ nɛˈfraɪtɪs / (AmE), / nɛˈfraɪtɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of the kidneys, often caused by infection, toxins, or autoimmune disease
inflammation of the kidneys
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with nephritis after she reported swelling and fatigue.
If left untreated, nephritis can lead to serious kidney damage.
Symptoms such as dark urine and high blood pressure may indicate nephritis.

Etymology

From Greek nephros (kidney) + -itis (inflammation).
