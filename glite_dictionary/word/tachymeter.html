tachymeter

1. NOUN

/ təˈkɪmətər / (AmE), / təˈkɪmɪtə / (BrE)
a type of theodolite used for rapid measurement of distance, especially in surveying
theodolite for rapid distance measurement

Examples

The surveyor used a tachymeter to quickly map out the construction site.
Modern tachymeters allow for efficient measurement even in difficult terrain.
Training in the use of a tachymeter is essential for field surveyors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theodolite - an instrument for measuring horizontal and vertical angles

Etymology

From Greek 'takhys' (swift) + 'metron' (measure), via French or German.
