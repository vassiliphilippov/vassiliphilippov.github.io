to unstrap

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈstræp / (AmE), / ʌnˈstræp / (BrE)
to remove the strap or straps from something
remove the strap or straps from

Examples

He hurriedly unstrapped his helmet after the race ended.
She unstrapped the baby from the car seat and carried her inside.
Before getting off the roller coaster, everyone must unstrap their safety harness.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: strap - to fasten or secure with a strap

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (meaning 'to reverse') + 'strap'.
