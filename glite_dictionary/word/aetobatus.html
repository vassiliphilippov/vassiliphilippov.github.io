Aetobatus

1. NOUN

/ iːˈtoʊbətəs / (AmE), / iːˈtɒbətəs / (BrE)
A genus of eagle rays in the family Myliobatidae, found in the Atlantic, Pacific, and Indian Oceans.
a genus of eagle rays

Examples

The spotted eagle ray belongs to the genus Aetobatus and is found in tropical waters worldwide.
Researchers studied the anatomy of several Aetobatus species to better understand their evolution.
Members of Aetobatus are known for their distinctive spotted patterns and wide pectoral fins.

Etymology

From Greek 'aetos' meaning 'eagle' and 'batis' meaning 'ray'.
