blastematic

1. ADJ

/ blæstəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / blɑːstəˈmætɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of blastemata, which are masses of undifferentiated cells capable of growth and regeneration
relating to blastemata (cell masses)
medical

Examples

The tumor showed distinct blastematic features under microscopic examination.
During limb regeneration, blastematic tissue forms at the site of injury.
Some researchers are studying the blastematic potential of stem cells in organ repair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blastemal - relating to or consisting of a blastema; tissue type

Etymology

From Greek blastēma (germ, sprout) + -atic, from -atikos.
