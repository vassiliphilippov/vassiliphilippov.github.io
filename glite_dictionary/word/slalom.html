slalom

1. VERB

/ ˈslɑːləm / (AmE), / ˈslɑːləm / (BrE)
to move or race in a winding, zigzag course around obstacles, especially while skiing
move in a zigzag, especially on skis

Examples

She expertly slalomed down the mountain, weaving between the flags.
The children loved to slalom around the cones on their rollerblades.
During the competition, he managed to slalom through the gates without missing any.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zigzag - to move in a series of sharp turns in alternating directions

Etymology

From Norwegian 'slalåm', meaning a winding ski race course.

2. NOUN

/ ˈslɑːləm / (AmE), / ˈslɑːləm / (BrE)
a downhill race in which competitors navigate a winding course marked by upright poles, typically on snow skis or similar vehicles
downhill race with winding course marked by poles

Examples

She completed the slalom course in record time at the Olympics.
The skier zigzagged through the poles during the challenging slalom.
Learning to control your speed is crucial for a beginner in slalom skiing.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: giant slalom - a slalom race with more widely spaced poles
  • Degree Lower: downhill - an alpine skiing race on a straight, steep course

Etymology

From Norwegian 'slalåm' meaning 'ski race over a winding course.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07478907-n
Synset POS: n
