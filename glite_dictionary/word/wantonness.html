wantonness

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑːntənnəs / (AmE), / ˈwɒntənnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of lacking restraint or control; reckless freedom from inhibition or worry
lack of restraint or control

Examples

His wantonness led him to ignore the consequences of his actions.
The wantonness of their celebrations shocked many in the quiet town.
She was criticized for the wantonness with which she spent her inheritance.

Synset Examples

she danced with abandon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abandon - reckless freedom from inhibition or worry
  • Antonym: restraint - self-control and discipline

Etymology

From Old English 'wanten' (to be deficient, lack), related to 'wanton.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɑntənnəs / (AmE), / ˈwɒntənnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being sexually immodest, lewd, or morally unrestrained
lewdness; sexual or moral recklessness

Examples

The film was criticized for the sheer wantonness of its explicit scenes.
She disapproved of his wantonness and reckless disregard for social norms.
Many viewed his actions as the wantonness of an undisciplined character.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lewdness - the state of being sexually indecent or obscene
  • Antonym: modesty - the quality of being modest or reserved, especially regarding sexuality

Etymology

From Middle English wantonnes, from wanton (reckless, lewd) + -ness.
