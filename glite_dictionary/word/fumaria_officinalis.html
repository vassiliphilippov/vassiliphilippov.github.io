Fumaria officinalis

1. NOUN

/ fjuːˌmɛəriə ˌɒfɪˈsɪnəlɪs / (AmE), / fjuːˌmeəriə ˌɒfɪˈsɪnəlɪs / (BrE)
a delicate European herb with soft grey-green leaves and spikes of small purplish flowers, formerly used in herbal medicine
a European herb with purplish flowers

Examples

Fumaria officinalis is often found growing wild in gardens and cultivated fields.
Herbalists once used Fumaria officinalis to treat a variety of ailments, though it is rarely used today.
The delicate pink flowers of Fumaria officinalis add a splash of color to the countryside in early summer.

Etymology

From Latin: fumaria meaning 'smoke of the earth' and officinalis meaning 'of the workshop (herbs/medicines)'.
