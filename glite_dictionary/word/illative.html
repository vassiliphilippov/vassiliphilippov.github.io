illative

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪlətɪv / (AmE), / ˈɪlətɪv / (BrE)
relating to or depending on inference; reached by reasoning rather than by direct evidence.
relating to inference or reasoning

Examples

The argument reached an illative conclusion based on previous evidence.
His illative reasoning impressed the members of the committee.
The court's decision was not direct but rather illative, inferred from several testimonies.

Synset Examples

an illative conclusion
inferential reasoning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inferential - relating to inference or reasoning

Etymology

From Latin illativus ('that brings in'), from illatus, past participle of inferre ('to bring in, infer').

3. ADJ

/ ˈɪlətɪv / (AmE), / ˈɪlətɪv / (BrE)
(of a word or phrase) expressing or introducing an inference; indicating that something follows from what has been said.
expressing inference or deduction

Examples

The word therefore is often used as an illative conjunction in formal writing.
In logical arguments, illative indicators help clarify the relationship between statements.
His analysis relied on several illative expressions to explain the conclusions drawn.
