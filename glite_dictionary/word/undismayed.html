undismayed

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.dɪsˈmeɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.dɪsˈmeɪd / (BrE)
not discouraged or shaken in purpose, especially in difficult situations.
not discouraged or unshaken in purpose

Examples

She remained undismayed even after hearing the disappointing news.
The hikers, though tired, were undismayed by the rough terrain ahead.
He was undismayed by the critics' negative reviews and continued with his work.

Synset Examples

wholly undismayed by the commercial failure of the three movies he had made

Related Terms

  • Synonym: undaunted - not intimidated or discouraged by difficulty, danger, or disappointment
  • Antonym: dismayed - having lost courage or resolution; discouraged

Etymology

Formed with the prefix 'un-' meaning 'not' and 'dismayed' from Old French 'esmaier' (to frighten, dismay).
