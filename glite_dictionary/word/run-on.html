run-on

1. ADJ

/ ˈrʌn ɑn / (AmE), / ˈrʌn ɒn / (BrE)
(of verse) continuing without a pause at the end of a line, causing the sense to flow into the next line.
of verse, continuing onto next line without pause

Examples

The poet frequently uses run-on lines to create a sense of movement.
This passage is notable for its run-on structure, which connects several images.
Her poems feature run-on sentences that ignore normal line breaks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enjambment - the continuation of a sentence without a pause beyond the end of a line of poetry
