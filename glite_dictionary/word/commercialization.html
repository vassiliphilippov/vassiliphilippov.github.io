commercialization

1. NOUN

/ kəˌmɜːrʃələˌzeɪʃən / (AmE), / kəˌmɜːʃəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of managing or running something principally for financial gain; bringing something into commercial use or trade
the act of making something commercial

Examples

The commercialization of space travel is rapidly changing the aerospace industry.
Many critics argue that the commercialization of sports damages their traditional values.
The commercialization of new technologies often leads to widespread innovation.

Synset Examples

my father considered the commercialization of Christmas to be a sacrilege
the government tried to accelerate the commercialization of this development
both companies will retain control over the commercialization of their own products

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to commercialize - to manage or exploit for profit

Etymology

From 'commercialize' + '-ation'; based on 'commerce' from Latin 'commercium'.
