imprisoned

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈprɪzənd / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɪzənd / (BrE)
kept in prison or in captivity; deprived of freedom.
kept in prison or captivity

Examples

After the trial, he was imprisoned for ten years for his crimes.
Many political dissidents have been imprisoned without a fair trial.
She felt imprisoned by the strict rules of her family.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: free - not imprisoned or confined; at liberty
  • Synonym: incarcerated - kept in prison

Etymology

From 'imprison' + '-ed'; ultimately from Old French 'emprisoner'.
