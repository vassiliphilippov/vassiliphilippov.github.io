municipal

1. ADJ

/ mjʊˈnɪsəpəl / (AmE), / mjuːˈnɪs.ɪ.pəl / (BrE)
Relating to a city, town, or local government, or to its governing body.
of or relating to a municipality or local government

Examples

The town council approved a new municipal swimming pool for the community.
He invested his money in municipal bonds issued by the city government.
The municipal elections will decide who manages the local services and infrastructure.

Synset Examples

municipal government
municipal bonds
a municipal park
municipal transportation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: civic - relating to a city or town, especially its administration; municipal

Etymology

From Latin 'municipalis', from 'municipium' (free town, municipality).

2. ADJ

/ mjuːˈnɪsɪpəl / (AmE), / mjuːˈnɪsɪpəl / (BrE)
relating to a city or town or its local government.
of a city or local government

Examples

The municipal government is responsible for maintaining local roads and public parks.
Residents pay taxes to support municipal services such as water supply and waste collection.
Elections for municipal offices are often held separately from national elections.

Synset Examples

international law … only authorizes a belligerent to punish a spy under its municipal law

Related Terms

  • Synonym: civic - relating to a city or town, especially its administration; synonym for 'municipal'

Etymology

From Latin 'municipalis' (of a citizen of a free town), from 'municipium' (town with self-government).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01042262-s
Synset POS: s
