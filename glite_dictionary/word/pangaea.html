Pangaea

1. NOUN

/ pænˈdʒiːə / (AmE), / panˈdʒiːə / (BrE)
A hypothetical supercontinent that included all Earth's landmasses before breaking apart during the Triassic period.
ancient supercontinent containing all landmasses

Examples

Millions of years ago, Pangaea began to break apart, eventually forming today's continents.
The theory of Pangaea helps explain similarities in fossil records across different continents.
Scientists believe that Earth’s climate was very different when Pangaea existed.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Laurasia - northern part of Pangaea after it split
  • Degree Higher: Gondwanaland - southern part of Pangaea after it split

Etymology

From Greek 'pan' (all) + 'Gaia' (earth, goddess of the Earth).
