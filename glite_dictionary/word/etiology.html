etiology

1. NOUN

/ ˌitiˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌiːtiˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The philosophical study of the origins or causes of things, especially as a branch of philosophy concerned with causation.
study of causation (philosophy)

Examples

He devoted his doctoral research to the etiology of natural phenomena.
Discussions about the etiology of the universe are central to many philosophical traditions.
The course covers both historical and contemporary approaches to etiology in philosophy.

Etymology

From Greek aitiologia, from aitia (cause) + -logia (study of).

2. NOUN

/ ˌiːtiˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌiːtɪˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the cause or origin of a disease or abnormal condition
cause or origin of a disease
medical

Examples

The etiology of the infection remained unknown despite numerous tests.
Doctors are studying the etiology of the new virus to find effective treatments.
Understanding the etiology of diabetes is crucial for developing prevention strategies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pathogenesis - the manner of development of a disease

Etymology

From Greek 'aitiologia', from 'aitia' (cause) + 'logia' (study).
