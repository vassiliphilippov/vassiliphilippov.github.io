salariat

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæləriˈæt / (AmE), / ˌsæləriˈæt / (BrE)
the class of workers who receive regular salaries rather than hourly wages; salaried employees as a group
class of salaried employees

Examples

The salariat has seen significant changes in job security over recent decades.
Membership in the salariat often brings additional benefits like health insurance and pensions.
As automation increased, the size of the salariat grew within the corporate structure.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: proletariat - the working class, especially industrial wage earners

Etymology

From French 'salariat', from 'salaire' (salary).
