dandy

1. NOUN

/ ˈdændi / (AmE), / ˈdandi / (BrE)
A sailing vessel with two masts, where a small mizzen mast is placed aft of the rudderpost.
two-masted sailing vessel

Examples

The old captain restored a dandy he found abandoned in the shipyard.
A dandy typically had a distinctive rig with its mizzen mast set behind the rudderpost.
Fishermen valued the dandy for its manoeuvrability and ease of handling in coastal waters.

3. NOUN

/ ˈdændi / (AmE), / ˈdændi / (BrE)
a man who pays great attention to his appearance and dress, often in an elegant or ostentatious way
fashion-conscious man

Examples

He considers himself a dandy, always stepping out in the latest designer suits.
In Victorian England, being a dandy was a sign of high social standing.
The dandy spent hours every morning perfecting his cravat and coiffure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fop - a man excessively concerned with his clothes and appearance
  • Synonym: beau - a fashionable man; a rich, stylish man

Etymology

From late 18th-century Scottish dialect 'dandie' (fastidious man).
