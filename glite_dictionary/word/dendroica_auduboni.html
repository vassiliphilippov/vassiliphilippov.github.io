Dendroica auduboni

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɛn.droʊˈaɪ.kə ˌɔ.dʊˈboʊ.ni / (AmE), / ˌdɛn.drɔɪˈiː.kə ˌɔː.djuːˈbəʊ.ni / (BrE)
A species of warbler commonly found in western North America, also known as the Audubon's warbler.
a warbler species of western North America

Examples

The Dendroica auduboni is a favorite among birdwatchers during spring migration in California.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Dendroica auduboni in the Rocky Mountains.
You can often spot Dendroica auduboni flitting among pine trees searching for insects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Audubon's warbler - common name for Dendroica auduboni

Etymology

Named after John James Audubon, American ornithologist and naturalist.
