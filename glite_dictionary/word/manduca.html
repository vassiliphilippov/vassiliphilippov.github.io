Manduca

1. NOUN

/ mænˈduːkə / (AmE), / mænˈdjuːkə / (BrE)
A genus of large moths whose larvae are commonly known as tobacco hornworms or tomato hornworms.
genus of moths (hornworms)

Examples

The larvae of Manduca are notorious for defoliating tomato plants in the garden.
Researchers studied the life cycle of Manduca to better understand insect development.
Gardeners often mistake Manduca caterpillars for other types of hornworms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sphinx - genus of sphinx moths related to Manduca

Etymology

From Latin mandūca (glutton, from manducare 'to chew'), alluding to the caterpillar's voracious feeding.

2. NOUN

/ mænˈduːkə / (AmE), / mænˈdjuːkə / (BrE)
a genus of large moths whose larvae are known as tobacco hornworms and tomato hornworms
genus of large moths (hornworm caterpillars)

Examples

The caterpillars of the Manduca genus are common pests on tomato plants.
Scientists studied Manduca moths to understand their development cycle.
The Manduca larvae are often called hornworms due to the horn-like projection on their tails.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hornworm - the large caterpillar larva of certain moths, including those in the genus Manduca

Etymology

From Latin Manduca, name given by naturalists for the genus.
