to gnash

1. VERB

/ næʃ / (AmE), / næʃ / (BrE)
(of teeth) to grind together, usually as a sign of anger or pain.
grind teeth together, show anger or pain

Examples

He could not help but gnash his teeth in frustration.
The villain gnashed his teeth when his plan failed.
She woke up in the night, gnashing her teeth from the pain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grind - to crush or break something into very small pieces or powder by rubbing it between two hard surfaces

Etymology

From Middle English 'gnaschen', possibly of imitative origin.
