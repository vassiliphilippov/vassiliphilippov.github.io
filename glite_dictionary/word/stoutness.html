stoutness

1. NOUN

/ ˈstaʊtnəs / (AmE), / ˈstaʊtnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being strong, courageous, and determined in attitude or character
strength and determination of character

Examples

Her stoutness in the face of adversity inspired everyone around her.
The team's stoutness during the final minutes secured their victory.
He admired the stoutness with which she defended her beliefs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fortitude - strength of mind that enables a person to face adversity
  • Antonym: weakness - the lack of strength or determination

Etymology

From Old French 'estout' (bold, proud), from Vulgar Latin '*extotare'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstaʊtnəs / (AmE), / ˈstaʊtnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being somewhat fat or having a heavy, solid body
quality of being somewhat fat

Examples

His stoutness was the result of years of little exercise and heavy meals.
Despite his stoutness, he was remarkably agile on the dance floor.
Doctors warned that increasing stoutness could lead to health complications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obesity - the condition of being very fat or overweight
  • Antonym: thinness - the quality of being thin or slender

Etymology

From Middle English 'stout', from Old French, ultimately from Latin 'stultus' (foolish), with meaning shift in English.
