soupiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuː.pinəs / (AmE), / ˈsuː.pinɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being thick or having the texture of heavy cream, like soup
thickness like heavy cream

Examples

The soupiness of the chowder made it incredibly satisfying on a cold night.
Her recipe achieved just the right soupiness without becoming too thick or gloopy.
If the sauce lacks soupiness, try simmering it longer to improve the texture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: soupy - having a consistency like soup; thick or creamy
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04943867-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈsuːpinəs / (AmE), / ˈsuːpinɪs / (BrE)
excessive or insincerely sentimental quality, especially in art or writing
excessive sentimentality

Examples

The soupiness of the film's final scene left many critics unimpressed.
Some readers disliked the soupiness in her latest novel, finding it overly sentimental.
The director tried to avoid any soupiness in the portrayal of the characters' emotions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maudlin - excessively or tearfully sentimental
