Chasidim

1. NOUN

/ xɑːˈsiːdɪm / (AmE), / xaˈsiːdɪm / (BrE)
A sect of Orthodox Jews originating in eastern Europe in the late 18th century, noted for strict observance of Mosaic law and distinct religious practices.
Jewish religious sect from 18th-century eastern Europe

Examples

The Chasidim gather every year to celebrate their founder's teachings.
Many Chasidim are easily recognized by their distinctive dress and rituals.
The beliefs of the Chasidim emphasize joy and devotion in daily religious life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hasidim - variant spelling of Chasidim, Jewish religious sect

Etymology

From Hebrew 'ḥasidim' (plural of 'ḥasid', meaning 'pious').
