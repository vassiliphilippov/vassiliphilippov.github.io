paragon

1. NOUN

/ ˈpær.əˌɡɑn / (AmE), / ˈpar.ə.ɡən / (BrE)
a person or thing regarded as a perfect example of a particular quality; a model of excellence or perfection
model of excellence or perfection

Examples

She was considered a paragon of virtue by everyone who knew her.
The chef's new restaurant is seen as a paragon of fine dining in the city.
He became the paragon against whom all other athletes were measured.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exemplar - a person or thing serving as a typical example or excellent model
  • Synonym: ideal - a person or thing regarded as perfect
  • Antonym: mediocrity - the quality of being average or ordinary, not outstanding

Etymology

From Old Italian 'paragone' (touchstone used to test gold), from Greek 'parakonan' (to sharpen).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpærəˌɡɑn / (AmE), / ˈpærəɡən / (BrE)
a person or thing regarded as a perfect example of a particular quality or concept
perfect example of a quality

Examples

She is considered the paragon of virtue in her community.
His artwork is a paragon of modern sculpture, admired by critics worldwide.
The company sought to become a paragon of sustainability in the industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exemplar - a typical or excellent example

Etymology

From Old Italian paragone, meaning 'touchstone', from Greek parakonē (touchstone used to test gold).
