Diodontidae

1. NOUN

/ daɪ.oʊˈdɒntɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌdʌɪ.ə(ʊ)ˈdɒntɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine fishes known as spiny puffers or porcupinefishes, characterized by their ability to inflate and their bodies covered with spines.
family of spiny puffers (porcupinefishes)

Examples

The Diodontidae are well known for inflating their bodies as a defense mechanism.
Biologists study the Diodontidae to learn more about unique adaptations among marine fish.
Members of the Diodontidae family are commonly referred to as porcupinefishes due to their sharp spines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porcupinefish - Common name for fish in the family Diodontidae

Etymology

From Greek 'di' (two) + 'odont' (tooth), referring to the fused teeth characteristic of the family, with Latin scientific suffix '-idae' for animal families.

2. NOUN

/ daɪ.oʊˈdɒn.tɪ.di / (AmE), / daɪ.əʊˈdɒn.tɪ.di / (BrE)
A family of marine fishes known as spiny puffers, characterized by their ability to inflate their bodies and their spiny skin.
family of spiny puffers

Examples

The Diodontidae are found in tropical and subtropical oceans around the world.
Members of the Diodontidae can inflate their bodies when threatened.
Researchers studied the defense mechanisms of several species within the Diodontidae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pufferfish - common name for various fish that inflate themselves as a defense

Etymology

From Greek 'di-' (two) + 'odous' (tooth), referring to their two fused teeth, used in the scientific family name.
