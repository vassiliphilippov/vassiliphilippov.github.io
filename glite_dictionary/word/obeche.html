obeche

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈbiːtʃi / (AmE), / əʊˈbiːtʃi / (BrE)
The pale, lightweight wood of an African tree, used especially for making veneers and plywood.
light African wood for veneers and plywood

Examples

The table was crafted from obeche to achieve a light, modern look.
Because obeche is easy to work with, it’s popular for model-making and furniture.
They chose obeche veneer to give the cabinets a smooth, pale finish.

Etymology

From Yoruba òbẹ́chẹ́, the name of the tree in West Africa.

2. NOUN

/ oʊˈbiː.tʃi / (AmE), / əˈbiː.tʃi / (BrE)
A large West African tree (Triplochiton scleroxylon) with broad, lobed leaves, small white flowers, and soft pale wood often used for plywood and veneers.
West African tree with soft pale wood

Examples

The lightweight wood of obeche is highly prized in the manufacture of plywood and furniture.
Carpenters often choose obeche because it is easy to work and takes stain well.
The leaves of the obeche tree are large and distinctively lobed, making it easy to identify in the forest.
