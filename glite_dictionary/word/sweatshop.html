sweatshop

1. NOUN

/ ˈswɛtˌʃɑp / (AmE), / ˈswɛtʃɒp / (BrE)
a factory or workshop, especially in the clothing industry, where workers are employed at very low wages and under poor or unsafe conditions
factory with poor, exploitative working conditions

Examples

The workers in the sweatshop endured long hours in dangerous conditions for little pay.
New regulations aim to combat the spread of sweatshops in the garment industry.
Many consumers are unaware that their clothes may have been made in a sweatshop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: factory - a building where goods are manufactured
  • Synonym: atelier - a workshop or studio, especially for an artist or designer

Etymology

From 'sweat' (to work hard, often in difficult conditions) + 'shop' (workplace), first used in the late 19th century.
