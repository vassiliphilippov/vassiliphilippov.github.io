deactivation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdiːˌæktɪˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːˌæktɪˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of disbanding or breaking up a military unit, typically by transferring or discharging its members.
disbanding of a military unit

Examples

After decades of service, the deactivation of the regiment was marked by a formal ceremony.
The deactivation of several bases followed the end of the conflict.
Personnel were reassigned during the deactivation of the military unit.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: activation - the process of making something active, especially a military unit being brought into service

Etymology

From de- (reversal) + activation; patterned after 'deactivate' + '-ion'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌdiːˌæktɪˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːˌæktɪˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something inactive or ineffective, especially by turning off power or neutralizing a device
making something inactive or ineffective

Examples

The deactivation of the alarm system was completed before the police arrived.
Proper deactivation of the old software is required before installing the new version.
Bomb squads are trained for the safe deactivation of explosive devices.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: activation - the act of making something active or operative

Etymology

From de- (removal, reversal) + activation.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00234481-n
Synset POS: n
