swish

1. VERB

/ swɪʃ / (AmE), / swɪʃ / (BrE)
to move or cause to move with a whistling or hissing sound, often with a smooth, sweeping motion
move with a whistling or hissing sound

Examples

The long dress swished elegantly as she walked across the room.
He swished his cane through the air, making a sharp sound.
The cat's tail swished back and forth in agitation.

Synset Examples

The bubbles swoshed around in the glass
The curtain swooshed open

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whoosh - move very quickly with a rushing sound

Etymology

Imitative origin, from the sound of something moving swiftly through air or liquid.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02192644-v
Synset POS: v

2. ADJ

/ swɪʃ / (AmE), / swɪʃ / (BrE)
Elegant, stylish, and fashionable; having an air of sophistication or luxury.
elegant and fashionable

Examples

The new hotel lobby has a distinctly swish design that impresses every guest who enters.
She arrived at the party wearing a swish black cocktail dress paired with diamond earrings.
He took her to a swish restaurant renowned for its gourmet cuisine and modern décor.

Synset Examples

classy clothes
a classy dame
a posh restaurant
a swish pastry shop on the Rue du Bac

Related Terms

  • Synonym: classy - stylish and sophisticated
  • Synonym: posh - elegant or stylishly luxurious
  • Antonym: shabby - in poor condition or not fashionable
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00978969-s
Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ swɪʃ / (AmE), / swɪʃ / (BrE)
a soft, brushing or rustling sound, often produced by something moving quickly through the air or lightly touching a surface
a soft brushing or rustling sound

Examples

The swish of the silk dress was barely audible as she walked across the room.
I heard the swish of the basketball going through the net.
The gentle swish of leaves in the breeze soothed him to sleep.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rustle - a soft, muffled crackling sound like that made by dry leaves or paper
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07411014-n
Synset POS: n
