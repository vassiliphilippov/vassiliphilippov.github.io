niblick

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɪblɪk / (AmE), / ˈnɪblɪk / (BrE)
an iron golf club with a heavy, lofted face, formerly used for short, high shots
old golf club with lofted face
archaic

Examples

He pulled out his niblick to escape the deep bunker near the green.
In early golf, the niblick was used for tricky, high-trajectory shots.
Collectors often display antique niblicks alongside other historic golfing equipment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wedge - a modern golf club used for short, high shots
  • Antonym: putter - a golf club with very little loft, used on the green

Etymology

Of unknown origin; used in late 19th and early 20th century golf in Scotland.
