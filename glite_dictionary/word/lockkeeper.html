lockkeeper

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑkˌkiːpər / (AmE), / ˈlɒkˌkiːpə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to operate and look after a lock on a canal or river
person operating a canal lock

Examples

The lockkeeper opened the gates to let the narrowboat pass through.
As a lockkeeper, she often worked early mornings and late evenings during the busy season.
The young boy watched as the lockkeeper expertly operated the heavy machinery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canal worker - a person working on or maintaining a canal

Etymology

From 'lock' (a device for raising and lowering boats) + 'keeper' (someone who looks after something).
