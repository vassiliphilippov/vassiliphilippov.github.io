lineal

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɪniəl / (AmE), / ˈlɪniəl / (BrE)
arranged in a straight line; relating to or resembling a line.
arranged in a straight line

Examples

The family tree had a lineal arrangement, showing each generation beneath the previous one.
He examined the lineal patterns created by the arrangement of the chairs.
The architect preferred a lineal design for the building’s façade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: linear - in a straight line; involving lines

Etymology

From Latin linealis, from linea (line).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00661768-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈlɪniəl / (AmE), / ˈlɪniəl / (BrE)
relating to or descended in a direct line from an ancestor, without deviation or interruption
direct line of descent

Examples

Only lineal descendants of the founder were allowed to inherit the estate.
They traced their lineal ancestry back to medieval times.
In some cultures, property passes only to the lineal heirs.

Synset Examples

lineal ancestors
lineal heirs
a direct descendant of the king
direct heredity

Related Terms

  • Antonym: collateral - descended via a branch other than a direct line from an ancestor

Etymology

From Latin linealis (pertaining to a line), from linea (line).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01419359-a
Synset POS: a
