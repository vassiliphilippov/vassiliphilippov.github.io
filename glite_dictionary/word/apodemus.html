Apodemus

1. NOUN

/ ˌæpəˈdiːməs / (AmE), / ˌapəˈdiːməs / (BrE)
A genus of small rodents commonly known as Old World field mice, native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.
genus of Old World field mice

Examples

The researcher studied populations of Apodemus in the rural countryside.
Several species within the Apodemus genus are important seed dispersers in European forests.
Field data revealed that Apodemus is particularly active at night.

Etymology

From Greek 'apodēmos' (away from home), used in scientific taxonomy.
