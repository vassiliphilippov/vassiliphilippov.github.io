scarlet

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɑrlət / (AmE), / ˈskɑːlɪt / (BrE)
a bright red color with a slight orange tinge
vivid red color

Examples

The parade was a sea of scarlet and gold.
She chose a paint in the exact shade of scarlet for her living room wall.
The cardinal's robe was a striking scarlet that stood out among the clergy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crimson - a rich deep red color inclining to purple
  • Synonym: vermilion - a brilliant red or scarlet pigment

Etymology

From Old French escarlate, from Medieval Latin scarlatum (a rich cloth, often dyed red).

2. ADJ

/ ˈskɑːr.lət / (AmE), / ˈskɑː.lɪt / (BrE)
of a brilliant red color with a slightly orange tinge, similar to the color of blood or ripe cherries
bright red color

Examples

Her dress was a striking shade of scarlet that caught everyone's attention.
The autumn leaves turned a beautiful scarlet before falling to the ground.
A scarlet sunrise lit up the sky early this morning.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: crimson - a deep red color, often with a bluish tinge
  • Synonym: vermilion - a brilliant red or scarlet pigment or color
  • Degree Lower: red - the color at the end of the spectrum next to orange and opposite violet
  • Synonym: ruby - a deep red color, named after the gemstone

Etymology

From Old French escarlate, from Medieval Latin scarlatum, of Persian origin.
