Cartesian

1. ADJ

/ kɑrˈtiʒən / (AmE), / kɑːˈtiːzɪən / (BrE)
of or relating to the French philosopher René Descartes or his philosophy, especially his emphasis on logical analysis and deductive reasoning.
relating to René Descartes or his philosophy

Examples

The Cartesian approach emphasizes deductive reasoning in scientific inquiry.
His ideas reflect a Cartesian understanding of mind and body.
Modern mathematics heavily relies on Cartesian coordinates and analytical methods.

Synset Examples

Cartesian linguistics

Etymology

From the name of René Descartes (Latinized as Cartesius) + -ian.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03038272-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ kɑrˈtiʒən / (AmE), / kɑːˈtiːzɪən / (BrE)
A follower of the philosophy or ideas of René Descartes.
follower of Descartes' philosophy

Examples

As a dedicated Cartesian, she believed that clear reasoning was the foundation of knowledge.
Many seventeenth-century scientists identified themselves as Cartesians in their methods and beliefs.
The debate between a Cartesian and an empiricist can often become quite lively.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Platonist - A follower of the philosophy of Plato
  • Synonym: Aristotelian - A follower of the philosophy of Aristotle

Etymology

From Latinized name Cartesius (René Descartes) + -an (denoting follower).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09917472-n
Synset POS: n
