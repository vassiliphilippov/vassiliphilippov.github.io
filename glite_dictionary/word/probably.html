probably

1. ADV

/ ˈprɑːbəbli / (AmE), / ˈprɒbəbli / (BrE)
Used to say that something is likely to happen or to be true but is not certain.
likely but not certain

Examples

She will probably arrive before noon, but I'm not completely sure.
It's probably going to rain later, so take an umbrella just in case.
They probably forgot about the meeting, given how quiet it's been.

Synset Examples

He is probably out of the country
in all likelihood we are headed for war

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: certainly - without any doubt; surely
  • Degree Lower: possibly - by chance; perhaps, with less certainty

Etymology

From probable + -ly; probable from Latin probabilis (provable, credible).

2. ADV

/ ˈprɑːbəbli / (AmE), / ˈprɒbəbli / (BrE)
Used to say that something is likely to happen or to be true, but is not certain.
likely to happen or be true

Examples

It will probably rain later this afternoon, so take an umbrella.
She will probably arrive at the station around six o'clock.
He probably forgot about the meeting since he didn't show up.

Synset Examples

he talked plausibly before the committee
he will probably win the election

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: possibly - might happen or be true, but less certain than probably
  • Degree Higher: certainly - sure to happen or true, expressing much greater certainty than probably

Etymology

From Middle English 'probabli', from Old French 'probable', from Latin 'probabilis' (provable, likely).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00297385-r
Synset POS: r
