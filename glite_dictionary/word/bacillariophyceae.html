Bacillariophyceae

1. NOUN

/ bəˌsɪləriˌɑːfəˈsiːiː / (AmE), / bəˌsɪləriˌɒfɪˈsiːiː / (BrE)
A class of mostly microscopic algae known as diatoms, found in marine and freshwater environments.
class of microscopic algae (diatoms)
medical

Examples

The Bacillariophyceae are important primary producers in both oceans and lakes.
Scientists often study Bacillariophyceae to monitor water quality and ecosystem health.
The unique silica cell walls of Bacillariophyceae have fascinated researchers for decades.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diatom - single-celled alga with a silica cell wall

Etymology

From New Latin, from 'Bacillaria' (a genus of diatoms) + Greek 'phyke' (seaweed) + Latin '-ceae' (denoting a class).

2. NOUN

/ bəˌsɪl.ər.ioʊˈfaɪ.si.i / (AmE), / bəˌsɪl.ərɪ.əʊˈfaɪ.si.iː / (BrE)
a class of mainly marine and freshwater eukaryotic algae, commonly known as diatoms, characterized by silica cell walls and important ecological roles
class of algae known as diatoms
medical

Examples

The Bacillariophyceae are an important group of algae involved in global oxygen production.
Scientists often study Bacillariophyceae to understand aquatic ecosystems and water quality.
Silica shells are a defining feature of the Bacillariophyceae, making them unique among algae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diatom - a microscopic algae of the class Bacillariophyceae

Etymology

From New Latin, from Bacillaria (a genus of diatoms) + -phyceae (suffix for algae classes).
