carnassial

1. ADJ

/ kɑrˈnæsiəl / (AmE), / kɑːˈnasɪəl / (BrE)
(of a tooth) adapted for shearing flesh, typically found in carnivorous mammals.
tooth adapted for shearing flesh

Examples

The tiger's carnassial teeth are perfectly shaped for cutting through tough meat.
Fossil evidence of carnassial teeth suggests this animal was a meat-eater.
The veterinarian explained that a broken carnassial tooth can be particularly painful for dogs.

Synset Examples

the carnassial teeth of carnivores

Etymology

From French carnassier (flesh-eating), from Latin caro, carnis (flesh).
