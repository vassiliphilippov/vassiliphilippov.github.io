steroid

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɛˌrɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈstɪə.rɔɪd / (BrE)
Any hormone that affects the development and growth of sex organs, such as testosterone and estrogen.
hormone affecting sex organ development
medical

Examples

Testosterone is a steroid responsible for the development of male sex characteristics.
Certain medical conditions may be treated with synthetic steroids that mimic natural hormones.
Estrogen is a type of steroid playing a crucial role in female reproductive health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: testosterone - a steroid hormone that stimulates development of male secondary sexual characteristics
  • Synonym: estrogen - steroid hormones that promote development and maintenance of female characteristics

Etymology

From International Scientific Vocabulary, ultimately from Greek 'stereos' (solid) and '-oid' (form, likeness).

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɛrˌɔɪd, ˈstiˌrɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈstɪərɔɪd, ˈstɛrɔɪd / (BrE)
any of a large class of fat-soluble organic compounds with a structure based on four fused carbon rings, including hormones and vitamins
class of organic compounds with four-ring structure
medical

Examples

Some athletes illegally use steroids to enhance their performance.
Cortisol is a natural steroid that helps regulate metabolism and immune response.
Doctors sometimes prescribe steroids to reduce inflammation in chronic illnesses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hormone - a chemical substance produced in the body that controls and regulates cells or organs
  • Degree Higher: anabolic steroid - a synthetic steroid hormone that resembles testosterone and promotes muscle growth

Etymology

From Greek 'stereos' (solid) + the chemical suffix '-oid' (like, resembling).
