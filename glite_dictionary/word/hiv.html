HIV

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪtʃ aɪ ˈviː / (AmE), / ˌeɪtʃ aɪ ˈviː / (BrE)
a virus that causes acquired immune deficiency syndrome (AIDS) by attacking the body's immune system, specifically the helper T cells
virus that causes AIDS
medical

Examples

Doctors warn that HIV can be transmitted through unprotected sexual contact.
Modern treatments allow people with HIV to live long, healthy lives.
He was diagnosed with HIV after routine blood tests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: AIDS - acquired immune deficiency syndrome, the disease caused by HIV

Etymology

Initialism of Human Immunodeficiency Virus.

2. NOUN

/ ˌeɪtʃ.aɪˈvi / (AmE), / ˌeɪtʃ.aɪˈviː / (BrE)
a virus that attacks the immune system and can lead to acquired immunodeficiency syndrome (AIDS)
human immunodeficiency virus
medical

Examples

He was diagnosed as HIV positive after a routine medical check-up.
Public health campaigns aim to reduce the spread of HIV through education and prevention.
Many people live long and healthy lives with HIV thanks to antiretroviral therapy.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: AIDS - a disease caused by HIV leading to a severe weakening of the immune system

Etymology

Abbreviation of human immunodeficiency virus.
