consequently

1. ADV

/ ˈkɑːn.sə.kwənt.li / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.sɪ.kwənt.li / (BrE)
as a result; therefore
as a result; therefore

Examples

It rained heavily all night; consequently, the match was postponed.
She forgot her umbrella and consequently got soaked on her way home.
He did not study for the exam and consequently failed it.

Synset Examples

he had good reason to be grateful for the opportunities which they had made available to him and which consequently led to the good position he now held

Related Terms

  • Synonym: therefore - for that reason; as a result
  • Synonym: accordingly - in a way that is appropriate to the particular circumstances; as a result
  • Antonym: however - used to introduce a statement that contrasts with or seems to contradict something that has been said previously

Etymology

From Latin consequentia (sequence, consequence) via French conséquent.

2. ADV

/ ˈkɑnsəkwəntli / (AmE), / ˈkɒnsɪkwəntli / (BrE)
As a result; therefore; for that reason.
as a result; therefore

Examples

She forgot to set her alarm and consequently was late for work.
The company lost major clients last year; consequently, its profits fell sharply.
The roads were icy, and consequently several accidents occurred.

Synset Examples

consequently, he didn't do it
continued to have severe headaches and accordingly returned to the doctor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: therefore - for that reason; as a result
  • Synonym: accordingly - in a way that is appropriate to the particular circumstances; as a consequence

Etymology

From Latin consequentem, present participle of consequī (to follow).
