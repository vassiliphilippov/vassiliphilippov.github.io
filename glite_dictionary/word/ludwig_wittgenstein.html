Ludwig Wittgenstein

1. PROPN

/ ˈlʊd.vɪɡ ˈvɪt.ɡən.ʃtaɪn / (AmE), / ˈlʊdvɪɡ ˈvɪtɡənʃtaɪn / (BrE)
(1889–1951) An Austrian-born British philosopher whose work greatly influenced logic, philosophy of language, and logical positivism.
Austrian-British philosopher (1889–1951)
british

Examples

Many consider Ludwig Wittgenstein one of the most important philosophers of the twentieth century.
The early work of Ludwig Wittgenstein focused on the relationship between language and reality.
Students in philosophy often study the theories of Ludwig Wittgenstein on logic and language.

2. PROPN

/ ˈlʊdˌwɪɡ ˈvɪtɡənˌstaɪn / (AmE), / ˈlʊdvɪɡ ˈvɪtɡənstaɪn / (BrE)
(1889–1951) Austrian-born British philosopher; a central figure in analytic philosophy and the philosophy of language.
Austrian-born British philosopher

Examples

Many scholars discuss Ludwig Wittgenstein's influence on twentieth-century philosophy.
Ludwig Wittgenstein wrote both the Tractatus and the Philosophical Investigations.
The later work of Ludwig Wittgenstein challenged traditional views on the meaning of language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Bertrand Russell - British philosopher, mathematician and Wittgenstein's mentor
  • Synonym: philosophy of language - The branch of philosophy most associated with Wittgenstein

Etymology

German surname; 'Ludwig' from German 'famous warrior', 'Wittgenstein' from a place name in Germany.
