Myristica

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈrɪstɪkə / (AmE), / mɪˈrɪstɪkə / (BrE)
a genus of tropical Asian evergreen trees in the family Myristicaceae, known for species such as the nutmeg tree
genus of evergreen trees including nutmeg

Examples

Myristica fragrans is best known for producing the spice nutmeg.
The tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia are home to several species of Myristica.
Botanists study the distribution and ecological role of Myristica trees in their native habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nutmeg - aromatic spice from seeds of Myristica fragrans

Etymology

From Latin *Myristica* (the genus name), from Greek *myristikos* (fragrant, for scent-producing plants).

2. NOUN

/ maɪˈrɪstɪkə / (AmE), / mɪˈrɪstɪkə / (BrE)
a genus of tropical Asian evergreen trees in the family Myristicaceae, including the nutmeg tree, with small white or yellow flowers and fleshy fruits
genus of tropical evergreen trees (nutmeg family)

Examples

The Myristica genus is best known for producing the nutmeg spice.
Several species within Myristica are prized for their aromatic seeds and fruits.
Botanists discovered a rare Myristica specimen while exploring the Indonesian rainforest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nutmeg - the hard aromatic seed from a tree of the genus Myristica, used as a spice
  • Synonym: Myristicaceae - the nutmeg family of tropical trees

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, from Greek myristikos (fragrant).
