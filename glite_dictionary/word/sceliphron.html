Sceliphron

1. NOUN

/ skəˈlɪfrɒn / (AmE), / skɛˈlɪfrɒn / (BrE)
A genus of wasps known as mud daubers, recognized for building nests out of mud.
genus of mud dauber wasps

Examples

Several species within the genus Sceliphron are known for their characteristic mud nests.
The Sceliphron wasp can often be seen carrying mud to build its nest under eaves.
Entomologists studied the life cycle of Sceliphron in various climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mud dauber - A type of wasp that builds nests from mud

Etymology

From Greek 'skeliphron', meaning prudent or thoughtful, used in taxonomy.

2. NOUN

/ skɛˈlɪfrɒn / (AmE), / skɛˈlɪfrɒn / (BrE)
A genus of solitary wasps commonly known as mud daubers, recognized for constructing nests from mud.
genus of mud dauber wasps

Examples

The Sceliphron wasp built its distinctive nest from carefully arranged mud pellets.
Researchers observed several Sceliphron species along the riverbanks during the summer.
Nests created by Sceliphron are often found attached to walls or cliffs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mud dauber - a type of wasp that builds mud nests

Etymology

From Greek skeliphron (careful, prudent), used as a taxonomic name.
