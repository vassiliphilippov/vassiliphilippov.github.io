hypoglossal

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpəˈɡlɑsəl / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəˈɡlɒsəl / (BrE)
anatomical term for the twelfth cranial nerve, which supplies the muscles of the tongue
twelfth cranial nerve
medical

Examples

Damage to the hypoglossal can result in difficulty speaking and swallowing.
The neurologist tested the patient's hypoglossal function by asking him to stick out his tongue.
Injury to the hypoglossal is rare but can affect tongue movement.

Etymology

From Greek hypo- (under) + glossa (tongue).
