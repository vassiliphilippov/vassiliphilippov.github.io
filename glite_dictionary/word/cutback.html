cutback

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌtˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈkʌtbæk / (BrE)
a reduction in the amount, size, or rate of something, especially expenditure or production
a reduction in amount or rate

Examples

The company announced a cutback in staff to reduce costs.
Due to budget cutbacks, the school canceled several extracurricular programs.
Government cutbacks have affected public transportation services throughout the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reduction - the act of making something smaller or less
  • Antonym: increase - the process of becoming or making something larger

Etymology

From the verb phrase 'cut back' (to reduce) + noun-forming suffix.
