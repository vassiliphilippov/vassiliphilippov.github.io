congestion

1. NOUN

/ kənˈdʒɛstʃən / (AmE), / kənˈdʒɛstʃən / (BrE)
A condition of overcrowding, particularly when movement is hindered or slowed, such as with traffic or people.
excessive crowding or overfilling

Examples

Morning congestion on the main highway caused commuters to be late for work.
The city is taking steps to reduce congestion in the downtown area.
During the holidays, shopping centers often suffer from severe congestion.

Synset Examples

traffic congestion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jam - a situation where movement is stopped or slowed by crowding
  • Antonym: clearance - the removal or reduction of congestion

Etymology

From Latin 'congestio' (a heaping up), from 'congerere' (to heap together).

2. NOUN

/ kənˈdʒɛstʃən / (AmE), / kənˈdʒɛstʃən / (BrE)
the excessive accumulation of blood or other fluid in a part of the body, often causing swelling or pressure
excess fluid or blood in a body part
medical

Examples

Nasal congestion made it difficult for her to breathe normally during her cold.
The doctor explained that chest congestion could be a sign of a respiratory infection.
Swelling and congestion often accompany allergic reactions in sensitive individuals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: edema - swelling caused by excess fluid in body tissues
  • Synonym: inflammation - localized physical condition with redness and swelling

Etymology

From Latin 'congestio', from 'congerere' meaning 'to heap up'.
