Teucrium canadense

1. NOUN

/ ˈtjuːkriəm kəˈnædənsi / (AmE), / ˈtjuːkriəm kəˈnædənsi / (BrE)
A perennial subshrub native to North America, with serrated leaves and cream-colored to pink or purple flowers in spikelike racemes; also known as Canada germander.
North American perennial subshrub; Canada germander

Examples

Teucrium canadense can be found growing along riverbanks and in moist meadows throughout much of North America.
Botanists studied the medicinal properties of Teucrium canadense in traditional remedies.
Gardeners appreciate Teucrium canadense for its attractive spikes of pinkish-purple flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Canada germander - Common name for Teucrium canadense

Etymology

Genus name from Latin 'Teucrium'; species name 'canadense' refers to Canada, its native region.
