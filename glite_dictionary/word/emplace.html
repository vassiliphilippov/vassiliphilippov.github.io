to emplace

1. VERB

/ ɛmˈpleɪs / (AmE), / ɪmˈpleɪs / (BrE)
to put or position something firmly in a particular place
put or position in place

Examples

The engineers emplaced the equipment at the construction site with great care.
A memorial stone was emplaced at the entrance to honor the founders.
They will emplace the new sculpture in the central garden next week.

Synset Examples

the box with the ancestors' ashes was emplaced on the top shelf of the house altar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: place - to put something in a particular position
  • Antonym: remove - to take something away from its place or position

Etymology

From French 'emplacer' (to station or put into place), from 'en-' + 'place' (place).

2. VERB

/ ɛmˈpleɪs / (AmE), / ɛmˈpleɪs / (BrE)
to put or position something, especially military equipment or artillery, in a particular place or position
position or place (esp. military equipment)

Examples

The artillery unit moved quickly to emplace its guns on the hilltop.
Engineers worked overnight to properly emplace the new missile launchers.
They needed several hours to emplace the heavy cannons before the battle began.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deploy - to move troops or equipment into position for action

Etymology

From French 'emplacer', from 'en-' (in) + 'place' (place).
