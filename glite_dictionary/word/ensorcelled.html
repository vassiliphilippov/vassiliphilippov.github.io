ensorcelled

1. ADJ

/ ɛnˈsɔr səld / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɔː səld / (BrE)
enchanted or bewitched; under the influence of a magic spell
under a magic spell

Examples

The mysterious forest left her feeling ensorcelled, as if she might never escape its grasp.
He spoke in such a mesmerizing way that everyone seemed ensorcelled by his words.
The ancient artifact was said to be ensorcelled and dangerous to unwary travelers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enchanted - placed under a magical spell; charmed
  • Synonym: bewitched - under the influence of magic; enchanted

Etymology

From French 'ensorceler' (to bewitch), from 'sorcier' (sorcerer), ultimately from Latin 'sortiarius'.
