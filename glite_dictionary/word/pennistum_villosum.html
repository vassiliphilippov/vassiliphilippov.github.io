Pennistum villosum

1. NOUN

/ pɛnˈɪstəm vɪˈloʊsəm / (AmE), / pɛnˈɪstəm vɪˈləʊsəm / (BrE)
A species of grass from northeastern tropical Africa, known for its feathery flower clusters (panicles).
African grass with feathery panicles

Examples

The ornamental appeal of Pennistum villosum makes it a popular choice in gardens.
Researchers have observed Pennistum villosum thriving in the wild areas of northeastern Africa.
The feathery panicles of Pennistum villosum sway gracefully in the wind.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Penni-' meaning feather, and 'villosum' meaning hairy or shaggy.
