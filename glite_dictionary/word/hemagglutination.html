hemagglutination

1. NOUN

/ ˌhiːməˌɡluːtəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌhiːməˌɡluːtɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
The clumping together of red blood cells, typically as a result of a specific reaction with an antibody or virus.
clumping of red blood cells
medical

Examples

The laboratory test detected hemagglutination in the blood sample, indicating a reaction to the virus.
Researchers study hemagglutination to understand how influenza infects red blood cells.
A positive hemagglutination test can confirm the presence of certain antibodies in the blood.

Etymology

From Greek 'haima' (blood) and 'agglutination' (clumping together).
