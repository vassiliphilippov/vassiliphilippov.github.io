unforced

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfɔrst / (AmE), / ʌnˈfɔːst / (BrE)
not brought about by coercion, pressure, or force; natural or voluntary.
not brought about by force

Examples

Her laughter sounded unforced and genuine, making everyone relax.
He gave an unforced apology, which helped resolve the conflict quickly.
The artist's brushstrokes were unforced, giving the painting a spontaneous feel.

Synset Examples

the confession was uncoerced

Related Terms

  • Antonym: forced - brought about by coercion or force
  • Synonym: voluntary - done by choice or free will

Etymology

Formed by prefixing 'un-' (not) to 'forced'.

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfɔrst / (AmE), / ʌnˈfɔːst / (BrE)
(of a quality, action, etc.) happening or expressed in a natural and effortless way; not done with evident strain or compulsion.
natural; not forced or strained

Examples

Her laughter had an unforced charm that made everyone feel comfortable.
The film’s dialogue felt refreshingly unforced, unlike in many comedies.
He delivered the speech in an unforced manner, making it seem genuine.

Synset Examples

a voice with a pleasingly unforced quality
his playing is facile and unstrained

Related Terms

  • Synonym: natural - existing in or caused by nature; not artificial or forced
  • Antonym: strained - showing signs of tension or effort; unnatural

Etymology

From un- (not) + forced (from force).
