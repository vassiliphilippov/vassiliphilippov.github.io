double-geared

1. ADJ

/ ˌdʌbəlˈɡɪrd / (AmE), / ˌdʌbəlˈɡɪəd / (BrE)
(of a machine or mechanism) equipped with a compound gearing that uses two pinions and two wheels in mesh to increase mechanical advantage.
equipped with compound gearing (2 pinions, 2 wheels)

Examples

The double-geared lifting mechanism of the crane allowed heavier loads to be raised safely.
Some older bicycles feature a double-geared system for smoother and more efficient pedaling.
The engineers recommended a double-geared winch to improve torque during heavy-duty operations.

Synset Examples

the double-geared lifting mechanism of a crane

2. ADJ

/ ˌdʌb.əlˈɡɪrd / (AmE), / ˌdʌb.əlˈɡɪəd / (BrE)
(of a machine, especially a lathe) equipped with an extra set of gears to reduce the speed of the spindle compared to the driving pulley.
provided with extra gears for speed reduction

Examples

The workshop recently installed a double-geared lathe for greater control at lower speeds.
To cut threads more accurately, a double-geared mechanism is essential in precision lathes.
The machinist explained that the double-geared system helps prevent overheating during prolonged operations.

Synset Examples
