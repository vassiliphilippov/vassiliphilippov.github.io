uncloudedness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈklaʊdɪdnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈklaʊdɪdnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being clear and free from cloudiness, especially in water or vision.
the state of being clear or not cloudy

Examples

The uncloudedness of the lake made it possible to see all the way to the bottom.
Her eyes had a remarkable uncloudedness after her surgery.
The uncloudedness of his intentions reassured everyone in the room.

Synset Examples

when she awoke the clarity was back in her eyes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clarity - the quality of being clear
  • Antonym: opacity - the quality of being opaque or not clear

Etymology

From 'unclouded' + '-ness', modeled on similar abstract nouns.
