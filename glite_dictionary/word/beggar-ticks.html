beggar-ticks

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛɡər tɪks / (AmE), / ˈbɛɡə tɪks / (BrE)
Any of several plants of the genus Bidens, having yellow flowers and small prickly fruits that stick to fur and clothing.
plant of the genus Bidens

Examples

The field was covered in beggar-ticks, their yellow blossoms bright against the green grass.
After our walk, the dog's fur was full of beggar-ticks stuck to it.
She picked the beggar-ticks off her socks after hiking through the meadow.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɛɡər tɪks / (AmE), / ˈbɛɡə tɪks / (BrE)
the barbed seeds of bur marigold plants (genus Bidens) that easily cling to clothing or animal fur
barbed seeds of bur marigolds

Examples

After our walk through the field, my socks were covered in beggar-ticks.
The sticky beggar-ticks can be difficult to remove from a dog's fur.
Children sometimes call beggar-ticks 'hitchhikers' because they cling to clothing so easily.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bur - a seed or dry fruit with hooks or teeth

Etymology

From the resemblance of the seeds to small ticks and their tendency to adhere like beggars seeking attention.
