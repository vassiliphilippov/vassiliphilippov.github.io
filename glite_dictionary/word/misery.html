misery

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪzəri / (AmE), / ˈmɪzəri / (BrE)
a state of great suffering and unhappiness caused by affliction or misfortune
great suffering or unhappiness

Examples

After losing his job, he sank into a deep misery that lasted for months.
Decades of war left the population living in constant misery.
She tried to hide her misery behind a forced smile.

Synset Examples

the misery and wretchedness of those slums is intolerable

Related Terms

  • Antonym: happiness - a state of being happy and content
  • Synonym: wretchedness - a state of being extremely unhappy or unfortunate

Etymology

From Old French miserie, from Latin miseria (wretchedness, unhappiness).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɪzəri / (AmE), / ˈmɪzəri / (BrE)
A feeling of great unhappiness or suffering.
intense unhappiness or suffering

Examples

He endured years of misery after losing his job.
Her illness caused her great physical and emotional misery.
The refugees spoke of their misery during the harsh winter.

Synset Examples

she was exhausted by her misery and grief

Related Terms

  • Antonym: happiness - a feeling of being happy
  • Synonym: suffering - the experience of pain or distress

Etymology

From Old French 'miserie', from Latin 'miseria' (wretchedness).

Synset POS: n
