sequestered

1. ADJ

/ sɪˈkwɛstɚd / (AmE), / sɪˈkwɛstəd / (BrE)
kept separate and secluded; isolated from others or from the outside world.
kept separate and secluded

Examples

The jury was sequestered for the duration of the high-profile trial.
She enjoyed her vacation in a sequestered mountain cabin far from the city.
The documents are kept in a sequestered section of the library.

Synset Examples

a sequestered jury

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isolated - separated from others; alone
  • Antonym: exposed - in a position where there is no protection; not secluded

Etymology

From Latin sequester (trustee, mediator), meaning one who keeps something apart.

2. ADJ

/ sɪˈkwɛstərd / (AmE), / sɪˈkwɛstəd / (BrE)
(of a place) quiet and private; sheltered and away from other people or activity.
quiet, private, or sheltered place

Examples

The writer worked on her novel in a sequestered cabin deep in the woods.
The conference was held in a sequestered courtyard away from the city noise.
They enjoyed a quiet afternoon in a sequestered corner of the garden.

Synset Examples

the cloistered academic world of books
sat close together in the sequestered pergola
sitting under the reclusive calm of a shade tree
a secluded romantic spot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secluded - quiet and private, not near other people or busy places
  • Synonym: isolated - far away from other places or people
  • Antonym: exposed - not protected or sheltered

Etymology

From Latin 'sequestrare' (to set aside), via Old French and Medieval Latin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01864995-s
Synset POS: s
