woodbine

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊdˌbaɪn / (AmE), / ˈwʊd.baɪn / (BrE)
a European climbing honeysuckle with fragrant red and yellow-white flowers, also known as Lonicera periclymenum.
European honeysuckle with fragrant flowers

Examples

A beautiful woodbine twined itself around the old garden gate.
In early summer, the scent of the woodbine filled the evening air.
They planted woodbine along the fence to attract bees and butterflies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honeysuckle - a climbing shrub with tubular fragrant flowers, genus Lonicera

Etymology

From Middle English 'wodebinde', from Old English 'wudubind' ('wood bind'), referring to its ability to bind or twine around trees and plants.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwʊdˌbaɪn / (AmE), / ˈwʊd.baɪn / (BrE)
A common North American climbing vine with compound leaves and bluish-black berrylike fruit, especially the Virginia creeper.
North American climbing vine

Examples

The woodbine climbed rapidly up the old fence, its leaves turning red in autumn.
Birds gathered to eat the bluish-black berries produced by the woodbine in late summer.
In the shady corner of the garden, a woodbine provided a dense, leafy cover.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Virginia creeper - common name for Parthenocissus quinquefolia, a common woodbine species
  • Synonym: honeysuckle - another climbing plant sometimes called woodbine in Britain

Etymology

From Middle English 'wodebinde', from Old English 'wudubind', originally referring to honeysuckle and related climbing plants.
