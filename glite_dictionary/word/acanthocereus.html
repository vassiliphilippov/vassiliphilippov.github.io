Acanthocereus

1. NOUN

/ əˌkænθəˈsɪriəs / (AmE), / əˌkanθəˈsɪəriəs / (BrE)
A genus of mostly trailing cacti with nocturnal white flowers, native to tropical America and the Caribbean.
genus of tropical trailing cacti

Examples

The Acanthocereus blooms only at night, showing its large white flowers to nocturnal pollinators.
Botanists discovered a new species in the Acanthocereus genus growing near the Caribbean coast.
Gardeners admire Acanthocereus for its unusual trailing stems and spectacular night flowers.

Etymology

From Greek akantha (thorn) + cereus (Latin for 'candle'), referring to the spiny, columnar form of these cacti.

2. NOUN

/ əˌkænθoʊsɪˈriːəs / (AmE), / əˌkænθəʊsɪˈrɪəs / (BrE)
A genus of mostly trailing cacti with nocturnal white flowers, native to tropical America and the Caribbean.
a genus of trailing cacti with white night-blooming flowers

Examples

The Acanthocereus is known for its dramatic white flowers that open at night.
Gardeners in the Caribbean often cultivate Acanthocereus for its unusual appearance and rapid growth.
Several species of Acanthocereus are adapted to arid climates and sandy soils.

Etymology

From Greek 'akantha' (thorn) and Latinized form 'cereus' (candle), referring to the plant's spiny and candle-like appearance.
