to masculinise

1. VERB

/ ˈmæskjəlɪˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmæskjʊlɪnaɪz / (BrE)
to cause to develop male or masculine characteristics, especially through hormones or genetic factors
cause to develop masculine traits
medical british

Examples

Hormone therapy may masculinise the body over time.
Some medical conditions can unexpectedly masculinise female patients.
The treatment was designed to masculinise certain physical features.

Synset Examples

the drugs masculinized the teenage girl

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to feminise - to cause to develop female or feminine characteristics

Etymology

From Latin masculinus (male, masculine) + English suffix -ise (to make or become).
