intelligent

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈtɛlədʒənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛlɪdʒənt / (BrE)
Having or showing a high ability to think, understand, and learn; able to reason well.
having a high ability to reason or learn

Examples

She is one of the most intelligent students in her class.
The intelligent solution to the problem required deep thinking and creativity.
Dogs are considered quite intelligent among animals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smart - having or showing quick intelligence or cleverness
  • Antonym: stupid - lacking intelligence or common sense

Etymology

From Latin 'intelligentem', present participle of 'intelligere' (to understand).
