Mary Stuart

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɛri ˈstuːərt / (AmE), / ˈmeəri ˈstjuːət / (BrE)
(1542–1587) Queen of Scotland from 1542 to 1567, who was forced to abdicate, fled to England, and was later executed for her involvement in plots against Elizabeth I.
Queen of Scotland (1542–1587), executed for sedition

Examples

Mary Stuart became Queen of Scotland when she was just six days old.
The imprisonment of Mary Stuart by Elizabeth I changed the course of British history.
Many historians debate whether Mary Stuart was a victim of circumstance or a threat to England.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mary, Queen of Scots - another name for Mary Stuart, Queen of Scotland
  • Antonym: Elizabeth I - Queen of England involved in Mary Stuart's imprisonment and execution
