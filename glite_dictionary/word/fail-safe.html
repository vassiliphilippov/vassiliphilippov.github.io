fail-safe

1. NOUN

/ ˈfeɪlˌseɪf / (AmE), / ˈfeɪlˌseɪf / (BrE)
A mechanism or system designed to return to a safe condition in the event of a failure or malfunction.
mechanism ensuring safety during failure

Examples

All nuclear reactors are equipped with at least one fail-safe to prevent catastrophic accidents.
The new braking system has a fail-safe that automatically activates if the main brakes fail.
Engineers designed the machine with several fail-safes to ensure user safety in case of malfunction.

Etymology

Formed from 'fail' and 'safe', used since mid-20th century in engineering contexts.

3. ADJ

/ ˈfeɪlˌseɪf / (AmE), / ˈfeɪlˌseɪf / (BrE)
designed to automatically protect against failure or malfunction and thereby eliminate danger or harm.
protecting from danger in case of failure

Examples

The laboratory installed a fail-safe system to shut down equipment in case of overheating.
All modern elevators are equipped with fail-safe brakes to prevent accidents.
Engineers tested the fail-safe mechanism by simulating a power failure.

Synset Examples
