polypeptide

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːliˈpɛp.taɪd / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˈpɛp.taɪd / (BrE)
a molecule consisting of a chain of ten or more amino acids linked by peptide bonds
chain of amino acids
medical

Examples

The scientist synthesized a polypeptide to study its biological function in the lab.
Insulin is an example of a hormone that is actually a polypeptide.
A polypeptide can fold into complex shapes depending on its sequence of amino acids.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: peptide - a molecule composed of two or more amino acids linked by peptide bonds
  • Degree Higher: protein - a large molecule composed of one or more long chains of amino acids

Etymology

From poly- (many) + peptide, based on Greek 'polys' (many) and 'peptos' (digested).
