archaean

1. ADJ

/ ɑrˈkiːən / (AmE), / ɑːˈkiːən / (BrE)
relating to the earliest known rocks, formed during the Precambrian Eon, or to the Archaean Eon itself
relating to the earliest known Precambrian rocks

Examples

Geologists have discovered new evidence in archaean rocks that suggests early life existed.
The archaean crust has been preserved in certain stable continental regions.
Researchers study archaean sediments to learn about Earth's early atmosphere.

Etymology

From Greek archaios (ancient), via scientific Latin Archaean (Eon).
