evaporation

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌvæpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌvapəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a liquid, especially water, turns into vapor and is removed, especially by heat or naturally from exposed surfaces.
the process of liquid turning into vapor

Examples

The evaporation of water from the lake increased during the hot summer months.
To speed up the evaporation of alcohol, leave the mixture uncovered in a warm place.
After the rain, evaporation left a thin layer of salt on the ground.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: condensation - the process by which vapor turns into liquid

Etymology

From Latin evaporatio (a steaming away, vanishing).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˌvæpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌvæpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
The process by which a liquid changes into a vapor, often as a result of heat.
change of liquid into vapor

Examples

The evaporation of water from the lake increased during the hot summer months.
Salt is left behind after the evaporation of seawater in salt pans.
Rapid evaporation can make puddles disappear quickly on a sunny day.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: condensation - the process by which a vapor becomes a liquid

Etymology

From Latin evaporatio, from evaporare (to evaporate).
