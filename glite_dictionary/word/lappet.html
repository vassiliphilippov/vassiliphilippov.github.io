lappet

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæp.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈlap.ɪt / (BrE)
A medium-sized moth with hairy larvae, known as lappet caterpillars, belonging to the family Lasiocampidae.
a type of hairy moth

Examples

The lappet is most active at dusk during the summer months.
Scientists collected several lappet specimens for their study of moth diversity.
The hairy caterpillar of a lappet can be found feeding on leafy shrubs.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlæp.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈlæp.ɪt / (BrE)
a small, decorative flap or fold on a garment or headdress, often used for ornamentation
decorative flap on clothing or headdress

Examples

The bride's veil was adorned with delicate lappets that framed her face beautifully.
Victorian-era bonnets often featured lappets hanging on either side.
She stitched a silk lappet onto her fancy dress for added elegance.

Etymology

From French *lappette*, diminutive of *lappe* (flap).

3. NOUN

/ ˈlæp.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈlæp.ɪt / (BrE)
a fleshy, often wrinkled and sometimes brightly colored fold of skin that hangs from the neck or throat of certain birds, such as chickens and turkeys, or lizards
fleshy fold of skin on birds or lizards

Examples

The turkey's bright red lappet wobbled as it walked around the yard.
Male chickens often have a prominent lappet beneath their beaks.
Many lizards display a colorful lappet to attract mates or ward off rivals.

Etymology

From Middle English *lapet*, diminutive of *lap* (flap, edge of a garment).
