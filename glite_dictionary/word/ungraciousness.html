ungraciousness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈɡreɪʃəsnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈɡreɪʃnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being rude or lacking in good manners or courtesy
rudeness or lack of courtesy

Examples

Her ungraciousness at the award ceremony disappointed both fans and organizers.
He apologized for his earlier ungraciousness toward the guests.
The manager's ungraciousness made the employees feel unwelcome.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rudeness - impolite or disrespectful behavior
  • Antonym: graciousness - the quality of being courteous and kind

Etymology

Formed from 'ungracious' (not gracious), with the suffix '-ness'.
