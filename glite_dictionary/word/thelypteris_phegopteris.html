Thelypteris phegopteris

1. NOUN

/ θəˌlɪptərɪs fɛˈgɒptərɪs / (AmE), / θɪˌlɪptərɪs fiːˈɡɒptərɪs / (BrE)
A species of beech fern native to North America and Eurasia, characterized by triangular fronds and found in moist woods.
a beech fern species of North America and Eurasia

Examples

The damp forest floor was covered with Thelypteris phegopteris thriving in the shade of old trees.
Many botanists study Thelypteris phegopteris for its unique triangular fronds.
We identified several clumps of Thelypteris phegopteris during our hike through the wooded valley.

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Thelypteris' and species name from Greek ‘phēgós’ (beech) and ‘pteris’ (fern).
