Pernis

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrnɪs / (AmE), / ˈpɜːnɪs / (BrE)
A genus of large birds of prey, commonly known as honey-buzzards, with dull brown plumage and white-streaked underparts, found in Europe and Asia.
a genus of honey-buzzard birds of prey

Examples

The Pernis species are known for their distinctive facial bristles used to protect against wasp stings.
Researchers observed a Pernis circling high above the forest looking for food.
Unlike other raptors, Pernis tends to have a more slender body and smaller talons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: honey-buzzard - a common name for birds in the genus Pernis, birds of prey that feed on bees and wasps

Etymology

From Latin 'Pernis', the scientific genus name given by Carl Linnaeus.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrnɪs / (AmE), / ˈpɜːnɪs / (BrE)
A genus of birds of prey, including the honey buzzards, characterized by dull brown plumage with white-streaked underparts, native to Europe and Asia.
genus of birds of prey (honey buzzards)

Examples

The European Pernis is often mistaken for a buzzard due to its similar coloring.
Scientists studied the migration patterns of Pernis populations across the continent.
Several species within the genus Pernis feed primarily on bees and wasps.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on Greek 'pernēs', a bird of prey mentioned by Aristotle.
