ulterior

1. ADJ

/ ʌlˈtɪriər / (AmE), / ʌlˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
occurring or coming at a later time or stage; subsequent or future
happening at a later stage

Examples

The court dismissed any ulterior objections that arose after the decision.
Research has focused on the immediate impact rather than the ulterior consequences.
Her plans had no ulterior stages; she acted only on the present situation.

Synset Examples

without ulterior argument
the mood posterior to

Related Terms

  • Synonym: posterior - coming after in time or order; later
  • Antonym: prior - existing or coming before in time, order, or importance

Etymology

From Latin 'ulterior' (farther, more distant), comparative of 'ulter' (on the other side).

2. ADJ

/ ʌlˈtɪriɚ / (AmE), / ʌlˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
existing beyond what is immediately obvious or stated; remote or further than what is apparent.
beyond what is immediate or obvious

Examples

His reasons for investing in the project are ulterior and not immediately clear to everyone.
She wondered if there was an ulterior motive behind his generous offer.
The debate strayed into issues ulterior to the original topic.

Synset Examples

a suggestion ulterior to the present discussion
without … any purpose, immediate or ulterior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hidden - not immediately obvious or visible; concealed

Etymology

From Latin 'ulterior' ( farther, more distant ).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00453324-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ʌlˈtɪriɚ / (AmE), / ʌlˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
(of a reason, motive, or purpose) not stated or shown openly; existing beyond what is obvious or admitted, often intentionally kept hidden.
hidden or undisclosed, especially motives

Examples

He suspected his friend had an ulterior motive for offering to help.
The company's generosity seemed suspicious, leading some to wonder about its ulterior intentions.
She assured us there was no ulterior agenda behind her proposal.

Synset Examples

subterranean motives for murder
looked too closely for an ulterior purpose in all knowledge

Related Terms

  • Antonym: overt - open and not secret; easily observed
  • Synonym: covert - not openly acknowledged or displayed; secret

Etymology

From Latin 'ulterior' (further, more distant), comparative of 'ulter' (on the other side).
