toetoe

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊiˌtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈtəʊtəʊ / (BrE)
A tall grass native to New Zealand, with feathery plumes, used by Māori people for thatching roofs and other purposes.
tall New Zealand grass used for thatching

Examples

The Māori traditionally used toetoe for thatching the roofs of their houses.
Large plumes of toetoe swayed in the coastal breeze near the village.
Gardeners sometimes plant toetoe for its ornamental appearance and hardy nature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pampas grass - a tall grass with similar feathery plumes from South America

Etymology

From Māori toetoe, the native name for the plant.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊ.eɪˌtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈtəʊ.eɪˌtəʊ / (BrE)
a tall perennial grass native to New Zealand, with large feathery flower heads, often found in wetlands and used for ornamental or traditional purposes
tall feathery grass from New Zealand

Examples

The toetoe swayed gracefully in the breeze along the riverbank.
Local weavers use dried toetoe for making traditional mats and decorations.
Clusters of toetoe give the landscape a striking, golden appearance in late summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pampas grass - a South American grass with similar tall feathery plumes, often grown ornamentally

Etymology

From Māori 'tōetoe', the indigenous name for the plant.
