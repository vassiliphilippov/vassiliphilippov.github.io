minacious

1. ADJ

/ mɪˈneɪʃəs / (AmE), / mɪˈneɪʃəs / (BrE)
threatening or menacing; indicating or suggesting the presence of danger or evil.
threatening; menacing

Examples

The villain gave a minacious glare that sent chills down everyone's spine.
Dark, minacious clouds gathered above the city, making the afternoon seem like night.
His minacious tone made it clear that opposition would not be tolerated.

Synset Examples

a baleful look
forbidding thunderclouds

ominous rumblings of discontent
sinister storm clouds
a sinister smile
his threatening behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: menacing - threatening or suggesting harm
  • Synonym: ominous - suggesting that something bad is going to happen
  • Antonym: benevolent - well meaning and kindly

Etymology

From Latin minax, minacis (threatening), from minari (to threaten).
