Gelsemium

1. NOUN

/ dʒɛlˈsiːmiəm / (AmE), / dʒɛlˈsiːmiəm / (BrE)
a genus of evergreen twining shrubs native to the Americas and southeastern Asia, some species of which are known for their toxic properties and showy yellow flowers
genus of evergreen twining shrubs

Examples

The gardener warned that Gelsemium plants are highly toxic if ingested.
In spring, the yellow flowers of Gelsemium provide a bright display along the fence.
Researchers are studying the medicinal properties of compounds in Gelsemium.

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Italian 'gelsomino' (jasmine).
