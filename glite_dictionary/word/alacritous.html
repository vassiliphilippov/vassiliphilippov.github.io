alacritous

1. ADJ

/ əˈlækrɪtəs / (AmE), / əˈlak.rɪ.təs / (BrE)
quick and eager to do something; showing cheerful readiness
quick and eager

Examples

She gave an alacritous reply when asked to join the team.
The staff were alacritous in assisting the new arrivals.
His alacritous attitude impressed the interview panel.

Synset Examples

an alacritous response to the invitation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eager - wanting to do or have something very much
  • Synonym: ready - prepared and willing to do something
  • Antonym: reluctant - unwilling and slow to do something

Etymology

From 'alacrity' (Latin alacritas, meaning liveliness or eagerness) with English adjectival suffix '-ous'.
