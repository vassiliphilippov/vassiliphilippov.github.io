stye

1. NOUN

/ staɪ / (AmE), / staɪ / (BrE)
a small, painful lump on the edge of the eyelid caused by a bacterial infection of an oil gland
painful eyelid lump (infection)
medical

Examples

She visited her doctor because a stye had developed on her lower eyelid.
A warm compress can help reduce the discomfort caused by a stye.
It is important not to squeeze a stye, as this can worsen the infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chalazion - a painless swelling on the eyelid caused by a blocked oil gland

Etymology

From Old English 'stīġe' (stye in the eye, suppurating swelling).
