cross-purpose

1. NOUN

/ krɔs ˈpɝːpəs / (AmE), / krɒs ˈpɜːpəs / (BrE)
a situation in which people have conflicting aims or misunderstand each other, often leading to confusion or ineffective action
conflicting aims or misunderstandings

Examples

Their discussions ended at cross-purpose because neither side understood the other’s goals.
If we continue to work at cross-purpose, the project will never be completed successfully.
The negotiations stalled as the parties spoke completely at cross-purpose throughout the meeting.

Synset Examples

Miscommunication led to efforts at cross-purpose rather than collaboration.
The two teams are working at cross-purpose, complicating the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conflict - serious disagreement or argument
  • Antonym: harmony - agreement or concord
