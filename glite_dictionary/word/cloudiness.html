cloudiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈklaʊdi.nɪs / (AmE), / ˈklaʊdɪnɪs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being cloudy, especially with reference to the sky or a substance's lack of clarity
the state of being cloudy

Examples

The cloudiness of the sky suggested that rain was likely later in the day.
Doctors were concerned about the cloudiness observed in the patient's urine sample.
In spring, the lake's cloudiness increases as melting snow washes in sediment.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear

2. NOUN

/ ˈklaʊdi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈklaʊdi.nɪs / (BrE)
the state or quality of the sky being covered with clouds; the degree to which clouds are present in the sky
degree of clouds covering the sky

Examples

The increasing cloudiness made it difficult to see the stars at night.
Weather reports predicted persistent cloudiness for the entire weekend.
Early morning cloudiness gave way to bright sunshine by noon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cloudy - (of the sky) full of or covered with clouds
  • Antonym: clearness - the state of being clear; absence of clouds

Etymology

From 'cloudy' + '-ness', forming a noun indicating the state of being cloudy.

3. NOUN

/ ˈklaʊdi.nɪs / (AmE), / ˈklaʊd.i.nɪs / (BrE)
a state of the sky being covered with clouds, often causing a gloomy or overcast appearance and reduced sunlight
state of the sky covered with clouds

Examples

The cloudiness made it feel like twilight even at noon.
After days of persistent cloudiness, the sun finally broke through the sky.
Meteorologists predicted increased cloudiness in the region by late afternoon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overcast - sky completely covered by clouds; dull or gray weather
  • Antonym: brightness - state of having much light; opposite of dim or gloomy

Etymology

From 'cloudy' + '-ness', based on Middle English 'cloudi'.

4. NOUN

/ ˈklaʊdi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈklaʊdɪnɪs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being covered with clouds or characterized by a lack of clear sky
state of being cloudy (sky or weather)

Examples

The cloudiness of the morning delayed the start of the picnic.
Due to heavy cloudiness, we couldn't see the stars last night.
Sudden cloudiness rolled in, and by noon the skies were completely gray.

Synset Examples

The cloudiness lifted, revealing a sunny afternoon.
The cloudiness of the day matched her mood.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear (opposite of cloudiness in weather or appearance)
  • Synonym: overcast - sky covered with clouds; dark and cloudy

Etymology

From 'cloudy' + '-ness', where 'cloudy' is from Old English 'cludig'.
