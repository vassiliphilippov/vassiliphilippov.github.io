to metastasise

1. VERB

/ məˈtæstəˌsaɪz / (AmE), / məˈtastəsaɪz / (BrE)
(of a disease, especially cancer) to spread from one part of the body to other parts.
(of cancer) spread within the body
medical british

Examples

Doctors discovered that the tumor had metastasised to the patient's liver and lungs.
If left untreated, the cancer may metastasise and become more difficult to manage.
The oncologist explained how rapidly some cancers can metastasise.

Synset Examples

the cancer had metastasized and the patient could not be saved

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to metastasize - (of a disease, especially cancer) to spread from one part of the body to another (American English spelling)

Etymology

From Greek 'metastasis' (removal, migration) via Late Latin and French.
