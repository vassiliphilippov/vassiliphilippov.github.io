Suksdorfia violaceae

1. NOUN

/ sʌksˈdɔːrfiə vaɪ.əˈleɪ.si.iː / (AmE), / sʌksˈdɔːfɪə vaɪ.əˈleɪ.sɪ.iː / (BrE)
A slender, delicate plant with wide, rounded, deeply lobed leaves and deep pink to violet funnel-shaped flowers, native to northwestern North America.
a plant with violet funnel-shaped flowers, native to NW North America

Examples

The Suksdorfia violaceae blooms in late spring, displaying vibrant violet flowers in mountain meadows.
Researchers studied the habitat of Suksdorfia violaceae to understand its distribution in the Pacific Northwest.
Gardeners appreciate Suksdorfia violaceae for its delicate appearance and striking violet blossoms.

Etymology

Genus name Suksdorfia honors Wilhelm N. Suksdorf, a 19th-century German-American botanist; species name from Latin 'violaceae' meaning 'violet-colored'.
