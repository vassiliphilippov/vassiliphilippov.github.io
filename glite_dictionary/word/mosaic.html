Mosaic

1. ADJ

/ moʊˈzeɪ.ɪk / (AmE), / məʊˈzeɪ.ɪk / (BrE)
Relating to Moses or to the laws and writings attributed to him, especially those in the Old Testament.
relating to Moses or his laws

Examples

The scholar gave a lecture on Mosaic traditions and their influence on Western law.
Many dietary rules in Judaism are based on Mosaic principles.
The concept of justice in Mosaic Law has been studied for centuries.

Synset Examples

Mosaic Law

Etymology

From Latin 'Mosaicus', from Greek 'Mōsaïkos', meaning 'of Moses'.

2. NOUN

/ moʊˈzeɪɪk / (AmE), / məʊˈzeɪɪk / (BrE)
an early freeware web browser developed in the 1990s that helped popularize the World Wide Web
early freeware web browser

Examples

The launch of Mosaic marked a turning point for the World Wide Web’s accessibility to the public.
Many early internet users remember browsing websites using Mosaic before more advanced browsers appeared.
Mosaic was instrumental in demonstrating the potential of multimedia on the internet.

Etymology

Named after a 'mosaic', reflecting the browser’s ability to display a variety of media types on a single page.
