Bassariscus astutus

1. NOUN

/ bæsəˈrɪskəs əˈstjuːtəs / (AmE), / basəˈrɪskəs asˈtjuːtəs / (BrE)
A raccoon-like omnivorous mammal of Mexico and the southwestern United States, noted for its long bushy tail with black and white rings; also known as the ringtail or ringtail cat.
raccoon-like mammal with ringed tail

Examples

The Bassariscus astutus is often found in rocky desert habitats throughout the American Southwest.
The distinctive bushy tail with black and white rings makes the Bassariscus astutus easy to identify.
Wildlife biologists observed a group of Bassariscus astutus foraging at dusk near the canyon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ringtail - common name for Bassariscus astutus, a small mammal of North America

Etymology

From modern Latin; Bassariscus from Greek 'bassaris' (fox), astutus from Latin meaning 'cunning', referring to its cleverness.
