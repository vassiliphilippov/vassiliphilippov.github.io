Pashtun

1. NOUN

/ pɑːʃˈtuːn / (AmE), / pæʃˈtuːn / (BrE)
a member of a people living chiefly in eastern and southern Afghanistan and northwestern Pakistan, known for their distinct language and culture
member of an ethnic group in Afghanistan and Pakistan

Examples

Many Pashtun families continue to follow traditional customs passed down for generations.
She is studying the history of the Pashtun people for her dissertation.
The majority of villagers in that region are Pashtun by heritage.

Synset Examples

Pathans are the predominant ethnic group in Afghanistan

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pathan - another term for a member of the Pashtun ethnic group

Etymology

From Pashto 'paṣ̌tūn', referring to the ethnic group.

2. NOUN

/ pɑːʃˈtuːn / (AmE), / pæʃˈtuːn / (BrE)
a member of an ethnic group speaking Pashto and living mainly in northwestern Pakistan and southeastern Afghanistan
member of a Pashto-speaking ethnic group

Examples

Many Pashtun communities are found on both sides of the Pakistan-Afghanistan border.
The traditional dress of a Pashtun man includes a turban and loose-fitting garments.
She spent several years working in Pashtun villages, learning about their culture and customs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pathan - another term for a Pashtun, especially in South Asia
  • Synonym: Afghan - a native or inhabitant of Afghanistan, sometimes used for Pashtuns

Etymology

From Pashto پښتون (paṡtūn), self-designation of the ethnic group.
