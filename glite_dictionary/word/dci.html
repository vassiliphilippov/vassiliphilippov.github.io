DCI

1. NOUN

/ di si ˈaɪ / (AmE), / diː siː ˈaɪ / (BrE)
the Director of Central Intelligence, formerly the head of the United States Intelligence Community and the Central Intelligence Agency
former CIA director (US)
american

Examples

The DCI testified before the Senate Intelligence Committee regarding national security matters.
In 1976, the DCI was responsible for overseeing both the CIA and the US Intelligence Community.
Many classified decisions required approval from the DCI during the Cold War era.

Etymology

Acronym of Director of Central Intelligence.
