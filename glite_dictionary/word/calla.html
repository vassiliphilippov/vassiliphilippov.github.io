calla

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæl.ə / (AmE), / ˈkal.ə / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Calla, especially the water arum, with heart-shaped leaves and a white spathe surrounding a yellow spadix, commonly found in wet places.
a plant of the genus Calla; water arum

Examples

A single calla stood out among the green reeds by the pond.
She arranged a bouquet featuring both lilies and calla blooms for the wedding.
Botanists studied the habitat of the wild calla in northern marshes.

Etymology

From Greek kallaia, a kind of plant, perhaps from kallos (beauty).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkælə / (AmE), / ˈkalə / (BrE)
A South African plant with a showy white spathe and yellow spadix, commonly grown as an ornamental.
South African ornamental plant with white spathe

Examples

The calla is prized for its elegant flowers in wedding bouquets.
She grows white calla in her garden every spring.
The florist used a single calla as the centerpiece of the arrangement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calla lily - Another name for the calla plant
  • Synonym: arum - A related plant genus with similar features

Etymology

From Latin 'calla', from Greek 'kallos' meaning 'beauty'.
