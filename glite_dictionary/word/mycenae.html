Mycenae

1. PROPN

/ maɪˈsiːni / (AmE), / maɪˈsiːniː / (BrE)
an ancient city in southern Greece, known as the center of Mycenaean civilization during the late Bronze Age
ancient city in Greece, Mycenaean center

Examples

Archaeologists have uncovered royal tombs and treasures in Mycenae.
Mycenae played a crucial role in Homeric legends, including the tale of Agamemnon.
We visited the ruins of Mycenae during our trip to southern Greece.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Μυκῆναι (Mukēnai), the name of the city.
