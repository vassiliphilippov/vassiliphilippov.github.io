Naja haje

1. NOUN

/ ˌneɪ.dʒə ˈhɑː.dʒeɪ / (AmE), / ˌneɪ.dʒə ˈhɑː.dʒiː / (BrE)
A species of cobra, native to Africa and known as the Egyptian cobra, historically used as a symbol of power by the Pharaohs.
a species of African cobra (Egyptian cobra)

Examples

The Naja haje is often depicted on the crowns of ancient Egyptian rulers.
Researchers observed a Naja haje basking in the sun near the Nile River.
The venom of the Naja haje can be dangerous to humans if untreated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cobra - a highly venomous snake, typically with a hood

Etymology

From New Latin, with 'Naja' derived from Sanskrit 'nāga' (snake), and 'haje' from Arabic 'ḥayya' (snake).
