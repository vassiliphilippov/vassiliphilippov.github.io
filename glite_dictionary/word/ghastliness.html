ghastliness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæs(t)li.nəs / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːs(t)li.nɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being shockingly frightful, horrible, or macabre.
the state of being ghastly or horrifying

Examples

The ghastliness of the crime scene left even the experienced detectives shaken.
She described the ghastliness of the accident in vivid detail.
War reveals the ghastliness that mankind is capable of.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ghastly - shockingly frightful or horrible

Etymology

From 'ghastly' + '-ness', where 'ghastly' comes from Middle English 'gastli' (frightful, inspired by terror).
