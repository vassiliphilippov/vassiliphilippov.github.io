seedman

1. NOUN

/ ˈsidˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈsiːdmən / (BrE)
a man who sells or deals in seeds, especially for planting
male dealer in seeds

Examples

The seedman suggested a variety of flowers for my new garden.
In the nineteenth century, a seedman was essential for farmers preparing to plant crops.
We ordered heritage vegetables from the local seedman last spring.

Etymology

From 'seed' + 'man', denoting occupation.
