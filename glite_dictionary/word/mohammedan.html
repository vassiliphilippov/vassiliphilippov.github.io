Mohammedan

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈhæmədən / (AmE), / məˈhæmɪdən / (BrE)
A follower of Mohammed; an old term for a Muslim, now considered outdated and sometimes offensive.
old term for Muslim; follower of Mohammed
archaic offensive

Examples

The old documents referred to the population as Mohammedan rather than Muslim.
Use of the term Mohammedan is discouraged today because it is considered outdated and inaccurate.
He wrote a book about the laws and customs of Mohammedan societies in the Middle Ages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Muslim - A follower of the religion of Islam

Etymology

From Mohammed + -an, via Medieval Latin 'Mahometanus' and Medieval French 'Mahometan'.

2. ADJ

/ moʊˈhæmədən / (AmE), / məˈhæmɪdən / (BrE)
of or relating to Muhammad, the founder of Islam, or to the religion of Islam (now considered archaic and often inappropriate; 'Muslim' is preferred)
relating to Muhammad or Islam (archaic)
archaic

Examples

In the 19th century, Western writers often referred to Islamic architecture as Mohammedan architecture.
The scholar wrote a detailed history of Mohammedan law in South Asia.
References to Mohammedan customs are found in many older academic texts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Muslim - a follower of the religion of Islam; relating to Islam or its followers

Etymology

From the name Muhammad (the prophet of Islam) with the English adjectival suffix '-an'.
