Lepisosteus osseus

1. NOUN

/ lɛpɪˈsɒstiːəs ˈɒsiəs / (AmE), / lɛpɪˈsɒstiːəs ˈɒsiəs / (BrE)
A species of primitive predatory North American fish, known as the longnose gar, covered with hard scales and possessing long jaws with needlelike teeth.
species of predatory North American fish

Examples

The Lepisosteus osseus can be found in slow-moving rivers throughout the eastern United States.
Biologists studied the jaw structure of Lepisosteus osseus as an example of ancient predatory fish.
Fossils indicate that Lepisosteus osseus has remained largely unchanged for millions of years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: longnose gar - Common name for Lepisosteus osseus, a North American gar fish

Etymology

From Greek lepis (scale) and osteon (bone); scientific Latin binomial.
