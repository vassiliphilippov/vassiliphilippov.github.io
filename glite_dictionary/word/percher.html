percher

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrtʃər / (AmE), / ˈpɜːtʃə / (BrE)
a bird with feet adapted for perching, especially one formerly classified in the now largely abandoned order Passeriformes.
bird with feet adapted for perching

Examples

The forest is home to many species of percher that fill the air with birdsong.
Some scientists no longer classify certain birds as perchers due to changes in taxonomy.
The robin is a common percher found in gardens and woodlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: passerine - songbird or perching bird of the order Passeriformes

Etymology

From 'perch' (to rest on something elevated) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrtʃər / (AmE), / ˈpɜːtʃə / (BrE)
a person who is seated or positioned on a perch, especially in a high or precarious place.
person seated on a perch

Examples

The daring percher balanced on the narrow ledge high above the crowd.
Acrobats and performers such as the tightrope percher amazed audiences with their skill.
The statue depicted a small percher resting atop the old city gate.

Etymology

Formed from 'perch' (a place to sit or rest, especially for a bird) with the agent suffix '-er'.
