photophobia

1. NOUN

/ ˌfoʊtəˈfoʊbiə / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəˈfəʊbiə / (BrE)
An abnormal fear of or sensitivity to light, often causing discomfort or pain in the eyes.
abnormal sensitivity or fear of light
medical

Examples

Migraine sufferers often experience photophobia during an attack.
Patients with meningitis may develop severe photophobia.
Wearing sunglasses indoors is sometimes a sign of photophobia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: light sensitivity - increased sensitivity of the eyes to light

Etymology

From Greek 'phōs, phōt-' (light) + '-phobia' (fear).

2. NOUN

/ ˌfoʊtəˈfoʊbiə / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəˈfəʊbiə / (BrE)
abnormal sensitivity to or discomfort in the eyes caused by exposure to light, often resulting in pain
sensitivity to light in the eyes
medical

Examples

The patient complained of photophobia after spending time in the brightly lit room.
Individuals with migraines often experience photophobia during an attack.
Wearing sunglasses helped alleviate her photophobia outdoors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: photosensitivity - an abnormal or heightened sensitivity to light, especially in the skin or eyes
  • Synonym: photophobia - pain or discomfort in the eyes due to light (the same concept)

Etymology

From Greek 'photo-' (light) and 'phobia' (fear).
