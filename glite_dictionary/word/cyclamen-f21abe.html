Cyclamen

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪkləmən / (AmE), / ˈsɪkləmən / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants native to Eurasia, recognized for their rounded heart-shaped leaves and attractive, usually pink or white flowers.
a genus of Eurasian flowering plants

Examples

My grandmother grows several varieties of Cyclamen in her garden every winter.
The delicate pink flowers of the Cyclamen are a favorite among horticulturalists.
Wild Cyclamen can be found blooming in woodlands throughout southern Europe.

Etymology

From Latin 'cyclamīnos', from Ancient Greek 'kyklamīnos', ultimately from 'kyklos' meaning 'circle', probably referring to the round tubers or flowers.
