Saul of Tarsus

1. PROPN

/ sɔl əv ˈtɑrsəs / (AmE), / sɔːl əv ˈtɑːsəs / (BrE)
the original name of Paul the Apostle, a Christian missionary and author of several books in the New Testament
original name of Paul the Apostle

Examples

Before his conversion, Saul of Tarsus was known for persecuting early Christians.
The dramatic journey of Saul of Tarsus on the road to Damascus is a well-known story in the New Testament.
Many historians study the life and writings of Saul of Tarsus to understand early Christianity.

Synset Examples

Paul's name was Saul prior to his conversion to Christianity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Paul the Apostle - Christian missionary and author of several New Testament books

Etymology

From Saul (a Hebrew given name) and Tarsus (an ancient city in modern-day Turkey).
