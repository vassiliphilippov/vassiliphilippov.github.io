acculturative

1. ADJ

/ əˈkʌl.tʃəˌreɪ.tɪv / (AmE), / əˈkʌl.tʃər.ə.tɪv / (BrE)
relating to or causing acculturation, especially as the process of cultural change and psychological change that results following meeting between cultures
relating to acculturation or cultural assimilation

Examples

The school developed acculturative programs to help immigrant students adapt.
Researchers studied the acculturative stress faced by people moving to a new country.
Language learning can play a significant acculturative role in multicultural societies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acculturation - the process of cultural change and adaptation resulting from contact between cultures

Etymology

From acculturation (from Latin 'ad-' to + 'cultura' cultivation) + -ive.
