to deny

1. VERB

/ dɪˈnaɪ / (AmE), / dɪˈnaɪ / (BrE)
(in law) to state that an allegation made by the opposing party is not true or not admitted, especially in a legal suit.
to formally dispute an allegation (legal)
legal

Examples

The defendant will deny the accusation in his official response to the court.
She instructed her lawyer to deny all claims in the complaint.
In their legal brief, the company formally denied the plaintiffs' allegations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to admit - to accept as true or valid (in law)

Etymology

From Old French *denier*, from Latin *denegare* (to refuse, deny).

2. VERB

/ dɪˈnaɪ / (AmE), / dɪˈnaɪ / (BrE)
to say that something is not true, especially by stating the contrary or rejecting an accusation
to declare something untrue

Examples

He strongly denied ever being involved in the scandal.
The company spokesperson denied the rumors about a merger.
She denied that she had received any secret payments.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to admit - to confess that something is true or to allow it is true
  • Synonym: to contradict - to assert the opposite of a statement; to deny the truth

Etymology

From Latin 'denegare' (to deny, refuse), from 'de-' (away) + 'negare' (to say no).
