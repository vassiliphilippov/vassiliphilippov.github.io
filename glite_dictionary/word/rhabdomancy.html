rhabdomancy

1. NOUN

/ ˈræb.dəˌmæn.si / (AmE), / ˈrab.də.mæn.si / (BrE)
the practice of searching for underground water or minerals using a divining rod
divining water or minerals with a rod

Examples

Some people still believe in rhabdomancy to locate underground springs.
The old farmer practiced rhabdomancy with a forked stick in search of water.
Skeptics dismiss rhabdomancy as a pseudoscience lacking empirical support.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dowsing - the practice of seeking water or minerals underground using a rod or pendulum

Etymology

From Greek 'rhabdos' (rod, wand) and 'manteia' (divination).
