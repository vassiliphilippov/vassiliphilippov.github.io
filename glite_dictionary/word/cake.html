cake

1. NOUN

/ keɪk / (AmE), / keɪk / (BrE)
a block or mass of a solid substance, especially one shaped for convenient use, such as soap or wax
block of a solid substance

Examples

He bought a cake of soap from the store.
A cake of beeswax was used for polishing the furniture.
The old laundry used a cake of washing powder for every load.

Synset Examples

a bar of chocolate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bar - a long block of solid substance, often referring to soap or chocolate

2. VERB

/ keɪk / (AmE), / keɪk / (BrE)
to form a hard layer or thick coating on the surface of something
form a hard coating or layer

Examples

After hiking through the mud, his boots were caked with dirt.
Snow caked the branches of the old pine tree during the storm.
Her hands were caked with flour after an afternoon of baking.

3. NOUN

/ keɪk / (AmE), / keɪk / (BrE)
a sweet baked food made from a mixture of flour, sugar, eggs, and fat, often with added flavoring, and usually cooked in an oven
sweet baked food

Examples

For her birthday, she baked a chocolate cake with strawberry icing.
The wedding cake was beautifully decorated with fresh flowers and pearls.
Would you like a slice of cake with your tea?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pie - a baked dish of fruit, meat, or vegetables with a pastry crust
  • Antonym: bread - food made of flour and water baked together

Etymology

From Old Norse 'kaka' (cake), related to Middle English 'cake'.

/ keɪk / (AmE), / keɪk / (BrE)

Each cake is made from finely ground fish mixed with spices.
