unfed

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfɛd / (AmE), / ʌnˈfɛd / (BrE)
not having been provided with food, nourishment, or support (literal or figurative).
not having received food or support

Examples

The rescued animals were weak and unfed after days of neglect.
A sense of resentment remained unfed, never growing into open conflict.
The unfed plants wilted quickly without the necessary nutrients.

Synset Examples

a grudge that remained unfed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hungry - feeling or displaying the need for food

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'fed' (past participle of 'feed').

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfɛd / (AmE), / ʌnˈfɛd / (BrE)
not having been given food; not fed.
not given food

Examples

The unfed puppies whimpered softly in the basket.
After a long day, he felt tired and unfed.
Rescue workers found several unfed animals in the abandoned building.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fed - having been given food

Etymology

From the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'fed', past participle of 'feed'.

WordNet ID: oewn-02309111-s
Synset POS: s
