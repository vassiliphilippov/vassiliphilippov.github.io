proturan

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈtʊrən / (AmE), / prəʊˈtjʊərən / (BrE)
A minute, primitive, wingless and eyeless insect of the order Protura, characterized by a cone-shaped head and typically inhabiting damp soil or decaying matter.
minute primitive wingless insect

Examples

Scientists discovered a rare proturan species while examining samples from the forest floor.
The proturan lacks eyes and wings, making it unique among soil-dwelling arthropods.
In biology class, we learned that a proturan primarily lives in damp leaf litter.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: insect - small arthropod animal with a three-part body
  • Degree Higher: arthropod - invertebrate animal having an exoskeleton, segmented body, and jointed appendages

Etymology

From New Latin 'Protura,' combining Greek 'protos' (first) and 'oura' (tail), referring to its primitive features.
