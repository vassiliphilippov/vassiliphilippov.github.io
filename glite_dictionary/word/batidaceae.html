Batidaceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌbætəˈdeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌbætɪˈdeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
A family of plants coextensive with the genus Batis, commonly known as saltworts, found in saline environments.
plant family containing saltworts

Examples

The Batidaceae are adapted to grow in coastal salt marshes and mangroves.
Researchers studied the salinity tolerance of plants in the Batidaceae family.
Only a few species belong to Batidaceae, making it a very small plant family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Batis - the only genus in the Batidaceae family

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus Batis + family suffix -aceae.
