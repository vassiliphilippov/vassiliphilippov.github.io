lady-slipper

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪdi ˌslɪpər / (AmE), / ˈleɪdi ˌslɪpə / (BrE)
a wild orchid of North America and Eurasia with a large, inflated pouchlike lip, often difficult to cultivate
a wild orchid with a pouchlike lip

Examples

We spotted a rare lady-slipper blooming along the forest trail.
The lady-slipper is admired for its unique, slipper-shaped flower.
Attempts to grow lady-slipper orchids in the garden have often failed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orchid - a plant of the family Orchidaceae, known for flowers of unusual shapes and colors

Etymology

Named for the shape of the flower's lip, which suggests a delicate slipper worn by a lady.
