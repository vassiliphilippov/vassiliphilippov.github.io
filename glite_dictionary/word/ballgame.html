ballgame

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔlˌɡeɪm / (AmE), / ˈbɔːlˌɡeɪm / (BrE)
a field game played with a ball, especially baseball.
field game played with a ball

Examples

The children spent the afternoon playing a ballgame in the park.
Baseball is often considered the most popular ballgame in America.
He had never attended a professional ballgame before last summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baseball - a game played with a bat and ball between two teams
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00472512-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɔlˌɡeɪm / (AmE), / ˈbɔːlˌɡeɪm / (BrE)
a situation or set of circumstances, especially one that is markedly different from those previously experienced; a particular context or environment, often used in the phrase 'a whole new ballgame' to mean a completely changed situation.
a radically different situation

Examples

With the new technology, it's a whole new ballgame for the industry.
Starting college is a completely different ballgame from high school life.
Negotiating with international partners is a different ballgame than working locally.

Synset Examples

HDTV looks the same but it's really a whole new ballgame

Etymology

Metaphorical extension from the literal 'ballgame', referring to a sporting event, to mean a situation with different rules or conditions.
