downside

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaʊn.saɪd / (AmE), / ˈdaʊn.saɪd / (BrE)
A disadvantage or negative aspect of something that is otherwise generally positive or beneficial.
disadvantage; negative aspect

Examples

The main downside of working from home is the lack of social interaction.
Although the new technology is impressive, it does have a significant downside.
There's always a downside to every major decision in life.

Synset Examples

there is a downside even to motherhood

Related Terms

  • Antonym: upside - an advantage or positive aspect of something
  • Synonym: disadvantage - an unfavorable condition or circumstance

Etymology

From 'down' + 'side', in figurative use from the 1970s.
