Bothrops

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊθ.rɑps / (AmE), / ˈbəʊ.θrɒps / (BrE)
A genus of highly venomous pit vipers found in Central and South America, commonly called fer-de-lance.
genus of venomous Latin American pit vipers

Examples

The Bothrops is responsible for many snakebite cases in Latin America.
Scientists studied the venom composition of a Bothrops specimen from Brazil.
Farmers in the region are taught how to recognize the dangerous Bothrops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fer-de-lance - common name for several venomous pit vipers of the genus Bothrops

Etymology

From Greek bothros (pit) + ops (face), referring to the pit organs on their faces.

2. NOUN

/ ˈboʊθrɑps / (AmE), / ˈbəʊθrɒps / (BrE)
a genus of venomous pit vipers found in Central and South America, including species known as fer-de-lance.
a genus of venomous pit vipers

Examples

The Bothrops genus includes some of the most dangerous snakes in South America.
Researchers studied the venom composition of different Bothrops species.
Many accidents in the Amazon are caused by bites from Bothrops snakes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fer-de-lance - a common name for several venomous snakes in the Bothrops genus

Etymology

From Greek 'bothros' (pit, trench) and 'ops' (face), referring to the pit organs on these snakes' heads.
