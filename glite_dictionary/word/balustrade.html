balustrade

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæləˌstreɪd / (AmE), / ˌbæl.əˈstreɪd / (BrE)
A railing supported by balusters, typically at the side of a staircase, balcony, or terrace, designed to prevent people from falling.
railing with balusters for safety

Examples

She admired the ornate balustrade lining the staircase in the old mansion.
Children leaned over the balustrade to watch the parade pass by below.
The stone balustrade provided both safety and elegance to the garden terrace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: railing - a fence or barrier made of rails

Etymology

From French 'balustrade', from Italian 'balaustrata', related to 'balaustro' (pomegranate flower, referring to the shape of the balusters).
