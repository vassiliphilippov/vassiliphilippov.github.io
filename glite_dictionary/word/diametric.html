diametric

1. ADJ

/ ˌdaɪəˈmɛtrɪk / (AmE), / ˌdaɪəˈmɛtrɪk / (BrE)
relating to, measured along, or situated on a diameter.
relating to a diameter

Examples

The engineer checked the diametric alignment of the shaft to ensure accuracy.
In geometry, a diametric line passes through the center of a circle, connecting two points on the circumference.
The strength of the pipe was tested along its diametric axis.

Synset Examples

the diametral plane

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diametral - relating to or measured along a diameter

Etymology

From Greek diameter, via Latin diametricus, relating to a diameter.

2. ADJ

/ ˌdaɪ.əˈmɛ.trɪk / (AmE), / ˌdaɪ.əˈmɛ.trɪk / (BrE)
completely opposed; being at opposite extremes
completely opposed; at opposite extremes

Examples

There are diametric differences between the two political parties' views on the issue.
Her beliefs are in diametric opposition to mine regarding this topic.
The committee members held diametric positions on climate policy, making agreement difficult.

Synset Examples

in diametric contradiction to his claims
diametrical (or opposite) points of view
opposite meanings
extreme and indefensible polar positions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opposite - completely different or contrary
  • Synonym: antithetical - directly opposed or contrasted; mutually incompatible

Etymology

From Greek diametrikos, from diametros (diameter), referring to being at opposite ends of a diameter.
