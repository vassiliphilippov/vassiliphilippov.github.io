horridness

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔrɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈhɒrɪdnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being extremely unpleasant or dreadful.
quality of being extremely unpleasant

Examples

The horridness of the crime shocked the entire community.
He couldn't shake off the horridness of the experience for days afterward.
Her expression revealed her dismay at the horridness of the meal served.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horror - an intense feeling of fear, shock, or disgust
  • Antonym: pleasantness - the quality of being pleasing or agreeable

Etymology

From horrid + -ness, where 'horrid' is from Latin horridus (frightful, dreadful).
