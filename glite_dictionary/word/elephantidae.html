Elephantidae

1. NOUN

/ ɛˌlæfənˈtaɪdiː / (AmE), / ɛˌlæfənˈtɪədiː / (BrE)
the biological family containing elephants and their extinct relatives, such as mammoths and mastodons
the elephant family (biological taxonomy)

Examples

The Elephantidae are distinguished by their large size and distinctive trunk.
Fossil evidence shows that Elephantidae once included many now-extinct species.
Biologists classify African and Asian elephants within the family Elephantidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elephant - a large, grey mammal with a trunk, native to Africa and Asia

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Elephantidae', based on 'elephas, elephant-' meaning 'elephant'.
