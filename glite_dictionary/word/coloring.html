coloring

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌlɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkʌlərɪŋ / (BrE)
a substance that is safe to eat and is added to food or drink to change or enhance its color
a substance added to food to provide color

Examples

Some fruit drinks contain artificial coloring to enhance their appearance.
The label states that no coloring has been added to these candies.
Red beet juice is often used as a natural coloring in foods.

Synset Examples

food color made from vegetable dyes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: food coloring - a substance used specifically to color food and drink
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʌlɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkʌlərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of changing the color of something; the application or effect of color
act of changing color; application of color

Examples

The coloring of the fabric took several hours to complete.
She enjoyed experimenting with different methods of coloring Easter eggs.
Natural coloring agents are often used in food production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dyeing - the process of coloring something by soaking it in a dye

Etymology

From the verb 'color' + -ing suffix.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkʌlɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkʌl(ə)rɪŋ / (BrE)
the visual appearance of something with regard to the colors it shows, caused by the light it reflects, emits, or transmits
visual attribute related to color

Examples

The autumn leaves have a beautiful coloring that attracts many visitors to the park.
Certain minerals can be identified by the unique coloring they display under light.
The bird's distinctive coloring helps it blend into its surroundings.

Synset Examples

a white color is made up of many different wavelengths of light

Related Terms

  • Synonym: color - the property possessed by an object of producing different sensations on the eye as a result of the way it reflects or emits light

Etymology

From the verb 'color' (from Old French 'colour', from Latin 'color'), with the suffix '-ing'.
