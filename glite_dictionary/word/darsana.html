Darsana

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑːr.sə.nə / (AmE), / ˈdɑː.sə.nə / (BrE)
Any of the six orthodox systems of Hindu philosophy, each offering a distinct view of the nature of reality and the means of liberation from karma.
Hindu philosophical system or viewpoint

Examples

Adi Shankara contributed significantly to the Advaita Vedanta Darsana.
Each Darsana presents a unique perspective on reality and the path to enlightenment.
The six Darsanas of Hinduism are often studied by scholars worldwide.

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'darśana' (seeing, view, philosophical system).
