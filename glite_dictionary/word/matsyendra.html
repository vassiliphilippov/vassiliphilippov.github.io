Matsyendra

1. NOUN

/ mʌtˈsjɛn.drə / (AmE), / matˈsjɛn.drə / (BrE)
In Hinduism and yoga, a seated twisting posture named after the sage Matsyendra.
yoga posture; a seated twist

Examples

The instructor demonstrated Matsyendra as a classic spinal twist in yoga class.
Many yoga practitioners perform Matsyendra to increase flexibility in the back and hips.
She studied the proper alignment for Matsyendra to avoid strain during her meditation.

Etymology

From Sanskrit Matsyendra, the name of a Hindu sage; from matsya (fish) + indra (lord).
