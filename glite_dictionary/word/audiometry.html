audiometry

1. NOUN

/ ˌɔdiˈɑmətɹi / (AmE), / ˌɔːdiˈɒmɪtɹi / (BrE)
The measurement of the range and sensitivity of a person's sense of hearing, typically using specific tests and procedures.
measurement of hearing sensitivity
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended an audiometry test to evaluate her hearing loss.
Routine audiometry is important for workers exposed to loud noise.
He specializes in audiometry and treats patients with hearing disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hearing test - a procedure to assess hearing ability

Etymology

From Latin 'audīre' (to hear) and Greek 'metria' (measuring).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑdiˈɑmətri / (AmE), / ˌɔːdiˈɒmɪtri / (BrE)
the measurement of a person's hearing ability using standardized tests and techniques
measurement of hearing ability
medical

Examples

The doctor recommended audiometry to assess the extent of her hearing loss.
Routine audiometry is part of many annual checkups for people working in noisy environments.
Modern audiometry uses advanced equipment to produce accurate results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: audiology - the branch of science and medicine concerned with the sense of hearing

Etymology

From Latin 'audīre' (to hear) and Greek '-metry' (process of measuring).
