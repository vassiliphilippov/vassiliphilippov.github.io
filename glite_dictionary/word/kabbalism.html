kabbalism

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːbəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈkæbəˌlɪzəm / (BrE)
The mystical and esoteric system of interpretation originating in Jewish tradition known as Kabbalah; in extended use, adherence to secret or arcane theological doctrines.
Jewish mystical tradition; secret theological doctrine

Examples

Her interest in kabbalism led her to study ancient Hebrew texts and mystical teachings.
Some scholars criticize excessive kabbalism as obscuring the practical aspects of religion.
The novel weaves elements of kabbalism into its plot, blending mystery with spiritual teachings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kabbalah - Jewish mystical tradition

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'Cabbalismus', based on Hebrew 'qabbālāh' (tradition, received doctrine).
