preteen

1. ADJ

/ priˈtin / (AmE), / priˈtiːn / (BrE)
Relating to or designed for children between the ages of 9 and 12.
for or about ages 9–12

Examples

The store offers a wide selection of preteen clothing for both boys and girls.
She organized a preteen birthday party with games and music for her daughter's friends.
That movie is especially popular with the preteen audience.

Synset Examples

a preteen party
preteen clothing

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: teen - a person aged 13 to 19; relating to teenagers

Etymology

From prefix 'pre-' (before) + 'teen', referring to the years before the teenage years.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpriˈtin / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈtiːn / (BrE)
a child who is not yet a teenager, typically between the ages of 9 and 12
child aged 9–12, not yet a teenager

Examples

The camp was designed specifically for preteens to foster confidence and teamwork.
Many preteens start to become more independent as they approach their teenage years.
The book series is popular among preteen readers for its relatable characters.

Synset Examples

little league is intended for the preteens

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: teenager - a person aged from 13 to 19 years old

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'pre-' (before) and 'teen', meaning before the teenage years.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09650451-n
Synset POS: n
