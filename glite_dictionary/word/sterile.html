sterile

1. ADJ

/ ˈstɛrəl / (AmE), / ˈstɪərʌɪl / (BrE)
lacking originality, creativity, or imaginative power; unproductive or uninspired
lacking creativity or originality

Examples

The committee rejected the sterile proposal, calling for more innovative ideas.
Critics described the play as sterile and lacking imaginative spark.
His lecture was informative but ultimately sterile, failing to inspire the audience.

Synset Examples

a sterile ideology lacking in originality
unimaginative development of a musical theme
uninspired writing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uninspired - lacking in inspiration or originality
  • Antonym: original - marked by creative or inventive power
  • Antonym: creative - showing creativity or originality

Etymology

From Latin sterilis (unfruitful, barren).

2. ADJ

/ ˈstɛrəl / (AmE), / ˈstɛrɪl / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) unable to produce offspring; incapable of reproducing.
unable to reproduce

Examples

The vet confirmed that the cat was sterile and could not have kittens.
After years of trying, the couple learned that one of them was sterile.
Some plants are bred to be sterile, so they cannot produce seeds.

Synset Examples

an infertile couple

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infertile - unable to reproduce or conceive offspring
  • Antonym: fertile - able to produce offspring or support reproduction

Etymology

From Latin 'sterilis' meaning 'unproductive, barren'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01005778-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ˈstɛrəl / (AmE), / ˈstɛr.aɪl / (BrE)
completely free from living microorganisms, especially harmful bacteria or viruses; using or relating to methods that keep something free from pathogens
free from microorganisms (medical and hygiene)
medical

Examples

All surgical equipment must be sterile before use on a patient.
The laboratory maintains a sterile environment to prevent contamination of experiments.
She wore gloves and a mask to keep everything as sterile as possible during the procedure.

Synset Examples

a sterile operating area
aseptic surgical instruments
aseptic surgical techniques

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aseptic - free from contamination caused by harmful bacteria, viruses, or other microorganisms
  • Antonym: contaminated - having been made impure by exposure to or addition of a poisonous or polluting substance

Etymology

From Latin sterilis (unproductive, barren).
