pertly

1. ADV

/ ˈpɝtli / (AmE), / ˈpɜːtli / (BrE)
in a bold, impudent, or forward manner; behaving or speaking with cheeky confidence or impertinence
in an impudent or forward manner

Examples

She answered the teacher pertly and made the whole class laugh.
The boy pertly challenged his parents' decision, surprising everyone.
The actress delivered her lines pertly, adding wit and mischief to the character.

Synset Examples

a lean, swarthy fellow was peering through the window, grinning impudently

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impudently - in an impertinent or disrespectful manner

Etymology

Formed from the adjective 'pert' (from Middle English, probably from Old French, meaning 'bold, impertinent') plus '-ly' adverb suffix.
