photometer

1. NOUN

/ foʊˈtɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / fəʊˈtɒmɪtə / (BrE)
an instrument for measuring the intensity or brightness of light
instrument for measuring light intensity

Examples

The scientist used a photometer to accurately measure the brightness of the stars.
Modern cameras often contain a built-in photometer to help adjust exposure settings.
Before the experiment, she calibrated the photometer to ensure precise readings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: light meter - an instrument for measuring the amount of light, especially in photography

Etymology

From Greek 'phōs' (light) + 'metron' (measure).

2. NOUN

/ foʊˈtɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / fəʊˈtɒmɪtə / (BrE)
An instrument for measuring the intensity of light, especially by comparing the brightness of a source with a standard source, either visually or using a photoelectric device.
instrument for measuring light intensity

Examples

The scientist used a photometer to measure the brightness of the lamp.
Modern cameras often contain a built-in photometer for automatic exposure control.
Before digital sensors, astronomers relied on a photometer to compare starlight intensities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: light meter - device for measuring the intensity of light

Etymology

From Greek phōt- (light) + -meter (measuring device).
