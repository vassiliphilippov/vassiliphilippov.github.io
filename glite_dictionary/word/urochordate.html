urochordate

1. NOUN

/ ˌjʊroʊˈkɔrˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˌjʊərəˈkɔːdeɪt / (BrE)
A primitive marine animal with a saclike, unsegmented body and a notochord visible in the larval stage; member of the subphylum Urochordata.
primitive marine animal (subphylum Urochordata)

Examples

Scientists collected several urochordate specimens from the coral reef for further study.
The urochordate is unique among chordates because its notochord appears only in its larval form.
Many textbooks describe the urochordate as an important link in the evolution of vertebrates.

Etymology

From Greek 'oura' (tail) and 'chordē' (string, cord), referring to the tail chord (notochord) present in the larval stage.
