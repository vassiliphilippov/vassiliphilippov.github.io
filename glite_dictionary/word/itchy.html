itchy

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪtʃi / (AmE), / ˈɪtʃi / (BrE)
feeling nervous, restless, or unable to relax; having a constant desire to move or act.
restless or unable to relax

Examples

She felt itchy sitting in the long meeting, unable to concentrate or keep still.
When he had nothing to do, he became itchy and started pacing the room.
After two weeks in the same place, they both grew itchy for some adventure.

Synset Examples

a constant fretful stamping of hooves
a restless child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restless - unable to rest or relax, constantly moving or active
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02414962-s

2. ADJ

/ ˈɪtʃi / (AmE), / ˈɪtʃi / (BrE)
Having or causing an unpleasant sensation on the skin that makes you want to scratch.
causing or feeling a need to scratch skin

Examples

After walking through tall grass, my arms became itchy and red.
Her mosquito bites were so itchy that she had to wear long sleeves.
The new wool sweater feels itchy against my skin.

Synset Examples

he had an itchy toe from the mosquito bite

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scratchy - causing discomfort or irritation, especially to the skin
  • Synonym: pruritic - relating to or characterized by itching, especially as a medical condition

Etymology

From 'itch' (Old English 'giccan') + '-y' (adjective suffix).
