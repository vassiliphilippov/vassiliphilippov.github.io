snappishness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsnæp.ɪʃ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsnæp.ɪʃ.nəs / (BrE)
a tendency to be irritable or easily angered, especially in speech or manner
tendency to be irritable or easily angered

Examples

His snappishness was more obvious whenever he felt stressed at work.
The teacher's snappishness made the students reluctant to ask questions.
She apologized for her snappishness after realizing she had been too harsh.

Synset Examples

his temper was well known to all his employees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritability - a tendency to become easily annoyed
  • Antonym: calmness - state of being calm or unperturbed

Etymology

From 'snappish' + '-ness', with 'snappish' first recorded in the 16th century, from 'snap'.
