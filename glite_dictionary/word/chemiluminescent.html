chemiluminescent

1. ADJ

/ ˌkɛməluˌmɪnəˈsɛnt / (AmE), / ˌkɛməluːmɪˈnɛsənt / (BrE)
emitting light as a result of a chemical reaction, without heat
emitting light through chemical reaction
medical

Examples

Bioluminescent organisms like fireflies are naturally chemiluminescent due to special enzymes in their bodies.
The lab technician observed a chemiluminescent reaction when the chemicals were mixed in the test tube.
Many modern forensic tests rely on chemiluminescent compounds to detect trace amounts of blood.

Synset Examples

fireflies are chemiluminescent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: luminescent - emitting light not caused by heat
  • Antonym: incandescent - emitting light as a result of being heated

Etymology

From 'chemiluminescence', from 'chemical' + 'luminescence'.
