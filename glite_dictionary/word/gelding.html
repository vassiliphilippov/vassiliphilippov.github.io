gelding

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɛldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡɛldɪŋ / (BrE)
A male horse that has been castrated, usually to make it more manageable or easier to train.
castrated male horse

Examples

The ranch purchased a new gelding for their riding school.
After being trained, the gelding became very gentle and easy to handle.
Unlike stallions, a gelding is often preferred for children and beginners.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stallion - an uncastrated adult male horse
  • Antonym: mare - an adult female horse

Etymology

From Old Norse *gelda* (to castrate); related to Old English *geldan* (to pay).
