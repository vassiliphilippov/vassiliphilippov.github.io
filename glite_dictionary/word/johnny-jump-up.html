Johnny-jump-up

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɑni ˌdʒʌmp ˈʌp / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒni ˌdʒʌmp ˈʌp / (BrE)
a small European wildflower (Viola tricolor) with purple, yellow, and white petals, often cultivated as a garden plant and a progenitor of the garden pansy
small European wildflower; progenitor of pansy

Examples

The Johnny-jump-up blooms early in the spring, bringing color to the garden.
Many gardeners plant Johnny-jump-ups for their vibrant petals and resilience.
The delicate Johnny-jump-up is often used to create a natural, wildflower effect in flower beds.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɑːni ˈdʒʌmp ʌp / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒni ˈdʒʌmp ʌp / (BrE)
A common North American violet with large pale blue or purple flowers that resemble pansies.
a type of violet with pansy-like flowers

Examples

Every spring, Johnny-jump-ups bloom in the meadow behind our house.
She planted Johnny-jump-ups along the garden path to add a splash of color.
Despite their small size, Johnny-jump-ups are surprisingly hardy and easy to grow.
