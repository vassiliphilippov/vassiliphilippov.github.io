to reinterpret

1. VERB

/ ˌriːɪn.tɚˈprɛt / (AmE), / ˌriːɪn.təˈprɛt / (BrE)
to interpret something in a different way or from a new perspective
interpret differently

Examples

Scholars continue to reinterpret the novel’s themes in light of recent social changes.
The director decided to reinterpret the classic play by setting it in modern times.
Over the years, historians have reinterpreted the event based on new evidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to interpret - to explain the meaning of something

Etymology

Formed from re- (again) + interpret (from Latin interpretari, to explain)

2. VERB

/ ˌriːɪnˈtɝːprət / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈtɜːprət / (BrE)
to assign a new or different meaning to something; to interpret again, especially in a different way.
assign a new or different meaning

Examples

The director chose to reinterpret the classic novel as a modern-day thriller.
Historians often reinterpret old documents in the light of new evidence.
Artists sometimes reinterpret traditional themes to reflect contemporary issues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to interpret - to explain the meaning of something

Etymology

From re- (again) + interpret, from Latin interpretari (to explain).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00625812-v
Synset POS: v
