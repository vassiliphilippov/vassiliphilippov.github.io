Anethum graveolens

1. NOUN

/ əˈniːθəm ˌɡræviˈoʊlənz / (AmE), / əˈniːθəm ˌɡræviˈəʊlɛnz / (BrE)
an aromatic herb, native to Eurasia, with feathery leaves and seeds used in cooking, also known as dill
aromatic herb; botanical name for dill

Examples

The seeds of Anethum graveolens are widely used as a spice in pickling recipes.
Botanists classify dill as Anethum graveolens in the Apiaceae family.
The delicate leaves of Anethum graveolens add flavor to many Mediterranean dishes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dill - common name for the aromatic herb Anethum graveolens

Etymology

From Latin 'anethum' (dill) and 'graveolens' (strong-smelling).
