Brachiopoda

1. NOUN

/ ˌbrækiˈɒpədə / (AmE), / ˌbrækɪˈɒpədə / (BrE)
A phylum of marine invertebrate animals that resemble mollusks but have upper and lower shells of different sizes.
phylum of marine invertebrates

Examples

Fossils of Brachiopoda are commonly found in Paleozoic rock layers.
Unlike mollusks, Brachiopoda have lophophores for feeding.
The scientist studied the shells of different species within Brachiopoda.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: mollusk - any invertebrate of the phylum Mollusca, such as snails, clams, or squids

Etymology

From Greek *brakhion* (arm) + *pous* (foot), referring to their arm-like feeding organs.
