bacciferous

1. ADJ

/ bækˈsɪfərəs / (AmE), / bækˈsɪfərəs / (BrE)
(of a plant) producing or bearing berries.
bearing berries (botany)

Examples

The bacciferous shrubs thrive in moist woodland areas.
Many birds are attracted to bacciferous plants for their abundant food supply.
The bacciferous characteristic of the bush makes it popular with gardeners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: berry - a small roundish fruit

Etymology

From Latin 'bacca' (berry) + '-ferous' (bearing).
