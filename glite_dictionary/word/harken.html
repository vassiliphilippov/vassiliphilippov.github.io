to harken

1. VERB

/ ˈhɑrkən / (AmE), / ˈhɑːkən / (BrE)
(archaic) To listen attentively or give heed; often used in the imperative.
(archaic) listen; pay attention
archaic

Examples

Harken to the story I am about to tell.
The leader called, "Harken!" and the crowd fell silent.
If you would harken to his advice, you might succeed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hearken - listen attentively (archaic spelling/variant)

Etymology

From Middle English *herken*, related to Old English *heorcian* (to listen).
