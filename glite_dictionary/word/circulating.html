circulating

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɝːkjəˌleɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɜːkjʊˌleɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
(of money, information, or objects) passing from person to person or place to place; in active movement or exchange.
moving from one to another; in circulation

Examples

The circulating rumors caused confusion among the staff.
Only circulating money is counted in the economy’s liquidity.
She inspected the circulating library books for any damage.

Synset Examples

circulating bills and coins

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in circulation - actively being passed around or exchanged
  • Antonym: static - not moving or being exchanged

Etymology

Present participle of the verb 'circulate', from Latin 'circulare' (to make round, circulate).
