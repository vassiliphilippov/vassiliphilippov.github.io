silverfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvərˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈsɪl.və.fɪʃ / (BrE)
a silvery variety of the goldfish, Carassius auratus, often kept as an aquarium fish
silvery type of goldfish

Examples

The pet store had a beautiful silverfish swimming among the other goldfish.
I bought a silverfish for my aquarium because of its unique color.
Silverfish are known for their metallic sheen and graceful movements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goldfish - a small golden or orange-red fish often kept in ponds or aquariums

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪlvərˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvəˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A small, silver-grey, wingless insect often found in houses, known to feed on book bindings, paper, and clothing.
small silver-grey household insect

Examples

I noticed a silverfish crawling across the bathroom floor last night.
Old books are sometimes damaged by silverfish that feed on the bindings.
We had to call an exterminator because our closet was infested with silverfish.

Etymology

Refers to the insect's silvery coloring and fish-like shape or movement.
