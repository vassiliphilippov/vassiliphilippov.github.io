suspension point

1. NOUN

/ səˈspɛnʃən pɔɪnt / (AmE), / səˈspɛnʃən pɔɪnt / (BrE)
(usually plural) a mark or one of three consecutive marks (‘…’) used in writing to indicate that something is omitted or that a sentence is incomplete.
punctuation mark indicating omission

Examples

The author used suspension points to suggest that the character's thought trailed off.
A series of suspension points can indicate hesitation or a pause in dialogue.
You should insert suspension points when omitting part of a quotation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ellipsis - the omission of a word or words replaced by three dots (‘…’); also the name for the mark itself
