bellowing

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛloʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɛləʊɪŋ / (BrE)
a very loud, deep shout or roar, like the sound made by an animal
a loud, deep shout or roar

Examples

The bellowing of the wounded animal echoed through the forest.
A sudden bellowing from the crowd startled everyone in the stadium.
The child's bellowing could be heard from the next room.

Synset Examples

his bellow filled the hallway

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roar - a loud, deep sound, often from an animal or crowd
  • Antonym: shriek - a high-pitched, loud, piercing cry

Etymology

From Middle English bellen (to roar), related to Old English bylgan.
