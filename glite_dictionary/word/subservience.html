subservience

1. NOUN

/ səbˈsɝː.vi.əns / (AmE), / səbˈsɜː.vi.əns / (BrE)
The state or quality of being useful or subordinate to achieving an end or carrying out a plan; submissive or subordinate behavior.
usefulness or submissiveness in serving a goal

Examples

All his actions were in subservience to the general plan.
She resented the expectation of complete subservience to her manager's wishes.
The committee valued creativity over blind subservience to old procedures.

Synset Examples

all his actions were in subservience to the general plan

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subordination - the act of placing in a lower rank or position; subjection
  • Antonym: independence - the state of being self-reliant, not subject to control by others

Etymology

From Latin 'subservientia', from 'subserviens', present participle of 'subservire' (to serve under).

2. NOUN

/ səbˈsɝː.vi.əns / (AmE), / səbˈsɜː.vi.əns / (BrE)
abject or excessively submissive behavior; willingness to obey others unquestioningly.
abject submissiveness or obedience

Examples

Her subservience to her boss made her unpopular with her colleagues.
The film depicted the subservience of women in a patriarchal society.
He rejected the subservience expected of him and spoke out for his rights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: servility - excessive willingness to serve or please others
  • Antonym: independence - freedom from control or influence of others

Etymology

From Latin 'subservientia', from 'subserviens' (being useful, subservient).

3. NOUN

/ səbˈsɝː.vi.əns / (AmE), / səbˈsɜː.vi.əns / (BrE)
the condition of being excessively willing to obey others or serve their interests; submissiveness or servility.
state of being submissive or servile

Examples

Her subservience to her boss was noticed by everyone in the office.
The politician was criticized for his subservience to powerful corporate interests.
Generations of subservience left the community hesitant to assert itself.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: servility - excessive willingness to serve or please others
  • Antonym: dominance - the state of being dominant or in control

Etymology

From Latin 'subserviens', present participle of 'subservire' (to serve under).
