lemniscus

1. NOUN

/ lɛmˈnɪskəs / (AmE), / lɛmˈnɪskəs / (BrE)
a bundle of sensory nerve fibers that transmit information to the thalamus in the brain
bundle of sensory nerve fibers to the thalamus
medical

Examples

The medial lemniscus plays a crucial role in transmitting touch and proprioceptive signals to the brain.
Damage to the lemniscus can result in sensory deficits and impaired coordination.
Researchers studied the function of the lateral lemniscus in auditory processing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tract - a bundle of nerve fibers in the central nervous system

Etymology

From Latin *lemniscus* (ribbon, band), from Greek *lēmniskos* (ribbon).
