Electrophorus electricus

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlektrəˈfɔrɪs ɪˌlɛkˈtrɪkəs / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəˈfɔːrəs ɪˌlɛkˈtrɪkəs / (BrE)
an eel-shaped freshwater fish of South America, notable for its ability to generate powerful electric shocks used for hunting and self-defense
South American electric eel species

Examples

The Electrophorus electricus can generate electric shocks to stun its prey in the murky waters of the Amazon.
Scientists have studied the Electrophorus electricus to understand how it produces electricity in its body.
A large Electrophorus electricus may produce a shock powerful enough to deter predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: electric eel - common name for Electrophorus electricus

Etymology

From Greek 'ēlektron' (amber, referring to electricity) and Latin 'phorus' (bearing), with 'electricus' meaning 'of electricity'.
