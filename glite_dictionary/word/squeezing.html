squeezing

1. NOUN

/ ˈskwizɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskwiːzɪŋ / (BrE)
the action of gripping and pressing something firmly, especially with the hand
the act of gripping and pressing firmly

Examples

She felt his squeezing of her hand before he let go.
After the squeezing of the lemon, all the juice was gone.
A gentle squeezing of the shoulder can be very reassuring.

Synset Examples

he gave her cheek a playful squeeze

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squeeze - the act of pressing something firmly, especially with the hand

Etymology

From the verb 'squeeze,' which likely comes from Middle English 'squesen,' perhaps of Scandinavian origin.
