whorl

1. NOUN

/ wɜrl / (AmE), / wɜːl / (BrE)
a pattern or structure consisting of something wound in a continuous series of loops or circles
a coil or series of loops

Examples

She admired the whorl of smoke rising from his cigarette.
He noticed a whorl in the bark pattern on the tree.
The potter shaped the clay into a tight whorl.

Synset Examples

a coil of rope

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coil - a length of something wound in a spiral or ring
  • Synonym: spiral - a curve that winds around a central point, gradually getting further from it

Etymology

From Middle English 'whorle', meaning a small wheel or spindle, perhaps related to Old English 'hweorfa' (something that turns).

2. NOUN

/ wɜrl / (AmE), / wɜːl / (BrE)
A strand or lock of hair, or any formation, that grows or forms in a spiral or circular pattern.
spiral-shaped lock of hair

Examples

A single whorl on her forehead made her hair stand out from the rest.
The barber noticed a stubborn whorl at the crown of his head.
Some cows have a distinctive whorl of hair between their horns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curl - A piece of hair that forms a spiral shape

Etymology

From Middle English whorle, probably a variant of whirl, influenced by Old English hweorfa (a circle or disk).

3. NOUN

/ wɜrl / (AmE), / wɜːl / (BrE)
a pattern or formation in which a number of similar parts, shapes, or lines circle around a common center point, especially as seen in leaves, petals, or circular markings
circular pattern or arrangement

Examples

The sunflower's seeds are arranged in a distinctive whorl pattern at its center.
Each petal grew outward from the stem in a perfect whorl.
His fingerprint had a unique whorl that made it easy to identify.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiral - curve winding around a center point in a continuous series

Etymology

From Old English *hwearf* (a turn, circle), related to the verb 'whirl'.
