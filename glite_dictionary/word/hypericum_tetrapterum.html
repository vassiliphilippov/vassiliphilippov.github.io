Hypericum tetrapterum

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈpɛrɪkəm ˌtɛtræpˈtɜrəm / (AmE), / hɪˈpɛrɪkəm ˌtɛtræpˈtiːrəm / (BrE)
A European perennial species of St John's wort found from Ireland and France to western Siberia, known for its yellow flowers.
a European species of St John's wort

Examples

Hypericum tetrapterum is commonly found in wet meadows throughout western Europe.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of Hypericum tetrapterum for its potential use in herbal treatments.
The bright yellow flowers of Hypericum tetrapterum make it easy to identify in the wild.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: St John's wort - plants of the genus Hypericum with yellow flowers

Etymology

From Greek *hyperikon* (St John's wort) and Latin *tetrapterum* (four-winged), referring to the plant's seed capsules.
