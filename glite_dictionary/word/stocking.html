stocking

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɑkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstɒkɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of supplying or replenishing a stock of something, such as animals, goods, or supplies
act of supplying or replenishing a stock

Examples

The hatchery is responsible for the stocking of rivers with young salmon each spring.
Effective stocking of shelves ensures customers can always find what they need.
The stocking of the lake with fish boosted local tourism.

Synset Examples

he supervised the stocking of the stream with trout

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stock - to supply or fill with a stock of something

Etymology

From the verb 'stock' (to supply or fill with stock) with the noun-forming suffix '-ing'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɑːkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstɒkɪŋ / (BrE)
A close-fitting garment, usually made of nylon, silk, or cotton, that covers the foot and the leg up to the knee or sometimes the thigh, typically worn in matched pairs.
close-fitting leg covering garment

Examples

She pulled up her stocking so that it covered her knee.
A run in her stocking made her late for work.
He bought a new pair of stockings as a gift for his wife.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sock - shorter hosiery covering the foot and part of the leg

Etymology

From Old English *stocc* (stump, stick), referring to the part of the leg, plus the diminutive suffix *-ing*.
