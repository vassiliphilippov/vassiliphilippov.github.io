Salix herbacea

1. NOUN

/ ˈseɪ.lɪks hərˈbeɪ.ʃi.ə / (AmE), / ˈseɪ.lɪks hɜːˈbeɪ.sɪə / (BrE)
A small, boreal shrubby willow with creeping stems and bright green glossy leaves, found in northern regions.
boreal shrubby willow species

Examples

Salix herbacea is often found in alpine and arctic tundra environments.
Researchers study Salix herbacea to understand plant adaptation in cold climates.
The bright leaves of Salix herbacea stand out against the rocky landscape.

Etymology

From Latin: 'salix' meaning 'willow' and 'herbacea' meaning 'herb-like'.
