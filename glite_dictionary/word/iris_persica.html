Iris persica

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪrɪs pərˈsɪkə / (AmE), / ˈaɪərɪs pəˈsɪkə / (BrE)
a bulbous iris native to Asia Minor, cultivated for its pale lilac-colored flowers
a bulbous iris species with pale lilac flowers

Examples

The delicate blooms of Iris persica add a touch of elegance to spring gardens.
Gardeners prize Iris persica for its pale lilac-colored flowers and unique fragrance.
Botanists studied Iris persica to learn more about the diversity of Asiatic bulbous plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iris - a genus of flowering plants with showy flowers

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'Persian iris', referring to its geographic origin.
