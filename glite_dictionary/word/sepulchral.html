sepulchral

1. ADJ

/ səˈpʌlkrəl / (AmE), / sɪˈpʌlkrəl / (BrE)
relating to a tomb or burial; suggestive of a grave or burial place
relating to a tomb or burial

Examples

The cathedral was filled with a sepulchral silence as the mourners entered.
He studied the sepulchral monuments in the ancient graveyard.
Her tone was so sepulchral that it unnerved everyone in the room.

Synset Examples

sepulchral inscriptions
sepulchral monuments in churches

Related Terms

  • Synonym: funereal - having the mournful, somber character of a funeral

Etymology

From Latin sepulcralis, from sepulcrum (tomb, burial place).

3. ADJ

/ səˈpʌlkrəl / (AmE), / sɪˈpʌlkrəl / (BrE)
Suggestive of a tomb, death, or the dead; gloomy and dismal in a way that evokes the atmosphere of a grave or burial place.
gloomy or deathlike; tomb-like

Examples

A sepulchral silence filled the abandoned house, making everyone uneasy.
Her voice took on a sepulchral tone as she described the tragedy.
The sepulchral atmosphere of the crypt made visitors whisper in hushed voices.

Synset Examples

a charnel smell came from the chest filled with dead men's bones
ghastly shrieks
the sepulchral darkness of the catacombs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: funereal - having the mournful, somber character of a funeral
  • Antonym: lively - full of life and energy

Etymology

From Latin sepulcralis, from sepulcrum (tomb, grave).
