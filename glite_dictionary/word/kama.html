Kama

1. PROPN

/ ˈkɑːmə / (AmE), / ˈkɑːmə / (BrE)
In Hinduism, the god of love and erotic desire.
Hindu god of love and desire

Examples

In Hindu mythology, Kama is often depicted wielding a bow made of sugarcane.
Devotees sometimes offer prayers to Kama for happiness and romance in life.
The legend describes how Shiva burned Kama to ashes with his third eye.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Mara - Buddhist demon symbolizing temptation and desire, considered the opposite of Kama

Etymology

From Sanskrit kāma (desire, love).
