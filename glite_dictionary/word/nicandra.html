Nicandra

1. NOUN

/ nɪˈkændrə / (AmE), / nɪˈkændrə / (BrE)
A genus of sturdy annual plants native to Peru, belonging to the nightshade family, with bell-shaped, purplish-blue flowers.
genus of annual plants native to Peru

Examples

Nicandra is often grown in gardens for its attractive blue flowers.
The fruits of Nicandra are contained in papery husks similar to those of a tomatillo.
Botanists first classified Nicandra in the 18th century after discovering it in Peru.

Etymology

Named after Nicander, a Greek poet and physician.
