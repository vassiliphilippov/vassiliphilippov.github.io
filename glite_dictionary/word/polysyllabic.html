polysyllabic

1. ADJ

/ ˌpɑːlɪsɪˈlæbɪk / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪsɪˈlæbɪk / (BrE)
(of a word) having more than one syllable; often used to describe words that are long or complex.
having many syllables

Examples

The teacher challenged the students to use more polysyllabic words in their essays.
She found the polysyllabic jargon of the medical report difficult to understand.
Children often struggle with reading polysyllabic words at first.

Synset Examples

sesquipedalian technical terms

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monosyllabic - having only one syllable

Etymology

From Greek 'poly-' (many) + 'syllabē' (syllable).

2. ADJ

/ ˌpɑːliˌsɪˈlæbɪk / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪsɪˈlæbɪk / (BrE)
(of a word) having more than three syllables; (of speech or writing) characterized by the use of long words.
having or using long words

Examples

The professor often used polysyllabic terms that confused his students.
Many scientific articles are filled with polysyllabic jargon.
Her writing style is dense and polysyllabic, making it difficult to read quickly.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monosyllabic - having only one syllable

Etymology

From Greek polysyllabos, from 'poly-' (many) + 'syllabē' (syllable).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02297137-s
Synset POS: s
