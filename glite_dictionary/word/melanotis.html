Melanotis

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛləˈnɑːtɪs / (AmE), / ˌmiːləˈnəʊtɪs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the thrush family Mimidae, found in Central America, known as blue mockingbirds.
genus of thrush family birds (Mimidae)

Examples

The Melanotis genus includes the striking blue mockingbird found in Mexico.
Researchers observed several Melanotis species during their fieldwork in Central America.
Unlike other mimids, Melanotis birds have vivid blue plumage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mimidae - family of birds including mockingbirds and thrashers

Etymology

From Greek 'melas' (black) and 'ōtis' (ear).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɛləˈnoʊtɪs / (AmE), / ˌmɛləˈnəʊtɪs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Mimidae, known as part of the mockingbird family.
genus of mockingbird-like birds

Examples

The blue mockingbird, a member of the genus Melanotis, is found in Mexico and Central America.
Ornithologists conducted a study on the vocalizations of Melanotis species.
There are only two species classified within the genus Melanotis.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: mimidae - family of birds including the mockingbirds and thrashers

Etymology

From Greek melas (black) + otis (ear), referring to the bird's coloration.
