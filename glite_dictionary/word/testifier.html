testifier

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛstəˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈtɛstɪfaɪə / (BrE)
a person who testifies or gives evidence, especially formally in a court or legal setting
person giving testimony, usually in court

Examples

The testifier recounted the events as she remembered them under oath.
Each testifier was asked to provide clear and honest answers to the attorney’s questions.
The credibility of the testifier was questioned by the defense lawyer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: witness - a person who gives evidence in court or in a legal context

Etymology

From testify + -er, patterned after agent noun formation.
