arishth

1. NOUN

/ əˈrɪʃt / (AmE), / əˈrɪʃt / (BrE)
A large semi-evergreen tree native to the East Indies, with a trunk that exudes gum, bitter bark used as a tonic, and seeds yielding aromatic oil; sometimes classified in the genus Melia.
large semi-evergreen tree of the East Indies

Examples

The arishth tree is valued for its medicinal bark and aromatic oil.
Local artisans collect gum from the arishth for traditional uses.
Farmers plant arishth along field boundaries to provide shade and resources.
