voyeuse

1. NOUN

/ vwɑːˈjɜːz / (AmE), / vwɑːˈjɜːz / (BrE)
an 18th-century chair designed for spectators at game tables, featuring a padded top rail for leaning on while observing the game
18th-century spectator's chair for game tables

Examples

The museum displayed a rare voyeuse from the Louis XVI period, complete with its original upholstery.
At the auction, the ornate voyeuse attracted the attention of antique furniture collectors.
She leaned comfortably on the padded rail of the voyeuse as she watched the card game unfold.

Etymology

From French 'voyeuse', feminine of 'voyeur' (spectator, observer).
