fair-mindedness

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɛrˈmaɪndɪdnɪs / (AmE), / ˌfeəˈmaɪndɪdnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of making judgments that are free from bias, discrimination, or dishonesty
impartial and honest judgment

Examples

Her fair-mindedness earned her respect among both colleagues and competitors.
The judge was praised for his fair-mindedness in handling the complicated case.
We need more fair-mindedness in public debates to ensure everyone is heard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impartiality - the state of being impartial; absence of bias
  • Antonym: bias - inclination or prejudice toward or against something

Etymology

Formed from 'fair-minded' (impartial, just) and suffix '-ness' (state or quality).
