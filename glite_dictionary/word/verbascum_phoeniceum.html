Verbascum phoeniceum

1. NOUN

/ vərˈbæskəm fəˈnɪsiəm / (AmE), / vəˈbæskəm fɪˈniːsɪəm / (BrE)
A species of Eurasian mullein with showy purple or pink flowers, often grown as an ornamental plant.
an ornamental Eurasian mullein species

Examples

The striking blooms of Verbascum phoeniceum add color to the early summer garden.
Gardeners often choose Verbascum phoeniceum for its ability to attract pollinators.
She planted a row of Verbascum phoeniceum alongside the fence for a vibrant display.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mullein - any of various plants of the genus Verbascum

Etymology

From Latin: 'Verbascum' is the genus name for mullens; 'phoeniceum' means 'purple/red' in Latin, referring to the flower color.
