to stratify

1. VERB

/ ˈstræt.əˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈstræt.ɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to arrange or form into layers or strata; to organize into groups based on levels or categories
arrange or form into layers

Examples

Geologists often stratify rock samples to study the different layers.
The data was stratified by age and income before analysis.
Societies can stratify themselves based on class, occupation, or ethnicity.

Synset Examples

The fish are stratified in barrels
The rock was stratified by the force of the water
A statistician stratifies the list of names according to the addresses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: layer - to arrange in layers

Etymology

From Medieval Latin stratificare, from Latin stratum (layer) + facere (to make).

5. VERB

/ ˈstræt̬.əˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈstræt.ɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
(in horticulture) To store or treat (seeds) by placing them between layers of earth or sand, usually to preserve them or promote germination.
to layer seeds in earth or sand for preservation or germination

Examples

Gardeners often stratify seeds to improve their chances of germination in the spring.
Some rare plants require you to stratify the seeds for several weeks before planting.
To break seed dormancy, horticulturists may stratify kernels in moist sand over winter.
