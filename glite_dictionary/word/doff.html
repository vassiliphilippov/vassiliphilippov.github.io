to doff

1. VERB

/ dɑːf / (AmE), / dɒf / (BrE)
to remove (an article of clothing), especially a hat, from the body or head
remove an article of clothing
archaic

Examples

As he entered the chapel, he quietly doffed his cap in respect.
In old movies, gentlemen often doffed their hats to greet a lady.
She doffed her heavy coat as soon as she stepped into the warm house.

Synset Examples

He doffed his hat

Related Terms

  • Antonym: don - to put on (clothing)

Etymology

From Middle English doffen (to do off), a contraction of 'do' and 'off'.
