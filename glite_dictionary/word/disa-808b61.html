disa

1. NOUN

/ ˈdiːsə / (AmE), / ˈdiːsə / (BrE)
A genus of orchids, native to Africa, known for their striking hooded flowers and dark green leaves.
a genus of orchids with hooded flowers

Examples

Many collectors prize the disa for its vibrant colors and unique hooded petals.
The western Cape region is famous for its wild disas that bloom along riverbanks.
She grew a rare disa orchid in her greenhouse, carefully tending to its delicate flowers.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: orchid - a flowering plant of the family Orchidaceae

Etymology

Named after the Swedish botanist Disa, possibly from Swedish legend.
