inefficaciousness

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌɛfɪˈkeɪʃəsnəs / (AmE), / ɪnˌɛfɪˈkeɪʃəsnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of lacking the ability to produce the intended effect; ineffectiveness.
lack of effectiveness

Examples

The utter inefficaciousness of the remedy left the patient frustrated.
Critics point to the inefficaciousness of such policies in addressing the root causes of the issue.
Despite repeated efforts, the inefficaciousness of the solution became increasingly apparent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ineffectiveness - lack of effectiveness or success
  • Antonym: efficacy - the ability to produce a desired result

Etymology

From Latin in- (not) + efficax (effectual) + -ness (noun suffix).
