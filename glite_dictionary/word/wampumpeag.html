wampumpeag

1. NOUN

/ ˌwɑːmpəmˈpiːæɡ / (AmE), / ˌwɒmpəmˈpiːaɡ / (BrE)
Small cylindrical beads made from polished shells, traditionally used by certain Native American peoples as jewelry or currency and often fashioned into strings or belts.
beads from shells used by Native Americans as jewelry or currency

Examples

The museum displayed a belt made of wampumpeag that was exchanged during a historic treaty.
He admired the intricate patterns created with wampumpeag by indigenous craftsmen.
In colonial times, wampumpeag was sometimes used as a form of currency in trade.

Etymology

From Narragansett or Massachusett "wampumpeag," meaning 'white strings (of shell beads)'.
