necktie

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛkˌtaɪ / (AmE), / ˈnɛk.taɪ / (BrE)
A long, narrow piece of fabric worn around the neck, under the collar of a shirt and tied in a knot at the front, typically as part of formal or business attire.
long cloth band tied around the neck

Examples

He adjusted his necktie before leaving for the interview.
A brightly colored necktie can add personality to a suit.
He received a silk necktie as a birthday present from his son.

Synset Examples

he stood in front of the mirror tightening his necktie
he wore a vest and tie

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tie - a piece of cloth worn around the neck and tied in a knot (short for necktie)

Etymology

From 'neck' + 'tie', referring to a tie worn around the neck.
