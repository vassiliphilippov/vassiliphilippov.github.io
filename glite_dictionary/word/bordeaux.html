Bordeaux

1. NOUN

/ bɔrˈdoʊ / (AmE), / bɔːˈdəʊ / (BrE)
A red or white wine produced in or around the Bordeaux region of France, or a wine of similar style.
type of French wine

Examples

He ordered a bottle of Bordeaux to go with the steak.
A fine Bordeaux can age for decades and improve in flavor.
Some Californian wineries try to create wines in the style of Bordeaux.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: claret - a type of red Bordeaux wine, particularly in British usage
  • Antonym: Burgundy - a type of wine from the Burgundy region of France, often contrasted with Bordeaux

Etymology

Named after Bordeaux, a region in southwestern France noted for its wine production.

2. PROPN

/ bɔːrˈdoʊ / (AmE), / bɔːˈdoʊ / (BrE)
A port city in southwestern France, famous as a major center of the wine trade.
French city known for wine trade

Examples

We spent a week exploring the historic streets of Bordeaux and tasting local wines.
Bordeaux has long been recognized as the heart of France’s wine industry.
The river Garonne runs through Bordeaux, adding to the city’s picturesque charm.

Etymology

From French Bordeaux, the name of the city.
