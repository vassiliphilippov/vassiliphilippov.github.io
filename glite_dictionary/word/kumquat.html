kumquat

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌmˌkwɑt / (AmE), / ˈkʌmˌkwɒt / (BrE)
A small, oval citrus fruit with a thin, sweet rind and very sour pulp, eaten whole or used in preserves and cooking.
small oval citrus fruit

Examples

The kumquat has a sweet edible skin and a tart flesh, making it unique among citrus fruits.
She bought a basket of fresh kumquats from the market to use in her marmalade recipe.
You can eat a kumquat whole, without peeling, because the rind is so thin and sweet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: citrus - a genus of fruit including oranges, lemons, limes, and related fruits

Etymology

From Cantonese 金橘 (gam1 gwat1), meaning 'golden orange'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʌmˌkwɑt / (AmE), / ˈkʌm.kwɒt / (BrE)
a small, orange-colored citrus fruit with a sweet edible skin and sour pulp, or the tree or shrub that bears this fruit
small orange-colored citrus fruit or tree

Examples

She decorated the cake with slices of fresh kumquat to add a citrus flavor.
The kumquat tree in our garden produced fruit all winter long.
I love the contrast of the sweet skin and tart pulp in a kumquat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: citrus - a type of fruit such as an orange, lemon, or lime

Etymology

From Cantonese gam^1 gwat^1, meaning 'golden orange'.
