dinner

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪnər / (AmE), / ˈdɪnə / (BrE)
the main meal of the day, usually eaten in the evening or at midday
main meal of the day

Examples

We usually have dinner at 7 o'clock every evening.
She invited all her colleagues over for dinner on Friday night.
After a long day of hiking, they enjoyed a hearty dinner at the lodge.

Synset Examples

dinner will be at 8
on Sundays they had a large dinner when they returned from church

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: lunch - a meal eaten in the middle of the day
  • Degree Lower: supper - a light evening meal, often after dinner

Etymology

From Old French 'disner', meaning 'breakfast, lunch', from Latin 'disjejunare' (to break the fast).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɪnɚ / (AmE), / ˈdɪnə / (BrE)
a formal or social gathering where a group of people meet to have an evening meal together
formal or social meal gathering

Examples

We were invited to a formal dinner at the ambassador’s residence.
The annual charity dinner raised thousands for the hospital.
He met his future business partner at a company dinner last year.

Synset Examples

guests should never be late to a dinner party

Related Terms

  • Synonym: banquet - a large and elaborate formal meal for many people
  • Synonym: reception - a formal social occasion, often with a meal or refreshments

Etymology

From Old French *disner* (to dine), from Vulgar Latin *desjunare* (to break one's fast).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08270736-n
Synset POS: n
