Luvaridae

1. NOUN

/ luːˈvɛrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / luːˈvɑːrɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, oceanic fish known as louvars, characterized by their laterally compressed bodies and belonging to the order Perciformes.
family of oceanic fish (louvars)

Examples

The Luvaridae are often found in temperate and tropical seas worldwide.
Unlike most of its relatives, the Luvaridae family contains only a single living species.
Marine biologists studied the anatomy of Luvaridae to better understand their evolutionary history.

Etymology

From modern Latin Luvaridae, based on the type genus Luvarus.
