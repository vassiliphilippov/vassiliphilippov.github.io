diurnal

1. ADJ

/ daɪˈɝːnəl / (AmE), / daɪˈɜːnəl / (BrE)
active or occurring during the daytime rather than at night
active during the day

Examples

Many flowers are diurnal and only open when the sun is out.
Owls are nocturnal, but most birds are diurnal and active in daylight.
The scientist studied the diurnal patterns of the animal’s behavior.

Synset Examples

diurnal animals are active during the day
diurnal flowers are open during the day and closed at night
diurnal and nocturnal offices

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nocturnal - active at night rather than during the day

Etymology

From Latin 'diurnalis' (daily), from 'dies' (day).

2. ADJ

/ daɪˈɝːnəl / (AmE), / daɪˈɜːnəl / (BrE)
having a daily cycle or occurring every day; recurring or operating during each 24-hour period
recurring every day or every 24 hours

Examples

The diurnal rotation of the Earth causes day and night.
Many animals have a diurnal activity pattern, being most active during daylight hours.
The hospital staff performs diurnal checks on patients to monitor their progress.

Synset Examples

diurnal rotation of the heavens

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nocturnal - active at night rather than during the day

Etymology

From Latin 'diurnalis', from 'dies' meaning 'day'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01975098-s
Synset POS: s
