Westminster

1. PROPN

/ ˈwɛstˌmɪnstər / (AmE), / ˈwɛsmɪnstə / (BrE)
a borough of central London on the River Thames, home to major government buildings including the Houses of Parliament, Buckingham Palace, and Westminster Abbey
central London borough with major government sites

Examples

The Prime Minister addressed the press outside Westminster after the parliamentary debate.
Many tourists visit Westminster to see the historic Abbey and the Houses of Parliament.
Major decisions about the UK's future are often made in Westminster.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: London - the capital city of the United Kingdom

Etymology

Named after Westminster Abbey, from Old English 'westmynster' meaning 'western monastery'.
