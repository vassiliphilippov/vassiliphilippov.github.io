automatically

1. ADV

/ ˌɔːtəˈmætɪkli / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəˈmætɪkli / (BrE)
in a mechanical manner or by means of a mechanism, without human intervention or control
by a mechanism; without human intervention

Examples

The lights turn off automatically when no one is in the room.
Her email address was automatically added to the mailing list.
This door locks automatically after it is closed.

Synset Examples

this door opens mechanically

Related Terms

  • Antonym: manually - by hand, done by a person rather than a machine

Etymology

From automatic + -ally; 'automatic' from Greek 'automatos' (self-acting).

WordNet ID: oewn-00114936-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ ˌɔːtəˈmætɪkli / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəˈmætɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that is done without conscious thought; as a reflex or by instinct.
without conscious thought; by reflex

Examples

She reached for her phone automatically as soon as she woke up.
When the doorbell rang, he automatically stood up to answer it.
He smiled automatically at the familiar joke, not really hearing it.

Synset Examples

he answered automatically

Related Terms

  • Synonym: instinctively - without conscious thought; by instinct
  • Antonym: consciously - with full awareness or intention

Etymology

From automatic + -ally, first attested in the 19th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00005948-r
Synset POS: r
