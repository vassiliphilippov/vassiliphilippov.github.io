percolation

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɝː.kəˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɜː.kəˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of making coffee by passing hot water through ground coffee in a percolator
making coffee in a percolator

Examples

Some people prefer percolation for a stronger cup of coffee.
I could smell the fresh aroma of percolation coming from the kitchen.
His morning ritual always includes the careful percolation of coffee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brewing - the process of making beverages such as coffee or tea

Etymology

From Latin 'percolare' meaning 'to strain through'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɜr.kəˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɜː.kəˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of a liquid passing slowly through a filter, especially to extract or purify a substance
filtration process for extraction or purification

Examples

Proper percolation of water through the coffee grounds produces a richer flavor.
The percolation process can help remove impurities from drinking water.
They tested the soil's percolation rate before installing the septic system.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filtration - the process of removing solid particles from a liquid or gas by passing it through a filter

Etymology

From Latin 'percolare' (to strain through), from 'per-' (through) + 'colare' (to strain).

3. NOUN

/ ˌpɝː.kəˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɜː.kəˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the movement and gradual passage of a liquid through a porous substance or filtering medium
liquid passing slowly through a filter

Examples

Scientists studied the percolation of groundwater through different layers of rock.
Efficient percolation is important for removing impurities from drinking water.
After heavy rain, the percolation of water slowed due to compacted soil.

Synset Examples

the percolation of rainwater through the soil
the infiltration of seawater through the lava

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filtration - the process of filtering something
  • Synonym: infiltration - the process of entering or permeating a substance, especially gradually

Etymology

From Latin 'percolare', meaning 'to strain through.'
