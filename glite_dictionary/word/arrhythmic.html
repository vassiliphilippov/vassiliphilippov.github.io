arrhythmic

1. ADJ

/ əˈrɪðmɪk / (AmE), / əˈrɪðmɪk / (BrE)
lacking rhythm or a regular pattern, especially in music or the beating of the heart.
lacking rhythm or regularity

Examples

Her footsteps were arrhythmic, echoing irregularly down the hallway.
The patient's pulse was arrhythmic, prompting further cardiac tests.
An arrhythmic melody challenged the dancers to maintain their coordination.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rhythmic - having rhythm or a regular repeating pattern

Etymology

From Greek prefix a- (not) + rhythmos (measured flow or movement).

2. ADJ

/ əˈrɪð.mɪk / (AmE), / əˈrɪð.mɪk / (BrE)
Lacking a regular or steady rhythm, especially in reference to a pulse or heartbeat.
not having a regular rhythm
medical

Examples

The doctor identified an arrhythmic pattern in the patient's heartbeat.
His breathing became arrhythmic after the strenuous activity.
She listened closely for any arrhythmic sounds with her stethoscope.

Synset Examples

an arrhythmic heartbeat

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rhythmic - having a regular rhythm

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (without) + 'rhythmos' (measured flow, rhythm).
