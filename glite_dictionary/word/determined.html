determined

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈtɝːmɪnd / (AmE), / dɪˈtɜːmɪnd / (BrE)
showing a firm decision or strong resolve to do something, even in the face of difficulties or opposition
having a strong resolve

Examples

Her determined attitude helped her overcome every obstacle in her career.
Despite criticism, they remained determined to finish the project on time.
The team's determined efforts finally led them to victory.

Synset Examples

a struggle against a determined enemy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resolute - admirably purposeful, unwavering
  • Antonym: undecided - not having made a decision

Etymology

From the verb 'determine', from Latin determinare (to set bounds, limit, decide).

3. ADJ

/ dɪˈtɚˌmɪnd / (AmE), / dɪˈtɜː.mɪnd / (BrE)
having made a firm decision and being resolved not to change it; strongly motivated to succeed
strongly motivated; resolute

Examples

She remained determined to finish the race despite the pain in her leg.
His determined attitude inspired the whole team to work harder.
They faced many obstacles, but he was determined to achieve his dreams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resolute - admirably purposeful and determined
  • Degree Higher: stubborn - having or showing dogged determination not to change one's attitude or position
  • Degree Lower: hesitant - unsure or slow in acting or speaking

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'determine' (late Middle English), from Old French 'determiner', from Latin 'determinare' (to limit, fix, decide).
