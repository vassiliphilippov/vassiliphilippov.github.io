Tb

1. NOUN

/ ti bi / (AmE), / tiː biː / (BrE)
the chemical symbol for terbium, a metallic element of the rare earth group used in lasers and found in minerals such as apatite, monazite, xenotime, and ytterbite
chemical symbol for terbium

Examples

Scientists use Tb to refer to terbium when discussing its properties in chemistry texts.
The mineral xenotime contains traces of Tb, making it an important ore for rare earth elements.
When labeling the periodic table, don’t forget to include Tb among the lanthanides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terbium - a metallic element of the rare earth group

Etymology

From the name 'terbium', which is named after Ytterby, a village in Sweden where its ore was first discovered.

2. NOUN

/ tiː biː / (AmE), / tiː biː / (BrE)
a unit of information equal to 1,000,000,000,000 (one trillion) bits, used especially in computing and telecommunications; abbreviation for terabit
unit of digital information (terabit)

Examples

The new internet connection offers speeds up to 1 Tb per second.
Storing the data required several high-capacity drives capable of handling multiple Tb.
Some advanced networks can transmit more than a Tb of data each day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terabit - a unit of digital information equal to one trillion bits
  • Antonym: TB - a unit of digital information equal to one trillion bytes (terabyte); not to be confused with Tb, which is terabit

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'terabit', from the prefix 'tera-' (meaning one trillion) and 'bit'.
