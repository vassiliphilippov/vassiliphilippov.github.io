Anthemis cotula

1. NOUN

/ ænˈθɛmɪs koʊˈtuːlə / (AmE), / ænˈθɛmɪs kɒˈtuːlə / (BrE)
A plant species, commonly known as mayweed or stinking chamomile, with white-rayed flowers and a strong, unpleasant smell, often found as a weed in fields and roadsides.
weed with white flowers and strong odor

Examples

Anthemis cotula commonly invades agricultural fields, outcompeting crops with its rapid growth.
The strong smell of Anthemis cotula makes it easily recognizable along country roadsides.
Gardeners often remove Anthemis cotula because it spreads quickly and is considered a noxious weed.

Etymology

From Greek 'anthemon' (flower) and Latin 'cotula' (small cup), referring to the plant's flower shape.
