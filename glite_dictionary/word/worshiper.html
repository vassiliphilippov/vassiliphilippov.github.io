worshiper

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɜrʃɪpər / (AmE), / ˈwɜːʃɪpə / (BrE)
a person who shows reverence and adoration for a deity or religious figure, especially in a formal religious setting
person who takes part in worship

Examples

Hundreds of worshipers gathered at the temple to celebrate the festival.
As a devoted worshiper, he attended church every Sunday morning.
In the quiet mosque, the worshipers knelt in silent prayer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: believer - a person who believes in religious faith or a particular doctrine

Etymology

From Middle English 'worschiper', from 'worship' + '-er'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɜːrʃɪpər / (AmE), / ˈwɜːʃɪpə / (BrE)
a person who admires someone or something so much that they fail to recognize any faults
person who admires excessively

Examples

He is such a worshiper of his boss that he ignores all her mistakes.
The biography was written by a worshiper who overlooked critical aspects of the subject's life.
Don't be a blind worshiper; everyone has flaws.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: admirer - person who regards someone with respect or warm approval
  • Antonym: critic - person who expresses an unfavorable opinion

Etymology

From Middle English 'worshipen' (to honor or venerate), with '-er' denoting agent noun.
