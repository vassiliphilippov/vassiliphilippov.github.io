Novocain

1. NOUN

/ ˈnoʊvəˌkeɪn / (AmE), / ˈnəʊvəˌkeɪn / (BrE)
A trademarked brand of procaine hydrochloride, once widely used as a local anesthetic in dentistry and minor surgery.
trademarked local anesthetic, procaine hydrochloride
medical

Examples

The dentist injected Novocain to numb my gums before the procedure.
After receiving Novocain, she felt no pain during the minor surgery.
Many routine dental treatments involved the use of Novocain in the past.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: procaine - a synthetic compound used as a local anesthetic

Etymology

Trademark, coined from Latin 'novus' (new) and 'caine' (from cocaine, denoting an anesthetic).
