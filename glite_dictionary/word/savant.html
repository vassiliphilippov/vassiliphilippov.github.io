savant

1. NOUN

/ səˈvɑnt / (AmE), / səˈvɑːnt / (BrE)
a person of great learning or knowledge, especially one distinguished in a particular scholarly field
a learned or scholarly person

Examples

The young savant impressed the academic community with his remarkable insights.
Although she was a savant in mathematics, she remained humble and approachable.
Many great savants have contributed to our understanding of science and philosophy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scholar - a learned person, especially one who has profound knowledge of a particular subject

Etymology

From French 'savant', present participle of 'savoir' (to know), from Latin 'sapere' (to be wise).
