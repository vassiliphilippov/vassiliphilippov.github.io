Bufonidae

1. NOUN

/ bjuːˈfɑːnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / bjuːˈfɒnɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of amphibians commonly known as true toads, characterized by dry, warty skin and parotoid glands.
family of true toads

Examples

The family Bufonidae includes more than 500 species of toads found throughout the world.
Members of Bufonidae are often recognized by their warty skin and stout bodies.
She studied the reproduction patterns of Bufonidae in her zoology research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toad - a tailless amphibian with dry, warty skin, typically of the family Bufonidae

Etymology

From Latin 'bufo' (toad) + scientific family suffix '-idae'.

2. NOUN

/ bjuːˈfɑːnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / bjuːˈfɒnɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A large family of toads, known as the true toads, comprising numerous species found across the world.
family of true toads

Examples

The Bufonidae family includes hundreds of species commonly known as true toads.
Scientists study the Bufonidae to learn about amphibian adaptation to varied environments.
Many frogs are mistaken for members of Bufonidae, but only true toads belong to this family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toad - Any tailless amphibian of the family Bufonidae

Etymology

From Latin 'bufon-' (toad) + '-idae' (family suffix in zoological taxonomy).
