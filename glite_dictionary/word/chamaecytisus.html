Chamaecytisus

1. NOUN

/ ˌkeɪmiːsaɪˈtaɪsəs / (AmE), / ˌkamɪsɪˈtɪsəs / (BrE)
A genus of small, late-flowering trees or subshrubs with yellow to red flowers and leathery or woody pods, sometimes formerly included in the genus Cytisus.
genus of flowering trees or subshrubs

Examples

Many gardeners appreciate the colorful blooms of Chamaecytisus in the late spring.
The Chamaecytisus genus includes several species with woody seed pods.
Some species that were once classified under Cytisus are now considered part of Chamaecytisus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cytisus - a related genus of flowering plants commonly known as brooms

Etymology

From Greek 'chamai' (on the ground) + 'kytisos' (a kind of shrub).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkeɪmiːˌsaɪˈtaɪsəs / (AmE), / ˌkamiːˌsɪˈtaɪsəs / (BrE)
A genus of small, late-flowering trees or subshrubs with yellow to red flowers and leathery or woody pods, formerly often included in the genus Cytisus.
a genus of flowering trees or subshrubs

Examples

The Chamaecytisus genus includes species commonly known for their bright yellow flowers.
Several varieties of Chamaecytisus thrive in Mediterranean climates.
Botanists have recently reclassified some species from Cytisus to Chamaecytisus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cytisus - a related genus of flowering plants; brooms

Etymology

From Greek 'chamai' (on the ground) + 'Cytisus' (a genus of legumes).
