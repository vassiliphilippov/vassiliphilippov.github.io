Danaidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌdæn.iˈaɪ.diː / (AmE), / ˌdan.ɪˈeɪ.dɪ.iː / (BrE)
a former family of usually tropical butterflies, including the monarch butterfly, characterized by their large size and slow, graceful flight
former butterfly family including monarchs

Examples

The Danaidae are known for their striking wing patterns and slow flight.
Many species previously in the Danaidae family are now classified as part of Nymphalidae.
Scientists once classified the monarch butterfly within Danaidae before taxonomic revisions.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: nymphalidae - the family of brush-footed butterflies, which now includes Danaidae as a subfamily
  • Synonym: monarch butterfly - a large, mainly orange and black butterfly, formerly classified under Danaidae

Etymology

From Latinized form of Greek Danais, a type of butterfly, from Danaos, a mythical ancestor of the Greeks.
