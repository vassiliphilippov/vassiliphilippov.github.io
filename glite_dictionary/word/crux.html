Crux

1. NOUN

/ krʌks / (AmE), / krʌks / (BrE)
a small, prominent constellation in the southern sky, also known as the Southern Cross, located in the Milky Way near Centaurus
a southern constellation, the Southern Cross

Examples

The Crux constellation is easily recognizable in the southern hemisphere's night sky.
Sailors have long used Crux for navigation when traveling far from land.
From their campsite in Australia, they admired the clear view of Crux above the horizon.

Etymology

From Latin 'crux' meaning 'cross'; named for its cross shape in the sky.
