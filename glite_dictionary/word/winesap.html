Winesap

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪnˌsæp / (AmE), / ˈwaɪnˌsæp / (BrE)
A variety of apple with crisp flesh and dark red skin, often used for eating fresh or making cider.
variety of crisp, dark red apple

Examples

This pie is made with fresh Winesap apples from the orchard.
I prefer snacking on a Winesap because of its crisp texture and tangy flavor.
Winesap apples are also popular for making homemade cider.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apple - the round fruit of a tree of the rose family, with crisp flesh and red, green, or yellow skin
