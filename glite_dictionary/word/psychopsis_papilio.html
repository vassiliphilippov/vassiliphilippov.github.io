Psychopsis papilio

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈkɑpsɪs pəˈpɪlioʊ / (AmE), / saɪˈkɒpsɪs pəˈpɪliəʊ / (BrE)
A species of orchid native to South America and Trinidad, known for its large yellow and reddish-brown flowers; sometimes classified in the genus Oncidium.
orchid species with striking yellow and reddish-brown flowers

Examples

The Psychopsis papilio is prized by collectors for its butterfly-shaped flowers.
In the greenhouse, I noticed a beautiful Psychopsis papilio among the other tropical orchids.
Some botanists prefer to classify Psychopsis papilio under the genus Oncidium due to its floral characteristics.

Etymology

From Greek 'psychē' meaning 'butterfly' and Latin 'papilio' also meaning 'butterfly,' referring to the flower's butterfly-like appearance.
