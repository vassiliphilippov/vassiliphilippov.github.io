Manhattan

1. PROPN

/ mænˈhætən / (AmE), / mænˈhætən / (BrE)
One of the five boroughs of New York City, situated primarily on Manhattan Island and known for its cultural, financial, and historic significance.
borough of New York City

Examples

She lives in Manhattan and commutes to Brooklyn for work.
The skyscrapers of Manhattan are famous around the world.
Tourists flock to Manhattan to see Times Square and Central Park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: New York City - the largest city in the United States, comprising five boroughs including Manhattan

Etymology

From the name of the Manhattan people, an Algonquian-speaking Native American tribe.

2. NOUN

/ mænˈhætən / (AmE), / mænˈhætən / (BrE)
a cocktail made of whiskey, sweet vermouth, and bitters, usually garnished with a cherry or twist of citrus peel
whiskey and vermouth cocktail

Examples

He ordered a Manhattan at the bar before dinner.
Would you prefer your Manhattan with rye or bourbon?
The classic Manhattan is usually served in a cocktail glass.

Etymology

Named after Manhattan, a borough of New York City, where the drink is said to have originated.
