uninformed

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnɪnˈfɔrmd / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪnˈfɔːmd / (BrE)
not having knowledge or information about a particular subject or situation
lacking knowledge or information

Examples

Many voters remained uninformed about the details of the new policy.
Her comment revealed that she was completely uninformed on the subject.
An uninformed decision can lead to serious mistakes.

Synset Examples

the uninformed public

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ignorant - lacking knowledge or awareness
  • Antonym: informed - having knowledge about something

Etymology

From the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'informed' (from Latin informare 'shape, instruct').
