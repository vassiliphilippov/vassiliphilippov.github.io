dimethylglyoxime

1. NOUN

/ daɪˌmɛθəlɡlaɪˈɒksaɪm / (AmE), / daɪˌmiːθəlɡlʌɪˈɒksɪm / (BrE)
a chemical compound used in analytical chemistry as a precipitating agent for detecting nickel and palladium ions
chemical precipitant for nickel or palladium
medical

Examples

The laboratory technician used dimethylglyoxime to test for the presence of nickel ions in the solution.
Adding dimethylglyoxime causes a red precipitate if palladium is present in the sample.
In quantitative analysis, chemists often rely on dimethylglyoxime as a selective reagent for nickel determination.

Etymology

From 'di-' meaning two, 'methyl' (from Greek 'methy' wine + 'hyle' wood), and 'glyoxime', from 'glyoxal' and 'oxime'.
