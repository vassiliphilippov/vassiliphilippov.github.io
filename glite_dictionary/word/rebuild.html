to rebuild

1. VERB

/ riˈbɪld / (AmE), / riːˈbɪld / (BrE)
to build something again after it has been damaged or destroyed
build again after damage

Examples

After the earthquake, the community came together to rebuild homes and businesses.
They decided to rebuild the bridge using stronger materials than before.
The cathedral was painstakingly rebuilt over several years after the fire.

Synset Examples

The house was rebuilt after it was hit by a bomb

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to restore - to return something to a former condition
  • Antonym: to demolish - to pull or knock down a building

Etymology

From re- (again) + build; re- from Latin, build from Old English 'byldan'.
