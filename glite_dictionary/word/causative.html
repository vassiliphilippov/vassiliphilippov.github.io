causative

1. ADJ

/ ˈkɔzətɪv / (AmE), / ˈkɔːzətɪv / (BrE)
acting as the cause of something; producing an effect or result.
producing an effect or result

Examples

Researchers looked for a causative link between the chemical exposure and the illness.
Unemployment is often seen as a causative factor in increased crime rates.
The study identified stress as a causative agent in the development of heart disease.

Synset Examples

poverty as a causative factor in crime

Related Terms

  • Synonym: causal - relating to or acting as a cause

Etymology

From Latin causativus (pertaining to a cause), from causa (cause).
