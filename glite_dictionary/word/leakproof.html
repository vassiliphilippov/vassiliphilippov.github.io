leakproof

1. ADJ

/ ˈlikˌpruf / (AmE), / ˈliːkˌpruːf / (BrE)
Not allowing any liquid or gas to escape; designed so that leaks cannot occur.
not allowing leaks

Examples

Make sure the container is leakproof before filling it with soup.
The new water bottle is completely leakproof, even when turned upside down.
She bought a leakproof lunch box for her son's school meals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: waterproof - impervious to water

Etymology

Formed from 'leak' + 'proof'. Modeled after similar compound adjectives like 'waterproof'.
