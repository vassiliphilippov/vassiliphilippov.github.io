cassava

1. NOUN

/ kəˈsɑːvə / (AmE), / kəˈsɑːvə / (BrE)
A tropical plant of the genus Manihot, whose large, fleshy roots yield a nutritious starch used for food.
tropical plant with starchy roots

Examples

Farmers in many tropical countries rely on cassava as a staple food source.
The starchy root of cassava can be processed to make tapioca.
Dried cassava chips are often used in animal feed and biofuel production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manioc - alternative name for cassava, a starchy root plant

Etymology

From Spanish 'casabe' (bread made from cassava), from Taino.

2. NOUN

/ kəˈsɑːvə / (AmE), / kəˈsɑːvə / (BrE)
A starchy root of a tropical plant, eaten as a staple food in many countries, and used as the source of tapioca.
starchy tropical root; staple food

Examples

Cassava is a staple crop in many African and South American countries.
Farmers harvest cassava roots and process them into flour or tapioca.
It is important to properly prepare cassava before eating, as the raw root can be toxic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tapioca - A starch extracted from cassava root, used in cooking and baking

Etymology

From Spanish 'casabe', ultimately from Taíno 'kasábi' (bread made from cassava).

4. NOUN

/ kəˈsɑvə / (AmE), / kəˈsɑːvə / (BrE)
a tropical plant of the genus Manihot with thick, starchy roots, cultivated for food and as a source of tapioca
tropical plant with starchy edible root

Examples

Cassava is a staple food for millions of people in Africa and South America.
The farmer dug up several large cassava roots to sell at the market.
Tapioca pearls used in desserts are made from cassava starch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manioc - another name for cassava
  • Synonym: tapioca - starch extracted from the cassava root

Etymology

From Portuguese 'cassava', from Tupi 'ka'sawa'.
