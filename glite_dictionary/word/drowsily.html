drowsily

1. ADV

/ ˈdraʊzɪli / (AmE), / ˈdraʊzɪli / (BrE)
In a manner that shows a feeling of being sleepy or tired.
in a sleepy or tired manner

Examples

He answered the phone drowsily, barely awake after a late night.
The cat blinked drowsily in the sun, uninterested in moving.
She smiled drowsily as she lay back on the pillow.

Synset Examples

‘Time to get up,’ she said drowsily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sleepily - in a way that shows you are tired or wanting to sleep

Etymology

From 'drowsy' + '-ly', where 'drowsy' comes from Middle English 'drousi'.
