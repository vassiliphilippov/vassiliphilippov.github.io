Eumeces callicephalus

1. NOUN

/ juːˈmiːsiːz ˌkælɪˈsɛfələs / (AmE), / juːˈmiːsiːz ˌkælɪˈsɛfələs / (BrE)
A species of skink found in rocky oak and pine mountain habitats in the southwestern United States and Mexico.
a species of mountain-dwelling skink

Examples

The Eumeces callicephalus is well adapted to life in rocky mountain environments.
Researchers observed several Eumeces callicephalus basking on sunlit stones in New Mexico.
Habitat loss poses a threat to the survival of Eumeces callicephalus in some regions.

Etymology

From Greek; 'Eumeces' is a genus name, 'callicephalus' means 'beautiful-headed'.
