combativeness

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈbæt̬.ɪv.nəs / (AmE), / kəmˈbæt.ɪv.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being eager or ready to fight or argue
readiness to fight or argue

Examples

Her combativeness often got her into heated debates with her colleagues.
The player's combativeness on the field inspired his teammates to push harder.
Some admired his combativeness, while others found it exhausting and unnecessary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aggressiveness - the quality of being aggressive
  • Antonym: peacefulness - the quality of being peaceful; absence of aggression

Etymology

From 'combative' + '-ness'; 'combative' from French 'combattre', from Latin 'combattere'.
