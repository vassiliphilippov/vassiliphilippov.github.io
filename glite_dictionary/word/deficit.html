deficit

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛfɪsɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛfɪsɪt / (BrE)
The amount by which something, especially money, is too small or falls short, particularly when liabilities exceed assets over a period.
the shortfall when liabilities exceed assets

Examples

The company reported a deficit of $10 million for the last fiscal year.
After several years of running a deficit, the organization finally broke even.
A growing budget deficit can lead to increased national debt.

Synset Examples

last year there was a serious budgetary deficit

Related Terms

  • Antonym: surplus - the amount by which assets or income exceed liabilities

Etymology

From French déficit, from Latin deficit (it is lacking).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɛfəsɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛfɪsɪt / (BrE)
an amount by which something is less than it should be, especially a shortfall in money or resources
amount less than expected or required

Examples

The organization struggled to overcome its large deficit after a year of reduced donations.
A sleep deficit can impact your ability to concentrate and remember information.
The country is addressing its budget deficit by reducing spending and raising taxes.

Synset Examples

new blood vessels bud out from the already dilated vascular bed to make up the nutritional deficit

Related Terms

  • Antonym: surplus - an amount that is more than what is needed or expected

Etymology

From French déficit, from Latin deficit ('it is lacking'), from deficere ('to fail, be lacking').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05121671-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈdɛfəsɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛfɪsɪt / (BrE)
a deficiency or impairment in mental or neurological functioning, resulting in reduced ability or performance in a specific area
deficiency in mental or brain functioning
medical

Examples

The patient exhibited a significant deficit in short-term memory following the injury.
Language deficits are common among children with certain learning disabilities.
Doctors are working to identify the cause of the cognitive deficit observed in some elderly patients.

Synset Examples

the people concerned have a deficit in verbal memory
they have serious linguistic deficits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impairment - the state of being diminished, weakened, or damaged, especially mentally or physically
  • Synonym: deficiency - a lack or shortage, especially in ability or function

Etymology

From French 'déficit', from Latin 'deficit' (he/she/it fails, is lacking).

4. NOUN

/ ˈdɛfəsɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛfɪsɪt / (BrE)
(sports) the amount by which a team or individual is trailing in the score during a game or competition.
the margin by which one is behind in a game

Examples

The team overcame a ten-point deficit in the second half to win the game.
Despite the early deficit, she remained confident and fought back to tie the match.
They couldn't recover from the goal deficit before the final whistle blew.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lead - the score by which a team is ahead in a game

Etymology

From French déficit, from Latin deficit (‘it is lacking’).

Synset POS: n
