Chlorophyceae

1. NOUN

/ klɔːroʊˈfaɪsi.iː / (AmE), / klɔːrəˈfʌɪsɪ.iː / (BrE)
a class of green algae characterized by flagella and a vivid green color due to unmixed chlorophyll pigments
class of green algae

Examples

The Chlorophyceae are commonly found in freshwater environments across the world.
Many scientists study Chlorophyceae to better understand photosynthesis in simple organisms.
The vivid green color of Chlorophyceae is due to their abundant chlorophyll content.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: green algae - photosynthetic algae with a green color due to chlorophyll

Etymology

From Greek 'chloros' (green) + 'phykos' (seaweed, algae), and '-eae' (indicating a class).

2. NOUN

/ ˌklɔːrəˈfaɪsi.iː / (AmE), / ˌklɔːrəˈfɪsiː.iː / (BrE)
a class of green algae characterized by the presence of flagella and a bright green color due to unmasked chlorophyll
a class of green algae

Examples

The Chlorophyceae are commonly found in freshwater environments around the world.
Scientists study the Chlorophyceae to better understand photosynthetic processes in algae.
Members of the Chlorophyceae class exhibit a distinctive bright green color.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: green algae - algae with chlorophyll and typically green color

Etymology

From Greek khlōros (green) + phykos (seaweed), with taxonomic suffix -eae.
