FORTRAN

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔr.træn / (AmE), / ˈfɔː.træn / (BrE)
a high-level programming language designed for mathematical and scientific computing, particularly known for its use in engineering and scientific applications.
high-level programming language for science

Examples

Many legacy scientific applications are still written in FORTRAN code.
She learned FORTRAN during her undergraduate engineering studies.
FORTRAN was widely used for numerical analysis in the twentieth century.

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'Formula Translation', developed in the 1950s by IBM.
