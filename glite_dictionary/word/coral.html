coral

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrəl / (AmE), / ˈkɒrəl / (BrE)
the unfertilized eggs (roe) of a lobster, which turn reddish when cooked and are used as a garnish or to color sauces
unfertilized lobster roe used in cooking

Examples

The chef sprinkled coral over the dish to add both color and flavor.
Fresh coral from the lobster can be incorporated into rich butter sauces.
She reserved the coral to use as a garnish for the seafood platter.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkɔrəl / (AmE), / ˈkɒrəl / (BrE)
Of a strong pink to yellowish-pink color, similar to that of red coral.
pink or yellowish-pink color

Examples

She wore a beautiful dress in coral for the summer party.
The artist chose a coral background to make the painting feel warm and lively.
Her lips were painted a bright coral shade that matched her earrings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salmon - a pinkish-orange color, similar to the flesh of a salmon
  • Synonym: peach - a soft pinkish-yellow color, like the flesh of a peach
  • Degree Lower: pink - a pale red color
  • Degree Higher: vermilion - a brilliant red or scarlet color

Etymology

From the color of red coral, a marine organism.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɔr.əl / (AmE), / ˈkɒr.əl / (BrE)
A marine colonial animal that produces a hard, calcareous skeleton, often forming reefs and found in various shapes in the ocean.
marine animal forming reefs

Examples

Colorful fish swim among the branches of coral in the shallow waters.
The Great Barrier Reef is made up of billions of tiny coral polyps.
Divers are warned not to touch the coral to protect it from damage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reef - a ridge of jagged rock, coral, or sand just above or below the surface of the sea

Etymology

From Latin 'corallium', from Ancient Greek 'korállion' (coral).

5. NOUN

/ ˈkɔr.əl / (AmE), / ˈkɒr.əl / (BrE)
the hard, stony skeleton of certain Mediterranean corals, typically red or pink, used especially in jewelry and ornaments
red or pink stony material used in jewelry

Examples

She wore a beautiful necklace made of coral.
The shop sells earrings carved from Mediterranean coral.
The museum displayed an ancient ring set with rare red coral.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coralline - related to or resembling coral, especially in color
  • Synonym: pearl - a hard, lustrous spherical mass formed within the shell of a pearl oyster; used in jewelry
  • Synonym: shell - the hard, protective outer layer created by an animal that lives in the sea

Etymology

From Latin coralium, from Ancient Greek korállion (κοράλλιον), meaning coral.
