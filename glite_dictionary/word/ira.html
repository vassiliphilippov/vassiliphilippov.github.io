IRA

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪˌɑrˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˌaɪˌɑːˈreɪ / (BrE)
a militant organization of Irish nationalists, active mainly in the 20th century, that used terrorism and guerrilla tactics to seek the removal of British authority from Northern Ireland and achieve Irish unification.
Irish nationalist militant organization

Examples

The British government negotiated a peace agreement with the IRA after decades of violence.
Several films have depicted the actions and motivations of the IRA during the Troubles.
Membership in the IRA was illegal and carried severe penalties under British law.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Irish Republican Army - the full name of the militant nationalist group known as the IRA

Etymology

Abbreviation of Irish Republican Army.

2. NOUN

/ ˌaɪˌɑːˈreɪ / (AmE), / ˌaɪˌɑːˈreɪ / (BrE)
an account that allows individuals to save for retirement with tax advantages, subject to yearly contribution limits
individual retirement savings account (US)
american

Examples

Many people open an IRA to save for their retirement.
You can contribute up to a certain amount each year to your IRA.
The benefits of a traditional IRA include deferred taxes on the growth of your investments.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Roth IRA - a type of IRA with different tax rules

Etymology

Acronym of Individual Retirement Account, coined in US legislation in 1974.
