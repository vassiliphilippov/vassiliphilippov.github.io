Eucinostomus

1. NOUN

/ juːsɪˈnɑːstəməs / (AmE), / juːsɪˈnɒstəməs / (BrE)
A genus of fish in the family Gerreidae, commonly known as mojarras, found in coastal waters.
a genus of mojarra fish

Examples

Species of Eucinostomus are often found in shallow coastal waters throughout the Americas.
The biologist identified the fish as belonging to the genus Eucinostomus.
Juvenile Eucinostomus are important to the diets of many larger fish in tropical estuaries.

Etymology

From Greek 'eu-' (good, well) + 'kinein' (to move) + 'stoma' (mouth), referring to its well-developed mouth.

2. NOUN

/ juːsɪˈnɑːstəməs / (AmE), / juːsɪˈnɒstəməs / (BrE)
A genus of fishes in the family Gerreidae, commonly known as mojarras, found in marine and brackish waters.
a genus of mojarras (fish)

Examples

Several species in the genus Eucinostomus are common in shallow coastal waters.
Scientists studied the diet variation among Eucinostomus collected from the estuary.
Juvenile Eucinostomus are often mistaken for other mojarra species due to their similar appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gerreidae - family of fishes known as mojarras

Etymology

From Greek 'eu' (good) + 'kinostoma' (movable mouth).
