ezo-yama-hagi

1. NOUN

/ eɪˌzoʊ ˌjɑːməˈhɑːgi / (AmE), / eɪˌzəʊ ˌjæməˈhɑːgi / (BrE)
An Asian shrub with conspicuous racemose rose-purple flowers, widely used as an ornamental plant, for erosion control, and as feed for wild birds.
Asian shrub with rose-purple flowers

Examples

The ezo-yama-hagi is often planted on slopes to help control soil erosion.
Gardeners value ezo-yama-hagi for its vibrant rose-purple blossoms and resilience.
Wild birds are frequently seen feeding on the seeds of ezo-yama-hagi shrubs.

Etymology

From Japanese *ezo-yama-hagi*, meaning 'Ezo mountain bush clover'.
