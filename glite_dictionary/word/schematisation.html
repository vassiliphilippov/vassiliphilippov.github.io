schematisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌskiː.mə.taɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌskiː.mə.tʌɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of representing something in the form of a diagram, outline, or simplified structure
representation as a diagram or outline

Examples

The engineer's schematisation of the network helped clarify its complex connections.
In art history, the schematisation of human figures was common in early periods.
Effective schematisation can make abstract concepts more accessible to students.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schematization - the American spelling of schematisation

Etymology

From Greek 'schēmatizein' (to form into a shape) via French 'schématisation'.

2. NOUN

/ skiːmətaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / skiːmətaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of reducing something to a scheme or simplified formula; representation in schematic form
reducing to a scheme or formula

Examples

The schematisation of data allows complex ideas to be represented more simply.
Her research focused on the schematisation of historical events into broad categories.
Through schematisation, the architect conveyed the building’s essential structure using diagrams.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schematization - alternative spelling chiefly used in American English

Etymology

From Greek 'schema' (form, figure) via French 'schématisation'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00193306-n
Synset POS: n
