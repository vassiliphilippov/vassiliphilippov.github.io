Israeli

1. ADJ

/ ɪzˈreɪli / (AmE), / ɪzˈreɪli / (BrE)
of or relating to Israel or its people; characteristic of Israel or its citizens.
relating to Israel or its people

Examples

She enjoys Israeli cuisine, especially dishes like falafel and hummus.
The Israeli government announced new economic policies.
Many Israeli artists are gaining international recognition for their work.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Palestinian - of or relating to Palestine or its people

Etymology

From Israel + English suffix -i, denoting association.

2. NOUN

/ ɪzˈreɪli / (AmE), / ɪzˈreɪli / (BrE)
A person who is a native or inhabitant of Israel.
person from Israel

Examples

Many Israelis speak both Hebrew and English fluently.
The museum showcases art by contemporary Israelis.
She met several young Israelis during her trip to Tel Aviv.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Palestinian - a native or inhabitant of Palestine

Etymology

From Arabic إِسْرَائِيلِيّ (isrāʔīliyy), from Hebrew יִשְׂרְאֵלִי (yisréʾéli, 'of Israel').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09735229-n
Synset POS: n
