ecologic

1. ADJ

/ ˌiːkəˈlɑːdʒɪk / (AmE), / ˌiːkəˈlɒdʒɪk / (BrE)
relating to or concerned with the science of ecology and the relationships between living organisms and their environment
relating to ecology or the environment

Examples

The team is conducting ecologic studies on how pollution affects marine life.
Many modern policies emphasize ecologic sustainability to protect future generations.
Climate change has significant ecologic impacts on species distribution.

Synset Examples

ecological research

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ecological - relating to ecology or the environment

Etymology

From ecology + -ic, modeled on 'ecological'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌiːkəˈlɑdʒɪk / (AmE), / ˌiːkəˈlɒdʒɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characterized by the relationships and interdependence of living organisms within their environment
relating to relationships of organisms and environment

Examples

The oil spill caused a severe ecologic crisis in the coastal region.
The committee studied the ecologic effects of deforestation on local wildlife.
Adopting ecologic farming methods can protect biodiversity and soil health.

Synset Examples

an ecological disaster

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ecological - relating to ecology; concerning the environment and relationships between living things

Etymology

From Greek oikos (house) + -logic, patterned after ecology.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02918091-a
Synset POS: a
