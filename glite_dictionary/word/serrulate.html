serrulate

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛr.jəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɛr.jʊ.leɪt / (BrE)
Having very small, fine teeth along the edge, like a minutely serrated margin; typically used in describing leaves or similar biological structures.
minutely serrated (esp. in biology)

Examples

The scientist noted that the leaf had a serrulate edge under the microscope.
Some insect wings possess a serrulate margin, which aids in camouflage.
The serrulate pattern on the shell made it easy to distinguish from other species.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: serrated - having a notched or saw-like edge

Etymology

From Latin 'serrula' (a little saw), diminutive of 'serra' (saw).
