geographical

1. ADJ

/ ˌdʒiːəˈɡræfɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːəˈɡræfɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the natural features, population, industries, etc. of a region or regions.
relating to geography

Examples

The country's geographical location makes it prone to earthquakes.
She specializes in geographical information systems at the university.
The geographical boundaries of the city have changed over the years.

Synset Examples

the north and south geographic poles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geographic - alternative of 'geographical', relating to geography

Etymology

From geography + -ical, ultimately from Greek geōgraphia (earth description).

2. ADJ

/ ˌdʒi.əˈɡræf.ɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌdʒiː.əˈɡræf.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
relating to the science of geography or to the physical features, area, or location of a place
relating to geography or location

Examples

The country is divided into regions based on geographical features such as rivers and mountains.
Her research focuses on the geographical distribution of rare plant species.
The geographical boundaries of the city have changed over the years due to expansion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geographic - relating to the science of geography

Etymology

From Greek geōgraphikos, from geōgraphia (geography).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03033057-a
Synset POS: a
