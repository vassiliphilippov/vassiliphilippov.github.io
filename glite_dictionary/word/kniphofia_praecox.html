Kniphofia praecox

1. NOUN

/ nɪˈfoʊfiə preɪˈkɒks / (AmE), / nɪˈfɒfiə priːˈkɒks / (BrE)
A species of flowering plant in the family Asphodelaceae, commonly known as red hot poker or torch lily, cultivated for its striking, spike-like inflorescences.
species of ornamental flowering plant

Examples

The vibrant blooms of the Kniphofia praecox attract many pollinators to summer gardens.
Gardeners often choose Kniphofia praecox for its bold color display and easy care.
The tall spikes of Kniphofia praecox add a dramatic accent to flower borders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red hot poker - common name for Kniphofia species, known for their brightly colored flower spikes

Etymology

Genus name from Johann Hieronymus Kniphof, an 18th-century German botanist; praecox is Latin for 'early' or 'premature.'
