demonstrative

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈmɑnstrətɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒnstrətɪv / (BrE)
serving to demonstrate or clearly show something; showing feelings openly or used to indicate or point out.
serving to demonstrate or indicate

Examples

The teacher used a demonstrative gesture to show the correct answer on the board.
Her demonstrative tone made it clear that she supported the proposal.
In grammar, a demonstrative pronoun is used to point to specific things.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indicative - suggesting or showing something

Etymology

From Latin 'demonstrativus' (serving to point out or show).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈmɑnstrətɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒnstrətɪv / (BrE)
A pronoun that is used to indicate or point to a specific person, thing, or idea, such as 'this', 'that', 'these', or 'those'.
pronoun that points to specific referents

Examples

The words demonstrative like 'this' and 'those' help clarify which objects are being referred to.
'This' and 'that' are examples of English demonstratives.
When learning a new language, mastering the use of demonstratives is essential for clear communication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: this - a demonstrative pronoun indicating something near in space or time
  • Synonym: that - a demonstrative pronoun indicating something farther away in space or time

Etymology

From Latin 'demonstrativus', from 'demonstratus', past participle of 'demonstrare' (to point out).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06336671-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ dɪˈmɑnstrətɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒnstrətɪv / (BrE)
(of a person) tending to show feelings, especially affection, openly.
openly expressive of emotion

Examples

He is very demonstrative and always hugs his friends when greeting them.
The children in her class are unusually demonstrative about their excitement.
Unlike his reserved brother, Mark is openly demonstrative with his feelings.

Synset Examples

an affectionate and demonstrative family

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reserved - reluctant to show emotions or feelings openly
  • Synonym: expressive - showing one's emotions or feelings openly

Etymology

From Latin demonstrativus, from demonstrare (to show).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00723740-a
Synset POS: a
