Paralichthys lethostigmus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpærəˈlɪkθɪs lɛˈθɒstɪɡməs / (AmE), / ˌpærəˈlɪkθɪs lɛˈθɒstɪɡməs / (BrE)
A species of flounder native to the southern United States, known for its flat body and both eyes on one side.
species of flounder in the southern US

Examples

Commercial fishers often catch Paralichthys lethostigmus in the coastal waters of the Gulf of Mexico.
As a child, she learned to identify Paralichthys lethostigmus while fishing with her grandfather in Louisiana.
The study examined the population trends of Paralichthys lethostigmus over the past decade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flounder - a type of flatfish with both eyes on one side of its head

Etymology

From Greek roots: 'paralichthys' meaning 'parallel fish' and specific epithet 'lethostigmus' from Greek 'lēthē' (forgetfulness) and 'stigma' (mark).
