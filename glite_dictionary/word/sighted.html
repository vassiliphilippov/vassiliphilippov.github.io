sighted

1. ADJ

/ ˈsaɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈsaɪtɪd / (BrE)
Having the ability to see; not blind.
able to see; not blind

Examples

All of the children in the group were sighted and able to participate in the activity.
The sighted volunteers helped to guide those with visual impairments through the museum.
Unlike his sighted classmates, Mark had to use special tools to read the materials.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: blind - unable to see

Etymology

From the verb 'see' + -ed.
