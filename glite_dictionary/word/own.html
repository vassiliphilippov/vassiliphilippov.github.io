own

1. ADJ

/ oʊn / (AmE), / əʊn / (BrE)
used after a possessive to emphasize that something belongs to a particular person or thing and not to anyone else.
emphasizing possession (adj.)

Examples

She wanted a place to call her own.
Every child needs their own room.
He always brings his own lunch to work.

Synset Examples

for your own use
do your own thing
she makes her own clothes
‘ain’ is Scottish

Related Terms

  • Synonym: personal - referring or relating to a particular person

Etymology

From Old English *āgen*, from Proto-Germanic *aiganą* (to possess).

2. VERB

/ oʊn / (AmE), / əʊn / (BrE)
to have legal possession or control of something; to possess.
to possess; to have legal ownership

Examples

She is hoping to own her own home one day.
He owns several rare books that are very valuable.
They owned the company for over twenty years before retiring.

Synset Examples

He owns three houses in Florida
How many cars does she have?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to possess - to have as property; own
  • Antonym: lease - to rent something from another, not own

Etymology

From Old English āgan (to possess, to have), related to German 'eigen' (own).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02209474-v
Synset POS: v
