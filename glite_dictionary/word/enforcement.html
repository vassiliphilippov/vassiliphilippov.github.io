enforcement

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɔrsmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɔːsmənt / (BrE)
the act of making people obey a law, rule, or obligation
the act of making people obey rules or laws

Examples

The police are responsible for the enforcement of the law.
Strict enforcement of safety regulations is essential in the workplace.
Due to lack of enforcement, many people ignored the city's ban on fireworks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: implementation - the process of putting a decision or plan into effect
  • Antonym: neglect - failure to give proper care or attention

Etymology

From Old French 'enforcier', from Latin 'inforciare' (to strengthen), from 'in-' (in) + 'fortis' (strong).
