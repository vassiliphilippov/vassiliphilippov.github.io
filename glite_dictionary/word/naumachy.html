naumachy

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɔməki, nɔˈmækaɪ / (AmE), / ˈnɔːməkɪ, nɔːˈmækaɪ / (BrE)
an ancient Roman spectacle involving a mock sea battle, often staged for public entertainment
mock sea battle in ancient Rome

Examples

The emperor organized a grand naumachy in a specially constructed arena filled with water.
Historians have studied the logistics of staging a naumachy in ancient times.
Visitors marveled at the scale of the reconstructed naumachy during the museum's exhibition.

Etymology

From Latin naumachia, from Ancient Greek naumakhia (naval battle).
