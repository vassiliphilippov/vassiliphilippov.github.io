Hemiptera

1. NOUN

/ hɛˈmɪp.tə.rə / (AmE), / hɛˈmɪp.tərə / (BrE)
A large order of insects that includes the true bugs, such as bedbugs, aphids, plant lice, and cicadas, characterized by their piercing-sucking mouthparts.
order of insects (true bugs, aphids, cicadas)

Examples

The cicada is a member of the order Hemiptera, known for its piercing-sucking mouthparts.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Hemiptera in the rainforests of Brazil.
Many agricultural pests, such as aphids, belong to the Hemiptera order.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bug - an insect, especially one belonging to the order Hemiptera

Etymology

From Greek hēmi- (half) + pteron (wing), referring to the half-membranous wings of many species.

2. NOUN

/ hɛˈmɪptəɹə / (AmE), / hɛˈmɪptərə / (BrE)
a large order of insects including true bugs, plant bugs, bedbugs, aphids, plant lice, and cicadas, characterized by having mouthparts adapted for piercing and sucking.
an order of insects including true bugs
medical

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species in the Hemiptera during their rainforest expedition.
Many agricultural pests belong to the insect order Hemiptera, including aphids and whiteflies.
Cicadas, shield bugs, and bedbugs are all members of the Hemiptera.

Etymology

From Greek hemipteron, from hemi- (half) + pteron (wing).
