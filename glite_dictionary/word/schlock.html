schlock

1. NOUN

/ ʃlɑk / (AmE), / ʃlɒk / (BrE)
Cheap, shoddy, or inferior goods or material.
shoddy or inferior merchandise

Examples

That novelty store sells nothing but schlock to unsuspecting tourists.
The television special was packed with cheap schlock instead of quality content.
He regretted wasting money on schlock that broke after one use.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: junk - things of little value or poor quality

Etymology

From Yiddish shlak (discard, nuisance); of uncertain further origin.
