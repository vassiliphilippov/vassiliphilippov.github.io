Dumetella carolinensis

1. PROPN

/ ˌduːməˈtɛlə ˌkærəˌlaɪˈnɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˌdjuːmɪˈtɛlə ˌkærəlaɪˈnɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A North American songbird of the mockingbird family, commonly known as the gray catbird, noted for its call that resembles a cat's mewing.
North American bird, gray catbird

Examples

The Dumetella carolinensis is commonly heard in dense shrubs during spring and summer.
Ornithologists studied the nesting habits of the Dumetella carolinensis in various habitats.
Birdwatchers often identify the Dumetella carolinensis by its distinctive catlike calls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gray catbird - North American songbird known for its catlike call

Etymology

From Latin 'dumetum' (thicket) and 'carolinensis' (of Carolina), referencing its habitat and geographic origin.
