pittance

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪt.əns / (AmE), / ˈpɪt.əns / (BrE)
a very small or inadequate amount of money paid as wages or allowance
very small amount of money paid

Examples

Many artists are paid a pittance for their work.
She struggled to make ends meet on the pittance she earned.
Despite their hard labor, the workers received only a pittance each week.

Synset Examples

they work all day for a mere pittance

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: salary - regular payment from employer to employee
  • Degree Higher: fortune - a large amount of money
  • Synonym: trifle - something of little value or importance

Etymology

From Old French 'pitance' (allowance of food), from Late Latin 'pietantia' (piety, charity).
