criminality

1. NOUN

/ ˌkrɪmɪˈnæləti / (AmE), / ˌkrɪmɪˈnalɪti / (BrE)
the state or quality of being criminal, or involvement in criminal activity
the state of being criminal

Examples

The report analyzes the causes of urban criminality over the past decade.
Rising criminality in the area has led to increased police patrols.
Factors such as poverty and lack of education can contribute to youth criminality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crime - an act that is against the law and punishable by a state or authority
  • Antonym: innocence - the state of being not guilty of a crime or offense

Etymology

From Medieval Latin criminalitas (quality of being criminal).
