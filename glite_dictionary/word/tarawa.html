1. PROPN

/ təˈrɑːwə / (AmE), / təˈrɑːwə / (BrE)
the capital of Kiribati, a group of atolls and islands in the central Pacific Ocean
capital of Kiribati (Pacific Ocean)

Examples

The government of Kiribati is based in Tarawa.
After several days at sea, the ship finally reached Tarawa.
Many international aid agencies operate offices in Tarawa due to its role as the capital.

2. PROPN

/ təˈrɑːwə / (AmE), / təˈrɑːwə / (BrE)
A group of battles in World War II in the Pacific (November 1943), in which United States Marines captured the islands from Japanese forces after intense fighting.
WWII battle in the Pacific, 1943

Examples

The battle of Tarawa was one of the bloodiest encounters in the Pacific theater of World War II.
Many Marines lost their lives during the assault on Tarawa in November 1943.
Historians often cite Tarawa as a turning point in the Allied campaign against Japan.

Etymology

Named after Tarawa Atoll, the site of the battle, in the Gilbert Islands (now Kiribati).
