overcrossing

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvərˈkrɔsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈkrɒsɪŋ / (BrE)
A bridge or passage built over a road or railway, designed for use by pedestrians.
bridge for pedestrians over a road or railway

Examples

The city recently constructed an overcrossing to help pedestrians cross the busy highway safely.
Students use the overcrossing to get from the school to the park.
Plans for the new shopping center include an overcrossing connecting both sides of the street.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: footbridge - a bridge for pedestrians only

Etymology

From over- (above) + crossing (a place where one crosses).
