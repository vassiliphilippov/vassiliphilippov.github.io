erratum

1. NOUN

/ ɛˈrɑːtəm / (AmE), / ɪˈrɑːtəm / (BrE)
An error in a printed work, typically resulting from a mechanical or typographical mistake, usually published with a correction notice.
printer's or publisher's error in print

Examples

The publisher issued an erratum to correct the misstated data in the report.
Please refer to the erratum on page two for the correct figure.
An erratum was included in the following issue to address the typographical mistake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corrigendum - an error to be corrected in a printed work

Etymology

From Latin 'erratum' (error), past participle of 'errare' (to err).
