Alliaria

1. PROPN

/ əˈlaɪ.əˌrɪə / (AmE), / əˈlɪə.ri.ə / (BrE)
a genus of herbs in the family Brassicaceae, characterized by broad leaves, white flowers, and long seed pods (siliques), including the commonly known garlic mustard.
plant genus including garlic mustard

Examples

The invasive plant Alliaria is often referred to as garlic mustard by gardeners.
Botanists studied the flowering patterns of several species within the Alliaria genus.
Alliaria can quickly dominate woodland habitats, displacing native vegetation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garlic mustard - a common herb in the Alliaria genus, also known as Alliaria petiolata

Etymology

From Latin 'allium' (garlic), referring to the garlic-like smell of some species.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæliˈɛriə / (AmE), / ˌalɪˈɑːrɪə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering herbs in the family Brassicaceae (Cruciferae), known for broad leaves, white flowers, and long seed pods.
genus of herbs with white flowers

Examples

Alliaria is known for its distinct garlic scent, especially when the leaves are crushed.
The species Alliaria petiolata, or garlic mustard, is considered invasive in some regions.
Botanists have studied the reproductive cycle of Alliaria in temperate forests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garlic mustard - common name for Alliaria petiolata, a species in this genus

Etymology

From Latin 'allium' (garlic), referring to the garlic-like smell of some species.
