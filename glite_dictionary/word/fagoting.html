fagoting

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæɡ.ə.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfæɡ.ət.ɪŋ / (BrE)
an embroidery technique in which groups of parallel threads are tied together with fagot stitches to create an openwork pattern
openwork embroidery tying threads with fagot stitches

Examples

She added delicate fagoting to the edges of the linen tablecloth.
The antique dress featured intricate fagoting along the seams for a decorative touch.
Learning fagoting requires patience and attention to detail in stitching.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embroidery - the art or process of decorating fabric with needle and thread

Etymology

From 'fagot' (a bundle of sticks or threads), reference to tying together strands.
