écarté

1. NOUN

/ eɪˈkɑːrteɪ / (AmE), / eɪˈkɑːteɪ / (BrE)
a card game for two players, played with 32 cards in which the king is the highest card
two-player card game with 32 cards

Examples

They spent the evening playing écarté and discussing old times.
In écarté, strategy and memory are crucial for winning against your opponent.
The rules of écarté are easy to learn but require skill to master.

Etymology

From French écarté, meaning 'discarded' or 'separated.'
