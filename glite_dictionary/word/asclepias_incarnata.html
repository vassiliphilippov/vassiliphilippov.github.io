Asclepias incarnata

1. NOUN

/ əˈskliːpiəs ɪnˈkɑrnətə / (AmE), / æˈskliːpiːəs ɪnˈkɑːnətə / (BrE)
A species of milkweed native to eastern North America, known for its dense branching and clusters of white, crimson, or purple flowers; commonly called swamp milkweed.
a species of milkweed plant (swamp milkweed)

Examples

The garden was filled with Asclepias incarnata, attracting butterflies all summer long.
Many conservationists plant Asclepias incarnata to support monarch butterfly populations.
With its bright blooms, Asclepias incarnata adds a splash of color to wetland areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swamp milkweed - common name for Asclepias incarnata
  • Degree Higher: milkweed - any plant of the genus Asclepias

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Asclepias' derived from Asclepius, the Greek god of healing; species Latin 'incarnata' meaning 'flesh-colored'.
