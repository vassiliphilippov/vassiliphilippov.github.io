mavin

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪ.vɪn / (AmE), / ˈmeɪ.vɪn / (BrE)
a person who is highly skilled or knowledgeable in a particular field; an expert or connoisseur
expert; skilled or knowledgeable person
american

Examples

The young programmer quickly became a mavin in artificial intelligence.
As a wine mavin, she is often asked to judge international competitions.
He relied on a financial mavin to guide his investments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expert - a person with comprehensive knowledge or skill in a subject
  • Antonym: amateur - a person who is not skilled or professional in a particular field

Etymology

From Yiddish *meyvn* (one who understands), from Hebrew *mevin* (one who understands).
