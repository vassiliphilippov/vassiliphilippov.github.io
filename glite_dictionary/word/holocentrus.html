Holocentrus

1. NOUN

/ ˌhoʊləˈsɛntrəs / (AmE), / ˌhəʊləˈsɛntrəs / (BrE)
A genus of marine fishes in the family Holocentridae, commonly known as squirrelfishes, found mainly in tropical seas.
genus of squirrelfishes

Examples

Researchers studied several species of Holocentrus in the coral reefs of the Caribbean.
The Holocentrus genus is known for its vibrant red coloration and large eyes.
Juvenile Holocentrus are often seen hiding among crevices during the day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squirrelfish - a common name for fishes in the family Holocentridae

Etymology

From Greek 'holos' (whole) and 'kentron' (spur, sting).

2. NOUN

/ ˌhoʊləˈsɛntrəs / (AmE), / ˌhɒləˈsɛntrəs / (BrE)
A genus of marine fishes in the family Holocentridae, commonly known as squirrelfishes, found in tropical and subtropical seas.
genus of squirrelfishes

Examples

The tropical reefs are home to the bright red Holocentrus species.
Marine biologists identified a new member of the Holocentrus genus during their expedition.
Aquarists often admire the vivid coloration of Holocentrus in large saltwater tanks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squirrelfish - common name for fish of the family Holocentridae

Etymology

From Greek 'holos' (whole) + 'kentron' (sharp point), referring to certain features of the fish.
