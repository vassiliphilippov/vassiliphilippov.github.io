hammertoe

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæmərˌtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈhæməˌtəʊ / (BrE)
a deformity in which a toe is permanently bent downward in a clawlike position, typically causing discomfort or difficulty walking
toe deformity with bent, clawlike shape
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her with a hammertoe on her right foot, which made walking painful.
Wearing poorly fitting shoes for years contributed to the development of hammertoes.
Surgery was recommended to correct the advanced hammertoe deformity.

Etymology

From the resemblance of the bent toe to the shape of a hammer.
