Betsy Ross

1. PROPN

/ ˈbɛtsi rɑs / (AmE), / ˈbɛtsi rɒs / (BrE)
(1752–1836) An American seamstress who, according to legend, made the first American flag at the request of George Washington.
legendary maker of the first American flag

Examples

The story of Betsy Ross sewing the first American flag is taught in many U.S. schools.
Historians debate whether Betsy Ross truly designed and made the original Stars and Stripes.
A museum in Philadelphia commemorates the life and legend of Betsy Ross.
