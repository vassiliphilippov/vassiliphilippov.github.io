arteria metatarsea

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈtɪriə ˌmɛtəˈtɑrsiə / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪəriə ˌmɛtəˈtɑːsiə / (BrE)
Any of the arteries that supply blood to the metatarsal region of the foot, including both dorsal and plantar branches.
artery supplying the metatarsal region of the foot
medical

Examples

The arteria metatarsea plays a crucial role in the blood supply to the forefoot.
Injury to the arteria metatarsea can lead to compromised circulation in the metatarsal area.
Surgeons must take care not to damage the arteria metatarsea during reconstructive foot procedures.

Etymology

From Latin 'arteria' (artery) and 'metatarsea' (metatarsal, pertaining to the metatarsus).
