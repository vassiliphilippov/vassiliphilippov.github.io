Lepidodendraceae

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛpɪdoʊˌdɛnˈdreɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌlɛpɪdəʊˌdɛnˈdreɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
an extinct family of fossil plants characterized by prominent, spirally arranged leaf scars on their trunks; commonly known as scale trees
extinct family of fossil scale trees

Examples

The fossilized trunks of Lepidodendraceae display distinctive spiral leaf scar patterns.
Paleontologists have identified Lepidodendraceae in coal deposits from the Carboniferous period.
The unique bark of Lepidodendraceae helps scientists reconstruct ancient forest environments.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'lepis' (scale), 'dendron' (tree), and '-aceae' (family suffix).

2. NOUN

/ lɛpɪˌdoʊdnˈdræsiˌi / (AmE), / lɛpɪˌdɒdɛnˈdræsiˌi / (BrE)
an extinct family of ancient, tree-like plants known for their conspicuous, spirally arranged leaf scars on the trunk, found as fossils
extinct family of fossil plants with spiral leaf scars

Examples

The fossilized trunks of Lepidodendraceae are often found in Carboniferous coal deposits.
Scientists study Lepidodendraceae to understand the evolution of ancient forests.
Distinctive leaf scars make Lepidodendraceae fossils easily recognizable under the microscope.

Etymology

From genus Lepidodendron (Greek 'lepis' meaning 'scale' and 'dendron' meaning 'tree') plus the Latin scientific family suffix '-aceae'.
