trophy

1. NOUN

/ ˈtroʊfi / (AmE), / ˈtrəʊfi / (BrE)
an object given as a prize for victory or achievement, especially in war or hunting, kept as a symbol of success
an award for victory or achievement, esp. in war or hunting

Examples

The hunter kept the antlers as a trophy of his successful expedition.
After the battle, the soldiers brought home a flag as a trophy of their victory.
Ancient warriors often displayed enemy weapons as a trophy to mark their triumph.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: award - something given as a prize or recognition of merit

Etymology

From Greek *tropaion* (monument of a victory), from *tropē* (a rout).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtroʊfi / (AmE), / ˈtrəʊfi / (BrE)
an object, such as a cup or plaque, awarded as a prize for a victory or achievement
object awarded for victory or achievement

Examples

The team lifted the trophy after winning the championship match.
This gold cup was his first major trophy as a professional athlete.
She displayed her science fair trophy proudly on her shelf.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: award - something given as a prize for achievement
  • Synonym: prize - something given to the winner of a competition
  • Antonym: penalty - a punishment for breaking a rule or law

Etymology

From Greek tropaion (a monument of victory), via Latin tropaeum.
