westward

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɛstwərd / (AmE), / ˈwɛstwəd / (BrE)
Moving or directed toward the west.
moving toward the west

Examples

They continued their westward journey across the plains.
Strong winds pushed the smoke westward into neighboring towns.
Explorers set out on a westward expedition in search of new lands.

Synset Examples

westbound pioneers

Related Terms

  • Antonym: eastward - moving toward the east
  • Synonym: westbound - traveling toward the west

Etymology

From Old English 'westweard', combining 'west' + '-ward'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00828077-s

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɛstwərd / (AmE), / ˈwɛstwəd / (BrE)
the direction or point on the compass that is directly opposite east, at 270 degrees
direction on the compass at 270 degrees

Examples

The sun sets in the westward every evening.
They sailed toward the westward, hoping to discover new lands.
The storm was approaching from the westward as predicted.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: west - the cardinal direction at 270 degrees; the opposite of east
  • Antonym: east - the cardinal compass point at 90 degrees; opposite of west

Etymology

From Old English 'westweard', meaning 'toward the west'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13856598-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADV

/ ˈwɛstwɚd / (AmE), / ˈwɛstwəd / (BrE)
toward the west; in the direction of the west.
toward the west

Examples

The pioneers moved westward in search of new land.
The clouds drifted westward as the wind picked up.
Many birds migrate westward during the autumn season.

Synset Examples

they traveled westward toward the setting sun

Related Terms

  • Antonym: eastward - toward the east; in the direction of the east
  • Degree Lower: northward - toward the north; in the direction of the north
  • Degree Lower: southward - toward the south; in the direction of the south

Etymology

From Old English 'westweard', meaning 'in the direction of the west'.
