depressor

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈprɛsɚ / (AmE), / dɪˈprɛsə / (BrE)
a nerve whose activity decreases the activity or tone of the body part it supplies
nerve that reduces activity or tone
medical

Examples

The surgeon identified a depressor responsible for reducing heart rate during the procedure.
Damage to the depressor may result in increased muscle tone in the affected area.
Stimulation of the depressor led to a noticeable decrease in blood pressure.

Etymology

From Latin 'deprimere' (to press down), via 'depressor' (one who presses down).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈprɛsər / (AmE), / dɪˈprɛsə / (BrE)
a medical instrument used to press down a part of the body, especially during an examination
medical instrument for pressing down body parts
medical

Examples

The doctor used a depressor to examine the patient's throat more easily.
A wooden depressor is commonly used during routine oral check-ups.
The kit included a sterile depressor for inspecting the mouth and throat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tongue depressor - flat instrument to press the tongue down during examination

Etymology

From Latin 'deprimere' (to press down), from 'de-' (down) + 'premere' (to press).

3. NOUN

/ dɪˈprɛsər / (AmE), / dɪˈprɛsə / (BrE)
a skeletal muscle that lowers or draws down a part of the body
muscle that draws a body part downward
medical

Examples

The depressor anguli oris muscle helps pull down the corners of the mouth.
In anatomy class, we studied how the depressor muscles counteract the action of levators.
Damage to a depressor muscle can affect a person's ability to lower their jaw.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: levator - a muscle that raises a body part

Etymology

From Latin deprimere (to press down).
