prox

1. ADJ

/ prɑks / (AmE), / prɒks / (BrE)
(dated) Of or in the next month after the present; used especially with dates.
of or in the next month (dated)
archaic

Examples

The meeting has been postponed to the 12th prox.
Please submit your final report by the 1st prox.
Her appointment is scheduled for the 6th prox.

Synset Examples

scheduled for the 6th prox

Related Terms

  • Antonym: instant - of the present month (with a date); abbrev. 'inst.'
  • Antonym: ultimo - of the previous month (with a date); abbrev. 'ult.'

Etymology

Shortened from Latin proximo mense (in the next month), from proximo ('next').
