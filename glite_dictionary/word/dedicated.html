dedicated

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɛdəˌkeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈdɛdɪkeɪtɪd / (BrE)
solemnly devoted or set apart for a particular purpose, especially a sacred or commemorative one
set apart for a sacred or special purpose

Examples

The newly built memorial was dedicated to honor the fallen soldiers.
Many churches are dedicated to specific saints or events in religious history.
A special room is dedicated as a place for quiet reflection and prayer.

Synset Examples

a life consecrated to science
the consecrated chapel
a chapel dedicated to the dead of World War II

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consecrated - officially declared as sacred

Etymology

From Latin dedicatus, past participle of dedicare (to dedicate, to consecrate).

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɛdɪˌkeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈdɛdɪkeɪtɪd / (BrE)
devoted to a particular task, purpose, or cause; demonstrating strong commitment or loyalty
devoted or committed to a purpose or cause

Examples

The organization relies on a team of dedicated volunteers who work tirelessly every week.
She is a dedicated scientist, always striving to advance her research.
His dedicated efforts helped the project succeed ahead of schedule.

Synset Examples

a dedicated dancer
dedicated teachers
dedicated to the proposition that all men are created equal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: committed - feeling dedication or loyalty to a cause, activity, or job
  • Synonym: devoted - very loving or loyal; dedicated to a cause or activity
  • Antonym: indifferent - having no particular interest or sympathy; unconcerned

Etymology

From Latin dedicatus, past participle of dedicare (to dedicate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00521136-a
Synset POS: a
