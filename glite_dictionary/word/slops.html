slops

1. NOUN

/ slɑps / (AmE), / slɒps / (BrE)
Cheap, ready-made clothing, especially as formerly supplied to sailors in Britain.
cheap clothing supplied to sailors
archaic british

Examples

The naval quartermaster distributed slops to the new recruits as part of their uniform.
In the 18th century, sailors often wore slops because they were affordable and easy to replace.
Records show that many seamen purchased slops from the ship’s store before setting sail.

Etymology

From Middle English 'sloppe' (outer garment), from Old English 'slop' (garment).

2. NOUN

/ slɑps / (AmE), / slɒps / (BrE)
wet food, typically kitchen waste mixed with water or milk, fed to pigs or other animals
wet animal feed made from kitchen waste

Examples

The farmer poured the slops into the pig trough every morning.
It is illegal in some countries to feed kitchen slops to livestock due to disease risk.
The children collected slops after dinner to take to the pigs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swill - liquid kitchen waste fed to animals, especially pigs

Etymology

From Middle English 'sloppe', related to the idea of a wet or sloppy mixture.
