to advertise

1. VERB

/ ˈæd.vɚˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈæd.və.taɪz / (BrE)
to make something known to the public, especially to call attention to something so that people become aware of it
to make something known publicly

Examples

It's not wise to advertise your travel plans on social media.
She didn't want to advertise her promotion until everything was official.
The organization tried to advertise their achievements to attract new members.

Synset Examples

Please don't advertise the fact that he has AIDS

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to publicize - to make information widely known
  • Antonym: to conceal - to hide or keep something secret

Etymology

From Latin 'advertere' (to turn toward), via Old French 'advertir'.

2. VERB

/ ˈæd.vərˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈæd.və.taɪz / (BrE)
to make the public aware of a product, service, or event in order to encourage people to buy or use it
promote a product or service

Examples

The company plans to advertise the new product in national newspapers and magazines.
She was hired to advertise the concert on social media platforms.
Many businesses advertise special offers to attract new customers.

Synset Examples

The salesman is aggressively pushing the new computer model
The company is heavily advertizing their new laptops

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to promote - to support or actively encourage others to buy or try something
  • Synonym: to publicize - to make something widely known to the public
  • Antonym: to conceal - to keep something secret or hidden

Etymology

From Latin 'ad' (to) + 'vertere' (to turn); influenced by French 'advertir' (to warn, inform).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00978685-v
Synset POS: v
