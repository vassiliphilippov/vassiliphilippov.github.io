amphibrach

1. NOUN

/ ˈæm.fɪˌbræk / (AmE), / ˈam.fɪ.brak / (BrE)
A metrical foot in poetry consisting of three syllables, with the first and third being unstressed and the second stressed (as in 'remember').
metrical foot: unstressed-stressed-unstressed

Examples

In the poem, each line begins with an amphibrach to create a gentle rhythm.
Poets often choose the amphibrach to add variation to their metrical patterns.
'Remember' is a word that illustrates the amphibrach foot in English.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iamb - metrical foot: unstressed-stressed
  • Synonym: trochee - metrical foot: stressed-unstressed
  • Degree Higher: dactyl - metrical foot: stressed-unstressed-unstressed
  • Degree Higher: anapest - metrical foot: unstressed-unstressed-stressed

Etymology

From Greek 'amphíbrakhys', meaning 'short on both sides', from 'amphi-' (on both sides) and 'brakhys' (short).
