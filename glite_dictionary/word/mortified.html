mortified

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɔrtəˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈmɔːtɪfaɪd / (BrE)
(of flesh or tissue) affected by death of tissue, especially as a result of disease or injury; gangrenous.
affected by tissue death (medical)
medical

Examples

The surgeon noted that the patient's foot was mortified and required urgent intervention.
If the tissue becomes mortified, amputation may be necessary to prevent the spread of infection.
Doctors treated the mortified area to stop the necrosis from advancing further.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gangrenous - affected by or resembling gangrene
  • Synonym: necrotic - relating to the death of cells or tissue

Etymology

From Latin 'mortificare' meaning 'to put to death', from 'mors' (death).

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɔːrtɪˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈmɔːtɪfaɪd / (BrE)
feeling extremely embarrassed or ashamed
deeply embarrassed or ashamed

Examples

She was mortified when she realized she had called her teacher 'mom' in front of the whole class.
He felt mortified by the rude comment made about his work.
The child looked mortified as everyone stared at his torn costume.

Synset Examples

too embarrassed to say hello to his drunken father on the street
humiliated that his wife had to go out to work
felt mortified by the comparison with her sister

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humiliated - feeling a loss of pride or dignity
  • Synonym: embarrassed - feeling awkward or self-conscious
  • Antonym: proud - feeling of satisfaction and self-respect

Etymology

From Latin 'mortificare' (to put to death, to subdue), from 'mort-' (death).
