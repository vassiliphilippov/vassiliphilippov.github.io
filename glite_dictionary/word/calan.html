Calan

1. NOUN

/ ˈkælæn / (AmE), / ˈkælæn / (BrE)
A proprietary drug (trade name for verapamil) used as a calcium channel blocker to treat hypertension, angina, heart failure, or migraine.
proprietary name for verapamil (calcium blocker drug)
medical

Examples

My doctor prescribed Calan to help manage my high blood pressure.
Patients taking Calan should avoid grapefruit juice due to possible drug interactions.
The pharmacist explained the possible side effects of Calan before dispensing the medication.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: verapamil - a medication used to treat high blood pressure and heart conditions
