polyanthus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːliˈænθəs / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˈænθəs / (BrE)
A primrose of a hybrid group cultivated for its varied, colorful flowers, derived from crosses between the oxlip, cowslip, and common primrose.
a hybrid primrose grown for its colorful flowers

Examples

The garden bed was full of bright polyanthus in shades of red, yellow, and purple.
She planted several polyanthus to add a splash of color to the border in early spring.
Horticulturists prize the polyanthus for its resilience and vibrant blossoms.

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek polys (many) + anthos (flower).
