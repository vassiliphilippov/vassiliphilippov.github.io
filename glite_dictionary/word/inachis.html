Inachis

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈneɪkɪs / (AmE), / ɪˈneɪkɪs / (BrE)
a genus of butterflies in the family Nymphalidae, known for species such as the European peacock butterfly.
genus of butterflies in Nymphalidae

Examples

The European peacock butterfly, once classified under Inachis, is a familiar sight in many gardens.
Research on Inachis has contributed to our understanding of butterfly mimicry and coloration.
Some taxonomists have debated the classification of Inachis within the Nymphalidae family.

Etymology

From New Latin, possibly named after Inachus, a figure in Greek mythology.
