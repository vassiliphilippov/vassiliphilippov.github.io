Hyacinthus orientalis albulus

1. NOUN

/ haɪ.əˈsɪn.θəs ɔːr.i.enˈteɪ.lɪs ælˈbjuː.ləs / (AmE), / haɪ.əˈsɪn.θəs ɒr.i.enˈteɪ.lɪs ælˈbjuː.ləs / (BrE)
A cultivated variety of the common hyacinth, having loosely flowered spikes with several growing from a single bulb.
a cultivar of common hyacinth with loose flower spikes

Examples

The Hyacinthus orientalis albulus is prized for its delicate, loosely packed flowers.
Gardeners often plant Hyacinthus orientalis albulus to add a lighter touch to spring displays.
Each bulb of Hyacinthus orientalis albulus can produce several graceful flower spikes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hyacinth - A bulbous flowering plant of the genus Hyacinthus

Etymology

From the botanical Latin 'Hyacinthus' (genus), 'orientalis' (eastern), and 'albulus' (whitish, pale).
