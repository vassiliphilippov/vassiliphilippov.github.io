tester

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛstər / (AmE), / ˈtɛstə / (BrE)
A flat canopy, especially one over a four-poster bed, often used as a decorative or protective covering.
canopy over a bed

Examples

The ornate tester above the bed was draped with velvet curtains.
Sunlight filtered softly through the fabric of the tester, casting shadows on the pillows below.
The antique bed still retained its original tester, intricately carved from oak.

Etymology

From Old French *testiere* (headpiece, canopy), from Latin *testa* (tile, shell).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛstər / (AmE), / ˈtɛstə / (BrE)
a person who administers a test to assess someone’s abilities, knowledge, or qualifications
person who gives a test

Examples

The tester carefully explained the instructions before starting the exam.
During the interview process, the tester asked technical questions to evaluate each candidate.
If you have any questions, please ask the tester before you begin the assessment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: examiner - a person who officially tests knowledge or skill

Etymology

From Middle English 'testen' (to test) + -er (agent noun suffix), ultimately from Latin 'testari' (to witness).
