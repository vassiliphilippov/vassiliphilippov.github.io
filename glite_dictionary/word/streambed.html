streambed

1. NOUN

/ ˈstriːmˌbɛd / (AmE), / ˈstriːmˌbɛd / (BrE)
the channel which is occupied or was formerly occupied by a stream or river
channel of a stream or river

Examples

The floodwaters carved a new path through the streambed after the heavy rains.
We found smooth stones scattered across the dry streambed during our hike.
Salmon swim upstream each year to spawn in their native streambeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: riverbed - the channel at the bottom of a river

Etymology

From 'stream' + 'bed'; patterned after similar compound nouns meaning the bottom or channel occupied by a liquid.
