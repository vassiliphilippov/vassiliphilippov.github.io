Adad

1. PROPN

/ əˈdɑd / (AmE), / əˈdɑːd / (BrE)
A Babylonian god of storms and wind in ancient Mesopotamian religion.
Babylonian god of storms and wind

Examples

Ancient texts describe Adad as a powerful force governing both storms and rain.
Priests offered sacrifices to Adad to ensure favorable weather for their crops.
The temple was dedicated to Adad, reflecting his importance in Babylonian society.

Etymology

From Akkadian 'Adad', the name of the deity.
