buoyant

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɔɪənt, ˈbuːjənt / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪənt, ˈbʊjənt / (BrE)
able to float or rise easily in liquid or air, or having the tendency to do so.
able to float or rise in liquid or air

Examples

The wooden raft was so buoyant that it easily supported their weight on the lake.
Helium makes balloons buoyant enough to rise quickly into the sky.
Cork is a naturally buoyant material commonly used in life vests.

Synset Examples

buoyant balloons
buoyant balsawood boats
a floaty scarf

Related Terms

  • Synonym: floaty - light and tending to float

Etymology

From French *bouyant*, from buoy (‘float’).

2. ADJ

/ ˈbɔɪənt / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪənt / (BrE)
cheerful and lively; showing a high spirit and lightheartedness
cheerful and lively

Examples

Even after the setback, her buoyant attitude inspired the whole team.
He is known for his buoyant personality, always ready to lighten the mood.
The children's buoyant laughter filled the playground on the sunny afternoon.

Synset Examples

buoyant spirits
his quick wit and chirpy humor
looking bright and well and chirpy
a perky little widow in her 70s

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheerful - happy and optimistic
  • Antonym: gloomy - sad or lacking in cheerfulness
  • Synonym: perky - cheerful, lively, and self-assured

Etymology

From French *bouyant*, from Old French *bouer* (to float), ultimately from Latin *bullire* (to bubble, to boil).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00364701-s
Synset POS: s
