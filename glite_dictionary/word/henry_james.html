Henry James

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɛnri ˈdʒeɪmz / (AmE), / ˈhɛnri ˈdʒeɪmz / (BrE)
(1843–1916) An American-born writer who became a British citizen, known for his novels exploring consciousness and perception, including 'The Portrait of a Lady' and 'The Turn of the Screw'.
American-British novelist (1843–1916)

Examples

Henry James was renowned for his psychological insight and complex narrative style.
Many scholars study the novels of Henry James to understand transatlantic literary relations.
'The Turn of the Screw' is one of Henry James's most famous works.
