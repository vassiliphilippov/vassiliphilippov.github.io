childbed

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃaɪldˌbɛd / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪldˌbɛd / (BrE)
the period from the onset of labor to the birth of a child; the process of childbirth
period of labor and childbirth
archaic medical

Examples

During her childbed, she was attended by a skilled midwife.
The risks associated with childbed were much greater before modern medicine.
Old records show that many women did not survive childbed in the past.

Synset Examples

she was in labor for six hours

Related Terms

  • Synonym: labor - the process of childbirth, especially the period of uterine contractions and delivery

Etymology

From Old English 'cildbedd', literally 'child bed', referring to the place or period of childbirth.
