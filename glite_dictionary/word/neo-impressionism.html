Neo-Impressionism

1. NOUN

/ ˌnioʊ ɪmˈprɛʃəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌniːəʊ ɪmˈprɛʃənɪzəm / (BrE)
A late 19th-century French art movement that developed from Impressionism, characterized by the use of systematic techniques such as pointillism to depict light and color.
late 19th-century French painting movement

Examples

Georges Seurat and Paul Signac are well-known pioneers of Neo-Impressionism.
The exhibition features major works from the Neo-Impressionism period, emphasizing color theory and technique.
Her studies on Neo-Impressionism revolutionized our understanding of post-Impressionist art.

Synset Examples

The neo-impressionism of Maximilien Luce and his supporter Robert Bernier, for instance, must be distinguished from that of Seurat and his champion Gustave Kahn, a difference that can be coded especially, but not exclusively, along lines of class and audience.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Impressionism - An earlier 19th-century art movement focusing on light and color
  • Synonym: pointillism - A painting technique using small dots of color

Etymology

From French 'néo-impressionnisme', combining 'néo-' (new) and 'impressionnisme' (Impressionism).
