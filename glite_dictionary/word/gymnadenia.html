Gymnadenia

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪmˌnæˈdiːniə / (AmE), / dʒɪmˌnæˈdiːniə / (BrE)
a small genus of terrestrial orchids found in North America and temperate Eurasia, known for their fragrant flowers and narrow leaves
genus of terrestrial orchids

Examples

Botanists discovered a new species of Gymnadenia in the alpine meadows.
The distinctive scent of Gymnadenia flowers attracts a variety of pollinators.
Several conservation efforts focus on preserving wild populations of Gymnadenia in Europe.

Etymology

From Greek 'gymnos' (naked) + 'aden' (gland), referring to the naked glands of the flower.
