booze

1. NOUN

/ buz / (AmE), / buːz / (BrE)
Strong alcoholic drink, especially distilled spirits; alcohol, especially in informal speech.
informal: alcoholic drink
informal

Examples

He hid a bottle of booze in his desk drawer at work.
They served plenty of booze at the New Year's Eve party.
She decided to give up booze after her health scare.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liquor - Strong alcoholic drink; distilled spirit
  • Synonym: drink - An alcoholic beverage
  • Antonym: soft drink - A drink without alcohol

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from Dutch 'busen', meaning 'to drink to excess'.

2. VERB

/ buːz / (AmE), / buːz / (BrE)
to drink alcohol, especially in large quantities, often in an informal or excessive manner
drink alcohol informally or excessively
informal slang

Examples

After work, they liked to booze at the local bar for hours.
He had promised to stop boozing every weekend, but he kept going out.
She got into trouble at university because she boozed too much during her first year.

Synset Examples

We were up drinking all night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drink - to consume a liquid, usually referring to alcohol in context
  • Antonym: to abstain - to deliberately avoid drinking alcohol or other intoxicants

Etymology

Originated in the mid-17th century from the noun 'booze,' possibly related to the Middle Dutch 'busen' (to drink to excess).
