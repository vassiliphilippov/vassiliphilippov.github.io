duty

1. NOUN

/ ˈduːti / (AmE), / ˈdjuːti / (BrE)
a moral or legal obligation; the social or moral force that compels someone to act in a certain way
moral or social obligation

Examples

She felt it was her duty to care for her elderly parents.
Citizens have a duty to obey the law and respect others.
He refused the offer, saying that his duty lay elsewhere.

Synset Examples

we must instill a sense of duty in our children
every right implies a responsibility; every opportunity, an obligation; every possession, a duty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obligation - something a person is bound to do, especially legally or morally
  • Synonym: responsibility - the state or fact of having a duty to deal with something

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman *duete*, from Old French *deu* (owed), from Latin *debitum* (something owed).

2. NOUN

/ ˈduːti / (AmE), / ˈdjuːti / (BrE)
A task or responsibility that you are required to perform because of moral or legal reasons.
work or responsibility required by morality or law

Examples

He took his duty as a doctor very seriously and always put his patients first.
Jury duty is a civic responsibility required by law.
She felt it was her duty to help her elderly neighbor with groceries.

Synset Examples

the duties of the job

Related Terms

  • Synonym: responsibility - A duty or obligation to do something
  • Synonym: obligation - An act or course of action to which a person is morally or legally bound
  • Antonym: right - A moral or legal entitlement to have or do something

Etymology

From Old French 'deu' (owed), from Latin 'debitum' (something owed).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈduːti / (AmE), / ˈdjuːti / (BrE)
a tax paid to the government, especially on goods imported into or exported from a country
tax on imports or exports

Examples

Importers must pay a duty on certain electronics brought into the country.
The government lowered the duty on automobiles to encourage trade.
When the duty was increased, the price of luxury goods rose significantly.

Synset Examples

they signed a treaty to lower duties on trade between their countries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tariff - a tax imposed on imported or exported goods
  • Synonym: excise - a tax levied on certain goods produced or sold within a country

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman 'dueté', from Old French 'deu' (owed), from Latin 'debitum' (what is owed).

Synset POS: n
