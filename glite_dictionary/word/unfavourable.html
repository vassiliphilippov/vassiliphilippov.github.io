unfavourable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfeɪvərəbl̩ / (AmE), / ʌnˈfeɪvərəbl / (BrE)
not likely to give approval, support, or success; not encouraging or pleasing.
not approving or encouraging
british

Examples

The weather forecast predicts unfavourable conditions for tomorrow's match.
His proposal received several unfavourable comments from the board members.
An unfavourable comparison was made between the new policy and the old one.

Synset Examples

unfavorable conditions
an unfavorable comparison
unfavorable comments
unfavorable impression

Related Terms

  • Antonym: favourable - approving or helpful, encouraging
  • Synonym: adverse - preventing success or development; harmful

Etymology

From un- (not) + favourable, based on favour (from Latin favorem, 'goodwill').

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfeɪ.vɚ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈfeɪ.vər.ə.bəl / (BrE)
(of winds or weather) tending to hinder progress or cause difficulties; not conducive to success or advantage.
hindering; not helpful (esp. of weather/winds)
british

Examples

The sailors were delayed by unfavourable winds on their journey.
The race was postponed due to unfavourable weather conditions.
Climbing the mountain became dangerous when the team encountered unfavourable winds at the summit.

Synset Examples

unfavorable winds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unfavorable - (of winds or weather) tending to hinder or oppose (American spelling)
  • Antonym: favourable - giving an advantage; helpful or positive (esp. of weather)

Etymology

From un- (not) + favourable, from Latin *favorem* (support).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00998815-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ ʌnˈfeɪvərəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈfeɪvərəbl / (BrE)
not giving or showing approval or support; likely to result in a negative or disadvantageous outcome.
likely to cause a negative outcome
british

Examples

The report gave an unfavourable assessment of the company's financial prospects.
He faced unfavourable conditions during his climb up the mountain.
Her proposal received an unfavourable response from the committee.

Synset Examples

a disadvantageous outcome
a well-known study from the 1970's showed that gender stereotyping placed women in a disadvantageous position
made an unfavorable impression

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unfavorable - the American English spelling of 'unfavourable'
  • Antonym: favourable - giving approval or support; positive or advantageous

Etymology

From prefix un- (not) + favourable (from Latin favorem, meaning support or approval).
