tremolo

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛməˌloʊ / (AmE), / ˈtrɛmələʊ / (BrE)
(music) a trembling effect produced by the rapid repetition of a single note or the rapid alternation between two notes.
musical trembling effect or rapid note repetition

Examples

The violinist used tremolo to add dramatic tension to the piece.
Guitar players often use a tremolo effect to create a shimmering sound.
The rapid tremolo of the piano gave the melody a sense of urgency.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vibrato - (music) a slight regular fluctuation in pitch to enrich musical tone

Etymology

Italian, from 'tremolo' meaning 'tremulous' or 'trembling', from Latin 'tremulus'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrɛməˌloʊ / (AmE), / ˈtrɛmələʊ / (BrE)
A wavering effect in a singer's voice, especially when excessive or uncontrolled; vocal vibrato.
wavering vocal effect; vocal vibrato

Examples

The singer's tremolo became more pronounced as she neared the end of the aria.
He was criticized for allowing his tremolo to overshadow the melody during his performance.
A slight tremolo in her voice revealed her nervousness on stage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vibrato - regular, slight variation in pitch in music or singing

Etymology

From Italian 'tremolo', from Latin 'tremulus' (trembling).
