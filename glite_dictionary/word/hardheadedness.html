hardheadedness

1. NOUN

/ hɑrdˈhɛdɪdnəs / (AmE), / hɑːdˈhɛdɪdnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being practical, stubborn, or unwilling to be influenced by emotions or opinions.
stubborn or practical attitude

Examples

Her hardheadedness made negotiations challenging, as she refused to compromise on any point.
Despite his hardheadedness, he was respected for sticking to his principles in difficult situations.
I admire her hardheadedness when it comes to making tough business decisions.

Synset Examples

His hardheadedness in refusing to accept on faith the deliverances even of psychologists such as Munsterberg on the nature of testimony has become classic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stubbornness - the quality of being stubborn
  • Synonym: obstinacy - the quality of being obstinate or difficult to persuade
  • Antonym: open-mindedness - willingness to consider new ideas

Etymology

From 'hardheaded' + '-ness', meaning the state or condition of being hardheaded.
