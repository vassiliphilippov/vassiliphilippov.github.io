Pooecetes

1. NOUN

/ puˈɛsɪtiːz / (AmE), / puˈɛsɪtiːz / (BrE)
A genus of songbirds in the family Fringillidae, containing the vesper sparrow (Pooecetes gramineus).
genus of sparrows (vesper sparrow)

Examples

The Pooecetes genus is best known for including the vesper sparrow.
Ornithologists studied the habitat preferences of Pooecetes species in grasslands.
Field guides list Pooecetes as a distinct genus among North American songbirds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vesper sparrow - a North American songbird, member of the genus Pooecetes

Etymology

From Greek 'poa' (grass) and 'oiketes' (dweller), indicating a grassland habitat.

2. NOUN

/ poʊ.iˈsiː.tiːz / (AmE), / pəʊ.iːˈsiː.tiːz / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Fringillidae, consisting of the vesper sparrow.
genus of birds (vesper sparrow)

Examples

The Pooecetes genus includes only one species, the vesper sparrow.
Ornithologists study Pooecetes to better understand the habitat needs of vesper sparrows.
The field guide listed Pooecetes under the family Fringillidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vesper sparrow - a North American songbird, only species in genus Pooecetes

Etymology

From New Latin Pooecetes, the genus name used in scientific classification.
