demoralising

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈmɔːrəˌlaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒr.ə.laɪ.zɪŋ / (BrE)
Causing someone to lose confidence or hope; undermining morale or self-reliance.
causing loss of confidence or morale
british

Examples

Losing three games in a row was utterly demoralising for the team.
The constant criticism had a demoralising effect on the staff's performance.
It can be demoralising to work hard and receive no recognition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demoralizing - causing loss of morale; US spelling
  • Antonym: encouraging - giving support or confidence; opposite of demoralising

Etymology

From 'demoralise' + '-ing'; 'demoralise' from French 'démoraliser'.
