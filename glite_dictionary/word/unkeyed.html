unkeyed

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈkid / (AmE), / ʌnˈkiːd / (BrE)
(of music) not based on or avoiding traditional Western tonality; not in a musical key.
music not in a traditional key

Examples

The composer chose an unkeyed style to evoke a sense of freedom and ambiguity.
Modernist music often features unkeyed passages that challenge traditional harmony.
The piece's unkeyed structure made it sound unfamiliar to the audience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atonal - not written in any key or mode; lacking a tonal center

Etymology

Formed by the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'keyed' (having a key, as in music).
