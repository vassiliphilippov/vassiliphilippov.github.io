amarelle

1. NOUN

/ ˌæməˈrɛl / (AmE), / ˌaməˈrɛl / (BrE)
A type of cultivated sour cherry tree that bears pale red fruit with colorless juice, as well as the fruit itself.
type of sour cherry tree or fruit

Examples

The orchard grows a rare variety of amarelle cherries prized for their pale color and tart flavor.
She baked a pie using amarelle cherries instead of the more common sweet ones.
Unlike morello cherries, amarelle varieties produce fruit with nearly colorless juice.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: morello - type of sour cherry with dark red fruit and juice

Etymology

From French 'amarelle', from Italian 'amarena' meaning 'sour cherry'.

2. NOUN

/ əˈmærəl / (AmE), / əˈmarɛl / (BrE)
A variety of sour cherry that is pale red in color and has colorless or nearly colorless juice.
pale red sour cherry with clear juice

Examples

The orchard grows several varieties of sour cherries, including the amarelle.
Chefs often prefer amarelle cherries for recipes that call for a pale color and tart flavor.
Unlike the morello, the juice from an amarelle is nearly colorless, giving desserts a delicate blush.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: morello - a dark red sour cherry with dark juice

Etymology

From French 'amarelle', ultimately from Latin 'amarus' (bitter).
