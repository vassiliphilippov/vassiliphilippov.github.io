bezzant

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛzənt / (AmE), / ˈbɛzənt / (BrE)
a gold coin of the Byzantine Empire, widely used in Europe during the Middle Ages
Byzantine gold coin, Middle Ages

Examples

The merchant paid for the spices with a single bezzant.
In medieval times, the bezzant was considered a symbol of wealth and power.
A collection of ancient bezzants was discovered during the excavation.

Etymology

From Old French 'besant', from Latin 'Byzantius' (of Byzantium).
