Hedera

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdərə / (AmE), / ˈhɛdərə / (BrE)
A genus of woody climbing or ground-creeping vines, commonly known as ivies, native to Europe and parts of Asia and North Africa.
genus of ivy plants

Examples

Hedera species are often used as decorative plants in gardens and homes.
English ivy, or Hedera helix, is a popular ground cover for shady areas.
Many old stone buildings in Europe are covered with lush Hedera vines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ivy - a climbing or trailing evergreen plant of the genus Hedera

Etymology

From Latin *hedera* (ivy).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdərə / (AmE), / ˈhiːdərə / (BrE)
a genus of Old World woody climbing or creeping plants, commonly known as ivies
genus of ivy plants

Examples

Many gardens in Europe feature Hedera as a decorative ground cover.
The Hedera species are renowned for their ability to climb walls and trees.
Botanists classify English ivy under the Hedera genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ivy - common name for plants of the genus Hedera

Etymology

From Latin *hedera* (ivy).
