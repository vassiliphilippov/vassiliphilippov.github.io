geometrical

1. ADJ

/ ˌdʒiːəˈmɛtrɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːəˈmɛtrɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to, or determined by the principles and methods of geometry
relating to geometry

Examples

The artist used geometrical shapes to create a sense of order and symmetry.
We learned about geometrical formulas for finding the area of different figures in class today.
The architect's design featured a striking blend of organic and geometrical patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geometric - relating to geometry (alternative spelling)

Etymology

From Latin geometricus, from Greek geōmetrikos (geometrical), from geōmetria (geometry).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02745944-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˌdʒiəˈmɛtrɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːəˈmɛtrɪkəl / (BrE)
having or characterized by simple geometric shapes or forms, especially in design or decoration
relating to geometric shapes or forms

Examples

The rug was decorated with bold geometrical patterns of triangles and rectangles.
She preferred geometrical shapes over ornate designs in her artwork.
The architect used a geometrical approach to create an elegant and modern building.

Synset Examples

a buffalo hide painted with red and black geometric designs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geometric - having a design made from simple shapes such as circles and squares

Etymology

From Greek geometrikos (geometrical), from geo- (earth) + metron (measure).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01988074-s
Synset POS: s
