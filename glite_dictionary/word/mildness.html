mildness

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪld.nəs / (AmE), / ˈmaɪld.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being gentle, lenient, or merciful, especially when showing tolerance toward others' faults or mistakes
gentleness or leniency, especially mercy

Examples

Her mildness in judging others won her universal respect.
The teacher's mildness allowed students to make mistakes without fear of harsh punishment.
He was known for the mildness of his criticism, always giving advice kindly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gentleness - the quality of being gentle or kind
  • Antonym: severity - the quality of being severe or harsh

Etymology

From Middle English mildnesse, from Old English mildnes (gentleness, mercifulness).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪld.nəs / (AmE), / ˈmaɪld.nɪs / (BrE)
weather characterized by comfortable temperatures and lack of extremes.
pleasantly moderate weather

Examples

The mildness of the spring weather was perfect for hiking every afternoon.
After weeks of harsh cold, we were grateful for the unexpected mildness in January.
Tourists flocked to the city, drawn by the mildness of its climate year-round.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gentleness - the quality of being gentle; lack of severity

Etymology

From Latin 'mildis' (mild) plus the noun-forming suffix '-ness.'

3. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪldnəs / (AmE), / ˈmaɪldnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being gentle, moderate, or not severe in manner, temperament, or force.
gentle or moderate quality

Examples

Her mildness in handling criticism won her the respect of her colleagues.
The mildness of the climate makes the region a popular tourist destination.
He approached the difficult conversation with mildness rather than anger.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gentleness - the quality of being kind and tender
  • Antonym: severity - the quality of being severe or harsh

Etymology

From 'mild' + '-ness'; 'mild' from Old English milde (gentle).
