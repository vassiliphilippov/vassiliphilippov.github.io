Felis concolor

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːlɪs ˈkɑn.kə.lɔr / (AmE), / ˈfiːlɪs ˈkɒn.kə.lɔː / (BrE)
The scientific name for the cougar, a large wild cat native to the Americas, resembling a lion in appearance.
scientific name for the cougar (a large American wild cat)

Examples

The scientific name Felis concolor is often used in zoological texts to refer to the mountain lion.
Researchers in South America are monitoring the population of Felis concolor in the Andes.
Unlike many cats, Felis concolor is known for its ability to adapt to diverse habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cougar - large American wild cat with a plain tawny coat
  • Synonym: mountain lion - another common name for the cougar
  • Synonym: puma - alternative name for the cougar

Etymology

From Latin; 'Fēlis' meaning 'cat', 'concolor' meaning 'of uniform color'.
