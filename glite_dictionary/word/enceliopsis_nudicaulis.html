Enceliopsis nudicaulis

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˌsɛliˈɑpsɪs ˌnudɪˈkɔlɪs / (AmE), / ɛnˌsɛliˈɒpsɪs ˌnjuːdɪˈkɔːlɪs / (BrE)
A species of herbaceous plant with a basal cluster of grey-green leaves and tall, leafless stalks, each topped with a single broad yellow flower; found in desert regions from Idaho to Arizona.
species of desert herb with yellow flowers

Examples

Enceliopsis nudicaulis thrives in the arid climates of the southwestern United States.
The broad yellow blooms of Enceliopsis nudicaulis are a striking sight in desert landscapes.
Botanists documented the presence of Enceliopsis nudicaulis during their survey of native plants.

Etymology

From genus name Enceliopsis, formed from Encelia (another plant genus) + Greek opsis (appearance); 'nudicaulis' from Latin nudus (bare) + caulis (stem).
