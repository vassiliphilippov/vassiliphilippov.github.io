Chelone glabra

1. NOUN

/ kəˈloʊni ˈɡleɪbrə / (AmE), / kɪˈləʊni ˈɡleɪbrə / (BrE)
a perennial herb native to marshlands in eastern and central North America, with waxy lance-shaped leaves and white to purple flowers; also called white turtlehead
North American marshland perennial herb

Examples

The Chelone glabra thrives in moist, shaded gardens with rich soil.
Botanists observed that Chelone glabra attracts various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
Because of its unique flowers, Chelone glabra is often used in native plant landscaping.

Etymology

Chelone from Greek 'khelōnē' (turtle), referencing flower shape; 'glabra' Latin for smooth or hairless.
