cook

1. NOUN

/ kʊk / (AmE), / kʊk / (BrE)
a person whose job or role is to prepare and cook food, either professionally or at home
person who prepares food

Examples

The cook prepared a delicious three-course meal for the guests.
As a professional cook, she works in a busy restaurant kitchen.
He is not a trained cook, but his family loves his meals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chef - a professional cook, especially the chief cook in a restaurant

Etymology

From Old English *coc*, from Latin *coquus* (cook).

2. VERB

/ kʊk / (AmE), / kʊk / (BrE)
to prepare or change something by heating, especially food or a mixture
prepare or alter by heating

Examples

The apothecary cooked several herbs together to make a healing potion.
If you cook the sauce too long, it will lose its flavor.
Some recipes require you to cook the mixture over low heat for hours.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bake - to cook something using dry heat, especially in an oven
  • Synonym: boil - to cook something in water or another liquid at its boiling point
  • Antonym: freeze - to preserve or harden something by subjecting it to cold

Etymology

From Old English *cocian*, related to Latin *coquere* (to cook, to prepare by heat).
