Salix alba vitellina

1. NOUN

/ ˈsælɪks ˈælbə vɪtɛˈlaɪnə / (AmE), / ˈsalɪks ˈalba vɪtɛˈliːnə / (BrE)
A European variety of white willow with greyish leaves and yellow-orange twigs, commonly used in basketry.
yellow-twigged European white willow

Examples

The yellow twigs of Salix alba vitellina are highly prized by basket makers.
In early spring, Salix alba vitellina displays striking, colorful branches in the landscape.
Gardeners often plant Salix alba vitellina along riverbanks to prevent erosion.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Salix alba' meaning 'white willow'; 'vitellina' from Latin 'vitellus' (yolk), referring to the yellow color of the twigs.
