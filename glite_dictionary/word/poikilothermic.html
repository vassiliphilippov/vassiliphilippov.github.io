poikilothermic

1. ADJ

/ ˌpɔɪkɪloʊˈθɜrmɪk / (AmE), / ˌpɔɪkɪləʊˈθɜːmɪk / (BrE)
(of animals) having a body temperature that varies with the temperature of the environment; not maintaining a constant internal temperature.
having body temperature that varies with environment
medical

Examples

Reptiles are poikilothermic and rely on external heat sources to regulate their body temperature.
Because amphibians are poikilothermic, their activity level changes with the surrounding temperature.
Unlike mammals, fish are poikilothermic and cannot maintain a steady internal heat.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: homeothermic - (of animals) maintaining a constant body temperature regardless of the environment

Etymology

From Greek 'poikilos' (varied) + 'thermē' (heat).
