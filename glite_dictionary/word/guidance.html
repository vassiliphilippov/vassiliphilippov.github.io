guidance

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡaɪdəns / (AmE), / ˈɡaɪdəns / (BrE)
the act or process of directing or controlling the path of something, especially in order to maintain its course
the act of directing or controlling a course

Examples

The pilot relied on ground guidance to keep the plane on the correct approach path.
Thanks to her guidance, the organization was able to weather the crisis and stay on course.
Satellite guidance systems help missiles maintain their trajectory toward the target.

Synset Examples

a new council was installed under the direction of the king

Related Terms

  • Synonym: direction - the management or control of the actions of something or someone

Etymology

From guide + -ance, patterned after French 'guidance'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡaɪdəns / (AmE), / ˈɡaɪdəns / (BrE)
advice or information aimed at resolving a problem or difficulty, especially as given by someone in authority or with experience.
advice or direction for decisions or actions

Examples

The teacher offered valuable guidance throughout the science project.
Without proper guidance, it can be difficult to make important decisions.
She sought guidance from her mentor when choosing a career path.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advice - recommendation or counsel offered to guide action

Etymology

From guide + -ance, based on French 'guidance'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡaɪdəns / (AmE), / ˈɡaɪdəns / (BrE)
the act of giving direction, advice, or showing someone how to do something or where to go
the act of giving direction or advice

Examples

We relied on her guidance throughout the project to avoid common mistakes.
Teachers are there to offer guidance to students when they need help choosing courses.
The hikers lost their way in the forest until a local villager provided guidance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: direction - advice or instructions on what to do
  • Synonym: assistance - help or support given to someone
  • Antonym: misguidance - the act of giving wrong or misleading guidance

Etymology

From guide + -ance, based on Old French 'guider' (to guide).
