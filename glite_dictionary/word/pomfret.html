pomfret

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːm.frɛt / (AmE), / ˈpɒm.frɪt / (BrE)
a deep-bodied, marine fish of the family Bramidae, found in the Atlantic and Pacific oceans and valued as food
a type of edible marine fish

Examples

Fresh pomfret is a delicacy enjoyed in many coastal cuisines.
The fisherman proudly displayed a large silver pomfret he had caught that morning.
Many markets sell both black and white pomfret as popular choices for grilling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bramidae - the family of fishes known as pomfrets

Etymology

From Portuguese *pampo*, referring to certain fish species.
