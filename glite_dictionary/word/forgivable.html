forgivable

1. ADJ

/ fərˈɡɪvəbl̩ / (AmE), / fəˈɡɪvəbl̩ / (BrE)
Easily excused or forgiven; not serious enough to warrant harsh punishment.
able to be forgiven

Examples

Her mistake was understandable and entirely forgivable under the circumstances.
Many people consider minor rudeness to be forgivable if someone is having a bad day.
The offense was deemed forgivable because no lasting harm was done.

Synset Examples

a venial error

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unforgivable - impossible to excuse or pardon
  • Synonym: venial - easily excused or pardoned, not serious

Etymology

From 'forgive' + '-able', formed in English.
