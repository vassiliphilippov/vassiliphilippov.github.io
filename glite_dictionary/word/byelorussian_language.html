Byelorussian language

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪəloʊˈrʌʃən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌbaɪələˈrʌsɪən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the East Slavic language spoken primarily in Belarus, also known as Belarusian.
Slavic language of Belarus

Examples

Many people in Belarus speak the Byelorussian language at home as well as in schools.
The Byelorussian language has its own unique alphabet based on Cyrillic script.
She took a course to improve her skills in the Byelorussian language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Belarusian language - the official Slavic language of Belarus
  • Degree Higher: Russian language - the Slavic language closely related to Byelorussian, spoken in Russia
  • Degree Higher: Ukrainian language - the East Slavic language closely related to Byelorussian, spoken in Ukraine

Etymology

From Byelorussia (former name of Belarus) + English language.
