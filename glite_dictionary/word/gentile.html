gentile

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛn.taɪl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛn.taɪl / (BrE)
a person who is not a member of a particular religion, especially as used by members of certain faiths such as Mormons and Hindus
person outside a specific religion

Examples

In the Mormon faith, anyone who is not a member of the church may be referred to as a gentile.
Hindus may sometimes use the term gentile for people outside their religion.
The community welcomed both members and gentiles to the festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outsider - a person who does not belong to a specific group or community

Etymology

From Latin gentilis (of the same clan or race, foreigner), from gens (clan, tribe).

3. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛn.taɪl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛn.taɪl / (BrE)
(historical, sometimes archaic) A person who is not Jewish; in some Christian contexts, a Christian as distinguished from Jews.
non-Jewish person; (historically) Christian
archaic

Examples

Historically, early converts were called gentiles to distinguish them from Jewish Christians.
Some church traditions use gentile as a term for non-Jewish believers.
In biblical discussions, Paul is described as the apostle to the gentiles.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Christian - A person who believes in Christianity
  • Antonym: Jew - A person belonging to the Jewish faith or ethnicity

Etymology

From Latin 'gentilis' (of a clan or tribe, heathen), from 'gens' (clan, tribe).
