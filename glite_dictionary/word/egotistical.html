egotistical

1. ADJ

/ ˌiːɡəˈtɪstɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌiːɡəˈtɪstɪk(ə)l / (BrE)
Having an exaggerated sense of self-importance or pride; being excessively self-centered or conceited.
excessively self-centered or conceited

Examples

His egotistical remarks alienated many of his colleagues.
She became increasingly egotistical after her sudden fame and success.
It's hard to work with someone so egotistical that they never listen to others' ideas.

Synset Examples

a conceited fool
an attitude of self-conceited arrogance
an egotistical disregard of others
so swollen by victory that he was unfit for normal duty
growing ever more swollen-headed and arbitrary
vain about her clothes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conceited - having an excessively high opinion of oneself
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest estimate of one’s own importance

Etymology

From ego + -istical, after 'egotist' (from Latin 'ego' meaning 'I').

2. ADJ

/ ˌiɡəˈtɪstɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌiːɡəˈtɪstɪkəl / (BrE)
Having or showing an exaggerated sense of one's own importance or abilities; excessively self-centered.
excessively self-centered or self-important

Examples

Her egotistical attitude made working with her very difficult for the rest of the team.
He was so egotistical that he dismissed anyone else's opinions without a second thought.
The egotistical celebrity was known for constantly boasting about his achievements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-centered - focused only on oneself and one's own needs
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's own importance

Etymology

From 'ego' (Latin for 'I') + '-istical', patterned after 'egoist'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02105974-s
Synset POS: s
