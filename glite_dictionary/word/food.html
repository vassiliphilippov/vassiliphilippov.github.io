food

1. NOUN

/ fuːd / (AmE), / fuːd / (BrE)

mental stimulus for thought

Examples

Her novel provides food for anyone interested in philosophy and the nature of consciousness.
The documentary gave us ample food for discussion during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: food for thought - something that warrants serious consideration or stimulates thinking

2. NOUN

/ fuːd / (AmE), / fuːd / (BrE)
Any solid substance that people or animals eat to provide nourishment and sustain life.
solid substance for nourishment

Examples

The restaurant serves delicious food from around the world.
She packed plenty of food for the long journey.
Healthy food choices are important for children.

Synset Examples

food and drink

Related Terms

  • Antonym: drink - a liquid that can be swallowed as refreshment or nourishment
  • Synonym: meal - an eating occasion made up of food
  • Degree Lower: snack - a small amount of food eaten between meals

Etymology

From Old English 'fōda', related to 'fōdan' (to feed), from Proto-Germanic *fōdō.

3. NOUN

/ fuːd / (AmE), / fuːd / (BrE)
Any substance consumed by animals or humans to provide energy and support growth and tissue repair.
substance for nourishment

Examples

Healthy food is essential for children's growth and development.
She brought enough food to feed the entire team after the game.
The rescue workers distributed food and water to those affected by the flood.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: drink - a liquid that can be swallowed as refreshment or nourishment
  • Synonym: meal - an instance of eating food, especially at a regular time

Etymology

From Old English 'fōda' (food, nourishment), related to 'fodder'.
