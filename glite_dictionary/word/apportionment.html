apportionment

1. NOUN

/ əˈpɔrʃənmənt / (AmE), / əˈpɔːʃənmənt / (BrE)
the act of distributing or allocating something among several people or things, especially according to a plan or set of rules
the act of sharing or allocating something

Examples

The apportionment of resources among the departments was decided at the committee meeting.
Every ten years, census data are used for the apportionment of congressional districts.
The judge oversaw the apportionment of damages between the parties involved in the lawsuit.

Synset Examples

the apportionment of seats in the House of Representatives is based on the relative population of each state

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allocation - the action or process of allocating or distributing something
  • Synonym: distribution - the action of sharing something out among recipients
  • Antonym: concentration - the act of bringing or coming together into a single mass or entity, as the opposite of distributing

Etymology

From the verb 'apportion', formed from Latin 'portionem' (part, share), with the prefix 'ad-' (to).
