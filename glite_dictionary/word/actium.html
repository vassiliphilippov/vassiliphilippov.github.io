Actium

1. PROPN

/ ˈæk.ti.əm / (AmE), / ˈæk.ti.əm / (BrE)
An ancient town on a promontory in western Greece, noted for its historical significance, especially as the site of the Battle of Actium in 31 BC.
ancient town in western Greece

Examples

The Battle of Actium marked a turning point in Roman history.
Archaeologists have uncovered ancient ruins at the site of Actium.
Many tourists visit Actium to learn about its role in Greek and Roman history.

Etymology

From Latin Actium, from Ancient Greek Ἄκτιον (Áktion).

2. PROPN

/ ˈæk.ti.əm / (AmE), / ˈæk.ti.əm / (BrE)
the site of a famous naval battle in 31 BC, where Antony and Cleopatra were defeated by Octavian's fleet under Agrippa
site of a major naval battle in 31 BC

Examples

Historians still debate the tactics used by Octavian at Actium.
The defeat at Actium marked the end of Antony and Cleopatra's power.
He visited the site of Actium during his trip to Greece.

Etymology

From Latin Actium, Greek Aktion, a place name in ancient Greece.
