personification

1. NOUN

/ pɚˌsɑnəfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / pəˌsɒnɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the representation of an abstract quality or idea as if it were a person, especially as a literary device
representation of ideas as people

Examples

The author used personification to give the wind human-like emotions.
In literature, personification can make abstract ideas more relatable to readers.
The phrase 'time waits for no one' is an example of personification.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metaphor - figure of speech in which a word is applied to an object or action to which it is not literally applicable

Etymology

From French 'personnification' and Latin 'persona' (person).

2. NOUN

/ pərˌsɑːnɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / pəˌsɒnɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
The representation of an abstract quality or idea as a person, or the attribution of human characteristics to something nonhuman, especially as a literary device.
attribution of human qualities to nonhuman things

Examples

The author uses personification to give the river a life of its own.
Describing justice as blind is a classic example of personification.
In poetry, personification helps readers connect with abstract concepts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anthropomorphism - attributing human traits or behavior to animals or objects

Etymology

From Late Latin personificatio (the act of personifying), from personificare (to personify), from persona (person) + facere (to make).

3. NOUN

/ pərˌsɑːnəfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / pəˌsɒnɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a person who embodies or represents a particular quality, idea, or characteristic
a person embodying a quality or idea

Examples

She is the very personification of kindness and generosity.
For many, Gandhi was the personification of peace and nonviolence.
The coach was seen as the personification of determination during the championship.

Synset Examples

she is the personification of optimism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embodiment - the tangible or visible form of an idea or quality

Etymology

From Latin 'personificare' (to personify), from 'persona' (person).
