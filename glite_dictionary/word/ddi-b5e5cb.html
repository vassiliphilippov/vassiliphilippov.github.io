ddI

1. NOUN

/ diː diː aɪ / (AmE), / diː diː aɪ / (BrE)
an antiviral drug used to treat HIV infection; a reverse transcriptase inhibitor, also known as didanosine
an antiviral drug for HIV (didanosine)
medical

Examples

Doctors often prescribe ddI in combination with other antiretroviral medications to manage HIV infection.
Some patients experience side effects while taking ddI as part of their treatment regimen.
Research on ddI has contributed significantly to advancements in HIV therapy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: didanosine - another name for ddI, an antiviral drug for HIV

Etymology

Acronym of dideoxyinosine, from 'di-' (two), 'deoxy-' (lacking oxygen), and 'inosine'.
