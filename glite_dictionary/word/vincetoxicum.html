Vincetoxicum

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɪnsəˈtɑksɪkəm / (AmE), / ˌvɪnsɪˈtɒksɪkəm / (BrE)
A genus of chiefly tropical American vines with heart-shaped leaves and large purple or greenish clusters of flowers, once reputed to have antidotal properties.
genus of tropical vines with large flowers

Examples

Several species within the genus Vincetoxicum have been studied for their medicinal properties.
Botanists identified the climbing vine as a member of Vincetoxicum due to its distinctive purple flowers.
Traditional remedies sometimes used Vincetoxicum as an antidote for certain types of poisons.

Etymology

From Latin vincetoxicum, from vincere (to conquer) + toxicum (poison), referring to its supposed antidotal powers.

2. NOUN

/ ˌvɪnsəˈtɑksɪkəm / (AmE), / ˌvɪnsɪˈtɒksɪkəm / (BrE)
A genus of chiefly tropical American vines with heart-shaped leaves and large purple or greenish flowers, formerly believed to have antidotal properties.
genus of tropical vines with supposed antidote powers

Examples

Several species in the Vincetoxicum genus are used in traditional medicine for their supposed healing properties.
The distinctive purple flowers are a hallmark of Vincetoxicum vines growing in the tropics.
Scientists continue to research whether Vincetoxicum possesses any real antidotal effects, as once believed.

Etymology

From Latin 'vincere' (to conquer) and 'toxicum' (poison), referring to reputed antidotal properties.
