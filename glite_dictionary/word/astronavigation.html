astronavigation

1. NOUN

/ ˌæstroʊˌnævɪˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌastrəʊˌnavɪˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of determining position and course by observing and measuring the positions of stars.
navigation using stars

Examples

The ancient sailors practiced astronavigation to cross vast oceans at night.
Modern spacecraft often use astronavigation to determine their positions during long journeys.
He studied astronavigation as part of his training to become an astronaut.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celestial navigation - The process of navigating by observing celestial bodies

Etymology

From astro- (relating to stars) + navigation.
