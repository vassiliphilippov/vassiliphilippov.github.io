picofarad

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɪkəˈfɛræd / (AmE), / ˌpɪkəˈfarad / (BrE)
A unit of capacitance equal to one trillionth (10⁻¹²) of a farad.
unit of capacitance (10⁻¹² farad)

Examples

The circuit requires a capacitor with a value of at least 100 picofarads.
Most ceramic capacitors are rated in picofarads or nanofarads due to their small size.
A picofarad is commonly abbreviated as 'pF' in electrical schematics.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: nanofarad - a unit of capacitance equal to one billionth (10⁻⁹) of a farad
  • Degree Higher: farad - the SI unit of capacitance
  • Degree Lower: femtofarad - a unit of capacitance equal to one quadrillionth (10⁻¹⁵) of a farad

Etymology

From 'pico-' (denoting a factor of 10⁻¹², from Spanish 'pico', meaning 'peak' or 'little') + 'farad' (unit of capacitance).
