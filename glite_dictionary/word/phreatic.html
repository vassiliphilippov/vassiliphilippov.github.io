phreatic

1. ADJ

/ friˈætɪk / (AmE), / frɪˈatɪk / (BrE)
relating to or denoting groundwater, especially underground water in the zone of saturation beneath the earth's surface.
relating to groundwater

Examples

The volcano experienced a phreatic eruption when groundwater came into contact with hot magma.
Scientists measured changes in the phreatic zone to assess the risk of sinkholes.
A phreatic surface marks the upper boundary of groundwater saturation in an aquifer.

Etymology

From Greek phrear, phreatos (well, spring) + -ic.
