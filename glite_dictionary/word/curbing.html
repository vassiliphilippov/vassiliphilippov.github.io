curbing

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɝːbɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɜːbɪŋ / (BrE)
an edge or border made of stones or concrete, forming the boundary between a sidewalk and a roadway
edge between sidewalk and street

Examples

The city replaced the old curbing along Main Street with new granite stones.
He tripped and fell while stepping off the curbing.
Workers repaired the damaged curbing after the accident.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curb - a stone or concrete edging to a street or path

Etymology

From 'curb' + '-ing', denoting the material or structure forming a curb.
