waggery

1. NOUN

/ ˈwæɡ.ər.i / (AmE), / ˈwæɡ.əri / (BrE)
humorous or mischievous behavior, especially of a playful or joking nature
playful or mischievous humor

Examples

His constant waggery made him a favorite among his classmates.
A touch of waggery lightened the otherwise solemn meeting.
She responded to his waggery with a smile, clearly enjoying the playful mood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mischief - playful misbehavior or troublemaking
  • Synonym: jest - a thing said or done for amusement; a joke
  • Antonym: seriousness - the quality of being serious

Etymology

From wag (person fond of making jokes) + -ery (noun-forming suffix).
