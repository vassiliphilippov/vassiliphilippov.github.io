flanker

1. NOUN

/ ˈflæŋkər / (AmE), / ˈflæŋkə / (BrE)
A player, typically a back, who is positioned wide of the line of scrimmage and primarily used as a pass receiver.
wide-positioned pass receiver (sports)

Examples

The coach decided to line up an extra flanker to stretch the defense.
As a flanker, she caught several passes in yesterday's game.
Each flanker waited for the perfect moment to run down the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wide receiver - an offensive player who specializes in catching passes

Etymology

From 'flank' (side) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈflæŋkər / (AmE), / ˈflæŋkə / (BrE)
a soldier assigned to guard the sides (flanks) of a military formation
soldier guarding the sides of a formation

Examples

The commander ordered a flanker to scout the edge of the column for threats.
As a flanker, his job was to spot any enemy movement approaching from the side.
The unit lost one of its flankers during the night march.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guard - a person who protects or watches over something or someone

Etymology

From 'flank' (side of a body or formation) + -er (agent noun suffix).
