vacuolisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌvækjuˌɑləˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvækjuːəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process by which vacuoles form within the cytoplasm of a cell or tissue, often as a result of pathological change.
formation of vacuoles in a cell or tissue
medical

Examples

Extensive vacuolisation of the liver cells was observed under the microscope.
The presence of vacuolisation indicates significant cellular injury.
Abnormal vacuolisation is commonly seen in certain neurodegenerative diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vacuole - a small cavity or sac in the cytoplasm of a cell

Etymology

From vacuole (from Latin 'vacuus', empty) plus -isation.
