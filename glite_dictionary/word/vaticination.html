vaticination

1. NOUN

/ ˌvætɪsɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvatɪsɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of prophesying or foretelling future events, especially as inspired by a divine source
prophecy; act of foretelling the future

Examples

The ancient seer's vaticination warned of disaster if the ritual was not performed.
Her vaticination about political upheaval was dismissed until it came true years later.
The poem is filled with mystical vaticination about the fate of civilizations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prophecy - act of predicting future events, often divinely inspired

Etymology

From Latin 'vaticinatio' (prophecy), from 'vaticinari' (to prophesy).
