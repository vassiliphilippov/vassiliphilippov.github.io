Deuteronomy

1. PROPN

/ ˌduːtəˈrɑːnəmi / (AmE), / ˌdjuːtəˈrɒnəmi / (BrE)
the fifth book of the Old Testament, containing a second statement of the Mosaic law
fifth book of the Old Testament

Examples

Many of the laws in Deuteronomy are reiterations of earlier commandments.
Scholars often debate the historical context in which Deuteronomy was written.
The sermon at church included a reading from the book of Deuteronomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pentateuch - the first five books of the Old Testament

Etymology

From Greek Deuteronomion (second law), via Latin Deuteronomium.
