sapphirine

1. ADJ

/ ˈsæfəˌriːn / (AmE), / ˈsæfɪˌraɪn / (BrE)
having the qualities of or resembling sapphire, especially in color or appearance; made of sapphire.
resembling or made of sapphire

Examples

The vase was adorned with sapphirine crystals that sparkled in the light.
A sapphirine glow filled the sky just before dawn.
She wore a dress of deep, sapphirine blue that caught everyone's attention.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sapphire - a precious blue gemstone

Etymology

From Latin 'sapphīrinus', from 'sapphīrus' (sapphire).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsæfəˌrin / (AmE), / ˈsæfɪˌriːn / (BrE)
A rare light blue or green mineral composed of aluminum and magnesium silicate, found as small grains in certain metamorphic rocks.
a rare light blue or green mineral

Examples

Geologists discovered sapphirine grains scattered throughout the ancient metamorphic rocks.
The vibrant blue-green color of sapphirine makes it a subject of interest for mineral collectors.
Scientists are studying the formation process of sapphirine in high-pressure environments.

Etymology

From 'sapphire' with the suffix '-ine', referring to its sapphire-like color.
