supportable

1. ADJ

/ səˈpɔrtəbəl / (AmE), / səˈpɔːtəbəl / (BrE)
Able to be endured, tolerated, or accepted, especially when unpleasant or difficult.
able to be endured or tolerated

Examples

Despite his criticism, her attitude remained supportable to those around her.
The pain was intense but just supportable enough that she could continue walking.
Working such long hours is only supportable for a short period before exhaustion sets in.

Synset Examples

sufferable punishment

Related Terms

  • Antonym: insupportable - impossible to bear or tolerate
  • Synonym: bearable - able to be endured or tolerated

Etymology

From Old French 'supportable', from Latin 'supportabilis', related to 'supportare' (to bear, endure).
