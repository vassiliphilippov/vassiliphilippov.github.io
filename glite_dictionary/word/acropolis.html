acropolis

1. NOUN

/ əˈkrɑːpəlɪs / (AmE), / əˈkrɒpəlɪs / (BrE)
A citadel or fortified part of an ancient Greek city, typically built on elevated ground.
fortified hilltop in ancient Greek cities

Examples

The most famous acropolis is in Athens, towering above the city.
Every ancient Greek town had an acropolis where residents could seek refuge in times of war.
Archaeologists uncovered new artifacts while excavating the remains of an ancient acropolis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: citadel - a fortress protecting or dominating a city

Etymology

From Greek akropolis, from akron (highest point) + polis (city).
