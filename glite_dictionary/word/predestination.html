predestination

1. NOUN

/ ˌpriːˌdɛstəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːˌdɛstɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
(theology) The doctrine or belief that all events, especially the salvation of individuals, are predetermined by God or fate.
doctrine that events are predetermined, esp. by God

Examples

The debate over predestination has divided theologians for centuries.
Some people find comfort in the idea of predestination, believing their lives follow a divine plan.
Calvin's teachings on predestination became a central tenet of Reformed theology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fate - the development of events beyond a person's control, regarded as predetermined
  • Antonym: free will - the ability to act at one's own discretion without being constrained by fate or divine will

Etymology

From Late Latin praedestinatio, from Latin praedestinare (to determine beforehand).

2. NOUN

/ ˌprɛdˌɛstəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːˌdɛstɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the belief that everything that happens has already been decided by fate or by a divine power and cannot be changed
the idea that events are preordained by fate or God

Examples

Many religious traditions discuss the concept of predestination in relation to human actions and divine will.
He questioned whether his life was governed by chance or by predestination.
In theology class, we studied arguments for and against the doctrine of predestination.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fate - the development of events outside a person's control, regarded as predetermined
  • Antonym: free will - the power of acting without the constraint of necessity or fate; the ability to act at one's own discretion

Etymology

From Late Latin praedestinatio (foreordination), from praedestinare (to predetermine).
