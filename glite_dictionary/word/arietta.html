arietta

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːriˈɛtə / (AmE), / ˌɑːriˈɛtə / (BrE)
A short and simple aria, typically in an opera or vocal composition.
short and simple aria (music)

Examples

The soprano sang an exquisite arietta near the end of the second act.
Mozart composed a charming arietta for the young singer.
The concert included an arietta that captivated the entire audience.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: aria - a long, elaborate solo song in an opera

Etymology

From Italian 'arietta', diminutive of 'aria'.
