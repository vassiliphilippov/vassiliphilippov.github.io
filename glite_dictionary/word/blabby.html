blabby

1. ADJ

/ ˈblæbi / (AmE), / ˈblæbi / (BrE)
talking too much, especially in a way that reveals secrets or lacks discretion
talkative; indiscreet

Examples

His blabby coworker couldn’t keep a secret for more than five minutes.
I tried not to sound blabby during the interview, but I still shared too much.
You can’t trust someone as blabby as that with confidential information.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: talkative - tending to talk a lot
  • Antonym: tight-lipped - unwilling to speak or reveal information; discreet
