gaolbird

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒeɪlˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪlˌbɜːd / (BrE)
An informal term for a criminal who has often been in prison.
habitual prisoner; persistent offender
informal british

Examples

Everyone in town knows he's a gaolbird with a long criminal record.
The novel features a gaolbird trying to start a new life after his release.
After years behind bars, the gaolbird vowed never to return to prison.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jailbird - a habitual criminal or prisoner

Etymology

From 'gaol' (British spelling of 'jail') + 'bird' (alluding to being caged).
