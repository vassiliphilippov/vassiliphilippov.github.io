Pagrus pagrus

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪɡrəs ˈpeɪɡrəs / (AmE), / ˈpæɡrəs ˈpæɡrəs / (BrE)
a species of food fish found in the Mediterranean Sea and Atlantic coasts of Europe and America, also known as the common seabream
a Mediterranean and Atlantic food fish species

Examples

The Mediterranean fishermen caught a large Pagrus pagrus before dawn.
Grilled Pagrus pagrus is considered a delicacy along many coastal towns.
Researchers studied the breeding habits of Pagrus pagrus in the Atlantic.

Etymology

From New Latin binomial nomenclature, with 'Pagrus' as the genus name, from Greek 'págros' (a kind of fish).
