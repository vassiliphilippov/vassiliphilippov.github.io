Arikara

1. NOUN

/ əˈrɪkərə / (AmE), / əˈrɪkərə / (BrE)
a member of a North American Indigenous people who formerly lived in the Dakotas, west of the Missouri River; also, the people collectively.
a Native American people of the Dakotas

Examples

The Arikara played a significant role in the early fur trade of the Great Plains.
She is studying the history and language of the Arikara people.
Many descendants of the Arikara now live in North Dakota.

Etymology

From the Arikara language, self-designation.

2. NOUN

/ əˈrɪkərə / (AmE), / əˈrɪkərə / (BrE)
the language of the Arikara people, belonging to the Caddoan language family and spoken in North Dakota
language of the Arikara people

Examples

Only a few elders continue to speak Arikara fluently in their community.
There are ongoing efforts to revive Arikara among younger generations.
The documentary explored the pronunciation and history of Arikara.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pawnee - the Caddoan language of the Pawnee people
  • Synonym: Caddoan - relating to the family of languages including Arikara and Pawnee

Etymology

From Arikara (the name of the people themselves), adopted into English.
