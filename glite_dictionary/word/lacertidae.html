Lacertidae

1. NOUN

/ ləˈsɜrtɪˌdi / (AmE), / ləˈsɜːtɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of lizards native to the Old World, commonly known as wall lizards or true lizards.
a family of Old World lizards

Examples

Scientists have discovered over 300 species within the Lacertidae family.
Members of Lacertidae are commonly found in Europe, Asia, and Africa.
The common wall lizard belongs to the family Lacertidae.

Etymology

From Latin 'lacerta' (lizard) plus scientific suffix '-idae' (used for animal families).

2. NOUN

/ ləˈsɜrtɪdiː / (AmE), / ləˈsɜːtɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of Old World lizards that includes many of the typical wall lizards found in Europe, Asia, and Africa.
family of Old World lizards

Examples

The Lacertidae are commonly known as wall lizards and are widely distributed across Europe.
Scientists have discovered a new species within the Lacertidae family in North Africa.
Members of the Lacertidae generally have streamlined bodies and long tails.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Latin 'lacerta' (lizard).
