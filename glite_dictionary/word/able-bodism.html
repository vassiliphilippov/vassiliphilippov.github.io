able-bodism

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪbəl bɑːdˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈeɪbl bɒdˌɪzəm / (BrE)
Discrimination in favor of able-bodied people and against those with disabilities.
discrimination favoring the able-bodied

Examples

Many activists are raising awareness about able-bodism in the workplace.
The university took steps to address able-bodism by improving accessibility on campus.
Recognizing able-bodism is crucial when working toward social inclusion for people with disabilities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ableism - discrimination against people with disabilities

Etymology

Formed from 'able-bodied' and the suffix '-ism', after patterns like 'racism'.
