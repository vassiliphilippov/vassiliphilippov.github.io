hedgerow

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdʒˌroʊ / (AmE), / ˈhɛdʒ.rəʊ / (BrE)
a fence or boundary formed by a row of closely planted shrubs or bushes, often found bordering fields or roads
a row of shrubs forming a fence

Examples

The path leads past a thick hedgerow dotted with wildflowers.
Farmers planted a hedgerow to keep their livestock from wandering onto the road.
Sparrows built their nests in the dense hedgerow by the old stone wall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hedge - a fence or boundary formed by dense shrubbery

Etymology

From Middle English 'hegge' (hedge) + 'row' (line), referring to a line of shrubs or bushes forming a boundary.
