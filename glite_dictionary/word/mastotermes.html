Mastotermes

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæstəˈtɜːrmiːz / (AmE), / ˌmɑːstəʊˈtɜːmiːz / (BrE)
A primitive genus of termites, with only one living species, regarded as one of the most ancient groups of termites.
primitive termite genus (mostly extinct)

Examples

The extinct species of Mastotermes provide clues about the evolution of termites.
Mastotermes darwiniensis is the only extant representative of its ancient genus.
Scientists study Mastotermes to understand the primitive characteristics of termites.

Etymology

From Greek 'mastos' (breast) and 'termes' (woodworm, termite).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmæstəˈtɜːrˌmiːz / (AmE), / ˌmastəʊˈtɜːmiːz / (BrE)
a primitive genus of termites, mostly extinct, sometimes regarded as the most primitive group of Isoptera
a primitive genus of termites

Examples

The extinct genus Mastotermes is known for its primitive features among termites.
Fossils of Mastotermes have provided valuable insight into the evolution of social insects.
The only living species of Mastotermes is found in northern Australia.

Etymology

From Greek mastos (breast) + termos (worm), referring to the shape and classification.
