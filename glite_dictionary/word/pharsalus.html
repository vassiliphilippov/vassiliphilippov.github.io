Pharsalus

1. PROPN

/ fɑrˈseɪləs / (AmE), / fɑːˈsɑːləs / (BrE)
an ancient town in Thessaly, Greece, known as the site where Julius Caesar defeated Pompey in 48 BC.
ancient Greek town, site of Caesar's victory over Pompey

Examples

The decisive battle near Pharsalus marked a turning point in Roman history.
Historians often debate the exact location of ancient Pharsalus.
Caesar's victory at Pharsalus ultimately led to the end of the Roman Republic.

Etymology

From Latin Pharsalus, from Ancient Greek Pharsalos (Φάρσαλος), the name of the town.
