flightlessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈflaɪt.lɪs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈflaɪt.ləs.nəs / (BrE)
The state or condition of being unable to fly, especially in animals such as birds.
condition of being unable to fly

Examples

The flightlessness of ostriches has led them to develop strong legs for running.
Scientists study the evolutionary reasons behind the flightlessness of certain island birds.
The unique flightlessness of the kiwi makes it vulnerable to introduced predators.

Synset Examples

The added bonus for the Galapagos cormorant was that there were no predators on the islands to counter the effect of flightlessness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flightless - unable to fly (of a bird, insect, etc.)

Etymology

From 'flightless' + '-ness', where 'flightless' is formed from 'flight' (Old English 'flyht') and the suffix '-less'.
