stove

1. NOUN

/ stoʊv / (AmE), / stəʊv / (BrE)
A device or apparatus used for providing heat, especially for warming a room or cooking.
heating or cooking apparatus

Examples

She turned on the stove to heat up the room during the chilly evening.
Many old cabins still rely on a wood-burning stove for warmth in winter.
The broken stove made it difficult to cook dinner or keep the house warm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heater - a device for generating warmth
  • Synonym: oven - an enclosed compartment for cooking, baking, or heating food

Etymology

From Middle English 'stove', from Middle Dutch or Middle Low German 'stove' (heated room, bath), related to heat.

2. NOUN

/ stoʊv / (AmE), / stəʊv / (BrE)
a kitchen appliance with one or more burners or heating elements used for cooking food
kitchen appliance for cooking food

Examples

She placed the saucepan on the stove and turned on the heat.
The kitchen was warm because the stove had been on all day.
After the storm, we had to use a gas stove to cook our meals.

Synset Examples

dinner was already on the stove

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oven - enclosed compartment for baking or roasting food

Etymology

From Middle English *stove*, meaning a heated room, from Old French and Middle Low German.
