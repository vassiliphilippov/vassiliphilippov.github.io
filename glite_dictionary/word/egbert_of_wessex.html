Egbert of Wessex

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛɡ.bɚt əv ˈwɛs.ɪks / (AmE), / ˈɛɡ.bət əv ˈwɛs.ɪks / (BrE)
(died 839) King of Wessex whose military victories established Wessex as the most powerful kingdom in England.
king of Wessex, died 839

Examples

Egbert of Wessex played a crucial role in uniting several Anglo-Saxon kingdoms under his rule.
Historians often cite Egbert of Wessex as the driving force behind Wessex's rise to dominance.
The reign of Egbert of Wessex marked the beginning of a new era in English history.

Etymology

Egbert: from Old English *Ecgbeorht*, meaning 'bright edge' (of a sword). Wessex: from Old English *Westseaxan*, meaning 'West Saxons'.
