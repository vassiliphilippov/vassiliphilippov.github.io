wraith

1. NOUN

/ reɪθ / (AmE), / reɪθ / (BrE)
A haunting or disturbing image or memory that lingers in the mind.
haunting mental image or memory

Examples

Even years later, the wraith of his childhood trauma haunted his dreams.
She struggled to escape the wraith of regret that followed her everywhere.
The wraith of past failures sometimes clouded his confidence.

Synset Examples

he looked like he had seen a ghost
it aroused specters from his past

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ghost - the spirit of a dead person; apparition
  • Synonym: specter - a ghost or something widely feared as a possible unpleasant or dangerous occurrence

Etymology

Of uncertain origin; possibly from Scots dialect, related to ghosts or apparitions.

2. NOUN

/ reɪθ / (AmE), / reɪθ / (BrE)
A ghost or spirit, especially one that is seen shortly before or after a person's death, often believed to be malevolent or haunting.
a haunting ghost or spirit

Examples

Many villagers claimed to have seen the wraith lurking in the moonlit cemetery.
The story tells of a wraith that haunts the corridors of the abandoned manor.
His face turned pale, as if he had just seen a wraith appear before him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ghost - the spirit of a dead person, especially one that is believed to appear to the living
  • Synonym: phantom - a ghost or figment of the imagination
  • Synonym: specter - a visible incorporeal spirit, especially one of a terrifying nature; ghost

Etymology

Origin uncertain; first recorded in Scots in the early 16th century; possibly related to Scottish Gaelic 'bhraith' (apparition).
