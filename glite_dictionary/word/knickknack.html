knickknack

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɪkˌnæk / (AmE), / ˈnɪk.næk / (BrE)
a small ornament or decorative object, especially one of little value
small decorative object

Examples

Sarah collects knickknacks from every country she visits.
The shelf was cluttered with colorful knickknacks and family photos.
He dusted each knickknack carefully, making sure the display looked perfect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trinket - a small ornament or item of jewelry that is of little value
  • Synonym: bauble - a small, showy trinket or decoration

Etymology

Reduplicative formation likely of imitative origin, first recorded in the 17th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnɪkˌnæk / (AmE), / ˈnɪk.næk / (BrE)
a small decorative object, especially one that is inexpensive and mass-produced
small decorative object

Examples

The mantelpiece was cluttered with knickknacks from all their travels.
She collects knickknacks and displays them on a shelf in her living room.
He gave away most of his old knickknacks before moving into the new apartment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trinket - a small ornament or item of jewelry
  • Synonym: bauble - a small, showy trinket or decoration

Etymology

Reduplication of 'knack' (a trick or small object), likely of imitative origin, first attested in the 17th century.
