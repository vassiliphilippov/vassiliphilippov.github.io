loch

1. NOUN

/ lɑk / (AmE), / lɒx / (BrE)
A lake or a sea inlet, especially in Scotland.
a Scottish lake or sea inlet
british

Examples

We took a boat trip across the beautiful loch in the Highlands.
Nessie is said to live in a Scottish loch.
The mist hovered just above the surface of the loch at dawn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lake - a large body of water surrounded by land

Etymology

From Scottish Gaelic 'loch', ultimately from Old Irish 'loch' (lake, pool).

2. NOUN

/ lɑk / (AmE), / lɒx / (BrE)
A long, narrow inlet of the sea in Scotland, especially one that is nearly surrounded by land.
Scottish sea inlet, almost landlocked

Examples

The famous loch is surrounded by steep hills and ancient forests.
Our boat sailed quietly along the calm waters of the loch.
Many legends have grown around Scotland’s mysterious lochs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fjord - A long, narrow, deep inlet of the sea between high cliffs, similar landform especially in Norway

Etymology

From Scottish Gaelic 'loch' (lake, sea inlet), related to Old Irish 'loch.'
