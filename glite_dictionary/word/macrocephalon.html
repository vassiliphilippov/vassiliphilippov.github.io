Macrocephalon

1. NOUN

/ ˌmækroʊˈsɛfəlɒn / (AmE), / ˌmakrə(ʊ)ˈsɛfəlɒn / (BrE)
A genus of large, black-and-white birds in the family Megapodiidae, commonly known as maleos, native to Sulawesi, Indonesia.
genus of birds; maleos

Examples

The Macrocephalon is notable for its unique nesting behavior in the volcanic soils of Sulawesi.
Researchers observed several Macrocephalon at the wildlife sanctuary during the breeding season.
Conservation efforts are crucial for protecting the natural habitat of the Macrocephalon.

Etymology

From Greek 'makros' (large) + 'kephalē' (head), referring to the bird's prominent head.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmækroʊˈsɛfəlɒn / (AmE), / ˌmakrəʊˈkɛfəlɒn / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the family Megapodiidae, consisting of the maleo, a large bird native to Sulawesi, Indonesia.
genus of birds (maleo) from Sulawesi

Examples

The Macrocephalon is represented by only one living species, the maleo, found in Sulawesi.
Ornithologists have studied the Macrocephalon for its unique egg-laying behavior.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect the habitats of Macrocephalon in Indonesia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maleo - a large, black-and-white bird from Sulawesi known for burying its eggs in sand

Etymology

From Greek makros (large) + kephalē (head), referring to the bird's relatively large head.
