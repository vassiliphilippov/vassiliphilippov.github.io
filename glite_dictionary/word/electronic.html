electronic

1. ADJ

/ ɪˌlɛkˈtrɑnɪk / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛkˈtrɒnɪk / (BrE)
Relating to electrons or involving devices or systems that operate by controlling the flow of electrons, often in scientific or technical contexts.
relating to electrons or their effects

Examples

The experiment measured electronic energy levels within the atom.
Studying electronic transitions is crucial in quantum physics.
The electronic charge distribution determines many properties of molecules.

Synset Examples

electronic energy

Etymology

From electron + -ic, denoting 'related to electrons'.

2. ADJ

/ ɪˌlɛkˈtrɑnɪk / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛkˈtrɒnɪk / (BrE)
relating to devices, systems, or technology that use the principles of electronics, especially involving the control of electrons or electronic circuits.
relating to electronics or electronic devices

Examples

Most modern music is produced using electronic instruments and equipment.
She replaced all her old appliances with electronic versions to save energy.
The company specializes in the development of electronic devices for consumers.

Synset Examples

electronic devices

Related Terms

  • Synonym: digital - relating to information represented by discrete values, often associated with computers
  • Antonym: mechanical - relating to machines or machinery, especially non-electronic ones

Etymology

From electronics + -ic, meaning pertaining to electronics, first used in the mid-20th century.
