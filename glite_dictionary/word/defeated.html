defeated

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfitɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈfiːtɪd / (BrE)
People who have been beaten in a competition, war, or other contest.
people who have been beaten or lost

Examples

After the battle, the defeated were given food and medical care by the victors.
Throughout history, the defeated have often been forced into exile or servitude.
The film explores the perspective of the defeated after a devastating civil war.

Synset Examples

the Romans had no pity for the defeated

Related Terms

  • Antonym: victors - people who win a contest or battle

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈfitɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈfiːtɪd / (BrE)
having failed to achieve success in something, especially in a way that causes disappointment or discouragement
unsuccessful; discouraged

Examples

She felt utterly defeated after her proposal was rejected by the committee.
After several defeated attempts, the team decided to rethink their strategy.
He looked defeated, his confidence shattered by another unexpected failure.

Synset Examples

disappointed expectations and thwarted ambitions
their foiled attempt to capture Calais
many frustrated poets end as pipe-smoking teachers
his best efforts were thwarted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frustrated - feeling distressed or unsatisfied because of unfulfilled goals
  • Antonym: successful - having achieved a desired aim or result

Etymology

From the verb 'defeat', ultimately from Old French 'desfait', past participle of 'desfaire' (undo).

3. ADJ

/ dɪˈfitɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈfiːtɪd / (BrE)
Having been beaten or overcome, especially in a contest, conflict, or competition; not victorious.
having been beaten or overcome

Examples

The army emerged from the battle defeated and demoralized.
She looked defeated after hearing the disappointing news.
The defeated team congratulated their opponents gracefully.

Synset Examples

the defeated enemy

Related Terms

  • Antonym: victorious - having won a victory; triumphant
  • Synonym: beaten - having been defeated or subdued

Etymology

From the verb 'defeat', from Old French 'defait', past participle of 'defaire' (to undo, defeat), from Latin 'disfacere'.

Synset POS: a
