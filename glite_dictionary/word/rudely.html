rudely

1. ADV

/ ˈruːdli / (AmE), / ˈruːdli / (BrE)
In an impolite or disrespectful manner.
in an impolite manner

Examples

He interrupted the speaker rudely during the meeting, which shocked everyone in the room.
The customer spoke rudely to the waiter and demanded special treatment.
She rudely turned her back on him while he was still talking.

Synset Examples

he treated her impolitely

Related Terms

  • Antonym: politely - in a courteous or respectful manner
  • Synonym: impolitely - in an impolite or rude manner

Etymology

From rude + -ly, with rude from Latin rudis (rough, uncivilized).
