Matteuccia

1. PROPN

/ ˌmætiˈuːtʃiə / (AmE), / ˌmætiˈuːtʃɪə / (BrE)
A small genus of deciduous ferns commonly known as ostrich ferns, found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
a genus of ferns (ostrich ferns)

Examples

Matteuccia is widely cultivated for its attractive, feathery foliage in shade gardens.
The most famous species of Matteuccia is commonly called the ostrich fern.
Scientists often debate whether to classify Matteuccia separately from the genus Onoclea.

Etymology

Named after Italian physicist Carlo Matteucci (1811–1868).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmæt.iˈuː.tʃi.ə / (AmE), / ˌmat.iˈuː.tʃi.ə / (BrE)
A small genus of deciduous ferns, sometimes included in the genus Onoclea, and in some classifications placed in the family Polypodiaceae.
genus of deciduous ferns

Examples

The Matteuccia genus includes the widely known ostrich fern.
Matteuccia ferns thrive in moist woodlands and shaded areas.
Botanists often debate whether to classify Matteuccia separately from Onoclea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Onoclea - a related genus of ferns sometimes grouped with Matteuccia

Etymology

Named after Italian physicist Carlo Matteucci (1811–1868).
