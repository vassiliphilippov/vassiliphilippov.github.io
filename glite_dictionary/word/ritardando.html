ritardando

1. ADV

/ ri.tɑrˈdɑn.doʊ / (AmE), / ri.tɑːˈdændəʊ / (BrE)
(music) Gradually slowing down the tempo of a piece or passage.
gradually slowing down in music

Examples

The orchestra responded to the conductor's gesture, bringing the piece to a close with a gentle ritardando.
The final bars were beautifully played ritardando, adding drama to the ending.
Watch carefully for the ritardando indicated before the last chord in the score.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: accelerando - (music) gradually increasing in tempo

Etymology

From Italian 'ritardando', present participle of 'ritardare' (to slow down).
