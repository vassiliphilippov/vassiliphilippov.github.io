triumphal

1. ADJ

/ traɪˈʌmfəl / (AmE), / traɪˈʌmfəl / (BrE)
relating to or celebrating a triumph, especially a victory or great achievement.
relating to or celebrating a triumph

Examples

The city held a triumphal parade to honor the championship team.
A triumphal arch stood at the entrance to the ancient city.
The king made a triumphal return after winning the war.

Synset Examples

a triumphal procession
a triumphal arch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: victorious - having won a victory

Etymology

From Latin triumphalis (pertaining to a triumph).

WordNet ID: oewn-03024925-a

2. ADJ

/ traɪˈʌmfəl / (AmE), / traɪˈʌmfəl / (BrE)
feeling, expressing, or relating to joy and pride resulting from a great victory or success
relating to victory or great success

Examples

The team made a triumphal return home after winning the championship.
The triumphal parade attracted thousands of supporters to the city center.
She gave a triumphal smile as she held up the award.

Synset Examples

rejoicing crowds filled the streets on VJ Day
a triumphal success
a triumphant shout

Related Terms

  • Synonym: triumphant - feeling or expressing joy after victory or success

Etymology

From Latin triumphalis (of or pertaining to triumph), from triumphus (triumph, victory procession).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00708342-s
Synset POS: s
