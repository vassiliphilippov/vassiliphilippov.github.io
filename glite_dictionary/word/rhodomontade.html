rhodomontade

1. NOUN

/ ˌroʊdəˌmɒnˈteɪd / (AmE), / ˌrəʊdəˌmɒnˈtɑːd / (BrE)
boastful or arrogant talk; vain and empty boasting
vain or boastful talk

Examples

The politician's speech was filled with rhodomontade but lacked any real substance.
Tired of his rhodomontade, she asked him to prove his claims.
Literary critics dismissed the memoir as mere rhodomontade rather than honest reflection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bragging - talking about yourself in a way that shows too much pride
  • Antonym: modesty - the quality of being modest and not boasting

Etymology

From French 'rhodomontade', from Italian 'rhodomontata', after Rodomonte, a boastful character in Italian epic poems.
