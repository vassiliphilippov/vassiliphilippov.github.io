adscititious

1. ADJ

/ ˌæd.sɪˈtɪʃ.əs / (AmE), / ˌad.sɪˈtɪʃ.əs / (BrE)
(of an element or quality) added from outside; not intrinsic or inherent to the original.
added or not inherent

Examples

His interest in art was more an adscititious habit than an inherent passion.
The ceremony included several adscititious traditions that were not part of the original ritual.
She regarded the decorations as adscititious rather than essential to the building's beauty.

Synset Examples

an adscititious habit rather than an inherent taste

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extrinsic - not part of the essential nature; coming from outside
  • Antonym: inherent - existing as a permanent, essential, or characteristic attribute

Etymology

From Latin adscititius (borrowed, assumed), from adsciscere (to admit, adopt).

2. ADJ

/ ædˌsɪˈtɪʃəs / (AmE), / adˌsɪˈtɪʃ.əs / (BrE)
Supplemental; added from an outside source rather than belonging naturally to something.
supplemental; added from outside

Examples

The scientist included several adscititious references in his report to strengthen his argument.
The cathedral features some adscititious chapels built long after the original structure was completed.
Her explanation contained adscititious details that, while interesting, were not crucial to understanding the story.

Synset Examples

adscititious vowels

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supplementary - providing something additional to what is already present
  • Antonym: essential - forming a necessary or intrinsic part of something

Etymology

From Latin adscititius (added, foreign), from adsciscere (to adopt, admit).
