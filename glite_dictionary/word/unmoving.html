unmoving

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈmuːvɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈmuːvɪŋ / (BrE)
not producing any emotional response; failing to inspire feelings of sympathy, excitement, or interest.
not causing emotion

Examples

Although the film was beautifully shot, its story was unmoving and left audiences indifferent.
Her speech was disappointingly unmoving, failing to connect with the audience.
The judge's unmoving response suggested he was unmoved by the defendant's pleas.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: moving - causing strong feelings of emotion

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈmuːvɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈmuːvɪŋ / (BrE)
not moving; without motion or activity.
not moving; motionless

Examples

The statue stood unmoving in the corner of the garden.
She remained unmoving as the argument continued around her.
The lizard stayed unmoving to avoid attracting attention from predators.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motionless - not moving at all
  • Synonym: still - not moving or making a sound
  • Antonym: moving - being in motion

Etymology

From 'un-' (not) + 'moving' (present participle of move).
