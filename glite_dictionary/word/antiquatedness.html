antiquatedness

1. NOUN

/ ænˈtɪkweɪtɪdnəs / (AmE), / ænˈtɪkweɪtɪdnəs / (BrE)
The state or quality of being outdated, old-fashioned, or no longer in use.
the state of being outdated or old-fashioned

Examples

Many critics argue that the antiquatedness of the policy makes it unsuitable for today's society.
The antiquatedness of the building was evident in its architecture and outdated facilities.
He was frustrated by the antiquatedness of the procedures still used in the office.

Synset Examples

Where these customs still exist they are practically symbolic for the antiquatedness of religion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obsolescence - the process of becoming obsolete or outdated
  • Antonym: modernity - the quality or condition of being modern

Etymology

From 'antiquated' + '-ness', formed from Latin 'antiquatus' (made old).
