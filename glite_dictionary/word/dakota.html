Dakota

1. NOUN

/ dəˈkoʊdə / (AmE), / dəˈkəʊtə / (BrE)
the collective area formed by the states of North Dakota and South Dakota in the United States
region comprising North and South Dakota

Examples

The prairies of Dakota stretch for miles under the open sky.
Many settlers moved to Dakota during the westward expansion in the 19th century.
Bison once roamed freely across all of Dakota.

Etymology

From the Dakota people, a Native American tribe, whose name derives from their own word for 'allies'.

2. NOUN

/ dəˈkoʊtə / (AmE), / dəˈkəʊtə / (BrE)
the Siouan language spoken by the Dakota people, primarily in the upper Midwest region of the United States
Siouan language of the Dakota people

Examples

He is studying the Dakota language at university to preserve his cultural heritage.
Linguists have published a comprehensive dictionary of Dakota.
Many children are now learning Dakota in schools thanks to revitalization efforts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sioux - the language and group of dialects spoken by the Sioux people

Etymology

From Dakota, the self-designation of the Dakota people, meaning 'friend' or 'ally' in their language.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ dəˈkoʊdə / (AmE), / dəˈkəʊtə / (BrE)
a member of a Siouan Native American people from the northern Mississippi valley, also called the Sioux
member of a Siouan Native American people

Examples

He is a proud Dakota who teaches the history of his people to young students.
Many Dakota traditions have been preserved through storytelling and ceremonies.
The Dakota fought to maintain their ancestral lands in the nineteenth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sioux - a group of Native American peoples including the Dakota, Lakota, and Nakota

Etymology

From the Dakota language Dakhóta (name for themselves, meaning 'allies' or 'friends').
