specialist

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɛʃəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃəlɪst / (BrE)
a doctor who concentrates on a particular branch of medicine, rather than general practice
doctor focused on a specific medical field

Examples

After seeing her family doctor, she was referred to a specialist for further tests.
The hospital has a renowned heart specialist on staff.
Many patients travel long distances to consult a specialist in rare diseases.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: generalist - a doctor who does not specialize, such as a general practitioner (GP)

Etymology

From Latin 'specialis' (of a particular kind).

2. NOUN

/ ˈspɛʃəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃ(ə)lɪst / (BrE)
a person who has expert knowledge or skill in a particular area of work or study
person with expertise in a specific field

Examples

The hospital referred him to a specialist for further treatment.
As a specialist in marine biology, she often conducts ocean research.
Many companies hire a specialist to handle their IT security needs.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: generalist - a person competent in several fields, not specialized
  • Synonym: expert - a person with a high level of knowledge or skill in a particular area

Etymology

From Latin 'specialis' (particular), from 'species' (appearance, form, kind).
