scenario

1. NOUN

/ səˈnɛrioʊ / (AmE), / sɪˈnɑːriəʊ / (BrE)
the setting or background for a work of art, literature, or drama
setting or background for an artistic work

Examples

The scenario of the novel is a small coastal village in the 19th century.
In the film, the futuristic scenario creates a sense of suspense and intrigue.
The artist chose an urban scenario as the backdrop for her latest series of paintings.

Synset Examples

the scenario is France during the Reign of Terror

Related Terms

  • Synonym: setting - the time and place in which a story takes place

Etymology

From Italian 'scenario' (scene), from Latin 'scaenarium' (theatrical stage).

2. NOUN

/ səˈnɛrioʊ, siˈnæriˌoʊ / (AmE), / sɪˈnɑːrɪəʊ, səˈnɑːrɪəʊ / (BrE)
an outline or synopsis of a play, film, or literary work, or a sequence of possible events in a particular situation
outline of a play or sequence of events

Examples

The director presented a scenario that captured the main events of the play.
Before writing the script, she carefully outlined the scenario for each scene.
The novel’s scenario was summarized in two pages for the publisher.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: synopsis - a brief summary of the plot of a novel, film, etc.

Etymology

From Italian 'scenario', from 'scena' (scene), ultimately from Latin 'scaena'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07025251-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ səˈnɛrioʊ / (AmE), / sɪˈnɑːriəʊ / (BrE)
A suggested or imagined sequence of possible events, especially used in planning or analysis.
possible sequence of events

Examples

The committee discussed every scenario to ensure they were prepared for any outcome.
In the worst-case scenario, the company would have to close several branches.
Analysts are modeling different scenarios for how the market could recover next year.

Synset Examples

planners developed several scenarios in case of an attack

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plan - a detailed proposal for doing or achieving something
  • Synonym: possibility - a thing that may happen or be the case

Etymology

From Italian 'scenario' (scene, setting), from Latin 'scaenarium' (theatrical stage).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06768822-n
Synset POS: n
