clubhead

1. NOUN

/ ˈklʌbˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈklʌbˌhɛd / (BrE)
the heavy, rounded part at the end of a golf club that strikes the ball
head of a golf club

Examples

The clubhead made a sharp sound as it connected with the golf ball.
Modern clubheads are often made from titanium to increase strength and reduce weight.
He noticed that the clubhead was misaligned, which affected his swing accuracy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clubface - the striking surface of a golf club's head
