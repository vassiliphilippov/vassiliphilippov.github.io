Vepse

1. NOUN

/ ˈvepsə / (AmE), / ˈvepsə / (BrE)
a Finnic language spoken by the Veps people, primarily in northwestern Russia
Finnic language of the Veps people

Examples

Only a small number of people still speak Vepse as their first language.
She is studying Vepse to document endangered Uralic languages.
Conference attendees discussed the revitalization efforts of Vepse in local schools.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Veps - a member of the Veps people, an ethnic group in Russia

Etymology

From Russian Вепсский (Vepsskiy), referring to the Veps people and their language.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɛp.seɪ / (AmE), / ˈvɛp.seɪ / (BrE)
a member of a small Finno-Ugric ethnic group native to northwestern Russia
member of a Finno-Ugric people of Russia

Examples

The Vepse have preserved many traditional customs despite outside influences.
A Vepse community still resides by Lake Ladoga in Russia.
She is proud of her Vepse heritage and speaks the language fluently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Vepsian - the Finno-Ugric language spoken by the Vepse people

Etymology

From Russian Вепс (Veps), of Finno-Ugric origin.
