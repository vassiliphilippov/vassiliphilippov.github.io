rachitis

1. NOUN

/ rəˈkaɪtɪs / (AmE), / rəˈkaɪtɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of the vertebral column, especially as a disease affecting the spine
inflammation of the spinal column
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with rachitis after experiencing severe pain in his spinal region.
Chronic rachitis can lead to deformities of the vertebral column if left untreated.
Specialists are researching the causes and treatments of rachitis in children.

Etymology

From Greek 'rhachis' (spine) + '-itis' (inflammation).

2. NOUN

/ ræˈkaɪtɪs / (AmE), / rəˈkaɪtɪs / (BrE)
A childhood disease caused by vitamin D deficiency, resulting in impaired calcium and phosphorus metabolism and softening of the bones; also known as rickets.
disease from vitamin D deficiency in children
medical

Examples

Poor nutrition and limited exposure to sunlight can lead to rachitis in young children.
Medical studies have shown that rachitis is preventable with adequate vitamin D intake.
Physicians diagnosed the patient with rachitis after observing bone deformities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rickets - softening and weakening of bones in children, usually due to vitamin D deficiency

Etymology

From Greek 'rhakhís' (spine) via Late Latin 'rachītis'.
