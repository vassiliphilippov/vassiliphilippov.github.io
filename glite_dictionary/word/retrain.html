to retrain

1. VERB

/ riˈtreɪn / (AmE), / riːˈtreɪn / (BrE)
to learn new skills or undergo training again, especially in order to change jobs or adapt to new circumstances
to train again or learn new skills

Examples

After losing her job in retail, she decided to retrain as a nurse.
Many workers have to retrain to keep up with changing technology.
He is considering whether to retrain for a career in teaching.

Synset Examples

He is retraining to become an IT worker

Related Terms

  • Synonym: train - to teach or be taught a skill or type of behavior through practice and instruction

Etymology

From prefix re- (again) + train.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00604574-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˌriˈtreɪn / (AmE), / ˌriːˈtreɪn / (BrE)
to learn or to teach someone new skills, especially so they can do a different job
to learn/teach new job skills

Examples

After losing her job, Maria decided to retrain as a computer programmer.
The company offered to retrain staff so they could work with new technologies.
Workers may need to retrain several times during their careers as industries change.

Synset Examples

We must retrain the linguists who cannot find employment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: train - to teach or be taught skills for a job or activity

Etymology

From re- (again) + train.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00605217-v
Synset POS: v
