synchroscope

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪŋk.roʊˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋ.krəˌskəʊp / (BrE)
an instrument used to determine whether two periodic motions, such as rotating shafts or propellers, are synchronized, especially in multi-engine aircraft
instrument for checking synchronization of motions

Examples

The copilot carefully monitored the synchroscope to ensure both engines were running at the same speed.
A faulty synchroscope can lead to unbalanced propellers and decrease flight efficiency.
Engineers used a synchroscope to match the generators before connecting them to the power grid.

Etymology

From Greek *syn* (together) + *chronos* (time) + English *-scope* (instrument for viewing or observing).
