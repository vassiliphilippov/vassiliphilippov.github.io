to de-iodinate

1. VERB

/ diˈaɪ.ə.dɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / diˈaɪ.ə.dɪˌneɪt / (BrE)
to remove iodine from (a compound or substance)
remove iodine from a substance
medical

Examples

The laboratory technician was asked to de-iodinate the sample before analysis.
Some enzymes have the ability to de-iodinate thyroid hormones in the body.
Researchers attempted to de-iodinate the compound to study its properties without iodine.

Synset Examples

de-iodinate the thyroxine

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to iodinate - to combine or treat with iodine

Etymology

From prefix 'de-' (removal) and 'iodinate' (to combine with iodine).
