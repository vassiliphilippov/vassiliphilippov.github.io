existent

1. ADJ

/ ɪɡˈzɪstənt / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɪstənt / (BrE)
having actual existence; real or currently existing.
having actual existence

Examples

The museum is dedicated to the preservation of existent species.
They decided to upgrade the existent infrastructure rather than build new facilities.
Only a few existent copies of the manuscript have survived.

Synset Examples

an attempt to refine the existent machinery to make it more efficient
much of the beluga caviar existing in the world is found in the Soviet Union and Iran

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nonexistent - not existing; not real or present
  • Synonym: existing - currently in existence; present or alive

Etymology

From Latin 'existens', present participle of 'existere' (to exist).

2. ADJ

/ ɪɡˈzɪstənt / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɪstənt / (BrE)
having real and actual being; currently existing in fact and not just imagined or possible
currently existing; real, not potential

Examples

Some people still believe that dragons are existent somewhere in the world.
All existent species on the island have adapted to its harsh climate.
He distinguished between existent threats and those merely hypothetical in his report.

Synset Examples

the predicted temperature and the actual temperature were markedly different
actual and imagined conditions

Related Terms

  • Antonym: existent - not currently existing; imaginary

Etymology

From Latin 'exsistere' (to exist, to stand forth), via Late Latin 'existentem' (present participle of 'exsistere').
