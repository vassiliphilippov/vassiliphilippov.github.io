cutis

1. NOUN

/ ˈkjuːtɪs / (AmE), / ˈkjuːtɪs / (BrE)
the layer of skin that covers the body, especially in medical or anatomical contexts
anatomical term for skin
medical

Examples

The surgeon examined the patient's cutis for signs of infection.
Inflammation of the cutis can lead to discomfort and other complications.
Certain medications may cause thinning of the cutis over time.

Synset Examples

your skin is the largest organ of your body

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skin - the soft outer covering of a vertebrate animal

Etymology

From Latin *cutis* (skin).
