to childproof

1. VERB

/ ˈtʃaɪldˌpruf / (AmE), / ˈtʃʌɪldˌpruːf / (BrE)
to make (a place or object) safe so that children cannot be harmed or cause damage
make safe for children

Examples

We hired a professional to childproof our new house before the baby arrived.
It's important to childproof cabinets and drawers to prevent accidents.
They spent the weekend childproofing the apartment for their toddler.

Synset Examples

childproof the apartment

Etymology

Formed from 'child' + 'proof', modeled after other compounds such as 'waterproof'.
