defined

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈfaɪnd / (AmE), / dɪˈfaɪnd / (BrE)
having clear and distinct outlines, edges, or boundaries; sharply marked or delineated
sharply marked in outline or boundary

Examples

In the early morning light, the mountains were defined against a pale pink sky.
Her jawline is very defined due to her fitness routine.
The artist used shading to make the shapes more defined in the drawing.

Synset Examples

hills defined against the evening sky
the setting sun showed the outlined figure of a man standing on the hill

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indistinct - not clearly marked or defined; blurred
  • Synonym: sharp - clearly distinct or delineated

Etymology

Past participle of 'define', from Latin 'definire' (to set bounds, limit).

Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈfaɪnd / (AmE), / dɪˈfaɪnd / (BrE)
clearly marked, outlined, or characterized; having distinct or definite limits or boundaries.
clearly marked or characterized

Examples

The contract has very defined terms for both parties.
She prefers eyebrows that look fuller and more defined.
His role within the team is clearly defined.

Synset Examples

lost in a maze of words both defined and undefined
each child has clearly defined duties

Related Terms

  • Antonym: undefined - not clearly or precisely characterized
  • Synonym: distinct - clearly separate and different

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'define', from Latin 'definire' (to set bounds, limit, define).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00700633-a
Synset POS: a
