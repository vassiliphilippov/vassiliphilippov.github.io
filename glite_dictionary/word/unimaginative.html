unimaginative

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.ɪˈmædʒ.ə.nə.tɪv / (AmE), / ˌʌn.ɪˈmædʒ.ɪ.nə.tɪv / (BrE)
lacking originality or creativity; not showing imagination or inventiveness.
lacking creativity or originality

Examples

The movie was criticized for its unimaginative storyline and predictable plot twists.
Her solution to the problem was practical but rather unimaginative.
He found the décor dull and unimaginative, lacking any sense of creativity.

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnɪˈmædʒɪnətɪv / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪˈmadʒɪnətɪv / (BrE)
not showing creativity or originality; dealing only with concrete facts or lacking imagination.
lacking creativity or imagination

Examples

The report was detailed but completely unimaginative, lacking any new perspectives.
His approach to solving problems is rigid and unimaginative.
Many critics felt the movie's plot was unimaginative and predictable.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: creative - having the ability to create, especially in an imaginative way
  • Antonym: original - new and different in a way that is not derived from something else; imaginative

Etymology

From 'un-' (not) + 'imaginative', based on Latin 'imaginari' (to picture to oneself).

3. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.ɪˈmædʒ.ɪ.nə.tɪv / (AmE), / ˌʌn.ɪˈmadʒ.ɪ.nə.tɪv / (BrE)
not able to think of new or interesting ideas; lacking originality or creativity.
lacking originality or creativity

Examples

The film's plot was criticized for being unimaginative and predictable.
She found the décor rather unimaginative, lacking any sense of style or flair.
His solutions to the problem were practical but somewhat unimaginative.

Synset Examples

stereotyped phrases of condolence
even his profanity was unimaginative

Related Terms

  • Antonym: creative - able to create new or original ideas
  • Antonym: original - new and different from anything that has been done before
  • Synonym: dull - lacking interest or excitement

Etymology

From the prefix un- (not) + imaginative, based on Latin 'imaginatio' (imagination).
