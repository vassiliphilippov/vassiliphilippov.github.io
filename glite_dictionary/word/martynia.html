Martynia

1. NOUN

/ mɑrˈtɪniə / (AmE), / mɑːˈtɪniə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants, sometimes known as unicorn plants, native to tropical or subtropical regions.
a plant genus known as unicorn plants

Examples

The Martynia genus is notable for its unusual hooked seed pods.
Some gardeners cultivate Martynia for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
Botanists have studied Martynia species for their adaptations to dry habitats.

Etymology

Named after the English botanist John Martyn (1699–1768).
