party

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːr.t̬i / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.ti / (BrE)
an event where people gather for socializing, entertainment, or celebration, often involving food, drinks, and activities
social gathering or celebration

Examples

We threw a big party for her graduation last weekend.
He was invited to a Halloween party at his friend's house.
The office party featured music, games, and a lot of delicious food.

Synset Examples

he planned a party to celebrate Bastille Day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celebration - a special occasion to honor or mark an event

Etymology

From Old French *partie* (division, group), from Latin *partita*, the noun use evolved to refer to groups of people gathering for specific purposes.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑrti / (AmE), / ˈpɑːti / (BrE)
a person or group involved in a legal proceeding as a participant, such as a plaintiff or defendant
person or group in a legal case
legal

Examples

Each party must submit evidence to support their case.

The party of the second part agrees to the conditions stated above.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: litigant - a person involved in a lawsuit
  • Degree Lower: plaintiff - the party who initiates a lawsuit
  • Degree Lower: defendant - the party against whom a lawsuit is filed

Etymology

From Old French partie (part, party), from Latin partīta, from pars (part).

5. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːrti / (AmE), / ˈpɑːti / (BrE)
an organized group of people with shared political aims or opinions, seeking to influence or control government policy, typically by contesting elections
political organization or group

Examples

He joined a new party that promised to fight corruption in the government.
The two main parties debated heavily before the election.
Forming a third party is often challenging in a system dominated by two major ones.

Synset Examples

in 1992 Perot tried to organize a third party at the national level

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faction - a small organized dissenting group within a larger one, especially in politics
  • Synonym: coalition - a temporary alliance of political parties forming a government or of states
  • Antonym: government - the group of people with the authority to govern a country or state

Etymology

From Latin 'partita', past participle of 'partire' (to divide), influenced by Old French 'partie' (part, faction).
