catoptric

1. ADJ

/ kəˈtɑptrɪk / (AmE), / kəˈtɒptrɪk / (BrE)
Relating to mirrors or the study of the reflection of light by mirrors.
relating to mirrors or mirror reflection

Examples

The telescope used a catoptric system to reflect light and form images.
Ancient Greeks studied catoptric phenomena to understand how mirrors produce images.
He specialized in catoptric science, designing devices that rely on precise mirror arrangements.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dioptric - relating to the refraction of light, especially by lenses

Etymology

From Greek katoptrikos, from katoptron (mirror), from kata (down) + optein (to see).
