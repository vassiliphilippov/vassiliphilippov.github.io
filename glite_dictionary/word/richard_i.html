Richard I

1. PROPN

/ ˈrɪtʃərd ðə ˈfɜrst / (AmE), / ˈrɪtʃəd ðə ˈfɜːst / (BrE)
(1157–1199) King of England from 1189 to 1199, famous for his leadership during the Third Crusade and known as Richard the Lionheart.
King of England (1157–1199), led the Third Crusade

Examples

Richard I was celebrated for his bravery and military leadership during the Crusades.
Many legends have grown around the exploits of Richard I, both in England and abroad.
On his return from the Holy Land, Richard I was captured and held for ransom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Richard the Lionheart - famous epithet of Richard I, emphasizing his bravery
