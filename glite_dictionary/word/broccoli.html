broccoli

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɑːkəli / (AmE), / ˈbrɒkəli / (BrE)
A vegetable with branched green stems and dense clusters of undeveloped flower buds, eaten as food.
edible green vegetable with flower buds

Examples

She steamed the broccoli and served it with a sprinkle of lemon juice.
Children are often encouraged to eat more broccoli because it's healthy.
I added chopped broccoli to the pasta for extra nutrition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cauliflower - a variety of cabbage with a large white flower head

Etymology

From Italian 'broccolo', meaning 'cabbage sprout', from 'brocco' (shoot, sprout).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbrɑːkəli / (AmE), / ˈbrɒkəli / (BrE)
A vegetable plant with dense clusters of small, tight green flower buds, typically eaten as a vegetable.
a green vegetable with tight flower buds

Examples

She steamed some broccoli for dinner.
The kids refused to eat their broccoli, even with cheese sauce.
Fresh broccoli is rich in vitamins and minerals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cauliflower - a white vegetable with a compact head of undeveloped flowers

Etymology

From Italian 'broccoli', plural of 'broccolo' (cabbage sprout, head), from 'brocco' (shoot, arm).
