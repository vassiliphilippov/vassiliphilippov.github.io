to unseat

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈsit / (AmE), / ʌnˈsiːt / (BrE)
to cause someone to fall from their seat, especially from a horse or vehicle
cause to fall from a seat, especially a horse

Examples

The wild horse unseated its rider within seconds of leaving the stable.
A sudden bump in the road nearly unseated the cyclist.
The rough waters threatened to unseat passengers from the small boat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dismount - to get off a horse or bicycle

Etymology

From un- (prefix meaning 'not' or 'reverse') + seat (noun).

WordNet ID: oewn-01547227-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ʌnˈsiːt / (AmE), / ʌnˈsiːt / (BrE)
to remove someone from a position of power, especially political office, often by winning an election against them or through other legal means
remove from office or position

Examples

The opposition party is determined to unseat the current president in the next election.
A corruption scandal could potentially unseat several senior officials.
Voters hoped their voices would unseat the unpopular mayor.

Synset Examples

The Republicans are trying to unseat the liberal Democrat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to oust - to remove someone from a position or place, especially through force or authority
  • Antonym: to install - to place someone in a position of power or office

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (reverse, remove) + 'seat' (used figuratively for office or position).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02396792-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ʌnˈsit / (AmE), / ʌnˈsiːt / (BrE)
to remove someone from a position of authority or power, especially a political office
remove from position or office

Examples

The opposition party hopes to unseat the incumbent governor in the upcoming election.
A major scandal was enough to unseat several longtime members of parliament.
Their campaign strategy is designed specifically to unseat political incumbents.

Synset Examples

His surprising speech could unseat a few incumbents.
Activists are working hard to unseat the current leadership.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to depose - to remove someone from office suddenly and forcefully
  • Antonym: to install - to place someone in a position of office or authority

Etymology

From un- (expressing removal) + seat (place of authority or office).
