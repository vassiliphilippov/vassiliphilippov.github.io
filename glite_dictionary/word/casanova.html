Casanova

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæsəˈnoʊvə / (AmE), / ˌkæsəˈnəʊvə / (BrE)
a man who is known for having many romantic or sexual relationships with women
man noted for many love affairs

Examples

He had a reputation as a Casanova in his younger years.
Everyone at the office knew him to be a bit of a Casanova.
Don't trust him with your heart; he's a real Casanova.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: womanizer - a man who often engages in casual sexual relationships with women
  • Synonym: philanderer - a man who frequently has affairs with women

Etymology

From the name of Giacomo Casanova (1725–1798), an Italian adventurer and author famous for his romantic exploits.

2. PROPN

/ ˌkæzəˈnoʊvə / (AmE), / ˌkæsəˈnəʊvə / (BrE)
An Italian adventurer, Giacomo Casanova (1725–1798), famous for his elaborate memoirs detailing his romantic and sexual escapades.
Italian adventurer and memoirist

Examples

Many biographies have been written about Casanova and his adventures across Europe.
The name Casanova has become synonymous with romantic escapades and charm.
Historians study Casanova both for his literary works and his colorful personal life.

Etymology

From the name of Giacomo Casanova, an Italian adventurer and writer.
