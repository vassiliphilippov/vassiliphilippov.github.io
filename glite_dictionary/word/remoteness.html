remoteness

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈmoʊtnəs / (AmE), / rɪˈməʊtnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being distant and unsympathetic in manner or attitude; aloofness
aloof or unsympathetic manner

Examples

Despite his brilliance, his remoteness made it difficult for colleagues to approach him.
Her remoteness during the meeting created an uncomfortable atmosphere among the team.
There was a certain remoteness in his manner that kept others at a distance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aloofness - the quality of being emotionally distant or detached
  • Antonym: friendliness - the quality of being friendly and approachable

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈmoʊtnəs / (AmE), / rɪˈməʊtnəs / (BrE)
The state or quality of being far away in distance, time, or relation; the condition of being remote.
state of being remote or distant

Examples

The remoteness of the mountain village makes it difficult to access in winter.
She felt a certain remoteness from her old friends after moving abroad.
The judge considered the remoteness of the defendant's actions when making his decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: distance - Amount of space between two points; separation in space
  • Antonym: closeness - State of being near in space, time, or relation; opposite of remoteness

Etymology

From 'remote' + suffix '-ness'. 'Remote' from Latin remotus, past participle of removere (remove).
