teg

1. NOUN

/ tɛɡ / (AmE), / tɛɡ / (BrE)
A sheep in its second year or before its first shearing.
two-year-old sheep

Examples

The farmer separated the tegs from the rest of the flock for special feeding.
After their first winter, the tegs were nearly ready for shearing.
At market, young tegs often fetched a good price for their tender meat.

Etymology

Probably from Old Norse *teg* (brood, litter).
