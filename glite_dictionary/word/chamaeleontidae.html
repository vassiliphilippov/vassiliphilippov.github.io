Chamaeleontidae

1. NOUN

/ kəˌmiːliˈɒntɪdiː / (AmE), / kəˌmiːliˈɒntɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of reptiles comprising the Old World chameleons, sometimes regarded as a superfamily in some classifications.
family of chameleons (Old World)

Examples

The family Chamaeleontidae includes a wide variety of chameleon species found across Africa, Europe, and Asia.
A recent study on Chamaeleontidae revealed unique adaptations in their vision and camouflage abilities.
Researchers debated whether Chamaeleontidae should be classified as a family or as a superfamily within Sauria.

Etymology

From scientific Latin, based on Greek 'khamaileōn' (chameleon) and '-idae' (family suffix).
