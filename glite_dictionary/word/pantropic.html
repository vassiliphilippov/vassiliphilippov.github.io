pantropic

1. ADJ

/ pænˈtrɑːpɪk / (AmE), / pænˈtrɒpɪk / (BrE)
(of an organism or phenomenon) distributed throughout the tropical regions of the world.
distributed throughout the tropics

Examples

The pantropic mosquito species are capable of transmitting diseases across several continents.
This plant is considered pantropic due to its presence in tropical habitats worldwide.
Scientists studied a pantropic virus that infects animals throughout the Earth's tropical zones.

Etymology

From Greek 'pan' (all) + 'tropikos' (of the tropics).
