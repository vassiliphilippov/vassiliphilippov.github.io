Commiphora myrrha

1. NOUN

/ kəˌmɪfərə ˈmɜrə / (AmE), / kəˌmɪfərə ˈmɜːrə / (BrE)
A tree native to eastern Africa and Asia, known for producing myrrh, an aromatic resin used in perfumes, incense, and traditional medicine.
tree producing myrrh resin

Examples

The resin from the Commiphora myrrha tree has been prized since ancient times for its fragrance.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of Commiphora myrrha in traditional herbal remedies.
Commiphora myrrha grows in arid regions and produces the valuable substance known as myrrh.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: myrrh - an aromatic resin obtained from certain trees, used in incense and perfumes

Etymology

From Latin Commiphora (genus name), from Greek kommi (gum) + phoros (bearing); myrrha from Greek myrrha (myrrh).
