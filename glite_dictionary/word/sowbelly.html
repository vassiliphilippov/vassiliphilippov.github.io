sowbelly

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊˌbɛli / (AmE), / ˈsəʊˌbɛli / (BrE)
Salt pork cut from the belly of a pig, often used as a staple food in traditional American cooking.
salt pork from a pig's belly
american

Examples

The pioneers relied on sowbelly and beans as their main source of food during long journeys.
Grandpa fried up some sowbelly for breakfast with eggs and biscuits.
She added chopped sowbelly to flavor the stew while it simmered.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salt pork - salted pork, especially from the belly, back, or sides of a pig

Etymology

From 'sow' (adult female pig) + 'belly'; refers to the cut of pork from a pig's underside.
