ironmonger

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪərnˌmʌŋɡər / (AmE), / ˈaɪənˌmʌŋɡə / (BrE)
a person or shop that sells hardware such as tools, nails, and other metal goods, especially in Britain
person or shop selling hardware
british

Examples

The ironmonger on Queen Street has been in business for over a century.
My grandfather worked as an ironmonger before he retired.
She visited the local ironmonger to buy a new hammer and some nails.

Synset Examples

in England they call a hardwareman an ‘ironmonger’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hardware store - shop selling hardware, especially in the US

Etymology

From iron + monger (dealer or trader); monger from Old English mangere (merchant, trader).

2. NOUN

/ ˈaɪərnˌmʌŋɡər / (AmE), / ˈaɪənˌmʌŋɡə / (BrE)
a person or shop that sells hardware such as tools, nails, and other metal goods, especially in Britain
hardware dealer or shop (mainly British)
british

Examples

The local ironmonger stocks all sorts of tools and gardening supplies.
Many older British towns still have a family-run ironmonger on the high street.
She visited the ironmonger to buy nails and a hammer for her DIY project.

Synset Examples

in Great Britain they used to call a hardware store an ‘ironmonger's shop’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hardware store - a shop selling hardware such as tools, nails, and other household goods

Etymology

From iron + monger (from Old English 'mangere', meaning dealer or trader).
