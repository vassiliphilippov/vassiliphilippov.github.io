sild

1. NOUN

/ sɪld / (AmE), / sɪld / (BrE)
A young herring, especially from Norway, that is canned and eaten like sardines.
young Norwegian herring canned as sardines

Examples

We enjoyed freshly canned sild on crisp rye bread during our visit to Bergen.
The delicatessen stocks several brands of Norwegian sild for traditional Scandinavian dishes.
Savoring pickled sild is a holiday tradition in some Northern European countries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herring - a silvery fish of the North Atlantic, used as food
  • Synonym: sardine - small, oily fish often canned for food

Etymology

From Norwegian 'sild' meaning 'herring'.
