ballock

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑlək / (AmE), / ˈbælək / (BrE)
either of the two male reproductive glands that produce sperm and secrete androgens; testicle (used in slang, vulgar, or archaic contexts)
testicle (slang/archaic/vulgar)
vulgar archaic

Examples

In old English texts, the word ballock was commonly used to refer to the testicle.
He made a crude joke about being kicked in the ballock during the fight.
Some Scottish dialects still retain the term ballock for humorous effect.

Synset Examples

she kicked him in the balls and got away

Related Terms

  • Synonym: testicle - either of the two oval organs that produce sperm in males

Etymology

Old English *beallucas*, diminutive of *beall*, meaning ‘testicle’.
