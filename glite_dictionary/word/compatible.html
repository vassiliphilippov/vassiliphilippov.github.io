compatible

1. ADJ

/ kəmˈpæt̬.ə.bəl / (AmE), / kəmˈpæt.ɪ.bəl / (BrE)
(of substances) capable of forming a homogeneous mixture without separation or chemical reaction.
able to mix without separating or reacting

Examples

These solvents are compatible and will not separate when mixed together.
In pharmaceutical preparations, ingredients must be compatible to ensure product stability.
The chemicals are compatible, so they can be combined without concern for unwanted reactions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miscible - (of liquids) able to be mixed together without separating
  • Antonym: immiscible - (of liquids) not able to be mixed together without separating

Etymology

From Late Latin compatibilis, from Latin compati (to suffer with, have sympathy).

2. ADJ

/ kəmˈpætəbl̩ / (AmE), / kəmˈpætəbl̩ / (BrE)
able to exist, work, or live together without conflict; harmonious or well-matched
able to exist together harmoniously

Examples

The software is only compatible with the latest version of Windows.
They are a highly compatible couple who rarely argue about anything.
Your goals should be compatible with your values and beliefs.

Synset Examples

a compatible married couple
her deeds were compatible with her ideology

Related Terms

  • Antonym: incompatible - unable to exist or work together without conflict
  • Synonym: harmonious - forming a pleasing or consistent whole; in agreement

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'compatibilis', from Latin 'compati' (to suffer with).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00509376-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ kəmˈpæt̬.ə.bəl / (AmE), / kəmˈpæt.ɪ.bəl / (BrE)
(of devices or components) able to work or be used together without special modification.
able to be used together without modification

Examples

This new printer is compatible with both Windows and Mac operating systems.
Make sure the charger you buy is compatible with your laptop model.
The software is not compatible with older versions of the operating system.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: incompatible - not able to be used together; not compatible

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'compatibilis', from Late Latin 'compati' (to sympathize).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00510964-a
Synset POS: a
