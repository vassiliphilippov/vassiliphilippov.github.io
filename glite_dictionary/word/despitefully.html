despitefully

1. ADV

/ dɪˈspaɪtfəli / (AmE), / dɪˈspaɪtfəli / (BrE)
In a malicious or spiteful manner; with the intention to hurt or offend.
maliciously or spitefully
archaic

Examples

They treated her despitefully even though she had done nothing wrong.
He accused his enemies of acting despitefully towards him.
The king was despitefully mocked by his rivals in the court.

Synset Examples

pray for them that despitefully use us

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spitefully - in a way that shows malice or the intention to offend
  • Antonym: kindly - in a kind or considerate manner

Etymology

From 'despiteful' (spiteful, malicious) + '-ly' (adverb suffix).
