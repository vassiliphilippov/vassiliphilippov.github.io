to mutilate

1. VERB

/ ˈmjuːtəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈmjuːtɪleɪt / (BrE)
to damage or injure something or someone severely, especially by cutting off or destroying parts
to damage or injure severely

Examples

The artist was devastated when vandals mutilated his sculpture in the park.
The rescue team found that the animal had been severely mutilated by a trap.
Careless editing can sometimes mutilate the meaning of an original text.

Synset Examples

The madman mutilates art work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to maim - to wound or injure so that part of the body is permanently damaged
  • Synonym: to disfigure - to spoil the appearance of something or someone
  • Antonym: repair - to restore something that is damaged to its original state

Etymology

From Latin mutilare (to cut off, mutilate), from mutilus (maimed, mutilated).
