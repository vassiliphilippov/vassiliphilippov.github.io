hepatomegaly

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɛpətoʊˈmɛɡəli / (AmE), / ˌhɛpətəˈmɛɡəli / (BrE)
Abnormal enlargement of the liver.
enlargement of the liver (medical)
medical

Examples

The ultrasound revealed hepatomegaly associated with fatty liver disease.
His physical examination noted mild hepatomegaly without any tenderness.
Doctors may check for hepatomegaly in patients with symptoms of liver dysfunction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enlargement - the act or state of being made larger
  • Synonym: cirrhosis - chronic liver disease characterized by degeneration of cells and enlargement

Etymology

From Greek *hēpar* (liver) + *megalia* (enlargement).
