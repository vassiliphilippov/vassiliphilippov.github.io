UPPP

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuː piː piː ˈpiː / (AmE), / ˌjuː piː piː ˈpiː / (BrE)
Surgical removal of excess tissue from the palate and oropharynx to open the airway, especially to treat severe snoring or sleep apnea.
surgical procedure for sleep apnea/snoring
medical

Examples

After years of disruptive snoring, Mark underwent UPPP to improve his breathing during sleep.
The doctor explained that UPPP is often recommended for patients who do not respond to less invasive treatments for sleep apnea.
Though UPPP can help reduce snoring, some patients may still require additional therapies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uvulopalatopharyngoplasty - the full medical name for UPPP

Etymology

Acronym for uvulopalatopharyngoplasty, from Latin roots referring to the uvula, palate, and pharynx.
