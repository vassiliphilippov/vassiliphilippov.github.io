agerasia

1. NOUN

/ ˌæd͡ʒəˈreɪziə / (AmE), / ˌad͡ʒəˈreɪzɪə / (BrE)
A condition or quality of retaining a youthful appearance despite advanced age.
youthful appearance in an elderly person
medical

Examples

Despite being in her seventies, her agerasia often surprised new acquaintances.
The actress attributed her remarkable agerasia to genetics and a healthy lifestyle.
Doctors noted signs of agerasia in the patient, who had no wrinkles despite his age.

Etymology

From Greek 'agērāsia', from 'a-' (not) + 'gēras' (old age).
