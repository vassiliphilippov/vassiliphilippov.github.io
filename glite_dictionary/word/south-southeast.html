south-southeast

1. ADV

/ saʊθ ˌsaʊθˈist / (AmE), / saʊθ ˌsaʊθˈiːst / (BrE)
to, toward, or in the direction between south and southeast; south-southeastward.
toward the direction between south and southeast

Examples

The hiking trail continues south-southeast for three miles before reaching the river.
The wind was blowing steadily from the south-southeast all afternoon.
They set sail south-southeast to reach the island before nightfall.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: south - direction toward the South Pole
  • Degree Higher: southeast - direction between south and east

Etymology

Formed from 'south' and 'southeast,' indicating a compass point between the two.

2. NOUN

/ saʊθ saʊθˈist / (AmE), / saʊθ saʊθˈiːst / (BrE)
the compass point midway between south and southeast, corresponding to a bearing of 157.5°
compass point between south and southeast

Examples

The wind was blowing steadily from the south-southeast all morning.
Sailors set their course toward the south-southeast to avoid the storm.
The compass needle pointed directly at south-southeast, guiding their navigation.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: southeast - the compass point at 135° between south and east
  • Degree Lower: south - the compass point at 180°, opposite north
