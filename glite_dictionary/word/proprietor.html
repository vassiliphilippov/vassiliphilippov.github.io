proprietor

1. NOUN

/ prəˈpraɪət̬ɚ / (AmE), / prəˈprʌɪətə / (BrE)
a person who owns a business or property, especially one who has legal possession of it
legal owner of a business or property

Examples

The proprietor greeted guests at the entrance of his café every morning.
After years of hard work, she became the sole proprietor of a successful boutique.
Every proprietor must ensure their business complies with local regulations.

Synset Examples

he is the owner of a chain of restaurants

Related Terms

  • Synonym: owner - a person who owns something
  • Antonym: tenant - a person who rents and occupies property owned by another

Etymology

From Latin *proprietarius* (owner), from *proprietas* (ownership).
