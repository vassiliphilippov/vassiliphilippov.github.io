parterre

1. NOUN

/ pɑrˈtɛr / (AmE), / pɑːˈtɛə / (BrE)
the part of a theater auditorium at the rear of the main floor, typically beneath the balconies, used for seating
rear main floor seating in a theater

Examples

Our tickets placed us in the parterre, offering a clear view of the stage.
During the intermission, people mingled in the aisles near the parterre.
The parterre often sells out quickly because of its excellent acoustics.

Etymology

From French parterre (an area on the ground level), originally meaning 'on the ground'; in theater use since the 18th century.

2. NOUN

/ pɑːrˈter / (AmE), / pɑːˈteə / (BrE)
an ornamental garden area in which flower beds and paths are arranged to form a decorative pattern, typically viewed from above
ornamental patterned flower bed

Examples

The palace gardens featured an elaborate parterre designed in the shape of a star.
Visitors admired the manicured parterre from the balcony above, noting the symmetry of the design.
Each spring, gardeners replant the parterre with thousands of colorful flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flowerbed - a section of ground in which flowers are grown

Etymology

From French 'parterre' meaning 'on the ground', from 'par' (by) and 'terre' (earth).
