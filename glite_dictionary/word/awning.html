awning

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔː.nɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɔː.nɪŋ / (BrE)
a covering of canvas or other material attached to a building or vehicle, used to protect from sun or rain
protective canopy; sunshade

Examples

We sat under the awning to escape the midday heat.
The shop installed a new red-and-white awning over its entrance.
During the rainstorm, everyone huddled beneath the awning for shelter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: canopy - a covering, usually of fabric, supported on poles or suspended above a bed, throne, or seat

Etymology

From 16th-century French 'auvent' (covering, shelter).
