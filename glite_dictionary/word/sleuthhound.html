sleuthhound

1. NOUN

/ ˈsluːθˌhaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈsluːθˌhaʊnd / (BrE)
A detective, especially one who persistently follows clues and investigates mysteries.
persistent detective or investigator

Examples

The private sleuthhound tracked down the missing heir with dogged determination.
Journalists acted like sleuthhounds, sniffing out every detail of the scandal.
Her reputation as a sleuthhound grew with each solved mystery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detective - a person, usually a police officer, whose job is to investigate crimes and catch criminals

Etymology

From 'sleuth', meaning detective, and 'hound', a dog bred for tracking; originally referred to a bloodhound used for tracking.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsluθˌhaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈsluːθ.haʊnd / (BrE)
a large, powerful dog breed of European origin with a keen sense of smell, used for tracking
breed of tracking dog with keen sense of smell

Examples

The police relied on a sleuthhound to follow the scent trail left by the thief.
In medieval Europe, nobles often kept a sleuthhound for hunting and tracking criminals.
Legends tell of a sleuthhound that could trace its quarry across miles of wilderness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bloodhound - a breed of hound famous for its sense of smell and tracking ability

Etymology

From Middle English 'sleuth hound', from Old Norse 'slóð' (track, trail) + 'hound'.
