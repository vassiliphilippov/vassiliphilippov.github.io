Thessalia

1. PROPN

/ θəˈseɪliə / (AmE), / θɛˈsælɪə / (BrE)
an ancient region in east central Greece, known for its fertile plains and significant role in Greek history; also called Thessaly.
ancient region of east central Greece

Examples

Many myths from Greek antiquity are set in Thessalia, reflecting its legendary past.
The plains of Thessalia made it one of the most productive agricultural regions of ancient Greece.
Travelers often visit Thessalia to experience its rich history and scenic beauty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Thessaly - the English name for the region of Thessalia

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Θεσσαλία (Thessalía), a region of Greece.
