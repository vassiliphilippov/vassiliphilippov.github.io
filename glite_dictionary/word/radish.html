radish

1. NOUN

/ ˈrædɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈrædɪʃ / (BrE)
a small, crisp, pungent edible root of a plant of the cabbage family, typically eaten raw in salads
pungent edible root vegetable

Examples

She sliced a fresh radish for her salad to add a spicy crunch.
The gardener pulled a large radish from the soil after only a few weeks.
Pickled radish is a common condiment in many Asian cuisines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turnip - a round root vegetable with white or yellow flesh

Etymology

From Latin 'radix' (root), via Old French 'radis'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈræd.ɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈræd.ɪʃ / (BrE)
A small Eurasian plant, widely cultivated for its crisp, pungent root, which is typically eaten raw as a vegetable.
edible pungent root vegetable

Examples

He sliced a radish thinly and added it to the salad.
The radish grows quickly and can be harvested in a few weeks.
Fresh radishes add a spicy crunch to sandwiches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turnip - a round root vegetable commonly used in cooking

Etymology

5. NOUN

/ ˈrædɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈrædɪʃ / (BrE)
A radish of Japanese origin with a long, hard, white root that is eaten raw or cooked; also known as daikon.
Japanese radish with white long root

Examples

The salad was topped with thinly sliced radish and carrots for extra crunch.
Japanese cuisine often uses radish pickled or grated in dishes like sashimi.
She bought a large radish at the market to use in her stir-fry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: daikon - large, white Japanese radish

Etymology

From Latin *radix* (root); the Japanese variety is commonly called 'daikon' from Japanese 大根 (daikon, 'big root').
