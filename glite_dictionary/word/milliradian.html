milliradian

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪliˈreɪdiən / (AmE), / ˌmɪlɪˈreɪdiən / (BrE)
a unit of angular measurement equal to one thousandth of a radian, used in mathematics and surveying
one thousandth of a radian (angular measure)

Examples

The scope was adjusted to account for a 2 milliradian deviation in the aim.
In ballistics, a milliradian is commonly used for precise targeting over long distances.
Engineers measured the angle change to within one milliradian for accuracy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radian - the SI unit of angular measurement

Etymology

From milli- (meaning 'one thousandth') + radian (unit of angle).
