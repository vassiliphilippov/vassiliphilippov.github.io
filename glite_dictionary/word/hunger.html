hunger

1. VERB

/ ˈhʌŋɡər / (AmE), / ˈhʌŋɡə / (BrE)
to feel a strong need or desire to eat food
feel strong need to eat

Examples

After hiking all day, I really hungered for a hot meal.
She hungers for fresh bread every morning.
Long before breakfast, the children hungered for something to eat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to starve - to suffer or die from not having enough food to eat

Etymology

Old English 'hungrian' (to hunger), from 'hungor' (hunger), related to German 'Hungern'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02126423-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈhʌŋɡər / (AmE), / ˈhʌŋɡə / (BrE)
to have a strong craving or great desire for something, especially food or success
to strongly crave or desire

Examples

She hungered for adventure and new experiences.
As the days passed, he hungered for news from home.
Many people hunger for success in their chosen fields.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to crave - to have an intense desire for something
  • Synonym: to yearn - to have an intense feeling of longing for something

Etymology

From Old English *hungrian* (to hunger), related to *hungor* (hunger), of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01191018-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈhʌŋɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈhʌŋɡə / (BrE)
to feel the need for food; to experience hunger
to feel hunger or need food

Examples

After a long day of hiking, we began to hunger for a warm meal.
Many children around the world still hunger every day.
She would sometimes hunger when her family had little to eat.

Synset Examples

Let's eat — I'm starving!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to starve - to suffer severely or die from hunger
  • Antonym: to eat - to consume food

Etymology

From Old English 'hungrian', related to 'hunger'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01190677-v
Synset POS: v

4. NOUN

/ ˈhʌŋɡər / (AmE), / ˈhʌŋɡə / (BrE)
the feeling of discomfort or weakness caused by the need for food
physiological need for food

Examples

After working all day without a break, he felt intense hunger.
The charity aims to reduce hunger in impoverished communities worldwide.
Children suffering from chronic hunger are at risk of developmental problems.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: starvation - suffering or death caused by prolonged lack of food
  • Degree Lower: appetite - desire to eat food
  • Antonym: satiety - the feeling of being full or satisfied after eating

Etymology

From Old English *hungor*, related to Old High German *hungar* and Latin *fames* (hunger).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14063016-n
Synset POS: n

5. NOUN

/ ˈhʌŋɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈhʌŋɡə / (BrE)
a strong desire or craving for something other than food or drink
strong desire or craving

Examples

His hunger for success drove him to work harder than anyone else in the company.
As a child, she had a hunger for knowledge that kept her reading constantly.
Many artists are inspired by a deep hunger for recognition and expression.

Synset Examples

a thirst for knowledge
hunger for affection

Related Terms

  • Synonym: craving - an intense desire for something
  • Synonym: thirst - a strong desire for something (not liquid)
  • Antonym: aversion - a strong dislike or disinclination

Etymology

Old English 'hungor', related to German 'Hunger' and Dutch 'honger'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04952431-n
Synset POS: n
