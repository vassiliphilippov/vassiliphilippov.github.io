sideburn

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪdbɜrn / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdbɜːn / (BrE)
A strip of facial hair grown down each side of the face in front of the ears, often with the chin and jaw clean-shaven.
facial hair in front of the ears

Examples

He decided to grow long sideburns to match his retro style.
Trim your sideburns regularly for a neat appearance.
Elvis Presley was famous for his iconic sideburns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beard - Hair growing on the chin and cheeks
  • Synonym: mustache - Facial hair grown on the upper lip

Etymology

Named after Ambrose Burnside, a 19th-century American army general known for his distinctive facial hair.
