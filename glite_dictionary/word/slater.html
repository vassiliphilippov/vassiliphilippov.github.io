slater

1. NOUN

/ ˈsleɪtər / (AmE), / ˈsleɪtə / (BrE)
A small terrestrial isopod crustacean with a flattened, elliptical body, commonly found in damp habitats.
small terrestrial isopod crustacean

Examples

Children often look for slaters under rocks while playing in the garden.
The damp basement was teeming with tiny slaters scurrying across the floor.
In Australia, a slater is a familiar sight in shady, moist places.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: woodlouse - a small terrestrial isopod with a segmented shell; another common name

Etymology

Probably from regional British English; the origin is uncertain, possibly from its habitat on stone (slate).
