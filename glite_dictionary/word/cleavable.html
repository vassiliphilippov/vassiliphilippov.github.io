cleavable

1. ADJ

/ ˈkliːvəbəl / (AmE), / ˈkliːvəbəl / (BrE)
able to be split or divided along a natural line or plane, especially by physical or chemical means.
able to be split or separated

Examples

This mineral is cleavable along flat planes.
The chemical linker is cleavable under certain conditions, allowing easy separation.
Scientists designed a cleavable peptide for targeted drug delivery.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uncleavable - not able to be cleaved or split

Etymology

From 'cleave' (to split, separate) + suffix '-able'.
