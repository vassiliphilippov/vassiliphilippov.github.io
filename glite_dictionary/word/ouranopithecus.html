Ouranopithecus

1. NOUN

/ ʊˌrænəˈpɪθɪkəs / (AmE), / ʊˌranəʊˈpɪθɪkəs / (BrE)
A genus of extinct hominids that includes two species, Ouranopithecus macedoniensis and Ouranopithecus turkae, known from fossils found in Greece and Turkey.
genus of extinct hominids

Examples

Fossils of Ouranopithecus have been discovered in northern Greece, providing insight into Miocene-era primates.
Some scientists believe that Ouranopithecus could be closely related to the ancestors of modern great apes.
The skeletal remains of Ouranopithecus suggest it was a large-bodied primate adapted to life on the ground.

Etymology

From Greek ouranos (sky, heaven) + pithekos (ape, monkey).

2. NOUN

/ ʊˌrænoʊˈpɪθɪkəs / (AmE), / ˌaʊərænəʊˈpɪθɪkəs / (BrE)
A genus of extinct hominids consisting of two species, Ouranopithecus macedoniensis and Ouranopithecus turkae, known from fossil remains in Europe and Asia.
genus of extinct hominids

Examples

The discovery of Ouranopithecus fossils provided new insights into early hominid evolution.
Scientists debated whether Ouranopithecus was a direct ancestor of modern humans.
The jawbone attributed to Ouranopithecus was remarkably well-preserved.

Etymology

From Greek ouranos (‘sky’ or ‘heaven’) and pithekos (‘ape’).
