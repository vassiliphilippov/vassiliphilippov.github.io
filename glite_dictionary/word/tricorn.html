tricorn

1. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪˌkɔrn / (AmE), / ˈtraɪˌkɔːn / (BrE)
a hat with the brim turned up to form three points, popular in the 18th century
three-cornered hat

Examples

The soldier wore a distinctive tricorn as part of his uniform.
During the reenactment, many participants donned a tricorn to represent colonial fashion.
A battered old tricorn sat on the shelf, a relic from another century.

Etymology

From Latin 'tri-' (three) + 'cornu' (horn), referring to three corners.
