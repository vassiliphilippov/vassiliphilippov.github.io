Hokkianese

1. NOUN

/ hɑːˌkiːəˈniːz / (AmE), / hɒkɪəˈniːz / (BrE)
a Chinese dialect spoken in the Fujian (Fukien) province of southeastern China and by overseas Chinese communities
Chinese dialect from Fujian province

Examples

Many overseas Chinese communities use Hokkianese as their primary language at home.
She learned Hokkianese from her grandmother, who was born in Fujian province.
The festival speeches were delivered in both Mandarin and Hokkianese.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Min Nan - a branch of Chinese dialects including Hokkianese

Etymology

From 'Hokkien', the Southern Min pronunciation for 'Fujian', with the English suffix '-ese' denoting language or people.
