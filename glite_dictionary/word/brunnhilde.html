Brunnhilde

1. PROPN

/ brʊnˈhɪldə / (AmE), / brʊnˈhɪldə / (BrE)
A Valkyrie in Norse legend and a queen in the German epic Nibelungenlied, known for her love of Siegfried, whom she had killed after deceit and then took her own life.
mythical Valkyrie and queen in German legend

Examples

The opera's heroine, Brunnhilde, is celebrated for her dramatic sacrifice.
Many scholars compare Brunnhilde to other tragic figures in classical mythology.
In the Nibelungenlied, Brunnhilde is both powerful and doomed by fate.

Etymology

From Old Norse Brynhildr, composed of 'brynja' (armor) and 'hildr' (battle).
