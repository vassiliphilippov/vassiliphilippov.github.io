collectable

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɛktəbl̩ / (AmE), / kəˈlɛktəbl̩ / (BrE)
an object considered to be worth collecting, especially by enthusiasts; not necessarily of high value or antique
an object worth collecting

Examples

That vintage lunchbox is considered a collectable among enthusiasts.
He keeps his most prized collectables in a glass display case.
Some collectables rise in value over time, while others remain inexpensive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collectible - an object worth collecting, especially as a hobby

Etymology

From 'collect' + '-able', indicating something that can be collected.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03073217-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ kəˈlɛktəbl̩ / (AmE), / kəˈlɛktəbl̩ / (BrE)
(of an amount of money) subject to or requiring payment; able to be collected, especially as specified in a financial context.
requiring payment; able to be collected (money)
legal

Examples

The account has a collectable balance that the company may pursue.
Only collectable debts will be included in this financial report.
If the invoice is not collectable, it must be written off as a loss.

Synset Examples

a collectible bill
a note payable on demand
a check payable to John Doe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: payable - (of an amount of money) required to be paid

Etymology

From Latin 'collectus', past participle of 'colligere' (to collect), plus '-able' forming an adjective.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00137973-s
Synset POS: s
