Girondism

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪˈrɑnˌdɪz(ə)m / (AmE), / dʒɪˈrɒnˌdɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The political doctrine and principles of the Girondists, a moderate republican faction active during the French Revolution.
doctrine of the Girondists

Examples

The historian specialized in the study of Girondism and its role during the French Revolution.
His political views gradually shifted from radical Jacobinism to the moderate principles of Girondism.
Many saw Girondism as representing the interests of the provincial middle class.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Jacobinism - the political principles of the Jacobins, a radical revolutionary faction opposed to the Girondists

Etymology

From French Girondisme, after Girondins (a political group from the Gironde region of France).
