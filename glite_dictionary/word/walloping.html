walloping

1. ADJ

/ ˈwɑləpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɒləpɪŋ / (BrE)
(informal) Very large; extremely great in size, amount, or degree.
very large; extremely great
informal

Examples

The company reported a walloping increase in profits last year.
She handed him a walloping slice of cake after dinner.
They suffered a walloping defeat in the championship game.

Synset Examples

a thumping loss

Related Terms

  • Synonym: huge - very large in size
  • Antonym: tiny - very small

Etymology

From the verb 'wallop' (to beat, strike forcefully).

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɑːləpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɒləpɪŋ / (BrE)
A heavy or decisive defeat; a thorough beating, especially in a sport or competition.
a heavy or decisive defeat
informal

Examples

The team suffered a walloping in the championship game, losing by six goals.
After last night's walloping, the coach promised big changes for the next match.
They gave their rivals a real walloping in front of a cheering crowd.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrashing - a decisive or severe beating or defeat
  • Antonym: victory - success in defeating an opponent

Etymology

From the verb 'wallop' (to strike heavily or defeat soundly), with the '-ing' suffix forming a noun.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07491763-n
Synset POS: n
