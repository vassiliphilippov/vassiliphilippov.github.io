transcription

1. NOUN

/ trænˈskrɪpʃən / (AmE), / trænˈskrɪpʃən / (BrE)
the arrangement and adaptation of a piece of music for a different instrument, voice, or ensemble than originally intended
musical arrangement/adaptation

Examples

The orchestra performed a transcription of a famous piano concerto.
This violin piece is actually a transcription of a well-known choral work.
She was praised for her brilliant transcription of the jazz standard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arrangement - a musical adaptation of a composition for a different instrument or ensemble

Etymology

From Latin transcribere (to transcribe), via French transcription.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ trænˈskrɪpʃən / (AmE), / trænˈskrɪpʃən / (BrE)
a written or printed version of spoken words, or something copied from one medium to another
written or printed copy of spoken words

Examples

The court reporter provided a transcription of the witness's testimony for the records.
I requested a transcription of the interview to review the details.
Accurate transcription ensures that spoken content can be preserved in written form.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transcript - a written or printed version of something, especially a record of spoken words

Etymology

From Latin 'transcribere' (to copy, write across) via French 'transcription'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06360967-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ trænˈskrɪpʃən / (AmE), / trænˈskrɪpʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of making a recording, especially an audio recording of speech, music, or other sounds.
act of making an audio recording

Examples

The journalist made a transcription of the interview using a digital recorder.
Music students listened to the transcription of the concert for their assignment.
The police reviewed the transcription of the suspect's phone calls.

Synset Examples

she watched the recording from a sound-proof booth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recording - the act of storing sound or visual material

Etymology

From Latin transcriptio (a copy, transcription), from transcribere (to transcribe).

4. NOUN

/ trænˈskrɪpʃən / (AmE), / trænˈskrɪpʃən / (BrE)
(Genetics) The process by which the genetic information in a DNA sequence is copied into messenger RNA (mRNA).
copying DNA into mRNA (genetics)
medical

Examples

DNA transcription is the first step in gene expression, converting genetic information into RNA.
Researchers studied the effects of mutations on transcription efficiency in the lab.
Errors during transcription can lead to the production of malfunctioning proteins.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: translation - Genetics: the process by which mRNA is decoded to produce a protein

Etymology

From Latin transcriptio (a copying, transcribing), from transcribere (to transcribe) + -ion.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13589441-n
Synset POS: n

sound or television recording (from broadcast)
