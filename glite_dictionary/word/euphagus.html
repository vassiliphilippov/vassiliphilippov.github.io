Euphagus

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuːfəɡəs / (AmE), / ˈjuːfəɡəs / (BrE)
A genus of North American blackbirds in the family Icteridae, which includes species such as the rusty blackbird and Brewer's blackbird.
genus of North American blackbirds

Examples

The genus Euphagus includes both the rusty blackbird and Brewer's blackbird.
Brewer's blackbird, scientifically known as Euphagus cyanocephalus, is commonly found in western North America.
Researchers studied the migration patterns of birds belonging to the Euphagus genus.

Etymology

From Greek 'euphagos', meaning 'eating well'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈjuːfəɡəs / (AmE), / ˈjuːfəɡəs / (BrE)
a genus of birds in the family Icteridae, comprising certain North American blackbirds.
genus of North American blackbirds

Examples

The rusty blackbird is classified under the genus Euphagus.
Birdwatchers often study the differences between species in the Euphagus genus.
Members of Euphagus are commonly found in wetland habitats across North America.

Etymology

From Greek 'eu-' (good) + 'phagos' (eater), referring to the birds' feeding habits.
