trawler

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɔlɚ / (AmE), / ˈtrɔːlə / (BrE)
a fisherman who catches fish using a trawl net
a fisherman using a trawl net

Examples

The experienced trawler set out at dawn, hoping for a good catch.
Many trawlers face harsh conditions at sea during the winter months.
He worked as a trawler before becoming captain of his own boat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trawlerman - a fisherman who works on a trawler boat

Etymology

From 'trawl', referring to the act of fishing with a trawl net, plus '-er' (agent suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrɔlɚ / (AmE), / ˈtrɔːlə / (BrE)
A fishing boat equipped with a trawl net for catching fish by dragging it through the water or along the sea floor.
fishing boat with a trawl net

Examples

The old trawler returned to port after a long day at sea.
Many modern trawlers are equipped with advanced navigation systems.
We watched as the trawler hauled in its massive catch early in the morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trawl - a large net dragged along the sea bottom for catching fish

Etymology

From 'trawl' (a fishing net dragged along the bottom) + '-er'.
