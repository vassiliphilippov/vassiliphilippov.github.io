Chirocephalus

1. NOUN

/ kaɪrəˈsɛfələs / (AmE), / kaɪrəʊˈsɛfələs / (BrE)
A genus of small freshwater crustaceans known as fairy shrimp, found in temporary water bodies such as ponds and pools.
genus of freshwater fairy shrimp

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species of Chirocephalus in a remote mountain pond.
The presence of Chirocephalus often indicates good water quality in temporary pools.
Our biology class studied the life cycle of Chirocephalus under microscopes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fairy shrimp - small freshwater crustaceans with slender bodies and long antennae
  • Synonym: Artemia - a related genus of brine shrimp found in saltwater environments

Etymology

From Greek 'cheir-' (hand) and 'kephalē' (head), referring to the shape of the head appendages.

2. NOUN

/ kaɪrəˈsɛfələs / (AmE), / kɪərəˈsiːfələs / (BrE)
A genus of small freshwater crustaceans commonly known as fairy shrimp.
a genus of fairy shrimp (crustacean)

Examples

Researchers discovered a new species of Chirocephalus in the mountain lake.
The eggs of Chirocephalus are highly resistant to desiccation.
Members of the genus Chirocephalus can often be found in temporary pools.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fairy shrimp - small freshwater crustaceans known for swimming upside down

Etymology

From Greek 'cheir' (hand) and 'kephalē' (head), referring to distinctive appendages near the head.
