Accipiter nisus

1. NOUN

/ ækˈsɪpɪtər ˈnaɪsəs / (AmE), / akˈsɪpɪtə ˈnaɪsəs / (BrE)
a small hawk of Eurasia and northern Africa, also known as the Eurasian sparrowhawk.
small Eurasian hawk species

Examples

The Accipiter nisus is a skilled hunter, known for its agility in wooded areas.
Birdwatchers in Europe often spot the Accipiter nisus gliding through urban gardens.
Female Accipiter nisus are usually larger than their male counterparts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sparrowhawk - another name for Accipiter nisus, a small hawk found in Eurasia and Africa

Etymology

From Latin: 'accipiter' meaning hawk and 'nisus', the specific name used by Linnaeus.
