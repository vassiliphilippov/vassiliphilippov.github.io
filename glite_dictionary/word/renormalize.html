to renormalize

1. VERB

/ riːˈnɔrməˌlaɪz / (AmE), / riːˈnɔːməlaɪz / (BrE)
to adjust or modify something again so that it conforms to a norm or standard, especially in mathematics or physics
adjust again to conform to a standard

Examples

After the unexpected result, the researcher decided to renormalize the data for accuracy.
In quantum field theory, scientists often need to renormalize equations to avoid infinite values.
The team had to renormalize the pressure readings after calibrating the instrument.

Synset Examples

normalize relations with China
normalize the temperature
normalize the spelling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to normalize - to make something conform to a standard or norm

Etymology

From re- (again) + normalize; influenced by technical use in physics (especially quantum field theory).
