Microstrobos niphophilus

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪ.kroʊˈstroʊ.bəs nɪˈfɑː.fɪ.ləs / (AmE), / ˌmɪ.krəʊˈstrəʊ.bɒs nɪˈfɒ.fɪ.ləs / (BrE)
A small shrub native to Tasmania, characterized by short stiff branches.
Tasmanian shrub with stiff branches

Examples

The Microstrobos niphophilus thrives in the alpine regions of Tasmania.
Botanists identified the Microstrobos niphophilus by its short stiff branches and small size.
Conservation efforts focus on protecting habitats where Microstrobos niphophilus naturally grows.

Etymology

From Greek 'mikros' (small) + 'strobilos' (cone) and 'niphophilus' (snow-loving) from Greek 'niphos' (snow) + 'philos' (loving).
