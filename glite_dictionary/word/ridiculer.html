ridiculer

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɪd.ɪ.kju.lər / (AmE), / ˈrɪd.ɪ.kjuː.lə / (BrE)
a person who uses ridicule, irony, or sarcasm to mock or amuse
one who mocks using ridicule or sarcasm

Examples

The ridiculer made the guests laugh at the absurdities of modern life.
As a ridiculer, she often offended people with her biting remarks.
Though he called himself a ridiculer, his sarcasm sometimes went too far.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: satirist - a person who uses satire to criticize or mock
  • Synonym: humorist - a person who writes or performs humorous material
  • Antonym: admirer - a person who regards someone or something with respect or warm approval

Etymology

From 'ridicule' (to mock) + '-er' (agent suffix).
