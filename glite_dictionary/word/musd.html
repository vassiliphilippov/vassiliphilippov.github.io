MusD

1. NOUN

/ mʌz ˈdi / (AmE), / mjʊz ˈdiː / (BrE)
A Doctor of Music degree; the highest academic degree in music conferred by a university.
Doctor of Music degree

Examples

After years of study, she finally earned her MusD from Oxford University.
The university appointed him as a professor after receiving his MusD.
Applicants for the MusD program must demonstrate exceptional musical ability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: DMus - Doctor of Music degree (alternative abbreviation)

Etymology

Abbreviation of Latin 'Musicae Doctor', meaning 'Doctor of Music'.
