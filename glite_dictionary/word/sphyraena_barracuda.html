Sphyraena barracuda

1. NOUN

/ sfɪˈriː.nə bəˈrɑː.kə.duː.ə / (AmE), / sfɪˈriː.nə ˌbær.əˈkjuː.də / (BrE)
A large greyish-brown species of barracuda, valued as a food and sport fish and sometimes dangerous to swimmers.
a large species of barracuda fish

Examples

Fishermen were excited to catch a Sphyraena barracuda during the tournament.
Swimmers are advised to be cautious in waters known for the presence of Sphyraena barracuda.
Grilled Sphyraena barracuda is a delicacy in some coastal communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barracuda - a large predatory tropical marine fish with a slender body and large jaws

Etymology

From New Latin genus Sphyraena and the Spanish 'barracuda'.
