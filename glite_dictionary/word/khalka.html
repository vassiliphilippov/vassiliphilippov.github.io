Khalka

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːlkə / (AmE), / ˈkælkə / (BrE)
the Mongolic language spoken by the Khalkha people, constituting the basis of the official language of Mongolia
official language of Mongolia (Khalkha dialect)

Examples

The official language of Mongolia is Khalka, which is widely understood throughout the country.
Linguists often study Khalka to better understand the grammar of Mongolian.
Many Mongolians use Khalka as their primary language at home and in school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mongolian - the official language of Mongolia, based on the Khalkha dialect

Etymology

From Khalkha (Mongolian: Халх), the largest subgroup of Mongol people in Mongolia.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːlkə / (AmE), / ˈkælkə / (BrE)
A member of the largest subgroup of Mongol people, living mainly in central and eastern Mongolia.
a Mongol ethnic group in Mongolia

Examples

The Khalka are the predominant Mongol group in modern Mongolia.
She studied the culture and traditions of the Khalka during her research trip.
Many Khalka people still practice traditional nomadic herding.

Etymology

From Mongolian Халх (Khalh), the ethnonym for this group.
