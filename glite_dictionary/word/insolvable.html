insolvable

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈsɑːlvəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɒlvəbəl / (BrE)
impossible to solve; not capable of being resolved or found a solution for.
impossible to solve

Examples

After hours of discussion, the committee declared the issue insolvable.
Some mathematical puzzles remain insolvable despite advances in technology.
The mystery at the heart of the novel appeared insolvable until the final chapter.

Synset Examples

an apparently insolvable problem
public finance … had long presented problems unsolvable or at least unsolved

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unsolvable - impossible to solve; not able to be resolved

Etymology

From Latin insolubilis, from in- 'not' + solvere 'to loosen, solve'.
