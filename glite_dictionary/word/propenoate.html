propenoate

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈpɛnoʊˌeɪt / (AmE), / prəʊˈpiːnəʊˌeɪt / (BrE)
a salt or ester derived from propenoic acid (acrylic acid)
salt or ester of propenoic acid

Examples

The chemist synthesized a new propenoate compound for use in polymer production.
Many adhesives are based on esters of propenoate to achieve strong bonding properties.
Some propenoates are used as additives in paints and coatings for improved durability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acrylate - a salt or ester of acrylic acid; another word for propenoate

Etymology

From propenoic acid (systematic name for acrylic acid) + -ate (denoting a salt or ester).
