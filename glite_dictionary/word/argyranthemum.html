Argyranthemum

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˌdʒɪrænˈθɛməm / (AmE), / ɑːˌɡɪrænˈθɛməm / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, native to Macaronesia, often known as marguerites; sometimes previously included in the genus Chrysanthemum
a genus of daisy-like flowering plants

Examples

The Argyranthemum blooms profusely throughout the summer, adding color to many gardens.
Gardeners often plant Argyranthemum for its attractive, daisy-like flowers and long flowering period.
In Macaronesia, wild Argyranthemum species can often be found growing along rocky hillsides.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chrysanthemum - another genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, closely related to Argyranthemum

Etymology

From Greek 'argyros' (silver) and 'anthemon' (flower), referring to the silvery leaves or flowers.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑr.dʒɪˈræn.θə.məm / (AmE), / ˌɑː.dʒɪˈræn.θɪ.məm / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, endemic to the Macaronesian islands, often called marguerites or Paris daisies
a genus of daisy-like flowering plants

Examples

Argyranthemum species are popular in gardens for their showy, daisy-like flowers.
The Argyranthemum thrives in sunny locations with well-drained soil.
Horticulturists often hybridize Argyranthemum to create new varieties with vibrant colors.

Etymology

From Greek argyros (silver) + anthemon (flower), referring to the silvery foliage and daisy-like flowers.
