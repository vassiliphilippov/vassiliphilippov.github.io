full-time

1. ADJ

/ ˈfʊlˌtaɪm / (AmE), / ˈfʊl.taɪm / (BrE)
Working or involving the whole of the normal or standard period of time for a particular activity, especially employment or study.
for the whole standard period (esp. work, study)

Examples

After graduation, she started her first full-time job at a marketing firm.
Many students work full-time during the summer to save money for college.
He switched from a full-time to a part-time position to spend more time with his family.

Synset Examples

a full-time job

Related Terms

  • Antonym: part-time - working or involving less than the normal or full amount of time
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01092828-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADV

/ ˈfʊlˌtaɪm / (AmE), / ˈfʊlˌtaɪm / (BrE)
For the standard number of hours considered normal or typical in an activity, especially a job.
for the standard number of hours (adverb)

Examples

After college, she hopes to work full-time in her chosen career.
He studied part-time last year but now attends classes full-time.
Since the promotion, Alex manages the store full-time.

Synset Examples

she works full-time

Related Terms

  • Antonym: part-time - for fewer than the standard number of hours

Etymology

From 'full' + 'time', originally used to distinguish from 'part-time' employment.
