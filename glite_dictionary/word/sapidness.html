sapidness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæpɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈsapɪdnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of having a pleasant or agreeable flavor
pleasant or agreeable flavor

Examples

The sapidness of the ripe fruit delighted everyone at the picnic.
Chefs constantly strive to enhance the sapidness of their dishes with fresh herbs and spices.
Despite its appealing appearance, the soup was surprisingly lacking in sapidness.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: insipidity - lack of flavor or tastelessness
  • Synonym: sapidity - pleasant taste; the quality of being sapid

Etymology

From Latin 'sapidus' (tasty, savory) + English suffix '-ness'.
