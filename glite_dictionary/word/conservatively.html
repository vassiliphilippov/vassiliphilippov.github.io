conservatively

1. ADV

/ kənˈsɜːrvətɪvli / (AmE), / kənˈsɜːvətɪvli / (BrE)
in a cautious or moderate way, especially to avoid risk or overstatement
in a cautious or moderate manner

Examples

The company invested conservatively to ensure steady growth and minimize losses.
She estimated the costs conservatively so there would be funds left over just in case.
Doctors tend to treat rare conditions conservatively before recommending surgery.

Synset Examples

we estimated the number of demonstrators conservatively at 200,000.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cautiously - in a careful and risk-avoiding manner
  • Antonym: recklessly - in a careless or heedless manner

Etymology

From conservative + -ly.
