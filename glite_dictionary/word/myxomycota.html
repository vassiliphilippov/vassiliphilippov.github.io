Myxomycota

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪksoʊmaɪˈkoʊtə / (AmE), / ˌmɪksəʊmaɪˈkəʊtə / (BrE)
A taxonomic group of slime molds, characterized by a noncellular, multinucleate vegetative phase and a spore-producing stage; sometimes classified within the kingdom Protoctista.
taxonomic group of slime molds

Examples

The Myxomycota are notable for their unique life cycle, alternating between unicellular and aggregate forms.
Scientists studying forest ecosystems often examine the diversity of Myxomycota present in decaying wood.
Unlike true fungi, Myxomycota exhibit a vegetative stage that is multinucleate and noncellular.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slime mold - simple organism in the group including Myxomycota

Etymology

From Greek myxa (slime, mucus) + mykes (fungus) + -ota (taxonomic suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmɪksoʊmaɪˈkoʊtə / (AmE), / ˌmɪksəʊmaɪˈkəʊtə / (BrE)
A former division of slime molds consisting of organisms that have a noncellular, multinucleate vegetative stage and a spore-producing stage; includes Myxomycetes and Acrasiomycetes, sometimes classified in the kingdom Protoctista.
division of slime molds (biology)
medical

Examples

Slime molds belonging to the Myxomycota are found in damp forest environments across the world.
Researchers studied the life cycle of Myxomycota to understand their unusual reproductive strategies.
The classification of Myxomycota has changed as our understanding of their genetics evolved.

Etymology

From Greek myxa (slime, mucus) + mykes (fungus) + -ota (suffix denoting division in taxonomy).
