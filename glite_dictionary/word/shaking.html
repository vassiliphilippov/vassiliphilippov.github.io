shaking

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃeɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
A quick, short, or repeated movement back and forth or up and down; a trembling or quivering motion.
a shaky or trembling motion

Examples

The sudden shaking of the ground startled everyone in the building.
He tried to hide the shaking of his hands during the speech.
The shaking of the leaves in the wind made a soft rustling sound.

Synset Examples

the shaking of his fingers as he lit his pipe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trembling - a quivering or shaking movement
  • Antonym: steadiness - the quality of being stable and not shaking

Etymology

Present participle of 'shake,' from Old English *sceacan* (to move quickly).

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃeɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of causing something to move quickly up and down or back and forth
act of quick repeated moving

Examples

The shaking of the building during the earthquake frightened everyone inside.
She could feel the shaking of the train as it sped down the track.
Too much shaking can damage delicate equipment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vibration - rapid continuous movement back and forth
  • Antonym: stillness - absence of movement or motion

Etymology

From the verb 'shake', of Old English origin.
