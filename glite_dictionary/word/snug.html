snug

1. NOUN

/ snʌg / (AmE), / snʌg / (BrE)
a small, comfortable, and private room, especially one in a pub
small private pub room
british

Examples

He preferred to sit in the snug away from the noisy bar.
The old pub still has a traditional snug for regulars seeking privacy.
We found a cozy snug in the corner where we could talk in peace.

Etymology

Originally from the adjective 'snug', meaning 'comfortable' or 'secure', possibly from Scandinavian origins.

Examples
