scallop

1. NOUN

/ ˈskæləp / (AmE), / ˈskæləp / (BrE)
a thin slice of meat, especially veal, usually fried or broiled
thin fried or broiled slice of meat

Examples

He ordered the veal scallop with a lemon butter sauce.
The chef prepared several chicken scallops for the guests.
She learned how to fry a scallop to a perfect golden brown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escalope - a thin slice of meat without bone, typically in French cuisine

Etymology

From Old French 'escalope' (shell), later applied to the shape of a cutlet.

4. NOUN

/ ˈskæləp / (AmE), / ˈskɒləp / (BrE)
a marine bivalve mollusc with a broad, fluted, fan-shaped shell, valued as food; some species swim by rapidly opening and closing their shells
edible marine bivalve with fan-shaped shell

Examples

The chef prepared seared scallops with a lemon-butter sauce.
We collected empty scallop shells along the beach during our walk.
Some scallops can swim by clapping their shells together rapidly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clam - another edible marine bivalve
  • Synonym: oyster - another common edible bivalve mollusc
  • Synonym: mussel - another type of edible bivalve mollusc

Etymology

From Old French *escalope* (shell), likely from Germanic roots.
