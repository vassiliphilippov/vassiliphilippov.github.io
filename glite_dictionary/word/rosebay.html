rosebay

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊzˌbeɪ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊzˌbeɪ / (BrE)
A late-spring-blooming rhododendron native to eastern North America, with rosy to pink-purple flowers.
North American rhododendron with pink flowers

Examples

The garden was filled with blooming rosebay in late spring.
We spotted a cluster of rosebay along the hiking trail in the Appalachian Mountains.
The large pink blossoms of the rosebay added color to the wooded landscape.

Etymology

From 'rose' and 'bay,' referring to its flower color and laurel-like leaves.
