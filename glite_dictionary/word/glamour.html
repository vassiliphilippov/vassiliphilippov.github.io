glamour

1. VERB

/ ˈɡlæmər / (AmE), / ˈɡlæmə / (BrE)
(archaic) To cast a magical spell over someone or something; to enchant or bewitch.
cast a magical spell over; bewitch
archaic

Examples

In old legends, witches would glamour unsuspecting travelers and lead them astray.
He claimed that the faeries had glamoured the woods, making it impossible to find his way home.
The sorceress tried to glamour the prince, but he resisted her spell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bewitch - to cast a spell over; to charm or enchant

Etymology

From Scots 'glamer', alteration of 'grammar', relating to occult learning.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡlæmɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡlæmə / (BrE)
An attractive or exciting quality that makes someone or something seem appealing, often in a romantic or showy way; alluring beauty or charm.
alluring beauty or charm

Examples

The actress's glamour captivated audiences everywhere she went.
Many people are drawn to the glamour of city life, dreaming of fame and excitement.
Despite her job's apparent glamour, the work was actually exhausting and routine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allure - the quality of being powerfully attractive or charming
  • Antonym: plainness - the quality of not being attractive or glamorous

Etymology

From Scots 'glamer' (magic, enchantment), related to grammar (in the sense of occult learning).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04692544-n
Synset POS: n
