Isurus glaucus

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈsuːrəs ˈɡlɔːkəs / (AmE), / aɪˈsjuːrəs ˈɡlɔːkəs / (BrE)
a species of blue-grey shark found in the southwest Pacific, valued as both a sport and food fish
species of blue-grey shark of the southwest Pacific

Examples

The Isurus glaucus is sought after for its fighting strength by sport fishermen.
Researchers studied the migratory patterns of Isurus glaucus in the Pacific Ocean.
Catches of Isurus glaucus have increased due to its growing popularity as a food fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shortfin mako - a large, fast shark species closely related to Isurus glaucus

Etymology

From Latin *Isurus* (genus name, from Greek is- 'equal' + oura 'tail') and Latinized Greek *glaukos* 'bluish-grey'.
