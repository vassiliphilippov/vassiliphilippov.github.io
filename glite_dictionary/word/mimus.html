Mimus

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪməs / (AmE), / ˈmiːməs / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Mimidae, comprising various species of mockingbirds.
genus of mockingbirds

Examples

The northern mockingbird, classified under the genus Mimus, is known for its vocal versatility.
Several species in the genus Mimus are native to the Americas.
Ornithologists have studied the unique singing patterns of Mimus birds extensively.

Etymology

From Latin mimus (mimic, actor), reflecting the birds' ability to mimic sounds.
