Dasyurus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdæziˌjʊrəs / (AmE), / ˈdasɪˌjʊərəs / (BrE)
A genus of carnivorous marsupials known as quolls, native to Australia and New Guinea, belonging to the family Dasyuridae.
genus of carnivorous marsupials (quolls)

Examples

Several species in the genus Dasyurus are commonly known as quolls.
The Dasyurus is an important predator in its ecosystem.
Researchers studied the diet of Dasyurus populations in Tasmania.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quoll - A common name for species in the Dasyurus genus, carnivorous marsupials of Australia and New Guinea

Etymology

From Greek dasys (hairy) + oura (tail), referring to the furry tail.

2. NOUN

/ ˌdæsiˈjʊrəs / (AmE), / ˌdasɪˈjʊərəs / (BrE)
A genus of carnivorous marsupials known as quolls or native cats, native to Australia and New Guinea.
genus of marsupials (quolls/native cats)

Examples

The Dasyurus genus includes several species of carnivorous marsupials found in Australia.
Scientists are studying the endangered spotted-tailed Dasyurus to develop better conservation strategies.
Many farmers have encountered a Dasyurus hunting rodents on their properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quoll - a common name for animals of the genus Dasyurus

Etymology

From Greek dasys (hairy) + oura (tail), referring to the bushy tail.
