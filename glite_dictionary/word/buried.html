buried

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɛrid / (AmE), / ˈbɛrɪd / (BrE)
Placed in a grave; covered by earth, typically after death.
placed in a grave

Examples

The buried treasure was discovered by archaeologists after centuries underground.
Families mourn for their buried loved ones each year on the anniversary of the tragedy.
Hundreds of buried soldiers were honored at the cemetery memorial service.

Synset Examples

the hastily buried corpses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interred - placed in a grave or tomb, usually with formal ceremony
  • Antonym: exhumed - removed from a grave; dug up after burial

Etymology

Past participle of 'bury', from Old English 'byrgan' (to bury).
