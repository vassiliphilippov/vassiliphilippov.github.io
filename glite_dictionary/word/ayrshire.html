Ayrshire

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛrˌʃɪr / (AmE), / ˈɛəʃə / (BrE)
a hardy breed of dairy cattle originating from Ayr, Scotland, known for high milk production and adaptability
breed of dairy cattle from Scotland

Examples

The Ayrshire is prized by farmers for its impressive milk yield and resilience.
Many Scottish farms raise Ayrshire cattle due to their hardiness in rough weather.
Our dairy recently added several Ayrshire cows to improve the quality of our milk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Jersey - a breed of small dairy cattle from Jersey, Channel Islands
  • Synonym: Holstein - a breed of large dairy cattle known for high milk yield

Etymology

Named after Ayrshire, a historic county in southwest Scotland, where the breed originated.
