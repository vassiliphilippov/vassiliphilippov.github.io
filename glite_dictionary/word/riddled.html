riddled

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɪdld / (AmE), / ˈrɪdld / (BrE)
damaged or permeated throughout by numerous holes or gaps
full of holes or gaps throughout

Examples

The old barn's roof was riddled with holes, allowing rain to pour in.
After years of neglect, the document was riddled with wormholes and barely legible.
The soldier's uniform was riddled with bullet holes after the battle.

Synset Examples

a sweater riddled with moth holes
cliffs riddled with caves
the bullet-riddled target

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perforated - having a number of holes; pierced through
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00684067-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈrɪdld / (AmE), / ˈrɪdld / (BrE)
(of something undesirable) present in and spread throughout every part of something.
permeated or full of (often something undesirable)

Examples

The report was riddled with inaccuracies and misleading statements.
Her mind was riddled with doubts as she made her decision.
The country was riddled with corruption at every administrative level.

Synset Examples

cities riddled with corruption

Related Terms

  • Synonym: permeated - spread or diffused throughout
  • Synonym: infested - full of or overrun by (often something undesirable)

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'riddle,' meaning to perforate or fill with holes, later extended to 'filled or permeated (with something)'.
