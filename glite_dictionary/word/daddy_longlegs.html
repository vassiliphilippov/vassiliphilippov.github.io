daddy longlegs

1. NOUN

/ ˈdædi ˈlɔŋˌlɛɡz / (AmE), / ˈdædi ˈlɒŋˌlɛɡz / (BrE)
a large, long-legged fly that resembles a mosquito but does not bite; also called crane fly.
large, long-legged fly (crane fly)

Examples

A daddy longlegs rested on the window, its delicate legs spread across the glass.
Many people mistake a daddy longlegs for a giant mosquito, but it does not bite.
After the rain, the garden was full of daddy longlegs lazily drifting through the air.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crane fly - another name for the same insect

2. NOUN

/ ˈdædi ˌlɔŋˌlɛgz / (AmE), / ˈdædi ˌlɒŋˌlɛɡz / (BrE)
an arachnid with a small rounded body and very long thin legs, commonly found in gardens and houses
spiderlike arachnid with long thin legs

Examples

A daddy longlegs was crawling across the kitchen ceiling.
Children are often fascinated by the delicate legs of a daddy longlegs.
Despite its appearance, the daddy longlegs is harmless to humans.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harvestman - another name for the arachnid of the order Opiliones
