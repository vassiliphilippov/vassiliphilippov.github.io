broken-backed

1. ADJ

/ ˈbroʊkən ˌbækt / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊkən ˌbækt / (BrE)
(of a horse) having the bones of the back fused together by bony growths, often resulting in limited movement and spinal deformity.
having the vertebrae of the back fused by bone (in horses)

Examples

The veterinarian diagnosed the horse as broken-backed after examining its rigid spine.
A broken-backed horse often shows stiffness and pain when walking or being ridden.
Treatment options for a broken-backed animal are limited due to the spinal damage.

2. ADJ

/ ˈbroʊkən ˌbækt / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊkən ˌbækt / (BrE)
having the spine or back broken or severely damaged; often used of animals, people, or objects such as books.
having a broken or damaged back/spine

Examples

The old library had many broken-backed books on its shelves.
The broken-backed horse struggled to stand after the fall.
Years of use left the family album broken-backed and worn.

Synset Examples

a broken-backed book
a broken-backed old horse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: broken - damaged or in pieces; no longer in working order

Etymology

Formed from 'broken' (past participle of 'break') + 'backed'.

3. ADJ

/ ˈbroʊ.kən ˌbækt / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊ.kən ˌbækt / (BrE)
(of a ship or similar structure) so damaged or weakened that it sags at both ends, typically due to structural failure.
sagging at both ends due to structural weakness

Examples

After the storm, the once-proud vessel was now broken-backed and barely afloat.
The old pier stood broken-backed after years of neglect and battering by the waves.
A broken-backed ship is considered beyond repair and must be taken out of service.
