inflection

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈflɛkʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈflɛkʃən / (BrE)
the way in which the voice rises and falls or changes in pitch or tone in speech; the patterns of stress and intonation in a language
variation of stress and intonation in speech

Examples

The actor used clever inflection to express subtle emotions in his speech.
English has a variety of inflection patterns that indicate surprise or doubt.
Her voice had a gentle inflection at the end of each question.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intonation - the rise and fall of the voice when speaking
  • Antonym: monotone - a continuing sound, especially of someone's voice, that is unchanging in pitch

Etymology

From Latin 'inflectere' (to bend in, turn aside).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈflɛkʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈflɛkʃən / (BrE)
a change in the form of a word, typically by adding a suffix, to indicate a change in grammatical function such as tense, number, or case
change in a word's form for grammar

Examples

In Latin, the inflection of verbs provides information about tense and person.
The English language uses fewer inflections than many other languages.
The change from "walk" to "walked" is an example of verb inflection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conjugation - variation of the form of a verb to express tense, mood, etc.
  • Synonym: declension - variation of the form of a noun, pronoun, or adjective to indicate number, case, and gender

Etymology

From Latin inflexio, inflexionis (a bending, modification), from inflectere (to bend in).

a bend or deviation from a straight or normal course, especially a change in direction or curvature
a bend or deviation in a line or course

Examples

There was a noticeable inflection in the path where the river curved to the east.
The architect pointed out the inflection in the building's otherwise straight façade.
Doctors studied the spine's inflection to assess the severity of the curvature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bend - a curve or angle in a straight object or course
  • Synonym: deviation - the action of departing from an established course or norm
  • Antonym: straightness - the condition of being straight, without bends or curves

Etymology

4. NOUN

/ ɪnˈflɛk.ʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈflɛk.ʃən / (BrE)
modulation of the loudness, pitch, or tone of the voice when speaking
modulation of voice pitch or tone

Examples

The teacher's inflection made the lesson much more engaging for the students.
You can often tell someone's mood from the inflection in their voice.
Actors use inflection to convey emotion and meaning in their performances.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intonation - the rise and fall in pitch of the voice
  • Antonym: monotone - a continuous, unchanging tone, without inflection

Etymology

From Latin inflectere (to bend in).
