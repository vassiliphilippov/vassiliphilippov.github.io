carbocyclic

1. ADJ

/ kɑr.boʊˈsaɪ.klɪk / (AmE), / kɑː.bəˈsɪk.lɪk / (BrE)
(of a chemical compound) having or relating to a ring structure composed exclusively of carbon atoms.
having a ring of carbon atoms
medical

Examples

Benzene is an example of a carbocyclic compound found in organic chemistry.
The synthesis focused on creating carbocyclic rings to increase molecular stability.
Researchers discovered a new carbocyclic antibiotic with promising properties.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: heterocyclic - having a ring structure containing atoms of at least two different elements

Etymology

From 'carbo-' (carbon) + 'cyclic' (forming a ring or cycle).
