fructification

1. NOUN

/ ˌfrʌktɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfrʌktɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or action of bearing fruit, especially in plants
the bearing of fruit

Examples

The apple trees reached fructification late in the summer, producing an abundant harvest.
Many plants require specific conditions for successful fructification.
Proper irrigation may enhance the rate of fructification in commercial orchards.

Etymology

From Latin 'fructificatio(n-)', from 'fructificare' (to bear fruit).

2. NOUN

/ ˌfrʌktɪfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfrʌktɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the organ or part of a plant, especially in ferns and mosses, where fruit or reproductive bodies are produced
fruiting/reproductive organ in plants

Examples

The fructification of the fern was visible under the leaves as small clusters.
Botanists studied the development of fructification in several moss species.
Each type of plant may display a distinct form of fructification.

Etymology

From Latin fructificatio (bearing fruit), from fructificare (to bear fruit).
