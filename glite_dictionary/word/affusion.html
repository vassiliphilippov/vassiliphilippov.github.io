affusion

1. NOUN

/ əˈfjuʒən / (AmE), / əˈfjuːʒən / (BrE)
the act of baptizing someone by pouring water on their head
baptism by pouring water on head

Examples

The priest performed the affusion by gently pouring water on the baby's head.
Some Christian denominations prefer affusion over immersion for baptism ceremonies.
During the ritual, affusion was chosen because the font was too small for full immersion.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: immersion - the act of baptizing by submerging fully in water
  • Synonym: aspersion - the act of baptizing by sprinkling water

Etymology

From Latin affusio (a pouring on), from affundere (to pour on).
