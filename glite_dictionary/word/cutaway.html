cutaway

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌtəˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈkʌtəˌweɪ / (BrE)
a formal man's coat with the front part cut away diagonally from the waist to the tails at the back, typically worn for morning dress
man's formal coat with diagonal front cut

Examples

He looked dashing in a classic cutaway at the wedding.
The butler wore a black cutaway with striped trousers.
Morning dress traditionally requires a cutaway and a waistcoat.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʌt.əˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈkʌt.ə.weɪ / (BrE)
a drawing or model that shows the interior of something by omitting or slicing away the outer parts
drawing/model showing interior by omitting exterior

Examples

The textbook included a detailed cutaway of the human heart to illustrate its chambers and valves.
A large cutaway model of the engine was displayed at the science fair.
The magazine showed a cutaway of the new smartphone design, revealing its internal components.

Etymology

From 'cut away', indicating the act of removing outer parts to reveal the inside.
