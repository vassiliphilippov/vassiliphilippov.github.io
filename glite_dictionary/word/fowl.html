fowl

1. NOUN

/ faʊl / (AmE), / faʊl / (BrE)
the flesh of a bird, especially chicken or other domestic or wild bird, when used as food
bird flesh used as food

Examples

The chef prepared a delicious dish using fowl from the local farm.
Roast fowl is often served during festive occasions.
She preferred the taste of wild fowl over domestic chicken.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poultry - domestic birds raised for their meat or eggs

Etymology

Old English 'fugol', meaning bird, related to Dutch 'vogel' and German 'Vogel'.

4. NOUN

/ faʊl / (AmE), / faʊl / (BrE)
A domesticated bird such as a chicken or duck, especially one kept for its eggs or meat, thought to descend from the red jungle fowl.
domesticated bird kept for eggs or meat

Examples

The farmer raised hundreds of fowl on his property each year.
It is important to keep fowl healthy to ensure a good supply of eggs.
Many rural households keep some fowl for food and as pets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chicken - a common domestic fowl kept for its eggs and meat
  • Synonym: duck - a waterfowl often domesticated for eggs or meat

Etymology

Old English *fugal*, related to German *Vogel* (bird).
