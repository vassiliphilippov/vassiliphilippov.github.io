Rosa damascena

1. NOUN

/ ˈroʊzə dæˌmæsˈseɪnə / (AmE), / ˈrəʊzə dæˌmasˈsiːnə / (BrE)
A species of hardy, highly fragrant, pink rose, cultivated especially in Asia Minor for its use in the production of attar of roses and as a parent of many hybrid varieties.
highly fragrant pink rose species

Examples

The essential oil distilled from Rosa damascena petals is treasured in perfume making.
Many perfumers praise the distinctive scent of Rosa damascena above all other rose varieties.
Flowers from Rosa damascena are often harvested at dawn to preserve their fragrance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: damask rose - Common name for Rosa damascena, the fragrant rose species

Etymology

From Latin 'rosa damascena', meaning 'Damask rose', referring to Damascus, Syria, where the flower was historically cultivated.
