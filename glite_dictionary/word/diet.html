diet

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪət / (AmE), / ˈdaɪət / (BrE)
The legislative assembly in certain countries, especially Japan.
legislative assembly (esp. in Japan)

Examples

The Japanese Diet passed the new legislation after several hours of debate.
The emperor addressed the opening of the Diet in a formal ceremony.
Japan's Diet consists of two houses: the House of Representatives and the House of Councillors.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'dieta' (daily assembly, governing session), from Latin 'dies' (day), originally referring to a day's work or session.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪət / (AmE), / ˈdaɪət / (BrE)
the usual food and drink that a person, animal, or group regularly eats and drinks
usual food and drink consumed

Examples

A panda’s diet consists mostly of bamboo.
Her diet is rich in fruit and vegetables.
The bird’s natural diet includes insects and seeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nutrition - the process of obtaining and using food for health and growth

Etymology

From Greek *diaita* (way of life, diet), via Latin *diaeta*.

3. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪət / (AmE), / ˈdaɪət / (BrE)
a regulated selection of foods, often prescribed for health or medical reasons
prescribed selection of foods

Examples

After his surgery, Mark followed a strict diet to help with his recovery.
Many people start a new diet in January to improve their health.
The doctor prescribed a low-sodium diet to control her blood pressure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regimen - a prescribed course of medical treatment, diet, or exercise

Etymology

From Greek *diaita* (way of life, regimen), via Latin *diaeta*.

4. VERB

/ ˈdaɪ.ət / (AmE), / ˈdaɪ.ət / (BrE)
to eat less or control what one eats, especially to improve health or lose weight
control food intake for health or weight

Examples

I'm trying to diet so that I can fit into my wedding dress.
He dieted for several months before the competition.
Many people diet in January after overeating during the holidays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fast - to abstain from some or all food or drink, especially for religious or health reasons

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French 'diète', from Latin 'diaeta' (way of life, regimen), from Greek 'diaita'.

6. VERB

/ ˈdaɪət / (AmE), / ˈdaɪət / (BrE)
to follow a specific eating regimen, usually for health or weight loss purposes
follow a regulated eating plan

Examples

After her surgery, she had to diet carefully to aid her recovery.
Many people diet before their summer vacations in order to feel more confident.
He has been dieting for months in hopes of lowering his cholesterol.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fast - to abstain from all or some kinds of food or drink, especially as a religious observance
  • Antonym: to overeat - to eat more food than your body needs, especially habitually

Etymology

From Old French *diete*, from Latin *diaeta* (mode of life, diet), from Greek *diaita* (way of life).
