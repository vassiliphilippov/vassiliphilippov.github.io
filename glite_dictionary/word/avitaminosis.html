avitaminosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪvaɪtəˌməˈnoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌeɪvɪtəˌmɪˈnəʊsɪs / (BrE)
a disease caused by the deficiency of one or more vitamins
vitamin deficiency disease
medical

Examples

Prolonged lack of fresh fruit and vegetables can result in avitaminosis.
Doctors diagnosed him with avitaminosis after he showed symptoms of vitamin deficiency.
A balanced diet can prevent avitaminosis and other nutritional disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypovitaminosis - disease caused by insufficient vitamin intake

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'a-' (without) + 'vitamin' + Greek '-osis' (condition or disease).
