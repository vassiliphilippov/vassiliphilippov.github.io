Salvia lancifolia

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæl.vi.ə ˌlæn.sɪˈfoʊ.li.ə / (AmE), / ˈsæl.vi.ə ˌlan.sɪˈfəʊ.li.ə / (BrE)
A species of sage native to western North America to Central America, known for its violet-blue flowers and widespread cultivation.
a species of sage with violet-blue flowers

Examples

Many gardeners choose Salvia lancifolia for its attractive violet-blue flowers.
The Salvia lancifolia thrives in sunny locations with well-drained soil.
Botanists have studied the distribution of Salvia lancifolia across Central America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sage - an aromatic plant used as a herb, especially Salvia officinalis

Etymology

From Latin: 'salvia' meaning sage and 'lancifolia' meaning lance-shaped leaves.
