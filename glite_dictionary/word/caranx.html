Caranx

1. NOUN

/ kəˈræŋks / (AmE), / kəˈræŋks / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish in the family Carangidae, including species commonly known as jacks and trevallies.
genus of marine fish (jacks, trevallies)

Examples

The Caranx genus is important to commercial and sport fisheries around the world.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Caranx group off the Pacific coast.
Several members of Caranx are prized for their speed and strength.

Etymology

From Latin 'caranx', meaning a kind of fish.

Examples

The taxonomic classification places Caranx within the family Carangidae.
