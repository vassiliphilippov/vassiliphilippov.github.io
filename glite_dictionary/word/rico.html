RICO

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈriːkəʊ / (BrE)
A U.S. federal law aimed at combating organized crime by imposing strong penalties and asset forfeiture for ongoing criminal enterprises.
U.S. law targeting organized crime
legal american

Examples

The prosecutors used RICO statutes to bring down the entire criminal organization.
Several companies were investigated under RICO for allegedly participating in a pattern of racketeering.
Defense attorneys argued that RICO charges were not applicable to their clients.

Etymology

Acronym of Racketeer Influenced and Corrupt Organizations Act, enacted in the U.S. in 1970.
