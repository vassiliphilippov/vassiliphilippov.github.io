nadir

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪdɚ / (AmE), / ˈneɪdɪə / (BrE)
the point on the celestial sphere directly below an observer, opposite the zenith
lowest point in the sky below observer

Examples

The star reached its nadir at midnight, disappearing below the horizon.
At the nadir, the satellite was directly beneath the station's position on Earth.
During the observation, the comet moved from the zenith toward the nadir.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: zenith - the point on the celestial sphere directly above an observer, opposite of nadir

Etymology

From Arabic *naẓīr* (counterpart, opposite), via Medieval Latin *nadir*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈneɪdɚ / (AmE), / ˈneɪdɪə / (BrE)
the lowest point or most unsuccessful moment of a situation; an extreme state of adversity
lowest point; moment of adversity

Examples

After the scandal, the company's reputation reached its nadir and struggled to recover.
Losing his job and his home marked the emotional nadir of his life.
The team's performance hit a nadir last season, but they are now showing signs of improvement.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: zenith - the highest point or culmination; especially in success

Etymology

From Arabic *nazīr* (counterpart, opposite), used in astronomy for the point opposite the zenith.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14510223-n
Synset POS: n
