Physidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪsɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfɪzɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, air-breathing freshwater snails belonging to the class Gastropoda.
family of freshwater snails

Examples

The Physidae are found in ponds and slow-moving rivers throughout North America.
Scientists studied the reproductive behavior of several species within the Physidae family.
A member of the Physidae, the common bladder snail can adapt to various freshwater habitats.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, formed from the type genus 'Physa' and the zoological suffix '-idae'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɪsɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfɪsɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of small, air-breathing freshwater snails commonly found in ponds, lakes, and streams
a family of freshwater snails

Examples

The Physidae are often found on the underside of pond plants.
Researchers studied the shell structure of several Physidae species in freshwater habitats.
Many aquarium enthusiasts keep Physidae in their tanks to help control algae.

Etymology

From the genus name Physa, from Greek phusa (bladder, bubble), referring to the snail's shape.
