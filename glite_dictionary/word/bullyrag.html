to bullyrag

1. VERB

/ ˈbʊl.iˌræɡ / (AmE), / ˈbʊl.iˌræɡ / (BrE)
to intimidate or harass someone with persistent scolding, threats, or bossiness
intimidate or harass persistently

Examples

The manager would often bullyrag his subordinates, making the workplace uncomfortable.
Her classmates tried to bullyrag her into joining their group, but she refused.
Some people mistakenly believe they can bullyrag others to get their way.

Synset Examples

Her big brother always bullied her when she was young

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bully - to seek to harm, intimidate, or coerce someone perceived as vulnerable
  • Synonym: to harass - to subject to aggressive pressure or intimidation
  • Antonym: to encourage - to give support or confidence to; opposite of bullying or intimidating

Etymology

Probably from 'bully' + 'rag' (to scold), both English slang terms of the 19th century.
