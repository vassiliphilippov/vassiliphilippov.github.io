Diomedea exulans

1. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪəˈmiːdiə ɛgˈzuːlənz / (AmE), / ˌdaɪəˈmiːdiə ɛgˈzjuːlænz / (BrE)
a very large species of albatross, primarily white with broad black wings, known for its extensive wingspan and long-distance flights
wandering albatross species

Examples

The Diomedea exulans is renowned for having the longest wingspan of any living bird.
Scientists tracked the migration patterns of the Diomedea exulans across the southern oceans.
The distinctive black and white plumage makes the Diomedea exulans easy to identify at sea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wandering albatross - common name for Diomedea exulans, a large oceanic bird

Etymology

From Latin: genus Diomedea (named after Greek mythological figure Diomedes) and exulans (wandering; from 'exulare', to exile or wander).
