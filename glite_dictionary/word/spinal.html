2. NOUN

/ ˈspaɪnəl / (AmE), / ˈspaɪnəl / (BrE)
A method of anesthesia in which an anesthetic is injected into the spinal canal, resulting in loss of sensation in the lower half of the body.
anesthesia by injection into spinal canal
medical

Examples

The patient received a spinal before undergoing surgery on her lower abdomen.
After the spinal, he was unable to move his legs for several hours.
Some people experience headaches after a spinal is administered.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epidural - A type of anesthesia injected into the epidural space, often used during childbirth

Etymology

From Latin spina (thorn, spine), relating to the spine or spinal cord.
