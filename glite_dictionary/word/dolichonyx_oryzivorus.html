Dolichonyx oryzivorus

1. NOUN

/ doʊˈlɪkoʊnɪks ɔːˌraɪzɪˈvɔːrəs / (AmE), / dəʊˈlɪkənɪks ɒˌraɪzɪˈvɔːrəs / (BrE)
a species of migratory American songbird, also known as the bobolink, recognized for its distinctive plumage and melodic song
migratory American songbird (bobolink)

Examples

The Dolichonyx oryzivorus can be spotted in North American meadows during the breeding season.
Ornithologists study the migration patterns of Dolichonyx oryzivorus as it travels from North to South America.
Farmers sometimes notice flocks of Dolichonyx oryzivorus feeding in rice fields during their migration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bobolink - common name for Dolichonyx oryzivorus

Etymology

From Greek dolichos (long), onyx (claw), and Latin oryza (rice), vorus (eating) – referring to the bird's long claws and rice-eating habit.
