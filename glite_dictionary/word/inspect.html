to inspect

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈspɛkt / (AmE), / ɪnˈspɛkt / (BrE)
to examine something carefully, especially to check accuracy or condition, often for official or verification purposes
examine carefully to verify or check

Examples

The safety officer will inspect all fire extinguishers to ensure they are working properly.
Engineers inspected the bridge thoroughly before reopening it to traffic.
Please inspect your answer sheet for any missing information before submitting it.

Synset Examples

audit accounts and tax returns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to examine - to look at something carefully in order to learn more about it
  • Antonym: overlook - to fail to notice or consider something

Etymology

From Latin inspectare, frequentative of inspicere (to look into).

WordNet ID: oewn-00698828-v

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈspɛkt / (AmE), / ɪnˈspɛkt / (BrE)
to look at something or someone carefully in order to discover information, especially about its quality or condition
examine carefully

Examples

The customs officer will inspect your luggage before you enter the country.
Before closing the store, she always inspects each aisle to ensure everything is in order.
Experts were called in to inspect the building for possible structural damage after the earthquake.

Synset Examples

Please inspect your father's will carefully

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to examine - to look at something carefully and thoroughly
  • Antonym: overlook - to fail to notice or inspect

Etymology

From Latin 'inspectare', frequentative of 'inspicere' (to look into), from 'in-' (into) + 'specere' (to look).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02169987-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɪnˈspɛkt / (AmE), / ɪnˈspɛkt / (BrE)
to visit or examine a place officially in order to check or evaluate it, especially for compliance or condition
visit officially to check or examine

Examples

The manager will inspect the new facility tomorrow morning.
Health officials regularly inspect restaurants to ensure cleanliness and safety standards are followed.
The head teacher inspected the classrooms during the annual review.

Synset Examples
