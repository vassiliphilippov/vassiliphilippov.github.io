raftered

1. ADJ

/ ˈræftərd / (AmE), / ˈrɑːftəd / (BrE)
(of a ceiling or roof) having rafters exposed or visible, especially as a design feature.
having visible rafters

Examples

The old cottage featured a beautiful raftered ceiling that added rustic charm.
We admired the raftered hall where the beams were left uncovered.
They chose a raftered design to give the living room a more spacious feel.

Synset Examples

a raftered ceiling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rafter - a sloping beam supporting a roof

Etymology

From 'rafter', meaning a sloping beam supporting a roof, plus the adjectival suffix '-ed'.
