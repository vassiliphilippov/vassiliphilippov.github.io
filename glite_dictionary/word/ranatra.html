Ranatra

1. NOUN

/ rəˈneɪ.trə / (AmE), / rəˈneɪ.trə / (BrE)
A genus of slender, elongate aquatic insects in the family Nepidae, commonly known as water scorpions.
genus of slender aquatic insects (water scorpions)

Examples

The Ranatra uses its long, slender body to blend in with underwater vegetation.
Scientists observed a Ranatra capturing small aquatic insects in the pond.
The unique breathing tube of Ranatra allows it to stay submerged for long periods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water scorpion - common name for insects of the family Nepidae, including Ranatra

Etymology

Modern scientific Latin, from earlier taxonomic classifications.

2. NOUN

/ rəˈneɪ.trə / (AmE), / rəˈneɪ.trə / (BrE)
A genus of long, slender aquatic insects commonly known as water scorpions, belonging to the family Nepidae.
genus of slender aquatic insects (water scorpions)

Examples

The Ranatra hunts by lying motionless in the water and ambushing prey.
Many species of Ranatra can be found in ponds and slow-moving streams.
Biologists identified the specimen as belonging to the genus Ranatra.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin 'Ranatra', used as a genus name in taxonomy.
