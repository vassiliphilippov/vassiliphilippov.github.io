taxation

1. NOUN

/ tækˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / tækˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
the money received by a government from taxes; government revenue from the collection of taxes.
government revenue from taxes

Examples

The country's taxation increased significantly after the new policies were implemented.
Rising taxation allowed the government to invest more in public services.
Debates continue over the fairness of taxation as a source of government income.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tax - a compulsory contribution to state revenue

Etymology

From Latin taxatio (a valuing, estimation), from taxare (to assess); influenced by French taxation.

2. NOUN

/ tækˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / tækˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of imposing charges on citizens' income, property, or activities to fund government expenses
government process of levying taxes

Examples

The government's plan for taxation was heavily debated in parliament.
Many citizens believe that fair taxation is essential for a healthy society.
He studied the effects of taxation on small businesses for his research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tax - a compulsory contribution to state revenue
  • Antonym: exemption - freedom from being required to pay tax

Etymology

From Latin 'taxatio', meaning estimation or appraisal.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13330021-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ tækˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / tækˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
the system or process by which a government imposes and collects taxes from its people or businesses
process of imposing and collecting taxes

Examples

The government introduced new taxation policies to increase revenue.
Heavy taxation can discourage investment and economic growth.
Fair taxation is essential for an equitable society.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tax - a compulsory financial charge or levy imposed by a government
  • Antonym: exemption - freedom from having to pay tax

Etymology

From Latin taxatio (an appraisal, a valuing), from taxare (to assess, rate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01162657-n
Synset POS: n
