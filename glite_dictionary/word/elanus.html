Elanus

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈleɪnəs / (AmE), / ɪˈleɪnəs / (BrE)
a genus of small, slender kites found in both the Old and New Worlds, known for their pale plumage and hovering flight
a genus of small kites (birds of prey)

Examples

The Elanus genus includes several species, such as the black-winged kite.
Birdwatchers often observe members of Elanus hovering in open grasslands.
The study focused on the migratory patterns of Elanus kites across continents.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin Elanus, from Greek elanos (kite, the bird).

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈleɪnəs / (AmE), / ɪˈleɪnəs / (BrE)
a genus of small kites found in both the Old and New Worlds, comprising several species of birds of prey
genus of small kites (birds of prey)

Examples

The Elanus genus includes several species commonly known as kites.
Researchers observed an Elanus hovering gracefully over the open field.
Species in the Elanus genus are found on multiple continents.

Etymology

From Greek elanos, meaning 'kite' (the bird).
