macrencephalic

1. ADJ

/ ˌmækrɛnˈsɛfəlɪk / (AmE), / ˌmakrɛnˈsɛfəlɪk / (BrE)
having a large brain case; possessing an abnormally large or well-developed brain
having a large brain case
medical

Examples

The paleontologist noted the macrencephalic structure of the ancient skull compared to modern species.
Some researchers believe that macrencephalic mammals are capable of higher cognitive processes.
In rare cases, infants may present as macrencephalic, requiring further neurological evaluation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: microencephalic - having an abnormally small brain

Etymology

From Greek makros (large) + enkephalos (brain) + -ic.
