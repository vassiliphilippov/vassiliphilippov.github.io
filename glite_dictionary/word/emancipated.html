emancipated

1. ADJ

/ ɪˈmæn.səˌpeɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ɪˈmæn.sɪ.peɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
free from traditional social or moral restraints; liberated, especially in behavior or attitude
free from social or moral restraints

Examples

Her parents were surprised by how emancipated her views on marriage had become.
The novel features an emancipated heroine who challenges societal expectations of women.
He admired her emancipated lifestyle and independent spirit.

Synset Examples

an emancipated young woman pursuing her career
a liberated lifestyle

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liberated - freed from social conventions or traditional restrictions
  • Antonym: conventional - adhering to accepted standards or customs, especially in behavior

Etymology

From Latin emancipatus, past participle of emancipare (to set free).
