policyholder

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑlɪsiˌhoʊldər / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪsiˌhəʊldə / (BrE)
a person or organization that owns an insurance policy and is entitled to its benefits
owner of an insurance policy

Examples

The policyholder must file a claim within thirty days of the incident.
As the policyholder, she is responsible for paying the premium regularly.
In case of any disputes, the rights of the policyholder are clearly stated in the contract.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insured - the person covered by an insurance policy

Etymology

From 'policy' (insurance contract) + 'holder' (one who possesses).
