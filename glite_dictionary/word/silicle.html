silicle

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪl.ɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪl.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
a short, broad, pod-like fruit characteristic of some cruciferous plants, typically less than twice as long as it is wide
short broad seed pod (botany)

Examples

The plant produces small silicles that contain several seeds each.
Botanists distinguish between a silique and a silicle based on their relative length and width.
The round silicle of shepherd's purse is a key identifying feature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silique - an elongated seed pod of certain plants, especially in the mustard family

Etymology

From Latin *silicula*, diminutive of *siliqua* (pod, husk).
