pneumothorax

1. NOUN

/ nuːmoʊˈθɔːræks / (AmE), / njuːməʊˈθɔːræks / (BrE)
The presence of air or gas in the pleural cavity, causing the lung to collapse.
air in the pleural cavity; collapsed lung
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed a pneumothorax after reviewing the chest X-ray.
Emergency surgery was required to treat the patient's pneumothorax.
A spontaneous pneumothorax can occur in otherwise healthy individuals, often without warning.

Etymology

From Greek pneumo- (air, lung) + thorax (chest).
