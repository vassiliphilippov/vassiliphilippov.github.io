conviviality

1. NOUN

/ kənˌvɪviˈæləti / (AmE), / kənˌvɪviˈælɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being friendly, lively, and sociable; a cheerful and sociable atmosphere or spirit
friendliness and sociability

Examples

Her birthday party was marked by an air of conviviality that everyone enjoyed.
The conviviality of the dinner made it easy for guests to relax and mingle.
We appreciated the conviviality of the office, which fostered teamwork and friendship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sociability - the quality of being social and outgoing
  • Synonym: mirth - great joy and amusement, especially as expressed in laughter
  • Antonym: gloom - a state of melancholy or depression

Etymology

From Latin 'convivium' (a feast), from 'con-' (together) + 'vivere' (to live).

2. NOUN

/ kənˌvɪviˈæləti / (AmE), / kənˌvɪvɪˈalɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being cheerful and friendly; a lively, festive atmosphere
cheerful sociability; festive atmosphere

Examples

The conviviality of the family dinner made everyone feel welcome and at ease.
There was an air of conviviality at the festival as people danced and laughed together.
His parties are famous for their warmth and conviviality, attracting friends from all walks of life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: merriment - cheerful or joyful fun and enjoyment
  • Antonym: gloom - a state of sadness or depression, lack of cheerfulness

Etymology

From Latin convivialis (pertaining to a feast or banquet), from convivium (banquet, feast).
