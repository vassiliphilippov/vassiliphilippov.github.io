to accommodate

1. VERB

/ əˈkɑməˌdeɪt / (AmE), / əˈkɒmədeɪt / (BrE)
to have enough space for someone or something; to hold without overcrowding
have enough room or space for

Examples

The conference room can accommodate up to sixty people comfortably.
Our garage cannot accommodate both cars during the winter months.
Does this venue accommodate wheelchair access for all guests?

Synset Examples

This hotel can accommodate 250 guests
The theater admits 300 people
The auditorium can't hold more than 500 people

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hold - to have the capacity to contain or carry something
  • Synonym: to admit - to allow entry or capacity for someone or something

Etymology

From Latin accommodare (to make fit, adapt).
