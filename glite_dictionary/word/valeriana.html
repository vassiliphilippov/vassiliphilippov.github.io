Valeriana

1. NOUN

/ vəˌlɪriˈænə / (AmE), / vəˌlɪəriˈɑːnə / (BrE)
a genus of widely distributed perennial herbs and some shrubs, including species used in herbal medicine
genus of perennial herbs and shrubs

Examples

Many species in the genus Valeriana are valued for their medicinal properties.
The roots of Valeriana are often harvested to make herbal remedies like valerian root extract.
Several Valeriana plants can be found growing in temperate regions across the world.

Etymology

From Latin 'Valeriana', the classical name for the plant.

2. NOUN

/ væˌlɛriˈænə / (AmE), / vəˌlɪəriˈɑːnə / (BrE)
A genus of perennial herbs and some shrubs, widely distributed and including the well-known valerian plant.
genus of perennial herbs and shrubs

Examples

The Valeriana genus includes over 250 different species worldwide.
Many herbal remedies originate from plants in the Valeriana genus.
Botanists study Valeriana to understand its medicinal properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: valerian - a plant of the species Valeriana officinalis, used as a sedative

Etymology

From Latin 'valeriana,' the name of the plant, possibly related to the personal name Valerius.
