cherrystone

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛr.iˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛr.iˌstəʊn / (BrE)
a small hard-shell clam, larger than a littleneck, often eaten raw or cooked
type of edible hard-shell clam

Examples

We served fresh cherrystones on the half shell at the party.
The chef prepared baked cherrystones stuffed with breadcrumbs and herbs.
Many seafood lovers enjoy cherrystones raw with a squeeze of lemon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quahog - an edible clam native to eastern North America
  • Degree Lower: littleneck - a type of small quahog clam smaller than a cherrystone

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃɛriˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛrɪstəʊn / (BrE)
A hard-shelled clam, especially a quahog, of medium size, larger than a littleneck but smaller than a chowder clam.
medium-sized edible clam (quahog)

Examples

The restaurant special features baked cherrystones stuffed with herbs and breadcrumbs.
We ordered a dozen cherrystones on the half shell for the appetizer.
Harvesters separate littlenecks, cherrystones, and chowder clams by their size.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quahog - an edible clam, especially of the species Mercenaria mercenaria
  • Degree Lower: littleneck - a small, young quahog used as food
  • Degree Higher: chowder clam - a large quahog, bigger than a cherrystone

Etymology

Named for its resemblance in size to a cherry stone.
