semitransparency

1. NOUN

/ sɛmɪtrænˈspærənsi / (AmE), / sɛmɪtrænˈspærənsi / (BrE)
The quality of allowing light to pass through, but not completely; partial transparency where light is diffused.
partial transmission of light; partial transparency

Examples

The semitransparency of the curtains allowed some sunlight to softly enter the room.
Artists often use layers of paint to achieve effects of semitransparency in their work.
The plastic had a semitransparency that obscured the objects placed behind it.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: transparency - The quality of allowing light to pass through fully
  • Antonym: opacity - The quality of not allowing any light to pass through

Etymology

From 'semi-' meaning 'half' or 'partial' + 'transparency', from Latin 'transparēre' (to appear through).
