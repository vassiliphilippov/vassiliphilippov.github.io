Abies lasiocarpa

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪ.bi.iːz ˌlæ.si.oʊˈkɑːr.pə / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.bi.iːz ˌlæ.si.əˈkɑː.pə / (BrE)
A medium to tall evergreen tree of the Rocky Mountains, also known as subalpine fir, characterized by its narrowly conic to columnar crown.
a Rocky Mountain fir tree species

Examples

The high-altitude forests are dominated by Abies lasiocarpa and Engelmann spruce.
Botanists studied the growth patterns of Abies lasiocarpa in disturbed environments.
The needles of Abies lasiocarpa are soft and have a pleasant aroma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subalpine fir - another common name for Abies lasiocarpa

Etymology

From Latin: 'Abies' (fir tree) and 'lasiocarpa' meaning 'woolly fruit'.
