synchronisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɪŋkrənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɪŋkrənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making two or more things happen at the same time or match in timing
making things happen at the same time
british

Examples

The synchronisation of the dancers' movements was impressive.
Accurate synchronisation of clocks is crucial in telecommunications.
They worked on the synchronisation of traffic lights to improve traffic flow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: synchronization - making things happen at the same time (American spelling)
  • Antonym: desynchronization - the process of causing things to be out of sync or timing

Etymology

From Greek 'synchronos' (occurring at the same time) via French and Latin; British spelling of 'synchronization'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɪŋkrənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɪŋkrənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of coordinating two or more things so that they occur or operate at the same time, especially by setting times or signals to match each other
act of coordinating timing

Examples

The synchronisation of the two clocks was essential for the experiment to proceed accurately.
To ensure perfect timing, movie directors pay close attention to the synchronisation of sound and picture.
The team practised the synchronisation of their watches before starting the timed challenge.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: synchronization - the act of causing things to happen at the same time (American English spelling)
  • Antonym: desynchronisation - the process of becoming out of synchronization

Etymology

From Greek syn- (together) + chronos (time) via French synchronisation.

3. NOUN

/ ˌsɪŋkɹənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɪŋkɹənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The state or process in which things happen at exactly the same time or move together in time.
simultaneous occurrence or coordination in time

Examples

The synchronisation of their dance moves made the performance truly impressive.
Accurate synchronisation of the project stages is crucial for on-time completion.
The synchronisation between the audio and video tracks was perfect throughout the film.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: synchrony - coincidence in time or simultaneous action
  • Antonym: asynchrony - lack of synchronisation; not occurring at the same time

Etymology

From Greek syn- (together) + chronos (time) via French 'synchronisation'.
