blindly

1. ADV

/ ˈblaɪndli / (AmE), / ˈblaɪndli / (BrE)
Without being able to see or without looking; in a way that involves no use of sight.
without seeing or looking

Examples

She reached blindly for her glasses in the dark.
He stumbled blindly down the hallway after the lights went out.
The cat pawed blindly at the toy under the couch.

Synset Examples

he felt around his desk blindly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sightlessly - without seeing; lacking sight
  • Antonym: visually - in a way that relates to sight or vision

Etymology

From 'blind' + '-ly', forming the adverb from the adjective 'blind'.

2. ADV

/ ˈblaɪndli / (AmE), / ˈblaɪndli / (BrE)
Without thinking, planning, or considering the consequences; without a rational basis or due preparation.
without thought or consideration

Examples

She followed his advice blindly, not questioning his motives at all.
They invested their money blindly, without researching the company first.
Making decisions blindly can often lead to regrettable mistakes.

Synset Examples

they bought the car blindly
he picked a wife blindly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recklessly - without regard for danger or consequences
  • Antonym: thoughtfully - with careful consideration or attention

Etymology

From 'blind' + '-ly', forming the adverb from the adjective.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: r
