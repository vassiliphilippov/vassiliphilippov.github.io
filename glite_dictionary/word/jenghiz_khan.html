Jenghiz Khan

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒɛŋɡɪz ˈkɑn / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛŋɡɪz ˈkɑːn / (BrE)
the Mongolian emperor (c. 1162–1227) who founded the Mongol Empire and led its expansion across Asia and into Europe
Mongolian emperor, founder of the Mongol Empire

Examples

Historians consider Jenghiz Khan one of the most influential military leaders in world history.
The armies led by Jenghiz Khan conquered vast territories across Asia and Europe.
Legends about Jenghiz Khan have inspired countless books, films, and scholarly studies.

Etymology

From Mongolian Чингис хаан (Chingis khaan), meaning 'universal ruler.'
