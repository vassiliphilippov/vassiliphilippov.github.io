to unsay

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈseɪ / (AmE), / ʌnˈseɪ / (BrE)
to retract or withdraw something that one has previously said
retract or withdraw a statement

Examples

She wished she could unsay the harsh things she had blurted out.
Once the words are spoken, you can't simply unsay them.
He quickly realized his mistake and tried to unsay his accusation.

Synset Examples

He swallowed his words

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to retract - to withdraw a statement or accusation
  • Synonym: to withdraw - to take back something that was said or offered

Etymology

From un- (reverse action) + say.
