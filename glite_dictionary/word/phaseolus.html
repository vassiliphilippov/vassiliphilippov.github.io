Phaseolus

1. NOUN

/ fəˈziː.əl.əs / (AmE), / fəˈziː.əl.əs / (BrE)
a genus of herbs native to warm regions, including most common American beans such as kidney, pinto, and black beans
genus of bean plants

Examples

The genus Phaseolus includes many species cultivated for their edible beans.
Researchers are studying wild Phaseolus populations to improve crop resilience.
Several varieties of Phaseolus are grown in Central and South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bean - the edible seed or pod of plants like those in the genus Phaseolus

Etymology

From Latin 'phaseolus' (a kind of bean), diminutive of Greek 'phasēlos'.
