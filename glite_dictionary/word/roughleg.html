roughleg

1. NOUN

/ ˈrʌfˌlɛɡ / (AmE), / ˈrʌf.lɛɡ / (BrE)
A large hawk of the Northern Hemisphere, noted for its feathered legs, that preys mainly on small rodents and benefits farmers by controlling pests.
large rodent-eating hawk with feathered legs

Examples

The roughleg is often seen soaring over open fields in search of mice or voles.
Farmers appreciate the roughleg because it helps control rodent populations on their land.
We spotted a roughleg perched atop a fence post, scanning the ground for prey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rough-legged buzzard - another term for roughleg, species of large buzzard in the Northern Hemisphere

Etymology

From 'rough' + 'leg', referring to the bird's feathered legs.
