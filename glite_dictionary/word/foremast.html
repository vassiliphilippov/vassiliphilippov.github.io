foremast

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrˌmæst / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌmɑːst / (BrE)
the mast nearest the bow in a ship with two or more masts
mast nearest the bow of a ship

Examples

The foremast was damaged during the storm, requiring immediate repairs.
Sailors climbed the foremast to adjust the sails for better wind.
The ship's foremast stood taller than any other up front.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: mainmast - the principal mast, usually the second from the bow

Etymology

From fore- (meaning 'front') and mast (from Old English mæst, 'pole, mast').
