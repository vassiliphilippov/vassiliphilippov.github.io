soberness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊbərnəs / (AmE), / ˈsəʊbənəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of not being affected by alcohol; the condition of being sober
state of not being intoxicated

Examples

She was proud of her soberness after years of struggling with addiction.
The driver's soberness was essential for everyone's safety on the road.
After the party, his soberness made him the responsible one to get everyone home.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sobriety - the state of being sober, especially from alcohol
  • Antonym: intoxication - the state of being intoxicated, especially from alcohol or drugs

Etymology

From sober + -ness, with 'sober' from Old French sobre, from Latin sobrius.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊbɚnəs / (AmE), / ˈsəʊbənəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being serious, solemn, or thoughtful in manner or attitude
quality of being serious or solemn

Examples

Despite the festive mood, her soberness reminded everyone of the importance of the occasion.
He conducted the meeting with an air of soberness that impressed the board members.
The soberness in his tone made it clear that the situation was no laughing matter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seriousness - the quality of being serious in manner or attitude
  • Synonym: gravity - seriousness of manner; solemnity
  • Antonym: levity - lack of seriousness, lightness in manner

Etymology

From sober + -ness, modeled on Old French 'sobrieté' and Latin 'sobrietas'.
