entail

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈteɪl / (AmE), / ɪnˈteɪl / (BrE)
Land or estate limited to a particular heir or line of heirs by fee tail.
land received by fee tail
legal

Examples

The family entail ensured the estate would pass only to male heirs.
When their father died, the entail transferred ownership of the manor to the eldest brother.
In literature, conflicts often arise due to an entail restricting who can inherit property.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fee tail - an inheritable estate in land restricted to certain heirs

Etymology

From Middle English 'entailen' in the sense of setting legal limitations on inheritance, from Anglo-French 'entailler' (to cut, carve), referring to restricting succession.

3. VERB

/ ɪnˈteɪl / (AmE), / ɪnˈteɪl / (BrE)
(Law) To limit the inheritance of property to a specified line of heirs, often so that it cannot be sold or otherwise disposed of.
restrict inheritance to specific heirs (legal)
legal

Examples

The family decided to entail the estate, ensuring it remained within their lineage for generations.
Under the old system, landowners could entail property, restricting who could inherit it.
To preserve the family fortune, the patriarch entailed his lands to his eldest son.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to bequeath - to leave property to someone in a will
  • Antonym: to inherit - to receive property as an heir

Etymology

From Old French *entailler* (to carve, cut) and Medieval Latin *intagliare*, influenced by the idea of carving out a specific succession.

5. NOUN

/ ɪnˈteɪl / (AmE), / ɪnˈteɪl / (BrE)
(law) The act of restricting the inheritance of property to certain heirs, creating a fee tail from a fee simple.
legal act of restricting inheritance
legal

Examples

The entail on the estate prevented it from being sold outside the family.
He challenged the entail in court, arguing for his right to inherit.
Many English manors were governed by an entail that favored male heirs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fee tail - an estate in land limited to specified heirs

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'entaliare', from Latin 'in-' (in) + 'taliare' (to cut).
