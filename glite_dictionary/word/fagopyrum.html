Fagopyrum

1. NOUN

/ fəˈɡɑːpɪˌrʌm / (AmE), / fəˈɡɒpɪˌrʌm / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the family Polygonaceae, which includes buckwheat.
plant genus including buckwheat

Examples

The genus Fagopyrum is best known for including buckwheat, an important food crop.
Several species within Fagopyrum are cultivated for their edible seeds.
Botanists often debate the correct classification of Fagopyrum within the Polygonaceae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buckwheat - an edible seed and the plant it comes from, commonly used as a cereal substitute

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, based on Latin 'fagus' (beech) and Greek 'pyron' (wheat).
