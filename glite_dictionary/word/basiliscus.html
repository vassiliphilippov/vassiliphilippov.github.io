Basiliscus

1. NOUN

/ bəˈsɪlɪskəs / (AmE), / ˌbæsɪˈlɪskəs / (BrE)
A genus of large, tropical American lizards of the family Iguanidae, commonly known as basilisks.
genus of lizards called basilisks

Examples

The Basiliscus genus is renowned for its members' ability to run across water surfaces.
Biologists observed a Basiliscus basking on a branch near the riverbank.
The male Basiliscus displays a prominent crest along its back and head.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basilisk - any lizard of the genus Basiliscus, known for running on water

Etymology

From Latin 'basiliscus', from Ancient Greek 'basiliskos' meaning 'little king'; originally referring to the mythical basilisk and later applied to the lizard genus.

2. NOUN

/ bəˈsɪlɪskəs / (AmE), / bəˈsɪlɪskəs / (BrE)
a genus of large, tropical lizards known as basilisks, native to Central and South America, notable for their ability to run across water surfaces
genus of lizards (basilisks)

Examples

The Basiliscus genus includes several species of lizards known for their unique locomotion.
Scientists documented a new behavior in a Basiliscus lizard observed in its rainforest habitat.
Members of the Basiliscus genus are often called 'Jesus lizards' because of their water-walking ability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: basilisk - a large tropical American lizard capable of running on water

Etymology

From Latin 'basiliscus' meaning 'little king', originally from Greek 'basilískos', diminutive of 'basileus' meaning 'king'.
