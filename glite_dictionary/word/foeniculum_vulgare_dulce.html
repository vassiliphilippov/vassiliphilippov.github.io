Foeniculum vulgare dulce

1. NOUN

/ foʊˌɛnɪkjuːləm vʌlˈɡɛər i ˈdʊl tʃeɪ / (AmE), / fəʊˌɛnɪkjuːləm vʌlˈɡɑːrɪ ˈdʊl tʃeɪ / (BrE)
A cultivated variety of fennel grown particularly for its edible, aromatic, bulbous stem base, commonly known as Florence fennel or finocchio.
cultivated fennel with edible bulbous stem base

Examples

Florentine cooks often use Foeniculum vulgare dulce in salads and pasta dishes for its unique flavor.
The farmer grows Foeniculum vulgare dulce alongside other Mediterranean herbs each spring.
You can slice Foeniculum vulgare dulce thinly and roast it to bring out its natural sweetness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fennel - an aromatic plant of the parsley family; its stalks and seeds are used in cooking
  • Synonym: Florence fennel - another name for Foeniculum vulgare dulce; the bulbous edible variety of fennel

Etymology

Latin botanical name meaning 'sweet fennel.'
