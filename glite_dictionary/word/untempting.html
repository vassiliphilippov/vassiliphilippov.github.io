untempting

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtɛmptɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈtɛmptɪŋ / (BrE)
not appealing or attractive; not causing a desire to have or do something.
not attractive or desirable

Examples

The food looked rather untempting after sitting out all night.
She found the grey, windowless room completely untempting.
Despite the special offers, the products remained untempting to most customers.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tempting - appealing or inviting

Etymology

Formed with the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'tempting' from Latin temptare (to try, to attempt).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtɛmp.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈtɛmpt.ɪŋ / (BrE)
not attractive or appealing to the senses; lacking temptation or allure.
not appealing or attractive

Examples

The untempting leftovers remained untouched in the refrigerator for days.
She stared at the untempting plate of cold vegetables and sighed.
Despite the fancy packaging, the meal looked rather untempting.

Synset Examples

untempting food

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tempting - attractive or appealing to the senses

Etymology

From un- (not) + tempting (from Latin temptare, to try).
