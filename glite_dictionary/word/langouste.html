langouste

1. NOUN

/ lænˈɡuːst / (AmE), / lɑ̃ˈɡuːst / (BrE)
A warm-water spiny lobster without large claws, commonly found in Australia, South Africa, Florida, and California, often marketed as frozen tails.
spiny lobster without claws, warm waters

Examples

The chef prepared a grilled langouste with garlic butter for the dinner special.
Fresh langoustes are a delicacy in many coastal restaurants along the Mediterranean.
He ordered a frozen langouste tail imported from Australia for the holiday meal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spiny lobster - a lobster of the family Palinuridae, having no large claws and a spiny body

Etymology

From French 'langouste', from Latin 'locusta' (locust, lobster).

2. NOUN

/ lænˈguːst / (AmE), / lɒŋˈɡuːst / (BrE)
a large edible marine crustacean of warm seas, with a spiny shell and no large claws, also known as a spiny lobster
spiny lobster; clawless edible crustacean

Examples

The chef prepared a delicious langouste with garlic butter and herbs.
In some regions, langouste is considered a delicacy valued even above lobster.
We ordered grilled langouste while dining at the seaside restaurant.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lobster - large marine crustacean with claws, commonly eaten as seafood

Etymology

From French 'langouste', from Old Provençal 'langosta', originally from Latin 'locusta' (locust, lobster).
