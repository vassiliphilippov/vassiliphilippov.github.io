ultracentrifugation

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌltrəsɛntrɪfjuˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌʌltrəsɛntrɪfjuˈɡeɪʃn̩ / (BrE)
The process of separating substances by spinning them at very high speeds in a centrifuge, especially to separate particles of different densities or sizes.
spinning samples at very high speeds
medical

Examples

The laboratory technician used ultracentrifugation to isolate the viral particles from the blood sample.
Researchers often rely on ultracentrifugation when separating proteins based on their size and density.
The accuracy of the results depends largely on the proper technique during ultracentrifugation.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: centrifugation - the process of separating substances using a centrifuge

Etymology

Formed from 'ultra-' (beyond) and 'centrifugation' (from Latin 'centrum' + 'fugare', to cause to flee).
