unnaturally

1. ADV

/ ʌnˈnætʃɚəli / (AmE), / ʌnˈnatʃrəli / (BrE)
in a way that is not natural or normal; by artificial or abnormal means
in an unnatural way

Examples

The colors in the photograph appeared unnaturally bright.
After the operation, he was unnaturally calm, which worried his doctors.
The cake tasted unnaturally sweet due to too much sugar substitute.

Synset Examples

artificially induced conditions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artificially - by artificial means; not naturally
  • Antonym: naturally - in a natural way; according to nature

2. ADV

/ ʌnˈnætʃrəli / (AmE), / ʌnˈnatʃrəli / (BrE)
In a way that is not normal, usual, or expected; in a manner that seems strange or artificial.
in an unnatural or abnormal way

Examples

His voice sounded unnaturally calm after hearing the bad news.
The sky was unnaturally green before the storm hit the town.
She smiled unnaturally, making everyone at the table uncomfortable.

Synset Examples

his other arm lay across his chest, unnaturally, as if placed there deliberately, for a purpose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artificially - In a way that is not natural, often produced by humans

3. ADV

/ ʌnˈnætʃɚəli / (AmE), / ʌnˈnatʃrəli / (BrE)
In a way that is different from what is normal or expected; in a manner that is not natural.
in a way that is not natural or normal

Examples

He smiled unnaturally, as if forcing himself to seem happy.
Her voice sounded unnaturally calm during the emergency.
The plant grew unnaturally large because of the special fertilizer.

Synset Examples

The early Church not unnaturally adopted the position that failure to see the messianic character of his work was really caused by the people's own blindness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artificially - in a way that is not natural, but made by people
  • Antonym: naturally - in a manner expected in nature or in normal conditions

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) + 'natural' + '-ly' (adverbial suffix).
