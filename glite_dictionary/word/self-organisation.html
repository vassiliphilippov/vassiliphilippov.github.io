self-organisation

1. NOUN

/ sɛlf ˌɔrɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / sɛlf ˌɔːɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of organizing oneself or one's own activities, often without external direction or control
act of organizing oneself or own group
british

Examples

The company's shift toward self-organisation empowered employees to take more initiative and responsibility for their work.
Many grassroots movements rely on self-organisation to address issues in their communities.
Training programs focus on self-organisation to help individuals manage their own professional development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-organization - act or process of organizing oneself (US spelling)
  • Degree Lower: organization - act of arranging or structuring something

Etymology

Formed from 'self-' (oneself) and 'organisation', from French 'organisation' and Latin 'organizatio'.
