strongroom

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrɔŋˌruːm / (AmE), / ˈstrɒŋˌruːm / (BrE)
A specially reinforced room designed to be burglarproof and fireproof, used for storing valuables securely.
secure, reinforced room for valuables

Examples

The bank's strongroom is protected by an alarm system and several layers of security.
Only authorized personnel were allowed access to the strongroom after hours.
The rare jewels are kept in a heavily guarded strongroom beneath the museum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vault - a secure room or compartment for valuables, often in banks

Etymology

From 'strong' + 'room'; in use since the 18th century to denote a secure room.
