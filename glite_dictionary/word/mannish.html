mannish

1. ADJ

/ ˈmænɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈmænɪʃ / (BrE)
(of a woman or her behavior or appearance) having qualities, traits, or characteristics considered typical of a man; masculine.
having qualities typical of a man

Examples

Her clothing style was considered a bit too mannish for the office in the 1950s.
The actress adopted a mannish swagger to portray her character convincingly.
He made a mannish joke that some people found old-fashioned.

Synset Examples

true mannish arrogance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: masculine - having qualities traditionally associated with men
  • Antonym: feminine - having qualities traditionally associated with women

Etymology

From man + -ish, meaning 'having qualities of or resembling a man'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈmænɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈmænɪʃ / (BrE)
(of a woman or her behavior, appearance, etc.) having qualities, characteristics, or a style typically associated with men.
having qualities typically considered masculine

Examples

Her suit and tie gave her a distinctly mannish look.
Some critics described her deep voice as sounding slightly mannish.
She adopted a mannish style, preferring practical shoes and short haircuts.

Synset Examples

a mannish stride

Related Terms

  • Synonym: masculine - having qualities or appearance traditionally associated with men
  • Antonym: feminine - having qualities or appearance traditionally associated with women

Etymology

From 'man' + '-ish', meaning 'like a man'.
