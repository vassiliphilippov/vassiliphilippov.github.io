Pythium debaryanum

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪθiəm diːˈbæriənəm / (AmE), / ˈpɪθiəm dɪˌbæriˈɑːnəm / (BrE)
a species of fungus that causes damping off disease in seedlings
fungus causing damping off in seedlings
medical

Examples

Pythium debaryanum is a common cause of seedling diseases in greenhouses worldwide.
Seedlings infected by Pythium debaryanum often wilt and collapse within days of germination.
Proper soil sterilization can help prevent outbreaks of Pythium debaryanum in nurseries.

Etymology

From genus name Pythium and the species name honoring French mycologist Louis Debary.
