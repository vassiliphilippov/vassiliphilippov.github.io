dispensability

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˌpensəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / dɪsˌpensəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality of being able to be done without or not essential.
ability to be dispensed with

Examples

The dispensability of certain features made the device more cost-effective.
Some questioned the dispensability of the extra staff during the budget cuts.
He was worried about his dispensability after the automation project was announced.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indispensability - the quality of being absolutely necessary

Etymology

From Latin 'dispensare' (to distribute) plus the suffix '-ability'.
