steal

1. VERB

/ stil / (AmE), / stiﾋ人 / (BrE)
to take something that does not belong to you without the owner's permission, especially secretly or by force
take without permission

Examples

Someone managed to steal my bike from the garage last night.
He tried to steal a glance at the answers during the test.
Thieves often steal valuables from unlocked cars.

Synset Examples

Someone stole my wallet on the train
This author stole entire paragraphs from my dissertation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to rob - to take property unlawfully, especially by force or threat
  • Antonym: give - to freely hand over or transfer possession to someone

Etymology

From Old English stelan (to commit theft), related to German stehlen.

2. NOUN

/ stil / (AmE), / stiﾋ人 / (BrE)
(in baseball) an act of advancing to the next base while a pitch is being thrown, without the help of a hit, walk, or error.
(baseball) act of advancing a base during a pitch

Examples

The player's quick steal in the ninth inning changed the outcome of the game.
With his record number of steals, he became a legend in baseball history.
She attempted a bold steal of second base during the playoff match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stolen base - an instance of successfully stealing a base in baseball

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ stil / (AmE), / stiﾋ人 / (BrE)
an unusually good bargain; something bought at a price much lower than its real value
an excellent bargain or deal
informal

Examples

That antique vase was a real steal at only twenty dollars.
Tickets to the concert were selling for half-price, which was an absolute steal.
I can't believe I got these designer shoes for such a steal at the outlet mall.

Synset Examples

she got a bargain at the auction
the stock was a real buy at that price

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bargain - something bought for less than the usual price
  • Antonym: rip-off - something that is unreasonably expensive
Source: WordNet

4. VERB

/ stil / (AmE), / stiﾋ人 / (BrE)
to move somewhere quietly and secretly so as not to be seen or heard
move quietly and secretly

Examples

She tried to steal out of the room without waking anyone.
He stole down the hallway, careful not to make a sound.
A cat steals across the garden at night, hunting for mice.

Synset Examples

The ship slipped away in the darkness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sneak - to move quietly and secretly, often to avoid being noticed
