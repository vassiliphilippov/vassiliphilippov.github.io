Au

1. NOUN

/ eɪ ˈjuː / (AmE), / eɪ ˈjuː / (BrE)
the chemical symbol for gold, a soft, yellow, precious metallic element
chemical symbol for gold

Examples

The chemical symbol Au stands for gold on the periodic table.
Jewelry made from Au is valued for its beauty and rarity.
Scientists analyzed the rock and detected traces of Au within it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gold - a soft yellow precious metal, chemical element with symbol Au

Etymology

From Latin 'aurum' (gold), the origin of the element's symbol.
