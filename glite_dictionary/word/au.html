AU

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ ˈju / (AmE), / ˌeɪ ˈjuː / (BrE)
a unit of length equal to the mean distance between the Earth and the Sun, used in astronomy (about 93 million miles or 150 million kilometers)
astronomical unit; mean Earth-Sun distance

Examples

The spacecraft traveled more than 2 AU from the Sun during its mission.
Astronomers often express distances within the solar system in AU.
Earth is about 1 AU away from the Sun by definition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: light-year - a unit of astronomical distance equal to the distance light travels in one year

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'astronomical unit'.
