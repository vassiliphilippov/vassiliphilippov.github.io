Lilium pardalinum

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪliəm pɑːˈdælɪnəm / (AmE), / ˈlɪlɪəm pɑːˈdælɪnəm / (BrE)
A species of lily native to western United States, known for its orange-red to crimson flowers spotted with maroon.
a lily of western US with spotted orange-red flowers

Examples

The vibrant blossoms of the Lilium pardalinum attract butterflies to many Californian gardens.
She planted Lilium pardalinum along the border for its dramatic, spotted flowers.
Many hikers admire the wild Lilium pardalinum during its summer bloom in the mountains.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lily - a flowering plant of the genus Lilium

Etymology

From Latin: 'Lilium' meaning 'lily' and 'pardalinum' from Greek 'pardalis' (leopard), referring to the flower's spotted appearance.
