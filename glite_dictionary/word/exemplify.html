to exemplify

1. VERB

/ ɪɡˈzɛmpləˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɛmplɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to illustrate or clarify something by giving an example
illustrate by an example

Examples

The teacher used a real-world scenario to exemplify the complex theory.
Her actions exemplify the company's commitment to customer service.
Several case studies exemplify the benefits of adopting new technology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to illustrate - to explain or decorate something by using pictures or examples
  • Synonym: to clarify - to make something clear or easier to understand

Etymology

From Medieval Latin exemplificare, from Latin exemplum (example).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01023137-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪɡˈzɛmplɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɛmplɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to be a typical example of; to illustrate or represent something as a typical case
be a typical example of

Examples

Her achievements exemplify the dedication required to succeed in this field.
The building exemplifies the best of modern architecture.
This painting perfectly exemplifies the style of the Impressionist movement.

Synset Examples

This compositional style is exemplified by this fugue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to typify - to be a typical example of

Etymology

From Medieval Latin exemplificare, from Latin exemplum (example) + -ficare (to make).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02729608-v
Synset POS: v
