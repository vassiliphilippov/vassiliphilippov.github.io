premiere

1. NOUN

/ prəˈmɪr / (AmE), / prɪˈmɪə / (BrE)
the first public performance or showing of a play, film, or piece of music
first public performance or showing

Examples

The film had its premiere at the Cannes Film Festival.
Tickets for the premiere sold out within hours of being released.
She wore a stunning dress to the Broadway premiere of the musical.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opening night - the first performance of a play

Etymology

From French 'première' (first, feminine of 'premier'), from Latin 'primarius' (of the first rank).

2. ADJ

/ prəˈmɪr / (AmE), / prəˈmɪə / (BrE)
preceding all others in time; earliest or first, especially in order or appearance
earliest; first in time or order

Examples

The film had its premiere screening at the festival.
We were invited to the premiere performance of the play.
The premiere episode set the tone for the rest of the series.

Synset Examples

the premiere showing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: first - coming before all others in time or order
  • Antonym: last - coming after all others in time or order

Etymology

From French 'première' (feminine of 'premier', meaning 'first').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01016452-s
Synset POS: s

3. VERB

/ prəˈmɪr / (AmE), / prɛˈmɪə / (BrE)
to perform or show a play, film, piece of music, etc. for the first time
to show or perform for the first time

Examples

The new play will premiere at the city theater next month.
The director premiered his latest film at the international festival.
The orchestra is premiering a symphony composed especially for the event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: debut - to perform or present for the first time; first public appearance

Etymology

From French 'première' (first performance), feminine of 'premier' (first).
