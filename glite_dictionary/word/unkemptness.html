unkemptness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈkɛmptnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈkɛmptnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being untidy or not cared for
state of being untidy

Examples

The unkemptness of his hair suggested he had just woken up.
Her room's unkemptness drove her mother crazy.
Years of neglect had resulted in the unkemptness of the once-beautiful garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: untidiness - the state of being untidy
  • Antonym: tidiness - the state of being neat and orderly

Etymology

Derived from 'unkempt' (not combed, untidy), with the suffix '-ness' forming a noun.
