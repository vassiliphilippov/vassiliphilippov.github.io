svelte

1. ADJ

/ svɛlt / (AmE), / svɛlt / (BrE)
thin, graceful, and attractively slender in appearance or build; elegantly slim.
thin and graceful in appearance

Examples

The dancer's svelte figure allowed her to move elegantly across the stage.
He admired her svelte silhouette in the evening gown at the party.
After months of training, the athlete became noticeably more svelte and toned.

Synset Examples

she was slender as a willow shoot is slender
a slim girl with straight blonde hair
watched her slight figure cross the street

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slender - gracefully thin; slim in form
  • Degree Lower: lanky - ungracefully thin and tall
  • Antonym: plump - slightly fat in a pleasant way

Etymology

From French 'svelte', from Italian 'svelto' (slender, lithe), past participle of 'svellere' (to stretch out).
