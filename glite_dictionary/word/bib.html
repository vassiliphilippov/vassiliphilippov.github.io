bib

1. NOUN

/ bɪb / (AmE), / bɪb / (BrE)
A piece of cloth or plastic fastened under a child's chin to protect clothing while eating.
protective cloth for feeding children

Examples

Don't forget to put a bib on the baby before feeding her.
The toddler's shirt stayed clean thanks to his colorful bib.
She wiped her mouth with her bib after finishing her meal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: napkin - a piece of cloth or paper used at meals to protect the clothes and wipe the mouth

Etymology

From Middle English bibben (to drink), perhaps imitative of the movement of drinking.

2. NOUN

/ bɪb / (AmE), / bɪb / (BrE)
The top part of an apron or similar garment that covers the chest.
top chest-covering part of an apron

Examples

The bib of her apron was splattered with flour after baking all afternoon.
He tucked the bib over his shirt to avoid any stains while painting.
The uniform includes an apron with a white bib for a traditional look.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apron - a protective garment covering the front of the body

Etymology

From Middle English bibben (to drink), bib (piece of cloth to protect clothes when drinking), possibly imitative.

drink alcohol moderately and regularly
