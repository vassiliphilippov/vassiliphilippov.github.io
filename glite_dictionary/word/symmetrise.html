to symmetrise

1. VERB

/ ˈsɪmɪˌtraɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪmɪtraɪz / (BrE)
To make something symmetric; to organize or arrange so that parts correspond or are balanced in form, structure, or arrangement.
make symmetric; arrange symmetrically
british

Examples

The scientist used a mathematical technique to symmetrise the data set for clearer analysis.
Architects often symmetrise building designs to achieve visual harmony.
She carefully symmetrised the pattern before starting to paint.

Synset Examples

symmetrized waves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to symmetrize - make symmetric (American spelling)
  • Antonym: asymmetrize - to make asymmetric

Etymology

From symmetry + -ise, modeled after French symétriser.
