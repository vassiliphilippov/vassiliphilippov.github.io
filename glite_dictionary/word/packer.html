packer

1. NOUN

/ ˈpækər / (AmE), / ˈpakə / (BrE)
a person or company that prepares and packages meat for sale, especially as a wholesaler in the meat industry.
meat industry wholesaler or company

Examples

The large packer supplies beef to supermarkets nationwide.
Labor negotiations at the local packer have caused meat shortages in the region.
Many ranchers sell their cattle directly to a packer for processing and distribution.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpækər / (AmE), / ˈpakə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to put goods or products into containers, boxes, or packages for storage or transport
person who packs goods for shipping or storage

Examples

The warehouse hired several new packers before the holiday rush.
Each packer was responsible for labeling every box correctly.
She worked as a packer on the night shift, preparing shipments for delivery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: packer - machine that packs items into containers

Etymology

From the verb 'pack' + agent noun suffix '-er'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈpækər / (AmE), / ˈpakə / (BrE)
a person who carries a backpack while hiking, especially over long distances or in wilderness areas
person who hikes with a backpack

Examples

On the trail, every experienced packer knows how to keep their load light and efficient.
As a seasoned packer, she always chooses her gear with care before a long hike.
The national park requires every packer to follow strict guidelines to minimize their impact on the environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: backpacker - a person who travels or hikes with a backpack
