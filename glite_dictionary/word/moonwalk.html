moonwalk

1. NOUN

/ ˈmuːnˌwɑk / (AmE), / ˈmuːnˌwɔːk / (BrE)
a dance step in which the dancer glides backward while appearing to walk forward
dance move creating the illusion of gliding

Examples

He amazed the audience when he performed the moonwalk with flawless precision.
Learning the moonwalk is a rite of passage for many aspiring dancers.
The popularity of the moonwalk soared after it appeared on live television.

Synset Examples

Michael Jackson perfected the moonwalk in the 1980s

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to backslide - to move backward smoothly; a similar gliding dance step

Etymology

Named after the moonwalk, an illusion of moving without apparent motion, popularized by Michael Jackson in the 1980s.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmunˌwɑk / (AmE), / ˈmuːnˌwɔːk / (BrE)
an exploratory walk made by an astronaut on the surface of the moon
astronaut's walk on the moon

Examples

The first moonwalk in 1969 was watched by millions around the world.
Neil Armstrong's famous words were spoken during his initial moonwalk.
The training for a moonwalk includes hours spent in a simulated lunar environment.
