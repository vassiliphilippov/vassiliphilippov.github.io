excogitation

1. NOUN

/ ɛkˌskɑdʒɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ɛkˌskɒdʒɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of thinking out, devising, or mentally creating something; deep thought or intellectual consideration
the act of thinking out or devising

Examples

Her excogitation led to a groundbreaking theory in quantum physics.
After hours of excogitation, he finally solved the puzzle.
This invention was the fruit of long and careful excogitation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invention - the creation of something new, especially a device or process
  • Synonym: reflection - serious thought or consideration

Etymology

From Latin excogitatio (deliberation, invention), from excogitare (to think out).

2. NOUN

/ ɛkˌskɑːdʒɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ɛkˌskɒdʒɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of thinking something out thoroughly and carefully, especially to achieve full understanding or a solution
careful and thorough thinking

Examples

After much excogitation, the committee finally reached a decision.
The invention was the result of her careful excogitation over many years.
He appreciated the depth of excogitation evident in her argument.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reflection - serious thought or consideration
  • Synonym: contemplation - thoughtful observation or study
  • Antonym: impulsiveness - acting without forethought

Etymology

From Latin excogitatio (invention, contrivance), from excogitare (to think out).
