acephalia

1. NOUN

/ eɪˌsɛˈfeɪliə / (AmE), / ˌæsɪˈfeɪlɪə / (BrE)
the absence of a head, especially as a congenital condition in certain organisms or pathological cases
absence of a head (medical/biological)
medical

Examples

The rare condition known as acephalia results in the total absence of a head in the developing embryo.
Some parasitic organisms display acephalia as part of their abnormal development.
The pathology report identified acephalia as the cause of the severe congenital deformity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acephaly - absence of a head

Etymology

From Greek a- (without) + kephalē (head).
