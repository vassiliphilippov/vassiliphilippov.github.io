antiphonal

1. NOUN

/ ænˈtɪf.ə.nəl / (AmE), / ænˈtɪf.ə.nəl / (BrE)
A book containing a collection of antiphons, typically used in Christian liturgical music.
book of liturgical antiphons

Examples

The choir director removed the old antiphonal from the shelf to prepare for the evening service.
Rare manuscripts, including a richly illuminated antiphonal, were on display at the museum.
Each monk was given an antiphonal to follow during the chanting of the prayers.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin antiphonale, related to antiphona (antiphon).

2. ADJ

/ ænˈtɪf.ə.nəl / (AmE), / ænˈtɪf.ə.nəl / (BrE)
(of music, singing, or speech) characterized by alternating responses or parts, especially by two groups or voices
characterized by alternating responses

Examples

The choir performed an antiphonal piece, with voices echoing from opposite sides of the church.
Their antiphonal shouts of joy filled the stadium during the final minutes of the game.
Her laughter was matched by an antiphonal chuckle from the other side of the room.

Synset Examples

responsive reading
antiphonal laughter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: responsive - reacting quickly and positively; responsive
  • Antonym: unison - performed or spoken together at the same time

Etymology

From Late Latin antiphonalis, from Greek antiphōnalos, relating to antiphon ('verse sung responsively').

3. ADJ

/ ænˈtɪf.ə.nəl / (AmE), / ænˈtɪf.ə.nəl / (BrE)
Relating to or resembling an antiphon or the responsive singing or chanting of a choir, especially in church services.
relating to an antiphon or responsive singing

Examples

The choir performed an antiphonal piece, with voices echoing from opposite sides of the cathedral.
During the ceremony, the priests and congregation engaged in antiphonal chanting.
The composer's use of antiphonal arrangements created a dramatic effect in the concert hall.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antiphon - a short chant in Christian ritual, sung as a refrain
  • Synonym: antiphony - responsive singing by two groups, especially in liturgy

Etymology

From medieval Latin 'antiphonalis', from 'antiphona' (antiphon).
