appendix

1. NOUN

/ əˈpɛndɪks / (AmE), / əˈpɛndɪks / (BrE)
a section of supplementary material at the end of a book, article, or document, providing additional information
supplementary material at book's end

Examples

Before starting the test, please review the appendix for a list of all relevant formulas.
All charts and raw data used in the study are included in the appendix.
The book contains an appendix that explains technical terms in detail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annex - supplementary material added at the end of a document

Etymology

From Latin *appendix* (an addition, something attached), from *appendere* (to hang upon).

2. NOUN

/ əˈpɛndɪks / (AmE), / əˈpɛndɪks / (BrE)
a small, tube-shaped pouch attached to the lower end of the cecum in the human body, which has no vital function
small pouch in the lower intestine
medical

Examples

The surgeon removed her inflamed appendix before it could rupture.
A burst appendix can cause a severe abdominal infection.
Doctors are not certain why the human body still has an appendix.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cecum - the first part of the large intestine

Etymology

From Latin appendix (an addition, appendage), from appendere (to hang upon).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05545209-n
Synset POS: n
