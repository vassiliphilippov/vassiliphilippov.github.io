to communize

1. VERB

/ ˈkɑːmjəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkɒmjʊnaɪz / (BrE)
to make property or resources owned collectively, especially by the state
make property owned collectively by the state

Examples

After the revolution, the new regime sought to communize agricultural land throughout the region.
Efforts to communize the factories were met with resistance from local workers.
The party's platform aimed to communize industries considered vital to national security.

Synset Examples

The new government communized all banks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to nationalize - to bring under state ownership or control
  • Antonym: to privatize - to transfer from public or state ownership to private control

Etymology

From French communiser, from commun (common).

2. VERB

/ ˈkɑːmjəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkɒmjʊnaɪz / (BrE)
to make something Communist or bring it into accordance with Communist principles
make or convert to Communist

Examples

The new regime sought to communize the entire economy within five years.
Radical leaders attempted to communize the land owned by wealthy farmers.
Some historians debate whether it is possible to communize a society without force.

Synset Examples

communize the government

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to nationalize - to transfer from private to state ownership or control
  • Antonym: to privatize - to transfer from public or government control to private ownership

Etymology

From French 'communiser,' from 'communisme' (communism) + '-ize.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00410206-v
Synset POS: v
