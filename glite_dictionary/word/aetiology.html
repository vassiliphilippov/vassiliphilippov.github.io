aetiology

1. NOUN

/ ˌiːtiˈɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌiːtiˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the philosophical study of the causes or origins of things, especially within a conceptual or abstract framework
the study of causes (philosophy)

Examples

The discussion turned toward the aetiology of existence in ancient Greek philosophy.
He wrote a treatise on the aetiology of natural phenomena.
Questions of aetiology are central to debates about the origins of the universe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: causality - the relationship between cause and effect

Etymology

From Greek 'aitiologia', from 'aitia' (cause) + 'logia' (study).

2. NOUN

/ ˌiːtiˈɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌiːtɪˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the cause or origin of a disease or abnormal condition
cause or origin of a disease
medical british

Examples

The aetiology of the illness remained a mystery for several years.
Doctors are still researching the aetiology of this rare neurological disorder.
Her paper discussed the aetiology of infectious diseases in urban environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: etiology - cause or origin of a disease (American spelling)

Etymology

From Greek aitiologia (giving a reason), from aitia (cause) + -logia (study).
