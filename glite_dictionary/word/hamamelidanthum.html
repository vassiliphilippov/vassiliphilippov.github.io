Hamamelidanthum

1. NOUN

/ hæməˌmiːlɪˈdænθəm / (AmE), / hæməˌmiːlɪˈdænθəm / (BrE)
A genus of fossil plants from the Oligocene period, with flowers similar to witch hazel, found in the Baltic region.
genus of Oligocene fossil plants

Examples

The extinct plant Hamamelidanthum was first discovered in fossil beds in the Baltic region.
Pollen grains of Hamamelidanthum show remarkable similarities to modern witch hazel species.
Scientists study Hamamelidanthum fossils to learn about Oligocene flora.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hamamelis - genus of flowering plants known as witch hazel

Etymology

From a combination of Hamamelis (the witch hazel genus) and Greek anthos (flower).
