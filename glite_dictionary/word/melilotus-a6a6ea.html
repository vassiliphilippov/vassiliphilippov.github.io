melilotus

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛlɪˈloʊtəs / (AmE), / ˌmɛlɪˈləʊtəs / (BrE)
A genus of erect annual or biennial plants, commonly known as sweet clover, grown especially for hay and soil improvement.
genus of sweet clover plants

Examples

Farmers often plant melilotus to enrich the soil with nitrogen.
The field was covered with blooming melilotus plants, attracting bees and other pollinators.
After harvesting the hay, the melilotus left behind improved the soil for next year's crops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet clover - Common name for melilotus, used especially in agriculture

Etymology

From New Latin, after Greek *melilōtos* (sweet clover), from *meli* (honey) and *lōtos* (lotus).
