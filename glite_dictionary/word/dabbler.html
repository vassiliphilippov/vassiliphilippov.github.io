dabbler

1. NOUN

/ ˈdæb.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈdæb.lə / (BrE)
a type of shallow-water duck that feeds by tipping forward in the water to reach food, rather than diving
shallow-water duck that feeds by dabbling

Examples

Mallards are a common species of dabbler found in ponds and lakes across North America.
Unlike divers, dabblers feed at the water's surface or by tipping forward to reach underwater plants.
The wetlands support many different dabblers, including teals and pintails.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: diver - a type of duck that feeds by diving underwater

2. NOUN

/ ˈdæb.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈdæb.lə / (BrE)
A person who takes part in an activity in a casual or superficial way, without serious commitment or deep knowledge.
person who engages casually in an activity

Examples

He's a dabbler in painting, never taking any formal lessons.
The club welcomes everyone, from professionals to complete dabblers.
Although she tried many hobbies, she was always a dabbler rather than a dedicated enthusiast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amateur - a person who engages in a pursuit on an unpaid rather than professional basis
  • Antonym: expert - a person with comprehensive and authoritative knowledge of or skill in a particular area

Etymology

From verb 'dabble', which possibly derives from frequentative of 'dab'.
