curiosity

1. NOUN

/ ˌkjʊriˈɑsəti / (AmE), / ˌkjʊəriˈɒsəti / (BrE)
A strong desire to learn or know about something; inquisitiveness.
desire to learn or know more

Examples

Children's curiosity often leads them to ask many questions about the world.
Her curiosity about ancient civilizations inspired her to become an archaeologist.
Despite his curiosity, he respected her wish for privacy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inquisitiveness - quality of eagerly seeking knowledge or information
  • Antonym: apathy - lack of interest or enthusiasm

Etymology

From Latin curiositas, from curiosus meaning 'careful, inquisitive'.

2. NOUN

/ kjʊriˈɑːsəti / (AmE), / kjʊəriˈɒsəti / (BrE)
an unusual or interesting object, especially one that is collected or displayed for its rarity or novelty
an unusual or interesting object

Examples

The old cabinet was filled with curiosities from her travels around the world.
At the museum, we saw a curiosity from the Victorian era that fascinated everyone.
The shop specializes in rare books and other curiosities for collectors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oddity - an unusual or peculiar object or person
  • Synonym: rarity - something uncommon, especially a rare object

Etymology

From Latin curiositas (desire of knowledge, inquisitiveness), from curiosus (careful, inquisitive).
