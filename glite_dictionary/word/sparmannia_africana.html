Sparmannia africana

1. NOUN

/ spɑrˈmɑːniə əˈfrɪkənə / (AmE), / spɑːˈmɑːniə əˈfrɪkənə / (BrE)
A large shrub native to South Africa, recognized for its conspicuously hairy branches and leaves, and clusters of prominent white flowers.
large hairy shrub of South Africa

Examples

The botanical garden features a beautiful Sparmannia africana in its South African section.
Sparmannia africana is admired for its clusters of striking white blossoms and hairy leaves.
Gardeners appreciate Sparmannia africana for its fast growth and decorative foliage.

Etymology

Named after Anders Sparrman, a Swedish naturalist, with 'africana' denoting its African origin.
