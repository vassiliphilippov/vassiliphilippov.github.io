mutineer

1. NOUN

/ ˌmjut̬.əˈnɪr / (AmE), / ˌmjuː.tɪˈnɪə / (BrE)
a person, especially a soldier or sailor, who rebels and refuses to obey the orders of those in authority
someone who rebels against authority, especially in the military

Examples

The captain ordered the arrest of the mutineer who had incited the crew to revolt.
During the trial, every mutineer stood silently as the charges were read aloud.
The ship's officers managed to regain control after capturing the leading mutineer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebel - a person who rises in opposition or armed resistance against authority
  • Antonym: loyalist - a person who remains loyal to the established ruler or government

Etymology

From French 'mutiner' (to revolt), from 'mutin' (rebellious).
