Paracheirodon axelrodi

1. NOUN

/ ˌparəkaɪˈroʊdɑn ˌæksəlˈroʊdi / (AmE), / ˌparəkɪəˈrəʊdɒn ˌæksəlˈrəʊdi / (BrE)
a small, brightly colored freshwater fish with red and blue markings, native to streams in Brazil and Colombia, popular in aquariums; also known as the cardinal tetra
a brightly colored freshwater aquarium fish

Examples

Many aquarists choose Paracheirodon axelrodi for its vivid red and blue stripes.
In their natural habitat, Paracheirodon axelrodi swim in large schools along the Rio Negro.
The pet store recommended keeping a group of Paracheirodon axelrodi to create a vibrant display in the tank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cardinal tetra - common name for Paracheirodon axelrodi, a small colorful aquarium fish

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'cheir' (hand) and 'odon' (tooth); species named after Herbert Axelrod, a tropical fish enthusiast.
