Carina

1. NOUN

/ kəˈriːnə / (AmE), / kəˈriːnə / (BrE)
a constellation in the Southern Hemisphere, shaped like a keel and containing the bright star Canopus
constellation in the Southern Hemisphere

Examples

The star Canopus is the brightest star in the Carina constellation.
Astronomers use Carina to help locate other constellations in the southern sky.
Many deep-sky objects have been discovered in Carina by stargazers.

Etymology

From Latin 'carina' meaning 'keel (of a ship)'.
