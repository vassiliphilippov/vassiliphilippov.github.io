nitramine

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪtrəˌmiːn / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtrəˌmiːn / (BrE)
A yellow crystalline explosive compound used in detonators and as a base for certain types of explosives.
crystalline explosive compound

Examples

The engineer handled the nitramine compound with extreme caution due to its sensitivity.
New detonator technology uses nitramine as its primary explosive material.
Laboratory tests showed that nitramine could be synthesized safely under controlled conditions.

Etymology

From nitro- (relating to nitrogen/nitrate) and amine (organic compound containing NH₂ group).
