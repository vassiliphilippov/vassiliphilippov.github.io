quarry

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɔri / (AmE), / ˈkwɒri / (BrE)
an open excavation in the ground from which stone, slate, or minerals are extracted
open pit for extracting stone or slate

Examples

The workers spent the day extracting limestone from the quarry.
Several abandoned quarries have filled with water and become popular swimming spots.
The granite used in the building was sourced from a local quarry.

Synset Examples

a British term for ‘quarry’ is ‘stone pit’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mine - an excavation in the earth for extracting minerals

Etymology

From Old French *quarriere* (stone quarry), from *quarre* (a square, block), from Latin *quadrāria*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkwɔːri / (AmE), / ˈkwɒri / (BrE)
an animal that is hunted or pursued, especially for food
the animal hunted or pursued

Examples

The hunters tracked their quarry through the dense forest until sunset.
The eagle circled high above, searching for its quarry on the ground below.
Elusive and swift, the deer managed to outrun its quarry for another day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prey - an animal hunted and killed by another for food
  • Antonym: predator - an animal that hunts other animals for food

Etymology

From Anglo-French *quirre* (entrails of a deer, placed on the hide and given to dogs) from Old French *cuiriee* (what is put on the hide), from Latin *corium* (skin, hide).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02155533-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈkwɔri / (AmE), / ˈkwɒri / (BrE)
to extract stone or other materials from a quarry
extract stone or minerals

Examples

The workers quarry limestone from the hillside every morning.
Much of the ancient city's marble was quarried nearby.
Modern techniques have made it possible to quarry granite much more efficiently.

Synset Examples

quarry marble

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mine - to extract minerals or other geological materials from the earth

Etymology

From Old French 'quarrie', from Vulgar Latin 'quadrāria' (place where stones are squared), from Latin 'quadrāre' (to make square).

4. NOUN

/ ˈkwɔri / (AmE), / ˈkwɒri / (BrE)
a person or animal that is the object of pursuit, especially one who is being hunted, attacked, or targeted
person or animal being pursued or hunted

Examples

For months, the detective tracked his quarry through the busy city streets.
In the novel, the mysterious figure was both the hunter and the quarry.
The lion stalked its quarry through the tall grass, waiting for the right moment to strike.

Synset Examples

he fell prey to muggers
everyone was fair game
the target of a manhunt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prey - an animal that is hunted or killed by another for food; a person who is easily victimized
  • Synonym: target - a person, object, or place selected as the aim of an attack

Etymology

From Old French *cuiriee* (what is hunted), from Vulgar Latin *corāta* (entrails of animals given to hounds), from Latin *cor* (heart).
