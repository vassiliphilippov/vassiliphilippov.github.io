fullness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʊlnəs / (AmE), / ˈfʊlnəs / (BrE)
the quality of a sensation or experience being rich, deep, and pleasing
rich and pleasing quality in sensation

Examples

The cello's tone had a surprising fullness that captivated the audience.
She admired the fullness of the wine's flavor.
The fullness of the colors in the sunset left everyone speechless.

Synset Examples

the music had a fullness that echoed through the hall
the cheap wine had no body, no mellowness
he was well aware of the richness of his own appearance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: richness - the quality of being rich in sound, taste, or color
  • Antonym: thinness - the quality of lacking richness or density

Etymology

From Old English 'fullnes', from 'full' + '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfʊlnəs / (AmE), / ˈfʊlnəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being filled to capacity; the condition of being full
the state of being full

Examples

After eating a large meal, she enjoyed a sense of fullness and satisfaction.
The fullness of the container made it difficult to carry without spilling.
Spring brings the fullness of life back to the countryside.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: emptiness - the state of being empty
  • Synonym: satiation - the feeling of being completely satisfied (especially with food)

/ ˈfʊlnəs / (AmE), / ˈfʊlnəs / (BrE)

Examples

Related Terms
