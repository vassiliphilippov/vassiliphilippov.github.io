honeydew

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌniˌdu / (AmE), / ˈhʌnɪdjuː / (BrE)
a large, round, smooth-skinned melon with pale green flesh and a greenish-white rind, popular as a sweet fruit
a type of sweet, pale green melon

Examples

She served chilled honeydew slices as a refreshing summer dessert.
I bought a ripe honeydew at the market for this week's fruit salad.
The honeydew was sweet and juicy, perfect for breakfast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melon - any of various large, juicy fruits with hard rinds

2. NOUN

/ ˈhʌniˌdu / (AmE), / ˈhʌnɪˌdjuː / (BrE)
a sweet, sticky substance excreted by aphids and some scale insects as they feed on plant sap
sweet substance secreted by insects

Examples

Several ants were attracted to the honeydew left on the leaves by aphids.
Bees collect honeydew from infested trees to make a unique type of honey.
After noticing a sticky coating on her plants, she realized it was caused by honeydew produced by scale insects.

Synset Examples

Honeydew is collected by certain species of birds, wasps, stingless bees and honey bees, which process it into a dark, strong honey (honeydew honey).
