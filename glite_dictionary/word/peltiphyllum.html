Peltiphyllum

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛltɪˈfɪləm / (AmE), / ˌpɛltɪˈfɪlɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Saxifragaceae, containing the species Darmera peltata, commonly known as Indian rhubarb.
a genus of plants including Indian rhubarb

Examples

The large, round leaves of Peltiphyllum make it a striking addition to water gardens.
Peltiphyllum is also commonly known as Indian rhubarb due to its thick, rhubarb-like stalks.
Botanists often refer to Peltiphyllum when discussing moisture-loving perennial plants in North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Darmera - a genus containing the species Darmera peltata, closely related to Peltiphyllum

Etymology

From Greek 'pelte' (small shield) and 'phyllon' (leaf), referring to the shield-shaped leaves of the plant.
