burled

1. ADJ

/ bɝld / (AmE), / bɜːld / (BrE)
(of wood) having a distinctive pattern in the grain caused by a burl, often valued for decorative uses.
wood with a patterned grain from a burl

Examples

The cabinet doors were crafted from burled walnut, giving the room a touch of elegance.
Collectors often seek out furniture made from burled wood for its unique swirling patterns.
He admired the burled veneer on the antique writing desk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burl - a rounded knotty growth on a tree, source of burled wood

Etymology

From 'burl' (a tree growth), combined with the adjectival suffix '-ed'.
