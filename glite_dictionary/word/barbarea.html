Barbarea

1. NOUN

/ bɑːrˈbɛriə / (AmE), / bɑːˈbæriə / (BrE)
A genus of biennial or perennial herbs in the mustard family, native to north temperate regions, commonly known as winter cress.
genus of herbs (winter cress)

Examples

Many wild salad mixes contain Barbarea among other edible greens.
The fields were dotted with yellow blossoms of Barbarea in early spring.
Botanists often study the distribution of Barbarea species across Europe.

Etymology

From Latin Barbarea, the name of the plant, said to be named after St. Barbara.

2. NOUN

/ ˌbɑːr.bəˈrɪə / (AmE), / ˌbɑː.bəˈrɪə / (BrE)
a genus of biennial or perennial herbs found in temperate regions, including the winter cress
genus of herbs, winter cress

Examples

The scientist studied the growth patterns of Barbarea in different climate conditions.
Several medicinal properties have been attributed to plants within the genus Barbarea.
Winter cress is one of the most recognized species in the Barbarea genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winter cress - common name for several species within the genus Barbarea

Etymology

Named after Saint Barbara, patron saint of artillerymen, due to the plant's explosive seed pods.
