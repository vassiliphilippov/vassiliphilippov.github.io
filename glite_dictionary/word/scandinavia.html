Scandinavia

1. PROPN

/ ˌskændəˈneɪviə / (AmE), / ˌskændɪˈneɪviə / (BrE)
the large peninsula in northern Europe occupied by Norway and Sweden, often loosely including Denmark and sometimes Finland and Iceland
peninsula in northern Europe (Norway & Sweden)

Examples

The breathtaking fjords of Scandinavia attract tourists from around the world.
Many winter sports competitions are held in Scandinavia each year.
She dreamed of traveling through Scandinavia to see both Norway and Sweden.

Etymology

From Latin 'Scandinavia', from Proto-Germanic 'Skaðinawio' (danger or damage island, i.e., Skadi's island).

2. PROPN

/ ˌskændəˈneɪviə / (AmE), / ˌskændɪˈneɪviə / (BrE)
a region in northern Europe consisting primarily of Norway, Sweden, and Denmark, sometimes also including Finland and Iceland, characterized by cultural and historical connections
region of northern Europe (Norway, Sweden, Denmark)

Examples

Many people dream of visiting Scandinavia to see the northern lights and stunning fjords.
The design style from Scandinavia is popular worldwide for its simplicity and functionality.
Winters in Scandinavia can be long and cold, but the landscapes are breathtaking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Nordic countries - the group of countries in northern Europe including Denmark, Finland, Iceland, Norway, and Sweden

Etymology

From Latin Scandināvia, used to refer to the Scandinavian Peninsula.
