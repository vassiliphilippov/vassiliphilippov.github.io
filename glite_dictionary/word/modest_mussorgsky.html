Modest Mussorgsky

1. PROPN

/ moʊˌdɛst muˈsɔrɡski / (AmE), / ˈmɒdɛst muːˈsɔːɡski / (BrE)
(1839–1881) A Russian composer known for his innovative operas and orchestral works, such as 'Pictures at an Exhibition' and 'Boris Godunov'.
Russian composer (1839–1881)

Examples

Many critics rank Modest Mussorgsky among the most innovative composers of the 19th century.
The opera 'Boris Godunov' was composed by Modest Mussorgsky and remains a masterpiece of Russian music.
In his lifetime, Modest Mussorgsky struggled financially but left a lasting impact on classical music.
