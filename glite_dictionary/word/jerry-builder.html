jerry-builder

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛri ˌbɪldər / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛri ˌbɪldə / (BrE)
a person who builds cheap, poorly made buildings, often to make a quick profit
builder of cheap, poor-quality buildings

Examples

The crooked developer was known as a jerry-builder in the neighborhood.
Complaints poured in about the safety hazards left behind by the notorious jerry-builder.
No one wanted to buy a house built by a jerry-builder after the roof collapsed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jerry-built - badly or cheaply constructed

Etymology

From the adjective 'jerry-built' (of poor quality), origin uncertain, possibly from the name Jerry as a generic person.
