deepness

1. NOUN

/ ˈdip.nəs / (AmE), / ˈdiːp.nəs / (BrE)
The distance measured downward, backward, or inward from a surface.
distance downward, backward, or inward

Examples

Scientists measured the deepness of the lake to determine if it could support large fish.
The deepness of the shelf allowed her to store extra-large books with ease.
We were surprised by the deepness of the closet, which provided much more room than expected.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdiːpnəs / (AmE), / ˈdiːpnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of having profound intellectual insight; the ability to understand complex or subtle ideas fully
quality of intellectual profundity

Examples

The deepness of her philosophical arguments impressed even the most experienced scholars.
He admired the deepness of thought that the novel evoked in its readers.
Some critics questioned the deepness of the article's analysis, finding it superficial.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depth - the quality of being deep in a literal or figurative sense
  • Antonym: shallowness - the quality of being superficial or lacking depth of thought

Etymology

Formed from 'deep' + '-ness', Old English root 'dēop'.
