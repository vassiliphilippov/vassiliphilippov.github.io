catchall

1. NOUN

/ ˈkætʃˌɔl / (AmE), / ˈkætʃˌɔːl / (BrE)
a container or place in which miscellaneous items are stored or collected
container for miscellaneous items

Examples

She keeps her keys and loose change in a catchall on the entryway table.
The drawer became a catchall for everything from pens to batteries.
He emptied the contents of the catchall and sorted through the clutter.

Etymology

Formed from 'catch' + 'all', indicating something that catches all things.
