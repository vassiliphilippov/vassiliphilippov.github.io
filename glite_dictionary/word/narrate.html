to narrate

1. VERB

/ nəˈreɪt / (AmE), / ˈnæreɪt / (BrE)
to provide spoken commentary for a film, broadcast, or performance, describing what is happening or giving additional information
provide commentary for a film or broadcast

Examples

He was chosen to narrate the award-winning documentary about climate change.
The actor will narrate the film, describing the events as they unfold.
She enjoys narrating stories for children's audio books and television shows.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to commentate - to provide a spoken account of events as they happen, especially on radio or television

Etymology

From Latin 'narratus', past participle of 'narrare' (to relate, tell).

2. VERB

/ nəˈreɪt, ˈnæˌreɪt / (AmE), / nəˈreɪt, ˈnæˌreɪt / (BrE)
to tell a story or describe events, often in detail; to give a spoken or written account of something
to tell or describe events in detail

Examples

The tour guide narrated the history of the castle as we walked through its halls.
She narrated the film, providing insights into each scene.
He enjoys narrating stories to his grandchildren every evening.

Synset Examples

Tell what happened
The father told a story to his child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to describe - to give an account of something with details
  • Synonym: to relate - to recount or tell; to give an account of
  • Antonym: to summarize - to give a brief statement of the main points; to sum up

Etymology

From Latin narratus, past participle of narrare (to tell, relate).
