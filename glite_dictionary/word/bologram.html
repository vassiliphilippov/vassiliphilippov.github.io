bologram

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊləˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈbɒləˌɡræm / (BrE)
a record or recording made by a bolometer, showing variations in radiant energy
record made by a bolometer

Examples

The scientist carefully examined the bologram to interpret the changes in infrared radiation.
A bologram can reveal valuable data about energy fluctuations in an experiment.
Researchers used a sensitive bolometer to create a detailed bologram of the observed phenomenon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bolometer - an instrument for measuring radiant energy

Etymology

From 'bolometer' and '-gram' (from Greek 'gramma', meaning something written or recorded).
