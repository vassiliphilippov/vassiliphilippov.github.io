Fauve

1. NOUN

/ foʊv / (AmE), / fəʊv / (BrE)
a member of a group of early 20th-century French painters who adopted fauvism, noted for their use of bold color and brushwork.
member of a fauvist French art movement

Examples

Henri Matisse was a leading Fauve and helped define the movement.
Several Fauve artists exhibited together at the 1905 Salon d'Automne.
The vibrant works of the Fauves shocked traditional art critics of their time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fauvism - an early 20th-century movement in painting emphasizing vivid color and bold brushwork

Etymology

From French 'fauve' (wild beast), originally used to describe the movement's bold style.
