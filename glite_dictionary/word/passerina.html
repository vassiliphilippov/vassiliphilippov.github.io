Passerina

1. NOUN

/ pæsəˈriːnə / (AmE), / pasəˈriːnə / (BrE)
A genus of small North American finches known as New World buntings.
genus of New World buntings

Examples

The indigo bunting belongs to the genus Passerina.
Recent studies have revised the classification within Passerina.
Several brightly colored songbirds are included in the Passerina genus.

Etymology

From Latin 'passerinus', meaning 'of sparrows'.

2. NOUN

/ pæsəˈriːnə / (AmE), / pæsəˈriːnə / (BrE)
A genus of small North American finches that includes the New World buntings.
genus of small North American finches

Examples

The Indigo Bunting is a species in the genus Passerina.
Researchers studied the migratory patterns of several Passerina species.
Birdwatchers spotted a painted bunting belonging to the Passerina genus in the reserve.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bunting - a small songbird related to finches, often colorful

Etymology

From Latin *passer* (sparrow), used in scientific naming.
