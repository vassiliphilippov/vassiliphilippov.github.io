optimum

1. ADJ

/ ˈɑp.tə.məm / (AmE), / ˈɒp.tɪ.məm / (BrE)
the best or most favorable possible in a particular situation or under certain restrictions
most favorable; best possible under conditions

Examples

The engineers designed the system for optimum efficiency in hot climates.
She adjusted the settings to achieve optimum performance from the machine.
It is important to maintain an optimum balance between work and rest.

Synset Examples

an optimum return on capital
optimal concentration of a drug

Related Terms

  • Synonym: optimal - most favorable or desirable; best
  • Antonym: worst - least desirable or least favorable

Etymology

From Latin 'optimum', neuter of 'optimus' meaning 'best'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑptɪməm / (AmE), / ˈɒptɪməm / (BrE)
the most favorable condition or degree for the growth, reproduction, or success of something
most favorable condition or degree

Examples

Farmers monitor soil conditions to achieve the optimum for crop growth.
The device operates at its optimum when the temperature is kept within a precise range.
She balanced her schedule to find the optimum between work and relaxation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peak - the highest level or greatest degree
  • Antonym: minimum - the lowest possible amount or degree

Etymology

From Latin 'optimum', neuter of 'optimus' (best).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05151111-n
Synset POS: n
