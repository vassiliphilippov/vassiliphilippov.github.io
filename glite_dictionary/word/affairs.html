affairs

1. NOUN

/ əˈfɛrz / (AmE), / əˈfeəz / (BrE)
matters or transactions that are of public, professional, or political importance
important public or professional matters

Examples

The prime minister is responsible for overseeing international affairs.
Recent scandals have brought the company's financial affairs into the spotlight.
She works as a journalist covering local affairs for the newspaper.

Synset Examples

news of current affairs
great affairs of state

Related Terms

  • Synonym: business - commercial or professional activities
  • Synonym: matters - topics under consideration or discussion

2. NOUN

/ əˈfɛrz / (AmE), / əˈfeəz / (BrE)
matters that are of personal or private concern, especially related to one's responsibilities, business, or private life
matters of personal concern

Examples

He spent the weekend organizing his affairs to prepare for the move.
It's important to keep your financial affairs in order as you get older.
When she passed away, her children found all her affairs neatly documented.

Synset Examples

get his affairs in order

Related Terms

  • Synonym: business - personal or commercial matters requiring attention
  • Synonym: matters - things that are concerns to someone

Etymology

From Old French 'afaire' (business, matter), from 'à faire' (to do).
