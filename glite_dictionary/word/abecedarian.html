Abecedarian

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪbɪsiˈdɛriən / (AmE), / ˌeɪbɪsiˈdɪəriən / (BrE)
A member of a 16th-century Anabaptist sect in Germany that rejected all forms of human knowledge and education.
member of a 16th-century anti-knowledge sect

Examples

The Abecedarian movement rejected traditional education and embraced spiritual simplicity.
Historians debate the motives of the Abecedarian sect in Reformation-era Germany.
As an Abecedarian, he believed that learning beyond the basics was unnecessary and even dangerous.

Etymology

From Latin abecedarius (relating to the alphabet), referring to those who limit knowledge to the basics.
