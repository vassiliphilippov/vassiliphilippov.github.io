abecedarian

1. ADJ

/ ˌeɪbɪsiˈdɛriən / (AmE), / ˌeɪbɪsiˈdɛːrɪən / (BrE)
arranged in alphabetical order, especially for the use of beginners or children learning the alphabet
alphabetically arranged; for beginners

Examples

The abecedarian list made it easy for students to find each word.
She created an abecedarian picture book to help children learn their letters.
For clarity, the glossary was arranged in abecedarian order.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alphabetical - arranged in the order of the alphabet

Etymology

From Latin 'abecedarius' (of the alphabet), from the first four letters: A, B, C, D.

2. NOUN

/ ˌeɪbɪsiˈdɛriən / (AmE), / ˌeɪbɪsiˈdɛːrɪən / (BrE)
a person who is just beginning to learn the basics of a subject; a novice or beginner
beginner; novice learner

Examples

Even as an abecedarian, she showed great enthusiasm for learning the piano.
The training program was designed for abecedarians with no prior experience in coding.
As an abecedarian in chess, he spent hours mastering the rules and basic strategies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: novice - a person new to and inexperienced in a job or situation
  • Antonym: expert - a person with a high level of knowledge or skill in a field

Etymology

From Latin abecedarius (of the alphabet), from A B C D.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
