pleonaste

1. NOUN

/ pliˈɑːnæst / (AmE), / pliˈɒnast / (BrE)
A dark-colored variety of spinel mineral containing iron, typically black or greenish-black.
dark iron-containing spinel mineral

Examples

The jeweler identified the gemstone as pleonaste because of its deep black color.
Some deposits of spinel produce pleonaste crystals with a distinctive metallic sheen.
The museum's collection includes several rare samples of pleonaste from Sri Lanka.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spinel - a hard glassy mineral of variable color used as a gemstone

Etymology

From French 'pléonaste', from Greek 'pleonasmos' (excess), reflecting its high iron content.
