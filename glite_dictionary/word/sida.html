Sida

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪdə / (AmE), / ˈsiːdə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical and subtropical subshrubs or herbs, some species of which are cultivated for their fibrous or mucilaginous properties.
genus of tropical herbs or subshrubs

Examples

Many species within the genus Sida are valued for their strong plant fibers.
Botanists have identified numerous medicinal compounds in Sida plants.
The tropical climate supports the growth of several Sida varieties.

Etymology

From Latin, the plant name 'sida,' used by Pliny; modern scientific use by Linnaeus.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsiːdə / (AmE), / ˈsiːdə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical subshrubs or herbs, some species of which are valued for their fiber or mucilaginous properties.
genus of tropical subshrubs or herbs

Examples

The fiber from certain species of Sida is used in making rope and twine.
Botanists are studying the medicinal properties of the Sida genus.
Several Sida plants thrive in the warm, humid climate of the region.

Etymology

From Latin *sida*, meaning 'water-lily', used by botanists for this genus.
