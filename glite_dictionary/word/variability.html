variability

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɛri.əˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌveəri.əˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being likely to change or vary; lack of consistency or predictability
quality of being changeable or inconsistent

Examples

The variability of the weather makes it hard to plan outdoor events.
There is significant variability in student test scores across different schools.
Scientists study the genetic variability within populations to understand evolution.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: consistency - the quality of always behaving or performing in a similar way
  • Synonym: variation - a change or slight difference in condition, amount, or level

Etymology

From Latin variabilis (changeable) + -ity.

2. NOUN

/ ˌvɛriəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌveəriəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being uneven or lacking uniformity; the tendency to change or vary.
quality of being uneven or changeable

Examples

There is considerable variability in temperature between different regions of the country.
Climate change is increasing the variability of weather patterns across the globe.
The test results showed a high degree of variability among participants.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: consistency - the quality of always behaving or performing in a similar way
  • Synonym: variation - the act or process of changing or varying

Etymology

From Latin variābilis (changeable), from variāre (to change).
