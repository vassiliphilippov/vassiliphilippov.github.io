Sphecoidea

1. NOUN

/ sfɪˈkɔɪdiə / (AmE), / sfɛˈkɔɪdiə / (BrE)
a superfamily of wasps that includes the families Sphecidae and Stizidae
a superfamily of wasps

Examples

Scientists studied several species within the Sphecoidea superfamily.
Many members of the Sphecoidea are solitary wasps known for their nesting behavior.
Fossil records show that Sphecoidea have existed for millions of years.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'sphex' (wasp genus) and '-oidea' (resembling, like).

2. NOUN

/ spiːˈkoʊɪdiə / (AmE), / spiːˈkɔɪdiə / (BrE)
A superfamily of wasps that includes families such as Sphecidae and Stizidae.
superfamily of wasps (Sphecidae, Stizidae, etc.)

Examples

The Sphecoidea are a group of solitary wasps known for their hunting behaviors.
Recent studies have reclassified several species within the Sphecoidea.
Members of the Sphecoidea display a wide range of nesting preferences.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'sphex' (wasp genus) and '-oidea' (having the form of, like).
