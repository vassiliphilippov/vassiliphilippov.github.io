émigré

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛmɪˌɡreɪ / (AmE), / ˈeɪmɪˌɡreɪ / (BrE)
a person who has left their own country, especially for political reasons, and settled in another
person who leaves their country, often for political reasons

Examples

The political émigré found a new home in Canada after fleeing his homeland.
Many Russian émigrés settled in Paris after the revolution.
As an émigré, she faced both opportunities and challenges in her adopted country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emigrant - a person who leaves their own country to settle permanently in another

Etymology

From French émigré (past participle of émigrer ‘emigrate’).
