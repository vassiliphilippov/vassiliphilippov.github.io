oligarch

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑːlɪˌɡɑrk / (AmE), / ˈɒlɪˌɡɑːk / (BrE)
a member of a small group of people who control a country, organization, or institution
member of a ruling elite or oligarchy

Examples

Several wealthy oligarchs were accused of influencing government decisions behind the scenes.
After the revolution, the former oligarch lost most of his power and assets.
The country was controlled by a few oligarchs who shaped its political and economic future.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plutocrat - a person whose power derives from wealth
  • Antonym: democrat - a person who supports democracy or is a member of the Democratic Party
  • Synonym: autocrat - a ruler who has absolute power

Etymology

From Greek oligarkhēs, from oligos (few) + arkhein (to rule).
