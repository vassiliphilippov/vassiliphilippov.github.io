outrageousness

1. NOUN

/ aʊtˈreɪdʒəsnəs / (AmE), / aʊtˈreɪdʒəsnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being shocking, excessive, or beyond what is considered acceptable or reasonable
the quality of being outrageous

Examples

The outrageousness of his behavior left everyone speechless.
She admired the outrageousness of the comedian’s jokes, even if they sometimes crossed the line.
Many critics commented on the outrageousness of the fashion designer's new collection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outrageous - very shocking, excessive, or unacceptable

Etymology

From 'outrageous' + '-ness'. Ultimately from Old French 'outrage' (excess) and English '-ness'.

2. NOUN

/ aʊtˈreɪdʒəsnəs / (AmE), / aʊtˈreɪdʒəsnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being shocking, excessive, or extremely unusual
state of being shocking or excessive

Examples

The outrageousness of his behavior stunned everyone in the room.
Many critics commented on the outrageousness of the film's costumes and sets.
She was known for the outrageousness of her sense of humor, always pushing boundaries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outrage - an extremely strong reaction of anger, shock, or indignation
  • Antonym: mildness - the quality of being gentle or moderate

Etymology

From 'outrageous' + '-ness', ultimately from Old French *outrage* (excess, insult).
