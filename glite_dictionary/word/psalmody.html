psalmody

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɑːlmədi / (AmE), / ˈsɑːlmədi / (BrE)
the act or practice of singing psalms or hymns, especially in a religious service
the singing of psalms or hymns

Examples

The tradition of psalmody dates back centuries in many Christian denominations.
She led the psalmody during the Sunday morning service with a clear, melodic voice.
Many medieval monasteries were renowned for their beautiful psalmody.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hymnody - the singing or composition of hymns

Etymology

From Late Latin psalmodia, from Ancient Greek psalmodia (singing to the harp, singing of psalms).
