tilde

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪl.deɪ / (AmE), / ˈtɪl.deɪ / (BrE)
a diacritical mark (~) placed over certain letters, as in Spanish 'ñ' or Portuguese vowels, to indicate special pronunciation
the ~ diacritical mark used in languages

Examples

The Spanish letter eñe uses a tilde to distinguish it from a regular n.
In Portuguese, a tilde over a vowel indicates nasalization.
Please type the tilde symbol above the 'n' to spell the word correctly.

Etymology

From Spanish 'tilde' (accent, diacritical mark), from Latin 'titulus' (title, inscription).
