pervasion

1. NOUN

/ pərˈveɪʒən / (AmE), / pəˈveɪʒən / (BrE)
the process of spreading through or being present throughout something
the process of permeating or spreading throughout

Examples

The pervasion of technology in daily life has changed how society functions.
There was a noticeable pervasion of the scent of roses throughout the garden.
The pervasion of fear after the announcement affected everyone's mood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: permeation - the act of permeating or passing through
  • Synonym: pervasion - the process of spreading through or being present throughout something

Etymology

From Latin pervasio, from pervadere (to pervade).
