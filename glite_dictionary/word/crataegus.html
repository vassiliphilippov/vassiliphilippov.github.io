Crataegus

1. NOUN

/ krəˈtiːɡəs / (AmE), / krəˈtiːɡəs / (BrE)
A genus of thorny shrubs and small trees known as hawthorn, found mainly in temperate regions and bearing small white or pink flowers and red or orange fruit.
genus of shrubs and small trees (hawthorn)

Examples

The Crataegus hedge in the garden blooms with delicate white flowers each spring.
Botanists classify many species of hawthorn under the genus Crataegus.
Birds often feed on the bright berries produced by Crataegus trees in autumn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawthorn - a thorny shrub or tree with white/pink flowers and red fruit

Etymology

From Greek krataigos, the hawthorn.
