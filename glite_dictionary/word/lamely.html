lamely

1. ADV

/ ˈleɪmli / (AmE), / ˈleɪmli / (BrE)
In a weak, unconvincing, or ineffectual manner.
in a weak or unconvincing way

Examples

She apologized lamely, clearly lacking a strong reason for her mistake.
He lamely defended his actions when questioned by the manager.
After forgetting his lines, the actor lamely tried to improvise.

Synset Examples

‘I don't know, Edward,’ she answered lamely

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weakly - in a way that shows little strength or conviction
  • Antonym: convincingly - in a way that is capable of persuading someone

Etymology

Formed from the adjective 'lame' + '-ly'. 'Lame' from Old English 'lama' (crippled, weak).
