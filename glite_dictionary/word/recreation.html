recreation

1. NOUN

/ ˌrɛkriˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːkriˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
an activity done for enjoyment, relaxation, and refreshment of health and spirits
activity for enjoyment and relaxation

Examples

The park is designed for recreation and relaxation for local residents.
After a stressful week, she enjoys swimming as her main form of recreation.
Many cities invest in sports facilities to promote public recreation.

Synset Examples

time for rest and refreshment by the pool
days of joyous recreation with his friends

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leisure - free time when one is not working
  • Antonym: work - activity involving mental or physical effort for achieving a result rather than for enjoyment

Etymology

From Latin 'recreatio' (restoration, recovery), from 'recreare' (to restore, refresh).

2. NOUN

/ ˌrɛkriˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛkriˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
an activity done for enjoyment, amusement, or relaxation, rather than for work
activity for enjoyment or relaxation

Examples

Reading novels is her favorite form of recreation after a long day at work.
The new community center offers a variety of recreation activities for children.
Outdoor recreation is encouraged to promote a healthy lifestyle.

Synset Examples

scuba diving is provided as a diversion for tourists
for recreation he wrote poetry and solved crossword puzzles
drug abuse is often regarded as a form of recreation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leisure - free time not occupied by work; time for recreation
  • Antonym: work - activity involving mental or physical effort done in order to achieve a result; antonym of recreation

Etymology

From Latin 'recreatio' (refreshment, restoration), from 'recreare' (to create anew, refresh).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00427931-n
Synset POS: n
