rewrite

1. VERB

/ ˌriˈraɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈraɪt / (BrE)
to write something again in order to improve it or adapt it for a new or different purpose.
write again for improvement or adaptation

Examples

The committee had to rewrite the policy to address recent changes.
She decided to rewrite her essay to make it clearer and more concise.
They will rewrite the script so it appeals to a younger audience.

Synset Examples

re-write a play for use in schools

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revise - to change or improve something by correcting or updating it

Etymology

From re- (again) + write.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01704793-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ riˈraɪt / (AmE), / riˈraɪt / (BrE)
to write something again, especially in a different form or with changes to its content or wording
to write again with changes

Examples

After receiving feedback, she decided to rewrite her essay from the beginning.
The director asked the screenwriter to rewrite the script to make it more engaging.
He rewrote the instructions so that they would be easier to follow.

Synset Examples

The student rewrote his thesis

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to edit - to prepare written material for publication by correcting and revising
  • Synonym: revise - to change something in order to improve it or make it more accurate

Etymology

From re- (again) + write.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00997883-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈriˌraɪt / (AmE), / ˈriːˌraɪt / (BrE)
a piece of writing that has been revised or written again, often to improve the original version
a revised or rewritten piece of writing

Examples

After receiving feedback, the author submitted a rewrite that addressed all the editor's concerns.
The script was so different after the rewrite that the story took a new direction.
The magazine published a rewrite of the controversial article to correct factual errors.

Synset Examples

the rewrite was much better

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revision - an edited or revised version of a text

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 're-' (again) and 'write'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06375556-n
Synset POS: n
