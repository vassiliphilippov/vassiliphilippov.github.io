Liomys irroratus

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪəˌmɪs ɪˌrɔːˈrɑːtəs / (AmE), / ˈlaɪəˌmɪs ɪˌrɒˈrɑːtəs / (BrE)
A species of large pocket mouse native to Mexico, known for storing food in its cheek pouches.
large pocket mouse species of Mexico

Examples

The Liomys irroratus is commonly found in dry forests and scrublands across central Mexico.
Researchers captured several Liomys irroratus specimens to study their nocturnal behavior.
Unlike many rodents, the Liomys irroratus carries seeds in its cheek pouches to store for later.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Liomys' from Greek 'leios' (smooth) + 'mys' (mouse), 'irroratus' meaning sprinkled or speckled in Latin.
