pork-barreling

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔrk ˌbæɹəˌlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɔːk ˌbær.əl.ɪŋ / (BrE)
The practice of securing government funds for local projects intended to please voters or legislators in a particular area, often for political advantage.
securing government funds for local political gain

Examples

The senator was accused of pork-barreling to secure funds for unnecessary local projects.
Critics claim that pork-barreling undermines trust in government spending.
He defended his record, insisting pork-barreling was necessary to improve his district.

Synset Examples

keeps his hold on his constituents through unashamed pork-barreling

Related Terms

  • Synonym: patronage - the support given by a patron, especially political favoritism
  • Synonym: graft - the use of political authority for personal gain, especially through bribery or corruption

Etymology

From the US political slang 'pork barrel', referring to government spending for localized projects. The term draws on the image of barrels of salt pork given out as rewards.
