unabridged

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.əˈbrɪdʒd / (AmE), / ˌʌn.əˈbrɪdʒd / (BrE)
(of a text) not shortened; presented in its complete form without any part omitted.
not shortened; complete (of a text)

Examples

The library only had the unabridged edition of the novel available.
She prefers listening to unabridged audiobooks on her long drives.
Make sure you read the unabridged version before the exam, as it contains key chapters.

Synset Examples

an unabridged novel

Related Terms

  • Antonym: abridged - (of a text) shortened by removing parts

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'un-' (not) + 'abridged', from Old French 'abregier' (to shorten).
