to detransitivise

1. VERB

/ diːˈtrænzɪˌtɪvaɪz / (AmE), / diːˈtrænzɪvʌɪz / (BrE)
(of a verb or grammatical process) to make or become intransitive; to change (a verb) so that it no longer takes a direct object.
make a verb intransitive

Examples

The linguist worked to detransitivise several verbs in the new language.
Some grammatical rules can detransitivise a verb, making it no longer require an object.
After the prefix was added, the verb detransitivised and could stand alone.

Synset Examples

removing the object will intransitivize the verbs

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to intransitivize - to make or become intransitive

Etymology

From de- (removal, reversal) + transitiv(e) + -ise (verb-forming suffix).
