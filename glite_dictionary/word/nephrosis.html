nephrosis

1. NOUN

/ nɛˈfroʊsɪs / (AmE), / nɛˈfrəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A non-inflammatory disease of the kidneys, especially affecting the renal tubules.
kidney disease (non-inflammatory)
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with nephrosis after routine blood and urine tests showed abnormal results.
Chronic nephrosis can lead to serious complications if left untreated.
Recent research offers new treatments for children suffering from minimal change nephrosis.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nephritis - inflammatory disease of the kidneys

Etymology

From Greek nephros (kidney) + -osis (diseased condition).

2. NOUN

/ nɛˈfroʊsɪs / (AmE), / nɪˈfrəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A kidney disorder characterized by swelling (edema) and the presence of large amounts of protein in the urine, often accompanied by increased blood cholesterol.
kidney disorder with protein in urine
medical

Examples

The child was diagnosed with nephrosis after tests revealed high levels of protein in her urine.
Treatment for nephrosis often includes medication and dietary changes to manage symptoms.
Doctors explained that nephrosis can be a complication of certain systemic diseases.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nephritis - inflammation of the kidneys

Etymology

From Greek nephros (kidney) + -osis (condition).
