baba

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑːbə / (AmE), / ˈbɑːbə / (BrE)
a small cake made with yeast, sometimes containing or soaked in rum or other liqueur.
yeast-leavened cake, often soaked in liqueur

Examples

For dessert, she ordered a baba soaked in rum and stuffed with whipped cream.
The bakery is famous for its light and fluffy baba cakes.
He enjoyed a traditional baba after his meal, drizzled with sweet syrup.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: savarin - a ring-shaped yeast cake similar to a baba, often soaked in syrup or liqueur

Etymology

From French 'baba', likely via Polish and Russian 'baba' (old woman, grandmother), named for its shape or origin.
