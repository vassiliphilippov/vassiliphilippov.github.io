mushy

1. ADJ

/ ˈmʌʃi / (AmE), / ˈmʌʃi / (BrE)
soft and pulpy in texture; having the consistency of mush.
soft and pulpy in texture

Examples

The bananas became mushy after sitting out in the heat all day.
She doesn't like her oatmeal too mushy and prefers it with some texture.
After boiling the vegetables too long, they turned out mushy and unappetizing.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: firm - solid or resistant to pressure
  • Synonym: soft - not hard or firm to the touch

Etymology

From English 'mush' (soft mass), with suffix '-y'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈmʌʃi / (AmE), / ˈmʌʃi / (BrE)
excessively or insincerely emotional, especially in a way that seems silly or sentimental
excessively sentimental or emotional

Examples

I find romantic movies too mushy for my taste.
His mushy letter made her laugh more than it moved her.
She tends to get mushy when talking about her childhood.

Synset Examples

a bathetic novel
maudlin expressions of sympathy
mushy effusiveness
a schmaltzy song
sentimental soap operas
slushy poetry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sentimental - having or arousing feelings of tenderness, sadness, or nostalgia
  • Synonym: maudlin - self-pityingly or tearfully sentimental, often through drunkenness
  • Antonym: unsentimental - not influenced by, showing, or expressing feelings or sentiment
