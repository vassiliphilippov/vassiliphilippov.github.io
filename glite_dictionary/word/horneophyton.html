Horneophyton

1. NOUN

/ hɔːrniˈɑːfaɪtən / (AmE), / hɔːniˈɒfɪtən / (BrE)
an extinct genus of Devonian fossil plants, regarded as one of the earliest vascular land plants, similar to Rhynia but smaller
extinct early vascular Devonian plant genus

Examples

Fossils of Horneophyton have provided valuable insight into the evolution of early land plants.
The small size of Horneophyton distinguishes it from the related genus Rhynia.
Paleobotanists study Horneophyton to understand vascular tissue development in ancient plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rhynia - an extinct genus of early vascular land plants from the Devonian period

Etymology

From the botanist Joseph Horne and Greek 'phyton' (plant).

2. NOUN

/ hɔrniˈɑfaɪtən / (AmE), / hɔːnɪˈɒfɪtən / (BrE)
an extinct genus of Devonian fossil plants considered among the earliest vascular land plants; similar to Rhynia but smaller
extinct genus of early vascular plants

Examples

The fossilized stems of Horneophyton have provided insight into the structure of early land plants.
Researchers discovered several specimens of Horneophyton in Devonian rock layers in Scotland.
Unlike Rhynia, Horneophyton was notably smaller and simpler in its anatomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Rhynia - extinct genus of Devonian vascular plants

Etymology

From Greek 'Horne-', honoring botanist Joseph Horne, and 'phyton' (plant).
