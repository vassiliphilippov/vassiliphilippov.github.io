friend

1. NOUN

/ frɛnd / (AmE), / frɛnd / (BrE)
A person who supports or backs a particular group, cause, politician, or team.
supporter of a cause or group

Examples

Many local friends of the hospital attended the fundraising dinner.
He has always been a loyal friend of the team, traveling to every away game.
The museum relies on donations from its friends to support new exhibitions.

Synset Examples

all their supporters came out for the game
they are friends of the library

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supporter - a person who approves of and encourages someone or something
  • Antonym: opponent - a person who opposes or competes against another

Etymology

From Old English frēond meaning 'one who loves or favors', related to Old English frēon 'to love'.

2. NOUN

/ frɛnd / (AmE), / frɛnd / (BrE)
a person who supports, cooperates with, or assists another, especially in times of need or adversity
supportive associate or helper

Examples

Whenever I'm facing difficulties, I can always count on my friend for help.
The president called the visiting diplomat a trusted friend of his country.
In emergencies, a true friend will come to your aid without hesitation.

Synset Examples

he's a good ally in fight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ally - a person or group that supports or cooperates with another
  • Antonym: enemy - a person who is actively opposed or hostile

Etymology

From Old English freond (one who is attached to another by feelings of personal regard and preference), related to 'free'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09804483-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ frɛnd / (AmE), / frɛnd / (BrE)
a person whom one knows and with whom one has a bond of mutual affection, typically exclusive of sexual or family relations
person with whom one has a bond of affection

Examples

She has been my friend since we started school together.
He invited all his friends to the party on Saturday night.
Despite their differences, they remained close friends throughout their lives.

Synset Examples

I have trouble remembering the names of all my acquaintances
we are friends of the family

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: acquaintance - a person one knows slightly, but who is not a close friend
  • Antonym: enemy - a person who is actively opposed or hostile to someone

Etymology

From Old English 'frēond', related to 'frēon' (to love, to favor).

4. NOUN

/ frɛnd / (AmE), / frɛnd / (BrE)
a person with whom one has a bond of mutual affection, typically one exclusive of sexual or family relations
a person you have affection and trust for

Examples

Sarah has been my best friend since elementary school.
He called his friend to share the good news.
Making a new friend can be both exciting and a bit intimidating.

Synset Examples

he was my best friend at the university

Related Terms

  • Antonym: enemy - a person who is actively opposed or hostile
  • Degree Lower: acquaintance - a person one knows slightly, but who is not a close friend
  • Synonym: companion - a person with whom one spends a lot of time; a close friend

Etymology

Old English 'frēond', from the Germanic base of 'free', meaning 'to love, to favor'.
