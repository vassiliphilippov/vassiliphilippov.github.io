program

1. NOUN

/ ˈproʊɡræm / (AmE), / ˈprəʊɡræm / (BrE)
A printed or written list of the events, performers, or participants in a theatrical performance, sporting event, etc.
schedule of events or participants at an event

Examples

The theater handed out a program that listed all the actors in the play.
Please keep your program to follow the schedule of matches for today’s tournament.
She collected a program as a souvenir from every concert she attended.

Synset Examples

you can't tell the players without a program

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schedule - a plan of activities or events and when they will happen

Etymology

From Greek 'programma' (written public notice), via Latin 'programma' and French 'programme'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06761180-n
Synset POS: n
