Cheilanthes gracillima

1. NOUN

/ kiˈælænˌθiːz ɡrɑːˈsɪlɪmə / (AmE), / kiˈælænˌθiːz ɡræˈsɪlɪmə / (BrE)
A small tufted fern native to northwestern North America, typically found in rocky habitats.
a small tufted fern of northwestern North America

Examples

The rare Cheilanthes gracillima can be spotted clinging to rocky slopes in the Sierra Nevada.
Botanists were excited to find a thriving patch of Cheilanthes gracillima during their field survey.
Gardeners often admire Cheilanthes gracillima for its delicate, tufted fronds.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Cheilanthes' is a genus name derived from Greek 'cheilos' (lip) and 'anthos' (flower), and 'gracillima' means 'most slender' in Latin.
