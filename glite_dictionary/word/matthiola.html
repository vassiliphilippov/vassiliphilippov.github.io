/ məˈθaɪoʊlə / (AmE), / məˈθaɪələ / (BrE)
a genus of Old World flowering plants grown as ornamentals, commonly known as stock
genus of ornamental flowering plants (stock)

Examples

Many gardeners plant Matthiola for its colorful, fragrant flowers.

2. NOUN

/ məˈθaɪ.ələ / (AmE), / məˈθaɪ.ələ / (BrE)
A genus of Old World flowering plants in the family Brassicaceae, commonly grown as ornamentals for their fragrant and colorful flowers.
genus of ornamental flowering plants

Examples

Many gardeners plant Matthiola for its beautiful, fragrant blossoms in early summer.
Several species of Matthiola are prized for their wide range of colors and ease of cultivation.
The Matthiola genus includes the well-known stock flowers often found in cottage gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stock - a common name for plants in the genus Matthiola
  • Degree Higher: Brassicaceae - the plant family to which Matthiola belongs

Etymology

Named after Italian physician and botanist Pietro Andrea Mattioli (1501–1577).
