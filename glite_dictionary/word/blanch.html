to blanch

1. VERB

/ blæntʃ / (AmE), / blɑːntʃ / (BrE)
(of a person) to become pale, often as a reaction to fear, shock, or similar emotion.
become pale from fear or shock

Examples

She blanched when she heard the unexpected news.
His face blanched with terror as the shadow loomed closer.
Many people blanch at the thought of speaking in public.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pale - lacking color in the face; ashen
  • Antonym: flush - to become red in the face, often from embarrassment or heat

Etymology

From Old French 'blanchir', from 'blanc' meaning 'white'.

2. VERB

/ blæntʃ / (AmE), / blɑːntʃ / (BrE)
to cook (vegetables or other food) briefly in boiling water, usually to soften, loosen skins, or prepare for freezing
briefly cook in boiling water

Examples

To preserve their color and texture, you should blanch the green beans for two minutes before freezing.
Chefs often blanch tomatoes to make removing their skins easier.
It's important to blanch almonds before peeling them for some recipes.

Synset Examples

Parboil the beans before freezing them

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to parboil - partly cook food by boiling it briefly

Etymology

From Old French *blanchir* (to whiten), from *blanc* (white).
