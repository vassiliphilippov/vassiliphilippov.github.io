Polyodontidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑlioʊˈdɒntɪdi / (AmE), / ˌpɒliəʊˈdɒntɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of large freshwater fish known as paddlefishes, characterized by their elongated, paddle-shaped snouts
family of paddlefishes

Examples

The Polyodontidae are primarily found in the rivers of eastern North America and China.
Unlike most other fish families, members of Polyodontidae possess a distinctive elongated snout.
Due to habitat loss, several species within the Polyodontidae family are now endangered.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paddlefish - a large freshwater fish with a long, paddle-shaped snout

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'poly-' (many) + 'odous' (tooth), referring to the many small teeth of these fishes.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɑliˌoʊdɑnˈtɪdi / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˌəʊdanˈtɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, freshwater fish known as paddlefishes, characterized by long, paddle-shaped snouts and found primarily in North America and China.
family of paddlefishes

Examples

The American paddlefish is the most well-known member of the Polyodontidae family.
Researchers studied fossil records of Polyodontidae to understand their evolutionary history.
Unlike sturgeons, Polyodontidae have a distinctive elongated snout used for sensing prey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paddlefish - Large freshwater fish of the family Polyodontidae with a long, paddle-shaped snout

Etymology

From Greek 'poly-' (many) + 'odous, odont-' (tooth), referring to the many small teeth some members possess; '-idae' is a zoological family suffix.
