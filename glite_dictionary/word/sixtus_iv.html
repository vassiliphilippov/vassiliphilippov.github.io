Sixtus IV

1. PROPN

/ ˈsɪks.təs ðə ˈfɔːrθ / (AmE), / ˈsɪks.təs ðə ˈfɔːθ / (BrE)
(1414–1484) Italian pope (1471–1484), known for consenting to the establishment of the Spanish Inquisition and for building the Sistine Chapel.
Italian pope, builder of Sistine Chapel

Examples

Sixtus IV commissioned the construction of the renowned Sistine Chapel in Vatican City.
The reign of Sixtus IV was marked by significant political and religious changes, including the start of the Spanish Inquisition.
Many art historians study the papacy of Sixtus IV for his patronage of Renaissance artists.

Etymology

From Latin 'Sixtus', a papal name, meaning 'the sixth', with the Roman numeral IV indicating he was the fourth pope by that name.
