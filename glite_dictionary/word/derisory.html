derisory

1. ADJ

/ dəˈraɪsəri / (AmE), / dɪˈraɪsəri / (BrE)
(of an amount, offer, or idea) so small or inadequate that it is intended to be ridiculous or to invite derision.
so small or inadequate as to invite ridicule

Examples

The company offered a derisory sum of money in compensation for the damage.
They made a derisory attempt to solve the problem, which only made matters worse.
His proposal was so derisory that no one took it seriously.

Synset Examples

the absurd excuse that the dog ate his homework
that's a cockeyed idea
ask a nonsensical question and get a nonsensical answer
a contribution so small as to be laughable
it is ludicrous to call a cottage a mansion
a preposterous attempt to turn back the pages of history
her conceited assumption of universal interest in her rather dull children was ridiculous

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ridiculous - deserving to be laughed at; absurd
  • Synonym: ludicrous - so foolish, unreasonable, or out of place as to be amusing; ridiculous

Etymology

From Latin derisorius (derisive), from deridere (to mock).
