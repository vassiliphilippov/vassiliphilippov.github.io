exposition

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.spəˈzɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌɛk.spəˈzɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
the section of a musical composition, especially in sonata form, where the main themes are first presented
section in music introducing main themes

Examples

In the first movement, the exposition introduces both the primary and secondary themes.
The composer repeated the exposition to reinforce the main musical ideas.
Listeners can often distinguish the exposition by its clear melodic statements.

Etymology

From Latin exposītiō (setting forth, explanation).

3. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.spəˈzɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌɛk.spəˈzɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
A clear and detailed explanation or account of an idea or theory, especially in writing.
detailed explanation or account

Examples

The professor's exposition of quantum mechanics made the complex subject much easier to understand.
Her clear exposition of the theory impressed everyone at the conference.
The textbook begins with an exposition of basic economic principles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explanation - a statement or account that makes something clear
  • Antonym: summary - a brief statement of the main points

Etymology

From Latin 'exponere' (to put forth, explain) via Old French 'exposicion'.
