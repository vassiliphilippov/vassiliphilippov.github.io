junkie

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌŋki / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌŋki / (BrE)
an informal term for a person addicted to narcotics or, more broadly, to drugs
person addicted to drugs (informal)
informal offensive

Examples

He spent years on the streets as a junkie, struggling to overcome his addiction.
The film tells the story of a young junkie trying to rebuild her life.
Some people use the word junkie to describe anyone with an intense dependency, but it's often considered offensive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: addict - a person who is addicted to a substance or activity
  • Synonym: user - a person who uses drugs, especially illegal drugs
  • Antonym: teetotaler - a person who never drinks alcohol or uses drugs

Etymology

From 'junk' (slang for narcotic drugs) + '-ie' (diminutive, familiar suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌŋki / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌŋki / (BrE)
An informal term for a person who is extremely enthusiastic about or addicted to a particular activity, interest, or hobby.
a person obsessively devoted to an activity or interest
informal

Examples

She became a real junkie for yoga after her first class.
He's always been a news junkie, checking updates every hour.
My friend is a tech junkie who loves to try every new gadget.

Synset Examples

a golf addict
a car nut
a bodybuilding freak
a news junkie

Related Terms

  • Synonym: addict - a person who is addicted to something, especially a substance or activity
  • Synonym: enthusiast - a person who is very interested in a particular activity or subject

Etymology

Originally from 'junk', slang for narcotic drugs, with '-ie' added as a diminutive or familiar form.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
