vesiculovirus

1. NOUN

/ vəˌsɪkjələˌvaɪrəs / (AmE), / vɪˌsɪkjʊləʊˌvaɪrəs / (BrE)
A genus of viruses that infects animals and causes vesicular stomatitis.
genus of viruses causing vesicular stomatitis
medical

Examples

The vesiculovirus is known for causing outbreaks of vesicular stomatitis in livestock.
Researchers studied the genetic structure of the vesiculovirus to develop better diagnostic tests.
A rapid response is required when a vesiculovirus infection is detected in a cattle population.

Etymology

From 'vesicular' (relating to small sacs or vesicles) + 'virus'.
