helm

1. NOUN

/ hɛlm / (AmE), / hɛlm / (BrE)
a position of leadership or control, especially as the person in charge of an organization or activity (used figuratively)
position of leadership or control

Examples

After decades at the company, she finally took the helm as CEO.
When new management is at the helm, drastic changes often follow.
The country prospered with an experienced leader at the helm.

Synset Examples

the President is at the helm of the Ship of State

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leadership - the action or position of leading a group of people or an organization

Etymology

From Old English helm (rudder; steering gear), related to Old High German helmo.

2. NOUN

/ hɛlm / (AmE), / hɛlm / (BrE)
the steering mechanism of a vessel, such as a wheel or tiller, used to guide its direction
steering mechanism of a vessel

Examples

The captain stood at the helm, calmly steering the ship through the storm.
He handed over the helm to his first mate during the night watch.
With one hand on the helm, she navigated the narrow channel carefully.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rudder - a flat piece, usually of wood, metal, or plastic, attached vertically to the stern of a boat for steering

Etymology

From Old English helm (rudder, ship’s steering apparatus), related to Old Norse hjalmr.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03518055-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ hɛlm / (AmE), / hɛlm / (BrE)
to steer or direct (a ship, boat, or, figuratively, an organization or enterprise); to be in control or leadership
to steer or lead; control

Examples

After years of hard work, she was finally chosen to helm the project.
The captain asked him to helm the ship in rough waters.
He was brought in to helm the struggling company and restore its reputation.

Synset Examples

helm the ship

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steer - to guide or control the course of a vehicle or vessel
  • Synonym: lead - to guide or direct a group or organization

Etymology

From Old English *helma* (rudder), originally referring to the handle or tiller for steering a ship.
