officeholder

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑː.fɪsˌhoʊl.dɚ / (AmE), / ˈɒ.fɪsˌhəʊl.də / (BrE)
a person who holds a position of authority or service, especially in government or an organization
person holding an official position

Examples

The new officeholder promised to fight corruption during her term.
Only eligible officeholders can vote in this committee meeting.
Every officeholder must take an oath before assuming responsibilities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incumbent - the current holder of an office or position

Etymology

Formed from 'office' + 'holder', referring to someone who holds an office.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑː.fɪsˌhoʊl.dɚ / (AmE), / ˈɒf.ɪsˌhəʊl.də / (BrE)
a person who has been appointed or elected to a position of authority or responsibility, especially in government or an organization
person holding a public or official post

Examples

After winning the election, she became the youngest officeholder in the city’s history.
Every public officeholder must take an oath of office before starting their duties.
Local officeholders attended the ceremony to show their support for the initiative.

Synset Examples

he is an officer of the court
the club elected its officers for the coming year

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incumbent - the current holder of an office or position

Etymology

From 'office' (a position of authority or trust) + 'holder' (one who holds).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10391149-n
Synset POS: n
