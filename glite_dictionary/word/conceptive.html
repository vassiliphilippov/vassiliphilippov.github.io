conceptive

1. ADJ

/ kənˈsɛptɪv / (AmE), / kənˈsɛptɪv / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) able to conceive offspring; capable of becoming pregnant.
capable of conceiving offspring

Examples

The doctor explained that only conceptive individuals can participate in this part of the study.
Certain medications can affect whether a woman remains conceptive.
In mammals, the period of being conceptive is strictly regulated by hormonal cycles.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: infertile - unable to conceive offspring
  • Synonym: fertile - able to produce offspring; capable of conceiving

Etymology

From Latin conceptivus, from concipere (to conceive).
