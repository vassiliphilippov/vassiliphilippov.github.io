stiff-necked

1. ADJ

/ ˈstɪfˌnɛkt / (AmE), / ˈstɪfˌnɛkt / (BrE)
unwilling to change your opinions or behavior; stubborn and obstinate
stubborn; unwilling to adapt

Examples

Her stiff-necked refusal to apologize only made things worse.
The company’s stiff-necked policies frustrated many of its employees.
He remained stiff-necked even when presented with clear evidence.

Synset Examples

Being stiff-necked can lead to missed opportunities.
The stiff-necked leadership resisted any changes.
His stiff-necked attitude made negotiations difficult.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stubborn - unwilling to change one's attitude or position
  • Antonym: flexible - willing to change or compromise

Etymology

Figurative use from the literal sense of having a stiff neck, traditionally meaning proud or obstinate; used in the Bible and English since the 16th century.
