beltway

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛltˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈbɛltˌweɪ / (BrE)
A highway that goes around the edge of a city, allowing traffic to avoid the city center.
highway encircling a city
american

Examples

Traffic was backed up for miles on the beltway during rush hour.
The new beltway will help reduce congestion downtown by diverting through traffic.
Many commuters use the beltway to avoid driving through the city center.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ring road - a road that goes around the edge of a town or city to reduce traffic in the center

Etymology

Formed from 'belt' (something that encircles) and 'way' (road or path), first used in mid-20th century American English.
