to waft

1. VERB

/ wɑft / (AmE), / wɑːft / (BrE)
(of sound, scent, or a light object) to be carried lightly and smoothly through the air or over water.
be carried lightly through air or over water

Examples

The scent of jasmine wafted through the open window on the evening breeze.
A gentle melody wafted from the concert hall and filled the courtyard.
Her words barely wafted above a whisper, lost to the hum of the room.

Synset Examples

Sounds wafted into the room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: float - to move slowly or lightly through the air or on water

Etymology

From Middle English 'waften' (to wave, brandish), probably from Low German or Dutch 'waft' (a movement through air).

2. VERB

/ wɑft, wæft / (AmE), / wɒft, wɑːft / (BrE)
to carry or move gently through the air, especially by a light breeze
to carry lightly through the air

Examples

The scent of fresh bread wafted from the bakery down the street.
A gentle breeze wafted the curtains into the room.
She watched the soap bubbles waft slowly across the garden.

Synset Examples

A breeze wafted through the door

Related Terms

  • Synonym: float - to move slowly or lightly through the air or on water

Etymology

From Middle English *waften* (to wave), probably from Old Norse *vápta* (to wrap up).
