mortice

1. VERB

/ ˈmɔr.tɪs / (AmE), / ˈmɔː.tɪs / (BrE)
to join pieces of wood by fitting a tenon into a mortise
join wood using tenon and mortise

Examples

The carpenter expertly morticed the beams together to form a sturdy frame.
After cutting the slots, he began morticing the joints for the window frame.
The craftsman taught his apprentice how to mortice two timbers securely.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tenon - a projection on the end of a piece of wood that is inserted into a mortise

Etymology

From Old French 'mortaise', of uncertain origin, probably from Arabic 'murtazz' (fastened).

2. VERB

/ ˈmɔːr.tɪs / (AmE), / ˈmɔː.tɪs / (BrE)
to cut a hole or recess in (a piece of wood or other material) to receive a tenon or similar projection.
to cut a hole for a tenon

Examples

A carpenter will carefully mortice the joint to ensure a perfect fit.
She morticed the frame before attaching the tenon.
They are morticing the wood for the new door frame.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tenon - a projection made to fit into a mortice

Etymology

From Old French 'mortaise', possibly ultimately from Arabic 'murtazz' (fastened).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01296385-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɔːrtɪs / (AmE), / ˈmɔːtɪs / (BrE)
A square or rectangular hole cut into wood or another material to receive a tenon and form a joint.
hole to receive a tenon in joinery

Examples

The carpenter carefully chiseled a mortice into the beam to fit the tenon.
Each joint was secured by a mortice and tenon, ensuring stability.
Learning to cut a perfect mortice is an essential woodworking skill.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tenon - a projecting piece shaped to fit into a mortice, forming a joint

Etymology

From Old French *mortaise*, of uncertain origin.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
