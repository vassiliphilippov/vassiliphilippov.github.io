saccade

1. NOUN

/ səˈkɑd / (AmE), / səˈkɑːd / (BrE)
a rapid, jerky movement, especially of the eye as it changes focus from one point to another
quick, jerky eye movement
medical

Examples

The doctor observed a rapid saccade as the patient shifted her gaze from left to right.
During reading, our eyes make quick saccades between words rather than moving smoothly.
A strong light caused an involuntary saccade in his peripheral vision.

Etymology

From French 'saccade' (jerk, jolt), from 'saccader' (to jerk).

2. NOUN

/ səˈkɑd / (AmE), / səˈkɑːd / (BrE)
a rapid, jerky movement of the eyes as they quickly shift from one point of focus to another
rapid eye movement

Examples

The doctor observed the patient's saccade patterns during the eye examination.
Video games can improve the speed of a person's saccade when tracking moving targets.
During reading, your eyes make several saccades as they jump from word to word.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fixation - the steady focusing of the eyes on a single object

Etymology

From French 'saccade' (a jerk), from 'saccader' (to jerk).
