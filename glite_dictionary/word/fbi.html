FBI

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛf.biːˈaɪ / (AmE), / ˌɛf.biːˈaɪ / (BrE)
the Federal Bureau of Investigation, the main federal law enforcement agency in the United States, responsible for investigating crimes and enforcing laws under the jurisdiction of the Department of Justice
main U.S. federal law enforcement agency
american

Examples

The FBI launched an investigation into the cyberattack last month.
She contacted the FBI after receiving threats from an anonymous source.
Several agents from the FBI visited the crime scene to collect evidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: CIA - the Central Intelligence Agency, a U.S. foreign intelligence service
  • Degree Lower: police - the civil force of a state responsible for maintaining public order and preventing crime

Etymology

Initialism of Federal Bureau of Investigation.
