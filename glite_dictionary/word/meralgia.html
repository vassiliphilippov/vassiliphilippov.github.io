meralgia

1. NOUN

/ məˈrældʒiə / (AmE), / məˈrældʒɪə / (BrE)
Pain or discomfort located in the thigh, especially due to nerve disorder or compression.
pain in the thigh
medical

Examples

After sitting for several hours, he experienced a sharp meralgia in his right leg.
The doctor diagnosed her with meralgia following complaints of persistent thigh pain.
Physical therapy is often recommended to alleviate the symptoms of meralgia.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'mēros' (thigh) and 'algos' (pain).
