shadowing

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃædoʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃadəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of following someone or something secretly in order to observe or monitor them
secret following or surveillance

Examples

The detective spent weeks shadowing the suspect before gathering enough evidence.
Government agents were accused of shadowing political dissidents during the crackdown.
He noticed that shadowing someone requires patience and careful observation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tailing - the act of following someone, especially in surveillance
  • Degree Lower: pursuit - the act of pursuing or chasing someone or something

Etymology

From the verb 'shadow' meaning 'to follow secretly', originally from Old English 'sceadwian' (to provide shade, protect).
