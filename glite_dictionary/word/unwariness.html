unwariness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈwɛri.nəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈweə.ri.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of not being cautious or watchful; a lack of alertness to danger or risk.
lack of caution or watchfulness

Examples

His unwariness in the busy city led him into trouble more than once.
The tourist's unwariness made her an easy target for pickpockets.
We must warn new employees that unwariness can result in costly mistakes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: wariness - the quality of being cautious and watchful
  • Synonym: caution - careful attention to potential danger or risk
  • Synonym: recklessness - the quality of being careless of danger

Etymology

From prefix un- (not) + wariness (from wary, meaning cautious or watchful).
