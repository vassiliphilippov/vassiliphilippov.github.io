Acheta assimilis

1. NOUN

/ əˈkiːtə əˈsɪmɪlɪs / (AmE), / əˈkiːtə əˈsɪmɪlɪs / (BrE)
A species of black cricket native to the Americas, known for damaging crops and sometimes invading houses.
a black American cricket species

Examples

Farmers sometimes struggle to control infestations of Acheta assimilis in their fields.
The chirping of Acheta assimilis can sometimes be heard inside homes during late summer.
Acheta assimilis is known to cause significant damage to cereal crops if left unchecked.

Etymology

From Latin, genus Acheta (a cricket) and species name assimilis (similar).
