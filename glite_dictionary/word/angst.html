angst

1. NOUN

/ æŋst / (AmE), / æŋst / (BrE)
A strong feeling of anxiety, dread, or apprehension, especially about the human condition or personal freedom.
deep anxiety or dread, often philosophical

Examples

Teenagers often experience angst as they try to find their place in the world.
His poetry expresses the existential angst of modern life.
She couldn't shake the angst that something important was missing from her life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anxiety - A feeling of worry, nervousness, or unease about something with an uncertain outcome
  • Synonym: fear - An unpleasant emotion caused by the threat of danger, pain, or harm
  • Antonym: calm - The absence of strong emotion, especially anxiety or fear

Etymology

From German 'Angst' (anxiety, fear), ultimately from Old High German 'angust'.
