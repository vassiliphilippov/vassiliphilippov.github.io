hydrographic

1. ADJ

/ ˌhaɪ.drəˈɡræf.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌhaɪ.drəˈɡræf.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to the study or mapping of bodies of water, especially the measurement of their physical characteristics
relating to mapping or study of water bodies

Examples

The team conducted a hydrographic survey of the river to update navigation charts.
She works for the national hydrographic office, specializing in mapping coastal waters.
New hydrographic equipment has improved the accuracy of underwater mapping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrography - the scientific study and mapping of bodies of water

Etymology

From French hydrographique, from hydrographie, originally from Greek hydōr (water) + graphia (writing).
