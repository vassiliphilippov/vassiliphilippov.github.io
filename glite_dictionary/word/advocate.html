advocate

1. NOUN

/ ˈæd.vəˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈæd.və.kət / (BrE)
A lawyer who represents and pleads the case of a client in a court of law.
lawyer who pleads cases in court

Examples

She hired an experienced advocate to defend her case in court.
The advocate presented convincing arguments to the jury.
As an advocate, he has represented several high-profile clients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: barrister - a lawyer entitled to represent clients in the higher courts
  • Synonym: solicitor - a lawyer advising clients and preparing cases, but not always representing them in court (UK)

Etymology

From Latin 'advocatus', past participle of 'advocare' (to call to one's aid).

2. VERB

/ ˈæd.vəˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈæd.və.keɪt / (BrE)
to publicly recommend or support a particular cause, policy, or action
recommend or support publicly

Examples

Many experts advocate taking regular exercise to maintain good health.
She continues to advocate for equal rights in the workplace.
Environmental groups strongly advocated reducing single-use plastics at the conference.

Synset Examples

The travel agent recommended strongly that we not travel on Thanksgiving Day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to recommend - to suggest that something is good or suitable
  • Antonym: to oppose - to disapprove of and attempt to prevent something

Etymology

From Latin 'advocatus' (a person called to aid), past participle of 'advocare'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈæd.və.keɪt / (AmE), / ˈæd.və.kət / (BrE)
a person who publicly supports or recommends a particular cause or policy
a person who supports or argues for a cause

Examples

She has always been a passionate advocate for environmental protection.
As an advocate of equal rights, he spoke at the rally.
The organization is seeking an advocate to help represent the needs of local children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: proponent - a person who puts forward an idea or proposal
  • Antonym: opponent - a person who disagrees with or fights against a cause

Etymology

From Latin 'advocatus' (one called to aid), from 'advocare' (to call to, summon).
