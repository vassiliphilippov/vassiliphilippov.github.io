Aegypius monachus

1. NOUN

/ iːˈdʒɪpiəs ˈmɒnəkəs / (AmE), / ɪˈdʒɪpɪəs ˈmɒnəkəs / (BrE)
A large Old World vulture native to southern Eurasia and northern Africa, also known as the cinereous vulture or black vulture.
large vulture of Eurasia and northern Africa

Examples

The Aegypius monachus is one of the largest birds of prey found in Europe.
Conservation efforts have helped increase the population of Aegypius monachus in recent years.
Unlike other vultures, the Aegypius monachus prefers forested habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cinereous vulture - another common name for Aegypius monachus
  • Degree Lower: griffon vulture - a related species of large vulture

Etymology

From Latin: 'Aegypius' from Greek 'aigyps' (vulture); 'monachus' meaning 'monk', referring to its hooded appearance.
