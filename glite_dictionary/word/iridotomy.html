iridotomy

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪr.ɪˈdɑː.tə.mi / (AmE), / ˌɪr.ɪˈdɒ.tə.mi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure involving an incision in the iris of the eye to enlarge the pupil or treat closed-angle glaucoma
surgical incision in the iris
medical

Examples

The ophthalmologist recommended an iridotomy to help relieve the patient's glaucoma symptoms.
After undergoing iridotomy, the patient reported improved vision and less pain in her eye.
Laser iridotomy is a common procedure for preventing acute angle-closure glaucoma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iridectomy - surgical removal of part of the iris

Etymology

From Greek iris (rainbow, iris of the eye) + -tomy (cutting, incision).
