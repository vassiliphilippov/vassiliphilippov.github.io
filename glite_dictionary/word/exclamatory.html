exclamatory

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈsklæməˌtɔri / (AmE), / ɪkˈsklæmətəri / (BrE)
expressing a sudden and strong emotion or feeling, often marked by an exclamation
expressing sudden, strong emotion

Examples

His exclamatory remark startled everyone in the room.
The teacher's voice grew exclamatory when she announced the surprise.
She wrote an exclamatory sentence to show her excitement.

Synset Examples

an emphatic no

Related Terms

  • Synonym: emphatic - expressing something forcibly and clearly

Etymology

From Latin exclamatorius, from exclamare (to cry out).
