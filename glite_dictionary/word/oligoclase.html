oligoclase

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɪɡəkleɪs / (AmE), / ɒˈlɪɡəkleɪz / (BrE)
a mineral of the plagioclase feldspar group, typically clear to white, that commonly forms in igneous rocks
a feldspar mineral found in rocks

Examples

The granite sample contained visible crystals of oligoclase mixed with quartz.
Geologists study oligoclase to understand the formation of certain igneous rocks.
Some gemstones are cut from clear varieties of oligoclase.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: feldspar - a group of rock-forming minerals including oligoclase

Etymology

From German Oligoklas, from Greek oligos (few) + klasis (breaking), referring to its cleavage.
