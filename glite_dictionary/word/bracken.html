bracken

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrækən / (AmE), / ˈbrækən / (BrE)
A large, coarse fern with wide, divided fronds, often growing abundantly as undergrowth in woodland or open areas.
large coarse fern

Examples

The hillside was covered in dense bracken that rustled with every step.
After the rain, the bracken seemed to glisten under the morning sun.
Deer could often be seen hiding in the tall bracken near the edge of the forest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fern - a plant with feathery or leafy fronds and no flowers, reproducing by spores

Etymology

From Middle English braken, of uncertain origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbræk.ən / (AmE), / ˈbræk.ən / (BrE)
a large, coarse fern, especially of the genus Pteridium, commonly found in temperate and subtropical regions
a large, coarse fern

Examples

The hillside was covered in dense bracken and heather.
After the fire, only the resilient bracken seemed to thrive in the scorched earth.
They hiked through the woods, pushing aside tall bracken that brushed their legs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fern - any plant of the group Pteridophyta, to which bracken belongs

Etymology

From Old Norse 'brækni', meaning fern.
