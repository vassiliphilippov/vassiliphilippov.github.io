freewheeling

1. ADJ

/ ˈfriˌwilɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfriːˌwiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
not concerned about rules or conventions; cheerfully irresponsible and unconstrained in attitude or behavior.
cheerfully irresponsible

Examples

Her freewheeling attitude made her popular at parties but sometimes got her into trouble.
The company thrived under a freewheeling management style that encouraged innovation.
After college, he enjoyed a few freewheeling years traveling across Europe.

Synset Examples

carefree with his money
freewheeling urban youths
had a harum-scarum youth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carefree - having no worries or anxieties
  • Synonym: reckless - without regard for consequences
  • Antonym: cautious - showing care about possible dangers or risks

Etymology

Formed from 'freewheel' (move or act freely) + '-ing'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈfriˌ(h)wilɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfriːˌwiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
not limited by rules, conventions, or responsibilities; carefree and uninhibited.
carefree, unconstrained by rules

Examples

Her freewheeling approach to management inspired creativity in the team.
He is known for his freewheeling sense of humor and spontaneous decisions.
The festival had a freewheeling atmosphere that encouraged everyone to join in.

Synset Examples

freewheeling foolishness
the versatility of his poetic freewheeling style

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uninhibited - not restrained or held back; expressing oneself freely
  • Antonym: restrained - kept under control; limited or restricted

Etymology

From 'freewheel' (to coast without using the pedals), originally referring to bicycle mechanisms.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02008336-s
Synset POS: s
