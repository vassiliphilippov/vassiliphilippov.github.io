isotherm

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪsəˌθɝm / (AmE), / ˈaɪsəʊˌθɜːm / (BrE)
A line drawn on a map or chart connecting points that have the same temperature at a particular time.
line on a map of equal temperature

Examples

The weather map displayed an isotherm passing through the city, indicating consistent temperatures across the region.
Meteorologists use isotherms to analyze temperature patterns and predict weather changes.
An isotherm helps visualize areas that share the same temperature at a specific moment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isobar - line connecting places of equal atmospheric pressure

Etymology

From Greek 'isos' (equal) + 'therme' (heat).
