episcleral vein

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈsklɛrəl veɪn / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈsklɪərəl veɪn / (BrE)
a small vein located in the sclera near the corneal margin, which drains into the anterior ciliary veins
vein in the eye's sclera near the cornea
medical

Examples

Increased pressure in the episcleral veins can lead to ocular complications.
The doctor examined the episcleral veins for signs of inflammation during the eye checkup.
Abnormalities in the episcleral vein may indicate underlying issues with eye drainage.

Etymology

From Greek 'epi-' (upon) + 'sclera' (the white of the eye), referring to location upon the sclera.
