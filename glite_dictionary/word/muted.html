muted

1. ADJ

/ ˈmjutɪd / (AmE), / ˈmjuːtɪd / (BrE)
(of sound) made softer, less loud, or less distinct than usual.
(of sound) softened or muffled

Examples

The music in the restaurant was muted so customers could talk comfortably.
She spoke in a muted tone, barely above a whisper.
Through the window, we heard the muted sounds of traffic outside.

Synset Examples

the dull boom of distant breaking waves
muffled drums
the muffled noises of the street
muted trumpets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muffled - (of sound) not loud because of being obstructed in some way

Etymology

From the verb 'mute', from Old French muter, from Latin mutare ‘to change’.

2. ADJ

/ ˈmjutɪd / (AmE), / ˈmjuːtɪd / (BrE)
(of sound) made quieter, softer, or less intense than usual; subdued in tone.
softened or subdued in sound

Examples

Her voice was muted, barely louder than a whisper over the phone.
The orchestra played with muted trumpets to create a gentler mood.
Their conversation took on a muted tone when the children were asleep nearby.

Synset Examples

hushed voices
muted trumpets
a subdued whisper
a quiet reprimand

Related Terms

  • Synonym: subdued - (of sound) quiet and controlled; not loud
  • Synonym: hushed - very quiet; made silent or less loud
  • Antonym: loud - producing much noise; not quiet or soft

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'mute', from Middle French 'muter', from Latin 'mutare' (to change).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01458319-s
Synset POS: s
