rimu

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːmuː / (AmE), / ˈriːmuː / (BrE)
a tall evergreen tree of New Zealand (Dacrydium cupressinum), valued for its timber and distinctive reddish bark
tall New Zealand timber tree

Examples

The furniture was beautifully crafted from rimu wood.
A mature rimu can reach a height of over 50 meters.
Conservation efforts are helping to protect native rimu forests.

Etymology

From Maori 'rimu', the indigenous name for the tree.
