medroxyprogesterone

1. NOUN

/ məˌdrɑksiˌproʊˈdʒɛstəˌroʊn / (AmE), / mɛˌdrɒksɪprəʊˈdʒɛstərəʊn / (BrE)
a synthetic progestin compound used to treat menstrual disorders and as part of hormone therapy
a synthetic progestin medication
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed medroxyprogesterone to help regulate her menstrual cycle.
Side effects of medroxyprogesterone may include headaches and changes in mood.
Women with certain health conditions should consult their physician before taking medroxyprogesterone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: progesterone - a natural female hormone involved in the menstrual cycle and pregnancy

Etymology

Formed from chemical roots 'methyl', 'oxy', and 'progesterone'. 'Progesterone' from 'pro-' (supporting) and 'gestation'.
