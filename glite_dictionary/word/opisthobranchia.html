Opisthobranchia

1. NOUN

/ oʊˌpɪsθoʊˈbræŋkiə / (AmE), / əʊˌpɪsθəˈbræŋkɪə / (BrE)
A large group of soft-bodied marine gastropod mollusks, such as sea slugs, characterized by gills located behind the heart and the absence of an operculum.
group of soft-bodied marine gastropods

Examples

Many species within the Opisthobranchia display vibrant colors as a warning to predators.
Researchers study the Opisthobranchia to better understand the diversity of marine mollusks.
Sea slugs, a popular subgroup of the Opisthobranchia, inhabit coral reefs worldwide.

Etymology

From Greek opistho- (behind) + branchia (gills).

2. NOUN

/ oʊˌpɪsθoʊˈbræŋkiə / (AmE), / əʊˌpɪsθəˈbraŋkɪə / (BrE)
a subclass of gastropod mollusks characterized by gills located behind the heart and typically lacking an operculum, including sea slugs, sea butterflies, and sea hares
a subclass of marine gastropod mollusks
medical

Examples

The brilliant colors of some Opisthobranchia make them popular subjects for underwater photography.
Biologists are studying the nervous systems of Opisthobranchia to better understand animal behavior.
Many members of the Opisthobranchia have evolved unique methods of defense against predators.

Etymology

From Greek 'opistho-' (behind) and 'branchia' (gills).
