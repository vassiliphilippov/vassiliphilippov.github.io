imperative

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɛrətɪv / (AmE), / ɪmˈperətɪv / (BrE)
A verb form or sentence that expresses a command, request, or instruction, typically in the imperative mood.
verb form expressing a command

Examples

The sentence 'Sit down' contains an imperative.
Teachers often use the imperative to give instructions in class.
In English, the imperative usually starts with the base form of the verb.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indicative - verb mood expressing statements of fact
  • Antonym: subjunctive - verb mood expressing wishes, hypotheticals, or necessities

Etymology

From Latin imperativus (commanding), from imperare (to command).
