dependent

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈpɛndənt / (AmE), / dɪˈpɛndənt / (BrE)
a person who relies on another, especially for financial support
person relying on another for support

Examples

She claimed her three children as dependents on her tax return.
The company offers health insurance for employees and their dependents.
After losing his job, Tom struggled to support all of his dependents.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: independent - not reliant on others; self-sufficient

Etymology

From Latin 'dependere' (to hang down, depend).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10024466-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈpɛndənt / (AmE), / dɪˈpɛndənt / (BrE)
relying on someone or something else for support, help, or what is needed to function or survive.
relying on others or things for support

Examples

After the accident, he became extremely dependent on his family for daily activities.
Many desert plants are dependent on very small amounts of rain to survive.
Some students remain financially dependent on their parents until they finish university.

Synset Examples

dependent on moisture

Related Terms

  • Antonym: independent - not relying on others; free from dependence

Etymology

From Latin 'dependens', present participle of 'dependere' (to hang from, depend).

3. ADJ

/ dɪˈpɛndənt / (AmE), / dɪˈpɛndənt / (BrE)
(of something) determined or controlled by something else; contingent upon another factor or condition.
contingent on something else

Examples

The success of the project is dependent on teamwork and cooperation.
His recovery is largely dependent on following the doctor's advice.
Economic growth in the area is dependent upon new infrastructure investments.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: independent - not depending on something else; free from external control

Etymology

From Latin 'dependere' (to hang down, to depend).

4. ADJ

/ dɪˈpɛndənt / (AmE), / dɪˈpɛndənt / (BrE)
Addicted to a drug and unable to function normally without it.
addicted to a drug

Examples

After years of substance abuse, he became dependent on painkillers.
Patients who are dependent on opioids often require supervised withdrawal.
She realized she was dependent on sleeping pills to get through the night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: addicted - physically or mentally dependent on a substance

Etymology

From Latin dependēns, dependere (to hang down, be dependent).

5. ADJ

/ dɪˈpɛndənt / (AmE), / dɪˈpɛndənt / (BrE)
(of a clause) unable to stand alone as a complete sentence and requiring another clause for meaning.
not able to stand alone (of a clause)

Examples

A dependent clause must be attached to an independent clause to form a complete sentence.
The sentence, 'Although it was raining, we went outside,' begins with a dependent clause.
Grammar exercises often ask students to identify dependent clauses within complex sentences.

Synset Examples

(of a country, territory, or person) subject to the authority, control, or sovereignty of another.
subject to authority or control of another

Examples

The island is a dependent province governed by a distant country.
