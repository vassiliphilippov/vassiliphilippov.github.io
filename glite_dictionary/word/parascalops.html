Parascalops

1. NOUN

/ ˌpærəˈskælɑps / (AmE), / ˌparəˈskalɒps / (BrE)
A genus of North American moles, known as Brewer's moles, found in eastern parts of the United States and Canada.
genus of North American moles

Examples

The Parascalops is primarily found in moist woodland habitats in eastern North America.
Scientists recently conducted a study on the burrowing patterns of Parascalops.
Unlike some other moles, Parascalops has specialized adaptations for efficient digging.

Etymology

From Greek 'para' (beside, near) + 'Scalops' (another genus of moles).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpærəˈskæləps / (AmE), / ˌparəˈskaləps / (BrE)
A genus of North American moles, commonly known as brewer's moles, in the family Talpidae.
genus of North American moles

Examples

The Parascalops genus includes the well-known brewer's mole native to eastern North America.
Researchers compared burrowing behaviors within Parascalops to other genera of moles.
Conservation efforts focus on protecting the habitats of Parascalops species.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining 'para-' (beside, near) and 'Scalops' (another mole genus).
