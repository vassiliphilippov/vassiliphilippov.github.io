Ocyurus chrysurus

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈsaɪjʊrəs kraɪˈsʊrəs / (AmE), / əʊˈsaɪjʊərəs kraɪˈsjʊərəs / (BrE)
A species of snapper found in the tropical Atlantic and Caribbean, recognized for a broad yellow stripe along its sides and tail; valued as a food fish.
a tropical Atlantic and Caribbean snapper with yellow stripes

Examples

The Ocyurus chrysurus is highly prized by both commercial and sport fishermen.
We spotted several Ocyurus chrysurus swimming near the coral reefs in the Bahamas.
Grilled Ocyurus chrysurus is a specialty at coastal Caribbean restaurants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yellowtail snapper - Common name for Ocyurus chrysurus

Etymology

From Greek *okyros* (swift) and *chrysos* (gold), referencing the fish’s appearance and movement.
