implantation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪm.plænˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪm.plænˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process or act of surgically placing something, such as an object or substance, into the human body
surgical insertion into the body
medical

Examples

After the implantation of the pacemaker, the patient's heartbeat returned to normal.
New techniques have made implantation of artificial joints safer and more efficient.
The implantation of dental implants requires careful preparation of the jawbone.

Synset Examples

the implantation of radioactive pellets in the prostate gland

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insertion - the act of inserting something

Etymology

From Latin implantatio, from implantare 'to plant in'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪm.plænˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪm.plænˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process in which a fertilized egg attaches itself to the lining of the uterus in placental mammals
attachment of fertilized egg to uterus
medical

Examples

Successful implantation is essential for pregnancy to continue beyond the earliest stages.
Doctors can monitor implantation during fertility treatments to assess the likelihood of pregnancy.
The process of implantation typically happens about a week after fertilization.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: conception - the process of becoming pregnant, especially fertilization of the egg
  • Degree Higher: gestation - the period of development in the uterus from implantation to birth

Etymology

From Latin implantare (to plant in), from in- (in) + plantare (to plant).

3. NOUN

/ ˌɪm.plænˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪm.plɑːnˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of planting or placing something in the ground
placing something in the ground

Examples

The implantation of the pole required careful measuring and digging.
Successful implantation of the sensors ensured the area was correctly monitored.
After the implantation of the tree saplings, the volunteers watered each one thoroughly.

Etymology

From Latin implanatio, from implantere, meaning 'to plant in'.
