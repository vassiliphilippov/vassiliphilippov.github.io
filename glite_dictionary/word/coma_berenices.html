Coma Berenices

1. PROPN

/ ˌkoʊmə ˌbɛrəˈnaɪsiz / (AmE), / ˌkəʊmə ˌbɛrəˈnaɪsiːz / (BrE)
a constellation in the Northern Hemisphere, located between Ursa Major and Boötes, known for containing a large cluster of galaxies
constellation in the Northern Hemisphere

Examples

The galaxy cluster in Coma Berenices is one of the richest known to astronomers.
You can spot Coma Berenices between the constellations of Ursa Major and Boötes on a clear night.
Many amateur astronomers observe Coma Berenices to find its distinctive star cluster.

Etymology

Latin for 'Berenice's Hair', named after Queen Berenice II of Egypt, who is said to have sacrificed her hair to the gods.
