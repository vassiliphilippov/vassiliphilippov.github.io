squinter

1. NOUN

/ ˈskwɪntər / (AmE), / ˈskwɪntə / (BrE)
a person who has strabismus (a condition in which the eyes do not properly align with each other)
person with misaligned eyes (strabismus)

Examples

The doctor referred the squinter to a specialist for further evaluation.
Children who are born a squinter may often develop normal vision if treated early.
He felt self-conscious as a squinter during his school years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: strabismus - a medical condition where the eyes are not properly aligned
