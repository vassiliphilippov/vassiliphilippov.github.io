restraint

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈstreɪnt / (AmE), / rɪˈstreɪnt / (BrE)
the act of limiting or controlling the actions or movement of someone or something
the act of limiting control

Examples

The court imposed a restraint on the company's trading activities.
Physical restraint was used to prevent him from injuring himself.
Strict restraint is often necessary in emergency situations.

Synset Examples

the unlawful restraint of trade

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restriction - a limiting condition or measure
  • Antonym: liberation - the act of setting someone free

Etymology

From Old French *restreinte*, past participle of *restreindre* (to restrain), from Latin *restringere* (to bind back).
