hazardous

1. ADJ

/ ˈhæzərdəs / (AmE), / ˈhaz.ə.dəs / (BrE)
involving risk, danger, or potential harm
involving risk or danger

Examples

The abandoned factory contains several hazardous chemicals that must be handled with care.
Working at construction sites can be a hazardous occupation without proper safety measures.
They warned us that driving on icy roads is especially hazardous at night.

Synset Examples

skydiving is a hazardous sport
extremely risky going out in the tide and fog
a wild financial scheme

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dangerous - likely to cause harm or injury
  • Antonym: safe - not likely to cause harm or danger

Etymology

From Old French hasard (a game of chance), from Arabic az-zahr (the dice).
