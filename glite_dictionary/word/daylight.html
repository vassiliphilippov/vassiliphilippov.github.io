daylight

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈdeɪ.laɪt / (BrE)
the natural light of the day; light from the sun during the daytime
natural light during the day

Examples

The room filled with daylight as the sun rose above the horizon.
He prefers to work in natural daylight rather than artificial lighting.
The flowers only bloom when exposed to enough daylight each day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sunlight - light from the sun

Etymology

From Middle English 'daylight', formed from 'day' + 'light'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈdeɪlaɪt / (BrE)
the period of time during which there is natural light from the sun between sunrise and sunset
period of natural light between sunrise and sunset

Examples

We should finish the hike while there is still daylight left.
Repairs are much easier to complete during daylight hours.
The garden looks beautiful in the bright daylight of noon.

Synset Examples

the dawn turned night into day
it is easier to make the repairs in the daytime

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nighttime - the period of darkness between sunset and sunrise
  • Degree Lower: dawn - the time when daylight first appears in the morning
  • Synonym: sunlight - light from the sun, especially when considered as shining on the earth

Etymology

Formed from 'day' + 'light' in Middle English.
