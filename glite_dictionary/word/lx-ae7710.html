lx

1. NOUN

/ ɛl ɛks / (AmE), / ɛl ɛks / (BrE)
A unit of illuminance equal to one lumen per square meter.
unit of illuminance, one lumen per square meter

Examples

In the laboratory, the lighting was measured at 500 lx.
A recommended workspace requires at least 300 lx for optimal visibility.
The sensor displayed a sudden rise to 1000 lx when the lights were turned on.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lux - unit of illuminance equal to one lumen per square meter

Etymology

Abbreviation of lux, from Latin 'lux' meaning 'light'.
