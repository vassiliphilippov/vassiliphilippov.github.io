Penelope

1. NOUN

/ pəˈnɛləpi / (AmE), / pɛˈnɛləpi / (BrE)
A genus of large, arboreal, turkey-like birds in the family Cracidae, commonly known as guans, found in Central and South America.
a genus of guans (arboreal game birds)

Examples

The Penelope genus includes several species that inhabit the forests of Central America.
Scientists are studying the mating habits of the Penelope guans in the Amazon region.
Members of the Penelope genus are important game birds for many local communities.

Etymology

Named after Penelope, a character in Greek mythology; used as a scientific genus name in zoology.

2. PROPN

/ pəˈnɛləpi / (AmE), / pəˈnɛləpi / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the wife of Odysseus, renowned for her faithfulness and devotion during his long absence after the Trojan War.
faithful wife of Odysseus in Greek mythology

Examples

In the Odyssey, Penelope cleverly delays her suitors by weaving and unweaving a shroud.
Some consider Penelope a symbol of unwavering loyalty and patience.
The story of Penelope has inspired countless works of art and literature.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Πηνελόπη (Pēnelópē), a name found in Homer.

3. NOUN

/ pəˈnɛləpi / (AmE), / pəˈnɛləpi / (BrE)
A genus of guans, which are large, arboreal, turkey-like birds native to Central and South America.
genus of turkey-like birds (guans)

Examples

The Penelope genus includes several important game birds found in South America.
Conservation efforts are being made to protect species within the Penelope genus from habitat loss.
Researchers have studied the diet of Penelope to understand their ecological role in the rainforest.

Etymology

From Greek mythological name Penelopē, used in taxonomy following tradition of using mythological or classical names.
