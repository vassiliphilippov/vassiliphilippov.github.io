heroic

1. ADJ

/ hɪˈroʊɪk / (AmE), / hɪˈrəʊɪk / (BrE)
having or showing the qualities, such as courage or bravery, that are admired in a hero or heroine
showing bravery or courage like a hero

Examples

She made a heroic effort to save the child from the burning house.
Their heroic actions during the crisis inspired the entire community.
Climbing the mountain in winter was a truly heroic achievement.

Synset Examples

the heroic attack on the beaches of Normandy
heroic explorers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brave - showing courage
  • Synonym: courageous - not deterred by danger or pain; brave
  • Antonym: cowardly - lacking courage; excessively afraid

Etymology

From Latin heroicus, from Greek hērōikos (pertaining to heroes).

3. NOUN

/ həˈroʊɪk / (AmE), / hɪˈrəʊɪk / (BrE)
A verse form used in poetry for grand or elevated themes, typically dactylic hexameter in classical poetry or iambic pentameter in English.
verse form for elevated themes

Examples

Milton's 'Paradise Lost' is written in heroic verse, utilizing blank verse iambic pentameter throughout.
Dactylic hexameter is the standard heroic form in ancient Greek and Latin epic poetry.
Many poets adopted the heroic as the preferred structure for their most ambitious works.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heroic couplet - a pair of rhymed lines in iambic pentameter, especially as used in 18th-century English verse

Etymology

From Latin heroicus, from Greek hēroïkos (pertaining to a hero).
