oasis

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈeɪsɪs / (AmE), / əʊˈeɪsɪs / (BrE)
A place or situation that provides refuge, relief, or a feeling of safety in an otherwise difficult, troubled, or dangerous environment.
place of safety or refuge

Examples

Her cottage in the countryside was an oasis of peace in the chaos of city life.
After hours of hiking, the shaded rest stop felt like an oasis for the weary travelers.
The public library became an oasis for students seeking quiet and comfort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sanctuary - a place of refuge or safety
  • Synonym: refuge - a condition of being safe or sheltered from pursuit, danger, or trouble

Etymology

From Arabic 'wāḥa' (desert oasis), via Greek 'oasīs'.

2. NOUN

/ oʊˈeɪsɪs / (AmE), / əʊˈeɪsɪs / (BrE)
An area in a desert where water is found and plants grow, making it fertile.
fertile area in a desert

Examples

The caravan stopped at the oasis to rest and refill their water supplies.
After days of traveling through the hot desert, they finally discovered a small oasis.
The village was built around an oasis, making it the only green spot for miles.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: desert - a barren, sandy region with little or no vegetation

Etymology

From Greek *oasis*, via Latin, referring to a fertile place in a desert.
