Lycaeon

1. NOUN

/ laɪˈsiːən / (AmE), / lɪˈsiːən / (BrE)
A genus of wild African dog, known as the African hunting dog, distinguished by its mottled coat and large, rounded ears.
genus of African hunting dog

Examples

The Lycaeon is known for its complex social structure and cooperative hunting techniques.
Several reserves in southern Africa are home to the rare Lycaeon.
Scientists are studying the behavior and conservation needs of the Lycaeon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: African wild dog - a large, social, African canid species with a mottled coat

Etymology

From Greek *Lukaíōn*, relating to the wolf (‘lýkos’).
