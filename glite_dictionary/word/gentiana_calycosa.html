Gentiana calycosa

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛn(t)ʃiˈænə ˌkælɪˈkoʊsə / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛn(t)siˈɑːnə ˌkælɪˈkəʊsə / (BrE)
A perennial herb of western North America, with tufted or sprawling growth and blue, green-spotted flowers.
perennial with blue, green-spotted flowers

Examples

The Gentiana calycosa thrives in moist mountain meadows during the summer.
We spotted a cluster of Gentiana calycosa along the hiking trail in California.
Gardeners often admire the deep blue flowers of Gentiana calycosa for their striking appearance.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Gentiana' is a plant genus named for King Gentius of Illyria; 'calycosa' refers to the prominent calyx.
