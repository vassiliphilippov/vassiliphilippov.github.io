Tulostoma

1. NOUN

/ tuːˈlɑstəˌmə / (AmE), / tjuːˈlɒstəmə / (BrE)
A genus of puffball fungi in the family Tulostomaceae, characterized by having a distinct stalk and spore sac.
genus of puffball fungi

Examples

Several species in the genus Tulostoma are found in desert environments.
The characteristic stalk of Tulostoma distinguishes it from other puffballs.
Researchers discovered a new Tulostoma species during their fieldwork in arid regions.

Etymology

From Greek toulos (knob) + stoma (mouth), referring to the structure of the fruiting body.

3. NOUN

/ tuːˈlɒstoʊmə / (AmE), / tjuːˈlɒstəmə / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Tulostomaceae, known as stalked puffballs, which produce spore-bearing fruiting bodies on stalks.
genus of stalked puffball fungi

Examples

The genus Tulostoma includes several species of stalked puffballs found in arid regions.
Fungi from the genus Tulostoma are known for their unique fruiting bodies atop slender stalks.
Researchers discovered a new species belonging to Tulostoma during their fieldwork in the desert.

Etymology

From Greek 'tulos' (knob, lump) + 'stoma' (mouth), referring to the fungi's appearance.
