indubitability

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌduːbɪtəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪnˌdjuːbɪtəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality of being impossible to doubt or dispute
the state of being unquestionable

Examples

The indubitability of the evidence made the jury's decision straightforward.
Despite her confidence, the indubitability of her claims was open to debate.
Scientists strive for indubitability in their findings, though absolute certainty is rare.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: certainty - the state of being certain
  • Antonym: doubt - uncertainty or lack of conviction

Etymology

From Latin 'indubitabilis' (undoubtable) + English suffix '-ity'.
