blaster

1. NOUN

/ ˈblæs.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈblɑː.stə / (BrE)
a worker whose job is to use explosives to break rock or demolish structures.
worker who uses explosives

Examples

The construction company hired an experienced blaster to remove the rock from the site.
As a blaster, safety procedures are his top priority on every job.
The blaster prepared the explosives carefully before starting the demolition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demolitionist - someone who demolishes buildings, especially with explosives

Etymology

From 'blast' (to explode, break apart) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
