rotund

1. ADJ

/ roʊˈtʌnd / (AmE), / rəʊˈtʌnd / (BrE)
(of a person) having a round, plump, or excessively fat body; rounded in shape.
round or plump in body shape

Examples

His rotund figure made him easy to spot in the crowd.
The mayor was known for his booming laugh and his rotund physique.
She drew a rotund character for her children's book illustration.

Synset Examples

a weighty man

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plump - pleasantly fat; rounded
  • Antonym: slender - thin in an attractive or healthy way

Etymology

From Latin 'rotundus' meaning 'round'.

2. ADJ

/ roʊˈtʌnd / (AmE), / rəˈtʌnd / (BrE)
(of sounds) full, rich, and deep in tone or resonance.
full and rich in sound

Examples

The actor's rotund voice carried beautifully to the back of the auditorium.
Her rotund tones gave the speech a majestic quality.
The choir's harmonies grew more rotund as their confidence soared.

Synset Examples

orotund tones
the rotund and reverberating phrase
pear-shaped vowels

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orotund - (of the voice or writing) full, round, and imposing

Etymology

From Latin *rotundus* (round), influenced in English by earlier use for rounded, rolling sounds.

3. ADJ

/ roʊˈtʌnd / (AmE), / rəˈtʌnd / (BrE)
(of a person or object) rounded or spherical in shape; having a full and plump form.
rounded or spherical in shape

Examples

The newborn baby had a rotund face, making everyone comment on his cherubic look.
A rotund pumpkin sat at the center of the autumn display.
The artist preferred drawing rotund figures, giving his paintings a sense of fullness and volume.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: round - shaped like a circle or sphere; having a curved shape
  • Antonym: angular - having angles or sharp corners; not rounded

Etymology

From Latin 'rotundus' (round, circular).
