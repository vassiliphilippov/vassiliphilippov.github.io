to abominate

1. VERB

/ əˈbɑːməˌneɪt / (AmE), / əˈbɒmɪneɪt / (BrE)
to feel intense dislike or hatred for something; to detest or loathe
to intensely dislike or detest

Examples

Many people abominate cruelty to animals and speak out against it.
He openly abominated the corrupt officials who betrayed their country.
She abominates dishonesty in any form, no matter how small.

Synset Examples

I loathe that man
She abhors cats

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to loathe - to feel intense dislike or disgust for
  • Synonym: to abhor - to regard with disgust and hatred
  • Antonym: love - to feel deep affection for

Etymology

From Latin abominari (to shun as an ill omen), from ab- (away from) + omen (a sign).

2. VERB

/ əˈbɑːmɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / əˈbɒmɪneɪt / (BrE)
To feel intense hatred or loathing for; to detest strongly.
to detest or hate intensely

Examples

Many people abominate any form of animal cruelty and work to prevent it.
He openly abominated the company's unethical business practices.
She abominates dishonesty in both personal and professional relationships.

Synset Examples

I abominate injustice in all its forms.
He abominates any form of cheating in sports.
She abominates the idea of harming animals for entertainment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to detest - to dislike intensely
  • Synonym: to loathe - to feel intense dislike or disgust for
  • Antonym: love - to feel deep affection or attachment for

Etymology

From Latin abominari (to detest as a bad omen), from ab- (away) + ominari (to predict as an omen).
