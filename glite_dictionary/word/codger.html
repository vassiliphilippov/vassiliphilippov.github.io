codger

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑdʒər / (AmE), / ˈkɒdʒə / (BrE)
an old man, especially one who is peculiar or amusing in some way, often used affectionately
an eccentric or amusing old man
informal

Examples

Everyone in the village knows the codger who sits by the fountain and tells wild stories.
My grandfather was a lovable codger who always had a joke ready.
Though he may seem like a grumpy codger, he has a heart of gold.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: geezer - an old man, often eccentric or odd
  • Synonym: fogey - an old-fashioned or fussy old man

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from the earlier 'cadger', meaning a beggar or peddler.
