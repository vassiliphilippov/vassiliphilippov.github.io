astasia

1. NOUN

/ əˈsteɪʒə / (AmE), / əˈsteɪzɪə / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by an inability to stand upright because of impaired muscular coordination.
inability to stand due to muscular incoordination
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with astasia after repeatedly failing to stand upright without support.
Neurological disorders can sometimes lead to astasia in affected individuals.
His sudden astasia puzzled the doctors until further tests revealed underlying nerve damage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abasia - inability to walk due to lack of muscular coordination

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (not) + 'stasis' (standing).
