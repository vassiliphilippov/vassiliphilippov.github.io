Chrysanthemum leucanthemum

1. NOUN

/ krɪˌsænθəməm luˌɛkænˈθɛməm / (AmE), / krɪˌsænθəməm ljuːˌɛkænˈθɛməm / (BrE)
A tall perennial Eurasian plant with white, daisy-like flowers, often naturalized in other regions; also known as oxeye daisy.
a Eurasian white-flowered perennial plant

Examples

The field was covered in blooming Chrysanthemum leucanthemum throughout the summer.
Gardeners often plant Chrysanthemum leucanthemum for its attractive white petals and yellow centers.
Some people consider Chrysanthemum leucanthemum an invasive weed, while others appreciate its beauty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oxeye daisy - common name for Chrysanthemum leucanthemum

Etymology

From Greek khrusanthemon (chrysanthemum): khrusos (gold) + anthemon (flower); Latin species name means 'white daisy'.
