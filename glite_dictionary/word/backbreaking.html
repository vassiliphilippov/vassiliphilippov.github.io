backbreaking

1. ADJ

/ ˈbækˌbreɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbækˌbreɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
Requiring extremely hard physical effort, often to the point of exhaustion.
requiring exhausting physical effort

Examples

The workers spent hours doing backbreaking labor in the fields.
Cleaning out the barn was a backbreaking task that took all day.
She found the construction work to be both dangerous and backbreaking.

Synset Examples

worked their arduous way up the mining valley
a grueling campaign
hard labor
heavy work
heavy going
spent many laborious hours on the project
set a punishing pace

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arduous - involving or requiring strenuous effort; difficult and tiring
  • Synonym: laborious - requiring considerable time and effort
  • Synonym: grueling - extremely tiring and demanding

Etymology

From 'back' + present participle of 'break', reflecting the extreme physical strain as if breaking one's back.
