chieftaincy

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃiːf.tən.si / (AmE), / ˈtʃiːf.tən.si / (BrE)
the position, authority, or period of rule of a chieftain
position or office of a chieftain

Examples

He inherited the chieftaincy after his father's death and became the new leader of the tribe.
The chieftaincy in that region has lasted for several centuries.
Contests over the chieftaincy often led to tension among the villagers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chieftain - the leader of a tribe or clan

Etymology

From 'chieftain' + '-cy', patterned after words like 'captaincy'. 'Chieftain' from Old French 'chevetain'.
