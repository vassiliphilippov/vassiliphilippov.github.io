pant

1. NOUN

/ pænt / (AmE), / pænt / (BrE)
a short, sharp puffing noise, especially the sound produced by a burst of steam from an engine
short puff of steam (especially from an engine)

Examples

The pant of the locomotive echoed through the station as it prepared to depart.
The engineer listened for the steady pant of steam to ensure everything was working correctly.
Every pant from the engine was a reminder of the train's immense power.

2. NOUN

/ pænt / (AmE), / pænt / (BrE)
(usually plural) an outer garment covering the body from the waist to the ankles, with a separate part for each leg.
trousers; leg-covering garment

Examples

He spilled coffee on his new pant during the meeting.
The tailor adjusted the length of her pant so it fit perfectly.
Each pant on display in the shop comes in several sizes.

Synset Examples

he had a sharp crease in his trousers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trouser - outer garment covering each leg, usually worn from waist to ankle
  • Synonym: pants - (plural) garment covering the waist to the ankles, each leg separately
  • Degree Lower: shorts - short trousers ending above or at the knee

Etymology

From French 'pantalon', via Italian 'pantalone', ultimately from the character 'Pantalone' in commedia dell'arte.

3. NOUN

/ pænt / (AmE), / pænt / (BrE)
a short or quick intake of breath, especially when one is out of breath or excited
short, quick breath

Examples

She let out a pant as she finished the race.
Each pant could be heard in the quiet, tense room.
With every pant, the dog's tongue lolled out a little farther.

Synset Examples

she gave a gasp and fainted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gasp - a sudden, short intake of breath
  • Degree Higher: wheeze - a whistling or rattling sound in the chest when breathing

Etymology

From the verb 'pant', which comes from Middle English 'panten', probably imitative of the sound of breathing heavily.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00838759-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ pænt / (AmE), / pænt / (BrE)
to say something while breathing quickly and heavily, especially because of exertion or excitement
say while out of breath

Examples

"Help!" she panted as she ran up the stairs.
He panted out his confession after sprinting across the yard.
The child panted the words, barely able to speak between breaths.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gasp - to say something while struggling to breathe

Etymology

From Middle English *panten* (to gasp), probably of imitative origin.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

5. VERB

/ pænt / (AmE), / pænt / (BrE)
to breathe quickly and audibly with short breaths, especially after exertion or excitement
to breathe heavily; gasp

Examples

She began to pant after climbing the long flight of stairs.
The dog panted in the heat, its tongue hanging out.
He panted for breath after finishing the last lap.

Synset Examples

The runners reached the finish line, panting heavily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gasp - inhale suddenly with mouth open, out of pain or astonishment
  • Synonym: wheeze - breathe with a whistling or rattling sound in the chest

Etymology

From Middle English panten, probably imitative of panting sound or from Old French pantaisier (to be breathless).

Source: WordNet
