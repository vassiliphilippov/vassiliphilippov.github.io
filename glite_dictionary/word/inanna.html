Inanna

1. PROPN

/ ɪˈnɑːnə / (AmE), / ɪˈnɑːnə / (BrE)
An ancient Sumerian goddess of love, fertility, and war, often identified as the consort of Dumuzi.
Sumerian goddess of love and war

Examples

The ancient Sumerians worshipped Inanna as a powerful goddess of love and war.
Mythological texts often describe Inanna's descent into the underworld.
Scholars draw parallels between Inanna and other goddesses like Ishtar and Aphrodite.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ishtar - Akkadian and Babylonian goddess often equated with Inanna
  • Synonym: Dumuzi - Sumerian god, consort of Inanna

Etymology

From Sumerian Inanna, meaning 'Lady of Heaven.'
