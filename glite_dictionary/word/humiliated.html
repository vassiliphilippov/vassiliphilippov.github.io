humiliated

1. ADJ

/ hjuˈmɪliˌeɪtɪd / (AmE), / hjuˈmɪlɪeɪtɪd / (BrE)
made to feel ashamed or embarrassed, especially because of a loss of dignity or pride
made to feel ashamed or embarrassed

Examples

She felt humiliated when her mistake was pointed out in front of everyone.
After losing the match by such a wide margin, the team members looked visibly humiliated.
He was humiliated by the unfair accusations made against him.

Synset Examples

too embarrassed to say hello to his drunken father on the street
humiliated that his wife had to go out to work
felt mortified by the comparison with her sister

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embarrassed - feeling awkward or self-conscious
  • Antonym: proud - feeling deep pleasure or satisfaction from one's own achievements; opposite of humiliated in this emotional sense

Etymology

From Latin humiliare (to humble, to abase), from humilis (low, humble).

2. ADJ

/ hjuˈmɪliˌeɪtɪd / (AmE), / hjuˈmɪlieɪtɪd / (BrE)
Made to feel ashamed or lowered in dignity, often as a result of being publicly disgraced or having one's pride wounded.
made to feel ashamed or degraded

Examples

She felt humiliated when she realized her mistake in front of everyone.
After the defeat, the team was completely humiliated and avoided the press.
He was humiliated by the public criticism of his work.

Synset Examples

brought low
a broken man
his broken spirit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ashamed - feeling guilt or embarrassment
  • Antonym: proud - feeling deep pleasure or satisfaction from one's achievements

Etymology

From Latin humiliatus, past participle of humiliāre, meaning ‘to humble or lower’.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01899370-s
Synset POS: s
