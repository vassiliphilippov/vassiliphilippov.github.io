lambskin

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæmˌskɪn / (AmE), / ˈlæmskɪn / (BrE)
the skin of a lamb, especially with the wool still attached, used for clothing or leather products
skin of a lamb with wool

Examples

The coat was lined with lambskin for extra warmth.
He bought a pair of lambskin gloves for the winter.
Traditional book covers were sometimes made from lambskin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sheepskin - the skin of a sheep with or without the wool

2. NOUN

/ ˈlæmˌskɪn / (AmE), / ˈlæm.skɪn / (BrE)
the prepared skin of a sheep or young goat, especially as used for writing or making parchment
skin of a lamb or goat, used for writing/parchment

Examples

The ancient manuscript was written on lambskin to ensure its durability.
She admired the texture of the lambskin pages in the old book.
During the Middle Ages, lambskin was commonly used for illuminated manuscripts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parchment - a stiff, flat, thin material made from animal skin, used for writing before paper
