grisly

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡrɪz.li / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪz.li / (BrE)
causing horror or disgust; shockingly repellent.
causing horror or disgust

Examples

The detective arrived at the scene of a grisly murder that shocked the entire city.
News reports described the grisly aftermath of the accident in vivid and disturbing detail.
Archaeologists uncovered grisly evidence of ancient human sacrifices.

Synset Examples

ghastly wounds
the grim aftermath of the bombing
the grim task of burying the victims
a grisly murder
gruesome evidence of human sacrifice
macabre tales of war and plague in the Middle ages
macabre tortures conceived by madmen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gruesome - causing repulsion or horror; grisly
  • Synonym: macabre - disturbing and horrifying because of involvement with or depiction of death and injury
  • Antonym: pleasant - giving a sense of happy satisfaction or enjoyment

Etymology

From Middle English grisly, from Old English grislic (terrible, dreadful), from gris- (to shudder, dread).
