sprout

1. NOUN

/ spraʊt / (AmE), / spraʊt / (BrE)
a young shoot or new growth on a plant, such as a new branch or bud
young plant shoot or bud

Examples

Tiny sprouts appeared on the branches in early spring.
She carefully pruned the sprouts to help the plant grow stronger.
After just a week in the soil, the seeds began to send up sprouts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shoot - a young branch or growth from a plant
  • Synonym: bud - a compact growth on a plant that develops into a leaf, flower, or shoot

Etymology

From Middle English *sprouten*, from Old English *sprūtan* (to sprout).

2. NOUN

/ spraʊt / (AmE), / spraʊt / (BrE)
a young shoot or bud that has just begun to grow, especially from a germinating seed
a young shoot or new bud

Examples

Tiny sprouts appeared on the branches after the first spring rain.
The gardener pointed to the green sprout emerging from the soil.
Each seed eventually produces a sprout before growing into a mature plant.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shoot - a young branch or stem of a plant
  • Synonym: bud - a compact growth on a plant that develops into a leaf, flower, or shoot

Etymology

From Middle English 'sprouten', from Old English 'sprūtan' (to sprout, spring up).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07735182-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ spraʊt / (AmE), / spraʊt / (BrE)
to produce new growth such as buds, shoots, or branches; to begin to grow or germinate
begin to grow or produce new shoots

Examples

After a few days in the soil, the seeds began to sprout.
When left in the pantry too long, onions will sprout green shoots.
New ideas can sprout in the minds of creative thinkers.

Synset Examples

the potatoes sprouted

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to germinate - to begin to grow (a seed)
  • Antonym: to wither - to become dry and shriveled (opposite of growing)

Etymology

From Middle English 'sprouten', related to Old English 'sprūtan' (to sprout).

4. VERB

/ spraʊt / (AmE), / spraʊt / (BrE)
(of a plant or seed) to begin to grow; to produce new shoots or growth.
begin to grow; produce shoots

Examples

Small green shoots started to sprout from the branches in early spring.
After the rain, weeds sprouted quickly all over the garden.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
