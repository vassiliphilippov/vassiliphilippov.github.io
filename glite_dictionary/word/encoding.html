encoding

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkoʊdɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈkəʊdɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of converting data or information into a coded form, often for the purpose of storage, transmission, or secrecy
conversion of data into code

Examples

The encoding of passwords protects them from unauthorized access.
Different file formats may use different encoding methods for text.
Efficient encoding is essential for streaming high-quality video over the internet.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: decoding - the process of converting coded information back to its original form

Etymology

Formed from 'encode' + '-ing'. 'Encode' from French 'encoder', from Latin 'codex' (book).
