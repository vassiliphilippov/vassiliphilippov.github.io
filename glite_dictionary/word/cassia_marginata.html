Cassia marginata

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæsiə mɑrˈdʒaɪnətə / (AmE), / ˌkæsiə mɑːˈdʒaɪnətə / (BrE)
an East Indian tree with long pods containing a black cathartic pulp used as a horse medicine
East Indian tree used in horse medicine
medical

Examples

The pods of Cassia marginata are commonly used as a traditional remedy for horses in India.
Veterinarians in the region sometimes prescribe Cassia marginata for its cathartic properties.
Farmers collect the black pulp from Cassia marginata to treat digestive issues in livestock.

Etymology

From Latin; genus name Cassia and species epithet marginata (meaning 'margined') in botanical Latin.
