extemporization

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˌstɛmpərɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˌstɛmpəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of performing, composing, or speaking without prior planning or preparation; improvisation
acting or performing without preparation

Examples

Her brilliant extemporization on the piano captivated the entire audience.
The meeting was saved by his quick extemporization when the technology failed.
Effective extemporization requires not just talent, but also confidence and experience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: improvisation - the act of creating or performing spontaneously without preparation

Etymology

From extemporize + -ation, with roots in Latin ex tempore ('out of the moment').
