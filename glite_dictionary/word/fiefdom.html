fiefdom

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːf.dəm / (AmE), / ˈfiːf.dəm / (BrE)
an organization or area that is controlled or dominated by one person or group, often for their own benefit
organization dominated by one person or group

Examples

The department had become her personal fiefdom over the years, with little outside oversight.
Some critics argue that the charity turned into a fiefdom controlled by its founder.
The mayor was accused of running the city like his private fiefdom.

Etymology

From Old French 'fief' (estate; fee) + '-dom' (domain, jurisdiction).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfiːf.dəm / (AmE), / ˈfiːf.dəm / (BrE)
An area or domain controlled by a feudal lord, especially during the Middle Ages.
area controlled by a feudal lord

Examples

The castle was at the center of the lord's fiefdom during the Middle Ages.
In the corporation, each department became a manager's personal fiefdom.
The region was once a powerful fiefdom rivaling the neighboring kingdoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: domain - an area of territory owned or controlled by a ruler or government
  • Degree Higher: kingdom - a country, state, or territory ruled by a king or queen

Etymology

From Old French 'fief' (estate), with the suffix '-dom' (domain, territory).
