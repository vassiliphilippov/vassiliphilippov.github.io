Amberboa moschata

1. NOUN

/ ˌæm.bərˈboʊ.ə mɒsˈkeɪ.tə / (AmE), / ˌæm.bəˈbəʊ.ə mɒsˈkɑː.tə / (BrE)
An Asian plant species, commonly cultivated for its sweetly fragrant pink flowers; sometimes classified under the genus Centaurea.
fragrant pink-flowered Asian plant

Examples

The Amberboa moschata in my garden attracts many bees with its sweet fragrance.
Many gardeners prize Amberboa moschata for its beautiful, pink blooms and pleasant scent.
You might see Amberboa moschata referred to as sweet sultan in plant catalogs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Centaurea - genus of flowering plants often including Amberboa

Etymology

From Latin; 'Amberboa' is a genus of flowering plants, 'moschata' refers to its musky scent.
