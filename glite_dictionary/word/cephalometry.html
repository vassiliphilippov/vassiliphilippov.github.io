cephalometry

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛf.əˈlɑː.mə.tri / (AmE), / ˌsɛf.əˈlɒ.mə.tri / (BrE)
the scientific measurement of the dimensions of the head, especially for medical or anthropological purposes
measurement of the head
medical

Examples

The orthodontist used cephalometry to evaluate the patient's jaw alignment.
Advances in cephalometry have improved the diagnosis of craniofacial abnormalities.
Researchers collected cephalometry data to study growth patterns in children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anthropometry - the scientific measurement of the human body

Etymology

From Greek kephalē (head) + -metry (measurement).
