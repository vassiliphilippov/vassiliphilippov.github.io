poppycock

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːpiˌkɑk / (AmE), / ˈpɒpɪˌkɒk / (BrE)
foolish or senseless talk; nonsense
foolish or senseless talk

Examples

His claims about time travel are pure poppycock, and nobody takes them seriously.
Don't give me that poppycock; I know what really happened here.
The article was dismissed by experts as complete poppycock.

Synset Examples

don't give me that stuff

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonsense - spoken or written words that have no meaning or make no sense
  • Synonym: balderdash - senseless talk or writing; nonsense
  • Antonym: sense - reasonable or rational talk or thinking

Etymology

Probably from Dutch 'pap' (soft food) + 'kak' (dung), indicating something worthless.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɑpiˌkɑk / (AmE), / ˈpɒpɪkɒk / (BrE)
Foolish or absurd talk; nonsense.
nonsense; foolish talk

Examples

Most experts agree that his theory is a bunch of poppycock.
She dismissed the rumors as utter poppycock.
The story he told us was absolute poppycock and no one believed it.

Synset Examples

Don't listen to him; it's all poppycock.
That excuse is just a load of poppycock!
Her claims about winning the lottery were pure poppycock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonsense - spoken or written words that have no meaning or make no sense
  • Synonym: rubbish - language or ideas that are absurd or untrue

Etymology

Perhaps from Dutch *pappekak* (soft dung), a fanciful term for nonsense.
