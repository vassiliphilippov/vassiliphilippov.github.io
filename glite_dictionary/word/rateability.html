rateability

1. NOUN

/ ˌreɪtəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌreɪtəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the state of being liable to assessment or taxation.
liability to assessment or taxation
legal

Examples

The council determined the rateability of several new properties in the district.
Unclear rateability can complicate the process of tax collection for local authorities.
He disputed the rateability of his land, claiming it qualified for an exemption.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taxability - the quality of being liable to taxes
  • Antonym: exemption - the state of being free from obligation or liability

Etymology

From 'rate' (tax, assessment) + '-ability' (capacity, state of being).
