bassoon

1. NOUN

/ bəˈsun / (AmE), / bəˈsuːn / (BrE)
A large woodwind instrument with a double reed, having a low pitch and forming the tenor voice of the woodwind family.
large low-pitched woodwind instrument

Examples

The bassoon adds a rich, deep sound to the orchestra.
She has been learning to play the bassoon for three years.
A solo for bassoon opened the second movement of the symphony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oboe - a woodwind instrument with a double reed and a higher pitch than the bassoon

Etymology

From French 'basson', from Italian 'bassone', augmentative of 'basso' (bass).
