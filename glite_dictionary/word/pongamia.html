Pongamia

1. NOUN

/ pɒnˈɡeɪmiə / (AmE), / pɒnˈɡeɪmiə / (BrE)
A tropical tree (Millettia pinnata), also known as Indian beech, valued for its oil-rich seeds and use in traditional medicine.
tropical tree; Indian beech

Examples

The seeds of Pongamia are widely used to produce biofuel in India.
Pongamia trees can tolerate saline soils, making them suitable for coastal plantations.
Scientists are studying Pongamia for its potential in sustainable agriculture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Indian beech - another name for the tree Pongamia (Millettia pinnata)

Etymology

From the genus name Pongamia, formerly used for this tree, of uncertain origin.

2. NOUN

/ pɒŋˈɡeɪ.mi.ə / (AmE), / pɒŋˈɡeɪ.mi.ə / (BrE)
A genus of trees in the pea family Fabaceae, consisting primarily of the species commonly known as Indian beech.
genus of trees including Indian beech

Examples

The seeds of Pongamia are used to extract oil for medicinal purposes.
Farmers in India have cultivated Pongamia for centuries.
Researchers are exploring the potential of Pongamia as a source of biodiesel.

Etymology

From modern Latin, coined from Telugu 'Pongamu', a name for the tree.
