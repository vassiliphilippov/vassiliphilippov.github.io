Impatiens

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpeɪʃəns / (AmE), / ɪmˈpeɪʃi.enz / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants, usually annual or perennial herbs with fleshy stems, native to most parts of the world except South America, Australia, and New Zealand
a genus of flowering plants (jewelweed)

Examples

Impatiens are popular garden plants, known for their brightly colored flowers and shade tolerance.
Many species in the Impatiens genus have explosive seed pods that scatter seeds when touched.
Some Impatiens varieties are grown indoors as houseplants due to their attractive blooms.

Etymology

From Latin 'impatiens' (impatient), referring to the way the seed pods burst open when touched.
