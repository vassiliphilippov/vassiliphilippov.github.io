bellwether

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛlˌwɛðər / (AmE), / ˈbɛlˌwɛðə / (BrE)
a person or thing that assumes a leadership role or indicates future trends
person or thing indicating future trends

Examples

The state has long been considered a bellwether for national election results.
Her early adoption of new technology made her a bellwether in the industry.
Analysts look to these companies as potential bellwethers of future market trends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leader - a person who leads others or commands a group

Etymology

From Middle English 'bellewether', literally a wether (castrated ram) with a bell around its neck leading a flock.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɛlˌwɛðər / (AmE), / ˈbɛlˌwɛðə / (BrE)
A sheep that leads the flock, often wearing a bell around its neck to help the shepherd locate the herd.
leading sheep in a flock, marked with a bell

Examples

The shepherd tied a small bell around the neck of the bellwether to keep track of the flock.
When the bellwether moved, the rest of the sheep instinctively followed.
The farmer relied on the bellwether to guide the other animals across the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leader - a person or thing that leads others

Etymology

From Middle English 'bellewether' (bell + wether, a castrated ram).
