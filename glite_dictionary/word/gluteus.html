gluteus

1. NOUN

/ ɡluˈtiːəs / (AmE), / ɡluːˈtɪəs / (BrE)
any of the three large muscles in each buttock that move the thigh
buttock muscle

Examples

After the marathon, her gluteus muscles were sore from the long run.
The trainer explained that squats are excellent for strengthening the gluteus.
An injury to the gluteus can make it difficult to walk or climb stairs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gluteal muscle - any of the muscles that make up the buttocks

Etymology

From Latin 'glūtēus' (of the buttocks), from Greek 'gloutos' (buttock).
