lexicalised

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɛksɪkəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈlɛksɪkəlaɪzd / (BrE)
(of an idea, concept, or phrase) expressed as a single word in a language.
expressed as a word in a language

Examples

The word 'edit' is a lexicalised form of the longer phrase 'to make editorial changes'.
Some idioms become so common that they are eventually lexicalised into single words.
In linguistics, we study how concepts are lexicalised in different languages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lexicalized - alternative American spelling with -z-

Etymology

From 'lexical' + '-ise'/'-ize'; ultimately from Greek lexis (word, speech).
