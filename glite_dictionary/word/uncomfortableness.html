uncomfortableness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈkʌmfərtəblnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈkʌmfətəblnəs / (BrE)
the state of being physically or emotionally uneasy, tense, or in pain
state of being uneasy or in pain

Examples

Her uncomfortableness during the interview was obvious to everyone in the room.
There was a sense of uncomfortableness when the topic turned to politics.
He shifted in his seat, trying to hide his uncomfortableness in the crowded train.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discomfort - a state of slight pain or uneasiness

Etymology

Formed from 'uncomfortable' + '-ness', following a common noun-forming pattern in English.

2. NOUN

/ ʌnˈkʌmfɚtəblnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈkʌmftəblnəs / (BrE)
The state or quality of feeling uncomfortable, especially due to embarrassment or self-consciousness.
the state of being uncomfortable

Examples

There was a clear uncomfortableness in his voice during the interview.
Her uncomfortableness became obvious when everyone turned to look at her.
He shifted in his seat with visible uncomfortableness as the conversation continued.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discomfort - the state of being uncomfortable or uneasy

Etymology

Formed from 'uncomfortable' + '-ness', following standard English noun formation.
