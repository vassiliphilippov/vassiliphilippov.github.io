diazonium

1. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪ.əˈzoʊ.ni.əm / (AmE), / ˌdaɪ.əˈzəʊ.ni.əm / (BrE)
a univalent cation with the formula R–N≡N+, where R is an aromatic hydrocarbon group, found in salts used for making azo dyes
univalent cation used in azo dye manufacture
medical

Examples

The diazonium ion is a crucial intermediate in the synthesis of azo dyes.
Chemists often work with diazonium salts during the production of aromatic compounds.
Formation of a diazonium compound enables easy introduction of different functional groups onto a benzene ring.

Etymology

From 'diazo' (from Greek 'dia' meaning 'through' and 'azo,' from nitrogen) + '-onium,' denoting a cation.
