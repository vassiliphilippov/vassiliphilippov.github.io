Dacrymyces

1. NOUN

/ ˌdæk.rəˈmaɪ.siːz / (AmE), / ˌdakrɪˈmʌɪsiːz / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Dacrymycetaceae, characterized by a bifurcate basidium without septa.
genus of fungi in Dacrymycetaceae

Examples

Researchers have discovered a new species within the genus Dacrymyces.
The fruiting bodies of Dacrymyces are often found on decaying wood.
Mycologists study Dacrymyces to understand fungal diversity in forest ecosystems.

Etymology

From Greek dakryon (tear) + mykēs (fungus).

a genus of fungi

Examples

Fungi of the genus Dacrymyces are commonly found on decaying wood in damp environments.
Researchers studied the reproductive structures of Dacrymyces to understand its unique basidium.
The yellow, jelly-like fruiting bodies of Dacrymyces are easily recognizable in forests.

Etymology

From Greek dakry (tear) and mykēs (fungus), referring to the jelly-like appearance of the fungi.
