gladsome

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡlæd.səm / (AmE), / ˈɡlad.səm / (BrE)
Feeling or expressing happiness and joy; cheerful, joyful.
joyful or cheerful
archaic

Examples

She wore a gladsome expression as she greeted her old friends at the party.
The children’s gladsome laughter filled the garden on a sunny afternoon.
A gladsome song lifted everyone’s spirits during the festival.

Synset Examples

a gladsome smile
a gladsome occasion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: joyful - feeling or expressing great happiness
  • Synonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic
  • Antonym: mournful - feeling, expressing, or inducing sadness or grief

Etymology

Formed from 'glad' + '-some', with '-some' from Old English ‘-sum’ meaning ‘characterised by’.
