to redouble

1. VERB

/ riˈdoʊbəl / (AmE), / riˈdʌbəl / (BrE)
to make something much greater or more intense, especially after it has already increased; to double again
to double again; increase greatly

Examples

She resolved to redouble her efforts after the initial setback.
As the storm worsened, the wind seemed to redouble in strength.
The company plans to redouble its commitment to customer service this year.

Synset Examples

The noise doubled and redoubled

Related Terms

  • Synonym: double - to make twice as much or as many

Etymology

From Old French 'redoubler', from Latin 're-' (again) + 'duplare' (to double).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00247361-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ riˈdʌbəl / (AmE), / riˈdʌbəl / (BrE)
to make something much more intense, stronger, or greater, especially suddenly or to a significantly higher degree
make more intense or greater

Examples

The soldiers redoubled their efforts after the enemy advanced.
The storm redoubled in strength overnight, causing widespread damage.
As the deadline approached, her determination redoubled and she worked through the night.

Synset Examples

The screaming redoubled

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to intensify - to become or make more intense

Etymology

From Old French *redoubler*, from re- (again) + *doubler* (to double), ultimately from Latin *duplus* (double).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00227468-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ riˈdʌb.əl / (AmE), / riˈdʌb.əl / (BrE)
to greatly increase the extent, intensity, or amount of something; to make much greater or more intense
increase greatly in intensity or amount

Examples

We must redouble our efforts if we are to finish the project on time.
The storm redoubled in strength overnight, causing severe flooding in the area.
Seeing little progress, she redoubled her commitment to mastering the new language.

Synset Examples

The enemy redoubled their screaming on the radio

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to intensify - to make or become more intense
  • Antonym: to diminish - to become less or reduce in intensity

Etymology

From Old French 'redoubler', from Latin 're-' (again) + 'dublare' (to double).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00291343-v
Synset POS: v
