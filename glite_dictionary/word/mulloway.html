mulloway

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌl.əˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈmʌl.əʊ.weɪ / (BrE)
a large food fish (Argyrosomus japonicus) native to Australia, valued for its flesh and sometimes difficult to distinguish from the maigre.
large Australian food fish
british

Examples

The fisherman was thrilled to catch a mulloway on his first trip out to sea.
Mulloway are highly prized by anglers along the southern coast of Australia.
Grilled mulloway features on the menu at several top Sydney restaurants.

Etymology

Originating in Australian English, from a native Australian language of New South Wales.
