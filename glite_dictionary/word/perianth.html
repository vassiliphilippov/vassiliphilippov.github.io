perianth

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛriˌænθ / (AmE), / ˈpɛriːænθ / (BrE)
the outer part of a flower, collectively comprising the calyx and corolla and enclosing the stamens and pistils
outer parts of a flower (calyx and corolla)

Examples

The tulip's perianth is brightly colored and attracts many pollinators.
Damage to the perianth can affect a flower's ability to reproduce successfully.
In some species, the perianth consists of segments that are difficult to distinguish from one another.

Etymology

From Greek *peri-* (around) + *anthos* (flower).
