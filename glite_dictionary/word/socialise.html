to socialise

1. VERB

/ ˈsoʊʃəlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃəlaɪz / (BrE)
to bring an institution, industry, or service under government or collective ownership or to organize it according to socialist principles
bring under socialist or state control

Examples

Many progressives advocate to socialise health care for better public access.
There was fierce debate over whether to socialise the energy sector.
The government decided to socialise the banks during the economic crisis.

Synset Examples

Health care should be socialized!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to nationalize - transfer from private to state ownership or control
  • Antonym: to privatize - transfer from public or state control to private ownership

Etymology

From French socialiser, from social (relating to society) + -ize.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00406778-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈsoʊʃəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃəlaɪz / (BrE)
to take part in social activities and interact with other people
interact socially; take part in social activities

Examples

She finds it easy to socialise at parties and quickly makes friends.
After moving to the city, he struggled to socialise and often felt lonely.
Retirement gave her more time to socialise with old school friends.

Synset Examples

He never socializes with his colleagues
The old man hates to socialize

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mingle - to move among and engage with others at a social event
  • Antonym: to isolate - to remain alone or apart from others; to avoid interaction

Etymology

From French 'socialiser' (to make social), from 'social' (social).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02394092-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈsoʊʃəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃəlaɪz / (BrE)
to train or prepare (someone), especially a child, to behave in a way that is acceptable in society
prepare for social life; teach social norms

Examples

Parents play a crucial role in helping their children socialise from an early age.
Some animals, like wolves, need to socialise with their pack to develop normal behavior.
Schools provide opportunities for students to socialise and learn accepted social norms.

Synset Examples

Children have to be socialized in school

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to educate - to give intellectual, moral, and social instruction to someone
  • Antonym: to isolate - to cause (a person or place) to be or remain alone or apart from others

Etymology

From French *socialiser* or directly from Latin *socialis* (of companionship, of society).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00406978-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈsoʊʃəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃəlaɪz / (BrE)
to train someone, especially a child or animal, to behave in a way that is acceptable to society or to a particular social group
train to behave acceptably in society

Examples

It is important to socialise children so they learn accepted manners and values.
The puppy was socialised with other dogs from a young age to prevent aggression.
Teachers play a key role in helping students socialise properly within the classroom.

Synset Examples

The children must be properly socialized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to civilize - to bring out of a savage or uneducated state; to improve socially
  • Synonym: to indoctrinate - to teach someone to accept a set of beliefs uncritically
  • Antonym: to ostracize - to exclude from a society or group

Etymology

From French socialiser, from social (relating to society).
