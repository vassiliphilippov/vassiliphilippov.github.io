Carduelinae

1. NOUN

/ kɑːrdjuːˈɛlɪniː / (AmE), / kɑːdjuːˈɛlɪniː / (BrE)
A subfamily of finches, including a variety of seed-eating passerine birds, used in scientific classifications.
subfamily of finches (birds)

Examples

The Carduelinae are known for their conical bills adapted for eating seeds.
Many colorful birds belong to the Carduelinae, including goldfinches and siskins.
Research on the Carduelinae helps ornithologists understand the evolution of finches.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: finch - a small songbird with a short bill

Etymology

From modern Latin Carduelinae, based on the type genus Carduelis.
