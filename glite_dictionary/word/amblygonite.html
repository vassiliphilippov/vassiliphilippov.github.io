amblygonite

1. NOUN

/ ˌæm.blɪˈɡoʊ.naɪt / (AmE), / ˌam.blɪˈɡəʊ.naɪt / (BrE)
a white or gray mineral consisting of lithium aluminum phosphate, used as a source of lithium
a white or gray lithium mineral

Examples

The geologist identified the specimen as amblygonite due to its characteristic pale color and structure.
Large deposits of amblygonite have made the region an important source of lithium.
Amblygonite is often found in granite pegmatites alongside other lithium-rich minerals.

Etymology

From Greek amblys (blunt) + gonia (angle), referring to the angles of the crystals.
