to sew

1. VERB

/ soʊ / (AmE), / səʊ / (BrE)
to join, fasten, or repair fabric or other material by making stitches with a needle and thread or a sewing machine
join or repair with stitches

Examples

She learned how to sew her own clothes when she was a teenager.
Can you sew this button back onto my shirt?
He enjoys sewing quilts as a relaxing hobby.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stitch - to make stitches in sewing; fastening with thread

Etymology

From Old English *siwan*, from Proto-Germanic *siwjanan*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01331921-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ soʊ / (AmE), / səʊ / (BrE)
to make (clothes or other items) by joining pieces of cloth together with a needle and thread
make clothes by joining cloth

Examples

She learned to sew her own dresses when she was a teenager.
My grandmother used to sew beautiful quilts for the family.
The designer decided to sew a custom gown for the wedding.

Synset Examples

Can the seamstress sew me a suit by next week?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stitch - to make stitches in material with a needle and thread
  • Degree Higher: to embroider - to decorate cloth by sewing patterns on it with thread
  • Antonym: knit - to make fabric or garments by interlocking yarn with needles

Etymology

From Old English *sīwan*, from Proto-Germanic *siwjaną*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01670222-v
Synset POS: v
