recapitulation

1. NOUN

/ ˌriː.kəˌpɪtʃ.əˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌriː.kəˌpɪt.jʊˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
(music) the section in a musical composition, especially in sonata form, where themes introduced earlier are repeated and developed, typically in the final part of a movement.
restatement of earlier musical themes

Examples

The symphony's final movement features a brilliant recapitulation of its main themes.
In classical sonata form, the recapitulation brings back the exposition's melodies, resolving harmonic tensions.
Her analysis focused on how Beethoven varied the recapitulation to create a dramatic effect.

Etymology

From Late Latin recapitulatio (the act of repeating in summary), from 're-' (again) + 'capitulum' (chapter, heading).

2. NOUN

/ riˌkæpɪtʃəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˌkæpɪtjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
(Biology) The appearance, during embryonic development, of characteristics or structures that were present in the evolutionary history of the species.
embryonic reappearance of ancestral traits
medical

Examples

The theory of recapitulation suggests that an embryo develops through stages resembling the evolutionary ancestors of its species.
Modern biology has challenged the idea that embryonic recapitulation exactly mirrors evolutionary history.
Observations supporting recapitulation can be seen in the gill slits found in human embryos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atavism - reversion to an ancestral type

Etymology

From Latin recapitulatio, from 're-' (again) + 'capitulum' (chapter, heading).

4. NOUN

/ ˌriːkəˌpɪtʃəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːkəˌpɪtjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
(music) The section of a musical composition, especially in sonata form, where earlier themes are restated, typically following the development.
musical section where earlier themes return

Examples

In the recapitulation, the composer skillfully restated the main themes from the exposition.
The recapitulation brings a satisfying sense of return in the final part of the sonata movement.
Music students often analyze the recapitulation to understand how themes are transformed throughout a piece.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: development - contrasting section in sonata form, between exposition and recapitulation

Etymology

From Late Latin recapitulatio (a summing up), from Latin recapitulare (to restate by heads or chapters).
