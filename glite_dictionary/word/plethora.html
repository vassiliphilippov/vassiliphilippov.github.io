plethora

1. NOUN

/ ˈplɛθ.ə.rə / (AmE), / ˈplɛθ.ərə / (BrE)
an amount that is much greater than what is necessary or can be managed; an extreme excess
extreme excess; overabundance

Examples

The city offers a plethora of dining options to choose from.
After her birthday, she found herself with a plethora of new books to read.
There is a plethora of information available online, making it hard to know where to start.

Synset Examples

an embarrassment of riches

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abundance - a very large quantity of something
  • Antonym: scarcity - a lack or shortage of something

Etymology

From Greek 'plēthōra' (fullness), via Late Latin.
