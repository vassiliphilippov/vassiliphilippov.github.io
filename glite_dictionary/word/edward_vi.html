Edward VI

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛd.wɚd sɪksθ / (AmE), / ˈɛd.wəd sɪksθ / (BrE)
(1537–1553) King of England and Ireland from 1547 to 1553, known for his Protestant reforms; son of Henry VIII and Jane Seymour.
King of England and Ireland, 1547–1553

Examples

Edward VI became king at the age of nine after his father, Henry VIII, died.
The reign of Edward VI witnessed significant religious changes in England.
Historians often discuss the rapid reforms that took place during Edward VI's short rule.
