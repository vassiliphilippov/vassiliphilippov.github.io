merrily

1. ADV

/ ˈmɛrəli / (AmE), / ˈmɛrɪli / (BrE)
In a cheerful and lively way; in a manner that shows happiness and enjoyment.
in a cheerful, joyful way

Examples

The children laughed merrily as they played in the garden.
She sang merrily while cleaning the house, brightening everyone's mood.
They set off merrily on their adventure, full of excitement and hope.

Synset Examples

they shouted happily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheerfully - in a happy, lively, and positive way
  • Antonym: gloomily - in a sad or depressed manner

Etymology

From Middle English 'merily', from 'mery' (merry) + '-ly'.
