Clinopodium grandiflorum

1. NOUN

/ klɪnəˈpoʊdiəm ˌɡrændɪˈflɔrəm / (AmE), / klɪnəˈpəʊdiəm ˌɡrandɪˈflɔːrəm / (BrE)
an aromatic herb with large pink flowers, native to southern and southeastern Europe, Anatolia, and northern Iran
aromatic herb with pink flowers

Examples

Clinopodium grandiflorum is often grown in herb gardens for its delightful aroma.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of Clinopodium grandiflorum extracts.
The large pink flowers of Clinopodium grandiflorum attract many pollinators in the summer.

Etymology

From Latin: 'clinopodium' (from Greek 'klinopodion', a type of herb) and 'grandiflorum' (having large flowers).
