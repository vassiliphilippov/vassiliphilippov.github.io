jagua

1. NOUN

/ hɑːˈɡwɑː / (AmE), / hɑːˈɡwɑː / (BrE)
A tree native to the West Indies and northern South America, bearing edible, succulent, orange-sized fruit.
tropical fruit tree of the Americas

Examples

The jagua produces large, juicy fruits enjoyed by local communities.
Researchers are studying the medicinal properties of the jagua tree.
She tasted the sweet flesh of a ripe jagua during her trip to Colombia.

Etymology

From Spanish 'jagua', of indigenous Taíno origin.
