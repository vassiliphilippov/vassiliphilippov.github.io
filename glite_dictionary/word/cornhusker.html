Cornhusker

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrnˌhʌskər / (AmE), / ˈkɔːnˌhʌskə / (BrE)
A native or resident of Nebraska, especially used as a nickname for people from that state or for athletes representing the University of Nebraska.
person from Nebraska; nickname for Nebraskan

Examples

As a proud Cornhusker, she always supports the University of Nebraska football team.
Many Cornhuskers attend the annual state fair in Lincoln.
His grandfather, a lifelong Cornhusker, remembers Nebraska before it became famous for football.

Etymology

From 'corn' + 'husker' (one who removes husks from corn), reflecting Nebraska's agricultural heritage.
