Blatta

1. NOUN

/ ˈblæt̬ə / (AmE), / ˈblætə / (BrE)
A genus of cockroaches in the family Blattidae, commonly found infesting buildings worldwide.
genus of cockroaches

Examples

Scientists studied the habits of Blatta species found in urban apartments.
The genus Blatta includes some of the most common household cockroaches.
Many pest control guides mention Blatta as a major cockroach group.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockroach - an insect of the order Blattodea, commonly found in human dwellings

Etymology

From Latin *blatta* (an insect that shuns the light, especially a cockroach).
