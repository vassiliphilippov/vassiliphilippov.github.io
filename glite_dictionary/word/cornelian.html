cornelian

1. NOUN

/ kɔrˈniːliən / (AmE), / kɔːˈniːliən / (BrE)
a translucent red or orange variety of chalcedony used as a gemstone
a reddish gemstone variety of chalcedony

Examples

The ancient ring was set with a beautifully polished cornelian.
She bought a necklace made of cornelian beads at the market.
The museum displayed a Roman cameo carved from cornelian.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carnelian - a red or orange variety of chalcedony used as a semi-precious stone

Etymology

From Latin 'cornelĭus', from Greek 'kornelion'; possibly related to the cornel cherry, due to similarity in color.
