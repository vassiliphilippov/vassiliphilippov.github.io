Chamaeleo

1. NOUN

/ kæˈmiːlioʊ / (AmE), / kəˈmiːlɪəʊ / (BrE)
a genus of lizards in the family Chamaeleontidae, comprising several species of chameleons found mainly in Africa and parts of Europe and Asia
genus of chameleons

Examples

The genus Chamaeleo includes several well-known species of chameleons.
Researchers observed the color-changing abilities of Chamaeleo in their natural habitat.
Specimens of Chamaeleo have been collected for scientific study since the 18th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chameleon - a lizard known for its ability to change color

Etymology

From Latin 'chamaeleon', from Greek 'khamailéōn' (chameleon).
