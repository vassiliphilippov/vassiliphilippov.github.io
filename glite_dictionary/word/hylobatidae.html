Hylobatidae

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌloʊbəˈtaɪdiː / (AmE), / hɪˌlɒbəˈtiːdiː / (BrE)
A family of primates commonly known as the lesser apes, including the gibbons and siamangs.
family of lesser apes (gibbons, siamangs)

Examples

The Hylobatidae are native to the forests of Southeast Asia.
Unlike the great apes, members of the Hylobatidae do not build nests.
Some taxonomists debate whether Hylobatidae should be considered a family or subfamily.

Etymology

From Modern Latin, from Greek hylobates (gibbon) + -idae (zoological family suffix).
