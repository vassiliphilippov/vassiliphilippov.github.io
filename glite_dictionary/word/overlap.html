overlap

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvərˈlæp / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈlæp / (BrE)
a part that extends over and covers another part, especially as a flap or layer
part extending over another

Examples

The shingles were installed with enough overlap to prevent water from seeping through.
Each piece of fabric has a two-inch overlap to ensure no gaps are left.
Check the overlap of the metal panels to guarantee a watertight seal.

Synset Examples

the lap of the shingles should be at least ten inches

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lap - a loose or projecting part that covers or envelops

Etymology

From over- + lap, with 'lap' in the sense of 'a loose or projecting part that covers another'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03647020-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˌoʊ.vɚˈlæp / (AmE), / ˌəʊ.vəˈlæp / (BrE)
a situation in which two or more events, activities, or periods partially coincide in time
partial coincidence in time

Examples

There was a significant overlap in their work schedules during the summer.
The meeting times have some overlap, which might cause conflicts for attendees.
An overlap of the two events meant we had to choose which one to attend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coincidence - the occurrence of events at the same time

Etymology

From over- (above) + lap, originally meaning 'to lie over' (as of layers) in Early Modern English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05055984-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvərˈlæp / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈlæp / (BrE)
an area or instance where two or more things, ideas, or activities share common features or have shared parts
shared area or common ground

Examples

The overlap between the two theories suggests they may be describing similar phenomena.
There is significant overlap in the skills needed for both jobs.
Despite their differences, their proposals had some overlap.

Synset Examples

there was no overlap between their proposals

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intersection - a point or set where things overlap or cross
  • Antonym: exclusive - not shared; having nothing in common

Etymology

From over- + lap; originally meaning 'to lap over' in the sense of extending over.

4. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈlæp / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈlæp / (BrE)
to extend over and partly cover a part of something else
extend over and cover a part

Examples

Make sure the tiles overlap slightly to prevent leaks.
Their schedules overlap by an hour every Wednesday.

Synset Examples

The roofs of the houses overlap in this crowded city

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cover - to put something over or on top of something else
  • Antonym: separate - to cause to move or be apart

Etymology

From over- (above) + lap (to fold or wind), dating to the late 16th century.

5. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈlæp / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈlæp / (BrE)
(of two things) to partly or completely cover the same area, time, or elements as each other; to coincide in part or in whole.
coincide partly or wholly

Examples

Our meetings often overlap, making it hard to attend both.
The two shifts overlap by an hour to ensure a smooth transition.
Her interests overlap with mine in several areas, especially art and music.

Synset Examples

Our vacations overlap

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to coincide - to occur at or during the same time
  • Antonym: conflict - to be in opposition or not align

Etymology

From over- + lap (to fold or extend over).
