perilune

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛrɪˌluːn / (AmE), / ˈpɛrɪˌluːn / (BrE)
the point in the orbit of a spacecraft or other body where it is closest to the Moon
closest point to the Moon in orbit

Examples

The spacecraft adjusted its course to ensure its perilune would be above the designated landing site.
Accurate calculations of perilune are essential for successful lunar missions.
Scientists monitored the probe as it passed its perilune at a distance of 100 kilometers from the Moon's surface.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: apolune - farthest point from the Moon in orbit

Etymology

From Latin 'peri-' (around, near) and Latin 'luna' (moon).
