to synthesise

1. VERB

/ ˈsɪnθəˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪnθəsaɪz / (BrE)
to combine different elements or substances to form a more complex whole, especially in scientific or intellectual contexts
combine elements into a whole

Examples

Modern chemists can synthesise complex molecules that do not exist in nature.
She attempted to synthesise traditional and modern perspectives in her research.
The computer can synthesise human speech with remarkable accuracy.

Synset Examples

his operas synthesize music and drama in perfect harmony
The liver synthesizes vitamins

Related Terms

  • Synonym: combine - to join or merge elements together
  • Antonym: to analyze - to break down into separate components

Etymology

From Greek suntithenai (to put together), via French synthétiser.
