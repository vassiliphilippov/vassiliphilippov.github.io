misplaced

1. ADJ

/ mɪsˈpleɪst / (AmE), / mɪsˈpleɪst / (BrE)
Put in the wrong place or position.
put in the wrong place

Examples

The keys were misplaced and we spent hours searching for them.
Please check your documents for any misplaced papers before submitting them.
A misplaced book can make it difficult for others to find what they need.

Synset Examples

She was penalized for a spelling mistake or a misplaced accent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lost - unable to be found; not in the correct place
  • Antonym: correct - placed in the right spot or position

Etymology

From the prefix 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'placed', from Latin 'placere' (to place).

2. ADJ

/ mɪsˈpleɪst / (AmE), / mɪsˈpleɪst / (BrE)
(of an object) lost temporarily, especially because it has been put in an unusual or forgotten place.
temporarily lost; put in the wrong place

Examples

I finally found the misplaced keys under the pile of magazines.
The misplaced documents caused a delay in the meeting.
After searching everywhere, she realized her phone was simply misplaced and not stolen.

Synset Examples

the mislaid hat turned up eventually
misplaced tickets

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mislaid - temporarily lost by being put somewhere and forgotten
  • Degree Higher: lost - unable to be found, not in one's possession

Etymology

From mis- (wrongly) + place (verb).
