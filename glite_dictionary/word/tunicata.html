Tunicata

1. NOUN

/ tuːnɪˈkeɪtə / (AmE), / tjuːnɪˈkeɪtə / (BrE)
A subphylum of marine animals, commonly known as tunicates, characterized by a sac-like body and a tough outer covering.
a subphylum of marine filter-feeders

Examples

Many marine biologists study Tunicata for their unique life cycles and evolutionary traits.
Tunicata are found in oceans worldwide, often attached to rocks or floating freely.
The tough covering that protects Tunicata is what gives these creatures their name.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tunicate - a marine animal of the subphylum Tunicata

Etymology

From Latin 'tunica' (tunic, coat), referring to the animal's outer covering.

2. NOUN

/ ˌtuːnɪˈkeɪtə / (AmE), / ˌtjuːnɪˈkeɪtə / (BrE)
A subphylum of marine invertebrates, known as tunicates, which have a sac-like body structure and are covered by a tunic.
subphylum of marine tunicates

Examples

All members of the subphylum Tunicata are found exclusively in marine environments.
Scientists studied the unique digestive systems found in Tunicata for evolutionary clues.
Larval stages of Tunicata display characteristics similar to chordates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tunicate - A marine invertebrate of the subphylum Tunicata

Etymology

From Latin tunica (coat) referring to the outer covering or tunic.
