leatherjacket

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛðɚˌdʒækɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɛðəˌdʒækɪt / (BrE)
Any of several tropical fish of the New World, characterized by their small embedded scales and tough, leathery skin.
a tropical fish with tough, leathery skin

Examples

The diver spotted a brightly colored leatherjacket swimming near the coral reef.
The aquarium features several species of leatherjacket from the tropical Atlantic.
Local fishermen often catch leatherjackets alongside other tropical fish.

Etymology

Named for its tough, leathery skin resembling a leather jacket.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɛðɚˌdʒækɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɛðəˌdʒækɪt / (BrE)
the tough-skinned larva of certain crane flies, often found in soil and known to damage grass roots
larva of certain crane flies

Examples

The gardener was concerned about the high number of leatherjackets damaging his lawn.
Leatherjacket infestations can cause patches of dead grass in the spring.
Many birds feed on leatherjackets as an important part of their diet.

Etymology

From 'leather' (tough animal skin) and 'jacket', referring to the larva's tough outer layer.

3. NOUN

/ ˈlɛðərˌdʒækɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɛðəˌdʒækɪt / (BrE)
any of several brightly colored tropical filefishes with tough, leathery skin
a tropical filefish with tough skin

Examples

While snorkeling, we spotted a brightly colored leatherjacket swimming near the coral reef.
The tough skin of the leatherjacket helps protect it from predators in tropical waters.
Some aquarium enthusiasts keep leatherjacket fish because of their vivid patterns and interesting shapes.

Etymology

From 'leather' + 'jacket', referring to the tough skin of the fish.
