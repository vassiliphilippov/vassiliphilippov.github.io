autogeny

1. NOUN

/ ɔːˈtɑːdʒəni / (AmE), / ɔːˈtɒdʒəni / (BrE)
the now-discredited scientific theory that living organisms can arise spontaneously from nonliving matter
theory of life emerging from nonliving matter

Examples

In the nineteenth century, many scientists debated the plausibility of autogeny.
The hypothesis of autogeny was eventually displaced by the concept of biogenesis.
Textbooks now refer to autogeny as a historical idea rather than current scientific theory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abiogenesis - the origin of life from nonliving matter
  • Antonym: biogenesis - the theory that living organisms arise only from other living organisms

Etymology

From Greek 'auto-' (self) + '-geny' (production), via scientific Latin.
