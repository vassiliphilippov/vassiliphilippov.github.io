Psilophyton

1. NOUN

/ saɪ.loʊˈfaɪ.tɒn / (AmE), / sɪ.ləˈfaɪ.tɒn / (BrE)
a genus of small, wiry, herbaceous plants of the Paleozoic era with underground rhizomes and apical sporangia, considered the type genus of the family Psilophytaceae
a genus of early Paleozoic herbaceous plants

Examples

Fossils of Psilophyton have provided crucial insights into early plant evolution.
The stems of Psilophyton were wiry and branched, with sporangia at the tips.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Psilophyton genus in a Devonian rock formation.

Etymology

From Greek psilos (bare) + phyton (plant).
