Nigroporus

1. NOUN

/ ˌnaɪɡroʊˈpɔrəs / (AmE), / ˌnɪɡrəʊˈpɔːrəs / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Polyporaceae, known for its wood-decaying species found in tropical and subtropical regions.
genus of wood-decaying fungi

Examples

Several species of Nigroporus play an important role in the decomposition of dead wood in rainforests.
Mycologists recently identified a rare Nigroporus growing on a decaying log in the Amazon.
The genus Nigroporus is distinguished by its dark-colored pores and tough fruiting bodies.

Etymology

From Latin 'niger' (black) and Greek 'poros' (pore), referring to the dark pores of some species.

2. NOUN

/ ˌnaɪɡrəˈpɔrəs / (AmE), / ˌnɪɡrəˈpɔːrəs / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Polyporaceae, known for bracket-like fruiting bodies commonly growing on wood.
genus of fungi in Polyporaceae

Examples

Species in the genus Nigroporus are found primarily in tropical regions.
Researchers identified several wood-rotting fungi, including Nigroporus and its relatives.
The bracket fungus Nigroporus is notable for its dark pores and tough texture.

Etymology

From Latin 'niger' (black) and Greek 'poros' (pore), referring to the dark-pored fruiting bodies.
