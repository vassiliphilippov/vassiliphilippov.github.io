sinfulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪnfəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪnfəlnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being sinful; estrangement from God or deviation from moral or religious law.
the state of being sinful

Examples

He often spoke about the sinfulness of human nature in his sermons.
Many religious traditions focus on ways to overcome sinfulness and seek forgiveness.
Her sense of sinfulness drove her to seek redemption and change her ways.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wickedness - the quality of being evil or morally wrong
  • Antonym: righteousness - the quality of being morally right or justifiable

Etymology

From 'sinful' + '-ness'; 'sinful' from Old English 'synful' (full of sin).
