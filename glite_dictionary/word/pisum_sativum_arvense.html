Pisum sativum arvense

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiːzəm səˈtiːvəm ɑːrˈvɛns / (AmE), / ˈpiːzəm səˈtiːvəm ɑːˈvɛns / (BrE)
A variety of the pea plant, native to the Mediterranean region and North Africa, widely cultivated especially as forage for livestock.
pea plant variety grown for forage

Examples

Pisum sativum arvense is often grown as green manure to enrich soil fertility.
Farmers in the Mediterranean region have cultivated Pisum sativum arvense for centuries as animal feed.
The forage quality of Pisum sativum arvense makes it a popular choice in sustainable agriculture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pea - a round, green seed of a leguminous plant or the plant itself

Etymology

From Latin: 'Pisum' (pea), 'sativum' (cultivated), 'arvense' (of the field).
