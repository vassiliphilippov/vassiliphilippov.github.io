Oneida

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈnaɪdə / (AmE), / əʊˈnaɪdə / (BrE)
a member of an Indigenous people, originally living east of Lake Ontario, belonging to the Iroquoian language family
member of an Iroquoian Indigenous people

Examples

Many Oneida relocated to Wisconsin during the 19th century.
As a child, she learned the traditions of her Oneida ancestors.
The Oneida played a key role in the history of the Iroquois Confederacy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Iroquois - a confederacy of Indigenous peoples including the Oneida

Etymology

From the Oneida people's own name for themselves, Onyota'a:ka, meaning 'People of the Standing Stone'.

2. NOUN

/ oʊˈnaɪdə / (AmE), / əʊˈnaɪdə / (BrE)
the Iroquoian language spoken by the Oneida people
Iroquoian language of the Oneida people

Examples

Few people are fluent in Oneida today, making language revitalization crucial for the community.
She is studying Oneida to connect more deeply with her heritage.
The dictionary includes common phrases from the Oneida language.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Mohawk - an Iroquoian language spoken by the Mohawk people
  • Synonym: Seneca - an Iroquoian language spoken by the Seneca people

Etymology

From the name of the Oneida people, themselves an Indigenous group in North America.
