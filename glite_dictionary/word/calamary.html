calamary

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæləˈmɑri / (AmE), / ˌkæləˈmɑːri / (BrE)
Squid prepared and eaten as food, especially in Italian cuisine.
squid served as food (Italian cuisine)

Examples

She ordered the calamary as an appetizer at the Italian restaurant.
Fried calamary is a popular dish at seafood festivals along the coast.
The chef recommended grilled calamary with lemon and herbs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calamari - squid prepared as food, especially fried

Etymology

From Italian 'calamaro', ultimately from Latin 'calamarius' (pertaining to a pen), due to the squid's pen-like internal shell.
