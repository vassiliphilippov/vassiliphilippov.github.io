to unstring

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈstrɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈstrɪŋ / (BrE)
to remove the strings from something, such as a musical instrument or a bow
remove strings from something

Examples

Before cleaning it, I need to unstring my violin.
The archer carefully unstrung his bow after the competition.
She started to unstring the old guitar for repairs.

Synset Examples

unstring my guitar

Related Terms

  • Antonym: string - to fit or furnish with strings

Etymology

From un- (meaning 'not, opposite of, remove') + string.
