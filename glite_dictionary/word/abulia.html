abulia

1. NOUN

/ əˈbuːliə / (AmE), / əˈbjuːlɪə / (BrE)
A loss or impairment of the ability to make decisions or act voluntarily; an abnormal lack of willpower or initiative, often seen in certain mental illnesses.
loss of willpower or initiative
medical

Examples

The patient displayed signs of abulia, making it difficult for him to make even simple choices.
Researchers have linked abulia to certain areas of brain damage following a stroke.
Her increasing abulia worried her family, as she became unable to carry out daily tasks.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek aboulia, from 'a-' (without) + 'boulē' (will).
