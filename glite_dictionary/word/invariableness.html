invariableness

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈvɛriəbəlnəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈveərɪəblnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of remaining unchanged or resistant to variation
state of being unchanging

Examples

The invariableness of his attitude impressed everyone during the crisis.
Scientists often rely on the invariableness of certain physical constants in their research.
The contract requires the invariableness of interest rates over the loan period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invariance - the quality of remaining unchanged
  • Antonym: variability - tendency to change or vary

Etymology

From Latin 'invariabilis' (unchangeable) + English suffix '-ness'.
