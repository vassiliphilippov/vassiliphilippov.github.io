Giovanni da Verrazano

1. PROPN

/ dʒiˈɑːvni dɑ vɛrəˈzɑːnoʊ / (AmE), / dʒiˈævni də ˌvɛrəˈzɑːnəʊ / (BrE)
(c. 1485–1528) An Italian explorer and navigator from Florence, known for charting the eastern coast of North America.
Italian explorer of eastern North America

Examples

Giovanni da Verrazano was the first European to enter New York Bay and explore its surroundings.
Many historians credit Giovanni da Verrazano with opening the way for later French exploration in North America.
The Verrazzano-Narrows Bridge in New York is named after Giovanni da Verrazano in honor of his achievements.

Etymology

Italian name; 'Giovanni' is the Italian form of 'John', 'da Verrazano' means 'from Verrazzano', his place of origin.
