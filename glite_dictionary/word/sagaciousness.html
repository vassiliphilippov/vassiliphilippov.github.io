sagaciousness

1. NOUN

/ səˈɡeɪʃəsnəs / (AmE), / səˈɡeɪʃəsnəs / (BrE)
The quality of having or showing keen judgment and discernment.
keen judgment; discernment

Examples

Her sagaciousness impressed the board, as she quickly identified the crucial issues in the proposal.
Many admired his sagaciousness when negotiating difficult matters.
Without sagaciousness, it's easy to be misled by superficial arguments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sagacity - the quality of being sagacious; wisdom, discernment
  • Antonym: foolishness - lack of good sense or judgment

Etymology

From Latin sagax (wise, discerning) + English suffix -ness.

2. NOUN

/ səˈɡeɪʃəsnəs / (AmE), / səˈɡeɪʃəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of having or showing good judgment and keen insight; wisdom
quality of being wise and insightful

Examples

Her sagaciousness in business negotiations earned her widespread respect among her colleagues.
The politician's sagaciousness was evident in his thoughtful responses to difficult questions.
We admire her sagaciousness when it comes to solving complex problems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wisdom - the ability to make sound decisions and judgments
  • Antonym: foolishness - lack of good sense or judgment

Etymology

From Latin 'sagax, sagacis' (quick, wise) via 'sagacious' + '-ness'.
