perquisite

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrkwəzɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɜːkwɪzɪt / (BrE)
a right or privilege that is reserved exclusively by a particular person or group, especially by hereditary or official position
exclusive right or privilege

Examples

Company cars are often considered a perquisite of senior management positions.
Owning land was once the perquisite of nobility in many European societies.
Voting rights were historically a perquisite limited to wealthy property owners.

Synset Examples

suffrage was the prerogative of white adult males

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prerogative - an exclusive or special right, power, or privilege

Etymology

From Latin perquisitum (something acquired), from perquirere (to search for thoroughly).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɜːr.kwɪ.zɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.kwɪ.zɪt / (BrE)
An additional benefit or privilege received as part of one's job, often seen as a right, beyond regular salary or wages.
an extra benefit of a job

Examples

One of the perquisites of her position is the use of a company car.
Generous holiday allowances are considered valuable perquisites by many employees.
Executive perquisites often include club memberships and travel expenses.

Synset Examples

a limousine is one of the fringe benefits of the job

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perk - an informal term for an extra benefit at work

Etymology

From Latin perquisitum (thing acquired), from perquirere (to search for, obtain).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13319034-n
Synset POS: n
