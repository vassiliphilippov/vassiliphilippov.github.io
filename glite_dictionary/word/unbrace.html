to unbrace

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈbreɪs / (AmE), / ʌnˈbreɪs / (BrE)
to release something from a state of tension, support, or bracing; to make less tight, tense, or secure
release from tension or support

Examples

The workers had to unbrace the scaffolding before they could move it.
She remembered to unbrace herself as the car came to a stop.
After the storm passed, they began to unbrace the windows and remove the boards.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: brace - to make something tight, tense, or secure; to support

Etymology

From un- (prefix meaning 'not' or 'reverse') + brace (to support or make tense).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00420819-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ʌnˈbreɪs / (AmE), / ʌnˈbreɪs / (BrE)
to remove a brace or braces from something; to release from a braced or secured position
remove a brace or braces from

Examples

After the repairs were completed, the workers unbraced the scaffolding.
He carefully unbraced the door to let some fresh air in.
The technicians need to unbrace this section before moving it.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: brace - to fasten or secure with a brace
  • Synonym: to unfasten - to open or undo something fastened

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (meaning 'to reverse an action') + 'brace'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00226006-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ʌnˈbreɪs / (AmE), / ʌnˈbreɪs / (BrE)
to remove or undo braces, ties, or supports from something; to unfasten or release from binding or constraining
to remove or undo braces or ties

Examples

He quickly unbraced the ropes holding the package together.
After the surgery, the doctor decided to unbrace her knee.
The carpenter had to unbrace the beams before starting the repair.

Synset Examples

They untied the prisoner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to untie - to remove or undo a tie or knot

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (not, reverse of) + 'brace' (support or fastening).
