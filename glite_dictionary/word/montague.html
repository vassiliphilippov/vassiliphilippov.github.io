Montague

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɑn.təˌgju / (AmE), / ˈmɒn.tə.gjuː / (BrE)
One of the two feuding families in Shakespeare's 'Romeo and Juliet', traditionally rivals of the Capulets.
rival family to the Capulets in Shakespeare

In literary studies, the conflict between Montague and Capulet families is often analyzed for its symbolic meaning.
