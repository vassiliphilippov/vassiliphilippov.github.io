bracer

1. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪsər / (AmE), / ˈbreɪsə / (BrE)
a protective covering worn on the wrist or forearm, used especially in archery, fencing, or similar sports to prevent injury
protective wrist or arm covering (sports)

Examples

She wore a bracer on her arm to prevent the bowstring from hitting her skin.
The fencing instructor recommended a leather bracer for added protection.
His collection included an antique bracer used by medieval archers.

Etymology

From Old French *brassiere*, from *bras* (arm).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbreɪsɚ / (AmE), / ˈbreɪsə / (BrE)
A drink, especially containing alcohol, taken to give strength or revive the spirits.
stimulating alcoholic drink

Examples

He poured himself a stiff bracer before heading out into the storm.
A quick bracer was just what she needed to face the cold morning.
Many believe a good bracer can lift one’s spirits after a long day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pick-me-up - a drink or medicine taken for quick reviving effect

Etymology

From Middle English *bracen* (to brace), from Old French *bracier* (to embrace, support).
