chatoyant

1. ADJ

/ ʃəˈtɔɪənt / (AmE), / ʃəˈtɔɪənt / (BrE)
Showing a change of color or luster when seen from different angles, especially describing gemstones or fabrics.
changing color/luster from different angles

Examples

The chatoyant surface of the stone shimmered in the sunlight.
Her dress was made of chatoyant silk that gleamed with shifting colors.
The jeweler admired the chatoyant effect in the cat's eye gemstone.

Synset Examples

changeable taffeta
chatoyant (or shot) silk
a dragonfly hovered, vibrating and iridescent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iridescent - showing luminous colors that seem to change from different angles
  • Antonym: dull - not bright, shiny, or varying in color

Etymology

From French 'chatoyant', present participle of 'chatoyer' (to shine like a cat's eye).
