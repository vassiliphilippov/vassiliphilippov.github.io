Santalum

1. NOUN

/ ˈsæn.təl.əm / (AmE), / ˈsan.təl.əm / (BrE)
A genus of parasitic trees native to Indonesia and Malaysia, including species known as sandalwood, valued for their fragrant wood and oils.
genus of sandalwood trees

Examples

The essential oil extracted from Santalum is highly prized in the perfume industry.
Several species within the Santalum genus are protected due to overharvesting.
Santalum trees are semi-parasitic, drawing nutrients from the roots of neighboring plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sandalwood - fragrant wood from Santalum species used in perfumes and carvings

Etymology

From Latin *santalum* (sandalwood), from Greek *santalon*, from Sanskrit *candana* (sandalwood).
