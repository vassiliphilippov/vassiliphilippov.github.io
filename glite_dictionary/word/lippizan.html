Lippizan

1. NOUN

/ lɪpɪˈzæn / (AmE), / lɪpɪˈzæn / (BrE)
A breed of compact, sturdy saddle horse originally bred and trained in Vienna, known for its intelligence, docility, and excellence in dressage.
A breed of saddle horse from Vienna used in dressage

Examples

The Spanish Riding School is famous for its elegant routines featuring the Lippizan horses.
He dreamed of training a Lippizan for competitive dressage one day.
As a foal, the Lippizan is usually dark-colored and gradually becomes white with age.

Synset Examples

a Lippizan is black or brown when born but turns white by the time it is five years old

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Lipizzaner - Another term for the Lippizan breed of horse

Etymology

From Lipizza (now Lipica in Slovenia), where the breed was developed.
