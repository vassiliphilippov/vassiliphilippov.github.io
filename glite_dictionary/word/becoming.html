becoming

1. ADJ

/ bɪˈkʌmɪŋ / (AmE), / bɪˈkʌmɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a piece of clothing, color, etc.) enhancing someone's appearance; flattering; making someone look attractive or suitable.
flattering; making someone look attractive

Examples

That dress is very becoming on you and really brings out the color of your eyes.
She wore a becoming shade of blue that suited her complexion perfectly.
His becoming manner made him popular at every social gathering.

Synset Examples

a becoming new shade of rose
a becoming portrait

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unbecoming - (of appearance or behavior) not flattering or appropriate

Etymology

Present participle of 'become', used as an adjective since the late 14th century.

2. ADJ

/ bɪˈkʌmɪŋ / (AmE), / bɪˈkʌmɪŋ / (BrE)
(of behavior or appearance) appropriate or suitable; fitting or proper according to custom or propriety.
fitting; appropriate; proper

Examples

Her becoming modesty was admired by everyone at the event.
It is not becoming for a guest to arrive excessively late to a dinner party.
He displayed becoming manners while meeting his partner's parents for the first time.

Synset Examples

her becoming modesty
comely behavior
it is not comme il faut for a gentleman to be constantly asking for money
seemly behavior

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unbecoming - not in keeping with standards of what is proper or suitable
  • Synonym: seemly - conforming to accepted notions of propriety or good taste
  • Synonym: proper - suitable or appropriate; following rules or customs

Etymology

From the verb 'become', of Old English origin, related to 'come'.

Source: WordNet
