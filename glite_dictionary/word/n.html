/ ɛn / (AmE), / ɛn / (BrE)

3. NOUN

/ ɛn / (AmE), / ɛn / (BrE)
The chemical symbol for nitrogen, a colorless, odorless, tasteless inert gas that makes up about 78 percent of Earth's atmosphere and is essential to all living things.
chemical symbol for nitrogen

Examples

The symbol N stands for nitrogen on the periodic table.
Atmospheric N is essential for plant growth after being converted to usable forms.
Liquid N is commonly used in cryogenic applications in laboratories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nitrogen - nonmetallic element, atomic number 7, colorless gas

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'nitrogen', from French 'nitrogène'.

4. NOUN

/ ɛn / (AmE), / ɛn / (BrE)
the symbol for newton, the SI unit of force equal to the force that gives a 1 kilogram mass an acceleration of 1 meter per second squared
SI unit of force (newton)

Examples

The force required to lift this object is about 10 N.
On the diagram, each arrow represents a force of 5 N acting on the body.
A pressure of 1 pascal is created when a force of 1 N is applied to an area of 1 square meter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: newton - SI unit of force (symbol: N)

Etymology

Named after Sir Isaac Newton (1642–1727), English mathematician and physicist.
