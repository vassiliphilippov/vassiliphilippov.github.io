to syllabize

1. VERB

/ ˈsɪləˌbaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪl.ə.baɪz / (BrE)
to pronounce or divide into syllables, especially by articulating each syllable separately and distinctly
to pronounce or divide into syllables

Examples

The teacher asked the student to syllabize each word slowly.
He syllabized the poem, making every sound clear for the audience.
To help with spelling, children often syllabize tricky words out loud.

Synset Examples

The poet syllabized the verses he read

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enunciate - to pronounce words or parts of words clearly

Etymology

From Medieval Latin syllabizare, from Greek syllabizein (to form syllables).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00982020-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈsɪləˌbaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪl.ə.baɪz / (BrE)
to divide a word into its constituent syllables
divide into syllables

Examples

Linguists often syllabize unfamiliar words to analyze their structure.
Could you syllabize this word for me, please?
Children learn how to syllabize words as part of reading instruction.

Synset Examples

syllabify the words

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to syllabify - to divide a word into syllables

Etymology

From Greek 'syllabizein', from 'syllabē' (syllable).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01566117-v
Synset POS: v
