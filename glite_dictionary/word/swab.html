swab

1. VERB

/ swɑb / (AmE), / swɒb / (BrE)
to clean or wash (a surface) using a swab or mop
clean with a swab or mop

Examples

The sailor was ordered to swab the deck until it shone.
Please swab the floors before the guests arrive.
After the surgery, the nurse carefully swabbed the operating table.

Synset Examples

swab the ship's decks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mop - to clean or soak up liquid using a mop

Etymology

From Dutch 'zwabberen' (to swab, mop).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01246742-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ swɑb / (AmE), / swɒb / (BrE)
A small piece of absorbent material, usually cotton, attached to the end of a stick, used for applying medication, cleaning wounds, or collecting samples.
absorbent cotton applicator or sampler

Examples

The nurse used a swab to clean the wound before applying the bandage.
A throat swab was taken to test for infection.
He dipped the swab in antiseptic and gently applied it to her cut.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cotton bud - a stick with absorbent material at each end, mainly for cleaning
  • Synonym: gauze - thin transparent fabric used for medical dressings

Etymology

Probably from Middle Dutch or Middle Low German *swabbe* (mop).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04374833-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ swɑb / (AmE), / swɒb / (BrE)
a cleaning implement consisting of absorbent material attached to a handle, used for cleaning floors or other surfaces
absorbent cleaning implement with a handle

Examples

He dipped the swab in the bucket before wiping the kitchen floor.
The janitor used a large swab to clean up the spill in the hallway.
After mopping, she rinsed the swab thoroughly and left it to dry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mop - a cleaning tool with a bundle of absorbent strings or material attached to a long handle

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *swabbe* (mop, swab, plug).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04374567-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ swɑb / (AmE), / swɒb / (BrE)
to apply a liquid to a surface, typically by dabbing or wiping with an absorbent material
apply liquid with an absorbent material

Examples

To disinfect the wound, she carefully swabbed it with an antiseptic solution.
The nurse swabbed his arm before administering the injection.
He swabbed the counter with a damp cloth to remove the spill.

Synset Examples

dab the wall with paint

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dab - to apply a small amount of something lightly or gently
  • Synonym: wipe - to clean or dry a surface by rubbing with a cloth or something similar

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *swabben* (to swab, mop), related to 'swabber'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
