eyry

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈɪəri / (BrE)
a lofty nest of a bird of prey such as a hawk or eagle, typically built in a high, inaccessible place
lofty nest of a bird of prey
archaic

Examples

The eagle returned to its eyry perched high on the rocky cliff.
From her vantage point, she could just make out the hawk's eyry among the pines.
Legends say the king built his fortress where the falcon made its eyry on the mountain peak.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aerie - a large nest of a bird of prey, especially an eagle, built high in a tree or on a cliff
  • Degree Lower: nest - a structure built by birds to lay eggs and rear young

Etymology

Variant of 'eyrie', from Middle English *eyrie*, from Old French *aire* (nest), from Latin *area* (open space).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪri / (AmE), / ˈɪəri / (BrE)
a house, nest, or dwelling located at a high elevation, especially one occupied by birds of prey
dwelling or nest at high elevation
archaic

Examples

The old castle stood atop the cliff like an eyry, unreachable and remote.
Golden eagles built their eyry on a ledge halfway up the mountain face.
On his travels, he encountered a hermit's eyry perched high above the valley.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aerie - the nest of a bird of prey, especially an eagle, typically built high in a tree or on a cliff

Etymology

Variant of 'aerie', from Old French *aire* (nest of a bird of prey), from Latin *area* (level ground, threshing floor).
