terrestrial

1. ADJ

/ təˈrɛstriəl / (AmE), / təˈrɛstriəl / (BrE)
concerned with earthly or worldly matters, as opposed to spiritual or heavenly things
relating to worldly matters

Examples

His interests were mainly terrestrial, focusing on politics and business rather than philosophy.
She set aside her terrestrial concerns and began to meditate on spiritual questions.
The novel explores both terrestrial desires and supernatural forces.

Synset Examples

mundane affairs
he developed an immense terrestrial practicality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mundane - concerned with ordinary or worldly matters

Etymology

From Latin terrestris (of the earth), from terra (earth).

2. ADJ

/ təˈrɛstriəl / (AmE), / təˈrɛstriəl / (BrE)
relating to or living on land rather than in the water or air.
living or existing on land

Examples

The rainforest is home to a wide variety of terrestrial animals.
Most orchids are terrestrial, but some species grow on trees.
Human beings are a terrestrial species, adapted for life on land.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: aquatic - living in or relating to water
  • Antonym: marine - related to the sea; living in the sea

Etymology

From Latin terrestris (of the earth), from terra (earth).
