hymenopteron

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪmɪˈnɑptəˌrɒn / (AmE), / ˌhɪmɪˈnɒptərɒn / (BrE)
An insect belonging to the order Hymenoptera, typically having two pairs of membranous wings and in many species an ovipositor adapted for stinging or piercing.
insect of order Hymenoptera (e.g. wasps, bees, ants)

Examples

The honeybee is a well-known hymenopteron that plays a crucial role in pollination.
Scientists discovered a rare hymenopteron species deep in the rainforest.
Many social insects, such as ants and wasps, are classified as hymenopterons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hymenopteran - An insect of the order Hymenoptera; more common alternative spelling

Etymology

From Greek hymēn (membrane) + pteron (wing), referring to their membranous wings.
