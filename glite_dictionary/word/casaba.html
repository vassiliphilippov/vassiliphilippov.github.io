casaba

1. NOUN

/ kəˈsɑbə / (AmE), / kəˈsɑːbə / (BrE)
A type of melon with a yellowish rind and sweet, whitish flesh.
a melon with yellow rind and whitish flesh

Examples

I picked a ripe casaba from the market for our picnic.
The fruit salad contained slices of casaba along with honeydew and cantaloupe.
Have you ever tasted casaba? Its sweet, mild flavor is perfect for summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melon - a large, round fruit with sweet pulpy flesh and many seeds

Etymology

From Turkish 'kavun' (melon), probably via a regional name from Kasaba, Turkey.
