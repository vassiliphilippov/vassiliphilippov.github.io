lye

1. NOUN

/ laɪ / (AmE), / laɪ / (BrE)
a strongly alkaline solution, especially of sodium hydroxide or potassium hydroxide, used for cleaning and making soap
strong alkaline chemical solution

Examples

The old recipe calls for mixing fat with lye to make soap.
Be careful when handling lye, as it can cause severe burns.
He used a solution of lye to clean the clogged drain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caustic soda - sodium hydroxide, used in the same way as lye

Etymology

From Old English *lēag* (washings, lye), related to washing ash with water.
