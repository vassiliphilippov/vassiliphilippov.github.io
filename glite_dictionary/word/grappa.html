grappa

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrɑːpə / (AmE), / ˈɡrapə / (BrE)
A strong Italian brandy made from the residue of grapes after they have been pressed for wine.
Italian brandy from pressed grape residue

Examples

After dinner, he enjoyed a small glass of grappa as a digestive.
The restaurant offers several varieties of grappa imported directly from Italy.
She found the taste of grappa much stronger than other spirits she had tried.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brandy - a strong alcoholic spirit distilled from wine or fermented fruit juice

Etymology

Italian, from 'grappa' (grape stalk), the drink's name.
