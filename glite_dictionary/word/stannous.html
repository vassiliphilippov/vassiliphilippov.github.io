stannous

1. ADJ

/ ˈstænəs / (AmE), / ˈstanəs / (BrE)
(of a compound) containing tin, especially in the divalent state (tin(II)); relating to or derived from tin.
containing or relating to tin (esp. divalent)

Examples

Toothpaste with stannous fluoride helps to prevent cavities and strengthen enamel.
The chemist analyzed a sample of stannous chloride for impurities.
Stannous compounds are commonly used in electroplating processes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: stannic - containing tin in the tetravalent (tin(IV)) state

Etymology

From Latin 'stannum' (tin) + '-ous', used to denote substances containing tin in the +2 oxidation state.
