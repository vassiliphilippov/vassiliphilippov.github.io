Bougainvillaea

1. NOUN

/ ˌbuːɡənˈvɪliə / (AmE), / ˌbʊɡənˈvɪliə / (BrE)
A genus of ornamental tropical woody vines with brightly colored bracts, popular in gardens and landscaping.
genus of ornamental tropical vines

Examples

The vibrant flowers of the Bougainvillaea cover the walls every spring.
A Bougainvillaea vine can quickly transform a plain fence into a colorful display.
Gardeners often train Bougainvillaea to grow along trellises for a dramatic effect.

Etymology

Named after French navigator Louis Antoine de Bougainville (1729–1811).
