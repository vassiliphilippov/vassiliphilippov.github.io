rectus

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛktəs / (AmE), / ˈrɛktəs / (BrE)
any of several straight muscles in the body, especially those in the abdomen, thigh, or eye
a straight muscle (anatomy)
medical

Examples

The rectus abdominis is essential for flexing the spinal column.
Surgeons made the incision close to the rectus muscle to access the affected area.
Eye movement disorders can be caused by issues with the lateral rectus muscle.

Etymology

From Latin 'rectus' (straight, upright), used in anatomical naming to indicate a straight muscle.
