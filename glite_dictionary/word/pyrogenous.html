pyrogenous

1. ADJ

/ paɪˈrɑːdʒənəs / (AmE), / paɪˈrɒdʒɪnəs / (BrE)
produced or formed under conditions involving intense heat, especially used of rocks or substances created by high temperatures
produced by intense heat

Examples

The sudden formation of pyrogenous rocks transformed the landscape.
Some minerals are classified as pyrogenous due to their origin in volcanic activity.
Geologists study pyrogenous layers to understand ancient volcanic eruptions.

Synset Examples

igneous rock is rock formed by solidification from a molten state; especially from molten magma
igneous fusion is fusion by heat alone
pyrogenic strata

Related Terms

  • Synonym: igneous - (of rocks) formed by solidification from molten material
  • Synonym: pyrogenic - produced or caused by heat, especially in geological contexts

Etymology

From Greek 'pyr-' (fire) + '-genous' (produced by).

2. ADJ

/ paɪˈrɑːdʒənəs / (AmE), / paɪˈrɒdʒənəs / (BrE)
producing or caused by fever; relating to substances or agents that cause fever.
producing or caused by fever
medical

Examples

The bacteria were found to release a pyrogenous substance that triggered fever in patients.
Some vaccines contain trace amounts of pyrogenous materials that must be removed.
Researchers identified a pyrogenous reaction as the cause of the patient's high temperature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pyretic - relating to or causing fever

Etymology

From Greek 'pyr' (fire) + '-genous' (producing).
