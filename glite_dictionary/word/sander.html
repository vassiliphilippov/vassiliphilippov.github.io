sander

1. NOUN

/ ˈsændɚ / (AmE), / ˈsændə / (BrE)
a power tool used for smoothing or polishing surfaces, especially wood, by abrasion with sandpaper moved by a motor
power tool for smoothing wood or surfaces

Examples

He used a sander to smooth the rough edges of the table.
The carpenter replaced the old sander with a newer, more powerful model.
Always wear a mask when operating a sander to avoid inhaling dust.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sandpaper - abrasive paper used for smoothing surfaces
  • Synonym: polisher - tool used for making surfaces smooth and shiny

Etymology

From 'sand' (abrasive material) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
