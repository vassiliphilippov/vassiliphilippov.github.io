doeskin

1. NOUN

/ ˈdoʊˌskɪn / (AmE), / ˈdəʊˌskɪn / (BrE)
A soft, fine leather made from the skin of a female deer or, sometimes, a lamb.
soft leather from deerskin or lambskin

Examples

She preferred gloves made of doeskin for their softness and warmth.
The tailor recommended a doeskin jacket for its luxurious feel.
He ran his hand over the smooth surface of the doeskin upholstery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suede - soft, napped leather with a velvety surface
  • Synonym: deerskin - leather made from the hide of a deer

Etymology

Formed from 'doe' (female deer) + 'skin'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdoʊˌskɪn / (AmE), / ˈdəʊˌskɪn / (BrE)
a fine, smooth, soft woolen fabric, often used for clothing or upholstery
fine soft woolen fabric

Examples

The tailor recommended a suit made of doeskin for its elegance and comfort.
She ran her fingers across the doeskin, admiring its luxurious texture.
Upholstered in grey doeskin, the chair added a touch of sophistication to the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flannel - a soft woven woolen fabric

Etymology

From 'doe' (a female deer) + 'skin', originally referring to the actual skin of a doe, later used for soft woolen fabric.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
