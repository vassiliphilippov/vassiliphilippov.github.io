Mercenaria mercenaria

1. NOUN

/ mərˌsiːˈnɛriə mərˌsiːˈnɛriə / (AmE), / mɜːˌsiːˈnɛəriə mɜːˌsiːˈnɛəriə / (BrE)
a large edible American clam with a heavy, rounded shell, historically used by some Native American peoples as shell money
an edible American clam species

Examples

Large Mercenaria mercenaria are often harvested along the Atlantic coast for use in chowders.
Some Native American tribes valued Mercenaria mercenaria shells for making wampum.
The scientist identified the shell as belonging to Mercenaria mercenaria.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quahog - common name for Mercenaria mercenaria, an edible clam

Etymology

From Latin 'mercenarius' (hired, paid) and used as a scientific name; relates to its use as shell money.
