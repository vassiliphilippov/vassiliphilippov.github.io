tup

1. NOUN

/ tʌp / (AmE), / tʌp / (BrE)
an uncastrated adult male sheep, especially as used in British English
uncastrated adult male sheep (British)
british

Examples

The farmer introduced a new tup to the flock for breeding season.
During the autumn, several tups were separated from the ewes.
In the market, the prize-winning tup attracted much attention from breeders.

Synset Examples

a British term is ‘tup’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ram - an uncastrated adult male sheep; primary term in general English

Etymology

From Middle English *topp*, possibly of Scandinavian origin, related to Old Norse *toppr* (tuft of hair).
