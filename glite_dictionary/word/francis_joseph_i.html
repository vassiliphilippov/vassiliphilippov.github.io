Francis Joseph I

1. PROPN

/ ˈfræn.sɪs ˈdʒoʊ.zəf ðə ˈfɜrst / (AmE), / ˈfræn.sɪs ˈdʒəʊ.zɛf ðə ˈfɜːst / (BrE)
(1830–1916) Emperor of Austria and king of Hungary, who reigned from 1848 to 1916.
Austrian emperor and Hungarian king (1830–1916)

Examples

Francis Joseph I ruled the Austro-Hungarian Empire for nearly seventy years.
The defeat of Francis Joseph I by Napoleon III at Magenta altered the course of European history.
Historians often examine the reforms introduced during the long reign of Francis Joseph I.
