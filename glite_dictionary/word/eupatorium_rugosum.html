Eupatorium rugosum

1. NOUN

/ juːˌpeɪtɔːriəm ruːˈɡoʊsəm / (AmE), / juːˌpætɔːˈrɪəm ruːˈɡəʊsəm / (BrE)
a North American perennial herb with clusters of small white flowers, also known as white snakeroot, which is toxic and associated with milk sickness in livestock and humans
North American toxic herb; white snakeroot

Examples

Cattle that graze on Eupatorium rugosum may develop symptoms of milk sickness.
The woodland was filled with clusters of Eupatorium rugosum in bloom late in summer.
Researchers are studying the toxic compounds found in Eupatorium rugosum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: white snakeroot - common name for Eupatorium rugosum, a toxic herb

Etymology

From Latin genus name Eupatorium and species epithet rugosum, meaning 'wrinkled'.
