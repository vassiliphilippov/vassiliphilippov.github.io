skid

1. VERB

/ skɪd / (AmE), / skɪd / (BrE)
to slide, typically sideways or out of control, often as a result of applying the brakes suddenly on a slippery surface
to slide out of control, often when braking

Examples

The car began to skid when he slammed on the brakes.
She lost control and skidded into the intersection during the storm.
If you skid on ice, try not to panic and steer gently.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slip - to slide unintentionally, usually for a short distance
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01875210-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ skɪd / (AmE), / skɪd / (BrE)
one of a pair of long, flat planks or supports, placed on the ground to make a track for rolling or sliding heavy objects
plank used for sliding objects

Examples

The workers placed a skid under the crate to help move it across the floor.
Engineers used a pair of skids to slide the heavy machinery into position.
A wooden skid can prevent cargo from tipping during transport.
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04235906-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ skɪd / (AmE), / skɪd / (BrE)
an instance of sliding unintentionally, typically by a vehicle losing traction on a slippery surface
an unintentional sliding movement (esp. of vehicles)

Examples

The car went into a skid on the icy road, but the driver regained control.
A sudden skid caused the cyclist to fall off his bike.
Experienced drivers know how to react if their vehicle goes into a skid during rain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slide - an act of moving smoothly along a surface while maintaining continuous contact with it

4. VERB

/ skɪd / (AmE), / skɪd / (BrE)
(of a vehicle or wheeled object) to slide uncontrollably, typically sideways, on a slippery surface.
slide uncontrollably (vehicle, wheels)

Examples

The bus started to skid on the icy hill.
He lost control and watched his bicycle skid across the gravel path.
If you brake too hard in the rain, your car may skid unexpectedly.

5. VERB

/ skɪd / (AmE), / skɪd / (BrE)
(of a vehicle, wheel, or similar) to slide sideways or obliquely, usually in an uncontrolled or accidental manner.
slide uncontrollably sideways

Examples

The car began to skid on the icy road, narrowly avoiding an accident.
If you brake too hard on gravel, your tires might skid out of control.
She lost her balance and felt her bicycle skid as she turned the corner.

Synset Examples

the wheels skidded against the sidewalk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slide - move smoothly along a surface while maintaining contact with it
  • Synonym: swerve - to turn aside suddenly, often to avoid something

Etymology

Probably from Scandinavian (compare Swedish 'skida' meaning 'ski' or 'slat'), originally referring to sliding on a smooth surface.

6. VERB

/ skɪd / (AmE), / skɪd / (BrE)
to lift or raise an object onto skids in order to support or move it more easily
raise onto skids for support or transport

Examples

We had to skid the heavy machinery before it could be loaded onto the truck.
Workers skidded the large crate with metal rollers for easy transportation.
