craniometer

1. NOUN

/ kreɪniˈɑmɪtər / (AmE), / kreɪnɪˈɒmɪtə / (BrE)
an instrument used for measuring the size and dimensions of the skull
instrument for measuring skull size
medical

Examples

The anthropologist used a craniometer to gather data about the ancient skulls.
A craniometer is essential for accurately measuring cranial capacity in forensic studies.
With the help of a craniometer, scientists compared the skulls of different primate species.

Etymology

From Greek 'kranion' (skull) + '-meter' (measuring device).
