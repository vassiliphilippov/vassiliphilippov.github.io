kvass

1. NOUN

/ kvɑs / (AmE), / kvɑːs / (BrE)
A mildly alcoholic, fermented beverage resembling beer, made from rye bread or barley and popular in Eastern Europe.
fermented drink made from rye or barley

Examples

In Russia, people often drink kvass during the summer to cool down.
She brewed her own kvass at home using stale rye bread and sugar.
Kvass is less alcoholic than beer and has a unique, tangy flavor.

Etymology

From Russian квас (kvas), meaning "leaven" or "fermented beverage."
