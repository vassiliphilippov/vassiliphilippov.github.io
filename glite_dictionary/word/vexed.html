vexed

1. ADJ

/ vɛkst / (AmE), / vɛkst / (BrE)
feeling or showing irritation, annoyance, or distress, especially from petty annoyances or persistent problems
annoyed or distressed

Examples

The vexed manager struggled to resolve the ongoing conflict among her employees.
His vexed expression told me he was bothered by more than just the noise outside.
Many citizens remain vexed about the lack of clear information from local government officials.

Synset Examples

harassed working mothers
a harried expression
her poor pestered father had to endure her constant interruptions
the vexed parents of an unruly teenager

Related Terms

  • Synonym: annoyed - feeling slightly angry or irritated
  • Antonym: calm - free from agitation or disturbance

Etymology

From Latin vexare (to shake, disturb).

2. ADJ

/ vɛkst / (AmE), / vɛkst / (BrE)
(of a problem or subject) difficult and much debated; problematic or disputed.
much debated or problematic

Examples

The vexed question of climate change continues to divide politicians worldwide.
Education funding remains a vexed issue in many developing countries.
They debated the vexed matter for hours without reaching a solution.

Synset Examples

the vexed issue of priorities
we live in vexed and troubled times

Related Terms

  • Synonym: controversial - giving rise to public disagreement or heated discussion
  • Antonym: settled - resolved or firmly decided; not in dispute

Etymology

From Old French vexer, from Latin vexare (to shake, harass, disturb).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00752662-s
Synset POS: s
