hypocorism

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈpɑːkəˌrɪzəm / (AmE), / hɪˈpɒkərɪzəm / (BrE)
a pet name or term of endearment, especially one formed by using a diminutive suffix
pet name; diminutive endearment

Examples

‘Billy’ is a hypocorism for ‘William’.
Many parents use a hypocorism when speaking to their young children.
In some cultures, a person may be called by a hypocorism more often than by their actual name.

Synset Examples

‘Billy’ is a hypocorism for ‘William’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diminutive - a word or suffix indicating smallness or affection

Etymology

From Greek hypokorisma (pet name), from hypokorizesthai (to use pet names).
