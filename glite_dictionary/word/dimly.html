dimly

1. ADV

/ ˈdɪmli / (AmE), / ˈdɪmli / (BrE)
In a way that is not bright, clear, or distinct.
in a faint or unclear manner

Examples

The room was dimly lit by a single candle on the table.
She could dimly remember her childhood home, but most details had faded.
The outline of the mountain appeared dimly through the thick morning fog.

Synset Examples

we perceived the change only dimly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: faintly - in a faint or not strong manner
  • Antonym: brightly - in a bright or clear manner

Etymology

From 'dim' + '-ly'; 'dim' from Old English 'dymm' (dark, gloomy).

2. ADV

/ ˈdɪmli / (AmE), / ˈdɪmli / (BrE)
In a way that is only faintly or poorly illuminated; with a dim light.
in a faint or dim light

Examples

The hallway was dimly lit by a single bulb.
Through the mist, she could dimly make out the outline of a house.
They could dimly hear the sound of music from across the street.

Synset Examples

a dimly lit room

Related Terms

  • Antonym: brightly - in a bright light
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00213262-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ ˈdɪmli / (AmE), / ˈdɪmli / (BrE)
In a way that lacks interest, energy, or vitality.
lacking interest or vitality

Examples

The speech was delivered dimly, leaving the audience unmoved.
Their enthusiasm for the project faded, and progress continued dimly.
The party was dimly attended and lacked any real excitement.

Synset Examples

a palely entertaining show

Etymology

From 'dim' + -ly, forming an adverb from the adjective 'dim'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00419049-r
Synset POS: r
