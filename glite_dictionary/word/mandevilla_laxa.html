Mandevilla laxa

1. NOUN

/ mænˈdɛvɪlə ˈlæksə / (AmE), / manˈdɛvɪlə ˈlæksə / (BrE)
A species of woody vine native to Argentina, cultivated for its glossy leaves and clusters of large, fragrant, creamy-white, funnel-shaped flowers.
woody vine with fragrant white flowers

Examples

The garden's centerpiece was a stunning Mandevilla laxa in full bloom by the patio.
Many gardeners choose Mandevilla laxa for its showy, fragrant flowers and attractive foliage.
She trained the Mandevilla laxa to climb the trellis near the front entrance.

Etymology

From the genus name Mandevilla (after Henry Mandeville, British diplomat and gardener) and the Latin 'laxa' meaning 'loose' or 'open'.
