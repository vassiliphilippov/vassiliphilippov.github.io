pebble

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛb.əl / (AmE), / ˈpɛb.əl / (BrE)
a small, smooth, rounded stone, especially one found on a beach or riverbed
small smooth rounded stone

Examples

The child picked up a shiny pebble from the riverbank.
After skipping the pebble across the lake, it sank quietly to the bottom.
The artist decorated the garden path with smooth white pebbles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stone - a hard, solid nonmetallic mineral matter
  • Degree Higher: boulder - a large rock or stone

Etymology

From Middle English *pebel*, probably of Germanic origin.
