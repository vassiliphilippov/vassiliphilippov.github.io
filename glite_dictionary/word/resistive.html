resistive

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈzɪstɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈzɪstɪv / (BrE)
exhibiting or relating to electrical resistance; offering opposition to the flow of electric current.
showing electrical resistance

Examples

The wire was designed to be highly resistive to control the current flow.
A resistive load converts electrical energy into heat.
Resistive materials are used in the construction of heating elements.

Synset Examples

resistive load

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conductive - allowing electricity to flow easily

Etymology

From 'resist' + '-ive'.

WordNet ID: oewn-03121833-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈzɪstɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈzɪstɪv / (BrE)
showing a tendency to oppose or defy authority or control; resistant or opposed to established rules or leadership
opposing authority; defiant

Examples

The crowd became increasingly resistive as the police tried to disperse them.
Her resistive attitude concerned the teachers during the new policy discussion.
Some employees are naturally resistive to changes in management style.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defiant - openly resisting or challenging authority
  • Antonym: compliant - willing to obey or conform to authority

Etymology

From resist + -ive, after the pattern of adjectives formed from verbs.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00699387-s
Synset POS: s
