mcmansion

1. NOUN

/ məkˈmænʃən / (AmE), / məkˈmænʃən / (BrE)
A large, modern house that is considered bland, mass-produced, and lacking in architectural integrity, often built in suburban developments.
large, mass-produced luxury house

Examples

Many suburban neighborhoods are filled with mcmansions that look almost identical.
He criticized the mcmansion for its lack of character and attention to detail.
The trend of building mcmansions surged in the 1990s and early 2000s.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: mansion - a large, impressive house

Etymology

From 'Mc-' (as in McDonald's, implying mass production) + 'mansion'.
