Hupa

1. NOUN

/ ˈhuːpə / (AmE), / ˈhuːpə / (BrE)
the Athapaskan language spoken by the Hupa people of northwestern California
Athapaskan language of the Hupa people

Examples

Linguists have studied the structure of Hupa for many decades.
She is fluent in both English and Hupa.
Children in the community are encouraged to learn Hupa as part of cultural preservation efforts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Athapaskan - family of languages to which Hupa belongs

Etymology

From Hupa, the self-designation of the people, meaning 'people of the Hoopa Valley'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhuːpə / (AmE), / ˈhuːpə / (BrE)
A member of a Native American people living in the Trinity River valley in northern California.
member of a Native American people of California

Examples

The Hupa maintain many traditional practices that have been passed down for generations.
A noted Hupa leader spoke at the cultural festival last weekend.
Several Hupa artists contributed their work to the museum exhibit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hupa - the language spoken by the Hupa people

Etymology

From the Hupa people's own name for themselves, sometimes rendered as 'Hoopa' due to English spelling conventions.
