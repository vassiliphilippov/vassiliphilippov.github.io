Stenopelmatidae

1. NOUN

/ stɛnoʊˌpɛlməˈtɪdiː / (AmE), / stɛnəʊˌpɛlməˈtɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, cricket-like insects known as long-horned grasshoppers, including the Jerusalem cricket and related species.
a family of long-horned grasshoppers

Examples

The Stenopelmatidae are often found in North and Central America, typically in burrows underground.
Entomologists studied several species within the Stenopelmatidae family to understand their nocturnal behaviors.
A Jerusalem cricket is a member of the insect family known as Stenopelmatidae.

Etymology

From modern Latin Stenopelmatidae, based on the type genus Stenopelmatus.

2. NOUN

/ ˌstɛnoʊˌpɛlˌmætəˈdaɪ / (AmE), / ˌstɛnəʊˌpɛlˌmætɪˈdiː / (BrE)
A family of long-horned grasshoppers, comprising various large, mainly nocturnal insects found in parts of the world, such as Jerusalem crickets and their relatives.
family of long-horned grasshoppers

Examples

Scientists have classified the Jerusalem cricket as a member of the Stenopelmatidae family.
The Stenopelmatidae are noted for their large size and unique mode of communication.
Many species within the Stenopelmatidae are found in arid regions across the Americas.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on type genus Stenopelmatus + the zoological family suffix -idae.
