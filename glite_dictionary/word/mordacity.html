mordacity

1. NOUN

/ mɔrˈdæsəti / (AmE), / mɔːˈdæsəti / (BrE)
a tendency to be biting or sharply sarcastic, especially in speech or manner
biting or sharply sarcastic tendency

Examples

Her mordacity during the debate left even her opponents momentarily speechless.
The critic's mordacity was evident in every sharply worded review he wrote.
He was known for the mordacity of his wit, always quick with a biting remark.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sarcasm - the use of irony to mock or convey contempt
  • Synonym: bitterness - harshness or acrimony in attitude or tone

Etymology

From Latin mordacitas (proneness to bite), from mordax (biting), from mordere (to bite).
