worshipper

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɜrʃɪpər / (AmE), / ˈwɜːʃɪpə / (BrE)
a person who admires someone or something excessively, often unable to see their faults
person who admires excessively

Examples

As a devoted worshipper of her favorite author, she never saw any flaws in his work.
His friends accused him of being a blind worshipper who refused to acknowledge the leader's mistakes.
You shouldn't be a worshipper of celebrities—everyone has faults.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: admirer - a person who admires someone or something
  • Antonym: critic - a person who points out faults or deficiencies

Etymology

From Middle English 'worschiper', based on 'worship' + '-er'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɜrʃɪpər / (AmE), / ˈwɜːʃɪpə / (BrE)
a person who takes part in religious worship or shows reverence for a deity or divine being
person who participates in religious worship

Examples

The temple was filled with worshippers from early morning until late at night.
Many worshippers gather here every Sunday to pray together.
As a lifelong worshipper, she attends services and observes all important ceremonies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: believer - a person who believes in a particular religion or faith

Etymology

From Middle English 'worshipe' (worship) + '-er' (one who does); ultimately from Old English 'weorðscipe'.
