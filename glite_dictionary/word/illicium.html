Illicium

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɪsiəm / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪsɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen trees and shrubs with aromatic leaves, including the star anise, native to East Asia and North America.
genus of aromatic evergreen trees and shrubs

Examples

The dried fruits of the Illicium tree are often used as a spice in Asian cuisine.
Botanists discovered a rare species of Illicium in the mountainous region.
The gardener planted several Illicium shrubs for their fragrant leaves and attractive flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: star anise - A spice made from the fruit of Illicium verum

Etymology

From Latin *illicium* (allurement), referring to the plant's fragrant properties.
