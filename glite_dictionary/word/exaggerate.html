to exaggerate

1. VERB

/ ɪɡˈzædʒəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzadʒəreɪt / (BrE)
to do something to an excessive or extreme degree
do something excessively

Examples

He tends to exaggerate his workout routine and ends up feeling exhausted.
If you exaggerate your efforts, you might injure yourself.
Don't exaggerate the decorations; sometimes less is more.

Synset Examples

He overdid it last night when he did 100 pushups

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overdo - to do something to an excessive degree; to do too much

Etymology

From Latin exaggerare, meaning 'to heap up' or 'enlarge.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02574914-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪɡˈzædʒəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzadʒəreɪt / (BrE)
to represent something as being larger, better, or worse than it really is
to overstate; to magnify beyond truth

Examples

He tends to exaggerate his skills on his resume.
Reporters sometimes exaggerate the importance of minor events.
She admitted she had exaggerated the size of the crowd.

Synset Examples

tended to romanticize and exaggerate this ‘gracious Old South’ imagery

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to overstate - to state something in too strong terms; exaggerate
  • Antonym: to understate - to describe something as less important than it really is

Etymology

From Latin exaggerare (to heap up, enlarge), from ex- (out) + agger (heap).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00841513-v
Synset POS: v
