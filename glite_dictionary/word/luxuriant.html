luxuriant

1. ADJ

/ lʌgˈʒɝiənt / (AmE), / lʌgˈʒʊəriənt / (BrE)
Rich and elaborate in detail or complexity, often producing a strong visual or sensory impression.
rich and detailed; elaborate

Examples

The palace was filled with luxuriant tapestries and gilded ornaments.
Her wedding dress featured a luxuriant lace pattern that caught everyone's eye.
The artist's luxuriant illustrations added depth and elegance to the book.

Synset Examples

an elaborate lace pattern

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elaborate - carefully planned or detailed; complicated in design
  • Synonym: ornate - highly decorated with complex patterns

Etymology

From Latin luxurians, luxuriant-, present participle of luxuriari (to be luxurious).

2. ADJ

/ lʌgˈʒʊriənt / (AmE), / lʌgˈzjʊəriənt / (BrE)
richly and profusely luxurious; displaying great luxury and providing sensuous pleasure or gratification.
profusely luxurious, giving sensuous pleasure

Examples

The guests were amazed at the luxuriant banquet set before them, filled with delicacies from around the world.
She prefers luxuriant fabrics, like silk or velvet, that please both the eye and touch.
The royal palace was adorned with luxuriant decorations, making every room opulent and inviting.

Synset Examples

an epicurean banquet
enjoyed a luxurious suite with a crystal chandelier and thick oriental rugs
Lucullus spent the remainder of his days in voluptuous magnificence
a chinchilla robe of sybaritic lavishness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: luxurious - rich and comfortable; characterized by luxury

Etymology

From Latin luxurian-, luxurians, present participle of luxuriari (to be luxuriant, indulge).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01301187-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ lʌgˈʒʊriənt / (AmE), / lʌgˈzjʊəriənt / (BrE)
(of plants or hair) growing thickly and abundantly; lush and rich in growth.
rich and abundant growth

Examples

The garden was filled with luxuriant ferns and vibrant flowering shrubs.
She admired the luxuriant hair that fell in waves down his back.
After the heavy rains, the hillside became covered in luxuriant green grasses.

Synset Examples

their riotous blooming

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lush - (of plant growth) rich and profuse; luxuriant
  • Antonym: sparse - thinly dispersed or scattered; the opposite of luxuriant for growth

Etymology

From Latin luxurians, luxuriant- (growing exuberantly), from luxuriari (to be luxuriant).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00015284-s
Synset POS: s
