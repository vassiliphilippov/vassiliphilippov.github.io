inpatient

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪnˌpeɪʃənt / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌpeɪʃənt / (BrE)
a patient who stays in a hospital while receiving treatment or care
patient who stays in hospital

Examples

The hospital has over fifty inpatients being treated for various conditions at any given time.
After her surgery, she was admitted as an inpatient for several days of monitoring.
Some procedures require the patient to be an inpatient rather than being treated and sent home the same day.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: outpatient - a patient who receives treatment without staying in hospital

Etymology

Formed from 'in' + 'patient'; by analogy to 'outpatient'.
