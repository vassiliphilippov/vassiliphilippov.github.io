ephedrine

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈfɛdrɪn, əˈfɛdrin / (AmE), / ɪˈfiːdrɪn, əˈfiːdrɪn / (BrE)
a white, odorless, powdered or crystalline alkaloid obtained from plants of the genus Ephedra or manufactured synthetically, used as a bronchodilator and stimulant, especially to treat asthma and bronchitis
stimulant and bronchodilator alkaloid
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed ephedrine to help manage his asthma symptoms.
Some cold medications used to contain ephedrine as a decongestant.
Ephedrine can increase heart rate and blood pressure if misused.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pseudoephedrine - a related stimulant and decongestant alkaloid

Etymology

From the genus name Ephedra, with the chemical suffix -ine.
