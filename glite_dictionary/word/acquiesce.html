to acquiesce

1. VERB

/ ˌækwiˈɛs / (AmE), / ˌækwiˈɛs / (BrE)
to accept or agree to something reluctantly but without protest
to agree or accept without protest

Examples

Although he had some reservations, John chose to acquiesce to the committee's decision.
The manager refused to acquiesce to demands for shorter working hours.
She quietly acquiesced as the plan took shape around her.

Synset Examples

The Maestro assented to the request for an encore

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assent - to agree or express approval
  • Antonym: dissent - to hold or express opinions that are at variance with those previously, commonly, or officially held

Etymology

From Latin 'acquiescere', meaning 'to rest, to become quiet'.
