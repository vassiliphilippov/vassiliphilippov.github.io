dissection

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈsɛkʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛkʃən / (BrE)
the act of cutting something apart in order to study its internal parts, especially in a scientific or medical context
cutting apart for study

Examples

The biology students performed a dissection of a frog to examine its internal organs.
Careful dissection of the heart revealed signs of disease.
The article provided a detailed dissection of the proposed law's weaknesses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anatomy - study of the structure of living things

Etymology

From Latin dissectio, from dissecare (to cut apart), from dis- (apart) + secare (to cut).

2. NOUN

/ daɪˈsɛkʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛkʃən / (BrE)
A detailed and critical examination or analysis of something, especially in order to understand its structure or meaning.
thorough analytical examination

Examples

The film offers a brilliant dissection of social inequalities in modern urban life.
Her essay provides a careful dissection of the author's use of irony and symbolism.
During the seminar, the professor led a systematic dissection of the case study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: analysis - detailed examination of the elements or structure of something

Etymology

From Latin dissectio (a cutting apart), from dissecare (to cut apart).

3. NOUN

A detailed and systematic analysis or examination of something, especially by separating it into parts for study or criticism.
detailed, critical analysis

Examples

The professor’s dissection of the novel revealed its underlying themes and symbolism.
Her dissection of the contract uncovered several hidden clauses that were unfavorable to the client.
The article offers a thorough dissection of the company’s financial performance over the last decade.
