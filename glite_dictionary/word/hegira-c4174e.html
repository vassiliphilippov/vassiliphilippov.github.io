hegira

1. NOUN

/ hɪˈdʒaɪrə / (AmE), / hɪˈdʒaɪrə / (BrE)
A journey or migration, especially one made to escape from a dangerous, hostile, or undesirable situation.
mass migration to escape danger

Examples

Thousands began a hegira to neighboring countries to escape the ongoing conflict.
The company’s collapse triggered a hegira of employees seeking better opportunities elsewhere.
After the storm, there was a hegira from the coastal towns to safer inland areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exodus - a mass departure of people

Etymology

From Arabic *hijra* (departure), originally referring to the Prophet Muhammad’s flight from Mecca to Medina.
