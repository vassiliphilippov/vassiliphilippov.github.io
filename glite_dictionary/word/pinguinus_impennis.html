Pinguinus impennis

1. NOUN

/ pɪŋˈɡwaɪnəs ɪmˈpɛnɪs / (AmE), / pɪŋˈɡwɪn.əs ɪmˈpɛn.ɪs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the great auk, a large flightless seabird that lived on islands in the North Atlantic and became extinct in the 19th century
extinct large flightless auk (great auk)

Examples

The extinction of Pinguinus impennis serves as a stark reminder of human impact on wildlife.
Scientists have studied the bones of Pinguinus impennis to learn more about its behavior and habitat.
Once common on rocky North Atlantic islands, Pinguinus impennis disappeared by the mid-1800s.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: great auk - the common English name for Pinguinus impennis, an extinct large flightless seabird

Etymology

From Latin: Pinguinus (genus name) and impennis ('without wings').
