dysplastic

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈplæstɪk / (AmE), / dɪsˈplæstɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or showing abnormal growth or development of cells, tissues, or organs, as seen in dysplasia.
showing abnormal cell or tissue growth
medical

Examples

The biopsy revealed a dysplastic lesion that required further observation.
Some moles can become dysplastic, increasing the risk of skin cancer.
Radiologists are trained to identify dysplastic changes in mammograms.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: normal - not showing abnormal growth or development
  • Synonym: dysplasia - abnormal development or growth of cells, tissues, or organs

Etymology

From dysplasia + -ic, meaning relating to or affected by dysplasia.
