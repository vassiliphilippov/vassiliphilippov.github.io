Eumops

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuːmɑps / (AmE), / ˈjuːmɒps / (BrE)
A genus of large bats known as mastiff bats, found in the Americas.
a genus of mastiff bats

Examples

Researchers studied the flight patterns of Eumops in the rainforests of South America.
The Eumops genus includes some of the fastest flying bats in the world.
Unlike most bats, Eumops typically prefer to roost in tall trees or man-made structures.

Etymology

From Greek 'eu-' meaning 'good, true' and 'mōps' meaning 'snub-nosed'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈjuː.mɑps / (AmE), / ˈjuː.mɒps / (BrE)
A genus of bats in the family Molossidae, commonly known as mastiff bats, found in the Americas.
a genus of mastiff bats

Examples

The Eumops bats are known for their large size among the free-tailed bats.
Researchers have observed Eumops species hunting insects at high altitudes.
Several species within the Eumops genus inhabit forests and savannas across South America.

Etymology

From Greek eu (good, true) + mops (a type of bat).
