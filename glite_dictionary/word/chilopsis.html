Chilopsis

1. NOUN

/ kɪˈlɑpsɪs / (AmE), / kɪˈlɒpsɪs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering shrub or small tree, commonly known as the desert willow, native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.
a genus of desert willow shrubs or trees

Examples

The Chilopsis is often planted in arid gardens because it tolerates drought well.
When blooming, Chilopsis produces delicate, trumpet-shaped flowers that attract hummingbirds.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Chilopsis in desert environments.

Etymology

From Greek 'cheilos' (lip) and 'opsis' (appearance), referring to the flower's shape.

The Chilopsis thrives in arid desert regions, showing remarkable drought resistance.
Gardeners in the Southwest often plant Chilopsis for its ornamental value and beautiful flowers.
The Chilopsis, or desert willow, attracts hummingbirds with its trumpet-shaped blossoms.
