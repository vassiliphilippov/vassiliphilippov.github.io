reversible

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈvɜrsəbəl / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːsəbəl / (BrE)
Able to be changed back to a previous condition, state, or position.
able to be changed back

Examples

Fortunately, the surgery is reversible if complications arise.
The company took a reversible action, allowing them to return to their original plan.
Her decision was reversible, so she changed her mind later on.

Synset Examples

a reversible decision is one that can be appealed or vacated

Related Terms

  • Antonym: irreversible - not able to be reversed
  • Synonym: undoable - able to be undone or reversed

Etymology

From late Latin reversibilis, from Latin revertere 'to turn back'.
