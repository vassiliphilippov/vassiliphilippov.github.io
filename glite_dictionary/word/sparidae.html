Sparidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɛr.ɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈspɑː.rɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine fishes, commonly known as porgies or scups, characterized by laterally compressed bodies and strong teeth.
family of marine fishes (porgies, scups)

Examples

Many Mediterranean cuisines feature grilled Sparidae species as a delicacy.
The family Sparidae includes both porgies and scups, known for their robust jaws.
Marine biologists are studying the habitat ranges of Sparidae around coral reefs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porgy - a common name for fish in the Sparidae family
  • Synonym: scup - another common name for fish in the Sparidae family

Etymology

From New Latin Sparidae, from Greek sparos (a kind of fish).
