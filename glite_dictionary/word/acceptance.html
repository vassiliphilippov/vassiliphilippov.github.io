acceptance

1. NOUN

/ əkˈsɛptəns / (AmE), / əkˈsɛptəns / (BrE)
A time draft that has been drawn on and accepted by a bank, signifying the bank's agreement to pay the draft at maturity.
bank-accepted time draft

Examples

The exporter received an acceptance from the buyer's bank to secure payment.
Financial institutions often use an acceptance as a negotiable instrument in international trade.
The company discounted the acceptance at its local bank to obtain immediate funds.
