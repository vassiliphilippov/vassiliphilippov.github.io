Tyrolean

1. ADJ

/ taɪˈroʊliən / (AmE), / taɪˈrəʊliən / (BrE)
of or relating to Tyrol, a region in the Alps, or its people, culture, or traditions
relating to Tyrol or its people

Examples

The band performed traditional Tyrolean music during the festival.
He wore a distinctive Tyrolean hat decorated with a feather.
The restaurant serves authentic Tyrolean dishes from the Alpine region.

Synset Examples

Tyrolean yodeling

Etymology

From Tyrol, a region in the Alps (now partly in Austria and Italy), with -ean suffix denoting origin or relation.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02982552-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ taɪˈroʊliən / (AmE), / taɪˈrəʊliən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of the Tyrol, a historical region in the Alps of western Austria and northern Italy
native or inhabitant of Tyrol

Examples

The Tyrolean welcomed us to their mountain village with traditional music and food.
Many Tyroleans are proud of their rich Alpine heritage and local customs.
She studied the history of the Tyrolean people and their unique culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Austrian - a native or citizen of Austria

Etymology

From Tyrol, the name of the Alpine region, plus English -ean.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09754455-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ taɪˈroʊliən / (AmE), / tɪˈrəʊliən / (BrE)
a soft green felt hat, often with a feather or brush, traditionally worn in the Tyrol region of the Alps
soft green Alpine hat, often with feather

Examples

He wore a traditional Tyrolean to the folk music festival.
The green Tyrolean on the rack caught her eye immediately.
Tourists often buy a Tyrolean as a souvenir from Austria.

Etymology

From Tyrol, a region in the Alps, with the suffix -ean indicating origin.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
