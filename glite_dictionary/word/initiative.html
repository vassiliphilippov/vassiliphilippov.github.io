initiative

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈnɪʃətɪv / (AmE), / ɪˈnɪʃətɪv / (BrE)
the first step or action in a series that sets something in motion
first step in a process

Examples

The organization launched a new initiative to promote sustainable farming in the region.
Her suggestion was the initiative that spurred a series of important reforms.
Investment in education was the government's first initiative after the election.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: start - the beginning of something

Etymology

From French initiative, from Latin initiātiō, meaning 'a beginning'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00240097-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈnɪʃətɪv / (AmE), / ɪˈnɪʃətɪv / (BrE)
the ability to assess and initiate things independently; readiness to embark on new or bold actions
readiness to begin new ventures or take action

Examples

She showed great initiative in organizing the community event.
Employers value workers who demonstrate initiative and creativity in problem-solving.
Taking the initiative can sometimes lead to unexpected opportunities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enterprise - readiness to undertake daring projects
  • Antonym: passivity - lack of initiative or action

Etymology

From French 'initiative', from Latin 'initiare' (to begin).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04843305-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ɪˈnɪʃətɪv / (AmE), / ɪˈnɪʃətɪv / (BrE)
Having the purpose or quality of setting something in motion; introductory or leading to a beginning.
serving to set in motion; introductory

Examples

The company entered the initiative stage of its ambitious expansion plans last year.
Negotiators struggled to agree on the initiative phase before moving to substantive issues.
In technology projects, an initiative effort is often needed to launch new development.

Synset Examples

the magazine's inaugural issue
the initiative phase in the negotiations
an initiatory step toward a treaty
his first (or maiden) speech in Congress
the liner's maiden voyage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inaugural - marking the beginning of something
  • Antonym: concluding - serving to bring something to an end

Etymology

From Latin initiātīvus, from initium (beginning).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01012940-s
Synset POS: s
