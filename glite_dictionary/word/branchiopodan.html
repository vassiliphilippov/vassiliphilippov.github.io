branchiopodan

1. NOUN

/ bræn.kiˈɑː.pə.dən / (AmE), / bræŋ.kɪˈɒp.ə.dən / (BrE)
A small aquatic crustacean typically having a carapace and numerous leaflike appendages used for swimming, breathing, and feeding; a member of the class Branchiopoda.
type of small aquatic crustacean

Examples

Many branchiopodans thrive in temporary freshwater pools, where they use their leaflike limbs to swim and breathe.
Scientists discovered a new species of branchiopodan in the isolated desert lake.
Some branchiopodans play a vital role in freshwater ecosystems by serving as food for fish and birds.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: crustacean - aquatic arthropod with a hard shell, such as crabs and shrimp

Etymology

From New Latin Branchiopoda, from Greek branchia (gills) + pous (foot).
