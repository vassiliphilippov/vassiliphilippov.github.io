to affiance

1. VERB

/ əˈfaɪəns / (AmE), / əˈfaɪəns / (BrE)
to betroth or formally promise in marriage; to give in marriage
to betroth or promise in marriage
archaic

Examples

The nobleman decided to affiance his daughter to a wealthy merchant.
In medieval times, families would often affiance their children at a young age.
She felt uncertain about being affianced to a man she barely knew.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to betroth - to promise to marry; formal engagement
  • Degree Higher: to marry - to join in marriage; to wed

Etymology

From Old French 'afiancer', from Latin 'affidare' (to trust).
