activator

1. NOUN

/ ˈæktɪˌveɪtɚ / (AmE), / ˈæktɪveɪtə / (BrE)
(Biology) A substance or molecule that increases the activity of an enzyme or stimulates the production of a gene product during DNA transcription.
substance that increases biological activity
medical

Examples

The presence of an activator can speed up the enzyme's reaction.
Researchers identified a new activator that increases gene expression.
Without the activator, the protein remains inactive in these cells.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: inhibitor - a substance that slows down or prevents a particular chemical reaction or other process

Etymology

From activate + -or (agent noun suffix).
