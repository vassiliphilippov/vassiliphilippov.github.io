carcinogen

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːrsənədʒən / (AmE), / kɑːˈsɪnədʒən / (BrE)
A substance capable of causing cancer in living tissue.
cancer-causing substance
medical

Examples

Exposure to even small amounts of this carcinogen can increase the risk of developing lung cancer.
Researchers are working to identify new industrial chemicals that act as carcinogens.
The label warns consumers that the product contains a known carcinogen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mutagen - an agent that causes genetic mutation
  • Synonym: teratogen - an agent that causes malformation of an embryo

Etymology

From Greek karkinos (cancer) + -gen (producing or causing).
