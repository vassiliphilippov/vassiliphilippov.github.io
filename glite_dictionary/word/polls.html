polls

1. NOUN

/ poʊlz / (AmE), / pəʊlz / (BrE)
the place where people go to cast their vote in an election
place to cast votes in an election

Examples

Voters lined up early at the polls to cast their ballots before work.
The polls will close at 8 p.m., so make sure to arrive on time.
Heavy rain didn't stop citizens from going to the polls on election day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polling station - official place where voting takes place

Etymology

From Middle English 'polle' (head), later used for ‘counting heads’ at elections, then extended to voting places.
