Mollusca

1. NOUN

/ məˈlʌskə / (AmE), / məˈlʌskə / (BrE)
A large phylum of invertebrate animals that includes snails, clams, squids, and octopuses, characterized by a soft unsegmented body, often enclosed in a calcareous shell.
phylum of soft-bodied invertebrate animals

Examples

The phylum Mollusca includes a wide variety of species, such as snails and octopuses.
Scientists discovered a new species within Mollusca while studying deep-sea habitats.
The anatomy of Mollusca is notable for its soft body and, in many cases, a protective shell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mollusk - any animal of the phylum Mollusca

Etymology

From Latin *molluscus* (soft), referring to their soft bodies.

2. NOUN

/ moʊˈlʌskə / (AmE), / məˈlʌskə / (BrE)
A large phylum of invertebrate animals that includes gastropods (such as snails and slugs), bivalves (such as clams and oysters), cephalopods (such as squids and octopuses), and chitons.
phylum of invertebrate animals (molluscs)

Examples

The phylum Mollusca includes well-known animals like snails, clams, and octopuses.
Scientists estimate there are over 85,000 species within the Mollusca.
Members of the Mollusca are found in nearly all aquatic and some terrestrial environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mollusc - an invertebrate animal of the phylum Mollusca

Etymology

From Latin 'molluscus' (soft), referring to their soft bodies.
