Gonorhynchus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡoʊnəˈrɪŋkəs / (AmE), / ˌɡɒnəˈrɪŋkəs / (BrE)
a genus of slender, cylindrical marine fishes lacking air bladders and teeth, notable for their elongated bodies
genus of slender marine fish

Examples

The Gonorhynchus is distinguished by its absence of an air bladder and its toothless mouth.
Marine biologists often study Gonorhynchus to understand adaptations to deep-sea environments.
Unlike many other fish, a Gonorhynchus swims close to the sea floor due to its body structure.

Etymology

From Greek 'gonos' (seed, offspring) + 'rhynchos' (snout), referring to the fish's elongated shape.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɡoʊ.nɔːˈrɪŋ.kəs / (AmE), / ˌɡɒ.nəˈrɪŋ.kəs / (BrE)
A genus of slender, cylindrical marine fishes that lack air bladders and teeth.
genus of toothless, air bladder-less marine fish

Examples

The Gonorhynchus is notable for lacking both teeth and an air bladder, making it unique among marine fishes.
Researchers collected several specimens of Gonorhynchus for the marine biology study.
Fishermen rarely encounter Gonorhynchus, as it prefers deep, offshore waters.

Etymology

From Greek gónos (seed, offspring) + rhynchos (snout), likely referring to the fish's shape.
