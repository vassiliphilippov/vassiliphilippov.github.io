puffing

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌfɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpʌfɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of blowing out smoke, especially from a cigarette, cigar, or pipe
blowing smoke from tobacco

Examples

She sat by the window, quietly puffing as the room filled with smoke.
Their endless puffing soon made the air thick and hazy.
His constant puffing annoyed the non-smokers nearby.

Synset Examples

they smoked up the room with their ceaseless puffing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smoking - the act of inhaling and exhaling tobacco smoke

Etymology

From the verb 'puff', likely from Middle English 'pouffen', imitative of a short blast of air.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpʌfɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpʌfɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of exhaling air forcefully from the mouth or nose
an act of forceful exhalation

Examples

After several puffing breaths, the runner finally reached the finish line.
The child's puffing blew the dandelion seeds into the air.
His puffing was audible as he climbed the steep stairs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exhalation - the process of breathing out air
  • Antonym: inhalation - the process of breathing in air

Etymology

From the verb 'puff', which comes from Middle English 'pouffe', probably of imitative origin.
