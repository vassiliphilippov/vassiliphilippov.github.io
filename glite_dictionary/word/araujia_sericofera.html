Araujia sericofera

1. NOUN

/ əˈraʊdʒiə sɛˌrikoʊˈfɛrə / (AmE), / əˈraʊdʒɪə sɛˌrɪkəʊˈfɪərə / (BrE)
a robust climbing shrub with fragrant white or pink flowers that trap nocturnal moths, native to South America
species of climbing shrub with moth-trapping flowers

Examples

Araujia sericofera is sometimes called the moth plant because its flowers can trap insects overnight.
Gardeners in temperate regions occasionally cultivate Araujia sericofera for its attractive, fragrant blossoms.
The twining vines of Araujia sericofera can quickly cover fences or trellises.

Etymology

From Latinized botanical names: genus Araujia, commemorating Portuguese botanist António de Araújo e Azevedo; species name from Latin 'sericeus' (silky) and '-fera' (bearing).
