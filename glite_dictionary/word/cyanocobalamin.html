cyanocobalamin

1. NOUN

/ saɪˌænəkoʊˈbæləˌmɪn / (AmE), / saɪˌanəʊkəʊˈbæləˌmɪn / (BrE)
a synthetic form of vitamin B12 used to treat vitamin B12 deficiency, particularly pernicious anemia
a synthetic form of vitamin B12
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed cyanocobalamin injections to treat her deficiency.
Vegetarians sometimes need cyanocobalamin supplements to maintain proper vitamin B12 levels.
Cyanocobalamin is often included in multivitamin tablets to support overall health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vitamin B12 - an essential nutrient important for nerve function and blood cell production

Etymology

From 'cyano-' (relating to cyanide group) + 'cobalamin' (vitamin B12 compound containing cobalt).
