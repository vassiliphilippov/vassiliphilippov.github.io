Agrostis alba

1. PROPN

/ əˈɡrɑstɪs ˈælbə / (AmE), / əˈɡrɒstɪs ˈalbə / (BrE)
A slender European grass, also known as redtop, found in shady areas and cultivated in northeastern America and temperate Asia.
a species of slender European grass (redtop)

Examples

Agrostis alba is commonly used in lawns and pastures due to its hardiness.
Botanists studied how Agrostis alba adapted to the colder climates of northeastern America.
The shady woodland featured a dense patch of Agrostis alba thriving beneath the trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redtop - common name for Agrostis alba, a type of grass

Etymology

From Greek 'agrōstis' (a kind of grass) and Latin 'alba' (white), referring to characteristics of the plant.
