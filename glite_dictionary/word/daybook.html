daybook

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪ.bʊk / (AmE), / ˈdeɪ.bʊk / (BrE)
a book in which daily financial transactions or business activities are recorded, especially before posting to official ledgers
accounting journal for daily transactions

Examples

The accountant made sure every sale was written in the daybook before the day ended.
She glanced at the old daybook to check last month's entries.
After correcting an error, he updated the daybook to reflect the new figures.

Synset Examples

he bought a new daybook

Related Terms

  • Synonym: journal - a book for recording financial transactions in chronological order

Etymology

From 'day' + 'book', indicating a daily record.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪ.bʊk / (AmE), / ˈdeɪ.bʊk / (BrE)
a book in which financial transactions are recorded daily, especially before they are categorized in the main ledger
book for recording daily transactions

Examples

The accountant entered each sale into the daybook at the close of business.
Before the ledger is updated, all transactions are first noted in the daybook.
She reviewed the previous week's entries in the daybook to ensure accuracy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: journal - a daily record of financial transactions in accounting

Etymology

From 'day' + 'book', indicating a book for daily entries.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13426339-n
Synset POS: n
