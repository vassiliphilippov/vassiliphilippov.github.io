stannic

1. ADJ

/ stæˈnɪk / (AmE), / stəˈnɪk / (BrE)
of or relating to tin, especially tin with a valency of four in its compounds
relating to tin; especially with valency four

Examples

The laboratory synthesized stannic chloride for use in chemical reactions.
Oxidation of tin in air can produce stannic oxide, also known as tin(IV) oxide.
Researchers studied the properties of stannic compounds to better understand their applications in industry.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: stannous - relating to divalent tin (tin with a valency of two)

Etymology

From Latin 'stannum' (tin), with the suffix '-ic' denoting a higher oxidation state.
