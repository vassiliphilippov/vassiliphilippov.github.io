Terrapene

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛrəˈpiːni / (AmE), / ˌtɛrəˈpiːni / (BrE)
A genus of North American box turtles, known for their domed shells and ability to close their shells tightly.
genus of North American box turtles

Examples

Researchers studied the habitat of Terrapene in several regions of the Midwest.
The Terrapene are known for their unique hinged shells, which offer extra protection from predators.
Due to habitat loss, some species within the genus Terrapene are now considered vulnerable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: box turtle - A North American turtle with a domed shell and hinged plastron

Etymology

From Modern Latin, coined as a genus name, possibly from Algonquian origin via 'terrapin'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌtɛrəˈpiːni / (AmE), / ˌtɛrəˈpiːni / (BrE)
A genus of North American box turtles characterized by a domed shell and hinged plastron that allow the animal to close its shell tightly.
genus of North American box turtles

Examples

The eastern box turtle, a member of the genus Terrapene, is commonly found in forests and meadows.
Researchers studied several species within the genus Terrapene to understand their unique shell structure.
Conservation efforts are underway to protect different species in the genus Terrapene from habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: box turtle - a turtle of the genus Terrapene, with a domed shell and hinged plastron

Etymology

From Latin, based on terra (earth) + pene (almost), referring to their terrestrial lifestyle.
