salutation

1. NOUN

/ ˌsæl.jəˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsæl.jʊˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A gesture or statement made to acknowledge someone’s arrival or departure, or to express goodwill, especially as a formal greeting.
formal greeting or gesture of goodwill

Examples

She offered a warm salutation as the guests entered the room.
The letter began with the traditional salutation 'Dear Sir or Madam.'
On entering the meeting, he gave a polite salutation to everyone present.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greeting - a polite word or sign of welcome or recognition
  • Antonym: farewell - a word or gesture used when parting

Etymology

From Latin salutatio (a greeting), from salutare (to greet, wish health).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsæləˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsaljʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a word or phrase used as a greeting at the beginning of a letter or speech
greeting at start of letter

Examples

The most common salutation in business letters is 'Dear Sir or Madam.'
She started the letter with a warm salutation before moving to the main message.
Please make sure to use an appropriate salutation when writing formal letters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greeting - a word or gesture of welcome or salutation

Etymology

From Latin 'salutatio' (a greeting), from 'salutare' (to greet).

3. NOUN

/ ˌsæl.jəˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsæl.jʊˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
An act of showing honor, respect, or courteous recognition, especially as a gesture or tribute.
an act of honor or respectful greeting

Examples

The conductor offered a salutation to the audience in honor of the composer's birthday.
A standing ovation is often given as a salutation to outstanding performers.
Their tribute began with a heartfelt salutation to the retiring professor.

Synset Examples

a musical salute to the composer on his birthday

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greeting - an expression of friendly or polite recognition or welcome
  • Antonym: farewell - an act of saying goodbye, often as a respectful recognition

Etymology

From Latin salutatio (a greeting, salutation), from salutare (to greet).
