Ipomoea alba

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪ.pəˈmiː.ə ˈæl.bə / (AmE), / ˌɪ.pəˈməʊ.iə ˈal.bə / (BrE)
A tropical climbing plant with large, fragrant white flowers that bloom at night, commonly known as the moonflower.
tropical climbing plant with white nocturnal flowers

Examples

The fragrant blooms of the Ipomoea alba open at dusk and attract nighttime pollinators.
Gardeners often plant Ipomoea alba on trellises to enjoy its large white flowers during summer evenings.
As twilight falls, the Ipomoea alba transforms the garden with its glowing blossoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moonflower - common name for Ipomoea alba, a night-blooming plant

Etymology

From Latin: 'Ipomoea' from Greek 'ips' (worm) and 'homoios' (resembling), referring to its twining habit; 'alba' meaning 'white' in Latin.
