camlet

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæmlət / (AmE), / ˈkamlɪt / (BrE)
A garment made from camlet, a luxurious fabric typically of silk and camel hair or goat hair, popular in the past.
garment made of camlet fabric
archaic

Examples

The wealthy merchant wore a flowing camlet trimmed with gold thread.
Her grandmother owned an old camlet that had been passed down for generations.
In the portrait, the nobleman is depicted in a richly colored camlet.

Etymology

From Old French 'camelot', possibly from Arabic 'khamlat' (pile fabric) or related to 'camel' (as camel hair was sometimes used).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkæmlət / (AmE), / ˈkæmlɪt / (BrE)
a rich woven fabric, originally made of silk and camel's hair, later sometimes wool or a blend, used for garments and upholstery
rich woven fabric (silk/camel hair)
archaic

Examples

The merchant displayed a bolt of fine camlet imported from the East.
In the portrait, the nobleman wore a coat made of deep green camlet.
During the 17th century, camlet was highly prized for its smooth texture and durability.

Etymology

From Old French 'camelot', perhaps from Arabic 'khamlat', meaning 'pile of cloth'.
