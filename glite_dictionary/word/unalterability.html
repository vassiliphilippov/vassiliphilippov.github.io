unalterability

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˌɔltɚəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ʌnˌɔːltərəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being impossible to change or alter.
state of being unchangeable

Examples

The unalterability of the contract ensures that the terms cannot be changed without agreement from all parties.
Philosophers have debated the unalterability of certain natural laws for centuries.
He admired the unalterability of her principles, even under pressure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immutability - the quality of being unchanging over time or unable to be changed
  • Antonym: alterability - the quality of being able to be changed

Etymology

From un- (not) + alter (to change) + -ability (capacity for).
