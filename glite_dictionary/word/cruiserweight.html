cruiserweight

1. NOUN

/ ˈkruːzɚˌweɪt / (AmE), / ˈkruːzəˌweɪt / (BrE)
A weight class in combat sports, especially boxing, for competitors heavier than middleweight but lighter than heavyweight or super heavyweight.
weight class between middleweight and heavyweight

Examples

The cruiserweight champion successfully defended his title last night.
After moving up from light heavyweight, she now fights in the cruiserweight division.
Many consider cruiserweight the most competitive class in professional boxing.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: middleweight - a lighter weight class below cruiserweight
  • Degree Higher: heavyweight - a heavier weight class above cruiserweight

Etymology

From cruiser + weight, patterning after other weight classes in boxing.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkruːzərˌweɪt / (AmE), / ˈkruːzəˌweɪt / (BrE)
a professional boxing weight class for boxers who weigh between 169 and 175 pounds (77 to 79.4 kilograms)
a boxing weight class (169–175 lbs)

Examples

The champion successfully defended his cruiserweight title in a thrilling twelve-round bout.
Several cruiserweight contenders are vying for a shot at the world title next year.
He decided to move up to cruiserweight after dominating the light heavyweight division.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: light heavyweight - a boxing weight class below cruiserweight (168–175 lbs)
  • Degree Higher: heavyweight - a boxing weight class above cruiserweight (over 200 lbs)

Etymology

From 'cruiser' (as in a naval vessel of moderate weight) and 'weight', by analogy with other boxing classifications like 'heavyweight'.
