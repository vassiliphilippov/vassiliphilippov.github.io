phenelzine

1. NOUN

/ fəˈnɛlˌzin / (AmE), / fəˈnɛl.ziːn / (BrE)
A monoamine oxidase inhibitor used as an antidepressant, especially to treat clinical depression; trade name Nardil.
antidepressant medication (monoamine oxidase inhibitor)
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed phenelzine to help manage her severe depression.
Patients taking phenelzine must avoid certain foods to prevent dangerous interactions.
Some individuals experience side effects when starting phenelzine for the first time.
