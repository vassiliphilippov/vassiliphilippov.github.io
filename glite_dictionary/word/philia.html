philia

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪliə / (AmE), / ˈfɪlɪə / (BrE)
a strong feeling of love, friendship, or affection, especially used to describe a particular type or form of love in philosophical or psychological contexts
type of love or affection

Examples

His deep philia for his friends was evident in everything he did.
In ancient philosophy, philia referred to the bond of friendship.
The term philia is often used to classify different kinds of love or preference.

Synset Examples

he had trouble expressing the affection he felt
the child won everyone's heart
the warmness of his welcome made us feel right at home

Related Terms

  • Synonym: affection - a gentle feeling of fondness or liking
  • Antonym: phobia - an extreme or irrational fear of something; the opposite of -philia as a suffix

Etymology

From Ancient Greek φιλία (philia) meaning 'friendship, affection, love'.
