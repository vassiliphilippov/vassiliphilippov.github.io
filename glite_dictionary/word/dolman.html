dolman

1. NOUN

/ ˈdoʊlmən / (AmE), / ˈdɒlmən / (BrE)
A close-fitting, short jacket with decorative braiding, traditionally worn over the shoulders by hussars.
a hussar's decorated short jacket

Examples

The officer's dolman was richly decorated with gold braid and frogging.
He draped his dolman over his shoulders before mounting his horse.
Antique military uniforms often include the distinctive dolman worn by cavalrymen.

Etymology

From Turkish 'dolaman' (a robe or cloak).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdoʊlmən / (AmE), / ˈdɒlmən / (BrE)
a woman's loose cloak with wide, cape-like sleeves that are set into the body of the garment without a traditional armhole seam.
a woman's cloak with wide sleeves

Examples

The antique shop displayed a beautifully embroidered dolman from the Victorian era.
She draped her dolman over her shoulders before stepping outside.
The fashion historian gave a presentation on the evolution of the dolman in women's outerwear.

Etymology

From Turkish 'dolaman', meaning a long robe.
