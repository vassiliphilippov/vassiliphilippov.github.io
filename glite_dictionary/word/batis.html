Batis

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˈbeɪtɪs / (BrE)
A small genus of low, straggling, dioecious shrubs forming the family Batidaceae, commonly found in coastal and saltmarsh environments.
genus of low dioecious shrubs (Batidaceae)

Examples

Batis is commonly found in subtropical salt marshes along the southeastern coast of the United States.
The unique reproductive system of Batis has made it a subject of botanical study.
Several coastal restoration projects have included Batis to help stabilize the shoreline.

Etymology

From Latin 'batis', derived from Greek 'batis', a plant name.
