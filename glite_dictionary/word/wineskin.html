wineskin

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪnˌskɪn / (AmE), / ˈwaɪn.skɪn / (BrE)
An animal skin, typically goatskin, sewn into a bag and used to store and serve wine.
animal skin used as a wine container

Examples

The old traveler carried a wineskin to quench his thirst during long journeys.
She poured red wine from a wineskin into the wooden cups around the fire.
The shepherd offered his guest a drink from his weathered wineskin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flask - a small container for liquids, typically used for alcohol
  • Synonym: bottle - a glass or plastic container with a narrow neck, used for liquids
