divan

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈvæn / (AmE), / dɪˈvɑːn / (BrE)
A council of state in some Muslim countries, especially in the Ottoman Empire.
Muslim council of state

Examples

The sultan convened the divan to discuss matters of governance.
Members of the divan held considerable power in the Ottoman administration.
Reforms were debated extensively within the divan before implementation.

Etymology

From Persian *dīwān* (council, assembly, or register), via Turkish and Arabic.
