pleasantness

1. NOUN

/ ˈplɛzəntnəs / (AmE), / ˈplɛzəntnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being pleasing, enjoyable, or agreeable.
quality of being pleasant or agreeable

Examples

The pleasantness of the afternoon was enhanced by a gentle breeze and birdsong.
We should strive to increase the pleasantness of our work environment.
Her smile added a touch of pleasantness to the otherwise tense meeting.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unpleasantness - the state or quality of being unpleasant or disagreeable

Etymology

From pleasant + -ness, modeled on similar noun formations.

2. NOUN

/ ˈplɛzəntnəs / (AmE), / ˈplɛz(ə)ntnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being pleasing, enjoyable, or giving a sense of satisfaction
quality of being pleasant or pleasing

Examples

The pleasantness of the music made everyone feel at ease during the event.
She commented on the pleasantness of the garden in the early morning light.
Many people visit the park for the pleasantness of its scenic views and peaceful paths.

Synset Examples

he was charmed by the sweetness of her manner
the pleasantness of a cool breeze on a hot summer day

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unpleasantness - the quality of being disagreeable or not pleasant
  • Synonym: sweetness - the quality of being pleasing, agreeable, or delightful

Etymology

From Middle English 'plesaunt' (pleasant) + '-ness' (forming nouns denoting a quality or state).
