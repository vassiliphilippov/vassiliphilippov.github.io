capitulation

1. NOUN

/ kəˌpɪtʃəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / kəˌpɪtjʊˈleɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
a formal document that sets out the terms under which one party surrenders to another
document detailing terms of surrender
legal

Examples

The general signed the capitulation after his forces were surrounded.
A copy of the capitulation was kept in the national archives for historical record.
The lengthy negotiations resulted in a capitulation that both sides agreed to honor.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin capitulatio (drawing up under headings), from Latin capitulum (chapter, heading).

2. NOUN

/ kəˌpɪtʃ.əˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / kəˌpɪt.jʊˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of surrendering or giving up, especially under agreed conditions
the act of surrendering under terms

Examples

The general announced the capitulation of his troops after a long siege.
Negotiators struggled to agree on the terms of capitulation before ending the conflict.
The sudden capitulation of the city came as a shock to its citizens.

Synset Examples

they were protected until the capitulation of the fort

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surrender - the act of giving up or yielding to another
  • Antonym: resistance - the act of opposing or withstanding

Etymology

From Latin 'capitulatio', meaning a drawing up of terms, from 'capitulum' (a heading, chapter).
