alendronate

1. NOUN

/ əˈlɛn.drəˌneɪt / (AmE), / əˈlɛn.drəˌneɪt / (BrE)
A drug used to prevent or treat osteoporosis, especially in postmenopausal women, often sold under the trade name Fosamax.
osteoporosis drug (Fosamax)
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed alendronate to help strengthen her bones after menopause.
Patients taking alendronate should follow specific instructions to avoid stomach irritation.
Researchers have studied the side effects of alendronate in long-term therapy for osteoporosis.
