kremlin

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɛm.lɪn / (AmE), / ˈkrɛm.lɪn / (BrE)
A fortified complex at the center of a Russian town, especially referring to the citadel of a historic Russian city.
citadel in a Russian town

Examples

The Moscow kremlin is a symbol of Russian power and history.
Many ancient Russian cities were built around a central kremlin for protection.
Tourists often visit the old kremlin to learn about the town's past.

Etymology

From Russian Кремль (Kreml’), meaning 'fortress' or 'citadel'.
