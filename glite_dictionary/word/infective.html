infective

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈfɛktɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɛktɪv / (BrE)
(of a disease, organism, or agent) caused by infection or capable of producing infection in others.
capable of causing infection
medical

Examples

The flu virus is highly infective, spreading quickly through schools and workplaces.
The doctor explained that the patient remained infective even after symptoms had disappeared.
Some bacteria can become less infective after exposure to antibiotics.

Synset Examples

viruses and other infective agents
a carrier remains infective without himself showing signs of the disease

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infectious - able to be passed from one person or organism to another, especially through infection

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'infectivus', from Latin 'inficere' (to stain, to infect).

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈfɛktɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɛktɪv / (BrE)
able to cause infection or disease; capable of transmitting disease to another organism.
able to cause disease; infectious
medical

Examples

The infective stage of the parasite is transmitted through contaminated water.
Doctors wore protective gear to avoid exposure to infective agents in the laboratory.
Certain strains of bacteria are highly infective and require special handling procedures.

Synset Examples

infective agents
pathogenic bacteria

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infectious - capable of causing infection; able to spread disease

Etymology

From Latin 'infectivus', from 'inficere' meaning 'to taint, stain'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01171978-s
Synset POS: s
