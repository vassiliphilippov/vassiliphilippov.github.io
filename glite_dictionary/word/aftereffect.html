aftereffect

1. NOUN

/ ˈæf.tɚ.ɪˌfɛkt / (AmE), / ˈɑːf.tər.ɪˌfɛkt / (BrE)
a delayed or secondary effect that follows the primary effect of an event, especially after using a drug or therapy
a delayed effect following an event or treatment

Examples

The patient experienced severe aftereffects several hours after the medication was given.
One common aftereffect of chemotherapy is fatigue that lasts for days.
Researchers are studying the long-term aftereffects of concussions in athletes.

Synset Examples

the drug had unexpected aftereffects

Related Terms

  • Synonym: side effect - an unwanted or secondary effect of a drug or therapy
  • Synonym: consequence - a result or effect of an action or condition

Etymology

Formed from 'after-' (following in time) and 'effect' (result, outcome).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæftərɪˌfɛkt / (AmE), / ˈɑːftərɪˌfɛkt / (BrE)
A result or consequence that appears after an event or action, especially after a delay.
delayed result or consequence

Examples

The most noticeable aftereffect of the storm was days of flooding in the valley.
Many people experience a mild headache as an aftereffect of the medication.
There can be psychological aftereffects following a traumatic event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consequence - A result or effect of an action or condition
  • Antonym: cause - A person or thing that gives rise to an action, phenomenon, or condition

Etymology

Formed from 'after-' + 'effect', modelled after patterns in English compounding.
