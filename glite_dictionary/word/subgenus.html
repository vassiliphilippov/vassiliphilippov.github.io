subgenus

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˈdʒiːnəs / (AmE), / sʌbˈdʒiːnəs / (BrE)
A taxonomic category in biology ranking below a genus and above a species, used to further classify organisms within a genus.
taxonomic rank below genus

Examples

Within each subgenus, the species are grouped according to specific shared traits.
The butterfly was classified into a newly described subgenus last year.
Researchers debated whether to elevate the subgenus to full genus status.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: genus - taxonomic rank above subgenus and below family
  • Degree Lower: species - taxonomic rank below subgenus

Etymology

From Latin sub- (under) + genus (kind, type).
