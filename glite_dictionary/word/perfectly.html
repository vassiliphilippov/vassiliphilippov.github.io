perfectly

1. ADV

/ ˈpɝː.fɪkt.li / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.fɪkt.li / (BrE)
In a perfect or faultless manner; without error or flaw.
in a flawless or complete way

Examples

She answered every question perfectly during the interview.
The cake was baked perfectly—not too dry and not too moist.
He perfectly captured the essence of the character in his performance.

Synset Examples

She performed perfectly on the balance beam
spoke English perfectly
solved the problem perfectly

Related Terms

  • Antonym: imperfectly - in a faulty or incomplete way
  • Synonym: flawlessly - in a way that is without any flaws

Etymology

From 'perfect' + '-ly', using the standard adverbial suffix.

2. ADV

/ ˈpɝː.fɪkt.li / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.fɪkt.li / (BrE)
completely and without any qualification; used to emphasize how true or complete something is.
completely; without qualification (intensifier)
informal

Examples

She was perfectly aware of the risks involved in her decision.
This solution works perfectly for most people in similar situations.
The evening went perfectly, with every detail managed smoothly.

Synset Examples

an absolutely magnificent painting
a perfectly idiotic idea
you're perfectly right
utterly miserable
you can be dead sure of my innocence
was dead tired
dead right

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absolutely - completely; without qualification (intensifier)
  • Synonym: utterly - completely; to a very high degree (intensifier)
  • Antonym: partially - in part; not completely

Etymology

From 'perfect' + '-ly'. 'Perfect' from Latin 'perfectus' (completed, excellent).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00009459-r
Synset POS: r
