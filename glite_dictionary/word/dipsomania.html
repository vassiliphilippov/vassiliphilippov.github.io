dipsomania

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪpsoʊˈmeɪniə / (AmE), / ˌdɪpsəˈmeɪnɪə / (BrE)
a persistent, uncontrollable craving for alcoholic drinks, especially in excessive amounts
uncontrollable craving for alcohol
medical

Examples

His struggle with dipsomania led to several stints in rehabilitation clinics.
Medical professionals debated whether dipsomania should be classified as a mental illness.
The film portrayed the tragic consequences of unchecked dipsomania in the protagonist's life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alcoholism - chronic dependence on or addiction to alcohol

Etymology

From Greek dipsa (thirst) + mania (madness, frenzy).
