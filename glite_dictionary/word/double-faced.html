double-faced

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʌbəl feɪst / (AmE), / ˌdʌb.əl ˈfeɪst / (BrE)
(of fabric) having finished or usable surfaces on both sides, often with different patterns or textures.
fabric with usable faces on both sides

Examples

The designer selected a luxurious double-faced wool for the coat, making it both attractive and warm.
Damask is known as a double-faced fabric, featuring a pattern on both sides.
My grandmother's quilt used double-faced material so it could display two beautiful designs.

Synset Examples

damask is a double-faced fabric

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reversible - able to be used with either side facing outward, especially of fabrics or garments

Etymology

Formed from 'double' (from Old French 'doble', from Latin 'duplus') and 'faced' (from Old French 'face', from Latin 'facies').

2. ADJ

/ ˈdʌbəl ˌfeɪst / (AmE), / ˌdʌb.əl ˈfeɪst / (BrE)
deliberately deceptive, pretending to have one set of feelings or intentions while secretly acting under another
deliberately deceptive; hypocritical

Examples

Despite his friendly words, she found him to be disturbingly double-faced and insincere.
Many politicians are criticized for their double-faced promises during election campaigns.
Nobody trusts a double-faced colleague who gossips behind everyone's back.

Synset Examples

she was a deceitful scheming little thing
a double-dealing double agent
a double-faced infernal traitor and schemer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypocritical - pretending to have virtues or beliefs that one does not actually possess
  • Antonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and sincere

Etymology

Formed from 'double' (meaning two) and 'faced', implying two faces or appearances.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01225960-s
Synset POS: s
