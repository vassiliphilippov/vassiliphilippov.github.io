lexicostatistics

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɛksɪkoʊstəˈstɪstɪks / (AmE), / ˌlɛksɪkəʊstəˈstɪstɪks / (BrE)
a statistical method in linguistics used to estimate the degree of difference or similarity between languages, often to infer how long ago they diverged from a common ancestor
statistical method for comparing languages

Examples

The team used lexicostatistics to measure how closely related the two dialects were.
Advances in lexicostatistics have helped researchers trace the origins of Indo-European languages.
Some linguists question the assumptions underlying lexicostatistics in estimating language divergence.

Etymology

From lexicon + statistics, formed in the mid-20th century during the development of comparative linguistic methods.
