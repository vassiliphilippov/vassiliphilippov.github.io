disestablishment

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪs.ɪˈstæb.lɪʃ.mənt / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.ɪˈstæb.lɪʃ.mənt / (BrE)
The act of ending an established system, organization, or official connection, especially the separation of a church from state authority.
ending of an established system or church-state connection

Examples

The disestablishment of the Church of Ireland marked a major change in religious policy.
Some politicians advocated for the disestablishment of state-funded institutions to promote separation of church and state.
After years of debate, disestablishment became a central issue in the parliamentary session.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: establishment - the process of founding or setting up something

Etymology

Formed by adding the prefix dis- (expressing reversal) to establishment.
