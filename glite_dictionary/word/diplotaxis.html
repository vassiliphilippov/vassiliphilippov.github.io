Diplotaxis

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪpləˈtæksɪs / (AmE), / ˌdɪpləˈtaksɪs / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the mustard family, commonly known as wall rockets, with yellow flowers and often found in Europe and North Africa.
genus of plants (wall rockets)

Examples

The Diplotaxis plants are commonly found growing along roadsides in the Mediterranean region.
Researchers studied the effects of drought on Diplotaxis to understand its resilience.
Gardeners appreciate Diplotaxis for its bright yellow flowers and hardy nature.

Etymology

From Greek diplos (double) + taxis (arrangement), referring to the plant's seed pods.

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɪpləˈtæksɪs / (AmE), / ˌdɪpləˈtaksɪs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the mustard family (Brassicaceae), commonly known as wall rocket.
a genus of wall rocket plants

Examples

Several species within the Diplotaxis genus are cultivated for their edible leaves.
The distinctive yellow flowers of Diplotaxis plants are often found growing on old walls and rocky places.
Botanists recently discovered a new species in the Diplotaxis genus in southern Europe.

Etymology

From Greek diploos (double) and taxis (arrangement), referring to double rows of seeds in the seedpod.
