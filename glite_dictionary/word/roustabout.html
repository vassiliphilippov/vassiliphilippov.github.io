roustabout

1. NOUN

/ ˈraʊstəˌbaʊt / (AmE), / ˈraʊstəˌbaʊt / (BrE)
a member of a ship's crew who performs unskilled manual labor
manual laborer on a ship

Examples

As a roustabout, he spent long hours scrubbing the ship's deck and hauling ropes.
The captain assigned the tasks to the roustabout, who tackled them without complaint.
Many young sailors started as a roustabout before learning more specialized maritime skills.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deckhand - a manual worker on a ship's deck

Etymology

Probably from US dialect 'roust' (to drive or force out) + 'about'.
