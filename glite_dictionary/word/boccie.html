boccie

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑtʃi / (AmE), / ˈbɒtʃi / (BrE)
An Italian bowling game played on a long, narrow court, usually dirt or sand, where players roll balls to get them closest to a smaller target ball.
Italian bowling game played on a narrow court

Examples

On Sunday afternoon, the family played boccie in the backyard.
The local park has a boccie court open to the public.
He learned to play boccie during his trip to Italy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bocce - Italian bowling game played on a long narrow court

Etymology

From Italian 'bocce', plural of 'boccia' (ball), from Late Latin 'bottia'.
