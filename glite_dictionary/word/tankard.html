tankard

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæŋkərd / (AmE), / ˈtaŋkəd / (BrE)
a large drinking vessel, typically with one handle and sometimes a hinged lid, used especially for beer
large handled drinking vessel

Examples

He poured ale into a pewter tankard and handed it to his guest.
The old tankard was displayed on the mantel as a family heirloom.
She raised her tankard in a toast to the happy couple.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mug - a large cup with a handle, typically for hot drinks

Etymology

From Middle Dutch *tanckaert*, diminutive of *tancke* (tank, can).
