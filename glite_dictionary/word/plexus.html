plexus

1. NOUN

/ ˈplɛksəs / (AmE), / ˈplɛksəs / (BrE)
a network of interwoven blood vessels, nerves, or lymphatic vessels in the body
network of vessels or nerves
medical

Examples

The brachial plexus controls movement and sensation in the arm and hand.
Damage to the solar plexus can cause intense pain and difficulty breathing.
Several nerves converge to form a plexus that coordinates digestive functions.

Etymology

From Latin 'plexus' (braid, network), from 'plectere' (to braid).
