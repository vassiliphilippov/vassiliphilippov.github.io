arccosine

1. NOUN

/ ɑrˈkoʊ.saɪn / (AmE), / ɑːˈkəʊ.saɪn / (BrE)
the inverse function of the cosine, giving the angle whose cosine is a specified value
inverse cosine function (math)

Examples

The arccosine of 0.5 is 60 degrees, since cos(60°) = 0.5.
In trigonometry, arccosine is often abbreviated as arccos.
To solve the equation, take the arccosine of both sides.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cosine - the trigonometric function for the ratio of the adjacent side to the hypotenuse in a right triangle

Etymology

From 'arc' (for arcus in Latin, meaning angle) + 'cosine' (from Latin 'cosinus').
