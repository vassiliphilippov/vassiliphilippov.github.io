Dracunculus vulgaris

1. NOUN

/ drəˈkʌŋkjələs vʌlˈɡɛrɪs / (AmE), / dræˈkʌŋkjʊləs vʌlˈɡɑːrɪs / (BrE)
A species of arum native to southern Europe, known for its large, dark purple spathe and distinctive unpleasant odor; also called dragon arum.
a southern European arum plant with a purple spathe

Examples

The garden’s centerpiece was a blooming Dracunculus vulgaris, attracting attention with its striking purple spathe.
Gardeners often plant Dracunculus vulgaris for its unusual appearance despite its strong odor.
We identified the mysterious flower as Dracunculus vulgaris after consulting a botanical guide.

Etymology

From Latin: 'dracunculus' meaning 'little dragon' and 'vulgaris' meaning 'common'.
