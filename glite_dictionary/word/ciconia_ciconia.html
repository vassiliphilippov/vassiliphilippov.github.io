Ciconia ciconia

1. NOUN

/ sɪˈkoʊniə sɪˈkoʊniə / (AmE), / sɪˈkəʊniə sɪˈkəʊniə / (BrE)
The scientific name for the common European stork, a large white bird with black wing feathers and a red bill.
scientific name of the European stork

Examples

The Ciconia ciconia migrates each year between Europe and Africa.
Researchers monitored several nests of Ciconia ciconia in the wetland reserve.
Unlike many other birds, Ciconia ciconia prefers open farmland for feeding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stork - Large, long-legged, long-necked wading bird

Etymology

From Latin 'Ciconia' meaning 'stork'.
