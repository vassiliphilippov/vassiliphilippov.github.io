to calve

1. VERB

/ kæv / (AmE), / kɑːv / (BrE)
(of cows, whales, or other animals) to give birth to a calf.
give birth to a calf (animal)

Examples

The cows usually calve in early spring when the weather is mild.
Every year, humpback whales calve in the warm waters of the bay.
She stayed up all night to help her heifer calve safely.

Synset Examples

the whales calve at this time of year

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to give birth - to bring forth offspring

Etymology

From Old English *calfian* (to bring forth a calf), from *calf* (young of a cow).

2. VERB

/ kæv / (AmE), / kɑːv / (BrE)
(of a glacier or iceberg) to break off and release a mass of ice into the sea.
(of glacier) release ice blocks

Examples

As the temperature rose, huge sections of the glacier began to calve into the bay.
Tourists watched as the iceberg suddenly calved, sending a wave across the water.
Scientists track how frequently glaciers calve to study climate change.

Synset Examples

The icebergs and glaciers calve

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fragment - to break into pieces, especially abruptly
  • Degree Lower: melt - to turn from solid to liquid, often as ice does in the heat (related process, not exact synonym)
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
