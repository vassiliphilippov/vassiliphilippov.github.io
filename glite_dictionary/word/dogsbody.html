dogsbody

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɔɡzˌbɑdi / (AmE), / ˈdɒɡzˌbɒdi / (BrE)
a person who is given the boring, unpleasant, or menial tasks that nobody else wants to do
person given menial or unpleasant tasks
british

Examples

He started out as a dogsbody at the company, making coffee and filing papers.
As the youngest member of the team, she often felt like the office dogsbody.
I'm tired of being treated like a dogsbody and doing everyone else's chores.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drudge - a person made to do hard, menial, or dull work
  • Synonym: skivvy - a servant or someone who does menial work, especially in a household

Etymology

Originally Royal Navy slang; from 'dog's body', a nickname for a pea-and-oatmeal dish served to sailors.
