viceroyalty

1. NOUN

/ ˌvaɪsˈroʊəlti / (AmE), / ˌvaɪsˈrəʊəlti / (BrE)
A country, province, or district governed by a viceroy as the representative of a monarch.
territory governed by a viceroy

Examples

During the colonial era, the viceroyalty of New Spain was a powerful administrative unit.
The Spanish crown established several viceroyalties in the Americas.
Historians study the administrative structure of each viceroyalty to understand its influence on local culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viceroy - the governor of a country or province representing a monarch

Etymology

Formed from 'viceroy' + '-alty', after French 'vice-royauté'.
