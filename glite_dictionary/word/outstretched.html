outstretched

1. ADJ

/ aʊtˈstrɛtʃt / (AmE), / aʊtˈstrɛtʃt / (BrE)
fully extended to the greatest possible length or reach, especially a limb or object
extended to full length

Examples

The eagle soared through the sky with its wings outstretched.
She offered her hand, outstretched, as a sign of peace.
A child was running across the field, arms outstretched in excitement.

Synset Examples

a kitten with one paw outstretched

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extended - stretched out to full length

Etymology

Past participle of 'outstretch', from Middle English 'outstrecche(n)', combining 'out' + 'stretch'.
