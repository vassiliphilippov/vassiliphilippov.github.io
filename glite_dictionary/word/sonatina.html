sonatina

1. NOUN

/ sɑːnəˈtiːnə / (AmE), / sɒn.əˈtiː.nə / (BrE)
A short and simple sonata, typically used as a term for a modest musical composition resembling a sonata but lighter or shorter in form.
short, simple sonata; musical composition

Examples

The young pianist performed a delightful sonatina at her first recital.
He composed a sonatina for violin and piano, perfect for intermediate students.
Many beginners start with a sonatina before attempting a full-length sonata.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sonata - a composition for one or more solo instruments, usually in several movements

Etymology

From Italian 'sonatina', diminutive of 'sonata'.
