hypotonus

1. NOUN

/ haɪˈpoʊ.tə.nəs / (AmE), / haɪˈpəʊ.tə.nəs / (BrE)
A condition in which muscular tissue has diminished tone or tension; the state of being hypotonic.
state of reduced muscle tone
medical

Examples

In newborns, hypotonus may indicate a neurological disorder.
The patient's hypotonus was most noticeable in the lower limbs.
Physical therapy can help manage hypotonus in children with developmental delays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypotonia - reduced muscle tone; abnormally low muscle tension
  • Antonym: hypertonus - state of excessive muscle tone; opposite of hypotonus

Etymology

From Greek 'hypo' (under) + 'tonos' (tone).
