hartley

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrtli / (AmE), / ˈhɑːtli / (BrE)
a logarithmic unit used to measure information or entropy, based on base 10 logarithms and powers of 10
unit of information or entropy (base 10)

Examples

The amount of information required to specify one of ten equally likely events is precisely one hartley.
Scientists sometimes use the hartley as a unit when describing information entropy in their equations.
One hartley is equal to the information content of an event with a probability of one-tenth.

Synset Examples

The ban pre-dates Shannon's use of bit as a unit of information by at least eight years, and remains in use in the early 21st Century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bit - unit of information based on base 2 logarithms
  • Synonym: nat - unit of information based on natural logarithms

Etymology

Named after Ralph V. L. Hartley, American electronics researcher.
