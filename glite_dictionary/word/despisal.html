despisal

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈspaɪzəl / (AmE), / dɪˈspaɪzəl / (BrE)
Strong contempt or scornful hatred towards someone or something.
feeling of scornful hatred

Examples

Her obvious despisal of the new manager created a tense atmosphere in the office.
He spoke with open despisal for anyone who disagreed with him.
The movie portrays the despisal of social norms by the main character.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contempt - the feeling that someone or something is worthless or beneath consideration
  • Antonym: admiration - a feeling of great respect and approval

Etymology

Formed from 'despise' + '-al', modelled after other abstract nouns.
