interferometer

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərfəˈrɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / ˌɪntəfəˈrɒmɪtə / (BrE)
a scientific instrument that measures wavelengths, distances, or changes in optical paths by analyzing interference patterns of waves, especially light or radio waves
instrument measuring waves using interference patterns

Examples

The Michelson interferometer played a crucial role in the development of modern physics.
Astronomers use a radio interferometer to achieve higher resolution images of distant galaxies.
Careful alignment of the interferometer is necessary to produce clear interference patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spectrometer - an instrument used to measure properties of light over a specific portion of the electromagnetic spectrum

Etymology

From interfere + -ometer, modeled after barometer and similar scientific instrument names.
