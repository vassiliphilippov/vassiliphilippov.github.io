precipitating

1. ADJ

/ prɪˈsɪpɪˌteɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / prɪˈsɪpɪteɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
causing something to happen suddenly or unexpectedly, especially an event, action, or change.
causing something sudden to happen

Examples

Financial instability was the precipitating factor in the company’s collapse.
The assassination was the precipitating event that led to the war.
A sudden drop in temperature acted as the precipitating cause of the thunderstorm.

Synset Examples

the completion of the railroad was the precipitating cause in the extinction of waterborne commerce

Related Terms

  • Synonym: triggering - causing something to start or happen
  • Antonym: preventing - stopping something from happening

Etymology

From Latin 'praecipitare' (to throw headlong), via the verb 'precipitate'.
