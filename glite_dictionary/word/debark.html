to debark

1. VERB

/ diˈbɑrk / (AmE), / dɪˈbɑːk / (BrE)
to leave a ship, aircraft, or other vehicle; to go ashore or disembark
to leave a ship, aircraft, or vehicle

Examples

After the long journey, the passengers were eager to debark and stretch their legs.
All crew members must debark before the ship is cleaned.
We waited patiently on the deck for permission to debark at the island port.

Synset Examples

The passengers disembarked at Southampton

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disembark - to leave a ship, aircraft, or other vehicle

Etymology

From French 'débarquer', from 'dé-' (off) + 'barque' (boat).
