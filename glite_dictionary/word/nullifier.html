nullifier

1. NOUN

/ ˈnʌlɪˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈnʌlɪˌfaɪə / (BrE)
a person who supports the idea that a state has the right to reject or invalidate federal laws considered unconstitutional
supporter of state's right to reject federal laws

Examples

During the 1830s, South Carolina nullifiers challenged federal authority through the Nullification Crisis.
A group of outspoken nullifiers organized protests against the enforcement of new federal tariffs.
As a nullifier, he argued that states should be able to disregard any federal law they viewed as unconstitutional.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nullification - the act of a state invalidating a federal law it deems unconstitutional

Etymology

From 'nullify' + '-er'; used historically in 19th-century American politics.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnʌlɪˌfaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈnʌlɪˌfaɪə / (BrE)
an official or person who invalidates or nullifies something, especially a legal or official document
person who invalidates something

Examples

The court appointed a nullifier to review and void suspicious contracts.
As the official nullifier, she had the authority to cancel the results of unfair elections.
He asked to meet the nullifier responsible for annulling his license.

Synset Examples

my bank check was voided and I wanted to know who the invalidator was

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invalidator - someone who makes something invalid

Etymology

From nullify + -er, meaning 'one who nullifies'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10234060-n
Synset POS: n
