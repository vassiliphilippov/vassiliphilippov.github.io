hypoproteinemia

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪproʊˌproʊtiˈnimiə / (AmE), / ˌhaɪprəʊˌprəʊtiːˈniːmiə / (BrE)
a medical condition characterized by an abnormally low level of protein in the blood, often indicating dietary deficiency or underlying health disorders
low blood protein condition
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed him with hypoproteinemia after reviewing his blood test results.
Severe hypoproteinemia can cause swelling due to decreased oncotic pressure.
Patients suffering from malnutrition often present with hypoproteinemia.

Etymology

From Greek hypo- (under) + protein + -emia (blood condition).
