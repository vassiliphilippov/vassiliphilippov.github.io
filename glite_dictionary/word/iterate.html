to iterate

1. VERB

/ ˈɪt̬əˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪt.ə.reɪt / (BrE)
to say, state, or perform something again; to repeat a statement or action
say or do again; repeat

Examples

The manager had to iterate the safety instructions several times for the new employees.
She chose to iterate her main point to ensure everyone understood.
The announcement was iterated throughout the day on the loudspeaker.

Synset Examples

She kept reiterating her request

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repeat - to say or do something again
  • Synonym: to reiterate - to say something again or repeatedly, often with emphasis

Etymology

From Latin iterare, iteratum (to do again), from iterum (again).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00960366-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈɪtəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪtəreɪt / (BrE)
to perform or repeat a process or set of instructions again, especially in computing or mathematics
repeat a process or operation

Examples

The algorithm will iterate until it finds a solution.
In this program, we iterate over each item in the list.
The engineer decided to iterate the process to improve accuracy.

Synset Examples

the function iterates

Related Terms

  • Synonym: repeat - to do something again

Etymology

From Latin iterare (to repeat), from iterum (again).

Synset POS: v
