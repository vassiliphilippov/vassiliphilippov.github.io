Dacrydium

1. NOUN

/ ˌdækˈrɪdiəm / (AmE), / ˌdækˈrɪdɪəm / (BrE)
A genus of Australasian evergreen trees or shrubs in the family Podocarpaceae, known for their needlelike leaves and often found in wet forests.
genus of Australasian evergreen trees or shrubs

Examples

Several species of Dacrydium are important in the rainforests of New Zealand and Southeast Asia.
The leaves of Dacrydium are usually narrow and needlelike, adapting well to wet environments.
Botanists studied the pollen of Dacrydium to learn about the region's prehistoric climate.

Etymology

From Greek dakryon (tear, drop), referring to the resin drops exuded by some species, combined with the Latin botanical suffix -ium.
