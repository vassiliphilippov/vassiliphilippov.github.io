ash-gray

1. ADJ

/ ˈæʃˌɡreɪ / (AmE), / ˈæʃˌɡreɪ / (BrE)
of a light, pale gray color resembling the color of ashes
light pale gray color

Examples

The walls were painted a soft ash-gray that made the room feel calm and spacious.
She wore an ash-gray scarf that complemented her outfit perfectly.
The sky turned ash-gray just before the storm began.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: gray - a neutral color between black and white
  • Degree Higher: white - the lightest color
  • Degree Lower: charcoal - a dark gray color

Etymology

Formed from 'ash' (the powdery residue after burning) and 'gray', referring to the color of ash.
