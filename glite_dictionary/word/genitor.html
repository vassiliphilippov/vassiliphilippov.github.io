genitor

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛnɪtər / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnɪtə / (BrE)
A biological father or mother; a natural parent, as distinguished from an adoptive parent.
biological parent

Examples

The child's genitor was never identified in the official records.
Sometimes, medical histories must be taken from both genitors to assess genetic risks.
In certain legal documents, the term genitor is used to specify a biological relationship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parent - a father or mother; a person who has a child
  • Antonym: adoptive parent - a parent who has legally adopted a child

Etymology

From Latin 'genitor', meaning 'begetter' or 'father', from 'gignere' (to beget).
