aminoaciduria

1. NOUN

/ əˌminoʊˌæsɪˈdjʊriə / (AmE), / əˌmiːnəʊˌasɪˈdjʊəriə / (BrE)
the abnormal presence of amino acids in the urine, typically indicating a metabolic disorder
abnormal amino acids in urine (medical)
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed aminoaciduria after abnormal results in the patient's urine test.
Infants with inherited metabolic disorders may develop aminoaciduria as one of the symptoms.
Detection of aminoaciduria can assist in identifying problems with kidney or liver function.

Etymology

From amino acid + -uria (from Greek ouron, meaning urine).
