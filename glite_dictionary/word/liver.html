liver

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɪvər / (AmE), / ˈlɪvə / (BrE)
(of a color) reddish-brown, resembling the color of a liver.
reddish-brown color

Examples

The artist chose a liver tone for the horse's coat in her painting.
She wore a liver colored dress that complemented her complexion.
The dog's fur was a rich liver shade, making it stand out at the show.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maroon - a dark reddish-brown color
  • Synonym: chestnut - a medium reddish-brown color

Etymology

From the noun 'liver', referring to the organ's color.

/ ˈlɪvər / (AmE), / ˈlɪvə / (BrE)

Examples

She bought a pound of chicken liver for tonight's dinner.

Related Terms

4. NOUN

/ ˈlɪvɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɪvə / (BrE)
a large, reddish-brown organ in the upper right part of the abdomen that produces bile, processes nutrients, detoxifies harmful substances, and performs various metabolic functions
large organ that processes nutrients and detoxifies the body

Examples

He was diagnosed with a disease that affects the liver.
Alcohol abuse can cause serious damage to your liver.
The doctor explained the important functions of the liver during the biology class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kidney - organ that filters waste from blood and produces urine
  • Synonym: heart - muscular organ that pumps blood through the body

Etymology

From Old English *lifer*, related to Old High German *lebra*, Old Norse *lifr* (liver).
