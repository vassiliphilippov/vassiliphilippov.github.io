thousand

1. ADJ

/ ˈθaʊzənd / (AmE), / ˈθaʊzənd / (BrE)
denoting a quantity consisting of 1,000 items or units
consisting of 1,000 units

Examples

She bought a pack of thousand paper clips for her office supplies.
More than a thousand people attended the concert in the city center.
He won a thousand dollars in the lottery last week.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hundred - denoting a quantity consisting of 100 items or units
  • Degree Higher: million - denoting a quantity consisting of 1,000,000 items or units

Etymology

From Old English þūsend, related to other Germanic languages.

2. NOUN

/ ˈθaʊzənd / (AmE), / ˈθaʊzənd / (BrE)
the number 1,000; the cardinal number equal to ten times one hundred
the number 1,000

Examples

There are more than thousand species of birds in this region.
He donated a thousand dollars to charity last year.
The book sold over one thousand copies in its first month.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: million - the number 1,000,000
  • Degree Lower: hundred - the number 100

Etymology

From Old English *þūsend*, from Proto-Germanic *þūsundī*.

3. NUM

/ ˈθaʊzənd / (AmE), / ˈθaʊz(ə)nd / (BrE)
Denoting a quantity consisting of 1,000 items or units.
a quantity of 1,000

Examples

The stadium has seats for over thousand spectators.
She won a prize of one thousand dollars in the competition.
A journey of a thousand miles begins with a single step.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: hundred - denoting a quantity consisting of 100 items or units
  • Degree Higher: million - denoting a quantity consisting of 1,000,000 items or units

Etymology

From Old English *þūsend*, of Germanic origin.
