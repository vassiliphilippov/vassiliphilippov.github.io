discomfort

1. NOUN

/ dɪsˈkʌmfərt / (AmE), / dɪsˈkʌmfət / (BrE)
a feeling of mental unease, pain, or distress
mental unease or distress

Examples

He tried to hide his discomfort during the tense meeting.
Her obvious discomfort made everyone feel uneasy.
Meditation can help relieve feelings of discomfort and stress.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: comfort - a state of ease and satisfaction, with no discomfort or distress
  • Synonym: distress - extreme anxiety, sorrow, or pain

Etymology

From Old French desconfort (distress, discouragement), from des- (expressing reversal) + conforter (to comfort).

2. NOUN

/ dɪsˈkʌmfɚt / (AmE), / dɪsˈkʌmfət / (BrE)
A feeling of slight pain or physical unease; the state of being tense or not completely comfortable.
slight pain or physical unease

Examples

She shifted in her seat, trying to ease the discomfort in her back.
After the surgery, mild discomfort is normal for a few days.
He hid his discomfort behind a polite smile during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pain - physical suffering or discomfort
  • Antonym: comfort - a state of physical ease and freedom from pain

Etymology

From Old French 'desconfort' (discomfort, discouragement), from des- (dis-) + confort (comfort), ultimately from Latin 'confortare' (to strengthen).
