archduchy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrtʃˌdʌtʃi / (AmE), / ˈɑːtʃˌdʌtʃi / (BrE)
A territory or domain ruled by an archduke or archduchess.
domain ruled by an archduke/archduchess

Examples

The Habsburg family controlled the archduchy of Austria for centuries.
In medieval Europe, each archduchy held a significant degree of autonomy.
The archduchy became a major power in Central Europe during the Renaissance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duchy - the domain ruled by a duke or duchess

Etymology

From German Erzherzogtum, combining 'arch-' (chief, principal) and 'duchy'.
