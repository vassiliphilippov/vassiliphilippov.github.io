fulminant

1. ADJ

/ ˈfʊlmɪnənt / (AmE), / ˈfʊlmɪnənt / (BrE)
(of a disease or symptom) appearing and developing suddenly and severely.
sudden and severe (esp. medically)
medical

Examples

The patient developed fulminant hepatitis within hours of admission.
Doctors were concerned by the fulminant onset of symptoms after exposure to the toxin.
The infection progressed in a fulminant manner, leaving little time for treatment.

Synset Examples

fulminant pain
fulminant fever

Related Terms

  • Synonym: acute - (of disease) severe, sudden onset

Etymology

From Latin 'fulminant-', present participle of 'fulminare' (to strike with lightning).
