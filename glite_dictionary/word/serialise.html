to serialise

1. VERB

/ ˈsɪriəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪəriəlaɪz / (BrE)
to arrange or organize things in a series or sequence
arrange in sequence
british

Examples

To ensure clarity, the teacher asked the students to serialise their ideas before presenting.
The archivist needed to serialise the documents by date to make retrieval easier.
You must serialise the data before importing it into the new database.

Synset Examples

Serialize the numbers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to serialize - arrange serially (American spelling)

Etymology

From serial + -ise, modelled after the word 'serial'.
