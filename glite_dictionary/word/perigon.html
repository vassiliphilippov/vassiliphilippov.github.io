perigon

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛrɪˌgɑn / (AmE), / ˈpɛrɪˌgɒn / (BrE)
an angle equal to 360 degrees; a full angle in geometry
angle of 360 degrees

Examples

A perigon is formed when a line completes a full circle around a point.
The wheel turned through a perigon as it completed one revolution.
An angle measuring 360 degrees is called a perigon in mathematical terminology.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: straight angle - an angle of 180 degrees
  • Degree Lower: reflex angle - an angle greater than 180 degrees but less than 360 degrees

Etymology

From Greek 'perigōnios' (all around an angle), from 'peri' (around) + 'gōnia' (angle).
