hypnotherapy

1. NOUN

/ ˌhɪp.nəˈθɛr.ə.pi / (AmE), / ˌhɪp.nəʊˈθɛr.ə.pi / (BrE)
the use of hypnosis as a therapeutic technique in treating psychological or medical conditions
the use of hypnosis in therapy
medical

Examples

Many people turn to hypnotherapy to help them quit smoking or overcome anxiety.
The clinic offers hypnotherapy sessions for people struggling with insomnia.
He decided to study hypnotherapy after seeing its benefits firsthand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypnosis - a trance-like state of focused attention and heightened suggestibility

Etymology

From Greek 'hypnos' (sleep) + 'therapy' (treatment).
