Zn

1. NOUN

/ ziː ˈɛn / (AmE), / ziː ˈɛn / (BrE)
the chemical symbol for the metallic element zinc, a bluish-white metal used in alloys and in protecting iron from rusting
symbol for the element zinc (metal)

Examples

The corrosion resistance of galvanized steel comes from its coating of Zn.
In chemistry class, we learned that Zn is essential for enzyme function.
Metal alloys containing Zn are often used in batteries and electronics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zinc - a bluish-white metallic element; chemical element Zn, atomic number 30

Etymology

From the Neo-Latin 'zincum', symbol assigned in international chemical nomenclature.
