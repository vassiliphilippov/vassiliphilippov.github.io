Darwinism

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɑrwɪˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈdɑːwɪˌnɪzəm / (BrE)
the theory of evolution which states that species evolve over time through natural selection, as proposed by Charles Darwin
theory of evolution by natural selection

Examples

Many textbooks explain the principles of Darwinism as the foundation of modern biology.
Opponents of Darwinism often promote alternative theories about the origins of life.
His research supported the main tenets of Darwinism through new fossil evidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evolution - the process by which different kinds of living organisms are thought to have developed
  • Antonym: creationism - the belief that the universe and life originated from specific acts of divine creation

Etymology

From Charles Darwin (1809–1882), English naturalist, with -ism.
