Elaphurus davidianus

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌlæfjʊrəs dæˌvɪdiˈænəs / (AmE), / ɛˌlæfjʊrəs dəˌvɪdiˈɑːnəs / (BrE)
a large Chinese deer, now surviving only in domesticated herds, also known as Père David's deer
large Chinese deer species; Père David's deer

Examples

The Elaphurus davidianus was once extinct in the wild but has been reintroduced to reserves in China.
Researchers studied the feeding habits of Elaphurus davidianus in managed herds.
The London Zoo has a small population of Elaphurus davidianus for conservation purposes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Père David's deer - common name for Elaphurus davidianus

Etymology

From New Latin, combining the genus name 'Elaphurus' (from Greek 'elaphos', deer) and the species name honoring Father Armand David, a French missionary and naturalist.
