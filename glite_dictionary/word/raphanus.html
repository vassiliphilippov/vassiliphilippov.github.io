Raphanus

1. NOUN

/ ˈræf.ə.nəs / (AmE), / ˈræf.ə.nəs / (BrE)
A genus of plants in the mustard family, including the cultivated radish.
plant genus including radishes

Examples

The genus Raphanus includes the common radish grown in many gardens.
Botanists studied wild species of Raphanus to understand their evolution.
Seeds from Raphanus plants are often used in agricultural research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: radish - an edible root vegetable related to Raphanus

Etymology

From Greek *rháphanos* (radish).
