anthropophaginian

1. NOUN

/ ˌænθɹəpəˈfeɪdʒɪniən / (AmE), / ˌanθɹəpəˈfeɪdʒɪnɪən / (BrE)
A member of a mythical or legendary tribe alleged to practice cannibalism; pertaining to anthropophagi.
member of a legendary cannibal tribe

Examples

The explorer wrote about his encounters with the mysterious anthropophaginian tribe in his journal.
Shakespeare referenced an anthropophaginian in the play 'Othello' to evoke exotic fears.
Researchers debated whether the tales of anthropophaginians had any basis in fact or were pure myth.

Synset Examples

The anthropophaginian legend intrigued the researchers.
The movie featured an anthropophaginian tribe in its plot.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anthropophagus - a cannibal, especially in mythology or legend

Etymology

Formed from 'anthropophagus' (from Greek anthrōpophagos, meaning 'man-eating') + '-ian'.
