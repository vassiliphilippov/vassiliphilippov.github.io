to examine

1. VERB

/ ɪɡˈzæmɪn / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzæmɪn / (BrE)
to look at or question something or someone thoroughly and closely, especially to learn or discover something
look at or question closely

Examples

The detective carefully examined the evidence found at the scene.
A doctor will examine you to determine the cause of your symptoms.
The committee plans to examine the new proposal before making a decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inspect - to look at something carefully
  • Synonym: to scrutinize - to examine something very carefully
  • Antonym: to ignore - to pay no attention to something

Etymology

From Latin 'examinare' (to weigh, consider closely), from 'examen' (weighing, examination).

2. VERB

/ ɪɡˈzæmɪn / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzæmɪn / (BrE)
to study or analyze something carefully and in detail in order to discover important facts or features
study or analyze in detail

Examples

The professor asked the class to examine the poem for hidden metaphors.
Before making a decision, she decided to examine all the available data carefully.
We need to examine the contract thoroughly before signing it.

Synset Examples

analyze a sonnet by Shakespeare
analyze the evidence in a criminal trial
analyze your real motives

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to analyze - examine methodically by separating into parts and studying their interrelations
  • Synonym: to inspect - look at (something) closely to assess its condition

Etymology

From Latin 'examinare', meaning to weigh, consider, or inspect.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00646245-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɪɡˈzæm.ɪn / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzæm.ɪn / (BrE)
to question someone closely, especially in an official or formal way, in order to find out facts or information
to question closely or formally

Examples

The lawyer began to examine the witness about his whereabouts on the night of the crime.
During the interview, she examined the candidate with a series of challenging questions.
The committee will examine all experts to ensure the facts are clear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to interrogate - to ask someone questions, often formally or aggressively
  • Synonym: grill - to question someone intensely
  • Antonym: to ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to someone or something

Etymology

From Latin 'examinare' (to weigh, consider), from 'examen' (tongue of a balance).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ɪɡˈzæm.ɪn / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzæm.ɪn / (BrE)
to look at something or someone carefully and thoroughly in order to find out information or detect problems
inspect carefully or thoroughly

Examples

Before making a decision, she always likes to examine all the facts available.
The scientist will examine the samples under a microscope later today.
Doctors usually examine patients before prescribing medication.

Synset Examples

The customs agent examined the baggage
I must see your passport before you can enter the country

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inspect - to look at something carefully in order to check its condition or discover any mistakes or problems
  • Synonym: to scrutinize - to examine something very carefully in order to discover information
  • Antonym: to ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to something or someone

Etymology

From Latin 'examinare' (to weigh, test, examine), from 'examen' (a weighing, examination).
