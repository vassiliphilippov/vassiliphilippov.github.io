decisive

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈsaɪsɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈsaɪsɪv / (BrE)
having the effect of deciding something or marking a significant turning point or crisis, especially one that determines the outcome.
crucial; marking a turning point

Examples

The team scored a decisive goal in the last minute of the match.
Her quick action proved to be decisive in saving the company.
The outcome hinged on a decisive moment during the negotiations.

Synset Examples

a critical point in the campaign
the critical test

Related Terms

  • Synonym: critical - of decisive importance; forming or having the nature of a turning point or crisis
  • Synonym: crucial - decisively important or critical

Etymology

From French décisif, from Latin decisivus, from decidere (to decide).

Source: WordNet

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈsaɪsɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈsaɪsɪv / (BrE)
having the power or quality to decide an outcome; determining the result of something
having the power to determine an outcome

Examples

Her decisive action during the crisis prevented a disaster.
The team's victory was sealed with a decisive goal in the final minutes.
Weather conditions were a decisive factor in the outcome of the match.

Synset Examples

cast the decisive vote
two factors had a decisive influence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conclusive - serving to settle or decide a question; decisive
  • Antonym: indecisive - not having or showing the ability to make decisions quickly and effectively; not producing a definite result

Etymology

From French décisif, from Medieval Latin decisivus, from Latin decidere (to decide).

Source: WordNet

3. ADJ

/ dɪˈsaɪsɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈsaɪsɪv / (BrE)
so clear or obvious that it leaves no doubt or room for question; unmistakable or undeniable.
clear and undeniable

Examples

Her victory in the debate was secured by a decisive argument that everyone agreed with.
The team held a decisive lead going into the final quarter, making their win almost certain.
After years of uncertainty, there was finally a decisive move to resolve the issue.

Synset Examples

had a decisive lead in the polls

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unmistakable - impossible to mistake for anything else; very distinctive or obvious
  • Antonym: ambiguous - open to more than one interpretation; not having one obvious meaning

Etymology

From French *décisif*, from Medieval Latin *decisivus* (‘capable of deciding’), from Latin *decidere* (‘to decide’).

4. ADJ

/ dɪˈsaɪsɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈsaɪsɪv / (BrE)
Having or showing the ability to make decisions quickly and with firmness.
able to make decisions confidently

Examples

Her decisive actions during the emergency saved many lives.
The committee was impressed by his decisive leadership style.
A decisive manager is critical for a project's success.

Synset Examples

an able and decisive young woman
we needed decisive leadership
she gave him a decisive answer

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indecisive - not able to make decisions quickly or firmly
  • Synonym: resolute - admirably purposeful, determined, and unwavering

Etymology

From French *décisif*, based on Latin *decidere* (to decide).
