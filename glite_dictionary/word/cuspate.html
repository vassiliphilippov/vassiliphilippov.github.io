cuspate

1. ADJ

/ ˈkʌs.peɪt / (AmE), / ˈkʌs.peɪt / (BrE)
having cusps or points; shaped with projecting points or prominences, especially at the edge.
having cusps or pointed projections

Examples

The mollusk's shell is characterized by its cuspate edges, making it recognizable among other species.
The geologist studied the cuspate foreland formed by wave action along the coastline.
Some leaves have a distinct cuspate tip that helps in the identification of the plant species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cuspidate - having a sharp or prominent cusp or point

Etymology

From Latin 'cuspa' (a point, tip) + '-ate' (having).
