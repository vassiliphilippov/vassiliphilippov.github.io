disceptation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsɛpˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɛpˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a formal or contentious debate or dispute, especially involving strong disagreement
a formal or heated dispute

Examples

The philosophical society scheduled a disceptation between rival theories of consciousness.
Intense disceptation erupted among the committee members over the allocation of funds.
Her essay examined the role of public disceptation in resolving social conflicts.

Synset Examples

they were involved in a violent argument

Related Terms

  • Synonym: argument - a reasoned or heated exchange of views
  • Synonym: debate - a formal discussion on a particular topic
  • Antonym: agreement - harmony or accordance in opinion

Etymology

From Latin disceptatio (debate, disputation), from disceptare (to debate, dispute).
