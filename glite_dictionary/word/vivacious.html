vivacious

1. ADJ

/ vɪˈveɪʃəs / (AmE), / vɪˈveɪʃəs / (BrE)
(especially of a woman) attractively lively and animated in manner or appearance.
lively and animated (esp. of a woman)

Examples

Her vivacious personality made her the center of attention at every party.
The vivacious hostess greeted each guest with a bright smile and cheerful words.
Children were drawn to their teacher's vivacious energy during the school play.

Synset Examples

a vibrant group that challenged the system
a charming and vivacious hostess
a vivacious folk dance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lively - full of life and energy
  • Synonym: animated - full of life or excitement; lively
  • Antonym: dull - lacking energy or interest; not lively

Etymology

From Latin vivax, vivac- (long-lived, lively), from vivere (to live).
