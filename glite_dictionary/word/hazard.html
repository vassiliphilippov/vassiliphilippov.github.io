hazard

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæzərd / (AmE), / ˈhazəd / (BrE)
An unknown and unpredictable factor or chance event that influences the outcome of something.
unknown factor influencing events

Examples

Success in the lottery depends largely on hazard rather than skill.
Life is full of hazard; we never know what might happen next.
They attributed the surprise result to hazard rather than intention.

Synset Examples

bad luck caused his downfall
we ran into each other by pure chance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chance - the occurrence of events without apparent cause
  • Synonym: luck - success or failure apparently brought by chance
  • Antonym: certainty - something that is certain or definite

Etymology

From Old French hasard (game of chance), possibly from Arabic az-zahr (the dice).

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈhæzɚd / (AmE), / ˈhæzəd / (BrE)
An obstacle, such as a sand trap or water, on a golf course designed to make play more challenging.
obstacle on a golf course

Examples

The ball landed in the hazard, making the shot more difficult.
Golfers must carefully avoid each hazard on the course to keep a good score.
A water hazard runs along the left side of the ninth fairway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obstacle - something that blocks one's way or prevents progress

Etymology

From Old French *hasard* (game of chance), later applied to risks and obstacles.

3. NOUN

/ ˈhæzərd / (AmE), / ˈhæzəd / (BrE)
something that may cause harm or danger; a risk or source of potential misfortune.
a source of danger or risk

Examples

Smoking in bed is a serious hazard that can lead to house fires.
Worn-out electrical wiring can be a hidden hazard in old buildings.
Proper signage helps reduce the risk of workplace hazards.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: danger - the possibility of harm or injury
  • Synonym: risk - the possibility that something bad may happen
  • Antonym: safety - the condition of being protected from danger

Etymology

From Old French 'hasard', possibly from Arabic 'az-zahr' (the dice).
