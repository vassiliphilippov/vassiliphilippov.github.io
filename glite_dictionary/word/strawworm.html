strawworm

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrɔˌwɜrm / (AmE), / ˈstrɔːˌwɜːm / (BrE)
the larva of the caddis fly that constructs a protective case of silk covered with sand or plant material
caddis fly larva making case of debris

Examples

The strawworm uses tiny bits of debris to create a protective case for itself.
Scientists observed a strawworm at the bottom of the stream, carrying its camouflaged shelter.
As children, we liked searching for strawworms under stones in shallow rivers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caddisworm - another term for the larva of the caddis fly

2. NOUN

/ ˈstrɔˌwɜrm / (AmE), / ˈstrɔːˌwɜːm / (BrE)
The larva of certain chalcid flies that damages the straw of wheat and other grains.
larva damaging wheat and grain straw

Examples

Farmers often struggle with strawworm infestations during the wheat harvest.
The strawworm can cause significant damage to grain straw if left untreated.
Agricultural guides provide advice on controlling strawworm populations in cereal crops.
