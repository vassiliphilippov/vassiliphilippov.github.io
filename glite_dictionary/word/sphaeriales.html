Sphaeriales

1. NOUN

/ sfɪəriˈeɪliːz / (AmE), / sfɪəriˈeɪliːz / (BrE)
a large order of ascomycetous fungi characterized by dark, hard fruiting bodies with distinct openings; often divided among several orders in modern classifications
order of ascomycetous fungi

Examples

The family Xylariaceae was once placed within the Sphaeriales but has since been reclassified.
Research into the Sphaeriales has advanced our understanding of fungal taxonomy.
Some mycologists debate the distribution of genera previously included in the Sphaeriales.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Sphaeria (a genus of fungi) + -ales (denoting an order).
