ANO

1. NOUN

/ ˌeɪ ˌɛn ˈoʊ / (AmE), / ˌeɪ ˌɛn ˈəʊ / (BrE)
a Palestinian militant organization, also known as the Abu Nidal Organization, that broke away from the PLO in 1974 and was responsible for attacks in multiple countries.
Palestinian militant group; Abu Nidal Organization

Examples

The ANO claimed responsibility for several attacks during the 1980s.
International security agencies regarded the ANO as a major threat for decades.
The history of the ANO is closely tied to developments in the Middle East conflict.

Synset Examples

in the 1980s the Fatah-RC was considered the most dangerous and murderous Palestinian terror group

Etymology

Acronym for Abu Nidal Organization, named after its founder Abu Nidal.
