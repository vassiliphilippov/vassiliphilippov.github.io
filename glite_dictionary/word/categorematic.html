categorematic

1. ADJ

/ ˌkætɪˌɡɔːrəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌkætɪˌɡɒrəˈmætɪk / (BrE)
(Of a word or phrase) capable of standing alone as the subject or (especially) the predicate of a proposition, as opposed to syntactic elements that only function within a phrase.
able to stand alone as a term in logic

Examples

In formal logic, only categorematic terms can function as independent subjects or predicates in propositions.
Articles and conjunctions are not categorematic, but nouns and verbs usually are.
A categorematic word, like 'man' or 'walk', can hold meaning on its own in logical analysis.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: syncategorematic - (Of a word or phrase) not able to stand alone as a subject or predicate, only with other terms

Etymology

From Medieval Latin categorematicus, from Greek kategorēmatikós (affirmable).
