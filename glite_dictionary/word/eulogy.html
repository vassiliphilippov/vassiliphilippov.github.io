eulogy

1. NOUN

/ ˈjuːlədʒi / (AmE), / ˈjuːlədʒi / (BrE)
a speech or piece of writing praising someone, especially after their death
speech or text praising someone after death

Examples

The priest delivered a moving eulogy at the funeral service.
She wrote a heartfelt eulogy to honor her late mentor.
His eulogy highlighted the generosity and kindness of the deceased.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: panegyric - a public speech or published text in praise of someone or something
  • Synonym: obituary - a notice of a person's death, often with a brief biography
  • Antonym: criticism - the expression of disapproval or negative judgment

Etymology

From Greek eulogia (praise, good words), from eu- (well) + -logia (speaking).

2. NOUN

/ ˈjuːlədʒi / (AmE), / ˈjuːlədʒi / (BrE)
a speech or piece of writing that praises someone, especially one given after their death
speech or writing praising someone, often for the dead

Examples

The priest delivered a moving eulogy at the funeral service.
She wrote a beautiful eulogy to honor her late grandfather.
His touching eulogy captured the essence of his friend’s character.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: panegyric - formal public speech or written verse in praise of someone or something
  • Antonym: invective - speech or writing that criticizes or insults someone strongly

Etymology

From Greek 'eulogia' (praise, good words), via Latin 'eulogium'.
