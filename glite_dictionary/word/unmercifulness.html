unmercifulness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈmɜːrsɪflnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈmɜːsɪfnəs / (BrE)
The quality of lacking kindness or forgiveness; an unwillingness to show mercy.
lack of mercy or kindness

Examples

The dictator's unmercifulness was evident in his harsh punishments for even minor offenses.
Her unmercifulness during negotiations made the opposing team wary of future deals.
Literature often explores the consequences of unmercifulness among characters facing moral dilemmas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mercilessness - the quality of showing no mercy
  • Antonym: mercy - compassion or forgiveness shown toward someone

Etymology

From unmerciful + -ness, indicating the state or quality of being unmerciful.
