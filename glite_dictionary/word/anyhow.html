anyhow

1. ADV

/ ˈɛniˌhaʊ / (AmE), / ˈɛnihaʊ / (BrE)
in any way or manner; by whatever means possible
in any way or manner possible

Examples

They managed to finish the project anyhow, despite all the obstacles.
If you can't fix it properly, just try to do it anyhow.
He found a way to enter the event anyhow, even without a ticket.

Synset Examples

they came anyhow they could
get it done anyway you can

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anyway - in any manner or by any means

Etymology

From 'any' + 'how', formed in English in the mid-16th century.

2. ADV

/ ˈɛniˌhaʊ / (AmE), / ˈenihaʊ / (BrE)
Used to indicate that a statement supports or explains a previous statement; in any case.
in any case; as an explanation or support

Examples

Anyhow, she decided to go ahead with the plan despite their objections.
I'm not sure what went wrong; anyhow, the project didn't succeed.
He missed the train, but anyhow, he still arrived on time.

Synset Examples

Anyhow, he is dead now
I think they're asleep; anyhow, they're quiet
I don't know what happened to it; anyway, it's gone
anyway, there is another factor to consider
I don't know how it started; in any case, there was a brief scuffle
in any event, the government faced a serious protest
but at any rate he got a knighthood for it

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anyway - used to confirm or support a previous statement; in any case
  • Synonym: in any case - no matter what has happened or will happen
