chlorine

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɔːriːn / (AmE), / ˈklɔːriːn / (BrE)
a chemical element, a yellow-green toxic gas, used as a disinfectant and in bleaching, and found naturally in salts such as in sea water
a chemical element, a yellow-green toxic gas

Examples

Swimming pools are often treated with chlorine to keep the water clean and safe.
The strong smell of chlorine lingered in the air after disinfecting the lab.
Chlorine is a vital element in the production of many household cleaning products.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chloride - a compound of chlorine with another element or group

Etymology

From Greek 'khlōros' meaning 'greenish-yellow'.
