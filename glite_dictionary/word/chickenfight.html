to chickenfight

1. VERB

/ ˈtʃɪk.ən.faɪt / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪk.ɪn.faɪt / (BrE)
To engage in a contest or fight while sitting on someone else's shoulders, typically as a game.
to fight while on someone's shoulders
informal

Examples

Every summer, the kids chickenfight in the lake for hours at a time.
We used to chickenfight at pool parties, trying to knock each other over.
Last weekend, my friends and I chickenfought until we were too tired to stand.

Etymology

Formed from 'chicken' + 'fight', referring to a playful contest resembling a fight while on shoulders.
