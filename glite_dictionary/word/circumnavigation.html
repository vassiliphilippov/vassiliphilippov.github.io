circumnavigation

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɝː.kəmˌnæv.ɪˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɜː.kəmˌnæv.ɪˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of traveling all the way around something, such as the Earth, especially by ship or airplane
traveling around something completely

Examples

The first successful circumnavigation of the world was completed by Magellan's crew in the 16th century.
Her solo circumnavigation of the globe inspired adventurers around the world.
Advances in technology have made circumnavigation by air much faster than by sea.

Synset Examples

Magellan's circumnavigation of the earth proved that it is a globe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: navigation - the process of planning and directing a course for travel, especially at sea
  • Synonym: to circumnavigate - to travel all the way around something, especially the Earth

Etymology

From Latin 'circumnavigatio', from 'circum' (around) + 'navigatio' (sailing).
