beluga

1. NOUN

/ bəˈluːɡə / (AmE), / bɪˈluːɡə / (BrE)
A small white whale found in Arctic and sub-Arctic waters, known for its distinctive color and lack of a dorsal fin.
white Arctic whale

Examples

We saw a beluga swimming near the edge of the ice floe.
The beluga is easily recognized by its white color and rounded forehead.
Many belugas migrate long distances each year in search of food.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: narwhal - an Arctic whale with a long spiral tusk
  • Synonym: orca - a large black-and-white predatory whale, killer whale

Etymology

From Russian 'beluga', from 'belyi' meaning 'white'.

2. NOUN

/ bəˈluːɡə / (AmE), / bɪˈluːɡə / (BrE)
a large sturgeon (Huso huso) found in the Black and Caspian seas, valued for its roe used as caviar and its isinglass
large sturgeon providing caviar

Examples

The beluga is prized for producing the most expensive variety of caviar.
Fishing for beluga has been restricted to protect this endangered species.
The roe of the beluga sturgeon is considered a delicacy around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sturgeon - a large fish; several species are sources of caviar

Etymology

From Russian белуга (beluga), meaning 'white', from белый (belyy) 'white', referring to the pale coloration.
