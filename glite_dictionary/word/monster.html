monster

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑnstɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɒnstə / (BrE)
an imaginary large and frightening creature, often combining parts of humans and animals
imaginary large frightening creature

Examples

The children were convinced a monster was hiding under the bed.
Mythical stories often feature a terrifying monster with fangs and claws.
The film's villain was a giant, fire-breathing monster that destroyed the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beast - a large, wild animal or imaginary creature
  • Synonym: creature - a being of any type, real or imaginary

Etymology

From Latin monstrum (divine omen, monster), from monere (to warn).
