shinplaster

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɪnˌplæstər / (AmE), / ˈʃɪnˌplɑːstə / (BrE)
A piece of paper money of little value, especially one issued with insufficient security; commonly used in the 19th century.
low-value, insecure paper money
archaic

Examples

During economic crises, many small businesses paid employees with shinplasters instead of coins.
Collectors often value shinplasters as relics of early American finance.
The government stopped issuing shinplaster notes when their value became unreliable.

Etymology

Originally American English, from the use of similar paper as a protective dressing for bruises on the shins.
