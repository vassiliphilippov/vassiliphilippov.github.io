vasosection

1. NOUN

/ ˌvæsoʊˈsɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌvasəʊˈsɛkʃən / (BrE)
A surgical incision into or division of the vas deferens, typically as a method of sterilization.
surgical division of the vas deferens
medical

Examples

The surgeon recommended a vasosection as a permanent form of contraception.
A vasosection is often performed under local anesthesia in outpatient procedures.
There are risks and benefits to consider before undergoing a vasosection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vasectomy - the surgical cutting and sealing of part of each vas deferens, especially as a means of sterilization

Etymology

From Latin 'vas' (vessel) and 'section' (cutting).
