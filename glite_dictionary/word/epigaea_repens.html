Epigaea repens

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈdʒiə rɪˈpɛnz / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈdʒiːə rɪˈpɛnz / (BrE)
A low-growing evergreen shrub native to eastern North America, with leathery leaves and clusters of fragrant pink or white flowers.
a North American evergreen shrub with fragrant flowers

Examples

In early spring, the Epigaea repens bursts into bloom, filling the forest floor with a sweet fragrance.
Botanists were excited to discover a healthy patch of Epigaea repens along the woodland trail.
The fragrant pink flowers of Epigaea repens make it a sought-after plant for native gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trailing arbutus - another name for Epigaea repens
  • Synonym: Mayflower - another common name for Epigaea repens

Etymology

Genus name from Greek epi (upon) + gaia (earth, ground); species epithet from Latin repens (creeping).
