Phalaenoptilus

1. NOUN

/ fəˌliːnəmpˈtɪləs / (AmE), / fəˌliːnəmˈtɪləs / (BrE)
A genus of nightjars in the family Caprimulgidae, including the common poorwill.
genus of nightjars (birds)

Examples

The genus Phalaenoptilus includes only one extant species, the common poorwill.
Researchers observed the behavior of Phalaenoptilus in its natural desert habitat.
Birdwatchers are often excited to spot a Phalaenoptilus during their nocturnal surveys.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: common poorwill - a species of nightjar (Phalaenoptilus nuttallii) found in North America
  • Degree Higher: Caprimulgidae - the family of birds known as nightjars

Etymology

From Greek phalaina (moth) + ptilon (feather).

a genus of birds in the nightjar family (Caprimulgidae), containing the common poorwill.
genus of nightjars (bird)

Examples

The Phalaenoptilus genus is best known for the common poorwill, a nocturnal bird of North America.
Researchers discovered a new behavioral trait in Phalaenoptilus species, particularly in their nesting habits.
Conservation efforts focus on protecting habitats crucial to Phalaenoptilus populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: poorwill - a nocturnal bird species in North America

Etymology

From Greek phalaina (moth) + ptilon (feather), reflecting the moth-like appearance of the bird's plumage.
