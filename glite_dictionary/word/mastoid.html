mastoid

1. ADJ

/ ˈmæs.tɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈmas.tɔɪd / (BrE)
relating to or situated in the region of the mastoid process of the temporal bone
relating to the mastoid process (anatomy)
medical

Examples

The doctor examined the mastoid area for signs of tenderness or swelling.
Infections such as mastoiditis can affect the mastoid bone behind the ear.
A mastoid abscess may require surgical intervention.

Etymology

From Greek mastoeidēs, from mastos (breast) + -oeidēs (form, shape).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmæstɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈmæstɔɪd / (BrE)
the prominent bone behind the ear, forming part of the temporal bone at the base of the skull
bony prominence behind the ear
medical

Examples

The doctor checked the area over the mastoid for any signs of tenderness or swelling.
Infection of the mastoid can lead to a serious medical condition called mastoiditis.
The surgeon made a small incision behind the mastoid to access the inner ear.

Etymology

From Greek 'mastoeidēs', meaning 'breast-shaped', referring to the shape of the bone.

3. ADJ

/ ˈmæstɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈmæstɔɪd / (BrE)
(anatomy) Relating to or resembling a nipple; having a nipple-like shape or structure, especially in reference to the mastoid process of the temporal bone.
nipple-like or relating to the mastoid process (anatomy)
medical

Examples

The mastoid process is located just behind the human ear.
Severe infections can sometimes spread to the mastoid area, requiring medical intervention.
The surgeon carefully examined the mastoid bone during the procedure.

Etymology

From Greek 'mastoides', from 'mastós' (breast, nipple) + '-oidēs' (form, shape).

Synset POS: a
