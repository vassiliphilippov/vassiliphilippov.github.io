lira

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪr.ə / (AmE), / ˈlɪə.rə / (BrE)
the basic monetary unit of Turkey
Turkish currency unit

Examples

She exchanged her dollars for Turkish lira before traveling to Istanbul.
The price of lunch was 25 lira at the local restaurant.
In recent years, the value of the lira has fluctuated significantly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Turkish lira - currency of Turkey since 2005, code TRY

Etymology

From Italian lira, from Latin libra (pound).

2. NOUN

/ ˈliːrə / (AmE), / ˈlɪərə / (BrE)
the former basic unit of currency in Italy, equal to 100 centesimi, used before the adoption of the euro
former Italian unit of currency

Examples

My grandmother still keeps a few old lira coins in a drawer for memories.
Before 2002, the price of a coffee in Rome was a few thousand lira.
Tourists used to find it confusing to convert their dollars to lira when traveling in Italy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: euro - the current unit of currency in Italy and many European countries

Etymology

From Italian lira, from Latin libra (pound, unit of weight).

3. NOUN

/ ˈliːrə / (AmE), / ˈlɪərə / (BrE)
the former basic unit of currency in Malta, equal to 100 cents, used before the adoption of the euro.
former currency of Malta

Examples

The Maltese lira was replaced by the euro in 2008.
He exchanged his liri for euros before leaving Malta.
Many older price tags can still be found showing amounts in lira.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: euro - the official currency of Malta since 2008

Etymology

From Italian lira, derived from Latin libra (pound, balance).
