drunk

1. ADJ

/ drʌŋk / (AmE), / drʌŋk / (BrE)
Feeling or appearing as if under the influence of alcohol; overwhelmed by a strong emotion or sensation.
as if under the influence; overwhelmed

Examples

He was so drunk with joy that he couldn't stop smiling all evening.
After reading the good news, she felt drunk with excitement.
The crowd was drunk with enthusiasm during the final match.

Synset Examples

felt intoxicated by her success
drunk with excitement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intoxicated - emotionally or physically affected as by alcohol; overwhelmed by emotion

Etymology

Past participle of drink, from Old English drincan, related to Old Norse drikka and German trinken.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00923501-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ drʌŋk / (AmE), / drʌŋk / (BrE)
A person who is intoxicated by alcohol; someone who is drunk.
a person who is intoxicated by alcohol

Examples

The drunk in the alley was singing loudly as people walked by.
Some people avoid bars because they don't like dealing with a drunk.
After the party, he was stumbling like a drunk down the street.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inebriate - a person who is drunk
  • Antonym: sober - not affected by alcohol; not drunk

Etymology

Past participle of 'drink', from Old English *druncen*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10056805-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ drʌŋk / (AmE), / drʌŋk / (BrE)
A person who habitually drinks excessive amounts of alcohol; a chronic drunkard.
habitual heavy drinker

Examples

The local bar is always full of drunks late at night.
Everyone knew him as the town drunk who spent his days at the pub.
She refused to date a drunk after her last relationship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alcoholic - a person addicted to alcohol
  • Antonym: teetotaler - a person who abstains from alcohol

Etymology

From Old English 'druncen', past participle of 'drincan' (to drink).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10057110-n
Synset POS: n

4. ADJ

/ drʌŋk / (AmE), / drʌŋk / (BrE)
Having consumed so much alcohol that normal physical and mental control is impaired; intoxicated.
intoxicated by alcohol

Examples

He was clearly drunk when he arrived at the party and could barely speak.
After a few glasses of wine, she started to feel drunk and giggled at everything.
The bar refused to serve anyone who appeared drunk or belligerent.

Synset Examples

a noisy crowd of intoxicated sailors
helplessly inebriated

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sober - not affected by alcohol; not drunk
  • Synonym: inebriated - affected by alcohol; intoxicated
  • Synonym: intoxicated - under the influence of alcohol or drugs

Etymology

Past participle of 'drink', from Old English 'druncen', past participle of 'drincan' (to drink).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00800854-a
Synset POS: a
