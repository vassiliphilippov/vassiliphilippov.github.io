abducting

1. ADJ

/ əbˈdʌktɪŋ / (AmE), / əbˈdʌktɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a muscle) causing movement of a body part away from the midline or from another part.
drawing body part away from midline (anatomy)
medical

Examples

The abducting muscles of the hip are vital for stable walking.
When the abducting nerve is damaged, eye movement away from the nose is impaired.
Physical therapy focused on strengthening the abducting muscles to restore shoulder function.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: adducting - (of a muscle) causing movement of a body part toward the midline

Etymology

From Latin 'abducere' (to lead away), with the present participle 'abducting.'
