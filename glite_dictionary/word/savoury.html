2. NOUN

/ ˈseɪvəri / (AmE), / ˈseɪvəri / (BrE)
Either of two aromatic herbs of the mint family, used as a culinary seasoning: summer savoury (Satureja hortensis) or winter savoury (Satureja montana).
aromatic herb used for seasoning

Examples

The recipe called for a pinch of savoury to enhance the flavor of the stew.
She grows both summer and winter savoury in her herb garden.
Dried savoury is often added to bean dishes for extra aroma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sage - aromatic herb of the mint family used in cooking

Etymology

From Old French 'savoure' (pleasing to the taste), from Latin 'sapor' (taste).

3. ADJ

/ ˈseɪvəri / (AmE), / ˈseɪvəri / (BrE)
Having a pleasantly spicy or salty taste rather than a sweet one.
pleasantly spicy or salty (taste)

Examples

The chef prepared a savoury sauce that perfectly complemented the grilled chicken.
She prefers savoury snacks like crackers and cheese over sweet treats.
The aroma of savoury spices filled the kitchen as the stew simmered on the stove.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sweet - having the taste characteristic of sugar or honey

Etymology

From Old French 'savouré', from Latin 'sapor' (taste, flavor).

4. ADJ

/ ˈseɪvəri / (AmE), / ˈseɪvəri / (BrE)
(of food) having a pleasant, often salty or spicy, taste rather than a sweet one.
pleasantly salty or spicy (not sweet)

Examples

The soup had a wonderfully savoury flavor that everyone enjoyed.
She prefers savoury snacks like cheese and crackers over sweet treats.
A savoury aroma filled the kitchen as the stew simmered on the stove.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sweet - having a taste similar to sugar or honey; the opposite of savoury (in food)

From Old French 'savoré', from 'savor' (taste, flavor), from Latin 'sapor'.

5. NOUN

/ ˈseɪvəri / (AmE), / ˈseɪvəri / (BrE)
A small aromatic or spicy dish served at the end of a meal or as an hors d'oeuvre, especially in British cuisine.
a spicy or aromatic small dish
british

Examples

At formal dinners, a savoury is sometimes served after dessert.
She prepared a delicious savoury of mushrooms on toast as a starter.
The restaurant's signature savoury features smoked salmon and dill.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sweet - a dish or course having a sugary taste, often served as dessert
  • Synonym: appetizer - a small dish served before the main meal to stimulate the appetite

Etymology

From Old French 'savore', from Latin 'sapor' (taste, flavor).
