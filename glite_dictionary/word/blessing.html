blessing

1. NOUN

/ ˈblɛsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈblɛsɪŋ / (BrE)
A beneficial or fortunate state or thing that brings happiness or well-being.
something beneficial or fortunate

Examples

Winning the scholarship was a real blessing for her family.
The rain was a blessing after months of drought.
He considers his good health a blessing every day.

Synset Examples

enjoy the blessings of peace
a spanking breeze is a boon to sailors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boon - a thing that is helpful or beneficial
  • Antonym: curse - something that causes harm or misery

Etymology

From Old English 'blēdsian' (to consecrate, make holy).

2. NOUN

/ ˈblɛsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈblɛsɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of asking God or a higher power for help, protection, or favor, typically through prayer
the act of praying for divine help or protection

Examples

The priest gave his blessing before the meal began.
She knelt in silence, offering a blessing for her daughter's safety.
Many families say a blessing at the start of their evening meal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benediction - a prayer asking for divine blessing, especially at the end of a religious service

Etymology

From Old English 'blētsian' (to consecrate or make holy), related to 'blōd' (blood).

Examples
