Rheum palmatum

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːəm ˌpælməˈtʌm / (AmE), / ˈruːəm ˌpalməˈtʌm / (BrE)
a species of rhubarb native to Asia, traditionally used in herbal medicine for its laxative properties
an Asian species of rhubarb (medicinal plant)
medical

Examples

Rheum palmatum is commonly used in traditional Chinese medicine for digestive issues.
The large, hand-shaped leaves of Rheum palmatum make it distinct from the common garden rhubarb.
Researchers are studying extracts from Rheum palmatum for their potential medicinal benefits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rhubarb - common name for several plants of the Rheum genus

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Rheum' is the genus name (from Greek 'rheon', rhubarb) and 'palmatum' means 'hand-shaped', referring to the leaves.
