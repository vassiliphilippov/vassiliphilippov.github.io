kidney

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪdni / (AmE), / ˈkɪdni / (BrE)
either of the two bean-shaped organs in the body that filter waste products from the blood and produce urine
bean-shaped organ that filters blood

Examples

Regular exercise contributes to healthy kidney function and overall wellbeing.
The doctor explained that the kidney helps remove toxins from the bloodstream.
She donated a kidney to her brother, saving his life.

Synset Examples

urine passes out of the kidney through ureters to the bladder

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liver - large organ involved in metabolism and detoxification in the body

Etymology

From Middle English 'kidenei', of uncertain origin, possibly from Old English 'cwið' (womb) + 'ey' (egg).
