bdellium

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛliəm / (AmE), / ˈdɛliəm / (BrE)
an aromatic gum resin obtained from certain trees, similar to myrrh, used in perfumes and incense
aromatic gum resin, like myrrh

Examples

The ancient text describes the use of bdellium in religious ceremonies.
Traders valued bdellium for its aromatic qualities and medicinal uses.
The scent of bdellium filled the air as the incense burned.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: myrrh - an aromatic gum resin used in perfumes and incense

Etymology

From Latin 'bdellium', from Greek 'bdellion', of Semitic origin.
