permeant

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɜːr.mi.ənt / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.mi.ənt / (BrE)
spreading or tending to spread throughout every part of a substance or area; able to penetrate or diffuse through.
spreading throughout; able to penetrate

Examples

The permeant aroma of spices lingered in every corner of the kitchen.
Fear became a permeant aspect of daily life during the crisis.
Some chemicals act as permeant agents, diffusing easily through membranes.

Synset Examples

armed with permeative irony … he punctures affectations
the pervasive odor of garlic
an error is pervasive if it is material to more than one conclusion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pervasive - spreading widely throughout an area or group
  • Synonym: permeable - allowing liquids or gases to pass through

Etymology

From Latin permeare (to pass through), related to 'permeate'.
