millimetre

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪlɪˌmiːtər / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪˌmiːtə / (BrE)
a metric unit of length equal to one thousandth of a metre
metric unit of length (1/1000 metre)
british

Examples

The insect measured just five millimetres long.
Rainfall increased by ten millimetres overnight in the city.
This ruler is marked in millimetres for precise measurements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: millimeter - American spelling of 'millimetre'
  • Degree Higher: centimetre - metric unit of length equal to one hundredth of a metre
  • Degree Lower: micrometre - metric unit of length equal to one millionth of a metre

Etymology

From French 'millimètre', from 'milli-' meaning 'thousandth' and 'mètre' (metre).
