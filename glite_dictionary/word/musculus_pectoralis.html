musculus pectoralis

1. NOUN

/ ˌmʌs.kjʊ.ləs ˌpɛk.təˈreɪ.lɪs / (AmE), / ˌmʌs.kjʊ.ləs ˌpɛk.təˈreɪ.lɪs / (BrE)
either of two large muscles located on the chest, connecting the front walls of the chest with the bones of the upper arm and shoulder
large chest muscle
medical

Examples

Damage to the musculus pectoralis can significantly limit arm movement.
The surgeon carefully examined the patient's musculus pectoralis before proceeding with the operation.
Anatomy students must learn to identify the musculus pectoralis on cadavers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pectoralis muscle - either of the two large muscles covering the chest, also known as 'pectoralis major' or 'pectoralis minor'

Etymology

From Latin 'musculus' (muscle) and 'pectoralis' (of the chest).
