headlinese

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdˌlaɪniz / (AmE), / ˈhɛdlaɪniːz / (BrE)
the distinctive, abbreviated style of language used in newspaper headlines, characterized by short words, omissions, and condensed grammar
abbreviated style used in news headlines

Examples

Many newspapers use headlinese to grab readers' attention quickly.
Learning headlinese can help editors fit news into limited space.
His writing struggled to break out of the terse patterns of headlinese.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: journalese - a style of language used by journalists that can be clichéd or inflated

Etymology

Formed from 'headline' + '-ese', imitating the style-naming suffix as in 'journalese'.
