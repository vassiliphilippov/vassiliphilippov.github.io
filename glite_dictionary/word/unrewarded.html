unrewarded

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnrɪˈwɔrdɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnrɪˈwɔːdɪd / (BrE)
having not received a reward, benefit, or acknowledgment for efforts or actions
not having received a reward or benefit

Examples

Her years of hard work went unrewarded, but she remained committed to her cause.
The experiment was difficult, but success was not guaranteed, so some efforts were unrewarded.
Many volunteers offered help unrewarded, seeking only the satisfaction of assisting others.

Synset Examples

they returned from the negotiations empty-handed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rewarded - having received a reward or benefit

Etymology

From un- (not) + rewarded, from reward (Middle English, from Old French 'rewarder', to regard, to reward).
