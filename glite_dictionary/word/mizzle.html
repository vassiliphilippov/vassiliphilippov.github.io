mizzle

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪz.əl / (AmE), / ˈmɪz.əl / (BrE)
Very light rain that is heavier than mist but lighter than a shower.
very light rain

Examples

A light mizzle made the streets damp but didn't require an umbrella.
The early morning mizzle created a soft haze over the hills.
We walked through the mizzle, barely getting wet from the fine rain.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: drizzle - light rain with small, fine drops
  • Degree Lower: mist - suspension of tiny droplets of water in the air

Etymology

From Middle English 'myselyng' (drizzling), likely related to 'mist' and 'drizzle'.

2. VERB

/ ˈmɪzəl / (AmE), / ˈmɪzəl / (BrE)
(of rain) to fall lightly in very fine drops; to drizzle lightly.
rain lightly; drizzle

Examples

It began to mizzle just as we reached the park, so we opened our umbrellas.
Yesterday afternoon, it mizzled for hours without ever turning into a proper rain.
Clouds gathered and soon it was mizzling over the quiet village street.

Synset Examples

When it drizzles in summer, hiking can be pleasant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drizzle - to rain lightly in very fine drops

Etymology

Perhaps from a blend of 'mist' and 'drizzle', or from Middle English 'misellen' (to make misty).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
