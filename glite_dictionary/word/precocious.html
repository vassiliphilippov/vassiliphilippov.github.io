precocious

1. ADJ

/ prɪˈkoʊʃəs / (AmE), / prɪˈkəʊʃəs / (BrE)
Appearing or developing earlier than is typical; unusually early in development or occurrence.
appearing or maturing early

Examples

The child's precocious ability to read astonished her teachers.
In some species, precocious flowering occurs even before the first leaves appear.
His precocious talent for mathematics was evident at a very young age.

Synset Examples

precocious flowers appear before the leaves as in some species of magnolias

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advanced - far ahead in development or progress
  • Antonym: belated - coming or happening later than expected

Etymology

From Latin praecox, praecocis (early ripening, premature).

2. ADJ

/ prɪˈkoʊʃəs / (AmE), / prɪˈkəʊʃəs / (BrE)
(of a child or their abilities) having developed certain abilities or inclinations at an earlier age than usual, especially showing mental maturity.
having abilities or maturity unusually early

Examples

The precocious child could read novels by the age of five.
Her precocious talent for music impressed her teachers and family alike.
He made a precocious decision to start his own business while still in high school.

Synset Examples

a precocious child
a precocious achievement

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: mature - showing the mental or emotional qualities of an adult

Etymology

From Latin praecox, praecocis (early ripening, premature).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01845744-a
Synset POS: a
