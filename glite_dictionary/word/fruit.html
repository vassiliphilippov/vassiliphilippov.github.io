fruit

1. NOUN

/ frut / (AmE), / fruːt / (BrE)
the result or outcome of an action or effort
result or product of action

Examples

After years of hard work, she finally enjoyed the fruit of her labor.
The reforms began to bear fruit after several years.
He is determined to see the fruit of his dedication.

Synset Examples

he lived long enough to see the fruit of his policies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: result - something that happens as a consequence; outcome
  • Synonym: reward - something given or received in return for effort

Etymology

From Old French 'fruit', from Latin 'fructus' (enjoyment, produce, fruit).

2. VERB

/ fruːt / (AmE), / fruːt / (BrE)
(of a plant or tree) to produce or cause to produce fruit.
to produce or cause to bear fruit

Examples

If apple trees are pruned correctly, they will fruit more abundantly each year.
The greenhouse conditions encouraged the plants to fruit earlier than usual.
With proper care, even young vines can be made to fruit in their second season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flower - to produce flowers

3. NOUN

/ frut / (AmE), / fruːt / (BrE)
The soft, edible, ripened reproductive body of a seed plant, typically sweet or sour in taste and often eaten as food.
edible ripened part of a plant

Examples

You should eat a piece of fruit with your breakfast every morning.
The apple tree bore a lot of fruit this year.
Bananas, oranges, and grapes are all types of fruit.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vegetable - edible part of a plant typically not sweet, such as roots or leaves

Etymology

From Old French 'fruit', from Latin 'fructus' (enjoyment, fruit), from 'frui' (to enjoy).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13155706-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ fruːt / (AmE), / fruːt / (BrE)
(of a plant or tree) to produce fruit as part of its natural cycle.
(of plants) to produce fruit

Examples

The apple trees finally fruited after several years of careful cultivation.
This species of cactus only fruits once every five years.
If the conditions are right, the plant will fruit abundantly in late summer.

Synset Examples

the trees fruited early this year

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flower - (of a plant) to produce flowers

Etymology

From Old French 'fruit', from Latin 'fructus' (enjoyment, produce), from 'frui' (to enjoy).
