biface

1. ADJ

/ ˈbaɪfeɪs / (AmE), / ˈbaɪfeɪs / (BrE)
having two faces or fronts; involving two surfaces or sides
having two faces or surfaces

Examples

The archaeologist discovered a biface stone tool at the excavation site.
The sculpture was designed as a biface piece, allowing viewers to appreciate it from both sides.
Ancient coins are sometimes biface, showing different images on each face.

Synset Examples

the Roman Janus is bifacial

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bifacial - having two faces or surfaces

Etymology

From Latin 'bi-' (two) + 'facies' (face).
