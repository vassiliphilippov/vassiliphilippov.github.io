shooting

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃuːtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃuːtɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of firing a gun or other projectile weapon
act of firing a weapon

Examples

The shooting started suddenly, causing everyone to duck for cover.
His accuracy improved with every shooting session at the range.
There was a loud shooting outside the stadium, alarming bystanders.

Synset Examples

his shooting was slow but accurate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: firing - the act of discharging a firearm

Etymology

From Middle English 'sheten' (to shoot) + -ing (forming action nouns).

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃuː.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃuː.tɪŋ / (BrE)
An incident in which someone is killed or injured by gunfire.
the act of killing or injuring by gunfire

Examples

There was a shooting outside the restaurant last night that shocked the entire neighborhood.
Police are investigating the shooting that left two teenagers injured.
After the shooting, the area was cordoned off while evidence was collected.

Synset Examples

when the shooting stopped there were three dead bodies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gunfire - the act of firing guns
  • Antonym: stabbing - an incident in which someone is wounded with a knife

Etymology

From the verb 'shoot', of Old English origin, related to Middle Low German 'schōten'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00226017-n
Synset POS: n
