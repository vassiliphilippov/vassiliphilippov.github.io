to interstratify

1. VERB

/ ˌɪntərˈstrætəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈstrætɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to arrange or be arranged in alternating layers or strata, especially of rocks or sediments
arrange in alternating layers (geology)

Examples

Several sedimentary deposits in the region are interstratified with layers of volcanic ash.
The geologist explained how different mineral beds interstratify over millions of years.
Clay and silt often interstratify during the formation of sedimentary rocks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stratify - to form or arrange in layers or strata

Etymology

From 'inter-' meaning 'between' and 'stratify' from Latin 'stratum' (layer).
