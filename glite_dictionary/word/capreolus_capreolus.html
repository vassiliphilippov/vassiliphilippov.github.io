Capreolus capreolus

1. NOUN

/ kæpˈriːələs kæpˈriːələs / (AmE), / kæpˈriːələs kæprɪˈəʊləs / (BrE)
A small, graceful deer native to Eurasian woodlands, known for its reddish coat and small forked antlers; also called the European roe deer.
small Eurasian deer, European roe deer

Examples

Wild Capreolus capreolus can often be observed grazing at the edge of European forests.
The distinctive forked antlers help identify a male Capreolus capreolus in the wild.
Conservation efforts in the region have led to a rise in the Capreolus capreolus population.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roe deer - common name for Capreolus capreolus

Etymology

From Latin, where 'capreolus' means 'little goat' or 'roe deer'.
