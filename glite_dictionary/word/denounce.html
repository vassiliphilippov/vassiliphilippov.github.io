to denounce

1. VERB

/ dɪˈnaʊns / (AmE), / dɪˈnaʊns / (BrE)
to publicly criticize or condemn someone or something, especially in a formal way
publicly criticize or condemn

Examples

Several human rights groups chose to denounce the policy as discriminatory and unfair.
She courageously denounced the corruption she had witnessed within the organization.
After the scandal, many people started denouncing the company in public forums.

Synset Examples

He denounced the government action
She was stigmatized by society because she had a child out of wedlock

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to condemn - to express strong disapproval of
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration of

Etymology

From Old French 'denoncier', from Latin 'denuntiare' (to announce, warn).

2. VERB

/ dɪˈnaʊns / (AmE), / dɪˈnaʊns / (BrE)
to publicly criticize or condemn someone or something strongly and openly
publicly criticize or condemn

Examples

The politician denounced corruption in the government during his speech.
Many activists denounce animal cruelty in public protests.
She bravely denounced the unfair treatment of her coworkers.

Synset Examples

He denounced the Nazis

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to condemn - to express complete disapproval of, typically in public
  • Antonym: praise - to express warm approval or admiration of someone or something

Etymology

From Old French 'denoncier' (to proclaim, announce), from Latin 'denuntiare' (to announce, warn).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00843259-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ dɪˈnaʊns / (AmE), / dɪˈnaʊns / (BrE)
to publicly give information about someone, especially to authorities, often exposing them to punishment
inform against; betray someone to authorities

Examples

After being pressured by the authorities, he finally denounced his former accomplice.
She refused to denounce her colleagues despite repeated threats.
During the interrogation, the suspect denounced several members of the group to the police.

Synset Examples

He told on his classmate who had cheated on the exam

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to betray - to give information about someone that puts them at risk, especially to authorities
  • Synonym: to expose - to make known something hidden, especially wrongdoing
  • Antonym: to protect - to keep someone safe from harm or exposure

Etymology

From Old French 'denoncier', from Latin 'denuntiare' (to announce, warn formally).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00843665-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ dɪˈnaʊns / (AmE), / dɪˈnaʊns / (BrE)
to announce the formal termination of an agreement or treaty
formally declare end of treaty or agreement

Examples

The country decided to denounce the long-standing trade agreement.
After repeated violations, the state formally denounced the treaty.
The government plans to denounce all bilateral pacts that no longer serve its interests.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to terminate - bring to an end, especially officially
  • Synonym: to abrogate - repeal or do away with a law, right, or formal agreement

Etymology

From Old French 'denoncier', from Latin 'denuntiare' (to proclaim, announce, denounce).
