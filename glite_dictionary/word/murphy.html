murphy

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɜːrfi / (AmE), / ˈmɜːfi / (BrE)
A regional or dialect word for a potato, especially used in Ireland and some parts of Britain.
regional term for potato
british

Examples

He grew murphys in his garden every year.
Would you like some boiled murphys with your dinner?
In old Irish tales, the murphy is a symbol of rural life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: potato - an edible tuber widely cultivated as a staple food

Etymology

Of uncertain origin, possibly from the surname 'Murphy', stereotypically associated with Irish people and thus, by extension, with potatoes.
