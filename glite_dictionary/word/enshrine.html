to enshrine

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈʃraɪn / (AmE), / ɪnˈʃraɪn / (BrE)
to regard or treat as sacred and inviolable; to preserve with great respect or reverence
hold as sacred or highly respected

Examples

The constitution enshrines the fundamental rights of all citizens.
Ancient traditions are enshrined in the rituals of this festival.
Her memory was enshrined in the hearts of those who loved her.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deify - to worship or regard as a god
  • Antonym: profane - to treat with irreverence or disrespect

Etymology

From en- (make) + shrine; originally meaning 'to place in a shrine'.

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈʃraɪn / (AmE), / ɪnˈʃraɪn / (BrE)
to enclose or preserve in a shrine, especially as an object of reverence; to preserve or cherish as sacred
to enclose or preserve as sacred

Examples

The relic was enshrined in the temple for centuries.
Many rights are enshrined in the country's constitution.
Her achievements are enshrined in the memories of her students.

Synset Examples

the saint's bones were enshrined in the cathedral

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to entomb - to place in a tomb, typically as a form of reverence or burial

Etymology

From en- (cause to be) + shrine, Middle English 'schrine', from Old French 'escrin'.
