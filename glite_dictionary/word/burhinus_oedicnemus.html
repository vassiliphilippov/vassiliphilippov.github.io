Burhinus oedicnemus

1. NOUN

/ bɝːˈhaɪnəs ˌiːdɪkˈniːməs / (AmE), / bɜːˈhɪnəs ˌiːdɪkˈniːməs / (BrE)
A species of large-headed, large-eyed shorebird, active at dawn and dusk or at night, found in the Old World and parts of tropical America, notable for its thickened knee joint; also known as the Eurasian stone-curlew.
species of nocturnal shorebird (Eurasian stone-curlew)
medical

Examples

Birdwatchers spotted a Burhinus oedicnemus foraging on the gravelly riverbank at dusk.
Conservation groups are studying the habitat of Burhinus oedicnemus across Europe.
The Burhinus oedicnemus is easily identified by its thick-kneed legs and large yellow eyes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eurasian stone-curlew - Common name for Burhinus oedicnemus, a nocturnal shorebird

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Burhinus' (from Greek 'bous' meaning ox and 'rhinos' meaning nose/beak), and species 'oedicnemus' (from Greek 'oidēma' swelling and 'knēmē' shin, referring to the swollen knee joint).
