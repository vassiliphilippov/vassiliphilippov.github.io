go-ahead

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊ əˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊ əˌhɛd / (BrE)
an official signal, permission, or instruction to begin or continue something
permission or signal to proceed

Examples

The project finally received the go-ahead from the board.
We can't start construction until we get the official go-ahead.
She gave me the go-ahead to start the new marketing campaign.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: green light - an official signal or permission to proceed
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06887441-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡoʊ əˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊ əˌhɛd / (BrE)
Eagerness or readiness to start new projects or undertake bold new ventures.
readiness to start new ventures

Examples

She has the go-ahead needed to lead the team into new territory.
Their go-ahead attitude helped the company grow rapidly.
Without a little go-ahead, innovative ideas are rarely put into action.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enterprise - willingness to initiate new projects
  • Antonym: timidity - lack of courage or initiative to undertake new ventures
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
