interchange

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪntərˈtʃeɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈɪntəˌtʃeɪndʒ / (BrE)
the act of exchanging one thing for another or the process of being exchanged
the act of exchanging things

Examples

The interchange of gifts is a common tradition during the holidays.
There was a lively interchange of ideas at the meeting.
An interchange of prisoners was arranged between the two countries.

Synset Examples

Adam was promised immortality in exchange for his disobedience
there was an interchange of prisoners

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exchange - the act of giving one thing and receiving another

Etymology

From Latin 'intercambium', from 'inter-' (between) + 'cambium' (exchange).

Source: WordNet
