Carassius vulgaris

1. NOUN

/ kəˈræsiəs vʌlˈɡɛərɪs / (AmE), / kəˈrasɪəs vʌlˈɡɑːrɪs / (BrE)
A species of European carp closely resembling the wild goldfish, often found in freshwater ponds and lakes.
a species of European carp

Examples

The pond was stocked with several Carassius vulgaris to create a natural ecosystem.
Researchers studied the differences in fin shape between Carassius vulgaris and domesticated goldfish.
Some anglers mistakenly refer to Carassius vulgaris as wild goldfish due to their similar appearance.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: goldfish - a small, usually orange ornamental fish

Etymology

From Latin: genus name Carassius, species epithet vulgaris meaning 'common'.
