Eptesicus serotinus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpˈtɛsɪkəs səˈrɒtɪnəs / (AmE), / ˌɛptɪˈsiːkəs ˌsɛrəʊˈtaɪnəs / (BrE)
A species of brown bat commonly found throughout Europe.
species of European brown bat

Examples

The Eptesicus serotinus is often seen hunting insects in rural European areas at dusk.
Researchers studied the roosting habits of Eptesicus serotinus populations near old buildings.
Conservation groups monitor Eptesicus serotinus numbers as an indicator of ecosystem health.

Etymology

From Greek *epten* (to fly) and Latin *serotinus* (late, evening), referring to its activity at dusk.
