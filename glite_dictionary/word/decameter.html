decameter

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛkəˌmiːtər / (AmE), / ˈdɛkəˌmiːtə / (BrE)
A metric unit of length equal to ten meters.
metric unit equal to ten meters

Examples

The new track is exactly one decameter long, making it easy to measure sprint distances.
The river widened by several decameters after the heavy rains.
In some scientific fields, using the decameter as a unit can simplify large-scale measurements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meter - the base unit of length in the metric system
  • Degree Higher: hectometer - a metric unit of length equal to 100 meters
  • Degree Lower: centimeter - a metric unit of length equal to one hundredth of a meter

Etymology

From French 'décamètre', from Greek 'deka' (ten) + French 'mètre' (meter).
