corruption

1. NOUN

/ kəˈrʌpʃən / (AmE), / kəˈrʌpʃən / (BrE)
the act or state of being morally depraved; impairment of virtue or moral principles
moral depravity or moral decay

Examples

The corruption of the city's leaders led to a decline in public morals.
He feared that exposure to such decadence would lead to spiritual corruption.
Writers of the period often lamented the corruption they witnessed in society.

Synset Examples

the luxury and corruption among the upper classes
moral degeneracy followed intellectual degeneration
its brothels, its opium parlors, its depravity
Rome had fallen into moral putrefaction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depravity - moral corruption or wickedness
  • Antonym: virtue - behavior showing high moral standards

Etymology

From Latin corruptio, from corrumpere meaning 'to destroy' or 'to spoil'.

2. NOUN

/ kəˈrʌpʃən / (AmE), / kəˈrʌpʃən / (BrE)
Dishonest or fraudulent conduct by those in power, typically involving bribery or the abuse of entrusted authority for personal gain.
dishonest or fraudulent conduct

Examples

Allegations of corruption have damaged the reputation of several government officials.
He lost his job after being accused of corruption and accepting bribes.
Fighting corruption is essential for building trust in public institutions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being honest or truthful
  • Synonym: fraud - wrongful or criminal deception intended to result in financial or personal gain

Etymology

From Latin 'corruptio' (spoiling, breaking), from 'corrumpere' (to destroy, spoil).
