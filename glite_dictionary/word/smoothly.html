smoothly

1. ADV

/ ˈsmuðli / (AmE), / ˈsmuːðli / (BrE)
In a way that is polite, diplomatic, and without awkwardness or interruption.
in a diplomatic or fluent manner

Examples

She smoothly changed the subject to avoid any conflict.
The meeting transitioned smoothly from one topic to another.
He smoothly handled the difficult questions from the audience.

Synset Examples

‘And now,’ he said smoothly, ‘we will continue the conversation’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diplomatically - in a diplomatic manner, tactfully
  • Antonym: awkwardly - in an awkward or clumsy manner

Etymology

From 'smooth' + '-ly', forming an adverb from the adjective 'smooth'.

2. ADV

/ ˈsmuðli / (AmE), / ˈsmuːðli / (BrE)
In an even and effortless way, without problems or difficulties.
without problems or difficulties

Examples

The transition between the two managers went smoothly and without conflict.
Her speech flowed smoothly from start to finish, impressing the audience.
If everything runs smoothly, the project should be completed ahead of schedule.

Synset Examples

put the plans into effect quickly and smoothly
despite of some mishaps, everything went swimmingly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: easily - without difficulty or effort
  • Antonym: roughly - with difficulty or lack of smoothness

Etymology

From "smooth" + "-ly" forming the adverb.
