duplicate

1. ADJ

/ ˈduːplɪkət / (AmE), / ˈdjuːplɪkət / (BrE)
exactly like something else; made to be an exact copy of an original
identical; exactly like another

Examples

Please ensure that the duplicate keys are kept in a safe place.
He accidentally submitted a duplicate application for the job.
The contract must be signed in duplicate for both parties to have a copy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: identical - exactly the same; alike in every way
  • Antonym: original - existing first or as the source from which a copy is made

Etymology

From Latin duplicatus, past participle of duplicare (to double), from duplex (twofold).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
