bluebottle

1. NOUN

/ ˈbluˌbɑt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈbluːˌbɒt.əl / (BrE)
a large fly with a metallic blue body, often found around decaying matter and known for its loud buzzing sound
a metallic blue blowfly

Examples

A bluebottle flew noisily around the kitchen, attracted by the garbage.
During the summer, bluebottles often gather near compost bins and waste areas.
He recognized the loud buzzing as coming from a bluebottle trapped against the window.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blowfly - a type of fly often found around decaying matter

2. NOUN

/ ˈbluˌbɑt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈbluːˌbɒtl̩ / (BrE)
an annual Eurasian plant (Centaurea cyanus) with showy heads of blue, purple, pink, or white flowers, often cultivated in gardens
a flowering plant with bright blue (or other colored) heads

Examples

The bluebottle added a splash of vivid color to her summer garden.
Wild bluebottles were seen blooming along the edges of the wheat fields.
She picked a delicate bluebottle and tucked it behind her ear.

Etymology

Alludes to the traditional blue color of the flower heads, resembling a small bottle in shape.
