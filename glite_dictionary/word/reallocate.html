to reallocate

1. VERB

/ ˌriːˈæləˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈaləˌkeɪt / (BrE)
to allocate or distribute something again in a different or updated way
allocate or distribute again

Examples

After the budget cuts, the committee had to reallocate funds to critical programs.
The company decided to reallocate staff to its new office in Berlin.
Due to shifting needs, we will reallocate resources from marketing to research.

Synset Examples

Congressional seats are reapportioned on the basis of census data

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to allocate - to assign or set apart for a particular purpose
  • Synonym: to redistribute - to distribute again or differently

Etymology

Formed from re- (again) + allocate, from Latin *allocare* (to assign).
