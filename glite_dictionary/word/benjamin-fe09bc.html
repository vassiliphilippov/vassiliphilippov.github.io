benjamin

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛn.dʒə.mɪn / (AmE), / ˈbɛn.dʒə.mɪn / (BrE)
A fragrant gum resin obtained from certain trees, used especially in treating skin irritation and in perfumes and incense.
fragrant gum resin used medicinally and in perfume

Examples

The apothecary recommended applying benjamin to soothe the irritated skin.
Perfumeries often use benjamin as a fixative in their most popular scents.
Aromatic benjamin was burned in rituals for its pleasant fragrance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benzoin - a balsamic resin used in medicine and perfumery, also called benjamin

Etymology

From French 'benjamin', ultimately from Arabic 'lubān jāwī' (gum from Java), referring to benzoin.
