Tanach

1. PROPN

/ təˈnɑx / (AmE), / təˈnax / (BrE)
the canonical collection of Jewish scriptures consisting of the Torah, the Prophets (Nevi'im), and the Writings (Ketuvim)
the Jewish scriptures; Hebrew Bible

Examples

Scholars have studied the Tanach for centuries to understand ancient Hebrew culture.
She learned to read Hebrew so she could study the Tanach in its original language.
The Tanach is central to Jewish education and religious practice.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Torah - the first division of the Tanach, the law or teachings
  • Synonym: Hebrew Bible - another name for the Tanach

Etymology

From Hebrew תנ"ך‎ (Tanakh), an acronym from Torah (Law), Nevi'im (Prophets), and Ketuvim (Writings).
