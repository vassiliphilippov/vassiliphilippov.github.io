Tungus

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌŋɡəs / (AmE), / ˈtʌŋɡəs / (BrE)
the Tungusic language spoken by the Evenki people in eastern Siberia
language of the Evenki in Siberia

Examples

Researchers traveled to Siberia to record folk tales in Tungus.
The Tungus language has several dialects spoken by different Evenki groups.
He is studying endangered languages and recently focused on Tungus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Evenki - the people who speak Tungus

Etymology

From Russian Тунгус (Tungus), referring to the Evenki people and their language.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʊŋɡəs / (AmE), / ˈtʌŋɡəs / (BrE)
A member of a nomadic people of eastern Siberia who speak Tungusic languages and are related to the Manchu.
member of a Tungusic Siberian people

Examples

The Tungus have traditionally lived by hunting and reindeer herding in Siberia.
A Tungus shaman performed rituals believed to heal the sick.
Many Tungus communities still maintain their unique cultural practices today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Evenki - A major Tungusic people of Siberia, also known as Evenks or Tungus
  • Synonym: Manchu - A Tungusic people from northeast China, related to the Tungus

Etymology

From Russian 'Тунгус' (Tungus), possibly from Evenki self-designation.
