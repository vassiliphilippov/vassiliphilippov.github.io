slumber

1. NOUN

/ ˈslʌmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈslʌmbə / (BrE)
A state of sleep or inactivity; a period during which something is dormant or quiescent.
state of sleep or inactivity

Examples

The city was in a deep slumber after the long celebration.
After years of slumber, the volcano finally erupted.
His creativity emerged from its slumber when he began painting again.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sleep - natural periodic state of rest for the mind and body
  • Antonym: wakefulness - state of being awake

Etymology

From Middle English 'slumeren', from Old English 'slumerian' (to doze).

2. NOUN

/ ˈslʌmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈslʌmbə / (BrE)
sleep; a natural state of rest in which your body and mind are inactive and you are not conscious of the world around you.
sleep; a state of rest

Examples

After reading her book, she drifted into a deep slumber on the couch.
The gentle rain outside made his slumber even more peaceful.
Children are said to grow during their slumber each night.

Synset Examples

he didn't get enough sleep last night
calm as a child in dreamless slumber

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sleep - the natural, periodic state of rest for the body and mind

Etymology

From Middle English *slumeren*, probably of Germanic origin; related to Old English *sluma* (sleep).

3. VERB

/ ˈslʌmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈslʌmbə / (BrE)
to sleep, especially in a peaceful or light way
to sleep lightly or peacefully

Examples

She slumbered peacefully while the rain tapped against the window.
Many animals slumber through the heat of the day.
He slumbered only briefly before his alarm woke him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sleep - to be in the state of sleep
  • Antonym: wake - to be awake; not asleep

Etymology

From Middle English 'slomren', probably related to Middle Dutch 'slumeren' (to slumber).
