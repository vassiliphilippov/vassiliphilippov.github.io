bead

1. VERB

/ bid / (AmE), / biːd / (BrE)
to decorate something by sewing beads onto it
decorate by sewing beads

Examples

The designer decided to bead the collar of the dress for extra sparkle.
She spent hours carefully beading the edges of the scarf.
They hired an artisan to bead intricate patterns onto the wedding gown.

Synset Examples

bead the wedding gown

Etymology

From Old English *bed* (a prayer bead), influenced by the use of beads in decoration.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01674406-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ bid / (AmE), / biːd / (BrE)
a small, typically spherical object; often a small, round drop of liquid or a piece of material used in jewelry or decoration
small round object or drop

Examples

Tiny beads of rain formed on the windowpane.
She wore a necklace strung with colorful beads.
Beads of sweat trickled down his face during the workout.

Synset Examples

he studied the shapes of low-viscosity drops
beads of sweat on his forehead

Related Terms

  • Synonym: droplet - a very small drop of liquid

Etymology

From Old English 'bed', meaning 'prayer bead'; later associated with small, round objects.

/ bid / (AmE), / biːd / (BrE)

string together like beads

She likes to bead tiny glass animals for her jewelry business.
After collecting seashells, we decided to bead them onto a long string for decoration.

Related Terms

Etymology

From Old English 'bede' (prayer bead), related to the act of threading beads for prayer.

4. VERB

/ bid / (AmE), / biːd / (BrE)
(of a liquid) to form small, rounded drops on a surface.
form into small drops

Examples

Sweat began to bead on his forehead as he ran up the hill.
Water will bead on a waxed car, repelling moisture.
Tiny droplets of dew beaded on the blades of grass in the early morning.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to coalesce - come together to form one mass or whole
  • Degree Higher: drip - to fall in drops

Etymology

From Middle English 'bede' (prayer bead), extended metaphorically to drops due to resemblance in shape.

/ bid / (AmE), / biːd / (BrE)

small pierced ball for stringing

Examples

The curtain was made entirely of wooden beads hanging from top to bottom.
Children often enjoy making friendship bracelets using colorful beads.

Examples

The cabinet features a delicate bead along its edges for added elegance.
A narrow bead was carved along the windowsill to match the room's trim.
The craftsman carefully sanded the bead to ensure a smooth finish on the table.
