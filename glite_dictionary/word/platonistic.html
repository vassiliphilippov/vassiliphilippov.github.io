Platonistic

1. ADJ

/ pleɪtəˈnɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌplætəˈnɪstɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of Platonism or its doctrines.
relating to Platonism or its doctrines

Examples

Her Platonistic interpretations of the text reveal a deep engagement with ancient philosophy.
The theory bears a Platonistic influence, emphasizing ideal forms over material reality.
Many modern philosophers borrow Platonistic concepts to discuss issues of universals and particulars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Platonic - relating to Plato or Platonism

Etymology

From 'Platonism' (the philosophy of Plato), plus suffix '-istic'.
