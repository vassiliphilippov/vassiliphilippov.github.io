Momotidae

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈmoʊtɪˌdi / (AmE), / məʊˈməʊtɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of brightly colored, medium-sized birds known as motmots, native to Central and South America
family of birds called motmots

Examples

The Momotidae are recognized for their distinctive racket-shaped tail feathers.
Researchers discovered a new species in the Momotidae family thriving in the Amazon rainforests.
Birdwatchers travel to Costa Rica to observe the colorful members of the Momotidae.

Etymology

From New Latin Momotidae, based on 'Momotus,' the type genus, plus the suffix '-idae' denoting a family.

2. NOUN

/ moʊˈmoʊ.təˌdiː / (AmE), / məˈməʊ.tɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of tropical birds in the order Coraciiformes, commonly known as motmots, found in Central and South America.
family of tropical birds (motmots)

Examples

The striking plumage of the Momotidae makes them popular among birdwatchers.
Several species within the Momotidae are known for their racket-shaped tails.
Habitat loss threatens many members of the Momotidae family in Central America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motmot - a bird of the family Momotidae with brightly colored plumage and long tails

Etymology

From New Latin Momotidae, based on Momotus (type genus) + -idae (family suffix).
