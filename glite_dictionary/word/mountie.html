Mountie

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaʊnti / (AmE), / ˈmaʊnti / (BrE)
A member of the Royal Canadian Mounted Police, the national police force of Canada.
member of the Royal Canadian Mounted Police
informal

Examples

A Mountie in his red uniform stood at attention during the ceremony.
My grandfather served as a Mountie in northern Canada for twenty years.
The movie features a heroic Mountie tracking criminals through the wilderness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Royal Canadian Mounted Police - national police force of Canada

Etymology

From 'mount(ed)' (horseback) + -ie (diminutive suffix); refers to police originally mounted on horses.
