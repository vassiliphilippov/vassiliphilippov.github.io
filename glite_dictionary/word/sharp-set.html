sharp-set

1. ADJ

/ ʃɑrpˈsɛt / (AmE), / ʃɑːpˈsɛt / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) extremely hungry; having a keen appetite.
extremely hungry

Examples

After hiking all morning, we were absolutely sharp-set by lunchtime.
The sharp-set travelers eagerly devoured everything that was offered to them.
She arrived home sharp-set and immediately started searching for something to eat.

Synset Examples

they were tired and famished for food and sleep
a ravenous boy
the family was starved and ragged
fell into the esurient embrace of a predatory enemy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ravenous - extremely hungry
  • Synonym: famished - very hungry
  • Degree Higher: starved - suffering or dying from hunger
  • Degree Lower: hungry - feeling the need for food

Etymology

From sharp (keen) + set (inclined), referring to the keen edge of hunger.
