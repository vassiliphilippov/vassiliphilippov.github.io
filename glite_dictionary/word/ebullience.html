ebullience

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈbʌliəns / (AmE), / ɪˈbʌlɪəns / (BrE)
the quality of being cheerful and full of energy; exuberant enjoyment or enthusiasm
cheerful enthusiasm; exuberance

Examples

The ebullience of the crowd was evident as the team claimed victory.
Her natural ebullience made her the life of every party she attended.
He greeted his old friend with such ebullience that others in the café turned to watch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exuberance - joyful enthusiasm or energy
  • Antonym: gloom - a state of sadness or lack of cheerfulness

Etymology

From Latin 'ebullientia' (a boiling up), from 'ebullire' (to boil up)
