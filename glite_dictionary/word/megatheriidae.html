Megatheriidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛɡəˌθɪriˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌmɛɡəˌθɪəriˈaɪdiː / (BrE)
an extinct family of large ground sloths that lived in the Americas during the Cenozoic era
extinct family of giant ground sloths

Examples

Fossils of Megatheriidae have been discovered throughout South and North America.
The Megatheriidae once roamed the ancient forests before their extinction at the end of the Ice Age.
Scientists believe that members of the Megatheriidae could weigh up to four tons and stand over six meters tall.

Etymology

From modern Latin, based on Greek mega- (great) and therion (beast).
