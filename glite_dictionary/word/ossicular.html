ossicular

1. ADJ

/ əˈsɪkjələr / (AmE), / ɒˈsɪkjʊlə / (BrE)
Relating to the ossicles—the three small bones—in the middle ear.
of the ossicles in the middle ear
medical

Examples

The ossicular chain transmits sound vibrations from the eardrum to the inner ear.
A disruption in ossicular function can result in conductive hearing loss.
Surgeons examined the ossicular structures for signs of damage after the head injury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ossicle - a small bone, especially one of the three in the middle ear

Etymology

From Latin 'ossiculum' (diminutive of 'os', meaning 'bone') + '-ar'.
