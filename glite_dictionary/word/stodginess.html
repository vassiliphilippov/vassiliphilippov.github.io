stodginess

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɑːdʒinəs / (AmE), / ˈstɒdʒinəs / (BrE)
the quality of being dull, overly solemn, or lacking in imagination or liveliness
quality of being dull or overly solemn

Examples

Her writing is accurate but marred by a certain stodginess that makes it hard to enjoy.
The ceremony was celebrated for its tradition but criticized for its stodginess.
Despite his intelligence, his lectures were marked by an air of stodginess that bored his students.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dullness - the quality of lacking interest or excitement
  • Synonym: pomposity - self-importance, grandiosity, or pretentiousness in manner
  • Antonym: vivacity - the quality of being lively and animated

Etymology

From 'stodgy' + '-ness', where 'stodgy' likely derives from English dialect 'stodge' (thick, heavy food).
