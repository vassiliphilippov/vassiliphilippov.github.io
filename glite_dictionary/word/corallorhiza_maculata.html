Corallorhiza maculata

1. NOUN

/ kəˌræl.əˈraɪ.zə ˌmæk.jʊˈleɪ.tə / (AmE), / kɒˌral.əˈraɪ.zə ˌmak.juˈleɪ.tə / (BrE)
A species of North American orchid with yellowish, reddish, or purplish-brown leafless stems and loose racemes of similar-colored flowers with white purple-spotted lips.
a species of North American orchid
medical

Examples

The forest floor was dotted with the unusual stems of Corallorhiza maculata in early summer.
Botanists studied the life cycle of Corallorhiza maculata during their fieldwork in Canada.
Corallorhiza maculata thrives in shady, moist woodlands throughout much of North America.

Etymology

From Latin: 'corallorhiza' meaning coral root (from 'corallium' coral + 'rhiza' root), and 'maculata' meaning spotted.
