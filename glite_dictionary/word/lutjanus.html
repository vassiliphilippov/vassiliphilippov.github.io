Lutjanus

1. NOUN

/ luːˈtʃeɪnəs / (AmE), / luːˈtʃeɪnəs / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish in the family Lutjanidae, commonly known as snappers.
genus of snappers (fish)

Examples

The genus Lutjanus is widely distributed in tropical and subtropical seas.
Several species within Lutjanus are important to commercial fisheries.
Juveniles of Lutjanus often inhabit mangroves before moving to coral reefs as adults.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: snapper - any fish of the family Lutjanidae, especially of the genus Lutjanus

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Malay 'ikan lutjan' (snapper).

2. NOUN

/ luˈdʒænəs / (AmE), / ljuːˈdʒænəs / (BrE)
a genus of marine fish in the family Lutjanidae, known commonly as snappers, found in tropical and subtropical waters
genus of snappers (marine fish)

Examples

The Lutjanus genus includes many species prized by commercial fisheries.
Scientists discovered a new species within Lutjanus during their expedition in the Pacific Ocean.
Most members of the Lutjanus genus are important predators in coral reef ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Lutjanidae - the family of marine fish including snappers
  • Synonym: snapper - a common name for fish in genus Lutjanus

Etymology

From Latinized form of Malay 'ikan lutjan', a type of fish.
