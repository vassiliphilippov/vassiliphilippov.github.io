kauri

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊri / (AmE), / ˈkaʊri / (BrE)
A tall timber tree of New Zealand, known for its straight-grained white wood.
tall New Zealand timber tree

Examples

The ancient kauri forests are protected as part of New Zealand's natural heritage.
Craftsmen prize kauri wood for its smooth texture and durability.
A single fallen kauri can yield enough timber for several large boats.

Etymology

From Māori 'kauri'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊri / (AmE), / ˈkaʊəri / (BrE)
a resin obtained from the kauri trees of New Zealand, often found as a fossil and used in making varnishes and linoleum
resin from New Zealand kauri trees used in varnishes

Examples

The craftsman used kauri to give the cabinet a beautiful glossy finish.
Old deposits of kauri were discovered while digging the new road.
The varnish contains kauri resin, making it exceptionally durable.

Etymology

From Maori 'kauri', the name of the tree.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊri / (AmE), / ˈkaʊəri / (BrE)
A pale, fine-grained wood from trees of the genus Agathis, especially Agathis australis, native to Australasia and valued for timber and resins.
pale wood from Agathis trees

Examples

The table was made from polished kauri, giving it a distinctive sheen.
Early settlers used kauri for building because of its strength and workability.
Antique shops in New Zealand often feature items carved from kauri wood.

Etymology

From Maori *kauri*, the native name for the tree and its wood.
