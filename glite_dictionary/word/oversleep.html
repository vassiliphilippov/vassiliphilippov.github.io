to oversleep

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈslip / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈsliːp / (BrE)
to sleep longer than intended, especially by accident and beyond the time you meant to wake up
sleep longer than intended

Examples

I accidentally overslept this morning and missed my first class.
If you oversleep, you might be late for work.
She tends to oversleep whenever she reads late into the night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sleep in - to sleep later than usual in the morning

Etymology

From over- (excessively) + sleep; first attested in Middle English.
