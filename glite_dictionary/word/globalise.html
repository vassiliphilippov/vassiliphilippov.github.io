to globalise

1. VERB

/ ˈɡloʊbəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɡləʊbəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something affect or involve the entire world; to extend to all parts of the globe
make worldwide in scope or application
british

Examples

Many companies now aim to globalise their operations for increased efficiency and reach.
The fashion industry has globalised trends, making similar styles popular across continents.
Efforts to globalise education seek to provide equal opportunities for students worldwide.

Synset Examples

Markets are being increasingly globalized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to globalize - American English spelling of 'globalise'
  • Antonym: to localise - make local in scope or application

Etymology

From 'global' + '-ise', formed in the 20th century after 'global'.
