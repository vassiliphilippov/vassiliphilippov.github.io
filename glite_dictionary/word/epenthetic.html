epenthetic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛpənˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛpɛnˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
(of a sound or letter) inserted into a word, or relating to epenthesis.
relating to the insertion of sounds in words

Examples

The inserted vowel in 'athlete' is an epenthetic sound that some speakers use.
An epenthetic consonant sometimes appears to break up difficult clusters in casual speech.
Linguists analyzed the epenthetic vowels present in children’s pronunciation of certain words.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: epenthesis - the insertion of a sound or letter within a word

Etymology

From Greek epenthetikos, from epentithenai (to insert).
