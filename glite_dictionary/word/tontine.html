tontine

1. NOUN

/ tɑnˈtin / (AmE), / tɒnˈtiːn / (BrE)
An arrangement in which participants contribute to a common fund, and as members die or withdraw, their shares are distributed among the survivors, until the last survivor takes the rest.
a shared fund distributed among surviving members

Examples

After her uncle passed away, she received a payout from the tontine he had joined as a young man.
A tontine was established among the group of investors, each hoping to outlive the others and claim the fund.
The novel's plot centers on a family quarrel over a very large tontine.

Etymology

From French tontine, named after Lorenzo Tonti, the 17th-century Italian banker who promoted the scheme.

2. NOUN

/ tɑnˈtin / (AmE), / tɒnˈtiːn / (BrE)
an investment scheme in which participants share benefits that are redistributed among survivors as members die, continuing until only one or a few remain or until a fixed term ends
survivor-based investment scheme

Examples

The town's elders invested in a tontine as a way to provide for their families' future.
After joining the tontine, she received larger dividends each year as older members passed away.
The concept of a tontine has inspired both financial systems and mystery novels.

Etymology

From French 'tontine', named after Lorenzo Tonti (c. 1602–1684), its inventor.
