runty

1. ADJ

/ ˈrʌnti / (AmE), / ˈrʌnti / (BrE)
(of a person, animal, or plant) unusually small or undersized; stunted in growth.
small or stunted in growth

Examples

The runty puppy had trouble keeping up with its bigger siblings.
He always felt runty standing next to his tall friends.
The runty tomato plant barely produced any fruit all summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: undersized - smaller than the usual size
  • Synonym: dwarfish - unusually small in stature or size
  • Antonym: large - of above average size

Etymology

From 'runt' (the smallest in a litter), with the suffix -y forming an adjective.

2. ADJ

/ ˈrʌn.ti / (AmE), / ˈrʌn.ti / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) unusually small and weak, especially compared to others of the same kind; stunted in growth.
unusually small or stunted

Examples

The runty puppy always came last to the food bowl at dinner time.
Although he was runty as a child, he grew to be quite strong.
Their runty tomato plants produced fewer fruits than usual this year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stunted - having been prevented from growing or developing properly
  • Synonym: dwarfish - very small in size; resembling a dwarf
  • Antonym: tall - of above average height or size

Etymology

From 'runt' (smallest in a litter) + adjective suffix '-y'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01397563-s
Synset POS: s
