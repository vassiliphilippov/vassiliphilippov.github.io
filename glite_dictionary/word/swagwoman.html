swagwoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈswæɡˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈswæɡˌwʊmən / (BrE)
An itinerant Australian female laborer who carries her personal belongings in a bundle as she travels in search of work.
female itinerant Australian laborer
archaic british

Examples

The legend of the swagwoman is rarely mentioned in traditional Australian folk songs.
She lived as a swagwoman, roaming the outback with only her bundle and her determination.
Early accounts record a swagwoman working alongside male swagmen during the Great Depression.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: swagman - an itinerant Australian male laborer with a bundle

Etymology

Modeled after 'swagman' (from 'swag' + 'man'), with 'woman' denoting a female counterpart; 'swag' refers to a traveler's bundle.
