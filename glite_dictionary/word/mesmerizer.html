mesmerizer

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛzməˌraɪzər / (AmE), / ˈmɛzməraɪzə / (BrE)
a person who induces hypnosis, especially by using mesmerism or a hypnotic technique
person who induces hypnosis

Examples

The skilled mesmerizer captivated the audience with his remarkable stage performance.
Under the guidance of the mesmerizer, the volunteer slowly drifted into a deep trance.
Victorian-era physicians sometimes acted as mesmerizers to treat physical and mental ailments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypnotist - a person skilled in hypnotism

Etymology

From 'mesmerize', named after Franz Mesmer, an 18th-century physician who developed theories of animal magnetism.
