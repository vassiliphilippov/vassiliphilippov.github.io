berth

1. NOUN

/ bɝːθ / (AmE), / bɜːθ / (BrE)
a fixed bed or sleeping place on a ship or train, often arranged in tiers
a bed on a ship or train

Examples

She climbed into the upper berth and pulled the curtain closed for privacy.
Each passenger was assigned a berth on the overnight train to Paris.
The ship offers comfortable berths for travelers during long voyages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bunk - a narrow bed stacked over or under another, especially on a ship or train

Etymology

From French *berce* (cradle) or Dutch *kooi* (cage, place to sleep), origin debated.

3. VERB

/ bɝːθ / (AmE), / bɜːθ / (BrE)
to provide a sleeping place, berth, or a space for a ship to dock.
provide a place to sleep or dock

Examples

The harbormaster berthed the ship safely alongside the pier.
The cruise line berths all passengers in comfortable state rooms.
They berth the yacht in the marina every summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dock - to bring a ship into a dock
  • Synonym: moor - to secure a boat by attaching it to a fixed object

Etymology

From Old English 'berþ' (bearing, bringing forth), influenced by nautical use since the 17th century.
