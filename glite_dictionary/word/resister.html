resister

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈzɪstər / (AmE), / rɪˈzɪstə / (BrE)
a person who actively opposes or obstructs something that others wish to carry out; someone who resists.
someone who resists or obstructs

Examples

As a committed resister, she refused to comply with the new regulations.
Many resisters were arrested during the protest against the policy.
The movement honored the courage of each resister who stood up for their beliefs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opponent - a person who opposes or competes with another
  • Antonym: supporter - a person who supports or backs an idea or cause

Etymology

From resist + -er, meaning 'one who resists'.

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈzɪstər / (AmE), / rɪˈzɪstə / (BrE)
a person who actively opposes or resists something, especially authority, control, or an opposing force
person who opposes or resists

Examples

During the occupation, several brave resisters helped hide families from the authorities.
The protesters viewed themselves as resisters fighting for social justice.
History often remembers the resister more than those who simply followed orders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opponent - a person who is against or competes with another
  • Antonym: conformist - a person who accepts established practices without resistance

Etymology

From Latin 'resistere' meaning 'to stand back' or 'to resist', via Old French and Middle English.
