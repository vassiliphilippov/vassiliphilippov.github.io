Annunciation

1. NOUN

/ əˌnʌnsiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌnʌnsiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
(in Christianity) the announcement by the angel Gabriel to the Virgin Mary that she would conceive and become the mother of Jesus, regarded as the incarnation of Christ.
Christian event: Gabriel's message to Mary

Examples

The Annunciation is celebrated by Christians each year on March 25th.
Many artists have been inspired to paint scenes of the Annunciation throughout history.
The church stained glass depicts the Annunciation in vibrant colors.

Etymology

From Latin *annuntiatio* ('announcement'), from *annuntiare* ('to announce').

2. NOUN

/ əˌnʌnsiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌnʌnsiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the festival commemorating the announcement by the angel Gabriel to the Virgin Mary that she would conceive Jesus; observed on March 25 in the Christian calendar
Christian festival marking Gabriel's announcement to Mary

Examples

Easter often falls close to the Annunciation in the Christian calendar.
The church held a special mass on the Annunciation to honor the Virgin Mary.
In England, the Annunciation is historically one of the quarter days for settling debts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Annunciation Day - alternative term for the Annunciation festival
  • Degree Higher: Christmas - the festival celebrating the birth of Jesus

Etymology

From Latin annuntiatio (announcement, proclamation), from annuntiare (to announce).
