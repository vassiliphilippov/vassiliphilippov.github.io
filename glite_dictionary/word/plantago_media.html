Plantago media

1. NOUN

/ plænˈteɪɡoʊ ˈmiːdiə / (AmE), / planˈteɪɡəʊ ˈmiːdɪə / (BrE)
A species of perennial plantain, native to Europe and western Asia, with hairy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers.
species of perennial plantain

Examples

Plantago media is often found growing in meadows and grassy fields across Europe.
The leaves of Plantago media are noticeably softer and hairier than those of other plantains.
Gardeners appreciate Plantago media for its delicate white blossoms and fragrant scent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plantain - a group of herbaceous plants in the genus Plantago

Etymology

From Latin: 'Plantago' (plantain) and 'media' (middle, intermediate).
