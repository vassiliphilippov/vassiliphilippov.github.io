simper

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪmpər / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpə / (BrE)
a silly or self-conscious smile, often in a coy or affected way
a silly or affected smile

Examples

She gave him a shy simper when he glanced her way.
The photograph captured the child's awkward simper perfectly.
He tried to hide his embarrassment behind a forced simper.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smirk - a smug, conceited, or silly smile

Etymology

From Middle English symperen (to smile in a silly manner), of uncertain origin.

2. VERB

/ ˈsɪmpɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpə / (BrE)
to smile in a self-conscious, affected, or silly way, often suggesting insincerity or derision
smile in a silly or affected way

Examples

She couldn't help but simper when the teacher praised her drawing in front of the class.
He tends to simper at his boss's jokes, even when they aren't funny.
Martha simpered awkwardly, trying to hide her embarrassment during the interview.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smile - to make a facial expression showing pleasure or amusement
  • Synonym: smirk - to smile in an irritatingly smug, conceited, or silly way

Etymology

Probably from Middle English (imitative of a thin, silly smile).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00029329-v
Synset POS: v
