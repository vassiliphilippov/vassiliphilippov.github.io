amphiprotic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæm.fəˈproʊ.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌam.fɪˈprəʊ.tɪk / (BrE)
(of a substance) able to act as either an acid or a base, capable of donating or accepting a proton in chemical reactions.
able to act as acid or base
medical

Examples

Water is an amphiprotic molecule because it can act as both an acid and a base.
Some ions are amphiprotic, meaning they can both donate and accept protons during reactions.
The amphiprotic nature of the compound makes it useful in various chemical processes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amphoteric - able to react as both an acid and a base

Etymology

From Greek 'amphi-' (both) + 'proton' (a chemical term for hydrogen ion) + '-ic'.
