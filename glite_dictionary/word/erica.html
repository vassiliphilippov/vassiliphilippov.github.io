erica

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛrɪkə / (AmE), / ˈɛrɪkə / (BrE)
A plant belonging to the genus Erica, commonly known as heath or heather, found chiefly in Europe and South Africa.
plant of the genus Erica (heath/heather)

Examples

The hillside was covered in blooming erica after the spring rains.
Botanists discovered a rare species of erica in the coastal region.
She planted several varieties of erica to add color to her garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heather - a low, evergreen shrub with small, scale-like leaves and pink or purple flowers

Etymology

From Latin *Erica*, borrowed from Ancient Greek ἐρείκη (ereíkē), meaning 'heath' or 'heather'.
