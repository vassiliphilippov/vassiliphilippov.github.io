clonal

1. ADJ

/ ˈkloʊ.nəl / (AmE), / ˈkləʊ.nəl / (BrE)
Relating to or derived from a clone or clones, especially in biology.
relating to a clone or clones

Examples

Many laboratory studies use clonal populations to ensure genetic consistency.
Certain cancers exhibit clonal expansion of mutated cells.
The clonal plants were all genetically identical to the parent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clone - an organism or cell produced asexually from one ancestor

Etymology

From 'clone' (from Greek klōn, meaning twig) + '-al' forming an adjective.
