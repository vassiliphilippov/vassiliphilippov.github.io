psychogenetic

1. ADJ

/ ˌsaɪkoʊdʒəˈnɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌsʌɪkə(ʊ)dʒɪˈnɛtɪk / (BrE)
Relating to, or caused by, psychological factors rather than physical ones, especially regarding the origin of a disorder.
due to psychological cause (esp. disorder)
medical

Examples

Some researchers believe that dissociative disorders are psychogenetic in nature.
The patient exhibited symptoms with no organic basis, indicating a possible psychogenetic origin.
A thorough evaluation is needed to distinguish between psychogenetic and physiological causes of the illness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychogenic - Originating in the mind or in mental or emotional conflict

Etymology

From psycho- (mind, psychological) + -genetic (origin, development).

2. ADJ

/ ˌsaɪkəˈdʒɛnɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəʊˈdʒɛnɪtɪk / (BrE)
relating to the origin and development of the mind or mental processes
of the origin and development of the mind
medical

Examples

The psychogenetic theory proposes that early childhood experiences significantly influence mental development.
Research into psychogenetic factors has improved our understanding of personality disorders.
The book provides an in-depth analysis of psychogenetic mechanisms in human behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: psychogenesis - the origin and development of mental processes

Etymology

From Greek 'psyche' (mind, soul) + 'genesis' (origin, creation).

WordNet ID: oewn-02917256-a
Synset POS: a
