murmuring

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɝːmərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɜːmərɪŋ / (BrE)
a complaint or expression of dissatisfaction uttered in a low and indistinct tone
low, indistinct complaint

Examples

There was a murmuring among the audience when the decision was announced.
Despite the official's reassurances, a quiet murmuring of discontent spread through the crowd.
His suggestion was met with a murmuring of disagreement from several members.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grumble - to complain about something in a quiet but angry way
  • Synonym: complaint - an expression of dissatisfaction or annoyance

Etymology

From the verb 'murmur', from Latin 'murmurare' (to murmur).

2. ADJ

/ ˈmɜrmərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɜːm(ə)rɪŋ / (BrE)
making a low, continuous, and indistinct sound, like soft voices or gentle waves
making a low, continuous sound

Examples

The murmuring river soothed her as she walked along its banks.
He heard murmuring voices in the quiet library, barely above a whisper.
In the forest, a murmuring wind passed gently through the tall trees.

Synset Examples

like murmuring waves
susurrant voices

Related Terms

  • Synonym: susurrant - making a soft, whispering or rustling sound

Etymology

Present participle of 'murmur', from Latin 'murmurare' (to murmur).

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɝːmɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɜːmərɪŋ / (BrE)
a low, continuous, and indistinct sound, often produced by people speaking softly or by natural phenomena
low, continuous indistinct sound

Examples

A gentle murmuring could be heard from the audience as they waited for the show to begin.
The murmuring of the stream created a peaceful atmosphere in the forest.
After the announcement, there was a murmuring of disappointment among the crowd.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whisper - speaking very softly using the breath but not the vocal cords
  • Synonym: babble - a continuous, low, confusing noise, often from people talking
  • Antonym: shout - a loud call or cry

Etymology

From Middle English 'murmur', from Old French, from Latin 'murmur' (a murmur, muttering).
