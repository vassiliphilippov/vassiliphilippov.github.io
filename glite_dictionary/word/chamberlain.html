chamberlain

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃeɪmbərlɪn / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪmbəlɪn / (BrE)
an officer who manages the financial affairs of a municipal corporation or royal household, especially historically.
municipal or royal treasurer (historical)

Examples

The city appointed a new chamberlain to oversee its budget and spending.
Historically, the chamberlain was responsible for collecting taxes and maintaining city accounts.
As chamberlain, she presented the annual financial report to the town council.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: treasurer - person in charge of financial matters

Etymology

From Old French chambrelein, from Medieval Latin cameralīnus, from Latin camera (chamber).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃeɪmbərlɪn / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪmbəlɪn / (BrE)
an officer who manages the household of a king, nobleman, or other important person, often with ceremonial duties
officer managing a royal or noble household

Examples

The king appointed a trusted chamberlain to oversee all the palace affairs.
As chamberlain, he organized receptions and managed the royal servants.
The chamberlain prepared the state rooms for the visiting dignitaries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steward - person who manages another's property or affairs

Etymology

From Old French 'chamberlain', from Medieval Latin 'camerlingus', from Late Latin 'camerarius' (chamber attendant).
