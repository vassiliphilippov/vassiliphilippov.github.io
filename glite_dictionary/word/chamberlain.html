Chamberlain

1. PROPN

/ ˈtʃeɪmbərˌleɪn / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪmbəleɪn / (BrE)
(Neville Chamberlain) British statesman (1869–1940), who served as Prime Minister and is known for his policy of appeasement towards Nazi Germany.
British Prime Minister (appeasement policy, 1937–1940)
british

Examples

Neville Chamberlain is often remembered for announcing 'peace for our time' after the Munich Agreement.
Historians continue to debate the effectiveness of Chamberlain's policy of appeasement before World War II.
During his tenure as Prime Minister, Chamberlain faced intense criticism for his foreign policy decisions.
