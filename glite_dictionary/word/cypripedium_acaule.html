Cypripedium acaule

1. NOUN

/ saɪprəˈpiːdiəm əˈkɔːli / (AmE), / sɪprɪˈpiːdɪəm əˈkɔːli / (BrE)
A species of pink woodland orchid native to eastern North America, known as the pink lady's slipper.
pink woodland orchid of North America

Examples

The Cypripedium acaule is often admired for its striking pink flowers along shaded forest paths.
Native to North American woodlands, Cypripedium acaule blooms in the late spring.
Botanists monitor populations of Cypripedium acaule to help conserve its natural habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lady's slipper - Common name for several species of orchids in the genus Cypripedium

Etymology

Genus from Greek 'kypris' (Venus) and 'pedilon' (sandal); species from Latin 'acaule' meaning 'stemless'.
