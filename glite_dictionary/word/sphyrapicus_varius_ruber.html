Sphyrapicus varius ruber

1. NOUN

/ sfɪəˈræpɪkəs ˈvɛər.i.əs ˈruː.bər / (AmE), / sfɪəˈræpɪkəs ˈveəriəs ˈruːbə / (BrE)
A subspecies of the yellow-bellied sapsucker found in western North America.
western North American sapsucker subspecies

Examples

The Sphyrapicus varius ruber is notable for its bright red cap and nape.
Birdwatchers in California frequently report sightings of Sphyrapicus varius ruber during migration season.
Unlike its eastern cousin, Sphyrapicus varius ruber has more extensive red coloring on its head.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yellow-bellied sapsucker - a North American woodpecker species, Sphyrapicus varius

Etymology

From scientific Latin: genus Sphyrapicus, species varius, subspecies ruber.
