antidotal

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.tɪˈdoʊ.təl / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈdəʊ.təl / (BrE)
Having the property of counteracting the effects of a poison; relating to an antidote.
counteracting a poison; antidote-related

Examples

The doctor administered an antidotal injection to treat the patient's snake bite.
Certain herbs are known for their antidotal properties against various toxins.
The report emphasized the need for rapid access to antidotal treatments in rural areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antidote - a medicine taken to counteract a particular poison

Etymology

From antidote + -al, modeled after Latin *antidotālis*.
