poll

1. NOUN

/ poʊl / (AmE), / pəʊl / (BrE)
the part of the head between the ears, especially in animals such as horses and cattle
part of the head between the ears (in animals)

Examples

The veterinarian examined the horse's poll for signs of injury.
The bridle should rest just behind the poll to avoid discomfort.
Grooming the poll is important for maintaining a healthy mane.

Etymology

From Old English 'pol' (head), used especially in reference to animals.

2. VERB

/ poʊl / (AmE), / pəʊl / (BrE)
to ask people for their votes or opinions, especially in an official or organized way
ask for votes or opinions

Examples

The organization will poll members to determine their preferred candidate.
The senator polled more than 60% of the total votes in last year's election.
They plan to poll residents about the new community project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: survey - to ask a group of people questions to collect information or opinions

Etymology

From Middle English 'polle' (head), later associated with counting heads (votes).

4. NOUN

/ poʊl / (AmE), / pəʊl / (BrE)
an activity in which a number of people are asked questions in order to find out what they think about a subject, often conducted by interviewing a random sample of people
survey of public opinion

Examples

The newspaper published the results of a poll on attitudes toward climate change.
A recent poll showed that most voters support the new policy.
They conducted a poll to find out which candidate was most popular.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: survey - a general method for collecting information from people by asking questions

Etymology

From Middle English *polle* (head), later extended to 'counting heads' in elections and surveys.

5. NOUN

/ poʊl / (AmE), / pəʊl / (BrE)
the process of counting votes in an election or other ballot
counting of votes in an election

Examples

The poll closed at 8 p.m., and the results were announced shortly after.
After the poll was completed, the official count revealed a narrow victory.
Volunteers stayed late to help with the poll and ensure accuracy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ballot - a process of voting, in writing and typically in secret
  • Synonym: election - a formal group decision-making process by which a population chooses an individual to hold public office

Etymology

From Middle English 'polle' (head), later referring to voting as counting heads.
