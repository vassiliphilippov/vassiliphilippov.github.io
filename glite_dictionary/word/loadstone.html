loadstone

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊdˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈləʊdˌstəʊn / (BrE)
a naturally occurring magnetic mineral, magnetite, capable of attracting iron and possessing magnetic polarity
natural magnetic mineral (magnetite)

Examples

Early sailors used a loadstone to help them navigate the seas.
The museum displayed a large, naturally occurring loadstone among its mineral collections.
A loadstone can attract small pieces of iron due to its magnetic properties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magnetite - a magnetic mineral, the chief source of iron ore
  • Synonym: magnet - an object or material that produces a magnetic field

Etymology

From Middle English *lode-stone* (guiding stone), from 'lode' (way, course) and 'stone'.
