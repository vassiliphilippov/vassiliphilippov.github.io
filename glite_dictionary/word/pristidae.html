Pristidae

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈstɪdiˌi / (AmE), / prɪˈstɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, primitive rays, commonly known as sawfishes, characterized by their elongated, saw-like snouts.
family of sawfishes (rays with saw-like snouts)

Examples

The Pristidae family includes several endangered species of sawfish.
Biologists study the unique snouts of Pristidae to understand their evolutionary history.
Conservation efforts focus on the habitat preservation of Pristidae in tropical and subtropical waters.

Etymology

From New Latin Pristidae, from Greek prístis (sawfish).
