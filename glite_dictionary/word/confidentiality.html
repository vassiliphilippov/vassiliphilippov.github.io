confidentiality

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑn.fɪˌdɛn.ʃiˈæ.lə.ti / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.fɪˌden.ʃiˈæl.ə.ti / (BrE)
the state of keeping something secret, especially to protect private or sensitive information from being disclosed
state of keeping something secret

Examples

Lawyers are required to maintain confidentiality regarding anything discussed with their clients.
Our company policy emphasizes the confidentiality of your medical records at all times.
Breaking confidentiality can have serious legal and ethical consequences.

Synset Examples

you must respect the confidentiality of your client's communications

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secrecy - the action of keeping something secret or private
  • Antonym: disclosure - the action of making new or secret information known

Etymology

From confidential +‎ -ity, influenced by Medieval Latin 'confidentialitas' (state of confidence or trust).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːn.fɪˌdɛn.ʃiˈæ.lə.ti / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.fɪˌden.ʃiˈæl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the state or quality of being kept secret or private, especially when referring to sensitive information
the state of keeping information secret

Examples

All employees must respect the confidentiality of client records.
Due to confidentiality agreements, we cannot disclose that information to the public.
Maintaining confidentiality is essential when dealing with sensitive medical data.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: secrecy - the condition of being kept secret or hidden

Etymology

From confidential + -ity, modeled after French 'confidentialité'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
