demagog

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛm.əˌɡɑɡ / (AmE), / ˈdɛməɡɒɡ / (BrE)
A political leader who seeks support by appealing to popular desires, emotions, or prejudices rather than by using rational argument.
political leader appealing to emotions/prejudices

Examples

The candidate was accused of being a demagog who played on the fears of the public.
Some historians warn of the dangers posed by a charismatic demagog in times of crisis.
His critics claimed he was a demagog rather than a responsible statesman.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demagogue - a political leader who seeks support by appealing to popular passions and prejudices

Etymology

From Greek dēmagogos (leader of the people), from dēmos (people) + agōgos (leader).
