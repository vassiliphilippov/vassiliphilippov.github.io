halftone

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæfˌtoʊn / (AmE), / ˈhɑːfˌtəʊn / (BrE)
A printed image produced by breaking up the original into small dots of varying size and spacing, commonly used in printing photographs.
a print made by photoengraving (dots method)

Examples

The magazine featured a halftone of the famous portrait on its cover.
Early newspapers often used halftones to reproduce photographs for mass printing.
She admired the delicate shading in the halftone print of the landscape.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: photogravure - an image produced from a photographic negative using photomechanical processes

Etymology

Formed from 'half' + 'tone', referring to the reproduction of intermediate tones in printing.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhæfˌtoʊn / (AmE), / ˈhɑːf.təʊn / (BrE)
an engraving or printing process that reproduces an illustration using small dots to create gradients of shade
engraving method for illustrations

Examples

The magazine used a halftone to accurately reproduce the photograph on its cover.
Old books often contain halftone engravings that give images a distinctive dot pattern.
He specialized in creating halftone plates for newspapers and illustrated journals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engraving - the process or art of cutting or carving a design on a hard surface
  • Synonym: photogravure - an intaglio printmaking or photo-mechanical process

Etymology

From 'half' + 'tone', referring to the technique's ability to reproduce tones between black and white.
