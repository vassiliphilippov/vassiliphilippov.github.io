patrimony

1. NOUN

/ ˈpætrəˌmoʊni / (AmE), / ˈpætrɪməni / (BrE)
Property or an endowment belonging by inheritance or donation, especially to a church.
property or endowment (esp. church)

Examples

The ancient monastery was sustained for centuries by a generous patrimony endowed by noble families.
The bishop safeguarded the church's patrimony through careful management of its lands and assets.
After the reforms, much of the church’s patrimony was seized by the state.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inheritance - property or money received from someone after death
  • Synonym: legacy - money or property left to someone in a will

Etymology

From Latin patrimonium, from pater (father).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpætrəˌmoʊni / (AmE), / ˈpætrɪməni / (BrE)
property or inheritance received by right of birth, especially that passed down from father to son or by primogeniture.
inheritance by right of birth

Examples

The family patrimony was divided among the three sons after their father's death.
Many ancient estates were preserved as patrimony and passed to the eldest heir.
Artworks collected over centuries became part of the town's cultural patrimony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inheritance - property or money received from someone after their death

Etymology

From Latin patrimonium, from pater (father) + -monium (suffix indicating property).
