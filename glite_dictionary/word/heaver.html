heaver

1. NOUN

/ ˈhiːvər / (AmE), / ˈhiːvə / (BrE)
a worker whose job is to lift or move heavy freight or goods, especially at a dockyard
worker who lifts heavy goods, esp. at docks

Examples

The heaver loaded crates onto the ship all morning.
Without the help of a skilled heaver, moving the heavy barrels would have taken much longer.
Many heavers worked at the bustling port during the busy season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stevedore - a person employed at a dock to load and unload ships

Etymology

From the verb 'heave' (to lift or haul), with agent suffix '-er'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhiːvər / (AmE), / ˈhiːvə / (BrE)
a bar or tool used as a lever, especially for twisting rope or heavy lifting
tool used as a lever or for twisting rope

Examples

The sailor used a heaver to tighten the rope around the mast.
A wooden heaver can help twist wire when building fences.
With a sturdy heaver, the workers managed to move the heavy crate into place.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lever - a rigid bar used to move or lift a load

Etymology

From the verb 'heave' (to lift or haul), plus the agentive suffix '-er'.
