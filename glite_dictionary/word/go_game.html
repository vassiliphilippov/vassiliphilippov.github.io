go (game)

1. NOUN

/ ɡoʊ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ / (BrE)
an ancient strategic board game for two players in which black and white counters are placed on a grid, with the aim of surrounding and capturing the opponent's counters
strategic board game involving capturing counters

Examples

He spent the evening playing go with his friends at the club.
Learning the rules of go can take a few minutes, but mastering the game takes a lifetime.
Some say that go is even more complex than chess in terms of strategy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Weiqi - the Chinese name for the game of Go
  • Synonym: baduk - the Korean name for the game of Go
  • Synonym: chess - a strategic board game for two players involving checkmate

Etymology

From Japanese 'go', meaning 'encircling game', derived from Chinese 'wéiqí'.
