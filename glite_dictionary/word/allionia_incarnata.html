Allionia incarnata

1. NOUN

/ əˌloʊni.ə ɪnˌkɑːrˈneɪtə / (AmE), / ælɪˌəʊniə ɪnˌkɑːˈneɪtə / (BrE)
A trailing plant found in the southwestern United States and Mexico, noted for its clusters of three deep pink flowers that appear as a single bloom, typically growing in dry gravelly or sandy soils.
species of trailing plant with pink flowers

Examples

Allionia incarnata is often called trailing windmills due to the shape of its blossoms.
The vibrant pink flowers of Allionia incarnata brighten the arid landscapes of the Southwest each spring.
Botanists admire Allionia incarnata for its unique floral structure, where three flowers appear fused into one.

Etymology

From the genus name 'Allionia', honoring Italian botanist Carlo Allioni, and Latin 'incarnata' meaning 'flesh-colored'.
