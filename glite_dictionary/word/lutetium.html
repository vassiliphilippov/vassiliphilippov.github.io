lutetium

1. NOUN

/ luˈtiʃiəm / (AmE), / luːˈtiːsiəm / (BrE)
a silvery-white trivalent metallic element of the rare earth group, found in association with yttrium and used in various specialized alloys and scientific research
a rare earth metallic element (Lu)

Examples

Some researchers use lutetium to make certain types of medical imaging agents.
The pure form of lutetium is difficult to obtain due to its scarcity in nature.
Yttrium and lutetium often occur together in the same mineral deposits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yttrium - a chemical element often found with lutetium

Etymology

From Latin Lutetia, the Roman name for Paris, where the element was first identified.
