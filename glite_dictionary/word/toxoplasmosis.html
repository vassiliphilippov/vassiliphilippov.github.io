toxoplasmosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɑːk.soʊ.plæzˈmoʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌtɒk.səʊ.plæzˈməʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
a disease caused by infection with the parasite Toxoplasma gondii, transmitted chiefly through contact with cat feces or undercooked meat, which can cause serious complications, especially in pregnant women and immunocompromised individuals
disease caused by Toxoplasma gondii infection
medical

Examples

Pregnant women are routinely tested for toxoplasmosis to protect the developing fetus.
Severe cases of toxoplasmosis can threaten the health of people with weakened immune systems.
Toxoplasmosis is often contracted by exposure to cat litter contaminated with the parasite.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toxoplasma - the parasite causing toxoplasmosis
  • Degree Higher: infection - the process of being infected by a pathogen

Etymology

From New Latin Toxoplasma (the genus of the parasite) + -osis (denoting a disease).
