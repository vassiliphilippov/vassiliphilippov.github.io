kiln-dried

1. ADJ

/ kɪlnˈdraɪd / (AmE), / kɪlnˈdraɪd / (BrE)
(of wood, clay, etc.) dried using a kiln, especially to reduce moisture content for processing or use.
dried in a kiln

Examples

Many furniture makers prefer using kiln-dried lumber for its durability and stability.
The potter selected kiln-dried clay to ensure the pieces would not crack during firing.
You should use kiln-dried firewood for a cleaner and more efficient burn.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: air-dried - dried by exposure to air rather than in a kiln

Etymology

From 'kiln', a furnace or oven for firing and drying, and 'dried', past participle of 'dry'.
