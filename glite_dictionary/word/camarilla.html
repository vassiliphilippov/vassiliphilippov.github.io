camarilla

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæməˈrɪlə / (AmE), / ˌkaməˈrɪlə / (BrE)
A small, often secret group of people who seek to direct or influence those in power, typically through intrigue or manipulation.
a secretive group seeking power

Examples

The president was rumored to be influenced by a powerful camarilla operating behind the scenes.
Suspicion grew that a camarilla was manipulating the minister's decisions for their own benefit.
Historical accounts reveal that a secret camarilla often swayed the monarch's choices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clique - a small, exclusive group of people

Etymology

From Spanish 'camarilla', diminutive of 'cámara' (chamber), originally meaning a group gathering in a chamber.
