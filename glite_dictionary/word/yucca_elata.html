Yucca elata

1. NOUN

/ ˈjʌkə ɪˈleɪtə / (AmE), / ˈjʌkə ɪˈleɪtə / (BrE)
a tall, tree-like yucca native to the southwestern United States, known for its slender trunk and clusters of white flowers
a tall yucca plant of the southwestern US

Examples

The Yucca elata can reach up to fifteen feet in height in its natural desert habitat.
Many Native American communities have traditionally used fibers from the Yucca elata for making baskets and rope.
During spring, the Yucca elata displays striking clusters of creamy white flowers atop its tall stalk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yucca - genus of plants with sword-shaped leaves and white flowers

Etymology

From the genus name 'Yucca' and the species epithet 'elata', from Latin 'elatus' meaning 'tall'.
