redux

1. ADJ

/ riˈdʌks / (AmE), / riˈdʌks / (BrE)
brought back; revived, especially in a new or updated form.
revived; brought back again

Examples

The film is a redux of a classic story, updated for a modern audience.
After years of obscurity, the brand has made a surprising redux in contemporary fashion.
His latest novel is like his earlier work, but with a redux twist reflecting current social issues.

Synset Examples

the Victorian era redux
‘Rabbit Redux’ by John Updike

Related Terms

  • Synonym: revived - restored or brought back to life or use
  • Synonym: resurrected - brought back to existence or use

Etymology

From Latin 'redux' (brought back), from 'reducere' (to bring back).
