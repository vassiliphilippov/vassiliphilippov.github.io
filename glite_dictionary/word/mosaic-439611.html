mosaic

1. NOUN

/ moʊˈzeɪɪk / (AmE), / məʊˈzeɪɪk / (BrE)
A light-sensitive layer forming the transducer surface in a television camera tube, used to convert optical images into electrical signals.
light-sensitive surface in a TV camera tube

Examples

The engineer replaced the damaged mosaic in the television camera tube to restore its image quality.
A high-quality mosaic ensures clearer video signals in broadcast equipment.
Advances in materials technology have improved the sensitivity of the mosaic in modern cameras.

Etymology

From Latin 'mosaicum', from Greek 'mousaikos' (of the Muses).

2. NOUN

/ moʊˈzeɪ.ɪk / (AmE), / məʊˈzeɪ.ɪk / (BrE)
a pattern or image composed of many small, often colored, pieces or elements arranged to create a design, or something that resembles such a pattern.
pattern made of many small pieces

Examples

The tablecloth featured a mosaic of bright colors and geometric shapes.
Satellite images revealed a mosaic of farmland stretching for miles.
She arranged the photos on her wall in a mosaic to create a beautiful display.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collage - an artistic composition made of various materials mounted on a surface

Etymology

From Latin 'mosaicus', from Greek 'mouseion' (a place devoted to the Muses, hence a place of study or artistic creation).

3. NOUN

/ moʊˈzeɪɪk / (AmE), / məʊˈzeɪɪk / (BrE)
A viral disease affecting certain plants, such as tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco, characterized by mottled and often shriveled leaves.
plant viral disease causing leaf mottling
medical

Examples

Cucumber plants infected with mosaic often show yellow and green patches on their leaves.
The tomato crop suffered severe losses due to mosaic spreading rapidly through the field.
Experts recommend using virus-resistant seeds to prevent mosaic in potato plants.

4. NOUN

/ moʊˈzeɪɪk / (AmE), / məʊˈzeɪɪk / (BrE)
an arrangement of aerial photographs combined to form a single composite image, often used in surveying or mapping
composite of aerial photographs

Examples

The survey team created a mosaic from hundreds of overlapping aerial images.
A detailed mosaic was produced to assist with the environmental study of the region.
Careful alignment is crucial when constructing a mosaic for accurate mapping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: photomosaic - a mosaic-like picture created from many smaller photographs

Etymology

From Greek *mousaikos* (of the Muses), via Latin *mosaicum*; originally from the art of assembling small pieces to form an image.

5. NOUN

/ moʊˈzeɪɪk / (AmE), / məʊˈzeɪɪk / (BrE)
a picture or pattern produced by arranging together small pieces of colored stone, glass, or other materials, used as decoration in art or architecture
artwork made of small colored pieces

Examples

The ancient floor was decorated with a stunning mosaic of geometric designs.
She is learning to create a mosaic using colored glass tiles for her art class.
Many cathedrals feature a large mosaic above the altar depicting religious scenes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tessellation - an arrangement of shapes closely fitted together in a repeated pattern without gaps or overlapping

Etymology

From Old French 'mosaique', from Italian 'mosaico', from Latin 'mosaicus', from Greek 'mousaikos' (pertaining to the Muses).
