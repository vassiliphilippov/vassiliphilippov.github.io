belligerency

1. NOUN

/ bəˈlɪdʒərəntsi / (AmE), / bəˈlɪdʒərəntsi / (BrE)
the state or condition of being engaged in active hostilities or warfare
state of active warfare or hostilities

Examples

The government's refusal to recognize the belligerency of the rebels complicated peace negotiations.
International law regulates the rights and duties of states involved in belligerency.
The sudden outbreak of belligerency caught the neighboring countries by surprise.

Synset Examples

the outbreak of hostilities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hostility - hostile behavior, unfriendliness or opposition

Etymology

From Latin belligerans (waging war), from bellum (war) + gerere (to wage, to carry on).

2. NOUN

/ bəˈlɪdʒɚənsi / (AmE), / bəˈlɪdʒərənsi / (BrE)
a hostile or warlike attitude or state; the condition of being engaged in war or conflict
hostile or warlike attitude; state of being at war

Examples

The country's belligerency alarmed its neighbors and prompted calls for international intervention.
Historically, recognition of belligerency has had significant implications in international law.
Despite its belligerency, the state eventually sought a diplomatic resolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hostility - hostile or unfriendly state
  • Antonym: peacefulness - state of being peaceful

Etymology

From Latin 'belligerans' (waging war), from 'bellum' (war) + 'gerere' (to wage), with the English suffix '-ency'.
