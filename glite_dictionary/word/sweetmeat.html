sweetmeat

1. NOUN

/ ˈswitˌmit / (AmE), / ˈswiːt.miːt / (BrE)
A small piece of sweet food, such as a preserve or confection, typically eaten as a treat.
small sweet delicacy or confection
archaic

Examples

During the festival, children received colorful sweetmeats wrapped in shiny paper.
The queen's banquet table was piled high with candied fruits and other sweetmeats.
He offered his guests a selection of sweetmeat after the meal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confection - a sweet food made by combining sugar with other ingredients
  • Synonym: pastry - a baked food made from dough and often filled with something sweet

Etymology

From Middle English 'swetmete', literally 'sweet meat', referring to sweet foods as opposed to savory meats.
