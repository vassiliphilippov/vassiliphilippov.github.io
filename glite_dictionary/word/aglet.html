aglet

1. NOUN

/ ˈæɡlət / (AmE), / ˈaɡlɪt / (BrE)
an ornamental tagged cord or braid, often worn on the shoulder of a uniform
decorative cord or braid on a uniform shoulder

Examples

The officer adjusted the aglet on his shoulder before stepping onto the parade ground.
Ceremonial uniforms often feature a golden aglet that drapes elegantly from the epaulet.
Each cadet was required to sew a silver aglet onto their jacket as part of the dress code.

Etymology

From Old French *aiguillette* (small needle), diminutive of *aiguille* (needle).

2. NOUN

/ ˈæɡlət / (AmE), / ˈaɡlɪt / (BrE)
a small sheath, often made of metal or plastic, placed on the end of a shoelace or ribbon to prevent it from fraying
metal or plastic tip of a shoelace

Examples

The aglet at the end of my shoelace broke off, making it hard to lace my shoes.
She replaced the frayed aglets on her hoodie strings with new plastic ones.
Most people don't realize the small plastic tip on their shoelaces is called an aglet.

Etymology

From Old French *aguillette*, diminutive of *aguille* (needle), from Latin *acucula*.
