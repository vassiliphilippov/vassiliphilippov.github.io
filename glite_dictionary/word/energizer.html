energizer

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛnɚˌdʒaɪzɚ / (AmE), / ˈɛnəˌdʒaɪzə / (BrE)
a person who imparts energy, vitality, and enthusiasm to others
person who energizes others

Examples

Julia is such an energizer at meetings that everyone feels motivated by her enthusiasm.
The team's energizer kept morale high during the long project.
Having an energizer in the group can really make a difference in productivity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: motivator - person who motivates others

Etymology

Formed from the verb 'energize' + agent noun suffix '-er'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛnərˌdʒaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈɛnədʒʌɪzə / (BrE)
a device that provides electrical energy to a circuit or system
device providing electrical energy

Examples

The electric fence uses an energizer to keep animals inside the enclosure.
A solar-powered energizer was installed to provide energy for the remote weather station.
Before connecting the device, make sure the energizer is turned off.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: power supply - a device that provides power to electric circuits

Etymology

From 'energize' + '-er', modeled after agent nouns; related to 'energy'.
