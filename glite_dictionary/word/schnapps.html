schnapps

1. NOUN

/ ʃnɑps / (AmE), / ʃnaps / (BrE)
A strong distilled spirit, especially one of Dutch or German origin and often made from potatoes or grains.
strong distilled liquor, often Dutch or German

Examples

He poured himself a glass of schnapps to warm up after the long walk.
In some German villages, homemade schnapps is still offered as a welcoming drink.
The bar featured a wide selection of fruit-flavored schnapps.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brandy - a strong alcoholic spirit distilled from wine or fermented fruit juice

Etymology

German Schnaps (spirit, dram), from schnappen (to snap up).
