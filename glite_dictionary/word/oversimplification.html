oversimplification

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvərˌsɪmplɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˌsɪmplɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of describing or explaining something in a way that makes it seem simpler than it really is, often resulting in misunderstanding or misrepresentation
excessively simple explanation; misrepresentation

Examples

The article's oversimplification of a complex subject led to several misunderstandings.
History often suffers from oversimplification when important facts are omitted for brevity.
His explanation was criticized for being an oversimplification that ignored key details.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: simplification - the process of making something simpler or easier to understand

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' (excessively) + 'simplification'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvərˌsɪmpləfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˌsɪmplɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making something simpler than it really is, often ignoring important details or complexities
excessive simplification

Examples

The report's explanation was an oversimplification of the real issues involved.
To say that stress causes illness is an oversimplification of a complex relationship.
His argument was dismissed as a mere oversimplification of the facts.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: simplification - the process of making something simpler

Etymology

From over- (excessively) + simplification (the process of making something simple).
