fob

1. NOUN

/ fɑb / (AmE), / fɒb / (BrE)
A short chain or ribbon attached to a pocket watch, used to secure it to a person's vest or waistband.
chain or ribbon for attaching pocket watch

Examples

He carefully tucked the fob of his pocket watch into his vest pocket.
The antique fob was made of gold and had an engraved family crest.
Victorian gentlemen often carried their watches on a chain or fob.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watch chain - a chain for attaching a watch to clothing

Etymology

Probably from Low German *Fobke* (pocket or small case).

2. NOUN

/ fɑb / (AmE), / fɒb / (BrE)
a small ornament or decorative attachment that hangs from a watch chain, often used as a charm or for practical purposes
ornament attached to a watch chain

Examples

He attached a gold fob to his pocket watch for a touch of elegance.
The antique dealer showed me a silver fob with an intricate design.
Her grandfather's watch still had a tiny fob dangling from its chain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watch chain - a chain attached to a pocket watch

Etymology

Probably from dialectal English *fob* (small pocket for valuables), origin uncertain.

3. VERB

/ fɑb / (AmE), / fɒb / (BrE)
to deceive or trick (someone), especially by making them accept something false or less valuable
deceive or trick someone
archaic

Examples

The merchant tried to fob me off with a cheap imitation instead of the genuine article.
She suspected he was trying to fob her with a false excuse.
They attempted to fob the customer by promising a refund they never intended to give.

Synset Examples

We tricked the teacher into thinking that class would be cancelled next week

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trick - to deceive or cheat someone
  • Synonym: dupe - to deceive someone, usually by trickery

Etymology

Probably from Old French *forbir* (to polish, embellish deceitfully).

4. NOUN

/ fɑb / (AmE), / fɒb / (BrE)
a small pocket in a vest designed to hold a pocket watch
vest pocket for a pocket watch
archaic

Examples

He kept his gold watch in the fob of his waistcoat.
The tailor stitched a small fob inside the vest for convenience.
In Victorian times, a gentleman's fob was an essential part of his attire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: watch pocket - A small pocket in clothing for carrying a watch

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly related to the Low German 'fobben' (to poke, fool), or 'Fuppe' (pocket).
