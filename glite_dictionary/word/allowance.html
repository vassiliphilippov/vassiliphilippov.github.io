allowance

1. NOUN

/ əˈlaʊəns / (AmE), / əˈlaʊəns / (BrE)
an amount of money or a quantity of something that is given to someone regularly or for a specific purpose
a sum or amount regularly given

Examples

She receives a weekly allowance from her parents for doing chores around the house.
The company provides a travel allowance to cover accommodation and meals during business trips.
Each member of the team has a monthly allowance for office supplies.

Synset Examples

travel allowance
my weekly allowance of two eggs
a child's allowance should not be too generous

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grant - a sum of money given by an organization for a particular purpose

Etymology

From Old French 'alouance' (permission, granting), from 'alouer' (to allocate).

/ əˈlaʊəns / (AmE), / əˈlaʊəns / (BrE)

Examples

A large allowance was established to cover potential decreases in property values.

7. VERB

/ əˈlaʊ.əns / (AmE), / əˈlaʊ.əns / (BrE)
to put (someone) on a fixed allowance, especially of food or money, often as a means of control or rationing
to set a fixed share or portion for someone

Examples

The children were allowanced only two sweets each evening to encourage moderation.
To ensure fairness, the residents were allowanced supplies based on household size.
