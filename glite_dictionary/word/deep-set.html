deep-set

1. ADJ

/ ˈdipˌsɛt / (AmE), / ˈdiːpˌsɛt / (BrE)
(of eyes or other features) positioned far back from the surface, giving a sunken or recessed appearance.
set far back; sunken appearance

Examples

Her deep-set eyes made her look mysterious and intense.
The artist captured his subject's deep-set cheekbones with remarkable detail.
Years of hardship gave him a face with deep-set features.

Synset Examples

hunger gave their faces a sunken look

Related Terms

  • Antonym: protruding - (of eyes or features) sticking out beyond the surrounding surface

2. ADJ

/ ˌdipˈsɛt / (AmE), / ˌdiːpˈsɛt / (BrE)
(of eyes or facial features) positioned far back from the surface of the face; recessed.
recessed eyes or features

Examples

Her deep-set eyes gave her a mysterious appearance.
He had deep-set features that made his expression look intense.
The character is described as a man with deep-set eyes and a furrowed brow.

Synset Examples

His deep-set features made him look more serious.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prominent - (of eyes or features) standing out from the surface of the face
