whelp

1. NOUN

/ wɛlp / (AmE), / wɛlp / (BrE)
a young offspring of a dog, wolf, or other member of the canine family
young of a canine animal

Examples

The whelp whimpered softly as its mother nuzzled it in the den.
Wolves typically keep their whelps hidden for several weeks after birth.
The biologist observed a litter of fox whelps playing near the entrance to their burrow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puppy - young dog

Etymology

From Old English hwelp (puppy), of Germanic origin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01324834-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ wɛlp / (AmE), / wɛlp / (BrE)
(of a female dog or other carnivorous mammal) to give birth to young, especially puppies.
give birth to puppies (animal verb)

Examples

The old shepherd watched as his prized collie whelped a healthy litter of pups.
She whelped four adorable puppies last spring.
The veterinarian assisted while the fox whelped in the den.

Synset Examples

the dog whelped

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to give birth - to produce offspring

Etymology

Old English hwelp (puppy, cub), related to medieval Dutch and Old Norse hvelpr.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00058239-v
Synset POS: v
