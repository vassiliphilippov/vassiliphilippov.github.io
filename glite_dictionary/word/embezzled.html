embezzled

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈbɛzəld / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɛzəld / (BrE)
(of money or property) taken dishonestly for one's own use, especially by someone trusted to manage it.
taken dishonestly for personal use

Examples

The accountant was found guilty of transferring embezzled assets into secret accounts.
Investigators discovered a large sum of embezzled money missing from the nonprofit's funds.
The report detailed the recovery of embezzled property by the authorities.

Synset Examples

the banker absconded with embezzled funds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stolen - taken without permission or legal right
  • Synonym: misappropriated - wrongfully taken for one's own use, especially money or property

Etymology

From embezzle (verb), ultimately from Old French 'embesillier'.
