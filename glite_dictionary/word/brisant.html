brisant

1. ADJ

/ briˈzɑnt / (AmE), / briˈzɑnt / (BrE)
relating to the shattering power or explosive strength of a substance, especially an explosive.
having shattering power (of explosives)

Examples

The new chemical compound proved to be especially brisant, capable of shattering concrete barriers.
A brisant explosive is preferred in demolition work for its high shattering effect.
Researchers measured the brisant properties of various formulations to determine which was most effective.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: explosive - a substance or device that causes an explosion

Etymology

From French 'brisant', from 'briser' (to break).
