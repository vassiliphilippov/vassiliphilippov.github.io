Homo sapiens neanderthalensis

1. NOUN

/ ˌhoʊmoʊ ˈsæpiːənz niːˌæn.dərˈθæl.ɛn.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌhəʊməʊ ˈsæpiːənz niːˌæn.dəˈθæl.ɛn.sɪs / (BrE)
an extinct robust subspecies of humans that lived in Europe and western Asia during the Middle Paleolithic period, commonly known as Neanderthals
extinct subspecies of robust early humans

Examples

Fossils of Homo sapiens neanderthalensis have been found in both Europe and Asia.
Recent studies show that Homo sapiens neanderthalensis shared some genes with modern humans.
The tools used by Homo sapiens neanderthalensis reveal their sophisticated hunting techniques.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Neanderthal - colloquial name for Homo sapiens neanderthalensis
  • Antonym: Homo sapiens sapiens - the subspecies to which all modern humans belong

Etymology

From Latin: 'Homo' (man), 'sapiens' (wise), 'neanderthalensis' (from Neanderthal, the valley in Germany where remains were first found)
