flexitarianism

1. NOUN

/ ˌflɛksɪˈtɛriəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌflɛksɪˈteəriənɪzəm / (BrE)
The practice of eating a mostly vegetarian diet while occasionally including meat or fish.
mostly vegetarian but flexible diet

Examples

Flexitarianism is gaining popularity among people who want to reduce their meat consumption without cutting it out entirely.
Many restaurants offer menus that cater to flexitarianism as well as vegetarian and vegan diets.
Her commitment to flexitarianism means she occasionally enjoys seafood, though her meals are mostly plant-based.

Synset Examples

Flexitarianism will be the next ‘mega trend’ leading to sales of vegetarian foods in the UK to grow by 10% by 2016, according to food trends agency The Food People.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: vegetarianism - the practice of eating no meat or fish
  • Degree Lower: veganism - the practice of eating no animal products at all

Etymology

Blend of 'flexible' and 'vegetarian', coined in English in the late 20th century.
