Hyperborean

1. NOUN

/ ˌhaɪpərˈbɔriən / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəˈbɔːrɪən / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a member of a legendary people believed by the ancient Greeks to inhabit a land of perpetual sunshine and happiness far to the north of Thrace.
mythical northern people in Greek mythology

Examples

According to the myth, the Hyperborean people lived in a paradise where the sun never set.
Some poets referenced the Hyperborean as symbols of peace and eternal happiness.
The explorers dreamed of finding a real land like that of the Hyperborean legend.

Etymology

From Greek Hyperboreoi, from hyper ('beyond') + Boreas ('the north wind').
