rating

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈreɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
a position or rank held by someone in a military or naval organization, especially below commissioned officer level
military or naval rank

Examples

He held the rating of petty officer in the navy.
Only enlisted personnel with the appropriate rating could carry out the duties required.
Promotions to a higher rating were based on time served and performance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rank - a position in an organization, especially the armed forces

2. NOUN

/ ˈreɪt̬ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈreɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of determining or assigning a value, rank, or worth to something
determining value or rank

Examples

The restaurant received a high rating from the food critics.
His job involves the rating of antique furniture for insurance purposes.
Each movie is given a rating based on its content and quality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evaluation - the act of judging or assessing something
  • Synonym: assessment - the act of evaluating or estimating the nature, quality, or ability of someone or something
  • Antonym: devaluation - the act of reducing or lowering the value, especially of currency or worth

Etymology

From the verb 'rate', from Middle English *raten*, from Old French *reter* (to estimate), from Latin *rata* (a reckoning).

3. NOUN

/ ˈreɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈreɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
a position or score given on a scale, especially to show how good someone or something is compared to others
standing or score on a scale

Examples

The movie received a high rating from critics.
Her performance improved her rating in the world tennis rankings.
We are proud of our top safety rating this year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ranking - a position in a list that shows how good someone or something is compared to others
  • Synonym: score - a number that expresses how well someone has done in a test or competition
  • Synonym: grade - a mark given in an examination or for a piece of schoolwork

Etymology

From rate + -ing; 'rate' from Old French 'rate', from Medieval Latin 'rata' (calculated), with '-ing' as a noun-forming suffix.

4. NOUN

/ ˈreɪ.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈreɪ.tɪŋ / (BrE)
an assessment or estimation of the value, quality, or importance of something
an appraisal or evaluation

Examples

The movie received a surprisingly high rating from critics.
He was disappointed by the low rating his proposal received.
The restaurant’s excellent rating attracted many new customers.

Synset Examples

he set a high valuation on friendship

Related Terms

  • Synonym: valuation - an estimation of something's worth, especially by an appraiser
  • Synonym: assessment - the act of evaluating or appraising a person or thing
  • Antonym: devaluation - the reduction in or underestimation of the worth or importance of something

Etymology

From the verb 'rate', ultimately from Latin 'ratus' (reckoned, determined).
