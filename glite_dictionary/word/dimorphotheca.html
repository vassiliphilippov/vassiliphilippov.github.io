Dimorphotheca

1. NOUN

/ daɪˌmɔrfəˈθikə / (AmE), / daɪˌmɔːfəˈθiːkə / (BrE)
A genus of South African herbs or subshrubs typically having yellow or brightly colored flowers, often cultivated as ornamentals.
genus of South African flowering plants

Examples

The Dimorphotheca bloomed early in the spring, spreading yellow and orange petals across the garden.
Several species in the Dimorphotheca genus are prized for their drought tolerance in ornamental landscaping.
Botanists discovered a new variety of Dimorphotheca thriving in arid regions of South Africa.

Etymology

From Greek 'di-' (two), 'morphē' (form), and 'theke' (case), referring to two forms of fruit produced by plants in this genus.
