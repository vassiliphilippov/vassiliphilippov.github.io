ransacked

1. ADJ

/ ˈræn.sækt / (AmE), / ˈræn.sækt / (BrE)
(of a place) having been thoroughly searched and stripped of valuables, typically in a forceful or lawless manner.
thoroughly looted; stripped of valuables

Examples

The house looked completely ransacked after the burglars had broken in and searched every room.
Returning to their ransacked office, they found papers scattered and computers missing.
The museum preserved some artifacts, but much of the collection was ransacked during the conflict.

Synset Examples

the robbers left the looted train
people returned to the plundered village

Related Terms

  • Synonym: looted - stolen from or stripped of valuables, especially during war or unrest
  • Synonym: plundered - having had goods stolen, especially by force or during war

Etymology

From Middle English 'ransaken', from Old Norse 'rannsaka' (to search a house).
