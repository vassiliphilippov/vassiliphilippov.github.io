fate

1. NOUN

/ feɪt / (AmE), / feɪt / (BrE)
a person's overall circumstances or condition in life, including everything that happens to them
overall circumstances in life

Examples

She believed her fate was to become a writer, no matter what happened.
Sometimes, people feel powerless to change their fate in life.
He accepted his fate with quiet determination and courage.

Synset Examples

whatever my fortune may be
deserved a better fate
has a happy lot
the luck of the Irish
a victim of circumstances
success that was her portion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fortune - chance or luck as it affects one's life
  • Synonym: lot - one's general situation or fate in life

Etymology

From Latin fatum (that which has been spoken; destiny, fate), past participle of fari (to speak).

2. VERB

/ feɪt / (AmE), / feɪt / (BrE)
to decree or designate (someone or something) to a particular outcome, typically by destiny or a higher power
determine or designate beforehand

Examples

Some believed he was fated to lead the country through difficult times.
It seemed as if their meeting was fated from the start.
The tragedy was fated to happen, despite all their precautions.

Synset Examples

She was destined to become a great pianist

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to destine - to intend or determine beforehand; to decree as a future event

Etymology

From Latin fatum (prophetic declaration, destiny).

Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ feɪt / (AmE), / feɪt / (BrE)
A power or agency believed to predetermine and control the course of future events; destiny.
supernatural power controlling events

Examples

Many ancient cultures believed that fate controlled a person's life from beginning to end.
She wondered if meeting him was simply a coincidence or the work of fate.
The story explores whether our lives are shaped by fate or our own choices.

Synset Examples

we are helpless in the face of destiny

Related Terms

  • Synonym: destiny - the hidden power believed to control future events
  • Antonym: chance - the occurrence of events in the absence of apparent intention or cause

Etymology

From Latin fatum (that which has been spoken, decree of the gods).

Source: WordNet

4. NOUN

/ feɪt / (AmE), / feɪt / (BrE)
a power or force that is believed to determine the course of events, or an inevitable future event, especially one thought to be predetermined or unavoidable
inevitable future event or destiny

Examples

Many believe it was fate that brought them together after so many years apart.
The hero bravely accepted his fate, no matter how uncertain it seemed.
Some people try to challenge their fate instead of accepting what appears inevitable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: destiny - the events that will necessarily happen to a person or thing in the future
  • Degree Lower: doom - a terrible fate; an unavoidable ill destiny
  • Antonym: chance - the occurrence of events without apparent cause or preordained purpose

Etymology

From Latin 'fatum' (that which has been spoken, destiny), from 'fari' (to speak).

Source: WordNet
