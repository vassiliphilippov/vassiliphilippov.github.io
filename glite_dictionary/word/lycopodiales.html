Lycopodiales

1. NOUN

/ laɪ.kəˌpoʊ.diˈeɪliːz / (AmE), / laɪ.kəˌpɒd.iˈeɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of lower vascular plants, chiefly comprising the family Lycopodiaceae and, in some classifications, also including the families Selaginellaceae and Isoetaceae
an order of lower vascular plants

Examples

Ferns and Lycopodiales are both examples of lower vascular plants.
The Lycopodiales include clubmosses, which are important in some ecosystems.
Scientists studied fossils of Lycopodiales to learn about plant evolution.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus name Lycopodium + -ales (order suffix).
