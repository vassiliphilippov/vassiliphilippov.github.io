flyblown

1. ADJ

/ ˈflaɪˌbloʊn / (AmE), / ˈflaɪˌbləʊn / (BrE)
(of food or other substances) spoiled and infested with eggs or larvae of flies.
spoiled and infested with fly eggs or larvae

Examples

The butcher warned us not to buy the flyblown meat on display at the market.
Several crates contained flyblown fruit that smelled unpleasant and attracted insects.
We had to throw away the flyblown cheese after discovering tiny larvae inside.

Synset Examples

flyblown meat
a sack of maggoty apricots

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maggoty - infested with maggots
  • Synonym: putrid - decaying or rotting with a foul smell

Etymology

From 'fly' (the insect) + 'blown', the past participle of 'blow', in the sense 'to deposit eggs'.
