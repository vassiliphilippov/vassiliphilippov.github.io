waiver

1. NOUN

/ ˈweɪvər / (AmE), / ˈweɪvə / (BrE)
A formal written statement in which a person voluntarily gives up a right, claim, or privilege.
formal written renouncement of a right
legal

Examples

Before participating, you must sign a waiver releasing the organizers from liability.
She refused to sign the waiver because she did not want to give up her rights.
The company requires a waiver for anyone accessing the restricted area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relinquishment - the act of giving up or surrendering a right or claim
  • Antonym: claim - a demand for something as a right

Etymology

From the verb 'waive' (to relinquish), from Anglo-French 'weiver.'
