lament

1. NOUN

/ ləˈmɛnt / (AmE), / ləˈmɛnt / (BrE)
an expression of sorrow, mourning, or regret, especially in the form of a cry, song, or poem
expression of sorrow or grief

Examples

The widow's lament echoed through the silent church.
Every culture has its own lament for the passing of loved ones.
He composed a touching lament following the loss of his friend.

Synset Examples

their pitiful laments could be heard throughout the ward

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dirge - a mournful song or piece of music for the dead
  • Synonym: elegy - a poem or song of serious reflection, typically for the dead
  • Antonym: rejoicing - great joy; opposite of lament

Etymology

From Latin lamentum (wailing, moan).

2. VERB

/ ləˈmɛnt / (AmE), / ləˈmɛnt / (BrE)
to express sorrow or grief, especially by speaking or crying out
express grief or sorrow aloud

Examples

At the funeral, many people lamented the passing of their beloved friend.
She lamented the loss of her childhood home for years.
The poet lamented the suffering of his people in his moving speech.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mourn - to feel or show deep sorrow or regret for someone or something lost
  • Antonym: to rejoice - to feel or show great joy or delight

Etymology
