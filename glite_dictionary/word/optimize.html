to optimize

1. VERB

/ ˈɑptɪˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɒptɪmaɪz / (BrE)
to make as effective, perfect, or functional as possible; to use something in the best possible way
make as effective or efficient as possible

Examples

The company hired a consultant to optimize their production processes.
You need to optimize your workflow to save time and resources.
We are constantly working to optimize our website for faster loading speeds.

Synset Examples

optimize your resources

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to maximize - to make as large or great as possible
  • Antonym: to minimize - to make as small as possible

Etymology

From Greek 'optimos' (best); modern use via 'optimal' + '-ize'.

2. VERB

/ ˈɑptɪˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɒptɪmaɪz / (BrE)
to make something as effective, perfect, or useful as possible, especially by improving its efficiency or performance
make as efficient or effective as possible

Examples

Engineers are working to optimize the design for greater fuel efficiency.
We need to optimize our website to load faster for users.
She optimized the storage system to save both time and money.

Synset Examples

optimize a computer program

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to improve - to make or become better
  • Synonym: to maximize - to increase something as much as possible
  • Antonym: to worsen - to make or become worse
  • Antonym: to minimize - to reduce something to the least possible amount or degree

Etymology

From Latin 'optimus' (best) and English suffix '-ize'.

Related Terms
