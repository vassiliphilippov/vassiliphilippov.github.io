levy

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛvi / (AmE), / ˈlɛvi / (BrE)
the act of enlisting people for compulsory military service
act of drafting into military service

Examples

The government issued a levy to build up its army during wartime.
During the 19th century, a levy could force young men into service.
The new levy caused widespread protests among the populace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conscription - the compulsory enlistment of people in military service
  • Degree Lower: recruitment - the act of enlisting people for service or work

Etymology

From Old French 'levée' (a raising), from Latin 'levare' (to raise).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01160551-n

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɛvi / (AmE), / ˈlɛvi / (BrE)
an amount of money, such as a tax or fee, that is officially imposed and collected by an authority
an imposed tax or fee

Examples

The government announced a new levy on sugar-sweetened drinks.
Failure to pay the levy on time can result in additional penalties.
A special levy was introduced to fund the construction of the new bridge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tax - a compulsory contribution to state revenue
  • Degree Lower: fine - a sum of money exacted as a penalty
  • Degree Higher: surcharge - an additional charge or payment

Etymology

From Old French *levée*, from lever "to raise" (from Latin *levare*)

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-13329886-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈlɛvi / (AmE), / ˈlɛvi / (BrE)
to assemble or enlist (troops or an army), especially by official order or authority
assemble or enlist troops by authority

Examples

The king decided to levy an army to defend the kingdom.
In times of crisis, governments may levy troops from the population.
The feudal lord was authorized to levy soldiers for the upcoming campaign.

Synset Examples

raise an army
recruit new soldiers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: recruit - enlist new people into the armed forces

Etymology

From Old French *levée* (a raising), from Latin *levare* (to raise).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01100863-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈlɛvi / (AmE), / ˈlɛvi / (BrE)
(of an authority) to impose and collect a tax, fee, fine, or other charge officially.
impose and collect officially (e.g., taxes, fines)
legal

Examples

The government decided to levy a new tax on luxury goods.
The court has the authority to levy fines against violators of the law.
If you fail to pay, the agency may levy the amount directly from your account.

Synset Examples

levy a fine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collect - to gather or receive (something due)
  • Synonym: to impose - to officially order something (such as a tax) to be paid or obeyed

Etymology

From Old French 'levée' (a raising), from 'lever' (to raise), from Latin 'levare' (to lift).
