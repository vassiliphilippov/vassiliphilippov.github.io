greyhound

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪˌhaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪhaʊnd / (BrE)
a tall, slender breed of dog known for its speed and keen sight, often used in racing
a fast, slender breed of dog

Examples

The greyhound raced down the track, leaving the other dogs far behind.
She adopted a retired greyhound and gave it a loving home.
The slender body and long legs make the greyhound one of the fastest dog breeds.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: whippet - a small, fast breed of dog related to the greyhound
  • Synonym: saluki - a tall, slender, fast hunting dog breed closely related to the greyhound

Etymology

From Old English grīghund (dog of a certain breed), with 'grey' not relating to color but possibly from a word meaning 'bitch' or 'female dog'.
