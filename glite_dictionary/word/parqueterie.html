parqueterie

1. NOUN

/ pɑrˈkɛtəri / (AmE), / pɑːˈkɛtəri / (BrE)
a decorative technique of laying small, patterned wood pieces in geometric designs to cover a floor
patterned wood floor inlay

Examples

The grand entrance hall was adorned with elegant parqueterie dating back to the 18th century.
Restoring the original parqueterie required skilled craftsmanship and careful attention to detail.
The intricate parqueterie design impressed every visitor to the historic mansion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parquet - wooden flooring arranged in geometric patterns

Etymology

From French 'parqueterie', related to 'parquet' (wooden flooring).
