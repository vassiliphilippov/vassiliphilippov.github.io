stroll

1. NOUN

/ stroʊl / (AmE), / strəʊl / (BrE)
a slow, relaxed walk, especially taken for pleasure in a public place
leisurely walk, especially in public

Examples

After dinner, we went for a pleasant stroll in the park.
They enjoyed a leisurely stroll along the riverbank.
A short stroll around the block helped clear his mind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: walk - an act of traveling or moving on foot

Etymology

Perhaps from dialectal German 'strollen' (to wander idly).

2. VERB

/ stroʊl / (AmE), / strəʊl / (BrE)
to walk in a slow, relaxed way, often for pleasure and without a definite aim
walk in a slow, relaxed manner

Examples

After dinner, they decided to stroll along the riverside, enjoying the fresh air.
On Sundays, I often stroll through the park without any particular destination.
We strolled down the old streets, admiring the historic buildings and colorful shops.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saunter - walk in a slow, relaxed manner, without hurry or effort
  • Antonym: stride - walk with long, decisive steps, typically in a purposeful way

Etymology

Probably from Old French *estroël* (wandering), related to Middle English *strollen* (to roam).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
