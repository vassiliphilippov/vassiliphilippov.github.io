chincherinchee

1. NOUN

/ tʃɪn.tʃəˈrɪn.ki / (AmE), / tʃɪn.tʃəˈrɪŋ.ki / (BrE)
A South African perennial plant with long-lasting spikes of white flowers, often used as cut flowers.
South African flowering plant used in floristry

Examples

The florist included a single chincherinchee in the winter bouquet for a touch of elegance.
Chincherinchee flowers are prized for their beauty and longevity in floral arrangements.
She grows chincherinchees in her garden and sends them to friends during the colder months.

Etymology

From Afrikaans *tjienkerientjee*, imitative of the sound of its stalks rubbed together.
