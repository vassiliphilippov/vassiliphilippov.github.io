blastema

1. NOUN

/ blæˈstiːmə / (AmE), / blɑːˈstiːmə / (BrE)
A mass of undifferentiated cells capable of growth and regeneration into an organ or body part.
mass of undifferentiated regenerative cells
medical

Examples

The limb of the salamander regenerated from a blastema formed at the site of the injury.
Researchers studied the differentiation process within the blastema during tissue regeneration.
A healthy blastema is crucial for successful organ development in certain species.

Etymology

From Greek *blastos* (bud, sprout) via New Latin *blastēma*.
