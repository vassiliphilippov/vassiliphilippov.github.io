PABA

1. NOUN

/ ˌpiːˌeɪˌbiːˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˌpiːˌeɪˌbiːˈeɪ / (BrE)
a metabolic acid found in yeast and liver cells, used in making dyes, drugs, and sun blockers; also known as para-aminobenzoic acid
metabolic acid used in dyes and sun blockers
medical

Examples

Some sunscreen products contain PABA to help block ultraviolet rays.
The biochemistry lab analyzed the PABA content in various yeast samples.
Doctors may recommend avoiding PABA if you have sensitive skin.

Etymology

Acronym from para-aminobenzoic acid.
