spice

1. NOUN

/ spaɪs / (AmE), / spaɪs / (BrE)
any of various aromatic vegetable substances, such as pepper or cinnamon, used to flavor food
pungent vegetable substance for flavoring food

Examples

A pinch of spice can transform a bland dish into something flavorful.
Turmeric is a commonly used spice in Indian cuisine.
She stores all her spice jars neatly on a rack.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herb - a plant used for flavoring food

Etymology

From Old French espice, from Latin species (kind, sort; spices).

2. VERB

/ spaɪs / (AmE), / spaɪs / (BrE)
to season food by adding herbs or spices to give it flavor.
to add spices or herbs for flavor

Examples

She likes to spice her soup with a pinch of cumin.
You can spice the chicken before roasting it for extra flavor.
He spiced the curry generously, making it deliciously aromatic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: season - add salt, herbs, or spices to food for flavoring

Etymology

From Old French 'espice', from Latin 'species' (kind, sort; also wares, spices).

/ spaɪs / (AmE), / spaɪs / (BrE)

quality of being highly spiced or flavored

Examples

You can increase the spice of the curry by adding more chili flakes.
Her cooking is known for its generous spice and bold flavors.

Related Terms

4. NOUN

/ spaɪs / (AmE), / spaɪs / (BrE)
an aromatic substance of plant origin used especially as a preservative; often added to food to prevent spoilage or enhance flavor
aromatic preservative from plants

Examples

Long ago, traders sailed for months to bring rare spices that preserved meat during the winter.
Salt, pepper, and other spices were once highly valued for keeping food fresh.
Before refrigeration, families relied on a variety of spices to prevent spoilage of their provisions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herb - a plant used for flavoring, food, medicine, or perfume

Etymology

From Old French *espice*, from Latin *species* (kind, sort, wares, spices).
