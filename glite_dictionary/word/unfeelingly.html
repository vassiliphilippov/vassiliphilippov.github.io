unfeelingly

1. ADV

/ ʌnˈfiːlɪŋli / (AmE), / ʌnˈfiːlɪŋli / (BrE)
in a way that shows a lack of emotion, sympathy, or compassion
without emotion or compassion

Examples

He spoke unfeelingly about their difficulties, showing no sympathy at all.
The manager unfeelingly dismissed the employee without a second thought.
She unfeelingly ignored the pleas for help from her classmates.

Synset Examples

he callously exploited their feelings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: callously - in a way that shows insensitivity and disregard for others

Etymology

From 'unfeeling' (without emotion) + '-ly' (adverb suffix).

2. ADV

/ ʌnˈfiːlɪŋli / (AmE), / ʌnˈfiːlɪŋli / (BrE)
In a way that shows no compassion or sympathy; without sensitive or kind feelings.
in a manner lacking compassion or sympathy

Examples

Her manager unfeelingly dismissed her concerns about the workload.
The policy was unfeelingly enforced, regardless of individual circumstances.
He unfeelingly ignored the cries for help from those in need.

Synset Examples

unfeelingly, she required her maid to work on Christmas Day

Related Terms

  • Antonym: compassionately - in a way that shows compassion or sympathy
  • Synonym: callously - in a way that shows an insensitive and cruel disregard for others

Etymology

From the adjective 'unfeeling' + adverbial suffix '-ly'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00340873-r
Synset POS: r
