oldster

1. NOUN

/ ˈoʊld.stər / (AmE), / ˈəʊld.stə / (BrE)
an elderly person, especially one who is noticeably old or advanced in years
elderly person

Examples

The park bench was occupied by an oldster reading the morning paper.
At the reunion, the youngest child and the oldest oldster both received special prizes.
Some oldsters stay active by joining walking clubs and social groups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: senior citizen - an elderly person, often retired
  • Antonym: youngster - a young person or child

Etymology

From 'old' + suffix '-ster', originally denoting a person associated with a particular quality or activity.
