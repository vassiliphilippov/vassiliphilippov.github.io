unknowingness

1. NOUN

/ ʌnˈnoʊɪŋnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈnəʊɪŋnəs / (BrE)
the state of not knowing; lack of awareness or knowledge, especially due to inattention or ignorance
state of not knowing

Examples

Her unknowingness about the rules led to several embarrassing mistakes.
He walked through the silent halls in a state of unknowingness, oblivious to the danger around him.
The artist captured the child's unknowingness in a simple, innocent expression.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge or awareness
  • Antonym: awareness - state of being conscious or informed

Etymology

From 'unknowing' (not knowing) + '-ness' (state or quality).

2. NOUN

/ ʌnˈnoʊɪŋnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈnəʊɪŋnəs / (BrE)
the state of being unaware or ignorant, especially of accepted or orthodox beliefs
state of being unaware or ignorant

Examples

Her unknowingness about the cultural customs led to some awkward moments during the meeting.
Philosophers have often discussed the value of unknowingness in the pursuit of wisdom.
His unknowingness of the group's core beliefs was evident from his remarks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge or awareness
  • Antonym: awareness - knowledge or perception of a situation or fact

Etymology

Formed from 'unknowing' + '-ness', modeled after the pattern of English abstract nouns.
