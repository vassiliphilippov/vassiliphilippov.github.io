spoilt

1. ADJ

/ spɔɪlt / (AmE), / spɔɪlt / (BrE)
(of food) no longer safe or suitable to eat because it has decayed or gone bad.
no longer safe to eat (food)
british

Examples

We had to throw away the spoilt milk because it smelled sour.
A spoilt apple can ruin the whole basket if not removed quickly.
After the power cut, all the meat in the freezer was spoilt.

Synset Examples

bad meat
a refrigerator full of spoilt food

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spoiled - (of food) no longer fit to eat due to decay; American variant spelling
  • Antonym: fresh - (of food) recently produced or made; not preserved or spoiled

Etymology

Past participle of "spoil", from Old French "espoillier", from Latin "spoliare" (to strip, plunder).

2. ADJ

/ spɔɪlt / (AmE), / spɔɪlt / (BrE)
(of a person, especially a child) having their character or behavior negatively affected by too much indulgence or attention.
harmed by too much pampering
british

Examples

The spoilt child demanded a new toy every day and threw a tantrum when refused.
She was so spoilt by her wealthy parents that she never learned to share.
Some say the actor's spoilt attitude comes from too much adoration and too few boundaries.

Synset Examples

a spoiled child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spoiled - having the character or disposition harmed by too much pampering; American English form

Etymology

Past participle of 'spoil', from Old French *espoillier*, from Latin *spoliare* (to strip, plunder).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01142402-s
Synset POS: s
