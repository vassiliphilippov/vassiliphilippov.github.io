Cappadocia

1. PROPN

/ ˌkæp.əˈdoʊ.ʃə / (AmE), / ˌkæp.əˈdəʊ.si.ə / (BrE)
an ancient region in eastern Asia Minor, now part of modern-day Turkey, known for its unique rock formations and historical significance
ancient region in eastern Asia Minor (Turkey)

Examples

During our trip to Turkey, we visited the stunning landscapes of Cappadocia.
Cappadocia is famous for its fairy chimneys and ancient cave dwellings.
Early Christians once sought refuge in the underground cities of Cappadocia.

Etymology

From Latin Cappadocia, from Ancient Greek Kappadokia.
