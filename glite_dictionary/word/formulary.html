formulary

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɔːrmjəˌleri / (AmE), / ˈfɔːmjʊləri / (BrE)
of, relating to, or having the character of a formula, especially one that is prescribed or established by rule or convention.
relating to or like a formula

Examples

His approach to the problem was distinctly formulary, lacking originality or innovation.
The doctor followed a formulary procedure when prescribing the medication.
They criticized the report for its formulary style and repetitive structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: formulaic - constituting or containing a formula; following a set form

Etymology

From Late Latin *formularius* (of or belonging to a formula).

Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɔːrmjəˌleri / (AmE), / ˈfɔːmjʊləri / (BrE)
a book or official list containing a collection of pharmaceutical products, including their formulas and methods of preparation
official list of pharmaceutical products and formulas
medical

Examples

The hospital's formulary lists all the approved medications available for patient care.
Doctors must consult the formulary before prescribing certain drugs to ensure they are authorized.
Changes to the formulary can impact which treatments are easily accessible for patients.

Synset Examples

postexposure prophylaxis is an integral part of the pharmacopeia in preventing severe disease after acute infections

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pharmacopeia - an official publication containing a list of medicinal drugs and their standards

Etymology

From Latin 'formularius', from 'formula' (a small form, rule, or method).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06423526-n
Synset POS: n
