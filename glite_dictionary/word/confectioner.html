confectioner

1. NOUN

/ kənˈfɛk.ʃə.nɚ / (AmE), / kənˈfɛk.ʃə.nə / (BrE)
a person or company that makes or sells sweets and candies
person/company making or selling sweets

Examples

The confectioner spent hours creating intricate chocolate sculptures for the wedding.
As a child, she dreamed of becoming a confectioner and opening her own bakery.
The local confectioner is famous for his unusual flavors of fudge and toffee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pastry chef - a chef specialized in making pastries and desserts

Etymology

From Old French *confectionnaire*, from Latin *confectio* (a making, preparing); see 'confection'.
