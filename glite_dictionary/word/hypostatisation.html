hypostatisation

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌpɑstəˌstaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / haɪˌpɒstətaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of regarding or treating something abstract as if it were a concrete or material thing.
treating the abstract as a concrete entity

Examples

Philosophers often warn against the hypostatisation of ideas such as justice or freedom.
The hypostatisation of social constructs can lead to misunderstandings about their true nature.
Her essay criticized the hypostatisation of the notion of 'the market' as if it were a living entity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reification - the process of treating an abstract concept as a real thing

Etymology

From Greek 'hypostasis' (substance, underlying reality) + English '-ation'.
