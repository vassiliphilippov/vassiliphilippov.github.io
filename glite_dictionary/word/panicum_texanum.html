Panicum texanum

1. NOUN

/ ˌpænɪkəm tɛkˈsænəm / (AmE), / ˌpænɪkəm tɛkˈsænəm / (BrE)
A species of annual weedy grass native to the southern United States, commonly used for hay.
a type of weedy grass used for hay

Examples

Panicum texanum is often harvested for hay in southern states.
Farmers sometimes struggle to control the spread of Panicum texanum in their fields.
The dense growth of Panicum texanum provides good forage for livestock during the summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Texas panicum - another name for Panicum texanum, a grass species

Etymology

From Latin 'panicum' (a type of millet) and 'Texanum', referring to Texas.
