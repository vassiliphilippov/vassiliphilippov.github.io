SSS

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛs ˌɛs ˈɛs / (AmE), / ˌɛs ˌɛs ˈɛs / (BrE)
an independent United States federal agency responsible for administering compulsory military service registration.
US agency for military conscription registration
american

Examples

All men aged 18 to 25 are required to register with the SSS in the United States.
The SSS maintains records used in the event of a military draft.
Concerns about fairness have often surrounded the SSS registration process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Selective Service System - the system of compulsory military service registration in the US

Etymology

Acronym of Selective Service System.
