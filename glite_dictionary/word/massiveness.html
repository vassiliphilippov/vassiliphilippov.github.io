massiveness

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæsɪvnəs / (AmE), / ˈmæsɪvnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being very large or heavy, especially in a way that is difficult to handle or manage
unwieldy largeness or heaviness

Examples

The massiveness of the sculpture made it impossible to move without special equipment.
She was awed by the massiveness of the ancient stone walls surrounding the city.
Despite its massiveness, the building appeared graceful against the skyline.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heaviness - the quality of being heavy
  • Antonym: lightness - the quality of being light in weight

Etymology

From 'massive' + '-ness'; 'massive' from Middle French massif (bulky, solid).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmæsɪvnəs / (AmE), / ˈmæsɪvnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being very large or heavy in mass or size
the state of being massive or heavy

Examples

The massiveness of the boulder made it impossible to move without machinery.
Historians were struck by the massiveness of the castle’s stone walls.
Scientists are still amazed at the massiveness of the planet compared to Earth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bulk - large size or mass
  • Antonym: lightness - the property of having little weight or mass

Etymology

Formed from 'massive' + '-ness', a noun-forming suffix.
