Vienna

1. PROPN

/ viˈɛnə / (AmE), / viˈɛnə / (BrE)
the capital and largest city of Austria, situated on the Danube River in northeastern Austria and known for its historical association with famous composers such as Beethoven, Brahms, Haydn, Mozart, Schubert, and Strauss
capital city of Austria

Examples

Many tourists visit Vienna each year to enjoy its rich musical history and beautiful architecture.
Mozart spent much of his adult life in Vienna, composing some of his greatest works there.
The coffee culture in Vienna is renowned around the world.

Etymology

From Latin 'Vienna', derived from the name of the ancient Roman settlement 'Vindobona'.
