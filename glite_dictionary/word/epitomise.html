to epitomise

1. VERB

/ ɪˈpɪtəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ɪˈpɪtəmaɪz / (BrE)
to be a perfect example of something or to embody the essential characteristics of something
be a perfect example of something
british

Examples

She truly epitomises the spirit of generosity through her selfless actions.
The new library epitomises modern architecture in both form and function.
His calm demeanor epitomises professionalism in a high-stress environment.

Synset Examples

The fugue typifies Bach's style of composition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to epitomize - embody the essential characteristics of or be a typical example of (US spelling)
  • Synonym: to exemplify - to be a typical example of

Etymology

From Greek 'epitomē' via Late Latin 'epitomare', meaning 'to abridge' or 'to summarise'.
