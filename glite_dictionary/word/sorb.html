sorb

1. NOUN

/ sɔrb / (AmE), / sɔːb / (BrE)
The acid, gritty-textured fruit of the service tree or related trees of the genus Sorbus.
acid fruit of the service tree

Examples

The sorb was once a popular wild fruit in parts of Europe.
You can make jelly from ripe sorbs collected in autumn.
The villagers harvested the sorb for its tart taste and medicinal uses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: service tree - a deciduous tree of the genus Sorbus, especially S. domestica

Etymology

From Latin *sorbus*, name of the service tree.

2. VERB

/ sɔrb / (AmE), / sɔːb / (BrE)
(of a substance) to take up and hold a liquid or gas, either by adsorption or absorption.
absorb or adsorb liquid or gas

Examples

Activated charcoal can sorb toxic gases from the air.
This material efficiently serbs both water and oil spills.
Scientists measured how quickly the compound would sorb carbon dioxide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to absorb - to soak up or take in (a substance) by physical or chemical action
  • Synonym: to adsorb - to accumulate a substance on a surface (by adhesion)

Etymology

From Latin 'sorbere' meaning 'to suck in'.
