regime

1. NOUN

/ rəˈʒim / (AmE), / rəˈʒiːm / (BrE)
a systematic plan or course of medical treatment, often including diet, exercise, or medication
systematic medical treatment plan
medical

Examples

After his diagnosis, the doctor prescribed a strict regime of medication and exercise.
She followed a special dietary regime to improve her heart health.
Changing your regime may help manage your symptoms more effectively.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: regimen - a prescribed course of medical treatment, way of life, or diet for the promotion or restoration of health

Etymology

From French 'régime', from Latin 'regimen' (rule, guidance).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈʒim / (AmE), / reɪˈʒiːm / (BrE)
a government, especially an authoritarian one, in power in a country or region; the system or form of rule
a government's system of rule

Examples

The military regime seized power after the coup d'état.
Many citizens were dissatisfied with the corrupt regime that ruled their nation for decades.
After years of conflict, the new regime promised democratic reforms.

Synset Examples

the government reduced taxes
the matter was referred to higher authorities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: government - the group of people with the authority to govern a country or state
  • Synonym: administration - the officials in the executive branch of government under a particular leader

Etymology

From French *régime*, from Latin *regimen* (rule, government).
