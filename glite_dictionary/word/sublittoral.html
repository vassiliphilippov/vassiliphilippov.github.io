sublittoral

1. ADJ

/ ˌsʌb.lɪˈtɔr.əl / (AmE), / ˌsʌb.lɪˈtɒr.əl / (BrE)
of or relating to the region of the continental shelf between the seashore and the edge of the continental shelf, or to marine organisms living there
relating to the continental shelf or its marine life

Examples

Many fish species inhabit the sublittoral zone, where sunlight can still penetrate the water.
The survey focused on sublittoral marine communities along the continental shelf.
Seaweed diversity is especially rich in the sublittoral regions just offshore.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: littoral - relating to the shore or coastal region

Etymology

From Latin sub- (under, below) + littoralis (of the shore).
