henbane

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛnˌbeɪn / (AmE), / ˈhɛnbeɪn / (BrE)
a poisonous European herb with sticky, hairy leaves and yellowish-brown flowers, known for yielding hyoscyamine and scopolamine
a poisonous Old World herb

Examples

The doctor cautioned that the seeds of henbane are extremely toxic if ingested.
In ancient times, henbane was used in potions for its hallucinogenic effects.
The sticky leaves of henbane can be easily identified in the wild.

Etymology

From Old English 'henbana', possibly meaning 'death of hens'.
