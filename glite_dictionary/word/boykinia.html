Boykinia

1. NOUN

/ bɔɪˈkɪniə / (AmE), / bɔɪˈkɪniə / (BrE)
A genus of perennial rhizomatous herbs with clusters of small, usually white flowers, native to North America and Japan.
genus of perennial herbs (plant)

Examples

The shady garden was planted with several Boykinia species to provide delicate blooms in the summer.
Botanists discovered a new variety of Boykinia in the mountains of western North America.
The moist woodland habitat is ideal for growing Boykinia alongside other shade-loving perennials.

Etymology

Named after Samuel Boykin, an American botanist (1786–1846), combined with the Latin suffix -ia used in genus names.

2. NOUN

/ bɔɪˈkɪniə / (AmE), / bɔɪˈkɪniə / (BrE)
A genus of perennial rhizomatous herbs in the saxifrage family, with small flowers in panicles, native to North America and Japan.
a genus of herbaceous plants

Examples

Botanists discovered a new species within the Boykinia genus in the mountains of California.
Many Boykinia species thrive in moist, shaded woodland habitats.
The delicate flowers of Boykinia make it a favorite among wildflower enthusiasts.

Etymology

Named after American botanist Samuel Boykin (1786–1846).
