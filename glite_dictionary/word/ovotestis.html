ovotestis

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvoʊˈtɛstɪs / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəʊˈtɛstɪs / (BrE)
A gonad that contains both testicular and ovarian tissue, occurring in some hermaphroditic individuals or animals.
gonad with both testicular and ovarian tissue
medical

Examples

The surgeon discovered an ovotestis during the procedure, which explained the patient's ambiguous genitalia.
In certain intersex conditions, an individual may develop an ovotestis rather than distinct testes or ovaries.
Scientists studied the reproductive mechanisms in snails that possess an ovotestis instead of separate gonads.

Etymology

From Latin 'ovum' (egg) + 'testis' (testicle), referring to both egg- and sperm-producing tissue.
