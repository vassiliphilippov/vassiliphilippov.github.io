microbalance

1. NOUN

/ ˈmaɪkroʊˌbæləns / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkrəʊˌbæləns / (BrE)
an instrument used for weighing extremely small masses with high precision, often used in laboratories
high-precision instrument for tiny weights

Examples

The laboratory purchased a new microbalance to accurately measure tiny chemical samples.
Dust particles can affect the results if the microbalance is not properly enclosed.
Researchers used a microbalance to weigh samples that were only a few micrograms.

Etymology

From micro- (small) + balance (weighing instrument).
