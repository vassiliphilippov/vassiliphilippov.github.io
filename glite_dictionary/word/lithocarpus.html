Lithocarpus

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈθɑːkɑːrpəs / (AmE), / lɪˈθəʊkɑːpəs / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen trees and shrubs in the beech family, commonly known as tanbark oaks, native to Asia and North America.
genus of trees known as tanbark oaks

Examples

The Lithocarpus genus includes several species of tanbark oaks found in Asia and North America.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Lithocarpus trees in the mountain forests.
The leaves of Lithocarpus are often used for ornamental purposes due to their glossy appearance.

Etymology

From Greek lithos (stone) + karpos (fruit), referencing the tree's hard acorns.

Leaves from the Lithocarpus tree are often used for traditional remedies in some Asian countries.
