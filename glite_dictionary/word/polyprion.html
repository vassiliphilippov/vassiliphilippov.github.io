Polyprion

1. NOUN

/ pɑːliˈpraɪən / (AmE), / pɒlɪˈprʌɪən / (BrE)
A genus of large marine fishes in the family Polyprionidae, including species commonly known as wreckfish.
genus of large marine fishes (wreckfish)

Examples

The Polyprion genus includes some of the largest wreckfish inhabiting deep ocean waters.
Scientists discovered a new species of Polyprion off the coast of New Zealand.
Commercial fisheries target Polyprion for its highly valued meat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wreckfish - a large marine fish found in deep waters, belonging to the genus Polyprion

Etymology

From Greek polýs (many) + prion (saw), referring to the serrated gill covers.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɑːliˈpraɪ.ən / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈpraɪ.ən / (BrE)
A genus of large marine fish in the family Polyprionidae, notably including the wreckfish.
genus of large marine fish

Examples

The Polyprion genus contains some of the largest species of wreckfish found in the Atlantic Ocean.
Scientists recently discovered a new species belonging to the Polyprion genus off the coast of New Zealand.
Commercial fisheries often seek members of the Polyprion genus due to their size and market value.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wreckfish - a large marine fish in the genus Polyprion

Etymology

From Greek 'poly-' (many) + 'prion' (saw), referring to the serrated features of some species.
