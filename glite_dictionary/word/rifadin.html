Rifadin

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪ.fə.dɪn / (AmE), / ˈraɪ.fə.dɪn / (BrE)
A trademarked antibacterial drug used to treat tuberculosis and other bacterial infections; also known as rifampin.
antibacterial drug, trademark for rifampin
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Rifadin as part of my tuberculosis treatment.
It is important to take Rifadin exactly as directed to avoid antibiotic resistance.
Some patients experience side effects while taking Rifadin for their infection.
