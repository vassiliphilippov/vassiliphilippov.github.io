amalgamative

1. ADJ

/ əˈmælɡəˌmeɪtɪv / (AmE), / əˈmalɡəmətɪv / (BrE)
tending to combine or unite different elements into a single entity; characterized by amalgamation.
tending to combine or unite

Examples

The company's amalgamative approach brought together several smaller firms to create a larger corporation.
Her leadership style was notably amalgamative, blending ideas from all team members.
The amalgamative nature of the policy was intended to foster unity among diverse groups.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to amalgamate - to combine or unite to form one structure
  • Antonym: separative - tending to cause separation

Etymology

From amalgamate (from Medieval Latin amalgamatus) + -ive.
