vesuvianite

1. NOUN

/ vəˈsuːviəˌnaɪt / (AmE), / vɪˈsjuːvɪəˌnaɪt / (BrE)
a bright green, yellow, or brown mineral of hydrated silicate, typically found as crystals in limestone and used as a gemstone
a mineral used as a gemstone

Examples

The museum displayed a rare vesuvianite crystal found in the Alps.
Some jewelers cut vesuvianite into cabochons for use in rings and pendants.
Limestone deposits often contain well-formed vesuvianite specimens prized by collectors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idocrase - another name for vesuvianite

Etymology

From Mount Vesuvius in Italy, where the mineral was first found, plus '-ite' for minerals.
