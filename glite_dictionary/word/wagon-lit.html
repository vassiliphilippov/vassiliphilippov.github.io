wagon-lit

1. NOUN

/ wæɡən ˈlɪt / (AmE), / ˌwæɡɒ̃ ˈliː / (BrE)
A railway carriage or passenger car equipped with sleeping berths for overnight travel.
railway sleeping car

Examples

He reserved a wagon-lit for his overnight journey from Paris to Venice.
The wagon-lit provided passengers with comfortable beds and privacy during the long trip.
Traveling across Europe in a wagon-lit was once considered the height of luxury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sleeper - a railway car with beds for passengers

Etymology

From French 'wagon-lit', meaning 'sleeping car' ('wagon' = carriage, 'lit' = bed).
