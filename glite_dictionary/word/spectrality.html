spectrality

1. NOUN

/ spɛkˈtræləti / (AmE), / spɛkˈtrælɪti / (BrE)
The quality or condition of being spectral or ghostly.
quality of being ghostly

Examples

The film explored the spectrality of memory, blurring the lines between the living and the dead.
Critics discuss the spectrality in Shakespeare's tragedies, focusing on characters that haunt the narrative.
Her novel is filled with a sense of spectrality, creating an atmosphere both mysterious and unsettling.

Synset Examples

Seen in this light, it is the spectrality of the figure, the fact that it's neither (completely) here nor there, that keeps the figure alive - or, more precisely, undead - never quite exhausted by a single, definitive instantiation but always at least potentially available for yet another serial iteration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spectral - relating to or resembling a ghost
  • Synonym: phantom - something apparent to sense but with no substantial existence

Etymology

From 'spectral' + '-ity', spectral from Latin 'spectrum' (appearance, image, ghost).
