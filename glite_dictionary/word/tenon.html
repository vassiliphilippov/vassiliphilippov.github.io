tenon

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛnən / (AmE), / ˈtɛnən / (BrE)
a projecting piece of wood made to fit into a corresponding hole (mortise) to form a joint
projecting wood for a mortise joint

Examples

The carpenter carefully cut a tenon to fit tightly into the mortise.
Each beam was joined using a traditional mortise and tenon joint.
He smoothed the edges of the tenon before assembling the frame.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mortise - a hole or recess cut into a part to receive a tenon

Etymology

From Old French *tenon* (a tenon), from *tenir* (to hold).
