Jean de La Fontaine

1. PROPN

/ ʒɑn də lɑ fɒnˈtɛn / (AmE), / ʒɑ̃ də la fɔ̃ˈtɛn / (BrE)
(1621–1695) French poet and writer best known for collecting and adapting Aesop's fables.
French poet and collector of fables

Examples

Many of Jean de La Fontaine's adaptations of Aesop's fables are still read today.
Students studied the works of Jean de La Fontaine in their French literature class.
The moral lessons in Jean de La Fontaine's fables remain relevant across generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Aesop - Ancient Greek storyteller traditionally credited with a collection of fables
