mail-cheeked

1. ADJ

/ ˈmeɪl ˌtʃikt / (AmE), / ˈmeɪl ˌtʃiːkt / (BrE)
(of certain fishes) having bony plates on the sides of the head.
having bony plates on head sides

Examples

The sturgeon is a mail-cheeked fish known for its distinctive armored head.
Some extinct species of fish were mail-cheeked, offering them extra protection from predators.
Researchers identified the fossil by its mail-cheeked features.

Etymology

From 'mail' (protective armor) and 'cheeked' (having cheeks), referring to armored cheeks.
