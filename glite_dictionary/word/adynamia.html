adynamia

1. NOUN

/ ˌædɪˈneɪmiə / (AmE), / ˌadɪˈneɪmɪə / (BrE)
A lack of strength or vigor, especially as a result of illness.
lack of strength or vigor
medical

Examples

The patient exhibited adynamia after several weeks of severe illness.
Cases of extreme adynamia may require special supportive care for recovery.
Her adynamia was so pronounced that even walking was difficult.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: weakness - lack of physical strength
  • Synonym: asthenia - abnormal physical weakness or lack of energy

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (without) + 'dynamis' (power, strength).
