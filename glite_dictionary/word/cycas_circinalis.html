Cycas circinalis

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪkəs sɜːrˌsɪnˈælɪs / (AmE), / ˈsʌɪkəs sɜːˌsɪnˈælɪs / (BrE)
A species of cycad native to southeastern India, with palmlike leaves and a stout trunk.
a cycad species from southeastern India

Examples

Cycas circinalis is commonly known as the queen sago in parts of India.
Botanists are studying Cycas circinalis for its resilience in dry climates.
The leaves of Cycas circinalis resemble those of a palm tree but are more rigid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cycad - a group of ancient, palm-like plants

Etymology

From Latin genus name Cycas and species name circinalis meaning 'rounded' or 'curved'.
