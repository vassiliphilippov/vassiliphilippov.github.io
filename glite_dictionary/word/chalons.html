Chalons

1. PROPN

/ ˈʃælənz / (AmE), / ˈʃælɒ̃ / (BrE)
the battle in 451 AD in which Attila the Hun was defeated by a coalition of Roman and Visigothic forces near Châlons-en-Champagne, France
battle where Attila the Hun was defeated (451 AD)

Examples

Many historians consider the Chalons battle a turning point in the decline of the Huns.
The defeat at Chalons marked the end of Attila's western campaigns.
Some ancient sources provide differing accounts of the armies at Chalons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Battle of Chalons - alternative name for the battle where Attila was defeated in 451 AD

Etymology

Named after Châlons-en-Champagne, the town near the battlefield in France.
