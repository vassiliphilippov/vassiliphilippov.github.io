sheatfish

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃitˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈʃiːtˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A large elongated catfish native to central and eastern Europe, known scientifically as Silurus glanis.
large European catfish

Examples

The sheatfish is one of the largest freshwater fish found in European rivers.
Anglers often travel to the Danube in hopes of catching a giant sheatfish.
Unlike most catfish, the sheatfish can grow to an impressive length of over two meters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wels - another name for the sheatfish (Silurus glanis)

Etymology

From German *Scheidfisch*, possibly related to 'sheath' due to the fish's shape.
