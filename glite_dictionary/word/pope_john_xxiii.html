Pope John XXIII

1. PROPN

/ poʊp dʒɑn ˌtwɛnti ˌθri / (AmE), / pəʊp dʒɒn ˌtwenti ˈθriː / (BrE)
(1881–1963) Italian pope from 1958 to 1963 who convened the Second Vatican Council, initiating significant reforms in the Roman Catholic Church.
Italian pope; convened Second Vatican Council

Examples

Pope John XXIII is remembered for initiating the Second Vatican Council and modernizing the Catholic Church.
Historians often cite Pope John XXIII as a progressive leader during a pivotal era in church history.
Many Catholics admired Pope John XXIII for his humility and openness to reform.
