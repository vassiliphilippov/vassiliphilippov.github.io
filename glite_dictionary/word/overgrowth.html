overgrowth

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvɚˈɡroʊθ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈɡrəʊθ / (BrE)
an excessive or dense growth of plants, or something similar, growing on or over something else
excessive growth covering something

Examples

The abandoned house was hidden beneath a thick overgrowth of weeds and vines.
Clearing the overgrowth revealed a forgotten garden path.
Wild animals often make their homes in the overgrowth near the river bank.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: undergrowth - a dense growth of shrubs or small trees beneath taller trees; lower vegetation

Etymology

From over- (excessive) + growth (act of growing).

2. NOUN

/ ˈoʊvərˌɡroʊθ / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəɡrəʊθ / (BrE)
excessive growth or enlargement of an organ, tissue, or body part, often resulting from abnormal hormone production or disease.
excessive or abnormal growth (medical)
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the swelling as an overgrowth resulting from a hormonal imbalance.
A pituitary tumor can cause overgrowth of bones and tissues, leading to health complications.
The patient's overgrowth of facial features was attributed to acromegaly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypertrophy - increase in the size of an organ or tissue due to enlargement of its cells

Etymology

From over- (excessive) + growth, modeled after similar medical compounds.
