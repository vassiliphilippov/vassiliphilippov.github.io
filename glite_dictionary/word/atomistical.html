atomistical

1. ADJ

/ ˌæt.əˈmɪs.tɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌat.əˈmɪs.tɪ.kəl / (BrE)
composed of or relating to separate and often disparate elements; based on the theory that things can be broken down into fundamental parts.
divided into separate elements

Examples

Some philosophers argue for an atomistical approach to society, viewing individuals as isolated units.
The report criticized the plan's atomistical structure, which ignored the need for cooperation between teams.
Modern art sometimes takes an atomistical form, breaking the subject into disconnected parts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atomistic - relating to or composed of atoms or fundamental units

Etymology

From atomistic + -al; ultimately from Greek atomos (indivisible) via Latin atomisticus.
