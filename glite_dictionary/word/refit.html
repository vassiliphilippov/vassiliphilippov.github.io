refit

1. VERB

/ riˈfɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈfɪt / (BrE)
to provide with new equipment or repair and restore for further use, especially by replacing or upgrading parts
to equip or restore again

Examples

The company decided to refit the old warehouse into a modern office space.
After years at sea, the navy ship was refitted with new technology.
We plan to refit all the classrooms with better lighting and projectors next year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to renovate - to restore to a good state of repair
  • Synonym: to restore - to bring back to its original state

Etymology

From re- (again) + fit (to make suitable), first attested in the mid 17th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈriˌfɪt / (AmE), / ˈriːfɪt / (BrE)
the process of repairing or equipping a ship, boat, or other vehicle again by replacing or fixing its parts
act of repairing or equipping a ship again

Examples

The cruise liner is in dry dock for a major refit before the next season.
After its refit, the warship returned to active duty in better condition than before.
The yacht needed a complete refit after years of neglect and harsh weather.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overhaul - a thorough examination and repair of a machine or system
  • Synonym: repair - the action of fixing something that is damaged or broken

Etymology

From re- (again) + fit (equip, make suitable).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01159762-n
Synset POS: n
