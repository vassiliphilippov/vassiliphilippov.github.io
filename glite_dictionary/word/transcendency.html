transcendency

1. NOUN

/ trænˈsɛndənsi / (AmE), / trænˈsɛndənsi / (BrE)
The state or quality of being above and beyond the limits of ordinary experience, especially material existence.
existence above ordinary limits

Examples

The philosopher spoke about the transcendency of spiritual values over material wealth.
Achieving transcendency is considered a central goal in many mystical traditions.
Some artists seek transcendency in their work, aiming to rise above everyday reality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transcendence - the state of being beyond ordinary limits
  • Antonym: immanence - state of being within or inherent

Etymology

From Latin 'transcendere' (to climb over, surpass) via Medieval Latin 'transcendentia'.

2. NOUN

/ trænˈsɛndənsi / (AmE), / trænˈsɛndənsi / (BrE)
the state of excelling, surpassing, or going beyond normal limits; the quality of being transcendent
state of going beyond usual limits

Examples

The novel explores the transcendency of the human spirit in the face of adversity.
Her achievements in science show a clear transcendency above her peers.
Meditation is often associated with the transcendency of the ordinary mind.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transcendence - the act or state of transcending limits or boundaries

Etymology

From Latin 'transcendere' (to climb over, surpass) via Medieval Latin 'transcendentia' and English -ency suffix.
