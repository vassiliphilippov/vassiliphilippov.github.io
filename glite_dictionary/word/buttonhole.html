buttonhole

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌtnˌhoʊl / (AmE), / ˈbʌtnˌhəʊl / (BrE)
A small slit or hole in fabric, usually reinforced, through which a button is passed to fasten clothing or other items.
hole for button in fabric

Examples

She carefully sewed a buttonhole on the new shirt to match the buttons.
Make sure each buttonhole is large enough for the button to pass through.
The coat was missing a buttonhole, so it couldn't be fastened properly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: button - a small fastener, typically round, used for joining two parts of a garment

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʌtnˌhoʊl / (AmE), / ˈbʌtnˌhəʊl / (BrE)
A small slit or hole in a piece of fabric, through which a button is passed to fasten two pieces together.
hole for a button in fabric

Examples

Her shirt was missing a buttonhole, so she couldn't fasten the top button.
The tailor carefully stitched each buttonhole by hand for a neat finish.
If your coat buttonhole is torn, a seamstress can usually repair it easily.

Etymology

From 'button' + 'hole', attested in English since the 17th century.

3. VERB

/ ˈbʌtənˌhoʊl / (AmE), / ˈbʌtənhəʊl / (BrE)
to stop someone and force them to listen to you, especially by talking to them persistently or detaining them for a favor or discussion
detain someone in persistent conversation

Examples

He managed to buttonhole the senator after the meeting to discuss his concerns.
At the reception, she was buttonholed by a journalist looking for an exclusive interview.
He spent ten minutes buttonholing me about his latest business idea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to accost - to approach and speak to someone forcefully or insistently

Etymology

From the noun 'buttonhole' (the slit for a button); from the idea of grabbing someone by the buttonhole to detain them.
