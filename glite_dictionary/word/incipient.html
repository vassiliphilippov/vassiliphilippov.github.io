incipient

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈsɪpiənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɪpiənt / (BrE)
just beginning to exist or develop; in an early stage
just beginning; in an early stage

Examples

The incipient conflict between the two communities was evident in their strained relations.
Scientists detected an incipient tumor during the early screening.
She had only an incipient understanding of the language when she first arrived.

Synset Examples

incipient civil disorder
an incipient tumor
a vague inchoate idea

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nascent - just coming into existence and beginning to display signs of future potential
  • Synonym: incipience - the state of being incipient; beginning to exist or appear
  • Antonym: fully-fledged - completely developed or established

Etymology

From Latin incipientem, present participle of incipere (to begin).
