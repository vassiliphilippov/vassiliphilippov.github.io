to slay

1. VERB

/ sleɪ / (AmE), / sleɪ / (BrE)
to kill someone deliberately and with premeditation
kill someone deliberately

Examples

The king ordered his general to slay their enemies before dawn.
The notorious bandit slew several travelers on the old road.
Legends say the hero slain the dragon to save the village.

Synset Examples

The mafia boss ordered his enemies murdered

Related Terms

  • Synonym: murder - kill someone unlawfully and deliberately
  • Synonym: kill - cause the death of a living being
  • Antonym: save - to rescue from harm or danger

Etymology

From Old English 'slēan' (to strike, kill).
