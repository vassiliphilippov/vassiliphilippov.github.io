Beta vulgaris cicla

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪtə vʊlˈɡɛrɪs ˈsɪklə / (AmE), / ˈbiːtə vʊlˈɡɑːrɪs ˈkɪklə / (BrE)
a variety of beet cultivated for its large edible leaves and stalks, commonly known as Swiss chard or chard
variety of beet grown for edible leaves and stalks

Examples

We sautéed the Beta vulgaris cicla leaves with garlic for a nutritious side dish.
The farmer grows several rows of Beta vulgaris cicla in his organic vegetable plot.
Many Mediterranean dishes feature Beta vulgaris cicla as a key ingredient.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Swiss chard - the common name for Beta vulgaris cicla, edible leafy vegetable
  • Degree Higher: beet - the general species to which Beta vulgaris cicla belongs

Etymology

From Latin: 'Beta' (beet), 'vulgaris' (common), 'cicla' (possibly from Greek 'kikhlē', a kind of plant).
