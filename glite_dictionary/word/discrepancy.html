discrepancy

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈskrɛpənsi / (AmE), / dɪˈskrɛpənsi / (BrE)
a lack of agreement or consistency between facts or claims; a difference between things that ought to be the same
a difference or inconsistency

Examples

The accountant noticed a discrepancy between the two financial reports.
There is a discrepancy in the witness's story that needs to be explained.
Scientists found a discrepancy between the expected and actual results of the experiment.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: consistency - the quality of always behaving or performing in a similar way
  • Antonym: agreement - the state of having the same opinion or belief
  • Synonym: difference - the way in which things are not the same

Etymology

From Latin discrepantia (discordance), from discrepans (to be discordant).

2. NOUN

/ dɪˈskrɛpənsi / (AmE), / dɪˈskrɛpənsi / (BrE)
a lack of agreement or consistency between facts, figures, or opinions; an instance of difference or disagreement
inconsistency or difference between things

Examples

The auditor found a discrepancy between the reported income and the actual bank statements.
There is a significant discrepancy in the results obtained by the two research teams.
They discussed how to resolve the discrepancy between the witness's testimony and the video evidence.

Synset Examples

a growing divergence of opinion

Related Terms

  • Antonym: consistency - agreement or harmony between parts or elements
  • Antonym: agreement - harmony or accord, especially between opinions or facts
  • Synonym: difference - a way in which things are not the same

Etymology

From Latin discrepantia (‘discordance’), from discrepans, present participle of discrepare (‘to differ, to be discordant’).
