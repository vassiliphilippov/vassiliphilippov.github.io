oscillation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːsəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɒsɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of moving repeatedly between two positions, states, or conditions, often in a regular rhythm
repeated movement or change between states

Examples

The oscillation of the stock market made investors uneasy.
She noticed a regular oscillation between optimism and pessimism in his attitudes.
The fan's oscillation helped cool the entire room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fluctuation - irregular rising and falling; variation
  • Antonym: stability - the state of being stable or unchanging

Etymology

From Latin oscillatio (a swinging), from oscillare (to swing).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑːsɪˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɒsɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a single complete movement or cycle of a process that repeats regularly, especially in physics or mechanics
one complete cycle of a repeated process

Examples

The pendulum completes one oscillation every two seconds.
The electrical circuit produced thousands of oscillations per second.
Each oscillation of the spring was carefully measured in the experiment.

Synset Examples

a year constitutes a cycle of the seasons

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cycle - a series of events that are regularly repeated in the same order

Etymology

From Latin oscillatio (the act of swinging), from oscillare (to swing).

3. NOUN

/ ˌɑːsəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɒsɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a regular or repeated variation in value, position, or state about a central point or mean, especially one occurring in a physical system
regular periodic variation about a mean

Examples

The oscillation of the pendulum gradually decreased as it lost energy.
Electrical oscillation in the circuit produces radio waves.
We measured the oscillation of the spring after it was stretched.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vibration - a rapid back-and-forth movement, often used in physics
  • Antonym: stability - the state of being steady or unchanging

Etymology

From Latin oscillatio (a swinging), from oscillare (to swing).
