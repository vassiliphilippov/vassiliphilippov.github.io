Transvaal

1. PROPN

/ trænzˈvɑl / (AmE), / tranzˈvɑːl / (BrE)
a former province of northeastern South Africa, originally inhabited by Bantu-speaking Africans and later colonized by the Boers
former province of northeastern South Africa

Examples

The Transvaal was officially established as a province in 1910.
Many gold mining towns sprang up in the Transvaal during the late nineteenth century.
Conflicts over land in the Transvaal played a crucial role in South African history.

Etymology

From Afrikaans, literally 'across the Vaal (River)'.
