purgatory

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrɡəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈpɜːɡətəri / (BrE)
A temporary state or place of great suffering or torment, especially one that must be endured before relief or improvement.
temporary state of suffering

Examples

After losing his job, he described the months spent searching for work as a purgatory.
The long wait in the hospital's emergency room felt like a kind of purgatory.
For many, the bureaucracy involved in immigration can be a bureaucratic purgatory.

Synset Examples

a purgatory of drug abuse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: torment - a state of great mental or physical suffering
  • Antonym: paradise - a state or place of perfect happiness

Etymology

From Latin purgatorium (a place of cleansing), from purgare (to cleanse).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɜːrɡəˌtɔːri / (AmE), / ˈpɜːɡət(ə)ri / (BrE)
(in Roman Catholic theology) a place or state after death where souls are purified to achieve the holiness necessary to enter heaven.
state or place for posthumous purification

Examples

Catholic doctrine teaches that some souls enter purgatory before reaching heaven.
He described the experience as a kind of emotional purgatory between hope and despair.
The church holds special masses to pray for those suffering in purgatory.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: heaven - the place regarded in various religions as the abode of God and the angels
  • Degree Lower: hell - a place regarded in religions as a realm of eternal suffering for the wicked after death

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'purgatorium', from Latin 'purgare' (to cleanse).
