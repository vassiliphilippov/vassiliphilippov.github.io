dissimulation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsɪˌmjuːˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪˌmjuːˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of concealing one's thoughts, feelings, or character; deliberate deception or pretense
the act of concealing or deceiving

Examples

Her dissimulation was so skillful that nobody suspected her true intentions.
The politician became a master of dissimulation to preserve his public image.
Behind his smiling face was a practiced dissimulation of his real feelings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deception - the act of deceiving someone
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and sincere

Etymology

From Latin dissimulatio (concealment), from dissimulare ‘to disguise, conceal’
