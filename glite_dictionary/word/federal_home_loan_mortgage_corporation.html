Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corporation

1. PROPN

/ ˌfɛdɚəl hoʊm loʊn ˈmɔrtɡɪdʒ ˌkɔrpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɛdərəl həʊm ləʊn ˈmɔːɡɪdʒ ˌkɔːpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a government-sponsored corporation in the United States, established to increase the availability of mortgage funds by creating a secondary market for residential mortgages
US government-sponsored mortgage corporation

Examples

Many banks sell their loans to the Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corporation to free up capital for new lending.
The Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corporation plays an important role in stabilizing the housing market.
Congress created the Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corporation in 1970 to promote homeownership.
