validness

1. NOUN

/ ˈvælɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈvælɪdnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being legally acceptable or effective
quality of being legally valid

Examples

The lawyer questioned the validness of the contract in court.
Some people disputed the validness of the election results.
The validness of the agreement depends on both parties signing it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: validity - the quality of being valid, especially legally or logically
  • Antonym: invalidity - the state of not being legally acceptable or effective

Etymology

From Latin 'validus' (strong) via French 'valide'.
