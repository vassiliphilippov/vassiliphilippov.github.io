Shawnee

1. NOUN

/ ʃɑːˈniː / (AmE), / ʃɔːˈniː / (BrE)
the Algonquian language traditionally spoken by the Shawnee people
Native American Algonquian language

Examples

The Shawnee language has a rich oral tradition passed down through generations.
Only a small number of people are fluent in Shawnee today.
She studied Shawnee to help preserve the tribe's cultural heritage.

Etymology

From Shawnee, the name of the indigenous people who speak the language.

2. NOUN

/ ʃɑːˈniː / (AmE), / ʃɔːˈniː / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people formerly living along the Tennessee River, now found in Oklahoma and other regions
member of a Native American people

Examples

A group of Shawnee settled in Ohio during the 18th century.
Many Shawnee traditions have been passed down through generations.
The Shawnee played a significant role in early American history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Algonquian - a family of Native American peoples and languages

Etymology

From Shawnee ša˙wano˙ki ('southerners'), a self-designation.
