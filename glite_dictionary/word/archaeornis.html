archaeornis

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrkɪˈɔːrnɪs / (AmE), / ˌɑːkɪˈɔːnɪs / (BrE)
an extinct primitive bird with teeth, a long feathered tail, and three free clawed digits on each wing
extinct primitive toothed bird

Examples

The fossil of an archaeornis provides crucial evidence for the evolution of birds.
Scientists studied the archaeornis to learn more about bird ancestry.
The archaeornis had distinctive clawed wings and a long tail, unlike modern birds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archaeopteryx - extinct primitive bird similar to archaeornis

Etymology

From Greek 'archaios' (ancient) + 'ornis' (bird).
