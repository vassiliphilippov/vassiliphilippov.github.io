icterogenic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɪktərəˈdʒɛnɪk / (AmE), / ˌɪktərəˈdʒɛnɪk / (BrE)
(of a substance or factor) causing or producing jaundice.
causing jaundice
medical

Examples

The doctor identified an icterogenic compound in the contaminated food sample.
Some medications have icterogenic effects and may cause yellowing of the skin or eyes.
An icterogenic agent can trigger liver dysfunction, leading to jaundice in patients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jaundice - yellow discoloration of the skin and eyes

Etymology

From Greek 'ikteros' (jaundice) + '-genic' (producing).
