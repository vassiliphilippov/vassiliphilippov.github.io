Li

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪθiəm / (AmE), / ˈlɪθiəm / (BrE)
the chemical symbol for lithium, a soft silver-white alkali metal that is the lightest known metal and occurs in several minerals
chemical symbol for lithium (element)

Examples

Batteries often use Li as a key component for storing energy efficiently.
In the periodic table, Li has the atomic number 3.
Scientists study the properties of Li to develop new technologies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lithium - a soft, silvery-white alkali metal, chemical element with symbol Li and atomic number 3

Etymology

Symbol from New Latin 'lithium', based on Greek 'lithos' (stone) due to its mineral origin.
