testing

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtɛstɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of giving someone a test, especially to assess their knowledge, ability, or progress
the act of administering a test

Examples

The school has implemented regular testing to monitor students' progress throughout the year.
Standardized testing is often criticized for its impact on education quality.
Many employers use aptitude testing to select suitable job candidates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: examination - a formal test of knowledge or proficiency
  • Synonym: assessment - a process of evaluating someone's ability or performance
  • Synonym: evaluation - the process of judging or calculating the quality of someone or something

Etymology

From the verb 'test' + -ing suffix.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtɛstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtɛstɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of examining or investigating the qualities or performance of something, often by means of experiments or assessments
the process of examining the characteristics of something

Examples

The new medicine is still undergoing testing before it can be approved for use.
Academic testing is an important part of the school curriculum.
The manufacturer performed extensive testing to ensure the car's safety.

Synset Examples

there are laboratories for commercial testing
it involved testing thousands of children for smallpox

Related Terms

  • Synonym: examination - a detailed inspection or investigation
  • Synonym: assessment - the evaluation or estimation of the nature, quality, or ability of someone or something

Etymology

From the verb 'test' + -ing.

3. NOUN

/ ˈtɛstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtɛstɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of subjecting something to experimental examination or trials to assess its quality, performance, or reliability
experimental examination or trials

Examples

All new products undergo rigorous testing before they reach the market.
The testing of the emergency system revealed several flaws.
Due to safety concerns, the testing of the drug was suspended.

Synset Examples

they agreed to end the testing of atomic weapons

Related Terms

  • Synonym: experiment - a scientific procedure undertaken to test a hypothesis
  • Degree Lower: inspection - the act of examining something closely
  • Degree Higher: evaluation - the process of judging or assessing something

Etymology

From the verb 'test,' which comes from Old French 'test' (earthen pot, crucible), from Latin 'testum' (earthen vessel).
