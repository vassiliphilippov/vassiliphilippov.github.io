Haliotis

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæliˈoʊtɪs / (AmE), / ˌhælɪˈəʊtɪs / (BrE)
the type genus of the family Haliotidae, comprising marine mollusks commonly known as abalones
genus of abalones (marine mollusks)

Examples

The genus Haliotis includes several species of abalone found in coastal waters worldwide.
Researchers studied the reproductive habits of Haliotis in their natural habitat.
The shell of Haliotis is prized for its iridescent inner layer, known as mother-of-pearl.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abalone - a type of edible marine mollusk with a flattened shell

Etymology

From Greek haliōtis, from hals (sea) + ōt- (ear) referring to the shell's shape.
