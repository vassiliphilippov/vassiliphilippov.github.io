pyrotechnics

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪ.ɹəˈtɛk.nɪks / (AmE), / ˌpaɪ.rəˈtɛk.nɪks / (BrE)
brilliant or dazzling skill or display, especially in a musical or artistic performance
brilliant display, especially in music

Examples

The pianist's pyrotechnics left the audience both astonished and delighted.
Her vocal pyrotechnics during the concerto earned her a standing ovation.
Critics praised the guitarist's pyrotechnics for bringing new energy to the classic piece.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: virtuosity - great technical skill in performing

Etymology

From Greek *pyrotechne* (the art of making fire), referring originally to fireworks but later figuratively to spectacular displays.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪ.rəˈtɛk.nɪks / (AmE), / ˌpʌɪ.rəˈtɛk.nɪks / (BrE)
The art or science of making and using fireworks for display or entertainment.
the art of making fireworks

Examples

The company is renowned for its expertise in pyrotechnics and outdoor displays.
She studied pyrotechnics before joining the fireworks manufacturing industry.
Modern pyrotechnics require a strong understanding of chemistry and safety regulations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fireworks - Devices containing combustibles for producing displays of light and noise

Etymology

From Greek 'pyro-' (fire) and 'technikē' (art, skill).
