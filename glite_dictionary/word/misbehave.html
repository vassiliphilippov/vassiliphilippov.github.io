to misbehave

1. VERB

/ ˌmɪsbɪˈheɪv / (AmE), / ˌmɪsbɪˈheɪv / (BrE)
to behave badly or in an unacceptable way
behave badly or improperly

Examples

If you misbehave in class, you might get sent to the principal's office.
The puppy started to misbehave when left alone for too long.
She warned her son not to misbehave while they were visiting their relatives.

Synset Examples

The children misbehaved all morning

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to behave - to act in a particular way, especially to be good or proper

Etymology

From prefix 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'behave'.
