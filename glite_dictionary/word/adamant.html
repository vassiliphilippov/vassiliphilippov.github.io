adamant

1. ADJ

/ ˈæd.ə.mənt / (AmE), / ˈæd.ə.mənt / (BrE)
Refusing to be persuaded or to change one's mind; unyielding.
unwilling to change one's mind

Examples

Despite everyone's objections, she remained adamant about her decision to move abroad.
The manager was adamant that the policy would not change under any circumstances.
He spoke adamantly against the proposal, unwilling to compromise.

Synset Examples

he is adamant in his refusal to change his mind
Cynthia was inexorable: she would have none of him
an intransigent conservative opposed to every liberal tendency

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inflexible - unwilling to change or compromise
  • Antonym: yielding - willing to give way or be influenced

Etymology

From Latin *adamantem* (unbreakable substance), from Greek *adamas* (unconquerable, hard metal).
