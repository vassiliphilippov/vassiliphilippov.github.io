Pomacanthus

1. NOUN

/ poʊməˈkænθəs / (AmE), / pəʊməˈkanθəs / (BrE)
A genus of large, brightly colored marine angelfishes found in tropical waters.
genus of marine angelfishes

Examples

The emperor angelfish belongs to the genus Pomacanthus and is prized by aquarists for its vivid colors.
Many Pomacanthus species inhabit coral reefs in the Indo-Pacific region.
A Pomacanthus can be identified by its distinctive body shape and bold stripes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: angelfish - any of several species of brightly colored marine fishes of the family Pomacanthidae

Etymology

From Greek 'poma' (cover) + 'akantha' (thorn), referring to the spined gill cover.

2. NOUN

/ poʊməˈkænθəs / (AmE), / pɒməˈkænθəs / (BrE)
A genus of marine angelfish, known for their bright colors and found in tropical seas.
a genus of marine angelfish

Examples

The brilliant colors of Pomacanthus species make them popular in home aquariums.
Researchers discovered a new member of the Pomacanthus genus in the Pacific Ocean.
Some Pomacanthus angelfish can grow up to 40 centimeters in length.

Etymology

From Greek 'poma' meaning 'cover' and 'akantha' meaning 'thorn', referring to the fin spines.
