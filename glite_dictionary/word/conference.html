conference

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːn.fɚ.əns / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.fər.əns / (BrE)
a formal meeting for discussion, especially on a particular topic, often with participants exchanging views or information
formal meeting for discussion

Examples

The annual medical conference attracts experts from around the world.
We had a video conference to discuss the progress of the project.
She will present her research at the upcoming environmental conference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seminar - a meeting for discussion or training, often academic
  • Synonym: workshop - a meeting in which people engage in intensive discussion or activity on a particular subject
  • Synonym: meeting - an assembly of people for a particular purpose

Etymology

From Latin 'conferentia' (bringing together, comparing), from 'conferre' (to bring together).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːn.fɚ.əns / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.fər.əns / (BrE)
an association of sports teams that organizes matches between its members, especially in college or professional sports leagues
sports league association

Examples

The Big Ten conference includes several major universities from the Midwest.
Our school's football team hopes to win the conference championship this year.
Teams from the same conference often become fierce rivals over the seasons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: league - an association of sports teams or clubs that compete with each other

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːn.fɚ.əns / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.fər.əns / (BrE)
a formal meeting for discussion, consultation, or the exchange of information, often with a set agenda
formal meeting or discussion

Examples

He will attend an international conference on climate change next week.
The annual conference provided opportunities for networking and learning new skills.
During the conference, experts presented their latest research findings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: convention - large formal meeting of people with shared interest
  • Synonym: seminar - meeting for discussion or training
  • Synonym: meeting - gathering of people for discussion

Etymology

From Latin 'conferre' (to bring together), via French and Medieval Latin.
