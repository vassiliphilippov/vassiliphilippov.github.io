to anoint

1. VERB

/ əˈnɔɪnt / (AmE), / əˈnɔɪnt / (BrE)
to put oil or an ointment on someone or something, often as part of a religious or ceremonial act
apply oil as part of a ceremony

Examples

During the ceremony, the priest anointed the king’s forehead with holy oil.
The nurse gently anointed the patient’s wounds with a healing salve.
In many traditions, babies are anointed as part of their baptism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consecrate - to declare something sacred, especially through a religious ceremony
  • Synonym: to bless - to make holy by prayer, especially as part of a religious rite

Etymology

From Latin 'inungĕre', from 'in-' (on) + 'ungĕre' (to smear with oil).

2. VERB

/ əˈnɔɪnt / (AmE), / əˈnɔɪnt / (BrE)
to choose or formally appoint someone to a position or office, often implying divine approval or authority
to officially choose or appoint

Examples

The board decided to anoint her as the company's new CEO.
Many considered him the obvious choice and expected the party to anoint him as their leader.
She was surprised when the committee chose to anoint her for the prestigious award.

Synset Examples

She was anointed the head of the Christian fundamentalist group

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to appoint - to officially choose someone for a job or position

Etymology

From Old French enoint, past participle of enoindre, from Latin inungere (to smear on oil).

3. VERB

/ əˈnɔɪnt / (AmE), / əˈnɔɪnt / (BrE)
To put oil or a similar substance on someone, especially as part of a religious ceremony; to officially choose or designate someone for a position or role.
put oil on ceremonially; appoint officially

Examples

The priest will anoint the sick during the special healing service.
She was anointed leader after months of speculation.
Traditionally, kings and queens have been anointed during their coronation ceremonies.

Synset Examples

The priest will anoint the baby during the ceremony.
She was anointed as the spokesperson for the charity.
His anointment as the team captain surprised everyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to appoint - to assign a job or role to someone

Etymology

From Old French 'enoindre', from Latin 'inung(u)ere', from 'in-' (on) + 'unguere' (to smear, anoint).
