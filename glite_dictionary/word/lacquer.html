lacquer

1. NOUN

/ ˈlækər / (AmE), / ˈlakə / (BrE)
a hard, shiny coating, often used to protect or decorate wood, metal, or other surfaces
hard, glossy protective coating

Examples

The antique table was restored with a fresh coat of lacquer to enhance its shine.
Many traditional Asian artworks are finished with black or red lacquer.
The guitar’s glossy lacquer made it stand out under the stage lights.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: varnish - a liquid used to provide a hard, clear, shiny surface coating
  • Antonym: matte - a finish or surface that is not shiny

Etymology

From French 'laque', from Portuguese 'laca', from Hindi 'lākh' (resin), of Sanskrit origin.

2. VERB

/ ˈlækər / (AmE), / ˈlækə / (BrE)
to cover or coat an object with a glossy, protective varnish called lacquer
to coat with lacquer

Examples

She decided to lacquer the old wooden table to restore its shine.
Artisans carefully lacquer decorative boxes to achieve a flawless finish.
It took several hours to lacquer all the shelves in the workshop.

Synset Examples

A lacquered box from China

Related Terms

  • Synonym: varnish - to apply a liquid that dries to form a hard, clear, shiny surface
  • Degree Lower: paint - to cover a surface with paint

Etymology

From French 'laqueur', from 'lacquer' (to varnish), from Spanish 'laca', from Arabic 'lakk', from Persian 'lak'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈlækər / (AmE), / ˈlakə / (BrE)
a black or clear resinous substance from certain trees, used as a natural varnish to give a hard, shiny finish to surfaces
natural varnish from tree resin

Examples

The craftsman coated the wooden box with several layers of lacquer to achieve a glossy sheen.
Japanese art often features intricate designs embedded in lacquer surfaces.
After years of exposure, the lacquer on the old furniture began to crack and peel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: varnish - a liquid applied to surfaces to form a hard, protective, shiny finish

Etymology

From French 'laqueur', from Portuguese 'laca', from Arabic 'lakk', from Sanskrit 'laksha' (resin, lacquer).

Synset POS: n
