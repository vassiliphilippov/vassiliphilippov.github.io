stilbesterol

1. NOUN

/ stɪlˈbɛstəˌrɔl / (AmE), / stɪlˈbɛstərɒl / (BrE)
A synthetic nonsteroidal estrogen, once used to treat menstrual disorders, but later found to cause cancer in daughters of treated women.
synthetic nonsteroidal estrogen hormone
medical

Examples

The use of stilbesterol in pregnancy has been discontinued due to health risks.
Researchers discovered that stilbesterol exposure was linked to reproductive tract cancers.
Doctors rarely prescribe stilbesterol today, given its association with serious side effects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diethylstilbestrol - a specific synthetic estrogen related to stilbesterol

Etymology

From 'stilbene', a chemical compound base, and 'sterol', relating to steroid structure.
