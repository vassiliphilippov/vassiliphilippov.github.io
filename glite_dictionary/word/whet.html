to whet

1. VERB

/ wɛt / (AmE), / wɛt / (BrE)
to sharpen (a blade or tool) by rubbing it, especially on a whetstone
sharpen by rubbing

Examples

He used a stone to whet the edge of his knife before cooking.
The blacksmith paused to whet his chisel between hammer blows.
She carefully whetted the blade until it gleamed in the firelight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sharpen - make or become sharp or sharper

Etymology

Old English hwettan, from hwæt (sharp).

2. VERB

/ wɛt / (AmE), / wɛt / (BrE)
to make (something such as an appetite, desire, or interest) sharper or more intense
make sharper or more intense

Examples

Reading the first chapter served to whet my curiosity about the rest of the novel.
The aroma of freshly baked bread can whet your appetite.
The advertisements were designed to whet public interest in the new product.

Synset Examples

whet my appetite

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sharpen - make or become sharp or sharper

Etymology

Old English *hwettan* (to sharpen), from Proto-Germanic *hwatjan*.
