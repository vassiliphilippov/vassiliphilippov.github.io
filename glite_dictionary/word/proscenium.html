proscenium

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈsiːniəm / (AmE), / prəˈsiːniəm / (BrE)
the part of a theater stage in front of the curtain, or the wall that separates the stage from the auditorium
front section of a theater stage

Examples

The actors emerged onto the proscenium to take their final bow.
A grand arch framed the proscenium in the old opera house.
Renovations included restoring the ornate details of the proscenium.

Etymology

From Latin 'proscaenium', from Greek 'proskēnion' (in front of the scene or stage).

2. NOUN

/ proʊˈsiːniəm / (AmE), / prəˈsiːniəm / (BrE)
the part of a theater stage that is in front of the curtain; more generally, the area between the curtain and the audience.
front part of theater stage

Examples

The actors stepped forward onto the proscenium to take their final bows.
During the performance, the lighting on the proscenium was especially dramatic.
She prefers theaters with a traditional proscenium rather than a thrust stage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apron - the part of a theater stage that extends past the proscenium arch toward the audience

Etymology

From Latin *proscaenium*, from Greek *proskēnion* (in front of the stage).
