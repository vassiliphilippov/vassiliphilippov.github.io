epipelon

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛpɪˈpiːlɒn / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈpiːlɒn / (BrE)
A community of cyanobacteria and eukaryotic algae that inhabit or are associated with fine-grained sediments in aquatic environments.
algae and cyanobacteria on fine sediments
medical

Examples

Scientists observed substantial growth of epipelon in the nutrient-rich lake sediment.
The epipelon layer plays a vital role in the aquatic ecosystem's oxygen balance.
Changes in water chemistry directly affect the diversity of epipelon communities.

Etymology

From Greek epi- (upon) + pelos (mud, clay).
