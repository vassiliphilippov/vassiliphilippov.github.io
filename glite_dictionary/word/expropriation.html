expropriation

1. NOUN

/ ɛkˌsproʊpriˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɛkˌsprəʊpriˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of taking property from its owner, especially by a public authority for public use or benefit
the act of taking property, usually by the state
legal

Examples

The government announced the expropriation of land to build a new highway.
Farmers protested against the sudden expropriation of their fields by the city council.
Debate over the expropriation of private property is common in discussions of urban development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: confiscation - the act of taking or seizing someone's property with authority, often as a penalty

Etymology

From Medieval Latin expropriatio (removal of property), from Latin ex- (out) + proprius (own).
