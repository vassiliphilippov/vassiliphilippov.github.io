pronunciation

1. NOUN

/ pɹəˌnʌnsiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / pɹəˌnʌnsiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
The way in which a word or language is spoken, especially as considered correct or accepted.
manner of uttering words

Examples

Her pronunciation of French words is nearly flawless.
The teacher corrected the student's pronunciation repeatedly during the lesson.
Regional dialects can affect your pronunciation of certain vowels.

Synset Examples

they are always correcting my pronunciation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enunciation - clear and distinct pronunciation of speech
  • Antonym: mispronunciation - incorrect way of pronouncing a word

Etymology

From Latin pronuntiatio (proclaiming, utterance), from pronuntiare (to proclaim).

2. NOUN

/ prəˌnʌnsiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / prəˌnʌnsiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the way in which a word or language is spoken, especially according to accepted or customary standards
way a word is spoken

Examples

Her pronunciation of English words has improved significantly since moving to London.
You can check the correct pronunciation of a word using an online dictionary.
The teacher corrected his pronunciation during the lesson.

Synset Examples

the pronunciation of Chinese is difficult for foreigners
that is the correct pronunciation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: articulation - the clear and precise pronunciation of words
  • Synonym: enunciation - the act of pronouncing words clearly

Etymology

From Latin pronuntiatio (a proclaiming, pronunciation), from pronuntiare (to proclaim) + -ion.
