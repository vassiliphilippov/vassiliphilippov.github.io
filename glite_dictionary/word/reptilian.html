reptilian

1. NOUN

/ rɛpˈtɪliən / (AmE), / rɛpˈtɪliən / (BrE)
any cold-blooded vertebrate belonging to the class Reptilia, including snakes, lizards, turtles, crocodiles, and extinct forms such as dinosaurs
cold-blooded vertebrate of class Reptilia

Examples

Scientists discovered fossilized remains of a new reptilian species in the desert.
The Komodo dragon is the largest living reptilian on Earth today.
Some reptilian traits have remained largely unchanged for millions of years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reptile - a cold-blooded egg-laying vertebrate of the class Reptilia

Etymology

From Latin reptilis (creeping), from repere (to creep), with suffix -ian.

2. ADJ

/ rɛpˈtɪliən / (AmE), / rɛpˈtɪlɪən / (BrE)
of or relating to reptiles (animals of the class Reptilia) or having characteristics resembling those of reptiles
relating to reptiles or reptile-like traits

Examples

The reptilian skin of the lizard helps it survive in harsh environments.
She noticed the reptilian movement of the crocodile as it slid into the water.
Some ancient myths describe dragons as fearsome reptilian beasts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reptile - a cold-blooded vertebrate of the class Reptilia

Etymology

From Latin 'reptilis' (creeping), from 'repere' (to creep), with '-ian' suffix.
