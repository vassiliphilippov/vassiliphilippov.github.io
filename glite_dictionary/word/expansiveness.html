expansiveness

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspænsɪvnəs / (AmE), / ɪkˈspansɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being grand or extensive in scale, or having a tendency to expand or extend
quality of being grand or expansive

Examples

The expansiveness of the desert made them feel small and insignificant.
Her speech was marked by the expansiveness of her vision for the future.
The expansiveness of the mansion reflected the owner's wealth and ambition.

Synset Examples

the expansiveness of their extravagant life style was soon curtailed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grandiosity - the quality of being grand or impressive in scale
  • Antonym: modesty - the quality of being moderate or understated, opposite of grandeur

Etymology

From 'expansive' + '-ness'; ultimately from Latin 'expandere' (to spread out).

2. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspænsɪvnəs / (AmE), / ɪkˈspansɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being friendly, open, and willing to talk or share ideas, especially in conversation
friendliness and openness in conversation

Examples

Her expansiveness made everyone feel welcome at the party.
The interviewer's expansiveness encouraged the guest to open up about personal topics.
He is admired for his warmth and expansiveness in any social setting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: friendliness - the quality of being friendly and pleasant
  • Antonym: reserve - the quality of being reluctant to speak freely; restraint

Etymology

From Latin expansivus (expansive), from expandere (to spread out), plus the suffix -ness.
