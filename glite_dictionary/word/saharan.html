Saharan

1. NOUN

/ səˈhɛrən / (AmE), / səˈhɑːrən / (BrE)
a Nilo-Saharan language spoken in parts of Chad.
a language spoken in Chad

Examples

Very few people outside Chad are familiar with the Saharan language.
Linguists have studied the structure of Saharan for decades.
The Saharan language belongs to the Nilo-Saharan family.

Etymology

From Sahara, referring to the Sahara Desert region.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07011190-n
Synset POS: n

2. ADJ

/ səˈhɛrən / (AmE), / səˈhɑːrən / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the Sahara Desert or its region
of or relating to the Sahara Desert

Examples

The Saharan climate is known for its extreme heat and aridity.
Camels are well-adapted to survive long journeys across Saharan sands.
Some ancient Saharan cultures are still studied by archaeologists today.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sub-Saharan - relating to the region of Africa south of the Sahara Desert

Etymology

From 'Sahara', the Arabic name for the desert, meaning 'desert'.
