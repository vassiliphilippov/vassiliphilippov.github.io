scrawny

1. ADJ

/ ˈskrɔni / (AmE), / ˈskrɔːni / (BrE)
very thin and bony, often in a way that is unattractive or suggests poor health
very thin and bony

Examples

Despite eating plenty, Tom remained scrawny all through his teenage years.
The stray cat looked scrawny and in need of a good meal.
After months of illness, her once strong frame appeared shockingly scrawny.

Synset Examples

a child with skinny freckled legs
a long scrawny neck
pale bony hands

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skinny - very thin, especially in a way that is unattractive
  • Synonym: bony - so thin that the bones are prominent
  • Antonym: plump - having a full or rounded shape, especially in a way considered attractive or healthy

2. ADJ

/ ˈskrɔːni / (AmE), / ˈskrɔːni / (BrE)
unusually thin and weak, often to the point of looking unhealthy
very thin and weak in appearance

Examples

The scrawny boy struggled to lift the heavy backpack.
Her cat looked scrawny after being lost for a week.
A row of scrawny trees lined the dry hillside.

Synset Examples

scrawny cattle
scrubby cut-over pine
old stunted thorn trees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skinny - very thin, especially in a way that is unattractive
  • Synonym: emaciated - abnormally thin or weak, usually due to illness or lack of food
  • Antonym: plump - slightly fat in a pleasant or attractive way
  • Antonym: robust - strong and healthy; vigorous

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from Scots 'scraw', meaning 'thin' or 'meager'.
