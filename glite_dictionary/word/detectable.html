detectable

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈtɛktəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈtɛktəbl / (BrE)
able to be discovered, noticed, or identified; capable of being detected.
able to be detected

Examples

There was a detectable increase in temperature after the new equipment was installed.
No detectable traces of the chemical were found in the water sample.
His voice carried a detectable hint of frustration during the interview.

Synset Examples

a detectable note of sarcasm
he continued after a perceptible pause

Related Terms

  • Antonym: indetectable - not able to be detected
  • Synonym: perceptible - able to be perceived or noticed

Etymology

From French détectable, from Latin detectus (discovered, uncovered) + -able.

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈtɛktəbl̩ / (AmE), / dɪˈtɛktəbl̩ / (BrE)
able to be discovered or identified through observation or investigation.
able to be detected

Examples

The virus was barely detectable in early stages of the infection.
There was a detectable increase in temperature after the experiment began.
No detectable differences were found between the two samples.

Synset Examples

after a noticeable pause the lecturer continued

Related Terms

  • Synonym: noticeable - easily seen or detected
  • Antonym: undetectable - unable to be detected

Etymology

Formed from 'detect' (from Latin 'detegere', meaning to uncover) + '-able'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01751468-s
Synset POS: s
