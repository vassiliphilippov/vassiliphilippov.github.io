garbage

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑrbɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːbɪdʒ / (BrE)
a message, text, or information that is considered worthless, nonsensical, or irrelevant
worthless or nonsensical information

Examples

The email was full of garbage and made no sense at all.
Sometimes spam filters block legitimate messages, thinking they're just garbage.
This report is mostly garbage and doesn't contain any useful information.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonsense - speech or writing that has no meaning or makes no sense
  • Synonym: rubbish - something that is worthless or nonsensical; commonly used in British English
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06624619-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑrbɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːbɪdʒ / (BrE)
waste material, especially unwanted or spoiled food that is discarded, typically from a kitchen
discarded or waste food

Examples

Please remember to put the garbage outside before the truck arrives.
The kitchen garbage needs to be taken out because it smells bad.
She separates food garbage from recyclables for composting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rubbish - waste material; things that are no longer wanted
  • Synonym: trash - things that are considered worthless or thrown away, especially in American English

Etymology

Probably from Anglo-Norman 'garbage' (refuse, waste parts of animals), related to 'garbe' (a sheaf, entrails).

american

Examples

Please put your empty bottles in the garbage by the door.
The kitchen garbage was overflowing and needed to be emptied.
Before leaving, she always checks that the garbage has been taken out.

Related Terms
