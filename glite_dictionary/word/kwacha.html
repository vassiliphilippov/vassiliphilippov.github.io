kwacha

1. NOUN

/ kwɑː.tʃə / (AmE), / kwɑː.tʃə / (BrE)
the basic monetary unit of Zambia, equal to 100 ngwee
Zambian currency unit

Examples

Prices in Lusaka are listed in both kwacha and US dollars.
She exchanged her dollars for kwacha at the airport.
The Zambian kwacha has seen fluctuations against the major global currencies.

Etymology

From Nyanja and Bemba, meaning 'dawn,' symbolizing the dawn of freedom.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑːtʃə / (AmE), / ˈkwætʃə / (BrE)
the basic unit of currency in Malawi
Malawi's currency unit

Examples

The shopkeeper quoted the price as 5,000 kwacha for the television.
He exchanged dollars for kwacha upon arriving in Malawi.
Tourists should check the latest kwacha exchange rates before traveling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kwacha - unit of currency in Zambia

Etymology

From Chichewa 'kwacha' (dawn), symbolizing the dawn of freedom.
