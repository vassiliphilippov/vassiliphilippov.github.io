Darlingtonia californica

1. NOUN

/ dɑːrˌlɪŋˈtoʊniə ˌkælɪˈfɔrnɪkə / (AmE), / dɑːˌlɪŋˈtəʊniə ˌkælɪˈfɔːnɪkə / (BrE)
A species of carnivorous plant native to northern California and Oregon, notable for its tubular, pitcher-like leaves and yellow-green flowers.
carnivorous plant of northern California and Oregon

Examples

Darlingtonia californica is often called the cobra lily because of its distinctive hooded leaves.
Collectors admire Darlingtonia californica for its unique insect-trapping pitchers.
The wetlands of northern California are home to populations of Darlingtonia californica.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cobra lily - common name for Darlingtonia californica

Etymology

Named after botanist William Darlington and the state of California.
