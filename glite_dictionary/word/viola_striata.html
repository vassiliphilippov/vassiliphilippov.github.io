Viola striata

1. NOUN

/ viˈoʊlə straɪˈeɪtə / (AmE), / vaɪˈəʊlə straɪˈɑːtə / (BrE)
A species of violet native to eastern North America, with leafy stems and large white or creamy flowers faintly marked with purple.
species of violet with white flowers

Examples

The woods were dotted with Viola striata, their pale flowers brightening the undergrowth.
Botanists collected samples of Viola striata for their study on native flora.
In early spring, Viola striata blooms add elegance to shaded gardens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: violet - a plant of the genus Viola, especially with purple or blue flowers

Etymology

From Latin; 'Viola' meaning violet, and 'striata' meaning striped, referring to the flower's markings.
