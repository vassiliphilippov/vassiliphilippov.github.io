e-mail

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːˌmeɪl / (AmE), / ˈiːmeɪl / (BrE)
A system for sending messages electronically from one computer user to another, typically across the internet.
system for electronic messaging

Examples

Many businesses rely on e-mail to communicate with their clients quickly and efficiently.
Before e-mail became popular, people sent letters through the post.
Some companies ban personal use of e-mail during working hours.

Synset Examples

you cannot send packages by electronic mail

Related Terms

  • Synonym: email - Electronic message sent over a computer network

Etymology

Formed from electronic mail, dating from the 1970s.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06289979-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ ˈiːˌmeɪl / (AmE), / ˈiːˌmeɪl / (BrE)
to send a message or information electronically via email
send a message electronically by email

Examples

I'll e-mail you the details as soon as I get home.
She e-mailed the report to her manager yesterday afternoon.
Please e-mail me if you have any further questions.

Synset Examples

she e-mailed me the good news

Related Terms

  • Synonym: email - send an electronic message

Etymology

From 'e-' meaning 'electronic' and 'mail.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01034323-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ˈiːˌmeɪl / (AmE), / ˈiːˌmeɪl / (BrE)
An address used as a person's identifier when communicating electronically via the email system.
electronic mail address

Examples

Please send your contact details, including your e-mail, to the office.
I changed my e-mail last month to make it more professional.
She couldn't remember whether the e-mail had a dot or an underscore in it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: email address - A specific string used to identify an account within the email system

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'electronic mail', first used in the 1970s.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-83993005-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈiːˌmeɪl / (AmE), / ˈiːˌmeɪl / (BrE)
A message sent and received electronically over a computer network, especially the Internet.
electronic message sent via the Internet

Examples

I received an e-mail from my manager this morning with the project details.
Please check your e-mail for the confirmed flight itinerary.
She responded to his e-mail within a few minutes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: email - Alternative (variant) spelling of e-mail, meaning electronic message system
  • Antonym: letter - Written or printed message sent to someone, traditionally by post

Etymology

Short for 'electronic mail', dating from the early days of digital communication.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-82169826-n
Synset POS: n
