to itemize

1. VERB

/ ˈaɪtəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈaɪtəmaɪz / (BrE)
to list things separately, usually in a systematic or detailed way
to list items one by one

Examples

Please itemize all your expenses for the trip in this report.
The accountant asked her to itemize her tax deductions before submitting the form.
We need to itemize each component of the budget for transparency.

Synset Examples

itemize one's tax deductions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: list - to make a list of

Etymology

From 'item' + '-ize', first used in English in the 19th century, based on 'item' meaning a distinct part or article.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00948620-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈaɪtəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈaɪtəmaɪz / (BrE)
to list or mention items one by one in detail
list items individually

Examples

Please itemize all the expenses in your report.
The company asked her to itemize every task completed during the project.
He carefully itemized his deductions on the tax form.

Synset Examples

She enumerated the many obstacles she had encountered
The doctor recited the list of possible side effects of the drug

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enumerate - to mention things one by one, to count
  • Synonym: list - to make a list of; enumerate

Etymology

From 'item' (Latin 'item', meaning 'also, likewise') plus '-ize'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00948137-v
Synset POS: v
