dough-faced

1. ADJ

/ ˈdoʊˌfeɪst / (AmE), / ˈdəʊˌfeɪst / (BrE)
having a pale, dull, or lifeless facial appearance, often as if tired or worn out.
having a pale, lifeless face

Examples

After staying up all night, she appeared dough-faced at breakfast.
The office workers returned from the late shift looking dough-faced and exhausted.
He always looked a bit dough-faced during the gloomy winter months.

Synset Examples

The tired children appeared dough-faced after the trip.
He looked dough-faced after that long meeting.

Etymology

From "dough" (soft, pale, uncooked bread mixture) + "faced" (having a specified facial appearance), referring to resemblance to the color or texture of dough.
