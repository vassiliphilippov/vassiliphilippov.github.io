Zoroaster

1. PROPN

/ ˌzɔːroʊˈæstər / (AmE), / ˌzɒrəʊˈæstə / (BrE)
the Persian prophet who founded Zoroastrianism (circa 628–551 BC)
founder of Zoroastrianism

Examples

Zoroaster is often credited as one of the world's earliest religious reformers.
Many ancient texts reference Zoroaster and his teachings.
The religion founded by Zoroaster is still practiced in parts of Iran and India.

Etymology

From Greek Zōroastres, derived from Old Persian Zarathustra.
