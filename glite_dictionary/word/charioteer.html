charioteer

1. NOUN

/ ˌtʃɛriəˈtɪr / (AmE), / ˌtʃɑːriəˈtɪə / (BrE)
a person who drives a chariot, especially in ancient times or races
driver of a chariot

Examples

The skilled charioteer led his team to victory in the Roman arena.
During the parade, a costumed charioteer waved to the cheering crowd.
Ancient Greek charioteers were celebrated for their speed and bravery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: driver - a person who drives a vehicle

Etymology

From Latin 'currus' (chariot), with the suffix '-eer' denoting a person associated with a job or activity.
