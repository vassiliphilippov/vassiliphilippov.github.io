Charioteer

1. NOUN

/ ˌʧɛriəˈtɪr / (AmE), / ˌtʃærɪəˈtɪə / (BrE)
a prominent constellation in the Northern Hemisphere, lying between the Great Bear (Ursa Major) and Orion, at the edge of the Milky Way; also called Auriga.
constellation in the Northern Hemisphere

Examples

The Charioteer is visible in the night sky during winter in the Northern Hemisphere.
Astronomers often use the star Capella to help locate the Charioteer constellation.
In mythology, the Charioteer is associated with a driver of a chariot carrying goats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Auriga - the Latin name for the Charioteer constellation

Etymology

Named after the Latin 'auriga' meaning 'charioteer', referring to its mythological depiction.
