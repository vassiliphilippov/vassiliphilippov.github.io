spaciously

1. ADV

/ ˈspeɪ.ʃəs.li / (AmE), / ˈspeɪ.ʃəs.li / (BrE)
in a way that provides plenty of space; with ample room
with plenty of space

Examples

The living room was spaciously decorated with only a few pieces of furniture.
Guests were seated spaciously to ensure everyone had enough personal space.
The exhibits were arranged spaciously, making it easy for visitors to walk around.

Synset Examples

the furniture was spaciously spread out

Related Terms

  • Antonym: cramped - in a way that lacks space

Etymology

From the adjective 'spacious' (from Old French spacieux, from Latin spatiosus, spacious) + -ly.
