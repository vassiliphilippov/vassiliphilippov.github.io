pancarditis

1. NOUN

/ ˌpæn.kɑrˈdaɪ.tɪs / (AmE), / ˌpan.kɑːˈdaɪ.tɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of all the layers of the heart, including the epicardium, myocardium, and endocardium
inflammation of the whole heart
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with pancarditis after presenting with chest pain and fever.
Untreated infections can sometimes lead to pancarditis, affecting all layers of the heart.
A thorough examination revealed signs consistent with pancarditis during the cardiac assessment.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: myocarditis - inflammation of the heart muscle (myocardium)
  • Degree Lower: pericarditis - inflammation of the pericardium (outer lining of the heart)

Etymology

From Greek pan- (all) + kardia (heart) + -itis (inflammation).
