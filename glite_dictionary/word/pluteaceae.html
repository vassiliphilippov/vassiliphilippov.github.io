Pluteaceae

1. NOUN

/ pluːtiˈeɪsiˌiː / (AmE), / pluːtiˈiːsii / (BrE)
A family of fungi in the order Agaricales characterized by free gill attachment and pink spores.
family of fungi with pink spores

Examples

Members of the Pluteaceae family are recognized by their free gills and pink spores.
The mushroom expert identified the specimen as belonging to the Pluteaceae.
Several edible species are found within the Pluteaceae family.

Etymology

From the genus Pluteus, combined with the standard biological suffix -aceae (denoting a family).

2. NOUN

/ pluːtiˈeɪsi.iː / (AmE), / pluːtɪˈeɪsɪiː / (BrE)
A family of fungi within the order Agaricales, characterized by free gill attachment and pink spores.
family of fungi with pink spores

Examples

The Pluteaceae include several genera of mushrooms often found on decaying wood.
Members of the Pluteaceae are known for their distinctive pink spore prints.
Mycologists recently discovered a new species belonging to the Pluteaceae family.

Etymology

From the genus name Pluteus, based on Latin 'pluteus' (shield), with scientific family suffix -aceae.
