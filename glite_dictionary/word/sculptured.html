sculptured

1. ADJ

/ ˈskʌlptʃɚd / (AmE), / ˈskʌlptʃəd / (BrE)
shaped or carved by cutting into a solid material, typically stone, wood, or metal, especially for artistic purposes.
shaped by carving or cutting

Examples

The museum displayed a sculptured bust of an ancient philosopher.
Ornate, sculptured pillars lined the grand cathedral's entrance.
Many fossils are preserved with exquisitely sculptured patterns on their shells.

Synset Examples

graven images
sculptured representations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carved - shaped by cutting material from the surface, often for decoration or art

Etymology

From Latin 'sculptura' (carving) via 'sculpture' + -ed (past participle).

2. ADJ

/ ˈskəlptʃɚd / (AmE), / ˈskʌlptʃəd / (BrE)
having a shape or form that resembles sculpture; shaped or molded in a way that suggests careful artistic design.
resembling sculpture in shape or form

Examples

The artist chose a sculptured approach for the vase, giving it a striking, three-dimensional quality.
Her sculptured cheekbones made her look like a model from a Renaissance painting.
The garden was filled with sculptured hedges, each neatly shaped into graceful curves.

Synset Examples

her finely modeled features
rendered with … vivid sculptural effect
the sculpturesque beauty of the athletes' bodies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sculptural - resembling or relating to sculpture
  • Synonym: sculpturesque - having the qualities of sculpture, especially in form or appearance

Etymology

From Latin 'sculptura' (sculpture), via French, meaning 'to carve.'
