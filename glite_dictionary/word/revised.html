revised

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈvaɪzd / (AmE), / rɪˈvaɪzd / (BrE)
altered by editing, rewording, or making changes such as adding or removing material
changed or edited version

Examples

The revised proposal included several new recommendations from the committee.
After receiving feedback, she submitted a revised draft of her essay.
The revised edition of the book features updated statistics and additional chapters.

Synset Examples

the amended bill passed easily

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amended - changed, especially officially or formally, often referring to documents or laws
  • Antonym: original - existing from the beginning; not altered or changed

Etymology

From Latin 'revidēre' (to look at again), via Old French and Middle English forms.

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈvaɪzd / (AmE), / rɪˈvaɪzd / (BrE)
(of a text or document) improved or brought up to date by making changes or corrections.
updated; improved or changed

Examples

The publisher released a revised version of the textbook this year.
Her report was revised to reflect the latest research findings.
Please refer to the revised guidelines for submitting your application.

Synset Examples

a revised edition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: updated - made more current or up to date
  • Antonym: original - existing from the beginning; not altered
