tattler

1. NOUN

/ ˈtætlər / (AmE), / ˈtatlə / (BrE)
any of several long-legged shorebirds of the genus Tringa, known for their loud whistling calls
long-legged shorebird with a loud call

Examples

The tattler is often seen along muddy shorelines searching for small invertebrates.
A loud call from the tattler echoed over the tidal flats at dawn.
Some birdwatchers travel great distances to catch a glimpse of a grey-tailed tattler.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtætlɚ / (AmE), / ˈtætlə / (BrE)
a person who reveals secrets or gossip about others indiscreetly
a person who gossips or tells secrets

Examples

Nobody trusted the tattler with sensitive information after she revealed their plans to everyone.
The classroom tattler was always quick to report misbehavior to the teacher.
He was labeled a tattler after spreading rumors about his coworkers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gossip - a person who habitually reveals personal or sensational facts about others
  • Synonym: blabbermouth - someone who cannot keep a secret
  • Antonym: confidant - a person with whom one shares a secret or private matter

Etymology

From English 'tattle' (to gossip) + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
