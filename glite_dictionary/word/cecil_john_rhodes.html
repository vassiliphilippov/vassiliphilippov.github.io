Cecil John Rhodes

1. PROPN

/ ˈsiːsəl dʒɑn roʊdz / (AmE), / ˈsiːsɪl dʒɒn rəʊdz / (BrE)
(1853–1902) A British colonial financier and statesman in South Africa, known for amassing a fortune in gold and diamond mining, colonizing territory now called Zimbabwe, and establishing the Rhodes Scholarship for study at Oxford University.
British colonialist and founder of Rhodes Scholarship

Examples

Cecil John Rhodes played a significant role in the colonization of southern Africa.
The Cecil John Rhodes legacy is often debated due to his involvement in colonialism.
Many students aspire to become Rhodes Scholars, a program started by Cecil John Rhodes.
