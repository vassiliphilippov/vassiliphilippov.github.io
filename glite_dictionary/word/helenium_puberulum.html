Helenium puberulum

1. NOUN

/ həˈliniəm pjuːˈbɛrələm / (AmE), / hɪˈlɛniəm pjuːˈbɛrʊləm / (BrE)
A species of sneezeweed native to the southwestern United States, especially southern California, known for its yellow flowers and commonly found in moist habitats.
species of sneezeweed plant

Examples

Helenium puberulum is often found in wet meadows across southern California.
Gardeners appreciate Helenium puberulum for its bright yellow blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
The restoration project included planting native species, such as Helenium puberulum, to support local ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sneezeweed - A common name for plants in the genus Helenium, often with yellow disk-shaped flowers

Etymology

From genus Helenium (named after Helen of Troy in Greek mythology) and Latin puberulus (downy, covered with fine soft hairs).
