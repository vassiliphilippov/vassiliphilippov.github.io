huffiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌfinɪs / (AmE), / ˈhʌfinɪs / (BrE)
A temporary feeling of annoyance or resentment, often expressed in a sulky or irritated manner.
a brief state of annoyance or resentment

Examples

Her huffiness faded quickly after she got an apology.
He excused her huffiness as just a bad mood.
Despite his huffiness, everyone could tell he wasn’t truly angry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritation - a feeling of being annoyed or irritated
  • Degree Higher: anger - a strong feeling of displeasure or hostility
  • Antonym: calmness - the state of being free from agitation or strong emotion

Etymology

From 'huffy' + the suffix '-ness', denoting a state or quality.
