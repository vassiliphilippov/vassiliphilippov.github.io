perjurer

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrdʒərər / (AmE), / ˈpɜːdʒ(ə)rə / (BrE)
A person who deliberately lies or gives false testimony under oath, especially in a court of law.
a person who commits perjury

Examples

The witness was later revealed to be a perjurer after inconsistencies in his story were uncovered.
A convicted perjurer may face serious legal consequences, including imprisonment.
It is unlawful for a perjurer to testify unless they confess to their false statements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: perjury - the act of deliberately giving false testimony under oath

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman and Old French 'perjurer', from Latin 'perjurare' (to perjure oneself).
