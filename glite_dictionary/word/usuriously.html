usuriously

1. ADV

/ juːˈsjʊr.i.əs.li / (AmE), / juːˈsjʊər.i.əs.li / (BrE)
To an excessive or exorbitant degree, especially in relation to charges or rates.
to an exorbitant or excessive degree

Examples

The landlord charged usuriously high rent for the tiny apartment.
Lenders who act usuriously are often condemned in financial regulations.
The merchant marked up the goods usuriously, deterring many potential buyers.

Synset Examples

prices are exorbitantly high in the capital

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exorbitantly - to an excessive, unreasonable degree

Etymology

From 'usurious' (from Latin 'usura', meaning 'interest'), with the adverbial suffix '-ly.'
