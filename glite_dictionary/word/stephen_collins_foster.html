Stephen Collins Foster

1. PROPN

/ ˈstiːvən ˈkɑlɪnz ˈfɑstər / (AmE), / ˈstiːvən ˈkɒlɪnz ˈfɒstə / (BrE)
(1826–1864) American songwriter renowned for popular songs reflecting the culture of the Southern United States before the Civil War.
American songwriter (1826–1864)

Examples

Stephen Collins Foster composed enduring American songs such as "Oh! Susanna" and "Camptown Races."
Many scholars consider Stephen Collins Foster the father of American music.
The work of Stephen Collins Foster is often performed at folk music festivals across the United States.
