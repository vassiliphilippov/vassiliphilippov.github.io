antivenene

1. NOUN

/ ˌæn.tiˈvɛn.iːn / (AmE), / ˌæntɪˈvɛniːn / (BrE)
A medical substance that neutralizes the effects of venom from snake, insect, or other animal bites.
antitoxin for animal venom
medical

Examples

The hospital stocked antivenene to treat victims of poisonous snake bites.
Immediate administration of antivenene can save lives after a scorpion sting.
Researchers are developing more effective antivenene for rare spider bites.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antivenom - a substance used to counteract the effects of venom
  • Synonym: antitoxin - a substance which neutralizes a specific toxin

Etymology

From 'anti-' (against) + 'venene', variant of 'venin' (venom).
