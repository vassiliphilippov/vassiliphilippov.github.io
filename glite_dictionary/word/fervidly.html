fervidly

1. ADV

/ ˈfɜːrvɪdli / (AmE), / ˈfɜːvɪdli / (BrE)
In a manner showing intense passion or enthusiasm.
with intense passion or enthusiasm

Examples

The speaker fervidly defended her beliefs against criticism from the audience.
Fans cheered fervidly for their team during the final minutes of the game.
He fervidly argued for environmental reforms at the city council meeting.

Synset Examples

both those for and against are fervently convinced they speak for the great majority of the people
a fierily opinionated book

Related Terms

  • Synonym: passionately - in a way that shows strong feelings
  • Antonym: calmly - in a way that shows lack of strong emotions

Etymology

From Latin fervidus (glowing, boiling).
