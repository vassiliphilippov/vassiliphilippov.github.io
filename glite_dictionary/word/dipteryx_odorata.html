Dipteryx odorata

1. NOUN

/ dɪpˈtɛrɪks oʊˈdɔrətə / (AmE), / dɪpˈtɛrɪks əʊˈdɔːrətə / (BrE)
A tall tropical South American tree whose fragrant black seeds, known as tonka beans, are used for flavoring.
tropical tree; source of tonka beans

Examples

The seeds of the Dipteryx odorata tree are prized for their sweet aroma and are used to flavor desserts.
In the Amazon rainforest, Dipteryx odorata can grow up to 30 meters tall.
Many perfumers value Dipteryx odorata for its unique, vanilla-like scent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tonka bean - the fragrant seed of Dipteryx odorata, used as flavoring

Etymology

From the genus name Dipteryx (Greek dipteros, meaning 'two-winged') and Latin odorata, meaning 'fragrant'.
