cacomixle

1. NOUN

/ ˌkækəˈmɪksəl / (AmE), / ˌkækəˈmɪksəl / (BrE)
a raccoon-like mammal native to Mexico and the southwestern United States, with a long bushy tail featuring black and white rings
raccoon-like mammal of Mexico and southwestern U.S.

Examples

The cacomixle is known for its agile climbing and nocturnal habits.
We spotted a cacomixle darting across the trail during our hike in Arizona.
Farmers sometimes find cacomixles foraging in their orchards at night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ringtail - raccoon-like mammal with a ringed tail, synonym for cacomixle

Etymology

From Nahuatl *tlakomiztli* (half cat, half mountain lion).
