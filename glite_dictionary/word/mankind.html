mankind

1. NOUN

/ ˈmænˌkaɪnd / (AmE), / ˈmænkaɪnd / (BrE)
all human beings collectively; the human race
the human race; all humans

Examples

Throughout history, mankind has made remarkable progress in science and technology.
We must work together to ensure the survival of mankind on this planet.
The search for knowledge is a defining trait of mankind.

Synset Examples

all the world loves a lover
she always used ‘humankind’ because ‘mankind’ seemed to slight the women

Related Terms

  • Synonym: humankind - all human beings collectively
  • Antonym: womankind - all women considered as a group

Etymology

From Old English 'mann' (man, human, person) + 'kind' (race, type, nature).
