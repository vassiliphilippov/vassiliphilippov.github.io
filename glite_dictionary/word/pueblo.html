Pueblo

1. NOUN

/ ˈpwɛbloʊ / (AmE), / ˈpwɛbləʊ / (BrE)
a member of any of several Native American peoples of the southwestern United States, known for living in villages (pueblos) built of adobe and stone
member of a southwestern Native American people

Examples

Many Pueblo still live in communities in New Mexico and Arizona today.
The Pueblo are famous for their traditional pottery and weaving.
Each Pueblo community has its own unique customs and ceremonies.

Etymology

From Spanish 'pueblo' (village, people), from Latin 'populus' (people).
