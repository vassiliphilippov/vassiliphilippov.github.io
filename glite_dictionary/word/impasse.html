impasse

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpæs / (AmE), / ˈæm.pæs / (BrE)
a situation in which progress is impossible, especially because people cannot agree
a deadlock or blockage in progress

Examples

After hours of discussion, the committee found itself at an impasse and couldn't move forward.
Talks between the two countries reached an impasse over border issues.
The project hit an impasse due to a lack of funding and conflicting priorities.

Synset Examples

reached an impasse on the negotiations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deadlock - a situation in which no progress can be made because of disagreement
  • Synonym: stalemate - a situation in which neither side can win or make progress

Etymology

From French 'impasse' (dead end), from 'in-' (not) + 'passer' (to pass).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɪmˌpæs / (AmE), / ˈæm.pæs / (BrE)
A street with only one entrance and exit; a dead-end street or cul-de-sac.
dead-end street; cul-de-sac

Examples

Their new house is located at the end of a quiet impasse, perfect for children to play.
The taxi driver mistakenly turned into an impasse and had to reverse back out to the main road.
Several elegant houses were built on the tree-lined impasse in the city center.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cul-de-sac - a street closed at one end; dead-end street
  • Antonym: through street - a street that continues without dead end

Etymology

From French 'impasse', meaning 'dead end' or 'blind alley'; from 'in-' (not) + 'passer' (to pass).

Synset POS: n
