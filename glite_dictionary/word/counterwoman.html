counterwoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊntərˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌwʊmən / (BrE)
a woman who serves customers at a counter, especially in a diner or similar establishment
female attendant at a counter

Examples

The counterwoman smiled as she took their breakfast order at the diner.
As a friendly counterwoman, she knew many regulars by name.
The cafe hired an experienced counterwoman to handle the morning rush.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: counterman - male attendant at a counter

Etymology

Formed from 'counter' (a surface for serving customers) + 'woman.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊntərˌwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌwʊmən / (BrE)
A woman whose job is to serve customers at a counter, especially in a diner or restaurant.
female counter attendant (diner/restaurant)

Examples

The counterwoman greeted each customer with a smile as they entered the diner.
After working as a counterwoman for years, she knew all the regulars by name.
A friendly counterwoman took our orders quickly and efficiently during the busy lunch rush.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: counterman - male who attends a counter (as in a diner)

Etymology

Formed from 'counter' + 'woman' by analogy with 'counterman.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-89117996-n
Synset POS: n
