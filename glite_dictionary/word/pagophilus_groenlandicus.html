Pagophilus groenlandicus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpeɪ.ɡoʊˈfaɪ.ləs ɡroʊn.lænˈdɪ.kəs / (AmE), / ˌpaɪ.ɡəʊˈfɪ.ləs ɡrən.lænˈdɪ.kəs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the harp seal, a common Arctic seal whose young are all white
scientific name for harp seal

Examples

The Pagophilus groenlandicus population migrates annually across the North Atlantic ice.
Young Pagophilus groenlandicus are prized for their distinctive white fur.
Scientists tracked several Pagophilus groenlandicus to study their unusual breeding behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harp seal - a common Arctic seal with pale, harp-shaped markings

Etymology

From Greek 'pagos' (ice) + 'philos' (loving), and Latin 'groenlandicus' (of Greenland).
