to ulcerate

1. VERB

/ ˈʌl.səˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈʌl.səˌreɪt / (BrE)
(of tissue) to develop ulcers or to cause ulcers to form.
to develop or cause ulcers (medical)

Examples

If left untreated, the wound may ulcerate and become infected.
Some medications can ulcerate the lining of the stomach.
If the skin continues to ulcerate, contact your doctor immediately.

Synset Examples

Her stomach was ulcerated

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to heal - to become healthy again; to recover from injury

Etymology

From Latin ulceratus, past participle of ulcerare ('to ulcerate'), from ulcus ('sore, ulcer').

WordNet ID: oewn-00019975-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈʌlsəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈʌlsəˌreɪt / (BrE)
(of a tissue or surface) to develop into or become affected by an ulcer.
develop into an ulcer
medical

Examples

If left untreated, the wound may ulcerate and become infected.
Some skin conditions cause the tissue to ulcerate over time.
The patient's mouth began to ulcerate as a side effect of the medication.

Synset Examples

Her stomach ulcerated

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to heal - to become healthy again; opposite of ulcerate

Etymology

From Latin ulceratus, past participle of ulcerare, from ulcus (sore, ulcer).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00538233-v
Synset POS: v
