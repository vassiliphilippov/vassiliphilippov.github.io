Dravidic

1. NOUN

/ drəˈvɪdɪk / (AmE), / drəˈvɪdɪk / (BrE)
a large family of languages spoken mainly in southern and central India and Sri Lanka, including Telugu, Tamil, Kannada, and Malayalam
a South Indian language family

Examples

Linguists have studied the Dravidic languages to trace their origins and development.
Tamil is one of the oldest and most prominent Dravidic languages.
The influence of Dravidic tongues can be seen in the vocabulary of some Indo-Aryan languages.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Indo-European - a large language family including most languages of Europe and many in South Asia

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'Drāviḍa' (southern India).
