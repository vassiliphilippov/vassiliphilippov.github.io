palely

1. ADV

/ ˈpeɪli / (AmE), / ˈpeɪli / (BrE)
In a way that lacks color, brightness, or vitality; faintly or weakly.
in a faint or weak manner

Examples

The moon shone palely through the thin clouds.
He smiled palely, trying to hide his fatigue.
The performance was only palely entertaining compared to her previous work.

Synset Examples

a palely entertaining show

Related Terms

  • Antonym: vividly - in a way that is full of color or life

Etymology

Formed from 'pale' + '-ly', the standard English adverbial suffix.

2. ADV

/ ˈpeɪlli / (AmE), / ˈpeɪlli / (BrE)
In a pale manner; lacking strong color or emotional vitality.
in a pale or colorless manner

Examples

She smiled palely, her face drained of all color after the shock.
The moon shone palely through the mist, casting a ghostly light over the field.
He spoke palely, his voice lacking its usual energy and enthusiasm.

Synset Examples

his wife, always palely appealing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pale - lacking strong color or intensity
  • Antonym: vividly - in a bright or intense manner
