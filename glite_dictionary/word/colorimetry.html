colorimetry

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɝː.əˌmɪtɹi / (AmE), / kəˈlɒɹ.ɪ.mə.tɹi / (BrE)
the quantitative measurement of the concentration of substances in solution by determining color intensity, typically using a colorimeter
measurement of chemical concentration by color

Examples

The laboratory uses colorimetry to determine the concentration of metal ions in water samples.
Advances in colorimetry have improved the accuracy of chemical analyses in environmental science.
Students learned about colorimetry as part of their introductory chemistry course.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spectrophotometry - the quantitative measurement of the absorption or transmission of light by a substance

Etymology

From French *colorimétrie*, based on Latin *color* (color) and Greek *metria* (measurement).
