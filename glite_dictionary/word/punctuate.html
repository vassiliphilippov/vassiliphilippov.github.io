to punctuate

1. VERB

/ ˈpʌŋktʃuˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈpʌŋktjʊeɪt / (BrE)
to insert punctuation marks into written text in order to clarify meaning or separate sentences and elements
insert punctuation marks into text

Examples

Please punctuate this paragraph correctly before submitting your assignment.
He often forgets to punctuate his sentences, making them hard to read.
The teacher showed the class how to punctuate direct speech properly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to edit - to prepare written material for publication by correcting and revising

Etymology

From Medieval Latin punctuare, from Latin punctum (point).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00190355-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈpʌŋktʃuˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈpʌŋktjʊeɪt / (BrE)
to interrupt or occur at intervals throughout something; to break up the continuity of something at intervals
to interrupt at intervals

Examples

Loud applause punctuated his speech several times throughout the evening.
The silence of the night was occasionally punctuated by distant howls of coyotes.
Her laughter punctuated the conversation, making the atmosphere light and cheerful.

Synset Examples

Her sharp questions punctuated the speaker's drone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interrupt - to break the continuity or uniformity of something

Etymology

From Medieval Latin punctuatus, past participle of punctuare 'to mark with points', from Latin punctum 'point'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00365131-v
Synset POS: v
