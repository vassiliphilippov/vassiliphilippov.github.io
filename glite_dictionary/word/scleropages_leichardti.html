Scleropages leichardti

1. NOUN

/ sklɛrəˌpeɪdʒiz laɪˈkɑːrdti / (AmE), / sklɪərəˌpeɪdʒiːz laɪˈkɑːdiː / (BrE)
A large freshwater fish native to Australian rivers, known for a red or pink spot on each scale.
Australian freshwater fish species

Examples

The Scleropages leichardti is highly prized by fish enthusiasts for its striking scales.
Researchers are working to protect the Scleropages leichardti from habitat loss.
Many aquarists are fascinated by the vivid coloration of Scleropages leichardti.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'skleros' (hard) and 'pago' (fasten), species named after explorer Ludwig Leichhardt.
