mezereon

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛzəˈriːən / (AmE), / ˌmɛzəˈriːən / (BrE)
A small European deciduous shrub (Daphne mezereum) with fragrant lilac-colored flowers, followed by red berries and highly toxic twigs.
European shrub with lilac flowers and red berries

Examples

The garden contained a rare mezereon with brilliant red berries in late summer.
You should be careful when handling mezereon because every part of the plant is poisonous.
In spring, the mezereon fills the air with its sweet scent before its leaves appear.

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *mezereon*, from Arabic *mazariyun*, referring to the plant.
