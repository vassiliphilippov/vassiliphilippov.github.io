nephrolith

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɛfroʊˌlɪθ / (AmE), / ˈnɛfrəʊˌlɪθ / (BrE)
A stone or calculus formed in the kidney; a kidney stone.
kidney stone; renal calculus
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with a nephrolith after experiencing severe flank pain.
Surgical removal of a nephrolith may be necessary if it causes obstruction.
Analysis of the nephrolith helped the doctors determine the underlying cause of stone formation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kidney stone - A hard mass formed in the kidneys from minerals and salts

Etymology

From Greek nephrós (kidney) and lithos (stone).
