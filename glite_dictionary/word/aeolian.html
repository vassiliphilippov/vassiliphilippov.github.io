Aeolian

1. ADJ

/ iˈoʊliən / (AmE), / iˈəʊliən / (BrE)
relating to or caused by the wind; of or pertaining to Aeolus, the Greek god of the winds.
relating to the wind or Aeolus

Examples

The Aeolian processes shaped the dunes over thousands of years.
She played a piece in the Aeolian mode on her violin.
Many deserts are dominated by Aeolian features created by persistent winds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eolian - alternative spelling meaning relating to, or caused by the wind

Etymology

From Latin Aeolius, from Ancient Greek Aiolos (the god of the winds).

2. NOUN

/ iˈoʊliən / (AmE), / iːˈəʊlɪən / (BrE)
A member of an ancient Greek people belonging to the Aeolic (Aeolian) linguistic division, native to regions including Boeotia, Thessaly, Lesbos, and parts of Asia Minor.
member of ancient Aeolian Greek group

Examples

The Aeolian dialect was spoken on the island of Lesbos in ancient times.
Many Aeolians migrated to Asia Minor, establishing their own colonies along the coast.
As an Aeolian, Sappho contributed significantly to early Greek poetry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Dorian - member of another ancient Greek tribe
  • Synonym: Ionian - member of the Ionian branch of Greeks
  • Synonym: Achaean - member of one of the main ancient Greek tribes

Etymology

From Greek Aiōlios, relating to Aeolis, the region inhabited by the Aeolians.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09730149-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ iˈoʊliən / (AmE), / iːˈəʊliən / (BrE)
An ancient Greek inhabitant of Aeolia, a region of Asia Minor and nearby islands.
ancient Greek from Aeolia

Examples

Many Aeolians migrated from Thessaly to Asia Minor during the early Greek migrations.
Legends say that the Aeolian dialect was prominent in ancient poetry and song.
The archaeological site revealed artifacts attributed to the Aeolian settlers.

Etymology

From Latin 'Aeolius', from Ancient Greek 'Aiolios', relating to Aeolus or Aeolia.
