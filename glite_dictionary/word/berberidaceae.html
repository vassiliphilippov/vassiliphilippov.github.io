Berberidaceae

1. NOUN

/ bɜr.bə.rɪˈdeɪ.siː.iː / (AmE), / bɜː.bər.ɪˈdeɪ.siː.iː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants, mainly shrubs and herbs, including barberry and similar species
family of shrubs and herbs (barberry family)

Examples

The Berberidaceae family includes many species known for their medicinal properties.
Several ornamental plants belong to the Berberidaceae family, adding color to gardens.
Botanists discovered a new shrub in the Berberidaceae family in the mountains.

Etymology

From modern Latin, formed from the genus name Berberis and standard botanical suffix -aceae (family).

2. NOUN

/ bɚˌbɛrɪˈdeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / bɜːˌbɛrɪˈdeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
A family of shrubs and herbs, including barberries and their relatives, native to temperate regions and known for their simple, often spiny leaves.
a family of shrubs and herbs (barberries and relatives)

Examples

The Berberidaceae family includes both ornamental and medicinal plants.
Many members of the Berberidaceae can be found in northern temperate forests.
Botanists study the Berberidaceae to learn about plant adaptations.

Etymology

From Berberis (the barberry genus) + Latin -aceae (denoting plant families).
