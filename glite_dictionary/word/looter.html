looter

1. NOUN

/ ˈluːtər / (AmE), / ˈluːtə / (BrE)
a person who steals goods, especially during a riot, war, or natural disaster
person who steals goods during unrest

Examples

The police arrested several looters who were breaking into abandoned shops after the hurricane.
During the chaos of war, looters ransacked homes and businesses.
The museum was heavily guarded to protect it from looters during the conflict.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thief - a person who steals, especially secretly or by stealth
  • Synonym: robber - a person who steals by force or threat
  • Synonym: plunderer - a person who takes goods by force, especially in war

Etymology

From loot (plunder or steal), from Hindi lūṭ (spoils, booty).
