technical

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛk.nɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈtɛk.nɪ.kəl / (BrE)
A pickup truck fitted with a machine gun or similar weapon, often used in conflict zones by irregular military forces.
armed pickup truck used in conflict

Examples

Several militias in the region rely on technicals for mobility and firepower.
A convoy of technicals advanced through the desert toward the city.
Journalists took cover as the technical approached, its mounted gun at the ready.

Etymology

Derived from a usage in Somalia, where the cost for mounting weapons was filed as 'technical assistance' by NGOs.

5. NOUN

/ ˈtɛk.nɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈtɛk.nɪ.kəl / (BrE)
(in basketball and other sports) a foul for unsportsmanlike conduct or other infractions that do not involve physical contact between players.

Examples

The referee gave him a technical for arguing too aggressively.
She was ejected from the game after receiving her second technical.
Coaches can also be assessed a technical if they behave inappropriately on the sidelines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foul - an unfair or illegal act in sports

Etymology

Derived from 'technical foul', where 'technical' refers to technicalities in the rules.
