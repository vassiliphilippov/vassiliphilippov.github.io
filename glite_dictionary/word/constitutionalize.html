to constitutionalize

1. VERB

/ kənˈstɪtuːʃənəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / kənˈstɪtjuːʃənəˌlaɪz / (BrE)
to provide with or subject to a constitution, especially as a country or organization
give a constitution to

Examples

The new leaders worked to constitutionalize the nation after the revolution.
It took years of negotiation to constitutionalize the country's political system.
Several former colonies were constitutionalized during the nineteenth century.

Synset Examples

The United States were constitutionalized in the late 18th century

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to codify - arrange laws or rules into a systematic code
  • Antonym: to abolish - to formally end a system or practice, especially a law or constitution

Etymology

From constitution + -alize.

2. VERB

/ kənˌstɪtuːʃə.nəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / kənˌstɪtjuːʃə.nəˌlaɪz / (BrE)
to incorporate (a principle, right, or organisation) into a constitution; to make something part of or governed by a constitution
make part of a constitution

Examples

After years of debate, the country decided to constitutionalize environmental protection.
The Supreme Court ruling effectively constitutionalized the right to free speech.
Activists are working to constitutionalize the right to access clean water.

Synset Examples

A woman's right to an abortion was constitutionalized in the 1970's

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to entrench - to establish firmly within a constitution or system
  • Antonym: to invalidate - to make not valid, especially legally

Etymology

From constitution + -alize, meaning to make constitutional.
