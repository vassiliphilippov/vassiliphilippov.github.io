Tertullian

1. PROPN

/ tərˈtʌliən / (AmE), / təˈtʌlɪən / (BrE)
(c. 160–c. 230 CE) A Carthaginian Christian theologian and writer whose works had a lasting influence on early Christian doctrine and Latin Christian literature.
early Christian theologian and writer

Examples

Many scholars cite Tertullian as a key figure in the development of Western Christianity.
Tertullian wrote extensively about the nature of the Trinity.
The works of Tertullian continue to be studied in theology courses around the world.

Etymology

Latinized form of the name Quintus Septimius Florens Tertullianus.
