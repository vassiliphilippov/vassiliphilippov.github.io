protozoologist

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊtoʊzoʊˈɑlədʒɪst / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtəʊzəʊˈɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
a scientist who specializes in the study of protozoa, a large group of single-celled microscopic organisms
scientist specializing in protozoa

Examples

As a protozoologist, she spent years researching the life cycles of amoebae.
The conference featured a renowned protozoologist discussing recent discoveries in parasitic protozoa.
He dreamed of becoming a protozoologist after observing microorganisms under his first microscope.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zoologist - a scientist who studies animals

Etymology

From protozoa (from Greek prōto- 'first' + zōon 'animal') + -logist (from Greek -logia, 'the study of').
