passivity

1. NOUN

/ pæˈsɪvɪti / (AmE), / pəˈsɪvɪti / (BrE)
the state or quality of accepting or allowing what happens without active response or resistance; lack of initiative or action
lack of active response or initiative

Examples

His passivity in the face of injustice frustrated his friends.
The teacher was concerned about the students' passivity during discussions.
Excessive passivity can hinder personal growth and problem-solving.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: apathy - lack of interest, enthusiasm, or concern
  • Antonym: initiative - the ability to assess and initiate things independently

Etymology

From Latin passivus (capable of suffering or feeling), from patī (to suffer).

2. NOUN

/ pæˈsɪvɪti / (AmE), / pæˈsɪvɪti / (BrE)
acceptance of or submission to the actions or influence of others, without active resistance or response
submissiveness or lack of resistance

Examples

His passivity in meetings made it difficult for his ideas to be heard.
Sometimes passivity is mistaken for agreement, though it may simply reflect reluctance to engage.
The teacher encouraged students to replace passivity with active participation in class discussions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: submission - the act of accepting or yielding to a superior force or to the will or authority of another person
  • Antonym: assertiveness - the quality of being self-assured and confident without being aggressive

Etymology

From Latin passivus (capable of suffering or feeling), from passus, past participle of patī (to suffer).
