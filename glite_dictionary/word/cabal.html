cabal

1. NOUN

/ kəˈbɑl / (AmE), / kəˈbɑːl / (BrE)
a secret group of people involved in plotting something harmful or illegal, especially in politics
secret plot or group in politics

Examples

Rumors spread about a cabal working behind the scenes to overthrow the government.
The investigation uncovered a cabal of powerful business leaders involved in illegal activities.
Some believed that a secret cabal was controlling election outcomes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: conspiracy - a secret plan by a group to do something unlawful or harmful

Etymology

From French 'cabale', from Medieval Latin 'cabala', from Hebrew 'qabbālāh' (tradition).

2. NOUN

/ kəˈbɑl / (AmE), / kəˈbɑːl / (BrE)
A secret group or faction seeking to influence or control events, often through intrigue or conspiratorial means.
a secret group seeking power

Examples

Rumors spread that a powerful cabal was manipulating the outcome of the election.
Some historians believe a cabal was responsible for the sudden change in policy.
Fearing opposition, the cabal held their meetings in absolute secrecy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clique - a small, exclusive group of people
  • Synonym: conspiracy - a secret plan by a group to do something unlawful or harmful

Etymology

From French cabale, from Medieval Latin cabbala, from Hebrew qabbālāh (tradition).

3. VERB

/ kəˈbɑl / (AmE), / kəˈbɑːl / (BrE)
to plot secretly or conspire, especially to achieve a political end
plot or conspire secretly

Examples

A small group tried to cabal against the leadership of the organization.
Rumors spread that the council members would secretly cabal to influence the decision.
Historians believe the ministers caballed at court to undermine the king's authority.

Synset Examples

They conspired to overthrow the government

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to conspire - to make secret plans jointly to commit an unlawful or harmful act
  • Synonym: plot - to make secret plans to achieve a particular aim, especially illegally or harmfully

Etymology

From French 'cabale', from Medieval Latin 'cabala', originally referring to secret mystical doctrines.
