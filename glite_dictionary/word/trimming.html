trimming

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtrɪmɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of adding decorative items or embellishment, especially to something such as a Christmas tree, garment, or surface
act of adding decorations or embellishments

Examples

Every year, the trimming of the Christmas tree was a cherished family tradition.
The tailor added final trimming to the dress before the show.
She enjoyed the trimming of cakes with colorful icing and sprinkles.

Synset Examples

the children had to be in bed before it was time for the trimming of the tree

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decoration - the process or result of making something more attractive with additions

Etymology

From the verb 'trim' (to decorate), originating from Old English 'trymman' (to strengthen, arrange).

2. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtrɪmɪŋ / (BrE)
a decorative edging or ornament added to the edge of a garment, curtain, or similar item
decorative edging of a garment

Examples

The dress was finished with delicate lace trimming along the sleeves.
He admired the gold trimming on her evening coat.
The designer chose velvet trimming to create a luxurious edge on the curtains.

Synset Examples

the trimming on a hat
the trim on a shirt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trim - decorative adornment, edge or finish of a garment

Etymology

From the verb 'trim', meaning 'to adorn or decorate', first used in this sense in the 16th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈtrɪmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtrɪmɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of cutting or shaping something to achieve a desired size or form
the act of cutting or shaping to size

Examples

I gave the hedge a careful trimming to keep it neat.
Proper trimming of fabric is essential for a tidy hem.
The gardener did a quick trimming before the guests arrived.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pruning - removing superfluous parts, especially from plants, to promote growth
  • Synonym: shaping - the act of giving something a particular shape

Etymology

From the verb 'trim', Middle English 'trimmen' (to make ready, arrange).
