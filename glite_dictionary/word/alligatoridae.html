Alligatoridae

1. NOUN

/ æˌlɪɡəˈtɛrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ælɪˌɡeɪtəˈraɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of large, primarily aquatic reptiles including alligators and caimans, distinguished by broad, flat snouts and found mainly in the Americas and China
family of reptiles: alligators and caimans

Examples

The Alligatoridae family includes both alligators and caimans.
Scientists study the evolutionary relationships between Alligatoridae and other reptiles.
Members of Alligatoridae can be found in both the Americas and China.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alligator - large aquatic reptile of the genus Alligator
  • Synonym: caiman - large reptile related to the alligator, native to Central and South America

Etymology

From New Latin, formed from 'alligator' and the scientific family suffix '-idae'.

2. NOUN

/ əˌlɪɡəˈtɔrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / əˌlɪɡəˈtɔːrɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of large aquatic reptiles that includes alligators and caimans, found chiefly in the Americas and China
family of reptiles: alligators, caimans

Examples

Members of the Alligatoridae family are found in freshwater habitats across the Americas and China.
The Alligatoridae include both alligators and caimans, which are distinguished from crocodiles by their broader snouts.
Modern representatives of Alligatoridae are important subjects in the study of reptilian evolution.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alligator - a large reptile with a broad, rounded snout, native to the Americas and China
  • Synonym: caiman - a Central and South American reptile closely related to the alligator

Etymology

From New Latin Alligatoridae, from Alligator (genus) + -idae (family suffix).
