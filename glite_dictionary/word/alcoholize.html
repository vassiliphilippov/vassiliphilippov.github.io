to alcoholize

1. VERB

/ ˈæl.kəˌlɑɪz / (AmE), / ˈæl.kəˌlaɪz / (BrE)
to treat or infuse with alcohol, or to convert into alcohol by chemical means
treat or infuse with alcohol

Examples

The chef decided to alcoholize the cherries by soaking them in brandy overnight.
Certain fruits are often alcoholized to enhance their flavor and preserve them.
You can alcoholize desserts to create a unique taste profile for special occasions.

Synset Examples

alcoholize the fruit and let them sit in the refrigerator

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to infuse - to soak or fill with a particular quality or ingredient

Etymology

Formed from 'alcohol' + '-ize', following regular verb formation patterns in English.

/ ˈælkəˌhɑːˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈalkəhɒˌlaɪz / (BrE)
to subject someone or something to the influence of alcohol, especially by causing intoxication
make subject to alcohol's effects

Examples

After the party, many guests were thoroughly alcoholized and needed a taxi home.
It is irresponsible to alcoholize children at such a young age.
The chef refused to alcoholize the dish for customers who requested a non-alcoholic version.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to intoxicate - to make someone lose control of their behavior or faculties due to alcohol or drugs

Etymology
