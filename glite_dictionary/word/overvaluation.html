overvaluation

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvərvælˈjuːeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəvælˈjuːeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act or process of assigning too high a value or price to something.
the act of valuing something too highly

Examples

The recent housing bubble was fueled by the overvaluation of real estate in many areas.
Analysts warned that overvaluation of tech stocks could lead to a market correction.
Her appraisal resulted in the overvaluation of the painting, which later sold for much less.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: undervaluation - the act of assigning too low a value or price to something

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'over-' (too much) and 'valuation' (assessment of value).

2. NOUN

/ ˌoʊvərvæl.juˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəljuːˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of estimating the value of something as higher than it really is
an appraisal that is too high

Examples

The recent stock market bubble was caused by the overvaluation of technology companies.
The auditor warned that the overvaluation of assets could mislead investors.
Due to overvaluation, the property's price far exceeded its actual worth.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: undervaluation - the act of appraising something as less valuable than it is

Etymology

From 'over-' (excessively) + 'valuation' (estimation of worth).

Synset POS: n
