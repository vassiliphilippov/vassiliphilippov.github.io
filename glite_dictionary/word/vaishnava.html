Vaishnava

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪʃnəvə / (AmE), / ˈvaɪʃnəvə / (BrE)
A devotee or follower of the Hindu deity Vishnu.
worshipper of Vishnu (Hinduism)

Examples

Many Vaishnavas observe special rituals during religious festivals dedicated to Vishnu.
The temple was built by a group of Vaishnava merchants in the 18th century.
She became a Vaishnava after learning about bhakti traditions in India.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Shaivite - a devotee or follower of the Hindu god Shiva

Etymology

From Sanskrit vaiṣṇava, meaning 'pertaining to Vishnu.'
