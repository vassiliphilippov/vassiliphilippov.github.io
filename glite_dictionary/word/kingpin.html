kingpin

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪŋˌpɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɪŋ.pɪn / (BrE)
the front pin in the triangular arrangement of ten pins in bowling
the front pin in bowling

Examples

The bowler aimed directly at the kingpin to maximize his chances of a strike.
If the kingpin falls, the other pins are more likely to topple as well.
She knocked down the kingpin with her very first roll.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɪŋˌpɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɪŋ.pɪn / (BrE)
a large, central bolt that forms the pivot in the steering mechanism of a motor vehicle
pivot bolt in steering mechanism

Examples

The mechanic replaced the worn kingpin to improve the truck's steering.
If the kingpin fails, it can seriously affect the control of your vehicle.
Regular maintenance checks should include inspection of the kingpin for signs of wear.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɪŋˌpɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɪŋˌpɪn / (BrE)
the most important or influential person in a group, organization, or activity
most important person; leader

Examples

As the kingpin of the operation, she controlled every important decision.
Police arrested the alleged kingpin of the drug cartel last night.
He always wanted to be the kingpin in his field, commanding respect from all his peers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boss - a person in charge; the leader of a group
  • Antonym: underling - a person of lower rank or authority; subordinate

Etymology

From 'king' + 'pin'; originally referred to the centrally placed pin in bowling.
