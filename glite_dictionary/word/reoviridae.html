Reoviridae

1. NOUN

/ ˌrioʊˈvɪrɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˌriːəʊˈvɪərɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of viruses transmitted by arthropods that can infect the gastrointestinal and respiratory systems of humans and animals
family of viruses affecting digestion and respiration
medical

Examples

Scientists are studying the Reoviridae to better understand their role in human diseases.
Certain types of Reoviridae can cause infections in both animals and humans.
The laboratory identified the virus as belonging to the Reoviridae family after extensive testing.

Etymology

From 'reo-' (respiratory enteric orphan virus) + Latin '-viridae' (virus family suffix).
