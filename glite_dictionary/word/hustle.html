hustle

1. VERB

/ ˈhʌsəl / (AmE), / ˈhʌs.əl / (BrE)
to move or cause someone to move energetically and quickly, often in a busy or determined way
move busily or energetically

Examples

The nurses had to hustle to get all the patients ready on time.
We watched the cooks hustle in the busy kitchen before dinner was served.
After the bell rang, students began to hustle from class to class.

Synset Examples

The cheerleaders bustled about excitedly before their performance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bustle - to move in an energetic and busy manner

Etymology

Origin unknown; first recorded in American English in the 19th century, possibly from Dutch 'husselen' (to shake, toss).

Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ ˈhʌsəl / (AmE), / ˈhʌsəl / (BrE)
to obtain or achieve something by energetic effort or cleverness, sometimes by persuasion or pressure
obtain by energetic effort or cleverness
informal

Examples

She managed to hustle a free lunch out of the manager by chatting with him.
You have to hustle if you want to get by in this city.
He hustled tickets to the concert by convincing people they were about to sell out.

Synset Examples

she hustled a free lunch from the waiter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to scrounge - obtain something by seeking or begging, often informally

Etymology

Perhaps from Dutch 'hutselen' (to shake or toss).

3. VERB

/ ˈhʌsəl / (AmE), / ˈhʌsəl / (BrE)
to pressure or urge someone forcefully into taking a particular action
to pressure or urge someone

Examples

The salesman tried to hustle me into buying an extended warranty.
She knows how to hustle her friends into joining her at events.
You can't hustle me into making a quick decision.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: coax - to gently persuade someone to do something
  • Degree Higher: bully - to use intimidation or abuse to force someone to act

Etymology

Probably from Dutch *husselen* (to shake, to toss).

4. NOUN

/ ˈhʌsəl / (AmE), / ˈhʌsəl / (BrE)
a dishonest scheme or swindle, especially one involving cheating at gambling or persuading someone to buy worthless property
a dishonest scheme or swindle

Examples

He fell for a hustle that promised high returns on a worthless investment.
Beware of casino hustles where players collude to cheat newcomers.
The antique watch turned out to be a hustle by a street vendor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scam - a dishonest scheme or fraud
  • Synonym: con - a trick or swindle to deceive someone
  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and fair

Etymology

Probably from Dutch 'husselen' (to shake, toss).

5. NOUN

/ ˈhʌsəl / (AmE), / ˈhʌsəl / (BrE)
a state of busy or energetic activity and movement, often involving hurry or commotion
busy or energetic activity; commotion

Examples

The morning hustle in the city streets made it hard to walk quickly.
After the hustle of the festival, the town felt unusually quiet.
She missed the daily hustle of working in a busy office.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bustle - energetic, often noisy, activity and movement
  • Antonym: calm - a state of peacefulness and lack of activity or commotion

Etymology

Originally from Dutch *husselen* (to shake, to toss), via obsolete English sense 'to shake, to push roughly'.
