to decommission

1. VERB

/ ˌdiː.kəˈmɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌdiː.kəˈmɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
to officially withdraw something, such as a ship or facility, from active service or use
withdraw from active service or use

Examples

The old power plant was decommissioned after decades of operation.
The navy decided to decommission several of its older ships.
Workers have begun decommissioning the factory following its closure.

Synset Examples

The warship was decommissioned in 1998

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to retire - to take out of use or service, especially after a period of use

Etymology

From de- (expressing reversal) + commission (authorization to act); originally referring to ships or military units.
