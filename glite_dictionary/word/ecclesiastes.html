Ecclesiastes

1. PROPN

/ ɪˌkliːziˈæstiz / (AmE), / ɪˌkliːziˈɑːstiːz / (BrE)
a book of the Old Testament containing reflections on the meaning and vanity of human life, traditionally attributed to Solomon
book of the Old Testament reflecting on life's meaning

Examples

Many scholars debate the real author of Ecclesiastes and its philosophical outlook.
He found solace in reading Ecclesiastes during times of uncertainty.
The themes explored in Ecclesiastes have inspired countless writers and thinkers throughout history.

Etymology

From Greek Ekklēsiastēs (member of an assembly), via Latin Ecclesiastes, translating Hebrew Qoheleth.
