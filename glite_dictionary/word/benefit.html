benefit

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɛnɪfɪt / (AmE), / ˈbɛnɪfɪt / (BrE)
an event, especially a performance, held to raise money for a charity or cause
charity fundraising event

Examples

The theater hosted a benefit to support local homeless shelters.
She performed at the benefit for cancer research last weekend.
Tickets for the benefit sold out within hours of being released.

2. VERB

/ ˈbɛnəfɪt / (AmE), / ˈbɛnɪfɪt / (BrE)
to be helpful or advantageous to someone or something
be helpful or advantageous

Examples

Regular exercise can benefit your overall health.
The new tax laws are designed to benefit small businesses.
Both students and teachers benefit from interactive learning methods.

Synset Examples

This will do you good

Related Terms

  • Antonym: harm - to cause damage or injury to

Etymology

From Latin 'beneficium' (a favor, benefit).

3. NOUN

/ ˈbɛnəfɪt / (AmE), / ˈbɛnɪfɪt / (BrE)
an advantage, improvement, or help that something provides, especially in promoting well-being
an advantage or help; improvement

Examples

Many employees receive health insurance as a benefit of their jobs.
The new community center will be a real benefit to local residents.
Regular exercise has numerous benefits for your mental and physical health.

Synset Examples

for the benefit of all

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advantage - a condition or circumstance that puts one in a favorable position
  • Antonym: drawback - a feature that renders something less acceptable; a disadvantage

Etymology

From Latin 'bene' (well) + 'facere' (to do), via Old French 'bienfait'.

4. VERB

/ ˈbɛnəfɪt / (AmE), / ˈbɛnɪfɪt / (BrE)
to receive an advantage or help from something; to be helped or improved by something
receive an advantage from something

Examples

Many local businesses benefited from the new shopping center.
Children benefit greatly from exercise and fresh air.
You will benefit if you follow your doctor's advice.

Synset Examples

She profited from his vast experience

Related Terms

  • Synonym: profit - to gain a financial or other advantage from something
  • Antonym: to suffer - to experience something bad or harmful instead of an advantage

Etymology

From Latin *bene* (well) + *facere* (to do), via Old French *beneficier* (to do good).

5. NOUN

/ ˈbenəfɪt / (AmE), / ˈbenɪfɪt / (BrE)
a payment or service provided by the government or an employer to help people in financial need, such as unemployment, sickness, or retirement
payment or service to help people in need

Examples

After losing his job, Mark applied for benefit to support his family.
Some parents receive child benefit to help with the cost of raising children.
She was grateful for the unemployment benefit during a tough period in her life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: allowance - an amount of money given regularly for a specific purpose
  • Synonym: grant - a sum of money given by a government or organization for a specific purpose
  • Synonym: welfare - government-provided financial support for people in need

Etymology

From Latin *beneficium* (a favor, benefit), from *bene* (well) + *facere* (to do).

Synset POS: n
