SLS

1. NOUN

/ ɛs ɛl ɛs / (AmE), / ɛs ɛl ɛs / (BrE)
a caustic cleaning and foaming agent (sodium lauryl sulfate) used in detergents, shampoos, and toothpastes, known to cause skin irritation in some people
detergent (sodium lauryl sulfate) used in personal care products

Examples

Check the label for SLS if you have sensitive skin.
Many eco-friendly brands advertise their products as free from SLS.
Some people experience mouth irritation from toothpaste containing SLS.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sodium lauryl sulfate - a chemical detergent and foaming agent

Etymology

Abbreviation of sodium lauryl sulfate, from sodium + lauryl (from lauric acid) + sulfate.
