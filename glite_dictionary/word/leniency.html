leniency

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːniənsi / (AmE), / ˈliːniənsi / (BrE)
the quality of being more merciful or less severe than expected, especially in punishing or judging someone
mercy or mildness in punishment

Examples

The judge showed unexpected leniency and reduced his sentence to probation.
Some parents believe that too much leniency leads to undisciplined children.
The teacher's leniency toward late homework surprised the students.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: severity - the quality of being very strict or harsh
  • Synonym: clemency - mercy or lenient treatment, especially in judging or punishing someone

Etymology

From Latin 'lenientia' (mildness, gentleness) via late Latin and French.

2. NOUN

/ ˈliːniənsi / (AmE), / ˈliːniənsi / (BrE)
the quality of being more permissive or tolerant than expected, often by yielding to the wishes of others
permissive or tolerant attitude

Examples

The teacher showed leniency towards students who had genuine reasons for missing the deadline.
Parents who practice excessive leniency may find it harder to set firm boundaries for their children.
The committee debated whether leniency should be extended to first-time offenders.

Synset Examples

too much indulgence spoils a child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indulgence - allowing someone to do what they want; permissiveness
  • Antonym: strictness - demanding exact obedience; severity

Etymology

From Latin 'lenientia' (mildness, gentleness), from 'lenis' (gentle, mild).

3. NOUN

/ ˈliːniənsi / (AmE), / ˈliːniənsi / (BrE)
the quality of being merciful, tolerant, or not as severe in judgment or punishment as expected
merciful or tolerant attitude

Examples

The judge showed leniency by reducing the prison sentence.
Parents sometimes regret their leniency when their children take advantage of it.
The teacher's leniency allowed several students to submit their assignments late.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: harshness - the quality of being severe or strict
  • Synonym: clemency - mercy or lenience shown to an offender

Etymology

From Latin 'lenientia' (gentleness, mildness).
