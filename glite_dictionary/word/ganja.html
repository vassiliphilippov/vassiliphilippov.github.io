ganja

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑndʒə / (AmE), / ˈɡændʒə / (BrE)
marijuana; the dried leaves and flowers of the cannabis plant, especially when used as a drug
marijuana; cannabis used as a drug
informal

Examples

The police arrested him for possessing ganja at the concert.
Many reggae songs reference the use of ganja as a spiritual and cultural practice.
He claimed that smoking ganja helped him to relax after a stressful day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marijuana - dried leaves and flowers of cannabis used as a drug
  • Synonym: cannabis - plant from which marijuana and similar drugs are obtained

Etymology

From Hindi गांजा (gānjā), meaning 'hemp, cannabis', of Sanskrit origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑn.dʒə / (AmE), / ˈɡæn.dʒə / (BrE)
marijuana, especially as smoked or used for its intoxicating or medicinal effects
marijuana; cannabis plant for smoking
informal

Examples

He rolled up some ganja and shared it with his friends around the fire.
Many people use ganja to help relieve chronic pain or stress.
The reggae musician sang about freedom and the spiritual use of ganja.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marijuana - the dried leaves of the cannabis plant used as a drug
  • Synonym: cannabis - the plant from which marijuana is produced

Etymology

From Hindi गांजा (gāñjā), from Sanskrit गञ्जा (gañjā, hemp).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-12417727-n
Synset POS: n
