Perognathus

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛrəɡˈneɪθəs / (AmE), / ˌpɛrəɡˈneɪθəs / (BrE)
A genus of small North American rodents known as pocket mice, characterized by external cheek pouches and silky fur.
genus of small North American pocket mice

Examples

The silky pocket mouse belongs to the genus Perognathus and is known for its soft fur.
Scientists captured several Perognathus specimens during their field study in the Arizona desert.
Perognathus species are adapted to arid environments and often feed on seeds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pocket mouse - Any small rodent belonging to the family Heteromyidae, including the genus Perognathus

Etymology

From Greek 'pērēn' (pouch) and 'gnathos' (jaw), referring to their cheek pouches.

2. NOUN

/ pɛrˈɑɡnəθəs / (AmE), / pɛˈrɒɡnəθəs / (BrE)
A genus of small rodents known as pocket mice, primarily found in western North America, including species such as the white-eared pocket mouse and silky pocket mouse.
genus of pocket mice rodents

Examples

The genus Perognathus includes several species of pocket mice found in arid regions of North America.
Researchers studying the habitat of Perognathus have discovered new insights into their burrowing behavior.
Some species within Perognathus are considered threatened due to habitat loss.

Etymology

From Greek 'pera' (pouch) + 'gnathos' (jaw), referring to the cheek pouches.
