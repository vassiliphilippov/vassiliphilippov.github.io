baster

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪstər / (AmE), / ˈbeɪstə / (BrE)
a person, especially a cook, who bastes roasting meat with melted fat or gravy
person who bastes meat while roasting

Examples

The baster carefully drizzled pan juices over the turkey every thirty minutes.
As a professional baster, she ensured the roast stayed moist and flavorful.
He was praised as the finest baster in the restaurant for his attention to detail.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baste - to pour juices or melted fat over food while cooking

Etymology

From base (verb: to pour liquid over meat while cooking) + -er (agent noun suffix).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪstər / (AmE), / ˈbeɪstə / (BrE)
A kitchen utensil consisting of a tube with a rubber bulb, used for drawing up and releasing melted fat or liquid to moisten meat during roasting.
tube with bulb for moistening meat

Examples

She used a baster to keep the turkey moist during roasting.
The baster was filled with melted butter and gently squeezed over the chicken.
After every 30 minutes, he grabbed the baster and basted the meat with its own juices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turkey baster - a baster specifically used for basting turkey or large poultry

Etymology

From base 'baste' (cooking term) + -er (agent noun suffix).
