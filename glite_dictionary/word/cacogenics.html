cacogenics

1. NOUN

/ ˌkækəˈdʒɛnɪks / (AmE), / ˌkækəˈdʒɛnɪks / (BrE)
the study of factors causing degeneration or decline in offspring or populations
study of factors causing degeneration
medical

Examples

The field of cacogenics examines how certain environmental factors can negatively affect heredity.
Some scientists in the early 20th century controversially advocated for cacogenics policies, which are now widely discredited.
Research into cacogenics often intersects with studies on genetics and population health.

Etymology

From Greek kakos (bad) + -genics (from genes, meaning produced by).
