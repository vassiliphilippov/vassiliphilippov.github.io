Belonidae

1. NOUN

/ bɛˈloʊnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / bɪˈlɒnɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of elongated, predatory fishes found in warm regions, known for their long jaws and slender bodies, resembling but not related to freshwater gars.
family of long-jawed predatory fishes

Examples

Species within the Belonidae family are often known as needlefish due to their shape.
The Belonidae are commonly found near the surface of tropical and subtropical seas.
Scientists studied the jaw structure of Belonidae to better understand their hunting techniques.

Etymology

From New Latin 'Belonidae', based on Greek 'belone' (needle), referring to the slender body shape.

2. NOUN

/ bəˈloʊnɪdiː / (AmE), / bɛˈlɒnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of ferocious, elongated fishes found in warm regions, resembling freshwater gars but not closely related to them.
family of elongated warm-region fishes

Examples

The Belonidae are commonly known as needlefishes because of their long, slender jaws.
Most species in the Belonidae family inhabit coastal marine waters in tropical and subtropical regions.
Unlike gars, Belonidae do not have ganoid scales but share a similar elongated shape.

Etymology

From New Latin Belonidae, based on the type genus Belone, from Greek belonē (needle).
