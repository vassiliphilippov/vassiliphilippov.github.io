Brachystegia speciformis

1. NOUN

/ ˌbrækiˈstiːdʒiə ˌspɛsɪˈfɔːrmɪs / (AmE), / ˌbrækɪˈstiːdʒɪə ˌspesɪˈfɔːmɪs / (BrE)
A small shrubby African tree with compound leaves and racemes of small fragrant green flowers, commonly found in savanna woodlands.
species of shrubby African tree

Examples

Conservationists are studying Brachystegia speciformis to learn more about its role in African ecosystems.
The leaves of Brachystegia speciformis are used locally for medicinal purposes.
During the rainy season, Brachystegia speciformis produces a profusion of fragrant green flowers.

Etymology

From genus name Brachystegia (Greek brakhys ‘short’ + stegos ‘roof’) and species name speciformis (Latin ‘having the form of’).
