polyarteritis

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑliˌɑrˈtɪrɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˌɑːˈtɪərɪtɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of several arteries in the body, typically as part of an underlying disease
inflammation of several arteries
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with polyarteritis after showing symptoms of systemic arterial inflammation.
Polyarteritis can lead to complications if the affected arteries supply vital organs.
Several blood tests confirmed the presence of polyarteritis in the immune-compromised individual.

Etymology

From Greek 'poly-' (many) + 'arteria' (artery) + '-itis' (inflammation).
