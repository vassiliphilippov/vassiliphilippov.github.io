Esox masquinongy

1. NOUN

/ ˌiːsɑks mæˈskwɪnənˌɡaɪ / (AmE), / ˌiːsɒks masˈkwɪnəndʒi / (BrE)
A large predatory freshwater fish of North America, also known as the muskellunge, prized as a sport fish.
large North American sport fish (muskellunge)

Examples

Anglers often travel long distances to catch the elusive Esox masquinongy in northern lakes.
The state record for Esox masquinongy weighed over sixty pounds.
Biologists studied the habitat of Esox masquinongy to improve conservation efforts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muskellunge - the common name for Esox masquinongy, a large freshwater sport fish
  • Degree Lower: pike - a related predatory freshwater fish

Etymology

From New Latin, combining genus name Esox (pike) and an Algonquian word, likely Ojibwe 'maashkinoozhe'.
