griffon

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡrɪf.ən / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪf.ən / (BrE)
A large vulture found in southern Europe and northern Africa, noted for its pale plumage and black wings.
large vulture of southern Europe and northern Africa

Examples

The griffon soared over the rocky cliffs, scanning for prey.
Wildlife photographers traveled to Spain to capture images of the rare griffon.
Conservationists are working to protect the griffon from habitat loss in North Africa.

Etymology

From French 'griffon', ultimately from Latin 'gryphus' (griffin).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡrɪf.ən / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪf.ɒn / (BrE)
a breed of very small, compact, wiry-coated dogs of Belgian origin, distinguished by a short bearded muzzle
small Belgian breed of wiry-coated dog

Examples

The Brussels griffon is known for its affectionate and lively temperament.
At the dog show, several griffons competed in the toy group category.
Her family adopted a small griffon from a local rescue shelter.

Etymology

French origin, from griffon (a breed of dog), related to Medieval Latin gryphus (griffin, a mythical creature).

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡrɪf.ɑn / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪf.ɒn / (BrE)
a breed of medium-sized dog with a long head, a downy undercoat, and a harsh, wiry outer coat, originally from Holland and developed in France
a breed of wiry-coated dog

Examples

The griffon needs regular grooming to keep its wiry coat in good condition.
We visited a breeder who specialized in the Belgian griffon dogs.
A griffon makes a lively and affectionate companion for active families.

Etymology

From French 'griffon', meaning 'wire-haired dog', from Old French 'griffon' (griffin), likening the dog's appearance to the legendary beast.

4. NOUN

/ ˈɡrɪf.ən / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪf.ən / (BrE)
a mythical creature with the head and wings of an eagle and the body of a lion
mythical eagle-lion creature

Examples

The ancient legend told of a griffon guarding a vast treasure in the mountains.
In medieval art, a griffon often symbolized strength and vigilance.
The fantasy novel features a heroic knight and his loyal griffon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gryphon - alternative spelling of griffon, mythical eagle-lion creature
  • Synonym: chimera - mythical creature with parts of various animals

Etymology

From Old French *griffon*, from Latin *gryphus*, from Greek *gruphōn* (curved, hooked), referring to the eagle-like beak.
