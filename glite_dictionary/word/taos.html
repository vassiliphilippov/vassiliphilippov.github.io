Taos

1. PROPN

/ ˈtaʊs / (AmE), / ˈtaʊs / (BrE)
A town in northern New Mexico, known for its vibrant artist colony and rich Native American and Spanish heritage.
artist colony and town in New Mexico

Examples

Every summer, painters and sculptors gather in Taos to find inspiration in its stunning landscapes.
The unique light and colors of Taos have attracted generations of artists and writers.
She visited Taos for an art retreat and was fascinated by its creative community.

Etymology

From Taos, a Native American pueblo in the area, itself from the Taos language meaning 'place of red willows'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtaʊs / (AmE), / ˈtaʊs / (BrE)
a member of the Pueblo people native to northern New Mexico, especially one from Taos Pueblo.
member of Pueblo people of northern New Mexico

Examples

The Taos maintain many traditional ceremonies and crafts.
A respected elder among the Taos shared stories of their ancestors.
Visitors to New Mexico often seek to learn more about the history of the Taos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Pueblo - member of any of various American Indian peoples of the southwestern U.S.

Etymology

From the name of the Taos Pueblo in New Mexico, ultimately from the Taos people's own name for their village, 'Táo' meaning 'place of red willows.'
