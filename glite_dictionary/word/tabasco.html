Tabasco

1. PROPN

/ təˈbæsk.oʊ / (AmE), / təˈbæs.kəʊ / (BrE)
A state in southeastern Mexico, located on the Gulf of Campeche.
Mexican state on Gulf of Campeche

Examples

She traveled across Tabasco to explore its diverse wildlife and rainforests.
The city of Villahermosa is the capital of Tabasco and a major cultural center.
Flooding is a recurring problem in Tabasco due to its tropical climate.

2. NOUN

/ təˈbæskoʊ / (AmE), / təˈbæs.kəʊ / (BrE)
A very spicy, tangy sauce made from fully-aged red peppers, known by the trademarked name Tabasco.
spicy pepper sauce (brand name)

Examples

She added a few drops of Tabasco to her soup to give it some heat.
Many people enjoy scrambled eggs with a dash of Tabasco on top.
He reached for the Tabasco bottle to spice up his pizza.

Etymology

Named after the Mexican state of Tabasco, where similar peppers are grown.
