Middle Low German

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪdəl loʊ ˈdʒɜrmən / (AmE), / ˌmɪdəl ləʊ ˈdʒɜːmən / (BrE)
the form of the Low German language spoken from around 1100 to 1500, used especially in northern Germany and parts of the Hanseatic League
Low German from 1100–1500

Examples

The Middle Low German language played a crucial role in the trade of the Hanseatic League.
Many manuscripts from the 14th century are written in Middle Low German.
Linguists study the vocabulary of Middle Low German to trace the evolution of modern northern German dialects.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Low German - a West Germanic language spoken mainly in northern Germany and the eastern Netherlands
  • Degree Higher: Modern Low German - the form of Low German used after 1500

Etymology

Formed from 'Middle' + 'Low German', referring to the historical stage of the language.
