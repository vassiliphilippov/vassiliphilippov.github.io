tomahawk

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɑː.məˌhɑk / (AmE), / ˈtɒ.məˌhɔːk / (BrE)
a light axe originally used as a tool or weapon by Indigenous peoples of North America.
light axe of Indigenous North Americans

Examples

The warrior carried a tomahawk as both a weapon and a tool.
She saw a tomahawk displayed in the museum’s Native American exhibit.
Traders would sometimes exchange goods for a finely crafted tomahawk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hatchet - a small, short-handled axe

Etymology

From Powhatan (an Algonquian language) *tamahaac* (tool for cutting).

2. VERB

/ ˈtɑːməˌhɑk / (AmE), / ˈtɒməhɔːk / (BrE)
to kill or strike with a tomahawk
kill or strike with a tomahawk

Examples

The warriors planned to tomahawk their enemies during the raid.
In the story, the explorer was nearly tomahawked by hostile natives.
He claimed to have tomahawked the tree trunk out of frustration.

Etymology

From Algonquian (Powhatan) *tamahaac* (axe). The verb is formed from the noun.

3. VERB

/ ˈtɑːməˌhɔk / (AmE), / ˈtɒməˌhɔːk / (BrE)
to strike or cut with a tomahawk (a type of light ax)
strike or cut with a tomahawk

Examples

The warrior tomahawked the post to demonstrate the weapon’s sharpness.
They tomahawked through the dense undergrowth, clearing a path for the group.
In the story, she tomahawks the rope to free herself from captivity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: axe - cut with an axe
  • Synonym: to cleave - split or sever (something)

Etymology

From Powhatan (Virginian Algonquian) *tamahaac*, meaning 'axe'.
