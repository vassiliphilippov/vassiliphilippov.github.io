trophotropic

1. ADJ

/ ˌtroʊfəˈtroʊpɪk / (AmE), / ˌtrɒfəˈtrəʊpɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characterized by trophotropism, especially in reference to growth responses or the parasympathetic nervous system.
relating to trophotropism; physiological growth response
medical

Examples

The trophotropic state is associated with relaxation and reduced metabolic activity.
A trophotropic stimulus can cause a plant organ to bend towards a nutrient source.
Certain relaxation techniques are said to promote the trophotropic response in the human body.

Etymology

From Greek 'trophē' (nourishment) and 'tropos' (turn, direction), via scientific Latin.
