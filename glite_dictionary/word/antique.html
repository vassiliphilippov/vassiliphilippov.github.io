antique

1. ADJ

/ ænˈtik / (AmE), / ænˈtiːk / (BrE)
(of an object) made in an earlier period and valued for its age and quality.
old and valuable due to age

Examples

The museum displayed several antique clocks from the 18th century.
She collected antique jewelry that once belonged to her grandmother.
They restored their farmhouse with antique wooden beams and fixtures.

Synset Examples

the beautiful antique French furniture

Related Terms

  • Antonym: modern - relating to the present or recent times

Etymology

From Latin 'antiquus' (ancient, old).

3. NOUN

/ ænˈtik / (AmE), / ænˈtiːk / (BrE)
an object, especially a piece of furniture or decorative item, made in a previous period and valued for its age, beauty, or rarity
object from former times of value or rarity

Examples

Her grandmother's house was filled with antique chairs and delicate china.
The antique vase sold at auction for thousands of dollars.
He loves searching for antique clocks at local flea markets.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: collectible - an object valued and sought by collectors
  • Antonym: modern - made in recent times; not old or antique

Etymology

From Latin antiquus (ancient, old).
