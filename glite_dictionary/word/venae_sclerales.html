venae sclerales

1. NOUN

/ ˈviːni sklɪˈræliz / (AmE), / ˈviːni sklɪˈraːliːz / (BrE)
The small veins that drain the sclera of the eye and serve as tributaries to the anterior ciliary veins.
veins draining the sclera of the eye
medical

Examples

The venae sclerales play a role in the venous drainage of the eyeball.
An obstruction in the venae sclerales can contribute to increased intraocular pressure.
Ophthalmic surgeons must be aware of the location of the venae sclerales during procedures near the eye's surface.

Etymology

From Latin: 'venae' (veins) and 'sclerales' (of the sclera).
