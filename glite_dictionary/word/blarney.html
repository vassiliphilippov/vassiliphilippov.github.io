blarney

1. NOUN

/ ˈblɑːrni / (AmE), / ˈblɑːni / (BrE)
talk that flatters or deceives in order to gain favor or advantage; smooth, persuasive flattery.
flattering or persuasive talk

Examples

She tried to win the job with a bit of blarney during the interview.
Don't let his charming blarney fool you into trusting him too quickly.
The politician was full of blarney, making promises he couldn't keep.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flattery - excessive or insincere praise, especially to further one's own interests

Etymology

From Blarney, a village in Ireland, reputed for the Blarney Stone, kissing which is said to give the gift of eloquence.

2. VERB

/ ˈblɑrni / (AmE), / ˈblɑːni / (BrE)
To influence or persuade someone by gentle urging, flattering talk, or cajoling.
persuade by flattery or charm

Examples

He tried to blarney his way out of trouble with a charming smile.
She blarneyed him into lending her the car for the weekend.
It's no use blarneying; I can see right through your compliments.

Synset Examples

He palavered her into going along

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cajole - to persuade with flattery or gentle urging
  • Synonym: coax - to gently and persistently persuade someone
  • Antonym: bully - to force someone to do something by intimidating them

Etymology

From Irish 'Blarney', a village in Ireland notorious for the legend of the Blarney Stone, from which the word's meaning derives.
