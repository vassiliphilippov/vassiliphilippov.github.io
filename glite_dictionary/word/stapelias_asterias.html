Stapelias asterias

1. NOUN

/ stəˈpiːliəs æˈstɪəriəs / (AmE), / stəˈpiːlɪəs æˈstɪərɪəs / (BrE)
A species of stapelia native to the Cape Province, known for its mostly dark red-brown flowers with flat, star-shaped corollas.
a species of stapelia with star-shaped flowers

Examples

The distinctive flowers of Stapelias asterias make it a unique addition to succulent collections.
In its native habitat, Stapelias asterias attracts insects with its unusual scent.
Stapelias asterias thrives in well-drained, sandy soil and warm climates.

Etymology

From genus name Stapelia, honoring botanist Johannes van Stapel, and species name 'asterias', Latin for 'star-like'.
