racism

1. NOUN

/ ˈreɪsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈreɪsɪzəm / (BrE)
The belief that certain races are inherently superior to others, often resulting in discrimination and prejudice against people based on their race.
prejudice or discrimination based on race

Examples

The organization is committed to fighting racism in all its forms.
Many schools are working to educate students about the dangers of racism.
She spoke out against racism at the international conference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prejudice - preconceived opinion not based on reason or experience
  • Synonym: discrimination - unjust treatment of different categories of people, especially on grounds such as race
  • Antonym: tolerance - the ability or willingness to tolerate the existence of opinions or behavior that one dislikes or disagrees with

Etymology

Formed from 'race' + '-ism', influenced by French 'racisme'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈreɪsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈreɪsɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief that some races are inherently superior to others, leading to discrimination or prejudice against people based on their race
belief in racial superiority; racial discrimination

Examples

Instances of racism in the workplace can have serious consequences for employees and employers alike.
The government has implemented new policies to combat racism in schools.
Open discussions about racism can help promote understanding and inclusivity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prejudice - preconceived opinion not based on reason or experience
  • Synonym: discrimination - unjust treatment of different categories of people, especially on grounds of race, age, or sex
  • Antonym: tolerance - acceptance of differences, especially among people of different races or backgrounds

Etymology

From race + -ism, formed in English in the early 20th century.
