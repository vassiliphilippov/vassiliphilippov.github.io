to supplicate

1. VERB

/ ˈsʌplɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsʌplɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to ask for something humbly or earnestly, especially in prayer
ask humbly or in prayer

Examples

They knelt to supplicate the favor of the gods before the ceremony began.
Desperate for help, she supplicated the authorities for mercy.
In times of great need, many choose to supplicate with sincere prayers.

Synset Examples

supplicate God's blessing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to beg - to ask someone earnestly or humbly for something
  • Synonym: to implore - to beg someone earnestly or desperately to do something
  • Antonym: demand - to ask forcefully or insistently for something

Etymology

From Latin supplicare (to beg, pray humbly), from supplex (humble, beseeching).

2. VERB

/ ˈsʌplɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsʌplɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to ask or beg for something in a humble and earnest way, especially from someone in authority or from God
to beg or pray humbly

Examples

The refugees supplicated for safe passage across the border.
He knelt to supplicate the king for mercy.
She would supplicate the gods for a good harvest every spring.

Synset Examples

supplicate for permission

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to beg - to ask earnestly or humbly for something
  • Synonym: to plead - to make an emotional appeal or request
  • Antonym: demand - to ask for something forcefully or authoritatively

Etymology

From Latin supplicare (to kneel, entreat), from sub- (under) + plicare (to fold).

3. VERB

/ ˈsʌplɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsʌplɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to ask for something earnestly and humbly, especially in prayer or a formal plea
ask humbly or earnestly for something

Examples

He fell to his knees and supplicated the judge for mercy.
The villagers supplicated the gods for rain during the long drought.
She supplicates daily for the safety of her family.

Synset Examples

He supplicated the King for clemency

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to beg - to ask someone earnestly or humbly for something
  • Synonym: to implore - to beg someone earnestly or desperately to do something

Etymology

From Latin supplicare (to kneel, pray), from sub- (under) + plicare (to fold).
