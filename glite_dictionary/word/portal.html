portal

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔrtəl / (AmE), / ˈpɔːtəl / (BrE)
a short vein that carries blood from parts of the digestive system to the liver, especially the hepatic portal vein
a vein carrying blood to the liver
medical

Examples

The portal carries nutrient-rich blood from the intestines to the liver for processing.
Any blockage in the portal can lead to serious liver complications.
Doctors measured the pressure in the portal to assess the patient's liver function.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hepatic portal vein - the main vein carrying blood from the digestive organs to the liver

Etymology

From Latin 'porta' (gate), referring to the vein entering the liver as a gateway.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɔrtəl / (AmE), / ˈpɔːtəl / (BrE)
(computing) A website that serves as an entry point to other websites, typically offering a range of services such as search engines, email, and news.
website acting as an entry point to others

Examples

The company's portal offers users access to news, weather, email, and a variety of online tools.
Many people set their browser homepage to a portal for convenience.
Yahoo! was one of the first major portals on the internet.

Synset Examples

a portal typically has search engines and free email and chat rooms etc.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: website - a set of related web pages under a single domain name

3. NOUN

/ ˈpɔrt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈpɔːtəl / (BrE)
a grand or imposing entrance, often used metaphorically for an entry point to a place or new experience
a grand entrance or gateway

Examples

The ornate portal of the castle was carved with intricate designs.
Many believe that education may serve as a portal to opportunity and growth.
As we walked through the ancient portal, we felt transported into another era.

Synset Examples

the portals of the cathedral
the portals of heaven
the portals of success

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gateway - an entrance or means of access, often metaphorical
  • Synonym: doorway - an entrance through a door; a passage into a place

Etymology

From Old French 'portal', from Medieval Latin 'portale' (gateway), from Latin 'porta' (gate).
