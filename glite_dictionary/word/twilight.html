twilight

1. NOUN

/ ˈtwaɪˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈtwaɪlaɪt / (BrE)
A period or state of decline following a time of success, especially toward the end of an era, life, or institution.
period of decline after success

Examples

Many believe the company is now in its twilight after years of dominating the industry.
The artist spent his twilight years mentoring young students rather than exhibiting new work.
Historians study the twilight of ancient Rome to understand how great empires fall.

Synset Examples

in the twilight of the empire

Related Terms

  • Antonym: heyday - the period of greatest success, popularity, or power

Etymology

From Old English 'twēonung' (doubt, ambiguity) and 'light'; originally referring to the dim light after sunset.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtwaɪˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈtwaɪ.laɪt / (BrE)
The soft diffused light in the sky from the sun just after it has set, before it becomes completely dark.
the time just after sunset

Examples

We sat outside watching the colors of twilight fade into night.
Birds became active again in the cool twilight after a hot day.
The garden appeared mysterious and peaceful in the soft twilight.

Synset Examples

he loved the twilight
they finished before the fall of night

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dusk - the darker stage of twilight in the evening
  • Antonym: dawn - the time just before sunrise

Etymology

From Middle English 'twiligt', from Old English 'twēon-lic' (having a double nature, ambiguous) and 'lēoht' (light).

3. NOUN

/ ˈtwaɪˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈtwaɪ.laɪt / (BrE)
the soft, diffused light visible in the sky just before sunrise or after sunset, when the sun is below the horizon but its rays are scattered by the atmosphere
faint light before sunrise or after sunset

Examples

We walked along the beach in the gentle twilight after the sun had set.
Bats begin to appear at twilight, swooping across the fading sky.
The city was bathed in a soft twilight that made everything look calm and mysterious.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: dawn - the first appearance of light before sunrise
  • Degree Higher: sunset - the time in the evening when the sun disappears below the horizon
  • Degree Higher: dusk - the period of time just after sunset

Etymology

From Middle English 'twilit', from Old English 'twī-' (double) + 'lēoht' (light).
