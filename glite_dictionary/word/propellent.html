propellent

1. ADJ

/ prəˈpɛl.ənt / (AmE), / prəˈpɛl.ənt / (BrE)
Tending to or capable of causing movement or propulsion.
causing movement or propulsion

Examples

The spacecraft uses a propellent force to escape Earth's gravity.
His ideas had a propellent impact on the technology industry.
A propellent breeze pushed the sailboat swiftly across the bay.

Synset Examples

propellant fuel for submarines
the faster a jet plane goes the greater its propulsive efficiency
universities … the seats of propulsive thought

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propulsive - Tending to propel; having the power to move forward.
  • Antonym: stationary - Not moving; having no motion.

Etymology

From Latin 'propellere', meaning 'to drive forward'.

2. NOUN

/ prəˈpɛlənt / (AmE), / prəˈpɛl.ənt / (BrE)
a substance that propels or provides thrust, especially as used in rockets or engines
substance that causes propulsion

Examples

Solid propellent is commonly used in model rockets for its reliability.
The researchers developed a new type of propellent for the spacecraft's thrusters.
Refueling the satellite required careful handling of the liquid propellent.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propellant - a substance used for propelling, especially fuel for rockets or engines

Etymology

From Latin 'propellere' meaning 'to drive forward', via the verb 'propel'.
