to encapsulate

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈkæpsjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈkapsjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to express something in a concise or summarized form; to condense or reduce in length while retaining essential meaning
summarize or condense something

Examples

The professor tried to encapsulate the complex theory in just a few sentences.
Her report perfectly encapsulates the main issues facing the project.
Can you encapsulate your argument in one paragraph for the summary?

Synset Examples

capsulize the news

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to summarize - to give a brief statement of the main points
  • Antonym: to expand - to express in a fuller, more detailed form

Etymology

From Latin capsula (small box), from capsa (box, case), referring to enclosing or containing.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00245302-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɪnˈkæpsjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈkæpsjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to enclose something in a capsule or other small container
enclose in a capsule

Examples

Many medicines are encapsulated to make them easier to swallow.
Scientists encapsulate the cells in protective coatings for storage.
The seeds are encapsulated within a hard shell.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enclose - to surround or close off on all sides

Etymology

From Latin capsula (small box) via modern scientific usage.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01590905-v
Synset POS: v
