peag

1. NOUN

/ piːɡ / (AmE), / piːɡ / (BrE)
small cylindrical beads made from polished shells, historically used by certain Native American peoples as jewelry or currency
shell beads used as jewelry or currency

Examples

Archaeologists discovered peag among the remains of the ancient settlement.
The colonists sometimes traded goods with Native Americans using peag as currency.
She wore a necklace made from strings of peag, passed down from her ancestors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wampum - traditional shell beads used as currency by Native Americans

Etymology

From Narragansett or Massachusett (Algonquian languages) word for 'bead.'
