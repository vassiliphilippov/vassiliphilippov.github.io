sloppiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈslɑːpinəs / (AmE), / ˈslɒpinɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being careless, untidy, or lacking order or attention to detail
lack of order or tidiness

Examples

His sloppiness at work eventually led to several mistakes in the reports.
The sloppiness of the room made it hard to find anything.
Teachers often complain about the sloppiness of students' handwriting.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: neatness - the quality of being tidy and orderly

Etymology

From 'sloppy' + '-ness', where 'sloppy' derives from 'slop' (to spill or splash).

2. NOUN

/ ˈslɑːpi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈslɒp.i.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of not being careful, neat, or thorough; carelessness or lack of precision
lack of care or precision

Examples

The sloppiness of his handwriting made it hard to read the exam answers.
She was criticized for the sloppiness in her research methods.
There is no room for sloppiness when it comes to surgery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carelessness - lack of attention or thought to avoiding harm or errors
  • Antonym: neatness - the quality of being tidy and orderly

Etymology

From "sloppy" + "-ness", where "sloppy" may come from "slop" (mud, dirty water).

Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈslɑːpi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈslɒp.i.nəs / (BrE)
excessive or insincere emotion, especially in a sentimental or maudlin way
excessive sentimental emotion

Examples

I found the movie's sloppiness distracting rather than moving.
She couldn't bear the sloppiness of the overly sentimental speech.
Critics accused the book of descending into emotional sloppiness near the end.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maudlin - excessively sentimental, especially in a weak or foolish way
  • Synonym: sentimentality - excessive tenderness, sadness, or nostalgia
  • Antonym: restraint - a measure or condition that keeps someone’s emotions under control

Etymology

From 'sloppy' (overly sentimental) + -ness.

4. NOUN

/ ˈslɑpiˌnəs / (AmE), / ˈslɒpɪnɪs / (BrE)
the state of ground being wet, muddy, or soaked with water
wet, muddy ground

Examples

After the heavy rain, the sloppiness of the football pitch made playing difficult.
The hikers complained about the sloppiness along the trail after last night's downpour.
We canceled our picnic because of the sloppiness in the park following the storm.

Synset Examples

the baseball game was canceled because of the wateriness of the outfield

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wetness - the state of being wet
  • Antonym: dryness - the state of being dry

Etymology

From 'sloppy' + '-ness', where 'sloppy' derives from 'slop', possibly of Middle Low German origin meaning 'mud'.
