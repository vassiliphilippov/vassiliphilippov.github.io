ore

1. NOUN

/ ɔːr / (AmE), / ɔː / (BrE)
rock or mineral from which metal can be extracted profitably
mineral/rock containing valuable metal

Examples

The miners extracted copper ore from deep underground.
Iron ore is a key raw material in steel production.
Geologists discovered a new vein of gold ore in the region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mineral - a naturally occurring substance, usually inorganic, with a definite chemical composition

Etymology

From Old English *ōra*, of Germanic origin, related to Dutch *oer* and German *Erz* (ore).

2. NOUN

/ ɔːr / (AmE), / ɔːr / (BrE)
a monetary subunit of currency in Denmark, Norway, and Sweden, equal to one hundredth of a krona or krone.
monetary subunit in Scandinavia (1/100 of krona/krone)

Examples

She found an old 50 ore coin while cleaning out her desk drawer.
The price increased by just a few ore per liter last week.
In Sweden, the ore is no longer used as physical currency but still exists electronically.

Etymology

From Swedish and Danish *øre*, and Norwegian *øre*, from Latin *aureus* (gold coin).
