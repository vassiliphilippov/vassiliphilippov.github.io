vacillating

1. ADJ

/ ˈvæsəˌleɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈvæsɪleɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
having or showing uncertainty in purpose or action; indecisive or wavering.
indecisive; wavering

Examples

Her vacillating attitude made it hard for the team to move forward.
He gave a vacillating response when asked about his decision.
Voters grew tired of the candidate’s vacillating stance on important issues.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: decisive - showing the ability to make decisions quickly and firmly
  • Synonym: wavering - being undecided between two opinions or courses of action

Etymology

From Latin vacillare (to sway to and fro, waver).
