Bradypus tridactylus

1. NOUN

/ brəˈdaɪpəs traɪˈdæktɪləs / (AmE), / brəˈdaɪpəs traɪˈdæk.tɪ.ləs / (BrE)
A species of three-toed sloth found in South America, distinguished by having three long claws on each forefoot and hindfoot.
a species of three-toed sloth

Examples

The Bradypus tridactylus moves so slowly that algae can grow on its fur.
Researchers observed a Bradypus tridactylus hanging upside down from the rainforest canopy.
The diet of Bradypus tridactylus mainly consists of leaves and fruits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: three-toed sloth - a common name for Bradypus tridactylus and related species
  • Degree Higher: sloth - a slow-moving arboreal mammal found in Central and South America

Etymology

From Greek 'bradys' (slow) and 'pous' (foot), combined with Latin 'tridactylus' (three-fingered).
