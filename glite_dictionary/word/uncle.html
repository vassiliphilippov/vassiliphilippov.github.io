uncle

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌŋ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈʌŋ.kəl / (BrE)
The brother of one's father or mother, or the husband of one's aunt.
male relative; parent's brother or aunt's husband

Examples

My uncle lives in Canada and visits us every summer.
She called her uncle for advice when she started her new job.
At the family reunion, we met all our uncles and aunts.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: aunt - the sister of your father or mother or the wife of your uncle
  • Antonym: nephew - a son of your brother or sister
  • Antonym: niece - a daughter of your brother or sister

Etymology

From Old French *oncle*, from Latin *avunculus* (maternal uncle).

2. NOUN

/ ˈʌŋkəl / (AmE), / ˈʌŋkl̩ / (BrE)
a person who offers support, guidance, or encouragement, especially in a friendly or informal role, not limited to family ties
supportive or advisory person (not just a relative)

Examples

He became a trusted uncle to many young artists trying to find their way.
During her first year abroad, she considered her mentor an uncle figure who always had time to listen.
Though not related by blood, Mr. Briggs played uncle to countless children in the neighborhood.

Synset Examples
