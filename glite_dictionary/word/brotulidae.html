Brotulidae

1. NOUN

/ broʊˈtjuːlɪˌdiː / (AmE), / brəʊˈtjuːlɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of chiefly deep-sea fishes related to the Ophidiidae, characterized by elongated bodies and found in various oceanic regions.
family of deep-sea fishes

Examples

Scientists discovered several new species within the Brotulidae family during the deep-sea expedition.
Many members of the Brotulidae are adapted to live at depths where few other fish can survive.
The study compared the skeletal structures of Brotulidae and Ophidiidae families.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ophidiidae - family of fishes closely related to Brotulidae (cusk-eels)

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Brotula and the family suffix -idae.

2. NOUN

/ broʊˈtuːlɪˌdiː / (AmE), / brəʊˈtjuːlɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of chiefly deep-sea fishes related to the cusk-eels (Ophidiidae), known for their elongated bodies and habitats in marine environments
a family of deep-sea fishes
medical

Examples

The Brotulidae are found primarily in deep-sea habitats worldwide.
Scientists often compare the anatomy of Brotulidae to that of their relatives, the Ophidiidae.
New species within the Brotulidae family are still being discovered by marine biologists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ophidiidae - the family of cusk-eels, related group of marine fishes

Etymology

From New Latin, from Brotula (a genus in the group) + -idae (zoological family suffix).
