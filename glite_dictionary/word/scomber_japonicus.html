Scomber japonicus

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɑmbər dʒəˈpoʊnɪkəs / (AmE), / ˈskɒmbə dʒəˈpɒnɪkəs / (BrE)
a species of small mackerel found in temperate and subtropical waters worldwide, especially in the Pacific Ocean
a species of small mackerel

Examples

The Scomber japonicus is a commercially important fish in many parts of the world.
Scientists study the migration patterns of Scomber japonicus to understand ocean ecosystems.
Fishermen often catch Scomber japonicus off the coast of California.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mackerel - a common name for fish of the Scombridae family

Etymology

From Latin; 'Scomber' meaning mackerel, and 'japonicus' indicating Japanese origin.
