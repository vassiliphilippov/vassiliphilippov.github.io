Comte de Mirabeau

1. PROPN

/ kɔnt də ˌmɪrəˈboʊ / (AmE), / kɒ̃t də ˌmɪrəˈbəʊ / (BrE)
(1749–1791) A French revolutionary and orator who played a prominent role in the early days of the French Revolution.
French revolutionary and orator (1749–1791)

Examples

Comte de Mirabeau was a leading voice for reform during the first phases of the French Revolution.
The eloquence of Comte de Mirabeau inspired many radical changes in eighteenth-century France.
Historians often debate the political legacy of Comte de Mirabeau.

Etymology

From French, literally 'Count of Mirabeau', a title held by Honoré Gabriel Riqueti.
