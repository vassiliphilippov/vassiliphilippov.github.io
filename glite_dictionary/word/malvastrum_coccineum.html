Malvastrum coccineum

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæl.vəˈstrʌm kɒkˈsɪn.i.əm / (AmE), / ˌmal.væˈstrʌm kɒkˈsɪn.i.əm / (BrE)
A species of false mallow native to the western United States, with racemes of red flowers; sometimes classified within the genus Malvastrum.
a false mallow plant with red racemose flowers

Examples

The arid gardens in New Mexico often feature the vibrant Malvastrum coccineum throughout the summer.
Botanists debated whether Malvastrum coccineum should be placed in a different genus.
Hikers in Colorado can spot Malvastrum coccineum blooming along dry, rocky paths.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: false mallow - a common name for Malvastrum coccineum and related plants

Etymology

From Latin; 'Malvastrum' means 'little mallow', and 'coccineum' means 'scarlet'.
