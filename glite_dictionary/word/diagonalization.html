diagonalization

1. NOUN

/ daɪˌæɡənələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / daɪˌæɡənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of transforming a square matrix into a diagonal matrix, typically by finding a basis in which the matrix representation has nonzero elements only on the main diagonal
matrix transformation to diagonal form
medical

Examples

The diagonalization of a matrix simplifies the computation of its powers.
Many algorithms in linear algebra depend on the diagonalization of square matrices.
The professor explained how diagonalization can reveal a matrix's eigenvalues.

Synset Examples

the diagonalization of a normal matrix by a unitary transformation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: diagonal - a straight line joining two opposite corners of a square or rectangle

Etymology

From diagonal + -ization, denoting the process of making something diagonal.
