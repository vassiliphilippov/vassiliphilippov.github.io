clearing

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈklɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of removing suspended solid particles from a liquid, often to make it clear or pure
removing solids from a liquid

Examples

The clearing of water is an essential step in many municipal treatment plants.
After filtration, the clearing of the solution increased its transparency.
Centrifugation aids in the clearing of biological samples by removing particulate matter.

2. NOUN

/ ˈklɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈklɪə.rɪŋ / (BrE)
an open area of land with few or no trees, especially in the middle of a forest
open area in a forest

Examples

The deer emerged cautiously from the trees into the clearing.
We pitched our tent in a sunny clearing surrounded by tall pines.
A cabin stood alone in the center of a small clearing.

3. NOUN

/ ˈklɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈklɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of removing suspicion or doubt, especially regarding a person's guilt or integrity
removal of suspicion or doubt

Examples

The new evidence led to the clearing of her name after years of suspicion.
He finally achieved the clearing of all charges against him in court.
Her public clearing by the authorities restored her reputation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exoneration - the act of officially clearing someone from blame or fault
  • Antonym: accusation - a charge or claim that someone has done something wrong

Etymology

From Old English *clǣrian* (to make clear), ultimately from Latin *clarus* (clear).
