nervus coccygeus

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɜːrvəs kɒkˈsɪdʒiəs / (AmE), / ˈnɜːvəs kɒkˈsɪdʒiəs / (BrE)
the lowest pair of spinal nerves, located at the base of the spinal column
lowest pair of spinal nerves
medical

Examples

In some individuals, the nervus coccygeus may be absent or fused with adjacent nerves.
During the anatomy lecture, the professor highlighted the importance of the nervus coccygeus in the spinal cord.
Injury to the nervus coccygeus is rare but can result in coccygeal pain.

Etymology

Latin: 'nervus' (nerve) + 'coccygeus' (of the coccyx, tailbone).
