attestant

1. NOUN

/ əˈtɛstənt / (AmE), / əˈtɛstənt / (BrE)
a person who affirms or vouches for the accuracy, truth, or genuineness of something
someone who attests or vouches for something

Examples

The document required the signature of an attestant to verify its authenticity.
As an attestant, she confirmed the facts of the agreement before the notary.
Each attestant must be present during the signing of the will.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: witness - a person who sees an event and can attest to its truth
  • Antonym: denier - person who denies the truth of something

Etymology

From Latin attestari (to bear witness), via French attestant.

2. NOUN

/ əˈtɛstənt / (AmE), / əˈtɛstənt / (BrE)
(law) a person who affirms the authenticity of a document or signature by signing as a witness.
legal witness to a document's authenticity
legal

Examples

The document required at least one attestant to verify its authenticity.
Each attestant must add their signature beneath the principal signer.
As the official attestant, she confirmed the will had been properly executed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: witness - a person who sees an event or signs a document to confirm its authenticity
  • Synonym: signatory - a person who has signed a document

Etymology

From French 'attestant', from 'attester' (to attest), from Latin 'attestari'.
