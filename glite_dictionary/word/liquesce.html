to liquesce

1. VERB

/ lɪˈkwɛs / (AmE), / lɪˈkwɛs / (BrE)
(of a solid) to become liquid, especially by the application of heat; to melt or liquefy.
become liquid; melt

Examples

At room temperature, the wax will liquesce and can be poured into molds.
Snowflakes quickly liquesce on contact with the warm pavement.
Some metals liquesce at relatively low temperatures, making them useful for casting.

Synset Examples

The solid butter will liquesce when heated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to liquefy - to make or become liquid
  • Antonym: to solidify - to make or become solid

Etymology

From Latin liquescere, from liquēre (to be liquid).
