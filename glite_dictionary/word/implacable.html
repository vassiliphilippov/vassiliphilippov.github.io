implacable

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈplækəbəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈplækəbl / (BrE)
unable to be appeased or pacified; relentless in opposition, anger, or hostility.
unable to be appeased or pacified

Examples

She remained implacable in her demands, refusing to compromise with management.
The army faced an implacable enemy along the border.
Despite his apologies, her anger toward him was implacable.

Synset Examples

an implacable enemy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relentless - showing or promising no abatement of severity or intensity
  • Synonym: unforgiving - unwilling to forgive or relent toward offenses or mistakes
  • Antonym: merciful - showing mercy; willing to forgive or be kind

Etymology

From Latin implacabilis (unappeasable), from in- 'not' + placabilis 'appeasable'.
