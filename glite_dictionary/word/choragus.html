choragus

1. NOUN

/ kɔˈreɪɡəs / (AmE), / kɒˈreɪɡəs / (BrE)
(in ancient Greece) the leader of a chorus or the sponsor of a musical or dramatic performance at a festival.
leader of a chorus in ancient Greece

Examples

The choragus played a crucial role in organizing the city's dramatic festivals.
Each choragus took great pride in leading their troupe during the competition.
As choragus, he was responsible for training both singers and dancers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chorus - a company of singers and dancers in ancient Greek drama

Etymology

From Greek *khorēgos* (leader of a chorus), from *khoros* (chorus) and *agein* (to lead).
