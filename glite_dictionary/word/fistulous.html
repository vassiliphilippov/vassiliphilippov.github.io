fistulous

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɪstʃu·ləs / (AmE), / ˈfɪstjʊləs / (BrE)
hollow and tube-shaped, like a reed; often used to describe passages or structures in medical or botanical contexts
hollow and tube-shaped; reed-like
medical

Examples

The surgeon discovered a fistulous tract connecting the abscess to the skin surface.
Some plants have fistulous stems, allowing air to flow through their hollow centers.
The radiograph revealed a fistulous passage formed as a complication of the infection.

Etymology

From Latin 'fistulosus', from 'fistula' (pipe, tube, reed).

2. ADJ

/ ˈfɪs.tʃə.ləs / (AmE), / ˈfɪs.tjʊ.ləs / (BrE)
relating to, or resembling a fistula; having the form or nature of a fistula.
relating to a fistula (medical)
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with a fistulous tract connecting the intestine and the skin.
Surgical intervention was required to repair the fistulous opening.
The doctor noted a fistulous discharge in the affected area.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fistula - an abnormal connection between two hollow spaces in the body

Etymology

From Latin 'fistulosus', from 'fistula' (pipe, tube, fistula).
