pointedness

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔɪntɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪntɪdnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of having a sharp or tapered tip
the state of having a sharp tip

Examples

The pointedness of the pencil made it ideal for fine sketches.
She admired the pointedness of the sculpture’s features.
Over time, erosion diminished the pointedness of the mountain peak.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bluntness - the quality of being not sharp or rounded

Etymology

From 'pointed' + '-ness', indicating the state or quality of being pointed.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɔɪntɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪntɪdnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of a remark, look, or action being obviously directed at a particular person or thing, often in a critical or significant way
quality of being intentionally directed or pointed

Examples

The pointedness of her comment made everyone in the room uncomfortable.
You could not ignore the pointedness in his tone during the meeting.
Critics noted the pointedness with which the author addressed social issues in her novel.

Synset Examples

the pointedness of his sarcasm was unmistakable

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bluntness - the quality of being uncompromisingly forthright or direct, often lacking subtlety or tact

Etymology

From 'pointed' + '-ness', forming a noun indicating the state or quality of being pointed.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
