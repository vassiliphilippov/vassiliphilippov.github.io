onychosis

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊ.nɪˈkoʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌɒn.ɪˈkəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
any disease or disorder affecting the nails, especially of the fingers or toes
disease or disorder of the nails
medical

Examples

The dermatologist diagnosed the patient with onychosis after observing several nail abnormalities.
Chronic exposure to chemicals can lead to various forms of onychosis in factory workers.
Effective treatment of onychosis depends on identifying the underlying cause of the nail disorder.

Etymology

From Greek onyx (nail) + -osis (disease, condition).
