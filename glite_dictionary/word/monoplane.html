monoplane

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːnəˌpleɪn / (AmE), / ˈmɒnəˌpleɪn / (BrE)
An airplane with only one main wing attached to its fuselage.
airplane with a single wing

Examples

The museum houses a restored monoplane from the early days of aviation.
She designed a lightweight monoplane for her senior engineering project.
The pilot preferred flying a monoplane to a biplane due to its superior speed.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: biplane - an airplane with two sets of wings, one above the other
  • Synonym: airplane - a powered flying vehicle with fixed wings and a weight greater than that of the air it displaces

Etymology

From mono- (single) + plane (short for aeroplane).
