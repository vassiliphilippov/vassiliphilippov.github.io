subsystem

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌbˌsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌsɪstəm / (BrE)
a system that forms part of a larger system
part of a larger system

Examples

Each module within the aircraft is a distinct subsystem responsible for a specific function.
The software's audio subsystem was upgraded to improve sound quality.
Engineers isolated the power subsystem to diagnose the failure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: system - a set of connected things or parts forming a complex whole

Etymology

Formed from prefix 'sub-' (under, part of) and 'system' (from Greek 'systēma').
