victory

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɪk.tə.ri / (AmE), / ˈvɪk.təri / (BrE)
The achievement of success in a struggle or contest; a win against an opponent or difficulty.
success in a contest or struggle

Examples

Their team celebrated a hard-fought victory after coming from behind in the final minutes.
The candidate claimed victory in the election with a clear majority of votes.
Securing the contract was a major victory for the small business.

Synset Examples

a narrow victory
the general always gets credit for his army's victory
clinched a victory
convincing victory
the agreement was a triumph for common sense

Related Terms

  • Antonym: defeat - failure to win; loss in a contest or struggle
  • Synonym: triumph - a great victory or notable success

Etymology

From Latin 'victoria' (victory), from 'victor' (conqueror).
