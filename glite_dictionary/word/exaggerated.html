exaggerated

1. ADJ

/ ɪɡˈzædʒəˌreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzædʒəˌreɪtɪd / (BrE)
made larger, greater, or more noticeable than is normal or necessary, often to an abnormal degree.
enlarged or overstated

Examples

The comedian's exaggerated expressions made the audience laugh uncontrollably.
She wore an exaggerated hat that was much too large for her head.
The report gave an exaggerated view of the problem, making it seem much worse than it actually was.

Synset Examples

thick lenses exaggerated the size of her eyes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overstated - expressed in a way that is too strong or exaggerated
  • Antonym: understated - expressed or done in a restrained or subtle way

Etymology

Past participle of 'exaggerate', from Latin 'exaggerare' (to heap up, magnify).

2. ADJ

/ ɪɡˈzædʒəˌreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzadʒəreɪtɪd / (BrE)
represented as greater or more important than is actually the case
made to seem more than it is

Examples

His exaggerated claims were quickly debunked by experts.
She gave an exaggerated sigh to show her annoyance.
The artist used exaggerated features to create a dramatic portrait.

Synset Examples

an exaggerated opinion of oneself

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overstated - expressed in too strong or exaggerated terms
  • Antonym: understated - presented as less important or smaller than is actually the case

Etymology

From Latin 'exaggerare' (to heap up), via medieval Latin 'exaggeratus,' past participle of 'exaggerare.'

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
