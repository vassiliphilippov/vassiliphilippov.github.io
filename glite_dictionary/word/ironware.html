ironware

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪərnˌwɛr / (AmE), / ˈaɪənˌweə / (BrE)
articles, tools, or implements made wholly or chiefly of iron or metal.
tools or articles made of iron

Examples

The antique shop was filled with old ironware and cooking utensils.
Heavy ironware hung from the kitchen walls, a testament to a bygone era.
Most of the ironware in the house was handcrafted by local blacksmiths.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hardware - tools, utensils, or fittings made of metal

Etymology

From iron (metal) + ware (manufactured goods), pattern after similar compounds like earthenware.
