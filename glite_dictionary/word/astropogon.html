Astropogon

1. NOUN

/ ˌæstrəˈpoʊɡɒn / (AmE), / ˌæstrəˈpɒɡɒn / (BrE)
A genus of small marine fish in the family Apogonidae, commonly known as cardinalfishes.
genus of cardinalfishes

Examples

The Astropogon genus includes several species of nocturnal reef fish.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the Astropogon genus.
Members of Astropogon are known for their distinctive mouthbrooding behavior.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Apogonidae - family of cardinalfishes

Etymology

From Greek 'astron' (star) + 'pōgōn' (beard).

2. NOUN

/ ˌæstrəˈpoʊɡɑn / (AmE), / ˌæstrəˈpəʊɡɒn / (BrE)
A genus of small marine fishes in the family Apogonidae, known as cardinalfishes, found in coastal tropical waters.
genus of marine cardinalfishes

Examples

The Astropogon genus includes several species of cardinalfish found in warm coastal waters.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Astropogon genus during their Caribbean expedition.
The unique jaw structure of Astropogon distinguishes it from other genera in the Apogonidae family.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Apogonidae - the family of marine fishes including cardinalfishes

Etymology

From Greek 'astro-' (star) + 'pogon' (beard), referring to features of the fish.
