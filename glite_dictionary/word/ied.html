IED

1. NOUN

/ ˌaɪˌiːˈdiː / (AmE), / ˌaɪˌiːˈdiː / (BrE)
an explosive device constructed and used in ways other than conventional military action, often created from improvised materials
improvised explosive device

Examples

Authorities found an IED hidden under the bridge.
Military personnel are trained to safely dispose of an IED if they encounter one.
An IED explosion caused significant damage to the convoy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bomb - an explosive weapon

Etymology

Acronym for improvised explosive device.
