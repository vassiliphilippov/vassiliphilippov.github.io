walk-on

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔk ɑn / (AmE), / ˈwɔːk ɒn / (BrE)
A small acting role in a play, film, or television show, often without spoken lines.
minor non-speaking acting role

Examples

He landed a walk-on in a popular television series.
Many aspiring actors start their careers with walk-on roles in theatres.
She made a brief walk-on during the final scene of the movie.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cameo - brief appearance by a well-known person in a performance
  • Antonym: leading role - main or principal part in a dramatic production

Etymology

From the phrase 'walk on,' describing the actor's action in simply walking onto the stage.

2. ADJ

/ ˈwɔk ˌɔn / (AmE), / ˈwɔːk ɒn / (BrE)
(of a part or role in a play, film, etc.) not involving speech or spoken lines; having no spoken dialogue.
nonspeaking acting role

Examples

She had a walk-on part in the movie, appearing without any lines.
He started his acting career with a walk-on role in a local theatre production.
Many actors begin with walk-on appearances before landing speaking parts.

Synset Examples

had a nonspeaking role in the play

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonspeaking - (of a role) not involving spoken lines
  • Degree Higher: bit part - small acting role with little or no lines

Etymology

From theatrical jargon: referring to actors who only need to walk onto the stage, without delivering lines.
