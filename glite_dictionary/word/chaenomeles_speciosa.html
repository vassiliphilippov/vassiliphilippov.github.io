Chaenomeles speciosa

1. NOUN

/ kiːˌnoʊˈmɛliːz spɪˈsiːoʊsə / (AmE), / kiːˌnəʊˈmiːliːz spɛˈsiːəʊsə / (BrE)
A species of deciduous thorny shrub native to China, noted for its red or white blossoms, commonly called flowering quince.
thorny shrub with red or white blossoms

Examples

The Chaenomeles speciosa in our garden blooms with vibrant red flowers each spring.
Many gardeners value Chaenomeles speciosa for its early blossoms and thorny branches, which make it an effective hedge.
Botanists collected samples of Chaenomeles speciosa from several regions in China for research.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flowering quince - common name for Chaenomeles speciosa

Etymology

From Latinized Greek 'chaeno-' (gaping) + 'meles' (apple), and Latin 'speciosa' (showy, beautiful).
