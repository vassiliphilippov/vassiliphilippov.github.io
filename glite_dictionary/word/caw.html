caw

1. NOUN

/ kɔ / (AmE), / kɔː / (BrE)
the harsh, raucous sound made by a crow, raven, or similar bird
harsh cry of a crow or raven

Examples

The loud caw of the crow echoed through the silent forest.
Early in the morning, a single caw broke the stillness of the air.
A caw from above made her look up to see a flock of ravens circling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: croak - a deep, hoarse sound made typically by a frog or a raven

Etymology

Imitative of the sound made by crows; first recorded in Middle English.

2. VERB

/ kɔ / (AmE), / kɔː / (BrE)
(of a crow or similar bird) to make a harsh, harsh-sounding cry.
make a harsh cry (of crows, etc.)

Examples

The crow began to caw loudly as soon as it landed on the fence.
Every morning, the rooks caw outside my window, waking me up early.
We listened as a flock of ravens cawed overhead in the dusk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: croak - make a low, rough sound like a frog or a raven
