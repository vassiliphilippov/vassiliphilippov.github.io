musculus abductor digiti minimi manus

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌs.kjə.ləs æbˈdʌk.tɚ ˈdɪ.dʒɪ.taɪ ˈmaɪ.nə.maː.nʊs / (AmE), / ˈmʌs.kjʊ.ləs æbˈdʌk.tə ˈdɪ.dʒɪ.tɪ ˈmɪ.nɪ.mɪ ˈman.ʊs / (BrE)
A muscle in the hand that abducts (moves away from the midline) the little finger.
muscle in the hand moving the little finger
medical

Examples

Injury to the musculus abductor digiti minimi manus can affect grip strength and dexterity of the little finger.
Surgeons located the musculus abductor digiti minimi manus during the delicate hand operation.
Proper stretching of the musculus abductor digiti minimi manus helps prevent strain in musicians who play string instruments.

Etymology

From Latin: musculus (muscle), abductor (drawer away), digiti minimi (of the little finger), manus (of the hand).
