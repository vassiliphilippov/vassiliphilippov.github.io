raffinose

1. NOUN

/ ˈræ.fəˌnoʊs / (AmE), / ˈræf.ɪ.nəʊs / (BrE)
a trisaccharide sugar found in sugar beets, cotton seeds, and certain cereals
a trisaccharide sugar in plants

Examples

Raffinose is commonly found in the seeds of legumes and can contribute to digestive discomfort.
The presence of raffinose in soybeans affects their nutritional value and digestibility.
Researchers are studying how raffinose content in crops impacts human and animal health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trisaccharide - a carbohydrate composed of three monosaccharides

Etymology

From French 'raffine' (refined), linked to the refining of sugar.
