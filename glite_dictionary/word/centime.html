centime

1. NOUN

/ sɑ̃ˈtim / (AmE), / sɒnˈtiːm / (BrE)
a fractional monetary unit equal to one hundredth of the basic currency in several countries, including France, Switzerland, and various African nations
a hundredth of a franc or similar unit

Examples

A coffee in Paris used to cost just one centime in the early twentieth century.
The price on the label was two francs and fifty centimes.
She collected old centime coins as a hobby.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cent - a fractional monetary unit equal to one hundredth of the basic currency in many countries

Etymology

From French centime, from Latin centesimus (hundredth).

2. NOUN

/ sɑnˈtim / (AmE), / sɒ̃ˈtiːm / (BrE)
a monetary unit equal to one hundredth of the basic currency unit in several French-speaking countries, especially France and Switzerland
currency unit; one-hundredth of the franc

Examples

She found a single centime lying on the street during her trip to Paris.
Back when the franc was still used, a loaf of bread cost just a few centimes.
The museum displayed rare centime coins from the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cent - a monetary unit equal to one hundredth of the basic unit in many countries

Etymology

From French 'centime', from Latin 'centesimus' (hundredth).
