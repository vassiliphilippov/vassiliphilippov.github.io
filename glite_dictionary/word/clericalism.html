clericalism

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɛr.ɪ.kəˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈklɛr.ɪ.kəˌlɪz.əm / (BrE)
support for increasing the influence and power of the clergy in secular or political affairs
support for increased power of clergy

Examples

The rise of clericalism alarmed many advocates of separation between church and state.
He criticized the government for its policy of clericalism and undue religious influence.
Debates about clericalism were common in nineteenth-century European politics.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: secularism - the principle of separating religion from government or civic affairs

Etymology

From French cléricalisme, based on clerical (relating to the clergy) + -ism.
