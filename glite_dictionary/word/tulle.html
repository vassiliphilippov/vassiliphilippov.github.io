tulle

1. NOUN

/ tʊl / (AmE), / tjuːl / (BrE)
A fine, lightweight net fabric, often starched, used for veils, tutus, and gowns.
fine net fabric for veils or dresses

Examples

The bride wore a delicate tulle veil that floated behind her as she walked down the aisle.
Layers of pink tulle gave the ballerina's tutu its signature airy look.
Designers often use tulle to add volume and elegance to evening gowns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: netting - an open-meshed fabric used in clothing or for catching fish

Etymology

From French 'tulle', named after the town of Tulle in southern France, where the fabric was originally made.
