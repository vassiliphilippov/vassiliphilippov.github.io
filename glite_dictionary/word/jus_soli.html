jus soli

1. NOUN

/ jus ˈsoʊlaɪ / (AmE), / juːs ˈsəʊli / (BrE)
The principle that a person's nationality or citizenship is determined by the place where they are born.
citizenship by place of birth
legal

Examples

Several countries, including the United States, grant citizenship based on jus soli.
Debates about immigration policy often reference the concept of jus soli.
He acquired citizenship through jus soli because he was born within the country's borders.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: jus sanguinis - The principle that nationality is determined by parentage rather than birthplace.

Etymology

From Latin 'jus' (law, right) + 'soli' (of the soil).
