sneer

1. VERB

/ snɪr / (AmE), / snɪə / (BrE)
to smile or speak in a way that shows scorn or contempt; to express disdain by curling the lip or using a mocking tone
to express scorn through a smile or words

Examples

He couldn't help but sneer at his rival's failed attempt.
She sneered when she saw the shabby decorations.
The critic sneered at the artist's latest work in his review.

Synset Examples

she sneered her contempt

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jeer - to speak or shout derisively; ridicule
  • Synonym: smirk - to smile in an irritatingly smug or conceited way
  • Antonym: praise - to express warm approval or admiration of someone or something

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'snerren' (to scorn).

2. VERB

/ snɪr / (AmE), / snɪə / (BrE)
to smile or speak in a way that shows contempt or scorn for someone or something
to show contempt through facial expression or words

Examples

He couldn't help but sneer when he heard the ridiculous explanation.
The critic sneered at the artist's latest work, dismissing it as amateurish.
She sneered at his attempt to apologize, making him feel even worse.

Synset Examples

she sneered at her little sister's efforts to play the song on the piano

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scorn - to show disdain or contempt for someone or something
  • Synonym: mock - to laugh at or tease in a scornful manner

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *sneren* (to scorn).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00032532-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ snɪr / (AmE), / snɪə / (BrE)
a facial expression in which the upper lip curls, showing contempt or scorn
facial expression showing contempt

Examples

The disdain in his sneer was obvious to everyone in the room.
She gave a dismissive sneer before turning away from her opponent.
With a cold sneer, he made it clear he thought little of their ideas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scowl - an angry or bad-tempered facial expression
  • Antonym: smile - a pleased, kind, or amused facial expression

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English *snerren* (to scorn), of imitative origin.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ snɪr / (AmE), / snɪə / (BrE)
a contemptuous or mocking remark intended to insult or show scorn
scornful or mocking remark

Examples

Her comment about his clothes was nothing more than a sneer.
He greeted their proposal with a sneer that made everyone uncomfortable.
The politician’s sneer at his opponent revealed his true feelings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: taunt - a remark made to anger, wound, or provoke someone
  • Antonym: compliment - a polite expression of praise or admiration

Etymology

From Middle Dutch 'snieren' (to scorn, to gape or grin), related to the German 'schneuzen' (to snort).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
