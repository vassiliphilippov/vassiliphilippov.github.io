Bambusa

1. NOUN

/ bæmˈbuːsə / (AmE), / bæmˈbuːsə / (BrE)
A genus of tall, clumping bamboos native to tropical and subtropical regions, known for their robust stems and use in construction and gardening.
a genus of tall, clumping bamboos

Examples

The gardener planted several Bambusa species along the edge of the courtyard for shade.
Some types of Bambusa are prized for their strong, straight stems used in furniture making.
Bambusa can grow rapidly in suitable climates, sometimes reaching full height in a single season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bamboo - a fast-growing woody grass

Etymology

From Latin, derived from Malay 'bambu' (bamboo).

2. NOUN

/ bæmˈbuːsə / (AmE), / bamˈbuːsə / (BrE)
A genus of tall, tender, clumping bamboos native to tropical and subtropical regions, often cultivated for their strong stems and ornamental value.
a genus of clumping bamboos

Examples

Many species within the Bambusa genus are preferred for building materials due to their sturdy culms.
Ornamental gardens often feature Bambusa because of its attractive leafy appearance and dense growth.
Some farmers plant Bambusa as a windbreak or to prevent soil erosion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bamboo - A giant grass with hollow woody stems; various species

Etymology

From Latin, from Malay *bambu* or *bambuʃ* (bamboo).
