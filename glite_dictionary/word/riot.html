riot

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪət / (AmE), / ˈraɪət / (BrE)
A wild or rowdy gathering, especially one involving excessive drinking and sexual activity.
wild, rowdy gathering (drinking/sex)

Examples

The party quickly turned into a riot, with guests drinking and dancing wildly.
The weekend retreat became a full-blown riot by midnight.
They were notorious for turning even quiet events into a riot whenever they showed up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: orgy - an excessively wild party, especially with sexual activity

Etymology

From Old French *riote* (dispute, quarrel), of uncertain origin.

3. NOUN

/ ˈraɪət / (AmE), / ˈraɪət / (BrE)
A violent public disturbance caused by a group of people acting in a disorderly and destructive way.
violent public disturbance

Examples

Several cities experienced a riot after the controversial court verdict was announced.
The government imposed a curfew to restore order following a night of riot in the capital.
Police struggled to control the riot as violence broke out among the crowd.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disturbance - a disruption of public order; commotion
  • Synonym: uproar - a state of noisy confusion or protest
  • Antonym: peace - a state of calm or absence of violence

Etymology

From Old French *riote* (dispute, quarrel).

6. NOUN

/ ˈraɪ.ət / (AmE), / ˈraɪ.ət / (BrE)
A violent disturbance of the peace by a crowd, often involving public disorder and property damage.
violent disturbance by a crowd

Examples

The city declared a state of emergency after a riot broke out downtown.
Several people were injured during the riot that erupted following the controversial verdict.
Police struggled to contain the riot as the violence escalated through the night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disturbance - an event causing public disorder or upset
  • Synonym: rebellion - an act of open resistance against authority
  • Degree Lower: protest - a public demonstration expressing strong objection
  • Degree Higher: uprising - an act of resistance or rebellion, often armed, against authority
