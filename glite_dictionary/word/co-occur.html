to co-occur

1. VERB

/ koʊ.əˈkɜr / (AmE), / kəʊ.əˈkɜː / (BrE)
(of two or more things) to happen or appear together, especially by chance or in connection with each other.
happen or appear together

Examples

Symptoms of the two conditions often co-occur in patients.
Many environmental factors co-occur and may influence each other.
Violence and poverty tend to co-occur in certain communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to occur - to happen or take place

Etymology

From co- (together) + occur; modeled on 'coexist' and similar formations.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02666520-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ koʊ.əˈkɝː / (AmE), / kəʊ.əˈkɜː / (BrE)
to happen or appear together with something else, especially regularly or more often than by chance
occur together with something else

Examples

The symptoms often co-occur in patients with the same condition.
Certain plant species tend to co-occur in specific ecological zones.
The words 'cause' and 'effect' frequently co-occur in scientific texts.

Synset Examples

The word ‘hot’ tends to cooccur with ‘cold’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to coincide - to happen at the same time as something else
  • Synonym: to accompany - to go with as an associate or companion

Etymology

From Latin co- (together) + occur (from Latin occurrere, to run to meet).
