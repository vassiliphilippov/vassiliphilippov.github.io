tetracaine

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛtrəˌkeɪn / (AmE), / ˈtɛtrəkeɪn / (BrE)
A crystalline compound used as a local anesthetic, often in the form of its hydrochloride salt.
local anesthetic compound
medical

Examples

The doctor applied tetracaine to numb the area before stitching the wound.
Ophthalmologists often use tetracaine drops prior to certain eye procedures.
Allergic reactions to tetracaine are rare but possible in sensitive patients.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: procaine - a local anesthetic of similar chemical structure

Etymology

From tetra- (four) and -caine (from cocaine, used in naming anesthetics).
