oilman

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔɪlˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈɔɪl.mæn / (BrE)
a man who works in the production or sale of petroleum or petroleum products
male worker in the petroleum industry

Examples

Her grandfather was an oilman who spent decades working on offshore rigs.
The wealthy oilman donated millions to local charities in Texas.
As an oilman, he traveled across several countries for drilling projects.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: oilwoman - a female worker in the petroleum industry
  • Synonym: petroleum engineer - person who designs and develops methods for extracting oil

Etymology

Formed from 'oil' + 'man'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔɪlˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈɔɪlˌman / (BrE)
a man who owns, operates, or works in the oil industry, especially as the proprietor or manager of oil wells
man who owns or operates oil wells

Examples

He started as an oilman in Texas before building a powerful energy company.
The wealthy oilman donated millions to local charities.
Many young men dreamed of becoming an oilman during the oil boom years.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: oilwoman - a woman who owns or operates oil wells
