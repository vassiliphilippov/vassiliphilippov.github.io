extraterrestrial

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛk.strə.təˈrɛs.tri.əl / (AmE), / ˌɛk.strə.təˈrɛs.tri.əl / (BrE)
originating, existing, or occurring outside the Earth or its atmosphere.
outside Earth or its atmosphere

Examples

Scientists are searching for extraterrestrial life in our solar system.
The signal might have an extraterrestrial origin, though researchers remain cautious.
Many science fiction stories feature extraterrestrial civilizations visiting Earth.

Synset Examples

is there extraterrestrial life?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: terrestrial - of or relating to the earth or its inhabitants

Etymology

From Latin 'extra' (outside) + 'terrestris' (of the earth).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.strə.təˈrɛs.tri.əl / (AmE), / ˌɛk.strə.təˈrɛs.tri.əl / (BrE)
a being or life form originating from outside the Earth or its atmosphere, especially in science fiction or discussions of space
a being from beyond Earth

Examples

Many scientists believe it is possible for extraterrestrial life to exist elsewhere in the universe.
The movie features a friendly extraterrestrial who visits Earth.
Some people claim to have seen extraterrestrial spacecraft in the night sky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alien - a being from another planet or world

Etymology

From Latin extra- (outside) + terrestris (of the earth).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09507595-n
Synset POS: n
