al-Qaida

1. PROPN

/ ælˈkaɪdə / (AmE), / ælˈkaɪdə / (BrE)
a militant Islamist organization founded in 1988, known for carrying out terrorist attacks worldwide, especially against the United States and its allies
militant Islamist terrorist organization

Examples

The United States government took decisive action against al-Qaida after the September 11 attacks.
Intelligence agencies monitor al-Qaida activities across several continents.
Several countries have coordinated efforts to dismantle al-Qaida's networks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: terrorist group - an organization that uses violent acts to achieve political aims

Etymology

From Arabic القاعدة‎ (al-qāʿida, 'the base' or 'the foundation').
