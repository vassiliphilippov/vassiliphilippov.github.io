al-Qa'ida

1. PROPN

/ æl kaɪˈiːdə / (AmE), / æl kɑːˈiːdə / (BrE)
an international terrorist organization founded in 1988, known for organizing attacks against the United States and other countries, and supporting radical Islamist groups worldwide.
international Islamist terrorist organization

Examples

The US government increased security measures after the al-Qa'ida attacks on September 11.
Several countries cooperate to disrupt al-Qa'ida operations around the world.
Intelligence agencies discovered evidence of al-Qa'ida cells operating in multiple regions.

Etymology

From Arabic القاعدة (al-qāʿida), literally meaning 'the base' or 'the foundation'.
