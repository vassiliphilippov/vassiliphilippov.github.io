boredom

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɔrdəm / (AmE), / ˈbɔːdəm / (BrE)
A state of feeling weary and restless due to lack of interest or excitement.
state of feeling bored

Examples

The lecture filled her with boredom, and she struggled to stay awake.
Many people experience boredom when they have nothing to do over the weekend.
After hours of waiting, boredom began to set in among the children.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: interest - state of wanting to know or learn about something
  • Synonym: tedium - the quality or state of being tedious

Etymology

From 'bore' (to weary by dullness) + '-dom' (state or condition).
