Arctostaphylos alpina

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrktoʊˈstæfɪloʊs ælˈpiːnə / (AmE), / ɑːktəˈstæfɪlɒs ælˈpiːnə / (BrE)
A small, creeping, deciduous shrub native to alpine and circumpolar regions, having bright red leaves in autumn and producing black or blue-black berries.
alpine deciduous shrub with dark berries

Examples

The leaves of Arctostaphylos alpina turn a striking red in the autumn months.
Scientists discovered a patch of Arctostaphylos alpina while surveying the alpine tundra.
The berries produced by Arctostaphylos alpina are often eaten by birds and small mammals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bearberry - any shrub of the genus Arctostaphylos with edible berries

Etymology

From Greek arktos (bear) + staphyle (bunch of grapes), and Latin alpinus (of the Alps).
