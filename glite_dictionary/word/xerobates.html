Xerobates

1. NOUN

/ zɪˈroʊbeɪtiːz / (AmE), / zɪəˈrəʊbeɪtiːz / (BrE)
A proposed genus of tortoises that includes the desert and Texas tortoises, recognized in some scientific classifications.
genus of desert and Texas tortoises

Examples

Recent research has suggested that the desert tortoise should be classified under the genus Xerobates.
Some herpetologists now refer to the Texas tortoise as Xerobates berlandieri.
The conservation plan included studies of the species within the genus Xerobates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gopherus - genus including desert tortoises in many classifications

Etymology

From Greek 'xēros' (dry) and 'bates' (one that walks), meaning 'walker of the dry (land)'.

2. NOUN

/ zɛrˈoʊbeɪtiːz / (AmE), / zɪəˈrəʊbɑːtiːz / (BrE)
a proposed genus of desert and Texas tortoises, based on recent taxonomic research
proposed genus of certain tortoises

Examples

Recent papers have classified the desert tortoise as a member of Xerobates.
There is ongoing debate among scientists over the validity of the genus Xerobates.
If confirmed, Xerobates will include several species of tortoises found in arid regions.

Etymology

From Greek 'xero-' meaning 'dry' and 'bates' meaning 'walker', referring to tortoises inhabiting dry environments.
