chamber-work

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃeɪmbər wɜrk / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪmbə wɜːk / (BrE)
a musical composition or performance intended for a small group of instruments, typically one player per part, rather than a full orchestra
music for a small group of instruments

Examples

The string quartet performed a challenging chamber-work by Haydn at the festival.
Many of Beethoven’s chamber-works are considered masterpieces of the form.
She has a special interest in Baroque chamber-work and often organizes small concerts for local audiences.

Synset Examples

She loves performing chamber-work with her friends.
The concert featured beautiful chamber-work by renowned composers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chamber music - music written for a small group of instruments, typically one player to a part

Etymology

From 'chamber' (room) and 'work' (composition), referring to music composed to be played in a room rather than a concert hall.
