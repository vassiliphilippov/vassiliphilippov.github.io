Microsporum

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈkroʊspɔːrəm / (AmE), / mʌɪˈkrəʊspɔːrəm / (BrE)
A genus of fungus in the family Moniliaceae, some species of which can cause ringworm in humans and animals.
genus of fungus causing ringworm
medical

Examples

The veterinarian identified the infection as being caused by Microsporum canis.
Dermatologists may test for the presence of Microsporum in persistent cases of ringworm.
Microsporum infections are more common in warm and humid climates.

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'mikros' (small) + 'sporum' (spore).
