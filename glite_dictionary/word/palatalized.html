palatalized

1. ADJ

/ ˈpælət̬əˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈpalətəlaɪzd / (BrE)
(of a sound) pronounced with the front or blade of the tongue near or touching the hard palate, as in the sound 'y' or the 'ch' in 'chin'.
(of a sound) articulated with tongue near hard palate

Examples

In Russian, many consonants can be palatalized, giving them a noticeably softer sound.
The English 'ch' in 'chin' is a palatalized consonant.
Her pronunciation of the word included a clearly palatalized 'l', as in some Slavic languages.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: non-palatalized - (of a sound) not pronounced with the tongue near the hard palate

Etymology

From 'palatal' (relating to the palate) + '-ized' (forming adjectives indicating process or result).
