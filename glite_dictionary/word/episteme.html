episteme

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈpɪstiˌmi / (AmE), / ɛˈpɪstɪmiː / (BrE)
a body of knowledge or ideas that defines what is considered intellectually certain and valid in a particular historical period
system of knowledge in a given era

Examples

Foucault argued that each historical era has its own episteme that shapes thought and inquiry.
Historians study how the reigning episteme influences what is accepted as scientific truth.
The shift from the medieval to the modern episteme dramatically altered Western philosophy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paradigm - a typical example or pattern of something; a model in science or thought

Etymology

From Ancient Greek ἐπιστήμη (epistēmē), meaning 'knowledge.'
