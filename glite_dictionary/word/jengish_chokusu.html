Jengish Chokusu

1. PROPN

/ dʒɛnˈgiːʃ tʃoʊˈkuːsuː / (AmE), / dʒɛnˈgiːʃ tʃɒˈkuːsuː / (BrE)
the highest mountain peak in the Tien Shan mountain range, located on the border between Kyrgyzstan and China, with an elevation of 7,439 meters (24,406 feet)
highest peak in the Tien Shan mountains

Examples

Climbers from around the world attempt to scale Jengish Chokusu each year.
The summit of Jengish Chokusu offers breathtaking views of the Tien Shan range.
Extreme weather conditions make Jengish Chokusu a challenging ascent even for experienced mountaineers.

Etymology

From Kyrgyz 'Jengish Chokusu' meaning 'Victory Peak'.
