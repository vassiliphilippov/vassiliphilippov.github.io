virago

1. NOUN

/ vɪˈrɑɡoʊ / (AmE), / vɪˈrɑːɡəʊ / (BrE)
an offensive term for a woman regarded as noisy, scolding, or domineering, especially in a harsh or overbearing manner
noisy, domineering woman (offensive)
offensive archaic

Examples

His neighbors described her as a virago who was always shouting insults from her porch.
He endured the criticisms of a literal virago for years before finally moving out.
The play featured a memorable virago whose domineering presence intimidated everyone on stage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrew - a bad-tempered or aggressively assertive woman
  • Synonym: harpy - an unpleasant, scolding woman

Etymology

From Latin 'virago' (a man-like or heroic woman), from 'vir' (man).

2. NOUN

/ vəˈrɑːɡoʊ / (AmE), / vɪˈrɑːɡəʊ / (BrE)
A woman of great strength or courage, often depicted as loud, aggressive, or domineering.
strong, aggressive woman

Examples

Many viewed the fearless political leader as a virago who intimidated her opponents.
In folklore, the virago fought alongside men in battle and earned their respect.
She dismissed critics who labeled her a virago because of her assertive personality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amazon - a strong, powerful woman

Etymology

From Latin 'virago', meaning 'man-like woman', from 'vir' (man).
