os nasale

1. NOUN

/ ɑs nəˈseɪli / (AmE), / ɒs nəˈsɑːli / (BrE)
An elongated, rectangular bone that forms the bridge of the nose.
bone forming the bridge of the nose
medical

Examples

The os nasale is fractured in many nasal injuries.
Humans have two os nasale bones located side by side.
Surgeons studied the os nasale before performing the reconstructive procedure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nasal bone - the bone forming the bridge of the nose

Etymology

From Latin 'os' (bone) and 'nasale' (of the nose).
