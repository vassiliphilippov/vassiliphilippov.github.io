overfed

1. ADJ

/ ˌoʊvərˈfɛd / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈfɛd / (BrE)
having eaten more food than is necessary or healthy; too well nourished or overweight due to excessive feeding
having eaten too much food

Examples

The overfed cat lounged lazily in the sun, too full to chase the birds outside.
Many people in wealthy societies are overfed, leading to health problems such as obesity.
Unlike the malnourished children in the photo, his overfed appearance was quite striking.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: underfed - having not eaten enough food

Etymology

Formed from 'over-' (meaning 'excess') and 'fed', past participle of 'feed'.
