elusiveness

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈluːsɪvnəs / (AmE), / ɪˈluːsɪvnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being difficult to find, catch, understand, or define
quality of being hard to catch or define

Examples

The elusiveness of the solution frustrated even the most experienced scientists.
She admired the elusiveness of the rare butterfly, which no one had managed to photograph.
The concept's elusiveness made it a hot topic among philosophers.

Synset Examples

the author's elusiveness may at times be construed as evasiveness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: evasion - the act of avoiding or escaping from something

Etymology

From elusive + -ness; ultimately from Latin eludere (to evade, escape).
