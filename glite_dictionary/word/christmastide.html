Christmastide

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrɪs·məsˌtaɪd / (AmE), / ˈkrɪs.məs.taɪd / (BrE)
the period from December 24 to January 6, traditionally observed as the Christmas season
the Christmas season period

Examples

Many families celebrate the entire Christmastide with special meals and gatherings.
The church choir performed carols throughout Christmastide.
Decorations remained in the town square for the duration of Christmastide.

Etymology

Formed from 'Christmas' + 'tide' (Old English tīd, meaning time or season).
