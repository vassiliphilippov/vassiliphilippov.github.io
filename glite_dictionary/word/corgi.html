corgi

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɔrɡi / (AmE), / ˈkɔːɡi / (BrE)
either of two Welsh breeds of small, long-bodied, short-legged dogs with erect ears and a fox-like head, especially the Pembroke Welsh Corgi and the Cardigan Welsh Corgi.
Welsh breed of small, short-legged dog

Examples

The queen was famously fond of her corgi, often seen walking the dogs in palace gardens.
Their playful corgi loves to chase after tennis balls in the backyard.
We adopted a corgi last year, and he's been a wonderful companion.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Pembroke Welsh Corgi - a breed of corgi with a shorter tail, originally from Pembrokeshire
  • Degree Higher: Cardigan Welsh Corgi - a breed of corgi with a long tail, originally from Cardiganshire

Etymology

From Welsh 'cor' (dwarf) + 'ci' (dog).
