Lilium michiganense

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪlioʊ ˌmɪʃɪˈɡænɛns / (AmE), / ˈlɪliəm ˌmɪtʃɪˈɡanəns / (BrE)
A lily native to central North America, noted for its recurved orange-red flowers with deep crimson spots.
species of North American lily

Examples

The Lilium michiganense brightened the summer wetland with its vivid blossoms.
Gardeners often seek Lilium michiganense for its striking orange-red, spotted flowers.
Several conservation groups are working to protect native Lilium michiganense populations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lily - a plant of the genus Lilium with large prominent flowers

Etymology

From Latin: genus name Lilium (lily) and michiganense, meaning 'of Michigan', referring to its native region.
