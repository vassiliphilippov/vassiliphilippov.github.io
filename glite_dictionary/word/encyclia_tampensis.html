Encyclia tampensis

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˈsɪkliə tæmˈpɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ɛnˈsɪklɪə tæmˈpɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A species of orchid native to Florida and the Bahamas, known for its showy, brightly colored flowers; sometimes placed in the genus Epidendrum.
a species of orchid in Florida and the Bahamas

Examples

The Encyclia tampensis blooms in late spring and is a favorite among orchid collectors.
Native to Florida's subtropical regions, Encyclia tampensis thrives in moist, well-drained soil.
Gardeners often refer to Encyclia tampensis as the 'Florida butterfly orchid' due to its vibrant flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Epidendrum tampense - synonymic scientific name, sometimes classified under Epidendrum

Etymology

From the genus name Encyclia and the Latinized form of Tampa, referencing its native region.
