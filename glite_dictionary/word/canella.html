canella

1. NOUN

/ kəˈnɛlə / (AmE), / kəˈnɛlə / (BrE)
the highly aromatic inner bark of Canella winterana, used as a condiment and as a tonic
aromatic bark used as a spice and tonic

Examples

The chef used canella to give the dessert a warm, spicy flavor.
In traditional medicine, canella was valued for its tonic properties.
Dried canella bark is sometimes substituted for cinnamon in Caribbean cuisine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cinnamon - aromatic spice made from the inner bark of trees, similar use as canella

Etymology

From Latin *cannella* (diminutive of *canna*, meaning 'reed' or 'cane').
