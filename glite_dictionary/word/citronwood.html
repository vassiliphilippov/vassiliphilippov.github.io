citronwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪt.rənˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈsɪt.rənˌwʊd / (BrE)
The wood of a citron tree, often used for carving or ornamental work.
wood from a citron tree

Examples

The artisan carved the box from citronwood for its beautiful color and grain.
Furniture made of citronwood was prized in the region for its durability.
He admired the subtle scent of freshly cut citronwood while working in his shop.

Etymology

From 'citron' (the citrus fruit) + 'wood'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɪtrənˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈsɪtrənˌwʊd / (BrE)
a durable, fragrant wood valued for construction, historically used in buildings such as the cathedral at Cordova, Spain
hard, fragrant wood used in construction

Examples

The carved doors were made from the fragrant citronwood found in southern forests.
Restorers imported citronwood to match the original beams of the ancient cathedral.
Because of its durability, citronwood was often chosen for historical construction projects.
