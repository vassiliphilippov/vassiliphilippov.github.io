residue

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛzɪˌdu / (AmE), / ˈrɛzɪdjuː / (BrE)
a small amount of something that remains after the main part has been removed or used
small amount left after removal

Examples

After the water evaporated, a thin residue of salt was left on the surface.
The cleaning process left a sticky residue on the windows.
He swept away the residue of dust from the floor before mopping.

Synset Examples

there was no remainder
he threw away the rest
he took what he wanted and I got the balance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remainder - the part left over after other parts have been taken away
  • Synonym: remnant - a small remaining quantity of something

Etymology

From Latin residuum (that which remains), from residere (to remain).

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛzəˌdu / (AmE), / ˈrɛzɪdjuː / (BrE)
a small amount of something that remains after the main part has been removed or used
remaining matter after removal

Examples

After the water evaporated, a thin residue of salt was left behind on the surface.
There was a sticky residue on the table after the juice spilled.
The chemist analyzed the residue remaining in the test tube.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remnant - a small remaining quantity of something
  • Degree Lower: trace - a very small amount of something left behind

Etymology

From Latin residuum (that which remains), from residere (to remain behind).
