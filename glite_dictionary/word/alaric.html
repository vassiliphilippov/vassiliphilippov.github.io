Alaric

1. PROPN

/ ˈælərɪk / (AmE), / ˈalərɪk / (BrE)
(c. 370–410) King of the Visigoths, notable for capturing and sacking Rome in 410.
King of the Visigoths who sacked Rome

Examples

Alaric was the first barbarian leader to capture the city of Rome.
Historians continue to debate Alaric's motives for marching on Rome in 410.
Many historians consider Alaric's sack of Rome a turning point in the fall of the Western Roman Empire.

Etymology

From Late Latin Alaricus, from Gothic Alareiks, meaning 'ruler of all'.
