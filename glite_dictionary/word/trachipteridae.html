Trachipteridae

1. NOUN

/ ˌtrækɪpˈtɛrɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌtrakɪpˈtɛrɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine fishes known as ribbonfishes, notable for their elongated, ribbon-like bodies.
family of ribbonfishes

Examples

The family Trachipteridae includes several species of ribbon-like fishes found in deep seas worldwide.
Scientists discovered a new member of the Trachipteridae in the Pacific Ocean.
Many members of the Trachipteridae are notable for their bright, metallic coloration and elongated bodies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ribbonfish - a long, thin, ribbon-shaped marine fish

Etymology

From New Latin Trachipteridae, based on the genus Trachipterus, from Greek trakhos (rough) + pteron (wing, fin).
