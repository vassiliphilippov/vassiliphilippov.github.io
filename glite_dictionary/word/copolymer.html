copolymer

1. NOUN

/ koʊˈpɑlɪmər / (AmE), / kəʊˈpɒlɪmə / (BrE)
A polymer formed from two or more different monomers chemically bonded together.
polymer from different monomers

Examples

The new material is a copolymer designed for high durability in automotive parts.
Researchers synthesized a copolymer using both styrene and butadiene monomers.
A copolymer often combines properties from its different monomer components.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polymer - a large molecule composed of many repeated subunits (monomers)

Etymology

From co- (together) + polymer, based on Greek 'poly-' (many) and 'meros' (part).
