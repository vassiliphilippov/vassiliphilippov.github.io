Musca

1. PROPN

/ ˈmʌskə / (AmE), / ˈmʌskə / (BrE)
a small constellation in the southern sky, located near the Southern Cross and Chamaeleon
constellation near the Southern Cross

Examples

The constellation Musca is best observed from the Southern Hemisphere during autumn.
Many amateur astronomers enjoy locating Musca because of its proximity to the Southern Cross.
Musca contains several interesting deep-sky objects for stargazers to explore.

Etymology

From Latin 'musca' meaning 'fly', referring to the constellation's resemblance to a fly.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmʌskə / (AmE), / ˈmʌskə / (BrE)
The type genus of the family Muscidae, including houseflies.
genus of flies including houseflies

Examples

The common housefly, classified as Musca domestica, is found worldwide.
Researchers studied various species within the genus Musca to understand their ecological impact.
Several members of Musca are known carriers of disease.

Etymology

From Latin musca (fly).

3. NOUN

/ ˈmʌskə / (AmE), / ˈmʌskə / (BrE)
A genus of flies in the family Muscidae, including the common housefly (Musca domestica).
genus of flies, including houseflies

Examples

The housefly belongs to the genus Musca and is widespread across the globe.
Researchers studied several species within the Musca genus to understand disease transmission.
The most well-known member of Musca is the common housefly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: housefly - a common fly found in homes; Musca domestica

Etymology

From Latin 'musca' meaning 'fly'.
