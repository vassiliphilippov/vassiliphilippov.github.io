Pinot

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiːnoʊ / (AmE), / ˈpiːnəʊ / (BrE)
A variety of grape, either purple or white, used particularly in making certain wines, notably Burgundies and champagnes.
type of wine grape; grape variety

Examples

The vineyard is famous for its Pinot grapes, which produce delicate and aromatic wines.
He prefers wines made from Pinot due to their light and fruity character.
French Burgundies are well-known for featuring the Pinot variety in their blends.

Etymology

From French 'pinot,' diminutive of 'pin' (pine), possibly from the pine cone shape of the grape cluster.
