premium

1. ADJ

/ ˈpriːmiəm / (AmE), / ˈpriːmiəm / (BrE)
(of a product or service) of higher quality or price than standard; superior or especially expensive.
of superior quality or higher price

Examples

We offer a premium service for customers who desire extra attention and quality.
Many people are willing to pay for premium brands due to their reputation for excellence.
The hotel’s premium rooms feature stunning views and luxurious amenities.

Synset Examples

premium gasoline at a premium price

Related Terms

  • Synonym: luxury - of great comfort or elegance, expensive
  • Antonym: inferior - of lower quality

Etymology

From Latin praemium (reward, prize), via Old French premium.

WordNet ID: oewn-02352428-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˈpriːmiəm / (AmE), / ˈpriːmiəm / (BrE)
a prize, bonus, or award given to customers as an inducement to purchase products or for participating in promotions.
bonus or prize given as an inducement

Examples

Customers who purchased a certain amount received a premium as a reward.
The company offered a premium to those who entered their contest.
Many cereal brands used to include a toy premium inside the box.

Synset Examples

they encouraged customers with a premium for loyal patronage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bonus - an extra payment or reward given in addition to what is due
  • Synonym: incentive - something that motivates a person to take action

Etymology

From Latin praemium (reward, prize).

3. NOUN

/ ˈpriːmi əm / (AmE), / ˈpriːmiəm / (BrE)
a payment or reward, often given by a government, for performing a specific act such as catching a criminal, killing a predatory animal, or enlisting in the military
reward/payment for specific acts

Examples

The government offered a premium to encourage enlistment in the army.
A premium was placed on the heads of notorious outlaws in the region.
Hunters could claim a premium for each wolf they killed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bounty - a monetary reward for capturing or killing someone or something

Etymology

From Latin praemium (reward, prize).

4. NOUN

/ ˈpriːmiəm / (AmE), / ˈpriːmiəm / (BrE)
an amount paid or charged in excess of the usual or nominal value, especially due to scarcity or high demand
extra amount paid above normal value

Examples

During the drought, farmers had to pay a premium for irrigation water.
Concert tickets for popular bands always sell at a premium on resale websites.
Because space downtown is limited, parking comes at a premium during rush hour.

Synset Examples

they paid a premium for access to water

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surcharge - an additional charge or payment above the regular price
  • Antonym: discount - an amount by which the regular price is reduced

Etymology

From Latin pretium (price, value).

5. NOUN

/ ˈpriːmiəm / (AmE), / ˈpriːmiəm / (BrE)
a regular payment made to an insurance company for coverage against loss, damage, illness, etc.
insurance payment

Examples

She pays a high premium every month to keep her health insurance active.
If you fail to pay your car insurance premium, your coverage may lapse.
The company raised the premium on its home insurance policies after the recent floods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: contribution - payment made regularly to an insurance or pension fund
  • Antonym: deductible - the amount paid out of pocket by the policyholder before insurance covers the rest

Etymology

From Latin 'praemium' (reward, prize), via Middle English and Old French.
