Jena

1. PROPN

/ ˈjeɪ.nə / (AmE), / ˈjeː.nə / (BrE)
a decisive battle in 1806 in which Napoleon Bonaparte defeated the Prussian army
battle where Napoleon defeated Prussia, 1806

Examples

Historians often cite Jena as a turning point in Napoleon's military career.
The Prussian defeat at Jena led to major reforms in their army and society.
Every October, the town of Jena commemorates the famous battle with ceremonies and reenactments.

Etymology

From the German name of the town 'Jena' in Thuringia, Germany, where the battle occurred.
