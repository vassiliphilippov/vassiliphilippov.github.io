entrance

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.trəns / (AmE), / ˈɛn.trəns / (BrE)
the act of entering a place or becoming involved in an event or situation
the act of entering

Examples

The bride made her entrance to the sound of applause.
His sudden entrance surprised everyone in the meeting room.
She practiced her entrance for the school play all week.

Synset Examples

she made a grand entrance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entry - the act of going in or the right to enter
  • Antonym: exit - the act of going out or leaving

Etymology

From Old French *entrer*, from Latin *intrare* (to enter).

Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: exit - the act of leaving or going out

Etymology

3. VERB

/ ɪnˈtræns / (AmE), / ɪnˈtrɑːns / (BrE)
to fill someone with wonder and delight, holding their entire attention; to put into a trance-like state
fill with wonder; put in a trance

Examples

The magician's performance seemed to entrance the entire audience, leaving them speechless.
She was so entranced by the music that she barely noticed the time passing.
The storyteller's voice could entrance even the most restless children.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to enchant - to fill with great delight; charm
  • Synonym: to hypnotize - to put someone into a hypnotic trance

Etymology

From French *en-*, into, and *transe*, trance, from Latin *transīre* (to cross).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
