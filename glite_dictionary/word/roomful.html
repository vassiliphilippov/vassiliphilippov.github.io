roomful

1. NOUN

/ ˈruːmˌfʊl / (AmE), / ˈruːmˌfʊl / (BrE)
as many people or things as can fill a room; the quantity a room will hold.
as many as fill a room

Examples

A roomful of guests greeted the newlyweds as they entered.
The comedian performed for a roomful of enthusiastic fans.
She donated a roomful of old books to the local library.

Etymology

From 'room' + suffix '-ful' (full of).
