to thresh

1. VERB

/ θrɛʃ / (AmE), / θrɛʃ / (BrE)
to separate seeds from a grain by beating
separate seeds from grain by beating

Examples

Farmers used to thresh wheat by hand before machines were invented.
It takes a lot of effort to thresh the rice using traditional methods.
After harvesting, workers threshed the barley in the barn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: winnow - to blow air through grain to remove chaff

Etymology

Old English *þrescan*, related to Gothic *þriskan* and German *dreschen* (to thresh).

2. VERB

/ θrɛʃ / (AmE), / θrɛʃ / (BrE)
to move or thrash about in a violent, uncontrolled, or agitated way
move or thrash about violently

Examples

The injured bird began to thresh about on the ground, struggling to fly.
He continued to thresh wildly in the water until help arrived.
With every nightmare, she would thresh in bed, her sheets tangled around her legs.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

Etymology
