filing

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
a small particle or shaving removed from a surface by a file, especially in metalworking or woodworking
small particle removed by a file

Examples

The mechanic brushed the metal filings off the workbench before starting a new job.
Tiny filings from the saw collected in a pile beneath the table.
After sharpening the blade, he carefully cleared away the steel filings.

3. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of officially submitting a document to a court or public office so that it becomes part of the public record
official submission of a document
legal

Examples

The filing of the lawsuit was announced in a press conference.
She made sure every filing was completed before the deadline.
His tax filing for this year showed several deductions.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: submission - the act of presenting something for consideration or judgement

Etymology

From the verb 'file', meaning to submit officially, which derives from Medieval Latin 'filare' (to string on a thread), referencing the stringing of documents onto a file.
