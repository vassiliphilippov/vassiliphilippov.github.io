Triglochin maritima

1. NOUN

/ trɪˈɡloʊ.kɪn məˈrɪ.tɪ.mə / (AmE), / trɪˈɡlɒ.kɪn məˈrɪ.tɪ.mə / (BrE)
A tufted perennial plant found in shallow water or marshland, sometimes poisonous to livestock; also known as sea arrowgrass.
a marshland plant; sea arrowgrass

Examples

Triglochin maritima grows abundantly in coastal marshes and other saline habitats.
Farmers are cautious of grazing their cattle near fields where Triglochin maritima is prevalent.
Botanists study Triglochin maritima to understand its adaptation to wet, salty environments.

Etymology

From New Latin, genus name 'Triglochin' and species epithet 'maritima' meaning 'of the sea.'
