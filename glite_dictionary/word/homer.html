homer

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊmər / (AmE), / ˈhəʊmə / (BrE)
an ancient Hebrew unit of capacity equal to 10 baths or 10 ephahs, used especially in biblical contexts
ancient Hebrew unit of capacity

Examples

A homer was typically used to measure large quantities of grain in ancient times.
The prophet mentions a homer of barley as a common offering.
Ten ephahs make a single homer, according to biblical measurements.

Etymology

From Hebrew ḥōmer, meaning 'heap' or 'pile'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊmər / (AmE), / ˈhəʊmə / (BrE)
(informal) A hit in baseball that allows the batter to round all the bases and score a run, also known as a home run.
baseball: hit scoring a run (home run)
informal american

Examples

He hit a towering homer in the ninth inning to win the game.
The rookie has already hit five homers this season.
Fans cheered loudly as their star player smashed another homer out of the park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: home run - a hit in baseball that allows the batter to circle all bases and score

Etymology

Shortened form of 'home run', referring to a hit enabling the batter to reach home plate.

/ ˈhoʊmər / (AmE), / ˈhəʊmə / (BrE)

informal american

Related Terms
