Joe Millerism

1. NOUN

/ ˌdʒoʊ ˈmɪlərɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌdʒəʊ ˈmɪlərɪzəm / (BrE)
a superficial or shallow argument, often lacking originality or depth
superficial or shallow argument

Examples

Critics accused the senator of relying on Joe Millerism rather than presenting substantive evidence.
The discussion quickly turned bland with a series of Joe Millerism that failed to persuade anyone.
Her essay was dismissed for its reliance on Joe Millerism and lack of critical analysis.

Synset Examples

His argument was filled with Joe Millerism, lacking depth.
Critics dismissed his ideas as mere Joe Millerism.
The report was criticized for its Joe Millerism approach.

Etymology

From 'Joe Miller', an English actor whose name became associated with stale or obvious jokes due to the publication of 'Joe Miller's Jests' (1739).
