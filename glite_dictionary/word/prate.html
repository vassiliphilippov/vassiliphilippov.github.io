prate

1. NOUN

/ preɪt / (AmE), / preɪt / (BrE)
idle, foolish, or irrelevant talk; chatter that lacks substance or importance
idle or foolish chatter
archaic

Examples

Tired of his endless prate, she excused herself from the conversation.
The meeting was filled with useless prate instead of practical ideas.
He dismissed their gossip as nothing more than idle prate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chatter - quick, trivial or purposeless talk
  • Synonym: babble - foolish or meaningless talk

Etymology

From Middle English praten, from Middle Dutch praten (to chatter).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07151837-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ preɪt / (AmE), / preɪt / (BrE)
to talk at length about trivial or unimportant matters, especially in a tedious or foolish way
talk tediously about trivial matters

Examples

Please don't prate about your accomplishments; get to the point.
The politicians continued to prate on topics that had little relevance to the debate.
She tends to prate endlessly when excited, often boring everyone in the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: babble - to talk rapidly and continuously in a foolish or excited way
  • Synonym: prattle - to talk at length in a foolish or inconsequential way

Etymology

From Middle English praten, from Middle Dutch praten (to chatter, prattle).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01038676-v
Synset POS: v
