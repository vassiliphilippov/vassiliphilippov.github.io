to misplace

1. VERB

/ mɪsˈpleɪs / (AmE), / mɪsˈpleɪs / (BrE)
to put something in the wrong place or position
to put in the wrong place or position

Examples

The editor noticed that the author had misplaced several modifiers in the first paragraph.
If you misplace a comma in the sentence, it can change the meaning entirely.
She accidentally misplaced the footnote reference, causing confusion for readers.

Synset Examples

misplaced modifiers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to displace - to move something from its usual or proper place

Etymology

From mis- (wrongly) + place, modeled after French 'méplacer.'

2. VERB

/ mɪsˈpleɪs / (AmE), / mɪsˈpleɪs / (BrE)
to put something in the wrong place so that you cannot find it again
to put something where it cannot be found

Examples

I always misplace my keys when I'm in a hurry.
She misplaced the receipt and couldn't return the item.
Please try not to misplace any important documents during the move.

Synset Examples

I misplaced my eyeglasses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lose - to fail to keep possession of something; to be unable to find something

Etymology

From mis- (wrongly) + place, Middle English, influenced by Old French 'mesplacer'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01505740-v
Synset POS: v
