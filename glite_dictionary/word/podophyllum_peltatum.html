Podophyllum peltatum

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊdəˈfɪləm pɛlˈteɪtəm / (AmE), / ˌpɒdəˈfɪləm pɛlˈtɑːtəm / (BrE)
A North American herbaceous plant with a poisonous rootstock and edible but tasteless fruit, commonly known as Mayapple.
North American plant with poisonous roots, edible fruit

Examples

The shaded forest floor was carpeted with Podophyllum peltatum in early spring.
Although Podophyllum peltatum has poisonous roots, its fruit can be eaten when fully ripe.
Botanists study Podophyllum peltatum for its unique medicinal compounds.

Etymology

From New Latin; genus name from Greek 'pous, podos' (foot) and 'phyllon' (leaf), referring to the shape of the leaves.
