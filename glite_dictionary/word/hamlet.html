hamlet

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæmlət / (AmE), / ˈhæmlət / (BrE)
A small settlement, usually smaller than a village, typically without its own church or governing body.
very small settlement

Examples

They live in a quiet hamlet surrounded by rolling hills and farmland.
The hamlet had only a dozen houses and no shops.
We stopped to rest at a small hamlet nestled by the river.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: village - a settlement larger than a hamlet but smaller than a town
  • Degree Higher: town - an urban area larger than a village

Etymology

From Old French 'hamlet', diminutive of 'ham' (village), from Frankish or Germanic origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhæmlət / (AmE), / ˈhæmlɪt / (BrE)
a small settlement, typically smaller than a village and without its own church or governing body
small settlement, smaller than a village or town

Examples

They moved to a quiet hamlet nestled in the hills.
The hamlet barely had a dozen houses huddled along a single lane.
Tourists often overlook the picturesque hamlet on their way to the city.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: village - a group of houses and associated buildings, larger than a hamlet but smaller than a town
  • Degree Higher: town - a human settlement larger than a village

Etymology

From Old French *hamlet*, diminutive of *ham* (village), of Germanic origin.
