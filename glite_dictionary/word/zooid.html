zooid

1. NOUN

/ ˈzoʊˌɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈzəʊˌɔɪd / (BrE)
an individual animal forming part of a colonial organism, such as a bryozoan or hydrozoan, often functioning semi-independently but connected to others in the colony
individual unit in a colonial animal

Examples

Each zooid in the coral colony has a specialized function.
Bryozoans are made up of hundreds of tiny zooids living together.
The feeding zooid uses its tentacles to capture plankton.

Etymology

From Greek zōion (animal) + -oid (like, form of).
