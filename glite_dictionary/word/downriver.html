downriver

1. ADV

/ ˌdaʊnˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˌdaʊnˈrɪvə / (BrE)
In the direction that a river or stream flows; away from its source.
in the direction the river flows

Examples

After lunch, we continued downriver in our canoe, enjoying the changing scenery.
Pollutants released upstream eventually made their way downriver to the town.
The boat drifted downriver faster than we expected due to the strong current.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: upriver - toward the source of a river
