Blephilia

1. NOUN

/ bləˈfɪliə / (AmE), / blɛˈfɪlɪə / (BrE)
a small genus of North American herbs in the mint family, commonly known as wood mints
genus of North American herbs (wood mints)

Examples

The Blephilia plants are easily recognized by their whorled purple flowers.
In the forest understory, Blephilia often grows alongside other wild mints.
Botanists studied the distribution of Blephilia in eastern North America.

Etymology

From Greek 'blepharis' (eyelash), referring to hair-like structures on the plant.
