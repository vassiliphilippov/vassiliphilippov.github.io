Astragalus

1. NOUN

/ əˈstræɡələs / (AmE), / əˈstræɡələs / (BrE)
a large genus of annual or perennial herbs and shrubs of the north temperate regions, in the pea family (Leguminosae), including many species known as milkvetches
a genus of plants in the pea family

Examples

The herbal supplement was made from the roots of Astragalus to boost the immune system.
Many species of Astragalus are being studied for their medicinal properties.
Botanists discovered a new variety of Astragalus growing in the rocky outcrop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: milkvetch - any plant of the genus Astragalus, commonly called milkvetch

Etymology

From Greek astrágalos, meaning 'ankle bone' or 'knucklebone', possibly referring to the shape of the seeds.
