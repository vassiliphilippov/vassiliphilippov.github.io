coaxer

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊksər / (AmE), / ˈkəʊksə / (BrE)
a person who tries to persuade someone by gentle encouragement or flattery
person who persuades gently

Examples

As a skilled coaxer, she managed to get everyone on board with her plan.
The salesman was a natural coaxer, gently convincing customers to upgrade their purchases.
Even the most stubborn child can sometimes be swayed by a patient coaxer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: persuader - someone who persuades others
  • Synonym: coax - to persuade someone gently

Etymology

From the verb 'coax' + '-er' agent suffix.
