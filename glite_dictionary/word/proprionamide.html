proprionamide

1. NOUN

/ ˌprəʊpriˈænəˌmaɪd / (AmE), / ˌprɒprɪˈænəmaɪd / (BrE)
A chemical compound that is the amide derivative of propionic acid (C2H5CONH2).
amide of propionic acid
medical

Examples

Proprionamide is commonly used as an intermediate in organic synthesis.
The laboratory synthesized proprionamide to study its melting point and solubility.
Researchers found that proprionamide decomposes at high temperatures.

Etymology

From 'propionic acid' + 'amide', indicating its structure as the amide of propionic acid.
