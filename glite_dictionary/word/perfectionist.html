perfectionist

1. NOUN

/ pərˈfɛkʃənɪst / (AmE), / pəˈfɛkʃənɪst / (BrE)
a person who refuses to accept any standard short of perfection and is dissatisfied with anything less than flawless results
person with extremely high standards

Examples

As a perfectionist, she spent hours revising her report to eliminate every minor error.
It's hard for a perfectionist to feel satisfied with their work, no matter how well they do.
His friends describe him as a perfectionist when it comes to organizing events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idealist - a person who seeks perfect or ideal outcomes
  • Antonym: realist - a person who accepts situations as they are and deals with them practically

Etymology

From Latin 'perfectio' (completion, perfection) + English '-ist' (one who practices or is concerned with something).
