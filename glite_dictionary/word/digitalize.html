to digitalize

1. VERB

/ ˈdɪdʒɪtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈdɪdʒɪtəlaɪz / (BrE)
to convert information or material into a digital form that can be used by a computer
convert to digital form

Examples

The company decided to digitalize all its old records to improve efficiency.
They are working to digitalize medical files for easier access by professionals.
Many museums are seeking funds to digitalize their collections for online visitors.

Synset Examples

he bought a device to digitize the data

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to digitize - to convert data or images into a digital form usable by a computer

Etymology

From 'digital' + '-ize', formed after 'digitize' by analogy.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00564901-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈdɪdʒɪtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈdɪdʒɪtəlaɪz / (BrE)
to administer digitalis to a patient so as to achieve a beneficial therapeutic effect without adverse reactions
administer digitalis therapeutically
medical

Examples

The doctor decided to digitalize the patient after signs of heart failure appeared.
Older patients may be more sensitive when you digitalize them, so careful dosage is required.
It is important not to digitalize a patient with kidney impairment without close monitoring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to digitize - to convert information into a digital (computer-readable) form

Etymology

Formed from 'digitalis' (name of the drug) + '-ize', indicating action.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00081262-v
Synset POS: v
