lobate

1. ADJ

/ ˈloʊˌbeɪt / (AmE), / ˈləʊˌbeɪt / (BrE)
(of a leaf, organ, or structure) having deeply indented margins but with lobes not completely separated.
deeply indented, not fully separated lobes

Examples

The lobate leaves of the tree distinguish it from similar species.
Several mollusks present a lobate foot, adapted for burrowing in sand.
On the satellite image, the glacier's lobate margin is clearly visible against the darker rock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lobed - having lobes or divided into lobes

Etymology

From Latin *lobatus*, from *lobus* (a lobe).

2. ADJ

/ ˈloʊˌbeɪt / (AmE), / ˈləʊˌbeɪt / (BrE)
having or shaped like a lobe or lobes, especially with rounded projections or divisions
having lobes or resembling a lobe

Examples

The lobate leaves of the oak tree make it easy to identify.
Scientists observed the lobate tail of the marine organism under a microscope.
The geologist described the lobate flow patterns of the ancient lava field.

Synset Examples

a lobate tongue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lobed - having lobes; divided into lobes

Etymology

From Latin 'lobātus', from 'lobus' (lobe).
