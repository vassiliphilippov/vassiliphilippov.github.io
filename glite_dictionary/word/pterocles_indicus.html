Pterocles indicus

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɛrəˈkliz ɪnˈdaɪkəs / (AmE), / ˌtɛrəˈkliːz ɪnˈdɪkəs / (BrE)
A species of sandgrouse native to India, known for its adaptation to arid environments.
Indian species of sandgrouse

Examples

The Pterocles indicus is well adapted to surviving in the dry, open landscapes of central India.
Researchers observed a flock of Pterocles indicus at a remote watering hole during the dry season.
Due to habitat loss, conservationists are monitoring local populations of Pterocles indicus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sandgrouse - a family of birds adapted to arid regions

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; 'Pterocles' from Greek 'pteron' (feather) + '-cles', 'indicus' meaning 'of India'.
