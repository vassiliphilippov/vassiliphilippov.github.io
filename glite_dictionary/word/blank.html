blank

1. NOUN

/ blæŋk / (AmE), / blæŋk / (BrE)
a space character used to separate words in writing or printing
space character in writing/printing

Examples

Always leave a blank between each word when typing your essay.
Do not replace the blank with a tab or another character in the document.
The computer could not process the file because a blank was missing between words.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: space - a blank area separating words in text
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ blæŋk / (AmE), / blæŋk / (BrE)
(of a sports team or player) to prevent the opposing team from scoring or winning in a game.
prevent opposing team from scoring (sports)

Examples

The pitcher managed to blank the visiting team through nine impressive innings.
If we blank our rivals tonight, we’ll move to first place in the league.
Their defense has blanked every opponent this season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shut out - to prevent an opponent from scoring any points
Source: WordNet

3. NOUN

/ blæŋk / (AmE), / blæŋk / (BrE)
a space left empty or a missing part, especially in a printed or written document to be filled in later
an empty space or missing part

Examples

Please fill in the blank with your name and address.
There was a noticeable blank in the middle of the page where the picture was supposed to be.
She paused, searching for the right word, but her mind drew a blank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gap - an empty space or opening in something that is normally continuous
  • Synonym: space - an empty area or interval; unoccupied area
  • Synonym: hole - an opening or empty space in something solid

Etymology

From Old French blanc (white), from Frankish *blank (shining, white).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

5. NOUN

/ blæŋk / (AmE), / blaŋk / (BrE)
a cartridge for a firearm that contains an explosive charge but no bullet, used to produce a gunshot sound without firing a projectile
cartridge with no bullet

Examples

The actor fired a blank during the rehearsal to simulate the sound of gunfire.
Police sometimes use blanks in training exercises for realism without the danger of live ammunition.
He mistakenly believed the gun was loaded with a blank, but proper precautions were still taken.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: live round - a cartridge containing both a bullet and an explosive charge
