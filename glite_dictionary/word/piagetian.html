Piagetian

1. ADJ

/ piˈɑːʒətiən / (AmE), / piˈæʒətiən / (BrE)
Relating to the theories or methods of Jean Piaget, especially his theories of cognitive development in children.
of or relating to Jean Piaget's theories

Examples

Many educators adopt a Piagetian approach when designing activities for young children.
The research was grounded in Piagetian concepts of cognitive development.
He criticized some aspects of the Piagetian model as being too rigid.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Piagetian theory - the theory of cognitive development developed by Jean Piaget

Etymology

From the name of Swiss psychologist Jean Piaget (1896–1980) with the adjective suffix -ian.
