Taraxacum kok-saghyz

1. NOUN

/ təˌræksəkəm ˌkɑkˈsɑːɡjəz / (AmE), / təˌræksəkəm ˌkɒkˈsɑːɡjəz / (BrE)
a perennial dandelion native to Kazakhstan, cultivated for its fleshy roots that are high in rubber content
dandelion species used for rubber production

Examples

Researchers are studying Taraxacum kok-saghyz as a sustainable alternative source of natural rubber.
The Taraxacum kok-saghyz plant is well-suited to temperate climates with moderate rainfall.
Cultivation of Taraxacum kok-saghyz increased during periods of rubber shortages in the twentieth century.

Etymology

From Russian 'koksagyz' (kok = 'root', sagyz = 'rubber, gum').
