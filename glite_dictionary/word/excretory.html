excretory

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈskriːtəˌri / (AmE), / ɪkˈskriːtəri / (BrE)
relating to the process of excretion or the organs involved in removing waste from the body
relating to excretion
medical

Examples

The excretory system removes waste products from the body.
Certain diseases can affect excretory organs like the kidneys and bladder.
Doctors studied how toxins are filtered through the excretory pathways.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: secretory - relating to secretion of substances

Etymology

From Latin 'excretorius', from 'excretus' (separated, discharged).
