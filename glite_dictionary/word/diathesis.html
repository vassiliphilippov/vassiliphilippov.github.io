diathesis

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈæθəsɪs / (AmE), / daɪˈæθɪsɪs / (BrE)
a constitutional tendency or predisposition to develop a particular disease or abnormal condition
predisposition to a disease
medical

Examples

A family diathesis for allergies was evident in his medical history.
The patient’s bleeding diathesis made surgery particularly risky.
Recent studies highlight the importance of genetic diathesis in developing mental disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predisposition - a tendency to develop a particular condition or act in a particular way

Etymology

From Greek diathesis (disposition, arrangement), from dia- (through) + tithenai (to place).
