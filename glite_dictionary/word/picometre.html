picometre

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪkoʊˌmiːtər / (AmE), / ˈpɪkəˌmiːtə / (BrE)
a metric unit of length equal to one trillionth of a meter (10⁻¹² metres)
metric unit; one trillionth of a meter

Examples

The wavelength of X-rays can be measured in picometres.
Some atomic radii are as small as 50 picometres.
The bond length between certain atoms is less than 100 picometres.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: nanometre - a metric unit of length equal to one billionth of a meter
  • Degree Lower: femtometre - a metric unit of length equal to one quadrillionth of a meter

Etymology

From pico- (prefix meaning 'one trillionth') plus metre, from French 'pico-' and 'mètre'.
