to smatter

1. VERB

/ ˈsmætər / (AmE), / ˈsmætə / (BrE)
to speak, write, or otherwise use a subject or language with only superficial or slight knowledge
speak with superficial knowledge

Examples

Although he claims to know French, he only smatters a few phrases.
She smattered some Italian during her trip, but most locals responded in English.
Many tourists smatter local languages without mastering them.

Synset Examples

She smatters Russian

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smattering - a small amount of knowledge about a subject

Etymology

Probably from Middle Dutch *smateren* (to chatter, talk foolishly).

2. VERB

/ ˈsmæt̬ər / (AmE), / ˈsmætə / (BrE)
to engage in an activity or subject in a superficial or amateurish way, without deep knowledge or skill
dabble in a superficial way

Examples

Though he liked to smatter in several languages, he spoke none fluently.
She tends to smatter in various crafts without mastering any.
He smattered in painting during college, but never pursued it seriously.

Synset Examples

She dabbles in astronomy
He plays around with investments but he never makes any money

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dabble - to take part in an activity in a casual or superficial way

Etymology

From Middle English smatre, probably imitative of incoherent or indistinct speech; related to 'smattering'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02421160-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈsmætər / (AmE), / ˈsmætə / (BrE)
to talk in a superficial or foolish way, often about something one knows little about
talk superficially or foolishly
archaic

Examples

He tends to smatter about subjects he barely understands.
The children smattered on about nonsense all afternoon.
She would smatter opinions on every topic at the dinner table.

Synset Examples

The two women babbled and crooned at the baby

Related Terms

  • Synonym: babble - to talk rapidly and continuously in a foolish, excited, or incomprehensible way
  • Synonym: prattle - to talk in a foolish or inconsequential way
  • Antonym: to speak - to talk or utter words (contrasting with foolish or superficial speech)

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'smateren,' meaning to chatter.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01039522-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈsmætər / (AmE), / ˈsmætə / (BrE)
to speak or know a language or subject only slightly or superficially
speak or know only superficially

Examples

He could only smatter a few words in French when he visited Paris.
Though she tried to smatter some Spanish during her trip, most locals spoke English to her instead.
After years away from Italy, I could just smatter a greeting and order coffee.

Synset Examples

He could only smatter a few words in French.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dabble - to take part in an activity in a superficial way

Etymology

From Middle English smateren (to chatter, talk noisily); probably imitative in origin.
