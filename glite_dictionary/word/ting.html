ting

1. NOUN

/ tɪŋ / (AmE), / tɪŋ / (BrE)
a light, clear, metallic sound, like that made by a small bell or glass
light clear metallic sound

Examples

A gentle ting signaled that the elevator had arrived.
She heard the ting of a spoon tapping against a teacup.
The tiny bell made a soft ting as it swayed in the breeze.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: clang - a loud, resonant metallic sound
  • Synonym: ping - a short, sharp, high-pitched sound
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07412697-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ tɪŋ / (AmE), / tɪŋ / (BrE)
to cause to make a high-pitched, metallic ringing sound
make a metallic ringing sound

Examples

The small bell tinged as she opened the door to the shop.
He ran his finger along the edge of the glass to ting it softly.
A spoon struck the cup and made it ting lightly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ring - make a clear, resonant or vibrating sound

Etymology

Imitative of the sound itself.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02184706-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ tɪŋ / (AmE), / tɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a bell or similar object) to make a light, metallic ringing sound.
make a light, metallic sound

Examples

The tiny bell began to ting as the wind picked up.
A spoon tinged against the side of the glass.
She smiled as the charm on her bracelet tinged softly with each movement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tingle - to make or experience a series of light ringing sounds
  • Degree Higher: clang - to make a loud, resonant metallic sound
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02140484-v
Synset POS: v
