Giardia

1. NOUN

/ dʒiˈɑrdiə / (AmE), / dʒiˈɑːdiə / (BrE)
A genus of flagellated protozoan parasites that live in the intestines of vertebrates, some species of which cause gastrointestinal illness in humans.
genus of intestinal protozoan parasites
medical

Examples

Giardia infections are a common cause of waterborne gastrointestinal illness.
Scientists identified Giardia in the stool samples of several patients.
Treatment for Giardia typically involves specific antiparasitic medications.

Etymology

Named after Alfred Mathieu Giard, French biologist.
