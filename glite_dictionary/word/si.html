SI

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛsˈaɪ / (AmE), / ˌɛsˈʌɪ / (BrE)
the International System of Units; the modern scientific metric system used worldwide, based on fundamental units such as the meter, kilogram, and second
International System of Units (metric system)

Examples

Most scientific journals require all measurements to be reported in SI units.
The SI system helps standardize measurements across different countries and disciplines.
Engineers use SI units like newtons and joules for calculations and documentation.

Synset Examples

Today the United States is the only country in the world not totally committed to the Systeme International d'Unites

Related Terms

  • Synonym: metric system - system of measurement based on the meter, liter, and gram

Etymology

Abbreviation of French 'Système International (d'Unités)'.
