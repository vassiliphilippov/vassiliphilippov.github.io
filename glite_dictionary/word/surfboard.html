surfboard

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝːf.bɔːrd / (AmE), / ˈsɜːf.bɔːd / (BrE)
a long, narrow, buoyant board used for riding on the surface of waves in surfing
buoyant board used for surfing

Examples

He waxed his surfboard before heading out to catch the morning waves.
Carrying a bright yellow surfboard, she ran across the beach toward the water.
After his lesson, he proudly balanced on his surfboard for a few seconds.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: bodyboard - shorter board used for riding waves while lying down

Etymology

From 'surf' (sea waves) + 'board' (flat piece of material); first recorded in late 19th century Hawaii.

2. VERB

/ ˈsɝːf.bɔrd / (AmE), / ˈsɜːf.bɔːd / (BrE)
to ride on waves in the sea while standing or lying on a surfboard
ride waves on a surfboard

Examples

She loves to surfboard every morning when the waves are high.
We watched the athletes surfboarding during the competition.
They surfboarded along the coast until sunset.

Synset Examples

Californians love to surf

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surf - to ride on ocean waves, typically using a board
