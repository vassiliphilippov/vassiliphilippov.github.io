Dovyalis

1. PROPN

/ doʊˈvaɪ.əlɪs / (AmE), / dəʊˈvaɪ.əlɪs / (BrE)
A small genus of sometimes spiny shrubs or small trees native to Africa, India, and Sri Lanka, some species of which bear edible fruit.
genus of spiny shrubs or small trees (Africa, India, Sri Lanka)

Examples

The Dovyalis genus includes several species that produce edible fruits prized in local markets.
Botanists discovered a new species of Dovyalis in the forests of Sri Lanka.
The sharp thorns of Dovyalis shrubs provide protection against grazing animals.

Etymology

From Greek 'dóry' (spear) and 'hálys' (thorn), referring to the spiny branches.
