Chlamyphorus

1. NOUN

/ klæˈmɪfərəs / (AmE), / klæˈmɪfərəs / (BrE)
A genus of small burrowing armadillos native to central Argentina, known as the pink fairy armadillo.
genus of small burrowing armadillos

Examples

The Chlamyphorus is also known as the pink fairy armadillo due to its pale coloration.
Scientists study Chlamyphorus to better understand the adaptation of mammals to underground life.
Unlike other armadillos, Chlamyphorus spends most of its life digging in sandy plains.

Etymology

From Greek 'chlamys' (cloak) + 'phoros' (bearing), referring to their cloak-like shell.
