handkerchief

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæŋkɚˌtʃiːf / (AmE), / ˈhæŋkətʃɪf / (BrE)
A small square piece of cloth used for wiping the nose, eyes, or face, or worn as a decorative accessory.
cloth for wiping nose or face

Examples

He pulled a handkerchief from his pocket and offered it to her when she started to cry.
She always carries a silk handkerchief as part of her outfit.
After sneezing, he discreetly used his handkerchief to wipe his nose.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tissue - soft disposable paper for wiping nose or eyes

Etymology

From hand + kerchief; 'kerchief' from Old French 'couvrechief', from 'couvrir' (to cover) and 'chef' (head).
