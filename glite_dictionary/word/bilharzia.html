bilharzia

1. NOUN

/ bɪlˈhɑːrziə / (AmE), / bɪlˈhɑːzɪə / (BrE)
A disease caused by infection with parasitic worms of the genus Schistosoma, common in tropical regions and characterized by symptoms depending on the site of infection.
parasitic disease caused by Schistosoma
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the patient with bilharzia after he returned from a trip to Africa.
Chronic bilharzia can lead to severe liver and bladder problems if left untreated.
Awareness campaigns have helped reduce the spread of bilharzia in many rural communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: schistosomiasis - another term for bilharzia; disease caused by Schistosoma infection

Etymology

From New Latin Bilharzia after Theodor Bilharz, the German physician who discovered the parasite.
