rial

1. NOUN

/ riˈɑl / (AmE), / riˈɑːl / (BrE)
the basic unit of currency in Iran, equal to 100 dinars
Iranian unit of currency

Examples

The price was listed as 10,000 rial for a cup of tea.
Many Iranians still think in terms of tomans, even though the official currency is the rial.
She exchanged her dollars for rials at the airport.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toman - a former unit of currency in Iran, equal to ten rials

Etymology

From Arabic ريـال‎ (riyāl), originally from the Spanish 'real' (royal), a historic coin.

2. NOUN

/ riˈɑl / (AmE), / riˈɑːl / (BrE)
the basic monetary unit of Yemen, equal to 100 fils.
currency unit of Yemen

Examples

The price of bread increased when the rial lost value against the dollar.
Tourists exchanged their dollars for Yemeni rials at the airport.
The new policy introduced a redesigned rial note to reduce counterfeiting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: riyal - the basic unit of currency in Iran, Oman, and several other countries

Etymology

From Arabic 'riyal', itself from Spanish 'real' (royal).

3. NOUN

/ riˈɑl / (AmE), / riˈɑːl / (BrE)
the basic monetary unit of Oman, equal to 1,000 baisa.
Omani currency unit

Examples

The value of the Omani rial is strong compared to many neighboring currencies.
She exchanged her dollars for rials at the airport upon arrival in Muscat.
Many hotels in Oman accept payment in both rial and major international currencies.

Etymology

From Arabic 'riyāl', itself from Spanish 'real' (a historical currency).
