gildhall

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɪld.hɔl / (AmE), / ˈɡɪld.hɔːl / (BrE)
the building or room used as the meeting place of a medieval guild, especially for official or social functions
medieval guild meeting place
archaic

Examples

The annual banquet was held in the grand gildhall at the center of town.
Medieval craftsmen would gather in the gildhall to discuss trade matters and settle disputes.
The old gildhall stands as a reminder of the city's rich mercantile history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guildhall - hall or building used for meetings by a guild

Etymology

From 'gild' (variant of 'guild', a medieval association of craftsmen or merchants) + 'hall' (a large room or building).
