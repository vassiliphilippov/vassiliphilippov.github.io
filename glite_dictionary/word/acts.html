Acts

1. PROPN

/ æks / (AmE), / æks / (BrE)
the fifth book of the New Testament, describing the early history of the Christian Church after Jesus's Ascension
New Testament book about the early Christian Church

Examples

Many scholars study Acts to understand the development of early Christianity.
The story of Paul's conversion can be found in Acts.
In his sermon, the pastor quoted a passage from Acts.

Etymology

From the Latin 'Acta Apostolorum' (Acts of the Apostles).
