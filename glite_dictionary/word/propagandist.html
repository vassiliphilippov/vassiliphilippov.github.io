propagandist

1. ADJ

/ ˌprɑː.pəˈɡæn.dɪst / (AmE), / ˌprɒ.pəˈɡæn.dɪst / (BrE)
relating to, or characteristic of, propaganda; promoting or spreading propaganda.
characterized by or relating to propaganda

Examples

The article offered a propagandist account of the historical event, ignoring important facts.
His films are often criticized for their propagandist content and lack of objectivity.
The speaker's propagandist tone made it clear which side he supported.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: propagandistic - relating to, or characteristic of propaganda

Etymology

From French 'propagandiste', from 'propagande' (propaganda).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03034253-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ ˌprɑː.pəˈɡæn.dɪst / (AmE), / ˌprɒ.pəˈɡæn.dɪst / (BrE)
a person who spreads information, especially biased or misleading information, to promote a particular political cause or point of view
person who spreads propaganda

Examples

The government hired a propagandist to influence public opinion during the campaign.
She was accused of being a propagandist for the regime after publishing that article.
As a skilled propagandist, he could sway crowds with persuasive rhetoric.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: advocate - a person who publicly supports or recommends a particular cause or policy
  • Antonym: opponent - a person who disagrees with or resists a cause or government

Etymology

From French 'propagandiste', derived from 'propaganda' (Latin 'propagare', to spread).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10502506-n
Synset POS: n
