disheartened

1. ADJ

/ dɪsˈhɑrtənd / (AmE), / dɪsˈhɑːtənd / (BrE)
Having lost hope, confidence, or enthusiasm; made discouraged.
made discouraged; lost enthusiasm

Examples

After receiving several rejection letters, he felt completely disheartened.
The disheartened team struggled to find motivation after their defeat.
She looked disheartened when her idea was dismissed by the group.

Synset Examples

desperate demoralized people looking for work
felt discouraged by the magnitude of the problem
the disheartened instructor tried vainly to arouse their interest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discouraged - having lost confidence or enthusiasm
  • Synonym: demoralized - having lost confidence or hope
  • Antonym: encouraged - having more hope or confidence

Etymology

From 'dishearten' (prefix 'dis-' expressing reversal + 'hearten').
