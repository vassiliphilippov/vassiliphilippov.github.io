to expedite

1. VERB

/ ˈɛk.spəˌdaɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛk.spɪ.daɪt / (BrE)
to make a process or action happen more quickly; to speed up or facilitate
make happen more quickly

Examples

The new software will expedite data processing for our team.
We hired additional staff to expedite the completion of the project.
If you want to expedite your application, please submit all required documents on time.

Synset Examples

This should expedite the process

Related Terms

  • Antonym: delay - to make something happen more slowly or later than planned
  • Synonym: to hasten - to cause something to happen sooner or more quickly

Etymology

From Latin 'expeditus' (unimpeded, ready, quick), from 'expedire' (to free, to prepare).

2. VERB

/ ˈɛk.spəˌdaɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛk.spɪ.daɪt / (BrE)
to make a process or action happen more quickly or efficiently
make happen more quickly

Examples

The company hired extra staff to expedite the delivery of goods during the holidays.
We hope the new software will expedite data processing and improve efficiency.
She called customer service to expedite the resolution of her complaint.

Synset Examples

I will try to expedite the matter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hasten - to make something happen sooner or more quickly
  • Antonym: delay - to make something happen later than planned

Etymology

From Latin expedire (to free, expedite).
