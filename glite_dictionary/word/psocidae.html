Psocidae

1. NOUN

/ soʊˈsɪdiː / (AmE), / sɒˈsɪdɪi / (BrE)
A family of small, soft-bodied insects that feed on decaying vegetation and are closely related to booklice.
family of insects related to booklice

Examples

Scientists discovered a new species within the Psocidae family in South America.
Members of the Psocidae are often found in damp environments, where they consume decaying plant material.
Unlike true lice, Psocidae do not bite humans and are harmless to books unless in large numbers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: booklouse - a small insect that feeds on mold or starchy materials, often found in old books

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Psocidae', based on the type genus 'Psocus', from Greek 'psōkos' (gnawed, rubbed).

2. NOUN

/ soʊˈsaɪdiː / (AmE), / sɒˈsɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of small, soft-bodied insects that feed on decaying vegetation, related to booklice.
family of insects related to booklice

Examples

Scientists recently discovered a new species belonging to the Psocidae family in the rainforest.
Members of the Psocidae are often found feeding on decaying leaves and bark.
Unlike termites, Psocidae insects are harmless to buildings and mainly eat decomposing plant material.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: booklouse - a small insect that feeds on paper and book bindings

Etymology

From New Latin Psocidae, based on the type genus Psocus.
