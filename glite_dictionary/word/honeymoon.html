honeymoon

1. NOUN

/ ˈhʌniˌmun / (AmE), / ˈhʌniːˌmuːn / (BrE)
a holiday taken by a newly married couple, usually immediately after their wedding, to celebrate their marriage
holiday for newlyweds

Examples

They traveled to the Maldives for their honeymoon after the wedding.
Many couples choose a romantic destination for their honeymoon.
Their honeymoon was filled with adventure and relaxation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wedding - a marriage ceremony and its associated celebrations

Etymology

From honey (sweet) + moon (month), originally referring to the first month of marriage.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhʌniˌmun / (AmE), / ˈhʌn.i.muːn / (BrE)
a period at the beginning of a relationship, business, or political arrangement when relations are especially good and harmonious
initial harmonious period of a relationship

Examples

The new CEO is enjoying a honeymoon phase, but tough decisions will come soon.
Their political alliance experienced a brief honeymoon before disagreements emerged.
At the start of the project, everyone was optimistic during the honeymoon period.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: golden age - a period of great happiness or prosperity
  • Antonym: trial - a period of difficulty or testing

Etymology

From Old English *hony* (honey) + *moone* (moon), originally referring to the first month of marriage.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-15319031-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈhʌniˌmun / (AmE), / ˈhʌn.i.muːn / (BrE)
(of a couple) to spend a holiday together just after getting married.
to go on a post-wedding holiday

Examples

They decided to honeymoon in the Maldives, enjoying the tropical beaches after their wedding.
Many couples choose to honeymoon abroad for a unique experience.
After the ceremony, Sarah and Tom honeymooned in the mountains to escape the crowds.

Synset Examples

they plan to honeymoon in Hawai'i

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vacation - a period of leisure travel or relaxation, especially away from home

Etymology

From 'honeymoon' (noun), originally referring to the first month after marriage, from Middle English 'hony moone'.

Synset POS: v
