weisenheimer

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪzənˌhaɪmər / (AmE), / ˈwaɪzənˌhaɪmə / (BrE)
a person who makes conceited, sarcastic, or insolent remarks; a smart-aleck
a smart-aleck; insolent person
informal

Examples

Everyone in class was annoyed by the new weisenheimer who constantly interrupted the teacher with sarcastic comments.
The boss doesn't appreciate a weisenheimer who thinks he knows better than everyone else.
Don't be such a weisenheimer; nobody likes a know-it-all at parties.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smart-aleck - a person who is irritating because they act as if they know everything
  • Synonym: wise guy - someone who makes sarcastic or clever remarks; a know-it-all

Etymology

From German surname 'Weisenheimer', adapted in American English slang in early 20th century, possibly influenced by (wise) + mock German name ending (-heimer).
