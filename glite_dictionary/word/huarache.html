huarache

1. NOUN

/ wɑˈrɑːtʃeɪ / (AmE), / wəˈratʃeɪ / (BrE)
A sandal with flat heels and an upper made of woven leather straps, traditionally worn in Mexico.
Mexican woven leather sandal

Examples

She bought a pair of handmade huaraches at the local market in Oaxaca.
The huarache is known for its flexible sole and comfortable fit.
He wore huaraches during the summer because they kept his feet cool.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sandal - A light shoe with an open upper, typically held on by straps

Etymology

From Mexican Spanish huarache, from Purépecha (Tarascan) warachi (sandal).
