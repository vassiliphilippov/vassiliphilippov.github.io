to unitise

1. VERB

/ ˈjuː.nəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈjuː.nɪ.taɪz / (BrE)
to divide bulk material and process it as discrete units, often for shipping, storage, or handling purposes
divide bulk material into units

Examples

Modern packaging systems often unitise products for more efficient shipping.
The company decided to unitise raw materials to streamline the production process.
It is safer and more cost-effective to unitise heavy goods before transportation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to unitize - U.S. spelling of 'unitise' (divide bulk material into units)

Etymology

From 'unit' + '-ise', modeled after similar English verbs.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02474216-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈjuːnəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈjuːnɪtʌɪz / (BrE)
to make or form into a single unit or set of units
make into a unit or units
british

Examples

The manufacturer decided to unitise its products for easier shipping and storage.
Efforts to unitise the oil fields improved operational efficiency and cooperation between companies.
The design team will unitise the various components into one standard module.

Synset Examples

unitize a car body

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to unitize - to make or form into a unit or units (American spelling)

Etymology

From 'unit' + '-ise', after the pattern of verbs meaning 'to make into', first used in the 20th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01388112-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈjuːnɪtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈjuːnɪtaɪz / (BrE)
to separate or classify into units; to organize something into distinct parts or sections
separate into units or sections
british

Examples

Management decided to unitise the production process to improve workflow.
The data was unitised into manageable sections for easier analysis.
There are plans to unitise various hospital departments for greater efficiency.

Synset Examples

The hospital was unitized for efficiency

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to unitize - American spelling of 'unitise'

Etymology

From "unit" + "-ise", modeled after similar words like "organise"; chiefly British spelling of "unitize".

Source: WordNet
