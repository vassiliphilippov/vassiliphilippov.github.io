arteria buccalis

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈtɪriə bʊˈkeɪlɪs / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪərɪə bʊˈkɑːlɪs / (BrE)
A branch of the maxillary artery that supplies blood to the buccinator muscle and the cheek.
artery supplying cheek and buccinator muscle
medical

Examples

The arteria buccalis is essential for delivering oxygenated blood to the tissues of the cheek.
During dental surgery, care must be taken not to damage the arteria buccalis.
An injury to the arteria buccalis can lead to significant bleeding in the cheek area.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: maxillary artery - main artery supplying structures of the maxilla

Etymology

From Latin; 'arteria' meaning 'artery' and 'buccalis' relating to the cheek.
