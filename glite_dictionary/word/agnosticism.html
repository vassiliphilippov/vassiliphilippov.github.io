agnosticism

1. NOUN

/ æɡˈnɑstɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / æɡˈnɒstɪˌsɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief that it is impossible to know whether ultimate knowledge, especially regarding the existence of God, is attainable
belief that ultimate knowledge is unattainable

Examples

His interest in philosophy led him to embrace agnosticism rather than commit to any particular faith.
Discussions about religion often include debates between theism, atheism, and agnosticism.
She explained her agnosticism as an honest admission of uncertainty rather than denial.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: atheism - the belief that there is no God

Etymology

From 'agnostic' (coined by Thomas Huxley, 1869) + -ism; from Greek 'agnōstos' (unknown, unknowable).

2. NOUN

/ æɡˈnɑstɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / æɡˈnɒstɪˌsɪzəm / (BrE)
The belief that ultimate knowledge of the existence or nature of God or the divine is impossible, or that such knowledge is unknown.
belief that God's existence is unknowable

Examples

Some people adopt agnosticism because they think the question of God's existence cannot be answered.
His journey from strict religious belief to agnosticism was shaped by years of philosophical inquiry.
The professor explained the distinction between atheism and agnosticism during the lecture.

Synset Examples

agnosticism holds that you can neither prove nor disprove God's existence

Related Terms

  • Antonym: atheism - the belief that God does not exist
  • Antonym: theism - belief in the existence of a god or gods

Etymology

From agnostic + -ism; agnostic from Greek 'a-' (not) + 'gnōsis' (knowledge).
