Erythrina corallodendrum

1. NOUN

/ ɪˌrɪθrɪnə kəˌrɑːləˈdɛndrəm / (AmE), / ɪˌrɪθrɪnə kəˌrɒlədɛnˈdrʌm / (BrE)
A species of deciduous shrub or small tree, native to the Caribbean, notable for its showy deep red flowers arranged in racemes and its red seeds marked with black spots.
Caribbean shrub with red flowers and seeds

Examples

The Erythrina corallodendrum adds a vibrant splash of color to tropical gardens.
Gardeners prize Erythrina corallodendrum for its striking red racemes and unique seeds.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of Erythrina corallodendrum leaves and flowers.

Etymology

From Greek *erythros* (red), referring to the flower color; genus name from Latinized Greek; specific epithet from Greek *korallion* (coral) and *dendron* (tree).
