coronion

1. NOUN

/ kəˈroʊniən / (AmE), / kəˈrəʊniən / (BrE)
the craniometric point at the tip of the coronoid process of the mandible
craniometric point on the mandible
medical

Examples

The surgeon precisely located the coronion before making an incision along the mandible.
The measurement from the coronion to the gonion is critical in anthropological studies.
In forensic examinations, the coronion serves as a reference point for jaw reconstruction.

Etymology

From Greek korōnē (anything curved, crow) + -ion (diminutive suffix), referring to the coronoid process in anatomy.
