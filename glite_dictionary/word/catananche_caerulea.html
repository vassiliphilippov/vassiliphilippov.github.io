Catananche caerulea

1. NOUN

/ kəˌtænənˌki səˈruːliə / (AmE), / kəˌtanankeɪ sɪəˈruːlɪə / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to southern Europe, known for its blue, daisy-like flowers with dark centers.
European plant with blue flowers

Examples

The garden featured several Catananche caerulea plants, admired for their striking blue petals.
Many florists appreciate Catananche caerulea for its ability to thrive in dry, sunny locations.
Dried flowers of Catananche caerulea are often used in decorative arrangements.

Etymology

From scientific Latin, with 'Catananche' as the genus and 'caerulea' from Latin meaning 'blue'.
