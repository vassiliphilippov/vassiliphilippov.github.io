anemograph

1. NOUN

/ əˈnɛm.əˌɡræf / (AmE), / əˈniː.mə.ɡrɑːf / (BrE)
An instrument for measuring and recording the speed and direction of the wind.
instrument that records wind speed and direction

Examples

The meteorology station uses an anemograph to continuously track wind patterns.
The anemograph produced a detailed chart showing gusts during the storm.
Technicians regularly calibrate the anemograph for accurate wind measurements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anemometer - instrument for measuring wind speed

Etymology

From Greek anemos (wind) + -graph (writing or recording instrument).
