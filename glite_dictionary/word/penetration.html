penetration

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛnəˈtreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɛnɪˈtreɪʃən / (BrE)
a military attack that breaks through an enemy's defenses and enters their territory
attack entering enemy territory

Examples

The army's unexpected penetration of the frontline changed the course of the battle.
Successful penetration of enemy territory requires careful planning and coordination.
Reports described a deep penetration into hostile areas by the special forces unit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incursion - a sudden attack or invasion into another's territory

Etymology

From Latin *penetratio(n-)*, from *penetrare* (to enter, pierce).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɛnəˈtreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɛnɪˈtreɪʃən / (BrE)
the depth to which something passes into or through another substance, especially the distance a projectile enters a target
depth something penetrates, e.g. a projectile

Examples

The bullet's penetration was measured at eighteen inches in ballistic gel.
Armor thickness is designed to reduce penetration from high-velocity rounds.
Engineers tested the penetration of different materials under controlled impact.

Etymology

From Latin penetratio(n-), from penetrare 'to enter, go into'.
