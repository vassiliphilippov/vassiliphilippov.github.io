Perdix perdix

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɜrdɪks ˈpɜrdɪks / (AmE), / ˈpɜːdɪks ˈpɜːdɪks / (BrE)
A species of partridge found widely across Europe and parts of Asia, known as the common partridge or gray partridge.
common European partridge species

Examples

The Perdix perdix is well adapted to farmland and grassy plains across Europe.
Ornithologists are concerned about the declining populations of Perdix perdix in several countries.
We spotted a covey of Perdix perdix feeding on seeds at the edge of the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: partridge - a medium-sized game bird with a plump body, related to pheasants

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Perdix' is the genus name for partridges.
