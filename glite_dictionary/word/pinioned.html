pinioned

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɪnjənd / (AmE), / ˈpɪnjənd / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) bound fast, especially with the arms or wings restrained or tied.
having arms or limbs tightly bound

Examples

The prisoner sat pinioned to the chair, unable to move his arms.
With her hands pinioned behind her back, she could not escape.
He felt utterly helpless, lying pinioned by the ropes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bound - tied up or fastened tightly
  • Antonym: unbound - not tied or restrained

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'pinion', from Old French *pignon* (wing), from Latin *penna* (feather).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɪnjənd / (AmE), / ˈpɪnjənd / (BrE)
(of birds) having had the flight feathers removed or bound to prevent flying.
having clipped or bound wings (birds)

Examples

The duck had been pinioned to prevent it from flying away from the pond.
With pinioned wings, the eagle could no longer soar above the mountains.
Zoo staff often keep birds pinioned for their safety and that of the visitors.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: unpinioned - not having wings clipped or bound

Etymology

From 'pinion', meaning 'a bird's wing', from Old French pignon, from Latin *pinna* (feather, wing).
