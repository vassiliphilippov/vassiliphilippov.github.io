co-ed

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊ ɛd / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ ɛd / (BrE)
a female student at a coeducational college or university, especially in the United States
female student at coeducational college
american

Examples

She was the only co-ed in her engineering class that year.
The fraternity planned a party and invited every co-ed from the dormitory.
In the 1920s, being a co-ed was considered quite progressive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: student - a person enrolled in a school or college

Etymology

Shortened form of 'coeducational,' originally referring to students at coeducational institutions.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkoʊˌɛd / (AmE), / ˈkəʊˌɛd / (BrE)
Attended by members of both sexes; open to both male and female participants.
attended by both sexes

Examples

The university recently became a co-ed institution after years of single-sex education.
They live in a co-ed dormitory, sharing common spaces with both men and women.
Many sports clubs are now co-ed to encourage participation from everyone.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: single-sex - attended by members of only one sex
  • Synonym: coeducational - relating to education for both sexes together

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'coeducational', from Latin 'co-' (together) + 'educare' (to educate).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
