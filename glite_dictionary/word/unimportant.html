unimportant

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌnɪmˈpɔrtnt / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪmˈpɔːtənt / (BrE)
Not significant or worthy of attention; lacking importance.
not significant; lacking importance

Examples

His contribution to the project was rather unimportant compared to others.
Please ignore any unimportant details in the report.
She realized that her worries were largely unimportant in the grand scheme of things.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: important - of great significance or value

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'important'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌnɪmˈpɔrtənt / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪmˈpɔːtənt / (BrE)
Not important; lacking significance or consequence.
not important; insignificant

Examples

Despite her mistake, it was just an unimportant detail in the final report.
He tried to ignore the unimportant distractions and focus on his studies.
Many of the emails he receives each day are unimportant and quickly deleted.

Synset Examples

a relatively unimportant feature of the system
the question seems unimportant

Related Terms

  • Antonym: important - having great significance or value
  • Synonym: insignificant - too small or unimportant to be worth consideration
  • Synonym: irrelevant - not connected with or relevant to something

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (not) + 'important', from Latin 'importans' (significant).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01283088-a
Synset POS: a
