Micropogonias undulatus

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪkroʊpoʊˈɡoʊniəs ˌʌndjʊˈleɪtəs / (AmE), / ˌmɪk.rə.pəˈɡəʊ.ni.æs ˌʌn.djuːˈleɪ.təs / (BrE)
A silvery-bodied species of croaker fish with dark markings and tiny barbels, commonly known as the Atlantic croaker.
species of silvery croaker fish

Examples

Fishermen often catch Micropogonias undulatus along the shores of the Atlantic coast.
The distinctive drumming sound helps identify Micropogonias undulatus during spawning season.
Biologists studied the feeding habits of Micropogonias undulatus in estuarine environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Atlantic croaker - common English name for Micropogonias undulatus

Etymology

From Greek 'mikros' (small) + 'pōgōn' (beard) and Latin 'undulatus' (wavy).
