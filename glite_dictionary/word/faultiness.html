faultiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔl.ti.nəs / (AmE), / ˈfɔːl.ti.nɪs / (BrE)
The state or quality of being faulty or defective.
state of being defective

Examples

The faultiness of the machine was quickly discovered during safety testing.
Critics pointed out the faultiness in the report's logic and evidence.
Repeated complaints highlighted the faultiness in the product's design.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fault - an imperfection or defect
  • Antonym: perfection - the state of being perfect, without flaws

Etymology

From 'faulty' + '-ness'. 'Faulty' from Old French 'faute' (lack, deficiency) and Latin 'fallita' (deceived).
