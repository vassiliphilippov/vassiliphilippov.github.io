Chari-Nile

1. NOUN

/ ˌʃɑriˈnaɪl / (AmE), / ˌʃɑːriˈnaɪl / (BrE)
a group of Nilo-Saharan languages spoken in parts of Sudan, the Democratic Republic of the Congo (formerly Zaire), Uganda, and Tanzania
group of Nilo-Saharan languages

Examples

The Chari-Nile languages are classified as a branch of the Nilo-Saharan language family.
Many communities in central Africa speak languages belonging to the Chari-Nile group.
Researchers have published new findings on the migration patterns of Chari-Nile speaking peoples.

Etymology

Named after the Chari and Nile rivers, whose regions correspond to the languages' distribution.
