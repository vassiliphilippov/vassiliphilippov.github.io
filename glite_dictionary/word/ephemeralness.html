ephemeralness

1. NOUN

/ əˈfɛmərəlnəs / (AmE), / ɪˈfɛm(ə)rəlnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of lasting for a very short period of time
quality of being short-lived

Examples

The ephemeralness of the rainbow made it all the more magical to behold.
She pondered the ephemeralness of childhood moments as her son grew up so quickly.
Philosophers have long debated the ephemeralness of human happiness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ephemerality - the property of being short-lived or transient
  • Antonym: permanence - the state of lasting indefinitely or remaining unchanged

Etymology

From Greek 'ephēmeros' (lasting a day) + English suffix '-ness'.
