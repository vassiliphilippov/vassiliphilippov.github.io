touchwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʌtʃˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈtʌtʃˌwʊd / (BrE)
dry, easily combustible material, such as decayed wood, used for starting a fire
material for starting a fire

Examples

The campers gathered some touchwood to help start their fire in the damp forest.
Dry touchwood is essential for lighting a fire with only a flint and steel.
Old tree stumps often supply plenty of touchwood for survivalists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tinder - material for catching fire from a spark

Etymology

From 'touch', meaning to ignite, and 'wood', referring to the material.
