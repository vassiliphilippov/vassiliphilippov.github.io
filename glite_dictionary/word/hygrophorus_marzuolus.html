Hygrophorus marzuolus

1. NOUN

/ haɪˌɡrɑːfəˌrəs mɑrˈzuːələs / (AmE), / haɪˌɡrɒfəˌrəs mɑːˈzjuːələs / (BrE)
a species of grey mushroom often found growing near melting snow banks in mountainous regions
a grey mushroom species found near melting snow

Examples

The rare Hygrophorus marzuolus often appears in the forest just as the winter snow begins to melt.
Mushroom hunters search for Hygrophorus marzuolus in early spring near receding snow banks.
Finding a patch of Hygrophorus marzuolus is considered a lucky discovery by many foragers.

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'hygros' (moist) + 'phoros' (bearing); species epithet from Latin 'Marzo' (March), indicating the season it appears.
