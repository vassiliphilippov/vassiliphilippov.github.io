palimony

1. NOUN

/ ˈpælɪˌmoʊni / (AmE), / ˈpælɪməni / (BrE)
Money paid by one partner to another after the end of a long-term, non-marital relationship, similar to alimony for married couples.
support paid after an unmarried partnership ends
legal american

Examples

After their separation, she filed a lawsuit seeking palimony for the years they lived together.
The court awarded him palimony because he had financially supported his partner.
Many legal systems do not recognize palimony claims in the same way as alimony.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alimony - money paid to a former spouse after divorce

Etymology

Blend of 'pal' (friend) and 'alimony', first used in the 1970s in the United States.
