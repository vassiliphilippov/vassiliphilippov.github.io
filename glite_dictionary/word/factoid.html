factoid

1. NOUN

/ ˈfæk.tɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈfæk.tɔɪd / (BrE)
an item of information that appears to be factual but is unverified, inaccurate, or invented, especially when accepted as true because it has been published or repeated widely
unverified or invented information appearing factual

Examples

Despite being repeated in several articles, the supposed quote turned out to be just a factoid.
He loves sharing strange factoids he finds on the internet, but many are actually unverified.
The book is full of factoids that sound believable but lack any supporting evidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: myth - a widely held but false belief or idea
  • Synonym: rumor - unverified information spread informally
  • Antonym: fact - a thing that is known or proved to be true

Etymology

Coined by Norman Mailer, from 'fact' + suffix '-oid' (resembling).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfæk.tɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈfæk.tɔɪd / (BrE)
A brief or trivial piece of information, especially one presented as a concise news item.
brief or trivial piece of information

Examples

The magazine included a factoid about how honey never spoils.
Did you know this factoid: the inventor of the Frisbee was turned into a Frisbee?
During the presentation, she shared an interesting factoid about the origins of pizza.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trivia - pieces of information that are interesting but not important

Etymology

Coined in the 1970s by American writer Norman Mailer, from 'fact' + the suffix '-oid' (meaning resembling or like).
