metycaine

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛtɪˌkeɪn / (AmE), / ˈmɛtɪˌkeɪn / (BrE)
a compound used as a local or spinal anesthetic, commonly in the form of its hydrochloride
a local or spinal anesthetic compound
medical

Examples

The surgeon administered metycaine before beginning the procedure to ensure the area was numb.
Allergic reactions to metycaine are rare but must be monitored during spinal anesthesia.
Researchers are testing new formulations of metycaine to improve its effectiveness and safety.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anesthetic - a substance that causes loss of sensation
