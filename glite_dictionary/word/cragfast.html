cragfast

1. ADJ

/ ˈkræɡˌfæst / (AmE), / ˈkræɡˌfɑːst / (BrE)
unable to move from a position on a steep rock face or crag, typically due to fear or difficulty climbing.
stranded on a crag or rock face

Examples

The hiker became cragfast halfway up the sheer cliff and called for help.
Rescuers arrived quickly to assist several cragfast climbers on the mountain.
She remained cragfast, unable to ascend or descend the narrow ledge.

Synset Examples

cragfast climbers

Etymology

Formed from 'crag' (a steep rock or cliff) and 'fast' (fixed or stuck in place).
