narwhale

1. NOUN

/ ˈnɑrˌweɪl / (AmE), / ˈnɑːwheɪl / (BrE)
a small Arctic whale, the male of which has a long, spiral ivory tusk projecting from its head
Arctic whale with spiral tusk

Examples

The narwhale is often called the 'unicorn of the sea' because of its distinctive tusk.
A male narwhale can have a tusk that grows up to three meters long.
Scientists study narwhales to learn more about how Arctic animals adapt to extreme conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: narwhal - a small Arctic whale with a long tusk

Etymology

Alteration of narwhal, from Danish 'narhval', from Old Norse 'nár' (corpse) and 'hvalr' (whale).
