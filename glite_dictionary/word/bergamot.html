bergamot

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɝː.ɡə.mɑt / (AmE), / ˈbɜː.ɡə.mɒt / (BrE)
A small Mediterranean citrus tree (Citrus bergamia) with aromatic pear-shaped fruit, whose oil is used in perfumery and as a flavoring, especially in Earl Grey tea.
Mediterranean citrus tree, fruit used in perfumes/teas

Examples

The distinctive scent of bergamot fills the air of the perfumery.
She prefers her tea with a hint of bergamot for its refreshing flavor.
The essential oil extracted from bergamot is prized in aromatherapy.

Etymology

From Italian 'bergamotto', possibly via Turkish from the town of Bergama.
