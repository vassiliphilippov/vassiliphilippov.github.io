aqua vitae

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːkwə ˈviːtaɪ / (AmE), / ˌakwə ˈviːtaɪ / (BrE)
A strong distilled spirit, such as brandy; in historical or literary use, any form of concentrated alcoholic liquor.
strong distilled spirit or brandy
archaic

Examples

In medieval times, doctors prescribed aqua vitae as a cure-all for many ailments.
He drank a small glass of aqua vitae to steady his nerves before the speech.
The historian described aqua vitae as an early form of distilled alcoholic beverage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brandy - a strong alcoholic drink distilled from wine or fermented fruit juice

Etymology

From Latin 'aqua vitae', meaning 'water of life'.
