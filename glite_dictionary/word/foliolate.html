foliolate

1. ADJ

/ ˈfoʊlioʊˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈfəʊliəˌleɪt / (BrE)
(chiefly in botany) having leaflets; having compound leaves composed of a specified number or type of leaflets, often used in combination.
having leaflets (botany)

Examples

The leaves of this species are foliolate, each composed of three distinct leaflets.
Many legumes possess foliolate foliage, making identification easier for botanists.
As the plant matures, foliolate structures become more prominent.

Synset Examples

‘foliolate’ is combined with the prefix ‘bi’ to form the word ‘bifoliolate’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compound-leaved - having leaves composed of several distinct leaflets

Etymology

From Latin foliolatus, from foliolum (diminutive of folium ‘leaf’).
