Parkinsonia

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɑrkɪnˈsoʊniə / (AmE), / ˌpɑːkɪnˈsəʊniə / (BrE)
A small genus of spiny shrubs or small trees, commonly found in arid and semi-arid regions, known for their green bark and yellow flowers.
genus of spiny shrubs or small trees

Examples

The Parkinsonia tree is a common sight in desert landscapes around the world.
Botanists identified the shrub as belonging to the genus Parkinsonia.
Many birds build their nests in the branches of Parkinsonia trees.

Etymology

Named after English botanist John Parkinson (1567–1650).
