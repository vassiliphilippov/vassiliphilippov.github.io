nitril

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪtrɪl / (AmE), / ˈnɪtrɪl / (BrE)
any of a class of organic compounds containing the cyano group (-CN) bonded to a carbon atom.
organic compound with a cyano group (-CN)
medical

Examples

The laboratory synthesized a new nitril to test for its potential as a solvent.
Many polymers, such as nitrile rubber, are made using nitril compounds.
Exposure to some nitril substances can be hazardous if proper safety gear is not worn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nitrile - class of organic compounds containing the -CN group

Etymology

From German *Nitril*, coined from nitro- and -il (suffix denoting chemical derivatives).
