blastocytoma

1. NOUN

/ ˌblæs.toʊ.saɪˈtoʊ.mə / (AmE), / ˌblɑː.stəʊ.saɪˈtəʊ.mə / (BrE)
a tumor consisting of immature, undifferentiated cells
tumor of immature, undifferentiated cells
medical

Examples

The physician diagnosed the patient with a blastocytoma after reviewing the biopsy results.
A blastocytoma often requires specialized treatment due to its aggressive nature.
Researchers are studying new therapies to treat blastocytoma in pediatric patients.

Etymology

From Greek 'blastos' (germ, sprout) + 'kytos' (cell) + '-oma' (tumor).
