calico

1. ADJ

/ ˈkælɪkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈkælɪkəʊ / (BrE)
having patches of different colors, often brightly colored, especially used to describe animals or fabric.
having patches of different colors

Examples

The calico cat slept in the sun, its fur a patchwork of orange, black, and white.
She wore a calico dress with bright, colorful patterns that cheered up the room.
Farmers admired the calico ponies grazing in the field, each with unique markings.

Synset Examples

a jester dressed in motley
the painted desert
a particolored dress
a piebald horse
pied daisies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piebald - having irregular patches of two colors, typically black and white, especially in horses
  • Synonym: motley - having a variety of colors or patches
  • Antonym: plain - not decorated or patterned; single-colored

Etymology

From Spanish 'calicó', originally from Calicut, India, where printed cottons were made.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkælɪˌkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈkælɪkəʊ / (BrE)
A coarse, plain-woven cotton cloth, often printed with a bright pattern.
coarse printed cotton cloth

Examples

The dress was made from a lovely calico with a bright floral print.
She bought several yards of calico to sew curtains for the kitchen.
Old-fashioned quilts were often pieced together from scraps of calico.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: muslin - lightweight cotton cloth, finer than calico

Etymology

From Calicut, the Indian city where the cloth was originally exported from.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02943180-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈkælɪˌkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈkælɪkəʊ / (BrE)
Made of calico fabric or having a mottled pattern of different colors, especially similar to that of printed calico cloth.
having a mottled or patchy pattern

Examples

She wore a beautiful calico skirt to the summer picnic.
The shelter has a playful calico kitten available for adoption.
The room was decorated with calico curtains in a cheerful floral pattern.

Synset Examples

calico dresses
a calico cat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spotted - having spots or patches of color
  • Antonym: plain - not decorated or patterned; simple

Etymology

From Calicut (now Kozhikode), India, where calico cloth was originally exported from.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02685792-a
Synset POS: a
