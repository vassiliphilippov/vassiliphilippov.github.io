jealously

1. ADV

/ ˈdʒɛləsli / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛləsli / (BrE)
In a way that shows jealousy or envy, especially by being protective or possessive.
in a jealous or envious manner

Examples

She jealously guarded her secret recipe from other chefs.
He jealously watched as his colleague received all the praise.
Parents sometimes jealously protect their children from outside influences.

Synset Examples

he guarded his privacy jealously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enviously - in a manner showing envy

Etymology

Formed from 'jealous' + '-ly'; 'jealous' from Old French 'jalous', from Late Latin 'zelosus' (full of zeal).

2. ADV

/ ˈdʒɛləsli / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛləsli / (BrE)
In a way that shows jealousy or envy of someone else's possessions, achievements, or advantages.
in a jealous or envious manner

Examples

She watched her sister open presents jealously, wishing she had received more gifts herself.
He jealously guarded his private collection from curious visitors.
The cat glanced jealously at the dog playing with its favorite toy.

Synset Examples

he looked at his friend's new car jealously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enviously - in a way that shows envy; with envy

Etymology

Formed from 'jealous' + adverbial suffix '-ly'; 'jealous' from Old French 'jalous', from Latin 'zelosus' (full of zeal).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00304549-r
Synset POS: r
