Hottonia palustris

1. NOUN

/ hɑːˈtoʊniə pəˈlʌstrɪs / (AmE), / hɒˈtɒniə pəˈlʌstrɪs / (BrE)
A European and western Asian aquatic plant of the primrose family (Primulaceae), with feather-like submerged leaves and violet flowers, also known as featherfoil.
European aquatic plant with violet flowers

Examples

The shallow pond was covered with blooming Hottonia palustris in early spring.
Many gardeners appreciate Hottonia palustris for its delicate, fern-like leaves and attractive flowers.
Ecologists study populations of Hottonia palustris in wetland restoration projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: featherfoil - Common name for Hottonia palustris, an aquatic plant

Etymology

From genus name Hottonia (after Dutch botanist Pierre Hotton) and Latin palustris (of marshes).
