red-blooded

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɛd ˌblʌdɪd / (AmE), / ˈrɛd ˌblʌdɪd / (BrE)
Full of vigor and energy; exhibiting strong physical or mental health.
vigorous and healthy

Examples

She is a truly red-blooded individual who never misses a morning run.
The doctor said he wished all his patients were as red-blooded as Tom.
A red-blooded outlook on life helped her overcome the illness quickly.

Synset Examples

a hearty glow of health

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hearty - vigorous, healthy, and strong; exhibiting robust health
  • Synonym: robust - strong, healthy, and full of energy
  • Antonym: frail - physically weak or delicate; lacking in strength or health

Etymology

From the idea that abundant red blood signifies robust health and vitality.

2. ADJ

/ ˈrɛd ˌblʌdəd / (AmE), / ˌrɛd ˈblʌdɪd / (BrE)
(of a person, especially a man) vigorous, passionate, and full of strong feelings; robust and spirited.
vigorous, passionate, robust (esp. a person)

Examples

Many consider him a red-blooded individual who is never afraid to speak his mind.
A true red-blooded American, she faced challenges with enthusiasm and determination.
The film is filled with red-blooded characters, each driven by their own fervent desires.

Synset Examples

She admired his red-blooded approach to life.
He was a red-blooded American, full of passion.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: timid - lacking courage or confidence, easily frightened
  • Synonym: passionate - having, showing, or caused by strong feelings or beliefs

Etymology

From 'red' + 'blooded', referring to the healthy color of blood and associating it with vigor.
