Dinocerata

1. NOUN

/ daɪˌnɑːsəˈreɪtə / (AmE), / daɪˌnɒsəˈreɪtə / (BrE)
an extinct order of large, primitive ungulate mammals known from the Paleocene and Eocene epochs
extinct order of primitive ungulate mammals

Examples

Fossils of Dinocerata have been discovered in North America and Asia.
The Dinocerata were among the earliest large land-dwelling mammals.
Scientists study the teeth and skulls of Dinocerata to understand their evolutionary history.

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Greek 'deinos' (terrible) and 'keras' (horn).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪnəˈsɛrətə / (AmE), / ˌdɪnəˈsɛːrətə / (BrE)
an extinct order of primitive hoofed mammals from the Paleocene and Eocene epochs, known for large size and distinctive horns and tusks
extinct order of early hoofed mammals
medical

Examples

Fossils of Dinocerata have been discovered in both North America and Asia.
The Dinocerata were among the largest mammals of the Eocene epoch.
The unique skull features of Dinocerata make them easily identifiable to paleontologists.

Etymology

From Greek dino- (terrible) + keras (horn), referring to their horns.
