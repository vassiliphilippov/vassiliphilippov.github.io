Pius XII

1. PROPN

/ ˌpaɪ.əs ˌtwɛlv / (AmE), / ˌpaɪ.əs ˌtwɛlv / (BrE)
(1876–1958) Pope of the Roman Catholic Church from 1939 to 1958, who maintained neutrality during World War II and was later criticized for not aiding persecuted Jews.
Pope (1939–1958); WWII era

Examples

During World War II, Pius XII maintained a policy of neutrality in international affairs.
Many historians debate the actions of Pius XII regarding the Holocaust.
The Vatican archives on Pius XII have been the subject of considerable scholarly research.

Etymology

Papal name; 'Pius' from Latin 'pius' (devout, dutiful); 'XII' is the Roman numeral for twelve.
