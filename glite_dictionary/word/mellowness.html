mellowness

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛloʊnəs / (AmE), / ˈmɛlənəs / (BrE)
a state of good humor, kindness, or relaxed enjoyment, often resulting from maturity or a relaxed mood
pleasant, relaxed mood or character

Examples

The mellowness in his voice made everyone feel at ease during the discussion.
She enjoyed the mellowness that settled over her after a glass of wine.
Many artists believe that true mellowness comes only with age and experience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serenity - the state of being calm and peaceful
  • Antonym: edginess - nervousness or irritability; a tense or uneasy state

Etymology

From mellow + -ness, 'mellow' from Middle English 'melowe', probably from Old English 'meoluc' (milk).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmɛloʊnəs / (AmE), / ˈmɛlənəs / (BrE)
a quality of being pleasantly smooth, rich, and full, often used to describe sensations such as taste, sound, or mood
pleasant richness or smoothness

Examples

The mellowness of the jazz band’s performance created a wonderful atmosphere in the lounge.
Aged cheese develops a mellowness that makes it especially enjoyable to eat.
There is a certain mellowness in his voice that comforts listeners.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: richness - the quality of being rich, full, and pleasing
  • Antonym: harshness - the quality of being rough or unpleasant, the opposite of mellowness

Etymology

From mellow + -ness, mellow from Old English 'melu' (soft, tender).

3. NOUN

/ ˈmɛl.oʊ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈmɛl.əʊ.nəs / (BrE)
a state of geniality and relaxation, often as a result of alcohol or marijuana
genial, relaxed mood (often from alcohol or marijuana)

Examples

After a couple of glasses of wine, his mellowness made the evening much more enjoyable.
The mellowness in the room was obvious as the party continued into the night.
Her mellowness after smoking was contagious, putting everyone at ease.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: relaxation - a state of being free from tension and anxiety
  • Degree Higher: intoxication - the state of being under the influence of alcohol or drugs
  • Antonym: edginess - a state of being tense, nervous, or irritable

Etymology

From mellow + -ness, meaning the quality or state of being mellow.

a gentle or soft quality, especially referring to the subdued or rich character of a color, light, or tone
softness or richness in color or tone

Examples

Painters often strive for a mellowness in their colors that brings warmth to their work.
There is a gentle mellowness to the autumn leaves that bright summer colors lack.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: softness - gentle or subdued quality, especially of color or sound
  • Antonym: harshness - unpleasantly rough quality in color, sound, or texture

Etymology

From mellow + -ness, modelled after "mellow" (from Middle English *melowe*, likely from Old English *melu* ‘soft’).

5. NOUN

/ ˈmɛl.oʊ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈmɛl.əʊ.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of having a ripe and full flavor, especially in fruit
ripe, full flavor (esp. of fruit)

Examples

The mellowness of the peach made it a delight to eat on a summer afternoon.
Grapes from this region are prized for their natural mellowness and sweetness.
The mellowness of the cheese indicated it had aged to perfection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ripeness - the state of being fully grown and developed, especially for eating
  • Antonym: blandness - the lack of strong flavor or taste

Etymology

From mellow + -ness; 'mellow' from Middle English 'melowe', probably from Old English 'melu' (meal, flour).
