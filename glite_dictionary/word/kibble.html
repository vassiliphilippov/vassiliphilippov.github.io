kibble

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈkɪbəl / (BrE)
an iron bucket used for hoisting materials such as ore or water from wells or mines
iron bucket for hoisting in mining

Examples

The miners filled the kibble with rocks and sent it up to the surface.
A rusty kibble was discovered at the abandoned mine site.
Water was hauled from the shaft using a heavy iron kibble.

Etymology

From German *Kübel* (bucket).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈkɪbəl / (BrE)
coarsely ground grain formed into small pellets, commonly used as dry food for pets such as dogs and cats
pellets of ground grain pet food

Examples

She poured kibble into the dog's bowl every morning before work.
Many veterinarians recommend switching your cat's kibble to one with higher protein content.
The bag of kibble was labeled for adult dogs of all sizes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pellet - small, hard ball or mass, especially of food or animal feed
  • Synonym: chow - food for animals; animal feed, especially in pellet form

Etymology

From Middle English 'kibelen' (to cudgel); later applied to chopped food stuffs.
