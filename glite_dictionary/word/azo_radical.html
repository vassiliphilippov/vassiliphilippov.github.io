azo radical

1. NOUN

/ ˈeɪzoʊ ˈræd.ɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈeɪzəʊ ˈræd.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
a bivalent chemical group (-N=N-) attached to two hydrocarbon groups, found in many organic compounds
bivalent chemical group -N=N- in organic chemistry
medical

Examples

The azo radical plays a crucial role in the synthesis of many dyes.
Organic chemists often study the stability of the azo radical.
Introduction of an azo radical can create vivid colors in textile fibers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: azo compound - any compound containing the azo group (-N=N-)

Etymology

From French 'azote' (nitrogen), from Greek 'a-' (not) + 'zoe' (life), referring to nitrogen's inability to support life. The term 'radical' is from Latin 'radix' (root).
