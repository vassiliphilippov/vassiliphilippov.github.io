supernatant

1. ADJ

/ ˌsuː.pərˈneɪ.tənt / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˈneɪ.tənt / (BrE)
(of a liquid) floating on the surface above a sediment or precipitate.
floating above sediment (esp. liquid)

Examples

After centrifugation, the supernatant liquid was carefully poured off.
The technician examined the supernatant for clarity before proceeding.
Skim the supernatant layer to separate it from the solid residue.

Synset Examples

the supernatant fat was skimmed off

Etymology

From Latin 'supernatans,' present participle of 'supernatare' meaning 'to swim above'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02360046-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ ˌsuː.pərˈneɪ.tənt / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˈneɪ.tənt / (BrE)
the clear liquid that remains above a solid residue after settling or centrifugation, especially in a laboratory setting
clear liquid above sediment

Examples

After centrifugation, the supernatant was carefully removed and transferred to a new tube.
The scientist analyzed the supernatant to check for dissolved proteins.
Allow the mixture to settle and then decant the supernatant without disturbing the sediment.

Etymology

From Latin supernatant-, present participle of supernatāre (to float above); from super- (above) + natāre (to swim).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-09474233-n
Synset POS: n
