Froelichia

1. NOUN

/ froʊˈliːkiə / (AmE), / frəʊˈliːkɪə / (BrE)
A genus of erect or prostrate herbs native to the Americas, characterized by spikes of woolly white flowers; also known as cottonweed.
a genus of American herbs with woolly flowers

Examples

The Froelichia growing along the roadside drew attention with its fluffy white flower spikes.
Many gardeners appreciate Froelichia for its ability to thrive in poor, sandy soils.
Researchers studied the unique pollination methods of several Froelichia species.

Etymology

Named after Josef Aloys von Froelich, German physician and botanist.

2. NOUN

/ froʊˈliːxiə / (AmE), / frəʊˈliːxɪə / (BrE)
A genus of erect or spreading herbs native to the Americas, characterized by woolly white flower spikes; commonly known as cottonweed.
genus of herbs called cottonweed

Examples

The distinctive woolly flowers of Froelichia make it easy to recognize in the wild.
Many gardeners cultivate Froelichia for its ornamental value and drought resistance.
Researchers recently discovered a new species within the Froelichia genus in South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cottonweed - common name for several plants, especially those in the genus Froelichia

Etymology

Named after German botanist Joseph Anton Froelich (1766–1841).
