xylose

1. NOUN

/ ˈzaɪˌloʊs / (AmE), / ˈzaɪləʊs / (BrE)
a sugar obtained from wood or straw, used especially as a sweetener for diabetics and in chemical research
a sugar from wood or straw

Examples

Scientists studied the properties of xylose extracted from birch wood.
Many sugar-free products for diabetics use xylose as a sweetener.
The laboratory uses xylose to investigate bacterial metabolism.

Etymology

From Greek 'xylon' (wood) + '-ose' (sugar).
