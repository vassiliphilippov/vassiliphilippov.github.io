asafoetida

1. NOUN

/ ˌæsəˈfɛtɪdə / (AmE), / ˌasəˈfiːtɪdə / (BrE)
A brownish, pungent gum resin obtained from certain plants, used as a spice in cooking and formerly for medicinal purposes.
pungent gum resin used as a spice

Examples

Many Indian recipes call for a pinch of asafoetida to enhance the flavor of lentil dishes.
The pungent smell of asafoetida can be overwhelming to those not used to it.
Historically, asafoetida was also used as a remedy for digestive problems.

Etymology

From Latin 'asa' (resin) + 'foetida' (stinking), reflecting its strong odor.
