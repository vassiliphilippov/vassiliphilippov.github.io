catalatic

1. ADJ

/ ˌkætəˈlætɪk / (AmE), / ˌkatəˈlatɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of the enzyme catalase.
relating to catalase enzyme
medical

Examples

The catalatic activity in liver cells helps break down hydrogen peroxide.
Researchers studied the catalatic properties of various microorganisms.
A deficiency in catalatic function can affect cellular detoxification processes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catalase - an enzyme that catalyzes the decomposition of hydrogen peroxide

Etymology

From catalase (enzyme) + -ic (forming adjectives).
