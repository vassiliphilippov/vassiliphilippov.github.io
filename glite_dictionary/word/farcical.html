farcical

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɑrsɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈfɑːsɪkəl / (BrE)
absurd or ridiculous, resembling a farce; extremely silly or improbable, often in a humorous way
absurd or like a farce

Examples

The play turned into a farcical series of misunderstandings that had the audience in stitches.
It was a farcical situation when the committee forgot to invite its own chairperson to the meeting.
Their attempt to escape was so badly planned it became almost farcical.

Synset Examples

the wild farcical exuberance of a clown
ludicrous green hair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ludicrous - so absurd or ridiculous as to be amusing
  • Antonym: serious - not joking or intended to amuse; solemn

Etymology

From French *farcical*, from Latin *farcire* (to stuff), relating to 'farce' as comic entertainment.
