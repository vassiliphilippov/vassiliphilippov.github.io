dacite

1. NOUN

/ ˈdeɪ.saɪt / (AmE), / ˈdeɪ.saɪt / (BrE)
a grey volcanic rock containing plagioclase, quartz, and other crystalline minerals
a type of grey volcanic rock

Examples

The mountain was primarily composed of dacite, giving it a distinctive grey hue.
Geologists collected samples of dacite from the volcanic outcrop for further analysis.
Some lava domes are formed almost entirely of dacite, resulting in unique geological structures.

Etymology

From French *dacit*, named after the Dacian (ancient European) region where it was first identified.
