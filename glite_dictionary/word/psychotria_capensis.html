Psychotria capensis

1. NOUN

/ saɪˌkəʊˈtriːə kəˈpɛnsɪs / (AmE), / sʌɪˌkɒtrɪə kəˈpɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A South African evergreen shrub or small tree with hard, tough wood, belonging to the coffee family (Rubiaceae).
South African evergreen shrub or tree

Examples

The hard wood of Psychotria capensis is valued in some regions for making tools.
Many gardeners cultivate Psychotria capensis for its attractive evergreen foliage.
Researchers studied Psychotria capensis to learn more about the flora of South Africa.

Etymology

From modern Latin: 'Psychotria' is the genus name (from Greek 'psyche' meaning 'spirit, soul'), and 'capensis' refers to the Cape region of South Africa.
