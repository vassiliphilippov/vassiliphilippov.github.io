downy

1. ADJ

/ ˈdaʊni / (AmE), / ˈdaʊni / (BrE)
soft and fluffy like down (the small, soft feathers of birds); covered with or resembling fine, soft hairs or feathers
soft and fluffy like down feathers

Examples

The chick's downy feathers kept it warm in the nest.
She wore a downy sweater that felt soft against her skin.
The leaves were covered in a downy layer of fine white hairs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fluffy - soft and light in texture, like wool or feathers
  • Synonym: silky - smooth, soft, and shiny like silk

Etymology

From 'down' (soft feathers) + '-y' (adjective suffix).

2. ADJ

/ ˈdaʊni / (AmE), / ˈdaʊni / (BrE)
covered with fine, soft hairs or down, like that found on a young bird or certain plants
covered with fine soft hairs

Examples

The downy feathers of the chick kept it warm during the cold nights.
She admired the downy surface of the peach as she picked it from the tree.
The seeds were covered in a downy layer, making them look almost like tiny clouds.

Synset Examples

downy milkweed seeds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fluffy - soft and light in texture, like down

Etymology

From 'down' (fine soft feathers) + '-y' (adjective suffix). 'Down' from Old Norse 'dúnn'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00215202-s
Synset POS: s
