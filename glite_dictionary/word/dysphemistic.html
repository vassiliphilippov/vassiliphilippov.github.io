dysphemistic

1. ADJ

/ dɪsfəˈmɪstɪk / (AmE), / dɪsfɛˈmɪstɪk / (BrE)
using or relating to dysphemism; deliberately harsh or offensive in expression.
expressing something in a harsh or offensive way

Examples

Some comedians use dysphemistic humor that deliberately shocks the audience.
Referring to someone as 'pig-headed' is a dysphemistic way to describe their stubbornness.
Tabloid headlines are often dysphemistic, using harsher words to attract attention.

Synset Examples

‘kick the bucket’ is a dysphemistic term for ‘die’

Related Terms

  • Antonym: euphemistic - using mild or indirect language to avoid offense
  • Synonym: dysphemism - a harsh or offensive expression used in place of a neutral one

Etymology

Formed from 'dysphemism' + '-istic'; 'dysphemism' from Greek 'dys-' (bad) + 'pheme' (speech).
