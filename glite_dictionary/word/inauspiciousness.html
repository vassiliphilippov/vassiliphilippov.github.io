inauspiciousness

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.ɔːˈspɪ.ʃəs.nəs / (AmE), / ˌɪn.ɔːˈspɪʃ.əs.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of suggesting that something will be unsuccessful or unlucky.
quality of indicating bad luck or no success

Examples

The inauspiciousness of the cloudy morning cast doubt over the wedding plans.
Some investors were deterred by the inauspiciousness of early market signals.
She worried that the inauspiciousness of his comments would affect the outcome of the project.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: auspiciousness - the quality of suggesting success or good fortune

Etymology

From 'inauspicious' (not conducive to success), itself from Latin 'inauspiciosus'.
