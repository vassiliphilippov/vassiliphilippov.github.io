Tyche

1. PROPN

/ ˈtaɪki / (AmE), / ˈtaɪki / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the goddess of fortune and chance, identified with the Roman goddess Fortuna.
Greek goddess of fortune

Examples

Many ancient cities worshipped Tyche as the deity who controlled their fate and prosperity.
In several classical sculptures, Tyche is depicted holding a cornucopia and a rudder.
The influence of Tyche can be seen in how people viewed luck and chance in Greek culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Fortuna - Roman goddess of fortune and fate

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Τύχη (Týchē), meaning ‘fortune’ or ‘luck’.
