felicitation

1. NOUN

/ fəˌlɪsɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / fɪˌlɪsɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of expressing good wishes or congratulations to someone, especially on a special occasion
expression of congratulations or good wishes

Examples

He sent a letter of felicitation to his friend on her wedding day.
The principal offered felicitation to the graduates during the ceremony.
My heartfelt felicitation on your well-deserved promotion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: congratulation - the act of congratulating someone

Etymology

From Latin felicitatio, from felix (happy, fortunate).

2. NOUN

/ fəˌlɪsɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / fɪˌlɪsɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
An expression of pleasure or congratulation for someone’s success or good fortune, especially in a formal or ceremonial context (usually used in plural).
formal expression of congratulation

Examples

She received numerous felicitations from colleagues after winning the award.
Please accept our warmest felicitations on your 25th wedding anniversary.
The mayor offered his felicitations to the graduates during the commencement ceremony.

Synset Examples

I sent them my sincere congratulations on their marriage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: congratulation - the act of expressing pleasure at another's success

Etymology

From Latin felicitatio (making happy), from felix (happy, fortunate).
