lycopod

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪkəˌpɑd / (AmE), / ˈlɪkəpɒd / (BrE)
a primitive, evergreen, moss-like plant that reproduces by spores in club-shaped cones (strobili), belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae
primitive moss-like plant (clubmoss)

Examples

Some lycopods can survive in environments where little else grows.
The forest floor was once dominated by giant lycopods during the Carboniferous period.
This museum exhibit features the fossil remains of prehistoric lycopod plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clubmoss - another term for lycopod, referring to similar primitive spore-bearing plants

Etymology

From New Latin Lycopodium (the type genus), from Greek lykos (wolf) + podion (diminutive of foot), referring to resemblance of some species’ shoots to a wolf’s foot.
