haddock

1. NOUN

/ ˈhædək / (AmE), / ˈhædək / (BrE)
a North Atlantic fish related to the cod, often used as food
North Atlantic food fish, related to cod

Examples

The fisherman caught a large haddock early this morning.
Grilled haddock is one of my favorite seafood dishes.
The chef served fresh haddock with lemon and herbs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cod - a large marine fish valued as food, related to haddock

Etymology

From Middle English hadoc, hadduc, of uncertain origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhædək / (AmE), / ˈhædək / (BrE)
the lean, white flesh of a fish similar to but smaller than cod, typically eaten baked, poached, sautéed, or fried
lean white fish, similar to cod, used as food

Examples

She ordered grilled haddock with a side of vegetables for dinner.
The fishmonger recommended using fresh haddock fillets for the recipe.
Baked haddock is a healthy option that is both delicious and easy to prepare.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cod - large white fish related to haddock

Etymology

From Middle English *hadok*, of uncertain origin, possibly related to Old French *hadot*.

Synset POS: n
