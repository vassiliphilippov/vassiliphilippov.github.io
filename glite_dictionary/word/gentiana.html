Gentiana

1. NOUN

/ dʒɛnˈtiːənə / (AmE), / dʒɛnˈtiːənə / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants in the gentian family (Gentianaceae), found in cool temperate regions worldwide; known for their usually blue, trumpet-shaped flowers
genus of flowering plants; gentians

Examples

The Alpine Gentiana brightened the rocky slopes with its vivid blue blossoms.
Many species of Gentiana are prized for their ornamental value in gardens.
Extracts from Gentiana roots have been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gentian - any plant of the genus Gentiana or their flowers

Etymology

From Latin *gentiana*, a plant name, possibly after Gentius, an Illyrian king said to have discovered its medicinal value.

2. NOUN

/ ˌdʒɛn.ʃiˈɑː.nə / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛn.tiˈɑː.nə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the family Gentianaceae, known for their usually blue, trumpet-shaped flowers, and found in temperate regions worldwide.
genus of flowering plants (Gentianaceae)

Examples

Several species of Gentiana are prized for their brilliant blue flowers in alpine gardens.
The roots of certain Gentiana plants are used in traditional herbal medicine.
We spotted a cluster of Gentiana growing wild near the mountain stream.

Etymology

From Latin *gentiana*, named after King Gentius of Illyria, who is said to have discovered the medicinal value of the plant.
