John Steinbeck

1. PROPN

/ dʒɑn ˈstaɪnˌbɛk / (AmE), / dʒɒn ˈstaɪnbɛk / (BrE)
(1902–1968) An American writer known for his novels depicting the lives of agricultural workers and the working class, such as 'The Grapes of Wrath' and 'Of Mice and Men'.
American novelist (1902–1968)

Examples

Many students read John Steinbeck's novels in high school literature classes.
The themes in John Steinbeck's writing often focus on poverty and social injustice.
John Steinbeck won the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1962.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Ernest Hemingway - American novelist and short story writer, contemporary of Steinbeck
