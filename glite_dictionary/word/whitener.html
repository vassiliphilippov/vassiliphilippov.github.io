whitener

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪtnər / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtənə / (BrE)
a substance or agent used to make something white or colorless
substance that makes things white or colorless

Examples

She added a laundry whitener to the wash to brighten her clothes.
Many people use a dental whitener to achieve a brighter smile.
This paint whitener helps restore the original color of faded walls.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bleach - a chemical that removes color or whitens

Etymology

From 'white' + '-ener', denoting something that whitens.
