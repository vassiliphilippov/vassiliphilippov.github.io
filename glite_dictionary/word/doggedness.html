doggedness

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɔɡɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈdɒɡɪdnəs / (BrE)
persistent determination to continue with something, despite difficulties or opposition.
persistent determination

Examples

Her doggedness in pursuing justice inspired everyone who knew her.
The team's doggedness paid off when they finally won the championship.
Despite repeated setbacks, his doggedness never wavered.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tenacity - the quality of being very determined
  • Synonym: perseverance - continued effort to do or achieve something despite difficulties
  • Antonym: resignation - the acceptance of something undesirable but inevitable

Etymology

From 'dogged' + '-ness', with 'dogged' from 'dog' in the sense of tenacity, referencing the unyielding nature of a dog.
