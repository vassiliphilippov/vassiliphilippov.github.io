Argusianus

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˌɡjuː.siˈeɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ɑːˌɡjuː.siˈeɪ.nəs / (BrE)
A genus of large pheasants native to Southeast Asia, known as argus pheasants, especially the great argus.
genus of argus pheasants

Examples

The Argusianus is renowned for the male's spectacular display of plumage.
Researchers have observed Argusianus in the rainforests of Borneo.
Conservation efforts focus on protecting the habitat of the Argusianus.

Etymology

From Argus, a figure in Greek mythology, and Latin suffix '-ianus', commonly used in zoological nomenclature.

2. NOUN

/ ɑːrˌɡjuːziˈænəs / (AmE), / ɑːˌɡjuːziˈænəs / (BrE)
A genus of large pheasants native to Southeast Asia, known for their striking plumage and elaborate courtship displays. It includes the great argus.
genus of large Southeast Asian pheasants

Examples

The Argusianus is renowned for the beautiful eye-like patterns on its feathers.
Scientists observed a male Argusianus performing an elaborate mating dance in the wild.
The great argus, a species within the genus Argusianus, inhabits the forests of Malaysia and Sumatra.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pheasant - a large long-tailed game bird

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Argus, a figure from Greek mythology noted for his many eyes, referring to the bird’s eye-like feather markings.
