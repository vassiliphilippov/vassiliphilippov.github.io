Thelypteris dryopteris

1. NOUN

/ θəˌlɪptərɪs daɪˈɑptərɪs / (AmE), / θəˌlɪptərɪs draɪˈɒptərɪs / (BrE)
A species of bright blue-green fern widely distributed in damp, acidic woodlands of the temperate Northern Hemisphere.
a species of blue-green woodland fern

Examples

In the shaded glen, clusters of Thelypteris dryopteris covered the forest floor.
Botanists noted that Thelypteris dryopteris thrives best in moist, acidic soil.
The distinctive blue-green fronds of Thelypteris dryopteris made identification easy for the students.

Etymology

From Greek 'thēlys' (female) and 'pteris' (fern); 'dryopteris' from Greek 'drys' (oak) and 'pteris' (fern).
