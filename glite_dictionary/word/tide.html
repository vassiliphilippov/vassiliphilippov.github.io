tide

1. VERB

/ taɪd / (AmE), / taɪd / (BrE)
to cause (a boat or object) to float or drift with the tide
cause to float with the tide

Examples

The fishermen tided the boat down the river at dawn.
Logs were tided to the sawmill using the current.
They tided their rafts to the harbor, taking advantage of the rising water.

2. NOUN

/ taɪd / (AmE), / taɪd / (BrE)
a tendency, movement, or condition that increases and decreases, like the rise and fall of the sea
a rising or falling tendency or condition

Examples

There is a tide of support building for the new policy.
A tide of optimism swept through the company after the announcement.
Public opinion can change like the tide, rising and falling with events.

Synset Examples

a rising tide of popular interest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wave - a sudden increase in a particular phenomenon or emotion
  • Antonym: ebb - a movement away from a high point, the flow back of the tide

Etymology

From Old English 'tīd' (time, season); evolved into the meaning of the in-and-out movement of the sea.

3. NOUN

/ taɪd / (AmE), / taɪd / (BrE)
the regular rise and fall of the level of the sea caused by the gravitational pull of the moon and sun
periodic rising and falling of the sea

Examples

The fishermen waited on shore for the tide to come in.
At high tide, the water reached the steps of the pier.
She checked the tide tables before planning her sailing trip.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ebb - the movement of the tide out to sea
  • Degree Higher: flood tide - the rising period of the tide
  • Degree Lower: low tide - the time when the sea is at its lowest level

Etymology

Old English *tīd* (time, season), later used specifically for sea movements.

4. VERB

/ taɪd / (AmE), / taɪd / (BrE)
(of water, people, or events) to rise, flow, or move forward in a steady, sweeping manner, like a tide.
to rise, flow, or move forward like a tide

Examples

Crowds began to tide into the stadium as the gates opened.
Emotions seemed to tide over her, leaving her breathless.
The noisy children tided through the museum in a great wave.

Synset Examples

surging waves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: surge - to move suddenly and powerfully forward or upward

Etymology

From Old English tīd (time, season, tide).

5. NOUN

/ taɪd / (AmE), / taɪd / (BrE)
The regular rise and fall of the sea level caused by the gravitational pull of the moon and sun.
rise and fall of sea level

Examples

The fishermen waited for the tide to come in before setting out to sea.
Many coastal animals time their activities according to the tide.
Low tide revealed a stretch of sandy beach that was usually underwater.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ebb - the movement of the tide out to sea; the receding phase of the tide
  • Synonym: flow - the rising movement of the tide toward the shore

Etymology

Old English *tīd* meaning 'time, period, season', later shifting to refer to the periodic movement of the sea.

6. VERB

/ taɪd / (AmE), / taɪd / (BrE)
(of a person or object) to be carried along by the movement of the tide in the sea or other body of water.
to be carried with the tide

Examples

The small boat began to tide down the estuary, carried by the evening current.
During the storm, several pieces of debris tided ashore overnight.
Without an anchor, we risked simply tiding out to sea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drift - to be carried slowly by air or water
