diminished

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈmɪnɪʃt / (AmE), / dɪˈmɪnɪʃt / (BrE)
(of a musical interval) made smaller by a semitone from a perfect or minor interval.
smaller musical interval by a semitone

Examples

In classical music, a diminished seventh chord creates a strong sense of tension.
A tritone is an example of a diminished fifth interval.
The composer used a diminished fourth to add dissonance to the piece.

Synset Examples

a diminished fifth

Related Terms

  • Antonym: augmented - (of a musical interval) increased by a semitone

Etymology

From Latin 'diminuere' (to lessen).

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈmɪnɪʃt / (AmE), / dɪˈmɪnɪʃt / (BrE)
(of an organ or body part) reduced in size or strength as a result of disease, injury, or lack of use.
reduced in size or strength (body part)

Examples

The patient's left leg was noticeably diminished after months in a cast.
Muscle tissue can become diminished if it is not exercised regularly.
After her accident, the strength in her right hand was diminished.

Synset Examples

partial paralysis resulted in an atrophied left arm

Related Terms

  • Synonym: atrophied - (of body tissue or an organ) wasted away, typically due to the degeneration of cells

3. ADJ

/ dɪˈmɪnɪʃt / (AmE), / dɪˈmɪnɪʃt / (BrE)
made or become smaller, weaker, or less; impaired by reduction in amount, importance, or intensity.
made smaller or less; impaired

Examples

The patient's vision was diminished after the accident.
Her influence in the company has diminished over the last year.
The medication helped restore hearing that had previously been diminished.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reduced - made smaller in size, amount, or degree
  • Antonym: increased - made greater in size or amount

Etymology

Past participle of 'diminish', from Old French 'deminut', from Latin 'deminuere' (to lessen).

/ dɪˈmɪnɪʃt / (AmE), / dɪˈmɪnɪʃt / (BrE)
made or seen as smaller, less important, or less valuable than before
made smaller or less important

Examples

Many felt that their achievements were diminished by comparisons to previous generations.
After the scandal, the company's reputation was diminished in the eyes of investors.
He left the meeting feeling diminished by the sharp criticism he received.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reduced - made smaller or less in amount, degree, or size

Etymology
