caulk

1. NOUN

/ kɑk / (AmE), / kɔːk / (BrE)
a waterproof material used to fill and seal joints or seams in buildings or other structures to prevent leakage
waterproof filler or sealant for joints

Examples

The plumber applied caulk around the bathtub to prevent water from seeping into the wall.
Before painting, make sure to fill any cracks with caulk for a smooth finish.
We used a silicone caulk to seal the window frames during the renovation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sealant - a substance used to block the passage of fluids through the surface or joints

Etymology

From Old Northern French cauquer (to press, drive in), related to Latin calcāre (to tread).

2. VERB

/ kɔk / (AmE), / kɔːk / (BrE)
to seal a joint or seam with a waterproof filler, especially in building or ship repair, to make it airtight or watertight
seal with waterproof filler

Examples

You should caulk around the bathtub to prevent leaks.
He carefully caulked the seams of the boat before launching it.
We plan to caulk the window frames this weekend for better insulation.

Synset Examples

caulk the window

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seal - to close tightly so that nothing can enter or escape

Etymology

From Old Northern French cauquer or Old French calquer, from Late Latin calcāre (to tread, press down).
