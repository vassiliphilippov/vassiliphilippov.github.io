Pholiota squarrosoides

1. NOUN

/ foʊˈliː.oʊ.tə skwəˌroʊˈsɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / fəˈlɪ.əʊ.tə skwəˌrəʊˈsɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A species of pale buff mushroom with tawny scales, found on decaying wood.
a buff-colored fungus with tawny scales

Examples

The forest ranger identified the mushroom as a Pholiota squarrosoides growing on a decaying log.
Collectors should be cautious, as Pholiota squarrosoides can be confused with other scaly mushrooms.
During our hike, we spotted several clusters of Pholiota squarrosoides on the damp wood.

Etymology

From Greek 'pholis' (scale), referencing the fungus's scaly cap; species epithet 'squarrosoides' means 'resembling being scaly'.
