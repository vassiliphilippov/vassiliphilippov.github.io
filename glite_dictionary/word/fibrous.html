fibrous

1. ADJ

/ ˈfaɪbrəs / (AmE), / ˈfaɪ.brəs / (BrE)
consisting of or resembling fibers; having a texture or structure made up of fibers, especially those used in making cordage such as jute
having fibers or fiber-like texture

Examples

The stem of the plant was fibrous and tough, making it ideal for rope making.
Many types of meat become fibrous if cooked for too long, resulting in a chewy texture.
Coconut husk is known for its fibrous material, widely used in making mats and brushes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: filamentous - composed of long, thread-like structures
  • Antonym: fibrousless - lacking fibers

Etymology

From Latin 'fibrosus', from 'fibra' (fiber).

2. ADJ

/ ˈfaɪbɹəs / (AmE), / ˈfaɪbɹəs / (BrE)
(of meat or other food) containing tough fibers, making it difficult to chew.
meat with tough, stringy fibers

Examples

The steak was so fibrous that I could barely cut through it with my knife.
She found the roast chicken too fibrous to enjoy and left most of it uneaten.
Older cuts of beef tend to become more fibrous and require longer cooking times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tough - (of food, especially meat) difficult to chew or cut

Etymology

From Latin 'fibrosus' (full of fibers), from 'fibra' (fiber).
