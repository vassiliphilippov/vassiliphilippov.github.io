Cosmocampus

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑzmoʊˈkæmpəs / (AmE), / ˌkɒzməˈkæmpəs / (BrE)
a genus of small pipefishes in the family Syngnathidae, found in marine environments worldwide
genus of small pipefishes

Examples

Researchers discovered a new species within the genus Cosmocampus along the Caribbean coastline.
The pipefishes of the Cosmocampus genus are known for their slender, elongated bodies.
Marine biologists often study Cosmocampus to understand the diversity within the Syngnathidae family.

Etymology

From Greek 'kosmos' (order, world) and 'kampē' (bend), referencing the shape and distribution of these fishes.
