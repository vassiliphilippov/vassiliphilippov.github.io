adder

1. NOUN

/ ˈædər / (AmE), / ˈadə / (BrE)
a machine or device that performs addition of numbers, especially as part of a computer or calculator
device for performing addition

Examples

The engineer designed an adder circuit to process binary numbers efficiently.
Modern processors contain complex adders to handle arithmetic operations swiftly.
A basic calculator uses an electronic adder to compute sums.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calculator - device for making mathematical calculations
  • Antonym: subtractor - a part or device that performs subtraction

Etymology

From 'add' + '-er', denoting something that performs addition.

2. NOUN

/ ˈædər / (AmE), / ˈadə / (BrE)
a small venomous snake, particularly the common European viper (Vipera berus), found in northern Eurasia
small venomous snake of northern Eurasia

Examples

The adder is the only venomous snake native to the UK.
We spotted an adder basking in the sun during our hike.
Although the adder's bite is rarely fatal, medical attention is still recommended.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viper - a venomous snake of the family Viperidae

Etymology

Old English 'nædre' (snake), later rebracketed from 'a nadder' to 'an adder'.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
