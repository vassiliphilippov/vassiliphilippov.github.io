Micrurus fulvius

1. NOUN

/ maɪˈkruːrəs ˈfʊlviəs / (AmE), / mɪˈkrʊərəs ˈfʊlviəs / (BrE)
A species of venomous coral snake found in southeastern United States and parts of Central America, known for its distinctive red, yellow, and black banding.
a venomous coral snake of southeast US and Central America
medical

Examples

The Micrurus fulvius is easily identified by its red, yellow, and black rings.
Venom from the Micrurus fulvius can cause serious neurotoxic effects if untreated.
Researchers studied the habitat of Micrurus fulvius across the southeastern United States.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eastern coral snake - Common name for Micrurus fulvius, a venomous snake
  • Degree Higher: coral snake - Any venomous snake of the subfamily Elapinae with brightly colored bands

Etymology

From modern Latin, combining Greek 'mikros' (small) and Latin 'fulvius' (tawny yellow).
