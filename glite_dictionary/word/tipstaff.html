tipstaff

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪpˌstæf / (AmE), / ˈtɪp.stɑːf / (BrE)
A staff with a metal tip, carried as a symbol of office by a bailiff, constable, or other official.
staff with metal tip as sign of office

Examples

The bailiff carried a tipstaff as he led the procession through the courtroom.
In some ceremonies, a tipstaff is used to signify official authority.
Historically, constables were identified by their distinctive tipstaff.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baton - a short stick carried by an official as a symbol of rank or authority

Etymology

From 'tip' (end, point) + 'staff' (rod, stick).
