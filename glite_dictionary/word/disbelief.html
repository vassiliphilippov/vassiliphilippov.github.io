disbelief

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsbɪˈlif / (AmE), / ˌdɪsbɪˈliːf / (BrE)
the feeling or state of being unable or unwilling to accept that something is true
inability or refusal to believe

Examples

When I told her the news, she stared at me in disbelief.
His story was met with disbelief by everyone in the room.
She shook her head in disbelief, unable to comprehend what had happened.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: belief - acceptance that something exists or is true, especially without proof

Etymology

From Middle English 'dis-' (expressing reversal) + 'belief', modelled after French 'dis-' equivalents.

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsbɪˈlif / (AmE), / ˌdɪsbɪˈliːf / (BrE)
the feeling or state of not accepting something as true or real; lack of belief or acceptance
refusal or inability to believe

Examples

Her face registered utter disbelief when she heard the news.
He stared at the magician in disbelief, unable to comprehend what he had just seen.
There was a collective disbelief in the audience after the sudden announcement.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: belief - acceptance that something exists or is true, especially without proof
  • Synonym: doubt - a feeling of uncertainty or lack of conviction

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French, from dis- (expressing reversal) + belief.
