vena temporalis

1. NOUN

/ ˈveɪnə tɛmˈpɔrəlɪs / (AmE), / ˈviːnə tɛmˈpɔːrælɪs / (BrE)
any of several veins that drain blood from the temporal region of the head
temporal region vein
medical

Examples

The surgeon carefully avoided the vena temporalis during the procedure.
An injury to the vena temporalis can lead to excessive bleeding in the temple area.
The anatomy textbook provided detailed diagrams of the vena temporalis and its branches.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: temporal vein - any vein draining the temple area of the head

Etymology

From Latin 'vena' (vein) and 'temporalis' (of the temple/temporal region).
