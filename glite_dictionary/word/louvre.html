Louvre

1. PROPN

/ ˈluːvɚ / (AmE), / ˈluːvr(ə) / (BrE)
the national museum and art gallery of France in Paris, noted for its collection of art including the Mona Lisa
famous art museum in Paris

Examples

Millions of people visit the Louvre every year to admire its art treasures.
The Louvre is home to the Mona Lisa and many other masterpieces.
She spent an afternoon exploring the galleries of the Louvre in Paris.

Etymology

French, named after the former royal palace, Le Louvre, which now houses the museum.

2. PROPN

/ ˈluːvɚ / (AmE), / ˈluːv(r) / (BrE)
the world-famous art museum located in Paris, France, noted for its extensive collection and the Mona Lisa.
famous art museum in Paris

Examples

Thousands of tourists flock to the Louvre each year to view its masterpieces.
The Louvre is home to one of the world's most recognizable paintings, the Mona Lisa.
During our trip to Paris, we spent an afternoon exploring the Louvre.

Synset Examples

We visited the Louvre to see the Mona Lisa in person.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: museum - a building in which objects of historical, scientific, or artistic interest are kept and shown to the public

Etymology

From French 'Louvre', the name of the royal palace where the museum is housed.
