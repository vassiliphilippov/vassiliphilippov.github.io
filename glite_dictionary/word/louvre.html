louvre

1. NOUN

/ ˈluːvər / (AmE), / ˈluːvə / (BrE)
one of a set of angled, parallel slats in a door or window designed to let in air but keep out rain or direct sunlight.
slat to admit air, keep out rain

Examples

The louvre in the window allowed cool air to circulate throughout the room.
Modern homes often use adjustable louvres to control ventilation and light.
He replaced the damaged louvre to improve the airflow through the pantry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shutter - a cover for a window, often with adjustable slats

Etymology

From French 'louvre', of obscure origin.
