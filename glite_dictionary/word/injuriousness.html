injuriousness

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈdʒʊriəsnəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʒʊəriəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of causing harm, damage, or injury
quality of causing harm or injury

Examples

The injuriousness of the chemical was evident after several lab tests showed severe side effects.
She warned about the injuriousness of smoking to long-term health.
Debates continue about the injuriousness of certain online behaviors to children's development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: harmfulness - the quality of causing harm
  • Antonym: beneficialness - the quality of being beneficial or helpful

Etymology

From injurious + -ness; injurious ultimately from Latin injuria (wrong, injury).
