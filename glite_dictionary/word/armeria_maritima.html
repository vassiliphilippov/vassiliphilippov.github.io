Armeria maritima

1. NOUN

/ ɑːrˈmɪəriə mərɪˈtiːmə / (AmE), / ɑːˈmɪəriə ˌmarɪˈtiːmə / (BrE)
a perennial plant with tufts of grass-like leaves and pink or purple flower heads, native to seacoasts and mountains in the north temperate zone, commonly known as thrift or sea pink
species of flowering plant; also called thrift/sea pink

Examples

Armeria maritima is often used to add color and texture to seaside gardens.
The tufts of Armeria maritima can thrive in poor, rocky soil near the coast.
Gardeners appreciate Armeria maritima for its low maintenance and attractive pink blossoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thrift - common name for Armeria maritima, a flowering plant with tufts of grass-like leaves

Etymology

From New Latin; genus name 'Armeria' and Latin 'maritima' (of the sea).
