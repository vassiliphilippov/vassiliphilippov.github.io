spelter

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɛltɚ / (AmE), / ˈspɛltə / (BrE)
impure zinc, especially in ingot form, typically containing about three percent lead and other impurities
impure zinc in ingot form

Examples

Sheets of spelter were used in the manufacturing process due to their cost-effectiveness.
The factory imported spelter from Europe to produce galvanized metal.
When melted, spelter releases fumes that require proper ventilation in the workshop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zinc - a bluish-silver metallic element, chemical symbol Zn

Etymology

From Dutch 'spijlder' (zinc), possibly of Middle Dutch origin.
