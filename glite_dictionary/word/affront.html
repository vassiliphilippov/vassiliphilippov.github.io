affront

1. NOUN

/ əˈfrʌnt / (AmE), / əˈfrʌnt / (BrE)
a deliberate act or remark that causes offense or shows a lack of respect
deliberate act or remark causing offense

Examples

Her refusal to shake hands was seen as a personal affront by the other guest.
Many considered his comments an affront to the community's values.
Ignoring the invitation altogether was a clear affront to tradition.

Synset Examples

turning his back on me was a deliberate insult

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insult - a remark or action that is rude or offensive
  • Antonym: compliment - a polite expression of praise or admiration

Etymology

From Old French 'afront', from Latin 'ad frontem' (to the face).

WordNet ID: oewn-01227516-n

2. VERB

/ əˈfrʌnt / (AmE), / əˈfrʌnt / (BrE)
to insult or treat someone openly with disrespect
openly insult or disrespect

Examples

The critic publicly affronted the artist with harsh comments about her work.
I was deeply affronted by his careless and dismissive tone during the meeting.
He did not mean to affront you with his blunt remarks, but his words sounded harsh.

Synset Examples

He insulted her with his rude remarks
the student who had betrayed his classmate was dissed by everyone

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insult - to speak or act in a way that is rude or offensive to someone
  • Synonym: to offend - to cause someone to feel hurt, upset, or angry
  • Antonym: respect - to show consideration or regard for someone

Etymology

From Old French 'affronter' (to confront), from Latin 'ad frontem' (to the face).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
