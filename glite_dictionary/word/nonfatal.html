nonfatal

1. ADJ

/ ˌnɑnˈfeɪtəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈfeɪtəl / (BrE)
not causing death; not resulting in death
not causing death

Examples

Although the accident was serious, her injuries were nonfatal and she made a full recovery.
Doctors confirmed he had suffered a nonfatal heart attack last year.
Many workplace incidents are nonfatal, but they still require proper reporting and care.

Synset Examples

nonfatal heart attack

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fatal - causing death

Etymology

From prefix non- (not) + fatal (from Latin fatalis, meaning deadly).
