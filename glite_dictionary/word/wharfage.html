wharfage

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔr.fɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈwɔː.fɪdʒ / (BrE)
a fee charged for the use of a wharf or quay, especially for loading, unloading, or storing cargo
fee for using a wharf or quay

Examples

The shipping company included wharfage in the total cost of transporting the goods.
Additional wharfage fees applied because the cargo remained on the quay overnight.
Importers often negotiate wharfage charges with the port authorities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dockage - a fee charged for using a dock

Etymology

From 'wharf' (platform built on the shore for loading and unloading ships) + '-age' (indicating a fee or charge).

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔrfɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːfɪdʒ / (BrE)
A platform built out from the shore into the water and supported by piles, used for providing access to ships and boats.
platform extending from shore to water for ships

Examples

Cargo was swiftly moved from the trucks onto the wharfage before being loaded onto the ship.
The port authorities charge a fee for use of the wharfage facilities.
The old wharfage has been renovated to accommodate larger vessels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dock - a platform for loading and unloading ships
  • Synonym: pier - a structure projecting into water used as a landing place or promenade

Etymology

From 'wharf' + '-age', denoting place or charge related to a wharf.
