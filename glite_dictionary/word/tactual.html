tactual

1. ADJ

/ ˈtæk.tʃu.əl / (AmE), / ˈtæk.tʃu.əl / (BrE)
relating to or produced by the sense of touch; tactile.
relating to the sense of touch

Examples

The therapist used materials with different tactual properties to help stimulate the patient's sense of touch.
Some people are especially sensitive to tactual sensations, noticing even slight changes in fabric texture.
The tactual feedback from the device makes it easier for users to operate without looking.

Synset Examples

tactile qualities
the tactual luxury of stroking silky human hair

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tactile - relating to the sense of touch

Etymology

From Latin tactilis (tangible, that can be touched), from tangere (to touch).

2. ADJ

/ ˈtæk.tʃu.əl / (AmE), / ˈtak.tjʊ.əl / (BrE)
relating to or arising from the sense of touch
relating to the sense of touch

Examples

The blind student relied on tactual cues to read braille efficiently.
Researchers measured the tactual sensitivity of participants using different textures.
The device provides tactual feedback when you press a virtual button.

Synset Examples

haptic data
a tactile reflex

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tactile - relating to the sense of touch
  • Synonym: haptic - relating to the sense of touch, especially in technology

Etymology

From Latin 'tactilis' (tangible, touchable), from 'tangere' (to touch).
