transducer

1. NOUN

/ trænzˈdusər / (AmE), / trænzˈdjuːsə / (BrE)
An electrical device that converts energy from one form into another, especially from physical signals to electrical signals or vice versa.
device converting energy forms

Examples

The microphone is a type of transducer that converts sound waves into electrical signals.
Ultrasound machines use a transducer to send and receive high-frequency sound waves.
An engineer tested the transducer to ensure it accurately converted pressure changes into voltage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sensor - a device that detects or measures physical properties and records, indicates, or otherwise responds

Etymology

From Latin 'transducere' (lead across), modeled after 'conductor'.
