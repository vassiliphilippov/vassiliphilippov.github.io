marionette

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæriəˈnɛt / (AmE), / ˌmærɪəˈnɛt / (BrE)
A puppet representing a human figure, manipulated from above by strings or wires attached to its limbs.
puppet controlled by strings

Examples

The puppeteer skillfully made the marionette dance across the stage.
As a child, she was fascinated by the marionette shows at the fair.
The marionette dangled from its strings, waiting for the performance to begin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: puppet - an object, often shaped like a person or animal, that can be moved by hand or strings

Etymology

From French 'marionnette', diminutive of 'Marion', a personal name.
