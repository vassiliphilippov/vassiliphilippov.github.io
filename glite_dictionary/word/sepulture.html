sepulture

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛp.əltʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɛp.əl.tjʊə / (BrE)
A burial chamber; a place of interment such as a tomb or grave.
burial chamber; tomb or grave

Examples

The ancient king was laid to rest in a grand sepulture beneath the temple.
Archaeologists recently discovered a hidden sepulture containing gold and pottery.
Poets often describe the earth as a vast sepulture for all living things.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tomb - a large burial chamber, typically an underground one
  • Synonym: grave - a place of burial for a dead body

Etymology

From Latin 'sepultura', meaning burial.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛp.əl.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɛp.əl.tjʊə / (BrE)
the act or process of burying a dead body; burial, especially with ritual observance
the act of burial

Examples

The ancient tombs revealed evidence of elaborate sepulture practices.
Religious customs governed every aspect of the sepulture of community members.
They gathered to witness the solemn sepulture of their beloved leader.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burial - the act of placing a dead body in the ground
  • Synonym: interment - the burial of a corpse in a grave or tomb

Etymology

From Latin 'sepultura' (burial), from 'sepelire' (to bury).
