origination

1. NOUN

/ əˌrɪdʒəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌrɪdʒɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of beginning something; the initial stage or starting point of an event or development
the act of beginning; starting point

Examples

The origination of the project can be traced back to 2010.
Understanding the origination of these problems helps us find effective solutions.
Many legends attempt to explain the origination of the universe.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: origin - the point or place where something begins
  • Antonym: end - the final part or conclusion of something

Etymology

From Medieval Latin originationem (the beginning of something), from Latin originare (to rise, begin).

2. NOUN

/ əˌrɪdʒ.əˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / əˌrɪdʒ.ɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of beginning something or causing something to start for the first time; the introduction of something new
the act of starting or introducing something new

Examples

The origination of the company can be traced back to 1987.
She played a key role in the origination of the new policy.
Many scientific breakthroughs are the result of creative origination.

Synset Examples

she looked forward to her initiation as an adult
the foundation of a new scientific society

Related Terms

  • Synonym: initiation - the action of beginning something or being introduced to something for the first time
  • Synonym: foundation - the act of establishing an institution or organization

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'originationem', from Latin 'origo' meaning 'origin'.
