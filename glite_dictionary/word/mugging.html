mugging

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌɡɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmʌɡɪŋ / (BrE)
an act of attacking someone in order to steal their money or belongings, typically in a public place
robbery by assault in public

Examples

He was the victim of a mugging while walking home late at night.
There has been a rise in muggings in the downtown area.
The police are working to reduce incidents of mugging in the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robbery - the crime of stealing from someone or somewhere, especially using violence or threat
  • Synonym: assault - a physical attack

Etymology

From verb 'mug' (attack and rob), likely from slang 'mug', meaning face (target for attack).
