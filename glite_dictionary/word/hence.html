hence

1. ADV

/ hɛns / (AmE), / hɛns / (BrE)
From this time; after the present time.
from this time; in the future

Examples

They plan to expand the business five years hence.
A month hence, the new law will be enforced.
The effects of this decision will be felt generations hence.

Synset Examples

a year hence it will be forgotten

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old English 'heonan' (away, hence), influenced by Old French 'hens'.

3. ADV

/ hɛns / (AmE), / hɛns / (BrE)
as a result of the fact just mentioned; for that reason; therefore.
for that reason; therefore

Examples

The roads were icy and hence dangerous to drive on last night.
She is the eldest student and hence expected to lead the group.
Their offer was too low and was hence rejected by the seller.
