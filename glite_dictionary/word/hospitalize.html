to hospitalize

1. VERB

/ ˈhɑːspɪtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈhɒspɪtəlaɪz / (BrE)
to admit someone to a hospital for medical care or treatment
admit to a hospital

Examples

The doctors decided to hospitalize him after he developed severe breathing problems.
If the symptoms worsen, we may need to hospitalize the patient for observation.
She was hospitalized last year due to a heart condition.

Synset Examples

Mother had to be hospitalized because her blood pressure was too high

Related Terms

  • Antonym: discharge - to officially allow someone to leave a hospital

Etymology

From hospital + -ize, meaning to make or to place in a hospital.
