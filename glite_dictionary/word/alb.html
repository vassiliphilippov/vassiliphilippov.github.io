alb

1. NOUN

/ ælb / (AmE), / ælb / (BrE)
A long white linen garment with sleeves, worn by priests and others participating in Christian liturgical services.
long white vestment worn by priests

Examples

The priest donned his alb before the morning Mass.
Her father carefully folded his white alb after the service.
The ornate border on the bishop's alb was embroidered by hand.

Etymology

From Latin 'albus' (white), referring to the garment's color.
