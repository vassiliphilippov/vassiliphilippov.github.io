Caesarism

1. NOUN

/ ˈsiːzərˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈsiːzərɪzəm / (BrE)
a political system in which a ruler exercises absolute power, often in the manner of Julius Caesar, without constitutional restraints
absolute rule by a dictator

Examples

After the coup, the country descended into Caesarism, with the general ruling by decree.
Modern historians often warn of the dangers of Caesarism in fragile democracies.
Some critics accused the leader of promoting Caesarism through his disregard for legal limits.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dictatorship - government by a single ruler with total power

Etymology

From Julius Caesar, the Roman dictator, combined with the suffix -ism.
