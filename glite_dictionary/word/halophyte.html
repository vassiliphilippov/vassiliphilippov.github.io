halophyte

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæləˌfaɪt / (AmE), / ˈhaləˌfʌɪt / (BrE)
a plant that naturally grows in very salty soil or environments
plant growing in salty soil

Examples

The mangrove tree is a well-known halophyte that thrives in coastal environments.
Researchers study halophytes to understand how plants survive in saline conditions.
Some halophyte species are used for soil reclamation in salt-affected regions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: glycophyte - a plant that is not tolerant of saline soil

Etymology

From Greek hals (salt) + phyton (plant).
