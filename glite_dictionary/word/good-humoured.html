good-humoured

1. ADJ

/ ɡʊd ˈhjuːmɚd / (AmE), / ɡʊd ˈhjuːməd / (BrE)
cheerful and pleasant in mood or attitude; having a kindly or agreeable disposition
cheerful and pleasant in manner
british

Examples

She remained good-humoured throughout the long and difficult meeting.
His good-humoured remarks lightened the mood during the tense negotiations.
Despite the disappointment, she responded in a good-humoured way.

Synset Examples

an amiable villain with a cocky sidelong grin

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amiable - having a friendly and pleasant manner
  • Antonym: ill-tempered - easily annoyed or made angry; having a bad temper

Etymology

From 'good' + 'humour', with 'humour' from Old French 'humor', from Latin 'humor' (moisture, fluid, mood).
