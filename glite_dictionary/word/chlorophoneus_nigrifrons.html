Chlorophoneus nigrifrons

1. PROPN

/ kloʊˌrɒfoʊˈniːəs ˌnɪɡrɪˈfrɒnz / (AmE), / klɒˌrɒfəniˈuːs ˌnɪɡrɪˈfrɒnz / (BrE)
A species of bush shrike found in parts of sub-Saharan Africa, known for its distinctive black forehead and vocalizations.
species of bush shrike

Examples

The Chlorophoneus nigrifrons can often be heard singing in the dense woodlands of East Africa.
Researchers observed a nesting pair of Chlorophoneus nigrifrons near the riverbank.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot a juvenile Chlorophoneus nigrifrons during their safari.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: genus name 'Chlorophoneus' (from Greek khlōros, 'green' + phōnē, 'sound/voice') and specific epithet 'nigrifrons' (Latin, 'black forehead').
