Chrysothamnus nauseosus

1. NOUN

/ kraɪsəˈθæmnəs nɔˈsiːəsəs / (AmE), / krɪsəˈθamnəs nɔːˈsiːəsəs / (BrE)
A species of aromatic shrub with slender, hairy branches and dense clusters of small yellow flowers, native to western North America and used as a source of yellow dye.
a species of aromatic shrub native to western North America

Examples

Chrysothamnus nauseosus is commonly found in arid regions across the western United States.
The Navajo people traditionally use Chrysothamnus nauseosus to produce a yellow dye for textiles.
Many jackrabbits seek shelter among the dense branches of Chrysothamnus nauseosus during the hottest parts of the day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rabbitbrush - common name for Chrysothamnus nauseosus and related shrubs

Etymology

From Greek *chrysos* (gold) and *thamnos* (shrub), with Latin *nauseosus* (nauseous).
