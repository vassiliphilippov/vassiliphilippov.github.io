bankable

1. ADJ

/ ˈbæŋ.kə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈbaŋ.kə.bəl / (BrE)
(of money, checks, etc.) acceptable for deposit or processing at a bank.
acceptable to or at a bank

Examples

Only bankable checks are accepted by the cashier.
Please ensure that your deposit contains only bankable funds.
The company needs to focus on generating bankable assets.

Synset Examples

bankable funds

Etymology

From 'bank' (financial institution) + '-able' (able to be).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00018107-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈbæŋkəbəl / (AmE), / ˈbaŋkəbl / (BrE)
(of a person, project, or product) considered likely to generate profit, revenue, or success, especially in business or entertainment.
likely to bring profit or success

Examples

The studio chose a bankable actor to ensure the film's commercial success.
Her latest project is considered highly bankable by investors.
Without a bankable name attached, the production struggled to attract funding.

Synset Examples

without bankable stars the film script aroused no interest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: profitable - yielding financial gain or profit
  • Antonym: unprofitable - not producing profit or financial gain

Etymology

From 'bank' (financial institution or money) + '-able' (able to), referring to something capable of producing profit.
