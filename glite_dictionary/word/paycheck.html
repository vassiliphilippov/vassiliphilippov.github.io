paycheck

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪˌtʃɛk / (AmE), / ˈpeɪˌtʃɛk / (BrE)
A check given to an employee as payment for work done or salary earned.
a check for wages or salary
american

Examples

He was thrilled when he received his first paycheck from his new job.
Many people rely on their paycheck to cover monthly expenses.
The company offers direct deposit instead of a paper paycheck.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salary - regular payment to an employee, typically monthly
  • Synonym: wage - payment for labor or services, usually hourly or daily

Etymology

From 'pay' (wages) + 'check' (written order for payment), first used in US English in early 20th century.
