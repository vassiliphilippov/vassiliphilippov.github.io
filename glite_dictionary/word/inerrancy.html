inerrancy

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈnɛrənsi / (AmE), / ɪˈnɛrənsi / (BrE)
the quality of being incapable of error; in theology, the belief that certain texts, especially the Bible, are free from error.
incapability of being wrong, esp. in theology

Examples

The doctrine of inerrancy holds that the Bible is without error in all its teachings.
Many theologians debate the concept of biblical inerrancy and its implications for faith.
He defended his position strongly, emphasizing the inerrancy of the scriptures.

Synset Examples

biblical inerrancy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: infallibility - the quality of being incapable of making mistakes or being wrong

Etymology

From Latin 'inerrantia,' from 'in-' (not) + 'errare' (to err, wander).
