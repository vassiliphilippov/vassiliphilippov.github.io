tacitly

1. ADV

/ ˈtæsɪtli / (AmE), / ˈtæsɪtli / (BrE)
In a way that is understood or implied without being stated directly; by unspoken agreement or understanding.
in an unspoken or implied manner

Examples

He tacitly agreed to the proposal by remaining silent throughout the meeting.
Management tacitly approved of the changes by not raising any objections.
Their partnership was tacitly understood, even though nothing was put in writing.

Synset Examples

they are tacitly expected to work 10 hours a day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: implicitly - in a way that is not directly expressed; without doubt or question
  • Antonym: explicitly - in a clear and detailed manner, leaving no room for confusion or doubt

Etymology

From Latin 'tacitus' (silent, unspoken) + English adverbial suffix '-ly'.
