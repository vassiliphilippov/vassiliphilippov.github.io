disk

1. NOUN

/ dɪsk / (AmE), / dɪsk / (BrE)
A flat, thin, circular object or plate.
flat circular object or plate

Examples

The waiter brought our appetizers on a small metal disk.
The moon appeared as a bright disk in the night sky.
He inserted a round disk into the machine to start it working.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disc - Alternative British spelling of 'disk', often used for flat circular objects.

Etymology

From Latin discus (quoit, disk), via Greek diskos (disk, platter).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03213277-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ dɪsk / (AmE), / dɪsk / (BrE)
A flat, circular device coated with magnetic material used to store digital information in computers and related systems.
magnetic storage device for computers

Examples

We need to back up all the important files on the disk before updating the software.
The computer cannot start because there is no disk in the drive.
Over time, dust can cause errors when reading data from a disk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hard disk - A rigid magnetic disk used for computer storage
  • Synonym: floppy disk - A flexible removable magnetic disk for data storage
  • Antonym: optical disc - A disk storing data read by lasers, such as a CD or DVD
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03712192-n
Synset POS: n

flat, circular object or surface

Examples

5. NOUN

/ dɪsk / (AmE), / dɪsk / (BrE)
a flat, circular object used for storing sound recordings, especially a phonograph record with a continuous groove that is played by a stylus
phonograph record; audio recording medium

Examples

He placed the vintage disk on the turntable and gently set the needle.
Collectors prize rare jazz disks for their unique cover art and sound quality.
The library has an extensive collection of classical music on disk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: record - a disc for storing sound; phonograph record

Etymology

From Latin 'discus' (quoit, disk), via French 'disque'.
