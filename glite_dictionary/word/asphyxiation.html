asphyxiation

1. NOUN

/ æsˌfɪksiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / æsˌfɪksɪˈeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the condition of being deprived of oxygen, which may lead to unconsciousness or death
state of oxygen deprivation
medical

Examples

The cause of death was determined to be asphyxiation due to smoke inhalation.
Improper use of certain chemicals can lead to accidental asphyxiation.
The victim suffered asphyxiation after being trapped in a poorly ventilated room.

Synset Examples

asphyxiation is sometimes used as a form of torture

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suffocation - struggling to breathe or being unable to get enough air

Etymology

From Greek 'asphyxia' (stopping of the pulse) via Latin and French.

2. NOUN

/ æsˌfɪksiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / æsˌfɪksiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
The state or process of being deprived of oxygen, resulting in unconsciousness or death; suffocation.
deprivation of oxygen; suffocation
medical

Examples

The cause of death was determined to be asphyxiation due to lack of oxygen.
Accidental asphyxiation can occur in confined spaces with poor ventilation.
The fire produced so much smoke that several people died of asphyxiation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suffocation - the process of dying from lack of air or inability to breathe
  • Antonym: resuscitation - the act of reviving someone from unconsciousness or apparent death

Etymology

From Greek 'asphyxia' (stopping of the pulse) + English '-ation'.
