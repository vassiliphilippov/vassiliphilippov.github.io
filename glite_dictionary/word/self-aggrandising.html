self-aggrandising

1. ADJ

/ ˌsɛlf əˈɡræn.daɪz.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf əˈɡræn.daɪz.ɪŋ / (BrE)
behaving in a way that seeks to make oneself seem more important or powerful than is actually the case
seeking to appear more important

Examples

His self-aggrandising remarks made him unpopular with the rest of the team.
The politician's self-aggrandising speeches often overshadowed the issues at hand.
Critics accused the author of being self-aggrandising in her memoir.

Synset Examples

big talk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boastful - showing excessive pride and self-satisfaction in one's achievements
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance

Etymology

From self- + aggrandising, the latter from French 'agrandir' (to enlarge), ultimately from Latin 'grandis' (large, great).

2. ADJ

/ ˌsɛlf əˈɡræn.dəˌzaɪ.zɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌself əˈɡræn.dɪ.zaɪ.zɪŋ / (BrE)
characterized by an attempt to make oneself appear more important, powerful, or capable than is true; relating to or displaying self-aggrandizement
seeking to make oneself seem more important

Examples

Her self-aggrandising speech made her colleagues feel uncomfortable.
The company's self-aggrandising advertisements exaggerated its achievements.
He has a self-aggrandising tendency to take credit for others' work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-aggrandizement - the action or process of promoting oneself as more powerful or important
  • Antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's own importance

Etymology

From self- + aggrandising, ultimately from French *agrandir* (to make larger) and Latin *grandis* (great).
