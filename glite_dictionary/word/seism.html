seism

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈsiːzəm / (BrE)
A shaking and vibration of the Earth's surface caused by underground movement along a fault or by volcanic activity; an earthquake or tremor.
earthquake or earth tremor

Examples

Scientists use sensitive instruments to detect even the smallest seism deep within the earth.
The recent volcanic eruption was preceded by a series of minor seisms.
After the seism, emergency crews checked for damage to buildings and roads.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: earthquake - A sudden, violent shaking of the Earth's surface
  • Synonym: tremor - A small or slight shaking movement, especially of the ground

Etymology

From Greek seismos (a shaking or earthquake), from seiein (to shake).
