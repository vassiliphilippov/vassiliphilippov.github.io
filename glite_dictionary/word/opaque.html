opaque

1. ADJ

/ oʊˈpeɪk / (AmE), / əʊˈpeɪk / (BrE)
not letting light pass through; not transparent or translucent
not allowing light to pass through

Examples

The bathroom window was made of opaque glass to ensure privacy.
Thick clouds made the sky completely opaque, blocking out the sun.
The liquid turned opaque after adding the chemical, making it impossible to see through.

Synset Examples

opaque windows of the jail
opaque to X-rays

Related Terms

  • Antonym: transparent - allowing light to pass through so that objects behind can be seen clearly
  • Antonym: translucent - allowing some light to pass through but not detailed shapes

Etymology

From Latin 'opacus' (shaded, dark).

2. ADJ

/ oʊˈpeɪk / (AmE), / əʊˈpeɪk / (BrE)
difficult or impossible to understand; not clear or transparent in meaning or expression.
hard to understand; not clear in meaning

Examples

The professor's lecture was so opaque that few students could follow his main points.
Some legal documents are written in opaque language that confuses most readers.
Her writing style can be opaque, making it difficult to grasp her arguments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: obscure - not clearly expressed or easily understood
  • Antonym: lucid - expressed clearly; easy to understand

Etymology

From Latin 'opaquus' (dark, shady).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00537516-s
Synset POS: s
