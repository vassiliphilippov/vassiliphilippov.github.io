manganese

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæŋɡəˌniz / (AmE), / ˈmaŋgəniːz / (BrE)
a hard, brittle, grayish-white metallic element (symbol Mn) that is not magnetic, used principally in making steel and found in many minerals
a metallic element, symbol Mn

Examples

Adding manganese to steel increases its strength and durability.
The geologist identified the mineral sample as containing high levels of manganese.
Industrial batteries often use manganese as an important component.

Etymology

From French manganèse, from Italian manganese, alteration of magnesia, from the region of Magnesia in Greece.
