spectrum

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɛk.trəm / (AmE), / ˈspɛk.trəm / (BrE)
A broad range of related objects, values, qualities, ideas, or activities, often arranged in order or sequence.
a wide range or series

Examples

There is a wide spectrum of opinions among the group members.
The artist's work covers a broad spectrum of styles and influences.
Mental health exists on a spectrum, not as separate categories.

Etymology

From Latin spectrum (appearance, image, apparition).

2. NOUN

/ ˈspɛktrəm / (AmE), / ˈspɛktrəm / (BrE)
A band of colours or a range of radiation energies arranged in order, especially as seen in a rainbow or produced by separating light or other radiation into its constituent wavelengths.
ordered range of colors or energies

Examples

The scientist examined the spectrum produced when white light passed through a prism.
Astronomers analyze the spectrum of starlight to learn about the composition of stars.
A rainbow displays the full visible spectrum after a storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rainbow - an arc of colours in the sky caused by sunlight and moisture

Etymology

From Latin *spectrum* (appearance, image, spectre), from *specere* (to look).
