later

1. ADJ

/ ˈleɪtər / (AmE), / ˈleɪtə / (BrE)
occurring or coming at or near the end of a period or stage; subsequent in time or development
happening toward the end

Examples

The symptoms became more severe in the later stages of the illness.
In his later years, he focused on writing poetry instead of novels.
The later chapters of the book explore advanced topics in detail.

Synset Examples

the late phase of feudalism
a later symptom of the disease
later medical science could have saved the child

Related Terms

  • Antonym: early - occurring at or near the beginning of a period or stage

2. ADV

/ ˈleɪtər / (AmE), / ˈleɪtə / (BrE)
at a time in the near or far future; after the present or a previously specified time
at a future time; afterwards

Examples

I'll talk to you later when I'm free.
She decided to finish the report later that night.
Let's meet for coffee later, after your meeting is over.

Synset Examples

By and by he'll understand
I'll see you later

Related Terms

  • Synonym: afterwards - at a later time; subsequently
  • Antonym: sooner - at an earlier time

Etymology

From late + -er, originally meaning 'more late' or 'at a more advanced time.'

3. ADV

/ ˈleɪtər / (AmE), / ˈleɪtə / (BrE)
at a time after the one already mentioned or implied
at a subsequent time

Examples

I'll call you later when I'm finished with my work.
They decided to meet for dinner later that night.
She didn't understand the joke at first, but later she laughed.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sooner - at an earlier time
  • Synonym: afterward - after the time in question; subsequently

Etymology

From Old English 'lætra', comparative of 'læt' (late).

comparative of 'late'; at a more advanced time

Examples
