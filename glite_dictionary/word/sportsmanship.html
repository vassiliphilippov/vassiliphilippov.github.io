sportsmanship

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɔrts.mənˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈspɔːts.mən.ʃɪp / (BrE)
fair and generous behavior or treatment of others, especially in a sporting contest; fairness in following the rules of the game.
fairness and respect in sports

Examples

Good sportsmanship requires that you respect your opponents and the referees at all times.
His display of sportsmanship after losing the match earned him praise from fans and officials.
Poor sportsmanship can ruin the experience for everyone involved in the game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fairness - impartial and just treatment or behavior without favoritism or discrimination
  • Antonym: gamesmanship - the use of dubious methods to win games

Etymology

Formed from 'sportsman' + '-ship', denoting the qualities of a good sportsman.
