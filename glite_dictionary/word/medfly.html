medfly

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛdˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmed.flʌɪ / (BrE)
a small fruit fly, especially the Mediterranean fruit fly, that damages citrus and other fruits by laying eggs that hatch inside the fruit
a fruit fly damaging citrus and other fruits

Examples

The orchard was quarantined after officials found evidence of medfly infestation.
A single medfly can cause serious damage to entire fruit crops by laying eggs inside the fruit.
Farmers use traps and chemical treatments to control the spread of the medfly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fruit fly - small flying insect whose larvae infest and ruin fruit

Etymology

Shortened from Mediterranean fruit fly.
