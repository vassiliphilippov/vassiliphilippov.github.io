bawd

1. NOUN

/ bɔd / (AmE), / bɔːd / (BrE)
(archaic) A woman who engages in sexual intercourse for money; a prostitute.
archaic term for prostitute
archaic

Examples

In Shakespeare's plays, the character of the bawd often plays a comedic yet dubious role.
The old bawd ran a disreputable inn near the city gate.
Historical records mention a notorious bawd who was arrested for running a brothel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prostitute - a person who engages in sexual activity for payment

Etymology

From Middle English bawde, possibly from Old French baude (merry, bold), later associated with prostitution.
