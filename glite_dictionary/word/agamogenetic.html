agamogenetic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæɡəˌmoʊdʒəˈnɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌaɡəˌməʊdʒɪˈnɛtɪk / (BrE)
(Biology) Relating to reproduction that does not involve the fusion of male and female gametes.
non-gamete-fusion reproductive process
medical

Examples

Some simple organisms reproduce through agamogenetic means, bypassing the need for fertilization.
Researchers observed that the plant's agamogenetic reproduction allowed for rapid colonization of the area.
Certain types of worms can undergo agamogenetic development, producing offspring without mating.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sexual - Involving the fusion of male and female gametes in reproduction
  • Synonym: asexual - Not involving sexual reproduction or the fusion of gametes

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (not) + 'gamete' (wife, marriage) + 'genesis' (origin, creation).
