Limanda ferruginea

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈmændə fɛrʊˈdʒiniə / (AmE), / lɪˈmandə fɛrʊˈdʒiːniə / (BrE)
An American flounder with a yellowish tail, commonly known as the yellowtail flounder, found in the northwestern Atlantic Ocean.
American flounder with a yellow tail

Examples

The fisherman was thrilled to catch a large Limanda ferruginea off the coast of Nova Scotia.
Scientists are studying the population trends of Limanda ferruginea in the Atlantic Ocean.
Grilled Limanda ferruginea is a delicacy in many seafood restaurants along the northeastern US coast.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yellowtail flounder - Common name for Limanda ferruginea

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Limanda' and species 'ferruginea', meaning 'rust-colored'.
