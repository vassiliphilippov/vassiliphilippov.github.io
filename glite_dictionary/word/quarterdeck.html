quarterdeck

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɔrtərˌdɛk / (AmE), / ˈkwɔːtədɛk / (BrE)
the part of a ship's upper deck near the stern, traditionally reserved for officers and ceremonial functions
stern area of a ship's upper deck

Examples

The captain addressed the crew from the quarterdeck.
Visitors were not allowed on the quarterdeck without permission.
During the ceremony, the officers stood on the quarterdeck to observe the proceedings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stern - rear part of a ship
  • Antonym: foredeck - the part of a ship's deck nearest the bow

Etymology

From 'quarter' (a part or division) and 'deck' (a floor on a ship), originally referring to a quarter portion of the deck.
