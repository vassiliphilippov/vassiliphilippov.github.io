Ravenala

1. NOUN

/ ˌrævəˈnɑːlə / (AmE), / ˌravəˈnɑːlə / (BrE)
A genus of woody tropical plants with tall trunks, including the traveler's palm, native to Madagascar and sometimes placed in the Musaceae family.
genus of woody tropical plants; traveler's palm

Examples

The Ravenala is often called the traveler's palm due to the way its leaves hold water.
Botanists recently reclassified the Ravenala into its own family distinct from Musaceae.
Tourists are always fascinated by the towering trunk of the Ravenala in Madagascar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Musaceae - the banana family of flowering plants

Etymology

From Malagasy 'ravinala' meaning 'forest leaf'.
