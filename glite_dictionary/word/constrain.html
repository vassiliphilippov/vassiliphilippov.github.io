to constrain

1. VERB

/ kənˈstreɪn / (AmE), / kənˈstreɪn / (BrE)
to limit someone or something, or restrict free movement or action
to restrict or limit movement or action

Examples

The prison walls constrain the movement of the inmates.
She felt constrained by the rigid rules of her office environment.
Laws are designed to constrain certain behaviors in society.

Synset Examples

This holds the local until the express passengers change trains
About a dozen animals were held inside the stockade
The illegal immigrants were held at a detention center
The terrorists held the journalists for ransom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to restrict - to limit the size, amount, or range of something
  • Synonym: to confine - to keep or restrict someone or something within certain limits
  • Antonym: free - to release from restriction or confinement

Etymology

From Latin 'constringere' (to bind together, constrict).

2. VERB

/ kənˈstreɪn / (AmE), / kənˈstreɪn / (BrE)
to severely restrict the scope, extent, or freedom of someone or something
restrict severely; limit tightly

Examples

Strict regulations constrain the ability of companies to expand into new markets.
Her options were severely constrained by financial limitations.
Efforts to improve efficiency may constrain staff from taking necessary breaks.

Synset Examples

tighten the rules
stiffen the regulations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to restrict - to limit the size, amount, or range of something
  • Synonym: limit - to place boundaries or limits on something
  • Antonym: free - to allow complete liberty; not restricted

Etymology

From Latin constrinǧere, constrict, bind together.

Source: WordNet

3. VERB

/ kənˈstreɪn / (AmE), / kənˈstreɪn / (BrE)
to compel or force someone to behave in a particular way, especially by limiting their freedom or options
compel or force to behave in a certain way

Examples

The strict rules constrain his ability to express himself freely.
She felt constrained to follow her parents' wishes despite her own dreams.
Legal obligations can sometimes constrain companies from acting quickly.

Synset Examples

Social relations impose courtesy
duty constrains one to act often contrary to one's desires or inclinations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to compel - force someone to do something
  • Synonym: to restrain - hold back from action or restrict movement
  • Antonym: free - not under restriction or compulsion

Etymology

From Latin 'constringere' (to bind together, tie tightly).
