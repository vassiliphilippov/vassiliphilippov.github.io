exasperated

1. ADJ

/ ɪɡˈzæspəˌreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɑːspəreɪtɪd / (BrE)
very annoyed and upset, often because something is difficult or frustrating
greatly annoyed or out of patience

Examples

She gave him an exasperated sigh when he forgot his keys again.
After waiting for hours, he sounded exasperated during the phone call.
The teacher had an exasperated expression after explaining the instructions for the third time.

Synset Examples

had an exasperated look on his face
felt exasperated beyond endurance

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frustrated - feeling annoyed and impatient because of inability to do something
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other emotions

Etymology

From Latin exasperare (to irritate, provoke), via past participle exasperatus.
