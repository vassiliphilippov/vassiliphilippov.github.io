informant

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɔrmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɔːmənt / (BrE)
a person who provides information to an individual or organization, often secretly or confidentially
person who supplies information, often secretly

Examples

The police relied on an informant to learn about the drug ring’s activities.
An anonymous informant leaked the documents to the press.
The researcher interviewed each informant to gather data for the study.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whistleblower - a person who exposes information about wrongdoing within an organization
  • Synonym: source - a person or document that provides information
  • Antonym: suspect - a person thought to be guilty in an investigation

Etymology

From Latin informant-, informans (present participle of informare 'to inform').

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɔrmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɔːmənt / (BrE)
a person who supplies information about an event, often to authorities or journalists; especially someone who reports what they have witnessed.
person who supplies information

Examples

The police relied on the informant to provide details about the robbery.
An anonymous informant explained to reporters exactly what happened at the scene.
The judge listened carefully as the informant described what she had witnessed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: witness - a person who sees an event, typically a crime or accident, take place

Etymology

From Latin informant-, informans, present participle of informare (to inform).
