to bereave

1. VERB

/ bɪˈriv / (AmE), / bɪˈriːv / (BrE)
to deprive someone of a loved one through death
deprive of a loved one by death

Examples

The tragic accident bereaved the family of their beloved daughter.
War has bereaved many communities of their young men.
Those who are bereaved often need support and comfort from friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to deprive - to prevent someone from having or using something
  • Antonym: console - to comfort someone who is grieving

Etymology

From Old English 'bereafian', meaning to deprive or rob.
