subtonic

1. NOUN

/ sʌbˈtɑnɪk / (AmE), / sʌbˈtɒnɪk / (BrE)
(music) the seventh note of the diatonic scale, lying a whole step below the tonic.
seventh note of the diatonic scale

Examples

In the key of C major, the subtonic is the note B♭.
Many folk melodies make use of the subtonic instead of the leading tone.
The subtonic provides a different harmonic feel compared to the traditional leading note.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leading tone - the seventh note of the scale, a half step below the tonic

Etymology

From Latin 'sub' (under) + 'tonic' (the key note).
