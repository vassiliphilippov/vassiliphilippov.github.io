so-so

1. ADJ

/ ˈsoʊ ˌsoʊ / (AmE), / ˈsəʊ ˌsəʊ / (BrE)
(of quality, condition, or performance) neither good nor bad; mediocre.
neither good nor bad; mediocre

Examples

The food at the restaurant was just so-so, nothing special.
When I asked how she was feeling, she said she was so-so.
His performance in the last game was merely so-so compared to his usual standard.

Synset Examples

an indifferent performance
a gifted painter but an indifferent actor
a so-so golfer
feeling only so-so

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mediocre - of only moderate quality; not very good
  • Degree Higher: excellent - extremely good; outstanding
  • Degree Lower: poor - of a low standard or quality

2. ADV

/ ˈsoʊˌsoʊ / (AmE), / ˈsəʊˌsəʊ / (BrE)
in a way that is satisfactory but not particularly good; moderately well
acceptably but not outstandingly

Examples

He answered the teacher's question so-so, without much confidence.
The cake turned out so-so, so I probably won't use that recipe again.
My performance at work has been so-so lately, but I'm hoping to improve.

Synset Examples

she plays tennis tolerably

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tolerably - in a way that is fairly good; acceptably
  • Synonym: mediocrely - in a mediocre or average manner
