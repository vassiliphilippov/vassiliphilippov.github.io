petroglyph

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛtrəˌɡlɪf / (AmE), / ˈpɛtrəˌɡlɪf / (BrE)
a carving or line drawing on rock, especially one made by prehistoric people
prehistoric rock carving or drawing

Examples

Ancient petroglyphs cover the canyon walls, depicting animals and human figures.
The archaeologists studied the petroglyph to learn about early human life in the region.
Some petroglyphs are thousands of years old and still clearly visible today.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pictograph - a painting or drawing on a rock, especially prehistoric

Etymology

From Greek 'petra' (rock) + 'glyphein' (to carve).
