ambrosial

1. ADJ

/ æmˈbroʊʒiəl / (AmE), / æmˈbrəʊziəl / (BrE)
exceptionally pleasing to the taste, smell, or mind; delicious and fragrant, often in a heavenly or divine sense
extremely pleasing; delicious and fragrant

Examples

The chef's signature dessert had an ambrosial aroma that filled the kitchen.
The garden was filled with the ambrosial scent of blooming jasmine and roses.
She described the honeyed figs as truly ambrosial, fit for the gods.

Synset Examples

a nectarous drink
ambrosial food

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nectarous - pleasant and sweet in taste, like nectar
  • Synonym: delicious - having a very pleasant taste or smell
  • Antonym: disgusting - arousing revulsion or strong dislike, especially in taste or smell

Etymology

From Greek ambrosios (immortal), related to ambrosia, the food of the gods in Greek mythology.

2. ADJ

/ æmˈbroʊʒiəl / (AmE), / æmˈbrəʊziəl / (BrE)
Extremely pleasing to the senses, especially taste or smell; worthy of the gods.
extremely pleasing; like food of the gods

Examples

The chef’s dessert was so ambrosial that guests couldn’t stop raving about it.
We breathed in the ambrosial scent of blooming jasmine on a warm summer evening.
Her garden was filled with ambrosial flowers that seemed almost magical.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delicious - highly pleasant to taste or smell
  • Synonym: divine - having the nature of or being a deity; heavenly in quality

Etymology

From Greek ambrosios (immortal, divine), from ambrotos (immortal), related to ambrosia, the food of the gods in Greek mythology.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01182785-s
Synset POS: s
