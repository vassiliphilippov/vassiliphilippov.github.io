decussate

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈkʌseɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈkʌseɪt / (BrE)
(of parts) crossed or arranged in the form of an X; intersecting at an angle so as to form an X-shape.
crossed in the form of an X

Examples

Some tree leaves are decussate, growing in pairs at right angles to those above and below.
In anatomy, the optic nerves decussate at the optic chiasm.
The decussate pattern can be observed in the arrangement of some plant stems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crossed - arranged so as to intersect; often used in similar contexts

Etymology

From Latin decussatus, past participle of decussare (to cross in the form of an X), from decussis (the Roman numeral for ten, X).

2. VERB

/ dɪˈkʌs.eɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈkʌs.eɪt / (BrE)
(especially of nerves, fibers, or structures) to cross or intersect so as to form a cross or X shape.
to cross or intersect as a cross

Examples

The optic nerve fibers decussate in the brain, forming a characteristic X-shaped structure.
In some plant species, the leaves decussate along the stem for optimal sunlight exposure.
During neuroanatomy class, we learned how nerve pathways decussate at specific points in the body.

Synset Examples

this nerve decussates the other
the fibers decussate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cross - to move, pass, or extend across something

Etymology

From Latin decussatus, past participle of decussare (to cross in the form of an X), from decussis (the numeral ten, represented as X).
