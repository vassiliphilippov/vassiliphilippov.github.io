priggishness

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪɡɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈprɪɡɪʃnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being excessively proper, self-righteous, or smug in matters of morality or manners
overly self-righteous or proper behavior

Examples

Her priggishness made it hard for her to relate to her more easygoing colleagues.
The novel satirizes the priggishness of Victorian society.
He was criticized for his priggishness when he lectured others about minor rules.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: priggish - excessively proper or self-righteous

Etymology

From 'priggish' (from 'prig', meaning a self-righteously proper person) + '-ness'.
