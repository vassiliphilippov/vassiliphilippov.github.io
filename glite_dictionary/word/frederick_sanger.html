Frederick Sanger

1. PROPN

/ ˈfrɛdərɪk ˈsæŋər / (AmE), / ˈfrɛdərɪk ˈsæŋə / (BrE)
(1918–2013) English biochemist who determined the sequence of amino acids in insulin and developed a key technique for sequencing DNA.
English biochemist and DNA sequencing pioneer

Examples

Frederick Sanger twice won the Nobel Prize in Chemistry for his groundbreaking work.
The Sanger sequencing method, named after Frederick Sanger, revolutionized genetics research.
Many scientists are inspired by the achievements of Frederick Sanger in biochemistry.
