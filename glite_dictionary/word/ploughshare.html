ploughshare

1. NOUN

/ ˈplaʊʃɛr / (AmE), / ˈplaʊʃeə / (BrE)
the main cutting blade of a plough, which slices through and loosens the top layer of soil
main cutting blade of a plough

Examples

The ploughshare sliced cleanly through the rich earth, preparing the ground for planting.
Years of use had dulled the ploughshare, so the farmer replaced it before the new season.
Archaeologists uncovered a bronze ploughshare dating back to the Iron Age.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plough - a farm implement used to turn over soil

Etymology

From Old English 'plōgescæru', from 'plōh' (plough) + 'scēaru' (share, cut, slice).
