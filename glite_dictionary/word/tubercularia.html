Tubercularia

1. NOUN

/ tuːˌbɝːkjəˈlɛəriə / (AmE), / tjuːˌbɜːkjʊˈleəriə / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Tuberculariaceae, characterized by nodules of red or pink conidia; some species cause dieback in woody plants.
genus of fungi with red or pink nodules

Examples

The fungus Tubercularia can cause serious dieback in several woody plant species.
Botanists recently discovered a new species in the genus Tubercularia.
Conidia of Tubercularia are typically pink or red in color.

Etymology

From Latin *tuberculum* (small swelling) + New Latin -aria (suffix for genera).

2. NOUN

/ tuːˌbɝːkjəˈlɛriə / (AmE), / tjuːˌbɜːkjʊˈleəriə / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Tuberculariaceae, characterized by nodules of red or pink conidia, some of which cause diebacks of woody plants.
genus of nodular fungi

Examples

Several species within the Tubercularia genus are known to affect the health of woody plants.
Researchers isolated Tubercularia from samples collected in the infected orchard.
The pinkish nodules typical of Tubercularia help mycologists identify the genus.

Etymology

From Latin 'tuberculum' (small swelling, nodule), referencing the nodular appearance.
