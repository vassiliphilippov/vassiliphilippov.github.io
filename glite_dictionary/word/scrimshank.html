to scrimshank

1. VERB

/ ˈskrɪmˌʃæŋk / (AmE), / ˈskrɪmʃæŋk / (BrE)
to avoid one's work or duties, especially by pretending to be ill or otherwise evading responsibility
avoid work or duties

Examples

He would often scrimshank whenever there was heavy lifting to be done.
Accused of trying to scrimshank his duties, the sailor faced strict punishment.
Some workers were known to scrimshank by feigning illness when the supervisor was nearby.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to shirk - to avoid or neglect a duty or responsibility

Etymology

Origin uncertain; first recorded in British naval slang in the late 19th century.
