livery

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪvəri / (AmE), / ˈlɪvəri / (BrE)
(Law, archaic) The formal delivery of possession or legal title of property from one person to another.
formal transfer of possession or title
archaic legal

Examples

The medieval ceremony of livery marked the official transfer of land ownership.
He received livery of seisin from the feudal lord, signifying his new rights.
Legal scholars discussed the historical process of livery during property transfers in England.

Etymology

From Old French livree (delivery), from livrer (to deliver), from Latin liberare (to set free).

/ ˈlɪvəri / (AmE), / ˈlɪvəri / (BrE)
(of a person or appearance) suffering from or suggesting a liver disorder or gastric distress, often characterized by a yellowish complexion.
suggesting liver disorder; sallow appearance
archaic

Examples

After weeks of illness, his livery complexion alarmed his family.
The doctor noted her livery skin tone and ordered blood tests.
A livery appearance can indicate problems with liver function.

Etymology

From Latin *līvēre* (to be bluish), relating to discoloration of the skin.

3. NOUN

/ ˈlɪvəri / (AmE), / ˈlɪvəri / (BrE)
the care, feeding, and stabling of horses for payment at a stable
boarding and care of horses for pay

Examples

The farm offers livery for horses, including daily feeding and turnout.
Many horse owners rely on professional livery services when they cannot care for their animals themselves.
She found a local livery to board her mare during the winter months.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stable - a building for keeping horses

Etymology

From Old French *liveree* (allowance, delivery of goods) and Medieval Latin *liberata* (things delivered), originally referring to provisions or allowances.

4. NOUN

/ ˈlɪvəri / (AmE), / ˈlɪvəri / (BrE)
a special uniform worn by servants, officials, or chauffeurs, especially in the service of a household or institution
special uniform for servants or chauffeurs

Examples

The butler was dressed in full livery for the evening reception.
Each chauffeur wore a matching livery emblazoned with the family crest.
The hotel's doormen are easily recognized by their distinctive livery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uniform - a set of standard clothing worn by members of an organization

Etymology

From Old French livrée, from livrer 'to deliver', originally referring to things delivered, especially clothing distributed to servants.
