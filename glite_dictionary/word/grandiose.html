grandiose

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡræn.diˌoʊs / (AmE), / ˈɡræn.di.əʊs / (BrE)
having an excessively grand or elaborate style or manner, especially in a way that is intended to impress or seems pretentious.
pretentiously grand or elaborate

Examples

His grandiose plans for the new building were far beyond their actual needs.
She spoke in a grandiose tone, as if she were addressing royalty.
They dismissed the proposal as too grandiose for the available budget.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretentious - attempting to impress by affecting greater importance than is actually possessed
  • Antonym: modest - having a humble or moderate opinion of oneself; not grand or boastful

Etymology

From French 'grandiose', from Italian 'grandioso', from 'grande' meaning 'grand'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɡræn.diˌoʊs / (AmE), / ˈɡræn.dɪ.əʊs / (BrE)
Impressive or magnificent in appearance or style, but often in a way that is excessive or intended to impress; more elaborate or complicated than necessary, often with a sense of pretentiousness or exaggeration.
impressively or pretentiously large or elaborate

Examples

The architect designed a grandiose building that was criticized for being overly extravagant.
Her grandiose plans for the party ended up being impractical and costly.
He often makes grandiose promises that he fails to keep.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretentious - attempting to impress by affecting greater importance or merit
  • Antonym: modest - not boastful or pretentious; simple or moderate

Etymology

From French grandiose, from Italian grandioso, from grande (great).
