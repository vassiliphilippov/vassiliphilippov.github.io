Indigofera

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪndɪˈɡoʊfərə / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈɡəʊfərə / (BrE)
a genus of tropical herbs and shrubs with odd-pinnate leaves and spurred flowers in long racemes or spikes, several species of which are used to produce indigo dye
genus of tropical plants used for indigo dye

Examples

Many species of Indigofera are cultivated for their use in producing natural indigo dye.
The Indigofera plants thrive in tropical and subtropical climates across the globe.
Researchers are studying Indigofera for its potential medicinal properties.

Etymology

From Latin, meaning 'indigo-bearing'.

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪndɪˈgoʊfərə / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈgɒfərə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical herbs and shrubs with pinnate leaves and spurred flowers, some species of which are used to produce indigo dye.
genus of tropical herbs/shrubs; source of indigo dye

Examples

Several plants in the Indigofera genus are cultivated for their valuable blue dye.
The leaves of Indigofera are harvested to extract natural indigo pigment.
Botanists have identified over 700 species within the Indigofera genus.

Etymology

From Latin indigo (indigo) + -fera (bearing), meaning 'indigo-bearing'.
