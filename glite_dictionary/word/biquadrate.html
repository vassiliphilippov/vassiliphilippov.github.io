biquadrate

1. NOUN

/ baɪˈkwɑːdreɪt / (AmE), / baɪˈkwɒdreɪt / (BrE)
an algebraic equation or function that is of the fourth degree, especially one in which the highest power of the unknown is four
algebraic equation of the fourth degree

Examples

The mathematician solved the biquadrate using advanced algebraic techniques.
A biquadrate equation is also known as a quartic equation in modern mathematics.
Students were asked to find the roots of a biquadrate function in the test.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quartic - an algebraic equation of the fourth degree

Etymology

From Latin 'biquadratus', from 'bi-' (two) + 'quadratus' (squared)
