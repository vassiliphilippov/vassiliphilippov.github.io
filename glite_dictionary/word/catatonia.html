catatonia

1. NOUN

/ ˌkætəˈtoʊniə / (AmE), / ˌkætəˈtəʊniə / (BrE)
A state of immobility and stupor, often associated with schizophrenia, in which a person may remain motionless for long periods and may occasionally display brief episodes of excitement.
state of stupor and immobility
medical

Examples

After the traumatic event, the patient entered a period of catatonia lasting several days.
Doctors observed signs of catatonia when she became completely unresponsive to any stimuli.
Certain medications are used to treat catatonia associated with severe psychiatric disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stupor - a state of near-unconsciousness or insensibility
  • Antonym: agitation - a state of anxiety or nervous excitement; the opposite of stillness in catatonia

Etymology

From German 'Katatonie', from Greek 'kata' (down) + 'tonos' (tone, tension).

2. NOUN

/ ˌkætəˈtoʊniə / (AmE), / ˌkætəˈtəʊniə / (BrE)
A state of abnormal mental and physical rigidity, often characterized by unresponsiveness and muscular stiffness, commonly seen in certain psychiatric conditions such as schizophrenia.
state of abnormal rigidity and unresponsiveness
medical

Examples

After the accident, he spent several days in catatonia, barely moving or responding to voices.
The patient’s catatonia was treated using a combination of medication and therapy.
Periods of catatonia may alternate with episodes of intense agitation in some psychiatric disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stupor - a state of near-unconsciousness or insensibility
  • Antonym: agitation - a state of anxiety or nervous excitement, often contrasted with catatonia

Etymology

From Greek 'katatonia', from 'kata-' (down) + 'tonos' (tone, tension).
