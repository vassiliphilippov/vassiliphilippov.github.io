tenia

1. NOUN

/ ˈtiːniə / (AmE), / ˈtiːnɪə / (BrE)
A narrow ribbon or band, especially one worn as a headband in classical times.
narrow headband or ribbon

Examples

In ancient Rome, a tenia was often used to keep one's hair in place.
The dancer wore a golden tenia around her head during the performance.
Textiles found at the site included a silk tenia decorated with intricate patterns.

Etymology

From Latin *taenia* (ribbon, band), from Ancient Greek ταινία (tainía, band, ribbon).
