cosmologic

1. ADJ

/ ˌkɑzməˈlɑdʒɪk / (AmE), / ˌkɒzməˈlɒdʒɪk / (BrE)
Relating to cosmology or the study of the origin, structure, and history of the universe.
relating to cosmology

Examples

The cosmologic model explains the large-scale structure of the universe.
She studied cosmologic theories for her graduate thesis in astrophysics.
Some scientists debate the merits of different cosmologic principles in modern physics.

Synset Examples

cosmologic science
cosmological redshift
cosmogonic theories of the origin of the universe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cosmological - relating to cosmology or the universe as a whole

Etymology

From cosmos (universe) + -logic (relating to study or theory). See cosmology.

2. ADJ

/ ˌkɑːz.məˈlɑː.dʒɪk / (AmE), / ˌkɒz.məˈlɒ.dʒɪk / (BrE)
relating to the branch of philosophy or science concerned with the nature and structure of the universe.
relating to the universe or cosmology

Examples

The book offers a cosmologic explanation for the origin of the universe.
He presented a cosmologic argument for the existence of a divine creator.
Modern physics frequently intersects with cosmologic questions about time and space.

Synset Examples

cosmologic philosophy
a cosmological argument is an argument that the universe demands the admission of an adequate external cause which is God

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cosmological - relating to the science or theory of the universe's origin and structure

Etymology

Formed from 'cosmo-' (universe) and '-logic' (relating to logic or study), modeled after 'cosmological'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02713737-a
Synset POS: a
