Euphagus carilonus

1. NOUN

/ juːˈfeɪɡəs ˌkæˈrɪloʊnəs / (AmE), / juːˈfeɪɡəs ˌkæˈrɪlənəs / (BrE)
A North American blackbird species whose bluish-black feathers develop rusty edges in the fall.
North American blackbird species

Examples

The Euphagus carilonus can often be seen flocking in wetlands during migration.
Naturalists study the changes in plumage coloration that make the Euphagus carilonus easy to identify in autumn.
Unlike other blackbirds, the Euphagus carilonus has distinct rusty edges on its feathers in fall.

Etymology

From New Latin: genus Euphagus + species epithet carilonus, used in scientific classification.
