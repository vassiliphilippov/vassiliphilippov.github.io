Ostrea

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑstriə / (AmE), / ˈɒstriə / (BrE)
A genus of marine bivalve mollusks that includes true oysters, serving as the type genus of the family Ostreidae.
genus of true oysters

Examples

The oldest known species in the genus Ostrea date back to the Cretaceous period.
Some commercial oysters belong to the genus Ostrea, prized for their delicate flavor.
Scientists identified the fossil as part of the Ostrea genus, closely related to modern oysters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oyster - A common name for marine bivalve mollusks in the family Ostreidae

Etymology

From Latin *ostrea* (oyster), itself from Greek *ostreon* (oyster).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɑstriə / (AmE), / ˈɒstriə / (BrE)
A genus of marine bivalve mollusks, commonly known as oysters, and the type genus of the family Ostreidae.

medical

Examples

The species in the genus Ostrea are found in many coastal waters around the world.
Scientists collected samples of Ostrea to study their shell structure.
Commercial oysters, such as Ostrea edulis, are highly valued in seafood markets.
