unsparing

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈspɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈspeərɪŋ / (BrE)
not gentle or merciful; harsh and ruthless, especially in criticism or action
harsh or ruthless; not gentle

Examples

The journalist wrote an unsparing account of the scandal, leaving no detail unexamined.
Her unsparing honesty often made colleagues uncomfortable but improved the team's performance.
The documentary offers an unsparing look at the hardships faced by the community.

Synset Examples

an unsparing critic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruthless - having no pity or compassion; merciless

Etymology

From un- (not) + sparing (from Old English *sperian*, to refrain, spare).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈspɛrɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈspɛːrɪŋ / (BrE)
very generous in giving, praise, or effort; not holding back or limiting what is given
very generous or unstinting

Examples

The philanthropist was unsparing in his support for local charities, funding numerous community projects.
She received unsparing praise from her teachers for her dedication and hard work.
Their unsparing generosity helped hundreds of families during the crisis.

Synset Examples

distributed gifts with a lavish hand
the critics were lavish in their praise
a munificent gift
his father gave him a half-dollar and his mother a quarter and he thought them munificent
unsparing generosity
his unstinted devotion
called for unstinting aid to Britain
lavish praise

Related Terms

  • Synonym: generous - willing to give more of something than is strictly necessary; giving freely
  • Antonym: stingy - unwilling to give or spend; ungenerous

Etymology

From un- (not) + sparing (reluctant to give or spend); thus, not sparing or not holding back.
