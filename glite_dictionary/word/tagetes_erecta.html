Tagetes erecta

1. NOUN

/ təˈdʒiːtiːz ɪˈrɛktə / (AmE), / təˈdʒiːtiːz ɪˈrɛktə / (BrE)
A tall annual plant native to Mexico and Central America, known for its large yellow to orange flower heads; also called African marigold.
tall marigold with large orange-yellow flowers

Examples

The garden was filled with Tagetes erecta, their bright blooms attracting butterflies and bees.
Many people plant Tagetes erecta to add color to their flower beds in summer.
Tagetes erecta is often used in traditional ceremonies in Mexico due to its vibrant appearance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: African marigold - common name for Tagetes erecta, a marigold species

Etymology

From Latin Tagetes (the genus, named after the Etruscan god Tages) and Latin erecta (upright, erect).
