Lucius Cornelius Sulla Felix

1. PROPN

/ ˈluːʃəs kɔːrˈniːliəs ˈsʌlə ˈfiːlɪks / (AmE), / ˈluːsɪəs kɔːˈniːlɪəs ˈsʌlə ˈfiːlɪks / (BrE)
(138–78 BC) A Roman general and statesman who became dictator and initiated significant constitutional reforms in Rome.
Roman general and dictator (138–78 BC)

Examples

Lucius Cornelius Sulla Felix played a pivotal role in the late Roman Republic.
The reforms enacted by Lucius Cornelius Sulla Felix influenced Roman governance for decades.
Many historians debate the legacy of Lucius Cornelius Sulla Felix as both a leader and a dictator.
