friary

1. NOUN

/ ˈfraɪ.ɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈfraɪ.əri / (BrE)
A monastery or residence where friars live and work.
monastery for friars

Examples

The old friary was converted into a museum after the last friars left.
A group of monks established a new friary in the rural countryside.
During our tour of the city, we visited the ruins of a medieval friary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monastery - a building where monks or nuns live and worship
  • Synonym: convent - a community or building for nuns or, historically, friars

Etymology

From Middle English 'friarie', from 'friar' plus the suffix '-y' (denoting a place).
