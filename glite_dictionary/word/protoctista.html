Protoctista

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊ.tɑkˈtɪs.tə / (AmE), / ˌprəʊ.tɒkˈtɪs.tə / (BrE)
A biological kingdom in some classification systems, including protozoa and various algae, distinguished from plants and animals.
kingdom of protozoa and algae

Examples

Many single-celled organisms are classified under the kingdom Protoctista.
Unlike plants or animals, Protoctista includes both autotrophic and heterotrophic species.
The classification of Protoctista is common in some modern biology textbooks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Protista - Former kingdom including protozoa and algae; largely replaced by Protoctista in some systems

Etymology

From Greek 'prōtos' (first) + 'ktistos' (created). Coined in the 19th century by biologist John Hogg.
