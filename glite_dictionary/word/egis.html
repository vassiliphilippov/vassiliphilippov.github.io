egis

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːdʒɪs / (AmE), / ˈiːdʒɪs / (BrE)
An armor plate that protects the chest; the front part of a cuirass.
chest armor plate; front of cuirass

Examples

The knight's egis was intricately engraved with his family's crest.
During the reenactment, the actor wore a replica egis as part of his medieval armor.
Historians believe the egis played a crucial role in deflecting blows to the chest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cuirass - a piece of armor consisting of breastplate and backplate fastened together

Etymology

From Greek 'aigis', meaning the shield of Zeus or Athena, later used for protection in general.
