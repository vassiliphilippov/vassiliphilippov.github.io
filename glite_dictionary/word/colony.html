colony

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːləni / (AmE), / ˈkɒləni / (BrE)
A visible cluster of microorganisms, such as bacteria or fungi, arising from the multiplication of a single parent cell on a nutrient medium.
group of microorganisms from one parent cell
medical

Examples

Each colony on the agar plate originated from a single bacterium.
The scientist counted the number of colonies to estimate the bacteria present.
A distinct colony with a unique color indicated a different species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: culture - growth of microorganisms in a controlled medium
  • Synonym: colony-forming unit - a unit used to estimate the number of viable bacteria or fungal cells

Etymology

From Latin colonia (settlement, farm, colony).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːləni / (AmE), / ˈkɒləni / (BrE)
A group of animals, plants, or microorganisms of the same kind living together or growing in close association.
group of organisms living together

Examples

The petri dish was covered with a colony of bacteria after two days.
An anthill is actually a colony of hundreds or thousands of ants working together.
Coral reefs are formed by a colony of tiny marine animals called polyps.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: community - a group of organisms living together, interacting in a shared environment

Etymology

From Latin colonia (settlement, farm), related to colere (to cultivate).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːləni / (AmE), / ˈkɒləni / (BrE)
A group of people from one country who settle in another country but maintain connections with their homeland; these settlers are nationals of their country of origin but typically live under a different government.
group of settlers from one country in another land

Examples

The French colony in Shanghai thrived throughout the early twentieth century.
She joined the American colony in Rome, where expatriates often gathered for social events.
The British colony in India maintained many of its own cultural traditions.

Synset Examples

the American colony in Paris

Related Terms

  • Synonym: settlement - a place where people establish a community

Etymology

From Latin colonia (settlement, farm), from colere (to cultivate, inhabit).

5. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːləni / (AmE), / ˈkɒləni / (BrE)
a country or area that is governed by a more powerful country at a distance, and is typically settled or controlled by people from that country
area controlled by a distant country

Examples

India was once a colony of the British Empire until it gained independence in 1947.
The settlers established a colony on the remote island in the eighteenth century.
Many countries around the world were former colonies of European powers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: territory - an area of land under the jurisdiction of a ruler or state
  • Antonym: metropolis - the parent state of a colony
  • Synonym: dependent territory - a region that belongs to another state but is not fully part of it

Etymology

From Latin colonia (settlement, farm), from colonus (farmer, settler).
