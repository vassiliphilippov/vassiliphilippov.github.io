immediateness

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈmiːdiətnəs / (AmE), / ɪˈmiːdiːətnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being immediate; the absence of anything intervening or mediating.
quality of being immediate

Examples

The immediateness of her response made it clear she was prepared.
Some people prefer emails for their immediateness compared to traditional letters.
The immediateness of live news can make events feel more urgent.

Synset Examples

the immediacy of television coverage

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immediacy - the quality of bringing one into direct and instant involvement

Etymology

From immediate + -ness, modeled after similar abstract nouns.

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈmidiətnəs / (AmE), / ɪˈmiːdiətnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being immediate; quickness or promptness of action or occurrence
the quality of being immediate

Examples

The immediateness of her reply surprised everyone in the room.
We admired the immediateness of the rescue team's response during the emergency.
The immediateness of modern news reporting has changed how people receive information.

Synset Examples

the immediacy of their response
the instancy of modern communication

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immediacy - the quality of bringing something into direct experience or attention; directness

Etymology

From immediate + -ness; based on Latin immediatus (without anything between).
