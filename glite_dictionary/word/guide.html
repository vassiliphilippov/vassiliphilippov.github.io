guide

1. NOUN

/ ɡaɪd / (AmE), / ɡaɪd / (BrE)
a structure or marking that directs the motion or positioning of something
structure or marking that directs movement

Examples

The guide along the conveyor belt ensures the boxes stay in place.
Painted lines serve as a guide for drivers merging onto the highway.
A metal guide channels the cable smoothly through the machinery.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marker - something that marks a position or direction
  • Antonym: barrier - a structure that blocks or restricts motion

2. NOUN

/ ɡaɪd / (AmE), / ɡaɪd / (BrE)
something that serves as a model or standard for making comparisons or judgments.
model or standard for comparison

Examples

The professor used last year’s project as a guide for students to follow.

His achievements serve as a guide to what is possible in this field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: benchmark - a standard or point of reference for comparison
  • Synonym: standard - a level of quality or attainment used as a measure or model

Etymology
