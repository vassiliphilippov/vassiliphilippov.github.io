abiogenetic

1. ADJ

/ ˌeɪˌbaɪoʊdʒəˈnɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌeɪˌbʌɪəʊdʒɪˈnɛtɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or resulting from abiogenesis; originating from non-living matter rather than from existing life.
arising from non-living matter

Examples

The theory suggests that life on Earth originated through an abiogenetic process billions of years ago.
Some scientists propose that certain organic molecules were formed in an abiogenetic manner under early Earth conditions.
Researchers study abiogenetic mechanisms to understand how simple chemicals could give rise to living systems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: abiogenetic - not derived from existing life
  • Antonym: biogenetic - relating to the production of living organisms from other living organisms

Etymology

Formed from 'abiogenesis' (from Greek 'a' meaning 'not' + 'bios' meaning 'life' + 'genesis' meaning 'origin') plus the adjectival suffix '-etic'.
