fluidounce

1. NOUN

/ ˈfluːɪdˌaʊns / (AmE), / ˈfluːɪdˌaʊns / (BrE)
a British imperial unit of capacity for liquids or dry substances, equal to 8 fluid drams or approximately 28.4 milliliters
British unit of liquid/dry capacity (about 28.4 ml)
british

Examples

The recipe calls for 4 fluidounces of milk, so measure carefully.
A British pint contains 20 fluidounces, unlike the American pint.
This bottle holds exactly eight fluidounces of cough syrup.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fluid ounce - US customary liquid unit (about 29.6 ml)

Etymology

From 'fluid' + 'ounce'; 'ounce' from Latin 'uncia' (twelfth part).
