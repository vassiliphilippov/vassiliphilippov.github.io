orthotropous

1. ADJ

/ ˌɔrˈθɑːtrəpəs / (AmE), / ˌɔːˈθɒtrəpəs / (BrE)
(of a plant ovule) completely straight with the micropyle situated at the apex.
of a straight plant ovule with apex micropyle
medical

Examples

In orchids, an orthotropous ovule is rare compared to other ovule types.
The botanist explained how an orthotropous ovule differs from an anatropous one.
Under the microscope, the orthotropous structure of the ovule was clearly visible.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anatropous - (of a plant ovule) inverted so the micropyle is near the base

Etymology

From Greek 'orthos' (straight) + 'tropos' (turn, direction).
