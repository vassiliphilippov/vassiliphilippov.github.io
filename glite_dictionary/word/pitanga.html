pitanga

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈtɑŋɡə / (AmE), / pɪˈtaŋɡə / (BrE)
A Brazilian tree (Eugenia uniflora) with spicy red fruit, often grown in subtropical regions.
Brazilian tree with spicy red fruit

Examples

The pitanga bears bright red fruits that are both sweet and tart when fully ripe.
Gardeners in Florida often plant pitanga trees for their ornamental and edible value.
I tasted a fresh pitanga for the first time while visiting Brazil.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Surinam cherry - Another common name for the same species, Eugenia uniflora, known for its red fruit.

Etymology

From Tupi (indigenous Brazilian language) 'pitanga', adopted into Brazilian Portuguese.
