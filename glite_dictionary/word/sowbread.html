sowbread

1. NOUN

/ ˈsoʊˌbrɛd / (AmE), / ˈsəʊˌbrɛd / (BrE)
a common European wild plant (Cyclamen hederifolium) with heart-shaped leaves and pink or purplish flowers, grown as an ornamental
European wild cyclamen plant

Examples

The forest floor was sprinkled with sowbread in full bloom during early spring.
Gardeners often plant sowbread for its attractive foliage and delicate flowers.
Wild boars are said to feed on the tubers of sowbread in its native habitat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cyclamen - a genus of plants with upswept flowers and rounded leaves

Etymology

From the belief that wild pigs (sows) ate its tubers; bread meaning food.
