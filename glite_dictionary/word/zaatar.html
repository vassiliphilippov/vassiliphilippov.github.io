zaatar

1. NOUN

/ zɑːˈtɑːr / (AmE), / zɑːˈtɑː / (BrE)
a Middle Eastern seasoning blend made from dried hyssop or thyme, sesame seeds, sumac, salt, and sometimes other spices
Middle Eastern spice blend

Examples

She sprinkled zaatar on her flatbread before baking it in the oven.
A small dish of olive oil and zaatar makes a delicious appetizer.
The recipe calls for a tablespoon of zaatar to season the roasted vegetables.

Etymology

From Arabic za‘tar, meaning 'hyssop' or the spice blend itself.
