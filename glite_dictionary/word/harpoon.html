harpoon

1. VERB

/ hɑrˈpun / (AmE), / hɑːˈpuːn / (BrE)
to strike, wound, or kill (an animal, especially a whale) with a harpoon.
strike or kill with a harpoon

Examples

They attempted to harpoon the massive whale from their small boat.
Early whalers would often harpoon several animals during a single voyage.
Modern regulations have limited the number of whales that can legally be harpooned.

Synset Examples

harpoon whales

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spear - to pierce or kill with a spear

Etymology

From French *harpon*, from Old French *harper* (to grip or seize), related to *harpe* (hook).

2. NOUN

/ hɑrˈpun / (AmE), / hɑːˈpuːn / (BrE)
a long spear with a barbed, pointed end that is thrown or fired at large fish or whales to catch them, usually with a rope attached
barbed spear used for hunting large fish or whales

Examples

The crew used a harpoon to catch the giant whale in the icy waters.
After throwing the harpoon, he quickly pulled on the rope to secure the fish.
Modern harpoons are sometimes launched from cannons instead of being thrown by hand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spear - a weapon with a pointed tip, used for thrusting; general term for pole weapons

Etymology

From French 'harpon', from Old French 'harpon' (fishhook), from 'harpe' (hook), of Germanic origin.
