dialectology

1. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪəlɛkˈtɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌdaɪəlɛkˈtɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the scientific study of dialects within a language, particularly their geographic or social variation
study of dialects in a language

Examples

She decided to specialize in dialectology during her graduate studies in linguistics.
Recent advances in dialectology have revealed unexpected patterns of language change in rural regions.
His book is widely regarded as a leading work in the field of dialectology.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: linguistics - the scientific study of language and its structure
  • Degree Higher: sociolinguistics - the study of how language varies and changes in social groups

Etymology

From Greek dialektos (dialect) + -logy (study of).
