Pilosella

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪloʊˈsɛlə / (AmE), / ˌpɪləʊˈsɛlə / (BrE)
A genus of perennial herbs with hairy leaves and stolons, native to Eurasia and North Africa; often considered closely related to Hieracium.
genus of hairy perennial herbs

Examples

The botanist identified several species of Pilosella growing in the alpine meadow.
Members of the genus Pilosella can be distinguished by their hairy leaves and creeping stolons.
Some taxonomists debate whether Pilosella should be a separate genus or included under Hieracium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hieracium - A related genus of perennial herbs known as hawkweeds

Etymology

From Latin *pilus* (hair), referencing the hairy nature of the plants.

2. NOUN

/ pɪloʊˈsɛlə / (AmE), / pɪləʊˈsɛlə / (BrE)
A genus of hairy perennial herbs with horizontal rhizomes and leafy or underground stolons, native to Eurasia and North Africa, often considered closely related to Hieracium.
a genus of perennial Eurasian herbs

Examples

The botanist identified the specimen as a member of the genus Pilosella.
Many species in Pilosella are characterized by their hairy leaves and yellow flowers.
Recent studies suggest that Pilosella should be classified separately from Hieracium.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hieracium - a closely related genus of flowering plants known as hawkweeds

Etymology

From Latin *pilosus* (hairy), referring to the plant's appearance.
