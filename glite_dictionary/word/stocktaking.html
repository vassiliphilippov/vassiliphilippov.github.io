stocktaking

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɑkˌteɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstɒkˌteɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of counting and recording all the goods and materials held in stock, typically for accounting or business purposes
checking and recording goods in stock

Examples

The annual stocktaking revealed discrepancies between the expected numbers and what was actually present in the warehouse.
All employees were required to assist with stocktaking at the end of the fiscal year.
After the holiday rush, the store scheduled a thorough stocktaking to assess inventory levels.

Synset Examples

an inventory may be necessary to see if anything is missing
they held an inventory every month

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inventory - a complete list of items such as goods in stock or contents of a building

Etymology

From 'stock' (goods kept for sale) + 'taking' (the act of taking or counting).

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɑkˌteɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstɒkˌteɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of reviewing or assessing a situation, progress, or resources, often in order to make decisions about the future.
review or assessment of a situation

Examples

At the end of the year, the team conducted a thorough stocktaking to evaluate their achievements and challenges.
The organization is undergoing a period of stocktaking to reassess its goals and strategies.
After the crisis, a national stocktaking revealed gaps in the country's preparedness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: review - an act of examining or assessing something
  • Synonym: assessment - an evaluation or appraisal of a situation

Etymology

From 'stock' (inventory or supply) + 'taking' (the act of taking), originally referring to counting goods in stock.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05755760-n
Synset POS: n
