bobsleigh

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑbz.leɪ / (AmE), / ˈbɒb.sleɪ / (BrE)
a long, narrow racing sled for two or more people, equipped with a steering mechanism, used especially in a timed winter sport on an ice track
long racing sled for 2 or more people

Examples

The Jamaican team famously competed in the bobsleigh event at the Winter Olympics.
Four athletes carefully climbed into the bobsleigh before the race began.
The bobsleigh hurtled down the icy track at incredible speed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bobsled - a long racing sled similar to a bobsleigh, used especially in North America

Etymology

From Swiss German 'Bob' (from English 'bob', to move up and down) + English 'sleigh'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɑb.sleɪ / (AmE), / ˈbɒb.sleɪ / (BrE)
a large, narrow sled for two or more people, used for racing down an ice track; originally, two short sleds connected in tandem.
winter racing sled for multiple people

Examples

The four-man bobsleigh team prepared for their race at the top of the icy track.
In its early days, the bobsleigh consisted of two smaller sleds joined together.
After the bobsleigh event, the athletes gathered to celebrate their victory.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bobsled - a type of sled used for racing, similar to bobsleigh

Etymology

From English 'bob' (to move up and down) and 'sleigh' (from Dutch 'slee', sled). Originally referred to two sleds coupled together.
