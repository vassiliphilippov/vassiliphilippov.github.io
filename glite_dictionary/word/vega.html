Vega

1. PROPN

/ ˈveɪɡə / (AmE), / ˈviːɡə / (BrE)
the brightest star in the constellation Lyra, one of the most luminous stars in the night sky
the brightest star in Lyra

Examples

Many ancient civilizations used Vega as a reference point for mapping the sky.
In the summer, Vega shines brightly overhead in the northern hemisphere.
Astronomers determined the distance to Vega using precise parallax measurements.

Etymology

From Arabic 'waqi' (falling [eagle]), via the phrase 'an-nasr al-waqi' (the falling eagle/vulture).
