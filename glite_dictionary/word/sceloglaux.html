Sceloglaux

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛləˈɡlaʊks / (AmE), / ˌsiːləˈɡlaʊks / (BrE)
A genus of owls in the family Strigidae, formerly found in New Zealand and containing the extinct laughing owl.
genus of New Zealand owls (laughing owl)

Examples

The extinct Sceloglaux was also known as the laughing owl of New Zealand.
Researchers studied the remains of Sceloglaux to learn about its unique calls.
Unlike most owl genera, Sceloglaux had an unusually expressive vocalization.

Etymology

From Greek skelos (leg) and glaux (owl).

2. NOUN

/ ˌskɛl.oʊˈɡlɔks / (AmE), / ˌskiː.ləʊˈɡlɔːks / (BrE)
A genus of owls in the family Strigidae, known for the species laughing owl (Sceloglaux albifacies), native to New Zealand.
genus of owls including the laughing owl

Examples

The extinct Sceloglaux, or laughing owl, was once widespread across New Zealand.
Scientists have classified the laughing owl under the genus Sceloglaux.
Fossil evidence of Sceloglaux has provided insights into New Zealand's unique bird fauna.

Etymology

From Greek 'skelos' (leg) + 'glaux' (owl), referencing physical features.
