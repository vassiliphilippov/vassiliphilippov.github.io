mitigable

1. ADJ

/ ˈmɪt̬.ɪ.ɡə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈmɪt.ɪ.ɡə.bəl / (BrE)
(of a problem, condition, etc.) capable of being alleviated, lessened, or made less severe.
capable of being lessened or alleviated

Examples

The environmental impact of the project is mitigable through proper planning.
Some side effects of the medication are mitigable with supportive care.
The risks associated with climate change are partially mitigable by reducing greenhouse gas emissions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: intractable - (of a problem or condition) hard to alleviate or manage

Etymology

From Latin mitigare (to soften) + -able (capable of).
