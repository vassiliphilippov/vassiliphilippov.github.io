consubstantial

1. ADJ

/ ˌkɑːnsəbˈstænʃəl / (AmE), / ˌkɒnsəbˈstænʃəl / (BrE)
of the same substance, essence, or nature, especially referring to the persons of the Christian Trinity as sharing the same substance.
of the same substance or essence

Examples

The doctrine maintains that the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit are consubstantial within the Holy Trinity.
The council declared Christ to be consubstantial with the Father, affirming their shared divine nature.
The term consubstantial is central to debates about the essence of the Trinity in Christian theology.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: identical - being the same in every respect
  • Antonym: different - not the same; distinct in nature

Etymology

From Late Latin consubstantialis (of the same substance), from Latin con- (with) + substantia (substance).
