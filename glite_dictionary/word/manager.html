manager

1. NOUN

/ ˈmænɪdʒər / (AmE), / ˈmænɪdʒə / (BrE)
a person responsible for controlling resources, finances, or operations in an organization or enterprise
person who controls resources or finances

Examples

The manager of the department approved the new budget proposal.
It is the manager's job to allocate resources effectively within the company.
As a project manager, she oversees both the financial and human resources.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: administrator - person responsible for running a business, organization, etc.
  • Synonym: supervisor - person overseeing work or workers
  • Antonym: employee - person who works for another, usually not controlling resources

Etymology

From Italian 'maneggiare' (to handle), from Latin 'manus' (hand).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmænədʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈmænɪdʒə / (BrE)
A person responsible for supervising and training an athlete or a sports team.
person who trains and directs a sports team or athlete

Examples

The football club appointed a new manager at the start of the season.
Her manager helped her improve her performance and win several tournaments.
The manager made a bold decision to change the team's tactics before the final game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coach - person who trains and instructs athletes or teams

Etymology

From Latin *manu agere* (to handle, to manage) via Italian *maneggiare* and French *manager*.
