numen

1. NOUN

/ ˈnuː.mən / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.mən / (BrE)
A divine power or spirit believed to be present in a place, object, or phenomenon, especially in ancient Roman religion.
divine spirit or power (esp. Roman religion)

Examples

Romans believed that each household had its own numen watching over it.
In ancient texts, the river was described as being inhabited by a protective numen.
The temple was built to honor the numen of the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genius - a guiding spirit or deity in Roman religion

Etymology

From Latin numen (nod, divine will, deity).
