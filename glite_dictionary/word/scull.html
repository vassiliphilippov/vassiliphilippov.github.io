scull

1. VERB

/ skʌl / (AmE), / skʌl / (BrE)
to propel a boat using one or two oars, especially by moving them from side to side
propel a boat with oars (sculls)

Examples

She expertly sculled the narrow boat down the river.
We took turns sculling as we crossed the calm lake.
He learned how to scull by watching experienced rowers.

Synset Examples

scull the boat

Related Terms

  • Synonym: row - to propel a boat with oars

2. NOUN

/ skʌl / (AmE), / skʌl / (BrE)
a narrow, light racing boat that is propelled using pairs of oars called sculls, usually by one or more rowers
racing boat propelled by sculls

Examples

The rower took her scull out on the river for early morning practice.
Each scull at the regatta was built for speed and agility.
He repaired his cracked scull before the next big race.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shell - a long, narrow racing boat used in competitive rowing

Etymology

From Middle English 'scolle', possibly from Old Norse 'skál' (bowl), referring to the shape of the boat.

3. NOUN

/ skʌl / (AmE), / skʌl / (BrE)
each of a pair of short oars used by a single rower to propel a boat, typically held one in each hand
short oar used in rowing

Examples

He gripped each scull firmly, ready to propel the boat across the lake.
The athlete dipped her sculls into the water with smooth, practiced strokes.
A single scull is different from a sweep oar, as it is shorter and handled with one hand.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oar - a pole with a flat blade, used to row or steer a boat
  • Degree Lower: paddle - a short pole with a broad blade at one or both ends, used to move a small boat through water

Etymology

From Middle English *sculle*, perhaps from Old Norse *skál* (bowl, basin), referring to the shape of the oar blade.

4. NOUN

/ skʌl / (AmE), / skʌl / (BrE)
A long oar mounted at the stern of a boat and moved side-to-side to propel it forward.
a long oar used to propel a boat

Examples

The fisherman used a single scull to maneuver his boat through the narrow channel.
She learned how to operate the scull efficiently to travel upstream.
A wooden scull rested against the stern, ready to be used for the journey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oar - a pole with a flat blade used to row or steer a boat

Etymology

From Middle English 'sculle' (oar or paddle), possibly of Scandinavian origin.
