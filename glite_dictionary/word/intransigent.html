intransigent

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈtrænzədʒənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtranzɪdʒ(ə)nt / (BrE)
unwilling or refusing to change one's views or to agree about something; uncompromising.
uncompromising; refusing to change views

Examples

The union remained intransigent, rejecting every offer put forward by the company.
His intransigent stance on environmental regulations frustrated many of his colleagues.
Negotiations stalled because both sides were intransigent in their demands.

Synset Examples

he is adamant in his refusal to change his mind
Cynthia was inexorable: she would have none of him
an intransigent conservative opposed to every liberal tendency

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adamant - refusing to be persuaded or change one's mind
  • Antonym: compromising - willing to settle differences by mutual concessions

Etymology

From Spanish 'intransigente', from Latin 'in-' (not) + 'transigere' (to come to an agreement).
