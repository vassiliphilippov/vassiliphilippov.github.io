to Romanize

1. VERB

/ ˈroʊməˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈrəʊmənaɪz / (BrE)
To write words, especially from another script, using the Latin (Roman) alphabet.
write using the Latin alphabet

Examples

After moving to Europe, he decided to Romanize his Chinese name for ease of communication.
Korean words are often Romanized differently depending on the system used.
The ancient text was carefully Romanized so Western scholars could study it.

Synset Examples

many shops in Japan now carry neon signs with Romanized names

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to transliterate - to represent or spell in the characters of another alphabet

Etymology

From Latin 'Romanus' (Roman), referring to the Latin/ Roman alphabet.
