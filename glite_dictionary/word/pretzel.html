pretzel

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɛtsəl / (AmE), / ˈprɛtsl̩ / (BrE)
A crisp, baked snack in the form of a knot or stick, usually glazed and sprinkled with salt.
salted baked snack shaped like a knot

Examples

She bought a hot pretzel from the street vendor on her way to work.
The bowl was filled with salted pretzels for the party guests.
He prefers his pretzel with mustard for dipping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cracker - a thin, crisp baked food often served as a snack

Etymology

From German 'Brezel', from Medieval Latin 'bracellus', a diminutive of 'bracchius' (arm), due to the twisted shape resembling arms folded in prayer.
