mundanity

1. NOUN

/ mʌnˈdænəti / (AmE), / mʌnˈdænɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being ordinary, commonplace, or lacking excitement.
ordinariness; commonplace quality

Examples

Many people seek adventure to break free from the mundanity of everyday life.
The film captures the mundanity of suburban existence with surprising sensitivity.
He was frustrated by the mundanity of his daily routine at the office.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ordinariness - the state of being ordinary
  • Antonym: remarkableness - the quality of being remarkable or unusual

Etymology

From Latin 'mundanus' (worldly, ordinary) via Old French 'mondanité'.
