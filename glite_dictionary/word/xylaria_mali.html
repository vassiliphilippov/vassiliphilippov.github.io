Xylaria mali

1. NOUN

/ zaɪˈlɛəriə ˈmælaɪ / (AmE), / zaɪˈlɛəriə ˈmaːli / (BrE)
a species of fungus that causes black root rot in apple trees
fungus causing root rot in apples

Examples

Orchards affected by Xylaria mali often show severe signs of black root rot.
Researchers are studying ways to control the spread of Xylaria mali in commercial apple farms.
Xylaria mali can devastate apple crops if not managed properly.

Etymology

From New Latin: 'Xylaria' referring to the genus of fungi, and 'mali' from Latin 'malus' (apple tree).
