genus Muntiacus

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒiːnəs mʌnˈtaɪəkəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs mʌnˈtɪəkəs / (BrE)
A genus of small deer native to South and Southeast Asia, commonly known as muntjacs.
a genus of small Asian deer (muntjacs)

Examples

The genus Muntiacus includes several species of small deer found throughout Asia.
Biologists have long debated the evolutionary relationships within the genus Muntiacus.
New evidence suggests that another species may belong to the genus Muntiacus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muntjac - a small deer native to Asia, from the genus Muntiacus

Etymology

From Latin 'genus' (kind, type) and 'Muntiacus', from Dutch 'muntjak' (muntjac).
