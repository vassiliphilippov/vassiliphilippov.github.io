methapyrilene

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛθəˈpɪrɪlin / (AmE), / ˌmiːθəˈpɪrɪliːn / (BrE)
An antihistamine drug formerly used to treat allergic conditions such as rhinitis, dermatitis, or itching (pruritus).
antihistamine drug (formerly used)
medical

Examples

Methapyrilene was once widely prescribed for the treatment of hay fever and other allergies.
Research into methapyrilene led to concerns about its long-term safety in humans.
Topical creams containing methapyrilene were used to relieve itching from insect bites.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antihistamine - a drug that counteracts the effects of histamine

Etymology

From 'metha-' (referring to methyl group) and 'pyrilene' (from pyridine chemical structure).
