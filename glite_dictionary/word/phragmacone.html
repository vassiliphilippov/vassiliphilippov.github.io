phragmacone

1. NOUN

/ ˈfræɡ.məˌkoʊn / (AmE), / ˈfræɡ.mə.kəʊn / (BrE)
the thin, conical, chambered internal shell (either straight or curved) of a belemnite, an extinct marine cephalopod
chambered internal shell of a belemnite

Examples

The fossilized phragmacone provided valuable information about the ancient belemnite's anatomy.
After careful excavation, the scientist uncovered a perfectly preserved phragmacone in the limestone.
The structure of the phragmacone allowed belemnites to control their buoyancy in the water.

Etymology

From Greek *phragma* (fence, partition) + *kōnos* (cone).
