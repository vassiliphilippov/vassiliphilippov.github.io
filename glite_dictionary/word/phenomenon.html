phenomenon

1. NOUN

/ fəˈnɑːməˌnɑːn / (AmE), / fəˈnɒmɪnən / (BrE)
an event, occurrence, or fact that can be observed or experienced, especially one that is perceptible by the senses
observable event or fact

Examples

The Northern Lights are a natural phenomenon that many travel far to witness.
Economic growth is a complex phenomenon influenced by multiple factors.
This strange phenomenon was later explained by advances in physics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: event - something that happens or takes place
  • Synonym: process - a series of actions or steps taken to achieve a result

Etymology

From Greek phainomenon (appearance), from phainesthai (to appear), from phainein (to show).

2. NOUN

/ fəˈnɑːməˌnɑn / (AmE), / fəˈnɒmɪnən / (BrE)
a remarkable or exceptional person, thing, or event; something impressive or outstanding that attracts attention
remarkable or exceptional occurrence

Examples

The scientist became a phenomenon for her groundbreaking discoveries in genetics.
Her rise to fame was a social media phenomenon that captured worldwide attention.
The rapid growth of the company became a business phenomenon studied by many economists.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marvel - something that causes wonder or astonishment
  • Synonym: miracle - an extraordinary and astonishing happening
  • Antonym: ordinary - not remarkable, lacking special features

Etymology

From Greek 'phainomenon' (appearance, thing appearing to view), from 'phainesthai' (to appear).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
