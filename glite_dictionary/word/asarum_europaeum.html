Asarum europaeum

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɛərəm ˌjʊroʊˈpiəm / (AmE), / əˈsɑːrəm jʊərəˈpiːəm / (BrE)
a thick, creeping evergreen herb native to western Europe, also known as European wild ginger
evergreen herb of western Europe

Examples

Asarum europaeum forms a dense ground cover in many European woodland gardens.
Botanists often use Asarum europaeum as a model species in evolutionary studies.
The leaves of Asarum europaeum stay green throughout the winter.

Etymology

From Latin: Asarum (genus name) + europaeum (of Europe).
