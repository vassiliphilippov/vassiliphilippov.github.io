cinnamene

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɪnəˌmiːn / (AmE), / ˈsɪnəmiːn / (BrE)
a colorless, oily liquid hydrocarbon used as a monomer for producing polystyrene plastics.
colorless oily liquid; polystyrene monomer

Examples

Cinnamene is commonly used in the production of polystyrene packaging materials.
Laboratory analysis revealed trace amounts of cinnamene in the sample.
Because of its volatility, cinnamene must be stored in tightly sealed containers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: styrene - a colorless oily hydrocarbon used to make polystyrene
  • Degree Higher: polystyrene - a synthetic resin made from the polymerization of styrene

Etymology

From cinnamon, due to its initial separation from storax, a plant resin related to cinnamon.
