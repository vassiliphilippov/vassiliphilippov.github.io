nainsook

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪnˌsʊk / (AmE), / ˈneɪnsʊk / (BrE)
a soft, lightweight cotton fabric, originally plain-woven, used especially for babies' clothing and underwear
soft lightweight cotton fabric

Examples

The baby was wrapped in a soft nainsook blanket for comfort.
This dress is made from white nainsook, perfect for warm weather.
Many vintage baby garments were crafted from fine nainsook.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: muslin - a plain-weave cotton fabric

Etymology

From Hindi *nainsukh*, literally 'eye's delight', from *nain* (eye) + *sukh* (pleasure).
