documentary

1. ADJ

/ ˌdɑː.kjəˈmen.tə.ri / (AmE), / ˌdɒk.jʊˈmen.tər.i / (BrE)
consisting of, relating to, or derived from documents, especially written or factual records
relating to documents or factual records

Examples

The lawyer submitted documentary evidence to support his client's claim.
Historical research often relies heavily on documentary sources.
The museum's collection includes many documentary records from the colonial era.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archival - relating to archives or records

Etymology

From Latin documentum (lesson, example, proof), from docere (to teach).

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɑːkjəˈmentəri / (AmE), / ˌdɒkjʊˈmentəri / (BrE)
a film or television program that presents factual information about a person, event, or subject
nonfiction film or TV program

Examples

The documentary about climate change was both informative and engaging.
She spent three years filming a documentary on wildlife conservation in Africa.
After watching the documentary, he felt inspired to learn more about the subject.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: feature film - a full-length film with a fictional narrative
  • Degree Higher: docudrama - a dramatized television program based on real events

Etymology

From Latin 'documentum' (lesson, example, proof), meaning something that teaches; later applied to nonfiction films in the early 20th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06629313-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˌdɑːk.jəˈmen.t̬ɛr.i / (AmE), / ˌdɒk.jʊˈmen.təri / (BrE)
(of a film, television program, radio broadcast, or photograph) presenting factual information about real-life events, typically using actual images or interviews with people involved.
factual about real-life events (film, TV, etc.)

Examples

The series is known for its documentary style, featuring real interviews with witnesses.
She won an award for her documentary photography capturing life in rural villages.
The documentary approach of the film brought attention to social issues.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: fictional - not based on real events; invented or imaginary
  • Synonym: factual - concerned with what is actually the case rather than interpretations

Etymology

From document + -ary, modeled after Latin documentum (lesson, example) and the suffix -ary indicating connected with.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01940473-s
Synset POS: s
