sloppy

1. ADJ

/ ˈslɑːpi / (AmE), / ˈslɒpi / (BrE)
untidy, messy, or lacking in neatness or order
messy or untidy

Examples

Her desk is always so sloppy that it's hard to find anything.
After the party, the entire living room looked sloppy and needed cleaning.
His sloppy handwriting made the essay difficult to read.

Synset Examples

a sloppy room
sloppy habits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: messy - untidy or dirty
  • Antonym: orderly - arranged in a neat, tidy way

2. ADJ

/ ˈslɑpi / (AmE), / ˈslɒpi / (BrE)
excessively or abnormally sentimental or emotional, especially in a way that seems insincere or lacking subtlety
overly sentimental or emotional

Examples

The movie was so sloppy that I found myself rolling my eyes at every emotional scene.
Her sloppy letter made it hard to take her feelings seriously.
Some critics dismissed the novel as sloppy and overly dramatic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sentimental - showing or causing excessive emotions, especially tenderness or sadness
  • Antonym: restrained - characterized by moderation and self-control, the opposite of excessive emotion

3. ADJ

/ ˈslɑpi / (AmE), / ˈslɒpi / (BrE)
(of soil or ground) soft, wet, and watery, often making it difficult to walk or support weight.
soil: soft, wet, and watery

Examples

After last night's rain, the garden was too sloppy to plant anything.
The football field became dangerously sloppy during the storm.
We struggled to walk through the sloppy field after the flood.

4. ADJ

/ ˈslɑpi / (AmE), / ˈslɒpi / (BrE)
Showing a lack of care, attention, or effort; done in a careless or untidy way.
careless; lacking attention or tidiness

Examples

His sloppy handwriting made the note difficult to read.
The report was full of sloppy mistakes that could have been avoided.
She received a warning from her manager for her sloppy attitude toward deadlines.

Synset Examples

a most haphazard system of record keeping

slipshod spelling
sloppy workmanship

Related Terms

  • Synonym: careless - not giving enough attention to avoiding harm or errors
  • Antonym: meticulous - showing great attention to detail; very careful and precise

Etymology

Originated in the early 19th century from 'slop' (muddy or wet), with the '-y' suffix forming adjectives.
