Pennisetum setaceum

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛnɪˈsiːtəm səˈteɪsiəm / (AmE), / ˌpɛnɪˈsiːtəm sɪˈtɑːsiəm / (BrE)
A tall perennial ornamental grass native to tropical Africa and Asia, known for its long, nodding flower plumes; commonly called fountain grass.
ornamental grass with long flower plumes

Examples

Gardeners often plant Pennisetum setaceum for its striking, feathery plumes and graceful arching leaves.
In some regions, Pennisetum setaceum is considered invasive due to its rapid spread.
The soft, pinkish spikes of Pennisetum setaceum sway beautifully in the summer breeze.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fountain grass - common name for Pennisetum setaceum

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Pennisetum' meaning 'feather bristle', and species 'setaceum' meaning 'bristly'.
