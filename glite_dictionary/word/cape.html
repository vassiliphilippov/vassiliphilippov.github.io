cape

1. NOUN

/ keɪp / (AmE), / keɪp / (BrE)
A sleeveless outer garment that fastens at the neck and hangs loosely from the shoulders, shorter than a cloak.
a short, sleeveless outer garment

Examples

She wore a red cape over her dress to the party.
The superhero's cape fluttered dramatically in the wind.
For the costume, all you need is a simple cape and a mask.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cloak - a long, loose outer garment with sleeves, usually worn over the shoulders

Etymology

From Latin 'cappa' meaning 'cloak'.

2. NOUN

/ keɪp / (AmE), / keɪp / (BrE)
a narrow piece of land that extends into a sea, ocean, or other large body of water
narrow land projecting into water

Examples

The ship sailed around the cape before heading north.
Cape Cod is a famous cape on the east coast of the United States.
Explorers often named new capes they discovered during their voyages.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: peninsula - a piece of land almost surrounded by water or projecting out into a body of water
  • Synonym: headland - a narrow piece of land that projects from a coastline into the sea

Etymology

From Latin 'caput' (head), via French 'cap', referring to 'headland'.
