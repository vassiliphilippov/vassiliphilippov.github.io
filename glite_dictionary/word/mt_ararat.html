Mt. Ararat

1. PROPN

/ ˌmaʊnt ˌærəˈræt / (AmE), / ˌmaʊnt ˈærəræt / (BrE)
a mountain in eastern Turkey, traditionally regarded as the landing place of Noah's Ark according to the Bible
mountain in eastern Turkey; Biblical Ark site

Examples

Noah's Ark is said to have come to rest on Mt. Ararat after the flood.
Many climbers travel to eastern Turkey to scale Mt. Ararat.
In biblical tradition, Mt. Ararat is associated with the story of survival and new beginnings.

Etymology

From the Hebrew 'Ararat' in the Bible, possibly referring to a region in ancient Armenia.
