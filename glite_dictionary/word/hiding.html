hiding

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
the state of being hidden, especially to avoid being found
state of being hidden

Examples

After the escape, he spent several months in hiding to avoid capture.
The witness was forced into hiding after receiving threats.
She emerged from hiding once she felt it was safe.

Synset Examples

he went into hiding

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concealment - the action of hiding something or preventing it from being found
  • Antonym: exposure - the state of being exposed or uncovered

Etymology

From the verb 'hide' + -ing, indicating the act or condition.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity of keeping something secret or concealed
keeping something secret

Examples

After the theft, the hiding of the stolen goods became a priority.
Their relationship was kept alive through careful hiding from nosy neighbors.
The hiding of the truth only made matters worse in the long run.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concealment - the act of hiding or keeping something out of sight

Etymology

From Old English hȳdan (to hide) with the suffix -ing.
