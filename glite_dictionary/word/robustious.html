robustious

1. ADJ

/ roʊˈbʌs.tʃəs / (AmE), / rəʊˈbʌs.tɪ.əs / (BrE)
noisy, energetic, and lacking in restraint or discipline; boisterous or unruly.
noisy and unruly; boisterous

Examples

The robustious laughter from the next room made it hard to concentrate on my work.
A robustious group of fans cheered loudly throughout the match.
His robustious behavior at the party drew both smiles and disapproving looks from the guests.

Synset Examples

a boisterous crowd
a social gathering that became rambunctious and out of hand
a robustious group of teenagers
beneath the rumbustious surface of his paintings is sympathy for the vulnerability of ordinary human beings
an unruly class

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boisterous - noisy, energetic, and cheerful; rowdy
  • Synonym: rambunctious - uncontrollably exuberant; boisterous
  • Synonym: unruly - disorderly or disruptive and not amenable to discipline or control

Etymology

Formed from 'robust', with the suffix '-ious'; first recorded in English in the 16th century, sometimes found in Shakespeare.
