strong-arm

1. ADJ

/ ˈstrɔŋ ɑrm / (AmE), / ˈstrɒŋ ɑːm / (BrE)
using or involving physical force, especially to overcome resistance or force compliance
involving physical force, especially to compel

Examples

The manager used strong-arm tactics to pressure employees into agreeing to the new rules.
Police were criticized for their strong-arm approach during the protests.
The company tried to strong-arm reluctant partners into signing the deal.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forcible - done by force
  • Synonym: coercive - using force or threats

2. VERB

/ ˈstrɔŋ ɑrm / (AmE), / ˈstrɒŋ ɑːm / (BrE)
to use physical force or threats to make someone do something
coerce with physical force or threats

Examples

The gang tried to strong-arm the shopkeeper into paying for protection.
He strong-armed his way past the security guards to get backstage.
The company was accused of strong-arming officials to win the contract.

Synset Examples

They strong-armed me when I left the restaurant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to coerce - to force someone to do something by use of threats or pressure
  • Antonym: to persuade - to convince someone by argument rather than force

3. VERB

/ ˈstrɔŋ ˌɑrm / (AmE), / ˈstrɒŋ ˌɑːm / (BrE)
to use force or threats to make someone do something; to coerce by intimidation or physical means
use force or intimidation to coerce

Examples

The union leader tried to strong-arm the company into accepting their demands.
She refused to let anyone strong-arm her into making a quick decision.
The politician was accused of strong-arming votes from his colleagues.

Synset Examples

The manager tried to strong-arm the employees into working overtime.
He was forced to strong-arm his competitor to secure the deal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to coerce - to force someone to do something by using threats or force
  • Synonym: to intimidate - to frighten or threaten someone, usually in order to persuade them to do something
