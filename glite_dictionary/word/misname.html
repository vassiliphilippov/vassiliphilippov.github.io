to misname

1. VERB

/ mɪsˈneɪm / (AmE), / mɪsˈneɪm / (BrE)
to call someone or something by a wrong or inappropriate name
call by a wrong name

Examples

The tour guide accidentally misnamed the ancient statue, causing confusion among the visitors.
If you misname a file on your computer, it can be hard to find later.
He claimed that historians often misname events, leading to misunderstandings about the past.

Synset Examples

These misnamed philanthropists

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to rename - to give a new name to something or someone

Etymology

From the prefix 'mis-' (wrongly) and 'name', after Middle English 'misnemen'.
