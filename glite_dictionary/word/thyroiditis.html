thyroiditis

1. NOUN

/ ˌθaɪrɔɪˈdaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌθaɪərɔɪˈdaɪtɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of the thyroid gland, often caused by infection, autoimmune disease, or other factors
inflammation of the thyroid gland
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with thyroiditis after experiencing neck pain and fatigue.
Hashimoto's thyroiditis is an autoimmune condition affecting the thyroid gland.
Treatment for thyroiditis depends on the underlying cause and the severity of symptoms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: thyroid gland - a gland in the neck that produces hormones regulating metabolism

Etymology

From thyroid (thyroid gland) + -itis (Greek, denoting inflammation).
