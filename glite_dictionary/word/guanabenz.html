guanabenz

1. NOUN

/ ɡwɑnəˌbɛnz / (AmE), / ɡwænəˌbɛnz / (BrE)
An antihypertensive drug that lowers blood pressure by acting on the central nervous system; formerly marketed under the trade name Wytensin.
antihypertensive medication
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed guanabenz to help control her high blood pressure.
Long-term use of guanabenz can sometimes cause drowsiness as a side effect.
After switching to guanabenz, his blood pressure became more stable.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antihypertensive - drug used to treat high blood pressure
