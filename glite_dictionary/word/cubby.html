cubby

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌbi / (AmE), / ˈkʌbi / (BrE)
a small, secluded room or compartment, often used for storage or as a private space
small secluded room or compartment

Examples

She kept her secret diary hidden in a cubby in her bedroom.
Each child puts their backpack in their own cubby when they arrive at school.
The dog found a comfortable cubby under the stairs to sleep in.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alcove - a small recessed section of a room
  • Synonym: nook - a small corner or recess, especially one offering seclusion or security

Etymology

Diminutive form of 'cub', orig. possibly from Old English 'cubbe', meaning 'stall' or 'pen'.
