Thylacinus

1. NOUN

/ θaɪˈlæsɪnəs / (AmE), / θɪˈlæsɪnəs / (BrE)
A genus of carnivorous marsupials, the most famous species of which is the extinct Tasmanian tiger or Tasmanian wolf (Thylacinus cynocephalus).
genus of marsupials including the Tasmanian tiger

Examples

The last known Thylacinus died in captivity in Hobart Zoo in 1936.
Scientists have sequenced the DNA of Thylacinus from preserved museum specimens.
Popular interest in the Thylacinus has grown due to its unique place in Australian fauna history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tasmanian tiger - the common name for Thylacinus cynocephalus, an extinct marsupial

Etymology

From Greek thylakos (pouch) + Latin -inus (belonging to).
