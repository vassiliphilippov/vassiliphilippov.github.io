boorishness

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʊrɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈbʊərɪʃnəs / (BrE)
rude or insensitive behavior; the quality of being coarse or uncouth
rude or insensitive behavior

Examples

His boorishness at the dinner table embarrassed everyone present.
The manager’s boorishness made working at the office unpleasant for many employees.
She was surprised by the boorishness of the guests, who ignored basic manners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boorish - rude and insensitive; resembling a boor
  • Antonym: courtesy - polite behavior; showing respect for others

Etymology

From 'boorish' + '-ness', ultimately from Dutch 'boer' (farmer).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʊrɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈbʊərɪʃnəs / (BrE)
rude or insensitive behavior; the quality of being coarse, uncouth, or lacking in refinement
rudeness; coarse behavior

Examples

His boorishness at the dinner table embarrassed everyone present.
You could sense a certain boorishness in the way he dismissed her opinion.
Despite his intelligence, his boorishness made him unwelcome at social events.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rudeness - the quality of being impolite or disrespectful
  • Antonym: politeness - behavior that is respectful and considerate of others

Etymology

From 'boorish' + '-ness', with 'boorish' from Dutch 'boer' (farmer).
