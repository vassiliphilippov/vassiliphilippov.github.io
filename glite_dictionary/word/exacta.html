exacta

1. NOUN

/ ɪɡˈzæktə / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzæktə / (BrE)
A bet in which the bettor must pick the first and second finishers in the correct order, especially in horse racing.
bet on first two finishers in order (horse racing)

Examples

She placed an exacta bet on horses three and seven, hoping they would finish first and second in that order.
Winning the exacta pays much more than just picking the winner alone.
Many bettors consider the exacta to be one of the most challenging types of wagers.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: quinella - a bet picking the first two finishers in any order
  • Degree Higher: trifecta - a bet picking the first three finishers in the correct order

Etymology

From American Spanish, derived from 'exacta combinación' (exact combination).
