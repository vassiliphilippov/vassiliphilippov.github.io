vouchee

1. NOUN

/ vaʊˈtʃi / (AmE), / vaʊˈtʃi / (BrE)
(law) a person who is called into court to defend a title to property in certain legal proceedings.
person summoned to court to defend a property title
legal

Examples

The vouchee must appear in court to justify the transfer of ownership.
In a common recovery case, the vouchee played a crucial procedural role.
Failure of the vouchee to respond could invalidate the claim to the land.

Etymology

From Anglo-French word 'voucher', to summon, influenced by Old French 'vocher' (to call, claim).
