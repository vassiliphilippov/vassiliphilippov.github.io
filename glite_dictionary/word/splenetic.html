splenetic

1. ADJ

/ spləˈnɛtɪk / (AmE), / splɪˈnɛtɪk / (BrE)
easily angered or excessively irritable; bad-tempered.
very irritable; bad-tempered

Examples

His splenetic remarks alienated many of his colleagues at work.
The critic was known for her splenetic reviews of mediocre plays.
After a long, stressful day, he became increasingly splenetic at the slightest provocation.

Synset Examples

bristly exchanges between the White House and the press
he became prickly and spiteful
witty and waspish about his colleagues

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irritable - easily annoyed or angered
  • Antonym: even-tempered - calm and not easily angered

Etymology

From Late Latin spleneticus, from Greek splēn (spleen), once believed to be the seat of bad temper.

2. ADJ

/ spləˈnɛtɪk / (AmE), / splɪˈnɛtɪk / (BrE)
of or relating to the spleen; or (by extension) characterized by irritability or spitefulness.
of the spleen; irritable
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that splenetic tissue showed signs of inflammation.
She was diagnosed with a splenetic disorder after persistent abdominal pain.
A splenetic crisis can occur if the spleen is suddenly injured.

Etymology

From Late Latin spleneticus, from Greek splēn (spleen).

Synset POS: a
