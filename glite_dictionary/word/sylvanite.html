sylvanite

1. NOUN

/ sɪlˈvænaɪt / (AmE), / sɪlˈvænaɪt / (BrE)
a silver-white mineral consisting of a gold and silver telluride, used as a source of gold
a gold and silver mineral ore

Examples

The geologist discovered several veins rich in sylvanite during the survey.
A valuable source of gold, sylvanite is found in certain mining regions of Australia and America.
The sample contained both quartz and sylvanite, making it an interesting find for mineral collectors.

Etymology

From Latin *silvanus* (of the forest), referring to the region where it was found (Transylvania).
