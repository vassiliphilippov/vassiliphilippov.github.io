Euphorbia cyparissias

1. NOUN

/ juːˈfɔr.bi.ə saɪ.pəˈrɪs.i.əs / (AmE), / juːˈfɔː.bɪ.ə sɪ.pəˈrɪs.i.əs / (BrE)
A species of perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia, known for its cypress-like foliage; often found as a weed in North America.
a species of spurge with cypress-like leaves

Examples

The garden was overrun with Euphorbia cyparissias, which spread quickly among the other plants.
Wild patches of Euphorbia cyparissias can be found along roadsides and in meadows.
She identified the distinctive cypress-shaped leaves as belonging to Euphorbia cyparissias.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spurge - any plant of the genus Euphorbia

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Euphorbia' (after Greek physician Euphorbus) and species 'cyparissias' referring to resemblance to cypress.
