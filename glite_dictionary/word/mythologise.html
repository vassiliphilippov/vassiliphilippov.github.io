to mythologise

1. VERB

/ mɪˈθɑlədʒaɪz / (AmE), / mɪˈθɒlədʒaɪz / (BrE)
To create, invent, or promote a myth about someone or something, often by exaggerating or idealising certain facts.
create or popularise a myth
british

Examples

The film tends to mythologise the life of its main character.
Some authors mythologise history to make their stories more appealing.
It is easy to mythologise the past when you ignore its problems.

Synset Examples

The poet mythologized that the King had three sons

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mythologize - create or popularise a myth (American spelling)
  • Antonym: to debunk - to expose the falseness of a myth or belief

Etymology

From Greek mythologia (story-telling), via French mythologiser.

2. VERB

/ mɪˈθɑːlədʒaɪz / (AmE), / mɪˈθɒlədʒaɪz / (BrE)
to make something or someone seem mythical or legendary, often by exaggerating or idealizing their qualities
turn into a myth or legend
british

Examples

Writers often mythologise historical figures, transforming them into larger-than-life characters.
Over the years, fans have begun to mythologise the band's early days, romanticising every detail.
Some cultures mythologise natural landmarks, weaving legends around their creation.

Synset Examples

The Europeans have mythicized Rte. 66

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mythologize - American spelling for 'mythologise'

Etymology

From 'mythology' + '-ise', following British English spelling; related to Greek 'mythos' (story).
