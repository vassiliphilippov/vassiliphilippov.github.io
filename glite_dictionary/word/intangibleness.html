intangibleness

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈtændʒəbəlnəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈtændʒɪb(ə)lnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being intangible; the state of not being able to be touched or grasped physically.
quality of being intangible

Examples

The intangibleness of her charm made her all the more fascinating.
Some values have an intangibleness that makes them hard to measure or define.
He spoke of the intangibleness of inspiration, comparing it to a fleeting shadow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: intangibility - the state of being intangible; inability to be touched
  • Antonym: tangibility - the quality of being able to be touched

Etymology

From 'intangible' + '-ness', with 'intangible' from Medieval Latin 'intangibilis' (not able to be touched).
