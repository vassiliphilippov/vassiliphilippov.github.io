to exorcise

1. VERB

/ ˈɛksɔrˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɛksəsaɪz / (BrE)
to drive out an evil spirit or supposed demon from a person or place using prayers or religious rituals
to expel an evil spirit by prayer

Examples

The priest attempted to exorcise the haunted house after repeated reports of strange activity.
They believe a shaman can exorcise evil spirits from the afflicted.
A team was called in to exorcise the demon believed to possess the old building.

Synset Examples

exorcise evil spirits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expel - to force out; to drive out something unwanted

Etymology

From Late Latin exorcizare, from Greek exorkizein (to adjure).
