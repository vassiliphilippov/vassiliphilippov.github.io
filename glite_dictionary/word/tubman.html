Tubman

1. PROPN

/ ˈtʌbmən / (AmE), / ˈtʌbmən / (BrE)
Harriet Tubman (1820–1913), an American abolitionist renowned for escaping slavery and leading other enslaved people to freedom via the Underground Railroad.
American abolitionist and Underground Railroad conductor

Examples

Tubman risked her life repeatedly to guide enslaved people to freedom.
Many schools in the United States are named after Tubman to honor her legacy.
The bravery of Tubman has inspired generations to fight for justice.
