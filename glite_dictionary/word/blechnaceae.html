Blechnaceae

1. NOUN

/ blɛkˈneɪsiˌi / (AmE), / blɛkˈneɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of ferns in certain classification systems, including genera such as Blechnum, Doodia, Sadleria, Stenochlaena, and Woodwardia
a family of ferns (plant taxonomy)

Examples

The genus Blechnum is the largest within the Blechnaceae family.
Recent studies have reclassified several species under Blechnaceae.
Fern enthusiasts enjoy collecting plants from the Blechnaceae family due to their diverse forms.

Etymology

From the genus name Blechnum, which comes from Greek *blechnon* (a kind of fern), and the standard botanical suffix -aceae for plant families.

2. NOUN

/ blɛkˈneɪsiˌi / (AmE), / blɛkˈneɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of ferns in some classification systems, including genera such as Blechnum, Doodia, Sadleria, Stenochlaena, and Woodwardia
family of ferns (botany)

Examples

Many species in the Blechnaceae family are cultivated for their attractive foliage.
The classification of Blechnaceae has changed as botanists revised fern families.
Researchers studied the distribution of Blechnaceae across tropical regions.

Etymology

From the type genus Blechnum, ultimately from Greek *blechnon* (a kind of fern) + botanical suffix -aceae.
