to break-dance

1. VERB

/ ˈbreɪkˌdæns / (AmE), / ˈbreɪkˌdɑːns / (BrE)
to perform energetic and acrobatic street dance moves, typically to hip-hop or funk music
perform street dance moves

Examples

He learned to break-dance by watching videos online.
Dozens of teenagers started to break-dance in the park after school.
She can break-dance better than anyone else in her class.

Synset Examples

Kids were break-dancing at the street corner

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dance - to move rhythmically to music, typically following a set sequence of steps

Etymology

From 'breakdance,' referring to dancing to the 'breaks' in a record; 1970s American hip-hop slang.
