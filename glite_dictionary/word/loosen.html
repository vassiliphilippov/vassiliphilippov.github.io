to loosen

1. VERB

/ ˈluːsən / (AmE), / ˈluː.sən / (BrE)
to make something less tight or firmly fixed; to cause to become loose
make less tight or secure

Examples

Could you please loosen the jar lid for me?
After walking for hours, he stopped to loosen his shoelaces.
She tried to loosen the knot, but it was very tight.

Synset Examples

undo the shoelace
untie the knot
loosen the necktie

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to tighten - to make something tight or tighter
  • Synonym: to untie - to open something by untying a knot

Etymology

From loose + -en (Old English origins).

WordNet ID: oewn-01287511-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈluːsən / (AmE), / ˈluːsən / (BrE)
(of rules, control, or restrictions) to become less severe or strict; to become more relaxed.
become less strict or severe

Examples

After the exam period, the university decided to loosen its library rules.
Some governments may loosen restrictions as the crisis improves.
If we loosen the dress code a little, everyone might feel more comfortable.

Synset Examples

The rules relaxed after the new director arrived

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to tighten - to make rules or controls more strict

Etymology

From Middle English lousen, from Old English lǣsan (to free, release), related to loose.
