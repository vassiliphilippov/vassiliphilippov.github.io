Cuculiformes

1. NOUN

/ kuːˌkuːlɪˈfɔːrmiːz / (AmE), / kjuːˌkjuːlɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
an order of birds that includes cuckoos, turacos, and their relatives
order of birds including cuckoos and turacos

Examples

The Cuculiformes are distributed worldwide, except in the polar regions.
Scientists identified several new species within the Cuculiformes based on genetic studies.
Unlike many other birds, some Cuculiformes exhibit brood parasitism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cuckoo - a bird of the family Cuculidae, known for laying eggs in other birds' nests

Etymology

From Latin 'cuculus' (cuckoo) + '-formes' (having the form of).

2. NOUN

/ kuːˌkuːlɪˈfɔːrmiːz / (AmE), / kjuːˌkjuːlɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
an order of birds including cuckoos, turacos, and their relatives
order of birds: cuckoos, turacos, etc.

Examples

The Cuculiformes include several species known for brood parasitism.
Cuckoos, part of the Cuculiformes order, are famous for their unique reproductive behavior.
Scientists study Cuculiformes to understand the evolution of nesting habits among birds.

Etymology

From Latin 'Cuculus' (cuckoo) + '-formes' (having the form of), used in zoological taxonomy.
