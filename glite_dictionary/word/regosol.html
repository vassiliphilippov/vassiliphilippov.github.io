regosol

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛɡoʊˌsɒl / (AmE), / ˈriːɡəʊˌsɒl / (BrE)
a type of soil consisting of loose, unconsolidated material formed from recently deposited alluvium or sand, with little or no soil development
soil formed from recent alluvium or sand
medical

Examples

The region's dominant soil is regosol, which does not retain water well.
Farmers found it challenging to grow crops in the sandy regosol.
River floodplains often have expanses of regosol due to recent sediment deposits.

Etymology

From Latin 'regosus' (full of gravel) + 'solum' (soil).
