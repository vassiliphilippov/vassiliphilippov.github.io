quotable

1. ADJ

/ ˈkwoʊtəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈkwəʊtəbl̩ / (BrE)
(of speech or writing) worth repeating or suitable to be quoted.
suitable to be quoted

Examples

The book was full of quotable lines that stuck with me long after reading.
She always has a quotable response for any situation.
His speeches are famous for being highly quotable in newspapers and magazines.

Synset Examples

what he said was not repeatable in polite company
he comes up with so many quotable phrases

Related Terms

  • Synonym: memorable - worth remembering or easily remembered
  • Antonym: unquotable - not suitable to be quoted

Etymology

From 'quote' + '-able', meaning able to be quoted.

2. ADJ

/ ˈkwoʊtəbəl / (AmE), / ˈkwəʊtəbəl / (BrE)
(of a statement, remark, or phrase) suitable for or worthy of being quoted.
suitable for being quoted

Examples

The speech was filled with quotable lines that were repeated by the media.
She always had a quotable response ready for any situation.
That book is full of quotable quotes worth sharing.

Synset Examples

a quotable slogan
his remarks are not quotable in mixed company

Related Terms

  • Synonym: memorable - (of words, events, or experiences) worth remembering or easily remembered
  • Antonym: unquotable - not suitable or worthy of being quoted

Etymology

From 'quote' + '-able', meaning able to be quoted.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02597398-s
Synset POS: s
