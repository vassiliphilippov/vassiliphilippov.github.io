zoomorphism

1. NOUN

/ zuˈɔːmɔrˌfɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌzəʊ.əˈmɔː.fɪ.zəm / (BrE)
the attribution of animal forms or characteristics to a god, deity, or other being, especially in art or literature.
attribution of animal traits to a deity or being

Examples

Ancient Egyptian religion is well known for its use of zoomorphism in representing gods as animals.
The artist's sculptures are striking examples of zoomorphism, blending human and animal features.
Literature often employs zoomorphism to illustrate deities' powers through animal traits.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: anthropomorphism - attribution of human characteristics to non-human entities

Etymology

From Greek zōion (animal) + morphē (form).
