phylloclade

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪl.əˌkleɪd / (AmE), / ˈfɪl.əˌkleɪd / (BrE)
a flattened stem that resembles and functions as a leaf, found especially in certain plants
flattened stem acting as a leaf
medical

Examples

The cactus has a phylloclade that performs photosynthesis instead of its small leaves.
Some species of Asparagus develop a phylloclade to adapt to arid conditions.
A botanist explained how the phylloclade differs structurally from an ordinary leaf.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cladode - a stem segment resembling a leaf

Etymology

From Greek phyllon (leaf) + klados (branch).
