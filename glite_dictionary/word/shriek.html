shriek

1. NOUN

/ ʃrik / (AmE), / ʃriːk / (BrE)
a loud, high-pitched sound, especially one resembling a human scream
high-pitched, piercing sound

Examples

A shriek echoed through the empty hallway, startling everyone nearby.
The brakes let out a piercing shriek as the bus stopped suddenly.
There was a shriek of alarm when the lights suddenly went out.

Synset Examples

he ducked at the screechings of shells
he heard the scream of the brakes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scream - a loud, high-pitched cry
  • Synonym: screech - a loud, harsh, piercing cry

Etymology

Probably of imitative origin, related to similar Middle English and Old Norse words.

2. VERB

/ ʃrik / (AmE), / ʃriːk / (BrE)
to utter a loud, high-pitched cry, often due to fear, pain, or excitement
utter a high-pitched cry

Examples

She was so startled she let out a loud shriek of terror.
The children began to shriek with laughter as they played in the yard.
He shrieked in pain when he accidentally stepped on the sharp stone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scream - to make a loud, piercing sound, usually from fear or pain
  • Antonym: whisper - to speak very softly, using one's breath rather than one's throat

Etymology

Probably imitative in origin, echoing a sharp cry.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00916373-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ ʃrik / (AmE), / ʃriːk / (BrE)
a loud, high-pitched, and piercing cry expressing terror, pain, excitement, or laughter
a sharp, piercing cry

Examples

A sudden shriek startled everyone in the quiet library.
The child let out a shriek when she saw the spider.
Their laughter turned into a shriek as the roller coaster took off.

Synset Examples

her screaming attracted the neighbors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scream - a loud, piercing cry expressing emotion or pain
  • Synonym: yell - a loud, sharp cry or shout
  • Antonym: whisper - a soft or confidential tone of voice

Etymology

From Middle English 'shriken', related to Old Norse 'skrīkja' (to screech).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07137720-n
Synset POS: n
