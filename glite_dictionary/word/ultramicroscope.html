ultramicroscope

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌltrəmaɪˈkroʊskoʊp / (AmE), / ˌʌltrəˈmʌɪkrəskəʊp / (BrE)
a type of light microscope that illuminates particles too small to be seen by ordinary microscopes by using scattered light
specialized light microscope for tiny particles

Examples

The scientist used an ultramicroscope to observe the colloidal particles suspended in the solution.
Advances in imaging have made the ultramicroscope an essential tool in nanotechnology research.
Unlike a regular microscope, the ultramicroscope allows visualization of particles much smaller than the wavelength of light.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: microscope - an optical instrument used to view very small objects

Etymology

From ultra- (beyond) + microscope.
