striking

1. ADJ

/ ˈstraɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstraɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
attracting attention by being unusual, impressive, or noticeable
very noticeable or impressive

Examples

The museum displayed a striking piece of modern art that captured everyone’s gaze.
She bore a striking resemblance to her grandmother at the same age.
What makes the building truly striking is its unusual glass facade.

3. NOUN

/ ˈstraɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstraɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of hitting or making contact between two things, especially with force.
the act of hitting or making contact

Examples

The striking of the clock could be heard throughout the quiet house.
Repeated striking of the bell signaled the end of the match.
Her hand was sore from the striking of the punching bag during training.

Synset Examples

repeated hitting raised a large bruise
after three misses she finally got a hit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hit - a successful strike or contact between two things
  • Antonym: miss - a failure to make contact

Etymology

From strike (verb) + -ing (noun-forming suffix).
