to circumnavigate

1. VERB

/ ˌsɜrkəmˈnævɪˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˌsɜːkəmˈnævɪɡeɪt / (BrE)
to travel all the way around something, especially the world, usually by ship or plane
travel all the way around (esp. the world)

Examples

In 1519, Magellan set out to circumnavigate the globe for the first time.
She hopes to circumnavigate the world by sailing solo.
Their goal was to circumnavigate Antarctica in a small research vessel.

Synset Examples

We compassed the earth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: circle - move all the way around something

Etymology

From Latin 'circum-' (around) + 'navigare' (to sail).
