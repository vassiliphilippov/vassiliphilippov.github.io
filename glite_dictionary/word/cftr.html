CFTR

1. NOUN

/ si ɛf ti ɑr / (AmE), / si ɛf ti ɑː / (BrE)
the gene that codes for a protein regulating the movement of chloride ions across cell membranes; mutations in this gene cause cystic fibrosis
gene mutated in cystic fibrosis
medical

Examples

The CFTR gene plays a crucial role in the development of cystic fibrosis.
Scientists are developing therapies to correct defective CFTR proteins in patients.
Genetic testing can identify mutations in the CFTR gene.

Etymology

Acronym of Cystic Fibrosis Transmembrane Conductance Regulator.
