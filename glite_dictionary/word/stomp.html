stomp

1. NOUN

/ stɑmp / (AmE), / stɒmp / (BrE)
a dance that involves rhythmical stamping steps, often performed to upbeat music
rhythmical dance involving stamping steps

Examples

They taught us the latest stomp at the dance workshop last weekend.
The club was packed as everyone joined in the stomp on the dance floor.
Her energetic stomp impressed the judges in the talent competition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tap dance - a dance characterized by rhythmical tapping of the toes and heels
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00534728-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ stɑmp / (AmE), / stɒmp / (BrE)
to walk heavily and noisily, usually showing anger or determination
walk with heavy, noisy steps

Examples

She stomped out of the room, slamming the door behind her.
The children stomped through the puddles in their boots.
He was so upset that he stomped up the stairs without saying a word.

Synset Examples

The men stomped through the snow in their heavy boots

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stamp - to bring down one's foot heavily; to walk with heavy steps

Etymology

Probably imitative of the sound of heavy steps, influenced by 'stamp'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01929291-v
Synset POS: v
