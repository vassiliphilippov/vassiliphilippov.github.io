Rhinolophidae

1. NOUN

/ raɪˌnɑːlˈɑːfəˌdi / (AmE), / raɪˌnɒlˈɒfɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of Old World bats known as leaf-nosed bats, characterized by a horseshoe-shaped fold of skin on the nose
a family of Old World leaf-nosed bats

Examples

The Rhinolophidae are distinguished by their complex noseleaf structures used in echolocation.
Several species within the Rhinolophidae family are found across Asia and Africa.
Researchers are studying how Rhinolophidae bats adapt to different habitats.

Etymology

From modern Latin 'Rhinolophidae', from Greek 'rhinos' (nose) and 'lophos' (crest, tuft).
