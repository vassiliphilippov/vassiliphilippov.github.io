strings

1. NOUN

/ strɪŋz / (AmE), / strɪŋz / (BrE)
the section of an orchestra composed of musicians who play stringed instruments, such as violins, violas, cellos, and double basses
section in orchestra playing stringed instruments

Examples

The strings played a haunting melody that filled the concert hall.
During the rehearsal, the conductor focused on getting the strings and winds to blend seamlessly.
A solo violinist from the strings received a standing ovation at the end of the performance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: woodwinds - the section of an orchestra that plays woodwind instruments
  • Antonym: brass - the section of an orchestra that plays brass instruments
  • Antonym: percussion - the section of an orchestra that plays percussion instruments
