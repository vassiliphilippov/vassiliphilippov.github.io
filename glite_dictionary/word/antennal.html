antennal

1. ADJ

/ ænˈtɛnəl / (AmE), / ænˈtiː.nəl / (BrE)
relating to or of the antennae, especially those of an insect or other arthropod.
relating to an antenna (biology)

Examples

The butterfly's antennal structures help it detect changes in the environment.
Scientists studied the antennal responses of bees to different scents.
Many moths have highly developed antennal lobes for sensing pheromones.

Synset Examples

antennal senses of insects

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antenna - a sensory appendage found in insects and some other arthropods

Etymology

From 'antenna' + '-al', forming an adjective describing something pertaining to antennae.
