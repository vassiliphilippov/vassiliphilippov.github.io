artful

1. ADJ

/ ˈɑrt.fəl / (AmE), / ˈɑːt.fəl / (BrE)
Clever or skillful, especially in a way that is not honest or straightforward; sly or cunning.
cunning; not straightforward

Examples

Her artful questions managed to uncover secrets without ever seeming intrusive.
He gave an artful explanation that avoided the main issue entirely.
The artful politician skillfully dodged direct answers during the debate.

Synset Examples

an ambitious, disingenuous, philistine, and hypocritical operator, who … exemplified … the most disagreeable traits of his time
a disingenuous excuse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cunning - clever at deceiving people; sly
  • Antonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and sincere

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French *arteful*, from *art* (skill).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɑrtfəl / (AmE), / ˈɑːtf(ə)l / (BrE)
clever or skillful, especially in achieving one's aims by using indirect or cunning methods.
clever and skillful, often using cunning

Examples

Her artful handling of the negotiations impressed everyone at the meeting.
The artful arrangement of colors in the painting drew the viewer's eye instantly.
He offered an artful reply that avoided answering the reporter's question directly.

Synset Examples

the artful dodger
an artful choice of metaphors

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crafty - clever at achieving aims by using deceit or trickery
  • Synonym: cunning - having or showing skill in achieving one's ends by deceit
  • Antonym: artless - without cunning or deceit; natural and simple

Etymology

From English 'art' + '-ful' (full of art, skill, or craft).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00149002-a
Synset POS: a
