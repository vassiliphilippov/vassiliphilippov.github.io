trifle

1. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪ.fəl / (AmE), / ˈtraɪ.fəl / (BrE)
A cold dessert consisting of layers of sponge cake soaked with fruit or jelly, often topped with custard, cream, and sometimes nuts or chocolate.
layered dessert with cake, fruit, and cream
british

Examples

Every Christmas, my grandmother prepares a delicious trifle with homemade custard and strawberries.
The restaurant's signature dessert is a chocolate and raspberry trifle served in a glass jar.
She decorated the top of the trifle with whipped cream and toasted almonds.

Etymology

From Old French 'trufle' (mockery, trifle), later applied to a light dessert in English.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtraɪfl̩ / (AmE), / ˈtraɪf(ə)l / (BrE)
A thing of little value or importance; something insignificant.
something of little importance

Examples

He dismissed the error as a mere trifle not worth discussing.
She doesn't waste her time on trifles and always focuses on what matters most.
To argue over such a trifle seems completely pointless to me.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bagatelle - something of little value or importance
  • Synonym: triviality - something trivial; an unimportant thing
  • Antonym: essential - something of vital importance or necessity

Etymology

From Old French *trufle* (mockery, trick), related to *truffer* (to mock, deceive).
