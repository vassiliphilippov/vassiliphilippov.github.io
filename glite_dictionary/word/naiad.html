naiad

1. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪˌæd / (AmE), / ˈneɪ.ad / (BrE)
A submerged aquatic plant of fresh or brackish water, with narrow leaves and small flowers, belonging to the genus Najas.
submerged aquatic plant of fresh water

Examples

The pond was filled with naiad, swaying gently beneath the surface of the water.
Scientists noted a sharp increase in naiad populations following efforts to reduce pollution in the lake.
She carefully identified the floating plant as a naiad after examining its slender leaves and tiny flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water nymph - a female spirit of water in mythology, also called a naiad
  • Synonym: Najas - the genus of aquatic plants referred to as naiad

Etymology

From Greek naïs, naiados (water nymph), later used in botany for plants living in water.

2. NOUN

/ ˈnaɪˌæd / (AmE), / ˈneɪ.ad / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a nymph inhabiting lakes, springs, rivers, and fountains.
water nymph in Greek mythology

Examples

According to legend, a naiad guarded the crystal-clear spring in the forest.
The poet described the naiads dancing beside ancient rivers under the moonlight.
She painted a scene depicting a naiad rising gracefully from a tranquil lake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nymph - a minor female nature deity in mythology, especially one associated with a particular location or landform

Etymology

From Greek *naiás*, *naiádos* meaning 'water nymph'.
