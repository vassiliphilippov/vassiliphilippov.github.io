to redo

1. VERB

/ ˌriˈdu / (AmE), / ˌriːˈduː / (BrE)
to do something again or make it new, often in order to improve or correct it
do again or make new

Examples

I need to redo this assignment because I made too many mistakes the first time.
She decided to redo her kitchen to give it a more modern look.
If you don't pass the test, you'll have to redo the entire course.

Synset Examples

She is remaking her image

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remake - to make something again or in a different way
  • Antonym: to undo - to reverse the effect of something done

Etymology

From re- (again) + do; first recorded in the 18th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01622744-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ riˈdu / (AmE), / ˌriːˈduː / (BrE)
to renovate or remodel (part of a building, room, etc.)
renovate or remodel (a room, building)

Examples

They decided to redo the kitchen with modern appliances and new tiles.
We're planning to redo the bathroom next spring.
After twenty years, it was time to redo the floors in the living room.

Synset Examples

We are remodeling these rooms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to renovate - to restore something to a good state of repair
  • Synonym: to remodel - to change the structure or shape of something, especially a building

Etymology

From re- (again) + do (perform an action).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00199854-v
Synset POS: v
