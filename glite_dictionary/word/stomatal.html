stomatal

1. ADJ

/ stoʊˈmætəl / (AmE), / stəˈmætəl / (BrE)
relating to or of the nature of a stoma or having mouthlike openings, especially in reference to plant tissues.
relating to stomata (plant pores)
medical

Examples

The number of stomatal openings on a leaf can affect its rate of transpiration.
Researchers measured the stomatal conductance to study how plants respond to drought.
Some species have adapted with a lower stomatal density to conserve water.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stoma - a small opening or pore, especially on a leaf surface

Etymology

From New Latin 'stoma' (mouth, opening) + English '-al' (adjectival suffix).

2. ADJ

/ stoʊˈmeɪ.təl / (AmE), / stəˈmɑː.təl / (BrE)
relating to or associated with stomata, the microscopic openings in plant leaves or stems
relating to plant stomata
medical

Examples

The rate of water loss depends largely on stomatal conductance.
Drought conditions can lead to stomatal closure to conserve water.
The scientist measured stomatal density across different leaf surfaces.

Synset Examples

stomatal openings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stomata - microscopic pores on plant surfaces for gas exchange

Etymology

From New Latin stomat-, stomata (mouth, opening) + -al (adjective suffix).
