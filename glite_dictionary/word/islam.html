Islam

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪzlɑm / (AmE), / ˈɪzlɑːm / (BrE)
The religion and civilization of Muslims collectively, based on the teachings of the prophet Muhammad and governed by the Qur'an.
the Muslim religion and civilization

Examples

Islam has shaped the cultures and societies of many regions around the world.
The spread of Islam in the 7th century led to the rise of many powerful empires.
In her studies, she focused on the art and architecture of Islam.

Synset Examples

Islam is predominant in northern Africa, the Middle East, Pakistan, and Indonesia

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Christianity - the religion based on the life and teachings of Jesus Christ
  • Antonym: Hinduism - the major religious and cultural tradition of South Asia

Etymology

From Arabic 'islām' meaning 'submission (to God)'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪˈslɑm / (AmE), / ˈɪzlɑːm / (BrE)
The religion founded by the Prophet Muhammad in Arabia in the 7th century, based on belief in one God (Allah) and outlined in the Koran.
major world religion founded by Muhammad

Examples

Islam is a complete way of life for its followers, influencing social, legal, and family matters.
Many people around the world practice Islam and observe its customs such as daily prayers and Ramadan fasting.
The spread of Islam in the 7th century had a profound impact on the history and culture of the Middle East.

Synset Examples

Islam is a complete way of life, not a Sunday religion
the term Muhammadanism is offensive to Muslims who believe that Allah, not Muhammad, founded their religion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Islamic - relating to Islam or its followers
  • Synonym: Muslim - a follower of Islam
  • Antonym: Christianity - the religion based on the life and teachings of Jesus Christ
  • Antonym: Judaism - the religion of the Jewish people

Etymology

From Arabic 'islām' meaning 'submission (to God)'.
