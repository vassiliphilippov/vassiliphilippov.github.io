to machinate

1. VERB

/ ˈmæk.əˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈmæk.ɪ.neɪt / (BrE)
to plan or scheme, especially with a devious or sinister intent; to plot systematically
plot or scheme systematically

Examples

The conspirators began to machinate against the government in secret.
He was accused of trying to machinate a hostile takeover of the company.
Despite their efforts to machinate, their plan was discovered and foiled.

Synset Examples

machinate a plot
organize a strike
devise a plan to take over the director's office

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plot - to make secret plans, usually to do something harmful or illegal
  • Synonym: to conspire - to secretly make plans with others to commit an unlawful or harmful act
  • Synonym: scheme - to make secret plans for achieving something, especially in a cunning or underhanded way

Etymology

From Latin 'machinari' (to contrive, plot), from 'machina' (machine, device).

2. VERB

/ ˈmæk.ɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈmæk.ɪ.neɪt / (BrE)
to plan or plot, especially in a secretive or underhanded way; to scheme
to plot or scheme secretly

Examples

The rebels began to machinate against the ruling party.
Rival companies may machinate to gain an unfair advantage in the market.
He was known to machinate behind the scenes to influence the outcome.

Synset Examples

They conspired to overthrow the government

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scheme - to make secret or devious plans
  • Synonym: plot - to secretly make plans to carry out an illegal or harmful act

Etymology

From Latin *machinari* (to contrive, devise), from *machina* (machine, device).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00708804-v
Synset POS: v
