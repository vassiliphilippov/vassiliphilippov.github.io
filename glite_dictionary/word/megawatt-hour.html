megawatt-hour

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛɡəˌwɑt ˈaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˌmɛɡəwɒt ˈaʊə / (BrE)
A unit of energy equal to one million watts used for one hour, commonly used to measure electricity consumption.
unit of energy equal to one million watt-hours

Examples

The solar farm generates enough electricity to supply over 500 megawatt-hours each year.
The city consumed 2,400 megawatt-hours during the hottest month of the summer.
One megawatt-hour can typically power about 330 homes for one hour.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: kilowatt-hour - a unit of energy equal to one thousand watt-hours

Etymology

From 'mega-' (meaning million), 'watt' (unit of power named after James Watt), and 'hour'.
