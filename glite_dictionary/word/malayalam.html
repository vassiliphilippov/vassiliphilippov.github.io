Malayalam

1. NOUN

/ məˌlɑːjəˈlɑːm / (AmE), / ˌmæləˈjɑːləm / (BrE)
a Dravidian language spoken in southwestern India, mainly in the state of Kerala and the union territories of Lakshadweep and Puducherry
Dravidian language of southwestern India

Examples

She learned to speak Malayalam fluently after moving to Kerala.
Many films in India are produced in the Malayalam language.
The Malayalam script looks different from other South Indian scripts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tamil - a Dravidian language spoken mainly in southern India and Sri Lanka

Etymology

From Malayalam *Malayāḷam*, literally ‘mountain region’.

2. NOUN

/ məˌlɑːjəˈlɑːm / (AmE), / ˌmaləˈjɑːləm / (BrE)
a Dravidian language closely related to Tamil, spoken predominantly in the southwestern Indian state of Kerala and the union territories of Lakshadweep and Puducherry
a Dravidian language of southwestern India

Examples

She learned to speak Malayalam after moving to Kerala for work.
The novel was originally written in Malayalam and later translated into English.
At home, we watch movies in Malayalam to stay connected to our culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tamil - a Dravidian language spoken in southern India and Sri Lanka

Etymology

From Malayalam *malayāḷam*, meaning 'mountain region' (mala 'mountain' + āḷam 'region').

Synset POS: n
