primitively

1. ADV

/ ˈprɪmətɪvli / (AmE), / ˈprɪmɪtɪvli / (BrE)
In a manner that relates to the original state, beginning, or earliest form of something.
in relation to origin or beginning

Examples

The custom was primitively religious in origin but gradually lost its sacred nature.
The species was primitively aquatic before adapting to life on land.
Rights were primitively assigned according to family hierarchy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: originally - from or in the beginning; at first

Etymology

From 'primitive' (from Latin 'primitivus', meaning 'first or earliest') + '-ly'.

2. ADV

/ ˈprɪmətɪvli / (AmE), / ˈprɪmətɪvli / (BrE)
In a primitive, simple, or unsophisticated style or manner.
in a primitive or unsophisticated manner

Examples

The huts were constructed primitively, using only stones and mud.
Many early tools were made primitively by chipping away at rocks.
The ceremony was performed primitively, much as it would have been centuries ago.

Synset Examples

rather primitively operated foundries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simply - in a simple manner
  • Antonym: sophisticatedly - in a sophisticated or refined manner

Etymology

From 'primitive' + -ly, from Latin 'primitivus' (first of its kind).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00433705-r
Synset POS: r
