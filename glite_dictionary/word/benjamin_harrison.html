Benjamin Harrison

1. PROPN

/ ˈbɛndʒəmɪn ˈhærəsən / (AmE), / ˈbɛndʒəmɪn ˈhærɪsən / (BrE)
(1833–1901) The 23rd President of the United States, serving from 1889 to 1893.
23rd U.S. President (1889–1893)

Examples

Benjamin Harrison signed the Sherman Antitrust Act into law during his presidency.
During Benjamin Harrison's term, electricity was installed in the White House for the first time.
Many historians study the foreign policies of Benjamin Harrison to understand late 19th-century America.
