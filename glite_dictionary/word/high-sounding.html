high-sounding

1. ADJ

/ ˈhaɪˌsaʊndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪˌsaʊndɪŋ / (BrE)
(of words or ideals) seeming important, impressive, or grand, but often lacking real meaning or sincerity; pretentious.
pretentious or grand-sounding (esp. of language)

Examples

His proposal was filled with high-sounding phrases but lacked any practical details.
The candidate's high-sounding promises impressed some voters but left others skeptical.
Despite her high-sounding ideals, her actions rarely matched her words.

Synset Examples

high-flown talk of preserving the moral tone of the school
a high-sounding dissertation on the means to attain social revolution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: high-flown - using grand or pretentious language
  • Synonym: pretentious - trying to seem more important or impressive than is actually the case
