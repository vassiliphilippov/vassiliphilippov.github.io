to admit

1. VERB

/ ədˈmɪt / (AmE), / ədˈmɪt / (BrE)
to allow or afford the possibility (usually used with 'of')
allow the possibility of

Examples

The evidence admits of various interpretations, so the jury must consider all possibilities.
This theory admits of no exceptions according to current understanding.
Such behavior admits of only one explanation—he must have been desperate.

Synset Examples

This problem admits of no solution
This short story allows of several different interpretations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to allow - permit or make possible
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02751361-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ədˈmɪt / (AmE), / ədˈmɪt / (BrE)
to allow someone or something to enter a place or have access to it
allow entrance or access

Examples

Only employees are allowed to admit visitors through this entrance.
The museum does not admit children under twelve without an adult.
This ticket will admit you to the evening performance.

Synset Examples

The French doors admit onto the yard

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to exclude - to prevent someone or something from entering a place

Etymology

From Latin 'admittere' (to allow to enter), from ad- (to) + mittere (send).
