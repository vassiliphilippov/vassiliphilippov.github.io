Tyre

1. PROPN

/ taɪr / (AmE), / taɪə / (BrE)
an ancient city and modern port in southern Lebanon, formerly a major Phoenician seaport noted for its trade and production of purple dye and silks.
ancient and modern city in Lebanon

Examples

The ancient city of Tyre was renowned for its production of the purple dye prized by royalty.
Many tourists visit Tyre to see its impressive Roman ruins and Mediterranean coastline.
In antiquity, Tyre played a vital role as a major Phoenician trading center.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Týros, from Phoenician Ṣūr meaning 'rock'.
