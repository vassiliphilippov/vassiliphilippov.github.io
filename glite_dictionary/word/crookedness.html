crookedness

1. NOUN

/ ˈkrʊkɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈkrʊkɪdnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being bent, curved, or not straight; the condition of having bends or angles
the state of being bent or not straight

Examples

The crookedness of the tree trunk made it look unique and old.
He tried to ignore the crookedness in the picture frame, but it still bothered him.
The ancient road’s crookedness added to the village’s charm.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: straightness - the quality of being straight

Etymology

From 'crooked' + '-ness', meaning the state or quality of being crooked.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkrʊkɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈkrʊkɪdnɪs / (BrE)
the state or quality of having a twisted or contorted shape or position
twisted or contorted shape

Examples

The mountain path was known for its crookedness, winding sharply through the hills.
You could see the crookedness of the old tree’s trunk against the skyline.
The sculpture captures the crookedness of a dancer’s body in mid-motion.

Synset Examples

they built a tree house in the tortuosities of its boughs
the acrobat performed incredible contortions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tortuosity - the quality or state of being twisted or winding
  • Antonym: straightness - the quality or condition of being straight

Etymology

From 'crooked' + '-ness', with 'crooked' from Old English 'crōc' (hook, bend).

3. NOUN

/ ˈkrʊkɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈkrʊkɪdnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being deceitful, dishonest, or underhanded
dishonesty; deceitfulness

Examples

The politician's crookedness was eventually exposed by the investigative journalists.
Many people were shocked by the crookedness that pervaded the company’s leadership.
She refused to participate in any crookedness, insisting on running a fair and honest campaign.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: honesty - the quality of being honest and truthful
  • Synonym: deceitfulness - the quality of being deceitful or misleading
  • Antonym: integrity - the quality of being honest and having strong moral principles

Etymology

From 'crooked' (not straight, dishonest) + '-ness' (forming nouns denoting a state or quality).
