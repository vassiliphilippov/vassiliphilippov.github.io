areola

1. NOUN

/ əˈriː.ə.lə / (AmE), / əˈriː.ə.lə / (BrE)
a small circular area of different color or texture from its surroundings, especially the ring of pigmented skin surrounding a nipple
small circular area (esp. around the nipple)
medical

Examples

The baby's mouth was positioned over the mother's areola during breastfeeding.
A red areola formed around the insect bite on his arm.
Doctors examined the areola for any unusual changes in size or color.

Etymology

From Latin areola (small open space), diminutive of area.

2. NOUN

/ əˈriːələ / (AmE), / əˈriːələ / (BrE)
a small space or area in a tissue or body part, such as the region between veins on a leaf or on an insect's wing
small space in tissue or body part

Examples

Each leaf showed a distinct areola bordered by its veins.
Insects such as dragonflies have a transparent areola on their wings.
The areola between two veins often contains specialized cells.

Etymology

From Latin 'areola', diminutive of 'area' (open space).

Source: WordNet
