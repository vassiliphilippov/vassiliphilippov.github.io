Mertensia virginica

1. NOUN

/ mɚˈtɛnʃə vɚˈdʒɪnɪkə / (AmE), / məˈtɛnsiə vɜːˈdʒɪnɪkə / (BrE)
A smooth, upright herb of eastern North America, known as Virginia bluebell, with entire leaves and showy blue flowers that are pink when in bud.
species of herbaceous plant (Virginia bluebell)

Examples

The woodland was carpeted with blooming Mertensia virginica in early spring.
Gardeners prize Mertensia virginica for its delicate blue flowers and ease of care.
We identified the wildflower as Mertensia virginica by its characteristic pink buds turning blue.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Virginia bluebell - Common English name for Mertensia virginica

Etymology

Genus name from German botanist Franz Carl Mertens; species name from 'Virginia,' where it was first identified.
