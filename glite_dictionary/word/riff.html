riff

1. VERB

/ rɪf / (AmE), / rɪf / (BrE)
(of a musician) to play short repeated phrases or improvisational patterns, especially in jazz, rock, or blues music.
play short repeated musical phrases

Examples

The guitarist began to riff on a funky blues progression, energizing the crowd.
Jazz musicians often riff during live performances, inventing melodies on the spot.
She riffed effortlessly, weaving intricate patterns into the song's structure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to improvise - to create and perform music spontaneously

Etymology

From jazz slang, derived from 'riff' (noun) meaning a short repeated musical phrase.

2. NOUN

/ rɪf / (AmE), / rɪf / (BrE)
a short, repeated musical phrase, especially in jazz, that is typically used as a background for a solo improvisation
repeated musical phrase in jazz

Examples

The guitarist played a catchy riff that set the tone for the entire song.
During the saxophone solo, the band kept a steady riff going in the background.
Classic jazz often features an energetic riff repeated beneath the improvisation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ostinato - a continually repeated musical phrase or rhythm

Etymology

Probably an abbreviation of 'refrain' or 'rhythmic figure', originally from jazz slang in the early 20th century.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
