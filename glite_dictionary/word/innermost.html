innermost

1. ADJ

/ ˈɪnɚˌmoʊst / (AmE), / ˈɪnəməʊst / (BrE)
most private, secret, or deeply felt; deepest within a person or thing
most private or deepest part

Examples

She shared her innermost thoughts with her closest friend.
He rarely expresses his innermost feelings in public.
Inside the artist's innermost world lie her deepest inspirations.

Synset Examples

one's innermost feelings

Related Terms

  • Antonym: outermost - farthest from the center or inside
  • Degree Lower: inner - located further in but not necessarily the deepest or most private

Etymology

From 'inner' + 'most', based on Old English 'innemest', meaning most inward.

2. ADJ

/ ˈɪnərˌmoʊst / (AmE), / ˈɪnəməʊst / (BrE)
Situated or occurring furthest inside; deepest within something, either literally or figuratively.
situated furthest inside; deepest within

Examples

She kept her innermost thoughts hidden from everyone around her.
At the center of the castle stood the innermost chamber, shrouded in mystery.
He finally admitted his innermost fears during the therapy session.

Synset Examples

the innermost chamber

Related Terms

  • Antonym: outermost - situated furthest toward the outside

Etymology

Formed from 'inner' + 'most', using the superlative suffix '-most'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00953673-s
Synset POS: s
