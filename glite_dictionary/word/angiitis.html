angiitis

1. NOUN

/ ˌændʒiˈaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌandʒiˈaɪtɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of a blood vessel or lymphatic duct
inflammation of a blood vessel
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed her condition as angiitis after reviewing the test results.
Various forms of angiitis can cause pain and swelling in affected tissues.
Prompt treatment of angiitis is important to prevent complications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vasculitis - inflammation of blood vessels

Etymology

From Greek angeion (vessel) + -itis (inflammation).
