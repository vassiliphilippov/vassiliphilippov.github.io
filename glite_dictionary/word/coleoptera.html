Coleoptera

1. NOUN

/ koʊliˈɑptərə / (AmE), / kəʊliˈɒptərə / (BrE)
the order of insects that comprises the beetles, characterized by hard forewings covering the flight wings
the insect order of beetles

Examples

Many species in the Coleoptera order display vibrant colors and intricate patterns.
Scientists estimate that Coleoptera make up nearly a quarter of all known animal species.
The student specialized in the study of Coleoptera during her entomology degree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beetle - an insect of the order Coleoptera, with hard wing cases

Etymology

From Greek 'koleos' (sheath) + 'pteron' (wing), referring to their hardened forewings.
