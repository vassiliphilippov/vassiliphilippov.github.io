3. NOUN

/ ˈskʌnər / (AmE), / ˈskʌnə / (BrE)
A strong feeling of dislike, disgust, or aversion towards someone or something, especially in Scottish English.
strong feeling of dislike or disgust (Scottish)
british

Examples

She took a scunner to broccoli as a child and never ate it again.
After his rude remarks, I couldn't help but feel a scunner towards him.
He has such a scunner for early mornings that he always arrives late.

Synset Examples

I felt a scunner at the thought of doing chores.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aversion - a strong dislike or disinclination
  • Synonym: disgust - a strong sense of distaste or revulsion

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'skonnen' (to loathe), related to Old Norse 'skyn' (discernment, perception).
