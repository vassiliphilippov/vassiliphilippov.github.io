debunking

1. NOUN

/ diˈbʌŋkɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈbʌŋkɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of exposing the falseness or pretensions of an idea, belief, or person
exposing falseness or pretensions

Examples

The investigative journalist was known for his fearless debunking of urban legends.
Her lecture focused on the debunking of common nutritional myths.
Recent research contributed to the debunking of several widely held scientific misconceptions.

Synset Examples

the debunking of religion has been too successful

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to debunk - to expose the falseness of a myth, idea, or belief

Etymology

From the verb "debunk," first used in the 1920s, derived from "bunk" (nonsense).
