Aberdare

1. PROPN

/ ˌæbərˈdɛr / (AmE), / ˌabəˈdɛə / (BrE)
A mining town in southern Wales, known historically for its coal industry.
mining town in southern Wales

Examples

The industrial history of Aberdare is closely linked to coal mining and ironworks.
She visited Aberdare on a tour through the valleys of southern Wales.
Many families in Aberdare have deep roots in the mining community.

Etymology

From Welsh Aberdâr, meaning 'mouth of the river Dare'.
