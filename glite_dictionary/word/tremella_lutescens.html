Tremella lutescens

1. NOUN

/ trɛˈmɛlə luːˈtɛsɛnz / (AmE), / trɪˈmiːlə luːˈtɛsɛnz / (BrE)
A species of yellow, jelly-like fungus commonly found on decaying wood.
yellow jelly fungus species

Examples

The fallen log was covered in bright yellow Tremella lutescens after the rain.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of Tremella lutescens in temperate forests.
Because of its gelatinous texture, Tremella lutescens is easily recognized by mushroom enthusiasts.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Tremella' meaning trembling (referring to its jelly-like texture), 'lutescens' meaning yellowish.
