religiousness

1. NOUN

/ rɪˈlɪdʒəsnəs / (AmE), / rɪˈlɪdʒəsnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being religious; devotion and piety in beliefs or practices.
quality of being religious or pious

Examples

Her religiousness is evident in the way she attends services every week.
The villagers admired his religiousness and often sought his advice on spiritual matters.
Despite growing up in a secular household, his religiousness became important later in life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: piety - the quality of being religious or reverent
  • Antonym: irreligiousness - the quality of lacking religious feeling or respect

Etymology

Formed from 'religious' + '-ness' (noun-forming suffix). 'Religious' from Latin 'religiosus'.

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈlɪdʒəsnəs / (AmE), / rɪˈlɪdʒəsnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being extremely conscientious or meticulous, especially as if guided by religious devotion.
extreme conscientiousness; devout attentiveness

Examples

His religiousness in following safety procedures kept everyone calm during the emergency.
She approached her daily exercise routine with a kind of religiousness that impressed her friends.
The chef's religiousness about cleanliness ensured the kitchen was always spotless.

Synset Examples

his care in observing the rules of good health amounted to a kind of religiousness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: devotion - deep commitment or enthusiasm for a cause or activity
  • Antonym: carelessness - lack of attention or thoroughness

Etymology

From 'religious' + '-ness', patterned after Middle English.
