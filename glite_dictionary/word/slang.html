slang

1. NOUN

/ slæŋ / (AmE), / slæŋ / (BrE)
informal language consisting of words and expressions that are not regarded as appropriate for formal situations, often used within particular groups.
informal, nonstandard language

Examples

The teenagers' conversation was filled with slang that their parents couldn't understand.
Some slang terms quickly spread online and become widely used.
Writers often use slang to make characters sound more authentic and relatable.

Synset Examples

their speech was full of slang expressions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: colloquialism - a word or phrase used in informal conversation but not in formal speech
  • Degree Higher: jargon - special words or expressions used by a particular group, often difficult for outsiders to understand

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from Scandinavian dialectal 'slangen' (to sling, throw), referring to 'slinging words.'

2. VERB

/ slæŋ / (AmE), / slæŋ / (BrE)
to use slang or vulgar language when speaking or writing
use slang or vulgar language

Examples

He tended to slang when he was angry, making his language quite colorful.
Some comedians slang their way through routines to get laughs from the audience.
She disliked coworkers who would constantly slang in the office environment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curse - to use offensive or profane language

Etymology

Derivative of the noun 'slang', originally of uncertain origin, first attested in late 18th century.

3. NOUN

/ slæŋ / (AmE), / slæŋ / (BrE)
very informal language, often unique to a particular group, used instead of standard words and not generally accepted in formal speech or writing
very informal group-specific language
slang

Examples

Teenagers often use slang that adults may not understand.
He was criticized for using too much slang in his speech.
Different regions develop their own unique slang expressions.

Synset Examples

they don't speak our lingo

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jargon - specialized language used by a particular profession or group
  • Synonym: colloquialism - a word or phrase used in informal language
  • Antonym: standard language - the form of a language accepted as the norm, especially in formal settings

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly from Scandinavian languages, related to Norwegian 'slengja kjeften' (to sling the jaw).

Synset POS: n
