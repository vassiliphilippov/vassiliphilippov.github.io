hazardousness

1. NOUN

/ ˈhæzərdəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈhazədəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being dangerous or involving risk
state of being dangerous

Examples

The hazardousness of the chemical plant was a major concern for local residents.
Inspections are carried out to assess the hazardousness of the working environment.
Due to its hazardousness, the area was declared off-limits to the public.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dangerousness - the quality of being dangerous
  • Antonym: safety - the condition of being safe, opposite of dangerousness

Etymology

From 'hazardous' + '-ness'; ultimately from Old French 'hasard' (game of chance).
