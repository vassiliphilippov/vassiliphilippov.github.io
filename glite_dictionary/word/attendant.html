attendant

1. ADJ

/ əˈtɛndənt / (AmE), / əˈtɛndənt / (BrE)
present at a particular event, place, or occasion; in attendance.
present at an event or place

Examples

The attendant guests all signed the register at the entrance.
Only attendant members may vote during the meeting.
The award was given to all attendant students at the ceremony.

Synset Examples

attendant members of the congregation

Related Terms

  • Antonym: absent - not present at an event or place

Etymology

From Old French 'atendant', present participle of 'atendre' (to attend).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01852738-s
Synset POS: s

2. NOUN

/ əˈtɛndənt / (AmE), / əˈtɛndənt / (BrE)
an event, condition, or circumstance that occurs at the same time as or in connection with another
event or situation accompanying another

Examples

There are risks attendant upon any marriage.
Economic growth often comes with various social attendants.
The decision caused a flurry of activity and its attendant consequences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concomitant - an event or circumstance that accompanies another

Etymology

From Old French 'atendant', present participle of 'atendre' (attend), from Latin 'attendere' (to stretch toward).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07299259-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ əˈtɛn.dənt / (AmE), / əˈtɛn.dənt / (BrE)
occurring as a result of or in connection with a particular event or situation
occurring as a result or consequence

Examples

The construction of the dam brought attendant environmental and social challenges.
He failed to anticipate the risks attendant upon such a venture.
There was a great deal of stress, with all the attendant consequences for her health.

Synset Examples

the resultant savings were considerable
collateral target damage from a bombing run

Related Terms

  • Synonym: consequent - following as a result or effect
  • Synonym: resultant - resulting from a particular cause
  • Synonym: incidental - accompanying but not a major part of something

Etymology

From Old French 'atendant', present participle of 'atendre', from Latin 'attendere' (to attend to).

4. NOUN

/ əˈtɛn.dənt / (AmE), / əˈtɛn.dənt / (BrE)
a person who is present at and participates in a meeting or event
person present at a meeting or event

Examples

The conference was well-organized for both speakers and attendants.
As an attendant at the annual shareholders' meeting, she voiced her opinions freely.
The meeting room quickly filled with attendants eager to hear the keynote address.

Synset Examples

he was a regular attender at department meetings
the gathering satisfied both organizers and attendees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: attendee - a person who is present at an event or meeting
  • Synonym: participant - a person who takes part in something
  • Antonym: organizer - a person who arranges or supervises an event

Etymology

From Old French 'attendant', present participle of 'attendre' (to attend), from Latin 'attendere' (to give heed to).

5. NOUN

/ əˈtɛndənt / (AmE), / əˈtɛndənt / (BrE)
a person whose job is to serve or assist customers or guests, especially in a public place or at an event
person who serves or assists others

Examples

The hotel attendant helped us with our luggage.
A parking attendant will direct you to an available space.
The restroom attendant keeps the area clean and well-stocked.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: servant - a person employed to perform domestic duties for others
  • Antonym: customer - a person who purchases goods or services

Etymology

From Latin 'attendere' (to attend to, to wait upon) via Old French 'atendant'.

Synset POS: n
