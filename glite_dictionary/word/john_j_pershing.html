John J. Pershing

1. PROPN

/ dʒɑn dʒeɪ ˈpɜrʃɪŋ / (AmE), / dʒɒn dʒeɪ ˈpɜːʃɪŋ / (BrE)
(1860–1948) United States Army general who commanded American forces in Europe during World War I.
US general in World War I

Examples

John J. Pershing led the American Expeditionary Forces to victory in Europe during World War I.
Many historians regard John J. Pershing as one of the most capable American military leaders.
A statue honoring John J. Pershing stands in Washington, D.C.
