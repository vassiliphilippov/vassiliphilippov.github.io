Avalokiteshvara

1. PROPN

/ əˌvoʊloʊˌkiːtɛʃˈvɑːrə / (AmE), / əˌvəʊlɒˌkiːtɛʃˈvɑːrə / (BrE)
A Bodhisattva in Mahayana Buddhism embodying the compassion of all Buddhas, often depicted as a male figure and widely revered across Asia.
Buddhist Bodhisattva of compassion

Examples

Many Buddhist temples house a statue of Avalokiteshvara as a symbol of compassion.
The legends of Avalokiteshvara have inspired art and poetry throughout Asia.
She offered prayers to Avalokiteshvara in hopes of finding relief for the suffering of others.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Guanyin - The Chinese manifestation of Avalokiteshvara, often depicted as female

Etymology

From Sanskrit Avalokiteśvara, meaning 'Lord who looks down (in compassion)'.
