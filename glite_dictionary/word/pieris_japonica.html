Pieris japonica

1. NOUN

/ piˈɛrɪs dʒəˈpoʊnɪkə / (AmE), / piˈɪərɪs dʒəˈpɒnɪkə / (BrE)
A broad-leaved evergreen shrub native to Asia, with glossy leaves and drooping clusters of white flowers, commonly cultivated as an ornamental plant.
Asiatic evergreen shrub with white flowers

Examples

The garden was brightened by the Pieris japonica in early spring, thanks to its elegant clusters of white blossoms.
Gardeners often choose Pieris japonica for shaded borders because of its attractive foliage.
The nursery had several varieties of Pieris japonica, each with slightly different leaf coloration.

Etymology

From Latinized botanical name: genus Pieris + Latin japonica (of Japan).
