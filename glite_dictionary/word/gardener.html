gardener

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑrdnɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːdnə / (BrE)
A person whose job or hobby is to take care of a garden.
someone who looks after a garden

Examples

The gardener watered the roses every morning to keep them healthy.
After retiring, my grandfather became an avid gardener and grew vegetables in his backyard.
The city hired a new gardener to maintain the public park grounds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horticulturist - a person who studies or practices growing fruits, vegetables, and flowers

Etymology

From Middle English 'gardinere', from Old Northern French 'gardinier' (gardener), from 'gardin' (garden).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡɑrdənər / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːdnə / (BrE)
a person whose job or hobby is to work in a garden, caring for plants, flowers, and lawns
person who works in a garden

Examples

The gardener trimmed the hedges and planted new flowers along the path.
My grandmother is an expert gardener and grows tomatoes every summer.
They hired a gardener to take care of the lawns in the large estate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horticulturist - a person skilled in the cultivation of gardens or plants

Etymology

From Middle English gardinere, from Old French jardinier, from jardin (garden).
