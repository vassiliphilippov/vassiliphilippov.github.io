icebox

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪsˌbɑks / (AmE), / ˈaɪsˌbɒks / (BrE)
a container used for keeping food cold, especially one that does not use electricity; a refrigerator, particularly in historical contexts
non-electric refrigerator; old-fashioned food cooler
archaic american

Examples

My grandmother still keeps milk in an icebox instead of a modern refrigerator.
Before electric refrigerators were common, families relied on an icebox to preserve their food.
The old cottage kitchen had an icebox tucked into the corner near the sink.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: refrigerator - an electrical appliance for keeping food cold

Etymology

Formed from 'ice' + 'box', as early refrigerators used ice to cool food.
