to locate

1. VERB

/ loʊˈkeɪt / (AmE), / ləʊˈkeɪt / (BrE)
to place or establish someone or something in a particular place; to assign a location to
assign or place in a particular location

Examples

The company decided to locate its headquarters in San Francisco.
Many artists locate their studios in vibrant neighborhoods.
Government agencies often locate offices near major transport links.

Synset Examples

The company located some of their agents in Los Angeles

Related Terms

  • Synonym: station - to assign to a specified place or position
  • Antonym: to disperse - to distribute or spread over a wide area (sometimes contrasted with centralizing or assigning to a point)

Etymology

From Latin locare (to place), from locus (place).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02338685-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈloʊˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ləʊˈkeɪt / (BrE)
to determine or indicate the exact place, site, or limits of something, especially using an instrument or systematic method
determine the exact position of something

Examples

Scientists used satellite images to locate the lost city.
Surveyors locate the boundaries before any construction begins.
She tried to locate the exact position of the leak in the pipe.

Synset Examples

Our sense of sight enables us to locate objects in space
Locate the boundaries of the property

Related Terms

  • Synonym: find - discover the location or position of something
  • Synonym: to identify - establish or indicate what or where something is
  • Antonym: to lose - be unable to find or determine the position of something

Etymology

From Latin 'locare' (to place), from 'locus' (place).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02700775-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈloʊˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ləʊˈkeɪt / (BrE)
to discover the exact position or place of something or someone, especially by searching or examining
find the position of something

Examples

It was difficult to locate the nearest gas station late at night.
Rescuers managed to locate the missing hikers after several hours of searching.
I can't seem to locate my keys anywhere in the house.

Synset Examples

Can you locate your cousins in the Midwest?
My search turned up nothing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: find - to discover or obtain something after searching
  • Antonym: to lose - to fail to keep or maintain; to be unable to find

Etymology

From Latin 'locare' (to place), from 'locus' (place).
