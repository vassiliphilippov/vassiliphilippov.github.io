Humulus americanus

1. NOUN

/ ˈhjuːmjələs əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈhjuːmjʊləs əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnəs / (BrE)
A North American plant species of the hop genus, sometimes mistaken for the European hop.
North American hop plant

Examples

The botanist identified the specimen as Humulus americanus during her field study.
Farmers sometimes grow Humulus americanus for ornamental purposes or habitat restoration.
Unlike its European cousin, Humulus americanus is not commonly used in beer brewing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hop - A climbing plant of the genus Humulus used in brewing

Etymology

From Latin, genus name 'Humulus' and species epithet 'americanus', meaning 'of America'.
