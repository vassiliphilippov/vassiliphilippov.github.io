Taxus floridana

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæksəs flɔːˈrɪdənə / (AmE), / ˈtaksəs flɒˈrɪdənə / (BrE)
A small bushy species of yew native to northern Florida, with spreading branches and very narrow leaves.
a North Florida species of yew

Examples

The endangered Taxus floridana grows only in a few locations in northern Florida.
Scientists are studying Taxus floridana for its unique chemical compounds.
Efforts are underway to conserve remaining populations of Taxus floridana.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yew - an evergreen tree or shrub with red berries, genus Taxus

Etymology

From Latin: Taxus (yew) + floridana (of Florida).
