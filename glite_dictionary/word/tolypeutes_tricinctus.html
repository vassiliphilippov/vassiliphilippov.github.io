Tolypeutes tricinctus

1. NOUN

/ ˌtɑlɪˈpjuːtiːz traɪˈsɪŋktəs / (AmE), / ˌtɒlɪˈpjuːtiːz traɪˈsɪŋktəs / (BrE)
A species of South American armadillo with three bands of bony plates, known as the Brazilian three-banded armadillo.
a South American armadillo species

Examples

The Tolypeutes tricinctus is capable of rolling itself into a tight ball for protection.
Habitat loss has made Tolypeutes tricinctus increasingly rare in its native Brazil.
Researchers are working to conserve Tolypeutes tricinctus due to its vulnerable status.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name Tolypeutes + species epithet tricinctus, meaning 'three-banded'.
