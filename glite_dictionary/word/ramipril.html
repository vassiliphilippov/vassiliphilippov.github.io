ramipril

1. NOUN

/ ˈræmɪprɪl / (AmE), / ˈramɪprɪl / (BrE)
an ACE inhibitor drug used to treat high blood pressure and heart failure, and to reduce the risk of heart attack and stroke
a drug for high blood pressure and heart conditions
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed ramipril to help control his blood pressure.
Some patients experience a dry cough while taking ramipril.
After his heart attack, he was started on ramipril as part of his treatment.
