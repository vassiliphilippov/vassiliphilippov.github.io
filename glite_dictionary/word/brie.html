Brie

1. NOUN

/ briː / (AmE), / briː / (BrE)
A soft, creamy white cheese from France, known for its mild flavor and edible rind.
soft creamy French cheese

Examples

We served slices of Brie with fresh strawberries at the party.
She prefers Brie over other cheeses because of its mild, creamy taste.
The platter included Brie, grapes, and crusty bread for a light lunch.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Camembert - a similar soft French cheese with a stronger flavor

Etymology

From French 'Brie', the region in northern France where the cheese originated.
