Euphorbia esula

1. NOUN

/ juːˈfɔr.bi.ə ˈɛs.ju.lə / (AmE), / juːˈfɔː.bɪ.ə ˈɛs.juː.lə / (BrE)
a tall perennial plant native to Europe and Asia, known as leafy spurge, considered a troublesome weed in parts of North America
a species of perennial weed (leafy spurge)

Examples

Euphorbia esula spreads aggressively, making it difficult to control in some pastures.
Many land managers struggle to eradicate Euphorbia esula from agricultural fields.
The bright green stems and yellow-green flowers are characteristic features of Euphorbia esula.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leafy spurge - common name for Euphorbia esula, a perennial weed

Etymology

From genus name Euphorbia, after Euphorbus (Greek physician), and Latin specific epithet esula (a type of spurge).
