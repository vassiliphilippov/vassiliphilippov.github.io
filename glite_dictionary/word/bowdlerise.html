to bowdlerise

1. VERB

/ ˈbaʊd.ləˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈbəʊd.lə.raɪz / (BrE)
to edit a text by removing or altering parts considered offensive or inappropriate
remove or alter offensive parts of a text

Examples

Some publishers chose to bowdlerise the novel to make it suitable for younger readers.
The director refused to bowdlerise his film for television broadcast.
Classic fairy tales are often bowdlerised to remove violence or disturbing themes.

Synset Examples

bowdlerize a novel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: censor - to suppress or remove inappropriate material

Etymology

From the name of Thomas Bowdler (1754–1825), who published an expurgated edition of Shakespeare.
