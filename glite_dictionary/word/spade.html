spade

1. VERB

/ speɪd / (AmE), / speɪd / (BrE)
to dig or move earth or other material with a spade
dig or move with a spade

Examples

In the spring, gardeners often spade fresh soil into their vegetable plots.
She spaded the old compost into the flower beds before planting new seeds.
He spent the afternoon spading the ground for the new tree.

Synset Examples

I spade compost into the flower beds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shovel - to dig or move material with a shovel

2. NOUN

/ speɪd / (AmE), / speɪd / (BrE)
A tool with a flat, typically rectangular blade and a long handle, used for digging or lifting earth, especially in gardening or construction.
tool for digging or lifting earth

Examples

He dug a deep hole in the garden using a spade.
The gardener leaned on his spade to rest for a moment.
Please fetch the spade from the shed so we can plant the tree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shovel - a tool with a broad blade used for lifting or moving material

Etymology

From Old English spadu, spada, related to Old Norse spaði (spade, paddle).

3. NOUN

/ speɪd / (AmE), / speɪd / (BrE)
A playing card of the suit with a black, leaf-shaped symbol, one of the four suits in a standard deck of cards.
a playing card suit with a black symbol

Examples

She played the ace of spades to win the trick.
If spades are trumps, you should lead with your strongest cards.
He had no spades left in his hand after the third round.

Synset Examples

she led a low spade
spades were trumps

Related Terms

  • Synonym: club - a playing card suit marked by a black trefoil symbol
  • Synonym: heart - a playing card suit marked by a red heart symbol
  • Synonym: diamond - a playing card suit marked by a red diamond symbol

Etymology

From Middle English spade (the suit), from Italian spada (‘sword’), reflecting the suit symbol.
