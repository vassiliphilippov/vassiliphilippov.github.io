cytidine

1. NOUN

/ ˈsaɪ.tɪˌdiːn / (AmE), / ˈsɪ.tɪ.diːn / (BrE)
a nucleoside formed by linking cytosine to a ribose sugar, found in RNA and DNA
a nucleoside in RNA and DNA
medical

Examples

Recent studies have shown that cytidine plays a critical role in RNA synthesis.
The scientist measured the levels of cytidine in the cell samples.
Supplementing with cytidine has been explored as a potential treatment for certain disorders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adenosine - a nucleoside formed from adenine and ribose
  • Synonym: thymidine - a nucleoside consisting of thymine and deoxyribose, found in DNA
  • Synonym: guanosine - a nucleoside formed from guanine and ribose

Etymology

From cytosine (a base) + -idine (a suffix for nucleosides).
