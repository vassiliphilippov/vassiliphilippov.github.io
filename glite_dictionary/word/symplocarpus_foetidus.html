Symplocarpus foetidus

1. NOUN

/ sɪm.pləˈkɑːr.pəs fiˈtɪd.əs / (AmE), / sɪm.pləˈkɑː.pəs fiˈtiːd.əs / (BrE)
A low-growing perennial swamp plant of eastern North America with a strong, unpleasant odor and small flowers enclosed in a mottled green or purple spathe.
North American swamp plant with unpleasant odor

Examples

Symplocarpus foetidus releases a pungent odor to attract pollinators in early spring.
Researchers studied the growth cycle of Symplocarpus foetidus in its natural wetland habitat.
The spathe of Symplocarpus foetidus is mottled green or purple and blooms before most other plants appear.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skunk cabbage - Common name for Symplocarpus foetidus, North American swamp plant with a strong odor

Etymology

From Greek symplōkḗ (connection) + karpós (fruit) and Latin foetidus (having a bad smell).
