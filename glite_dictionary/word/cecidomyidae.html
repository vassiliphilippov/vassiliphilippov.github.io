Cecidomyidae

1. NOUN

/ səˌsɪdəˈmɪɪdiː / (AmE), / sɛˌsɪdəʊˈmɪɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small flies commonly known as gall midges, which are known for causing abnormal growths (galls) on plants.
family of small flies (gall midges)

Examples

Cecidomyidae are a family of tiny flies that induce galls on plants.
Some species in the Cecidomyidae family can be serious agricultural pests.
Entomologists study Cecidomyidae to understand their impact on ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gall midge - small fly that causes galls on plants
  • Synonym: Cecidomyiidae - alternative, more common spelling for the same fly family

Etymology

From modern Latin Cecidomyidae, based on Greek 'kēkid-' (gall) and 'myia' (fly).
