calcination

1. NOUN

/ ˌkæl.sɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkæl.sɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of heating a substance to a high temperature to cause thermal decomposition or to convert metals into their oxides
heating a substance to form oxides

Examples

The calcination of limestone produces lime used in cement manufacturing.
During the extraction process, calcination helps remove volatile substances from the ore.
Careful control of temperature is essential in the calcination of minerals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: roasting - heating an ore in air to remove impurities or convert it to an oxide

Etymology

From Latin 'calcinatio', from 'calcinare' (to burn lime), from 'calx' (lime).
