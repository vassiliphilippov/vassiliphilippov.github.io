foreman

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɔr.mən / (AmE), / ˈfɔː.mən / (BrE)
A person who supervises and directs a group of workers, especially in a factory or on a building site.
supervisor of workers

Examples

The foreman assigned the tasks for the day before the shift started.
She discussed her safety concerns with the foreman on the construction site.
As a foreman, he ensures that all projects are completed on schedule.

Synset Examples

if you want to leave early you have to ask the foreman

Related Terms

  • Synonym: supervisor - person who oversees others' work

Etymology

From Middle English 'foreman' (leading man), from 'fore-' (before) + 'man'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɔrˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈfɔːmən / (BrE)
the member of a jury who is chosen to act as its leader and spokesperson
leader and spokesperson of a jury

Examples

The foreman delivered the jury's verdict in a clear and steady voice.
After a long deliberation, the foreman announced the decision to the court.
The judge asked the foreman if the jury had reached a unanimous verdict.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foreperson - gender-neutral term for the leader of a jury

Etymology

From 'fore-' (meaning 'before' or 'in front') + 'man'.
