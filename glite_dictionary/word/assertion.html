assertion

1. NOUN

/ əˈsɜrʃən / (AmE), / əˈsɜːʃən / (BrE)
the act of stating something confidently and forcefully as a fact
a confident statement or claim

Examples

Her assertion that she was innocent was supported by the evidence.
The scientist's assertion was later confirmed by further studies.
He made the assertion without offering any proof.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: claim - a statement that something is true, often without proof
  • Antonym: denial - the statement that something is not true

Etymology

From Latin assertio (a declaration, affirmation), from asserere (to claim, affirm).

2. NOUN

/ əˈsɝː.ʃən / (AmE), / əˈsɜː.ʃən / (BrE)
a confident and forceful statement of fact or belief, made without necessarily providing supporting evidence
confident statement or claim

Examples

She made the assertion that the project would fail without further funding.
His assertion that he had never met her was met with skepticism by the jury.
The article questions the assertion that technology always improves our lives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: claim - a statement that something is true, often without proof
  • Antonym: denial - a statement that something is not true

Etymology

From Latin 'assertio', from 'asserere' (to claim, to affirm).
