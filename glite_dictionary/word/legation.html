legation

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / lɪˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
a permanent diplomatic mission in a foreign country, headed by a minister rather than an ambassador
a diplomatic mission led by a minister

Examples

The country's legation in Paris was established in the 19th century.
He worked at the British legation before it was upgraded to an embassy.
Many smaller states maintain a legation rather than an embassy in some capitals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embassy - a permanent diplomatic mission headed by an ambassador

Etymology

From Latin 'legatio' (embassy, deputation), from 'legatus' (envoy, deputy).

2. NOUN

/ lɪˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / lɪˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
the official position or office of a legate, especially a diplomatic representative of the Pope or, in historical contexts, a lesser diplomatic mission than an embassy.
the office of a diplomatic representative or legate

Examples

He was appointed to the legation in Rome as the Pope's representative.
During the 19th century, many European countries maintained a legation rather than an embassy in smaller capitals.
The legation was responsible for negotiating treaties and maintaining diplomatic relations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: legate - an official representative, especially a diplomatic messenger or envoy

Etymology

From Latin 'legatio', meaning 'delegation' or 'office of a legate'.
