exoergic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛksoʊˈɜrdʒɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛksəʊˈɜːdʒɪk / (BrE)
(of a nuclear reaction) occurring with the release of energy.
releasing energy (nuclear/chemical reaction)

Examples

The fusion of deuterium and tritium is an exoergic nuclear reaction, producing substantial amounts of energy.
Scientists observed that the chemical process was exoergic, resulting in the spontaneous release of heat.
Unlike endoergic reactions, exoergic reactions can proceed without external energy input.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: endoergic - (of a nuclear reaction) occurring with absorption of energy

Etymology

From Greek 'exo' (outside) + 'ergon' (work).
