to refrigerate

1. VERB

/ rɪˈfrɪdʒəˌreɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈfrɪdʒəreɪt / (BrE)
to keep something at a low temperature, especially to preserve it from spoiling
keep something cold to preserve it

Examples

Raw meat should be refrigerated immediately to prevent it from going bad.
After you open the bottle, remember to refrigerate the juice to keep it fresh.
Many medications must be refrigerated to remain effective.

Synset Examples

many foods must be refrigerated or else they will spoil

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: freeze - to store at a temperature below the freezing point to preserve

Etymology

From Latin 'refrigerare', meaning 'to make cool'.

2. VERB

/ rɪˈfrɪdʒəˌreɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈfrɪdʒəreɪt / (BrE)
to make or keep something cold, especially by placing it in a refrigerator
to cool by using a refrigerator

Examples

You must refrigerate any leftovers within two hours to prevent spoilage.
After opening, please refrigerate the sauce to keep it fresh.
Some medications require you to refrigerate them after purchase.

Synset Examples

refrigerate this medicine

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chill - to make something cold, but not necessarily in a refrigerator
  • Antonym: heat - to make or become hot or warm

Etymology

From Latin 'refrigerare' (to make cool), from 're-' (again) + 'frigus' (cold).
