Lymantria

1. NOUN

/ lɪˈmæntɹiə / (AmE), / lɪˈman.tri.ə / (BrE)
A genus of moths in the family Lymantriidae, including several pests such as the gypsy moth.
genus of moths (pest species)

Examples

The Lymantria genus includes the destructive gypsy moth.
Researchers studied the spread of Lymantria in North American forests.
Some species of Lymantria are responsible for severe defoliation of trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gypsy moth - a species of moth (Lymantria dispar), notorious pest

Etymology

From Greek 'lymantria' meaning 'destroyer'.
