twitter

1. VERB

/ ˈtwɪtər / (AmE), / ˈtwɪtə / (BrE)
(of a bird) to make a series of short, high-pitched sounds; to chirp or trill.
make high-pitched bird sounds

Examples

The birds twitter merrily in the trees each morning.
Sparrows twittered on the fence as the sun rose.
Outside the window, dozens of swallows were twittering excitedly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chirp - to make a series of short, high-pitched sounds (as a bird or insect)
  • Synonym: warble - to sing softly and with a succession of constantly changing notes

Etymology

From Middle English 'twiteren' (to chirp, twitter), of imitative origin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtwɪdər / (AmE), / ˈtwɪtə / (BrE)
a series of light, rapid, high-pitched sounds, especially the chirping of small birds
high-pitched birds' chirping

Examples

We woke up to the gentle twitter of sparrows outside our window.
The forest was full of the twitter of birds at dawn.
A sudden twitter in the hedge made her stop and listen.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chirp - a short, sharp sound made by small birds or insects
  • Synonym: warble - a continuous, trilling or quavering sound made by a bird

Etymology

Imitative of the sound made by birds; Middle English 'twiteren'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07394177-n
Synset POS: n
