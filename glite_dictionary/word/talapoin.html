talapoin

1. NOUN

/ ˈtæləˌpɔɪn / (AmE), / ˈtaləˌpɔɪn / (BrE)
A small guenon monkey found in the swampy forests of central and western Africa, noted for being the smallest of the guenon monkeys.
small African guenon monkey

Examples

The talapoin is known for its playful nature and sociable groups in the wild.
A researcher observed a family of talapoins near the riverbank early in the morning.
Unlike larger monkeys, the talapoin prefers dense, swampy forests for its habitat.

Etymology

From Portuguese 'talapoin', of African origin, first recorded in the 18th century.
