mobile

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊˌbil / (AmE), / ˈməʊˌbiːl / (BrE)
a decorative structure made of balanced parts that move in response to air currents, typically suspended from above
decorative hanging structure that moves

Examples

The artist designed a beautiful mobile to hang above the baby's crib.
A gentle breeze caused the colored glass pieces of the mobile to spin and shimmer.
The museum features an enormous mobile suspended from the ceiling in the main hall.

Etymology

From French 'mobile' (capable of moving), from Latin 'mobilis'. Popularized for art objects by sculptor Alexander Calder in the 20th century.
