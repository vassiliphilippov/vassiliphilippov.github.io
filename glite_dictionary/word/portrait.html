portrait

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɔrtrət / (AmE), / ˈpɔːtrɪt / (BrE)
a detailed written or spoken description of a person's appearance and character
detailed description of a person in words

Examples

The author creates a vivid portrait of the main character through careful detail.
Her memoir offers a moving portrait of her family and their struggles.
The journalist painted a compelling portrait of the city’s vibrant culture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: depiction - a representation of someone or something in words or images
  • Degree Lower: caricature - a written or drawn representation that exaggerates features for comic effect

Etymology

From Old French 'portraire' (to portray), from Latin 'protrahere' (to draw forth).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɔrtrət / (AmE), / ˈpɔːtrɪt / (BrE)
a likeness of a person, especially one made by a painter, photographer, or other artist
artistic likeness of a person

Examples

She hung a beautiful portrait of her grandmother over the fireplace.
The artist is known for his expressive portraits of historical figures.
This portrait captures not just the appearance but the spirit of its subject.

Synset Examples

the photographer made excellent portraits

Related Terms

  • Antonym: landscape - a painting or photograph of natural scenery
  • Synonym: likeness - the quality of being alike; resemblance

Etymology

From Old French 'portraire' (to portray), from Latin 'portrahere' (to draw, depict).
