to Islamize

1. VERB

/ ˈɪsləˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɪsləˌmaɪz / (BrE)
to convert (a person, group, or region) to Islam, or to make Islamic in character or practice
convert to Islam; make Islamic

Examples

The government attempted to Islamize the education system in the 1980s.
Several regions were gradually Islamized through trade and migration.
Policies designed to Islamize the country have been a topic of debate.

Synset Examples

The Mughals Islamized much of Northern India in the 16th century

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to Christianize - to convert to Christianity
  • Antonym: to secularize - to make secular; remove religious influence

Etymology

From Islam + -ize (forming verbs meaning 'to make or become').

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00386356-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈɪzlɑːˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɪzlɑːmaɪz / (BrE)
to cause a person, group, or institution to conform to Islamic laws, principles, or customs
make conform to Islamic law or custom

Examples

The government sought to Islamize the education system by introducing more religious content.
In the 1980s, some leaders wanted to Islamize local banking practices.
There was debate over whether new laws would gradually Islamize the country's legal framework.

Synset Examples

Islamize the dietary laws

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to Christianize - to cause to conform to Christian laws or customs

Etymology

From Islam + -ize, following the pattern of verbs like Christianize.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00386923-v
Synset POS: v
