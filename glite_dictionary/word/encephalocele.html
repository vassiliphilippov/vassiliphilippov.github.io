encephalocele

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˈsɛf.ə.ləˌsil / (AmE), / ɛnˈsɛf.ə.ləˌsiːl / (BrE)
A congenital condition in which brain tissue protrudes through an opening in the skull.
congenital skull defect involving brain protrusion
medical

Examples

The newborn was diagnosed with an encephalocele shortly after birth.
Surgical intervention may be required to treat an encephalocele in infants.
Prenatal imaging revealed a possible encephalocele in the fetus.

Etymology

From Greek 'enkephalos' (brain) + 'kēlē' (hernia, swelling).
