myotic

1. ADJ

/ maɪˈɑːtɪk / (AmE), / maɪˈɒtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or causing the constriction of the pupil of the eye.
causing constriction of the pupil
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a myotic agent to reduce the patient's pupil size.
Pilocarpine is a common myotic used in ophthalmology.
Exposure to certain chemicals may have a myotic effect on the eyes.

Synset Examples

a miotic drug

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mydriatic - relating to or causing dilation of the pupil of the eye

Etymology

From Greek 'myein' (to close) and the suffix '-otic', relating to a condition or process.

2. NOUN

/ maɪˈɑtɪk / (AmE), / mʌɪˈɒtɪk / (BrE)
a drug that causes the pupil of the eye to constrict
drug causing pupil constriction
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a myotic to help treat the patient's glaucoma.
After applying the myotic, her pupils became noticeably smaller.
Certain myotics are used during eye examinations to control pupil size.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: miotic - drug causing constriction of the pupil

Etymology

From Greek 'myein' (to close the eyes) + '-otic' (relating to), by analogy with 'miosis'.
