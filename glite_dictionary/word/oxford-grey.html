Oxford-grey

1. ADJ

/ ˈɑksfərd ɡreɪ / (AmE), / ˈɒksfəd ɡreɪ / (BrE)
A dark shade of grey, typically associated with formal wear or uniforms.
a dark shade of grey

Examples

He wore an Oxford-grey suit to the interview, looking both stylish and professional.
The designer recommended Oxford-grey for the living room walls to create a sophisticated atmosphere.
Many school uniforms include Oxford-grey trousers as part of the dress code.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: grey - a color intermediate between black and white
  • Degree Higher: charcoal - a very dark shade of grey

Etymology

Named after Oxford, referencing the traditional colors used by the University of Oxford.
