Neohygrophorus angelesianus

1. NOUN

/ ˌniː.oʊ.haɪˌɡrɑːˈfɔːrəs ˌæn.dʒəˌliːˈzeɪ.ən.əs / (AmE), / ˌniː.əʊ.haɪˌɡrɒˈfɔː.rəs ˌæn.dʒəˌliːˈzeɪ.ən.əs / (BrE)
A species of fungus characterized by a small brown convex cap with a depressed center, waxy wine-colored gills, and a brown stalk; typically found fruiting in or near melting snow banks in the western mountains of North America.
a species of fungus found in western North America

Examples

The Neohygrophorus angelesianus is often discovered fruiting along melting snow banks in the Sierra Nevada.
Mycologists carefully documented the distinctive cap and gills of Neohygrophorus angelesianus during their survey.
The waxy, wine-colored gills make Neohygrophorus angelesianus easy to identify for experienced mushroom hunters.

Etymology

From Neo- (new), Hygrophorus (a genus of fungi), and angelesianus (referring to Los Angeles region where first described).
