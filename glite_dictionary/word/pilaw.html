pilaw

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈlɑ / (AmE), / pɪˈlɑː / (BrE)
a dish of rice cooked in seasoned broth, often with onions, vegetables, and sometimes meat, poultry, or seafood
seasoned rice dish, often with meat or seafood

Examples

My grandmother taught me how to make her famous chicken pilaw for special occasions.
The restaurant served a fragrant seafood pilaw garnished with fresh herbs.
She prefers pilaw over plain steamed rice because of its rich flavors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pilaf - a seasoned rice dish cooked in broth, sometimes with added meat or vegetables

Etymology

From Turkish 'pilav', which ultimately derives from Persian 'pilāw'.
