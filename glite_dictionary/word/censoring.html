censoring

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnsərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of removing or suppressing parts of books, films, letters, or other communication considered objectionable or sensitive
removal of sensitive or objectionable content

Examples

The government's censoring of the news sparked public outrage.
Many writers faced censoring during the political upheaval.
Parents groups called for the censoring of violent scenes in the film.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: censorship - the practice or system of censoring

Etymology

From the verb 'censor,' ultimately from Latin 'censere' (to assess, to judge).

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛnsərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of examining and removing or suppressing information thought to be objectionable or sensitive, especially for political or security reasons
removal or suppression of sensitive information

Examples

The government was accused of censoring reports about the protests to control public opinion.
Strict censoring of letters during wartime is intended to prevent valuable information from reaching the enemy.
Media censoring can make it difficult for people to know what is truly happening in their country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: censorship - the practice or policy of censoring information
  • Antonym: publication - the act of making information public

Etymology

From Latin 'censere' (to assess), via 'censor' (an official who examines material).
