anglicisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌæŋɡlɪsɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌæŋɡlɪsaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something English in form, style, or character
process of making English in style

Examples

The anglicisation of local names was common during the colonial era.
Many foods underwent anglicisation when brought to Britain, changing flavors and ingredients.
The anglicisation of words from other languages can alter their spelling and pronunciation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to anglicize - to make English in form or character

Etymology

From 'Anglicise' + '-ation', based on Latin roots meaning 'to make English'.
