gosan-chiku

1. NOUN

/ ˌgoʊsɑnˈtʃiːku / (AmE), / ˌgəʊsænˈtʃiːkuː / (BrE)
A small bamboo of southeastern China, known for its slender, flexible stems when young.
small, flexible bamboo of southeastern China

Examples

The gosan-chiku planted in the garden swayed gracefully in the spring breeze.
She specializes in rare Asian plants, including gosan-chiku and other ornamental bamboos.
Many landscapers admire the elegant appearance of gosan-chiku in traditional Chinese gardens.

Etymology

From Japanese 'gosan-chiku', referring to a type of bamboo.
