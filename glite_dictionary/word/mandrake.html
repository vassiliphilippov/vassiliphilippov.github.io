mandrake

1. NOUN

/ ˈmænˌdreɪk / (AmE), / ˈmæn.dreɪk / (BrE)
a plant of the nightshade family, native to southern Europe and North Africa, having purple or white flowers, yellow berries, and a forked root, traditionally thought to have magical powers
a plant with forked root and mythic powers

Examples

In ancient folklore, the mandrake was believed to scream when uprooted from the ground.
The wizard used a dried mandrake root in his potion.
Botanists have studied the properties of the Mediterranean mandrake for centuries.

Etymology

From Middle English 'mandragora', from Latin 'mandragora', from Greek 'mandragoras'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmænˌdreɪk / (AmE), / ˈmæn.dreɪk / (BrE)
the root of the mandrake plant, traditionally used in medicine or as a narcotic and often associated with folklore
root of the mandrake plant, used medicinally

Examples

Ancient herbalists prized the mandrake for its supposed magical and healing properties.
The legend says that pulling a mandrake from the ground would cause it to scream.
Some traditional medicines still use extracts from the mandrake root.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mandragora - alternative name for the mandrake plant

Etymology

From Middle English 'mandragora', from Latin 'mandragora', from Greek 'mandragoras'.
