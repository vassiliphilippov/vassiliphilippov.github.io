removed

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈmuvd / (AmE), / rɪˈmuːvd / (BrE)
separate or apart in time, space, or relation; distant from something in time or connection
separate or distant in time, relation, etc.

Examples

The era of steam engines feels increasingly removed from our daily lives.
His style of painting seems quite removed from modern trends.
We're talking about issues that are very removed from our current concerns.

Synset Examples

distant events
the remote past or future

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remote - distant in time or space
  • Antonym: close - near in time or relation

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈmuːvd / (AmE), / rɪˈmuːvd / (BrE)
(of a relative) separated by a specified number of generations in degree of descent from a common ancestor.
separated by generations in kinship

Examples

My father has a cousin who is my first cousin once removed.
Their relationship is distant; they are second cousins twice removed.
Aunt Mary is actually my great-aunt, so she is also considered removed in family terms.

Synset Examples

a cousin once removed

Related Terms

  • Antonym: related - connected by familial relationship

Etymology

From remove, in the sense of distance or separation, from Latin remotus (removed, distant).
