cackle

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæk.əl / (AmE), / ˈkæk.əl / (BrE)
the loud, sharp sound made by a hen after laying an egg or by a person laughing in a high-pitched manner
sound made by a hen or harsh laughter

Examples

We heard the hen's cackle echoing through the barnyard after she laid her egg.
A farmer can often tell when a hen has just laid an egg by her distinctive cackle.
The morning was quiet except for the occasional cackle from the chicken coop.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cluck - short, abrupt sound made by a hen
  • Synonym: laugh - to make sounds with the voice that show amusement

Etymology

Probably imitative in origin, reflecting the sound itself.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07393037-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈkækəl / (AmE), / ˈkækəl / (BrE)
a loud, harsh laugh that resembles the sound made by a hen
harsh, hen-like laugh

Examples

Her cackle echoed throughout the room, startling everyone nearby.
The movie villain's evil cackle sent shivers down my spine.
After hearing the joke, he burst into a loud cackle that drew attention.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: giggle - a light, silly laugh
  • Degree Lower: snicker - a sly or suppressed laugh

Etymology

From Middle English cakelen, imitative of a hen's cry.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07142159-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ˈkækəl / (AmE), / ˈkækəl / (BrE)
to laugh in a loud, unpleasant, or shrill manner, often suggestive of amusement or mockery
laugh loudly and unpleasantly

Examples

The witch began to cackle as she stirred her bubbling potion.
He couldn't help but cackle at his friend's terrible joke.
The old woman cackled with delight as she recounted the prank.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guffaw - to laugh loudly and boisterously
  • Degree Lower: chuckle - to laugh quietly or to oneself

Etymology

Middle English *cakelen*, of imitative origin.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

/ ˈkækəl / (AmE), / ˈkækəl / (BrE)
(of a hen) to make a shrill, loud, repeated sound; to squawk in a way characteristic of hens.

Examples

Every morning, I hear the chickens cackling in the backyard as they lay eggs.
After laying an egg, the old hen proudly cackled for several minutes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cluck - make a short, guttural sound (of hens)

Etymology

From Middle English ‘cackelen’, of imitative origin, referring to the sound made by hens.

6. NOUN

/ ˈkækəl / (AmE), / ˈkækəl / (BrE)
loud, harsh, and unpleasant talk or laughter, often from a group of people.
noisy, harsh talk or laughter

Examples

The room was filled with the cackle of gossipy neighbors.
Amid the cackle of students, the teacher struggled to regain their attention.
Their cackle echoed down the hallway, making it hard to concentrate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chatter - rapid and trivial talk, often meaningless
  • Synonym: babble - continuous, foolish, or incoherent talk

Etymology

From Middle English 'cakelen', from Old English 'cacian' (to cackle, chatter), imitative of the sound.
