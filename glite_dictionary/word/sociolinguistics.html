sociolinguistics

1. NOUN

/ ˌsoʊsiəˌlɪŋˈɡwɪstɪks / (AmE), / ˌsəʊsiəʊlɪŋˈɡwɪstɪks / (BrE)
the study of how language is influenced by social and cultural factors
study of language in social context

Examples

She decided to specialize in sociolinguistics after taking an introductory linguistics course.
Sociolinguistics examines how language varies between different social groups.
The professor's research in sociolinguistics focuses on dialects in urban communities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: linguistics - the scientific study of language

Etymology

From 'socio-', relating to society, and 'linguistics', the scientific study of language.
