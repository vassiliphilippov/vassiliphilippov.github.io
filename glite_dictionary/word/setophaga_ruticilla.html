Setophaga ruticilla

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛtəˈfɑːɡə ˌruːtɪˈsɪlə / (AmE), / ˌsɛtəˈfəʊɡə ˌruːtɪˈsɪlə / (BrE)
A species of flycatching warbler native to eastern North America, known as the American redstart, with males displaying bright orange patches on the sides, wings, and tail.
species of North American warbler, American redstart

Examples

During migration, the Setophaga ruticilla can be seen flitting through forest canopies.
Birdwatchers eagerly spotted a male Setophaga ruticilla displaying its vivid orange markings.
Researchers are studying the breeding patterns of Setophaga ruticilla in northern regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: American redstart - common name for Setophaga ruticilla

Etymology

From Latin roots: 'Setophaga' meaning 'moth-eating', and 'ruticilla' meaning 'red-tailed'.
