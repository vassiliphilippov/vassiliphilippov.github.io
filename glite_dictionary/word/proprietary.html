proprietary

1. ADJ

/ prəˈpraɪəˌtɛri / (AmE), / prəˈprʌɪət(ə)ri / (BrE)
(of a product) protected by a trademark, patent, or copyright, and made or sold exclusively by the owner of those rights.
protected by trademark or exclusive rights

Examples

Many medicines are sold under both proprietary and generic names.
The company decided to keep its software proprietary rather than release it as open source.
He was sued for using a proprietary formula without permission.

Synset Examples

‘Tylenol’ is a proprietary drug of which ‘acetaminophen’ is the generic form

Related Terms

  • Antonym: generic - not protected by trademark or exclusive rights; referring to nonproprietary forms

Etymology

From Latin proprietarius, from proprietas (ownership, property).
