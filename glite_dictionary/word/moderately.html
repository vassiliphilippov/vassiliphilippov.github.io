moderately

1. ADV

/ ˈmɑdɚətli / (AmE), / ˈmɒdərətli / (BrE)
to a certain extent or degree; not very or extremely, but more than slightly.
to a certain extent; not extremely

Examples

The weather was moderately warm, making it perfect for a picnic.
She was moderately impressed with the presentation, though she saw room for improvement.
The hotel is moderately priced, making it affordable for most travelers.

Synset Examples

pretty big
pretty bad
jolly decent of him
the shoes are priced reasonably
he is fairly clever with computers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: somewhat - to a small degree or extent
  • Antonym: extremely - to a very great degree

Etymology

From Late Latin moderatus (within bounds), from Latin moderari (to moderate).

2. ADV

/ ˈmɑː.dɚ.ət.li / (AmE), / ˈmɒd.ər.ət.li / (BrE)
To a moderate degree; without excess; in a way that is neither too much nor too little.
to a moderate degree

Examples

He tries to eat moderately to stay healthy.
Their house is moderately priced compared to others in the neighborhood.
The weather was moderately warm for this time of year.

Synset Examples

he drinks moderately

Related Terms

  • Antonym: excessively - to a much greater degree than is necessary
  • Degree Lower: slightly - to a small degree; a little
  • Degree Higher: greatly - to a very large extent or degree

Etymology

From 'moderate' + '-ly', where 'moderate' comes from Latin *moderatus* (within bounds, restrained).
