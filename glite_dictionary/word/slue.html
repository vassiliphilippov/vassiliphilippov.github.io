to slue

1. VERB

/ sluː / (AmE), / sluː / (BrE)
to turn sharply or change direction suddenly, especially by turning around a fixed point
to turn sharply or change direction suddenly

Examples

The car began to slue sideways on the icy road.
She slued the chair around to face the window.
The ship's bow suddenly slued to starboard during the storm.

Synset Examples

The car cut to the left at the intersection
The motorbike veered to the right

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to veer - to change direction suddenly
  • Synonym: swerve - to turn aside abruptly from a straight course

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly a variant of 'slew' (past tense of 'slay'), or influenced by nautical slang.

2. VERB

/ sluː / (AmE), / sluː / (BrE)
to turn, veer, or slide sideways or obliquely, often suddenly or in an uncontrolled manner
turn or slide sideways uncontrollably

Examples

The car began to slue across the icy road after hitting a patch of black ice.
The trailer suddenly slued to the left, nearly tipping over.
As the ship rocked in the heavy storm, the deck chairs slued from side to side.

Synset Examples

the wheels skidded against the sidewalk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skid - slide uncontrollably, usually sideways
  • Synonym: swerve - suddenly change direction, especially to avoid something

Etymology

Perhaps a variant of 'slew', possibly from Middle English 'slouwen' (to slip, slide).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01874268-v
Synset POS: v
