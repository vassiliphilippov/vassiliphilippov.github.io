Dentaria diphylla

1. NOUN

/ dɛnˈtɛəriə ˈdaɪfɪlə / (AmE), / dɛnˈtɛəriə ˈdɪfɪlə / (BrE)
A North American herb with pungent, scaly or toothed roots, also known as two-leaved toothwort.
North American herb with scaly roots

Examples

Dentaria diphylla grows wild in moist woodlands across eastern North America.
Indigenous peoples historically used Dentaria diphylla for medicinal purposes.
The pungent roots of Dentaria diphylla are a distinctive feature of the species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toothwort - common name for plants in the same genus

Etymology

From Latin: 'Dentaria' (toothed, referring to the root), 'diphylla' (two-leaved).
