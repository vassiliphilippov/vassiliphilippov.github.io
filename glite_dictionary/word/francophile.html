Francophile

1. NOUN

/ ˈfrænsəˌfaɪl / (AmE), / ˈfrænsəˌfaɪl / (BrE)
A person who admires France, its people, culture, or customs.
person who admires France or French culture

Examples

As a lifelong Francophile, she spends every summer in Paris.
The museum's director is a noted Francophile and often hosts French artists.
Because I'm a Francophile, my bookshelves are filled with French literature.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Francophobe - a person who dislikes France or French people

Etymology

From French 'francophile', from 'France' + '-phile' (lover of).
