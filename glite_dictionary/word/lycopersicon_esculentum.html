Lycopersicon esculentum

1. NOUN

/ laɪˌkɑːpərˈsaɪkən ˌɛskjuːˈlɛntəm / (AmE), / lɪˌkɒpəˈsaɪkən ˌɛskjʊˈlɛntəm / (BrE)
the scientific name for the domesticated tomato plant, widely grown for its edible fruit
the domesticated tomato plant

Examples

The fruit of Lycopersicon esculentum is commonly used in salads and sauces.
Botanists studied the disease resistance of several Lycopersicon esculentum varieties.
Greenhouses often cultivate Lycopersicon esculentum for year-round tomato production.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Solanum lycopersicum - current scientific name for the tomato plant

Etymology

From Greek 'lykopersikon' (wolf peach) and Latin 'esculentum' (edible).
