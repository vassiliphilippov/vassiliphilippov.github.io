minocycline

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪnoʊˈsaɪkliːn / (AmE), / ˌmɪnəˈsɪkliːn / (BrE)
An antibiotic in the tetracycline family, used to treat various bacterial and rickettsial infections.
tetracycline antibiotic drug
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed minocycline to treat her skin infection.
Some patients experience side effects while taking minocycline for acne.
Your pharmacist will explain how to take minocycline safely and effectively.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tetracycline - an antibiotic group to which minocycline belongs
  • Synonym: doxycycline - a related tetracycline antibiotic drug

Etymology

Derived from the 'mino-' prefix (of uncertain origin) added to 'tetracycline', the drug class.
