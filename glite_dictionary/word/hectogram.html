hectogram

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛktəˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈhɛktəˌɡram / (BrE)
a metric unit of mass equal to 100 grams
metric unit of 100 grams

Examples

The package contains exactly one hectogram of cheese.
Recipe instructions called for a hectogram of flour to be added to the mixture.
Scientists measured the specimen’s weight as half a hectogram.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: gram - a metric unit of mass equal to one thousandth of a kilogram
  • Degree Higher: kilogram - a metric unit of mass equal to 1,000 grams

Etymology

From French 'hectogramme,' from Greek 'hekaton' (hundred) + French 'gramme' (gram).
