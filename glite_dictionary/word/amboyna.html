amboyna

1. NOUN

/ æmˈbɔɪ.nə / (AmE), / æmˈbɔɪ.nə / (BrE)
A tree native to Southeast Asia, known for its reddish wood with a mottled or striped black grain, valued for decorative woodworking.
reddish tropical wood or tree

Examples

The craftsman selected a piece of amboyna to make the luxurious desktop veneer.
Collectors prize antique boxes made from amboyna for their rich color and striking grain patterns.
The furniture's amboyna panels gleamed in the sunlight, showing off their beautiful striations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burl - wood with a unique grain, often used decoratively

Etymology

From Ambon, an island in Indonesia, from which the wood was originally exported.

2. NOUN

/ æmˈbɔɪnə / (AmE), / æmˈbɔɪnə / (BrE)
A mottled, curly-grained wood from the Pterocarpus indicus tree, valued for decorative work in fine furniture and veneers.
decorative wood from Pterocarpus indicus

Examples

The antique desk was made of rare amboyna, which gave it a richly patterned surface.
Artisans often choose amboyna for luxury inlays and veneers due to its beautiful grain.
He admired the polished amboyna panels on the cabinet in the gallery.

Etymology

From Ambon, an island in Indonesia, where the wood was first exported.
