ensign

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.saɪn / (AmE), / ˈɛn.sɪn / (BrE)
A flag or emblem flown as a symbol of nationality, especially on a ship.
national flag or emblem

Examples

The ship proudly displayed the national ensign as it entered the harbor.
Each country's ensign was raised during the opening ceremony.
The royal navy changed its ensign after the nation became a republic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flag - a piece of cloth with a distinctive design used as a symbol or signal

Etymology

From Latin 'insignia' (mark, emblem).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.saɪn / (AmE), / ˈɛn.saɪn / (BrE)
a flag or standard, especially one flown on a ship to indicate its nationality
national flag flown by a ship

Examples

The ship raised its ensign as it entered the harbor.
Every British naval vessel must fly the White Ensign.
The crew saluted the ensign before lowering it at sunset.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flag - a piece of cloth with a distinctive design used as a symbol, signalling device, or decoration

Etymology

From Latin 'insignia' (badge, mark), through Old French 'enseigne'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.saɪn / (AmE), / ˈɛn.sɪn / (BrE)
an officer holding the lowest commissioned rank in the United States Navy or Coast Guard, ranking below lieutenant junior grade
junior officer rank in US Navy/Coast Guard

Examples

She became an ensign after graduating from the Naval Academy.
The ensign was assigned to his first shipboard posting this year.
As an ensign, he carried out orders from higher-ranking officers.

Etymology

From Latin 'insignia' (badge, mark), via Old French 'enseigne'; adapted in English as a military title.
