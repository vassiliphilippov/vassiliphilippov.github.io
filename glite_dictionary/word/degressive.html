degressive

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈɡrɛsɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈɡrɛsɪv / (BrE)
(of taxes) decreasing in rate as the taxable amount increases, especially below a certain threshold.
tax rate decreasing with increasing amount

Examples

A degressive tax system imposes lower rates on higher amounts within a specified range.
The new legislation introduced a degressive scale of charges for smaller businesses.
The subsidy was granted on a degressive basis, reducing as income increased.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: progressive - (of taxes) increasing in rate as the taxable amount increases

Etymology

From French 'dégressif', from Latin 'degress-', stem of 'degradi', meaning to step down.

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈɡrɛsɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈɡrɛsɪv / (BrE)
(of a process, rate, or amount) decreasing by stages or in steps, especially at a diminishing rate.
decreasing by stages or steps

Examples

The company applied a degressive tax rate, so larger incomes were taxed incrementally less.
Certain subsidies are granted on a degressive basis, decreasing over time.
The degressive pattern of the scale means that reductions are made at each step.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: progressive - increasing or advancing by stages

Etymology

From French *dégressif*, from Latin *degressus* (to step down).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02495724-s
Synset POS: s
