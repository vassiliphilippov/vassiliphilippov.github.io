contriteness

1. NOUN

/ kənˈtraɪtnəs / (AmE), / kənˈtraɪtnəs / (BrE)
the state of feeling deep regret and sorrow for wrongdoing or sin, often motivated by the fear of punishment
deep regret or sorrow for wrongdoing

Examples

The look of contriteness on his face made it clear he understood the gravity of his mistake.
Her contriteness was evident in the heartfelt letter of apology she sent to everyone affected.
He spoke with genuine contriteness, confessing his errors and asking for forgiveness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remorse - a deep feeling of regret or guilt for a wrong committed
  • Antonym: impenitence - lack of regret or sorrow for one's actions

Etymology

From Latin 'contritus' (worn out, ground to pieces), past participle of 'conterere' (to grind down, wear away).
