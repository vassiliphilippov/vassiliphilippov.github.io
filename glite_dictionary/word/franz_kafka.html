Franz Kafka

1. PROPN

/ frænz ˈkɑfə / (AmE), / frants ˈkafkə / (BrE)
(1883–1924) A Czech novelist and short story writer who wrote in German, known for works depicting surreal, nightmarish situations and isolated individuals.
Czech novelist and writer of existential fiction

Examples

Many readers find Franz Kafka's works both disturbing and thought-provoking.
The surreal, bureaucratic nightmare described by Franz Kafka has influenced countless authors.
Students in the literature class discussed the existential themes found in Franz Kafka's novels.
