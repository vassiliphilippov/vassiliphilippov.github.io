milord

1. NOUN

/ miˈlɔrd / (AmE), / mɪˈlɔːd / (BrE)
A respectful form of address for an English lord, especially used historically or in literary contexts.
respectful address for an English lord

Examples

The butler greeted his master with a formal bow, saying, "Good evening, milord."
In the grand hall, all eyes turned as milord entered accompanied by his guests.
She never referred to him by name, always calling him milord out of respect.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: my lord - polite form of address for a lord

Etymology

From French 'milord', from English 'my lord'.
