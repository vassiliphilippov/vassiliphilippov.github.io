to refurbish

1. VERB

/ riˈfɝː.bɪʃ / (AmE), / rɪˈfɜː.bɪʃ / (BrE)
to make (something, especially a building or furniture) clean, bright, or like new again by repairing or decorating it
to renovate or redecorate

Examples

They decided to refurbish the old cottage before moving in.
The company spent millions to refurbish its headquarters after the fire.
She plans to refurbish the antique table by sanding and repainting it.

Synset Examples

we refurbished the guest wing
My wife wants us to renovate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to renovate - to restore something to a good state of repair
  • Synonym: to restore - to bring back to original condition

Etymology

From re- (again) + furnish, from Old French *furnir* (to furnish or equip).
