to jaywalk

1. VERB

/ ˈdʒeɪˌwɑk / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪˌwɔːk / (BrE)
to cross a street or road illegally or recklessly, often by ignoring traffic lights or not using a pedestrian crossing
cross a street illegally or dangerously

Examples

It's dangerous to jaywalk in busy city traffic, so always use the crosswalk.
She was fined for jaywalking across the main road without waiting for the light to change.
While running late, he decided to jaywalk instead of walking to the corner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cross - to go from one side of something to the other

Etymology

From American English 'jay' (naive person) + 'walk', originally implying the person was inexperienced in city traffic.
