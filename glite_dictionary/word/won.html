won

1. NOUN

/ wʌn / (AmE), / wɒn / (BrE)
The basic monetary unit of North Korea, equal to 100 chon.
currency of North Korea

Examples

North Korean shops primarily accept payment in won rather than foreign currencies.
The exchange rate for the North Korean won can fluctuate significantly on the black market.
Travelers should be aware that importing or exporting won is strictly regulated.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: won - the basic unit of money in South Korea

Etymology

From Korean 원 (won), meaning 'circle' or 'round', modeled after the Chinese character 圓 (yuan).

2. ADJ

/ wʌn / (AmE), / wʌn / (BrE)
(of a contest, game, or situation) certain of victory; secured and no longer subject to loss or defeat.
certain of victory; cannot be lost

Examples

After scoring that goal, it was a won match for their team.
She relaxed once she realized it was a won battle.
With all the key moves played, it’s essentially a won game for us now.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lost - unable to be won or recovered

Examples

She exchanged her dollars for won at the airport currency counter.
South Korea's won has appreciated against the US dollar this year.
