acclivitous

1. ADJ

/ əˈklɪvɪtəs / (AmE), / əˈklɪvɪtəs / (BrE)
sloping upward; rising or ascending in an upward direction
sloping upward

Examples

The acclivitous path made the hike more strenuous toward the summit.
Vines clung to the acclivitous sides of the valley.
The road was so acclivitous that cyclists struggled to maintain their pace.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: declivitous - sloping downward

Etymology

From Latin 'acclivitas' (an upward slope), from 'acclivis' (sloping upward).
