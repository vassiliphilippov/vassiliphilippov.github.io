misfortunate

1. ADJ

/ mɪsˈfɔɹ.tʃə.nət / (AmE), / mɪsˈfɔː.tjʊ.nət / (BrE)
deserving or arousing pity, especially due to bad luck or unfortunate circumstances; pitiable.
deserving pity due to bad luck

Examples

The misfortunate child was left alone after the disaster struck the village.
Many felt compelled to help the misfortunate victims of the flood.
Stories of misfortunate characters often evoke deep empathy from readers.

Synset Examples

a hapless victim
miserable victims of war
the shabby room struck her as extraordinarily pathetic
piteous appeals for help
pitiable homeless children
a pitiful fate
Oh, you poor thing
his poor distorted limbs
a wretched life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unfortunate - suffering from bad luck; unlucky, pitiable
  • Antonym: fortunate - lucky, experiencing good fortune
  • Synonym: wretched - extremely unhappy or in a bad situation

Etymology

From 'misfortune' (bad luck) + '-ate,' modeled after 'fortunate.'
