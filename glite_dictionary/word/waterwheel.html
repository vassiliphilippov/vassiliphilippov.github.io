waterwheel

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɔtərˌwiːl / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəˌwiːl / (BrE)
A large wheel driven by flowing or falling water, used especially in the past for supplying power to a machine or mill.
wheel powered by water for driving machinery

Examples

The old mill still uses a waterwheel to grind grain, just as it did centuries ago.
Engineers studied the ancient waterwheel to design more efficient hydroelectric turbines.
Visitors admired the large waterwheel turning slowly at the edge of the river.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: turbine - a machine for producing continuous power in which a wheel or rotor is made to revolve by a fast-moving flow of water, steam, gas, air, or other fluid

Etymology

From Middle English waterwhele, from Old English wæter (water) + hwēol (wheel).

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɔt̬ɚˌwiːl / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəˌwiːl / (BrE)
a large wheel with buckets or paddles around its rim, driven by water and used to raise water from a stream, pond, or river, often for irrigation or powering machinery
wheel for raising or using water power

Examples

The old mill still had a waterwheel powered by the nearby river.
Farmers once relied on the waterwheel to lift water for their crops.
Tourists admired the ancient waterwheel turning slowly in the afternoon sun.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water mill - a mill powered by a waterwheel

Etymology

Formed from 'water' + 'wheel'; refers to a wheel powered by water.
