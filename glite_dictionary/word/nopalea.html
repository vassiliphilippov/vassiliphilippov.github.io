Nopalea

1. NOUN

/ noʊˈpɑːliə / (AmE), / nəʊˈpaliə / (BrE)
A genus of cactus native to Mexico and Central America, known for its scarlet or reddish flowers.
genus of cacti with scarlet flowers

Examples

The Nopalea species are prized for their striking scarlet blooms in desert gardens.
Botanists studied the Nopalea to better understand how some cacti adapt to arid environments.
Some varieties of Nopalea are cultivated for ornamental use and their edible pads.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Opuntia - another genus of cacti related to Nopalea

Etymology

From Latinized form of Nahuatl 'nopalli' (meaning prickly pear).
