banjo

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæn.dʒoʊ / (AmE), / ˈban.dʒəʊ / (BrE)
a stringed musical instrument with a long neck and a circular body, typically played by plucking or strumming
stringed instrument with circular body

Examples

He entertained the crowd with lively tunes on his banjo.
She taught herself to play the banjo after years of guitar practice.
A bluegrass band usually features a banjo as one of its main instruments.

Etymology

Probably from Portuguese 'bandolim' (a kind of stringed instrument) or from an African origin.
