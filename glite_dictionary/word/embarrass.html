to embarrass

1. VERB

/ ɪmˈbær.əs / (AmE), / ɪmˈbær.əs / (BrE)
to make (someone) feel awkward, self-conscious, or ashamed
make someone feel awkward or self-conscious

Examples

He didn't mean to embarrass her with his comment, but she blushed anyway.
Try not to embarrass your friends when you introduce them at the party.
The mistake in his report could embarrass him in front of his boss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to humiliate - to make someone feel very ashamed or foolish
  • Antonym: comfort - to make someone feel less embarrassed or upset

Etymology

From French 'embarrasser', from Italian 'imbarazzare', ultimately from Spanish 'embarazar' (to hinder).
