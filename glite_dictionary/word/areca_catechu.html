Areca catechu

1. NOUN

/ əˈriːkə kəˈtiːku / (AmE), / əˈriːkə kəˈtiːkjuː / (BrE)
A species of palm tree native to Southeast Asia, whose orange or scarlet fruit, known as betel nut, is chewed in many cultures.
Southeast Asian palm tree; betel nut palm

Examples

The Areca catechu is widely cultivated throughout Southeast Asia for its valuable fruit.
Research has shown that Areca catechu contains important alkaloids.
Many traditional ceremonies incorporate the nuts harvested from Areca catechu.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: betel nut - the seed of Areca catechu, chewed in many Asian cultures

Etymology

From genus Areca and species epithet catechu, both of which are New Latin terms used in botanical classification.
