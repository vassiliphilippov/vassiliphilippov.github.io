bolster

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊl.stɚ / (AmE), / ˈbəʊl.stə / (BrE)
A long, firm pillow, usually placed across a bed underneath the regular pillows for support or decoration.
long pillow for bed support or decoration

Examples

She arranged the bolster beneath the pillows to make the bed look more inviting.
Many traditional beds feature a cylindrical bolster for added comfort and style.
The hotel supplied an extra bolster for guests who preferred firmer support.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pillow - a cushion used to support the head while sleeping

Etymology

From Old English bolster (a long cushion or pillow), related to Old Norse bolstr, German Polster.
