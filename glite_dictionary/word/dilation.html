dilation

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / daɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making an opening or aperture larger or wider
act of expanding an aperture
medical

Examples

Pupil dilation occurs in response to low light conditions.
The doctor noted the dilation of the patient's bronchi during the examination.
Certain medications can cause dilation of blood vessels, reducing blood pressure.

Synset Examples

the dilation of the pupil of the eye

Related Terms

  • Antonym: constriction - the act of making narrower or smaller
  • Synonym: expansion - the process of becoming larger or more extensive

Etymology

From Latin dilatatio, from dilatare (to spread out, enlarge).

2. NOUN

/ daɪˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / daɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a lengthy spoken or written discussion on a particular topic, often formal or detailed
lengthy discussion on a topic
archaic

Examples

The philosopher's dilation on morality lasted well into the evening.
Her novel contains a fascinating dilation on the nature of forgiveness.
The speech began as a simple greeting but turned into a lengthy dilation about the country's history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discourse - a formal and orderly expression of thought on a subject
  • Synonym: treatise - a formal written work about a particular subject
  • Antonym: summary - a brief statement of the main points

Etymology

From Latin 'dilatio' (delay, expansion), via Old French and Late Latin.
