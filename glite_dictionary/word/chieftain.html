chieftain

1. NOUN

/ ˈtʃiːf.tən / (AmE), / ˈtʃiːf.tɪn / (BrE)
the leader or head of a tribe or clan
leader of a tribe or clan

Examples

The chieftain addressed his people with pride and authority during the ceremony.
Each clan had its own chieftain who was responsible for maintaining peace and order.
When the old chieftain died, his eldest son was chosen to succeed him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chief - leader or head of a group or organization

Etymology

From Old French 'chevetain', from Late Latin 'capitaneus', meaning chief or leader.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtʃiːf.tən / (AmE), / ˈtʃiːf.tɪn / (BrE)
the leader of a tribe, clan, or group of people, especially in traditional societies
leader of a group or tribe

Examples

The chieftain welcomed the visitors into the village with a grand ceremony.
After the war, the chieftain worked to unite the rival clans under his rule.
Legend has it that the ancient chieftain still watches over the valley.

Synset Examples

a captain of industry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chief - the person with the highest rank or authority

Etymology

From Old French "chevetain", based on Latin "capitaneus" (chief, leader).
