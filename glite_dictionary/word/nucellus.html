nucellus

1. NOUN

/ nuːˈsɛləs / (AmE), / njuːˈsɛləs / (BrE)
the central part of a plant ovule, containing the embryo sac
central part of a plant ovule

Examples

The nucellus surrounds and nourishes the developing embryo sac within the ovule.
In some plants, the nucellus remains prominent even after fertilization.
Scientists studied the structure of the nucellus to better understand seed development.

Etymology

From Latin *nucellus* (little nut), diminutive of *nux* (nut).
