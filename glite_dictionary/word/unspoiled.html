unspoiled

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈspɔɪld / (AmE), / ʌnˈspɔɪld / (BrE)
not having been spoiled, decayed, or damaged; remaining in a pure or original condition.
not spoiled or decayed

Examples

The fruit looked unspoiled despite being picked days ago.
She prefers to cook with unspoiled ingredients for the best flavor.
The meat was stored properly and remained unspoiled until dinner.

Synset Examples

the meat is still good

Related Terms

  • Antonym: spoiled - having been ruined, decayed, or deteriorated

Etymology

From the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'spoiled', past participle of 'spoil'.

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈspɔɪld / (AmE), / ʌnˈspɔɪld / (BrE)
(of something) not decayed, decomposed, or otherwise damaged; still in its original, undeteriorated condition.
not decayed or decomposed

Examples

The fruit was surprisingly unspoiled even after a week in the pantry.
We found an unspoiled loaf of bread at the back of the cupboard.
Despite the humidity, the cheese remained unspoiled for several days.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: spoiled - decayed or decomposed; damaged
  • Synonym: fresh - recently obtained; not decayed or spoiled

Etymology

Formed from 'un-' (not) and 'spoiled', from Old French 'espoillier' (to strip, ruin).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00625935-s
Synset POS: s
