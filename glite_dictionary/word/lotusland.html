lotusland

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊ.t̬əs.lænd / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.təs.lænd / (BrE)
a place or state of perfect contentment, often marked by self-indulgence and a disregard for practical concerns
an idyllic, self-indulgent place or state

Examples

After retiring, he moved to a coastal lotusland where worries seemed to melt away.
They accused him of living in a lotusland, ignoring the difficult realities around him.
The island was described as a modern lotusland, attracting those in search of leisure and pleasure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paradise - a place or state of perfect happiness
  • Synonym: utopia - an imagined perfect place or state of things

Etymology

Alludes to the 'land of the lotus-eaters' in Homer’s Odyssey, where people lived in languorous contentment after eating lotus fruit.
