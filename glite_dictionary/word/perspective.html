perspective

1. NOUN

/ pərˈspɛktɪv / (AmE), / pəˈspɛktɪv / (BrE)
a particular way of thinking about or considering something, especially one that is influenced by your beliefs or experiences
way of thinking about something

Examples

It's important to consider different perspectives when making a decision.
From my perspective, the project was a great success.
Travel can help you gain a new perspective on life.

Synset Examples

consider what follows from the positivist view

Related Terms

  • Synonym: viewpoint - a person's opinion or way of thinking about something
  • Synonym: standpoint - an attitude or position from which something is considered
  • Synonym: angle - a particular way of approaching or considering an issue

Etymology

From Latin perspectivus (of sight, optical), from perspicere (to look through).

2. NOUN

/ pərˈspɛktɪv / (AmE), / pəˈspɛktɪv / (BrE)
the way objects appear to the observer based on their position and distance in space, especially as represented in a drawing or painting
appearance of objects in relation to distance

Examples

The artist used perspective to create a sense of depth in the painting.
From this vantage point, the perspective makes the buildings seem much smaller.
Learning the rules of perspective helps illustrators draw more realistic scenes.

Etymology

From Latin 'perspectiva' (science of optics), from 'perspicere' (to look through).
