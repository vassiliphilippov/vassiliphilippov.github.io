linewoman

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪnwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈlaɪnwʊmən / (BrE)
A female worker who installs or repairs electrical or telephone lines.
female electrical or telephone line worker

Examples

The company recently hired its first linewoman to work on the city’s electrical grid.
As a linewoman, she often has to climb tall utility poles, even in bad weather.
The documentary featured a linewoman discussing the physical demands of her job.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lineman - a worker who installs or maintains electrical or telephone lines

Etymology

Formed from 'line' (as in power or telephone lines) + 'woman', modeled after 'lineman'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪnwʊmən / (AmE), / ˈlaɪnwʊmən / (BrE)
a female surveyor whose job is to mark positions with a range pole
female surveyor who marks positions

Examples

The linewoman measured the distance between the markers with great care.
As the only linewoman on the surveying team, she took pride in her precision.
The experienced linewoman showed the new recruits how to properly use the range pole.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: linesman - a person who marks positions or assists in surveying (gender-neutral or male)
  • Synonym: lineman - a male or gender-neutral surveyor or worker marking lines/positions

Etymology

Formed from 'line' + 'woman', analogous to 'lineman'.
