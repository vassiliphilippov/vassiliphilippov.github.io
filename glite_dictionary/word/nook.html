nook

1. NOUN

/ nʊk / (AmE), / nʊk / (BrE)
a sheltered or secluded place, often small or hidden from view
sheltered or secluded place

Examples

She found a quiet nook in the library to read her book.
The cat curled up in a nook by the fireplace.
They enjoyed lunch in a sunny nook of the garden.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corner - a place where two lines or surfaces meet, forming an angle

Etymology

Possibly from Middle English *noke* (corner, angle), of uncertain origin.

2. NOUN

/ nʊk / (AmE), / nʊk / (BrE)
a corner or recess, especially one formed by the meeting of two walls, often considered a small, sheltered, or hidden place
corner or small recess formed by walls

Examples

She placed her armchair in the cozy nook by the fireplace.
A small nook in the library was perfect for reading quietly.
The cat found a nook between the bookcase and the wall to nap in.

Synset Examples

a piano was in one corner of the room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corner - the point where two lines, edges, or sides meet; an interior angle

Etymology

Middle English noke, of uncertain origin, perhaps related to Old Norse "núkr" (corner).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03114532-n
Synset POS: n
