rond-de-cuir

1. NOUN

/ ʁɔ̃ də kɥiʁ / (AmE), / ʁɔ̃ də kɥiʁ / (BrE)
A ballet movement in which the leg is moved in a circular motion, usually with the toe tracing a circle on the floor or in the air.
ballet movement; circular leg motion

Examples

The lead dancer executed a graceful rond-de-cuir across the stage.
Her teacher corrected her position during the rond-de-cuir exercise.
Each ballet routine began with a slow rond-de-cuir to warm up the legs.

Synset Examples

She performed a perfect rond-de-cuir during the ballet.
The choreographer added a rond-de-cuir to the routine.

Etymology

French, literally 'round of leather', comparing the movement to drawing a circle with the toe as if on leather.
