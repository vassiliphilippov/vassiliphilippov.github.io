scarecrow

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɛrˌkroʊ / (AmE), / ˈskeəˌkrəʊ / (BrE)
An object made to resemble a human, set up to scare birds away from fields or gardens.
figure to scare birds from fields

Examples

The farmer placed a scarecrow in the middle of the field to keep birds away from the crops.
After a week, the birds seemed to get used to the scarecrow and ignored it.
Children sometimes dress up the old scarecrow with hats and scarves during autumn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: effigy - a sculpture or model of a person

Etymology

From 'scare' + 'crow'; earliest use in Middle English.
