to scranch

1. VERB

/ skræntʃ / (AmE), / skræntʃ / (BrE)
to make a loud, crushing sound, usually by treading or grinding something hard
make a crushing or crunching noise

Examples

You could hear the gravel scranch beneath his boots as he walked up the drive.
The tires began to scranch over the loose stones at the edge of the road.
She loved the way the snow would scranch underfoot on a cold, crisp morning.

Synset Examples

his shoes were crunching on the gravel

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crunch - to make a loud crushing sound

Etymology

Variant of 'scrunch' or possibly 'crunch,' influenced by dialectal forms.
