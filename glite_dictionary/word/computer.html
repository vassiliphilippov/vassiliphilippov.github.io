computer

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈpjuːtər / (AmE), / kəmˈpjuːtə / (BrE)
(historical) A person who makes calculations, especially with mathematical or scientific data, or who operates calculating machines.
person skilled in calculation (historic)
archaic

Examples

In the early 20th century, a computer was a vital part of every astronomical observatory.
A team of computers would check the engineers’ calculations by hand.
Before the advent of modern machines, a computer was often a job title given to mathematically skilled women.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calculator - a person or device that calculates numbers
  • Synonym: mathematician - an expert in mathematics

Etymology

From compute + -er; originally referred to people who calculated, before the modern sense of an electronic machine.

2. NOUN

/ kəmˈpjuːtɚ / (AmE), / kəmˈpjuːtə / (BrE)
an electronic device capable of processing, storing, and retrieving information according to programmed instructions
electronic device for processing information

Examples

The office just bought a new computer for the accounting department.
He spends hours every day working on his computer.
Students often use a computer to complete their assignments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calculator - an electronic device used for making mathematical calculations

Etymology

From compute + -er, originally describing people who performed calculations; later applied to machines.
