lm

1. NOUN

/ ɛl ˈɛm / (AmE), / ɛl ˈɛm / (BrE)
A unit of luminous flux equal to the amount of light emitted in a solid angle of one steradian by a point source of one candela radiating uniformly.
unit of luminous flux (lumen)

Examples

The brightness of a projector is measured in lm, so higher numbers mean a brighter image.
This bulb has a light output of 800 lm, comparable to a traditional 60-watt bulb.
Technical specifications often list both power consumption in watts and light output in lm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lumen - unit of luminous flux in the SI system

Etymology

Abbreviation of 'lumen', from Latin 'lumen' (light).
