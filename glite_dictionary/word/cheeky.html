cheeky

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃiki / (AmE), / ˈtʃiːki / (BrE)
Behaving in a disrespectful or impudently bold manner, often in a playful or amusing way.
impudently bold or disrespectful, often playfully
british

Examples

The child gave his teacher a cheeky grin after making a joke in class.
She made a cheeky comment about her boss’s new haircut, making everyone laugh.
It was rather cheeky of him to ask for a second helping before anyone else was served.

Synset Examples

a brash newcomer disputed the age-old rules for admission to the club
a nervy thing to say

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impudent - not showing due respect; offensively bold
  • Degree Lower: bold - showing willingness to take risks; confident and courageous
  • Degree Higher: insolent - showing a rude and arrogant lack of respect

Etymology

From 'cheek' (impudence) + '-y'.
