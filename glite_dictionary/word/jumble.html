jumble

1. VERB

/ ˈdʒʌm.bəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌm.bəl / (BrE)
to mix things together in a confused or disordered way
mix up without order

Examples

Don't jumble the photos, please organize them by date.
She tends to jumble her ideas during presentations, making them hard to follow.
The wind began to jumble the leaves across the yard.

Synset Examples

She jumbles the words when she is supposed to write a sentence

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mix - combine or blend into one mass or mixture
  • Antonym: sort - arrange systematically in groups or categories

Etymology

From Middle English *jumblen*, frequentative of *jumble* (to move about disorderly), possibly of imitative origin.

/ ˈdʒʌm.bəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌm.bəl / (BrE)

Examples

Related Terms

3. VERB

/ ˈdʒʌm.bəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌm.bəl / (BrE)
to mix things together in a confused or untidy way; to bring into random order
mix up or confuse

Examples

The papers on his desk were completely jumbled after the wind blew through the window.
She managed to jumble the pieces of the puzzle, making it harder to solve.
Don't jumble your thoughts—try to present your ideas clearly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mix up - to put things together so that they are not in order
  • Antonym: to arrange - to put things in a neat or desired order

Etymology

Probably from Middle English *jumble*, frequentative of *jump*; influenced in form by *rumble*.

4. NOUN

/ ˈdʒʌm.bəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌm.bəl / (BrE)
a small, flat, ring-shaped cake or cookie, typically crisp and often flavored with caraway or other spices
small, ring-shaped cake or cookie
archaic

Examples

In the old cookbook, there was a recipe for spiced jumbles shaped like rings.
We baked a batch of jumbles for the village fair, using my grandmother's recipe.
These jumbles are crisp on the outside and delightfully fragrant with caraway seeds.

Etymology

From early modern English, possibly from Middle English 'gemmel' (twin, paired), referring to the twisted shape, or from the French 'jumelle' (twin), due to the way these cakes were often shaped.
