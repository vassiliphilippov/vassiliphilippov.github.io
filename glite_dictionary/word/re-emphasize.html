to re-emphasize

1. VERB

/ ˌriːˈɛmfəˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˌriːˈɛmfəsaɪz / (BrE)
to state or stress something again, especially to give added importance to it
to emphasize something again

Examples

The director re-emphasized the importance of safety during the meeting.
Let me re-emphasize that attendance is mandatory for everyone involved.
She re-emphasized her main point before concluding the presentation.

Synset Examples

The director re-emphasized the need for greater productivity

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: to emphasize - to give special importance or prominence to something

Etymology

From re- (again) + emphasize; based on the pattern of English prefixed verbs.
