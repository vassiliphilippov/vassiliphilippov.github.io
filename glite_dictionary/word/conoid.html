conoid

1. NOUN

/ ˈkoʊ.nɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ.nɔɪd / (BrE)
A solid geometric shape with a circular base that tapers smoothly to a point; a cone-like form.
solid shape with circular base tapering to a point

Examples

The architect designed a skylight in the shape of a conoid to allow more natural light in.
In geometry class, we learned how to calculate the volume of a conoid.
Many ancient towers were built in a conoid form for structural stability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cone - a solid with a circular base tapering to a point

Etymology

From Greek *konoeidēs*, from *kōnos* (cone) + *-eidēs* (form, shape).
