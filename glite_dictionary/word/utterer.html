utterer

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌt̬ərər / (AmE), / ˈʌtərə / (BrE)
a person or organism that produces vocal sounds; someone or something that utters spoken words or noises
someone or something that utters sounds

Examples

The utterer of the threat was never identified by the police.
As an utterer of wisdom, she was highly respected in her community.
Parrots are known as effective utterers of human words.

Synset Examples

an utterer of foul oaths
is the giraffe a vocalizer?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speaker - a person who speaks

Etymology

From the verb 'utter' (to speak or make a sound), plus agentive suffix '-er'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʌtɚɚ / (AmE), / ˈʌtərə / (BrE)
a person who circulates or attempts to pass off forged banknotes or counterfeit coins as genuine
one who passes counterfeit money
legal

Examples

The police arrested the utterer after he tried to spend several fake twenty-dollar bills.
In court, the accused utterer denied knowing the coins were counterfeit.
Authorities believe the utterer had been working with a larger forgery ring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: counterfeiter - a person who makes fraudulent copies of money or documents

Etymology

From 'utter' (to put into circulation or pass), ultimately from Middle English 'uteren' (to utter, speak), from Old English 'ūterian'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10763200-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈʌtərər / (AmE), / ˈʌtərə / (BrE)
a person who expresses something in speech or writing; especially one who speaks publicly or at length
person who expresses in speech or writing

Examples

The utterer of such harsh words offended many in the audience.
He was known as an utterer of wise proverbs in his community.
During the debate, each utterer was given five minutes to make their case.

Synset Examples

the speaker at commencement
an utterer of useful maxims

Related Terms

  • Synonym: speaker - a person who speaks, especially formally in public

Etymology

From 'utter' (to speak) + -er (agent noun suffix).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10649550-n
Synset POS: n
