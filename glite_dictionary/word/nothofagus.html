Nothofagus

1. NOUN

/ noʊˈθɑːfəɡəs / (AmE), / nɒˈθɒfəɡəs / (BrE)
A genus of southern beech trees native to temperate regions of the Southern Hemisphere, excluding Africa.
genus of southern beech trees

Examples

The forests of Patagonia are dominated by Nothofagus species, providing habitat for many animals.
Scientists studied the growth rings of Nothofagus trees to understand climate changes over time.
In New Zealand, Nothofagus forests are home to a variety of unique bird species.

Etymology

From Greek nothos (false) + phagos (beech).

2. NOUN

/ noʊˈθɑfəɡəs / (AmE), / nɒˈθɒfæɡəs / (BrE)
A genus of trees and shrubs known as southern beeches, native to the temperate regions of the Southern Hemisphere, except Africa.
genus of southern beech trees

Examples

The forests in Patagonia are dominated by several species of Nothofagus.
Researchers discovered a fossilized Nothofagus leaf dating back millions of years.
Many Nothofagus trees display brilliant autumn colors in southern Chile.

Etymology

From Greek 'nothos' (false) + 'Fagus' (beech).
