cloakroom

1. NOUN

/ ˈkloʊkˌruːm / (AmE), / ˈkləʊkˌruːm / (BrE)
a room where visitors can temporarily leave their coats and other belongings, especially in public buildings or events
room to leave coats temporarily

Examples

Please leave your bag in the cloakroom before entering the gallery.
She picked up her coat from the cloakroom after the performance ended.
The cloakroom attendant handed them a numbered ticket for their belongings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coatroom - a room where coats and other items can be left temporarily

Etymology

From 'cloak' (a type of outer garment) + 'room', indicating a room for cloaks and coats.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkloʊkˌruːm / (AmE), / ˈkləʊkruːm / (BrE)
a private lounge adjoining a legislative chamber, used by members for relaxation or informal discussions
lounge near a legislative chamber

Examples

Members of parliament often meet privately in the cloakroom between debates.
She waited for her colleague in the cloakroom adjacent to the chamber.
The senator stepped into the cloakroom to have a quiet conversation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lobby - a waiting room or area near the entrance of a building, especially in a legislative context

Etymology

From 'cloak' + 'room', originally meaning a room for hanging cloaks.
