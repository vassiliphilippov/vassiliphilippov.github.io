phaeochromocytoma

1. NOUN

/ ˌfiː.oʊˌkroʊ.moʊ.saɪˈtoʊ.mə / (AmE), / ˌfiː.əʊˌkrɒ.məʊ.saɪˈtəʊ.mə / (BrE)
A rare, usually benign tumor of the adrenal gland causing excessive production of epinephrine, often leading to high blood pressure.
tumor of the adrenal gland causing high blood pressure
medical

Examples

The doctor suspected a phaeochromocytoma after the patient's sudden hypertension episodes.
Surgery is often recommended to remove a phaeochromocytoma from the adrenal gland.
Untreated phaeochromocytoma can lead to life-threatening complications due to high blood pressure.

Etymology

From Greek 'phaios' (dusky), 'chroma' (color), and 'kytos' (cell), referring to the tumor's appearance.
