Argive

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrˌɡaɪv / (AmE), / ˈɑːɡaɪv / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of the ancient Greek city of Argos
person from ancient Argos

Examples

An Argive hero played a major role in several Greek myths.
The Argive army marched against Thebes in the famous legend.
Many poets celebrated the bravery of the Argive people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Argos - an ancient city in Greece

Etymology

From Greek *Argivos* (inhabitant of Argos).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɑrˌɡaɪv / (AmE), / ˈɑːɡaɪv / (BrE)
relating to the ancient Greek city of Argos or its inhabitants
of or relating to Argos, ancient Greece

Examples

The Argive army marched bravely against Troy in the ancient epic.
She studied Argive pottery as part of her research into Greek art.
Legends tell of the Argive heroes' exploits in battles and quests.

Etymology

From Latin Argivus, from Greek Argivos (of Argos).
