pinesap

1. NOUN

/ ˈpaɪnˌsæp / (AmE), / ˈpaɪn.sæp / (BrE)
a fleshy, tawny or reddish woodland plant (Monotropa hypopitys) that resembles Indian pipe and grows in humus-rich soils of eastern North America
North American woodland plant (Monotropa hypopitys)

Examples

The forest floor was dotted with clusters of pinesap poking through the leaf litter.
Unlike most plants, pinesap lacks chlorophyll and relies on decaying matter for nutrients.
We spotted a rare colony of pinesap while hiking in the Appalachian woods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Indian pipe - similar woodland plant (Monotropa uniflora)

Etymology

From 'pine' + 'sap', referring to its appearance and habitat near pine trees.
