catachrestic

1. ADJ

/ ˌkæt.əˈkrɛs.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌkat.əˈkrɛs.tɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characterized by catachresis; involving the improper or strained use of words.
relating to misuse of words

Examples

The poem’s catachrestic metaphors confused many readers unaccustomed to such strained language.
His explanation was so catachrestic that it bordered on nonsense.
Critics accused the novel of relying on catachrestic expressions to appear more complex than it really was.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: catachresis - the misuse or strained use of words

Etymology

From Greek *katachrēstikos*, from *katachrēsis* (misuse).
