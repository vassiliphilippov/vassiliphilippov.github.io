underfoot

1. ADV

/ ˌʌndərˈfʊt / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈfʊt / (BrE)
beneath one's feet; on the ground where people walk or stand.
beneath the feet

Examples

Careful, the floor is slippery underfoot after cleaning.
They felt the crunch of dry leaves underfoot as they walked through the woods.
After the rain, the grass was wet and muddy underfoot.

Synset Examples

trampled the beans underfoot
green grass growing underfoot
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00246377-r
Synset POS: r

2. ADV

/ ˌʌndərˈfʊt / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈfʊt / (BrE)
in a position where one's progress is hindered by things or people in the way
in the way; hindering movement

Examples

With all these boxes underfoot, it was hard to move around the apartment.
The children were always underfoot while she tried to clean the house.
Visitors complained about luggage left underfoot in the hallway.

Synset Examples

a house with children and pets and toys always underfoot

Related Terms

  • Synonym: in the way - blocking or hindering progress

Etymology

Formed from 'under' + 'foot', originally meaning literally beneath one's feet, later extended to figurative use.
