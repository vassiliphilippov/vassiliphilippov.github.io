to socialize

1. VERB

/ ˈsoʊʃəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃəlaɪz / (BrE)
to prepare (someone) to behave in a way that is acceptable in society; to train to participate in social life
prepare for life in society

Examples

It is important to socialize children so they understand how to get along with others.
Schools play a key role in helping young people socialize into the broader community.
Some parents worry about how best to socialize their children for life in a diverse society.

Synset Examples

Children have to be socialized in school

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to acculturate - to adopt the cultural traits or social patterns of a group
  • Synonym: to civilize - to bring out of a savage or uneducated state

Etymology

From French 'socialiser', from 'social' meaning 'social'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00406978-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈsoʊʃəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃəlaɪz / (BrE)
to train someone, especially a child or animal, to behave in a way that is acceptable in a social environment
train for social behavior

Examples

Young children need to be socialized so that they can interact well with others.
Puppies should be socialized early to prevent behavioral problems later.
The program helps parents understand how to socialize their toddlers.

Synset Examples

The children must be properly socialized

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to acculturate - to adapt or cause to adapt to a new culture
  • Synonym: to civilize - to bring out of a primitive or savage state; to make civil

Etymology

From French socialiser, from Latin socialis (sociable, allied).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02391987-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ˈsoʊʃəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃəlaɪz / (BrE)
to participate in social activities and interact with other people
to participate in social activities

Examples

At the company picnic, everyone had a chance to socialize and enjoy the afternoon.
She finds it easy to socialize with people from different backgrounds.
After moving to a new city, he made an effort to socialize more and meet his neighbors.

Synset Examples

He never socializes with his colleagues
The old man hates to socialize

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mingle - to move among and talk to other people at a social event
  • Antonym: to isolate - to remain apart from others; to keep away from social contact

Etymology

From French *socialiser*, derived from Latin *socialis* (of companionship, sociable).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02394092-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ˈsoʊʃəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something conform to socialist ideas or bring under collective or governmental control
bring under socialist or collective control

Examples

The new government plans to socialize key industries to ensure universal access.
Some oppose efforts to socialize higher education, fearing a loss of quality.
Healthcare advocates argue that it is time to fully socialize medicine in the country.

Synset Examples

Health care should be socialized!

Etymology

From socialist, modeled after earlier forms such as 'nationalize'.

Source: WordNet
