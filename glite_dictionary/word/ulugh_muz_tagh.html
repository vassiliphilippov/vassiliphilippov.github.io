Ulugh Muz Tagh

1. PROPN

/ uːˈluː mʊz ˈtɑːɡ / (AmE), / uːˈluː mʊz ˈtɑːɡ / (BrE)
a mountain peak in the Kunlun range of China, rising to 25,340 feet (7,724 meters).
mountain in the Kunlun range, China

Examples

The expedition team successfully reached the summit of Ulugh Muz Tagh after weeks of climbing.
Ulugh Muz Tagh is considered one of the highest and most remote peaks in the Kunlun mountains.
Researchers studied glacial formations on Ulugh Muz Tagh to understand the region's climate history.

Etymology

From Turkic languages, 'Ulugh' meaning 'great' and 'Muz Tagh' meaning 'ice mountain'.
