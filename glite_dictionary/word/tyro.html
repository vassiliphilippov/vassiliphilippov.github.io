tyro

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪ.roʊ / (AmE), / ˈtʌɪ.rəʊ / (BrE)
a person who is new to a particular activity, subject, or field; a beginner or novice
a beginner or novice

Examples

As a tyro in the kitchen, he often burned his meals.
The class was designed for the tyro rather than the experienced artist.
Even a tyro can complete this easy hiking trail without much difficulty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: novice - a person new to and inexperienced in a job or situation
  • Antonym: expert - a person with comprehensive and authoritative knowledge or skill in a particular area

Etymology

From Latin 'tiro' (young soldier, recruit, beginner).
