Rachycentridae

1. NOUN

/ ˌreɪsiːˈsɛntrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌreɪsɪˈsɛntrɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine fish that consists solely of the cobia.
family of fish containing only the cobia

Examples

The family Rachycentridae includes only one living species, the cobia.
Marine biologists study Rachycentridae to better understand pelagic fish evolution.
The classification of Rachycentridae helps distinguish cobia from closely related fish families.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cobia - a large, predatory marine fish in tropical and subtropical waters

Etymology

From New Latin, combining Rachycentron (genus name of cobia) + -idae (family suffix in zoology).
