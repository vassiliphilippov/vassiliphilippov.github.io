gibberellin

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪbəˈrɛlɪn / (AmE), / dʒɪbəˈrɛlɪn / (BrE)
a plant hormone that regulates growth and development, often promoting stem elongation, seed germination, and flowering
a plant growth hormone
medical

Examples

Researchers applied gibberellin to the plants to accelerate their growth rate in the greenhouse.
The discovery of gibberellin revolutionized our understanding of plant development processes.
Some fruits grow larger when gibberellin is used to promote cell expansion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: auxin - another major plant growth hormone

Etymology

From New Latin Gibberella (the fungus genus from which it was first isolated) + -in (chemical suffix).
