painstaking

1. ADJ

/ ˈpeɪnzˌteɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpeɪnzteɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
showing or involving diligent care and great effort; done very carefully and thoroughly.
done with great care and effort

Examples

She completed the restoration with painstaking attention to every detail.
His painstaking research uncovered new information previously unknown to historians.
Preparing the report was a painstaking process that required checking every figure twice.

Synset Examples

conscientious application to the work at hand
painstaking research
scrupulous attention to details

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meticulous - showing great attention to detail; very careful and precise
  • Antonym: careless - not giving sufficient attention or thought; lacking care

Etymology

From pain + staking, originally meaning 'taking pains'.
