Digitaria sanguinalis

1. NOUN

/ dɪˌdʒɪtəriə sæŋɡwɪˈneɪlɪs / (AmE), / dɪˌdʒɪtəriːə sæŋɡwɪˈneɪlɪs / (BrE)
A species of grass native to Europe, often grown for hay, and widely considered a weed in the United States.
a species of grass and common weed

Examples

Digitaria sanguinalis is commonly found invading lawns and gardens in temperate regions.
Farmers often try to control the spread of Digitaria sanguinalis to protect their crops.
Although Digitaria sanguinalis is used as forage in some areas, it is mostly treated as a weed in North America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crabgrass - common name for several Digitaria grasses, including Digitaria sanguinalis

Etymology

From Latin: 'digitaria' referring to 'finger-like' (the shape of the flower clusters) and 'sanguinalis' meaning 'blood-red'.
