gainer

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡeɪnər / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪnə / (BrE)
A dive in which the diver kicks their feet forward and executes a complete backward somersault, entering the water feet first and facing away from the diving board.
a backward somersault dive

Examples

The diver performed a perfect gainer off the springboard, impressing the judges.
Learning how to do a gainer can be challenging for beginner divers.
She received her highest score of the competition for a well-executed gainer.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡeɪnər / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪnə / (BrE)
a person who gains weight, especially over a period of time or as a result of a specific diet or lifestyle
person who gains weight

Examples

As a dedicated gainer, she carefully tracked her calorie intake and weight progress.
Many fitness programs cater to gainers as well as people looking to lose weight.
The nutritionist advised the gainer to focus on healthy sources of calories.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: loser - a person who loses weight

Etymology

From the verb 'gain' plus the agent suffix '-er'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈɡeɪnər / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪnə / (BrE)
a person who gains an advantage, benefit, or profit from a situation
person who gains advantage or profit

Examples

In any negotiation, there is usually a gainer and a loser.
The new policy turned out to be a major benefit for the biggest gainer in the market.
He emerged the sole gainer from the chaotic merger process.

Synset Examples

she was clearly the gainer in that exchange

Related Terms

  • Antonym: loser - a person who loses, especially in competition with another

Etymology

From 'gain' + '-er', formed in English.
