to itinerate

1. VERB

/ ɪˈtɪnəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈtɪnəreɪt / (BrE)
to travel from place to place, especially as part of one's work
travel from place to place for work

Examples

She was required to itinerate throughout the region to oversee the training workshops.
The sales representative would itinerate between several cities each month.
Many early preachers would regularly itinerate to reach remote congregations.

Synset Examples

an itinerating merchant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: travel - to go from one place to another, typically over a distance
  • Antonym: stay - to remain in one place

Etymology

From Medieval Latin itineratus, past participle of itinerari (to travel), from Latin iter, itineris (journey).
