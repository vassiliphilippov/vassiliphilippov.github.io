pallet

1. NOUN

/ ˈpælət / (AmE), / ˈpælɪt / (BrE)
A hand tool with a flat blade used by potters for mixing and shaping clay.
potter's flat-bladed mixing tool

Examples

The potter used a pallet to smooth the edges of the clay bowl.
You should wet the pallet before mixing the colored slip.
Her toolkit included a pallet alongside various sponges and brushes.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpælət / (AmE), / ˈpælɪt / (BrE)
a flat, portable platform on which goods are stacked, stored, or moved, typically using a forklift
portable platform for goods

Examples

The boxes of electronics were stacked neatly on a pallet ready for shipping.
A forklift operator moved the heavy pallet across the warehouse floor.
Each pallet can hold up to a thousand kilograms of produce.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skid - a low platform on runners, used for moving goods

Etymology

From Middle French palette, diminutive of pale (spade, shovel), originally referring to a small board.

3. NOUN

/ ˈpælɪt / (AmE), / ˈpælɪt / (BrE)
A mattress filled with straw or a pad made of quilts, used as a simple bed.
simple straw or quilt mattress

Examples

He slept on a pallet in the corner of the room.
The children made a pallet on the floor for their sleepover.
After a long day, the farmer collapsed onto his humble pallet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bed - a piece of furniture for sleep or rest

Etymology

From Middle French *pale* 'straw', diminutive *pallet*.

4. NOUN

/ ˈpælɪt / (AmE), / ˈpælɪt / (BrE)
a flat, usually oval or oblong board used by artists to mix their paints and sometimes to refer to an artist's range of colors
artist's paint-mixing board; range of colors

Examples

The artist carefully mixed red and yellow on his pallet to create a warm orange hue.
She cleaned her pallet after each painting session to preserve the purity of her colors.
Each painter develops a unique pallet that reflects their personal style and mood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: palette - artist's paint-mixing board; range of colors (alternative spelling)

Etymology

From French *palette*, diminutive of *pale* meaning 'shovel, blade'.
