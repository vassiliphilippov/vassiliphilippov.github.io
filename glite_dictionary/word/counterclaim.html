counterclaim

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊntərˌkleɪm / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌkleɪm / (BrE)
A claim made in response to a previous claim, especially by a defendant in a lawsuit against the plaintiff.
claim made in response to another (legal)
legal

Examples

The defendant filed a counterclaim accusing the plaintiff of breach of contract.
The court considered both the original claim and the counterclaim before making a decision.
In response to the lawsuit, she submitted a counterclaim seeking damages.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: claim - a demand or request for something considered one's due

Etymology

From 'counter-' (against) + 'claim'; dating from the mid-19th century in legal usage.

2. VERB

/ ˈkaʊntərˌkleɪm / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌkleɪm / (BrE)
to make a claim in response to or in opposition to a previous claim, especially in a legal context
make a claim against another claim

Examples

The defendant decided to counterclaim for damages after being sued.
When faced with accusations, she chose to counterclaim instead of settling.
Their company counterclaimed that the original lawsuit was unfounded.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: claim - to state or assert something as a fact
  • Antonym: to concede - to admit or acknowledge something as true, often reluctantly

Etymology

Formed from 'counter-' (against) + 'claim'.

Synset POS: v
