scrod

1. NOUN

/ skrɑd / (AmE), / skrɒd / (BrE)
the flesh of young Atlantic cod, haddock, or pollock, especially when prepared as food
flesh of young cod or similar fish

Examples

The restaurant special tonight is broiled scrod served with lemon and herbs.
Many New England seafood dishes feature scrod as the main ingredient.
He ordered a plate of fresh scrod for lunch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cod - a large marine fish with firm white flesh
  • Synonym: haddock - a small, silvery North Atlantic fish related to the cod

Etymology

Origin uncertain, perhaps from obsolete Dutch schrood (piece cut off, slice) or related to 'shred'.

2. NOUN

/ skrɑd / (AmE), / skrɒd / (BrE)
A young Atlantic cod or haddock, especially one that has been split and boned for cooking.
young Atlantic cod or haddock, split and boned

Examples

The restaurant is famous for its grilled scrod served with lemon and herbs.
She bought fresh scrod from the market to make a traditional New England dinner.
Many local menus feature baked scrod as a classic seafood option.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: haddock - a species of fish related to cod, popular as food
  • Synonym: cod - a large marine fish, valued as food

Etymology

Origin uncertain, possibly from New England dialect or American English, attested since the 19th century.
