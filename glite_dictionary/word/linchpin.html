linchpin

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪntʃˌpɪn / (AmE), / ˈlɪntʃ.pɪn / (BrE)
A pin passed through the end of an axle to keep a wheel in position.
pin used to secure a wheel on an axle

Examples

The mechanic inserted the linchpin to ensure the wheel would not come loose during travel.
Without a secure linchpin, the cart's wheel could easily fall off the axle.
They checked every linchpin on the wagon before setting off on the journey.

Etymology

From Middle English lynspin, from lynis (axletree) + pin.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɪntʃˌpɪn / (AmE), / ˈlɪntʃ.pɪn / (BrE)
A person or thing that is the most important part of a group, system, or organization, providing support and stability.
essential element or main support

Examples

As the project linchpin, she ensured the team's success despite many challenges.
Good communication is often the linchpin of a successful business relationship.
He has become the linchpin of the entire organization through his leadership and vision.

a person or thing that is the most important part of a group, system, or project, holding everything together or making it work successfully
crucial or central element

Examples

Her excellent organizational skills made her the linchpin of the entire operation.
Good communication is often the linchpin of successful teamwork.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cornerstone - an essential, foundational element
  • Synonym: keystone - a central, supporting element
