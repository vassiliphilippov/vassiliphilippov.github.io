timpanist

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɪmpənɪst / (AmE), / ˈtɪmpənɪst / (BrE)
a person who plays the timpani (kettledrums), especially in an orchestra
person who plays the timpani

Examples

The timpanist played a dramatic solo during the symphony's finale.
Orchestras rely on the skill and timing of a talented timpanist.
She became the principal timpanist after years of dedicated practice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: percussionist - a person who plays percussion instruments

Etymology

From Italian 'timpanista', from 'timpano' (drum), itself from Latin 'tympanum'.
