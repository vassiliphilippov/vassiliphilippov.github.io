greatcoat

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡreɪtˌkoʊt / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪt.kəʊt / (BrE)
a large, heavy coat worn over other clothing, typically in winter, for warmth
heavy winter overcoat

Examples

The old soldier still kept his greatcoat hanging by the door for cold nights.
He buttoned his greatcoat tightly before stepping into the snow.
During the parade, officers wore formal uniforms with their greatcoats draped over their shoulders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overcoat - a long coat worn over other clothes for warmth

Etymology

From 'great' (large) + 'coat'.
