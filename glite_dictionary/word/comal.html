comal

1. ADJ

/ ˈkoʊməl / (AmE), / ˈkəʊməl / (BrE)
(of seeds) having a tuft or tufts of hair; comose.
having a tuft of hair (of seeds)

Examples

The botanist noted the comal seeds dispersed easily in the wind.
Some cotton plants produce distinctly comal fibers on their seeds.
The comal tufts help certain seeds attach to animal fur for dispersal.

Synset Examples

comate (or comose) seeds
a comal tuft

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comose - (of seeds) having a tuft of hair

Etymology

Related to Latin coma (hair), also linked to comose.
