Digitalis

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪdʒɪˈtælɪs / (AmE), / ˌdɪdʒɪˈteɪlɪs / (BrE)
A genus of Eurasian plants with alternate leaves and racemes of showy, bell-shaped flowers, including the common foxgloves.
genus of flowering plants (foxgloves)
medical

Examples

The Digitalis genus is well known for its beautiful purple flowers.
Many heart medications are derived from compounds found in Digitalis plants.
Gardeners appreciate Digitalis for its tall spikes and striking floral displays.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foxglove - A common name for plants in the genus Digitalis

Etymology

From Latin 'digitalis' (of the finger), referring to the finger-shaped flower.
