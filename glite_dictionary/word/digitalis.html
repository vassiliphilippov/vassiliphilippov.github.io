digitalis

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪdʒɪˈtælɪs / (AmE), / ˌdɪdʒɪˈteɪlɪs / (BrE)
a drug made from the foxglove plant, used as a cardiac stimulant to treat certain heart conditions
cardiac drug from foxglove
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed digitalis to help regulate his heartbeat.
Patients taking digitalis must have their pulse monitored regularly to avoid toxicity.
Digitalis is extracted from the foxglove plant and used in the treatment of certain heart diseases.

Etymology

From Latin 'digitalis', meaning 'of a finger', referring to the foxglove plant (Digitalis purpurea), whose flowers resemble fingers.

2. NOUN

/ ˌdɪdʒɪˈtælɪs / (AmE), / ˌdɪdʒɪˈtɑːlɪs / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Digitalis, commonly known as foxglove, cultivated for its tall spikes of bell-shaped flowers and as a source of the drug digitalis.
a genus of flowering plants, foxglove

Examples

The bright purple flowers of digitalis are seen in many cottage gardens.
Digitalis is valued in medicine for its heart-regulating properties.
She planted digitalis along the fence for its tall, colorful blooms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foxglove - Another name for plants of the genus Digitalis

Etymology

From Latin 'digitalis' (of or relating to a finger), from 'digitus' (finger), referring to the flower's shape.
