to brabble

1. VERB

/ ˈbræbəl / (AmE), / ˈbræbəl / (BrE)
to argue noisily or quarrel about petty or trivial matters
quarrel about trifles
archaic

Examples

The two colleagues began to brabble over who should take credit for the minor detail.
She disliked it when her children brabbled over the smallest things at the dinner table.
Rather than brabbling, let us focus on finding a solution to the problem.

Synset Examples

Let's not quibble over pennies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: quibble - to argue or raise objections about a trivial matter
  • Synonym: squabble - to argue noisily about something unimportant

Etymology

Probably from Middle Dutch *brabbelen* (to babble, quarrel).
