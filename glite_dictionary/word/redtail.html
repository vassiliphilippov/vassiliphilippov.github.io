redtail

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛdˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈrɛdˌteɪl / (BrE)
A large North American hawk with dark brown feathers and a distinctive reddish-brown tail, commonly known as the red-tailed hawk.
North American hawk with a reddish-brown tail

Examples

We spotted a redtail soaring above the fields during our hike.
A redtail perched on the fence, watching for small animals to hunt.
The distinctive cry of the redtail echoed across the valley.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red-tailed hawk - A large North American hawk with a reddish-brown tail

2. NOUN

/ ˈrɛdˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈrɛdˌteɪl / (BrE)
A European songbird with a reddish breast and tail, related to Old World robins.
European songbird with reddish tail

Examples

The redtail perched briefly on the garden wall before fluttering away.
Many birdwatchers hope to catch a glimpse of the elusive redtail during spring migration.
A redtail was spotted singing from the top of the old oak tree.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: redstart - A European songbird with a red tail, closely related to the redtail

Etymology

From 'red' + 'tail', referring to the bird's tail coloration.
