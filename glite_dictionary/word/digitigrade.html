digitigrade

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪdʒɪtəˌɡreɪd / (AmE), / ˈdɪdʒɪtɪɡreɪd / (BrE)
an animal that walks on its toes so that only the digits (toes) touch the ground, as in cats or dogs
animal that walks on its toes (e.g. cat, dog)

Examples

Dogs and cats are classic examples of digitigrade animals, moving silently on their toes.
Unlike bears, which are plantigrade, cheetahs are digitigrade and built for speed.
The fossil was identified as a digitigrade, suggesting it walked on its toes like modern wolves.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: plantigrade - animal that walks with the whole foot on the ground (e.g. humans, bears)

Etymology

From Latin 'digitus' (finger, toe) + '-grade' (walking).

2. ADJ

/ ˈdɪdʒɪtɪˌɡreɪd / (AmE), / ˈdɪdʒ.ɪ.tɪ.ɡreɪd / (BrE)
(of mammals) walking on the toes with the posterior part of the foot raised, as in cats, dogs, and horses.
walking on toes with heels raised

Examples

Most cats are digitigrade animals, moving silently on their toes.
Dogs have a digitigrade gait, which contributes to their speed and agility.
Unlike humans, who are plantigrade, horses are digitigrade mammals.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: plantigrade - walking with the entire sole of the foot on the ground, as humans and bears do
  • Degree Higher: unguligrade - walking on the tips of the toes, typically with hooves, as in horses and deer

Etymology

From Latin 'digitus' (finger, toe) + '-grade' (from 'gradus', meaning 'step').
