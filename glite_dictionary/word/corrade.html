to corrade

1. VERB

/ kəˈreɪd / (AmE), / kəˈreɪd / (BrE)
(of water, wind, or other agents) to wear away or erode (rock or earth) by abrasion or friction.
wear away by abrasion or erosion

Examples

Over time, glaciers can corrade entire valleys, leaving behind smoothed rock surfaces.
The river continued to corrade the canyon walls, deepening the gorge year by year.
Wind and sand combine to corrade the exposed limestone cliffs along the shore.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to erode - to gradually wear away or destroy, especially by natural forces
  • Synonym: to abrade - to wear away by rubbing or scraping

Etymology

From Latin 'corradere' (to scrape together), from 'com-' (together) + 'radere' (to scrape).
