III John

1. PROPN

/ ˌθɜrd ˌdʒɑn / (AmE), / ˌθɜːd ˌdʒɒn / (BrE)
the third epistle in the New Testament, traditionally attributed to Saint John the Apostle
third New Testament epistle of John

Examples

Many biblical scholars have debated the authorship of III John.
The message of hospitality is central to III John in the New Testament.
Our Bible study group will discuss III John next week.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: II John - the second New Testament epistle attributed to John
  • Degree Lower: I John - the first New Testament epistle attributed to John

Etymology

'III' is the Roman numeral for 'three', combined with the name John, referring to the Apostle.
