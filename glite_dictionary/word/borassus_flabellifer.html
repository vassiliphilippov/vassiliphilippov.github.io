Borassus flabellifer

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈræsəs fləˌbɛləˈlɪfər / (AmE), / bəˈræsəs fləˌbɛləˈlɪfə / (BrE)
a tall fan palm native to Africa, India, and Malaysia, yielding hard wood, sweet sap used for palm wine and sugar, and leaves for thatching and weaving.
species of tall fan palm tree

Examples

The leaves of the Borassus flabellifer are often used for making baskets and mats in rural villages.
Farmers tap the Borassus flabellifer to collect its sweet sap for making palm wine.
In some regions, the hard wood from Borassus flabellifer is prized for construction and furniture.

Etymology

From Latin, combining genus Borassus and species flabellifer, meaning 'fan-bearing'.
