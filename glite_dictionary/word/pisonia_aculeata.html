Pisonia aculeata

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈsoʊniə ˌækjuˈleɪtə / (AmE), / pɪˈsəʊniə ˌækjʊˈleɪtə / (BrE)
a small spiny tree native to the West Indies and tropical regions, known for its sticky fruits that cling to animal fur and feathers
a spiny West Indian tree species

Examples

The Pisonia aculeata produces sticky fruits that often attach to birds, aiding in seed dispersal.
Botanists studying tropical flora frequently encounter the spiny Pisonia aculeata tree.
Several islands in the Caribbean feature dense thickets of Pisonia aculeata.

Etymology

From New Latin, with 'Pisonia' named after Willem Piso, a Dutch naturalist, and 'aculeata' from Latin 'aculeatus' meaning 'having prickles'.
