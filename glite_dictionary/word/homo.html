Homo

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊmoʊ / (AmE), / ˈhəʊməʊ / (BrE)
the genus of primates that includes modern humans and their close extinct relatives, characterized by superior intelligence, articulate speech, and upright posture
the genus including humans and related species

Examples

The genus Homo first appeared about 2.8 million years ago in Africa.
Fossils of Homo erectus have been found in both Africa and Asia.
Anthropologists study the evolution of species within the Homo genus.

Etymology

From Latin *homo* (man, human being), used as a genus name in scientific classification.
