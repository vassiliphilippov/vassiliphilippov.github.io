Father

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑːðər / (AmE), / ˈfɑːðə / (BrE)
(in Christianity) God when regarded as the first person of the Trinity.
God as the first person of the Trinity

Examples

The congregation prayed to the Father for guidance and strength.
Many Christians address God as the Father in their prayers.
The doctrine of the Trinity teaches that the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit are one God.

Synset Examples

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɑðər / (AmE), / ˈfɑːðə / (BrE)
(in Christianity) one of the early and influential theologians whose writings established official church doctrine, especially from the 2nd to 7th centuries.
early influential Christian theologian

Examples

Many of the writings of the early Fathers continue to shape Christian theology today.
Augustine is perhaps the most influential Latin Father of the Church.
The Greek Fathers played a crucial role in developing the doctrine of the Trinity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Church Father - a leading theologian and writer of early Christianity

Etymology

From Latin 'pater' (father), used as a title of respect for high-ranking or influential Christian leaders.

3. NOUN

/ ˈfɑðɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɑːðə / (BrE)
a title or form of address for a priest, especially in the Roman Catholic or Orthodox Church.
title for a priest (Catholic/Orthodox)

Examples

During the service, Father Michael delivered a moving sermon.
The children greeted Father Joseph warmly every Sunday.
She scheduled a meeting with Father O'Brien to discuss the wedding arrangements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Padre - title for a military chaplain or Catholic priest
