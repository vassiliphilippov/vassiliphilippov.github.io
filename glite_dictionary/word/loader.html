loader

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊdər / (AmE), / ˈləʊdə / (BrE)
a person who assists a shooter by loading guns for them while shooting game
person who loads guns for a shooter

Examples

During the pheasant hunt, his loader kept the guns ready at all times.
The loader handed the next shotgun to the shooter with practiced efficiency.
Each loader was responsible for ensuring safety and speed in reloading during the event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assistant - a person who helps someone else with a task

2. NOUN

/ ˈloʊdər / (AmE), / ˈləʊdə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to load and unload goods, especially onto or from ships in a port
person who loads/unloads cargo at a port

Examples

The loader efficiently moved crates from the dock onto the ship.
After a long shift, the loaders finally finished unloading the cargo vessel.
He worked as a loader at the busy harbor for over ten years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stevedore - a person employed at a dock to load and unload cargo from ships

Etymology

From load + -er, denoting a person or thing that loads.
