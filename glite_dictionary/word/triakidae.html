Triakidae

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈækɪˌdi / (AmE), / traɪˈækɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small sharks with smooth skin, lacking spines on their dorsal fins.
a family of small, smooth-skinned sharks

Examples

The Triakidae family is known for its members' smooth skin and absence of dorsal fin spines.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the Triakidae off the coast of California.
Many members of the Triakidae are found in temperate and tropical seas worldwide.

Etymology

From Modern Latin Triakidae, based on the type genus Triakis, itself from Greek triakis (three-pointed).

2. NOUN

/ traɪˈækɪˌdi / (AmE), / traɪˈækɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of small sharks with smooth skins and lacking spines on their dorsal fins
family of small sharks

Examples

The Triakidae are found in temperate and tropical waters around the globe.
Scientists studied the unique teeth structure of several members of the Triakidae family.
Unlike some other sharks, those in the Triakidae are known for lacking dorsal fin spines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: houndshark - common name for members of the family Triakidae

Etymology

From New Latin Triakidae, based on the type genus Triakis.
