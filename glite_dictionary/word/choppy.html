choppy

1. ADJ

/ ˈtʃɑpi / (AmE), / ˈtʃɒpi / (BrE)
(of water or the sea) rough and having small waves.
rough with small waves (sea/water)

Examples

The boat rocked in the choppy water, making some passengers uneasy.
Strong winds made the usually calm lake quite choppy today.
We decided to postpone our swim because the ocean looked too choppy.

Synset Examples

choppy seas

Related Terms

  • Antonym: calm - (of water or the sea) free from waves or disturbance

2. ADJ

/ ˈtʃɑpi / (AmE), / ˈtʃɒpi / (BrE)
having abrupt or uneven transitions, often lacking smoothness or flow, especially in writing, speech, or movement.
having abrupt or uneven transitions

Examples

The author's choppy prose made the story difficult to follow.
His presentation felt choppy, jumping from one topic to another without transitions.
Editing can help turn a choppy draft into a more polished and coherent piece.

Synset Examples

choppy prose

Related Terms

  • Antonym: smooth - even, without abrupt changes or interruptions, especially in writing or speech

Etymology

From chop (to move abruptly or unevenly) + -y.
