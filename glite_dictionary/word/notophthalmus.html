Notophthalmus

1. NOUN

/ noʊ.tɒfˈθæl.məs / (AmE), / nəʊ.tɒfˈθæl.məs / (BrE)
A genus of newts native to North America, including several species known for their aquatic and terrestrial life stages.
a genus of North American newts

Examples

The eastern newt, classified under the genus Notophthalmus, is common in eastern North America.
Scientists study Notophthalmus to better understand amphibian metamorphosis and habitat requirements.
Several species within the genus Notophthalmus exhibit bright red juvenile stages known as efts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: newt - a small amphibian of the family Salamandridae

Etymology

From Greek 'notos' (back) + 'ophthalmos' (eye), referring to distinguishing features of the genus.

2. NOUN

/ ˌnoʊtəfˈθælməs / (AmE), / ˌnɒtəfˈθælməs / (BrE)
A genus of North American newts in the family Salamandridae, including the Eastern newt and related species.
genus of North American newts

Examples

The Notophthalmus genus is known for its members' ability to regenerate lost limbs.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Notophthalmus genus in the wetlands.
The Eastern newt, a member of Notophthalmus, is commonly found in North American ponds.

Etymology

From Greek 'noto-' (back) and 'ophthalmos' (eye), referring to the position of the eyes.
