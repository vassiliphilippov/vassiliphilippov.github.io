discount

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɪsˌkaʊnt / (AmE), / ˈdɪsˌkaʊnt / (BrE)
Interest deducted in advance from the principal of a loan, typically calculated on an annual basis.
interest deducted in advance on a loan

Examples

The bank charged a discount on the loan, reducing the amount I received.
By applying a discount, the lender collects its interest before the borrower receives the funds.
A bill of exchange is often sold at a discount reflecting the interest deducted in advance.

Etymology

From Latin 'dis-' (apart) + 'computare' (to calculate).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɪsˌkaʊnt / (AmE), / ˈdɪsˌkaʊnt / (BrE)
an amount or percentage by which the usual price of something is reduced
reduction from usual price

Examples

All store items are available at a 20% discount this week.
She was offered a special discount for being a regular customer.
He bought the shoes at a discount during the holiday sales.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rebate - a partial refund to someone who has paid too much money for tax, rent, or a utility

Etymology

From Anglo-Norman 'descompte', from Late Latin 'discomputare' (to deduct).

3. VERB

/ ˈdɪsˌkaʊnt / (AmE), / ˈdɪsˌkaʊnt / (BrE)
to reduce the usual price of something; to offer something for sale at a lower price than usual
reduce the price of something

Examples

Many retailers discount older electronics to clear out inventory before new models arrive.
We will discount your meal if you show your student ID.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French 'descompter', from Medieval Latin 'discomputare' (to deduct).
