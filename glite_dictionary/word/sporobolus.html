Sporobolus

1. NOUN

/ spəˈrɑːbələs / (AmE), / spəˈrɒbələs / (BrE)
a large genus of grasses, known as dropseed or rush grass, found worldwide and including many annual and perennial species
genus of grasses (dropseeds)

Examples

The prairie was dominated by Sporobolus species, particularly during the summer months.
Botanists identified several rare Sporobolus grasses in the nature reserve.
Many soil conservation efforts use Sporobolus because of its adaptability to diverse environments.

Etymology

From Greek *sporos* (seed) + *bolos* (a throw), referring to the way seeds are dispersed.

2. NOUN

/ spəˈrɑːbələs / (AmE), / spəˈrɒbələs / (BrE)
A genus of annual and perennial grasses found worldwide, including species such as dropseed or rush grass.
genus of grasses (dropseed, rush grass)

Examples

The prairie is covered with several species of Sporobolus that thrive in dry conditions.
Researchers studied Sporobolus to better understand grassland ecosystems.
Some Sporobolus grasses are valued for their drought resistance in landscaping.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dropseed - common name for grasses in the genus Sporobolus
  • Synonym: rush grass - another name for some species in the genus Sporobolus

Etymology

From Greek 'sporos' (seed) + 'bolos' (throw), referring to the way seeds are dispersed.
