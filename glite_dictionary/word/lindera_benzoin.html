Lindera benzoin

1. NOUN

/ lɪnˈdɛrə bɛnˈzoʊ.ɪn / (AmE), / lɪnˈdɪərə bɛnˈzəʊ.ɪn / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub native to eastern North America, with aromatic leaves and bark, yellow flowers, and scarlet or yellow berries; also called spicebush.
Aromatic shrub of eastern North America

Examples

The bright red berries of Lindera benzoin attract many birds in the fall.
Gardeners often plant Lindera benzoin for its fragrant foliage and winter interest.
Native peoples traditionally used the leaves and stems of Lindera benzoin to make tea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spicebush - Common name for Lindera benzoin, an aromatic North American shrub

Etymology

From the genus name Lindera (honoring Swedish botanist Johan Linder) and the species epithet benzoin (referring to its aromatic quality).
