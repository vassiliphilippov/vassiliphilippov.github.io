untrue

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtru / (AmE), / ʌnˈtruː / (BrE)
(of a surface, edge, or fitting) not accurately aligned, fitted, or level; out of proper shape or position.
not accurately fitted or level

Examples

The carpenter noticed that the frame was slightly untrue and needed to be adjusted.
The window was difficult to close because it was installed untrue.
Years of settling left the old house with floors that were noticeably untrue.

Synset Examples

the frame was out of true
off-level floors and untrue doors and windows

Related Terms

  • Antonym: true - properly aligned or accurately fitted

Etymology

Prefix un- (not) + true (in proper shape or alignment).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtru / (AmE), / ʌnˈtruː / (BrE)
not loyal or faithful to a person, duty, or obligation; betraying trust
not faithful or loyal; betraying trust

Examples

She felt hurt when her closest friend was untrue to her during a difficult time.
Being untrue to one's principles can lead to regret and disappointment.
He realized he had been untrue to his promise and tried to make amends.

Synset Examples

is untrue to his highest opportunity and duty

Related Terms

  • Antonym: faithful - loyal and constant to a person, cause, or belief
  • Antonym: loyal - showing firm and constant support or allegiance
  • Synonym: to betray - to be disloyal to a person, group, or principle

Etymology

From Old English 'un-' (not) + 'trēowe' (faithful, true).
