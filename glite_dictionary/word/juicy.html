juicy

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒusi / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːsi / (BrE)
(of an offer, contract, or opportunity) very lucrative or offering large rewards.
lucrative; offering large rewards

Examples

It's a juicy assignment that everyone in the office wants.
She was offered a juicy contract to join the rival company.
They secured a juicy deal that will boost their revenues for years.

Synset Examples

a juicy contract
a nice fat job

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lucrative - producing a great deal of profit
  • Synonym: profitable - yielding profit or financial gain

Etymology

Derived from 'juice', figuratively extended to mean attractive or rewarding (as something full of desirable content).

3. ADJ

/ ˈdʒusi / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːsi / (BrE)
Full of juice; containing a lot of liquid, especially in a way that makes it pleasant to eat or drink.
full of juice; very moist

Examples

The juicy orange burst with flavor when I bit into it.
She loves eating juicy watermelon slices on hot summer days.
Choose the ripest, most juicy peaches for the best dessert.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dry - not containing much liquid or moisture

Etymology

From juice + -y, first recorded in English in the late 15th century.
