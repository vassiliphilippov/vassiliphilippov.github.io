Cercidiphyllum japonicum

1. NOUN

/ sɚˌsɪdəˈfɪləm ˌjæpəˈnɪkəm / (AmE), / səˌsɪdɪˈfɪləm ˌjapəˈnɪkəm / (BrE)
A rapidly growing deciduous tree native to China and Japan, cultivated for its blue-green, sweet-scented leaves that turn yellow to scarlet in autumn.
species of ornamental tree from East Asia

Examples

The autumn foliage of the Cercidiphyllum japonicum is renowned for its vivid colors.
Botanists appreciate the Cercidiphyllum japonicum for its unique heart-shaped leaves and sweet scent.
They planted a Cercidiphyllum japonicum in the botanical garden to enhance its collection of Asian trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: katsura tree - common English name for Cercidiphyllum japonicum

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining Greek *kerkos* (tail), *diphýllos* (two-leaved), and Latin *japonicum* (Japanese).
