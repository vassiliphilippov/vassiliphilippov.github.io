dole

1. NOUN

/ doʊl / (AmE), / dəʊl / (BrE)
money given by the government to unemployed people as financial support
government unemployment benefit
british

Examples

He has been living on the dole since he lost his job last year.
Many young people are forced to claim the dole in difficult economic times.
The government has announced changes to dole payments this month.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unemployment benefit - regular payments made by a government to unemployed people

Etymology

From Middle English dole (portion, share), from Old English dāl (deal, division).

2. NOUN

/ doʊl / (AmE), / dəʊl / (BrE)
a portion of money, food, or clothing given as charity or relief to the needy
charitable portion or allowance

Examples

Every week, the church handed out a dole of bread to families in need.
During the famine, villagers depended on the dole for survival.
He survived the harsh winter thanks to the dole distributed by local charities.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: charity - voluntary giving of help, typically money, to those in need
  • Synonym: alms - money or goods given to the poor as charity

Etymology

From Old English dāl (portion, share), related to 'deal'.
