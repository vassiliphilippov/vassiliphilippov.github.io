Harpers Ferry

1. PROPN

/ ˈhɑrpərz ˈfɛri / (AmE), / ˈhɑːpəz ˈfɛri / (BrE)
a town in northeastern West Virginia, famous as the site of abolitionist John Brown's raid on a federal arsenal in 1859
historic town in West Virginia, USA

Examples

Many tourists visit Harpers Ferry to learn about its role in American history.
The raid at Harpers Ferry was a turning point in the abolitionist movement.
She attended a reenactment of John Brown's raid at Harpers Ferry.

Etymology

Named after Robert Harper, who established a ferry there in the 18th century.
