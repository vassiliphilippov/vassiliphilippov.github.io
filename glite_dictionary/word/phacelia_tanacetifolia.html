Phacelia tanacetifolia

1. NOUN

/ fəˈsiːliə ˌtænəsɛˈtɪfoʊliə / (AmE), / fəˈsiːliə ˌtanəsɛˈtɪfəliə / (BrE)
a hairy annual plant native to California and Mexico, with dense clusters of small blue to lilac or mauve flowers, often grown as an ornamental or for attracting pollinators
hairy annual plant with blue or lilac flowers

Examples

Phacelia tanacetifolia is often used as a cover crop to attract bees and improve soil health.
The gardener planted rows of Phacelia tanacetifolia for its beautiful blue flowers and pollinator benefits.
In California, fields of Phacelia tanacetifolia bloom in spring, creating a vibrant display.

Etymology

From New Latin, with 'Phacelia' from Greek phakelos (bundle), and 'tanacetifolia' meaning 'with leaves like tansy' (Tanacetum).
