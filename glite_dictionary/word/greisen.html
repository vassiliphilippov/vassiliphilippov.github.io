greisen

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡraɪzən / (AmE), / ˈɡraɪz(ə)n / (BrE)
a type of granitic rock composed mainly of quartz and mica, often associated with tin and tungsten mineralization
granitic rock containing quartz and mica

Examples

The mine is located in a region rich in greisen deposits.
Geologists often study greisen to understand mineralization processes.
Tin ores are frequently found within greisen bodies in granitic terrains.

Etymology

From German 'Greisen', meaning 'grey rock'.
