manageability

1. NOUN

/ ˌmæn.ɪ.dʒəˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (AmE), / ˌmæn.ɪ.dʒəˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being easy to manage, control, or handle
quality of being manageable

Examples

The manageability of the new software made it popular among office workers.
When choosing a pet, their manageability is an important factor to consider.
She admired the manageability of his hair, which always looked neat without much effort.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manageableness - the quality of being manageable; an alternative noun form
  • Antonym: unmanageability - the quality of being unable to be managed or controlled

Etymology

From 'manageable' + '-ity', patterned after similar English nouns.
