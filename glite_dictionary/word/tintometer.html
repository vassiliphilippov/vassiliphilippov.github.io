tintometer

1. NOUN

/ tɪnˈtɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / tɪnˈtɒmɪtə / (BrE)
a device used to measure the amount of a substance present in a sample by analyzing the color produced with particular reagents
device for measuring substances by color analysis

Examples

The laboratory technician used the tintometer to determine the concentration of iron in the water sample.
A tintometer provides accurate results in colorimetric analysis, making it essential for chemical testing.
After adding the reagent, the scientist placed the solution in a tintometer to quantify the results.

Etymology

From 'tint' + '-ometer' (Greek 'metron', meaning measure), constructed in the 19th century for instruments measuring color.
