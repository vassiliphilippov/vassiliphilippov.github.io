marquis

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑːrk(w)ɪs / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkwɪs / (BrE)
A nobleman ranking above a count and below a duke in certain European countries, especially France.
nobleman above a count, below a duke

Examples

The French marquis hosted lavish parties at his château every summer.
Upon his father's death, he inherited the title of marquis and vast estates along the border.
Historical novels often feature a mysterious marquis at the king's court.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: duke - a nobleman ranking immediately below a prince or king
  • Degree Lower: count - a European nobleman ranking below a marquis
  • Synonym: marquess - British spelling and form of 'marquis'

Etymology

From Old French marquis, from marque (march, border region).
