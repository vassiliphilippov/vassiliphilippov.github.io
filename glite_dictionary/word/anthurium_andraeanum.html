Anthurium andraeanum

1. NOUN

/ ænˈθuːriəm ˌændriˈeɪənəm / (AmE), / ænˈθjʊəriəm ˌændriˈeɪənəm / (BrE)
A species of anthurium widely grown as an ornamental plant, known for its bright scarlet spathe and spadix.
ornamental plant with bright scarlet spathe

Examples

The Anthurium andraeanum is prized for its striking red and glossy flowers used in floral arrangements.
She decorated her living room with several pots of Anthurium andraeanum to add a splash of color.
Many gardeners appreciate Anthurium andraeanum for its long-lasting blooms and attractive foliage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flamingo flower - another common name for Anthurium andraeanum

Etymology

From Latin genus name 'Anthurium' (from Greek 'anthos' meaning flower + 'oura' meaning tail), and species name honoring French botanist Édouard André.
