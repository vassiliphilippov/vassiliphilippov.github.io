Minuartia

1. NOUN

/ mɪˈnuːˌɑːrtiə / (AmE), / mɪˈnjuːˌɑːtɪə / (BrE)
a genus of mostly perennial herbaceous plants in the Northern Hemisphere, often with mat-forming habit, commonly known as sandworts
a plant genus of perennial herbs; sandworts

Examples

The alpine garden was filled with Minuartia species that formed cushion-like mats.
Botanists studied Minuartia to understand arctic and alpine plant adaptations.
Several Minuartia plants thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arenaria - a related plant genus often called sandworts

2. NOUN

/ mɪˈnwɑːrtiə / (AmE), / mɪˈnwɑːtɪə / (BrE)
A genus of mostly perennial, mat-forming herbs native to the Northern Hemisphere, commonly known as sandworts, often previously classified in the genus Arenaria.
genus of mat-forming herbs (sandworts)

Examples

Several species of Minuartia can be found carpeting rocky slopes in alpine regions.
Botanists often debate whether Minuartia should be separated from the genus Arenaria.
The delicate white flowers of Minuartia typically bloom in early summer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Arenaria - a closely related plant genus formerly including Minuartia species
  • Synonym: sandwort - common name for plants of the genus Minuartia

Etymology

Named after the Spanish botanist Juan Minuart (1693–1768).
