Dicrostonyx

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈkrɑstənɪks / (AmE), / daɪˈkrɒstənɪks / (BrE)
A genus of small Arctic rodents known as collared lemmings, notable for their seasonal color change from brown or gray in summer to white in winter.
genus of Arctic collared lemmings

Examples

The Dicrostonyx change color in winter to blend with the snow.
Researchers studied the habitat preferences of Dicrostonyx in the Arctic tundra.
Unlike other lemmings, Dicrostonyx have a distinctive seasonal fur coat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lemming - a small rodent known for population fluctuations

Etymology

From Greek dikros (two-edged) + stonyx (sharp point), referring to a characteristic of their teeth.

2. NOUN

/ daɪˈkrɑːstəˌnɪks / (AmE), / dɪˈkrɒstənɪks / (BrE)
A genus of rodents in the family Cricetidae, commonly known as collared lemmings, found in Arctic and subarctic regions.
genus of collared lemmings

Examples

The Dicrostonyx adapts to cold Arctic winters by changing the color of its fur.
Researchers observed several Dicrostonyx species in the tundra during the breeding season.
Unlike other rodents, the Dicrostonyx develops white winter coats for camouflage.

Etymology

From Greek 'dikros' (forked, double) and 'stonyx' (nose), referring to their distinctive skull shape.
