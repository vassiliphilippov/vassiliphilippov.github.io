lazybones

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪziˌboʊnz / (AmE), / ˈleɪzɪˌbəʊnz / (BrE)
a person who habitually avoids work or exertion; a lazy person
a habitually lazy person

Examples

Come on, lazybones, get out of bed and start your day!
Stop being such a lazybones and help us with the chores.
Everyone thinks he's a lazybones because he rarely finishes his work on time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idler - a person who avoids work or spends time in an aimless way
  • Synonym: sluggard - a lazy, sluggish person

Etymology

From the adjective "lazy" and "bones," used humorously since the 16th century to refer to a lazy person.
