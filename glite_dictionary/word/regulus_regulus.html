Regulus regulus

1. NOUN

/ ˈrɛɡjələs ˈrɛɡjələs / (AmE), / ˈrɛɡjʊləs ˈrɛɡjʊləs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the goldcrest, a small European songbird with a black-bordered yellow crown patch
European goldcrest bird species

Examples

The Regulus regulus is commonly known as the goldcrest in Britain and Europe.
Ornithologists often refer to the goldcrest by its scientific name, Regulus regulus.
A distinctive feature of the Regulus regulus is its bright yellow crown patch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: goldcrest - small European songbird with a yellow crown

Etymology

From Latin 'regulus' meaning 'little king', referring to the bird's crown-like head markings.
