locomotive

1. ADJ

/ ˌloʊkəˈmoʊtɪv / (AmE), / ˌləʊkəˈməʊtɪv / (BrE)
relating to or capable of movement or locomotion.
relating to movement or locomotion

Examples

The cheetah's locomotive abilities make it the fastest land animal.
Scientists are studying the locomotive systems of fish to design better underwater robots.
Certain diseases can impair the locomotive functions of the human body.

Etymology

From Latin 'locus' (place) + Modern Latin 'motivus' (serving to move).

2. NOUN

/ ˌloʊ.kəˈmoʊ.tɪv / (AmE), / ˌləʊ.kəˈməʊ.tɪv / (BrE)
a railway vehicle with its own engine that provides the power to move a train
railway engine that pulls trains

Examples

The museum displays a historic locomotive that once pulled passenger trains across the country.
Steam powered locomotives revolutionized railway transportation in the nineteenth century.
The engineer climbed aboard the locomotive to check its controls before departure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: engine - machine that converts energy into mechanical force or motion

Etymology

From Latin 'locomotivus', from 'locus' (place) + 'motivus' (of motion).
