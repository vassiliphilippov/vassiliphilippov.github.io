helpless

1. ADJ

/ ˈhɛlpləs / (AmE), / ˈhɛlplɪs / (BrE)
Unable to take action or protect oneself; lacking strength or power.
unable to act or help oneself

Examples

After the accident, she felt completely helpless and did not know what to do.
The kitten was helpless without its mother to care for it.
He became helpless with laughter, unable to catch his breath.

Synset Examples

lying ill and helpless
helpless with laughter

Related Terms

  • Synonym: powerless - lacking power or ability; unable to act
  • Antonym: strong - having physical power or ability; able to act

Etymology

From Middle English 'helpeles', from 'help' + '-less'.

2. ADJ

/ ˈhɛlpləs / (AmE), / ˈhɛlplɪs / (BrE)
unable to take care of oneself or do things without help; powerless or unable to act effectively
unable to help oneself or act effectively

Examples

She felt completely helpless after losing her keys in the city.
The puppies were helpless without their mother.
As the storm grew stronger, we watched helpless from inside the house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: powerless - lacking strength or ability to act
  • Antonym: empowered - having power or ability to act

Etymology

From Old English help (help) + -less (without).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01233489-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈhɛlpləs / (AmE), / ˈhɛlpləs / (BrE)
Unable to take care of oneself or act independently; lacking the ability to control or influence events.
unable to manage or act independently

Examples

After the accident, he felt completely helpless and unsure of what to do next.
The kittens were so young and helpless that they needed constant care.
Watching the storm destroy their home, they stood by, feeling utterly helpless.

Synset Examples

as helpless as a baby

Related Terms

  • Synonym: powerless - lacking power or ability to act
  • Antonym: capable - having the ability to do something

Etymology

From Old English 'help leas', meaning 'without help'.
