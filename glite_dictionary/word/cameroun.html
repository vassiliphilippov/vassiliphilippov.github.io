Cameroun

1. PROPN

/ kæməˈruːn / (AmE), / kæməˈruːn / (BrE)
A country in west-central Africa, officially known as the Republic of Cameroon; this spelling is often used in French and in some English texts, especially historical or diplomatic.
alternative spelling of Cameroon (African country)

Examples

The signing of the peace treaty took place in Cameroun in 1961.
She spent her childhood in Cameroun before moving to Europe.
The wildlife reserves in Cameroun are renowned for their diversity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Cameroon - the usual English spelling for the country in Central Africa

Etymology

From French 'Cameroun', itself from Portuguese 'Rio dos Camarões' (River of Prawns).
