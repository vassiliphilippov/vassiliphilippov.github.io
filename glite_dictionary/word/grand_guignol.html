Grand Guignol

1. NOUN

/ ɡrɑn ɡiːˈnjoʊl / (AmE), / ɡrɑ̃ ɡiːˈnjɒl / (BrE)
a dramatic entertainment or play featuring shocking, gruesome, or horrific scenes, often intended to thrill or horrify the audience
play with gruesome or horrific scenes

Examples

The theatre troupe's latest production was a true Grand Guignol, filled with gore and chilling surprises.
Some critics found the film's Grand Guignol elements excessive, but horror fans loved it.
He has a talent for turning everyday events into scenes worthy of Grand Guignol.

Etymology

From French 'Grand-Guignol', the name of a Parisian theatre famous for such plays, literally 'Big Puppet' or 'Big Doll'.
