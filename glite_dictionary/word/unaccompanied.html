unaccompanied

1. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.əˈkʌm.pə.nid / (AmE), / ˌʌn.əˈkʌm.pənid / (BrE)
(of music, singing, or performance) performed without musical accompaniment.
without musical accompaniment

Examples

The singer performed unaccompanied in a hauntingly beautiful rendition of the song.
He played the entire piece unaccompanied on his violin, astonishing the audience.
The choir sang unaccompanied during the moving finale.

Synset Examples

the soloist sang unaccompanied

Related Terms

  • Antonym: accompanied - performed with musical accompaniment

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) and 'accompanied', from Old French 'acompagnier', based on 'company'.

2. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.əˈkʌm.pə.nid / (AmE), / ˌʌn.əˈkʌm.pə.nid / (BrE)
(of a state or event) happening or existing without something else that might usually be present at the same time.
happening or existing without something else

Examples

The patient had a fever unaccompanied by any other symptoms.
She entered the room unaccompanied, drawing everyone's attention.
An increase in price unaccompanied by improved quality can frustrate customers.

Synset Examples

a headache unaccompanied by other symptoms

Related Terms

  • Antonym: accompanied - existing or happening together with something else

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'un-' (not) + 'accompanied' (from French 'accompagner', from 'compagnon' meaning companion).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02118766-s
Synset POS: s

without anyone or anything else; alone.
alone; without company

Examples

Synset Examples

Related Terms

4. ADJ

/ ˌʌn.əˈkʌm.pə.nid / (AmE), / ˌʌn.əˈkʌm.pə.nid / (BrE)
not having anyone with you; without an escort or companion.
without a companion or escort

Examples

She traveled unaccompanied to the city for the first time.
Children are not allowed to enter the theater unaccompanied by an adult.
The unaccompanied passenger waited quietly at the airport.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alone - having no one else present
  • Antonym: escorted - accompanied by a guide or protector

Etymology

From un- (not) + accompanied, past participle of accompany (from Old French acompaignier, from Latin ad- + companio meaning companion).
