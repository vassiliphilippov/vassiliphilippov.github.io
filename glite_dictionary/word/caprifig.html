caprifig

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæprɪˌfɪɡ / (AmE), / ˈkæprɪˌfɪɡ / (BrE)
a wild variety of the common fig, used to facilitate the pollination of cultivated fig varieties
wild fig variety aiding pollination

Examples

The grove included a single caprifig to ensure the pollination of the main crop.
Farmers sometimes plant caprifigs near their orchards to increase fig yields.
Without a caprifig, certain fig varieties will not produce edible fruit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fig - the fruit of the fig tree (Ficus carica)

Etymology

From Latin caprificus, from caper (goat) and ficus (fig).
