Pomolobus

1. NOUN

/ poʊˈmɑːloʊbəs / (AmE), / pɒˈmɒləbəs / (BrE)
a genus of North American river herrings, including species such as the alewife and the blueback herring
genus of North American river herrings

Examples

The genus Pomolobus includes several important species for commercial fishing.
Some biologists place the alewife in the genus Pomolobus rather than Alosa.
Taxonomic revisions have moved certain shad species in and out of Pomolobus over time.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alewife - a species of fish sometimes classified in Pomolobus

Etymology

From Greek pomolos (lid, cover) + lobos (lobe); scientific Latin formation.

2. NOUN

/ poʊˈmɑːloʊbəs / (AmE), / pɒˈmɒləbəs / (BrE)
A genus of North American river herrings, sometimes used to classify the alewife and related species.
a genus of North American river herrings

Examples

The alewife is sometimes classified in the genus Pomolobus by ichthyologists.
Several species in the genus Pomolobus are important to commercial fisheries.
Researchers debated whether to assign the blueback herring to Pomolobus or a different genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alewife - a species of herring sometimes included in Pomolobus

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek *pomē* (cover, lid) and *lobos* (lobe), referring to specific anatomical features of the fishes.
