itinerant

1. NOUN

/ aɪˈtɪnɚənt / (AmE), / aɪˈtɪnərənt / (BrE)
a person who travels from place to place, especially for work or business
person who travels for work

Examples

The region attracted many itinerants who found seasonal work on farms.
He lived as an itinerant for years, never settling in any one city.
During the Great Depression, countless itinerants traveled in search of jobs.

Synset Examples

itinerant traders

Related Terms

  • Synonym: traveler - person who travels from place to place
  • Antonym: settler - person who stays permanently in one place

Etymology

From Latin 'itinerans', present participle of 'itinero' (to travel), from 'iter' (journey).

2. ADJ

/ aɪˈtɪnɚənt / (AmE), / aɪˈtɪnərənt / (BrE)
traveling from place to place, especially for work or duty
traveling from place to place for work

Examples

The company hired several itinerant workers to help during harvest season.
As an itinerant musician, she performed in a different city every week.
During the 19th century, many itinerant preachers traveled across the countryside.

Synset Examples

itinerant labor
an itinerant judge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nomadic - moving from place to place rather than settling permanently
  • Antonym: settled - living in one place, not moving frequently

Etymology

From Latin 'iterare' (to travel over, repeat), from 'iter' (journey).

Synset POS: s
