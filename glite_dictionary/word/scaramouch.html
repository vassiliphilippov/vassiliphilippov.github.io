Scaramouch

1. NOUN

/ ˈskærəˌmuːtʃ / (AmE), / ˈskærəmʊtʃ / (BrE)
a stock character in Italian commedia dell'arte, depicted as a boastful but cowardly buffoon
boastful cowardly commedia dell'arte character

Examples

The actor played Scaramouch with exaggerated bravado and nervous gestures.
In literature, Scaramouch is often used to describe a boastful coward.
The pantomime included classic characters such as Harlequin and Scaramouch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Harlequin - comic servant character in commedia dell'arte
  • Synonym: Zanni - rustic clown character in commedia dell'arte

Etymology

From Italian 'Scaramuccia', a character name, meaning 'little skirmisher'.
