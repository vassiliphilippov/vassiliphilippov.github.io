aerodynamics

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛroʊdaɪˈnæmɪks / (AmE), / ˌeərəʊdaɪˈnæmɪks / (BrE)
the branch of science concerned with the properties of moving air and the interaction between the air and solid bodies moving through it
science of air movement and its effects

Examples

The engineers studied aerodynamics to improve the fuel efficiency of the new car.
Understanding aerodynamics is essential in the design of airplanes and rockets.
Cyclists wear tight suits to reduce drag and take advantage of better aerodynamics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hydrodynamics - the branch of science concerned with the motion of fluids, especially liquids

Etymology

From Greek 'aēr' (air) + 'dynamis' (power).
