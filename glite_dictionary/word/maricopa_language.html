Maricopa language

1. NOUN

/ ˌmærɪˈkoʊpə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌmærɪˈkəʊpə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the Yuman language historically spoken by the Maricopa and Halchidhoma peoples of Arizona, United States
Yuman language of the Maricopa people

Examples

Few people today are fluent speakers of the Maricopa language.
A new dictionary was published to help preserve the Maricopa language.
Linguists are studying the structure of the Maricopa language to document its unique grammar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Yuman - a family of languages spoken in the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico

Etymology

Named after the Maricopa people, indigenous to Arizona; 'language' from Latin 'lingua'.
