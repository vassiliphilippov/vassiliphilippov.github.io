Icteria virens

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈtɪriə vaɪˈrɛnz / (AmE), / ɪkˈtɪəriə vɪˈriːnz / (BrE)
A large American warbler, commonly known as the yellow-breasted chat, noted for its bright yellow breast and its ability to mimic the songs of other birds.
a large American warbler, yellow-breasted chat

Examples

Birdwatchers often look for the Icteria virens during spring migration in North America.
The Icteria virens is remarkable for its wide repertoire of imitated bird calls.
Researchers tracked the movements of Icteria virens to study habitat preferences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yellow-breasted chat - common name for Icteria virens

Etymology

From New Latin, Icteria from Greek ikteros (jaundice/yellow), virens from Latin (becoming green, greenish).
