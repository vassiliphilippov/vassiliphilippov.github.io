guff

1. NOUN

/ ɡʌf / (AmE), / ɡʌf / (BrE)
foolish talk or statements that are not true; nonsense, especially in a way that is not to be taken seriously
foolish or false talk; nonsense
informal

Examples

Don't give me any more guff—just answer the question.
He talks a lot of guff but never gets things done.
I'm tired of listening to their political guff on television.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nonsense - spoken or written words that have no meaning or make no sense
  • Antonym: truth - the quality or state of being true
