expectation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛkˌspɛkˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛk.spɛkˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A strong belief or assumption that something will happen or is likely to happen in the future.
belief about what will happen

Examples

The company's earnings report failed to meet investors' expectations.
Her high expectation of success motivated her to work harder.
When reality did not match his expectations, he felt disappointed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anticipation - expectancy or prediction, often with excitement
  • Degree Lower: hope - a feeling of desire for a particular outcome
  • Degree Higher: certainty - something sure or inevitable

Etymology

From Latin expectatio (an awaiting), from expectare (to expect).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɛkˌspɛkˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛk.spɛkˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A strong feeling or belief that something is about to happen, especially something positive or anticipated.
feeling that something will happen

Examples

As the concert began, a sense of expectation filled the air.
There is always an expectation of rain during the monsoon season.
Her heart was pounding with expectation as she waited for the results.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anticipation - a feeling of looking forward to something
  • Antonym: surprise - a feeling resulting from something unexpected happening

Etymology

From Latin 'expectatio', from 'expectare' meaning 'to await'.

3. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.spɛkˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛk.spekˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A strong belief that something will happen or be the case in the future, especially with confidence or anticipation.
belief that something will happen

Examples

Her expectation of getting the job was based on her impressive qualifications.
There was a general expectation that interest rates would rise soon.
He waited with great expectation for the results of the competition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anticipation - the action of anticipating something; expectation
  • Antonym: doubt - a feeling of uncertainty or lack of conviction

Etymology

From Latin 'expectatio' (an awaiting), from 'exspectare' (to look out for).

4. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.spɛkˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛk.spɛkˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
In statistics and probability, the mean or average value of a random variable, calculated as the sum of all possible values divided by their number.
mean value in statistics

Examples

The expectation of a fair die roll is 3.5.
You can calculate the expectation of a random variable by summing the products of possible values and their probabilities.
Statisticians often refer to the expectation as the mean or average in probability distributions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mean - the average value in a set of numbers

Etymology

From Latin *expectatio* (the act of waiting), adapted for mathematical use in probability.
