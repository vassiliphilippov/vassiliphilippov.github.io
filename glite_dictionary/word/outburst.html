outburst

1. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌbɝst / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌbɜːst / (BrE)
a sudden, strong expression of emotion, often uncontrolled
sudden, strong expression of emotion

Examples

There was an outburst of laughter when the comedian told his joke.
She apologized for her outburst during the meeting.
His sudden outburst of anger surprised everyone in the room.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eruption - a sudden, forceful expression or release (especially of feeling)

2. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌbɝst / (AmE), / ˈaʊtbɜːst / (BrE)
a sudden, strong occurrence of something, especially an emotion or a natural phenomenon
sudden intense occurrence

Examples

The sudden outburst of applause startled the speaker.
After a quiet day, there was an outburst of rain that caught everyone by surprise.
An outburst of laughter erupted from the back of the classroom.

Synset Examples

an outburst of heavy rain
a burst of lightning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eruption - a sudden, violent occurrence, especially of a volcano or emotion
  • Synonym: explosion - a violent and sudden burst, often with noise and force

Etymology

From out + burst, modeled after 'burst out.'

3. NOUN

/ ˈaʊtˌbɝst / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌbɜːst / (BrE)
a sudden and intense display of activity, emotion, or force, often marked by violence or excitement
a sudden violent or forceful disturbance

Examples

The volcano's outburst sent ash and smoke high into the sky.
There was an outburst of laughter after the comedian's unexpected joke.
An outburst of violence disrupted the otherwise peaceful protest.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eruption - a sudden, violent outpouring or outbreak
  • Antonym: calm - a period or state of tranquility and order

Etymology

From out + burst, dating from the early 19th century.
