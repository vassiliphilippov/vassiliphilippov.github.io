neutralised

1. ADJ

/ ˈnuːtrəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈnjuːtrəlaɪzd / (BrE)
made neutral in some way; deprived of distinctive characteristics or made ineffective.
made neutral or ineffective

Examples

After the acid was added, the solution was neutralised and became harmless.
The team's efforts were neutralised by the opponent's strong defense.
When a distinctive flavor is neutralised, the dish can taste quite bland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to neutralize - make neutral or ineffective

Etymology

From 'neutralise', from French 'neutraliser', from 'neutre' (neutral).
