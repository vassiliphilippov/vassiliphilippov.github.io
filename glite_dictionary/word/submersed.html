submersed

1. ADJ

/ səbˈmɝst / (AmE), / səbˈmɜːst / (BrE)
(of a plant or other organism) growing or remaining entirely under water.
growing under water

Examples

The biologist studied the submersed plants in the freshwater lake.
Many species of submersed vegetation provide habitat for fish and aquatic invertebrates.
After weeks of rain, parts of the forest floor became submersed beneath the rising river.

Synset Examples

viewing subaqueous fauna from a glass-bottomed boat
submerged leaves

Related Terms

  • Synonym: submerged - completely covered or hidden under water

Etymology

From Latin 'submersus', past participle of 'submergere' (to submerge).

2. ADJ

/ səbˈmɜrst / (AmE), / səbˈmɜːst / (BrE)
located or occurring below the surface of water; submerged.
beneath the surface of water

Examples

The lake contains many submersed plants that provide shelter for fish.
Signs warn swimmers about the presence of submersed rocks near the shore.
Research on submersed vegetation is crucial for understanding aquatic ecosystems.

Synset Examples

submerged rocks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: submerged - completely covered with or under water

Etymology

From Latin 'submersus', past participle of 'submergere' (to sink under).
