bifilar

1. ADJ

/ baɪˈfaɪlər / (AmE), / baɪˈfɪlə / (BrE)
having or using two filaments or wires, especially in scientific or technical contexts
having or using two filaments or wires

Examples

The engineer designed a bifilar coil to reduce electromagnetic interference.
A bifilar suspension was chosen for the sensitive measurement apparatus.
The experiment requires a bifilar wire arrangement to achieve the desired results.

Synset Examples

a bifilar suspension of a waving part of an instrument

Etymology

From Latin bi- (two) + filum (thread).
