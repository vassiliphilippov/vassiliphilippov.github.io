affectedness

1. NOUN

/ əˈfɛktɪdnəs / (AmE), / əˈfɛktɪdnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being artificial or pretentious in manner, speech, or appearance; a deliberate display meant to impress others.
artificial or pretentious manner

Examples

Her speech was full of affectedness, making it hard to believe her sincerity.
There is a certain affectedness in the way he draws out his words to impress the audience.
Critics accused the artist of affectedness rather than genuine creativity in her latest work.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretentiousness - the quality of trying to appear more important or clever than you are
  • Antonym: sincerity - the quality of being genuine, honest, or without pretense

Etymology

From 'affected' (meaning pretended, artificial) + '-ness', denoting a state or quality.

2. NOUN

/ əˈfɛktɪdnəs / (AmE), / əˈfɛktɪdnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being artificial, pretentious, or insincere, especially with the intention to impress others
artificial or pretentious behavior

Examples

Her affectedness at the party made it hard for anyone to take her seriously.
The critic accused the actor of affectedness in his performance, calling it unnatural.
Behind her elaborate manners was a clear sense of affectedness that some found off-putting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pretentiousness - the quality of trying to appear more important or impressive than is actually the case
  • Antonym: authenticity - the quality of being genuine or real

Etymology

From 'affected' (insincere or pretentious) + '-ness' (state or quality).

Synset POS: n
