vesicant

1. ADJ

/ ˈvɛsɪkənt / (AmE), / ˈvɛsɪkənt / (BrE)
(of a substance) causing blisters on the skin or mucous membrane.
causing blisters (medical, chemical)
medical

Examples

Exposure to the vesicant chemical resulted in painful blisters on the worker's skin.
Certain mustard gases are known to act as vesicant agents during chemical warfare.
The dermatologist explained that contact with the vesicant plant could cause severe skin reactions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blistering - causing blisters; forming blisters

Etymology

From Latin vesica (bladder, blister), via French vésicant.

2. NOUN

/ ˈvɛsɪkənt / (AmE), / ˈvɛsɪkənt / (BrE)
a substance that causes blistering of the skin or mucous membranes, especially used of certain chemical agents such as mustard gas
blister-causing chemical agent
medical

Examples

Exposure to the vesicant caused severe blisters on the worker's hands and arms.
Mustard gas is a notorious vesicant used during World War I combat.
Healthcare professionals must handle vesicant drugs with extreme caution to prevent accidental injury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blistering agent - a chemical that causes blisters on contact

Etymology

From Latin vesica (bladder, blister) plus -ant (agent noun suffix).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
