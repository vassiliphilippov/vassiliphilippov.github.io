Mussorgsky

1. PROPN

/ mʊˈsɔrɡski / (AmE), / mʊˈsɔːɡski / (BrE)
Modest Mussorgsky (1839–1881), a Russian composer known for his operas and orchestral works, including 'Pictures at an Exhibition' and 'Boris Godunov'.
Russian composer (1839–1881)

Examples

Mussorgsky composed some of the most innovative works in Russian classical music.
The opera 'Boris Godunov' by Mussorgsky is regularly performed worldwide.
Many pianists attempt to master Mussorgsky's 'Pictures at an Exhibition.'

Etymology

Russian family name, from Modest Petrovich Mussorgsky.
