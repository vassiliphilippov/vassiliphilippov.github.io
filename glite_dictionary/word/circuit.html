circuit

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɝː.kɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.kɪt / (BrE)
a regular route or series of events or venues that a group of people visit or participate in, especially over a period of time
a series of regular events or venues

Examples

She became well known on the jazz circuit in New York.
Many authors spend years traveling the literary circuit giving readings and talks.
The Formula 1 racing circuit attracts drivers from all over the world.

Synset Examples

she's a familiar name on the club circuit
on the lecture circuit
the judge makes a circuit of the courts in his district
the international tennis circuit

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tour - a journey for pleasure in which several places are visited

Etymology

From Latin circuitus (a going around), from circuire (to go around).

5. NOUN

/ ˈsɝː.kɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.kɪt / (BrE)
(law) One of the judicial divisions of a state or of the United States, originally so named because judges traveled (rode circuit) to hold court in different places; specifically, one of the twelve regions of the United States covered by a circuit court of appeals.
judicial division under a circuit court of appeals
legal

Examples

In the Ninth circuit, the court's decisions set precedent for several western U.S. states.
The case was appealed to the federal circuit covering their region.
Judges once traveled from town to town to preside over each circuit court session.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: court of appeals - a court that hears appeals from lower courts

Etymology

From Latin 'circuitus' (a going around), referring to the judges' travels around their district.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-08347441-n
Synset POS: n

6. NOUN

/ ˈsɝː.kɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.kɪt / (BrE)

electrical path or loop

Examples

The technician checked the circuit to find out why the light wasn't working.
If you break the circuit, the current will stop flowing immediately.
A short circuit can cause electrical fires if not repaired promptly.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: network - an interconnected system of wires or circuits

Etymology

From Latin 'circuitus' (a going around), from 'circum' (around) + 'ire' (to go).
