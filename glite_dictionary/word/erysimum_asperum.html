Erysimum asperum

1. NOUN

/ əˈrɪsɪməm ˈæspərəm / (AmE), / ɪˌrɪsɪməm ˈaspərəm / (BrE)
A biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to western North America, with orange-yellow flowers; also known as prairie rocket.
a prairie plant with orange-yellow flowers

Examples

Large swathes of the grassland were dotted with striking orange Erysimum asperum blooms.
Botanists identified the specimen as Erysimum asperum, native to the prairies of North America.
Gardeners value Erysimum asperum for its bright flowers and adaptability to dry soils.

Etymology

From New Latin, where 'Erysimum' is the genus name and 'asperum' means 'rough' in Latin.
