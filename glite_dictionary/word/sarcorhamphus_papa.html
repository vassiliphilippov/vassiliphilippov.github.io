Sarcorhamphus papa

1. NOUN

/ sɑrˌkɔrˈhæmfəs ˈpɑpə / (AmE), / sɑːkɔːˈhæmfəs ˈpɑːpə / (BrE)
A large black-and-white vulture, commonly known as the king vulture, found in South and Central America, distinguished by its colorful head and wattles.
king vulture species of Central and South America

Examples

The Sarcorhamphus papa is easily recognized by its striking head and neck coloration.
While exploring the rainforest, researchers spotted a Sarcorhamphus papa gliding overhead.
Unlike other vultures, the Sarcorhamphus papa has a unique combination of black, white, and orange plumage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: king vulture - common name for Sarcorhamphus papa, a large New World vulture

Etymology

From Greek 'sarx' (flesh), 'rhamphos' (beak), and Latin 'papa' (bishop, alluding to the vulture's colorful head resembling a bishop's robe).
