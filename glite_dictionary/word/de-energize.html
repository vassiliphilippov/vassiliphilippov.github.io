to de-energize

1. VERB

/ diˈɛn.ərˌdʒaɪz / (AmE), / diˈɛn.ə.dʒaɪz / (BrE)
to disconnect from a source of energy or power; to make powerless or inactive, especially in an electrical context
disconnect from power; make inactive

Examples

Before performing maintenance, always de-energize the equipment to ensure safety.
The technician de-energized the circuit before starting repairs on the panel.
It's important to de-energize the system to prevent accidental electric shocks.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to energize - to supply with energy or power

Etymology

Formed from the prefix de- (removal) and energize (to supply with energy).
