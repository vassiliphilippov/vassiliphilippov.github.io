effortlessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛfərtləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈɛfətləsnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being easy to do or requiring little or no effort.
quality of requiring little effort

Examples

Her dance had such effortlessness that it looked almost magical.
He spoke with a effortlessness that made the language seem easy to learn.
The effortlessness of his running style impressed the entire team.

Synset Examples

such effortlessness is achieved only after hours of practice

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ease - absence of difficulty or effort
  • Antonym: difficulty - the quality of requiring a lot of effort

Etymology

From 'effortless' + '-ness', where 'effortless' derives from 'effort' (from Old French 'esforz', from Latin 'exfortiare', to strengthen).
