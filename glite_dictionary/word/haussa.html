Haussa

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaʊsə / (AmE), / ˈhaʊsə / (BrE)
the chief member of the Chadic family of Afroasiatic languages, spoken in West Africa and widely used as a trading language
a major West African language

Examples

Many merchants in Nigeria speak Haussa to communicate with traders from different regions.
She studied Haussa at university to research West African linguistics.
The movie was subtitled in Haussa for a broader audience in northern Nigeria.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Hausa - the most common spelling for the language and people

Etymology

From the native word 'Hausā' (Hausa language; name of the people).
