squid

1. NOUN

/ skwɪd / (AmE), / skwɪd / (BrE)
a fast-moving marine animal with a long, soft body, ten arms, and triangular fins, belonging to the cephalopod mollusks
marine animal with ten arms

Examples

Fishermen often catch squid at night using bright lights to attract them.
The chef prepared a delicious dish of grilled squid with lemon.
Some species of squid can change color rapidly to blend in with their surroundings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: octopus - marine animal with eight arms
  • Synonym: cuttlefish - marine mollusk similar to squid and octopus

Etymology

Perhaps from Italian *squido* or from Old Norse *squid* (a cuttlefish), but origin is uncertain.

2. NOUN

/ skwɪd / (AmE), / skwɪd / (BrE)
The flesh of a marine cephalopod, often served as food, especially in cuisines such as Italian.
edible marine cephalopod

Examples

Many Italian restaurants serve squid fried or grilled as an appetizer.
She ordered stuffed squid with tomato sauce for her main course.
Fresh squid is popular in Mediterranean cooking and is often served as calamari.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: calamari - squid when prepared as food, especially in Italian cuisine
