Hubbard

1. PROPN

/ ˈhʌbərd / (AmE), / ˈhʌbəd / (BrE)
a mountain peak in southeastern Alaska, part of the Coast Range, standing 14,950 feet (4,557 meters) high
peak in southeastern Alaska, part of Coast Range

Examples

Mount Hubbard is renowned for its steep slopes and remote location on the Alaska-Yukon border.
Climbers face severe weather conditions when attempting to reach the summit of Hubbard.
From the air, the snow-capped peak of Hubbard stands out against the surrounding wilderness.
