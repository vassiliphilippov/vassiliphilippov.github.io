pitch-black

1. ADJ

/ pɪtʃ blæk / (AmE), / pɪtʃ blæk / (BrE)
completely or intensely dark; having no light at all.
extremely dark; completely black

Examples

She couldn't see her hand in front of her face in the pitch-black room.
The tunnel was pitch-black except for the faint glow of his flashlight.
The night was pitch-black after the storm knocked out all the streetlights.

Synset Examples

a black moonless night
through the pitch-black woods
it was pitch-dark in the cellar

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitch-dark - completely or intensely dark
  • Synonym: jet-black - completely black in color or very dark

Etymology

The term 'pitch' refers to a thick, black, tar-like substance; 'pitch-black' draws a comparison to its darkness.
