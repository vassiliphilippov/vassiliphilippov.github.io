Boehmenism

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊmənˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈbəʊmənˌɪzəm / (BrE)
the mystical theological doctrine of Jakob Boehme, emphasizing direct personal experience of God and influencing certain Christian movements, such as the Quakers.
theological doctrine of Jakob Boehme

Examples

The influence of Boehmenism can be traced in early Quaker writings and spiritual practices.
Some scholars regard Boehmenism as a bridge between medieval mysticism and modern Protestant thought.
His fascination with Boehmenism led him to study the works of Jakob Boehme extensively.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mysticism - belief in direct knowledge of spiritual truth through experience
  • Degree Lower: Quakerism - the beliefs and practices of the Quaker religious movement

Etymology

From the name of Jakob Boehme (1575–1624), German Christian mystic.
