Asplenium pinnatifidum

1. NOUN

/ æsˈpliːniəm pɪˌnætɪˈfaɪdəm / (AmE), / æsˈpliːniəm pɪˌnatɪˈfɪdəm / (BrE)
A species of spleenwort fern native to eastern and southern regions of the United States, known for its deeply lobed leaves.
a species of spleenwort fern

Examples

The shady slopes of the Appalachian Mountains are home to Asplenium pinnatifidum.
Botanists sometimes refer to Asplenium pinnatifidum as the 'lobed spleenwort'.
Gardeners value Asplenium pinnatifidum for its delicate and unique fronds.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spleenwort - any fern of the genus Asplenium

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: 'Asplenium' (the fern genus), and Latin 'pinnatifidum', meaning 'with pinnately divided leaves'.
