credential

1. NOUN

/ krəˈdɛnʃəl / (AmE), / krɪˈdɛnʃəl / (BrE)
an official document that proves a person's qualifications, identity, or status
document proving qualifications or status

Examples

The doctor displayed his credentials on the wall of his office.
Applicants must submit their academic credentials with their application forms.
Her impressive credentials made her the top choice for the position.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: certificate - an official document attesting to a fact, such as completion of a course
  • Synonym: diploma - an official document certifying the completion of a course or program
  • Synonym: identification - the process or document that proves a person's identity

Etymology

From Medieval Latin 'credentiale', from Latin 'credentia' (belief, trust).
