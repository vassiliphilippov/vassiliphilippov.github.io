Oxyura

1. NOUN

/ ɑkˈsaɪʊrə / (AmE), / ɒkˈsʌɪʊərə / (BrE)
A genus of stiff-tailed ducks, including the ruddy duck and its relatives, found in the Americas and elsewhere.
genus of stiff-tailed ducks

Examples

The Oxyura genus is known for its distinctive stiff tail feathers.
Researchers observed several species within the Oxyura genus at the wetland reserve.
Birdwatchers often hope to catch a glimpse of the rare Oxyura during migration season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ruddy duck - a species of North American duck with a stiff tail, belonging to the genus Oxyura

Etymology

From Greek oxys (sharp) + oura (tail), referring to the stiff tail feathers.
