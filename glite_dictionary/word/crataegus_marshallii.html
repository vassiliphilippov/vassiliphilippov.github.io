Crataegus marshallii

1. NOUN

/ krəˈtiːɡəs mɑrˈʃæliː / (AmE), / krəˈtiːɡəs mɑːˈʃæliː / (BrE)
A species of hawthorn native to the southern United States, characterized by its pinnately lobed leaves.
species of hawthorn tree

Examples

The Crataegus marshallii is easily recognized by its deeply lobed leaves and small white flowers.
Botanists often study Crataegus marshallii to understand the diversity of North American hawthorns.
In southern gardens, Crataegus marshallii is valued for its ornamental appearance and resilience.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hawthorn - a thorny shrub or tree belonging to the genus Crataegus

Etymology

From Latin Crataegus (the genus name for hawthorns), named after botanist Humphry Marshall.
