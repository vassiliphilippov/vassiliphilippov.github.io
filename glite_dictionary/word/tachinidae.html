Tachinidae

1. NOUN

/ təˈkɪnɪdiː / (AmE), / təˈkɪnɪdiː / (BrE)
A large family of flies whose larvae are internal parasites of other insects.
family of parasitic flies

Examples

The larvae of Tachinidae play a crucial role in controlling caterpillar populations in agriculture.
Scientists studied several species within the Tachinidae family to better understand insect parasitism.
Many gardeners welcome Tachinidae because they help reduce pests naturally.

Etymology

From modern Latin *Tachinidae*, based on Greek *tachinos* (swift), the type genus being *Tachina*.

2. NOUN

/ təˈkɪnəˌdiː / (AmE), / takɪˈnɪːdiː / (BrE)
a large family of parasitic flies whose larvae develop inside other insects, often used in biological control.
family of parasitic flies

Examples

The larvae of Tachinidae are important natural enemies of many pest insects.
Many species in the Tachinidae family are used as biological control agents.
Researchers studied the impact of Tachinidae on the population of caterpillar pests.

Etymology

From Modern Latin Tachinidae, from the type genus Tachina.
