procedure

1. NOUN

/ prəˈsidʒɚ / (AmE), / prəˈsiːdʒə / (BrE)
a series of actions conducted in a certain order or manner, especially in relation to practical or mechanical work
series of actions or steps

Examples

The company updated its safety procedure for handling hazardous materials.
Learning the correct procedure is essential when assembling the machine.
He followed the maintenance procedure step by step to repair the equipment.

Synset Examples

the operations in building a house
certain machine tool operations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: process - a series of actions or steps taken to achieve a particular end

Etymology

From French 'procédure', from Latin 'procedere' (to go forward)

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00578105-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ proʊˈsiːdʒər / (AmE), / prəˈsiːdʒə / (BrE)
a series of actions that are done in a certain way or order to achieve a particular result
a course of action to achieve a result

Examples

The application procedure for the grant is quite straightforward.
She carefully explained each step of the safety procedure before we began.
Following the correct procedure helps ensure accuracy and consistency in the laboratory.

Synset Examples

the procedure of obtaining a driver's license
it was a process of trial and error

Related Terms

  • Synonym: process - a series of actions taken to achieve an end
  • Synonym: method - a particular way of doing something

Etymology

From French 'procédure', from Old French 'proceder' (to proceed), from Latin 'procedere' (to go forward).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01025762-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ prəˈsidʒɚ / (AmE), / prəˈsiːdʒə / (BrE)
a set of instructions or operations in a computer program designed to perform a specific task
set of instructions in programming

Examples

The print procedure handles all output to the screen in this program.
You must call the initialization procedure before accessing the database.
Each procedure in the code is responsible for a different task.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: function - a block of code that performs a specific task in programming
  • Synonym: subroutine - a sequence of program instructions that perform a specific task, packaged as a unit

Etymology

From Latin 'procedere' (to go forward), through Old French and Middle English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06594579-n
Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ prəˈsidʒɚ / (AmE), / prəˈsiːdʒə / (BrE)
an established or official way of conducting legal or parliamentary actions and proceedings
official way of conducting legal/parliamentary actions

Examples

The lawyer explained the court procedure to the defendant before the trial began.
Strict procedure must be followed when voting on new legislation in parliament.
Any deviation from established procedure can lead to the dismissal of the case.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: process - a series of actions or steps taken to achieve a result

Etymology

From French 'procédure', from 'procéder' (to proceed), from Latin 'procedere'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01025578-n
