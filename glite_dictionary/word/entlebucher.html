EntleBucher

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛntləbjuːkər / (AmE), / ˈɛntləbjuːkə / (BrE)
A breed of small, sturdy working dog from Switzerland, recognized as the smallest of the four Sennenhund breeds.
Swiss working dog breed

Examples

The EntleBucher is known for its agility and intelligence, making it a great herding dog.
She adopted an EntleBucher from a Swiss rescue organization last year.
Many people admire the striking tri-color coat of the EntleBucher.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Appenzeller - Sennenhund breed slightly larger than the EntleBucher
  • Degree Higher: Bernese Mountain Dog - Largest of the Sennenhund breeds

Etymology

From German 'Entlebucher', meaning 'from Entlebuch', a region in Switzerland.
