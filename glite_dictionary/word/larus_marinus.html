Larus marinus

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛɹəs məˈɹaɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈlɑːrəs məˈriːnəs / (BrE)
the great black-backed gull, a large white gull with a black back and wings, found along the coasts of the North Atlantic
great black-backed gull species

Examples

A Larus marinus was spotted on the rocky coastline, towering over other gulls.
The Larus marinus is known for its powerful build and aggressive behavior toward other seabirds.
Birdwatchers traveled to the bay to observe the rare sighting of a Larus marinus.

Etymology

From Latin: 'larus' meaning gull and 'marinus' meaning of the sea.
