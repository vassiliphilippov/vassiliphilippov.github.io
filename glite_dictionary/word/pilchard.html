pilchard

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɪl.tʃərd / (AmE), / ˈpɪl.tʃəd / (BrE)
A small, oily fish of the herring family, found in large schools along the coasts of Europe, smaller and rounder than the common herring.
a small, oily European fish related to the herring

Examples

Canned pilchard is a popular ingredient in Mediterranean cuisine.
Fishermen caught a large school of pilchards off the Cornish coast.
The diet of the local seals includes pilchard and other small fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herring - an edible fish of the family Clupeidae, closely related to the pilchard

Etymology

From Middle English pilcherd, perhaps from Cornish pilcherd or related to French pilchard.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɪl.tʃərd / (AmE), / ˈpɪl.tʃɑːd / (BrE)
a small, oily fish of the herring family, often canned for food
small oily fish, often canned

Examples

Canned pilchard is a staple food in many parts of the world.
Fishermen hauled in nets filled with shimmering pilchards early this morning.
The recipe calls for smoked pilchard fillets served with a lemon dressing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sardine - small, oily fish in the herring family, often canned

Etymology

From Old French *pilchard*, of uncertain origin, possibly from an Italian dialect.
