pessimism

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɛsɪˌmɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈpɛsɪmɪzəm / (BrE)
the tendency to see the worst aspect of things or believe that the worst will happen
belief that things will turn out badly

Examples

Her pessimism about the economy made her hesitant to invest.
A wave of pessimism spread through the team after several defeats.
Despite his pessimism, the project was completed successfully.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: optimism - the tendency to be hopeful and confident about the future

Etymology

From French pessimisme, from Latin pessimus (worst).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpɛsɪˌmɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈpɛsɪmɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the tendency to see the worst aspect of things or believe that the worst will happen
expectation of negative outcomes

Examples

Her pessimism made it difficult for her to enjoy good news, as she always expected something to go wrong.
A sense of pessimism spread throughout the company after the disappointing sales results were announced.
Although pessimism can sometimes help you prepare for challenges, excessive negativity may prevent you from taking risks.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: optimism - the tendency to be hopeful and expect positive outcomes

Etymology

From Latin 'pessimus' (worst) + '-ism' (doctrine or practice).
