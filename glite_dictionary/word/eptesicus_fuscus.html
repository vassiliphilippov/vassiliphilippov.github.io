Eptesicus fuscus

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛptəˈsikus ˈfʌskəs / (AmE), / ˌɛptɪˈsiːkəs ˈfʌskəs / (BrE)
a rather large North American brown bat widely distributed across the continent; also known as the big brown bat
large North American brown bat species

Examples

The Eptesicus fuscus is commonly found roosting in attics and barns across North America.
Researchers studied the diet of Eptesicus fuscus to better understand its role in controlling insect populations.
At dusk, you can often see Eptesicus fuscus flying over fields in search of food.

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Eptesicus' and species name 'fuscus' meaning 'dark' or 'brown'.
