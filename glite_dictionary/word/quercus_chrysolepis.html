Quercus chrysolepis

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɜrkəs ˌkraɪsoʊˈlɛpɪs / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs ˌkraɪsəʊˈlɛpɪs / (BrE)
A medium-sized evergreen oak tree native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico, with oblong, leathery, often spiny-edged leaves.
species of evergreen oak tree

Examples

The leaves of the Quercus chrysolepis are thick and often have spiny edges.
Researchers studied the distribution of Quercus chrysolepis in the canyon ecosystem.
The acorns of Quercus chrysolepis provide food for many animals in the region.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Quercus' meaning 'oak'; 'chrysolepis' from Greek 'chryso-' (gold) and 'lepis' (scale), referring to the golden scales on its acorn cups.
