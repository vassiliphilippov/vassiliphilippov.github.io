impetuousness

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈpɛtʃuəsnəs / (AmE), / ɪmˈpetjuəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of acting or doing something quickly and without thought or care; rash impulsiveness
rash impulsiveness

Examples

Her impetuousness often got her into trouble at work, as she rarely paused to consider the consequences.
The impetuousness of youth sometimes leads to bold but risky decisions.
He regretted his impetuousness after realizing he had spoken without thinking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impulsiveness - the tendency to act without forethought
  • Antonym: caution - careful consideration before action

Etymology

From late Middle English, based on Old French 'impetueux', from Latin 'impetuosus' (violent, forceful).
