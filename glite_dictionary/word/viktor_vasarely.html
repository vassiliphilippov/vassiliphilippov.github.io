Viktor Vasarely

1. PROPN

/ ˈvɪktɔr vəˈsɑːrəli / (AmE), / ˈvɪktɔː ˌvasaːˈreli / (BrE)
(1908–1997) A French painter, born in Hungary, known as a pioneer of op art, noted for his abstract geometric patterns and optical illusions.
French-Hungarian op art painter (1908–1997)

Examples

Viktor Vasarely is often called the father of op art due to his innovative use of geometric forms.
The museum featured an exhibition dedicated to the works of Viktor Vasarely.
Art students study Viktor Vasarely to understand the development of optical illusion in modern art.
