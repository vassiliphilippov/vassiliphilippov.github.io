conniving

1. ADJ

/ kəˈnaɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / kəˈnaɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
Involved in secretly planning to do something dishonest, illegal, or harmful; scheming or plotting with others for a wrongful purpose.
secretly plotting something wrong

Examples

The conniving employees plotted to embezzle funds from the company.
She played the role of a conniving villain who deceived everyone around her.
Despite his conniving ways, no one suspected him of the betrayal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scheming - making secret or devious plans
  • Antonym: honest - acting or being truthful and morally upright

Etymology

Present participle of 'connive', from Latin 'connivere' (to wink), adopted into English in the early 17th century.

2. ADJ

/ kəˈnaɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / kəˈnaɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
behaving in a scheming, cunning, or secretly cooperative way to achieve a personal goal, usually dishonestly or at the expense of others
scheming or manipulative behavior

Examples

The conniving manager manipulated his team to get all the credit for their work.
She played a conniving character who always plotted against her rivals in the novel.
His conniving ways eventually caught up with him when his deceptions were exposed.

Synset Examples

the most calculating and selfish men in the community

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scheming - planning or acting in a secret or dishonest way
  • Antonym: honest - free of deceit and untruthfulness; sincere

Etymology

Present participle of 'connive', from Latin 'connivēre' (to shut the eyes, to wink).
