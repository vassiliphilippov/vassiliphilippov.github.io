wriggly

1. ADJ

/ ˈrɪɡli / (AmE), / ˈrɪɡli / (BrE)
moving with twisting, turning, or squirming motions, like a worm or snake
twisting or squirming in motion

Examples

The child tried to hold the wriggly fish, but it slipped from her hands.
A wriggly puppy can be difficult to bathe properly.
He found a wriggly worm in the garden soil.

Synset Examples

wiggly worms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: squirmy - moving in a squirming or twisting way
  • Synonym: wiggly - moving or shaped with small twisting motions

Etymology

From the verb 'wriggle' (to twist or squirm), formed with the suffix '-y'.
