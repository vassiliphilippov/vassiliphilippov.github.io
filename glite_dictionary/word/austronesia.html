Austronesia

1. PROPN

/ ˌɔːstroʊˈniːʒə / (AmE), / ˌɔːstrəʊˈniːzɪə / (BrE)
A region comprising the islands of the central and South Pacific, including Indonesia, Melanesia, Micronesia, and Polynesia.
region of Pacific islands

Examples

The languages spoken in Austronesia are remarkably diverse, with hundreds of distinct dialects.
Many anthropologists study the migration patterns of peoples throughout Austronesia.
Austronesia includes some of the most remote inhabited islands on Earth.

Etymology

Formed from 'Austro-' (south) and Greek 'nēsos' (island), originally used in linguistic and anthropological studies.
