Hsuan Chiao

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃwɑːn ˈtʃiːaʊ / (AmE), / ˈʃwæn ˈtʃaɪ.əʊ / (BrE)
A popular Chinese philosophical and religious system based on the teachings of Lao-tzu, characterized by belief in many gods, alchemy, divination, and magic.
Chinese philosophical and religious system (Taoism)

Examples

Many scholars study Hsuan Chiao to understand traditional Chinese beliefs and practices.
The principles of Hsuan Chiao influence Chinese art, medicine, and philosophy even today.
Alchemical experiments were a significant part of Hsuan Chiao's religious practices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Taoism - Chinese religious and philosophical tradition based on the teachings of Lao-tzu

Etymology

From Mandarin 玄教 (Xuán jiào), meaning 'mysterious teaching'; another name for Taoism.
