drool

1. NOUN

/ druːl / (AmE), / druːl / (BrE)
saliva that dribbles or spills from the mouth, often unintentionally
saliva spilling from the mouth

Examples

The baby wiped the drool from her chin with a small bib.
The dog left a trail of drool on the kitchen floor after his meal.
He was embarrassed by the drool that escaped during his nap.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saliva - the watery liquid secreted into the mouth
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ druːl / (AmE), / druːl / (BrE)
to let saliva flow from the mouth, usually unintentionally
let saliva flow from the mouth

Examples

The baby started to drool while he was teething.
The dog would drool at the sight of food.
She tried not to drool during her nap on the couch.

Synset Examples

The baby drooled

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dribble - to let saliva or liquid flow in small drops

3. VERB

/ druːl / (AmE), / druːl / (BrE)
(informal) To show strong desire or eagerness for something, often in a way that is obvious or exaggerated.
show strong desire or eagerness for something
informal

Examples

I can't help but drool over the latest gadgets in the store.
The children drooled at the sight of the enormous chocolate cake.
He was practically drooling with envy when his friend showed off the new car.

Synset Examples

She was salivating over the raise she anticipated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to salivate - to show eager anticipation for something, often used figuratively
  • Synonym: to covet - to long for something that belongs to someone else
