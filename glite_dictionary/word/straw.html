straw

1. NOUN

/ strɔ / (AmE), / strɔː / (BrE)
a thin tube of paper or plastic for sucking liquids into the mouth
thin tube for drinking

Examples

She sipped her lemonade through a straw to avoid spilling it.
After the party, dozens of plastic straws were left on the table.
Many restaurants now offer paper straws instead of plastic ones for environmental reasons.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: drinking straw - a tube for transferring a beverage from its container to the mouth

Etymology

From Old English strēaw (stalk of grain), later extended to mean drinking tube due to resemblance.

2. VERB

/ strɔ / (AmE), / strɔː / (BrE)
to cover or provide with straw, especially as bedding for animals
cover or provide with straw

Examples

After cleaning the barn, she carefully strawed the stalls for the horses.
The farmer straws the chicken coop every winter to keep the hens warm.
During the blizzard, extra effort was made to straw the animals' shelters.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

3. NOUN

/ strɔ / (AmE), / strɔː / (BrE)
dried stalks of cereal plants, used as bedding for animals or as material for weaving, that remain after the grain and chaff have been removed.
plant stalks used for bedding or weaving

Examples

The farmer piled the straw in the barn to use as bedding for the animals.
A woven basket made of straw sat on the kitchen table.
After the harvest, fields are often left with scattered straw on the ground.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hay - dried grass used as animal fodder

Etymology

From Old English 'streaw', related to 'strew' from Proto-Germanic *strawam.
