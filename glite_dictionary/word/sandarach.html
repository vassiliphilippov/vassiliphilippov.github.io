sandarach

1. NOUN

/ ˈsændəˌræk / (AmE), / ˈsændəræk / (BrE)
a brittle, faintly aromatic yellow or red resin obtained from certain North African trees, used chiefly in varnishes and lacquers
aromatic resin used in varnishes

Examples

The cabinetmaker used sandarach to prepare a traditional varnish for the antique desk.
Artists in the Renaissance often mixed sandarach with oil to make a durable finish.
The faint, pleasant aroma of sandarach filled the workshop as the resin was heated.

Etymology

From Arabic *ṣandarus*, from Medieval Latin *sandaracha*, originally from Greek *sandarakē* (red lead, later the resin).
