sticklerism

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪklɚˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈstɪkləˌɪzəm / (BrE)
The practice or attitude of being excessively strict or inflexible in adhering to rules or conventions.
excessive strictness about rules

Examples

Her sticklerism made group projects challenging, as she would not allow any deviation from the plan.
A culture of sticklerism can stifle creativity and innovation in the workplace.
Despite his sticklerism, he was admired for his commitment to fairness and accuracy.

Synset Examples

‘We, the intellectually curious, may soon find ourselves trapped in a pen, fenced in by rule-bound sticklerism and overzealous concern for our personal safety, unless we exercise our civil liberties and our curiosity,’ he declaims.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pedantry - excessive concern with minor details or rules

Etymology

From 'stickler' (one who insists on something) + '-ism'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstɪkləˌrɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈstɪklərɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
Strict insistence on or adherence to established rules or standards, especially in matters of grammar or correctness.
strict observance of rules or standards

Examples

His sticklerism for grammar often gets on people's nerves during group projects.
She admired his sticklerism when it came to upholding academic standards.
Despite his sticklerism, he sometimes overlooked minor spelling errors.

Synset Examples

His sticklerism for grammar often annoys his friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pedantry - excessive concern with minor rules and details

Etymology

From 'stickler' (one who insists on something) + '-ism' (state or practice).
