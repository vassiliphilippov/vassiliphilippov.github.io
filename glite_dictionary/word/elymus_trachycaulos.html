Elymus trachycaulos

1. NOUN

/ ˈiːlɪməs ˌtrækiˈkɔːləs / (AmE), / ˈiːlɪməs ˌtrækiˈkɔːləs / (BrE)
A North American grass species cultivated in the western United States as a forage crop.
species of forage grass

Examples

Elymus trachycaulos thrives in dry climates and is a staple forage crop on many western ranches.
Researchers are evaluating Elymus trachycaulos for its potential to restore native grasslands.
The distinctive seed head of Elymus trachycaulos helps farmers identify the grass in the field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wildrye - common name for grasses in the Elymus genus

Etymology

From genus Elymus (Latin, a kind of wild wheat) and species name trachycaulos from Greek 'trachys' (rough) and 'kaulos' (stem).
