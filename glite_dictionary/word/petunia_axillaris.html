Petunia axillaris

1. NOUN

/ pəˈtuniə ækˈsɪlərɪs / (AmE), / pɪˈtjuːnɪə ækˈsɪlɪərɪs / (BrE)
a species of annual herb with large, fragrant white flowers that bloom at night, commonly found in South America
a white-flowered night-blooming petunia species

Examples

The Petunia axillaris fills the evening air with its sweet scent as the white flowers open at dusk.
Gardeners value Petunia axillaris for its large, night-blooming blossoms and ease of cultivation.
Researchers study Petunia axillaris to understand flower fragrance and pollination by nocturnal insects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: petunia - a genus of flowering plants with brightly colored funnel-shaped flowers

Etymology

From Latin; 'Petunia' is from an indigenous South American word for tobacco; 'axillaris' means 'of the axil' (the angle between stem and leaf) in Latin.
