blowhole

1. NOUN

/ ˈbloʊˌhoʊl / (AmE), / ˈbləʊˌhəʊl / (BrE)
the nostril or spiracle on the top of the head of a whale, dolphin, or other cetacean, used for breathing
nostril or breathing hole of cetaceans

Examples

A whale surfaces and exhales forcefully through its blowhole, creating a spout of mist.
Marine biologists study the shape and function of the dolphin's blowhole.
The blowhole closes tightly when the whale dives underwater.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nostril - either of the two external openings of the nose

2. NOUN

/ ˈbloʊˌhoʊl / (AmE), / ˈbləʊˌhəʊl / (BrE)
a hole through which gas or air is released, especially in rock or metal
hole for escape of gas or air

Examples

A blowhole in the rock allowed steam to escape from deep within the earth.
Engineers discovered a blowhole in the metal casting, indicating a flaw in the manufacturing process.
Small blowholes formed naturally along the coastline, releasing bursts of air with each crashing wave.

Etymology

From 'blow' (to expel air) + 'hole'.
