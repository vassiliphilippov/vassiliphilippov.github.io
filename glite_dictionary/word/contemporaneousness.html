contemporaneousness

1. NOUN

/ kənˌtɛmpəˈreɪniəsnəs / (AmE), / kənˌtɛmpəˈreɪniəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being contemporary or existing at the same time as something else
quality of being contemporary or current

Examples

The contemporaneousness of the art exhibition drew visitors interested in modern trends.
Historians debated the contemporaneousness of the two settlements based on archaeological findings.
Her writing has a distinct contemporaneousness that resonates with present-day readers.

Synset Examples

a shopping mall would instill a spirit of modernity into this village

Related Terms

  • Synonym: modernity - the quality or condition of being modern

Etymology

From contemporaneous + -ness, with 'contemporaneous' from Latin 'contemporaneus' (of the same time).

2. NOUN

/ kənˌtɛm.pəˈreɪ.ni.əs.nəs / (AmE), / kənˌtɛm.pəˈreɪ.ni.əs.nəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of existing or occurring in the same period of time as something else
the state of being in the same time period

Examples

The contemporaneousness of the two scientific discoveries sparked a debate over originality.
Art historians study the contemporaneousness of different artistic movements to understand their influences.
It's remarkable that the fossils show contemporaneousness despite being found on different continents.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: simultaneity - the fact of two things happening at the same time

Etymology

From 'contemporaneous' + '-ness'; 'contemporaneous' from Latin 'contemporaneus' (with time).
