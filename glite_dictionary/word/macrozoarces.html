Macrozoarces

1. NOUN

/ ˌmækrəʊˈzoʊɑːrˌsiːz / (AmE), / ˌmakrəʊˈzəʊɑːsiːz / (BrE)
A genus of marine fish in the family Zoarcidae, commonly known as eelpouts.
genus of marine eelpouts

Examples

Researchers discovered a new species within the genus Macrozoarces in the North Pacific.
The Macrozoarces are notable for their elongated bodies and soft fins.
Marine biologists study Macrozoarces to understand adaptations to cold ocean environments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Zoarcidae - the eelpout family of fishes

Etymology

From Greek 'makros' (large) + 'Zoarces' (a related fish genus).

2. NOUN

/ ˌmækrəʊˈzɔːrsiːz / (AmE), / ˌmakrəʊˈzɔːsiːz / (BrE)
A genus of marine fishes in the family Zoarcidae, known as eelpouts, found in the northwestern Pacific Ocean.
a genus of marine fishes (eelpouts)

Examples

The Macrozoarces genus includes several eel-like fish species native to cold Pacific waters.
Researchers collected specimens of Macrozoarces for their marine biodiversity survey.
Macrozoarces species display unique adaptations to life in deep, cold environments.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Zoarcidae - the family of eelpouts, marine ray-finned fishes

Etymology

From Greek makros (large) + zoarces (life-giver, genus name in Greek).
