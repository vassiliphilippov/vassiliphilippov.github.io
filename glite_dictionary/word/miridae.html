Miridae

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɪrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈmɪrɪdiː / (BrE)
a large family of insects, commonly known as leaf bugs, that feed on plant sap and are found worldwide
family of leaf bugs (insects)

Examples

Scientists collected several specimens of Miridae in the meadow for their study.
Many species in the Miridae family are important agricultural pests.
The diversity of Miridae makes them a fascinating subject for entomologists.

Etymology

From Modern Latin Miridae, the family name, derived from the genus Miris.
