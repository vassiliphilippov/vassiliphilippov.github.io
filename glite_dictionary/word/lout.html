lout

1. NOUN

/ laʊt / (AmE), / laʊt / (BrE)
a rough, awkward, or stupid person, often lacking in manners or social grace
rough, awkward, or stupid person

Examples

He acted like a complete lout at the dinner party, upsetting everyone with his rude jokes.
The coach warned the team not to behave like louts during the ceremony.
Despite his large size, he was no lout, but rather a gentle and thoughtful young man.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oaf - a clumsy or unintelligent person
  • Synonym: boor - an unrefined, ill-mannered person
  • Antonym: gentleman - a courteous, refined man

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English *louten* (to bow, stoop), influenced by Old Norse *lútr* (bent, stooping).
