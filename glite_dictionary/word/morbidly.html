morbidly

1. ADV

/ ˈmɔrbɪdli / (AmE), / ˈmɔːbɪdli / (BrE)
In a manner that shows an unhealthy interest in disturbing or unpleasant subjects, especially death or disease.
in a morbid or unhealthy way

Examples

She was morbidly fascinated by stories of unsolved murders.
He became morbidly obsessed with the idea of his own mortality.
The documentary explored the morbidly curious side of human nature.

Synset Examples

he was morbidly fascinated by dead bodies

Related Terms

  • Synonym: morbid - having an unhealthy interest in disturbing subjects, especially death

Etymology

From 'morbid' + '-ly'; 'morbid' from Latin 'morbidus' (diseased).
