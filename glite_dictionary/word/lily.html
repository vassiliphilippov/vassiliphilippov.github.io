lily

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪli / (AmE), / ˈlɪli / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Lilium, known for its large, showy, bell-shaped or pendulous flowers, typically grown as ornamentals.
a flowering plant of the genus Lilium

Examples

The lily in her garden bloomed beautifully each spring.
He chose a white lily for the centerpiece of the bouquet.
Botanists studied the rare variety of lily found only in this region.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flower - the reproductive structure found in flowering plants

Etymology

From Old English 'lilie', from Latin 'lilium', of Greek origin.
