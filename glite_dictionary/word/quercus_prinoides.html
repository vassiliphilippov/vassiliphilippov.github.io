Quercus prinoides

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɜrkəs praɪˈnɔɪdiːz / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs praɪˈnɔɪdiːz / (BrE)
A deciduous shrubby tree native to northeastern and central United States, known for its sweet edible nuts and tendency to form dense thickets.
a shrubby tree with edible nuts in the US

Examples

Dense thickets of Quercus prinoides are often found along the edges of prairies.
The sweet nuts of the Quercus prinoides attract various wildlife in the region.
Botanists noted the adaptability of Quercus prinoides to poor, rocky soils.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Quercus' meaning oak, and 'prinoides' indicating resemblance to the oak genus 'Prinus.'
