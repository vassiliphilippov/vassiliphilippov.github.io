cantle

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæntəl / (AmE), / ˈkæntəl / (BrE)
the raised, curved back part of a saddle's seat, providing support for the rider
the back part of a saddle seat

Examples

The rider leaned against the cantle as the horse picked up speed.
The ornate leatherwork on the cantle matched the saddle's overall design.
She secured the saddlebag just behind the cantle before setting off on the trail.

Etymology

From Old French *cantel* (corner, edge), diminutive of *cant* (corner), from Latin *canthus* (iron tire of a wheel).
