Strombus gigas

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrɑmbəs ˈdʒaɪgəs / (AmE), / ˈstrɒmbəs ˈdʒɪgəs / (BrE)
A large tropical marine mollusk, also known as the queen conch, with a distinctive spiral shell, found in the Caribbean Sea and western Atlantic Ocean.
queen conch species of sea snail

Examples

Many divers travel to the Bahamas to observe the beautiful Strombus gigas in its natural habitat.
The shell of the Strombus gigas is often used to make jewelry and decorative items.
Overfishing has led to a decline in Strombus gigas populations in the Caribbean.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: queen conch - Common name for Strombus gigas

Etymology

From Latin, Strombus (a type of sea snail) and gigas (giant).
