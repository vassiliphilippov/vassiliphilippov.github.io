aleatory

1. ADJ

/ ˈeɪliəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈeɪliətəri / (BrE)
depending on chance or luck; random or unpredictable, especially as a defining characteristic.
dependent on chance or luck

Examples

The outcome of the investment was highly aleatory, depending on shifting market conditions.
His style of music incorporated aleatory elements, allowing performers to make spontaneous choices during a performance.
Many board games introduce an aleatory factor by using dice to determine moves.

Synset Examples

the aleatory element in life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: random - made, done, or happening without method or conscious decision
  • Antonym: certain - known for sure; established beyond doubt

Etymology

From Latin aleatorius (pertaining to a gambler), from alea (dice, game of chance).
