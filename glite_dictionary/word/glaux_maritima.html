Glaux maritima

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlaʊks məˈrɪtɪmə / (AmE), / ˈɡlaʊks məˈrɪtɪmə / (BrE)
a small, fleshy herb found along North American seashores and in brackish marshes, bearing pink or white flowers
a coastal, marsh-dwelling herb

Examples

Glaux maritima is often found growing along tidal salt marshes in northern climates.
Researchers studied the salt tolerance of Glaux maritima in coastal ecosystems.
The brackish marshes near the shore are home to several plants, including Glaux maritima.

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Glaux' is Greek for 'owl' (original genus name); 'maritima' means 'of the sea'.
