trawl

1. NOUN

/ trɔl / (AmE), / trɔːl / (BrE)
a long fishing line with many shorter lines and hooks attached, usually suspended between buoys, used for catching fish
long fishing line with many hooks

Examples

The fishermen checked the trawl each morning to see how many fish they had caught.
He lowered the trawl into the water, making sure every hook was properly baited.
After several hours, they pulled up the trawl filled with a variety of sea creatures.

Etymology

Probably from Dutch *traal* (fishing net), related to Middle Dutch *trālen* (to drag).

2. VERB

/ trɔl / (AmE), / trɔːl / (BrE)
to fish by dragging a large net (a trawl) through the water behind a boat or trawler.
fish using a trawl net from a boat

Examples

The fleet will trawl off the coast for several months during the fishing season.
They trawled the deep waters hoping for a big catch of cod.
Local fishermen are prohibited from trawling in protected marine areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fish - to catch or try to catch fish

Etymology

From Dutch 'traal' (dragnet), related to the act of dragging a net.

Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ trɔl / (AmE), / trɔːl / (BrE)
a large conical net dragged along the sea bottom or at great depths to catch fish
conical fishnet dragged through water for fishing

Examples

The fishermen lowered the trawl into the ocean before dawn.
A massive trawl was hauled onto the deck, filled with flounder and shrimp.
Scientists deployed a trawl to study deep-sea species in the Atlantic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trawl net - a net used for trawling

Etymology

Probably from Dutch *traal* (drag-net), from Middle Dutch *trale*.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
