Macruridae

1. NOUN

/ məˈruːrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / məˈruːrɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of deep-sea fish known as grenadiers, characterized by their elongated bodies and tapering tails.
family of deep-sea fish (grenadiers)

Examples

The Macruridae are commonly found at great depths in the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans.
Scientists discovered a new species within the Macruridae while exploring the deep sea.
Members of the Macruridae typically have large heads and slender, tapering bodies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grenadier - common name for fishes of the Macruridae family

Etymology

From Greek makros (long) + oura (tail), referring to the long-tailed appearance of fish in this family.
