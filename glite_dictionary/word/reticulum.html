Reticulum

1. PROPN

/ rəˈtɪkjələm / (AmE), / rɪˈtɪkjʊləm / (BrE)
a small constellation in the southern sky, located near Dorado and Hydrus
constellation in the southern sky

Examples

Many southern hemisphere stargazers search for Reticulum during clear, dark nights.
Reticulum is best observed in the months of December and January.
Astronomers often use stars in Reticulum as reference points for locating nearby galaxies.

Etymology

From Latin 'reticulum' meaning 'little net', referring to the net-like shape of the constellation.
