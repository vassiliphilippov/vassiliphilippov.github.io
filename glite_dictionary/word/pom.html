pom

1. NOUN

/ pɑm / (AmE), / pɒm / (BrE)
(slang, often disparaging) A British person, especially as referred to by Australians or New Zealanders.
(slang) British person (disparaging)
slang offensive

Examples

Some Australians refer to British tourists as poms during cricket matches.
He didn't mind being called a pom after moving to Sydney.
The word pom can sometimes be used affectionately, but is often considered offensive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pommy - Slang term for a British person

Etymology

Origin uncertain; possibly shortening of 'pomegranate', Australian rhyming slang for 'immigrant'.
