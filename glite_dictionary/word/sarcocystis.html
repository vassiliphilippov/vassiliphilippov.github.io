Sarcocystis

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɑr.kəˈsɪs.tɪs / (AmE), / ˌsɑː.kəˈsɪs.tɪs / (BrE)
a genus of parasitic protozoans of the order Sarcosporidia, known to infect the muscles of various animals
a genus of parasitic protozoans

Examples

Sarcocystis infections can cause significant disease in livestock, leading to economic losses for farmers.
Scientists identified the presence of Sarcocystis in muscle tissue samples from wild deer.
Proper cooking of meat can help prevent transmission of Sarcocystis to humans and other animals.

Etymology

From Greek sarx (flesh) + kystis (bladder).

Infection with Sarcocystis is diagnosed by examining tissue samples under a microscope.
