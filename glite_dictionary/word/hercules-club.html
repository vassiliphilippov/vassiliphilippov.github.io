Hercules'-club

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɝːkjəliːz klʌb / (AmE), / ˈhɜːkjəliːz klʌb / (BrE)
A small, clump-forming deciduous tree or shrub native to the eastern United States, known for its spiny stems.
spiny tree or shrub of eastern US

Examples

The Hercules'-club is easily recognized by its thick, spiny branches.
Gardeners in the eastern United States occasionally plant Hercules'-club as an ornamental shrub.
The bark and roots of Hercules'-club have been used in traditional remedies.

Etymology

Named after the mythical hero Hercules, referring to the stout, club-like appearance of the branches.
