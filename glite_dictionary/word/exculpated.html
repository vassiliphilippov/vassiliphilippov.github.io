exculpated

1. ADJ

/ ɛkˈskʌlpeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ɛkˈskʌlpeɪtɪd / (BrE)
freed from blame or guilt; officially declared not guilty of wrongdoing
freed from blame or guilt

Examples

After new evidence was presented, the defendant was exculpated and released from custody.
The investigation exculpated the manager from any involvement in the fraud.
Once his innocence was proven, he felt exculpated in the eyes of his colleagues.

Synset Examples

is absolved from all blame
was now clear of the charge of cowardice
his official honor is vindicated

Related Terms

  • Synonym: absolved - freed from blame or responsibility
  • Synonym: vindicated - shown or proven to be right, justified, or innocent
  • Antonym: condemned - declared to be guilty or sentenced for a wrongdoing

Etymology

From Latin exculpatus, past participle of exculpare (to clear from blame), from ex- (out of) + culpa (blame).
