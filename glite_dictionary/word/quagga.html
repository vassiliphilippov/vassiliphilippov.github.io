quagga

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑɡə / (AmE), / ˈkwæɡə / (BrE)
an extinct mammal of South Africa that resembled a zebra, with stripes mainly on the front part of its body; extinct since the late 19th century
extinct South African zebra-like mammal

Examples

The quagga once roamed the grasslands of South Africa but is now extinct.
A mounted quagga specimen is on display at the natural history museum.
The extinction of the quagga was due to excessive hunting in the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zebra - an African wild horse with black-and-white stripes

Etymology

From Afrikaans 'quagga', from Khoikhoi, imitative of the animal's call.
