to finalize

1. VERB

/ ˈfaɪnəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfaɪnəlaɪz / (BrE)
to complete the last details of something, making it definite or finished
to make final or complete

Examples

We need to finalize our travel arrangements by the end of this week.
The contract hasn't been finalized yet, so we can't start work.
After several revisions, they were finally ready to finalize the report.

Synset Examples

let's finalize the proposal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complete - to finish making or doing something
  • Synonym: finish - to bring to an end; complete

Etymology

Formed from 'final' + '-ize'; attested from early 19th century.
