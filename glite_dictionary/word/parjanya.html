Parjanya

1. PROPN

/ pɑrˈdʒɑːnjə / (AmE), / pɑːˈdʒʌnjə / (BrE)
In Hindu mythology, the god of rain, sometimes identified with Indra.
Hindu god of rain

Examples

In ancient texts, Parjanya is often invoked to bring rain and fertility to the land.
The people offered prayers to Parjanya during prolonged periods of drought.
According to the Rigveda, Parjanya controls the clouds and storms.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Indra - Chief deity in Hinduism, ruler of the heavens and sometimes god of rain.

Etymology

From Sanskrit पर्जन्य (parjanya), meaning 'rain' or 'rain god'.
