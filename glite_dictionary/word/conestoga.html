Conestoga

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑː.nəˈstoʊ.ɡə / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.ɪˈstəʊ.ɡə / (BrE)
a large covered wagon with broad wheels, used by early United States pioneers to cross prairies in the 19th century
a large pioneer wagon used in 19th-century America

Examples

Early settlers loaded their belongings into a Conestoga for the long trek across the plains.
The museum has a restored Conestoga on display, showing how pioneers traveled westward.
The sturdy design of the Conestoga made it ideal for transporting supplies over rough terrain.

Etymology

Named after the Conestoga Valley and Conestoga people in Pennsylvania, U.S.A.
