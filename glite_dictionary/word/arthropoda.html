Arthropoda

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑːrθrəˈpoʊdə / (AmE), / ˌɑːθrəˈpəʊdə / (BrE)
A large phylum of invertebrate animals with jointed limbs and segmented bodies, including insects, arachnids, crustaceans, millipedes, and centipedes.
phylum of jointed-limb invertebrates

Examples

Insects, spiders, and crustaceans all belong to the phylum Arthropoda.
The exoskeleton is a defining feature of animals classified under Arthropoda.
Scientists study Arthropoda to understand evolutionary adaptations among invertebrates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arthropod - a member of the phylum Arthropoda

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'arthron' (joint) + 'pous, podos' (foot).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɑrθrəˈpoʊdə / (AmE), / ˌɑːθrəˈpəʊdə / (BrE)
A large phylum of invertebrate animals characterized by jointed limbs, segmented bodies, and exoskeletons, including insects, arachnids, crustaceans, millipedes, and centipedes.
phylum of joint-legged invertebrates

Examples

Spiders, crabs, and ants all belong to the phylum Arthropoda.
The defining feature of Arthropoda is their exoskeleton made of chitin.
Biology students studied the evolutionary history of Arthropoda in their zoology class.

Etymology

From New Latin 'arthropodus', from Greek 'arthron' (joint) + 'pous' (foot).
