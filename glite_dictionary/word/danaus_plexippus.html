Danaus plexippus

1. NOUN

/ dəˈneɪ.əs plɛkˈsɪp.əs / (AmE), / dəˈneɪ.əs plɛkˈsɪp.əs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the monarch butterfly, a large migratory American butterfly with deep orange wings marked with black and white; its larvae feed on milkweed
scientific name for the monarch butterfly

Examples

The Danaus plexippus is renowned for its remarkable migration from North America to central Mexico each year.
Scientists tracked the movements of Danaus plexippus using tiny tags attached to their wings.
Caterpillars of Danaus plexippus feed exclusively on milkweed plants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monarch butterfly - large orange-and-black American butterfly; common name for Danaus plexippus

Etymology

From Greek mythological names Danaus and Plexippus, used in modern Latin for butterfly taxonomy.
