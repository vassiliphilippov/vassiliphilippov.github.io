George VI

1. PROPN

/ dʒɔrdʒ sɪksθ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔːdʒ ðə sɪksθ / (BrE)
(1895–1952) King of the United Kingdom and the Dominions of the British Commonwealth from 1936 to 1952, and Emperor of India until 1947; succeeded Edward VIII.
King of the United Kingdom, 1936–1952

Examples

The film depicted the challenges faced by George VI during World War II.
George VI delivered a famous radio broadcast to rally the British people in 1939.
Many coins and stamps from the era bear the image of George VI.

Related Terms
