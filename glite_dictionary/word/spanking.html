spanking

1. NOUN

/ ˈspæŋ.kɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspæŋ.kɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of hitting someone's buttocks as a punishment, especially for a child
hitting the buttocks as punishment

Examples

The teacher gave the misbehaving boy a spanking as a warning.
Some parents no longer believe in spanking as a form of discipline.
She remembered getting a spanking once when she was very young.

Synset Examples

he gave the brat a good spanking

Related Terms

  • Synonym: smacking - the act of hitting lightly with the open hand
  • Degree Higher: beating - the act of striking repeatedly, usually more severe

2. ADJ

/ ˈspæŋkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspæŋkɪŋ / (BrE)
quick, brisk, and energetic; lively or spirited in motion or effect
quick and energetic

Examples

We set off at a spanking pace to reach the summit before noon.
A spanking breeze filled the sails and pushed the boat swiftly across the bay.
With a spanking start, the runners were quickly out of sight.

Synset Examples

a brisk walk in the park
a lively gait
a merry chase
traveling at a rattling rate
a snappy pace
a spanking breeze

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brisk - active, fast, and energetic
  • Synonym: lively - full of life and energy

Etymology

Sense evolved in 18th–19th c., from 'spank' (to strike with the open hand), suggesting something strong or lively.
