to add

1. VERB

/ æd / (AmE), / æd / (BrE)
to calculate the total of two or more numbers or amounts by putting them together
calculate a sum

Examples

If you add these figures, the total comes to 100.
Remember to add the sales tax to the bill.
She asked me to add up the numbers in the spreadsheet.

Synset Examples

Add all the people in this town to those of the neighboring town

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to subtract - to take away one number from another; opposite of add
  • Synonym: sum - the result of adding two or more numbers

Etymology

From Latin 'addere', meaning 'to put to'; ad- (to) + dare (give).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
