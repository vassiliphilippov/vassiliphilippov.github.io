to remodel

1. VERB

/ riˈmɑːdəl / (AmE), / riˈmɒdəl / (BrE)
to change the structure or appearance of something, especially a building or a part of it
alter the structure of a building

Examples

We plan to remodel the kitchen next spring to make it more modern.
They decided to remodel the old house before moving in.
The company spent millions to remodel its headquarters after the merger.

Synset Examples

We are remodeling these rooms

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to renovate - to restore to good condition or make new again
  • Synonym: to restore - to repair or return something to its original condition

Etymology

From re- (again) + model (to shape or form).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00199854-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ riˈmɑdəl / (AmE), / rɪˈmɒdəl / (BrE)
to cast, shape, or model again; to form anew by altering the original structure or appearance
cast or form anew; reshape

Examples

After the artist broke the clay, she decided to remodel the statue into a completely new shape.
The jeweler will remodel the pendant by melting and recasting the gold.
Because the prototype was flawed, engineers had to remodel its structure before production.

Synset Examples

She had to recast her image to please the electorate in her home state

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reshape - to shape again or differently
  • Synonym: to recast - to cast or form again in a new shape

Etymology

From re- (again) + model, from Italian modellare or French modeler, ultimately from Latin modulus (a small measure, standard).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01662480-v
Synset POS: v
