zymosis

1. NOUN

/ zaɪˈmoʊsɪs / (AmE), / zɪˈməʊsɪs / (BrE)
a process in which an agent causes an organic substance to break down into simpler substances, especially the anaerobic breakdown of sugar into alcohol (fermentation)
biological breakdown process, especially fermentation

Examples

Pasteur's research clarified the role of yeast in zymosis.
Without oxygen, the fruits undergo zymosis and produce alcohol.
The winemaker closely monitored the zymosis to ensure a high-quality vintage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fermentation - the chemical breakdown of a substance (such as sugar) by bacteria, yeasts, or other microorganisms

Etymology

From Greek *zymōsis* (fermentation), from *zymoun* (to leaven), from *zymē* (leaven).

2. NOUN

/ zaɪˈmoʊsɪs / (AmE), / zaɪˈməʊsɪs / (BrE)
(medical) the development and spread of an infectious disease, especially one caused by fungi or fermentation-like processes.
development and spread of an infectious disease
medical

Examples

The physician observed rapid zymosis after exposure to the pathogen.
Early theories about zymosis helped shape our understanding of contagious diseases.
Fungal infections in crops often spread through zymosis within the field.

Etymology

From Greek zymōsis (fermentation), from zymoun (to ferment), from zymē (leaven).
