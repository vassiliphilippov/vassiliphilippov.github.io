mutilated

1. ADJ

/ ˈmjuː.t̬ə.leɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈmjuː.tɪ.leɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
Having a part of the body severely damaged, removed, or rendered useless.
having a body part severely damaged or removed

Examples

The mutilated statue was missing both arms after the war.
She cared for animals that had been mutilated by traps.
The victim was found with mutilated limbs, a sign of extreme violence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maimed - having a body part permanently injured or disfigured
  • Antonym: intact - not damaged or impaired in any way

Etymology

From Latin mutilatus, past participle of mutilare (to cut off, maim).
