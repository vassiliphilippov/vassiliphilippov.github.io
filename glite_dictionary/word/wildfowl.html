wildfowl

1. NOUN

/ ˈwaɪld.faʊl / (AmE), / ˈwaɪld.faʊl / (BrE)
The flesh of wild game birds, used as food.
flesh of wild game birds (food)

Examples

The chef prepared a rich stew using wildfowl and fresh herbs.
Many traditional European dishes highlight the flavors of wildfowl such as duck and pheasant.
Hunters often bring back wildfowl to be roasted over an open fire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: game - The flesh of wild animals or birds hunted for food

Etymology

From 'wild' + 'fowl', referring to undomesticated birds hunted for food.
