Peristediinae

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɛrɪˈstɛdiːɪˌniː / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪˈstiːdɪˌniː / (BrE)
a subfamily of fish in some classifications, comprising the armored searobins within the family Triglidae
subfamily of armored searobins (fish)

Examples

The Peristediinae are notable for their armored bodies and bottom-dwelling habits.
Marine biologists are studying the distribution of Peristediinae in the western Pacific.
Some older texts list Peristediinae as a separate subfamily within Triglidae.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Triglidae - the family of sea robins, including Peristediinae in some classifications

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus Peristedion (type genus) and the taxonomic suffix -inae (denoting subfamilies).

A subfamily of fish in some classifications, known as the armored searobins, within the family Triglidae.
subfamily of armored searobins (fish)

Examples

The Peristediinae are distinguished by their armored, spiny bodies.
Researchers discovered a new species in the subfamily Peristediinae along the continental shelf.
Fishermen occasionally encounter members of the Peristediinae, though they are not commonly caught.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Triglidae - Family of fish known as sea robins or gurnards

Etymology

From New Latin, based on the type genus Peristedion and the suffix -inae used for animal subfamilies.
