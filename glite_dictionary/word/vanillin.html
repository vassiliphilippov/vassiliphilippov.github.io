vanillin

1. NOUN

/ vəˈnɪlɪn / (AmE), / vəˈnɪlɪn / (BrE)
a crystalline compound that is the chief component of the extract of vanilla beans, used in flavorings and perfumes
flavor compound from vanilla beans

Examples

The distinctive aroma of vanilla ice cream comes mainly from vanillin.
Synthetic vanillin is often used as a less expensive alternative to natural vanilla extract.
Chemists analyze samples of vanillin to determine their purity for use in perfumes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vanilla - a substance obtained from vanilla beans, used for flavoring

Etymology

From vanilla + -in, indicating a chemical compound, first used in the late 19th century.
