collagenase

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlædʒəˌneɪs / (AmE), / kəˈladʒ.ə.neɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that breaks down collagen and gelatin by catalyzing their hydrolysis
enzyme that digests collagen
medical

Examples

The application of collagenase can assist in wound healing by removing dead tissue.
Researchers studied the effect of collagenase on breaking down scar tissue.
Certain bacteria produce collagenase to help them invade host tissues.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enzyme - a substance that speeds up biochemical reactions

Etymology

From 'collagen' + '-ase', a suffix used for enzymes.
