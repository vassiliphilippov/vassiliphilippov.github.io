celiac

1. ADJ

/ ˈsiːliˌæk / (AmE), / ˈsiːliːæk / (BrE)
relating to the abdominal cavity, especially in medical contexts
of the abdomen or abdominal cavity
medical

Examples

The celiac artery supplies blood to the stomach, liver, and spleen.
She was diagnosed with a disorder affecting her celiac region.
Surgeons operated near the celiac plexus to relieve persistent pain.

Etymology

From Greek 'koiliakos' (abdominal), from 'koilia' (belly, abdomen).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsiːliˌæk / (AmE), / ˈsiːliˌak / (BrE)
relating to or prescribed for celiac disease, a condition causing intolerance to gluten
relating to celiac disease
medical

Examples

She follows a celiac diet to manage her condition.
The restaurant offers several celiac menu options for guests with gluten intolerance.
A strict celiac regimen can help prevent health complications for those affected.

Synset Examples

a celiac diet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: celiac disease - an autoimmune disorder where ingestion of gluten leads to damage in the small intestine

Etymology

From Greek koiliakos (pertaining to the belly), from koilia (belly).
