Pan troglodytes schweinfurthii

1. NOUN

/ pæn troʊˈɡlɒdəˌtiz ˌʃvaɪnˈfʊrθiː / (AmE), / pan trɒˈɡlɒdɪtiːz ˌʃvaɪnˈfʊəθiː / (BrE)
A subspecies of the common chimpanzee found in east-central Africa, distinguished by its long hair and close relation to the central chimpanzee.
eastern subspecies of chimpanzee

Examples

Researchers observed Pan troglodytes schweinfurthii using tools to extract insects from tree bark.
Habitat destruction poses significant threats to the survival of Pan troglodytes schweinfurthii populations.
The long hair of Pan troglodytes schweinfurthii helps distinguish it from other chimpanzee subspecies.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Pan troglodytes - the common chimpanzee species

Etymology

From Latin genus and species names; 'schweinfurthii' honors German botanist Georg August Schweinfurth.
