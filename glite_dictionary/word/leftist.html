leftist

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɛftɪst / (AmE), / ˈlɛftɪst / (BrE)
Supporting or relating to the principles or policies of the political left, especially those advocating social equality and government involvement in economic affairs.
supporting the political left

Examples

Many young voters are increasingly drawn to leftist ideas and policies.
The government's new tax plan received heavy criticism from leftist groups.
Although her parents are conservatives, she holds strongly leftist views.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rightist - supporting principles of the political right
  • Synonym: liberal - favoring reform and open to new ideas, especially in politics

Etymology

From 'left' (political left) + '-ist' (denoting a person who practices or is concerned with something).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɛftɪst / (AmE), / ˈlɛftɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports or belongs to the political left, especially someone who advocates progressive or socialist policies
person with left-wing political views

Examples

She is known as a prominent leftist in local politics.
The demonstration attracted many leftist activists and supporters.
During the debate, a leftist challenged the conservative panelist's views on social policy.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: rightist - a person who belongs to the political right
  • Synonym: progressive - a person who advocates social reform, often associated with the political left

Etymology

From 'left', referring to the political left, with the suffix '-ist' indicating an adherent or supporter. The use of 'left' in politics originates from the seating arrangement during the French Revolution, where radicals sat to the left of the presiding officer.
