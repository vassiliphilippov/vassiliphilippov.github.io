baobab

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪ.oʊˌbæb / (AmE), / ˈbaʊ.əˌbæb / (BrE)
an African tree with a very thick trunk and large gourd-like fruit containing edible pulp, also known as monkey bread
African tree with thick trunk and edible fruit

Examples

The baobab tree stores water in its massive trunk to survive dry seasons.
Local markets sell the fruit of the baobab, which is prized for its nutritional value.
Legend says that the baobab was planted upside down, giving it its unique shape.

Etymology

From Arabic *būħibāb*, possibly of African origin.
