virgule

1. NOUN

/ ˈvɝːɡjuːl / (AmE), / ˈvɜːɡjuːl / (BrE)
a punctuation mark (/) used to separate related items of information, such as alternatives or fractional values
the slash punctuation mark (/)

Examples

Please use a virgule to indicate options in your answer, such as 'yes/no'.
In the fraction 1/2, the virgule separates the numerator from the denominator.
The virgule can be used instead of 'or' to present alternatives in writing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slash - the punctuation mark (/)

Etymology

From French 'virgule', from Latin 'virgula' (little twig, small rod).
