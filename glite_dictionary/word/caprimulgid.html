caprimulgid

1. NOUN

/ kæprɪˈmʌl dʒɪd / (AmE), / ˌkæprɪˈmʌl dʒɪd / (BrE)
A bird of the family Caprimulgidae, commonly known as nightjars; mostly nocturnal or crepuscular, with mottled plumage and large eyes, feeding on insects.
a nightjar; bird of the family Caprimulgidae

Examples

The caprimulgid is best known for its haunting, nocturnal call throughout summer nights.
Researchers studied the camouflage techniques of several caprimulgids in their native habitats.
A caprimulgid will often rest motionless on the ground during the day, blending with leaf litter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nightjar - a nocturnal insect-eating bird, also in Caprimulgidae

Etymology

From modern Latin Caprimulgidae, from Latin caprimulgus (nightjar), from capra (goat) + mulgere (to milk).
