Quinora

1. NOUN

/ kwɪˈnɔrə / (AmE), / kwɪˈnɔːrə / (BrE)
A cardiac drug (trade name Quinora) used to treat certain heart arrhythmias.
cardiac drug used for arrhythmia
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed Quinora to help manage the patient's irregular heartbeat.
Some patients may experience side effects when taking Quinora for their heart condition.
It's important to take Quinora exactly as directed to avoid complications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Quinidex - trade name for a similar cardiac arrhythmia drug
  • Synonym: quinidine - drug used to treat heart arrhythmias, main active compound in Quinora
