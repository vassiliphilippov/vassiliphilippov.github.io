bellicosity

1. NOUN

/ bɛlɪˈkɑsəti / (AmE), / bɛlɪˈkɒsəti / (BrE)
an inclination or tendency to be aggressive or eager to fight
aggressive or warlike inclination

Examples

The politician's bellicosity alarmed his colleagues and worried the public.
Her bellicosity often led to unnecessary arguments at work.
Despite his reputation for bellicosity, he preferred peaceful solutions whenever possible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: belligerence - a hostile or warlike attitude or nature

Etymology

From Latin 'bellicositas', from 'bellicosus' meaning 'warlike', from 'bellum' (war).
