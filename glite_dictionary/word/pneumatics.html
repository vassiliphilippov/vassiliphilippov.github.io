pneumatics

1. NOUN

/ nuːˈmætɪks / (AmE), / njuːˈmætɪks / (BrE)
the branch of physics and engineering that deals with the mechanical properties of gases and the use of gas pressure for mechanical purposes
study of gas mechanics and applications

Examples

The engineer specialized in pneumatics to design air-powered machinery for the factory.
Modern train braking systems often rely on pneumatics rather than hydraulic pressure.
She attended a lecture on pneumatics and learned about the behavior of gases under pressure.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: hydraulics - the branch of science concerned with the mechanical properties of liquids

Etymology

From Greek pneumatikos (of air, of wind), from pneuma (wind, breath).
