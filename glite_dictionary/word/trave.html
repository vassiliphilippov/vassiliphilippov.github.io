trave

1. NOUN

/ ˈtreɪv / (AmE), / ˈtreɪv / (BrE)
A horizontal structural beam or bar that extends across an opening or between supports, often used in architecture or construction.
horizontal beam or bar in construction

Examples

The ancient cathedral was supported by a massive trave spanning the nave.
Workers carefully placed the trave on top of the stone columns during construction.
Each trave was carved from oak to ensure maximum strength.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beam - a long, sturdy piece of wood or metal used in construction

Etymology

From Latin 'trabs, trabis' meaning beam or timber.
