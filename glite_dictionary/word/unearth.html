to unearth

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈɜrθ / (AmE), / ʌnˈɜːθ / (BrE)
to find or discover something, especially through careful investigation or search
discover through investigation

Examples

The researchers managed to unearth new evidence after years of searching the archives.
Journalists worked hard to unearth the truth behind the company’s sudden collapse.
During the investigation, detectives unearthed several documents that had been hidden for decades.

Synset Examples

The CIA unearthed a plot to kill the President

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to discover - to find something for the first time
  • Antonym: to bury - to cover something so as to hide it

Etymology

From Middle English 'un-' (not) + 'earth' (to cover with earth), originally meaning 'to dig up from the ground'.

2. VERB

/ ʌnˈɜrθ / (AmE), / ʌnˈɜːθ / (BrE)
to discover something by digging it up from the ground; to bring to light something buried or hidden
discover by digging up

Examples

Archaeologists hope to unearth new artifacts at the ancient site.
Construction workers unearthed a centuries-old foundation while digging for the new building.
They plan to unearth fossils believed to be millions of years old.

Synset Examples

Schliemann excavated Troy
excavate gold

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to excavate - to make a hole or channel by removing earth; to uncover by digging
  • Antonym: to bury - to put something in the ground and cover it; to hide underground

Etymology

From un- (not) + earth, originally meaning 'to remove from the earth'.
