wilderness

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪl.dɚ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈwɪl.də.nəs / (BrE)
(politics) A metaphorical state of having lost influence, power, or favor, especially after defeat or exclusion from decision-making.
state of political disfavor or exclusion

Examples

After the election loss, the party spent years in the wilderness before regaining power.
His controversial views left him in the political wilderness for a decade.
She returned from the wilderness to lead her team to success.

Synset Examples

he led the Democratic party back from the wilderness

2. NOUN

/ ˈwɪldərnəs / (AmE), / ˈwɪldənəs / (BrE)
A large or confusing mass of things, making it difficult to see or understand individual parts.
a confusing profusion or mass

Examples

Documents were lost in a wilderness of paperwork.
The city skyline was a wilderness of glass and steel.
His ideas got buried in a wilderness of technical jargon.

Synset Examples

the duties of citizenship are lost sight of in the wilderness of interests of individuals and groups
a wilderness of masts in the harbor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jumble - an untidy or confusing mixture

Etymology

From Old English 'wildēornes' (wild-deer place), with sense extended figuratively to any confused or dense mass.

3. NOUN

/ ˈwɪldɚnəs / (AmE), / ˈwɪldənɪs / (BrE)
an area of land that has not been significantly modified by human activity, remaining in a natural and uninhabited state
wild, uninhabited natural area

Examples

We spent two weeks hiking through the vast wilderness of Alaska.
After the fire, the once-cultivated land quickly reverted to wilderness.
The national park aims to protect the remaining wilderness for future generations.

Synset Examples

it was a wilderness preserved for the hawks and mountaineers

Related Terms

  • Synonym: desert - a dry, barren area of land
  • Synonym: forest - a large area covered chiefly with trees and undergrowth
  • Antonym: civilization - an area with settled communities and human development

Etymology

From Old English 'wildēornes' (land inhabited only by wild animals).
