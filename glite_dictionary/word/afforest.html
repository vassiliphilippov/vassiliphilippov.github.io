to afforest

1. VERB

/ əˈfɔr.əst / (AmE), / əˈfɒr.ɪst / (BrE)
to plant trees on land that has not been forested before, in order to create a forest
create a forest on non-forested land

Examples

Government agencies plan to afforest large stretches of desert land.
Many countries are making efforts to afforest hillsides to combat soil erosion.
They hope to afforest the empty fields with native species of trees.

Synset Examples

afforest the mountains

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to deforest - to remove trees or forests from an area of land

Etymology

From Medieval Latin afforestare, from ad- (to) + forest.
