propylthiouracil

1. NOUN

/ ˌproʊpɪlˌθaɪoʊjʊˈræsɪl / (AmE), / ˌprəʊpɪlˌθaɪəʊjʊˈræsɪl / (BrE)
a crystalline drug used to reduce the activity of the thyroid gland, especially in the treatment of hyperthyroidism and goiter
antithyroid drug for treating hyperthyroidism
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed propylthiouracil to help control her overactive thyroid gland.
After starting propylthiouracil, the patient's symptoms of hyperthyroidism began to improve.
In some cases, propylthiouracil is used when other antithyroid medications are not effective.

Etymology

From 'propyl', 'thio-', and 'uracil', indicating its chemical components and structure.
