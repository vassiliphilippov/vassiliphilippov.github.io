to concentre

1. VERB

/ kənˈsɛn.tɚ / (AmE), / kənˈsɛn.tə / (BrE)
to bring into focus or alignment; to converge or cause to converge
bring into focus or converge

Examples

The discussion began to concentre on the core issues facing the company.
His thoughts seemed to concentre intensely as the deadline approached.
Various forces would ultimately concentre and produce a significant impact on the community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: concentrate - focus one's attention or bring together

Etymology

From Latin concentrare (to bring to a common center), based on con- (together) + centrum (center).
