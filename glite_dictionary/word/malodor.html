malodor

1. NOUN

/ ˌmælˈoʊdɚ / (AmE), / ˌmalˈəʊdə / (BrE)
an unpleasant or offensive smell
offensive or unpleasant smell

Examples

The kitchen had a persistent malodor that would not go away, no matter how much we cleaned.
A strong malodor wafted through the alley, making everyone wrinkle their noses in disgust.
The doctor noted a distinct malodor coming from the wound, indicating a possible infection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stink - a strong unpleasant smell
  • Antonym: fragrance - a pleasant smell
  • Synonym: odor - a smell, especially one that is distinctive

Etymology

From Latin 'malus' (bad) + 'odor' (smell).
