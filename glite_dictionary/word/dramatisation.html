dramatisation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdræmətaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdræmətaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of adapting a story or event into a play, film, or other dramatic work; a dramatic representation of something
a dramatic adaptation or representation
british

Examples

The dramatisation of the novel was broadcast on national television.
Her dramatisation brought the historical events to life for the audience.
We attended a dramatisation of Shakespeare's Hamlet last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dramatization - the American English spelling; a dramatic representation or adaptation

Etymology

From English 'dramatise' + '-ation'; ultimately from Greek 'drāma' (drama).
