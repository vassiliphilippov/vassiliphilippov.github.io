pleating

1. NOUN

/ ˈplitɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpliːtɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of folding fabric into parallel pleats or narrow folds, often as a decorative or functional feature
the act of making parallel folds in fabric

Examples

The tailor demonstrated the technique of pleating the skirt for a neat finish.
Proper pleating gives the curtain its elegant drape and structure.
She admired the intricate pleating along the bodice of the evening gown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pleat - a fold in cloth sewn into place

Etymology

From 'pleat' + -ing; 'pleat' from Middle English, perhaps from Old French 'pli', 'pleit' (fold).
