Simenon

1. PROPN

/ ˌsiːməˈnɒn / (AmE), / ˌsiːməˈnɒ̃ / (BrE)
Georges Simenon (1903–1989), a Belgian-born French writer known for his detective novels featuring Inspector Maigret.
Belgian-born French writer, creator of Maigret

Examples

Simenon's Maigret novels have been translated into dozens of languages around the world.
Many critics consider Simenon one of the greatest crime writers of the twentieth century.
I bought a first edition of a Simenon novel at the book fair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Maigret - Fictional French police detective created by Georges Simenon
