dogleg

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɔɡˌlɛɡ / (AmE), / ˈdɒɡ.lɛɡ / (BrE)
a sharp bend or angle, especially one resembling the shape of a dog's hind leg
sharp bend or angle

Examples

The road took a sudden dogleg to the left after the bridge.
The par-four hole features a challenging dogleg around the water hazard.
Architects added a dogleg in the corridor to maximize space.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɔɡˌlɛɡ / (AmE), / ˈdɒɡˌlɛɡ / (BrE)
A part of a golf hole where the fairway bends sharply, typically making it harder to reach the green directly.
sharp bend in a golf fairway

Examples

The fifth hole is a challenging dogleg to the right, requiring a precise tee shot.
She carefully aimed her shot to avoid the trees at the dogleg.
Many players struggle with the long dogleg on the back nine.

Etymology

From the resemblance to a dog's bent leg; adopted in golf terminology in the early 20th century.
