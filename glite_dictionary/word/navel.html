navel

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪ.vəl / (AmE), / ˈneɪ.vəl / (BrE)
The small, central scar on the abdomen marking the spot where the umbilical cord was attached in the womb; bellybutton.
scar on the abdomen; bellybutton

Examples

The newborn's navel was carefully cleaned by the nurse.
She has a ring piercing in her navel that she got last year.
The scientist explained how the navel marks where the umbilical cord once was.

Synset Examples

you were not supposed to show your navel on television
they argued whether or not Adam had a navel
she had a tattoo just above her bellybutton

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bellybutton - informal term for the navel

Etymology

From Old English *nafela*, related to German *Nabel* and Latin *umbilicus* (navel, umbilicus).

2. NOUN

/ ˈneɪ.vəl / (AmE), / ˈneɪ.vəl / (BrE)
the central point or middle of something, especially regarded as important or symbolic
central point or middle

Examples

Some ancient cultures referred to their capital city as the navel of the world.
The small island was once considered the navel of trade routes in the region.
To her, the hilltop temple marked the navel of spiritual life in the valley.

Synset Examples

the Incas believed that Cuzco was the navel of the universe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: center - the middle point of something, especially an area or object
  • Antonym: periphery - the outer edge of an area or object

Etymology

From Old English nafela, related to Old High German nabalo (navel), from Proto-Germanic *nabōlô.
