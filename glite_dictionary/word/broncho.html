broncho

1. NOUN

/ ˈbrɑn.tʃoʊ / (AmE), / ˈbrɒn.tʃəʊ / (BrE)
A wild or partly tamed horse of western North America, often used in rodeos.
mustang or wild horse (western US)

Examples

The cowboy managed to ride the broncho for eight seconds in the rodeo.
Legends often speak of the wild broncho roaming the open plains.
He broke his arm trying to tame a stubborn young broncho.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bronco - A wild or untamed horse of the western United States

Etymology

Variant of 'bronco', from Spanish 'bronco' (rough, rude, wild).
