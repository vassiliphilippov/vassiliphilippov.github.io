Delphinus delphis

1. NOUN

/ dɛlˈfaɪnəs ˈdɛlfɪs / (AmE), / dɛlˈfaɪnəs ˈdɛlfɪs / (BrE)
the scientific name for the common dolphin, a black-and-white dolphin known for leaping high out of the water
common dolphin's scientific name

Examples

The Delphinus delphis is known for its spectacular leaps above ocean waves.
Researchers tracked the migration patterns of Delphinus delphis along the Atlantic coast.
Many marine reserves protect populations of Delphinus delphis due to their ecological importance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: common dolphin - a dolphin species known for its speed and acrobatics

Etymology

From Latin: 'delphinus' (dolphin), 'delphis' (dolphin) as a specific epithet.
