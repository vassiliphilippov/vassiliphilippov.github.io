Ceratonia

1. NOUN

/ sɛrəˈtoʊniə / (AmE), / sɛrəˈtəʊniə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the pea family, Fabaceae, which includes the carob tree (Ceratonia siliqua).
botanical genus of the carob tree

Examples

The genus Ceratonia is best known for its species Ceratonia siliqua, the carob tree.
Botanists classify the carob under the genus Ceratonia.
Seeds from Ceratonia trees have been used as a source of food and gum.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carob - the edible pod of the carob tree

Etymology

From New Latin Ceratonia, derived from Greek keratonia (carob tree).

2. NOUN

/ ˌsɛrəˈtoʊniə / (AmE), / ˌsɛrəˈtəʊniə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the pea family, Fabaceae, which includes the carob tree.
genus of carob trees

Examples

The genus Ceratonia is native to the Mediterranean region.
Only one species of Ceratonia, the carob tree, is commonly cultivated.
Botanists have long debated the classification of Ceratonia within the pea family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carob - tree of the species Ceratonia siliqua, cultivated for its edible pods

Etymology

From Greek keratonia (carob tree), from keras (horn, referring to the shape of the pod).
