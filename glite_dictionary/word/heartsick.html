heartsick

1. ADJ

/ ˈhɑrtˌsɪk / (AmE), / ˈhɑːtˌsɪk / (BrE)
deeply sad or filled with sorrow, especially because of disappointment or loss
deeply sad; full of sorrow

Examples

She felt heartsick after hearing the devastating news.
He was heartsick over the end of his relationship.
The heartsick mother waited anxiously for any word of her missing child.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: heartbroken - overcome by grief or sorrow, especially from loss or disappointment

Etymology

Formed from 'heart' + 'sick' to convey emotional suffering.

2. ADJ

/ ˈhɑrtˌsɪk / (AmE), / ˈhɑːtˌsɪk / (BrE)
Deeply distressed or despondent, especially due to grief, disappointment, or loss of hope.
deeply distressed; despondent

Examples

She felt so heartsick after hearing the news of her brother’s departure.
He was heartsick when his dream job slipped away.
The heartsick look in her eyes made it clear she was struggling to cope.

Synset Examples

despondent about his failure
too heartsick to fight back

Related Terms

  • Synonym: despondent - feeling or showing profound hopelessness and dejection
  • Antonym: hopeful - feeling or inspiring optimism about a future event

Etymology

From heart + sick, denoting sickness of the heart in a figurative sense (emotional pain).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01233026-s
Synset POS: s
