allopathic

1. ADJ

/ ˌæləˈpæθɪk / (AmE), / ˌaləˈpæθɪk / (BrE)
relating to or denoting allopathy, the conventional system of medical practice that aims to combat disease by using remedies that produce effects different from those caused by the disease itself.
relating to allopathic (conventional) medicine
medical

Examples

Many patients opt for allopathic treatments when dealing with acute illnesses.
She is studying allopathic medicine at a well-known university.
Some people prefer alternative options to allopathic care for chronic pain.

Synset Examples

allopathic remedies

Related Terms

  • Antonym: homeopathic - relating to homeopathy, an alternative system of medicine

Etymology

From French allopathique, based on Greek allos (other) + -pathy (disease, suffering).
