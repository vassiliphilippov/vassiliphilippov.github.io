manacle

1. NOUN

/ ˈmænəkəl / (AmE), / ˈmanəkl / (BrE)
a metal restraint for the wrist, usually one of a pair, that can be locked to limit someone's movement
metal restraint for the wrist

Examples

The prisoner struggled against the manacle that bound his wrist to the chair.
A rusty manacle lay on the abandoned cell floor, evidence of its grim past.
With a click, the guard snapped the manacle shut around her wrist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handcuff - metal fastening for securing a person's wrists together

Etymology

From Old French 'manicle', from Latin 'manicula' (little hand), diminutive of 'manus' (hand).

2. VERB

/ ˈmænəkəl / (AmE), / ˈmænəkəl / (BrE)
to confine or restrain someone using manacles or similar restraints
to restrain with handcuffs or manacles

Examples

The guard proceeded to manacle the prisoners before transporting them to another facility.
New laws should not manacle artists in the pursuit of creative freedom.
They manacled the protestors to prevent them from escaping during the demonstration.

Synset Examples

The police handcuffed the suspect at the scene of the crime

Related Terms

  • Synonym: handcuff - to put handcuffs on someone

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old French 'manicle', from Latin 'manicula' (little hand).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
