princess

1. NOUN

/ ˈprɪn.sɛs / (AmE), / ˈprɪn.səs / (BrE)
a female member of a royal family, especially the daughter of a king or queen or the wife of a prince
female royal; king's or queen's daughter

Examples

As a young princess, she was admired by everyone for her kindness and grace.
The princess attended the charity gala with her parents, the king and queen.
Every child at the party wanted to dress up as a princess from their favorite fairy tale.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: prince - a male member of a royal family, especially the son of a king or queen

Etymology

From Old French 'princesse', from Medieval Latin 'principissa', feminine of 'princeps' (prince).

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɪnsɛs / (AmE), / ˈprɪnsɪs / (BrE)
An affectionate term used to refer to a woman or girl who is cherished or treated with special care.
cherished woman or girl (affectionate)

Examples

Her father always calls her his princess, especially on her birthday.
You’re my princess and I want you to feel special every day.
He whispered, 'Good night, princess,' before turning off the lights.

Synset Examples

He called her his princess after the surprise dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: queen - used affectionately to refer to a beloved woman
  • Synonym: prince - used affectionately to refer to a beloved man
