distinguished

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈstɪŋɡwɪʃt / (AmE), / dɪˈstɪŋɡwɪʃt / (BrE)
very successful, respected, and admired for achievements or character
respected and admired for achievements

Examples

She was recognized as a distinguished leader for her contributions to science.
The university honored its most distinguished graduates at the annual ceremony.
He has enjoyed a long and distinguished career in public service.

Synset Examples

our distinguished professor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eminent - famous and respected within a particular field
  • Synonym: notable - worthy of attention or notice; remarkable
  • Antonym: obscure - not well known; hidden from fame

Etymology

Past participle of 'distinguish', from Latin distinguere (to separate, distinguish).

2. ADJ

/ dɪˈstɪŋɡwɪʃt / (AmE), / dɪˈstɪŋɡwɪʃt / (BrE)
Having a dignified and impressive appearance or manner, befitting an eminent or respected person.
dignified and eminent in appearance or manner

Examples

The professor had a distinguished look, with silver hair and a calm demeanor.
Several distinguished guests attended the royal gala last night.
His distinguished bearing made him stand out in any crowd.

Synset Examples

his distinguished bearing
the monarch's imposing presence
she reigned in magisterial beauty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dignified - having or showing a composed or serious manner worthy of respect
  • Synonym: imposing - grand and impressive in appearance

Etymology

From the past participle of 'distinguish', from Old French 'distinguer', from Latin 'distinguere' (to separate, discern).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00755631-s
Synset POS: s
