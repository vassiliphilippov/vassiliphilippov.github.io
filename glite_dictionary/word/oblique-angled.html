oblique-angled

1. ADJ

/ əˈbliːk ˌæŋɡəld / (AmE), / əˈbliːk ˌæŋɡəld / (BrE)
(of a shape or figure) having one or more oblique (not right or straight) angles.
having oblique (non-right) angles

Examples

The triangle in the diagram is clearly oblique-angled rather than right-angled.
An oblique-angled parallelogram does not have any right angles.
Surveyors encounter oblique-angled plots when mapping irregular fields.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: right-angled - having a right (90-degree) angle

Etymology

From oblique (from Latin obliquus, slanting) + angled.
