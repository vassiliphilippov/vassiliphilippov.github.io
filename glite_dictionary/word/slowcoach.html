slowcoach

1. NOUN

/ ˈsloʊˌkoʊtʃ / (AmE), / ˈsləʊˌkəʊtʃ / (BrE)
a person who moves, acts, or thinks slowly, especially one who causes delay for others
person who is slow or causes delay
informal british

Examples

Don't be such a slowcoach, or we'll miss the train!
She was always called a slowcoach because she took her time getting ready.
Hurry up, slowcoach—everyone else is already outside.

Synset Examples

in England they call a slowpoke a ‘slowcoach’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slowpoke - someone who moves or acts slowly (primarily American English)

Etymology

Formed from 'slow' + 'coach', originally referring to a slow-moving stagecoach and later applied figuratively to people.
