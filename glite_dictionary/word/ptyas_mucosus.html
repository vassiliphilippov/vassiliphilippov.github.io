Ptyas mucosus

1. NOUN

/ ˈtaɪ.əs mjuˈkoʊ.səs / (AmE), / ˈtʃʌɪ.əs mjuːˈkəʊ.səs / (BrE)
a large nonvenomous ratsnake native to South and Southeast Asia, often found near human habitation
large South Asian nonvenomous snake

Examples

The Ptyas mucosus is frequently seen in rice fields and urban areas across India.
When threatened, Ptyas mucosus may produce a loud hissing sound to deter predators.
Farmers welcome Ptyas mucosus for its role in controlling the rodent population.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ratsnake - a type of nonvenomous snake that feeds mainly on rodents

Etymology

From New Latin; 'Ptyas' from Greek, meaning 'spitter', and 'mucosus' from Latin, meaning 'slimy' or 'mucous'.
