unstaged

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsteɪdʒd / (AmE), / ʌnˈsteɪdʒd / (BrE)
(of a play, performance, etc.) not performed or presented on a stage.
not performed on stage

Examples

The script remained unstaged for decades despite its critical acclaim.
Many unstaged works by the playwright were published posthumously.
His play was unstaged due to budget constraints.

Etymology

From un- (not) + staged (presented on stage).
