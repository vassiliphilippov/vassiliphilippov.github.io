Robert Burns

1. PROPN

/ ˈroʊbərt bɝːnz / (AmE), / ˈrɒbət bɜːnz / (BrE)
(1759–1796) A celebrated Scottish poet famous for his poems written in Scots and English, often focusing on themes of love, nature, and Scottish identity.
Scottish poet (1759–1796)

Examples

Every year, people around the world recite Robert Burns' poetry on Burns Night.
Many of Robert Burns' verses have become part of Scottish tradition.
The statue in the square is a tribute to Robert Burns and his literary legacy.
