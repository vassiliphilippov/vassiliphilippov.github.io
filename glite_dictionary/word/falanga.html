falanga

1. NOUN

/ fəˈlɑːŋɡə / (AmE), / fəˈlæŋɡə / (BrE)
A form of torture in which the soles of the feet are beaten with whips, sticks, or cudgels.
beating soles of feet as torture
legal

Examples

The prisoners reported that they had been subjected to falanga during their interrogation.
Medical examiners found bruising on the feet consistent with falanga.
Human rights organizations are campaigning to ban the use of falanga as a method of torture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bastinado - a form of punishment or torture involving beating the soles of the feet

Etymology

From Italian or Spanish 'falanga', ultimately from Greek 'phalagg-', meaning 'log' or 'stick'.
