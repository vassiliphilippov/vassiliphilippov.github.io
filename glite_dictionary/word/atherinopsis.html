Atherinopsis

1. NOUN

/ æθəˌrɪˈnɑpsɪs / (AmE), / ˌæθərɪˈnɒpsɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small fish in the family Atherinidae, commonly known as silversides, found along the Pacific coast of North America.
genus of silverside fish

Examples

The genus Atherinopsis is native to the Pacific coastal waters of North America.
Biologists have identified several species within the Atherinopsis genus.
Atherinopsis is an important group for studying the evolution of silverside fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Atherinidae - the family of fish commonly called silversides

Etymology

From Greek 'atherina' (a kind of fish) and 'opsis' (appearance).

2. NOUN

/ æθəˌrɪˈnɑpsɪs / (AmE), / æθəˌrɪˈnɒpsɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small, silvery fishes in the family Atherinidae, commonly found in coastal waters of the Pacific Ocean.
genus of small silvery fishes

Examples

The Atherinopsis genus includes several Pacific silverside species found along North American coasts.
Researchers studied the behavior of juvenile Atherinopsis in tidal pools.
Members of the Atherinopsis genus are important in coastal food webs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Atherinidae - family of silversides (fish) to which Atherinopsis belongs

Etymology

From Greek 'athēr' (a kind of fish) and '-opsis' (appearance).
