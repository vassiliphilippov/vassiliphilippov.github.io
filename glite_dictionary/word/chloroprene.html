chloroprene

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɔːrəˌprin / (AmE), / ˈklɔːrəpriːn / (BrE)
A colorless, volatile liquid hydrocarbon used in the production of synthetic rubber, especially neoprene.
chemical used to make neoprene

Examples

Industrial production of neoprene relies on the polymerization of chloroprene.
The company imported several tons of chloroprene for its manufacturing process.
Long-term exposure to chloroprene may pose health risks to workers.

Etymology

From 'chloro-' (indicating chlorine) and 'prene', an alteration of butadiene.
