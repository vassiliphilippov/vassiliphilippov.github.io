pibit

1. NOUN

/ ˈpiːˌbɪt / (AmE), / ˈpiːˌbɪt / (BrE)
a unit of digital information equal to 1,125,899,906,842,624 (2^50) bits, or 1,024 tebibits
unit of digital information (2^50 bits)
medical

Examples

Modern data centers can store several pibits of information across thousands of servers.
A pibit is significantly larger than a terabit or a tebibit, making it useful for measuring vast datasets.
The laboratory measured the experiment's output at 2.5 pibits per second, a record-breaking rate.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: tebibit - a unit of information equal to 2^40 bits
  • Degree Higher: exbibit - a unit of information equal to 2^60 bits

Etymology

Formed from 'pebi-' (from 'peta binary') + 'bit'.
