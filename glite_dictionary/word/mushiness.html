mushiness

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌʃi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈmʌʃɪnɪs / (BrE)
a quality of being excessively sentimental or overly emotional, especially in a way that seems insincere or false
excessive or false sentimentality

Examples

The movie's mushiness made some viewers roll their eyes in disbelief.
She disliked the mushiness of romantic comedies, preferring something more realistic.
There was a certain mushiness in his speech that seemed insincere to many listeners.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sentimentality - excessive expression of feelings of tenderness or sadness
  • Antonym: hardness - lack of tenderness or sentimentality

Etymology

From 'mushy' (overly sentimental), itself from 'mush' (soft mass).

2. NOUN

/ ˈmʌʃi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈmʌʃɪnɪs / (BrE)
The characteristic of being soft and pulpy in texture, often resembling mush.
soft and pulpy texture

Examples

I found the mushiness of the overripe banana quite unappetizing.
The mushiness of her old pillow made it uncomfortable to sleep on.
Some people dislike the mushiness in romantic movies, preferring more subtle emotions.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: firmness - the state of being solid or rigid
  • Synonym: softness - the quality of being soft

Etymology

From 'mushy' + '-ness', denoting state or quality.
