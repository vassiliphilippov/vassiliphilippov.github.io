practiced

1. ADJ

/ ˈpræktɪst / (AmE), / ˈprɑːktɪst / (BrE)
experienced and skillful as a result of much practice or training.
skillful from much practice

Examples

She gave a practiced performance that impressed everyone in the audience.
With a practiced hand, the chef diced the vegetables swiftly and accurately.
His practiced eye quickly noticed the mistake in the report.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skilled - having skill; proficient
  • Antonym: inexperienced - lacking experience or skill

2. ADJ

/ ˈpræk.tɪst / (AmE), / ˈpræk.tɪst / (BrE)
having experience, training, or skill in a particular activity, especially as a result of repeated practice
experienced; skilled through practice

Examples

The lawyer gave a practiced response to the difficult question.
She has a practiced hand when it comes to calligraphy.
Years of practiced movements made the surgeon's work appear effortless.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skilled - having skill; skilled, experienced
  • Antonym: inexperienced - lacking experience or skill

Etymology

Past participle of the verb ‘practice’.
