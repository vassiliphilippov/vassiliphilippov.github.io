granted

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡræn.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈɡrɑːn.tɪd / (BrE)
used to admit or accept that something is true before making another point
used to accept a point before making another

Examples

Granted, he is young, but he has shown great maturity for his age.
Granted that the weather is unpredictable, we should still plan the event.
Granted, it’s an expensive purchase, but it will last for years.

Synset Examples

given the engine's condition, it is a wonder that it started

Related Terms

  • Synonym: admittedly - used to express concession before a contrasting statement
  • Antonym: nevertheless - in spite of what has just been said
