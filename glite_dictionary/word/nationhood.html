nationhood

1. NOUN

/ ˈneɪʃənˌhʊd / (AmE), / ˈneɪʃənhʊd / (BrE)
the condition of being an independent nation or having the qualities that constitute a nation
state or status of being a nation

Examples

After years of struggle, the region finally achieved nationhood and was recognized internationally.
Debates about nationhood often center on issues of identity and sovereignty.
The celebrations marked the 50th anniversary of the country's nationhood.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: statehood - the condition of being a recognized state, especially in a political sense

Etymology

Formed from 'nation' + the suffix '-hood' (denoting state or condition).
