Filicopsida

1. NOUN

/ ˌfɪlɪˈkɑpsɪdə / (AmE), / ˌfɪlɪˈkɒpsɪdə / (BrE)
A class of vascular plants comprising the true ferns, characterized by large, divided leaves and reproducing by spores.
class of ferns

Examples

All members of Filicopsida reproduce by means of spores rather than seeds.
The garden houses several rare species within the Filicopsida class.
Researchers studied the fossil record to trace the evolution of Filicopsida over millions of years.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fern - a non-flowering vascular plant with feathery fronds; member of Filicopsida

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Latin 'filix' (fern) + Greek 'opsis' (appearance).

2. NOUN

/ fɪlɪˈkɑːpsɪdə / (AmE), / fɪlɪˈkɒpsɪdə / (BrE)
A class of plants comprising all ferns, characterized by large, divided leaves and reproducing by spores.
class of plants: ferns

Examples

Scientists classify all true ferns under the class Filicopsida.
The fossil record of Filicopsida dates back millions of years.
In the botanical garden, specimens from Filicopsida are displayed in a special fern house.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fern - A nonflowering vascular plant reproducing by spores

Etymology

From Latin 'filix' (fern) and Greek 'opsis' (appearance).
