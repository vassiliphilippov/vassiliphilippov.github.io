deserving

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈzɝvɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈzɜːvɪŋ / (BrE)
worthy of being given help, praise, punishment, or another treatment; having qualities that merit a particular response, often used to describe those seen as entitled to aid or recognition.
worthy of a particular treatment

Examples

Many charities focus on helping the deserving poor in the community.
She received an award as a deserving student for her hard work and dedication.
Not everyone agreed that the organization was truly deserving of public support.

Synset Examples

an idea worth considering
the deserving poor
a deserving cause

Related Terms

  • Antonym: undeserving - not worthy of a particular treatment

Etymology

From the verb 'deserve', from Old French 'deservir', from Latin 'deservire' (to serve zealously).
