Araxes

1. PROPN

/ əˈræksiːz / (AmE), / əˈræksiːz / (BrE)
a river rising in northeastern Turkey, flowing eastward through Armenia into the Caspian Sea; historically known as Araxes
river flowing from Turkey through Armenia to the Caspian Sea

Examples

The ancient Greeks referred to the Aras river as the Araxes.
The Araxes forms part of the border between Armenia and Iran.
Her research traced the historical settlements along the Araxes basin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Aras - modern name for the Araxes river

Etymology

From Latin Araxes, from Ancient Greek Ἀράξης (Aráxēs); ancient name of the river now largely called Aras.
