Brassica rapa perviridis

1. NOUN

/ brəˌsɪkə ˈræpə ˌpɜr.vɪˈri.dɪs / (AmE), / brəˌsɪkə ˈræpə ˌpɜː.vɪˈrɪ.dɪs / (BrE)
An Asian leafy green plant cultivated for its edible foliage and sometimes its swollen root, used in cooking and salads.
Asian leafy green vegetable

Examples

Komatsuna, a variety of Brassica rapa perviridis, is commonly used in Japanese cuisine.
The gardener grew several rows of Brassica rapa perviridis for its tender green leaves.
Research has shown that Brassica rapa perviridis is rich in vitamins and beneficial phytochemicals.

Etymology

Latin: 'Brassica' meaning cabbage, 'rapa' meaning turnip, 'perviridis' meaning very green.
