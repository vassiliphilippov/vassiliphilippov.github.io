2. NOUN

/ prɔn / (AmE), / prɔːn / (BrE)
A small edible sea animal with a shell and ten legs, similar to a shrimp.
edible shrimp-like sea animal

Examples

She ordered grilled prawn salad for lunch at the seaside restaurant.
Fresh prawns are often sold at the fish market every morning.
The chef sautéed the prawns in garlic butter for a delicious appetizer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrimp - small, slender decapod crustacean similar to prawn

Etymology

From Middle French *praon*, of uncertain origin, possibly related to Middle Dutch *prael* (a kind of shellfish).

3. NOUN

/ prɔn / (AmE), / prɔːn / (BrE)
A small edible shellfish with a long body and legs, similar to a shrimp, commonly used as food.
small edible shellfish similar to shrimp

Examples

She ordered a plate of prawn curry at the seaside restaurant.
Fresh prawns are often served grilled with a squeeze of lemon.
The chef recommends using prawn stock to enhance the flavor of seafood soup.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shrimp - a small edible crustacean similar to a prawn

Etymology

From Middle English *praune*, probably from Middle French *praun* or from Dutch *praaun*.
