larceny

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑːrsəni / (AmE), / ˈlɑːsəni / (BrE)
the unlawful taking of someone else's property with the intent to permanently deprive them of it; theft
theft; unlawful taking of property
legal

Examples

He was charged with larceny after stealing several items from the store.
The court found her guilty of grand larceny due to the high value of the stolen goods.
Petty larceny is considered a misdemeanor in many jurisdictions.

Synset Examples

the thieving is awful at Kennedy International

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theft - the act of stealing something
  • Synonym: burglary - breaking into a building to commit a crime, usually theft
  • Synonym: robbery - the act of taking property unlawfully from a person or place by force or threat

Etymology

From Old French larcin (theft), from Latin latrocinium (robbery).
