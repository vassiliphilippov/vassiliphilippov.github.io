lysis

1. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪsɪs / (AmE), / ˈlaɪsɪs / (BrE)
the disintegration or destruction of cells, especially by rupture of the cell membrane, often as a result of infection or chemical action
destruction of cells (biochemistry)
medical

Examples

The sample was treated with a detergent to induce lysis of bacterial cells.
The infection caused the lysis of red blood cells, leading to anemia.
Rapid cell lysis can release toxins into the bloodstream.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lysis - breakdown of cells by chemical or physical means
  • Synonym: lysis - dissolution of cells, especially in medicine or biology

Etymology

From Greek lysis (a loosening, dissolution), from lyein (to loosen).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlaɪsɪs / (AmE), / ˈlaɪsɪs / (BrE)
The gradual decline of disease symptoms, especially in an acute illness, indicating recovery.
gradual decline of disease symptoms (medical)
medical

Examples

After several days, the patient showed signs of lysis, with his fever steadily decreasing.
The doctor explained that lysis was a positive indicator of recovery from the infection.
In some illnesses, lysis occurs naturally as the body overcomes the pathogen.

Etymology

From Greek lysis (loosening, dissolution), from lyein (to loosen).
