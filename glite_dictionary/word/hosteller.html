hosteller

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑːs.tə.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈhɒs.tə.lə / (BrE)
a person who owns or manages a hotel
hotel owner or manager

Examples

The experienced hosteller greeted every guest with a warm smile.
Sarah aspired to become a successful hosteller in the city.
As a hosteller, he ensured the hotel's operations ran smoothly every day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hotelier - a person who owns or manages a hotel

Etymology

From Old French 'hostelier' (innkeeper), from 'hostel' (inn).

WordNet ID: oewn-10207609-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɑstələr / (AmE), / ˈhɒstələ / (BrE)
a person who stays in hostels, especially while traveling
person lodging in hostels

Examples

The hosteller checked in late and quickly unpacked in her dorm room.
As a seasoned hosteller, he prefers communal living over private hotels.
Each hosteller must bring their own bed linen and toiletries.

Synset Examples

a youth hosteller

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guest - a person who stays at a place temporarily
  • Antonym: resident - a person who lives somewhere permanently or long-term

Etymology

From hostel + -er, influenced by Middle English 'hostiler' (innkeeper) from Old French 'hostelier'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10206569-n
Synset POS: n
