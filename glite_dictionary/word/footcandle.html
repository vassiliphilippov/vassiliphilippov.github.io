footcandle

1. NOUN

/ ˈfʊtˌkændl / (AmE), / ˈfʊtˌkændl / (BrE)
a unit of illuminance equal to the illumination cast on a surface by a source of one candle at a distance of one foot
unit of illuminance

Examples

The laboratory was illuminated to a level of 100 footcandles for the experiment.
Museum exhibits should be lit with no more than 15 footcandles to prevent damage.
He measured the brightness in footcandles using a handheld light meter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lux - unit of illuminance in the metric system

Etymology

Formed from 'foot' (unit of length) and 'candle' (unit of luminous intensity).
