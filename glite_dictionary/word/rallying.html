rallying

1. NOUN

/ ˈræliɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈræliɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of gathering people together for a common purpose, particularly to show support or take collective action.
act of gathering in support or action

Examples

The rallying of local residents led to changes in the council’s decision.
A sense of rallying swept through the community after the announcement.
Their rallying behind the cause inspired others to join.

Synset Examples

the bell was a signal for the rallying of the whole neighborhood

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assembly - a group gathered together for a common purpose
  • Antonym: dispersal - the act of scattering or breaking up a group

Etymology

From the verb 'rally', from French 'rallier' (to reunite).

2. ADJ

/ ˈræliɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈræliɪŋ / (BrE)
bringing people together to inspire unity and renewed effort, especially in support of a cause or action.
inspiring unity and renewed effort

Examples

The leader delivered a rallying speech to boost the troops' morale.
They issued a rallying call to supporters ahead of the decisive vote.
Her words had a rallying effect on the divided community.

Synset Examples

a rallying cry

Related Terms

  • Synonym: inspiring - arousing enthusiasm or motivation

Etymology

From the verb 'rally', from French 'rallier' (to reunite), based on Latin 'ad-' (to) + 'alligare' (to bind).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00870400-s
Synset POS: s

3. NOUN

/ ˈræliɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈræliɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of gathering strength, support, or resources for a renewed effort, often after a setback
mustering for renewed effort

Examples

With a sudden rallying of their supporters, the team made a remarkable comeback.
The general's rallying of his troops turned the tide of the battle.
After a period of doubt, her rallying inspired the whole group to keep going.

Synset Examples

he singled to start a rally in the 9th inning
he feared the rallying of their troops for a counterattack

Related Terms

  • Synonym: counterattack - a tactical attack made in response to an enemy's attack

Etymology

From the verb 'rally', which comes from French 'rallier' (to reunite, to rally).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00046387-n
Synset POS: n
