direful

1. ADJ

/ ˈdaɪɚfəl / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəf(ə)l / (BrE)
causing fear, dread, or terror; extremely bad or threatening, often used in literary or archaic contexts.
causing fear or dread (literary/archaic)
archaic

Examples

The news of the sudden invasion struck the people as truly direful.
In his direful anger, he swore retribution upon them all.
A direful silence fell over the city after the disaster.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dreadful - causing or involving great suffering, fear, or unhappiness
  • Synonym: terrible - causing fear, dread, or terror; extremely bad
  • Antonym: comforting - making you feel better, less worried or frightened

Etymology

From Latin 'dirus' (fearful, ominous) + English '-ful'.
