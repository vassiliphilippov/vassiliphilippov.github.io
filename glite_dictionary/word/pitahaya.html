pitahaya

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɪtəˈhaɪə / (AmE), / ˌpɪtəˈhaɪə / (BrE)
A brightly colored edible fruit of certain cacti, typically with red or yellow skin and sweet flesh, also known as dragon fruit.
edible fruit from cactus plant

Examples

The pitahaya has a vibrant pink skin and a sweet, refreshing flavor.
Farmers markets often sell fresh pitahayas during the summer season.
I added slices of pitahaya to the fruit salad for extra color and taste.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dragon fruit - another common name for pitahaya

Etymology

From Spanish 'pitahaya', from Taíno, the name for the fruit-bearing cactus.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɪtəˈhaɪ.ə / (AmE), / ˌpɪtəˈhɑː.jə / (BrE)
A cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, known for its edible, juicy fruit.
cactus with edible fruit

Examples

The pitahaya is prized for its sweet, refreshing taste and striking appearance.
Local farmers cultivate pitahayas for both domestic markets and export.
I tried a slice of pitahaya at the botanical garden's fruit festival.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dragon fruit - the fruit of several cactus species, also known as pitaya

Etymology

From Spanish 'pitahaya', from Taíno (indigenous Caribbean language).
