certified

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɝː.təˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.tɪ.faɪd / (BrE)
officially recognized as having met specific requirements or standards, often by a certificate or authority.
officially recognized as qualified

Examples

She is a certified yoga instructor with years of experience.
Only certified electricians are allowed to perform this type of work.
He became a certified translator after passing a rigorous exam.

Synset Examples

a certified public accountant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: qualified - officially recognized as having the necessary skills, qualifications, or knowledge

Etymology

From the verb 'certify', from Old French 'certifier', from Latin 'certificare' (to make certain).

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɝː.təˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.tɪ.faɪd / (BrE)
Regarded as officially insane and treated accordingly.
officially declared insane

Examples

After the hearing, he was declared certified and sent to a mental institution.
The novel's villain is not just evil, but certified, according to the doctors in the story.
In those days, anyone deemed certified risked losing all personal rights and autonomy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insane - mentally ill; not of sound mind

Etymology

From the verb 'certify', from Old French certifier, from Late Latin certificare.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02082724-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˈsɝː.t̬ə.faɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.tɪ.faɪd / (BrE)
officially recognized as having met certain standards or qualifications, especially through documentation or examination
officially qualified or licensed

Examples

She hired a certified accountant to handle her finances.
Only certified electricians are allowed to install the wiring in this building.
After passing several exams, he became a certified lifeguard.

Synset Examples

a registered pharmacist
a registered hospital

Related Terms

  • Synonym: licensed - officially given permission to do something, especially a profession
  • Antonym: unauthorized - not having official permission or approval

Etymology

From certify + -ed, originally from Latin 'certificare' (to make certain).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01965069-s
Synset POS: s
