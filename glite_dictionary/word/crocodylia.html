Crocodylia

1. NOUN

/ ˌkrɑːkəˈdɪliə / (AmE), / ˌkrɒkəˈdɪliə / (BrE)
an order of large, mostly aquatic, reptiles that includes crocodiles, alligators, caimans, and gavials
order of crocodilian reptiles

Examples

The order Crocodylia includes not only crocodiles but also alligators and caimans.
Scientists have studied the evolution of Crocodylia for centuries.
Members of Crocodylia are among the largest living reptiles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crocodile - large aquatic reptile with a long snout, member of Crocodylia
  • Degree Lower: Alligatoridae - family within Crocodylia containing alligators and caimans

Etymology

From Greek krokódeilos (crocodile) via Modern Latin Crocodylia.
