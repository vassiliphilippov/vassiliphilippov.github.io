extortionate

1. ADJ

/ ɪkˈstɔrʃənət / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɔːʃənət / (BrE)
(of a price or demand) much higher than is reasonable; excessive or exorbitant.
excessively high (especially price or demand)

Examples

Many families were unable to afford the extortionate rent in the city center.
The mechanic charged an extortionate fee for a simple repair.
They protested against the extortionate cost of basic utilities.

Synset Examples

exorbitant rent
extortionate prices
spends an outrageous amount on entertainment
usurious interest rate
unconscionable spending

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exorbitant - (of prices, amounts, etc.) unreasonably high
  • Antonym: reasonable - not extreme or excessive in amount, moderate
  • Synonym: outrageous - shockingly high, excessive

Etymology

From extortion + -ate, formed by analogy to similar adjectives.
