nap

1. NOUN

/ næp / (AmE), / næp / (BrE)
a short period of sleep, usually taken during the day and not in bed
a short daytime sleep

Examples

He took a quick nap on the sofa before dinner.
Sometimes I feel much better after a short afternoon nap.
The baby fell asleep for a nap in her stroller.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doze - to sleep lightly or briefly
  • Synonym: snooze - a short, light sleep, especially during the day
  • Degree Higher: sleep - the state of resting with closed eyes and reduced consciousness

Etymology

Possibly from Middle English *nappen*, from Old English *hnappian* (to doze).
