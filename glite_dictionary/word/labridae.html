Labridae

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæbrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈlæbrɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a large family of marine fishes, known as wrasses, characterized by bright colors and found chiefly in tropical seas
family of wrasse fishes

Examples

The Labridae family includes more than 600 species of wrasses worldwide.
Colorful members of the Labridae can often be seen around coral reefs.
Researchers studied the feeding habits of several Labridae species in the Red Sea.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wrasse - a marine fish of the family Labridae

Etymology

From modern Latin Labridae, from Labrus (the type genus), from Greek labros (greedy).
