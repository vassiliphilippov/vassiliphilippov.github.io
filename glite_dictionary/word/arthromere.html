arthromere

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑrθroʊˌmɪr / (AmE), / ˈɑːθrəʊˌmɪə / (BrE)
any of the body segments of a jointed animal, such as an arthropod
body segment of a jointed animal

Examples

Each insect's body is divided into a series of arthromeres that form distinct regions.
The segmentation of crustaceans can be observed by counting their arthromeres.
In arachnids, each arthromere has its own set of nerves and muscles.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: somite - body segment in animals, especially in embryonic development

Etymology

From Greek 'arthron' (joint) and 'meros' (part, segment).
