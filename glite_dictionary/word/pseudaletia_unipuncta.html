Pseudaletia unipuncta

1. NOUN

/ ˌsjuːdəˈliːʃiə ˌjuːnɪˈpʌŋktə / (AmE), / ˌsjuːdəˈliːʃɪə ˌjuːnɪˈpʌŋktə / (BrE)
a moth species whose larvae, known as armyworms, are destructive to crops and often move in large groups
moth species, larvae called armyworms

Examples

Pseudaletia unipuncta infestations have caused severe damage to wheat fields across the region.
The larvae of Pseudaletia unipuncta, commonly known as armyworms, can devastate crops in a short time.
Farmers monitor their fields closely during summer for signs of Pseudaletia unipuncta outbreaks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: armyworm - larval stage of certain moths, feeds on crops

Etymology

From New Latin; genus 'Pseudaletia' and species 'unipuncta', meaning 'one-spotted'.

2. NOUN

/ suːdəˈliːʃiə juːnɪˈpʌŋktə / (AmE), / sjuːdəˈliːtiə juːnɪˈpʌŋktə / (BrE)
a species of noctuid moth whose larvae, known as armyworms, travel in large groups and can destroy grass and grain crops
species of noctuid moth, known as armyworm

Examples

Farmers worry each year that Pseudaletia unipuncta will damage their wheat fields.
The larvae of Pseudaletia unipuncta can devastate entire crops when they swarm.
Entomologists monitor populations of Pseudaletia unipuncta to predict potential outbreaks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: armyworm - larval stage of certain moth species that moves in groups and destroys crops

Etymology

From New Latin; 'Pseudaletia' from Greek elements 'pseudo-' (false) and 'Aletia' (another moth genus), 'unipuncta' meaning 'one-spotted' in Latin.
