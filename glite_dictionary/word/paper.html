paper

1. NOUN

/ ˈpeɪpɚ / (AmE), / ˈpeɪpə / (BrE)
Material manufactured in thin sheets from wood pulp, rags, or other fibrous substances, used for writing, printing, or drawing on.
material used for writing or printing

Examples

He prefers to read documents on paper rather than on a computer screen.
The invention of paper revolutionized written communication around the world.
They ran out of paper in the printer and had to buy more.

Synset Examples

the notion of an office running without paper is absurd

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parchment - material made from animal skin used for writing

Etymology

From Latin 'papyrus', via Old French 'papier'.
