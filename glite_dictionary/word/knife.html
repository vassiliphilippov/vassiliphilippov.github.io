knife

1. NOUN

/ naɪf / (AmE), / naɪf / (BrE)
A weapon consisting of a handle and a blade with a sharp point, used for stabbing or thrusting.
pointed stabbing weapon

Examples

The suspect was found carrying a knife with a sharp, gleaming blade.
He drew his knife in self-defense during the altercation.
The ancient warrior brandished a ceremonial knife at his enemies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dagger - a short knife with a pointed and edged blade, used as a weapon
  • Degree Higher: sword - a long-bladed weapon used for cutting or thrusting

Etymology

From Old English *cnīf* (knife, dagger), related to Old Norse *knífr* (knife).

2. NOUN

/ naɪf / (AmE), / naɪf / (BrE)
A tool with a sharp, pointed blade and a handle, used for cutting.
sharp-edged cutting tool

Examples

She carefully cut the vegetables with a sharp knife.
He always carries a pocket knife when hiking in the woods.
The chef selected his favorite knife for slicing the fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dagger - a short knife with a pointed and edged blade
  • Antonym: spoon - an eating or cooking implement with a bowl-shaped end

Etymology

From Old English 'cnīf', related to Old Norse 'knīfr' (knife, blade).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03628765-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ naɪf / (AmE), / naɪf / (BrE)
to attack or wound someone with a knife.
attack or wound with a knife

Examples

He was knifed outside the nightclub last night.
The suspect allegedly knifed his way through the crowd to escape.
She saw someone knifing open a package behind the store.

Synset Examples

The victim was knifed to death

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stab - to thrust a pointed weapon into

Etymology

From Old Norse 'knifr', originally a noun; the verb usage is derived from the noun.
