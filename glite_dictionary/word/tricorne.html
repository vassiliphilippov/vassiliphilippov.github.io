tricorne

1. NOUN

/ traɪˈkɔrn / (AmE), / trɪˈkɔːn / (BrE)
a hat with the brim turned up on three sides to form three points, popular in the 18th century
three-cornered hat, 18th-century style

Examples

The actor wore a tricorne as part of his colonial costume for the reenactment.
Paintings from the era often depict gentlemen in powdered wigs and tricorne hats.
He adjusted his tricorne before addressing the crowd during the parade.

Etymology

From French tricorne, from tri- (three) + corne (horn, corner).
