Acanthisittidae

1. NOUN

/ əˌkænθɪˈsɪtɪdiː / (AmE), / əˌkanθɪˈsɪtɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of tiny passerine birds native to New Zealand, known as the New Zealand wrens.
family of New Zealand wrens (birds)

Examples

The Acanthisittidae are considered one of the most ancient passerine families in the world.
Only a few species of Acanthisittidae now survive in isolated parts of New Zealand.
Recent studies have shed light on the evolutionary history of the Acanthisittidae.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: New Zealand wren - Any species of bird in the Acanthisittidae family

Etymology

From New Latin, from genus name Acanthisitta + -idae (family suffix in zoology).

2. NOUN

/ əˌkænθɪˈsɪtəˌdi / (AmE), / əˌkanθɪˈsɪtədiː / (BrE)
A family of small passerine birds known as New Zealand wrens, endemic to New Zealand.
family of New Zealand wrens (birds)

Examples

The Acanthisittidae represent one of the most ancient lineages of songbirds.
All members of the Acanthisittidae are native to New Zealand.
Scientists study the ecology and evolution of Acanthisittidae to learn about island biogeography.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on genus Acanthisitta and the family suffix -idae.
