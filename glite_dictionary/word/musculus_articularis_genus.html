musculus articularis genus

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌs.kjʊ.ləs ɑrˌtɪk.jəˈleɪ.rɪs ˈdʒiː.nəs / (AmE), / ˈmʌs.kjʊ.ləs ɑːˌtɪk.jʊˈleə.rɪs ˈdʒiː.nəs / (BrE)
a small muscle of the knee joint that lifts the synovial membrane during extension of the leg
knee joint muscle
medical

Examples

The musculus articularis genus helps to prevent the synovial membrane from being pinched during knee extension.
Injury to the musculus articularis genus can sometimes cause discomfort around the front of the knee.
Anatomists study the musculus articularis genus to better understand knee joint movement.

Etymology

From Latin: musculus (muscle), articularis (articular/joint), genus (knee).
