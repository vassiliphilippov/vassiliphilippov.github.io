lemur

1. NOUN

/ ˈliːmɚ / (AmE), / ˈliːmə / (BrE)
a small, primate mammal native to Madagascar, with large eyes, a fox-like face, and a long furry tail, known for its arboreal lifestyle
a tree-dwelling primate of Madagascar

Examples

The lemur used its long tail for balance as it leapt between tree branches.
Children at the zoo were fascinated by the large eyes of the lemur.
Conservation efforts are crucial to protect endangered species of lemur in Madagascar.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: primate - an order of mammals including lemurs, monkeys, and apes
  • Synonym: prosimians - primitive primates including lemurs and related species

Etymology

From Latin 'lemurēs' (ghosts, spirits), referring to their nocturnal, elusive nature.
