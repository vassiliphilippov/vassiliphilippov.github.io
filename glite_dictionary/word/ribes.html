Ribes

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪbiːz / (AmE), / ˈriːbiːz / (BrE)
A genus of flowering shrubs that produces currants or gooseberries, native to the Northern Hemisphere.
genus of shrubs producing currants or gooseberries

Examples

The Ribes genus includes both cultivated and wild species of currants.
Gardeners often plant Ribes shrubs to attract birds and bees.
Some Ribes plants are valued for their edible fruits.

Etymology

From Latin 'ribes', borrowed from Arabic 'rībās' (a type of plant).
