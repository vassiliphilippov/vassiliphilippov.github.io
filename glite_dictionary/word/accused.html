accused

1. NOUN

/ əˈkjuzd / (AmE), / əˈkjuːzd / (BrE)
a person or group of people who are officially charged with a crime in a court of law
person charged with a crime
legal

Examples

The accused pleaded not guilty to all charges.
During the trial, the accused remained silent while the evidence was presented.
The judge reminded the jury that the accused is presumed innocent until proven guilty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: defendant - person being sued or accused in a court of law
  • Antonym: plaintiff - person who brings a case against another in a court of law

Etymology

From the verb 'accuse', from Old French 'acuser', from Latin 'accusare' (to call to account, accuse).
