excitation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛksaɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛksaɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
A state of being emotionally aroused or stirred up, often characterized by heightened feelings or agitation.
state of emotional arousal

Examples

The announcement created a wave of excitation among the students.
She tried to hide her excitation but her smile gave her away.
After the thrilling match, there was a noticeable excitation in the crowd.

Synset Examples

his face was flushed with excitement and his hands trembled
he tried to calm those who were in a state of extreme inflammation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excitement - a feeling of great enthusiasm and eagerness
  • Antonym: calm - a state of being peaceful and quiet, not emotionally aroused

Etymology

From Latin excitatio (an awakening, arousing), from excitare (to arouse).

3. NOUN

/ ˌɛksaɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛksaɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a nerve, muscle, or gland is stimulated by neural or electrical activity, leading to increased activity or response
neural or electrical stimulation of an organ or tissue
medical

Examples

The excitation of the nerves caused the muscle to contract rapidly.
During a heartbeat, electrical excitation spreads throughout the cardiac muscle.
Recent studies measure the excitation of glands in response to hormonal signals.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stimulation - the act of rousing to activity or increased action

Etymology

From Latin excitare (to arouse, stimulate) + -tion.
