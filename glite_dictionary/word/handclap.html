handclap

1. NOUN

/ ˈhændˌklæp / (AmE), / ˈhændˌklæp / (BrE)
a clap of the hands, typically to show approval or appreciation
the act of clapping hands

Examples

A single handclap echoed through the silent auditorium.
Her performance was met with a spontaneous handclap from the audience.
The teacher signaled the end of the activity with a loud handclap.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: applause - the act of clapping to show approval

Etymology

From 'hand' + 'clap', both of Old English origin.
