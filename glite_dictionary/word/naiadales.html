Naiadales

1. NOUN

/ ˌnaɪ.əˈdeɪ.liːz / (AmE), / ˌnaɪ.əˈdeɪ.liːz / (BrE)
an order of aquatic monocotyledonous herbaceous plants, including pondweeds and related families
order of aquatic monocot plants

Examples

The aquatic plants classified in the Naiadales are found in freshwater habitats worldwide.
Many botanists study the diversity of species within the Naiadales order.
Pondweeds, part of the Naiadales, play a vital role in aquatic ecosystems.

Etymology

From New Latin, after 'Naias' (the type genus) and the suffix '-ales' used for plant orders.
