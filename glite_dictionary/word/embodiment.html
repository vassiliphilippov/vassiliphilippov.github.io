embodiment

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈbɑdi.mənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɒd.i.mənt / (BrE)
A tangible or visible form of an idea, quality, or feeling; the representation or expression of something in a concrete form.
tangible form of an idea or quality

Examples

She is the very embodiment of kindness, always helping others in need.
This statue is considered the embodiment of ancient wisdom in the community.
His bravery became the embodiment of the team's fighting spirit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: personification - the attribution of human characteristics to something non-human or the representation of an abstract quality in human form
  • Synonym: incarnation - a living embodiment of a deity or spirit, or a concrete or physical form of a quality

Etymology

From French *embodiment* or *embodir*, based on English 'embody'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪmˈbɑdimənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɒdɪmənt / (BrE)
a person or thing that represents or is a visible or tangible form of an idea, quality, or feeling
concrete expression of an abstract concept

Examples

The statue was considered the embodiment of freedom by the city's residents.
Mother Teresa is often described as the embodiment of compassion and selflessness.
For many, the hero of the novel becomes the embodiment of hope and resilience.

Synset Examples

a circle was the embodiment of his concept of life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: personification - the representation of an abstract quality in human form
  • Synonym: incarnation - a concrete or physical form of a quality or idea

Etymology

From 'embody' + '-ment'; 'embody' from Old French 'embodier'.

3. NOUN

/ ɪmˈbɑdi.mənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɒdɪ.mənt / (BrE)
a person or thing that represents or is the perfect example of an idea, quality, or concept
person or thing representing an idea

Examples

She is the very embodiment of kindness, always willing to help others.
For many, he became the embodiment of national pride during the competition.
This painting is considered the embodiment of Renaissance ideals.

Synset Examples

the embodiment of hope
the incarnation of evil
the very avatar of cunning

Related Terms

  • Synonym: incarnation - a person who embodies in the flesh a deity, spirit, or abstract quality
  • Synonym: personification - a person or thing regarded as representing a quality or concept

Etymology

From embody + -ment, ultimately based on Middle English 'embodyen' and French 'embodier'.
