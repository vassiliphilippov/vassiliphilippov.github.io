Lathyrus palustris

1. NOUN

/ ləˈθaɪərəs pəˈlʌstrɪs / (AmE), / ləˈθaɪərəs pəˈlʌstrɪs / (BrE)
a scrambling perennial plant of damp or marshy areas in Eurasia and North America, with purplish flowers; also known as marsh pea.
a marshland perennial plant with purplish flowers

Examples

The Lathyrus palustris thrives in wetlands across northern Europe and North America.
Researchers observed that Lathyrus palustris blooms from late spring to midsummer in marshy habitats.
Gardeners often plant Lathyrus palustris to attract pollinators to wetland restoration projects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marsh pea - common name for Lathyrus palustris

Etymology

From Latin: 'Lathyrus' (a genus of legumes), 'palustris' (of marshes).
