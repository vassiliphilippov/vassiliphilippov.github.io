to plead

1. VERB

/ plid / (AmE), / pliːd / (BrE)
to offer (a particular reason or excuse) as a defense or explanation, especially in a court of law
offer an excuse or defense

Examples

The defendant decided to plead self-defense during the trial.
She tried to plead ignorance when asked about the missing documents.
His lawyer advised him not to plead guilty to the charges.

Synset Examples

She was pleading insanity

Related Terms

  • Synonym: claim - to state that something is true, usually without proof

Etymology

From Old French 'plaider' (to argue a case), from Latin 'placitare'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00895886-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ plid / (AmE), / pliːd / (BrE)
(of a defendant) to formally state in a court of law whether you are guilty or not guilty of a crime.
declare guilt or innocence in court
legal

Examples

The defendant chose to plead not guilty at the arraignment.
He will plead guilty in exchange for a reduced sentence.
After consulting his lawyer, John decided to plead not guilty.

Synset Examples

She pleaded not guilty

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confess - to admit to a crime or wrongdoing, especially in court
  • Antonym: to deny - state that one is not guilty or not responsible for a crime

Etymology

From Old French *plaider* (to plead a case), from *plait* (lawsuit).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00762621-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ plid / (AmE), / pliːd / (BrE)
(legal) To formally state in a court of law whether one is guilty or not, or to make allegations or responses to claims in a legal proceeding.
formally state a case in court
legal

Examples

The defendant will plead not guilty at tomorrow's hearing.
Her lawyer advised her to plead self-defense during the trial.
The suspect refused to plead when asked about the charges in court.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to defend - to speak or write in favor of; support or justify
  • Antonym: to prosecute - to initiate legal proceedings against; bring to trial

Etymology

From Old French *plaider* (to argue a case), from Latin *placitare* (to plead, to argue).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01018325-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ plid / (AmE), / pliːd / (BrE)
to ask for something in an earnest or emotional way; to appeal or beg seriously
ask or beg earnestly

Examples

She pleaded for a second chance after making a mistake.
He pleaded with his parents to let him go to the concert.
The witness pleaded earnestly with the judge for leniency.

Synset Examples

I pleaded with him to stop

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to beg - to ask someone earnestly or humbly for something
  • Synonym: to implore - to beg someone earnestly or desperately to do something

Etymology

From Old French *plaider* (to argue a case), from Medieval Latin *placitare* (to plead a case).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00761546-v
Synset POS: v
