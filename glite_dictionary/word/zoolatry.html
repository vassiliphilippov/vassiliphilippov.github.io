zoolatry

1. NOUN

/ zuˈɑːlətri / (AmE), / zuˈɒlətri / (BrE)
the worship of animals, especially as deities or sacred beings
the worship of animals

Examples

Some ancient Egyptian religions practiced zoolatry, revering animals like cats and crocodiles as sacred.
Modern societies rarely engage in zoolatry, but it has been common throughout human history.
The anthropologist wrote extensively about zoolatry among indigenous peoples in South America.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: animal worship - worship of animals as part of religious or spiritual practice
  • Degree Higher: idolatry - worship of idols or physical objects as gods

Etymology

From Greek zôion (animal) + -latry (worship), after Greek 'zōolatreia'.
