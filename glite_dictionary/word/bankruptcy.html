bankruptcy

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæŋkˌrʌptsi / (AmE), / ˈbaŋkrəptsi / (BrE)
A complete lack of a particular quality or abstract property, especially used metaphorically (e.g., moral bankruptcy).
a complete lack of a quality (metaphorical use)

Examples

The leader's decision revealed a bankruptcy of principles within the organization.
Many critics pointed out the moral bankruptcy of the regime.
His argument demonstrated a profound intellectual bankruptcy.

Synset Examples

spiritual bankruptcy
moral bankruptcy
intellectual bankruptcy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insolvency - the inability to pay debts; actual financial bankruptcy
  • Degree Lower: poverty - the state of being poor; complete lack, often of material wealth

Etymology

From Italian *banca rotta* (broken bench), via French *banqueroute*, referring originally to insolvency.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbæŋkˌrʌptsi / (AmE), / ˈbæŋkrəptsi / (BrE)
The legal status of being unable to pay debts owed as they become due, often resulting in court proceedings to resolve outstanding obligations.
legal inability to pay debts

Examples

After years of mismanagement, the company finally filed for bankruptcy.
Personal bankruptcy can have long-lasting effects on your credit score.
The threat of bankruptcy forced the business to restructure its finances.

Synset Examples

the company had to declare bankruptcy
fraudulent loans led to the failure of many banks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insolvency - state of being unable to pay debts; very similar in meaning

Etymology

From Italian *banca rotta* (broken bench), referring to the breaking of a moneychanger's bench to indicate insolvency.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-14502017-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈbæŋkˌrʌptsi / (AmE), / ˈbaŋkrʌptsi / (BrE)
The legal process by which a person or business that is unable to pay their debts seeks relief from some or all of their financial obligations, often involving the distribution of assets among creditors.
legal process for insolvent debtors
legal

Examples

After months of struggling to pay its creditors, the company finally filed for bankruptcy.
Declaring bankruptcy allowed her to start rebuilding her finances from scratch.
During the bankruptcy proceedings, the court divided the assets among all the creditors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insolvency - Inability to pay debts owed

Etymology

From Italian *banca rotta* (broken bench), referring to the banking practice of breaking a moneylender's bench to signify insolvency.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
