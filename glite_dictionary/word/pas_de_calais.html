Pas de Calais

1. PROPN

/ pɑ də kæˈleɪ / (AmE), / pa də kaˈlɛ / (BrE)
the strait between the English Channel and the North Sea, forming the shortest crossing between England and the European continent
strait between English Channel and North Sea

Examples

Many ships travel daily through the Pas de Calais due to its strategic location.
The Pas de Calais is known for being the narrowest part of the English Channel.
During World War II, the Allies feigned an invasion across the Pas de Calais.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: English Channel - the sea separating southern England from northern France

Etymology

From French 'Pas de Calais', meaning 'strait of Calais'.
