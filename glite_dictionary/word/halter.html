halter

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɔltər / (AmE), / ˈhɔːltə / (BrE)
a woman's top that is held up by straps which fasten behind the neck and back, leaving the back and arms uncovered
woman's top with straps tied at neck and back

Examples

She chose a bright red halter for the beach party.
The halter reveals her bare back and shoulders beautifully.
Many summer collections feature the halter as a popular style.

Etymology

From earlier use meaning 'a strap around the neck' from Old English 'hæltor' (halter for horses or cattle).

2. NOUN

/ ˈhɔltɚ / (AmE), / ˈhɔːltə / (BrE)
either of the small, knobbed structures behind the wings of dipterous insects, used to maintain balance during flight
balancing organ in some insects

Examples

The halter of a fly are essential for its ability to stabilize in the air.
Unlike other insect wings, the halter are reduced to small, club-shaped structures.
Scientists studied the movement of the halter to understand insect flight mechanics.

Etymology

From New Latin *halter*, from Greek *haltēr* (a thing to jump with, dumbbell), alluding to its shape.

3. NOUN

/ ˈhɔltər / (AmE), / ˈhɔːltə / (BrE)
A rope or strap placed around the head of a horse or other animal, used for leading or tying it.
headgear for leading a horse

Examples

The groom fitted the horse with a new halter before leading it out of the stable.
She struggled to put the halter on the restless foal.
The old leather halter was worn but still sturdy enough to use.

Etymology

From Old English *hæltor*, related to *healtan* (to limp, halt).

4. NOUN

/ ˈhɔltər / (AmE), / ˈhɔːltə / (BrE)
a rope or noose used by a hangman to execute a condemned person by hanging
rope used for hanging execution

Examples

The condemned man shuddered at the sight of the halter beside the gallows.
Stories of outlaws often ended with the halter claiming another life.
He narrowly escaped the halter thanks to a last-minute pardon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: noose - a loop with a running knot, used to hang people or catch animals

Etymology

From Old English 'hæltan' (to restrain); related to German 'Halter' (holder, halter).
