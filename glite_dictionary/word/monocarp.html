monocarp

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɑː.nəˌkɑːrp / (AmE), / ˈmɒn.əˌkɑːp / (BrE)
A plant that flowers, produces fruit once, and then dies.
plant that fruits once before dying

Examples

The agave is a well-known monocarp, blooming spectacularly just once before it dies.
Some gardeners are fascinated by the lifecycle of a monocarp like the century plant.
After years of growth, the bamboo revealed itself as a monocarp by flowering and perishing shortly after.

Etymology

From Greek monos (single) + karpos (fruit).
