Gerridae

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛrɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛrɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of insects in the order Hemiptera, commonly known as water striders, which are adapted to living on the surface of water.
family of insects known as water striders

Examples

The Gerridae can be found gliding effortlessly across ponds and streams.
Members of the Gerridae family are known for their ability to walk on water due to specialized leg structures.
Biologists conducted a study on the distribution of Gerridae in urban wetlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water strider - common name of insects in the family Gerridae, known for walking on water

Etymology

From New Latin 'Gerridae', based on the type genus 'Gerris'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛr.əˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛr.ɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of insects known as water striders, characterized by their ability to walk on the surface of water.
family of water strider insects

Examples

The Gerridae are often found gliding effortlessly across still ponds and lakes.
Scientists study the hydrophobic legs of Gerridae to understand how these insects walk on water.
Several species within the Gerridae family inhabit both tropical and temperate regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: water strider - an insect capable of walking on water

Etymology

From New Latin Gerridae, from the genus name Gerris, from Greek gerris (a type of water insect).
