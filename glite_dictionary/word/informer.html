informer

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈfɔrmər / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɔːmə / (BrE)
a person who secretly provides confidential or incriminating information about someone to the police or an authority, often for personal gain.
one who gives secret information to authorities

Examples

The police relied on an informer to help them break up the smuggling ring.
After acting as an informer, he was placed under witness protection.
Many criminals fear being exposed by an informer within their own ranks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whistleblower - a person who exposes wrongdoing within an organization
  • Synonym: betrayer - someone who betrays trust, especially by giving information to an enemy or authority
  • Antonym: accomplice - a person who helps another commit a crime or wrongdoing

Etymology

From Middle English, from Anglo-Norman 'enformer', from Latin 'informare' (to inform).
