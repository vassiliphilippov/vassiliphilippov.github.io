spindlelegs

1. NOUN

/ ˈspɪndlˌlɛɡz / (AmE), / ˈspɪndlˌlɛɡz / (BrE)
legs that are long and thin, resembling the slender shape of a spindle.
long, thin legs

Examples

The ballet dancer's spindlelegs gave her a graceful, airy appearance on stage.
He was teased at school for his spindlelegs, but they made him an excellent runner.
The artist exaggerated the model's spindlelegs to create a whimsical character.

Etymology

From 'spindle' (a slender rod) and 'legs', describing a visual resemblance.

2. NOUN

/ ˈspɪndlˌlɛɡz / (AmE), / ˈspɪndlˌlɛɡz / (BrE)
a thin person with long, thin legs
thin person with long, thin legs
informal

Examples

He was always called spindlelegs because of his gangly appearance.
Despite being nicknamed spindlelegs, she excelled in long-distance running.
The tallest boy in class, known as spindlelegs, towered above his friends.

Etymology

From 'spindle' (a slender rod) + 'legs', referring to thin, rod-like legs.
