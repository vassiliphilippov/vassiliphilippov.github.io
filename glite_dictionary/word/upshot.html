upshot

1. NOUN

/ ˈʌpˌʃɑt / (AmE), / ˈʌpʃɒt / (BrE)
the final result or outcome of a process or situation, especially one that is unexpected or arrived at after a series of events
final result; outcome of events

Examples

The upshot of the investigation was that no charges were filed.
After months of negotiations, the upshot was that both sides agreed to compromise.
The upshot of all his hard work was a well-deserved promotion.

Synset Examples

the magnetic effect was greater when the rod was lengthwise
his decision had depressing consequences for business
he acted very wise after the event

Related Terms

  • Synonym: outcome - the result or effect of an action or event
  • Synonym: result - a consequence, effect, or outcome of something
  • Antonym: cause - a person or thing that gives rise to an action, phenomenon, or condition

Etymology

From the late Middle English phrase 'up shot,' originally referring to the final shot in an archery match.
