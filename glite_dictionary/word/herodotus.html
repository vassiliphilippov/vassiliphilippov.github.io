Herodotus

1. PROPN

/ həˈrɑːdəˌtəs / (AmE), / hɛˈrɒdətəs / (BrE)
(c. 485–425 BC) An ancient Greek historian, often called the 'father of history', renowned for his accounts of the wars between the Greeks and Persians.
ancient Greek historian, 'father of history'

Examples

Many historians regard Herodotus as the first true historian for his systematic approach to recording events.
The writings of Herodotus offer valuable insights into the ancient Greek world and its conflicts.
In his epic work, Herodotus documented the causes and consequences of the Persian Wars.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Thucydides - Ancient Greek historian who succeeded Herodotus, noted for his account of the Peloponnesian War

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἡρόδοτος (Hēródotos).
