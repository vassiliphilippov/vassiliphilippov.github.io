piezometer

1. NOUN

/ ˌpaɪ.iˈzɑː.mə.tər / (AmE), / ˌpaɪ.iˈzɒ.mɪ.tə / (BrE)
an instrument for measuring high pressures, especially those in fluids or gases
instrument for measuring high pressures

Examples

The piezometer was installed at several depths to monitor groundwater pressure changes.
Engineers used a piezometer to assess the pressure of fluids within the rock formations.
A sudden spike in readings from the piezometer alerted the team to increased reservoir pressure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: manometer - an instrument for measuring the pressure of gases or liquids

Etymology

From Greek 'piezein' (to press) and '-meter' (measurer).
