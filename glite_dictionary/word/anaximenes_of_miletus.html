Anaximenes of Miletus

1. PROPN

/ ˌæn.əkˈsɪm.ə.niz əv maɪˈliː.təs / (AmE), / ˌan.ækˈsɪm.ɪ.niːz əv mɪˈliː.təs / (BrE)
(c. 585–c. 528 BC) A pre-Socratic Greek philosopher from Miletus, associated with Anaximander, who believed air is the fundamental substance of the universe.
pre-Socratic Greek philosopher of air as the origin of all things

Examples

The philosopher Anaximenes of Miletus argued that air is the source of all matter.
Many pre-Socratic thinkers, such as Anaximenes of Miletus, sought unified explanations for natural phenomena.
In his surviving fragments, Anaximenes of Miletus describes change as a process of rarefaction and condensation of air.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ἀναξιμένης (Anaximenēs) of Μίλητος (Miletos).
