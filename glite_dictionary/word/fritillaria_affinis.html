Fritillaria affinis

1. NOUN

/ frɪˌtɪləriə əˈfaɪnɪs / (AmE), / frɪˌtɪlərɪə əˈfaɪnɪs / (BrE)
A herbaceous plant native to northwestern America, known for its green-and-purple bell-shaped flowers.
herb with green-and-purple bell-shaped flowers

Examples

Fritillaria affinis grows wild in the damp woodlands of the Pacific Northwest.
The intricate pattern on the petals of Fritillaria affinis makes it a favorite among wildflower enthusiasts.
Native peoples used the bulbs of Fritillaria affinis as a traditional food source.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Fritillaria' meaning a type of flower, and 'affinis' meaning related or similar.
