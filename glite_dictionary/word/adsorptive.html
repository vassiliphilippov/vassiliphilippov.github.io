adsorptive

1. ADJ

/ ædˈsɔːrptɪv / (AmE), / ædˈsɔːptɪv / (BrE)
having the ability or tendency to attract and hold substances on its surface by adsorption
having the ability to adsorb

Examples

Activated charcoal is highly adsorptive and is used to remove toxins from the digestive tract.
The filter's adsorptive properties allow it to trap contaminants effectively.
Clay minerals have adsorptive surfaces that attract various chemical molecules.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adsorbent - a material having the ability to adsorb substances onto its surface
  • Antonym: nonadsorptive - lacking the ability or tendency to adsorb

Etymology

From 'adsorb' (to accumulate on a surface) + '-ive' (forming adjectives).
