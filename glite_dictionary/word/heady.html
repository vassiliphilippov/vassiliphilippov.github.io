heady

1. ADJ

/ ˈhɛdi / (AmE), / ˈhɛdi / (BrE)
extremely exciting, exhilarating, or intoxicating, as if caused by alcohol or drugs.
extremely exciting or intoxicating

Examples

After the team's victory, a heady sense of triumph swept through the crowd.
The heady aroma of spring flowers filled the warm air.
It was a heady time in her life, full of passion and possibility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exhilarating - making you feel very excited and happy
  • Synonym: intoxicating - causing a feeling of wild excitement or happiness
  • Antonym: sobering - making you feel serious or less excited

Etymology

From 'head' + -y, referring originally to affecting the head, especially through drink.

2. ADJ

/ ˈhɛdi / (AmE), / ˈhɛdi / (BrE)
Showing a defiant disregard for danger or consequences; reckless or rash.
recklessly bold; rash

Examples

In a heady moment, she decided to confront her critics with no concern for the risks.
His heady disregard for safety made him popular with thrill-seekers but worried his family.
They launched a heady assault on the fort, ignoring the obvious dangers.

Synset Examples

foolhardy enough to try to seize the gun from the hijacker
became the fiercest and most reckless of partisans
a reckless driver
a rash attempt to climb Mount Everest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reckless - heedless of danger or the consequences of one's actions
  • Antonym: cautious - careful to avoid danger or risk
  • Synonym: foolhardy - recklessly bold or rash
  • Synonym: rash - acting without careful consideration of consequences

Etymology

From head + -y, originally meaning 'apt to go to one’s head', later extended to 'rash, wild'.

Source: WordNet
