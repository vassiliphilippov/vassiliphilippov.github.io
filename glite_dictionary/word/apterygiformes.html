Apterygiformes

1. NOUN

/ ˌæptəˌrɪdʒɪˈfɔrmiːz / (AmE), / ˌæptərɪdʒɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
An order of flightless, ground-dwelling birds characterized by vestigial wings, long bills, and small eyes; known primarily as kiwis.
order of flightless birds (kiwis)

Examples

The Apterygiformes are native to New Zealand and include all species of kiwi.
Unlike most birds, Apterygiformes possess only vestigial wings, rendering them unable to fly.
Scientists study Apterygiformes to understand the evolution of flightlessness in birds.

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (without) + 'pteryx' (wing) + 'formes' (shape/form), referring to birds without wings.

2. NOUN

/ æpˌtɛrɪdʒəˈfɔrmiːz / (AmE), / æpˌtɛrɪdʒɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
an order of flightless, ground-dwelling birds with vestigial wings, long bills, and small eyes, comprising the kiwis
order of flightless birds (kiwis)

Examples

The kiwi is the most well-known member of the Apterygiformes.
Scientific studies have focused on the evolutionary history of Apterygiformes.
There are five recognized species within the bird order Apterygiformes.

Etymology

From Greek 'a-' (without) + 'pterux' (wing) + Latin '-formes' (having the form of).
