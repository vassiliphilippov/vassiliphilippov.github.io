impressiveness

1. NOUN

/ ɪmˈprɛsɪvnəs / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɛsɪvnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of making a strong or vivid impression on the mind
quality of being impressive

Examples

The impressiveness of the ancient cathedral left all the tourists in awe.
She spoke about the impressiveness of his achievements during the award ceremony.
The impressiveness of the performance was enhanced by the dramatic lighting effects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impressive - having the ability to impress
  • Antonym: ordinariness - the quality of being unremarkable or ordinary

Etymology

From 'impressive' + '-ness', ultimately from Latin 'imprimere' (to press into).

2. NOUN

/ ɪmˈprɛsɪvnəs / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɛsɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being impressive, especially by being grand, splendid, or striking in appearance or effect
quality of being impressive or grand

Examples

The impressiveness of the cathedral left tourists speechless.
We were all struck by the impressiveness of his presentation.
She admired the impressiveness of the mountain landscape.

Synset Examples

the grandness of the architecture
impressed by the richness of the flora

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grandeur - the quality of being grand or magnificent
  • Synonym: splendor - magnificent and splendid appearance
  • Antonym: mediocrity - the quality of being average or unremarkable

Etymology

Formed from 'impressive' + '-ness', denoting the state or quality.
