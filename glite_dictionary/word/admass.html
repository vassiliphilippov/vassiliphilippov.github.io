admass

1. NOUN

/ ˈædˌmæs / (AmE), / ˈædˌmæs / (BrE)
(chiefly British) The section of the public that is easily influenced by advertising and mass media.
segment of public influenced by media
british

Examples

Many companies tailor their marketing strategies to appeal to the admass.
Some critics argue that the admass is too susceptible to celebrity endorsements.
The rise of social media has changed the way the admass is targeted by advertisers.

Etymology

Blend of 'advertising' and 'mass' (as in mass media).
