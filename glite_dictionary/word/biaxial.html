biaxial

1. ADJ

/ baɪˈæk.si.əl / (AmE), / baɪˈæk.si.əl / (BrE)
having or involving two axes, especially in reference to crystals or mechanical systems
having two axes

Examples

Some minerals form biaxial crystals with distinct optical properties.
The material exhibits biaxial strength, resisting force along two separate axes.
Researchers measured the biaxial orientation of the polymer film.

Synset Examples

biaxial crystals

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uniaxial - having only one axis

Etymology

From bi- (two) + axial (relating to an axis), based on Latin roots.

2. ADJ

/ baɪˈæksiəl / (AmE), / baɪˈæk.si.əl / (BrE)
having or relating to two axes; involving two distinct axes of symmetry or rotation.
having two axes

Examples

The biaxial stress test measures the material's strength under two perpendicular forces.
Certain crystals, such as mica, are classified as biaxial because they have two optical axes.
Advanced cameras use biaxial stabilization to reduce image blur from different directions.

Synset Examples

biaxial crystals

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: uniaxial - having one axis
  • Degree Higher: triaxial - having three axes

Etymology

From bi- (two) + axis + -al, indicating 'having two axes'.
