cayuse

1. NOUN

/ kaɪˈjus / (AmE), / kaɪˈjuːz / (BrE)
a small, hardy horse of western North America, originally bred by Indigenous peoples, especially in the Pacific Northwest
a small native horse of western North America

Examples

The rancher rode his cayuse across the open prairie at dawn.
In the old West, cowboys often relied on a sturdy cayuse for long cattle drives.
She admired the spirit and endurance of the cayuse her grandfather once owned.

Etymology

From the name of the Cayuse people of the Pacific Northwest.
