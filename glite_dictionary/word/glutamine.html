glutamine

1. NOUN

/ ɡluˈtæmin / (AmE), / ɡluːˈtɑːmiːn / (BrE)
A crystalline amino acid found in proteins and important for protein metabolism in the body.
amino acid found in proteins
medical

Examples

Athletes sometimes take glutamine supplements to help muscle recovery after intense exercise.
Glutamine is essential for many metabolic processes in the human body.
Research has shown that glutamine levels can decrease significantly during times of physical stress.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: amino acid - organic compound forming proteins

Etymology

From German 'Glutamin', from Latin 'gluten' (glue) + '-amine' (compound derived from ammonia).
