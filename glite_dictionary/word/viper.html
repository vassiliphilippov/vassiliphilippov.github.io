viper

1. NOUN

/ ˈvaɪpər / (AmE), / ˈvaɪpə / (BrE)
A venomous snake of the family Viperidae, native to the Old World, characterized by long, hollow fangs used to inject venom.
a venomous snake of the Old World

Examples

The bite of a viper can be extremely dangerous if left untreated.
Some species of viper are found in deserts, while others inhabit forests or grasslands.
She mistook the harmless snake for a viper and quickly backed away.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adder - a common name for several species of viper

Etymology

From Latin 'vipera' (snake, viper).
