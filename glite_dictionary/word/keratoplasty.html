keratoplasty

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɛr.ə.təˌplæsti / (AmE), / kɛˈratəˌplasti / (BrE)
a surgical operation in which all or part of a damaged or diseased cornea is replaced with healthy corneal tissue from a donor
corneal transplant surgery
medical

Examples

After being diagnosed with a severe corneal disease, she underwent keratoplasty to restore her vision.
Advances in keratoplasty have significantly improved outcomes for patients with corneal damage.
The surgeon explained the risks and benefits of keratoplasty before the procedure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corneal transplant - a surgical procedure to replace a damaged cornea with donor tissue

Etymology

From Greek 'kerato-' (horn, in reference to the cornea) + '-plasty' (molding, surgical formation).
