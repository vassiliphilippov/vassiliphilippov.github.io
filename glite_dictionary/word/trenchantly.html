trenchantly

1. ADV

/ ˈtrɛntʃəntli / (AmE), / ˈtrɛn.tʃənt.li / (BrE)
In a forceful, effective, and incisive manner; with keen sharpness or vigour.
in a forceful, incisive way

Examples

The politician trenchantly criticized the government's policies during the debate.
She trenchantly exposed the flaws in the argument, leaving no room for doubt.
The journalist trenchantly analyzed the causes behind the economic crisis.

Synset Examples

he defended his client's civil rights trenchantly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: forcefully - in a strong and assertive way
  • Antonym: mildly - in a gentle or soft manner

Etymology

Formed from 'trenchant' (from Old French 'trenchant', present participle of 'trenchier', to cut).
