infatuation

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˌfætʃuˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌfatjʊˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
A person or thing for whom one has an intense but short-lived passion or admiration.
object of intense but brief passion

Examples

His latest infatuation was a glamorous actress he saw in a magazine.
She realized her infatuation with the new car was fading after a few weeks.
The puppy became his brief infatuation before he returned to his old hobbies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crush - an intense and usually short-lived infatuation for someone

Etymology

From Latin infatuatio, from infatuare (to make foolish).

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˌfætʃuˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌfatjʊˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
A strong but short-lived and often unreasonable feeling of love or admiration for someone or something, typically experienced during adolescence.
short-lived strong love or admiration

Examples

His infatuation with his classmate lasted only a few weeks before he lost interest.
Teenagers often mistake their first infatuation for true love.
She recognized her feelings as mere infatuation rather than genuine affection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crush - a brief but intense feeling of attraction for someone, often used among adolescents
  • Degree Higher: love - a deep affection or attachment toward someone or something
  • Antonym: disinterest - lack of interest or passion toward someone or something

Etymology

From Latin infatuatus (made foolish, inspired with folly), from in- (in) + fatuus (foolish).

3. NOUN

/ ɪnˌfætʃuˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌfatjʊˈeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
an intense but usually short-lived and foolish passion or admiration for someone or something
intense, short-lived passion or admiration

Examples

As a teenager, she had an intense infatuation with a famous actor.
His infatuation with the new hobby faded after just a few weeks.
It's easy to mistake infatuation for true love in the beginning of a relationship.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crush - a brief but intense infatuation for someone
  • Degree Higher: love - a deep romantic or affectionate attachment
  • Antonym: indifference - lack of interest or concern

Etymology

From Latin 'infatuatio' (delusion, folly), from 'infatuare' (to make foolish).
