quandong

1. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑːn.dɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈkwɒn.dɒŋ / (BrE)
An Australian tree with hard white timber, glossy green leaves, white flowers, and glossy blue one-seeded fruit.
Australian tree with blue fruit

Examples

The wood of the quandong is highly prized for its hardness and pale color.
Many bushwalkers in Australia enjoy the vibrant blue fruit of the quandong.
A mature quandong can provide both shade and edible fruit in arid regions.

Etymology

From Wiradjuri (an Australian Aboriginal language) *guwandhang*.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑn.dɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈkwɒn.dɒŋ / (BrE)
An edible fruit of several Australian trees, especially the Brisbane quandong tree, often used in cooking and preserves.
Australian edible fruit

Examples

The chef made a tart using fresh quandong for a unique Australian flavor.
We tasted some wild quandongs during our hike in the outback.
Quandong jam is a popular treat in parts of Australia.

Etymology

From Wiradjuri (an Aboriginal Australian language) 'quandang', referring to the fruit.

3. NOUN

/ ˈkwɑn.dɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈkwɒn.dɒŋ / (BrE)
An Australian tree (Santalum acuminatum) known for its edible red fruit and nutlike seed, used as a food source.
Australian tree with edible fruit and seed

Examples

The Aboriginal people have used the quandong for food and medicine for centuries.
We picked several ripe quandongs during our bush walk in Australia.
The chef made a delicious jam from the fruit of the quandong.

Etymology

From Wiradjuri (an Aboriginal Australian language) 'guwandhang'.

/ ˈkwɑnˌdɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈkwɒn.dɒŋ / (BrE)
An edible, bright red Australian fruit with a tart flavor, commonly used for desserts and in making jam.
red Australian fruit used in desserts or jam

Examples

She made a delicious pie using fresh quandongs from the local market.
The tartness of the quandong pairs well with sweet desserts.
Traditional Australian jam often features the red fruit of the quandong.

Etymology

From Wiradjuri (an Australian Aboriginal language) 'quandong', referring to the native plant.
