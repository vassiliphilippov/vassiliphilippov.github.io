incontinence

1. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkɑːn.tən.əns / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɒn.tɪ.nəns / (BrE)
The inability to control the elimination of urine or feces from the body.
inability to control urination or defecation
medical

Examples

The patient experienced incontinence after the surgery, requiring additional care.
Products designed for incontinence can help people maintain their independence.
Some medications can increase the risk of incontinence in elderly patients.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: continence - the ability to control urination or defecation

Etymology

From Latin 'incontinentia', meaning lack of restraint.

2. NOUN

/ ɪnˈkɑntənəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɒntɪnəns / (BrE)
lack of self-restraint or control, especially with regard to indulgence in sensuous pleasures.
lack of self-restraint (esp. sensual pleasures)
archaic

Examples

Unable to resist temptation, his incontinence ultimately led to his downfall.
Ancient philosophers debated the consequences of incontinence for moral character.
The play portrays a tragic hero whose incontinence with respect to desire brings ruin.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: continence - self-restraint, especially regarding sensual pleasures

Etymology

From Latin incontinentia (lack of self-restraint).
