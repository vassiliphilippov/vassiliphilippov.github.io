Toxotidae

1. NOUN

/ toʊkˈsɑtəˌdi / (AmE), / tɒkˈsɒtɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small tropical fishes known as archerfishes, noted for their ability to shoot jets of water to knock insects off overhanging vegetation.
family of archerfishes

Examples

Members of the Toxotidae family are famous for their unique hunting strategy.
The aquarium features several species from the Toxotidae family, including the well-known archerfish.
Scientists study Toxotidae to better understand how animals use water as a hunting tool.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archerfish - a fish that shoots water to capture prey

Etymology

From New Latin Toxotidae, from the type genus Toxotes, from Greek toxotes (archer).
