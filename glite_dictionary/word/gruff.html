gruff

1. ADJ

/ ɡrʌf / (AmE), / ɡrʌf / (BrE)
(of a voice or sound) low and rough, often sounding unfriendly, harsh, or as if produced by illness or emotion.
deep and harsh-sounding (voice or sound)

Examples

The old man greeted us with a gruff voice, making him seem intimidating at first.
After shouting all day, her speech became gruff and raspy.
The dog's gruff barking startled everyone in the house.

Synset Examples

gruff voices
the dog's gruff barking
hoarse cries
makes all the instruments sound powerful but husky

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hoarse - rough and harsh in sound, usually due to illness or overuse
  • Antonym: soft - gentle and not harsh in sound

2. ADJ

/ ɡrʌf / (AmE), / ɡrʌf / (BrE)
(of a voice, manner, or person) rough, brusque, and unfriendly in a way that seems stern or forbidding.
rough or stern in manner or voice

Examples

His gruff voice startled the children into silence.
Although his manner was gruff, he had a kind heart.
She received a gruff reply when she asked for help.

Synset Examples

crusty remarks
a crusty old man
his curmudgeonly temper
gruff manner
a gruff reply

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brusque - abrupt or offhand in speech or manner
  • Antonym: gentle - mild and kind in manner or speech

Etymology

Probable imitative origin, reflecting the sound of a harsh voice; related to Dutch 'grof' (coarse, rough).
