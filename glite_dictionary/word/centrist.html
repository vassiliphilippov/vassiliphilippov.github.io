centrist

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛntrɪst / (AmE), / ˈsɛntrɪst / (BrE)
Supporting or advocating moderate or middle-of-the-road political views, avoiding extreme positions associated with either the left or the right.
advocating moderate political views

Examples

She takes a centrist approach, preferring compromise over ideological extremes.
The new party's centrist policies appeal to voters disillusioned with partisanship.
His centrist stance often puts him at odds with both liberals and conservatives.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moderate - advocating or holding moderate political views or policies
  • Antonym: radical - advocating extreme or far-reaching political ideas
  • Antonym: conservative - favoring traditional views and values; tending to oppose change
  • Antonym: liberal - favoring progress or reform in political and social matters

Etymology

From 'centre' + '-ist', meaning one whose views are in the political centre.

2. NOUN

/ ˈsɛntrɪst / (AmE), / ˈsɛntrɪst / (BrE)
a person whose political opinions are moderate and not extreme, typically favoring neither the left nor the right
person with moderate political views

Examples

As a centrist, she prefers policies that blend ideas from both major parties.
The new candidate appealed to centrist voters in the upcoming election.
Debates between left-wing, right-wing, and centrist politicians were featured on the news.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: moderate - a person who holds moderate views, especially in politics
  • Antonym: radical - a person with extreme political views
  • Antonym: extremist - a person with very extreme political or religious views

Etymology

From 'center' + '-ist', denoting adherence to the center in politics.
