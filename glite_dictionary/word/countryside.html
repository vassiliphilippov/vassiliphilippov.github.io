countryside

1. NOUN

/ ˈkaʊn.triˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈkʌn.tri.saɪd / (BrE)
land and scenery of rural areas, typically outside towns and cities
rural area or landscape

Examples

They moved to the countryside to enjoy a quieter, slower-paced life.
The English countryside is famous for its rolling hills and green fields.
We spent the weekend exploring the beautiful countryside around the village.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rural - relating to the countryside rather than the town
  • Antonym: city - a large town or urban area
  • Antonym: urban - relating to city or town life

Etymology

Formed from 'country' + 'side', first used in the late 16th century.
