Ribes grossularia

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪbiːz ɡrɒˈsʊləriə / (AmE), / ˈraɪbiːz ɡrɒˈsʊləriə / (BrE)
a species of spiny shrub native to Eurasia, known for its greenish purple-tinged flowers and yellow-green or red-purple ovoid berries, commonly called gooseberry
Eurasian gooseberry shrub

Examples

The berries of Ribes grossularia are often used to make pies and preserves.
Botanists studied the growth patterns of Ribes grossularia in various soil types.
A hedge of Ribes grossularia separates the garden from the orchard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gooseberry - common name for the fruit of Ribes grossularia

Etymology

From Latin Ribes (a plant genus) and grossularia (from 'grossular', relating to gooseberry).
