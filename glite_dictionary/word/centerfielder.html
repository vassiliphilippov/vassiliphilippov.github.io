centerfielder

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛntərˌfildər / (AmE), / ˌsɛntəˈfiːldə / (BrE)
the player who defends the center area of the outfield in baseball or softball
baseball/softball outfield position
american

Examples

The centerfielder made a spectacular catch to end the inning.
She trained hard to become the starting centerfielder on her college team.
The team relies on their centerfielder for strong defense and quick throws.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: outfielder - player who defends the outfield in baseball or softball
  • Synonym: left fielder - player positioned in left field
  • Synonym: right fielder - player positioned in right field

Etymology

From American English 'center field' + '-er' (agent noun suffix).
