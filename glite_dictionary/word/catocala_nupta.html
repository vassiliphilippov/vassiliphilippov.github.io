Catocala nupta

1. NOUN

/ ˌkætəˈkeɪlə ˈnuːptə / (AmE), / ˌkætəˈkɑːlə ˈnjuːptə / (BrE)
a species of moth with dull forewings and red-marked hind wings, commonly known as the red underwing
a moth species with red-marked hind wings

Examples

The Catocala nupta is easily identified by its striking red-marked hind wings.
Several Catocala nupta moths were spotted near the riverbank last summer.
Entomologists often study the coloring patterns of Catocala nupta to understand mimicry in moths.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red underwing - common name for Catocala nupta, a moth with red-marked hind wings

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, genus Catocala and Latin nupta meaning 'bride', referring to the species epithet.
