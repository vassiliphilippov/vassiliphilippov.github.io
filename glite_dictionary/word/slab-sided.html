slab-sided

1. ADJ

/ ˈslæbˌsaɪdɪd / (AmE), / ˈslæbˌsaɪdɪd / (BrE)
having flat, often vertical sides; resembling or characterized by the appearance of broad, flat slabs, especially in architecture or design.
having flat or vertical sides

Examples

The new museum is a slab-sided structure that dominates the cityscape.
Her pottery featured slab-sided walls that gave it a distinctive modern appearance.
The designer opted for slab-sided panels to achieve a minimalist aesthetic.

Synset Examples

The sculpture was slab-sided, giving it a unique look.
The building had a slab-sided design that looked modern.

Etymology

Formed from 'slab' (a large, flat piece) and 'sided' (having sides).
