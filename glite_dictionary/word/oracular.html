oracular

1. ADJ

/ ɔˈrækjələr / (AmE), / əˈrækjʊlə / (BrE)
relating to, resembling, or characteristic of an oracle or prophecy; giving wise or authoritative decisions or opinions
relating to an oracle

Examples

The priestess gave an oracular response that was difficult to interpret.
His advice was so oracular that no one could tell what he really meant.
Ancient Greek leaders often sought oracular guidance before making important decisions.

Synset Examples

able by oracular means to expose a witch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prophetic - foretelling or predicting future events
  • Degree Lower: cryptic - having a meaning that is mysterious or obscure

Etymology

From Latin oracularis, from oraculum (oracle).

2. ADJ

/ ɔˈrækjələr / (AmE), / ɔːˈrækjʊlə / (BrE)
(of a statement or person) mysterious and difficult to interpret, as if uttered by an oracle; obscurely prophetic.
mysterious and prophetic; like an oracle

Examples

The professor's comments were so oracular that nobody understood what he was predicting.
She received an oracular warning in her dream, full of symbols and hidden messages.
His oracular statements left us puzzled about what might happen next.

Synset Examples

Delphic pronouncements
an oracular message

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prophetic - accurately describing or predicting what will happen in the future
  • Synonym: cryptic - having a meaning that is mysterious or obscure

Etymology

From Latin oracularis, from oraculum (oracle).

3. ADJ

/ ɔːˈrækjələr / (AmE), / ɔːˈrækjʊlə / (BrE)
(of speech or writing) mysterious or difficult to interpret, like that of an oracle.
mysterious or difficult to interpret

Examples

The poet's language became oracular, leaving many readers puzzled by its hidden meanings.
Her oracular response only deepened the mystery surrounding the project.
Critics struggled to understand the oracular messages in his later works.

Synset Examples

the oracular sayings of Victorian poets
so enigmatic that priests might have to clarify it
an enigmatic smile

Related Terms

  • Synonym: enigmatic - difficult to interpret or understand; mysterious
  • Antonym: clear - easy to perceive, understand, or interpret

Etymology

From Latin 'oracularis', from 'oraculum' (oracle).
