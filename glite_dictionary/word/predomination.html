predomination

1. NOUN

/ ˌpriː.dɑː.məˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriː.dɒm.ɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The state or quality of being predominant or having greater power or influence than others.
state of being predominant

Examples

The predomination of large corporations in the market has reduced competition.
Cultural predomination by one region can shape national identity over centuries.
Debates continued over the predomination of certain political ideologies in the country.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: predominance - the state of being superior in power or influence
  • Antonym: subordination - the state of being under the authority or control of another

Etymology

From Latin praedominari (to rule over), via Late Latin praedominatio.

Examples
