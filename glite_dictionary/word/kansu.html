Kansu

1. PROPN

/ ˈkænˌsuː / (AmE), / ˈkan.suː / (BrE)
a historical province in north-central China, formerly part of the Silk Road, now commonly known as Gansu.
historical Chinese province (now Gansu)

Examples

The Silk Road passed through Kansu on its way to Central Asia.
In the early 20th century, several Western explorers traveled across Kansu.
The climate of Kansu is characterized by dry, cool winters and warm summers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gansu - modern name for the same Chinese province, north-central China

Etymology

From Chinese 甘肃 (Gānsù), formerly romanized as 'Kansu'.
