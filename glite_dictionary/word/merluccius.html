2. NOUN

/ mɜrˈluːʃiəs / (AmE), / mɜːˈluːsiəs / (BrE)
A genus of predatory marine fish known as hakes, found in the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans and containing several commercially significant species.
genus of predatory marine hakes

Examples

Several commercially important hake species belong to the genus Merluccius.
The genus Merluccius is distributed across both the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans.
Marine biologists study the life cycles of species within the genus Merluccius.

Etymology

From Latin, diminutive of 'merlucius' (hake).
