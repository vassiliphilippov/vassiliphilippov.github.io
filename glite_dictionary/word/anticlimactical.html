anticlimactical

1. ADJ

/ ˌæn.ti.klaɪˈmæk.tɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌæn.ti.klaɪˈmæk.tɪ.kəl / (BrE)
relating to, or having the effect of an anticlimax; describing something that becomes less impressive or ends in a trivial or disappointing way
relating to an anticlimax

Examples

The ending of the film was strangely anticlimactical, leaving the audience unsatisfied.
His speech started strong but finished on an anticlimactical note, disappointing many listeners.
The final match was unexpectedly anticlimactical after all the excitement leading up to it.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anticlimactic - relating to a disappointing or less impressive ending

Etymology

Formed by adding -al to 'anticlimactic', from 'anticlimax' (from Greek anti- + climax).
