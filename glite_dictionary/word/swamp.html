swamp

1. VERB

/ swɑmp / (AmE), / swɒmp / (BrE)
to drench or submerge, especially with water; to flood or overwhelm completely
to drench or submerge; to flood

Examples

The surprise downpour swamped the football field within minutes.
During the flood, water swamped homes near the river banks.
The rescue team was nearly swamped by the rising tide.

Synset Examples

The tsunami swamped every boat in the harbor

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flood - to cover with water, especially suddenly; to inundate

2. NOUN

/ swɑmp / (AmE), / swɒmp / (BrE)
A difficult or confusing situation from which it is hard to escape.
confusing or difficult situation

Examples

She managed to navigate the bureaucratic swamp and get the permits approved.
He realized he was in a financial swamp when the unexpected bills started arriving.

3. NOUN

/ swɑmp / (AmE), / swɒmp / (BrE)
an area of low-lying, often waterlogged land, typically with woody plants, that is usually flooded for part of the year
wetland area with woody plants

Examples

The boat slowly glided through the swamp, surrounded by tall cypress trees.
After heavy rains, parts of the trail became a muddy swamp.
Many rare species thrive in the unique habitat of a swamp.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: marsh - wetland dominated by grasses and reeds, not woody plants
  • Synonym: bog - wet, spongy ground with poor drainage, dominated by moss

Etymology

From Middle English 'swampe', likely of Germanic origin, related to Old Norse 'svop' (sponge).

4. VERB

/ swɑmp / (AmE), / swɒmp / (BrE)
to overwhelm or fill with a large amount of something, especially beyond capacity
overwhelm or flood with something

Examples

The customer service team was swamped with requests after the product launch.
Her schedule was so busy that she felt completely swamped by her responsibilities.
Heavy rain can easily swamp the drains, causing local flooding.

Synset Examples

The images flooded his mind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: flood - to fill or cover completely, often with water or another substance
