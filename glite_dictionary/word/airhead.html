airhead

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛrˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈeəˌhɛd / (BrE)
a position of strategic importance secured by airborne troops in enemy territory, used as a base for further operations
bridgehead held by airborne troops

Examples

The paratroopers established an airhead behind enemy lines to facilitate the arrival of reinforcements.
Securing the airhead proved crucial for the success of the mission.
After capturing the airhead, the division quickly built defensive positions and awaited further orders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beachhead - a position on a hostile shore captured and held as a base for further advance
  • Synonym: bridgehead - a fortified position established to secure a crossing point

Etymology

Formed from 'air' and 'head', after 'bridgehead', referring to a beachhead seized by airborne forces.

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛrˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈeəˌhɛd / (BrE)
an informal term for a silly, unintelligent, or scatterbrained person
silly or scatterbrained person
informal

Examples

Everyone thinks she's an airhead, but she's actually quite clever.
Don't be such an airhead; you forgot your keys again!
The comedian played an airhead character in the movie, always getting into silly situations.

Synset Examples
