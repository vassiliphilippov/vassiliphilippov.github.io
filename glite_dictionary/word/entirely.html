entirely

1. ADV

/ ɪnˈtaɪɚli / (AmE), / ɪnˈtaɪəli / (BrE)
completely or solely; with no other thing or person included or involved.
completely; solely, without others involved

Examples

The responsibility for the mistake was entirely his.
The school is entirely devoted to supporting children with special needs.
She completed the project entirely on her own, without any help.

Synset Examples

was entirely to blame
a school devoted entirely to the needs of problem children
he works for Mr. Smith exclusively
did it solely for money
the burden of proof rests on the prosecution alone
a privilege granted only to him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: completely - totally; with everything included
  • Antonym: partly - to a limited extent; not fully

Etymology

From Latin 'integer' (whole) via Middle English and Old French.

2. ADV

/ ɪnˈtaɪɚli / (AmE), / ɪnˈtaɪəli / (BrE)
completely; to the full or whole extent
completely; fully

Examples

The audience was entirely silent during the emotional performance.
He relied entirely on her advice to make the final decision.
The plan is entirely different from anything we've tried before.
