pauperization

1. NOUN

/ ˌpɔːpərɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɔːpəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the condition or process of becoming extremely poor; a state of extreme poverty or destitution
state of extreme poverty

Examples

The region suffered severe pauperization after the collapse of its main industry.
Rapid urban growth led to the pauperization of many families.
Social reforms were proposed to counteract the widespread pauperization in the country.

Synset Examples

their indigence appalled him
a general state of need exists among the homeless

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impoverishment - the process or result of making or becoming poor
  • Antonym: wealth - an abundance of valuable possessions or money

Etymology

From pauper (Latin for 'poor') + -ization (forming nouns indicating process or result).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpɔpərɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɔːpəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or act of making someone or a group poor; the reduction to poverty.
the act of making someone poor

Examples

The war led to the pauperization of many families across the country.
Critics argue that certain policies can result in the pauperization of vulnerable communities.
International aid organizations aim to prevent the pauperization of at-risk populations during economic crises.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: impoverishment - the process of becoming poor or making others poor
  • Antonym: enrichment - the process of making someone richer

Etymology

From French 'pauperisation', from Latin 'pauper' (poor).
