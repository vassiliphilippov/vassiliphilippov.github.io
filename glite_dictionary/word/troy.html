Troy

1. PROPN

/ trɔɪ / (AmE), / trɔɪ / (BrE)
An ancient city in Asia Minor, famous as the site of the legendary Trojan War.
ancient city in Asia Minor; site of the Trojan War

Examples

The ruins of Troy attract historians and tourists from around the world.
According to legend, Helen's abduction sparked the war against Troy.
Archaeologists have uncovered ancient walls at the site believed to be Troy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Trojan War - legendary war fought at Troy in Greek mythology

Etymology

From Latin Troia, from Ancient Greek Τροία (Troía), the name of the city.
