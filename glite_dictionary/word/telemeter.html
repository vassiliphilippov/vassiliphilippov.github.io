telemeter

1. NOUN

/ təˈlɛm.ɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / tɪˈlɛm.ɪ.tə / (BrE)
a scientific instrument that measures and transmits data about distant events or conditions to a receiving station
instrument for remote measurement and data transmission

Examples

The weather balloons use a telemeter to send atmospheric data back to researchers on the ground.
Each rocket is equipped with a telemeter to monitor its performance during flight.
A telemeter allowed scientists to study volcanic eruptions without being near the danger zone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: transmitter - device that sends information over a distance

Etymology

From Greek 'tēle' (far) + 'metron' (measure).
