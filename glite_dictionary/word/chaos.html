Chaos

1. PROPN

/ ˈkeɪ.ɑs / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ.ɒs / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the primeval void or deity representing the infinite empty space that existed before the creation of the universe.
the primordial void in Greek mythology

Examples

According to Greek myths, Chaos existed before anything else in the universe.
Some ancient texts describe Chaos as the mother of all things, including the Earth and sky.
The poet Hesiod wrote that Chaos was the first of the primordial deities.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Χάος (Kháos), meaning 'abyss, void'.
