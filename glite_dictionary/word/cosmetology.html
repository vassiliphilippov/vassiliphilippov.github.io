cosmetology

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɑːzməˈtɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌkɒzmɪˈtɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the professional practice of beautifying the hair, face, and skin, including hairdressing, skincare, and cosmetics application.
the practice of beautifying hair, face, and skin

Examples

She decided to study cosmetology after high school to become a professional stylist.
Cosmetology programs often include training in skincare, hairdressing, and makeup application.
The state requires a license to practice cosmetology in a salon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: beautician - a person trained in cosmetology
  • Degree Higher: dermatology - the medical study of skin and its diseases

Etymology

From Greek kosmētikos (skilled in adornment) + -logy (study of).
