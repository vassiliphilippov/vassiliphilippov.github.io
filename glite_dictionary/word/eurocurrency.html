Eurocurrency

1. NOUN

/ ˈjʊroʊˌkɜːrənsi / (AmE), / ˈjʊərəʊˌkʌrənsi / (BrE)
Money deposited in a bank outside its country of origin, especially the currency of major financial and industrial countries held abroad for lending and borrowing purposes.
currency held abroad for international loans

Examples

Many international loans are provided in Eurocurrency to avoid national banking regulations.
The Asian financial crisis was partly triggered by excessive Eurocurrency lending.
Large corporations often borrow in Eurocurrency markets to secure better interest rates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Eurodollar - US dollars deposited in banks outside the United States

Etymology

Formed from 'Euro-' (originally referring to Europe but now any foreign) + 'currency'.
