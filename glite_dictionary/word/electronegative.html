electronegative

1. ADJ

/ ɪˌlɛktrəˈnɛɡətɪv / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊˈnɛɡətɪv / (BrE)
having a tendency to attract electrons, or carrying a negative electric charge, especially in the context of atoms or molecules.
having tendency to attract electrons or a negative charge
medical

Examples

Chlorine is more electronegative than hydrogen, so it tends to attract electrons in a bond.
Oxygen atoms are highly electronegative, which explains their strong pull on shared electrons.
When an atom is electronegative, it can form polar molecules due to uneven charge distribution.

Synset Examples

electrons are negative

Related Terms

  • Antonym: electropositive - having a tendency to donate electrons or carry a positive charge

Etymology

From electro- (relating to electricity) + negative.
