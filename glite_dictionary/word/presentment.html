presentment

1. NOUN

/ prɪˈzɛntmənt / (AmE), / prɪˈzɛntmənt / (BrE)
the act of presenting something to sight or view; a show or display
the act of presenting; display

Examples

The artist’s presentment of the landscape captured everyone’s attention at the gallery.
Her dramatic presentment of the facts left no doubt about her argument.
The ceremony included the presentment of awards to outstanding community members.

Synset Examples

the presentation of new data
he gave the customer a demonstration

Related Terms

  • Synonym: presentation - the act of presenting; a formal introduction
  • Synonym: display - the act of showing something

Etymology

From Old French 'presentement', from présenter (to present).

2. NOUN

/ prɪˈzɛntmənt / (AmE), / prɪˈzɛntmənt / (BrE)
an official accusation of a crime made by a grand jury on its own initiative, without a prosecutor's request
official accusation by a grand jury
legal

Examples

The grand jury issued a presentment even though the prosecutor declined to bring charges.
The judge reviewed the presentment to determine whether there was enough evidence to proceed.
A presentment differs from an indictment because it originates from the jury's own investigation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indictment - a formal charge or accusation of a serious crime by a grand jury, usually initiated by a prosecutor

Etymology

From Old French 'presentement', from présenter 'to present'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01189953-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ prɪˈzɛntmənt / (AmE), / prɪˈzɛntmənt / (BrE)
a formal written document, especially one that is presented for acceptance or payment, such as a bill of exchange or promissory note.
formal document for payment or acceptance
legal

Examples

The bank will process the presentment before releasing any funds to the beneficiary.
Failure to accept the presentment within the stipulated time may result in penalties.
He submitted the presentment to the drawee for official acceptance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bill of exchange - a written order to pay a specified amount
  • Synonym: promissory note - a written promise to pay a specified sum

Etymology

From Old French *presentement*, from Latin *praesentare* (to present).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07205822-n
Synset POS: n
