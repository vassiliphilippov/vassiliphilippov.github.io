carbonic

1. ADJ

/ kɑrˈbɑnɪk / (AmE), / kɑːˈbɒnɪk / (BrE)
relating to or containing carbon, or producing carbon, especially in a chemical context
relating to carbon or containing carbon

Examples

The solution contains carbonic acid, which is found in carbonated drinks.
A carbonic compound typically has at least one atom of carbon in its structure.
Scientists studied the carbonic properties of the mineral sample.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carbonaceous - consisting of, containing, or resembling carbon

Etymology

From French 'carbonique', from 'carbone' (carbon).
