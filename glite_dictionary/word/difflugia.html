difflugia

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈfluːdʒiə / (AmE), / dɪˈfluːɡiə / (BrE)
A genus of protozoans that have an ovoid shell made of cemented sand grains.
genus of shelled protozoans

Examples

Under the microscope, the difflugia was visible, carrying its shell of sand grains.
Researchers often study difflugia to understand how protozoans construct their protective casings.
The presence of difflugia in water samples can indicate specific environmental conditions.

Etymology

From New Latin Difflugia, the genus name.
