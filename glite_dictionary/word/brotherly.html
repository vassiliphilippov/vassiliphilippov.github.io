brotherly

1. ADJ

/ ˈbrʌð.ɚ.li / (AmE), / ˈbrʌð.ə.li / (BrE)
showing affection, loyalty, or kindness typically characteristic of a brother
like a brother; affectionate

Examples

He gave his younger sister a brotherly hug before leaving for college.
Their brotherly bond remained strong even after years apart.
She appreciated his brotherly concern whenever she was feeling down.

Synset Examples

brotherly feelings
close fraternal ties

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fraternal - relating to brothers; characteristic of brothers

Etymology

From Middle English 'brotherly', from Old English 'brōðorlic' (brother-like).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00292337-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADV

/ ˈbrʌð.ɚ.li / (AmE), / ˈbrʌð.ə.li / (BrE)
(archaic) In a manner characteristic of a brother; like a brother would behave.
in a brother-like manner (archaic adverb)
archaic

Examples

He welcomed his friend brotherly and with open arms.
The king spoke brotherly to his subjects, winning their affection.
She treated the orphan brotherly, offering kindness and support.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brotherlike - in the manner of a brother

Etymology

Formed from 'brother' + '-ly' (adjectival and adverbial suffix).

Synset POS: r
