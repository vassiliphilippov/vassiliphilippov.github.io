bonny

1. ADJ

/ ˈbɑːni / (AmE), / ˈbɒni / (BrE)
attractive or pleasing to look at, especially in a healthy and lively way
attractive; pleasing in appearance

Examples

She wore a bonny blue dress to the village fair.
The old sailor spoke fondly of his bonny home by the sea.
What a bonny child you have!

Synset Examples

my bonny lass
there's a bonny bay beyond
a comely face
young fair maidens

Related Terms

  • Synonym: comely - pleasant to look at; attractive
  • Degree Higher: beautiful - pleasing the senses or mind aesthetically
  • Antonym: plain - not attractive or beautiful

Etymology

From Middle English 'boni', probably from Scots, influenced by Old French 'bon' (good).
