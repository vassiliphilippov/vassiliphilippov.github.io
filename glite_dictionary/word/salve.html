salve

1. NOUN

/ sæv / (AmE), / sɑːv / (BrE)
a semisolid preparation, often containing medicinal substances, applied externally to soothe or heal skin irritations
medicinal ointment for skin irritation
medical

Examples

She applied a medicinal salve to the burn to help it heal.
This herbal salve is known for soothing dry, cracked skin.
After the hike, he rubbed a cooling salve on his sunburnt shoulders.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ointment - a smooth oily substance used for medical or cosmetic purposes
  • Synonym: balm - a fragrant ointment or preparation used to heal or soothe the skin

Etymology

From Old English sealf, related to Old High German salba (ointment).

2. VERB

/ sæv / (AmE), / sɑːv / (BrE)
to apply a salve or ointment to (a wound or area), usually to promote healing or soothe pain
apply a healing ointment

Examples

The nurse carefully salved the child's scraped knee to prevent infection.
Ancient remedies often involved salving wounds with herbal ointments.
After the rash appeared, she immediately salved her skin to soothe the irritation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to anoint - to smear or rub with oil or an oily substance, especially for ritual purposes or healing
  • Degree Higher: to heal - to make or become healthy or whole again

Etymology

From Old English 'sealfian', from 'sealf' (ointment)

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00085659-v
Synset POS: v

3. NOUN

/ sæv / (AmE), / sælv / (BrE)
something that soothes, heals, or provides relief, especially from pain or distress, either physically or emotionally.
remedy or soothing relief

Examples

The kind words acted as a salve for her wounded pride.
After a stressful day, music is often a salve to my nerves.
He searched for a salve to ease his feelings of guilt.

Synset Examples

he needed a salve for his conscience

Related Terms

  • Synonym: balm - something that soothes or heals
  • Synonym: palliative - something that relieves pain or discomfort
  • Antonym: irritant - something that causes discomfort or pain

Etymology

From Old English *sealf* (salve, ointment), related to Old High German *salba*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00260235-n
Synset POS: n

4. VERB

/ sæv / (AmE), / sæv / (BrE)
to save someone or something from ruin, destruction, or harm
save from ruin or harm

Examples

The timely intervention managed to salve the company from bankruptcy.
She hoped her apology would salve her damaged reputation.
Only quick action by the firefighters could salve the historic building from destruction.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: save - to rescue from danger or harm
  • Antonym: to destroy - to damage something so badly that it no longer exists or cannot be repaired

Etymology

From Old English 'sealfian' (to anoint, heal), related to the noun 'salve'.
