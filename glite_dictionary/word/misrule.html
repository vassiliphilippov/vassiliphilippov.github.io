misrule

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɪsˈruːl / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˈruːl / (BrE)
bad or dishonest government; the situation in which a country or organization is ruled in a corrupt or inefficient way.
bad or dishonest government

Examples

The king's misrule led to widespread unrest and rebellion.
Years of misrule left the country struggling with poverty and corruption.
The historian analyzed the period of misrule that followed the civil war.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maladministration - inefficient or dishonest management, especially in government
  • Antonym: good government - rule characterized by honesty and efficiency

Etymology

From Middle English, from Anglo-Norman mesrerule, from mis- (wrongly) + rule.
