Scheldt

1. PROPN

/ ʃɛlt / (AmE), / ʃɛlt / (BrE)
a river in western Europe that rises in France, flows northeast through Belgium, and empties into the North Sea
river in western Europe

Examples

The Scheldt is a vital waterway for shipping between Antwerp and the North Sea.
Environmental efforts have improved the water quality of the Scheldt in recent years.
They took a boat trip along the Scheldt to admire the scenic views.

Etymology

From Dutch 'Schelde', the name of the river.
