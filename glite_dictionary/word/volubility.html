volubility

1. NOUN

/ ˌvɑːləˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌvɒljʊˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of speaking or writing fluently and with ease
fluency and ease in speech or writing

Examples

Her volubility impressed the panel, as she answered every question with ease and confidence.
Despite his volubility, his arguments sometimes lacked substance.
The volubility of the spokesperson helped smooth relations during the press conference.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fluency - the ability to express oneself easily and articulately
  • Synonym: eloquence - fluent or persuasive speaking or writing

Etymology

From Latin 'volubilitas' (fluidity, fluency), from 'volubilis' (rolling, fluent).
