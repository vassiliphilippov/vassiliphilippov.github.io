archaistic

1. ADJ

/ ɑrˈkeɪɪstɪk / (AmE), / ɑːˈkeɪɪstɪk / (BrE)
imitative of or characterized by an archaic style or manner, especially in language, art, or writing
imitative of archaic style or manner

Examples

The author used an archaistic tone to evoke the language of medieval times.
Some artists adopt archaistic techniques to give their work an antique appearance.
Her archaistic phrasing made the poem sound as though it belonged to a bygone era.

Synset Examples

archaistic writing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: archaic - very old or old-fashioned; from an earlier period

Etymology

From Greek 'archaïstikos', from 'archaios' (ancient).
