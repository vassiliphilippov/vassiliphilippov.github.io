NASA

1. PROPN

/ ˈnæsə / (AmE), / ˈnæsə / (BrE)
The National Aeronautics and Space Administration, an independent agency of the United States government responsible for the civilian space program, as well as aeronautics and aerospace research.
U.S. space and aeronautics agency
american

Examples

NASA launched the Perseverance rover to explore the surface of Mars.
Many groundbreaking discoveries in space science have been made by NASA.
After graduating, she applied for an internship at NASA to work on satellite technology.

Etymology

Acronym from National Aeronautics and Space Administration, formed in 1958.
