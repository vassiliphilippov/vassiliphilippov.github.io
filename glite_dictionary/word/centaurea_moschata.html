Centaurea moschata

1. NOUN

/ sɛnˈtɔriə mɒsˈkɑtə / (AmE), / sɛnˈtɔːrɪə mɒsˈkɑːtə / (BrE)
An Asian plant, also known as sweet sultan, widely cultivated for its sweetly fragrant pink flowers; sometimes placed in the genus Centaurea.
fragrant Asian flowering plant

Examples

The Centaurea moschata blooms filled the garden with a delightful fragrance.
She decided to plant Centaurea moschata for its beautiful pink flowers and pleasant scent.
Botanists sometimes debate the proper classification of Centaurea moschata within its genus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sweet sultan - Common name for Centaurea moschata

Etymology

From Latin: Centaurea, the genus name, and moschata, meaning 'musky,' referring to its scent.
