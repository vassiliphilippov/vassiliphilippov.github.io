isogon

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪsəˌɡɑn / (AmE), / ˈaɪsəˌɡɒn / (BrE)
A polygon in which all angles are equal; an equiangular polygon.
equiangular polygon

Examples

A regular hexagon is a type of isogon because all its angles are equal.
The mathematician studied a rare isogon with unequal sides but congruent angles.
An isogon need not have sides of equal length, only equal interior angles.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: polygon - a closed figure with three or more straight sides
  • Degree Higher: regular polygon - a polygon with all sides and all angles equal

Etymology

From Greek isos (equal) + gōnia (angle).
