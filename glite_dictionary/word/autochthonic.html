autochthonic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɔːtəˈkθɑːnɪk / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəˈkθɒnɪk / (BrE)
Originating in the place where it is found; indigenous or native to a particular location.
native or originating where found

Examples

The autochthonic flora of the region has adapted to its harsh environment.
Researchers studied the autochthonic traditions of the indigenous tribes.
Certain minerals are considered autochthonic because they form in place rather than being transported from elsewhere.

Synset Examples

the autochthonal fauna of Australia includes the kangaroo
autochthonous rocks and people and folktales
endemic folkways
the Ainu are indigenous to the northernmost islands of Japan

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indigenous - originating or occurring naturally in a particular place
  • Synonym: endemic - native or restricted to a certain country or area
  • Antonym: allochthonous - originating in a place other than where found; non-native

Etymology

From Greek 'autochthōn' (native, sprung from the land itself) + '-ic'.
