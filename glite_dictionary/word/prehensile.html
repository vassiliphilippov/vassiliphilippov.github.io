prehensile

1. ADJ

/ priˈhɛnsaɪl / (AmE), / priˈhɛnsaɪl / (BrE)
(of a body part) adapted for grasping or holding, especially by wrapping around objects.
adapted for grasping or holding

Examples

The monkey used its prehensile tail to swing from branch to branch.
Chameleons have prehensile tails that help them balance in trees.
Many marsupials possess prehensile feet for climbing and gripping.

Synset Examples

a monkey's prehensile tail

Etymology

From Latin prehensilis, from prehendere 'to grasp', via French.

2. ADJ

/ prɪˈhɛnsaɪl / (AmE), / prɪˈhɛnsaɪl / (BrE)
(figurative) Immoderately desirous of acquiring, especially wealth or possessions; grasping or greedy.
greedy or grasping (figurative use)

Examples

The company's prehensile executives cut costs ruthlessly to maximize profits.
Her prehensile interests made her unpopular with her less materialistic friends.
He showed a prehensile attitude when negotiating for a higher salary, never satisfied with reasonable offers.

Synset Examples

grasping commercialism

grew richer and greedier
prehensile employers stingy with raises for their employees

Related Terms

  • Synonym: greedy - having an intense and selfish desire for something, especially wealth, power, or food
  • Synonym: grasping - eager for gain, greedy or acquisitive
  • Antonym: generous - showing a readiness to give more of something than is strictly necessary or expected

Etymology

From Latin praehen sile, from prehendere 'to grasp'.
