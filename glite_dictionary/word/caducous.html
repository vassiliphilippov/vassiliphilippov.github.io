caducous

1. ADJ

/ kəˈduːkəs / (AmE), / kəˈdjuːkəs / (BrE)
(of plant or animal parts) shed or falling off at an early stage of development.
shed or falling off early (biology)

Examples

The flowers of this tree have a caducous calyx that drops soon after blooming.
Most amphibians possess caducous gills that disappear as they mature.
Some plants have caducous leaves that fall off while the plant is still young.

Synset Examples

most amphibians have caducous gills
the caducous calyx of a poppy

Etymology

From Latin cadūcus, meaning 'falling, perishable'.

2. ADJ

/ kəˈduːkəs / (AmE), / kəˈdjuːkəs / (BrE)
(of a plant part or organ) shed or falling off at an early stage of development.
falling off early (biology, botany)

Examples

Many poppy flowers have a caducous calyx that falls off as the bloom opens.
The caducous leaves of some trees drop soon after they emerge in spring.
In some amphibians, the gills are caducous and disappear as the animal matures.

Synset Examples

most amphibians have caducous gills
the caducous calyx of a poppy

Related Terms

  • Antonym: persistent - (of a plant part) remaining attached for a long time, opposite of caducous
  • Synonym: deciduous - (of leaves, teeth, etc.) shed at a certain stage, similar to caducous but not as early

Etymology

From Latin caducus (liable to fall; falling).
