Pope Urban V

1. PROPN

/ poʊp ˌɜrbən ˈfaɪv / (AmE), / pəʊp ˌɜːbən ˈfaɪv / (BrE)
(1310–1370) The French Pope from 1362 to 1370 who attempted to reestablish the papacy in Rome, later returning to Avignon, and was canonized in 1870.
French Pope (1362–1370); canonized saint

Examples

Pope Urban V sought to restore the papacy to Rome during his tenure in the 14th century.
The canonization of Pope Urban V took place centuries after his death, in 1870.
Many historians credit Pope Urban V with important reforms in the church despite ongoing wars in Europe.
