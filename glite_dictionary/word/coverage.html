coverage

1. NOUN

/ ˈkʌv.ɚ.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkʌv.ər.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
the reporting and presentation of news stories in newspapers, radio, or television
news reporting and presentation

Examples

Many viewers criticized the election coverage for lacking depth and balance.
The channel provided live coverage of the royal wedding throughout the day.
She felt the newspaper's coverage of environmental issues was particularly insightful.

Synset Examples

they accused the paper of biased coverage of race relations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reporting - the act of presenting news or information
  • Antonym: bias - unfairness or partiality in news coverage

Etymology

From 'cover' + '-age', originally meaning the act of covering; later extended to reporting news events.

2. NOUN

/ ˈkʌv.ɚ.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkʌv.ər.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
the total amount and type of insurance that someone has
total amount and type of insurance held

Examples

You should review your health insurance coverage every year to make sure it meets your needs.
After the accident, he realized his auto coverage was not enough to cover the damages.
The company increased its life insurance coverage for all employees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insurance - arrangement providing compensation for specified loss, damage, illness, or death

3. NOUN

/ ˈkʌv.ɚ.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkʌv.ər.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
the extent or degree to which something is included, dealt with, or covered, especially in reference to topics, subjects, or issues
extent to which something is covered

Examples

The report has broad coverage of environmental issues.
Her thesis lacked adequate coverage of recent research.
The magazine's coverage of art exhibitions is both thorough and insightful.

Synset Examples

the dictionary's coverage of standard English is excellent

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scope - range or extent of something addressed or covered
  • Synonym: breadth - the width or extent of something, especially in scope or coverage

Etymology

From cover + -age, dating from the late 19th century.
