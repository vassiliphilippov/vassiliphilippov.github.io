faecalith

1. NOUN

/ ˈfiːkəlɪθ / (AmE), / ˈfiːkəlɪθ / (BrE)
A hard mass of fecal matter that can develop in the intestines, often causing blockage or discomfort.
hard mass of fecal matter
medical british

Examples

The surgeon discovered a large faecalith obstructing the patient's colon.
An untreated faecalith can lead to severe abdominal pain and infection.
Doctors often use imaging studies to detect a faecalith in the intestines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fecalith - hard mass of fecal matter (US spelling)

Etymology

From Latin 'faex' (dregs, sediment) + Greek 'lithos' (stone).
