Onosmodium

1. NOUN

/ ˌoʊ.nɑːsˈmoʊ.di.əm / (AmE), / ˌəʊ.nɒzˈməʊ.di.əm / (BrE)
A genus of perennial herbs native to North America, belonging to the family Boraginaceae.
genus of North American perennial herbs

Examples

The genus Onosmodium includes several species commonly known as marbleseed.
Onosmodium plants are distinguished by their rough, hairy leaves and small, pale flowers.
Botanists often study Onosmodium to understand the diversity within the Boraginaceae family.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Boraginaceae - the borage or forget-me-not family of flowering plants

Etymology

From Modern Latin, formed from Greek 'onosma' (a plant name, 'bugloss') and New Latin '-odium' (a typical botanical suffix).
