to redact

1. VERB

/ rɪˈdækt / (AmE), / rɪˈdakt / (BrE)
to put (something) into a particular form or style of writing; to formulate or edit (text) in a specific way
formulate or edit in a style or language

Examples

The lawyer carefully redacted the contract to ensure clarity and precision.
Can you redact this passage in a more formal tone?
Her speech was redacted to fit the academic style required for publication.

Synset Examples

I wouldn't put it that way
She cast her request in very polite language

Related Terms

  • Synonym: phrase - to express in a particular way (to phrase)

Etymology

From Latin redactus, past participle of redigere (to bring back, reduce) via French rédiger (to compose, draft).

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ rɪˈdækt / (AmE), / rɪˈdækt / (BrE)
to prepare a written work for publication by editing, revising, or adapting its content
edit or prepare for publication

Examples

Before releasing the report, the agency had to redact sensitive information.
She spent weeks redacting the manuscript to correct errors and clarify unclear sections.
The legal team redacted confidential names from the documents before submitting them to the court.

Synset Examples

Edit a book on lexical semantics
she edited the letters of the politician so as to omit the most personal passages

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to edit - to prepare written material for publication by correcting or revising it
  • Synonym: censor - to remove or suppress sensitive content from a text

Etymology

From Latin redactus, past participle of redigere (to drive back, to bring together).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00200761-v
Synset POS: v
