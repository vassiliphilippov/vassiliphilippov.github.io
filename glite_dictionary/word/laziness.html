laziness

1. NOUN

/ ˈleɪzi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈleɪzɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being unwilling to work or use energy; apathy or inactivity, traditionally considered a vice or sin
unwillingness to work or use energy

Examples

His laziness kept him from reaching his full potential at work.
She blamed her poor grades on laziness rather than a lack of intelligence.
In some traditions, laziness is considered one of the seven deadly sins.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indolence - avoidance of activity or exertion; synonym for laziness
  • Antonym: diligence - careful and persistent work or effort; opposite of laziness

Etymology

From lazy + -ness; ultimately from Middle Low German lasich.

2. NOUN

/ ˈleɪzi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈleɪzɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being unwilling to work or use energy; a tendency to avoid effort or activity
lack of willingness to work

Examples

Her laziness caused her to miss several important deadlines at work.
Despite his obvious intelligence, he let laziness keep him from achieving his goals.
They blamed the team's defeat on a general sense of laziness during practice.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: indolence - avoidance of activity or exertion
  • Antonym: diligence - careful and persistent work or effort

Etymology

From Latin 'lasi-vus' (luxurious, indolent), via Old French 'laschete' and Middle English 'lasy' + '-ness'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-04644465-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈleɪzi.nəs / (AmE), / ˈleɪzi.nəs / (BrE)
a state or atmosphere characterized by relaxed and easy activity, often inducing comfort or a lack of urgency
relaxed, easygoing atmosphere or activity

Examples

The laziness of the summer afternoon made it hard for anyone to work.
There was a pleasant laziness about the morning as we sipped our coffee on the porch.
After weeks of stress, she embraced the laziness that came with her vacation.

Synset Examples

the laziness of the day helped her to relax

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leisureliness - the quality of being relaxed and unhurried

Etymology

From lazy + -ness.
