vena cerebri magna

1. NOUN

/ ˌveɪ.nə səˈriː.braɪ ˈmæɡ.nə / (AmE), / ˌviː.nə səˈreɪ.brɪ ˈmaɡ.nə / (BrE)
a large cerebral vein formed by the junction of the two internal cerebral veins and continuing into the straight sinus
major cerebral vein
medical

Examples

The vena cerebri magna plays an important role in draining blood from deep structures of the brain.
A blockage in the vena cerebri magna can lead to increased intracranial pressure.
Surgeons must be careful not to damage the vena cerebri magna during operations near the center of the brain.

Etymology

From Latin, literally 'great brain vein' (vena = vein, cerebri = of the brain, magna = great).
