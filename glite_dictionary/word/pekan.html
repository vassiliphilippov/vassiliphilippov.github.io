pekan

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈkæn / (AmE), / pɪˈkæn / (BrE)
A large, dark brown, carnivorous mammal native to North American forests, also known as the fisher.
North American carnivorous mammal (fisher)

Examples

The pekan is known for its agility in climbing trees to hunt prey.
Trappers once sought pekan pelts for their soft, valuable fur.
The forested regions of Canada are home to the elusive pekan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fisher - A carnivorous mammal of North America, closely related and often synonymous with pekan

Etymology

From Canadian French "pékan," of Algonquian origin.
