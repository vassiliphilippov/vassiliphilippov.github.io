Taiwanese

1. ADJ

/ ˌtaɪwəˈniz / (AmE), / ˌtaɪwəˈniːz / (BrE)
Relating to Taiwan, its people, language, or culture.
of or relating to Taiwan

Examples

Many Taiwanese dishes are influenced by both Chinese and Japanese cuisines.
The company has a Taiwanese branch located in Taichung.
She is proud of her Taiwanese heritage and traditions.

Synset Examples

the Taiwanese capital is Taipeh

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Taiwan - an island country in East Asia
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03134068-a
Synset POS: a

2. NOUN

/ taɪwəˈniz / (AmE), / taɪwəˈniːz / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of Taiwan
person from Taiwan

Examples

Many Taiwanese live and work in cities all around the world.
The Taiwanese have a rich and diverse culture influenced by many traditions.
She became friends with a Taiwanese during her exchange year in Taipei.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Chinese - a native or inhabitant of China

Etymology

From Taiwan (the name of the island and country) + -ese (denoting nationality or language, from Portuguese -ês).

3. NOUN

/ taɪˈwɑːniːz / (AmE), / taɪˈwɑːniːz / (BrE)
any of the forms of Chinese spoken in Taiwan or originally from Fujian (Fukien) province, especially Minnan (Hokkien, Southern Min).
a Chinese language variety spoken in Taiwan and Fujian

Examples

Many people in southern Fujian speak Taiwanese as their first language.
She is studying Taiwanese to communicate with her grandparents.
Taiwanese differs significantly from Mandarin Chinese in tone and vocabulary.

Etymology

From Taiwan + -ese, denoting language or people.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06943410-n
Synset POS: n
