to opalize

1. VERB

/ ˈoʊ.pə.laɪz / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.pə.laɪz / (BrE)
to replace or convert (a material) into opal, typically through natural geological processes
convert into opal

Examples

In this region, volcanic ash can opalize wood over millions of years.
Some fossils have opalized due to unique geological conditions.
The minerals in the soil began to slowly opalize ancient tree trunks.

Synset Examples

opalized tree trunks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opalization - the process of turning into opal

Etymology

From opal + -ize, patterned after similar mineralogical terms.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00116893-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈoʊpəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈəʊpəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make or become opalescent; to give an iridescent, milky appearance like that of an opal
make or become opalescent

Examples

The minerals in the cave walls began to opalize, creating a shimmering effect.
Certain conditions in the earth can cause wood to opalize over millions of years.
Artists sometimes try to opalize glass to imitate the look of real gemstones.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: opalescent - having a milky iridescence like an opal

Etymology

From 'opal', the gemstone, plus the verb-forming suffix '-ize'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00117038-v
Synset POS: v
