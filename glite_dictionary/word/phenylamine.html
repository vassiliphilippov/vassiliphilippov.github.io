phenylamine

1. NOUN

/ ˈfinəlˌæmin / (AmE), / ˈfiːnɪlˌæmiːn / (BrE)
A poisonous, oily liquid amine (also known as aniline), obtained from nitrobenzene and used in making dyes, plastics, and medicines.
oily poisonous amine (aniline) used in dyes

Examples

Phenylamine is a key ingredient in the manufacture of many synthetic dyes.
Exposure to phenylamine can pose health risks due to its toxic properties.
Chemists often refer to phenylamine as aniline when discussing its industrial applications.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aniline - an oily, colorless amine used in dyes; synonym of phenylamine

Etymology

From phenyl + amine, based on chemical structure derived from benzene.
