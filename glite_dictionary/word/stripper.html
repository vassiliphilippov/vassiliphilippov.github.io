stripper

1. NOUN

/ ˈstrɪpɚ / (AmE), / ˈstrɪpə / (BrE)
A worker who strips the stems from moistened tobacco leaves and binds the leaves together into bundles.
worker who processes tobacco leaves

Examples

The factory hired additional strippers during the busy tobacco season.
As a stripper, her tasks included removing stems from leaves and tying them into bundles.
Many strippers worked long hours in the warehouse to keep up with production.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstrɪpər / (AmE), / ˈstrɪpə / (BrE)
a performer who provides erotic entertainment by undressing, typically to music, in front of an audience
performer who undresses erotically

Examples

The club hired a famous stripper for Saturday night's show.
She worked as a stripper to pay her college tuition.
The bachelor party featured a professional stripper as entertainment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exotic dancer - a performer who entertains by dancing erotically, sometimes removing clothes

Etymology

From the verb 'strip' (to remove clothing) + -er (agent noun suffix).

4. NOUN

/ ˈstrɪpər / (AmE), / ˈstrɪpə / (BrE)
a chemical substance used to remove paint, varnish, or similar coatings from surfaces
chemical for removing paint or varnish

Examples

Apply the stripper to the wood and wait for the paint to bubble up.
This stripper is effective for removing old varnish from furniture.
Always use gloves when handling a chemical stripper to protect your skin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paint remover - a substance used to strip paint from surfaces

Etymology

From 'strip' + '-er', indicating something that strips or removes.
