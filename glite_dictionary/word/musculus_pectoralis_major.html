musculus pectoralis major

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌs.kjə.ləs ˌpɛk.təˈreɪ.lɪs ˈmeɪ.dʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈmʌs.kjʊ.ləs ˌpɛk.təˈreɪ.lɪs ˈmeɪ.dʒə / (BrE)
a large skeletal muscle of the chest that adducts and rotates the arm
major chest muscle
medical

Examples

During the surgery, the musculus pectoralis major was carefully separated to access the underlying tissues.
Bodybuilders work to increase the size of their musculus pectoralis major for a more prominent chest appearance.
An injury to the musculus pectoralis major can significantly reduce shoulder strength.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pectoralis major - the large chest muscle that adducts and rotates the arm

Etymology

From Latin 'musculus pectoralis major', meaning 'greater muscle of the chest'.
