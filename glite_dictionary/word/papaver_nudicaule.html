Papaver nudicaule

1. NOUN

/ pəˈpeɪvər nuːdɪˈkaʊli / (AmE), / pəˈpeɪvə njuːdɪˈkaʊli / (BrE)
A species of perennial poppy native to subarctic regions, with fragrant white, yellow, orange, or peach flowers, commonly known as the Iceland poppy.
a subarctic perennial poppy species

Examples

The Papaver nudicaule blooms early in the summer, brightening gardens with its delicate petals.
Botanists admire Papaver nudicaule for its ability to thrive in colder climates.
The gardener ordered seeds of Papaver nudicaule to add a splash of color to the border.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Iceland poppy - common name for Papaver nudicaule, a poppy with showy flowers

Etymology

From Latin: 'Papaver' (poppy) and 'nudicaule' (with bare stem), describing the plant's appearance.
