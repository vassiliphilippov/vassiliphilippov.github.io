expositor

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˈspɑːzətər / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɒzɪtə / (BrE)
a person who explains, interprets, or sets forth ideas, often in a formal or scholarly manner
person who explains or interprets

Examples

The renowned expositor clarified many difficult passages in the ancient manuscript.
During the lecture, the expositor answered every question from the audience with patience and detail.
She has become a respected expositor of modern art theories.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interpreter - a person who explains or translates spoken or written words
  • Synonym: commentator - a person who discusses or explains an event, text, or topic

Etymology

From Latin expositor (one who sets forth or explains), from exponere (to explain).
