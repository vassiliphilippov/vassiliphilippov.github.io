viremia

1. NOUN

/ vaɪˈriːmiə / (AmE), / vaɪˈriːmiə / (BrE)
the presence of a virus in the bloodstream, often leading to the spread of infection within the body
presence of virus in the bloodstream
medical

Examples

The patient developed viremia shortly after being exposed to the virus.
Blood tests confirmed the presence of viremia in several cases of the outbreak.
Effective treatment can prevent viremia from progressing to more severe complications.

Synset Examples

viremia spread the smallpox virus to the internal organs

Etymology

From Latin 'virus' (poison) + Greek '-emia' (condition of the blood).
