bong

1. NOUN

/ bɑŋ / (AmE), / bɒŋ / (BrE)
a dull, resonant sound, especially the stroke of a bell
a deep resonant bell sound

Examples

The bong of the church clock echoed through the quiet village.
At midnight, the final bong from the bell tower signaled the start of the new year.
A deep bong resounded from somewhere in the darkness, making everyone turn their heads.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clang - a loud, resonant metallic sound
  • Degree Lower: ding - the sound of a small bell
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07391054-n
Synset POS: n

2. VERB

/ bɑŋ / (AmE), / bɒŋ / (BrE)
(of a bell or similar object) to make a loud, deep, resonant sound.
make a loud, deep ringing sound

Examples

The church bell bonged at midnight, echoing through the quiet village.
A distant clock bonged, marking the passing of another hour.
As the ceremony began, the great bell bonged solemnly in the tower.

Synset Examples

the big bell bonged

Related Terms

  • Synonym: clang - make a loud resonant metallic sound
  • Degree Lower: chime - make melodious ringing sounds, as a bell
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02188949-v
Synset POS: v
