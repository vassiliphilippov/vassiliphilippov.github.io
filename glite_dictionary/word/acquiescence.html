acquiescence

1. NOUN

/ ˌækwiˈɛsəns / (AmE), / ˌakwɪˈɛsns / (BrE)
the reluctant acceptance of something without protest
acceptance without protest

Examples

Her acquiescence to the new rules surprised everyone, as she had always been outspoken before.
The organization's silent acquiescence was interpreted as agreement with the proposal.
He nodded in acquiescence, choosing not to argue further.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compliance - the act of complying with a wish or command
  • Antonym: resistance - refusal to accept or comply with something

Etymology

From Latin acquiescentia, from acquiescere (to rest, to become quiet).

2. NOUN

/ ˌækwiˈɛsəns / (AmE), / ˌakwɪˈɛsns / (BrE)
the reluctant acceptance of something without protest or the act of agreeing or consenting to a proposal or statement
reluctant agreement or acceptance

Examples

There was a silent acquiescence to the new policy among the employees.
She nodded in acquiescence when asked if she would attend the meeting.
His acquiescence to the decision surprised everyone, as he had opposed it earlier.

Synset Examples

he gave his assent eagerly
a murmur of acquiescence from the assembly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: assent - the expression of approval or agreement
  • Antonym: dissent - the holding or expression of opinions that differ with those previously held or commonly expressed

Etymology

From Latin acquiescentia (quiet rest, acquiescence), from acquiescere (to remain at rest, to acquiesce).
