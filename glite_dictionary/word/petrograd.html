Petrograd

1. PROPN

/ ˌpɛtroʊˈɡrɑd / (AmE), / ˌpɛtrəˈɡrɑːd / (BrE)
The name of Saint Petersburg, Russia, used from 1914 to 1924; formerly the capital of Russia.
historical name for Saint Petersburg

Examples

During the First World War, the city was known as Petrograd and played a crucial role in Russian history.
My great-grandfather emigrated from Petrograd to the United States in 1917.
The Bolshevik Revolution began in Petrograd, changing the course of Russian politics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Saint Petersburg - current name of the Russian city formerly called Petrograd
  • Synonym: Leningrad - name of the city from 1924 to 1991

Etymology

From Russian 'Петроград' (Petrograd), meaning 'Peter's City', named after Peter the Great.
