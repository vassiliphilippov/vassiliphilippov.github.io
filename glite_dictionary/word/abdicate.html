to abdicate

1. VERB

/ ˈæb.dɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈab.dɪ.keɪt / (BrE)
to formally give up a position of power or responsibility, especially that of a monarch or leader
formally give up a position of power

Examples

After months of political pressure, the president decided to abdicate his office and retire from public life.
Rumors spread quickly that the queen might abdicate due to ill health.
He refused to abdicate his responsibilities despite the mounting challenges.

Synset Examples

The King abdicated when he married a divorcee

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to resign - to voluntarily leave a job or position
  • Synonym: to relinquish - to give up or let go of something, especially power or responsibility
  • Antonym: to assume - to take on a position of power or responsibility

Etymology

From Latin abdicare, meaning 'to renounce' or 'to disown'.
