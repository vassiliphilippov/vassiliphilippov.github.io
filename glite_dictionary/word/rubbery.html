rubbery

1. ADJ

/ ˈrʌb.ɚ.i / (AmE), / ˈrʌb.ər.i / (BrE)
(of food) having a tough, elastic texture that is difficult to chew.
tough, elastic, hard to chew

Examples

The steak was so rubbery that it took me minutes to chew one bite.
She complained that the calamari was rubbery and overcooked.
If the eggs are cooked too long, they can become rubbery and unpleasant to eat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tough - (of food) difficult to cut or chew
  • Antonym: tender - (of food) easy to cut or chew

Etymology

From 'rubber' + '-y', referring to the elastic quality of rubber.

2. ADJ

/ ˈrʌb.ɚ.i / (AmE), / ˈrʌb.ər.i / (BrE)
having a texture that is flexible and tough like rubber
flexible and tough like rubber

Examples

The steak was overcooked and had a rubbery texture that made it hard to chew.
After hours in the water, my fingers felt rubbery and wrinkled.
The toy's rubbery material made it safe for children to squeeze and bend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: elastic - able to resume its normal shape after stretching or compression
  • Antonym: rigid - not able to be bent or changed easily; stiff

Etymology

From 'rubber' + '-y', forming an adjective meaning 'like rubber.'

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: s
