humid

1. ADJ

/ ˈhjuː.mɪd / (AmE), / ˈhjuː.mɪd / (BrE)
(of air or weather) containing a high amount of water vapor; damp or moist.
containing much water vapor (air/weather)

Examples

The humid summer made everyone feel sticky and uncomfortable.
After the rain, the air became noticeably humid inside the house.
We avoided outdoor sports due to the humid conditions that day.

Synset Examples

humid air
humid weather

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dry - lacking moisture; not humid
  • Synonym: damp - slightly wet or moist

Etymology

From Latin 'humidus' (moist, wet).
