e'er

1. ADV

/ ɛr / (AmE), / ɛə / (BrE)
(Archaic) At all times; ever; always.
archaic form of 'ever'
archaic

Examples

The poet wrote, 'Love conquers e'er the hardest heart.'
Would he e'er return to his childhood home, she wondered.
No one has e'er seen such a wondrous sight.

Synset Examples

I will always be there to help you
always arrives on time
there is always some pollution in the air
ever hoping to strike it rich
ever busy

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ever - at any time; always

Etymology

Contraction of 'ever', used chiefly in poetry and archaic texts.
