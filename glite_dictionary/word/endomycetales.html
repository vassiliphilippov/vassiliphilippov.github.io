Endomycetales

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛndoʊmaɪˈsiːteɪliːz / (AmE), / ˌɛndəʊmaɪˈsiːteɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of fungi in which the zygote or a single cell develops directly into an ascus
an order of fungi; ascus develops from one cell
medical

Examples

The yeast belonging to the Endomycetales order are frequently studied in microbiology laboratories.
Some species within the Endomycetales have important roles in fermentation industries.
The morphological diversity among the Endomycetales demonstrates their evolutionary adaptations.

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'endon' (within) + 'mykes' (fungus) + '-ales' (order suffix).

medical

Examples

The order Endomycetales includes several yeast-like fungi important in fermentation.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin, combining 'endo-' (within), 'mycete' (fungus), and the suffix '-ales' for orders.
