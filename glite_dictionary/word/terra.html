Terra

1. PROPN

/ ˈtɛr.ə / (AmE), / ˈtɛr.ə / (BrE)
(in Roman mythology) the goddess of the earth, and the protector of marriage and fertility; equivalent to the Greek Gaea.
Roman earth goddess

Examples

In ancient texts, Terra is often invoked as the mother of all living things.
Roman farmers would sometimes pray to Terra for fertile soil and abundant crops.
The myth of Terra highlights her role as a guardian of marriage and fertility.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gaea - Greek goddess of the earth; counterpart of Terra

Etymology

From Latin *Terra* (earth, land), also the name of the Roman earth goddess.
