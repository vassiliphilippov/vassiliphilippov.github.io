Engraulis encrasicholus

1. NOUN

/ ɛnˈɡrɔːlɪs ɛnˌkreɪsɪˈkoʊləs / (AmE), / ɛnˈɡrɔːlɪs ɛnˌkreɪsɪˈkɒləs / (BrE)
a species of small fish in the anchovy family, prized for its flavor and commonly preserved or used in sauces and relishes
species of anchovy, used in food

Examples

The Mediterranean diet often includes Engraulis encrasicholus for its rich, salty flavor.
Canned Engraulis encrasicholus are frequently used to prepare traditional Caesar salad dressing.
Overfishing has raised concerns about the sustainability of Engraulis encrasicholus in European waters.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anchovy - small fish used for food and flavoring

Etymology

From Latin: genus 'Engraulis' + species 'encrasicholus', from Greek 'engraulis', a kind of fish.
