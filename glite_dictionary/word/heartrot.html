heartrot

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrtˌrɑt / (AmE), / ˈhɑːtˌrɒt / (BrE)
A disease of trees in which the central heartwood decays, often caused by fungi.
disease causing decay of a tree's core

Examples

Foresters worry that heartrot can severely weaken old trees.
The oak showed signs of heartrot after years of fungal infection.
Heartrot often goes unnoticed until the tree is felled or topples in a storm.

Etymology

From 'heart' (the central part) + 'rot' (decay), referring to the rotting of the heartwood.
