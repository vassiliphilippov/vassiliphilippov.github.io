proration

1. NOUN

/ proʊˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / prəʊˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the allocation or limitation of production or distribution of something, such as crude oil or natural gas, to a proportional share of the total capacity of each producer
proportional allocation or limitation

Examples

Due to market oversupply, the government implemented proration of oil production among companies.
During the contract negotiation, proration was used to ensure each party received a fair share.
The utility company announced a proration of gas distribution during the winter shortage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pro rata - in proportion (Latin term used for proportional division)

Etymology

From Latin 'prorata', meaning 'according to the proportion' (pro rata).
