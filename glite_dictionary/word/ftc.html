FTC

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛf ˌti ˈsi / (AmE), / ˌɛf ˌtiː ˈsiː / (BrE)
the Federal Trade Commission, an independent agency of the U.S. government responsible for promoting fair competition and enforcing antitrust laws
U.S. government agency promoting fair trade
american

Examples

The FTC launched an investigation into deceptive advertising practices.
Consumers can file complaints about fraud directly with the FTC.
The new privacy guidelines were introduced by the FTC this year.

Etymology

Abbreviation of Federal Trade Commission.
