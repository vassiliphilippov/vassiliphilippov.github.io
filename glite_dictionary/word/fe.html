Fe

1. NOUN

/ ɛf i / (AmE), / ɛf iː / (BrE)
the chemical symbol for iron, a heavy, ductile, magnetic, silver-white metallic element used in construction, tools, armaments, and found in hemoglobin for oxygen transport
chemical symbol for iron

Examples

The periodic table lists Fe as the symbol for iron.
Red blood cells need Fe to transport oxygen throughout the body.
Steel contains a high percentage of Fe as its primary component.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: iron - a chemical element, symbol Fe

Etymology

From Latin 'ferrum' (iron), which is the origin of the chemical symbol.
