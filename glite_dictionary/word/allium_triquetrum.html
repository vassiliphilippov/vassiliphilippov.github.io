Allium triquetrum

1. NOUN

/ ˈæliəm traɪˈkwɛtrəm / (AmE), / ˈæliəm trɪˈkwɛtrəm / (BrE)
A species of wild leek native to parts of Europe, characterized by its triangular leaves and white, bell-shaped flowers; often naturalized in Britain.
species of wild leek with triangular leaves

Examples

The Allium triquetrum is easily identified by its triangular stems and white flowers.
Gardeners often remove Allium triquetrum because it can spread rapidly in mild climates.
You can use young leaves of Allium triquetrum as a mild, onion-like addition to salads.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: three-cornered leek - Common name for Allium triquetrum

Etymology

From Latin: 'Allium' (garlic/leek genus) + 'triquetrum' (three-cornered, referring to the triangular leaves).
