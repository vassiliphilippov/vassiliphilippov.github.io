Hamamelites

1. NOUN

/ ˌhæməˈmiːlɪtiːz / (AmE), / ˌhæməˈmiːlɪtiːz / (BrE)
A genus of extinct fossil plants with leaves resembling those of the witch hazel.
genus of fossil plants

Examples

Paleobotanists discovered leaves of Hamamelites in the sediment layers of the ancient lake.
The unique venation pattern of Hamamelites helped identify the plant's relationship to modern witch hazel.
Studies of Hamamelites fossils reveal important information about prehistoric plant evolution.

Etymology

From New Latin; related to Hamamelis (witch hazel genus) + -ites (used in fossil genus names).

2. NOUN

/ ˌhæməˈmiːlaɪtiːz / (AmE), / ˌhæməˈmiːlɪtiːz / (BrE)
A genus of extinct fossil plants whose leaves resemble those of the witch hazel.
genus of fossil plants

Examples

The extinct genus Hamamelites was first described based on leaf fossils found in Europe.
Scientists compared the leaf structure of Hamamelites to that of modern witch hazel.
Many Hamamelites fossils have been discovered in Eocene geological formations.

Etymology

Formed from modern Latin, based on Hamamelis (witch hazel) + Greek '-ites' (connected with).
