coalescency

1. NOUN

/ koʊ.əˈlɛs.ən.si / (AmE), / kəʊ.əˈlɛs.ən.si / (BrE)
the process or state of coming together to form a single whole; the union of distinct parts.
the act of uniting into one

Examples

The coalescency of several small organizations led to a much larger entity.
In biology, the coalescency of plant cells can be observed under certain conditions.
Social change often results from the coalescency of diverse movements into a unified force.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coalescence - the process of joining to form one mass or whole

Etymology

From Latin 'coalescere' (to grow together) and the suffix '-ency' denoting a state or condition.
