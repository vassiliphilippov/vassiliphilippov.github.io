fingerflower

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɪŋɡɚˌflaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɪŋɡəˌflaʊə / (BrE)
A tall European biennial or perennial plant with clusters of large tubular pink-purple flowers; its leaves produce digitalis and are poisonous to livestock.
plant with tubular purple flowers; source of digitalis

Examples

The garden's border was lined with beautiful fingerflowers in full bloom.
Caution is needed, as fingerflower leaves can be toxic to animals if ingested.
Pharmaceutical companies extract digitalis from the leaves of the fingerflower.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: foxglove - a tall plant with tubular purple flowers, source of digitalis
