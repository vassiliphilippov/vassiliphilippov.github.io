perambulation

1. NOUN

/ pəˌræmbjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / pəˌræmbjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
an official walk around the boundaries of a territory, such as a parish or manor, to assert and record them
official boundary-walking of territory
archaic

Examples

The annual perambulation of the parish boundaries attracted a large crowd of villagers.
Historically, a perambulation was performed by officials to resolve disputes over land ownership.
Records of the old forest perambulation provide insight into medieval land management.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: boundaries - limits or dividing lines of an area

Etymology

From Latin perambulatio (‘a walk through’), from perambulare (‘to walk through’).

2. NOUN

/ pəˌræm.bjəˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / pəˌræm.bjʊˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A leisurely walk, especially taken in a public place.
leisurely walk in public

Examples

She enjoyed a quiet perambulation through the park every Sunday morning.
The streets were perfect for an early evening perambulation after dinner.
During their perambulation of the gardens, they admired the spring flowers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stroll - a slow, relaxed walk
  • Synonym: saunter - a slow, relaxed walk, often with no particular destination
  • Antonym: march - to walk with regular, measured steps, often in military formation

Etymology

From Latin perambulationem (a walking around), from perambulare (to walk through).
