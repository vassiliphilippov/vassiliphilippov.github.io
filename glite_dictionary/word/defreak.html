to defreak

1. VERB

/ dɪˈfriːk / (AmE), / dɪˈfriːk / (BrE)
to tidy or organize a messy area or restore order after chaos
tidy or restore order after chaos
informal

Examples

After her roommates left, she had to defreak the entire apartment before guests arrived.
He spent his Saturday morning defreaking his cluttered office desk.
Once the event was over, the team quickly defreaked the conference room.

Synset Examples

He tried to defreak his living space after the party.
It took hours to defreak the house after the kids left.
