adiabaticity

1. NOUN

/ ˌæd.i.əˌbæt.ɪˈsɪt.i / (AmE), / ˌad.ɪ.əˌbat.ɪˈsɪt.i / (BrE)
The quality or condition of a process occurring without transfer of heat or change in entropy, especially under the assumption of a quasistatic process.
state of no heat transfer in a process
medical

Examples

In thermodynamics, adiabaticity is crucial for understanding certain idealized processes.
Maintaining adiabaticity ensures that a system changes state without exchanging heat with its surroundings.
Researchers measured the degree of adiabaticity during the experiment to determine energy conservation.

Synset Examples

The aim of this article is to study the adiabaticity of the ramping process of an ac dipole as a function of the different parameters involved.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: adiabatic - of or relating to a process without heat transfer

Etymology

From 'adiabatic', after Greek 'adiabatos' (impassable) + '-ity'.
