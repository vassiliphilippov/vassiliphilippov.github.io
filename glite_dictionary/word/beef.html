beef

1. NOUN

/ biːf / (AmE), / biːf / (BrE)
(informal) A complaint or objection; something one is dissatisfied or angry about.
informal complaint or objection
informal slang

Examples

He always has a beef with the way the manager runs meetings.
Do you have a beef about the new policy, or are you just joking?
If you have a beef with your neighbor, you should talk it out.

Synset Examples

I have a gripe about the service here

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gripe - a complaint or grievance (informal)
  • Synonym: complaint - an expression of dissatisfaction or annoyance

2. VERB

/ biːf / (AmE), / biːf / (BrE)
to complain, especially habitually or in a persistent way; to express dissatisfaction about something
to complain persistently
informal american

Examples

He's always beefing about the quality of the food in the cafeteria.
You shouldn't beef so much if you're not willing to help fix the problem.
The employees beefed to management about the lack of air conditioning during the summer.

Synset Examples

What was he hollering about?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grumble - to complain about something in a quiet, slightly angry way
  • Synonym: gripe - to complain about something persistently
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration

Etymology

Informal use in American English, possibly from earlier expressions such as 'have a beef' meaning 'have a complaint.'

3. NOUN

/ biːf / (AmE), / biːf / (BrE)
the flesh of a cow, bull, or steer, used as food
meat from a cow, bull, or steer

Examples

She ordered a beef steak with mashed potatoes for dinner.
The chef prefers to use fresh beef for making hamburgers.
Many people enjoy slow-cooked beef in stews and soups.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: pork - meat from a pig
  • Degree Lower: veal - meat from a young calf
  • Synonym: mutton - meat from an adult sheep

Etymology

From Old French *buef*, from Latin *bovem* (ox, cow).

4. NOUN

/ biːf / (AmE), / biːf / (BrE)
Cattle that are bred and raised for their meat.
cattle raised for meat

Examples

The ranch specializes in raising beef for high-quality steaks and roasts.
Farmers are working to improve the health and productivity of their beef herds.
Grass-fed beef are often preferred for their leaner meat.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dairy cattle - Cattle bred primarily for milk production

Etymology

From Old French *boef*, from Latin *bos* (ox, cow).
