tobramycin

1. NOUN

/ ˌtoʊbrəˈmaɪsən / (AmE), / ˌtəʊbrəˈmaɪsɪn / (BrE)
an antibiotic used to treat infections caused by Gram-negative bacteria, often administered for severe bacterial infections
an antibiotic drug
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed tobramycin to treat the patient's lung infection.
Patients with cystic fibrosis may receive tobramycin via inhalation to help control bacteria.
Allergic reactions to tobramycin are rare but possible and should be reported immediately.

Etymology

From 'tobramycin,' named after its bacterial source Streptomyces tenebrarius, with '-mycin' indicating an antibiotic derived from Streptomyces.
