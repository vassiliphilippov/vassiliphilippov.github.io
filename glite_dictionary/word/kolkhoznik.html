kolkhoznik

1. NOUN

/ kɑlˈxɑz.nɪk / (AmE), / kɒlˈxɒz.nɪk / (BrE)
A member of a kolkhoz (a collective farm in the former Soviet Union).
member of a Soviet collective farm

Examples

The kolkhoznik rose early each day to tend the communal fields with his neighbors.
During Soviet times, a kolkhoznik might receive special recognition for exceeding agricultural targets.
She interviewed a retired kolkhoznik about life on the collective farm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: kolkhoz - a collective farm in the former Soviet Union

Etymology

From Russian колхозник (kolkhozhnik), from колхоз (kolkhoz, 'collective farm') + -ник (-nik, 'person').
