venality

1. NOUN

/ viˈnæləti / (AmE), / viːˈnælɪti / (BrE)
willingness to sell one's influence, services, or power for personal gain; openness to bribery or corruption
openness to bribery or corruption

Examples

The politician's venality shocked even his most cynical critics.
Widespread venality in the system led to public distrust of government officials.
She refused to tolerate venality in any form, demanding honesty from her employees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corruption - dishonest or fraudulent conduct by those in power, typically involving bribery
  • Antonym: integrity - the quality of being honest and having strong moral principles

Etymology

From Latin venalis (for sale, open to bribery), via French vénalité.
