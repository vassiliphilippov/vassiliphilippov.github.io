Celtis

1. NOUN

/ ˈsɛltɪs / (AmE), / ˈkɛltɪs / (BrE)
A genus of trees and shrubs with berrylike fruit, commonly known as hackberries.
genus of trees and shrubs (hackberries)

Examples

The Celtis genus includes several species of hackberries native to temperate regions.
A landscaper recommended planting a Celtis tree for its tolerance to urban conditions.
The berrylike fruits of the Celtis attract many species of birds.

Etymology

From Latin *celtis*, a name used by Pliny for a tree with sweet fruit.
