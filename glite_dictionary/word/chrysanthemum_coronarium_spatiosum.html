Chrysanthemum coronarium spatiosum

1. NOUN

/ kraɪˌsænθəˈmʌm kəˌroʊˈnɛəriəm spəˈtɪoʊsəm / (AmE), / kraɪˌsænθɪˈmiːəm kɒˌrəʊˈneəriəm spæˈtɪəsəm / (BrE)
a cultivated variety of chrysanthemum grown for its succulent, edible leaves, commonly used in Asian cooking
edible-leaf chrysanthemum variety used in Asian cuisine

Examples

In Chinese stir-fries, chefs often use Chrysanthemum coronarium spatiosum for its tender, aromatic leaves.
You can find fresh Chrysanthemum coronarium spatiosum at Asian supermarkets during the spring season.
The unique flavor of Chrysanthemum coronarium spatiosum leaves enhances many traditional Japanese hot pot dishes.

Etymology

From Latin: 'chrysanthemum' (golden flower), 'coronarium' (for garlands), 'spatiosum' (spacious, wide).
