Pilularia globulifera

1. NOUN

/ pɪˌluːˈlɛriə ɡloʊˌbjuːlɪˈfɪrə / (AmE), / pɪˌljuːˈlɛəriə ɡlɒˌbjuːlɪˈfɪərə / (BrE)
a small European water fern that grows near the margins of bodies of water or in wet acidic soil, characterized by small, globular sporocarps
a species of European water fern

Examples

Pilularia globulifera is often found in shallow, acidic ponds throughout western Europe.
Conservationists are monitoring populations of Pilularia globulifera due to its rarity in certain regions.
The tiny, round sporocarps make Pilularia globulifera easy to distinguish from other aquatic ferns.

Etymology

From Latin: 'Pilularia' meaning 'little pill' (referring to the sporocarps), 'globulifera' meaning 'bearing small spheres'.
