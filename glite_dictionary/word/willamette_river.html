Willamette River

1. PROPN

/ wɪˈlæmət ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / wɪˈlæmət ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in western Oregon, United States, that flows north into the Columbia River near Portland
a river in Oregon, USA

Examples

The Willamette River runs through the heart of Portland, providing scenic views and recreational opportunities.
Many residents enjoy kayaking and fishing in the Willamette River during the summer months.
Heavy rainfall caused the Willamette River to rise rapidly, prompting flood warnings in the area.

Etymology

From the Native American Clackamas language, likely referencing a settlement or locality along the river.
