efferent

1. NOUN

/ ˈefəˌrənt / (AmE), / ˈefər(ə)nt / (BrE)
a nerve that carries impulses away from the central nervous system toward muscles or glands
a nerve carrying impulses away from the CNS
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that the efferent nerves transmit signals from the brain to the muscles.
Damage to an efferent pathway can result in impaired motor function.
Unlike afferent nerves, efferent fibers carry information away from the spinal cord.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: afferent - a nerve carrying impulses toward the central nervous system

Etymology

From Latin 'efferens', present participle of 'efferre' (to carry out).

2. ADJ

/ ˈɛf.ə.rənt / (AmE), / ˈɛf.ər.ənt / (BrE)
(of nerves or nerve impulses) conveying information or signals away from the central nervous system or a central organ.
carrying signals from the CNS
medical

Examples

The doctor explained that efferent nerves carry impulses away from the central nervous system to the muscles.
Damage to efferent pathways can result in loss of motor function.
The research focused on how efferent signals influence muscle contraction.

Synset Examples

efferent nerves and impulses

Related Terms

  • Antonym: afferent - (of nerves) conveying signals toward the central nervous system

Etymology

From Latin 'efferent-', from 'efferre' (to carry out).
