herm

1. NOUN

/ hɝːm / (AmE), / hɜːm / (BrE)
A statue consisting of a squared stone pillar topped with a carved head, especially of Hermes, used in ancient Greece as a boundary marker or signpost.
Greek stone pillar statue with a head

Examples

The ancient Greeks placed a herm at the entrance of the city as a protective symbol.
A marble herm of Hermes stood at the crossroads, marking the boundary between farms.
In the museum, you can see a classical herm with a weathered face on top of a pillar.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: herma - alternative form or variant of 'herm' referring to the same type of statue

Etymology

From Greek *hermēs* (a boundary marker), named after the god Hermes.
