Synanceja verrucosa

1. NOUN

/ sɪˌnænˈsiːdʒə vɛˈruːkəsə / (AmE), / sɪˌnænˈsiːdʒə vɛˈruːkəsə / (BrE)
A species of venomous tropical marine fish, commonly known as the stonefish, that resembles a piece of rock and can deliver a powerful sting.
venomous tropical marine fish (stonefish)

Examples

The venom of the Synanceja verrucosa can cause excruciating pain and requires immediate medical attention.
Divers are warned to watch for the Synanceja verrucosa, which often blends perfectly with the rocky seabed.
A researcher carefully handled the Synanceja verrucosa to avoid its dangerously sharp spines.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stonefish - common name for Synanceja verrucosa, a venomous marine fish

Etymology

From the Latin 'verrucosa' meaning 'warty,' and from the genus name 'Synanceja'.
