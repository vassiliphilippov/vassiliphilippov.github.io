Muscivora forficata

1. NOUN

/ ˌmʌsɪˈvɔːrə fɔːrˈfɪkətə / (AmE), / ˌmʌsɪˈvɔːrə fɔːˈfɪkətə / (BrE)
A gray flycatcher of the southwestern United States, Mexico, and Central America, notable for its long forked tail, white breast, and salmon to scarlet markings; also known as the scissor-tailed flycatcher.
a species of flycatcher bird with a long forked tail

Examples

The Muscivora forficata is often spotted performing dazzling aerial displays during mating season.
Bird watchers travel to Texas each spring to observe flocks of Muscivora forficata.
The distinctive forked tail of the Muscivora forficata makes it easy to identify in flight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scissor-tailed flycatcher - Common name for Muscivora forficata

Etymology

From Latin: 'musca' (fly) + 'vorare' (to devour) + 'forficatus' (scissor-shaped), referring to its shape and insect-eating habit.
