resource

1. NOUN

/ ˈriːˌsɔrs / (AmE), / rɪˈzɔːs / (BrE)
a source of help, supply, or support that can be used when needed
source of help or support

Examples

The local library is a valuable resource for students and researchers.
Many developing countries lack the financial resources to improve their infrastructure.
Online learning platforms have become an important educational resource in recent years.

Synset Examples

the local library is a valuable resource

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asset - a useful or valuable thing or person

Etymology

From Old French 'ressource', from Latin 'resurgere' (to rise again).

2. NOUN

/ rɪˈsɔrs / (AmE), / rɪˈzɔːs / (BrE)
the ability to act effectively and creatively, especially in difficult situations; resourcefulness
resourcefulness, ability to handle problems

Examples

She is a woman of remarkable resource, always finding solutions in tough situations.
During the crisis, his resource impressed everyone on the team.
The job requires someone with great resource and determination.

Synset Examples

a man of resource

Related Terms

  • Synonym: resourcefulness - ability to deal skillfully and promptly with new or difficult situations

Etymology

From Old French 'ressource', from Medieval Latin 'resurgere' (to rise again, recover), referring to means of overcoming difficulty.

3. NOUN

/ ˈriːˌsɔrs / (AmE), / rɪˈzɔːs / (BrE)
a supply of something, such as money, materials, or energy, that can be used when needed, especially to function effectively or achieve a goal
available supply or source of support

Examples

The company uses its financial resources to invest in new technology.
Water is a vital natural resource for every country.
Teachers often rely on a variety of resources to enhance classroom learning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: asset - a useful or valuable thing or person
  • Antonym: liability - something disadvantageous or a financial obligation

Etymology

From Old French 'ressource', from Latin 'resurgere' (to rise again, spring up).
