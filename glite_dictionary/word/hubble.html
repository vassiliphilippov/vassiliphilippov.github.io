Hubble

1. PROPN

/ ˈhʌbəl / (AmE), / ˈhʌbəl / (BrE)
Edwin Hubble (1889–1953), a United States astronomer who discovered that the universe is expanding, with galaxies receding at a rate proportional to their distance.
U.S. astronomer, discovered universe expansion

Examples

Hubble's observations fundamentally changed our understanding of the universe.
Many astronomers refer to Hubble when discussing the expanding universe.
The Hubble Space Telescope was named in honor of the famous astronomer.
