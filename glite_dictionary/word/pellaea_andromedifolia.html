Pellaea andromedifolia

1. NOUN

/ pəˈliː.ə ænˌdrɒ.mə.dəˈfoʊ.li.ə / (AmE), / pɛˈliː.ə ænˌdrɒ.mə.dɪˈfəʊ.lɪ.ə / (BrE)
an evergreen fern native to California and Baja California, commonly found in rocky hillsides and coastal regions
an evergreen fern of California and Baja California

Examples

Pellaea andromedifolia thrives on rocky slopes in southern California.
Gardeners sometimes cultivate Pellaea andromedifolia for its attractive, evergreen foliage.
When hiking in Baja California, you may spot Pellaea andromedifolia among the natural vegetation.

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Pellaea', species epithet from Greek 'andromeda' (a mythological figure) and Latin 'folia' (leaves).
