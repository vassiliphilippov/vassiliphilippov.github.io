therapeutic

1. NOUN

/ ˌθɛrəˈpjutɪk / (AmE), / ˌθɛrəˈpjuːtɪk / (BrE)
A medicine or treatment used to cure disease or relieve pain.
medicine or treatment for healing
medical

Examples

The new drug proved to be an effective therapeutic for the treatment of chronic pain.
Researchers are searching for better therapeutics to manage the symptoms of the disease.
Some patients respond well to the standard therapeutic, while others require alternative treatments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remedy - a medicine or treatment for a disease or injury

Etymology

From Greek therapeuō (to treat medically).

2. ADJ

/ ˌθɛrəˈpjutɪk / (AmE), / ˌθɛrəˈpjuːtɪk / (BrE)
relating to the treatment of illness or the practice of therapy; having healing or beneficial effects.
relating to treatment or therapy

Examples

The doctor recommended a therapeutic exercise program to aid recovery.
Some people find painting to be a deeply therapeutic activity.
A therapeutic approach can be beneficial for those struggling with anxiety.

Synset Examples

therapeutic approach to criminality

Related Terms

  • Synonym: curative - having the ability to cure or heal
  • Antonym: harmful - causing injury or damage

Etymology

From Greek therapeutikos (inclined to serve, cure), from therapeuein (to attend, treat).
