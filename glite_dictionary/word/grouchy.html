grouchy

1. ADJ

/ ˈɡraʊtʃi / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊtʃi / (BrE)
easily annoyed or irritable; often in a bad mood
easily annoyed; irritable

Examples

He tends to be grouchy in the mornings before he's had his coffee.
After a long day at work, she was feeling grouchy and snapped at her kids.
Don't mind him—he's just grouchy because he didn't sleep well last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grumpy - bad-tempered and irritable
  • Antonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic

Etymology

Probably from 'grouch' (grumble, complain) + '-y'.
