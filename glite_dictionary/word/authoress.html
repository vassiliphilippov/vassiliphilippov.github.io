authoress

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːθərəs / (AmE), / ˈɔːθərɪs / (BrE)
a female author (now considered outdated or sometimes offensive); a woman who writes books, articles, or other works
female author (outdated term)
archaic

Examples

The authoress received critical acclaim for her first novel in the nineteenth century.
Several Victorian-era authoresses published under pseudonyms to gain greater respect.
The literary club invited a famous authoress to speak about her experiences in a male-dominated field.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: author - a person who writes books or other works
  • Synonym: writer - a person who writes literary or other works

Etymology

Formed from 'author' + '-ess', a Latin-derived suffix denoting female gender.
