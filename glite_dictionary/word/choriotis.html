Choriotis

1. NOUN

/ kɔːrˈiːətɪs / (AmE), / kɒrˈɪətɪs / (BrE)
A genus of large ground-dwelling birds in the bustard family, native to Australia; commonly known as the Australian bustard.
genus of Australian bustards

Examples

The Choriotis is well-adapted to the open grasslands of northern Australia.
Many photographers visit the Outback hoping to spot a Choriotis in the wild.
The Choriotis performs elaborate courtship displays during the breeding season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bustard - large, heavy-bodied, ground-dwelling bird of the family Otididae

Etymology

From Greek *chōrios* (of a separate place), used in zoological nomenclature.

2. NOUN

/ kɔːriˈoʊtɪs / (AmE), / kɒrɪˈəʊtɪs / (BrE)
A genus of large terrestrial birds in the bustard family, native to Australia; includes the Australian bustard.
genus of Australian bustards

Examples

The Choriotis is notable for its impressive size and flight capabilities.
Birdwatchers were excited to spot a rare Choriotis in the outback.
Recent studies have focused on conserving the habitat of the Choriotis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bustard - large terrestrial bird, family Otididae

Etymology

From Greek 'chōriōtēs' (of the country, rustic), used in taxonomy.
