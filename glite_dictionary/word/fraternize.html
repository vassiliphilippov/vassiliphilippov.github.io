to fraternize

1. VERB

/ ˈfrætərˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfrætənaɪz / (BrE)
to be friendly with someone, especially when one is not supposed to; to associate with others as if they were brothers, often with members of an opposing group or enemy
be friendly with someone, esp. an enemy

Examples

It was strictly forbidden for soldiers to fraternize with the local population during the occupation.
Management discouraged employees from fraternizing with competitors at the trade conference.
Despite the rivalry, the players often fraternize off the field and share meals together.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to socialize - to interact or mix socially with others
  • Antonym: to avoid - to keep away from or not associate with

Etymology

From Latin fraternizare, from fraternus (brotherly) + -ize.
