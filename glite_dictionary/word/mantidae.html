Mantidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæn.tɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˈmæn.tɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of insects commonly known as mantises, characterized by their elongated bodies and grasping forelegs
family of insects known as mantises

Examples

The Mantidae are easily recognized by their triangular heads and spiny forelegs.
Many gardeners appreciate Mantidae species as natural pest control agents.
Scientists have discovered new species within the Mantidae family in tropical regions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mantis - an insect known for its upright posture and grasping forelegs

Etymology

From modern Latin Mantidae, based on the genus name Mantis.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmæntɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈmæntɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of predatory insects commonly known as mantises, characterized by their elongated bodies and grasping forelegs
family of predatory insects (mantises)

Examples

The Mantidae are known for their remarkable camouflage, allowing them to ambush prey with ease.
Many species within the Mantidae family can be found in tropical and temperate regions worldwide.
Scientists have discovered several new genera within the Mantidae during recent field studies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mantis - any of various predatory insects characterized by their upright posture and grasping forelegs

Etymology

From Modern Latin Mantidae, based on the type genus Mantis.
