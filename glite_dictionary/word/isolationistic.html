isolationistic

1. ADJ

/ ˌaɪsəˌleɪʃəˈnɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəˌleɪʃəˈnɪstɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or supporting isolationism, especially in a political context.
pertaining to isolationism

Examples

His isolationistic attitude prevented him from supporting international agreements.
The leader's isolationistic policies drew both criticism and praise from various groups.
Many historians debate whether the country was ever truly isolationistic in the early twentieth century.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: isolationist - a person who supports isolationism

Etymology

From 'isolationism' + '-istic', by analogy with similar adjectival forms.
