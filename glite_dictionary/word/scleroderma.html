Scleroderma

1. NOUN

/ sklɛr.əˈdɜr.mə / (AmE), / sklɛr.əˈdɜː.mə / (BrE)
A genus of poisonous fungi with hard-skinned fruiting bodies, commonly known as false truffles.
genus of poisonous fungi (false truffles)

Examples

The poisonous fungus Scleroderma is often mistaken for edible truffles by amateur foragers.
Researchers discovered several new species within the Scleroderma genus in the forest soil.
Because Scleroderma has a hard outer skin, it can be difficult to cut open.

Etymology

From Greek 'skleros' (hard) + 'derma' (skin), referring to the tough outer cover.

2. NOUN

/ ˌsklɛrəˈdɜrmə / (AmE), / ˌsklɛrəˈdɜːmə / (BrE)
A genus of poisonous fungi with hard outer skins, known as false truffles.
genus of poisonous fungi (false truffles)

Examples

The poisonous Scleroderma mushroom is sometimes mistaken for edible truffles.
Researchers collected several species from the Scleroderma genus for laboratory analysis.
Due to its tough exterior, Scleroderma is often called a 'hard-skinned' fungus.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: false truffle - a common name for Scleroderma fungi

Etymology

From Greek *skleros* (hard) + *derma* (skin), referring to the tough outer layer of the fungi.
