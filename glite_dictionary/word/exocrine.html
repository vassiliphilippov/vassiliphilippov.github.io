exocrine

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛksəˌkraɪn / (AmE), / ˈɛksəʊkrɪn / (BrE)
Relating to glands that secrete substances outward through a duct, rather than into the bloodstream.
relating to glands that secrete externally
medical

Examples

The pancreas has both endocrine and exocrine functions.
Certain digestive enzymes are produced by exocrine glands in the mouth and pancreas.
Sweat glands are a type of exocrine gland because they release their secretion onto the skin.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: endocrine - relating to glands that secrete hormones directly into the blood

Etymology

From Greek 'exo-' (outside) and 'krinein' (to separate).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɛk.soʊˌkraɪn / (AmE), / ˈɛk.səʊˌkraɪn / (BrE)
a gland that releases its secretions externally through a duct, rather than into the bloodstream
gland secreting through a duct
medical

Examples

The pancreas acts as an exocrine, producing digestive enzymes released into the intestine.
Sweat glands are an example of an exocrine in the human body.
Damage to an exocrine can affect the secretion of essential fluids.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: endocrine - a gland that secretes hormones directly into the bloodstream

Etymology

From Greek exo- (outside) + krinein (to separate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05336415-n
Synset POS: n
