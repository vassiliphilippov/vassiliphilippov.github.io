Ambystoma maculatum

1. NOUN

/ æmˈbɪstoʊmə mækjuˈleɪtəm / (AmE), / æmˈbɪstəmə mækjʊˈleɪtəm / (BrE)
A species of glossy black North American salamander marked with yellow spots; commonly known as the spotted salamander.
a spotted North American salamander

Examples

The Ambystoma maculatum emerges from its burrow to breed in spring ponds.
Researchers discovered a healthy population of Ambystoma maculatum in the wooded wetlands.
Because of habitat loss, the conservation status of Ambystoma maculatum has become a concern.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spotted salamander - Common name for Ambystoma maculatum, a North American salamander with yellow spots

Etymology

From Greek 'amblys' (blunt) + 'stoma' (mouth); 'maculatum' from Latin (spotted).
