to animize

1. VERB

/ ˈænɪˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈænɪˌmaɪz / (BrE)
to give lifelike or animate qualities to something; to impart the appearance or characteristics of life to
give lifelike qualities to

Examples

The artist sought to animize the statue, making it appear almost capable of movement.
Modern films often animize objects to make them more relatable to viewers.
They animized the landscape in their story, giving trees and rivers distinct personalities.

Synset Examples

animated cartoons

Related Terms

  • Synonym: animate - to give life or motion to

Etymology

From Latin anima (soul, spirit) + -ize (verb forming suffix).
