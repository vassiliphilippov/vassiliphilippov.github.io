scabiosa

1. NOUN

/ skəˈbaɪ.ə.sə / (AmE), / skəˈbɪə.sə / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Scabiosa, having showy flower heads, commonly known as pincushion flowers.
a genus of flowering plants (pincushion flowers)

Examples

The scabiosa in her garden bloomed in shades of lavender and blue.
Gardeners appreciate scabiosa for attracting bees and butterflies.
He planted several scabiosa along the walkway for their vibrant flowers.

Etymology

From Latin scabiosa, from scabies meaning 'itch', once believed to cure scabies.
