Scabiosa

1. NOUN

/ skəˈbioʊsə / (AmE), / skəˈbiːəʊsə / (BrE)
a genus of annual or perennial herbs or subshrubs, chiefly native to the Mediterranean region, with characteristic pincushion-like flowers
genus of Mediterranean flowering plants

Examples

The Scabiosa adds a delicate touch to flower arrangements with its soft petals.
Gardeners often choose Scabiosa for its long blooming season and attractive appearance.
Wild Scabiosa can be found growing along Mediterranean hillsides.

Etymology

From Latin *scabiosa* (a kind of plant), derived from *scabies* (itch); the plant was once thought to cure scabies.
