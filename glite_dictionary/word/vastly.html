vastly

1. ADV

/ ˈvæstli / (AmE), / ˈvɑːstli / (BrE)
to a very great extent or degree; immensely
to a very great degree

Examples

The price of housing has vastly increased in the last decade.
Her opinion differed vastly from his on almost every topic.
Technology has vastly improved communication around the world.

Synset Examples

He had vastly overestimated his resources
was immensely more important to the project as a scientist than as an administrator

Related Terms

  • Synonym: immensely - to a very great degree
  • Antonym: slightly - to a small extent or degree

Etymology

From the adjective 'vast' + the adverbial suffix '-ly'. 'Vast' from Latin 'vastus' (immense, great).
