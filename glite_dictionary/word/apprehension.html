apprehension

1. NOUN

/ ˌæprɪˈhɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˌæprɪˈhɛnʃən / (BrE)
A feeling of anxiety or fear that something bad or unpleasant may happen; fearful anticipation.
anxiety about future events

Examples

She waited for the results with a sense of apprehension that made it hard to sleep.
There was clear apprehension in his voice as he described the risks.
As the storm approached, the villagers watched the sky in apprehension.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anxiety - a feeling of worry or unease about something with an uncertain outcome
  • Synonym: dread - great fear or apprehension about a future event
  • Antonym: relief - a feeling of reassurance and relaxation following release from anxiety or distress

Etymology

From Latin apprehensio, from apprehendere (to seize, to grasp).

3. NOUN

/ ˌæprɪˈhɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˌap.rɪˈhɛn.ʃən / (BrE)
A feeling of fear or anxiety about something unpleasant or worrying that may happen.
fear or anxiety about the future

Examples

She approached the meeting with a sense of apprehension, unsure of how her proposal would be received.
There is growing apprehension among residents about the proposed changes to the neighborhood.
Despite her apprehension, she decided to accept the job offer and move to a new city.

Synset Examples

the student looked around the examination room with apprehension

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anxiety - a feeling of worry or unease, typically about an imminent event
  • Synonym: fear - an unpleasant emotion caused by threat of danger or pain
  • Antonym: confidence - a feeling of self-assurance; the opposite of apprehension or anxiety

Etymology

From Latin 'apprehensio' meaning 'a seizing, perception' from 'apprehendere' (to seize, take hold of).
