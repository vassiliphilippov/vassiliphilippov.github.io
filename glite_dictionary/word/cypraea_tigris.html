Cypraea tigris

1. NOUN

/ saɪˈpriːə ˈtaɪɡrɪs / (AmE), / sɪˈpriːə ˈtaɪɡrɪs / (BrE)
a large species of cowrie whose glossy, spotted shell is used for ornament and decoration
tiger cowrie species; ornamental shell

Examples

The shell of the Cypraea tigris is prized by collectors for its striking pattern.
Ancient cultures used Cypraea tigris shells as decorative ornaments and sometimes as currency.
She found a beautiful Cypraea tigris shell while snorkeling near the coral reef.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cowrie - marine mollusk with glossy, domed shell

Etymology

From Latin, where 'Cypraea' is the genus name and 'tigris' means 'tiger', referencing the spotted shell pattern.
