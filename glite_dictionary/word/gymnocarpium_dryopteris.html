Gymnocarpium dryopteris

1. NOUN

/ dʒɪm.nəˈkɑːr.pi.əm draɪˈɒp.tə.rɪs / (AmE), / dʒɪm.nəˈkɑː.pi.əm drɪˈɒp.tə.rɪs / (BrE)
a bright blue-green fern widely found in damp acidic woodlands of the temperate Northern Hemisphere
a species of blue-green fern

Examples

The woodland floor was carpeted with Gymnocarpium dryopteris thriving in the cool, damp shade.
Botanists identified the distinctive fronds as belonging to Gymnocarpium dryopteris during their field survey.
Gymnocarpium dryopteris is often favored in shaded gardens for its delicate appearance.

Etymology

From Greek 'gymnos' (naked) and 'karpos' (fruit), referring to the exposed spores; 'dryopteris' from Greek 'dryas' (oak) and 'pteris' (fern).
