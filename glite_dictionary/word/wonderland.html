wonderland

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʌndərˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈwʌndəlænd / (BrE)
a place or scene of great or strange beauty or wonder, often seeming magical or dreamlike
a magical or extraordinary place

Examples

After the snowfall, the valley was transformed into a wonderland of sparkling white.
At night, the festival lights turned the park into a glowing wonderland.
Children imagined their backyard as a secret wonderland filled with magical creatures.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paradise - an ideal or perfect place of happiness
Source: WordNet

2. NOUN

/ ˈwʌndɚˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈwʌndəlænd / (BrE)
an imaginary place full of marvels, surprises, or delightful things
imaginary land of marvels

Examples

As a child, she imagined her backyard as a magical wonderland full of hidden treasures.
The snow turned the city park into a sparkling wonderland overnight.
Alice’s journey through wonderland is one of the most beloved stories in children’s literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: utopia - an imagined perfect place or state of things
