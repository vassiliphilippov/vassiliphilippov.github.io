ortygan

1. NOUN

/ ˈɔːr.tɪ.ɡæn / (AmE), / ˈɔː.tɪ.ɡæn / (BrE)
any of several East Indian birds, especially certain species of quail or partridge.
East Indian bird, often a quail or partridge

Examples

Hunters in the region often search for the elusive ortygan during the early morning hours.
The ortygan is known for its distinct call, which can be heard across the rice fields at dusk.
Local folklore sometimes features the ortygan as a symbol of luck and abundance.
