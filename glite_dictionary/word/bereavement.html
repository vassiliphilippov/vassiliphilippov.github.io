bereavement

1. NOUN

/ bəˈrivmənt / (AmE), / bɪˈriːvmənt / (BrE)
the state of sadness or grief caused by the loss, especially the death, of a loved one
state of loss after a loved one's death

Examples

She struggled to cope with the bereavement after her husband's passing.
Counseling can help those going through bereavement find support and comfort.
The family received many messages of sympathy during their period of bereavement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grief - deep sorrow, especially caused by someone's death
  • Synonym: mourning - the expression of deep sorrow for someone who has died

Etymology

From Old English *bereafian* (to deprive, rob), related to 'bereave'.
