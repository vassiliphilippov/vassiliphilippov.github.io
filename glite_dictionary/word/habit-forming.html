habit-forming

1. ADJ

/ ˈhæb.ɪt ˌfɔr.mɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhæb.ɪt ˌfɔː.mɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a substance or behavior) likely to cause addiction or dependency through repeated use or exposure.
causing addiction or dependency

Examples

Many painkillers are considered habit-forming and should be taken only as prescribed.
Some people worry that caffeine can be habit-forming if consumed in large amounts on a daily basis.
The doctor warned him about the habit-forming potential of those sleeping pills.

Synset Examples

addictive drugs
addictive behavior

Related Terms

  • Synonym: addictive - causing or tending to cause addiction

Etymology

From 'habit' (customary practice) + 'forming' (causing to develop), referring to the development of addictive habits.

2. ADJ

/ ˈhæbɪt ˌfɔrmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhæbɪt ˌfɔːmɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a substance, activity, etc.) causing a tendency to repeat the behavior, often leading to dependence or addiction.
causing a tendency to repeat; addictive

Examples

Many prescription drugs are labeled as habit-forming and must be taken with caution.
Social media apps can be surprisingly habit-forming for people of all ages.
The doctor explained that some sleeping pills are habit-forming and should only be used temporarily.

Synset Examples

That video game is so habit-forming, I can't stop playing.
She warned me that the new series is quite habit-forming.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: addictive - causing or tending to cause addiction
