stimulation

1. NOUN

/ ˌstɪmjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌstɪmjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
something that encourages or arouses activity, interest, or enthusiasm.
something that arouses activity or interest

Examples

Daily mental stimulation is important for keeping your brain healthy.
The new project provided the stimulation he needed to stay motivated at work.
Children need a variety of sensory stimulation for proper development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: encouragement - the act of giving support or confidence
  • Antonym: inhibition - a feeling that makes one self-conscious and unable to act in a relaxed and natural way

Etymology

From Latin stimulatio, from stimulare (‘to goad, prick’).

4. NOUN

/ ˌstɪmjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌstɪmjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
The action of encouraging a physiological response in nerves, organs, or tissues by applying a stimulus.
encouragement of activity in nerves or organs
medical

Examples

Electrical stimulation of the brain can trigger muscle movement.
The doctor explained that stimulation of these nerves reduces pain.
Visual stimulation is important for early childhood development.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excitation - the process of increasing the activity of an organ or tissue
  • Antonym: inhibition - the process of restraining or suppressing activity, especially in nerves or organs

Etymology

From Latin 'stimulatio' (egging on), from 'stimulare' (to goad).
