petty

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɛti / (AmE), / ˈpɛti / (BrE)
having a lower or minor rank, power, or status; subordinate or unimportant in position.
having low rank or status

Examples

He was promoted from a petty clerk to a manager within two years.
Many petty officials wield little real power but enforce strict rules.
Being treated as a petty employee frustrated her ambitions.

Synset Examples

a lowly corporal
petty officialdom

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minor - of lesser importance, seriousness, or significance
  • Synonym: lowly - low in rank or status

Etymology

From Middle English 'pety', from Old French 'petit' (small, little).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɛti / (AmE), / ˈpɛti / (BrE)
Showing a narrow-minded or mean attitude about unimportant matters; small-minded.
narrow-minded, small-minded

Examples

She dismissed his petty little comments as not worth her time.
It's hard to work in a team divided by petty grudges and complaints.
He showed a petty attitude towards anyone who disagreed with him.

Synset Examples

petty little comments
disgusted with their small-minded pettiness

Related Terms

  • Synonym: small-minded - having a narrow or mean outlook
  • Antonym: broad-minded - having a tolerant or liberal outlook

Etymology

From Middle English *pety*, from Old French *petit* (small, little).
