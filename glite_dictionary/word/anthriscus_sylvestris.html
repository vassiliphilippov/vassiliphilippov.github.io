Anthriscus sylvestris

1. NOUN

/ ænˈθrɪskəs sɪlˈvɛstrɪs / (AmE), / ænˈθrɪskəs sɪlˈvɛstrɪs / (BrE)
a coarse, erect biennial plant native to Europe and Asia, often considered a weed in eastern North America; also known as cow parsley
biennial plant, also called cow parsley

Examples

Anthriscus sylvestris often grows along country roads and hedgerows in the spring.
Gardeners sometimes mistake Anthriscus sylvestris for similar looking plants like poison hemlock.
The rapid spread of Anthriscus sylvestris has led to concerns about its impact on local biodiversity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cow parsley - common name for Anthriscus sylvestris

Etymology

From Latin: genus name 'Anthriscus' and species epithet 'sylvestris' meaning 'of the woods'.
