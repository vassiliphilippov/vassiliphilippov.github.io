Illyrian

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɪriən / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪəriən / (BrE)
a nearly extinct Indo-European language formerly spoken along the Dalmatian coast in the western Balkans.
extinct Indo-European language of the western Balkans

Examples

During the Iron Age, the Illyrian language was widely spoken across the western Balkans.
Scholars have tried to reconstruct Illyrian from a few surviving inscriptions and place names.
The decline of the Illyrian language occurred after Roman conquest and Latinization.

Etymology

From Latin Illyrius, referring to the ancient region of Illyria.
