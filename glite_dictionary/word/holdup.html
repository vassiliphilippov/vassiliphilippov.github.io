holdup

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊld.ʌp / (AmE), / ˈhəʊld.ʌp / (BrE)
a delay or stoppage in progress; an act of causing something to be delayed
delay or stoppage

Examples

There was a minor holdup on the highway due to construction.
The project suffered a holdup when some materials failed to arrive.
Sorry for the holdup, but we needed to double-check your information.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delay - a period of time by which something is late or postponed
  • Synonym: obstruction - something that blocks or hinders progress

Etymology

From phrasal verb 'hold up' (to delay), later used as a noun.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊldˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈhəʊldʌp / (BrE)
an act of stealing from someone by using threats or violence, especially a robbery at gunpoint
armed robbery; robbery at gunpoint

Examples

The bank holdup lasted only five minutes before the police arrived.
She was traumatized after witnessing a holdup at the convenience store.
The news reported another armed holdup on Main Street last night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: robbery - the act of taking property unlawfully from a person or place by force or threat
  • Antonym: burglary - the act of entering a building illegally to commit a crime, especially theft

Etymology

From the phrasal verb 'hold up', meaning to rob, especially by threats or violence.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00783566-n
Synset POS: n
