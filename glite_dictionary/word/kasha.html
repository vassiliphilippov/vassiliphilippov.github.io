kasha

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑː.ʃə / (AmE), / ˈkæʃ.ə / (BrE)
buckwheat groats that are boiled or baked, commonly eaten as a cereal or side dish in Eastern European cuisine.
boiled or baked buckwheat groats

Examples

For breakfast, she had a hot bowl of kasha sweetened with honey.
Many traditional Russian dishes include kasha as a hearty side.
He often prepares kasha with mushrooms and onions for dinner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: buckwheat - a grain used to make kasha

Etymology

From Russian каша (kasha), meaning 'porridge, gruel'.
