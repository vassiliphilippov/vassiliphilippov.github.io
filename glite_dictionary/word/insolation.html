insolation

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.səˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.səˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the therapeutic exposure of the body to sunlight, especially for medical purposes
therapeutic exposure to sunlight

Examples

The doctor recommended daily insolation to help treat his skin condition.
Some health resorts used to offer insolation as part of their therapy programs.
Long periods of insolation can sometimes cause more harm than good if not supervised.

Etymology

From Latin 'insolatio' (exposure to the sun), from 'insolare', from 'in-' (in) + 'sol' (sun).

2. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.səˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.səˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
sudden physical collapse caused by exposure to intense sunlight or excessive heat
sudden collapse from sun or heat
medical

Examples

The hiker suffered from insolation after hours of walking under the intense summer sun.
Without adequate shade, children at the camp were at risk of insolation.
Doctors attributed several cases of fainting at the festival to insolation caused by extreme heat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sunstroke - sudden illness caused by excessive exposure to the sun

Etymology

From Latin 'insolatio', from 'insolare' (to expose to the sun).

3. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.səˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.səˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the amount of solar radiation reaching a particular area over a specific period of time
incoming solar radiation

Examples

Deserts typically receive high levels of insolation throughout the year.
Variations in insolation have a significant effect on Earth's climate.
The researchers measured the insolation at different elevations daily.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: irradiance - the amount of light energy from a source striking a surface per unit area
  • Antonym: shade - relative darkness caused by shelter from direct sunlight

Etymology

From Latin 'insolatio', from 'insolare' (to expose to the sun).
