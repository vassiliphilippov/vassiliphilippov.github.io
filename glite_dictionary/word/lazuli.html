lazuli

1. NOUN

/ ˈlæzjʊˌlaɪ / (AmE), / ˈlæzjʊli / (BrE)
An azure-blue semiprecious stone, often used in jewelry and ornamentation.
azure-blue semiprecious stone

Examples

The ancient Egyptians prized lazuli for its intense blue color.
A necklace set with lazuli stones gleamed in the display case.
Artists once ground lazuli to create vivid blue pigments for painting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lapis lazuli - bright blue metamorphic rock used as a gemstone, containing lazurite

Etymology

From Medieval Latin *lazulum*, from Arabic *lāzaward* (heaven, lapis lazuli), ultimately from Persian *lāzhward* (place name).
