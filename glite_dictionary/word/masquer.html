masquer

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæskər / (AmE), / ˈmæskə / (BrE)
A person who takes part in a masquerade, especially one wearing a mask or disguise.
participant in a masquerade

Examples

Each masquer concealed their identity behind an elaborate mask.
The ballroom was filled with every kind of masquer imaginable, from jesters to nobles.
As a seasoned masquer, she spent hours preparing her costume for the event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: masquerader - a participant in a masquerade

Etymology

From Italian 'maschera' (mask), via French 'mascarade'.
