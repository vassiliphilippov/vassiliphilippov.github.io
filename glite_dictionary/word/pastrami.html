pastrami

1. NOUN

/ pəˈstrɑː.mi / (AmE), / pəˈstrɑː.mi / (BrE)
A highly seasoned and smoked cut of beef, typically served in thin slices, often used in sandwiches.
highly seasoned smoked beef

Examples

He ordered a pastrami sandwich on rye with extra mustard.
The deli specializes in homemade pastrami that is smoked on-site daily.
Slices of pastrami were piled high on the platter at the family gathering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: corned beef - salt-cured beef, often used in sandwiches
  • Synonym: salami - a type of cured sausage made from fermented and air-dried meat

Etymology

From Yiddish *pastrame*, likely from Romanian *pastramă*, related to Turkish *pastırma* (pressed or salted meat).
