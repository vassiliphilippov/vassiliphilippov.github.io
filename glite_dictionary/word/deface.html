to deface

1. VERB

/ dɪˈfeɪs / (AmE), / dɪˈfeɪs / (BrE)
to spoil or damage the surface or appearance of something
to ruin the appearance of something

Examples

Vandals repeatedly defaced the city walls with spray paint.
Someone defaced the sign by scratching out the letters.
The ancient temple was defaced by years of neglect and weathering.

Synset Examples

scars defaced her cheeks
The vandals disfigured the statue

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to disfigure - to spoil the appearance of; to deform

Etymology

From Old French 'desfacier', from Latin 'dis-' (reversal) + 'facies' (face, appearance).
