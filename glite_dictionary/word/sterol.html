sterol

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɛrˌɑl / (AmE), / ˈstɪərɒl / (BrE)
any of a group of natural steroid alcohols occurring in plants and animals, typically waxy and insoluble, such as cholesterol
type of natural steroid alcohol

Examples

Sterol molecules like cholesterol are vital components of cell membranes.
The laboratory analysis identified plant-derived sterols in the food sample.
Some dietary supplements add sterols to help manage cholesterol levels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cholesterol - a common sterol found in animal tissues

Etymology

From 'steroid' + 'alcohol', indicating its chemical structure.
