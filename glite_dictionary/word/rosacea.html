rosacea

1. NOUN

/ roʊˈzeɪʃə / (AmE), / rəʊˈzeɪsɪə / (BrE)
A chronic skin condition, usually affecting adults, in which facial blood vessels enlarge, causing redness and sometimes pimples, especially on the central face.
chronic skin condition with facial redness
medical

Examples

The dermatologist diagnosed her persistent facial redness as rosacea.
Many people with rosacea notice their symptoms worsen in hot weather.
Treatment options for rosacea include topical creams and lifestyle changes.

Etymology

From Latin 'rosaceus' meaning 'rose-colored'.
