impersonally

1. ADV

/ ɪmˈpɜrsənəli / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɜːsənəli / (BrE)
in a way that lacks warmth, personal interest, or emotional involvement.
in an impersonal manner

Examples

The manager addressed the concerns impersonally, focusing only on the facts.
He always responds impersonally to emails, never adding any personal touch.
Feedback was given impersonally, without regard for anyone's feelings.

Synset Examples

he treated his patients impersonally

Related Terms

  • Antonym: personally - in a personal or intimate manner

2. ADV

/ ɪmˈpɝːsənəli / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɜːsənəli / (BrE)
in a way that does not involve personal feelings or individual influence; objectively or without emotional involvement.
in an objective, detached manner

Examples

The manager addressed the staff impersonally, avoiding any mention of individual concerns.
News anchors are trained to report impersonally to maintain objectivity.
She always responds impersonally to criticism, never letting it affect her mood.

Synset Examples

when I told him about Russ I found it difficult to speak impersonally

Related Terms

  • Synonym: objectively - in a way that is not influenced by personal feelings
  • Antonym: emotionally - in a way that involves strong feelings
