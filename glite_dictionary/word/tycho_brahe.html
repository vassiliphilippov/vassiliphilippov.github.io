Tycho Brahe

1. PROPN

/ ˈtaɪkoʊ ˈbrɑː(h)i / (AmE), / ˈtɪkəʊ ˈbrɑː(h)ə / (BrE)
(1546–1601) a Danish astronomer whose detailed observations of the planets laid the foundation for Kepler's laws of planetary motion.
Danish astronomer (1546–1601)

Examples

Tycho Brahe made precise astronomical observations that changed our understanding of the solar system.
In science class, we learned that Tycho Brahe greatly influenced Johannes Kepler.
The life and discoveries of Tycho Brahe are discussed in many astronomy textbooks.
