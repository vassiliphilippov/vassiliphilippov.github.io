2. NOUN

/ ˈroʊməˌni, ˈrɑməni / (AmE), / ˈrɒməni / (BrE)
a member of a traditionally itinerant people originating from northern India, now living worldwide, especially in Europe, North Africa, and North America, noted for their distinct language and culture
member of an itinerant ethnic group originally from India

Examples

The Romany have a rich oral tradition of music and storytelling.
Many Romany migrated across Europe over the centuries, adapting to different cultures.
A Romany community settled in the outskirts of the city, maintaining their distinct way of life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Gypsy - a member of a traditionally itinerant ethnic group of Indian origin, now living worldwide

Etymology

Probably from Romani 'Romany people, person', from Romani rom (man, husband).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10173755-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ ˈroʊməˌni / (AmE), / ˈrɒməni / (BrE)
the language of the Romani people, belonging to the Indo-Aryan branch of the Indo-European language family, spoken by Romani communities across Europe and beyond.
Indic language spoken by the Romani people

Examples

She is fluent in both Romany and English, thanks to her family heritage.
There are several dialects of Romany spoken throughout Europe.
The class offered an introduction to Romany grammar and vocabulary.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Romani - a member of the Romani people
  • Synonym: Rom - a Romani man

Etymology

From Romani *romani čhib* (Romani language), with 'Romani' meaning 'human, man'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06982496-n
Synset POS: n
