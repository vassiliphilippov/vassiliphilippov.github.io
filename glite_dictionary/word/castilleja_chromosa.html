Castilleja chromosa

1. NOUN

/ kæˌstɪliˈeɪ krəˈmoʊsə / (AmE), / kæˌstɪliˈeɪ krəˈməʊsə / (BrE)
a paintbrush plant native to the dry lands of the western United States, characterized by erect stems and dense spikes of bright orange to red flowers
a western US paintbrush plant species

Examples

The deserts of Utah are home to the vibrant Castilleja chromosa blooming in spring.
Hikers admired the dense red spikes of Castilleja chromosa alongside the trail.
Researchers collected samples of Castilleja chromosa to study its adaptation to arid climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: paintbrush - any plant of the genus Castilleja, with brightly colored bracts
  • Synonym: Indian paintbrush - common name for several Castilleja species

Etymology

From the genus name 'Castilleja', named after Spanish botanist Domingo Castillejo, and the species name 'chromosa', from Greek 'chroma' (color), referring to its bright flowers.
