Agkistrodon piscivorus

1. NOUN

/ æɡˌkɪstrəˌdɒn pɪˈsɪvərəs / (AmE), / æɡˌkɪstrəˌdɒn pɪˈsɪvərəs / (BrE)
A venomous semiaquatic snake native to swamps and wetlands of the southern United States, also known as the cottonmouth or water moccasin.
venomous semiaquatic snake (cottonmouth) of the southern US

Examples

Hikers in the swampy woods were warned about the presence of Agkistrodon piscivorus along the trails.
The Agkistrodon piscivorus is notorious for its potent venom and aggressive defense when threatened.
Researchers observed a juvenile Agkistrodon piscivorus basking on a fallen log near the water's edge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cottonmouth - common name for Agkistrodon piscivorus, a venomous snake
  • Synonym: water moccasin - another common name for Agkistrodon piscivorus

Etymology

From Greek agkistron (fishhook) + odon (tooth), and Latin piscivorus (fish-eating).
