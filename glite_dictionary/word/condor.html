condor

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑn.dɔr / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.dɔː / (BrE)
A very large New World vulture with a bare head and neck, found in South America and western North America.
large New World vulture

Examples

The Andean condor is one of the largest flying birds in the world.
We watched a condor soar above the mountains during our trip to Peru.
Conservation efforts have helped increase the California condor population.

Etymology

From Spanish *cóndor*, from Quechua *kuntur* (condor).
