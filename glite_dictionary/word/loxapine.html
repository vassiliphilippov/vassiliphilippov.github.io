loxapine

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɑksəˌpiːn / (AmE), / ˈlɒksəpiːn / (BrE)
A tranquilizer used, especially under the trade name Loxitane, to treat schizophrenia and other psychiatric disorders.
antipsychotic drug for schizophrenia
medical

Examples

The psychiatrist prescribed loxapine to help manage her symptoms of schizophrenia.
Some patients respond better to loxapine than to other antipsychotic medications.
Side effects of loxapine may include drowsiness and dry mouth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: antipsychotic - a drug used to treat psychotic disorders
