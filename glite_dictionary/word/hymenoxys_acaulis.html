Hymenoxys acaulis

1. NOUN

/ haɪməˌnɒksɪs əˈkɔlɪs / (AmE), / hɪməˌnɒksɪs əˈkɔːlɪs / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to west central North America, with tufted basal leaves and short, leafless stalks each bearing a solitary yellow flower head.
a North American perennial plant with yellow flower heads

Examples

Hymenoxys acaulis thrives on dry hillsides and open plains throughout the western United States.
The bright yellow flower heads of Hymenoxys acaulis stand out against its tufted green leaves.
Botanists surveyed the natural habitat of Hymenoxys acaulis to study its ecological adaptations.

Etymology

From the genus name Hymenoxys (Greek hymen 'membrane' + oxys 'sharp') and species name acaulis (Latin for 'stemless').
