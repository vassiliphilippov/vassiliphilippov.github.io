gambler

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡæmblər / (AmE), / ˈɡamblə / (BrE)
A person who risks money or valuables on the outcome of a game, race, or other event, hoping to win more.
person who bets for gain or excitement

Examples

The gambler placed all his chips on a single number, hoping for a big win.
She was known as a fearless gambler in high-stakes poker games.
Even the best gambler can lose everything with one bad decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bettor - a person who bets, especially professionally
  • Synonym: risk-taker - a person who takes risks; someone willing to gamble
  • Antonym: non-gambler - someone who does not gamble

Etymology

From the verb 'gamble,' probably an alteration of 'game' + '-le.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡæmblər / (AmE), / ˈɡæmblə / (BrE)
a person who risks money on games of chance or sporting events, hoping to win more money
person who bets money on games or events

Examples

The gambler placed a large bet on the outcome of the final match.
Many gamblers believe in lucky streaks, even when the odds are against them.
He became a gambler after visiting the casino with his friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bettor - someone who bets money, especially on sports or games
  • Synonym: player - person participating in a game (can overlap with gambler)
  • Synonym: risk-taker - person who takes risks (broader, but related)
  • Antonym: teetotaler - person who abstains from alcohol (often an antonym in gambling culture)

Etymology

From 'gamble' (to play games of chance for money) + -er (agent noun suffix).
