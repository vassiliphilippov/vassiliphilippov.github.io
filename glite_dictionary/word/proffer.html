proffer

1. VERB

/ ˈprɑːfər / (AmE), / ˈprɒfə / (BrE)
to hold out or offer something to someone for acceptance or rejection
formally offer for acceptance or rejection

Examples

He proffered his resignation to the board, hoping for understanding.
She proffered a handkerchief to her crying friend.
The committee proffers several solutions to address the issue.

Synset Examples

She offered us all a cold drink

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offer - to present or give something for acceptance, refusal, or consideration

Etymology

From Old French 'profre', from Latin 'proferre' (to bring forward).

WordNet ID: oewn-02302103-v

2. NOUN

/ ˈprɑːfər / (AmE), / ˈprɒfə / (BrE)
an offer or proposal presented for acceptance or rejection
an offer or proposal

Examples

The company's proffer of assistance was appreciated by the community.
She considered his proffer carefully before making a decision.
Their generous proffer was accepted without hesitation.

Synset Examples

it was a suggestion we couldn't refuse

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offer - an act of presenting something for acceptance
  • Synonym: proposal - a suggestion or plan put forward for consideration
  • Antonym: refusal - the act of declining or rejecting something offered

Etymology

From Old French profrer, from Latin proferre (to bring forth).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07177331-n
Synset POS: n
