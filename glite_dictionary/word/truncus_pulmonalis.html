truncus pulmonalis

1. NOUN

/ ˈtrʌŋ.kəs ˌpʊl.məˈneɪ.lɪs / (AmE), / ˈtrʌŋ.kʊs ˌpʊl.məˈnaː.lɪs / (BrE)
the large artery that carries deoxygenated blood from the right ventricle of the heart to the lungs, dividing into the right and left pulmonary arteries
main artery from heart to lungs
medical

Examples

The truncus pulmonalis branches into the right and left pulmonary arteries to supply blood to both lungs.
During the operation, the surgeon carefully isolated the truncus pulmonalis before proceeding.
Blockage in the truncus pulmonalis can lead to serious complications affecting lung function.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pulmonary artery - either of the arteries that carry blood from the heart to the lungs

Etymology

From Latin 'truncus pulmonalis', meaning 'pulmonary trunk'.
