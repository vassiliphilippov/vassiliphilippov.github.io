ejective

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈdʒɛktɪv / (AmE), / ɪˈdʒɛktɪv / (BrE)
A consonant sound produced by suddenly releasing air compressed at the glottis, common in some languages.
type of consonant sound

Examples

Linguists have identified several ejective sounds in the Caucasian language family.
The presence of a ejective in a word can make pronunciation challenging for English speakers.
Some African languages make frequent use of ejective consonants in their phonology.

Synset Examples

The vast majority of ejective consonants noted in the world's languages consists of stops or affricates, and all ejective consonants are obstruents.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: implosive - a consonant produced with an inward airstream, opposite to ejective

Etymology

From Latin 'ejectivus', from 'ejectus' (thrown out).
