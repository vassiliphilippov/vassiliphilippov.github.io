bonxie

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɑŋksi / (AmE), / ˈbɒŋksi / (BrE)
a large brown seabird (the great skua), found in northern Europe, especially around the Shetland Islands
large seabird; the great skua

Examples

The bonxie soared over the waves, scanning the surface for prey.
Tourists to Shetland are often thrilled to spot a bonxie during bird-watching tours.
A pair of bonxies fiercely defended their nest on the moorland.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skua - a group of predatory seabirds closely related to the bonxie

Etymology

From Shetland dialect, probably of Norse origin.
