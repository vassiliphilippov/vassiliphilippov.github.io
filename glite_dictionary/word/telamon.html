telamon

1. NOUN

/ ˈtɛləmɒn / (AmE), / ˈtɛləmɒn / (BrE)
An architectural figure in the form of a man, used as a supporting column or pillar.
male figure used as a supporting column

Examples

The ornate telamon at the entrance held up the building’s balcony.
Many ancient structures feature a telamon as a prominent decorative support.
Visitors admired the detailed carving on each telamon in the hall.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: caryatid - a sculpted female figure serving as an architectural support
  • Synonym: atlas - a male figure used as a column or support in architecture

Etymology

From Greek *Telamōn* (a mythological hero), referring to strength, from Greek architecture.
