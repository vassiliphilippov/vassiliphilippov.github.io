loneliness

1. NOUN

/ ˈloʊnli.nəs / (AmE), / ˈləʊnli.nəs / (BrE)
a tendency to seek solitude or prefer being alone
disposition toward being alone

Examples

She always showed a natural loneliness, preferring quiet corners to crowded rooms.
Despite his loneliness, he felt comfortable and at peace by himself.
His loneliness was not sadness, but rather a strong preference for solitude.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solitude - the state of being alone, often by choice
  • Antonym: gregariousness - a tendency to seek the company of others

Etymology

From 'lonely' + '-ness', with 'lonely' deriving from 'alone'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈloʊnli.nəs / (AmE), / ˈləʊnli.nɪs / (BrE)
The state of being alone and feeling sad or isolated as a result.
condition of feeling alone and isolated

Examples

He experienced profound loneliness after moving to a new city.
Many elderly people struggle with loneliness during the holidays.
The novel explores themes of loneliness and human connection.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solitude - the state of being alone, especially when this is peaceful or pleasant
  • Antonym: companionship - the state of being with someone; company

Etymology

From 'lonely' + '-ness'; 'lonely' derives from 'alone'.

3. NOUN

/ ˈloʊnli.nəs / (AmE), / ˈləʊn.li.nəs / (BrE)
A feeling of sadness because one has no friends or company, or feels abandoned.
feeling of sadness from being alone

Examples

After moving to a new city, she struggled with a deep sense of loneliness.
The sudden loneliness following his friend's departure was hard to bear.
For many elderly people, loneliness can be as debilitating as a physical illness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solitude - the state of being alone, often by choice
  • Antonym: company - the state of being with others

Etymology

From 'lonely' (alone, solitary) + -ness (state or quality).
