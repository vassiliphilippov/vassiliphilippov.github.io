untucked

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈtʌkt / (AmE), / ʌnˈtʌkt / (BrE)
not tucked in or lacking tucks, especially referring to the state of clothing or fabric
not tucked in (e.g., shirt, sheet)

Examples

His shirt remained untucked even during the important meeting.
The nurse noticed the patient's blanket had become untucked while he slept.
I prefer to wear T-shirts untucked for a more relaxed look.

Synset Examples

the sheet came untucked
plain untucked shirt front

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tucked - folded or inserted to hold in place, especially clothing or fabric

Etymology

Formed by prefixing 'un-' (not) to 'tucked' (past participle of 'tuck').
