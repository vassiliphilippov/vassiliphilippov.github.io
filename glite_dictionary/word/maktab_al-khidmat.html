Maktab al-Khidmat

1. NOUN

/ ˈmæk.təb æl xɪdˈmæt / (AmE), / ˈmæktəb æl xɪdˈmæt / (BrE)
A militant organization founded by Osama bin Laden in the 1980s to finance and recruit fighters for the Afghan conflict against the Soviet Union.
terrorist/militant organization founded by bin Laden

Examples

Maktab al-Khidmat played a crucial role in organizing foreign fighters during the Soviet-Afghan war.
The creation of Maktab al-Khidmat marked the beginning of Osama bin Laden's involvement in global militancy.
Many recruits passed through Maktab al-Khidmat before its transformation into more radical organizations.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: al-Qaeda - a militant Islamist organization formed by Osama bin Laden

Etymology

From Arabic مكتب الخدمات‎ (maktab al-khidmat), meaning 'Services Office.'
