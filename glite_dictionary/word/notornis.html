notornis

1. NOUN

/ noʊˈtɔrnɪs / (AmE), / nəʊˈtɔːnɪs / (BrE)
a large, rare flightless bird of New Zealand, resembling a gallinule, also known as the takahe
flightless New Zealand bird, takahe

Examples

The discovery of the notornis in the 20th century astonished ornithologists worldwide.
A group of conservationists is working to protect the habitat of the endangered notornis.
Visitors to New Zealand's Fiordland sometimes hope to catch a glimpse of the elusive notornis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: takahe - a large, flightless bird native to New Zealand

Etymology

From Greek 'notos' (south) + 'ornis' (bird), referring to its southern habitat.
