Notornis

1. NOUN

/ noʊˈtɔrnɪs / (AmE), / nəʊˈtɔːnɪs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the rail family (Rallidae), often associated with the South Island takahē of New Zealand.
genus of birds in the rail family

Examples

The rare South Island takahē belongs to the genus Notornis.
Scientists debated whether the extinct bird found was truly a member of Notornis.
The characteristics of Notornis set it apart from other genera in the rail family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Porphyrio - a genus of birds closely related to Notornis in the rail family

Etymology

From Greek notos (south) + ornis (bird).
