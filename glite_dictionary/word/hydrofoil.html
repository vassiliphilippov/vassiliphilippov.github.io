hydrofoil

1. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪdroʊˌfɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdrəʊˌfɔɪl / (BrE)
a structure shaped like a flat or curved plate that lifts a boat or similar object as it moves through water, reducing drag and allowing higher speeds
a lifting surface for vessels in water

Examples

The new ferry is equipped with a hydrofoil that enables it to glide smoothly above the waves.
Fish fins can act as natural hydrofoils to help them maneuver quickly in the water.
Engineers tested a redesigned hydrofoil to improve the boat's speed and efficiency.

Synset Examples

the fins of a fish act as hydrofoils

Related Terms

  • Synonym: airfoil - a structure shaped to produce lift when moving through air

Etymology

From Greek 'hydro-' (water) and 'foil' (thin sheet of metal)

2. NOUN

/ ˈhaɪ.droʊˌfɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ.drəʊˌfɔɪl / (BrE)
A fast boat fitted with winglike structures (hydrofoils) beneath the hull, which lift the boat above the water's surface at speed, reducing drag.
a speedboat with lifting underwater wings

Examples

The hydrofoil accelerated smoothly as it rose above the surface of the lake.
We took a hydrofoil from the mainland to the island, cutting the travel time in half.
The innovative design of a hydrofoil allows boats to travel much faster than traditional vessels.

Synset Examples

the museum houses a replica of the jet hydroplane that broke the record

Etymology

From hydro- (relating to water) + foil (a thin flat strip or structure).
