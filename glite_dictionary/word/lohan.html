lohan

1. NOUN

/ loʊˈhɑːn / (AmE), / ləʊˈhɑːn / (BrE)
In Buddhism, a person who has attained nirvana and is regarded as worthy of respect; an arhat.
a Buddhist who has attained nirvana

Examples

The temple was adorned with statues of lohans in various poses of meditation.
She studied the life of the lohan who achieved nirvana through years of devotion.
Stories of the lohan inspire many Buddhists to pursue spiritual awakening.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: arhat - a person who has attained enlightenment in Buddhism

Etymology

From Chinese 羅漢 (luóhàn), a shortened form of 羅漢那 (luóhànnà), from Sanskrit arhat (worthy).
