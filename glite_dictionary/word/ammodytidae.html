Ammodytidae

1. NOUN

/ ˌæməˈdɪtəˌdi / (AmE), / ˌæməˈdɪtɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of slender, elongated fish known as sand lances, found in marine environments and notable for their burrowing behavior.
family of fish known as sand lances

Examples

The Ammodytidae are an important food source for many seabirds and marine predators.
Researchers observed the Ammodytidae quickly burrowing into the sandy seabed at low tide.
Members of the Ammodytidae family are often called sand eels, though they are not true eels.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sand lance - a small, slender marine fish noted for burrowing in sand

Etymology

From Greek 'ammos' (sand) + 'dytes' (diver), referring to their sand-burrowing habit.

2. NOUN

/ ˌæməˈdaɪtɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌaməˈdɪtɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of slender, elongated marine fishes known as sand lances, often found buried in sandy seabeds.
family of sand lances (marine fishes)

Examples

The Ammodytidae are known for their habit of burrowing into sandy sea floors to avoid predators.
Many seabirds rely on members of the Ammodytidae family as a primary food source.
Researchers observed a sudden decline in local Ammodytidae populations due to changes in water temperature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sand lance - A slender, sand-burrowing marine fish

Etymology

From Greek 'ammos' (sand) + 'dytes' (diver), referring to their burrowing behavior.
