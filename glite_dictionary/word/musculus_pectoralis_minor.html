musculus pectoralis minor

1. NOUN

/ ˈmʌs.kjə.ləs ˌpɛk.təˈreɪ.lɪs ˈmaɪ.nər / (AmE), / ˈmʌs.kjʊ.ləs ˌpɛk.təˈreɪ.lɪs ˈmaɪ.nə / (BrE)
A thin, triangular muscle of the upper chest beneath the pectoralis major, which draws down the scapula or raises the ribs.
muscle beneath pectoralis major in chest
medical

Examples

The musculus pectoralis minor helps to stabilize the scapula during arm movements.
Tightness in the musculus pectoralis minor can contribute to poor posture and shoulder pain.
The surgeon carefully avoided damaging the musculus pectoralis minor during the procedure.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: pectoralis major - the larger chest muscle located above the pectoralis minor

Etymology

From Latin: 'musculus' (muscle), 'pectoralis' (of the chest), 'minor' (smaller).
