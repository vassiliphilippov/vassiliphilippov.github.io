Vietnam

1. PROPN

/ viˈɛtˌnɑm / (AmE), / viːˈɛt.næm / (BrE)
A country in Southeast Asia, located on the eastern coast of the Indochina Peninsula, officially known as the Socialist Republic of Vietnam.
country in Southeast Asia

Examples

She traveled across Vietnam to learn about its culture and cuisine.
Vietnam has a long coastline along the South China Sea.
After years of colonization, Vietnam gained independence from France in 1945.

Etymology

From Vietnamese 'Việt Nam', meaning 'Viet of the South'.

2. NOUN

/ viˈɛtnɑm, ˌviətˈnɑm / (AmE), / viˈɛtnæm, ˌviətˈnæm / (BrE)
the prolonged conflict from 1954 to 1975 between North Vietnam (supported by China and other communist allies) and South Vietnam (supported by the United States and other non-communist allies), commonly known as the Vietnam War.
war in Vietnam (1954–1975); the Vietnam War

Examples

Many young Americans protested against the Vietnam war during the late 1960s.
The legacy of Vietnam has influenced U.S. foreign policy for decades.
His father served in Vietnam and rarely spoke about his experiences.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Vietnam War - The conflict in Vietnam from 1954 to 1975 between communist and non-communist forces

Etymology

From Vietnamese 'Việt Nam', meaning 'Viet South'; used internationally since the 19th century as the country's name.
