to decorticate

1. VERB

/ dɪˈkɔrtɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈkɔːtɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to remove the outer layer or covering from something, especially bark from a tree or outer tissue from an organ
remove the outer layer

Examples

Foresters often decorticate branches before using them in craftwork.
In surgery, doctors can decorticate an organ to remove diseased tissue.
To make the wood usable, craftsmen must first decorticate the logs.

Synset Examples

decorticate a tree branch

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peel - to remove the outer covering or skin from something

Etymology

From Latin decorticare, from de- (off) + cortex (bark, rind).

2. VERB

/ dɪˈkɔrtɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈkɔːtɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to remove the outer layer (cortex) from an organ or other structure, especially in medical or biological contexts
remove the outer layer of an organ
medical

Examples

The surgeon needed to decorticate the kidney before proceeding with the next step of the operation.
Researchers decorticated several brain samples to study the underlying neural pathways.
After infection, it may be necessary to decorticate the affected lung lobe to prevent further complications.

Etymology

From Latin de- (removal) + cortex (bark).
