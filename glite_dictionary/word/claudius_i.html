Claudius I

1. PROPN

/ ˈklɔːdiəs ðə ˈfɜrst / (AmE), / ˈklɔːdɪəs ðə ˈfɜːst / (BrE)
(10 BC–AD 54) Roman Emperor from AD 41 to AD 54, known for consolidating the Roman Empire and leading the conquest of southern Britain.
Roman Emperor, ruled AD 41–54

Examples

Claudius I became Roman Emperor after the assassination of Caligula in AD 41.
Under Claudius I, the Romans launched a successful invasion of Britain.
The reign of Claudius I is remembered for significant expansion and infrastructure projects in the empire.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: Caligula - Roman Emperor before Claudius I, assassinated in AD 41
  • Degree Higher: Nero - Roman Emperor after Claudius I, his adopted son

Etymology

Latin Claudius, a Roman family name.
