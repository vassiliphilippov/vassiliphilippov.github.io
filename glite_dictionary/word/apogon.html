Apogon

1. NOUN

/ ˈæpəˌɡɑn / (AmE), / ˈæpəʊɡɒn / (BrE)
A genus of small marine fishes in the family Apogonidae, commonly known as cardinalfishes.
type genus of cardinalfishes

Examples

The Apogon species are commonly found in coral reefs throughout the Indo-Pacific region.
Researchers discovered a new Apogon species off the coast of Papua New Guinea.
Many Apogon fish are known for their distinctive bright coloration and nocturnal habits.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: Apogonidae - the family of cardinalfishes to which Apogon belongs

Etymology

From Greek apogon (offspringless), referring to the type species lacking certain fin rays.
