vermiculated

1. ADJ

/ vərˈmɪkjəˌleɪtɪd / (AmE), / vəˈmɪkjʊleɪtɪd / (BrE)
decorated with or marked by wormlike, irregular lines or patterns, especially as seen in architectural stonework
decorated with wormlike markings

Examples

The ancient temple featured vermiculated stone panels that caught the sunlight at different angles.
Many Renaissance buildings are adorned with vermiculated rustication on their lower levels.
The artist used a special tool to create a vermiculated texture on the clay vase.

Synset Examples

vermicular (or vermiculated) stonework

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vermicular - having the form or appearance of a worm

Etymology

From Latin vermiculatus, from vermiculus (little worm), diminutive of vermis (worm).
