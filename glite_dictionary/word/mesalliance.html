mesalliance

1. NOUN

/ ˌmeɪsəˈlaɪəns / (AmE), / ˌmeɪsˈaljɑːns / (BrE)
A marriage with a person considered to be of lower social status or standing.
marriage to a lower social status

Examples

Her family's disapproval stemmed from what they saw as a mesalliance with someone far below their station.
The scandal surrounding their mesalliance dominated conversations in high society for months.
He faced ridicule for his mesalliance, but he insisted that love mattered more than class.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: alliance - a union or association formed for mutual benefit, especially between countries or organizations

Etymology

From French mésalliance, from mes- (mis-, wrong) + alliance (alliance, marriage).
