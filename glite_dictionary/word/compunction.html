compunction

1. NOUN

/ kəmˈpʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / kəmˈpʌŋkʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
a feeling of guilt or moral scruple that prevents or follows the doing of something bad
a feeling of guilt or remorse

Examples

She felt a sudden compunction after speaking harshly to her friend.
He donated anonymously, hoping it would ease his compunction about the incident.
Without any compunction, the thief took the money and fled.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: remorse - deep regret or guilt for a wrong committed
  • Synonym: regret - a feeling of sadness or disappointment over something that has happened

Etymology

From Latin 'compunctio' (pricking, remorse), from 'compungere' (to prick hard, sting).
