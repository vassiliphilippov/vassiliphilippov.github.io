stillroom

1. NOUN

/ ˈstɪlˌruːm / (AmE), / ˈstɪl.ruːm / (BrE)
a room in a large house, especially in the past, used for preparing drinks, preserves, and for storing liquors and tea
room for preparing drinks in a large house

Examples

The housekeeper oversaw the stillroom, ensuring the teas and preserves were perfectly prepared.
The old manor's stillroom was filled with jars of homemade jam and bottles of cordial.
Tours of the estate include a visit to the restored stillroom where staff once prepared drinks for the family.

Etymology

From 'still' (distill) + 'room', originally referring to a place for distilling.
