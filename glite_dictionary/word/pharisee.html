Pharisee

1. NOUN

/ ˈfær.əˌsi / (AmE), / ˈfær.ɪ.si / (BrE)
a member of a Jewish sect active in the time of Jesus, known for strict observance of traditional Jewish law and customs
member of ancient Jewish sect

Examples

In ancient times, a Pharisee was recognized for his devotion to religious laws.
The New Testament often describes the interactions between Jesus and the Pharisees.
Even today, the term Pharisee sometimes describes someone overly concerned with rules.

Etymology

From Latin Pharisaeus, from Greek Pharisaios, from Aramaic Pĕrīshayyā (separated ones).

2. NOUN

/ ˈfærəˌsi / (AmE), / ˈfærəsi / (BrE)
A person who is hypocritically self-righteous or judges others harshly, often regarding moral or religious matters.
person who is self-righteous or hypocritical

Examples

After his lecture, some students whispered that he sounded like a true Pharisee in his harsh criticism.
Don't be such a Pharisee; try to show more understanding towards others' mistakes.
Her tendency to judge others harshly made her seem like a modern-day Pharisee.

Synset Examples

He acted like a Pharisee, judging others harshly.
In discussions, she often sounded like a Pharisee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypocrite - a person who pretends to have moral standards or beliefs to which their own behavior does not conform
  • Antonym: saint - a person regarded as virtuous or holy (antonym in context of moral judgment)

Etymology

From Late Latin 'Pharisaeus', from Greek 'Pharisaios', from Aramaic 'perīšayyā', meaning 'separated ones'.
