Chloranthus

1. NOUN

/ kloʊˈrænθəs / (AmE), / kləʊˈrænθəs / (BrE)
A genus of perennial herbaceous plants and shrubs, type genus of the family Chloranthaceae, native to Asia and the Pacific islands.
genus of plants in Chloranthaceae

Examples

The genus Chloranthus is known for its simple, fragrant flowers.
Several species of Chloranthus are used in traditional medicine in East Asia.
Botanists have classified Chloranthus among primitive angiosperms due to its unique features.

Etymology

From Greek 'chloros' (green) and 'anthos' (flower).

2. NOUN

/ kloʊˈɾænθəs / (AmE), / klɒˈɹænθəs / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants in the family Chloranthaceae, native to Asia and known for their simple, aromatic foliage and small greenish flowers.
a genus of Asian aromatic plants

Examples

The Chloranthus genus includes several herbaceous species native to East Asia.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Chloranthus genus in the mountains of Taiwan.
Gardeners appreciate Chloranthus for its fragrant leaves and subtle green flowers.

Etymology

From Greek *chloros* (green) + *anthos* (flower).
