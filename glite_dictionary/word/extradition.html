extradition

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.strəˈdɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌɛk.strəˈdɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
the official process by which one country or state surrenders a person accused or convicted of a crime to another country or state where the crime was committed or where the prosecution is sought
legal surrender of a person to another country or state
legal

Examples

The government approved the extradition of the suspect to face charges in another country.
After months of legal proceedings, his extradition was finally ordered by the court.
International treaties often govern the process of extradition between nations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rendition - the surrender or handing over of a person, similar to extradition but sometimes with less formal or legal process

Etymology

From French 'extradition', from Latin 'ex-' (out) + 'traditio' (a handing over).
