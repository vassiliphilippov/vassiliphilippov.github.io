educated

1. ADJ

/ ˈɛdʒəˌkeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈɛdjʊkeɪtɪd / (BrE)
Having received a good education; possessing a high level of knowledge, especially more than average.
having received an education

Examples

She is an educated woman who speaks four languages fluently.
Many educated people support lifelong learning and skill development.
He comes from a highly educated family with a tradition in academia.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: uneducated - lacking an education or schooling
  • Synonym: learned - having much knowledge acquired by study

Etymology

Past participle of the verb 'educate,' from Latin 'educare' (to bring up, rear, educate).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00833284-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˈɛdʒəˌkeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈɛdjuːkeɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a guess, opinion, or decision) based on knowledge and understanding of the facts involved, rather than just intuition.
based on knowledge or full understanding

Examples

She made an educated guess about the winner based on recent statistics.
It is better to make an educated decision than to rely on assumptions.
His educated opinion helped the team develop the right approach.

Synset Examples

an educated guess
an enlightened electorate

Related Terms

  • Synonym: informed - (of a decision, opinion, etc.) based on information and understanding
  • Degree Lower: guess - an estimate made without sufficient information

Etymology

From Latin educatus, past participle of educare (to educate, bring up).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01310330-s
Synset POS: s
