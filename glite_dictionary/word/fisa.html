FISA

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪsə / (AmE), / ˈfaɪsə / (BrE)
an abbreviation for the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act, a United States law passed in 1978 to set procedures for surveillance of foreign intelligence activities and to establish the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Court
US law for foreign intelligence surveillance procedures
legal american

Examples

The government must obtain a FISA warrant before conducting certain types of foreign intelligence surveillance.
Discussions about FISA have increased since new concerns about domestic spying were raised.
The court established under FISA operates largely in secret to protect national security interests.

Etymology

Acronym of Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act (1978), United States.
