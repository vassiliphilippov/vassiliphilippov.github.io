hill

1. NOUN

/ hɪl / (AmE), / hɪl / (BrE)
In baseball, the slight elevation on the field where the pitcher stands to throw the ball.
slightly raised pitcher's area in baseball

Examples

The veteran walked slowly to the hill, ready to pitch the final inning.
After the rain delay, the grounds crew quickly worked to dry the hill.
Coaches often visit the hill to give advice to struggling pitchers.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pitcher’s mound - the elevated area from which the pitcher delivers the ball in baseball
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03797867-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ hɪl / (AmE), / hɪl / (BrE)
A naturally raised area of land, not as high or craggy as a mountain.
naturally raised area of land

Examples

She hiked up the hill to get a better view of the city.
A small white cottage sat at the foot of the hill.
Children love to roll down the grassy hill in the park.

Synset Examples

they loved to roam the hills of West Virginia

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: mountain - a large natural elevation of the earth's surface
  • Degree Lower: mound - a small natural or artificial hill
  • Synonym: knoll - a small, rounded hill
  • Antonym: valley - a low area of land between hills or mountains

Etymology

Old English *hyll*, related to Dutch *hille* and German *Hügel*.

3. VERB

/ hɪl / (AmE), / hɪl / (BrE)
to form or heap up earth or other material into a mound, especially around the base of a plant.
form into a mound (esp. in gardening)

Examples

Farmers often hill soil around potato plants to protect the tubers.
It is important to hill your corn as it grows for better support.
After planting, the gardener hilled the earth around the seedlings.

4. NOUN

/ hɪl / (AmE), / hɪl / (BrE)
An artificial heap or bank of earth or stones, especially one built as a structure or barrier.
artificial mound of earth or stones

Examples

Archaeologists discovered an ancient ceremonial hill constructed from carefully placed stones.
The soldiers took cover behind a hill built to shield them from enemy fire.
A small hill marked the burial site in the old battlefield.

Synset Examples

they built small mounds to hide behind

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mound - a natural or artificial elevation of earth or stones

Etymology

Old English *hyll* (hill, natural or artificial elevation).
