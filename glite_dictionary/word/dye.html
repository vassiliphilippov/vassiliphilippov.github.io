dye

1. VERB

/ daɪ / (AmE), / daɪ / (BrE)
to change the color of something by using a special substance
to color with a dye

Examples

She decided to dye her hair bright red for the summer.
You can dye old shirts to give them a fresh look.
The fabric must be thoroughly cleaned before you dye it.

Synset Examples

Please dye these shoes

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bleach - to remove color from something, especially by chemical means

Etymology

Old English *dēag* (dye, color), related to Old Norse *deygja* (to dye).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00283850-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ daɪ / (AmE), / daɪ / (BrE)
a substance used to change the color of something, such as fabric, hair, or other materials, by soaking or applying it
substance for coloring fabrics or hair

Examples

She used a bright red dye to color her hair.
The fabric was soaked in blue dye overnight.
Natural dye from plants is often used in traditional crafts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stain - a substance used to add color to something, often for marking or coloring materials
  • Antonym: bleach - a substance used to remove color from something

Etymology

From Old English *deāg* (dye), related to Old Norse *deyja* (to dye).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-15009942-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ daɪ / (AmE), / daɪ / (BrE)
to apply a coloring substance to a specimen, especially for microscopic study
apply color to a specimen for study

Examples

The technician dyed the cell sample to make the nuclei more visible under the microscope.
Histologists commonly dye tissue slices before examining them for abnormalities.
Special solutions are used to dye bacteria so that their structures can be observed clearly.

Synset Examples

The laboratory worker dyed the specimen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stain - to apply a special dye to a specimen for microscopic examination

Etymology

From Old English 'deġan' (to dye) related to Old Norse 'deyja'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00284175-v
Synset POS: v
