dissatisfaction

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsˌsætɪsˈfækʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪsˌsætɪsˈfækʃən / (BrE)
a feeling of being unhappy or displeased with something; lack of satisfaction
lack of satisfaction; discontent

Examples

The decision was met with widespread dissatisfaction among the employees.
Her dissatisfaction grew as the project faced repeated delays and obstacles.
Customers expressed dissatisfaction about the quality of the food served at the restaurant.

Synset Examples

he was never slow to express his dissatisfaction with the service he received

Related Terms

  • Antonym: satisfaction - a feeling of pleasure when a need or desire is fulfilled
  • Synonym: discontent - lack of contentment; dissatisfaction

Etymology

From dis- (expressing reversal) + satisfaction, modeled after Late Latin *dissatisfactio*.
