dissolvent

1. NOUN

/ dɪˈzɑlvənt / (AmE), / dɪˈzɒlvənt / (BrE)
a liquid substance used to dissolve other substances, especially in chemical processes; a solvent
liquid that dissolves other substances

Examples

The laboratory technician poured a small amount of dissolvent into the beaker to dissolve the powdered compound.
Acetone is frequently used as a dissolvent in industrial cleaning processes.
Because the paint was stubborn, they recommended a stronger dissolvent to remove it completely.

Synset Examples

the solvent does not change its state in forming a solution

Related Terms

  • Synonym: solvent - a substance that dissolves another to form a solution

Etymology

From Latin dissolvent- (loosening), from dissolvere (to loosen, dissolve).
