uxoricide

1. NOUN

/ ʌkˈsɔrɪˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ʌkˈsɒrɪˌsaɪd / (BrE)
A husband who murders his wife.
a man who kills his wife

Examples

The detective suspected the man was an uxoricide after finding evidence at the crime scene.
Many novels explore the motives behind an uxoricide, delving into the psychology of such a crime.
The notorious uxoricide was sentenced to life in prison after the trial.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: mariticide - a wife who murders her husband

Etymology

From Latin 'uxor' (wife) + '-cide' (killing).
