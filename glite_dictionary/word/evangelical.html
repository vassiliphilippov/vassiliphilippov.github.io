evangelical

1. ADJ

/ ˌiːvænˈdʒɛlɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌiːvænˈdʒelɪkəl / (BrE)
Relating to the Christian gospel, especially as expressed in the first four books of the New Testament.
relating to the Christian gospel

Examples

The evangelical message emphasizes personal faith and the authority of scripture.
Many early Protestant movements were deeply evangelical in character.
His evangelical beliefs shaped his approach to community service.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gospel - the teachings of Christianity, especially those found in the first four books of the New Testament

Etymology

From Medieval Latin evangelicus, from Greek euangelion meaning 'good news, gospel'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02934127-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ ˌiːˌvænˈdʒɛlɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌiːˌvænˈdʒɛlɪkəl / (BrE)
showing passionate or zealous enthusiasm for a particular cause or belief, often with a missionary fervor.
ardently enthusiastic about a cause

Examples

Her evangelical commitment to environmental protection inspired everyone around her.
He spoke in evangelical tones about the need for change within the organization.
The volunteers approached the project with an almost evangelical zeal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: zealous - having or showing great energy or enthusiasm in pursuit of a cause
  • Synonym: passionate - showing or caused by strong feelings or beliefs

Etymology

From Greek *euangelikos* (of the gospel), via Latin *evangelicus*.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00889973-s
Synset POS: s

3. ADJ

/ ˌivænˈdʒɛlɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌiːvanˈdʒɛlɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to a branch of the Christian church that emphasizes personal conversion, the authority of the Bible, and the importance of sharing the Christian message.
of a Christian movement stressing conversion and the Bible

Examples

Many evangelical Christians believe in spreading their faith through missionary work.
The politician addressed a largely evangelical audience during his campaign.
Her views are influenced by her evangelical upbringing and church community.

Synset Examples

evangelical Christianity
an ultraconservative evangelical message

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fundamentalist - strict adherence to the basic principles of a religion, especially Christianity
  • Antonym: liberal - in Christian context, referring to progressive or less literal interpretations of scripture

Etymology

From Medieval Latin evangelicus, from Greek euangelion (good news, gospel).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02934318-a
Synset POS: a
