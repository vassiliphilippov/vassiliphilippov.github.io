drone

1. NOUN

/ droʊn / (AmE), / drəʊn / (BrE)
a pipe on a bagpipe that produces a single continuous tone
pipe of a bagpipe producing a continuous tone

Examples

The musician skillfully tuned the drone of his bagpipe before the performance.
A deep hum came from the drone while the melody was played on the chanter.
The bagpipe's drone creates a haunting background note that defines its sound.

2. NOUN

/ droʊn / (AmE), / drəʊn / (BrE)
a male bee in a colony, especially honeybees, whose only function is to mate with the queen and which does not gather nectar or pollen
male bee whose function is to mate with the queen

Examples

The drone spends its life inside the hive until it mates with a queen.
After mating, the drone dies, completing its only purpose in the colony.
Unlike the worker bee, a drone does not gather pollen or nectar.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: worker - female bee in a colony that does not mate but gathers nectar and pollen
  • Antonym: queen bee - fertile female bee and the mother of most bees in the colony

Etymology

From Old English drān (male bee), related to Middle Low German drōne and Old High German treno.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02209831-n
Synset POS: n

3. NOUN

/ droʊn / (AmE), / drəʊn / (BrE)
a person who is habitually slow, lazy, or takes longer than necessary to do something; someone who lags behind
person who is slow or lags behind

Examples

The new manager quickly grew frustrated with the office drone who never met his deadlines.
She hated being labeled a drone just because she preferred to work at a slower pace.
Among the eager volunteers, there was always at least one drone dragging their feet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laggard - someone who makes slow progress and falls behind others
  • Synonym: sluggard - a person who is habitually lazy or slow
  • Antonym: go-getter - an energetic, ambitious person who is determined to succeed

Etymology

From Old English *drān* (male bee, idler).

5. NOUN

/ droʊn / (AmE), / drəʊn / (BrE)
a continuous, low, monotonous sound or tone that does not change in pitch
continuous, unchanging sound or tone

Examples

The drone of the engine made it hard to concentrate during the lecture.
Her voice settled into an unvaried drone that lulled the audience to sleep.
The bagpipes produce a constant drone beneath the melody.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monotone - a continuing sound, especially of someone's voice, that is unchanging in pitch
  • Synonym: hum - a low, steady continuous sound

Etymology

From Old English 'drān' (male bee), later extended to refer to monotonous sounds.

to make a continuous, low, dull, monotonous sound
make a dull monotonous sound

Examples

7. VERB

/ droʊn / (AmE), / drəʊn / (BrE)
to speak in a slow, dull, monotonous tone, often at length
to speak in a monotonous tone

Examples

The professor tended to drone on during his lectures, making it hard for students to stay awake.
He droned through the entire speech, barely changing his tone.
As the meeting dragged on, she found herself tuning out her boss, who continued to drone about company policy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: monotone - a continuing sound, especially of a person's voice, that is unchanging in pitch
  • Synonym: to intone - to say or recite with little rise and fall of the pitch of the voice

Etymology

From Old English drān, drǣn (male bee), later extended metaphorically to dull sound or tone.
