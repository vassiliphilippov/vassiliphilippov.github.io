Bassia scoparia

1. NOUN

/ ˈbæsiə skoʊˈpɛriə / (AmE), / ˈbæsɪə skəʊˈpɑːrɪə / (BrE)
A Eurasian annual plant with densely branched stems and foliage that turns purple-red in autumn, also known as burning bush or summer cypress.
Eurasian annual plant; red autumn foliage

Examples

The gardens were lined with vibrant Bassia scoparia, their leaves a brilliant purple-red in autumn.
Some landscapers prefer Bassia scoparia for its striking color and dense foliage.
In autumn, the Bassia scoparia plants in the park attract photographers seeking seasonal beauty.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: summer cypress - Common name for Bassia scoparia
  • Synonym: burning bush - Another common name for Bassia scoparia

Etymology

From New Latin; 'Bassia' after Italian physician and botanist Ferdinando Bassi, and 'scoparia' from Latin 'scoparius' (broom-like, referring to the shape).
