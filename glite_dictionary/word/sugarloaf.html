sugarloaf

1. NOUN

/ ˈsʌɡərˌloʊf / (AmE), / ˈsʌɡəˌləʊf / (BrE)
a large cone-shaped mass of refined sugar, formerly the standard form in which sugar was produced and sold
a large conical loaf of sugar

Examples

In the eighteenth century, families would buy a sugarloaf and break it apart at home.
A sugarloaf was often chipped into pieces using special tongs.
He displayed an old sugarloaf in his collection of kitchen artifacts.

Etymology

From 'sugar' + 'loaf'; originally referring to the molded mass of sugar produced in this shape.
