trichrome

1. ADJ

/ ˈtraɪˌkroʊm / (AmE), / ˈtrʌɪkrəʊm / (BrE)
Having or involving three colors.
having or involving three colors

Examples

The histologist used a trichrome stain to differentiate between muscle and connective tissue.
Modern printers can reproduce rich images thanks to trichrome color processes.
The butterfly's wings displayed a beautiful trichrome pattern of red, yellow, and blue.

Synset Examples

trichromatic vision
a trichromatic printing process
trichromatic staining is the staining of tissue samples differentially in three colors
tricolor plumage
a tricolor process in photography

Related Terms

  • Antonym: monochrome - having or involving only one color
  • Synonym: trichromatic - having or involving three colors or three color receptors

Etymology

From Greek 'tri-' (three) and 'chroma' (color).
