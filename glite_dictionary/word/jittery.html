jittery

1. ADJ

/ ˈdʒɪtɚi / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪtəri / (BrE)
characterized by quick, jerky, or nervous movements; prone to tremors or restlessness
showing quick, jerky, or nervous movements

Examples

After drinking too much coffee, I felt jittery all morning.
The car took a jittery path along the rocky road.
Her jittery hands made it hard to write a straight line.

Synset Examples

a jittery ride

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nervous - easily agitated or alarmed; tending to be anxious
  • Antonym: calm - not showing nervousness or anxious movements

Etymology

From 'jitter', possibly of imitative origin, meaning a feeling of nervousness, + -y.

2. ADJ

/ ˈdʒɪtəri / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪtəri / (BrE)
feeling nervous, anxious, or unable to relax; easily startled or tense
nervous or tense

Examples

I always feel jittery before giving a big presentation.
Too much coffee in the morning makes her jittery and restless.
He became jittery when he heard footsteps behind him late at night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nervous - easily agitated or alarmed; anxious or apprehensive
  • Antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other strong emotions

Etymology

From the noun 'jitters' (nervousness), possibly of imitative origin (imitating a shaking or trembling sound), with the suffix '-y'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02415166-s
Synset POS: s
