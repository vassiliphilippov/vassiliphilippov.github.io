Ophisaurus

1. NOUN

/ oʊˈfɪsɔːrəs / (AmE), / ˌɒfɪˈsɔːrəs / (BrE)
A genus of legless lizards, commonly known as glass lizards, that superficially resemble snakes but are actually lizards.
a genus of legless lizards (glass lizards)

Examples

The Ophisaurus is often mistaken for a snake because of its long, limbless body.
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the Ophisaurus genus in Southeast Asia.
Unlike true snakes, Ophisaurus retain remnants of ear openings and eyelids.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glass lizard - a common name for lizards of the genus Ophisaurus
  • Degree Higher: lizard - a type of reptile with scaly skin, typically with legs and external ears
  • Degree Higher: snake - a legless reptile, often confused with glass lizards due to similar appearance

Etymology

From Greek 'ophis' (snake) and 'sauros' (lizard), referring to their snake-like appearance.

2. NOUN

/ ˌoʊfɪˈsɔrəs / (AmE), / ˌɒfɪˈsɔːrəs / (BrE)
A genus of legless lizards commonly known as glass lizards, found in North America, Europe, and Asia.
a genus of legless lizards (glass lizards)

Examples

The Ophisaurus can shed its tail to escape predators.
Unlike snakes, Ophisaurus species have movable eyelids and external ear openings.
Researchers discovered a new Ophisaurus species in Southeast Asia.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glass lizard - a legless lizard of the genus Ophisaurus

Etymology

From Greek 'ophis' (snake) + 'sauros' (lizard), reflecting their snake-like appearance.
