paddy

1. NOUN

/ ˈpædi / (AmE), / ˈpædi / (BrE)
A field, usually flooded, where rice is grown.
flooded field for growing rice

Examples

The farmers worked all day in the paddy under the hot sun.
After the monsoon, the paddy fields were lush and green.
Her family owns several paddy fields in the countryside.

Etymology

From Malay *padi* (rice plant, rice in the husk).

2. NOUN

/ ˈpædi / (AmE), / ˈpædi / (BrE)
rice in the husk, either still growing in the field or harvested but not yet milled
rice in the husk

Examples

The farmers harvested the paddy fields just before the rainy season began.
You can see paddy drying in the sun along the village roads.
Freshly threshed paddy is stored in large sacks to await milling.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rice - the edible starchy cereal grain of an Asian grass

Etymology

From Malay *padi* (rice in the husk).
