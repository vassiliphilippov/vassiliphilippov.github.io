demarche

1. NOUN

/ dəˈmɑrʃ / (AmE), / deɪˈmɑːʃ / (BrE)
an action, step, or maneuver made in political or diplomatic affairs, often as an official representation or protest
diplomatic or political maneuver

Examples

The ambassador delivered a formal demarche to the foreign ministry regarding the proposed policy changes.
A diplomatic demarche was issued in response to the rising border tensions.
Government officials discussed how best to coordinate their demarche with allied nations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: move - an action taken to achieve an aim, especially in politics or games
  • Synonym: initiative - a new plan or process to achieve something or solve a problem, especially in politics or diplomacy

Etymology

From French démarche (a step, gait; maneuver), from démarcher (to walk, to move)
