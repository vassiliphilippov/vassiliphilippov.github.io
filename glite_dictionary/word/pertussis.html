pertussis

1. NOUN

/ pərˈtʌsɪs / (AmE), / pəˈtʌsɪs / (BrE)
a highly contagious infectious disease of the respiratory tract, especially affecting children, characterized by severe coughing fits; also known as whooping cough
infectious respiratory disease (whooping cough)
medical

Examples

The doctor diagnosed the child's persistent cough as pertussis after running several tests.
Vaccination is the most effective way to prevent pertussis outbreaks in the community.
Despite being vaccinated, she contracted a mild case of pertussis due to a recent exposure.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: whooping cough - another name for pertussis

Etymology

From Latin 'pertussis', from 'per-' (thoroughly) + 'tussis' (cough).
