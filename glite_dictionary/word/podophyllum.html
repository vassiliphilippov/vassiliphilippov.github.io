Podophyllum

1. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊdəˈfɪləm / (AmE), / ˌpɒdəˈfɪləm / (BrE)
A genus of perennial herbs with rhizomes, native to North America and Asia, known for their umbrella-like leaves and medicinal properties.
a genus of perennial rhizomatous herbs

Examples

The roots of Podophyllum contain compounds used in traditional medicine.
Botanists discovered a new species within the Podophyllum genus in the Himalayas.
Podophyllum plants are recognized for their large, lobed leaves and unique fruit.

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek 'pous, podos' (foot) + 'phullon' (leaf), referring to the shape of the leaves.

2. NOUN

/ ˌpoʊdəˈfɪləm / (AmE), / ˌpɒdəˈfɪləm / (BrE)
A genus of perennial rhizomatous herbs native to eastern North America and Asia, known for their umbrella-like leaves and medicinal properties.
genus of perennial herbs (mayapples)

Examples

The Podophyllum is commonly referred to as the mayapple in North America.
Several species within the Podophyllum genus are used in traditional medicine.
Gardeners value Podophyllum for its unique foliage and ornamental appeal.

Etymology

From Greek 'pous, podos' (foot) and 'phyllon' (leaf), referring to the shape of the leaves.
