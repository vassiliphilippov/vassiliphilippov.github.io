Halesia tetraptera

1. NOUN

/ həˈliːziə ˌtɛtəpˈtɪrə / (AmE), / həˈliːzɪə ˌtɛtrəpˈtɪərə / (BrE)
A medium-sized deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States, valued for its showy white bell-shaped flowers.
a flowering tree native to southeastern US

Examples

The Halesia tetraptera is often planted in gardens for its attractive spring blossoms.
Botanists studied the fruit structure unique to Halesia tetraptera.
Several mature Halesia tetraptera line the path in the arboretum, creating a beautiful canopy.

Etymology

From Neo-Latin: genus name 'Halesia' honors English botanist Stephen Hales; species from Greek 'tetra' (four) + 'pteron' (wing), referring to the four-winged fruit.
