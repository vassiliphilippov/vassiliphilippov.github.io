Oligoporus leucospongia

1. NOUN

/ ˌɑlɪˈɡɑpɔɹəs ˌljuːkoʊˈspʌndʒiə / (AmE), / ˌɒlɪˈɡɒpərəs ˌljuːkəʊˈspʌndʒɪə / (BrE)
A species of pore fungus with a soft, whitish, cottony cap, found on conifer logs in high-elevation forests of the western United States and nearby Canada.
a species of whitish fungus found on conifer logs

Examples

The soft cap of Oligoporus leucospongia can be seen growing on fallen pine logs in alpine forests.
Scientists collected samples of Oligoporus leucospongia to study its ecological role in decay.
During their hike, they photographed clusters of Oligoporus leucospongia in the forest understory.

Etymology

From Greek 'oligos' (few), 'poros' (pore), 'leukos' (white), and 'spongia' (sponge), referring to the appearance and structure of the fungus.
