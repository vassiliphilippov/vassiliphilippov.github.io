precordium

1. NOUN

/ priˈkɔrdiəm / (AmE), / priˈkɔːdiəm / (BrE)
the area on the front of the chest overlying the heart and lower chest, especially as examined by a doctor
area of chest over the heart
medical

Examples

The doctor placed his stethoscope on the patient's precordium to listen for abnormal heart sounds.
Tenderness in the precordium may indicate an underlying cardiac or gastrointestinal issue.
Palpation of the precordium revealed a visible pulsation over the left ventricle.

Etymology

From Latin 'praecordium' (the diaphragm, the breast, or midriff).
