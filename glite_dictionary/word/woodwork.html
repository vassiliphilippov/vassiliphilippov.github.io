woodwork

1. NOUN

/ ˈwʊdˌwɜrk / (AmE), / ˈwʊdwɜːk / (BrE)
the activity or skill of making things out of wood, especially as a craft or trade
the craft of making things from wood

Examples

He took up woodwork as a hobby after retiring from teaching.
Students in the workshop learn the basics of woodwork and tool safety.
The cabin's interior featured finely crafted woodwork and custom furniture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carpentry - the activity or occupation of making or repairing things in wood
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00608890-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈwʊdˌwɝk / (AmE), / ˈwʊdˌwɜːk / (BrE)
the parts made of wood in a building or piece of furniture, especially the moldings, staircases, doors, or trim
parts made of wood in buildings or furniture

Examples

The original woodwork in the house has been beautifully restored.
He carefully polished the woodwork on the staircase until it gleamed.
The craftsmen paid special attention to the details of the woodwork in the new library.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: carpentry - the activity or skill of making things from wood

Etymology

From 'wood' + 'work', meaning work done or made from wood.
