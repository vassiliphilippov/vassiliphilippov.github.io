Microtus richardsoni

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪkroʊtəs rɪˈtʃɑrd.sə.ni / (AmE), / ˌmɪkrətəs rɪˈtʃɑːdsəni / (BrE)
a large species of vole native to western North America, also known as Richardson's vole
species of vole in western North America

Examples

The Microtus richardsoni inhabits alpine meadows across parts of the Rocky Mountains.
Researchers observed the Microtus richardsoni for signs of environmental adaptation.
Unlike smaller voles, the Microtus richardsoni can weigh up to twice as much as similar species.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: vole - a small rodent resembling a mouse or rat

Etymology

From New Latin, where 'Microtus' is the genus (from Greek 'mikros' meaning small and Latin 'otus' meaning ear), and 'richardsoni' honors naturalist Sir John Richardson.
