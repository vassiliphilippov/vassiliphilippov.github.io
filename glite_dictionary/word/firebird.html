firebird

1. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪərˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəbɜːd / (BrE)
a tropical American flycatcher, especially the adult male, with bright scarlet and black plumage, found as far north as southern Texas and Arizona
a brightly colored tropical American bird

Examples

A brilliant firebird was spotted near the riverbank, its red plumage glowing in the sunlight.
The male firebird is easily recognized by its vivid scarlet and black feathers.
During their trip to Arizona, the group observed a rare firebird perched on a tree branch.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfaɪɚˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəˌbɜːd / (BrE)
A brightly colored bird, especially a male with vivid red plumage and black wings and tail; sometimes used for the oriole or similar species.
brightly colored red and black bird

Examples

The male firebird is easy to spot thanks to its brilliant red feathers.
Legends often describe the firebird as a beautiful and elusive creature.
In the morning light, a firebird perched on the branch, its colors shimmering.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oriole - a brightly colored bird with a similar appearance
