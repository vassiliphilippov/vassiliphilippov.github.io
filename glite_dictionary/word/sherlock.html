Sherlock

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɜːrlɑk / (AmE), / ˈʃɜːlɒk / (BrE)
(informal) A detective or someone who investigates, especially used humorously or allusively; derived from the famed fictional detective Sherlock Holmes.
informal term for a detective
informal

Examples

Come on, Sherlock, tell us how you figured out who ate the last cookie.
She thinks she's a real Sherlock after guessing the surprise party.
Nice work, Sherlock—you found the missing keys under the couch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detective - a person, especially a police officer, whose occupation is to investigate and solve crimes
  • Synonym: sleuth - an informal or old-fashioned term for a detective

Etymology

From the name of Sherlock Holmes, the famous fictional detective created by Arthur Conan Doyle.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃɝːlɑk / (AmE), / ˈʃɜːlɒk / (BrE)
(informal) a person who displays keen observational or deductive skills, especially in solving mysteries or problems, often alluding to Sherlock Holmes.
person skilled in observation or deduction
informal

Examples

Don’t try to play Sherlock; we all know who broke the vase.
With his Sherlock instincts, he pieced the evidence together quickly.
The security team needs a real Sherlock to crack this case.

Synset Examples

Everyone called her the Sherlock of the group.
She approached the case with Sherlock-like intuition.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: detective - a person who investigates crimes
  • Synonym: sleuth - a person who investigates and solves mysteries

Etymology

From the fictional detective Sherlock Holmes, created by Sir Arthur Conan Doyle (1887).
