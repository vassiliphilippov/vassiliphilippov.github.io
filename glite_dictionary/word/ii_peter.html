II Peter

1. PROPN

/ ˌsɛkənd ˈpiːtər / (AmE), / ˌsɛkənd ˈpiːtə / (BrE)
the second book of the New Testament attributed to Saint Peter the Apostle, addressing Christian conduct and false teachings
New Testament book attributed to Saint Peter

Examples

Many scholars debate the authorship of II Peter in the New Testament.
In church this week, the sermon focused on a passage from II Peter.
II Peter contains warnings against false prophets and encouragement for Christian perseverance.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: I Peter - the first New Testament book attributed to Saint Peter

Etymology

From Latin 'secundus' (second) and the name 'Peter', after the Apostle Peter.
