Struthionidae

1. NOUN

/ struːˈθaɪ.ə.nɪˌdi / (AmE), / struːˈθɪə.nɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of large, flightless terrestrial birds, including the ostriches, native to Africa and formerly Asia
family of large birds including ostriches

Examples

The Struthionidae are distinguished by their long necks and powerful legs.
Fossil records indicate that Struthionidae once inhabited regions outside Africa.
The common ostrich is the only living member of the Struthionidae family.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ostrich - a large, flightless bird native to Africa

Etymology

From New Latin, formed from Greek 'strouthion' (ostrich) plus '-idae' for zoological family names.

2. NOUN

/ struːˌθiːəˈnaɪdiː / (AmE), / struːˌθiːəˈnaɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, flightless, terrestrial birds, including the ostriches.
family of ostriches

Examples

The Struthionidae are known for their powerful legs and inability to fly.
Unlike most birds, members of the Struthionidae family have only two toes on each foot.
Fossil records show that the Struthionidae once had a much wider geographic range.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ostrich - a large flightless bird native to Africa; member of Struthionidae

Etymology

From New Latin Struthionidae, based on the genus Struthio (ostrich) + -idae (family suffix in zoological taxonomy).
