crowdfunding

1. NOUN

/ ˈkraʊdˌfʌndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkraʊdˌfʌndɪŋ / (BrE)
The practice of raising small amounts of money from a large number of people, typically via the Internet, to fund a project, venture, or cause.
funding by many small contributions

Examples

The startup raised enough capital through crowdfunding to launch its first product.
Many independent films now rely on crowdfunding to cover production costs.
She turned to crowdfunding to finance her medical expenses after her insurance claim was denied.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fundraising - the process of seeking financial support for a cause or project

Etymology

From 'crowd' + 'funding', first popularized in the early 21st century with the rise of online platforms.
