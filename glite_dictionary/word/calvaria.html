calvaria

1. NOUN

/ kælˈvɛriə / (AmE), / kælˈveəriə / (BrE)
the upper dome-like part of the skull that encloses the brain
upper dome of the skull
medical

Examples

The neurosurgeon carefully drilled into the calvaria to access the patient's brain.
Fractures of the calvaria are less common than those of the facial bones.
The thickness of the calvaria varies between individuals and can be measured by CT scan.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: skull - the bony structure that forms the head in vertebrates

Etymology

From Latin 'calvaria' (skull, bald head).
