panspermia

1. NOUN

/ pænˈspɝː.mi.ə / (AmE), / pænˈspɜː.mi.ə / (BrE)
The hypothesis that life exists throughout the universe and can be spread to habitable bodies by microorganisms carried on meteoroids, asteroids, or comets.
theory that life spreads through space via microorganisms

Examples

Some scientists support panspermia as a possible explanation for the origins of life on Earth.
The discovery of complex organic molecules on comets has revived interest in the theory of panspermia.
If panspermia is true, life on Earth could have an extraterrestrial origin.

Etymology

From Greek pan- (all) + sperma (seed).
