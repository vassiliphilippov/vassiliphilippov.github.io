barony

1. NOUN

/ ˈbærəni / (AmE), / ˈbærəni / (BrE)
The estate, rank, or domain belonging to a baron.
estate or rank of a baron

Examples

The medieval barony was passed from father to son for generations.
Many Scottish families can trace their heritage to a barony established centuries ago.
After the war, the old barony was divided among several heirs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baron - a member of the lowest order of the British nobility

Etymology

From Old French baronie, from baron (baron).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɛrəni / (AmE), / ˈbærəni / (BrE)
The rank, dignity, or lands held by a baron or baroness.
rank or domain of a baron or baroness

Examples

The estate has been part of the barony for over two centuries.
She inherited the barony after her father's death.
The old barony was known for its vast forests and rich history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baron - a member of the lowest order of the British nobility
  • Synonym: peerage - the title and rank of a peer or noble

Etymology

From Old French baronie, from baron (baron).

3. NOUN

/ ˈbærəni / (AmE), / ˈbærəni / (BrE)
the land or territory held by a baron; the domain of a baron
domain of a baron

Examples

The barony was passed down through generations of the same noble family.
He inherited a vast barony along with the title of baron.
During medieval times, a barony often included several villages and farmlands.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: baron - a member of the lowest rank of the British nobility

Etymology

From Old French *baronie* (baronage, baronry), from *baron* (baron).
