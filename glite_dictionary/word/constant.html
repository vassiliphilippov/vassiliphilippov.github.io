constant

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːnstənt / (AmE), / ˈkɒnstənt / (BrE)
a quantity or number with a fixed value in a particular mathematical context
fixed value in mathematics

Examples

In the equation y = mx + b, the 'b' term is a constant.
Pi, often represented as 3.14159, is a well-known constant in mathematics.
The speed of light in a vacuum is a universal physical constant.

Synset Examples

the velocity of light is a constant

Related Terms

  • Antonym: variable - a quantity that can change in value in a mathematical context

Etymology

From Latin constans, constant- (standing firm), present participle of consistere (to stand firm).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːn.stənt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.stənt / (BrE)
a quantity or value that stays the same and does not change in a particular context, especially in mathematics or science.
unchanging value or quantity

Examples

In this equation, the value of constant C remains the same regardless of the variable x.
The speed of light is a universal constant in physics.
During the experiment, temperature was held as a constant to ensure accurate results.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: variable - a quantity that can change or vary

Etymology

From Latin 'constans' (standing firm, unchanging), present participle of 'constare' (to stand firm).

Synset POS: n
