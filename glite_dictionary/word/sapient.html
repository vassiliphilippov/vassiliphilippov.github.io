sapient

1. ADJ

/ ˈseɪ.pi.ənt / (AmE), / ˈseɪ.pi.ənt / (BrE)
having or showing great wisdom and sound judgement; acutely insightful and wise.
wise and insightful

Examples

The professor’s sapient comments impressed everyone in the seminar.
She offered sapient advice that helped her friends avoid many mistakes.
His sapient observations about human nature were ahead of his time.

Synset Examples

much too perspicacious to be taken in by such a spurious argument
observant and thoughtful, he was given to asking sagacious questions
a source of valuable insights and sapient advice to educators

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sagacious - having or showing keen mental discernment and good judgement
  • Synonym: wise - having or showing experience, knowledge, and good judgment
  • Antonym: foolish - lacking good sense or judgment

Etymology

From Latin 'sapiens', present participle of 'sapere' (to be wise, have taste).
