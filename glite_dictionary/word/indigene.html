indigene

1. NOUN

/ ˈɪndɪˌdʒin / (AmE), / ˈɪndɪˌdʒiːn / (BrE)
a person native to a particular place; an indigenous person
an indigenous person

Examples

The museum exhibit features tools used by the indigene communities before colonization.
Many indigenes continue to practice their traditional crafts and customs.
The new law aims to protect the cultural heritage of the indigene populations.

Synset Examples

the art of the natives of the northwest coast
the Canadian government scrapped plans to tax the grants to aboriginal college students

Related Terms

  • Synonym: native - a person born in a particular place; indigenous inhabitant
  • Antonym: immigrant - a person who comes to live permanently in a foreign country

Etymology

From Latin 'indigena' (native, sprung from the land).
