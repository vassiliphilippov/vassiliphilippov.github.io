club

1. VERB

/ klʌb / (AmE), / klʌb / (BrE)
to gather and spend time together, especially for social or recreational purposes
gather socially; spend time together

Examples

Every Friday night, the group would club at a local café after work.
Students often club on campus to discuss their shared interests.
In the summer, neighbors club in the park for barbecues and games.

Synset Examples

They always club together

Related Terms

  • Synonym: meet - to come together for a purpose
  • Synonym: to assemble - to gather together in one place
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02598267-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ klʌb / (AmE), / klʌb / (BrE)
to hit someone or something forcefully with a heavy stick or similar blunt object
hit with a heavy stick or bludgeon

Examples

The hunter clubbed the animal to stop it from escaping.
He clubbed the burglar with a flashlight in self-defense.
Protesters accused the police of clubbing demonstrators during the rally.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bludgeon - to hit with a heavy object or weapon

Etymology

From Old Norse 'klubba' (cudgel, club).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01426583-v
Synset POS: v
