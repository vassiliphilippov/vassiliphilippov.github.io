smarta

1. NOUN

/ ˈsmɑːrtə / (AmE), / ˈsmɑːtə / (BrE)
a member of a group of Brahmins in Hinduism who uphold nonsectarian orthodoxy according to the Vedanta school of thought
member of a Hindu orthodox Brahmin group (Vedanta)

Examples

The smarta tradition emphasizes adherence to the scriptures known as Smriti.
As a smarta, he participates in rituals honoring several Hindu deities.
The smartas play a significant role in preserving Hindu philosophical teachings.

Etymology

From Sanskrit *smārta* (relating to traditional law or custom), from *smṛti* (that which is remembered, tradition).
