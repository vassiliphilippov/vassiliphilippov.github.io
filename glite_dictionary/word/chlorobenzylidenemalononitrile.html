chlorobenzylidenemalononitrile

1. NOUN

/ ˌklɔːroʊˌbɛnˌzɪləˌdiːn məˌloʊnoʊˈnaɪtraɪl / (AmE), / ˌklɔːrəʊˌbɛnˌzɪliːdiːn məˌləʊnəˈnaɪtraɪl / (BrE)
a chemical compound used as a tear gas, stronger than CN gas, that can cause skin burns and serious pulmonary effects
a type of tear gas compound
medical

Examples

Exposure to chlorobenzylidenemalononitrile can cause intense irritation of the eyes and respiratory system.
Police used grenades containing chlorobenzylidenemalononitrile to disperse the demonstrators.
It is important to wear protective gear when handling chlorobenzylidenemalononitrile due to the risk of serious burns.

Etymology

Formed from chemical root words: 'chloro-' (chlorine), 'benzylidene' (containing a benzylidene group), and 'malononitrile' (a compound with two cyano groups).
