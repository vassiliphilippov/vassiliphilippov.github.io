to misdirect

1. VERB

/ ˌmɪs.dəˈrɛkt / (AmE), / ˌmɪs.dɪˈrɛkt / (BrE)
to send to the wrong place or person by putting an incorrect address on something
send to the wrong place by mistake

Examples

The package was misdirected and ended up across town.
Please check the address so the letter is not misdirected again.
The company accidentally misdirected several important documents last week.

Synset Examples

misdirect the letter

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to redirect - send something to a different address or recipient
  • Antonym: to deliver - bring something to the intended recipient or location

Etymology

From prefix 'mis-' (wrongly) + 'direct' (to guide or send); attested since the 17th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00993057-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˌmɪsdəˈrɛkt / (AmE), / ˌmɪsdɪˈrɛkt / (BrE)
to send someone or something in the wrong direction, or to provide misleading guidance
send or guide wrongly

Examples

The teacher accidentally misdirected the students to the wrong classroom.
Tourists were frequently misdirected by outdated online maps.
She didn't mean to misdirect you; the library entrance really did move.

Synset Examples

The pedestrian misdirected the out-of-town driver

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to misguide - to guide someone incorrectly or give faulty advice
  • Antonym: direct - to point or guide in the right direction

Etymology

From Latin prefix 'mis-' (wrongly) + direct.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02004320-v
Synset POS: v
