Lilium canadense

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɪliəm kəˈneɪdənsi / (AmE), / ˈlɪliəm kəˈnædənsɪ / (BrE)
A species of lily native to the eastern United States, known for its nodding yellow or reddish flowers with brown spots.
North American species of lily

Examples

The Lilium canadense blooms in early summer, adding vibrant color to woodland gardens.
Botanists study Lilium canadense for its unique downward-facing flowers.
She photographed a beautiful patch of Lilium canadense during her hike in the Appalachians.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lily - a plant of the genus Lilium, with large prominent flowers

Etymology

From Latin, 'Lilium' meaning 'lily' and 'canadense' meaning 'of Canada'.
