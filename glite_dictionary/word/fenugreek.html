fenugreek

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛnjəˌɡrik / (AmE), / ˈfɛnjuːɡriːk / (BrE)
A plant with aromatic seeds used especially as a seasoning in curry and other dishes.
aromatic spice seed used in curries

Examples

The recipe calls for a teaspoon of fenugreek seeds for extra flavor.
Indian cuisine often uses fenugreek in curries and stews.
Some herbal teas include fenugreek for its distinctive aroma.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cumin - aromatic spice seed used in cooking

Etymology

From Latin *fenum Graecum* (Greek hay), referring to its use as animal fodder.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɛnjəˌɡrik / (AmE), / ˈfɛnjʊˌɡriːk / (BrE)
an annual herb of southern Europe and Asia, with off-white flowers and aromatic seeds used in cooking and traditional medicine
aromatic herb with medicinal and culinary seeds

Examples

The recipe calls for a teaspoon of fenugreek seeds to add a nutty flavor.
Many herbalists recommend fenugreek for its potential health benefits.
She grows fenugreek in her garden to use in homemade curries.

Etymology

From Latin 'fenugraecum', from Greek 'phoinigrecon' meaning 'Greek hay'.
