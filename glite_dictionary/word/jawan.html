jawan

1. NOUN

/ dʒəˈwɑːn / (AmE), / dʒəˈwɑːn / (BrE)
(India) a private soldier or male constable, especially in the army or police.
private soldier or male constable (India)
british

Examples

The jawan stood guard at the entrance to the military base.
Several jawans were awarded medals for their bravery during the operation.
Families gathered to welcome the returning jawan after his deployment.

Etymology

From Hindi जवान (javān), meaning 'young man' or 'soldier'.
