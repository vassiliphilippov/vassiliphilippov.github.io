servile

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɜːrvaɪl, sərˈvaɪl / (AmE), / ˈsɜːvaɪl, ˈsɜːvɪl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of slaves, or appropriate for a servant or slave.
of or relating to slaves or servants

Examples

The rebels fought to end the servile system that kept their people in bondage.
Historic records detail several servile insurrections during the Roman era.
Laws once forbade slaves from performing any work except the most servile tasks.

Synset Examples

Brown's attempt at servile insurrection
the servile wars of Sicily
servile work

Etymology

From Latin servilis, from servus (slave).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01069915-s

2. ADJ

/ ˈsɝː.vaɪl, ˈsɝː.vəl / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.vaɪl, ˈsɜː.vɪl / (BrE)
having or showing an excessive willingness to serve or please others; overly submissive or fawning.
excessively submissive; fawning

Examples

She refused to adopt a servile attitude, even when faced with authority.
His servile flattery made him unpopular with his colleagues.
The manager demanded servile obedience from all her employees.

Synset Examples

spoke in a servile tone
the incurably servile housekeeper
servile tasks such as floor scrubbing and barn work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: submissive - willing to submit to others; meek
  • Synonym: obsequious - obedient or attentive to an excessive degree
  • Antonym: assertive - having a confident and forceful personality

Etymology

From Latin servilis (of a slave), from servus (slave).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00793641-a
Synset POS: a
