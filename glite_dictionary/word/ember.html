ember

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈɛmbə / (BrE)
a small piece of wood or coal that remains after a fire and glows with heat without flames
a glowing piece from a dying fire

Examples

She watched the last ember of the campfire fade in the cool night air.
A single ember landed on the rug, threatening to start a fire.
They sat quietly, warming their hands by the glowing embers in the fireplace.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cinder - a small piece of partly burned coal or wood without flame but with residual heat

Etymology

From Old English *æmerge*, related to Old Norse *eimyrja* (a hot coal).
