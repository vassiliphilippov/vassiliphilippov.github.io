Carpodacus mexicanus

1. NOUN

/ kar-poʊˈdækəs ˌmɛk.sɪˈkæn.əs / (AmE), / kaː.pəʊˈdæk.əs ˌmek.sɪˈkeɪ.nəs / (BrE)
A small finch native to western North America and Mexico, commonly known as the house finch.
species of small finch (house finch)

Examples

A flock of Carpodacus mexicanus gathered at the feeder outside the window.
The range of Carpodacus mexicanus has expanded to cover much of the United States.
Researchers observed the nesting habits of Carpodacus mexicanus during the spring season.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: house finch - common name for Carpodacus mexicanus

Etymology

From New Latin, with 'Carpodacus' from Greek 'karpos' (fruit) + 'dakno' (to bite), and 'mexicanus' referring to Mexico.
