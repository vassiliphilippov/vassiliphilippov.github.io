cacique

1. NOUN

/ kəˈsiːk / (AmE), / kəˈsiːk / (BrE)
A tropical American bird of the oriole family, typically with black and red or black and yellow plumage.
a tropical American oriole with black and red or yellow color

Examples

The striking black-and-yellow cacique perched high in the rainforest canopy.
Birdwatchers traveled to South America to spot a rare cacique among the flocks.
Researchers studied the nesting habits of the caciques in the Amazon basin.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: oriole - A colorful passerine bird in the family Icteridae

Etymology

From Spanish 'cacique', originally meaning 'native chief', later applied to these birds.
