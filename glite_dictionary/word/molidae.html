Molidae

1. NOUN

/ ˈmoʊlɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈmɒlɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of large, laterally flattened marine fishes known as ocean sunfishes, notable for their unusual shape and size.
family of ocean sunfishes

Examples

The family Molidae includes some of the heaviest bony fishes in the world.
Several species within Molidae display unusual swimming behavior.
Research on Molidae has provided insight into the evolution of specialized marine fish.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ocean sunfish - a large, flattened fish of the family Molidae

Etymology

From Modern Latin, based on the type genus 'Mola'.
