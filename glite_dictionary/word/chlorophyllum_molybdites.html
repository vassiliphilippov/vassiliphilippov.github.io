Chlorophyllum molybdites

1. NOUN

/ kloʊˈrɒfɪləm ˌmɑːlɪbˈdaɪtiːz / (AmE), / klɒˈrɒfɪləm ˌmɒlɪbˈdaɪtiːz / (BrE)
a poisonous mushroom with a large white cap covered in brown scales, commonly found in lawns and known to form 'fairy rings'; causes gastrointestinal distress if ingested
a poisonous mushroom species

Examples

Many cases of mushroom poisoning in North America are due to accidental consumption of Chlorophyllum molybdites.
The Chlorophyllum molybdites can sometimes be mistaken for edible mushrooms by amateur foragers.
After eating Chlorophyllum molybdites, he had to seek medical attention due to severe stomach cramps.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: false parasol - common name for Chlorophyllum molybdites

Etymology

Genus name from Greek 'chloros' (green) + 'phyllon' (leaf); species name from molybdenum, referencing its color.
