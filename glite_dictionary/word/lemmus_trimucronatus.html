Lemmus trimucronatus

1. NOUN

/ ˈlɛməs ˌtraɪmjuːˈkroʊnətəs / (AmE), / ˈlɛməs ˌtrɪmjuːˈkrɒnətəs / (BrE)
a species of lemming found in northwestern Canada and Alaska, also known as the northwestern lemming.
a lemming species of northwestern Canada and Alaska

Examples

The Lemmus trimucronatus is well adapted to the harsh climate of northern Alaska.
Researchers observed the burrowing habits of Lemmus trimucronatus during the Arctic summer.
Populations of Lemmus trimucronatus fluctuate greatly depending on predation and food availability.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lemming - a small, short-tailed rodent of Arctic tundra

Etymology

From Latin: Lemmus, the genus name; trimucronatus, meaning 'three-pointed' or 'three-keeled'.
