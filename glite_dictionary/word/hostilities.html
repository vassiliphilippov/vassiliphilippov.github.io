hostilities

1. NOUN

/ həˈstɪl.ə.tiz / (AmE), / hɒˈstɪl.ɪ.tiz / (BrE)
acts of warfare or fighting between opposing forces, especially during a conflict or war
acts of warfare; fighting

Examples

After years of tension, hostilities finally erupted between the two neighboring countries.
The treaty marked the official end of all hostilities in the region.
International observers arrived shortly after the outbreak of hostilities to monitor the situation.

Synset Examples

the outbreak of hostilities

Related Terms

  • Synonym: warfare - military conflict between groups
  • Antonym: peace - absence of war or conflict

Etymology

From Middle French hostilité, from Latin hostilitas (enmity, animosity) from hostis (enemy).
