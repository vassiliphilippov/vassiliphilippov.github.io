Sitotroga cerealella

1. NOUN

/ saɪ.təˈtroʊ.ɡə ˌsɪə.riˈæl.ə / (AmE), / sɪ.təˈtrəʊ.ɡə ˌsɪə.riˈæl.ə / (BrE)
A small moth whose larvae feed on kernels of stored grains, causing damage to cereal crops; also known as the Angoumois grain moth.
moth that infests stored grains

Examples

The infestation of Sitotroga cerealella can result in significant losses of stored wheat.
Farmers must inspect their grain regularly to detect the presence of Sitotroga cerealella.
Research has focused on natural predators that can control Sitotroga cerealella populations in silos.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Angoumois grain moth - common name for Sitotroga cerealella

Etymology

From Modern Latin, combining 'Sitotroga' (genus) and 'cerealella' (relating to cereals).
