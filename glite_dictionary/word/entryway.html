entryway

1. NOUN

/ ˈɛn.triˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈɛn.tri.weɪ / (BrE)
a passage, doorway, or hall providing access to a building, room, or other area
a passage or doorway providing access

Examples

She hung her coat on a hook just inside the entryway.
The grand entryway was lined with marble floors and tall pillars.
Please remove your shoes in the entryway before entering the house.

Synset Examples

they waited at the entrance to the garden
beggars waited just outside the entryway to the cathedral

Related Terms

  • Synonym: entrance - an opening or passage for entering a place
  • Antonym: exit - a way out; an opening for leaving a place

Etymology

From 'entry' + 'way', formed in English.
