Dacrydium bidwilli

1. NOUN

/ ˌdækɹiˈdiːəm bɪdˈwɪli / (AmE), / ˌdakɹɪˈdiːʊm bɪdˈwɪli / (BrE)
a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand, belonging to the podocarp family and notable for its needle-like leaves and compact form
New Zealand shrub or small tree

Examples

The Dacrydium bidwilli thrives in the damp, cool forests of New Zealand's North Island.
Botanists are studying how Dacrydium bidwilli contributes to local biodiversity.
Gardeners appreciate Dacrydium bidwilli for its distinctive foliage and resilience in various soils.

Etymology

From genus name Dacrydium (Greek: ‘tear’ + ‘form’), and named after botanist John Carne Bidwill.
