appendicularia

1. NOUN

/ əˌpɛndɪkjəˈlɪriə / (AmE), / əˌpɛndɪkjʊˈlɪəriə / (BrE)
a small, free-swimming, tadpole-shaped marine tunicate that lives in open water and resembles the larvae of other tunicates
tadpole-shaped pelagic tunicate
medical

Examples

Scientists observed that appendicularia filter small particles from the seawater using their mucous houses.
The unique body shape of appendicularia distinguishes them from other planktonic organisms in the ocean.
Marine biologists study appendicularia to understand the early evolution of tunicates and chordates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: larvacean - another name for appendicularia, a group of free-swimming tunicates

Etymology

Modern Latin, from Latin 'appendicula' (small appendage) + '-aria'
