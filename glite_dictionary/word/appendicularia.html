Appendicularia

1. NOUN

/ əˌpɛndɪkjəˈlɪəriə / (AmE), / əˌpɛndɪkjʊˈlɪərɪə / (BrE)
A genus of small, free-swimming marine animals in the class Larvacea, belonging to the phylum Chordata.
genus of small marine chordates

Examples

The genus Appendicularia is notable for its members' transparent, tadpole-like appearance.
Scientists have discovered new species within the Appendicularia genus along the Pacific coast.
Members of Appendicularia play a significant role in oceanic food webs.

Etymology

From Latin 'appendicula' (a little appendage) + '-aria', used in zoological naming.
