improperly

1. ADV

/ ɪmˈprɑːpɚli / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɒpəli / (BrE)
In a way that is not proper, correct, or suitable; inappropriately or incorrectly.
in an unsuitable or incorrect way

Examples

The report was rejected because it was improperly formatted.
If the machine is assembled improperly, it could be dangerous to use.
The witness claimed the evidence had been improperly handled by the police.

Synset Examples

he checked whether the wound had healed improperly

Related Terms

  • Antonym: properly - in a proper or correct manner
  • Synonym: incorrectly - in a way that is not correct

Etymology

From 'improper' (from Latin improprius: not proper) + '-ly'.
