tarp

1. NOUN

/ tɑrp / (AmE), / tɑːp / (BrE)
a large sheet of strong, flexible, waterproof material, often made of canvas or plastic, used to cover and protect things from rain or weather
waterproof sheet for covering

Examples

The campers used a tarp to keep their firewood dry overnight.
We covered the equipment with a tarp before the storm began.
A blue tarp flapped in the wind, shielding the tent from rain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tarpaulin - a large, heavy waterproof cloth used as covering

Etymology

Shortened form of 'tarpaulin', originally from tar and 'pall' (a cloth covering).
