wizardry

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɪzərdri / (AmE), / ˈwɪzədri / (BrE)
exceptional skill or creative ability, especially in a particular activity or field
exceptional creative skill or ability

Examples

Her wizardry with numbers makes her an invaluable asset to the company.
The chef’s culinary wizardry transformed simple ingredients into a gourmet meal.
Fans marveled at the technical wizardry displayed in the new computer game.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: genius - exceptional intellectual or creative power or ability
  • Synonym: mastery - comprehensive knowledge or skill in a subject or activity

Etymology

From Middle English 'wysard' (wizard) + '-ry', meaning the practice or condition of being a wizard.
