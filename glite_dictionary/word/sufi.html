Sufi

1. NOUN

/ ˈsuːfi / (AmE), / ˈsuːfi / (BrE)
A Muslim who follows Sufism, the mystical tradition within Islam focused on seeking direct experience of God.
Muslim follower of Islamic mysticism (Sufism)

Examples

The Sufi poet Rumi is renowned worldwide for his spiritual writings.
Many Sufi practices emphasize music and dance as forms of devotion.
She studied the history of Sufi orders in Iran and Turkey.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sufism - the mystical tradition of Islam

Etymology

From Arabic ṣūfī, meaning 'one who wears wool,' referring to the simple woolen garments of early Islamic mystics.

2. ADJ

/ ˈsuːfi / (AmE), / ˈsuːfi / (BrE)
relating to Sufism or to the Sufis, the mystical tradition within Islam
relating to Sufism or Sufis

Examples

The poet is famous for his Sufi verses, which explore themes of love and divinity.
She wore traditional clothing at the Sufi festival held in the city.
Many scholars have studied the profound influence of Sufi philosophy on literature.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Sufism - the mystical Islamic belief system and practice

Etymology

From Arabic ṣūfī, meaning a member of a mystical Islamic order.
