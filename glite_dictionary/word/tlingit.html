Tlingit

1. NOUN

/ ˈklɪŋɪt, ˈtlɪŋɪt / (AmE), / ˈklɪŋɪt, ˈtlɪŋɪt / (BrE)
A member of an Indigenous people living in southern Alaska, known for their seafaring traditions.
member of an Indigenous people of southern Alaska

Examples

The Tlingit have a rich tradition of carving totem poles and canoes.
A group of Tlingit elders gathered to share their stories with the younger generation.
Many Tlingit continue to practice traditional fishing methods on the Alaskan coast.

Etymology

From the Tlingit language *Łingít*, meaning 'person'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈtlɪŋɡɪt / (AmE), / ˈtlɪŋɡɪt / (BrE)
the language of the Tlingit people, belonging to the Na-Dene language family, spoken in southeastern Alaska and western Canada
Na-Dene language of the Tlingit people

Examples

She studied the endangered Tlingit language at university.
Many elders still speak Tlingit fluently in their daily lives.
A new dictionary was published to help preserve Tlingit for future generations.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Tlingit - a member of the indigenous people of southeastern Alaska and western Canada

Etymology

From the Tlingit word *Lingít*, meaning 'person' or 'people'.
