brim

1. NOUN

/ brɪm / (AmE), / brɪm / (BrE)
the top edge of a vessel or other container
top edge of a container

Examples

Tears filled her eyes to the brim, but she didn’t let them fall.
He poured the wine up to the brim of the glass.
The pot was full to the brim with soup.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rim - the outer, often curved or raised edge of something

Etymology

From Old English *brymme* (edge, border, margin).

2. NOUN

/ brɪm / (AmE), / brɪm / (BrE)
the projecting edge around the bottom of the crown of a hat
edge at the base of a hat's crown

Examples

He adjusted the brim of his hat to shade his eyes from the sun.
The wide brim of her summer hat kept her cool on the beach.
Raindrops collected on the brim of his old felt hat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brim - the upper edge of a vessel or other container

Etymology

From Old English *brymme* (edge, margin).

3. VERB

/ brɪm / (AmE), / brɪm / (BrE)
(of a container or a person's eyes) to be full to the point of overflowing or to be filled with a particular quality or emotion.
to be full to the point of overflowing

Examples

Her heart brimmed with joy at the sight of her family.
The glass brimmed with water, ready to spill at any moment.
His eyes brimmed with tears as he listened to the moving story.

Synset Examples

His eyes brimmed with tears

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overflow - to flow over the edge
  • Synonym: spill - to come out of a container

4. VERB

/ brɪm / (AmE), / brɪm / (BrE)
to fill something to the very top; to make full to the point of overflowing
fill to the top

Examples

She brimmed the glass with sparkling water for her guest.
The river threatened to brim over its banks after the heavy rains.
He brimmed the jug until the milk nearly spilled.

Synset Examples

brim a cup to good fellowship

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overflow - to be so full that the contents spill over
  • Antonym: empty - to remove contents from; not full

Etymology

From Middle English 'brym' (edge, margin), related to the brim of a vessel.
