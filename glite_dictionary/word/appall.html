to appall

1. VERB

/ əˈpɔl / (AmE), / əˈpɔːl / (BrE)
to fill someone with shock, fear, or strong dismay; to horrify or deeply disturb
to shock or horrify

Examples

The cruelty of the experiment appalled the observers.
It appalls me to think people could behave like that.
Many were appalled by the amount of litter left after the concert.

Synset Examples

I was horrified at the thought of being late for my interview
The news of the executions horrified us

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to horrify - to shock or disgust someone deeply
  • Antonym: delight - to give great pleasure or joy to someone

Etymology

From Old French 'appaler', from Latin 'ad-' (to) + 'pallere' (to grow pale).

2. VERB

/ əˈpɔl / (AmE), / əˈpɔːl / (BrE)
to shock or horrify someone by something unpleasant or offensive
to shock or horrify

Examples

The level of violence in the movie appalled some viewers.
It appalls me that people can treat animals so cruelly.
Her lack of empathy truly appalled everyone at the meeting.

Synset Examples

The scandalous behavior of this married woman shocked her friends

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shock - to surprise and horrify someone; similar meaning
  • Synonym: to horrify - to cause someone to feel horror; similar meaning

Etymology

From Old French appalir, from a- (to) + palir (grow pale).

Synset POS: v
