hastate

1. ADJ

/ ˈhæˌsteɪt / (AmE), / ˈhas.teɪt / (BrE)
(of a leaf or other structure) shaped like a spearhead, with two pointed lobes spreading outward at the base.
spearhead-shaped, with spreading lobes (botanical)

Examples

The wildflower is easily identified by its hastate leaves, which resemble tiny spearheads.
Botanists noted the hastate shape of the lower leaves on the specimen.
Some arrowhead plants display hastate foliage, with lobes extending outward from the base.

Etymology

From Latin 'hastatus', from 'hasta' meaning 'spear'.
