keeper

1. NOUN

/ ˈkiːpɚ / (AmE), / ˈkiːpə / (BrE)
a person responsible for taking care of buildings, grounds, animals, or objects
person responsible for care or supervision

Examples

The lion keeper feeds and looks after the big cats every morning.
Each building has its own keeper responsible for maintenance and security.
She worked for years as a keeper at the city zoo.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caretaker - a person employed to look after a building or animals
  • Synonym: warden - a person responsible for the supervision of a particular place or group

Etymology

From Middle English keper, from Old English cēapere (one who keeps or guards).

2. NOUN

/ ˈkiːpər / (AmE), / ˈkiːpə / (BrE)
a person who is responsible for or looks after another person or group of people
person in charge of others

Examples

Many religions teach that you are your brother's keeper and should help others in need.
As the team keeper, she was trusted to guide new members and ensure everyone worked together.
He felt like a keeper to his younger siblings after their parents passed away.

Synset Examples

am I my brother's keeper?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: guardian - a person who protects or cares for someone or something
  • Synonym: caretaker - a person employed to look after people or property

Etymology

From Old English *ceapere* (one who insists on something, guardian), related to 'keep.'

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
