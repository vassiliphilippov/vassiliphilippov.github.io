small-capitalisation

1. ADJ

/ smɔl ˌkæpɪtəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / smɔːl ˌkæpɪtəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
(of a company or its shares) having a market capitalization of less than one billion dollars, typically considered small relative to others in the market.
having a small market capitalization (under $1 billion)

Examples

Investors seeking higher growth prospects often prefer small-capitalisation stocks.
The fund focuses primarily on small-capitalisation companies in emerging markets.
He shifted his portfolio from large companies to small-capitalisation firms for greater diversification.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: large-capitalisation - having a large market capitalization (usually over $10 billion)
  • Degree Higher: mid-capitalisation - having a medium market capitalization (typically $1-$10 billion)
