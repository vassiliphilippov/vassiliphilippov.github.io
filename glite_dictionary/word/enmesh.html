to enmesh

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈmɛʃ / (AmE), / ɪnˈmɛʃ / (BrE)
to entangle or catch someone or something in a net or a complicated situation
to entangle in a net or complicated situation

Examples

The legal system can sometimes enmesh innocent people in lengthy trials.
She found herself enmeshed in a web of lies and deceit.
Debris enmeshed the boat’s propeller, forcing the crew to stop for repairs.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to entangle - to twist together or catch in a snarl or entwining mass

Etymology

From en- (to put into) + mesh (net) in late Middle English.
