motherly

1. ADV

/ ˈmʌðərli / (AmE), / ˈmʌðəli / (BrE)
In a way that is typical of or appropriate to a caring mother; in a maternal manner.
in a caring, maternal way

Examples

She comforted the children motherly during the storm.
He spoke to his team motherly, offering warmth and guidance.
The nurse cared for her patients motherly, showing great compassion.

Synset Examples

she loved her students almost maternally

Related Terms

  • Synonym: maternally - in a maternal or motherly manner

Etymology

From 'mother' + '-ly', meaning 'in the manner of a mother.'

2. ADJ

/ ˈmʌðɚli / (AmE), / ˈmʌðəli / (BrE)
Having qualities traditionally regarded as characteristic of a mother, such as warmth, affection, and nurturing.
warm and nurturing, like a mother

Examples

She has a motherly way of comforting anyone who is upset.
His teacher gave him a motherly hug after he won the prize.
Maria's motherly concern was evident when she stayed up all night with her sick child.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: paternal - having qualities typical of a father
  • Synonym: maternal - relating to or characteristic of a mother

Etymology

From Middle English 'moderly', from 'moder' (mother) + '-ly' (having the qualities of).

Synset POS: s
