Peromyscus maniculatus

1. NOUN

/ pɛrəˈmaɪskəs ˌmænɪˈkjuːlətəs / (AmE), / pɛrəˈmɪskəs ˌmanɪˈkjuːlatəs / (BrE)
A small brownish mouse native to North America, commonly known as the deer mouse, noted for its wide distribution.
a North American deer mouse species

Examples

The Peromyscus maniculatus is often used in scientific research due to its adaptability.
We spotted a Peromyscus maniculatus scurrying across the forest floor at dusk.
Unlike house mice, Peromyscus maniculatus prefers wooded and grassy habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: deer mouse - common name for Peromyscus maniculatus

Etymology

From Latin: genus 'Peromyscus' (pouch mouse) + species 'maniculatus' (having little hands).
