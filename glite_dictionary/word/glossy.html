glossy

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡlɑːsi / (AmE), / ˈɡlɒsi / (BrE)
A photograph printed on smooth, shiny paper.
photograph on shiny paper

Examples

The editor asked for a glossy for the magazine's cover story.
She keeps a collection of old family glossies in a photo album.
He prefers glossy prints because the colors look more vibrant.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: matte - a photograph printed on dull, non-glossy paper
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡlɑsi / (AmE), / ˈɡlɒsi / (BrE)
A magazine printed on high-quality, shiny paper, typically featuring many photographs and advertisements.
magazine on shiny paper

Examples

She bought a glossy at the airport to read on the flight.
Fashion trends are often showcased in popular glossies.
The new glossy covers celebrity news and lifestyle topics.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: magazine - a periodical publication containing articles and illustrations
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-06608931-n
Synset POS: n

3. ADJ

/ ˈɡlɑsi / (AmE), / ˈɡlɒsi / (BrE)
(of paper, fabric, or leather) having a smooth and shiny surface, especially one achieved by pressing between rollers.
smooth and shiny (surface)

Examples

The magazine was printed on expensive glossy paper that made the photos stand out.
She prefers photos developed on a glossy finish for their vibrant look.
The glossy leather handbag caught the sunlight as she walked.

Synset Examples

calendered paper
glossy paper

Related Terms

  • Antonym: matte - not shiny; having a dull surface

Etymology

From gloss (shine, luster) + -y.

Examples
