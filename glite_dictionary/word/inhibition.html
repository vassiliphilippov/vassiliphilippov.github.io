inhibition

1. NOUN

/ ˌɪn(h)ɪˈbɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn(h)ɪˈbɪʃən / (BrE)
the state or quality of holding back or restraining one's impulses, desires, or actions; self-conscious restraint
quality of being restrained or self-conscious

Examples

Her natural inhibition prevented her from speaking in front of large groups.
Because of his inhibition, he seldom expressed his true feelings.
The therapy aimed to reduce social inhibition and build confidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: restraint - self-control or holding back one's actions or emotions
  • Antonym: uninhibited - free from restraint or self-consciousness

Etymology

From Latin inhibere (to hold in, restrain) via Late Latin inhibitionem.

2. NOUN

/ ɪn.hɪˈbɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ɪn.hɪˈbɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
the act of prohibiting or preventing something, especially by law, rule, or authority; an instance of forbidding
act of prohibiting; instance of forbidding

Examples

The new law imposed an inhibition on smoking in public places.
There was an inhibition of imports during the trade embargo.
Medical inhibition of certain substances is sometimes necessary to protect patient health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prohibition - the act of formally forbidding something
  • Antonym: allowance - the act of allowing or permitting something

3. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.hɪˈbɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.hɪˈbɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
(Psychology) the conscious suppression or restraint of unwanted thoughts or desires.
conscious suppression of thoughts or desires

Examples

The patient's strong inhibition prevented him from expressing his true feelings to the therapist.
Through therapy, she learned to lower her inhibition and share her anxieties openly.
His inhibition about discussing personal matters made group therapy challenging.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: repression - (Psychology) the unconscious exclusion of distressing thoughts
  • Synonym: suppression - the act of consciously stopping oneself from thinking or feeling something

Etymology

From Latin inhibitiō (a restraining, hindering), from inhibēre (to hold in, check).

4. NOUN

/ ˌɪn.hɪˈbɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.hɪˈbɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
(Physiology) The process by which nerve signals slow down or prevent the activity of an organ or part of the body.
physiological prevention of organ activity
medical

Examples

The inhibition of the heart rate by the vagus nerve is well documented.
Some medications work by increasing neuronal inhibition within the brain.
Research shows that inhibition of certain enzymes can reduce inflammation in tissues.

Synset Examples

the inhibition of the heart by the vagus nerve

Related Terms

  • Antonym: excitation - (Physiology) stimulation that increases the activity of an organ or part

Etymology

From Latin inhibere (to restrain); see 'inhibit'.
