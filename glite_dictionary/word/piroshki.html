piroshki

1. NOUN

/ pɪˈrɔʃki / (AmE), / pɪˈrɒʃki / (BrE)
A small baked or fried turnover with a filling such as meat, cheese, or fruit, originating in Russian cuisine.
small Russian stuffed pastry

Examples

I bought some freshly baked piroshki at the Russian market for lunch.
Her grandmother taught her to make piroshki filled with potatoes and mushrooms.
The restaurant serves piroshki with a variety of sweet and savory fillings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pierogi - Polish dumplings filled with various ingredients
  • Synonym: empanada - Spanish or Latin American turnover pastry with savory or sweet filling

Etymology

From Russian пирожки (pirozhki), plural of пирожок (pirozhok), diminutive of пирог (pirog, 'pie').
