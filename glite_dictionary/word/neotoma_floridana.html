Neotoma floridana

1. NOUN

/ niˈoʊtəmə flɔrɪˈdænə / (AmE), / niːˈɒtəmə flɒrɪˈdanə / (BrE)
a large greyish-brown wood rat native to the southeastern United States, also known as the Eastern woodrat
a large wood rat of southeastern U.S.

Examples

The habitat of Neotoma floridana includes dense forests and areas with abundant ground cover.
Researchers studied the nest-building behavior of Neotoma floridana in different regions.
Sightings of Neotoma floridana are becoming less common due to habitat loss.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eastern woodrat - common name for Neotoma floridana, a large wood rat species of the southeastern U.S.

Etymology

From Latin; Neotoma (genus name) from Greek 'neos' (new) + 'tomos' (section), floridana refers to Florida.
