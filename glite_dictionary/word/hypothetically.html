hypothetically

1. ADV

/ ˌhaɪpəˈθɛtɪkli / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəˈθɛtɪkəli / (BrE)
used to describe a situation, event, or action that is supposed or imagined for the purpose of argument or investigation, rather than based on actual facts
in a supposed or imagined way

Examples

Hypothetically, what would you do if you won the lottery tomorrow?
Scientists sometimes talk hypothetically to explore possible outcomes before testing theories.
The lawyer spoke hypothetically about scenarios that might never occur.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: theoretically - in a way based on theory rather than actual experience

Etymology

From 'hypothetical' + '-ly'; 'hypothetical' from Greek 'hypothetikos' (able to be supposed).
