mainspring

1. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪnˌsprɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmeɪnˌsprɪŋ / (BrE)
the principal spring that drives the mechanism of a clock, watch, or similar device as it uncoils
principal spring driving a clock or watch

Examples

The broken mainspring caused the old pocket watch to stop working.
A skilled watchmaker replaced the mainspring to restore the clock's function.
Without a tightened mainspring, the music box would not play its tune.

Etymology

From main (chief, principal) + spring (coil of metal), first used for clockwork in the 16th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˈmeɪnˌsprɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmeɪnˌsprɪŋ / (BrE)
the principal spring in a mechanical clock or watch that stores energy and drives the mechanism
principal driving spring in a clock or watch

Examples

The mainspring of the clock broke, leaving it unable to keep time.
After years of use, he needed to replace the worn mainspring in his antique watch.
A skilled horologist can repair a damaged mainspring without replacing the entire mechanism.

Synset Examples

The mainspring of the clock had finally worn out.

Etymology

From main (principal, chief) + spring (mechanical coil), denoting its central role in powering the mechanism.
