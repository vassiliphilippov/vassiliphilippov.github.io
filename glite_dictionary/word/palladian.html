Palladian

1. ADJ

/ pəˈleɪdiən / (AmE), / pəˈleɪdɪən / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of the style of architecture developed by Andrea Palladio, featuring classical forms, symmetry, and grandeur.
style of architecture by Andrea Palladio

Examples

The new city hall was designed in a Palladian style with grand columns and symmetry.
Many English country houses display distinctive Palladian elements inspired by classical Roman architecture.
The architect chose a Palladian motif to give the building an air of timeless elegance.

Synset Examples

the much imitated arch and column compositions known as the Palladian motif

Etymology

From Andrea Palladio (1508–1580), Italian architect, whose work inspired the style.
