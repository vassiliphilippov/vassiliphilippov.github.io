puppy

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʌpi / (AmE), / ˈpʌpi / (BrE)
A young dog, especially one less than a year old.
a young dog

Examples

The children were excited to see the newborn puppy at the animal shelter.
She adopted a puppy and named him Max.
When the puppy barked for the first time, everyone laughed.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: dog - a domesticated carnivorous mammal; adult form of a puppy

Etymology

From Middle French *poupee* (doll, toy), possibly influenced by 'pup'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈpʌpi / (AmE), / ˈpʌpi / (BrE)
an inexperienced or naive young person, especially one regarded as presumptuous or overconfident
an inexperienced young person

Examples

He was hardly more than a puppy when he started working at the firm.
The young puppy strutted into the meeting, brimming with confidence but lacking experience.
Don’t let that cocky puppy fool you; he still has a lot to learn.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: novice - a person new to and inexperienced in a job or situation
  • Synonym: greenhorn - a person who is new to or inexperienced at something
  • Antonym: veteran - a person with long experience in a particular field

Etymology

From Middle French 'poupeé' (doll, toy), later meaning a young dog; figurative use for inexperienced youth attested since the 16th century.
