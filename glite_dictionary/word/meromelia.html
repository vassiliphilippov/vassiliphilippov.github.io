meromelia

1. NOUN

/ ˌmɛroʊˈmiːliə / (AmE), / ˌmɛrəˈmiːlɪə / (BrE)
A congenital condition in which part of an arm or leg is missing at birth.
congenital absence of limb part
medical

Examples

The newborn was diagnosed with meromelia in his right arm by the pediatrician.
Cases of meromelia require careful planning for prosthetic fitting and physical therapy.
Doctors noticed that meromelia can result from environmental or genetic factors during pregnancy.

Etymology

From Greek 'meros' (part) + 'melia' (limb).
