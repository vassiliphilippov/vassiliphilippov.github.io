biomedicine

1. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪoʊˈmɛdəsɪn / (AmE), / ˌbʌɪəʊˈmɛdɪsɪn / (BrE)
The branch of medical science that applies biological and physiological principles to clinical practice and research.
branch of medical science using biology
medical

Examples

Advances in biomedicine have led to better treatments for many diseases.
She is pursuing a career in biomedicine because she is interested in medical research.
The conference brought together leading experts in biomedicine from around the world.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: medicine - the science or practice of the diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of disease

Etymology

From bio- (life) + medicine; influenced by the rise of biology-based approaches in medicine in the 20th century.

2. NOUN

/ ˌbaɪ.oʊˈmɛd.ɪ.sən / (AmE), / ˌbaɪ.əʊˈmɛd.ɪ.sɪn / (BrE)
the branch of medical science that applies biological and physiological principles to clinical practice, including the study of how organisms respond to environmental stresses
medical science applying biology to medicine
medical

Examples

Advances in biomedicine have led to better treatments for patients with chronic diseases.
Researchers in biomedicine are investigating how astronauts' bodies adapt to the conditions of space.
The conference focused on the latest developments in biomedicine and their impact on healthcare.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: medicine - the science and practice of diagnosing, treating, and preventing disease
  • Synonym: biotechnology - the use of living systems and organisms in technology

Etymology

From bio- (Greek bios, 'life') + medicine.
