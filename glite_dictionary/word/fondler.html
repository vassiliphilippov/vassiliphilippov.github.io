fondler

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɑndlər / (AmE), / ˈfɒndlə / (BrE)
a person who touches someone, especially intimate parts, in a way that is inappropriate or unwelcome; a molester
person who inappropriately touches others

Examples

The police arrested the suspected fondler after several complaints were made.
Parents are urged to teach children how to respond if they encounter a fondler.
The article detailed the patterns of behavior commonly associated with a fondler.

Synset Examples

the woman charged that her jailer was a fondler
not all fondlers are sexual perverts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: molester - a person who assaults or abuses someone, especially sexually
  • Synonym: abuser - a person who cruelly or violently treats another person, especially repeatedly

Etymology

From 'fondle' (to caress or touch), from English 'fond.'

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɑndlər / (AmE), / ˈfɒndlɐ / (BrE)
a person who gently fondles or caresses someone, especially in a loving or affectionate way
person who fondles lovingly

Examples

He became known as a gentle fondler during their long romantic evenings together.
Rumors spread that the famous actor was a notorious fondler at parties.
Her new boyfriend was an affectionate fondler, always knowing how to offer comfort with a touch.

Synset Examples

they are heavy petters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caresser - a person who caresses
  • Synonym: petter - a person who pets or fondles

Etymology

From 'fondle' (to handle gently or caress) + '-er' suffix indicating a person who does something.
