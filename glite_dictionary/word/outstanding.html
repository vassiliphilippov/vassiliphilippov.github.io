outstanding

1. ADJ

/ aʊtˈstændɪŋ / (AmE), / aʊtˈstændɪŋ / (BrE)
Exceptionally good; clearly superior to others in excellence.
remarkably excellent or superior

Examples

Her outstanding performance won her several awards.
The museum is known for its outstanding collection of modern art.
He was promoted because of his outstanding leadership skills.

Synset Examples

did outstanding work in human relations
an outstanding war record

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excellent - of extremely high quality
  • Antonym: ordinary - not exceptional or special

Etymology

Formed from 'out' + 'stand' with the present participle ending '-ing'; figurative use since 17th century.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02351928-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ aʊtˈstændɪŋ / (AmE), / aʊtˈstandɪŋ / (BrE)
Remarkable or notable; attracting attention because of being especially good or prominent.
remarkable; attracting special attention

Examples

Her outstanding performance in the play earned her a standing ovation from the entire audience.
One outstanding feature of the exhibition is the artist's use of bold colors.
The report highlights several outstanding achievements by the research team this year.

Synset Examples

an outstanding fact of our time is that nations poisoned by anti semitism proved less fortunate in regard to their own freedom
a new theory is the most prominent feature of the book
salient traits
a spectacular rise in prices
a striking thing about Picadilly Circus is the statue of Eros in the center
a striking resemblance between parent and child

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prominent - Easily noticeable or standing out
  • Synonym: striking - Noticeable or impressive in a way that attracts attention
  • Antonym: ordinary - With no special or distinctive features; normal

Etymology

From out- + stand (meaning literally 'standing out').

3. ADJ

/ aʊtˈstændɪŋ / (AmE), / aʊtˈstændɪŋ / (BrE)
(of money) not yet paid; still owed.
(of money) unpaid; still owed

Examples

Our company still has several outstanding invoices from last month.
The total outstanding amount on the credit card is due next week.
Please pay any outstanding bills before the end of the month.

Synset Examples

outstanding bills
undischarged debts
the amount still outstanding

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unpaid - not yet paid
  • Antonym: paid - already paid

4. ADJ

/ aʊtˈstændɪŋ / (AmE), / aʊtˈstændɪŋ / (BrE)
extremely good and important; distinguished from others in excellence or significance.
exceptional; of major significance

Examples

The scientist received an award for her outstanding contributions to the field of physics.
His outstanding performance on the exam impressed all the teachers.
The gallery featured several outstanding works of modern art.

Synset Examples

a great work of art
Einstein was one of the outstanding figures of the 20th century

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exceptional - unusually good; much better than average
  • Antonym: ordinary - not special or different; normal

Etymology

Formed from out- + stand + -ing, originally meaning 'prominent, projecting.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01281928-s
Synset POS: s
