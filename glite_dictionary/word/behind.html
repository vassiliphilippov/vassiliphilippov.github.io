behind

1. ADV

/ bɪˈhaɪnd / (AmE), / bɪˈhaɪnd / (BrE)
still remaining in a place or condition after others have left or moved away.
still remaining after departure

Examples

She finished her meal quickly while the others were still behind.
Don't leave your backpack behind when you get off the bus.
He moved to another country, leaving all his friends behind.

Synset Examples

when he died he left much unfinished work behind
left a large family behind
the children left their books behind
he took off with a squeal of tires and left the other cars far behind

Related Terms

  • Antonym: ahead - in or toward a position in front; earlier in time

Etymology

From Old English behindan ('at the back of, after'), from be ('by') + hindan ('from behind').
