dearth

1. NOUN

/ dɝːθ / (AmE), / dɜːθ / (BrE)
A lack or scarcity of something; an insufficient supply or number.
a lack or scarcity; insufficiency

Examples

There was a dearth of evidence to support the claim, making prosecution difficult.
The region suffered from a dearth of clean water during the long drought.
A dearth of qualified candidates slowed down the hiring process.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: abundance - a very large quantity of something

Etymology

From Middle English 'derthe', from Old English 'deorthu', related to 'dear' (scarce, precious), meaning scarcity or famine.

2. NOUN

/ dɜrθ / (AmE), / dɜːθ / (BrE)
A lack or scarcity of something; an insufficient supply.
lack or scarcity; insufficiency

Examples

There is a dearth of affordable housing in the city.
A dearth of evidence made it difficult to solve the case.
The company faced a dearth of qualified applicants for the position.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: abundance - a very large quantity of something
  • Synonym: scarcity - the state of being scarce or in short supply

Etymology

From Middle English *derthe* (scarcity, dearness), from 'dear' (expensive, valued) + '-th' (suffix forming nouns).
