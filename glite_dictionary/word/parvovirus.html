parvovirus

1. NOUN

/ ˈpɑːrvoʊˌvaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˈpɑːvəʊˌvaɪərəs / (BrE)
Any of a group of viruses with a small icosahedral protein shell that contain DNA and cause disease in animals, such as dogs and cattle.
virus causing disease in animals
medical

Examples

The veterinarian diagnosed the puppy with parvovirus, a highly contagious disease among dogs.
Farmers are concerned about outbreaks of parvovirus in their cattle herds.
Scientists study parvovirus to better understand how it infects animal cells.

Etymology

From Latin 'parvus' (small) + 'virus', referring to the small size of the virus.
