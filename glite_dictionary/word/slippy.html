slippy

1. ADJ

/ ˈslɪpi / (AmE), / ˈslɪpi / (BrE)
(informal, chiefly British) Tending to cause slipping or sliding; slippery.
slippery; tending to cause slips (chiefly British)
informal british

Examples

Be careful—the floor is slippy where the ice melted.
The rocks by the riverbank get really slippy after it rains.
My boots kept sliding on the slippy pavement.

Synset Examples

slippery sidewalks
a slippery bar of soap
the streets are still slippy from the rain

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slippery - tending to cause slipping or sliding
