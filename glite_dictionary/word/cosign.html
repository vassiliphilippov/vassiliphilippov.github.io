to cosign

1. VERB

/ koʊˈsaɪn / (AmE), / kəʊˈsaɪn / (BrE)
to sign a document jointly with another person, especially to guarantee a loan or agreement
sign jointly (often to guarantee a loan)

Examples

Both parents were required to cosign the loan application for their son.
She agreed to cosign the apartment lease with her friend.
If you cosign this contract, you share responsibility for the debt.

Synset Examples

Husband and wife co-signed the lease

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sign - to write your name on a document to indicate approval or agreement

Etymology

From Latin com- (together) + signare (to sign), via English 'sign'.

2. VERB

/ ˈkoʊˌsaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkəʊˌsaɪn / (BrE)
to sign a document jointly with another person, especially to guarantee a loan or obligation
sign jointly, usually for a loan

Examples

Sarah asked her father to cosign the loan application so she could buy her first car.
If you cosign on a mortgage, you are equally responsible for the debt.
Before agreeing to cosign a friend's lease, consider the potential financial risks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to endorse - to sign the back of a financial document to make it payable to someone else

Etymology

From Latin co- (together) + signare (to sign); modelled on 'co-' meaning 'together with'.

Source: WordNet
