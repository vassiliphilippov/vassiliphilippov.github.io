Croix de Guerre

1. NOUN

/ krwɑ də ɡɛr / (AmE), / krwɑː də ɡɛː / (BrE)
A French military decoration awarded for acts of gallantry in combat.
French military decoration for bravery

Examples

He was awarded the Croix de Guerre for his bravery during the battle.
The general proudly displayed his Croix de Guerre among other medals.
Many allied soldiers received the Croix de Guerre from the French government after World War I.

Etymology

From French, literally 'cross of war'.
