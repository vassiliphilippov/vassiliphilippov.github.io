to bring

1. VERB

/ brɪŋ / (AmE), / brɪŋ / (BrE)
to have someone or something come with you to a place; to cause to be present through accompaniment
cause to come with; accompany

Examples

You can bring your friend to the concert if you'd like.
Please bring your documents when you come to the office.
She always brings her dog with her to the park.

Synset Examples

Can I bring my cousin to the dinner?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: take - to move something or someone away from the speaker's location

Etymology

From Old English *bringan*, of Germanic origin.
