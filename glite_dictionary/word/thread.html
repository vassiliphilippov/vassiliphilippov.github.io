thread

1. VERB

/ θrɛd / (AmE), / θrɛd / (BrE)
to pass a thread through the eye of a needle or through something similar
pass a thread through

Examples

She carefully threaded the needle before starting her embroidery.
He struggled to thread the fishing line through the tiny hook.
With steady hands, the surgeon threaded the suture through the delicate tissue.

Synset Examples

Etymology

From Old English 'thrǣdan', related to 'thrǣd' (thread).
