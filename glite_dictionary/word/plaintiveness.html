plaintiveness

1. NOUN

/ ˈpleɪntɪvnəs / (AmE), / ˈpleɪntɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality of expressing sadness or sorrow
quality of expressing sadness

Examples

There was a distinct plaintiveness in her voice as she recounted the story.
The plaintiveness of the music moved everyone in the audience.
He spoke with a plaintiveness that revealed his hidden sorrow.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: melancholy - a feeling of deep sadness
  • Antonym: cheerfulness - the quality of being happy and lively

Etymology

From 'plaintive' (French plaintif; from Latin planctus, meaning 'lament').
