Dorian

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɔːriən / (AmE), / ˈdɔːriən / (BrE)
of or relating to the ancient Greek inhabitants of Doris, their Doric dialect, or their culture and art.
relating to ancient Greek Dorians or their culture

Examples

The ruins displayed several Dorian architectural features characteristic of the period.
She specialized in the study of Dorian dialects for her thesis on ancient Greek linguistics.
The museum's new exhibit explores Dorian influences on Mediterranean pottery.

Etymology

From Latin 'Dorius', from Greek 'Dōrios', relating to Dorian people.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɔriən / (AmE), / ˈdɔːrɪən / (BrE)
a member of an ancient Greek people who originated from Doris and migrated into Greece around 1100 BC
member of an ancient Greek people

Examples

The Dorians played a key role in the transformation of ancient Greek society.
Many historians believe that the arrival of the Dorians marked the beginning of the Greek Dark Ages.
Archaeological evidence suggests the Dorians brought new cultural practices to southern Greece.

Etymology

From Latin Dorianus, from Greek Dōrios, relating to Doris, a district in ancient Greece.

3. NOUN

/ ˈdɔriən / (AmE), / ˈdɔːriən / (BrE)
a member of an ancient Greek people who settled in the southern and eastern Peloponnese, Crete, Rhodes, and other regions during the prehistoric period
member of an ancient Greek people

Examples

Many historians believe the Dorian migration significantly shaped ancient Greek civilization.
Excavations in Crete have uncovered artifacts associated with the Dorians.
The language spoken by the Dorian Greeks differed from that of the Ionians.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Ionian - member of another major ancient Greek people
  • Synonym: Achaean - another division of prehistoric Greeks
  • Synonym: Aeolian - member of another prehistoric Greek division

Etymology

From Latin Dorius, from Greek Dōrios, relating to Doris, a district in central Greece.
