to outweigh

1. VERB

/ aʊtˈweɪ / (AmE), / aʊtˈweɪ / (BrE)
(of one thing) to be heavier than (another thing).
be heavier than something else

Examples

The large gold statue outweighs the small one by several kilograms.
Even with all the books removed, the box still outweighs the suitcase.
An elephant easily outweighs a horse.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exceed - to go beyond in degree or amount

Etymology

From out- (meaning 'more than') + weigh.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01109955-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ aʊtˈweɪ / (AmE), / aʊtˈweɪ / (BrE)
to be more important, valuable, or significant than something else; to have greater weight or influence
be more important or significant than

Examples

The benefits of the new policy outweigh the potential drawbacks.
Her desire for adventure outweighed her fear of the unknown.
In this case, the risks clearly outweigh the rewards.

Synset Examples

these considerations outweigh our wishes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: override - to be considered more important than something else
  • Synonym: counterbalance - to balance or offset the weight or effect of something

Etymology

From out- (meaning 'beyond') + weigh, formed in late Middle English.

Source: WordNet
