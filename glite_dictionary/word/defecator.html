defecator

1. NOUN

/ ˈdɛfəˌkeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈdiːfɪˌkeɪtə / (BrE)
(Obsolete) a device or agent that cleanses or purifies, especially one used to remove impurities from juices and syrups.
(obsolete) purifying device or agent
archaic

Examples

The sugar refinery once used a defecator to clarify raw juice.
Old manufacturing texts describe a defecator as essential for improving syrup quality.
Early chemists relied on a defecator to purify their solutions.

Etymology

From Latin defaecare (to cleanse from dregs), via 'defecate'.
