Callirhoe

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɪrˌoʊ.i / (AmE), / kəˈlɪrəʊ.i / (BrE)
A small genus of North American flowering herbs, usually bearing red or purple flowers, belonging to the mallow family.
genus of North American herbs

Examples

The garden's centerpiece was a vibrant cluster of Callirhoe in full bloom.
Botanists recently discovered a new species within the Callirhoe genus.
The distinctive red flowers of Callirhoe are highly attractive to pollinators.

Etymology

From Greek Kallirhoe, a mythological name; used as a botanical genus.
