Sheraton

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃɛrətən / (AmE), / ˈʃɛrətən / (BrE)
a style of furniture that originated in England around 1800, characterized by simple design, straight lines, and classical ornamentation
a furniture style with simple, classical design

Examples

The antique dealer displayed a beautiful Sheraton sideboard from the early nineteenth century.
Her living room is decorated with several Sheraton chairs, noted for their elegance and symmetry.
Collectors often seek out Sheraton pieces because of their timeless appeal and craftsmanship.

Etymology

Named after Thomas Sheraton (1751–1806), an English furniture designer.
