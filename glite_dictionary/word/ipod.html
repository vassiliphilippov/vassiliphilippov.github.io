iPod

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪ.pɑd / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.pɒd / (BrE)
A portable electronic device designed for storing and playing digital music and other media files, originally developed by Apple Inc.
portable Apple music player

Examples

She listened to her favorite songs on her iPod during the flight.
Apple revolutionized music listening habits when it launched the original iPod in 2001.
Even though smartphones are common now, some people still cherish their old iPods.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: MP3 player - a portable device for playing digital audio files

Etymology

Coined by Apple Inc. in 2001, with 'pod' inspired by the phrase 'a pod of music' and the popularity of the word 'i' in Apple's product naming.
