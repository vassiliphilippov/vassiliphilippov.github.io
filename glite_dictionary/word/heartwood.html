heartwood

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɑrtˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈhɑːtˌwʊd / (BrE)
the hard, dense, inner wood of a tree trunk, usually darker than the outer sapwood, and no longer actively involved in sap transport
central, non-living wood of a tree

Examples

The rich color of the cabinet came from the heartwood of an old walnut tree.
Unlike the outer sapwood, heartwood is more resistant to decay and insect damage.
Furniture makers often prefer heartwood for its durability and attractive appearance.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sapwood - the softer, lighter outer wood of a tree, actively conducting water
