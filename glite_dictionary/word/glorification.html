glorification

1. NOUN

/ ˌɡlɔːrəfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɡlɔːrɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or state of giving great honor or praise to someone or something; exaltation
the act of giving great honor or praise

Examples

The film is a glorification of bravery in the face of adversity.
Many critics saw the statue as a glorification of a controversial figure.
She spoke against the glorification of violence in popular culture.

Synset Examples

he valued glory above life itself

Related Terms

  • Synonym: exaltation - the action of praising or raising someone to a higher status
  • Synonym: honor - high respect or esteem
  • Antonym: disgrace - the loss of honor or respect

Etymology

From Medieval Latin glorificatio (the act of glorifying), from glorificare (to glorify).

3. NOUN

/ ˌɡlɔːrɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɡlɔːrɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of portraying something as ideal, admirable, or better than it really is
the portrayal of something as ideal

Examples

The movie's glorification of gangster life was criticized by many reviewers.
Some historians argue that the glorification of the past often ignores important facts.
The glorification of war in literature can influence young minds.

Synset Examples

the idealization of rural life was very misleading

Related Terms

  • Synonym: idealization - the action of representing something as perfect or better than in reality
  • Antonym: denigration - the act of unfairly criticizing someone or something

Etymology

From Latin glorificatio (praise, exaltation), from glorificare (to glorify), from gloria (glory).
