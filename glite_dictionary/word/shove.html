shove

1. VERB

/ ʃʌv / (AmE), / ʃʌv / (BrE)
to push someone or something in a rough or forceful way
push roughly or forcefully

Examples

He had to shove the heavy door open with his shoulder.
People began to shove each other as the crowd moved forward.
She shoved her way through the busy market.

Synset Examples

the people pushed and shoved to get in line

Related Terms

  • Synonym: push - to move something or someone by pressing against it

Etymology

Possibly from Middle English *shoven*, from Old English *scūfan* (to push, shove).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01587622-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ʃʌv / (AmE), / ʃʌv / (BrE)
a strong or sudden push given to someone or something
a strong push

Examples

With a quick shove, he managed to close the sticking window.
She gave her brother a playful shove as they walked down the path.
The crowd surged forward with a collective shove when the doors opened.

Synset Examples

he gave the door a shove

Related Terms

  • Synonym: push - the act of applying force to move something away
  • Antonym: pull - the act of drawing something toward oneself

Etymology

Probably from Middle English 'shoven', from Old English 'scūfan' (to push).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00114546-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ ʃʌv / (AmE), / ʃʌv / (BrE)
to push someone or something roughly, especially so as to move or make contact with them forcefully
push roughly while moving

Examples

He had to shove his way through the crowd to catch the train.
The children shoved each other playfully as they entered the playground.
A woman accidentally shoved him aside while rushing to board the bus.

Synset Examples

The passengers jostled each other in the overcrowded train

Related Terms

  • Synonym: push - to move something or someone by exerting force against them
  • Synonym: jostle - to push, elbow, or bump against someone roughly
  • Antonym: pull - to move something toward oneself, opposite of push

Etymology

Probably of Low German origin; compare Low German 'schoven' (to push) and Dutch 'schuiven'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01875673-v
Synset POS: v

4. VERB

/ ʃʌv / (AmE), / ʃʌv / (BrE)
to push something or someone forcefully or roughly, especially in a hurried or careless way; to press or force something into a position with effort
push forcefully or roughly

Examples

She shoved all her clothes into the suitcase in a hurry.
He shoved the door open with his shoulder when it was stuck.
Quickly shoving the papers into his bag, he rushed out of the office.

Synset Examples

Stuff money into an envelope
She thrust the letter into his hand

Related Terms

  • Synonym: push - to move something or someone by pressing against it with force
  • Synonym: thrust - to push something suddenly or violently in a specified direction
  • Antonym: pull - to move something toward oneself by gripping and drawing

Etymology

Perhaps from Middle English 'shoven', from Old English 'scūfan' (to push, shove, advance).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
