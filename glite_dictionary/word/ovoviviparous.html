ovoviviparous

1. ADJ

/ ˌoʊvoʊvɪˈvɪpərəs / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəʊvɪˈvɪpərəs / (BrE)
(of an animal) producing young by means of eggs that hatch within the body, so that the young are born live.
producing live young from eggs hatched in the body
medical

Examples

Certain species of sharks are ovoviviparous, with embryos developing inside eggs retained within the mother's body.
The reptile's ovoviviparous reproduction allows the young to hatch internally before birth.
Unlike most frogs that lay eggs in water, some are ovoviviparous and give birth to live young.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: oviparous - producing young by laying eggs that hatch outside the body
  • Antonym: viviparous - giving birth to live young that have developed inside the body

Etymology

From Latin 'ovum' (egg) + 'vivus' (alive) + '-parous' (bearing).
