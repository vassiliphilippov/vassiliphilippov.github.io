latria

1. NOUN

/ ləˈtriːə / (AmE), / ləˈtrɪə / (BrE)
(in Christian theology) the worship or adoration given to God alone, distinct from the veneration shown to saints.
worship due only to God

Examples

The doctrine of latria teaches that only God is worthy of true worship.
In Catholic theology, latria is reserved exclusively for the divine, distinguishing it from other forms of honor.
Misunderstanding the boundaries between latria and dulia has led to much debate among theologians.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: dulia - veneration given to saints or angels
  • Degree Lower: hyperdulia - veneration accorded especially to the Virgin Mary, higher than dulia but less than latria

Etymology

From Late Latin latria (adoration, worship), from Ancient Greek latreia (service, worship).
