cespitose

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛspɪˌtoʊs / (AmE), / ˈsɛspɪtəʊs / (BrE)
(of plants) growing in small, dense clumps or tufts.
growing in dense clumps (of plants)

Examples

The cespitose grass formed a thick mat across the hillside.
Many alpine plants exhibit a cespitose growth habit to survive harsh weather.
Botanists classified the species as cespitose due to its dense, tufted clumps.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tufted - growing in tufts or clumps (of plants)

Etymology

From Latin caespitosus (full of turf, turf-like).
