cor pulmonale

1. NOUN

/ kɔr ˌpʊl.məˈnɑː.li / (AmE), / kɔː ˌpʊl.məˈnɑː.leɪ / (BrE)
a serious heart condition in which the right ventricle becomes enlarged as a result of disease affecting the lungs or pulmonary blood vessels
enlargement of the heart's right ventricle due to lung disease
medical

Examples

The diagnosis of cor pulmonale indicated that her chronic lung disease had affected her heart.
Treatment of cor pulmonale requires addressing both the heart and the underlying pulmonary condition.
Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease is a leading cause of cor pulmonale in adults.

Etymology

From Latin 'cor' (heart) and 'pulmonalis' (of the lungs).
