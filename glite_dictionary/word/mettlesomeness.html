mettlesomeness

1. NOUN

/ ˈmɛt.əl.səm.nəs / (AmE), / ˈmɛt.əl.səm.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being courageous, spirited, and full of energetic determination
spirited or courageous quality

Examples

Her mettlesomeness in the face of danger inspired everyone around her.
The team's mettlesomeness was evident as they refused to give up, even when losing.
Stories of mettlesomeness often serve as motivation during tough times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: courage - the ability to do something that frightens one
  • Antonym: timidity - lack of courage or confidence

Etymology

From 'mettlesome' + '-ness'; 'mettle' from an alteration of 'metal', meaning spirited quality.
