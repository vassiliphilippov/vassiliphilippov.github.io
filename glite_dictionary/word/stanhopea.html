Stanhopea

1. NOUN

/ stænˈhoʊpiə / (AmE), / stænˈhəʊpiə / (BrE)
A genus of tropical American epiphytic orchids known for their large, fragrant, and striking flowers.
genus of tropical American orchids

Examples

The Stanhopea orchid is prized by collectors for its unique fragrance and large blooms.
Several species of Stanhopea are native to Central and South America.
Botanists discovered a new Stanhopea species in the cloud forests of Ecuador.

Etymology

Named after Philip Henry Stanhope, 4th Earl Stanhope, a British scientist and politician.
