stanhopea

1. NOUN

/ stænˈhoʊpiə / (AmE), / stænˈhəʊpiə / (BrE)
A genus of orchids native to Mexico to Brazil, characterized by a single large leaf and loose racemes of large, fragrant flowers in various colors.
a genus of orchids with fragrant flowers

Examples

The botanical garden added a rare stanhopea to its orchid collection this year.
The striking fragrance of the stanhopea filled the greenhouse during its brief blooming period.
Collectors often seek out stanhopea species for their showy and unusual flowers.

Etymology

Named after Philip Henry Stanhope, 4th Earl Stanhope, an English scientist and patron of botany.
