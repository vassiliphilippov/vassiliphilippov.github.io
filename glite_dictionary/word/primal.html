primal

1. ADJ

/ ˈpraɪ.məl / (AmE), / ˈpraɪ.məl / (BrE)
most important; fundamental or essential in nature.
fundamental; essential

Examples

The desire for safety is a primal need for every human being.
Food and water are primal necessities that must be satisfied before higher needs can be pursued.
Trust is a primal component in any successful relationship.

Synset Examples

a cardinal rule
the central cause of the problem
an example that was fundamental to the argument
computers are fundamental to modern industrial structure

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fundamental - forming a necessary base or core; of central importance
  • Synonym: primary - of chief importance; principal

Etymology

From Latin 'primalis' (first), from 'primus' (first).

2. ADJ

/ ˈpraɪ.məl / (AmE), / ˈpraɪ.məl / (BrE)
existing from the earliest time; original or ancient.
originating in the earliest times

Examples

The archaeologists uncovered primal tools used by early humans.
Some psychologists believe that primal fears influence our modern anxieties.
The landscape had a primal beauty, untouched by civilization.

Synset Examples

aboriginal forests
primal eras before the appearance of life on earth
the forest primeval
primordial matter
primordial forms of life

Related Terms

  • Synonym: primordial - existing at or from the beginning of time; primeval
  • Synonym: primeval - belonging to the earliest ages; ancient

Etymology

From Latin primus (first, earliest).
