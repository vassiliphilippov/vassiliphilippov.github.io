coccus

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːkəs / (AmE), / ˈkɒkəs / (BrE)
a spherical or nearly spherical bacterium
spherical bacterium
medical

Examples

Clusters of coccus bacteria were observed under the microscope.
Streptococcus is a well-known kind of coccus that causes throat infections.
The scientist explained how a coccus differs from rod-shaped bacteria.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bacillus - a rod-shaped bacterium

Etymology

From New Latin, from Greek kokkos (berry, grain).
