circumstantially

1. ADV

/ ˌsɜrkəmˈstænʃəli / (AmE), / ˌsɜːkəmˈstænʃəli / (BrE)
in detailed or minute manner; with attention to all circumstances or particulars
in detailed or minute manner

Examples

The witness described the events circumstantially, leaving no detail unexplained.
He was able to account for his whereabouts circumstantially during the entire evening.
The novel is circumstantially rich, with every aspect of the setting carefully described.

Synset Examples

our inability to see everything minutely and clearly is due merely to the infirmity of our senses

Related Terms

  • Synonym: minutely - in a very detailed manner

Etymology

From Latin circumstantia (surrounding condition) + -ly.

2. ADV

/ ˌsɝː.kəmˈstæn.ʃəli / (AmE), / ˌsɜː.kəmˈstæn.ʃəli / (BrE)
In a way that depends on or relates to particular circumstances or conditions.
in relation to circumstances

Examples

The suspect was circumstantially linked to the crime through a series of coincidences.
She was circumstantially forced to abandon her original plans due to unforeseen events.
He was circumstantially implicated in the scandal, though no direct evidence was found.

Synset Examples

he was convicted circumstantially

Related Terms

  • Antonym: directly - in a direct manner, without intervening factors

Etymology

From circumstantial + -ly, ultimately from Latin circumstantia (standing around, surrounding conditions).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00501538-r
Synset POS: r

3. ADV

/ ˌsɝːkəmˈstænʃəli / (AmE), / ˌsɜːkəmˈstænʃəli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to or depends on the specific circumstances; with regard to the particular details of a situation.
in relation to circumstances

Examples

The evidence was presented circumstantially, outlining all the relevant details of the event.
She was circumstantially implicated in the incident, though there was no direct proof of her involvement.
The report was circumstantially accurate but lacked emotional depth.

Synset Examples

the account was circumstantially accurate

Etymology

From circumstantial + -ly; circumstantial from Latin circumstantia (standing around, circumstance).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00501406-r
Synset POS: r

4. ADV

/ ˌsɜrkəmˈstænʃəli / (AmE), / ˌsɜːkəmˈstænʃəli / (BrE)
In a way that happens without advance planning or as a result of circumstances; incidentally.
without advance planning; incidentally

Examples

They met circumstantially at a café while traveling abroad.
The discovery was made circumstantially, not through any deliberate search.
She became involved in the project circumstantially, after helping a friend in need.

Synset Examples

they met accidentally

Related Terms

  • Synonym: accidentally - happening by chance, not planned
  • Antonym: deliberately - with intention or planning

Etymology

From 'circumstantial' + '-ly', ultimately from Latin 'circumstantia' (standing around, circumstance).
