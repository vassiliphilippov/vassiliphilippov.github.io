scurry

1. NOUN

/ ˈskɜri / (AmE), / ˈskʌri / (BrE)
a hurried and often confused movement or rush, typically made by people or small animals
hurried, confused rush or movement

Examples

There was a scurry of activity as the team prepared for the meeting.
We heard a scurry in the bushes and saw a rabbit dart away.
The unexpected announcement caused a sudden scurry in the newsroom.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rush - a sudden movement forward; hurry
  • Synonym: scramble - hurried and often disorganized movement

Etymology

Late 17th century (originally dialect): of unknown origin.

2. VERB

/ ˈskɜːri / (AmE), / ˈskʌri / (BrE)
to move quickly and with short, hurried steps, often because you are frightened or in a hurry
move quickly with short steps

Examples

The mice scurried across the kitchen floor when the lights were turned on.
Shoppers scurried through the rain, eager to get home before the storm worsened.
She was late, so she scurried down the hallway to her meeting.

Synset Examples

so terrified by the extraordinary ebbing of the sea that they scurried to higher ground

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scamper - to run quickly and lightly
  • Antonym: to dawdle - to move slowly, take one's time

Etymology

Perhaps imitative or from dialect English 'scurr' (to hurry); possibly related to the Swedish 'skorra' (to rattle).
