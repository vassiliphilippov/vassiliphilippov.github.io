obsessiveness

1. NOUN

/ əbˈsɛsɪvnəs / (AmE), / əbˈsɛsɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality of thinking about something or someone too much, or being unable to stop doing something repeatedly
state of being obsessive

Examples

Her obsessiveness about cleanliness made living with her difficult at times.
The writer's obsessiveness drove him to revise his manuscript countless times.
Some people view obsessiveness as a strength, while others see it as a flaw.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compulsiveness - the quality of being compelled to do things repeatedly
  • Antonym: detachment - the state of being objective or aloof

Etymology

From 'obsessive' + '-ness'. 'Obsessive' from Latin 'obsessus', past participle of 'obsidere' (to besiege).
