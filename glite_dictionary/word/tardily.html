tardily

1. ADV

/ ˈtɑrdɪli / (AmE), / ˈtɑːdɪli / (BrE)
in a slow, delayed, or late manner; without speed or promptness.
in a slow or delayed manner

Examples

The group arrived tardily, missing the important announcement at the beginning of the event.
He grudgingly replied tardily to the invitations, well after the deadline had passed.
Spring came tardily that year, with the trees remaining bare into late April.

Synset Examples

he spoke slowly
go easy here — the road is slippery
glaciers move tardily
please go slow so I can see the sights

Related Terms

  • Synonym: slowly - at a slow speed; not quickly
  • Antonym: quickly - at a fast speed; rapidly

Etymology

From Latin 'tardus' (slow) via Old French 'tardif' and English '-ly'.

2. ADV

/ ˈtɑːrdɪli / (AmE), / ˈtɑːdɪli / (BrE)
in a slow, delayed, or late manner; later than expected or usual.
in a slow or delayed manner

Examples

The report was submitted tardily, missing the official deadline.
She responded tardily to the invitation, after all the seats were taken.
Awards were bestowed tardily, long after the achievements had taken place.

Synset Examples

the train arrived late
we awoke late
the children came late to school
notice came so tardily that we almost missed the deadline
I belatedly wished her a happy birthday

Related Terms

  • Synonym: late - at or after the expected or usual time
  • Antonym: promptly - at the scheduled or correct time, without delay

Etymology

From Latin tardus (slow) via French tardif (slow, late) and English suffix -ly.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00100817-r
Synset POS: r
