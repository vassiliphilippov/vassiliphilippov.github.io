Buddleia

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌdliə / (AmE), / ˈbʌdliːə / (BrE)
a genus of shrubs or small trees native to warm regions, often grown for their fragrant, colorful flowers
a genus of flowering shrubs or trees

Examples

Many gardeners plant Buddleia to attract butterflies to their gardens.
The purple blooms of Buddleia are a common sight in summer.
She pruned the Buddleia bushes to encourage new growth.

Etymology

Named after English botanist Adam Buddle (1662–1715).
