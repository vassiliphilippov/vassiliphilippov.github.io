anticlockwise

1. ADV

/ ˌæn.tiˈklɑːk.waɪz / (AmE), / ˌæn.tiˈklɒk.waɪz / (BrE)
in a direction opposite to the way the hands of a clock move; counterclockwise
in the opposite direction to a clock's hands
british

Examples

Turn the wheel anticlockwise to loosen the bolt.
The dancers moved anticlockwise around the maypole during the festival.
Traffic on the roundabout flows anticlockwise in the UK.

Synset Examples

please move counterclockwise in a circle!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: counterclockwise - in a direction opposite to the hands of a clock (US equivalent)

Etymology

Formed from 'anti-' (against) + 'clockwise'. Common in British English.

2. ADJ

/ ˌæn.tiˈklɑːkˌwaɪz / (AmE), / ˌæntiˈklɒkwaɪz / (BrE)
In the direction opposite to the rotation of the hands of a clock.
opposite direction to a clock's hands
british

Examples

Turn the screw anticlockwise to loosen it.
The runners must move anticlockwise around the track.
She rotated the dial anticlockwise until it clicked.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: counterclockwise - in the direction opposite to the rotation of the hands of a clock (American usage)
  • Antonym: clockwise - in the direction of the rotation of the hands of a clock

Etymology

Formed from 'anti-' (against, opposite) + 'clockwise', chiefly British.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00444074-a
Synset POS: a
