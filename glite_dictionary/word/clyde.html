Clyde

1. PROPN

/ klaɪd / (AmE), / klaɪd / (BrE)
a river in western Scotland that flows from the southern uplands into the Firth of Clyde, navigable by oceangoing vessels as far as Glasgow
major river in western Scotland

Examples

The Clyde has played a vital role in the industrial history of Glasgow.
Many shipyards once lined the banks of the Clyde.
Tourists enjoy taking boat trips along the scenic Clyde.
