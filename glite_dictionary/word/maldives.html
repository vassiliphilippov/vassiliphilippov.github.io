Maldives

1. PROPN

/ mælˈdivz, ˈmældivz / (AmE), / ˈmɔːldiːvz, mɔːlˈdiːvz / (BrE)
a country consisting of a group of atolls and islands in the Indian Ocean, southwest of Sri Lanka, known for its beaches and resorts; an independent republic since 1965.
island country in the Indian Ocean

Examples

She spent her honeymoon in the Maldives, enjoying the crystal-clear waters and white sandy beaches.
The Maldives is renowned for its luxury resorts and diverse marine life.
Climate change poses a significant threat to low-lying island nations like the Maldives.

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'malā' (garland) + 'dvīpa' (island).

2. PROPN

/ ˈmælˌdiːvz, mælˈdiːvz / (AmE), / ˈmældiːvz, ˈmɔːldiːvz / (BrE)
an island country consisting of about 1,200 coral islands (about 220 inhabited), located in the Indian Ocean southwest of India and Sri Lanka
island country in the Indian Ocean

Examples

Many tourists dream of vacationing in the Maldives because of its crystal-clear waters and white sandy beaches.
The Maldives is known for its unique marine biodiversity and coral reefs.
In recent years, climate change has become a serious concern for the low-lying islands of the Maldives.

Etymology

From Arabic 'Mahal' (palace) and Sanskrit 'dvīpa' (island).
