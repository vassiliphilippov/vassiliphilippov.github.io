pointed

1. ADJ

/ ˈpɔɪntɪd / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪntɪd / (BrE)
expressing criticism, meaning, or reference in a direct and obvious way, often making someone feel uncomfortable
direct and obvious (often critical)

Examples

She made a pointed remark about the team’s lack of preparation.
During the meeting, he gave his boss a pointed look.
The article contains several pointed criticisms of government policy.

Synset Examples

a pointed critique
a pointed allusion to what was going on
another pointed look in their direction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blunt - not subtle or indirect in expression, but often lacking tact
  • Antonym: subtle - not obvious or direct, delicately implied
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00769422-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈpɔɪntɪd / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪntɪd / (BrE)
having a sharp or tapered end or tip; coming to a point
having a sharp or tapered end

Examples

The pencil has a pointed tip so you can write precisely.
She wore shoes with extremely pointed toes to the party.
The leaves of this plant are narrow and pointed.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: blunt - not having a sharp edge or point; dull
