self-analysis

1. NOUN

/ ˌsɛlf əˈnæləsɪs / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf əˈnæləsɪs / (BrE)
the examination and evaluation of one's own thoughts, feelings, and motives
examination of one's own thoughts and motives

Examples

Through regular self-analysis, she gained a deeper understanding of her motivations.
Many people avoid self-analysis because it can be uncomfortable to confront one's own flaws.
Psychotherapy often encourages patients to engage in self-analysis.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: introspection - the examination of one's own thoughts and feelings

Etymology

From 'self' (referring to oneself) and 'analysis' (from Greek 'analusis', meaning loosening, a breaking up).
