cephaloridine

1. NOUN

/ sɛˌfælɔˈraɪdiːn / (AmE), / sɛˌfaləˈrɪdiːn / (BrE)
a broad-spectrum semisynthetic antibiotic derived from cephalosporin, used to treat bacterial infections
broad-spectrum antibiotic derived from cephalosporin
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed cephaloridine to combat the patient's severe infection.
Researchers studied the effects of cephaloridine on drug-resistant bacteria.
Some patients may experience allergic reactions to cephaloridine.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: cephalosporin - a group of antibiotics derived from Acremonium fungi

Etymology

From 'cephalosporin' (an antibiotic class) + '-idine', a chemical suffix.
