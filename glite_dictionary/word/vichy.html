Vichy

1. PROPN

/ ˈviːʃi / (AmE), / ˈviːʃi / (BrE)
a town in central France known for its hot mineral springs and as the capital of unoccupied France during World War II
French town; WWII capital; mineral springs

Examples

During World War II, Vichy served as the administrative center of unoccupied France.
Many tourists visit Vichy to experience its famous mineral springs and spas.
The Vichy government collaborated with Nazi Germany during the war.

2. PROPN

/ ˈviːʃi / (AmE), / ˈviːʃi / (BrE)
The regime of France that collaborated with Nazi Germany during the Second World War, based in the city of Vichy from 1940 to 1944.
collaborationist French regime (1940–44)

Examples

The legacy of Vichy continues to influence contemporary debates in France.
Historians often analyze the actions of the Vichy government during World War II.
The film depicted the struggles of those who resisted Vichy authority.

Synset Examples

The term Vichy evokes a controversial period in French history.

Etymology

From the name of the city Vichy in central France, where the government was based.
