Lepomis

1. NOUN

/ lɛˈpoʊmɪs / (AmE), / lɛˈpɒmɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small freshwater fish in the family Centrarchidae, commonly known as sunfishes or bream, native to North America.
genus of North American sunfish

Examples

The pond was teeming with Lepomis, attracting local fishermen.
Several species within the genus Lepomis are popular among recreational anglers.
Biologists studied the reproductive behavior of Lepomis to better understand freshwater ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sunfish - common name for fishes in the genus Lepomis
  • Synonym: bream - another common name for various freshwater fish including Lepomis

Etymology

From Greek lepis (scale) and omis (shoulder).

2. NOUN

/ lɛˈpoʊmɪs / (AmE), / lɛˈpəʊmɪs / (BrE)
A genus of freshwater fish in the family Centrarchidae, commonly known as sunfish, native to North America.
genus of North American sunfish

Examples

The bluegill is one of the most well-known species in the Lepomis genus.
Freshwater anglers often target various Lepomis species for sport.
Researchers studied the behavior of Lepomis in their natural habitats.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sunfish - common name for fish species in the genus Lepomis

Etymology

From Greek 'lepis' (scale) and 'pōma' (cover, lid), referring to the prominent gill cover of these fish.
