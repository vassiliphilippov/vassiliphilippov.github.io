guy

1. NOUN

/ ɡaɪ / (AmE), / ɡaɪ / (BrE)
An effigy of Guy Fawkes, traditionally burned on a bonfire during Guy Fawkes Day in the UK.
effigy of Guy Fawkes burned on Bonfire Night
british

Examples

Children helped to make a guy out of old clothes and newspapers for the bonfire.
Every November 5th, communities gather to burn a guy and set off fireworks.
He proudly carried their homemade guy to the park for Bonfire Night.

Etymology

Named after Guy Fawkes (1570–1606), whose failed plot to blow up Parliament is commemorated.

2. VERB

/ ɡaɪ / (AmE), / ɡaɪ / (BrE)
to steady or support (something) with a guy wire or cable
support with a wire or cable

Examples

Engineers guyed the radio tower to increase its stability during storms.
The workers guyed the large tent to keep it from blowing away in the wind.
To prevent the pole from falling, they decided to guy it securely with steel cables.

Synset Examples

Etymology

3. NOUN

/ ɡaɪ / (AmE), / ɡaɪ / (BrE)
A cable, wire, or rope used to steady or brace something, especially a tent or a mast.
supporting cable or rope

Examples

The engineer tightened the guy to keep the radio tower steady during strong winds.
Each tent was secured with a guy at every corner.
The mast collapsed when a guy snapped during the storm.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stay - a rope, wire, or rod used to support a mast

Etymology

From Old French *guier* (to guide), related to the sense of guiding or supporting.

/ ɡaɪ / (AmE), / ɡaɪ / (BrE)
an informal term for a man or boy; a male person
informal word for a man or boy
informal

Examples

He's a really nice guy and always helps his friends.
That guy over there is waiting for you.
A group of guys came into the café, laughing and joking.

Synset Examples

a nice guy
the guy's only doing it for some doll

Related Terms

  • Synonym: man - an adult male human
  • Antonym: woman - an adult female human

Etymology

From Guy Fawkes (1570–1606), whose effigy was burned on Guy Fawkes Night; the meaning shifted from referring to a grotesquely dressed person to any man or boy.
