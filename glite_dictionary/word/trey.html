trey

1. NOUN

/ treɪ / (AmE), / treɪ / (BrE)
a playing card with three pips or spots; the three of any suit in a standard deck of cards
a playing card with three spots

Examples

He was dealt a trey of clubs and a queen in the first round.
The trey can sometimes be a lucky card in certain games.
She placed the trey face up on the table to start the game.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: deuce - a playing card with two pips or spots
  • Degree Higher: four - a playing card with four pips or spots

Etymology

From Old French trei (three), from Latin tres (three).

2. NOUN

/ treɪ / (AmE), / treɪ / (BrE)
The number three, especially as shown on playing cards or dice.
three in cards or dice

Examples

He drew a trey of hearts from the deck.
The roll showed a trey on one of the dice.
She bet on the trey and won when a three turned up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: three - the cardinal number after two

Etymology

From Old French 'treis', from Latin 'tres' meaning 'three'.
