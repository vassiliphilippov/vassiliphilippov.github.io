cottonseed

1. NOUN

/ ˈkɑːtnˌsiːd / (AmE), / ˈkɒtnˌsiːd / (BrE)
the seed of the cotton plant, used as a source of oil, meal, and for planting new crops
seed of the cotton plant

Examples

Farmers often save the best cottonseed from each harvest to plant for the next season.
Cottonseed oil is commonly used in cooking and food processing industries.
The factory processes thousands of tons of cottonseed into oil and meal every year.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: seed - the grain or ripened ovule of a flowering plant used for sowing
