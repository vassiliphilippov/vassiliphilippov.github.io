Brigit

1. PROPN

/ ˈbrɪdʒɪt / (AmE), / ˈbrɪdɪt / (BrE)
A Celtic goddess of fire, fertility, agriculture, household arts, and wisdom, later associated with Saint Bridget in Christian tradition.
Celtic goddess of fire, fertility, and wisdom

Examples

Many ancient Celtic rituals celebrate Brigit as the protector of hearth and home.
Legends say Brigit inspired poets, craftsmen, and healers throughout Ireland.
The festival of Imbolc is dedicated to Brigit and marks the beginning of spring.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Saint Bridget - a major Irish saint, possibly syncretized with Brigit

Etymology

From Old Irish 'Brigit', meaning 'the exalted one'.
