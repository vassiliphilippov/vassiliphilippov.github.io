Tucana

1. PROPN

/ tuˈkɑːnə / (AmE), / tjuˈkɑːnə / (BrE)
a large, faint constellation in the southern sky, containing most of the Small Magellanic Cloud
a southern sky constellation

Examples

The constellation Tucana can be observed best from the Southern Hemisphere during the spring.
Astronomers often study Tucana because it contains most of the Small Magellanic Cloud.
Our guide pointed out Tucana in the clear night sky, explaining its significance.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: constellation - a group of stars forming a recognizable pattern

Etymology

From Latin, literally 'the toucan', after the bird genus.
