clocks

1. NOUN

/ klɑks / (AmE), / klɒks / (BrE)
a small European weed (Erodium cicutarium), now widespread in North America, with reddish, fernlike leaves and small reddish-purple flowers, often grown for forage.
a weed with reddish-purple flowers, also called Erodium cicutarium

Examples

Children often picked the clocks from the field and watched their seeds scatter in the wind.
The pasture was overrun with clocks after a rainy spring.
Farmers sometimes use clocks as forage for their livestock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Erodium cicutarium - the scientific name for clocks, a species of flowering plant in the geranium family
