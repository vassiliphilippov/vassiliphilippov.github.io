gracefully

1. ADV

/ ˈɡreɪs.fə.li / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪs.fəli / (BrE)
in a smooth, elegant, and attractive way; with grace.
in an elegant manner

Examples

The dancer moved gracefully across the stage, captivating everyone in the audience.
He accepted the award gracefully, thanking his team for their support.
Even under criticism, she responded gracefully and maintained her composure.

Synset Examples

he did not have a chance to grow up graciously

Related Terms

  • Antonym: awkwardly - in a clumsy or uncomfortable manner
  • Synonym: elegantly - in a stylish and graceful manner

Etymology

From 'graceful' + '-ly'. 'Graceful' from Old French 'gracieux', from Latin 'gratia' (favor, charm).

2. ADV

/ ˈɡreɪsfəli / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪsfəli / (BrE)
In a way that is elegant, smooth, and attractive in movement or form.
in an elegant or smooth way

Examples

The ballerina moved gracefully across the stage, captivating the entire audience.
He accepted the award gracefully, thanking everyone who supported him.
The swan glided gracefully over the still surface of the lake.

Synset Examples

she swooped gracefully

Related Terms

  • Antonym: awkwardly - in a manner that lacks grace or smoothness
  • Synonym: elegantly - in an elegant and refined manner

Etymology

From 'graceful' + '-ly'. 'Graceful' from French *gracieux*, from Latin *gratia* (favor, charm).
