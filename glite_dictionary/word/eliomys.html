Eliomys

1. NOUN

/ iˈlioʊmɪs / (AmE), / iˈlɪəmɪs / (BrE)
A genus of small rodents known as garden dormice, found in Europe and North Africa.
genus of rodents (garden dormice)

Examples

The Eliomys are easily identified by their bushy tails and distinctive facial markings.
Scientists observed that Eliomys populations fluctuate with seasonal changes in their habitats.
The garden dormouse, a species of Eliomys, is often found in wooded areas across southern Europe.

Etymology

From Greek hēlios (sun) + mys (mouse).

2. NOUN

/ ˌiːliˈoʊmɪs / (AmE), / ˌɛlɪˈəʊmɪs / (BrE)
a genus of rodents in the family Gliridae, commonly known as garden dormice or lerots, found in Europe and North Africa.
a genus of dormice (rodents)

Examples

The genus Eliomys includes several species of nocturnal rodents.
Scientists studied the hibernation patterns of Eliomys in Mediterranean habitats.
The garden dormouse, classified under Eliomys, is known for its distinctive black eye markings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: garden dormouse - a species (Eliomys quercinus) within the genus Eliomys

Etymology

From Greek ēlios (sun) + mys (mouse), used in scientific taxonomy.
