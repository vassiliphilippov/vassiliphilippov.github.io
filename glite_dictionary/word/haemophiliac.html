haemophiliac

1. NOUN

/ hiˌmoʊˈfɪliˌæk / (AmE), / hiːˌməʊˈfɪlɪˌak / (BrE)
a person who has hemophilia and is prone to uncontrollable bleeding due to a deficiency of blood-clotting factors
person with hemophilia
medical british

Examples

The doctor explained that a haemophiliac must take special care to avoid injuries.
As a child, Simon learned how to manage life as a haemophiliac.
Emergency medical staff are trained to respond quickly when a haemophiliac is admitted with bleeding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hemophiliac - American English spelling: a person with hemophilia

Etymology

From hemophilia, modeled after similar medical terms with -ac suffix indicating a person affected by a disease.
