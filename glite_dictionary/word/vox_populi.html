vox populi

1. NOUN

/ vɑks ˈpɑpjəˌlaɪ / (AmE), / vɒks ˈpɒpjʊlaɪ / (BrE)
the opinions or beliefs commonly held by the majority of people; popular opinion
public opinion; popular sentiment

Examples

In politics, leaders often claim to represent the vox populi when introducing new policies.
The protest reflected the vox populi demanding social and economic reforms.
Journalists sometimes conduct street interviews to gauge the vox populi on current affairs.

Synset Examples

he asked for a poll of public opinion

Related Terms

  • Synonym: public opinion - the collective opinion of many people on an issue

Etymology

From Latin 'vox populi', meaning 'voice of the people'.
