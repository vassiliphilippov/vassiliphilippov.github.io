Metternich

1. PROPN

/ ˈmɛtərˌnɪk / (AmE), / ˈmɛtəˌnɪk / (BrE)
Klemens von Metternich (1773–1859), an Austrian statesman and diplomat who played a leading role in European politics during the early 19th century.
Austrian statesman and diplomat (1773–1859)

Examples

Historians often debate how much influence Metternich had on the Congress of Vienna.
Metternich is credited with shaping the conservative order in post-Napoleonic Europe.
The foreign policy of Austria under Metternich aimed to suppress revolutionary movements.

Etymology

From the German surname Metternich.
