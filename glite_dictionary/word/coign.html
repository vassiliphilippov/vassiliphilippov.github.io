coign

1. NOUN

/ kɔɪn / (AmE), / kɔɪn / (BrE)
An expandable metal or wooden wedge used by printers to lock up a form within a chase.
printing wedge for securing type

Examples

The printer adjusted the coign to make sure the form was held tightly in the chase.
Several coigns were required to keep the type from shifting during the press run.
He learned to set type and use a coign as part of his apprenticeship in the print shop.

Etymology

From Old French 'coing' (wedge, corner), from Latin 'cuneus' (wedge).

2. NOUN

/ kɔɪn / (AmE), / kɔɪn / (BrE)
The keystone at the apex of an arch in architecture.
keystone of an arch

Examples

Skilled masons carefully positioned the coign as the final piece of the arch.
The architect emphasized the importance of the coign in distributing the weight above the doorway.
During restoration, the original coign was found to be intricately carved with symbols.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: keystone - the central stone at the apex of an arch

Etymology

From Old French *coing* (wedge, corner), from Latin *cuneus* (wedge).
