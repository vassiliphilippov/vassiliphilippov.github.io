gape

1. NOUN

/ ɡeɪp / (AmE), / ɡeɪp / (BrE)
an expression or look of openmouthed astonishment or wonder
openmouthed look of astonishment

Examples

The child's face broke into a gape as the magician pulled a rabbit from the hat.
He sat there in a gape throughout the entire performance, unable to hide his awe.
Everyone watched in silent gape as the unexpected announcement was made.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stare - a fixed look with eyes wide open
  • Antonym: glance - a brief or hurried look

Etymology

From Middle English gapen, from Old Norse gapa (to open the mouth).

2. NOUN

/ ɡeɪp / (AmE), / ɡeɪp / (BrE)
an act of staring in amazement, often with the mouth open
a stare of amazement, mouth open

Examples

He stood in the museum, his mouth in a wide gape as he admired the ancient sculpture.
The students listened to the astronaut's story, eyes wide and faces frozen in a collective gape.
Her obvious gape at the magician’s disappearing act made everyone around her chuckle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stare - a long fixed look with the eyes wide open
  • Antonym: glance - a brief or hurried look

Etymology

From Middle English 'gapien', from Old Norse 'gapa' (to open the mouth wide).

3. VERB

/ ɡeɪp / (AmE), / ɡeɪp / (BrE)
to be or become wide open, often in a way that is noticeable or surprising
be or become wide open

Examples

The door began to gape after the strong wind hit it.
A hole gaped in the roof after the storm.
The cave gaped before them, dark and mysterious.

Related Terms

4. VERB

/ ɡeɪp / (AmE), / ɡeɪp / (BrE)
to look at something with your mouth open in surprise or wonder
stare in amazement, mouth open

Examples

The children could only gape at the magician's amazing tricks.
We stood there gaping in disbelief at the sudden snowfall in June.
Tourists gaped at the towering skyscrapers as they entered the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stare - to look fixedly or vacantly at someone or something
  • Antonym: glance - to take a brief or hurried look

Etymology

From Old Norse 'gapa' meaning 'to open the mouth wide'.
