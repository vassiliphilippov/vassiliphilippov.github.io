prewar

1. ADJ

/ ˌpriːˈwɔr / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈwɔː / (BrE)
Existing or belonging to a period before a specific war, especially before World War I or II.
existing before a war

Examples

The city still retains many prewar buildings in its historic center.
Economic growth finally returned to prewar levels in the 1950s.
Her grandmother shared stories about life in the prewar countryside.

Synset Examples

prewar levels of industrial production

Related Terms

  • Antonym: postwar - existing or occurring after a war

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'pre-' (before) and 'war'.
