pullback

1. NOUN

/ ˈpʊlˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈpʊl.bæk / (BrE)
a decorative cord, loop, or band used to draw curtains or draperies to the side and hold them open
decorative device for holding curtains aside

Examples

The new satin pullbacks matched the living room's color scheme perfectly.
She replaced the old pullbacks with modern metallic ones to give the room a fresh look.
Without pullbacks, the thick curtains would obscure much of the daylight.

Synset Examples

the draperies were drawn to the sides by pullbacks

2. NOUN

/ ˈpʊlˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈpʊlˌbæk / (BrE)
an act of withdrawing troops or forces from a position, especially in an orderly or strategic manner
an orderly military withdrawal

Examples

After months of fighting, the general ordered a pullback to regroup his forces.
International leaders debated whether a gradual pullback would ensure stability in the region.
The pullback was seen as a sign that peace talks might succeed.

Synset Examples

the pullback is expected to be over 25,000 troops

Related Terms

  • Synonym: withdrawal - the act of removing troops from a place
  • Antonym: advance - the act of moving troops forward
