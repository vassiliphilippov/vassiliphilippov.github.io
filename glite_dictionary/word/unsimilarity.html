unsimilarity

1. NOUN

/ ˌʌnsɪˈmɪlərəti / (AmE), / ˌʌnsɪˈmɪləriːti / (BrE)
the state or quality of being dissimilar or unlike; lack of similarity
lack of similarity; difference

Examples

The unsimilarity between the two paintings was immediately apparent to the art expert.
Genetic analysis revealed a surprising unsimilarity in their DNA sequences.
Her explanation emphasized the unsimilarity of the two historical events.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: similarity - the state of being similar or alike
  • Synonym: dissimilarity - the quality of being unlike or different

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) and 'similarity.'
