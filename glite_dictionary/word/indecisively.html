indecisively

1. ADV

/ ˌɪndɪˈsaɪsɪvli / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈsaɪsɪvli / (BrE)
In a way that shows an inability to decide or reaches no clear conclusion or result.
in an undecided or inconclusive manner

Examples

He answered the question indecisively, unsure of what he truly believed.
The meeting ended indecisively, with no decision reached on the proposal.
She glanced indecisively at the menu, unable to choose what to order.

Synset Examples

the battle ended indecisively: neither side had clearly won but neither side admitted defeat

Related Terms

  • Antonym: conclusively - in a manner that leaves no doubt; decisively

Etymology

From 'indecisive' + '-ly'. 'Indecisive' from Latin 'indecisus' (not decided).

2. ADV

/ ˌɪndɪˈsaɪsɪvli / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈsaɪsɪvli / (BrE)
in a way that shows lack of firmness or the ability to make decisions quickly and confidently
in an uncertain or hesitant manner

Examples

She answered indecisively, unsure of what she truly wanted.
He indecisively moved between the two options, unable to make up his mind.
They discussed the matter indecisively, never reaching a firm conclusion.

Synset Examples

‘I don't know,’ he said indecisively

Related Terms

  • Antonym: decisively - in a way that shows the ability to make decisions quickly and confidently

Etymology

From indecisive + -ly; 'indecisive' from Latin 'indecisus' (not decided) + English suffix '-ly'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00300164-r
Synset POS: r
