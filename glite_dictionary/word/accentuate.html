to accentuate

1. VERB

/ ækˈsɛntʃuˌeɪt / (AmE), / əkˈsɛntjʊˌeɪt / (BrE)
to emphasize or put stress on a certain syllable or word when speaking or pronouncing
emphasize or stress in speech

Examples

When learning Spanish, you must accentuate the correct syllable to sound natural.
The teacher asked us to accentuate the first syllable of each word in that exercise.
Some languages accentuate different parts of words, altering their meaning.

Synset Examples

In Farsi, you accent the last syllable of each word

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to emphasize - to give special importance or prominence to something
  • Synonym: stress - to give particular emphasis or attention to a syllable or word in speech

Etymology

From Medieval Latin accentuatus, past participle of accentuare (to accent), from Latin accentus (accent).

2. VERB

/ ækˈsɛntʃuˌeɪt / (AmE), / ækˈsɛntjʊeɪt / (BrE)
to make something more noticeable or prominent; to emphasize
emphasize, make more prominent

Examples

The lighting was designed to accentuate the beauty of the artwork on display.
She wore a belt to accentuate her waist.
This report will accentuate the importance of sustainable practices in business.

Synset Examples

Dr. Jones emphasizes exercise in addition to a change in diet

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to emphasize - to give special importance or prominence to something
  • Antonym: to downplay - to make something appear less important

Etymology

From Latin accentuatus, past participle of accentuare (to accent), from accentus (accent).
