oxime

1. NOUN

/ ˈɑkˌsaɪm / (AmE), / ˈɒksaɪm / (BrE)
a chemical compound containing the -C=NOH group, formed by the reaction of an aldehyde or a ketone with hydroxylamine
compound with -C=NOH group
medical

Examples

The chemist synthesized an oxime from acetone and hydroxylamine in the laboratory.
Detection of an oxime group in the sample confirmed the expected reaction had occurred.
Some medications act as oxime derivatives to reverse the effects of nerve agents.

Etymology

From German Oxim, from 'Ox' (oxygen) + 'Imin' (imine).
