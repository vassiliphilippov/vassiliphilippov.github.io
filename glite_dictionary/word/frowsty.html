frowsty

1. ADJ

/ ˈfraʊsti / (AmE), / ˈfraʊsti / (BrE)
(of a place or atmosphere) smelling stale and stuffy; having an unpleasant, unclean smell.
stale and stuffy-smelling
british

Examples

The air in the basement was frowsty after months without ventilation.
She opened the windows to dispel the frowsty smell from the old classroom.
Their coats picked up a frowsty scent after being stored in the damp hallway.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: musty - smelling damp, moldy, or stale
  • Antonym: fresh - clean or recently made, opposite of stale

Etymology

Origin unknown; first recorded in the late 19th century, chiefly British.
