disapprobation

1. NOUN

/ ˌdɪsˌæprəˈbeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪsˌæprəˈbeɪʃən / (BrE)
Strong disapproval, especially on moral grounds; the act of expressing or feeling condemnation.
strong moral disapproval

Examples

The committee expressed its disapprobation of the policy in a formal statement.
His actions attracted widespread disapprobation from the community.
She spoke with evident disapprobation about their reckless behavior.

Synset Examples

his uncompromising condemnation of racism

Related Terms

  • Synonym: condemnation - the expression of very strong disapproval
  • Antonym: approval - the belief that something is good or acceptable

Etymology

From Latin disapprobatio, from dis- (not) + approbatio (approval).
