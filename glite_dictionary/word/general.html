general

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɛnɚəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnərəl / (BrE)
the head of a religious order or congregation, especially in the Roman Catholic Church
head of a religious order

Examples

The Jesuit general addressed the congregation in Rome.
After being elected general, she implemented reforms within the order.
The general of the order oversees many communities worldwide.

Etymology

From Latin generalis (relating to all), later used as a title in religious orders.
