Schlumbergera gaertneri

1. NOUN

/ ˌʃlʌm.bərˈɡɛr.ə ɡɑrtˈnɛr.aɪ / (AmE), / ˌʃlʌm.bəˈɡɜː.rə ɡɑːtˈnɛr.i / (BrE)
A species of spring-blooming South American cactus with oblong segments and coral-red flowers, often cultivated as a houseplant.
spring-blooming cactus with coral-red flowers

Examples

The Schlumbergera gaertneri produces striking coral-red flowers in early spring.
Many gardeners keep Schlumbergera gaertneri as a decorative houseplant for its colorful blooms.
Unlike other cacti, Schlumbergera gaertneri prefers cooler temperatures and indirect sunlight.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Easter cactus - common name for Schlumbergera gaertneri

Etymology

Named after botanist Frederick Schlumberger and German botanist Joseph Gaertner.
