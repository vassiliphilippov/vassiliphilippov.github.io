diagram

1. VERB

/ ˈdaɪ.əˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈdaɪ.əɡræm / (BrE)
to represent something by means of a diagram, especially to make a schematic or technical drawing that shows how it works or is constructed
to make a schematic drawing

Examples

The engineer diagrammed the new circuit on the whiteboard.
She will diagram the molecular structure for the class tomorrow.
After reviewing the process, we diagrammed each step to identify potential improvements.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to illustrate - to make something clear by drawing or explaining with pictures

Etymology

From Greek 'diagramma' (geometric figure), from 'diagraphein' (to mark out by lines).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01697348-v
Synset POS: v

2. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪəˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəɡræm / (BrE)
A simple drawing or plan that explains how something works or shows the relationship between different parts of something.
drawing explaining parts or workings

Examples

The teacher drew a diagram on the board to show the process of photosynthesis.
This electrical diagram illustrates how the circuit should be connected.
By studying the diagram, I finally understood how the machine operated.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: chart - a sheet exhibiting information in tabular form
  • Synonym: figure - a diagram or illustration in a book or article
  • Synonym: sketch - a rough or unfinished drawing or painting

Etymology

From Greek 'diagramma', meaning 'that which is marked out by lines', from 'diagraphein' (to mark out by lines).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03191083-n
Synset POS: n
