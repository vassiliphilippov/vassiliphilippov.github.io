unreasonable

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈriːzənəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈriːzənəbl / (BrE)
(of actions, demands, or amounts) excessive or beyond what is normal or acceptable.
excessive; beyond normal limits

Examples

The rent increase was unreasonable given the state of the property.
She found his requests completely unreasonable and refused to comply.
They were charged an unreasonable amount for basic services.

Synset Examples

excessive charges
a book of inordinate length
his dress stops just short of undue elegance
unreasonable demands

Related Terms

  • Synonym: excessive - more than is necessary, normal, or desirable
  • Antonym: moderate - kept within reasonable limits; not excessive

Etymology

From un- (not) + reasonable, from Old French raisonable, from Latin rationabilis.

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈɹiːzənəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈɹiːzənəbl̩ / (BrE)
not guided by or based on good sense; not fair or acceptable; excessive or inappropriate
not fair, excessive, or not sensible

Examples

It would be unreasonable to expect her to finish all that work in one day.
He made an unreasonable demand for immediate payment.
Some landlords set unreasonable rents that tenants cannot afford.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: reasonable - having sound judgment; fair and sensible
  • Synonym: irrational - not logical or reasonable

Etymology

From un- (not) + reasonable, from Latin rationabilis (reasonable).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01951277-a
Synset POS: a
