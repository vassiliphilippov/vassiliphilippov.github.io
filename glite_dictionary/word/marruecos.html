Marruecos

1. PROPN

/ maˈrwe.kos / (AmE), / maˈrwe.kɒs / (BrE)
the Spanish name for Morocco, a country in northwestern Africa with a largely Muslim population, which gained independence from France in 1956
Spanish name for Morocco (country)

Examples

She traveled to Marruecos last summer to learn about its culture and history.
The official language of Marruecos is Arabic, but many people also speak French and Spanish.
Tourists are often attracted to Marruecos for its vibrant markets and beautiful architecture.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Morocco - a country in northwestern Africa with a largely Muslim population

Etymology

Spanish form of the Arabic name for Morocco, al-Maghrib.
