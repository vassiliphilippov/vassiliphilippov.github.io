logagraphia

1. NOUN

/ ˌlɔɡəˈɡræfiə / (AmE), / ˌlɒɡəˈɡræfɪə / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by the loss of the ability to write or to express thoughts in writing due to a brain lesion.
loss of ability to write (medical)
medical

Examples

The neurologist diagnosed the patient with logagraphia after observing her difficulty in expressing thoughts through writing.
Following his stroke, he struggled with logagraphia, making written communication nearly impossible.
Research into logagraphia has improved our understanding of how brain injuries affect language abilities.

Etymology

From Greek 'logos' (word) and 'graphein' (to write), with -ia denoting a condition.
