unwanted

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈwɑːntɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈwɒntɪd / (BrE)
not wanted or desired; regarded as undesirable or unnecessary
not wanted; undesirable

Examples

The old building had become an unwanted eyesore in the neighborhood.
She received several unwanted phone calls after her number was leaked.
They donated their unwanted clothes to charity at the end of the season.

Synset Examples

undesirable impurities in steel
legislation excluding undesirable aliens
removed the unwanted vegetation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: undesirable - not wanted or lacking pleasing qualities
  • Antonym: desired - wanted or wished for

Etymology

From prefix 'un-' (not) + 'wanted' (past participle of want).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈwɑːntɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈwɒntɪd / (BrE)
not desired or needed; not wanted by anyone
not wanted or needed

Examples

The shelter was seeking homes for several unwanted dogs.
He threw away unwanted mail without opening it.
Many teenagers feel unwanted at some point in their lives.

Synset Examples

tried to give away unwanted kittens

Related Terms

  • Antonym: desired - wanted or wished for
  • Antonym: welcome - received gladly or with pleasure
  • Synonym: superfluous - unnecessary, especially through being more than enough

Etymology

From 'un-' (not) + 'wanted', the past participle of 'want'.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02537893-a
Synset POS: a
