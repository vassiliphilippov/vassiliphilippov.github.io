unsullied

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsʌlid / (AmE), / ʌnˈsʌlid / (BrE)
not spoiled, stained, or made less pure; spotlessly clean or untainted.
not spoiled or stained; perfectly clean

Examples

The hero's reputation remained unsullied despite numerous rumors and accusations.
Her white dress was still unsullied after a long evening at the party.
They admired the unsullied beauty of the untouched mountain lake.

Synset Examples

the unsullied snow of mountains

Related Terms

  • Antonym: sullied - spoiled or stained; no longer pure
  • Synonym: pristine - clean and fresh as if new; unspoiled

Etymology

From un- (not) + sully, after French souiller (to soil), from Latin suillare (to make dirty).

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈsʌlid / (AmE), / ʌnˈsʌlid / (BrE)
not spoiled, stained, or damaged; completely pure or untarnished, especially referring to a reputation or character.
not spoiled or tarnished, especially a reputation

Examples

Despite years in politics, her unsullied reputation remained intact.
The ancient artifact was remarkably unsullied by centuries of exposure.
He was known throughout the village for his unsullied honesty and integrity.

Synset Examples

his unsullied name
an untarnished reputation

Related Terms

  • Antonym: tarnished - damaged or stained, especially a reputation

Etymology

From the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'sullied', past participle of 'sully' (to stain or spoil).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00248114-s
Synset POS: s
