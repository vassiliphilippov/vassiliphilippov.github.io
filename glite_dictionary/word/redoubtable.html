redoubtable

1. ADJ

/ rɪˈdaʊtəbəl / (AmE), / rɪˈdaʊtəbəl / (BrE)
(of a person or thing) inspiring fear or respect through being impressively large, powerful, or capable.
inspiring fear or respect

Examples

He faced his redoubtable opponent without showing any sign of fear.
The general was known as a redoubtable figure on the battlefield.
Despite her small stature, she was a redoubtable leader who commanded respect.

Synset Examples

the formidable prospect of major surgery
a tougher and more redoubtable adversary than the heel-clicking, jackbooted fanatic
something unnerving and prisonlike about high grey wall

Related Terms

  • Synonym: formidable - inspiring fear or respect through being impressively large or powerful
  • Synonym: fearsome - inspiring fear; frightening

Etymology

From Old French redoutable, from redouter (to dread), from Latin re- (again, intensifier) + dubitāre (to doubt, fear).

2. ADJ

/ rɪˈdaʊtəbəl / (AmE), / rɪˈdaʊtəbəl / (BrE)
inspiring fear or respect; worthy of respect or honor, often because of strength, skill, or impressive qualities
formidable or worthy of respect

Examples

She was a redoubtable opponent in the courtroom, respected by all her peers.
The general's redoubtable reputation made his enemies wary of confrontation.
He earned his position through redoubtable achievements and unwavering dedication.

Synset Examples
