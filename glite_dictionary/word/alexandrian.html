Alexandrian

1. NOUN

/ ˌælɪɡˈzændriən / (AmE), / ˌalɪɡˈzandriən / (BrE)
a person who is a native or inhabitant of Alexandria, especially the city in Egypt
native or resident of Alexandria, Egypt

Examples

Many Alexandrians still celebrate the city's ancient traditions.
The museum featured artifacts discovered by Alexandrians in the 19th century.
She is proud to be an Alexandrian, with a deep connection to the city's rich history.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Egyptian - a native or inhabitant of Egypt

Etymology

From Alexandria, the name of the Egyptian city founded by Alexander the Great, plus -an (denoting affiliation or origin).

2. ADJ

/ ˌælɪɡˈzændriən / (AmE), / ˌalɪɡˈzændrɪən / (BrE)
of or relating to Alexander the Great, his empire, or the period influenced by his rule.
relating to Alexander the Great or his empire

Examples

The Alexandrian conquest spread Greek culture throughout much of the ancient world.
Her research focused on Alexandrian influences in early Mediterranean art.
Many cities were renamed during the Alexandrian period.

Etymology

From Alexander (the Great) + -ian, denoting relation to Alexander the Great or his era.
