gamboge

1. NOUN

/ ɡæmˈboʊdʒ / (AmE), / ɡæmˈbəʊdʒ / (BrE)
a yellow gum resin obtained from various Asian trees, used as a pigment and formerly as a purgative
a yellow gum resin and pigment

Examples

The artist preferred to use gamboge for its rich yellow hue in his landscapes.
Traditionally, gamboge was also valued for its medicinal properties as a purgative.
The bright color of the tapestry was achieved by dyeing the fabric with gamboge.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: yellow ochre - an earth pigment containing iron oxide, used as a yellow pigment

Etymology

From Latin gambogium, from Cambodia, where it was first obtained.

2. NOUN

/ ɡæmˈboʊʒ / (AmE), / ɡæmˈbəʊʒ / (BrE)
a strong, deep yellow color, resembling the shade of the gum resin from which its name derives
strong yellow color

Examples

The artist chose gamboge for the sunflowers to make them stand out against the dark background.
The walls were painted a warm shade of gamboge, brightening the entire room.
Her dress, in brilliant gamboge, caught everyone's attention at the gala.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: yellow - a color like that of gold or the sun
  • Degree Higher: goldenrod - a deeper, more orangish yellow color

Etymology

From Latin 'gambogium', via French 'gomme-gutte', from Cambodian town 'Kampuchea' (now Cambodia), source of the resin.
