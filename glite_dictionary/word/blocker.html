blocker

1. NOUN

/ ˈblɑkər / (AmE), / ˈblɒkə / (BrE)
a football player whose main responsibility is to prevent defensive players from stopping an offensive play by blocking them
football player who blocks defenders

Examples

The left guard is a key blocker in every running play.
As a blocker, he protected the quarterback from the opposing defense.
The coach praised his blocker for opening up a path for the running back.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tackle - a player positioned on the offensive line in American football

2. NOUN

/ ˈblɑkər / (AmE), / ˈblɒkə / (BrE)
a drug or class of drugs that inhibits or blocks a specific biological process, especially by preventing certain substances from acting in the body
a drug that blocks a biological process
medical

Examples

The doctor prescribed a blocker to help manage her high blood pressure.
Many allergies are treated with histamine blockers to reduce symptoms.
Beta blockers are often used to slow the heart rate in cardiac patients.

Etymology

From block + -er, indicating an agent that blocks.
