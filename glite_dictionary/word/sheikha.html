sheikha

1. NOUN

/ ʃeɪˈkɑ / (AmE), / ʃeɪˈkɑː / (BrE)
the wife or female counterpart of a sheik in Arab society, often holding a position of respect
wife or female counterpart of a sheik

Examples

The sheikha attended the cultural festival alongside her husband, the sheik.
In Dubai, the sheikha is known for her philanthropic work.
Several journalists interviewed the sheikha about her initiatives to support education in her community.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sheik - an Arab leader or elder

Etymology

From Arabic شيخة (*shaykha*), feminine of شيخ (*sheikh*).
