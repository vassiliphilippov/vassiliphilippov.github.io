importunate

1. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɔɹ.tʃən.ət / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɔː.tʃən.ət / (BrE)
persistently and annoyingly demanding or insistent, especially in making requests or appeals
persistently demanding or insistent

Examples

The importunate salesman called her office every day until she agreed to a meeting.
She grew tired of her friend's importunate requests for money.
His importunate questioning made everyone uncomfortable.

Synset Examples

an importunate job applicant

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insistent - refusing to be discouraged; persistent in a demand
  • Synonym: persistent - continuing firmly or obstinately in a course of action

Etymology

From Latin 'importunus' (inconvenient, troublesome).

2. ADJ

/ ɪmˈpɔrtʃənət / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɔːtʃənət / (BrE)
Persistent in making repeated requests or demands, often to the point of being annoying or intrusive.
persistently demanding; annoyingly insistent

Examples

The salesperson was so importunate that I eventually left the store.
She found her colleague's importunate questions increasingly tiresome.
Despite my refusals, he remained importunate in asking for an answer.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: insistent - demanding attention; persistent
  • Synonym: unrelenting - not yielding in strength, severity, or determination
  • Antonym: polite - showing good manners or respect

Etymology

From Latin 'importunus' (inconvenient, troublesome), via Old French and late Middle English.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-92266961-a
Synset POS: a
