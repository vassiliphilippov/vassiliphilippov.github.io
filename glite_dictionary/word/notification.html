notification

1. NOUN

/ ˌnoʊtɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnəʊtɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a formal request for payment, especially one given in writing to remind someone of a debt or obligation
formal request for payment

Examples

The company sent a notification of overdue payment to the client.
After receiving the notification, he settled his outstanding balance immediately.
A second notification was issued when the first one was ignored.

Synset Examples

the notification stated the grace period and the penalties for defaulting

Related Terms

  • Synonym: demand - an insistent and formal request for payment of a debt
  • Synonym: reminder - something that causes someone to remember a payment is due

Etymology

From Medieval Latin notificatio(n-), from Latin notificare ‘make known’.

Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ ˌnoʊ.tə.fəˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌnəʊ.tɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of formally informing someone or giving notice of something; a message that communicates information or a warning
formal act of informing; message of notice

Examples

You will receive a notification by email once your order has been shipped.
She posted a notification on the bulletin board to alert everyone about the meeting.
The app sends a push notification every time you get a new message.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: announcement - a formal public statement about a fact, occurrence, or intention
  • Degree Lower: warning - a statement or event that indicates a possible or impending danger, problem, or other unpleasant situation

Etymology

From Medieval Latin notificatio (announcement), from notificare (to make known).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-07227084-n
Synset POS: n
