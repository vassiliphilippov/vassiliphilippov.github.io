Emma Willard

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛm.ə ˈwɪl.ərd / (AmE), / ˈɛmə ˈwɪləd / (BrE)
(1787–1870) United States educator and early advocate for women's higher education.
US educator, campaigner for women's higher education

Examples

Emma Willard founded the Troy Female Seminary, one of the first higher education institutions for women in the United States.
Historians often credit Emma Willard with paving the way for educational reforms that benefited women nationwide.
During her lifetime, Emma Willard wrote numerous books advocating for improvements in women's education.
