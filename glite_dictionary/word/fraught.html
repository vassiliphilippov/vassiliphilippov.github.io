fraught

1. ADJ

/ frɔt / (AmE), / frɔːt / (BrE)
(of a situation or thing) filled with or destined to result in something undesirable; attended with a specified quality.
filled with (especially something undesirable)

Examples

The expedition was fraught with dangers they had not anticipated.
Negotiations became increasingly fraught as the deadline approached.
Her voice was fraught with emotion when she delivered the news.

Synset Examples

words fraught with meaning
an incident fraught with danger
a silence pregnant with suspense

Related Terms

  • Synonym: laden - heavily loaded or weighed down
  • Antonym: free from - not containing or accompanied by (something undesirable)

Etymology

From Middle English, from Old English *fraht* (cargo), related to freight.

2. ADJ

/ frɔt / (AmE), / frɔːt / (BrE)
(of a situation or relationship) full of anxiety or distress; causing or characterized by tension or worry.
full of distress or tension

Examples

The discussions became increasingly fraught as the deadline approached.
Their marriage was often fraught with misunderstandings and arguments.
Negotiations between the two countries have been fraught since the incident.

Synset Examples

a fraught mother-daughter relationship

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tense - (of a situation) full of nervousness or anxiety
  • Antonym: calm - (of a situation) not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other strong emotions

Etymology

From Middle English fraught (freighted, laden), from Old English frahtian (to load), related to freight.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02467160-s
Synset POS: s
