shapelessness

1. NOUN

/ ˈʃeɪpləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪplɪsnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of lacking a distinct or definite shape; being amorphous or formless.
the state of being without a definite shape

Examples

The heavy fog added an eerie shapelessness to the landscape.
The artist's sculpture conveyed a sense of shapelessness and fluidity.
He was frustrated by the shapelessness of his ideas and struggled to bring them into focus.

Synset Examples

Despite its grotesque gargoyle shapelessness it was, of course, adorable

Related Terms

  • Synonym: formlessness - lack of structure or defined form
  • Synonym: amorphousness - the quality of being without a clear shape or form

Etymology

From 'shapeless' + '-ness', forming nouns of quality or state.

2. NOUN

/ ˈʃeɪpləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪplɪsnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of lacking a defined or aesthetically pleasing shape
lack of a defined or pleasing shape

Examples

The sculpture's shapelessness challenged traditional notions of beauty.
Many criticized the building for its shapelessness and lack of character.
Over time, the old sofa had sunk into utter shapelessness.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: formlessness - the quality of lacking shape or structure
  • Antonym: gracefulness - the quality of having an attractive or pleasing shape

Etymology

From 'shapeless' (without shape) + '-ness' (suffix forming nouns indicating a state or quality).
