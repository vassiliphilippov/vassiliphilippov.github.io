concentric

1. ADJ

/ kənˈsɛntrɪk / (AmE), / kənˈsɛn.trɪk / (BrE)
(of circles or other shapes) having a common center.
having a common center

Examples

The artist drew several concentric circles on the canvas for the design.
Tree trunks often reveal concentric rings that show their age.
Traffic moved smoothly around the city's concentric ring roads.

Synset Examples

concentric rings

Related Terms

  • Antonym: eccentric - not having the same center; not concentric

Etymology

From Latin 'concentricus' (having a common center), from 'com-' (together) + 'centrum' (center).
