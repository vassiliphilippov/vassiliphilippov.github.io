sudatorium

1. NOUN

/ suːdəˈtɔːriəm / (AmE), / suːdəˈtɔːrɪəm / (BrE)
A room or structure, especially in ancient Roman bathhouses, designed for taking hot air or steam baths.
Roman steam or hot air bath room

Examples

The ancient Romans built a sudatorium as part of their elaborate public bath complexes.
During the tour, the guide explained the purpose of the sudatorium and its role in Roman hygiene.
Archaeologists uncovered the remains of a sudatorium beneath the villa's foundation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: caldarium - hot-water room in a Roman bathhouse

Etymology

From Latin sudatorium, from sudare (to sweat).
