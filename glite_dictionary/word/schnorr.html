to schnorr

1. VERB

/ ʃnɔr / (AmE), / ʃnɔː / (BrE)
to try to obtain something, especially money or goods, by begging or freeloading; to cadge or wheedle.
obtain by cadging or wheedling
informal

Examples

Whenever we go out, he tries to schnorr drinks from everyone at the bar.
She managed to schnorr a ride to the concert by promising snacks.
They were tired of him always schnorring cigarettes instead of buying his own.

Synset Examples

he is always shnorring cigarettes from his friends

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cadge - to obtain something by begging
  • Synonym: mooch - to obtain something without paying or permission, by freeloading

Etymology

From Yiddish *shnoren* (to beg), ultimately from German *schnorren* (to beg).
