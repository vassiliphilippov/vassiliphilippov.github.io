declivitous

1. ADJ

/ dɪˈklɪvɪtəs / (AmE), / dɪˈklɪvɪtəs / (BrE)
sloping downward steeply; having a fairly steep downward inclination.
steeply sloping downward

Examples

The path wound down a declivitous hillside to the riverbank.
Hikers navigated the declivitous trails with caution after the rainstorm.
The old house was built on a declivitous slope overlooking the valley.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: steep - having a sharp inclination or slope
  • Synonym: precipitous - extremely steep

Etymology

From Latin declivis (sloping downward) + English -ous.
