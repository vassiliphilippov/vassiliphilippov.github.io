Zea mays everta

1. NOUN

/ zi ə ˈmeɪz ɪˈvɜrtə / (AmE), / ziːə ˈmeɪz ɪˈvɜːtə / (BrE)
A type of maize with small ears and kernels that burst open and expand when heated, commonly known as popcorn.
a maize variety used for popcorn

Examples

Zea mays everta kernels are specially grown for making popcorn.
Scientists study Zea mays everta to understand why its kernels pop when heated.
Farmers often cultivate Zea mays everta because of its popularity as a snack.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: popcorn - popular snack food made from puffed corn kernels

Etymology

From scientific Latin: Zea (genus of grasses, from Greek 'zeia'), mays (maize), everta (Latin 'evertō' meaning 'to burst out').
