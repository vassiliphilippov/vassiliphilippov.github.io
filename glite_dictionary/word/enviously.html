enviously

1. ADV

/ ˈɛnviəsli / (AmE), / ˈɛnviəsli / (BrE)
In a way that shows envy or jealousy of someone else's possessions, qualities, or achievements.
in an envious or jealous manner

Examples

She glanced enviously at her colleague's promotion certificate.
The children stared enviously at the chocolate cake in the window.
He spoke enviously about his neighbor's success.

Synset Examples

he looked at his friend's new car jealously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: jealously - in a jealous manner; with envy

Etymology

From 'envious' + '-ly'; 'envious' is from Old French *envieus*, from Latin *invidiosus*, from *invidia* (envy).
