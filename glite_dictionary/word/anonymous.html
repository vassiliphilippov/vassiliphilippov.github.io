anonymous

1. ADJ

/ əˈnɑːnəməs / (AmE), / əˈnɒnɪməs / (BrE)
(of a person or thing) having a name, identity, or source that is not known or made public.
having unknown name or identity

Examples

The report was submitted by an anonymous source who did not want to be identified.
She received an anonymous letter containing helpful advice.
An anonymous donor contributed a large sum to the charity.

Synset Examples

anonymous authors
anonymous donors
an anonymous gift

Related Terms

  • Synonym: nameless - lacking a known name or identity
  • Antonym: identified - having an identity or source that is known or recognized

Etymology

From Greek anonymos, from an- (without) + onyma (name).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00121384-a
Synset POS: a

2. ADJ

/ əˈnɑːnəməs / (AmE), / əˈnɒnɪməs / (BrE)
without any name acknowledged, as that of the author, contributor, etc.; having an unknown or withheld identity or source.
of unknown name or identity

Examples

The college received a large donation from an anonymous benefactor.
Several anonymous letters were sent to the newspaper office.
Police often receive tips from anonymous callers.

Synset Examples

anonymous authors
anonymous donors
an anonymous gift

Related Terms

  • Synonym: unnamed - not having a known or identified name
  • Antonym: identified - having a known name or identity

Etymology

From Greek anonymos, from an- (without) + onyma (name).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00121384-a
Synset POS: a

3. ADJ

/ əˈnɑːnɪməs / (AmE), / əˈnɒnɪməs / (BrE)
Lacking distinctive features or individuality; not easily identified or distinguished.
lacking individuality or distinct features

Examples

Rows of anonymous buildings lined the industrial estate.
The décor of the hotel lobby was deliberately anonymous to appeal to all tastes.
He worked as an anonymous bureaucrat for decades, never standing out from his colleagues.

Synset Examples

brown anonymous houses
anonymous bureaucrats in the Civil Service

Related Terms

  • Synonym: featureless - having no distinct features or characteristics
  • Antonym: distinctive - having a quality or characteristic that makes something different and easily noticed

Etymology

From Greek anonymos (nameless), from an- (without) + onyma (name).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00238244-s
Synset POS: s
