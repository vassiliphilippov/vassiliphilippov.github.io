jiggle

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʒɪɡəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪɡəl / (BrE)
a slight, irregular shaking or wobbling movement
slight irregular shaking motion

Examples

The jiggle of the camera made the image blurr slightly.
She noticed a jiggle in the pudding as she set it on the table.
There was a slight jiggle in his walk after the long car ride.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shake - to move back and forth or up and down with quick movements
  • Synonym: wobble - an unsteady or rocking motion

Etymology

From the verb 'jiggle,' a frequentative of 'jig.'

2. VERB

/ ˈdʒɪɡəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪɡl̩ / (BrE)
to move lightly or quickly up and down or to and fro in short, rapid movements
move quickly back and forth

Examples

Please don't jiggle the table while I'm writing.
She jiggled the handle to get the door open.
The coins jiggled in his pocket as he walked.

Synset Examples

Don't jiggle your finger while the nurse is putting on the bandage!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shake - to move (something) up and down or from side to side with quick movements
  • Synonym: wiggle - to move or cause to move up and down or from side to side with small quick movements

Etymology

Probably a frequentative formation from 'jog' or 'joggle', influenced by similar-sounding words.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01902275-v
Synset POS: v
