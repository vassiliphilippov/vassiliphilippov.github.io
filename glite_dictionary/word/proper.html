proper

1. ADJ

/ ˈprɑːpɚ / (AmE), / ˈprɒpə / (BrE)
(of a place or thing) referring only to the thing itself, not including surrounding or related areas; strictly limited to what is specified.
strictly limited to what is specified

Examples

She lives in the city proper, not in the suburbs.
Only members of the organization proper are allowed to vote.
The castle proper is open to visitors, but the grounds are private.

Synset Examples

the city proper
his claim is connected with the deed proper

Etymology

From Latin 'proprius' (one's own, special).

WordNet ID: oewn-01108582-s
Synset POS: s

2. ADJ

/ ˈprɑːpər / (AmE), / ˈprɒpə / (BrE)
suitable or appropriate for a particular purpose, situation, or person.
suitable or appropriate

Examples

It is important to wear proper safety equipment when working with chemicals.
Make sure you address the letter to the proper department to avoid delays.
He waited for the proper moment to ask his question.

Synset Examples

everything in its proper place
the right man for the job
she is not suitable for the position

Related Terms

  • Synonym: suitable - appropriate for a particular person, purpose, or situation
  • Antonym: inappropriate - not suitable or fitting for a particular situation or purpose

Etymology

From Latin 'proprius' (one's own, special).

3. ADJ

/ ˈprɑːpər / (AmE), / ˈprɒpə / (BrE)
suitable or appropriate for a particular purpose or situation; correct according to social or moral standards.
appropriate; suitable; correct

Examples

It is important to wash your hands before eating to maintain proper hygiene.
Wearing a suit and tie is considered proper attire for the ceremony.
They received proper training before starting their new jobs.

Synset Examples

proper medical treatment
proper manners

Related Terms

  • Antonym: improper - not suitable or appropriate; not correct
  • Synonym: appropriate - suitable or fitting for a particular purpose

Etymology

From Latin proprius (one's own, special, proper).

4. ADJ

/ ˈprɑːpɚ / (AmE), / ˈprɒpə / (BrE)
having all the qualities that are usual or expected for a particular type of thing; real or full in the way that is considered normal or correct
real, full, or typical; as expected

Examples

After weeks of snacking, he finally enjoyed a proper meal.
This is the first proper holiday I've had in years.
You should wear a proper coat if it's cold outside.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: improper - not according to accepted standards or rules; not proper
  • Synonym: real - actually existing or happening and not imagined or supposed

From Latin 'proprius' (one's own, particular).
