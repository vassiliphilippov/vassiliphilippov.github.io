eleventy

1. NOUN

/ ɪˈlɛvən(t)i / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛv(ə)nti / (BrE)
a fictitious or exaggerated number, often used humorously to suggest a very large or indefinite quantity
humorous exaggerated number

Examples

She told me the story eleventy times before I finally believed her.
The repairs cost eleventy dollars, according to his joke.
He gave me eleventy reasons why he couldn't attend the meeting.

Synset Examples

He claimed to have eleventy excuses for being late.
She was asked eleventy times to clean her room.

Etymology

Humorous formation based on 'eleven' and patterns of English numerals, popularized by J.R.R. Tolkien.
