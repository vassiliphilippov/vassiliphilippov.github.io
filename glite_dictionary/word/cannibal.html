cannibal

1. NOUN

/ ˈkæn.ɪ.bəl / (AmE), / ˈkæn.ɪ.bəl / (BrE)
a person who eats the flesh of other human beings
person who eats human flesh

Examples

The story described a tribe whose leader was a notorious cannibal feared by everyone.
Survivors of the crash were accused of turning to cannibal practices out of desperation.
The film features a fictional society ruled by cannibals living in isolation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anthropophage - a person who eats human flesh

Etymology

From Spanish 'caníbal', originally from Taíno 'Caniba', referring to the Carib people.
