plagiarization

1. NOUN

/ ˌpleɪdʒəriˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpleɪdʒərɪˈzeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the act of copying someone else's work or ideas and presenting them as your own without proper acknowledgment
the act of copying others' work as your own

Examples

The university has strict policies to prevent plagiarization in academic papers.
He was accused of plagiarization after submitting a copied essay.
Detecting plagiarization in research articles is an important part of the peer review process.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: plagiarism - the practice of taking someone else's work or ideas and passing them off as one's own
  • Antonym: originality - the quality of being novel or unique, not derived from something else

Etymology

From Latin 'plagiarius' (kidnapper) via 'plagiarize' + '-ation'.
