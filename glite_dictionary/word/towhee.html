towhee

1. NOUN

/ ˈtoʊˌhi / (AmE), / ˈtəʊˌhiː / (BrE)
A North American songbird of the finch family, typically with a long tail and often distinctive coloring.
North American songbird with a long tail

Examples

A towhee scratched among the leaves in search of food.
We spotted a spotted towhee perched on a low branch.
The distinctive call of the towhee can be heard in wooded areas.

Etymology

From the onomatopoeic call of the bird, imitated as 'towhee'.
