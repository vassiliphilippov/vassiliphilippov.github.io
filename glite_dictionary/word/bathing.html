bathing

1. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪðɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbeɪðɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of washing yourself or someone else, especially in water
washing oneself or another

Examples

Daily bathing is important for good hygiene.
The nurse assisted with the patient's bathing this morning.
Many cultures have special rituals or customs around bathing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bathing - immersing oneself in water for pleasure, not just washing

Etymology

From the verb 'bathe', from Old English bathian (to wash in a bath).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbeɪðɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɛɪðɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of immersing the body in water or exposing it to sunshine, typically for cleansing, relaxation, or health reasons
immersing body in water or sunshine

Examples

Bathing in the cool lake was the perfect way to end the hot summer day.
Daily bathing is important for personal hygiene and health.
Many people enjoy bathing in the sun during their holidays at the beach.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: swimming - moving through water by bodily motion
  • Synonym: sunbathing - lying in the sun for pleasure or health

Etymology

From verb 'bathe', from Old English 'baþian' (to wash, bathe).
