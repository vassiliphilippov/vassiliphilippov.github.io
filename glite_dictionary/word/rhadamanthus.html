Rhadamanthus

1. PROPN

/ ˌrædəˈmænθəs / (AmE), / ˌrædəˈmænθəs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, one of the judges of the dead in the underworld, famed for his justice.
mythological judge of the dead

Examples

In ancient tales, Rhadamanthus was renowned for his stern and fair judgments.
Many sculptures depict Rhadamanthus seated alongside other judges in the underworld.
The name Rhadamanthus has become synonymous with impartial justice.

Etymology

From Ancient Greek Ῥαδάμανθυς (Rhadámanthys), a mythological figure.
