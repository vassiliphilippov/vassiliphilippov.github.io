decadent

1. ADJ

/ ˈdɛkədənt / (AmE), / ˈdɛkədənt / (BrE)
characterized by excessive self-indulgence and moral deterioration; marked by decline or decay, especially as a result of luxury or self-indulgence
excessively self-indulgent and morally decaying

Examples

The novelist chronicled the decadent lifestyle of wealthy socialites in 1920s Paris.
Critics argue that the city's decadent practices led to its eventual downfall.
They threw a decadent party filled with opulent decorations and extravagant food.

Synset Examples

a decadent life of excessive money and no sense of responsibility

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-indulgent - characterized by doing exactly what one wants, especially when this involves pleasure or idleness; synonym for indulgence in pleasure
  • Synonym: depraved - morally corrupt or wicked, often associated with moral decay
  • Antonym: virtuous - having or showing high moral standards; opposite of morally decaying

Etymology

From French *décadent*, from Latin *decadere* (to fall down, decay).

2. NOUN

/ ˈdɛk.ə.dənt / (AmE), / ˈdɛk.ə.dənt / (BrE)
a person who is considered to be excessively self-indulgent, especially in the pursuit of pleasure, or morally or artistically declined
person indulging in moral or artistic self-indulgence

Examples

In the Parisian salons, the poet was viewed as a true decadent for his extravagant tastes and disregard for convention.
Early critics accused the novelist of being a decadent obsessed only with pleasure and surface beauty.
The movement was led by a small group of decadents who rejected traditional values in favor of art for art's sake.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: aesthete - a person who has or professes to have refined sensitivity toward the beauties of art or nature

Etymology

From French décadent, from décadence (decline), ultimately from Latin decadentia.

3. ADJ

/ ˈdɛkədənt / (AmE), / ˈdɛkədənt / (BrE)
characterized by or appealing to luxury and self-indulgence; marked by excessive pleasure or extravagance
luxuriously self-indulgent or extravagant

Examples

The hotel's decadent brunch buffet featured oysters, champagne, and gourmet desserts.
She treated herself to a decadent chocolate cake after a long week at work.
They enjoyed a decadent weekend getaway at a luxury spa resort.

Synset Examples

I spent a decadent day at a spa

Related Terms

  • Synonym: luxurious - characterized by luxury; rich and comfortable
  • Antonym: austere - severely simple or lacking luxury

Etymology

From French décadent, from Latin decadere (to fall, decay)
