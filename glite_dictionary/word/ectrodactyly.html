ectrodactyly

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛk.troʊˈdæk.tə.li / (AmE), / ˌɛk.trəˈdæk.tɪ.li / (BrE)
a congenital abnormality in which some fingers or toes are absent
congenital absence of some fingers or toes
medical

Examples

The child was diagnosed with ectrodactyly shortly after birth.
Individuals with ectrodactyly may have difficulties with certain manual tasks.
Research into the genetic causes of ectrodactyly continues to advance.

Etymology

From Greek 'ektroma' (abortion, miscarriage) + 'dactylos' (finger, toe).
