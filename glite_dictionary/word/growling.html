growling

1. NOUN

/ ˈɡraʊlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊlɪŋ / (BrE)
a low, threatening sound made by animals, especially as a warning or sign of anger
animal's low, threatening sound

Examples

The growling of the dog warned us not to get too close.
She was startled by the low growling coming from the bushes.
The lion's growling echoed throughout the zoo.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: roaring - a loud, deep sound made by animals such as lions
  • Degree Lower: murmuring - a soft, low sound, often indistinct

2. NOUN

/ ˈɡraʊlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊlɪŋ / (BrE)
a low, gruff, or angry sound like that made by an animal, often expressing displeasure or aggression
a gruff or angry growl-like sound

Examples

A low growling came from the back of the darkened room, making everyone uneasy.
He responded to the criticism with a growling that silenced the room.
The growling of the guard dog kept intruders at bay throughout the night.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: growl - to make a low, guttural sound of anger or hostility
