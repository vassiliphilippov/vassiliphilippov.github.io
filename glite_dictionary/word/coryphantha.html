coryphantha

1. NOUN

/ ˌkɔːriˈfænθə / (AmE), / ˌkɒrɪˈfænθə / (BrE)
A cactus of the genus Coryphantha, native to North and Central America, known for its rounded stem and tubercles.
a genus of cactus

Examples

Many collectors value the coryphantha for its unusual tubercle patterns.
The coryphantha thrives in arid environments with plenty of sunlight.
I bought a rare coryphantha at the desert plant show last weekend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cactus - a plant belonging to the family Cactaceae with thick, fleshy stems

Etymology

From Greek 'koryphē' (summit, top), referring to the growth habits.
