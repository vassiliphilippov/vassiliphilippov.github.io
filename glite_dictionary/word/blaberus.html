Blaberus

1. NOUN

/ bləˈbɛr.əs / (AmE), / bləˈbɛə.rəs / (BrE)
A genus of large cockroaches native to Central and South America, known for their considerable size and used in scientific research and as pets.
genus of giant cockroaches

Examples

The Blaberus genus includes some of the largest cockroaches in the world.
Researchers often use Blaberus species to study insect physiology.
Some pet owners keep Blaberus roaches because of their impressive size and calm demeanor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cockroach - a large, brown or black insect that lives in warm houses and buildings

Etymology

From New Latin, genus name used in scientific classification.
