Pastinaca sativa

1. NOUN

/ pæs.tɪˈneɪ.kə səˈtiː.və / (AmE), / pas.tɪˈneɪ.kə səˈtɪ.və / (BrE)
A strong-scented plant cultivated for its edible long, white root; commonly known as the parsnip.
species of plant; parsnip

Examples

Pastinaca sativa is grown in many gardens for its sweet, earthy-flavored root.
The edible root of Pastinaca sativa can be roasted, boiled, or mashed like potatoes.
Farmers reported a particularly good crop of Pastinaca sativa this year due to favorable weather conditions.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: parsnip - the edible root of Pastinaca sativa

Etymology

From Latin *pastinaca* (parsnip) and *sativa* (cultivated).
