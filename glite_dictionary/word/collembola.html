Collembola

1. NOUN

/ kəˈlɛmbələ / (AmE), / kɒˈlɛmbələ / (BrE)
A group of minute, wingless arthropods commonly known as springtails, found in soil and leaf litter.
group of minute, wingless arthropods (springtails)
medical

Examples

Collembola are plentiful in forest soils, where they help decompose organic matter.
Scientists often use Collembola to study soil health and micro-ecosystems.
The leaf litter was teeming with tiny Collembola bouncing around.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: springtail - small, wingless arthropod with a springing organ

Etymology

From Greek 'kollembolon' (springing instrument), from 'kolla' (glue) + 'embolē' (peg, stopper).

2. NOUN

/ kəˈlɛm.bə.lə / (AmE), / kɒˈlɛm.bələ / (BrE)
an order of minute, wingless arthropods commonly known as springtails, characterized by their ability to jump using a forked appendage
order of minute, wingless springtails
medical

Examples

The forest soil is teeming with Collembola, playing an essential role in decomposition.
Scientists examine specimens from the order Collembola to study soil health.
Unlike insects, Collembola are wingless and possess a unique jumping organ.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: springtail - minute, jumping arthropod of Collembola

Etymology

From New Latin, based on Greek 'kolla' (glue) + 'embolon' (peg), referring to their fused body parts.
