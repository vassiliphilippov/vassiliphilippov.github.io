ricegrass

1. NOUN

/ ˈraɪsˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈraɪsˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
any grass of the genus Oryzopsis, native to North America and known for its grain-like seeds and tolerance of arid environments
a type of wild grass (genus Oryzopsis)

Examples

The dunes along the coast are stabilized by native ricegrass.
Wild ricegrass can be an important food source for grazing animals in arid regions.
Botanists studied the distribution of ricegrass in the western United States.

Etymology

From 'rice' and 'grass', referring to its grain-like seeds.
