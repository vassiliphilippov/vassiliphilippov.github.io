Boletus edulis

1. NOUN

/ boʊˈliːtəs ˈɛdʒʊlɪs / (AmE), / bəʊˈliːtəs ˈɛdʒʊlɪs / (BrE)
A large, edible mushroom with a thick, bulbous stalk and a convex cap, highly prized as a choice edible fungus; also known as the porcini.
an edible mushroom; the porcini

Examples

The chef prepared a rich risotto with fresh Boletus edulis mushrooms.
Many foragers consider Boletus edulis a prized find in European woodlands.
Dried Boletus edulis adds an intense, earthy flavor to soups and stews.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: porcini - the common name for Boletus edulis, an edible mushroom

Etymology

From Latin: 'boletus' (mushroom) and 'edulis' (edible).
