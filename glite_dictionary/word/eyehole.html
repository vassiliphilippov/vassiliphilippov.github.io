eyehole

1. NOUN

/ ˈaɪˌhoʊl / (AmE), / ˈaɪˌhəʊl / (BrE)
a small hole, usually round and edged, made in cloth or leather to allow the passage of a cord, hook, or bar
hole in fabric for a cord or hook

Examples

She threaded the ribbon through each eyehole on her corset.
The leather belt's eyehole tore from repeated use.
Make sure the curtain rod fits easily into the eyehole.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: eyelet - a small hole with a reinforced edge, especially in fabric, for a lace or cord

Etymology

From 'eye' (hole resembling an eye) + 'hole'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈaɪˌhoʊl / (AmE), / ˈaɪˌhəʊl / (BrE)
a small hole in a door, mask, or other object through which one can look or peep
small hole for looking through

Examples

The old wooden door had an eyehole at eye level so you could see visitors.
She looked through the eyehole in her mask during the masquerade ball.
Steam escaped from the eyehole in the oven as the bread baked.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: peephole - a small hole in a door or wall for peeking through

Etymology

From ‘eye’ + ‘hole’, formed by compounding.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-03911327-n
Synset POS: n
