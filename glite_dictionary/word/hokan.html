Hokan

1. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊkən / (AmE), / ˈhəʊkən / (BrE)
A proposed family of Amerindian languages formerly spoken in California and parts of the southwestern United States and northwest Mexico.
family of Amerindian languages

Examples

Linguists have debated the classification of the Hokan language family for over a century.
Some Hokan languages are now extinct, while others are only sparsely documented.
The Hokan hypothesis connects languages spoken in California, Arizona, and northern Mexico.

Etymology

From Hokan, a term based on words meaning 'two' in several of the constituent languages.

2. NOUN

/ ˈhoʊkən / (AmE), / ˈhəʊkən / (BrE)
A member of any of a group of Native American peoples believed to have spoken related languages in western North America, or the family of languages itself.
member (or language family) of Native American peoples in western North America

Examples

The museum displayed artifacts from several Hokan cultures that once inhabited the region.
Some linguists continue to debate whether the Hokan languages have a common origin.
Though the Hokan language family is not widely spoken today, its influence is still present in local place names.

Etymology

Coined by linguists; probably based on a Native American word for 'no' used in several Hokan languages.
