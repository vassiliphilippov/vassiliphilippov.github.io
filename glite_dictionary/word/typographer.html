typographer

1. NOUN

/ taɪˈpɑgrəfər / (AmE), / taɪˈpɒɡrəfə / (BrE)
a person who arranges and designs type for printing or digital publication
person who arranges type for printing

Examples

As a skilled typographer, she paid close attention to the spacing and alignment of the letters.
Modern typographers often use digital tools to create visually striking layouts.
The typographer redesigned the book to make it more readable and attractive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: typesetter - a person who sets written material into type

Etymology

From Greek 'typos' (impression) + '-grapher' (one who writes or records).
