bittersweet

1. ADJ

/ ˌbɪt̬ɚˈswit / (AmE), / ˌbɪtəˈswiːt / (BrE)
Having a taste that is a combination of bitterness and sweetness.
tasting both bitter and sweet

Examples

The chocolate had a bittersweet flavor that lingered on her tongue.
He savored the bittersweet taste of the orange peel candy.
This coffee is surprisingly bittersweet, balancing the dark roast with a hint of sugar.

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: sweet - Having the pleasant taste characteristic of sugar or honey.
  • Degree Lower: bitter - Having a sharp, pungent taste or smell; not sweet.

Etymology

Formed from the combination of 'bitter' and 'sweet'.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbɪtərˌswit / (AmE), / ˈbɪtəˌswiːt / (BrE)
a poisonous perennial vine (Solanum dulcamara) of the nightshade family, native to Eurasia and naturalized in North America, with violet flowers and oval red berries; also known as woody nightshade.
poisonous vine with violet flowers and red berries

Examples

The tangled stems of bittersweet climbed over the old wooden fence.
Children should avoid the bittersweet berries, as they are toxic if ingested.
Bittersweet is often found growing wild along riverbanks and hedgerows.

Etymology

From Middle English bitterswete, from the taste of the plant's roots, being at first bitter and then sweet.

3. NOUN

/ ˌbɪtərˈswit / (AmE), / ˌbɪtəˈswiːt / (BrE)
A climbing shrub native to North America, known for its yellow capsules that contain bright red seeds; often grown for ornamental purposes.
North American climbing shrub with yellow capsules and red seeds

Examples

The garden featured a cluster of bittersweet growing along the fence, their red seeds bright against the autumn leaves.
She used sprigs of bittersweet to decorate the mantelpiece for a rustic touch.
Wild birds often feed on the berries of bittersweet during the winter months.

Etymology

Refers to the slightly bitter yet sweet flavor or appearance of the plant's berries.

4. ADJ

/ ˌbɪt̬.ɚˈswit / (AmE), / ˌbɪt.əˈswiːt / (BrE)
Containing a mixture of both happy and sad feelings.
both happy and sad at once

Examples

The ending of the novel was bittersweet, leaving readers feeling both happy and sad.
Graduation day was a bittersweet moment for the whole family.
They shared a bittersweet farewell, knowing they might never meet again.

Synset Examples

a movie with a bittersweet ending

Related Terms

  • Degree Lower: melancholy - a feeling of deep, pensive sadness
  • Degree Higher: poignant - evoking a keen sense of sadness or regret, often mixed with pleasure
  • Antonym: joyful - feeling, expressing, or causing great pleasure and happiness (opposite in emotional tone)

Etymology

From 'bitter' + 'sweet', originally describing taste, later applied to emotions.
