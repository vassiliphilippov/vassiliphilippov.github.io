preconception

1. NOUN

/ ˌpriːkənˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːkənˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
An opinion or idea formed before having enough evidence or knowledge, often leading to bias or misunderstanding.
an opinion formed before sufficient evidence

Examples

She entered the meeting with no preconceptions about the participants.
It's important to challenge your preconceptions when learning something new.
His preconception of the country changed after he visited.

Synset Examples

he did not even try to confirm his preconceptions

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prejudice - an unreasonable dislike or preference formed without enough reason
  • Synonym: bias - a tendency to prefer one thing over another, often unfairly

Etymology

From Latin 'prae-' (before) + 'concipere' (to conceive).

2. NOUN

/ ˌpriːkənˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːkənˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
an opinion or idea formed before having enough information or experience, often resulting in bias
an opinion formed beforehand; bias

Examples

Her preconception about the new employee was quickly proven wrong after their first project together.
It's important to challenge your own preconceptions when learning about different cultures.
He approached the case without any preconception, striving to remain impartial.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: prejudice - an unfavorable opinion formed beforehand without knowledge or reason
  • Synonym: bias - inclination or prejudice for or against something or someone

Etymology

From Latin prae- (before) + conceptio (a comprehending, conception).
