Scaphiopus multiplicatus

1. NOUN

/ skæˈfiːoʊpəs ˌmʌltɪplɪˈkeɪtəs / (AmE), / skæˈfiːəpəs ˌmʌltɪplɪˈkeɪtəs / (BrE)
A species of spadefoot toad native to the southwestern United States, known for its burrowing behavior and adaptability to arid environments.
species of spadefoot toad (US Southwest)

Examples

The Scaphiopus multiplicatus spends much of its life underground to escape the desert heat.
Scientists observed several Scaphiopus multiplicatus emerging after heavy rainfall in Arizona.
Because of its distinctive spade-shaped feet, Scaphiopus multiplicatus is well adapted to digging in sandy soils.

Etymology

From New Latin: genus name 'Scaphiopus' (from Greek 'skaphe' meaning 'spade' and 'pous' meaning 'foot') and Latin 'multiplicatus' meaning 'multiplied'.
