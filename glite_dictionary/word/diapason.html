diapason

1. NOUN

/ ˌdaɪ.əˈpeɪ.zən / (AmE), / ˌdaɪ.əˈpɑː.zən / (BrE)
either of the two main stops on a pipe organ, known for producing a characteristic, full-bodied tone
principal pipe organ stop

Examples

The organist chose the diapason to create a rich foundation for the hymn.
The sound of the diapason filled the cathedral with warm, resonant tones.
Adjusting the diapason stop altered the balance of the organ's ensemble.

Etymology

From Greek 'diapasōn' (through all), via Latin and Old French.
