laconically

1. ADV

/ ləˈkɑːnɪkli / (AmE), / ləˈkɒnɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that uses very few words, often to the point of seeming terse or dry.
using very few words; tersely

Examples

She replied laconically, offering only a single word in response to each question.
He described the situation laconically, without any embellishment or unnecessary detail.
When asked about his plans, he answered laconically and avoided further discussion.

Synset Examples

‘I know that’, he said dryly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: tersely - in a brief and direct way using few words
  • Antonym: verbosely - using or expressed in more words than are needed

Etymology

From 'laconic' + '-ally'; 'laconic' from Greek Lakonikos, relating to Laconia (Sparta), whose people were known for their terse speech.
