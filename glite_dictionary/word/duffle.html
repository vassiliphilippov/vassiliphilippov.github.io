duffle

1. NOUN

/ ˈdʌfəl / (AmE), / ˈdʌfəl / (BrE)
A coarse, heavy woolen fabric, often used for making coats and blankets.
heavy woolen fabric

Examples

The winter coat was made from thick duffle to keep out the cold.
Sailors often used duffle blankets during long voyages.
Traditional duffle provided both warmth and durability in harsh weather.

Etymology

From Dutch 'duffel', the name of a town in Belgium where the cloth was originally made.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdʌfəl / (AmE), / ˈdʌf(ə)l / (BrE)
a large cylindrical bag made of sturdy cloth, used especially for carrying personal belongings
large cylindrical bag for belongings

Examples

He packed all his clothes into a blue duffle before leaving for college.
The soldiers carried their gear in worn duffles during the march.
She tossed her duffle onto the bed as soon as she arrived at the hotel.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: duffel bag - another name for a duffle; a cylindrical cloth bag

Etymology

From Duffel, the name of a town in Belgium known for its heavy woolen cloth used to make such bags.
