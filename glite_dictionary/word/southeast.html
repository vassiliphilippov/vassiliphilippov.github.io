southeast

1. NOUN

/ ˌsaʊθˈist / (AmE), / ˌsaʊθˈiːst / (BrE)
The direction halfway between south and east on the compass, at 135 degrees.
compass direction between south and east

Examples

The sun rises in the southeast during the winter months.
A strong wind blew from the southeast all afternoon.
They traveled in the direction of the southeast to reach the next village.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: northeast - the direction between north and east on the compass
  • Antonym: southwest - the direction between south and west on the compass

Etymology

From Old English 'sūþ' (south) + 'east' (east).
