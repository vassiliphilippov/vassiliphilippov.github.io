quest

1. NOUN

/ kwɛst / (AmE), / kwɛst / (BrE)
a long or arduous search for something, often one involving pursuit or adventure
long or difficult search for something

Examples

The knight embarked on a quest to find the lost treasure.
Her lifelong quest for knowledge led her to travel the world.
They joined a quest to discover new sources of clean energy.

Synset Examples

a quest for diamonds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: search - the act of looking for something
  • Synonym: pursuit - the action of pursuing or striving for something

Etymology

From Old French *queste* (search), from Latin *quaerere* (to seek).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00948466-n
Synset POS: n

2. NOUN

/ kwɛst / (AmE), / kwɛst / (BrE)
an attempt to achieve or find something, especially something important or challenging over a period of time
a long or difficult search for something

Examples

Their quest for the lost city took them deep into the jungle.
She is on a quest to discover the truth about her family's past.
The knight embarked on a dangerous quest to retrieve the legendary sword.

Synset Examples

the pursuit of love
life is more than the pursuance of fame
a quest for wealth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: search - an act of trying to find something
  • Synonym: pursuit - the action of pursuing or striving for something
  • Synonym: hunt - an effort to find or obtain something

Etymology

From Old French *queste*, based on Latin *quaerere* (to seek).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-05778388-n
Synset POS: n

3. VERB

/ kwɛst / (AmE), / kwɛst / (BrE)
to search or seek for something, typically with determination or adventure
search or seek determinedly

Examples

The detective tirelessly quested for the missing evidence despite many setbacks.
As the sun set, the group continued questing for a route across the mountains.
He quests after forgotten truths hidden in ancient texts.

Synset Examples

Things that die with their eyes open and questing
The animal came questing through the forest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: search - try to find something by looking or seeking

Etymology

From Old French 'quester', from Latin 'quaerere' (to seek, ask).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02245663-v

4. VERB

/ kwɛst / (AmE), / kwɛst / (BrE)
(of a dog, especially in hunting) to search for the scent or track of game.
(of dogs) search for a scent in hunting

Examples

The hunting dog was trained to quest for pheasants in the field.
During the hunt, the beagle quested along the hedge for the scent of rabbits.
Their spaniel eagerly quests through the underbrush whenever they visit the woods.

Synset Examples

The dog went off and quested

Related Terms

  • Synonym: search - to look for something carefully and thoroughly
  • Synonym: track - to follow the trail or scent of something

Etymology

From Old French *quester* (to seek, search), from Latin *quaerere* (to seek).
