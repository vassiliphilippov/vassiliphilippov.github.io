contrapuntal

1. ADJ

/ ˌkɑːn.trəˈpʌn.təl / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.trəˈpʌn.təl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of counterpoint in music, where two or more melodic lines are combined harmonically but remain independent in rhythm and contour.
relating to counterpoint in music

Examples

The piece features a beautifully contrapuntal texture that highlights each instrument's unique melodic line.
Her composition displays a masterful understanding of contrapuntal technique.
Bach's fugues are renowned for their complex contrapuntal structures.

Synset Examples

contrapuntal base

Related Terms

  • Antonym: homophonic - of or relating to music with chordal or one main melodic line texture

Etymology

From Italian 'contrappunto' (counterpoint), from Latin 'contra' (against) + 'punctus' (point, note).

2. ADJ

/ ˌkɑːn.trəˈpʌn.tl̩ / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.trəˈpʌn.tl̩ / (BrE)
relating to or involving two or more independent but harmonically interdependent melodic lines; characteristic of counterpoint in music.
relating to independent, harmonically linked melodies in music

Examples

The composer is renowned for his contrapuntal writing, especially in his fugues.
They admired the contrapuntal interplay between the violin and the cello in the quartet.
Her analysis focused on the contrapuntal structure of the Baroque masterpiece.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polyphonic - having two or more simultaneous independent melodic lines
  • Antonym: homophonic - musical texture with one main melody and chordal accompaniment

Etymology

From Italian 'contrappunto' (counterpoint), from 'contra-' (against) + 'punto' (point, note).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01548336-a
Synset POS: a
