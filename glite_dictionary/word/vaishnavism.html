Vaishnavism

1. NOUN

/ vaɪʃˈnɑːvɪzəm / (AmE), / vaɪʃˈnɑːvɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A branch of Hinduism in which the followers worship Vishnu as the supreme deity.
Hindu tradition devoted to Vishnu

Examples

Many of the temple rituals in South India are influenced by Vaishnavism.
He studied the philosophy and devotional practices associated with Vaishnavism.
The art exhibition featured pieces inspired by Vaishnavism and its rich symbolism.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Shaivism - Hindu tradition worshipping Shiva as the supreme god

Etymology

From Sanskrit 'Vaiṣṇava' (pertaining to Vishnu) + English '-ism'.

2. NOUN

/ vaɪʃˈnɑːvɪzəm / (AmE), / vaɪʃˈnævɪzəm / (BrE)
A major tradition within Hinduism devoted to the worship of Vishnu as the supreme god.
Hindu sect devoted to Vishnu

Examples

Many devotees of Vaishnavism gather annually at temples during religious festivals.
He developed a deep interest in Vaishnavism after reading about its philosophies.
Vaishnavism emphasizes devotion and love towards Lord Vishnu.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: Shaivism - Hindu tradition worshipping Shiva as the supreme god

Etymology

From Sanskrit *Vaiṣṇava* (follower of Vishnu) + English '-ism'.
