Joseph ben Matthias

1. PROPN

/ ˈdʒoʊzəf bɛn mæθˈaɪəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒəʊzɪf bɛn mæθˈaɪəs / (BrE)
Jewish general and historian (37–c.100 AD), who led the Jewish revolt against the Romans before becoming a historian under the name Flavius Josephus.
Jewish general and historian, known as Josephus

Examples

Joseph ben Matthias was initially a commander in Galilee during the war against Rome.
The writings of Joseph ben Matthias, under the name Josephus, are crucial sources for historians.
Few know that Joseph ben Matthias later became a Roman citizen and prolific author.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Flavius Josephus - Romanized name of Joseph ben Matthias, Jewish historian

Etymology

Hebrew: Yosef ben Matityahu, later known as Flavius Josephus in Rome.
