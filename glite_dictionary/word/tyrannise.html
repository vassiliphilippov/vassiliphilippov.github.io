to tyrannise

1. VERB

/ tɪˈrænəˌzaɪz / (AmE), / tɪˈranʌɪz / (BrE)
to rule or exercise power over someone in a cruel, oppressive, or autocratic way
to rule in a cruel and oppressive way
british

Examples

The dictator continued to tyrannise the population for decades.
She refused to let her supervisor tyrannise her any longer at work.
Some parents are accused of tyrannising their children with strict and unfair rules.

Synset Examples

her husband and mother-in-law tyrannize her

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tyrannize - to rule over in a cruel manner (American spelling)
  • Synonym: to oppress - to keep someone in hardship through unjust exercise of authority
  • Antonym: to liberate - to set someone free from oppression

Etymology

From Greek 'tyrannos' (absolute ruler) via Latin and Old French.

2. VERB

/ ˈtɪrəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈtɪərənaɪz / (BrE)
to rule or exercise power over others in a cruel and oppressive way, like a tyrant
to rule oppressively like a tyrant

Examples

The dictator continued to tyrannise the population for decades, silencing all opposition.
Some leaders tyrannise their citizens through strict laws and constant surveillance.
History is full of rulers who tyrannised their countries until they were finally overthrown.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to oppress - to keep someone in subservience and hardship, especially by the unjust exercise of authority
  • Antonym: to liberate - to set someone free from a situation, especially imprisonment or oppression

Etymology

From Greek 'tyrannos' (tyrant) via Old French and Medieval Latin.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02593331-v
Synset POS: v
