boastfulness

1. NOUN

/ ˈboʊstfəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈbəʊstfəlnəs / (BrE)
the quality of talking with excessive pride and self-satisfaction about one's achievements, possessions, or abilities
excessive pride in oneself or one's achievements

Examples

His boastfulness quickly alienated him from his colleagues.
The team's boastfulness before the match made their defeat even more humiliating.
Her boastfulness was evident as she recounted all her accomplishments.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: modesty - the quality of being unassuming or moderate about one's abilities
  • Synonym: bragging - talking boastfully about one's achievements or possessions

Etymology

From 'boastful' + '-ness', with 'boast' from Middle English 'bosten'.
