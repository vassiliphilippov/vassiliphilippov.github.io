Loxia curvirostra

1. NOUN

/ ˌloʊksiə ˌkɜrvɪˈrɑstrə / (AmE), / ˌlɒksɪə ˌkɜːvɪˈrɒstrə / (BrE)
A species of finch known as the red crossbill, characterized by a bill whose tips cross when closed, adapted for extracting seeds from conifer cones.
species of finch with crossed bill

Examples

The Loxia curvirostra uses its unusual bill to extract seeds from pine cones.
Researchers studied how the Loxia curvirostra adapts to different conifer forests.
A flock of Loxia curvirostra was spotted feeding high in the spruce trees.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: red crossbill - common name of Loxia curvirostra, a finch with a crossed bill

Etymology

From Neo-Latin; genus name 'Loxia' from Greek loxos (slanting), and species name 'curvirostra' from Latin curvus (curved) + rostrum (beak).
