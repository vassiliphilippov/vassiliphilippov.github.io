Oscar Wilde

1. PROPN

/ ˈɑskar waɪld / (AmE), / ˈɒskə waɪld / (BrE)
(1854–1900) Irish writer, poet, and wit known for his plays, epigrams, and novel 'The Picture of Dorian Gray'.
Irish writer and wit (1854–1900)

Examples

Oscar Wilde was famous for his sharp wit and memorable quotations.
Many consider Oscar Wilde's play 'The Importance of Being Earnest' to be his masterpiece.
Books by Oscar Wilde are still widely read and performed today.
