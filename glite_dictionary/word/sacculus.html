sacculus

1. NOUN

/ ˈsækjələs / (AmE), / ˈsak.jʊ.ləs / (BrE)
A small sac or pouch in the body, especially a small chamber of the membranous labyrinth of the inner ear.
small body sac or pouch, esp. inner ear
medical

Examples

The sacculus is one of the two vestibular sacs in the inner ear, playing a role in balance.
Damage to the sacculus can result in difficulties with spatial orientation and equilibrium.
Scientists studied the anatomy of the sacculus in various mammals to understand its function.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: saccule - another term for sacculus, especially in anatomy

Etymology

From Latin 'sacculus' meaning 'little bag' or 'small sack'.
