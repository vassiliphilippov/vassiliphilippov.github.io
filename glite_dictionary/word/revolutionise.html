to revolutionise

1. VERB

/ ˌrɛvəˈluːʃənaɪz / (AmE), / ˌrɛvəˈluːʃənaɪz / (BrE)
To fill someone or something with revolutionary ideas or to radically transform a situation or way of thinking.
to fill with revolutionary ideas; to radically transform
british

Examples

The new leader sought to revolutionise the party by introducing bold policies.
Her ideas helped revolutionise the way we view education.
The innovation promises to revolutionise communication in remote areas.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to revolutionize - fill with revolutionary ideas (American spelling)

Etymology

From French révolutionner, based on révolution (revolution), ultimately from Latin revolutio.

2. VERB

/ ˌrɛvəˈluːʃəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˌrɛvəˈluːʃənaɪz / (BrE)
to change something completely and fundamentally, especially in a way that is new or innovative
change fundamentally; innovate radically
british

Examples

The invention of smartphones has revolutionised the way we communicate and access information.
New medical technologies revolutionised patient care over the last decade.
Online learning platforms are revolutionising education across the world.

Synset Examples

E-mail revolutionized communication in academe

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to revolutionize - to change something completely and fundamentally (American spelling)
  • Synonym: to transform - to make a thorough or dramatic change in form or appearance
  • Degree Lower: to modify - to make partial or minor changes to something

Etymology

From 'revolution' + '-ise', modeled after French 'révolutionner', influenced by 'revolution.'

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00125441-v
Synset POS: v
