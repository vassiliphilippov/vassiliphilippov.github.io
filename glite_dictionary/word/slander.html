slander

1. NOUN

/ ˈslændər / (AmE), / ˈslɑːndə / (BrE)
A false spoken statement that damages a person's reputation.
false spoken damaging statement

Examples

He threatened to sue the newspaper for slander after they published the false allegations.
During the trial, she denied the slander that had been spoken about her in public.
The company filed a lawsuit, claiming that the remarks amounted to slander.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: libel - a published false statement damaging to a person’s reputation
  • Synonym: defamation - the act of damaging someone's reputation with false statements
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration

Etymology

From Old French 'esclandre', from Late Latin 'scandalum' (scandal).

2. VERB

/ ˈslændɚ / (AmE), / ˈslɑːndə / (BrE)
to make false spoken statements that damage the reputation of someone
make false damaging spoken statements

Examples

It is illegal to slander someone in the media with baseless accusations.
He threatened to sue anyone who tried to slander his character.
Politicians sometimes slander their opponents during heated campaigns.

Synset Examples

The journalists have defamed me!
The article in the paper sullied my reputation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: libel - to make false written statements damaging to someone's reputation
  • Synonym: to defame - to damage someone's good reputation by false statements
  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration for someone or something

Etymology

From Old French *esclandre*, from Late Latin *scandalum* (cause of offense, scandal).
