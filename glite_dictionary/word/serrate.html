serrate

1. ADJ

/ ˈsɛˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɛːreɪt / (BrE)
(of an edge or margin) having a notched or saw-like appearance, with teeth pointing toward the apex.
having a saw-toothed edge

Examples

The leaf's edge was distinctly serrate, resembling the teeth of a saw.
Many shark species have serrate teeth to help them tear through flesh.
Under the microscope, the serrate margin of the wing became clearly visible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: serrated - having or denoting a jagged edge; saw-toothed

Etymology

From Latin 'serratus', from 'serra' meaning 'saw'.

2. VERB

/ ˈsɛˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɛrəteɪt / (BrE)
to make the edge of something saw-toothed or jagged
make saw-toothed or jagged

Examples

The craftsman used a special tool to serrate the knife's blade for better cutting.
Some leaves naturally serrate their margins as they grow.
Engineers decided to serrate the edge of the metal strip for increased grip.

Synset Examples

serrate the edges of the teeth

Related Terms

  • Synonym: notch - to cut or make a V-shaped cut
  • Antonym: smooth - to make even or without jagged edges

Etymology

From Latin serratus (saw-shaped), from serra (saw).
