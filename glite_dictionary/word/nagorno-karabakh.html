Nagorno-Karabakh

1. PROPN

/ nəˌɡɔrnoʊ kɑːrəˈbɑːk / (AmE), / nəˌɡɔːnəʊ kəˈrɑːbæk / (BrE)
A mountainous region in the South Caucasus, formerly an unrecognized de facto state, internationally recognized as part of Azerbaijan but predominantly inhabited by ethnic Armenians and the subject of long-standing conflict.
disputed region in the South Caucasus

Examples

The decades-old conflict over Nagorno-Karabakh has drawn international attention.
Peace talks regarding Nagorno-Karabakh resumed after new clashes erupted.
He wrote a thesis on the political history of Nagorno-Karabakh.

Etymology

From Russian ‘Nagorny Karabakh’ (Mountainous Karabakh), with influences from Russian ‘nagorny’ (mountainous) and Turkish–Persian ‘Karabakh’.
