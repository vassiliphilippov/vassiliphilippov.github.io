buckeye

1. NOUN

/ ˈbʌkˌaɪ / (AmE), / ˈbʌk.aɪ / (BrE)
A tree of the genus Aesculus, having palmate leaves and large clusters of white to red flowers, followed by shiny brown inedible seeds.
tree with palmate leaves and shiny brown seeds

Examples

The buckeye produces shiny brown seeds that resemble the eye of a deer.
Clusters of red flowers make the buckeye especially attractive in the spring.
Gardeners often plant buckeye trees for their ornamental beauty, despite the seeds being inedible.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horse chestnut - tree with similar appearance and seeds, often used synonymously

Etymology

From the resemblance of the seed to a deer's (buck's) eye.

2. NOUN

/ ˈbʌkˌaɪ / (AmE), / ˈbʌk.aɪ / (BrE)
the inedible nut-like seed of the horse chestnut tree, especially of North American species
inedible horse chestnut seed

Examples

Children collected the shiny buckeye from beneath the horse chestnut tree in the park.
Despite its appealing appearance, the buckeye is toxic and should not be eaten.
She carried a buckeye in her pocket for good luck, as her grandfather used to do.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: horse chestnut - a tree with large leaves and spiky fruits, producing inedible seeds called buckeyes

Etymology

From the resemblance of the seed to the eye of a male deer (buck).
