Ezra Loomis Pound

1. PROPN

/ ˈɛzrə ˈlumɪs paʊnd / (AmE), / ˈɛzrə ˈluːmɪs paʊnd / (BrE)
(1885–1972) An American poet and critic who lived much of his life in Europe and was a major influence on the development of modernist literature.
American modernist poet and critic

Examples

Ezra Loomis Pound was instrumental in shaping early twentieth-century poetry through his criticism and advocacy.
Many modern poets have acknowledged the influence of Ezra Loomis Pound on their work.
The literary contributions of Ezra Loomis Pound continue to be studied in universities worldwide.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: T. S. Eliot - British-American poet and close contemporary
  • Synonym: modernism - A literary movement influenced by Pound
