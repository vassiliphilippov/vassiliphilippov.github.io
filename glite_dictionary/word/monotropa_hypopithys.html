Monotropa hypopithys

1. NOUN

/ ˌmoʊ.nəˈtroʊ.pə ˌhaɪ.poʊˈpɪ.θɪs / (AmE), / ˌmɒ.nəˈtrəʊ.pə ˌhɪ.pəʊˈpɪ.θɪs / (BrE)
A fleshy, tawny or reddish saprophytic herb resembling the Indian pipe, found in woodland humus in eastern North America.
saprophytic woodland herb species

Examples

The rare Monotropa hypopithys is often found in shaded woodland areas rich in organic matter.
Botanists were excited to discover a cluster of Monotropa hypopithys during their forest survey.
Unlike green plants, Monotropa hypopithys does not photosynthesize and obtains nutrients from decaying matter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: Indian pipe - a related saprophytic plant of similar appearance

Etymology

From scientific Latin: genus name Monotropa and species epithet hypopithys.
