ornament

1. VERB

/ ˈɔrnəˌmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈɔːnəˌmɛnt / (BrE)
to make more attractive by adding decorative details, colour, or objects
decorate or embellish

Examples

They decided to ornament the hall with flowers and candles for the wedding.
The ancient temple was ornamented with intricate carvings and gold.
Artists often ornament their manuscripts with colorful illustrations.

Synset Examples

Decorate the room for the party
beautify yourself for the special day

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to decorate - to make something look more attractive by adding items or color
  • Synonym: to beautify - to make beautiful or more beautiful
  • Synonym: to embellish - to make something more attractive by adding decorative details

Etymology

From Latin ornamentum (equipment, embellishment), from ornare (to adorn).

2. NOUN

/ ˈɔːrnəmənt / (AmE), / ˈɔːnəmənt / (BrE)
an object or decoration that is used to make something more attractive or beautiful
an object used to decorate

Examples

She hung a shiny ornament on the Christmas tree.
The vase on the table was used mainly as an ornament.
Each shelf was lined with a different kind of ornament.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: decoration - something that decorates or beautifies
  • Synonym: adornment - a thing which adorns or decorates

Etymology

From Latin ornamentum (equipment, embellishment), from ornare (to equip, adorn).
