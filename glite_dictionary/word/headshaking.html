headshaking

1. NOUN

/ ˈhɛdˌʃeɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɛdˌʃeɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of moving one's head from side to side to indicate denial, disbelief, or bemusement
side-to-side head movement to indicate denial or disbelief

Examples

The interviewer noticed his nervous headshaking when the tough questions began.
She answered the offer with a firm headshaking, refusing to accept it.
His headshaking suggested he was not convinced by their explanation.

Synset Examples

I could tell from their headshakes that they didn't believe me

Related Terms

  • Antonym: nod - the act of moving the head up and down, usually to indicate agreement
