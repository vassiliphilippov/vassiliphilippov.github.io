enteroceptor

1. NOUN

/ ˌɛntəroʊˈsɛptər / (AmE), / ˌɛntərəʊˈsɛptə / (BrE)
a sensory receptor that responds to stimuli originating inside the body, such as from internal organs or tissues
receptor sensing internal body stimuli
medical

Examples

The enteroceptor plays a crucial role in regulating digestive functions by detecting changes inside the gut.
Researchers have identified several types of enteroceptors that monitor blood pressure.
Damage to enteroceptors can lead to impaired detection of internal bodily changes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: exteroceptor - a receptor that responds to stimuli from outside the body

Etymology

From Greek 'enteron' (intestine, inside) + 'receptor'.
