intriguing

1. ADJ

/ ɪnˈtriɡɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈtriːɡɪŋ / (BrE)
Arousing one's curiosity or interest; fascinating.
arousing curiosity or interest

Examples

The documentary presented an intriguing perspective on ancient civilizations.
She found the book's plot to be genuinely intriguing from start to finish.
He asked an intriguing question that made everyone think deeply.

Synset Examples

our team came up with some most intriguing finds

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fascinating - extremely interesting or charming
  • Antonym: boring - not interesting; dull

Etymology

From the verb 'intrigue', from French 'intriguer', meaning 'to plot, scheme'.

2. ADJ

/ ɪnˈtriɡɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈtriːɡɪŋ / (BrE)
Arousing curiosity or interest in a way that is unusual, mysterious, or disturbing.
arousing curiosity or fascination

Examples

The detective found the old diary to be intriguing and full of hidden secrets.
She wore an intriguing smile that made everyone wonder what she was thinking.
The plot of the film was so intriguing that I couldn't look away.

Synset Examples

an intriguing smile

Related Terms

  • Synonym: fascinating - Extremely interesting or charming
  • Antonym: boring - Not interesting; dull

Etymology

From French *intriguer* (to plot), from Italian *intricare* (to entangle).
