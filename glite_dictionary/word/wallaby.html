wallaby

1. NOUN

/ ˈwɑːləˌbi / (AmE), / ˈwɒləbi / (BrE)
a small or medium-sized marsupial native to Australia and nearby islands, similar to a kangaroo but generally smaller and often more brightly colored.
small or medium-sized Australian marsupial

Examples

The wallaby bounded quickly across the open field, disappearing into the bushes.
We spotted a rare rock wallaby during our hike in the national park.
Unlike the kangaroo, the wallaby tends to live in forests and shrubland.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: kangaroo - a large marsupial native to Australia, with powerful hind legs

Etymology

From Dharug (an Australian Aboriginal language) 'walaba'.
