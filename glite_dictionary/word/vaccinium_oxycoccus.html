Vaccinium oxycoccus

1. NOUN

/ vækˈsɪniəm ɒkˈsaɪkəkəs / (AmE), / vækˈsɪnɪəm ɒksɪˈkɒkəs / (BrE)
A species of small, red-fruited trailing cranberry found in Arctic and cool regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
species of cranberry plant

Examples

Vaccinium oxycoccus is commonly found in peat bogs across northern Europe and North America.
The berries of Vaccinium oxycoccus are smaller and tarter than those of the American cranberry.
Researchers study Vaccinium oxycoccus for its potential health benefits and natural antioxidants.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cranberry - the common name for several species of small, red, acidic berries

Etymology

From Latin; 'Vaccinium' refers to the blueberry or cranberry genus, and 'oxycoccus' combines Greek 'oxys' (sharp, acid) and 'kokkos' (berry).
