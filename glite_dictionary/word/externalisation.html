externalisation

1. NOUN

/ ɪkˌstɜrnələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˌstɜːnəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of attributing one's own thoughts, feelings, or motives to external sources or outside causes.
attributing to outside causes

Examples

His externalisation of blame prevented him from recognizing his own mistakes.
In psychology, externalisation is a defense mechanism seen in patients with certain disorders.
The persistent externalisation of problems can be a barrier to personal growth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: projection - psychological process of attributing one's feelings to others

Etymology

From 'external' + '-isation', following the pattern of 'externalize'.

2. NOUN

/ ɪkˌstɜrnələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˌstɜːnəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of expressing or embodying something internal in an outward or visible form.
embodiment in an outward form
british

Examples

The externalisation of his emotions made it easier for others to understand him.
Many artists view their work as an externalisation of their inner thoughts and feelings.
Therapists often encourage the externalisation of problems to help clients gain perspective.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: externalization - the act of expressing something internally in an outward form (American spelling)
  • Antonym: internalisation - the process of integrating something into one's internal understanding or behaviour

Etymology

From 'external' + '-isation', influenced by French 'externalisation'.
