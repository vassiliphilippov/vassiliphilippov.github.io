filmish

1. ADJ

/ ˈfɪlmɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈfɪlmɪʃ / (BrE)
having qualities or characteristics reminiscent of a film, especially in visual style or presentation
resembling the style of a film

Examples

The director used filmish lighting effects to create a dramatic atmosphere.
Critics praised the show's filmish visuals for their sophistication and depth.
Her photographs had a distinctly filmish quality, reminiscent of old Hollywood movies.

Synset Examples

The filmish visuals made the series feel more cinematic.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cinematic - having the qualities of a motion picture

Etymology

From 'film' + the suffix '-ish', meaning 'like' or 'having the quality of'.
