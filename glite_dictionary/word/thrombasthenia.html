thrombasthenia

1. NOUN

/ ˌθrɑːmbəˈsθiːniə / (AmE), / ˌθrɒmbəˈsθiːniə / (BrE)
a rare inherited blood disorder in which platelets fail to function normally, leading to excessive bleeding
a rare blood disorder causing excessive bleeding
medical

Examples

The patient was diagnosed with thrombasthenia after experiencing unexplained bruising and bleeding.
Genetic testing confirmed that she suffered from thrombasthenia, explaining her history of frequent nosebleeds.
Because thrombasthenia affects platelet function, injuries can lead to prolonged bleeding.

Etymology

From Greek 'thrombos' (clot) + 'asthenia' (weakness).
