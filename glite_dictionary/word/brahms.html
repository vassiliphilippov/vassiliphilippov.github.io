Brahms

1. PROPN

/ brɑmz / (AmE), / brɑːmz / (BrE)
Johannes Brahms (1833–1897), a German composer and pianist known for developing the Romantic style in lyrical and classical music.
German Romantic composer (1833–1897)

Examples

Many musicians regard Brahms as one of the greatest composers of the Romantic era.
The orchestra performed a stunning rendition of Brahms' Symphony No. 4 last night.
Her piano recital featured works by Brahms alongside pieces by Chopin and Liszt.

Etymology

From the surname of Johannes Brahms, the German composer.
