methylbenzene

1. NOUN

/ ˌmiːθəlˈbɛnzin / (AmE), / ˌmiːθəlˈbɛnziːn / (BrE)
a colorless, flammable liquid hydrocarbon obtained from petroleum or coal tar, used as a solvent and in high-octane fuels; also known as toluene
colorless flammable liquid hydrocarbon (toluene)

Examples

The laboratory used methylbenzene as a solvent for several chemical reactions.
High-octane gasoline often contains additives like methylbenzene to improve performance.
Exposure to methylbenzene should be carefully controlled due to its potential health effects.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: toluene - another name for methylbenzene, a colorless flammable liquid hydrocarbon

Etymology

From 'methyl' (a chemical prefix indicating a CH₃ group) and 'benzene' (aromatic hydrocarbon).
