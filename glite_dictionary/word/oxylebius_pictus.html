Oxylebius pictus

1. NOUN

/ ɑk.sɪˈliː.bi.əs ˈpɪk.təs / (AmE), / ɒk.sɪˈliː.bi.əs ˈpɪk.təs / (BrE)
A species of greenling fish with a whitish body marked by black bands, commonly found in the Pacific Ocean.
species of greenling fish

Examples

Oxylebius pictus is often seen swimming among rocky reefs along the Pacific coastline.
Marine biologists study Oxylebius pictus to understand its habitat preferences and behavior.
The distinctive black bands make Oxylebius pictus easy to identify for local divers.

Etymology

From scientific Latin; genus name Oxylebius and species name pictus (Latin: painted, decorated).
