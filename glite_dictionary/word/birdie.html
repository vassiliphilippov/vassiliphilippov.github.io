birdie

1. NOUN

/ ˈbɝːdi / (AmE), / ˈbɜːdi / (BrE)
A lightweight object with a rounded base and feathers or plastic, used in badminton instead of a ball; also called a shuttlecock.
badminton projectile; shuttlecock
american

Examples

The player hit the birdie with a powerful smash, sending it flying over the net.
We replaced the old birdie because the feathers were damaged during the last match.
A new set of rackets and a pack of birdies came with the badminton kit.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shuttlecock - equipment hit back and forth in badminton

2. VERB

/ ˈbɝːdi / (AmE), / ˈbɜːdi / (BrE)
(in golf) to score one stroke under par on a hole.
score one under par (golf)

Examples

He managed to birdie the last three holes to win the tournament.
She hopes to birdie the par-3 eighth after a perfect tee shot.
If you birdie this hole, you'll move into first place.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: bogey - (golf) a score of one over par on a hole
  • Degree Higher: eagle - (golf) a score of two under par on a hole

Etymology

From the noun 'birdie', an American slang term for something excellent, adopted in golf in the early 20th century.

3. NOUN

/ ˈbɝːdi / (AmE), / ˈbɜːdi / (BrE)
(Golf) A score of one stroke under par on a hole.
golf: one under par on a hole

Examples

She made a birdie on the 15th hole, moving up the leaderboard.
After sinking a long putt, he celebrated his first birdie of the round.
Scoring a birdie can give a player a real confidence boost.

Related Terms

  • Degree Higher: par - the standard number of strokes set for a hole or course in golf
  • Degree Lower: eagle - a score of two strokes under par on a hole in golf

Etymology

From 'bird' (early 20th-century American slang for something excellent), adapted in golf.
