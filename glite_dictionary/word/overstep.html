to overstep

1. VERB

/ ˌoʊvərˈstɛp / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈstɛp / (BrE)
To go beyond what is allowed or acceptable; to exceed a limit or boundary.
go beyond a limit or boundary

Examples

You must not overstep your authority when managing the team.
The journalist was accused of overstepping ethical boundaries in her reporting.
He apologized for overstepping the mark with his comments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exceed - to go beyond a limit or amount
  • Synonym: to transgress - to do something that is not allowed, especially by law or moral rule
  • Antonym: to obey - to follow rules or limits; to not go beyond what is allowed

Etymology

From prefix 'over-' (beyond) + 'step' (walk) in Middle English.
