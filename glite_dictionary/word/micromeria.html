Micromeria

1. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪkrəˈmɪriə / (AmE), / ˌmɪkrəˈmɪəriə / (BrE)
A large genus of fragrant herbs belonging mainly to the Old World, in the mint family (Lamiaceae).
genus of fragrant Old World herbs

Examples

Several species of Micromeria are used for their aromatic leaves in herbal teas.
The botanist discovered a rare Micromeria growing on the Mediterranean hillside.
Studies have shown that Micromeria plants have potential medicinal properties.

Etymology

From Greek 'mikros' (small) and 'meros' (part), referring to the small size of some plant parts.

2. NOUN

/ ˌmaɪkrəˈmɪriə / (AmE), / ˌmɪkrəˈmɪəriə / (BrE)
A genus of aromatic herbs found mainly in the Old World, belonging to the mint family (Lamiaceae).
genus of aromatic herbs in the mint family

Examples

Many species in the Micromeria genus are valued for their pleasant aroma and culinary uses.
The botanist discovered a rare Micromeria growing on the rocky hillside.
Members of Micromeria are often found in Mediterranean climates.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mint - aromatic herbs in the Lamiaceae family

Etymology

From Greek mikros (small) + meros (part), referring to the plant's small parts.
