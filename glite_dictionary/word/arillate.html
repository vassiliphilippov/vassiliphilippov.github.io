arillate

1. ADJ

/ əˈrɪl.eɪt / (AmE), / əˈrɪl.eɪt / (BrE)
(of seeds) having a fleshy and usually brightly colored outer covering called an aril.
having a fleshy aril (seed covering)

Examples

The arillate seeds of the plant attract birds with their bright red coverings.
Many tropical plants bear arillate seeds to encourage animal dispersal.
The botanist noted that the fruit contained both arillate and non-arillate seeds.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: exarillate - (of seeds) lacking an aril or fleshy covering

Etymology

From Latin arillus (aril) + -ate (forming adjectives).
