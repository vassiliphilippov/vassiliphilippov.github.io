anthropophagous

1. ADJ

/ ænˌθrɑːˈpɑfəɡəs / (AmE), / ænˌθrɒˈpæfəɡəs / (BrE)
(of an animal or being) feeding on human flesh; relating to cannibalism.
feeding on human flesh

Examples

Some authors describe the mythical giants as anthropophagous creatures that threatened early humans.
The concept of anthropophagous beings appears in folklore from many different cultures.
Researchers studied whether certain rare animals could be considered anthropophagous based on their diet.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cannibalistic - relating to eating the flesh of one's own species

Etymology

From Greek anthrōpophagos (man-eating), from anthrōpos (human) + phagein (to eat).
