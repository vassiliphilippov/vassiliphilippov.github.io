to sporulate

1. VERB

/ ˈspɔr.ju.leɪt / (AmE), / ˈspɔː.rjʊ.leɪt / (BrE)
(of a fungus, bacterium, or protist) to produce or form spores.
produce or form spores
medical

Examples

Certain bacteria sporulate when conditions become unfavorable for growth.
Mushrooms sporulate under moist, warm conditions, releasing spores into the air.
If the yeast fails to sporulate in the laboratory, the experiment may have been set up incorrectly.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to germinate - to begin to grow from a seed or spore

Etymology

From Latin spora (spore) + -ulate (forming verbs).

2. VERB

/ ˈspɔːr.jə.leɪt / (AmE), / ˈspɔːr.jʊ.leɪt / (BrE)
(of a plant, fungus, or microorganism) to produce or release spores.
produce or release spores
medical

Examples

Some fungi sporulate when environmental conditions become unfavorable.
After infection, the bacteria quickly sporulate and spread throughout the tissue.
Many ferns sporulate in late summer as part of their reproductive cycle.

Synset Examples

plants sporulate

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to germinate - to begin to grow from a spore or seed

Etymology

From Latin spora (seed, sowing) + -ulate (forming verbs).
