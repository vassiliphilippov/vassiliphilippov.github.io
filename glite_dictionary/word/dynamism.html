dynamism

1. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪnəˌmɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈdɑːnəmɪzəm / (BrE)
any of various theories or philosophical systems that explain the universe as the result of an immanent force or energy, rather than matter or static principles
theory explaining universe through force or energy

Examples

In some branches of philosophy, dynamism offers an alternative to materialism in explaining the nature of reality.
Historically, dynamism has been influential in debates over the relationship between matter and energy.
The philosopher argued that dynamism better accounts for the apparent vitality of the universe.

Etymology

From Greek dynamis (power, force) via French dynamisme.

2. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪnəˌmɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈdɪnəmɪzəm / (BrE)
the quality of being full of energy, enthusiasm, and a strong sense of activity, especially in a person's character
energetic quality in personality

Examples

Her dynamism made her an effective leader who inspired her team daily.
The new manager's dynamism transformed the entire office atmosphere within weeks.
A sense of dynamism is essential for anyone hoping to succeed in a fast-paced industry.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: energy - the strength and vitality required for sustained activity
  • Synonym: vitality - the state of being strong and active; energy
  • Antonym: passivity - acceptance of what happens, without active response

Etymology

From Greek dunamis (power, force) via French dynamisme.

3. NOUN

/ ˈdaɪnəˌmɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈdɪənəmɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
vigorous energy and determination, especially in activities requiring active strength of body or mind.
vigorous energy and determination

Examples

Her dynamism and enthusiasm inspired the entire team.
The manager's dynamism brought fresh ideas to the company.
He tackled the project with admirable dynamism despite the challenges.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: energy - the capacity for vigorous activity
  • Synonym: vitality - the state of being strong and active; energy
  • Antonym: lethargy - a lack of energy and enthusiasm

Etymology

From Greek 'dynamis' (power, force) via French 'dynamisme'.
