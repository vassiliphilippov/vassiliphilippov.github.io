to unload

1. VERB

/ ʌnˈloʊd / (AmE), / ʌnˈləʊd / (BrE)
to remove goods or cargo from a vehicle, container, or similar receptacle
remove cargo from a vehicle or container

Examples

The workers began to unload the boxes from the truck at sunrise.
It took nearly an hour to unload the cargo ship at the port.
Please unload your suitcases from the car so we can take them inside.

Synset Examples

unload the truck
offload the van

Related Terms

  • Antonym: load - to put goods or cargo onto a vehicle or into a container

Etymology

From un- (expressing reversal) + load; first recorded in the early 17th century.

2. VERB

/ ʌnˈloʊd / (AmE), / ʌnˈləʊd / (BrE)
to remove cargo, passengers, or goods from a vehicle or container
remove cargo or goods from a vehicle

Examples

The workers began to unload the boxes from the truck.
It took an hour to unload all the passengers at the station.
Please unload your groceries at the counter before parking your car.

Synset Examples

unload the cargo
drop off the passengers at the hotel

Related Terms

  • Antonym: load - to put cargo or goods onto a vehicle or container

Etymology

From un- (expressing reversal) + load; first attested in the late Middle English period.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01492094-v
Synset POS: v
