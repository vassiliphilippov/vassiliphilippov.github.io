Carcharhinus obscurus

1. NOUN

/ kɑr.kəˈraɪ.nəs əbˈskjʊər.əs / (AmE), / kɑː.kəˈraɪ.nəs ɒbˈskjʊə.rəs / (BrE)
A relatively slender blue-grey shark found in tropical and temperate waters around the world, commonly known as the dusky shark.
species of dusky shark

Examples

The Carcharhinus obscurus is often caught in commercial fisheries for its fins and meat.
Divers sometimes encounter the Carcharhinus obscurus near coral reefs in warm, temperate waters.
Scientists have tagged several Carcharhinus obscurus to study their long-distance migration patterns.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dusky shark - Common name for Carcharhinus obscurus, a blue-grey shark species

Etymology

From Greek *karcharos* (sharp or jagged) and *rhinos* (nose), with Latin *obscurus* (dark or dusky).
