singly

1. ADV

/ ˈsɪŋɡli / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡli / (BrE)
one at a time; separately or individually, rather than as part of a group
one at a time; individually

Examples

Each item must be packaged singly to prevent damage.
Guests entered singly, giving their names at the door.
The problems were addressed singly rather than as a group.

Synset Examples

taken individually, the rooms were, in fact, square
the fine points are treated singly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: individually - separately; one by one
  • Antonym: collectively - as a group; all together

Etymology

From Middle English sing(e)ly, from Old French sengle (single) + -ly.

2. ADV

/ ˈsɪŋɡli / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡli / (BrE)
one at a time; separately rather than together or in pairs or groups.
one at a time; separately

Examples

The students were called to the stage singly rather than in groups.
Please place the documents singly into the feeder so the machine doesn't jam.
The chairs were arranged singly along the wall rather than in rows.

Synset Examples

they were arranged singly

Related Terms

  • Synonym: individually - separately, one person or thing at a time
  • Antonym: together - as a group or collectively

Etymology

From Latin 'singulus' meaning single or one at a time.
