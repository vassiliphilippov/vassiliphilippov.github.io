2. NOUN

/ ˈlɛt̬ɪs / (AmE), / ˈlɛtɪs / (BrE)
a leafy green vegetable, typically eaten raw in salads, from plants of the species Lactuca sativa
a green leafy salad vegetable

Examples

She bought a fresh head of lettuce to make a salad for dinner.
The sandwich came with tomato and lettuce on whole grain bread.
Farmers harvested several crates of lettuce early in the morning.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: salad - a cold dish of various mixtures of raw or cooked vegetables

Etymology

From Old French *laitues*, plural of *laitue*, from Latin *lactuca* (lettuce), from *lac* (milk), referring to the milky juice of the plant.

3. NOUN

/ ˈlɛt̬.ɪs / (AmE), / ˈlɛt.ɪs / (BrE)
Any of various cultivated plants of the genus Lactuca, especially Lactuca sativa, grown for its edible leaves used in salads.
a leafy green vegetable (Lactuca genus)

Examples

She added fresh lettuce to her sandwich for a crunchy texture.
Different varieties of lettuce are popular in salads around the world.
The chef prefers to use organic lettuce in his recipes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: romaine - a type of lettuce with long crisp leaves

Etymology

From Old French 'laitues', from Latin 'lactuca' (milk plant), referring to its milky juice.
