bicycle

1. VERB

/ ˈbaɪ.sɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈbaɪ.sɪ.kl̩ / (BrE)
to ride a bicycle
to ride a bicycle

Examples

She likes to bicycle along the river in the mornings.
He bicycled to work every day last summer.
They spent the afternoon bicycling around the park.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cycle - to ride a bicycle

Etymology

From French 'bicyclette', based on 'bi-' (two) and Greek 'kyklos' (wheel).

2. NOUN

/ ˈbaɪ.sɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈbaɪ.sɪ.kəl / (BrE)
a vehicle with two wheels that is powered by a person pushing pedals with their feet
two-wheeled pedal-powered vehicle

Examples

She learned to ride a bicycle when she was five years old.
Every morning, he commutes to work on his bicycle.
They parked their bicycle outside the café while they had lunch.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bike - a shortened, informal term for bicycle
  • Degree Higher: tricycle - a vehicle with three wheels, especially for children
  • Degree Lower: unicycle - a vehicle with one wheel, usually ridden for performance or skill

Etymology

From French 'bicyclette', based on Latin 'bi-' (two) and Greek 'kyklos' (wheel).

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n
