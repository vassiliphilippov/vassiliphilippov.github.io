unpainted

1. ADJ

/ ʌnˈpeɪntɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈpeɪntɪd / (BrE)
(of a person's face) not wearing makeup; natural-looking.
(of face) without makeup

Examples

She preferred to keep her unpainted face and let her natural beauty show.
In the morning light, her unpainted cheeks glowed with innocence.
The actress appeared onstage with an unpainted face, surprising her audience.

Synset Examples

her sweet unpainted face

Etymology

From un- (not) + painted; 'paint' was historically used to refer to makeup.

2. ADJ

/ ʌnˈpeɪntɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈpeɪntɪd / (BrE)
not covered with paint, or lacking a fresh or current coat of paint
not covered with paint

Examples

The barn stood out with its unpainted wooden walls weathered by the sun.
They left the fence unpainted to preserve its natural look.
Several unpainted chairs were stacked in the corner of the workshop.

Synset Examples

an unpainted house
unpainted furniture

Related Terms

  • Antonym: painted - covered with paint

Etymology

Formed from the prefix 'un-' (not) + 'painted', past participle of 'paint'.
