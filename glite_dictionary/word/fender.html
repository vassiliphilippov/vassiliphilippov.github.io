fender

1. NOUN

/ ˈfɛndɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɛndə / (BrE)
An inclined metal frame attached to the front of a locomotive to clear obstacles from the track.
inclined metal frame on a locomotive

Examples

The old steam engine's fender was dented from years of clearing debris.
Engineers inspected the fender after the locomotive hit a fallen branch on the tracks.
A broken fender can make the locomotive vulnerable to obstacles on the railway.

2. NOUN

/ ˈfɛndɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɛndə / (BrE)
a part of a vehicle that covers and protects the wheel area, preventing mud, water, or debris from being thrown up
protective covering over a vehicle's wheels

Examples

The car's fender was dented after the minor accident.
Rain splashed against the fender as the truck drove through the puddle.
He polished the fender to remove the mud and keep the car looking clean.

Synset Examples

in Britain they call a fender a ‘wing’

Related Terms

  • Synonym: wing - British term for the covering over a vehicle's wheel

Etymology

From Old English *fend* (to ward off, defend) + -er.

3. NOUN

/ ˈfɛn.dɚ / (AmE), / ˈfen.də / (BrE)
a low metal frame or guard placed in front of a fireplace to keep coals or ashes from falling onto the floor
fireplace guard for coals or ashes

Examples

The ornate fender kept burning coals from rolling onto the carpet.
Please polish the brass fender before the guests arrive for dinner.
In the old cottage, a battered iron fender surrounded the hearth.

Etymology

From fend (to ward off, protect) + -er (agent noun suffix); related to the idea of protecting from falling coals.

Source: WordNet

Synset POS: n

4. NOUN

/ ˈfɛn.dɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɛn.də / (BrE)
a cushion-like device, often made of rubber or rope, placed against a boat, dock, or other structure to absorb shock and prevent damage from impact
cushioning device to prevent damage from impact

Examples

We tied the fender to the side of the boat to avoid damage while docking.
The marina requires every boat to have at least one fender on each side.
As the yacht approached the pier, the crew prepared the fenders to soften the impact.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: bumper - a bar or pad that absorbs impact, as on cars or boats
