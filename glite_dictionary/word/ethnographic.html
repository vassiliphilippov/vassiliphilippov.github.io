ethnographic

1. ADJ

/ ˌɛθ.nəˈɡræf.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛθ.nəˈɡræf.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to the scientific description of peoples and cultures with their customs, habits, and mutual differences
relating to ethnography or cultural study

Examples

The researcher published several ethnographic studies on rural communities in Southeast Asia.
We attended a lecture about ethnographic methods used in social anthropology.
Her ethnographic fieldwork revealed fascinating cultural rituals previously undocumented.

Synset Examples

ethnographical data

Related Terms

  • Synonym: ethnographical - alternative form of 'ethnographic'
  • Synonym: ethnography - the scientific description of peoples and cultures

Etymology

From Greek ethnos (nation, people) + -graphic (from graphein, to write).
