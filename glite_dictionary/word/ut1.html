UT1

1. NOUN

/ ˌjuːˌtiː ˈwʌn / (AmE), / ˌjuːˌtiː ˈwʌn / (BrE)
a precise form of Universal Time, based on the rotation of the Earth and measured at the prime meridian in Greenwich, used in astronomy and navigation
precise astronomical time standard based on Earth's rotation

Examples

Astronomers often use UT1 as a reference for precise celestial measurements.
The difference between UT1 and UTC is carefully monitored due to variations in Earth's rotation.
International clocks are sometimes synchronized with UT1 for scientific experiments.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: UTC - Coordinated Universal Time, the global civil time standard

Etymology

Abbreviation of Universal Time 1, a refinement of Universal Time.
