lancewood

1. NOUN

/ ˈlænsˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈlɑːnsˌwʊd / (BrE)
A strong, elastic wood obtained from certain tropical trees, especially used for shafts and sporting goods.
strong elastic wood from tropical trees

Examples

The craftsman chose lancewood for the fishing rod because of its flexibility and strength.
Early carriage makers valued lancewood for making durable shafts and frames.
Many walking sticks are still crafted from lancewood due to its resilience.

2. NOUN

/ ˈlænsˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈlɑːnsˌwʊd / (BrE)
A durable, straight-grained wood from certain tropical trees, used for building, cabinetwork, and tool handles.
a strong, straight-grained wood used in construction and tools

Examples

The craftsman chose lancewood for his walking stick because of its strength and elasticity.
High-quality bows are sometimes made from lancewood due to its straight grain.
The furniture maker imported lancewood for use in building durable cabinet frames.

Etymology

Named for its traditional use in making lances and spear shafts, referring to several tropical trees supplying such wood.
