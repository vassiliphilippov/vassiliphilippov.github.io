Marchantiaceae

1. NOUN

/ mɑːrˌkænʃiˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / mɑːˌkæntɪˈeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
A family of liverworts with flattened, usually dichotomously branched thalli, commonly found in moist habitats.
family of liverworts (plants)

Examples

The Marchantiaceae are commonly studied in botany classes focused on non-vascular plants.
Several members of the Marchantiaceae family thrive in damp, shaded locations along riverbanks.
Researchers discovered a new species within the Marchantiaceae during their fieldwork in the Amazon rainforest.

Etymology

From New Latin Marchantia (the type genus, named after Italian botanist Nicolas Marchant) + -aceae (indicating a plant family).

2. NOUN

/ mɑːrˌkænʃiˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / mɑːˌkantɪaɪˈsiːiː / (BrE)
a family of liverworts characterized by prostrate, usually dichotomously branched thalli
family of liverworts with branched thalli

Examples

The Marchantiaceae are often studied due to their unique reproductive structures.
Members of the Marchantiaceae family can be found in moist, shady environments.
Researchers observed the dichotomous branching of thalli in Marchantiaceae specimens.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: liverwort - a non-vascular plant belonging to the division Marchantiophyta

Etymology

From the genus Marchantia, named after Italian botanist Nicolò Marchant, with the suffix -aceae denoting a botanical family.
