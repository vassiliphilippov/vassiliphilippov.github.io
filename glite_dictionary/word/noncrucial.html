noncrucial

1. ADJ

/ nɑnˈkruːʃəl / (AmE), / nɒnˈkruːʃəl / (BrE)
Not very important or decisive; not crucial.
not very important; not crucial

Examples

His supervisor assured him that the mistake was noncrucial and would not affect the final outcome.
We postponed several noncrucial meetings to focus on urgent matters.
During the audit, they ignored noncrucial details to concentrate on major discrepancies.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: crucial - extremely important or decisive

Etymology

Formed with the prefix 'non-' meaning 'not' + 'crucial'.

2. ADJ

/ nɑnˈkruʃəl / (AmE), / nɒnˈkruːʃl / (BrE)
Not crucial; not essential or critical to the outcome.
not crucial; not essential

Examples

The meeting was postponed because it was considered noncrucial to the project's success.
Please prioritize the important tasks and handle the noncrucial ones later.
In an emergency, focus on vital procedures and leave noncrucial activities until the situation stabilizes.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: crucial - of great importance; decisive or critical

Etymology

Formed with the prefix 'non-' (not) and 'crucial' (essential or decisive).
