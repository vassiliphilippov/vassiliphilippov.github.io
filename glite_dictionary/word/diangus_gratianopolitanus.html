Diangus gratianopolitanus

1. NOUN

/ daɪˈænɡəs ˌɡræʃiəˌnɑːpəˈleɪnəs / (AmE), / daɪˈaŋɡəs ˌɡræʃiəˌnɒpəˈleɪnəs / (BrE)
a mat-forming perennial plant native to central Europe, known for its large, fragrant pink or red flowers
European mat-forming flowering plant

Examples

The Diangus gratianopolitanus is often cultivated in rock gardens for its attractive flowers.
Botanists have documented Diangus gratianopolitanus populations across several central European countries.
The sweet scent of Diangus gratianopolitanus attracts many pollinators during the summer months.

Etymology

From Latin; 'Diangus' is the genus name, 'gratianopolitanus' refers to being from Grenoble, France (ancient Gratianopolis).
