stylist

1. NOUN

/ ˈstaɪlɪst / (AmE), / ˈstaɪlɪst / (BrE)
an artist or writer who is distinguished for their mastery of a particular style
artist or writer skilled in a particular style

Examples

The novelist is known as a stylist whose language is both rich and precise.
As a literary stylist, he transformed simple stories into elegant prose.
Critics praised the painter as a stylist for her distinctive use of color and form.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: artist - a person skilled in a creative activity

Etymology

From style + -ist, denoting a person who practices or is concerned with something.

2. NOUN

/ ˈstaɪlɪst / (AmE), / ˈstaɪlɪst / (BrE)
a person whose job is to cut, shape, and beautify hair; a hairdresser or professional in hair styling.
person who styles or cuts hair

Examples

She has been my stylist for over five years and always knows exactly what I want.
The stylist recommended a new haircut to frame my face.
After moving to the city, it took me a while to find a stylist I could trust.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hairdresser - a person who cuts and styles hair professionally
  • Synonym: barber - a person who cuts men's hair and trims beards

Etymology

From style + -ist, meaning a person who is concerned with or skilled in a particular style.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-10175409-n
Synset POS: n
