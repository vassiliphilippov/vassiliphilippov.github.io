to foretell

1. VERB

/ fɔrˈtɛl / (AmE), / fɔːˈtɛl / (BrE)
to state or predict what will happen in the future before it occurs
predict or announce in advance

Examples

Many believed the old woman could foretell the future with astonishing accuracy.
Several economic analysts tried to foretell the impact of the new policy.
Legends claim that comets in the sky foretell major changes in the world.

Synset Examples

Call the outcome of an election

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to predict - to say what will happen in the future
  • Synonym: to prophesy - to state something will happen in the future, often with divine inspiration
  • Degree Lower: guess - to estimate or suppose without sufficient information to be certain

Etymology

From Old English 'foretellan' (to tell beforehand), from 'fore-' (before) + 'tell'.

2. VERB

/ fɔrˈtɛl / (AmE), / fɔːˈtɛl / (BrE)
to indicate or predict a future event, especially by signs or omens
predict or indicate a future event

Examples

Dark clouds on the horizon seemed to foretell a coming storm.
Legends say that the wise woman could foretell disasters by reading the stars.
The sudden silence in the forest seemed to foretell some unexpected danger.

Synset Examples

These signs bode bad news

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to predict - to say that something will happen in the future
  • Synonym: to prophesy - to state that something will happen in the future as if by divine inspiration
  • Synonym: to foresee - to see or know about something before it happens

Etymology

From Old English 'fore-' (before) + 'tellan' (to tell).

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00873606-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ fɔrˈtɛl / (AmE), / fɔːˈtɛl / (BrE)
to say what will happen in the future, especially using special knowledge or powers; to predict or be a sign of a future event
predict or indicate a future event

Examples

Many ancient cultures believed priests could foretell future events through omens and visions.
The dark clouds seemed to foretell a coming storm.
She claimed that her dreams foretold disasters before they happened.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to predict - to say that an event or action will happen in the future
  • Synonym: to prophesy - to say that a specified thing will happen in the future
  • Antonym: retrospect - to consider or review past events

Etymology

From Old English 'fore-' (before) + 'tellan' (to tell).
