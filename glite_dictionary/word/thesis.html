thesis

1. NOUN

/ ˈθiːsɪs / (AmE), / ˈθiːsɪs / (BrE)
an idea or statement that is put forward as a premise to be maintained or proved, but has not yet been proved
unproved proposition in an argument

Examples

The philosopher presented a controversial thesis that challenged traditional beliefs.
In the debate, her main thesis was that economic inequality leads to social unrest.
Every scientific theory begins as a thesis that must be tested through evidence.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: hypothesis - a proposed explanation made on the basis of limited evidence
  • Antonym: antithesis - the direct opposite or contrast of a thesis

Etymology

From Greek *thesis* (a placing, proposition).

2. NOUN

/ ˈθiː.sɪs / (AmE), / ˈθiː.sɪs / (BrE)
a long piece of writing based on original research, submitted as part of the requirements for a university degree, especially a doctorate or master's degree
long research paper for a degree

Examples

She spent three years researching and writing her thesis on early modern philosophy.
His thesis was praised for its originality and depth of analysis.
Many students feel anxious about defending their thesis in front of a committee.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dissertation - a long essay, especially one written for a doctoral degree

Etymology

From Greek *thesis* (a placing, proposition, thesis), via Latin *thesis*.

Synset POS: n
