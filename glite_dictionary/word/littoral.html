littoral

1. ADJ

/ ˈlɪtərəl / (AmE), / ˈlɪtərəl / (BrE)
relating to or situated on the shore of the sea or a lake
of or near the shore

Examples

The littoral zone is home to a diverse array of marine life.
Many plants in the littoral region have adapted to saltwater conditions.
The army was tasked with defending the littoral areas against invasion.

Etymology

From Latin littoralis, from litus (shore, coast).

2. NOUN

/ ˈlɪtərəl / (AmE), / ˈlɪtərəl / (BrE)
the part of the shore of a sea, lake, or ocean, especially the region lying between the high- and low-water marks
the region along a shore or coast

Examples

Many species of birds nest in the littoral along the lake during spring.
The littoral was rich with tidal pools and marine life.
Erosion has dramatically changed the littoral in this region over the past decade.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coast - the land next to the sea; seashore
  • Antonym: inland - situated away from the coast or shore

Etymology

From Latin 'litoralis', from 'litus' (shore).
