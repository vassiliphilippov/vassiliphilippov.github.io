Littorinidae

1. NOUN

/ lɪˌtɔrəˈnɪdiː / (AmE), / lɪˌtɒrɪˈnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, mostly marine snails, commonly known as periwinkles, found on rocky shores worldwide.
family of small marine snails (periwinkles)
medical

Examples

Scientists collected specimens of Littorinidae from the rocky tidepools.
Many species in the Littorinidae family are adapted to life in harsh coastal environments.
The Littorinidae are commonly studied to understand how marine snails survive wave action.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: periwinkle - a common name for snails in the Littorinidae family

Etymology

From the genus name Littorina, from Latin 'littoralis' meaning 'of the seashore', plus taxonomic suffix -idae.
