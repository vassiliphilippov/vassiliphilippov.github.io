to take too far

1. VERB

/ teɪk tu fɑr / (AmE), / teɪk tʃuː fɑː / (BrE)
to extend or continue something beyond what is considered reasonable or acceptable
go beyond reasonable limits

Examples

Sometimes comedians take too far their jokes and offend people in the audience.
You must be careful not to take too far your criticism of others.
I think the teacher took too far the punishment for being late.

Synset Examples

They took the joke too far and upset everyone.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go too far - to act beyond the limits of what is acceptable
