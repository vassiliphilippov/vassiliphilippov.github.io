to heat up

1. VERB

/ hit ʌp / (AmE), / hiːt ʌp / (BrE)
to become hot or hotter; to make something become hot or hotter
become hot or make hot

Examples

If you leave the oven on, the kitchen will heat up quickly.
The rivalry between the teams is expected to heat up as the finals approach.
Can you heat up the soup before dinner?

Synset Examples

The room heated up quickly

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to cool down - (of a person or thing) to become cooler or less hot
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00373318-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ hit ʌp / (AmE), / hiːt ʌp / (BrE)
to make something hot or hotter; to raise the temperature of something.
make hot or hotter

Examples

Could you heat up the soup before dinner?
If you heat up the engine too quickly, it might get damaged.
Leftovers can easily be heated up in the microwave.

Synset Examples

the sun heats the oceans
heat the water on the stove

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to warm up - to make or become warm; to increase in warmth
  • Antonym: to cool down - to make or become cooler
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00371917-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ hit ʌp / (AmE), / hiːt ʌp / (BrE)
to become or make something become more intense, active, or exciting
become or make more intense

Examples

Tensions began to heat up as the debate progressed.
Competition between the two companies really heated up last year.
Things are heating up in the race for the championship.

Synset Examples

Emotions were screwed up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to intensify - to become or make more intense
  • Antonym: to cool down - to become or make less intense
