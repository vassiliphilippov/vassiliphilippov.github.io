to collect dust

1. VERB

/ kəˈlɛkt dʌst / (AmE), / kəˈlɛkt dʌst / (BrE)
to remain unused or neglected for a period of time, often gathering literal or figurative dust
remain unused or neglected

Examples

The old trophies sat on the shelf and began to collect dust.
Her unused bicycle has been collecting dust in the garage for months.
Many childhood dreams end up collecting dust as priorities change.

Synset Examples

Books left unread tend to collect dust on the shelf.
His ambitions started to collect dust after years of procrastination.
The old guitar has been collecting dust in the corner.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gather dust - to become covered with dust from disuse
