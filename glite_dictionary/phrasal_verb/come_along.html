to come along

1. VERB

/ kʌm əˈlɔŋ / (AmE), / kʌm əˈlɒŋ / (BrE)
to develop, improve, or make progress, especially in a positive way
develop or improve positively

Examples

My Spanish skills have really come along since I started taking classes.
How is your new project coming along?
After a slow start, the renovations are finally coming along nicely.

Synset Examples

He progressed well in school
My plants are coming along
Plans are shaping up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: progress - to move forward or improve over time
  • Synonym: advance - to move forward or develop further
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kʌm əˈlɔŋ / (AmE), / kʌm əˈlɒŋ / (BrE)
to appear or become available, especially suddenly or unexpectedly
to appear or become available

Examples

A new opportunity may come along when you least expect it.
Digital cameras came along and revolutionized photography.
The right person will come along eventually, so don’t rush things.

Synset Examples

Then the computer came along and changed our lives
Homo sapiens appeared millions of years ago

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to appear - to come into sight; become visible or present
  • Antonym: to vanish - to disappear entirely; cease to be visible or present
