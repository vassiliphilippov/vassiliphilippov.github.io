to bargain for

1. VERB

/ ˈbɑrɡən fɔr / (AmE), / ˈbɑːɡɪn fə / (BrE)
to expect or be prepared for something, especially something difficult or surprising
expect or be prepared for

Examples

She got much more work than she had bargained for when she accepted the job.
This project is taking longer than we had bargained for.
The difficulty of the exam was more than the students had bargained for.

Synset Examples

The unexpected snowstorm was more than one bargained for.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to anticipate - to regard as probable; expect or predict
  • Synonym: reckon on - to expect something to happen
