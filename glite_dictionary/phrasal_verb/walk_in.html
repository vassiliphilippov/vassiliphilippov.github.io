to walk in

1. VERB

/ wɒk ɪn / (AmE), / wɔːk ɪn / (BrE)
to enter a place by walking, especially in a casual or unannounced manner
enter a place by walking

Examples

She always walks in without knocking, surprising everyone in the room.
He walked in as the meeting was about to end, drawing everyone's attention.
Please don't walk in when the movie has already started.

Synset Examples

She walks in at all hours, as if she lived here

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to walk into - to enter a place by walking, often with emphasis on arrival or entry
