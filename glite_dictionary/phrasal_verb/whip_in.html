to whip in

1. VERB

/ wɪp ɪn / (AmE), / wɪp ɪn / (BrE)
to add or incorporate something quickly and with little effort
add or include quickly

Examples

She managed to whip in a few last-minute changes before the deadline.
If you want, you can whip in some fresh herbs at the end for more flavor.
He whipped in a quick joke during his presentation to lighten the mood.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to throw in - to add something extra without much planning
  • Antonym: to leave out - to omit or exclude something
