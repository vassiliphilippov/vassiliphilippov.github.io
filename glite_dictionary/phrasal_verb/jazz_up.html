to jazz up

1. VERB

/ dʒæz ʌp / (AmE), / dʒæz ʌp / (BrE)
to make something more interesting, lively, or exciting
make more lively or interesting
informal

Examples

She decided to jazz up her living room with some colorful cushions.
The chef likes to jazz up his dishes with unusual spices.
You can jazz up your outfit by adding a bright scarf.

Synset Examples

juice up a party
pep up your paper

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to liven up - make something more lively or energetic
  • Synonym: to brighten up - make something more cheerful or attractive

Etymology

From 'jazz', American English slang for energy or excitement, early 20th century.
