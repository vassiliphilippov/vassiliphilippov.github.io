to serve notice

1. VERB

/ sɝv ˈnoʊtɪs / (AmE), / sɜːv ˈnəʊtɪs / (BrE)
to formally inform someone, especially by giving official written notification
formally inform someone; give official notice

Examples

The landlord decided to serve notice to the tenants before the lease expired.
The company will serve notice to all employees regarding the new policy.
He intends to serve notice if there is no improvement in working conditions.

Synset Examples

They decided to serve notice to the tenants.
The company will serve notice to all employees next week.
He was going to serve notice about the upcoming changes.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to notify - to inform someone officially
  • Synonym: to warn - to inform someone in advance of possible danger or problem
