to dropitis kick in

1. VERB

/ drɑːˈpaɪtɪs kɪk ɪn / (AmE), / drɒˈpaɪtɪs kɪk ɪn / (BrE)
to experience a sudden, irresistible urge to buy unnecessary items, often triggered by exposure to new products or sales
to suddenly feel compelled to shop impulsively
informal slang

Examples

Every time there's a big sale, his dropitis kick in and he can't resist grabbing new gadgets.
The moment new sneakers hit the store, my dropitis kick in and I start shopping online.
Watching fashion influencers makes her dropitis kick in and she instantly wants to buy everything they recommend.

Synset Examples

His dropitis kicked in when he saw the new gadgets.

Etymology

Formed from slang 'dropitis' (derived from 'drop' referring to product releases) combined with the phrasal verb 'kick in' meaning 'to begin suddenly'.
