to touch off

1. VERB

/ tʌtʃ ɔf / (AmE), / tʌtʃ ɒf / (BrE)
to cause something, especially a process or situation, to start suddenly; to provoke or trigger an event or reaction
provoke or trigger an event

Examples

The scandal touched off a series of protests across the country.
A single spark could touch off a forest fire in these dry conditions.
Her comments touched off a heated debate among the audience.

Synset Examples

trigger a reaction
actuate the circuits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trigger - to cause something to start
  • Synonym: to set off - to cause something to begin or happen
  • Antonym: to quell - to put an end to (especially by force); to suppress

2. VERB

/ tʌtʃ ɔf / (AmE), / tʌtʃ ɒf / (BrE)
to cause something to start or happen suddenly, especially something violent or explosive
trigger or initiate suddenly

Examples

The politician's speech touched off angry demonstrations across the city.
A spark can touch off a massive forest fire if the conditions are right.
The announcement touched off a heated argument among the group.

Synset Examples

The fireworks will touch off at midnight.
The news article could touch off protests across the city.
His comment was enough to touch off a heated debate.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: trigger - to cause something to begin
  • Synonym: to spark off - to cause something to happen, especially suddenly or violently
  • Antonym: to prevent - to stop something from happening
