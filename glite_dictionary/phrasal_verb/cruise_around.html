to cruise around

1. VERB

/ kruːz əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / kruːz əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to drive or travel slowly and leisurely around an area, especially without a specific destination
drive or travel leisurely without a set route

Examples

They spent the afternoon cruising around town, enjoying the nice weather.
He likes cruising around the city at night to relax after work.
We were cruising around looking for a good spot to eat.

Synset Examples

The charvette is perfect for cruising around the city.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to drive around - to drive a vehicle around an area
  • Synonym: to wander - to move or travel without a fixed course or aim

Etymology

From 'cruise', originally from Dutch 'kruisen' (to cross), meaning to travel for pleasure, combined with 'around'.
