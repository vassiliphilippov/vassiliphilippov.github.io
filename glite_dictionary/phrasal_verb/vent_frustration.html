to vent frustration

1. VERB

/ vɛnt frəˈstreɪʃən / (AmE), / vɛnt frʌˈstreɪʃən / (BrE)
to express strong negative emotions or anger, often by talking, shouting, or other outward actions
express anger or negative emotions

Examples

After a stressful day at work, he needed to vent frustration by going for a run.
It's important to vent frustration in healthy ways rather than bottling it up.
She called her sister just to vent frustration about her team's poor performance.

Synset Examples

She found it helpful to vent her frustrations to a friend.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: let off steam - to express feelings, especially anger or frustration, to relieve tension
