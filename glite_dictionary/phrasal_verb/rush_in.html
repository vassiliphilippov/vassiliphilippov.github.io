to rush in

1. VERB

/ rʌʃ ɪn / (AmE), / rʌʃ ɪn / (BrE)
to enter a place suddenly or quickly, often without careful thought or consideration
enter quickly without thinking

Examples

As soon as the store opened, customers began to rush in looking for bargains.
He didn't think before he decided to rush in and help with the situation.
People often rush in to offer advice, even when it's not wanted.

Synset Examples

They always rush in when the doors open.
He tends to rush in during emergencies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to burst in - to enter a place abruptly and noisily
  • Antonym: to hesitate - to pause before doing something, often due to uncertainty
