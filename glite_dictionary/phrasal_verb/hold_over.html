to hold over

1. VERB

/ hoʊld ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / həʊld ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to keep goods unsold so they can be offered for sale in the next season
keep goods to sell next season

Examples

Due to poor sales, the company decided to hold over its summer inventory until next year.
Farmers sometimes hold over their crops for better market prices in the following season.
The shop didn't sell all its winter coats, so it will hold over the remaining stock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stockpile - to accumulate goods for future use or sale
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ hoʊld ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / həʊld ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to use a threat or information to intimidate or have power over someone
intimidate or control by a threat

Examples

She threatened to hold over the secret unless he complied.
He can't hold over that mistake forever; it's time to move on.
The manager tried to hold over the staff by hinting at possible layoffs.

Synset Examples

She was holding it over him

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to intimidate - to frighten or threaten someone in order to control them

3. VERB

/ hoʊld ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / həʊld ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to continue to serve in an official position after the normal term has expired, often temporarily until a successor is appointed
continue term of office beyond usual period

Examples

The governor will hold over until a replacement is appointed.
He was asked to hold over in office for an additional three months.
Sometimes, officials hold over if there is a delay in confirming their successors.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to remain - to continue to exist or stay in a position

4. VERB

/ hoʊld ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / həʊld ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to postpone something so that it happens at a later time
postpone to a later time

Examples

Due to the storm, the committee decided to hold over the meeting until next week.
The theater will hold over the film's release because the renovations aren't finished.
We agreed to hold over discussion of the new policy to the next session.

Synset Examples

let's postpone the exam

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to postpone - to delay something until a later time
  • Antonym: to bring forward - to change something so it happens earlier

5. VERB

/ hoʊld ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / həʊld ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to keep someone or something in a position, condition, or state from an earlier time or period
keep from an earlier period

Examples

Due to the popularity of the play, the theater decided to hold over its run for another week.
Some members of the committee were held over from last year to help with the transition.
The law was held over from colonial times despite changes in government.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to retain - to continue to have or keep something
  • Synonym: to carry over - to transfer or extend to another time or situation
