to draw blood

1. VERB

/ drɔ bʌd / (AmE), / drɔː blʌd / (BrE)
to take a blood sample from someone for medical testing; also, to cause an injury that makes someone bleed, sometimes used figuratively to mean inflicting harm, especially in a confrontation or argument.
take a blood sample; cause to bleed or harm

Examples

The nurse needed to draw blood from the patient for a routine test.
During the boxing match, one punch finally drew blood.
She knew her words would draw blood in the heated argument.

Synset Examples

In the heated argument, he knew he would draw blood.
The doctor needed to draw blood for testing.
The athlete managed to draw blood after a tough match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: take blood - to remove blood, especially for medical reasons
