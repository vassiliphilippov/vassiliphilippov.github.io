to take stage

1. VERB

/ teɪk steɪdʒ / (AmE), / teɪk steɪdʒ / (BrE)
to become the center of attention; to attract focus onto oneself, especially in a public setting
become the center of attention

Examples

The young actor managed to take stage with his powerful performance.
When she entered the room, her charisma allowed her to immediately take stage.
He loves to take stage at every opportunity, always drawing attention to himself.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to steal the show - to attract the most attention and praise, often unexpectedly
