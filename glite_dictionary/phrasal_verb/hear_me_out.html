to hear me out

1. VERB

/ hɪr mi aʊt / (AmE), / hɪə mi aʊt / (BrE)
to listen to someone until they have finished speaking, especially before judging or interrupting
listen to someone fully before responding

Examples

Hear me out before you decide you don't like the idea.
She asked him to hear her out before making any judgment.
If you just hear me out, I think you'll understand my perspective.

Synset Examples

Please hear me out before making a decision.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to listen - to give attention to someone or something in order to hear them
