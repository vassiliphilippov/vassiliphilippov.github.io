to nickel and dime

1. VERB

/ ˈnɪkəl ənd daɪm / (AmE), / ˈnɪkəl ənd daɪm / (BrE)
to charge or extract small amounts of money repeatedly, often excessively or unfairly
charge small amounts repeatedly
informal american

Examples

The hotel tried to nickel and dime us with extra fees for everything.
Many customers feel frustrated when companies nickel and dime them on routine services.
He was annoyed that his paycheck was nickel and dimed by so many small deductions.

Synset Examples

The company is known to nickel and dime its employees.
They nickel and dime you for every little service.
Stop nickel and diming me; just give me the total!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overcharge - to charge too much for something
  • Synonym: to rip off - to cheat someone by charging too much

Etymology

From U.S. coin values 'nickel' (5 cents) and 'dime' (10 cents), suggesting trivial amounts.
