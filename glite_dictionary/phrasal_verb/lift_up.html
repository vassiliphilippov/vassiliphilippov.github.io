to lift up

1. VERB

/ lɪft ʌp / (AmE), / lɪft ʌp / (BrE)
to fill someone with high spirits or optimism; to encourage or inspire
fill with optimism or high spirits

Examples

Her kind words really lifted up my spirits during a difficult week.
A good book can lift up your mood when you feel down.
His encouragement helped lift up the whole team's morale before the final match.

Synset Examples

Music can uplift your spirits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: uplift - to raise the spirits or mood; to encourage
  • Antonym: to discourage - to cause someone to lose confidence or enthusiasm

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ lɪft ʌp / (AmE), / lɪft ʌp / (BrE)
to raise something or someone to a higher position or level; to pick up and hold or move upwards.
raise to a higher position

Examples

He bent down to lift up the box from the floor.
She lifted up her child so he could see over the crowd.
The wind lifted up the kite into the sky.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raise - to move to a higher position; similar meaning
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01980103-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ lɪft ʌp / (AmE), / lɪft ʌp / (BrE)
to raise or move something or someone to a higher position; to elevate.
raise or elevate to a higher position

Examples

She asked him to lift up the box onto the shelf.
The crowd cheered as the team captain lifted up the trophy.
He had to lift up his head to see over the fence.

Synset Examples

The elevator can lift up to six people.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: raise - to lift or move to a higher position
  • Antonym: lower - to move something to a lower position
