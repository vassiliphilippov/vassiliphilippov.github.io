to follow up

1. VERB

/ ˈfɑloʊ ˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈfɒləʊ ˌʌp / (BrE)
to take further action on something already started, especially to ensure completion or resolution
take further action to complete

Examples

After the meeting, she decided to follow up with an email summarizing the main points.
The police promised to follow up any new leads in the investigation.
If you don't hear back, it's important to follow up on your application.

Synset Examples

Did he go through with the treatment?
He implemented a new economic plan
She followed up his recommendations with a written proposal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to follow through - to continue an activity or task to its completion
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00486949-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈfɑː.loʊ ʌp / (AmE), / ˈfɒl.əʊ ʌp / (BrE)
to take further action to increase the effectiveness or success of something that has already been done
take further action for effectiveness

Examples

The marketing team will follow up the email campaign with a series of phone calls.
It's important for teachers to follow up feedback with practical suggestions.
After the interview, the journalist decided to follow up the story with further investigation.

Synset Examples

The doctor followed up the surgery with radiation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pursue - to continue or proceed with a course of action
