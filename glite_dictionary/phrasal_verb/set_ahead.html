to set ahead

1. VERB

/ sɛt əˈhɛd / (AmE), / sɛt əˈhɛd / (BrE)
to move (something, especially a clock or watch) forward to a later time
move something forward, especially a clock or watch

Examples

Remember to set ahead your clocks one hour when daylight saving time begins.
We had to set ahead all the watches in the house after traveling east.
The staff will set ahead the conference room clock so everyone arrives on time.

Synset Examples

we have to advance clocks and watches when we travel eastward

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to set back - to move something to an earlier time, especially a clock or watch
