to cast aside

1. VERB

/ kæst əˈsaɪd / (AmE), / kɑːst əˈsaɪd / (BrE)
to discard or reject something or someone as unimportant or no longer needed
discard; reject as unimportant

Examples

After the new policy was introduced, many old practices were cast aside.
She decided to cast aside her doubts and move forward with her plan.
Once he realized the coat was torn, he quickly cast aside the garment.

Synset Examples

Put away your worries

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discard - to throw something away or get rid of it
  • Antonym: to retain - to keep; to continue to have something

2. VERB

/ kæst əˈsaɪd / (AmE), / kɑːst əˈsaɪd / (BrE)
to deliberately disregard, ignore, or discard someone or something as unimportant or irrelevant
disregard or discard as unimportant

Examples

She chose to cast aside her doubts and pursue the opportunity.
It's not always easy to cast aside bad habits built up over years.
They decided to cast aside tradition in favor of innovation.

Synset Examples

They cast aside their differences for the project.
She chose to cast aside her doubts and try.
He decided to cast aside his fears and speak up.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: discard - to throw something away or get rid of it
  • Synonym: to ignore - to pay no attention to something or someone
  • Antonym: to consider - to think about something carefully
