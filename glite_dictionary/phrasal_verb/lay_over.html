to lay over

1. VERB

/ leɪ ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / leɪ ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to interrupt a journey temporarily, especially to stay somewhere overnight before continuing to your destination
interrupt a journey temporarily

Examples

We had to lay over in Paris because our connecting flight was delayed until the next morning.
Many passengers choose to lay over in Tokyo to rest before continuing their journey to Australia.
If you lay over in a city, you can often take advantage of free sightseeing tours.

Synset Examples

We had to stop over in Venezuela on our flight back from Brazil

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to stop over - to pause or stay at a place during a journey
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02659017-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ leɪ ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / leɪ ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to place something on top of another object so that it covers or partly covers it
place one thing on top of another

Examples

She carefully laid over a blanket on her sleeping child to keep him warm.
You should lay over the filter sheet on top of the liquid before pouring.
He laid over a fresh page onto the drawing to trace the outline again.

Synset Examples

can you superimpose the two images?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to superimpose - place or lay one thing over another, typically so both are still evident
  • Synonym: cover - to place something over something else and hide it from view
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01471892-v
Synset POS: v
