to know what's going on

1. VERB

/ noʊ wʌts ɡoʊɪŋ ɑn / (AmE), / nəʊ wɒts ɡəʊɪŋ ɒn / (BrE)
to be well-informed about the current situation or events; to understand what is happening
be well-informed or aware of events
informal

Examples

He always seems to know what's going on at work, even before the meetings.
If you want to know what's going on in the city, check the local news.
It took me a while to know what's going on in this complicated project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be in the know - to have special knowledge about something
  • Antonym: be out of the loop - to not be informed about something
