to punt the project

1. VERB

/ pʌnt ðə ˈprɑːdʒɛkt / (AmE), / pʌnt ðə ˈprɒdʒɛkt / (BrE)
to postpone or set aside a project, usually to focus on other priorities
delay or set aside a project
informal american

Examples

Due to budget cuts, they had to punt the project until next year.
After the client changed their requirements, the team decided to punt the project for now.
Instead of rushing, we might as well punt the project and focus on urgent deadlines.

Synset Examples

They decided to punt the project to focus on urgent tasks.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delay - to postpone or put off
  • Synonym: to shelve - to decide not to proceed with something, either temporarily or permanently

Etymology

Informal use of "punt" (from American football, meaning to kick something downfield or defer action).
