to bring to bear

1. VERB

/ brɪŋ tə bɛr / (AmE), / brɪŋ tə beə / (BrE)
to use or apply something, such as influence, effort, or resources, in order to achieve a result
apply or use to achieve an effect

Examples

The committee will bring to bear all available evidence in its investigation.
She brought to bear her considerable experience to solve the difficult problem.
New technologies have been brought to bear to improve patient care.

Synset Examples

The new members brought to bear new concerns to the U.N.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to apply - to put to practical use
  • Synonym: to exert - to put forth or bring to bear, as in force or influence

2. VERB

/ bɹɪŋ tə bɛr / (AmE), / bɹɪŋ tə bəː / (BrE)
to use influence, knowledge, or effort in order to achieve a result or deal with a problem
apply influence, knowledge, or effort

Examples

The committee will bring to bear all its expertise to solve the issue.
He tried to bring to bear every argument in his favor during the negotiation.
Various forms of pressure were brought to bear to achieve the desired outcome.

Synset Examples

He decided to bring his experience to bear on the new initiative.
She will bring all her resources to bear on the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to apply - to put to practical use; employ
  • Synonym: to exert - to make use of (an influence, quality, or resource)
