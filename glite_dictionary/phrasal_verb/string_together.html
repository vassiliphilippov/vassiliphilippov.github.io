to string together

1. VERB

/ strɪŋ təˈɡɛðɚ / (AmE), / strɪŋ tʊˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
to combine or arrange a series of things in a sequence, especially words, ideas, or events
combine or arrange in sequence

Examples

She tried to string together a few sentences in Spanish for the tourists.
It can be challenging to string together consistent wins in a tough season.
He managed to string together all the clues to solve the puzzle.

Synset Examples

They managed to string together a compelling argument.
Let's string together our ideas for the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assemble - to come together or bring together into a single group or whole

Etymology

From 'string' (to thread objects onto a string) + 'together.'

2. VERB

/ strɪŋ təˈɡɛðər / (AmE), / strɪŋ təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
to connect or combine things in a sequence, especially to create something meaningful from separate parts
connect or combine in a sequence

Examples

She strung together a few sentences in French during the meeting.
The documentary strung together several interviews to tell a powerful story.
After years of training, he finally strung together a series of victories.

Synset Examples

He strung together a beautiful melody on his piano.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assemble - to put something together from parts
  • Antonym: to disperse - to scatter or separate things

Etymology

From the verb 'string' (to thread items) + 'together'.
