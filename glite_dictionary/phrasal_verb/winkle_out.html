to winkle out

1. VERB

/ ˈwɪŋkəl aʊt / (AmE), / ˈwɪŋkəl aʊt / (BrE)
to remove or force someone or something out of a place or position, often with effort or difficulty
remove or force out with effort

Examples

It took hours to winkle out the old files from the back of the cabinet.
She finally winkled out the truth from her reluctant friend.
The reporter managed to winkle out some valuable information from the witnesses.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extract - remove or take out, especially by effort
  • Synonym: to dislodge - remove from a position or place

Etymology

From 'winkle', a type of small shellfish; by analogy with extracting the creature from its shell.

2. VERB

/ ˈwɪŋkəl aʊt / (AmE), / ˈwɪŋkəl aʊt / (BrE)
to force or extract someone or something from a place or position, often with difficulty
extract or force out with effort

Examples

It took hours to winkle out the truth from him.
The auditors managed to winkle out the hidden funds.
She was finally winkled out of her comfortable job.

Synset Examples

The committee winkled out the unqualified candidates

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extract - to remove or take out, especially by effort or force
  • Synonym: to evict - to force someone to leave a place

Etymology

From 'winkle' (a type of small edible sea snail), alluding to the effort required to extract the snail from its shell.
