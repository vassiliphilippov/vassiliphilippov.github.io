to reel with shock

1. VERB

/ ɹil wɪð ʃɑk / (AmE), / ɹiːl wɪð ʃɒk / (BrE)
to be so shocked or surprised by something that you are temporarily unable to react or think clearly
to be stunned or overwhelmed by shock

Examples

She began to reel with shock when she heard the unexpected announcement.
He could only reel with shock after witnessing the accident.
After the verdict, they all reeled with shock, unable to speak for several minutes.

Synset Examples

The news made her reel with shock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: reel - to lose balance as if about to fall, often from a blow or strong emotion
  • Synonym: stagger - to walk or move unsteadily, as if about to fall
