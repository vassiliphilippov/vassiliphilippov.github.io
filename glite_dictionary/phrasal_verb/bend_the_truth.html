to bend the truth

1. VERB

/ bɛnd ðə truθ / (AmE), / bɛnd ðə truːθ / (BrE)
to misrepresent or slightly distort the facts, usually to make something seem more favorable or less harsh
misrepresent or distort the facts

Examples

Sometimes politicians bend the truth to suit their agenda.
I didn't want to hurt her feelings, so I bent the truth a little.
He has a habit of bending the truth whenever he's in trouble.

Synset Examples

It's easy to bend the truth when lying.
He tends to bend the truth in his favor.
Don't bend the truth; just tell it like it is.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: lie - to say something that is not true
  • Antonym: to tell the truth - to state facts accurately without alteration
