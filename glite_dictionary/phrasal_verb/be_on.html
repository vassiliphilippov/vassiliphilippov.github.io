to be on

1. VERB

/ bi ɑn / (AmE), / bi ɒn / (BrE)
to be broadcast or shown on television or radio
be broadcast on TV or radio

Examples

The movie will be on at 8 p.m. tonight.
Is the football game on now, or does it start later?
That show used to be on every Friday night when I was a child.

Synset Examples

The news won't be on tonight

Related Terms

  • Synonym: air - to be broadcast on television or radio
