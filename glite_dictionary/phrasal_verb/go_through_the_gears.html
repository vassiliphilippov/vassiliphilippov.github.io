to go through the gears

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈθru ðə ɡɪrz / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈθruː ðə ɡɪəz / (BrE)
to shift sequentially through the transmission settings of a vehicle, such as when accelerating or decelerating
shift through a vehicle's transmission settings

Examples

The sports car can go through the gears rapidly for quick acceleration.
She loves the feeling when her motorcycle goes through the gears on an open road.
Listen for any unusual sounds while you go through the gears during your driving test.

Synset Examples

The car seemed to go through the gears smoothly.
He watched her go through the gears expertly while driving.
The bike will go through the gears effortlessly on the hill.
