to believe in luck

1. VERB

/ bəˈliv ɪn lʌk / (AmE), / bɪˈliːv ɪn lʌk / (BrE)
to trust that luck or chance influences events in life or outcomes
trust in luck or chance

Examples

Although Maria doesn't believe in luck, she always carries a rabbit's foot just in case.
It's common for athletes to believe in luck before important games or competitions.
Some people believe in luck so strongly that they follow elaborate rituals.

Synset Examples

Some people believe in luck, while others do not.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: believe in fate - to trust in destiny or predetermined outcomes
  • Synonym: be superstitious - to have irrational beliefs in supernatural influences on events
