to take a look

1. VERB

/ teɪk ə lʊk / (AmE), / teɪk ə lʊk / (BrE)
to look at something, especially in order to examine or inspect it closely
look at something attentively

Examples

Let me take a look at your report before you submit it.
You should take a look at the website for more information.
Could you take a look at my car? It's making a strange noise.

Synset Examples

Have a look at this!
Get a load of this pretty woman!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to have a look - to look at something (synonymous phrasing)
