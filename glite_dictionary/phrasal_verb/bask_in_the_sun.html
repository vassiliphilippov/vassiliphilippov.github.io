to bask in the sun

1. VERB

/ bæsk ɪn ðə sʌn / (AmE), / bɑːsk ɪn ðə sʌn / (BrE)
to relax and enjoy the warmth and light from the sun
to relax in warm sunlight

Examples

The elderly man loved to bask in the sun on his porch every morning.
After months of cold weather, we finally got to bask in the sun at the beach.
The lizard moved onto the rock to bask in the sun and warm itself.

Synset Examples

The walrus at the zoo loved to bask in the sun.
The cat loves to bask in the sun all day.
We sat outside to bask in the sunshine.
Children love to bask in the sunshine during summer.
After the rain, we finally got to bask in the sunshine.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sunbathe - to sit or lie in the sun, especially to get a suntan
  • Synonym: to bask - to lie or sit enjoying warmth, especially from the sun
