to chalk out

1. VERB

/ tʃɑk aʊt / (AmE), / tʃɔːk aʊt / (BrE)
to make a rough plan, outline, or sketch of something
make a rough plan or outline

Examples

Before starting construction, the architect will chalk out the initial design for the team.
The coach plans to chalk out a new strategy for the upcoming match.
Let's chalk out our roles for the project before we begin.

Synset Examples

sketch the building

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sketch out - to make a rough drawing or outline of

Etymology

From the use of chalk for drawing outlines or sketches, especially on blackboards or other surfaces.
