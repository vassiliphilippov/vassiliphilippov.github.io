to strike a light

1. VERB

/ straɪk ə laɪt / (AmE), / straɪk ə laɪt / (BrE)
to produce a flame or ignition, typically by striking a match or flint
produce a flame or ignition

Examples

She rummaged in her bag to strike a light for the lantern.
It was too dark to see, so he quickly tried to strike a light.
After several attempts, they finally managed to strike a light and warm themselves by the fire.

Synset Examples

He asked her to strike a light for the candle.
Can you strike a light? It’s too dark here.
They need to strike a light before starting the campfire.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ignite - to catch fire or cause to catch fire
