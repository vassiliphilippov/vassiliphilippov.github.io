to usher in

1. VERB

/ ˈʌʃər ɪn / (AmE), / ˈʌʃə ɪn / (BrE)
to cause or mark the beginning of something new; to signal the start of an event, era, or situation
mark or cause the beginning of something

Examples

The new policy will usher in a wave of reforms across the country.
Technological advances often usher in significant changes in society.
The CEO hopes the strategy will usher in an era of growth for the company.

Synset Examples

The fall of the Berlin Wall ushered in the post-Cold War period

Related Terms

  • Synonym: initiate - to cause something to begin
  • Antonym: to conclude - to bring to an end
