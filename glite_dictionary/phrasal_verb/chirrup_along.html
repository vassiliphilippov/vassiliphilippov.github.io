to chirrup along

1. VERB

/ ˈtʃɪrəp əˈlɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪrəp əˈlɒŋ / (BrE)
to sing or make cheerful, repetitive sounds in harmony with music or another sound source, often in a light or playful manner
sing or make sounds in harmony with something

Examples

Every morning, the children chirrup along with their favorite television theme tunes.
As the band played, the audience began to chirrup along, filling the hall with light-hearted sounds.
She enjoyed chirruping along to the birdsong during her walks in the park.

Synset Examples

He loves to chirrup along with the radio.
