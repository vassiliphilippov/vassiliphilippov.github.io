to do ill

1. VERB

/ du ɪl / (AmE), / duː ɪl / (BrE)
to cause harm or commit wrongful acts
to do harm or wrong

Examples

Spreading malicious gossip is one way people can do ill to others.
He would rather do ill than admit his own mistakes.
You should strive to do ill to none and good to all.

Synset Examples

You shouldn't do ill by spreading false rumors.
His actions will do ill to his reputation.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to do good - to do what is morally right or beneficial
  • Synonym: harm - physical or moral injury or damage
