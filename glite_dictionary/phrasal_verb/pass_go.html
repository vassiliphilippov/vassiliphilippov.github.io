to pass go

1. VERB

/ pæs ɡoʊ / (AmE), / pɑːs ɡəʊ / (BrE)
to begin a journey, process, or activity, often implying progress to the next stage without delay
begin a journey or process

Examples

You can't collect the reward until you pass go and complete the first step.
He decided not to pass go and went straight to the manager's office to complain.
Before you pass go, make sure you understand all the requirements for the application.

Synset Examples

He was so late he might as well not pass go.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get started - to begin an activity or process

Etymology

From the board game Monopoly, where players collect money after passing the 'Go' square.
