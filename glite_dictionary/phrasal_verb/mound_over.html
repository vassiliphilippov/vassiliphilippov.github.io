to mound over

1. VERB

/ maʊnd ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / maʊnd ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to cover or bury (something) with a mound or heap of earth or material
cover with a mound

Examples

After the seeds are planted, they are carefully mounded over to protect them from birds.
The archaeologists mounded over the remains to preserve the site.
Earth was mounded over the roots to shield them from frost.

Synset Examples

The huts can be mounded over to form shelters

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cover - to put something over or on top of
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01610775-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ maʊnd ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / maʊnd ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to form or build a mound covering or over something
form a mound covering something

Examples

The gardener mounded over the roots to protect them from the cold.
After the dog buried its bone, it mounded over the spot with dirt.
Heavy rain caused soil to mound over the pathway, making it difficult to walk.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cover - to place something over or upon, as for protection or concealment
