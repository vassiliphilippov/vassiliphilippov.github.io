to move along

1. VERB

/ muːv əˈlɔŋ / (AmE), / muːv əˈlɒŋ / (BrE)
to go away from a place or to continue moving forward, especially when told to do so by someone in authority; to proceed without delaying
to proceed or leave, especially when told to

Examples

The police officer told the crowd to move along and not block the entrance.
After a brief pause to take photos, we decided to move along the trail.
If you have no further questions, let's move along to the next topic.

Synset Examples

Please move along; there's nothing to see here.
The crowd was asked to move along quietly.
He told her to move along if she wasn’t interested.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go away - to leave a place
  • Antonym: to linger - to stay somewhere longer than necessary
