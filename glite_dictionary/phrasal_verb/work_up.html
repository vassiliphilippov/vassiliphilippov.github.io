to work up

1. VERB

/ wɝk ʌp / (AmE), / wɜːk ʌp / (BrE)
to develop or produce something, such as an idea, plan, or design, with effort
develop or produce something by effort

Examples

She managed to work up a new marketing strategy in just a few days.
The artists will work up your sketches into a finished logo.
After several meetings, they finally worked up a proposal for the project.

Synset Examples

His colleagues worked out his interesting idea
We worked up an ad for our client

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to develop - to grow or cause to grow and become more mature, advanced, or elaborate
  • Synonym: to conceive - to form or devise in the mind (an idea or plan)

2. VERB

/ wɜrk ʌp / (AmE), / wɜːk ʌp / (BrE)
to gradually develop or increase something such as confidence, courage, or enthusiasm.
develop or increase (emotion/quality)

Examples

He’s trying to work up enough enthusiasm for the new project.
It took time to work up confidence before applying for the job.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
