to break out of one's comfort zone

1. VERB

/ breɪk ˈaʊt əv wʌnz ˈkʌmfɚt zoʊn / (AmE), / breɪk ˈaʊt ɒv wʌnz ˈkʌmfət zəʊn / (BrE)
to deliberately do things that are unfamiliar or challenging in order to grow or improve oneself, rather than staying with familiar or easy experiences
do something new or challenging

Examples

Taking a solo trip helped her break out of her comfort zone and discover new strengths.
He decided to break out of his comfort zone by joining a public speaking club.
Starting a new job forced me to break out of my comfort zone and learn quickly.

Synset Examples

He managed to break out of his comfort zone.
She had to bust out of her comfort zone to succeed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: step out of one's comfort zone - to move beyond what is familiar or easy
