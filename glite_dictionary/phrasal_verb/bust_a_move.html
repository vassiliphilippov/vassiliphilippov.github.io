to bust a move

1. VERB

/ bʌst ə muːv / (AmE), / bʌst ə muːv / (BrE)
to dance energetically or start dancing, especially in a lively or impressive way
to dance energetically
informal american

Examples

Everyone cheered when he decided to bust a move at the wedding reception.
As soon as the music came on, she was ready to bust a move.
If you really want to impress them, you have to bust a move on the dance floor.

Synset Examples

He always knows how to bust a move at parties.
It's time to bust a move and hit the dance floor!
She was ready to bust a move as the music started.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dance - to move rhythmically to music

Etymology

American English slang, popularized in the late 1980s by hip-hop culture and the 1989 song 'Bust a Move' by Young MC.
