to pull up short

1. VERB

/ pʊl ʌp ʃɔrt / (AmE), / pʊl ʌp ʃɔːt / (BrE)
to stop suddenly and abruptly, often before intended or expected
stop suddenly and abruptly

Examples

The driver pulled up short when he saw the child run into the street.
She pulled up short at the sound of the alarm, not knowing what had happened.
When the fog grew thicker, the train pulled up short just outside the station.

Synset Examples

The police car pulled up short and then turned around fast

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stop short - to stop suddenly or unexpectedly
