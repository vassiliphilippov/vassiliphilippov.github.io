to mug up

1. VERB

/ mʌɡ ʌp / (AmE), / mʌɡ ʌp / (BrE)
to study something intensively, especially in preparation for an examination
study intensively before a test or exam
informal british

Examples

I had to mug up on world history the night before the exam.
She mugged up all the key formulas just in time for her math test.
Before the interview, he spent hours mugging up on current affairs.

Synset Examples

I had to bone up on my Latin verbs before the final exam

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cram - study intensively over a short period
  • Synonym: to bone up - study intensively, especially in a short time

Etymology

Originated in British slang, possibly from 'mug' meaning 'face' or 'person', evolving to mean 'cram'.
