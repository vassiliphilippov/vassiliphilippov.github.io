to look back

1. VERB

/ lʊk bæk / (AmE), / lʊk bæk / (BrE)
to turn one's head or eyes to see what is behind oneself
turn to see behind oneself

Examples

As I left the building, I didn't look back even once.
She heard a noise behind her and quickly looked back.
He was told not to look back while climbing the stairs.

Synset Examples

don't look back while you walk

Related Terms

  • Synonym: glance back - to quickly look behind oneself
  • Antonym: to look forward - to turn one's gaze ahead or think about the future
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02136564-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ lʊk bæk / (AmE), / lʊk bæk / (BrE)
to think about or remember the past, especially a particular period or set of events
to remember or reflect on the past

Examples

When I look back on my years in college, I remember many wonderful experiences.
She often looked back at her childhood with a sense of nostalgia.
It's important not to look back with regret, but to learn from your mistakes.

Synset Examples

she reviewed her achievements with pride

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reflect - to think deeply or carefully about past events
  • Synonym: to reminisce - to talk or think about past experiences with pleasure
  • Antonym: to forget - not to remember something from the past
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00698180-v
Synset POS: v
