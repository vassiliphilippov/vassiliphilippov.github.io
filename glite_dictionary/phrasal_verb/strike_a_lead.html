to strike a lead

1. VERB

/ straɪk ə lid / (AmE), / straɪk ə liːd / (BrE)
to discover a helpful clue or promising opportunity, especially during an investigation or search for information
find a clue or promising opportunity

Examples

After days of research, the detective finally managed to strike a lead in the complex case.
The archaeologist was thrilled to strike a lead that could reveal the city's lost history.
Journalists often work tirelessly to strike a lead before their competitors do.

Synset Examples

He was excited to strike a lead for his story.
She managed to strike a lead on the missing person case.
The journalist hopes to strike a lead in the investigation.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: find a clue - to discover a piece of helpful information in an investigation
  • Antonym: to hit a snag - to encounter a problem or obstacle (opposite sense)

Etymology

Draws on the original sense of 'lead' as a clue and the mining idiom 'strike a lode' (to discover a vein of valuable ore).
