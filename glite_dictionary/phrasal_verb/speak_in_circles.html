to speak in circles

1. VERB

/ spiːk ɪn ˈsɝːkəlz / (AmE), / spiːk ɪn ˈsɜːkəlz / (BrE)
to talk in a way that avoids making a clear or direct point, often by repeating or being vague
talk without making a clear point

Examples

Whenever asked about his plans, he would speak in circles, never giving a straight answer.
Stop speaking in circles and just say what's on your mind.
During the meeting, she spoke in circles, leaving everyone confused about her true intentions.

Synset Examples

Stop speaking in circles and get to the point.
She tends to speak in circles during interviews.
He was speaking in circles, avoiding the question.
Stop talking in circles and get to the point.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to beat around the bush - to avoid talking about what is important
  • Antonym: to get to the point - to state directly what one means
