to suck out

1. VERB

/ sʌk aʊt / (AmE), / sʌk aʊt / (BrE)
to remove something from a place by creating suction
remove by suction

Examples

The dentist had to suck out the excess saliva during the procedure.
He tried to suck out the poison from the insect bite before help arrived.
A vacuum pump was used to suck out all the air from the container.

Synset Examples

aspirate the wound

Related Terms

  • Synonym: extract - to remove or take out, especially by effort or force
  • Antonym: to inject - to force (a liquid, especially a drug or vaccine) into someone or something
