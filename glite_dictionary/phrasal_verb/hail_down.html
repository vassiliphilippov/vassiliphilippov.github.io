to hail down

1. VERB

/ heɪl daʊn / (AmE), / heɪl daʊn / (BrE)
to signal or call for a vehicle, especially a taxi or bus, to stop so that you can get in
signal a vehicle to stop

Examples

It took her several minutes to hail down a taxi in the rain.
You can hail down a bus on this route if there's a safe spot to stop.
Tourists often struggle to hail down a cab during rush hour.

Synset Examples

He waved his arms to hail down the bus.
They managed to hail down a ride after waiting long.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to flag down - to signal a driver to stop by waving

Etymology

From 'hail' meaning 'call to attract attention' + 'down' indicating the direction or act of stopping.
