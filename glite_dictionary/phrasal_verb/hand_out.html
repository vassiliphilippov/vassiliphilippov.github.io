to hand out

1. VERB

/ hænd aʊt / (AmE), / hænd aʊt / (BrE)
to give something to a number of people in a group; to distribute.
distribute to several people

Examples

The volunteers handed out blankets to the people affected by the storm.
Could you hand out these forms to everyone in the meeting?
Every Halloween, she loves to hand out candy to the neighborhood children.

Synset Examples

The teacher handed out the exams

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to distribute - to give or deliver (something) to people; synonym of 'hand out'
  • Antonym: collect - to bring things or people together, especially from different places; antonym of 'hand out' in context of giving out items
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02206426-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ hænd aʊt / (AmE), / hænd aʊt / (BrE)
to solicit or ask for something, especially in expectation of receiving a gift, favor, or monetary help (often used as 'have one's hand out')
to ask or solicit for something

Examples

At city hall, he always has his hand out for funding opportunities.
The organization had its hand out to sponsors for support.
She's not afraid to put her hand out when she needs help.

Synset Examples

He always has his hand out for donations at charity events.
She handed out taffy at the fair.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to beg - to ask someone earnestly or humbly for something
  • Synonym: request - to politely or formally ask for something
Source: Idiom List
WordNet ID: n/a
Synset POS: None
