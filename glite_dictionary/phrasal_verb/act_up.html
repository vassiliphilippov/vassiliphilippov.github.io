to act up

1. VERB

/ ækt ʌp / (AmE), / ækt ʌp / (BrE)
(of a part of the body or a medical condition) to cause pain or become a problem again after a period of improvement.
(of a body part/condition) cause pain again
informal

Examples

My knees start to act up whenever the weather changes.
Her allergy tends to act up in the springtime.
If your back acts up again, you should see a doctor.

Synset Examples

My arthritis is acting up again

2. VERB

/ ækt ʌp / (AmE), / ækt ʌp / (BrE)
to behave badly or in a silly or troublesome way; to misbehave
behave badly or mischievously

Examples

The children began to act up as soon as the teacher left the room.
If your dog starts to act up, put it back on its leash.
His old knee injury tends to act up in cold weather.

Synset Examples

The children acted up when they were not bored

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to misbehave - to behave badly or improperly
  • Antonym: to behave - to act in a proper or correct way

3. VERB

/ ækt ʌp / (AmE), / ækt ʌp / (BrE)
to behave badly or disruptively, or to fail to function properly
behave badly; malfunction
informal

Examples

The children began to act up as soon as their parents left the room.
My printer tends to act up whenever I need it most.
If your knee starts to act up again, you should see a doctor.

Synset Examples

The kids started to act up as the show went on.
My computer tends to act up during updates.
He always acts up when he's tired.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to misbehave - to behave badly
  • Synonym: malfunction - to fail to function properly
