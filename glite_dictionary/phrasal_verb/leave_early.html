to leave early

1. VERB

/ liv ˈɜrli / (AmE), / liːv ˈɜːli / (BrE)
to depart from a place before the expected or usual time
depart before expected time

Examples

I have to leave early today to make it to my appointment on time.
She often leaves early to avoid the evening rush hour.
If you leave early, please let your manager know in advance.

Synset Examples

Let's leave early, 'cause traffic will be bad.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to arrive late - to get to a place after the expected or planned time
