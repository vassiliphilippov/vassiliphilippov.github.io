to key out

1. VERB

/ ki aʊt / (AmE), / kiː aʊt / (BrE)
to identify a plant, animal, or specimen by using a key, especially a dichotomous key, in biology or botany
identify using a biological key

Examples

The students were able to key out the plant species using the field guide.
She carefully keyed out the mushroom to ensure it was safe to eat.
We spent the afternoon keying out insects under the supervision of our biology professor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to identify - to recognize or be able to name someone or something
