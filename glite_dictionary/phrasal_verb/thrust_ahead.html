to thrust ahead

1. VERB

/ θrʌst əˈhɛd / (AmE), / θrʌst əˈhɛd / (BrE)
to move forward forcefully, often by pushing others out of the way
push forward forcefully

Examples

She managed to thrust ahead in the crowded hallway to get to the door first.
Determined to win, he thrust ahead of his competitors during the final lap.
Despite the resistance, the protesters thrust ahead toward the front lines.

Synset Examples

she barged into the meeting room

Related Terms

  • Synonym: push ahead - to continue moving forward, often with effort or determination
  • Synonym: to barge in - to enter a place or join a situation suddenly and rudely
