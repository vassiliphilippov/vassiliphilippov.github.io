to shield from

1. VERB

/ ʃild frəm / (AmE), / ʃiːld frəm / (BrE)
to protect someone or something from harm or danger
protect from harm or danger

Examples

He wore a heavy coat to shield from the freezing wind.
Parents often try to shield from their children the harsh realities of life.
She held her bag above her head to shield from the sudden downpour.

Synset Examples

He wore a cloak to shield himself from the rain.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: protect from - to keep someone or something safe from harm or danger
  • Antonym: expose to - to make someone or something unprotected from harm or danger
