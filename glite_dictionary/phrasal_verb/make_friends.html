to make friends

1. VERB

/ meɪk frɛndz / (AmE), / meɪk frɛndz / (BrE)
to form new friendships or establish a social relationship with someone
form new friendships

Examples

It's sometimes difficult to make friends in a new city.
Children tend to make friends quickly on the playground.
She struggled to make friends at her new school because she was very shy.

Synset Examples

Make friends as you go through life.
His vindictive protectiveness made it hard for her to make friends.
Her reticence made it hard for her to make friends.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to befriend - to act as a friend to someone
  • Antonym: make enemies - to become the enemy of someone
