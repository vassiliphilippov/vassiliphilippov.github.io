to hand down

1. VERB

/ hænd daʊn / (AmE), / hænd daʊn / (BrE)
to give or leave something to someone who is younger or in a later generation, especially as part of an inheritance or tradition
give by inheritance or succession

Examples

This antique vase was handed down from my great-grandmother to my mother and now to me.
Folk stories are often handed down through generations by word of mouth.
He plans to hand down his knowledge of the craft to his apprentices.

Synset Examples

This ring was handed down through many generations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to inherit - receive (money, property, or a title) as an heir at the death of the previous holder
  • Synonym: to pass on - to give something to someone, especially after your death

2. VERB

/ hænd daʊn / (AmE), / hænd daʊn / (BrE)
to give or pass something, especially knowledge, traditions, or possessions, to a younger person or the next generation
pass to a younger person or next generation

Examples

Stories like these are often handed down through generations in a family.
The company’s secret recipe has been handed down from parent to child for decades.
He hopes to hand down his values to his children.

Synset Examples

They decided to hand down their knowledge to the interns.
She will hand down her grandmother's ring to her daughter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pass on - to give something to someone who will come after
