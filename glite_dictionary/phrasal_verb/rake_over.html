to rake over

1. VERB

/ reɪk ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / reɪk ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to examine, discuss, or think about something thoroughly, especially something unpleasant or complicated
examine or discuss thoroughly

Examples

Before we make a decision, let's rake over all the available evidence one more time.
She didn't want to rake over old arguments from years ago.
The committee spent hours raking over the financial reports.

Synset Examples

Let's rake over the project details before the meeting.
We need to rake over our options carefully.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to go over - to examine or review something carefully
  • Antonym: to leave alone - to refrain from discussing or disturbing
