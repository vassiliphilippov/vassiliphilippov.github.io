to rule out

1. VERB

/ ruːl aʊt / (AmE), / ruːl aʊt / (BrE)
to prevent the possibility of something; to decide that something is not possible or is not a suitable option
eliminate as a possibility

Examples

We cannot rule out the possibility of rain tomorrow.
Her alibi helped rule out her involvement in the crime.
Technical issues have been ruled out as the cause of the problem.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exclude - to deliberately not include something
  • Antonym: to consider - to regard or treat as possible or likely
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02635471-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ruːl aʊt / (AmE), / ruːl aʊt / (BrE)
to decide that something is not possible or not suitable, often by following rules or evidence
exclude as a possibility

Examples

The doctor was able to rule out meningitis after reviewing the test results.
We can't rule out the possibility of rain during the event.
The evidence presented will help the court rule out any suspicion of fraud.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to exclude - to deliberately leave something out or not include it
  • Antonym: to allow - to permit or accept something as possible
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01150028-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ruːl aʊt / (AmE), / ruːl aʊt / (BrE)
To dismiss something as a possibility or eliminate it from consideration.
dismiss as a possibility

Examples

The doctor was able to rule out any serious illness after reviewing the test results.
After several interviews, we decided to rule out Tom for the position.
The detective ruled out theft as a motive in this case.

Synset Examples

John was ruled out as a possible suspect because he had a strong alibi
This possibility can be eliminated from our consideration

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to eliminate - to remove or get rid of something
  • Antonym: to consider - to think about or take into account
