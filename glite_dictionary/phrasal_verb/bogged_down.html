to bogged down

1. VERB

/ bɑɡd daʊn / (AmE), / bɒɡd daʊn / (BrE)
to become stuck or unable to progress because of being hindered by difficulties, details, or obstacles
hindered or stuck, unable to progress

Examples

We got bogged down in paperwork and missed the deadline.
The meeting bogged down when the discussion turned to minor issues.
She felt bogged down by her responsibilities at work.

Synset Examples

We got bogged down in details during the discussion.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: stuck - unable to move or progress
  • Antonym: freed up - no longer hindered or encumbered, able to progress
