to belt down

1. VERB

/ bɛlt daʊn / (AmE), / bɛlt daʊn / (BrE)
to drink something quickly or in one go, usually an alcoholic beverage
drink quickly or entirely
informal

Examples

He managed to belt down his beer before the train left the station.
She belted down a glass of water after her run in the sun.
They were known to belt down shots at every celebration.

Synset Examples

He downed three martinis before dinner
She killed a bottle of brandy that night
They popped a few beers after work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: down - to drink something quickly
  • Antonym: sip - to drink something slowly in small amounts
