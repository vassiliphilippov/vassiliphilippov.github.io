to stand trial

1. VERB

/ stænd ˈtraɪəl / (AmE), / stænd ˈtraɪəl / (BrE)
to appear in a court of law to be judged for an alleged crime or offense
be judged in court for a crime or offense

Examples

After months of investigation, the officials finally had to stand trial for corruption charges.
She was anxious and nervous as she prepared to stand trial.
Several witnesses were called when the businessman stood trial on fraud allegations.

Synset Examples

They are set to stand trial for their actions.
He will stand trial next week for the charges.
The accused will stand trial in front of a jury.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: be tried - to undergo a trial in court
