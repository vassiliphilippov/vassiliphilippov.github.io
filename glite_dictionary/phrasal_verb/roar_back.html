to roar back

1. VERB

/ rɔr bæk / (AmE), / rɔː bæk / (BrE)
to reply or react aggressively, loudly, or defiantly, especially in speech
respond angrily or aggressively

Examples

Despite the harsh comments, he chose to roar back rather than stay silent.
When criticized during the meeting, she didn't hesitate to roar back at her colleagues.
If you insult him, prepare for him to roar back with a fierce reply.

Synset Examples

When she insulted him, he decided to roar back.
He couldn't help but roar back at the criticism.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: retort - to reply sharply or angrily
  • Synonym: to snap back - to reply quickly and sharply
