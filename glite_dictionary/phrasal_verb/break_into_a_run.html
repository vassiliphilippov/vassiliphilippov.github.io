to break into a run

1. VERB

/ breɪk ˈɪntu ə rʌn / (AmE), / breɪk ˈɪntuː ə rʌn / (BrE)
to suddenly begin running, typically after walking or standing still
suddenly start running

Examples

When the thunder rumbled, the children broke into a run toward their house.
Realizing she was late for her meeting, Susan broke into a run as soon as she left the elevator.
As soon as the park gates opened, the crowd broke into a run to claim the best picnic spots.

Synset Examples

Seeing the bus, she had to break into a run.
When it started to rain, they broke into a run.
He saw the dog chasing him and broke into a run.
