to frighten off

1. VERB

/ ˈfraɪtn̩ ɑf / (AmE), / ˈfraɪtn̩ ɒf / (BrE)
to make someone or something go away or not do something by making them afraid or worried
scare away or discourage

Examples

The loud noises were enough to frighten off the intruders.
Rising costs may frighten off potential investors.
The presence of the guard dog can frighten off burglars.

Synset Examples

dashed by the refusal

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to scare off - to make someone or something go away by frightening them
  • Synonym: to deter - to discourage someone from doing something by instilling doubt or fear
