to rake together

1. VERB

/ reɪk təˈɡɛðər / (AmE), / reɪk təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
to gather or assemble resources, money, people, or things, often from various sources and sometimes with difficulty or hurriedly
gather or assemble things or people hastily

Examples

After a lot of effort, they managed to rake together enough money to pay the rent.
We had to rake together a team at the last minute for the competition.
She tried to rake together all the necessary documents before the deadline.

Synset Examples

We need to rake together a team for the event.
They decided to rake together their resources for the project.
Let's rake together some funds for charity.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gather - to collect things or people together
  • Synonym: to scrape together - to collect or amass something with difficulty

Etymology

From the verb 'rake' (to gather or collect), combined with 'together' to indicate assembling from different places.
