to play a part

1. VERB

/ pleɪ ə pɑrt / (AmE), / pleɪ ə pɑːt / (BrE)
to take on a role in a performance or to contribute to the achievement of something
to act in a role or contribute to an outcome

Examples

Each team member must play a part in reaching our goals.
She was thrilled to play a part in the new film.
Doctors and nurses play a part in improving healthcare outcomes every day.

Synset Examples

She will play a part in the school play.
Everyone must play a part in keeping the environment clean.
He played a part in organizing the charity event.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to participate - to take part or become involved in an activity
