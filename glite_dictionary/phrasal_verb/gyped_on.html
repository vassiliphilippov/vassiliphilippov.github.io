to gyped on

1. VERB

/ ˈdʒaɪpt ɑn / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɪpt ɒn / (BrE)
to be cheated or swindled out of something, especially by being charged too much or receiving less than expected
be cheated or swindled
informal offensive

Examples

I felt gyped on the concert tickets when I saw how bad our seats were.
Many customers complained they had been gyped on the online sale.
He was worried the repair shop gyped on the bill by adding extra charges.

Synset Examples

She thought she was gyped on the used car deal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cheated - to act dishonestly or unfairly to gain an advantage
  • Synonym: ripped off - to be cheated, especially by being overcharged

Etymology

Variant of 'gyp', possibly from 'Gypsy', considered offensive due to negative ethnic stereotyping.
