to quarantine oneself

1. VERB

/ ˈkwɔːrənˌtin wʌnˈsɛlf / (AmE), / ˈkwɒrənˌtiːn wʌnˈsɛlf / (BrE)
to isolate oneself from others to prevent the spread of disease, especially after possible exposure to an infection
isolate oneself due to possible infection

Examples

After traveling abroad, she decided to quarantine oneself for two weeks to protect her family.
Doctors recommend that anyone exposed to the virus should quarantine oneself immediately.
During the outbreak, many people had to quarantine oneself in their homes.

Synset Examples

He had to quarantine himself after returning from abroad.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: self-isolate - to separate oneself from others to avoid transmitting disease

Etymology

From Italian 'quaranta' (forty), relating to ships isolated for forty days to prevent plague; combined with 'oneself'.
