to burst in on

1. VERB

/ bɝst ɪn ɑn / (AmE), / bɜːst ɪn ɒn / (BrE)
to enter a place or situation suddenly and unexpectedly, especially so as to interrupt others
enter suddenly and interrupt

Examples

The manager burst in on the meeting without knocking.
She didn't want anyone to burst in on her while she was rehearsing.
He burst in on their private conversation, startling them both.

Synset Examples

He burst upon our conversation

Related Terms

  • Synonym: interrupt - to stop the continuous progress of an activity or conversation
