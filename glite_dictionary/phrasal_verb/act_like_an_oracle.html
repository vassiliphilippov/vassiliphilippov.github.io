to act like an oracle

1. VERB

/ ækt laɪk ən ˈɔrəkəl / (AmE), / ækt laɪk ən ˈɒrəkəl / (BrE)
to predict future events or outcomes with great accuracy, as if possessing special insight or wisdom
predict with great accuracy

Examples

Everyone was amazed when she acted like an oracle and described the results before they happened.
He tends to act like an oracle during meetings, making remarkably accurate predictions.
Don’t expect me to act like an oracle—I can’t foresee every possible outcome.

Synset Examples

He acted like an oracle, predicting every outcome.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to prophesy - to predict or foretell future events
  • Degree Lower: guess - to form an opinion without sufficient evidence

Etymology

From the English word 'oracle', meaning a prophetic figure, with the phrase using 'act like' to signify imitation of such a role.
