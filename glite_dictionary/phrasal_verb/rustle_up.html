to rustle up

1. VERB

/ ˈrʌsəl ʌp / (AmE), / ˈrʌsəl ʌp / (BrE)
to prepare or find something with little planning or effort, often quickly or at short notice
prepare or gather quickly, often with little notice
informal

Examples

Let me rustle up some coffee for our guests while you take a seat.
She can rustle up a meal from almost nothing in the fridge.
If you give me five minutes, I'll rustle up some extra chairs for everyone.

Synset Examples

He managed to rustle up a few friends for the party.
I'll rustle up dinner while you set the table.
Can you rustle up some snacks for the guests?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to whip up - to prepare or produce something quickly
