to fly open

1. VERB

/ flaɪ ˈoʊpən / (AmE), / flaɪ ˈəʊpən / (BrE)
to open suddenly and quickly, often with force or as a result of pressure
open suddenly and forcefully

Examples

The window suddenly flew open when the wind picked up.
His locker door flew open as he tried to force it shut.
The old book's cover flew open the moment she picked it up.

Synset Examples

the doors flew open in the strong gust

Related Terms

  • Synonym: burst open - to open suddenly and violently
  • Antonym: to slam shut - to close suddenly and forcefully (opposite action)
