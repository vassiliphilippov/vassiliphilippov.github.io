to pour forth

1. VERB

/ pɔr fɔrθ / (AmE), / pɔː fɔːθ / (BrE)
(of liquid or something abstract) to flow or issue out in drops or small amounts, or as if in such a manner.
to flow or issue out in drops or small amounts

Examples

Tears began to pour forth as she listened to the sad news.
Blood would pour forth from the wound if not treated quickly.
At the end of the speech, applause poured forth from the crowd.

Synset Examples

shed tears
spill blood
God shed His grace on Thee

Related Terms

  • Synonym: shed - to allow to flow or fall, as with tears or blood
  • Synonym: spill - to allow liquid to flow or fall accidentally or freely
