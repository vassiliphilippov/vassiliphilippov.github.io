to come together

1. VERB

/ kʌm təˈɡɛðər / (AmE), / kʌm təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
(of people or things) to move toward each other and meet, especially in an embrace or to enclose something.
move toward and meet, often in an embrace

Examples

When the group saw each other again, they came together in a warm hug.
The petals come together to protect the flower's center at night.
Her hands came together around the fragile bird, cradling it gently.

Synset Examples

Her arms closed around her long lost relative

Related Terms

  • Synonym: embrace - to hold someone closely in one's arms

2. VERB

/ kʌm təˈɡɛðɚ / (AmE), / kʌm təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
(of people or groups) to unite or collaborate for a common purpose or cause.
unite or collaborate for a common purpose

Examples

After the earthquake, neighbors came together to help each other recover.
Activists come together every year to raise awareness for the cause.
There was a sense of hope as the divided groups finally came together to discuss peace.

Synset Examples

After the hurricane passed, the community came together to rebuild.
The povo came together to support local businesses.
The community came together to rebuild the playground.
The community came together to beat as one against the common threat.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to unite - to come or bring together for a common purpose
  • Synonym: to collaborate - to work jointly with others
  • Antonym: to fall apart - to break up or disintegrate
