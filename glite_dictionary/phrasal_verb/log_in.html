to log in

1. VERB

/ lɑɡ ˈɪn / (AmE), / lɒɡ ˈɪn / (BrE)
to enter a computer system or website by providing a username and password or other required credentials
access a computer system or website

Examples

Make sure to log in before you try to access your email.
He forgot his password and couldn't log in to the company server.
After logging in, she checked her messages and calendar.

Synset Examples

Have you logged in lately?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to log off - to exit a computer system or website
  • Synonym: to sign in - to access a computer system or service by entering credentials
  • Antonym: to log out - to disconnect from a computer system or website

Etymology

From 'log' (record or enter) + 'in'; usage developed with computer technology in the late 20th century.

2. VERB

/ lɑɡ ɪn / (AmE), / lɒɡ ɪn / (BrE)
to enter a computer system or online account by typing your username and password
enter a computer system or online account

Examples

You need to log in before you can access your email account.
After forgetting her password, Maria couldn't log in to the company website.
He always makes sure to log in early for his online classes.

Synset Examples

Have you logged in lately?

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to log out - to exit a computer system or account
  • Synonym: to sign in - to enter a computer system or account, synonym of 'log in'
  • Antonym: sign out - to exit a computer system or account, synonym of 'log out'

Etymology

From "log" (to make an entry in a logbook) + "in" (as in entering a system); usage rose with computer technology.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02253955-v
Synset POS: v
