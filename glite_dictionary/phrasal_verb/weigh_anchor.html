to weigh anchor

1. VERB

/ weɪ ˈæŋkɚ / (AmE), / weɪ ˈaŋkə / (BrE)
to raise the anchor of a ship in preparation for departure
raise a ship's anchor

Examples

The crew prepared to weigh anchor as the sun began to rise.
We will weigh anchor at dawn and set out for the open sea.
The captain gave the order to weigh anchor immediately.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: anchor - a heavy object used to moor a vessel

Etymology

Nautical phrase: 'weigh' from Old English 'wegean' (to lift), meaning 'raise' in this context.
