to notch up

1. VERB

/ nɑtʃ ʌp / (AmE), / nɒtʃ ʌp / (BrE)
to achieve, win, or record something, typically an amount, score, or success
achieve or record a success or amount

Examples

The team hopes to notch up another victory in the championship this year.
She notched up several awards for her excellent research.
The company has notched up record sales for the third month in a row.

Synset Examples

They managed to notch up a significant victory.
We can notch up another success before the deadline.
He hopes to notch up more sales this quarter.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to achieve - to successfully bring about or reach a desired objective
  • Synonym: to attain - to succeed in achieving something difficult
  • Antonym: to lose - to fail to win or achieve something

Etymology

From the noun 'notch' (a V-shaped cut or mark), figuratively alluding to marking achievements by cutting notches.
