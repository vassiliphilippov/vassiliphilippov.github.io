to mouth off

1. VERB

/ maʊθ ɔf / (AmE), / maʊθ ɒf / (BrE)
to speak in a rude, loud, or disrespectful way, especially to someone in authority
speak rudely or disrespectfully
informal

Examples

Don't mouth off to your teacher if you want to avoid detention.
He got into trouble for mouthing off during the meeting.
She tends to mouth off when she's frustrated with the rules.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: backtalk - impudent or disrespectful reply; talking back
  • Synonym: speak out of turn - to speak when one is not supposed to, often rudely
