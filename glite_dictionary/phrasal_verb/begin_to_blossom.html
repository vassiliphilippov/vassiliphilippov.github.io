to begin to blossom

1. VERB

/ bɪˈɡɪn tə ˈblɑsəm / (AmE), / bɪˈɡɪn tə ˈblɒsəm / (BrE)
to start to develop or flourish, especially in terms of ability, character, or success
start to develop or flourish

Examples

After a few months in her new job, her confidence began to blossom.
The business began to blossom once they launched their online store.
As the weather warmed, the city's cultural life began to blossom again.

Synset Examples

Her talent began to blossom during high school.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: blossom - to develop and become more successful or confident
  • Antonym: fade - to lose strength or flourish less
