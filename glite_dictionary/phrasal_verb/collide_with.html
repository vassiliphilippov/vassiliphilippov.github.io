to collide with

1. VERB

/ kəˈlaɪd wɪð / (AmE), / kəˈlaɪd wɪð / (BrE)
to hit something or someone with force while moving, resulting in a sudden impact
hit with force while moving

Examples

Two trains collided with each other on the bridge last night.
The cyclist lost control and collided with a parked car.
If you don't pay attention on the road, you might collide with another vehicle.

Synset Examples

The car hit a tree
He struck the table with his elbow

Related Terms

  • Synonym: crash into - to strike something violently and noisily while moving
  • Antonym: to avoid - to keep away from coming into contact with something

Etymology

From Latin 'collidere', from 'com-' (together) + 'laedere' (to strike, hurt).
