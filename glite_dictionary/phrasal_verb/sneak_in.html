to sneak in

1. VERB

/ snik ɪn / (AmE), / sniːk ɪn / (BrE)
to enter a place secretly or without being noticed, especially to avoid being caught or stopped
enter somewhere secretly

Examples

He managed to sneak in through the back door without anyone noticing.
The reporters tried to sneak in to get a glimpse of the celebrity.
Sometimes, students sneak in snacks despite the school's rules.

Synset Examples

He sneaked in under cover of darkness
In this essay, the author's personal feelings creep in

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to slip in - to enter quickly and quietly
  • Antonym: to sneak out - to leave a place secretly
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01231430-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ sniːk ɪn / (AmE), / sniːk ɪn / (BrE)
to insert or include something casually or without drawing attention
insert or include unobtrusively

Examples

She managed to sneak in one last comment before the meeting ended.
The editor tried to sneak in an advertisement between the articles.
He likes to sneak in jokes during his lectures to keep the mood light.

Synset Examples

She slipped in a reference to her own work

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to slip in - to insert or add something furtively or unobtrusively
  • Degree Lower: mention - to refer to something briefly or casually
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01027611-v
Synset POS: v
