to home in

1. VERB

/ hoʊm ɪn / (AmE), / həʊm ɪn / (BrE)
to move or be guided accurately toward a target, especially with the help of automatic guidance or navigational aids
move accurately toward a target

Examples

The missile was able to home in on its intended target with remarkable accuracy.
Her research began to home in on the key issues affecting climate change.
As they approached the beacon, the navigation system started to home in on the signal.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to zero in - focus closely on or aim at a target

Etymology

From 'home' (to move or return to home) plus the preposition 'in', originally used in contexts of navigational targeting.
