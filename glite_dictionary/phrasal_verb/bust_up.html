to bust up

1. VERB

/ bʌst ʌp / (AmE), / bʌst ʌp / (BrE)
to smash or break something forcefully or into pieces, often suddenly or violently
smash or break forcefully
informal

Examples

The kids got angry and decided to bust up their old toys.
He accidentally busted up the vase while cleaning the shelf.
Someone tried to bust up the locked door during the commotion.

Synset Examples

The kid busted up the car

Related Terms

  • Synonym: break - separate into pieces as a result of a blow, shock, or strain
  • Synonym: smash - shatter or violently break something into pieces
