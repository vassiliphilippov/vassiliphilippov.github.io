to pipe in

1. VERB

/ paɪp ɪn / (AmE), / paɪp ɪn / (BrE)
to transport (a substance such as gas, oil, or water) to a place through pipes
transport via pipes

Examples

Natural gas is piped in to the factory for heating and production.
During the renovation, clean water was piped in from a nearby source.
They plan to pipe in fresh air to improve ventilation in the building.

Synset Examples

We have to pipe in oil

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pump in - to move (a liquid or gas) into a place using a pump

2. VERB

/ paɪp ɪn / (AmE), / paɪp ɪn / (BrE)
to bring or send something, especially sound, music, or other substances, into a place through pipes or a similar conduit
to bring in through pipes or a conduit

Examples

Music was piped in during the entire dinner service.
They piped in fresh air to ventilate the underground bunker.
Water is piped in from the reservoir to supply the town.

Synset Examples

Music was piped into the offices

3. VERB

/ paɪp ɪn / (AmE), / paɪp ɪn / (BrE)
to introduce or supply (a substance such as air, water, or sound) using pipes or a similar system
supply using pipes (air, water, sound, etc.)

Examples

They decided to pipe in a stream of calming music to the waiting area.
Many buildings pipe in fresh air to improve ventilation and air quality.
The chef piped in chocolate filling to the center of each pastry.

Synset Examples

They decided to pipe in some fresh air.
