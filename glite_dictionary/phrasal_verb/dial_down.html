to dial down

1. VERB

/ daɪəl daʊn / (AmE), / daɪəl daʊn / (BrE)
to reduce the intensity, amount, or level of something; to make something less extreme or pronounced
reduce intensity or amount

Examples

The manager asked everyone to dial down the excitement in the office before the client arrived.
If you don't dial down your criticism, people will stop listening.
She had to dial down her expectations after hearing the team's feedback.

Synset Examples

We should dial down the tension before the meeting.
He decided to dial down his participation in the event.
Please dial down the volume; it's too loud.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to tone down - to make something less harsh or forceful
  • Antonym: to turn up - to increase the intensity or amount of something, especially sound or activity

Etymology

From the use of a 'dial' to adjust settings on radios or other devices, meaning to turn the dial downward to decrease something.
