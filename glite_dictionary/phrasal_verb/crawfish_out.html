to crawfish out

1. VERB

/ ˈkrɔːˌfɪʃ aʊt / (AmE), / ˈkrɔːˌfɪʃ aʊt / (BrE)
to withdraw from a commitment or retreat from an earlier position or promise
withdraw from a commitment or promise
american informal

Examples

When the negotiations got tough, the company decided to crawfish out of the deal.
You promised to help us move this weekend, so don’t crawfish out now!
After realizing the risk involved, she tried to crawfish out of her commitment.

Synset Examples

We'll have to crawfish out from meeting with him
He backed out of his earlier promise
The aggressive investment company pulled in its horns

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to back out - to withdraw from an agreement or commitment
  • Synonym: to renege - to go back on a promise, agreement, or commitment

Etymology

American English, from the behavior of a crawfish moving backward to escape danger.
