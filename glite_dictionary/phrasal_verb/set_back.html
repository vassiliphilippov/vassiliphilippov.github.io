to set back

1. VERB

/ sɛt bæk / (AmE), / sɛt bæk / (BrE)
to cause delay or hinder progress in something or someone
to delay or hinder progress

Examples

The unexpected rain set back the construction project by several weeks.
Her illness set back her studies, but she eventually caught up.
Bad weather can set back travel plans significantly.

Synset Examples

His late start set him back

Related Terms

  • Synonym: delay - make something happen later than planned
  • Antonym: advance - move forward or promote progress
