to stand shilly-shally

1. VERB

/ stænd ˈʃɪli ˌʃæli / (AmE), / stænd ˈʃɪli ˌʃæli / (BrE)
to be indecisive or hesitate; to waver between options without making a decision
to be indecisive; to hesitate
archaic

Examples

Whenever a big decision comes up, he tends to stand shilly-shally and delay making a choice.
Don't stand shilly-shally—take action before it's too late!
She stood shilly-shally at the crossroads, uncertain about which path to take.

Synset Examples

He tends to stand shilly-shally when faced with tough choices.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hesitate - pause before taking action due to uncertainty
  • Antonym: to decide - to make a choice or come to a resolution

Etymology

From the reduplicative phrase "shilly-shally," an alteration of "shall I, shall I?" (18th century).
