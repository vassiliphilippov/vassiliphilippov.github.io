to trigger off

1. VERB

/ ˈtrɪɡɚ ɔf / (AmE), / ˈtrɪɡə ɒf / (BrE)
to cause something to start or happen, especially suddenly or forcefully
cause to start; set in motion

Examples

His careless remark triggered off a heated argument among the group.
The announcement of the merger triggered off panic in the stock market.
The fire alarm was triggered off by cigarette smoke in the kitchen.

Synset Examples

trigger a reaction
actuate the circuits

Related Terms

  • Synonym: initiate - to begin or start something
  • Synonym: to provoke - to cause or stimulate a reaction or feeling
  • Antonym: halt - to stop the progress or development of something
