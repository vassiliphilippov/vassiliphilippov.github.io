to hush up

1. VERB

/ hʌʃ ʌp / (AmE), / hʌʃ ʌp / (BrE)
to try to prevent people from knowing about a mistake, crime, or something embarrassing or damaging
cover up a mistake or wrongdoing

Examples

The company tried to hush up the data breach but it was eventually revealed to the public.
She attempted to hush up the mistake to avoid embarrassing her team.
Officials were accused of hushing up the incident for political reasons.

Synset Examples

Let's not whitewash the crimes of Stalin
She tried to gloss over her mistakes

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cover up - to try to stop people knowing the truth about a mistake or crime
  • Antonym: to expose - to reveal the truth about something hidden or secret
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02152539-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ hʌʃ ʌp / (AmE), / hʌʃ ʌp / (BrE)
to make someone stop talking or become quiet
make someone stop talking

Examples

The teacher asked the students to hush up during the assembly.
She tried to hush up the children so they would not wake the baby.
He quickly hushed up the group when the meeting started.

Synset Examples

Please silence the children in the church!

Related Terms

  • Synonym: silence - cause to stop making noise or speaking
  • Synonym: quiet - to make or become quiet
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00462448-v
Synset POS: v
