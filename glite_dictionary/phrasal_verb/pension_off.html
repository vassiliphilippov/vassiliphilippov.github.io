to pension off

1. VERB

/ ˈpɛnʃən ɑf / (AmE), / ˈpɛnʃən ɒf / (BrE)
to allow or force someone to leave employment with a pension, especially because of age or ill health
make someone retire with a pension

Examples

After forty years of service, the company decided to pension off its oldest engineer.
Many organizations pension off their staff when they reach sixty-five.
He was pensioned off early due to poor health.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to retire - to stop working permanently, typically due to age

2. VERB

/ ˈpɛnʃən ˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈpɛnʃən ɒf / (BrE)
to allow or force an employee to leave their job, usually because of age or health, and give them a pension
retire with a pension, often involuntarily

Examples

The company decided to pension off several senior employees at the end of the year.
Many workers are pensioned off when they reach the official retirement age.
After thirty-five years of service, he was finally pensioned off with full benefits.

Synset Examples

The director was pensioned off when he got senile

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to retire - to stop working, typically because of age
  • Antonym: to appoint - to assign to a position or office
