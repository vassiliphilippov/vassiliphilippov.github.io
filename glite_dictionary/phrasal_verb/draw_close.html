to draw close

1. VERB

/ drɔ kloʊs / (AmE), / drɔː kləʊs / (BrE)
to move nearer to someone or something; to approach.
move towards; approach

Examples

As winter drew close, the days became shorter and colder.
The children drew close to the fire to warm themselves.
Night was drawing close as we reached the top of the hill.

Synset Examples

We were approaching our destination
They are drawing near
The enemy army came nearer and nearer

Related Terms

  • Synonym: approach - to come near or nearer to something or someone
  • Antonym: to recede - to move further away from a previous position
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02057865-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ drɔ kloʊs / (AmE), / drɔː kləʊs / (BrE)
to pull someone or something toward oneself, often in a gesture of comfort or protection
pull someone/something toward oneself

Examples

She gently drew close the frightened puppy to calm it down.
As the wind picked up, he drew close his coat around him for warmth.
He drew close his friend for a reassuring hug.

Synset Examples

He drew the crying child close

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pull in - to move something inward toward oneself
  • Antonym: to push away - to move something away from oneself
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01612857-v
Synset POS: v
