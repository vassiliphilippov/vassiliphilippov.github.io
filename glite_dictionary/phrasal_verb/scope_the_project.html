to scope the project

1. VERB

/ skoʊp ðə ˈprɑːdʒɛkt / (AmE), / skəʊp ðə ˈprɒdʒɛkt / (BrE)
to assess, evaluate, or define the extent and requirements of a project before starting it
assess or evaluate a project's parameters

Examples

We need to scope the project carefully before setting any deadlines.
Before any development begins, the team will scope the project to identify challenges.
He was asked to scope the project and present his findings to the management.

Synset Examples

He wanted to scope the project before committing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assess - to evaluate or estimate the nature or quality of something
  • Synonym: to evaluate - to judge or determine the significance, worth, or quality of something

Etymology

From the verb 'scope' meaning to assess or examine, derived from the noun 'scope' (extent or range).
