to breeze through

1. VERB

/ bɹiz θru / (AmE), / bɹiːz θruː / (BrE)
to succeed in dealing with something quickly and easily
succeed easily at something

Examples

She managed to breeze through the interview and got the job on the spot.
Most students find this assignment difficult, but Lily seemed to breeze through it.
If you study regularly, you'll breeze through the final exams without any trouble.

Synset Examples

She sailed through her exams
You will pass with flying colors
She nailed her astrophysics course

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sail through - to succeed in dealing with something easily
  • Antonym: struggle with - to experience difficulty in dealing with something
