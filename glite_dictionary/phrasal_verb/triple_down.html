to triple down

1. VERB

/ ˈtrɪp.əl daʊn / (AmE), / ˈtrɪp.əl daʊn / (BrE)
to increase one's commitment or level of effort, especially after a setback or during a risky situation
increase commitment or effort
informal

Examples

Despite declining sales, the company decided to triple down on its new product line.
Realizing the importance of education, she chose to triple down on her studies this semester.
Instead of backing off, he decided to triple down and work even harder than before.

Synset Examples

He chose to triple down on his investment despite the risks.
They plan to triple down on their efforts next quarter.
The company decided to triple down on their marketing strategy.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to double down - to strengthen one's commitment to a course of action

Etymology

Modeled after 'double down,' from gambling, where the phrase means to double a bet in blackjack, with 'triple' denoting an even greater increase.
