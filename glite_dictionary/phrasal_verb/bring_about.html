to bring about

1. VERB

/ brɪŋ əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / brɪŋ əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
to cause something to happen or exist
cause to happen

Examples

The government hopes to bring about significant improvements in education over the next decade.
Her actions finally brought about the changes everyone was waiting for.
It takes determination to bring about real social reform.

Synset Examples

This procedure produces a curious effect
The new law gave rise to many complaints
These chemicals produce a noxious vapor
the new President must bring about a change in the health care system

Related Terms

  • Synonym: cause - to make something happen
  • Synonym: effect - to make something happen, especially a change
  • Antonym: to prevent - to stop something from happening

2. VERB

/ brɪŋ əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / brɪŋ əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
to turn something, especially a boat, in the opposite direction
turn in the opposite direction (nautical)

Examples

The captain ordered the crew to bring about the vessel as the storm clouds gathered.
As soon as the danger became clear, they managed to bring about the yacht quickly.
Experienced sailors know when to bring about a boat to avoid rougher waters.

Synset Examples

they brought about the boat when they saw a storm approaching

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to turn around - to move or cause to move so as to face the opposite direction
