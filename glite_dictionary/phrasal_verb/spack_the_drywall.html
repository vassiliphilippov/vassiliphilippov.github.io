to spack the drywall

1. VERB

/ spæk ðə ˈdraɪˌwɔl / (AmE), / spæk ðə ˈdraɪˌwɔːl / (BrE)
to apply joint compound or filler to the seams or surface of drywall
apply filler to drywall seams or surfaces

Examples

After hanging the new panels, the contractor had to spack the drywall before sanding.
It's important to spack the drywall properly to ensure a smooth paint finish.
We watched a video on how to spack the drywall to fix holes and uneven surfaces.

Synset Examples

She learned how to spack the drywall effectively.
He decided to spack the walls before painting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: spackle - to fill holes or cracks, especially in walls, with a paste-like substance

Etymology

From 'spackling', a North American term meaning to fill cracks or holes with Spackle (a trademark for patching compound), extended as a verb.
