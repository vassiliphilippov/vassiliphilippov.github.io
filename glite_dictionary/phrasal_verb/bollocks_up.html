to bollocks up

1. VERB

/ ˈbɑːləks ʌp / (AmE), / ˈbɒlək sʌp / (BrE)
to make a mess of or ruin something, especially by handling it poorly; to botch
to mess up; to ruin (Brit. slang)
british slang vulgar

Examples

He really bollocksed up his driving test and failed it again.
Don’t bollocks up this report like you did last time!
They bollocksed up the whole project by missing the deadline.

Synset Examples

I botched the dinner and we had to eat out
the pianist screwed up the difficult passage in the second movement

Related Terms

  • Synonym: botch - to carry out a task badly or carelessly
  • Synonym: to screw up - to make a serious mistake or do something badly

Etymology

British slang; from 'bollocks' (testicles, nonsense), used figuratively for mistakes or messes.
