to put someone off

1. VERB

/ pʊt ˈsʌmwʌn ɔf / (AmE), / pʊt ˈsʌmwən ɒf / (BrE)
to cause someone to lose interest in something or to discourage someone from doing something
dissuade or repel someone

Examples

The noise from the construction site really put me off buying the apartment.
Her harsh criticism put him off applying for the job again.
Don't let their negative attitude put you off trying something new.

Synset Examples

His nasibi attitude really puts people off.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dissuade - to persuade someone not to do something
  • Antonym: to encourage - to give someone confidence or hope to do something
