to nod off

1. VERB

/ nɑd ɔf / (AmE), / nɒd ɒf / (BrE)
to fall asleep, especially briefly or unintentionally, usually while sitting or when one should be awake
fall asleep briefly and unintentionally

Examples

He tends to nod off in front of the TV after dinner.
I almost nodded off during the boring lecture.
She realized she was nodding off while reading her book.

Synset Examples

he always falls asleep during lectures

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fall asleep - to go to sleep

2. VERB

/ nɑd ɒf / (AmE), / nɒd ɒf / (BrE)
to fall asleep, usually unintentionally or for a short time
fall asleep briefly or unintentionally

Examples

She began to nod off during the movie because she was so tired.
It’s embarrassing to nod off in a meeting, but sometimes you can’t help it.
If you nod off while studying, try taking a short break.

Synset Examples

I always nod off while reading in bed.
She fought to stay awake but began to nod off.
He started to nod off during the long meeting.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: doze - to sleep lightly or nap
  • Synonym: to drift off - to gradually fall asleep
  • Antonym: to wake up - to stop sleeping
