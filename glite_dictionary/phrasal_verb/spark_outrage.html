to spark outrage

1. VERB

/ spɑrk ˈaʊtˌreɪdʒ / (AmE), / spɑːk ˈaʊtˌreɪdʒ / (BrE)
to cause widespread or intense public anger
provoke public anger

Examples

The politician's remarks sparked outrage across the country.
The decision to increase taxes sparked outrage among local residents.
Publishing the controversial article immediately sparked outrage on social media.

Synset Examples

His comments were radioactive and sparked outrage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: provoke outrage - to incite or arouse strong public anger
  • Degree Lower: spark criticism - to provoke strong public disapproval
