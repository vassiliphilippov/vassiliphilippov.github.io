to throw brickbats

1. VERB

/ θroʊ ˈbrɪkˌbæts / (AmE), / θrəʊ ˈbrɪkˌbæts / (BrE)
to criticize or attack someone or something harshly, especially in a public way
criticize harshly

Examples

Despite her good intentions, the press continued to throw brickbats at her policies.
Social media users were quick to throw brickbats after the company's controversial announcement.
Some people prefer to throw brickbats rather than provide constructive feedback.

Synset Examples

The critics threw brickbats at the new movie.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration

Etymology

From 'brickbat', meaning a piece of brick used as a missile, figuratively extended to mean a critical or insulting remark.
