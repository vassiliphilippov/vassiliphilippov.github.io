to sign on

1. VERB

/ saɪn ɑn / (AmE), / saɪn ɒn / (BrE)
to agree to become a member of an organization or to start working for an employer, especially by signing a contract
agree to join by signing a contract

Examples

After graduation, she decided to sign on with a large law firm.
The new coach will sign on for a two-year contract next week.
Many seasonal workers sign on during the busy holiday period.

Synset Examples

They signed two new pitchers for the next season

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to sign up - to register or enroll for something
