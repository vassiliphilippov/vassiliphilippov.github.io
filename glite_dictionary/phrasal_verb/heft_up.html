to heft up

1. VERB

/ hɛft ʌp / (AmE), / hɛft ʌp / (BrE)
to lift or raise something, especially with effort or to test its weight
lift or raise, especially with effort

Examples

He hefted up the heavy box and carried it to the next room.
She hefted up her backpack to check if she'd packed everything she needed.
The worker hefted up the sack of flour with a grunt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to lift up - to raise something to a higher position
  • Synonym: hoist - to raise or lift something, especially with a mechanical device

Etymology

From 'heft' meaning weight or to lift, possibly altered from 'heave'; combined with 'up' as a verbal particle.
