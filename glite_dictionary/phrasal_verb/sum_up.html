to sum up

1. VERB

/ sʌm ʌp / (AmE), / sʌm ʌp / (BrE)
to express the essential facts or main points of something in a concise way; to be a summary of something
state the main points concisely

Examples

To sum up, the project's success relied on teamwork and dedication.
One sentence cannot sum up everything she has achieved in her lifetime.
The final paragraph should sum up the main arguments of the essay.

Synset Examples

The abstract summarizes the main ideas in the paper

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to summarize - to give a brief statement of the main points
  • Synonym: recap - to repeat the main points of something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02758570-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ sʌm ʌp / (AmE), / sʌm ʌp / (BrE)
to find the total of several numbers or amounts by adding them together
add together to find a total

Examples

If you sum up these expenses, you'll see how much we spent on the trip.
They asked her to sum up the votes from each department.
Can you sum up all the donations we have received so far?
