to pull together

1. VERB

/ pʊl təˈɡɛðɚ / (AmE), / pʊl təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
to assemble or gather people or things; to organize different elements into a unified whole
gather or assemble; bring together

Examples

We need to pull together a team for the new project.
She managed to pull together all the required documents before the deadline.
Let's pull together our resources to solve this problem.

Synset Examples

gather some stones
pull your thoughts together

Related Terms

  • Synonym: gather - to bring together in one group or place
  • Synonym: collect - to bring or gather things together
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01383303-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ pʊl təˈɡɛðər / (AmE), / pʊl təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
to unite people or organize them effectively in order to achieve a common goal
to unite or organize effectively

Examples

If we all pull together, we can finish the project ahead of schedule.
The team really pulled together during the crisis and managed to solve the problem.
She encouraged everyone to pull together to help the community recover after the storm.

Synset Examples

They need to pull the team together for the project.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: coordinate - to organize people or things so that they work together effectively
  • Synonym: to collaborate - to work together with others to achieve something
  • Antonym: to fall apart - to become disorganized or stop functioning effectively
