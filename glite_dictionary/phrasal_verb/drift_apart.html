to drift apart

1. VERB

/ drɪft əˈpɑrt / (AmE), / drɪft əˈpɑːt / (BrE)
(of people) to gradually lose closeness or contact over time, often due to changes in circumstances or interests.
gradually lose closeness or contact

Examples

After college, the two friends drifted apart and rarely saw each other again.
Over the years, we have slowly drifted apart due to our different lifestyles.
Despite living in the same city, they found themselves drifting apart as their interests changed.

Synset Examples

The two women, who had been roommates in college, drifted apart after they got married

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grow apart - to become less close or friendly over time

2. VERB

/ drɪft əˈpɑrt / (AmE), / drɪft əˈpɑːt / (BrE)
to gradually become less close or connected with someone, often due to changes over time
gradually lose closeness with someone

Examples

After they went to different universities, they began to drift apart and lost touch.
Many old friends drift apart when their lives take different directions.
The siblings realized they had drifted apart over the years, seeing each other only at holidays.

Synset Examples

Over time, their interests caused them to drift apart.
Friends often drift apart after high school.
It's sad to see families drift apart over misunderstandings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: grow apart - to gradually become less close to someone
