to hammer home

1. VERB

/ ˈhæmɚ hoʊm / (AmE), / ˈhamə həʊm / (BrE)
to emphasize something strongly or make it very clear, especially by repeating it often
to emphasize strongly, make clear

Examples

The coach tried to hammer home the need for teamwork during practice.
News reports continue to hammer home the seriousness of the issue.
She hammered home her argument by providing clear and convincing evidence.

Synset Examples

She used examples to hammer home the message clearly.
He wanted to hammer home his point during the meeting.
The teacher tried to hammer home the importance of punctuality.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to emphasize - to give special importance to something
  • Synonym: underline - to emphasize or stress an idea
