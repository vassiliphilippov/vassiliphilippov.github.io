to snatch someone's purse

1. VERB

/ snætʃ ˈsʌmwʌnz pɝs / (AmE), / snætʃ ˈsʌmwʌnz pɜːs / (BrE)
to steal someone's handbag quickly by grabbing it, often unexpectedly and with force
to steal a handbag by grabbing it quickly

Examples

Be careful on crowded trains—someone might snatch your purse when you least expect it.
The police arrested a man who tried to snatch an old lady's purse in the market.
She screamed for help after a motorcyclist snatched her purse and sped away.

Synset Examples

The thief will snatch your purse if you're not careful.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: pickpocket - steal from people's pockets or bags secretly
  • Synonym: mug - to attack and rob someone in a public place
