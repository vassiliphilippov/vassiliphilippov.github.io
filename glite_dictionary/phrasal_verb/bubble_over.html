to bubble over

1. VERB

/ ˈbʌbəl ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˈbʌbəl ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
(of a person) to be so full of a particular feeling, such as joy or excitement, that it cannot be contained and is clearly expressed.
overflow with emotion or excitement

Examples

The children bubbled over with excitement when they saw the puppy.
She was bubbling over with ideas after the conference.
He bubbled over with enthusiasm as he described his new project.

Synset Examples

The children bubbled over with joy
My boss was bubbling over with anger

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overflow - to be so full that it spills out

2. VERB

/ ˈbʌb.əl ˈoʊ.vɚ / (AmE), / ˈbʌb.əl ˈəʊ.və / (BrE)
(of a person) to be so full of emotion or excitement that it is impossible to contain it.
be unable to contain strong emotion

Examples

The children couldn't help but bubble over with excitement on Christmas morning.
During the award ceremony, her gratitude began to bubble over in her speech.
His enthusiasm for the project bubbled over and inspired the whole team.

Synset Examples

With happiness, her emotions began to bubble over.
His joy started to bubble over during the celebration.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: overflow - to flow over the brim; spill out
  • Synonym: to bubble up - to surge or well up, especially of emotion or liquid
