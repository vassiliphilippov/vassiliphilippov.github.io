to mix in

1. VERB

/ mɪks ɪn / (AmE), / mɪks ɪn / (BrE)
to add something as an additional element or part, often by stirring or combining it with other ingredients
add as an additional element or part

Examples

Once the batter is smooth, gently mix in the chocolate chips.
She likes to mix in some herbs to enhance the flavor of the soup.
After you mix in the paint, the color should look even throughout.

Synset Examples

mix water into the drink

Related Terms

  • Synonym: combine - to join or merge two or more things
Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ mɪks ɪn / (AmE), / mɪks ɪn / (BrE)
to combine one substance or ingredient with another by stirring or blending them together
add one thing to another and mix

Examples

After you add the flour, mix in the chocolate chips thoroughly.
He forgot to mix in the spices before cooking the sauce.
Eggs should be mixed in gradually to prevent lumps.

Synset Examples

At this stage of making the cake, blend in the nuts

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to blend in - to mix smoothly with another substance
  • Synonym: stir in - to add and mix an ingredient by stirring
