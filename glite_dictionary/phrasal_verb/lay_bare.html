to lay bare

1. VERB

/ leɪ bɛr / (AmE), / leɪ beə / (BrE)
to reveal or expose something that was hidden, usually openly and completely
reveal or expose openly

Examples

The investigation will lay bare the truth behind the scandal.
The documentary aims to lay bare the realities of poverty in the city.
She chose to lay bare her struggles in the hope of inspiring others.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to conceal - to hide or keep something secret
