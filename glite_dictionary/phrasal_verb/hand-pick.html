to hand-pick

1. VERB

/ ˈhænd ˌpɪk / (AmE), / ˈhænd ˌpɪk / (BrE)
to choose (someone or something) personally and with great care, often for a special purpose
select personally and carefully

Examples

The chef hand-picked each ingredient for the menu.
She hand-picks her assistants to ensure they are highly qualified.
They spent hours hand-picking the best candidates for the scholarship.

Synset Examples

the director hand-picked his new team

Related Terms

  • Synonym: select - to choose from a number of alternatives
  • Synonym: to choose - to make a choice among options
