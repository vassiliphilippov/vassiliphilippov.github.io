to thresh about

1. VERB

/ θrɛʃ əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / θreʃ əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
to move, toss, or flail about violently, especially when agitated or distressed
move or flail violently

Examples

The sick child began to thresh about in bed as his fever worsened.
She threshed about in the water, desperate to stay afloat.
During the nightmare, he would thresh about until he finally woke up.

Synset Examples

The feverish patient thrashed around in his bed

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to thrash about - to move or toss around violently, especially in pain or agitation

Etymology

From 'thresh', meaning to beat or flail, originally referring to separating grain; extended to vigorous, erratic movement.
