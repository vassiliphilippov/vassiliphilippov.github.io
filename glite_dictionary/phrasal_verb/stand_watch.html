to stand watch

1. VERB

/ stænd wɑtʃ / (AmE), / stænd wɒtʃ / (BrE)
to remain on duty as a guard, especially to protect someone or something
be on guard duty

Examples

After the storm, the soldiers stood watch throughout the night to protect the camp.
One of us must stand watch at the entrance to warn of any danger.
She volunteered to stand watch while the rest of the group slept.

Synset Examples

We must stand sentinel to protect ourselves
The jewels over which they kept guard were stolen

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to keep watch - to remain on guard
  • Synonym: to keep guard - to act as a guard
