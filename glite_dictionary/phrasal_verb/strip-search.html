to strip-search

1. VERB

/ ˈstrɪp sɜrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈstrɪp sɜːtʃ / (BrE)
to search someone for concealed objects, such as weapons or drugs, by requiring them to remove some or all of their clothing
search someone by requiring removal of clothing

Examples

The security officers had to strip-search the suspect after finding suspicious items in his bag.
At the border crossing, a traveler was strip-searched due to unusual behavior.
Police may strip-search detainees if they suspect the person is hiding something illegal.

Synset Examples

He was strip-searched at the airport

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frisk - to pat down and search someone for concealed objects
