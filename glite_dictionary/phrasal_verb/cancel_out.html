to cancel out

1. VERB

/ ˈkæn.səl aʊt / (AmE), / ˈkæn.səl aʊt / (BrE)
to neutralize or counteract the effect of something so that it no longer has an impact
neutralize the effect

Examples

The two forces cancel out and the object remains still.
Her efforts were canceled out by unforeseen circumstances.
Adding acid can sometimes cancel out the effect of the base.

Synset Examples

The new tax effectively cancels out my raise
The ‘A’ will cancel out the ‘C’ on your record

Related Terms

  • Synonym: offset - to balance or compensate for something
