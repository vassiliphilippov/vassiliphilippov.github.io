to carve out

1. VERB

/ kɑrv aʊt / (AmE), / kɑːv aʊt / (BrE)
to establish or create something for oneself through sustained effort or determination
establish or achieve through effort

Examples

After years of hard work, she finally carved out a successful career in finance.
He managed to carve out a small niche for himself in the competitive tech industry.
The company hopes to carve out a larger share of the market next year.

Synset Examples

She carved out a reputation among her male colleagues

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to build up - to gradually develop or accumulate something
  • Antonym: to destroy - to damage or put an end to something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01762370-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ kɑrv aʊt / (AmE), / kɑːv aʊt / (BrE)
to establish or create something by separating it from a larger whole, often through effort or determination
establish by separating from a whole

Examples

The new company managed to carve out a significant market share in just two years.
She hopes to carve out a niche for herself in the fashion industry.
The government plans to carve out a new district from the surrounding area.

Synset Examples

the new start-up company carved out a large chunk of the market within a year

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to create - to bring something into existence
  • Antonym: annex - to add or attach, often by force
Source: WordNet

Synset POS: v
