to handle oneself

1. VERB

/ ˈhændl̩ ˌwʌnˈsɛlf / (AmE), / ˈhændl̩ ˌwʌnˈsɛlf / (BrE)
to conduct oneself appropriately or with composure, especially in difficult, stressful, or challenging situations.
act with composure or self-control

Examples

During the heated debate, she was able to handle herself with remarkable grace.
He struggled to handle himself after receiving the unexpected news, but soon regained his composure.
Learning to handle oneself under pressure is a valuable skill in professional life.

Synset Examples

She knows how to handle herself in a crisis.
It's important to handle oneself calmly during interviews.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: compose oneself - to calm oneself, especially when agitated or upset
  • Antonym: to lose one's cool - to lose composure or self-control
