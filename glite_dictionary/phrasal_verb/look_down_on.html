to look down on

1. VERB

/ lʊk daʊn ɑn / (AmE), / lʊk daʊn ɒn / (BrE)
to think that you are better or more important than someone else; to regard someone or something with contempt or disdain
regard with contempt

Examples

She tends to look down on people who have less education than she does.
He always looked down on his coworkers, thinking he was superior to them.
It’s wrong to look down on others based on their background or appearance.

Synset Examples

the new neighbor looks down on us because our house is very modest

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to despise - to feel contempt or a deep repugnance for
  • Antonym: respect - to admire someone or something deeply, as a result of their abilities, qualities, or achievements

2. VERB

/ lʊk ˈdaʊn ɑn / (AmE), / lʊk ˈdaʊn ɒn / (BrE)
to think that you are better or more important than someone, often treating them with contempt or disrespect
regard as inferior or with contempt

Examples

She tends to look down on anyone she considers less educated.
It's wrong to look down on someone just because of their job.
The wealthy family would often look down on their neighbors.

Synset Examples

She tends to look down on people who don't share her views.
He feels they look down on him for his job.
Don't look down on others; we're all equal here.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disdain - the feeling that someone or something is unworthy of respect
  • Antonym: to look up to - to admire or respect someone
