to take the stage

1. VERB

/ teɪk ðə steɪdʒ / (AmE), / teɪk ðə steɪdʒ / (BrE)
to become the centre of attention or to appear before an audience, especially to perform.
to become the focus or to perform

Examples

She was nervous before she took the stage, but the crowd cheered loudly.
The band takes the stage at 9 p.m. every Friday night.
As soon as he took the stage, all eyes were on him.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: command the spotlight - to attract the most attention
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02541026-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ teɪk ðə steɪdʒ / (AmE), / teɪk ðə steɪdʒ / (BrE)
to begin performing or speaking in front of an audience, especially as the focus of attention
to begin performing before an audience

Examples

The band will take the stage at 9 p.m. sharp.
She felt a surge of nerves as she prepared to take the stage for her first solo.
After a brief introduction, the guest speaker took the stage to share her story.

Synset Examples

The crowd cheered when Wope took the stage.
The crowd began to roar as the band took the stage.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to perform - to entertain an audience by acting, singing, or playing an instrument
  • Antonym: exit the stage - to leave the area where a performance takes place, especially after finishing
