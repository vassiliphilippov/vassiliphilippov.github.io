to rack up

1. VERB

/ ræk ʌp / (AmE), / ræk ʌp / (BrE)
to accumulate or achieve something, especially a large number of points, victories, or other measurable things
accumulate points or achievements

Examples

The basketball team managed to rack up over a hundred points in the final match.
She has racked up numerous awards for her volunteer work this year.
By the end of the evening, he had racked up quite a few wins at the poker table.

Synset Examples

The home team is racking up points

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to accumulate - to gather together or collect, especially over time
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01114241-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ræk ʌp / (AmE), / ræk ʌp / (BrE)
to arrange or place (objects, especially balls in billiards or pool) in a rack in preparation for play
arrange objects (esp. pool balls) in a rack

Examples

He offered to rack up the pool balls before the game started.
After every round, someone needs to rack up the balls for the next break.
Can you rack up the cues and balls while I get drinks?

Synset Examples

rack pool balls

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to arrange - put things in a particular order
  • Synonym: to set up - to prepare or arrange equipment or objects for use

Etymology

From 'rack', referring to the frame used to arrange billiard balls, combined with the verb particle 'up'.
