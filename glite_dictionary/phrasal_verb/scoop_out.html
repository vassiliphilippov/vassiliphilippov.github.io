to scoop out

1. VERB

/ skuːp aʊt / (AmE), / skuːp aʊt / (BrE)
to remove the inside of something by using a scoop or similar tool, often to make it hollow
remove the inside by scooping

Examples

She used a spoon to scoop out the seeds from the pumpkin.
You need to scoop out the center of the bread before adding the filling.
The archaeologist carefully scooped out soil from the ancient pot.

Synset Examples

scoop out a melon

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hollow out - to make empty inside by removing the inside material

WordNet ID: oewn-01284897-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ skuːp aʊt / (AmE), / skuːp aʊt / (BrE)
to remove something from a container or place by using a scoop or a similar movement, often forming a hollow
remove by scooping

Examples

She scooped out the seeds from the melon before serving it.
The workers scooped out mud from the drain after the heavy rain.
Use this spoon to scoop out the ice cream into your bowl.

Synset Examples

scoop the sugar out of the container

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dig out - to remove something by digging
  • Antonym: fill - to put something into a space or container
