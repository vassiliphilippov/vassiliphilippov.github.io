to hew out

1. VERB

/ hjuː aʊt / (AmE), / hjuː aʊt / (BrE)
to make or shape something, especially by cutting with a tool such as an axe, chisel, or saw
cut or shape by chopping

Examples

Workers carefully hewed out the steps from the solid rock.
The settlers had to hew out a clearing in the dense forest to build their homes.
An artist hewed out an impressive statue from a single block of marble.

Synset Examples

hew out a path in the rock

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to carve - to shape by cutting, especially wood or stone
  • Synonym: to sculpt - to shape or mold into a form, especially in art

Etymology

From Old English *heawan* (to cut, strike), combined with the particle 'out'.
