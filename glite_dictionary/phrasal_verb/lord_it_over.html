to lord it over

1. VERB

/ lɔrd ɪt ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / lɔːd ɪt ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to act in a superior or domineering manner toward someone; to assert authority or power in a way that shows excessive pride or arrogance
act in a domineering or superior way

Examples

He likes to lord it over his coworkers whenever he gets a promotion.
Don’t lord it over your friends just because you won the competition.
She was accused of lording it over the new employees.

Synset Examples

He is lording it over the students

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to domineer - to behave in an arrogant or overbearing way

Etymology

From the noun 'lord' meaning a person with authority or power, used as a verb in the phrase since the 16th century.

2. VERB

/ lɔrd ɪt oʊvər / (AmE), / lɔːd ɪt əʊvə / (BrE)
to behave in a superior or domineering manner toward others; to act as if you are better or more important than someone else
act in a superior, domineering way

Examples

He likes to lord it over his younger siblings whenever their parents are around.
After her promotion, she began to lord it over her coworkers in meetings.
They resented the way he would lord it over everyone at the office.

Synset Examples

She tends to lord it over her friends when she wins.
He loves to lord it over his colleagues with his title.
Don’t lord it over me just because you got promoted.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to dominate - to have control over; to exercise power
  • Antonym: humble - to make someone feel less important or proud

Etymology

From the noun "lord" (a person of authority) and the phrasal construction "it over" meaning to exercise power over.
