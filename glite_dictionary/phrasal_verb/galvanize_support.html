to galvanize support

1. VERB

/ ˈɡælvəˌnaɪz səˈpɔrt / (AmE), / ˈɡal.və.naɪz səˈpɔːt / (BrE)
to cause people to become excited or actively supportive of something
to stimulate or excite support

Examples

The politician's passionate plea managed to galvanize support among undecided voters.
Activists are working hard to galvanize support for environmental reforms in the city.
Her announcement was intended to galvanize support for the struggling charity.

Synset Examples

The speech helped galvanize support for the new initiative.
The coach aimed to galvanize the team before the final match.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: mobilize support - to organize people or resources for support

Etymology

From 'galvanize', meaning to stimulate to action, derived from Luigi Galvani, Italian scientist.
