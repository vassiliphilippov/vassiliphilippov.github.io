to break loose

1. VERB

/ breɪk lus / (AmE), / breɪk luːs / (BrE)
to escape from confinement or control; to get free by force or effort
escape from confinement or control

Examples

The prisoners managed to break loose during the riot.
One of the horses broke loose and ran into the crowd.
When the storm intensified, several boats broke loose from their moorings.

Synset Examples

The convicted murderer escaped from a high security prison

Related Terms

  • Synonym: escape - to get away from a place where you are being kept or held
  • Synonym: to break out - to escape from a place or situation
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02078906-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ breɪk lus / (AmE), / breɪk luːs / (BrE)
to suddenly become free or uncontrollable, especially with force, noise, or violence
suddenly become free or uncontrollable

Examples

The crowd began to break loose when the doors opened.
All chaos seemed to break loose after the announcement.
Suddenly, a storm broke loose over the city, catching everyone by surprise.

Synset Examples

His anger exploded

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to erupt - to burst out suddenly or violently
  • Synonym: to explode - to burst forth or emerge violently or loudly
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00307321-v
Synset POS: v
