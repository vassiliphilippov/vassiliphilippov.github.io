to stay in control

1. VERB

/ steɪ ɪn kənˈtroʊl / (AmE), / steɪ ɪn kənˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
to continue to have authority over a situation or to remain calm and composed
maintain authority or composure

Examples

During the crisis, the manager was able to stay in control and reassure her team.
It's important to stay in control of your emotions in stressful situations.
He tried hard to stay in control when the debate grew heated.

Synset Examples

They need to stay in control during the negotiations.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: lose control - fail to maintain authority or composure
