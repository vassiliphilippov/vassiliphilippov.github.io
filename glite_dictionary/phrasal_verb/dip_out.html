to dip out

1. VERB

/ dɪp aʊt / (AmE), / dɪp aʊt / (BrE)
(informal) To leave a place, event, or situation unexpectedly or quietly, often without notifying others.
leave unexpectedly or quietly (informal)
informal

Examples

I had to dip out before dessert because I wasn’t feeling well.
Whenever the conversation gets dull, he just dips out without saying goodbye.
They dipped out of the party quietly to avoid being noticed.

Synset Examples

She tends to dip out of social events unexpectedly.
They decided to dip out of the meeting early.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: leave - to go away from a place or event

2. VERB

/ dɪp aʊt / (AmE), / dɪp aʊt / (BrE)
(informal) To leave abruptly or avoid participating, especially to escape responsibility.
leave abruptly; avoid responsibility
informal slang american

Examples

Whenever there's work to be done, he dips out and disappears.
She dipped out of the meeting before her turn to present.
If you don't want to deal with this, you can't just dip out.

Synset Examples

He always dips out when it's time to help with chores.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to duck out - to leave quickly in order to avoid something or someone
