to bottom the house

1. VERB

/ ˈbɑtəm ðə haʊs / (AmE), / ˈbɒtəm ðə haʊs / (BrE)
to renovate, paint, or thoroughly clean a house in order to enhance its appearance
thoroughly renovate or clean a house

Examples

Before selling, they decided to bottom the house with new paint and fixtures.
She planned to bottom the house from top to bottom before the party.
Every spring, we bottom the house to keep it looking fresh and tidy.

Synset Examples

She suggested we bottom the house to enhance its curb appeal.
They decided to bottom the house with a fresh coat of paint.
We need to bottom the house before the guests arrive.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to spring-clean - to thoroughly clean a house, typically once a year
  • Synonym: to renovate - to restore to a good state by repairing or updating
  • Synonym: to decorate - to make more attractive by adding items or by painting
