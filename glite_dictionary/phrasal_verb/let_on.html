to let on

1. VERB

/ lɛt ɑn / (AmE), / lɛt ɒn / (BrE)
to reveal information that was intended to be kept secret; to disclose something known only to a few people
reveal secret information

Examples

Don't let on to Sarah that we're planning a surprise party for her.
He refused to let on about what had happened at work.
She tried not to let on how nervous she was before the interview.

Synset Examples

The auction house would not disclose the price at which the van Gogh had sold
The actress won't reveal how old she is
bring out the truth
he broke the news to her
unwrap the evidence in the murder case
The newspaper uncovered the President's illegal dealings

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to reveal - to make something known that was secret or hidden
  • Synonym: to disclose - to make information known; especially something previously secret
  • Antonym: hide - to keep something secret or unknown

2. VERB

/ lɛt ɑn / (AmE), / lɛt ɒn / (BrE)
to reveal or disclose information, especially something that was meant to be kept secret
reveal or disclose information

Examples

He knows more about the situation than he lets on.
She tried not to let on that she was nervous during the interview.
Please don't let on to anyone that we're planning a surprise party.

Synset Examples

Don't underestimate that yokel; he knows more than he lets on.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to divulge - to make known (private or sensitive information)
  • Antonym: to conceal - to keep something secret; to prevent from being known
