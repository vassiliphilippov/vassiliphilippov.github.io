to spring up

1. VERB

/ sprɪŋ ʌp / (AmE), / sprɪŋ ʌp / (BrE)
to appear, develop, or begin to exist quickly or suddenly, especially in large numbers or amounts
to appear or develop quickly or suddenly

Examples

New cafes seem to spring up on this street every month.
A sense of hope began to spring up after the announcement.
Rumors about the merger quickly sprang up throughout the office.

Synset Examples

A new religious movement originated in that country
a love that sprang up from friendship
the idea for the book grew out of a short story
An interesting phenomenon uprose

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to arise - to begin to exist or become noticeable
  • Antonym: to vanish - to disappear suddenly or completely

2. VERB

/ sprɪŋ ʌp / (AmE), / sprɪŋ ʌp / (BrE)
to appear, develop, or be created quickly and unexpectedly
appear or develop suddenly

Examples

New businesses often spring up in thriving neighborhoods.
Concerns began to spring up after the new policy was announced.
Wildflowers quickly spring up in the fields after the first rains.

Synset Examples

New cafes continue to spring up around the city.
Wildflowers spring up in the fields after the rain.
Ideas seemed to spring up during our brainstorming session.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to emerge - to come into view or become apparent
  • Antonym: to vanish - to disappear suddenly or completely
