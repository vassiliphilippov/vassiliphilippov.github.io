to thresh out

1. VERB

/ θrɛʃ aʊt / (AmE), / θrɛʃ aʊt / (BrE)
to discuss a problem, idea, or details thoroughly in order to reach a clear conclusion or solution
discuss thoroughly to clarify or solve

Examples

The committee will thresh out the key issues at tomorrow's meeting.
We stayed up late to thresh out the terms of the agreement.
Management and staff must thresh out their differences before the project can proceed.

Synset Examples

Let's thresh out our ideas during the meeting.
We will thresh out the problems together.
They need to thresh out the details before signing.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hash out - to discuss something in detail in order to resolve it

Etymology

From the agricultural term "thresh" (to separate grain from chaff), figuratively extended to mean separating the important parts of an idea.
