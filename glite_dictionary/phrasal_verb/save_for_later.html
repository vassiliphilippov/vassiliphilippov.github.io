to save for later

1. VERB

/ seɪv fər ˈleɪtər / (AmE), / seɪv fə ˈleɪtə / (BrE)
to keep something for future use or to postpone using or consuming something until a later time
keep for future use

Examples

She decided to save for later the last piece of cake.
I always save for later some money in case of emergencies.
You can save for later any articles you find interesting.

Synset Examples

He told her to save it for later.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to set aside - to reserve something for a specific purpose or future use
  • Antonym: to use up - to consume all of something
