to take to

1. VERB

/ teɪk tə / (AmE), / teɪk tə / (BrE)
to begin to like or to start doing something regularly, especially as a habit or occupation
develop a habit or start doing regularly

Examples

After moving to the countryside, she took to painting every morning.
He took to cycling as a way to relax after work.
The puppy took to its new home very quickly.

Synset Examples

She took to drink
Men take to the military trades
He took to visiting bars

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to give up - to stop doing something, especially a habit

/ teɪk tu / (AmE), / teɪk tʊ / (BrE)
