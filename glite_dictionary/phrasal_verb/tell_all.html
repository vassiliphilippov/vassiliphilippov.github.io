to tell all

1. VERB

/ tɛl ɔl / (AmE), / tɛl ɔːl / (BrE)
To reveal everything, especially secret, intimate, or previously unknown details about a person or event.
reveal everything, especially secrets

Examples

The former employee decided to tell all about what happened behind closed doors.
In her memoir, she plans to tell all about her years in show business.
Tabloid magazines often pay large sums for celebrities willing to tell all.

Synset Examples

He promised to tell all in the upcoming interview.
The documentary aims to tell all about his life.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to divulge - to make known something private or secret
  • Antonym: to conceal - to keep something secret, not reveal
