to invest bigly

1. VERB

/ ɪnˈvɛst ˈbɪɡli / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɛst ˈbɪɡli / (BrE)
to invest a very large amount, especially in an emphatic or informal manner
invest a large amount (informal, emphatic)
informal american

Examples

Many tech firms chose to invest bigly in artificial intelligence this year.
After seeing early results, she decided to invest bigly in the booming real-estate market.
He warned his friends not to invest bigly without thorough research.

Synset Examples

He invested bigly in the new startup.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: invest heavily - to invest a large or considerable amount

Etymology

Bigly derives from 'big' + '-ly', revived in modern usage in American informal speech.
