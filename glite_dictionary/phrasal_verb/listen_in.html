to listen in

1. VERB

/ ˈlɪs.ən ɪn / (AmE), / ˈlɪs.ən ɪn / (BrE)
to listen quietly to a conversation or broadcast, often without actively participating or being noticed
listen quietly without participating

Examples

She likes to listen in on her neighbors when they are arguing next door.
He didn't say anything during the meeting; he just listened in to everyone else's opinions.
Curious children sometimes listen in on adult conversations without being noticed.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to eavesdrop - listen secretly to a private conversation
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02174748-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ˈlɪs.ən ɪn / (AmE), / ˈlɪs.ən ɪn / (BrE)
to secretly listen to a conversation, usually without the speaker's knowledge or permission
secretly listen to a conversation

Examples

He decided to listen in on their phone call to find out what they were planning.
It’s rude to listen in when others are having a private conversation.
She suspected someone might be listening in on their conversation through the wall.

Synset Examples

the jealous man was eavesdropping on his wife's conversations

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to eavesdrop - to secretly listen to someone else's conversation
