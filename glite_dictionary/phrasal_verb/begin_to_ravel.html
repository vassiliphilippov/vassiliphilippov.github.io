to begin to ravel

1. VERB

/ bɪˈɡɪn tə ˈrævəl / (AmE), / bɪˈɡɪn tə ˈravl̩ / (BrE)
to start becoming confused or coming apart; to lose clarity or structure
to start becoming confusing or falling apart

Examples

As they added more details, the plot began to ravel and lost its original logic.
His explanation began to ravel when he was questioned further.
After the leader resigned, the organization quickly began to ravel.

Synset Examples

The story began to ravel after the twist.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to unravel - to undo or come apart; to clarify or separate threads
  • Synonym: to fall apart - to break up or lose structure
