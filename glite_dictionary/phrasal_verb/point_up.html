to point up

1. VERB

/ pɔɪnt ʌp / (AmE), / pɔɪnt ʌp / (BrE)
to emphasize or make something more noticeable, especially by identifying or highlighting it
emphasize or highlight something

Examples

The report points up the urgent need for reform in the system.
Her quick reaction pointed up the risks of ignoring safety procedures.
The new study points up several issues with the current approach.

Synset Examples

This novel points up the racial problems in England

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to emphasize - to give special importance or prominence to something
  • Antonym: to downplay - to make something seem less important or significant

2. VERB

/ pɔɪnt ʌp / (AmE), / pɔɪnt ʌp / (BrE)
to emphasize or draw attention to something; to make something more noticeable or clear
emphasize or highlight something

Examples

The investigation helped point up the weaknesses in their security system.
Several mistakes in the report point up the need for better training.
Her comments pointed up the main issues we need to address.

Synset Examples

Sales have really started to point up this quarter.
This study will point up the need for changes.
The report will point up the areas for improvement.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: highlight - to emphasize or make something stand out
