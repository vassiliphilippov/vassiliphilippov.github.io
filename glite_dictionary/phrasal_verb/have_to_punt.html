to have to punt

1. VERB

/ hæv tə pʌnt / (AmE), / hæv tə pʌnt / (BrE)
to choose to delay or avoid making a decision or taking action, often because of uncertainty or difficulty
defer or delay making a decision
informal american

Examples

The board couldn’t agree on the merger and decided they would have to punt until next month.
It’s a tough call, so we may have to punt and revisit the issue later.
Every time a controversial topic comes up, the committee seems to have to punt.

Synset Examples

If we can’t reach a consensus, we might have to punt.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to defer - put off an action or decision to a later time
  • Synonym: to postpone - delay taking action or making a decision

Etymology

Originally from American football, where 'to punt' means to kick the ball downfield, metaphorically meaning to postpone immediate action.
