to parch the fields

1. VERB

/ pɑrtʃ ðə fiːldz / (AmE), / pɑːtʃ ðə fiːldz / (BrE)
to dry out fields due to intense heat, especially from the sun
dry fields from heat

Examples

A prolonged drought can parch the fields, leaving crops withered and brown.
Farmers worried that another week of sun would parch the fields beyond recovery.
If we don't get rain soon, the relentless heat might parch the fields completely.

Synset Examples

The sun will parch the fields if it doesn't rain soon.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: scorch - to burn the surface of something with heat

Etymology

From 'parch' (Middle English, from Old French *parchier*, meaning to dry by heat) + 'the fields'.
