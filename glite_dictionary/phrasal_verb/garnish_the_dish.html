to garnish the dish

1. VERB

/ ˈɡɑrnɪʃ ðə dɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːnɪʃ ðə dɪʃ / (BrE)
to add small amounts of food or decoration to a dish to enhance its appearance or presentation
decorate food for presentation

Examples

Before serving, the chef likes to garnish the dish with edible flowers for a finishing touch.
Many cooking shows teach you how to garnish the dish to impress your guests visually.
You can garnish the dish with lemon zest to add color and flavor.

Synset Examples

She garnished the dish with a fresh sprig of parsley.
She garnished the dish with fresh dillweed for flavor.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to decorate - to make something look more attractive by adding items to it
  • Synonym: to adorn - to make more beautiful or attractive

Etymology

From Old French 'garnir' (to equip, furnish), related to the verb 'garnish' (to decorate), used in culinary contexts.
