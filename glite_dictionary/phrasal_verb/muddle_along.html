to muddle along

1. VERB

/ ˈmʌd.əl əˈlɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈmʌd.əl əˈlɒŋ / (BrE)
to continue with difficulty or without any clear plan, dealing with problems as they arise rather than following a definite course
to carry on confusedly or without a clear plan
informal

Examples

Many small businesses simply muddle along during tough economic times.
Despite having no clear strategy, they managed to muddle along and keep the project alive.
Sometimes you just have to muddle along until things become clearer.

Synset Examples

They decided to muddle along until they find solutions.
We just muddle along without any clear plan.
Sometimes, it’s better to muddle along than to rush.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to get by - to manage to survive or continue with difficulty
  • Synonym: struggle - to experience difficulty and make a great effort to do something
  • Antonym: plan - to make arrangements or a detailed proposal
