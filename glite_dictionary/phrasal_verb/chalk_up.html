to chalk up

1. VERB

/ tʃɔk ʌp / (AmE), / tʃɑːk ʌp / (BrE)
to record or register a point, score, or achievement, especially in a game or competition
record a score or achievement

Examples

At the end of each round, the referee will chalk up the team's points on the scoreboard.
The club managed to chalk up another victory, solidifying their lead in the league.
She was determined to chalk up her highest score yet in this tournament.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: score - to gain points in a game or test

Etymology

Originally from the use of chalk to record scores on a board.

Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00951873-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ tʃɑk ʌp / (AmE), / tʃɑːk ʌp / (BrE)
to accumulate or record a particular amount, score, or achievement, sometimes as a debt
to accumulate or record (e.g. a score, debt, or achievement)

Examples

He managed to chalk up another victory before the season ended.
She has chalked up considerable debts over the past year.
The team chalked up a record number of wins this season.

Synset Examples

he chalked up $100 in the course of the evening

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to accrue - to accumulate or receive over time
  • Synonym: to incur - to become subject to something unwelcome, such as a debt

Etymology

From the practice of recording scores or debts with chalk on a board.

Source: WordNet
