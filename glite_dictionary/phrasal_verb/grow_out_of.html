to grow out of

1. VERB

/ ɡroʊ aʊt ʌv / (AmE), / ɡrəʊ aʊt ɒv / (BrE)
to stop doing, using, or being affected by something as a result of maturing or developing
cease to do or use as one matures

Examples

Most children eventually grow out of their childhood fears.
She managed to grow out of her habit of biting her nails.
As he entered college, he began to grow out of his interest in comic books.

Synset Examples

Kids tend to grow out of their toys quickly.
She hopes to grow out of her shyness eventually.
He seems to have grown out of his rebellious phase.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to outgrow - to become too grown-up for something; to lose interest in as one matures
