to ring hollow

1. VERB

/ rɪŋ ˈhɑloʊ / (AmE), / rɪŋ ˈhɒləʊ / (BrE)
to sound insincere, unconvincing, or lacking in real value or truth
to seem insincere or unconvincing

Examples

After so many broken promises, his assurances ring hollow to the community.
Their sudden enthusiasm for charity work rings hollow given their past indifference.
The politician's apologies rang hollow in the wake of the scandal.

Synset Examples

The promise seemed to ring hollow in the end.
Her words ring hollow without any action.
His apologies ring hollow after everything he did.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: sound hollow - to seem insincere or lacking substance
  • Antonym: to ring true - to appear genuine or authentic

Etymology

Metaphorical extension of 'hollow' (empty or without real substance) applied to the sound of a 'ring'.
