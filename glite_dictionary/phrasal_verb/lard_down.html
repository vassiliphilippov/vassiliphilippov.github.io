to lard down

1. VERB

/ lɑrd daʊn / (AmE), / lɑːd daʊn / (BrE)
to make something richer, heavier, or more indulgent by adding fat, extra ingredients, or by smothering it
make richer or heavier by adding fat or extras

Examples

The chef chose to lard down the sauce with extra butter and cream.
Too many authors lard down their books with unnecessary details.
She tends to lard down her meals with cheese and rich toppings.

Synset Examples

He decided to lard down the recipe for a richer taste.
She likes to lard down her pancakes with extra syrup.
They tended to lard down the pizza with toppings.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to smother - to cover thickly or excessively
  • Synonym: to enrich - to make richer or better by adding something

Etymology

From 'lard' (to cover or enrich with fat) + 'down' (in the sense of applying or laying on).
