to sell short

1. VERB

/ sɛl ʃɔrt / (AmE), / sɛl ʃɔːt / (BrE)
to sell securities, commodities, or foreign currency not owned by the seller, hoping to buy them back later at a lower price and gain a profit
to sell securities not owned, betting on a price drop

Examples

Many investors sell short when they anticipate a fall in stock prices.
She decided to sell short several tech stocks during the market correction.
They lost money because they sold short just before the market rallied.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: short - holding a position in a security or commodity with the expectation that its value will fall

2. VERB

/ sɛl ʃɔrt / (AmE), / sɛl ʃɔːt / (BrE)
to underestimate the real value, ability, or potential of someone or something
underestimate someone's value or ability

Examples

She tends to sell short her own achievements, though she's actually very talented.
Don't sell short your abilities just because the task looks difficult.
It's a mistake to sell short young people; they can often produce brilliant ideas.

Synset Examples

Don't sell your students short — they are just shy and don't show off their knowledge

Related Terms

  • Synonym: underestimate - to judge something to be less important or valuable than it really is
  • Antonym: overestimate - to estimate something as being greater than it actually is

Etymology

Originally from finance, where 'sell short' means to sell shares you do not own, expecting their price to fall; adopted figuratively.
