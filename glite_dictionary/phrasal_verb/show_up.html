to show up

1. VERB

/ ʃoʊ ʌp / (AmE), / ʃəʊ ʌp / (BrE)
to become visible or noticeable; to appear so as to be seen or attract attention
become visible or noticeable

Examples

The stain didn't show up until the shirt was under bright light.
Mistakes often show up when you least expect them.
The cracks in the wall only show up in the sunlight.

Synset Examples

His good upbringing really shows
The dirty side will show

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to appear - become visible or come into sight

2. VERB

/ ʃoʊ ʌp / (AmE), / ʃəʊ ʌp / (BrE)
to appear or become visible somewhere, often unexpectedly or after being absent
to appear or become visible

Examples

He didn't show up for the meeting this morning.
Crowds began to show up at the concert hours before it started.
The missing document finally showed up on my desk.

Synset Examples

She turned up at the funeral
I hope the list key is going to surface again

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to turn up - to arrive or appear, especially unexpectedly
  • Antonym: to vanish - to disappear suddenly and completely

3. VERB

/ ʃoʊ ʌp / (AmE), / ʃəʊ ʌp / (BrE)
to arrive at a place, especially unexpectedly or after having been expected
to arrive at a place

Examples

We were all surprised when John showed up at the party unannounced.
Despite the rain, dozens of people showed up for the concert in the park.
She usually shows up to meetings a few minutes early.

Synset Examples

She laughed when he showed up in a palitard.
He showed up underpanted, forgetting to bring extra shorts.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to arrive - to reach a place, especially at the end of a journey
  • Synonym: to turn up - to arrive, often unexpectedly or after being awaited
  • Antonym: leave - to go away from a place
