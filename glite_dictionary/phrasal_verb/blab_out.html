to blab out

1. VERB

/ blæb aʊt / (AmE), / blab aʊt / (BrE)
to reveal confidential information or secrets, often carelessly or unintentionally
divulge secrets carelessly

Examples

Don’t blab out the surprise party plans to anyone.
She accidentally blabbed out the secret during lunch.
He tends to blab out confidential information when he’s nervous.

Synset Examples

Be careful — his secretary talks

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to spill the beans - to reveal secret information
  • Antonym: to keep quiet - to not reveal information

Etymology

From 'blab' (Middle English, to babble or talk idly) + out (as a verb particle meaning 'forth').
