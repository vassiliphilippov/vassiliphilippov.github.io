to finish up

1. VERB

/ ˈfɪnɪʃ ʌp / (AmE), / ˈfɪnɪʃ ʌp / (BrE)
to complete something entirely or bring an activity to an end, especially after a period of work
complete something entirely; bring to an end

Examples

Let me just finish up this email and then I'll be ready to leave.
She stayed late at the office to finish up the project before the deadline.
We should finish up our work so we can relax over the weekend.

Synset Examples

I finally got through this homework assignment

Related Terms

  • Synonym: finish - to bring something to an end; complete
Source: WordNet

2. VERB

/ ˈfɪnɪʃ ʌp / (AmE), / ˈfɪnɪʃ ʌp / (BrE)
to complete an activity or task, especially the last part of it
complete an activity or task

Examples

Let's finish up the project before the deadline tomorrow.
She always likes to finish up her meals with a cup of tea.
You should finish up any work left before leaving the office.

Synset Examples

He ended up marrying his high school sweetheart
he wound up being unemployed and living at home again

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complete - to finish making or doing something; bring to an end
  • Antonym: start - to begin doing or working on something
