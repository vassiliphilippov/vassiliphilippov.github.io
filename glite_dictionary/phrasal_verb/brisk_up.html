to brisk up

1. VERB

/ brɪsk ʌp / (AmE), / brɪsk ʌp / (BrE)
to become brisk; to become more lively, active, or energetic
become more lively or active

Examples

Business tends to brisk up during the holiday season.
The coach hoped the team would brisk up after halftime.
Sales brisked up once the new advertisement was launched.

Synset Examples

business brisked up

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to liven up - to become more lively or interesting
  • Antonym: to slacken - to become slower or less active
