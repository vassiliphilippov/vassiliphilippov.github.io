to gross out

1. VERB

/ ɡroʊs aʊt / (AmE), / ɡrəʊs aʊt / (BrE)
to cause someone to feel disgusted or sickened, especially because of something unpleasant or offensive
cause disgust or distaste
informal

Examples

That horror movie really grossed out my little brother.
Maggots in the trash will gross out most people.
She was grossed out by the smell coming from the fridge.

Synset Examples

This spoilt food disgusts me

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disgust - to cause a strong feeling of dislike or revulsion
  • Antonym: delight - to please someone greatly

2. VERB

/ ɡroʊs aʊt / (AmE), / ɡrəʊs aʊt / (BrE)
to cause someone to feel disgusted or sickened, especially to the point of losing composure or nerve
cause disgust or revulsion
informal

Examples

The horror movie really grossed out the entire audience.
He was grossed out by the sight of the spoiled food.
Don’t gross out your friends with stories about medical procedures.

Synset Examples

When he saw the accident, he freaked out

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disgust - a strong feeling of dislike or revulsion
  • Synonym: to nauseate - to make someone feel sick or disgusted

3. VERB

/ ɡroʊs aʊt / (AmE), / ɡrəʊs aʊt / (BrE)
to disgust or repulse someone, or to feel extreme disgust
to cause or feel disgust
informal

Examples

The smell of rotten eggs really grosses out my little brother.
I was totally grossed out by the sight of moldy food in the fridge.
Watching that surgery video will probably gross out most people.

Synset Examples

The kids were grossed out by the poop water in the puddle.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: disgust - strong feeling of dislike or revulsion
  • Synonym: repulse - to cause distaste or aversion
