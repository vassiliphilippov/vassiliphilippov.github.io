to burnish resume

1. VERB

/ ˈbɝː.nɪʃ rɪˈzuːm / (AmE), / ˈbɜː.nɪʃ rɪˈzjuːmeɪ / (BrE)
to improve or enhance one's resume to make it more impressive or attractive to potential employers
improve or enhance one's resume

Examples

Many students take internships to burnish resume before entering the job market.
He enrolled in advanced courses to burnish resume for competitive graduate schools.
Some applicants volunteer to burnish resume and stand out among thousands of candidates.

Synset Examples

He needed to burnish his resume before applying.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: polish resume - to make minor improvements to one's resume

Etymology

Burnish from Old French 'burniss-', from burnir 'to polish'; resume from French 'résumé' (summary).
