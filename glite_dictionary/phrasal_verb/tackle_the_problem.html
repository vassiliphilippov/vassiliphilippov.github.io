to tackle (the problem)

1. VERB

/ ˈtækəl ðə ˈprɑbləm / (AmE), / ˈtækəl ðə ˈprɒbləm / (BrE)
to confront or deal with a problem or difficulty in a determined and energetic way
deal with a problem energetically

Examples

The management team decided to tackle the problem head-on during the staff meeting.
Scientists around the world are working together to tackle the problem of climate change.
Despite her fear, she tackled the problem with creativity and persistence.

Synset Examples

He tackled the problem with enthusiasm as ever.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: address (the problem) - to think about or begin to deal with a problem
  • Antonym: ignore (the problem) - to deliberately not deal with a problem

Etymology

From Middle English 'takel' (equipment, gear); figurative use developed in 19th–20th century.
