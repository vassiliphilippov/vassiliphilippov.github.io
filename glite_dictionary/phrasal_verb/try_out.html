to try out

1. VERB

/ traɪ aʊt / (AmE), / traɪ aʊt / (BrE)
to perform or compete in order to be chosen for a role, position, or membership, especially in a play, team, or group
perform to be chosen for a role or team

Examples

She decided to try out for the lead part in the school play.
Many athletes will try out for the national team next month.
He wants to try out for the role of Romeo in the production.

Synset Examples

She auditioned for a role on Broadway

Related Terms

  • Synonym: audition - to give a performance to demonstrate suitability for a role

2. VERB

/ traɪ aʊt / (AmE), / traɪ aʊt / (BrE)
to test or experiment with something new in order to gain experience or discover its qualities
test or experiment with something new

Examples

She decided to try out yoga to see if it would help her relax.
The chef likes to try out new recipes every weekend.
He's going to try out a different route to work tomorrow.

Synset Examples

Related Terms
