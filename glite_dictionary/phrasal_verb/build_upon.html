to build upon

1. VERB

/ bɪld əˈpɑn / (AmE), / bɪld əˈpɒn / (BrE)
to use something as a basis for further development, ideas, or action
use as a foundation for development

Examples

Each new scientific theory tends to build upon the discoveries of the past.
The team decided to build upon the previous project's success rather than start from scratch.
Good writing often builds upon the ideas and research that have come before.

Synset Examples

what is this new evidence built on?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: expand on - to provide more detail or information about something
  • Synonym: elaborate - to develop or present in greater detail
