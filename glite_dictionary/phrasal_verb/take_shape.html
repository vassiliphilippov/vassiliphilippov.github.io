to take shape

1. VERB

/ teɪk ʃeɪp / (AmE), / teɪk ʃeɪp / (BrE)
to begin to develop and become more clearly defined or organized
begin to develop and become definite

Examples

After months of discussion, the new policy finally began to take shape.
You could see the sculpture slowly taking shape as she worked on it.
Our travel itinerary is starting to take shape with all the bookings confirmed.

Synset Examples

our plans began to take shape

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to emerge - become apparent, important, or prominent
  • Synonym: to materialize - become real or actual; appear in physical form

2. VERB

/ teɪk ʃeɪp / (AmE), / teɪk ʃeɪp / (BrE)
to begin to form or become clear and definite; to develop into a distinct or organized form
begin to form clearly; develop

Examples

The new policy began to take shape after several rounds of discussion.
As construction continued, the stadium slowly started to take shape.
Her vision for the company is beginning to take shape thanks to the team's efforts.

Synset Examples
