to burst in

1. VERB

/ bɝːst ɪn / (AmE), / bɜːst ɪn / (BrE)
to enter a place suddenly and unexpectedly, often interrupting others
enter suddenly and unexpectedly

Examples

The children burst in during the middle of our discussion, startling everyone.
Without knocking, he burst in and demanded an explanation.
She couldn't believe her friend would just burst in without any warning.

Synset Examples

They burst in, interrupting the quiet atmosphere.
She decided to burst in and surprise everyone.
He burst in right when the meeting started.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to barge in - to enter a place or conversation rudely or abruptly
  • Synonym: to burst out - to suddenly begin doing something, such as speaking or laughing
