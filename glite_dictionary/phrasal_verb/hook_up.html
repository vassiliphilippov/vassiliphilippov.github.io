to hook up

1. VERB

/ hʊk ʌp / (AmE), / hʊk ʌp / (BrE)
to connect or link something, especially by means of cables, wires, or pipes, so that it works as part of a system
connect or link (devices, systems, etc.)

Examples

The electrician will hook up the new stove to the power supply tomorrow.
Can you hook up my phone to the speaker so we can play music?
They had to hook up the computer to the internet before starting the update.
