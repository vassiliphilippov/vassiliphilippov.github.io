to have in

1. VERB

/ hæv ɪn / (AmE), / hæv ɪn / (BrE)
to invite or arrange for someone, especially a worker or professional, to come into one's home or premises to provide a service
arrange for someone to come in for a service

Examples

We're going to have in a decorator to repaint the living room.
Did you have in a plumber to fix the leak, or did you do it yourself?
They often have in caterers for big family parties.

Synset Examples

They will have the painters in next week for renovations.
We decided to have the painters in for a fresh look.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to call in - to ask someone to come and provide a service or help
