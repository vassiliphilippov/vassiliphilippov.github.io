to switch teams

1. VERB

/ swɪtʃ timz / (AmE), / swɪtʃ tiːmz / (BrE)
to change one's allegiance or group, especially by leaving one team, organization, or group to join another
change group or allegiance

Examples

After getting a better offer, she decided to switch teams next season.
It's common in professional sports for players to switch teams throughout their careers.
He was criticized for choosing to switch teams after the project began.

Synset Examples

It's not easy to switch teams mid-project.
After the season, she will switch teams for better opportunities.
He decided to switch teams for a fresh start.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: change sides - to join the opposite faction or group
