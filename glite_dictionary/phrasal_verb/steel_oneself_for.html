to steel oneself for

1. VERB

/ stil wʌnˈsɛlf fɔr / (AmE), / stiːl wʌnˈself fə / (BrE)
to mentally or emotionally prepare oneself for something difficult, unpleasant, or challenging
prepare oneself mentally for difficulty

Examples

She tried to steel herself for the bad news that was about to come.
He paused for a moment, steeling himself for the difficult conversation ahead.
Before opening the letter, Maria steeled herself for disappointment.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to brace oneself for - to prepare oneself mentally or physically for something unpleasant
  • Synonym: to prepare for - to make ready for something

Etymology

Figurative use of 'steel' meaning 'to make hard or strong,' influenced by the noun 'steel' (the hard metal).
