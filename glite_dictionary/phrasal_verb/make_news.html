to make news

1. VERB

/ meɪk nuːz / (AmE), / meɪk njuːz / (BrE)
to become the subject of news reports; to attract public attention by being considered newsworthy or notable
to become newsworthy or notable

Examples

The discovery of the ancient tomb quickly made news around the world.
Her innovative research has begun to make news in scientific circles.
When the small company landed a major contract, it made news in the business community.

Synset Examples

They hope their new product will make news this year.
Her charity work is starting to make news locally.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to make headlines - to be featured prominently in news reports
