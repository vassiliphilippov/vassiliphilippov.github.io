to catch air

1. VERB

/ kætʃ ɛr / (AmE), / katʃ eə / (BrE)
to jump high off the ground, becoming airborne, especially while performing a sport or trick
jump and become airborne (esp. in sports)
informal

Examples

With enough speed, you can catch air over the hill.
She loves to catch air on her mountain bike whenever the trail gets bumpy.
The skier managed to catch air and perform a backflip before landing.

Synset Examples

You can really catch air with that new board.
The snowboarder tried to catch air off the ramp.
He was thrilled to catch air on his skateboard.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: get air - to jump and become airborne, especially in sports
