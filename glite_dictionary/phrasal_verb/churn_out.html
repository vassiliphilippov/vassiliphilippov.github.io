to churn out

1. VERB

/ tʃɜrn aʊt / (AmE), / tʃɜːn aʊt / (BrE)
to produce something quickly and in large quantities, often without much thought or care; to perform a task in a mechanical or routine way
produce something routinely or mechanically

Examples

The factory continues to churn out cheap toys for export every year.
She can churn out a new novel in just a few months.
Unfortunately, these students only churn out essays without real understanding.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: produce - to make or create something
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01717954-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ tʃɜrn aʊt / (AmE), / tʃɜːn aʊt / (BrE)
to produce large quantities of something quickly, often without much care or attention to quality
produce something quickly in large amounts

Examples

The factory used to churn out thousands of toys every month before closing down.
She managed to churn out three novels in just two years.
Some websites churn out misleading information just to attract more visitors.

Synset Examples

He churns out papers, but they are all about the same topic

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to mass-produce - to produce goods in large quantities by machinery

Etymology

From the verb 'churn' (to agitate), suggesting repetitive, mechanical production.
