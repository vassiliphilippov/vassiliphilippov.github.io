to top off

1. VERB

/ tɑp ɔf / (AmE), / tɒp ɒf / (BrE)
to fill something to the top or to the point of almost overflowing
fill to the top; almost overflowing

Examples

Would you like me to top off your coffee before you leave?
She quickly topped off the tank so they wouldn't run out of gas.
After pouring the wine, he topped off each glass with a splash of soda.

Synset Examples

She topped off the cup

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to fill up - to make something full
  • Antonym: empty - to remove all contents
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00454249-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ tɑp ɔf / (AmE), / tɒp ɒf / (BrE)
to bring to a satisfying or suitable conclusion, especially by adding something extra as the final touch
finish or conclude with something extra

Examples

They decided to top off the evening with a walk along the river.
After a delicious meal, they topped off the celebration with dessert.
She likes to top off her outfits with a bright scarf.

Synset Examples

They topped off their dinner with a cognac
top the evening with champagne

Related Terms

  • Synonym: complete - to finish making or doing
  • Synonym: to cap off - to finish or conclude, often with a final touch
