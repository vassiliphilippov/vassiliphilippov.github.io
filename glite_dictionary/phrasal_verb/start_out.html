to start out

1. VERB

/ stɑrt aʊt / (AmE), / stɑːt aʊt / (BrE)
to begin a journey or set off for a place
begin a journey; set off

Examples

We decided to start out early so we could beat the morning traffic.
The hikers started out from the campsite just after sunrise.
She starts out for work every morning at 7 a.m.

Synset Examples

The family took off for Florida

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to set out - to begin a journey or to leave a place to go somewhere
  • Synonym: to depart - to leave, especially in order to start a journey
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02018017-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ stɑrt aʊt / (AmE), / stɑːt aʊt / (BrE)
to take the first steps in an activity or process; to begin something, especially a journey, project, or career.
begin an activity or process

Examples

When I start out on a new project, I like to plan everything carefully.
She started out as a waitress before becoming the restaurant manager.
Many companies start out small before growing into large businesses.

Synset Examples

We began working at dawn
Who will start?
Get working as soon as the sun rises!
The first tourists began to arrive in Cambodia
He began early in the day
Let's get down to work now

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to begin - to start to do something
  • Antonym: finish - to bring an activity or process to an end
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00346415-v
Synset POS: v
