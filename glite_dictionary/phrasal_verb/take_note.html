to take note

1. VERB

/ teɪk noʊt / (AmE), / teɪk nəʊt / (BrE)
to pay careful attention to something or notice it in order to remember or consider it
pay careful attention

Examples

Please take note of the instructions before starting the exam.
The officials took note of the public's concerns and promised to review the policy.
She is always taking note of the latest trends in technology.

Synset Examples

Take note of this chemical reaction

Related Terms

  • Synonym: notice - to become aware of something
  • Antonym: to ignore - to pay no attention to something
