to talk into

1. VERB

/ tɑk ˈɪntu / (AmE), / tɔːk ˈɪntuː / (BrE)
to persuade someone to do something, often by explaining reasons or convincing them
persuade someone to do something

Examples

She finally talked into joining the hiking club, even though he was hesitant at first.
It took some effort, but we talked into accepting the new proposal.
He wouldn’t have tried the spicy food if they hadn’t talked into it.

Related Terms

  • Antonym: to talk out of - persuade someone not to do something
  • Synonym: to persuade - cause someone to do something by reasoning or argument
