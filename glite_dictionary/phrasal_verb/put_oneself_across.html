to put oneself across

1. VERB

/ pʊt wʌnˈsɛlf əˈkrɔs / (AmE), / pʊt wʌnˈsɛlf əˈkrɒs / (BrE)
to express oneself clearly and effectively so that others understand one’s ideas or personality
express oneself effectively

Examples

She practiced her speech to make sure she could put herself across confidently.
He failed to put himself across during the group discussion, so his ideas weren't heard.
It's not always easy to put oneself across clearly in a foreign language.

Synset Examples

She knows how to put herself across in interviews.
He struggled to put himself across during the presentation.
It’s important to put oneself across clearly when speaking.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to come across - to be perceived in a particular way
