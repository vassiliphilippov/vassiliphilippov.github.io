to go through

1. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈθruː / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈθruː / (BrE)
to eat or use something completely and quickly, especially with great appetite or enthusiasm
consume completely, esp. with appetite

Examples

The kids can go through a box of cereal in one morning.
We went through all the snacks by the end of the party.
It's amazing how quickly they go through groceries every week.

Synset Examples

Some people can down a pound of meat in the course of one meal
The teenagers demolished four pizzas among them

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to devour - eat something eagerly or in large amounts
  • Synonym: to consume - to use up or eat up completely
  • Antonym: save - to keep and not use up
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01199565-v
Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈθru / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈθruː / (BrE)
to consider or examine something carefully and in detail; to work through thoroughly.
examine or consider thoroughly

Examples

Let's go through the list one more time to be sure we didn't miss anything.
She asked her accountant to go through her tax returns carefully.
The teacher will go through the instructions step by step before we begin the test.

Synset Examples

We worked through an example

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to work through - to deal with or examine something fully
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-01164263-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ ɡoʊ ˈθruː / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈθruː / (BrE)
to experience or endure a difficult or unpleasant situation, especially over a period of time
undergo a difficult experience

Examples

After losing his job, he had to go through a very tough period.
We all go through challenges at some point in our lives.
She went through a painful divorce last year.

Synset Examples

We had many trials to go through
he saw action in Viet Nam

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to undergo - to experience or be subjected to something (usually unpleasant or difficult)
  • Synonym: to endure - to suffer patiently through something difficult or unpleasant
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-02114674-v
Synset POS: v
