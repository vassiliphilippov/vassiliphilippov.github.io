to floor it

1. VERB

/ flɔr ɪt / (AmE), / flɔː ɪt / (BrE)
to press the accelerator of a vehicle to the floor in order to drive as fast as possible; to accelerate quickly
accelerate a vehicle very quickly
informal

Examples

When the police siren sounded behind him, he decided to floor it and speed away.
If we're going to catch the next train, you'd better floor it.
She floored it as soon as the traffic cleared.

Synset Examples

When the light turned green, he decided to floor it.
She told him to floor it if they wanted to make it on time.
He floored it as soon as the race started.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to accelerate - to increase the speed of a vehicle
  • Antonym: to slow down - to reduce speed or go more slowly

Etymology

From the verb "floor" (to press down to the floor) and "it" as a pronoun; refers to pressing the accelerator pedal to the floor.
