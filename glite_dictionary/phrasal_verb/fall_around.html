to fall around

1. VERB

/ fɔl əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / fɔːl əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to move about, tumble, or play in a carefree or jolly manner, often in a physical and spontaneous way
to tumble or play in a carefree manner

Examples

The children fall around on the playground, giggling loudly.
After the show, everyone fell around laughing and tumbling in the grass.
Puppies often fall around when they get excited and chase each other.

Synset Examples

They laugh and fall around on the grass.
The kids love to fall around in the leaves.
The puppy would fall around the yard, chasing butterflies.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: frolic - to play and move about cheerfully, excitedly, or energetically
