to rope down

1. VERB

/ roʊp daʊn / (AmE), / rəʊp daʊn / (BrE)
to descend a steep surface or cliff by sliding down a rope, usually using special equipment; to rappel
descend by sliding down a rope

Examples

After reaching the summit, they decided to rope down the north face of the mountain.
The rescue team had to rope down the cliff to reach the stranded hikers.
Before you rope down, make sure your harness and knots are secure.

Synset Examples

The ascent was easy — roping down the mountain would be much more difficult and dangerous
You have to learn how to abseil when you want to do technical climbing

Related Terms

  • Synonym: rappel - descend a rock face using a rope
  • Antonym: to ascend - move or go up
