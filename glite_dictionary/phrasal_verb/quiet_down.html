to quiet down

1. VERB

/ ˈkwaɪ.ət daʊn / (AmE), / ˈkwaɪ.ət daʊn / (BrE)
to become quiet or quieter; to reduce noise or activity.
become quiet or quieter

Examples

The teacher asked the children to quiet down so that class could begin.
After a few minutes, the noisy crowd finally quieted down.
The wind will quiet down this evening, making it easier to sleep.

Synset Examples

The audience fell silent when the speaker entered

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to calm down - to become calmer or less agitated
  • Synonym: to settle down - to become calmer or less noisy

2. VERB

/ ˈkwaɪət daʊn / (AmE), / ˈkwaɪət daʊn / (BrE)
to become silent or calm, or to make someone or something less noisy or excited
become silent or calm

Examples

The teacher asked the class to quiet down before starting the lesson.
After a few minutes, the children finally quieted down and listened to the story.
The wind began to quiet down as evening approached.

Synset Examples

Her stern expression made the students quiet down.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to calm down - to become less agitated or excited
  • Synonym: to settle down - to become quieter, more relaxed, or less active
  • Antonym: to liven up - to become more lively or energetic
