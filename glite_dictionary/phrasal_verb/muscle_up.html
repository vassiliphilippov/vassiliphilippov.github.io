to muscle up

1. VERB

/ ˈmʌsəl ʌp / (AmE), / ˈmʌsl ʌp / (BrE)
to gather strength, determination, or courage in preparation for a challenge
gather strength or courage
informal

Examples

Before the interview, she told herself to muscle up and remain confident.
They need to muscle up if they want to overcome these obstacles.
It's time to muscle up and face your fears head-on.

Synset Examples

He decided to muscle up and tackle the tough project.
You need to muscle up before the big game.
To win, they must muscle up and stay focused.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: brace oneself - prepare mentally or emotionally for something difficult
  • Synonym: to psych up - make oneself mentally ready for a challenge
  • Antonym: to back down - withdraw from a challenge; give up
