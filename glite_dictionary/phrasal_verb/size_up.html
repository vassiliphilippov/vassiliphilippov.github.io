to size up

1. VERB

/ saɪz ʌp / (AmE), / saɪz ʌp / (BrE)
to look at someone or something carefully in order to make a judgment or form an opinion
examine to form a judgment

Examples

He took a moment to size up the competition before the race began.
She quickly sized up the situation and decided to call for help.
As he walked into the room, everyone sized up the new manager with curiosity.

Synset Examples

he scrutinized his likeness in the mirror

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to assess - to evaluate or estimate the nature, ability, or quality of something
  • Synonym: to appraise - to assess the value or quality of someone or something
  • Antonym: to ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to someone or something

Etymology

From the verb "size" (to estimate or classify by size) combined with the particle "up" indicating completion or thoroughness; origins in late 19th-century American English.
