to inch forward

1. VERB

/ ɪntʃ ˈfɔrwɚd / (AmE), / ɪntʃ ˈfɔːwəd / (BrE)
to move slowly and gradually, often with caution or in small increments
move forward slowly and cautiously

Examples

The car began to inch forward through the heavy traffic.
He had to inch forward to see the display more clearly.
Nervous about being noticed, she inched forward in her chair.

Synset Examples

The parade started to inch forward as the music played.
They had to inch forward to get a better view.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: creep - to move slowly and carefully, especially in order to avoid being heard or noticed
  • Degree Higher: advance - to move forward, typically in a purposeful way
  • Antonym: stop - to cease movement or operation

Etymology

From the noun 'inch' meaning a small unit of length, used figuratively for small movement; 'forward' meaning ahead.
