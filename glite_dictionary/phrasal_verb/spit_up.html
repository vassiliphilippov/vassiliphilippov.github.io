to spit up

1. VERB

/ spɪt ʌp / (AmE), / spɪt ʌp / (BrE)
to give or hand over something, especially money, reluctantly or under pressure
give something reluctantly
informal

Examples

The company finally had to spit up the missing funds after weeks of delay.
He didn't want to, but eventually he spit up the information they needed.
We all had to spit up extra cash to cover the unexpected repairs.

Synset Examples

He coughed up some money for his children's tuition

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to cough up - to give or hand over money reluctantly; to surrender reluctantly

2. VERB

/ spɪt ʌp / (AmE), / spɪt ʌp / (BrE)
to discharge phlegm, sputum, or saliva from the mouth, typically by forcefully expelling it from the lungs or throat
discharge phlegm or saliva from the mouth

Examples

He started to spit up phlegm after a week of having a cold.
Babies sometimes spit up milk after feeding, but adults may spit up phlegm when they are ill.
The doctor asked him how often he spits up mucus during the day.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to expectorate - to cough or spit out phlegm from the throat or lungs
