to cavern out

1. VERB

/ ˈkævərn aʊt / (AmE), / ˈkævən aʊt / (BrE)
To hollow out something deeply or extensively, as if making a cavern.
hollow out extensively

Examples

The engineers began to cavern out a new tunnel beneath the mountain.
Years of erosion gradually caverned out the limestone cliffs along the coast.
Workers used specialized equipment to cavern out storage spaces within the rock.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to hollow out - to remove the inside of something so that it becomes empty or concave
