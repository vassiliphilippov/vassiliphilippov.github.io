to tangle with

1. VERB

/ ˈtæŋɡəl wɪð / (AmE), / ˈtaŋɡl wɪð / (BrE)
to become involved in a conflict or difficult situation with someone or something, often involving opposition or struggle
get involved in conflict with

Examples

She didn’t want to tangle with her boss over such a minor issue.
Many leaders have tried to tangle with bureaucracy, but few have succeeded.
He regrets tangling with the law during his youth.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to confront - to face someone or something, especially in a challenging way
  • Antonym: to avoid - to keep away from or not become involved with
