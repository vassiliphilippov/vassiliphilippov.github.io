to splash some paint

1. VERB

/ splæʃ səm peɪnt / (AmE), / splæʃ səm peɪnt / (BrE)
to apply paint to a surface in a quick, casual, or unrefined manner, often without concern for precision
apply paint quickly or casually

Examples

He decided to splash some paint on the canvas and see what happened.
Sometimes it's fun to just splash some paint on the wall without worrying about technique.
She splash some paint across the boards to create a vibrant background.

Synset Examples

Related Terms

  • Synonym: dab - to touch lightly or apply in small amounts, especially paint
