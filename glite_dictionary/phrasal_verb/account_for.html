to account for

1. VERB

/ əˈkaʊnt fɔr / (AmE), / əˈkaʊnt fə / (BrE)
to be the reason or explanation for something; to explain the cause of something
be the reason or explanation for

Examples

Lower sales this quarter account for the company's reduced profit.
The sudden increase in temperature can account for the melting of the ice.
Lack of evidence may account for the jury's decision.

Synset Examples

The recession accounts for the slow retail business

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to explain - to make something clear or easy to understand
  • Synonym: cause - to make something happen; the reason for something
  • Degree Lower: result from - to happen as a result of something

Synset POS: v

2. VERB

/ əˈkaʊnt fɔr / (AmE), / əˈkaʊnt fə / (BrE)
to give an explanation or justification for something; to provide reasons for or explain the cause of something
give an explanation for something

Examples

He could not account for the missing money during the audit.
The sudden drop in temperature can be accounted for by the cold front moving in.
Each employee must account for their whereabouts during working hours.

Synset Examples

Can you account for all these absences?

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to explain - to make something clear or easy to understand by describing or giving reasons for it
  • Synonym: excuse - to try to remove blame by giving reasons
Source: WordNet
WordNet ID: oewn-00941652-v
Synset POS: v

3. VERB

/ əˈkaʊnt fər / (AmE), / əˈkaʊnt fə / (BrE)
to provide an explanation or justification for something; to be the cause of something
explain or justify; be the cause of

Examples

The sudden drop in temperature may account for the increased number of colds in the area.
His illness accounts for his recent absence from work.
The company could not account for the missing funds during the audit.

Synset Examples

He always forgets his keys; that accounts for the milk in the coconut.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to explain - make something clear or easy to understand; give a reason for
  • Synonym: to justify - show or prove to be right or reasonable
