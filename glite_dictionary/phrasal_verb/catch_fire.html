to catch fire

1. VERB

/ kætʃ ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / kætʃ ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
to start burning or burst into flames suddenly
begin to burn

Examples

The curtains began to catch fire after being too close to the candle.
Be careful not to let the paper catch fire in the fireplace.
The old barn caught fire during the thunderstorm.

Synset Examples

Marsh gases ignited suddenly
The oily rags combusted spontaneously

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to ignite - to catch fire or cause to catch fire
  • Antonym: to extinguish - to cause a fire or light to stop burning
