to luck through

1. VERB

/ lʌk θru / (AmE), / lʌk θruː / (BrE)
to succeed at something by chance rather than by skill or effort
succeed by chance, not effort

Examples

He didn't study for the test but managed to luck through anyway.
Some teams just luck through the early rounds of the tournament without real strategy.
I often luck through interviews despite feeling unprepared.

Synset Examples

She managed to luck through the exam despite minimal studying.
Sometimes you just have to luck through tough times.

Related Terms

  • Synonym: to pull off - to succeed in doing something difficult
  • Antonym: to fail - to not succeed in achieving something
